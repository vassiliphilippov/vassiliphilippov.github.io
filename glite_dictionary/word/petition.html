petition

1. VERB

/ pəˈtɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˈtɪʃən / (BrE)
To make a formal written request to an authority or official, usually signed by many people, asking for a particular action or decision.
to formally request in writing

Examples

The citizens decided to petition the local government for better public transportation.
She plans to petition the court for custody of her children.
Environmental groups petitioned the mayor, urging him to enact stricter pollution controls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: request - to ask for something formally or politely
  • Degree Higher: demand - to ask for something forcefully or insistently

Etymology

From Latin 'petitio' (a request, solicitation), from 'petere' (to seek, request).

2. NOUN

/ pəˈtɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˈtɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
A formal written request, typically one signed by many people, appealing to an authority concerning a particular cause.
formal written request to authority

Examples

Over 5,000 people signed the petition asking for better public transport.
The students submitted a petition to the principal to extend library hours.
The city council considered the petition for a new playground during their meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: request - an act of asking politely or formally for something

Etymology

From Latin petitio, petitionis (a request), from petere (to seek).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06525357-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ pɪˈtɪʃən / (AmE), / pɪˈtɪʃən / (BrE)
A solemn or reverent request, especially one addressed to a deity or divine being.
reverent request to a deity

Examples

His nightly petition was for the health and happiness of his family.
The ancient people offered a petition to the gods for rain.
Many religions include some form of petition in their rituals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prayer - a solemn request for help or expression of thanks addressed to God or another deity
  • Synonym: supplication - the action of asking or begging for something earnestly or humbly, often to a deity

Etymology

From Latin 'petitio' (a request or entreaty), from 'petere' (to seek or ask).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
