eclecticism

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈklɛktɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˈklɛktɪsɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The practice of selecting ideas, styles, or methods from a diverse range of sources rather than adhering to a single system or doctrine.
choosing from various sources or styles

Examples

The architect’s eclecticism is evident in her use of both classical and modern design elements.
Literary eclecticism has led to the blending of genres within the novel.
He approaches music with a sense of eclecticism, drawing inspiration from many traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eclectic - selecting from various sources; not following one system

Etymology

From Greek eklektikos (selective), from eklegein (to select).
