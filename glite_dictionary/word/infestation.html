infestation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnfɛˈsteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnfɛsˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a large number of insects or other pests that attack plants, animals, or places, often causing damage
swarm of pests or insects causing damage

Examples

This year's crops suffered extensive damage from a infestation of locusts.
After noticing the infestation, the farmers immediately sprayed the fields.
The apple orchard was devastated by an infestation of caterpillars.

Synset Examples

a plague of grasshoppers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plague - large and uncontrollable outbreak of insects or pests

Etymology

From Latin infestatio (attack, harassment), from infestare (to attack).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnfɛˈsteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnfɛˈsteɪʃən / (BrE)
The presence of a large number of animals or insects, especially those that cause damage or disease, in a place where they are not wanted.
presence of many parasites or pests

Examples

The crops suffered a severe infestation of locusts, destroying much of the harvest.
A flea infestation can cause health problems for both pets and their owners.
The building was shut down due to a infestation of rats.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infection - the invasion of the body by pathogenic microorganisms
  • Synonym: parasitism - the practice of living as a parasite in or on another organism

Etymology

From Latin infestatio, from infestare (to attack or disturb).
