Leonid Ilyich Brezhnev

1. PROPN

/ liˈoʊnɪd ɪˈliɪtʃ ˈbrɛʒˌnɛv / (AmE), / liˈəʊnɪd ɪˈliːɪtʃ ˈbrɛʒnɛf / (BrE)
(1906–1982) Soviet statesman who was General Secretary of the Communist Party and effectively leader of the Soviet Union from 1964 to 1982.
Soviet leader (1964–1982)

Examples

Leonid Ilyich Brezhnev held power in the Soviet Union for nearly two decades.
During the era of Leonid Ilyich Brezhnev, the Soviet Union experienced a period referred to as the Brezhnev Stagnation.
Many major events in Cold War history occurred under the leadership of Leonid Ilyich Brezhnev.
