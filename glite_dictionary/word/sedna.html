Sedna

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɛdnə / (AmE), / ˈsɛdnə / (BrE)
a distant planetoid of rock and ice, about three-quarters the size of Pluto, discovered in 2003 beyond the orbit of Neptune; currently one of the most distant known objects orbiting the Sun
distant planetoid beyond Neptune, discovered in 2003

Examples

NASA scientists announced the discovery of Sedna in 2003, expanding our understanding of the outer solar system.
The orbit of Sedna takes it far beyond Pluto, making it one of the most remote objects known.
Astronomers are fascinated by Sedna because its unusual path could hint at more undiscovered bodies beyond Neptune.

Etymology

Named after Sedna, the Inuit goddess of the sea.
