silt

1. VERB

/ sɪlt / (AmE), / sɪlt / (BrE)
(of a river, harbor, etc.) to become filled or blocked with silt.
become blocked with fine sediment

Examples

Many small harbors silt after heavy rains, making navigation difficult.
If the canals are not maintained, they may eventually silt and become unusable.
Over the years, the reservoir began to silt, reducing its water storage capacity.

Synset Examples

The river silted up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to silt up - become blocked with silt

2. NOUN

/ sɪlt / (AmE), / sɪlt / (BrE)
fine sand, clay, or other material carried by running water and deposited as sediment, especially in a channel or harbor
fine sediment deposited by water

Examples

The river deposited a thick layer of silt on the floodplain after the storm.
Farmers often find the silt left by annual floods fertile for crops.
Over time, silt gradually filled the old harbor, making it unusable.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sand - loose granular material resulting from the erosion of rocks
  • Degree Lower: clay - a sticky fine-grained earth that can be molded when wet
  • Synonym: mud - soft, wet, earthy matter

Etymology

Middle English, perhaps from Old French 'cilte', of Germanic origin.
