okay

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊˌkeɪ / (AmE), / əʊˈkeɪ / (BrE)
An official approval, permission, or endorsement to proceed with something.
official approval or permission

Examples

We finally got the okay from our manager to start the project.
The team cannot proceed without the client's okay first.
He gave me the okay to use his car for the weekend.

Synset Examples

they gave us the O.K. to go ahead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authorization - official permission or approval
  • Antonym: denial - refusal to approve or endorse

Etymology

Most likely from the initial letters of 'oll korrect', a humorous misspelling of 'all correct' popularized in 19th-century America.

to give formal approval or permission to something; to sanction
give permission or sanction
informal american

Examples
