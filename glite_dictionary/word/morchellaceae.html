Morchellaceae

1. NOUN

/ mɔːrˌkɛləˈsiːiː / (AmE), / mɔːˌkɛləˈsiːɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of edible fungi that includes the true morels
family of fungi including true morels

Examples

The Morchellaceae family is prized by mushroom hunters for its delicious morels.
Many chefs consider fungi from the Morchellaceae among the most desirable wild mushrooms.
Recent studies have expanded the classification within the Morchellaceae to include several new genera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morel - an edible mushroom with a honeycombed cap, belonging to Morchella genus

Etymology

From genus name Morchella + Latin -aceae (suffix for family names in taxonomy).
