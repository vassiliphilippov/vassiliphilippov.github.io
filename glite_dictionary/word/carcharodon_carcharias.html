Carcharodon carcharias

1. NOUN

/ kɑːrˌkærəˈdɑn kɑːrˈkaɪəriəs / (AmE), / kɑːˌkærəˈdəʊn kɑːˈkeəriəs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the great white shark, a large, aggressive predatory shark found in warm seas, known for occasional attacks on humans
scientific name of the great white shark

Examples

The Carcharodon carcharias can reach lengths of over six meters and is feared by swimmers worldwide.
Marine biologists study the behavior and migration patterns of Carcharodon carcharias to better understand this apex predator.
The movie Jaws was inspired by encounters with Carcharodon carcharias off the coast of New England.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: great white shark - large predatory shark species, also known as Carcharodon carcharias
  • Degree Higher: shark - a large marine fish with a cartilaginous skeleton and a reputation as a predator

Etymology

From Greek karcharos (sharp) and odous, odontos (tooth), meaning 'sharp tooth'.
