Styrax

1. NOUN

/ ˈstaɪˌræks / (AmE), / ˈstaɪ.raks / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous or evergreen shrubs and small trees, some species of which are known for producing aromatic resins such as storax.
a genus of shrubs and small trees

Examples

Botanists recently described a new species within the Styrax genus.
Many perfumes contain extracts from Styrax trees.
The Styrax in my garden blooms with clusters of white flowers in spring.

Etymology

From Latin *styrax*, from Ancient Greek *styrax* (a type of tree or resin).
