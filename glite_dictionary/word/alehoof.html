alehoof

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪlˌhuːf / (AmE), / ˈeɪlˌhuːf / (BrE)
A trailing European plant of the mint family, with rounded leaves and small purplish flowers, often grown in hanging baskets and sometimes known as ground-ivy.
aromatic plant of the mint family

Examples

The leaves of alehoof were traditionally used in herbal remedies.
Alehoof can be found growing wild in many North American gardens.
Gardeners appreciate alehoof for its trailing habit and small purple flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground-ivy - another common name for alehoof

Etymology

From Middle English alehoof (originally 'ale' + 'hoof', old word for 'plant'), as it was used in brewing ale.
