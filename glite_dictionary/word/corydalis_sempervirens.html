Corydalis sempervirens

1. NOUN

/ kɔːɹɪˈdeɪlɪs sɛmpərˈvaɪrənz / (AmE), / kɒrɪˈdalɪs sɛmpəˈvʌɪrənz / (BrE)
A glaucous herb native to northeastern United States and Canada, with loose clusters (racemes) of yellow-tipped pink flowers; sometimes classified in the genus Fumaria.
North American flowering herb of the poppy family

Examples

The rocky hillside was dotted with blooming Corydalis sempervirens in spring.
Botanists observed that Corydalis sempervirens thrives in disturbed forest areas.
She identified the plant as Corydalis sempervirens by its pink and yellow-tipped flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fumaria - A genus of flowering plants sometimes used to classify Corydalis sempervirens

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Corydalis' meaning 'crested lark' (from Greek korudallis), and 'sempervirens' meaning 'evergreen' or 'always green'.
