Procellaria aequinoctialis

1. NOUN

/ proʊˌsɛləriə ˌiːkwɪnɒkˈtiːælɪs / (AmE), / prəʊˌsɛləriə ˌiːkwɪnɒkˈtiːælɪs / (BrE)
A large species of black petrel found in the southern oceans, distinguished by a white mark on its chin.
species of large black petrel with white chin mark

Examples

The Procellaria aequinoctialis is often seen following ships in the South Atlantic.
Researchers tracked the migration patterns of Procellaria aequinoctialis during the breeding season.
Birdwatchers hope to spot the distinctive white chin of the Procellaria aequinoctialis during their southern cruises.

Etymology

From Latin: 'procellaria' (pertaining to storms) and 'aequinoctialis' (of the equator).
