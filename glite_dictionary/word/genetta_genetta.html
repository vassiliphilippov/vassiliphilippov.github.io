Genetta genetta

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈnɛtə dʒəˈnɛtə / (AmE), / dʒɛˈnɛtə dʒɛˈnɛtə / (BrE)
A small, agile carnivorous mammal of the Old World, with a spotted coat and a long ringed tail, also known as the common genet.
Old World carnivorous mammal (common genet)

Examples

The Genetta genetta is native to Africa but has also established populations in southwestern Europe.

With its characteristic spotted fur, the Genetta genetta is easily distinguished from other members of its family.

Related Terms

Etymology

From New Latin genus 'Genetta', based on French 'genette' from Spanish 'gineta'.
