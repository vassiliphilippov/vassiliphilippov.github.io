dodderer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːdərər / (AmE), / ˈdɒdərə / (BrE)
an elderly person who moves in a weak or unsteady way, especially due to old age or frailty
elderly person who moves unsteadily

Examples

The old dodderer shuffled down the path, leaning heavily on his cane.
Despite being thought of as a dodderer, he still had a sharp wit and keen memory.
She refused to be seen as just another dodderer in the nursing home.

Etymology

From the verb 'dodder' (to tremble or totter), possibly of Scandinavian origin.
