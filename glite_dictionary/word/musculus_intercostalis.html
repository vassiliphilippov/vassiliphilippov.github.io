musculus intercostalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌs.kjəl.əs ˌɪn.tərˈkoʊ.stæl.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kjʊ.ləs ˌɪn.təˈkɒs.tæl.ɪs / (BrE)
Any of the muscles situated between the ribs that contract during inspiration to aid breathing.
muscle between the ribs
medical

Examples

During deep breathing exercises, the musculus intercostalis plays a key role in expanding the ribcage.
The doctor explained that pain between the ribs might involve the musculus intercostalis.
In anatomy class, we learned how the musculi intercostales assist in inhalation and exhalation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intercostal muscle - a muscle located between the ribs

Etymology

From Latin 'musculus' (muscle) and 'intercostalis' (between the ribs).
