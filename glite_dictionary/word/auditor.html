auditor

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːdɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪtə / (BrE)
a person who listens attentively or is a member of an audience
person who listens attentively

Examples

The auditor sat quietly and absorbed every word of the lecture.
Each auditor in the hall was required to remain silent and attentive.
As an auditor at the conference, she was not allowed to participate in discussions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: listener - a person who listens

Etymology

From Latin 'audītor' (listener), from 'audīre' (to hear).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔːdɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪtə / (BrE)
A student who attends a course without receiving academic credit for it.
student attending a class without credit

Examples

As an auditor, she could attend classes but not participate in exams.
The university allows anyone to become an auditor for a small fee.
He enrolled as an auditor to learn more without the pressure of grades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: student - a person who is studying at a school or college

Etymology

From Latin 'auditor' (listener, hearer), from 'audire' (to hear).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09842555-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈɔdɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪtə / (BrE)
a qualified person who officially examines and verifies the financial accounts and records of an organization
person who examines financial accounts

Examples

The company's auditor found several irregularities in the annual financial report.
An external auditor will review the organization's accounts to ensure compliance.
She trained for years to become a certified auditor at a leading firm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accountant - person who keeps or inspects financial accounts

Etymology

From Latin 'auditor' (hearer, examiner), from 'audire' (to hear).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
