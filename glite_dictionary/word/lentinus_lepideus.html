Lentinus lepideus

1. NOUN

/ lɛnˈtaɪnəs lɛˈpɪdiəs / (AmE), / lɛnˈtɪnʊs lɛˈpɪdɪəs / (BrE)
A species of fungus with a scaly cap, white flesh, a ring on the stalk with scales below the ring, and an odor reminiscent of licorice.
a species of fungus with a scaly cap

Examples

The mushroom identified as Lentinus lepideus was growing on the old railway tie.
Researchers studied the enzymatic properties of Lentinus lepideus for industrial applications.
With its distinct scaly cap, Lentinus lepideus is easily recognized by mycologists.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Lentinus' (fungus genus), 'lepideus' (scaly, from 'lepis' meaning scale).
