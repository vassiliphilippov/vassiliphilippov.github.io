to restrain

1. VERB

/ rɪˈstreɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈstreɪn / (BrE)
to prevent someone or yourself from doing something, especially by controlling emotions or actions
prevent action or expression

Examples

She struggled to restrain her laughter during the serious meeting.
The teacher had to restrain the students from running in the hallway.
He tried hard to restrain his anger when he heard the news.

Synset Examples

hold back your anger
keep your cool
she struggled to restrain her impatience at the delays

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suppress - forcibly put an end to or prevent from being expressed
  • Antonym: release - allow or enable to escape from confinement; set free

Etymology

From Old French restreindre, from Latin restringere (to bind back, check).

2. VERB

/ rɪˈstreɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈstreɪn / (BrE)
to prevent someone or something from moving freely, acting, or escaping, often by holding them back or confining them within certain limits
hold back or limit movement

Examples

The police had to restrain the suspect to prevent him from fleeing the scene.
Parents sometimes need to restrain their children from dangerous activities.
She could barely restrain herself from laughing during the serious meeting.

Synset Examples

This holds the local until the express passengers change trains
About a dozen animals were held inside the stockade
The illegal immigrants were held at a detention center
The terrorists held the journalists for ransom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confine - to keep or restrict someone or something within certain limits
  • Antonym: release - to set free from confinement or restraint

Etymology

From Old French restreindre, from Latin restringere (to bind fast, check).

3. VERB

/ rɪˈstreɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈstreɪn / (BrE)
to prevent someone or something from doing something, especially by physical force or by exercising control
prevent from doing something

Examples

The police officers had to restrain the suspect during the arrest.
He struggled to restrain his laughter during the serious meeting.
Please restrain your dog when visitors arrive.

Synset Examples

security guards restrained the reporter from throwing another shoe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prevent - to stop something from happening or someone from doing something
  • Synonym: to restrain - to keep within limits (another sense of restrain)
  • Antonym: to allow - to let someone do something; to permit

Etymology

From Old French restraindre, from Latin restringere (to bind fast, check).

4. VERB

/ rɪˈstreɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈstreɪn / (BrE)
to prevent someone or something from moving freely or acting as they wish, usually by physical means or other controls
restrict movement or actions

Examples

The police officer had to restrain the man to prevent him from running away.
Strong straps are used to restrain patients who might harm themselves.
She struggled to restrain her excited dog as it tried to chase the squirrels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restrict - to limit the size, amount, or range of something
  • Antonym: release - to allow someone or something to move freely

Etymology

From Old French restreindre, from Latin restringere (to bind fast, confine).
