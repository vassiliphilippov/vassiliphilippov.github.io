crackling

1. NOUN

/ ˈkræk.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkræk.lɪŋ / (BrE)
the crisp, browned residue left after rendering animal fat, often eaten as a snack or used in cooking
residue from rendered animal fat

Examples

She sprinkled crackling over her salad for extra crunch.
The chef saved the crackling to add flavor to the stew.
Crackling is a traditional treat made from pork fat in many cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scratching - crispy pork rind or skin, especially in British English

Etymology

Derived from 'crackle', referring to the crackling sound made during rendering.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkræk.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkræk.lɪŋ / (BrE)
a series of sharp, snapping noises, like those made by burning wood or static electricity
series of sharp snapping sounds

Examples

The crackling of the fire made the cabin feel warm and cozy.
She listened to the crackling of the old vinyl record as the music began to play.
A sudden crackling in the radio interrupted their conversation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pop - a short, sharp, explosive sound
  • Synonym: snap - a sudden, sharp cracking sound
