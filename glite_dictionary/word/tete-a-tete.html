tete-a-tete

1. NOUN

/ teɪtə teɪt / (AmE), / tet ə tet / (BrE)
a private or intimate conversation between two people, especially in a quiet or secluded setting
private conversation between two people

Examples

The two colleagues enjoyed a long tete-a-tete over coffee in the quiet lounge.
After dinner, they moved to the garden for a private tete-a-tete.
A brief tete-a-tete with her manager helped clarify several issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: one-on-one - a private meeting or conversation between two people
  • Antonym: group discussion - a conversation involving more than two people

Etymology

From French tête-à-tête, literally 'head to head'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌteɪtəˈtɛt / (AmE), / ˌteɪtəˈtɛt / (BrE)
a small sofa designed to seat two people facing or next to each other, often arranged for private conversation
a small sofa for two people

Examples

The couple sat on the tete-a-tete near the window, enjoying the view.
She bought a vintage tete-a-tete for the reading nook in her living room.
The elegant tete-a-tete made the formal parlor more inviting for intimate conversations.

Etymology

From French tête-à-tête (literally 'head to head'), originally referring to a private conversation.

3. ADV

/ teɪtə teɪt / (AmE), / teɪt ə teɪt / (BrE)
In private, with only two people and no one else present; privately and intimately between two individuals.
in private between two people

Examples

We spent the afternoon talking tete-a-tete in the quiet café.
The two leaders met tete-a-tete to discuss sensitive issues.
After the party, they enjoyed a long tete-a-tete on the balcony.

Synset Examples

we talked tete-a-tete

Related Terms

  • Synonym: privately - in a private manner; without others present
  • Antonym: in public - in a place or situation where others are present and can see

Etymology

From French 'tête-à-tête' meaning 'head to head'.
