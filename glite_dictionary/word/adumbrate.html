to adumbrate

1. VERB

/ ˈæd.əmˌbreɪt / (AmE), / ˈad.ʌm.breɪt / (BrE)
to describe or outline something briefly or in a general way; to give a summary of the main points.
describe or outline briefly

Examples

The historian sought to adumbrate the causes of the conflict in her lecture.
He attempted to adumbrate his proposal before the details were finalized.
The introduction will adumbrate the topics covered in the rest of the book.

Synset Examples

sketch the outline of the book
outline his ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to summarize - give a brief statement of the main points
  • Synonym: outline - give the main features or general idea of something

Etymology

From Latin adumbratus, past participle of adumbrare (to shade, represent in outline), from ad- (to) + umbra (shadow).

2. VERB

/ ˈædʌmˌbreɪt / (AmE), / ˈædʌmbreɪt / (BrE)
to suggest or disclose (something) partly or indirectly; to hint at or give a faint outline of
hint at; suggest partially

Examples

The author adumbrates the novel's tragic ending in the opening chapter.
His speech adumbrated the economic challenges that would soon emerge.
By mentioning recent events, she adumbrated a coming announcement without revealing the details.

Synset Examples

I insinuated that I did not like his wife

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to insinuate - to suggest or hint (something) in an indirect way
  • Synonym: to imply - to indicate or suggest without being explicitly stated

Etymology

From Latin adumbratus, past participle of adumbrare (to shade, represent in outline).
