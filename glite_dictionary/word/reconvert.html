to reconvert

1. VERB

/ ˌriːkənˈvɝːt / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈvɜːt / (BrE)
to change something back to a previous form, state, or use
convert back to a previous form

Examples

The museum plans to reconvert the building into its original gallery space.
After years of industrial use, the land was reconverted to farmland.
Hollywood studios are reconverting classic movies into digital formats.

Synset Examples

Hollywood is reconverting old films

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convert - to change something into a different form or use

Etymology

From re- (again) + convert, ultimately from Latin convertere (to turn around).
