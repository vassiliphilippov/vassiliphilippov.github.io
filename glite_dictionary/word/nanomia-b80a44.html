nanomia

1. NOUN

/ nəˈnoʊmiə / (AmE), / nəˈnəʊmiə / (BrE)
A genus of small siphonophores with clusters of swimming bells and long tentacles, resembling pieces of fuzzy rope, found in marine environments.
a genus of marine siphonophores

Examples

The biologist collected a specimen of nanomia during the research expedition.
Underwater lights revealed a long, delicate colony of nanomia drifting gracefully.
Scientists study nanomia to understand how its tentacles capture small prey in the ocean.

Etymology

From Greek 'nano-' (dwarf) + '-mia', used in scientific taxonomy.
