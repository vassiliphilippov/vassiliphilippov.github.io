CRO

1. NOUN

/ siː ˌɑːr ˈoʊ / (AmE), / siː ɑː ˈrəʊ / (BrE)
an instrument that displays electrical signals as waveforms on a screen, used to observe varying electrical quantities visually
cathode ray oscilloscope (electronic instrument)

Examples

The technician used a CRO to analyze the voltage fluctuations in the circuit.
Before digital devices became common, engineers relied on the CRO for waveform monitoring.
Learning how to operate a CRO is essential in many electronics laboratory courses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oscilloscope - an instrument for displaying electrical signals on a screen

Etymology

Acronym of 'cathode ray oscilloscope'.
