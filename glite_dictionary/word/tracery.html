tracery

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪsəri / (AmE), / ˈtreɪsəri / (BrE)
ornamental stonework, typically in the upper part of a Gothic window, featuring an open pattern of interlacing ribs or lines
ornamental stonework in Gothic windows

Examples

The cathedral's stained-glass windows were set in elaborate tracery.
Medieval architects often used intricate tracery to embellish church facades.
The delicate stone tracery cast beautiful shadows on the floor when the sun shone through.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filigree - delicate ornamental work of fine wire

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *tracerie*, from Old French *tracier* (to trace).
