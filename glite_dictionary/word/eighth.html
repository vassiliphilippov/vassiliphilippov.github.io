eighth

1. NOUN

/ eɪtθ / (AmE), / eɪtθ / (BrE)
Each of the eight equal parts into which something is divided; 1/8.
one part in eight equal parts

Examples

She cut the cake into eight eighth so each guest could have a piece.
An eighth of the pie was left on the plate after everyone had eaten.
Just one eighth of the project remains to be completed.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: quarter - one part in four equal parts
  • Degree Lower: sixteenth - one part in sixteen equal parts

Etymology

From Middle English 'eighth', from Old English 'eahtoða', from 'eahta' (eight).

2. ADJ

/ eɪtθ / (AmE), / eɪtθ / (BrE)
coming after the seventh in a series; 8th in position or order
8th in a sequence or order

Examples

She finished in eighth place in the final race.
Their apartment is on the eighth floor of the building.
This is the eighth time I've called you today.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: seventh - 7th in a sequence or order
  • Degree Higher: ninth - 9th in a sequence or order

Etymology

Formed from 'eight' with the ordinal suffix '-th'.

3. NOUN

/ eɪtθ / (AmE), / eɪtθ / (BrE)
The person or thing that is number eight in a sequence; ordinal form of eight.
person or thing in position eight

Examples

Our team finished in eighth place in the tournament this year.
She was the eighth to arrive at the party, just as the dinner started.
The runner in eighth position tried to catch up to the leaders.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: seventh - person or thing in position seven
  • Degree Higher: ninth - person or thing in position nine

Etymology

From Middle English 'eighthe', from Old English 'eahta' (eight) + '-th'.
