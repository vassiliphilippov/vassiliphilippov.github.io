tractable

1. ADJ

/ ˈtræk.tə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈtræk.tə.bəl / (BrE)
easily managed or influenced; willing to accept suggestions or directions
easy to manage or influence

Examples

The new employee proved to be quite tractable and adapted quickly to the team's routines.
Children can be more tractable when given clear instructions and positive reinforcement.
Unlike his brother, Tom was unusually tractable and seldom challenged his parents' decisions.

Synset Examples

a tractable student
an amenable child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amenable - open and responsive to suggestion; easily persuaded or controlled
  • Antonym: intractable - hard to control or deal with

Etymology

From Latin tractābilis (that can be drawn or handled), from tractāre (to handle, manage).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtræktəbəl / (AmE), / ˈtræktəbl / (BrE)
(of people or things) easy to control or influence; easy to deal with or manage.
easy to control or influence

Examples

The new puppy proved to be surprisingly tractable during training sessions.
Some chemicals are more tractable than others, making them easier to process industrially.
The problem is complex but still tractable with the right approach.

Synset Examples

tractable young minds
the natives … being … of an intelligent tractable disposition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: docile - easily taught or controlled
  • Synonym: manageable - easy to manage or control
  • Antonym: intractable - difficult or impossible to manage or control

Etymology

From Latin tractabilis (that can be handled), from tractare (to handle, manage).

WordNet ID: oewn-02460186-a
