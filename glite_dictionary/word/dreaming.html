dreaming

1. NOUN

/ ˈdriːmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdriːmɪŋ / (BrE)
the experience of seeing mental images, emotions, or stories while sleeping
mental activity during sleep

Examples

She often wakes up feeling inspired after a night of dreaming.
Scientific studies have explored the psychological significance of dreaming.
Children are sometimes frightened by dreaming about monsters.

Synset Examples

I had a dream about you last night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dream - a series of thoughts, images, or emotions occurring during sleep; noun form
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05776550-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈdriːmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdriːmɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of having imaginative thoughts or visions while awake; indulging in fantasies or reveries
indulging in waking fantasies or reveries

Examples

Her constant dreaming made it hard for her to concentrate on her schoolwork.
He was lost in dreaming about distant places and adventures during the meeting.
Sometimes, a little dreaming can lead to creative solutions to real problems.

Synset Examples

he lives in a dream that has nothing to do with reality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daydreaming - having pleasant, imaginative thoughts while awake
  • Synonym: reverie - a state of being pleasantly lost in one's thoughts
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05776803-n
Synset POS: n
