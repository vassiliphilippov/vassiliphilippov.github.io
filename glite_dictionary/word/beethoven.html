Beethoven

1. PROPN

/ ˈbeɪtoʊvə(n) / (AmE), / ˈbeɪthəʊvən / (BrE)
Ludwig van Beethoven (1770–1827), a German composer and pianist, widely regarded as one of the greatest composers in Western music history.
famous German composer (1770–1827)

Examples

The symphonies of Beethoven are performed by orchestras around the world.
Many consider Beethoven to be the bridge between the Classical and Romantic eras in music.
Even after losing his hearing, Beethoven continued to compose masterpieces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mozart - Austrian composer of classical music, earlier than Beethoven
  • Synonym: Bach - German composer of Baroque music, earlier than Beethoven

Etymology

From the composer’s full name, Ludwig van Beethoven, of Flemish and German origin.
