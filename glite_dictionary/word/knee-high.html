knee-high

1. NOUN

/ ˈniˌhaɪ / (AmE), / ˈniːˌhaɪ / (BrE)
a sock or stocking that reaches up to just below the knees
sock or stocking reaching the knee

Examples

She prefers to wear knee-highs in the winter for extra warmth.
The school uniform required white knee-highs with black shoes.
He bought a pack of colorful knee-highs for his daughter.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03628140-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADV

/ ˈniˌhaɪ / (AmE), / ˈniːˌhaɪ / (BrE)
reaching as high as the knees; extending up to the knee level.
reaching up to the knees

Examples

The snow was knee-high by the time we finished shoveling the driveway.
She wore knee-high boots with her dress to the party.
The wheat in the field is already knee-high this early in the season.

Synset Examples

we were standing knee-deep in the water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knee-deep - reaching as high as the knees, especially used to describe depth of water, mud, etc.

3. ADJ

/ ˈniː haɪ / (AmE), / ˈniː hʌɪ / (BrE)
reaching as high as the knees; extending up to the knee in height.
reaching up to the knees in height

Examples

The wild grass in the meadow grew knee-high by summer.
She splashed through the knee-high water on the flooded street.
Children love to run in knee-high snow during the winter.

Synset Examples

The grass is knee-high after the recent rains.
The flowers are knee-high and blooming beautifully.
