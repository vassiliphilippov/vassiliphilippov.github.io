scouring

1. NOUN

/ ˈskaʊɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskaʊərɪŋ / (BrE)
the action of searching through an area thoroughly in order to find something
thorough search of an area

Examples

The police organized a scouring of the nearby woods for clues.
Despite a thorough scouring of the house, her ring was never found.
A scouring of the archives turned up several forgotten documents.

Synset Examples

scouring the entire area revealed nothing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: searching - trying to find something by looking carefully
  • Synonym: sweeping - moving across an area in a thorough way, often searching

Etymology

From the verb 'scour', ultimately from Middle English 'scouren' (to clean, to run swiftly).

2. NOUN

/ ˈskaʊ.ɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskaʊ.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of cleaning a surface by rubbing it vigorously with a brush and soap and water
vigorous cleaning by rubbing with soap and water

Examples

She spent the afternoon scouring the kitchen floor to remove the stubborn stains.
The old pots required a lot of scouring before they shone again.
After hours of scouring, the tiles finally looked clean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrubbing - the act of cleaning something by rubbing it hard with a brush
  • Degree Lower: washing - the act of cleaning something with water or another liquid

Etymology

From the verb 'scour', from Middle English 'scouren', from Middle Dutch 'scuren' (to polish, clean).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00252647-n
Synset POS: n
