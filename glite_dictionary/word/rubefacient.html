rubefacient

1. NOUN

/ ˌruːbəˈfeɪʃənt / (AmE), / ˌruːbɪˈfeɪʃənt / (BrE)
a substance or medicine applied to the skin to cause redness by dilating the capillaries, often to relieve pain or inflammation
substance causing redness of the skin
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a rubefacient to reduce the pain in his sore shoulder.
Applying a rubefacient can increase blood flow to the affected area and ease discomfort.
Certain herbal oils have long been used as natural rubefacients for treating joint pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterirritant - a substance producing irritation in one area to relieve symptoms elsewhere

Etymology

From Latin rubefacient-, stem of rubefaciens, present participle of rubefacere (to make red).
