guaranine

1. NOUN

/ ɡwɑːrəˌniːn / (AmE), / ɡwɑːrəˌniːn / (BrE)
Caffeine derived from the seeds of the guarana plant.
caffeine from guarana plant

Examples

Many energy drinks list guaranine as an ingredient, referring to caffeine from guarana.
Researchers found that guaranine has the same physiological effects as regular caffeine.
The label indicated that the beverage contained natural guaranine extracted from guarana seeds.

Synset Examples

Guaranine and caffeine are chemically identical.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caffeine - a stimulant compound found in coffee, tea, and other plants

Etymology

From 'guarana' (the plant) + suffix '-ine' denoting chemical substances.
