eluding

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈluːdɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪˈluːdɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of avoiding capture, especially by means of cleverness or cunning
the act of avoiding capture or escape

Examples

The thief's eluding of the police lasted for several weeks before he was finally caught.
Her eluding of the press was so skillful that no photographs were taken for months.
The getaway driver's eluding of authorities required precise planning and quick thinking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escape - the act of successfully getting away, especially from confinement
  • Antonym: capture - the act of catching or being caught

Etymology

From the verb 'elude', from Latin 'eludere' (to escape, evade by playing).
