viaticus

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈætɪkəs / (AmE), / vaɪˈatɪkəs / (BrE)
The practice of purchasing life insurance policies from terminally ill policyholders, typically at a discounted price, for the purpose of collecting the benefits upon their death.
purchase of life insurance from terminally ill policyholders
legal

Examples

Several companies specialize in the viaticus market to help patients with urgent financial needs.
The rise of viaticus transactions has led to new regulations in the insurance industry.
Financial advisors discussed the ethical implications of engaging in viaticus purchases.

Etymology

From Latin 'viaticus' (of a journey), relating to provisions for a journey, later used in finance and insurance.
