vow

1. VERB

/ vaʊ / (AmE), / vaʊ / (BrE)
to make a serious or solemn promise to do something or refrain from doing something
make a solemn promise

Examples

She vowed to help her community after the hurricane.
He vowed to keep the secret no matter what happened.
The politician vowed that he would fight corruption in the government.

Synset Examples

He vowed never to drink alcohol again

Related Terms

  • Synonym: promise - to declare that one will do something or refrain from doing something
  • Synonym: to swear - to make a solemn statement or promise, often invoking something sacred

Etymology

From Old French *vou* (a vow), from Latin *votum* (a vow, promise, wish).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00888280-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ vaʊ / (AmE), / vaʊ / (BrE)
a solemn promise or pledge to do something or behave in a certain way, often with religious or formal significance
a solemn promise or pledge

Examples

She made a vow to never lie again after the incident.
Monks often take a vow of silence as part of their discipline.
He broke his vow and revealed the secret to everyone.

Synset Examples

they took vows of poverty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oath - a solemn promise, often invoking a divine witness
  • Synonym: pledge - a serious or formal promise or agreement
  • Antonym: curse - a solemn utterance intended to invoke harm or misfortune on someone

Etymology

From Old French 'veu', from Latin 'votum' (a vow, solemn promise), from 'vovere' (to vow).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07243411-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ vaʊ / (AmE), / vaʊ / (BrE)
to dedicate someone or something to a deity or sacred purpose by making a solemn promise or declaration
dedicate by a solemn promise to a deity

Examples

The ancient tribe would vow their first harvest to the goddess each year.
He decided to vow his life to the service of his god.
In a moving ceremony, the family vowed their child to the temple's care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dedicate - to devote for a special purpose, often religious

Etymology

From Old French *vou*, from Latin *votum* (a vow, wish, promise to a god), from *vovere* (to vow).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00888499-v
Synset POS: v
