liquor

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪkər / (AmE), / ˈlɪkə / (BrE)
the liquid in which vegetables or meat have been cooked, often used as a broth or base for soups and sauces
cooking liquid from meat or vegetables

Examples

After boiling the beans, she used the flavorful liquor to make soup.
The chef recommended saving the vegetable liquor for sauces.
Ham is often served with some of its cooking liquor poured over the top.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broth - liquid in which meat, fish, or vegetables have been simmered
  • Synonym: stock - flavored liquid base for soups and sauces, made by simmering bones, meat, or vegetables

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪkər / (AmE), / ˈlɪkə / (BrE)
A strong alcoholic drink that is distilled rather than fermented, such as whiskey, vodka, or rum.
distilled alcoholic drink

Examples

The bartender poured a shot of liquor into the glass.
Some countries place high taxes on imported liquor.
She doesn't drink beer or wine, but she sometimes enjoys liquor at parties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spirit - strong distilled alcoholic drink
  • Antonym: beer - alcoholic beverage made from fermented grain, not distilled
  • Antonym: wine - alcoholic beverage made from fermented fruit, not distilled

Etymology

From Old French 'licor' (liquid), from Latin 'liquor' (fluid, liquid).

/ ˈlɪkər / (AmE), / ˈlɪkə / (BrE)
a liquid substance used or obtained in an industrial process, typically as a solution, emulsion, or suspension
liquid used in an industrial process

Examples

During the extraction process, the pulp is separated from the black liquor.
The spent liquor must be properly neutralized to prevent environmental contamination.
