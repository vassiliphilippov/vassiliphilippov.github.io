hurriedness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɜrid.nəs / (AmE), / ˈhʌr.id.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being hurried, especially characterized by excessive haste and possible carelessness
state of being excessively hasty

Examples

His hurriedness caused him to make several mistakes during the interview.
You could sense the hurriedness in her voice as she tried to explain.
The hurriedness of his actions led to an accident in the laboratory.

Synset Examples

he soon regretted his haste

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haste - excessive speed or urgency of movement or action
  • Antonym: deliberation - careful consideration before action

Etymology

From 'hurried' (past participle of 'hurry') + '-ness' (noun-forming suffix).
