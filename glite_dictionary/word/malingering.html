malingering

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɪŋɡərɪŋ / (AmE), / məˈlɪŋɡərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of pretending to be ill or incapacitated in order to avoid duty or work
pretending illness to avoid work

Examples

The doctor was suspicious of the patient's malingering during the medical examination.
Cases of malingering can be difficult to prove without proper testing.
The company implemented strict policies to address frequent malingering among employees.

Synset Examples

they developed a test to detect malingering

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feigning - pretending to be affected by something
  • Synonym: simulation - imitation of symptoms or conditions, not genuine
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and not pretending

Etymology

From French *malingre* (sickly), influenced by *mal* (bad) + *ingre* (sickly), with the English verb form '-ing'.
