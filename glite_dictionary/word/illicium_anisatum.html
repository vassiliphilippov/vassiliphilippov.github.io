Illicium anisatum

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɪsiəm ˌænɪˈseɪtəm / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪsiəm ˌænɪˈsɑːtəm / (BrE)
A small evergreen shrub or tree native to Japan and Taiwan, known for its non-fragrant flowers and star-shaped fruit, used as a spice but toxic if ingested.
Japanese star anise tree (toxic plant)

Examples

The seeds of Illicium anisatum contain toxic compounds and should never be consumed.
Unlike true star anise, Illicium anisatum is highly poisonous if ingested.
Botanists have documented the distribution of Illicium anisatum in forests across Japan and Taiwan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: star anise - species Illicium verum, used as a spice, not toxic

Etymology

From Latin *illicium* (allurement, charm) and Neo-Latin *anisatum* (anise-like), referring to the plant's resemblance to anise.
