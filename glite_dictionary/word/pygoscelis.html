Pygoscelis

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈɡɒsəˌlaɪs / (AmE), / pɪˈɡɒsɪˌliːs / (BrE)
A genus of penguins in the family Spheniscidae, which includes species such as the gentoo, Adélie, and chinstrap penguins.
genus of penguins

Examples

Scientists discovered a new fossil belonging to the Pygoscelis genus in Antarctica.
The Adélie penguin is a well-known species within the genus Pygoscelis.
Members of Pygoscelis are easily recognized by the white stripe running across their heads.

Etymology

From Greek 'pygē' (rump) + 'skelos' (leg), referring to the penguins' distinctive posture.

2. NOUN

/ paɪˈɡɑːsəlɪs / (AmE), / paɪˈɡɒsəlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of penguins in the family Spheniscidae, including the Adélie, chinstrap, and gentoo penguins.
a genus of penguins

Examples

The Adélie penguin belongs to the genus Pygoscelis.
Several species within the genus Pygoscelis are adapted to life in icy Antarctic waters.
Scientists are studying the evolutionary history of Pygoscelis penguins.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Spheniscidae - the family of penguins

Etymology

From Greek 'pygē' (rump) and 'skelos' (leg), referring to the penguin's short legs.
