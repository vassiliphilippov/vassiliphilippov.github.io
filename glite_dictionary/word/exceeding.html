exceeding

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈsiːdɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪkˈsiːdɪŋ / (BrE)
very great in amount, degree, or intensity; exceptional
very great; exceptional

Examples

The project was completed in an exceeding short amount of time.
She displayed exceeding talent from a very young age.
The mountain was covered in exceeding beauty at sunrise.

Synset Examples

a night of exceeding darkness
an exceptional memory
olympian efforts to save the city from bankruptcy
the young Mozart's prodigious talents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exceptional - unusually good; outstanding
  • Antonym: ordinary - with no special or distinctive features; normal

Etymology

From Middle English exceden, from Old French exceder, from Latin excedere (to go beyond).
