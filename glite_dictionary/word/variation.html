variation

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɛriˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌveərɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
(in ballet) a solo dance or dance figure performed by a single dancer within a larger work.
solo dance in ballet

Examples

The principal dancer captivated the audience with her flawless variation in Act II.
Each performer rehearsed their variation intensively for the upcoming gala.
His favorite variation is from the ballet 'La Bayadère.'

Etymology

From Latin variatio (change, diversity), via French variation.
