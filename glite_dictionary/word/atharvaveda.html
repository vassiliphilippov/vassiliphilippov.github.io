Atharvaveda

1. PROPN

/ əˌθɑːrvəˈveɪdə / (AmE), / əˌθɑːvɜːˈveɪdə / (BrE)
one of the four Vedas, consisting of a collection of mantras, spells, and incantations, forming a key text of Hinduism
Hindu scripture; collection of mantras

Examples

Scholars have studied the Atharvaveda to understand ancient Indian rituals and beliefs.
The Atharvaveda contains hymns and incantations for daily life and healing.
Unlike the other Vedas, the Atharvaveda includes practical advice for health and prosperity.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Veda - any of the ancient sacred scriptures of Hinduism

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Atharvaveda', named after the sage Atharvan and 'Veda' meaning 'knowledge'.
