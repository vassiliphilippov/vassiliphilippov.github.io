Vitis

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˈvɪtɪs / (BrE)
A genus of woody vines in the family Vitaceae, which includes grapevines producing edible grapes.
genus of grapevines

Examples

Vitis species are widely cultivated for their fruit, especially grapes used in winemaking.
Many wild Vitis varieties are valued for their resistance to disease.
Some Vitis plants can be found growing along riverbanks and forest edges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grapevine - a woody vine that produces grapes

Etymology

From Latin *vitis* (vine).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˈviːtɪs / (BrE)
A genus of woody vines with simple leaves and small flowers, including many species of grape.
genus of grapevines

Examples

Species from the genus Vitis are grown worldwide for their fruit.
Botanists study the characteristics of Vitis to improve grape cultivation.
The family Vitaceae includes Vitis and several other vine genera.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Vitaceae - the plant family that includes Vitis and related genera
  • Synonym: grape - the fruit that grows on Vitis species

Etymology

From Latin *vitis* (vine, grapevine).
