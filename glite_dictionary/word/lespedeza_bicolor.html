Lespedeza bicolor

1. NOUN

/ lɛˈspɛdəzə baɪˈkɔlər / (AmE), / lɛˈspɛdɪzə baɪˈkɒlə / (BrE)
A species of Asian shrub with showy racemose rose-purple flowers, often grown as an ornamental plant, used for erosion control, and as feed for wild birds.
an Asian flowering shrub; ornamental and erosion control plant

Examples

The Lespedeza bicolor is popular among gardeners for its striking purple flowers.
Efforts to control soil erosion included planting Lespedeza bicolor along the riverbanks.
Wild birds flock to fields where Lespedeza bicolor has been established as a food source.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Lespedeza' (after Spanish governor Vicente Manuel de Céspedes) and Latin 'bicolor' meaning 'two-colored'.
