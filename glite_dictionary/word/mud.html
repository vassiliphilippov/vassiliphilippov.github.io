mud

1. NOUN

/ mʌd / (AmE), / mʌd / (BrE)
Slanderous or damaging remarks intended to harm someone's reputation.
slanderous or damaging remarks

Examples

The tabloids were full of mud aimed at the politician before the election.
It's easy to throw mud, but hard to prove any of the allegations.
Tired of all the mud being slung in the media, she decided to ignore the rumors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slander - false spoken statements damaging to a person's reputation
  • Synonym: defamation - the act of damaging someone's good reputation

2. VERB

/ mʌd / (AmE), / mʌd / (BrE)
to cover or plaster a surface with mud, typically for construction or decoration purposes
to plaster with mud

Examples

The workers mudded the walls to prepare them for painting.
She learned how to mud a traditional hut as part of the cultural workshop.
After they finished framing, it was time to mud the drywall seams.

3. NOUN

/ mʌd / (AmE), / mʌd / (BrE)
soft, wet earth or soil, typically formed after rain or near water
soft wet earth or soil

Examples

After the heavy rain, the children splashed in the mud in the backyard.
Her boots were covered in mud after the long hike through the forest.
The car got stuck in the mud on the unpaved country road.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dirt - matter in or on the ground; earth, soil, or filth
  • Degree Higher: clay - stiff, sticky fine-grained earth
  • Degree Lower: dust - fine, dry particles of matter

Etymology

Possibly from Middle English *mudde*, related to Middle Low German *mudde*.
