irregularity

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌrɛɡjəˈlærəti / (AmE), / ɪˌrɛɡjʊˈlarəti / (BrE)
the state or quality of occurring without a fixed pattern, principle, or rate; the condition of being irregular.
lack of regular pattern or order

Examples

The train schedules are plagued by irregularity, making it hard for commuters to plan ahead.
The irregularity of her heartbeat concerned the doctor.
There was noticeable irregularity in the way the payments were received.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: regularity - the state of being regular or following a fixed pattern

Etymology

From Latin irregularitas, from irregularis (not regular).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌrɛɡjəˈlærəti / (AmE), / ɪˌrɛɡjʊˈlarəti / (BrE)
a behavior or action that violates a rule, etiquette, custom, or moral principle
violation of rules or norms

Examples

The investigation uncovered several irregularities in the company's financial records.
Any irregularity in the admission process must be reported immediately.
He was suspended after allegations of irregularity in his conduct during the exam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misconduct - improper or unlawful behavior
  • Synonym: transgression - an act that goes against a law or rule
  • Antonym: conformity - compliance with rules or norms

Etymology

From Late Latin irregularitas, from Latin irregularis ('not regular').

3. NOUN

/ ɪˌrɛɡjəˈlærəti / (AmE), / ɪˌrɛɡjʊˈlarɪti / (BrE)
An infrequent or difficult evacuation of the bowels; a medical symptom suggestive of intestinal obstruction or related conditions.
infrequent or difficult bowel evacuation (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor asked if she had experienced any irregularity in her bowel movements lately.
Chronic irregularity can sometimes point to a more serious digestive issue.
A high-fiber diet is recommended to reduce irregularity and promote regular bowel function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constipation - difficulty in emptying the bowels, often associated with hardened stool

Etymology

From Latin 'irregularitas', from 'irregularis' (not according to rule).
