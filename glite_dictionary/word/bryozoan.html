bryozoan

1. NOUN

/ braɪ.oʊˈzoʊ.ən / (AmE), / brʌɪ.əˈzəʊ.ən / (BrE)
a small aquatic invertebrate animal that lives in colonies, resembling moss and composed of many tiny polyps, each with tentacles used for feeding
colonial aquatic animal resembling moss

Examples

The scientist carefully examined the bryozoan colonies attached to the rock.
Each bryozoan in the group contributed to building their moss-like structure.
Divers discovered a rare bryozoan species growing on underwater seaweed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moss animal - another name for a bryozoan; colonial aquatic invertebrate

Etymology

From Greek bryon (moss) + zōion (animal).
