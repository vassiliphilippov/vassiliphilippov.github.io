constellation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːns.təˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.stəˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a group of stars forming a recognizable pattern, traditionally named after mythological figures, animals, or objects, as observed from Earth
group of stars forming a pattern

Examples

Orion is perhaps the most easily recognized constellation in the night sky.
She learned to identify each constellation while stargazing as a child.
The ancient Greeks named the constellation after a mythical hunter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asterism - a prominent pattern or group of stars, typically smaller than a constellation

Etymology

From Latin 'constellatio', meaning 'a set of stars'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːn.stəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.stəˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A group or arrangement of related things or people considered as a unit or system, especially when thought of as influencing an outcome.
arrangement of related elements or factors

Examples

A unique constellation of factors led to the company's rapid success.
In his speech, he addressed the constellation of problems facing urban communities.
The festival resulted from a fortunate constellation of creative minds working together.

Synset Examples

the outcome depends on the configuration of influences at the time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: configuration - the arrangement of parts or elements; shape or form
  • Synonym: combination - a joining or grouping of different elements

Etymology

From Latin 'constellatio', meaning 'set of stars'.
