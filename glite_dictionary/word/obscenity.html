obscenity

1. NOUN

/ əbˈsɛnəti / (AmE), / əbˈsiːnɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being obscene; offensive or indecent behavior, language, or images
the quality of being obscene

Examples

The comedian's act was criticized for its obscenity and lack of tact.
Some people believe that too much obscenity on television leads to declining morals.
The book was banned in several countries due to perceived obscenity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decency - the quality of being proper and respectable
  • Synonym: indecency - offensive or improper behavior or language

Etymology

From Latin obscēnitās (offensiveness, indecency), from obscēnus (offensive, foul).

2. NOUN

/ əbˈsɛnəti / (AmE), / əbˈsiːnəti / (BrE)
an obscene act, gesture, or expression, especially one considered offensive or indecent by societal standards
offensive or indecent act

Examples

The comedian's performance was interrupted because one obscenity after another shocked the audience.
Public displays of obscenity are strictly prohibited by local law.
He was fined for shouting an obscenity at the referee during the match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indecency - behavior or acts that are not considered proper by societal standards
  • Antonym: decency - behavior that conforms to accepted standards of morality

Etymology

From Latin obscēnitas (indecency), from obscēnus (offensive).

3. NOUN

/ əbˈsɛnəti / (AmE), / əbˈsiːnəti / (BrE)
an offensive or indecent word or expression, especially one regarded as taboo or inappropriate in polite conversation
offensive or indecent word or phrase

Examples

Many countries have laws to restrict obscenity in public media.
The comedian's routine was filled with obscenity, shocking several members of the audience.
He was fined for shouting an obscenity at the referee during the match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profanity - language that is disrespectful or irreverent, especially toward religious things
  • Synonym: expletive - an obscene or profane word, particularly used as an utterance
  • Antonym: euphemism - a mild or indirect word substituted for one that is considered too harsh or blunt

Etymology

From Latin obscaenitas (foulness, indecency), from obscaenus (foul, disgusting).
