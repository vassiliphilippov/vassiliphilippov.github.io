luridness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʊr.ɪd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈljʊə.rɪd.nɪs / (BrE)
an unnatural lack of color in the skin, often due to bruising, illness, or emotional distress
unnatural paleness of the skin
medical

Examples

The luridness of her cheeks alarmed the nurse, who feared she was seriously ill.
After the accident, luridness spread across his face where the bruise was forming.
His luridness was noticed by everyone, hinting at a deep emotional shock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pallor - an unhealthy pale appearance
  • Antonym: rosiness - a healthy pink or reddish color of the skin

Etymology

From Latin luridus (pale yellow, ghastly); related to 'lurid'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlʊrɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈljʊərɪdnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being sensational, shocking, or intended to provoke strong reactions, often by appealing to vulgar or morbid interests
sensational or shocking quality

Examples

The article's luridness was criticized as an attempt to manipulate public opinion.
Some tabloids are known for the luridness of their front-page stories.
She disliked the luridness in the way the crime was reported in the media.

Synset Examples

the tabloids relied on sensationalism to maintain their circulation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensationalism - the use of exciting or shocking stories to provoke interest

Etymology

From 'lurid' + '-ness'. 'Lurid' from Latin 'luridus' (pale yellow, ghastly).

3. NOUN

/ ˈlʊrɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈljʊərɪdnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being shockingly vivid, sensational, or ghastly; an intense or gruesome vividness
quality of being lurid or ghastly

Examples

The luridness of the crime scene left the detectives shaken for days.
Some critics were put off by the luridness in the novel's most violent chapters.
The painting's luridness made it controversial among more traditional art lovers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lurid - sensationally shocking or ghastly

Etymology

From 'lurid' + '-ness', where 'lurid' is from Latin 'luridus' (pale yellow, ghastly).
