Mongolic

1. NOUN

/ mɑŋˈɡɑːlɪk / (AmE), / mɒŋˈɡɒlɪk / (BrE)
a family of languages of the Altaic group, mainly spoken in Mongolia and surrounding regions
Altaic language family spoken in Mongolia

Examples

Linguists classify Khalkha and Buryat as part of the Mongolic language family.
The Mongolic languages are believed to have originated in the region of present-day Mongolia.
He is researching endangered Mongolic dialects in northern China.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mongolian - the main language of Mongolia, belonging to the Mongolic family

Etymology

From Mongolia + -ic, referring to Mongolia and its related languages.
