genial

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒiniəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːniəl / (BrE)
(of weather, climate, or atmosphere) pleasantly mild and conducive to comfort; kindly or warming.
pleasantly mild or comforting (weather, climate)

Examples

We enjoyed a picnic under the genial sunshine in early spring.
Thanks to its genial climate, the region attracts visitors all year round.
His genial manner put everyone at ease during the meeting.

Synset Examples

a dry climate kind to asthmatics
the genial sunshine
hot summer pavements are anything but kind to the feet

Related Terms

  • Antonym: harsh - unpleasantly rough or severe (of weather or conditions)

Etymology

From Latin 'genialis' (festive, pleasant), from 'genius' (spirit).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʒiːniəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːniəl / (BrE)
friendly and cheerful; warmly pleasant or good-natured
friendly and cheerful

Examples

The manager was known for his genial personality and welcoming attitude toward customers.
She greeted the guests with a genial smile that immediately put everyone at ease.
Despite his fame, the author remained a genial and approachable person.

Synset Examples

an affable smile
an amiable gathering
cordial relations
a cordial greeting
a genial host

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affable - friendly, good-natured, or easy to talk to
  • Synonym: cordial - warm and friendly
  • Antonym: unfriendly - not friendly or kind

Etymology

From Latin 'genialis' (festive, pleasant), related to 'genius' (spirit of a place or person).

/ ˈdʒiːniəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːniəl / (BrE)
relating to the chin or the central area of the lower jaw, especially in anatomy or dentistry.
of the chin or lower jaw (anatomy)
medical

Examples

The surgeon noted the irregularity in the genial region during the procedure.
Fractures involving the genial area can affect the attachment of tongue muscles.
Dental implants were placed near the genial tubercles of the jaw.

Etymology

From Latin 'genialis' (of the chin), from Greek 'genias, geniados' (chin, bearded).
