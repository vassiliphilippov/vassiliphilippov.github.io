orris

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɪs / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪs / (BrE)
A plant of the iris family, especially German iris, with large white or purple-tinged flowers and a fragrant rhizome used in perfumes.
a type of iris plant with fragrant root

Examples

The roots of the orris are used to make a fragrant powder.
She planted orris in her garden to enjoy both the flowers and the scent.
The perfumer added a hint of orris to enhance the floral notes of the fragrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iris - a genus of flowering plants with showy flowers

Etymology

From Latin 'iris', meaning 'iris' (the plant), via Old French 'orri(s)'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrɪs / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪs / (BrE)
the fragrant rhizome of certain iris species, especially the Florentine iris, used in perfumes, medicines, and for flavoring
fragrant iris root used in perfumery and medicine

Examples

The perfumer added a touch of orris to the floral fragrance to give it depth.
Dried orris is often used as a natural fixative in potpourri blends.
Historically, apothecaries prescribed orris root for a variety of ailments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iris - a plant genus with showy flowers, some roots of which produce orris

Etymology

From Old French 'iris', from Latin 'iris' (rainbow, iris plant).
