calling

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɔːlɪŋ / (BrE)
a profession or occupation, especially one for which a person feels particularly suited or trained
profession or occupation for which one is suited

Examples

After years of searching, she finally found her true calling as a teacher.
He felt that medicine was more than just a job; it was his calling.
Many people struggle to discover their calling in life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vocation - a strong feeling of suitability for a particular career or occupation
  • Synonym: profession - a paid occupation, especially one that involves prolonged training

Etymology

From Middle English 'calle', from Old English 'ceallian' (to call).
