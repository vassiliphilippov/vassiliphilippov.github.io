Cardium edule

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːrdiəm ˈɛdjuːli / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdiəm ˈɛdjuːli / (BrE)
a common European cockle with an edible, rounded shell, found in sandy coasts and mudflats
common edible European cockle

Examples

Fishermen along the coast collect Cardium edule for both local markets and export.
The delicate flavor of Cardium edule makes it a favorite in European seafood dishes.
Researchers are studying Cardium edule populations to assess the health of regional estuaries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockle - an edible, marine bivalve mollusc with a rounded shell

Etymology

From Latin: 'cardium' meaning heart (referring to the shell's shape) and 'edule' meaning edible.
