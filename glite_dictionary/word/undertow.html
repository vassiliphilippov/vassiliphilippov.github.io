undertow

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndɚˌtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈʌndətəʊ / (BrE)
a current of water below the surface, moving seaward from the shore after waves have broken
seaward undercurrent after waves break

Examples

Swimmers should beware of the undertow at this beach, as it can pull even strong swimmers out to sea.
After the waves crashed onto the shore, an undertow swept the sand from beneath my feet.
Lifeguards posted warning signs about the dangerous undertow near the rocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rip current - a strong, narrow current flowing outward from the shore

Etymology

From 'under' + 'tow', referring to a current that pulls under the surface.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌn.dɚˌtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈʌn.də.təʊ / (BrE)
a hidden or underlying feeling or influence that is contrary to the most apparent or dominant emotion or tendency
hidden or conflicting feeling/influence

Examples

Despite her cheerful manner, there was an undertow of anxiety beneath her words.
The novel's hopeful ending carries an undertow of sadness that lingers with the reader.
His speech was inspiring, but an undertow of bitterness could be sensed by close listeners.

Synset Examples

his account had a poignant undertow of regret

Etymology

Metaphorical use of 'undertow', originally from the current beneath surface waves, extended to feelings in the mid-19th century.
