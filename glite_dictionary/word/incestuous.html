incestuous

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsɛstʃuəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛstjuːəs / (BrE)
involving or relating to incest; characterized by sexual relations between close relatives
relating to incest

Examples

The novel explores an incestuous relationship that shocks the entire community.
There are strict laws prohibiting incestuous marriages in many countries.
The royal family's incestuous unions were meant to preserve their bloodline.

Etymology

From Latin *incestuōsus* (impure, incestuous), from *incestus* (incest).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsɛstʃuəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛstjuːəs / (BrE)
(of relationships or activities) excessively close or exclusive, resembling or suggestive of incest, especially by involving only a small group with limited outside contact.
excessively close or exclusive relationships

Examples

The committee's incestuous relationships prevented new ideas from coming in.
Some countries have an incestuous media environment where only a few voices are ever heard.
His incestuous association with the same business partners raised eyebrows among competitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cliquish - tending to form exclusive groups
  • Synonym: exclusive - not admitting others to join; closed group

Etymology

From Latin incestuosus (unchaste), from incestum (incest).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02759094-a
Synset POS: a
