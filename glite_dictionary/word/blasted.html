blasted

1. ADJ

/ ˈblæs.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈblɑː.stɪd / (BrE)
(informal, intensifier) Used to express annoyance or emphasis, as a mild expletive, especially before a noun.
used as a mild expletive for emphasis
informal

Examples

I can't open this blasted jar no matter how hard I try.
The blasted alarm went off at 5 a.m. and woke everyone up.
I've told you a hundred times to fix that blasted door.

Synset Examples

he's a blasted idiot
it's a blamed shame
a blame cold winter
not a blessed dime
I'll be damned (or blessed or darned or goddamned) if I'll do any such thing
he's a damn (or goddam or goddamned) fool
a deuced idiot
an infernal nuisance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damned - mild expletive used for emphasis or annoyance
  • Synonym: blessed - euphemistic expletive used for mild emphasis
  • Synonym: infernal - used as an intensifier for annoyance

Etymology

From 'blast' (explosion or strong gust), used attributively; its use as an intensifier developed in the 17th–18th centuries as a euphemism for 'damned'.
