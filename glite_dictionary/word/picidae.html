Picidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈpɪsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
The family of birds that includes woodpeckers and their close relatives.
woodpecker family of birds

Examples

There are more than two hundred species in the Picidae family worldwide.
Researchers study the Picidae to learn about adaptations for pecking wood.
The Picidae exhibit unique behaviors among birds, such as drumming on trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodpecker - a bird known for pecking at wood to find insects

Etymology

From Latin 'Picus' (woodpecker), with '-idae' denoting family in zoological taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈpɪsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of birds comprising the woodpeckers, characterized by their strong bills, stiff tails, and ability to peck into wood.
family of birds: woodpeckers

Examples

The Picidae are distinguished by their specialized skulls that allow them to hammer into trees.
Researchers studied several members of the Picidae family in the Amazon rainforest.
All species within the Picidae share a unique foot structure called zygodactyly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodpecker - a bird that pecks at wood to find insects or create nest holes

Etymology

From modern Latin Picidae, from Latin picus (woodpecker).
