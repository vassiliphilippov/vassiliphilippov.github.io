to reave

1. VERB

/ riːv / (AmE), / riːv / (BrE)
(archaic) to steal or plunder, especially by force; to take as spoils
steal or plunder by force (archaic)
archaic

Examples

The invaders sought to reave every village of its treasures.
Stories from old times speak of bandits who would reave both cattle and crops.
As the city burned, desperate men tried to reave what little they could find.

Synset Examples

During the earthquake people looted the stores that were deserted by their owners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steal - to take something without permission and without intending to return it
  • Synonym: plunder - to steal goods, especially during war or disorder

Etymology

Old English *reafian* (to rob, plunder), related to Dutch *roven*, German *rauben*.
