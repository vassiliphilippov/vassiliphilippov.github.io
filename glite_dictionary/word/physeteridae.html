Physeteridae

1. NOUN

/ faɪˌsɛtəˈrɪdiː / (AmE), / fɪˌsiːtəˈriːdiː / (BrE)
A family of large toothed whales that includes the sperm whale and closely related extinct forms.
family of sperm whales

Examples

The Physeteridae family is best known for its largest member, the sperm whale.
Scientists study Physeteridae fossils to understand the evolution of toothed whales.
All living members of Physeteridae are now represented by a single extant species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sperm whale - a large, toothed whale species; Physeter macrocephalus

Etymology

From New Latin Physeter (genus name for the sperm whale) + -idae (a standard zoological family suffix).
