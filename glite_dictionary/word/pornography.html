pornography

1. NOUN

/ pɔːrˈnɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / pɔːˈnɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
magazines, books, pictures, films, or other materials depicting sexual acts or nudity, created primarily to stimulate sexual desire rather than for artistic or literary value
sexually explicit material for arousal

Examples

Many countries have specific laws regarding the distribution and viewing of pornography.
She argued that some forms of art had been unfairly labeled as pornography.
The internet has made access to pornography much easier than in the past.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erotica - art or literature intended to evoke sexual desire, often with aesthetic or literary value
  • Synonym: obscenity - offensive or indecent material, especially that violating moral standards

Etymology

From Greek 'pornographos' (writing about prostitutes), from 'pornē' (prostitute) + 'graphos' (writing).
