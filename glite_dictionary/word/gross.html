gross

1. NOUN

/ ɡroʊs / (AmE), / ɡrəʊs / (BrE)
a group or unit of twelve dozen (144) items, especially used as a standard quantity in commerce
twelve dozen (144) items

Examples

The store ordered a gross of pencils for the new school year.
He bought two gross of eggs to prepare for the festival.
The factory shipped out a gross of bottles each day.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dozen - a group of twelve items
  • Degree Higher: great gross - a group of 1728 items (twelve gross)

Etymology

From Old French *gros* (large, thick), referring to a large dozen; the sense of 'twelve dozen' arose in medieval trade.

Always check if a salary figure refers to the gross or net amount.
