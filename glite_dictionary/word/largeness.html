largeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑrdʒ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈlɑːdʒ.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being large in size or extent
state of being large

Examples

The largeness of the hall made it perfect for hosting big events.
She was amazed by the largeness of the ancient oak tree in the park.
His generosity matched the largeness of his estates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bigness - the quality of being big or large
  • Antonym: smallness - the quality of being small in size or extent

Etymology

From large + -ness, forming a noun indicating the state or quality of being large.
