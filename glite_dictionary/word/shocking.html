shocking

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃɑkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃɒkɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing a strong feeling of surprise and disapproval; extremely offensive to moral sensibilities or damaging to reputation.
offensive or scandalous; morally shocking

Examples

The journalist described the shocking details of the scandal in his report.
Her shocking actions caused an uproar in the small community.
Many considered the novel too shocking to be published in its era.

Synset Examples

scandalous behavior
the wicked rascally shameful conduct of the bankrupt
the most shocking book of its time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scandalous - causing general public outrage by a perceived offense
  • Synonym: shameful - worthy of or causing shame or disgrace
  • Antonym: innocuous - not harmful or offensive

Etymology

From the verb 'shock' + -ing, dating to the 18th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃɑkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃɒkɪŋ / (BrE)
Extremely vivid, graphic, or sensational; intended to startle, disturb, or shock the audience.
extremely vivid or sensational

Examples

The report contained shocking images that horrified the public.
The novel's shocking depiction of violence stirred controversy among critics.
She was captivated by the shocking headlines in the tabloid newspapers.

Synset Examples

lurid details of the accident

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lurid - sensational, shocking, or explicit, particularly in a graphic way
  • Antonym: mild - not strong or serious in effect or character

Etymology

From the verb 'shock', ultimately from French 'choquer' (to collide).

Synset POS: s
