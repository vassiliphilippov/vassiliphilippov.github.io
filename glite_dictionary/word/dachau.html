Dachau

1. PROPN

/ ˈdɑːkaʊ / (AmE), / ˈdaːkaʊ / (BrE)
A Nazi concentration camp for Jews and other prisoners, established in 1933 near Munich in southern Germany.
Nazi concentration camp near Munich

Examples

During World War II, thousands of people were imprisoned and killed at Dachau.
A visit to Dachau serves as a powerful reminder of the atrocities committed during the Holocaust.
Many survivors of Dachau shared their stories to ensure that history would not be forgotten.

Etymology

From the name of the town Dachau in Bavaria, Germany, where the camp was established.
