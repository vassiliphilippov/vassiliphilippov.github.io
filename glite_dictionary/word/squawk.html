squawk

1. NOUN

/ skwɑk / (AmE), / skwɔːk / (BrE)
a loud, harsh, and often high-pitched noise, especially one made by a bird or a person
a loud, harsh noise (often by birds)

Examples

The squawk of the parrot could be heard from across the yard.
A sudden squawk startled the group of hikers on the trail.
She covered her ears at the squawk of feedback from the microphone.

Synset Examples

she awoke to the squawk of chickens
the squawk of car horns
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07410046-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ skwɑk / (AmE), / skwɔːk / (BrE)
An informal complaint or protest; an objection.
informal objection or complaint
informal

Examples

If you have a squawk about your bill, please speak to the manager.
Nobody listened to his squawk over the new company policy.
The editor received more than one squawk after the article was published.

Synset Examples

I have a gripe about the service here

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gripe - a trivial complaint or objection

Etymology

Imitative of the harsh, abrupt sound made by a bird, later used figuratively for complaints.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07224625-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ skwɑk / (AmE), / skwɔːk / (BrE)
to make a loud, harsh, abrupt cry or screech, as a bird or a person expressing complaint or shock
make a harsh, abrupt cry or protest

Examples

The parrot squawked loudly every time someone walked past its cage.
She squawked in protest when the teacher announced a pop quiz.
Suddenly, a gull squawked overhead, startling everyone on the beach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screech - make a loud, harsh, piercing sound
  • Antonym: whisper - speak very quietly, using one's breath

Etymology

Imitative origin; resembles the sound itself.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01050811-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ skwɑk / (AmE), / skwɔːk / (BrE)
to complain loudly or angrily, especially in a way that seems unnecessary or irritating
complain loudly or irritably
informal

Examples

Every time I ask for help with chores, my brother starts to squawk about how busy he is.
She squawked for days after the new policy was introduced at work.
Stop squawking and do something to fix the problem.

Synset Examples

What was he hollering about?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance
  • Synonym: whine - to complain in a feeble or petulant way

Etymology

Imitative of the harsh cry of a bird, extended figuratively to complaining.
