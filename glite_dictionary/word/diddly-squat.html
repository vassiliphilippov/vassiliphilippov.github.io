diddly-squat

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪd.li ˌskwɑt / (AmE), / ˈdɪd.li ˌskwɒt / (BrE)
a very small or worthless amount; nothing at all
a trivial or worthless amount
informal

Examples

He doesn't know diddly-squat about cars, so don't ask him for advice.
After months of work, we've achieved diddly-squat in terms of results.
They offered me diddly-squat for my old laptop, so I decided to keep it.

Synset Examples

you don't know jack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nothing - not anything; zero
  • Synonym: jack - (slang) nothing; not anything at all

Etymology

Probably a fanciful formation influenced by 'diddly' (meaning 'nothing') and 'squat' (slang for 'nothing').
