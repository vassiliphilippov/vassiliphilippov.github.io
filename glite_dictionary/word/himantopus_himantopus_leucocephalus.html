Himantopus himantopus leucocephalus

1. NOUN

/ hɪˌmæntəpəs hɪˌmæntəpəs ˌluːkoʊˈsɛfələs / (AmE), / hɪˌmantəpʊs hɪˌmantəpʊs ˌluːkəʊˈsɛfələs / (BrE)
A subspecies of stilt found in the southwest Pacific region, especially Australia and New Zealand, characterized by mostly white plumage with black wings and a black nape of the neck.
subspecies of stilt bird (Australia/NZ)

Examples

The Himantopus himantopus leucocephalus is easily identified by its black wings and white body.
Researchers observed several Himantopus himantopus leucocephalus nesting along the New Zealand wetlands.
Birdwatchers traveled to Australia to photograph the rare Himantopus himantopus leucocephalus in its natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pied stilt - Common name for Himantopus himantopus leucocephalus

Etymology

From Neo-Latin taxonomy: 'Himantopus' from Greek 'himantopous' (strap-footed); 'leucocephalus' from Greek 'leukos' (white) and 'kephalē' (head).
