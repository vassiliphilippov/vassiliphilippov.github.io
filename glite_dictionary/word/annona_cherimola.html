Annona cherimola

1. NOUN

/ əˈnoʊ.nə ˌtʃɛr.ɪˈmoʊ.lə / (AmE), / əˈnəʊ.nə ˌtʃɛ.rɪˈməʊ.lə / (BrE)
A small tropical American tree with round or oblong edible fruit, known as cherimoya, widely cultivated for its sweet, creamy pulp.
species of tropical fruit tree (cherimoya)

Examples

The Annona cherimola is cultivated in subtropical regions for its delicious fruit.
Botanists studied the growth requirements of Annona cherimola in different climates.
Farmers in South America export Annona cherimola to markets around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cherimoya - the fruit of Annona cherimola

Etymology

From Latin 'Annona' (a genus of tropical trees) and 'cherimola', from Quechua 'chirimuya' (cold seeds).
