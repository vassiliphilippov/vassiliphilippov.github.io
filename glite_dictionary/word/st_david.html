St. David

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈdeɪvɪd / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈdeɪvɪd / (BrE)
the patron saint of Wales, who lived circa 520–600, celebrated for his role in spreading Christianity in Wales
patron saint of Wales

Examples

Every year, the Welsh celebrate St. David's Day on March 1st.
Many churches in Wales are dedicated to St. David.
According to legend, St. David performed miracles during his lifetime.

Etymology

From Latin 'Sanctus Davidus', referring to David, patron saint of Wales.
