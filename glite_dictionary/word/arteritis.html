arteritis

1. NOUN

/ ɑr.təˈraɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ɑː.təˈraɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
Inflammation of one or more arteries.
inflammation of an artery
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with arteritis after observing swelling in the artery.
Certain autoimmune diseases can increase the risk of developing arteritis.
Early detection of arteritis is essential to prevent severe complications.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: phlebitis - inflammation of a vein
  • Synonym: vasculitis - inflammation of blood vessels

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'artēria' (artery) and '-itis' (inflammation).
