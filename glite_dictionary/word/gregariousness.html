gregariousness

1. NOUN

/ ɡrɪˈɡɛriəsnəs / (AmE), / ɡrɪˈɡɛːrɪəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of enjoying the company of others; sociability
the quality of being sociable

Examples

Her gregariousness made her popular at every event she attended.
The manager's gregariousness helped create a friendly atmosphere in the office.
Despite his gregariousness, he occasionally enjoyed quiet moments alone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sociability - the quality of being sociable
  • Antonym: aloofness - the quality of being distant or reserved

Etymology

From Latin 'gregarius' (belonging to a flock), from 'grex' (flock, herd) + English '-ness'.
