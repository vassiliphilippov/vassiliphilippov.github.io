to outpoint

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈpɔɪnt / (AmE), / aʊtˈpɔɪnt / (BrE)
to score more points than an opponent, especially in boxing or other competitions decided by points rather than by a knockout
win by scoring more points (esp. in boxing)

Examples

The challenger managed to outpoint the champion after twelve exciting rounds.
Our team was able to outpoint their rivals thanks to a strong final performance.
She outpointed every other competitor to win the gold medal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outscore - to score more points than someone else

Etymology

Formed from 'out-' (meaning 'more than' or 'beyond') and 'point' (referring to scoring points).
