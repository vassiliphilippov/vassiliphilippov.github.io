Origanum

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɪɡənəm / (AmE), / ɒˈrɪɡənəm / (BrE)
A genus of aromatic herbs in the mint family, including oregano and marjoram, known for their culinary and medicinal uses.
genus of aromatic herbs (oregano, marjoram)

Examples

The culinary herb oregano belongs to the Origanum genus.
Several species of Origanum are cultivated for their fragrant leaves.
Traditional medicine often uses extracts from plants in the Origanum genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oregano - an aromatic herb used in cooking, especially Italian cuisine
  • Synonym: marjoram - an aromatic herb related to oregano, used as a seasoning

Etymology

From Greek origanon (oregano, marjoram).
