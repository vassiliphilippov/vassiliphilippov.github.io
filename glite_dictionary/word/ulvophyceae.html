Ulvophyceae

1. NOUN

/ ʌlˌvoʊfəˈsiːiː / (AmE), / ʌlˌvɒfɪˈsiːiː / (BrE)
a class of green algae, sometimes classified as synonymous with Chlorophyceae in certain taxonomic systems
a class of green algae
medical

Examples

Members of the Ulvophyceae are commonly found in marine environments.
Recent studies have focused on the evolution of Ulvophyceae within green algae.
Some classification systems place sea lettuce in the class Ulvophyceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chlorophyceae - a class of green algae, sometimes overlapping with Ulvophyceae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Ulva (a genus of green algae) + -phyceae (a suffix used for algal classes).

2. NOUN

/ ˌʌlvoʊˈfaɪsiiː / (AmE), / ˌʌlvəʊˈfaɪsiːiː / (BrE)
A class of green algae in some classifications, sometimes considered an alternative name for Chlorophyceae.
class of green algae
medical

Examples

Scientists discovered new species within the Ulvophyceae during their marine expedition.
The Ulvophyceae are primarily found in marine environments around the world.
Some textbooks refer to green algae in this group as Ulvophyceae instead of Chlorophyceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chlorophyceae - class of green algae

Etymology

From Latin 'ulva' (a kind of seaweed) + Greek 'phyke' (seaweed), with scientific suffix '-eae' for class names.
