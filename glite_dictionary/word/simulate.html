to simulate

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪmjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪmjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to create a representation or imitation of something, especially in order to study or test it
create an imitation or model

Examples

The software can simulate complex weather patterns for research purposes.
The experiment was designed to simulate conditions found on Mars.
Video games often simulate real-world physics to enhance realism.

Synset Examples

The pilots are trained in conditions simulating high-altitude flights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to imitate - to copy the behavior or appearance of someone or something

Etymology

From Latin simulatus, past participle of simulare (to imitate), from similis (like, resembling).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01726857-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈsɪmjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪmjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to pretend to have a feeling, quality, or state in order to deceive; to make a pretense of
pretend to have a feeling or state

Examples

He tried to simulate calmness, though inside he was terrified.
Some students simulate interest when the lesson gets boring.
She managed to simulate surprise at the unexpected party.

Synset Examples

She assumed indifference, even though she was seething with anger
he feigned sleep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to feign - to pretend to be affected by a feeling, state, or injury
  • Synonym: pretend - to behave as if something is true when it is not

Etymology

From Latin simulatus, past participle of simulare (to imitate, to pretend), from similis (like).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01725631-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈsɪmjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪmjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to imitate the appearance or behavior of someone or something, often to make it seem real or genuine
imitate appearance or behavior

Examples

The actor was able to simulate the famous politician’s gestures perfectly on stage.
Some children like to simulate their teachers’ voices when playing school at home.
With lots of practice, you can simulate a convincing British accent.

Synset Examples

The mime imitated the passers-by
Children often copy their parents or older siblings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to imitate - copy the behavior or appearance of someone or something
  • Synonym: mimic - imitate someone, especially to entertain or ridicule
  • Synonym: copy - make a reproduction of something; do as someone else does

Etymology

From Latin simulatus, past participle of simulare (to imitate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01746767-v
Synset POS: v
