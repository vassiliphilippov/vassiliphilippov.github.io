forsooth

1. ADV

/ fərˈsuθ / (AmE), / fəˈsuːθ / (BrE)
(archaic or humorous) Used to affirm a statement or, more often, to express surprise or disbelief, originally meaning 'in truth.'
(archaic) expressing truth or disbelief
archaic

Examples

Forsooth, he claimed he knew nothing of the matter, which I find hard to believe.
The knight, forsooth, declared his undying loyalty to the crown.
You are a scholar, forsooth! I would never have guessed given your behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indeed - used to emphasize a statement or response
  • Synonym: verily - truly; certainly

Etymology

From Old English 'for sōþ' meaning 'in truth'.
