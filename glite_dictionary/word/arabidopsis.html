Arabidopsis

1. NOUN

/ ˌær.ə.bɪˈdɑp.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌær.ə.bɪˈdɒp.sɪs / (BrE)
a genus of plants in the mustard family with white, yellow, or purplish flowers, closely related to Arabis
genus of plants in the mustard family

Examples

Arabidopsis is widely used as a model organism in plant research.
The genome of Arabidopsis has been fully sequenced to aid genetic studies.
Scientists studied the effects of drought on Arabidopsis seedlings in the laboratory.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Arabis' (a related genus) and Greek 'opsis' (appearance).

2. NOUN

/ ˌærəbɪˈdɑpsɪs / (AmE), / ˌærəbɪˈdɒpsɪs / (BrE)
a genus of plants in the mustard family with white, yellow, or purplish flowers, closely related to Arabis; includes several species studied in biology, especially Arabidopsis thaliana.
a genus of small flowering plants in the mustard family

Examples

Arabidopsis is widely used as a model organism in plant biology research.
The genome of Arabidopsis thaliana was the first plant genome to be sequenced.
Some gardeners use Arabidopsis for educational demonstrations due to its rapid lifecycle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arabis - a related genus of plants in the mustard family

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'arabid-' (from 'Arabis', a genus name) + '-opsis' (resembling).
