chrysanthemum

1. NOUN

/ krəˈsænθəˌmum / (AmE), / krɪˈsænθɪməm / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Chrysanthemum, with showy, brightly colored flowers, widely cultivated as an ornamental flower
a flowering plant with large, colorful blooms

Examples

The chrysanthemum is often associated with autumn festivals in many cultures.
She arranged a bouquet featuring a single large chrysanthemum in the center.
Many gardeners choose to plant chrysanthemums for their vivid fall colors.

Etymology

From Greek 'chrysos' (gold) + 'anthemon' (flower).

2. NOUN

/ krɪˈsænθəməm / (AmE), / krɪˈsanθɪməm / (BrE)
a plant of the daisy family with brightly colored, showy flower heads, often cultivated as an ornamental flower
flowering plant with showy blooms

Examples

She placed a single chrysanthemum in the blue vase on the table.
The garden was filled with rows of blooming chrysanthemums in every color imaginable.
Her favorite flower is the chrysanthemum because of its vibrant petals and long-lasting blooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daisy - a small grassland plant with a yellow disk and white rays

Etymology

From Greek khrusanthemum, from khrusos (gold) + anthemon (flower).
