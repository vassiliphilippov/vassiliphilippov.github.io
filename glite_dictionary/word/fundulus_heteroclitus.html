Fundulus heteroclitus

1. NOUN

/ fʌnˈduːləs ˌhɛtərəˈklaɪtəs / (AmE), / fʌnˈdʒuːləs ˌhɛtərəˈklaɪtəs / (BrE)
A small silver-and-black killifish native to saltwater marshes along the Atlantic coast of the United States, commonly known as the mummichog.
species of saltwater killifish (mummichog)

Examples

Researchers conducted a study on Fundulus heteroclitus to understand its adaptability to changing environments.
The salt marsh was teeming with Fundulus heteroclitus during high tide.
Fishermen often use Fundulus heteroclitus as bait for larger fish species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mummichog - Common name for Fundulus heteroclitus, a small killifish found in North American saltwater marshes

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Fundulus' (a genus name, from Latin 'fundus' meaning 'bottom, base') and 'heteroclitus' (from Greek 'heteros' meaning 'different' and 'klitos' meaning 'inclined, sloped').
