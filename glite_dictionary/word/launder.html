to launder

1. VERB

/ ˈlɔːndər / (AmE), / ˈlɔːndə / (BrE)
to wash and clean clothes, sheets, etc., with water and usually soap or detergent
wash and clean clothes or fabrics

Examples

She decided to launder all of the bedding before her guests arrived.
It is recommended to launder your workout clothes after each use.
He carefully laundered the curtains according to the care label instructions.

Synset Examples

Wash the towels, please!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wash - to clean something with water and usually soap

Etymology

From Old French 'lavander', from Vulgar Latin 'lavandaria', from Latin 'lavare' (to wash).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01538034-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈlɔːndər / (AmE), / ˈlɔːndə / (BrE)
to conceal the origins of illegally obtained money by transferring it through legitimate businesses or transactions, making it appear legal.
to make illegal money appear legal

Examples

Criminals often launder money through offshore accounts to avoid detection.
She was arrested for attempting to launder the proceeds of fraud.
The company was investigated for allegedly helping clients launder illicit funds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: money laundering - the process of concealing the origins of illegally obtained money
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00189281-v
Synset POS: v
