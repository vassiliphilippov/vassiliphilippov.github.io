Attlee

1. PROPN

/ ˈætli / (AmE), / ˈætli / (BrE)
(1883–1967) British statesman and leader of the Labour Party, Prime Minister (1945–1951), who established the welfare state in Britain.
British Labour politician, Prime Minister 1945–1951

Examples

Attlee became Prime Minister immediately after World War II and led major social reforms in Britain.
Historians often credit Attlee with laying the foundations of modern British welfare.
The legacy of Attlee's government still influences British politics today.
