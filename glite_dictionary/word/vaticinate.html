to vaticinate

1. VERB

/ vəˈtɪsɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / vəˈtɪsɪneɪt / (BrE)
to foretell or prophesy, especially through or as if through divine inspiration
to prophesy or foretell

Examples

The oracle claimed to vaticinate the fate of the entire kingdom.
Many mystics vaticinate dire consequences if humanity ignores environmental warnings.
Ancient prophets were believed to vaticinate future events with divine guidance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prophesy - to predict what will happen in the future, especially by divine inspiration
  • Synonym: to predict - to forecast or state something as likely to happen in the future

Etymology

From Latin vaticinari (to prophesy), from vates (seer, prophet).

2. VERB

/ vəˈtɪsɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / vəˈtɪsɪneɪt / (BrE)
to foretell or prophesy something, especially by supernatural means
to foretell or prophesy

Examples

The oracle claimed to vaticinate the king's fate before his coronation.
Many ancient priests would vaticinate events to inspire awe among their followers.
Although he tried to vaticinate the outcome, no one took his warnings seriously.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prophesy - to predict or reveal something as if by divine inspiration
  • Synonym: to predict - to state what will happen in the future

Etymology

From Latin vāticinātus, past participle of vāticinārī (to prophesy), from vātēs (seer, prophet).
