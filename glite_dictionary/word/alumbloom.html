alumbloom

1. NOUN

/ ˈæləmˌbluːm / (AmE), / ˈæləmˌbluːm / (BrE)
Any of several perennial herbs of the genus Heuchera, often grown for their ornamental foliage and small clustered flowers.
herb of the genus Heuchera

Examples

The garden was filled with alumbloom, giving a soft texture to the shady borders.
She planted alumbloom alongside ferns for a lush, natural look.
Several varieties of alumbloom are prized for their colorful, patterned leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coral bells - another common name for Heuchera plants

Etymology

From 'alum' (a reference to the plant's use as astringent) and 'bloom' (flower).
