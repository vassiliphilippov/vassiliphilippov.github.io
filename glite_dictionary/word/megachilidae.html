Megachilidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəˈkɪlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡəˈkɪlɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of bees that includes leaf-cutting and mason bees, known for their distinctive nesting behaviors
family of leaf-cutting and mason bees

Examples

The Megachilidae are well known for their use of leaves and resin to construct nests.
Gardeners appreciate Megachilidae species for their role as efficient pollinators.
Research on Megachilidae helps scientists understand the evolution of solitary bee behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bee - insect of the family Apoidea known for pollination and honey production

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'mega' (large) and 'cheilos' (lip), referring to their large mandibles.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəˈkɪlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡəˈkɪlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of bees that includes leaf-cutting and mason bees, noted for their nest-building behavior using leaves or mud
a family of leaf-cutting and mason bees

Examples

The Megachilidae are well known for their remarkable nest-building using leaves or mud.
Gardeners often observe Megachilidae carrying leaf fragments back to their nests.
Many species in the Megachilidae family are important pollinators of wildflowers and crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leafcutter bee - a bee that cuts leaves to build its nest (genus Megachile)
  • Synonym: mason bee - a bee that builds nests from mud (genus Osmia and others)

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Megachilidae', based on the genus 'Megachile', from Greek 'mega-' (large) and 'cheilos' (lip).
