wane

1. VERB

/ weɪn / (AmE), / weɪn / (BrE)
to decrease gradually in size, amount, strength, or intensity
to decrease or diminish

Examples

During the winter, daylight hours wane and darkness sets in earlier each evening.
Support for the proposed law has begun to wane sharply in recent weeks.
His enthusiasm for the project waned after several setbacks.

Synset Examples

Interest in his novels waned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to diminish - to become or make smaller or less
  • Antonym: increase - to become or make greater in size, amount, or degree

Etymology

From Old English 'wanian' (to lessen), related to Old Norse 'vana' (to decrease).

3. VERB

/ weɪn / (AmE), / weɪn / (BrE)
(of the moon or a similar celestial body) to decrease in the visible illuminated portion after a full moon, becoming less bright or full.
of the moon: to decrease in size or brightness

Examples

The wane of the moon was clearly visible after the full moon last week.
As the days passed, we noticed that the crescent wane became thinner in the night sky.
After being full for several nights, the moon began to wane gradually.

Synset Examples

the moon is waning

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wax - (of the moon) to increase in visible illuminated portion after a new moon

Etymology

From Old English wanian (to lessen), related to wan (lacking, deficient).

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ weɪn / (AmE), / weɪn / (BrE)
A gradual decline in size, strength, power, or number.
gradual decline or decrease

Examples

After the festival ended, excitement was on the wane throughout the city.
The power of the empire was already in wane by the early 18th century.
