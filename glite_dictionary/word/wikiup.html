wikiup

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪk.i.ˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈwɪk.i.ˌʌp / (BrE)
A hut or lodge made with a frame covered in brush, grass, or matting, traditionally used by some Native American peoples of the southwestern United States.
Native American brush hut or lodge

Examples

The Apache family constructed a wikiup for shelter during their travels.
Several old wikiups can still be seen in the desert regions of Arizona.
A traditional wikiup is made with branches and covered with grass mats.

Etymology

From a North American indigenous language, possibly from the Algonquian word 'wi·kiyo·p' (dwelling).
