Potoroinae

1. NOUN

/ poʊˌtoʊroʊˈiːnaɪ / (AmE), / pəʊˌtɒrəʊˈiːniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of small marsupials known as rat kangaroos, native to Australia.
subfamily of rat kangaroos

Examples

The Potoroinae are an important group of marsupials endemic to Australia.
Scientists study the Potoroinae to understand the evolution of marsupials.
Several species within the Potoroinae are considered vulnerable or endangered due to habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rat kangaroo - small marsupial of Australia, related to kangaroos but smaller

Etymology

From scientific Latin, based on Potoroos (genus name) and -inae (zoological suffix for subfamilies).
