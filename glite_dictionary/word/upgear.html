to upgear

1. VERB

/ ʌpˈɡɪr / (AmE), / ʌpˈɡɪə / (BrE)
to enhance or improve something, especially an approach or strategy, by making it more effective or advanced
enhance or improve, especially a strategy

Examples

The management decided to upgear their strategy to stay ahead of the competition.
We must upgear our marketing efforts if we want to reach new customers.
Our team has been encouraged to upgear their approach to problem-solving.

Synset Examples

We need to upgear our strategy to meet demands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upgrade - raise something to a higher standard or improve it
  • Synonym: to improve - make or become better

Etymology

Formed from 'up' (to a higher level) and 'gear' (equipment, mechanism, or to prepare), modeled after terms like 'upgrade' and 'upskill'.
