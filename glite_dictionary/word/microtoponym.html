microtoponym

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkroʊˈtɑpənɪm / (AmE), / ˌmʌɪkrəʊˈtɒpənɪm / (BrE)
a name given to a minor or small geographical feature, such as a field, path, bridge, or ditch
name of a small geographical feature

Examples

The researcher documented each microtoponym used by local farmers to refer to their fields.
A microtoponym like 'Gate Corner' distinguishes a small area within the village.
Preserving microtoponyms helps maintain the cultural heritage of rural landscapes.

Synset Examples

Larrain is the name of a farmhouse in Astigarraga (Gipuzkoa) and a microtoponym in the same province.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: toponym - a place name, especially one derived from a topographical feature

Etymology

From Greek micro- (small) + toponym (place name).
