Coumadin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkuː.mə.dɪn / (AmE), / ˈkuː.mə.dɪn / (BrE)
A brand name for warfarin, an anticoagulant drug used to prevent and treat blood clots such as thrombi or emboli.
anticoagulant drug (warfarin) brand name
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Coumadin to reduce the risk of further blood clots.
Patients taking Coumadin require regular blood tests to monitor their dosage.
He developed severe bruising due to his Coumadin therapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warfarin - an anticoagulant medication; the generic substance underlying Coumadin
