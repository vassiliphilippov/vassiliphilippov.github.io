to irritate

1. VERB

/ ˈɪrɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪrɪteɪt / (BrE)
(of a stimulus) to cause a biological part, such as an organ, tissue, or cell, to respond with an action or reaction, such as motion, contraction, or nervous impulse.
stimulate a biological reaction
medical

Examples

Applying salt can irritate the surface cells of the tongue, causing a sharp sensation.
Certain chemicals irritate nerve endings in the skin, producing a reaction.
If pollen irritates the lining of the nose, it may trigger sneezing.

Synset Examples

irritate the glands of a leaf

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stimulate - encourage a biological or physiological activity
  • Antonym: calm - to reduce activity or irritation in biological tissues

Etymology

From Latin irritare (to excite, provoke).

2. VERB

/ ˈɪrɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪrɪteɪt / (BrE)
to make someone feel annoyed, impatient, or slightly angry, especially by repeatedly doing something or through minor disturbances
to annoy or provoke slight anger

Examples

The constant noise from the construction site began to irritate the nearby residents.
Her habit of humming during meetings tends to irritate her coworkers.
I didn't mean to irritate you by reminding you again.

Synset Examples

Mosquitoes buzzing in my ear really bothers me
It irritates me that she never closes the door after she leaves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to annoy - to make someone a little angry or impatient
  • Synonym: to provoke - to cause a particular reaction or feeling, especially anger
  • Antonym: to soothe - to make someone calmer or less angry

Etymology

From Latin 'irritare' (to excite, provoke).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01791774-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈɪrɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪrɪteɪt / (BrE)
(of a substance or action) to cause a part of the body to become sore, inflamed, or painful by chafing, inflaming, or stimulating abnormally.
cause soreness or inflammation

Examples

The spicy food can irritate the lining of your stomach if eaten in excess.
Certain chemicals in cosmetics may irritate sensitive skin.
Continuous rubbing of the shoe can irritate your heel, causing blisters.

Synset Examples

Aspirin irritates my stomach

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inflame - to cause inflammation in a part of the body

Etymology

From Latin irritatus, past participle of irritare (to excite, provoke).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00063062-v
Synset POS: v
