dormancy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔrmənsi / (AmE), / ˈdɔːmənsi / (BrE)
a state of quiet and inactivity, especially as a period in which a living thing is temporarily not growing or developing
state of inactivity or rest

Examples

Bears enter a state of dormancy during the winter months to conserve energy.
The seeds remained in dormancy until the following spring when they began to germinate.
Periods of dormancy are common in many plants and animals as a survival strategy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hibernation - the condition or period of an animal or plant spending the winter in a dormant state

Etymology

From Latin dormīre (to sleep), via French dormance.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɔrmənsi / (AmE), / ˈdɔːmənsi / (BrE)
a period or state in which something is inactive or not growing, often temporarily
a state of temporary inactivity

Examples

The seeds entered dormancy during the winter to survive the cold temperatures.
After years of dormancy, the company finally launched a new product.
Volcanic dormancy can last for centuries before an eruption occurs.

Synset Examples

the volcano erupted after centuries of dormancy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inactivity - the state of being inactive
  • Antonym: activity - the state of being active

Etymology

From Latin 'dormire' (to sleep), via French 'dormance'.
