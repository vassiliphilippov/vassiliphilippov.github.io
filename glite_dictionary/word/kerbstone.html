kerbstone

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɝːbˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈkɜːbstəʊn / (BrE)
a long, narrow stone or concrete block forming the edge between a pavement and a roadway
stone forming pavement edge
british

Examples

The car mounted the kerbstone and came to a stop on the pavement.
Workers replaced the damaged kerbstone along the busy street.
She tripped on a loose kerbstone while crossing the road.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curbstone - a paving stone forming part of a curb, primarily in American English

Etymology

From British English 'kerb' (edge of pavement) + 'stone'.
