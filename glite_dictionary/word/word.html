Word

1. NOUN

/ wɜrd / (AmE), / wɜːd / (BrE)
(in Christian theology) the divine Word of God; Jesus Christ, regarded as the second person of the Trinity and the incarnation of God's word.
the divine Word; Jesus Christ in Christian theology

Examples

In Christian doctrine, the Word was made flesh and lived among us.
The Gospel of John refers to Christ as the Word who existed from the beginning.
Many theologians have debated the meaning of the term 'Word' in the context of the Trinity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Logos - philosophical and theological term for 'word' or 'principle', especially in Christianity

Etymology

From Greek 'Logos' (word, reason), as translated in the opening of the Gospel of John.

2. NOUN

/ wɜrd / (AmE), / wɜːd / (BrE)
(in Christian use) The sacred writings of the Christian religions; the Bible, often referred to as 'the Word'.
the Christian Bible; holy scripture

Examples

He dedicated his life to preaching the Word to every corner of the globe.
Many people find comfort and guidance in the Word.
Some believers carry a small copy of the Word wherever they go.

Synset Examples

he went to carry the Word to the heathen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scripture - the sacred writings of Christianity contained in the Bible

Etymology

From Old English 'word', ultimately from Proto-Germanic *wurdą, with special religious use capitalized in Christian tradition.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
