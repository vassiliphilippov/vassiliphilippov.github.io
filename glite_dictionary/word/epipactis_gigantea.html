Epipactis gigantea

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈpæktɪs dʒaɪˈgæn.ti.ə / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈpæktɪs dʒɪˈɡæn.tɪə / (BrE)
A species of orchid native to western North America, found growing along streams and ponds, with leafy stems and greenish-brown and pinkish flowers borne in the axils of the upper leaves.
a species of North American orchid

Examples

Epipactis gigantea thrives in moist, shady areas along the banks of streams in California.
The garden features a native species called Epipactis gigantea, admired for its unique flowers.
Researchers study Epipactis gigantea to understand its adaptation to aquatic environments.

Etymology

From Greek 'epipaktis' (a kind of orchid) and Latin 'gigantea' (giant), indicating its large size.
