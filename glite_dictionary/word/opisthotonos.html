opisthotonos

1. NOUN

/ əˌpɪsθəˈtoʊnəs / (AmE), / əˌpɪsθəˈtəʊnɒs / (BrE)
a condition of severe spasm in which the body is bent backward and the head and heels are forced backward due to muscle contractions
severe body spasm with backward arching
medical

Examples

The patient developed opisthotonos after the onset of severe tetanus symptoms.
In cases of meningitis, intense muscle contractions can result in opisthotonos.
Doctors observed opisthotonos as a sign of neurological disturbance during the examination.

Etymology

From Greek opisthotonos, from opistho- (behind) + tonos (tension).
