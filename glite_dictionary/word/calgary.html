Calgary

1. PROPN

/ ˈkælɡəri / (AmE), / ˈkælɡəri / (BrE)
A city in southern Alberta, Canada, known as a major center for oil, gas, and technology industries.
city in southern Alberta, Canada

Examples

Calgary hosted the Winter Olympics in 1988, attracting international attention to the city.
Many young professionals are moving to Calgary because of its booming technology sector.
The skyline of Calgary is dominated by modern skyscrapers and the iconic Calgary Tower.

Etymology

Named after Calgary on the Isle of Mull, Scotland.
