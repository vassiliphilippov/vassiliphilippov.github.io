sordidly

1. ADV

/ ˈsɔːrdɪdli / (AmE), / ˈsɔːdɪdli / (BrE)
In a sordid or squalid manner; involving dirty, morally degraded, or unpleasant conditions.
in a filthy or morally degraded way

Examples

The politician acted sordidly, accepting bribes behind closed doors.
The room was sordidly neglected, with trash piled in every corner.
She described his sordidly manipulative actions in her memoir.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sordid - dirty or morally degraded

Etymology

From 'sordid' + '-ly'. 'Sordid' comes from Latin 'sordidus' (dirty, filthy).
