covenant

1. VERB

/ ˈkʌvənənt / (AmE), / ˈkʌvənənt / (BrE)
to enter into a formal and binding agreement or promise, especially by legal contract
enter into a formal agreement

Examples

The two companies covenanted not to compete with each other for five years.
The landlord and tenant covenant to respect the terms set out in the lease.
In 1650, the Scottish nobles covenanted with the king for religious freedoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contract - make a formal and legally binding agreement

Etymology

From Old French *covenant* (agreement), from Latin *convenire* (to come together, agree).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02480203-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌvənənt / (AmE), / ˈkʌvənənt / (BrE)
a formal written agreement between two or more parties, especially nations, to do or refrain from doing something
formal written agreement; treaty
legal

Examples

The two countries signed a covenant pledging mutual cooperation and support.
Breaking a covenant can have serious legal and diplomatic consequences.
The leaders negotiated every term before finally agreeing to the covenant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treaty - a formally concluded and ratified agreement between countries
  • Synonym: contract - a legally binding agreement between parties

Etymology

From Old French covenant, from covenir (to agree), from Latin convenire (to come together, agree).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈkʌvənənt / (AmE), / ˈkʌvənənt / (BrE)
(in the Bible) An agreement made by God with his people, in which God makes certain promises and requires certain conduct in return.
Biblical agreement between God and his people

Examples

In the Old Testament, God made a covenant with Abraham and his descendants.
The rainbow served as a sign of the covenant between God and humanity.
Many biblical scholars study the different covenants found throughout scripture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contract - a legally binding agreement between two or more parties
  • Degree Lower: promise - a declaration that one will do or refrain from doing something

From Old French covenant, from Latin convenire 'to come together, agree'.
