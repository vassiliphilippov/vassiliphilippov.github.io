subtitle

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbˌtaɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.taɪ.təl / (BrE)
a translation of dialogue in a foreign film or television programme, usually shown as text at the bottom of the screen
translation text shown in films/TV

Examples

The French film had English subtitles to help the audience understand the dialogue.
I prefer watching movies with subtitles rather than dubbed versions.
He turned on the subtitles because the characters were speaking too quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caption - text displayed on a screen to provide information

Etymology

From Latin sub- (under) + English title.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbˌtaɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌtaɪtəl / (BrE)
a secondary title below the main title of a book, article, or work, often giving additional information or clarification
secondary or explanatory title

Examples

The subtitle of the book explained its focus in more detail.
She suggested adding a subtitle to clarify the purpose of her report.
The magazine article's subtitle drew readers in with an intriguing question.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: title - the name of a book, composition, or work

Etymology

From Latin sub- (under) + English title.

3. VERB

/ ˈsʌbˌtaɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌtʌɪt(ə)l / (BrE)
to provide (a film, video, or broadcast) with subtitles, usually as a written translation of the dialogue
add subtitles to video or film

Examples

They decided to subtitle the documentary so that international audiences could understand it.
Many foreign films are subtitled in English for wider distribution.
She has experience subtitling movies for both television and streaming platforms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caption - to provide a short title or explanation below an image or video

Etymology

Formed from 'sub-' (under) and 'title', reflecting the placement of captions underneath video images.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
