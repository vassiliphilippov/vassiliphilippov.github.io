dubbing

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʌbɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or result of adding a new soundtrack, especially dialogue or narration, to a film or video, often in a different language from the original
new soundtrack added to a film

Examples

The foreign movie used English dubbing to make it accessible to a wider audience.
Poorly done dubbing can distract viewers from the storyline.
The studio hired voice actors for the dubbing of the animated series.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: subtitling - the process of adding written translation to the bottom of a film screen
  • Synonym: voice-over - spoken commentary in a film not accompanied by the speaker's image

Etymology

From 'dub', meaning to add or replace sound, originally from Old French 'dober' (to confer knighthood), later extended in film and audio contexts.
