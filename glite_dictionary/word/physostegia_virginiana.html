Physostegia virginiana

1. NOUN

/ ˌfaɪsoʊˈstiːdʒə vərˌdʒɪniˈænə / (AmE), / ˌfɪsəʊˈstiːdʒɪə vɜːdʒɪˈneɪənə / (BrE)
A North American perennial plant with spikes of two-lipped pink or white flowers, commonly known as obedient plant.
a North American flowering plant (obedient plant)

Examples

The Physostegia virginiana flourishes in moist garden beds and attracts hummingbirds with its colorful blossoms.
We planted Physostegia virginiana along the fence for its beautiful summer flowers.
Because its stems stay in position when bent, Physostegia virginiana is also called the obedient plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obedient plant - common name for Physostegia virginiana

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus name from Greek 'physa' (bladder) and 'stege' (covering); species from Virginia, USA.
