Nativity

1. NOUN

/ nəˈtɪv.ə.ti / (AmE), / nəˈtɪv.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
(in Christianity) the birth of Jesus Christ, believed to have been conceived without a human father and often celebrated at Christmas.
the birth of Jesus Christ in Christianity

Examples

The church displayed a beautiful scene of the Nativity during the Christmas season.
Christians around the world celebrate the Nativity on December 25th.
Art depicting the Nativity often includes Mary, Joseph, and the infant Jesus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Christmas - Christian festival celebrating the birth of Jesus

Etymology

From Latin 'nativitas', meaning 'birth', from 'nasci' (to be born).
