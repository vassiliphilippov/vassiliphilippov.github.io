sumptuary

1. ADJ

/ ˈsʌmp.tʃuˌɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈsʌmp.tjʊ.ə.ri / (BrE)
(of a law or regulation) intended to limit personal expenditure or regulate personal behavior, especially in relation to luxury and extravagance.
intended to regulate personal expenditure or behavior

Examples

Many medieval societies enacted sumptuary laws to prevent people from spending excessively on clothing.
The city council debated a sumptuary regulation that would restrict flashy advertising displays.
Such sumptuary rules were often designed to reinforce social hierarchies and moral codes.

Synset Examples

sumptuary laws discouraging construction of large houses on small plots
sumptuary laws forbidding gambling

Etymology

From Latin sumptuarius (relating to expense), from sumptus (expenditure).
