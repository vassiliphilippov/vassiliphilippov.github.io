satiated

1. ADJ

/ ˈseɪʃiˌeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈseɪʃieɪtɪd / (BrE)
having had enough or more than enough of something, especially food; fully satisfied
fully satisfied, especially with food

Examples

After a large dinner, I felt completely satiated and couldn't eat another bite.
She was satiated by the rich and abundant meal served at the banquet.
Once the guests were satiated, the host brought out a selection of desserts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: full - having eaten enough food; not hungry anymore
  • Antonym: hungry - feeling the need for food; not full

Etymology

From Latin satiatus, past participle of satiare (to fill, satisfy), from satis (enough).
