full-blown

1. ADJ

/ fʊl bloʊn / (AmE), / fʊl bləʊn / (BrE)
Having developed completely, with all the qualities or characteristics expected; fully developed.
fully developed; complete

Examples

The country was facing a full-blown crisis after the government collapsed.
By morning, her minor cold had turned into a full-blown flu.
The discussion erupted into a full-blown argument over dinner.

Synset Examples

a full-blown financial crisis

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incipient - beginning to develop or exist; not yet fully formed
