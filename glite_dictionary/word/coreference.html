coreference

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈrɛfərəns / (AmE), / kəʊˈrɛfərəns / (BrE)
The grammatical relation between two words or phrases that refer to the same person or thing in a sentence or discourse.
words sharing the same referent

Examples

In the sentence 'John said he would come,' coreference occurs between 'John' and 'he'.
Understanding coreference is essential for building effective natural language processing systems.
Mistakes in coreference resolution can cause confusion in pronoun interpretation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anaphora - the use of a word referring back to a previous word or phrase

Etymology

From Latin 'co-' (together) + 'reference' (referring), influenced by linguistic terminology.
