decadency

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈkædənsi / (AmE), / dɪˈkædənsi / (BrE)
the state of moral or cultural decline characterized by excessive self-indulgence or deterioration in values
state of moral or cultural decline

Examples

Many historians attribute the empire's downfall to widespread decadency among the ruling classes.
Some religious leaders warn against the decadency of modern culture.
The novel vividly portrays a society slipping into decadency and moral chaos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decadence - state of decline or decay in morals or culture
  • Antonym: virtue - moral excellence

Etymology

Formed from 'decadent' + '-cy', similar to 'decadence'; ultimately from Latin 'decadentia' (decaying).
