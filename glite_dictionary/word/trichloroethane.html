trichloroethane

1. NOUN

/ ˌtraɪˌklɔːroʊˈɛθeɪn / (AmE), / ˌtrɪˌklɔːrəʊˈiːθeɪn / (BrE)
a heavy, colorless, highly toxic liquid chemical used as a solvent in cleaning electronic components, in dry cleaning, and as a fumigant; can cause cancer and damage to the liver and lungs
toxic solvent and cleaning chemical

Examples

Exposure to trichloroethane in poorly ventilated areas can be hazardous to health.
Some older electronics were cleaned using trichloroethane before its dangers were well understood.
Due to environmental concerns, the use of trichloroethane as a solvent has greatly declined.

Etymology

From tri- (three) + chloro- (chlorine) + ethane, indicating three chlorine atoms attached to an ethane structure.
