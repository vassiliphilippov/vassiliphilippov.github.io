puka

1. NOUN

/ ˈpuːkə / (AmE), / ˈpuːkə / (BrE)
A South American shrub or small tree with long, shiny evergreen leaves and clusters of green or yellow flowers.
South American shrub or small tree

Examples

The puka is valued for its attractive evergreen foliage in local gardens.
Researchers discovered a rare puka specimen thriving in the rainforests of Bolivia.
The botanical guide described the puka as a species with clusters of yellow flowers.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpuːkə / (AmE), / ˈpuːkə / (BrE)
a small New Zealand tree (Meryta sinclairii) with large, glossy, resinous leaves and clusters of greenish-white flowers
New Zealand tree with large leaves

Examples

The glossy leaves of the puka make it a popular choice for subtropical gardens.
We saw a rare puka in flower during our hike in northern New Zealand.
The puka thrives in sheltered coastal areas due to its sensitivity to frost.

Etymology

From Maori 'puka', the tree's indigenous name.
