Zoysia japonica

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɔɪziə dʒəˈpoʊnɪkə / (AmE), / ˈzɔɪziə dʒəˈpɒnɪkə / (BrE)
A species of lawn grass native to East Asia, commonly grown in China, Japan, and the United States for turf and lawns.
species of lawn grass

Examples

Zoysia japonica is often chosen for golf courses because of its resilience and attractive appearance.
Many homeowners prefer Zoysia japonica for their lawns due to its low maintenance needs.
The park replaced its old turf with Zoysia japonica to create a dense, green carpet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zoysiagrass - any grass of the genus Zoysia

Etymology

From the genus name Zoysia, named after Austrian botanist Karl von Zois, and Latin japonica, meaning 'of Japan'.
