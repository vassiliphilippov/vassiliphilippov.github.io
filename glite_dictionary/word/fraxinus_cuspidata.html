Fraxinus cuspidata

1. NOUN

/ frækˈsaɪ.nəs ˌkʌs.pɪˈdeɪ.tə / (AmE), / frækˈsɪ.nəs ˌkʌs.pɪˈdɑː.tə / (BrE)
A species of ash tree native to the southwestern United States, characterized by its shrubby habit and fragrant white flowers.
a shrubby ash tree with fragrant white flowers

Examples

The Fraxinus cuspidata flourishes in the arid environments of the Southwest.
Botanists are studying the Fraxinus cuspidata for its ability to resist drought.
Gardeners appreciate Fraxinus cuspidata for the fragrance of its springtime flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ash tree - a common deciduous tree of the genus Fraxinus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Fraxinus' meaning 'ash tree' and 'cuspidata' meaning 'pointed'.
