adequacy

1. NOUN

/ ˈæd.ə.kwə.si / (AmE), / ˈad.ɪ.kwə.si / (BrE)
the quality of being sufficient or suitable for a particular purpose
being sufficient or suitable

Examples

The committee assessed the adequacy of the safety measures put in place.
Her adequacy for the role was never in question, given her years of experience.
There was some debate about the adequacy of the evidence to support the claim.

Synset Examples

he questioned the sufficiency of human intelligence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sufficiency - the state of being sufficient; adequacy
  • Antonym: inadequacy - the state of being insufficient or not enough

Etymology

From Latin adaequatus (made equal to, suitable), from adaequare (to make equal).

2. NOUN

/ ˈædɪkwəsi / (AmE), / ˈadɪkwəsi / (BrE)
the quality of being sufficient or suitable to meet a requirement or need
the state of being sufficient or suitable

Examples

The adequacy of the emergency shelters was questioned after the hurricane.
She doubted the adequacy of her preparation for the exam.
We need to assess the adequacy of our current resources before launching the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sufficiency - the quality of being enough for a purpose
  • Antonym: inadequacy - the state of being insufficient or not enough

Etymology

From Latin adaequatus, past participle of adaequare (to make equal), from ad- (to) + aequus (equal).

Synset POS: n
