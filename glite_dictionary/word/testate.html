testate

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɛsteɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɛsteɪt / (BrE)
(of a person) having made a legally valid will before death.
having made a valid will
legal

Examples

The estate was distributed smoothly because the deceased died testate.
It is important to be testate to ensure your wishes are respected after your death.
The law provides different procedures for testate and intestate estates.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intestate - dying without having made a valid will

Etymology

From Latin testatus, past participle of testari 'to make a will'.

WordNet ID: oewn-02453826-a

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛˌsteɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɛsteɪt / (BrE)
A person who has made a legally valid will before death.
person who made a will
legal

Examples

The property was distributed according to the wishes expressed by the testate in the document.
When a testate dies, the executor must follow the instructions outlined in the will.
The court determined the testate's capacity to make a valid will at the time of signing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intestate - a person who has died without making a will

Etymology

From Latin 'testatus', past participle of 'testari' (to make a will).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10722985-n
Synset POS: n
