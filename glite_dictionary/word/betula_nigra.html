Betula nigra

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈtjuːlə ˈnaɪɡrə / (AmE), / bɪˈtjuːlə ˈnɪɡrə / (BrE)
a species of birch native to swamps and river bottoms in the eastern United States, known for its reddish-brown bark; also called river birch
a species of birch tree native to eastern US

Examples

The distinctive peeling bark of the Betula nigra makes it easily identifiable along riverbanks.
Gardeners often plant Betula nigra in wet areas because of its tolerance for flooding.
The Betula nigra thrives in the moist soils of the southeastern United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: river birch - common name for Betula nigra, a birch tree of riverbanks and swamps in eastern North America

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Betula' (birch), species epithet 'nigra' meaning 'black'.
