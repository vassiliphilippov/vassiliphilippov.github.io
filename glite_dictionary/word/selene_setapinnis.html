Selene setapinnis

1. NOUN

/ səˈliːni sɛtəˈpɪnɪs / (AmE), / sɛˈliːni sɛtəˈpɪnɪs / (BrE)
A species of silvery marine fish with a very flat body, commonly known as the Atlantic moonfish.
a species of flat silvery marine fish

Examples

The Selene setapinnis is often called the Atlantic moonfish due to its distinctive silvery appearance.
Fishermen sometimes catch Selene setapinnis while trawling in coastal waters.
Marine biologists study Selene setapinnis to understand its role in ocean ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moonfish - another name for Selene setapinnis, a flat silvery marine fish

Etymology

From Latin: Selene (genus name) and setapinnis (species name, Latin for 'bristle-fin').
