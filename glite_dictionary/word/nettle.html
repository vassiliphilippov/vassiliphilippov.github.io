nettle

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈnɛt.əl / (BrE)
a plant with stinging hairs, especially of the genus Urtica, that can cause skin irritation when touched
stinging plant causing skin irritation

Examples

She accidentally brushed against a nettle and felt a sharp stinging sensation on her hand.
Many herbal teas use dried nettles as a key ingredient for their beneficial properties.
There are several types of nettle growing wild along the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stinging nettle - a common nettle species with stinging hairs (Urtica dioica)

Etymology

Old English *netel*, related to Dutch *netel* and German *Nessel*.

2. VERB

/ ˈnɛtəl / (AmE), / ˈnɛtəl / (BrE)
to cause annoyance or irritation; to disturb or provoke, especially by minor offenses
to annoy or irritate

Examples

Her critical remarks often nettle her colleagues during meetings.
I was nettled by his constant interruptions while I was speaking.
The delays at the airport began to nettle even the most patient travelers.

Synset Examples

Mosquitoes buzzing in my ear really bothers me
It irritates me that she never closes the door after she leaves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to irritate - to make someone annoyed or a part of the body sore or painful
  • Synonym: to annoy - to make someone slightly angry
  • Antonym: to soothe - to make someone calmer or less irritated

Etymology

From Old English netel, originally referring to the stinging plant; figurative sense based on the irritation caused by the nettle plant.

3. VERB

/ ˈnɛt̬.əl / (AmE), / ˈnɛt.əl / (BrE)
to sting or irritate someone as if with nettles, causing a sharp stinging pain or sensation
to sting or irritate sharply

Examples

The journalist's questions seemed to nettle the politician, making him visibly uncomfortable.
She was nettled by his rude remarks and could not hide her irritation.
His constant criticism can nettle even the most patient person.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to irritate - to make someone annoyed or slightly angry
  • Antonym: to soothe - to gently calm or relieve pain or discomfort

Etymology

From Old English netle (the plant nettle), used figuratively for stinging.
