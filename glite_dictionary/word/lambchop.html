lambchop

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæmˌtʃɑp / (AmE), / ˈlæmˌtʃɒp / (BrE)
A small, thick slice of meat cut from the rib or loin of a lamb, usually served grilled or fried.
slice of meat from a lamb's ribs or loin

Examples

He ordered a juicy lambchop with a side of mashed potatoes for dinner.
The aroma of grilled lambchops filled the kitchen on Sunday afternoon.
She prefers her lambchop cooked medium-rare to keep it tender and flavorful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porkchop - chop cut from a pig
  • Degree Higher: lamb - young sheep, or its meat
  • Degree Higher: muttonchop - chop cut from mature sheep

Etymology

From 'lamb' (young sheep) + 'chop' (a slice of meat).
