incomplete

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnkəmˈpliːt / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəmˈpliːt / (BrE)
not finished; not having all the necessary or expected parts or stages complete
not finished or lacking parts

Examples

The project remains incomplete due to a lack of funds.
She turned in an incomplete application, so it could not be processed.
Many puzzles are frustrating when even one piece is incomplete.

Synset Examples

his thesis is still incomplete
an uncompleted play

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unfinished - not finished or completed
  • Antonym: complete - having all the necessary parts; finished

Etymology

From Latin incompletus, from in- (not) + completus (filled, finished).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01008130-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.kəmˈplit / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kəmˈpliːt / (BrE)
not having all the necessary parts, elements, or steps; not finished or total
not finished or lacking some parts

Examples

The report was incomplete and missed several important details.
Because the puzzle was incomplete, we couldn't finish it.
The medical form was returned because it was incomplete.

Synset Examples

an incomplete account of his life
political consequences of incomplete military success
an incomplete forward pass

Related Terms

  • Antonym: complete - having all necessary parts; finished; whole

Etymology

From Latin incompletus, from in- (not) + completus (complete).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00526155-a
Synset POS: a
