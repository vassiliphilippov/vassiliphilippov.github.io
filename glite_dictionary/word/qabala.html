Qabala

1. NOUN

/ kəˈbɑːlə / (AmE), / kəˈbɑːlə / (BrE)
A system of esoteric or occult knowledge modeled on or resembling the Jewish Kabbalah, often kept secret within certain mystical or spiritual traditions.
esoteric system resembling Kabbalah

Examples

The order teaches a form of Qabala that blends ancient mysticism with modern psychology.
Many occultists study Qabala to gain insight into spiritual symbolism.
He wrote several books on Qabala and its influence on Western magic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kabbalah - Jewish mystical tradition involving esoteric interpretation of scriptures

Etymology

Variant spelling of Kabbalah, from Hebrew *qabbālāh* (tradition).
