Negro

1. NOUN

/ ˈniːɡroʊ / (AmE), / ˈniːɡrəʊ / (BrE)
(archaic and offensive) A person of Black African ancestry. This term is now considered outdated and offensive.
archaic and offensive term for a Black person
archaic offensive

Examples

The term Negro is now recognized as offensive and should be avoided in modern usage.
He read a novel from the 1920s where African Americans were often referred to as Negroes.
Historical census records in the United States categorized some people as Negro rather than Black.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Black - a person with dark skin, especially of African or Caribbean descent
  • Synonym: Negroid - an archaic anthropological term for people of African ancestry

Etymology

From Spanish and Portuguese 'negro' meaning 'black', from Latin 'niger'.
