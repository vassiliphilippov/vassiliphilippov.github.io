plea

1. NOUN

/ pliː / (AmE), / pliː / (BrE)
an earnest or humble request for help or support, especially from someone in authority
an earnest request for help

Examples

The refugees made a desperate plea for food and shelter.
Her plea for leniency touched the judge's heart.
The mayor issued a plea for calm after the protests erupted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appeal - an earnest or urgent request

Etymology

From Old French *plait* (lawsuit, plea), from Latin *placitum* (something agreed upon, decree).

2. NOUN

/ pliː / (AmE), / pliː / (BrE)
(Law) A formal response by a defendant to a criminal charge, stating guilt, innocence, or another answer addressing the facts of the case.
formal response by a defendant in court
legal

Examples

The defendant entered a plea of not guilty to all charges.
Her lawyer advised her to change her plea before the trial began.
The judge asked for the defendant's plea at the arraignment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: demurrer - an objection that an opponent's point is irrelevant or invalid, without answering the substance of the claim

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman and Old French *plait*, from Latin *placitum* (decree, decision).

3. NOUN

/ pliː / (AmE), / pliː / (BrE)
(Law) A formal statement by or on behalf of a defendant responding to a charge or claim in a court of law, stating whether they admit or deny the allegations and on what grounds.
formal legal response in court
legal

Examples

The defendant entered a plea of not guilty at the arraignment.
His lawyer filed a plea arguing that the case should be dismissed on technical grounds.
After hearing the plea, the judge scheduled a hearing for next month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defense - an argument or plea made in defense of oneself in court
  • Degree Higher: guilty plea - a formal admission of guilt in court
  • Degree Lower: not guilty plea - a formal statement of innocence in response to a charge

Etymology

From Anglo-French *plee* (law case, lawsuit), from Old French *plait*, from Latin *placitum* (decree, opinion).

Synset POS: n
