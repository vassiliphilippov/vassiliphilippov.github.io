gallfly

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡælˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡalˌflʌɪ / (BrE)
A small solitary wasp that induces the formation of galls on oaks and other plants in which its larvae develop.
small wasp that induces plant galls

Examples

The oak tree was covered in strange lumps caused by the larvae of the gallfly.
Scientists studied the life cycle of the gallfly to understand how it induces gall formation.
Several species of gallfly are important in forestry and horticulture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gall wasp - another term for gallfly; small wasp that causes plant galls

Etymology

From 'gall' (growth on plants) + 'fly' (insect); actually a wasp rather than a true fly.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡælˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡal.flaɪ / (BrE)
An insect resembling a fragile mosquito that lays eggs in plants, causing the formation of galls.
insect that causes galls on plants

Examples

The gallfly lays its eggs on oak leaves, causing the tissue to swell and form a gall.
Many gardeners are concerned about the damage a gallfly can cause to their plants.
Biologists studied how the presence of a gallfly affects plant health and biodiversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gall wasp - a related insect that also induces galls on plants

Etymology

From 'gall' (abnormal plant growth) + 'fly' (insect).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡælˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡɔːlˌflaɪ / (BrE)
an insect that lays its eggs in plants, causing swellings called galls where its larvae feed
an insect that causes galls in plants

Examples

The oak tree was infested with gallflies, which caused strange swellings on the leaves.
Scientists studied the life cycle of the gallfly to understand plant-insect interactions.
Some species of gallfly are known to target specific types of plants for egg-laying.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gall wasp - a type of gallfly, especially of the family Cynipidae, that induces plant galls

Etymology

From 'gall' (a growth on plants caused by insects) + 'fly'.
