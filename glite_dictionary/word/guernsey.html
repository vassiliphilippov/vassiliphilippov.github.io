Guernsey

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɜrnzi / (AmE), / ˈɡɜːnzi / (BrE)
a breed of dairy cattle, originally from the island of Guernsey, known for their high-quality milk production
breed of dairy cattle from Guernsey

Examples

The farmer recently added a Guernsey to his herd to improve milk production.
Their award-winning cheese is made exclusively from Guernsey milk.
A group of Guernseys grazed peacefully in the lush pasture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jersey - a breed of dairy cattle from the island of Jersey

Etymology

Named after the island of Guernsey in the English Channel, where the breed originated.

2. PROPN

/ ˈɡɜrnzi / (AmE), / ˈɡɜːnzi / (BrE)
an island in the English Channel to the northwest of Jersey, one of the Channel Islands and a British Crown dependency.
one of the Channel Islands in the English Channel

Examples

She spent her summer vacation on Guernsey, enjoying the island's picturesque scenery.
The history of Guernsey is deeply intertwined with both Britain and France.
Ferries operate regularly between Guernsey and the nearby island of Jersey.

Etymology

From Old Norse personal name 'Grani' + Old Norse 'ey' (island).

3. PROPN

/ ˈɡɜrnzi / (AmE), / ˈɡɜːnzi / (BrE)
an island in the English Channel, northwest of Jersey, known as a British Crown dependency
Channel Island; British Crown dependency

Examples

The beautiful beaches on Guernsey attract visitors from all over the world.
He spent his summer volunteering on the island of Guernsey.
Many traditions unique to Guernsey reflect its rich history in the Channel Islands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jersey - the largest of the Channel Islands, southeast of Guernsey

Etymology

Name probably of Norman French origin, possibly from Old Norse *Grani* (a personal name) + 'ey' (island).
