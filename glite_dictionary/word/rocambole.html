rocambole

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈkæmbəl / (AmE), / rəʊˈkæmbɒl / (BrE)
A European leek closely related to garlic, cultivated and used in cooking for its mild flavor.
a European leek used like garlic or leeks

Examples

I planted rocambole in my herb garden for its delicious, mild flavor.
The chef added finely chopped rocambole to the soup for extra aroma.
Unlike regular garlic, rocambole has a softer, subtler taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garlic - a strong-smelling, pungent-tasting bulb used as a flavoring in cooking

Etymology

From French 'rocambole', possibly from Spanish 'rocambol' or Catalan 'rocambol'.
