villager

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪlədʒər / (AmE), / ˈvɪlɪdʒə / (BrE)
a person who lives in or comes from a village, especially one who has spent most of their life there
person living in a village

Examples

The curious villager watched as tourists explored the narrow lanes.
Many villagers gather at the market square every morning to trade goods.
She was known as a helpful villager who always assisted her neighbors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: townsman - a person who lives in a town, especially as contrasted with a villager

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French *village* (village) + *-er* (denoting a person).
