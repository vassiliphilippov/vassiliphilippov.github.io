wail

1. VERB

/ weɪl / (AmE), / weɪl / (BrE)
to make a long, loud, high-pitched cry of pain, sorrow, or grief
emit a long, loud cry

Examples

The baby began to wail when her mother left the room.
He would wail in pain after stubbing his toe on the table.
Ambulance sirens wailed through the quiet night.

Synset Examples

wail in self-pity
howl with sorrow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: howl - to make a long, loud, mournful cry

Etymology

From Old Norse *væla* (to cry, lament).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01048804-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ weɪl / (AmE), / weɪl / (BrE)
a prolonged, loud cry expressing pain, grief, or sorrow
loud cry of grief or pain

Examples

The wail of the bereaved mother echoed through the silent corridors.
A distant wail rose above the howling wind during the storm.
Her wail was so heart-rending that it brought tears to those who heard it.

Synset Examples

their pitiful laments could be heard throughout the ward

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lament - an expression or cry of grief or sorrow
  • Synonym: sob - an act or sound of crying with convulsive catching of the breath

Etymology

From Old Norse 'væla' (to cry or lament).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07226610-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ weɪl / (AmE), / weɪl / (BrE)
to cry out in pain, grief, or anger, often with a sobbing or mournful sound; to cry weakly or softly
cry out in pain or grief

Examples

The child began to wail softly after falling off her bicycle.
She wailed in pain when she touched her injured arm.
Late at night, you could hear someone wailing quietly in the hallway.

Synset Examples

she wailed with pain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sob - to cry noisily, making loud, convulsive gasps
  • Synonym: to weep - to shed tears; to cry
  • Antonym: laugh - to make sounds expressing amusement or happiness

Etymology

From Old Norse *væla* (to lament).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00065796-v
Synset POS: v
