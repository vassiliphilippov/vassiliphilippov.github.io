Pterocarpus macrocarpus

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛroʊˈkɑːrpəs ˌmækroʊˈkɑːrpəs / (AmE), / ˌtɛrəˈkɑːpəs ˌmækrəʊˈkɑːpəs / (BrE)
A tree native to India and Burma, known for its valuable wood that resembles mahogany.
tree with mahogany-like wood, native to India and Burma

Examples

The dense wood of Pterocarpus macrocarpus is highly prized for furniture making.
Conservationists are concerned about the overharvesting of Pterocarpus macrocarpus in its native habitats.
The botanical garden features a mature specimen of Pterocarpus macrocarpus alongside other tropical trees.

Etymology

From Greek pteron (wing) and karpos (fruit), referencing the winged fruit of the genus; 'macrocarpus' means large-fruited in Greek.
