ostentatious

1. ADJ

/ ˌɑːstən'teɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˌɒstənˈteɪʃəs / (BrE)
designed to impress or attract notice, especially by being showy or pretentious
intended to impress or attract notice

Examples

She wore an ostentatious diamond necklace to the gala.
His ostentatious display of wealth made him unpopular with his neighbors.
The hotel lobby was decorated in an ostentatious manner, with gold accents everywhere.

Synset Examples

an ostentatious sable coat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: showy - displaying wealth or possessions in a loud way to attract attention
  • Antonym: modest - not seeking to attract attention or admiration; opposite of ostentatious

Etymology

From Latin 'ostentatio' (display), from 'ostendere' (to show).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɑː.stɛnˈteɪ.ʃəs / (AmE), / ˌɒs.tɛnˈteɪ.ʃəs / (BrE)
Characterized by a vulgar or pretentious display designed to attract notice or impress others.
showy or pretentious display

Examples

She wore an ostentatious necklace encrusted with huge diamonds to the party.
The hotel lobby was decorated with ostentatious gold fixtures and velvet drapes.
His ostentatious generosity was intended to impress rather than to help.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: showy - making an obvious display to attract attention
  • Antonym: modest - not attracting attention or showing off

Etymology

From Latin ostentatio (display, showing-off), from ostentare (to display).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02403542-s
Synset POS: s
