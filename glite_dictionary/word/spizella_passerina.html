Spizella passerina

1. NOUN

/ spaɪˈzɛlə pæsəˈraɪnə / (AmE), / spaɪˈzɛlə pæsəˈriːnə / (BrE)
the scientific name for the Chipping Sparrow, a small North American finch commonly found in urban and open areas
scientific name for the Chipping Sparrow

Examples

The Spizella passerina can often be seen hopping around suburban gardens in the spring.
Birdwatchers noted several Spizella passerina singing from the treetops early in the morning.
The delicate nest built by the Spizella passerina was hidden among the low branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chipping sparrow - a small North American sparrow with a rufous cap and a clear, trilling song

Etymology

From Latin, genus 'Spizella' (meaning 'little finch') and species 'passerina' (referring to a bunting or small sparrow).
