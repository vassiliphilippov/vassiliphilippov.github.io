Prunus dulcis amara

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs ˈdʌlsɪs əˈmɑːrə / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs ˈdʊlsɪs əˈmɑːrə / (BrE)
A variety of almond tree with white blossoms, producing poisonous bitter nuts, from which an oil is extracted for flavoring and medicinal purposes.
bitter almond tree variety

Examples

Prunus dulcis amara is cultivated in some regions for its oil, despite the toxicity of the raw nuts.
The essential oil derived from Prunus dulcis amara is commonly used as a flavoring agent in liqueurs.
Only processed products of Prunus dulcis amara are safe for human consumption due to the presence of amygdalin in the nuts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bitter almond - the nut produced by Prunus dulcis amara

Etymology

From Latin 'Prunus' (plum tree genus), 'dulcis' (sweet), and 'amara' (bitter).
