contestation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːn.təˈsteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.təˈsteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of disputing or arguing strongly about something; a heated debate or controversy
heated dispute or argument

Examples

The new policy has sparked fierce contestation among community members.
Academic circles are often marked by intense contestation over theories and findings.
There was a prolonged contestation between the two political parties after the election results.

Synset Examples

they were involved in a violent argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dispute - a disagreement or argument about something important
  • Synonym: argument - a verbal exchange expressing opposite views
  • Antonym: agreement - a situation where parties reach a consensus or harmony

Etymology

From French 'contestation', from Latin 'contestatio' (a calling to witness, challenge).
