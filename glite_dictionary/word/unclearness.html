unclearness

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌnˌklɪrnəs / (AmE), / ˈʌŋˌklɪənəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being difficult to understand or lacking clarity.
the state of not being clear

Examples

The unclearness of his explanation left everyone more confused than before.
Legal documents often suffer from unclearness, making them hard for non-experts to interpret.
There was some unclearness about the deadline, which led to missed submissions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to understand

Etymology

From un- (not) + clearness.
