to preform

1. VERB

/ priˈfɔrm / (AmE), / priˈfɔːm / (BrE)
to form or shape something in advance, or to determine its shape beforehand
to shape in advance

Examples

The factory will preform the metal sheets before final assembly.
Some musical instruments are preformed by machines for consistency.
They chose to preform the plastic parts to speed up production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shape - to give a particular form to something

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'pre-' (before) and 'formare' (to form).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01663976-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ priˈfɔrm / (AmE), / priːˈfɔːm / (BrE)
to shape or form something into an early or preliminary version that resembles the desired final shape
form into a preliminary shape

Examples

The parts are preformed before final assembly.
Glass is preformed into tubes before being blown into bottles.
Engineers often preform the sheets to ensure accurate fitting during installation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shape - to give a defined form to something

Etymology

From Latin 'prae-' (before) + 'formare' (to form).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01664146-v
Synset POS: v
