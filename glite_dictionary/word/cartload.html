cartload

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrtˌloʊd / (AmE), / ˈkɑːt.ləʊd / (BrE)
an amount that fills a cart, often used informally to refer to a large quantity of something
the quantity a cart can hold

Examples

We brought home a cartload of fresh vegetables from the market.
The farmer delivered another cartload of hay to the barn.
They sold a cartload of old books at the garage sale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: truckload - the amount that fills a truck
