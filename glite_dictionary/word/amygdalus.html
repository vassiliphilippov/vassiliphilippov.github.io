Amygdalus

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɪɡdələs / (AmE), / əˈmɪɡdələs / (BrE)
a former genus of trees including the peach and almond, now classified under the genus Prunus
former botanical genus for peach and almond trees

Examples

The genus Amygdalus once included both the almond and the peach before reclassification.
In older botanical texts, peaches are often referred to as Amygdalus persica.
Taxonomists have now merged Amygdalus into the broader genus Prunus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Prunus - the genus now encompassing peach, almond, and related trees

Etymology

From Latin *amygdalus* (almond tree), from Greek *amygdalos* (almond).
