Peronospora

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrəˈnɑspəɹə / (AmE), / ˌpɛrəˈnɒspərə / (BrE)
A genus of destructive downy mildew fungi that are plant pathogens, affecting crops and other vegetation.
genus of downy mildew fungi
medical

Examples

Peronospora species can devastate entire crops of spinach and lettuce if left untreated.
The farmer consulted an expert after noticing signs of Peronospora infection on his grapevines.
Controlling Peronospora outbreaks requires careful monitoring and the use of resistant plant varieties.

Etymology

From Greek 'peronē' (pin) and 'spora' (seed, spore).

2. NOUN

/ pɛrəˈnɒspərə / (AmE), / pɛrəˈnɒspərə / (BrE)
A genus of destructive downy mildews that infect a wide variety of plants, causing significant agricultural damage.
genus of downy mildews (plant pathogens)
medical

Examples

The grape crop was devastated by Peronospora last season, causing severe losses for local farmers.
Researchers are developing new strategies to control Peronospora outbreaks in vegetable greenhouses.
A key symptom of Peronospora infection is the presence of white downy growth on the underside of leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Plasmopara - a related genus of downy mildews
  • Degree Lower: Ustilago - genus of smut fungi, also plant pathogens
  • Degree Higher: Phytophthora - genus of more aggressive plant-pathogenic water molds

Etymology

From Greek *peronē* (pin, brooch) and *spora* (seed, sowing); used as a genus name in botanical Latin.
