missing

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
not present, lost, or not existing where expected or needed.
not present or lost

Examples

Several files are missing from the folder and need to be recovered.
The missing child was finally found safe and sound after a long search.
There was a missing screw in the chair, making it unstable.

Synset Examples

the thumb is absent
her appetite was lacking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absent - not present in a place, at an occasion, or as part of something
  • Antonym: present - existing or being in a particular place

Etymology

Present participle of 'miss', from Old English 'missan'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a thing or person) not able to be found; absent or lost.
not able to be found; absent

Examples

The police are searching for a missing child in the area.
I can't find my keys; they seem to be missing.
She realized her wallet was missing after the meeting.

Synset Examples

missing in action
a missing person

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lost - unable to be found; gone
  • Antonym: found - located or discovered after being lost
