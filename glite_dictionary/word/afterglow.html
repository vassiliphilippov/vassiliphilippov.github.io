afterglow

1. NOUN

/ ˈæf.tɚˌɡloʊ / (AmE), / ˈɑːf.təˌɡləʊ / (BrE)
a faint light or glow sometimes visible in the sky after the sun has set
faint glow after sunset

Examples

The sunset ended, but a beautiful afterglow lingered on the horizon.
We watched the pink and orange afterglow as night slowly set in.
Photographers gathered to capture the magical afterglow that followed the stormy evening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twilight - the soft light in the sky when the sun is below the horizon

Etymology

Formed from 'after-' (following) and 'glow' (shine softly).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæf.tɚˌɡloʊ / (AmE), / ˈɑːf.təˌɡləʊ / (BrE)
a feeling of pleasure or satisfaction that remains after a positive or enjoyable experience
lingering pleasure after an event

Examples

He basked in the afterglow of his recent promotion.
The afterglow of her wedding day lasted for weeks.
There was a warm afterglow in the room after everyone shared their good news.

Synset Examples

he basked in the afterglow of his victory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: euphoria - a feeling or state of intense excitement and happiness
  • Antonym: letdown - a feeling of disappointment following excitement or success

Etymology

From 'after-' (following) + 'glow' (light), originally describing the lingering light after sunset.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
