Lorenzo de' Medici

1. PROPN

/ loʊˈrɛnzoʊ deɪ ˈmɛdɪtʃi / (AmE), / ləˈrɛnzəʊ deɪ ˈmɛdɪtʃi / (BrE)
(1449–1492) Italian statesman and scholar who was a major patron of the arts and humanism during the Renaissance in Florence, supporting figures such as Michelangelo, Leonardo da Vinci, and Botticelli.
Renaissance Florentine statesman and patron of the arts

Examples

Lorenzo de' Medici played a pivotal role in fostering the Italian Renaissance through his patronage of artists and scholars.
Many of the most famous Renaissance works were made possible by the support of Lorenzo de' Medici.
As head of Florence, Lorenzo de' Medici balanced political diplomacy with a passion for culture.

Etymology

From Italian, his name means 'Lorenzo of the Medici family'.

2. PROPN

/ loʊˈrɛntsoʊ deɪ ˈmɛdɪtʃi / (AmE), / lɒˈrɛntsəʊ deɪ ˈmɛdɪtʃi / (BrE)
(1449–1492) Italian statesman and scholar, known for his influential rule over Florence and generous patronage of Renaissance artists and scholars.
Italian Renaissance statesman and patron of the arts

Examples

During the Italian Renaissance, Lorenzo de' Medici played a central role as a statesman and patron of the arts.
Michelangelo found early support and mentorship from Lorenzo de' Medici in Florence.
The political influence of Lorenzo de' Medici helped shape the future of Renaissance Europe.
