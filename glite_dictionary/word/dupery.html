dupery

1. NOUN

/ ˈduːpəri / (AmE), / ˈdjuːpəri / (BrE)
the act or practice of deceiving or tricking someone; deliberate deception or trickery intended to gain an advantage
deception; trickery

Examples

The scheme was an elaborate dupery designed to cheat investors out of their money.
They quickly saw through his dupery and refused to cooperate.
History is full of tales of dupery and cunning plots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceit - the act of deceiving someone
  • Synonym: fraud - wrongful or criminal deception intended for financial or personal gain
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and free from deceit

Etymology

From French *duperie* (deception), from *duper* (to deceive).
