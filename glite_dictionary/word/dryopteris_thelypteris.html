Dryopteris thelypteris

1. NOUN

/ draɪˈɑptərɪs ˌθɛlɪpˈtɛrɪs / (AmE), / draɪˈɒptərɪs ˌθɛlɪpˈtɛrɪs / (BrE)
A fern species with deeply divided fronds, commonly found in wet habitats in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
fern species found in wet places

Examples

Dryopteris thelypteris is often found in marshes and other wetland environments across North America.
Botanists studied the adaptability of Dryopteris thelypteris in restored wetland areas.
You can identify Dryopteris thelypteris by its finely divided fronds and preference for moist soil.

Etymology

From Greek 'dryas' (oak), 'pteris' (fern), and 'thelypteris' (female fern).
