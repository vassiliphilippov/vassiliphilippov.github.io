karyoplasm

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæriəˌplæzəm / (AmE), / ˈkariəʊˌplaz(ə)m / (BrE)
the protoplasm found inside the nucleus of a cell, distinct from the cytoplasm
protoplasm within a cell nucleus
medical

Examples

The karyoplasm contains the nucleolus and chromatin material inside the cell nucleus.
Researchers isolated the karyoplasm to study the nuclear enzymes.
Changes in the karyoplasm can indicate various cellular diseases.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cytoplasm - the protoplasm outside the nucleus of a cell

Etymology

From Greek 'karyon' (nut, kernel, nucleus) + 'plasma' (molded, formed).
