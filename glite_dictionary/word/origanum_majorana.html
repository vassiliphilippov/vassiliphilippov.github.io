Origanum majorana

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɪɡənəm məˈdʒɔrəˌnə / (AmE), / ɒˈrɪɡənəm məˈdʒɒrənə / (BrE)
An aromatic herb native to the Mediterranean and Turkey, commonly known as sweet marjoram, used in cooking and herbal medicine.
aromatic herb (sweet marjoram)

Examples

Origanum majorana is often used in Mediterranean cuisine to add a mild, sweet flavor to dishes.
The gardener planted Origanum majorana next to the thyme and basil in her herb plot.
Researchers study Origanum majorana for its potential health benefits and essential oil properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marjoram - the common name for Origanum majorana, an aromatic herb

Etymology

From Latin *origanum majorana*, the scientific name for sweet marjoram.
