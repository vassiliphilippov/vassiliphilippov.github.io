antimicrobial

1. ADJ

/ ˌæntɪmaɪˈkroʊbiəl / (AmE), / ˌæntɪmaɪˈkrəʊbiəl / (BrE)
Capable of destroying or inhibiting the growth of disease-causing microorganisms.
stopping or killing microorganisms
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an antimicrobial ointment to prevent infection.
Many soaps advertise their antimicrobial properties to attract health-conscious consumers.
Proper use of antimicrobial agents is crucial to prevent drug resistance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antibiotic - a substance that kills or inhibits the growth of bacteria
  • Synonym: antiviral - a drug that suppresses the growth of viruses

Etymology

From Greek anti- (against) + mikros (small) + bios (life) + Latin -alis (pertaining to).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ti.maɪˈkroʊ.bi.əl / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.maɪˈkrəʊ.bi.əl / (BrE)
A substance or agent that kills or inhibits the growth of microorganisms, especially those that cause disease.
agent that destroys microorganisms
medical

Examples

Proper use of antimicrobial agents can help prevent the spread of infections in hospitals.
This spray contains a powerful antimicrobial that kills bacteria on contact.
Scientists are researching new natural antimicrobials to combat drug-resistant pathogens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antibiotic - A drug used to treat bacterial infections
  • Synonym: disinfectant - A chemical agent used to destroy or inhibit microorganisms on surfaces

Etymology

From Greek 'anti-' (against) + 'mikros' (small) + 'bios' (life).

Synset POS: n
