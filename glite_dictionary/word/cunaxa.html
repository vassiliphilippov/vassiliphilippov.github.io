Cunaxa

1. PROPN

/ kuːˈnæksə / (AmE), / kjuːˈnæksə / (BrE)
A battle fought in 401 BC near Babylon, where Artaxerxes II defeated his younger brother Cyrus the Younger, who attempted to seize the Persian throne.
battle in 401 BC between Artaxerxes II and Cyrus the Younger

Examples

The Battle of Cunaxa was a decisive conflict in Persian history.
Xenophon provided a detailed account of Cunaxa in his work Anabasis.
Historians often discuss the strategic mistakes made at Cunaxa.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Κύναξα (Kúnaxa), the name of the battle site in Babylonia.
