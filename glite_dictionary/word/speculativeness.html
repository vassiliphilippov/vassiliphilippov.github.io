speculativeness

1. NOUN

/ spəˈkjuleɪtɪvnəs / (AmE), / spɛkˈjuːlətɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being based on conjecture or supposition rather than fact or investigation
quality of being speculative or based on conjecture

Examples

The speculativeness of his theory made it difficult for others to accept.
Many critics objected to the speculativeness of the proposal, citing a lack of concrete data.
Her argument was marked by a surprising degree of speculativeness despite her usually rigorous methods.

Synset Examples

her work is highly contentious because of its speculativeness and lack of supporting evidence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speculation - the forming of a theory or conjecture without firm evidence
  • Antonym: certainty - the quality of being reliably true; known for sure

Etymology

From 'speculative' (from Latin 'speculativus', meaning 'contemplative') + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ spɛk.jə.lə.tɪv.nəs / (AmE), / spɛk.jʊˈleɪ.tɪv.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being speculative, especially with regard to financial risk or uncertainty of outcomes
quality of being speculative or risky (finance)

Examples

The speculativeness of certain investments makes them attractive to risk-tolerant individuals.
He assessed the speculativeness of the new venture before deciding to invest.
Regulations were introduced to reduce the speculativeness in the stock market.

Synset Examples

he rejected stocks that didn't pay dividends because of their speculativeness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: riskiness - the quality of involving risk or danger; lack of safety
  • Antonym: conservatism - the quality of being conservative, avoiding unnecessary risks (especially in finance)

Etymology

From speculate + -ive + -ness; ultimately from Latin speculari (to observe)
