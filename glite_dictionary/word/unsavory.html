unsavory

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈseɪvəri / (AmE), / ʌnˈseɪvəri / (BrE)
morally offensive or regarded with disapproval; unpleasant or distasteful because of questionable morality
morally offensive; distasteful

Examples

The politician was linked to several unsavory business deals that damaged his reputation.
She tried to avoid unsavory characters on her way home at night.
The club closed after allegations of unsavory activities surfaced in the press.

Synset Examples

an unsavory reputation
an unsavory scandal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disreputable - not considered to be respectable in character or appearance
  • Antonym: respectable - regarded by society to be good, proper, or correct

Etymology

From un- (not) + savory (from Old French savoure, from Latin sapor, meaning taste).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈseɪvəri / (AmE), / ʌnˈseɪvəri / (BrE)
Having an unpleasant smell or taste.
unpleasant in smell or taste

Examples

The leftovers had become unsavory and had to be thrown away.
The unsavory odor from the garbage drifted through the open window.
Cheese that has sat out too long often turns unsavory both in taste and smell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unpalatable - not pleasant to taste
  • Synonym: tasteless - lacking flavor or taste
  • Antonym: savory - pleasing in taste or smell

Etymology

From un- (not) + savory (pleasing to the taste), ultimately from Old French savoure (taste), from Latin sapor.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01721424-s
Synset POS: s
