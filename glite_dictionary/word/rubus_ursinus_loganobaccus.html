Rubus ursinus loganobaccus

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːbəs ɜːrˈsaɪnəs ˌloʊɡəˈnobəkəs / (AmE), / ˈruːbəs ɜːˈsaɪnəs ˌlɒɡənəʊˈbakəs / (BrE)
A type of red-fruited bramble native to the region from Oregon to Baja California, also known as the loganberry.
a red-fruited bramble native to western North America

Examples

The Rubus ursinus loganobaccus produces large, tangy berries ideal for jam making.
Many home gardeners in California cultivate Rubus ursinus loganobaccus for its flavorful fruit.
Rubus ursinus loganobaccus thrives in the mild coastal climates of Oregon and northern California.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loganberry - another name for Rubus ursinus loganobaccus

Etymology

From Latin: Rubus (bramble), ursinus (bear-like), and loganobaccus (referring to James H. Logan, the originator, and bacca meaning berry).
