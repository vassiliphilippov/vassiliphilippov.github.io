Jericho

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒɛrɪˌkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛrɪkəʊ / (BrE)
An ancient city in the West Bank near the northern end of the Dead Sea, noted in the Old Testament as the first city taken by the Israelites under Joshua.
ancient city near the Dead Sea, biblical reference

Examples

The walls of Jericho are a famous symbol in biblical history.
Archaeologists have uncovered ancient ruins in Jericho dating back thousands of years.
Many tourists visit Jericho to learn about its role in the story of Joshua.

Etymology

From Hebrew Yərīḥō, possibly meaning 'fragrant' or 'city of the moon'.
