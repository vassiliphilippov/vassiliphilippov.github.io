Gerbera jamesonii

1. NOUN

/ dʒərˈbɛrə ˌdʒæməˈsoʊni.aɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡɜːbərə ˌdʒæmɪˈsəʊnɪ.aɪ / (BrE)
A widely cultivated South African perennial plant with flower heads featuring orange to flame-colored rays, commonly known as the Barberton daisy.
South African flowering plant with orange rays

Examples

The Gerbera jamesonii adds a bright splash of color to gardens in the summer.
Florists often use Gerbera jamesonii in bouquets for its vibrant, eye-catching blooms.
Gerbera jamesonii requires plenty of sunlight and well-drained soil to thrive.

Etymology

Genus name from German naturalist Traugott Gerber; species named after Robert Jameson, British botanist.
