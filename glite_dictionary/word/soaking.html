soaking

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of making something or someone completely wet
the act of making completely wet

Examples

After a long soaking in the rain, his clothes were completely drenched.
She gave the dirty dishes a thorough soaking before scrubbing them clean.
The garden needed a good soaking after weeks without rain.

Synset Examples

he gave it a good drenching

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drenching - the act of wetting thoroughly

Etymology

From the verb 'soak,' probably of Old English origin.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊ.kɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ.kɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of becoming thoroughly wet or saturated by being immersed in water or another liquid; the act of soaking.
the act or process of becoming saturated by immersion

Examples

After a long day of hiking, a soaking in the hot tub was exactly what she needed.
The beans require an overnight soaking before they can be cooked properly.
The old shirt came clean after a good soaking in soapy water.

Synset Examples

a good soak put life back in the wagon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soak - the act of immersing something in liquid

Etymology

4. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊkɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of immersing something in liquid, especially to clean or soften it.
the act of soaking, especially for washing

Examples

The shirt needed a good soaking to remove the stubborn stains.
A long soaking in warm water will help loosen the paint.
He gave his feet a soaking after the long hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immersion - the act of putting something completely in liquid

Etymology

From the verb 'soak' + -ing.
