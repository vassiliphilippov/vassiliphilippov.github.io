unconnected

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.kəˈnɛk.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kəˈnɛk.tɪd / (BrE)
not having a relationship by birth or family; not related.
not related by family

Examples

Although we share the same last name, we are completely unconnected by birth.
The guests at the reunion were mostly unconnected to each other by family ties.
He was surprised to learn that his new coworker was unconnected to the famous family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unrelated - not connected by relationship or family

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'un-' (not) + 'connected'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.kəˈnɛk.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kəˈnɛk.tɪd / (BrE)
lacking continuity, logical sequence, or orderly arrangement; not connected or related.
lacking continuity or connection

Examples

The novel's plot felt unconnected, with scenes jumping from one place to another without explanation.
Her presentation was a series of unconnected ideas that puzzled the audience.
My unconnected thoughts made it hard to concentrate on the exam.

Synset Examples

a confused set of instructions
a confused dream about the end of the world
disconnected fragments of a story
scattered thoughts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disconnected - not joined or linked; lacking coherence or continuity
  • Antonym: coherent - logical and consistent; forming a unified whole

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'un-' (not) + 'connected'.

3. ADJ

/ ˌʌnkəˈnɛktɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnkəˈnɛktɪd / (BrE)
not joined, linked, or related to something else; having no connection.
not joined or related

Examples

The two incidents are completely unconnected and have nothing to do with each other.
He felt isolated and unconnected from the rest of the group.
Many small villages remain unconnected to the main electricity grid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disconnected - not connected or having lost connection
  • Antonym: connected - joined or linked together; related

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + connected, past participle of connect.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00569857-a
Synset POS: a
