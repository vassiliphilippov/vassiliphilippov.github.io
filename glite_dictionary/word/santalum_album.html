Santalum album

1. NOUN

/ sænˈteɪləm ˈæl.bəm / (AmE), / sanˈtɑːləm ˈæl.bəm / (BrE)
A tree native to Indonesia and Malaysia, known for its fragrant yellowish heartwood, often used to make chests and with insect-repelling properties.
sandalwood tree of Indonesia and Malaysia

Examples

The wood of Santalum album is highly valued for its fragrance and durability.
Traditional carvings in India often use Santalum album because of its fine grain.
Many perfumes contain oil extracted from Santalum album trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandalwood - fragrant wood from the Santalum tree

Etymology

From Latin: Santalum (genus name), album meaning 'white.'
