celeriac

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɪriˌæk / (AmE), / səˈlɪəriˌak / (BrE)
a round, knobby root vegetable with a celery-like flavor, grown for its edible, aromatic root
edible root vegetable related to celery

Examples

You can mash celeriac with potatoes for a flavorful side dish.
The soup featured cubes of celeriac simmered with carrots and leeks.
She picked up a large celeriac at the farmer's market for tonight's dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celery - a leafy green vegetable with long, crisp stalks and aromatic leaves

Etymology

From French céleri (celery) + -ac (from Latin -acus, used for plant names).

2. NOUN

/ səˈlɛriˌæk / (AmE), / sɛˈlɪəriˌak / (BrE)
A variety of celery cultivated for its large, round, edible root, which is aromatic and used as a vegetable.
edible root vegetable

Examples

The soup was made with fresh celeriac from the farmer's market.
Roasted celeriac makes a delicious and healthy side dish.
She peeled and chopped the celeriac before boiling it with potatoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celery - a plant grown for its crunchy edible stalks

Etymology

From French céleri (celery) + -ac, perhaps via German Sellerie and Latin selinon (celery).
