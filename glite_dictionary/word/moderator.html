moderator

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.dəˌreɪ.tər / (AmE), / ˈmɒd.əˌreɪ.tər / (BrE)
a person who presides over a discussion, debate, or forum to ensure the proceedings are orderly and fair
person overseeing a discussion or debate

Examples

The moderator introduced the topic and made sure everyone had a chance to speak.
During the online forum, the moderator removed several inappropriate comments.
A skilled moderator can prevent heated debates from turning into arguments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chairperson - person who leads a meeting or discussion
  • Antonym: participant - person taking part in a discussion, but not presiding

Etymology

From Latin 'moderator', meaning 'one who moderates or restrains', from 'moderari' (to moderate).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːdəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / ˈmɒdəreɪtə / (BrE)
A substance used in a nuclear reactor to slow down neutrons, increasing the likelihood of nuclear fission.
substance that slows neutrons in a reactor

Examples

Heavy water is often used as a moderator in certain types of nuclear reactors.
The moderator absorbs some energy from the neutrons produced during fission.
Finding an effective moderator material is vital for the efficiency of a nuclear power plant.

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːdəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / ˈmɒdəˌreɪtə / (BrE)
a person who mediates disputes and helps to prevent conflict or violence between parties
person who mediates disputes

Examples

The meeting was heated until the moderator stepped in and calmed everyone down.
As an experienced moderator, she managed to keep both sides talking constructively.
A skilled moderator can defuse tense situations before they escalate into violence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mediator - a person who acts as an intermediary to resolve conflict
  • Synonym: arbitrator - a person officially appointed to settle a dispute
  • Antonym: agitator - a person who urges others to protest or rebel

Etymology

From Latin 'moderator' (one who controls or guides), from 'moderari' (to moderate, regulate).

/ ˈmɑːdəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / ˈmɒdəˌreɪtə / (BrE)

officer presiding in Presbyterian assembly

Examples

The moderator called the assembly to order for the opening address.
She was elected moderator of the synod for the next three years.
As moderator, he was responsible for ensuring proper procedure during debates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chairperson - person who presides over a meeting or assembly

From Latin moderator (one who presides, ruler), from moderari (to regulate).
