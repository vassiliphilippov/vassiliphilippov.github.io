Trachelospermum jasminoides

1. NOUN

/ trəˌkiːloʊˈspɜrməm ˌjæzmɪˈnɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / trəˌkiːlɒˈspɜːməm ˌjæzmɪˈnɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
an evergreen woody vine native to China, with glossy dark green leaves and highly fragrant white star-shaped flowers, also known as star jasmine
evergreen vine with fragrant white flowers

Examples

The garden’s fence was beautifully covered in Trachelospermum jasminoides during the summer months.
Many gardeners choose Trachelospermum jasminoides for its sweet-scented blooms and lush foliage.
Our patio trellis is adorned with Trachelospermum jasminoides, adding a wonderful fragrance to the evenings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: star jasmine - common name for Trachelospermum jasminoides

Etymology

From Greek *trachelos* (neck) + *sperma* (seed) and Latinized species epithet referring to resemblance to jasmine.
