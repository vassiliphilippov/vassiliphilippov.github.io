to barbarize

1. VERB

/ ˈbɑːrbəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈbɑːbəraɪz / (BrE)
To make crude, savage, or uncivilized in behavior or speech.
make crude or savage

Examples

Some argue that constant violence in media can barbarize viewers, desensitizing them to real suffering.
The corrupt regime barbarized its people through years of oppression and brutality.
Centuries of war threatened to barbarize even the most advanced societies.

Synset Examples

his years in prison have barbarized the young man

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to civilize - to make more cultured or refined
  • Synonym: to brutalize - to make brutal or cruel

Etymology

From barbarian + -ize; related to Latin 'barbarus' (foreign, uncivilized).

2. VERB

/ ˈbɑːrbəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈbɑːbəraɪz / (BrE)
To become crude, savage, or uncivilized in behavior or language.
become uncivilized or crude

Examples

After years of conflict, the community began to barbarize as respect for laws and customs eroded.
Some critics argued that the film would barbarize popular taste with its crude humor and violence.
If we abandon education and civil discourse, our conversations may slowly barbarize into shouting and insults.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to civilize - to bring out of a savage or uncultured state
  • Synonym: to brutalize - to make brutal or less humane

Etymology

From Medieval Latin barbarizare (to make barbarous), from Latin barbarus (barbarian).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
