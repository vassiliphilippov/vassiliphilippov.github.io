goaded

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡoʊdɪd / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊdɪd / (BrE)
compelled or driven to take action, especially by persistent urging or outside pressure
compelled by urging or pressure

Examples

She felt goaded into defending herself by their constant criticism.
The protesters were goaded into action by the government's inaction.
He became goaded by the relentless taunts of his classmates.

Synset Examples

mobs goaded by blind hatred

Related Terms

  • Synonym: driven - motivated or compelled to act by a force or need
  • Synonym: provoked - stimulated to act, usually by incitement or annoyance
  • Antonym: restrained - prevented from acting by restriction or force

Etymology

From verb 'goad', from Old English 'gad' (goad, point).
