Aster novae-angliae

1. NOUN

/ ˈæstər ˌnoʊ.vi ˈæŋgli.i / (AmE), / ˈæstə ˌnəʊ.vi ˈæŋgli.aɪ / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to eastern North America, known for its showy purplish flowers and as a parent of the Michaelmas daisy.
a North American perennial with showy purple flowers

Examples

The garden was brightened in the fall by a large clump of Aster novae-angliae.
Many gardeners choose Aster novae-angliae for its vibrant late-season blooms.
Pollinators are attracted to the purple flowers of Aster novae-angliae throughout autumn.

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'New England aster', reflecting the region where it is native.
