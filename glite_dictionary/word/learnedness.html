learnedness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɜrnɪdnɪs / (AmE), / ˈlɜːnɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of having profound scholarly knowledge; great learning or erudition
quality of having deep scholarly knowledge

Examples

Her learnedness impressed the committee during the interview.
The professor's learnedness was evident in every lecture she gave.
Despite his learnedness, he remained humble and approachable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erudition - extensive knowledge acquired chiefly from books; learning

Etymology

Formed from 'learned' + '-ness', after similar constructions indicating state or quality.
