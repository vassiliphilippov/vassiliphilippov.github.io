neoexpressionism

1. NOUN

/ ˌniːoʊ.ɪkˈsprɛʃ.əˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊ.ɪkˈsprɛʃ.ə.nɪz.əm / (BrE)
a modern art movement of the 1980s in Europe and the United States, characterized by vivid colors, emotionally intense, crudely drawn or raw paintings, and a return to figurative representation
modern art movement with vivid, expressive works

Examples

The artist's bold use of color and rough brushwork placed her firmly within the neoexpressionism movement.
During the 1980s, neoexpressionism gained popularity in both Europe and the United States.
Critics often debate whether neoexpressionism has lasting significance compared to earlier art movements.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: expressionism - 20th-century art movement focusing on representing emotional experience
  • Degree Higher: abstract expressionism - mid-20th-century art movement focused on abstract forms and expressive brushwork

Etymology

From neo- (new) + expressionism (an earlier modern art movement), indicating a revival or new form of expressionism.
