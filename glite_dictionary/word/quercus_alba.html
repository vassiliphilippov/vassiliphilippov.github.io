Quercus alba

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜːrkəs ˈælbə / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs ˈalba / (BrE)
A large, slow-growing deciduous tree of eastern North America, known as the white oak, with stout spreading branches and leaves usually having seven rounded lobes; valued for its strong, durable hardwood.
species of white oak tree

Examples

The sturdy wood of the Quercus alba is highly prized for furniture and flooring.
Botanists identified the ancient tree as a Quercus alba based on its leaves and acorns.
Many parks in the eastern United States feature mature specimens of Quercus alba as prominent shade trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white oak - common name for Quercus alba, a species of oak tree with light-colored wood

Etymology

From Latin: 'quercus' meaning 'oak' and 'alba' meaning 'white'.
