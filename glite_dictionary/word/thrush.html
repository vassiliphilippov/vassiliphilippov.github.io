thrush

1. NOUN

/ θrʌʃ / (AmE), / θrʌʃ / (BrE)
A small to medium-sized songbird, typically having brownish upper feathers and a spotted breast, found in many parts of the world.
songbird with spotted breast

Examples

A wood thrush was singing its distinctive song in the early morning.
The brown thrush builds its nest in low trees or shrubs.
We spotted several thrushes pecking for worms after the rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackbird - a common European thrush with all-black plumage
  • Synonym: robin - a type of thrush with a red breast

Etymology

From Old English 'þrysce', referring to the songbird.

2. NOUN

/ θrʌʃ / (AmE), / θrʌʃ / (BrE)
(old-fashioned) A female singer of popular music, especially light entertainment.
female popular singer (old-fashioned)
archaic

Examples

She became known as a talented thrush in jazz clubs during the 1940s.
The band was searching for a new thrush to front their act.
Vintage magazines often featured profiles of the latest thrush in the music scene.

Related Terms

Etymology

3. NOUN

/ θrʌʃ / (AmE), / θrʌʃ / (BrE)
A fungal infection of the mouth, especially common in infants or people with weakened immune systems, causing whitish patches and discomfort.
oral fungal infection
medical

Examples

The pediatrician diagnosed the baby with thrush after examining the white patches inside her mouth.
Debilitated patients are especially prone to developing thrush due to their compromised immune systems.
Antifungal medication is often prescribed to treat thrush in infants and adults.
