retrospect

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛtrəˌspɛkt / (AmE), / ˈrɛtrəʊˌspɛkt / (BrE)
the act of thinking about or reviewing past events or situations
review or contemplation of the past

Examples

In retrospect, she realized how significant that meeting was to her career.
The decision seemed unwise in retrospect, but it felt right at the time.
Looking back in retrospect, we can see how the plan could have been improved.

Synset Examples

in retrospect

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recollection - the action or faculty of remembering something
  • Antonym: anticipation - the act of looking forward; expectation of future events

Etymology

From Latin 'retrospectus' (a looking back), from 'retro-' (backward) + 'specere' (to look).

2. VERB

/ ˈrɛtrəˌspɛkt / (AmE), / ˈrɛtrəʊˌspɛkt / (BrE)
to look back on or think about the past; to review or consider a past period or event
look back on the past

Examples

In times of crisis, she would often retrospect on her childhood for comfort.
After the celebration, the team paused to retrospect on their journey to success.
He likes to retrospect about how decisions made years ago shaped his life.

Synset Examples

she reviewed her achievements with pride

Related Terms

  • Synonym: review - to look at or examine again, often for evaluation or remembrance
  • Synonym: to reminisce - to recall and talk about past experiences with pleasure

Etymology

From Latin 'retrospectare' (to look back), from 'retro-' (backwards) + 'specere' (to look).
