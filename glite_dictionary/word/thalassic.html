thalassic

1. ADJ

/ θəˈlæsɪk / (AmE), / θəˈlasɪk / (BrE)
relating to the seas, especially smaller or inland seas
relating to seas or inland seas

Examples

The geology course included a section on thalassic environments and their unique ecosystems.
Researchers studied thalassic sediments from the ancient Mediterranean for clues about climate change.
The region’s economy was shaped by its proximity to thalassic bodies of water rather than the open ocean.

Synset Examples

deposits of sediment in gulfs and seas rather than in the ocean proper are known as thalassic deposits

Etymology

From Greek thalassa (sea).
