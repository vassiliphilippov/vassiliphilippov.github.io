Ectopistes

1. PROPN

/ ɛkˈtɑpɪstiz / (AmE), / ɛkˈtɒpɪstiːz / (BrE)
A genus of pigeons in the family Columbidae, including the extinct passenger pigeon.
genus of pigeons (includes passenger pigeon)

Examples

The extinct passenger pigeon, Ectopistes migratorius, once numbered in the billions across North America.
Researchers have studied fossils belonging to the genus Ectopistes to understand its evolutionary history.
Unlike other pigeons, Ectopistes was known for its migratory habits and enormous flocks.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Columbidae - the family of pigeons and doves
  • Synonym: passenger pigeon - an extinct species of pigeon from this genus

Etymology

From Greek 'ektos' (outside) + 'pistos' (faithful), used as a genus name in taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.toʊˈpaɪstiːz / (AmE), / ˌɛk.təʊˈpɪstiːz / (BrE)
A genus of pigeons in the family Columbidae, best known for including the extinct passenger pigeon.
genus of pigeons (Columbidae)

Examples

The extinct passenger pigeon belonged to the genus Ectopistes.
Scientists have been studying fossils from the Ectopistes genus to trace their evolutionary history.
There is only one recognized species in Ectopistes, the passenger pigeon.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Columbidae - the family of birds that includes pigeons and doves
  • Synonym: passenger pigeon - a species, Ectopistes migratorius, formerly of this genus

Etymology

From Greek 'ektos' (outside) + 'pistos' (faithful, reliable); scientific nomenclature from taxonomy.
