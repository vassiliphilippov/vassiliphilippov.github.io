Iraqi dinar

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈrɑːki dɪˈnɑr / (AmE), / ɪˈrɑːki dɪˈnɑː / (BrE)
the basic unit of currency in Iraq, equal to 1,000 fils
currency unit of Iraq

Examples

The price of a loaf of bread in Baghdad is 250 Iraqi dinar.
He exchanged his dollars for Iraqi dinar at the bank before traveling.
The Iraqi dinar has experienced significant changes in value over the years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dinar - the currency unit used in several countries

Etymology

From Arabic 'dīnār', itself from Latin 'denarius'.
