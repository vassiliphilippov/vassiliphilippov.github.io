burbling

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɜrbəlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɜːblɪŋ / (BrE)
(of speech or tone) expressed in a lively, enthusiastic, and unrestrained manner, often with a bubbling quality.
enthusiastically lively (of speech or tone)

Examples

The children's burbling voices could be heard from across the playground.
He launched into a burbling description of his recent adventures.
Her burbling laughter filled the quiet room with joy.

Synset Examples

a novel told in burbly panting tones

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enthusiastic - showing intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval
  • Antonym: monotonous - dull, tedious, and repetitious; lacking in variety and interest

Etymology

From the verb 'burble', imitating the sound of bubbling or gurgling water.
