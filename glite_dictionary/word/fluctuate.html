to fluctuate

1. VERB

/ ˈflʌktʃuˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈflʌktjʊeɪt / (BrE)
to change continually in level, strength, or value; to rise and fall unpredictably or irregularly
change frequently; rise and fall

Examples

Oil prices continued to fluctuate throughout the year, making it difficult to predict costs.
Her mood tends to fluctuate depending on the weather.
Interest rates may fluctuate as the economy recovers.

Synset Examples

The stock market fluctuates

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to vary - change or differ; to become different in some way
  • Antonym: to stabilize - make or become unlikely to change, fail, or decline

Etymology

From Latin 'fluctuare' (to surge, to fluctuate), from 'fluctus' (a wave).

2. VERB

/ ˈflʌktʃueɪt / (AmE), / ˈflʌktʃueɪt / (BrE)
to cause something to rise and fall or change continually in a wavelike pattern
cause to change constantly or wavelike

Examples

The central bank can fluctuate interest rates to control inflation.
Wind patterns fluctuate the water's surface, creating gentle waves on the lake.
Their decisions may fluctuate oil prices throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to vary - to change or alter in amount or level, often repeatedly
  • Antonym: to stabilize - to make or become unlikely to change, fail, or decline

Etymology

From Latin fluctuare (to move like a wave), from fluctus (a wave).
