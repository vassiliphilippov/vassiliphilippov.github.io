dangerousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪndʒərəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈdeɪndʒərəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being dangerous or likely to cause harm
quality of being dangerous

Examples

The dangerousness of the abandoned building concerned the neighbors.
Scientists study the dangerousness of the chemicals before they are approved for use.
His parole was denied because of the dangerousness he was believed to pose to society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: danger - the possibility of harm or injury
  • Antonym: safety - the condition of being protected from danger

Etymology

From dangerous + -ness.
