royal

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɔɪ.əl / (AmE), / ˈrɔɪ.əl / (BrE)
of, relating to, or befitting a king, queen, or monarch; belonging to the rank or status of a monarch.
of the rank of a monarch

Examples

The couple attended a royal banquet held at the palace.
Many believe she has royal blood in her veins.
The royal decree changed the course of the nation's history.

Synset Examples

of royal ancestry
princes of the blood royal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regal - of, resembling, or fit for a monarch

Etymology

From Old French 'reial', from Latin 'regalis' (regal, kingly), from 'rex' (king).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɔɪ.əl / (AmE), / ˈrɔɪ.əl / (BrE)
A male red deer (stag) with antlers having twelve or more branches.
stag with twelve or more-branched antlers

Examples

The gamekeeper spotted a magnificent royal grazing near the edge of the forest.
During hunting season, encountering a royal is considered a rare privilege.
In Scotland, legends speak of the elusive royal with its impressive antlers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: imperial - stag with antlers of 14 or more branches
  • Synonym: stag - an adult male deer, especially a red deer
