shoot

1. VERB

/ ʃut / (AmE), / ʃuːt / (BrE)
to kill (a person or animal) by firing a bullet, arrow, or other missile at them
kill by firing a missile

Examples

The poacher tried to shoot the endangered elephant, but the rangers stopped him just in time.
During the robbery, the criminals threatened to shoot anyone who resisted.
Hunters are not allowed to shoot deer out of season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to gun down - to shoot and kill someone, often suddenly or deliberately
  • Antonym: spare - to refrain from killing or injuring

Etymology

Old English *sceotan* (to shoot, hurl, cast), from Proto-Germanic *skeutaną*.
