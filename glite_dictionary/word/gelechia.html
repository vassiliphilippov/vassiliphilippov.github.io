Gelechia

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈliːkiə / (AmE), / dʒəˈliːkɪə / (BrE)
A genus of small moths in the family Gelechiidae, including species such as the pink bollworm, known for affecting crops.
genus of small moths (Gelechiidae)

Examples

The pink bollworm, a destructive pest of cotton, belongs to the genus Gelechia.
Many Gelechia species have larvae that are serious agricultural pests.
Researchers studied the distribution of Gelechia in several regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gelechiidae - the family of moths to which Gelechia belongs

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, created as a genus name in taxonomic classification.

2. NOUN

/ ɡəˈliːkiə / (AmE), / ɡɛˈlɛkiə / (BrE)
A genus of moths in the family Gelechiidae, known as the type genus, which includes species such as the pink bollworm.
a genus of moths including pink bollworms

Examples

The Gelechia genus includes several pest species that damage crops such as cotton.
A well-known member of the Gelechia genus is the pink bollworm.
Entomologists study the life cycle of Gelechia moths to develop pest control methods.

Etymology

From New Latin Gelechia, used in taxonomic classification.
