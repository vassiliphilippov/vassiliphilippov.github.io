Orient

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔriənt / (AmE), / ˈɔːriənt / (BrE)
(dated or literary) The countries of Asia, especially East Asia and Southeast Asia; the East.
the countries of Asia (dated/literary)
archaic

Examples

He traveled to the Orient in search of rare spices and textiles.
Many artworks in Europe were inspired by the mystique of the Orient.
In nineteenth-century literature, the Orient often symbolized exoticism and mystery.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Occident - the countries of the West

Etymology

From Latin 'orient-', past participle of 'oriri' (to rise), originally referring to the east where the sun rises.
