troglodyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɑːɡləˌdaɪt / (AmE), / ˈtrɒɡ.lə.daɪt / (BrE)
a person who lives in a cave, especially as a member of a prehistoric or primitive people; sometimes used figuratively to describe someone regarded as old-fashioned or reclusive
person who lives in a cave

Examples

The archaeologist explained that the ancient village was once inhabited by troglodytes who carved homes into the cliffs.
He called his boss a troglodyte for refusing to use modern technology at work.
In some remote regions, troglodytes still dwell in cave houses as their ancestors did centuries ago.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hermit - a person living in solitude, often for religious reasons
  • Synonym: recluse - a person who lives a solitary life and tends to avoid others

Etymology

From Greek *trōglodutēs*, from *trōglē* (hole, cave) + *dyein* (to enter).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrɑːɡləˌdaɪt / (AmE), / ˈtrɒɡləˌdaɪt / (BrE)
a person who lives in seclusion, especially one who deliberately avoids society
person living in seclusion

Examples

Rumors spread that a troglodyte lived in the woods, never speaking to anyone.
The novelist became a troglodyte after his retirement, rarely leaving his mountain cabin.
Critics called her a troglodyte for shunning social events and communicating only by letter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hermit - a person living in solitude as a religious discipline
  • Synonym: recluse - a person who lives a solitary life and tends to avoid other people
  • Antonym: socialite - a person who is well-known in fashionable society and frequently attends social events

Etymology

From Greek 'trōglodutēs' (cave-dweller), from 'trōglē' (hole, cave) + 'dyein' (to go in).
