goniometer

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡoʊniˈɑːmɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˌɡɒniˈɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument for measuring the angular direction of incoming radio signals, used especially in radio direction-finding
instrument measuring radio signal direction

Examples

The technician used a goniometer to accurately determine the direction of the radio transmission.
Modern search and rescue teams rely on a goniometer to locate emergency beacons quickly.
By rotating the goniometer, the engineer was able to pinpoint the source of interference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: direction finder - device that determines the direction of a radio signal

Etymology

From Greek *gōnia* (angle) + *-meter* (measure).
