browser

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraʊzɚ / (AmE), / ˈbraʊzə / (BrE)
a person who looks around casually without intending to buy or find anything specific, often in a shop or library
person browsing casually (esp. in a shop)

Examples

The store manager greeted every browser with a friendly smile, never pressuring anyone to buy.
Although I walked through the gallery as a browser, I ended up falling in love with a painting.
Librarians encourage even a casual browser to explore new sections of the library.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shopper - a person who is shopping or makes purchases
  • Synonym: window shopper - someone who looks at goods in shop windows without intending to buy

Etymology

From 'browse' (to look through), influenced by the verb's sense of feeding or looking idly, first used in this sense in the 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbraʊzər / (AmE), / ˈbraʊzə / (BrE)
a computer program that enables users to access and view websites on the internet
web viewing computer program

Examples

Most people use a browser like Chrome or Firefox to access the internet.
Make sure your browser is updated to the latest version for better security.
This website may not display correctly if your browser is out of date.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: web browser - a software application for accessing information on the World Wide Web

Etymology

From the verb 'browse' (to look through), combined with the '-er' agent suffix.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06583477-n
Synset POS: n
