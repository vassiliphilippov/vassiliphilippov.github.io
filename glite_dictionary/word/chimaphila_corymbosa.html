Chimaphila corymbosa

1. NOUN

/ kaɪˌmæfɪlə kəˈrɪmbəsə / (AmE), / kɪˌmæfɪlə kəˈrɪmbəsə / (BrE)
A Eurasian herb with white or pinkish flowers arranged in a terminal cluster (corymb).
a Eurasian flowering herb

Examples

The rare Chimaphila corymbosa was found blooming along the forest's edge in early summer.
Botanists noted the white flowers of the Chimaphila corymbosa during their field survey.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Chimaphila corymbosa for its attractive pinkish corymbs.

Etymology

From Greek *cheima* (winter) + *philos* (loving), referring to the genus, and Latin *corymbosus* (clustered in corymbs).
