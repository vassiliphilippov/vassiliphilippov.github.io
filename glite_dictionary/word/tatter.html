tatter

1. NOUN

/ ˈtætər / (AmE), / ˈtætə / (BrE)
a small torn piece of cloth, paper, or similar material
small torn piece of cloth, paper, etc.

Examples

She wiped her eyes with a tatter she found in her pocket.
The flag hung in a tatter from the old pole.
He patched his shirt with a tatter of blue cotton.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rag - a piece of old, torn cloth

Etymology

From Middle English tater, from Old Norse tǫturr (rag, scrap).
