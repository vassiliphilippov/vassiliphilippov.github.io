Leuciscus cephalus

1. NOUN

/ luˈsɪskəs ˈsɛfələs / (AmE), / luˈsɪskəs ˈkɛfaləs / (BrE)
A European freshwater game fish with a thick, spindle-shaped body, commonly known as the European chub.
European freshwater game fish (European chub)

Examples

Anglers often try to catch Leuciscus cephalus in the rivers of central Europe.
The Leuciscus cephalus prefers clear, fast-flowing streams with gravel beds.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of Leuciscus cephalus to understand its spawning behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chub - Common name for Leuciscus cephalus, a European freshwater fish

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining the genus name Leuciscus and species name cephalus, from Greek 'kephalē' meaning 'head'.
