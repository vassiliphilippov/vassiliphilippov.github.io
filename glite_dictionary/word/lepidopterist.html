lepidopterist

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛpɪˈdɑptərɪst / (AmE), / ˌlɛpɪˈdɒptərɪst / (BrE)
a person who studies or collects butterflies and moths
person studying butterflies and moths

Examples

The lepidopterist spent years cataloguing rare species of butterflies in the rainforest.
At the museum, a lepidopterist explained how moths are attracted to light.
Several famous lepidopterists have contributed to the understanding of butterfly migration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entomologist - a person who studies insects

Etymology

From Greek 'lepis, lepid-', meaning 'scale', and 'pteron', meaning 'wing', referring to the scaled wings of butterflies and moths.
