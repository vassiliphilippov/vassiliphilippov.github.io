Bayesian

1. ADJ

/ ˈbeɪziən / (AmE), / ˈbeɪzɪən / (BrE)
relating to statistical methods or inference based on Bayes' theorem, which involves updating probabilities as more evidence becomes available.
relating to Bayes' theorem or Bayesian statistics

Examples

The Bayesian approach allows prior knowledge to be incorporated into the statistical analysis.
Researchers used a Bayesian model to estimate the probability of various outcomes.
His expertise lies in Bayesian statistics, particularly in data science applications.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: frequentist - relating to a contrasting school of statistical inference

Etymology

From the name of Thomas Bayes (1701–1761), English mathematician, combined with the adjective-forming suffix '-ian'.
