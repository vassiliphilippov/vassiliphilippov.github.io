remembrance

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈmɛm.brəns / (AmE), / rɪˈmɛm.brəns / (BrE)
an act or token of honoring or recognizing meritorious service or achievement, especially in a ceremonial context
act or token honoring service or achievement

Examples

On Veterans Day, a special remembrance was held to honor the soldiers' bravery.
The medal was awarded as a remembrance of her outstanding contribution to the community.
The ceremony included a moment of remembrance for those who lost their lives in service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commemoration - the act of remembering and showing respect for someone or something

Etymology

From Old French 'remembrance', from Latin 'rememorari' (to remember).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈmɛm.brəns / (AmE), / rɪˈmɛm.brəns / (BrE)
the ability to remember or recall past events or experiences
capacity to recall past events

Examples

The old photograph brought a wave of remembrance about his childhood.
She lit a candle in remembrance of her late grandmother.
His excellent remembrance of names impressed everyone at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: memory - the faculty by which the mind stores and remembers information
  • Antonym: forgetfulness - the inability to recall or remember things

Etymology

From Old French 'remembrance', from Latin 'rememorari' (to remember again).
