courtesy

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɝː.t̬ə.si / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.tə.si / (BrE)
polite behavior that shows respect for other people
polite behavior; respectful manner

Examples

She accepted the invitation with great courtesy and warmth.
Basic courtesy is expected in all professional settings.
He spoke to the elderly woman with courtesy and respect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: politeness - the quality of being polite
  • Antonym: rudeness - lack of respect or courtesy; impoliteness

Etymology

From Old French 'courtoisie', from 'courtois' (courteous), ultimately from Latin 'cohors' (court).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɝːtəsi / (AmE), / ˈkɜːtəsi / (BrE)
A polite, respectful, or considerate remark or expression intended to show good manners or respect.
polite or respectful remark

Examples

His kind courtesy made everyone feel welcome at the meeting.
She thanked the host with a simple courtesy before leaving the party.
It is a common courtesy to greet your neighbor with a smile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: politeness - the quality of being polite
  • Antonym: rudeness - lack of courtesy; impoliteness

Etymology

From Old French 'courtoisie', from 'courtois' (courteous), ultimately from Medieval Latin 'curtensis' (of a court).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɝː.t̬ə.si / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.tə.si / (BrE)
a polite action or gesture that shows respect or consideration for others
polite or considerate act or gesture

Examples

It was a simple courtesy to hold the door open for the person behind me.
The manager offered us coffee as a courtesy when we arrived for the meeting.
Sending a thank-you note is considered a common courtesy after receiving a gift.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: politeness - behavior that is respectful and considerate of other people
  • Antonym: rudeness - lack of manners or respect; discourteous behavior or acts

Etymology

From Old French 'courtoisie', based on 'courtois' (courteous).
