cruciate

1. ADJ

/ ˈkruːʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkruːʃiːət / (BrE)
shaped like a cross; having a cross-shaped structure.
shaped like a cross

Examples

The knee contains the cruciate ligaments, which cross each other inside the joint.
Some plants have cruciate leaves, with veins that form a cross.
An X-ray revealed a cruciate fracture pattern in the bone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cross-shaped - shaped in the form of a cross

Etymology

From Latin cruciatus (cross-shaped), from crux (cross).
