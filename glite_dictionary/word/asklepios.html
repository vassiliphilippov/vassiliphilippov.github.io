Asklepios

1. PROPN

/ æˈskliːpiˌɑs / (AmE), / æsˈkliːpiˌɒs / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the god of medicine and healing, son of Apollo, known to the Romans as Aesculapius.
Greek god of medicine and healing

Examples

The ancient Greeks prayed to Asklepios for cures to their illnesses.
Temples dedicated to Asklepios were often built near healing springs.
According to myth, Asklepios was skilled in the art of medicine, surpassing all mortals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aesculapius - Roman name of Asklepios, god of medicine and healing
  • Degree Lower: Hygeia - daughter of Asklepios, goddess of health and cleanliness
  • Degree Higher: Panacea - daughter of Asklepios, goddess of universal remedy

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἀσκληπιός (Asklēpiós).
