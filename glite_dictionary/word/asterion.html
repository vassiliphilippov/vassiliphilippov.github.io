asterion

1. NOUN

/ əˈstɪriˌɑn / (AmE), / əˈstɪəriən / (BrE)
the point on the human skull where the lambdoid, occipitomastoid, and parietomastoid sutures meet
craniometric point on the skull
medical

Examples

During the surgery, the surgeon identified the asterion as a key landmark for orientation.
The location of the asterion can vary slightly between individuals.
Medical students learn to palpate the asterion as part of their anatomy training.

Etymology

From Greek astērion, diminutive of astēr (star), named for its star-like suture meeting.
