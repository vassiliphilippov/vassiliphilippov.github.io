Brassica oleracea botrytis

1. NOUN

/ brəˌsɪkə oʊˌlɛɹiˈeɪʃə bɑˈtraɪtɪs / (AmE), / brəˌsɪkə əʊˌlɛːrɪˈeɪʃə bəˈtrɪtɪs / (BrE)
A cultivated plant with a large, dense, edible head of white flower buds, commonly known as cauliflower.
botanical name for cauliflower

Examples

The leaves of Brassica oleracea botrytis help protect the head from sunlight and pests.
Botanists classify cauliflower as Brassica oleracea botrytis within the cabbage family.
Many varieties of Brassica oleracea botrytis are grown for their tender, white florets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cauliflower - a vegetable with a white, compact head of undeveloped flowers

Etymology

From Latin: Brassica (cabbage) + oleracea (vegetable) + botrytis (bunched like grapes).
