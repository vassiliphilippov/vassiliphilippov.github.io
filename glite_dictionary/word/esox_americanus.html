Esox americanus

1. NOUN

/ ˈiː.sɑks əˌmɛr.ɪˈkeɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈiː.sɒks əˌmɛr.ɪˈkeɪ.nəs / (BrE)
a small, spirited species of pickerel found in the Atlantic coastal states of North America
a small pickerel species of North America

Examples

The Esox americanus is highly prized by local anglers for its spirited fight.
Several specimens of Esox americanus were collected from the river last summer.
Unlike its larger relatives, the Esox americanus rarely exceeds a foot in length.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pickerel - a small species of predatory freshwater fish related to the pike
  • Degree Higher: northern pike - a larger freshwater fish related to pickerel

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Latin 'Esox' (a genus of pike) and 'americanus' meaning 'American'.
