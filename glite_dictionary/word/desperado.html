desperado

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛspəˈrɑdoʊ / (AmE), / ˌdɛspəˈrɑːdəʊ / (BrE)
a bold, reckless outlaw, especially one of the American frontier
a daring outlaw

Examples

The notorious desperado was finally captured after a week-long manhunt in the desert.
Legends tell of a desperado who robbed trains across the frontier.
He styled himself as a modern desperado, but in reality, he seldom broke the law.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outlaw - a person who has broken the law and is sought by the authorities

Etymology

From Spanish 'desperado', from 'desesperar' (to despair).
