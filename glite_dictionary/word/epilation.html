epilation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the removal of hair from the surface of the skin, often by mechanical or chemical means
removal of hair from skin
medical

Examples

Many people choose epilation as a method for long-term hair removal.
After undergoing epilation, her skin felt smooth and hair-free.
Certain medications can cause epilation as a side effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depilation - removal of hair above the surface of the skin

Etymology

From Latin 'epilatio', from 'e-' (out) + 'pilus' (hair).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛpəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the removal of hair from the body, especially by pulling it out by the roots
removal of body hair

Examples

Professional salons often offer epilation services such as waxing and threading.
After undergoing laser epilation, she noticed a significant reduction in hair growth.
Certain animals require epilation of their skin before processing in the leather industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depilation - removal of hair, especially by cutting or shaving

Etymology

From Latin 'epilare', from 'e-' (out) + 'pilus' (hair).
