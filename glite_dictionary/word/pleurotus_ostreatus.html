Pleurotus ostreatus

1. NOUN

/ plurˈoʊtəs ɒˈstriaɪtəs / (AmE), / pluːˈrəʊtəs ɒsˈtriːətəs / (BrE)
A species of edible mushroom, commonly called the oyster mushroom, characterized by a soft greyish cap and usually growing in clusters on dead wood.
a species of edible oyster mushroom

Examples

Pleurotus ostreatus is often used in vegetarian dishes for its meaty texture and mild flavor.
The chef sautéed fresh Pleurotus ostreatus with garlic and herbs as a side dish.
We found clusters of Pleurotus ostreatus growing on a fallen log during our hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oyster mushroom - Edible mushroom species with oyster-shaped cap

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Pleurotus' from Greek 'pleura' (side) and 'otus' (ear), and 'ostreatus' from Latin 'ostrea' (oyster), referring to the mushroom's shape.
