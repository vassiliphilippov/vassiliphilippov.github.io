candidate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkændəˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkændɪdət / (BrE)
a person who is running for election to a public office, especially in politics
person running for public office

Examples

The candidate gave a passionate speech about healthcare reform during the debate.
Voters will choose between three candidates in the upcoming election.
Each candidate must submit campaign finance reports by the end of the month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nominee - person formally suggested for an office or honor
  • Antonym: incumbent - currently holding office rather than seeking election

Etymology

From Latin 'candidatus' (clothed in white), because Roman office-seekers wore white togas.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkændəˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkændɪdət / (BrE)
a person who is being considered for a position, office, honor, or prize
person considered for a position or honor

Examples

She was a strong candidate for the position of team leader.
After careful consideration, the committee chose three candidates for the award.
Each candidate must submit a resume and cover letter before the deadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: applicant - a person who applies for something, especially a job
  • Synonym: nominee - a person who has been nominated for an office or award
  • Antonym: incumbent - the current holder of an office or position

Etymology

From Latin candidatus (clothed in white), from candidus (white), as candidates in Rome wore white togas.
