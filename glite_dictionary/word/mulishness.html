mulishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjuːlɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈmjuːlɪʃnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being stubborn or unyielding, especially when unreasonable or difficult to manage
quality of being stubborn or obstinate

Examples

Her mulishness made it impossible to reach any kind of compromise.
Despite their pleas, his mulishness kept him from admitting he was wrong.
We were all frustrated by the mulishness with which she refused every suggestion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stubbornness - the quality of being stubborn or difficult to persuade
  • Antonym: flexibility - the quality of being willing to change or adapt

Etymology

From 'mulish' (like a mule in obstinacy) + -ness.
