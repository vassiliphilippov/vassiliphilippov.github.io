officially

1. ADV

/ əˈfɪʃəli / (AmE), / əˈfɪʃəli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to an office or authority, or as recognized by an official body or by law.
formally recognized or authorized

Examples

Officially, he is the head of the department, but others handle daily operations.
The country was officially recognized as independent in 1964.
She is officially responsible for managing the project budget.

Synset Examples

officially, he is in charge
officially responsible

Related Terms

  • Synonym: formally - in accordance with convention or etiquette

Etymology

From 'official' + '-ly'; 'official' from Latin 'officialis' (attendant to a magistrate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00115217-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ əˈfɪʃəli / (AmE), / əˈfɪʃəli / (BrE)
in a way that is authorized or approved by an authority, especially a government or other official body
with official authorization

Examples

The treaty was officially signed by all participating countries.
He didn’t officially announce his retirement until last week.
The product hasn’t been officially released in stores yet.

Synset Examples

the club will be formally recognized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: formally - in a way that follows official rules or conventions
  • Antonym: unofficially - without official authorization or approval

Etymology

From 'official' + '-ly', with 'official' from Latin 'officialis' (attendant to a magistrate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00188003-r
Synset POS: r
