valetudinary

1. ADJ

/ ˌvæləˈtuːdnˌɛri / (AmE), / ˌvælɪˈtjuːdɪnəri / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of a person who is excessively concerned about their health or is in poor health.
relating to a valetudinarian
archaic

Examples

She adopted a valetudinary attitude, constantly worrying about even the slightest ailments.
His valetudinary concerns became a recurring theme in family discussions.
Though young, he lived a valetudinary life, following strict diets and health regimens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valetudinarian - a person who is unduly anxious about their health

Etymology

From Latin 'valetudinarius' (sickly, infirm), from 'valetudo' (health).
