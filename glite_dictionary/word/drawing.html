drawing

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɔɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdrɔːɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of extracting or taking liquid, electricity, or another resource from a source
act of extracting a resource

Examples

The drawing of electricity from the main grid is carefully monitored.
After the drawing of blood, the nurse labeled the vial for testing.
Frequent drawing of groundwater has lowered the water table in the area.

Synset Examples

the drawing of water from the well

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extraction - the act of removing something, especially by effort
  • Antonym: injection - the act of forcing a substance into something, often the opposite action

Etymology

From Old English *dragan* (to draw, drag), via draw + -ing.
