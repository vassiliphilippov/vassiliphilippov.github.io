enamored

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈnæmɚd / (AmE), / ɪˈnæməd / (BrE)
filled with love, admiration, or fascination, often to an excessive or foolish degree
filled with love or admiration

Examples

She became enamored of the city during her summer abroad.
He was so enamored with her that he ignored all advice from his friends.
The audience seemed enamored by the actor’s charismatic performance.

Synset Examples

he was infatuated with her
Narcissus was a beautiful Greek youth who became enamored of his own reflection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infatuated - possessed by an intense but short-lived passion for someone or something

Etymology

From Old French enamerer, from Latin in- (in) + amor (love).
