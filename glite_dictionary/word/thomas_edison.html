Thomas Edison

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɑməs ˈɛdɪsən / (AmE), / ˈtɒməs ˈɛdɪsən / (BrE)
(1847–1931) United States inventor known for pioneering inventions such as the phonograph, the incandescent light bulb, and the motion picture camera.
American inventor (phonograph, light bulb, kinetoscope)

Examples

Thomas Edison is often credited with inventing the first practical light bulb.
The phonograph was developed by Thomas Edison in the late 19th century.
Many people consider Thomas Edison one of the greatest inventors in history.
