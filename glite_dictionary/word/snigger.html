snigger

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnɪɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈsnɪɡə / (BrE)
a quiet or disrespectful laugh, especially one expressing scorn or amusement at someone
a quiet, disrespectful laugh

Examples

Her rude comment drew a loud snigger from the back of the classroom.
He tried to hide his snigger when the teacher stumbled on the words.
A snigger escaped from the group as the speaker made a mistake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snicker - a suppressed or disrespectful laugh; chiefly American English

Etymology

Probably imitative in origin, similar to 'snicker'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07142768-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈsnɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈsnɪɡə / (BrE)
to laugh quietly, especially in a sly or disrespectful way
laugh quietly, often slyly

Examples

The boys sniggered as their teacher mispronounced the word.
She tried not to snigger during the serious meeting.
I could hear someone sniggering at the back of the classroom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snicker - to laugh quietly or suppressedly, chiefly in American English

Etymology

Of imitative origin, similar to 'snicker', possibly from dialectal words imitating a suppressed laugh.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00030003-v
Synset POS: v
