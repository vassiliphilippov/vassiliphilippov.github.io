ptosis

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
drooping of the upper eyelid caused by paralysis or weakness of the muscles responsible for raising it
drooping of the upper eyelid
medical

Examples

The patient developed ptosis in her left eye following the surgery.
Early signs of nerve damage can include mild ptosis and difficulty moving the eye.
Treatment options for ptosis depend on the underlying cause and severity.

Etymology

From Greek ptōsis (a falling), from piptein (to fall).
