jaggy

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒæɡi / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɡi / (BrE)
having a sharply uneven or rough surface or outline, often with projecting points or notches
having a sharply uneven surface or edge

Examples

The mountain's jaggy peaks were outlined against the evening sky.
He ran his hand over the jaggy surface of the old stone wall.
Her drawing showed the jaggy coastline in great detail.

Synset Examples

the jagged outline of the crags
scraggy cliffs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jagged - having a rough or sharply uneven edge or surface
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface

Etymology

From 'jag', meaning a sharp projection or point, + '-y', forming adjectives.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʒæɡi / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɡi / (BrE)
having a rough, uneven, or irregularly notched edge or surface, as if cut or gnawed
having a rough or notched edge

Examples

The top of the old fence was dangerously jaggy and needed to be replaced.
She wore a scarf with a soft texture to avoid the jaggy edges of the wool sweater.
From the peak, the mountain's jaggy silhouette stood out against the morning sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jagged - having rough, sharp points protruding

Etymology

From 'jag', probably of Scandinavian origin, meaning a sharp projection or cut.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02254647-s
Synset POS: s
