hemostat

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːməˌstæt / (AmE), / ˈhiːməstæt / (BrE)
a surgical instrument used to stop bleeding by clamping blood vessels during operations
surgical instrument for stopping bleeding
medical

Examples

The surgeon asked for a hemostat to control the bleeding vessel.
After clamping the artery with a hemostat, the bleeding stopped immediately.
Nurses routinely sterilize every hemostat after surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forceps - a surgical instrument for grasping or holding objects

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' (blood) and English 'stat' (to stop).
