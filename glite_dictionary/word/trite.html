trite

1. ADJ

/ traɪt / (AmE), / traɪt / (BrE)
(of a remark, idea, or phrase) lacking originality or freshness; dull on account of overuse.
lacking originality; overused

Examples

The plot of the movie was so trite that most critics found it boring.
He avoided using trite expressions in his speech to keep the audience engaged.
Her jokes, though once funny, have now become rather trite through constant repetition.

Synset Examples

his remarks were trite and commonplace
hackneyed phrases
a stock answer
repeating threadbare jokes
parroting some timeworn axiom
the trite metaphor ‘hard as nails’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hackneyed - lacking significance through having been overused
  • Antonym: original - fresh and not derived from something else; new

Etymology

From Latin 'tritus' (worn), past participle of 'terere' (to rub).
