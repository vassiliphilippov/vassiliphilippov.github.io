cadger

1. NOUN

/ ˈkædʒər / (AmE), / ˈkadʒə / (BrE)
a person who habitually seeks to obtain things, especially food or money, without paying or giving anything in return; someone who mooches
person who habitually begs or mooches

Examples

Whenever there's a party, Tom turns into a real cadger, always expecting others to pay for his drinks.
His friends grew tired of him being such a cadger every time they went out for lunch.
The neighborhood cadger soon became well known for showing up whenever free food was offered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mooch - to ask for or obtain something without paying or returning the favor
  • Synonym: sponger - a person who lives off others by continually taking advantage of their generosity

Etymology

From the verb 'cadge' (to beg), of uncertain origin, possibly from Middle English 'cadge' (to carry, hawk wares).
