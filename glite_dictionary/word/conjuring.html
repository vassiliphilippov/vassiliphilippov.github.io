conjuring

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌndʒɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkʌndʒərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of calling up a spirit or devil by magical or supernatural means
calling up spirits or devils by magic

Examples

The old mansion was once the site of conjuring rituals intended to summon spirits.
Villagers believed that conjuring evil beings could have grave consequences.
The story describes a night of conjuring in which a devil appeared in the firelight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summoning - the act of calling or bringing to presence, especially through supernatural powers

Etymology

From Latin 'coniurare' (to swear together, conspire).
