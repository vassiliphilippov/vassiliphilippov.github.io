florid

1. ADJ

/ ˈflɔrəd / (AmE), / ˈflɒrɪd / (BrE)
Elaborately or excessively decorated, ornate, or showy, especially in style or language.
elaborately ornate or decorated

Examples

The author's florid prose made the novel both captivating and exhausting to read.
Their living room was decorated in a florid style, with intricate patterns everywhere.
The architect's florid designs reflected a love for excessive ornamentation.

Synset Examples

flamboyant handwriting
the senator's florid speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ornate - Elaborately decorated or adorned
  • Antonym: plain - Not decorated or elaborate; simple or basic

Etymology

From Latin 'floridus' (flowery), from 'flos' (flower).

2. ADJ

/ ˈflɔr.ɪd / (AmE), / ˈflɒr.ɪd / (BrE)
having a reddish or rosy complexion; inclined to a healthy, reddish color often associated with outdoor life
having a reddish or rosy complexion

Examples

The old sailor's florid face suggested a lifetime spent outdoors.
Her cheeks grew florid after a day in the cold wind.
He had a florid complexion that seemed to radiate good health.

Synset Examples

a ruddy complexion
Santa's rubicund cheeks
a fresh and sanguine complexion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruddy - having a healthy, red complexion
  • Antonym: pale - lacking color; having little or no color in the face

Etymology

From Latin 'floridus' (flowery, blooming), from 'flos' (flower).
