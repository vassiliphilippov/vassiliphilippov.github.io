Afghan

1. ADJ

/ ˈæfˌɡæn / (AmE), / ˈæfɡæn / (BrE)
Relating to Afghanistan or its people.
of or relating to Afghanistan or its people

Examples

She bought an Afghan rug while traveling in Kabul.
Many Afghan traditions are passed down through generations.
The Afghan climate can be harsh, with hot summers and cold winters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Afghanistan - country in South-Central Asia

Etymology

From Persian *Afġān*, referring to the people of Afghanistan.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæfˌɡæn / (AmE), / ˈæfɡæn / (BrE)
the Iranian language spoken in Afghanistan and Pakistan, commonly referred to as Pashto or Dari, and used as the official language of Afghanistan.
the official language of Afghanistan

Examples

Many people in Kabul speak Afghan at home and in public offices.
He is fluent in both Urdu and Afghan.
The constitution declares Afghan as the official national language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pashto - the primary Eastern Iranian language spoken in Afghanistan and northwest Pakistan
  • Synonym: Dari - the variety of Persian spoken in Afghanistan

Etymology

From Persian Afghān, referring to the people and language of Afghanistan.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06987792-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈæfˌɡæn / (AmE), / ˈæf.ɡæn / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Afghanistan.
person from Afghanistan

Examples

The Afghan welcomed us into his home with great hospitality.
Thousands of Afghans have settled abroad in recent decades.
She is proud of her Afghan heritage and traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Afghani - the official currency of Afghanistan

Etymology

From Persian افغان (Afġān), referring to the Pashtun people or those from Afghanistan.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09708831-n
Synset POS: n
