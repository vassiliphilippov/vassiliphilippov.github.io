epithelioma

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpəˌθiliˈoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˌθiːlɪˈəʊmə / (BrE)
A malignant tumor arising from epithelial tissue.
malignant tumor of epithelial tissue
medical

Examples

The biopsy confirmed that the growth was an epithelioma requiring immediate treatment.
After surgery, the doctor explained that the epithelioma had not spread to surrounding tissues.
Early detection of an epithelioma can significantly improve the chances of successful recovery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carcinoma - a cancer arising in epithelial tissue
  • Antonym: sarcoma - a malignant tumor arising from connective or other non-epithelial tissue

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'epithelium' (surface tissue) and '-oma' (tumor).
