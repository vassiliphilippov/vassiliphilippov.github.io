Pterocarya

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrəˈkɛəriə / (AmE), / ˌtɛrəˈkɑːrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous trees in the walnut family, native to southeastern Europe and Asia, known as wingnuts for their winged fruit.
genus of wingnut trees

Examples

Scientists discovered a new Pterocarya specimen in the river valley.
The Pterocarya is prized for its attractive winged seeds.
Botanists classify wingnut trees under the genus Pterocarya.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wingnut - a common name for trees of the genus Pterocarya

Etymology

From Greek pteron (wing) + karyon (nut).

2. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrəˈkɛriə / (AmE), / ˌtɛrəˈkɑːrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous trees in the walnut family, native to Asia, commonly known as wingnuts due to their winged fruit.
a genus of Asian wingnut trees

Examples

Several species in the Pterocarya genus are valued as ornamental trees in parks and gardens.
The fruits of Pterocarya trees are distinctive because of their papery wings.
Botanists are studying Pterocarya to understand its relationship with other members of the walnut family.

Etymology

From Greek pteron (wing) + karyon (nut).
