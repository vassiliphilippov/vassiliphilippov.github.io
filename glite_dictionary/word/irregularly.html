irregularly

1. ADV

/ ɪˈrɛɡjələrli / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛɡjʊləli / (BrE)
in a way that does not follow a regular pattern, schedule, or rate; inconsistently or unevenly.
not regularly or evenly

Examples

The trains have been running irregularly due to the ongoing maintenance work.
He visits his grandparents irregularly, sometimes months apart.
The patient was breathing irregularly after the surgery.

Synset Examples

her letters arrived irregularly
the stomach mucosa was irregularly blackened

Related Terms

  • Antonym: regularly - in a constant or uniform manner

2. ADV

/ ɪˈrɛɡjələrli / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛɡjʊləli / (BrE)
in a way that is not even, uniform, or regular; at uneven or inconsistent intervals or rates.
in an uneven or inconsistent way

Examples

The machine started working irregularly after the power outage.
He checks his email irregularly, so replies may be delayed.
Clouds appeared irregularly across the sky, breaking up the sunshine.

Synset Examples

the patient is breathing irregularly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: regularly - at uniform or consistent intervals

Etymology

From Latin 'irregularis' (not regular) + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.

3. ADV

/ ɪˈrɛɡjʊlɚli / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛɡjʊləli / (BrE)
in a way that does not follow a regular pattern, shape, or arrangement; unevenly or unpredictably.
in an uneven or unpredictable manner

Examples

The artist painted the walls irregularly to give the room a rustic feel.
Rain fell irregularly throughout the afternoon, making it hard to plan outdoor activities.
The coastline is irregularly shaped, with many small coves and inlets.

Synset Examples

irregularly shaped solids

Related Terms

  • Antonym: regularly - in a regular manner; following a pattern or schedule
  • Synonym: unevenly - in an uneven or irregular way

Etymology

From 'irregular' (from Latin 'irregularis', from 'in-' not + 'regularis' regular) + '-ly'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
