Phoradendron

1. NOUN

/ fɔːrəˈdɛndrən / (AmE), / ˌfɒrəˈdɛndrən / (BrE)
A genus of American parasitic plants of the sandalwood family, commonly known as false mistletoe, that attach to trees and shrubs.
genus of American parasitic plants (false mistletoe)

Examples

The oak in the park was heavily infested with Phoradendron last winter.
Researchers studied how Phoradendron affects the growth of host trees.
Some species of Phoradendron are used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistletoe - a parasitic plant associated with Christmas traditions

Etymology

From Greek phor- (thief) and dendron (tree), referring to its parasitic habit.

2. NOUN

/ ˌfɔːrəˈdɛndrən / (AmE), / ˌfɒrəˈdɛndrən / (BrE)
A genus of American parasitic plants, commonly known as false mistletoe, resembling the Old World mistletoe.
American parasitic plant genus (false mistletoe)

Examples

Some species of Phoradendron are commonly referred to as false mistletoe in North America.
The Phoradendron infest the branches of various trees, absorbing water and nutrients from their hosts.
Researchers identified the plant as Phoradendron during their botanical survey of the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistletoe - a parasitic plant similar to Phoradendron

Etymology

From Greek phorá (thief) + déndron (tree), referring to the plant's parasitic nature.
