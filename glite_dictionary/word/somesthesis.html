somesthesis

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈmɛsθəsɪs / (AmE), / səʊˈmɛsθəsɪs / (BrE)
the faculty of bodily perception encompassing sensations from the skin, muscles, joints, and internal organs, including touch, temperature, pain, and proprioception
the sense of bodily feeling and position
medical

Examples

Loss of somesthesis can result from nerve damage affecting the skin and muscles.
Researchers studied how somesthesis contributes to balance in patients with neurological disorders.
Animals with a well-developed somesthesis can respond more effectively to changes in their environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proprioception - the sense of the relative position of one’s own body parts
  • Synonym: kinesthesis - the sense of movement and body position
  • Antonym: exteroception - perception of external stimuli via the skin and sensory organs

Etymology

From Greek soma (body) + aisthēsis (perception, sensation).
