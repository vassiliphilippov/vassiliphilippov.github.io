patchouli

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtʃuːli / (AmE), / pəˈtʃuːli / (BrE)
a strong, musky perfume made from the oil of the patchouli plant, often used in incense and fragrances
perfume from the patchouli plant

Examples

The room was filled with the heady scent of patchouli.
She dabbed a little patchouli on her wrists before leaving the house.
Many people associate the smell of patchouli with the 1960s counterculture.

Etymology

From Tamil 'paccuḷi', referring to the plant.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈtʃuːli / (AmE), / pəˈtʃuːli / (BrE)
A small shrubby plant native to tropical Asia, especially India, whose strongly scented leaves yield a fragrant oil used in perfumes and incense.
fragrant tropical plant; source of perfume oil

Examples

The earthy aroma of patchouli filled the room as she burned the scented candle.
Many perfumes use patchouli oil as a base note for its distinctive fragrance.
She cultivated patchouli in her garden to harvest the aromatic leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mint - aromatic plant of the genus Mentha

Etymology

From Tamil pachchai (green) + ilai (leaf).
