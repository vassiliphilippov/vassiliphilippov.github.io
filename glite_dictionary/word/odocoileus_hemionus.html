Odocoileus hemionus

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊ.dəˈsiː.li.əs ˌhiː.miˈoʊ.nəs / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.dəˈsiː.li.əs ˌhiː.miˈəʊ.nəs / (BrE)
a North American deer species, commonly known as the mule deer, noted for its long ears and forked antlers
mule deer species of North America

Examples

Researchers observed several Odocoileus hemionus grazing on the open plain at dusk.
The Odocoileus hemionus is distinguished by its large ears and distinctive antler shape.
Conservation efforts have helped stabilize the population of Odocoileus hemionus in many regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mule deer - a North American deer with long ears and forked antlers

Etymology

From Latin: Odocoileus (genus name) meaning 'hollow-tooth' and hemionus meaning 'half-ass' (referring to ear size resemblance to mules).
