brioche

1. NOUN

/ briˈoʊʃ / (AmE), / briˈɒʃ / (BrE)
a light, slightly sweet bread or roll, rich with eggs and butter
rich, sweet egg bread or roll

Examples

For breakfast, she enjoyed a freshly baked brioche with her coffee.
The bakery specializes in traditional French breads such as brioche and baguette.
A slice of brioche makes the perfect base for gourmet French toast.

Etymology

French, from Old French brier (to knead) + -oche (diminutive suffix).
