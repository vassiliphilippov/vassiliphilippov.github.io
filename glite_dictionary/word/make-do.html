make-do

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪkˌdu / (AmE), / ˈmeɪkˌduː / (BrE)
an improvised or temporary substitute used to meet an urgent need or emergency
improvised or temporary substitute

Examples

We used an old curtain as a make-do for the door until we could buy a replacement.
Their shelter was only a make-do, but it kept them dry through the storm.
She fashioned a make-do out of cardboard when she lost the lid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improvisation - the act of making or inventing something without preparation
  • Synonym: stopgap - a temporary way of dealing with a problem or satisfying a need

Etymology

From the phrase 'make do,' meaning to manage with what is available.
