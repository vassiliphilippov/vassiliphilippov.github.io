limerick

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪmɚɪk / (AmE), / ˈlɪmərɪk / (BrE)
a humorous poem of five lines with a rhyme scheme of aabba, typically with a witty or absurd subject
humorous five-line poem

Examples

He read a clever limerick at the poetry contest, making everyone laugh.
The children enjoyed writing limericks about their pets for English class.
A classic limerick often begins with 'There once was a man from...'.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clerihew - a whimsical, four-line biographical poem with an aabb rhyme scheme

Etymology

Named after the city of Limerick in Ireland, possibly from a tradition of fanciful extemporaneous verse at social gatherings.
