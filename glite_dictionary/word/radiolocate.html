to radiolocate

1. VERB

/ ˌreɪdioʊˈloʊkeɪt / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊˈləʊkeɪt / (BrE)
to determine the position of something using radar technology
find position using radar

Examples

The search team used advanced equipment to radiolocate the missing boat.
Air traffic controllers can radiolocate aircraft in poor visibility conditions.
Engineers radiolocated the source of the signal before sending a rescue team.

Synset Examples

The pilot managed to radiolocate the lost aircraft

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to locate - to discover the exact place or position of something

Etymology

From 'radio-' (relating to radio waves) + 'locate'.
