to forgive

1. VERB

/ fərˈɡɪv / (AmE), / fəˈɡɪv / (BrE)
to officially stop requiring someone to pay money that they owe
cancel a debt or payment requirement

Examples

The bank decided to forgive half of his outstanding loan.
After years of financial hardship, her student debt was finally forgiven.
Charitable organizations sometimes forgive small loans when borrowers cannot repay them.

Synset Examples

I forgive you your debt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pardon - to officially allow someone not to suffer punishment or obligation

Etymology

From Old English 'forgiefan', from 'for-' (completely) + 'giefan' (to give).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02570341-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ fərˈɡɪv / (AmE), / fəˈɡɪv / (BrE)
to stop feeling angry or resentful toward someone for an offense, mistake, or wrongdoing; to grant pardon to someone.
stop blaming or pardon someone

Examples

After a long talk, she decided to forgive her friend for the misunderstanding.
It is not always easy to forgive someone who has hurt you deeply.
Many people find it healing to forgive themselves for past mistakes.

Synset Examples

I forgave him his infidelity
She cannot forgive him for forgetting her birthday

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pardon - to officially release someone from punishment or forgive a fault
  • Antonym: to condemn - to express strong disapproval of or to sentence to punishment

Etymology

From Old English forġiefan (to give, grant, forgive), from for- (completely) + ġiefan (to give).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00905339-v
Synset POS: v
