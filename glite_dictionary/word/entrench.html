to entrench

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈtrɛntʃ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrɛntʃ / (BrE)
to establish something very firmly so that it is difficult to change or remove
to establish firmly and securely

Examples

The company's management became entrenched and resistant to any suggestions for change.
These beliefs have become deeply entrenched in society over the years.
They are working to entrench their power within the organization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embed - to fix firmly and deeply in a surrounding mass
  • Antonym: to dislodge - to remove from a fixed or established position

Etymology

From French *en-*, meaning 'in', and *trancher*, meaning 'to cut'.

WordNet ID: oewn-01533813-v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈtrɛntʃ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrɛntʃ / (BrE)
(of troops or individuals) to establish oneself in a position of protection, especially by digging or occupying a trench or secured defensive area.
occupy a trench or secured position (military)

Examples

The soldiers began to entrench themselves as nightfall approached.
To withstand the enemy assault, the unit quickly entrenched behind sandbags and barbed wire.
After hours of digging, the platoon was well entrenched and ready for any attack.

Synset Examples

The troops dug in for the night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fortify - to make a place stronger and safer against attack
  • Antonym: surrender - to stop resisting an enemy; to yield

Etymology

From French *en–* (in) + *tranchée* (trench).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01533912-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈtrɛntʃ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrɛntʃ / (BrE)
to firmly establish an attitude, idea, or principle so that it is difficult to change
make ideas or principles firmly established

Examples

The current administration hopes to entrench a culture of transparency within government institutions.
Educational reforms seek to entrench equal opportunities for all students.
Many companies now entrench diversity and inclusion policies into their core values.

Synset Examples

The policy aims to entrench equality in the workplace.
We need to entrench our values in the company mission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embed - to fix firmly and deeply in a surrounding mass
  • Synonym: to instill - to gradually establish an idea or attitude in someone's mind
  • Antonym: to undermine - to weaken or erode the foundation of something established

Etymology

From French 'en-' (in) + 'trenchier' (to cut, carve, dig a trench).
