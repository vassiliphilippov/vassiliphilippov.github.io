exclusion

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈskluʒən / (AmE), / ɪkˈskluːʒən / (BrE)
the state of being formally excluded or excommunicated from a community, especially a religious one
the state of being excommunicated

Examples

After violating the code of conduct, he faced exclusion from the church community.
In medieval times, exclusion from a religious order could have serious social consequences.
Her actions eventually led to her exclusion by church authorities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excommunication - the formal removal of a person from participation in the sacraments and services of the Christian Church

Etymology

From Latin exclusio (shutting out), from excludere (to shut out).

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˈskluʒən / (AmE), / ɪkˈskluːʒən / (BrE)
the state or process of being prevented from entering or being part of something
state of being kept out or left out

Examples

The new policy led to the exclusion of several minority groups from the program.
Social exclusion can have lasting effects on people's mental well-being.
His exclusion from the meeting was seen as unfair by many colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inclusion - the act of including or being included

Etymology

From Latin 'excludere' (to shut out), via Old French 'exclusion'.

3. NOUN

/ ɪkˈskluʒən / (AmE), / ɪkˈskluːʒən / (BrE)
the act of deliberately leaving someone or something out or not including them
the act of omitting

Examples

The deliberate exclusion of her name from the list upset her.
Some minorities feel a sense of exclusion from mainstream society.
The exclusion of certain data affected the results of the study.

Synset Examples

with the exception of the children, everyone was told the news

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inclusion - the act of including someone or something
  • Synonym: omission - the act of leaving out or neglecting

Etymology

From Latin excludere (to shut out), via Old French exclusion.

4. NOUN

/ ɪkˈskluʒən / (AmE), / ɪkˈskluːʒən / (BrE)
the act of preventing someone or something from entering or being included; the act of forcing someone or something out
the act of preventing entry or participation

Examples

The new policy led to the exclusion of several members from the club.
Her repeated tardiness eventually resulted in her exclusion from the team.
Some critics argue that the law promotes social exclusion rather than integration.

Synset Examples

the ejection of troublemakers by the police
the child's expulsion from school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expulsion - the act of being forced out, especially from an organization or place
  • Antonym: inclusion - the act of including or being included

Etymology

From Latin 'excludere' (to shut out), via Old French 'exclusion'.
