sorely

1. ADV

/ ˈsɔɹ.li / (AmE), / ˈsɔː.li / (BrE)
To a very great degree or seriously; extremely.
to a very great extent; extremely

Examples

She sorely regretted her decision after realizing its consequences.
The team was sorely disappointed by the loss in the finals.
Resources are sorely needed in the affected region after the disaster.

Synset Examples

I missed him sorely
we were sorely taxed to keep up with them

Related Terms

  • Synonym: severely - To an intense or great degree
  • Synonym: badly - To a serious or great degree

Etymology

From sore + -ly; 'sore' from Old English 'sār' (painful, grievous) + '-ly' (adverbial suffix).

2. ADV

/ ˈsɔrli / (AmE), / ˈsɔːli / (BrE)
In a way that shows pain, suffering, or distress.
in a painful or distressed way

Examples

She was sorely wounded in the accident and had to be hospitalized for weeks.
After hearing the bad news, he looked sorely distressed and needed comfort.
He moved sorely after the surgery, careful with every step.

Synset Examples

she moved painfully forward
sorely wounded

Related Terms

  • Synonym: painfully - in a manner that shows pain or discomfort
  • Antonym: pleasantly - in a pleasing or enjoyable way

Etymology

From Old English 'sār' (painful, sore) + '-ly'.
