suppresser

1. NOUN

/ səˈprɛsər / (AmE), / səˈprɛsə / (BrE)
a person who suppresses something, especially dissent, information, or free expression
person who suppresses

Examples

The regime's most notorious suppresser was feared by the people.
As a dedicated suppresser of facts, she altered official reports before publication.
Throughout history, many have resisted the rule of the suppresser.

Synset Examples

dictators are suppressors of free speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oppressor - someone who keeps others in subjugation or hardship
  • Antonym: liberator - someone who frees others from oppression or restraint

Etymology

From the verb suppress, from Latin suppressus (pressed down), agent suffix -er.

2. NOUN

/ səˈprɛsər / (AmE), / səˈprɛsə / (BrE)
a gene that inhibits the phenotypic expression of another gene, especially a mutant gene
gene that inhibits expression of another gene
medical

Examples

A mutation in the suppresser can reactivate the original gene's expression.
Researchers discovered a new suppresser that counteracts the effects of the disease-causing gene.
The presence of the suppresser altered the inheritance pattern of the trait.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppressor gene - a gene that slows down or prevents the expression of another gene

Etymology

From Latin 'suppressus', past participle of 'supprimere' (to press down, restrain).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ səˈprɛsər / (AmE), / səˈprɛsə / (BrE)
a device used to reduce or eliminate unwanted electrical currents in a circuit
device that suppresses electrical currents

Examples

The electrician installed a suppresser to protect sensitive equipment from voltage spikes.
Using a suppresser can help prevent interference in audio systems.
The laboratory required each machine to have an electrical suppresser fitted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppressor - a device that reduces or eliminates electrical noise or interference

Etymology

From suppress + -er, meaning 'one that suppresses'.
