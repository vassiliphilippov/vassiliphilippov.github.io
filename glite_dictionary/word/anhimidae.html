Anhimidae

1. NOUN

/ ænˈhɪmɪˌdiː / (AmE), / anˈhɪmɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of South American birds known as screamers, distinguished by their loud calls and spurred wings.
family of South American birds (screamers)

Examples

The Anhimidae are unique among waterfowl for their distinctive calls and bony spurs on the wings.
Birdwatchers traveled to the wetlands to observe members of the Anhimidae in their natural habitat.
Unlike most birds in their region, the Anhimidae have a diet consisting mainly of aquatic plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screamer - A bird of the family Anhimidae, known for its loud call

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Anhima, from Greek an-, ‘not’ + hemeīn, ‘to scream’.
