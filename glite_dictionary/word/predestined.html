predestined

1. ADJ

/ ˌpriˈdɛstɪnd / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈdɛstɪnd / (BrE)
(of an event or outcome) determined in advance by fate or divine will; established or arranged unalterably before it happens.
determined in advance by fate or destiny

Examples

Some people believe that their lives are predestined and cannot be changed by personal effort.
The two lovers seemed predestined to meet, as if their meeting was written in the stars.
He felt his success was predestined, the result of plans made long before he was born.

Synset Examples

his place in history was foreordained
a sense of predestinate inevitability about it
it seemed predestined since the beginning of the world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foreordained - determined in advance; ordained beforehand

Etymology

From Medieval Latin praedestinatus, past participle of praedestinare (to predetermine).
