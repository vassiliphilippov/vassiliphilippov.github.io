sneezewort

1. NOUN

/ ˈsniːzˌwɔrt / (AmE), / ˈsniːzˌwɜːt / (BrE)
A Eurasian plant with loose clusters of white, button-shaped flowers and long, grey-green leaves, which can cause sneezing when the leaves are powdered.
Eurasian plant causing sneezing when powdered

Examples

My grandmother grew sneezewort in her garden for its medicinal uses.
The botanist collected samples of sneezewort along the riverbank.
Crushed sneezewort leaves were once believed to induce sneezing and clear the sinuses.

Etymology

From 'sneeze' + 'wort', with 'wort' from Old English 'wyrt' meaning plant or herb.
