capturer

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæptʃɚɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæptʃərə / (BrE)
a person who captures and holds people or animals
person or animal who captures others

Examples

The capturer skillfully set the trap to catch the elusive fox.
The hostages were guarded day and night by their capturer.
After a long pursuit, the capturer finally apprehended the fugitive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: captor - a person who takes someone captive
  • Antonym: rescuer - a person who frees someone from danger or captivity

Etymology

From 'capture' + '-er', indicating a person who performs the action.
