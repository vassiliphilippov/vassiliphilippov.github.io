to unbraid

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈbreɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈbreɪd / (BrE)
to undo the interlaced strands of a braid, especially in hair or fabric
to undo braids

Examples

She asked her mother to unbraid her hair before bedtime.
After the dance, he carefully unbraided the colorful threads from the bracelet.
It took an hour to unbraid all the intricate plaits from her hair.

Synset Examples

unbraid my hair

Related Terms

  • Antonym: braid - to interweave strands, especially of hair or fabric, to form a braid

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (reversal) + 'braid'.
