Plantago

1. PROPN

/ plænˈteɪɡoʊ / (AmE), / planˈtɑːɡəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of small, mostly herbaceous plants in the family Plantaginaceae, found worldwide and commonly known as plantains.
genus of herbaceous plants (plantains)

Examples

Several species in the genus Plantago are used in herbal medicine for their soothing properties.
The botanist collected samples of Plantago from different regions for her study.
Common plantain, or Plantago major, can often be found growing alongside roads and in lawns.

Etymology

From Latin 'plantago', a plantain (herb).

2. NOUN

/ plænˈteɪɡoʊ / (AmE), / planˈteɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of small, mainly herbaceous plants in the plantain family (Plantaginaceae), found throughout the world.
genus of plantain family herbs

Examples

Several species within the Plantago genus are used in traditional medicine.
The seeds of Plantago are a popular source of dietary fiber called psyllium.
We found a patch of Plantago growing along the edge of the meadow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plantain - common name of any plant of the Plantago genus

Etymology

From Latin *plantago*, the name for plantain plants.
