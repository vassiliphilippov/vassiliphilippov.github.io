sashay

1. NOUN

/ sæˈʃeɪ / (AmE), / sæˈʃeɪ / (BrE)
A square dance figure in which partners circle each other, taking sideways steps.
square dance figure involving sideways steps

Examples

Each couple performed a sashay as the caller gave instructions.
The dancers moved gracefully through the sashay in perfect unison.
During the final sashay, the crowd cheered for their smooth moves.

Etymology

From French 'chassé' (a gliding dance step).

2. VERB

/ sæˈʃeɪ / (AmE), / sæˈʃeɪ / (BrE)
to walk in a confident, lively, and often exaggerated way, typically to attract attention or impress others
walk in a showy or confident manner

Examples

She sashayed into the room, turning every head with her dramatic entrance.
Confident contestants sashay down the runway during the fashion show.
He sashayed across the stage as if he owned the place.

Synset Examples

He struts around like a rooster in a hen house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strut - to walk in a proud or vain manner
  • Synonym: swagger - to walk or behave in a very confident and typically arrogant or aggressive way

Etymology

From French 'chassé' (a gliding dance step), past participle of 'chasser' (to chase).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01920627-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ sæˈʃeɪ / (AmE), / sæˈʃeɪ / (BrE)
to move in a confident, gliding, or sideways manner, often with an exaggerated, swaying motion
move with a gliding, sideways motion

Examples

She sashayed gracefully across the dance floor, catching everyone’s attention.
The model sashayed sideways to avoid the obstacle on the runway.
He sashayed past the waiting crowds with a confident smile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strut - to walk in a proud, confident way
  • Antonym: shuffle - to walk by dragging one's feet along or without lifting them fully off the ground

Etymology

From French 'chassé' (to chase), a dance step involving a gliding movement.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01873189-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ sæˈʃeɪ / (AmE), / sæˈʃeɪ / (BrE)
(of a dancer) to perform a chasse step; to glide or move sideways in a balletic manner.
to perform a chasse step in ballet

Examples

The ballerina sashayed gracefully across the stage during the performance.
As part of their routine, the dancers sashayed from one side of the studio to the other.
He learned how to sashay elegantly as his ballet skills improved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chasse - a gliding dance step with feet coming together

Etymology

From French 'chassé' (chased), a ballet term, influenced by 'sashay'.
