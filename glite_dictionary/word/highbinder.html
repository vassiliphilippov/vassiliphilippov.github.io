highbinder

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪˌbaɪndər / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌbaɪndə / (BrE)
a corrupt or unscrupulous politician, especially one involved in dishonest or illegal practices
corrupt or unscrupulous politician
archaic american

Examples

The newspaper accused the mayor of being a highbinder involved in bribery and fraud.
Many citizens were frustrated by the influence of highbinders in city government.
He was determined not to become a highbinder like some of his predecessors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: politician - a person actively involved in politics
  • Antonym: statesman - an experienced and respected political leader

Etymology

Originally American English, likely from Chinese dialectal Cantonese ‘hai bun’ (to cause trouble), adopted in the 19th century to refer to members of criminal Chinese societies, and later applied to corrupt politicians.
