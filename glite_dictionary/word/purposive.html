purposive

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɜːrpəsɪv / (AmE), / ˈpɜːpəsɪv / (BrE)
having or showing an intention or purpose; done with a particular aim
having a purpose or aim

Examples

The experiment was designed to study purposive behavior in animals.
Her actions were clearly purposive, not random.
A purposive approach helps us achieve our long-term goals.

Synset Examples

purposive behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purposeful - having or showing determination or resolve

Etymology

From 'purpose' (Middle English), with suffix '-ive' meaning 'having the nature of'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɝːpəsɪv / (AmE), / ˈpɜːpəsɪv / (BrE)
having or showing intention, purpose, or design in action or behavior; purposeful
having or showing purpose or intention

Examples

The scientist's purposive experiments led to significant breakthroughs.
Animals are capable of purposive behavior when searching for food.
The committee took a purposive approach in addressing the issue.

Synset Examples

purposive behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purposeful - having or showing determination or resolve; intentional
  • Antonym: random - lacking purpose or plan; haphazard

Etymology

From 'purpose' + '-ive', first recorded in the late 19th century.
