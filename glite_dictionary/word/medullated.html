medullated

1. ADJ

/ məˈdʌleɪtɪd / (AmE), / mɛˈdjʊleɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of neurons) having a sheath of myelin surrounding the axon.
having a myelin sheath (of neurons)
medical

Examples

The scientist observed that the medullated fibers conducted nerve impulses much faster than the unmyelinated ones.
Damage to medullated nerves can disrupt normal motor function and sensation.
During the lesson, the professor explained how the medullated axons differ from non-medullated ones in structure and speed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unmyelinated - (of neurons) not covered with a myelin sheath
  • Synonym: myelinated - (of neurons) having a myelin sheath

Etymology

From Latin 'medulla' (marrow), referring to the protective or core layer.
