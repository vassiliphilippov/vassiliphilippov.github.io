Shakespearean

1. NOUN

/ ʃeɪkˈspɪəriən / (AmE), / ʃeɪkˈspɪəriən / (BrE)
a person who studies or specializes in the works of William Shakespeare, or an actor performing in Shakespeare's plays
a person specializing in Shakespeare or his works

Examples

As a leading Shakespearean, she frequently lectures on Elizabethan drama.
The cast includes a renowned Shakespearean who has performed in over twenty of the Bard's plays.
Many aspiring actors dream of becoming a Shakespearean on the London stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shakespearean - relating to or characteristic of Shakespeare or his works (adjective sense)

Etymology

From the name William Shakespeare plus the English suffix -an (relating to or belonging to).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʃeɪkspɪˈriən / (AmE), / ˌʃeɪkspəˈrɪən / (BrE)
of or relating to William Shakespeare or his works.
relating to Shakespeare or his works

Examples

She delivered a brilliant performance in a Shakespearean tragedy.
The director staged the play in a distinctly Shakespearean style.
Many actors dream of playing a leading role in a Shakespearean production.

Synset Examples

Shakespearean plays

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shakesperean - alternative spelling of Shakespearean

Etymology

From the name William Shakespeare + -an (adjective forming suffix).
