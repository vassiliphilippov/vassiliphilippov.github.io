hurl

1. NOUN

/ hɝl / (AmE), / hɜːl / (BrE)
a forceful or violent throw of an object
violent throw

Examples

The player made a tremendous hurl of the ball across the field.
With a sudden hurl, she sent the stone flying into the lake.
The vase broke after his angry hurl across the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throw - an act of propelling something with force through the air
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00106299-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ hɜrl / (AmE), / hɜːl / (BrE)
to say something, especially words or accusations, with great force or anger
say something with force or anger

Examples

During the heated debate, he began to hurl accusations at his opponent.
She was upset when her colleague hurled insults at her in front of everyone.
In frustration, he hurled criticism at the team's performance after the match.

Synset Examples

hurl insults
throw accusations at someone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throw - to propel something forcefully through the air by a movement of the arm and hand
  • Antonym: whisper - speak very quietly and softly

3. VERB

/ hɜrl / (AmE), / hɜːl / (BrE)
to make a forceful or thrusting forward movement, especially suddenly or with great energy
make a thrusting forward movement

Examples

The soldier hurled himself at the enemy line, surprising everyone with his courage.
With a sudden burst of energy, she hurled forward to block the door before it closed.
He hurled himself into the debate, determined to make his point heard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrust - to push or drive with force, as in a forward movement
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02066140-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ hɜrl / (AmE), / hɜːl / (BrE)
to throw something with great force
throw forcefully

Examples

He picked up a stone and hurled it across the river.
Crowds began to hurl insults at the speaker during the protest.
She hurled her phone onto the couch in frustration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throw - to propel something through the air with the hand

Etymology

Possibly from Old Norse *hurla* (to whirl, toss), related to Middle English *hurle* (to throw, tumble).
