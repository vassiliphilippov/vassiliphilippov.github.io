to formicate

1. VERB

/ ˈfɔrmɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmɪˌkeɪt / (BrE)
to move or crawl about in a manner resembling ants; to swarm or move like ants
to crawl about like ants

Examples

The insects began to formicate across the spilled sugar, creating a moving black trail.
Fear made her skin crawl, as if a thousand unseen legs were formicating over her body.
Debris and small creatures seemed to formicate over the forest floor after the rain.

Etymology

From Latin 'formica' meaning 'ant.'
