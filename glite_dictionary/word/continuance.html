continuance

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtɪn.ju.əns / (AmE), / kənˈtɪn.ju.əns / (BrE)
the state or quality of lasting or continuing for a period of time; endurance
state of lasting or continuing

Examples

The continuance of the tradition depends on the community’s support.
Despite the difficulties, her continuance in the role inspired many others.
They questioned the continuance of the policy given the recent changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duration - the length of time that something lasts
  • Antonym: discontinuance - the act of ending or ceasing; a stop

Etymology

From Old French continuance, from Latin continuare (to make continuous).
