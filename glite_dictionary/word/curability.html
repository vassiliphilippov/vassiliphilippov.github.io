curability

1. NOUN

/ ˌkjʊrəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌkjʊərəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the ability or quality of being able to be cured or healed
ability to be cured or healed

Examples

The curability of many early-stage cancers gives patients hope for recovery.
Doctors discussed the curability of the disease with the patient's family.
Research is ongoing into the curability of various rare conditions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incurability - the quality of not being able to be cured
  • Synonym: cure - remedy or treatment to make an illness go away

Etymology

From cure + -ability, modeled after words like 'durability'; 'cure' from Latin 'curare' (to care for).
