Acer platanoides

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪsər ˌplætəˈnɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˈeɪsə ˌplætəˈnɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
a large Eurasian maple tree, commonly known as the Norway maple, with five-lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn and cultivated in many varieties
Norway maple tree species

Examples

The Acer platanoides is often planted along city streets for its resilience.
In autumn, the leaves of the Acer platanoides turn a striking yellow.
Gardeners value Acer platanoides for its rapid growth and dense foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Norway maple - common name for Acer platanoides

Etymology

From Latin: *Acer* (maple) and *platanoides* (plane-tree-like, from resemblance to Platanus leaves).
