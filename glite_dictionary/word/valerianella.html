Valerianella

1. NOUN

/ vəˌlɛriəˈnɛlə / (AmE), / vəˌlɪəriəˈnɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of small, annual herbaceous plants native to the Old World, some species of which have become naturalized elsewhere.
genus of annual plants (corn salads)

Examples

Valerianella is often cultivated in Europe as a salad green known as corn salad.
Botanists discovered a new species of Valerianella growing in the wild fields.
Some gardeners prefer Valerianella because it matures quickly and resists frost.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on the related genus 'Valeriana.'

2. NOUN

/ vəˌlɛriəˈnɛlə / (AmE), / vəˌlɛəriəˈnɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of annual herbs native to the Old World, widely naturalized, known for producing small, edible salad plants such as corn salad.
a genus of annual herbs (botany)

Examples

Valerianella is commonly found growing wild in fields across Europe.
The botanist noted several species of Valerianella in the meadow.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Valerianella species for their edible leaves.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the Latin root 'valeriana' (valerian).
