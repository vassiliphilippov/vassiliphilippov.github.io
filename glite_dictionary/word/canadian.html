Canadian

1. NOUN

/ kəˈneɪ.di.ən / (AmE), / kəˈneɪ.di.ən / (BrE)
a person who is a native or inhabitant of Canada
native or inhabitant of Canada

Examples

As a proud Canadian, she celebrated Canada Day with her family.
The Canadian hockey team won the gold medal.
Many Canadians are known for their politeness and friendliness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Canadian - of or relating to Canada (adjective)

From Canada + -ian, indicating origin or association.

/ kəˈneɪdiən / (AmE), / kəˈneɪdiən / (BrE)
the Canadian River; a river rising in northeastern New Mexico, flowing east across the Texas panhandle and into Oklahoma, where it becomes a tributary of the Arkansas River
a river in New Mexico, Texas, and Oklahoma

Examples

The Canadian provides much-needed water to communities along its course in Texas and Oklahoma.
In the early 1800s, explorers often camped by the banks of the Canadian.
Flooding of the Canadian caused significant damage in several Oklahoma towns last spring.

Etymology

Named after early French explorers who referred to its waters or region as 'Canadian'.
