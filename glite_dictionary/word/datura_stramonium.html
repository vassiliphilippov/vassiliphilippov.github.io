Datura stramonium

1. NOUN

/ dəˈtʊrə strəˈmoʊniəm / (AmE), / dəˈtjʊərə strəˈməʊniəm / (BrE)
A tall, annual tropical weed with foul-smelling leaves, large white or violet trumpet-shaped flowers, and prickly fruit; highly poisonous.
a poisonous tropical weed with trumpet-shaped flowers

Examples

The seeds of Datura stramonium are highly toxic and should never be ingested.
Many rural fields are invaded by Datura stramonium during the late summer.
Researchers discovered that Datura stramonium contains compounds used historically in traditional medicine, though it is extremely dangerous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jimsonweed - another name for Datura stramonium
  • Synonym: thorn apple - another name for Datura stramonium

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Datura' from Hindi 'dhatura' and 'stramonium' of uncertain origin, possibly Medieval Latin.
