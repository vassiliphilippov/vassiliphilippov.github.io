Sinornis

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈnɔrnɪs / (AmE), / saɪˈnɔːnɪs / (BrE)
a genus of small, sparrow-sized fossil birds from the Jurassic to Cretaceous periods, known for a keeled breastbone and vestigial tail, discovered in China
primitive fossil bird genus from China

Examples

The discovery of Sinornis provided important clues about the evolution of modern birds.
Paleontologists were excited to unearth a nearly complete Sinornis skeleton in northeastern China.
Unlike Archaeopteryx, Sinornis had a more advanced breastbone that allowed for improved flight.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Archaeopteryx - an earlier genus of primitive bird-like dinosaurs

Etymology

From Latinized Greek, meaning 'Chinese bird' ('Sinae' for China + 'ornis' for bird).

2. NOUN

/ saɪˈnɔrnɪs / (AmE), / saɪˈnɔːnɪs / (BrE)
An extinct genus of enantiornithine birds that lived during the Early Cretaceous period, known from fossils found in China.
extinct genus of early enantiornithine birds

Examples

The discovery of Sinornis fossils provided valuable insight into the evolution of early birds.
Paleontologists believe Sinornis was capable of powered flight, unlike many of its contemporaries.
The unique skeletal structure of Sinornis distinguishes it from modern bird species.

Etymology

From Latin 'Sina' (China) and Greek 'ornis' (bird), meaning 'Chinese bird'.
