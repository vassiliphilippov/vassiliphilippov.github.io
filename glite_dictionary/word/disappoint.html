to disappoint

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪnt / (BrE)
to fail to meet the hopes or expectations of someone
fail to meet expectations

Examples

I'm sorry to disappoint you, but the tickets are already sold out.
The final episode of the series did not disappoint its loyal fans.
He didn't want to disappoint his parents by failing the exam.

Synset Examples

Her boyfriend let her down when he did not propose marriage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to let down - to fail to support or help someone as expected; to disappoint
  • Antonym: to satisfy - to meet the expectations, needs, or desires of someone

Etymology

From Old French désapointer, from des- (dis-) + appointer (to appoint).
