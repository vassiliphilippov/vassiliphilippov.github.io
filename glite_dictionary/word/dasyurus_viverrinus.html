Dasyurus viverrinus

1. NOUN

/ ˌdæsiˈjʊərəs vɪvəˈraɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌdæsɪˈjʊərəs vɪvəˈrɪnəs / (BrE)
A carnivorous, arboreal, cat-like marsupial native to Australia and Tasmania, also known as the eastern quoll.
carnivorous marsupial of Australia and Tasmania

Examples

The Dasyurus viverrinus is now considered extinct on mainland Australia but still survives in Tasmania.
Researchers are studying the diet of Dasyurus viverrinus as part of conservation efforts.
A wildlife sanctuary has successfully bred several Dasyurus viverrinus in captivity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eastern quoll - common name for Dasyurus viverrinus

Etymology

From Latinized scientific nomenclature: genus 'Dasyurus' (from Greek 'dasys' meaning hairy and 'oura' meaning tail) and species 'viverrinus' (from Latin 'viverrinus', meaning 'ferret-like').
