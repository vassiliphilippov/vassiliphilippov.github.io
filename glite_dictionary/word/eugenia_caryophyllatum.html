Eugenia caryophyllatum

1. NOUN

/ juːˈdʒiniə ˌkærioʊfɪˈleɪtəm / (AmE), / juːˈdʒiːniə ˌkærɪəʊˈfɪleɪtəm / (BrE)
A tropical evergreen tree with red flowers, widely cultivated for its flower buds, which are the source of cloves.
tropical evergreen tree; source of cloves

Examples

Eugenia caryophyllatum is grown extensively in tropical regions for its highly valued flower buds.
The dried flower buds of Eugenia caryophyllatum are harvested to produce cloves used in cooking and medicine.
Researchers studied the essential oil content extracted from Eugenia caryophyllatum flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clove - the aromatic dried flower bud used as a spice

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name Eugenia, combined with specific epithet from Greek 'karyophyllon' (clove).
