enigma

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnɪɡmə / (AmE), / ɪˈnɪɡmə / (BrE)
something or someone that is mysterious, puzzling, or difficult to understand; a riddle or mystery.
mysterious or puzzling thing or person

Examples

The disappearance of the ancient civilization remains an enigma to historians.
Despite years of research, the origin of the disease is still an enigma.
She was an enigma to her colleagues, always polite but very private.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mystery - something difficult or impossible to understand or explain
  • Synonym: puzzle - something that is difficult to understand or solve

Etymology

From Latin 'aenigma', from Greek 'ainigma' (riddle).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈnɪɡmə / (AmE), / ɪˈnɪɡmə / (BrE)
A person, thing, or situation that is mysterious, puzzling, or difficult to understand or explain.
something mysterious or puzzling

Examples

The disappearance of the ship in the Bermuda Triangle remains an enigma to this day.
Despite years of research, the origin of the ancient stones is still an enigma.
He was an enigma to all who knew him, rarely speaking about his past.

Synset Examples

how it got out is a mystery
it remains one of nature's secrets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mystery - something difficult or impossible to understand or explain
  • Synonym: puzzle - something that is difficult to understand or solve
  • Antonym: solution - the act of solving a problem or mystery

Etymology

From Latin 'aenigma', from Greek 'ainigma' (riddle, obscure speech).
