runniness

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌni.nəs / (AmE), / ˈrʌni.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being runny; the property of flowing easily or having a thin, liquid consistency
state of being runny or fluid

Examples

The runniness of the sauce made it difficult to keep on the plate.
Check the runniness of the egg yolks before serving breakfast.
She prefers yogurt with a thicker texture, so the runniness of this brand disappointed her.

Synset Examples

adding lead makes the alloy easier to cast because the melting point is reduced and the fluidity is increased
they believe that fluidity increases as the water gets warmer
