skate

1. VERB

/ skeɪt / (AmE), / skeɪt / (BrE)
to move smoothly over a surface wearing skates, especially on ice or roller skates
move wearing skates

Examples

Every winter, children love to skate on the frozen lake near their town.
He learned to skate backwards after several months of practice.
They decided to skate together at the new indoor rink.

Synset Examples

The Dutch often skate along the canals in winter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rollerblade - to skate using in-line skates (Rollerblades)
  • Degree Lower: slide - to move smoothly along a surface while maintaining continuous contact
  • Synonym: glide - to move smoothly and continuously along a surface

Etymology

From Middle Dutch 'schaets' (stilt, skate).

2. NOUN

/ skeɪt / (AmE), / skeɪt / (BrE)
a large edible marine fish of the ray family with a long snout and thick tail, having broad pectoral fins that merge with the head and swimming by undulating the edges of these fins
large flat marine ray with edible flesh

Examples

The fisherman caught a large skate off the coast and brought it to shore.
Skate is often served grilled or poached in seafood restaurants.
Scientists studied the swimming patterns of the skate to learn about its unique movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ray - any of various cartilaginous fishes with flattened bodies and enlarged pectoral fins

Etymology

Perhaps from Old Norse skata (a kind of fish), related to Old High German scato.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01503801-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ skeɪt / (AmE), / skeɪt / (BrE)
A boot, shoe, or frame with a set of wheels or a blade attached, worn on the foot to glide smoothly over ice or a hard surface for sport or recreation.
sports shoe with wheels or blade for gliding

Examples

He strapped on his skates and headed onto the ice.
Her new roller skates made her the fastest on the block.
Don't forget to bring your skates to the rink tomorrow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roller skate - shoe with small wheels for skating on hard surfaces
  • Synonym: ice skate - shoe with a metal blade for skating on ice

Etymology

From Dutch 'schaats' (stilt, skate), via French 'eschete'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04232791-n
Synset POS: n
