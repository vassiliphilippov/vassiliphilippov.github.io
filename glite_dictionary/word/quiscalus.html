Quiscalus

1. NOUN

/ kwɪsˈkeɪləs / (AmE), / kwɪsˈkeɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of American blackbirds known as grackles, found in North and South America, often recognized for their iridescent plumage.
genus of grackles (blackbirds)

Examples

The purple grackle belongs to the genus Quiscalus and is common in eastern North America.
Many species within Quiscalus are noted for their glossy black feathers and social behavior.
Ornithologists often study Quiscalus populations to understand avian adaptation in urban environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grackle - a songbird of the American blackbird family with a long tail

Etymology

New Latin, from Spanish *quiscal*, a local name for the grackle.
