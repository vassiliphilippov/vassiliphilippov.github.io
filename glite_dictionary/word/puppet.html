puppet

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌp.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈpʌp.ɪt / (BrE)
A doll representing a person or animal, with a hollow head and a cloth body, designed to be worn over the hand and controlled by the fingers.
doll worn on hand and manipulated by fingers

Examples

The child placed the puppet on her hand and made it talk to the audience.
During the show, the puppet danced and sang under the bright lights.
He crafted a colorful puppet out of felt and buttons for the school play.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marionette - a puppet worked by strings or wires from above

Etymology

From Middle French 'poupette', diminutive of 'poupe' (doll).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpʌpət / (AmE), / ˈpʌpɪt / (BrE)
a person who is controlled by others and used to perform tasks, often unpleasant or dishonest, on their behalf
person controlled by others

Examples

Many believed the new leader was merely a puppet for the powerful corporation.
She refused to become a puppet and insisted on making her own decisions.
He was accused of being a political puppet, carrying out someone else's agenda.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tool - a person used by another to accomplish tasks, often without autonomy
  • Synonym: pawn - someone used by others for their own purposes

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈpʌp.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈpʌp.ɪt / (BrE)
A small figure representing a person or animal that is moved by strings or by hand, especially in entertainment.
figure controlled by strings or hand

Examples

The children watched in fascination as the puppet danced across the stage.
She created a puppet theater in her living room to entertain her friends.
Each puppet was skillfully crafted with colorful costumes and expressive faces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marionette - puppet operated by strings

Etymology

From Middle French 'poupette', diminutive of 'poupe' (doll), from Latin 'pupa' (girl, doll).
