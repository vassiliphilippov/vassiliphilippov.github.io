nauseous

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɔːʃəs / (AmE), / ˈnɔːziəs / (BrE)
feeling as if you are about to vomit; affected by nausea.
feeling sick to the stomach

Examples

The strong smell of paint made me feel nauseous all afternoon.
She became nauseous during the long, bumpy car ride to the mountains.
Many people feel nauseous after riding a rollercoaster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: queasy - feeling sick to the stomach
  • Synonym: sick - feeling ill, especially wanting to vomit

Etymology

From Latin 'nauseosus', from 'nausea' (sea-sickness).

2. ADJ

/ ˈnɔːʃəs, ˈnɑːʃəs / (AmE), / ˈnɔːsɪəs, ˈnɔːʃəs / (BrE)
causing or able to cause nausea or a feeling of sickness
causing nausea; sickening

Examples

The garbage gave off a nauseous odor that lingered in the air for hours.
The patient complained about the nauseous fumes coming from the cleaning chemicals.
A nauseous mixture of spoiled food was found in the refrigerator.

Synset Examples

a nauseating smell
nauseous offal
a sickening stench

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nauseating - causing nausea or disgust
  • Antonym: pleasant - giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment

Etymology

From Latin 'nauseosus', from 'nausea' meaning seasickness.

Source: WordNet
