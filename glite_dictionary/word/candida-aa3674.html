Candida

1. NOUN

/ ˈkændədə / (AmE), / ˈkændɪdə / (BrE)
A genus of yeast-like fungi, some species of which can cause infections in humans, especially in immunocompromised individuals.
genus of yeast-like fungi
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that an overgrowth of Candida can lead to an infection known as candidiasis.
Researchers are studying the resistance of Candida species to common antifungal drugs.
Candida is commonly found in the human body, but it can cause problems when it multiplies excessively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Monilia - a former genus name sometimes used for certain species now classified under Candida

Etymology

From Latin 'candida' meaning 'white', due to the whitish appearance of the organism.
