President Andrew Johnson

1. PROPN

/ ˈprɛzɪdənt ˈændru ˈdʒɑnsən / (AmE), / ˈprɛzɪdənt ˈændru ˈdʒɒnsən / (BrE)
(1808–1875) the 17th President of the United States, who became president after Abraham Lincoln's assassination and was later impeached but acquitted.
17th U.S. President; Lincoln's successor, impeached and acquitted

Examples

After Lincoln's assassination, President Andrew Johnson assumed office and faced the challenge of uniting a divided nation.
The impeachment trial of President Andrew Johnson was a turning point in U.S. political history.
Historians often debate the policies and legacy of President Andrew Johnson during the Reconstruction era.
