Brodiaea elegans

1. NOUN

/ broʊˌdaɪ.iː.ə ˈɛlɪɡənz / (AmE), / brəʊˌdiː.iː.ə ˈɛlɪɡənz / (BrE)
A flowering plant native to northern Oregon to southern California, bearing an umbel of violet or blue-violet flowers atop a leafless stalk.
a flowering plant with violet umbels

Examples

The field was covered in Brodiaea elegans, their violet flowers swaying in the spring breeze.
Hikers in California can often spot Brodiaea elegans blooming along grassy hillsides.
Botanists documented the range of Brodiaea elegans from Oregon to southern California.

Etymology

From the genus name Brodiaea, after Scottish botanist James Brodie, and Latin elegans (elegant, fine).
