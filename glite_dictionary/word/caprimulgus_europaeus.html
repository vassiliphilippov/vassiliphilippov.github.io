Caprimulgus europaeus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæprɪˈmʌlɡəs juːˌroʊˈpiːəs / (AmE), / ˌkæprɪˈmʌlɡəs juːˌrəʊˈpiːəs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the European nightjar, a nocturnal bird found across Europe and western Asia, also known as the Old World goatsucker.
species of nocturnal bird (European nightjar)

Examples

The Caprimulgus europaeus is most often observed at dusk when it becomes active.
Some birdwatchers travel to protected heathlands to spot the elusive Caprimulgus europaeus.
Researchers tagged several Caprimulgus europaeus to learn more about their migration patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nightjar - A nocturnal bird with a soft, camouflaged plumage and distinctive call.

Etymology

From Latin: 'caprimulgus' meaning 'goatsucker', and 'europaeus' meaning 'European'.
