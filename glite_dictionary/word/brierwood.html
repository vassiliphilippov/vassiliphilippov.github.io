brierwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪərˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈbraɪəˌwʊd / (BrE)
Dense wood from the root of the briar (Erica arborea), especially used to make tobacco pipes.
wood from briar root, used for pipes

Examples

He purchased a finely crafted brierwood pipe from the tobacconist.
The durability of brierwood makes it ideal for carving into detailed shapes.
Many collectors prize brierwood pipes for their quality and unique grain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: briar - a thorny shrub, especially Erica arborea

Etymology

From 'brier' (briar, the shrub Erica arborea) + 'wood.'
