apology

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / əˈpɒlədʒi / (BrE)
a formal written defense of a belief, opinion, or course of action
formal written defense

Examples

His apology for the controversial policy was published in several academic journals.
The book is considered an apology for freedom of expression and religious tolerance.
Throughout history, many philosophers have written an apology to defend their beliefs against critics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apologia - a formal written defence, especially of one's opinions

Etymology

From Greek apologíā (a speech in defense), from apologeisthai (to speak in one's defense).

2. NOUN

/ əˈpɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / əˈpɒlədʒi / (BrE)
a very poor or inadequate example of something; something that falls far short of what is expected or required.
a poor or inferior example

Examples

The hotel breakfast was an apology for a meal, consisting only of stale bread and weak coffee.
His explanation was such an apology for a reason that no one believed him.
The building was an apology for a shelter, offering little protection from the cold.

Synset Examples

it was an apology for a meal
a poor excuse for an automobile

Etymology

From Greek apologia (a speech in defense), via Late Latin apologīa and Old French apologie.

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ əˈpɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / əˈpɒlədʒi / (BrE)
a statement or action in which you express regret or say sorry for having hurt or upset someone
expression of regret or saying sorry

Examples

Please accept my apology for being late to the meeting.
He offered a sincere apology after breaking her favorite vase.
After their argument, she wrote a heartfelt apology to her friend.

Synset Examples

he wrote a letter of apology to the hostess

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excuse - a reason or explanation given to justify a fault or offense
  • Synonym: regret - a feeling of sadness about something that has happened
  • Antonym: accusation - a charge or claim that someone has done something wrong

Etymology

From Greek apologia (a speech in defense), via Latin apologia.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06645870-n
Synset POS: n
