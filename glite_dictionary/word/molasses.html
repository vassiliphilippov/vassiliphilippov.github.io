molasses

1. NOUN

/ məˈlæsɪz / (AmE), / məˈlæsɪz / (BrE)
A thick, dark syrup produced by boiling down juice from sugar cane or sugar beet, especially during the process of refining sugar.
thick dark syrup from sugar refining

Examples

Grandma's gingerbread recipe calls for a cup of molasses to add depth of flavor.
The thick molasses oozed slowly from the overturned jar onto the counter.
In colonial times, molasses was widely used as a sweetener before refined sugar became common.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treacle - a similar syrup made during sugar refining, often used as a synonym in British English

Etymology

From Portuguese *melaço*, from Latin *mel* (honey).
