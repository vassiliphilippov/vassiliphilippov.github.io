Hylocereus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪloʊsəˈriːəs / (AmE), / ˌhɪləsɪˈriːəs / (BrE)
A genus of climbing or epiphytic tropical American cacti with angular stems and mostly white, very fragrant flowers, including the plants producing dragon fruit.
genus of tropical American cacti

Examples

The Hylocereus species are well known for producing the vibrant dragon fruit.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Hylocereus in both wild and cultivated environments.
Many gardeners appreciate Hylocereus for its striking flowers and exotic appearance.

Etymology

From Greek hylē (wood, forest) + cereus (Latin for 'candle', a cactus genus), referring to its woody climbing habit.

2. NOUN

/ haɪloʊsəˈriːəs / (AmE), / hɪləsɪəˈriːəs / (BrE)
A genus of climbing or epiphytic tropical American cacti with angular stems and mostly white, very fragrant flowers.
genus of tropical American cacti

Examples

The dragon fruit is produced by several species within the genus Hylocereus.
Horticulturists admire Hylocereus for its striking flowers and epiphytic growth habit.
Recent taxonomic revisions have reclassified some Hylocereus species into another genus.

Etymology

From Greek 'hyle' (wood, forest) and Latinized form of 'Cereus' (a cactus genus).
