half-evergreen

1. ADJ

/ ˌhæf ˈɛvɚˌɡriːn / (AmE), / ˌhɑːf ˈɛvəˌɡriːn / (BrE)
(of a plant) retaining some but not all of its leaves throughout the year; not completely evergreen or deciduous.
retaining some leaves all year (botany)

Examples

Many shrubs in temperate zones are half-evergreen, holding onto some leaves till late winter.
The half-evergreen nature of this plant makes it valuable for year-round structure in the garden.
When grown in mild climates, the species behaves as a half-evergreen rather than dropping all its leaves.

Synset Examples

Brambles or blackberry are deciduous, semi-evergreen scrambling shrub

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: evergreen - retaining leaves throughout the entire year
  • Degree Lower: deciduous - shedding leaves annually
