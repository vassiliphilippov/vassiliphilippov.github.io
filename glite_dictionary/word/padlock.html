padlock

1. NOUN

/ ˈpædˌlɑk / (AmE), / ˈpædˌlɒk / (BrE)
a detachable lock with a hinged shackle, used for securing gates, doors, or containers and which can be fastened by snapping it shut
detachable lock with a hinged shackle

Examples

He used a padlock to secure his bicycle to the fence.
The storage unit was protected by a heavy padlock on the door.
She forgot the combination for the padlock on her locker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lock - mechanical device used to fasten something securely

Etymology

From pad (of uncertain origin) + lock (from Old English loc).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03880277-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈpædˌlɑk / (AmE), / ˈpædˌlɒk / (BrE)
to fasten or secure something with a padlock
to fasten with a padlock

Examples

They decided to padlock the gates every night for security reasons.
After hours, the owner padlocks the door to prevent theft.
She padlocked her bicycle to the metal railing before going inside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lock - to fasten or secure with a lock

Etymology

From 'padlock', originally from Middle English 'padlok', of uncertain origin, possibly referring to the movable link or fastening.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01344694-v
Synset POS: v
