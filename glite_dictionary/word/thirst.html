thirst

1. NOUN

/ θɝst / (AmE), / θɜːst / (BrE)
the feeling of needing or wanting to drink something; a physiological need for liquid
feeling of needing to drink

Examples

After running in the sun, her thirst became unbearable.
He drank a glass of water to quench his thirst.
During the drought, people suffered a constant thirst for clean water.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hunger - a physiological need for food

Etymology

From Old English 'thurst', related to Old High German 'durst' and Latin 'turgescere' (to become swollen).

2. VERB

/ θɜrst / (AmE), / θɜːst / (BrE)
to feel the need to drink liquid; to experience thirst
to feel the need to drink

Examples

After running for an hour in the sun, I really thirst for a cold glass of water.
Hikers often thirst in the desert due to the dry heat.
She thirsted all day because she forgot her water bottle.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hunger - to feel the need to eat
  • Synonym: to crave - to have a strong desire for something

Etymology

From Old English *þyrstan* (to thirst), related to *þurst* (thirst).

3. NOUN

/ θɜrst / (AmE), / θɜːst / (BrE)
a strong desire or craving for something, especially not related to food or drink
strong desire or craving (not related to drink)

Examples

Though he achieved much, his thirst for success remained unquenched.
Her thirst for adventure led her to travel the world.
Many writers are driven by a thirst for recognition and understanding.

Synset Examples

a thirst for knowledge
hunger for affection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hunger - a strong desire for something (especially not food)

Etymology

Old English 'thurst', from Proto-Germanic *thurstu- (desire, thirst).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04952431-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ θɜrst / (AmE), / θɜːst / (BrE)
to strongly desire or yearn for something, especially in a figurative sense
to strongly desire or yearn for

Examples

She thirsts for adventure and new experiences.
After years of neglect, the community thirsted for change.
He found himself thirsting for knowledge that books alone could not provide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to crave - to feel a powerful desire for something
  • Synonym: to yearn - to have an intense feeling of longing for something
  • Antonym: to loathe - to feel intense dislike or disgust for

Etymology

From Old English 'thurstian' (to thirst), related to the noun 'thirst'.
