seamed

1. ADJ

/ siːmd / (AmE), / siːmd / (BrE)
having or joined together by a seam or seams
having a seam or seams

Examples

The old leather boots were seamed and cracked from years of use.
Her hands were seamed with age and hard work.
The jacket was carefully seamed along the shoulders for durability.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: seamless - having no seams; smoothly continuous
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02261720-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ siːmd / (AmE), / siːmd / (BrE)
(especially of skin) marked with lines, ridges, or scars resembling seams.
marked with lines or seams (esp. skin)

Examples

His cheeks were seamed by years of hard labor under the sun.
The old man's seamed hands told stories of a lifetime of work.
Her seamed forehead reflected the hardships she had endured.

Synset Examples

their lined faces were immeasurably sad
a seamed face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lined - marked with lines or wrinkles (especially on the face)
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even texture or surface without lines or seams

Etymology

From the verb 'seam' meaning to mark with a line or scar, from Old English 'seam' (a sewing line).
