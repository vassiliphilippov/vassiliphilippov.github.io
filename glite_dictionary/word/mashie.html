mashie

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæʃi / (AmE), / ˈmaʃi / (BrE)
an iron golf club, formerly used for middle-distance shots, roughly equivalent to a modern 5-iron
historical golf club for middle-distance shots
archaic

Examples

He used a mashie to approach the green from 150 yards out.
In early golf tournaments, the mashie was a common club for careful approach shots.
Collectors often seek out antique mashies as part of classic golf sets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iron - a type of golf club with a flat metal head

Etymology

Perhaps from Scottish, of uncertain origin, dating from the late 19th century.
