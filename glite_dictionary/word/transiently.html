transiently

1. ADV

/ ˈtræn.zi.ənt.li / (AmE), / ˈtræn.zi.ənt.li / (BrE)
For only a short period; temporarily or briefly.
for a short time; temporarily

Examples

The electrical circuit was transiently disrupted during the test.
Symptoms may transiently worsen before improving with this treatment.
The server was transiently unavailable due to scheduled maintenance.

Synset Examples

these three pions may actually be joined together transiently as a compound particle during the interchange process

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temporarily - for a short time; not permanently
  • Antonym: permanently - in a way that lasts or remains forever

Etymology

From 'transient' (from Latin 'transient-', present participle of 'transire' meaning 'to go across') + adverbial suffix '-ly'.
