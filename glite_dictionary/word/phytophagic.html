phytophagic

1. ADJ

/ faɪtəˈfeɪdʒɪk / (AmE), / fʌɪtəˈfeɪdʒɪk / (BrE)
(of animals) feeding on plants; herbivorous.
feeding on plants (of animals)

Examples

Many insect species are phytophagic, consuming leaves, stems, or roots as their primary food source.
The caterpillar is considered phytophagic because its diet consists solely of plant material.
Researchers observed a phytophagic beetle population thriving in the new greenhouse environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herbivorous - feeding on plants (general adjective)

Etymology

From Greek 'phyto-' (plant) and '-phagos' (eating).
