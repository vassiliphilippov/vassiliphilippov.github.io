Annona reticulata

1. NOUN

/ əˈnoʊnə ˌrɛtɪkjuːˈleɪtə / (AmE), / əˈnəʊnə ˌrɛtɪkjʊˈlɑːtə / (BrE)
A small tropical American tree that bears a bristly, heart-shaped, acidic fruit, also known as the custard apple or bullock's heart.
tropical tree with heart-shaped fruit

Examples

The Annona reticulata thrives in warm, humid climates and produces edible fruit each year.
Farmers in Central America often cultivate Annona reticulata for its nutritious fruit.
Botanists studied the unique leaf patterns and fruit texture of Annona reticulata.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: custard apple - the fruit of Annona reticulata; edible tropical fruit

Etymology

From Latin: 'Annona' (genus of tropical trees) and 'reticulata' (net-like, referring to the fruit's surface).
