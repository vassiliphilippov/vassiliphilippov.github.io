Pseudotaxus

1. NOUN

/ suː.doʊˈtæksəs / (AmE), / sjuː.dəʊˈtæksəs / (BrE)
A genus of yew-like evergreen shrubs, containing only Pseudotaxus chienii, known as the white-berry yew, native to China.
genus of evergreen shrubs (white-berry yew)

Examples

The rare Pseudotaxus is valued by horticulturists for its unique white berries.
Botanists studied the growth pattern of Pseudotaxus in its native habitat in China.
A specimen of Pseudotaxus was planted in the botanical garden for conservation purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Taxus - genus of true yews

Etymology

From Greek ‘pseudo-’ (false) + Taxus (genus of yew trees), indicating its resemblance to true yews.

2. NOUN

/ suːdoʊˈtæksəs / (AmE), / sjuːdəʊˈtæksəs / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen conifers in the yew family (Taxaceae), containing only the species Pseudotaxus chienii, known as the white-berry yew, native to China.
genus of evergreen conifers, white-berry yew

Examples

The Pseudotaxus is distinguished from other yew genera by its distinctive white berries.
Botanists have identified only one species within the genus Pseudotaxus.
Pseudotaxus is native to forests in southeastern China and valued as an ornamental tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Taxus - genus of true yews

Etymology

From Greek pseudo- (false) + Latin taxus (yew), referring to its resemblance to true yews.
