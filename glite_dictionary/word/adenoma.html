adenoma

1. NOUN

/ ˌæd.əˈnoʊ.mə / (AmE), / ˌad.ɪˈnəʊ.mə / (BrE)
A benign tumor formed from glandular tissue.
benign tumor of glandular tissue
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the growth as a adenoma after reviewing the biopsy results.
Most adenomas are noncancerous, but they can sometimes become malignant if untreated.
The patient underwent surgery to remove an adrenal adenoma that was causing hormonal imbalance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: carcinoma - a malignant tumor of epithelial origin

Etymology

From Greek 'adēn' (gland) + '-oma' (tumor).
