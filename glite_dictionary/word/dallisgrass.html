dallisgrass

1. NOUN

/ ˈdælɪsˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈdalɪsˌɡras / (BrE)
a tall, tufted perennial grass native to tropical America, commonly used as pasture and forage grass and naturalized in the southern United States
a pasture and forage grass in the southern U.S.

Examples

The ranchers planted dallisgrass to provide grazing for their cattle.
Dallisgrass can quickly take over lawns in warm, humid climates.
Efforts to control dallisgrass often involve both manual removal and herbicides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crabgrass - a common lawn weed grass

Etymology

Named after A.T. Dallis, a 19th-century American farmer who promoted its use.
