relatively

1. ADV

/ ˈrɛl.ə.tɪv.li / (AmE), / ˈrɛl.ə.tɪv.li / (BrE)
in a way that is compared to something else or considered in relation to something else, rather than independently
in comparison to something else

Examples

The new software is relatively easy to use compared to the old version.
The patient is relatively healthy for someone of his age.
Although the exam was difficult, she found it relatively straightforward.

Synset Examples

the situation is relatively calm now

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comparatively - to a moderate degree as compared to something else

Etymology

From 'relative' + adverbial suffix '-ly'; 'relative' from Latin 'relativus' (having reference or relation).
