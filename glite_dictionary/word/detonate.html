to detonate

1. VERB

/ ˈdɛtəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛtəneɪt / (BrE)
to cause something, especially an explosive, to explode with a sudden and violent release of energy
cause to explode violently

Examples

The technicians carefully detonated the old landmine.
Authorities decided to detonate the suspicious package as a precaution.
The fireworks were detonated at midnight to celebrate the holiday.

Synset Examples

We exploded the nuclear bomb

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to explode - to burst or cause to burst violently and noisily

Etymology

From Latin 'detonare' (to thunder down), from 'de-' (down) + 'tonare' (to thunder).

2. VERB

/ ˈdɛtəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛt.ə.neɪt / (BrE)
to explode or cause to explode suddenly and violently, releasing energy, especially as a result of a chemical reaction
explode or cause to explode violently

Examples

The demolition crew prepared the charges to detonate the old building safely.
Authorities evacuated the area before the device could detonate.
The fireworks are designed to detonate at a specific altitude for maximum effect.

Synset Examples

the bomb detonated at noon
The Molotov cocktail exploded

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to explode - to burst or cause to burst with a loud sound and force
  • Antonym: to defuse - to remove the fuse from a bomb, thereby preventing it from exploding

Etymology

From Latin 'detonare' (to thunder down), from 'de-' (down) + 'tonare' (to thunder).
