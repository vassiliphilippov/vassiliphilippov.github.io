FDR

1. PROPN

/ ˌɛf.diːˈɑr / (AmE), / ˌɛf.diːˈɑː / (BrE)
the abbreviation for Franklin D. Roosevelt, the 32nd President of the United States (1882–1945), who led the country during the Great Depression and World War II
abbreviation for Franklin D. Roosevelt, U.S. president

Examples

Many historians consider FDR one of the most influential American presidents of the twentieth century.
During FDR's presidency, the New Deal programs significantly changed the role of government.
People often quote FDR's famous line: 'The only thing we have to fear is fear itself.'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Franklin D. Roosevelt - 32nd President of the United States (1882–1945)

Etymology

Initialism of Franklin Delano Roosevelt.
