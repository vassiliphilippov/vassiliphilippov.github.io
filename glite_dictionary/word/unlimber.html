to unlimber

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈlɪmbər / (AmE), / ʌnˈlɪmbə / (BrE)
to loosen or prepare the body or limbs for movement or action, especially through gentle stretching or exercise
loosen body or limbs for movement

Examples

After sitting at his desk all morning, Mark decided to unlimber his legs with a short walk.
The yoga instructor encouraged everyone to unlimber before starting the session.
It's important to unlimber your muscles to prevent injuries during exercise.

Synset Examples

She took a moment to unlimber her legs after the long flight.
The dancers had to unlimber their bodies for the performance.
You need to unlimber your arms before the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to warm up - to prepare the body for exercise by gentle activity
  • Synonym: to limber up - to make one's body more flexible before activity

Etymology

Originally from military use meaning 'to detach a gun from its limber'; now used figuratively for preparing the body.
