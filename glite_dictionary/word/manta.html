manta

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæntə / (AmE), / ˈmæntə / (BrE)
A very large species of tropical ray with triangular pectoral fins, which feeds on plankton and small fish and is generally harmless.
large tropical ray (fish)

Examples

Divers were thrilled to spot a manta gliding gracefully through the clear blue water.
The manta can reach a width of over six meters, making it one of the largest rays in the ocean.
Harpooning a manta is dangerous because of its massive size and power.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manta ray - another term for manta

Etymology

From Spanish 'manta' (cloak), referring to the shape of the ray.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæn.tə / (AmE), / ˈmæn.tə / (BrE)
A large woolen blanket or wrap, traditionally used as a cloak or shawl, especially in Spanish-speaking regions.
a large traditional blanket or shawl

Examples

Maria wrapped a colorful manta around her shoulders against the evening chill.
The vendor sold handwoven mantas at the market, each with unique patterns.
He admired the intricate designs embroidered on the traditional manta.

Etymology

From Spanish 'manta' meaning blanket, ultimately from Late Latin 'mantum' (cloak).
