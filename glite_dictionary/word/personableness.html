personableness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrsənəblnəs / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsənəblnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being pleasant and attractive in social situations; having a likable and engaging manner
quality of being personable or socially attractive

Examples

Her personableness made her a favorite among both colleagues and clients.
Many leaders succeed because their personableness helps them connect with others easily.
We were struck by his personableness, which made the meeting far more enjoyable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: personability - the quality of being personable; likable social character
  • Antonym: unpleasantness - the state of being unpleasant or disagreeable in social situations

Etymology

From 'personable' + '-ness', where 'personable' derives from Middle English, meaning pleasing in person.
