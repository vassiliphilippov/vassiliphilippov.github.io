incompetence

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkɑːmpətəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɒmpɪtəns / (BrE)
The inability to do something successfully or as it should be done, due to lack of ability or qualifications.
lack of ability or qualifications

Examples

His incompetence as a manager led to several missed opportunities for the company.
The pilot was dismissed due to gross incompetence during an emergency landing.
She blamed the delays on the incompetence of the technical support staff.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: competence - the ability to do something successfully or efficiently

Etymology

From Latin incompetentia (not suitable), from in- (not) + competentia (fitness, suitability).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkɑːmpətəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɒmpɪtəns / (BrE)
(Medical) The inability of a bodily organ or part to perform its normal function properly.
organ's failure to function properly (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient's mitral valve incompetence led to abnormal blood flow within the heart.
Doctors diagnosed adrenal gland incompetence after observing symptoms of hormone imbalance.
Sphincter incompetence can result in urinary incontinence in elderly patients.

Etymology

From Latin incompetens (not suitable, not adequate).
