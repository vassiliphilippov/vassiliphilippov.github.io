Mastodontidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæs.toʊˈdɒn.tɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌmæs.təˈdɒn.tɪˌdiː / (BrE)
an extinct family of large, elephant-like mammals, commonly known as mastodons, that lived from the late Miocene to the end of the Pleistocene.
extinct family of prehistoric mammals (mastodons)

Examples

Fossils of the Mastodontidae have been found across North America and Eurasia.
The Mastodontidae were distinguished by their unique molar teeth structure.
Many museums display skeletons of the prehistoric Mastodontidae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mastodon - an extinct elephant-like mammal belonging to Mastodontidae
  • Degree Lower: Mammutidae - a related extinct family of proboscidean mammals

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Mastodon' (the type genus) and the family suffix '-idae'.
