wright

1. NOUN

/ raɪt / (AmE), / raɪt / (BrE)
A person who makes or repairs something, especially used in combination with another word referring to what is made or repaired.
maker or repairer, usually in combinations

Examples

As a skilled wright, he crafted furniture that lasted for generations.
In medieval times, a ship wright was essential to naval trade and exploration.
The village engaged a wheel wright to fix the broken wagons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: playwright - a person who writes plays
  • Synonym: shipwright - a person who builds or repairs ships

Etymology

From Old English 'wyrhta' (worker, maker).
