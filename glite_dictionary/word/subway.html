subway

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.weɪ / (BrE)
An underground tunnel or passage designed for pedestrians to cross beneath a road or railway.
underground pedestrian passage
british

Examples

The city installed a new subway so pedestrians could safely cross the busy highway.
We took shelter in a subway during the sudden thunderstorm.
In London, you often need to use a subway to reach the other side of the street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: underpass - a passage under a road or railway for vehicles or pedestrians

Etymology

From 'sub-' meaning 'under' and 'way', first used in the early 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.weɪ / (BrE)
an underground railway system in a city, operated by electric trains
underground city railway system

Examples

She takes the subway to work every morning because it's faster than driving.
Tourists often get confused by the subway map during their first visit to New York City.
The city has invested in expanding its subway system to reduce traffic congestion.

Synset Examples

in Paris the subway system is called the ‘metro’ and in London it is called the ‘tube’ or the ‘underground’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metro - an urban rapid transit railway system, especially underground
  • Synonym: underground - the underground railway system, especially in London
  • Synonym: tube - the underground railway system in London

Etymology

From sub- (under) + way; first used in late 19th century for underground roads or passages.
