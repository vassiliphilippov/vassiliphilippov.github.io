spectacular

1. NOUN

/ spɛkˈtækjələr / (AmE), / spɛkˈtækjʊlə / (BrE)
a lavish and impressive show or performance, often with elaborate effects
lavish, impressive performance or show

Examples

The Las Vegas spectacular attracted thousands of tourists every night.
This year's holiday spectacular was even grander than the last.
The director is known for producing a Broadway spectacular each season.

Synset Examples

they put on a Christmas spectacular

Related Terms

  • Synonym: show - an entertainment or performance for an audience

Etymology

From Latin spectaculum (a public show).
