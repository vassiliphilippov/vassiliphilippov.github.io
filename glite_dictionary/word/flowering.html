flowering

1. ADJ

/ ˈflaʊ.ɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflaʊ.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a plant) producing flowers; bearing blossoms or blooms.
producing flowers or blooms

Examples

The flowering shrubs brightened up the entire garden in spring.
This species belongs to the cactus family but is actually a flowering plant.
She specializes in photographing flowering trees throughout the seasons.

Synset Examples

a flowering plant

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonflowering - (of a plant) not producing flowers

Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈflaʊ.ɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflaʊ.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or period during which a plant develops flowers; the act of producing blossoms
development of flowers on a plant

Examples

The cherry trees are at their flowering peak in early April.
Spring marks the flowering of many wild plants in the countryside.
This rose bush has a long flowering season lasting throughout the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blooming - the process of producing flowers

Etymology

From the verb 'flower' + -ing, originally from Old French 'flur' (flower) via Latin 'florem' (flower).

3. NOUN

/ ˈflaʊ.ɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflaʊ.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
The development or reaching of a peak, especially in a culture, art, or intellectual activity; a period of full maturity or achievement.
the peak or development stage, especially culturally

Examples

The Renaissance was known as the flowering of European art and culture.
We have witnessed the flowering of his poetic talent over the years.
The city experienced a flowering of creativity during the period of prosperity.

Synset Examples

the flowering of antebellum culture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blossoming - The process of coming into full development or fruition.
  • Antonym: decline - A gradual decrease or loss in strength or quality; the opposite state after a peak.

Etymology

From 'flower' + '-ing'; figurative use dates to mid-19th century.
