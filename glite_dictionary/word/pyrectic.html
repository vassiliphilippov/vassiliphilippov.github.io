pyrectic

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈrɛktɪk / (AmE), / paɪˈrɛktɪk / (BrE)
a substance that causes an increase in body temperature
substance causing fever
medical

Examples

The researchers tested several pyrectics to observe their effect on body temperature.
Exposure to certain pyrectic agents can result in fever-like symptoms in laboratory animals.
Doctors monitor patients closely when administering a pyrectic to avoid dangerous spikes in temperature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyrogen - substance that induces fever

Etymology

From Greek 'pyretos' (fever) + '-ic'.

2. ADJ

/ paɪˈrɛktɪk / (AmE), / paɪˈrɛktɪk / (BrE)
Relating to, having, or causing fever.
having or causing fever
medical

Examples

The patient developed a pyrectic reaction after receiving the medication.
His pyrectic symptoms persisted for several days despite treatment.
The doctor noted a pyrectic episode in the patient's medical chart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: febrile - having or showing the symptoms of a fever

Etymology

From Greek pyr (fire, fever) + -ektos (thrown out).
