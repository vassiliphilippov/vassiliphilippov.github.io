Arisarum vulgare

1. NOUN

/ ˌærɪˈsɑːrəm vʊlˈɡɛəri / (AmE), / ˌærɪˈsarəm vʊlˈɡɛːri / (BrE)
A tuberous perennial plant with a cowl-shaped maroon or violet-black spathe, native to the Mediterranean region, Canary Islands, and Azores.
Mediterranean perennial plant

Examples

The shady woodland was dotted with Arisarum vulgare blooming among the fallen leaves.
Botanists observed that Arisarum vulgare thrives in well-drained Mediterranean soils.
In spring, the maroon spathes of Arisarum vulgare add rich color to the garden.

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining the genus name Arisarum (from Greek arisaron, a type of arum) and Latin vulgare (common).
