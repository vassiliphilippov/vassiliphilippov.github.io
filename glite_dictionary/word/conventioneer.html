conventioneer

1. NOUN

/ kənˌvɛn(t)ʃəˈnɪr / (AmE), / kənˌvɛn(t)ʃəˈnɪə / (BrE)
a person who attends a convention, especially one held for members of a profession, association, or interest group
person attending a convention

Examples

Hundreds of conventioneers filled the hotel lobby during the annual tech conference.
The city prepares special events each summer for visiting conventioneers.
He registered as a conventioneer for the medical convention in Chicago.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delegate - a person chosen or elected to act for others, especially at a conference or meeting

Etymology

Formed from 'convention' and the suffix '-eer', denoting a person associated with a particular activity.
