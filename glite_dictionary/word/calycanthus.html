Calycanthus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkælɪˈkænθəs / (AmE), / ˌkælɪˈkanθəs / (BrE)
A genus of aromatic shrubs, native to North America and East Asia, with showy, often fragrant flowers, commonly called sweetshrub or Carolina allspice.
genus of aromatic shrubs (sweetshrub)

Examples

The Calycanthus in her garden filled the air with a spicy fragrance in spring.
Botanists admire the unusual flowers of Calycanthus for their dark crimson petals.
Many gardeners plant Calycanthus shrubs for their attractive appearance and aromatic scent.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'kalyx' (calyx) and 'anthos' (flower).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkælɪˈkænθəs / (AmE), / ˌkælɪˈkanθəs / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous shrubs in the family Calycanthaceae, known for their aromatic flowers and commonly called sweetshrubs or allspice.
genus of aromatic shrubs (sweetshrub)

Examples

The fragrant flowers of the Calycanthus are often planted in gardens for their pleasant aroma.
Several species within the Calycanthus genus are native to North America.
Botanists studied the morphological differences between two varieties of Calycanthus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allspice - common name for plants of the genus Calycanthus

Etymology

From Greek 'kalyx' (calyx, flower cup) + 'anthos' (flower).
