centroid

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntrɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrɔɪd / (BrE)
the center of mass of a geometric object of uniform density; the point at which it could be balanced.
center of mass of a shape or object

Examples

The centroid of a triangle is the intersection of its medians.
Engineers calculated the centroid to ensure the structure would remain balanced.
In computer graphics, finding the centroid helps in object detection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: center of gravity - the point where the entire weight of an object acts

Etymology

Formed from Greek 'kentron' (center) and 'eidos' (form).
