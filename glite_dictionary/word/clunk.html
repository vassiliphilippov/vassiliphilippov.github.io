clunk

1. VERB

/ klʌŋk / (AmE), / klʌŋk / (BrE)
to make or move with a dull, heavy sound, like that of a horse's hooves striking the ground
to make a dull, heavy sound

Examples

The old elevator clunked up to the tenth floor before shuddering to a stop.
He clunked across the porch in his heavy boots.
As the car shifted gears, the transmission clunked loudly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clatter - to make a loud, rattling sound
  • Synonym: thud - to make a dull sound by striking something heavily
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02189411-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ klʌŋk / (AmE), / klʌŋk / (BrE)
a heavy, dull sound produced by the impact of heavy objects
a heavy, dull impact sound

Examples

The door closed with a loud clunk as the wind slammed it shut.
He dropped the toolbox on the floor, and it made a heavy clunk.
There was a metallic clunk every time the old engine started.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clang - a loud, resonant metallic sound
  • Synonym: thud - a dull, heavy sound, usually of something falling

Etymology

Imitative of the sound it describes; similar to 'clink' and 'clank,' with onomatopoeic origin.
