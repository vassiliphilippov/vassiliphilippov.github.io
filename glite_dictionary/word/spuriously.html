spuriously

1. ADV

/ ˈspjʊriəsli / (AmE), / ˈspjʊəriəsli / (BrE)
In a false, illegitimate, or deceitful manner; not genuinely or validly.
in a false or deceitful way

Examples

The evidence was spuriously presented to make the case seem stronger than it was.
He claimed, spuriously, that he had never seen the document before.
Some arguments are constructed spuriously to mislead the public.

Synset Examples

the lawyer argued spuriously that his client knew nothing of the burglary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: falsely - in a manner that is not true or genuine
  • Antonym: genuinely - in a sincere and real manner

Etymology

From spurious + -ly, with 'spurious' from Latin 'spurius' (illegitimate).
