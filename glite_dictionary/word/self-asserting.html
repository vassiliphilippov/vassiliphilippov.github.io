self-asserting

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlf əˈsɜrtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf əˈsɜːtɪŋ / (BrE)
aggressively confident in expressing one's opinions or asserting oneself
aggressively self-assured

Examples

Her self-asserting manner sometimes intimidated her colleagues during meetings.
The self-asserting statements made it clear he would not back down easily.
People appreciated her self-asserting attitude when negotiating contracts.

Synset Examples

an energetic assertive boy who was always ready to argue
pointing directly at a listener is an assertive act

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assertive - confidently self-assured; not aggressive
  • Antonym: submissive - willing to yield to others; meek or passive

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' and the participle of 'assert', ultimately from Latin 'assertus'.
