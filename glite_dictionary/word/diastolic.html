diastolic

1. ADJ

/ ˌdaɪəˈstɑːlɪk / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəˈstɒlɪk / (BrE)
relating to the phase of the heartbeat when the heart muscle relaxes and allows the chambers to fill with blood
relating to the heart's relaxation phase
medical

Examples

Her diastolic blood pressure was higher than normal at her last check-up.
The doctor explained the difference between systolic and diastolic readings.
A low diastolic number may indicate that your heart is not relaxing properly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: systolic - relating to the phase of the heartbeat when the heart contracts

Etymology

From Greek diastolē (dilatation, interval) via Late Latin diastolicus.
