unarmed

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɑrmd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɑːmd / (BrE)
not carrying or equipped with weapons.
not carrying weapons

Examples

The protestors marched unarmed to show they meant no harm.
He walked into the meeting unarmed, trusting everyone there.
The police found the suspect was unarmed when they arrested him.

Synset Examples

went alone and unarmed
unarmed peasants were shot down
unarmed vehicles

Related Terms

  • Antonym: armed - carrying or equipped with weapons

Etymology

From the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'armed' (equipped with weapons).

WordNet ID: oewn-00144185-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɑrmd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɑːmd / (BrE)
(of plants or animals) lacking barbs, stings, or thorns.
not having barbs, stings, or thorns

Examples

The rose variety is unarmed and safer to handle than others with sharp thorns.
Some bees are unarmed, meaning they lack a stinger and cannot sting humans.
This cactus is unarmed, making it a popular choice for gardens with children.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: armed - (of plants or animals) provided with barbs, stings, or thorns

Etymology

From un- (not) + armed (provided with arms or weapons).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00147263-a
Synset POS: a
