spurned

1. ADJ

/ spɝːnd / (AmE), / spɜːnd / (BrE)
Rejected or rebuffed, especially by a lover, often suddenly and without warning.
rejected or rebuffed, especially in love

Examples

After years together, she felt utterly spurned when he left without explanation.
Many spurned lovers struggle to move on from heartbreak.
He wrote poetry about his experiences as a spurned admirer.

Synset Examples

jilted at the altar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jilted - suddenly rejected, especially by a lover

Etymology

Past participle of 'spurn', from Old English *spurnan* (to kick away).
