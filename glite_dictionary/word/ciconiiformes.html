Ciconiiformes

1. NOUN

/ sɪˌkoʊniˈɪfɔrmiːz / (AmE), / sɪˌkəʊniˈɪfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of chiefly tropical, marsh-dwelling wading birds with long legs and bills, including herons, storks, spoonbills, flamingos, and ibises
order of long-legged wading birds (herons, storks etc.)

Examples

The Ciconiiformes are found in marshes and wetlands around the world.
Scientists debated the classification of flamingos within the Ciconiiformes.
Herons and storks are both members of the order Ciconiiformes.

Etymology

From modern Latin Ciconiiformes, from Latin ciconia (stork) + -formes (having the form of).

2. NOUN

/ saɪˌkoʊni.əˈfɔːrmiːz / (AmE), / sɪˌkəʊnɪ.ɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of chiefly tropical, marsh-dwelling, fish-eating wading birds with long legs and bills, including herons, storks, spoonbills, flamingos, and ibises
an order of wading birds (herons, storks, flamingos, etc.)

Examples

There are several species of Ciconiiformes found in the wetlands of Africa.
The migration patterns of Ciconiiformes have long fascinated ornithologists.
Herons, a type of Ciconiiformes, are commonly seen wading in shallow waters.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on 'Ciconia' (the stork genus) and the suffix '-formes' meaning 'having the form of'.
