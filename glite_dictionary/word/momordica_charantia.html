Momordica charantia

1. NOUN

/ moʊˌmɔːrdɪkə kəˈræntiə / (AmE), / məʊˌmɔːdɪkə kəˈræntɪə / (BrE)
A tropical vine of the gourd family cultivated for its edible yellow-orange fruit, commonly known as bitter melon.
a tropical vine; bitter melon

Examples

Momordica charantia is widely grown in Asia and Africa for its edible fruit.
The fruit of Momordica charantia is often used in traditional medicines.
Researchers are studying Momordica charantia for its potential health benefits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bitter melon - the common name for Momordica charantia, an edible tropical vine fruit

Etymology

From Latin genus name Momordica and species charantia, used in botanical nomenclature.
