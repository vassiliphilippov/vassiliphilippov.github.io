Euphausia pacifica

1. NOUN

/ juːˌfeɪʒiə pəˈsɪfɪkə / (AmE), / juːˌfeɪziə pəˈsɪfɪkə / (BrE)
a species of small, shrimp-like crustacean known as North Pacific krill, important as a food source in marine ecosystems
species of North Pacific krill

Examples

Euphausia pacifica serves as a crucial food source for many marine animals, including whales and fish.
Scientists noted a decline in Euphausia pacifica populations in response to changing ocean temperatures.
The abundance of Euphausia pacifica supports a diverse ecosystem in the North Pacific Ocean.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Euphausia', the genus name, and 'pacifica', meaning 'of the Pacific'.
