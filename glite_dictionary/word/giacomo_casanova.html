Giacomo Casanova

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒɑːkəˌmoʊ ˌkæsəˈnoʊvə / (AmE), / ˈdʒækəməʊ ˌkæsəˈnəʊvə / (BrE)
(1725–1798) An Italian adventurer and memoirist famous for his romantic exploits and vivid accounts of his life.
Italian adventurer and memoirist

Examples

Many biographies have been written about Giacomo Casanova and his adventurous life.
The museum in Venice features artifacts related to Giacomo Casanova's travels and romances.
Historians credit Giacomo Casanova with providing a unique insight into 18th-century European society through his memoirs.

Etymology

Italian personal name Giacomo Casanova.
