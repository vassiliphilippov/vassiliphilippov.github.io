to downplay

1. VERB

/ ˈdaʊnˌpleɪ / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌpleɪ / (BrE)
to make something seem less important or significant than it really is
make seem less important

Examples

During the interview, he tried to downplay his involvement in the scandal.
The company spokesperson attempted to downplay the risks associated with the new product.
She always downplays her accomplishments, preferring to let her work speak for itself.

Synset Examples

he played down his royal ancestry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to minimize - to reduce something to the smallest possible degree
  • Antonym: to exaggerate - to represent something as being larger or more important than it really is

Etymology

Formed from 'down' + 'play', meaning to play down, i.e., to lower in emphasis.

2. VERB

/ ˈdaʊnˌpleɪ / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌpleɪ / (BrE)
to make something seem less important or serious than it is
minimize the importance of

Examples

The company tried to downplay the impact of the scandal in its official statement.
She tends to downplay her achievements, even though she's very successful.
Officials attempted to downplay the seriousness of the situation to avoid public panic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to play down - to make something seem less important or serious
  • Antonym: to emphasize - to give special importance or prominence to something

Etymology

Formed from 'down' + 'play', modeled on 'play down'.
