Erse

1. NOUN

/ ɜrs / (AmE), / ɜːs / (BrE)
the Gaelic language of Scotland or Ireland, especially Scottish Gaelic; also used historically to refer to Irish Gaelic
Gaelic language of Scotland or Ireland

Examples

He is studying Erse as part of his research on Celtic languages.
Many poems were originally written in Erse and later translated to English.
The revival of Erse has become an important part of Scottish cultural identity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gaelic - the Celtic language of Scotland or Ireland

Etymology

From Scots, alteration of Irish or Scottish Gaelic 'Gàidhlig' (Gaelic), originally used to distinguish 'Irish' (language) from English or Scots.
