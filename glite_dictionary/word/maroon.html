maroon

1. NOUN

/ məˈruːn / (AmE), / məˈruːn / (BrE)
a type of exploding firework used as a warning signal, especially in emergencies or to alert rescue teams
exploding firework warning signal

Examples

The coastguard fired a maroon to alert the crew to the emergency.
Hearing the loud explosion of the maroon, everyone gathered at the dock for instructions.
A maroon was set off to signal the beginning of the rescue operation.

Etymology

Possibly from French 'maron' (chestnut), referring to the sound or form, but exact origin unclear.

4. NOUN

/ məˈruːn / (AmE), / məˈruːn / (BrE)
a person who has been left stranded, especially on an island, and unable to leave
a stranded person, especially on an island

Examples

After the shipwreck, the only other maroon I saw was waving desperately from the shore.
The story follows a maroon and his struggle to survive alone on a deserted island.
Years later, the maroon was found alive, having built a shelter from palm leaves.

Synset Examples

when the tide came in I was a maroon out there

Related Terms

  • Synonym: castaway - a person who has been shipwrecked and stranded

Etymology

From the practice of marooning, itself from Spanish 'cimarrón' meaning 'wild' or 'escaped slave'.

/ məˈruːn / (AmE), / məˈruːn / (BrE)
to leave someone in a place from which they cannot escape and with little hope of being rescued

Examples

He was accidentally marooned in the small town when his car broke down.
During the mutiny, the captain was marooned on a remote rock with only a small supply of food.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
