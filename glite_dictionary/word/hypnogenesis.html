hypnogenesis

1. NOUN

/ hɪp.noʊˈdʒɛn.ɪsɪs / (AmE), / hɪp.nəʊˈdʒɛn.ɪsɪs / (BrE)
the process or act of inducing sleep or hypnosis
induction of sleep or hypnosis
medical

Examples

The doctor used hypnogenesis to help the patient overcome anxiety.
Studying hypnogenesis can reveal much about the mechanisms of sleep induction.
Early research into hypnogenesis led to advancements in hypnosis therapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypnosis - a trance-like state of focused attention and increased suggestibility

Etymology

From Greek hypnos (sleep) + genesis (origin, production).
