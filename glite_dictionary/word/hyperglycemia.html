hyperglycemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpərɡlaɪˈsiːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəɡlʌɪˈsiːmɪə / (BrE)
An abnormally high concentration of glucose in the blood, often associated with diabetes.
abnormally high blood sugar
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that hyperglycemia can cause serious health problems if left untreated.
After eating too many sugary snacks, Kevin experienced a sudden bout of hyperglycemia.
Managing diet and exercise is crucial for people trying to avoid hyperglycemia.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypoglycemia - abnormally low blood sugar

Etymology

From Greek 'hyper-' (over, excessive) + 'glyc-' (sweet) + '-emia' (condition of the blood).
