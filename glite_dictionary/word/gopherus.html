Gopherus

1. PROPN

/ ɡoʊˈfɛrəs / (AmE), / ˌɡəʊˈfɛr.əs / (BrE)
A genus of burrowing tortoises native to North America, commonly known as gopher tortoises.
genus of North American burrowing tortoises

Examples

The Gopherus genus includes several species adapted to life in arid environments.
Scientists studied the burrowing behavior of Gopherus in the southeastern United States.
Conservation programs have been established to protect Gopherus populations threatened by habitat loss.

Etymology

From Latinized form of 'gopher' (an American English word for the animal) plus Latin '-us', used in taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ goʊˈfɛr.əs / (AmE), / gəʊˈfɛr.əs / (BrE)
A genus of North American tortoises known as gopher tortoises, characterized by their burrowing habits and found in arid or semi-arid regions.
a genus of North American gopher tortoises

Examples

The genus Gopherus includes tortoises adapted to digging extensive burrows.
Species in Gopherus are protected due to habitat loss.
Scientists have studied the unique shell structure of the Gopherus genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gopher tortoise - a common name for several species in the genus Gopherus

Etymology

From Modern Latin *Gopherus*, named after the common name 'gopher tortoise'.
