/ ˌtʃɑrlz ðə ˈfɜrst / (AmE), / ˌtʃɑːlz ði ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
The king of England, Scotland, and Ireland from 1625 to 1649, whose conflict with Parliament led to the English Civil War and his execution.
King of England, 1625–1649

Examples

Historians still debate the decisions made by Charles I during his tumultuous reign.
Charles I was executed in 1649 after being tried for treason by the English Parliament.
