moxie

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑksi / (AmE), / ˈmɒksi / (BrE)
Courage, determination, or force of character; the ability to face difficulty with spirit and resilience.
courage and determination
informal american

Examples

It takes real moxie to stand up to powerful people in your industry.
Despite criticism from the press, she showed a lot of moxie throughout the campaign.
That kid’s got moxie—he never backs down from a challenge.

Synset Examples

he didn't have the guts to try it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guts - courage, determination, or nerve
  • Synonym: bravery - courageous behavior or character
  • Antonym: cowardice - lack of bravery

Etymology

Originally the name of a 19th-century American soft drink, later adopted in US slang for bravery or nerve.
