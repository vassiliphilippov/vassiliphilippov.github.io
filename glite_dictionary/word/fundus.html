fundus

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌn.dəs / (AmE), / ˈfʌn.dəs / (BrE)
(anatomy) the base or part of a hollow organ that is farthest from its opening, such as in the uterus or stomach.
base of a hollow organ (anatomy)
medical

Examples

During the ultrasound, the doctor measured the fundus to assess the baby's growth.
The surgeon identified the fundus of the stomach before proceeding with the operation.
Inflammation of the uterine fundus can complicate certain pregnancies.

Synset Examples

the uterine fundus
the fundus of the stomach

Etymology

From Latin fundus (bottom, base).
