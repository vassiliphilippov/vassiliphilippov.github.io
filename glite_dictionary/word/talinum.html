Talinum

1. NOUN

/ təˈliːnəm / (AmE), / təˈlɪnəm / (BrE)
A genus of mainly American herbs with somewhat succulent stems and leaves, some species of which are cultivated as ornamentals or for food.
a genus of succulent American herbs

Examples

Many gardeners grow Talinum for its attractive foliage and small, colorful flowers.
The leaves of Talinum triangulare are sometimes used in salads or as a cooked vegetable.
Researchers studied how drought conditions affect Talinum species in arid regions.

Etymology

From New Latin Talinum, the genus name in botanical Latin, possibly from Greek 'thalos' (young shoot).

2. NOUN

/ təˈlaɪnəm / (AmE), / təˈlɪnəm / (BrE)
A genus of mainly American, somewhat succulent herbs in the family Talinaceae, some species of which are grown as ornamentals or for food.
a genus of succulent herbs

Examples

Many gardeners cultivate Talinum for its attractive, fleshy leaves and bright flowers.
The leaves of some Talinum species are consumed as a leafy vegetable in parts of the Americas.
We observed several varieties of Talinum thriving in the rocky garden beds.

Etymology

From New Latin Talinum, based on botanical naming conventions.
