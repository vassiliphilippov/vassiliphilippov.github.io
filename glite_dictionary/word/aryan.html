Aryan

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛr.i.ən / (AmE), / ˈɑː.ri.ən / (BrE)
relating to the group of ancient peoples who spoke the Indo-European languages and their descendants, originally referring to certain peoples of northern India, Iran, and Europe
relating to ancient Indo-European peoples

Examples

The Aryan migration played a significant role in shaping the culture of ancient India.
Scholars have long debated the origins and movements of the Aryan peoples.
Linguistic evidence links the Aryan tribes to early Indo-European languages.

Synset Examples

Indo-European migrations

Etymology

From Sanskrit ā́rya (noble, honorable), used as a self-designation by ancient Indo-Iranian peoples.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03085993-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛriən, ˈɛəriən / (AmE), / ˈɛːrɪən / (BrE)
a member of the prehistoric people who spoke Proto-Indo-European, regarded as the ancestors of many modern European and Indian populations
member of Proto-Indo-European people

Examples

Some historians believe the Aryan migration played a significant role in shaping ancient civilizations in Europe and Asia.
The language spoken by the Aryan peoples is considered the ancestor of many modern European tongues.
Linguists study Aryan roots to trace the origins of Indo-European languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Indo-European - relating to the family of languages descended from Proto-Indo-European

Etymology

From Sanskrit *ā́rya* (noble), later used in linguistics for the Indo-European peoples.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛəriən / (AmE), / ˈɛəriən / (BrE)
(in Nazi doctrine) a person of Caucasian, especially Northern European, descent, considered by the Nazis to be racially superior and not Jewish.
person of 'Nordic' descent (as per Nazi ideology)
offensive

Examples

According to Nazi theory, only Aryan blood was considered pure and superior.
The Nazis excluded Jews and other groups from their definition of the Aryan race.
Many pseudo-scientific texts of the time promoted the idea of Aryan supremacy, fueling discrimination and violence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Jew - a member of the people and cultural community whose traditional religion is Judaism
  • Synonym: Caucasian - a person belonging to the group of peoples traditionally classified as Caucasoid

Etymology

From Sanskrit *ā́rya* (‘noble’), later adopted by 19th-century racial theorists; used with a specific ideological meaning by the Nazis.
