whitecoat

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪtˌkoʊt / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtˌkəʊt / (BrE)
a doctor or other medical professional, especially as referred to by the white coat worn in hospitals or clinics
doctor or medical professional

Examples

The whitecoat explained the procedure clearly to ease the patient's worries.
During my hospital stay, several whitecoats checked on me every morning.
Many people feel anxious when a whitecoat approaches them with a stethoscope.

Synset Examples

The doctor in the whitecoat entered the room with a smile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doctor - a person qualified to practice medicine
  • Synonym: physician - a medical doctor

Etymology

From 'white' + 'coat', referring to the traditional white jacket worn by medical professionals.
