glitch

1. NOUN

/ ɡlɪtʃ / (AmE), / ɡlɪtʃ / (BrE)
A sudden, usually minor fault or defect in a machine, system, or computer program that causes it to function incorrectly or unexpectedly.
minor fault in a machine or system

Examples

The software update fixed the glitch that caused the program to crash.
Due to a minor glitch in the system, the payment was processed twice.
Engineers worked overnight to resolve the unexpected glitch in the power grid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malfunction - failure to function properly
  • Synonym: bug - an error or defect in software or hardware

Etymology

Origin unknown; popularized in the 1960s in engineering and computing contexts.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14488259-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ɡlɪtʃ / (AmE), / ɡlɪtʃ / (BrE)
(of a device, system, or process) to suffer a minor malfunction or unexpected problem.
to have a minor malfunction

Examples

The software began to glitch right before the update finished installing.
During the broadcast, the microphone glitched and caused a moment of silence.
If the game keeps glitching, try restarting your console.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malfunction - to fail to function properly

Etymology

Perhaps originally from Yiddish 'glitsh' (a slip), or possibly imitative.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-90015741-v
Synset POS: v
