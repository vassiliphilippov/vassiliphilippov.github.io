waxberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈwæksˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈwaksˌbɛri / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to western North America, bearing spikes of pink flowers and round white berries.
shrub with pink flowers and white berries

Examples

The waxberry thrives in rocky soils and open woodlands.
She admired the clusters of white berries on the waxberry in late summer.
Many birds enjoy eating the fruit of the waxberry shrub.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snowberry - a shrub or its white berry, closely related in genus

2. NOUN

/ ˈwæksˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈwaksˌbɛri / (BrE)
A deciduous aromatic shrub of eastern North America that bears grey-green wax-coated berries.
deciduous shrub with wax-coated berries

Examples

The waxberry produces clusters of small, aromatic berries popular with local wildlife.
In the autumn, the leaves of the waxberry turn brilliant shades of red and orange.
She planted waxberry shrubs in her garden to attract birds.
