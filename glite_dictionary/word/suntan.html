suntan

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌtæn / (AmE), / ˈsʌn.tæn / (BrE)
A browning or darkening of the skin caused by exposure to sunlight.
browning of skin from sun exposure

Examples

After a week at the beach, she had a noticeable suntan on her arms and face.
Many people use sunscreen to prevent getting a suntan or sunburn.
His golden suntan made it clear he had spent the summer outdoors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tan - a golden-brown color of the skin due to sun exposure

Etymology

Formed from 'sun' and 'tan'; 'tan' from earlier usage meaning to make brown.

2. VERB

/ ˈsʌnˌtæn / (AmE), / ˈsʌn.tæn / (BrE)
to become brown or darker in colour by exposure to the sun
become tanned by sun exposure

Examples

After a week at the beach, she managed to suntan evenly all over.
Some people suntan quickly, while others burn easily.
He prefers to suntan in the backyard rather than use artificial tanning beds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tan - to become brown or darkened by the sun
