hackberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈhækˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈhækˌbɛri / (BrE)
Any of several trees of the genus Celtis, typically with inconspicuous flowers and small berrylike fruits.
tree of the genus Celtis

Examples

The hackberry is valued for its hardy wood and ability to grow in various soil types.
Several hackberry trees shaded the edge of the old playground.
Birds are attracted to hackberry because of its plentiful berrylike fruits.

Etymology

Possibly from 'hack' (Old English 'haccian', to chop) + 'berry', referring to the fruit.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhækˌbɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈhækˌbɛr.i / (BrE)
A small edible dark purple to black berry with a large pit, produced by certain trees of the genus Celtis, native especially to the southern United States.
small edible dark purple to black berry

Examples

Many birds feed on the hackberry during the late summer months.
She picked a ripe hackberry from the tree and tasted its sweet flesh.
Old country recipes sometimes include hackberries as a wild fruit ingredient.

Etymology

From 'hack', likely a variant of the tree name, and 'berry'.
