devices

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈvaɪsɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈvaɪsɪz / (BrE)
plans, methods, or inclinations; particularly one's own ideas or ways of doing things (used especially in the phrase 'left to one's own devices').
personal plans or inclinations (esp. in 'left to one's own devices')

Examples

When left to their own devices, the children quickly invented a new game to play.
She worried about what he would do if left to his own devices all day.
The manager allowed the team to be left to their own devices on the project.

Synset Examples

eventually the family left the house to the devices of this malevolent force
the children were left to their own devices

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plan - a detailed proposal for doing or achieving something
  • Synonym: scheme - a large-scale systematic plan or arrangement

Etymology

From Old French 'devis', from Latin 'divisus', past participle of 'dividere' (to divide), originally meaning 'disposition' or 'scheme'.
