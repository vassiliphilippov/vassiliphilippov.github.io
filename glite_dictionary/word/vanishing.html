vanishing

1. NOUN

/ ˈvænɪʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈvænɪʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the sudden disappearance of something from sight or existence
sudden disappearance

Examples

The magician's greatest trick was the vanishing of the rabbit from the hat.
There was a sudden vanishing of the ship into the thick fog.
The novel's plot centers on the mysterious vanishing of several villagers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disappearance - the fact of someone or something ceasing to be visible

Etymology

From the verb 'vanish,' ultimately from Old French 'esvanir' and Latin 'evanescere' (to disappear).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvænɪʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈvanɪʃɪŋ / (BrE)
a sudden or mysterious disappearance, often in a way that cannot be easily explained
sudden or mysterious disappearance

Examples

The sudden vanishing of funds from the account raised immediate suspicion.
Her novel tells the story of the vanishing of an entire village overnight.
The magician's most impressive trick was the vanishing of a live elephant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disappearance - the act of ceasing to be visible or present
  • Antonym: appearance - the act of becoming visible or present
