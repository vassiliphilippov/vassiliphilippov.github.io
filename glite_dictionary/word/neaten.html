to neaten

1. VERB

/ ˈniːtən / (AmE), / ˈniːtən / (BrE)
to make (something) tidy, neat, or orderly; to improve the appearance of by arranging properly
make tidy or orderly

Examples

She took a minute to neaten her hair before the meeting.
Please neaten the desk when you're finished working.
He always tries to neaten his appearance before going out.

Synset Examples

He is always well-groomed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tidy - to make neat and orderly
  • Antonym: to mess up - to make untidy or disordered

Etymology

Formed from 'neat' + '-en' (verb-forming suffix).

2. VERB

/ ˈniːtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈniːtn̩ / (BrE)
to make (something) neat, tidy, or orderly; to arrange or put in order
make neat or tidy

Examples

She decided to neaten her desk before starting work.
Could you please neaten the living room before the guests arrive?
He spent an hour neatening the garden after the storm.

Synset Examples

Tidy up your room!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tidy - to make neat or arranged
  • Antonym: to mess up - to make disordered or messy

Etymology

Formed from 'neat' + '-en' (verbal suffix).
