Tuvalu

1. PROPN

/ tuˈvɑlu / (AmE), / tuːˈvɑːluː / (BrE)
a small island country in the Pacific Ocean, formerly part of the British colony of Gilbert and Ellice Islands, which became independent in 1978
island country in the Pacific Ocean

Examples

The capital city of Tuvalu is Funafuti, located on one of its nine islands.
Rising sea levels pose a serious threat to Tuvalu and its inhabitants.
After gaining independence in 1978, Tuvalu joined the United Nations.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Gilbert and Ellice Islands - former British colony in the Pacific, divided into Kiribati and Tuvalu
  • Degree Higher: Kiribati - island country in the Pacific, also once part of the Gilbert and Ellice Islands colony

2. PROPN

/ tuːˈvɑːluː / (AmE), / tuːˈvɑːluː / (BrE)
A country in the Pacific Ocean consisting of a group of small coral islands in Micronesia, southwest of Hawaii.
country in the Pacific Ocean

Examples

The Prime Minister of Tuvalu addressed the United Nations about climate change.
Many people in Tuvalu depend on fishing for their livelihood.
Rising sea levels pose a significant threat to the existence of Tuvalu.

Etymology

From Tuvaluan Tuvalu, meaning 'eight standing together' (referring to the eight traditionally inhabited islands).
