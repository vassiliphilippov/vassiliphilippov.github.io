blithe

1. ADJ

/ blaɪð / (AmE), / blaɪð / (BrE)
showing a cheerful and carefree disposition; happy and lighthearted, often seeming not to worry or care about anything
cheerful and carefree

Examples

Despite the looming deadline, she maintained a blithe attitude and made everyone laugh.
He wandered through the meadow, singing with a blithe spirit.
Her blithe disregard for the rules sometimes got her into trouble.

Synset Examples

was loved for her blithe spirit
a merry blithesome nature
her lighthearted nature
trilling songs with a lightsome heart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carefree - free from anxiety or worry
  • Synonym: lighthearted - cheerful and unconcerned
  • Antonym: worried - anxious or troubled about possible problems

Etymology

From Old English *blīthe* (joyous, kind), related to Old High German *blīdi* (gentle, friendly).

2. ADJ

/ blaɪð / (AmE), / blaɪð / (BrE)
showing a casual and cheerful indifference considered to be improper or careless
cheerfully indifferent; lacking due concern

Examples

He faced the warnings with blithe disregard, completely unconcerned about the risks.
Her blithe attitude toward deadlines frustrated her more meticulous colleagues.
They entered the negotiations with blithe ignorance of the challenges ahead.

Synset Examples

spoke with blithe ignorance of the true situation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: careless - not giving sufficient attention or thought to avoiding harm or errors
  • Antonym: concerned - worried or troubled about something

Etymology

Old English blīthe (joyous, kind, cheerful), related to Old Norse bliðr and Gothic bleiþs.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00547820-s
Synset POS: s
