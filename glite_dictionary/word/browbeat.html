to browbeat

1. VERB

/ ˈbraʊˌbit / (AmE), / ˈbraʊˌbiːt / (BrE)
to intimidate someone, usually through stern or threatening words or manner
intimidate by threats or stern manner

Examples

The manager tried to browbeat the staff into working overtime without extra pay.
She refused to be browbeaten by his aggressive questioning.
During the debate, he attempted to browbeat his opponent into submission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intimidate - to frighten or overawe someone, especially to make them do what one wants
  • Antonym: to encourage - to give someone support or confidence

Etymology

From 'brow' (forehead) + 'beat', originally referring to furrowing one's brow to threaten.

2. VERB

/ ˈbraʊˌbit / (AmE), / ˈbraʊˌbiːt / (BrE)
to intimidate or bully someone, especially through harsh words or actions
intimidate or bully someone

Examples

He tried to browbeat the witnesses into changing their testimony.
The coach would often browbeat the players for making mistakes during practice.
She refused to let her boss browbeat her into taking on more work than she could handle.

Synset Examples

Her big brother always bullied her when she was young

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bully - to seek to harm, intimidate, or coerce someone
  • Synonym: to intimidate - to frighten or threaten someone, usually in order to persuade them to do something
  • Antonym: to encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to someone

Etymology

From 'brow' + 'beat', originally meaning to beat down with a stern look (16th century).
