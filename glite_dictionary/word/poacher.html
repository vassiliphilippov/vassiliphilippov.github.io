poacher

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊ.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈpəʊ.tʃə / (BrE)
a small slender fish (family Agonidae) of the northern Pacific Ocean, having a body covered with bony plates
small armored northern Pacific fish

Examples

The aquarium features a display of the rare poacher fish from the northern Pacific.
Researchers studied the bony plates that protect the poacher from predators.
A poacher can grow up to eight inches long in its deep-water habitat.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊ.tʃər / (AmE), / ˈpəʊ.tʃə / (BrE)
a pan or other cooking vessel used for poaching food such as eggs or fish
a pan for poaching food

Examples

He used a poacher to make perfectly soft eggs for brunch.
The fish was prepared in a special poacher with aromatic herbs.
My grandmother gave me her old egg poacher for my first kitchen.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊ.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈpəʊ.tʃə / (BrE)
a person who hunts or fishes illegally on land that belongs to someone else or is protected by law
someone who hunts or fishes illegally

Examples

The poacher was caught trespassing in the national park with illegal traps.
Local authorities are cracking down on poachers to protect endangered wildlife.
He worked as a gamekeeper for years, always on the lookout for poachers in the woods.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hunter - a person who hunts animals, especially for sport or food

Etymology

From the verb 'poach', which comes from Middle English pochen (to poke, thrust), later used for illegal hunting.
