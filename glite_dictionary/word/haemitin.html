haemitin

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːmɪtɪn / (AmE), / ˈhiːmɪtɪn / (BrE)
a complex red pigment containing iron, found in hemoglobin, which binds to oxygen in the blood
red iron-containing pigment in blood
medical

Examples

The color of blood is mainly due to haemitin, which binds oxygen molecules.
In medical research, haemitin is studied for its role in oxygen transport.
A deficiency in haemitin can affect the efficiency of red blood cells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heme - iron-containing compound forming the nonprotein part of hemoglobin

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' (blood) and '-in' (a chemical suffix).
