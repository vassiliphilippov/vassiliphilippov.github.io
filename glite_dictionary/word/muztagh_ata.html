Muztagh Ata

1. PROPN

/ ˈmʊz.tɑː ɑːˈtɑː / (AmE), / ˈmʌztɑː ɑːˈtɑː / (BrE)
a high mountain peak (7,546 m; 23,891 ft) in the Kunlun Shan range in Xinjiang, China, near the Indian border
mountain peak in western China

Examples

Many climbers dream of summiting Muztagh Ata because of its accessible slopes and stunning views.
The snow-capped peak of Muztagh Ata towers over the surrounding landscape of Xinjiang.
Our expedition to Muztagh Ata required careful preparation due to its extreme altitude.

Etymology

From Uyghur ‘muz tagh’ (ice mountain) + ‘ata’ (father).
