koruna

1. NOUN

/ kɔˈruːnə / (AmE), / kəˈruːnə / (BrE)
the basic unit of currency in the Czech Republic, equal to 100 haléřů
Czech unit of currency

Examples

I exchanged my euros for Czech koruna when I arrived in Prague.
The price of lunch was 200 koruna at the local restaurant.
Tourists often wonder how many koruna they will need for a day in the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crown - the basic monetary unit of several countries, including those in Scandinavia

Etymology

Czech, literally 'crown', from Latin 'corona' (crown).

2. NOUN

/ kɔːˈruːnə / (AmE), / kɔːˈruːnə / (BrE)
the former basic unit of currency in Slovakia, equal to 100 heller, replaced by the euro in 2009.
Slovakia's former currency unit

Examples

The koruna was replaced by the euro in Slovakia in 2009.
Prices in Slovakia used to be listed in koruna before the currency changed.
She found some old koruny in a drawer while cleaning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crown - the basic monetary unit formerly used in some Central European countries

Etymology

From Slovak 'koruna', from Latin 'corona' (crown).
