seawards

1. ADV

/ ˈsiːwɚdz / (AmE), / ˈsiːwədz / (BrE)
towards the sea or in the direction of the sea
towards the sea

Examples

The fishermen pointed their boats seawards as the sun began to rise.
She walked seawards along the shore, drawn by the sound of the waves.
Clouds gathered seawards, warning of the approaching storm.

Synset Examples

the sailor looked seaward

Related Terms

  • Antonym: landwards - in the direction toward the land

Etymology

From 'sea' + suffix '-wards' meaning 'towards'.
