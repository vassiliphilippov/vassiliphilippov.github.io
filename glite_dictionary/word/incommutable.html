incommutable

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnkəˈmjutəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəˈmjuːtəbl̩ / (BrE)
not capable of being altered, changed, or substituted; unchangeable.
not capable of being changed or altered

Examples

The rules governing the ancient order were considered incommutable by its members.
His position was based on incommutable principles that could not be compromised.
Many religious doctrines are regarded as incommutable truths by their followers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immutable - unchanging over time or unable to be changed
  • Antonym: changeable - able to be changed; open to alteration

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (not) + 'commutare' (to change, exchange).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.kəˈmjuː.tə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kəˈmjuː.tə.bəl / (BrE)
not able to be exchanged, substituted, or replaced by something else; not interchangeable.
not able to be substituted or exchanged

Examples

She was known for her incommutable sense of responsibility to her team.
For this rare disease, an incommutable treatment is necessary and cannot be substituted.
The judge considered the penalty incommutable due to the severity of the crime.

Synset Examples

a rare incommutable skill

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interchangeable - able to be exchanged or substituted one for another

Etymology

From Latin prefix in- (not) + commutable (from commutare, to exchange).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00921730-s
Synset POS: s
