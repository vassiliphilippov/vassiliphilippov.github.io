century

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntʃəri / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.tʃər.i / (BrE)
A period of 100 years.
100-year period

Examples

The twentieth century witnessed incredible advances in technology and medicine.
Her family has owned that house for over a century.
Many ancient cities were built over several centuries.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: decade - A period of 10 years
  • Degree Higher: millennium - A period of 1,000 years

Etymology

From Latin 'centuria' (group of one hundred).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.tʃɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.tʃʊr.i / (BrE)
A group or unit of one hundred, especially as ten groups of ten; historically, a subdivision of a Roman legion.
group of one hundred

Examples

A Roman century was composed of ten squads of ten men each.
The commander led his century into formation on the parade ground.
In arithmetic exercises, grouping into a century can simplify counting large numbers.

Etymology

From Latin 'centuria' (group of one hundred), from 'centum' (hundred).
