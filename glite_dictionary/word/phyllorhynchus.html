Phyllorhynchus

1. NOUN

/ fɪl-oʊ-ˈrɪŋ-kəs / (AmE), / fɪl-ə-ˈrɪŋ-kəs / (BrE)
A genus of small, burrowing, leaf-nosed snakes found in southwestern North America.
a genus of leaf-nosed snakes

Examples

The Phyllorhynchus snake is well adapted to life in sandy deserts.
Researchers have observed Phyllorhynchus hunting for insects at night.
Many herpetologists are fascinated by the unique snout of the Phyllorhynchus.

Etymology

From Greek 'phyllon' (leaf) + 'rhynchos' (snout).

2. NOUN

/ fɪlˌɔːˈrɪŋkəs / (AmE), / fɪlˌɒˈrɪŋkəs / (BrE)
A genus of small, leaf-nosed snakes native to North America, classified in the family Colubridae.
a genus of leaf-nosed snakes

Examples

The genus Phyllorhynchus is recognized for its distinctive leaf-shaped snout among snakes.
Several species within Phyllorhynchus inhabit arid regions of the southwestern United States.
Researchers have noted the burrowing behavior typical of Phyllorhynchus snakes.

Etymology

From Greek 'phyllon' (leaf) and 'rhynchos' (snout), referring to the leaf-shaped nose of the snakes.
