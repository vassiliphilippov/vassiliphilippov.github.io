to industrialize

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈdʌstriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌstriəlaɪz / (BrE)
to develop industries in a country or region; to make or become industrial
develop industry; become industrial

Examples

As countries industrialize, their economies often experience rapid growth and urbanization.
Government incentives helped the region industrialize after the war, leading to higher employment rates.
Some developing nations have yet to fully industrialize and remain largely agricultural.

Synset Examples

The nations of South East Asia will quickly industrialize and catch up with the West

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to urbanize - to make or become urban, to develop towns and cities

Etymology

From industry + -ize, patterned after French industrialiser.

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈdʌstriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌstriəlaɪz / (BrE)
to organize the production of something in a way that uses large-scale industry, especially using machinery and mass production techniques
to organize production into industry

Examples

Many countries sought to industrialize their agriculture during the 20th century.
The region rapidly industrialized following the construction of several large factories.
Efforts to industrialize traditional crafts met with resistance from local artisans.

Synset Examples

The Chinese industrialized textile production

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mechanize - to introduce machinery into a process
  • Antonym: deindustrialize - to reduce industrial activity or dismantle industries

Etymology

From 'industry' + '-ize', first used in the 19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00578056-v
Synset POS: v
