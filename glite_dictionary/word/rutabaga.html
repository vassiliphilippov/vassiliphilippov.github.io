rutabaga

1. NOUN

/ ˌruːtəˈbeɪɡə / (AmE), / ˌruːtəˈbɑːɡə / (BrE)
a plant of the cabbage family with a large round yellowish root, eaten as a vegetable
edible root vegetable; plant of cabbage family

Examples

She added a cubed rutabaga to the stew for extra flavor.
In some countries, the rutabaga is known as a swede.
Roasted rutabaga is a popular side dish during the winter months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swede - a European term for rutabaga

Etymology

From Swedish 'rotabagge' (root bag).

2. NOUN

/ ˌruːtəˈbeɪɡə / (AmE), / ˌruːtəˈbɑːɡə / (BrE)
a large, yellow-fleshed root vegetable of a plant of the cabbage family, used as food
a yellow root vegetable; swede

Examples

She diced a rutabaga to add to the hearty stew for extra flavor.
In Scotland, rutabaga is often called 'neep' and served with haggis.
Farmers harvested a bumper crop of rutabagas this autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swede - British English term for rutabaga

Etymology

From Swedish dialect *rotabagge* ('root bag').
