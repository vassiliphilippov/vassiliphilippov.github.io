bedwetter

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛdˌwɛtər / (AmE), / ˈbɛdˌwɛtə / (BrE)
a person, typically a child, who urinates involuntarily while asleep in bed
someone who urinates involuntarily during sleep

Examples

As a child, he was a bedwetter, which made him feel embarrassed at sleepovers.
Many parents worry if their child is still a bedwetter at age six.
Support groups can help both bedwetters and their families cope with the issue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enuretic - a person who suffers from enuresis (involuntary urination)

Etymology

From 'bed' + 'wetter', referring to one who wets the bed.
