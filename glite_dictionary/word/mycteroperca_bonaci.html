Mycteroperca bonaci

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪktəroʊˈpɜrkə boʊˈnɑsi / (AmE), / ˌmɪktərəʊˈpɜːkə bəˈnɑːsi / (BrE)
A large species of dark-colored grouper with a thick head and rough scales, found in the western Atlantic Ocean.
a large dark grouper species

Examples

The Mycteroperca bonaci is highly prized by anglers for its strength and size.
Reefs in the Gulf of Mexico are home to the Mycteroperca bonaci.
A fresh catch of Mycteroperca bonaci was on display at the local fish market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black grouper - common name for Mycteroperca bonaci
  • Degree Higher: grouper - large fish of the subfamily Epinephelinae

Etymology

From New Latin, the scientific designation for this species of grouper.
