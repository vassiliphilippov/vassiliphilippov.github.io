Prunus pensylvanica

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs ˌpɛn.sɪlˈvæn.ɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs ˌpɛn.sɪlˈveɪ.nɪ.kə / (BrE)
a small shrubby North American wild cherry tree with small, bright red, acidic fruit, commonly known as pin cherry.
a North American wild cherry tree

Examples

The forest undergrowth was dotted with Prunus pensylvanica, adding flashes of red to the landscape.
Ecologists often study Prunus pensylvanica because its fruit supports a wide range of wildlife.
Gardeners value Prunus pensylvanica for its hardiness and its role in restoring disturbed soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pin cherry - another common name for Prunus pensylvanica

Etymology

From Latin: 'Prunus' (plum, cherry) and 'pensylvanica,' referring to Pennsylvania.
