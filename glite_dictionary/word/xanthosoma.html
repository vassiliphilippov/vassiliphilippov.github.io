Xanthosoma

1. NOUN

/ zænˈθoʊsəmə / (AmE), / zanˈθəʊsəmə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American perennial plants with large tuberous roots, often cultivated for their edible corms or as ornamental foliage.
genus of tropical American tuberous plants

Examples

The leaves of Xanthosoma are often grown as attractive foliage plants in tropical gardens.
Many communities in South America use the roots of Xanthosoma as a staple food.
Researchers are studying Xanthosoma for its potential nutritional benefits.

Etymology

From Greek xanthos (yellow) + soma (body), referring to plant parts.
