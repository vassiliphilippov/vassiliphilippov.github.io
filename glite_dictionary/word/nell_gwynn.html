Nell Gwynn

1. PROPN

/ nɛl ɡwɪn / (AmE), / nɛl ɡwɪn / (BrE)
(1650–1687) An English actress and comedienne celebrated as a prominent figure in Restoration theatre and known for being the mistress of King Charles II.
English actress; mistress of Charles II

Examples

During the Restoration period, Nell Gwynn became famous for her wit and charm on the London stage.
Many historians study the influence Nell Gwynn had on the royal court of Charles II.
A new play at the Globe Theatre is based on the life of Nell Gwynn.
