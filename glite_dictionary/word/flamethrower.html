flamethrower

1. NOUN

/ ˈfleɪmˌθroʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈfleɪmˌθrəʊə / (BrE)
A weapon that projects a stream of burning fuel, used to set targets on fire from a distance.
weapon projecting burning fuel

Examples

The soldiers advanced cautiously behind the cover of a flamethrower during the assault.
He watched documentaries about World War II and saw footage of flamethrowers in action.
The use of a flamethrower is heavily restricted under international law.

Etymology

Compound of 'flame' and 'thrower', modeled on German Flammenwerfer (literally 'flame-thrower').
