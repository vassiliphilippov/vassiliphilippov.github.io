to steepen

1. VERB

/ ˈstiːpən / (AmE), / ˈstiːpən / (BrE)
to make or become steeper or more pronounced in slope
make or become steeper

Examples

Heavy rainfall can steepen the sides of riverbanks, increasing the risk of erosion.
The trail steepens sharply after the first mile, making the climb much more challenging.
As construction continued, the workers had to steepen the embankment to fit the new road.

Synset Examples

The landslides have steepened the mountain sides

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to flatten - to make or become flat or flatter

Etymology

From 'steep' + '-en', a verb-forming suffix.

2. VERB

/ ˈstiːpən / (AmE), / ˈstiːpən / (BrE)
to become or make something more steep; to slope more sharply.
become or make more steep

Examples

The hiking trail began to steepen as we approached the mountain ridge.
The economic downturn caused the decline to steepen unexpectedly.
As the path steepened, we found it harder to keep our footing.

Synset Examples

The mountain side has steepened

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to flatten - to become or make less steep or more level

Etymology

From 'steep' (adj.) + '-en' (verb-forming suffix).
