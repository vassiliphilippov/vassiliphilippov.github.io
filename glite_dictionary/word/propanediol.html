propanediol

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈpæn.i.daɪˌɒl / (AmE), / ˌprəʊ.pænˈiː.dɪ.ɒl / (BrE)
A sweet, colorless, viscous liquid used as an antifreeze, in brake fluid, and as a humectant in cosmetics and personal care products.
sweet, colorless viscous liquid (chemical)

Examples

Many lotions contain propanediol for its moisturizing properties.
The technician added propanediol to the antifreeze solution.
Exposure to large amounts of propanediol can sometimes cause skin irritation.

Etymology

From propane (the parent hydrocarbon) + diol (compound with two alcohol groups).
