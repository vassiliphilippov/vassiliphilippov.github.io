mercenary

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɜr.səˌnɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.sən.əri / (BrE)
motivated mainly by a desire for money or other material gain rather than by principles
motivated by profit rather than principles

Examples

She accused him of having a mercenary attitude toward relationships, caring more about money than loyalty.
Many critics saw the movie as a mercenary attempt to cash in on a popular trend.
He took on the job for entirely mercenary reasons, not because he believed in the cause.

Synset Examples

a commercial book
preached a mercantile and militant patriotism
a mercenary enterprise
a moneymaking business

Related Terms

  • Antonym: selfless - motivated by concern for others rather than personal gain
  • Synonym: greedy - having or showing an intense and selfish desire for wealth or possessions

Etymology

From Latin mercenarius (hired, one who works for pay), from merces (wages, reward).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɜːr.sə.ˌner.i / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.sən.əri / (BrE)
a person who is hired to fight in a foreign army or for a foreign cause, usually for personal gain rather than for a cause or loyalty
soldier hired to fight for another country

Examples

The company hired a mercenary to train their private security forces.
During the conflict, several mercenaries joined the battle for financial gain.
He was criticized for acting like a mercenary, fighting only for money, not for a cause.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soldier of fortune - a person who fights for personal gain rather than loyalty; synonym for mercenary
  • Antonym: patriot - a person who supports and defends their own country; an antonym in terms of loyalty

Etymology

From Latin mercenarius (hired worker), from merces (wages, reward).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10329281-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈmɜːr.səˌner.i / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.sən.əri / (BrE)
primarily concerned with making money at the expense of ethics or other considerations; motivated chiefly by material gain.
motivated by material gain

Examples

His motives for helping were strictly mercenary, as he expected generous payment.
The decision to accept the offer was criticized as cold and mercenary.
She refused to take a mercenary approach to her art, insisting that passion was more important than profit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: materialistic - focused excessively on material possessions or money
  • Antonym: altruistic - showing selfless concern for the well-being of others

Etymology

From Latin mercenarius (hired, serving for pay), from merces (wages, reward).

Source: WordNet

4. ADJ

/ ˈmɜːr.səˌner.i / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.sən.əri / (BrE)
(of a person or their actions) primarily concerned with making money, especially at the expense of ethics or loyalty, or serving for pay in a foreign army.
motivated by money or serving for pay

Examples

Many considered the soldiers' actions to be mercenary, since they fought only for personal profit.
He took a mercenary interest in the job, caring little for the company's values.
The government hired mercenary forces from abroad to bolster its military.

Synset Examples

mercenary killers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avaricious - having an extreme greed for wealth or material gain
  • Antonym: patriotic - showing love and support for one's own country, not mercenary

Etymology

From Latin 'mercenarius' (hired, serving for wages), from 'merces' (wages, reward).
