Pinus parryana

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪ.nəs pəˈrjaː.nə / (AmE), / ˈpaɪ.nəs paˈrɪ.ɑː.nə / (BrE)
A species of five-needled pinyon pine native to southern California and northern Baja California, sometimes with three or four needles due to hybridization.
a pinyon pine species of California and Baja California

Examples

The Pinus parryana thrives in the dry, rocky hillsides of southern California.
Researchers observed that Pinus parryana sometimes bears only three or four needles per bundle due to hybridization.
Many birds rely on the seeds of Pinus parryana for food in their natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pinyon pine - any of several pine tree species with edible seeds, mainly found in southwestern North America

Etymology

From Latin, Pinus (pine) and named after botanist Charles C. Parry.
