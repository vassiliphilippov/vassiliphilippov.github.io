2. NOUN

/ deɪ / (AmE), / deɪ / (BrE)
(Astronomy) The time period for one complete rotation of the Earth relative to a particular star (usually the vernal equinox), about four minutes shorter than a solar day.
period of Earth's rotation relative to a star

Examples

A sidereal day is about four minutes shorter than a solar day.
Astronomers use the concept of a sidereal day to measure star positions.
The rotation period that defines a sidereal day is based on distant stars, not the Sun.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solar day - the time interval of one rotation of the Earth relative to the Sun
