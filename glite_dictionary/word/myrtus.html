Myrtus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɜrtəs / (AmE), / ˈmɜːtəs / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen shrubs or small trees in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae, commonly known as myrtle.
genus of evergreen shrubs (myrtle)

Examples

The Myrtus genus includes the common myrtle, widely cultivated for its fragrant leaves and white flowers.
Botanists study Myrtus for its importance in horticulture and traditional medicine.
In Mediterranean gardens, Myrtus is often grown as an ornamental shrub.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myrtle - common name for plants of the genus Myrtus

Etymology

From Latin 'myrtus', meaning 'myrtle'.
