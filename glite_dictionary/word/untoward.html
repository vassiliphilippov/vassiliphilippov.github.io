untoward

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtɔrd / (AmE), / ʌnˈtɔːd / (BrE)
(of behavior or events) not in keeping with accepted standards of what is proper or appropriate in polite society; improper or inappropriate.
improper; socially inappropriate

Examples

His comments at the meeting were considered untoward and offended several participants.
The principal addressed the student's untoward behavior during the assembly.
Any untoward conduct at the formal dinner will not be tolerated.

Synset Examples

was buried with indecent haste
indecorous behavior
language unbecoming to a lady
unseemly to use profanity
moved to curb their untoward ribaldry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improper - not in keeping with accepted standards of behavior
  • Antonym: decent - conforming to accepted standards; proper

Etymology

From Middle English untoward (“not toward, froward”), from un- (not) + toward (favorable, promising).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtɔrd / (AmE), / ʌnˈtɔːd / (BrE)
unexpected, inappropriate, or unfavorable, often causing trouble or difficulty.
unfavorable or inappropriate

Examples

Nothing untoward happened during their journey, despite the stormy weather.
The company assured customers that no untoward activity had been detected on their accounts.
She apologized if her remarks seemed untoward during the meeting.

Synset Examples

adverse circumstances
made a place for themselves under the most untoward conditions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adverse - having a negative or unfavorable effect
  • Antonym: favorable - beneficial or advantageous

Etymology

From un- (not) + toward (proper, favorable) in Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01000455-s
Synset POS: s
