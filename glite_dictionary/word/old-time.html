old-time

1. ADJ

/ ˈoʊldˌtaɪm / (AmE), / ˈəʊldˌtaɪm / (BrE)
attractively old-fashioned; characteristic of or resembling a previous era, especially in an appealing way.
attractively old-fashioned

Examples

The festival featured old-time music and traditional folk dancing.
She decorated her kitchen with old-time appliances and vintage signs.
The movie's old-time charm reminded viewers of classic Hollywood films.

Synset Examples

houses with quaint thatched roofs
a vaulted roof supporting old-time chimney pots

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quaint - pleasingly old-fashioned or quaint
  • Antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times
