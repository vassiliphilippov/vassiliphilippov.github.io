Sphenisciformes

1. NOUN

/ sfɛˌnɪsɪˈfɔrmiːz / (AmE), / sfɛˌnɪsɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of flightless aquatic birds comprising the penguins, found mainly in the Southern Hemisphere
the order of birds including penguins

Examples

All known species of Sphenisciformes are adapted for life in water.
The closest living relatives of Sphenisciformes are thought to be other water birds.
Researchers have identified several extinct members of the order Sphenisciformes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penguin - a flightless seabird of the southern hemisphere

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek *spheniskos* (‘little wedge’) and *-formes* (‘in the form of’).
