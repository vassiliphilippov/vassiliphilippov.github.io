Hemingwayesque

1. ADJ

/ ˌhɛmɪŋˈweɪˌɛsk / (AmE), / ˌhɛmɪŋˈweɪˌɛsk / (BrE)
resembling or characteristic of the writing style of Ernest Hemingway, especially in its simplicity, succinctness, and understatement
in the style of Ernest Hemingway

Examples

His novel was praised for its Hemingwayesque prose and vivid imagery.
The film’s dialogue is so Hemingwayesque that fans of the author will instantly recognize the influence.
Critics noted a Hemingwayesque restraint in her short stories, focusing on what was left unsaid.

Etymology

Formed from the name of Ernest Hemingway (American writer, 1899–1961) plus the suffix -esque.
