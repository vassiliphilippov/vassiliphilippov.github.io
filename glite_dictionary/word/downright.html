downright

1. ADV

/ ˈdaʊnˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈdaʊn.raɪt / (BrE)
(adverb) Thoroughly; completely or absolutely.
completely; absolutely (adverb)

Examples

Her explanation was downright confusing, leaving everyone more puzzled than before.
Some of his comments were downright rude.
The film was downright terrifying in certain scenes.

Synset Examples

he is outright dishonest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outright - completely; without reservation; thoroughly (adverb)

Etymology

From Middle English 'dounright', from 'down' + 'right', originally meaning 'straight down'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdaʊnˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈdaʊn.raɪt / (BrE)
(of a person or statement) plain and blunt in honesty, often to the point of being direct or frank.
bluntly honest; direct

Examples

His downright response surprised everyone in the room.
She is known for her downright honesty, even when the truth is uncomfortable.
It's refreshing to get a downright answer instead of vague politeness.

Synset Examples

a downright answer
a downright kind of person

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frank - open, honest, and direct in speech or writing
  • Synonym: blunt - uncompromisingly forthright in speech
  • Antonym: tactful - having or showing skill and sensitivity in dealing with others or with difficult issues

Etymology

From Middle English dounright, formed from 'down' + 'right', originally meaning 'straight down', later used figuratively for absolute or complete.

3. ADJ

/ ˈdaʊnˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈdaʊn.raɪt / (BrE)
(of something bad or negative) complete; total; used to emphasize the extent of something.
complete; total (emphatic)

Examples

The accusation was a downright lie, with no basis in fact.
His behavior at the party was downright rude and unacceptable.
What you did was downright dangerous, and you knew the risks.
