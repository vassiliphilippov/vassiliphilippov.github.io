Indian

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪndiən / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən / (BrE)
relating to India or its people, languages, or culture.
of or relating to India

Examples

She wore a beautiful Indian dress to the wedding.
The festival showcased Indian music and traditional dances.
We enjoyed an Indian meal at the new restaurant downtown.

Synset Examples

the Indian subcontinent
Indian saris

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Indian (noun) - a person from India or of Indian descent

Etymology

From Latin Indianus, from India.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02939660-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪndiən / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən / (BrE)
relating to the indigenous peoples of the Americas, especially those of North America, or to their cultures or languages
relating to Native Americans

Examples

Many museums display Indian artifacts, such as pottery and arrowheads.
She studied Indian languages at the university for her anthropology degree.
The festival celebrates traditional Indian music and dance from various tribes.

Synset Examples

Native American religions
Indian arrowheads

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Native American - a member of the indigenous peoples of the Americas, especially the United States

Etymology

From India, applied by early European explorers who believed they had reached the Indies; historically used for Native Americans.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02939379-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiən / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of India
person from India

Examples

As an Indian, she celebrates Diwali every year with her family.
The Indian was proud to represent his country in the international competition.
Many Indians have made significant contributions to science and technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Indian-American - a person of Indian origin who is an American citizen or resident

Etymology

From Latin 'Indus', from Greek 'Indos', referring to the Indus River and the region of India.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09693046-n
Synset POS: n
