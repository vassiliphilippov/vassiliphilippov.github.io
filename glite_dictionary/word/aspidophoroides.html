Aspidophoroides

1. PROPN

/ ˌæspɪdəˌfɔːrɔɪˈiːdiːz / (AmE), / ˌæspɪdəˌfɔːrɔɪˈiːdiːz / (BrE)
A genus of small, elongated marine fishes commonly known as alligatorfishes, found in northern waters.
genus of alligatorfishes

Examples

Specimens of Aspidophoroides were collected during the Arctic marine survey.
The bony plates of Aspidophoroides protect it from predators in its cold-water habitat.
Researchers published new findings on the distribution of Aspidophoroides species in the North Atlantic.

Etymology

From Greek aspis (shield) and phoroeides (bearing), referring to their protective body plates.

2. NOUN

/ æˌspɪdəˌfɔːrɔɪˈdiːz / (AmE), / ˌaspɪdəˌfɔːrəʊˈiːdiːz / (BrE)
A genus of small marine fishes known as alligatorfishes, found in northern Pacific and Arctic waters.
genus of alligatorfishes

Examples

The Aspidophoroides genus includes several species of armored alligatorfishes found in cold waters.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Aspidophoroides genus.
The distinctive bony plates of Aspidophoroides help protect these small fishes from predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alligatorfish - a common name for species in the genus Aspidophoroides

Etymology

From Greek aspid-, meaning 'shield,' and phor-, meaning 'bearing,' referring to the fish's bony plates.
