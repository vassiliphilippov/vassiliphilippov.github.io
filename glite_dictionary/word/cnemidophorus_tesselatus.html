Cnemidophorus tesselatus

1. NOUN

/ nɛmɪˌdɑfəˈrəs tɛsəˌleɪtəs / (AmE), / nɛmɪˌdɒfəˈrəs tɛsəˌleɪtəs / (BrE)
A species of whiptail lizard with distinctive marked patterns, native to southeastern Colorado to eastern Chihuahua.
species of checkered whiptail lizard

Examples

The Cnemidophorus tesselatus is known for its distinct checkered pattern along its back.
Researchers studied the habitat range of the Cnemidophorus tesselatus in southern Colorado.
The Cnemidophorus tesselatus can often be found basking in the desert sun.

Etymology

From Greek 'knemis' (greave, a piece of armor for the leg) and Latin 'tessellatus' (checkered, mosaic).
