shaper

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪpər / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪpə / (BrE)
a machine tool used to cut or shape metal or wood by moving a cutting tool back and forth across the workpiece
machine tool for shaping metal or wood

Examples

The engineer adjusted the shaper to create a precise groove in the metal bar.
Our workshop recently invested in a new shaper for woodworking projects.
Operating a shaper requires careful attention to safety protocols.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: planer - a machine tool for smoothing or shaping wood or metal, typically on a larger scale than a shaper

Etymology

From the verb 'shape' + -er, denoting a tool or person that shapes.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪ.pɚ / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪ.pə / (BrE)
a person who makes, forms, or fashions things; someone who shapes materials or objects.
a person who makes or shapes things

Examples

He was a skilled shaper of surfboards in his youth.
The team needs a shaper who can turn ideas into reality.
Every industry relies on at least one talented shaper to develop new products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: craftsman - a skilled worker who makes things by hand
  • Synonym: builder - someone who builds or constructs something

Etymology

From 'shape' + '-er', where '-er' forms agent nouns.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10303761-n
Synset POS: n
