caviar

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæviˌɑr / (AmE), / ˈkæv.i.ɑː / (BrE)
The salted eggs of sturgeon or other large fish, eaten as a delicacy, often served as an hors d'oeuvre.
salted fish eggs delicacy

Examples

She ordered fresh caviar as a special treat for the party.
The chef served the blinis topped with a spoonful of caviar.
True caviar comes from sturgeon, but other fish roe may be labeled as such.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roe - the eggs of fish, especially when eaten as food

Etymology

From Italian 'caviale', from Turkish 'havyar', from Persian 'khaviar' (egg-bearing).
