to untangle

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈtæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈtæŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
to separate things that are twisted together or caught in one another, especially fibers or threads, so that they are no longer tangled
separate things that are tangled

Examples

She carefully began to untangle the string that held the package together.
It took him an hour to untangle the knots in his fishing line.
The cat had to be rescued after getting completely untangled from the cords.

Synset Examples

unravel the thread

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tangle - to become twisted together in a confused mass
  • Synonym: to unravel - to undo twisted, knitted, or woven threads

Etymology

From un- (expressing reversal) + tangle (from Middle English, probably of Scandinavian origin).

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈtæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈtæŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
to free someone or something from a difficult situation or a complicated problem; to disentangle
free from difficulty or complication

Examples

It took hours to untangle the complex legal issues involved in the case.
She tried hard to untangle herself from the financial mess she was in.
Management is working to untangle the confusion over the new policy.

Synset Examples

I cannot extricate myself from this task

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to extricate - to free someone or something from a constraint or difficulty
  • Antonym: tangle - to become or make twisted together or complicated

Etymology

un- (prefix meaning 'not' or 'reverse of') + tangle (from Middle English 'tangilen').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01588680-v
Synset POS: v
