Calidris

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlaɪdrɪs / (AmE), / kəˈlɪdrɪs / (BrE)
A genus of shorebirds in the family Scolopacidae that includes knots and surfbirds.
genus of shorebirds (knots, surfbirds)

Examples

The genus Calidris includes several species of small wading birds found along coasts worldwide.
Many birdwatchers hope to spot a member of the Calidris genus during migration season.
The plumage of most Calidris species blends perfectly with sandy shorelines.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Greek 'kalidris', a term for a kind of grey shorebird.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪdrɪs / (AmE), / kəˈlɪdrɪs / (BrE)
a genus of wading birds in the family Scolopacidae that includes the knots and surfbirds
genus of wading birds (knots, surfbirds)

Examples

The genus Calidris is well known for its migratory shorebirds, such as the red knot and the sanderling.
Several species within Calidris are found in coastal habitats around the world.
Researchers observed the feeding behaviors of Calidris during the spring migration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knot - a type of bird in the genus Calidris
  • Synonym: surfbird - a species related to Calidris in the family Scolopacidae

Etymology

From Neo-Latin 'Calidris', from Ancient Greek 'kalidris', a term used by Aristotle for some gray-colored water bird.
