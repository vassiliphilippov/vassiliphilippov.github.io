ballad

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæləd / (AmE), / ˈbæləd / (BrE)
A narrative song or poem with a recurring refrain, typically telling a dramatic or romantic story.
narrative song with a refrain

Examples

The folk singer performed a haunting ballad about lost love.
Many classic ballads recount tales of bravery and adventure.
He wrote a modern ballad that quickly became popular on the radio.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: song - a short piece of music with words
  • Antonym: ode - a lyric poem typically addressed to a particular subject

Etymology

From Old Provençal 'balada' (song, dance), from Late Latin 'ballare' (to dance).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæl.əd / (AmE), / ˈbæl.əd / (BrE)
A narrative poem, often of folk origin, composed to be sung or recited, typically in short stanzas and telling a popular story.
folk narrative poem or song

Examples

The ancient ballad recounted the legend of a brave knight and his quest.
She wrote a ballad about her hometown that was later set to music.
Many ballads were passed down orally before being written down.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ode - a lyric poem, typically formal and elaborate
  • Antonym: lyric - a type of poetry expressing personal feelings

Etymology

From Old French ballade (dancing song), from Provençal balada, from balar (to dance).
