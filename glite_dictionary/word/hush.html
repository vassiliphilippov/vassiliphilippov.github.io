hush

1. VERB

/ hʌʃ / (AmE), / hʌʃ / (BrE)
to make someone stop talking or become quiet; to silence someone or something
make someone or something quiet

Examples

The librarian hushed the noisy students quickly.
She put a finger to her lips to hush everyone during the movie.
He tried to hush the crowd so the speaker could begin.

Synset Examples

Please silence the children in the church!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silence - to make someone or something stop making noise or talking

3. VERB

/ hʌʃ / (AmE), / hʌʃ / (BrE)
To run water over the ground to erode soil and reveal underlying layers and valuable minerals, especially as a mining technique.
to wash soil for minerals using water

Examples

Miners would hush the hillside in search of lead ore.
The crew hushed the valley, hoping to uncover gold beneath the surface.
Traditional techniques sometimes involved hushing to expose valuable mineral veins.

Etymology

From the noun sense 'hush,' a mining term from the 18th–19th centuries. Possibly from the imitation of the sound of rushing water.
