camelpox

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæməlˌpɑks / (AmE), / ˈkæməlˌpɒks / (BrE)
a contagious viral disease affecting camels, closely related to smallpox
a viral disease of camels
medical

Examples

Veterinarians in the region have reported several new cases of camelpox among herds.
Researchers are studying the similarities between camelpox and other orthopoxviruses.
Effective control measures are needed to prevent outbreaks of camelpox in desert communities.

Synset Examples

with a little genetic engineering, camelpox could be used as a bioweapon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smallpox - a serious infectious disease caused by the variola virus

Etymology

Formed from 'camel' + 'pox', patterned after 'smallpox'.
