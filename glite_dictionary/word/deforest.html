to deforest

1. VERB

/ dɪˈfɔr.ɪst / (AmE), / dɪˈfɒr.ɪst / (BrE)
to remove all or most of the trees from an area, especially as part of land clearing or logging
remove trees from an area

Examples

Many rainforests are deforested each year to make room for agriculture.
If we deforest these mountains, the soil will quickly erode.
The area was severely deforested after decades of illegal logging.

Synset Examples

The landscape was deforested by the enemy attacks

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to reforest - to plant trees on land that was deforested

Etymology

From de- (removal) + forest, modeled on French déforester.
