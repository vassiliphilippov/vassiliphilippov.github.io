incontinent

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkɑːntɪnənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɒntɪnənt / (BrE)
(of a person) unable to control urination or defecation.
unable to control urination or defecation
medical

Examples

The patient became incontinent after his stroke and required extra care.
Elderly people can often become incontinent and may need special assistance.
He felt embarrassed about being incontinent during his hospital stay.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: continent - able to control urination and defecation

Etymology

From Latin incontinens, from in- (not) + continens (restraining).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪnkənˌtɪnənt / (AmE), / ˈɪnkəntɪnənt / (BrE)
Unable to control the bladder or bowels, resulting in involuntary urination or defecation.
unable to control urination or defecation
medical

Examples

The nurse helped the elderly patient who was incontinent due to his illness.
Many people feel embarrassed to discuss incontinent episodes with their doctor.
Being incontinent can significantly affect a person's confidence and daily life.

Synset Examples

He felt embarrassed about being incontinent at his age.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: continent - able to control urination or defecation

Etymology

From Latin 'incontinens', from 'in-' (not) + 'continens' (restraining).
