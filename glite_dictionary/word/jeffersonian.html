Jeffersonian

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛfərˈsoʊniən / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛfəˈsəʊniən / (BrE)
a person who supports or follows the ideas and principles of Thomas Jefferson, especially those related to democracy and government
follower of Thomas Jefferson's ideas

Examples

Many Jeffersonians opposed the expansion of federal power in the early United States.
The historian wrote extensively about influential Jeffersonians during the early 19th century.
Modern political debates often echo the concerns of Jeffersonians regarding individual liberty.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Federalist - a supporter of a strong central government, often contrasted with Jeffersonians

Etymology

From the name Thomas Jefferson (1743–1826), third U.S. president, with the suffix -ian indicating follower or adherent.

2. ADJ

/ ˌdʒɛfərˈsoʊniən / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛfəˈsəʊniən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of Thomas Jefferson or his principles, especially as reflected in American political thought and democracy.
relating to Thomas Jefferson or his principles

Examples

His political philosophy was distinctly Jeffersonian in its emphasis on individual liberties.
The new constitution reflected many Jeffersonian ideals.
She wrote an essay exploring Jeffersonian democracy in early America.

Synset Examples

Jeffersonian democracy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Hamiltonian - relating to Alexander Hamilton or his principles

Etymology

From the name Thomas Jefferson (1743–1826), third President of the United States, with the suffix -ian forming an adjective.
