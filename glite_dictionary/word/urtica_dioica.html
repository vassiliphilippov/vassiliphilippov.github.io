Urtica dioica

1. NOUN

/ ɜːrˈtaɪkə daɪˈoʊɪkə / (AmE), / ɜːˈtɪkə dɪˈəʊɪkə / (BrE)
A perennial plant species, commonly known as stinging nettle, native to Eurasia and established in North America, with broad, coarsely toothed leaves and stinging hairs.
species of stinging nettle

Examples

The leaves of Urtica dioica can cause skin irritation if touched without protection.
Many herbal teas use dried Urtica dioica for its traditional health benefits.
Botanists studied the spread of Urtica dioica in North American forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stinging nettle - common name for Urtica dioica

Etymology

From Latin: 'urtica' meaning nettle, and 'dioica' meaning with separate male and female plants.
