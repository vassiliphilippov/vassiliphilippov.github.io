Pampas

1. PROPN

/ ˈpæmpəs / (AmE), / ˈpampəs / (BrE)
the extensive, fertile grasslands in central and northern Argentina, stretching into Uruguay and southern Brazil, known for their agriculture and cattle ranching
fertile grasslands in Argentina and South America

Examples

Gauchos once roamed the Pampas, herding cattle across the open plains.
The Pampas are famous for producing some of the world's best beef.
Much of Argentina's wheat is grown on the fertile soils of the Pampas.

Etymology

From Spanish 'pampa' (plain), originally from Quechua 'pampa' meaning 'plain'.
