Mangifera

1. NOUN

/ mænˈɡɪfərə / (AmE), / maŋˈɡɪfərə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical trees native to Asia, including the mango, that bear fleshy fruit.
genus of tropical trees including the mango

Examples

The genus Mangifera contains several species, the most famous of which produces the common mango.
Botanists have identified over 30 species within the Mangifera genus across South and Southeast Asia.
Wild varieties of Mangifera are important for the genetic diversity of cultivated mangoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mango - the edible fruit of Mangifera indica

Etymology

From Latin, from Malay 'mangga' (mango) + Latin '-fera' (bearing).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmæŋˈgɪfərə / (AmE), / ˌmæŋˈgɪfərə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical Asian trees in the cashew family, including the mango, that bear fleshy, edible fruit
genus of tropical trees including the mango

Examples

The Mangifera genus comprises several tropical trees, most notably the mango.
Botanists have identified dozens of species within the Mangifera group in Southeast Asia.
Many varieties of Mangifera are cultivated for their sweet, juicy fruits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mango - the edible fruit of Mangifera indica

Etymology

From Latin 'mangifera', meaning mango-bearing, from 'mango' + 'ferre' (to bear).
