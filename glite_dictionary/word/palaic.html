Palaic

1. NOUN

/ pəˈleɪɪk / (AmE), / pəˈleɪɪk / (BrE)
an extinct Anatolian language formerly spoken in the northwest region of present-day Turkey
extinct Anatolian language of ancient Turkey

Examples

Scholars have discovered several religious texts written in Palaic.
The Palaic language became extinct during the first millennium BCE.
Linguists compare Palaic with Hittite to study early Anatolian cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hittite - an ancient Anatolian language closely related to Palaic
  • Synonym: Luwian - another Anatolian language related to Palaic

Etymology

From Latin Palaicus, referring to the ancient region of Pala in Anatolia.
