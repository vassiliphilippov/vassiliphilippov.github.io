lysozyme

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪsəˌzaɪm / (AmE), / ˈlaɪsəʊzaɪm / (BrE)
an enzyme present in bodily fluids such as saliva, sweat, and tears that destroys the cell walls of certain bacteria, protecting against infection
enzyme that destroys bacterial cell walls
medical

Examples

The presence of lysozyme in saliva helps protect the mouth from bacterial infections.
Scientists studied how lysozyme breaks down the cell walls of bacteria.
Tears contain lysozyme, which serves as a natural defense against pathogens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enzyme - a substance produced by organisms that acts as a catalyst for biochemical reactions

Etymology

From Greek 'lysis' (dissolution) + 'enzyme'.
