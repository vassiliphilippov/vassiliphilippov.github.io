Bartlett

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːrtlət / (AmE), / ˈbɑːtlɪt / (BrE)
A variety of pear that is juicy and yellow when ripe, commonly grown for its sweet flavor.
a variety of juicy yellow pear

Examples

She bought a ripe Bartlett at the market for her dessert.
The Bartlett is often used in fruit salads because of its juicy sweetness.
They planted several Bartlett pear trees in their backyard orchard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pear - an edible fruit produced by the pear tree

Etymology

Named after Enoch Bartlett, an American horticulturist who promoted the variety in the early 19th century.
