grippe

1. NOUN

/ ɡrɪp / (AmE), / ɡrɪp / (BrE)
an old-fashioned term for influenza, a highly contagious viral disease characterized by fever and respiratory symptoms
old term for influenza (the flu)
archaic medical

Examples

Doctors reported several cases of grippe during the harsh winter.
In the past, grippe epidemics caused widespread illness and disruption.
He was bedridden for a week after catching grippe from a coworker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: influenza - a highly contagious viral respiratory illness; the flu

Etymology

From French 'grippe' (seizure, influenza), first used in the 18th century.
