Yorkshire

1. PROPN

/ ˈjɔrk.ʃɪr / (AmE), / ˈjɔːk.ʃə / (BrE)
a historic county in northern England, formerly a large administrative area until 1974, now divided into several smaller counties.
historic county in northern England

Examples

He was born and raised in Yorkshire and still has a strong regional accent.
Many scenes from classic novels are set in the rolling hills of Yorkshire.
After 1974, Yorkshire was divided into several new administrative counties.

Etymology

From Old English Eoforwīc (York) + shire (county, district).
