bluffly

1. ADV

/ ˈblʌf.li / (AmE), / ˈblʌf.li / (BrE)
In a blunt, direct, and sometimes abrupt manner.
in a blunt, direct manner

Examples

The manager responded bluffly to the difficult question, refusing to sugarcoat his opinion.
He bluffly told the committee exactly what he thought of their proposal.
She answered bluffly, making it clear she had no patience for small talk.

Synset Examples

he spoke bluntly
he stated his opinion flat-out
he was criticized roundly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluntly - in a very direct and uncompromising manner
  • Antonym: subtly - in a delicate and indirect way

Etymology

From 'bluff' (direct, straightforward) + '-ly' (adverbial suffix).
