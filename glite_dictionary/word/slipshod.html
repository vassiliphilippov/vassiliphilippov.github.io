slipshod

1. ADJ

/ ˈslɪpˌʃɑd / (AmE), / ˈslɪpʃɒd / (BrE)
done in a careless or negligent way, showing a lack of attention to detail or effort
careless; lacking attention to detail

Examples

His report was criticized for its slipshod research and lack of supporting evidence.
The builder's slipshod methods resulted in a house full of problems.
Customers complained about the slipshod service at the restaurant.

Synset Examples

a most haphazard system of record keeping
slapdash work
slipshod spelling
sloppy workmanship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sloppy - showing a lack of care, attention, or effort
  • Antonym: meticulous - showing great attention to detail; very careful and precise

Etymology

From 'slip' + 'shod', originally meaning 'wearing slippers or loose shoes', later applied figuratively to carelessness.
