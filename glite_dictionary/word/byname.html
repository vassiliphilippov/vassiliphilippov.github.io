byname

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪˌneɪm / (AmE), / ˈbaɪneɪm / (BrE)
a familiar or alternative name given to a person, often used instead of their real name
familiar or alternative name

Examples

He became known by the byname 'the Conqueror' after his famous victory.
The poet's byname was widely recognized throughout the literary circles of his era.
Many kings are remembered more by their byname than by their actual name.

Synset Examples

Joe's mother would not use his nickname and always called him Joseph
Henry's nickname was Slim

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nickname - an informal, often humorous name given to a person

Etymology

From Middle English 'biname', from Old English 'binama' (a second name).
