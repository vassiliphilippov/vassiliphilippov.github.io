insemination

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌsɛmɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌsɛmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of sowing seeds in the ground, or, figuratively, of introducing something such as germs or ideas into a place or medium
the act of sowing or introducing seeds, germs, or ideas

Examples

In spring, the insemination of the fields by farmers marks the start of the cultivation cycle.
Some authors refer to the insemination of new ideas into society as a gradual process.
The insemination of bacteria into the nutrient solution is a key step in the experiment.

Etymology

From Latin 'inseminare' (to sow, implant), from 'in-' (in) + 'semen' (seed).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˌsɛm.əˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌsɛm.ɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of introducing semen into the genital tract of a female to achieve fertilization
introduction of semen for fertilization
medical

Examples

Artificial insemination has become a popular option for couples experiencing infertility.
The veterinarian performed insemination on the prize mare to ensure the continuation of her bloodline.
Advancements in insemination techniques have increased the success rate of breeding programs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fertilization - the union of male and female gametes to form a zygote

Etymology

From Latin 'inseminare', meaning 'to sow, implant seed'.
