pectus

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛktəs / (AmE), / ˈpɛktəs / (BrE)
the part of the body between the neck and the diaphragm; the chest, especially as referred to in anatomy or zoology
the chest (anatomical term)
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with a deformity of the pectus known as pectus excavatum.
During the examination, the doctor noted some tenderness in the pectus.
Certain birds have a pronounced pectus which aids in flight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chest - the front surface of a person's or animal's body between the neck and the abdomen

Etymology

From Latin 'pectus' meaning 'chest' or 'breast'.
