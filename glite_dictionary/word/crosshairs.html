crosshairs

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔsˌhɛrz / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌheəz / (BrE)
the center of interest or a target of attention, especially where someone or something is under scrutiny or attack
center of interest; target of focus

Examples

The celebrity's controversial remarks put her directly in the crosshairs of the media.
With declining profits, the company found itself in the crosshairs of investors demanding change.
Environmental groups are in the crosshairs as industry presses for fewer regulations.

Synset Examples

the war on terrorism has put Saddam Hussein in the crosshairs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotlight - public attention or focus
  • Synonym: bullseye - the center of a target; main focus

Etymology

From 'cross' + 'hairs', originally referring to the fine lines in optical sights used to aim at a target.
