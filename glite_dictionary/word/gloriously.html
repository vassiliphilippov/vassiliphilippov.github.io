gloriously

1. ADV

/ ˈɡlɔːriəsli / (AmE), / ˈɡlɔːriəsli / (BrE)
In a glorious or splendid manner; with great beauty, triumph, or delight.
in a glorious or splendid manner

Examples

The team emerged gloriously victorious after a hard-fought match.
She gloriously ignored the doubts and followed her dreams.
The sun set gloriously behind the mountains, painting the sky with vibrant colors.

Synset Examples

where others had failed he had gloriously succeeded

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnificently - in a magnificent manner
  • Synonym: splendidly - in a splendid manner
  • Antonym: miserably - in a miserable manner (opposite)

Etymology

From glorious + -ly, ultimately from Latin 'gloriosus'.

2. ADV

/ ˈɡlɔːriəsli / (AmE), / ˈɡlɔːriəsli / (BrE)
In a way that is magnificent, wonderful, or full of glory; with great delight or splendor.
in a magnificent or wonderful manner

Examples

The sun set gloriously behind the mountains, painting the sky with golden light.
She felt gloriously free as she danced across the field.
The team celebrated gloriously after winning the championship.

Synset Examples

how gloriously happy she had been during those few fleeting moments of time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnificently - in a splendid or impressive way
  • Synonym: beautifully - in a beautiful manner
  • Antonym: drably - in a dull or dreary manner

Etymology

From 'glorious' + -ly; 'glorious' from Latin 'gloriosus' (full of glory).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00350707-r
Synset POS: r
