St. Lawrence

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈlɔːrəns / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈlɒrəns / (BrE)
A Christian martyr of the 3rd century, famed for his charitable acts and his death by being roasted on a gridiron in Rome.
Christian martyr; 3rd-century Roman saint

Examples

Many churches are named after St. Lawrence in honor of his legacy.
According to legend, St. Lawrence was martyred by being roasted alive on a gridiron.
People celebrate the feast of St. Lawrence on August 10th every year.

Etymology

From Latin Laurentius, meaning 'from Laurentum', a city in Italy.

2. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈlɔːrəns / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈlɒrəns / (BrE)
a major river in North America that flows from the Great Lakes to the Gulf of Saint Lawrence and the North Atlantic Ocean, forming part of the border between Canada and the United States.
North American river flowing to the Atlantic

Examples

The St. Lawrence River serves as an important shipping route between the Great Lakes and the Atlantic Ocean.
Many species of whales can be spotted in the Gulf of St. Lawrence during summer.
The city of Montreal is located on an island in the St. Lawrence River.

Etymology

Named after Saint Lawrence, a Christian martyr; the river was named by French explorer Jacques Cartier, who arrived at the river on Saint Lawrence's feast day.
