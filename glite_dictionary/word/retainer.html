retainer

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈteɪnɚ / (AmE), / rɪˈteɪnə / (BrE)
a dental device worn in the mouth to keep teeth or a dental prosthesis in place after orthodontic treatment
dental appliance for maintaining teeth alignment
medical

Examples

After getting her braces removed, Maria wore a retainer to keep her teeth straight.
The dentist recommended using a retainer at night for several months.
Losing his retainer meant he had to get a replacement to maintain his dental alignment.

Etymology

From Middle English retener, from Old French retenir (to retain), from Latin retinere.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈteɪnər / (AmE), / rɪˈteɪnə / (BrE)
a sum of money paid in advance to secure the services of a professional, especially a lawyer
advance fee for professional services

Examples

The lawyer asked for a retainer before starting any work on the case.
They paid a monthly retainer to keep the consultant available for advice.
A large retainer was required to secure the services of the architect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deposit - sum of money paid as a guarantee or advance for services
  • Synonym: fee - payment made for professional services

Etymology

From Old French retenir (to keep), from Latin retinere (to hold back).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13344335-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈteɪnər / (AmE), / rɪˈteɪnə / (BrE)
a person who is employed to work in the service of another, especially as a servant in a household
household servant or attendant

Examples

The nobleman called for his most trusted retainer to bring him the message.
In medieval times, a retainer might serve a single family for generations.
The retainer prepared the guest room before the master's arrival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: servant - a person employed to perform domestic duties

Etymology

From Old French retenir (to retain), from Latin retinere (to hold back).
