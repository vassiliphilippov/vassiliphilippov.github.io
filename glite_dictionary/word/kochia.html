Kochia

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊʃi.ə / (AmE), / ˈkəʊʃɪə / (BrE)
a genus of plants in the goosefoot family, often grown for ornamental purposes and commonly called summer cypress
a genus of ornamental plants (summer cypress)

Examples

Many gardeners plant Kochia for its bright green foliage that turns red in autumn.
The drought-tolerant Kochia thrives in hot, dry conditions and poor soils.
In landscaping, Kochia is often used as a border or hedge plant due to its bushy growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summer cypress - an ornamental plant with bushy, feathery foliage, related to Kochia

Etymology

From the New Latin Kochia, named after German botanist Wilhelm Daniel Joseph Koch (1771–1849).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊkiə / (AmE), / ˈkəʊkiə / (BrE)
A small genus of annual or perennial herbs and shrubs in the family Amaranthaceae, commonly known as summer cypress.
genus of plants; summer cypress

Examples

The ornamental plant Kochia is often grown in gardens for its bright, bushy foliage.
Some farmers use Kochia as a fodder crop due to its drought resistance.
During autumn, Kochia turns a striking red, making landscapes unusually colorful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bassia - genus to which some species formerly classified as Kochia now belong

Etymology

Named after Wilhelm Daniel Joseph Koch, a German botanist.
