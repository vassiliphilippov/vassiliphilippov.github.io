laudator

1. NOUN

/ lɔːˈdeɪtər / (AmE), / lɔːˈdeɪtə / (BrE)
A person who expresses or communicates high praise, especially in a formal or public setting.
someone who gives high praise

Examples

The retiring professor received a glowing speech from his laudator at the farewell ceremony.
As a renowned laudator, she was often invited to deliver eulogies at important events.
The laudator praised the artist's work as both innovative and inspiring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: praiser - a person who praises someone or something
  • Antonym: critic - a person who expresses unfavorable judgments

Etymology

From Latin laudator, meaning 'one who praises', from laudare (to praise).
