Inachis io

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈneɪkɪs ˈiː.oʊ / (AmE), / ɪˈneɪkɪs ˈiː.əʊ / (BrE)
A European butterfly, commonly known as the peacock butterfly, with reddish-brown wings each marked with a purple eyespot.
European butterfly with eyespot markings

Examples

The brilliant Inachis io can often be spotted fluttering in European gardens during summer.
Children observe the stunning eyespots on the wings of an Inachis io as it lands on a flower.
After emerging in spring, the Inachis io becomes a spectacular sight in meadows across Britain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peacock butterfly - Common English name for Inachis io

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, named after Io, a figure in Greek mythology; 'Inachis' derives from Inachus, a river god and father of Io.
