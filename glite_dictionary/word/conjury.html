conjury

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌn.dʒə.ri / (AmE), / ˈkʌn.dʒə.ri / (BrE)
The act of summoning a spirit or devil, especially through magical rites or incantations.
act of summoning a spirit or devil

Examples

The old book described the secrets of conjury and summoning lost souls.
Fear filled the air as the wizard began his dark conjury by candlelight.
Legends warn of villages destroyed by reckless conjury in the past.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conjuration - the act of summoning a spirit by incantation

Etymology

From Latin 'coniūrāre' (to conspire, to swear together), via Old French and Middle English.
