Quercus petraea

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs pəˈtriːə / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs pəˈtriːə / (BrE)
A species of deciduous European oak, commonly known as the sessile oak, valued for its tough, elastic wood.
European sessile oak species

Examples

The durable timber from Quercus petraea is highly prized in European furniture making.
Many English woodlands are dominated by Quercus petraea and other native oak species.
Botanists often distinguish Quercus petraea from its close relative, the English oak, based on leaf shape and habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sessile oak - Common name for Quercus petraea, a European oak species
  • Degree Lower: Quercus robur - English oak, another major European oak species

Etymology

From Latin: *Quercus* (oak) + *petraea* (of rocks), referring to its typical habitat.
