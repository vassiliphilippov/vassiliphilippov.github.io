determinative

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈtɜːrmənətɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈtɜːmɪnətɪv / (BrE)
(Grammar) A word, typically from a limited set, that modifies a noun and determines its reference within a noun phrase, such as articles and demonstratives.
a noun modifier determining reference

Examples

The word 'the' is a determinative that specifies which noun is meant in a sentence.
In English grammar, a determinative like 'some' or 'those' helps clarify meaning.
Many languages use a determinative before nouns to indicate definiteness.

Etymology

From Latin determinativus (serving to determine).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈtɜːrmɪnətɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈtɜːmɪnətɪv / (BrE)
having the power or quality of deciding or determining the outcome of something; decisive.
decisive; determining

Examples

His vote was determinative in passing the new policy.
The evidence presented was determinative in the jury's decision.
That match proved to be the determinative event of the tournament.

Synset Examples

cast the deciding vote
the determinative (or determinant) battle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decisive - having the ability to decide something quickly and with confidence; determining an outcome
  • Antonym: indecisive - not able to make decisions quickly or effectively; not clearly determining the outcome

Etymology

From Latin determinativus ('serving to determine'), from determinare ('to determine').
