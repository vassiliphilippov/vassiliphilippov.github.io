amp

1. NOUN

/ æmp / (AmE), / æmp / (BrE)
the standard unit of electric current in the International System of Units, equal to one coulomb per second; ampere
unit of electric current (ampere)

Examples

The circuit breaker is rated for 20 amps of current.
He measured the electrical current and found it was 15 amps.
Most household appliances use just a few amps of electricity.

Synset Examples

a typical household circuit carries 15 to 50 amps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ampere - the SI base unit of electric current

Etymology

Shortened form of 'ampere', from French 'Ampère', named after André-Marie Ampère (1775–1836), French physicist.
