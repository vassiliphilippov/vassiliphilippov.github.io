plat

1. NOUN

/ plæt / (AmE), / plæt / (BrE)
a map, drawn to scale, showing the divisions, streets, and lots of a piece of land, especially for survey or legal purposes
map of land divisions or lots

Examples

The property developer submitted a plat to the city for approval of the new subdivision.
According to the plat, the lot extends all the way to the riverbank.
Before construction can begin, a detailed plat must be filed with the county office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plan - a diagram or drawing showing the layout of something

Etymology

From Middle English plat (flat surface, map), from Old French plat (flat, plate).

2. VERB

/ plæt / (AmE), / plæt / (BrE)
to make a map or plan of (an area of land), especially for the purpose of dividing it into lots
draw a map or plan of land

Examples

The company hired a surveyor to plat the new subdivision before construction began.
Developers must plat the land according to city regulations before selling lots.
The town was carefully platted with wide streets and public parks.

Synset Examples

Plat the town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plot - to make a map or plan, especially of land; to plan out

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *plat* (flat piece or plot of land), from Old French *plat* (flat, level).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
