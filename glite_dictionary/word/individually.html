individually

1. ADV

/ ˌɪndəˈvɪdʒuəli / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈvɪdjʊəli / (BrE)
Separately; considered one at a time or apart from others.
separately; one at a time

Examples

Each member of the team was evaluated individually during the interview process.
The artworks are priced individually, not as a set.
Although the students worked individually, they shared their results with the class.

Synset Examples

taken individually, the rooms were, in fact, square
the fine points are treated singly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: collectively - As a group; together rather than separately

Etymology

From 'individual' + '-ly', where 'individual' is from Medieval Latin 'individualis'.
