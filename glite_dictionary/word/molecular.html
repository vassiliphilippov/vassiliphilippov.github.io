molecular

1. ADJ

/ məˈlɛkjələr / (AmE), / məˈlɛkjʊlə / (BrE)
relating to the simplest or most basic structural or organizational units of something; elementary.
relating to basic or elementary structure

Examples

The scientists focused on the molecular aspects of human perception in their research.
Understanding the molecular foundations of behavior can help explain complex psychological phenomena.
Her analysis broke the process down to its most molecular components.

Synset Examples

proceed by more and more detailed analysis to the molecular facts of perception

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elementary - relating to the basic or fundamental nature of something

Etymology

From Latin 'molecula', diminutive of 'moles' (mass).

2. ADJ

/ məˈlɛkjəlɚ / (AmE), / məˈlɛkjʊlə / (BrE)
relating to or produced by or consisting of molecules
relating to molecules

Examples

The scientist studied the molecular composition of the new compound.
Oxygen in the air often exists in its molecular form, as O₂.
Understanding molecular interactions is key to chemistry and biology.

Synset Examples

molecular structure
molecular oxygen
molecular weight is the sum of all the atoms in a molecule

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atomic - relating to atoms

Etymology

From molecule + -ar, ultimately from Latin 'molecula' (small mass).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02911952-a
Synset POS: a
