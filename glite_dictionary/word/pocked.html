pocked

1. ADJ

/ pɑkt / (AmE), / pɒkt / (BrE)
marked with pitted scars, especially as if caused by smallpox, acne, or other eruptive skin diseases
marked with small pitted scars

Examples

His face was pocked from years of adolescent acne.
The ancient statue was pocked and weathered by centuries of rain and wind.
The dessert's surface was oddly pocked with tiny holes, giving it an unappetizing appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitted - marked with small hollows or pits
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even, flat surface without pits or scars

2. ADJ

/ pɑkt / (AmE), / pɒkt / (BrE)
Marked with small holes or pits, especially as a result of damage or wear.
marked with holes or pits

Examples

The old road was badly pocked after years of heavy truck traffic.
His face was pocked with scars from teenage acne.
The metal surface became pocked due to long-term exposure to corrosive chemicals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitted - marked with small pits or indentations

Etymology

From Middle English *pokked* (covered with pocks), related to 'pock' (a pustule or pit).
