Voltaire

1. PROPN

/ voʊlˈtɛər / (AmE), / vɒlˈtɛə / (BrE)
(1694–1778) A French writer, philosopher, and leading figure of the 18th-century Enlightenment, known for his advocacy of freedom of speech, religion, and separation of church and state.
French Enlightenment writer and philosopher

Examples

Many scholars regard Voltaire as a founding voice of the Enlightenment.
In his writings, Voltaire argued passionately for civil liberties and social reform.
The influence of Voltaire can be seen in modern discussions on religious tolerance.

Etymology

Pen name of François-Marie Arouet, 18th-century French philosopher and author.
