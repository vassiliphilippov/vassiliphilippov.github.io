majuscular

1. ADJ

/ məˈdʒʌs.kjələr / (AmE), / məˈdʒʌs.kjʊ.lə / (BrE)
Relating to or written in majuscules; having the characteristics of uppercase or capital letters.
written in or like capital letters

Examples

The ancient manuscript was written entirely in majuscular script.
Some typographers specialize in designing majuscular alphabets for monumental inscriptions.
In early medieval texts, the distinction between minuscule and majuscular writing was significant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: majuscule - a large, uppercase letter, especially in ancient manuscripts

Etymology

From Latin 'maiusculus' (somewhat larger), diminutive of 'maior' (greater).
