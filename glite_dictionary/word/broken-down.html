broken-down

1. ADJ

/ ˈbroʊkəndaʊn / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊkəndaʊn / (BrE)
(of a thing) in very poor condition due to age or neglect; dilapidated or ruined.
in very poor or dilapidated condition

Examples

The old barn was completely broken-down after years of neglect.
They lived in a broken-down house at the edge of town.
We had to replace the broken-down machinery at the factory.

Synset Examples

a street of bedraggled tenements
a broken-down fence
a ramshackle old pier
a tumble-down shack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dilapidated - in a state of disrepair or ruin
  • Synonym: ramshackle - badly made or in disrepair

2. ADJ

/ ˈbroʊkən daʊn / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊkən daʊn / (BrE)
(of a machine, vehicle, or structure) no longer working properly; not in working order, often due to age or disrepair.
not in working order, especially due to age

Examples

They had to walk home after their broken-down car stopped on the highway.
The old, broken-down barn was barely standing after the storm.
We passed several broken-down washing machines at the back of the repair shop.

Synset Examples

had to push the broken-down car
a broken-down tractor fit only for children to play on

Related Terms

  • Synonym: out of order - not working or not functioning properly
  • Antonym: functioning - operating or working properly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02132267-s
Synset POS: s
