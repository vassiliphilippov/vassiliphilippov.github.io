harlotry

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrlətri / (AmE), / ˈhɑːlətri / (BrE)
the practice or act of engaging in sexual intercourse for money; prostitution
engaging in sex for payment; prostitution

Examples

The city was once notorious for harlotry and vice.
He spoke out against the moral decline and the spread of harlotry in society.
Throughout history, harlotry has often been both condemned and romanticized in literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prostitution - the practice or occupation of engaging in sexual activity for payment

Etymology

From Old French ‘harloterie’, derived from ‘harlot’ (prostitute or rogue).
