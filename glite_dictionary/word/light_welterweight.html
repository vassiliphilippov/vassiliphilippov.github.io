light welterweight

1. NOUN

/ laɪt ˈwɛltərˌweɪt / (AmE), / laɪt ˈwɛltəˌweɪt / (BrE)
a boxer in an amateur or professional competition who weighs no more than 140 pounds (63.5 kilograms)
a boxer weighing up to 140 pounds

Examples

The boxer moved up to the light welterweight division after winning the title at lightweight.
He competed as a light welterweight in the amateur championships last year.
Several famous champions have held the light welterweight title over the years.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: welterweight - a boxer in a weight class above lightweight and below middleweight
  • Degree Lower: lightweight - a boxer lighter than the light welterweight category

Etymology

From 'light' (referring to weight category) + 'welterweight' (a class in boxing).
