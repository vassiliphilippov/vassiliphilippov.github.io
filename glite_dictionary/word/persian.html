Persian

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrʒən / (AmE), / ˈpɜːʃən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Iran, especially one who belongs to the principal ethnic group of that country
a native or inhabitant of Iran

Examples

Many Persians celebrate Nowruz, the Persian New Year, with family gatherings.
As a Persian, he is fluent in Farsi and takes pride in his cultural heritage.
The museum features artifacts from ancient Persian civilizations dating back thousands of years.

Synset Examples

the majority of Irani are Persian Shiite Muslims

Etymology

From Latin 'Persa', from Greek 'Persēs', from Old Persian 'Pārsa' (an inhabitant of Parsa).

Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɜrʒən / (AmE), / ˈpɜːʃən / (BrE)
of or relating to Iran, its people, language, or culture
of Iran or its people, language, or culture

Examples

He studied Persian history at university.
The museum features a collection of Persian carpets and art.
She speaks fluent Persian and often visits her relatives in Tehran.

Synset Examples

Iranian mountains
Iranian security police

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iranian - of or relating to Iran, its people, or language
  • Synonym: Persia - former name for the country of Iran or relating to its culture

Etymology

From Latin 'Persia' via Ancient Greek 'Persís', ultimately from Old Persian 'Pārsa' (the name of an Iranian people and their land).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03086619-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːʒən / (AmE), / ˈpɜːʃən / (BrE)
the language of Iran (historically known as Persia), in any of its ancient or modern forms
the language of Iran (Persia)

Examples

He is fluent in both English and Persian.
Many famous poets wrote in Persian during the medieval period.
The ancient texts were translated from Old Persian into modern languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Farsi - modern Persian language spoken in Iran

Etymology

From Latin 'Persianus', from Greek 'Persikos', meaning 'of Persia'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06986787-n
Synset POS: n
