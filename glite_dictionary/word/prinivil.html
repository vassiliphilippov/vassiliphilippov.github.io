Prinivil

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪnɪvɪl / (AmE), / ˈprɪnɪvɪl / (BrE)
A trade name for lisinopril, an ACE inhibitor used to treat high blood pressure and after heart attacks.
antihypertensive drug, trade name
medical

Examples

After his heart attack, the doctor prescribed Prinivil to help manage his blood pressure.
Some patients experience a dry cough as a side effect of Prinivil.
Prinivil is often taken once daily to control hypertension.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lisinopril - an ACE inhibitor drug used to treat high blood pressure and heart failure
