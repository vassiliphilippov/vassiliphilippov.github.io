Crotalus mitchellii

1. NOUN

/ kroʊˈtæləs mɪˈtʃɛli.aɪ / (AmE), / krəʊˈtæləs mɪˈtʃɛli.aɪ / (BrE)
A species of rattlesnake found in southwestern Arizona and Baja California, characterized by markings that usually blend with its surroundings.
a rattlesnake of southwestern Arizona and Baja California

Examples

Hikers in Baja California should be cautious of the Crotalus mitchellii that inhabit rocky outcrops.
The pattern of the Crotalus mitchellii helps it blend into its desert environment.
Researchers found a Crotalus mitchellii near the border of southwestern Arizona.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rattlesnake - a venomous American snake with a segmented rattle at the end of its tail

Etymology

From Latin; Crotalus from Greek 'krotalon' (rattle), 'mitchellii' honoring American naturalist Silas Weir Mitchell.
