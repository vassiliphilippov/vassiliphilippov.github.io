Combretaceae

1. NOUN

/ kəmˌbrɛtəˈsiːiː / (AmE), / kɒmˌbrɛtəˈsiːiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical trees and shrubs in the order Myrtales, including genera such as Combretum and Terminalia.
family of tropical trees and shrubs (botany)

Examples

Several species within the Combretaceae family are valued for their medicinal properties.
The Combretaceae exhibit a remarkable diversity across tropical and subtropical regions.
Botanists identified the specimen as belonging to the Combretaceae.

Etymology

From genus name Combretum + standard botanical family suffix -aceae.

a family of tropical trees and shrubs
medical

Examples

The Combretaceae family includes many species adapted to dry climates.
Some members of the Combretaceae are important in traditional medicine for their healing properties.
Botanists have identified over 600 species within the Combretaceae.

Etymology

From the type genus Combretum, from Latinized Greek kombretos (a kind of plant).
