La Fayette

1. PROPN

/ lɑː fəˈjɛt / (AmE), / la fəˈjɛt / (BrE)
A French soldier and aristocrat (1757–1834) who served as a general under George Washington in the American Revolution and became a significant figure in both American and French history.
French general in the American Revolution

Examples

La Fayette played a key role in securing French support for the American Revolutionary cause.
Many cities in the United States are named after La Fayette in honor of his contributions.
La Fayette's close friendship with George Washington is well documented in American history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Marquis de Lafayette - French general who fought in the American Revolution

Etymology

From French, full name: Marie-Joseph Paul Yves Roch Gilbert du Motier, Marquis de La Fayette.
