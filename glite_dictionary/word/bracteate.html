bracteate

1. ADJ

/ ˈbræk.ti.eɪt / (AmE), / ˈbræk.ti.eɪt / (BrE)
(Botany) Having bracts; bearing bracts.
having bracts (botany)

Examples

Many flowering plants in the family Asteraceae are bracteate, with small leaf-like structures beneath the flower heads.
The bracteate inflorescence provided protection and support for the emerging flowers.
This species can be distinguished from others by its bracteate stems, which are lined with narrow bracts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ebracteate - lacking bracts; without bracts (botany)

Etymology

From Latin bracteatus (covered with plates or scales, having bracts), from bractea (thin plate, bract).
