spatula

1. NOUN

/ ˈspætʃələ / (AmE), / ˈspatjʊlə / (BrE)
A hand tool with a thin, flexible blade, used for mixing, spreading, or lifting soft substances, especially in cooking or laboratory work.
tool with thin, flexible blade for mixing or spreading

Examples

She used a spatula to smooth the icing on the cake.
The chemist stirred the mixture gently with a spatula.
Flip the pancakes with a spatula to cook both sides evenly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palette knife - a similar flexible blade tool used in painting or cooking

Etymology

From Latin *spatula* (a broad, flat piece), diminutive of *spatha* (broad, flat tool or weapon).

2. NOUN

/ ˈspætʃələ / (AmE), / ˈspætjʊlə / (BrE)
A kitchen utensil with a broad, flat, flexible blade used for lifting, turning, or spreading food.
flat kitchen tool for lifting or turning food

Examples

She used a spatula to flip the pancakes without breaking them.
The chef always keeps a spatula nearby while grilling burgers.
Carefully slide the spatula under the cookies before lifting them off the tray.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turner - a kitchen utensil for turning food while cooking

Etymology

From Latin *spatula*, diminutive of *spatha* (broad, flat tool or weapon).
