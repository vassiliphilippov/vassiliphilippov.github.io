Oecanthus

1. NOUN

/ iˈkænθəs / (AmE), / iˈkanθəs / (BrE)
A genus of tree crickets found mainly in North America and known for their musical chirping.
genus of tree crickets

Examples

The Oecanthus genus includes several species of tree crickets known for their melodic songs.
Several Oecanthus species play important roles in their ecosystems as both prey and pollinators.
Entomologists identified the insect as belonging to the Oecanthus genus due to its distinctive chirps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree cricket - any cricket of the subfamily Oecanthinae, known for living in trees and shrubs

Etymology

From Greek 'oikos' (house) and 'anthos' (flower), as some species are found in flowers.

2. NOUN

/ iˈkænθəs / (AmE), / iˈkænθəs / (BrE)
a genus of crickets known as tree crickets, found in many parts of the world; members are slender, nocturnal insects producing distinctive songs.
a genus of tree crickets

Examples

The delicate wings of the Oecanthus amplify its mellow nighttime song.
Many horticulturists welcome Oecanthus species as beneficial insects in their gardens.
Scientists identified a new Oecanthus species in the South American rainforest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree cricket - any cricket of the subfamily Oecanthinae, including Oecanthus

Etymology

From Greek 'oikos' (house) and 'anthos' (flower), referring to the cricket's habitat.
