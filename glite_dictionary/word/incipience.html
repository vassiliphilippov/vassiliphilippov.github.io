incipience

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɪpiəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪpiəns / (BrE)
the beginning or initial stage of existence or development; the state of beginning to exist or become apparent
beginning stage or emergence

Examples

The incipience of technological change can be difficult to predict.
Her research focused on the incipience of language in early childhood.
Many revolutions fail to be recognized in their incipience, only becoming clear in hindsight.

Synset Examples

he placed the incipience of democratic faith at around 1850
it is designed to arrest monopolies in their incipiency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: onset - the start or beginning of something, especially something unpleasant
  • Synonym: outset - the very start or beginning of something
  • Antonym: conclusion - the end or finish of an event or process

Etymology

From Latin incipientia (beginning), from incipere (to begin).
