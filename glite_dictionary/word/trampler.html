trampler

1. NOUN

/ ˈtræmplər / (AmE), / ˈtræmplə / (BrE)
a person who walks with a heavy, noisy step or stamps forcefully on the ground
person who walks or stamps heavily

Examples

The trampler left muddy footprints all over the kitchen floor.
She scolded her son for being a trampler when he ran inside.
You could hear the trampler coming down the hallway from a mile away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stomper - someone who stamps or walks heavily
  • Antonym: tiptoer - someone who walks quietly on their toes

Etymology

From the verb 'trample', from Middle English 'tramplen', of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtræmp.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈtræm.plə / (BrE)
a person or animal that injures others by trampling them
person or animal who injures by trampling

Examples

The unruly crowd surged forward, and one unfortunate fan became the victim of a trampler.
During the stampede, several tramplers were later identified by security footage.
A trampler can cause serious injuries in a chaotic situation like that.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trample - to tread heavily so as to injure, crush, or destroy
  • Synonym: stomper - someone who stomps heavily, possibly causing injury
