Eupatorium capillifolium

1. NOUN

/ juːˌpeɪtɔːriəm ˌkæpɪlɪˈfoʊliəm / (AmE), / juːˌpeɪtɔːrɪəm ˌkapɪlɪˈfəʊlɪəm / (BrE)
a weedy plant native to the southeastern United States, characterized by divided leaves and clusters of greenish flowers
weedy Southeastern US plant with divided leaves

Examples

The fields were overgrown with Eupatorium capillifolium during late summer.
Botanists identified the unusual greenish flowers as those of Eupatorium capillifolium.
Gardeners often remove Eupatorium capillifolium because it can quickly spread in warm climates.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Eupatorium' (from Greek 'Eupatorion,' after Mithridates Eupator) and Latin 'capillifolium' meaning 'hair-leaved.'
