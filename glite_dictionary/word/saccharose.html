saccharose

1. NOUN

/ ˈsækəˌroʊs / (AmE), / ˈsak.ə.rəʊs / (BrE)
a complex sugar (disaccharide) found in many plants, commonly used as a sweetening agent; also known as sucrose
a type of sugar; sucrose

Examples

Many fruits and vegetables naturally contain saccharose as a source of sweetness.
The chemist measured the concentration of saccharose in the sample.
Beet sugar is rich in saccharose and is widely used in food production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sucrose - the common table sugar; the disaccharide composed of glucose and fructose

Etymology

From French 'saccharose', from Greek 'sakkharon' (sugar) + '-ose' (sugar suffix).
