fluorochrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈflʊr.oʊˌkroʊm / (AmE), / ˈflɔː.rə.krəʊm / (BrE)
a fluorescent chemical compound used to stain specimens in fluorescence microscopy
fluorescent stain for microscopy
medical

Examples

The scientist added a fluorochrome to the slide to visualize the cell structures.
Certain antibodies are tagged with a fluorochrome to track proteins under a microscope.
A variety of fluorochromes can be selected depending on the required emission wavelength.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fluorophore - a chemical part that causes fluorescence in a molecule

Etymology

From 'fluoro-' (fluorescence) + Greek 'khrōma' (color).
