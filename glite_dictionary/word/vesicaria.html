Vesicaria

1. NOUN

/ vɛsɪˈkɛəriə / (AmE), / vɛsɪˈkɛːrɪə / (BrE)
A small genus of herbs, chiefly found in the Mediterranean region, commonly known as bladderpods.
Mediterranean genus of bladderpod plants

Examples

Many species in the genus Vesicaria are adapted to dry, rocky soils near the Mediterranean coast.
Botanists studied the unique seed pods of Vesicaria to understand their ecological role.
The yellow flowers of Vesicaria make it easily recognizable in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bladderpod - a common name for plants in the genus Vesicaria

Etymology

From Latin *vesica* (bladder), referring to the shape of the seed pods.

2. NOUN

/ ˌvɛsɪˈkɛəriə / (AmE), / ˌvɛsɪˈkɛːrɪə / (BrE)
A small genus of herbs in the Brassicaceae (mustard) family, mainly native to the Mediterranean region, commonly known as bladderpods.
genus of Mediterranean herbs (bladderpods)

Examples

The genus Vesicaria is known for its distinctive bladder-like seed pods.
Botanists discovered several new species within Vesicaria during their expedition in southern Europe.
Many Vesicaria species thrive in the dry, rocky soils of the Mediterranean coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Brassicaceae - the mustard family of plants

Etymology

From Latin 'vesica' meaning 'bladder', referring to the bladder-like seed pods.
