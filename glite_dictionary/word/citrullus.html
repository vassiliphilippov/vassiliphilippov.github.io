Citrullus

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈtruːləs / (AmE), / sɪˈtrʌləs / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the gourd family (Cucurbitaceae), including the watermelon and related species
genus of plants including watermelons

Examples

The genus Citrullus includes several species grown for their edible fruit.
Botanists have studied the evolution of Citrullus to better understand watermelon cultivation.
Most commercial watermelons are classified under Citrullus lanatus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watermelon - the large, typically green fruit of Citrullus lanatus

Etymology

From Latin *citrullus* (watermelon).

2. NOUN

/ sɪˈtruːləs / (AmE), / sɪˈtrʌləs / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the gourd family (Cucurbitaceae), including watermelons and related species, native to Africa and Asia.
genus of plants including watermelons

Examples

The Citrullus genus encompasses species that thrive in warm, arid environments.
Many plant breeders study Citrullus to improve watermelon varieties.
The wild relatives of Citrullus offer important genetic diversity for crop development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watermelon - a large, round fruit with hard green rind and sweet watery red or yellow flesh

Etymology

From Latin 'citrullus' (gourd, watermelon).
