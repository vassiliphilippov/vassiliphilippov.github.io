durbar

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɜːrbɑːr / (AmE), / ˈdɜːbɑː / (BrE)
a large reception room in the palace of an Indian prince where audiences and formal meetings are held
audience/reception room in Indian palace

Examples

The Maharaja welcomed his guests in the splendid durbar.
The royal portraits hung along the walls of the durbar.
Ceremonial meetings were often held in the palace durbar.

Etymology

From Hindi दरबार (darbār), from Persian دربار (darbār) meaning 'court' or 'audience hall'.
