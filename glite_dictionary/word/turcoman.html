Turcoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜːrkəmæn / (AmE), / ˈtɜːkəmən / (BrE)
a member of a Turkic ethnic group originating from Turkmenistan and surrounding regions
member of a Turkic ethnic group

Examples

The Turcoman people have a rich tradition of storytelling and music.
Many Turcomans settled in the region centuries ago and have maintained their cultural identity.
A Turcoman rug is highly valued for its intricate patterns and craftsmanship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turkmen - a member of the Turkic ethnic group from Turkmenistan

Etymology

From Turkish 'Türkmen' via Medieval Latin 'Turcomannus'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrkəˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈtɜːkəmæn / (BrE)
the Turkic language spoken by the Turkoman people, principally in Turkmenistan and surrounding regions
the Turkic language of the Turkoman people

Examples

Few people outside the region speak Turcoman as their native language.
The grammar of Turcoman shows clear similarities to other Turkic languages.
She is studying Turcoman to research local folklore and oral traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turkmen - the official language of Turkmenistan, a Turkic language

Etymology

From Persian 'Turkmān', referring to the Turkoman people.

Synset POS: n
