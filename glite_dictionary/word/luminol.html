luminol

1. NOUN

/ ˈluː.mə.nɒl / (AmE), / ˈluː.mɪ.nɒl / (BrE)
a chemical compound used in forensic science that glows with a blue light when it reacts with blood, enabling the detection of trace amounts at crime scenes
chemical used to detect blood with glowing reaction

Examples

The forensic team sprayed luminol at the crime scene to reveal hidden blood stains.
After applying luminol, a faint blue glow appeared on the suspect's shirt, indicating the presence of blood.
Luminol is a valuable tool for investigators trying to reconstruct violent events.

Synset Examples

The reaction that the luminol and hydrogen peroxide creates doesn't last long at all -- maybe a couple of minutes.

Etymology

From Latin 'lumen' (light) + '-ol' (chemical suffix for alcohols), referencing its light-emitting properties.
