Lepiota naucina

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛpiˈoʊtə naʊˈsɪnə / (AmE), / ˌlɛpɪˈəʊtə naʊˈkɪnə / (BrE)
A species of agaric mushroom with a greyish white cap and gills that change from pink to dingy red.
a species of white agaric mushroom

Examples

The Lepiota naucina is sometimes mistaken for edible species, so caution is advised when foraging.
In the field guide, Lepiota naucina is described as having gills that turn from pink to red with age.
Researchers collected several Lepiota naucina specimens to study their spore patterns and toxicity.

Etymology

From the genus name Lepiota (Greek lepis 'scale') and the species name naucina (Latin naucinus 'relating to ships', possibly referring to habitat or appearance).
