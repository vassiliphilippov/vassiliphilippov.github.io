bacillar

1. ADJ

/ bəˈsɪlər / (AmE), / bəˈsɪlə / (BrE)
relating to, produced by, or containing bacilli (rod-shaped bacteria)
relating to bacilli bacteria
medical

Examples

The infection displayed a marked bacillar pattern under microscopic examination.
The presence of bacillar forms in the blood suggested a bacterial etiology.
Tuberculosis is a classic example of a bacillar disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacillary - relating to bacilli; alternative form of 'bacillar'

Etymology

From Latin 'bacillus' meaning 'small rod' + '-ar.'

2. ADJ

/ bəˈsɪlər / (AmE), / bəˈsɪlə / (BrE)
shaped like or resembling a bacillus; rod-shaped, especially in reference to bacteria.
rod-shaped; resembling a bacillus
medical

Examples

Under the microscope, the cell exhibited a distinct bacillar form.
Some bacteria show bacillar or rod-like shapes, while others are spherical.
The pathologist noted the bacillar structure of the infectious agent in the sample.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacilliform - shaped like a bacillus; rod-shaped

Etymology

From New Latin bacillus (rod-shaped bacterium) + -ar (forming adjectives).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02152403-s
Synset POS: s
