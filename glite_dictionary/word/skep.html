skep

1. NOUN

/ skɛp / (AmE), / skɛp / (BrE)
a domed container, traditionally made of coiled straw, used as a beehive.
traditional domed beehive of straw

Examples

The old farmhouse still had a skep in the garden, although it was no longer used for keeping bees.
Traditionally, beekeepers would craft a skep from bundles of straw coiled tightly together.
A row of skeps lined the stone wall, each buzzing with activity in the summer sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beehive - a structure in which bees are kept

Etymology

From Old English 'sceppe', meaning 'basket, hamper'.

2. NOUN

/ skɛp / (AmE), / skɛp / (BrE)
a large round wicker basket, especially one traditionally used on farms or for keeping bees
large round wicker basket (farm use)

Examples

The farmer filled the skep with freshly picked apples from the orchard.
Traditional beekeepers often used a straw skep to house their bees.
She admired the craftsmanship of the old skep displayed at the agricultural fair.

Etymology

From Old English 'sceppe', related to Old Norse 'skeppa' (bushel measure).
