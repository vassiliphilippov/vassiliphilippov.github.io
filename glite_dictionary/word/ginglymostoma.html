Ginglymostoma

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡɪŋɡlimoʊˈstoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌɡɪŋɡlɪmɒˈstəʊmə / (BrE)
A genus of nurse sharks found in warm coastal waters, known for their broad heads and barbels near the mouth.
genus of nurse sharks

Examples

Researchers observed a group of Ginglymostoma swimming near the coral reef.
The Ginglymostoma is distinguished by its rounded head and distinctive barbels.
Aquariums often house Ginglymostoma due to their docile nature and adaptability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nurse shark - a common name for sharks of the family Ginglymostomatidae

Etymology

From Greek 'ginglymos' (hinge) + 'stoma' (mouth), referring to their jaw morphology.
