damaged

1. ADJ

/ ˈdæmɪdʒd / (AmE), / ˈdæmɪdʒd / (BrE)
harmed, injured, or spoiled so that it is less useful or valuable
harmed or spoiled; less useful or valuable

Examples

Some of the books were damaged during the move.
Insurance will not cover damaged property from natural disasters.
The damaged car was taken to the mechanic for repairs.

Synset Examples

I won't buy damaged goods
the storm left a wake of badly damaged buildings

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intact - not damaged; complete and whole
  • Degree Higher: ruined - completely destroyed or severely damaged
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00682414-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈdæmɪdʒd / (AmE), / ˈdamɪdʒd / (BrE)
(of a reputation, character, etc.) having lost respect or status, especially as a result of unjust criticism or accusations.
(of reputation) brought into disrepute

Examples

Rumors and false accusations left her reputation permanently damaged in the community.
After the scandal, the company struggled to recover from its damaged public image.
The athlete's damaged credibility made it difficult for him to secure new sponsorships.

Synset Examples

a discredited politician
her damaged reputation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discredited - (of a person or idea) having lost respect or trust
  • Antonym: restored - (of reputation) returned to a good standing

Etymology

From the verb 'damage', from Old French 'damager' (to inflict loss or injury).
