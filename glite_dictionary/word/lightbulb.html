lightbulb

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪtˌbʌlb / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtˌbʌlb / (BrE)
an electric lamp with a glass enclosure that contains a wire filament which produces light when heated by an electric current
electric lamp with a filament inside a glass bulb

Examples

I replaced the old lightbulb in the living room with a new energy-efficient one.
Please make sure the lamp is unplugged before changing the lightbulb.
The kitchen was dark because the lightbulb had burned out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lamp - a device for giving light

Etymology

Formed from 'light' + 'bulb', referring to its function and shape.
