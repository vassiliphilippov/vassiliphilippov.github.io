ludicrously

1. ADV

/ ˈluː.dɪ.krəs.li / (AmE), / ˈluː.dɪ.krəs.li / (BrE)
in a way that is so absurd, unreasonable, or exaggerated that it arouses laughter or ridicule
in a laughably absurd manner

Examples

Their claims were ludicrously exaggerated to make a point.
The hat was ludicrously large for her small head.
He was ludicrously underdressed for such a formal event.

Synset Examples

her income was laughably small, but she managed to live well

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absurdly - in a way that is ridiculously unreasonable or illogical
  • Antonym: seriously - in a way that is not intended to cause laughter or ridicule

Etymology

Formed from 'ludicrous' (from Latin 'ludicrus', from 'ludus' meaning 'play, sport') + '-ly'.
