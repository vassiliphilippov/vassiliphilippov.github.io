ever

1. ADV

/ ˈɛvɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛvə / (BrE)
(especially in the phrase 'ever so') Used to emphasize the degree of an adjective or adverb; very.
emphasizer meaning 'very' (esp. 'ever so')

Examples

The cake was ever so delicious that everyone asked for seconds.
He is ever so grateful for your help during his studies.
She looked ever so pleased when she received the award.

Synset Examples

she was ever so friendly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: very - to a high degree; used to emphasize an adjective or adverb

Etymology

From Old English 'ǣfre', of uncertain origin.

2. ADV

/ ˈɛvər / (AmE), / ˈɛvə / (BrE)
at any time; used to refer to a time, whether in the past, present, or future, often in questions and negative statements.
at any time

Examples

Have you ever visited Japan?
Hardly anyone has ever seen such a rare bird.
If you ever need help, just ask.

Synset Examples

did you ever smoke?
the best con man of all time

Related Terms

  • Antonym: never - not at any time; not ever

Etymology

From Old English 'ǣfre' (ever), of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00147423-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ˈɛvər / (AmE), / ˈɛvə / (BrE)
at all times; always; on every occasion
at all times; always

Examples

She is ever optimistic, no matter what challenges she faces.
The museum is ever open to the public during daylight hours.
He has ever been loyal to his friends throughout his life.

Synset Examples

ever hoping to strike it rich
ever busy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: always - at all times; on every occasion
  • Antonym: never - at no time; not ever

Etymology
