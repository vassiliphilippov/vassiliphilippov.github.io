ischemia

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈskiːmiə / (AmE), / ɪˈskiːmɪə / (BrE)
A restriction of blood supply to a part of the body, often due to a blocked blood vessel, resulting in a shortage of oxygen needed for cellular metabolism.
reduced blood supply to body tissue
medical

Examples

Early detection of ischemia can prevent irreversible damage to heart tissue.
The patient’s leg pain was caused by ischemia due to a blocked artery.
MRI scans revealed areas of ischemia in the brain after the stroke.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: anemia - a condition in which the blood lacks enough healthy red blood cells

Etymology

From Greek 'iskhaimos' (stopping blood), from 'iskhein' (to keep back) + 'haima' (blood).
