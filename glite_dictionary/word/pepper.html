pepper

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛpər / (AmE), / ˈpɛpə / (BrE)
A pungent spice made from the dried, ground berries of the pepper plant, used to season or flavor food.
pungent seasoning spice

Examples

He sprinkled some pepper over his scrambled eggs for extra flavor.
The chef always prefers to use freshly ground pepper in her recipes.
Please pass the salt and pepper shakers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: salt - a common white crystalline substance used for seasoning
  • Degree Higher: chili - a hot-tasting pod of a type of capsicum used for seasoning

Etymology

From Old English *pipor*, from Latin *piper* (pepper), from Sanskrit *pippali*.

4. NOUN

/ ˈpɛpər / (AmE), / ˈpɛpə / (BrE)
A climbing woody vine (Piper nigrum) native to southern India and Sri Lanka, cultivated for its dark red berries, known as peppercorns, which are the source of black, white, and green pepper used as a spice.
woody vine with peppercorns (Piper nigrum)

Examples

The pepper vine thrives in the humid climate of southern India.
Farmers harvest the ripe pepper berries before they are dried to make black pepper.
Wild pepper can be found growing in parts of Sri Lanka and Burma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black pepper - spice made from dried peppercorns

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'pippalī', via Latin 'piper'; source of English 'pepper'.

5. NOUN

/ ˈpɛpər / (AmE), / ˈpɛpə / (BrE)
Any of several tropical plants of the genus Capsicum, which bear fruits (peppers) used as vegetables or spices.
tropical plant of the genus Capsicum

Examples

The pepper plants thrived in the warm, humid climate of the greenhouse.
She grows several varieties of pepper in her backyard garden, including jalapeños and bell peppers.
Capsaicin, found in the fruit of the pepper plant, is what makes some peppers taste hot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Capsicum - genus of tropical plants producing peppers
  • Degree Lower: bell pepper - mild, sweet pepper of the species Capsicum annuum
  • Degree Higher: chili pepper - hot, pungent pepper of various species of Capsicum

Etymology

From Old English *pipor*, from Latin *piper*, from Greek *peperi*, ultimately from Sanskrit *pippalī* (long pepper).

6. NOUN

/ ˈpɛpɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɛpə / (BrE)
A hollow, fleshy fruit of various cultivated varieties of plants of the genus Capsicum, often eaten as a vegetable, and ranging in flavor from sweet to hot.
the fruit of Capsicum, e.g., bell pepper, chili

Examples

She added chopped pepper and onions to the salad.
Red pepper can be either sweet or spicy, depending on the variety.
The market had an impressive selection of hot pepper varieties from around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chili - hot-tasting pepper of Capsicum
  • Degree Lower: bell pepper - large, sweet variety of Capsicum fruit
  • Degree Higher: chili pepper - small, hot-tasting variety of Capsicum fruit

Etymology

From Old English piper, from Latin piper, from Greek peperi, originally used for black pepper but broadened to include Capsicum varieties.
