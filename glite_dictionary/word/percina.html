Percina

1. NOUN

/ pərˈsaɪnə / (AmE), / pəˈsiːnə / (BrE)
A genus of small freshwater fish in the family Percidae, commonly known as darters, found in North America.
genus of North American freshwater darter fish

Examples

Several species within the Percina genus are considered endangered due to habitat loss.
Biologists conducted a survey on the population of Percina in the river basin.
The colorful patterns of Percina make them fascinating subjects for freshwater ecologists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Etheostoma - another genus of darters in the family Percidae
  • Degree Higher: Percidae - the family of fish including perches and darters

Etymology

From Latin, used in taxonomy as a diminutive of 'perca' (perch).

2. NOUN

/ pərˈsaɪnə / (AmE), / pəˈsiːnə / (BrE)
a genus of small freshwater fishes in the family Percidae, known as darters, native to North America
a genus of freshwater fishes (darters)

Examples

The genus Percina includes several species of darters found in streams across North America.
Biologists are studying the habitat preferences of fishes in the Percina genus.
Many members of the Percina genus are indicators of clean, well-oxygenated water.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Percidae - the family of fish to which Percina belongs

Etymology

From New Latin, possibly based on Latin 'perca' (perch).
