deserter

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈzɝːtər / (AmE), / dɪˈzɜːtə / (BrE)
a person who abandons their duty or post, especially someone who leaves military service without permission
person who abandons a duty, especially military

Examples

The army punished the deserter for leaving his post during battle.
After months on the run, the deserter was finally captured by authorities.
During wartime, being labeled a deserter carried severe consequences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defector - a person who abandons allegiance to join another side
  • Antonym: loyalist - a person who remains loyal to a cause or country

Etymology

From Old French *deserteur*, from Latin *desertor* (one who abandons).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈzɝːtər / (AmE), / dɪˈzɜːtə / (BrE)
a person who leaves or abandons a cause, group, or belief, especially in a disloyal or treacherous manner
person who abandons a cause or group

Examples

Many viewed him as a deserter after he switched political parties.
The group treated the deserter with suspicion and anger.
She felt betrayed when her longtime friend became a deserter of their shared beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traitor - a person who betrays a friend, country, principle, etc.
  • Antonym: loyalist - a person who remains loyal to a cause, party, or group

Etymology

From desert (to abandon) + -er, meaning one who deserts; from Old French deserter, from Late Latin desertare.
