Polyodon

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑliˈoʊdən / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈəʊdən / (BrE)
A genus of primitive freshwater fish known as paddlefish, native to North America and China, and the type genus of the family Polyodontidae.
type genus of paddlefish

Examples

The American paddlefish, a member of the genus Polyodon, is native to the Mississippi River.
Scientists study Polyodon to understand early evolutionary traits in fish.
Only one living species of Polyodon remains today, highlighting its rarity.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Polyodontidae - the family of paddlefish

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) and 'odous, odontos' (tooth), referring to the fish's teeth.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈoʊdən / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈəʊdən / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater paddlefish, the type genus of the family Polyodontidae, found in North America and China.
genus of paddlefish

Examples

The American paddlefish belongs to the genus Polyodon.
Fossils of the genus Polyodon have been found in ancient riverbeds.
Research on Polyodon has improved understanding of evolutionary biology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paddlefish - large freshwater fish with a paddle-shaped snout, member of the family Polyodontidae

Etymology

From Greek 'polys' (many) + 'odous, odont-' (tooth).
