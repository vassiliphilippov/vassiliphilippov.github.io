gangland

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæŋˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈɡæŋˌlænd / (BrE)
the world or environment of organized crime and criminal gangs
the criminal underworld

Examples

He was known to have connections in the gangland of Chicago.
The murder was believed to be the result of a gangland feud.
Reporters risked their lives investigating gangland activities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: underworld - the world of organized crime
  • Antonym: law-abiding society - members of society who obey the law, contrasted with gangland

Etymology

From 'gang' + 'land', denoting the territory or domain of criminal gangs.
