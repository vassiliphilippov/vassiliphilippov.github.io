instruction

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈstrʌkʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈstrʌkʃən / (BrE)
a detailed explanation or order that tells you how to do something or how something should be done
direction for doing something

Examples

Make sure to read the instructions before assembling the furniture.
The teacher gave clear instructions for the assignment.
If you follow the instructions closely, the recipe should turn out perfectly.

Synset Examples

he gave directions faster than she could follow them

Related Terms

  • Synonym: direction - a general order or guidance about how to do something
  • Synonym: command - an authoritative order
  • Synonym: order - an authoritative instruction to do something

Etymology

From Latin 'instructio' (an arrangement, preparation, teaching), from 'instruere' (to build, instruct).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06799486-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈstrʌkʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈstrʌkʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of teaching, especially in a formal setting; the activities involved in imparting knowledge or skill to others
the act or process of teaching

Examples

All new employees must attend instruction on workplace safety during their first week.
The school offers specialized instruction for children with learning disabilities.
His success in mathematics was due to excellent instruction from dedicated teachers.

Synset Examples

he received no formal education
our instruction was carefully programmed
good classroom teaching is seldom rewarded

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teaching - the act or profession of a teacher
  • Synonym: education - the process of receiving or giving systematic instruction
  • Antonym: learning - the acquisition of knowledge or skills through study

Etymology

From Latin 'instructio' (a teaching, preparation), from 'instruere' (to build, equip).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00884975-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈstrʌkʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈstrʌkʃən / (BrE)
(computing) a single operation or command written as part of a computer program, typically executed by a computer's processor.
a line of code in a computer program

Examples

Each instruction in the source code is executed sequentially by the processor.
The program crashed due to an invalid instruction in line 42.
An optimized compiler generates efficient machine instructions for faster execution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: command - a directive to a computer to perform a specific task

Etymology

From Latin 'instructio' (arrangement, training, teaching).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06597067-n
Synset POS: n
