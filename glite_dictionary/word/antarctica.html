Antarctica

1. PROPN

/ ænˈtɑːrktɪkə / (AmE), / ænˈtɑːktɪkə / (BrE)
the extremely cold continent surrounding the South Pole, almost entirely south of the Antarctic Circle and covered by a thick ice cap
continent at the South Pole

Examples

Antarctica is home to some of the harshest weather conditions on Earth.
Scientists from around the world conduct research in Antarctica during the summer months.
The ice sheets of Antarctica contain most of the planet’s fresh water.

Synset Examples

Antarctica is twice the size of Australia

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Arctic - region around the North Pole; opposite of Antarctica

Etymology

From Greek antarktikos, meaning 'opposite the Arctic'.
