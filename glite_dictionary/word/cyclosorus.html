Cyclosorus

1. PROPN

/ ˌsaɪkləˈsɔːrəs / (AmE), / ˌsɪkləˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
A small genus of terrestrial ferns native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Southern Hemisphere.
genus of tropical and subtropical ferns

Examples

The genus Cyclosorus includes several species adapted to wetland environments.
Several members of Cyclosorus are recognized for their distinctive circular sori.
Botanists discovered a new Cyclosorus species in the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

Etymology

From Greek 'kyklos' (circle) and 'soros' (heap), referring to the circular clusters of sporangia.

2. NOUN

/ saɪˈkloʊsərəs / (AmE), / sɪˈklɒsərəs / (BrE)
A small genus of terrestrial ferns found in tropical and subtropical regions of the Southern Hemisphere.
genus of tropical and subtropical ferns

Examples

The Cyclosorus genus is distinguished by its circular arrangement of sori on the leaves.
Botanists have discovered new species in the Cyclosorus group in South America.
Many wetlands in the tropics are dominated by ferns from the Cyclosorus genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - a non-flowering vascular plant with feathery leaves and reproducing by spores

Etymology

From Greek 'kyklos' (circle) and 'soros' (heap), referring to the circular arrangement of sori (spore cases) on the ferns' leaves.
