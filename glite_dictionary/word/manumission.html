manumission

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæn.jəˈmɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌman.juˈmɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the act of formally freeing someone from slavery or servitude
formal freeing from slavery

Examples

The manumission of hundreds of enslaved people marked a turning point in the colony's history.
His will provided for the manumission of all his household slaves upon his death.
Debates over manumission became increasingly common as the abolitionist movement gained strength.

Synset Examples

he believed in the manumission of the slaves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emancipation - the process of being set free from legal, social, or political restrictions

Etymology

From Latin 'manumissio', from 'manumittere' (to release from one's hand, set free), from 'manus' (hand) + 'mittere' (let go).
