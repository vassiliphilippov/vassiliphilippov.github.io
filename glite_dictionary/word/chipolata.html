chipolata

1. NOUN

/ ˌtʃɪpəˈlɑtə / (AmE), / ˌtʃɪpəˈlɑːtə / (BrE)
A small, thin sausage, typically seasoned and often used in British cooking.
small, thin sausage

Examples

The children enjoyed a chipolata with their breakfast each morning.
She added a chipolata to the plate to complete the traditional roast dinner.
The butcher offered both classic pork sausages and spicy chipolatas at the market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sausage - a food made from ground meat, seasoned and packed in a casing

Etymology

From French 'chipolata', probably from Italian 'cipollata' (onion stew), from 'cipolla' (onion).
