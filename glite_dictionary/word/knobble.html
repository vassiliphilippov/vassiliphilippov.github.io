knobble

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑbəl / (AmE), / ˈnɒbəl / (BrE)
A small rounded lump or protuberance; a small knob.
a small knob or rounded lump

Examples

Small knobbles formed along the branch where new buds were growing.
The old walking stick had a knobble at the top for grip.
She ran her finger over the knobble on the surface of the pottery.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: knob - a rounded lump or handle

Etymology

Diminutive or variant of 'knob', possibly from Middle Low German *knobbe* (knob, lump).
