quahaug

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑːˌhɑːɡ / (AmE), / ˈkwɑːhɔːɡ / (BrE)
a large edible clam of the Atlantic coast of North America, whose heavy shell was used as money by some Native American peoples
an edible North American clam

Examples

Fresh quahaugs were served at the seafood festival.
Many early settlers learned to eat quahaugs from Native Americans.
The quahaug shells were once used as wampum for trading.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clam - a marine bivalve mollusc with a shell

Etymology

From Narragansett (an Algonquian language) *poquauhock*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkəʊˌhɑːɡ / (AmE), / ˈkwɑːhɔːɡ / (BrE)
A large, round clam with a hard shell, native to the Atlantic coast of North America, often used in chowders and other dishes.
large Atlantic hard-shelled clam

Examples

The fisherman brought back a bucket of quahaugs for dinner.
Many New England recipes feature chopped quahaugs in hearty chowders.
She pried open the hard shell of the quahaug to add to her seafood stew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clam - a shelled mollusk, typically found in both saltwater and freshwater

Etymology

From Narragansett *poquauhock*, an Algonquian language of the Narragansett people.
