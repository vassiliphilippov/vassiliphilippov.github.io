fratricide

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrætrɪˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈfræt.rɪ.saɪd / (BrE)
The act of killing one's own brother or sister.
the killing of one's sibling

Examples

The ancient legend tells of a tragic fratricide that divided the kingdom.
He was accused of fratricide after a bitter family dispute turned violent.
Historians debate whether the ruler's death should be classified as regicide or fratricide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sororicide - the killing of one's sister
  • Synonym: patricide - the killing of one's father
  • Synonym: matricide - the killing of one's mother
  • Synonym: parricide - the killing of a close relative, especially a parent

Etymology

From Latin fratricidium, from frater (brother) + -cidium (killing).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfræt.rɪˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈfræt.rɪ.saɪd / (BrE)
the act of accidentally injuring or killing a member of one's own side, especially by military fire; friendly fire
injury or death of an ally by one's own side (military)

Examples

The incident resulted in fratricide, as the troops mistakenly fired on their own unit.
Military commanders are trained to minimize the risk of fratricide during joint operations.
Modern warfare technology aims to reduce fratricide by improving identification of friendly forces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: friendly fire - unintentional attack on one's own forces

Etymology

From Latin fratricidium, from frater (brother) + caedere (to kill); extended in military jargon to mean 'friendly fire.'

3. NOUN

/ ˈfrætrɪˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈfræt.rɪ.saɪd / (BrE)
a person who kills their own brother or sister
person who kills a brother or sister

Examples

The police arrested the fratricide after a lengthy investigation.
In ancient stories, a fratricide was often considered the worst kind of villain.
The court struggled to understand the motive behind the fratricide's actions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sororicide - a person who kills their sister
  • Synonym: parricide - a person who kills their parent or a close relative
  • Synonym: patricide - a person who kills their father
  • Synonym: matricide - a person who kills their mother

Etymology

From Latin fratricida (brother-killer), from frater (brother) + -cida (killer).
