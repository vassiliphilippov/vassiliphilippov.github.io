edition

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
a single published issue of a newspaper, magazine, or other periodical
issue of a newspaper or magazine

Examples

I picked up the morning edition of the newspaper from the front porch.
This special edition features exclusive interviews with the athletes.
They announced a limited edition of the Saturday paper due to the holiday.

Synset Examples

he read it in yesterday's edition of the Times

Related Terms

  • Synonym: issue - an individual copy or release of a periodical publication

Etymology

From Latin 'editio' (publication, edition), from 'edere' (to publish).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
a particular form or version in which a text, especially a book, is published
version of a published text

Examples

The latest edition of the novel includes a new foreword by the author.
She collects every edition of that magazine, dating back to the first printing.
This encyclopedia's third edition has been updated with recent discoveries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: version - a particular form of something differing from an earlier or other forms

Etymology

From Latin 'editio' (bringing forth, publication), from 'edere' (to publish).

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ɪˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
all the copies of a book, newspaper, recording, or other publication produced and made available at the same time, often distinguished by format, time, or content.
set of copies issued at one time

Examples

The limited edition of the novel sold out in just one day.
She collects every edition of her favorite magazine.
This morning's edition contains an exclusive interview with the author.

Synset Examples

the first edition appeared in 1920
it was too late for the morning edition
they issued a limited edition of Bach recordings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impression - all the copies of a book printed at one time without change

Etymology

From Latin 'editio' meaning 'publication', from 'edere' (to bring forth, publish).

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ ɪˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
a person or thing that is a slightly different form or version of another of the same kind
slightly different version of something

Examples

He is a younger edition of his father, sharing the same mannerisms and expressions.
The restaurant released a vegan edition of their popular burger last month.
This model is a limited edition with a new color scheme and extra features.
