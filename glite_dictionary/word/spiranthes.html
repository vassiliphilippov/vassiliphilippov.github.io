Spiranthes

1. NOUN

/ spaɪˈrænθiːz / (AmE), / spaɪˈrænθiːz / (BrE)
A genus of terrestrial orchids with spirally arranged, mostly white flowers, found in temperate and tropical regions worldwide.
genus of terrestrial orchids

Examples

Spiranthes thrives in both wetlands and grasslands across North America.
Botanists are conducting a survey to catalogue rare Spiranthes species.
The delicate twisting flowers of Spiranthes make it distinctive among wild orchids.

Etymology

From Greek 'speira' (coil, spiral) + 'anthos' (flower), referring to the spiral arrangement of the flower spike.
