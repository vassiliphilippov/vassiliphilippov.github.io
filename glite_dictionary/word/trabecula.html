trabecula

1. NOUN

/ trəˈbɛkjələ / (AmE), / trəˈbiːkjʊlə / (BrE)
A rod-shaped band or column of fibrous tissue that supports or divides an organ into parts, as in organs like the spleen or penis.
rod-like structure dividing or supporting organs
medical

Examples

The spleen's internal framework is formed by a network of trabeculae.
Damage to the trabecula can weaken the structural integrity of certain organs.
Researchers examined the trabeculae in heart tissue to understand cardiac function.

Etymology

From Latin 'trabecula', diminutive of 'trabs' (beam).
