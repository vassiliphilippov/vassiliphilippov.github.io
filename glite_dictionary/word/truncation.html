truncation

1. NOUN

/ trʌŋˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / trʌŋˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of cutting off a part, especially the replacement of an edge or solid angle by a plane in geometry or gemstone cutting
cutting off an edge or angle

Examples

The truncation of the crystal allowed it to reflect light more evenly.
In geometry, truncation can create new shapes by cutting off corners or edges.
Precise truncation enhances the brilliance of a finished diamond.

Etymology

From Latin truncare (to lop, cut off) + -ation (forming nouns of action/process).

2. NOUN

/ trʌŋˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / trʌŋˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of cutting something short by removing a part, or the state of being shortened
the act of shortening or cutting off

Examples

The truncation of the article made it easier to read but left out key details.
Genetic disorders can sometimes cause the truncation of important proteins in the body.
The editor called for a truncation of the novel to reduce its length.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shortening - the act of making shorter
  • Antonym: extension - the act of expanding or making longer

Etymology

3. NOUN

/ trʌŋˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / trʌŋˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of shortening something by cutting part of it off; the state of being shortened or cut short.
the act or result of shortening

Examples

The truncation of the essay left out some important arguments.
Due to truncation, only the first three digits were displayed on the calculator.
In geometry, truncation refers to the cutting off of a solid's corner or edge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abridgment - the act of shortening a text without losing the sense
  • Antonym: extension - the act of making something longer or larger

Etymology

From Latin truncatio, from truncare (to cut off).
