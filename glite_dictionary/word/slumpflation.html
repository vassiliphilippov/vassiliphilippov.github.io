slumpflation

1. NOUN

/ slʌmpˈfleɪʃən / (AmE), / slʌmpˈfleɪʃən / (BrE)
A situation in which economic depression occurs alongside rising inflation.
economic depression with rising inflation

Examples

During the 1970s, several countries experienced slumpflation that challenged traditional economic policies.
Experts debated whether slumpflation required different solutions than either inflation or recession alone.
The government struggled to address the effects of slumpflation on unemployment and the cost of living.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stagflation - a situation of stagnant growth and high inflation

Etymology

Blend of 'slump' (economic downturn) and 'inflation'.
