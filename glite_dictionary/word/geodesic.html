geodesic

1. ADJ

/ ˌdʒiːəˈdɛsɪk / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəʊˈdɛsɪk / (BrE)
relating to or determined by geodesy; pertaining to the shortest path between two points on a curved surface such as the Earth.
relating to geodesy or shortest path on a surface

Examples

The geodesic measurements helped scientists determine the exact shape of the Earth.
He explained that a straight line on a globe is actually a geodesic curve.
Modern GPS systems rely on geodesic calculations for accuracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geodetic - relating to geodesy

Etymology

From Greek 'geodaisios', from 'geōdaisia' (division of the earth).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiːəˈdɛsɪk / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəˈdiːsɪk / (BrE)
the shortest path between two points on a curved surface, such as a straight line on a plane or an arc of a great circle on a sphere
the shortest line on a curved surface

Examples

The airline route follows a geodesic across the surface of the globe.
On a sphere, a geodesic is an arc of a great circle connecting two points.
Mathematicians use the concept of a geodesic when studying curved spaces in geometry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: great circle - a circle on a sphere with a center that includes the sphere's center; the shortest path between two points on a sphere
  • Degree Higher: curve - a continuously bending line without angles
  • Degree Lower: straight line - a line with no curvature; the shortest distance between two points in a plane

Etymology

From French géodésique, based on Greek geōdaisía (division of the earth).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13893053-n
Synset POS: n
