localisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌloʊkələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌləʊkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Physiology) The principle that specific functions are associated with definite, circumscribed areas in a particular part or organ, especially in the brain.
assigning function to body region (esp. brain)
medical

Examples

The concept of localisation suggests that language abilities reside in specific areas of the brain.
Recent studies challenge the strict localisation of memory functions within the hippocampus.
Localisation of sensory functions allows neurologists to diagnose certain types of brain injuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: localization - US spelling of 'localisation', same meaning

Etymology

From French localisation, from localiser (to localize), ultimately from Latin locus (place).

2. NOUN

/ ˌloʊkəlɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌləʊkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of determining or identifying the exact place or position of something.
determining the location of something

Examples

The localisation of the sound allowed the rescue team to find the trapped miners.
Accurate localisation of the tumor is critical for successful surgery.
Using GPS, the localisation of the lost hiker was achieved within minutes.

Synset Examples

he got a good fix on the target

Related Terms

  • Synonym: location - a place or position
  • Synonym: identification - the act of establishing what something is

Etymology

From French 'localisation', from 'localiser' (to localize), based on Latin 'locālis' (relating to place).
