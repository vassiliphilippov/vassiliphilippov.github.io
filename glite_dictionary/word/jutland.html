Jutland

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒʌtˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌt.lənd / (BrE)
a peninsula in northern Europe, forming the mainland part of Denmark and a northern portion of Germany
peninsula in Denmark and Germany

Examples

The majority of Denmark's population lives on Jutland and its surrounding islands.
During our trip to Europe, we explored the scenic landscapes of Jutland.
The Battle of Jutland was a major naval engagement during World War I.

Etymology

From Danish Jylland, from Old Norse Jutland (land of the Jutes).

2. PROPN

/ ˈdʒʌtˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌtlənd / (BrE)
a major naval battle of World War I (1916) fought between British and German fleets off the coast of Denmark, notable for its indecisive outcome
World War I naval battle off Denmark, 1916

Examples

The Jutland battle remains the largest naval confrontation of World War I.
Many historians debate whether any side truly gained a strategic advantage at Jutland.
Commemorations for those lost at Jutland are held annually in both Britain and Germany.

Etymology

From the name of the Jutland peninsula in Denmark, near where the battle took place.
