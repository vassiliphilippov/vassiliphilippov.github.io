appetizingness

1. NOUN

/ ˌæpəˈtaɪzɪŋnɪs / (AmE), / ˌæpɪˈtaɪzɪŋnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being appealing to the appetite or stimulating the desire to eat
the quality of stimulating the appetite

Examples

The appetizingness of the meal was clear from everyone's eager anticipation.
Chefs pay close attention to the appetizingness of their presentations.
The dish may be healthy, but its lack of appetizingness discouraged diners.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unappetizingness - the quality of not being appealing to the appetite
  • Synonym: appetizing - having an appealing appearance or smell that stimulates the appetite

Etymology

From 'appetizing' + '-ness', formed to denote the state or quality of being appetizing.
