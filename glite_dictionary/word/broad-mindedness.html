broad-mindedness

1. NOUN

/ brɔdˈmaɪndɪdnəs / (AmE), / brɔːdˈmaɪndɪdnəs / (BrE)
willingness to accept or tolerate different opinions, beliefs, or behaviors that are unusual or different from one's own.
tolerance of differing opinions or behaviors

Examples

Her broad-mindedness allowed her to appreciate different cultures and ideas without prejudice.
In this position, broad-mindedness is a valuable trait, as you will encounter people with many viewpoints.
His broad-mindedness helped resolve conflicts within the team by welcoming diverse perspectives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tolerance - the ability or willingness to tolerate something, especially opinions or behavior that one does not agree with
  • Synonym: open-mindedness - willingness to consider new ideas; unprejudiced
  • Antonym: narrow-mindedness - unwillingness to accept views, beliefs, or behavior that differ from one's own

Etymology

From 'broad-minded' + '-ness', modeled after compound adjectives in English.
