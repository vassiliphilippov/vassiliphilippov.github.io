pageantry

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪdʒəntri / (AmE), / ˈpeɪdʒəntri / (BrE)
elaborate and colourful ceremony or display, especially involving a procession or performance
elaborate ceremonial display

Examples

The coronation was full of pageantry and tradition.
Tourists flock to see the pageantry of the annual parade.
Despite the pageantry, the ceremony remained meaningful to local residents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spectacle - a visually striking performance or display

Etymology

From pageant + -ry, influenced by Old French *pajerie* (pageant, ceremonial display).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪdʒəntri / (AmE), / ˈpeɪdʒəntri / (BrE)
elaborate and colorful display or ceremony, often involving costumes, parades, or historical reenactments
elaborate ceremonial display or parade

Examples

The coronation was marked by a dazzling display of pageantry unmatched in recent memory.
Renaissance fairs often recreate the pageantry of medieval tournaments and festivals.
The Olympic opening ceremony is famous for its elaborate pageantry and spectacle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spectacle - a visually striking performance or display
  • Synonym: pomp - ceremonial or splendid display, often for effect
  • Antonym: modesty - lack of display or showiness

Etymology

From Middle English 'pagyntrie', related to 'pageant'.
