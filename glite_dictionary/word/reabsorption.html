reabsorption

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːˌæbˈzɔrpʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːəbˈzɔːpʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a substance produced or separated by the body is broken down and taken up again by the body, especially in biological tissues
the process of a body absorbing something again
medical

Examples

The kidneys play a vital role in the reabsorption of water and nutrients.
Failure of reabsorption in the intestines can lead to serious health issues.
Bone reabsorption increases with age, affecting bone density.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absorption - the process of taking in or soaking up

Etymology

From re- (again) + absorption, ultimately from Latin absorbere (to swallow up).
