inkle

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈɪŋkəl / (BrE)
a narrow linen or cotton tape used as trimming or for decoration on clothing or textiles
narrow decorative linen/cotton tape

Examples

The seamstress added a strip of inkle along the edge of the garment for a decorative finish.
Antique linens often featured intricate patterns woven into colored inkle.
She learned how to weave inkle bands on a traditional loom for her craft fair display.

Etymology

Probably from Middle English (as a surname or trade name), origin obscure.
