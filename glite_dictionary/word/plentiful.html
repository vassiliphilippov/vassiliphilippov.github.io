plentiful

1. ADJ

/ ˈplɛn.tɪ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈplɛn.tɪ.f(ə)l / (BrE)
producing or existing in large amounts; more than enough
existing or produced in abundance

Examples

Mangoes are plentiful in the summer months.
Fresh water is plentiful in this region due to the many rivers.
Job opportunities became less plentiful during the economic downturn.

Synset Examples

the bountiful earth
a plentiful year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abundant - existing or available in large quantities
  • Antonym: scarce - insufficient for the demand or rare

Etymology

From Old French *plentif* (full, abundant), from Latin *plenitudo* (fullness).

3. ADJ

/ ˈplɛntɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈplentɪfəl / (BrE)
existing or present in large amounts or numbers; abundant
existing in large quantity or number

Examples

Fresh fruits and vegetables are plentiful during the summer months.
Jobs were plentiful after the economic boom of the 1990s.
Rainfall has been plentiful this year, leading to a good harvest.

Synset Examples

rhinoceroses were once plentiful here

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abundant - existing or available in large quantities
  • Antonym: scarce - in short supply; not enough

Etymology

From Middle English, from Anglo-Norman *plentiful*, from Old French *plente* (fullness, plenty).
