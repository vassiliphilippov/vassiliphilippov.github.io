bone-idle

1. ADJ

/ boʊn ˈaɪdəl / (AmE), / bəʊn ˈaɪdl / (BrE)
(especially British, informal) extremely lazy or unwilling to work; habitually inactive.
extremely lazy; habitually inactive
informal british

Examples

He remained bone-idle all weekend, barely moving from the couch.
My brother is so bone-idle that he never helps with the chores.
They accused her of being bone-idle because she preferred reading to working.

Synset Examples

He remained bone-idle all weekend, watching TV.
After losing his job, he became bone-idle.
Being bone-idle isn't an option for her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idle - not active or not working; lazy

Etymology

Formed from "bone" (emphasizing to the core) + "idle" (lazy), chiefly British slang dating from the early 20th century.
