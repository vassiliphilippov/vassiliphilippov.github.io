Rock Island

1. PROPN

/ rɑk ˈaɪ.lənd / (AmE), / rɒk ˈaɪ.lənd / (BrE)
a city in northwest Illinois, located on the Mississippi River, known for its historical significance and its role as a Union prison site during the American Civil War
city in northwest Illinois, USA

Examples

My grandfather grew up in Rock Island and often shared stories about the old riverfront.
The Union prison in Rock Island housed thousands of Confederate soldiers during the Civil War.
After visiting Chicago, we drove along the Mississippi to explore Rock Island.
