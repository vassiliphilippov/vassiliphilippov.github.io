liquidiser

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪkwɪˌdaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪdaɪzə / (BrE)
an electric kitchen appliance with rotating blades used to mix, chop, or puree food into liquid or semi-liquid form
electric kitchen appliance for blending food
british

Examples

He used the liquidiser to turn the cooked vegetables into a smooth soup.
After adding the fruit, she switched on the liquidiser to make a fresh smoothie.
The recipe recommends using a liquidiser to blend all the ingredients together quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blender - an electric appliance for mixing or puréeing food

Etymology

From 'liquid' + '-iser', influenced by 'to liquefy'; more common in British English.
