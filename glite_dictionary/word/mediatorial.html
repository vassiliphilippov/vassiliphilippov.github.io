mediatorial

1. ADJ

/ ˌmiːdiəˈtɔːriəl / (AmE), / ˌmiːdɪəˈtɔːrɪəl / (BrE)
relating to a mediator or to the role, actions, or duties of a mediator
pertaining to a mediator's role or duties

Examples

The judge appreciated his mediatorial approach in resolving disputes between the two parties.
Her mediatorial skills were crucial in brokering the peace agreement.
The role required a strong sense of ethics and a mediatorial mindset.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mediator - a person who attempts to make people involved in a conflict come to an agreement

Etymology

From Latin *mediator* (one who intervenes), with English suffix *-ial* forming adjectives.
