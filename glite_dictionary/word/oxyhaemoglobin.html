oxyhaemoglobin

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑksiˌhiːməˈɡloʊbɪn / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪˌhiːməˈɡləʊbɪn / (BrE)
the bright red substance formed when hemoglobin combines with oxygen in the lungs, responsible for transporting oxygen in the blood
hemoglobin combined with oxygen
medical

Examples

Oxyhaemoglobin is responsible for the red color of oxygenated blood.
The rapid release of oxygen from oxyhaemoglobin is vital for cellular respiration.
Doctors measured the patient's oxyhaemoglobin levels to assess how well his lungs functioned.

Synset Examples

oxyhemoglobin transports oxygen to the cells of the body

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemoglobin - the protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen

Etymology

From 'oxy-' (relating to oxygen) and 'haemoglobin' (from Greek 'haima' meaning 'blood' and 'globin', a type of protein).
