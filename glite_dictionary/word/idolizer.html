idolizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪdəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈaɪdəlaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who worships idols or gives excessive admiration to someone or something
person who worships idols

Examples

The ancient temple drew many idolizers from distant lands.
Some fans become such devoted idolizers that they lose sight of reality.
He was accused of being an idolizer because of his obsession with celebrity culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idolater - a person who worships idols
  • Antonym: iconoclast - a person who attacks or criticizes cherished beliefs or institutions

Etymology

From 'idolize' + '-er', ultimately from Greek 'eidōlon' (image, idol).

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪdəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈaɪdəˌlaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who admires or worships someone or something excessively or blindly
a person who idolizes another

Examples

He became an idolizer of the famous musician, following her every career move.
As an idolizer, she refused to accept any criticism of her favorite actor.
The movie star's idolizer sent fan mail every week without fail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admirer - a person who admires someone or something
  • Antonym: critic - a person who expresses disapproval or finds fault

Etymology

From 'idolize' (to worship as an idol) plus agent suffix '-er'.
