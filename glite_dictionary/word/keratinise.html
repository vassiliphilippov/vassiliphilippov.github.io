to keratinise

1. VERB

/ ˈkɛr.ə.təˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɛr.ə.tɪˌnaɪz / (BrE)
(of tissue) to become impregnated with keratin, making it tough or horny; to undergo the process of becoming keratinized.
to become impregnated with keratin
medical british

Examples

As cells move towards the surface of the skin, they keratinise and eventually die.
Some animal structures, like hooves and horns, are formed when tissues keratinise.
The process by which epithelial cells keratinise helps protect the body from dehydration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to keratinize - to become impregnated with keratin (US spelling)

Etymology

From keratin + -ise, meaning 'to make or become like keratin'.

2. VERB

/ ˈkɛr.ə.tɪˌnaɪz / (AmE), / kəˈræt.ɪ.naɪz / (BrE)
(of cells or tissue) to undergo or cause to undergo a process in which keratin is produced and accumulates, often leading to hardening.
to convert into keratin
british medical

Examples

The cells in the outer layer of the skin gradually keratinise to provide a tough barrier.
Some epithelial tissues keratinise as a protective adaptation.
If the tissue fails to keratinise normally, it may become vulnerable to infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to keratinize - convert into keratin (American spelling)
  • Antonym: dekeratinize - remove keratin or prevent keratinisation

Etymology

From keratin + -ise (British spelling for verb formation), 'keratin' from Greek 'keras' (horn).
