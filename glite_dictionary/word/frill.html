frill

1. NOUN

/ frɪl / (AmE), / frɪl / (BrE)
(Paleontology) a bony plate that curves upward behind the skull of many ceratopsian dinosaurs, often forming a distinctive crest or shield.
bony plate on skull of ceratopsian dinosaurs

Examples

The frill of the Triceratops provided protection and may have been used for display.
Paleontologists studied the fossilized frill to learn about dinosaur evolution.
Some ceratopsians had enormous frills adorned with spikes or other ornamentation.

2. NOUN

/ frɪl / (AmE), / frɪl / (BrE)
a distinctive growth of feathers, hair, or skin around the neck of a bird or other animal
feathers or hair around an animal's neck

Examples

The lizard raised its frill when it felt threatened by the predator.
Some birds display a colorful frill during mating rituals to attract partners.
The young boy was fascinated by the dinosaur's large neck frill in the museum exhibit.

Etymology

From Middle English frile, from Middle Dutch frille (border, ruffle).

decorative object or trimming

Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embellishment - a decorative detail or feature added to something
