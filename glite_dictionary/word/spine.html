spine

1. NOUN

/ spaɪn / (AmE), / spaɪn / (BrE)
a small, sharp-pointed projection on a stem or leaf, similar to a thorn or prickle
sharp projection on a plant

Examples

The cactus is covered in spines to protect it from animals.
Some roses have spines on their stems that can scratch your skin.
The plant’s leaves are edged with tiny spines that deter herbivores.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thorn - a sharp-pointed projection on a plant
  • Synonym: prickle - a small, sharp outgrowth on a plant

Etymology

From Latin spina (thorn, backbone, spine).

2. NOUN

/ spaɪn / (AmE), / spaɪn / (BrE)
the part of a book's cover that encloses the bound edge of its pages and displays information such as the title and author when the book is shelved upright
narrow edge of a book's cover binding the pages

Examples

She wrote her name in ink on the spine of her textbook so it wouldn't be lost.
The spine of this old novel is cracked and barely holds the pages together.
When organizing his library, he arranged the books so the titles on each spine lined up neatly.

Synset Examples

the title and author were printed on the spine of the book

Etymology

From Latin spina (thorn, backbone)

3. NOUN

/ spaɪn / (AmE), / spaɪn / (BrE)
the series of vertebrae that forms the central supporting structure of the skeleton and protects the spinal cord
series of vertebrae; backbone

Examples

She injured her spine in a car accident, which required months of rehabilitation.
The human spine consists of thirty-three vertebrae stacked in a column.
X-rays revealed a fracture in his lower spine after the fall.

Synset Examples

the fall broke his back

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backbone - the main support structure of the body, another word for spine

Etymology

From Latin 'spina' (thorn, backbone).

4. NOUN

/ spaɪn / (AmE), / spaɪn / (BrE)
a stiff, sharp-pointed projection on an animal, plant, or other object, such as a thorn or needle-like structure
sharp-pointed projection

Examples

The cactus is covered in sharp spines that protect it from animals.
The sea urchin's spines can easily puncture the skin if you are not careful.
Some fish have poisonous spines on their fins as a defense mechanism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thorn - a sharp, pointed projection on a plant stem or branch
  • Synonym: quill - a stiff, sharp hair or spine, especially on a porcupine

Etymology

From Latin 'spina' (thorn, backbone).

5. NOUN

/ spaɪn / (AmE), / spaɪn / (BrE)
a sharp, stiff projection on an animal, such as a porcupine's quill, a bony ridge, or the stiff ray of a fish's fin
sharp, rigid animal projection

Examples

The porcupine raised its spines in defense when threatened by the predator.
The fish's dorsal fin contains several sharp spines to protect it from attackers.
A sea urchin's body is covered with long, movable spines for defense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quill - a stiff, sharp, hollow spine of a porcupine or hedgehog

Etymology

From Latin spina (thorn, backbone).
