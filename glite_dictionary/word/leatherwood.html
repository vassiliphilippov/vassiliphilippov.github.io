leatherwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛðɚˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈlɛðəˌwʊd / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States, the West Indies, and Brazil, known for its slender clusters of white flowers and colorful foliage.
shrub or small tree with white flowers and colorful leaves

Examples

The garden featured a rare leatherwood with colorful autumn leaves.
Botanists studied the leatherwood for its unique flowers and bark.
In some regions, leatherwood is used as an ornamental plant in landscaping.

Etymology

Named for its tough, leathery bark and wood.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛðərˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈlɛðəˌwʊd / (BrE)
a deciduous shrub of eastern North America with tough, flexible branches, pliable bark, and small yellow flowers
a North American deciduous shrub with flexible branches

Examples

The hikers stopped to examine a leatherwood growing along the forest path.
Native Americans once used the bark of leatherwood to make cords and ropes.
In spring, the leatherwood produces delicate yellow flowers before its leaves fully emerge.

Etymology

Named for its tough, leather-like bark and wood.
