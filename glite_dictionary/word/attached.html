attached

1. ADJ

/ əˈtætʃt / (AmE), / əˈtatʃt / (BrE)
(of a building) joined to another building by a common sidewall; not freestanding.
joined to another building by a sidewall

Examples

They bought an attached house in a quiet residential area.
The city is filled with rows of attached townhouses.
The new development will feature both attached and detached homes.

Synset Examples

a block of attached houses

Related Terms

  • Antonym: detached - (of a building) not joined to another building or structure

2. ADJ

/ əˈtætʃt / (AmE), / əˈtætʃt / (BrE)
feeling fondness or affection for someone or something
feeling fond or affectionate

Examples

She became very attached to her foster family during her childhood.
Many children grow attached to their favorite toys and carry them everywhere.
He is attached to his old car, even though it breaks down often.

Synset Examples

she was very attached to her father

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fond - having an affection or liking for
  • Antonym: detached - emotionally uninvolved or indifferent

3. ADJ

/ əˈtætʃt / (AmE), / əˈtatʃt / (BrE)
(of a person) in an exclusive romantic or sexual relationship; committed to someone
in a committed romantic relationship

Examples

She was already attached when he asked her out.
Are you attached or single?
He became very attached to his partner over the years.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: single - not in a romantic relationship
  • Antonym: unattached - not in an exclusive relationship

4. ADJ

/ əˈtætʃt / (AmE), / əˈtætʃt / (BrE)
joined to something or someone in a close association, either physically or in a formal relationship
joined or connected closely

Examples

The company only accepts applications from attached research institutes.
She felt deeply attached to her adopted family.
These documents were attached to the email for your reference.

Synset Examples

affiliated clubs
all art schools whether independent or attached to universities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affiliated - closely associated with a larger body or organization
  • Synonym: connected - joined or linked together
  • Antonym: detached - not joined or connected; separate

Etymology

From the Old French 'atachier' (to attach), influenced by the Anglo-Norman French.
