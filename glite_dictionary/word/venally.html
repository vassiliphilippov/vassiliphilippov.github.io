venally

1. ADV

/ ˈvɛnəli / (AmE), / ˈviː.nəl.i / (BrE)
In a corrupt or bribable manner.
in a corrupt manner

Examples

The official awarded the contract venally, accepting a bribe instead of following proper procedures.
She behaved venally, ignoring the rules for her own personal gain.
It was discovered that the committee acted venally by favoring those who offered gifts.

Synset Examples

he acted dishonestly when he gave the contract to his best friend

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corruptly - in a corrupt manner
  • Antonym: honestly - in a truthful and fair way

Etymology

From 'venal,' from Latin 'venalis' (that is for sale).
