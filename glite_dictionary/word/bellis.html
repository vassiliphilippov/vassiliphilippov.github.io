Bellis

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛlɪs / (AmE), / ˈbɛlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, including the common daisy (Bellis perennis).
a plant genus including the common daisy

Examples

Bellis is a genus widely known for the common daisy found in lawns and meadows.
Horticulturists often recommend planting Bellis species for early spring blooms.
The Bellis perennis is valued for its resilience and attractive flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daisy - a small grassland plant with a yellow disk and white rays

Etymology

From Latin 'bellis', the name for the daisy.
