massicotite

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæsɪˈkɔːtaɪt / (AmE), / ˌmasɪˈkɒtaɪt / (BrE)
A mineral form of lead monoxide, usually found as a yellow powder and used as a pigment.
mineral form of lead monoxide pigment

Examples

The artist mixed massicotite with oil to create a rich yellow paint.
Samples of massicotite have been found in the lead mines of Derbyshire.
Due to its lead content, massicotite is handled with great care in laboratories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: massicot - a yellow pigment made from lead monoxide

Etymology

From 'massicot', the name for the yellow pigment, with -ite signifying a mineral.
