Erythronium

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌrɪθˈroʊniəm / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪθˈrəʊniəm / (BrE)
A genus of perennial bulbous herbs of the lily family, native to northern temperate regions, commonly known as dogtooth violet, trout lily, or adder's tongue.
genus of perennial bulbous herbs (dogtooth violet)

Examples

In early spring, the Erythronium blooms add a splash of color to the woodland floor.
Gardeners appreciate the delicate flowers of Erythronium for their beauty and resilience.
Several species in the Erythronium genus are native to North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogtooth violet - common name for Erythronium species
  • Synonym: trout lily - another common name for some Erythronium species
  • Synonym: adder's tongue - another common name for some Erythronium species

Etymology

From Greek 'erythros' meaning 'red', referring to the reddish color of some species.
