gypsywort

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪpsiˌwɜrt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪpsiˌwɜːt / (BrE)
A hairy Eurasian herb (Lycopus europaeus) with two-lipped white flowers, usually found in wet places.
a Eurasian herb with white flowers

Examples

Gypsywort is often found growing along riverbanks and marshes in Europe.
The herbalist explained that gypsywort was once used as a natural dye.
Several insects were attracted to the gypsywort blooming by the pond.

Etymology

From 'gypsy' + 'wort', with 'wort' meaning 'plant'; traditionally believed to be used by Romani people as a dye.
