heliotrope

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːliəˌtroʊp / (AmE), / ˈhiːlɪəˌtrəʊp / (BrE)
A green variety of chalcedony with red spots, also known as bloodstone, used as a gemstone.
a green gemstone with red spots (bloodstone)

Examples

The ancient Romans valued heliotrope for its supposed mystical properties.
A pendant made of heliotrope glimmered in the sunlight.
Many collectors seek out rare forms of heliotrope to add to their gemstone collections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bloodstone - a green gemstone with red spots, synonym for heliotrope

Etymology

From Greek hēliotropion (turning towards the sun), originally referring to the plant and later the stone.
