chancellorship

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæn.səˌlɚ.ʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈtʃæn.səl.ə.ʃɪp / (BrE)
the office or position of a chancellor
the office of a chancellor

Examples

After years of dedication, she finally attained the chancellorship of the university.
The chancellorship comes with significant administrative and ceremonial responsibilities.
Debate over the chancellorship dominated the political discussions for several months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chancellor - the chief official in certain governments or universities

Etymology

From chancellor + -ship, denoting the state or office of being a chancellor.
