to assign

1. VERB

/ əˈsaɪn / (AmE), / əˈsaɪn / (BrE)
to select someone or something for a particular purpose or duty
select for a specific purpose

Examples

The manager will assign each intern to a different department.
Sarah was assigned to organize the annual meeting this year.
The system can automatically assign tasks based on skill sets.

Synset Examples

The teacher assigned him to lead his classmates in the exercise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to appoint - to assign a job or role to someone
  • Synonym: delegate - to give someone a duty or task
  • Antonym: remove - to take away a person from a role or task

Etymology

From Latin 'assignare' (to mark out, allot), from 'ad' (to) + 'signare' (to mark).
