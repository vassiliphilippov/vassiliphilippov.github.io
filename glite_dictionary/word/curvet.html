curvet

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɜrvət / (AmE), / ˈkɜːvɪt / (BrE)
a light leap made by a horse in which both hind legs leave the ground before the forelegs come down, especially as a showy movement in classical horsemanship
a horse's graceful leap

Examples

The stallion performed a perfect curvet, delighting the audience with its grace.
In classical dressage, the curvet is a challenging maneuver for both horse and rider.
The horse’s sudden curvet startled those watching from the fence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capriole - a leap in which a horse jumps and kicks out with the hind legs

Etymology

From Italian 'curvetta', diminutive of 'curva' (curve), relating to a rounded movement.

2. VERB

/ ˈkɜrvət / (AmE), / ˈkɜːvɪt / (BrE)
(of a horse) to leap or prance gracefully with both hind legs leaving the ground.
leap of a horse with hind legs off ground

Examples

The stallion began to curvet proudly around the ring, drawing applause from the audience.
During the parade, the trained horse would curvet on command, astonishing the crowd with its elegance.
She watched the show horse curveting across the arena, its mane flowing with every graceful leap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prance - (of a horse) to move with high, springy steps

Etymology

From French 'courbette', diminutive of 'courber' (to curve), from Latin 'curvare'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
