fatalist

1. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪt̬əlɪst / (AmE), / ˈfeɪtəlɪst / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of fatalism or fatalists.
relating to fatalism

Examples

She had a fatalist outlook on life, accepting whatever happened as unavoidable.
His fatalist beliefs made him indifferent to change.
The novel explores fatalist themes, questioning the nature of free will.

Synset Examples

a fatalist person
fatalistic thinking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fatalistic - believing that all events are predetermined and inevitable

Etymology

From 'fatalist' (noun), ultimately from Latin 'fatum' (fate).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪtəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈfeɪtəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that all events are predetermined and inevitable, and who accepts that they cannot change their destiny
person who believes fate controls events

Examples

As a fatalist, she believed that no amount of effort could change what was meant to be.
The fatalist in him accepted bad luck without complaint.
Despite her friends' optimism, she remained a fatalist about her future.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: determinist - one who believes that all events are determined by previously existing causes
  • Antonym: optimist - someone who tends to be hopeful and confident about the future
  • Antonym: pessimist - someone who expects the worst possible outcome

Etymology

From French *fataliste*, from 'fatal' (relating to fate), ultimately from Latin *fatalis* (ordained by fate).
