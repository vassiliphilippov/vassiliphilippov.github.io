outer

1. ADJ

/ ˈaʊtɚ / (AmE), / ˈaʊtə / (BrE)
situated on or toward the outside; external or farther from the center.
external; on or toward the outside

Examples

The outer walls of the castle were built for protection.
She zipped up her outer jacket before heading into the snow.
We explored the outer regions of the solar system.

Synset Examples

outer reality

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inner - situated on or toward the inside; opposed to 'outer'

Etymology

From Old English ūtera, comparative of ūt (out).

2. ADJ

/ ˈaʊtər / (AmE), / ˈaʊtə / (BrE)
situated on or relating to the outside; farther from the center or middle of something
on the outside; farther from the center

Examples

The outer walls of the castle were built to withstand attacks.
She lives in the outer suburbs, far from the city center.
The spaceship entered the outer atmosphere of the planet.

Synset Examples

spent hours adorning the outer man
the outer suburbs

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inner - situated on or relating to the inside; closer to the center

Etymology

From Middle English 'outer', alteration of 'utter', from Old English 'ūtera', comparative of 'ūt' (out).

3. ADJ

/ ˈaʊtər / (AmE), / ˈaʊtə / (BrE)
situated on or toward the outside; external to something else, especially to the body or a particular surface
on or toward the outside; external

Examples

The outer walls of the building need to be repainted.
She wore a jacket to protect herself from the outer cold.
Medical students study the outer structure of the ear in anatomy class.

Synset Examples

the outer ear

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inner - located on or toward the inside

Etymology

From Middle English 'outer', from Old English 'ūtera', comparative of 'ūt' (out).
