guppy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌpi / (AmE), / ˈɡʌpi / (BrE)
a small, brightly colored freshwater fish native to South America and the West Indies, commonly kept in home aquariums
small colorful freshwater aquarium fish

Examples

My son has a guppy in his bedroom aquarium.
The guppy is known for its vibrant tail patterns.
We bought several guppies to add to our fish tank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poeciliid - a member of the fish family Poeciliidae, which includes the guppy

Etymology

Named after Robert John Lechmere Guppy, who discovered the species in Trinidad.
