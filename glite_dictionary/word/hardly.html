hardly

1. ADV

/ ˈhɑrdli / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdli / (BrE)
only just; with great difficulty or almost not at all.
only just; barely

Examples

He could hardly believe his eyes when he saw the surprise party.
The engine was so old it hardly worked anymore.
She was so tired she could hardly keep her eyes open.

Synset Examples

prejudices die hard
he was so dizzy he could hardly stand up straight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barely - only just; by a very small margin
  • Synonym: scarcely - only just; almost not
  • Antonym: easily - without difficulty or effort

Etymology

From hard + -ly, first used in Middle English.

2. ADV

/ ˈhɑrdli / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdli / (BrE)
Almost not; only just; scarcely.
almost not; scarcely

Examples

I can hardly hear what you are saying over the noise.
It was so dark that she could hardly see her own hands.
We hardly ever go out to restaurants these days.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barely - only just; almost not

Etymology

3. ADV

/ ˈhɑrdli / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdli / (BrE)
Only a very short time before a particular moment or event.
only a very short time before

Examples

We hardly finished dinner before the guests arrived.
She had hardly stepped outside when it started to rain.
The movie had hardly begun when his phone rang.

Synset Examples

we hardly knew them
had scarcely rung the bell when the door flew open
would have scarce arrived before she would have found some excuse to leave

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarcely - only just or almost not; similar to 'hardly'

Etymology

From hard + -ly; Middle English hardli, meaning 'with difficulty'.

Source: WordNet
