horsefly

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrsˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈhɔːsflaɪ / (BrE)
A large, stout-bodied fly whose females feed on the blood of horses and other animals, often causing irritation.
large biting fly parasitic on animals

Examples

Be careful when riding—the horsefly bites can be very painful.
A large horsefly buzzed around the stable, annoying the horses.
We used insect repellent to protect the animals from horseflies in the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deerfly - a small biting fly related to the horsefly

Etymology

From horse + fly, referring to flies often found around horses.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɔːrsˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈhɔːsˌflaɪ / (BrE)
a large, fast-flying fly whose female feeds on the blood of animals, often causing painful bites
large bloodsucking fly

Examples

A horsefly landed on my arm and gave me a painful bite.
Farmers often complain about horseflies bothering their cattle in the summer.
She quickly swatted the horsefly before it could bite her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deerfly - a smaller related fly that also feeds on animal blood
