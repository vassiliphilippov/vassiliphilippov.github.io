Thomomys bottae

1. NOUN

/ toʊˈmoʊmɪs ˈbɑːteɪ / (AmE), / təˈmɒmɪs ˈbɒteɪ / (BrE)
a species of pocket gopher found in valleys and mountain meadows of the western United States
a species of pocket gopher

Examples

The Thomomys bottae is commonly found in the grasslands of California.
Researchers studied the burrowing patterns of Thomomys bottae to understand their impact on soil structure.
Unlike other gophers, Thomomys bottae prefers habitats in valleys and mountain meadows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pocket gopher - burrowing rodent native to North and Central America

Etymology

From Latin: 'Thomomys' is the genus name for pocket gophers; 'bottae' honors Italian naturalist Paul-Émile Botta.
