azure

1. ADJ

/ ˈæʒər / (AmE), / ˈæʒə / (BrE)
of a deep, bright, somewhat purplish blue color, like that of a clear sky
deep bright blue color

Examples

The sky was a clear, azure blue as the sun rose above the horizon.
She wore an azure dress that matched her eyes perfectly.
The azure waters of the lagoon sparkled in the midday sun.

Synset Examples

October's bright blue weather

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: blue - the color of the clear sky or the deep sea
  • Degree Higher: sapphire - brilliant blue color, resembling that of a sapphire

Etymology

From Old French 'azur', from Arabic 'lazaward' (lapis lazuli).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈæʒə / (BrE)
A bright blue color, resembling the color of a clear sky.
bright blue color

Examples

The artist chose a rich azure for the sky in her painting.
She wore a dress of soft azure that matched her eyes.
The waters of the Mediterranean sparkled in a deep shade of azure under the afternoon sun.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: blue - the color between green and violet in the spectrum
  • Degree Higher: cobalt blue - a deep, vivid blue color

Etymology

From Old French azur, from Arabic lāzaward (lapis lazuli, blue pigment).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04976419-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈæʒər / (AmE), / ˈæʒə / (BrE)
(literary) to make or become blue, especially sky-blue in color.
make or become sky-blue

Examples

The early dawn slowly azured the eastern sky.
Spring skies azure the mountain peaks with a delicate hue.
As she painted the canvas, she skillfully azured the background to capture the calm serenity of the sea.

Synset Examples

Morning azured the village

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue - the color blue

Etymology

From Old French azur, from Arabic lāzaward (lapis lazuli), ultimately from Persian.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00285718-v
Synset POS: v
