behest

1. NOUN

/ bəˈhɛst / (AmE), / bɪˈhɛst / (BrE)
an authoritative command or urgent request, especially given by someone in a position of power
authoritative command or request

Examples

At the king’s behest, the troops marched into the city.
She left the party early at her mother's behest.
The investigation was launched at the behest of the board of directors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: command - an order given by someone in authority
  • Degree Lower: request - an act of asking for something to be given or done

Etymology

From Old English *behǣs* (a vow, command), related to 'hest' (command).
