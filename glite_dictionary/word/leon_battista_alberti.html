Leon Battista Alberti

1. PROPN

/ ˌleɪˌɑːn bɑːˈtistə ælˈbɛrti / (AmE), / ˌleɪˌɒn bætˈtɪstə ælˈbɜːti / (BrE)
(1404–1472) Italian architect, painter, and writer, recognized as a key theorist and innovator of Renaissance architecture.
Italian Renaissance architect and theorist

Examples

The works of Leon Battista Alberti greatly influenced Renaissance architecture across Europe.
Scholars often reference Leon Battista Alberti's treatise 'De re aedificatoria' in architectural studies.
Florence boasts several buildings designed or inspired by Leon Battista Alberti.
