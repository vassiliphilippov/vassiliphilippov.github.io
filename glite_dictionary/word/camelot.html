Camelot

1. PROPN

/ ˈkæməˌlɑt / (AmE), / ˈkæməlɒt / (BrE)
in Arthurian legend, the legendary castle and court associated with King Arthur, famed as a place where justice, truth, and chivalry prevailed
mythical castle of King Arthur

Examples

The legends tell of Camelot as a shining example of chivalry and honor.
Many have tried to locate the real Camelot, but its existence remains a mystery.
Artists often depict Camelot as a grand castle surrounded by green hills and forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Avalon - legendary island paradise in Arthurian legend

Etymology

First recorded in Old French as 'Camelot', appearing in medieval romances.
