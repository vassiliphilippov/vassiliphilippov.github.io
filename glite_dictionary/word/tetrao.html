Tetrao

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛtroʊ / (AmE), / ˈtɛtrɪəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of large birds in the grouse family (Tetraonidae), including the capercaillies.
genus of grouse, including capercaillies

Examples

The Tetrao genus includes several species of capercaillies found in Eurasian forests.
Scientists have studied the behavior of Tetrao to better understand their mating rituals.
Members of the Tetrao genus are known for their striking plumage and large size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capercaillie - a large woodland grouse native to Europe

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, originally Latin *tetrao* (a kind of grouse or capercaillie).
