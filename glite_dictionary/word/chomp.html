chomp

1. VERB

/ tʃɑmp / (AmE), / tʃɒmp / (BrE)
to chew food with loud or vigorous movements of the jaws
to chew noisily or vigorously

Examples

The child began to chomp on his apple as soon as he left the kitchen.
She chomped her gum noisily during the entire movie.
We could hear the horses chomping grass in the field nearby.

Synset Examples

The boy chomped his sandwich

Related Terms

  • Synonym: munch - to chew with steady or audible action
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01177270-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ tʃɑmp / (AmE), / tʃɒmp / (BrE)
an act of biting or chewing vigorously with the teeth and jaws
an act of biting or chewing

Examples

He took a big chomp out of the apple, juice running down his chin.
The dog gave the bone a fierce chomp and broke it in half.
With each chomp, the sound of crunching celery filled the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bite - an act of using the teeth to cut into something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00840477-n
Synset POS: n
