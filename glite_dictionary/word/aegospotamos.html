Aegospotamos

1. PROPN

/ ˌiːɡəˈspɑtəˌmɒs / (AmE), / ˌiːɡɒspəˈteɪmɒs / (BrE)
an ancient river in Thrace (now in Turkey), notable as the site where the Spartan fleet defeated the Athenians in the final battle of the Peloponnesian War in 404 BC
ancient river, site of final Peloponnesian War battle

Examples

The Battle of Aegospotamos marked the end of the Peloponnesian War.
Historians still debate the precise location of Aegospotamos in modern Turkey.
Lysander's victory at Aegospotamos changed the course of Greek history.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Αἰγὸς ποταμός (Aigos Potamos, 'Goat River').

2. PROPN

/ iːˌɡɑːspəˈteɪmɒs / (AmE), / iːˌɡɒspəˈtɑːmɒs / (BrE)
a creek in present-day Turkey, where the Spartan fleet defeated the Athenians in 405 BC, ending the Peloponnesian War.
creek in Turkey, site of major ancient battle

Examples

The decisive naval defeat of the Athenians took place at Aegospotamos in 405 BC.
Historians still study the tactics used at Aegospotamos to understand ancient warfare.
Tourists sometimes visit the site near modern-day Gallipoli, where Aegospotamos once flowed.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Αἰγοσπόταμος (Aigospotamos), meaning 'goat river'.

Synset POS: n
