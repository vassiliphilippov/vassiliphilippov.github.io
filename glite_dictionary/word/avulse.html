to avulse

1. VERB

/ əˈvʌls / (AmE), / əˈvʌls / (BrE)
to separate or tear away by avulsion, especially by sudden force
to separate or tear away by force
medical

Examples

The surgeon had to avulse the damaged tendon during the procedure.
Trauma to the finger can avulse the nail from its bed.
A rapid current may avulse land from a riverbank, changing the property boundary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to detach - to separate something from something else

Etymology

From Latin avulsus, past participle of avellere (to tear off)
