foaming

1. ADJ

/ ˈfoʊmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfəʊmɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a liquid) producing or containing a mass of small bubbles on the surface; frothy.
emitting or filled with bubbles

Examples

The river was foaming at the base of the waterfall after the heavy rain.
She poured a glass of foaming beer for her friend.
The baker scraped off the foaming yeast mixture and added it to the dough.

Synset Examples

bubbling champagne
foamy (or frothy) beer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frothy - full of or covered with a mass of small bubbles
  • Synonym: bubbling - producing bubbles or in the process of emitting gas as bubbles

Etymology

Present participle of "foam," from Old English *fāmian* (to foam).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfoʊmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfəʊmɪŋ / (BrE)
producing or covered with a mass of small bubbles of lathery sweat or saliva, especially as a result of exhaustion or disease.
covered with lathery sweat or saliva

Examples

The dog was foaming at the mouth due to the disease.
After the long race, his horse was foaming with sweat.
She rushed to help the foaming man who had collapsed on the ground.

Synset Examples

the rabid animal's frothing mouth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frothing - producing or covered with a mass of small bubbles, usually from the mouth; often used for animals or people in distress

Etymology

Present participle of 'foam', from Old English *fām* (foam).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
