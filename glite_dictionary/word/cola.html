Cola

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊlə / (AmE), / ˈkəʊlə / (BrE)
A large genus of African evergreen trees that produce kola nuts, used especially as a source of caffeine and flavoring.
genus of African trees yielding kola nuts

Examples

Many species within the Cola genus are cultivated for their caffeine-rich nuts.
The Cola tree is native to the tropical forests of West Africa.
Botanists have identified over 100 species in the Cola genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kola nut - the nut produced by trees of the Cola genus, used as a stimulant and flavoring

Etymology

Modern Latin, from West African languages (related to the word 'kola').

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊlə / (AmE), / ˈkəʊlə / (BrE)
A genus of African trees in the family Malvaceae, known for producing kola nuts.
genus of African trees producing kola nuts

Examples

The Cola genus includes several species that produce the caffeine-rich kola nut.
Botanists study the Cola trees to understand their ecological role in African forests.
Extracts from the Cola plant are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kola nut - the seed of the Cola tree, chewed for its stimulant properties

Etymology

From Latinized form of the West African word 'kola', referring to the nut produced by these trees.
