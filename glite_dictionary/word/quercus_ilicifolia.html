Quercus ilicifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs ɪˌlɪsɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs ɪˌlɪsɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
a shrubby oak native to the southeastern United States, often forming dense thickets
shrubby oak native to southeastern U.S.

Examples

The dense thickets of Quercus ilicifolia provide important habitat for many bird species.
Botanists surveyed several stands of Quercus ilicifolia in the region for conservation studies.
Quercus ilicifolia, also known as bear oak, thrives in poor, sandy soils.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Quercus' meaning 'oak' and 'ilicifolia' meaning 'with holly-like leaves'.
