eccentrically

1. ADV

/ ɪkˈsɛntrɪkli / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɛntrɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner that is unconventional, unusual, or odd.
in an unusual or unconventional manner

Examples

She eccentrically decorated her home with furniture made from recycled tires.
The professor dressed eccentrically, pairing a bright orange hat with purple shoes.
He always acts eccentrically at parties, telling jokes that no one expects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bizarrely - in a very strange or unusual manner
  • Antonym: conventionally - in a way that follows accepted standards or customs

Etymology

From 'eccentric' + '-ly'; 'eccentric' from Greek ekkentros (out of center).

2. ADV

/ ɪkˈsɛntrɪkli / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɛntrɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that is not symmetrically positioned with respect to the center; off-center or away from the central axis.
in a way not centered; off-center

Examples

The gear rotates eccentrically when the shaft is misaligned.
The painting was hung eccentrically on the wall, giving the room a quirky look.
The wheel spun eccentrically, causing a noticeable wobble.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: symmetrically - in a symmetrical, centered manner

Etymology

From 'eccentric' + '-ally', ultimately from Greek 'ekkentros' (out of the center).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
