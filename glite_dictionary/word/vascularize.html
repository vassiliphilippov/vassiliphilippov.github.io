to vascularize

1. VERB

/ ˈvæskjələrˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvæskjʊləˌaɪz / (BrE)
to develop or supply with blood vessels, especially in tissue or organs
develop blood vessels in tissue
medical

Examples

The implanted tissue began to vascularize within a few days.
If the area does not vascularize properly, the graft may fail.
Scientists studied how quickly the artificial organ would vascularize after transplantation.

Synset Examples

The egg yolk vascularized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vascular - relating to blood vessels

Etymology

From 'vascular' (relating to blood vessels) + '-ize' (verb-forming suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00582748-v

2. VERB

/ ˈvæskjələrˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvaskjʊləraɪz / (BrE)
to form blood vessels in (a tissue or structure); to supply with blood vessels
to supply with blood vessels
medical

Examples

As the wound heals, the surrounding tissue begins to vascularize naturally.
Researchers hope to vascularize artificial organs to improve their viability for transplants.
Tumor cells may vascularize rapidly to support their growth.

Synset Examples

the yolk sac is gradually vascularized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vascular - relating to blood vessels

Etymology

From Latin 'vasculum' (small vessel) plus English '-ize'.
