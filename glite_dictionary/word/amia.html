Amia

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.mi.ə / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.mi.ə / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater fish, including the bowfin, that is the only living genus of the family Amiidae.
genus of freshwater fish (bowfin)

Examples

The only living species in the genus Amia is the bowfin, native to North America.
Fossil evidence indicates the genus Amia dates back to the Jurassic period.
Researchers studied the evolutionary history of Amia to understand ancient fish lineages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bowfin - a species of freshwater fish in the genus Amia (Amia calva)

Etymology

From Greek amia (a kind of fish).
