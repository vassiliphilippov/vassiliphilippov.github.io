to detick

1. VERB

/ diˈtɪk / (AmE), / diˈtɪk / (BrE)
to remove ticks from (an animal, especially a pet)
remove ticks from an animal

Examples

The veterinarian had to detick the dog after it returned from the woods.
You should regularly check and detick your pets during tick season.
She spent the afternoon carefully deticking her cat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tick - a small parasitic arachnid that attaches to animals and people

Etymology

Formed by the prefix de- (removal) + tick.
