flatbread

1. NOUN

/ ˈflætˌbrɛd / (AmE), / ˈflætˌbrɛd / (BrE)
A type of bread made from flour, water, and sometimes other ingredients, and baked in thin, flat shapes, typically without yeast.
thin, flat, often unleavened bread

Examples

She served the curry with freshly baked flatbread on the side.
The restaurant offers several types of flatbread, including naan and pita.
For a quick lunch, I topped a flatbread with vegetables and cheese.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pita - A type of Middle Eastern flatbread that is often hollow inside
  • Synonym: naan - A soft and leavened Indian flatbread
