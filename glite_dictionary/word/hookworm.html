hookworm

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʊkˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈhʊkˌwɜːm / (BrE)
a parasitic roundworm with hooked mouthparts that attaches to the intestines of humans or animals and feeds on blood
parasitic intestinal roundworm
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that a hookworm infection could cause anemia in children.
Farmers are at risk of contracting hookworm from contaminated soil.
Effective sanitation and footwear can help prevent hookworm infestations.

Etymology

From 'hook' + 'worm', referencing the worm's hooked mouthparts.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhʊkˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈhʊkˌwɜːm / (BrE)
An infestation of the intestines by hookworms, typically contracted when the larvae enter the body through the skin.
intestinal infection by hookworms
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with hookworm after she returned from her trip abroad.
Unsanitary conditions can increase the risk of hookworm infections in a community.
Symptoms of hookworm include abdominal pain, diarrhea, and anemia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ascariasis - infestation with roundworms (Ascaris) in the intestines
  • Synonym: tapeworm - parasitic intestinal worm often causing infection

Etymology

From 'hook' (a curved shape) + 'worm,' referring to the parasite's shape.
