Pyralididae

1. NOUN

/ pɪˌræləˈdɪdiː / (AmE), / pɪˌralɪˈdɪːdiː / (BrE)
a family of moths that includes bee moths, corn borers, and flour moths, some of which are pests of stored products and crops
family of moths including pests and stored product moths

Examples

The Pyralididae include many species that can damage grains in storage.
Some members of the Pyralididae are known for infesting beehives.
Researchers studied the life cycle of the Pyralididae to improve pest control methods.

Etymology

From New Latin Pyralididae, based on the type genus Pyralis.

2. NOUN

/ pɪˌrælɪˈdɪdi / (AmE), / pɪˌralɪˈdɪːdiː / (BrE)
a large family of moths that includes bee moths, corn borers, and flour moths, many of which are pests of crops and stored products
a family of moths including pests like flour moths

Examples

Several members of the Pyralididae family are considered significant agricultural pests.
Entomologists study the life cycles of Pyralididae to develop better pest control methods.
Some species in the Pyralididae family infest stored grain and flour products.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Pyralididae', based on 'Pyralis', a genus of moths, and the suffix '-idae' denoting animal families.
