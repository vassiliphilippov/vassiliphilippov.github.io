inorganic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnɔːrˈɡænɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪnɔːˈɡænɪk / (BrE)
not consisting of or derived from living organisms; lacking the qualities of living things
not from living organisms

Examples

The scientist studied several inorganic compounds found in rocks.
Glass is considered inorganic because it does not come from a living source.
The soil in this region contains both inorganic matter and decaying plant material.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: organic - relating to or derived from living organisms

Etymology

From prefix in- (not) + organic, based on Latin organicus (relating to an organ).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪnɔrˈɡænɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪnɔːˈɡænɪk / (BrE)
(of a chemical compound or substance) not containing carbon or not derived from living matter.
not containing carbon; not organic

Examples

Hydrochloric acid is classified as an inorganic compound in chemistry.
Unlike sugars and proteins, minerals are considered inorganic nutrients.
The study of inorganic chemistry focuses on elements and compounds that lack carbon atoms.

Synset Examples

hydrochloric and sulfuric acids are called inorganic substances

Related Terms

  • Antonym: organic - of or relating to carbon-based compounds or substances derived from living matter

Etymology

From in- (not) + organic, after French inorganique.

WordNet ID: oewn-01683903-a
Synset POS: a
