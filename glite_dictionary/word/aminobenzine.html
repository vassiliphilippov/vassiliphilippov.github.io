aminobenzine

1. NOUN

/ əˌmiːnoʊˈbɛnziːn / (AmE), / əˌmiːnəʊˈbɛnziːn / (BrE)
A poisonous, oily liquid amine derived from nitrobenzene, used in the manufacture of dyes, plastics, and medicines.
poisonous liquid used in dyes and plastics
medical

Examples

Aminobenzine is a key intermediate in the production of synthetic dyes.
Exposure to aminobenzine can pose serious health risks if proper precautions are not taken.
Researchers are studying safer alternatives to aminobenzine in industrial processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aniline - a colorless, oily, poisonous aromatic amine used in dyes and chemicals

Etymology

From amino- (denoting the presence of an amino group) and benzene (the aromatic hydrocarbon base).
