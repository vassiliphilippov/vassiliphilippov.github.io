pommy

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑmi / (AmE), / ˈpɒmi / (BrE)
(informal, sometimes offensive) A British person, especially as referred to in Australia or New Zealand, often with a disparaging or playful tone.
(informal, sometimes offensive) British person (Austral./NZ)
informal offensive british

Examples

They always called the new teacher a pommy, even though she had been living in Australia for years.
Some Australians use the term pommy to refer to people from England, sometimes in jest.
He didn’t mind the nickname pommy and often joked about his British roots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Brit - a British person (informal)
  • Synonym: limey - a British person, especially a sailor (informal, dated)

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from 'pomegranate' (rhyming slang for 'immigrant'). First recorded in Australia in the early 20th century.
