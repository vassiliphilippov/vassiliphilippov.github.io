inexact

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnɪɡˈzækt / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪɡˈzækt / (BrE)
not completely accurate or precise; lacking in exactness.
not completely accurate or precise

Examples

His explanation was inexact and left out important details.
Because of the inexact measurements, the results were inconsistent.
The old map provides only an inexact location of the lost city.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exact - completely correct or precise; free from error
  • Synonym: imprecise - not exact or accurate

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + exactus (precise, completed).
