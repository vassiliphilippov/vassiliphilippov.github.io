laboratory

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæbrəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ləˈbɒrətəri / (BrE)
A room or building equipped for scientific experiments, research, or teaching, or for the manufacture of drugs or chemicals.
room for scientific experiments

Examples

The new cancer drug was developed in the laboratory before being tested on patients.
Students must wear safety goggles at all times in the chemistry laboratory.
Our laboratory is equipped with the latest microscopes and computers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lab - informal shortening of 'laboratory'

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'laboratorium', from Latin 'laborare' (to work).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlæbrəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ləˈbɒrətəri / (BrE)
a situation or environment resembling a laboratory as it provides opportunities for observation, practice, and experimentation, especially for testing new ideas or policies
environment for testing or experimentation

Examples

The small country became a laboratory for innovative political reforms.
Urban schools often serve as a laboratory for new teaching methods.
His research team viewed the remote village as a unique laboratory for studying community economics.

Synset Examples

the new nation is a testing ground for socioeconomic theories
Pakistan is a laboratory for studying the use of American troops to combat terrorism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: testing ground - a place or situation where methods or ideas can be tried out

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'laboratorium', meaning 'a place for labor or work'.
