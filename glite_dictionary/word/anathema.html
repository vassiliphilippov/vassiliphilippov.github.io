anathema

1. NOUN

/ əˈnæθəmə / (AmE), / əˈnaθəmə / (BrE)
A formal curse issued by a religious authority, usually accompanied by excommunication from the church.
religious curse with excommunication

Examples

The medieval church placed heretics under anathema for their beliefs.
Excommunication and anathema were powerful tools used by the clergy to maintain doctrinal control.
Receiving anathema meant complete separation from the religious community.

Etymology

From Latin 'anathema', from Greek 'anathema' (a thing devoted to evil, curse).

2. NOUN

/ əˈnæθəmə / (AmE), / əˈnaθəmə / (BrE)
a person who is intensely disliked or detested
a detested or hated person

Examples

The politician became an anathema to environmental groups after his controversial policies.
For some critics, the director's new film was an anathema rather than a triumph.
She considered hypocrisy to be an anathema, and she avoided it at all costs.

Synset Examples

he is an anathema to me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pariah - an outcast; a person who is rejected by society
  • Synonym: enemy - a person who is actively opposed or hostile

Etymology

From Latin 'anathema', originally from Greek 'anathema' (a thing devoted, especially to evil); later used in the sense of 'accursed person'.
