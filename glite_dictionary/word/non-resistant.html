non-resistant

1. NOUN

/ nɑn rɪˈzɪstənt / (AmE), / nɒn rɪˈzɪstənt / (BrE)
a person who believes in or practices passive resistance, refusing to use force even in self-defense, especially for religious or moral reasons
person who believes in passive resistance

Examples

The non-resistant refused to retaliate even when provoked by violence.
As a committed non-resistant, she participated in peaceful protests during the civil rights movement.
Quaker communities often counted many non-resistants among their members.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pacifist - a person who believes that war and violence are unjustifiable
  • Antonym: militant - favoring confrontational or violent methods

Etymology

From non- (not) + resistant, modeled on terms like 'nonresistance' used in 19th-century religious contexts.
