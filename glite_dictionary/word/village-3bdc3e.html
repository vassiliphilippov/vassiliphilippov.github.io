village

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈvɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
A group of houses and other buildings, usually smaller than a town, situated in a rural area.
small rural settlement

Examples

She grew up in a small village nestled between the mountains.
The entire village gathers for the annual summer festival.
Tourists often visit the picturesque fishing village on the coast.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: town - a settlement larger than a village but smaller than a city
  • Degree Lower: hamlet - a very small settlement, smaller than a village

Etymology

From Old French *village*, from Latin *villaticum* (farmstead).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈvɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
a group of houses and associated buildings, larger than a hamlet but smaller than a town, situated in a rural area and forming a community
small rural community; smaller than a town

Examples

The entire village gathered in the square to celebrate the festival.
She grew up in a remote village where everyone knew each other.
Tourists often visit the old fishing village to experience traditional coastal life.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: town - an urban area larger than a village but smaller than a city
  • Degree Lower: hamlet - a small settlement, smaller than a village
  • Degree Higher: city - a large urban settlement, larger than a town

Etymology

From Old French 'village', from Latin 'villaticum' (farmstead), from 'villa' (country house).
