cowslip

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊˌslɪp / (AmE), / ˈkaʊ.slɪp / (BrE)
A wild plant of the primrose family, commonly found in the British Isles, with clusters of fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.
wild yellow-flowered spring plant

Examples

We found a patch of cowslip blooming in the meadow near the river.
The cowslip is cherished for its delicate scent and bright yellow flowers.
Gardeners often cultivate cowslip to add color to their spring borders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primrose - a spring-flowering plant of the same genus with pale yellow flowers

Etymology

From Old English *cū-sloppe*, meaning 'cow-slop' (from where it often grows in meadows grazed by cattle).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊˌslɪp / (AmE), / ˈkaʊ.slɪp / (BrE)
a flowering plant of the primrose family, with clusters of fragrant yellow flowers, native to Europe and Asia; also, a similar swamp plant with yellow, buttercup-like flowers found in Europe and North America
a yellow-flowered plant of the primrose family

Examples

The meadow was full of cowslips blooming in the spring sunshine.
She pressed a cowslip between the pages of her journal as a keepsake.
American cowslip grows in wetland areas and adds a splash of yellow to the landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primrose - a plant of the primrose family with pale yellow flowers

Etymology

From Old English *cū-slyppe* (‘cow-slop’), probably referring to the plant's common growth in cow pastures.
