dichotomisation

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌkɑːtəmaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / daɪˌkɒtəmaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of dividing something into two distinct and opposite groups or categories.
dividing into two categories

Examples

The dichotomisation of opinions in the debate made compromise nearly impossible.
Medical research warns against the dichotomisation of continuous data, as it can oversimplify results.
The social scientist criticised the dichotomisation of gender roles in the study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dichotomy - a division into two contrasting groups or entities

Etymology

From dichotomy, after Greek 'dikhotomia' (a cutting in two), plus suffix '-isation'.
