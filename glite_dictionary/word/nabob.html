nabob

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪ.bɑb / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.bɒb / (BrE)
a governor or high official in India, especially during the period of the Mogul empire
governor in Mogul India

Examples

During the Mughal era, the nabob held considerable power over his province.
The British often negotiated with the local nabob to secure trade agreements.
Historical documents describe the wealth and influence of a nabob in 18th-century India.

Etymology

From Hindi 'nawwāb', from Arabic 'nuwwāb', plural of 'nā'ib' (deputy, viceroy).

2. NOUN

/ ˈneɪ.bɑːb / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.bɒb / (BrE)
a very rich or influential person, especially one who gained wealth in the East, such as India
wealthy or influential person (historical)

Examples

The British colonial officer returned from India as a nabob with a grand estate.
In 18th-century England, many viewed the wealthy nabob with suspicion and envy.
Having made his fortune overseas, he became something of a local nabob upon his return.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnate - a wealthy and influential person, especially in business
  • Synonym: tycoon - a wealthy, powerful businessperson or industrialist

Etymology

From Hindi 'nawāb', via Urdu, referring to a Muslim ruler or governor in India.
