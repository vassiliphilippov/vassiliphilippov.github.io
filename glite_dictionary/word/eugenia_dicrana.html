Eugenia dicrana

1. NOUN

/ juːˈdʒiːniə daɪˈkreɪnə / (AmE), / juːˈdʒiːniə dɪˈkraːnə / (BrE)
A tree native to extreme southern Florida and the West Indies, recognized for its thin scaly bark, aromatic fruits and seeds, and hard, heavy, close-grained zebrawood.
tropical tree yielding zebrawood

Examples

The wood of Eugenia dicrana is highly valued for its striking zebra-like stripes.
Botanists found a thriving population of Eugenia dicrana in the wilds of southern Florida.
Because of its aromatic fruit, Eugenia dicrana attracts a variety of local wildlife.

Etymology

From the genus name *Eugenia*, from Greek *eugenēs* (well-born), and Latinized species name.
