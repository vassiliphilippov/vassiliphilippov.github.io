paleomammalogy

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪlioʊˌmæməˈlɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌpæliəʊˌmaməˈlɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study of ancient mammals based on fossil evidence and other paleontological methods
study of ancient mammals
medical

Examples

She decided to specialize in paleomammalogy because of her fascination with prehistoric creatures.
Advances in paleomammalogy have provided new insights into the evolution of mammals.
The museum's new exhibit features discoveries from the field of paleomammalogy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paleontology - the scientific study of fossils and ancient life forms
  • Degree Lower: mammalogy - the branch of zoology concerned with mammals

Etymology

From Greek palaios (ancient), Latin mamma (breast; mammal), and -logy (study of).
