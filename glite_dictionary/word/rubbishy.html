rubbishy

1. ADJ

/ ˈrʌbɪʃi / (AmE), / ˈrʌbɪʃi / (BrE)
of low quality, value, or worth; cheap and inferior
cheap and inferior; worthless

Examples

She quickly discarded the rubbishy pamphlets cluttering the waiting room table.
Most of the souvenirs sold at the market were rather rubbishy and cheaply made.
He regretted spending money on such a rubbishy gadget that broke after one use.

Synset Examples

rubbishy newspapers that form almost the sole reading of the majority
trashy merchandise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trashy - of very poor quality; worthless
  • Synonym: worthless - having no real value or use
  • Antonym: valuable - worth a lot of money or importance

Etymology

From 'rubbish' (waste or refuse) + '-y' (adjective suffix).
