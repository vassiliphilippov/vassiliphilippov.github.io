wickedness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪkɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈwɪkɪdnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being morally wrong or evil; morally objectionable behavior.
the quality of being evil or morally wrong

Examples

The villagers were shocked by the wickedness of the crime committed in their midst.
She was drawn to tales of wickedness and redemption.
He tried to hide his wickedness behind a charming smile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evil - profound immorality and wickedness, especially when regarded as a supernatural force
  • Antonym: virtue - behavior showing high moral standards

Etymology

From Old English 'wicednes' (wickedness), from 'wicede' (wicked).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪkɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈwɪkɪdnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being morally wrong or evil; the absence of moral or spiritual values.
absence of moral or spiritual values

Examples

Many legends speak of the wickedness lurking in the shadows of ancient castles.
She was shocked by the wickedness of his actions.
Throughout history, people have struggled to overcome wickedness with goodness and compassion.

Synset Examples

the powers of darkness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evil - profound immorality and wickedness
  • Antonym: virtue - behavior showing high moral standards; moral excellence

Etymology

From Old English *wicednes* (wicked, baneful), based on *wicca* (wizard, sorcerer) with -ness.

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɪkɪdnɪs / (AmE), / ˈwɪkɪdnɪs / (BrE)
Separation from God due to sinfulness or moral corruption, often used in religious contexts.
estrangement from God; sinfulness

Examples

Many sermons warn against wickedness as a path leading away from God.
He prayed for forgiveness and redemption from his life of wickedness.
They believed that wickedness brought spiritual separation from the divine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sinfulness - state of being sinful or morally wrong
  • Antonym: righteousness - conformity to divine or moral law

Etymology

From Old English *wiccednes* (“wickedness; badness; immorality”), from *wicca* (“sorcerer; wizard”).
