to brominate

1. VERB

/ ˈbroʊ.mɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊ.mɪˌneɪt / (BrE)
to treat or react (a substance) with bromine, especially by introducing bromine atoms into a compound
treat with bromine
medical

Examples

The laboratory technician will brominate the solution to test its reactivity.
Many flame retardants are produced by brominating organic compounds.
The reaction yielded a brominated compound after the sample was brominated overnight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to chlorinate - to treat or react a substance with chlorine
  • Synonym: to iodinate - to treat or react a substance with iodine

Etymology

From bromine + -ate, modeled after similar chemical terms.

2. VERB

/ ˈbroʊ.məˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊ.mɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
to treat or react with bromine, especially by introducing bromine into another chemical compound
to react with or add bromine

Examples

To make the compound less reactive, chemists decided to brominate the solution.
The company brominates drinking water to disinfect it in some areas.
This substance was brominated using laboratory equipment before further testing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to chlorinate - to treat or react with chlorine
  • Synonym: to oxidize - to combine with or introduce oxygen

Etymology

From bromine (the chemical element) + -ate (verb-forming suffix).
