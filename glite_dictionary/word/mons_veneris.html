mons veneris

1. NOUN

/ mɑnz ˈvɛnərɪs / (AmE), / mɒnz ˈvɛnərɪs / (BrE)
the rounded mound of fatty tissue covering the pubic bone in women, typically more prominent after puberty
mound of tissue above female pubic bone
medical

Examples

The mons veneris becomes more pronounced during puberty due to hormonal changes.
Some women may experience discomfort around the mons veneris after shaving.
The surgeon explained the anatomy of the mons veneris before the procedure.

Etymology

From Latin 'mons Veneris' meaning 'mount of Venus', linking to the Roman goddess of love.
