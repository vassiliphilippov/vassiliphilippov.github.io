wrapped

1. ADJ

/ ræpt / (AmE), / ræpt / (BrE)
Enclosed or covered securely, especially in paper or other material.
enclosed or covered securely

Examples

Each wrapped present was placed carefully under the tree.
She handed me a wrapped sandwich for lunch.
The wrapped package was labeled with my name.

Synset Examples

gaily wrapped gifts

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unwrapped - not enclosed or covered; without a covering

2. ADJ

/ ræpt / (AmE), / ræpt / (BrE)
fully absorbed or engrossed in something; giving complete attention to something, often used with 'in' (e.g., wrapped in thought)
fully absorbed or engrossed; attentive

Examples

She sat wrapped in thought, not noticing the time passing by.
The children listened, wrapped in wonder at the magician's tricks.
He was so wrapped in the story that he didn't hear the phone ring.

Synset Examples

so intent on this fantastic … narrative that she hardly stirred
rapt with wonder
wrapped in thought

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engrossed - giving all your attention to something
  • Synonym: absorbed - deeply interested or involved; preoccupied
  • Antonym: distracted - unable to concentrate because attention is diverted

3. ADJ

/ ræpt / (AmE), / ræpt / (BrE)
covered or enclosed completely or partially, as if with a wrap, cloak, or another covering
covered or enclosed as with a wrap

Examples

The ancient statue was wrapped in layers of protective cloth before being moved.
She stood outside, wrapped in a warm woolen blanket against the morning chill.
The mountain peaks were wrapped in mist, creating a mysterious atmosphere.

Synset Examples

leaf-clothed trees
fog-cloaked meadows
a beam draped with cobwebs
cloud-wrapped peaks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: covered - having something on all or part of a surface
  • Antonym: bare - not covered; exposed or uncovered

Etymology

Past participle of 'wrap', from Middle English 'wrappen'.
