Olympia

1. PROPN

/ əˈlɪmpiə / (AmE), / əˈlɪmpiə / (BrE)
the capital city of the state of Washington, located in western Washington on Puget Sound
capital city of Washington, USA

Examples

The state legislature meets in Olympia every year to pass new laws.
We visited Olympia on our road trip along the Pacific Northwest.
Olympia offers beautiful views of Puget Sound and the surrounding mountains.

Etymology

Named after the nearby Olympic Mountains, which were named for Mount Olympus in Greece.

2. PROPN

/ oʊˈlɪmpiə / (AmE), / əˈlɪmpiə / (BrE)
an ancient plain in northwestern Peloponnese, Greece, famous as the chief sanctuary of Zeus and the site of the original Olympic Games.
ancient Greek site of the original Olympic Games

Examples

Many tourists visit Olympia to see the ruins of the ancient stadium and temples.
The original Olympic Games were held at Olympia in honor of Zeus.
Archaeologists uncovered fascinating artifacts at Olympia dating back to classical Greece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Olympic Games - international sporting event originating in Olympia, Greece

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ὀλυμπία (Olympia), the name of the sanctuary of Zeus.
