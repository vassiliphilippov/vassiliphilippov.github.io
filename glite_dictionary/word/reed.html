reed

1. NOUN

/ rid / (AmE), / riːd / (BrE)
a tall, slender-leaved plant of the grass family, typically growing in marshy places, with hollow stems; especially species such as Arundo and Phragmites.
tall marsh plant with hollow stems

Examples

The riverbank was lined with thick reeds swaying gently in the wind.
Herons often build their nests among the reeds near the lake.
They used dried reed to make simple musical instruments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush - a tall, grasslike plant of wet places, similar to reed

Etymology

Old English *hreod*, of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ rid / (AmE), / riːd / (BrE)
A musical instrument that produces sound by means of a vibrating reed, typically found in woodwind families.
musical instrument using a vibrating reed

Examples

The saxophone is a type of reed instrument commonly used in jazz.
Oboe players must carefully select the best reed for their instrument.
She collects antique reeds and displays them in her music studio.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodwind - a group of musical instruments, including those with vibrating reeds

Etymology

From Old English *hreod* (reed, pipe), related to the plant but also extended to musical pipes; see also similar uses in Germanic languages.

3. NOUN

/ rid / (AmE), / riːd / (BrE)
a thin strip of stiff material that vibrates to produce a tone in some wind instruments when air is blown across it
vibrating strip in wind instruments

Examples

The clarinetist fitted a new reed onto his mouthpiece before the concert.
She soaked the reed in water to make it more flexible for her oboe.
Many saxophonists carry spare reeds to replace broken ones during performances.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mouthpiece - the part of a wind instrument into which the player blows
