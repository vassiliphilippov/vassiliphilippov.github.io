antisocial

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.tiˈsoʊ.ʃəl / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˈsəʊ.ʃəl / (BrE)
not liking to spend time with other people; avoiding the company of others.
avoiding company of others

Examples

Although he seemed antisocial, he was simply reserved and preferred time alone.
Her antisocial behavior made it hard for her to make new friends at school.
Don't mistake being antisocial for being rude; some people just need more personal space.

Synset Examples

standoffish and antisocial
he's not antisocial, just shy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sociable - enjoying the company of others

Etymology

From Greek anti- (against) + socialis (of companionship, society).

2. ADJ

/ ˌæn.tiˈsoʊ.ʃəl / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˈsəʊ.ʃəl / (BrE)
hostile to or disruptive of accepted standards of social behavior, especially in a way that violates the rights of others
hostile or disruptive of social norms

Examples

Vandalizing public property is considered an antisocial act by most communities.
Persistent antisocial behavior can lead to serious legal consequences.
The new law aims to tackle antisocial activities in urban areas.

Synset Examples

criminal behavior or conduct that violates the rights of other individuals is antisocial
crimes … and other asocial behavior
an antisocial deed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asocial - avoiding social interaction; indifferent to social norms

Etymology

From anti- (against) + social, based on Latin socialis (of companionship, social).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02257692-s
Synset POS: s
