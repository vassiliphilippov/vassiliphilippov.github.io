Vithar

1. PROPN

/ ˈviːθɑr / (AmE), / ˈviːθɑː / (BrE)
In Norse mythology, a member of the Aesir and son of Odin, who avenges his father by killing Fenrir during Ragnarok.
Norse god, son of Odin

Examples

According to legend, Vithar avenged Odin by slaying the wolf Fenrir at Ragnarok.
The god Vithar is often depicted as silent and steadfast in Norse myths.
Some scholars interpret Vithar's actions at Ragnarok as symbols of vengeance and renewal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vidar - Alternate spelling of Vithar, Norse god who slays Fenrir

Etymology

From Old Norse 'Víðarr', a personal name in Norse mythology.
