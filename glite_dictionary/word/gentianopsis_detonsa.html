Gentianopsis detonsa

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛn.ʃi.əˈnɑp.sɪs dɪˈtɔn.sə / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛn.tʃi.əˈnɒp.sɪs dɪˈtɒn.sə / (BrE)
A species of fringed gentian with pale-blue to blue-purple flowers, native to northern regions across Eurasia and North America.
species of fringed gentian flower

Examples

The delicate blue flowers of Gentianopsis detonsa brighten many northern meadows in late summer.
Botanists were excited to discover a rare patch of Gentianopsis detonsa during their field survey.
Pollinators such as bees play an important role in the life cycle of Gentianopsis detonsa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fringed gentian - a group of gentians with fringed petals

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name from Latin 'gentiana' (gentian), species name 'detonsa' meaning 'shorn' or 'trimmed'.
