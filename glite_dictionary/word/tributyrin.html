tributyrin

1. NOUN

/ ˌtraɪˈbjuːtɪrɪn / (AmE), / ˌtrɪˈbjuːtɪrɪn / (BrE)
a bitter, oily triglyceride of butyric acid, used especially in microbiology as a substrate to test for the presence of lipase enzymes
bitter, oily triglyceride of butyric acid
medical

Examples

The laboratory used tributyrin agar to test for the bacterial production of lipase.
Tributyrin is often present in butter and is responsible for some of its properties.
Researchers added tributyrin to the culture medium to observe enzymatic activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butyrin - a mixture of triglycerides of butyric acid

Etymology

From Latin 'tri-' (three) and 'butyrum' (butter), referring to its three butyric acid groups.
