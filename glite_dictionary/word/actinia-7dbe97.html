actinia

1. NOUN

/ ækˈtɪniə / (AmE), / ækˈtɪnɪə / (BrE)
a sea anemone or related marine animal belonging to the order Actiniaria
type of sea anemone

Examples

The children were fascinated by the colorful actinia clinging to the rocks in the tidal pool.
An actinia uses its stinging tentacles to capture small prey in the water.
Marine biologists found a rare species of actinia along the coral reef.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea anemone - marine animal related to corals, with stinging tentacles

Etymology

From New Latin *Actinia*, from Greek *aktis* (ray, beam) due to the ray-like tentacles.
