proline

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊˌlin / (AmE), / ˈprəʊliːn / (BrE)
an amino acid found in many proteins, especially collagen, important for the structure of such proteins
an amino acid in proteins
medical

Examples

Proline is essential for the structure of collagen in connective tissues.
Because proline is a non-essential amino acid, the body can synthesize it.
Scientists found a high concentration of proline in the muscle proteins of that species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amino acid - organic compounds forming the building blocks of proteins

Etymology

From pyrrolidine, the parent compound, and -ine, used for amino acids.
