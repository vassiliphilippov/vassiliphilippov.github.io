limply

1. ADV

/ ˈlɪmpli / (AmE), / ˈlɪmpli / (BrE)
in a way that lacks stiffness or firmness; without rigidity
in a loose or floppy manner

Examples

The flag hung limply in the windless air.
She shook his hand, and it felt limply in her grasp.
After the marathon, he stumbled limply to the finish line.

Synset Examples

the body was hanging limply from the tree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: floppily - in a floppy or loose manner

Etymology

From the adjective 'limp' + adverbial suffix '-ly'.
