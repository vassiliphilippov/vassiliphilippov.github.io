to generate

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒɛnəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnəreɪt / (BrE)
to produce energy, especially electricity or power, through a physical or chemical process
produce energy or electricity

Examples

Modern wind turbines generate enough electricity to power thousands of homes.
The new engine design can generate more power using less fuel.
During the blackout, we used a generator to generate electricity for basic appliances.

Synset Examples

We can't generate enough power for the entire city
The hydroelectric plant needs to generate more electricity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: produce - to make or create something, especially as a result of a process

Etymology

From Latin generare (to beget, produce).

4. VERB

/ ˈdʒɛnəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnəreɪt / (BrE)
(of a person, animal, or plant) to produce offspring by reproduction.
produce offspring by reproduction

Examples

Some species of fish are able to generate hundreds of offspring in a single season.
In rare cases, a single plant may generate a new lineage through self-fertilization.
The ancient myth claimed that gods could generate children with mortals.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to procreate - to produce young; to reproduce
