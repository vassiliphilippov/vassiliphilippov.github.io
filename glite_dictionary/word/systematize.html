to systematize

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪstəməˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪstəmətaɪz / (BrE)
to arrange or organize according to a system or method, making something systematic
arrange or organize systematically

Examples

The new manager worked hard to systematize the office procedures for greater efficiency.
Scientists have tried to systematize the vast amount of data collected during the experiment.
She helped systematize the library by categorizing all the books by subject.

Synset Examples

systematize our scientific knowledge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to organize - to arrange or put in order systematically

Etymology

From Greek 'systēmatizein' (to organize), from 'systēma' (system).
