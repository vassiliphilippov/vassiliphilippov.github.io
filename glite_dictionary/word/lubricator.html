lubricator

1. NOUN

/ ˈluː.brɪˌkeɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈluː.brɪˌkeɪ.tə / (BrE)
a substance used to reduce friction between surfaces by making them smooth or slippery
substance that reduces friction

Examples

The engine requires a special lubricator to ensure smooth operation.
A good lubricator can extend the life of moving mechanical parts.
Scientists developed a new synthetic lubricator for industrial machinery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lubricant - a substance that reduces friction between surfaces in mutual contact

Etymology

From Latin 'lubricare' (to make slippery) via 'lubricate' and the suffix '-or'.
