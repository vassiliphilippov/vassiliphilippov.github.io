roentgenium

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛntˈɡɛniəm / (AmE), / ˌrɛntˈɡɛniəm / (BrE)
A radioactive transuranic chemical element with the symbol Rg and atomic number 111.
radioactive transuranic element (Rg, atomic number 111)

Examples

Scientists first synthesized roentgenium in 1994 by bombarding bismuth with nickel ions.
Because of its short half-life, roentgenium has no known practical applications.
The chemical symbol for roentgenium is Rg.

Etymology

Named after Wilhelm Röntgen (1845–1923), the discoverer of X-rays.
