atrabilious

1. ADJ

/ ˌætrəˈbɪliəs / (AmE), / ˌætrəˈbɪlɪəs / (BrE)
Irritable or melancholy in temperament; easily angered or gloomy.
irritable; gloomy in temperament
archaic

Examples

His atrabilious remarks soured the mood of the entire evening.
The atrabilious old man rarely smiled at his neighbors.
Under stress, she became distinctly atrabilious and snapped at minor annoyances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritable - easily annoyed or angered
  • Synonym: melancholy - sadness or gloominess in temperament

Etymology

From Latin 'atra bilis' meaning 'black bile', an ancient medical theory of temperament.
