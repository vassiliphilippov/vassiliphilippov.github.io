mafioso

1. NOUN

/ mɑːfiˈoʊsoʊ / (AmE), / mæfiˈəʊsəʊ / (BrE)
a member of the Mafia, especially in the United States
member of the Mafia crime syndicate

Examples

The police arrested a notorious mafioso during the raid.
He lived in fear of retribution from the local mafioso after refusing to pay protection money.
Several mafiosi were put on trial for organized crime activities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mobster - member of a criminal gang, especially the Mafia

Etymology

Italian, from 'mafia' + '-oso' (full of)

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːfiˈoʊsoʊ / (AmE), / ˌmæfiˈəʊsəʊ / (BrE)
a member of the Sicilian Mafia or similar criminal organization
member of the Sicilian Mafia

Examples

The police investigated the suspected mafioso for involvement in several illegal activities.
He played the role of a notorious mafioso in the film set in Sicily.
Rumors spread that a powerful mafioso had moved into the neighborhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gangster - a member of a criminal gang

Etymology

Italian, from mafia (Mafia) + -oso (having the quality of)

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10300192-n
Synset POS: n
