Pyrenomycetes

1. NOUN

/ paɪˌrɛnəmaɪˈsiːtiːz / (AmE), / paɪˌrɛnəʊmaɪˈsiːtiːz / (BrE)
a former class of fungi characterized by having perithecia as fruiting bodies; includes powdery mildews, ergot, and Neurospora
class of fungi with perithecia
medical

Examples

Many plant diseases are caused by Pyrenomycetes due to their parasitic nature.
Scientists study Pyrenomycetes to understand the development of fungal fruiting bodies.
Some Pyrenomycetes include fungi responsible for producing important antibiotics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ascomycetes - class of fungi to which Pyrenomycetes formerly belonged
  • Antonym: Basidiomycetes - class of fungi distinct from Pyrenomycetes

Etymology

From Greek 'pyrēn' (stone or pit) + '-mycetes' from 'mykēs' (fungus), referring to the perithecium structure.

2. NOUN

/ paɪˌrɛnoʊmaɪˈsiːtiːz / (AmE), / paɪˌriːnəʊmaɪˈsiːtiːz / (BrE)
a class of fungi characterized by having fruiting bodies called perithecia, including organisms such as powdery mildews, ergot, and Neurospora
a class of fungi with perithecial fruiting bodies

Examples

Many plant diseases are caused by fungi in the Pyrenomycetes class.
Researchers isolated a new species of Pyrenomycetes from decaying wood.
The structure of perithecia is a distinguishing feature of the Pyrenomycetes.

Etymology

From Greek 'pyrēn' (stone, pit) + 'mykēs' (fungus), via New Latin.
