dipteron

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪptəˌrɑn / (AmE), / ˈdɪptərɒn / (BrE)
An insect of the order Diptera, typically having a single pair of functional wings and mouthparts adapted for sucking, lapping, or piercing.
insect of the order Diptera (true fly)

Examples

The mosquito is a type of dipteron found all over the world.
Several species of dipteron are important pollinators.
Unlike beetles, a dipteron has only one pair of wings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fly - a common name for insects of the order Diptera

Etymology

From Greek dipteron, from di- (two) + pteron (wing).
