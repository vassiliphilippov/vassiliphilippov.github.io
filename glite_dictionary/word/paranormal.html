paranormal

1. ADJ

/ ˌpærəˈnɔrməl / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈnɔːməl / (BrE)
(of events or phenomena) not explicable by known scientific laws and therefore typically regarded as outside the range of normal experience.
(of events) not explainable by science

Examples

Many people claim to have experienced paranormal activity in the old mansion.
The documentary explored several paranormal phenomena, including ghosts and telekinesis.
He refuses to believe in paranormal explanations without strong scientific evidence.

Synset Examples

what seemed to be paranormal manifestations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supernatural - beyond what is natural or explainable by natural law
  • Antonym: normal - conforming to scientific laws; not paranormal

Etymology

From Greek 'para' (beside, beyond) + 'normal'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌpɛrəˈnɔrməl / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈnɔːməl / (BrE)
Not scientifically explainable; beyond or deviating from what is normal or usual, especially outside normal sensory experiences.
beyond normal scientific explanation

Examples

Many people claim to have experienced paranormal activity, such as seeing ghosts or unexplained lights.
The documentary examines several cases of paranormal phenomena in rural communities.
Scientists generally remain skeptical about the existence of paranormal abilities like telepathy and clairvoyance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supernatural - attributed to a force beyond scientific understanding
  • Antonym: normal - conforming to a standard; regular

Etymology

From para- (beside, beyond) + normal.
