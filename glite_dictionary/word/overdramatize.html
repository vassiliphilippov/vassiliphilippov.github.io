to overdramatize

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈdræmətaɪz / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈdræmətaɪz / (BrE)
to present or describe something as more dramatic or serious than it really is
make something seem overly dramatic

Examples

Try not to overdramatize the events; it was just a minor disagreement.
The movie overdramatizes what actually happened during the expedition.
He tends to overdramatize his achievements when talking to his friends.

Synset Examples

She is overdramatizing her child's failure in the physics class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exaggerate - to represent something as greater than it is

Etymology

From over- (excessively) + dramatize, which comes from drama.
