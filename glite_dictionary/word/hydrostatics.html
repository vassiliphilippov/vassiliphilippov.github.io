hydrostatics

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdrəˈstætɪks / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪdrəʊˈstatɪks / (BrE)
the branch of physics that deals with the mechanical properties and behavior of fluids at rest
mechanics of stationary fluids

Examples

He specialized in hydrostatics during his postgraduate physics studies.
The principles of hydrostatics explain how pressure is transmitted in all directions in a fluid at rest.
Understanding hydrostatics is essential when designing dams and ships.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: fluid mechanics - the study of the mechanics of fluids, both in motion and at rest
  • Antonym: hydrodynamics - the study of the motion of fluids

Etymology

From Greek hydōr (water) + statikos (causing to stand), via French hydrostatique.
