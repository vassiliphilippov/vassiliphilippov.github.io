braille

1. NOUN

/ breɪl / (AmE), / breɪl / (BrE)
a system of writing and printing for the blind, in which combinations of raised dots represent letters, numerals, and punctuation
writing system for the blind using raised dots

Examples

She learned to read braille after losing her sight.
All elevator buttons in the building are labeled with braille.
Books written in braille help blind students access information.

Etymology

Named after Louis Braille (1809–1852), French inventor of the system.

2. VERB

/ ˈbreɪl / (AmE), / ˈbreɪl / (BrE)
to transcribe text using the braille system of raised dots for the visually impaired
transcribe using the braille system

Examples

The library will braille all essential documents for visually impaired students.
After the meeting, she offered to braille the report for her colleague.
They are planning to braille the menus so everyone can read them.

Etymology

From the name of Louis Braille (1809–1852), French inventor of the system.
