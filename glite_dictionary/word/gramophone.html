gramophone

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræməˌfoʊn / (AmE), / ˈɡræməfəʊn / (BrE)
an old-fashioned device for playing music recorded on discs, in which the sound is produced by a needle vibrating in a groove and amplified acoustically
antique record player

Examples

The old gramophone in the corner played nostalgic tunes from another era.
She carefully placed the record on the gramophone and lowered the needle.
Collectors often seek out vintage gramophones for their unique sound and historic value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phonograph - an early type of record player, similar to a gramophone

Etymology

From Greek 'gramma' (letter) + 'phone' (sound); trademarked by the Gramophone Company.
