complexion

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈplɛkʃən / (AmE), / kəmˈplɛkʃən / (BrE)
the natural color, texture, and overall appearance of the skin, especially of the face
appearance of the skin of the face

Examples

She has a beautiful complexion that rarely breaks out.
After spending the summer outside, his complexion became much darker.
The moisturizer improved the texture of her complexion over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skin - the thin layer of tissue covering the body

Etymology

From Latin complexio (combination), later sense developed to mean physical appearance.

2. NOUN

/ kəmˈplɛkʃən / (AmE), / kəmˈplɛkʃən / (BrE)
the natural color, texture, and appearance of the skin on a person's face
the color and appearance of facial skin

Examples

She has a fair complexion that is easily sunburned in the summer.
Regular exercise and a healthy diet improved his complexion.
Her complexion was rosy after coming in from the cold.

Etymology

From Latin 'complexio' (combination), from 'complecti' (to entwine, embrace).

4. NOUN

/ kəmˈplɛkʃən / (AmE), / kəmˈplɛkʃən / (BrE)
(archaic) The combination of qualities, such as dryness and warmth or the four humors, once believed to determine a person's health and temperament.
archaic: mix of qualities determining health/temperament
archaic

Examples

In ancient medicine, a person's complexion was thought to influence their character.
He was believed to have a melancholic complexion, affecting both his mood and appearance.
Medieval physicians diagnosed illnesses based on an imbalance in one's complexion.

Etymology

From Latin 'complexio' (combination), from 'complecti' (to encircle, embrace).
