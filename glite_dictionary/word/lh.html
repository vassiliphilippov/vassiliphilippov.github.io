LH

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛl ˈeɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˌɛl ˈeɪtʃ / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates ovulation in females and the release of androgens in males
hormone stimulating ovulation or androgen release
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered blood tests to check her LH levels.
A surge in LH triggers ovulation during the menstrual cycle.
In males, LH stimulates the production of testosterone in the testes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luteinizing hormone - a hormone stimulating ovulation or androgen release

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'luteinizing hormone'.
