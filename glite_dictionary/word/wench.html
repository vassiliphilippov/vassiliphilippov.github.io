wench

1. NOUN

/ wɛntʃ / (AmE), / wɛntʃ / (BrE)
(dated, sometimes humorous or offensive) A young woman, especially one of low social status; formerly used as an informal or disparaging term.
young woman (dated, informal, possibly offensive)
archaic informal

Examples

In the tavern, the old sailor winked at the serving wench as she brought him ale.
She played the role of a charming wench in the Shakespearean festival.
The story describes a plucky street wench rising above her circumstances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: girl - a female child or young woman
  • Antonym: lad - a male youth or young man

Etymology

From Middle English wenche, shortened from wenchel, from Old English wencel (child).
