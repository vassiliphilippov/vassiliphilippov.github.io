Scientology

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪənˈtɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌsaɪənˈtɒlədʒi / (BrE)
A religion founded by L. Ron Hubbard in 1955, focusing on the power of a person's spirit to overcome past painful experiences through self-knowledge and spiritual fulfillment.
a religion founded by L. Ron Hubbard

Examples

Many celebrities have joined Scientology in search of personal growth.
The practices of Scientology have been subject to both criticism and praise over the years.
She attended several Scientology seminars to learn about its teachings.

Etymology

From science + -ology (the study of), coined by L. Ron Hubbard in the 20th century.
