italic

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈtælɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈtælɪk / (BrE)
a style of handwriting in which the letters slant to the right, often used for emphasis or distinction
handwriting with right-slanting letters

Examples

She practiced her italic until her slanted letters looked elegant.
The invitation was written entirely in italic, making it look formal.
Learning italic can improve the legibility of your handwriting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: italic - typeface with slanted letters for emphasis

Etymology

From Latin 'Italicus', referring to Italy, where the style originated during the Renaissance.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈtælɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈtælɪk / (BrE)
A style of typeface in which the letters slant to the right, often used for emphasis or distinction.
style of slanting typeface

Examples

Please print the book titles in italic for the bibliography.
The word was set in italic to emphasize its importance.
Scientific names are traditionally written in italic in academic articles.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: roman - upright style of typeface, as opposed to italic
  • Synonym: oblique - style of typeface similar to italic but sloped without script features

Etymology

From Latin 'Italicus', meaning 'of Italy'; originally referring to cursive handwriting styles used in Italy.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06840394-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ɪˈtælɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈtælɪk / (BrE)
(of type or lettering) having letters that slant to the right, used especially for emphasis or distinction.
slanting style of type or letters

Examples

All book titles in the list should be printed in italic type.
Her name appeared in italic letters for emphasis.
The software allows you to switch between italic and bold styles easily.

Synset Examples

italic characters

Related Terms

  • Antonym: roman - upright standard style of type as opposed to italic
  • Synonym: oblique - slanted typeface similar to italic but without the design features of true italic

Etymology

From Latin 'Italicus' (Italian), referencing the style first used in Italy.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
