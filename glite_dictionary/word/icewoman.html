icewoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪsˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈaɪsˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman whose job is to cut and deliver ice, typically in the period before widespread domestic refrigeration
woman who cuts and delivers ice

Examples

The icewoman made her rounds every morning before the sun grew too hot.
In those days, having an icewoman deliver blocks of ice was common in the city.
She worked as an icewoman during the summer, hauling heavy loads to homes and businesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iceman - a man who cuts and delivers ice

Etymology

Formed from 'ice' + 'woman', following the model of occupational terms like 'ice man' and 'postwoman'.
