sycophant

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪkəˌfænt / (AmE), / ˈsɪkəfənt / (BrE)
a person who acts obsequiously toward someone important in order to gain advantage; a servile flatterer
servile flatterer; self-seeking flatterer

Examples

He surrounded himself with sycophants who praised his every decision.
No one trusted the sycophant because his compliments were always self-serving.
Instead of offering honest feedback, she played the sycophant to win the boss's favor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flatterer - a person who praises others insincerely for personal gain
  • Synonym: toady - a person who behaves obsequiously to someone important
  • Antonym: critic - a person who expresses an unfavorable opinion

Etymology

From Latin 'sycophanta', from Greek 'sykophantes' (informer, trickster).
