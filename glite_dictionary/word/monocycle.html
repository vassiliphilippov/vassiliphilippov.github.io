monocycle

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.nəˌsaɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.əˌsaɪ.kəl / (BrE)
a type of cycle with a single wheel, typically powered by pedals and balanced by the rider
cycle with a single wheel

Examples

The circus performer amazed the crowd by riding a monocycle across a tightrope.
Learning to balance on a monocycle takes a lot of practice and patience.
He can ride both a monocycle and a traditional two-wheeled bicycle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unicycle - a vehicle with a single wheel and usually without handlebars
  • Degree Higher: bicycle - a vehicle with two wheels powered by pedals

Etymology

From mono- (meaning 'one') and cycle (from Greek 'kyklos', 'circle, wheel').
