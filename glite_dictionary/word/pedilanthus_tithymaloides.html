Pedilanthus tithymaloides

1. NOUN

/ ˌpiːdɪˈlænθəs ˌtɪθɪməˈlɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˌpiːdɪˈlænθəs ˌtɪθɪməˈlɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
a low tropical American shrub with powerful emetic properties, cultivated for its zigzag stems and bright bracts
a tropical American shrub with emetic properties

Examples

Pedilanthus tithymaloides is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique zigzag stems and colorful flowers.
Some traditional medicines utilize extracts from Pedilanthus tithymaloides for their emetic effects, though caution is advised.
Gardeners should be aware that Pedilanthus tithymaloides contains substances that can induce vomiting if ingested.

Etymology

From modern Latin; genus name from Greek 'pedilon' (slipper) and 'anthos' (flower), species from 'tithymalus' (spurge) and Latin '-oides' (resembling).
