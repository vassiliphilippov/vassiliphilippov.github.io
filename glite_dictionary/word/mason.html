mason

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ.sən / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.sən / (BrE)
a person whose job is to build or work with stone, brick, or similar materials
stone or brick worker

Examples

The skilled mason carefully repaired the cracks in the old stone wall.
She hired a mason to construct a fireplace in the living room.
Ancient cathedrals often required hundreds of masons to finish the intricate stonework.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bricklayer - a person who builds walls and other structures with bricks

Etymology

From Old French maçon, from Frankish or Latin origins; related to manual work.
