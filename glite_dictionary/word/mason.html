Mason

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪsən / (AmE), / ˈmeɪsən / (BrE)
A member of the secret fraternal organization known as the Freemasons, dedicated to mutual assistance, brotherhood, and moral uprightness.
member of the Freemasons organization

Examples

His grandfather was a well-respected Mason who participated in many local charitable projects.
During the ceremony, several Masons wore symbolic aprons and regalia.
Many rumors surround the activities of the Mason lodge in town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Freemason - a member of the Freemasons fraternal order

Etymology

Derived from the earlier use of 'mason' (a builder in stone); the fraternal order traces its symbolism to medieval stonemasons' guilds.
