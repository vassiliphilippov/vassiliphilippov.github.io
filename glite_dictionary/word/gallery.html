gallery

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæləri / (AmE), / ˈɡæləri / (BrE)
A covered corridor, often along the side of a building, supported by arches or columns.
covered corridor with arches or columns

Examples

The palace features a beautiful gallery overlooking the gardens.
The visitors strolled through the marble gallery lined with ancient statues.
Each gallery provided a shaded passageway along the length of the monastery.

Etymology

From Old French galerie, from Medieval Latin galeria (gallery, porch).
