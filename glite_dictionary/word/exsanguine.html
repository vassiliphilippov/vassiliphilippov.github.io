exsanguine

1. ADJ

/ ɛkˈsæŋɡwɪn / (AmE), / ɛkˈsaŋɡwɪn / (BrE)
Lacking blood or apparently bloodless.
lacking blood
archaic

Examples

The vampire left his victim exsanguine on the cold stone floor.
Under the harsh lights, her face looked utterly exsanguine and drained of color.
The artist depicted the ghost as an exsanguine figure drifting through the mist.

Synset Examples

the bloodless carcass of my Hector sold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bloodless - without blood; pale or lacking vitality

Etymology

From Latin 'exsanguis' (bloodless, without blood), from 'ex-' (out of) + 'sanguis' (blood).
