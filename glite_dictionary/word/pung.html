pung

1. NOUN

/ pʌŋ / (AmE), / pʌŋ / (BrE)
a light one-horse sleigh consisting of a box mounted on runners, traditionally used in snowy regions of North America.
a box-shaped one-horse sleigh

Examples

The farmer used his pung to transport firewood through the snowy fields.
Children enjoyed riding in the old pung during the winter festival.
A restored pung was displayed at the local history museum.

Etymology

Possibly from Algonquian (e.g., Micmac), related to words for sled.
