TCP/IP

1. NOUN

/ ti si pi aɪ pi / (AmE), / tiː siː piː aɪ piː / (BrE)
a set of communication protocols used for interconnecting network devices on the internet, including the Transmission Control Protocol (TCP) and the Internet Protocol (IP)
set of protocols for internet communication

Examples

Many websites rely on TCP/IP to ensure their data reaches users securely and reliably.
Understanding TCP/IP is essential for anyone pursuing a career in computer networking.
The development of TCP/IP revolutionized how computers communicate over long distances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protocol - a set of rules governing the exchange of data between devices

Etymology

Acronym for Transmission Control Protocol/Internet Protocol.
