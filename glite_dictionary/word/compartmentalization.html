compartmentalization

1. NOUN

/ kəmˌpɑːrtˌmɛntələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / kəmˌpɑːtmɛntəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of dividing something into separate sections or categories, especially to organize or isolate parts
the act of dividing into sections or categories

Examples

The compartmentalization of tasks allowed the team to work more efficiently on the project.
His extreme compartmentalization of personal and professional life surprised his colleagues.
Modern biology often involves the compartmentalization of functions within different organelles of a cell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classification - the act of arranging into classes or categories

Etymology

From 'compartmentalize' + '-ation'; ultimately from French compartiment (compartment) and Latin compartiri (to divide).

2. NOUN

/ kəmˌpɑrtˌmɛntələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / kəmˌpɑːtˌmentəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a mild or unconscious psychological process in which conflicting thoughts, emotions, or memories are separated into distinct categories to reduce anxiety or discomfort
division of mental states into separate compartments

Examples

Psychologists often discuss compartmentalization as a coping mechanism for dealing with stressful experiences.
Through compartmentalization, she was able to keep her work and personal lives distinct from each other.
Excessive compartmentalization may lead to difficulties in processing conflicting emotions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dissociation - the process of disconnecting or separating mental processes from each other

Etymology

From 'compartment' (Middle French) + '-alization', formed after ‘compartmentalize’.
