sclerotic

1. ADJ

/ skləˈrɑːtɪk / (AmE), / skləˈrɒtɪk / (BrE)
of or relating to the sclera, the tough outer layer of the eyeball.
relating to the sclera of the eye
medical

Examples

The surgeon needed to repair the sclerotic tissue damaged during the accident.
An infection in the sclerotic coat can lead to pain and vision disturbances.
Researchers are studying the properties of sclerotic fibers in various animals.

Synset Examples

sclerotic tissue

Etymology

From Greek 'sklērōtikos', from 'sklērós' (hard), referring to the hard outer layer of the eyeball.

2. ADJ

/ skləˈrɑːtɪk / (AmE), / skləˈrɒtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or affected by sclerosis; abnormally hardened, especially of body tissues or organs.
relating to abnormal hardening (sclerosis)
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the patient suffered from sclerotic blood vessels, which restricted blood flow.
Regulators criticized the sclerotic financial system for its inability to adapt to modern challenges.
The disease can cause sclerotic changes in the skin, leading to stiffness and discomfort.

Synset Examples

a sclerotic patient

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hardened - made or become hard or firm
  • Antonym: softened - made or become less hard, more soft

Etymology

From Greek 'sklērōtikos' (hardening), via Late Latin 'scleroticus'.
