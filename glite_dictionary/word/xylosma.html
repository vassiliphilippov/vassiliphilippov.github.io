Xylosma

1. NOUN

/ zaɪˈlɒzmə / (AmE), / zaɪˈlɒzmə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American and Asiatic spiny evergreen trees and shrubs in the family Salicaceae.
genus of tropical evergreen trees and shrubs

Examples

The Xylosma genus includes a variety of ornamental shrubs prized for their glossy leaves.
Several species of Xylosma are cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens around the world.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the Xylosma genus in southeast Asia.

Etymology

From Greek 'xylon' (wood) and 'osma' (smell), referring to the scent of the wood in some species.
