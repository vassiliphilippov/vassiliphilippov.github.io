outcaste

1. ADJ

/ ˈaʊtˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌkɑːst / (BrE)
Not belonging to or having been expelled from a caste, and thus having no place or status in society.
excluded from a caste; socially marginalized

Examples

The new law was intended to assist outcaste communities that faced discrimination.
He lived much of his life as an outcaste after being expelled from his village.
The novel explores the struggles of an outcaste protagonist searching for acceptance.

Synset Examples

the foreigner was a casteless person

Related Terms

  • Synonym: casteless - not belonging to any caste
  • Synonym: pariah - a social outcast; one who is despised or avoided

Etymology

From 'out-' + 'caste', modeled on 'caste' (from Portuguese 'casta', lineage, race).

2. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌkɑːst / (BrE)
A person who does not belong to any caste, especially one who has been excluded from the traditional caste system, often facing social ostracism.
person outside the caste system

Examples

He was treated as an outcaste after breaking the village traditions.
Throughout history, outcastes have often been forced to live on the outskirts of society.
Being labeled an outcaste brought immense hardship to her and her family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pariah - an outcast; someone despised or avoided by others

Etymology

Formed from 'out-' (outside) + 'caste', from Portuguese 'casta' (race, lineage).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10406214-n
Synset POS: n
