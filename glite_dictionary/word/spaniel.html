spaniel

1. NOUN

/ ˈspæniəl / (AmE), / ˈspæn.i.əl / (BrE)
a dog of a breed with a long silky coat and long, drooping ears, originally bred as a gun dog for flushing out game
a breed of dog with long ears and silky coat

Examples

The family adopted a spaniel because of its gentle temperament and playful nature.
Her spaniel loves running through the fields during their morning walks.
You can easily recognize a spaniel by its long, silky ears and expressive eyes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retriever - a breed of gun dog used for retrieving game

Etymology

From Old French *espaigneul* (Spanish dog), from Latin *Hispaniolus*, meaning 'Spanish.'
