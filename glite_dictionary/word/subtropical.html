subtropical

1. ADJ

/ ˌsʌbˈtrɑpɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˈtrɒpɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the geographic regions immediately north or south of the tropics, typically with hot, humid summers and mild winters
relating to the subtropics

Examples

The region's subtropical climate makes it a popular destination for tourists year-round.
Many plants native to subtropical areas require warm temperatures to thrive.
Winters in subtropical zones are generally mild and rarely experience frost.

Synset Examples

even near the equator vegetation at 5000 ft is subtropical rather than tropical

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tropical - relating to or characteristic of the tropics
  • Degree Lower: temperate - relating to regions with moderate climate between the tropics and polar regions

Etymology

Formed from sub- (below) and tropical (from Greek *tropikos*, pertaining to a turn), referring to regions bordering the tropics.
