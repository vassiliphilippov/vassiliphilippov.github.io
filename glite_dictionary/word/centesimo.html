centesimo

1. NOUN

/ tʃɛnˈteɪsɪˌmoʊ / (AmE), / tʃɛnˈteːsɪməʊ / (BrE)
A fractional monetary unit equal to one hundredth of the main currency in several countries, such as Panama, Italy (formerly), Uruguay, and Chile.
fractional monetary unit in some countries

Examples

The coin is worth one centesimo and is no longer in circulation.
Tourists found a few centesimo coins left in their wallets after visiting Panama.
Until 2002, the Italian lira was divided into one hundred centesimo units.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cent - a fractional monetary unit equal to one hundredth of the basic unit

Etymology

From Italian 'centesimo' (hundredth), from Latin 'centesimus' (hundredth).
