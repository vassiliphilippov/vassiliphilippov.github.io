amygdalotomy

1. NOUN

/ əˌmɪɡdəˈlɑːtəmi / (AmE), / əˌmɪɡdəˈlɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which fibers of the amygdala are severed to reduce symptoms such as extreme uncontrollable violence
surgical severing of amygdala fibers
medical

Examples

The patient underwent an amygdalotomy after other treatments failed to control his violent behavior.
Researchers discussed the ethical implications of performing amygdalotomy on individuals with severe aggression.
Due to its risks, amygdalotomy is rarely performed in modern neurosurgery.

Etymology

From Greek amygdalē (almond, amygdala) + -tomy (cutting).
