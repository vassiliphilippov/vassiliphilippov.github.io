reflation

1. NOUN

/ riˈfleɪʃən / (AmE), / riˈfleɪʃən / (BrE)
An act or policy of increasing the supply of money or credit in an economy, especially after a period of deflation, to restore the previous price level or stimulate activity.
restoring price levels after deflation

Examples

The government implemented reflation policies to counteract the effects of the recent recession.
Economists debated whether reflation would lead to sustainable growth or trigger uncontrollable inflation.
Following years of falling prices, reflation was necessary to restore consumer confidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inflation - a general increase in prices and fall in the purchasing value of money
  • Antonym: deflation - a general decrease in prices, often linked to reduced spending

Etymology

From re- (again) + inflation, modeled after 'inflation'.
