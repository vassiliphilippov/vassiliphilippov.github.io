cubicle

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːbɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈkjuːbɪkəl / (BrE)
A small enclosed space or compartment, especially one in which a monk or nun lives.
small enclosed space or compartment

Examples

Each monk was assigned a cubicle with only a bed and a desk.
The monastery's ancient cubicles were barely large enough for one person.
She enjoyed the solitude of her cubicle after a long day of prayer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cell - a small room in which a prisoner or monk lives

Etymology

From Latin cubiculum (bedroom, sleeping chamber).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːbɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈkjuːbɪkl̩ / (BrE)
a small enclosed area in a room, separated by walls or partitions for a particular purpose
small enclosed area in a room

Examples

She worked in a cubicle surrounded by stacks of paperwork.
Each employee has their own cubicle in the large office.
I changed into my swimsuit in the changing cubicle at the pool.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: booth - a small, enclosed space for one person
  • Antonym: open-plan - an area without divided cubicles or rooms

Etymology

From Latin 'cubiculum' (bedroom, sleeping compartment).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːbɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈkjuːbɪk(ə)l / (BrE)
a small partitioned space, typically for individual work or study, such as in an office or library
small partitioned workspace or study area

Examples

She sat in her cubicle at the library, reading quietly for hours.
He reserved a cubicle to study for his exams in peace.
Every cubicle was occupied during finals week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: booth - a small enclosed space for one person
  • Synonym: carrel - a small individual study space, especially in a library

Etymology

From Latin cubiculum (bedroom), from cubare (to lie down).

Synset POS: n
