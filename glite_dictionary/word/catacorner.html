catacorner

1. ADJ

/ ˈkætəˌkɔrnɚ / (AmE), / ˈkætəkɔːnə / (BrE)
Situated or moving on a diagonal line; diagonally opposite or across from something.
diagonally opposite or across
informal american

Examples

The bakery is catacorner from the post office, right across the intersection.
They built their house catacorner to the playground so the kids could see it from their window.
The two restaurants are located catacorner from each other at the crossroads.

Synset Examples

set off in a catty-corner direction across the vacant lot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diagonal - (of a line or direction) joining two opposite corners, slanting across

Etymology

Alteration of 'cater-corner', from Middle English 'cater' meaning 'four' (from Old French 'quatre') + 'corner'.
