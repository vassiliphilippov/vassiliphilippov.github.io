smidgen

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmɪdʒən / (AmE), / ˈsmɪdʒɪn / (BrE)
A very small amount; a tiny or scarcely detectable quantity.
a tiny amount

Examples

Add just a smidgen of salt to enhance the flavor.
There wasn't even a smidgen of evidence to support his theory.
She only gave me a smidgen of her time before she hurried off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bit - a small amount
  • Synonym: trace - a very slight amount
  • Synonym: dash - a small quantity, especially of seasoning

Etymology

Probably a variant of 'smidgeon', of unknown origin, possibly Scots.
