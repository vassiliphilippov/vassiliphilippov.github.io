vestibule

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛstɪˌbjuːl / (AmE), / ˈvɛstɪbjuːl / (BrE)
a large entrance hall or lobby next to the outer door of a building, often serving as a waiting area or reception room
entrance hall or lobby

Examples

The hotel's vestibule was elegantly decorated with marble floors and chandeliers.
Please wait for me in the vestibule before the meeting starts.
A cold draft swept through the vestibule each time the front doors opened.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foyer - an entrance hall or lobby, especially in a theater or hotel
  • Synonym: lobby - a large entrance or reception area inside a building

Etymology

From Latin 'vestibulum' (entrance court), referring to the entrance of a house.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɛstəˌbjuːl / (AmE), / ˈvɛstɪbjuːl / (BrE)
a bodily cavity that serves as an entrance to another cavity, especially in anatomical structures such as the ear or vagina
bodily entrance cavity (anatomy)
medical

Examples

The vestibule of the ear helps transmit sound from the outer to the inner structures.
Inflammation was found in the vestibule of the patient's nose during examination.
Surgeons examined the vaginal vestibule for any abnormal growths.

Etymology

From Latin 'vestibulum' (entrance, forecourt).
