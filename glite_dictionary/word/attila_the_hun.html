Attila the Hun

1. PROPN

/ əˈtɪlə ðə hʌn / (AmE), / əˈtɪlə ðə hʌn / (BrE)
A king of the Huns (circa 406–453), famed as the most successful barbarian invader of the Roman Empire.
king of the Huns, invader of Rome

Examples

Attila the Hun was feared throughout Europe for his relentless campaigns against the Roman Empire.
Historians often debate the strategies that made Attila the Hun such a formidable ruler.
Stories about Attila the Hun have become legendary in both Eastern and Western history.

Etymology

From the name Attila, possibly of Gothic origin meaning 'little father', combined with 'the Hun' to specify his leadership of the Huns.
