toff

1. NOUN

/ tɑf / (AmE), / tɒf / (BrE)
an informal term for a person from the upper classes or who is wealthy and has a high social position, often used in a slightly mocking or derogatory way
an upper-class or wealthy person (informal)
informal british

Examples

The wealthy toff strolled confidently into the exclusive club.
He was dressed like a toff, with a top hat and cane, drawing everyone's attention.
The newspaper article criticized the government as being run by disconnected toffs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snob - a person who believes they are superior in taste or status
