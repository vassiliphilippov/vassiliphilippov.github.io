equestrian

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈkwɛstriən / (AmE), / ɪˈkwɛstriən / (BrE)
a person who rides horses, especially one skilled in horse riding
person skilled in horse riding

Examples

As a skilled equestrian, she competed in international show jumping events.
The young equestrian practiced daily to improve his performance.
The rodeo attracted both amateur and professional equestrians from across the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horseman - a person who rides horses, especially skillfully
  • Antonym: novice - someone without much experience (opposite of skilled equestrian)

Etymology

From Latin 'eques' (horseman) + '-ian'.

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈkwɛstriən / (AmE), / ɪˈkwɛs.tri.ən / (BrE)
connected with horse riding or horseback riders.
relating to horse riding

Examples

The city hosts an annual equestrian competition attended by riders from across the country.
She admired the equestrian statue of the famous general in the square.
His equestrian skills impressed everyone at the riding school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horseriding - the activity or sport of riding horses

Etymology

From Latin 'equestrianus', from 'eques' (horseman), from 'equus' (horse).
