Platycerium alcicorne

1. NOUN

/ ˌplætɪˈsɪəriəm ˌælsɪˈkɔrn / (AmE), / ˌplatɪˈsɪəriəm ˌalsɪˈkɔːn / (BrE)
A species of staghorn fern native to Australia, southeastern Asia, and Polynesia, often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
a species of staghorn fern

Examples

The Platycerium alcicorne is often displayed mounted on wood in botanical gardens.
Many plant enthusiasts grow Platycerium alcicorne indoors because of its unique, decorative fronds.
Native to Australia and Southeast Asia, Platycerium alcicorne thrives in warm, humid climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: staghorn fern - fern species in the genus Platycerium characterized by antler-shaped fronds

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name Platycerium (from Greek platys 'broad' + keras 'horn') and species name alcicorne (from Latin alces 'elk' + cornu 'horn').
