notorious

1. ADJ

/ noʊˈtɔːriəs / (AmE), / nəˈtɔːriəs / (BrE)
famous or well known for something bad or negative; having a bad reputation
famous for something bad

Examples

The city became notorious for its high crime rate in the 1990s.
He is a notorious con artist who deceived hundreds of people.
The restaurant is notorious for its terrible service and rude staff.

Synset Examples

a notorious gangster
the tenderloin district was notorious for vice
the infamous Benedict Arnold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infamous - famous for being bad or wicked
  • Antonym: renowned - known or talked about by many people, especially for something good

Etymology

From Latin notorius (well-known), from notus (known).
