autarchy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːˌtɑːrki / (AmE), / ˈɔːˌtɑːki / (BrE)
A policy or condition of national economic self-sufficiency and independence from external trade or aid.
national economic self-sufficiency

Examples

The country adopted a policy of autarchy to minimize its reliance on foreign goods.
In wartime, autarchy can help a nation survive international blockades.
Many economists debate the practicality of autarchy in a globalized world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autarky - economic self-sufficiency, especially as a national policy

Etymology

From Greek autarkhia, from autarkēs (self-sufficiency), from autos (self) + arkhein (to rule).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔː.tɑɹ.ki / (AmE), / ˈɔː.tɑː.ki / (BrE)
A political system in which one individual holds absolute power; rule by a single person.
rule by a single individual

Examples

Under the autarchy, citizens had no say in political decisions.
The novel depicts life under an oppressive autarchy.
Historians debated the rise and fall of several autarchies throughout history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autocracy - government by one person with absolute power
  • Antonym: democracy - government by the people or their elected representatives

Etymology

From Greek autarkhia (absolute rule), from autos (self) + arkhein (to rule).
