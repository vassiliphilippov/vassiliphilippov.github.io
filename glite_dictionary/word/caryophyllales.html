Caryophyllales

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɛˌrioʊfəˈleɪliz / (AmE), / ˌkærɪˌɒfɪˈleɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of flowering plants that includes families such as cacti, carnations, and amaranths, formerly known as Centrospermae.
order of flowering plants
medical

Examples

The botanical garden has a remarkable collection of Caryophyllales, including rare species of cacti and carnations.
Research on Caryophyllales has provided new insights into plant evolution.
Many edible plants, such as quinoa and beets, are members of the Caryophyllales order.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Caryophyllus (the carnation) + -ales (order suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɛri.oʊfaɪˈleɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌkærɪ.ɒfɪˈleɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of flowering plants that includes carnations, cacti, amaranths, and related families, corresponding roughly to the older group Centrospermae
order of flowering plants including carnations and cacti

Examples

The family of cacti belongs to the Caryophyllales order.
Botanists recently revised the classification within Caryophyllales based on genetic evidence.
Many ornamental flowers are found within the Caryophyllales group.

Etymology

From the genus Caryophyllus (the type genus, now Dianthus) + Modern Latin -ales (order suffix).
