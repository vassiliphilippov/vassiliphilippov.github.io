bleacher

1. NOUN

/ ˈbliːtʃər / (AmE), / ˈbliːtʃə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to whiten cloth, flour, or other materials by bleaching them
worker who whitens materials by bleaching

Examples

The textile factory employed a skilled bleacher to ensure the fabric was perfectly white.
In the past, a bleacher would use sunlight and chemicals to whiten linens.
The old mill was once famous for its expert bleacher who supplied bright cloth to local markets.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dyer - a person who dyes cloth or other materials

Etymology

From 'bleach' + '-er', denoting a person who bleaches.
