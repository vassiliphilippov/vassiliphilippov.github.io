effectual

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈfɛktʃuəl / (AmE), / ɪˈfɛktʃuəl / (BrE)
having legal force; valid or effective, especially in producing an intended result or upholding a right
having legal force or effect
legal

Examples

The contract was only effectual after both parties signed it.
An effectual deed is required to transfer ownership of the property legally.
The court ruled that the agreement was not effectual due to missing signatures.

Synset Examples

a sound title to the property

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valid - having a sound basis in logic or fact; legally acceptable

Etymology

From Late Latin effectualis (effective), from Latin effectus (result, effect).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈfɛktʃuəl / (AmE), / ɪˈfɛktʃuəl / (BrE)
producing the intended result; effective in bringing about a desired outcome
producing the intended result

Examples

Her efforts were effectual in bringing about changes to the policy.
Only an effectual remedy will solve the problem once and for all.
He wrote an effectual letter that finally got the issue addressed.

Synset Examples

an air-cooled motor was more effective than a witch's broomstick for rapid long-distance transportation
effective teaching methods
effective steps toward peace
made an effective entrance
his complaint proved to be effectual in bringing action
an efficacious law

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effective - producing a desired effect or result
  • Antonym: ineffectual - not producing the desired result; ineffective

Etymology

From Latin effectualis, from effectus (accomplishment).
