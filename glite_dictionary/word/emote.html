to emote

1. VERB

/ ɪˈmoʊt / (AmE), / ɪˈməʊt / (BrE)
(of an actor) to express emotion, especially in an exaggerated way.
express emotion (esp. as an actor)

Examples

The actor tended to emote excessively during dramatic scenes, distracting from the realism of his performance.
Directors often remind inexperienced performers not to emote too much during subtle moments.
She learned to emote more naturally on stage, conveying genuine feeling to the audience.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to underact - to act with less emotion than appropriate or expected
  • Synonym: to overact - to exaggerate one's acting, especially emotion

Etymology

From Latin 'emotus', past participle of 'emovere' (to move out or away).
