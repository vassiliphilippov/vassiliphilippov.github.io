bilingualism

1. NOUN

/ baɪˈlɪŋɡwəlɪzəm / (AmE), / baɪˈlɪŋɡwɪlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The ability to speak and understand two languages fluently.
ability to speak two languages

Examples

Many countries encourage bilingualism in schools to give students more job opportunities.
Her bilingualism makes her a valuable asset in an international company.
Research has shown that bilingualism can benefit cognitive development.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: multilingualism - the ability to speak and understand multiple languages
  • Degree Lower: monolingualism - the condition of knowing or using only one language

Etymology

From French 'bilinguisme,' from Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'lingua' (tongue, language).
