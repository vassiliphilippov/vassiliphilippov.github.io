seasonably

1. ADV

/ ˈsiːzənəbli / (AmE), / ˈsiːzənəbli / (BrE)
at an appropriate or opportune time; suitably for the occasion
at an appropriate time

Examples

We arrived seasonably for the meeting and caught all the opening remarks.
The report was submitted seasonably, allowing the board to make a decision promptly.
Her advice was offered seasonably and made all the difference in our planning.

Synset Examples

your letter arrived apropos

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timely - occurring at a suitable or opportune moment
  • Antonym: untimely - occurring at an inappropriate or inconvenient time

Etymology

From Latin 'sationabilis' (of sowing) via Old French 'sesonable' (timely); linked to 'season'.

2. ADV

/ ˈsiːzənəbli / (AmE), / ˈsiːzənəbli / (BrE)
in a way that is appropriate for or typical of the season; as expected for the time of year
appropriately for the season

Examples

The weather was seasonably warm for late spring, making it perfect for an outdoor picnic.
Apples are seasonably available in the fall, when they are harvested fresh from orchards.
The chef changes the menu seasonably to incorporate the freshest ingredients each month.

Synset Examples

it was seasonably cold

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unseasonably - not typical for the season; out of season

Etymology

From 'seasonable' + '-ly', ultimately from Old French 'saison' (season).
