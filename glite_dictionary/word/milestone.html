milestone

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪlstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈmaɪlstəʊn / (BrE)
an important event or stage in the development of something, especially a person's life or a project
important event or stage

Examples

Graduating from college was a major milestone in her life.
The company reached an important milestone when it launched its first product.
Buying their first house was a significant milestone for the young couple.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: landmark - an important event or turning point

Etymology

Originally referred to a stone set up beside a road to mark distances in miles.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪlstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈmaɪlstəʊn / (BrE)
a stone marker placed alongside a road indicating the distance to a particular place
stone distance marker by a road

Examples

The old milestone at the crossroads marks five miles to the next town.
Many milestones from the Roman period can still be found along ancient roads.
A weathered milestone stood hidden among the overgrown grass beside the highway.

Etymology

From mile + stone, literally 'stone showing miles' along a road.

3. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪlstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈmaɪlstəʊn / (BrE)
an important event or significant achievement in a person's life, development, or history
significant achievement or event

Examples

Graduating from university was a major milestone in his life.
The launch of their new product marked an important milestone for the company.
Learning to walk is a developmental milestone for babies.

Synset Examples

She achieved a big milestone in her career.
Completing the project on time was a huge milestone.
Their first anniversary was a major milestone in their relationship.
Graduating from college was a significant milestone for her.
Being old enough to vote is a significant milestone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: landmark - an event or development marking a turning point or significant stage
  • Antonym: setback - an event that delays progress

Etymology

Originally referred to actual stone markers indicating miles, now used figuratively for important stages or achievements.
