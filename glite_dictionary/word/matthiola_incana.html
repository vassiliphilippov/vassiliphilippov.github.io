Matthiola incana

1. NOUN

/ məˈθaɪ.ələ ɪnˈkeɪ.nə / (AmE), / məˈθiː.ələ ɪŋˈkɑː.nə / (BrE)
A European plant with spikes of sweet-scented flowers, widely cultivated as an ornamental; also known as stock.
ornamental plant with fragrant flowers

Examples

Matthiola incana is popular in gardens for its colorful and fragrant blooms.
Florists often use Matthiola incana in bouquets for its pleasant scent and attractive appearance.
The botanical garden featured a vibrant display of Matthiola incana along the walkways.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stock - common name for Matthiola incana, an ornamental flower

Etymology

Named after Italian physician and botanist Pietro Andrea Mattioli (1501–1577); 'incana' is Latin for 'grayish'.
