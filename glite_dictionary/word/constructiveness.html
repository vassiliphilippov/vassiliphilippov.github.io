constructiveness

1. NOUN

/ kənˈstrʌktɪvnəs / (AmE), / kənˈstrʌktɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being useful for building up or improving something, especially by helping to develop or resolve issues positively
quality of being helpful or improving

Examples

Her constructiveness during the team's discussions helped everyone move forward productively.
The constructiveness of his feedback allowed me to make real improvements in my work.
We value constructiveness over criticism in our organization, encouraging solutions rather than blame.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constructive - serving to build up or improve
  • Antonym: destructiveness - the quality of causing damage or harm

Etymology

From 'constructive', based on Latin 'constructus' (built) + '-ness' (forming nouns of quality).
