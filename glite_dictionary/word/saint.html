saint

1. NOUN

/ seɪnt / (AmE), / seɪnt / (BrE)
A person who is recognized for being exceptionally holy, virtuous, or devoted, often canonized by the Christian Church.
person recognized for exceptional holiness

Examples

Mother Teresa was often called a saint for her charitable work with the poor.
After his death, he was declared a saint by the Church for his compassion and faith.
Many people look up to her as a modern-day saint because of her selflessness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sinner - a person who transgresses divine law by committing sin

Etymology

From Latin 'sanctus' meaning 'holy'.

2. VERB

/ seɪnt / (AmE), / seɪnt / (BrE)
to officially declare (a dead person) to be a saint, especially by canonization in the Christian Church
declare someone a saint

Examples

The Church decided to saint her after investigating her miracles.
He was sainted centuries after his death by the Vatican.
It took years of debate before the cleric was finally sainted.

Synset Examples

After he was shown to have performed a miracle, the priest was canonized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to canonize - officially declare someone a saint

Etymology

From Old French 'seint', from Latin 'sanctus' (holy, consecrated).

3. NOUN

/ seɪnt / (AmE), / seɪnt / (BrE)
A person who has died and has been formally declared a saint by canonization, especially in the Christian Church.
person declared a saint by canonization

Examples

After years of devotion and miracles, she was officially recognized as a saint by the Church.
The city was named after a famous saint who lived there centuries ago.
Many believers pray to a saint for guidance and protection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: martyr - someone who is killed because of their religious or other beliefs

Etymology

From Latin sanctus (holy, consecrated).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10566702-n
Synset POS: n
