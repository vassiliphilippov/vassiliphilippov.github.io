futurity

1. NOUN

/ fjuːˈtʊrəti / (AmE), / fjʊˈtjʊərɪti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being in the future; future time or existence
quality of being in the future

Examples

The author speculated about the futurity of humanity in her latest book.
He invested in stocks with a view to the futurity of his savings.
Philosophers have long debated the concept of futurity and its impact on human choices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: future - the time yet to come; the period after the present

Etymology

From Latin 'futuritas', from 'futurus' meaning 'about to be'.

2. NOUN

/ fjuˈtʃʊrəti / (AmE), / fjʊˈtjʊərɪti / (BrE)
the time that is still to come; the future
the time yet to come

Examples

Philosophers often speculate about the nature of futurity and what it holds for humanity.
The contract contains clauses that address events in futurity rather than present circumstances.
Her hopes were firmly tied to futurity, believing that better times would soon arrive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: future - the time that will come after the present
  • Antonym: past - the time before the present

Etymology

From Latin 'futuritas', from 'futurus' (about to be, future).
