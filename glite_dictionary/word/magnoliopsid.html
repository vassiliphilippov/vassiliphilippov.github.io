magnoliopsid

1. NOUN

/ mægˌnoʊliˈɑːpsɪd / (AmE), / mæɡˌnəʊlɪˈɒpsɪd / (BrE)
A flowering plant of the group characterized by having two cotyledons (seed leaves) and stems that grow by depositing new tissue on the outside; a dicotyledon.
flowering plant with two cotyledons
medical

Examples

The oak tree is a classic example of a magnoliopsid found in temperate forests.
Most garden vegetables, like beans and tomatoes, are classified as magnoliopsids.
Researchers discovered a new species of magnoliopsid thriving in the tropical rainforest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dicotyledon - a flowering plant with two cotyledons

Etymology

From modern Latin *Magnoliopsida*, from *Magnolia* (a genus name) + Greek *-opsis* (appearance) + *-id* (forming nouns).
