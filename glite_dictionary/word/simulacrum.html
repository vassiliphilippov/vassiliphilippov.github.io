simulacrum

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪmjəˈleɪkrəm / (AmE), / ˌsɪmjʊˈleɪkrəm / (BrE)
An image or representation of someone or something, especially a likeness of a person, often in sculpture or art.
representation or image, often in art

Examples

The museum displayed a simulacrum of the ancient queen carved out of marble.
An artist created a lifelike simulacrum of the writer for the anniversary exhibition.
The bronze simulacrum stood in the public square, honoring the city's founder.

Synset Examples

the coin bears an effigy of Lincoln
the emperor's tomb had his image carved in stone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effigy - a sculpture or model of a person
  • Synonym: image - a representation of the external form of a person or thing

Etymology

From Latin simulacrum (likeness, image, figure).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɪməˈleɪkrəm / (AmE), / ˌsɪmjʊˈleɪkrəm / (BrE)
something that looks like or represents something else, but is often an insubstantial, vague, or superficial likeness
insubstantial likeness or imitation

Examples

The statue was only a simulacrum of the great leader, capturing little of his true spirit.
In the digital age, we often mistake a simulacrum of reality for reality itself.
Her smile was a mere simulacrum of happiness, masking her true feelings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: semblance - the outward appearance or apparent form of something
  • Synonym: representation - the action of depicting or portraying something
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

From Latin simulacrum (likeness, image), from simulare (to make like, imitate).
