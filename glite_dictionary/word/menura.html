Menura

1. NOUN

/ məˈnʊrə / (AmE), / mɛˈnjʊərə / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Menuridae, consisting of the lyrebirds native to Australia.
genus of lyrebirds

Examples

The Menura genus includes the superb lyrebird, famed for its remarkable tail feathers.
Researchers observed a rare display by a Menura species in the Australian forest.
The intricate calls of Menura have made these birds subjects of many studies on avian mimicry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lyrebird - songbird of the genus Menura, famed for its tail and mimicry

Etymology

From Greek 'menoura' (moon-tailed), referring to the shape of the lyrebird's tail.

2. NOUN

/ məˈnjuːrə / (AmE), / mɪˈnjʊərə / (BrE)
a genus of birds comprising the lyrebirds, the sole genus of the family Menuridae
genus of lyrebirds

Examples

The genus Menura is best known for its remarkable lyrebirds native to Australia.
Researchers have studied the tail structures of birds in the Menura genus for their unique display.
The Menura comprises the only members of the family Menuridae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lyrebird - an Australian bird known for its tail and mimicking ability

Etymology

From Greek menoura, meaning 'moon-tailed', referring to the lyrebird's tail shape.
