Arava

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɑːvə / (AmE), / əˈrɑːvə / (BrE)
A trademarked drug (leflunomide) taken orally to treat rheumatoid arthritis by inhibiting the proliferation of white blood cells and reducing joint inflammation.
trade name for a rheumatoid arthritis drug
medical

Examples

After being diagnosed with rheumatoid arthritis, her doctor prescribed Arava to manage her symptoms.
Some patients taking Arava experience fewer flare-ups and improved joint mobility.
It is important to have regular liver function tests while on Arava.
