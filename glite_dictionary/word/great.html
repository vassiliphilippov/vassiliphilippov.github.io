great

1. ADJ

/ ɡreɪt / (AmE), / ɡreɪt / (BrE)
of major significance or importance; remarkable or outstanding in character, quality, or effect
of major significance or importance

Examples

She played a great role in the development of modern science.
The museum is home to several great works of art.
He is remembered as one of the great leaders of his generation.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: important - of great significance or value
  • Synonym: outstanding - remarkably good or excellent
  • Antonym: minor - of lesser or secondary importance

Etymology

From Old English grēat (big, coarse), of Germanic origin.
