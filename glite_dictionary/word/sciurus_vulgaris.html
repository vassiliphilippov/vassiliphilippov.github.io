Sciurus vulgaris

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈjʊərəs vʌlˈɡɛərɪs / (AmE), / saɪˈjʊərəs vʌlˈɡɑːrɪs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the Eurasian red squirrel, a common reddish-brown squirrel found in Europe and parts of Asia.
Eurasian red squirrel species

Examples

The Sciurus vulgaris is easily recognized by its bushy tail and tufted ears.
Unlike the grey squirrel, the Sciurus vulgaris prefers coniferous forests.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Sciurus vulgaris in Norway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red squirrel - Common name for Sciurus vulgaris, a species of reddish-brown squirrel

Etymology

From Latin: 'sciurus' (squirrel) and 'vulgaris' (common).
