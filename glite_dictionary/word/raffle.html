raffle

1. VERB

/ ˈræf.əl / (AmE), / ˈræf.əl / (BrE)
to offer or dispose of something as a prize in a lottery, typically by selling numbered tickets
dispose of by lottery

Examples

The school plans to raffle a bike to raise funds for charity.
They raffled off their old television at the community fair yesterday.
We are raffling dinner vouchers to support the local animal shelter.

Synset Examples

We raffled off a trip to the Bahamas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lottery - a means of raising money by selling numbered tickets and giving prizes by chance

Etymology

From French *rafle* (a sweep, a snatch), related to 'rifle' in the sense of seizing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈræfəl / (AmE), / ˈrɑːfəl / (BrE)
A competition in which numbered tickets are sold and prizes, usually goods, are given to the holders of numbers drawn at random.
lottery drawing for goods as prizes

Examples

The school held a raffle to raise funds for new equipment.
She was excited to win a bicycle in the charity raffle.
Each guest bought a ticket for the raffle at the community fair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lottery - a game of chance where winners are drawn randomly for prizes, often money

Etymology

Probably from French *rafle* (a kind of dice game, act of grabbing), from *rafler* (to sweep up).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00509683-n
Synset POS: n
