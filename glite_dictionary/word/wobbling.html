wobbling

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɑb.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɒb.lɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a sound) fluctuating unsteadily in pitch, volume, or tone.
fluctuating unsteadily (esp. of sound)

Examples

The engine made a strange wobbling noise as it struggled to start.
We listened to the distant, wobbling whine of an ambulance siren.
Her voice had a wobbling quality as she sang the uncertain notes.

Synset Examples

a low-pitched wobbling sound

Related Terms

  • Antonym: steady - not fluctuating; consistent in pitch or tone
