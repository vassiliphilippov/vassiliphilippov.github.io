Blackshirt

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌʃɜrt / (AmE), / ˈblakˌʃɜːt / (BrE)
a member of the Italian Fascist party, especially one who wore a black shirt as part of their uniform before World War II
member of Italian Fascist party (pre–WWII)

Examples

The Blackshirts played a significant role in Mussolini's rise to power in Italy.
Many Blackshirts marched in rallies to show their support for the Fascist regime.
The violence committed by the Blackshirts against political opponents was well-documented.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fascist - a member of a fascist political party, especially in Italy during Mussolini's rule
  • Synonym: Brownshirt - member of the Nazi stormtroopers in Germany, known for their brown uniforms

Etymology

From the black shirts worn as part of the uniform by Italian Fascists; modeled after the Italian 'camicie nere' (black shirts).
