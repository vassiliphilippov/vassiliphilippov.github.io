dilettante

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɪləˈtænti / (AmE), / ˌdɪlɪˈtænti / (BrE)
Having a superficial or amateurish interest in a subject, without real commitment or knowledge.
superficial; amateurish

Examples

Her dilettante approach to photography was obvious when she lost interest after a month.
The committee dismissed his report as the work of a dilettante rather than a serious scholar.
Despite his dilettante efforts, he never developed real skill in music.

Synset Examples

his dilettantish efforts at painting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amateur - someone engaging in a pursuit as a pastime, not a professional
  • Antonym: professional - someone skilled and experienced in a field

Etymology

From Italian 'dilettante', meaning 'lover of the arts', from 'dilettare' (to delight), from Latin 'delectare'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪləˈtænt / (AmE), / ˌdɪlɪˈtænti / (BrE)
a person who engages in an art or other activity without serious commitment or deep knowledge, often pretending to have expertise
amateur with pretended expertise

Examples

Though he played the piano, many considered him a dilettante rather than a true musician.
She criticized the dilettante historians who wrote books without real research.
John moved from hobby to hobby, always a dilettante and never a master.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amateur - a person who engages in a pursuit without being a professional
  • Antonym: expert - a person with a high degree of knowledge or skill

Etymology

From Italian 'dilettante' (one who takes delight), from Latin 'delectare' (to delight).

Synset POS: n
