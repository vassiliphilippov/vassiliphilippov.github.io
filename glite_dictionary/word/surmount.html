to surmount

1. VERB

/ sərˈmaʊnt / (AmE), / səˈmaʊnt / (BrE)
to deal successfully with a difficulty or obstacle; to overcome
overcome, deal with successfully

Examples

After months of hard work, she finally surmounted the language barrier.
The company surmounted several financial obstacles to reach profitability.
With determination, he surmounted the challenges of his new job.

Synset Examples

He overcame his shyness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overcome - succeed in dealing with a problem or difficulty
  • Synonym: to conquer - successfully overcome a problem or weakness
  • Antonym: to succumb - fail to resist pressure or temptation

Etymology

From Old French 'surmonter', from sur- (over) + monter (to mount)

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01111038-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ sɚˈmaʊnt / (AmE), / səˈmaʊnt / (BrE)
to reach the highest point of (something), especially after climbing or overcoming it
reach the highest point of

Examples

The climbers managed to surmount the steep north face before dawn.
After hours of hiking, we finally surmounted the ridge and took in the view.
It takes strength and determination to surmount such a formidable peak.

Synset Examples

We scaled the Mont Blanc

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scale - climb up to the top of
  • Antonym: to descend - go down from a higher to a lower place

Etymology

From Old French surmonter, from sur- (over) + monter (to mount), from Latin mons (mountain).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02003114-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ sərˈmaʊnt / (AmE), / səˈmaʊnt / (BrE)
to be set on top of something; to be placed above as a covering or ornament
to be placed on top of something

Examples

A tall spire surmounts the church tower, creating a striking silhouette against the sky.
The family crest surmounted the ornate gate at the entrance to the estate.
A golden eagle statue surmounts the pedestal in the city square.

Synset Examples

The scarf surmounted the gown

Related Terms

  • Synonym: top - to cover or be placed on the highest part of something

Etymology

From Old French surmonter, from sur- (above) + monter (to mount).

Source: WordNet
