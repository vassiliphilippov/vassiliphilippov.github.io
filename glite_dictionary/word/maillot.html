maillot

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪ.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.ɒt / (BrE)
A woman's one-piece swimsuit, especially in a style resembling a leotard.
one-piece women's swimsuit

Examples

She looked elegant in her red maillot by the pool.
The fashion magazine featured the latest designer maillots for summer.
Unlike bikinis, a maillot provides more coverage and support.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swimsuit - garment worn for swimming
  • Antonym: bikini - two-piece women's swimsuit

Etymology

From French 'maillot' (tight-fitting garment).

2. NOUN

/ maɪˈjoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.ɒt / (BrE)
A pair of tight-fitting stretch pants worn especially by dancers or gymnasts.
tights for dancers or gymnasts

Examples

The ballet dancer pulled on her maillot before rehearsal.
His maillot fit perfectly, allowing him to move freely during the routine.
The gymnast packed her maillot along with her other equipment for the competition.

Etymology

From French 'maillot' (knitwear, jersey).
