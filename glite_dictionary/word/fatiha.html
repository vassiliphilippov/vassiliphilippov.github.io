Fatiha

1. NOUN

/ fæˈtiːhə / (AmE), / fæˈtiːhə / (BrE)
The first chapter (sura) of the Quran, recited as the central prayer in Islam during daily prayers and special occasions.
opening sura of the Quran; central Islamic prayer

Examples

Before beginning the ceremony, the imam recited the Fatiha with the congregation.
Muslims around the world recite the Fatiha multiple times each day during prayer.
The Fatiha is often read at the start of important events in Islamic tradition.

Etymology

From Arabic fātiḥa(t) (opening).
