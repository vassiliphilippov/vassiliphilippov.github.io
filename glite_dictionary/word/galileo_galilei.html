Galileo Galilei

1. PROPN

/ ɡəˌliːlioʊ ɡæləˈleɪi / (AmE), / ɡaˌliːliəʊ ɡalɪˈleɪi / (BrE)
(1564–1642) Italian astronomer, physicist, and mathematician, known for pioneering use of the telescope in astronomy and major contributions to science.
Italian astronomer and scientist (1564–1642)

Examples

Galileo Galilei is often called the father of modern observational astronomy.
Many of Galileo Galilei's discoveries challenged the scientific beliefs of his time.
The telescope invented by Galileo Galilei revolutionized the study of the cosmos.

Etymology

From Italian, his given and family names.
