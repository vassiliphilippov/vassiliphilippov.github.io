laxity

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæk.sɪ.ti / (AmE), / ˈlæk.sɪ.ti / (BrE)
the state of being loose or relaxed, especially in a physical or physiological sense, such as muscles or bodily tissues
state of being physically loose
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that increased laxity in the ligaments can lead to joint instability.
Some digestive problems can result from laxity of the intestinal muscles.
Regular exercise can help reduce the laxity of weakened muscles over time.

Synset Examples

baths can help the laxness of the bowels

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tightness - the state of being tightly drawn, usually in a physiological sense

Etymology

From Latin laxitas (looseness), from laxus (loose).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlæk.sə.ti / (AmE), / ˈlæk.sɪ.ti / (BrE)
A lack of strictness or care; the quality of being careless and neglectful.
lack of strictness or care

Examples

The school was criticized for its laxity in enforcing safety protocols.
Under her leadership, laxity was replaced by discipline and attention to detail.
Regulatory laxity contributed to the financial crisis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strictness - the quality of being strict or precise
  • Synonym: negligence - failure to take proper care
  • Synonym: carelessness - lack of attention or care

Etymology

From Latin laxitas (looseness), from laxus (loose).
