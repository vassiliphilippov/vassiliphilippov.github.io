coordinating

1. ADJ

/ ˈkoʊ.ɔr.dnˌeɪ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / kəʊˈɔː.dɪ.neɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a conjunction or connective) connecting words, phrases, or clauses of equal grammatical importance or structure.
connecting equal grammatical elements

Examples

The word 'and' is a coordinating conjunction commonly used in English.
In the sentence 'You can have tea or coffee,' 'or' functions as a coordinating conjunction.
A coordinating clause can stand alone and is joined by connectors like 'but' or 'yet'.

Synset Examples

‘and’ in ‘John and Mary’ or in ‘John walked and Mary rode’ is a coordinating conjunction; and so is ‘or’ in ‘will you go or stay?’

Related Terms

  • Antonym: subordinating - (of a conjunction) joining a subordinate clause to a main clause
  • Synonym: coordinate - of equal importance or rank; also, to bring parts into proper relation

Etymology

From the verb 'coordinate', which derives from Latin 'coordinatus', meaning 'arranged in order'.
