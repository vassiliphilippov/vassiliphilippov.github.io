slyness

1. NOUN

/ ˈslaɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈslaɪ.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being clever at achieving one's aims by deceit or trickery
quality of being cunning or deceptive

Examples

The slyness in Tom's eyes betrayed his mischievous intentions.
Her slyness allowed her to get what she wanted without being detected.
He always approached business negotiations with a certain slyness that gave him an edge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cunning - skill in achieving one's ends by deceit; synonym for slyness
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest; opposite of slyness

Etymology

From the adjective 'sly' + -ness suffix, where 'sly' is from Middle English sli(g)h, from Old Norse slœgr (cunning).
