Mastotermes electromexicus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæs.təˈtɜr.miːz ˌɪ.lɛk.troʊˈmɛk.sɪ.kəs / (AmE), / ˌmæs.təˈtɜː.miːz ˌɪ.lɛk.trəʊˈmɛk.sɪ.kəs / (BrE)
an extinct species of termite found fossilized in amber in southern Mexico
extinct termite species from Mexican amber

Examples

Fossils of Mastotermes electromexicus have provided important clues about termite evolution.
Researchers discovered Mastotermes electromexicus preserved in Miocene-age Mexican amber.
The morphology of Mastotermes electromexicus closely resembles that of modern species in the same genus.

Etymology

From Latin roots: Mastotermes (a termite genus) and electromexicus (referring to amber and Mexico).
