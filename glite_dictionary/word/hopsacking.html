hopsacking

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑpˌsækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɒpˌsækɪŋ / (BrE)
a loosely woven, coarse fabric made of cotton or linen, commonly used in clothing and upholstery
coarse, loosely woven fabric

Examples

The jacket was tailored from a durable hopsacking for a classic, textured look.
Designers often choose hopsacking for lightweight summer suits because of its breathability.
She admired the natural color and rustic feel of the hopsacking used in the upholstery.

Etymology

From 'hop sack', referring to sacks once used for carrying hops, transferred to similar woven fabric.
