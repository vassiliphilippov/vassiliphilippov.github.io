Pseudocolus

1. NOUN

/ suːˈdɑːkələs / (AmE), / sjuːˈdɒkələs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Clathraceae, known for their unique fruiting body shapes.
genus of fungi in Clathraceae family

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species in the genus Pseudocolus during their expedition in Southeast Asia.
The fruiting bodies of Pseudocolus are notable for their striking structure and odor.
Mycology textbooks often include images of Pseudocolus to illustrate diversity among Clathraceae fungi.

Etymology

From Greek pseudo- (false) + Latin colus (spindle).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsuː.doʊˈkoʊləs / (AmE), / ˌsjuː.dəʊˈkəʊləs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Clathraceae, known for species with unusual fruiting body shapes.
genus of fungi in Clathraceae

Examples

The species Pseudocolus fusiformis is found in many temperate regions around the world.
Researchers discovered a new member of the Pseudocolus genus in a rainforest environment.
Fungi in the Pseudocolus genus are often recognized by their distinctive fruiting structures.

Etymology

From Greek 'pseudo-' (false) + 'colus', of uncertain origin, referring to the genus's misleading similarity to related fungi.
