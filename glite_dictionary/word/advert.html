advert

1. NOUN

/ ˈæd.vɝːt / (AmE), / ˈæd.vɜːt / (BrE)
an advertisement, especially a notice promoting a product, service, or event in print, broadcast, or online media
advertisement; public promotion
british

Examples

Did you see the advert for the new mobile phone on television last night?
They're running a clever advert in all the magazines this month.
I found my first job through an advert in the local newspaper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advertisement - a notice or announcement promoting a product, service, or event

Etymology

Shortened form of 'advertisement'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07263469-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ əˈdvɜrt / (AmE), / ədˈvɜːt / (BrE)
to refer to something in an indirect or passing way; to allude to
refer to indirectly; allude

Examples

In his speech, he adverted to the recent policy changes without giving details.
She adverted only briefly to the difficulties the company faced.
The report adverts to earlier research in the field, but does not cite it specifically.

Synset Examples

He alluded to the problem but did not mention it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to allude - refer to indirectly or hint at
  • Antonym: mention - refer to something explicitly

Etymology

From Latin 'advertere' (to turn toward).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01028737-v
Synset POS: v
