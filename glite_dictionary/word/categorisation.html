categorisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkætəɡəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkatəɡərʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of arranging things into categories or classes.
arranging into categories

Examples

The categorisation of animals can be based on their physical characteristics.
His job involves the categorisation of books in the library.
Researchers studied the categorisation of stimuli in human memory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classification - the act of arranging into groups based on shared qualities

Etymology

From 'category' + '-isation'; 'category' from Greek 'katēgoria' (accusation, category).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkætəɡəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkatɪɡəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the action or process of placing things into groups or categories based on shared characteristics
the act of grouping things into categories
british

Examples

The categorisation of books by genre makes it easier to find what you're looking for.
Scientists use categorisation to organize species based on shared features.
Her categorisation of the artwork was praised for its attention to detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: categorization - the act of distributing things into classes or categories (American spelling)

Etymology

From Late Latin 'categoricatio' and English suffix '-ation', based on 'category'.

3. NOUN

/ ˌkætəɡɚəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkatəɡəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or result of arranging people or things into classes or categories
act or result of classifying
british

Examples

The categorisation of books by genre makes them easier to find in the library.
Accurate categorisation of specimens is crucial for scientific research.
There has been debate about the categorisation of certain movies as comedies or dramas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classification - the action or process of classifying something
  • Synonym: grouping - the action of putting things into groups

Etymology

From Late Latin 'categoria' (category) + English '-isation', influenced by French 'catégorisation'.
