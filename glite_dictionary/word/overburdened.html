overburdened

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊ.vɚˈbɝː.dənd / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.vəˈbɜː.dənd / (BrE)
having too much to carry, deal with, or cope with, especially in terms of work, responsibility, or worry
having too much to carry or deal with

Examples

Hospitals in the city have become overburdened with patients during the outbreak.
She felt overburdened by the constant demands of her job and family life.
The committee is already overburdened and cannot take on more projects at this time.

Synset Examples

bowed down with troubles
found himself loaded down with responsibilities
overburdened social workers
weighed down with cares

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burdened - carrying a heavy load or responsibility
  • Antonym: unburdened - not weighed down by responsibilities or concerns

Etymology

From 'over-' (excessively) + 'burden' (load).
