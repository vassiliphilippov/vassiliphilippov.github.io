squatness

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwɑːtnəs / (AmE), / ˈskwɒtnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being short and broad in shape
quality of being short and broad

Examples

The squatness of the old stone cottage gave it a sturdy appearance.
She disliked the squatness of the new office building, preferring taller structures.
The dog’s squatness made it look even more adorable as it waddled down the street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stubbiness - the property of being short and thick
  • Antonym: tallness - the property of being tall

Etymology

From 'squat' (short and broad) plus '-ness', forming a noun.
