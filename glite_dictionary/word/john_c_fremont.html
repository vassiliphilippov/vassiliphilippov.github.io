John C. Fremont

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn siː friːˈmɒnt / (AmE), / dʒɒn siː friːˈmɒnt / (BrE)
(1813–1890) United States explorer, military officer, and politician, known for mapping much of the American West and Northwest.
U.S. explorer and politician (1813–1890)

Examples

John C. Fremont played a vital role in expanding the knowledge of the American West during the 19th century.
Many history textbooks mention John C. Fremont as an influential figure in U.S. exploration.
The city of Fremont, California, is named after John C. Fremont.
