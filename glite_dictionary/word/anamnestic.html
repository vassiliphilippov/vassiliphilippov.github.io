anamnestic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.əmˈnɛs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.amˈnɛs.tɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or aiding memory or recollection, especially in a medical or historical context.
aiding memory; related to recollection
medical

Examples

The doctor reviewed the patient's anamnestic data before making a diagnosis.
Her anamnestic response helped the psychologist understand her childhood experiences.
An anamnestic questionnaire was given to assess the patient's medical history.

Etymology

From Greek anamnēstikos (able to recall), from anamnesis (remembrance).
