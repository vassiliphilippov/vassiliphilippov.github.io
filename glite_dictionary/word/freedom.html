freedom

1. NOUN

/ ˈfri.dəm / (AmE), / ˈfriː.dəm / (BrE)
The state of being exempt from an obligation or duty; immunity from a required responsibility.
immunity from an obligation or duty

Examples

The new policy granted employees freedom from mandatory overtime hours.
He received freedom from jury duty because of his age.
Religious institutions often enjoy freedom from certain taxes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exemption - the process of being freed from an obligation or liability
  • Antonym: obligation - a duty or commitment

Etymology

Old English *frēodōm* (power of self-determination).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfri.dəm / (AmE), / ˈfriː.dəm / (BrE)
The power or right to act, speak, or think as one wants without hindrance or restraint.
the state of being free

Examples

The constitution guarantees freedom of speech for all citizens.
Many nations have fought hard to win their freedom from colonial powers.
She values her freedom above anything else in life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liberty - the state of being free within society from oppressive restrictions
  • Antonym: oppression - prolonged cruel or unjust treatment or control

Etymology

From Old English 'frēodōm' (state of free, freedom), from 'frēo' (free) + '-dōm' (state, condition).
