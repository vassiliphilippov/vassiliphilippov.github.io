sanguinary

1. ADJ

/ ˈsæŋɡwəˌneri / (AmE), / ˈsaŋɡwɪnəri / (BrE)
involving or characterized by bloodshed; marked by eager violence or cruelty
involving bloodshed or violence

Examples

The rebels launched a sanguinary assault against the fortress, leaving many casualties.
Historians often describe the medieval battles as particularly sanguinary affairs.
The conflict escalated into a sanguinary struggle that shocked the world.

Synset Examples

this bitter and sanguinary war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bloody - involving or characterized by the shedding of blood
  • Antonym: peaceful - free from disturbance or bloodshed; not violent

Etymology

From Latin sanguinarius (of blood, bloodthirsty), from sanguis (blood).

/ ˈsæŋ.ɡwəˌner.i / (AmE), / ˈsaŋ.ɡwɪ.nər.i / (BrE)
having a strong desire for bloodshed or violence; bloodthirsty.
eager for bloodshed or violence

Examples

The general developed a reputation for his sanguinary tactics on the battlefield.
The revolution was followed by a sanguinary purge of political opponents.
Her sanguinary imagination filled the stories with violence and conflict.

Synset Examples

bloody-minded tyrants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bloodthirsty - eager to shed blood; violently inclined
  • Antonym: peaceful - not inclined to violence or bloodshed; calm and tranquil

Etymology

From Latin sanguinarius (of blood, bloodthirsty), from sanguis (blood).
