matchboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈmætʃ.bɔrd / (AmE), / ˈmætʃ.bɔːd / (BrE)
a board with a groove cut into one edge and a tongue into the other, allowing them to fit tightly together, commonly used in flooring and paneling
board with tongue and groove edges

Examples

Each matchboard was precisely cut to ensure a snug fit against the next one.
They installed a pine matchboard ceiling in the old cottage to preserve its rustic style.
The craftsman replaced the damaged section with new matchboards that matched the original flooring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tongue and groove - method of fitting similar objects together, edge to edge

Etymology

From 'match' + 'board', referring to boards fitting or matching together.
