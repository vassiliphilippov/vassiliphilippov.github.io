esprit de corps

1. NOUN

/ ɛsˌprit də ˈkɔr / (AmE), / ɛsˌpriː də ˈkɔː / (BrE)
the feeling of pride, loyalty, and unity shared by the members of a group that motivates them to work together and strive for success
group spirit, morale, or shared pride

Examples

The team's esprit de corps was evident in the way they supported each other during the competition.
Military units often develop a strong esprit de corps that helps them face challenges together.
Our company's esprit de corps has been essential to overcoming difficult times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morale - the confidence, enthusiasm, and discipline of a person or group at a particular time

Etymology

From French 'esprit de corps' (spirit of the body/group).
