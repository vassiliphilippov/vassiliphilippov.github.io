Grecian

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡriːʃən / (AmE), / ˈɡriːʃən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Greece, especially in historical or literary usage
native or resident of Greece
archaic

Examples

The ancient Grecian was known for his wisdom and philosophy.
Several Grecians settled in the city during the Hellenistic period.
In the story, a young Grecian journeys across the Mediterranean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Greek - a native or inhabitant of modern Greece

Etymology

From Latin Graecianus, from Graecia (Greece).

/ ˈɡriʃən / (AmE), / ˈɡriːʃən / (BrE)
relating to Greece, its people, language, or culture, especially in an artistic or historical context
relating to Greece or the Greeks

Examples

The museum displayed a collection of Grecian vases from the fifth century BC.
She wore a flowing Grecian gown to the formal gala.
Many buildings in the city feature Grecian columns and architectural styles.
