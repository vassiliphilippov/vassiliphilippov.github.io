Giovanni de'Medici

1. PROPN

/ dʒoʊˈvɑːni deɪ ˈmɛdɪtʃi / (AmE), / dʒɒˈvɑːni deɪ ˈmɛdɪtʃi / (BrE)
(1475–1521) Italian pope (Pope Leo X), son of Lorenzo de'Medici, who excommunicated Martin Luther and granted Henry VIII the title 'Defender of the Faith'.
Italian pope, excommunicated Luther

Examples

Giovanni de'Medici became Pope Leo X in 1513, marking a significant period in Church history.
As pope, Giovanni de'Medici was known for his patronage of the arts during the Renaissance.
The excommunication of Martin Luther by Giovanni de'Medici had a profound impact on the Reformation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pope Leo X - papal title of Giovanni de'Medici

Etymology

Italian name; Giovanni is the Italian form of John, de'Medici meaning 'of the Medici family.'
