Geometridae

1. NOUN

/ dʒiːˈɑːmɪtrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / dʒiːˈɒmɪtrɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of moths whose larvae are known as measuring worms due to their characteristic looping movement
family of moths with larvae called measuring worms

Examples

The larvae of the Geometridae are commonly known as inchworms or measuring worms.
Many members of the Geometridae family display effective camouflage on tree bark.
Scientists study Geometridae to understand patterns of mimicry and evolution in moths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: measuring worm - the larva of Geometridae moths, noted for its looping gait

Etymology

From New Latin Geometridae, from genus Geometra (type genus), from Greek geōmetria (geometry), referring to the larval movement as if 'measuring the earth'.
