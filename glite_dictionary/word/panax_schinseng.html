Panax schinseng

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæn.æks ˈʃɪn.sɛŋ / (AmE), / ˈpan.aks ˈʃɪn.sɛŋ / (BrE)
A Chinese herb with palmately compound leaves, small greenish flowers, and forked aromatic roots, traditionally believed to have medicinal properties.
Chinese medicinal herb with aromatic roots
medical

Examples

Traditional Chinese medicine often incorporates Panax schinseng for its reputed healing properties.
The forked roots of Panax schinseng are carefully harvested and dried for export.
Researchers continue to investigate the medicinal benefits of Panax schinseng in modern therapies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ginseng - an herbal root used in medicine with similar properties

Etymology

From New Latin, genus name 'Panax' meaning 'all-healing', and Chinese 'schinseng', an older romanization of 'ginseng.'
