centrepiece

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.tɚˌpis / (AmE), / ˈsɛntəˌpiːs / (BrE)
the central or most important feature of something
most important feature
british

Examples

The new stadium became the centrepiece of the city’s redevelopment project.
Education reform was regarded as the centrepiece of their election campaign.
The exhibition’s centrepiece was a sculpture by a renowned local artist.

Synset Examples

education was the centerpiece of the Democratic Party's political platform

Related Terms

  • Synonym: centerpiece - American English spelling of 'centrepiece'

Etymology

From 'centre' (Middle English, from Old French, from Latin 'centrum') + 'piece'. Spelling reflects British English usage.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.tɚˌpis / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.təˌpiːs / (BrE)
an object or decoration placed in the middle of a table or display as an attractive focal point
object placed in the center for decoration

Examples

The floral centrepiece was the highlight of the dining table.
She arranged candles as a centrepiece for the party.
A large sculpture served as the centrepiece of the exhibition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: centerpiece - American English spelling of 'centrepiece'

Etymology

From centre + piece, modeled after French 'pièce centrale'.
