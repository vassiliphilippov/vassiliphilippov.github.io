insectivore

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɛktəˌvɔr / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛktɪˌvɔː / (BrE)
an animal or organism that feeds mainly on insects
organism that primarily eats insects

Examples

Some bats are insectivores, consuming thousands of insects each night.
The anteater is a classic example of an insectivore in the animal kingdom.
Many birds are insectivores during the summer months, feeding on abundant insects.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: herbivore - organism that eats mainly plants
  • Synonym: carnivore - organism that eats mainly animals

Etymology

From Latin 'insectum' (insect) + '-vorus' (devouring).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɛktɪˌvɔr / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛktɪˌvɔː / (BrE)
a small mammal that primarily eats insects, such as moles and shrews
small insect-eating mammal

Examples

The shrew is an example of an insectivore found in many temperate regions.
Most moles are insectivores that feed underground on worms and insects.
Many insectivores are nocturnal, searching for prey during the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carnivore - an animal that feeds on flesh
  • Antonym: herbivore - an animal that feeds only on plants

Etymology

From Latin insectum (insect) + vorare (to devour, eat).
