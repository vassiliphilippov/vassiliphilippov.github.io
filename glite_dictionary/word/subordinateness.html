subordinateness

1. NOUN

/ səˈbɔrdɪnətnəs / (AmE), / səˈbɔːdɪnətnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being subordinate or of lesser importance compared to something else.
state of being subordinate

Examples

The subordinateness of her position meant she had little influence over major decisions.
He resented the subordinateness assigned to his contributions during the project.
Cultural traditions sometimes reinforce the subordinateness of certain groups in society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subordination - the act or state of being subordinate
  • Antonym: dominance - the state of being dominant or having higher importance

Etymology

From subordinate + -ness, modeled after 'subordinate' (from Latin subordinatus, past participle of subordinare 'place in order, rank beneath').
