impalpable

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpælpəbəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpalpəbl / (BrE)
Unable to be felt by touch; intangible or not perceptible to the sense of touch.
unable to be felt by touch

Examples

The aroma was so faint as to be almost impalpable.
His anxiety was impalpable, but she sensed its presence.
There was an impalpable difference in the air that he couldn't quite define.

Synset Examples

an impalpable pulse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intangible - unable to be touched or grasped; not having physical presence
  • Antonym: palpable - able to be touched or felt; tangible

Etymology

From Latin 'impalpabilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'palpare' (to touch gently).

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpæl.pə.bəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpælpəbəl / (BrE)
incapable of being perceived by the senses, especially the sense of touch
unable to be felt or touched

Examples

The distinction between reality and illusion was almost impalpable in the dream sequence.
Dust in the air is often so fine as to be impalpable to the touch.
There was an impalpable tension in the room that no one could quite explain.

Synset Examples

the intangible constituent of energy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intangible - unable to be touched; not having physical presence
  • Antonym: palpable - able to be touched or felt; tangible

Etymology

From Late Latin impalpabilis, from Latin in- 'not' + palpabilis 'that may be touched'.

WordNet ID: oewn-02400435-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpælpəbəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpalpəbl / (BrE)
unable to be touched or felt; not easily perceived by the senses or the mind.
not able to be touched or perceived

Examples

The difference between the two proposals was so impalpable that few could distinguish them.
A sense of anxiety filled the room, almost impalpable yet undeniably present.
The perfume left an impalpable trace in the air long after she had gone.

Synset Examples

an impalpable cloud
impalpable shadows
impalpable distinctions
as impalpable as a dream

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intangible - unable to be touched or grasped; not having a physical presence
  • Antonym: palpable - able to be touched or felt; easily perceived

Etymology

From Latin 'impalpabilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'palpabilis' (that can be touched), from 'palpare' (to touch gently).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01721906-a
Synset POS: a
