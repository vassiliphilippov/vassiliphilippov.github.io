symbolic

1. ADJ

/ sɪmˈbɑlɪk / (AmE), / sɪmˈbɒlɪk / (BrE)
serving as a symbol or using symbolism to represent ideas or qualities rather than literal meanings
representing ideas by symbols

Examples

The red rose in the painting has a symbolic meaning of love and passion.
Many religious ceremonies are deeply symbolic in their use of colors and objects.
Her gesture was a symbolic act of reconciliation.

Synset Examples

symbolic art

Related Terms

  • Synonym: figurative - departing from a literal use of words; metaphorical
  • Antonym: literal - taking words in their usual or most basic sense

Etymology

From Greek 'symbolikos' (of tokens or signs), via Late Latin 'symbolicus'.

2. ADJ

/ sɪmˈbɑlɪk / (AmE), / sɪmˈbɒlɪk / (BrE)
serving as a sign or representation of something abstract; using or involving symbols.
representing something abstract

Examples

The dove is symbolic of peace in many cultures.
Wearing black clothing can be symbolic of mourning or respect for the deceased.
The handshake was symbolic of their agreement on the new partnership.

Synset Examples

a crown is emblematic of royalty
the spinning wheel was as symbolic of colonical Massachusetts as the codfish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emblematic - serving as a symbol of a particular quality or concept
  • Synonym: representative - serving as a typical example or symbol of something
  • Antonym: literal - taking words in their usual or most basic sense without metaphor or symbolism

Etymology

From Greek symbolikos (of or pertaining to a symbol), from symbolon (sign, token).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01989251-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ sɪmˈbɑːlɪk / (AmE), / sɪmˈbɒlɪk / (BrE)
relating to or using symbols; representing ideas or qualities instead of literal meaning.
relating to or using symbols

Examples

The dove is symbolic of peace in many cultures.
They studied symbolic logic in their advanced mathematics class.
His gesture was symbolic rather than practical, meant to convey a deeper message.

Synset Examples

symbolic logic
symbolic operations
symbolic thinking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: figurative - representing something else by means of a symbol or image
  • Antonym: literal - taking words in their usual or most basic sense

Etymology

From Late Latin symbolicus, from Greek symbolikos (serving as a symbol), from symbolon (symbol).
