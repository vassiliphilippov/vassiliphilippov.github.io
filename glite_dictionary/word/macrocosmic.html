macrocosmic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmæk.rəˈkɑ.zmɪk / (AmE), / ˌmæk.rəʊˈkɒz.mɪk / (BrE)
relating to or constituting the macrocosm; concerning the universe or the whole of a complex structure.
relating to the universe or macrocosm

Examples

The book examines the macrocosmic forces that shape human civilizations.
Some philosophers believe in a connection between the microcosmic and macrocosmic orders.
Her research adopts a macrocosmic perspective on climate change, focusing on global patterns.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: microcosmic - relating to a microcosm; concerning a small representative system or world

Etymology

From Greek makros (large) + kosmos (world, order).
