Bungarus fasciatus

1. NOUN

/ bʌŋˈɡɛərəs fæˈsiːeɪtəs / (AmE), / bʌŋˈɡɑːrəs fæsɪˈeɪtəs / (BrE)
A large, venomous snake of the krait family, characterized by black and yellow bands, native to South and Southeast Asia.
venomous banded krait snake

Examples

The Bungarus fasciatus is easily identified by its striking black and yellow bands.
Scientists studied the venom composition of Bungarus fasciatus to develop new antivenom therapies.
In some regions, the Bungarus fasciatus is feared for its powerful neurotoxic bite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: krait - any member of the genus Bungarus, venomous Asian snakes

Etymology

From Latinized Neo-Latin, 'Bungarus' from Hindi 'bungar', a krait; 'fasciatus' meaning banded or striped in Latin.
