ECF

1. NOUN

/ i si ɛf / (AmE), / iː siː ɛf / (BrE)
(in physiology) abbreviation for extracellular fluid, the liquid outside cells containing proteins and electrolytes, including blood plasma and interstitial fluid.
extracellular fluid; liquid outside cells
medical

Examples

A medical team measured the patient's ECF levels to assess his hydration status.
Sodium concentration is generally higher in the ECF than inside cells.
Loss of ECF can lead to a drop in blood pressure and shock.

Synset Examples

the body normally has about 15 quarts of extracellular fluid

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extracellular fluid - liquid outside cells, including blood plasma and interstitial fluid

Etymology

Abbreviation of extracellular fluid.
