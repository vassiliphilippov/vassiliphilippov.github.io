redwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌwʊd / (BrE)
either of two very large coniferous trees native to California, especially the coastal redwood (Sequoia sempervirens) and the giant sequoia (Sequoiadendron giganteum), known for their great height and reddish bark.
giant Californian coniferous tree

Examples

The ancient redwood forests of California attract visitors from around the world.
A single redwood can live for more than a thousand years.
Conservationists are working to protect the remaining stands of redwood trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sequoia - a type of very tall tree related to the redwood, native to California

Etymology

Named for the characteristic reddish color of the bark and wood.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌwʊd / (BrE)
The valuable wood of either of two immense coniferous trees native to California, known for its strength, durability, and reddish color.
wood from California redwood trees

Examples

The furniture was crafted from redwood for its distinctive color and natural resistance to decay.
Many decks in California are made with durable redwood planks.
Harvesting redwood has become more regulated to protect the ancient forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sequoia - wood of the sequoia tree

Etymology

From 'red' + 'wood', referring to the reddish color of the wood.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌwʊd / (BrE)
The soft, reddish wood obtained from either of two species of sequoia trees, used especially for building and furniture.
soft reddish wood from sequoia trees

Examples

The deck was built from durable redwood, which resists rotting and insects.
He admired the rich color of the redwood panels in the old library.
Furniture made from redwood is both beautiful and long-lasting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sequoia - a coniferous tree of the genus Sequoia, known for great size and longevity
