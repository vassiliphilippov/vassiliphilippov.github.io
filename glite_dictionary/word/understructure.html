understructure

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndɚˌstrʌktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌstrʌktʃə / (BrE)
the lowest supporting part of a building or structure; a base or foundation that supports the main structure above it
lowest supporting part of a structure

Examples

The engineers carefully inspected the understructure of the bridge for any signs of damage.
Before constructing the building, workers ensured the understructure was stable and secure.
Heavy rains caused erosion that threatened the understructure of the ancient temple.

Synset Examples

it was built on a base of solid rock
he stood at the foot of the tower

Related Terms

  • Antonym: superstructure - the part of a building or structure above its base or foundation
  • Synonym: foundation - the solid base on which a structure is built
  • Synonym: base - the supporting bottom layer of a structure

Etymology

From 'under-' (beneath) + 'structure' (something constructed).
