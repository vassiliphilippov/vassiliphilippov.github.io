nomination

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑːməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɒmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of officially naming someone as a candidate for a position, office, or award
the act of officially naming a candidate

Examples

Her nomination for the award surprised everyone in the company.
He waited anxiously for the official nomination as the party’s candidate for mayor.
The nomination process includes several rounds of selection and interviews.

Synset Examples

the Republican nomination for Governor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appointment - the act of assigning a job or position to someone

Etymology

From Latin 'nominationem' (a naming), from 'nominare' (to name).

2. NOUN

/ ˌnɑːməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɒmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
an address or formal speech, especially at a political convention, putting forward the name of a candidate for election
proposing a candidate in a formal speech

Examples

The senator's nomination of the candidate drew enthusiastic applause from the crowd.
Her nomination speech was praised for its passion and clarity.
At the convention, the nomination lasted less than five minutes but was very impactful.

Synset Examples

the nomination was brief and to the point

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to nominate - to formally put forward a candidate for a position or office

Etymology

From Latin nominatio (a naming), from nominare (to name).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07255585-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌnɑː.məˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɒ.mɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The act or condition of being formally proposed as a candidate for an appointment or election.
being proposed as a candidate

Examples

She was delighted by her nomination for the board of directors.
The nomination of a candidate sparked debate among party members.
Winning the nomination was only the first step toward securing the job.

Synset Examples

there was keen competition for the nomination
his nomination was hotly protested

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appointment - the act of assigning a job or position to someone
  • Synonym: election - the process of choosing someone for a position by voting

Etymology

From Latin nominatio (a naming), from nominare (to name).

WordNet ID: oewn-13949568-n
Synset POS: n
