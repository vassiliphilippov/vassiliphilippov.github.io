bronco

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɑːŋkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈbrɒŋkəʊ / (BrE)
a wild or partially tamed horse, especially one used in rodeos in North America
wild or partly tamed horse
american

Examples

The cowboy struggled to ride the bronco as it bucked wildly in the arena.
She watched in amazement as the young wrangler tamed the spirited bronco.
Many rodeo events feature skilled riders attempting to stay on a bucking bronco.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mustang - a wild horse of the plains of North America

Etymology

From Spanish 'bronco' (rough, coarse); adopted into American English in the 19th century.
