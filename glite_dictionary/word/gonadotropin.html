gonadotropin

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡoʊ.nə.dəˈtroʊ.pɪn / (AmE), / ˌɡəʊ.nə.dəˈtrəʊ.pɪn / (BrE)
a hormone secreted by the anterior pituitary gland or placenta that stimulates the activity of the gonads and regulates reproductive processes
hormone stimulating gonads
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered a blood test to check her gonadotropin levels.
Certain fertility treatments involve injections of gonadotropin.
Low gonadotropin can lead to reproductive health issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hormone - a chemical substance that regulates bodily functions
  • Synonym: gonadotropin-releasing hormone - hormone that stimulates the release of gonadotropins
  • Synonym: gonad - an organ that produces gametes; a testis or ovary

Etymology

From Greek 'gonad' (sex gland) + '-tropin' from Greek 'tropos' (a turn, change).
