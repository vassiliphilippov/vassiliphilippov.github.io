Periplaneta

1. PROPN

/ ˌpɛrəpləˈniːtə / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪpləˈniːtə / (BrE)
a genus of large cockroaches found worldwide, including the American cockroach (Periplaneta americana)
genus of large cosmopolitan cockroaches

Examples

The species Periplaneta americana is commonly referred to as the American cockroach.
Entomologists often study Periplaneta to understand cockroach behavior and pest control methods.
Unlike other genera, Periplaneta includes some of the largest urban cockroaches.

Etymology

From Greek 'peri-' (around) + 'planētēs' (wanderer), meaning 'wanderer around'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɛr.ə.pləˈniː.tə / (AmE), / ˌpɛr.ɪ.pləˈniː.tə / (BrE)
A genus of large, cosmopolitan cockroaches, including several common pest species.
genus of large cockroaches

Examples

The Periplaneta genus includes the notorious American cockroach, a major pest in many cities.
Scientists collected specimens of Periplaneta from several continents for their study.
Members of Periplaneta are known for their adaptability and resilience in urban environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockroach - a large, brown or black insect that likes to live in kitchens and other warm places

Etymology

From Greek 'peri' (around) + 'planētēs' (wanderer), referring to the roaming nature of these insects.
