fuchsia

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːʃə / (AmE), / ˈfjuːʃə / (BrE)
a tropical shrub or small tree with showy, pendulous flowers, typically purple, red, or white, widely cultivated as an ornamental plant
ornamental shrub with drooping, colorful flowers

Examples

We planted a fuchsia in the garden for its beautiful flowers.
The fuchsia blooms attracted hummingbirds and butterflies all summer long.
Her favorite plant is the fuchsia because of its vibrant, dangling blossoms.

Etymology

Named after German botanist Leonhart Fuchs (1501–1566).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːʃə / (AmE), / ˈfjuːʃə / (BrE)
a vivid purplish-red color, named after the flower of the same name
vivid purplish-red color

Examples

She chose a fuchsia dress to stand out at the party.
The artist mixed reds and purples until he achieved the perfect fuchsia for the flowers.
Bright fuchsia accents gave the room a lively, modern feel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magenta - a purplish-red color similar to fuchsia
  • Degree Lower: pink - a pale red color
  • Degree Higher: crimson - a strong, deep red color

Etymology

Named after the fuchsia plant, itself named after German botanist Leonhart Fuchs.
