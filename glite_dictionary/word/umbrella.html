umbrella

1. ADJ

/ ʌmˈbrɛl.ə / (AmE), / ʌmˈbrɛl.ə / (BrE)
covering or including many different items, groups, or elements under a single entity or category.
covering many items or groups

Examples

The association acts as an umbrella body for several regional charities.
This committee serves an umbrella function, coordinating efforts across departments.
The policy provides umbrella coverage for a range of risks.

Synset Examples

an umbrella organization
umbrella insurance coverage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inclusive - including everything or everyone within its scope
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00531080-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ əmˈbrɛlə / (AmE), / ʌmˈbrɛlə / (BrE)
a protective formation of military aircraft maintained over ground operations or targets to defend against attack
formation of planes providing aerial protection

Examples

Allied forces maintained an umbrella of fighter planes over the advancing troops.
The city was protected by an umbrella of bombers and interceptors throughout the campaign.
Commanders requested a stronger air umbrella before commencing the ground assault.

Synset Examples

an air umbrella over England

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shield - anything that protects or defends

Etymology

From Italian 'ombrella', diminutive of 'ombra' (shade), from Latin 'umbra' (shadow).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00831579-n
Synset POS: n

/ əmˈbrɛlə / (AmE), / ʌmˈbrɛlə / (BrE)

something that unites or includes related items

Examples

Many non-profit groups work under the umbrella of the national charity organization.
Environmental regulations fall under the broad umbrella of public health policies.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover - something that protects or encloses

4. NOUN

/ əmˈbrɛl.ə / (AmE), / ʌmˈbrɛl.ə / (BrE)
A lightweight handheld device consisting of a collapsible canopy mounted on a central rod, used for protection against rain or sunlight.
handheld collapsible canopy for rain or sun

Examples

She opened her umbrella as soon as it started to rain.
He forgot his umbrella on the bus and got soaked on the walk home.
A colorful umbrella can brighten up the gloomiest of days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parasol - a light umbrella used to give shade from the sun

Etymology

From Italian 'ombrella', diminutive of 'ombra' meaning 'shade', from Latin 'umbra'.
