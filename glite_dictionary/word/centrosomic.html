centrosomic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛntrəˈsoʊmɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɛntrəˈsəʊmɪk / (BrE)
of or relating to a centrosome
related to the centrosome
medical

Examples

The centrosomic structure plays a crucial role in cell division.
Researchers observed centrosomic abnormalities in several cancer cell lines.
Proper centrosomic function is essential for accurate chromosome segregation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: centrosome - an organelle near the nucleus in animal cells

Etymology

From 'centrosome' + -ic, indicating relation.
