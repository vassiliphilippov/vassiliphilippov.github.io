imaum

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɑm / (AmE), / ɪˈmɑːm / (BrE)
(in Islam) a man who leads prayers in a mosque; among Shiites, a recognized authority on Islamic theology and law, and a spiritual guide.
Islamic prayer leader; Shiite spiritual authority

Examples

The imaum began the call to prayer as the congregation gathered in the mosque.
For Shiites, the imaum holds significant religious and legal authority as a spiritual guide.
During the ceremony, the imaum recited verses from the Quran and offered prayers for the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imam - Muslim religious leader who leads prayers at a mosque

Etymology

From Arabic *imām* (leader) via Persian and Urdu *imām*; variant spelling of imam.
