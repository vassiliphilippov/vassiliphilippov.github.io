catty-corner

1. ADJ

/ ˈkætiˌkɔrnər / (AmE), / ˈkætiˌkɔːnə / (BrE)
in a diagonal position or direction, especially across the corner of a space or intersection; slanted or positioned on a line between two nonadjacent corners.
diagonally across or slanted
informal american

Examples

The bakery is catty-corner from the post office at the intersection.
They placed the chairs catty-corner to maximize space in the small room.
We ran catty-corner across the empty lot to take a shortcut.

Synset Examples

set off in a catty-corner direction across the vacant lot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diagonal - joining two opposite corners of a square or rectangle

Etymology

Alteration of 'cater-corner', from Middle French 'quatre' (four), referring to the four corners of a square.
