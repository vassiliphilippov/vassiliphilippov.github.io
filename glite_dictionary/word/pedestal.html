pedestal

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛdəˌstɛl / (AmE), / ˈpɛdɪstl̩ / (BrE)
a base or support on which a column, statue, or structure stands, typically in architecture
base or support for column or statue

Examples

The sculpture stood proudly on its marble pedestal in the center of the hall.
Each column rises from a separate pedestal carved with intricate designs.
After restoration, the statue was placed back onto its original pedestal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plinth - a heavy base supporting a statue or vase

Etymology

From French *piédestal*, from Italian *piedistallo*, from *piede* (foot) + *stallo* (stall, place).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɛdəstəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛdɪstl / (BrE)
A position in which someone is greatly admired or regarded as superior, often excessively or unrealistically.
position of great esteem or admiration

Examples

Ever since her success, her fans have placed her on a pedestal and treat her as a role model.
Don't put anyone on a pedestal; everyone makes mistakes.
He felt uncomfortable being put on a pedestal at work after the promotion.

Synset Examples

they put him on a pedestal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to idolize - to admire or revere someone excessively
  • Antonym: to disparage - to speak of someone as unimportant or inferior

Etymology

From French piédestal, from Italian piedistallo, literally 'foot of a stall'.
