ventilation

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɛn.t̬əˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɛn.tɪˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of breathing, in which air is inhaled into the lungs and carbon dioxide is exhaled
process of breathing
medical

Examples

The patient's ventilation was monitored carefully throughout the surgery.
Adequate ventilation is essential for maintaining healthy blood oxygen levels.
Mechanical ventilation may be required for patients who cannot breathe on their own.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: respiration - the process of breathing; taking in oxygen and releasing carbon dioxide

Etymology

From Latin 'ventilatio' (the act of fanning or winnowing), from 'ventilare' (to fan).

2. NOUN

/ ˌvɛn.t̬ɪˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɛn.tɪˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of supplying fresh air and removing stale or foul air from a space.
supplying fresh air; removing stale air

Examples

Proper ventilation in a room is necessary to maintain good indoor air quality.
The hospital upgraded its ventilation system to reduce the spread of airborne diseases.
During renovations, they improved ventilation to keep the workspace safe and comfortable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aeration - the process of introducing air into a material, especially soil or liquid

Etymology

From Latin 'ventilatio', from 'ventilare' (to fan, ventilate).

3. NOUN

/ ˌvɛn.tɪˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɛn.tɪˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a system or means in a building that provides fresh air and removes stale air, often by mechanical methods
system for providing fresh air

Examples

The building's ventilation was upgraded to improve air quality throughout the offices.
She adjusted the ventilation to let in more fresh air during the meeting.
Poor ventilation can lead to a buildup of harmful gases and unpleasant odors.

Synset Examples

she was continually adjusting the ventilation

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: air conditioning - system for controlling air temperature and humidity

Etymology

From Latin ventilatio (fanning, airing), from ventilare (to fan, to winnow).

4. NOUN

/ ˌvɛn.tɪˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɛn.tɪˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of discussing or debating a matter thoroughly and openly, especially one of public interest
open discussion or debate

Examples

The ventilation of these controversial policies helped the community reach a consensus.
A detailed ventilation of the proposal was necessary before a final vote could be taken.
Public ventilation of the issue allowed everyone to express their viewpoints openly.

Synset Examples

such a proposal deserves thorough public discussion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discussion - the act of talking about something with others
  • Synonym: debate - a formal argument or discussion of opposing points

Etymology

From Latin 'ventilatio', meaning 'a fanning', used figuratively for 'examination, discussion'.
