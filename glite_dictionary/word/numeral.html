numeral

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːmərəl / (AmE), / ˈnjuːmərəl / (BrE)
a symbol or group of symbols used to represent a number.
symbol representing a number

Examples

Children learn to write the numerals 1 through 10 in kindergarten.
The clock uses Roman numerals instead of regular numbers.
You must enter a valid numeral in this box to proceed.

Synset Examples

he learned to write the numerals before he went to school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digit - a single symbol used to make up numerals

Etymology

From Latin numeralis (relating to a number), from numerus (number).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06820056-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈnumɚəl / (AmE), / ˈnjuːmərəl / (BrE)
relating to or denoting numbers.
relating to numbers

Examples

She provided a numeral code for each item in the inventory.
In grammar, a numeral adjective specifies the quantity of a noun.
The ancient manuscript included a unique numeral system not seen today.

Synset Examples

a numeral adjective
numerical order
a numeric symbol

Related Terms

  • Synonym: numerical - relating to numbers; expressed in numbers
  • Synonym: numeric - relating to or representing numbers

Etymology

From Latin 'numeralis' (of or belonging to a number), from 'numerus' (number).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02780705-a

3. NOUN

/ ˈnuː.mər.əl / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.mər.əl / (BrE)
a word or symbol expressing a number, especially as a part of speech in grammar.
a word expressing a number

Examples

In the sentence 'She has three cats', the word three is a numeral.
Roman numerals such as I, V, and X are used on some clock faces.
Ordinal numerals like 'second' and 'third' indicate position or order.

Synset Examples

Numerals may be attributive, as in ‘two dogs’, or pronominal, as in ‘I saw two (of them)’.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: number - a word or symbol representing a value or quantity

Etymology

From Latin numeralis (of or belonging to a number), from numerus (number).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-92464376-n
Synset POS: n
