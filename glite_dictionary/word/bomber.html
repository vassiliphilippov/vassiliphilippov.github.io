bomber

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːmɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɒmə / (BrE)
a military aircraft designed to carry and drop bombs during flight
military aircraft for dropping bombs

Examples

The old bomber was retired from service after decades of missions.
During the raid, a bomber dropped several tons of explosives on the target.
Modern bombers can fly long distances without refueling.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fighter - a military aircraft designed for air-to-air combat
  • Synonym: warplane - an aircraft designed for war, especially one used in combat

Etymology

From 'bomb' + '-er', indicating an aircraft that bombs.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːmɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɒmə / (BrE)
a person who plants or detonates bombs, often with the intention to cause harm or destruction
person who plants bombs

Examples

The police arrested the bomber before he could plant the device in the building.
Security cameras captured footage of the suspected bomber near the subway entrance.
The city recovered quickly after the bomber was apprehended by authorities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrorist - person who uses violence for political aims

Etymology

From 'bomb' + '-er', meaning one who bombs.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːmər / (AmE), / ˈbɒmə / (BrE)
(US, regional) A large sandwich made from a long crusty roll split lengthwise and filled with meats, cheese, and other condiments; also known as a sub, hoagie, or grinder in other regions.
large sandwich in a long roll (US regional)
american

Examples

For lunch, I ordered a turkey and cheese bomber from the deli.
He took his first bite of the enormous bomber and grinned with delight.
Some people call it a sub, but around here it's known as a bomber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sub - a large sandwich in a split long roll, also called submarine sandwich
  • Synonym: hoagie - a large sandwich filled with meats and cheese, US regional term
  • Synonym: grinder - a large sandwich on a long roll, US regional term
