punctilio

1. NOUN

/ pʌŋkˈtɪlioʊ / (AmE), / pʌŋkˈtɪlɪəʊ / (BrE)
a fine or petty point of conduct or procedure, especially as related to formal etiquette or ceremony
minor point of etiquette or formality

Examples

She observed every punctilio of proper table manners at the banquet.
The ambassador was a stickler for official punctilio and protocol in all meetings.
Despite the urgency, they did not neglect a single punctilio of the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: etiquette - conventional requirements of social behavior

Etymology

From Italian 'puntiglio', from Latin 'punctum' (a point).

2. NOUN

/ pʌŋkˈtɪlioʊ / (AmE), / pʌŋkˈtɪlɪəʊ / (BrE)
A small or precise detail of conduct or procedure, especially as related to etiquette or formalities.
fine point of etiquette or procedure

Examples

The ambassador observed every punctilio of diplomatic protocol at the reception.
She insisted on following every punctilio in the traditional wedding ceremony.
His attention to punctilio sometimes made him seem distant or overly formal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: formality - an established procedure or custom, especially in etiquette
  • Antonym: informality - the state of being relaxed and not following strict rules or customs

Etymology

From Italian 'puntiglio', from Latin 'punctum' (a point).
