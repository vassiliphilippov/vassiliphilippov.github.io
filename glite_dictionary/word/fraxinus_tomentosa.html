Fraxinus tomentosa

1. NOUN

/ ˌfræksɪnəs toʊˈmɛntoʊsə / (AmE), / ˌfræksɪnəs təˈmɛntəsə / (BrE)
A timber tree of central and southeastern United States with hairy branchlets and a swollen trunk base, also known as the hairy ash.
timber tree with hairy branchlets

Examples

The Fraxinus tomentosa is valued for its durable wood in furniture making.
You can identify Fraxinus tomentosa by its hairy branchlets and swollen trunk base.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Fraxinus tomentosa in southeastern forests.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Fraxinus' meaning ash tree and 'tomentosa' meaning covered with dense, matted hairs.
