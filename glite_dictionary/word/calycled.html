calycled

1. ADJ

/ ˈkælɪkəld / (AmE), / ˈkælɪkəld / (BrE)
(of a plant part) having a calyculus; surrounded by an additional whorl of sepals or bracteoles beneath the calyx.
having a calyculus (botany)

Examples

The flower is calycled, meaning it has an extra whorl of small sepals beneath the main calyx.
Some species in this genus are distinctly calycled and can be identified by their outer bracteoles.
A calycled structure can help protect the developing flower bud.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calyculate - having a small cup-shaped structure (calyculus)

Etymology

From calyx (the outermost whorl of a flower), from Greek *kalyx* (case, shell), with the suffix -ed (having).
