bleat

1. NOUN

/ blit / (AmE), / bliːt / (BrE)
the characteristic cry of a sheep or goat, or any similar sound
the cry of a sheep or goat

Examples

We heard the bleat of sheep echoing across the valley.
A faint bleat came from behind the barn as the lamb called for its mother.
The nervous bleat of a goat broke the silence of the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baa - the cry of a sheep (verb and noun)

Etymology

From Old English *blætan* (to bleat, cry), imitative in origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07392531-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ blit / (AmE), / bliːt / (BrE)
(of a sheep, goat, or calf) to make a characteristic wavering cry; to speak or complain in a weak or plaintive way.
make a wavering animal cry; complain plaintively

Examples

The lost lamb began to bleat for its mother.
Children visiting the farm laughed when the goats bleated loudly.
He continued to bleat about the unfair decision, but no one listened.

Synset Examples

The lambs were bleating

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whine - to complain in a feeble or irritating way
  • Antonym: moo - the characteristic sound made by a cow

Etymology

Old English *blætan* (to bleat), of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01050202-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ blit / (AmE), / bliːt / (BrE)
to speak in a weak or complaining way, especially with a whining tone
to speak in a whining or complaining tone

Examples

Stop bleating about your problems and do something to fix them.
He bleated his excuses, hoping someone would feel sorry for him.
She bleats on and on whenever things don't go her way.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whine - complain in a feeble or petulant way
  • Synonym: to complain - express dissatisfaction or annoyance

Etymology

From Old English *blǣtan* (to bleat, cry like a sheep).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01050364-v
Synset POS: v
