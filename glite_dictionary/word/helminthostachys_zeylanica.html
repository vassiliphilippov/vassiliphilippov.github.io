Helminthostachys zeylanica

1. NOUN

/ hɛlˌmɪnθoʊˈstæskɪs zeɪˈlænɪkə / (AmE), / hɛlˌmɪnθəˈstaskɪs zeɪˈlænɪkə / (BrE)
An Australasian fern species characterized by clusters of sporangia on the stems of its fertile fronds.
a species of Australasian fern

Examples

Helminthostachys zeylanica prefers moist, shaded habitats in subtropical and tropical regions.
Researchers collected samples of Helminthostachys zeylanica for a study on fern diversity.
The fertile fronds of Helminthostachys zeylanica are easy to recognize by their unique sporangia clusters.

Etymology

From Greek 'helminthos' (worm) and 'stachys' (spike); 'zeylanica' refers to Ceylon (Sri Lanka).
