Monotropaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːnəˌtroʊpˈeɪsiˌiː / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˌtrəʊpˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of saprophytic herbaceous plants, sometimes classified within Pyrolaceae, including genera such as Monotropa and Sarcodes
family of saprophytic herbaceous plants

Examples

The Monotropaceae are known for their lack of chlorophyll and their dependency on fungi.
Botanists sometimes include Monotropaceae within the family Pyrolaceae based on molecular studies.
Ghost plants and snow plants are members of the Monotropaceae family.

Etymology

From the type genus Monotropa, from Greek monos (single) + tropos (turn), with the botanical suffix -aceae denoting a plant family.

2. NOUN

/ məˌnoʊtrəˈpeɪsiː / (AmE), / məˌnɒtrəˈpeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of saprophytic herbaceous plants, sometimes included in the family Pyrolaceae, including genera such as Monotropa and Sarcodes
a family of saprophytic herbaceous plants

Examples

The rare ghost plant belongs to the Monotropaceae family and lacks chlorophyll.
Botanists once classified Monotropa and Sarcodes together in Monotropaceae.
Recent studies debate whether Monotropaceae should remain a separate family or be merged with Pyrolaceae.

Etymology

From genus name Monotropa and standard botanical family suffix -aceae.
