preclinical

1. ADJ

/ ˌpriːˈklɪnɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈklɪnɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the early stage of a disease when symptoms have not yet appeared and accurate diagnosis is not possible.
before symptoms appear in a disease
medical

Examples

The preclinical stage of Alzheimer's disease may last for many years before symptoms develop.
Researchers are studying biomarkers to detect preclinical changes in the brain.
Early intervention in the preclinical phase could improve patient outcomes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clinical - relating to the observation and treatment of actual patients rather than theory or laboratory studies

Etymology

From prefix 'pre-' (before) + 'clinical' (relating to the observation and treatment of actual patients).
