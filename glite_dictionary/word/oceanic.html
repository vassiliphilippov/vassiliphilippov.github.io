Oceanic

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈʃænɪk / (AmE), / əʊˈsiːənɪk / (BrE)
a group of languages constituting the eastern subfamily of the Malayo-Polynesian family, spoken in the islands of the Pacific Ocean
eastern subfamily of Malayo-Polynesian languages

Examples

The Oceanic languages are spoken across a vast area of the Pacific islands.
Linguists have conducted detailed studies of Oceanic syntax and phonology.
Fijian is one of the most well-known Oceanic languages.

Etymology

From 'ocean' + '-ic', referring to languages of the Pacific Ocean region.
