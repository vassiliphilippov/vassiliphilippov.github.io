Yersinia pestis

1. NOUN

/ jɚˈsɪniə ˈpɛstɪs / (AmE), / jəˈsɪniə ˈpɛstɪs / (BrE)
the bacterial species that causes plague in humans and other mammals
bacterium causing plague
medical

Examples

Yersinia pestis is notorious for causing the Black Death in medieval Europe.
Scientists studied Yersinia pestis to understand how the plague spread so rapidly.
The discovery of Yersinia pestis revolutionized our knowledge of infectious diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plague - a serious infectious disease or epidemic, especially the bubonic plague

Etymology

Named after Swiss bacteriologist Alexandre Yersin and from Latin pestis (plague).
