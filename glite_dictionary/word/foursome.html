foursome

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔr.səm / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.səm / (BrE)
a group of four people considered together as one unit
group of four people

Examples

The golf club paired us into a foursome for the tournament.
Our dinner foursome meets every month to try a new restaurant.
They formed a talented foursome for the local trivia night.

Synset Examples

he joined a barbershop quartet
the foursome teed off before 9 a.m.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: trio - a group of three people considered together
  • Degree Lower: threesome - a group of three people considered as a unit
  • Degree Higher: quintet - a group of five people considered as a unit

Etymology

Formed from 'four' + the suffix '-some' (group of), modeled after words like 'twosome', 'threesome'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɔːr.səm / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.səm / (BrE)
a group or set of four people or things
group of four

Examples

The team divided into two foursomes for the tournament.
We formed a foursome to play bridge every Saturday night.
A foursome of friends set out on a road trip across the country.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: trio - a group of three people or things
  • Degree Higher: quintet - a group of five people or things

Etymology

From 'four' + '-some', denoting a group of four.
