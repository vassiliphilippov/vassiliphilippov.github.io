to miniaturize

1. VERB

/ ˈmɪniətʃəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɪniətʃəraɪz / (BrE)
to design or construct something on a much smaller scale than usual
make something smaller in size

Examples

Advancements in technology have allowed engineers to miniaturize complex electronic circuits.
The company managed to miniaturize the camera without sacrificing image quality.
Scientists are working to miniaturize medical devices to make them less invasive.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to enlarge - to make something bigger or larger

Etymology

From miniature + -ize, based on the noun 'miniature' (a small-scale representation).
