to sabo

1. VERB

/ ˈseɪboʊ / (AmE), / ˈseɪbəʊ / (BrE)
(informal) To deliberately undermine, sabotage, or cause harm to a person, plan, or project.
deliberately sabotage or undermine
informal

Examples

He tried to sabo the project by giving the wrong files to the team.
Please don't sabo your classmates during the group assignment.
Rumors spread that someone might sabo the upcoming event.

Synset Examples

To sabo the project, he leaked confidential details.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sabotage - to deliberately destroy, damage, or obstruct something

Etymology

Shortened from "sabotage"; used in Singaporean English and other regional varieties.
