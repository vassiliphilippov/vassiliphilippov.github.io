astatic

1. ADJ

/ əˈstætɪk / (AmE), / əˈstatɪk / (BrE)
not static, stable, or steady; prone to change or fluctuation.
not static or stable; unstable

Examples

The engineer designed an astatic system to prevent the buildup of unwanted oscillations.
An astatic galvanometer can measure very small electrical currents due to its design.
Because of the astatic nature of the device, its readings varied more than expected.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: static - not moving, active, or changing; stable
  • Synonym: unstable - likely to change or fail; not stable

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'statikos' (causing to stand).
