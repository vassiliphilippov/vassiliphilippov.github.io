Trapa

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪpə / (AmE), / ˈtrɑːpə / (BrE)
A small genus of Eurasian aquatic perennial herbs commonly known as water chestnut.
genus of aquatic plants (water chestnut)

Examples

The edible seeds of Trapa have been used as food in Asia for centuries.
Researchers studied various species within the Trapa genus to understand their ecological impact.
The leaves of Trapa float on the surface of ponds and lakes.

Etymology

From Latin 'trapa', from a type of caltrop, as the plant's fruit resembles the caltrop in shape.
