dinocerate

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪnəˈsɛrət / (AmE), / ˌdɪnəˈsɪərət / (BrE)
a member of an extinct order of large, herbivorous ungulate mammals known as Dinocerata, which lived during the Paleocene and Eocene epochs
extinct ungulate mammal of Dinocerata

Examples

The fossilized skeleton of a dinocerate was discovered in the ancient riverbed.
Scientists believe the dinocerate had three pairs of blunt horns on its skull.
Unlike modern hoofed animals, the dinocerate possessed both tusks and horns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ungulate - hoofed mammal

Etymology

From New Latin Dinocerata, from Greek deinos (terrible) + keras (horn), referring to their horned skulls.
