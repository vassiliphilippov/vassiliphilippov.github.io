Lynx

1. NOUN

/ lɪŋks / (AmE), / lɪŋks / (BrE)
A genus of medium-sized wild cats, including the Eurasian lynx, Canada lynx, Iberian lynx, and bobcat, characterized by tufted ears, short tails, and spotted coats.
genus of wild cats with tufted ears

Examples

The Lynx genus contains four living species, each adapted to different regions.
Wildlife biologists study the differences between members of the Lynx genus, such as the bobcat and the Eurasian lynx.
The Lynx is known for its distinctive ear tufts and solitary behavior in forested areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cat - domesticated and wild members of the family Felidae

Etymology

From Latin *lynx*, from Greek *lunx* (lynx).

2. NOUN

/ lɪŋks / (AmE), / lɪŋks / (BrE)
A text-based web browser that displays web pages as plain text, typically used on command-line interfaces or in minimal computing environments.
popular text-based web browser

Examples

Many system administrators use Lynx to quickly check websites from the command line.
Because Lynx is so lightweight, it can load web pages much faster on older computers.
Some universities still provide access to Lynx for visually impaired users who prefer text browsing.

Etymology

Named after the animal 'lynx'; released in 1992 as a text browser.
