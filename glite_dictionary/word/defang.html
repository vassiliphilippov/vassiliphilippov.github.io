to defang

1. VERB

/ diˈfæŋ / (AmE), / diˈfæŋ / (BrE)
to remove the fangs from an animal, especially a snake or other venomous creature
remove the fangs from an animal

Examples

The wildlife expert carefully defanged the cobra before the demonstration.
Some zoos defang their snakes to ensure the safety of handlers and visitors.
After the procedure, the veterinarian confirmed that the snake had been successfully defanged.

Synset Examples

defang the poisonous snake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disarm - to take weapons away from someone or render harmless

Etymology

From prefix 'de-' (removal) + 'fang' (a long, pointed tooth).

2. VERB

/ diˈfæŋ / (AmE), / diˈfæŋ / (BrE)
to make (something, such as a threat or criticism) harmless or less powerful; to reduce the severity or impact of something, such as a scandal.
make harmless or less severe

Examples

The company attempted to defang the scandal by releasing all relevant documents.
A carefully worded statement helped to defang the scandal before it could do serious damage.
The politician's team worked quickly to defang the scandal through transparency and accountability.

Synset Examples

They tried to defang the scandal with a public apology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to neutralize - to make something ineffective or harmless
  • Antonym: to exacerbate - to make a problem or situation worse

Etymology

From de- (removal) + fang; originally meaning 'to remove the fangs from', later used figuratively.

3. VERB

/ dɪˈfæŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈfaŋ / (BrE)
to make (something, such as criticism or opposition) less powerful or effective
make something less powerful or threatening

Examples

The company attempted to defang the criticism by being more transparent with the public.
Quick action by the leadership helped to defang much of the opposition's outrage.
They revised the proposal in order to defang any negative feedback from stakeholders.

Synset Examples

The new policy aims to defang the criticism of the company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to weaken - to make something less strong
  • Synonym: to neutralize - to make something harmless or ineffective
  • Antonym: to strengthen - to make something stronger or more powerful

Etymology

From de- (removal) + fang; originally meant to remove the fangs from an animal, now used figuratively.
