to disgorge

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈɡɔrdʒ / (AmE), / dɪsˈɡɔːdʒ / (BrE)
(of a substance) to pour out or allow to pour out in large amounts, often suddenly or forcefully; to discharge contents forcefully or in a stream.
pour or discharge forcefully

Examples

The volcano began to disgorge thick black smoke into the sky.
The overturned truck disgorged its cargo across the busy highway.
During the flood, the river disgorged tons of debris onto the nearby fields.

Synset Examples

spill the beans all over the table

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spill - to cause or allow a liquid or substance to overflow or escape

Etymology

From Old French *desgorger* (to disgorge, vomit), from des- (dis-) + gorge (throat).

2. VERB

/ dɪsˈɡɔrdʒ / (AmE), / dɪsˈɡɔːdʒ / (BrE)
to eject the contents of the stomach through the mouth; to vomit or throw up.
to vomit; eject stomach contents

Examples

The patient began to disgorge the meal he had just eaten, alarming the nurses.
After eating spoiled fish, she couldn't help but disgorge violently.
The dog disgorged the odd object it had swallowed earlier in the day.

Synset Examples

After drinking too much, the students vomited
He purged continuously
The patient regurgitated the food we gave him last night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vomit - to eject the contents of the stomach through the mouth
  • Synonym: to regurgitate - to bring swallowed food up again to the mouth
  • Synonym: purge - to rid the body of unwanted substances, especially by vomiting

Etymology

From Old French *desgorger*, from *des-* (dis-) + *gorge* (throat).
