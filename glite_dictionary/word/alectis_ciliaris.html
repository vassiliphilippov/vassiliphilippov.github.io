Alectis ciliaris

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɛktɪs sɪˈlɪərɪs / (AmE), / əˈlɛktɪs sɪˈlɪərɪs / (BrE)
A large fish of the jack family, noted for greatly elongated front rays on its dorsal and anal fins, found in tropical and subtropical seas; also known as the African pompano.
large tropical fish (African pompano)

Examples

The Alectis ciliaris is prized by sport fishers for its striking appearance and vigorous fight.
Juvenile Alectis ciliaris have long, trailing fin filaments that shorten as they mature.
Marine biologists observed a school of Alectis ciliaris near the coral reef.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: African pompano - common name for Alectis ciliaris, a large game fish

Etymology

From scientific Latin; genus name Alectis and species name ciliaris, meaning 'fringed' or 'hair-like' in Latin, referring to the filamentous fin rays.
