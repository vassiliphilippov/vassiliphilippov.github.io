razzle-dazzle

1. NOUN

/ ˈræzəl ˌdæzəl / (AmE), / ˈræzl ˌdæzl / (BrE)
an exciting and complicated action or display, especially in sports or entertainment, intended to impress or confuse opponents or an audience
exciting, complex display or play

Examples

The coach called for some razzle-dazzle to catch the defense off guard.
The film was all about visual razzle-dazzle rather than a deep plot.
Fans love the team's unexpected razzle-dazzle on the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: showmanship - use of impressive techniques to entertain or impress

Etymology

Reduplicative compound formed from 'razzle' and 'dazzle'; first attested in 19th-century English.
