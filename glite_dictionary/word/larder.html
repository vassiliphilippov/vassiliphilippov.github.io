larder

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑrdər / (AmE), / ˈlɑːdə / (BrE)
a supply of food stored for use, especially in a household
supply of stored food for a household

Examples

After a week without shopping, their larder was almost empty.
She stocked the larder with canned goods before winter arrived.
There's always something delicious waiting in their larder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pantry - a small room or closet where food, dishes, and utensils are kept

Etymology

From Middle English 'lardere', from Old French 'lardier', from 'lard' (bacon, fat).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɑrdər / (AmE), / ˈlɑːdə / (BrE)
a small room or cupboard used for storing food, especially before refrigerators were common
small room or cupboard for storing food

Examples

She went to the larder to get some cheese for lunch.
The cottage had a cool, stone-walled larder to keep food fresh.
After restocking the larder, they had enough supplies for the week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pantry - a small room or closet in which food, utensils, and kitchen supplies are stored

Etymology

From Old French 'lardier', from 'lard' meaning bacon, from Latin 'laridum'.
