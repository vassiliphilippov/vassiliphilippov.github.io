baronetcy

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑrənətˌsi / (AmE), / ˈbærənətˌsi / (BrE)
The rank or dignity of a baronet, or the hereditary title held by a baronet.
hereditary title of a baronet

Examples

He inherited the baronetcy after his father passed away.
The baronetcy has been in the family for several generations.
Upon receiving the baronetcy, he gained new social status and responsibilities.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: barony - title or rank of a baron

Etymology

From 'baronet' (a hereditary title), with suffix '-cy' denoting office or rank.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːrənətsi / (AmE), / ˈbærənətsi / (BrE)
the rank, dignity, or hereditary position of a baronet
rank of a baronet
british

Examples

After his father died, he inherited the baronetcy and the family estate.
The baronetcy was established in the seventeenth century by royal decree.
He was proud to have been granted a baronetcy for his services to the crown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baronet - a hereditary title below a baron and above a knight

Etymology

From baronet + -cy, patterned after 'dukedom', 'earldom', etc.
