dihydroxyphenylalanine

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌhaɪdroʊksi fəˌniːl əˈleɪnɪn / (AmE), / daɪˌhɪdrɒksi fiːˌniːl əˈleɪnɪn / (BrE)
an amino acid formed in the liver and converted into dopamine in the brain, also known as L-DOPA
amino acid precursor to dopamine
medical

Examples

The medication increases the level of dihydroxyphenylalanine in the brain to help control symptoms of Parkinson's disease.
Researchers are studying how dihydroxyphenylalanine is metabolized in patients with neurological disorders.
Dihydroxyphenylalanine plays a crucial role in the production of dopamine in the nervous system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: L-DOPA - another name for dihydroxyphenylalanine, used in treatment of Parkinson's disease
  • Degree Higher: dopamine - a neurotransmitter produced from dihydroxyphenylalanine

Etymology

From di- (two) + hydroxy (relating to -OH groups) + phenylalanine (an amino acid).
