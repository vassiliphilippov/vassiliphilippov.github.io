ikon

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪˌkɒn / (AmE), / ˈaɪkɒn / (BrE)
a conventional religious painting, typically of a saint or other holy figure, painted in oil on a small wooden panel and venerated in the Eastern Orthodox Church
religious painting venerated in Eastern Orthodoxy

Examples

The church displayed an ancient ikon of the Virgin Mary, revered by parishioners for centuries.
He studied the technique of painting a traditional Russian ikon during his visit to Moscow.
Restorers carefully cleaned the darkened surface of the ikon without damaging the original colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: icon - religious painting, especially in Eastern Orthodox tradition

Etymology

From Russian 'ikona', from Greek 'eikōn' (image, likeness).
