volatilized

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɑː.lə.təˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈvɒl.ə.təˌlaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a substance) converted into a gas or vapor, especially by the application of heat.
converted to gas or vapor

Examples

The chemicals were volatilized during the heating process, resulting in a noticeable odor.
Once volatilized, the substance rose quickly and dispersed into the atmosphere.
Many pesticides are volatilized after they are applied, which can affect air quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: volatile - easily evaporated at normal temperatures; liable to change rapidly
  • Antonym: condensed - changed from gas or vapor into a liquid

Etymology

From 'volatilize' (to make volatile), from French 'volatiliser', from Latin 'volatilis' (flying, fleeting).
