Calypso bulbosa

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪpsoʊ bʌlˈboʊsə / (AmE), / kəˈlɪpsəʊ bʌlˈbəʊsə / (BrE)
A rare orchid of north temperate bogs, with a solitary white to pink flower marked with purple at the tip, growing above a single basal leaf.
rare bog orchid with solitary pinkish flower

Examples

The Calypso bulbosa blooms in early spring, adding a touch of color to the forest floor.
Many botanists consider Calypso bulbosa an indicator of undisturbed habitat.
Photographers travel far to capture the delicate beauty of Calypso bulbosa in its natural setting.

Etymology

From Greek 'Kalypso' (a mythical nymph) and Latin 'bulbosa' (bulbous).
