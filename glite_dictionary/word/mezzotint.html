mezzotint

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛz.oʊˌtɪnt / (AmE), / ˈmɛz.əʊ.tɪnt / (BrE)
A print made using a technique of engraving on a copper or steel plate, where the surface is roughened to create gradations of light and shade.
print made by a shading engraving technique

Examples

The artist produced a beautiful mezzotint that captured the subtleties of twilight.
This museum has one of the largest collections of 18th-century mezzotints in the country.
She studied the mezzotint technique to better understand how light and shadow affect mood in art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engraving - A print made from an engraved plate, block, or other surface

Etymology

From Italian mezzatinta, from mezza ('half') + tinta ('tint, tone').
