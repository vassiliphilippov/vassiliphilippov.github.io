warship

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔrˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈwɔːʃɪp / (BrE)
A ship belonging to a nation's armed forces that is equipped and used for fighting in wars.
military ship for warfare

Examples

The country's navy launched a new warship to strengthen its maritime defense capabilities.
A fleet of warships patrolled the waters near the disputed territory.
The museum features a decommissioned warship that once served in World War II.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battleship - a large armored warship with a main battery consisting of heavy caliber guns
  • Antonym: peace ship - a ship used for peaceful purposes, not war

Etymology

Formed from 'war' and 'ship'; attested from the late 16th century.
