dragonhead

1. NOUN

/ ˈdræɡənˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈdræɡənˌhɛd / (BrE)
An American herb with sharply serrated lance-shaped leaves and spikes of blue or violet flowers, of the genus Dracocephalum.
American herb with blue/violet flowers

Examples

The dragonhead thrives in well-drained soils and is often grown in herb gardens.
We admired the spikes of violet flowers blooming on the dragonhead near the fence.
Some gardeners plant dragonhead to attract bees and butterflies to their yards.

Etymology

Named for the shape of the flower, which is said to resemble a dragon's head, from Greek drakōn (dragon) + kephalē (head).
