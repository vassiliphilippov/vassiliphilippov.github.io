lymphoma

1. NOUN

/ lɪmˈfoʊmə / (AmE), / lɪmˈfəʊmə / (BrE)
A type of malignant tumor that originates in the lymphatic system, forming in lymph tissue.
cancer of the lymphatic system
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with lymphoma after experiencing persistent swollen lymph nodes.
Treatments for lymphoma have advanced significantly in the past decade.
Symptoms of lymphoma may include fever, night sweats, and unintentional weight loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cancer - a disease caused by uncontrolled cell growth

Etymology

From New Latin lymph + -oma (indicating a tumor).
