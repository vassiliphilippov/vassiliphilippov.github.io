backstroke

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌstroʊk / (AmE), / ˈbækstrəʊk / (BrE)
A swimming stroke performed on the back, in which the arms are lifted alternately out of the water and brought forward in a circular motion, and the legs execute a flutter kick.
a swimming stroke on the back

Examples

Sarah swam the backstroke to win her race at the swim meet.
He practiced the backstroke daily to improve his performance.
The backstroke requires good coordination between the arms and legs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freestyle - a swimming stroke where the swimmer is face down and alternates their arms and legs

2. VERB

/ ˈbækˌstroʊk / (AmE), / ˈbækˌstrəʊk / (BrE)
to swim on one's back using a backward arm motion and flutter kick
swim on one's back

Examples

She backstroked across the pool with smooth, even strokes.
During the race, he decided to backstroke the final lap to conserve his energy.
The children practiced backstroking during their swimming lesson.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freestyle - swim using any preferred stroke, usually the front crawl
  • Synonym: breaststroke - swim with the chest forward and arms moving in a half-circle simultaneously
  • Synonym: butterfly - swim using a dolphin-like kick and overhand arm movement

Etymology

From 'back' + 'stroke', indicating the swimming stroke performed on the back.
