Equisetum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkwɪˈsiːtəm / (AmE), / ˌiːkwɪˈsiːtəm / (BrE)
a genus of non-flowering plants known as horsetails, characterized by hollow, jointed stems and small scale-like leaves
genus of plants known as horsetails

Examples

The fossil record shows that Equisetum survived for millions of years virtually unchanged.
Botanists examined the Equisetum growing along the riverbank for signs of pollution.
Equisetum is often considered a living fossil due to its ancient lineage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horsetail - common name for plants of the genus Equisetum

Etymology

From Latin 'equisetum', meaning 'horsetail', from 'equus' (horse) + 'saeta' (bristle, hair).

2. NOUN

/ ˌekwəˈsiːtəm / (AmE), / ˌɛkwɪˈsiːtəm / (BrE)
A genus of non-flowering, spore-producing plants known as horsetails, which are the only living members of the family Equisetaceae.
a genus of plants known as horsetails

Examples

The ancient Equisetum species thrived in prehistoric swamps.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of various Equisetum plants.
You can often find Equisetum growing along streams and wet meadows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horsetail - any plant of the genus Equisetum

Etymology

From Latin *equisetum* (horsetail), from *equus* (horse) + *saeta* (bristle).
