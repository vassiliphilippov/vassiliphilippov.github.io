Pyrola elliptica

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈroʊlə ɛˈlɪptɪkə / (AmE), / paɪˈrəʊlə ɛˈlɪptɪkə / (BrE)
A North American evergreen herb with small pinkish bell-shaped flowers and oblong leaves, formerly used for treating shinplasters.
North American evergreen herb with bell-shaped flowers

Examples

The forest floor was covered with clusters of Pyrola elliptica, their delicate flowers adding a touch of pink to the greenery.
Botanists studied the medicinal properties of Pyrola elliptica in relation to traditional remedies for skin ailments.
You can identify Pyrola elliptica by its oblong leaves and bell-shaped pinkish flowers in late spring.

Etymology

From genus name 'Pyrola' (Neo-Latin, diminutive of Latin 'pyrus', pear) and Latin 'elliptica' (elliptical), describing the leaf shape.
