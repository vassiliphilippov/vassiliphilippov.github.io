Homo heidelbergensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊmoʊ ˌhaɪdəlˌbɜrˈgɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhəʊməʊ ˌhaɪdəlˌbɜːˈgɛnsɪs / (BrE)
an extinct species of primitive human that lived in Europe and parts of Africa during the Middle Pleistocene period, considered to be an ancestor of both Neanderthals and modern humans
extinct ancient human species

Examples

Fossils of Homo heidelbergensis provide important clues about human evolution.
Scientists believe Homo heidelbergensis used tools and controlled fire.
Remains of Homo heidelbergensis have been found in Europe and Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Neanderthal - an extinct species or subspecies of archaic humans

Etymology

From Latin Homo (man) and Heidelberg (a city in Germany) with Latinized scientific suffix -ensis (from a place), named after where its remains were first discovered.
