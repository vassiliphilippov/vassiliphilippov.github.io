exhortation

1. NOUN

/ ɛɡˌzɔrˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ɛɡˌzɔːˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
an act of urging or encouraging someone earnestly to do something
earnest urging or encouragement

Examples

The coach's exhortation inspired the team to play harder in the second half.
Despite repeated exhortations from her friends, she refused to change her mind.
His passionate exhortation to donate was met with generous support from the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encouragement - the act of giving support, confidence, or hope
  • Synonym: admonition - a firm warning or reprimand

Etymology

From Latin exhortatio (an encouragement), from exhortari ‘to encourage’.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛɡˌzɔrˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛɡˌzɔːˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
an earnest urging or appeal intended to persuade someone to take action or adopt a belief
earnest urging or appeal

Examples

The coach's exhortation inspired the team to give their best effort in the final minutes of the game.
She ignored his exhortation to study harder, believing she could pass without it.
Despite repeated exhortations from parents, the children continued to play outside after dark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encouragement - the act of giving someone support or confidence
  • Synonym: admonition - a warning or firm advice
  • Antonym: discouragement - the act of making someone less confident or enthusiastic

Etymology

From Latin exhortatio (an encouragement), from exhortari (to encourage, urge).
