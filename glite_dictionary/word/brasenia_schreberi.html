Brasenia schreberi

1. NOUN

/ brəˈsiːniə ˈʃrɛbəri / (AmE), / brəˈsiːniə ˈʃrɛbəri / (BrE)
An aquatic plant with floating oval leaves and purple flowers, found in lakes and slow-moving streams, often used in aquariums.
aquatic plant with floating leaves and purple flowers

Examples

The aquarium featured a cluster of Brasenia schreberi at the water's surface.
Researchers found healthy populations of Brasenia schreberi in the protected wetland area.
Many aquarists value Brasenia schreberi for its decorative floating leaves and ease of care.

Etymology

Genus name from Latinized form of the botanist C.C. Brasen; species named after Johann Christian Daniel von Schreber.
