vice-presidency

1. NOUN

/ ˌvaɪsˈprɛzɪdənsi / (AmE), / ˌvaɪsˈprɛzɪdənsi / (BrE)
the position or term of office of a vice president
the office or term of a vice president

Examples

His vice-presidency was marked by significant diplomatic efforts and reforms.
During her vice-presidency, the company expanded into new international markets.
The vice-presidency is often seen as a stepping stone to higher office.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: presidency - the office or term of a president

Etymology

From vice- (Latin, 'in place of') + presidency (from Latin 'praesidens', presiding).

2. NOUN

/ ˌvaɪsˈprɛzɪdənsi / (AmE), / ˌvaɪsˈprɛzɪdənsi / (BrE)
the position, authority, and responsibilities of a vice president
office of a vice president

Examples

He announced his candidacy for the vice-presidency after years of public service.
The vice-presidency requires strong leadership and the ability to step in for the president if needed.
During her vice-presidency, she championed several important policy initiatives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: presidency - the office of president
  • Synonym: vice president - person holding the office of vice president

Etymology

Formed from 'vice-' (from Latin vice, meaning 'in place of') and 'presidency'.
