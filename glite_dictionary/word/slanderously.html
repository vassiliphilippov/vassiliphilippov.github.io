slanderously

1. ADV

/ ˈslændərəsli / (AmE), / ˈslɑːndərəsli / (BrE)
In a manner that involves making false and damaging statements about someone; in a slanderous or defamatory way.
in a slanderous manner

Examples

The politician claimed he was slanderously accused of corruption in the press.
She warned them not to speak slanderously about their competitors.
His reputation suffered because he was slanderously portrayed in the media.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defamatorily - in a defamatory manner

Etymology

From 'slander' + '-ous' + '-ly'; 'slander' from Old French 'esclandre', from Latin 'scandalum' (scandal).
