musculus serratus posterior

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌs.kjʊ.ləs sɛˈreɪ.təs pəˈstɪr.i.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kjʊ.ləs sɛˈreɪ.təs pɒˈstɪə.ri.ə / (BrE)
a skeletal muscle of the back that draws the rib cage backward and downward
back muscle that moves rib cage down and back
medical

Examples

In respiratory therapy, the musculus serratus posterior may be examined for signs of injury.
The musculus serratus posterior plays an important role during forced exhalation.
Weakness in the musculus serratus posterior can affect a patient's ability to expand the rib cage.

Etymology

From Latin: 'musculus' (muscle), 'serratus' (saw-shaped), 'posterior' (located at the back).
