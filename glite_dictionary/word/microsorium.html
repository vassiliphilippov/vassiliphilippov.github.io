Microsorium

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkrəˈsɔriəm / (AmE), / ˌmɪkrəˈsɔːrɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of tropical ferns, often epiphytic, found from Africa to Asia and Polynesia to Australia.
genus of tropical ferns

Examples

Some species of Microsorium are popular as ornamental plants in aquariums and terrariums.
The leaves of Microsorium are usually leathery and deep green, well adapted to humid environments.
Botanists have studied Microsorium to understand how ferns grow on trees without soil.

Etymology

From Greek 'micros' (small) + 'soron' (urn), referring to aspects of the sporangia.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkrəˈsɔːriəm / (AmE), / ˌmɪkrəˈsɔːriəm / (BrE)
a genus of tropical ferns, usually epiphytic, found from Africa through Asia and Polynesia to Australia.
genus of tropical epiphytic ferns

Examples

The Microsorium genus includes several ornamental fern species popular in aquariums.
Biologists studied the adaptability of Microsorium to various tropical climates.
Many Microsorium ferns are found growing on tree trunks in Southeast Asian rainforests.

Etymology

From Greek 'mikros' (small) and 'sōrós' (heap), referring to the sori (spore clusters) of the ferns.
