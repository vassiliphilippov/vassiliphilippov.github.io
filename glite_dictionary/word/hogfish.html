hogfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔɡˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈhɒɡˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a large wrasse (Lachnolaimus maximus) of the western Atlantic, especially from the Caribbean region, noted for its protruding snout
a wrasse fish with a protruding snout

Examples

Divers spotted a beautiful hogfish near the coral reef.
The hogfish is prized by anglers for its firm, white flesh.
We learned about the unique feeding habits of the hogfish during our marine biology class.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑɡˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈhɒɡˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a large marine fish of the western Atlantic, especially noted for the male's snout, which resembles that of a pig; a species of wrasse (Lachnolaimus maximus) often found on coral reefs
a wrasse fish with a pig-like snout

Examples

The diver spotted a brightly colored hogfish hiding among the coral.
Some people enjoy eating hogfish because of its delicate, mild flavor.
The male hogfish is easily recognized by its long, pig-like snout.
