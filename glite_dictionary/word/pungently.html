pungently

1. ADV

/ ˈpʌndʒəntli / (AmE), / ˈpʌndʒəntli / (BrE)
In a way that is sharply affecting the senses or expressed in a strong, direct manner.
in a pungent manner

Examples

She criticized the proposal pungently, leaving no room for misunderstanding.
The cheese smelled pungently as soon as he opened the package.
He spoke pungently about the failures in the project.

Synset Examples

he wrote pungently about his contemporaries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharply - in a sharp or incisive manner

Etymology

From pungent + -ly; 'pungent' from Latin 'pungens', present participle of 'pungere' (to prick, sting).

2. ADV

/ ˈpʌndʒəntli / (AmE), / ˈpʌndʒəntli / (BrE)
In a way that has a strong, sharp taste or smell; with a pungent quality.
with a strong, sharp taste or smell

Examples

The garlic was pungently strong in the spicy sauce.
She wrinkled her nose at the pungently aromatic cheese.
The kitchen smelled pungently of onions after dinner was prepared.

Synset Examples

the soup was pungently flavored

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pungent - having a strong, sharp taste or smell

Etymology

From "pungent" (from Latin pungens, pungent- ‘pricking’) + -ly.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
