Chester William Nimitz

1. PROPN

/ ˈtʃɛstər ˈwɪliəm ˈnɪmɪts / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛstə ˈwɪliəm ˈnɪmɪts / (BrE)
(1885–1966) United States admiral who commanded the Pacific Fleet during World War II and played a key role in defeating the Japanese navy.
US admiral, Pacific Fleet commander in WWII

Examples

During World War II, Chester William Nimitz played a pivotal role in the United States' naval victories in the Pacific.
The aircraft carrier USS Nimitz was named in honor of Chester William Nimitz's contributions to the Navy.
Military historians often study the strategies employed by Chester William Nimitz in major Pacific battles.
