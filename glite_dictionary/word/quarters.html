quarters

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɔrtərz / (AmE), / ˈkwɔːtəz / (BrE)
rooms or buildings where people live, especially provided for particular groups such as soldiers or staff.
rooms or buildings for people to live in

Examples

The company provided comfortable quarters for its overseas staff.
After the long journey, the explorers were relieved to find warm quarters.
The new recruits were shown to their quarters in the barracks.

Synset Examples

he found quarters for his family
I visited his bachelor quarters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accommodation - a place where someone lives or stays
  • Synonym: lodging - a place to sleep or stay, usually temporarily

Etymology

From Old French 'quartier' (a quarter, portion of a town), from Latin 'quartarius' (fourth part).
