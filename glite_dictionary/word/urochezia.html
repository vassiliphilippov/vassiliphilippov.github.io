urochezia

1. NOUN

/ ˌjʊroʊˈkiːziə / (AmE), / ˌjʊərəʊˈkiːziə / (BrE)
the passage of urine through the anus, typically due to an abnormal connection between the urinary tract and rectum
passage of urine from the anus (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor suspected urochezia after noting urine in the patient's rectal discharge.
Surgical intervention may be required to treat cases of urochezia caused by fistulas.
Urochezia is a rare but serious symptom often associated with traumatic injury or congenital malformations.

Etymology

From Greek 'ouron' (urine) + 'chezia' (defecation or discharge).
