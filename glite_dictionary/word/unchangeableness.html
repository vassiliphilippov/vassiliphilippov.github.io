unchangeableness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈtʃeɪndʒəbəlnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈtʃeɪndʒəb(ə)lnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being unchangeable; a tendency to remain unchanged.
the state of being unchangeable

Examples

The unchangeableness of the law made it difficult to implement necessary reforms.
Many philosophies emphasize the unchangeableness of certain natural principles.
She admired his unchangeableness in the face of constant pressure to adapt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immutability - the quality of being unchanging over time

Etymology

From unchangeable + -ness, forming a noun meaning 'state of being unchangeable'.
