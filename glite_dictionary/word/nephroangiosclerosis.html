nephroangiosclerosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɛfroʊˌændʒioʊskləˈroʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌnɛfrəʊˌændʒiəʊskləˈrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A kidney disease characterized by the hardening and thickening of the small arteries in the kidneys, often associated with high blood pressure, leading to reduced blood flow and possible kidney failure.
hardening of kidney arteries due to hypertension
medical

Examples

The patient's chronic hypertension eventually led to nephroangiosclerosis and compromised kidney function.
Pathological examination revealed extensive nephroangiosclerosis in both kidneys.
Early detection of nephroangiosclerosis can help prevent progression to kidney failure.

Etymology

From Greek 'nephro-' (kidney), 'angio-' (vessel), and 'sclerosis' (hardening).
