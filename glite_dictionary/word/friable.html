friable

1. ADJ

/ ˈfraɪ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈfraɪ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
easily crumbled or reduced to powder; easily broken into small fragments.
easily crumbled or broken

Examples

The soil in this region is remarkably friable, making it ideal for planting crops.
The archaeologist handled the friable parchment with great care to avoid damaging it further.
Over time, the old bricks became so friable that they could be crushed by hand.

Synset Examples

friable sandstone
friable carcinomatous tissue
friable curds formed in the stomach
crumbly cookies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crumbly - easily breaking into small pieces or fragments

Etymology

From Latin 'friabilis', from 'friare' (to crumble).
