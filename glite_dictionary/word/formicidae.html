Formicidae

1. NOUN

/ fɔrˈmɪsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / fɔːˈmɪsɪdiː / (BrE)
the family of insects comprising all ants
scientific family of ants

Examples

Nearly every continent hosts a diverse population of Formicidae species in its ecosystems.
The family Formicidae includes more than 12,000 described species of ants.
Entomologists study Formicidae to understand social organization and communication in insect societies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ant - a small insect belonging to the family Formicidae

Etymology

From Latin *formica* (ant), with the suffix -idae used for animal families.

Researchers discovered a new species within the Formicidae in the Amazon rainforest.
All members of the Formicidae family display highly organized social behavior.

Related Terms

From Latin formica (ant), with '-idae' as a standard suffix for animal family names in taxonomy.
