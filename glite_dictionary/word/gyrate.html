to gyrate

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒaɪˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌɪəreɪt / (BrE)
to move in a rapid spiral or circular motion, especially around a central point or axis
revolve or spin quickly in a circle

Examples

The dancers began to gyrate wildly as the music picked up speed.
The helicopter blades gyrate overhead, creating a strong downward draft.
As the planet orbits the sun, it also gyrates around its own axis.

Synset Examples

The dervishes whirl around and around without getting dizzy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spin - to rotate rapidly on an axis
  • Synonym: whirl - to move rapidly in a circular manner
  • Synonym: to rotate - to turn around a central point or axis

Etymology

From Latin gyratus, past participle of gyrare (to turn in a circle), from Greek guros (circle).

2. VERB

/ ˈdʒaɪˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪəreɪt / (BrE)
to move or spin around in circles or spirals
move or spin in circles

Examples

The dancers gyrated wildly to the rhythm of the drums under the flashing lights.
Columns of smoke gyrated upward as the fire intensified in the old warehouse.
He gyrated his hips in time with the music, drawing cheers from the crowd.

Synset Examples

the muscles and nerves of his fine drawn body were coiling for action
black smoke coiling up into the sky
the young people gyrated on the dance floor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rotate - turn around a central point or axis
  • Synonym: spiral - move in a spiral course or shape
  • Synonym: spin - turn around rapidly
  • Degree Lower: to oscillate - move back and forth in a regular rhythm

Etymology

From Latin gyratus, past participle of gyrare, from Greek guros (circle).
