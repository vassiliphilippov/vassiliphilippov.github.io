Maktab al-Khidamat

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːk.tɑːb æl xiːˈdɑː.mæt / (AmE), / ˈmæk.tæb æl ˌxɪˈdɑː.mæt / (BrE)
A militant organization founded in the 1980s by Osama bin Laden and others to finance and recruit foreign fighters for the Afghan war against the Soviet Union; known as a predecessor to al-Qaeda.
militant group precursor to al-Qaeda

Examples

The Maktab al-Khidamat played a key role in recruiting fighters for the Afghan-Soviet war.
Historians regard Maktab al-Khidamat as an important precursor to al-Qaeda.
Intelligence reports detailed the global fundraising network managed by Maktab al-Khidamat.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: al-Qaeda - international terrorist organization founded by Bin Laden

Etymology

From Arabic 'maktab al-khidamat' meaning 'Office of Services'.
