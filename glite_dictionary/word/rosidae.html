Rosidae

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈsaɪdiː / (AmE), / rəʊˈsɪdiː / (BrE)
a major subclass of flowering plants, mainly trees, shrubs, and herbs, characterized by polypetalous flowers and including families such as Rosaceae, Euphorbiaceae, and Myrtaceae.
subclass of flowering plants
medical

Examples

The Rosidae contain many economically important families, such as the rose and myrtle families.
New research has clarified the evolutionary relationships within the Rosidae.
Botanists have debated the boundaries of Rosidae based on genetic evidence.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name 'Rosa' (rose) and the suffix '-idae' indicating a taxonomic group.

2. NOUN

/ roʊˈsaɪdiː / (AmE), / rəʊˈsɪdiː / (BrE)
a large subclass of flowering plants, mostly trees, shrubs, and herbs with polypetalous flowers, that includes the rose family and about 108 other families
subclass of flowering plants (botany)

Examples

The rose is a well-known member of the Rosidae subclass.
Many fruit-bearing plants, such as apples and pears, belong to Rosidae.
Botanists often classify these flowering herbs under Rosidae.

Etymology

From Latin 'rosa' (rose) + taxonomic suffix '-idae', used for botanical groups.
