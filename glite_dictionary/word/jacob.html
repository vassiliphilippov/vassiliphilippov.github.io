Jacob

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒeɪ.kəb / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪ.kəb / (BrE)
(in the Bible) the son of Isaac and Rebekah, brother of Esau, and father of the twelve patriarchs of Israel; also known as Israel.
Biblical patriarch, son of Isaac

Examples

Jacob was the father of the twelve tribes of Israel according to biblical tradition.
The story of Jacob and Esau is a central theme in the Book of Genesis.
After wrestling with the angel, Jacob was given the name Israel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Israel - Jacob's name after wrestling with God; also the name of the people and nation descended from him

Etymology

From Hebrew Yaʿaqōb, via Latin Jacobus and Greek Iakōbos.
