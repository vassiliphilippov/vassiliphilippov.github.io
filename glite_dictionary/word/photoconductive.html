photoconductive

1. ADJ

/ ˌfoʊ.t̬oʊ.kənˈdʌk.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌfəʊ.təʊ.kənˈdʌk.tɪv / (BrE)
Relating to or exhibiting increased electrical conductivity as a result of exposure to light.
responding to light with increased conductivity

Examples

The sensor uses a photoconductive material to detect changes in light intensity.
This photoconductive property makes cadmium sulfide useful in light meters.
Researchers are developing new photoconductive polymers for use in solar cells.

Synset Examples

selenium is a photoconductive substance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photoconductivity - increase in electrical conductivity due to light exposure

Etymology

From photo- (relating to light) and conductive (able to conduct electricity).
