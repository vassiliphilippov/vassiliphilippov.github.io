Dasyatis

1. PROPN

/ ˌdæsiˈætɪs / (AmE), / ˌdæsɪˈeɪtɪs / (BrE)
A genus of stingrays in the family Dasyatidae, commonly known as whiptail stingrays, found in coastal waters worldwide.
genus of whiptail stingrays

Examples

The genus Dasyatis includes several species commonly known as whiptail stingrays.
Marine biologists study the habitat preferences of Dasyatis in temperate and tropical waters.
A large Dasyatis can often be seen gliding along sandy sea floors in coastal regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stingray - a type of cartilaginous fish with a flat body and long tail, some of which are in the genus Dasyatis

Etymology

From Greek dasys (hairy, rough) + Latin -atis (suffix used in zoological taxonomy).
