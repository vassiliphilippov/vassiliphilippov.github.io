endoneurium

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.doʊˈnʊr.i.əm / (AmE), / ˌɛn.dəʊˈnjʊə.ri.əm / (BrE)
the delicate layer of connective tissue that surrounds individual nerve fibers within a nerve
connective tissue around nerve fibers
medical

Examples

The endoneurium protects each nerve fiber from damage and provides support within the nerve.
During the dissection, the surgeon carefully avoided disturbing the endoneurium surrounding the axons.
Damage to the endoneurium can impair nerve function and slow down the healing process.

Etymology

From Greek 'endon' (within) + 'neuron' (nerve).
