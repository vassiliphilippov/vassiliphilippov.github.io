Lonicera xylosteum

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈnɪsərə zaɪˈlɒstiəm / (AmE), / ləˈnɪsərə zaɪˈlɒstiəm / (BrE)
A cultivated Eurasian shrub of the honeysuckle family, with paired yellowish-white flowers and scarlet berries, also known as fly honeysuckle.
a Eurasian shrub with twin yellowish-white flowers and red berries

Examples

The berries of the Lonicera xylosteum are bright red but considered mildly toxic to humans.
Gardeners often plant Lonicera xylosteum for its decorative flowers and dense foliage.
Lonicera xylosteum prefers shady woodlands and is native to parts of Central and Eastern Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fly honeysuckle - common English name for Lonicera xylosteum

Etymology

From Latin: 'Lonicera' after botanist Adam Lonicer, and 'xylosteum' meaning woody stem.
