Butea frondosa

1. NOUN

/ ˈbjuːti.ə frɒnˈdoʊsə / (AmE), / ˈbjuːti.ə frɒnˈdəʊsə / (BrE)
an East Indian tree (Butea monosperma) with bright vermilion, velvety flowers, yielding a yellow dye
East Indian tree with bright vermilion flowers

Examples

The Butea frondosa is celebrated for its striking vermilion blossoms during the spring.
Textile workers traditionally used dye extracted from Butea frondosa to color fabrics yellow.
Many birds visit Butea frondosa trees to feed on their nectar-laden flowers.

Etymology

From the genus name Butea (named after John Stuart, 3rd Earl of Bute) and Latin frondosa (leafy).
