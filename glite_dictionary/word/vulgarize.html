to vulgarize

1. VERB

/ ˈvʌlɡjəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈvʌlɡəˌraɪz / (BrE)
to make something simpler or less refined in order to appeal to the general public; to bring into common use, sometimes by lowering the level of sophistication
make more accessible or less refined for the public

Examples

The publisher decided to vulgarize the essay to reach a broader audience.
Critics argue that television tends to vulgarize classical literature when adapting it for mass consumption.
His goal was not to vulgarize science, but to make its concepts understandable without distortion.

Synset Examples

They popularized coffee in Washington State
Relativity Theory was vulgarized by these authors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to popularize - to make something widely known or used

Etymology

From Latin 'vulgaris' (common, of the multitude), via French 'vulgariser'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00972247-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈvʌlɡəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈvʌlɡəraɪz / (BrE)
to behave or act in a crude, coarse, or unrefined manner
to act in a crude or coarse manner

Examples

After a few drinks, he tends to vulgarize and offend those around him.
Critics accused the comedian of trying to vulgarize intellectual debates with crude jokes.
She worried that reality TV would vulgarize popular culture even further.

Synset Examples

The drunkard tends to vulgarize

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to coarsen - to make or become crude, rough, or unrefined
  • Antonym: to refine - to make something more cultured or elegant; to improve

Etymology

From French 'vulgariser', from Latin 'vulgaris' (common, ordinary).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00011691-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈvʌlɡəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈvʌlɡəraɪz / (BrE)
To make something less refined or sophisticated, often by lowering its quality, taste, or status.
make less refined or more common

Examples

Many critics argue that reality television has vulgarized popular culture.
Some believe that translating classic poetry can vulgarize its original beauty.
The press often vulgarizes serious issues to attract more readers.

Synset Examples

The Press has vulgarized Love and Marriage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to debase - reduce in quality or value; degrade

Etymology

From Latin 'vulgaris' (common, of the masses), via French 'vulgariser' and late Latin forms.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00583395-v
Synset POS: v
