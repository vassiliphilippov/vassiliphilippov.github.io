enthusiastically

1. ADV

/ ɪnˌθuziˈæstɪkli / (AmE), / ɪnˌθjuːziˈæstɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that shows intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval
in an eager, excited manner

Examples

The team cheered enthusiastically when their captain scored a goal.
She greeted the news enthusiastically, with a big smile and a clap.
Volunteers enthusiastically helped set up the community event despite the rainy weather.

Synset Examples

he extolled her virtues sky-high

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eagerly - in a keen or impatiently desirous manner
  • Antonym: apathetically - in a manner showing or feeling no interest or concern

Etymology

From 'enthusiastic' + '-ally'; 'enthusiastic' from Greek 'enthousiastēs' (person inspired by a god).

2. ADV

/ ɪnˌθuziˈæstɪkli / (AmE), / ɪnˌθjuːziˈæstɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that shows intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval.
in an enthusiastic manner

Examples

She accepted the invitation enthusiastically and began planning right away.
The team enthusiastically supported their coach's new strategy.
He enthusiastically volunteered for every community project he heard about.

Synset Examples

they discussed the question enthusiastically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eagerly - in a keen or enthusiastic manner
  • Antonym: apathetically - in a way that shows little or no emotion or enthusiasm

Etymology

Formed from 'enthusiastic' + '-ally', dating from the 18th century.
