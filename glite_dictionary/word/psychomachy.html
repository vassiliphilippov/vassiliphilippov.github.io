psychomachy

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈkɑːməki / (AmE), / sʌɪˈkɒməki / (BrE)
A conflict within the soul, especially as an inner struggle between good and evil.
internal struggle of the soul

Examples

Medieval literature often depicts the psychomachy as a battle between angels and demons within the human soul.
In his novel, the protagonist's psychomachy drives the narrative toward a moral resolution.
The concept of psychomachy illuminates many religious and philosophical texts discussing inner conflict.

Synset Examples

Later echoes of medieval psychomachy can be found in Shakespeare's 144th sonnet and in Tennyson's poem ‘The Two Voices’ (1842).

Etymology

From late Latin psychomachia, from Greek psukhē (soul) + -machia (battle).
