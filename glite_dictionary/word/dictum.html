dictum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪktəm / (AmE), / ˈdɪktəm / (BrE)
A formal or authoritative statement or pronouncement.
authoritative statement; pronouncement

Examples

The judge's dictum clarified how the law should be interpreted in similar cases.
She quoted a famous dictum to support her argument during the debate.
The leader's dictum became a guiding principle for the organization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maxim - a general truth or rule of conduct expressed concisely
  • Synonym: adage - a traditional saying expressing a common observation
  • Synonym: edict - an official order or proclamation issued by authority

Etymology

From Latin 'dictum' (a thing said), past participle of 'dicere' (to say).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪktəm / (AmE), / ˈdɪktəm / (BrE)
an opinion or remark made by a judge in a legal case that is not essential to the decision and therefore not legally binding as precedent
a judge's non-binding legal opinion
legal

Examples

The judge's dictum in this case provided useful guidance for future proceedings.
Although interesting, her remarks were considered merely a dictum and not a binding part of the verdict.
Legal scholars often debate the influence of a dictum on the development of new case law.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obiter dictum - a judge's incidental remark not central to the decision
  • Antonym: holding - a court's determination of a matter of law pivotal to its decision

Etymology

From Latin dictum, meaning 'saying' or 'remark'.
