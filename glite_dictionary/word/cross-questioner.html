cross-questioner

1. NOUN

/ krɔs ˈkwɛstʃənər / (AmE), / krɒs ˈkwɛstʃənə / (BrE)
a person who questions a witness closely, especially about statements made earlier, often in a legal context
person who questions a witness closely

Examples

The sharp cross-questioner quickly exposed inconsistencies in the witness's story.
As a cross-questioner, she was known for her attention to detail and relentless pursuit of the truth.
During the trial, the cross-questioner focused on the testimony given the previous day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cross-examiner - person who cross-examines a witness in court

Etymology

From 'cross-question' (to question closely or critically), with agentive suffix '-er'.
