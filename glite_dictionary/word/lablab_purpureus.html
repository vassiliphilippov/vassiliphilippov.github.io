Lablab purpureus

1. NOUN

/ læbˌlæb pɝːˈpjʊəriəs / (AmE), / læbˌlæb pjʊəˈpjʊəriəs / (BrE)
a perennial climbing plant of the pea family, native to the Old World tropics, with trifoliate leaves, fragrant purple flowers, and edible maroon pods; cultivated as an ornamental and vegetable, especially in the Indian subcontinent
climbing plant with edible pods and purple flowers

Examples

The seeds of Lablab purpureus are often used in curries and stews.
Gardeners prize Lablab purpureus for its vibrant, fragrant flowers.
In some regions, Lablab purpureus is grown both as an ornamental and a food crop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyacinth bean - common name for Lablab purpureus

Etymology

From Latin, combining lablab (bean name) and purpureus (purple), referring to its flower color.
