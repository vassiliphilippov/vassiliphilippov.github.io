six-gun

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪks ɡʌn / (AmE), / ˈsɪks ɡʌn / (BrE)
a revolver, especially one with six chambers for bullets
a revolver, usually with six chambers

Examples

He drew his six-gun and fired a warning shot into the air.
The cowboy always carried a trusty six-gun on his hip.
Collectors seek authentic six-guns from the Old West era.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revolver - a pistol with a revolving cylinder containing several chambers for bullets
