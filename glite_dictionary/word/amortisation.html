amortisation

1. NOUN

/ əˌmɔːrtəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌmɔːtɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of gradually paying off a debt or obligation through regular payments over a period of time
gradual repayment of a debt
british

Examples

The company planned for the amortisation of its bank loan over ten years.
Regular monthly payments will ensure the amortisation of your mortgage by the end of the term.
Changes in accounting rules affect the amortisation of intangible assets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amortization - US spelling of amortisation

Etymology

From French 'amortir' (to kill, extinguish) + '-isation'.

2. NOUN

/ əˌmɔːrtəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌmɔːtɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of gradually reducing the value of an asset or the amount of a debt by spreading payments or costs over a set period of time
gradual reduction of value or debt over time

Examples

The company used amortisation to allocate the cost of its patents over their useful life.
Annual reports often detail the amortisation of intangible assets such as trademarks and goodwill.
Effective amortisation helps businesses manage expenses and report accurate profits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depreciation - reduction in value of a tangible asset over time

Etymology

From Late Latin *amortisatio* (killing, extinction), from *amortizare* (to deaden).
