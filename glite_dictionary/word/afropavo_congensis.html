Afropavo congensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæfroʊˈpeɪvoʊ kɒnˈdʒɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌafrəʊˈpɑːvəʊ kɒnˈdʒɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A species of brightly colored peafowl native to the Congo Basin, also known as the Congo peafowl.
species of Congo peafowl

Examples

The Afropavo congensis is native to the dense rainforests of the Congo Basin.
Unlike other peafowl, both male and female Afropavo congensis have brilliant plumage.
Scientists are working to protect the habitat of the endangered Afropavo congensis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Congo peafowl - Common name for Afropavo congensis, a species of peafowl

Etymology

From Latin: 'Afropavo' (African peafowl) and 'congensis' (of the Congo).
