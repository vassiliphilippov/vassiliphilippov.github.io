limnologically

1. ADV

/ lɪm.nəˈlɑ.dʒɪ.kli / (AmE), / lɪm.nəˈlɒ.dʒɪ.kli / (BrE)
in a manner relating to the scientific study of inland waters (lakes, rivers, and wetlands)
in a manner relating to limnology

Examples

The research was conducted limnologically to assess the health of the freshwater ecosystem.
Their findings, interpreted limnologically, suggest significant changes in the lake's nutrient cycles.
He analyzed the river limnologically, focusing on water chemistry and aquatic organisms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limnology - the scientific study of inland waters

Etymology

From 'limnology' + '-ally', based on the Greek 'limnē' (lake, marsh).
