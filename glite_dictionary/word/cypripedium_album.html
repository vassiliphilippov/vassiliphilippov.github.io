Cypripedium album

1. NOUN

/ sɪˌprɪpiːdiəm ˈælbəm / (AmE), / sɪˌprɪpiːdiəm ˈælbəm / (BrE)
A pale pink wild orchid of northeastern America, known for its distinctive inflated, pouch-like lip.
pale pink North American wild orchid

Examples

The rare Cypripedium album can be found in certain protected forests of northeastern America.
Botanists are working hard to conserve the natural habitat of Cypripedium album.
The inflated pouchlike lip of the Cypripedium album makes it especially attractive to orchid enthusiasts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lady's slipper - a common name for orchids in the genus Cypripedium

Etymology

From Latin 'Cypripedium' (genus name, from Greek 'Kypris' [Aphrodite] and 'pedilon' [slipper]) and Latin 'album' (white).
