Jatropha stimulosus

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈtroʊfə stɪˈmjuːləsəs / (AmE), / dʒəˈtrɒfə stɪˈmjuːləsəs / (BrE)
a species of stinging herb native to tropical America, known for its irritating hairs.
stinging herb of tropical America

Examples

Many rural communities avoid contact with Jatropha stimulosus due to its stinging hairs.
Researchers are studying the effects of Jatropha stimulosus extracts on insect populations.
The field was overgrown with Jatropha stimulosus, making it difficult to walk through safely.

Etymology

From genus name 'Jatropha' (Greek iatros 'physician' + trophe 'nourishment') and Latin 'stimulosus' (prickly, stimulating).
