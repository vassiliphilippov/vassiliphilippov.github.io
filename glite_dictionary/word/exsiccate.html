to exsiccate

1. VERB

/ ɛkˈsɪkeɪt / (AmE), / ɛkˈsɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to remove moisture from something; to dry out completely
dry out completely; remove moisture

Examples

The botanist will exsiccate these plant samples for further analysis.
Specimens must be exsiccated to prevent decay during transport.
After several hours in the oven, the minerals had completely exsiccated.

Synset Examples

In the desert, you get dehydrated very quickly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dehydrate - to remove water from something; to become dry

Etymology

From Latin exsiccare, from ex- (out) + siccare (to dry), from siccus (dry).
