swathe

1. VERB

/ sweɪð / (AmE), / swɒð, sweɪð / (BrE)
to wrap or envelop completely, especially in cloth or bandages
wrap or envelop in cloth or coverings

Examples

The nurse gently swathed the newborn in a soft blanket.
Thick fog swathed the harbor, hiding the ships from view.
He swathed his injured arm with a clean bandage.

Synset Examples

swaddled the infant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrap - to cover or enclose something in material

Etymology

Old English swāðian (to bind or wrap), related to swath.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01302857-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ sweɪð / (AmE), / swɒð / (BrE)
a long strip of cloth used to wrap or bind something, especially as a bandage
enveloping bandage or strip of cloth

Examples

The nurse wrapped a fresh swathe around his injured arm.
Her leg was encased in a thick swathe of white gauze after the operation.
He carefully removed the swathe to check the healing wound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bandage - a strip of material used to bind a wound or protect an injured part

Etymology

From Old English 'swæth', related to 'swath', meaning a track or strip.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04376112-n
Synset POS: n
