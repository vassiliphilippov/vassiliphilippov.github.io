deadened

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛdənd / (AmE), / ˈdɛdənd / (BrE)
(of a feeling, sensation, or emotion) made or become less intense or less strong.
made less intense

Examples

After years of exposure, his senses became deadened to the noise of the city.
Painkillers left her with a deadened sensation in her arm.
The shock of the news was deadened by her calm demeanor.

Synset Examples

the deadened pangs of hunger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dull - to make (something) less intense or sharp
  • Antonym: intensified - made or become stronger or more intense

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɛdənd / (AmE), / ˈdɛdənd / (BrE)
lacking physical sensation or responsiveness; numb.
lacking sensation; numb

Examples

The dentist worked quickly on the deadened nerve in her tooth.
After the accident, he had a deadened feeling in his left leg.
Her emotions seemed deadened by years of hardship.

Synset Examples

his gums were dead from the novocain
she felt no discomfort as the dentist drilled her deadened tooth
a public desensitized by continuous television coverage of atrocities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: numb - unable to feel, especially as a result of cold or anesthesia

Etymology

From the verb 'deaden' (to make dead or less active), derived from 'dead'.
