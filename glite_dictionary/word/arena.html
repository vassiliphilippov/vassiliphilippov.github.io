arena

1. NOUN

/ əˈriːnə / (AmE), / əˈriːnə / (BrE)
In ancient Rome, the central area of an amphitheater where contests and spectacles took place, originally covered with sand.
central area of a Roman amphitheater

Examples

In ancient Rome, gladiators fought in the arena before cheering crowds.
The sand of the arena was stained with the marks of the fierce battle.
Historians have reconstructed what the original arena at the Colosseum might have looked like.

Etymology

From Latin 'harena' or 'arena' (sand, sandy place), referring to the sand covering the floor.

2. NOUN

/ əˈrinə / (AmE), / əˈriːnə / (BrE)
a large enclosed area, often with tiered seats, used for sports events, concerts, or other entertainment
sports/events venue

Examples

The boxing match will take place in the new arena downtown.
Thousands of fans filled the arena to watch their team compete in the finals.
After the concert, the arena was quickly cleared for the next event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stadium - a large structure with rows of seats for spectators, used for sports or other events
  • Synonym: field - an open area used for sports; a playing field

Etymology

From Latin 'harena' (sand, sandy place, arena), originally referring to the sand covering the floor of such venues.
