antiknock

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.taɪˌnɑk / (AmE), / ˈæn.tiˌnɒk / (BrE)
a compound added to gasoline to reduce engine knocking or pinging
compound reducing engine knocking

Examples

This fuel contains an antiknock additive, which helps engines run more smoothly.
The introduction of tetraethyl lead was once a common antiknock solution.
Using antiknock compounds improved both mileage and engine longevity.

Etymology

From 'anti-' (against) + 'knock' (engine knocking).

2. ADJ

/ ˈæn.ti.nɑk / (AmE), / ˈæn.ti.nɒk / (BrE)
(of a substance or property) suppressing or preventing engine knock in combustion engines.
suppressing engine knock

Examples

The new fuel contains an antiknock additive to improve engine performance.
Engineers tested the antiknock properties of several gasoline blends.
The antiknock rating is crucial when evaluating fuel for high-compression engines.

Synset Examples

antiknock properties
antiknock rating

Related Terms

  • Synonym: octane rating - a measure of a fuel's ability to resist engine knock

Etymology

From the prefix "anti-" meaning against, and "knock" referring to engine knocking.
