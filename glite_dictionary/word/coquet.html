coquet

1. VERB

/ koʊˈkɛt / (AmE), / kəʊˈkɛt / (BrE)
to behave flirtatiously, especially without serious intentions
behave flirtatiously
archaic

Examples

She would often coquet with admirers at the parties, enjoying the attention but never pursuing anything serious.
He seemed to coquet with every woman he met, charming but noncommittal.
They watched her coquet with the young gentlemen, drawing laughs and glances throughout the afternoon.

Synset Examples

The guys always try to chat up the new secretaries
My husband never flirts with other women

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flirt - to behave as if attracted to someone, but not seriously

Etymology

From French 'coqueter', from 'coquet' (flirt), diminutive of 'coq' (rooster).

2. NOUN

/ koʊˈkɛt / (AmE), / kəʊˈkɛt / (BrE)
a man who behaves in a seductive manner, especially one who uses his charm to exploit women
seductively manipulative man

Examples

He was known as a coquet who always had women falling for his charms.
Some saw him as a harmless flirt, others as a skillful coquet preying on gullible women.
The story follows a notorious coquet and his string of ruined relationships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coquette - a flirtatious woman, especially one who exploits men
  • Synonym: seducer - a man who entices others into sexual activity

Etymology

French, masculine of 'coquette', from 'coq' (rooster).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
