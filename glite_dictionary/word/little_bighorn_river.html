Little Bighorn River

1. PROPN

/ ˈlɪt̬əl ˈbaɪˌhɔrn ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈlɪtl ˈbaɪˌhɔːn ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river flowing from northern Wyoming into the Bighorn River in southern Montana, notable as the site of the Battle of the Little Bighorn
river in Wyoming and Montana, USA

Examples

The Little Bighorn River is famous as the site of Custer's Last Stand in 1876.
Many tourists visit the Little Bighorn River to learn about its historical significance.
The banks of the Little Bighorn River are rich in wildlife and natural beauty.
