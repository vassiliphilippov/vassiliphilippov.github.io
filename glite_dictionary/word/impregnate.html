to impregnate

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈprɛɡˌneɪt / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɛɡ.neɪt / (BrE)
to make (a woman or female animal) pregnant
make someone pregnant

Examples

The scientist managed to impregnate the female mouse in the laboratory.
She became upset when she discovered that he had impregnated another woman.
Many farmers use artificial means to impregnate their cattle.

Synset Examples

He impregnated his wife again

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fertilize - to cause (an egg, female animal, or plant) to develop a new individual by introducing male reproductive material

Etymology

From Latin 'impregnare', from 'in-' (in) + 'pregnare' (to make pregnant), from 'praegnans' (pregnant).

3. VERB

/ ɪmˈprɛɡˌneɪt / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɛɡ.neɪt / (BrE)
(of a male or external agent) to fertilize an egg and initiate the process of development; to make (an egg or female) pregnant by introducing sperm or pollen.
to fertilize and start development

Examples

In artificial breeding, technicians often impregnate animals using donor sperm.
The scientist explained how pollen can impregnate an ovule to create new plants.
Once the egg is impregnated, it begins to divide rapidly.

Synset Examples

the egg was impregnated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fertilize - to cause an egg or plant seed to develop by joining with a male cell

Etymology

From Late Latin 'impregnatus', past participle of 'impregnare', from Latin 'in-' (in) + 'praegnans' (pregnant).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ɪmˈprɛɡ.neɪt / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɛɡ.neɪt / (BrE)
to fill something with a particular quality, substance, or feeling
fill with a quality or substance

Examples

The vivid colors impregnate the fabrics, making them even more striking.
Her words impregnated the atmosphere with hope and optimism.
The scent of jasmine impregnated the entire garden at dusk.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to permeate - to spread throughout something; to pervade

Etymology

From Latin 'impregnare' (to make fertile, to fill, imbue).
