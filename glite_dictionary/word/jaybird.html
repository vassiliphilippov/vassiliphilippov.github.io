jaybird

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒeɪˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪˌbɜːd / (BrE)
A bird of the jay family found in eastern North America, known for its bright blue plumage and grey breast; commonly refers to the blue jay.
North American blue jay bird

Examples

A bright jaybird perched on the fence, chirping loudly for all to hear.
We spotted several jaybirds in the backyard, searching for acorns and insects.
The vivid blue feathers of the jaybird caught the sunlight as it flitted through the trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue jay - A common North American jay with blue and white plumage

Etymology

Formed from 'jay' (the type of bird) + 'bird'.
