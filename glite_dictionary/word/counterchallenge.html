to counterchallenge

1. VERB

/ ˌkaʊntərˈtʃælɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntəˈtʃælɪndʒ / (BrE)
to challenge in response to a previous challenge
challenge in return

Examples

When the company was accused of fraud, it immediately counterchallenged the claims made by its rival.
The witness counterchallenged the prosecutor's assumptions during the cross-examination.
After being questioned about her credentials, Dr. Lee counterchallenged with evidence of her qualifications.

Synset Examples

The authentication was counterchallenged

Related Terms

  • Synonym: challenge - to invite someone to compete or prove something

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'counter-' (against, in return) and 'challenge'; modeled on similar formations.
