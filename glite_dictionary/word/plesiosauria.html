Plesiosauria

1. NOUN

/ pliːzi.oʊˈsɔːri.ə / (AmE), / pliːzɪəˈsɔːrɪə / (BrE)
an order of extinct marine reptiles with broad bodies, long necks, and flippers, that lived during the Mesozoic Era
order of extinct marine reptiles

Examples

The fossil record of Plesiosauria provides valuable insight into marine life of the Mesozoic Era.
Scientists discovered a nearly complete skeleton belonging to the order Plesiosauria in the sedimentary rock.
Members of Plesiosauria were known for their distinctive long necks and paddle-like limbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plesiosaur - any reptile of the order Plesiosauria

Etymology

From Greek 'plēsios' (near) + 'sauros' (lizard), meaning 'near to lizards'.

2. NOUN

/ pliːsi.oʊˈsɔːri.ə / (AmE), / pliːsɪ.əˈsɔːri.ə / (BrE)
A suborder of extinct, large marine reptiles known as plesiosaurs, having a small head, long neck, broad body, and flippers; lived during the Mesozoic Era.
suborder of extinct marine reptiles (plesiosaurs)

Examples

The fossils of the Plesiosauria reveal much about ancient marine life.
Scientists classify these long-necked predators as belonging to the suborder Plesiosauria.
Many members of the Plesiosauria were apex predators in their ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plesiosaur - an extinct marine reptile with a long neck and small head

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'plēsios' (near) + 'sauros' (lizard).
