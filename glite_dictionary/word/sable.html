sable

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈseɪbl̩ / (BrE)
a very dark shade of black, often used to describe color, especially in heraldry and literature.
a very dark shade of black

Examples

The artist chose sable to create a stark background for the portrait.
Her gown was a rich sable that shimmered subtly in the candlelight.
The flag was divided into white and sable quarters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black - the darkest color, the absence of light

Etymology

From Old French *sable*, from Middle Low German *sabel*, ultimately from Slavic origins.

The sable is prized for its rich, dark brown fur.

Related Terms
