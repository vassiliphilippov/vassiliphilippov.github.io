rampage

1. NOUN

/ ˈræmˌpeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈræmpeɪdʒ / (BrE)
a period of violent and uncontrollable behavior, often involving damage or destruction
violent and uncontrollable behavior

Examples

The angry mob went on a rampage through the city streets.
Several stores were looted during the rampage that lasted for hours.
After being provoked, the animal broke free and went on a wild rampage across the farm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frenzy - a state of uncontrolled excitement or wild behavior
  • Synonym: riot - a violent disturbance of the peace by a crowd
  • Antonym: calm - a state of peace and tranquility, absence of violence

2. VERB

/ ˈræmˌpeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈræm.peɪdʒ / (BrE)
to move through a place in a violent, uncontrolled, or destructive way
act violently or destructively

Examples

Angry protesters began to rampage through the city, smashing windows and overturning cars.
The escaped animals rampaged across the farm, causing chaos in their wake.
Football fans rampaged after their team lost the match, leaving a trail of damage downtown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wreak havoc - cause great damage or destruction
  • Synonym: to devastate - destroy or ruin something completely

Etymology

Originated as a noun in the 18th century, possibly from ramp (to rush wildly) + -age (forming nouns).
