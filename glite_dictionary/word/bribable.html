bribable

1. ADJ

/ ˈbraɪ.bə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈbraɪ.bə.bəl / (BrE)
easily influenced by bribery; capable of being corrupted or persuaded by a bribe.
susceptible to bribery or corruption

Examples

The investigation exposed several bribable officials in the organization.
She refused to deal with anyone who seemed even remotely bribable.
A bribable judge can undermine faith in the entire justice system.

Synset Examples

corruptible judges
dishonest politicians
a purchasable senator
a venal police officer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corruptible - easily corrupted or bribed
  • Antonym: incorruptible - not susceptible to corruption or bribery

Etymology

From bribe + -able, patterned after the formation of other adjectives meaning capable of being acted on.
