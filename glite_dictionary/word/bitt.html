bitt

1. NOUN

/ bɪt / (AmE), / bɪt / (BrE)
a strong post on a ship or quay for securing mooring lines
post for securing mooring lines (nautical)

Examples

The crew tied the rope securely around the bitt on the deck.
When docking the vessel, the captain instructed the sailors to use the bitt for the mooring lines.
A heavy chain was fastened to the bitt at the edge of the pier.

Synset Examples

the road was closed to vehicular traffic with bollards

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bollard - a short post used to prevent vehicles from entering an area or for mooring boats

Etymology

Probably from Middle Dutch 'bitte', meaning a post or stake.

2. VERB

/ bɪt / (AmE), / bɪt / (BrE)
(Nautical) To secure (a rope or cable) by winding it around a bitt, a post on the deck of a ship.
fasten with a ship's bitt

Examples

The crew quickly bitted the mooring line as the ship docked.
Before leaving the harbor, the sailors bitted the anchor cable for safety.
He gave orders to bitt the heavy rope to secure the vessel.

Synset Examples

bitt the ship line

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fasten - to attach or secure firmly

Etymology

From the noun 'bitt', referring to a strong post on a ship's deck, probably of Scandinavian origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01289157-v
Synset POS: v
