tetrasaccharide

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛtrəˈsækəˌraɪd / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˈsækəraɪd / (BrE)
a carbohydrate that yields four monosaccharide units when completely hydrolysed
carbohydrate yielding four monosaccharides
medical

Examples

The scientist identified a new tetrasaccharide in the plant extract.
Complete hydrolysis of this tetrasaccharide produced four separate monosaccharides.
Researchers are studying the role of tetrasaccharides in cellular communication.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: trisaccharide - a carbohydrate yielding three monosaccharide units on hydrolysis
  • Degree Higher: pentasaccharide - a carbohydrate yielding five monosaccharide units on hydrolysis

Etymology

From Greek 'tetra-' (four) and 'sacchar' (sugar), with -ide denoting a chemical compound.
