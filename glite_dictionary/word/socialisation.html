socialisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊʃələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsəʊʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a person learns and adopts the customs, values, and behaviors of their society or social group
the process of learning societal norms
british

Examples

Primary socialisation takes place within the family, teaching children basic values and expectations.
Schools play a significant role in the socialisation of young people.
Rapid changes in technology have affected the way socialisation occurs among teenagers.

Synset Examples

the socialization of children to the norms of their culture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: socialization - US spelling of 'socialisation'

Etymology

From French 'socialisation', from Latin 'socialis' (of companionship, of allies), relating to 'socius' (companion).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊʃələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsəʊʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of meeting and engaging with others for social purposes; participation in social activities.
the act of meeting for social purposes
british

Examples

The party offered plenty of opportunities for socialisation among new employees.
Clubs and team sports encourage socialisation outside of the classroom.
Too much socialisation during work hours can reduce productivity.

Synset Examples

there was too much socialization with the enlisted men

Related Terms

  • Synonym: socialization - the act of meeting for social purposes (American spelling)

Etymology

From French 'socialisation', from 'socialiser' (to make social), ultimately from Latin 'socialis'.

3. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊʃələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsəʊʃəlʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of transferring private property or businesses to collective or government ownership, especially according to socialist principles
conversion to collective or state ownership
british

Examples

After the revolution, there was a rapid socialisation of all major industries.
The socialisation of healthcare aimed to make services accessible to everyone.
Debates arose regarding the benefits and drawbacks of the socialisation of railways.

Synset Examples

the socialization of medical services

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nationalization - the process of bringing an industry or assets under government control
  • Antonym: privatization - the transfer of business, industry, or service from public to private ownership and control

Etymology

From French 'socialisation', based on 'socialiser' (to make social) and 'social' (pertaining to society).

Source: WordNet
