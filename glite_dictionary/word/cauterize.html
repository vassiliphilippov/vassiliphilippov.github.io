to cauterize

1. VERB

/ ˈkɔːtəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɔːtəraɪz / (BrE)
to burn or destroy tissue using a hot iron, electric current, or caustic substance, typically to stop bleeding or prevent infection
burn or destroy tissue for medical purposes
medical

Examples

The surgeon decided to cauterize the wound to stop the bleeding.
If the tissue becomes infected, the doctor may cauterize it to prevent the spread of bacteria.
Early medicine often used heated metal instruments to cauterize wounds after surgery.

Synset Examples

The surgeon cauterized the wart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burn - to damage or destroy by fire or heat

Etymology

From Greek kauteriazein (to brand with a hot iron), from kautēr (burning or branding iron).

2. VERB

/ ˈkɔtəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɔːtəraɪz / (BrE)
to burn, sear, or freeze tissue, typically to stop bleeding or prevent infection, using a hot instrument, electric current, or chemical agent
burn tissue to stop bleeding or infection
medical

Examples

The surgeon decided to cauterize the wound to stop the bleeding.
In some cases, doctors may cauterize tissue to prevent infection from spreading.
The veterinarian cauterized the injury to seal the blood vessels.

Synset Examples

The surgeon cauterized the wart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burn - to damage or destroy by fire or heat

Etymology

From Greek kautērion (branding iron), from kaiein (to burn), via Latin cauterizare.

3. VERB

/ ˈkɔːtəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɔːtəraɪz / (BrE)
to make someone insensitive or unfeeling; to deaden someone's emotions or moral sensibilities
make someone unfeeling or insensitive

Examples

Repeated exposure to violence can cauterize one's sense of compassion.
Years of disappointment had cauterized his hopes and feelings.
She tried to cauterize her conscience to avoid feeling any guilt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to harden - make or become emotionally insensitive or less affected by feelings
  • Antonym: to soften - make or become more sensitive, gentle, or compassionate

Etymology

From Greek kautēriazein (to burn with a hot iron), from kautēr (burning or branding iron).
