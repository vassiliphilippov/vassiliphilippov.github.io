chimaera

1. NOUN

/ kaɪˈmɪrə / (AmE), / kɪˈmɪərə / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a fire-breathing female monster with the head of a lion, the body of a goat, and the tail of a serpent.
mythical monstrous creature (lion, goat, serpent)

Examples

The ancient vase depicted the hero battling the chimaera in vivid detail.
According to legend, the chimaera terrorized the people of Lycia.
Many artists have painted their own interpretations of the fearsome chimaera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chimera - alternate spelling, same mythological monster

Etymology

From Greek Khimaira (mythical she-goat or monster).

2. NOUN

/ kaɪˈmɪərə / (AmE), / kaɪˈmɪərə / (BrE)
An imagined monster or grotesque fantasy, especially something hoped for but impossible to achieve.
grotesque or fanciful imaginary creation

Examples

The idea of a perfect society is sometimes dismissed as a chimaera by critics.
His plans to solve the problem overnight proved to be a chimaera.
She filled her novel with chimaeras and fantastical visions that enchanted readers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fantasy - something produced by the imagination, especially an impossible or improbable idea
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

From Greek 'khimaira', a mythical fire-breathing creature.

3. NOUN

/ kaɪˈmɪrə / (AmE), / kɪˈmɪərə / (BrE)
A deep-sea fish with a slender body, smooth skin, and a long, threadlike tail, closely related to sharks.
deep-sea fish with tapering body and long tail

Examples

The chimaera glided silently through the inky blackness of the ocean depths.
Scientists were excited to discover a new species of chimaera off the coast of New Zealand.
Because of its strange appearance, the chimaera is often called a "ghost shark."

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ratfish - another name for chimaera fish species
  • Degree Lower: shark - cartilaginous fish related to chimaeras

Etymology

From Latin 'chimaera', from Greek 'khimaira' (she-goat, mythological monster).
