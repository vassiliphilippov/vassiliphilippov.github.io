to strive

1. VERB

/ straɪv / (AmE), / straɪv / (BrE)
to make a great effort to achieve or obtain something
make a determined effort

Examples

Despite many setbacks, she continued to strive for excellence in her work.
Scientists around the world strive to find solutions to pressing global challenges.
He has always striven to be honest and fair in his dealings with others.

Synset Examples

straining our ears to hear

Related Terms

  • Synonym: struggle - to try very hard to do something difficult
  • Antonym: to relax - to stop making an effort or to rest

Etymology

From Old French *estriver* (quarrel, compete), from a Germanic source.

2. VERB

/ straɪv / (AmE), / straɪv / (BrE)
to make great efforts to achieve or obtain something
make a great effort

Examples

Despite many obstacles, she continued to strive for success.
The company always strives to provide the best customer service possible.
He had striven for years to master the piano.

Synset Examples

we endeavor to make our customers happy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endeavor - to try hard to do or achieve something
  • Synonym: attempt - to make an effort to achieve or complete something
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to care for or attempt something

Etymology

From Old French 'estriver' (to quarrel, compete), of Germanic origin.
