Entomophthora

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛntəˌmɑfˈθɔrə / (AmE), / ˌɛntəməfˈθɔːrə / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Entomophthoraceae, known for being parasitic on insects.
genus of insect-parasitic fungi

Examples

The fungus Entomophthora infects flies and other insects, often resulting in their death.
Researchers study Entomophthora as a potential biological control for pest insects.
During the late summer, outbreaks of Entomophthora can decimate local insect populations.

Etymology

From Greek 'entomon' (insect) and 'phthora' (destruction), referring to insect-destroying properties.
