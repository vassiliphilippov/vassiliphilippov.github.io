envelope

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.vəˌloʊp / (AmE), / ˈɛn.və.ləʊp / (BrE)
a flat, usually rectangular paper or plastic container used to enclose a letter, document, or small object
paper container for letters or documents

Examples

She carefully placed the invitation inside a envelope and sealed it.
Please write your address on the back of the envelope.
A thick envelope arrived in the mail this morning containing the official documents.

Etymology

From French 'enveloppe', from 'envelopper' meaning 'to wrap up'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.vəˌloʊp / (AmE), / ˈɛn.və.ləʊp / (BrE)
a layer or covering surrounding something, especially a layer of gas or fluid around an object
a surrounding layer, especially gas or fluid

Examples

The comet's envelope was visible through the telescope as a faint halo of gas.
A protective envelope of atmosphere shields the planet from harmful radiation.
Scientists studied the envelope of plasma surrounding the star.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.vəˌloʊp / (AmE), / ˈɛn.və.ləʊp / (BrE)
A curve that is tangent to each member of a given family of curves, forming their boundary or limit.
curve tangent to a family of curves

Examples

The parabola serves as the envelope of this family of straight lines.
In advanced geometry, students study the envelope formed by shifting ellipses.
The concept of an envelope helps in visualizing the outer limits of multiple curves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tangent - a straight line that touches a curve at one point without crossing it

the maximum performance limits or capabilities within which a system or device, especially an aircraft, can safely operate
maximum operating limits (esp. of a system or aircraft)

Examples

Test pilots are always looking for new ways to push the envelope of their aircraft.
The engineers warned that operating the system beyond its envelope could be dangerous.
Innovative companies constantly try to expand the envelope of what's technologically possible.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
