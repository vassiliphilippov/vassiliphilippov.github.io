bowsprit

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊˌsprɪt / (AmE), / ˈbaʊ.sprɪt / (BrE)
a spar that extends forward from the bow of a ship, used to secure sails or rigging
spar projecting from a ship's bow

Examples

The ship's bowsprit jutted out over the crashing waves.
He carefully inspected the bowsprit for signs of damage after the storm.
Older sailing vessels often had an ornately carved bowsprit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spar - a strong pole used on a ship to support sails or rigging

Etymology

From Middle Low German *bōchsprēt* ('bow pole').
