Clintonia borealis

1. NOUN

/ klɪnˈtoʊniə bɔːˈriːəlɪs / (AmE), / klɪnˈtəʊniə bɔːˈrɪəlɪs / (BrE)
a woodland plant native to temperate North America, having yellow, nodding flowers and small round blue fruits
woodland plant with yellow flowers and blue fruit

Examples

We spotted a patch of Clintonia borealis while hiking through the forest.
The bright blue berries of Clintonia borealis stand out against its broad leaves in late summer.
Botanists often study Clintonia borealis to monitor the health of woodland ecosystems.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Clintonia', honoring DeWitt Clinton, and Latin 'borealis' meaning 'northern'.
