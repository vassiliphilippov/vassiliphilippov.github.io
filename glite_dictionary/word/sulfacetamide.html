sulfacetamide

1. NOUN

/ sʌl.fəˈsiː.təˌmaɪd / (AmE), / sʌl.fəˈsiː.təˌmaɪd / (BrE)
a sulfonamide antibiotic used to treat bacterial infections, especially of the eyes and skin
an antibiotic for bacterial eye and skin infections
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed sulfacetamide eye drops to treat my conjunctivitis.
Some people may experience mild irritation after applying sulfacetamide ointment to the skin.
If you are allergic to sulfa drugs, you should avoid medications containing sulfacetamide.

Etymology

From 'sulfa' (referring to sulfonamide drugs) + 'acetamide' (a chemical group).
