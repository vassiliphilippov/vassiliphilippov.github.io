hematology

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiː.məˈtɑː.lə.dʒi / (AmE), / ˌhiː.məˈtɒl.ə.dʒi / (BrE)
the branch of medicine concerned with the study and treatment of disorders of the blood and blood-forming organs
medical study of blood and blood diseases
medical

Examples

She decided to specialize in hematology after medical school.
Recent advances in hematology have improved the treatment of blood disorders.
The hospital's hematology department is renowned for research in leukemia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hematologist - a doctor specializing in hematology

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + -logy (study of).
