laden

1. ADJ

/ ˈleɪdən / (AmE), / ˈleɪdn̩ / (BrE)
heavily loaded with or having a large amount of something
heavily loaded or burdened

Examples

The table was laden with delicious food during the festival.
He struggled to carry the laden suitcase up the stairs.
Her voice was laden with emotion as she spoke of her childhood.

Synset Examples

a tray loaded with dishes
table laden with food
‘ladened’ is not current usage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loaded - carrying or bearing a load
  • Synonym: burdened - carrying a heavy weight or responsibility
  • Antonym: unburdened - not carrying a heavy load or responsibility

Etymology

From Old English 'hladen', past participle of 'hladan' (to load).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01089350-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈleɪdn̩ / (AmE), / ˈleɪd(ə)n / (BrE)
heavily burdened with a particular feeling, quality, or mental state
burdened emotionally or mentally

Examples

After the accident, she was laden with guilt for months.
His voice sounded laden with sadness as he spoke of his past.
The air in the room felt laden with tension before the announcement.

Synset Examples

laden with grief
oppressed by a sense of failure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burdened - weighed down by something, emotionally or physically
  • Synonym: oppressed - weighed down by adverse emotions or circumstances

Etymology

Past participle of Old English ladian (to load).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00873280-s
Synset POS: s

3. VERB

/ ˈleɪdən / (AmE), / ˈleɪdən / (BrE)
to put a heavy or full load on or into something
to place a heavy load on

Examples

The workers laden the ship with containers every morning.
After the harvest, the farmers laden the carts with fresh produce.
The moving crew ladened the truck until it was completely full.

Synset Examples

load a car
load the truck with hay

Related Terms

  • Synonym: load - to put a load or cargo onto; to fill with a load

Etymology

Old English *hladen* (to heap up, load).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01492646-v
Synset POS: v
