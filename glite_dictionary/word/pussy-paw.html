pussy-paw

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʊsi pɔ / (AmE), / ˈpʊsi pɔː / (BrE)
A plant native to western North America, known for its pink clusters of densely packed flowers on prostrate stems that resemble the pads of a cat's feet.
western North American plant with pink clustered flowers

Examples

Clusters of pussy-paw bloom in the spring along the forest floor.
We saw patches of pussy-paw during our hike in the Sierra Nevada.
The soft, pink flowers of pussy-paw brightened the undergrowth beneath the pine trees.

Etymology

From 'pussy' (cat) + 'paw', referring to the flower clusters' resemblance to a cat's footpad.
