Parthenium

1. NOUN

/ pɑːrˈθiːniəm / (AmE), / pɑːˈθiːniəm / (BrE)
A genus of North American herbs and shrubs, typically with small terminal panicles of ray flowers, belonging to the family Asteraceae.
genus of North American herbs and shrubs

Examples

Parthenium species are commonly found in disturbed areas across North America.
Many gardeners struggle to control the invasive Parthenium weed in their fields.
The flowers of Parthenium are small and typically arranged in flat-topped clusters.

Etymology

From Greek parthenion, a plant name, perhaps related to 'parthenos' (virgin).

2. NOUN

/ pɑrˈθiːniəm / (AmE), / pɑːˈθiːniəm / (BrE)
A genus of North American herbs and shrubs, characterized by terminal clusters of small ray flowers, including several species such as Parthenium hysterophorus.
a genus of North American herbs and shrubs

Examples

Parthenium hysterophorus is a well-known invasive species in many parts of the world.
The botanist specialized in the study of the Parthenium genus and its environmental impacts.
We found several specimens of Parthenium growing alongside the native grasses in the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feverfew - a plant commonly known as feverfew, in the genus Tanacetum, previously classified under Parthenium

Etymology

From Latin 'parthenium', from Greek 'parthenion', traditionally associated with the plant feverfew.
