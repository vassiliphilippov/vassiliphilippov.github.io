counterintelligence

1. NOUN

/ ˌkaʊntərɪnˈtɛlədʒəns / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntərɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns / (BrE)
Activities aimed at detecting and preventing espionage, sabotage, or other actions by hostile intelligence organizations.
spy detection and prevention activities

Examples

The agency strengthened its counterintelligence operations after discovering a breach in their security protocols.
He specialized in counterintelligence, working to uncover enemy spies within the country.
During the Cold War, counterintelligence activities were crucial to national security.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: espionage - the practice of spying, typically by governments to obtain political or military information

Etymology

Formed from 'counter-' (against) and 'intelligence' (in the sense of information gathering or espionage).
