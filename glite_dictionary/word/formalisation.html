formalisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɔːrmələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɔːməlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something formal by establishing explicit rules or procedures, especially in language, theory, or organization
making something formal by rules or procedures
british

Examples

The formalisation of the company's code of conduct helped clarify expectations for all employees.
Mathematics often requires the formalisation of intuitive concepts into precise definitions and axioms.
The committee recommended the formalisation of procedures to ensure consistency in decision-making.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: formalization - the process of making something formal (US spelling)
  • Antonym: informalisation - the process of making something less formal

Etymology

From formal + -isation, modelled after 'formalise', influenced by French 'formalisation'.
