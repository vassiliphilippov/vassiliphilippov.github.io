Dadaism

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːdəˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdɑːdəˌɪzəm / (BrE)
an avant-garde art movement of the early 20th century, characterized by a rejection of traditional aesthetics and logic, often promoting irrationality and nonsense
early 20th-century avant-garde art movement

Examples

Many artists embraced Dadaism as a protest against the horrors of World War I.
The gallery held an exhibition showcasing works inspired by Dadaism.
Some critics dismissed Dadaism as nonsensical, while others praised its innovative spirit.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Surrealism - a 20th-century avant-garde movement in art and literature that sought to release the creative potential of the unconscious mind

Etymology

From French 'Dadaïsme', derived from 'dada', a playful word chosen by its founders.
