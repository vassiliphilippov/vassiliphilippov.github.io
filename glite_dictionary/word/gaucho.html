gaucho

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡaʊtʃoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡaʊtʃəʊ / (BrE)
a cowboy of the South American Pampas, especially in Argentina, Uruguay, and southern Brazil
a cowboy from the South American Pampas

Examples

The gaucho rode across the open plains, herding cattle under the wide sky.
Traditional gaucho attire includes bombachas, a wide-brimmed hat, and boots.
She learned about the life of a gaucho while traveling through Argentina.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowboy - a person who herds and tends cattle, especially in the western US

Etymology

From Spanish 'gaucho', of uncertain origin, possibly from Quechua or Mapuche.
