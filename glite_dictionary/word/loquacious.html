loquacious

1. ADJ

/ loʊˈkweɪʃəs / (AmE), / ləˈkweɪʃəs / (BrE)
tending to talk a great deal; talkative, often about trivial matters
talkative; fond of trivial conversation

Examples

Despite her shy appearance, Maria can be quite loquacious once she feels comfortable.
The loquacious host kept the conversation lively throughout the dinner party.
He found his new colleague too loquacious, often distracted by endless small talk.

Synset Examples

kept from her housework by gabby neighbors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chatty - informal; fond of talking in a friendly, informal way
  • Antonym: taciturn - reserved or uncommunicative in speech
  • Synonym: garrulous - excessively or pointlessly talkative

Etymology

From Latin loquax (talkative), from loqui (to speak) + -ous.
