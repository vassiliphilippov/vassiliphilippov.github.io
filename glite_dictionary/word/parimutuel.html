parimutuel

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæriˈmjuːtʃuəl / (AmE), / ˌparɪˈmjuːtʃʊəl / (BrE)
A betting system in which all bets of a particular type are pooled, and winnings are shared among those who made successful bets, after deductions.
a system of pooled betting

Examples

Betting at the racetrack is usually conducted using the parimutuel system.
The parimutuel allowed for fairer odds, as all bettors shared the winnings proportionally.
Some countries have legalized parimutuel betting on sports other than horse racing.

Etymology

From French 'pari mutuel' (mutual betting).
