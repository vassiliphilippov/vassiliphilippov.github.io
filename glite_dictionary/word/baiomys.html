Baiomys

1. NOUN

/ baɪˈoʊmɪs / (AmE), / baɪˈəʊmɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small rodents in North and Central America known as pygmy mice.
genus of pygmy mice

Examples

The genus Baiomys includes some of the smallest rodents native to North America.
Researchers studied the behavior of Baiomys in their natural habitat.
Unlike other mice, Baiomys are adapted to drier environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mouse - small rodent in the family Muridae
  • Synonym: rodent - a mammal of the order Rodentia, characterized by strong constantly growing incisors

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Greek 'baios' (small) and 'mys' (mouse).

2. NOUN

/ baɪˈoʊmɪs / (AmE), / baɪˈəʊmɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small rodents known as pygmy mice, found in parts of North and Central America.
genus of small rodents (pygmy mice)

Examples

The Baiomys genus includes some of the smallest mice in North America.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Baiomys genus in Mexico.
Unlike other rodents, Baiomys mice are adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pygmy mouse - any small rodent belonging to the genus Baiomys

Etymology

From Greek 'baios' (small, little) and 'mys' (mouse).
