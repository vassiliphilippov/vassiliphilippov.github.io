sandboy

1. NOUN

/ ˈsændˌbɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈsand.bɔɪ / (BrE)
A historical term for a boy or young man who sold sand, especially for spreading on floors, now used chiefly in the phrase 'happy as a sandboy' meaning extremely happy.
boy or young man who sold sand (historic)
archaic british

Examples

The cheerful sandboy delivered fresh sand to the local tavern each morning.
In Victorian London, a sandboy could often be seen carrying heavy sacks through the streets.
The phrase 'happy as a sandboy' originates from the supposedly joyful demeanor of these historic sand sellers.

Etymology

From 'sand' + 'boy', referring to youths who sold sand for household use in Britain during the 18th and 19th centuries.
