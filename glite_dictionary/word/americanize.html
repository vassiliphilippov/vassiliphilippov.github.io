to Americanize

1. VERB

/ əˈmɛr.ɪ.kəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / əˈmɛr.ɪ.kə.naɪz / (BrE)
to make something or someone American in character, form, or style; to adopt American customs, values, or institutions
make American in character or style

Examples

Living in California for a decade has thoroughly Americanized her tastes and habits.
Many companies try to Americanize their products to appeal to U.S. consumers.
Some critics argue that globalization is helping to Americanize cultures around the world.

Synset Examples

The year in the US has completely Americanized him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anglicize - to make English in form or character
  • Synonym: to Europeanize - to make European in character or adopt European customs

Etymology

From 'American' + '-ize', formed in English after similar terms (e.g., 'anglicize').

2. VERB

/ əˈmɛr.ɪ.kəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / əˈmɛr.ɪ.kə.naɪz / (BrE)
to become or make more characteristic of the United States or its people, customs, or culture
to become or make American in character

Examples

After moving to New York, he gradually began to Americanize his accent and habits.
Several companies chose to Americanize their brands to appeal to the U.S. market.
As immigrants spent more time in the country, their traditions started to Americanize.

Synset Examples

After a year in Iowa, he has totally Americanized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anglicize - make or become English in form or character
  • Synonym: to Europeanize - make or become European in character

Etymology

Formed from 'American' + '-ize', following a common pattern for verbs meaning 'to make like'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00410568-v
Synset POS: v
