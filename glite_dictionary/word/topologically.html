topologically

1. ADV

/ ˌtɑː.pəˈlɑː.dʒɪ.kli / (AmE), / ˌtɒ.pəˈlɒ.dʒɪ.kli / (BrE)
In a manner relating to the properties preserved under continuous deformation in topology.
in terms of topology

Examples

The two shapes are topologically equivalent, though their forms appear very different.
A coffee mug and a doughnut are considered the same topologically because each has one hole.
This map may look distorted, but it is accurate topologically.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: topology - the mathematical study of shapes and spaces

Etymology

From topology (Greek topos, ‘place’, and -logy) + -ally.
