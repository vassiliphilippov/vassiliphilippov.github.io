shyness

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃaɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈʃaɪ.nəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being nervous, timid, or uncomfortable around other people, especially in social situations
nervousness or timidity around others

Examples

Her shyness made it difficult for her to speak in front of the class.
He tried to overcome his shyness by joining a public speaking club.
Despite his shyness, he made new friends at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timidity - the quality of being easily frightened or lacking confidence
  • Antonym: confidence - the feeling of self-assurance or boldness

Etymology

From shy + -ness, first attested in the 17th century.
