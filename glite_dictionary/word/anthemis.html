Anthemis

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.θə.mɪs / (AmE), / ˈæn.θə.mɪs / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, commonly known as chamomile or dog fennel
a plant genus including chamomile and dog fennel

Examples

Many herbal remedies use extracts from Anthemis species for their calming effects.
The garden features several varieties of Anthemis with daisy-like flowers.
Researchers compared the essential oils of Anthemis with those of other medicinal plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chamomile - a common name for plants in the genus Anthemis

Etymology

From Latin *Anthemis*, from Greek *anthemon* (flower, blossom).

2. NOUN

/ ˈænθəmɪs / (AmE), / ˈænθəmɪs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the daisy family (Asteraceae), commonly known as dog fennel, which includes several species of aromatic herbs with feathery leaves and daisy-like flowers.
a genus of flowering plants (dog fennel)

Examples

The fields were filled with wild Anthemis blooming in the spring sunshine.
Some species of Anthemis are cultivated for their ornamental flowers.
Botanists study the medicinal properties of several plants in the genus Anthemis.

Etymology

From Greek *anthemon* meaning 'flower'.
