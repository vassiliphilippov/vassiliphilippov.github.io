perfected

1. ADJ

/ pərˈfɛktɪd / (AmE), / pəˈfɛktɪd / (BrE)
(of plans, ideas, etc.) brought to a state of complete development or flawlessness.
fully developed or flawless

Examples

Her performance displayed a perfected technique that impressed the judges.
After years of research, they introduced a perfected version of the engine to the market.
The chef served a perfected recipe that blended tradition with innovation.

Synset Examples

a graceful but not yet fully perfected literary style

Related Terms

  • Antonym: imperfect - not perfect or complete; having faults or mistakes

Etymology

From Latin 'perfectus', past participle of 'perficere' (to complete, to finish).
