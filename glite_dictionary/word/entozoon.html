entozoon

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.təˈzoʊ.ɑn / (AmE), / ˌɛntəˈzəʊ.ɒn / (BrE)
a parasite that lives inside the internal organs of an animal, especially referring to intestinal worms
internal animal parasite (esp. worms)
medical archaic

Examples

The veterinarian discovered an entozoon in the dog's intestines during the examination.
Previous studies have described how an entozoon can disrupt its host’s digestive processes.
Infections caused by an entozoon often lead to malnutrition in livestock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endoparasite - a parasite that lives inside the host's body

Etymology

From Greek ento- (within) + zōon (animal).
