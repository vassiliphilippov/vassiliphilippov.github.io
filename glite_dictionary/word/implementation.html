implementation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪmpləmenˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪmplɪmenˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of putting a plan, decision, or agreement into effect; the process of carrying something out.
the act of carrying something out

Examples

The implementation of the new system took several months to complete.
The success of any strategy depends on effective implementation.
There are several challenges associated with the implementation of new technology in schools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: execution - the act of carrying out a plan or order
  • Antonym: planning - the process of making plans for something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'implementatio', from Latin 'implēre' (to fill up, accomplish).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪmpləmenˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪmplɪmenˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of putting a plan, decision, or agreement into effect
the act of carrying out a plan or order

Examples

The successful implementation of the new software improved productivity throughout the company.
The government faced difficulties in the implementation of the health care reforms.
Effective communication is vital for the implementation of organizational changes.

Synset Examples

the agency was created for the implementation of the policy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: execution - the carrying out or putting into effect of a plan, order, or course of action
  • Synonym: enforcement - the act of compelling observance of or compliance with a law, rule, or obligation
  • Antonym: abandonment - the action of giving up on a plan, idea, or course of action

Etymology

From Latin 'implēre' (to fill up) via 'implementum' (a means, implement) and French 'implémenter'.
