Sir Martin Frobisher

1. PROPN

/ sɪr ˈmɑrtɪn ˈfroʊbɪʃər / (AmE), / sə ˈmɑːtɪn ˈfrəʊbɪʃə / (BrE)
(1535–1594) An English navigator and explorer who led expeditions in search of the Northwest Passage and participated in the defeat of the Spanish Armada.
English explorer and naval commander (1535–1594)

Examples

Sir Martin Frobisher led several voyages to the Arctic in search of the Northwest Passage.
Historians credit Sir Martin Frobisher with playing a crucial role in defending England from the Spanish Armada.
The exploits of Sir Martin Frobisher are still studied by maritime scholars today.
