styrax

1. NOUN

/ ˈstaɪræks / (AmE), / ˈstaɪræks / (BrE)
a shrub or small tree of the genus Styrax, known for its fragrant bell-shaped flowers that hang below dark green leaves
a genus of fragrant-flowered shrubs or trees

Examples

The styrax in her garden filled the evening air with its sweet scent.
Botanists studied various species of styrax found in the region.
The styrax produces clusters of beautiful, white, bell-shaped flowers every spring.

Etymology

From Latin *styrax*, from Greek *stúrax*, the scientific name for the tree.
