clamant

1. ADJ

/ ˈklæmənt / (AmE), / ˈklæmənt / (BrE)
(formal) loud and urgent; making a vehement or insistent outcry, often in a conspicuously offensive manner.
loud and urgent; vociferous

Examples

The clamant protestors demanded immediate action outside the city hall.
The issue is so clamant that it cannot be ignored by the authorities any longer.
A clamant chorus of voices filled the courtroom as the verdict was announced.

Synset Examples

blatant radios
a clamorous uproar
strident demands
a vociferous mob

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vociferous - loud and forceful in expressing opinions or feelings
  • Synonym: clamorous - making a loud and confused noise
  • Synonym: blatant - very obvious and offensive, often noisy
  • Antonym: quiet - making little or no noise

Etymology

From Latin 'clamans', present participle of 'clamare' (to cry out, shout).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkleɪmənt / (AmE), / ˈklæm.ənt / (BrE)
demanding urgent attention; pressing or insistent, especially in a way that calls for immediate action.
demanding urgent attention

Examples

The committee addressed the clamant issues facing the community without delay.
After the disaster, there were clamant calls for assistance from the affected regions.
There is a clamant need for reforms in the education system to improve its effectiveness.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urgent - requiring immediate attention or action
  • Synonym: pressing - requiring quick or immediate action or attention
  • Antonym: minor - of less importance or urgency

Etymology

From Latin 'clamans', present participle of 'clamare' (to cry out).
