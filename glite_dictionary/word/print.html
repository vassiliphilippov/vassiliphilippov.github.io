print

1. NOUN

/ prɪnt / (AmE), / prɪnt / (BrE)
a fabric with a decorative pattern dyed or stamped onto it, usually by engraved rollers
fabric with a decorative stamped pattern

Examples

She wore a summer dress made from a cheerful floral print.
The shop specializes in imported batik prints and other fabrics.
This season's collection features several vibrant animal print designs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pattern - repeated decorative design on a surface, especially fabric

2. NOUN

/ prɪnt / (AmE), / prɪnt / (BrE)
the state of being published in a printed form, especially as a book, magazine, or newspaper
published printed form

Examples

Many classic novels remain in print decades after publication.
The article is now available both online and in print.
Her first book went out of print last year.

Synset Examples

we've got to get that story into print

Related Terms

  • Antonym: out of print - no longer published or available in printed form

Etymology

From Latin 'printem,' influenced by French 'empreinte' (impression), relating to the act of pressing or imprinting.

3. NOUN

/ prɪnt / (AmE), / prɪnt / (BrE)
A picture or design made by pressing paper against an engraved or etched surface covered with ink.
picture/design made from an engraving

Examples

The museum displayed a rare print from the eighteenth century.
She bought a beautiful print of a landscape by a famous artist.
Limited edition prints are often signed and numbered by the artist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engraving - a print made from an engraved plate, block, or other surface
  • Synonym: etching - a print produced by the process of etching
  • Antonym: painting - an image made with paint, not printing

Etymology

From Old French *printe*, from Latin *premere* (to press).
