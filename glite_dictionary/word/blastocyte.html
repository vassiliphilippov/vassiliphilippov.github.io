blastocyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈblæs.toʊ.saɪt / (AmE), / ˈblɑː.stəʊ.saɪt / (BrE)
An undifferentiated embryonic cell in the early stages of development.
undifferentiated embryonic cell
medical

Examples

The embryo consists of a group of blastocytes before the cells begin to specialize.
Researchers extracted stem cells from the blastocyte stage for further study.
A single blastocyte can eventually develop into various specialized tissues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blastomere - an early embryonic cell resulting from cleavage of the fertilized egg

Etymology

From Greek 'blastos' (germ, sprout) + 'kytos' (cell).
