rejection

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈdʒɛkʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɛkʃən / (BrE)
an immunological reaction in which the body refuses to accept a transplanted organ, tissue, or other foreign substance
immunological refusal of transplant or foreign matter
medical

Examples

The patient developed acute rejection after the kidney transplant, requiring immediate medical attention.
Preventing rejection is a major challenge in the field of organ transplantation.
Doctors prescribed immunosuppressants to lower the risk of rejection of the new heart.

Synset Examples

rejection of the transplanted liver

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acceptance - the act of taking or receiving something offered or transplanted

Etymology

From Latin rejectio, from rejicere (to throw back).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈdʒɛkʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɛkʃən / (BrE)
The act of refusing to accept, believe, or approve something or someone.
the act of refusing or dismissing

Examples

She faced rejection after sending her manuscript to several publishers.
His application for the scholarship ended in rejection.
Despite many rejections, he remained determined to pursue his dreams.

Synset Examples

his proposals were met with rejection

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acceptance - the act of accepting or consenting to something
  • Synonym: refusal - the act of declining or refusing something

Etymology

From Latin 'rejectio', from 'reicere' meaning 'to throw back'.

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈdʒɛkʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɛkʃən / (BrE)
The act or state of being refused or dismissed by others, or the process of refusing to accept something or someone.
the state of being rejected

Examples

After facing rejection from several publishers, she finally found one willing to take a chance.
He took the rejection hard, but it motivated him to work even harder.
The fear of rejection can sometimes prevent people from expressing their true feelings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acceptance - the process or state of being accepted

Etymology

From Latin rejectio (the act of throwing back), from rejicere (to throw back).
