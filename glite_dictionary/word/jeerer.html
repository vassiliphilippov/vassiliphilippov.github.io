jeerer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪrər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪərə / (BrE)
a person who mocks or ridicules others, often in a contemptuous or derisive way
someone who mocks or ridicules others

Examples

The jeerer stood at the edge of the crowd, shouting insults at the performers.
He ignored the jeerers and continued with his speech, unfazed by their mockery.
Even among friends, being called a jeerer is not a compliment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mock - to make fun of someone or something
  • Synonym: taunter - someone who taunts or insults
  • Antonym: supporter - someone who supports instead of mocking

Etymology

From the verb 'jeer', which may be of Scandinavian origin (compare Old Norse gera, to mock).
