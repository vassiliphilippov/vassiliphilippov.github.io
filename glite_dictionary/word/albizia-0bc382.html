Albizia

1. NOUN

/ ælˈbɪziə / (AmE), / ælˈbɪziə / (BrE)
a genus of trees and shrubs native to the Old World tropics, known for their feathery foliage and fragrant flowers
genus of tropical trees and shrubs

Examples

The Albizia tree is valued for its delicate, fern-like leaves and shade.
Many species in the Albizia genus are commonly planted as ornamental trees in tropical gardens.
Researchers studied the growth habits of Albizia to understand its ecological impact.

Etymology

Named after Filippo degli Albizzi, an Italian nobleman who introduced one species to Europe in the 18th century.
