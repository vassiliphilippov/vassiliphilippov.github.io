monoamine

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːnoʊˈæmin / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəʊˈæmiːn / (BrE)
A molecule containing one amine group, especially one acting as a neurotransmitter in the brain.
a molecule with one amine group
medical

Examples

Serotonin and dopamine are both classified as monoamines in neuroscience.
Some antidepressant drugs work by increasing levels of monoamines in the brain.
The breakdown of monoamines is regulated by the enzyme monoamine oxidase.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neurotransmitter - a chemical substance that transmits signals in the nervous system

Etymology

From mono- (one) and amine (an organic compound derived from ammonia).
