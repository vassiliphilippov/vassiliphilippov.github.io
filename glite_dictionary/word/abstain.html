to abstain

1. VERB

/ əbˈsteɪn / (AmE), / əbˈsteɪn / (BrE)
to deliberately choose not to cast a vote, usually in a formal decision-making process or election
choose not to vote

Examples

Three members chose to abstain from voting on the controversial policy.
You may abstain if you do not wish to take a side in the election.
Although he attended the meeting, he decided to abstain during the final vote.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vote - to express a choice or opinion in a collective decision

Etymology

From Latin abstinere (to withhold), from ab- (from) + tenere (to hold).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02468849-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əbˈsteɪn / (AmE), / əbˈsteɪn / (BrE)
to deliberately choose not to do or consume something, especially something considered undesirable or unhealthy
deliberately choose not to do or consume

Examples

During Lent, many people abstain from eating meat as a sign of devotion.
She decided to abstain from dessert in order to stick to her diet.
Because he was driving, Tom chose to abstain from drinking alcohol at the party.

Synset Examples

I abstain from alcohol

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refrain - to stop oneself from doing something

Etymology

From Latin 'abstinere', meaning 'to withhold'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01198570-v
Synset POS: v
