racking

1. ADJ

/ ˈrækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrækɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a pain or experience) causing great physical or mental suffering.
causing intense suffering

Examples

She was kept awake all night by a racking cough that made her chest ache.
The loss of his friend brought on a period of racking grief and loneliness.
He endured racking pain after the accident, unable to find relief.

Synset Examples

a wrenching pain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrenching - causing emotional or physical pain; distressing
  • Synonym: agonizing - causing intense pain or suffering
  • Antonym: relieving - reducing pain or distress; soothing

Etymology

From the verb 'rack', meaning to cause extreme physical or mental pain, from the medieval instrument of torture known as the rack.
