Diospyros lotus

1. NOUN

/ diˈɑːspɪrɒs ˈloʊtəs / (AmE), / dʌɪˈɒspɪrɒs ˈləʊtəs / (BrE)
An Asian species of persimmon tree cultivated for its small yellow or purplish-black edible fruit, highly valued in some cultures.
Asian persimmon tree with edible fruit

Examples

The Diospyros lotus produces sweet fruit that is enjoyed fresh or dried.
Farmers in Central Asia have cultivated Diospyros lotus for centuries due to its valuable crop.
The small, dark fruit of the Diospyros lotus ripens in autumn and is a delicacy in local markets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: persimmon - Edible fruit of trees in the genus Diospyros

Etymology

From Greek *dios* (divine) + *pyros* (wheat) and Latin *lotus* (lotus tree).
