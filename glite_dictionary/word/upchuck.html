to upchuck

1. VERB

/ ˈʌp.tʃʌk / (AmE), / ˈʌp.tʃʌk / (BrE)
to eject the contents of the stomach through the mouth; to vomit
vomit; throw up
informal american

Examples

The smell of rotten eggs almost made me upchuck at the breakfast table.
He tried to run after the ride, but ended up upchucking behind a tree.
She laughed so hard she thought she might upchuck her lunch.

Synset Examples

After drinking too much, the students vomited

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vomit - to eject the contents of the stomach through the mouth
  • Synonym: to throw up - to vomit (informal phrasal verb)

Etymology

Probably imitative, formed in U.S. English in the early 20th century.
