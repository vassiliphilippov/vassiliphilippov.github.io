Deutsch-Wagram

1. PROPN

/ ˈdɔɪtʃ ˌvɑːgrɑːm / (AmE), / ˈdɔɪtʃ ˌvɑːɡram / (BrE)
A town in northeastern Austria, known as the site of the 1809 Battle of Wagram during the Napoleonic Wars.
town in northeastern Austria

Examples

The Battle of Deutsch-Wagram in 1809 was a significant event in European history.
We visited Deutsch-Wagram during our trip through Austria last summer.
Many historians study Deutsch-Wagram because of its importance in the Napoleonic Wars.

Etymology

From German 'Deutsch' (German) + 'Wagram' (place name).
