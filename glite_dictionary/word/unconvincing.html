unconvincing

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnkənˈvɪnsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌnkənˈvɪnsɪŋ / (BrE)
not capable of persuading someone; failing to make someone believe that something is true or acceptable
not persuasive; not credible

Examples

Her explanation for being late was totally unconvincing.
The movie's plot felt rushed and unconvincing, disappointing many fans.
He gave an unconvincing apology that left everyone skeptical.

Synset Examples

a flimsy argument
as unconvincing as a forced smile

Related Terms

  • Antonym: convincing - able to persuade someone; causing someone to believe something is true

Etymology

From un- (not) + convincing, from Latin convincere (to conquer, to convince).

2. ADJ

/ ʌn.kənˈvɪn.sɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌn.kənˈvɪn.sɪŋ / (BrE)
Not able to make someone believe that something is true or valid; not persuasive.
not persuasive or believable

Examples

Her explanation for being late was unconvincing and left everyone suspicious.
The film's ending felt unconvincing and disappointed many viewers.
His argument sounded unconvincing despite his confident tone.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: convincing - able to persuade someone that something is true or valid

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'convincing', from Latin 'convincere' (to overcome or refute).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01805119-s
Synset POS: s
