parietal

1. ADJ

/ pəˈraɪ.ɪ.t̬əl / (AmE), / pəˈraɪ.ɪ.təl / (BrE)
of or relating to the parietal bones in the cranium or the parts of the body near these bones, especially in the head.
relating to the parietal bones of the skull
medical

Examples

Damage to the parietal lobe can affect spatial awareness and perception.
The parietal bones form the sides and roof of the skull.
The doctor explained the function of the parietal cortex in sensory information processing.

Synset Examples

parietal lobe

Etymology

From Latin parietalis (of a wall), from paries (wall).
