bathos

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪ.θɑs / (AmE), / ˈbeɪ.θɒs / (BrE)
(in literature or speech) an effect of anticlimax created by an unintentional lapse in mood from the sublime to the trivial or ridiculous, often resulting in triteness or triviality of style.
anticlimax or triteness in style

Examples

The novel's emotional moments are ruined by frequent bathos that makes them feel trite.
Critics complained that the film fell into bathos with its over-the-top melodrama.
The speech descended into bathos when the president made an awkward joke.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anticlimax - a disappointing end to an exciting series of events

Etymology

From Greek bathos (depth), introduced into English in the 18th century by Alexander Pope.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪˌθɑs / (AmE), / ˈbeɪθɒs / (BrE)
(in literature or speech) an effect of anticlimax created by an unintentional lapse in mood from the sublime to the trivial or ridiculous, often resulting in insincere or excessive sentimentality.
anticlimax or insincere sentimentality in writing or speech

Examples

The movie's attempt at drama was ruined by accidental bathos in the final scene.
His speech sank into bathos when he suddenly started quoting greeting card clichés.
The novel teeters between genuine poignancy and overwrought bathos.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: pathos - a quality that evokes pity or sadness
  • Synonym: sentimentality - excessive tenderness, sadness, or nostalgia

Etymology

From Greek bathos (depth), coined in English in the 18th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪ.θɑs / (AmE), / ˈbɑː.θɒs / (BrE)
An effect of anticlimax created by a sudden change in tone from the serious or sublime to the trivial or ridiculous.
anticlimax; sudden shift from serious to trivial

Examples

The novel's ending was pure bathos, shifting suddenly from high drama to a petty squabble.
His speech, intended to inspire, descended into bathos when he told a joke about lost socks.
Critics complained that the film's attempt at tragedy resulted in unintended bathos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anticlimax - an event or conclusion that is strikingly less important or dramatic than expected
  • Antonym: pathos - a quality that evokes pity or sadness (contrasted with bathos)
  • Antonym: climax - the most intense or exciting point; the contrast to bathos

Etymology

From Greek bathos (depth); related to Greek bathys (deep), contrasting with 'pathos'.
