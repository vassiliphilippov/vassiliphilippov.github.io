quadrilateral

1. ADJ

/ ˌkwɑːdrɪˈlætərəl / (AmE), / ˌkwɒdrɪˈlætərəl / (BrE)
having four sides; relating to or shaped like a quadrilateral
having four sides

Examples

The artist designed a quadrilateral window that fit perfectly in the wall.
Every quadrilateral shape in the pattern was painted a different color.
They studied the properties of quadrilateral figures in geometry class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quadrangular - having four angles and sides

Etymology

From Latin quadri- (four) + latus (side).

2. NOUN

/ kwɑːdrɪˈlætərəl / (AmE), / kwɒdrɪˈlætərəl / (BrE)
a flat, closed shape with four straight sides and four angles
four-sided polygon

Examples

The teacher drew a quadrilateral on the board and labeled each of its four sides.
Any shape with exactly four straight edges is classified as a quadrilateral.
A square is a special type of quadrilateral where all sides and angles are equal.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: rectangle - a quadrilateral with four right angles
  • Degree Lower: parallelogram - a quadrilateral with opposite sides parallel
  • Degree Higher: polygon - a closed plane figure with at least three straight sides and angles
  • Degree Lower: triangle - a three-sided polygon

Etymology

From Latin 'quadri-' (four) + 'latus' (side).
