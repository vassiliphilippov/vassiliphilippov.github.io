malocclusion

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæləˈkluʒən / (AmE), / ˌmaləˈkluːʒ(ə)n / (BrE)
(Dentistry) A condition in which the upper and lower teeth do not align or fit together properly when the jaws are closed.
improper alignment of teeth
medical

Examples

The orthodontist diagnosed Emily with a malocclusion and recommended braces to correct it.
People with severe malocclusion may experience difficulty chewing or speaking clearly.
Early intervention can help prevent complications caused by malocclusion in children.

Etymology

From Latin 'mal-' (badly) + 'occludere' (to shut up, close).
