Odobenidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊdoʊˈbɛnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌəʊdəˈbɛnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of large marine mammals that includes walruses and their extinct relatives.
family of marine mammals including walruses

Examples

The Odobenidae family consists of the modern walrus and several extinct genera.
Fossils from the Odobenidae have helped scientists trace the evolutionary history of pinnipeds.
Only one living species of Odobenidae exists today—the walrus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walrus - a large marine mammal with tusks, native to Arctic regions

Etymology

From New Latin Odobenidae, based on the genus Odobenus.

2. NOUN

/ ˌoʊdoʊˈbiːnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌəʊdəʊˈbiːnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of large marine mammals including the living walrus and extinct related forms.
family of walruses and extinct relatives

Examples

The Odobenidae family contains both modern walruses and several extinct relatives.
Fossils from the Odobenidae shed light on the evolution of marine mammals.
Researchers have discovered a new species within the Odobenidae in North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walrus - A large, flippered marine mammal with tusks, found in Arctic regions

Etymology

From genus name Odobenus (meaning 'tooth-walker'), from Greek odoús ‘tooth’ and baínō ‘to walk’.
