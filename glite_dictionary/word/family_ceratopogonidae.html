family Ceratopogonidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli sɛrətoʊˌpɒɡəˈnaɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli sɛrətəʊˌpɒɡəˈnaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, bloodsucking flies known as biting midges or sand flies, often found near water and capable of biting humans and animals.
family of small biting midges (sand flies)

Examples

Lakeside campers were plagued by family Ceratopogonidae, whose bites caused severe itching.
Many species in the family Ceratopogonidae are important vectors for animal diseases.
Entomologists collected samples of family Ceratopogonidae near the marsh to study their breeding habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biting midge - small fly of the family Ceratopogonidae that bites humans and animals
  • Synonym: sand fly - common name for various small biting flies including members of Ceratopogonidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Ceratopogonidae', based on Greek 'keras' (horn) + 'pōgōn' (beard).
