Philomachus pugnax

1. NOUN

/ fɪˌloʊˈmækəs ˈpʌɡ.næks / (AmE), / fɪˌlɒˈmak.əs ˈpʌɡ.naks / (BrE)
A species of Eurasian sandpiper known as the ruff, with breeding males displaying an elaborate, erectile neck ruff.
a Eurasian sandpiper, the ruff

Examples

During migration, large flocks of Philomachus pugnax can be seen feeding in marshy wetlands.
The male Philomachus pugnax sports an impressive neck ruff to attract females during the breeding season.
Researchers observed the courtship displays of Philomachus pugnax in their natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruff - common name for Philomachus pugnax

Etymology

From Greek 'philos' (loving) + 'machē' (battle); species name 'pugnax' from Latin (combative), referring to the males' fighting behavior.
