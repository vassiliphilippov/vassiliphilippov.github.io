soybean

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔɪˌbiːn / (AmE), / ˈsɔɪˌbiːn / (BrE)
A bean from an Asian plant, widely grown for use as food, animal feed, and a source of oil.
edible bean and oil source plant

Examples

Tofu is made from soybeans processed into curd.
Farmers often rotate crops with soybeans to improve soil quality.
Soybean oil is extracted from soybeans for use in cooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soya bean - another term for soybean

Etymology

From soy (variant of soya) + bean; originally from Japanese 'shōyu' (soy sauce) and 'mame' (bean), via older European borrowings from Chinese.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɔɪˌbin / (AmE), / ˈsɔɪˌbiːn / (BrE)
an erect, bushy annual plant native to Asia, cultivated for its nutritious, oil-rich seeds used for food, oil, and animal feed
Asian plant grown for edible seeds

Examples

Farmers in many countries grow soybean as a major cash crop.
The protein content of soybean makes it valuable for vegetarian diets.
After harvest, soybean plants can also be used to improve soil fertility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soya bean - alternative spelling for soybean, especially in British English

Etymology

From Japanese 'daizu', translated as 'soya bean' in English.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɔɪˌbin / (AmE), / ˈsɔɪˌbiːn / (BrE)
A high-protein edible seed from a leguminous plant, widely used as food and in the production of oil and animal feed.
edible high-protein seed or plant

Examples

Tofu is made from soybeans, which are soaked and ground to produce soy milk.
The use of soybean oil has increased significantly in processed foods.
Farmers planted hundreds of acres with soybeans this spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soya bean - alternative spelling of 'soybean', chiefly British

Etymology

From Japanese *shōyu* (soy sauce) and English *bean*.
