reseda

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsiːdə / (AmE), / rɪˈsiːdə / (BrE)
any plant of the genus Reseda, including herbaceous flowering plants such as mignonette, known for their fragrant flowers.
a genus of fragrant flowering plants

Examples

The reseda in her garden smelled sweet in the summer air.
Botanists often study reseda for its medicinal properties and fragrant flowers.
A patch of reseda brightened the edge of the path with its delicate blooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mignonette - a common name for some species in the genus Reseda

Etymology

From Latin 'resedare' (to calm, assuage), the ancient name of the plant.
