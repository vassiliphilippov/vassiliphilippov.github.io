gravida

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrævɪdə / (AmE), / ˈɡravɪdə / (BrE)
the number of times a woman has been pregnant, regardless of the outcome
number of times pregnant (medical)
medical

Examples

During her checkup, the doctor noted she was gravida two, since this was her second pregnancy.
The chart listed the patient as gravida three, indicating three total pregnancies.
In clinical records, gravida is often paired with 'para' to describe obstetric history.

Synset Examples

in her third pregnancy a woman is said to be gravida three

Etymology

From Latin gravida (pregnant woman).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡrævɪdə / (AmE), / ˈɡravɪdə / (BrE)
a woman who is pregnant, especially referenced in medical contexts to indicate the number of pregnancies she has had
a pregnant woman (medical term)
medical

Examples

The nurse recorded that the patient was a gravida of two, indicating her second pregnancy.
As a first-time gravida, she attended regular prenatal classes.
The doctor explained the test results to the young gravida during her appointment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: para - a woman who has given birth one or more times

Etymology

From Latin gravida (pregnant woman, pregnant), from gravis (heavy).
