blather

1. NOUN

/ ˈblæðɚ / (AmE), / ˈblɑːðə / (BrE)
talk or writing that is foolish, meaningless, or nonsensical; foolish gibberish.
foolish or nonsensical talk

Examples

The politician's speech was nothing but blather from start to finish.
Tired of his constant blather, she tuned him out.
Let's skip the blather and get straight to the point.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - speech or writing that makes no sense
  • Synonym: babble - continuous foolish or incoherent talk

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse *blathra* (to talk nonsense).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06622685-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈblæðər / (AmE), / ˈblɑːðə / (BrE)
to talk at length in a foolish or meaningless way
talk foolishly or meaninglessly

Examples

He tends to blather on about things he doesn't understand.
She was blathering nervously during the entire meeting.
Stop blathering and get to the point.

Synset Examples

The two women babbled and crooned at the baby

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babble - to talk rapidly and continuously in a foolish way
  • Synonym: prattle - to talk at length in a foolish or inconsequential way

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse *blathra* (to talk nonsense).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01039522-v
Synset POS: v
