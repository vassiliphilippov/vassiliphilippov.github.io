to pamper

1. VERB

/ ˈpæmpɚ / (AmE), / ˈpæmpə / (BrE)
to treat someone with excessive care and attention, often more than necessary or appropriate
treat with excessive indulgence

Examples

She loves to pamper herself with long, luxurious baths every weekend.
It's not good to pamper your pet with too many treats.
The hotel staff will pamper you throughout your stay.

Synset Examples

grandparents often pamper the children
Let's not mollycoddle our students!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spoil - give a child everything they want and indulge excessively
  • Synonym: to indulge - allow oneself or another excessive pleasure
  • Antonym: discipline - train to obey rules or a code of behavior, often strictly

Etymology

Probably from Middle Dutch *pamperen* (to cram with food).
