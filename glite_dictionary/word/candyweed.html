candyweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈkændiˌwid / (AmE), / ˈkændiˌwiːd / (BrE)
A bog plant found in the pine barrens of the southeastern United States, noted for its spikes of irregular yellow-orange flowers.
bog plant with yellow-orange flowers

Examples

The botanist took several photographs of the candyweed growing near the edge of the swamp.
In the spring, the candyweed brightens the pine barrens with its distinctive yellow-orange spikes.
Researchers discovered that the candyweed only thrives in acidic bogs of the region.
