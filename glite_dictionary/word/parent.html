parent

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛr.ənt / (AmE), / ˈpeə.rənt / (BrE)
an organism, such as a plant or animal, that produces offspring or from which young are derived
an organism that produces offspring

Examples

The genetic makeup of the parent determines many traits in its offspring.
When a parent plant is healthy, it often produces more vigorous seeds.
In many species, only one parent is involved in caring for the young.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: offspring - an animal's young; the product of reproduction

Etymology

From Old French 'parent', from Latin 'parens' (father or mother, progenitor), present participle of 'parere' (to bring forth, give birth).

WordNet ID: oewn-00006150-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛr.ənt / (AmE), / ˈpeə.rənt / (BrE)
a father or mother; a person who gives birth to, adopts, or raises a child
father or mother; a child's guardian

Examples

Both of my parents attended the school play last night.
As a single parent, she works hard to support her children.
Every parent wants the best for their child.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: child - a young human being below the age of puberty
  • Synonym: guardian - a person who is legally appointed to look after a child

Etymology

From Old French *parent*, from Latin *parens* (father, mother, ancestor).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10419190-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈperənt / (AmE), / ˈpeərənt / (BrE)
to raise and care for a child until they reach adulthood
raise and care for a child

Examples

Many people find it difficult to parent effectively during stressful times.
She has parented three children as a single mother.
They are parenting their adopted son with love and patience.

Synset Examples

raise a family
bring up children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raise - to care for and bring up a child
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to care for a child properly

Etymology

From Latin parens (parent, ancestor), present participle of parere (to bring forth, give birth).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
