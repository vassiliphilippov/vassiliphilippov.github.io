prophetical

1. ADJ

/ prəˈfɛdɪkəl / (AmE), / prəˈfɛtɪkəl / (BrE)
Foretelling or predicting future events, especially by supernatural or divine inspiration.
predicting the future; relating to prophecy

Examples

Her prophetical warnings about the dangers went unheeded and later proved accurate.
The poem struck a prophetical tone, predicting great changes to come.
Many regarded the old man's prophetical utterances as mere superstition.

Synset Examples

prophetic writings
prophetic powers
words that proved prophetic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prophetic - foretelling events; relating to prophecy

Etymology

From Late Latin propheticalis, from Greek prophētikos (pertaining to a prophet).
