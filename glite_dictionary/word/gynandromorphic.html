gynandromorphic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɡaɪˌnændroʊˈmɔːrfɪk / (AmE), / ˌɡaɪˌnændrəˈmɔːfɪk / (BrE)
Having both male and female morphological characteristics, especially used in reference to certain animals or insects.
having both male and female features
medical

Examples

Some butterflies exhibit a gynandromorphic appearance, with one half male and the other half female.
The scientist was fascinated by the gynandromorphic bird found in the wild.
A gynandromorphic lobster may have distinct coloration patterns on different sides of its body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hermaphroditic - having both male and female reproductive organs

Etymology

From Greek 'gynē' (woman), 'anēr, andros' (man), and 'morphē' (form).
