epochal

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛp.ə.kəl / (AmE), / ˈiː.pək.əl / (BrE)
Extremely significant or important, especially in bringing about or marking the start of a new era or development.
highly significant; marking a new era

Examples

The invention of the internet was an epochal event in the history of human communication.
Scientists described the launch of the new telescope as an epochal achievement for astronomy.
The fall of the Berlin Wall was an epochal moment that changed the course of European history.

Synset Examples

epochal decisions made by Roosevelt and Churchill
an epoch-making discovery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: momentous - of great importance or significance
  • Synonym: historic - famous or important in history
  • Antonym: trivial - of little value or importance

Etymology

From epoch + -al, formed after French epochal or directly from 'epoch'.
