graceless

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡreɪsləs / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪsləs / (BrE)
lacking social grace, elegance, or polish; awkward or clumsy in manner or style.
lacking social grace or elegance

Examples

His graceless comments made everyone at the table uncomfortable.
She gave a graceless curtsy before shuffling awkwardly out of the room.
Despite his intelligence, his graceless manner often alienated his colleagues.

Synset Examples

too gauche to leave the room when the conversation became intimate
their excellent manners always made me feel gauche

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gauche - lacking social grace; awkward
  • Antonym: elegant - showing grace or refined taste

Etymology

From 'grace' + '-less', meaning without grace.

3. ADJ

/ ˈɡreɪsləs / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪsləs / (BrE)
Lacking grace or elegance; clumsy or awkward in movement, appearance, or style.
lacking grace or elegance; clumsy

Examples

The graceless movements of the actor distracted from his otherwise good performance.
She found his graceless behavior at the dinner party rather embarrassing.
The statue looked oddly graceless compared to the artist's other works.

Synset Examples

a graceless production of the play
his stature low … his bearing ungraceful

Related Terms

  • Antonym: graceful - having grace or elegance; opposite of graceless
  • Synonym: clumsy - awkward in movement or handling things

Etymology

From grace + -less, meaning 'without grace'.
