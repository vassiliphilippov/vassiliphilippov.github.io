unused

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈjuːzd / (AmE), / ʌnˈjuːzd / (BrE)
not having been used, worn, or utilized yet; still new or clean
not yet used or soiled

Examples

The guest room had an unused bed with crisp white sheets.
He found an unused notebook at the bottom of his desk drawer.
There was an unused ticket left on the kitchen counter.

Synset Examples

a fresh shirt
a fresh sheet of paper
an unused envelope

Related Terms

  • Antonym: used - no longer new; already put to use
  • Synonym: fresh - new or clean, not previously used

Etymology

From un- (not) + used (past participle of 'use').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00419717-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈjuːzd / (AmE), / ʌnˈjuːzd / (BrE)
not being used; not in active use or operation
not in use; inactive

Examples

The classroom remained unused throughout the summer holidays.
They donated their unused tickets to someone else.
Several unused computers were gathering dust in the office.

Synset Examples

the machinery sat idle during the strike
idle hands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idle - not working or being used; inactive
  • Antonym: used - being utilized or in operation

Etymology

From un- (not) + used (past participle of use).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00037301-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈjust / (AmE), / ʌnˈjuːzd / (BrE)
not having been used or put into service; in a new or untouched condition
not used; still new or untouched

Examples

The company donated unused office supplies to local schools.
He found an unused notebook at the bottom of his bag.
Our kitchen still has an unused appliance sitting in the box.

Synset Examples

we bought an unused car for a change

Related Terms

  • Antonym: used - having been employed or put into service

Etymology

From un- (not) + used (past participle of 'use').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01647702-s
Synset POS: s
