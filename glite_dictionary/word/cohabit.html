to cohabit

1. VERB

/ koʊˈhæbɪt / (AmE), / kəʊˈhæbɪt / (BrE)
(of two people) to live together and have a sexual relationship, especially without being married to each other.
live together as an unmarried couple

Examples

They decided to cohabit before making any plans to get married.
In some countries, couples who cohabit for several years gain legal rights similar to married couples.
It has become more common for partners to cohabit rather than marry immediately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to live together - to share a home with someone, usually as a couple

Etymology

From Latin cohabitare, from co- (together) + habitare (to dwell).

2. VERB

/ koʊˈhæbɪt / (AmE), / kəʊˈhæbɪt / (BrE)
to exist or be present together in the same place or time, especially in reference to different things or groups
exist or occur together

Examples

Several rare animal species cohabit peacefully within the protected forest.
The two faiths have managed to cohabit in the same region for centuries.
In this garden, exotic and native plants cohabit harmoniously.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to coexist - to exist at the same time or in the same place

Etymology

From Latin cohabitare (to live together), from co- (together) + habitare (to dwell).
