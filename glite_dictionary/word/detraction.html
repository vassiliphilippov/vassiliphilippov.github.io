detraction

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈtrækʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈtrækʃən / (BrE)
a remark or act that undermines or belittles someone or something; a petty disparagement
petty disparagement; belittling remark

Examples

Every achievement was met with some form of detraction from her jealous peers.
He shrugged off the detraction and focused on his work.
The editor's review was fair and free of personal detraction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disparagement - the act of speaking about someone or something in a derogatory or belittling manner
  • Antonym: praise - the expression of approval or admiration

Etymology

From Latin detractionem (a withdrawing, disparagement), from detrahere (to drag down, take away).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈtrækʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈtrækʃən / (BrE)
the act of reducing the worth or value of someone or something, especially by making unfair or damaging remarks about them
unfair criticism; act of discrediting

Examples

Her comments were seen as a deliberate detraction from his achievements.
The newspaper's article was full of detraction, damaging his reputation.
Despite criticism and detraction, she remained confident in her abilities.

Synset Examples

let it be no detraction from his merits to say he is plainspoken

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disparagement - the act of speaking about someone in a derogatory manner
  • Antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration

Etymology

From Latin 'detractionem' (a taking away), from 'detrahere' (to take away).
