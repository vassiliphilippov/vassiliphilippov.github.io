womanish

1. ADJ

/ ˈwʊmənɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈwʊmənɪʃ / (BrE)
displaying qualities or behavior regarded as characteristic of women, often used disapprovingly for men
characteristic of women (often disapproving)

Examples

He dismissed his womanish fears and pressed on through the darkness.
The novel criticized the hero for his womanish tears during hardship.
Some regarded elaborate grief as a womanish display, inappropriate for a soldier.

Synset Examples

womanish tears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feminine - having qualities traditionally associated with women
  • Antonym: manly - having qualities traditionally associated with men

Etymology

Formed from 'woman' + '-ish', an adjective-forming suffix.

2. ADJ

/ ˈwʊmənɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈwʊmənɪʃ / (BrE)
displaying traits or behavior regarded as characteristic of women, often implying delicacy, sensitivity, or timidity; sometimes used disparagingly.
displaying traditionally feminine traits

Examples

He was criticized for his womanish sensitivity in a society that valued toughness.
Some people see expressing emotions as womanish, but that's an outdated stereotype.
She rejected the idea that caring for others made her womanish or weak.

Synset Examples

She dismissed the notion that being womanish was a bad thing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feminine - having qualities traditionally associated with women
  • Antonym: manly - having qualities traditionally associated with men

Etymology

From 'woman' + '-ish', Old English origins, meaning 'like a woman'.
