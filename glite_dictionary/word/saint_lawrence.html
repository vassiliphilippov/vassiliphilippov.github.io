Saint Lawrence

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈlɔrəns / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈlɒrəns / (BrE)
A Christian martyr of the 3rd century, venerated as a saint, who was reputedly executed by being roasted on a gridiron after presenting the poor as the true treasures of the Church.
Christian martyr and saint (died 258)

Examples

Many churches across Europe are dedicated to Saint Lawrence in honor of his sacrifice.
The feast of Saint Lawrence is celebrated on August 10 in the Catholic tradition.
Legends say that Saint Lawrence showed extraordinary courage when facing his execution.

2. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈlɔr(ə)ns / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈlɒrəns / (BrE)
a major river in North America that flows northeast from Lake Ontario to the Gulf of Saint Lawrence and the North Atlantic Ocean
major North American river

Examples

The Saint Lawrence River plays a crucial role in shipping between the Great Lakes and the Atlantic Ocean.
Many migratory birds stop along the Saint Lawrence during their seasonal journeys.
Ice can make travel on the Saint Lawrence dangerous in the winter months.

Etymology

Named after St. Lawrence, a Christian martyr; the river was so named by the French explorer Jacques Cartier, who arrived there on St. Lawrence's feast day (August 10, 1535).
