declarative

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈklær.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈklær.ət.ɪv / (BrE)
(of a mood of verbs) expressing a statement or declaration, rather than a question, command, or wish.
expressing a statement (grammar)

Examples

In English, most sentences are in the declarative mood, simply stating facts or opinions.
'He went to the store' is an example of a declarative sentence.
The teacher explained how to change a declarative sentence into a question.

Synset Examples

indicative mood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indicative - the verb mood used for making factual statements

Etymology

From Late Latin *declarativus* ('serving to declare'), from Latin *declarare* ('to declare').

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈklɛrəˌtɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈklærətɪv / (BrE)
a grammatical mood that is used to make statements or express facts
mood expressing statements or facts

Examples

A declarative is used to make statements rather than ask questions or give commands.
Most basic English sentences are in the declarative mood.
She wrote the sentence as a declarative, not as a question.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interrogative - a grammatical mood used for asking questions
  • Antonym: imperative - a mood expressing commands or requests
  • Synonym: indicative - a grammatical mood used for factual statements

Etymology

From Latin 'declarativus', from 'declarare' (to make clear, declare).

3. ADJ

/ dɪˈklɛrəˌtɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈklærətɪv / (BrE)
relating to or having the characteristic of a declaration or statement, especially in grammar or computing.
in the form of a declaration or statement

Examples

The teacher asked the students to write a declarative sentence about their weekend.
In computer science, a declarative language tells the computer what to do, not how to do it.
Her answer was direct and declarative, leaving no room for ambiguity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interrogative - having the form or force of a question, especially in grammar
  • Antonym: imperative - giving an order or command, especially in grammar

Etymology

From Latin declarativus (serving to declare), from declarare (to declare).
