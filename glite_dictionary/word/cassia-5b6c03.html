Cassia

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæsiə, ˈkæʃə / (AmE), / ˈkasiə, ˈkaʃə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae, which includes several species often classified as members of the genus Senna or Chamaecrista.
genus of leguminous plants

Examples

Some herbal teas are made from leaves of the Cassia genus.
Botanists have debated the classification of several species within Cassia.
Certain types of Cassia are valued for their medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Senna - a related genus of flowering leguminous plants
  • Synonym: Chamaecrista - another related genus of leguminous plants

Etymology

From Latin *cassia*, from Greek *kassia*, used for aromatic plants.
