waffle

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːfəl / (AmE), / ˈwɒfəl / (BrE)
A flat cake with a grid pattern, made from leavened batter or dough cooked in a waffle iron.
cake cooked in a waffle iron

Examples

She served homemade waffles with fresh strawberries and whipped cream for breakfast.
The smell of freshly baked waffle filled the kitchen every Sunday morning.
I ordered a Belgian waffle drizzled with maple syrup at the café.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pancake - flat cake cooked on a griddle or frying pan

Etymology

From Dutch 'wafel', meaning 'honeycomb' or 'waffle'.

2. VERB

/ ˈwɑːfəl / (AmE), / ˈwɒf(ə)l / (BrE)
to be indecisive or avoid committing oneself, especially when expressing an opinion or making a decision
to be indecisive or vacillate

Examples

When asked about policy changes, the minister began to waffle rather than give a clear answer.
She tends to waffle whenever someone asks her an important question.
The chairman waffled for several minutes before finally announcing the decision.

Synset Examples

Authorities hesitate to quote exact figures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to vacillate - to waver between different opinions or actions
  • Synonym: to hesitate - to pause before saying or doing something, often due to uncertainty
  • Antonym: to decide - to choose or make a determination

Etymology

Perhaps from Scottish 'waff', meaning to yelp or bark, later developing the figurative sense of speaking vaguely or indecisively.
