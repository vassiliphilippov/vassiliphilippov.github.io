constipation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnstəˈpeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnstɪˈpeɪʃən / (BrE)
A medical condition in which a person has difficulty emptying the bowels, typically with infrequent, hard, or painful stools.
difficulty in bowel movements
medical

Examples

Changes in diet can help relieve constipation in many patients.
After several days of constipation, she decided to consult her doctor.
Some medications are known to cause constipation as a side effect.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diarrhea - an abnormally frequent and watery bowel movement

Etymology

From Latin constipatio (crowding together, compression), from constipare (to press together).
