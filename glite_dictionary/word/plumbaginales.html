Plumbaginales

1. NOUN

/ plʌmˌbædʒɪˈneɪliːz / (AmE), / plʌmˌbædʒɪˈneɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of flowering plants that is coextensive with the family Plumbaginaceae and is sometimes included in the order Primulales
an order of flowering plants

Examples

The Plumbaginales are characterized by simple leaves and often colorful flowers.
Botanists sometimes classify the Plumbaginales within the order Primulales.
The majority of species in the Plumbaginales are found in saline environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Plumbaginaceae - the family of flowering plants commonly known as the plumbago or leadwort family

Etymology

From the family name Plumbaginaceae, ultimately from Latin 'plumbago' (leadwort).

2. NOUN

/ plʌmˌbædʒɪˈneɪliːz / (AmE), / plʌmˌbædʒɪˈneɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of flowering plants that is coextensive with the family Plumbaginaceae, usually included in the order Primulales.
an order of flowering plants

Examples

The plant family known as Plumbaginales is now often merged with the order Primulales.
Some botanists continue to recognize Plumbaginales as a distinct order for classification purposes.
Not many garden plants belong to the order Plumbaginales compared to other botanic groups.

Etymology

From New Latin Plumbaginales, based on Plumbaginaceae (the leadwort family).
