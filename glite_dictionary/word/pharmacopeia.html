pharmacopeia

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɑrməkəˈpiə / (AmE), / ˌfɑːməkəˈpiːə / (BrE)
an official publication that lists pharmaceutical drugs along with their formulas, standards, and methods of preparation
official drug reference book
medical

Examples

The new edition of the pharmacopeia includes updated guidelines for drug preparation and testing.
Medical professionals often refer to the pharmacopeia for information on dosage and drug standards.
The hospital's pharmacy must comply with all regulations stated in the national pharmacopeia.

Synset Examples

postexposure prophylaxis is an integral part of the pharmacopeia in preventing severe disease after acute infections

Related Terms

  • Synonym: formulary - an official list of approved medications

Etymology

From Greek pharmakopoiia (drug-making or preparation), from pharmakon (drug) + poiein (to make).
