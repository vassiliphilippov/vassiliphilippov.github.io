Geastrum coronatum

1. NOUN

/ dʒiˈæstrəm ˌkɔrəˈneɪtəm / (AmE), / dʒiˈæstrəm ˌkɒrəˈneɪtəm / (BrE)
A species of earthstar fungus with a bluish spore sac and purplish brown gleba; its outer layer splits into rays that bend backward to raise the spore sac at maturity.
a species of earthstar fungus

Examples

The distinctive rays of Geastrum coronatum make it easy to identify among other earthstars.
Botanists observed that Geastrum coronatum prefers growing in sandy woodland soils.
Photographers are drawn to the unusual beauty of Geastrum coronatum in its mature, star-like form.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earthstar - a fungus known for its star-like shape when mature

Etymology

From Latin: 'Geastrum' from Greek 'gē' (earth) + 'astron' (star); 'coronatum' meaning crowned.
