Erato

1. PROPN

/ əˈreɪtoʊ / (AmE), / ɪˈrɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the Muse of lyric and love poetry.
Muse of lyric and love poetry (Greek myth)

Examples

In classical literature, Erato is often portrayed holding a lyre, symbolizing her connection to music and love.
The poet dedicated his ode to Erato, hoping for inspiration in the art of romantic verse.
Many artists have depicted Erato surrounded by roses, representing passion and creativity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Muse - any of the nine goddesses of the arts in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἐρατώ (Eratṓ), meaning 'desired' or 'lovely'.
