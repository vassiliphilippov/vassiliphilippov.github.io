Pinicola

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪ.nɪˈkoʊ.lə / (AmE), / ˌpɪ.nɪˈkəʊ.lə / (BrE)
A genus of finches in the family Fringillidae, which includes the pine grosbeak and the crimson-browed finch.
genus of finches, including pine grosbeak

Examples

The pine grosbeak is a member of the genus Pinicola, commonly found in northern forests.
The bright plumage of Pinicola species makes them popular among birdwatchers.
Scientists recently reviewed the classification of the Pinicola genus in light of genetic studies.

Etymology

From Latin pinus (pine) + -cola (inhabitant), meaning 'inhabitant of pines'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɪnɪˈkoʊlə / (AmE), / ˌpɪnɪˈkəʊlə / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Fringillidae, which includes the pine grosbeak and the crimson-browed finch.
genus of birds including the pine grosbeak

Examples

The Pinicola genus includes species that are found in northern forests around the world.
Ornithologists observed a rare member of the Pinicola genus during their expedition.
The pine grosbeak, a colorful bird in the Pinicola genus, is known for its melodic calls.

Etymology

From Latin pinus (pine) and -cola (dweller), meaning 'pine dweller'.
