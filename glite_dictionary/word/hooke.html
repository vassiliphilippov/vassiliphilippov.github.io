Hooke

1. PROPN

/ hʊk / (AmE), / hʊk / (BrE)
Robert Hooke (1635–1703), English scientist known for formulating the law of elasticity, contributions to biology, physics, and astronomy, and introducing the term 'cell'.
English scientist, formulated Hooke's law, introduced 'cell' in biology

Examples

Hooke formulated the law of elasticity, now known as Hooke's law.
The microscopic structure of cork was first described by Hooke in the 17th century.
Many early scientific discoveries in physics and biology are attributed to Hooke.
