Roridula

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɔːˈrɪdʒʊlə / (AmE), / ˌrɒːˈrɪdjʊlə / (BrE)
a genus of insectivorous undershrubs native to South Africa, sometimes classified in the family Droseraceae
genus of insect-eating South African plants

Examples

The Roridula genus includes plants that trap insects with sticky leaves.
South African botanists have documented several species of Roridula in mountainous regions.
Unlike most insect-eating plants, Roridula relies on symbiotic insects to digest its prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Droseraceae - the sundew family of insectivorous plants

Etymology

From Latin *roridus* (dewy), referring to the sticky, dew-like secretions of the plant.

2. NOUN

/ rɔːˈrɪdjələ / (AmE), / rɒˈrɪdjʊlə / (BrE)
a genus of low-growing, sticky, perennial woody shrubs native to South Africa, known for their viscid leaves
South African sticky-leaved shrub genus

Examples

The Roridula plant traps insects on its sticky leaves but relies on other insects to digest them.
Many collectors admire the unusual appearance of Roridula in their native plant gardens.
Researchers have studied Roridula to understand its unique method of obtaining nutrients from trapped prey.

Etymology

From Latin 'roridus' meaning 'dewy' or 'moist', referring to the appearance of its sticky leaves.
