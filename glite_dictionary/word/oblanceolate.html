oblanceolate

1. ADJ

/ oʊˈblænsiəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ɒbˈlænsiəˌleɪt / (BrE)
(of a leaf) having a shape that is broad and rounded at the tip and tapering toward the base.
leaf shape: broad tip, tapering base
medical

Examples

The plant’s oblanceolate leaves distinguish it from other species in the region.
Botanists described the tree as having thick, oblanceolate foliage.
A key feature is the oblanceolate shape of its leaf blades.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lanceolate - (of a leaf) shaped like a lance-head, broadest below the middle and tapering to the tip

Etymology

From Latin 'ob-' (inverted, reverse) + 'lanceolatus' (like a lance).
