Stefan Wyszynski

1. PROPN

/ ˈstɛfɑn vɪˈʃɪnski / (AmE), / ˈstefan vɪˈʃɪnski / (BrE)
(1901–1981) Polish Roman Catholic cardinal who helped persuade the Soviet authorities to permit greater religious freedom in Poland.
Polish cardinal and leader for religious freedom

Examples

Stefan Wyszynski played a crucial role in maintaining religious practices in Poland during Soviet rule.
Many Poles view Stefan Wyszynski as a symbol of resilience and faith under communism.
The canonization process for Stefan Wyszynski was celebrated by Catholics worldwide.
