mufti

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌf.ti / (AmE), / ˈmʌf.ti / (BrE)
civilian clothes worn by someone who normally wears a military or other official uniform
civilian dress for uniformed personnel

Examples

On weekends, the soldiers were allowed to wear mufti instead of their usual uniforms.
The school organizes a mufti day where students can wear casual clothes for charity.
He arrived at the meeting in mufti, which surprised his colleagues who were used to seeing him in uniform.

Etymology

From Arabic 'mufti' (an Islamic legal expert), originally used informally by British army officers to refer to civilian dress.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌf.ti / (AmE), / ˈmʌf.ti / (BrE)
an Islamic scholar who is qualified to interpret and expound on Muslim religious law
Islamic religious law interpreter

Examples

The mufti was called upon to issue a ruling regarding the local community's dispute.
As the highest religious authority, the mufti offers guidance on complex legal matters.
Many people sought the advice of the mufti before making important decisions.

Etymology

From Arabic 'muftī' (one who gives a fatwa or legal opinion).
