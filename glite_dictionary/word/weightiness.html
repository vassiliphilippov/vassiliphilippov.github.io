weightiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪtinəs / (AmE), / ˈweɪtɪnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being important, serious, or significant
importance or significance

Examples

The weightiness of her remarks made everyone pause and consider their actions.
Many critics admired the weightiness of the film's themes and message.
He tried to add some weightiness to the discussion by referencing historical events.

Synset Examples

his opinion carries great weight
the progression implied an increasing weightiness of the items listed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: importance - the state of being important
  • Synonym: seriousness - the quality of being serious or grave
  • Antonym: triviality - the quality of being trivial or unimportant

Etymology

From 'weighty' (important, significant) plus the noun-forming suffix '-ness.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈweɪ.ti.nəs / (AmE), / ˈweɪ.ti.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being heavy or having considerable weight
the condition of being heavy

Examples

The weightiness of the box surprised everyone who tried to lift it.
Lead is notable for its extreme weightiness compared to other metals.
The sculpture's weightiness required a reinforced platform for display.

Synset Examples

the heaviness of lead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heaviness - the quality of being heavy in weight
  • Antonym: lightness - the quality of having little weight

Etymology

Formed from 'weighty' + '-ness'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05034695-n
Synset POS: n
