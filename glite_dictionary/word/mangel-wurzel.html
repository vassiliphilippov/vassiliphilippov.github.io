mangel-wurzel

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæŋɡəlˌwɝːzəl / (AmE), / ˈmaŋɡlˌwɜːzəl / (BrE)
A type of beet with a large yellowish root, cultivated mainly for cattle feed.
beet grown chiefly as cattle feed

Examples

The farmer grew acres of mangel-wurzel to provide feed for his livestock.
In the winter, cattle are often fed a diet rich in mangel-wurzel and hay.
The large yellow root of the mangel-wurzel is highly nutritious for farm animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beetroot - root vegetable of the species Beta vulgaris

Etymology

From German Mangelwurzel, literally 'root of the beet', from Mangel (beet) + Wurzel (root).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæŋɡəlˌwɝzəl / (AmE), / ˈmaŋɡəlˌwɜːzəl / (BrE)
A variety of beet with a large root, grown chiefly as food for cattle and other livestock.
root vegetable grown as livestock feed

Examples

The farmer harvested the mangel-wurzel to feed his cattle during the winter.
In traditional British agriculture, mangel-wurzels were a staple crop for livestock.
She studied the nutritional value of mangel-wurzel as animal fodder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beet - root vegetable, Beta vulgaris, with edible leaves and root

Etymology

From German 'Mangelwurzel', literally 'root of the mangel (beet)'.
