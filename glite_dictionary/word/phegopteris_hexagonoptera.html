Phegopteris hexagonoptera

1. NOUN

/ fiːˌgɒptərɪs ˌhɛksaɡəˈnɒptərə / (AmE), / fiːˌgɒptərɪs ˌhɛksəɡəˈnɒptərə / (BrE)
A species of beech fern native to North American woodlands, noted for its straw-colored stripes.
a North American beech fern species

Examples

The Phegopteris hexagonoptera thrives in moist, shaded woodlands throughout eastern North America.
Botanists easily recognize Phegopteris hexagonoptera by its unique straw-colored stripes running along the leaflets.
Gardeners often plant Phegopteris hexagonoptera in shaded areas to create a natural, woodland effect.

Etymology

From Greek 'phegos' (beech), 'pteris' (fern), and Latinized Greek 'hexagonopteron' (six-angled wing), referring to aspects of the frond's shape.
