reminder

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈmaɪndər / (AmE), / rɪˈmaɪndə / (BrE)
an experience, event, or object that makes you remember something you had forgotten or might forget
something that prompts remembering

Examples

Her old diary served as a reminder of her childhood dreams and ambitions.
The sound of rain was a painful reminder of the night he left.
He set a reminder on his phone so he wouldn’t forget the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prompt - something that triggers action or memory
  • Synonym: mnemonic - something aiding memory
  • Antonym: distraction - something that diverts attention or memory

Etymology

From remind + -er, first recorded in English in the 15th century.

2. NOUN

/ ɹɪˈmaɪndɚ / (AmE), / ɹɪˈmaɪndə / (BrE)
a message or note that helps someone remember to do something or recall information
a message to help remember something

Examples

I set a reminder on my phone to call my grandmother tomorrow.
The teacher gave the students a gentle reminder about the homework deadline.
She left a sticky note as a reminder to buy milk on the way home.

Synset Examples

he ignored his wife's reminders

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prompt - a cue or signal to remember or act

Etymology

From remind + -er, modeled after other agent nouns.
