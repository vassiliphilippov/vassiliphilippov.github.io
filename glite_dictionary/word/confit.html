confit

1. NOUN

/ kɑnˈfi / (AmE), / kɒnˈfi / (BrE)
A piece of meat, especially duck, cooked slowly in its own fat and preserved in it.
meat cooked and preserved in its own fat

Examples

The chef served confit of duck with a side of roasted vegetables.
After making the confit, she carefully stored it for future use.
Many French restaurants offer confit as one of their signature dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preserve - to keep food in a condition so it can be eaten later

Etymology

From French 'confit' (preserved), related to 'confire' (to preserve).
