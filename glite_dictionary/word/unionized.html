unionized

1. ADJ

/ ˈjuːniəˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪənaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a substance) not converted into ions; remaining in a neutral, uncharged form in solution.
not converted into ions (chemistry)

Examples

In this experiment, the acid remained largely unionized in the organic solvent.
The drug is absorbed more efficiently in its unionized form.
Chemists measured both the ionized and unionized particles in the solution.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ionized - converted into ions

2. ADJ

/ ˈjuːniəˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈjuːniəˌnaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a group of workers or a workplace) organized as or belonging to a labor union.
organized as or belonging to a labor union

Examples

The company employs many unionized workers who negotiate their contracts collectively.
A unionized workplace often has better benefits for its employees.
After a lengthy campaign, the staff finally became unionized.

Synset Examples

organized labor
unionized workers
a unionized shop

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organized - formed into a group for a purpose, especially in a labor context
  • Antonym: nonunionized - not represented or organized by a labor union

Etymology

Formed from 'union' + '-ize' + '-ed', referring to labor unions.
