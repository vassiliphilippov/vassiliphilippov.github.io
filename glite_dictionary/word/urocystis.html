Urocystis

1. NOUN

/ ˌjʊroʊˈsɪstɪs / (AmE), / ˌjʊərəʊˈsɪstɪs / (BrE)
A genus of smut fungi in the family Tilletiaceae, some species of which infect grasses and crops.
genus of smut fungi

Examples

Several species of Urocystis can cause diseases in important cereal crops.
The identification of Urocystis in the sample was confirmed by microscopy.
Researchers are studying the life cycle of Urocystis to develop better fungicides.

Etymology

From Greek 'ouron' (urine) and 'kystis' (bladder), referring to the bladder-like spore sacs of the fungus.

2. NOUN

/ ˌjuː.roʊˈsɪstɪs / (AmE), / ˌjʊə.rəʊˈsɪstɪs / (BrE)
A genus of smut fungi in the family Tilletiaceae, known for infecting plants and producing characteristic spores.
genus of smut fungi

Examples

The plant was infected with a species of Urocystis, causing smut symptoms on its leaves.
Researchers identified the fungus as belonging to the Urocystis genus after examining the spores.
Several crops are vulnerable to diseases caused by Urocystis fungi.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'uro-' (relating to urine or tail) and Greek 'kystis' (bladder), referring to the spore shape.
