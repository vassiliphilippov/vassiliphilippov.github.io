jury

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʊri / (AmE), / ˈdʒʊəri / (BrE)
A group of people chosen to judge and decide the results of a competition.
group judging a competition

Examples

The jury awarded first prize to a talented young pianist.
Each artwork was carefully evaluated by the jury before the winners were chosen.
A panel of experts served as the jury for the science fair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panel - A group of people assembled for a special purpose, often to judge or discuss.

Etymology

From Old French *juree* (a sworn body of men), from Medieval Latin *jurata* (a sworn association), from Latin *jurare* (to swear).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝː.i / (AmE), / ˈdʒʊə.ri / (BrE)
A group of people sworn to decide the facts and deliver a verdict in a court trial based on the evidence presented.
group that decides a court case
legal

Examples

The jury deliberated for several hours before reaching a verdict.
The defense attorney carefully explained the evidence to the jury during the trial.
It took the jury only thirty minutes to find the defendant not guilty.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: judge - person in a court who presides and ensures law is followed

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *juré* (sworn), from Latin *jurare* (to swear).
