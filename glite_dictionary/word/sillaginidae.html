Sillaginidae

1. NOUN

/ sɪləˈdʒɪnəˌdiː / (AmE), / sɪlədʒɪˈnɪːdiː / (BrE)
a family of small food fishes found in shallow waters of the Pacific region, especially around Indonesia
family of small Pacific food fishes

Examples

The Sillaginidae are commonly caught by local fishermen in the waters around Indonesia.
Marine biologists studied several species within the Sillaginidae family to monitor their populations.
Dishes featuring fish from the Sillaginidae are popular in many coastal Asian communities.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name Sillago plus the suffix -idae, used for animal family names.
