Psephurus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːfərəs / (AmE), / ˈsiːfərəs / (BrE)
A genus of large freshwater paddlefish native to the Yangtze River in China, now believed to be extinct.
genus of extinct paddlefish

Examples

The extinct Psephurus genus included only one species, the Chinese paddlefish.
Scientists searched the Yangtze River for any surviving members of Psephurus.
Research into Psephurus has provided insights into paddlefish evolution.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Polyodontidae - family of paddlefish

Etymology

From Greek psephos (pebble) and oura (tail), referring to the fish's appearance.

2. NOUN

/ siːˈfʊrəs / (AmE), / siːˈfjʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater fish in the family Polyodontidae, known as the Chinese paddlefish, native to China but now considered extinct.
genus of Chinese paddlefish

Examples

The Psephurus genus was once represented by the Chinese paddlefish in the Yangtze River.
Scientists have declared the Psephurus lineage extinct after extensive studies failed to find any specimens.
The Psephurus fish were known for their elongated snouts and unique appearance.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Polyodontidae - family of paddlefish
  • Synonym: Chinese paddlefish - a species within the genus Psephurus

Etymology

From Greek psephos (pebble) + oura (tail), used in scientific taxonomy.
