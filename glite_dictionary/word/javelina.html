javelina

1. NOUN

/ ˌhævəˈliːnə / (AmE), / ˌhævəˈliːnə / (BrE)
A wild, pig-like mammal with dark grey bristly hair and an indistinct white collar, found in semi-desert regions of Mexico and the southwestern United States.
wild pig-like mammal of the Americas

Examples

We spotted a javelina rooting around in the brush near the campsite.
Javelinas are common in the deserts of Arizona and Texas.
The javelina quickly disappeared into the undergrowth when it sensed our presence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peccary - another term for pig-like mammals of the family Tayassuidae native to the Americas

Etymology

From Spanish jabalina, diminutive of jabalí (wild boar), from Arabic ḥabālī (mountain swine).
