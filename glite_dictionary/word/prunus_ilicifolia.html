Prunus ilicifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs ɪˌlɪsɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs ɪˌlɪsɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub or small tree native to California, with spiny, leathery leaves, white flowers, and edible plum-like fruit
evergreen Californian shrub with spiny leaves

Examples

The leaves of Prunus ilicifolia are thick and spiny, resembling those of holly.
Many birds are attracted to the fruit of Prunus ilicifolia in the wild.
Gardeners often plant Prunus ilicifolia for its drought resistance and ornamental value.

Etymology

From Latin: Prunus (plum) and ilicifolia (holly-leaved).
