orison

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːr.ɪ.sən / (AmE), / ˈɒr.ɪ.zən / (BrE)
a prayer or earnest petition, especially a reverent plea to a deity
prayer; reverent petition to a deity
archaic

Examples

Each evening, she whispered an orison before going to sleep.
The monk spent hours in silent orison in the small chapel.
Shakespeare often used the word orison in his plays to mean 'prayer.'

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prayer - an earnest request or an act of speaking to God or another deity

Etymology

From Old French *orison*, from Latin *oratio* (speech, prayer).
