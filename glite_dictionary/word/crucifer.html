crucifer

1. NOUN

/ ˈkruːsəfər / (AmE), / ˈkruːsɪfə / (BrE)
a plant belonging to the family Cruciferae (now Brassicaceae), such as cabbage, mustard, or broccoli
plant in the cabbage and mustard family

Examples

Broccoli and cauliflower are both types of crucifer commonly found in grocery stores.
Some crucifers are known for their health benefits due to their high vitamin content.
Farmers rotate crucifers with other crops to improve soil health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Brassicaceae - the family of plants that includes crucifers

Etymology

From New Latin Cruciferae, meaning 'cross-bearing', referring to the shape of the flowers' petals.
