calyceal

1. ADJ

/ kəˈlɪsiəl / (AmE), / kəˈlɪsiːəl / (BrE)
relating to or resembling a calyx, especially the calyx of a flower or anatomical structure such as the kidney
relating to a calyx (botany or anatomy)

Examples

The doctor noted a calyceal abnormality on the ultrasound of the patient's kidney.
Certain flowers have a highly distinctive calyceal structure that aids in protection and pollination.
A calyceal diverticulum may be detected during a urological examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calyx - the sepals of a flower, collectively forming the outer floral envelope

Etymology

From calyx (Latin, 'cup' or 'outer covering of a flower') + -eal (adjectival suffix).
