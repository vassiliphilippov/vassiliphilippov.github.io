to outdraw

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈdrɔ / (AmE), / aʊtˈdrɔː / (BrE)
to draw a gun faster than another person, especially in a contest or gunfight
draw a gun faster than another

Examples

In the duel, he managed to outdraw his opponent by a split second.
The sheriff was known to outdraw any outlaw who challenged him.
Few could outdraw the legendary gunslinger in the Old West.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outshoot - to shoot better or faster than someone else

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'out-' (meaning 'to surpass') plus 'draw.'
