Saint Gregory I

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈɡrɛɡəri ðə ˈfɜrst / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈɡrɛɡəri ðə ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
(c. 540–604) Pope of the Roman Catholic Church, renowned for his leadership and for being declared a saint and Doctor of the Church.
Pope and saint, known as Gregory the Great

Examples

Saint Gregory I is credited with instigating the first recorded large-scale mission from Rome to convert the Anglo-Saxons in England.
The Gregorian Chant is named after Saint Gregory I, who encouraged the standardization of church music.
Many historians consider Saint Gregory I one of the most influential popes of the early Middle Ages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pope Gregory the Great - another name for Saint Gregory I, pope and saint
