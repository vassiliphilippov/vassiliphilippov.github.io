deplorably

1. ADV

/ dɪˈplɔːrəbli / (AmE), / dɪˈplɔːrəbli / (BrE)
In a manner deserving strong condemnation; in a very bad or unfortunate way.
in a very bad or unfortunate manner

Examples

The building was deplorably maintained, with peeling paint and broken windows.
The prisoners were kept in deplorably overcrowded cells.
He behaved deplorably during the debate, interrupting and insulting the other speakers.

Synset Examples

he was sadly neglected
it was woefully inadequate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woefully - in an extremely sad or inadequate way
  • Antonym: admirably - in a way that deserves admiration or approval

Etymology

From deplorable + -ly, the adverbial suffix.
