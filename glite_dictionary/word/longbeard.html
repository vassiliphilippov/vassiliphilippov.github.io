longbeard

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔŋˌbɪrd / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋˌbɪəd / (BrE)
a stoneware drinking jug with a long neck, decorated with a caricature of Cardinal Bellarmine, commonly used in the 17th century
17th-century stoneware jug with a caricature

Examples

The museum displayed a rare longbeard among other 17th-century artifacts.
Collectors value the detailed artwork on the necks of original longbeards.
He poured ale from a longbeard into his cup during the historical reenactment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bellarmine jug - a stoneware jug with a bearded face, synonymous term

Etymology

From the appearance of a bearded face on the jug's neck, often referring to Cardinal Bellarmine.
