Chamaemelum nobilis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkeɪ.miˈmiː.ləm ˈnoʊ.bɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / ˌkeɪ.məˈmiː.ləm ˈnɒb.ɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
A Eurasian plant with apple-scented foliage, white-rayed flowers, and feathery leaves, used medicinally and sometimes known as Roman chamomile.
Roman chamomile plant

Examples

Chamaemelum nobilis is often used in herbal teas for its calming effect.
The garden was filled with the sweet scent of Chamaemelum nobilis in bloom.
Many traditional remedies use extracts from Chamaemelum nobilis for soothing digestive discomfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Roman chamomile - Common English name for Chamaemelum nobilis, a medicinal herb

Etymology

From Latin: 'chamaemelum' meaning chamomile, and 'nobilis' meaning notable or noble.
