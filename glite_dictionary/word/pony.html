pony

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊni / (AmE), / ˈpəʊni / (BrE)
An informal term for a racehorse, especially in the context of horse racing or betting.
informal: racehorse (betting)
informal

Examples

He enjoys betting on the ponies at the racetrack every Saturday.
Last weekend, she won big after picking the winning pony.
The excitement was palpable as the ponies thundered down the final stretch.

Synset Examples

he liked to bet on the ponies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: racehorse - a horse used for racing, especially in professional settings

2. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊni / (AmE), / ˈpəʊni / (BrE)
a small horse of any of several breeds, typically less than five feet (1.5 meters) high at the shoulder, often gentle in temperament
small breed of horse

Examples

The children took turns riding the pony at the farm.
She has always wanted a pony for her birthday.
The ponies in the competition were gentle and well-trained.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: horse - large domesticated ungulate used for riding and work

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic *ponaidh* or possibly from French *poulenet* (little foal).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊni / (AmE), / ˈpəʊni / (BrE)
a word-for-word translation or a crib used by students to help understand a foreign text, often regarded as cheating
a literal translation or study aid for foreign texts

Examples

He was caught using a pony to translate his Latin homework.
The use of a pony is discouraged in language exams to promote genuine learning.
She relied on a pony to get through her ancient Greek class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crib - a translation or set of notes used to aid study, especially considered cheating

Etymology

Originally American English; possibly from the idea of a 'pony' as a small or easy version of something, meaning an accessible help or aid.
