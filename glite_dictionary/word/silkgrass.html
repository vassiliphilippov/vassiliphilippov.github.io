silkgrass

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlkˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈsɪlkˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a valuable forage grass found in dry upland areas and plains of western North America to northern Mexico
a forage grass of western North America

Examples

Ranchers often rely on silkgrass as a nutritious source of fodder for their cattle.
The silkgrass flourished across the dry plains after the rainy season.
Botanists are studying the drought resistance of silkgrass in western regions.

Etymology

From 'silk' (for its silky appearance) + 'grass'.
