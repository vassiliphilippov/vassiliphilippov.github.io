unconscionable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkɑnʃənəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈkɒnʃənəbl / (BrE)
So morally wrong or unreasonable as to be completely unacceptable; lacking in conscience or principles.
completely unacceptable; lacking conscience

Examples

It was an unconscionable act of greed that harmed many innocent people.
They charged an unconscionable amount of money for a simple repair.
His unconscionable lies destroyed her reputation.

Synset Examples

a conscienceless villain
brash, unprincipled, and conscienceless
an unconscionable liar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conscienceless - lacking conscience
  • Antonym: principled - having strong moral principles

Etymology

From Late Latin 'unconscionabilis', from Latin 'conscientia' (knowledge, conscience) + '-able'.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkɑn.ʃə.nə.bəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈkɒn.ʃə.nə.bəl / (BrE)
(of an action, amount, etc.) excessive or beyond what is reasonable or acceptable.
excessive or unreasonable

Examples

Their landlord charged an unconscionable amount of money for a tiny apartment.
It would be unconscionable to expect people to work under such dangerous conditions.
The company was criticized for its unconscionable profit margins.

Synset Examples

exorbitant rent
extortionate prices
spends an outrageous amount on entertainment
usurious interest rate
unconscionable spending

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exorbitant - (especially of a price or amount) much too high
  • Antonym: reasonable - fair and sensible; not excessive

Etymology

From late Latin 'unconscionabilis', from Latin 'conscientia' (conscience).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01538377-s
Synset POS: s
