putrescence

1. NOUN

/ pjuˈtrɛsəns / (AmE), / pjuːˈtrɛsns / (BrE)
the process or state of decaying and rotting, especially with a foul smell, as in organic matter
state of rotting or decay

Examples

The putrid odor signaled the putrescence of forgotten food in the back of the refrigerator.
Studies of soil health often include measures of putrescence from decomposing organic material.
The abandoned building was filled with the unmistakable putrescence of decaying matter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decay - the process of rotting or decomposition
  • Synonym: putrefaction - the process of organic matter rotting
  • Antonym: freshness - the state of being fresh, not decayed

Etymology

From Latin putrescentia, from putrescere (to rot).

2. NOUN

/ pjuˈtrɛsəns / (AmE), / pjuˈtrɛsəns / (BrE)
The state or process of decaying and producing a foul smell; rottenness.
state or process of rotting

Examples

The putrescence of the abandoned fruit attracted swarms of flies to the orchard.
Within days, the putrescence of the meat became unbearable to those nearby.
The biologist studied the process of putrescence in decaying plant material.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decay - the process of rotting or decomposition
  • Synonym: putrefaction - the process of decay or rotting in a body or organic matter
  • Antonym: freshness - state of being fresh, not decayed

Etymology

From Latin 'putrescentia', from 'putrescere' (to rot, become rotten).
