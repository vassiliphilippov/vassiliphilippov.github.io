stele

1. NOUN

/ ˈstiːli / (AmE), / ˈstiːli / (BrE)
the central cylinder of vascular tissue within the stem or root of a vascular plant, typically cylindrical in shape
central vascular core of a plant stem or root

Examples

The stele of a dicot root contains both xylem and phloem arranged in a characteristic star shape.
Scientists examined the stele to determine the pattern of vascular tissue in the plant stem.
Damage to the stele can disrupt the flow of water and nutrients within the plant.

Etymology

From Greek stēlē (pillar, column).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstiːli / (AmE), / ˈstiːli / (BrE)
An upright stone slab or pillar, often inscribed or carved, erected as a monument or marker, especially in ancient times.
ancient inscribed stone slab

Examples

The Rosetta Stone is a famous stele inscribed with three scripts.
Archaeologists discovered a stele dating back to the ancient Egyptian civilization.
The museum displayed a Mayan stele depicting a royal figure and hieroglyphs.

Etymology

From Greek stēlē (block of stone, gravestone, pillar).
