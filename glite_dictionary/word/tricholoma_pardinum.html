Tricholoma pardinum

1. NOUN

/ trɪkəˈloʊmə pɑrˈdaɪnəm / (AmE), / trɪkəˈləʊmə pɑːˈdɪnəm / (BrE)
A species of poisonous mushroom with a pale cap covered in fine grey fibrils, found in woodlands of Europe and North America.
a species of poisonous mushroom

Examples

The hiker accidentally picked a Tricholoma pardinum while foraging in the woods.
Experts advise avoiding Tricholoma pardinum due to its highly toxic properties.
The spotted cap of Tricholoma pardinum makes it easy to confuse with edible species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mushroom - a fungal fruiting body, often edible or inedible
  • Synonym: toadstool - another term for poisonous mushrooms

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining the genus name 'Tricholoma' (from Greek tricho- 'hair' + loma 'fringe') and the species name 'pardinum', from Latin 'pardus' (leopard), referring to its spotted cap.
