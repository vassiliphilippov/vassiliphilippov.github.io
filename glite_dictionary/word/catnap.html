catnap

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæt.næp / (AmE), / ˈkæt.næp / (BrE)
A short, light sleep, usually taken during the day and not in bed.
a short, light sleep

Examples

He closed his eyes and enjoyed a quick catnap during his lunch break.
After a long meeting, she took a catnap on the office couch.
Sometimes a brief catnap in the afternoon can boost your energy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nap - a short sleep, especially during the day
  • Synonym: doze - to sleep lightly or briefly

Etymology

Formed by analogy with a cat's habit of taking brief naps.

2. VERB

/ ˈkætˌnæp / (AmE), / ˈkatˌnap / (BrE)
to take a short, light sleep, usually during the day
take a short, light nap

Examples

After working all morning, she decided to catnap on the sofa for a few minutes.
He often catnaps during his lunch break to recharge.
You can catnap on the train if you're feeling tired.

Synset Examples

She naps everyday after lunch for an hour

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nap - to sleep briefly, especially during the day
  • Synonym: doze - to sleep lightly or nap

Etymology

From 'cat' + 'nap', referring to the way cats sleep lightly and frequently. Term dates to mid-19th century.
