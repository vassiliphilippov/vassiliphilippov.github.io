warrigal

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔrɪɡəl / (AmE), / ˈwɒrɪɡəl / (BrE)
A wolflike yellowish-brown wild dog native to Australia; also known as a dingo.
Australian wild dog; dingo

Examples

The warrigal is an important figure in many Aboriginal stories.
Farmers often worry about warrigals preying on their livestock.
Unlike domestic dogs, the warrigal is well adapted to the harsh Australian outback.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dingo - A wild dog found in Australia, also called warrigal

Etymology

From Dharug (an Australian Aboriginal language) *warrigal* (wild dog).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɒr.ɪ.ɡəl / (AmE), / ˈwɒr.ɪ.ɡəl / (BrE)
An Australian wild horse, also known as a brumby.
Australian wild horse
british

Examples

A herd of warrigals galloped across the dusty plains at dawn.
The outback is home to many warrigals that roam freely.
They spotted a lone warrigal drinking by the creek during their trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brumby - a wild horse in Australia

Etymology

From Dharug (an Aboriginal language of the Sydney region) *warrigal*, meaning 'wild' or 'wild dog'.
