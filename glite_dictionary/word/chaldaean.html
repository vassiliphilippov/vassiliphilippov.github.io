Chaldaean

1. ADJ

/ kælˈdiːən / (AmE), / kalˈdiːən / (BrE)
of or relating to ancient Chaldea, its people, language, or culture.
relating to ancient Chaldea or its people

Examples

The ancient texts were written in Chaldaean script.
A Chaldaean priest was renowned for his knowledge of the stars.
He specializes in Chaldaean history and archaeology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Babylonian - of or relating to Babylonia or its people, language, or culture

Etymology

From Latin Chaldaeus, from Greek Chaldaios, from Chaldea (an ancient region in Mesopotamia).

Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ kælˈdiən / (AmE), / kalˈdiːən / (BrE)
A wise man skilled in occult learning, especially one from ancient Babylonia.
wise man in occult learning (Babylonian)

Examples

The king consulted a Chaldaean to interpret the strange dreams that troubled his sleep.
In ancient times, a Chaldaean was revered for his knowledge of the stars and mystical arts.
The wise Chaldaean studied sacred texts by candlelight, seeking hidden truths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magi - a member of a priestly caste skilled in astrology and magic, especially in ancient Persia and Babylonia

Etymology

From Latin 'Chaldaeus', from Greek 'Khaldaios', meaning 'Babylonian'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09925418-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ kælˈdiːən / (AmE), / kælˈdiːən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of ancient Chaldea, a region in southern Babylonia (modern-day Iraq)
inhabitant of ancient Chaldea

Examples

The museum features artifacts created by a Chaldaean craftsman.
Many scholars have studied the astronomical knowledge of the Chaldaeans.
A Chaldaean would have spoken a dialect of the Akkadian language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Babylonian - an inhabitant of ancient Babylonia

Etymology

From Latin Chaldaeus, from Greek Khaldaîos, from Akkadian Kaldu (Chaldea).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09661306-n
Synset POS: n
