Tylenchidae

1. NOUN

/ taɪˈlɛntʃɪˌdiː / (AmE), / taɪˈlɛntʃɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of nematode worms that includes several subfamilies, some of which are plant parasites.
family of nematode worms

Examples

Members of the Tylenchidae family can cause crop damage by parasitizing plant roots.
The Tylenchidae include important species for agricultural nematology research.
Scientists classified the new nematode under the Tylenchidae family due to its morphological traits.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Tylenchus + suffix -idae indicating animal family.

2. NOUN

/ taɪˈlɛntʃɪˌdi / (AmE), / tɪˈlɛntʃɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of nematode worms that includes the subfamilies Atylenchinae, Boleodorinae, Ecphyadophorinae, Tylenchinae, and Tylodorinae
family of nematode worms

Examples

Many plant-parasitic nematodes belong to the Tylenchidae family and can impact crop yields.
The scientist discovered a new species within Tylenchidae during her soil survey.
Members of Tylenchidae are often studied for their interactions with agricultural plants.

Etymology

From the genus name Tylenchus, which comes from Greek 'tylos' (knot) and 'enchos' (spear), with the suffix -idae for families.
