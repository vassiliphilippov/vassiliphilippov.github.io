Kyrgyzstan

1. PROPN

/ kɜrˈgiːstæn / (AmE), / kɪəˈɡɪstæn / (BrE)
a landlocked country in Central Asia, bordered by Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan, Tajikistan, and China; became independent from the Soviet Union in 1991
landlocked Central Asian country

Examples

She traveled through Kyrgyzstan to experience its stunning mountain landscapes.
The president of Kyrgyzstan announced new economic reforms last year.
Much of Kyrgyzstan is covered by the towering Tian Shan mountains.

Etymology

From the Russian 'Киргизстан' (Kirgizstan), from Kyrgyz 'Кыргызстан' (Kyrgyzstan), meaning 'land of the Kyrgyz'.
