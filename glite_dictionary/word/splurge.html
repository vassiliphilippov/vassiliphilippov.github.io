splurge

1. NOUN

/ splɝdʒ / (AmE), / splɜːdʒ / (BrE)
an instance of spending or indulging oneself excessively, especially in a showy or extravagant way
an act of excessive indulgence or spending

Examples

After months of saving, she finally went on a splurge at her favorite clothing store.
His latest tech splurge left his bank account nearly empty.
The couple treated themselves to a fancy dinner as a little splurge on their anniversary.

Synset Examples

an orgy of shopping
an emotional binge
a splurge of spending

Related Terms

  • Synonym: binge - a period of excessive indulgence, especially in eating or drinking
Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ splɝdʒ / (AmE), / splɜːdʒ / (BrE)
an act of spending money freely or extravagantly, often to show off or make an impression
extravagant display or spending

Examples

She went on a splurge and bought designer clothes she didn't really need.
After receiving his bonus, Mark treated himself to a weekend splurge at a luxury hotel.
They admired the couple's latest splurge, a brand-new sports car parked outside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spree - a lively or wild outburst of activity, especially spending or enjoyment
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ splɝdʒ / (AmE), / splɜːdʒ / (BrE)
to behave in a showy or ostentatious way, often displaying wealth or luxury
be ostentatious or show off

Examples

He loves to splurge at parties, always wearing designer clothes and flashy jewelry.
The celebrity splurged on a lavish wedding just to impress her fans.
They were splurging in the VIP section, making sure everyone noticed their expensive champagne.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to show off - to behave in a way meant to attract attention or admiration
  • Synonym: boast - to talk with excessive pride and self-satisfaction

4. VERB

/ splɝdʒ / (AmE), / splɜːdʒ / (BrE)
to spend a lot of money on something in order to indulge oneself, especially in an extravagant or self-indulgent way
spend extravagantly to indulge oneself

Examples

After getting her bonus, she decided to splurge on a fancy dinner.
Sometimes it's nice to splurge a little on things you love.
He rarely splurges, but he couldn't resist those designer shoes.

Synset Examples

I splurged on a new TV

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to spend - to pay out money for something
