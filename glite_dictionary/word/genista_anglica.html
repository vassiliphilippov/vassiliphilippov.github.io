Genista anglica

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈnɪstə æŋˈlɪkə / (AmE), / dʒɪˈnɪstə æŋˈlɪkə / (BrE)
a prickly shrub with yellow flowers native to the moors of New England and Europe, belonging to the pea family
prickly yellow-flowered moorland shrub

Examples

The Genista anglica is valued by some gardeners for its vibrant yellow blossoms.
Walking across the moor, she noticed patches of Genista anglica among the heather.
Genista anglica thrives in acidic, sandy soils where few other shrubs can grow.

Etymology

From Latin: Genista (the genus name), possibly from a Celtic root; anglica meaning 'English'.
