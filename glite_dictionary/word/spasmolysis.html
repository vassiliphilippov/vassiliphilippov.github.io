spasmolysis

1. NOUN

/ spæzˈmɑlɪsɪs / (AmE), / spæzˈmɒlɪsɪs / (BrE)
the relaxation or relief of muscle spasms, especially as a result of medical treatment
relief of muscle spasms (medical)
medical

Examples

The drug was administered to induce spasmolysis in the patient's cramped muscles.
Effective spasmolysis can provide quick relief to those suffering from frequent muscle spasms.
Research continues into new therapies that promote spasmolysis without significant side effects.

Etymology

From Greek 'spasmos' (spasm) + 'lysis' (loosening, dissolving).
