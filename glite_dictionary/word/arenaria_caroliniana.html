Arenaria caroliniana

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛrəˈnɛriə ˌkɛrəˌlaɪˈniːənə / (AmE), / ˌarɪˈnɛːrɪə ˌkarəˌlʌɪˈnɪːənə / (BrE)
A deep-rooted perennial plant native to the southeastern United States, belonging to the sandwort genus.
deep-rooted perennial plant of the southeastern US

Examples

Botanists discovered a thriving patch of Arenaria caroliniana in the pine forest reserve.
Arenaria caroliniana is well adapted to sandy soils and dry conditions.
The delicate white flowers of Arenaria caroliniana appear each spring in the southeastern United States.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Arenaria' meaning 'sandwort' and 'caroliniana' referring to the Carolinas in the United States.
