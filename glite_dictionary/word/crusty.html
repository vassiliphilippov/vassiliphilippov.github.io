crusty

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrʌsti / (AmE), / ˈkrʌsti / (BrE)
having a hard, crisp outer layer or covering, like a crust
having a hard outer crust

Examples

The bakery is famous for its crusty sourdough bread.
After a day in the sun, mud had dried into a crusty layer on his boots.
She loves the crusty top of a freshly baked casserole.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: soft - not hard or firm to the touch

Etymology

From 'crust' + '-y', formed from Latin 'crusta' (rind, shell) via Old French and Middle English.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkrʌsti / (AmE), / ˈkrʌsti / (BrE)
(of a person or their manner) brusque, surly, or forbidding; easily irritated or gruff.
gruff or surly in manner

Examples

The crusty old professor snapped at his students for talking during class.
Despite his crusty demeanor, he had a kind heart underneath.
Her crusty remarks sometimes made people uncomfortable, but she was honest.

Synset Examples

crusty remarks
a crusty old man
his curmudgeonly temper
gruff manner
a gruff reply

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gruff - rough or abrupt in manner or speech
  • Synonym: curmudgeonly - bad-tempered or surly, especially in older people
  • Antonym: pleasant - friendly and agreeable in manner

Etymology

From 'crust', originally describing something with a hard outer layer; figurative use from the late 19th century for people with a tough or harsh exterior.
