carrefour

1. NOUN

/ ˈkærəˌfʊr / (AmE), / ˈkærəˌfʊə / (BrE)
an intersection where two or more streets or roads cross each other; a crossroads or junction
a road or street junction; crossroads

Examples

The small market stood at the busy carrefour where villagers from every direction would meet.
We waited for the parade to reach the main carrefour before joining the crowd.
At the ancient carrefour, four dusty paths converged beneath the shade of a single oak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crossroads - an intersection of two or more roads

Etymology

From French 'carrefour' (crossroads), from Late Latin 'quadrifurcus' (four-forked).
