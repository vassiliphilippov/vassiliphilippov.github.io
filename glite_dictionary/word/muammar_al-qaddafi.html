Muammar al-Qaddafi

1. PROPN

/ muˈɑːmɑr ɑl kəˈdɑːfi / (AmE), / muˈɑːmɑːr æl kəˈdæfi / (BrE)
(1942–2011) The Libyan political leader who ruled Libya from 1969, after deposing the monarchy in a military coup, until 2011; known for imposing socialist policies and promoting Islamic orthodoxy.
Libyan leader (1969–2011)

Examples

Muammar al-Qaddafi ruled Libya for over four decades until his fall in 2011.
Many world leaders criticized the human rights record of Muammar al-Qaddafi.
The military coup led by Muammar al-Qaddafi in 1969 dramatically changed Libya's political landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gaddafi - Alternative spelling of Muammar al-Qaddafi, Libyan leader (1969–2011)
  • Antonym: Libyan monarchy - Monarchy in Libya that was overthrown by Muammar al-Qaddafi in 1969
