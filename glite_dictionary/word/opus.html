opus

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.pəs / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.pəs / (BrE)
a musical work, especially one created by a composer, often numbered to indicate its order among a composer's works
a musical or artistic work

Examples

Beethoven's Ninth Symphony is his most famous opus.
The orchestra performed a new opus by the young composer.
Each opus in the collection shows a different stage of his musical development.

Synset Examples

the composition is written in four movements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: composition - a musical or artistic creation
  • Synonym: piece - a musical or artistic work

Etymology

From Latin opus (work, labor, accomplishment).
