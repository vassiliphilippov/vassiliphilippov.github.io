Cirsium

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜr.si.əm / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.si.əm / (BrE)
A genus of thistle plants known as plume thistles, widely found in temperate regions and characterized by their spiny leaves and fluffy, feathery seed heads.
genus of thistle plants (plume thistles)

Examples

The field was dotted with Cirsium, their purple flowers standing out against the grass.
Gardeners often remove Cirsium to prevent it from spreading in cultivated beds.
Some species of Cirsium provide food for butterflies and birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thistle - plant with prickly leaves and stems, in the family Asteraceae

Etymology

From Latin *cirsium*, from Ancient Greek *kirsion* (a kind of thistle).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɜrsiəm / (AmE), / ˈsɜːsiəm / (BrE)
A genus of thistle plants, commonly known as plume thistles, found in the family Asteraceae.
a genus of thistle plants

Examples

Several species within the Cirsium genus are native to North America and Europe.
The tall, spiny stems of Cirsium attract many types of pollinators.
Gardeners often remove Cirsium to control the spread of thistles in their fields.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, derived from Greek 'kirsion,' a kind of thistle.
