velum

1. NOUN

/ ˈviːləm / (AmE), / ˈviːləm / (BrE)
a membrane or membranous covering attached to the immature fruiting body of certain mushrooms
membranous covering in immature mushrooms

Examples

The velum protects the spore-producing surface of the mushroom until it matures.
As the mushroom grows, the velum breaks, leaving remnants on the stem or cap.
Careful observation of the velum can help identify certain mushroom species.

Etymology

From Latin 'vēlum' meaning 'sail' or 'covering'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈviːləm / (AmE), / ˈviːləm / (BrE)
a muscular flap at the back of the mouth that closes off the nasopharynx during swallowing or speaking
muscular flap at the back of the mouth
medical

Examples

During swallowing, the velum lifts to prevent food from entering the nasal cavity.
A cleft velum can lead to speech difficulties in children.
The doctor explained how the velum functions in separating the oral and nasal passages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soft palate - the soft part at the back of the roof of the mouth, synonymous with velum

Etymology

From Latin velum (sail, curtain, covering).
