to interrogate

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈtɛrəˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛr.ə.ɡeɪt / (BrE)
(in telecommunications) To transmit a signal to elicit a specific response from a device or system.
send a signal to get a response (tech)

Examples

The central computer will interrogate the sensors every five minutes to check their status.
In radio frequency identification, a reader interrogates the tag by sending out a signal.
The server interrogates each connected device to authenticate it before allowing access.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: query - request information from a computer or database
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00975562-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈtɛrəˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛr.ə.ɡeɪt / (BrE)
to question someone thoroughly and formally, often in order to get information, especially by the police or during official investigations
question formally or aggressively

Examples

The detective began to interrogate the suspect about his whereabouts during the incident.
Officers have the right to interrogate witnesses in order to gather more evidence for the case.
She was interrogated for hours about the missing documents.

Synset Examples

The suspect was questioned by the police
We questioned the survivor about the details of the explosion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: question - to ask someone questions, often to seek information

Etymology

From Latin interrogare (to ask, to question), from inter- (among) + rogare (to ask).
