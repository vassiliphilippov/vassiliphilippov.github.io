mellisonant

1. ADJ

/ məˈlɪsənənt / (AmE), / məˈlɪsənənt / (BrE)
(of a sound) pleasing to the ear; sweet-sounding.
pleasing or sweet-sounding (of sound)

Examples

The mellisonant notes of the piano filled the hall with warmth.
Her voice was so mellisonant that everyone fell silent to listen.
They admired the mellisonant harmonies of the choir during the concert.

Synset Examples

the dulcet tones of the cello

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mellifluous - (of a voice or sound) pleasant to hear, sweet-sounding
  • Antonym: cacophonous - having a harsh, unpleasant sound

Etymology

From Latin 'mellis' (honey) + 'sonant' (sounding).
