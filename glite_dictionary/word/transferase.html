transferase

1. NOUN

/ trænˈsfɛrˌeɪs / (AmE), / trɑːnˈsfɪəˌeɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that catalyzes the transfer of a specific chemical group from one molecule to another
enzyme transferring chemical groups
medical

Examples

A transferase plays a crucial role by shifting a phosphate group from ATP to another molecule.
The laboratory identified a defect in a specific transferase enzyme.
Certain genetic disorders are linked to abnormal activity of transferase enzymes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enzyme - a protein that acts as a catalyst in living organisms

Etymology

From 'transfer' + '-ase', common suffix for enzymes.
