Pyrola rotundifolia

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈroʊlə roʊˌtʌndɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / paɪˈrəʊlə rəʊˌtʌndɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A Eurasian and North American evergreen plant with rounded leaves and fragrant creamy-white flowers, commonly known as round-leaved wintergreen.
species of evergreen plant

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with Pyrola rotundifolia, its white blossoms emitting a delicate scent.
Botanists recorded the presence of Pyrola rotundifolia across much of northern Europe and North America.
During the hike, we identified Pyrola rotundifolia by its rounded leaves and creamy-white flowers.

Etymology

From Latin: 'pyrola' (a diminutive of 'pyrus', pear, referencing the pear-like leaves) and 'rotundifolia' (round-leaved).
