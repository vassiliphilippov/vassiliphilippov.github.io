enthronement

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈθroʊnmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈθrəʊnmənt / (BrE)
the formal ceremony of placing a monarch on the throne and investing them with the symbols of sovereign authority
ceremony of installing a monarch

Examples

The enthronement of the new king was attended by leaders from around the world.
Her enthronement marked a new era for the monarchy in the country.
The tradition of a formal enthronement ceremony goes back several centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coronation - the ceremony of crowning a king or queen

Etymology

From Old French 'enthroner', from Latin 'in-' + 'thronus' (throne).
