methyldopa

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛθiˈldəʊpə / (AmE), / ˌmiːθɪlˈdəʊpə / (BrE)
an antihypertensive drug used to treat high blood pressure, known by the trade name Aldomet
antihypertensive drug for high blood pressure
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed methyldopa to help manage her high blood pressure.
Patients taking methyldopa should regularly monitor their liver function.
During pregnancy, methyldopa is often considered a safe option for controlling hypertension.

Etymology

From 'methyl' (relating to a methyl group) and 'dopa' (dihydroxyphenylalanine, an amino acid).
