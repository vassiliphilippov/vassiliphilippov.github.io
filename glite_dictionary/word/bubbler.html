bubbler

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌb.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈbʌb.lə / (BrE)
a public drinking fountain that provides a jet of water for drinking
drinking fountain (chiefly US regional)
american

Examples

I stopped at the bubbler in the park to get a drink of water.
Many schools have a bubbler in the hallway for students to use.
In Wisconsin, people often refer to a drinking fountain as a bubbler.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drinking fountain - a public device that supplies water for drinking

Etymology

American English regionalism; from 'bubble' + '-er'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03246038-n

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌblɚ / (AmE), / ˈbʌblə / (BrE)
a device that forces air or gas through a liquid, causing bubbles, often used in laboratories or aquariums
device for bubbling air or gas through liquid

Examples

The aquarium contains a bubbler to keep the water oxygenated.
Laboratory technicians used a bubbler to mix gases with the solution.
A bubbler can help maintain a healthy environment for fish by increasing air circulation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerator - a device that introduces air into a substance
