Herpestes

1. NOUN

/ hɚˈpɛstiːz / (AmE), / hɜːˈpɛstiːz / (BrE)
A genus of small to medium-sized carnivorous mammals known as mongooses, native to southern Eurasia and Africa.
genus of mongooses

Examples

The Indian grey mongoose belongs to the genus Herpestes.
Several species within Herpestes are skilled at hunting venomous snakes.
Scientists studied the habitats of various Herpestes species in Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mongoose - small carnivorous mammal noted for killing snakes

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'herpestes' (a kind of long-bodied animal, from 'herpein', to creep).
