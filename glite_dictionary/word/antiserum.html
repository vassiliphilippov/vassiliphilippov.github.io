antiserum

1. NOUN

/ ˌæntiˈsɪrəm / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈsɪərəm / (BrE)
a blood serum that contains antibodies against specific antigens and is used to confer immunity to a particular disease
blood serum with disease-specific antibodies
medical

Examples

The doctor administered an antiserum to protect the patient from the effects of the venom.
Large quantities of antiserum were produced during the outbreak to provide immediate immunity.
Scientists are developing a new antiserum to combat the emerging virus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serum - the clear fluid part of blood that remains after clotting
  • Synonym: antibody - a protein produced by the immune system to neutralize pathogens

Etymology

From anti- (against) + serum; first used in late 19th century medical science.
