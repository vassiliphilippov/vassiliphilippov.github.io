roundabout

1. NOUN

/ ˈraʊndəˌbaʊt / (AmE), / ˈraʊndəˌbaʊt / (BrE)
a road junction where traffic moves in a circle around a central island
circular road junction

Examples

The city installed a new roundabout to improve traffic flow at the busy intersection.
Many drivers are unsure about the correct way to enter a roundabout.
There were flowers planted in the center of the roundabout near the school.

Synset Examples

the accident blocked all traffic at the rotary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rotary - a road junction similar to a roundabout, used especially in American English
  • Antonym: intersection - a point where two or more roads cross each other
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04473168-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈraʊndəˌbaʊt / (AmE), / ˈraʊndəbaʊt / (BrE)
not direct; taking a longer or more circuitous route or approach than necessary
indirect or circuitous (of a route, action, etc.)

Examples

We took a roundabout way home to avoid the roadworks.
His explanation of the issue was unnecessarily roundabout and confusing.
They reached the village by a roundabout route through the mountains.

Synset Examples

a scenic but devious route
a long and circuitous journey by train and boat
a roundabout route avoided rush-hour traffic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: direct - going straight to the destination without deviation
  • Synonym: circuitous - longer than the most direct way; indirect

Etymology

From 'round about', formerly used as an adverb meaning 'in a circuitous manner'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈraʊndəˌbaʊt / (AmE), / ˈraʊndəbaʊt / (BrE)
not done or said directly; indirect in manner, speech, or conduct
indirect; not straightforward

Examples

Her answer was so roundabout that no one understood what she truly meant.
He took a roundabout approach to ask for help, never stating his needs outright.
We eventually got the message, but it came in the most roundabout way possible.

Synset Examples

the explanation was circuitous and puzzling
a roundabout paragraph
hear in a roundabout way that her ex-husband was marrying her best friend

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circuitous - not direct; longer than the most straightforward way
  • Antonym: direct - straightforward and without deviation or indirection

Etymology

From the phrase 'round about', Middle English 'round aboute' (around, about).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00770909-s
Synset POS: s

4. NOUN

/ ˈraʊndəˌbaʊt / (AmE), / ˈraʊndəˌbaʊt / (BrE)
a large, rotating machine with seats, often shaped like horses or animals, on which children ride for amusement, especially at fairs or amusement parks
rotating amusement ride for children
british

Examples

The children laughed excitedly as the roundabout spun faster under the bright lights of the fairground.
She promised her son a ride on the roundabout after he finished his ice cream.
Dozens of kids queued up to get a seat on the colorful roundabout in the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carousel - rotating amusement ride with seats or animals for children
  • Synonym: merry-go-round - amusement ride that rotates with seats for riders

Etymology

From 'round about' meaning turning in a circle; this sense especially common in British English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02969645-n
Synset POS: n
