Turnix sylvatica

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝː.nɪks sɪlˈvætɪkə / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.nɪks sɪlˈvætɪkə / (BrE)
a species of buttonquail with distinctive striped plumage, found in Asia and parts of Africa
species of striped buttonquail

Examples

The Turnix sylvatica is often found in grassy or woodland habitats across South and Southeast Asia.
Conservationists are concerned about the declining numbers of Turnix sylvatica in some regions.
Unlike true quails, the Turnix sylvatica exhibits unique nesting behaviors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buttonquail - small terrestrial birds of the family Turnicidae

Etymology

From Latin: Turnix (genus name for buttonquails), sylvatica (of the woods).
