Tayassu

1. NOUN

/ taɪˈæs.uː / (AmE), / taɪˈas.juː / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Tayassuidae, consisting of certain species of peccaries native to the Americas.
genus of peccaries (Tayassuidae)

Examples

The fossil record indicates that Tayassu had a wider distribution in prehistoric times.
Researchers observed a group of wild Tayassu in the dense forests of South America.
The genus Tayassu includes species commonly known as white-lipped peccaries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peccary - a pig-like hoofed mammal found in the Americas

Etymology

From Latinized form of the Tupi word 'tayaçu', meaning grande boar or large pig.

2. NOUN

/ taɪˈæs.uː / (AmE), / taɪˈæs.juː / (BrE)
A genus of peccaries (hoofed mammals) in the family Tayassuidae, native to Central and South America.
genus of peccaries

Examples

The Tayassu genus includes several species of wild pigs native to the Americas.
Scientists observed a herd belonging to the Tayassu in the Amazon rainforest.
The white-lipped peccary is classified under the Tayassu genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peccary - pig-like hoofed mammal found in the Americas

Etymology

From the Portuguese 'tayaçu', derived from Tupi, referring to the white-lipped peccary.
