airmanship

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛr.mənˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈeə.mən.ʃɪp / (BrE)
skill and knowledge in operating an aircraft safely and effectively; the art of piloting.
skill in piloting aircraft

Examples

His exceptional airmanship helped the team complete the mission safely.
She received several awards for her airmanship during the training exercises.
Good airmanship requires both technical skill and sound judgment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pilotage - the act or skill of piloting, especially of an aircraft or ship

Etymology

From 'airman' (a person who operates aircraft) + '-ship' (denoting skill or art).
