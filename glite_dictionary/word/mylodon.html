Mylodon

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪlədɒn / (AmE), / ˈmaɪlədɒn / (BrE)
A genus of extinct ground sloths belonging to the family Mylodontidae, sometimes classified under Megatheriidae.
genus of extinct ground sloths

Examples

The remains of a Mylodon were discovered in a cave in southern Chile.
Scientists believe that the Mylodon lived during the Pleistocene epoch.
The Mylodon is notable for its large, flat teeth adapted for grinding plants.

Etymology

From Greek 'mylos' (molar) and 'odous' (tooth), referring to its teeth.
