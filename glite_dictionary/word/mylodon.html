mylodon

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪlədɒn / (AmE), / ˈmɪlədɒn / (BrE)
a large, extinct genus of ground sloths from the Pleistocene epoch in South America, known for its bear-like size and edentate (toothless or reduced tooth) features
extinct giant ground sloth genus

Examples

The mylodon roamed South America during the Pleistocene, feeding on tough vegetation.
Fossilized skin and bones of mylodon have been discovered in Patagonian caves.
Paleontologists believe that the extinction of the mylodon was due to climate change and human activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground sloth - any of several extinct large, slow-moving mammals of the family Megatheriidae

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name Mylodon, from Greek 'mylos' (molar, grinder) and 'odous, odont-' (tooth).
