Scirpus cyperinus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪr.pəs saɪ.pəˈraɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪə.pəs sɪ.pəˈraɪ.nəs / (BrE)
A species of sedge native to eastern North America, characterized by numerous clustered woolly spikelets.
a North American sedge species

Examples

The wet meadows were dotted with Scirpus cyperinus, standing tall among the grasses.
Many ecologists note that Scirpus cyperinus provides important habitat for wetland wildlife.
Gardeners sometimes plant Scirpus cyperinus for its decorative woolly spikelets in native landscapes.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Scirpus' meaning 'bulrush', and 'cyperinus' meaning 'like a sedge' from 'cyperus'.
