Sputnik

1. NOUN

/ ˈspʊtnɪk, spuːtˈnɪk / (AmE), / ˈspʊtnɪk, spʌtˈnɪk / (BrE)
the name of the first Russian artificial satellite, and also used generally for Russian artificial satellites launched into orbit
first Russian artificial satellite

Examples

The launch of Sputnik in 1957 marked the beginning of the space age.
Many scientists were amazed at how quickly Sputnik completed an orbit around the Earth.
After hearing about Sputnik, the United States accelerated its own space program.

Synset Examples

Sputnik was the first man-made satellite to orbit the earth

Etymology

From Russian Спутник (Sputnik), meaning 'companion' or 'satellite'.
