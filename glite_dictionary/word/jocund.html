jocund

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɑːkənd / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒkənd / (BrE)
cheerful and lighthearted; full of high-spirited joy or merriment
cheerful and lighthearted

Examples

The children were in a jocund mood as they played in the sunshine.
Wordsworth describes the daffodils in his poem as a jocund company.
Her jocund laughter filled the quiet room, lifting everyone's spirits.

Synset Examples

when hearts were young and gay
a poet could not but be gay, in such a jocund company
the jolly crowd at the reunion
jolly old Saint Nick
a jovial old gentleman
have a merry Christmas
peals of merry laughter
a mirthful laugh

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joyful - feeling, expressing, or causing great pleasure and happiness
  • Synonym: jovial - cheerful and friendly
  • Antonym: gloomy - causing or feeling depression or sadness

Etymology

From Latin jocundus (pleasant, agreeable), from iocus (joke).
