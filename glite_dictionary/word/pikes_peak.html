Pike's Peak

1. PROPN

/ ˈpaɪks piːk / (AmE), / ˈpaɪks piːk / (BrE)
a prominent mountain peak in the Rocky Mountains of central Colorado, United States, rising to 14,109 feet (4,301 meters)
mountain peak in Colorado, USA

Examples

Every summer, hikers from around the world attempt to reach the summit of Pike's Peak.
The breathtaking view from Pike's Peak attracts thousands of tourists each year.
She trained for months before tackling the challenging trails up Pike's Peak.

Etymology

Named after American explorer Zebulon Pike, who led an expedition to the area in 1806.
