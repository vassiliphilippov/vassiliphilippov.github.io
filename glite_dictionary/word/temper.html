temper

1. VERB

/ ˈtɛmpɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpə / (BrE)
to harden and improve the elasticity of metal, especially steel, by reheating it and then cooling it in oil or water
to heat-treat and harden metal

Examples

The blacksmith will temper the steel blade to make it stronger and less brittle.
After casting, the parts must be tempered in oil to achieve the desired hardness.
Engineers often temper metal tools to improve their durability and flexibility.

Synset Examples

temper steel

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to anneal - to heat metal or glass and allow it to cool slowly to remove internal stresses and toughen it

Etymology

From Old French *temprer*, from Latin *temperare* (to moderate, mix, restrain).

/ ˈtɛmpɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpə / (BrE)

Examples
