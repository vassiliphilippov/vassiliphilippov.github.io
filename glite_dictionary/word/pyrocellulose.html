pyrocellulose

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪroʊˈsɛljuloʊs / (AmE), / ˌpaɪərəʊˈsɛljʊləʊs / (BrE)
A form of nitrocellulose with less nitrogen than guncotton, used especially in making smokeless powder.
type of nitrocellulose used in smokeless powder

Examples

Smokeless powders are typically made using pyrocellulose as a primary component.
The manufacturing process for pyrocellulose requires careful handling due to its flammable nature.
Compared to guncotton, pyrocellulose contains less nitrogen and is more stable for ammunition use.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: guncotton - highly nitrated cotton; a form of nitrocellulose used as an explosive
  • Synonym: nitrocellulose - highly flammable compound formed by nitrating cellulose

Etymology

From Greek 'pyr' (fire) and 'cellulose', indicating a cellulose-based compound related to fire or combustion.
