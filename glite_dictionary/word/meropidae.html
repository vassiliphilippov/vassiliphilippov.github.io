Meropidae

1. NOUN

/ məˈrɑːpɪˌdiː / (AmE), / məˈrɒpɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of brightly colored, slender birds known as bee-eaters, found mainly in Africa, Eurasia, and Australasia.
family of bee-eating birds

Examples

The Meropidae are prized by birdwatchers for their vivid plumage and acrobatic flight.
Members of the Meropidae family are commonly found near rivers and open woodlands.
Conservationists are studying the impact of habitat loss on Meropidae populations across Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bee-eater - any member of the Meropidae family, colorful bird feeding mostly on bees and wasps

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'merops' (bee-eater) + '-idae' (zoological family suffix).
