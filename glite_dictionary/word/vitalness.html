vitalness

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪ.tl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈvaɪ.tl.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being absolutely necessary or essential
state of being essential or indispensable

Examples

The vitalness of clean water to human survival cannot be overstated.
He spoke with passion about the vitalness of education in modern society.
Many failed to grasp the vitalness of these safety measures until an accident occurred.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indispensability - the quality of being absolutely necessary
  • Antonym: unimportance - lack of significance or necessity

Etymology

From Latin 'vitalis' (of or belonging to life) + English '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪtlnəs / (AmE), / ˈvaɪtlnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being essential to maintain life or necessary for existence
quality of being essential to life

Examples

The vitalness of clean drinking water cannot be overstated in any community.
Doctors emphasized the vitalness of timely vaccination to prevent the spread of disease.
She spoke passionately about the vitalness of protecting our environment for future generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vitality - the state of being strong and active; energy

Etymology

From 'vital' (from Latin 'vitalis' meaning 'of or belonging to life') + '-ness'.
