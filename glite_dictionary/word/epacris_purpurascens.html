Epacris purpurascens

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈpækrɪs pɜːrpjʊˈræskɛnz / (AmE), / ɪˈpækrɪs pɜːpjʊˈrɑːskɛnz / (BrE)
A small shrub native to southern and western Australia, characterized by pinkish to rosy purple tubular flowers.
Australian shrub with purple tubular flowers

Examples

The garden path was lined with Epacris purpurascens in full bloom, adding a splash of color.
Botanists study Epacris purpurascens for its adaptation to Australian climates.
She planted Epacris purpurascens for its attractive, tubular pink flowers.

Etymology

From genus name Epacris (from Greek epi 'upon' + akris 'summit') and Latin purpurascens, 'becoming purple'.
