Turkistan

1. PROPN

/ tɜrˈkɪstæn / (AmE), / tɜːˈkɪstæn / (BrE)
a historical region of Central Asia that was an important center of trade between the East and the West
historical region of Central Asia

Examples

Merchants traveled along the Silk Road passing through Turkistan on their way to China.
The city's rich architecture reflects the diverse cultures that once flourished in Turkistan.
Throughout history, Turkistan served as a vital crossroads for trade and cultural exchange.

Etymology

From Persian 'Turkistān', meaning 'land of the Turks'.
