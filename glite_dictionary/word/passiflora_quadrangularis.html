Passiflora quadrangularis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæsɪˈflɔrə ˌkwɑːdræŋˈgjuːlərɪs / (AmE), / ˌpæsɪˈflɔːrə ˌkwɒdræŋˈgjʊlərɪs / (BrE)
a tropical American passionflower with large edible fruit known as the granadilla
species of passionflower with large edible fruit

Examples

The Passiflora quadrangularis is cultivated in many tropical regions for its impressive fruit.
In the botanical gardens, several Passiflora quadrangularis vines were in full bloom.
The juice of Passiflora quadrangularis is enjoyed as a refreshing drink in many countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant granadilla - large fruit of Passiflora quadrangularis
  • Synonym: passionflower - plant of the genus Passiflora

Etymology

From Latin: Passiflora (the genus from 'passion flower') + quadrangularis (Latin for 'four-angled', referring to the shape of the stem).
