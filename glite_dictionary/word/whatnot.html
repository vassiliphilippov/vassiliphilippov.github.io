whatnot

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʌt.nɑt / (AmE), / ˈwɒt.nɒt / (BrE)
An item or collection of miscellaneous curios or objects, often displayed on a stand or shelf.
miscellaneous curios or objects

Examples

She decorated her shelves with old books and various whatnots she found at antique shops.
The living room was full of whatnots, from porcelain figures to tiny wooden boxes.
He dusted off the whatnot in the corner, arranging the trinkets it held.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knick-knack - a small decorative object, especially in a house

Etymology

From 'what not', literally meaning 'whatever else', used to refer to various unspecified things.
