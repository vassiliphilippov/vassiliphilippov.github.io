swill

1. NOUN

/ swɪl / (AmE), / swɪl / (BrE)
food waste mixed with water or milk, used as feed for pigs or other livestock
kitchen waste used as animal feed

Examples

The farmer collected the kitchen swill each morning to feed the pigs.
Buckets of swill were poured into the trough for the hungry animals.
Please don't throw away the leftovers; they'll go into the swill for the livestock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slop - kitchen refuse fed to pigs

Etymology

Old English *swillan* (to wash, rinse), related to the sense of liquid food waste.

2. VERB

/ swɪl / (AmE), / swɪl / (BrE)
to drink something, especially alcohol, in large quantities and often quickly or greedily
drink large amounts (esp. alcohol) quickly

Examples

He would often swill beer with his friends after work.
The actors swilled champagne backstage following the successful performance.
She swilled the cold water, grateful for relief from the heat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gulp - to swallow quickly or in large mouthfuls, especially of liquid
  • Antonym: sip - to drink (a liquid) by taking small mouthfuls

Etymology

From Old English *swillan* (to wash, drink greedily).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01171713-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ swɪl / (AmE), / swɪl / (BrE)
to feed pigs with liquid or semi-liquid food waste, often called swill
to feed pigs with food waste

Examples

Every morning, the farmer would swill the pigs in the trough behind the barn.
They used leftover kitchen scraps to swill the hungry sows.
After collecting all the restaurant waste, he went out to swill the livestock.

Etymology

Old English swillan (to wash, rinse out); related to giving liquid feed to animals.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
