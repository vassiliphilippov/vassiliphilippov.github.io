twiner

1. NOUN

/ ˈtwaɪnər / (AmE), / ˈtwaɪnə / (BrE)
a person or thing that entwines or forms something by twisting or interlacing threads, fibers, or strands
person or thing that entwines or twists

Examples

The rope factory employed a skilled twiner to create strong cords from natural fibers.
She worked as a twiner, carefully twisting the threads to make durable twine.
In traditional basketry, the twiner interlaces reeds to form sturdy containers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weaver - a person who weaves fabric

Etymology

From 'twine' (verb: to twist together) + -er (agent suffix).
