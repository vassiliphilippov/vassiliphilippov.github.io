missile

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈmɪsaɪl / (BrE)
an object or weapon that is thrown or projected at a target, but is not self-propelled
projectile weapon (not self-powered)

Examples

The soldier picked up a missile and hurled it at the approaching enemy.
The crowd began to throw stones and other missiles during the protest.
Ancient warriors often used spears as missiles in battle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: projectile - an object that is thrown or fired, especially as a weapon
  • Degree Higher: missile - a self-propelled, guided weapon designed to deliver a payload to a target

Etymology

From Latin missilis (able to be thrown), from mittere (to send).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈmɪsaɪl / (BrE)
a weapon that is propelled through the air, typically by a rocket or jet engine, and is designed to deliver an explosive warhead to a target; it may be guided or unguided.
explosive airborne weapon

Examples

The country tested a new missile capable of reaching distant targets.
A missile exploded in the desert during military exercises.
They displayed recovered parts of the missile at the press conference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: projectile - an object fired or thrown as a weapon
  • Synonym: rocket - a missile propelled by a rocket engine
  • Degree Lower: drone - an unmanned aerial vehicle used as a weapon or for surveillance

Etymology

From Latin missilis (that may be thrown), from mittere (to send).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
