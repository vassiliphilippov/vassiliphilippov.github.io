encirclement

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɝːkəlmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɜːklmənt / (BrE)
the act of surrounding and isolating an area or force, especially as a military tactic to cut off supplies or escape
military tactic of surrounding an area

Examples

The rapid encirclement of the city forced its defenders to surrender within days.
Military historians have studied the encirclement maneuver used during the campaign.
The army executed a successful encirclement, cutting off the enemy's supply routes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: siege - a military operation of surrounding and attacking a fortified place
  • Synonym: blockade - an act of sealing off a place to prevent goods or people from entering or leaving

Etymology

From 'encircle' + '-ment', based on the verb 'encircle'.
