negligence

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛɡlədʒəns / (AmE), / ˈnɛɡlɪdʒəns / (BrE)
failure to take proper care in doing something, especially resulting in harm or damage; in law, the failure to exercise the care that a reasonably prudent person would in the same situation.
failure to take proper care; legal carelessness
legal

Examples

The patient's condition worsened due to the doctor's negligence.
He was found liable for negligence after the accident.
Strict safety measures are enforced to prevent negligence in the workplace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carelessness - lack of attention or consideration resulting in mistakes or accidents
  • Antonym: diligence - careful and persistent work or effort

Etymology

From Latin negligentia, from negligere (to neglect).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɛɡlədʒəns / (AmE), / ˈnɛɡlɪdʒəns / (BrE)
failure to take proper care over something, or a lack of attention to responsibilities; neglect of duty.
lack of care or neglect of duty

Examples

Due to his negligence, several important documents were misplaced.
The company was sued for negligence after failing to maintain safety standards.
His negligence in handling the situation resulted in serious consequences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carelessness - lack of attention and caution
  • Antonym: diligence - careful and persistent effort or work

Etymology

From Latin negligentia (carelessness), from neglegere (to neglect).
