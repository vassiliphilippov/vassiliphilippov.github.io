Malcolmia maritima

1. NOUN

/ mælˈkoʊlmiə məˈrɪtɪmə / (AmE), / mælˈkəʊlmɪə məˈrɪtɪmə / (BrE)
an erect branching herb native to the coasts of southwestern Greece and southern Albania, cultivated for its fragrant clusters of white, pink, red, or lilac flowers; also known as Virginia stock.
a coastal flowering herb; Virginia stock

Examples

Malcolmia maritima is often grown in cottage gardens for its colorful and fragrant spring blooms.
The sandy soils near the coast provide ideal conditions for Malcolmia maritima to flourish naturally.
Gardeners appreciate Malcolmia maritima for its ability to attract bees and butterflies.

Etymology

Genus name from the botanist William Malcolm; species epithet from Latin 'maritima' meaning 'of the sea'.
