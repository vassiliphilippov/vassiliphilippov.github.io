kinsfolk

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪnzˌfoʊk / (AmE), / ˈkɪnzˌfəʊk / (BrE)
people who are related to you by blood; relatives, especially extended family members considered collectively.
people related by blood; relatives

Examples

All my kinsfolk gathered for the family reunion last summer.
She sent invitations to friends and kinsfolk for the wedding.
After the patriarch died, the property was divided among his kinsfolk.

Synset Examples

his family has lived in Massachusetts since the Mayflower

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relatives - people connected to you by blood or marriage
  • Synonym: family - a group of people related to each other

Etymology

From Middle English kin (family) + folk (people).
