magnificence

1. NOUN

/ mæɡˈnɪfəsəns / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɪfɪsəns / (BrE)
the quality of being magnificent, impressive, or splendid in appearance, style, or manner
splendid or impressive quality

Examples

The magnificence of the ancient palace left the tourists in awe.
She admired the magnificence of the mountain landscape at sunrise.
The concert was held in a hall famous for its architectural magnificence.

Synset Examples

for magnificence and personal service there is the Queen's hotel
his ‘Hamlet’ lacks the brilliance that one expects
it is the university that gives the scene its stately splendor
an imaginative mix of old-fashioned grandeur and colorful art
advertisers capitalize on the grandness and elegance it brings to their products

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splendor - great brightness or magnificence; splendid appearance
  • Synonym: grandeur - impressive beauty or scale
  • Antonym: plainness - lack of adornment or show; simple appearance

Etymology

From Latin magnificentia (grandness, greatness of deed), from magnificus (noble, eminent).

2. NOUN

/ mægˈnɪfəsəns / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɪfɪsəns / (BrE)
the quality of being impressively beautiful, elaborate, or grand in size or appearance
impressive grandeur or splendor

Examples

The magnificence of the palace left all the visitors in awe.
We were captivated by the magnificence of the mountain landscape at sunrise.
The magnificence of the concert hall enhanced the orchestra's performance.

Synset Examples

the grandness of the architecture
impressed by the richness of the flora

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splendor - brilliant or impressive appearance; grandeur
  • Synonym: grandeur - the quality of being grand or splendid
  • Antonym: meanness - lack of quality or grandeur; shabbiness

Etymology

From Latin magnificentia (greatness, grandeur), from magnificus (noble, great).
