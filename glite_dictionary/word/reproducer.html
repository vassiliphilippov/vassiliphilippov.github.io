reproducer

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːprəˈduːsər / (AmE), / ˌriːprəˈdjuːsə / (BrE)
an audio device designed to reproduce and amplify sound signals, typically used in sound systems or recording equipment
audio device that reproduces sound

Examples

The studio installed a high-fidelity reproducer for better sound quality.
Old phonographs were often equipped with a mechanical reproducer to play records.
The technician adjusted the reproducer to eliminate distortion during playback.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loudspeaker - a device that converts electrical signals into sound
  • Synonym: amplifier - an electronic device that increases the strength of audio signals

Etymology

From reproduce + -er, denoting something that reproduces, especially in audio technology.
