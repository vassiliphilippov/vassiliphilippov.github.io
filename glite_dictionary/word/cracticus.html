Cracticus

1. NOUN

/ krækˈtɪkəs / (AmE), / krækˈtɪkəs / (BrE)
a genus of Australian birds in the family Cracticidae, commonly known as butcherbirds
genus of Australian butcherbirds

Examples

The genus Cracticus includes several species of butcherbirds native to Australia.
Ornithologists have debated the classification of Cracticus in relation to other members of the Cracticidae family.
The grey butcherbird, a member of Cracticus, is known for its melodious song and territorial behavior.

Etymology

From Modern Latin, created as a scientific genus name; based on Greek 'kraktikos' meaning 'noisy'.
