backstairs

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌstɛrz / (AmE), / ˈbækˌsteəz / (BrE)
a staircase located at the back or rear of a building, often used by servants or for less formal access
staircase at the back of a building

Examples

The servants used the backstairs to move quietly between floors.
Renovations uncovered the old backstairs hidden behind a false wall.
She slipped down the backstairs to avoid being seen by the guests.

Etymology

From 'back' + 'stairs', indicating its position at the rear of a building.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbækˌstɛrz / (AmE), / ˈbækˌstɛəz / (BrE)
secret and dishonorable; done in a sly, furtive, or underhanded manner
secretive or underhanded

Examples

The politician was accused of backstairs dealings with powerful lobbyists.
She resented the backstairs gossip that followed her every move.
His rise to power was marked by backstairs maneuvering and secret alliances.

Synset Examples

backstairs gossip
his low backstairs cunning
backstairs intimacies
furtive behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furtive - attempting to avoid notice or attention, typically because of guilt
  • Synonym: clandestine - kept secret or done secretively
  • Antonym: open - done or shown publicly, not secret or hidden

Etymology

From the literal 'back stairs'—less public stairs in a house, suggesting things done out of sight.
