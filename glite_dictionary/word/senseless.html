senseless

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛnsləs / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsləs / (BrE)
lacking sense, understanding, or good judgment; foolish or unreasonable.
lacking sense or good judgment

Examples

It would be senseless to invest more money in a failing business.
His senseless actions got him into trouble at work.
They argued for hours over a senseless misunderstanding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foolish - lacking good sense or judgment
  • Antonym: reasonable - having sound judgment; fair and sensible

Etymology

From Middle English senseles, from sense + -less.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɛnsləs / (AmE), / ˈsɛnslɪs / (BrE)
(of an action, event, etc.) lacking any good sense or reason; unreasonable or pointless.
lacking reason or purpose

Examples

The attack was a senseless act of violence that shocked the community.
It seems senseless to continue arguing when no one is listening.
Hundreds of animals died in a senseless accident caused by negligence.

Synset Examples

mindless violence
reasonless hostility
a senseless act

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pointless - having no purpose or effect
  • Antonym: reasonable - guided by or showing reason

Etymology

From Middle English senseles (without feeling or sense), from sense + -less.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
