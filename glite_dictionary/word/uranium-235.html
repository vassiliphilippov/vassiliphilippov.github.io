uranium-235

1. NOUN

/ jʊˌreɪniəm tu ˈθɜːrti faɪv / (AmE), / jʊˌreɪniəm tu ˈθɜːti faɪv / (BrE)
an isotope of uranium with mass number 235, capable of sustaining nuclear chain reactions and used as fuel in nuclear reactors and weapons
isotope of uranium used in reactors and weapons

Examples

The fuel for many nuclear power plants is enriched uranium-235.
Scientists use uranium-235 for radiometric dating to determine the age of rocks.
A single atom of uranium-235 can release a large amount of energy when it undergoes fission.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: uranium-238 - another common isotope of uranium, less fissile than uranium-235
  • Degree Higher: plutonium-239 - an artificial element with properties similar to uranium-235, used in nuclear reactors and weapons

Etymology

Uranium from the planet Uranus; 235 indicates its atomic mass.
