to apprise

1. VERB

/ əˈpraɪz / (AmE), / əˈpraɪz / (BrE)
to inform or notify (someone) about something; to give notice to.
to inform or notify

Examples

Please apprise me of any changes to the schedule as soon as possible.
The manager apprised the team of the new company policies during the meeting.
She is responsible for apprising stakeholders about project developments.

Synset Examples

I advised him that the rent was due

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inform - to give information to someone
  • Synonym: to notify - to officially tell someone about something

Etymology

From French 'appriser' (to inform), from Latin 'appretiare' (to value, estimate).

2. VERB

/ əˈpraɪz / (AmE), / əˈprʌɪz / (BrE)
to inform or notify someone about something
inform; make aware of

Examples

The manager apprised the staff of the new safety protocols this morning.
She was regularly apprised of the project's progress.

Synset Examples

Have the students been apprised of the tuition hike?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inform - to give information; notify
  • Synonym: to notify - to formally inform someone of something

Etymology

From French 'appriser', from Late Latin 'appretiare' (to value, appraise).
