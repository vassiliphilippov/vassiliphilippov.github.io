peeve

1. NOUN

/ piv / (AmE), / piːv / (BrE)
A state of being annoyed or irritated; an annoyed mood.
annoyed or irritated mood

Examples

After getting stuck in traffic for an hour, she was left in a real peeve.
His constant complaints put everyone around him in a peeve.
You could tell by his tone that he was in a bit of a peeve today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritation - a feeling of being annoyed or angry
  • Antonym: calm - a peaceful or relaxed state of mind

Etymology

Perhaps from the verb 'peeve' (to annoy), first recorded in the early 20th century.

2. VERB

/ piv / (AmE), / piːv / (BrE)
to annoy, irritate, or make someone resentful
to annoy or irritate

Examples

It really peeves me when people are late to meetings.
Her attitude peeved everyone in the group.
He was peeving his sister by playing loud music all night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to annoy - to irritate or make slightly angry
  • Synonym: to irritate - to make someone angry or impatient
  • Antonym: please - to make someone feel happy or satisfied

Etymology

Probably derived from 'peevish', itself of uncertain origin, first attested as a noun in early 20th-century American English.
