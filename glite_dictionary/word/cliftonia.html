Cliftonia

1. NOUN

/ klɪfˈtoʊniə / (AmE), / klɪfˈtəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen shrubs or small trees in the bayberry family (Myricaceae), chiefly represented by the species titi, native to the southeastern United States.
genus of evergreen shrubs or trees in SE US

Examples

In the wetlands of Georgia, Cliftonia is often found growing alongside cypress and tupelo trees.
The white, fragrant flowers of Cliftonia attract bees and other pollinators each spring.
Many gardeners appreciate Cliftonia for its attractive glossy leaves and its tolerance of poorly-drained soils.
