adrenocorticotrophin

1. NOUN

/ əˌdriːnoʊˌkɔrtɪkoʊˈtroʊfɪn / (AmE), / əˌdriːnəʊˌkɔːtɪkəʊˈtrəʊfɪn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates the adrenal cortex to release its hormones
pituitary hormone stimulating adrenal cortex
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that adrenocorticotrophin helps control how the body responds to stress.
A deficiency of adrenocorticotrophin can result in reduced adrenal cortex function.
Researchers studied how adrenocorticotrophin levels change in patients with pituitary disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adrenocorticotropic hormone - another term for adrenocorticotrophin

Etymology

From adreno- (adrenal) + cortico- (cortex) + -trophin (Greek 'trophe' meaning nourishment).
