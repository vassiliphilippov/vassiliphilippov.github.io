shaping

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of giving a particular shape to something
act of giving shape to something

Examples

The artist devoted hours to the careful shaping of the sculpture.
Modern manufacturing relies on precise shaping techniques to produce components.
Proper shaping of the dough is essential for making good bread.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: molding - the process of shaping something by using a mold

Etymology

From the verb 'shape' + -ing, denoting the action or process.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00927021-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃeɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
Having the ability to form, mold, or fashion into a particular shape or structure.
forming or molding

Examples

The sculptor used several shaping tools to create fine details on the statue.
Parental guidance can be a powerful shaping influence on children's behavior.
Exposure to different cultures has had a shaping effect on her worldview.

Synset Examples

a formative influence
a formative experience
the plastic forces of nature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: formative - serving to form or give shape
  • Synonym: molding - having an ability to shape or mold

Etymology

From the verb 'shape', which originates from Old English 'sceapan' or 'scieppan' (to form, create).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00588059-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of giving a particular shape or form to something
process of forming or shaping

Examples

The shaping of the clay took skill and patience.
Technological advances have transformed the shaping of metal and other materials.
Urban planners are involved in the shaping of city landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: formation - the action of forming or process of being formed

Etymology

From the verb 'shape' + -ing.
