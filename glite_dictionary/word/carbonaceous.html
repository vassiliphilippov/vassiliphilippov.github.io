carbonaceous

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːrbəˈneɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˌkɑːbəˈneɪʃəs / (BrE)
consisting of, containing, or yielding carbon; relating to or characteristic of carbon or its compounds.
containing or yielding carbon

Examples

The coal seam is primarily carbonaceous and contains plant fossils.
Scientists study carbonaceous meteorites to learn about the early solar system.
The carbonaceous residue left after combustion can be analyzed for its composition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbonic - relating to carbon, as in carbonic acid
  • Antonym: carbon-free - not containing carbon
  • Degree Higher: carbon-rich - containing a high amount of carbon

Etymology

From Latin 'carbonaceus' (pertaining to charcoal), from 'carbo' (charcoal).
