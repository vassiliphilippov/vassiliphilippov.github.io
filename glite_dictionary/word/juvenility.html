juvenility

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒuːvəˈnɪləti / (AmE), / ˌdʒuːvəˈnɪləti / (BrE)
The state or quality of being young or immature, often marked by a lack of experience or maturity.
youthfulness; immaturity

Examples

Her juvenility was apparent in the way she handled the difficult situation.
The manager criticized the team's juvenility and urged them to act more professionally.
He gradually overcame his juvenility as he gained more experience in the field.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: maturity - the state of being fully developed or experienced

Etymology

From Latin iuvenilis (youthful), from iuvenis (young).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒuːvəˈnɪləti / (AmE), / ˌdʒuːvəˈnɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being youthful, especially freshness and vitality typical of a young person
freshness and vitality of youth

Examples

The juvenility of the team energized the whole company during the project.
There was a certain juvenility in his ideas, full of hope and originality.
Despite his age, he maintained a juvenility that surprised everyone around him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: youthfulness - the quality of being young

Etymology

From Latin juvenilis (youthful) + -ity.
