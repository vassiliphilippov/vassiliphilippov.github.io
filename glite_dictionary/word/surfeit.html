surfeit

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.fɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.fɪt / (BrE)
an excessive amount of something; the state of being more than full or having more than is needed or wanted.
an excessive amount; overabundance

Examples

After the feast, I felt a surfeit of food weighing me down.
The internet provides a surfeit of information on every possible topic.
A surfeit of riches can sometimes be as problematic as poverty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excess - an amount of something that is more than necessary, permitted, or desirable
  • Antonym: scarcity - a lack or shortage of something

Etymology

From Old French 'surfet', past participle of 'surfaire' (to overdo), from Latin 'superfacere' ('super' over + 'facere' to do).

2. VERB

/ ˈsɝː.fɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.fɪt / (BrE)
to supply or feed (oneself or someone) to excess, especially to the point of being sickened or no longer desiring more
to feed or indulge to excess

Examples

Eating too many sweets can easily surfeit even the most ardent chocolate lover.
He surfeited himself on rich foods during the holidays and felt unwell afterwards.
If you surfeit on distractions, productivity inevitably suffers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satiate - to satisfy fully, especially with food or pleasure
  • Antonym: to starve - to suffer or die from lack of food

Etymology

From Old French *surfaire* (to overdo), from Latin *superfacere* ('to do in excess').

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.fɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.fɪt / (BrE)
an excessive amount of food or drink, especially to the point of feeling overly full
excessive amount of food or drink

Examples

After the holiday feast, he was suffering from a surfeit of turkey and desserts.
There was a surfeit of food at the banquet, much more than anyone could eat.
Children often experience a surfeit of sweets during Halloween.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glut - an excessively abundant supply; overabundance
  • Synonym: excess - more than is necessary, permitted, or desirable
  • Antonym: scarcity - the state of being scarce or in short supply; shortage

Etymology

From Old French *sorfait* (excess), from *surfaire* (to overdo), from Latin *super-* (above) + *facere* (to do).

4. VERB

/ ˈsɝː.fɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.fɪt / (BrE)
to supply, feed, or fill to excess; to cause someone to have too much of something and feel sick or repelled by it
to supply or fill to excess

Examples

After being surfeited with rich foods all week, she craved something plain.
The guests were surfeited with countless desserts at the banquet.
He warned that the children might be surfeited with too much television.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satiate - to satisfy fully, especially with food; to fill to excess
  • Antonym: to starve - to suffer or die from extreme hunger

Etymology

From Old French *surfaire* (to overdo), from sur- (over) + faire (to do), from Latin *facere* (to do, make).

5. NOUN

/ ˈsɜr.fɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.fɪt / (BrE)
an excessive amount or overabundance of something, especially to the extent that it causes prices or values to fall
an excessive or overabundant amount

Examples

A surfeit of wheat on the market led to a dramatic drop in prices.
The company was hurt by a surfeit of competing products flooding the shelves.
After the technology boom, some investors feared a surfeit of stocks would devalue the whole sector.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excess - an amount of something that is more than necessary, permitted, or desirable
  • Antonym: scarcity - a situation in which something is not enough in supply

Etymology

From Old French *surfaire* (to overdo), from Latin *super-* (over) + *facere* (to do).
