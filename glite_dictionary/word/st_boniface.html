St. Boniface

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈbɑːnɪfɪs / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈbɒnɪfɪs / (BrE)
(c. 675–754) An Anglo-Saxon Christian missionary and saint, known for spreading Christianity in parts of present-day Germany and martyred in Frisia.
Anglo-Saxon Christian missionary and saint

Examples

Many churches across Europe are named after St. Boniface to honor his missionary work.
The feast day of St. Boniface is celebrated on June 5 by many Christian communities.
History lessons often mention St. Boniface as the Apostle of Germany.

Etymology

From Latin Bonifatius, meaning 'doer of good'; 'Saint' denotes canonization in the Christian tradition.
