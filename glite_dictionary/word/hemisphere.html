hemisphere

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛm.ɪˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈhɛm.ɪ.sfɪə / (BrE)
either of the two halves of the cerebrum, the largest part of the brain, separated by a groove.
half of the cerebrum in the brain
medical

Examples

Damage to the left hemisphere of the brain can affect speech and language abilities.
The right hemisphere controls the muscles on the left side of the body.
Doctors studied how the two hemispheres communicate with each other.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cerebrum - the principal and most anterior part of the brain

Etymology

From Greek hēmisphairion, from hēmi- (half) + sphaira (sphere).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛmɪˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈhɛmɪsfɪə / (BrE)
A half of a sphere, especially used to refer to either of the two halves of the earth divided by the equator or to any similar division.
half of a sphere; often refers to halves of the earth

Examples

The northern hemisphere experiences winter while the southern hemisphere has summer.
The brain is divided into the left and right hemisphere by a deep groove.
We studied maps showing each hemisphere of the globe separately.

Etymology

From Latin 'hemisphaerium', from Greek 'hēmisphairion' ('hemi-' meaning 'half' + 'sphaira' meaning 'sphere').

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɛmɪˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈhɛmɪsfɪə / (BrE)
Either of the two halves of the Earth, usually divided into northern and southern, or eastern and western, by the equator or a meridian.
half of the Earth, e.g. northern or southern

Examples

The equator divides the Earth into the Northern and Southern hemispheres.
Australia is located in the Southern hemisphere.
Many migratory birds cross from one hemisphere to the other each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: globe - the earth as a whole; a spherical planet
  • Synonym: equator - the imaginary line dividing the northern and southern hemispheres

Etymology

From Greek 'hēmisphairion', from 'hēmi-' (half) + 'sphaira' (sphere).
