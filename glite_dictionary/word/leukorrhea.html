leukorrhea

1. NOUN

/ luːkəˈriːə / (AmE), / ljuːkəˈriːə / (BrE)
a white or yellowish vaginal discharge, often a symptom of infection or irritation
vaginal mucous discharge, symptom
medical

Examples

She visited a doctor after noticing leukorrhea and mild discomfort.
Persistent leukorrhea may be a sign of a vaginal infection that requires treatment.
The patient was concerned about her unusual leukorrhea and sought medical advice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discharge - abnormal matter flowing from the body; related medical symptom

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'leukos' (white) + 'rhein' (to flow).
