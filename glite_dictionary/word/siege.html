siege

1. NOUN

/ siːdʒ / (AmE), / siːdʒ / (BrE)
a military operation in which armed forces surround a place and cut off essential supplies, with the aim of compelling the surrender of those inside
military operation surrounding a place

Examples

The castle held out for months under a brutal siege.
During the siege, food supplies quickly ran out for the trapped inhabitants.
The army planned a siege to force the city to surrender.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blockade - the act of sealing off a place to prevent goods or people from entering or leaving
  • Degree Higher: assault - a sudden, violent attack, often as part of a siege
  • Antonym: retreat - the act of withdrawing military forces, opposite of a siege

Etymology

From Old French *siege* (seat, blockade), from Medieval Latin *sedium* (a sitting, blockade) from Latin *sedere* (to sit).
