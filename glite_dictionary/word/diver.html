diver

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪvər / (AmE), / ˈdaɪvə / (BrE)
a person who swims or works underwater, often using special equipment such as a scuba tank or diving suit
person who works or swims underwater

Examples

The diver explored the coral reef in search of rare sea creatures.
Professional divers must be trained to handle emergencies underwater.
A skilled diver can assess and repair damage to underwater pipelines.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: scuba diver - a diver who uses a self-contained underwater breathing apparatus

Etymology

From the verb 'dive', ultimately from Old English 'dyfan' (to dip, submerge).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪvər / (AmE), / ˈdaɪvə / (BrE)
a large, somewhat primitive, fish-eating diving bird of the Northern Hemisphere, with webbed feet placed far back on the body; related to the grebes, also known as a loon
large Northern Hemisphere diving bird; loon

Examples

The common diver can be seen on remote northern lakes during the breeding season.
In Europe, the great northern diver is a rare but impressive visitor.
Naturalists watched a diver disappear beneath the surface in search of fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loon - another name for the diver, a large fish-eating diving bird

3. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪvər / (AmE), / ˈdaɪvə / (BrE)
a person who dives into water, especially as a sport or occupation
person who dives into water

Examples

The diver plunged gracefully into the pool from the highest platform.
A team of divers explored the wreckage at the bottom of the sea.
The diver wore a special wetsuit to protect against the cold water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swimmer - a person who swims
  • Degree Lower: snorkeler - a person who swims using a snorkel
  • Degree Higher: scuba diver - a person who dives underwater with scuba equipment

Etymology

From the verb 'dive' + '-er', denoting a person who performs the action.
