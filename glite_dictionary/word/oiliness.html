oiliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔɪliˌnəs / (AmE), / ˈɔɪliːnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being oily; the presence of oil or a greasy texture or appearance
the quality of being oily

Examples

The oiliness of the salad dressing made the lettuce taste rich and smooth.
She noticed the oiliness of her skin after exercising.
Too much shampoo can strip your hair of its natural oiliness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greasiness - the quality of being greasy

Etymology

From 'oily' + '-ness' (state or quality).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔɪli.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɔɪlɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being excessively smooth, smug, or self-serving in manner or speech
smug or self-serving manner

Examples

His oiliness during the meeting made everyone uncomfortable and suspicious of his motives.
There was a certain oiliness in her compliments that made them seem insincere.
The politician's oiliness was evident in his overly flattering remarks to the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unctuousness - excessive or insincere flattery or earnestness
  • Antonym: sincerity - the quality of being free from pretense, deceit, or hypocrisy

Etymology

From 'oily' + '-ness', originally referring to the property of containing oil and later extended metaphorically to describe personality traits.
