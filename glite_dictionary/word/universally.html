universally

1. ADV

/ ˌjuːnɪˈvɝːsəli / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪˈvɜːsəli / (BrE)
In every case or situation; by everyone without exception.
in every case; by everyone

Examples

The new law was universally welcomed by human rights organizations.
That theory is universally accepted within the scientific community.
Good manners are universally appreciated, regardless of culture.

Synset Examples

people universally agree on this

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: generally - in most cases; commonly

Etymology

From universal + -ly, based on Latin universalis (of or belonging to all).
