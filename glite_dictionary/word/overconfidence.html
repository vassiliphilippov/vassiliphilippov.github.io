overconfidence

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərˈkɑnfɪdəns / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈkɒnfɪdəns / (BrE)
The state of being too confident, especially when this is not justified by the situation.
excessive or unjustified confidence

Examples

His overconfidence led him to underestimate the difficulty of the task.
The team’s overconfidence was their downfall in the championship game.
Despite warnings, her overconfidence made her ignore the risks involved.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: confidence - the feeling or belief that one can rely on someone or something
  • Degree Higher: arrogance - the quality of being excessively proud or self-important

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (meaning excessively) + 'confidence'.
