edema

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈdimə / (AmE), / ɪˈdiːmə / (BrE)
A condition in which an excessive amount of watery fluid collects in the tissues or cavities of the body, causing swelling.
swelling caused by excess fluid in the body
medical

Examples

The patient developed edema in his legs after the surgery.
Doctors are monitoring her for signs of edema as part of her treatment.
Severe allergic reactions can sometimes cause facial edema.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swelling - an area of the body that becomes larger than normal
  • Degree Higher: inflammation - a localized physical condition marked by redness, swelling, heat, and pain

Etymology

From Greek 'oidēma' (swelling), from 'oidein' (to swell).
