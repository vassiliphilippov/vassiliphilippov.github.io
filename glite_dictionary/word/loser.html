loser

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːzər / (AmE), / ˈluːzə / (BrE)
A person, especially a gambler, who loses a bet or contest.
person who loses a bet or contest

Examples

He was a loser at the poker table last night, losing his entire stake.
The casino was full of winners and losers after the big game.
Even a seasoned loser sometimes gets lucky and wins a round.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: winner - a person who wins a competition or bet

Etymology

From lose + -er, forming an agent noun.

2. NOUN

/ ˈluːzər / (AmE), / ˈluːzə / (BrE)
A person or team that does not win a competition or contest.
person or team that loses a contest

Examples

After the game, the loser shook hands with the winner and congratulated her.
Every contest must have a loser, even if the competition is close.
The losers of the tournament still received medals for their efforts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: winner - person or team that comes first in a contest

Etymology

From lose + -er, formed in Middle English.

3. NOUN

/ ˈluːzər / (AmE), / ˈluːzə / (BrE)
a person who habitually fails or is repeatedly unsuccessful, especially in competitions or in life
person who consistently fails

Examples

Despite practicing every day, he remained a loser in the tennis league.
She refuses to think of herself as a loser, despite several setbacks.
In high school, some students cruelly labeled him a loser because he wasn't popular.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: winner - a person who wins or is successful

Etymology

From lose + -er, denoting one who loses.

4. NOUN

/ ˈluːzər / (AmE), / ˈluːzə / (BrE)
a person who fails to win, succeed, or achieve in a particular activity or in life in general, often used in a derogatory or informal way
a person who fails or is unsuccessful
informal

Examples

Don't be such a loser; just go for it!
He felt like a loser after failing the exam for the third time.
Some people unfairly label others as losers because of their mistakes.
