Warren G. Harding

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɔrən dʒi ˈhɑrdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɒrən dʒiː ˈhɑːdɪŋ / (BrE)
(1865–1923) the 29th President of the United States, whose administration was involved in several scandals, most notably the Teapot Dome affair.
29th U.S. President (1865–1923)

Examples

Warren G. Harding was elected president in 1920 on a promise of a 'return to normalcy.'
The Teapot Dome scandal became synonymous with official corruption during Warren G. Harding's administration.
Many historians debate the legacy of Warren G. Harding due to the controversies surrounding his presidency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: president - the elected head of a republican state
