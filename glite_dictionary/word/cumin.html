cumin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkuːmɪn, ˈkʌmɪn / (AmE), / ˈkjuːmɪn, ˈkʌmɪn / (BrE)
The aromatic seeds of a plant of the parsley family, used as a spice in cooking, especially in Asian, Middle Eastern, and Latin American cuisine.
aromatic spice seed

Examples

Sprinkle a pinch of cumin over the roasted vegetables for extra flavor.
The recipe called for ground cumin and coriander to create a rich, aromatic blend.
She always keeps a jar of cumin seeds in her spice rack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caraway - aromatic seeds used as a spice, similar to cumin

Etymology

From Old French cumin, from Latin cuminum, from Greek kuminon.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːmɪn / (AmE), / ˈkʌmɪn / (BrE)
A small annual plant of the parsley family, native to the Mediterranean region, cultivated for its aromatic seeds used as a spice.
aromatic Mediterranean spice plant

Examples

The recipe calls for a teaspoon of ground cumin to enhance the flavor.
She sprinkled roasted cumin seeds on top of the curry.
Cumin is often used in Middle Eastern and Indian cuisines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caraway - A similar aromatic plant with seeds used as spice

Etymology

From Old French coumin, from Latin cuminum, from Greek kuminon, of Semitic origin.
