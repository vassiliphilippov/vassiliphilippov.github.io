Latinism

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæt.ən.ɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈlat.ɪ.nɪz.əm / (BrE)
A word, phrase, or expression that is borrowed from Latin or constructed in the style of Latin.
a word or phrase borrowed from Latin

Examples

The English word 'video' is a well-known Latinism.
His writing is full of Latinisms that can make it difficult to read.
Many scientific terms in English are actually Latinisms.

Etymology

From Latin 'Latinus' (Latin) + '-ism'.
