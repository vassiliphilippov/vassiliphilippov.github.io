blustery

1. ADJ

/ ˈblʌstəri / (AmE), / ˈblʌstəri / (BrE)
(of weather) characterized by strong, gusty, and often noisy winds.
windy and gusty (weather)

Examples

The blustery wind knocked over several trash cans on our street last night.
We had to postpone our picnic because of the blustery weather.
On a blustery afternoon, it's best to stay indoors with a good book.

Synset Examples

blustering (or blusterous) winds of Patagonia
a cold blustery day

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - free from wind or storm
  • Synonym: gusty - characterized by or blowing in sudden strong bursts (wind)

Etymology

From 'bluster' (to blow in noisy gusts, especially of wind) + '-y' (adjective-forming suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈblʌstəri / (AmE), / ˈblʌstəri / (BrE)
Aggressively loud and domineering in manner; apt to bully or browbeat others.
aggressively loud or domineering

Examples

The new manager's blustery style intimidated many of the staff at first.
Her blustery speech drowned out any attempt at disagreement during the meeting.
He tried to mask his uncertainty with a blustery display of confidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bullying - using strength or influence to intimidate
  • Antonym: gentle - kind and mild in manner

Etymology

From verb 'bluster' (to blow violently or to talk loudly and aggressively) + -y.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00791587-s
Synset POS: s
