Mirounga

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈruːŋɡə / (AmE), / mɪˈruːŋɡə / (BrE)
A genus of large marine mammals known as elephant seals, consisting of two species found in the northern and southern hemispheres.
genus of elephant seals

Examples

Mirounga includes both the northern and southern elephant seals.
The largest member of the Mirounga genus can weigh several tons.
Researchers are studying Mirounga populations to understand the effects of climate change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elephant seal - a very large marine mammal with an inflatable proboscis, native to Pacific coastal regions

Etymology

From Modern Latin 'Mirounga', based on an Australian Aboriginal word for the elephant seal.

2. NOUN

/ mɪˈruːŋɡə / (AmE), / mɪˈruːŋɡə / (BrE)
A genus of large marine mammals known as elephant seals, characterized by their distinctive trunk-like snouts and found in subantarctic and Antarctic regions.
genus of elephant seals

Examples

The Mirounga genus includes the largest of all seal species.
Scientists studied the migration patterns of Mirounga in the Southern Ocean.
A colony of Mirounga can be found on isolated subantarctic islands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elephant seal - either of two large seals of the genus Mirounga

Etymology

From a word in an Australian Aboriginal language, adopted as the genus name in scientific Latin.
