Martin

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɑrtɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtɪn / (BrE)
(also Saint Martin) A 4th-century French bishop who is a patron saint of France, known for his acts of charity; died in 397.
French patron saint and bishop (died 397)

Examples

Every year, many pilgrims visit the shrine of Martin in Tours.
Martin is celebrated in France for sharing his cloak with a beggar.
The legend of Martin inspired many works of art throughout Europe.

Etymology

From Latin Martinus, derived from Mars, the Roman god of war.
