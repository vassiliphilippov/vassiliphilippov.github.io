comely

1. ADJ

/ ˈkʌmli / (AmE), / ˈkʌmli / (BrE)
pleasant to look at; attractive, especially in a wholesome or simple way.
attractive in appearance

Examples

The princess was known for her comely appearance and gentle nature.
He found her smile both warm and comely when she welcomed him inside.
She wore a comely dress that accentuated her graceful figure.

Synset Examples

my bonny lass
there's a bonny bay beyond
a comely face
young fair maidens

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: beautiful - very attractive or pleasing to the senses
  • Antonym: plain - not beautiful or attractive in appearance

Etymology

From Old English *cymlīc* (lovely), from *cymen* (to please).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkʌmli / (AmE), / ˈkʌmli / (BrE)
proper or appropriate; conforming to accepted standards of behavior or appearance
proper; in accordance with custom

Examples

It is considered comely to thank your hosts at the end of a meal.
Her comely conduct earned her the respect of her peers.
Their comely behavior reflected well on their upbringing.

Synset Examples

her becoming modesty
comely behavior
it is not comme il faut for a gentleman to be constantly asking for money
seemly behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seemly - proper or appropriate in manner or appearance
  • Antonym: unseemly - not proper or appropriate

Etymology

From Old English 'cymlic' (beautiful, suitable), from 'cymen' (to fit, suit).
