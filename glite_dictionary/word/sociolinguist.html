sociolinguist

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊsi.oʊˈlɪŋɡwɪst / (AmE), / ˌsəʊsi.əʊˈlɪŋɡwɪst / (BrE)
a person who studies the relationship between language and society, focusing on how social and cultural factors affect the way language is used
an expert in language and society

Examples

As a sociolinguist, Dr. Carter researches how dialects change across urban communities.
The sociolinguist presented her findings on language use in multicultural classrooms.
Many sociolinguists study how gender influences patterns of speech.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sociolinguistics - the study of how language is influenced by social and cultural factors

Etymology

From 'socio-' (relating to society) and 'linguist'.
