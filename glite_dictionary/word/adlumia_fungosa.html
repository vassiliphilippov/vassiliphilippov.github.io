Adlumia fungosa

1. NOUN

/ ˌæd.luˈmaɪ.ə fʌŋˈɡoʊ.sə / (AmE), / ˌad.luːˈmʌɪ.ə fʌŋˈɡəʊ.sə / (BrE)
A climbing vine native to North America, with finely divided leaves and clusters of white or pinkish tubular flowers; also known as the climbing fumitory.
a North American climbing vine

Examples

The rare Adlumia fungosa was spotted along the shaded woodland stream during our hike.
Adlumia fungosa is often admired by botanists for its delicate, feathery foliage and graceful blooms.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Adlumia fungosa for its unusual appearance and attractive flowers.

Etymology

Genus named after John Adlum, American horticulturist; species from Latin fungus, perhaps for texture.
