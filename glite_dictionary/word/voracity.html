voracity

1. NOUN

/ vɔˈræsəti / (AmE), / vəˈræsəti / (BrE)
Extreme eagerness or hunger, especially for food; greediness.
extreme eagerness or greed (esp. for food)

Examples

His voracity at the dinner table surprised everyone.
The voracity of the wildfire left acres of forest destroyed.
She pursued knowledge with a voracity that impressed her professors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gluttony - excessive eating or drinking

Etymology

From Latin 'voracitas' (greediness), from 'vorax' (greedy).

2. NOUN

/ vəˈræsəti / (AmE), / vəˈræsɪti / (BrE)
great hunger or insatiable eagerness, especially for food; excessive desire to eat
excessive hunger or eagerness

Examples

His voracity at the dinner table surprised everyone present.
The lion's voracity made it the dominant hunter of the region.
She approached the new project with a voracity that impressed her colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gluttony - habitual greed or excess in eating

Etymology

From Latin voracitas (greed, voraciousness), from vorax (greedy, ravenous).
