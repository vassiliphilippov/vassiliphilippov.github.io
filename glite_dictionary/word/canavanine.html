canavanine

1. NOUN

/ ˌkænəˈvænɪniːn / (AmE), / ˌkanəˈvanɪniːn / (BrE)
an amino acid found especially in the seeds of the jack bean and some other legumes, structurally similar to arginine.
an amino acid from jack bean seeds
medical

Examples

Canavanine is often used in research to study the effects of non-protein amino acids in plants.
The seeds of the jack bean contain high concentrations of canavanine.
Some insects have evolved resistance to canavanine toxicity in legume seeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amino acid - a compound containing both amino and carboxyl groups
  • Degree Higher: arginine - an amino acid structurally similar to canavanine

Etymology

From jack bean's botanical name Canavalia; suffix -ine used in chemical nomenclature.
