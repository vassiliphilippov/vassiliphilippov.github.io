straining

1. NOUN

/ ˈstreɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstreɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of interpreting or presenting something in a way that distorts its original meaning or intent
distorting something's intended meaning

Examples

His constant straining of the facts made his argument less credible.
The lawyer's straining of the contract's language was obvious to the judge.
Critics accused him of straining the author's words to suit his theory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misrepresentation - the action of giving a false or misleading account of something
  • Degree Lower: interpretation - the act of explaining or understanding the meaning of something (without distortion)

2. ADJ

/ ˈstreɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstreɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
taxing one to the limit; requiring great effort or endurance
requiring great effort or endurance

Examples

After a straining climb to the summit, they finally enjoyed the view.
Completing the marathon was a straining experience both physically and mentally.
She took on the straining responsibility of caring for the entire team.

Synset Examples

his final, straining burst of speed
a strenuous task
your willingness after these six arduous days to remain here

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strenuous - requiring or using great exertion
  • Synonym: arduous - involving or requiring strenuous effort; very difficult
  • Antonym: easy - not difficult or demanding

3. NOUN

/ ˈstreɪ.nɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstreɪ.nɪŋ / (BrE)
an intense or violent exertion; the act or process of making a strong effort or applying force
intense exertion or effort

Examples

The straining of lifting the heavy box left her breathless.
After hours of straining during the marathon, he finally crossed the finish line.
The bridge began to make noises from the straining of supporting so much weight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effort - a vigorous or determined attempt
  • Antonym: relaxation - the act of releasing tension or effort

Etymology

Present participle of 'strain,' from Old French estreindre, from Latin stringere (to draw tight, bind).
