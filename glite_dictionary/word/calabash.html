calabash

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæləˌbæʃ / (AmE), / ˈkæləbæʃ / (BrE)
a bottle or container made from the dried, hollowed shell of a bottle gourd, often used for storing liquids or as a musical instrument.
bottle made from a dried gourd shell

Examples

She drank cool water from a calabash while resting under the tree.
A beautifully painted calabash was displayed at the cultural exhibition.
The musician used a calabash as a percussion instrument during the performance.

Etymology

From Spanish *calabaza* (gourd), ultimately from Arabic *qarʿa(y) al-yad* (bottle gourd).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.əˌbæʃ / (AmE), / ˈkæl.ə.bæʃ / (BrE)
a round gourd from the calabash tree, commonly used as a container or utensil after being dried
round gourd of the calabash tree

Examples

After being dried, the calabash can be turned into a water container.
He carved a beautiful bowl from a large calabash.
Traditional musicians sometimes use a calabash as a percussion instrument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gourd - a hard-shelled fruit often used as a container or utensil

Etymology

From Spanish 'calabaza' (gourd), likely from Arabic 'qar'a yabisa' (dry gourd).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.əˌbæʃ / (AmE), / ˈkæl.ə.bæʃ / (BrE)
A tropical climbing plant of the gourd family, grown for its hard-shelled, bottle-shaped fruits often used as containers or utensils.
gourd plant with hard, bottle-shaped fruits

Examples

The craftsman carved a bowl out of a calabash to use at the market.
Many traditional musical instruments are made from dried calabashes.
The calabash vine winds its way over the garden fence every summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gourd - any of various hard-shelled fruits of the gourd family

Etymology

From Spanish *calabaza* (gourd), likely of Arabic origin.

Collectors prize antique calabashes for their beautiful craftsmanship and history.

From Spanish 'calabaza' (gourd), likely from Arabic 'qarʿa yabisa' (dry gourd), via Portuguese.
