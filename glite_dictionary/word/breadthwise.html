breadthwise

1. ADV

/ ˈbrɛdθˌwaɪz / (AmE), / ˈbrɛdθˌwaɪz / (BrE)
in the direction of the breadth, or across the width of something rather than its length
across the width of something

Examples

The fabric was cut breadthwise to ensure the pattern matched.
Lay the plank breadthwise across the beams for added stability.
Fold the paper breadthwise before slicing it into strips.

Synset Examples

cut the cloth breadthwise

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lengthwise - in the direction of the length; along the longest side

Etymology

From 'breadth' (width, from Old English brǣdu) + '-wise' (in the manner or direction of).
