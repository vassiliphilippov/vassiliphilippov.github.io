Combretum erythrophyllum

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈbriːtəm ˌɛrɪˌθroʊˈfɪləm / (AmE), / kɒmˈbriːtəm ˌɛrɪˌθrəʊˈfɪləm / (BrE)
A small South African tree, typically found growing along stream banks, with creamy yellow, fragrant flowers.
South African tree with yellow flowers

Examples

The Combretum erythrophyllum brightens the riverbanks with its abundant yellow blossoms each spring.
Many birds are attracted to the fragrant flowers of Combretum erythrophyllum during the blooming season.
Conservationists recommend planting Combretum erythrophyllum to help prevent erosion along stream banks.

Etymology

From genus name Combretum (from Latin) and species epithet erythrophyllum (from Greek 'erythros' meaning red and 'phyllon' meaning leaf).
