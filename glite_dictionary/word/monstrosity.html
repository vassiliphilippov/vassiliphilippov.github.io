monstrosity

1. NOUN

/ mɑnˈstrɑsəti / (AmE), / mɒnˈstrɒsɪti / (BrE)
something that is very ugly, frightening, or unnatural, especially in its appearance or form
something very ugly or unnatural

Examples

The abandoned factory was considered a monstrosity by the townspeople.
Many people thought the new building was an architectural monstrosity.
Some viewed the sculpture as art, while others saw it as a hideous monstrosity.

Synset Examples

they regarded the atom bomb as a monstrosity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monster - a large, frightening, imaginary creature
  • Antonym: beauty - something beautiful or pleasing to the senses

Etymology

From French 'monstruosité', from Latin 'monstruositas' (unnaturalness, monstrosity).

2. NOUN

/ mɑnˈstrɑsəti / (AmE), / mɒnˈstrɒsəti / (BrE)
A person or animal that is extremely abnormal, deformed, or unusually shaped, often in a way that causes shock or horror.
an extremely abnormal or deformed person or animal

Examples

The villagers shunned the child, believing him to be a monstrosity because of his unusual appearance.
In the circus, they once displayed a monstrosity with two heads, drawing horrified crowds.
Her compassion extended even to those whom others called monstrosities, refusing to judge by appearances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monster - a large, frightening, imaginary or real creature

Etymology

From Latin 'monstrōsitās', from 'monstrum' (monster).
