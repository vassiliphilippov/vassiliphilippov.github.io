squeegee

1. VERB

/ ˈskwiːdʒi / (AmE), / ˈskwiːdʒi / (BrE)
to clean or wipe a surface, especially glass, using a squeegee.
to clean with a squeegee

Examples

He carefully squeegeed the shower doors after every use to prevent water spots.
After washing the car, she squeegeed the windows for a streak-free finish.
The workers squeegeed the rainwater off the stadium seats before the game started.

Synset Examples

squeegee the windows

2. NOUN

/ ˈskwiːdʒi / (AmE), / ˈskwiːdʒi / (BrE)
A T-shaped tool with a rubber blade, used for removing liquid, especially water, from a surface such as windows.
tool for removing water from surfaces

Examples

She used a squeegee to remove the soapy water from the window.
The car wash attendant wiped the windshield with a squeegee.
After his shower, he ran a squeegee over the glass door to prevent streaks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wiper - a tool for cleaning or wiping, such as a windshield wiper

Etymology

Probably from earlier dialectal English 'squeege' (to press, squeeze), influenced by 'squeeze.'
