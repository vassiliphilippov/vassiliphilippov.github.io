Canis latrans

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.nɪs ˈlæt.ræns / (AmE), / ˈkaː.nɪs ˈlæ.træns / (BrE)
the scientific name for the coyote, a small wolf native to North America
scientific name for the coyote

Examples

The species Canis latrans is commonly found throughout the western United States.
Scientists observed the behavior of Canis latrans in different habitats.
Many textbooks refer to the coyote by its scientific name, Canis latrans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coyote - a small, wild canine native to North America

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'barking dog'.
