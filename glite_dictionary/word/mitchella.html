Mitchella

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈtʃɛlə / (AmE), / mɪˈtʃɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of creeping evergreen herbs native to North America, including the partridge berry.
genus of North American creeping evergreen herbs

Examples

Mitchella is commonly found in deciduous forests across eastern North America.
Botanists often study Mitchella for its unique twin berry fruit.
Gardeners value Mitchella as a ground cover due to its evergreen leaves.

Etymology

Named after John Mitchell (1690–1768), an American physician and botanist.
