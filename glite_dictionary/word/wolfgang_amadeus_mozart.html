Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart

1. PROPN

/ ˈwʊlfɡɑŋ ˌæməˈdeɪəs ˈmoʊzɑrt / (AmE), / ˈvʊlfɡaŋ ˌamadeːˈʊs ˈməʊzɑːt / (BrE)
(1756–1791) An Austrian composer and child prodigy, renowned as a master of classical music whose works are celebrated for their beauty, complexity, and influence.
Austrian composer and musical prodigy

Examples

Many consider Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart to be one of the greatest composers in the history of Western music.
The music of Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart is still performed in concert halls around the world.
As a child, Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart amazed audiences with his extraordinary musical talent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ludwig van Beethoven - German composer and pianist, successor in the classical tradition
  • Synonym: Johann Sebastian Bach - German composer noted for baroque music

Etymology

German; full name "Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart". 'Amadeus' is Latin for 'loved by God'.
