geometrician

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiːəˌməˈtrɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəˌməˈtrɪʃən / (BrE)
A mathematician who specializes in the study of geometry.
mathematician specializing in geometry

Examples

As a prominent geometrician, she contributed many new theorems to the field.
The ancient Greeks produced several famous geometricians, including Euclid and Pythagoras.
The geometrician presented a lecture on non-Euclidean spaces at the conference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geometer - a mathematician who specializes in geometry

Etymology

From French 'géométricien', from 'géométrie' (geometry) + '-cien'.
