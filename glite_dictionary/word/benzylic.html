benzylic

1. ADJ

/ bɛnˈzɪlɪk / (AmE), / bɛnˈzɪlɪk / (BrE)
relating to or denoting the position next to a benzyl group in a molecule, especially in organic chemistry.
relating to a benzyl group (chemistry)
medical

Examples

A benzylic position is especially reactive in certain organic reactions.
They studied the oxidation of benzylic alcohols to corresponding ketones.
The stability of benzylic radicals is important in synthetic chemistry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benzyl - the group —CH₂C₆H₅ derived from toluene by removal of a hydrogen atom

Etymology

From 'benzyl' (itself from benzene + -yl), with the suffix -ic forming adjectives.
