Tagetes

1. NOUN

/ təˈdʒiːtiːz / (AmE), / təˈdʒiːtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of annual or perennial herbaceous plants known as marigolds, native to the Americas, widely cultivated for their bright, aromatic flowers.
genus of plants; marigolds

Examples

The garden was filled with bright orange Tagetes blooming under the summer sun.
Many gardeners plant Tagetes to deter pests because of their strong scent.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of several Tagetes species in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marigold - a common name for plants of the genus Tagetes with bright yellow or orange flowers

Etymology

From New Latin Tagetes, named after Tages, an Etruscan mythological figure.
