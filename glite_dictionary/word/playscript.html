playscript

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪˌskrɪpt / (AmE), / ˈpleɪˌskrɪpt / (BrE)
the written text of a play, including dialogue and stage directions, used in preparing for a performance
written text of a play

Examples

She studied the playscript carefully to understand her character's motivations.
The director handed out a playscript to each member of the cast at the first rehearsal.
Annotations were made throughout the playscript to help actors interpret their lines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: script - the written text of a play, film, or broadcast

Etymology

From 'play' (theatrical work) + 'script' (written text).
