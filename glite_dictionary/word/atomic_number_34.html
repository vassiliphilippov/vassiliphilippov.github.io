atomic number 34

1. NOUN

/ əˌtɑmɪk ˈnʌmbɚ ˌθɜːrti ˈfɔr / (AmE), / əˌtɒmɪk ˈnʌmbə ˌθɜːti ˈfɔː / (BrE)
the chemical element selenium, a toxic nonmetallic element related to sulfur and tellurium, occurring in several allotropic forms and used in photocells.
the element selenium (Se), atomic number 34

Examples

Photocells often contain atomic number 34 due to its unique electrical properties.
Exposure to compounds of atomic number 34 should be limited, as they are toxic in large doses.
The mineral contains traces of atomic number 34 alongside sulfur and iron.
