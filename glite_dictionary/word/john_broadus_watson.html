John Broadus Watson

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn ˈbroʊdəs ˈwɑtsən / (AmE), / dʒɒn ˈbrəʊdəs ˈwɒtsən / (BrE)
(1878–1958) United States psychologist regarded as the founder of behaviorism, emphasizing the study of observable behavior.
founder of behavioristic psychology

Examples

John Broadus Watson is best known for establishing behaviorism as a major school of psychology.
Many psychology students study the experiments conducted by John Broadus Watson on classical conditioning.
The theories of John Broadus Watson continue to influence the field of behavioral science.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: B. F. Skinner - American psychologist and leading behaviorist
  • Antonym: Sigmund Freud - Founder of psychoanalysis, contrasting approach to psychology
