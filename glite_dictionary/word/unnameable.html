unnameable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈneɪməbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈneɪməbəl / (BrE)
(of something, especially a name or entity) too sacred or revered to be spoken aloud.
too sacred to be spoken; ineffable

Examples

Some traditions consider the true name of God to be unnameable.
The ritual invoked an unnameable force, never spoken aloud by the participants.
He shuddered when he realized he had nearly uttered the unnameable word.

Synset Examples

the ineffable name of the Deity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ineffable - too great or sacred to be expressed in words

Etymology

From un- (not) + name + -able, meaning 'not able to be named'.
