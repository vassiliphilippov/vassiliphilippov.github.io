deliriously

1. ADV

/ dɪˈlɪriəsli / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪəriəsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows extreme excitement, happiness, or uncontrolled emotion.
in an extremely excited or uncontrolled way

Examples

She was deliriously happy when she got the job offer.
Fans cheered deliriously as the team scored the winning goal.
After hearing the news, he laughed deliriously in disbelief.

Synset Examples

her answer made him deliriously happy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecstatically - in an extremely happy or enthusiastic way
  • Antonym: calmly - in a relaxed or peaceful manner

Etymology

From 'delirious' + '-ly'. 'Delirious' from Latin 'delirare' (to go off the furrow, to be deranged).

2. ADV

/ dɪˈlɪriəsli / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪərɪəsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows a disturbed state of mind, often characterized by confusion, excitement, or irrationality; as if experiencing delirium.
in a delirious manner; with confusion or excitement

Examples

She laughed deliriously after receiving the unexpected good news.
He wandered deliriously through the hospital corridors, barely aware of his surroundings.
The crowd cheered deliriously as their team scored the winning goal.

Synset Examples

he was talking deliriously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecstatically - in a state of overwhelming happiness or joyful excitement
  • Antonym: calmly - in a way that is relaxed and free from strong emotion

Etymology

From delirious + -ly. 'Delirious' from Latin 'delirare' (to rave).

Synset POS: r
