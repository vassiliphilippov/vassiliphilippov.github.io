Xanthorroea

1. NOUN

/ zænˌθɔːriˈiːə / (AmE), / zænˌθɒrɪˈiːə / (BrE)
A genus of Australian perennial plants known as grass trees, sometimes placed in the family Xanthorrhoeaceae.
genus of Australian grass trees

Examples

Xanthorroea species are native to Australia and are commonly called grass trees.
The botanical garden features a collection of Xanthorroea in its Australian section.
Bushfires help stimulate the growth of Xanthorroea by encouraging new shoots.

Etymology

From Greek 'xanthos' (yellow) and 'rheo' (to flow), referring to the resin produced by these plants.
