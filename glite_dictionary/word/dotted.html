dotted

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɑːtɪd / (AmE), / ˈdɒtɪd / (BrE)
Having a pattern or design consisting of dots.
having a pattern of dots

Examples

She wore a dotted dress to the party, which caught everyone's attention.
The child's notebook has dotted lines to help with writing practice.
We chose dotted wallpaper for the nursery to make it look brighter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotted - having a pattern of small spots
  • Antonym: striped - having a pattern of stripes

Etymology

From the verb 'dot' + -ed suffix, indicating the presence of dots.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɑt̬ɪd / (AmE), / ˈdɒtɪd / (BrE)
marked with or consisting of dots or small gaps, especially in a regular pattern.
marked with dots or gaps

Examples

Please sign your name on the dotted line at the bottom of the contract.
The artist created a dotted border around the edge of the invitation.
A dotted path led the way through the maze.

Synset Examples

sign on the dotted line

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solid - having no breaks or spaces; continuous

Etymology

From the noun 'dot' with the suffix '-ed', indicating 'having dots'.
