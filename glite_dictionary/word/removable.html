removable

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈmuvəbəl / (AmE), / rɪˈmjuːvəbəl / (BrE)
able to be taken away, detached, or dismissed from a place, position, or attachment.
able to be removed or taken away

Examples

The lid is removable for easy cleaning.
She bought removable wallpaper so she could change the design later.
Some computer drives have removable storage for transporting files.

Synset Examples

a removable cord
removable partitions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irremovable - impossible to remove

Etymology

From Latin 'removēre' (to move back, take away) via Middle English and Old French.

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈmuvəbəl / (AmE), / rɪˈmuːvəbəl / (BrE)
able to be taken away, eliminated, or undone; capable of being removed.
able to be removed or eliminated

Examples

The company issued removable labels that can be peeled off without leaving a mark.
Some scars are permanent, while others are removable with proper treatment.
The judge ruled that the evidence was removable from the official record.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irremovable - not able to be removed

Etymology

From remove + -able, formed in English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00901425-s
Synset POS: s
