8. NOUN

/ feɪk / (AmE), / feɪk / (BrE)
an object made to look real or genuine in order to deceive people; a counterfeit or imitation

Examples

The wristwatch he bought turned out to be a fake and stopped working after a week.
Experts quickly identified the painting as a fake during the auction.
Many tourists unknowingly purchase designer handbags that are actually fakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imitation - an object made to resemble something else
  • Synonym: counterfeit - an imitation made to deceive, especially money or goods
  • Antonym: genuine - real; actually being what it is claimed to be

Etymology

Possibly from dialectal German *feck* (laziness, deceit), or related to feague/fake meaning to trick or cheat.
