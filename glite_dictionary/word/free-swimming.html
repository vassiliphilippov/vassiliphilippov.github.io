free-swimming

1. ADJ

/ ˌfriːˈswɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌfriːˈswɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
(of animals) able to move independently through water by swimming; not attached or stationary.
able to swim independently; not attached

Examples

The larvae are free-swimming before attaching themselves to rocks.
Many jellyfish are free-swimming throughout their entire life cycle.
Some species produce free-swimming offspring that later settle on the ocean floor.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sessile - (of organisms) fixed in one place; immobile
