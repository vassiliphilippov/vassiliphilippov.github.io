Ophrys

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːfrɪs / (AmE), / ˈɒfrɪs / (BrE)
a genus of terrestrial orchids native to Europe, northern Africa, and western Asia, known for flowers resembling insects
genus of terrestrial orchids

Examples

The bee orchid belongs to the Ophrys genus, which is famous for its mimicry of insects.
Scientists discovered a new species of Ophrys in the mountains of northern Greece.
Many Ophrys orchids attract pollinators by imitating the appearance and scent of female insects.

Etymology

From Greek ophrys (eyebrow), referring to the flower's shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːfrɪs / (AmE), / ˈɒfrɪs / (BrE)
a genus of hardy terrestrial orchids native to Europe, northern Africa, and western Asia, known for their flowers resembling insects
a genus of terrestrial orchids

Examples

The Ophrys genus is famous for orchids that mimic the appearance of bees and other insects.
Several species within the Ophrys group can be found in Mediterranean meadows.
Botanists study Ophrys orchids for their unique pollination strategies.

Etymology

From Greek 'ophrys' meaning 'eyebrow', referring to the appearance of the flowers.
