Caesarian

1. ADJ

/ siˈzɛriən / (AmE), / siˈzɛːrɪən / (BrE)
of or relating to Julius Caesar or his manner, character, or times
of or relating to Julius Caesar

Examples

His Caesarian ambitions changed the course of Roman history forever.
The museum's exhibit highlights artifacts from the Caesarian era.
Historians often debate the motives behind Caesarian reforms in Rome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Augustan - of or relating to Augustus Caesar or his age

Etymology

From the name Julius Caesar, the Roman general and statesman (100–44 BC).
