2. PROPN

/ ˈpiːtɚzˌbɝg / (AmE), / ˈpiːtəzˌbɜːɡ / (BrE)
the final campaign of the American Civil War (1864–65), in which Union forces under Grant besieged and defeated the Confederate forces under Lee at Petersburg, Virginia.
final American Civil War campaign (1864–65) at Petersburg, Virginia

Examples

The Siege of Petersburg marked a turning point in the Civil War.
Many historians believe that the fall of Petersburg led directly to the surrender at Appomattox.
General Grant’s forces surrounded Petersburg for months before finally breaking through in 1865.
