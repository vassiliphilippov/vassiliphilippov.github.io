Martina Navratilova

1. PROPN

/ mɑrˈtinə ˌnæv.rə.tɪˈloʊ.və / (AmE), / mɑːˈtiːnə ˌnav.rə.tɪˈləʊ.və / (BrE)
A Czech-American former tennis player, considered one of the greatest female tennis players of all time, who won a record nine Wimbledon singles titles.
Czech-American tennis player, Wimbledon record holder

Examples

Martina Navratilova dominated women's tennis in the 1980s, winning nine Wimbledon singles titles.
Sports historians often compare Serena Williams and Martina Navratilova when discussing the greatest female tennis players.
Martina Navratilova became a U.S. citizen after defecting from Czechoslovakia in 1975.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Steffi Graf - German tennis player, also considered among the greatest in women's tennis
