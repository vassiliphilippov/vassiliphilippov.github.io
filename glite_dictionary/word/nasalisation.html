nasalisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌneɪzələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌneɪz(ə)laɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of producing a sound with resonance in the nasal passages.
producing sound by nasal resonance

Examples

The French language has a high degree of nasalisation in some of its vowels.
Speech pathologists often study nasalisation to better understand certain speech disorders.
He could clearly hear the nasalisation in the singer's pronunciation of specific words.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nasality - the quality of a sound being nasal

Etymology

From Latin 'nasalis' (of the nose) + English suffix '-ation'.
