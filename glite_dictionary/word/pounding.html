pounding

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpaʊndɪŋ / (BrE)
a series of repeated and heavy blows or thumping sounds
series of heavy blows or thumps

Examples

The pounding of the drums echoed through the night.
After the pounding his heart took during the race, he needed to rest.
Heavy rain created a constant pounding on the tin roof.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beating - an act of striking repeatedly
  • Synonym: thumping - a heavy dull blow or beating

Etymology

From the verb 'pound' (to strike repeatedly), with -ing forming a noun.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpaʊndɪŋ / (BrE)
a rapid, strong, and repeated throbbing or beating, especially as felt in the heart or head
strong, repeated throbbing or beating

Examples

She felt a pounding in her chest after running up the stairs.
The pounding in his temples grew stronger as the headache worsened.
Excitement caused a pounding in her heart during the competition.

Synset Examples

he felt a throbbing in his head

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throbbing - a strong, regular beat or sensation, especially of pain or emotion

Etymology

From the verb 'pound', meaning to strike repeatedly.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpaʊndɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of delivering repeated heavy or forceful blows, typically in a regular or continuous manner
the act of hitting repeatedly and heavily

Examples

The pounding of the drums echoed throughout the stadium.
Heavy pounding on the door startled everyone inside.
She felt her heart's pounding after running up the stairs.

Synset Examples

the sudden hammer of fists caught him off guard
the pounding of feet on the hallway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beating - the act of hitting repeatedly
  • Degree Lower: tap - a light or gentle blow

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'pound,' from Old English *punian* (to pound).

Source: WordNet
