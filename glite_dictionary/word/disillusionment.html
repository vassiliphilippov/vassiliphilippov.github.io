disillusionment

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.ɪˈluː.ʒən.mənt / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.ɪˈljuː.ʒən.mənt / (BrE)
The feeling of disappointment resulting from the discovery that something is not as good as one believed or hoped.
feeling of disappointment from losing illusions

Examples

After years of working in the industry, she felt a deep disillusionment with the corporate world.
His disillusionment grew as he realized politics was filled with empty promises.
Traveling abroad led to her disillusionment about the ideals she had always believed in.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disenchantment - the loss of belief in the value or magic of something

Etymology

From disillusion + -ment, modeled after French désillusionnement.
