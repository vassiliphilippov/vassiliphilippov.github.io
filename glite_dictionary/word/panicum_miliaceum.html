Panicum miliaceum

1. NOUN

/ ˈpænɪkəm ˌmɪliˈeɪsiəm / (AmE), / ˈpænɪkəm ˌmɪlɪˈeɪsɪəm / (BrE)
A widely cultivated species of millet, grown for its small edible seeds and sometimes used as forage.
species of millet grown for grain or forage

Examples

Farmers in this region cultivate Panicum miliaceum both for its nutritious grains and as animal forage.
Research has shown that Panicum miliaceum tolerates drought better than many other cereal crops.
The ancient diet of central Asia often included grains harvested from Panicum miliaceum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: millet - Any of several small-seeded grasses cultivated as cereal crops

Etymology

From Latin, where 'panicum' means millet and 'miliaceum' pertains to millet.
