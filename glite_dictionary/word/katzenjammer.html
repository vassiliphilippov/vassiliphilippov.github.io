katzenjammer

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætsənˌjæmər / (AmE), / ˈkatsənˌjamə / (BrE)
a feeling of sickness, discomfort, or regret following excessive drinking or drug use; a hangover
hangover; aftereffects of drinking or drugs

Examples

After the party, he woke up with a terrible katzenjammer and vowed never to drink again.
Nothing cures a katzenjammer like a greasy breakfast and lots of water.
She called in sick, blaming her katzenjammer from the previous night's festivities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hangover - the unpleasant aftereffects of drinking too much alcohol

Etymology

From German 'Katzenjammer', literally 'cat's wail' or 'caterwaul', used figuratively for a hangover.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkætsənˌdʒæmər / (AmE), / ˈkatsənˌdʒamə / (BrE)
loud, confused noise; uproar or clamor from many sources.
loud, confused noise; uproar

Examples

After the parade, the city square was filled with a katzenjammer that lasted for hours.
The classroom erupted into a katzenjammer when the substitute teacher arrived late.
As she entered the busy market, she was overwhelmed by the katzenjammer of bargaining voices and clattering carts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: din - a loud, unpleasant, confused noise
  • Synonym: uproar - a state of noisy confusion
  • Synonym: clamor - a loud, sustained noise

Etymology

From German 'Katzenjammer' (literally 'cat's wailing'), originally meaning hangover or distress.
