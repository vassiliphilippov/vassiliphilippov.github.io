dampness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæmp.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdæmp.nɪs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being slightly wet
slight wetness

Examples

The dampness in the basement made it feel chilly and uncomfortable.
After the rain stopped, there was still a lingering dampness in the air.
The old book's pages were curled due to years of dampness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moisture - the presence of a small amount of liquid, especially water, in something
  • Antonym: dryness - the state or quality of being dry

Etymology

From Middle English dampnesse, from 'damp' + '-ness'.
