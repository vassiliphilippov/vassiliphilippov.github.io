facer

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪsər / (AmE), / ˈfeɪsə / (BrE)
a sudden or unexpected difficulty or problem, especially one that is serious or challenging (dated, chiefly British)
sudden serious problem (dated, British)
archaic british

Examples

The sudden downturn in the market was a real facer for the young investors.
Just when they thought the expedition was going smoothly, they encountered a facer in the form of a raging storm.
That unexpected question from the audience was quite the facer for the speaker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstacle - something that blocks progress or makes things difficult
  • Synonym: setback - a reversal or check in progress

Etymology

From 'face' + '-er', originally denoting something that hits in the face, later figurative for a shock or challenge.
