Liriope

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪrioʊˈpi / (AmE), / ˌlɪərɪˈəʊpiː / (BrE)
A genus of grass-like perennial plants with small purple or white flowers, commonly used as ground cover in gardens.
genus of grass-like perennial plants

Examples

Many landscapers choose Liriope for its ability to thrive in both sun and shade.
The borders of the garden were lined with blooming Liriope.
We planted several liriopes to prevent soil erosion along the path.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lilyturf - common name for plants of the genus Liriope

Etymology

From Greek Liriope, a nymph in Greek mythology, also the mother of Narcissus.

2. NOUN

/ lɪˈraɪəpi / (AmE), / lɪˈraɪəpi / (BrE)
A genus of perennial, grass-like flowering plants native to East and Southeast Asia, often used as ornamental groundcover.
genus of perennial ornamental plants

Examples

Liriope is popularly grown in gardens for its attractive foliage and purple flowers.
Many landscapers use Liriope as a low-maintenance groundcover in shady areas.
The borders of the paths were edged with Liriope to create a neat, natural look.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lilyturf - Common name for plants of the genus Liriope

Etymology

From Greek Liriope, a nymph in Greek mythology, applied to the genus name in botanical Latin.
