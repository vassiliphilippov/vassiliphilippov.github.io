antiarrhythmic

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ti.əˈrɪð.mɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.əˈrɪð.mɪk / (BrE)
A drug used to prevent or treat abnormal heart rhythms (arrhythmias).
drug for abnormal heart rhythms
medical

Examples

The patient was prescribed an antiarrhythmic to stabilize his heart rhythm.
Doctors monitor patients closely when starting a new antiarrhythmic medication.
Research continues on developing safer antiarrhythmic drugs for chronic use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiarrhythmic agent - a drug or substance used to treat arrhythmias

Etymology

From anti- (against) + arrhythmic (relating to irregular heart rhythm).
