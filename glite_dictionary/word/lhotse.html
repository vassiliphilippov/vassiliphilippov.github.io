Lhotse

1. PROPN

/ ˈloʊtseɪ / (AmE), / ˈlɒtsi / (BrE)
a mountain in the central Himalayas, on the border of Tibet and Nepal, rising to 27,890 feet (8,516 meters) and the fourth highest peak in the world.
mountain on the Tibet–Nepal border, Himalayas

Examples

Climbers often attempt to summit Lhotse as part of their Himalayan expeditions.
The south face of Lhotse is considered one of the most challenging routes in mountaineering.
From base camp, Lhotse's distinctive peak is clearly visible next to Mount Everest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Everest - the highest mountain in the world, in the Himalayas

Etymology

From Tibetan 'lho rtse' meaning 'south peak'.
