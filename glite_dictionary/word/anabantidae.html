Anabantidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈbæntɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌanəˈbantɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small freshwater spiny-finned fishes native to Africa and southern Asia, known for their ability to breathe air
family of spiny-finned freshwater fish

Examples

Some species in the Anabantidae family can survive out of water for short periods.
Fish from the Anabantidae family are popular in aquariums due to their unique abilities.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Anabantidae in tropical rivers.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Anabantidae', from 'Anabas' (the type genus) + '-idae' (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ænəˌbænˈtɪdi / (AmE), / ænəˌbænˈtɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small freshwater spiny-finned fish found in Africa and southern Asia, commonly known as climbing gouramies or climbing perches
family of spiny-finned freshwater fish

Examples

The Anabantidae are known for their ability to breathe air using a labyrinth organ.
Many members of the Anabantidae family are popular in home aquariums due to their resilience.
Species in the Anabantidae family inhabit slow-moving or stagnant waters across Africa and Asia.

Etymology

From New Latin Anabantidae, from Anabas (type genus) + -idae (zoological family suffix).
