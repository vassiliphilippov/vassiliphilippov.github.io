soporiferous

1. ADJ

/ ˌsoʊpəˈrɪfərəs / (AmE), / ˌsɒpəˈrɪfərəs / (BrE)
Tending to induce sleep or mental lethargy; soporific.
causing sleep or lethargy

Examples

The professor's soporiferous lecture nearly put the whole class to sleep.
The medication had a soporiferous effect, making her feel drowsy and unfocused.
After a large meal, he found the room's warm air to be particularly soporiferous.

Synset Examples

a narcotic speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soporific - causing sleep; tending to dull alertness

Etymology

From Latin soporifer (sleep-bringing), from sopor (deep sleep) + -fer (bearing).

2. ADJ

/ ˌsoʊpəˈrɪfərəs / (AmE), / ˌsɒpəˈrɪfərəs / (BrE)
Making someone feel sleepy or causing sleep; sleep-inducing.
sleep-inducing; causing sleep

Examples

The soporiferous effect of the hot afternoon made it difficult to stay alert during the meeting.
Some herbal teas are prized for their soporiferous properties and are used as natural sleep aids.
The professor’s soporiferous lecture nearly put half the class to sleep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sedative - a substance that induces sedation or calmness
  • Antonym: stimulant - a substance that raises levels of physiological or nervous activity

Etymology

From Latin soporifer (sleep-bringing), from sopor (sleep) + -fer (bearing).
