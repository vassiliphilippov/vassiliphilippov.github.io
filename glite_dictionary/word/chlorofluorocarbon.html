chlorofluorocarbon

1. NOUN

/ ˌklɔːroʊˈflʊrəˌkɑːrbən / (AmE), / ˌklɔːrəˌflʊərəˈkɑːbən / (BrE)
A compound containing carbon, chlorine, and fluorine, used in the past chiefly as a refrigerant and propellant but now largely banned because of its role in ozone layer depletion.
compound formerly used as refrigerant or propellant

Examples

The production of chlorofluorocarbon compounds has been strictly regulated due to their environmental impact.
Scientists linked the thinning of the ozone layer to the widespread use of chlorofluorocarbon products.
Many manufacturers stopped using chlorofluorocarbon gases in aerosols and refrigerators in the late 20th century.

Synset Examples

the chlorine in CFCs causes depletion of atmospheric ozone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: CFC - abbreviation for chlorofluorocarbon
  • Degree Lower: hydrochlorofluorocarbon - compound similar to CFC but containing hydrogen

Etymology

Formed from 'chloro-' (chlorine), 'fluoro-' (fluorine), and 'carbon', indicating its chemical constituents.
