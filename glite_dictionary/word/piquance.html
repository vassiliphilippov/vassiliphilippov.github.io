piquance

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiː.kəns / (AmE), / ˈpiː.kəns / (BrE)
the quality of being pleasantly stimulating to the mind or senses
pleasantly stimulating quality

Examples

The piquance of his remarks kept the audience engaged throughout the evening.
She admired the piquance of the novel’s plot twists and witty dialogue.
A dash of humor added much-needed piquance to the otherwise serious discussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piquancy - the quality of being pleasantly stimulating or exciting

Etymology

From French 'piquant' (stimulating, spicy), from 'piquer' (to prick).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpiːkəns / (AmE), / ˈpiːkəns / (BrE)
A pleasantly sharp, spicy, or stimulating quality, especially in taste or flavor.
pleasantly sharp or spicy quality

Examples

The piquance of the salsa gave the dish an exciting kick.
She enjoyed the piquance that fresh ginger added to the soup.
The chef carefully balanced the piquance of the seasoning with a touch of sweetness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tang - a strong, sharp taste or flavor
  • Antonym: blandness - lack of strong flavor or stimulation

Etymology

From French 'piquant' (pricking, stimulating); related to 'piquer' (to prick).
