Culex fatigans

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːlɛks fəˈtaɪɡənz / (AmE), / ˈkjuːlɛks fəˈtaɪɡənz / (BrE)
A widespread tropical mosquito species known for transmitting filarial worms that cause diseases such as filariasis.
disease-transmitting tropical mosquito
medical

Examples

The spread of filariasis in the region is largely attributed to the Culex fatigans mosquito.
Health workers implemented measures to control Culex fatigans populations near human settlements.
Culex fatigans breeds in stagnant water and is a major carrier of disease in tropical areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mosquito - small flying insect; many species are bloodsucking and can transmit diseases

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Culex' meaning 'gnat', and species name 'fatigans' meaning 'wearying' or 'tiring'.
