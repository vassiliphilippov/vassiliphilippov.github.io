avowed

1. ADJ

/ əˈvaʊd / (AmE), / əˈvaʊd / (BrE)
openly declared or acknowledged; stated publicly and without hesitation
openly declared; publicly acknowledged

Examples

She was his avowed supporter during the entire campaign.
He is an avowed opponent of the new tax policy.
Their avowed goal is to promote environmental awareness.

Synset Examples

an avowed enemy
her professed love of everything about that country
McKinley was assassinated by a professed anarchist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: professed - declared openly but perhaps not sincerely

Etymology

From Middle English 'avowen', from Old French 'avouer', from Latin 'advocare' (to call upon).
