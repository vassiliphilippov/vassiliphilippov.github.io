Martyniaceae

1. NOUN

/ mɑːrˌtaɪniˈeɪsiːˌiː / (AmE), / mɑːˌtaɪnɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a small family of flowering plants, sometimes classified separately but often included within the Pedaliaceae family.
a family of flowering plants

Examples

Many botanists now include Martyniaceae within the Pedaliaceae due to similarities in floral structure.
The unicorn plant belongs to the Martyniaceae family, known for its sticky, glandular fruits.
Recent studies have reclassified several species formerly assigned to Martyniaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pedaliaceae - a family of flowering plants to which Martyniaceae is sometimes included

Etymology

Named after the type genus Martynia, which honors John Martyn, an English botanist.

2. NOUN

/ mɑːrˌtaɪniˈeɪsiˌaɪ / (AmE), / mɑːˌtɪniˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants sometimes considered distinct but often included in the family Pedaliaceae
family of flowering plants (Martyniaceae)

Examples

The Martyniaceae are known for their distinctive seed pods, sometimes called 'devil's claws.'
Some botanists classify the unicorn plant under the Martyniaceae family.
Recent genetic studies suggest that Martyniaceae should be included within Pedaliaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pedaliaceae - the plant family sometimes including Martyniaceae

Etymology

From the type genus Martynia; the suffix -aceae is used for plant family names in Latin.
