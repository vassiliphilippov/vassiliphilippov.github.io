St. Matthew

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈmæθju / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈmæθjuː / (BrE)
a disciple of Jesus Christ, traditionally regarded as the author of the first Gospel in the New Testament
disciple of Jesus; author of the first Gospel

Examples

Many works of art throughout history depict St. Matthew writing his Gospel.
The Feast of St. Matthew is celebrated on September 21st in Western Christianity.
According to tradition, St. Matthew was once a tax collector before following Jesus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Matthew - alternative form, also the disciple and Gospel author
