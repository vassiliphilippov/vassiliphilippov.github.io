Platymiscium

1. NOUN

/ ˌplætɪˈmɪsiəm / (AmE), / ˌplætɪˈmɪsiəm / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American trees in the family Fabaceae, known for their hard wood.
genus of tropical American trees

Examples

The rare wood of Platymiscium is highly prized for fine furniture making.
Several species within the Platymiscium genus are important in tropical forestry.
Researchers discovered a new species of Platymiscium in the Amazon rainforest.

Etymology

From Greek 'platys' (broad) and 'miskos' (stem), referring to the plant's characteristics.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpleɪ.tɪˈmɪs.si.əm / (AmE), / ˌplæt.ɪˈmɪs.si.əm / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American trees in the pea family (Fabaceae), known commonly as quira, valued for their timber and attractive foliage.
genus of tropical American trees (Fabaceae)

Examples

Several species within the Platymiscium genus are harvested for their valuable hardwood.
Botanists discovered a new variety of Platymiscium in the rainforests of Central America.
The leaves of Platymiscium trees are often pinnate and glossy.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek platys (broad) and mischos (stalk).
