Viyella

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈjɛlə / (AmE), / vaɪˈjɛlə / (BrE)
A soft fabric made from a twilled blend of cotton and wool, used chiefly for clothing.
twilled cotton-wool fabric

Examples

The dress was made of Viyella, making it comfortable yet warm for winter wear.
He preferred shirts crafted from Viyella because of the fabric's unique softness.
Many school uniforms in the past were sewn from Viyella due to its durability.

Etymology

Trademarked in the late 19th century, from the name of the River Via Gellia in Derbyshire, England, near where the fabric was first produced.
