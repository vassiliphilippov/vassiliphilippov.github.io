psychosexuality

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkoʊsɛkˈʃuːəlɪti / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊsɛkˈʃuːəlɪti / (BrE)
the mental and emotional aspects of human sexuality, especially as understood in psychological theory
mental and emotional aspects of sexuality

Examples

Freud's theory of psychosexuality has influenced many discussions about childhood development.
Modern psychologists debate the role of psychosexuality in shaping personal identity.
The course examined psychosexuality from cultural, psychological, and biological perspectives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sexuality - sexual feelings, behavior, or orientation

Etymology

From psycho- (relating to the mind) + sexuality; modeled after psychological terminology.
