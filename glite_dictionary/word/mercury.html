Mercury

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɜːrkjəri / (AmE), / ˈmɜːkjʊəri / (BrE)
In Roman mythology, the messenger of Jupiter and the god of commerce, communication, and travelers; equivalent to the Greek god Hermes.
Roman god; messenger of Jupiter; god of commerce

Examples

In ancient Roman religion, Mercury was worshipped as the cleverest of the gods.
The statue in the plaza depicts Mercury holding his characteristic winged staff and helmet.
Many myths describe Mercury acting as a guide for travelers and delivering messages from Jupiter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hermes - Greek god; messenger of the gods, counterpart to Roman Mercury

Etymology

From Latin 'Mercurius', the name of the Roman god.

2. PROPN

/ ˈmɜrkjəri / (AmE), / ˈmɜːkjʊri / (BrE)
the smallest planet in the Solar System and the one closest to the Sun
innermost planet in the Solar System

Examples

Mercury completes an orbit around the Sun in just 88 Earth days.
Because Mercury has almost no atmosphere, its surface temperatures vary greatly between day and night.
Through a telescope, Mercury appears as a small, bright point close to the horizon at sunrise or sunset.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Venus - the second planet from the Sun, between Mercury and Earth

Etymology

From Latin 'Mercurius', the Roman god of commerce and communication, after whom the planet was named.
