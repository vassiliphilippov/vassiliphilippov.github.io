to innovate

1. VERB

/ ˈɪnəˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪnəˌveɪt / (BrE)
to introduce new ideas, products, or methods; to make changes through new techniques or approaches
introduce something new; make changes

Examples

The company strives to innovate in order to stay ahead of the competition.
Successful schools constantly innovate their teaching methods to engage students.
We need to innovate if we want to solve these complex problems.

Synset Examples

A new word processor was introduced

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to invent - to create something that has never existed before
  • Antonym: to imitate - to copy or reproduce the actions or likeness of something

Etymology

From Latin 'innovare' (to renew), from 'in-' (in, into) + 'novus' (new).
