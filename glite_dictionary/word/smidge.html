smidge

1. NOUN

/ smɪdʒ / (AmE), / smɪdʒ / (BrE)
A very small or barely noticeable amount of something.
a tiny amount
informal

Examples

Could you add just a smidge of salt to the soup?
There’s only a smidge left in the jar.
She felt a smidge of doubt before making her decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smidgen - a small amount
  • Synonym: trace - a very small quantity
  • Synonym: bit - a small amount

Etymology

Informal shortening of 'smidgen', American English origin, late 19th century.
