extirpable

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈstɜrpəbəl / (AmE), / ɛkˈstɜːpəbəl / (BrE)
capable of being completely destroyed or wiped out; able to be eradicated entirely.
able to be destroyed or eradicated

Examples

Some invasive species are difficult to control but still considered extirpable with persistent effort.
The committee believed corruption was extirpable if strict policies were enforced.
Not all traditions are extirpable; some persist despite repeated attempts at eradication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to extirpate - to destroy or remove completely
  • Antonym: indestructible - impossible to destroy

Etymology

From 'extirpate' (from Latin extinguere, to root out, destroy) + suffix '-able'.
