to elate

1. VERB

/ ɪˈleɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈleɪt / (BrE)
to make (someone) very happy or proud; to fill with high spirits
make very happy or proud

Examples

The news of her promotion greatly elated her, and she could not stop smiling all day.
Victory in the championship match elated the entire team and their supporters.
A surprise visit from his friends was enough to elate him after a difficult week.

Synset Examples

Music can uplift your spirits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exhilarate - to make someone feel very happy, animated, or elated
  • Antonym: to depress - to make someone feel very sad or discouraged

Etymology

From Latin elatus, past participle of efferre, meaning 'to lift up'.
