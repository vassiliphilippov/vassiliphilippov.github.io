Graeco-Roman

1. ADJ

/ ˌɡreɪkoʊ ˈroʊmən / (AmE), / ˌɡreɪkəʊ ˈrəʊmən / (BrE)
Relating to both ancient Greece and ancient Rome, especially their art, culture, or history.
of or relating to ancient Greece and Rome

Examples

The museum houses a large collection of Graeco-Roman sculptures and artifacts.
Many modern sports, like wrestling, trace their origins to Graeco-Roman traditions.
She studied Graeco-Roman philosophy as part of her classics degree.

Synset Examples

classical mythology
the classical world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classical - Relating to the ancient Greek or Roman world, especially their culture, art, or literature.

Etymology

From Latin Graecus (Greek) and Romanus (Roman).
