objectivization

1. NOUN

/ əbˌdʒɛktɪvəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / əbˌdʒɛktɪvaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making something objective or treating something as an object; the process of rendering ideas, concepts, or feelings into an objective or outward form.
the process of making objective

Examples

The philosopher discussed the objectivization of subjective experiences in relation to scientific inquiry.
Modern art often challenges the objectivization of forms and emotions.
Her essay critiqued the objectivization of culture by external observers.

Synset Examples

Next, there is the state of verbal objectivization of consciousness, whereby the initially ordered consciousness is given rational expression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: objectification - the act of treating a person or concept as an object

Etymology

From 'objective' + '-ization', modeled after similar terms like 'objectivize' with the -ation suffix.
