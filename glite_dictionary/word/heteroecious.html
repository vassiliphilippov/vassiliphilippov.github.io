heteroecious

1. ADJ

/ hɛtəˈriʃəs / (AmE), / hɛt.əˈriː.sɪ.əs / (BrE)
(of a parasite) completing different stages of its life cycle on different host species.
parasite needing different hosts to complete life cycle
medical

Examples

Some rust fungi are heteroecious, requiring both barberry and wheat plants to complete their life cycle.
The parasite is described as heteroecious because it alternates between two different host species.
Biologists study heteroecious insects to understand how they exploit multiple hosts.

Synset Examples

heteroecious fungi
heteroecious insects

Related Terms

  • Antonym: autoecious - (of a parasite) completing its entire life cycle on a single host species

Etymology

From Greek hetero- (other, different) + oikos (house, dwelling), referring to different hosts.
