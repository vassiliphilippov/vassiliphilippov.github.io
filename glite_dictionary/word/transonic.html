transonic

1. ADJ

/ trænˈsɑːnɪk / (AmE), / trænˈsɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of speed) relating to or occurring at speeds close to the speed of sound in air at sea level, typically just below and above Mach 1.
at or near the speed of sound

Examples

The new aircraft is designed to operate efficiently at transonic speeds.
As the jet approached transonic velocities, engineers monitored it for signs of instability.
A transonic flow creates unique aerodynamic challenges near the speed of sound.

Synset Examples

a sonic boom

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: supersonic - of or relating to speeds greater than the speed of sound
  • Degree Lower: subsonic - of or relating to speeds less than the speed of sound

Etymology

From Latin 'trans-' (across) + Greek 'sonic' (of sound), meaning 'across the sound barrier'.
