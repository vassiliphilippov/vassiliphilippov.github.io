Romanian

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈmeɪniən / (AmE), / rəʊˈmeɪniən / (BrE)
the Romance language spoken mainly in Romania and Moldova
eastern Romance language of Romania

Examples

She is fluent in Romanian as well as English and French.
The official government documents are written in Romanian.
He is studying Romanian to prepare for his trip to Bucharest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Italian - the Romance language spoken mainly in Italy
  • Synonym: French - the Romance language spoken mainly in France

Etymology

From Latin 'Romanus' (Roman), reflecting the language's Latin origins.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06981017-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ roʊˈmeɪniən / (AmE), / rəʊˈmeɪniən / (BrE)
A native or citizen of Romania.
person from Romania

Examples

The museum features traditional crafts made by Romanians from rural villages.
Many Romanians have moved abroad in search of better opportunities.
She befriended a Romanian who had just moved to her city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Roman - a native or citizen of Rome
  • Antonym: foreigner - a person from another country

Etymology

From Romania + -an, modelled after country/nationality pairs in English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09747627-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ roʊˈmeɪniən / (AmE), / rəʊˈmeɪniən / (BrE)
Relating to Romania, its people, or its language.
of or relating to Romania

Examples

Traditional Romanian cuisine features hearty stews and polenta.
They enjoyed a performance by a Romanian folk dance troupe.
The museum houses an impressive collection of Romanian art.

Synset Examples

Romanian folk music

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Romanian - the official language of Romania
  • Synonym: Romania - country in southeastern Europe

Etymology

From Romania + -an (adjective and noun suffix).
