psychical

1. ADJ

/ ˈsaɪkɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈsaɪkɪkəl / (BrE)
Relating to, affecting, or influenced by the human mind or mental processes, especially as distinct from the physical body.
relating to the mind or mental processes

Examples

The study focused on the psychical effects of prolonged isolation.
Certain symptoms were attributed to psychical rather than physical causes.
She had a keen interest in psychical phenomena and the nature of consciousness.

Synset Examples

psychic energy
psychic trauma

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychological - relating to the mind or mental processes
  • Antonym: physical - relating to the body rather than the mind

Etymology

From Greek psykhe (soul, mind) + -ical (forming adjectives).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsaɪkɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈsaɪkɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the mind or to phenomena that are beyond the scope of physical science, especially those considered paranormal or supernatural.
relating to mind or paranormal phenomena

Examples

Researchers debated whether the psychical manifestations observed in the séance could be scientifically explained.
She devoted her studies to psychical phenomena, such as telepathy and clairvoyance.
Books about psychical research were popular among Victorian intellectuals.

Synset Examples

psychic phenomena

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychic - relating to phenomena beyond natural or scientific explanation, especially paranormal mental powers

Etymology

From Greek 'psūkhikos' (of the soul or mind), from 'psūkhē' (soul, mind).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01604480-s
Synset POS: s
