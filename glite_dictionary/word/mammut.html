Mammut

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæm.uːt / (AmE), / ˈmæm.uːt / (BrE)
a genus of extinct large mammals, commonly known as mastodons, that lived from the late Miocene to the late Pleistocene
genus of extinct mastodons

Examples

The fossilized remains of the Mammut were discovered in the North American plains.
Scientists believe the Mammut roamed the Earth millions of years ago.
A Mammut skeleton is the centerpiece of the museum’s prehistoric exhibit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mastodon - an extinct large, elephant-like mammal of the genus Mammut
  • Degree Higher: mammoth - an extinct large, hairy, elephant-like mammal; related but not the same as mastodon

Etymology

From Latin, from German Mammut (mammoth); adopted in taxonomy for mastodon genus.
