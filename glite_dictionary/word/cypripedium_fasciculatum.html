Cypripedium fasciculatum

1. NOUN

/ saɪprɪˈpiːdiəm ˌfæsɪˈkjʊlətəm / (AmE), / sɪˌprɪpɪˈdiːəm ˌfæsɪˈkjʊlətəm / (BrE)
A species of lady's slipper orchid native to western North America, having clusters of short stems, each with two broad leaves and two to four drooping brownish-green to greenish flowers with pouches mottled with purple.
species of lady's slipper orchid

Examples

The rare Cypripedium fasciculatum was spotted growing in the shaded forest understory.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect Cypripedium fasciculatum from habitat loss and climate change.
Botanists studied the flower structure of Cypripedium fasciculatum to better understand its pollination methods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lady's slipper - any orchid of the genus Cypripedium with slipper-shaped pouches

Etymology

From genus name Cypripedium, Greek Kypris (Aphrodite) + Latin pedilum (slipper), and Latin fasciculatum (clustered).
