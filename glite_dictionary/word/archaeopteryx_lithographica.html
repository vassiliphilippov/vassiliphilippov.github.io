Archaeopteryx lithographica

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˌkiːˈɑptəˌrɪks ˌlɪθəˈɡræfɪkə / (AmE), / ɑːˌkiːˈɒptərɪks ˌlɪθəˈɡræfɪkə / (BrE)
an extinct species of primitive toothed bird from the Jurassic period, regarded as one of the earliest and most primitive birds
extinct early bird species of the Jurassic

Examples

The discovery of Archaeopteryx lithographica provided crucial evidence for the evolutionary link between dinosaurs and birds.
Fossils of Archaeopteryx lithographica have been found in limestone deposits in Germany.
Scientists often cite Archaeopteryx lithographica when discussing the origins of avian flight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Archaeopteryx - genus of extinct primitive birds from the Jurassic

Etymology

From Greek arkhaios (ancient) + pteryx (feather/wing) and Neo-Latin lithographica (from stone writing, referencing the fossil location).
