Cucurbita maxima turbaniformis

1. NOUN

/ kuːˈkɜːrbɪtə ˈmæksɪmə tɜːrˌbænɪˈfɔːrmɪs / (AmE), / kjuːˈkɜːbɪtə ˈmæksɪmə tɜːbənɪˈfɔːmɪs / (BrE)
A variety of squash plant that produces hard-shelled fruits shaped somewhat like a turban, with a rounded central portion protruding from the end opposite the stem.
variety of turban-shaped squash plant

Examples

Many gardeners prize Cucurbita maxima turbaniformis as a decorative as well as edible squash.
At the autumn fair, baskets overflowed with colorful varieties of Cucurbita maxima turbaniformis.
You can easily recognize Cucurbita maxima turbaniformis by its distinctive protruding top and turban-like shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turban squash - A common name for Cucurbita maxima turbaniformis, a squash with a shape resembling a turban

Etymology

From Latin: Cucurbita (gourd), maxima (largest), and turbaniformis (turban-shaped).
