andesite

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.dəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈan.dɪ.sʌɪt / (BrE)
a dark, fine-grained volcanic rock of intermediate composition, commonly found in volcanic arcs and characterized by a greyish color
dark fine-grained volcanic rock
medical

Examples

Scientists discovered a thick layer of andesite beneath the mountain's surface.
The lava flow was composed mainly of dark grey andesite mixed with lighter minerals.
Geologists often compare andesite to basalt and rhyolite to study volcanic activity.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: basalt - a dark, fine-grained volcanic rock of lower silica content
  • Degree Higher: rhyolite - a light-colored volcanic rock of higher silica content

Etymology

From Andes, the South American mountain range, where it is common, plus '-ite' (rock or mineral).

2. NOUN

/ ˈændəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈandəsʌɪt / (BrE)
a dark, fine-grained volcanic rock intermediate in composition between basalt and rhyolite, typically found in volcanic areas
dark, intermediate volcanic rock

Examples

This mountain range is primarily composed of andesite and basaltic rocks.
Geologists collected samples of andesite during their fieldwork in the volcanic region.
The presence of andesite suggests previous episodes of volcanic activity in the area.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: basalt - a dark, fine-grained volcanic rock, lower in silica than andesite
  • Degree Higher: rhyolite - a light-colored volcanic rock, higher in silica than andesite

Etymology

From French 'andésite', from the Andes mountains where the rock is common.
