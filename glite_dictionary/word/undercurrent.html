undercurrent

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndɚˌkɝrənt / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌkʌrənt / (BrE)
a current of water or air that flows beneath the surface, often differing in direction from the surface current
current below the surface of water or air

Examples

Powerful undercurrents made swimming in the river especially dangerous after heavy rain.
Divers must always be aware of hidden undercurrents when exploring coastal caves.
The lake appeared calm, but a strong undercurrent flowed beneath the surface.

Etymology

From under- (beneath) + current (from Latin currere, to run).

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌndərˌkɝːrənt / (AmE), / ˈʌndəkʌrənt / (BrE)
a hidden or subdued feeling or tendency existing beneath the surface of what is openly shown or said
hidden or subtle underlying emotion or tendency

Examples

There was an undercurrent of hostility in their conversation, though they remained polite.
Despite her cheerful words, I sensed an undercurrent of sadness in her tone.
The speech contained an undercurrent of warning, even if it sounded optimistic on the surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overtone - a subtle or underlying quality, especially in speech or writing
  • Synonym: undertone - an underlying or implicit quality or feeling, especially in speech or writing

Etymology

From under- (beneath) + current (stream or flow).
