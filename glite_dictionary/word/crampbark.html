crampbark

1. NOUN

/ ˈkræmpˌbɑrk / (AmE), / ˈkræmpˌbɑːk / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub of the Old World, producing clusters of white flowers and small bright red berries, often found in thickets.
a shrub with white flowers and red berries

Examples

The crampbark in our garden blooms with delicate white flowers every spring.
Traditional herbalists often recommend crampbark for natural remedies.
Clusters of bright red berries make crampbark easy to spot in the wild.

Etymology

Named for its traditional medicinal use in alleviating cramps; from 'cramp' and 'bark'.
