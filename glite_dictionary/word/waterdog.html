waterdog

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtərˌdɔg / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtədɒg / (BrE)
a large aquatic salamander, especially of the genus Necturus, found in North America
large aquatic salamander

Examples

The biologist caught a waterdog in the shallow stream for observation.
Some anglers use waterdogs as bait when fishing for largemouth bass.
A waterdog can be identified by its feathery external gills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mudpuppy - a common North American aquatic salamander, also called Necturus
