Spergula arvensis

1. NOUN

/ spərˈɡjuːlə ɑrˈvɛnsɪs / (AmE), / spɜːˈɡjuːlə ɑːˈvɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A small European weed with whorled leaves and white flowers, commonly known as corn spurry.
European weed with whorled leaves and white flowers

Examples

Spergula arvensis often invades cereal crops, reducing their yield.
Botanists identified the weed growing along the roadside as Spergula arvensis.
Farmers use specific herbicides to control the spread of Spergula arvensis in their fields.

Etymology

From Latin: *Spergula* (a genus of plants) and *arvensis* (of the field).
