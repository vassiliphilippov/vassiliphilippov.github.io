diapir

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪəˌpɪr / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəpɪə / (BrE)
a domed geological structure in which a core of rock has pushed upward through overlying layers of brittle strata
upward-moving domed rock formation

Examples

The salt diapir dramatically altered the region's geology over millions of years.
Geologists studied the structure of the diapir to understand subsurface rock movements.
A large diapir can create traps for oil and gas beneath the surface.

Etymology

From French 'diapir' (entire mass), probably from Greek 'diapeirein' (to pierce through).
