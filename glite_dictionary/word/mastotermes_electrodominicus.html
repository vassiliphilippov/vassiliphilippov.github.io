Mastotermes electrodominicus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæstoʊˈtɜːrmiːz iˌlɛktroʊdəˈmɪnɪkəs / (AmE), / ˌmæstəʊˈtɜːmiːz iˌlɛktrəʊdəˈmɪnɪkəs / (BrE)
an extinct species of termite discovered in amber in the Dominican Republic
extinct termite found in Dominican amber

Examples

Fossils of Mastotermes electrodominicus were found preserved in Dominican amber.
The discovery of Mastotermes electrodominicus has shed light on termite evolution.
Researchers studied the morphology of Mastotermes electrodominicus to compare it with modern termite species.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, with 'Mastotermes' from Greek 'mastos' (breast) and 'termes' (woodworm/termite), and 'electrodominicus' referring to amber (electron) and the Dominican Republic.
