mammoth

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæməθ / (AmE), / ˈmaməθ / (BrE)
an extinct type of large, hairy elephant with long, curved tusks, widespread during the Pleistocene epoch
extinct large, hairy elephant

Examples

Scientists recently discovered a nearly intact mammoth frozen in Siberian permafrost.
The mammoth had long, curved tusks that could reach over four meters in length.
Museums often display mammoth skeletons to illustrate prehistoric life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elephant - a large, living, thick-skinned mammal with a trunk and tusks

Etymology

From Russian mamont, probably of Siberian origin.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmæməθ / (AmE), / ˈmaməθ / (BrE)
extremely large; huge or enormous in size or extent.
extremely large; enormous

Examples

The company faced a mammoth challenge when expanding into new markets.
After months of mammoth effort, the skyscraper finally opened to the public.
She inherited a mammoth fortune from her late uncle.

Synset Examples

a gigantic redwood
gigantic disappointment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gigantic - very large; enormous
  • Synonym: enormous - very large in size or quantity
  • Synonym: huge - extremely large
  • Antonym: tiny - very small

Etymology

From the Russian 'mamont', referring originally to the prehistoric animal, later used figuratively for size.
