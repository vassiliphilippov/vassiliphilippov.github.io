Limulus

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪmjʊləs / (AmE), / ˈlɪmjʊləs / (BrE)
A genus of arthropods known as horseshoe crabs, including the living species Limulus polyphemus.
genus of horseshoe crabs

Examples

The American Limulus is often studied for its unique blue blood.
Fossil records show that Limulus has changed little over millions of years.
Researchers use Limulus amoebocyte lysate to test for bacterial contamination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horseshoe crab - large marine arthropod with a hard, domed shell, related to Limulus

Etymology

From Latin Limulus, meaning 'somewhat oblique, sidelong', used as a genus name in zoology.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪmjʊləs / (AmE), / ˈlɪmjʊləs / (BrE)
A genus of horseshoe crabs, type genus of the family Limulidae.
genus of horseshoe crabs

Examples

Scientists often study the blood of Limulus for its unique clotting properties.
The fossil record reveals that the genus Limulus has existed for hundreds of millions of years.
Several species within the genus Limulus are found along the Atlantic coast of North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horseshoe crab - A marine arthropod of the order Xiphosura, including species in the genus Limulus

Etymology

From Latin *Limulus*, meaning 'somewhat oblique or askew', used as a genus name in taxonomy.
