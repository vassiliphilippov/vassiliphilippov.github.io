metronidazole

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtroʊˈnaɪdəˌzoʊl / (AmE), / ˌmɛtrəʊˈnɪdəzəʊl / (BrE)
An antibiotic and antiprotozoal medication used to treat infections such as trichomoniasis, giardiasis, and certain bacterial infections.
antibiotic and antiprotozoal medication
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed metronidazole to treat her bacterial infection.
Patients with giardiasis often require a course of metronidazole.
It is important to avoid alcohol while taking metronidazole due to potential side effects.

Etymology

From 'metro-' (possibly referring to the uterus, initially used for gynecological infections) + 'nitro' (nitro group in chemistry) + 'imidazole' (chemical name of the core structure).
