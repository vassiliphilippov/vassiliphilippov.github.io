cinnabar

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪn.əˌbɑr / (AmE), / ˈsɪn.əˌbɑː / (BrE)
a large red-and-black European moth (Tyria jacobaeae), whose larvae feed on ragwort leaves, and which has been introduced to control ragwort.
a red-and-black European moth

Examples

The cinnabar is easily recognized by its striking red and black wings.
Farmers released thousands of cinnabar moths to help control ragwort in the fields.
A cinnabar moth larva can be seen feeding on the leaves of a ragwort plant.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɪn.əˌbɑr / (AmE), / ˈsɪn.əˌbɑː / (BrE)
of a vivid red to reddish-orange color, resembling the mineral cinnabar.
vivid red to reddish-orange in color

Examples

A scarf of cinnabar silk drew every eye in the room.
The artist used a cinnabar hue to suggest intensity and warmth in his painting.
Her lips were painted a striking cinnabar shade for the evening gala.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vermilion - bright red to reddish-orange color

Etymology

From Latin *cinnabaris*, referring to the mineral with this distinct color.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɪnəˌbɑr / (AmE), / ˈsɪnəbɑː / (BrE)
a heavy, bright red mineral (mercuric sulfide) that is the main ore of mercury
red mineral, ore of mercury

Examples

The miners extracted cinnabar from deep within the mountain.
Pure cinnabar is toxic due to its high mercury content.
Artists once used ground cinnabar to create vivid red pigments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vermilion - a bright red pigment made from cinnabar

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *cinnabaris*, from Greek *kinnabari*, of Oriental origin.
