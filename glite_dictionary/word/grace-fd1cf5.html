grace

1. NOUN

/ ɡreɪs / (AmE), / ɡreɪs / (BrE)
polite and thoughtful behavior that shows respect and consideration for others, especially in difficult or challenging situations
polite and considerate behavior

Examples

Even under criticism, she responded with remarkable grace and dignity.
He accepted the disappointing news with good grace.
Displaying grace under pressure sets a positive example for others.

Synset Examples

a place where the company of others must be accepted with good grace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courtesy - polite behavior; respectful and considerate acts
  • Antonym: rudeness - lack of respect or consideration for others

Etymology

From Latin 'gratia' (favor, charm, thanks), via Old French 'grace'.

2. NOUN

/ ɡreɪs / (AmE), / ɡreɪs / (BrE)
an additional period of time allowed after a deadline to fulfill an obligation without penalty

Examples

The bank offered a three-month grace after the loan's due date due to hardship.
Her utility company granted her a grace so she could pay her bill later.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grace period - a period of extra time allowed after the deadline for fulfilling an obligation

4. NOUN

/ ɡreɪs / (AmE), / ɡreɪs / (BrE)
(in Christian theology) the free and unearned favor or kindness granted by God to humans, especially as manifested in the salvation of sinners.

Examples

She thanked God for the grace that helped her through difficult times.
The concept of divine grace is central to many Christian doctrines.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: mercy - compassion or forgiveness shown, especially by God in religion
