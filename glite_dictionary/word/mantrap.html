mantrap

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænˌtræp / (AmE), / ˈmæn.træp / (BrE)
a device designed to catch or hold people who trespass on property
trap for catching trespassers

Examples

The abandoned estate still had an old mantrap hidden near the entrance.
Trespassers were warned that a mantrap was set in the woods.
He narrowly avoided injury after stumbling upon a rusty mantrap on the property.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trap - a device for catching or holding animals or people
  • Synonym: snare - a device for catching animals or people, typically with a noose

Etymology

From 'man' + 'trap', combining the words for human and a device for catching.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmænˌtræp / (AmE), / ˈmæn.træp / (BrE)
a very attractive or seductive woman, especially one regarded as likely to ensnare or manipulate men
very attractive or seductive woman
informal

Examples

The media dubbed her a mantrap after seeing how many men were drawn to her.
Rumors spread that the nightclub singer was a notorious mantrap.
He warned his friend to stay away from her, calling her a real mantrap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: femme fatale - an attractive and seductive woman, especially one who ultimately brings disaster to a man who becomes involved with her
