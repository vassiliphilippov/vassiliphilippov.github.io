aflame

1. ADJ

/ əˈfleɪm / (AmE), / əˈfleɪm / (BrE)
feeling or showing intense excitement or passion; burning with eagerness or desire
intensely excited or passionate

Examples

Her cheeks were aflame with excitement as she received the award.
He was aflame with desire after their brief meeting.
The crowd stood aflame with anticipation before the big announcement.

Synset Examples

his face all ablaze with excitement
he was aflame with desire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: afire - on fire; filled with passion or emotion
  • Synonym: aroused - awakened or excited, especially sexually or emotionally

Etymology

From Middle English, formed by the prefix 'a-' (in, on) + 'flame' (fire, passion).

2. ADJ

/ əˈfleɪm / (AmE), / əˈfleɪm / (BrE)
burning with fire or glowing with intense heat, color, or emotion
burning or glowing with fire or emotion

Examples

The old barn was soon aflame after the lightning strike.
Her cheeks were aflame with embarrassment when she made the mistake.
The city skyline was aflame with the colors of the setting sun.

Synset Examples

forests set ablaze (or afire) by lightning
even the car's tires were aflame
a night aflare with fireworks
candles alight on the tables
houses on fire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ablaze - burning fiercely; on fire
  • Synonym: afire - on fire; burning
  • Synonym: alight - burning; on fire; lit up

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'a-' (on, in) + 'flame'.

Source: WordNet
