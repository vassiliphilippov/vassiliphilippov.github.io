Angra Mainyu

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑːŋɡrə maɪˈnjuː / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡrə ˈmaɪnjuː / (BrE)
The spirit of evil and chaos in Zoroastrianism, regarded as the cosmic arch-enemy of Ormazd (Ahura Mazda).
evil spirit in Zoroastrianism

Examples

Many Zoroastrian texts describe Angra Mainyu as the eternal adversary of Ahura Mazda.
According to the faith, Angra Mainyu represents all that is evil and destructive in the world.
The dualistic cosmology of Zoroastrianism centers on the struggle between Angra Mainyu and Ormazd.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Ahura Mazda - the supreme god of Zoroastrianism, representing good

Etymology

From Avestan 'Angra Mainyu' meaning 'destructive spirit'.
