Arapahoe

1. NOUN

/ əˈræpəˌhoʊ / (AmE), / əˈræpəhəʊ / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people originally inhabiting eastern Colorado and Wyoming, now mainly living in Oklahoma and Wyoming.
member of a Native American people

Examples

The Arapahoe traditionally lived in the plains of Colorado and Wyoming.
The museum features artifacts from the Arapahoe and other Plains tribes.
Many Arapahoe now reside in communities in Oklahoma and Wyoming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arapaho - Alternative spelling for Arapahoe, member of the same Native American people

Etymology

From the name of the tribe in their own language, possibly via French.

2. NOUN

/ ˌær.əˈpoʊ / (AmE), / ˌær.əˈpəʊ / (BrE)
the Algonquian language traditionally spoken by the Arapaho people of the western United States
Algonquian language of the Arapaho people

Examples

The Arapahoe language has been spoken for centuries by the indigenous Arapaho people.
She is studying Arapahoe to help preserve Native American languages.
Few fluent speakers of Arapahoe remain, so revitalization efforts are important.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arapaho - member of the Arapaho people

Etymology

From the name of the Arapaho people, an indigenous group of the Great Plains.
