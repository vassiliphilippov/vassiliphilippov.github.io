connective

1. ADJ

/ kəˈnɛktɪv / (AmE), / kəˈnɛktɪv / (BrE)
serving to connect or link things together
serving to connect or link

Examples

The connective passages in the play helped the audience follow the story.
Certain phrases act as connective elements between paragraphs in academic writing.
In the body, connective tissue supports and binds other tissues together.

Synset Examples

connective remarks between chapters
connective tissue in animals
conjunctive tissue in plants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conjunctive - serving to join; connective

Etymology

From Latin 'connectivus', from 'connectere' (to bind together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00566245-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ kəˈnɛktɪv / (AmE), / kəˈnɛktɪv / (BrE)
a device or means that serves to connect things together, especially in a technical or mechanical context
device or means that connects things

Examples

The technician used a special connective to link the two cables securely.
Without the proper connective, the circuit will not function correctly.
She searched her toolbox for a suitable connective for the water pipes.

Synset Examples

he soldered the connection
he didn't have the right connector between the amplifier and the speakers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connector - a device for joining electrical circuits together

Etymology

From Latin conectere (to bind together), via connect + -ive.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03095830-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ kəˈnɛktɪv / (AmE), / kəˈnɛktɪv / (BrE)
a word, especially a conjunction or other function word, used to link words, phrases, clauses, or sentences
a word that joins parts of sentences

Examples

In the sentence "She likes tea and coffee," the word "and" acts as a connective.
Writers often rely on connective words like "however" or "therefore" to improve coherence.
The teacher explained that "because" is a connective that introduces a reason.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conjunction - a word used to connect clauses or sentences

Etymology

From Latin connectivus (joining together), from connectere (to bind together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06336819-n
Synset POS: n
