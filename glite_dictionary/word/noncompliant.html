noncompliant

1. NOUN

/ nɑn.kəmˈplaɪ.ənt / (AmE), / nɒn.kəmˈplaɪ.ənt / (BrE)
a person who refuses to obey rules or follow prescribed instructions, especially in medical or legal contexts.
person who refuses to comply

Examples

The doctor noted in the chart that the patient was noncompliant with the prescribed medication regimen.
Schools sometimes face challenges when dealing with noncompliant students who disrupt the learning environment.
The new policy aims to improve follow-up with noncompliant individuals who miss their appointments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defiant - openly resisting authority or rules

Etymology

Formed from 'non-' (not) and 'compliant' (willing to comply).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09853385-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˌnɑn.kəmˈplaɪ.ənt / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.kəmˈplaɪ.ənt / (BrE)
refusing or failing to obey rules, regulations, or requests; not compliant
not obeying rules or authority

Examples

The school has a strict dress code, and students who are noncompliant may receive detention.
Many patients were noncompliant with their medication schedules, leading to complications.
Her refusal to follow the team's instructions was seen as noncompliant behavior.

Synset Examples

brought up to be aggressive and defiant
a defiant attitude

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compliant - willing to comply with rules or requests

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' (not) + 'compliant' (yielding, obedient).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00698972-a
Synset POS: a
