Serpens

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɜr.pənz / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.pənz / (BrE)
a constellation in the equatorial region of the Northern Hemisphere, located near Ophiuchus and Corona Borealis
a constellation in the Northern Hemisphere

Examples

The constellation Serpens stretches across the sky near Ophiuchus each summer night.
Ancient astronomers named Serpens for its resemblance to a serpent.
Many amateur stargazers enjoy trying to identify Serpens among the stars.

Etymology

From Latin 'serpens' (serpent), referring to the constellation's snake-like shape.
