tailing

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈteɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of following someone secretly, typically to observe their movements or gather information
act of secretly following someone

Examples

The detective spent hours tailing the suspect through the crowded streets.
There are strict rules police must follow when tailing someone without their knowledge.
He suspected that he was under tailing by private investigators hired by his rival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surveillance - close observation of a person or group, especially by police

Etymology

From the verb 'tail' (to follow closely), itself probably from Old French 'tailler' (to cut, hence to follow closely behind).
