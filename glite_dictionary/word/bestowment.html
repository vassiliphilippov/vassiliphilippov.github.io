bestowment

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈstoʊmənt / (AmE), / bɪˈstəʊmənt / (BrE)
the act of conferring an honor, award, or gift upon someone
the act of giving an honor or gift

Examples

The bestowment of the award was met with enthusiastic applause.
She felt honored by the bestowment of such a rare distinction.
His speech preceded the bestowment of gifts to each student.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conferral - the act of formally giving an honor or title

Etymology

From the verb 'bestow' + -ment, modeled after similar noun formations.

2. NOUN

/ bəˈstoʊmənt / (AmE), / bɪˈstəʊmənt / (BrE)
the act of giving or presenting something, or the thing that is given
the act of giving or a gift

Examples

The bestowment of the scholarship changed her life and opened many opportunities.
His speech acknowledged the bestowment of honors upon the distinguished guests.
She treasured the bestowment as a symbol of her grandmother's love.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gift - a thing given willingly to someone without payment; a present
  • Synonym: award - a prize or other mark of recognition given in honor of an achievement
  • Antonym: withdrawal - the act of taking something away

Etymology

From the verb 'bestow' + '-ment', patterned after other nouns of action.

Synset POS: n
