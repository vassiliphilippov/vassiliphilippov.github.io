indignant

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdɪɡ.nənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɪɡ.nənt / (BrE)
feeling or showing anger or annoyance at what is perceived as unfair treatment
feeling anger at unfairness

Examples

She was indignant when she learned she had been unfairly blamed for the problem.
Many citizens grew indignant at the corruption revealed in the local government.
His indignant response showed how deeply he felt the injustice.

Synset Examples

an indignant denial
incensed at the judges' unfairness
a look of outraged disbelief
umbrageous at the loss of their territory

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: outraged - extremely angry or shocked at something unjust
  • Degree Lower: irritated - slightly angry or annoyed
  • Synonym: resentful - feeling bitterness or indignation at having been treated unfairly

Etymology

From Latin indignans, present participle of indignari (to consider as unworthy), from in- (not) + dignus (worthy).
