lidar

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪˌdɑr / (AmE), / ˈliːdɑː / (BrE)
a system for measuring distances or detecting objects using laser light, similar to radar but using optical wavelengths
laser-based distance and object detection system

Examples

The autonomous vehicle used lidar to map its surroundings and avoid obstacles on the road.
Scientists are using lidar to study changes in forest canopy structure over time.
Aviation safety experts are exploring lidar as a tool to detect air turbulence ahead of aircraft.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radar - a system for detecting the presence, direction, distance, and speed of objects using radio waves

Etymology

Acronym from 'light detection and ranging', modeled after 'radar'.
