taper

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪpɚ / (AmE), / ˈteɪpə / (BrE)
a shape that gradually becomes narrower toward one end or to a point
shape narrowing to a point

Examples

The candle has a slender taper that ends in a delicate flame.
Artists often draw leaves with a gentle taper at the tip.
The tower’s taper gives it a sleek, modern appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cone - a three-dimensional geometric shape narrowing to a point

Etymology

From Old French *taper* (to taper), from Latin *tapēre* (to diminish, become thin).

2. NOUN

/ ˈteɪ.pər / (AmE), / ˈteɪ.pə / (BrE)
the property of gradually becoming narrower toward one end, as in a wedge or cone
gradual narrowing toward a point

Examples

The taper of the mountain peak made it easy to distinguish against the sky.
Engineers considered the taper of the new bridge supports for better wind resistance.
The candle's gentle taper allowed it to fit perfectly into the holder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cone - a three-dimensional geometric shape that narrows to a point

Etymology

From Old French *taper* (to taper), from Latin *tāpūria* (wick, small candle).

3. VERB

/ ˈteɪpər / (AmE), / ˈteɪpə / (BrE)
to make or become gradually narrower at one end, often coming to a point
become or make gradually narrower

Examples

The craftsman used a knife to taper the end of the wooden pole.
These jeans taper towards the ankle for a slimmer fit.
She tapered the candle so it would fit into the holder.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: narrow - become or make less wide

Etymology

From Old French *taper*, from *tape* (a taper, candle) from Latin *taeda* (pine torch).

4. NOUN

/ ˈteɪpər / (AmE), / ˈteɪpə / (BrE)
a slender candle, typically consisting of a stick of wax with a wick in the middle, used for lighting
slender candle with a wick

Examples

She lit a taper and placed it carefully on the windowsill.
In the dim library, only a single taper provided light for reading.
He used a long taper to ignite the fire in the old fireplace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candle - a block of wax with a central wick for burning

Etymology

From Old French taper (candle), from Latin 'papyrus' (wick or torch made from papyrus).

5. VERB

/ ˈteɪpɚ / (AmE), / ˈteɪpə / (BrE)
to become gradually smaller, fewer, or less; to decrease gradually in size, amount, or intensity
diminish or become smaller gradually

Examples

Attendance at the event began to taper as the evening wore on.
Her interest in the project gradually tapered over the months.
The pain should taper off after you start the new medication.

Synset Examples

Interest tapered off

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to diminish - to decrease or make smaller or less
  • Antonym: increase - to become or make greater in size, amount, or degree

Etymology

From Middle English 'tapere', from Old French 'taper', meaning 'to taper, diminish', likely from Latin 'taper' meaning 'to reduce'.
