connexion

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / kəˈnɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
A relationship or link between things, people, or events; an instance of things being connected.
relationship or link between things
british archaic

Examples

There is no connexion between the two incidents in the report.
He struggled to find any connexion with his family's past.
Some scholars prefer the spelling 'connexion' in academic writing.

Synset Examples

there was a connection between eating that pickle and having that nightmare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connection - relationship or link between things

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'connection', from Latin 'connexio' (a joining together), through French and English.

british

Related Terms

3. NOUN

/ kəˈnɛkʃən / (AmE), / kəˈnɛkʃən / (BrE)
A device or part that joins two or more things together; a connector.
device that joins things; connector
british

Examples

The electrician installed a new connexion between the two wires to ensure the circuit was complete.
If the connexion is loose, the signal may become unreliable or noisy.
Older British texts often use the word connexion where modern usage prefers 'connection.'

Synset Examples

Related Terms
