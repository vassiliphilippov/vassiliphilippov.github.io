Stereospondyli

1. NOUN

/ ˌstɛri.oʊˈspɑndi.laɪ / (AmE), / ˌstɪəri.əʊˈspɒndɪlaɪ / (BrE)
An extinct group of amphibians with vertebrae whose component elements are fused into a single piece, formerly classified as a suborder of Stegocephalia.
extinct amphibian group with fused vertebrae

Examples

Fossils of Stereospondyli have provided valuable insights into the evolution of early amphibians.
Scientists debated whether Stereospondyli should remain a distinct suborder within Stegocephalia.
The unique structure of Stereospondyli vertebrae distinguishes them from other prehistoric amphibians.

Etymology

From Greek stereos (solid) + spondylos (vertebra).
