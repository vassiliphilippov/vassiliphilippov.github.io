to bewail

1. VERB

/ bɪˈweɪl / (AmE), / bɪˈweɪl / (BrE)
to express great sorrow or regret, often by wailing or lamenting
express deep sorrow or regret

Examples

She continued to bewail the loss of her childhood home.
The old man would bewail his missed opportunities late into the night.
Many citizens publicly bewailed the government's decision to cut social programs.

Synset Examples

I deplore this hostile action
we lamented the loss of benefits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lament - to express grief or sorrow, often openly
  • Synonym: to deplore - to feel or express strong disapproval of; to regret deeply

Etymology

From Middle English bewaillen, from be- (intensive) + wail (to lament).
