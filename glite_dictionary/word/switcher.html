switcher

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɪtʃər / (AmE), / ˈswɪtʃə / (BrE)
a person who administers punishment by using a switch or whip
person who punishes with a switch or whip

Examples

The overseer was known as a switcher who kept order through fear.
Old plantation narratives sometimes referred to the cruel switcher who enforced discipline.
He earned a reputation as the switcher, punishing those who broke the rules.
