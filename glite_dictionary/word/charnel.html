charnel

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɑːrnəl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːnəl / (BrE)
gruesomely suggestive of death or decay, especially resembling or associated with a place where bodies or bones are kept; macabre or deathlike.
suggestive of death or decay

Examples

A charnel odor lingered in the abandoned crypt.
The novel's setting is a charnel landscape, filled with reminders of war and death.
He recoiled from the charnel scene of devastation after the disaster.

Synset Examples

a charnel smell came from the chest filled with dead men's bones
ghastly shrieks
the sepulchral darkness of the catacombs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sepulchral - suggestive of a tomb; gloomy or dismal
  • Synonym: ghastly - causing great horror or fear; macabre

Etymology

From Old French *charnel* (of the flesh), from Medieval Latin *carnāle*, from Latin *caro* (flesh).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑrnəl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːnəl / (BrE)
A building or vault where corpses or bones are stored, often associated with historical or religious sites.
building for storing bones or corpses

Examples

The ancient monastery contained a charnel beneath the chapel, holding centuries-old skeletons.
Tourists visited the charnel to learn about burial practices of the medieval community.
Deep in the forest, they discovered a forgotten charnel filled with human bones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ossuary - a container or room into which the bones of dead people are placed

Etymology

From Old French *charnel*, from Late Latin *carnāle* (place for flesh), from Latin *caro* (flesh).
