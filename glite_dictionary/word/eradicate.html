to eradicate

1. VERB

/ ɪˈrædɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈrædɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to destroy or remove something completely, especially something harmful
destroy or eliminate completely

Examples

Scientists hope to eradicate the disease from the country within ten years.
The organization launched a campaign to eradicate illiteracy in rural areas.
We must act quickly to eradicate the invasive species before it spreads further.

Synset Examples

the plague wiped out an entire population

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to eliminate - remove or get rid of completely
  • Synonym: to wipe out - destroy or remove completely
  • Synonym: to exterminate - destroy completely, especially a group of living things

Etymology

From Latin eradicare (to root out), from radix (root).

2. VERB

/ əˈrædɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈrædɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to destroy or remove something completely, especially something bad
destroy or remove completely

Examples

The government launched a campaign to eradicate illiteracy within the next decade.
Scientists are working to eradicate diseases such as malaria and polio from the world.
New regulations aim to eradicate corruption in public office.

Synset Examples

the vestiges of political democracy were soon uprooted
root out corruption

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to eliminate - remove or get rid of, especially something unwanted
  • Synonym: to exterminate - destroy completely, especially pests or enemies
  • Antonym: preserve - keep something in its original or existing state

Etymology

From Latin eradicare (to root out), from e- (out) + radix (root).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01666012-v
Synset POS: v
