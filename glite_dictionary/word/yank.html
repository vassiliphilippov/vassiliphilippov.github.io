Yank

1. NOUN

/ jæŋk / (AmE), / jaŋk / (BrE)
(chiefly informal) a person from the United States; an American, especially as referred to by non-Americans.
an American person (informal)
informal

Examples

During the soccer match, the British fans chanted at the visiting Yank.
As a Yank living in Paris, she quickly learned to adapt to local customs.
The group of Yanks stood out because of their accents and enthusiasm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yankee - an American, especially a person from the northern United States

Etymology

Shortened form of 'Yankee', origin uncertain but possibly from Dutch 'Janke', diminutive of Jan (John).

2. NOUN

/ jæŋk / (AmE), / jæŋk / (BrE)
(historical, especially US) A person from the northern states of the United States, particularly during the American Civil War.
person from northern US (esp. Civil War period)
american

Examples

During the Civil War, a Yank might be found fighting for the Union Army.
He was proud to tell stories about his great-grandfather, who was a Yank from New York.
The southern soldiers often referred to their northern adversaries as Yanks in letters home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yankee - an American from the northern US, especially during the Civil War
  • Antonym: Rebel - a person from the southern US during the American Civil War

Etymology

Shortened form of 'Yankee', of uncertain origin, possibly from Dutch 'Janke', diminutive of Jan (John).
