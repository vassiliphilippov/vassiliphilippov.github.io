to yank

1. VERB

/ jæŋk / (AmE), / jæŋk / (BrE)
to pull or move something with a sudden, forceful movement
pull suddenly and forcefully

Examples

She had to yank the drawer open because it was stuck.
He yanked the headphones out of his bag and put them on.
Don't yank the cord or you might break it.

Synset Examples

He turned the handle and jerked the door open

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jerk - to pull or move with a quick, sharp motion
  • Synonym: tug - to pull something with a sharp or strong movement

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, attested from the late 19th century.
