anthropolatry

1. NOUN

/ ˌænθrəˈpɑlətri / (AmE), / ˌænθrəˈpɒlətri / (BrE)
the worship of human beings as deities or objects of reverence
worship of human beings

Examples

Throughout history, anthropolatry has arisen in cultures where rulers demand divine status.
The cult's anthropolatry was condemned by mainstream religious authorities.
Debates about anthropolatry often reference the deification of pharaohs in ancient Egypt.

Etymology

From Greek anthrōpos (human) + -latreia (worship).
