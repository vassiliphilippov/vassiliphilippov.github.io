amyotonia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæm.ioʊˈtoʊni.ə / (AmE), / ˌæm.i.əˈtəʊ.ni.ə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which there is a lack of normal muscular tension or tone.
lack of normal muscular tension
medical

Examples

The child was diagnosed with amyotonia after exhibiting very weak muscle resistance.
Doctors observed clear signs of amyotonia in the patient's limbs during the neurological exam.
Amyotonia can be a symptom of various neuromuscular disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypotonia - abnormally low muscle tone
  • Antonym: muscle tone - the normal tension or resistance to movement in muscles

Etymology

From Greek a- (without) + myo- (muscle) + tonos (tone).
