to incise

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈsaɪz / (AmE), / ɪnˈsaɪz / (BrE)
to cut or carve into a surface, especially with a sharp instrument
to cut or carve into a surface

Examples

The surgeon had to incise the patient's abdomen to remove the appendix.
The artist will incise delicate patterns into the metal plate.
Carefully incise a line along the top of the pastry to create a decorative design.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut - to make an opening or wound in a surface with a sharp-edged tool

Etymology

From Latin 'incidere' (to cut into), from 'in-' (into) + 'caedere' (to cut).
