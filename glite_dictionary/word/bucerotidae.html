Bucerotidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌbjuːsəˈrɑːtəˌdi / (AmE), / ˌbjuːsəˈrɒtɪdiː / (BrE)
The family of large tropical birds known as hornbills, characterized by long, down-curved bills often with a casque on the upper mandible.
family of birds including hornbills
medical

Examples

The Bucerotidae family is known for birds with unique bills and elaborate casques.
Scientists studied the nesting habits of several Bucerotidae species in Southeast Asia.
Conservation efforts have been launched to protect endangered members of the Bucerotidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hornbill - a tropical bird with a large curved bill, often with a hornlike casque

Etymology

From modern Latin Bucerotidae, from Greek boukerōtos 'horned like an ox' (bous 'ox' + keras, kerat- 'horn').

2. NOUN

/ ˌbjuːsəˌroʊˈtɪdi / (AmE), / ˌbjuːsərəˈtɪːdiː / (BrE)
A family of birds known as hornbills, characterized by their long, curved bills and often large casque on the upper mandible.
family of birds: hornbills

Examples

The Bucerotidae are easily recognized by their large, curved bills and prominent casques.
Many species within the Bucerotidae family are found in the forests of Africa and Asia.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect endangered members of the Bucerotidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hornbill - a bird with a long curved bill from the family Bucerotidae

Etymology

From scientific Latin Bucerotidae, based on the type genus Buceros, from Greek boukerōs (with cow horns).
