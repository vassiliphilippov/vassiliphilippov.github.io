to overcompensate

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈkɒmpənˌseɪt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈkɒmpənseɪt / (BrE)
to try to make up for a weakness or deficiency by exaggerating other qualities or behaviors, often to an excessive degree
to excessively make up for a weakness

Examples

After losing the game, he tried to overcompensate by bragging about his achievements.
When people feel insecure, they may overcompensate by acting overly confident.
She tends to overcompensate for her shyness by being excessively talkative.

Synset Examples

he is compensating for being a bad father

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: to compensate - to make up for something undesirable by counterbalancing it

Etymology

From 'over-' (excessively) + 'compensate' (from Latin compensare, to counterbalance).

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈkɑːmpənˌseɪt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈkɒmpənˌseɪt / (BrE)
to take excessive action in an attempt to correct or counterbalance a perceived problem or mistake, often resulting in an overcorrection
make excessive correction or response

Examples

Sometimes people overcompensate for their insecurities by acting overly confident.
He tends to overcompensate for small errors by making unnecessary changes.
The manager worried that she might overcompensate and impose too many rules after the mistake.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: to compensate - to make up for something, especially a loss or disadvantage

Etymology

From over- (excessive) + compensate (from Latin compensare ‘weigh one thing against another’).

Synset POS: v
