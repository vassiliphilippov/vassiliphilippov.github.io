Crocodylus porosus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkroʊkəˈdaɪləs pəˈroʊsəs / (AmE), / ˌkrɒkəˈdaɪləs pəˈrəʊsəs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the estuarine crocodile, a large species found in eastern Asia and Pacific islands.
scientific name for estuarine crocodile

Examples

The Crocodylus porosus is the largest living species of crocodile.
Researchers tagged several Crocodylus porosus individuals to study their migration patterns.
The habitat of Crocodylus porosus extends from eastern India to northern Australia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estuarine crocodile - large crocodile species inhabiting estuaries and coastal regions
  • Synonym: saltwater crocodile - common name for Crocodylus porosus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Crocodylus' (crocodile), 'porosus' (porous, referencing skin texture).
