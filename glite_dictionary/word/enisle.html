to enisle

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈaɪl / (AmE), / ɪnˈaɪl / (BrE)
to isolate someone or oneself, as though turning into or placing on an island; to make separate from others
to isolate; make into an island

Examples

After moving to the cabin in the woods, he chose to enisle himself from society.
She sometimes needs to enisle herself to recharge after busy weeks.
The monk decided to enisle himself for meditation and reflection.

Synset Examples

She felt the urge to enisle after a long day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to isolate - to set apart or separate from others

Etymology

From English prefix 'en-' (make into) + 'isle' (island), after French 'enîler'.
