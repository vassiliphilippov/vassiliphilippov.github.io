diazo

1. ADJ

/ ˈdaɪˌæzoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʌɪəzəʊ / (BrE)
relating to or containing a diazonium group or compound, particularly in chemistry.
containing a diazonium group (chemistry)

Examples

The diazo process was once commonly used in the reproduction of engineering drawings.
Chemists often study diazo compounds for their unique reactivity.
A diazo group can be detected using specific reagents during analysis.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: azo - relating to or containing the divalent group —N=N—

Etymology

From French diazo-, derived from 'diazonium,' which is based on Greek 'dia' (through, across) and 'azote' (nitrogen).
