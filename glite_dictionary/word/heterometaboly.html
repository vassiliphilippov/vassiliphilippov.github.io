heterometaboly

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛt̬əroʊˈmɛtəˌboʊli / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəʊˈmɛtəbəli / (BrE)
Development in insects with incomplete metamorphosis, where there is no pupal stage before reaching maturity.
incomplete metamorphosis in insects (no pupa)
medical

Examples

Grasshoppers undergo heterometaboly, lacking a pupal stage in their development.
The process of heterometaboly results in insect nymphs resembling miniature adults.
In contrast to butterflies, cockroaches experience heterometaboly during their life cycles.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: holometaboly - development with complete metamorphosis, including a pupal stage

Etymology

From Greek hetero- (different) + metabole (change).
