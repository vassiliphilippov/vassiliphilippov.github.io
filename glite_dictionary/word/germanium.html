germanium

1. NOUN

/ dʒɚˈmeɪniəm / (AmE), / dʒɜːˈmeɪniəm / (BrE)
a brittle, gray, crystalline chemical element (symbol Ge), a semiconducting metalloid used especially in transistors and other electronic devices
chemical element, semiconducting metalloid

Examples

The transistor is made from germanium to enhance its electrical conductivity.
High-purity germanium crystals are used in radiation detection equipment.
Researchers discovered trace amounts of germanium in certain mineral ores.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silicon - a nonmetallic chemical element with similar semiconductor properties

Etymology

From German *Germanium*, named after Germany, where it was discovered in 1886.
