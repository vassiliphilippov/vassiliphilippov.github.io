Todus

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊdəs / (AmE), / ˈtəʊdəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Todidae, comprising the small, brightly colored birds called todies, native to the Caribbean.
genus of birds called todies

Examples

The genus Todus encompasses five species found throughout the Caribbean islands.
Researchers observed the nesting behavior of Todus in Jamaica's forests.
Unlike other genera, Todus has members with vividly colored plumage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tody - a small, colorful Caribbean bird of the genus Todus

Etymology

From Latin *todus* (a kind of bird).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊdəs / (AmE), / ˈtəʊdəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Todidae, comprising small brightly colored birds known as todies, native to the Caribbean.
genus of Caribbean todies

Examples

The Todus genus is recognized for its tiny, vibrantly colored species.
Birdwatchers visit the Caribbean to observe various Todus species in their natural habitat.
Among the best-known members of Todus is the Jamaican tody.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Todidae - the family of birds to which Todus belongs

Etymology

From Latin 'todus', the classical name for an unknown small bird, now applied to this avian genus.
