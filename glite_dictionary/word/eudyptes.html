Eudyptes

1. NOUN

/ juːˈdɪp.tiːz / (AmE), / juːˈdɪp.tiːz / (BrE)
a genus of crested penguins, including the rockhopper and macaroni penguins, characterized by distinctive yellow crests above the eyes
genus of crested penguins

Examples

Several species within the Eudyptes genus are noted for their elaborate yellow crests.
Researchers are studying the breeding behaviors of Eudyptes in the sub-Antarctic islands.
The Eudyptes penguins are distinguished from other genera by their thick red bills and yellow eyebrow tufts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penguin - flightless seabird native to the Southern Hemisphere

Etymology

From Greek 'eu' (good) + 'dyptes' (diver).

2. NOUN

/ juˈdɪptiz / (AmE), / juːˈdɪptiːz / (BrE)
A genus of crested penguins found in the subantarctic regions, including rockhopper penguins.
a genus of crested penguins

Examples

The Eudyptes genus includes several species of crested penguins found on subantarctic islands.
Researchers are studying the migration patterns of Eudyptes penguins to understand climate impacts.
Members of the Eudyptes genus, such as the macaroni penguin, are known for their distinctive yellow crests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rockhopper penguin - a species of crested penguin known for hopping over rocks

Etymology

From Ancient Greek euduptēs, meaning 'good diver'.
