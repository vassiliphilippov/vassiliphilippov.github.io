Canis mesomelas

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪnɪs ˌmɛsəˈmiːləs / (AmE), / ˈkaːnɪs ˌmɛsəˈmiːləs / (BrE)
A jackal native to Africa, distinguished by its black back; also known as the black-backed jackal.
African black-backed jackal species

Examples

The Canis mesomelas is commonly seen in savannas and open grasslands of southern Africa.
Researchers observed a Canis mesomelas hunting in a small pack near the border of the reserve.
Unlike other jackals, the Canis mesomelas is recognized for the distinctive black stripe along its back.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black-backed jackal - African jackal species with a black back

Etymology

From Latin: 'Canis' meaning dog, 'mesomelas' meaning 'middle-black'.
