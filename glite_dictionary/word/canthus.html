canthus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkænθəs / (AmE), / ˈkænθəs / (BrE)
either of the corners of the eye where the upper and lower eyelids meet
corner of eye where eyelids meet
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the redness at the inner canthus of her eye.
Tears often collect at the outer canthus before rolling down the cheek.
Swelling of the canthus can be a sign of an eye infection.

Etymology

From Greek kanthos (corner of the eye).
