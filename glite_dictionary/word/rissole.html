rissole

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪsoʊl / (AmE), / ˈrɪsəʊl / (BrE)
a small, rounded ball or patty of minced cooked meat or fish, coated in egg and breadcrumbs and deep-fried or baked
ball or patty of minced meat or fish, coated and fried

Examples

My grandmother used to prepare delicious rissoles for Sunday lunch.
The restaurant serves traditional beef rissoles with mashed potatoes and gravy.
She made a batch of salmon rissoles for the picnic.

Etymology

From French 'rissole', from Latin 'russeolus', diminutive of 'russus' (red), referring to the reddish color after cooking.
