furtive

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɝːtɪv / (AmE), / ˈfɜːtɪv / (BrE)
attempting to avoid notice or attention, typically because of guilt or a belief that discovery would lead to trouble; secretive or sly.
secretive and sly

Examples

The cat cast a furtive glance at the open door before sneaking outside.
She gave him a furtive smile when no one was looking.
His furtive movements made everyone suspicious of his intentions.

Synset Examples

backstairs gossip
his low backstairs cunning
backstairs intimacies
furtive behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secretive - inclined to conceal feelings and intentions
  • Antonym: open - not secretive or hidden; frank

Etymology

From French 'furtif', from Latin 'furtivus' (stolen, secret), from 'furtum' (theft).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɝːtɪv / (AmE), / ˈfɜːtɪv / (BrE)
attempting to avoid notice or attention, typically because of guilt or a belief that discovery would lead to trouble; secretive and stealthy
secretive; attempting to avoid notice

Examples

She cast a furtive glance over her shoulder, worried she was being followed.
The student's furtive movements made the teacher suspicious of cheating.
He made a furtive attempt to slip out of the room unnoticed.

Synset Examples

a furtive manner
a sneak attack
stealthy footsteps
a surreptitious glance at his watch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stealthy - behaving or done in a cautious and secretive manner
  • Antonym: open - not secret or hidden; revealing everything
  • Synonym: secretive - inclined to conceal feelings and intentions or not to disclose information

Etymology

From Latin furtivus (stolen, secret), from furtum (theft).
