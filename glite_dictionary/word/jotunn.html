jotunn

1. NOUN

/ ˈjoʊ.tʊn / (AmE), / ˈjəʊ.tʊn / (BrE)
(in Norse mythology) a giant belonging to a race often at odds with the gods (the Aesir); also called a jotun.
mythological Norse giant

Examples

In Norse legends, the jotunn were often depicted as adversaries of the gods.
Loki himself was a jotunn before he joined the ranks of the Aesir.
The jotunn were said to dwell in the land of Jotunheim, far from human civilization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant - a being of great size and strength, often appearing in myths and folklore

Etymology

From Old Norse 'jötunn' (giant).
