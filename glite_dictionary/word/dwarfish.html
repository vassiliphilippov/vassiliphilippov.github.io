dwarfish

1. ADJ

/ ˈdwɔr.fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdwɔː.fɪʃ / (BrE)
Unusually small in size; much smaller than is typical or expected.
unusually small in size

Examples

The dwarfish shrubs barely reached his knees in the garden.
Some breeds are intentionally raised to remain dwarfish for companionship in small homes.
The artist captured the vastness of the mountains by painting a dwarfish cottage at their base.

Synset Examples

dwarf tree
toy or dwarfish dogs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tiny - very small in size
  • Antonym: giant - very large in size

Etymology

From 'dwarf' + '-ish'; 'dwarf' from Old English 'dweorg' or 'dweorh', meaning 'small person or creature'.
