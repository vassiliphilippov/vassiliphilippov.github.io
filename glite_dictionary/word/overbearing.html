overbearing

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvərˈberɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbeərɪŋ / (BrE)
unpleasantly or arrogantly domineering; showing an attitude of superiority and lack of respect for others
arrogantly domineering or bossy

Examples

Her overbearing attitude made it difficult for others to express their opinions at work.
The coach's overbearing manner discouraged many players from speaking up.
He found his boss increasingly overbearing as deadlines approached.

Synset Examples

some economists are disdainful of their colleagues in other social disciplines
haughty aristocrats
his lordly manners were offensive
walked with a prideful swagger
very sniffy about breaches of etiquette
his mother eyed my clothes with a supercilious air
a more swaggering mood than usual

Related Terms

  • Synonym: domineering - asserting one's will over others in an arrogant way
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance

Etymology

From over- (excessively) + bear (to carry, to behave); sense of 'domineering' dates from early 17th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvərˈbɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbeərɪŋ / (BrE)
unpleasantly or arrogantly domineering; expecting unquestioning obedience or control over others
domineering; arrogantly controlling

Examples

Her overbearing attitude made her colleagues feel uncomfortable in the office.
He had an overbearing manner that pushed everyone away from him.
The coach's overbearing instructions left no room for the players' ideas.

Synset Examples

the timid child of authoritarian parents
insufferably overbearing behavior toward the waiter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authoritarian - favoring or enforcing strict obedience to authority
  • Antonym: submissive - ready to conform to the authority or will of others
  • Synonym: domineering - asserting one’s will over another in an arrogant way

Etymology

From 'over-' (excessive) + 'bearing' (behavior, from Old English 'beran' meaning to bear or carry).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00790912-s
Synset POS: s
