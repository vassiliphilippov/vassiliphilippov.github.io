deaf

1. NOUN

/ dɛf / (AmE), / dɛf / (BrE)
people who have a severe hearing impairment, especially those who cannot hear at all or have great difficulty hearing
people with severe hearing impairment

Examples

Many of the deaf prefer to use sign language to communicate.
There are special schools designed to meet the needs of the deaf.
Technology has greatly improved accessibility for the deaf in recent years.

Synset Examples

many of the deaf use sign language

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hearing-impaired - having reduced or limited ability to hear

Etymology

From Old English 'dēaf' (lacking the power of hearing), of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07962535-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ dɛf / (AmE), / dɛf / (BrE)
lacking or deprived of the ability to hear wholly or partly
unable to hear partly or wholly

Examples

He was born deaf and learned to communicate using sign language.
Many deaf students attend specialized schools for their education.
After the accident, she became partially deaf in one ear.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hearing - able to hear, not deaf
  • Degree Lower: hard of hearing - having partial hearing loss

Etymology

Old English *dēaf*, related to Dutch *doof* and German *taub*, meaning 'deaf'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00685207-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ dɛf / (AmE), / dɛf / (BrE)
Unwilling or refusing to listen or pay attention to something; unresponsive, especially used with 'to'.
unwilling to listen or pay attention

Examples

He remained deaf to all their pleas and continued with his plan.
The committee was deaf to calls for a public inquiry.
She pleaded with him, but he was deaf to her warnings.

Synset Examples

deaf to her warnings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unresponsive - not reacting to something; not giving a response
  • Antonym: receptive - willing to listen or accept new ideas
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01197871-s
Synset POS: s

4. VERB

/ dɛf / (AmE), / dɛf / (BrE)
(of a loud noise or other cause) to make someone unable to hear, either temporarily or permanently.
to make unable to hear

Examples

The explosion was so loud it nearly deafed him for life.
Construction work outside her window almost deafed her during the day.
Repeated exposure to loud music can eventually deaf a person.

Synset Examples

a deafening noise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deafen - to make someone unable to hear, especially temporarily, because of a loud noise

Etymology

From Old English 'dēaf' (deprived of the power of hearing).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02194386-v
Synset POS: v
