rustiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌstinɪs / (AmE), / ˈrʌstinɪs / (BrE)
the condition or quality of being coated, covered, or affected by rust
state of being rusty or covered in rust

Examples

The old tools had a noticeable rustiness that made them difficult to use.
Years of neglect led to the rustiness of the garden gate's hinges.
The bike's rustiness was obvious after it was left out in the rain all winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rusty - covered with or affected by rust

Etymology

From 'rusty' + '-ness', formed in English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14602910-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈrʌsti.nəs / (AmE), / ˈrʌsti.nɪs / (BrE)
lack of skill or ability, especially due to not practicing or having not done something for a long time
lack of skill from disuse or inexperience

Examples

After years away from the piano, his rustiness was obvious during the recital.
Some rustiness is to be expected when returning to a sport after a long break.
Her rustiness in speaking French disappeared after a few weeks in Paris.

Synset Examples

his rustiness showed when he was asked to speed up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ineptitude - lack of skill or ability
  • Synonym: awkwardness - lack of grace or ease; clumsiness
  • Antonym: proficiency - a high degree of skill; competence

Etymology

From 'rusty' (covered with rust; impaired by lack of practice) + '-ness'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05656601-n
Synset POS: n
