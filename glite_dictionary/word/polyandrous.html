polyandrous

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɑːliˈændrəs / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈændrəs / (BrE)
(of a woman or female animal) having more than one husband or mate at the same time.
having multiple husbands or mates

Examples

The tribe was known for its polyandrous marriage arrangements, where one woman had several husbands.
Certain species of shorebirds are polyandrous, with females mating with multiple males each season.
In some cultures, polyandrous unions were practiced to keep property within the family.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monandrous - having only one husband or mate at a time

Etymology

From Greek polyandros (with many men), from poly- (many) + anēr, andros (man).
