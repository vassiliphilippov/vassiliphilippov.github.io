brisance

1. NOUN

/ brɪˈsɑːns / (AmE), / brɪˈsɑːns / (BrE)
The shattering or crushing power of an explosive, especially as measured by the speed at which its pressure wave propagates.
shattering power of an explosion

Examples

The brisance of the compound made it ideal for demolition purposes.
Researchers measured the brisance of several new explosive materials in the laboratory.
The brisance of the blast shattered all nearby windows within seconds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explosiveness - the quality of being explosive

Etymology

From French 'brisance', from 'briser' meaning 'to break'.
