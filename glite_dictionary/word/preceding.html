preceding

1. ADJ

/ prɪˈsidɪŋ / (AmE), / prɪˈsiːdɪŋ / (BrE)
existing or coming before something or someone in time or order
coming before in time or order

Examples

Please refer to the preceding chapter for more information on this topic.
The preceding events led directly to the current situation.
In the preceding year, sales had been much lower.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: following - coming after in time or order
  • Synonym: previous - having existed or happened before something else

Etymology

Present participle of 'precede', from Latin 'praecedere' (to go before).

2. ADJ

/ prɪˈsiːdɪŋ / (AmE), / prɪˈsiːdɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person) having formerly held a particular position or office before someone else.
held a position previously

Examples

The preceding president set new policies that continue to influence the company today.
Her preceding manager left detailed notes for the transition.
The preceding chairperson was well respected by the organization's members.

Synset Examples

a retiring member of the board

Related Terms

  • Synonym: former - having previously filled a particular role or position
  • Antonym: successor - a person who follows another in an office or position

Etymology

Present participle of 'precede', from Latin 'praecedere' (to go before).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01299212-s
Synset POS: s
