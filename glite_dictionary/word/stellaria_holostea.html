Stellaria holostea

1. NOUN

/ stɛˈlɛriə hoʊˈlɒstiə / (AmE), / stɛˈlɑːriə həˈlɒstiːə / (BrE)
A low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia, also known as 'greater stitchwort,' with small white star-shaped flowers.
a herbaceous plant with white star-shaped flowers

Examples

Stellaria holostea can often be found blooming in woodland clearings during spring.
Gardeners appreciate Stellaria holostea for its delicate white flowers and spreading habit.
Some herbalists have used Stellaria holostea historically to relieve minor aches and pains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greater stitchwort - common English name for Stellaria holostea

Etymology

From Latin: 'Stellaria' meaning 'little star,' referring to the shape of its flowers; 'holostea' from Greek 'holosteon' meaning 'entire bone,' possibly alluding to the stem.
