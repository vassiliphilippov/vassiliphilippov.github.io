disquieting

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈkwaɪ.ɪ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪsˈkwaɪ.ɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
causing a feeling of worry, unease, or anxiety
causing worry or unease

Examples

The news reports were particularly disquieting to residents living near the border.
She found the silence after the argument strangely disquieting.
A disquieting thought crept into his mind as he reviewed the documents.

Synset Examples

the disquieting sounds of nearby gunfire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disturbing - causing anxiety or uneasiness
  • Antonym: soothing - having a calming, comforting effect

Etymology

From the verb 'disquiet' (to make uneasy), itself from Old French 'desquieter' and ultimately based on Latin 'quietus' (calm, quiet).
