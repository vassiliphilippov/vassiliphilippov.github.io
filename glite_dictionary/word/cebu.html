Cebu

1. PROPN

/ seɪˈbuː / (AmE), / seɪˈbuː / (BrE)
an important seaport and city on the island of Cebu in the Philippines
major city and seaport in the Philippines

Examples

Many tourists visit Cebu each year to enjoy its beautiful beaches and vibrant city life.
The shipping industry plays a crucial role in the economy of Cebu.
Flights from Manila to Cebu are available several times a day.

Etymology

From Cebuano Sugbo (native name for the area); Spanish spelling 'Cebú'.

2. PROPN

/ seɪˈbuː / (AmE), / seɪˈbuː / (BrE)
an island in the central Philippines, known for its historical significance and excellent harbor
island in the central Philippines

Examples

Many tourists visit Cebu for its beautiful beaches and lively culture.
The port of Cebu has been a trading hub for centuries.
After landing in Manila, we caught a flight to Cebu for the next part of our trip.

Etymology

From Cebuano and Spanish 'Cebú,' the island's native and colonial names.
