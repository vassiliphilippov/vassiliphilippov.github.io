geode

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːoʊd / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːəʊd / (BrE)
a rock containing a cavity lined with crystals or mineral matter
rock containing a crystal-lined cavity

Examples

The center of the geode sparkled with amethyst crystals.
She cracked open a geode to reveal a beautiful array of quartz inside.
Collectors often display geodes for their colorful, crystal-lined interiors.

Etymology

From French 'géode', from Latin 'geodes' (precious stone), from Ancient Greek 'γεώδης' (geōdēs, 'earthy').
