Chinchilla laniger

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪnˈtʃɪlə ləˈnɪdʒər / (AmE), / tʃɪnˈtʃɪlə ləˈnɪdʒə / (BrE)
A small South American rodent with extremely soft pearly grey fur, native to the Andes and widely bred in captivity for its fur.
South American rodent bred for soft fur

Examples

The Chinchilla laniger is highly prized for its luxurious and dense fur.
Many farms breed Chinchilla laniger specifically for the international fur trade.
Conservationists are working to protect wild Chinchilla laniger populations in the Andes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chinchilla - small rodent with soft grey fur, bred for fur

Etymology

From Spanish 'chinchilla' (from indigenous Quechua), and Latin 'laniger' meaning 'wool-bearing'.
