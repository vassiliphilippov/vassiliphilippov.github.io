colourcast

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlərˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈkʌləˌkɑːst / (BrE)
A television broadcast that is transmitted in color rather than black and white.
television program broadcast in color
british

Examples

The BBC's first colourcast attracted millions of viewers eager to see the new technology.
My grandfather remembers watching the first ever colourcast on his old set in the 1960s.
To celebrate the anniversary, the channel replayed the historic Wimbledon colourcast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broadcast - a program transmitted by television or radio

Etymology

Formed from 'colour' and 'broadcast', reflecting the transmission of TV in color.
