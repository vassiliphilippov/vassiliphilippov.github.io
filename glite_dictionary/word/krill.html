krill

1. NOUN

/ krɪl / (AmE), / krɪl / (BrE)
Small, shrimp-like marine crustaceans that are an important food source for many larger animals, such as baleen whales.
small marine crustaceans; whale food

Examples

Baleen whales migrate long distances to feed on abundant krill in polar waters.
Scientists are studying the effects of climate change on krill populations.
The pink color of some penguins' feathers comes from eating large amounts of krill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrimp - small, elongated crustacean similar to krill

Etymology

From Norwegian 'krill' (small fry of fish, young fish).
