thrombokinase

1. NOUN

/ ˌθroʊmboʊˈkɪneɪs / (AmE), / ˌθrɒmbəʊˈkɪneɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme released from blood platelets that converts prothrombin into thrombin during the process of blood clotting
enzyme involved in blood clotting
medical

Examples

Thrombokinase is essential in the conversion of prothrombin to thrombin during blood clotting.
A deficiency in thrombokinase can lead to impaired coagulation and excessive bleeding.
Researchers studied the effect of various drugs on the activity of thrombokinase in platelets.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: thrombin - an enzyme in blood plasma involved in clot formation

Etymology

From Greek 'thrombos' (clot) and 'kinase' (an enzyme that catalyzes a transfer of a phosphate group).
