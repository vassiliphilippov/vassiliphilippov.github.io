to offboard

1. VERB

/ ˈɔfˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌbɔːd / (BrE)
to formally transition or remove an employee from a company, typically by completing required exit procedures
formally transition an employee out of a company

Examples

All employees who resign must be properly offboarded to ensure security compliance.
The HR team is responsible for offboarding departing staff members effectively.
After being offboarded, she returned all company property and deactivated her accounts.

Synset Examples

The company will offboard employees next month.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: onboard - to integrate a new employee into a company

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'onboard', using the prefix 'off-' + 'board'.
