Equisetatae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkwəˈsiːtəˌtiː / (AmE), / ˌiːkwɪˈsiːtətiː / (BrE)
A group of non-flowering vascular plants comprising the horsetails and closely related forms.
group of plants including horsetails

Examples

The Equisetatae thrived during the Carboniferous period, dominating the landscape.
Modern Equisetatae are often found in wet habitats near streams and marshes.
Paleobotanists study fossilized Equisetatae to learn about prehistoric plant life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horsetail - a non-flowering plant of the genus Equisetum

Etymology

From Latin 'equisetum' (horsetail), the type genus of the group.
