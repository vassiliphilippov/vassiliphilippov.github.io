unfeathered

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfɛðɚd / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɛðəd / (BrE)
not having feathers; lacking feathers, especially where they are normally present
lacking feathers

Examples

The young chicks are born unfeathered and vulnerable to cold.
An unfeathered bird is often unable to fly or maintain body temperature.
The breed is distinguished by its unfeathered legs.

Synset Examples

a featherless biped
the unfeathered legs of an Orpington

Related Terms

  • Synonym: featherless - without feathers; lacking feathers

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + feathered.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01002749-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfɛðɚd / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɛðəd / (BrE)
(of a bird or part of a bird) lacking feathers.
not having feathers (of a bird)

Examples

The newborn chicks were completely unfeathered and needed constant warmth.
An unfeathered wing cannot help a bird fly.
The museum displayed an unfeathered bird skeleton for educational purposes.

Synset Examples

the unfeathered brood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: featherless - lacking feathers
  • Antonym: feathered - having feathers

Etymology

Formed from the prefix "un-" (not) + "feathered".

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01100341-s
Synset POS: s
