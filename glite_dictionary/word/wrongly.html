wrongly

1. ADV

/ ˈrɔŋ.li / (AmE), / ˈrɒŋ.li / (BrE)
in a way that is not just, fair, or correct; unjustly or incorrectly
unjustly or incorrectly

Examples

He was wrongly accused of stealing the money.
The computer wrongly calculated the total amount due.
She believed her friend had wrongly blamed her for the mistake.

Synset Examples

wouldst not play false and yet would wrongly win

Related Terms

  • Antonym: justly - in a fair or morally right way
  • Synonym: incorrectly - in a way that is not correct

Etymology

From Old English 'wrang' (wrong) + '-ly', an adverbial suffix.

2. ADV

/ ˈrɔŋli / (AmE), / ˈrɒŋli / (BrE)
In an incorrect or mistaken manner.
in an incorrect manner

Examples

She was wrongly accused of the crime and later cleared of all charges.
The data was wrongly entered into the system, causing several errors.
He believed wrongly that she had already left the building.

Synset Examples

he decided to reveal the details only after other sources had reported them incorrectly
she guessed wrong

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incorrectly - in an incorrect manner
  • Antonym: rightly - in a correct or justified manner

Etymology

From wrong + -ly, forming an adverb from the adjective 'wrong.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00205553-r
Synset POS: r
