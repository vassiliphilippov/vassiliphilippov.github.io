Melia Azadirachta

1. NOUN

/ məˈliːə ˌæzədəˈræktə / (AmE), / mɪˈliːə ˌazəˌdɪəˈræktə / (BrE)
a large semi-evergreen tree native to the East Indies, with a trunk that exudes gum, a bitter bark used as a tonic, and seeds that yield an aromatic oil; also known as the neem tree
tree species of the East Indies; the neem tree

Examples

The leaves of the Melia Azadirachta are commonly used in traditional Indian medicine.
The bark of Melia Azadirachta is valued for its bitter tonic properties.
Farmers often plant Melia Azadirachta to provide shade and natural pest control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neem - common name for Melia Azadirachta, a medicinal tree of the East Indies

Etymology

From Latin genus names Melia and Azadirachta; the species is widely known as the neem tree (Azadirachta indica).
