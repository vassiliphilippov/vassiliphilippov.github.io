clatter

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈklætə / (BrE)
a loud, continuous rattling or banging noise, often caused by hard objects striking each other rapidly
loud, rattling noise

Examples

The clatter of dishes in the kitchen signaled that dinner was nearly ready.
She was woken by the clatter of footsteps in the hallway.
The old cart moved down the street with a loud clatter of wheels.

Synset Examples

the shutters clattered against the house
the clatter of iron wheels on cobblestones

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rattle - a rapid succession of short, sharp noises
  • Synonym: clang - a loud, resonant metallic sound

Etymology

Imitative; from Middle English 'clatren', of imitative origin.

2. VERB

/ ˈklæt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈklætə / (BrE)
to make a loud, rattling sound, often by striking hard objects together repeatedly
make a loud rattling noise

Examples

The plates began to clatter as the train sped over the tracks.
She accidentally clattered the pots and pans while cooking dinner.
Footsteps clattered down the empty hallway late at night.

Synset Examples

clattering dishes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rattle - make a rapid succession of short, sharp sounds
  • Degree Higher: crash - make a sudden loud noise as of something breaking

Etymology

Imitative in origin, echoing the sound of rattling.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02176571-v
Synset POS: v
