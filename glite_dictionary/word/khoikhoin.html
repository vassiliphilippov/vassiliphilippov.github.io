Khoikhoin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔɪˌkɔɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɔɪˌkɔɪn / (BrE)
any of the Khoisan languages formerly spoken by the pastoral people of Namibia and South Africa, especially the Khoikhoi.
a Khoisan language of Namibia and South Africa

Examples

The Khoikhoin language has many unique click sounds unfamiliar to English speakers.
Many words in modern Afrikaans have roots in Khoikhoin vocabulary.
Anthropologists have documented several dialects of Khoikhoin across southern Africa.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Khoisan - a family of African languages including Khoikhoin

Etymology

From Khoikhoi, the name of the ethnic group whose language it is.
