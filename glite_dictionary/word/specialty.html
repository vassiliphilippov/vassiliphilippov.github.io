specialty

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛʃəlti / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəlti / (BrE)
a particular area of work or study to which someone has devoted their career or expertise
main area of work or expertise

Examples

Her specialty is environmental law, and she has practiced it for over a decade.
Pediatric medicine is the specialty he chose after medical school.
The chef's specialty is French pastries, which are highly acclaimed.

Synset Examples

his specialization is gastroenterology

Related Terms

  • Synonym: specialization - the process of concentrating on and becoming expert in a particular subject or skill

Etymology

From Latin 'specialitas', from 'specialis' (special).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00584498-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɛʃəlti / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəlti / (BrE)
a particular skill, asset, or area in which a person excels
thing a person is especially good at

Examples

Baking is my specialty, especially when it comes to making bread.
Negotiation has always been his specialty in the business world.
Her specialty is solving difficult math problems quickly.

Synset Examples

cooking is his forte

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forte - a thing at which someone excels
  • Antonym: weakness - an area in which someone is not strong

Etymology

From Old French especialté, from Latin specialitas (quality of being special).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05166608-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈspɛʃəlti / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəlti / (BrE)
a distinctive or distinguishing feature or characteristic of a person, thing, or group
distinguishing trait or feature

Examples

For many years, her patience was considered her greatest specialty by friends and family.
This region’s specialty is its unique blend of cultural traditions.
Attention to detail is one of the company’s main specialties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: characteristic - a typical feature or quality that makes someone or something recognizable
  • Synonym: feature - an important or noticeable part or characteristic

Etymology

From Latin specialitas (individuality, particularity), via Old French specialté.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04771180-n
Synset POS: n
