marqueterie

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑrkəˈtɛri / (AmE), / ˌmɑːkəˈtɛri / (BrE)
A decorative technique in which pieces of veneer are inlaid to form patterns or pictures, especially used to ornament furniture.
decorative inlaid veneer on furniture

Examples

The antique cabinet was decorated with intricate marqueterie depicting floral scenes.
Experts restored the faded marqueterie on the tabletop to its former glory.
He studied traditional marqueterie techniques to create custom furniture pieces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marquetry - Decorative work in which veneers are inlaid into a surface to form patterns or pictures

Etymology

French marqueterie, from marqueter (to variegate, spot, or mark).
