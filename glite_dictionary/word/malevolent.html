malevolent

1. ADJ

/ məˈlɛvələnt / (AmE), / məˈlɛvələnt / (BrE)
having or showing a desire to cause harm to others; having a harmful or evil influence
having a harmful or evil influence

Examples

The malevolent influence of the dictator was felt throughout the country.
People believed that the ruins were haunted by a malevolent spirit.
She gave him a malevolent glare that sent shivers down his spine.

Synset Examples

malevolent stars
a malefic force

Related Terms

  • Antonym: benevolent - having a kind or well-meaning influence or disposition
  • Synonym: malicious - intending or intended to do harm

Etymology

From Latin malevolens, from male (badly) + volens (wishing), present participle of velle (to wish).

2. ADJ

/ məˈlɛvələnt / (AmE), / məˈlɛvələnt / (BrE)
having or showing a wish to do evil to others; motivated by intense ill will or hatred.
wishing or intending evil to others

Examples

Some believed the haunted house was inhabited by a malevolent spirit.
He always wore a malevolent grin when speaking about his rivals.
The villain in the story was truly malevolent, plotting revenge at every turn.

Synset Examples

a gossipy malevolent old woman
failure made him malevolent toward those who were successful

Related Terms

  • Antonym: benevolent - well meaning and kindly
  • Synonym: malicious - intending or intended to do harm

Etymology

From Latin malevolens, malevolent-, from male (badly) + volens (wishing), present participle of velle (to wish).
