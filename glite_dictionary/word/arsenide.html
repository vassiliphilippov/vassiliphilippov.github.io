arsenide

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːr səˌnaɪd / (AmE), / ˈɑː səˌnaɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound in which arsenic is combined with a more electropositive element or group
compound of arsenic with a more positive element
medical

Examples

Gallium arsenide is widely used in the manufacture of semiconductors.
The miner identified the dark mineral as an iron arsenide.
Safety measures are important when handling substances containing arsenide.

Etymology

From arsenic + -ide, denoting a binary compound.
