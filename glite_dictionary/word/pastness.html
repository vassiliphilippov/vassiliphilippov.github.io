pastness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæst.nəs / (AmE), / ˈpɑːst.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of belonging to or existing in the past.
the state of being in the past

Examples

The concept of pastness is central to many philosophical discussions about time.
The use of the past tense conveys a sense of pastness in the narrative.
Museums preserve objects whose primary value lies in their pastness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: presentness - the quality or state of being present
  • Antonym: futurity - the quality or state of being in the future

Etymology

From 'past' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality.
