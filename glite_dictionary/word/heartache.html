heartache

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrˌteɪk / (AmE), / ˈhɑːteɪk / (BrE)
intense emotional pain or sorrow, especially caused by loss or disappointment in love or bereavement.
intense emotional sorrow or grief

Examples

She felt a deep heartache after her best friend moved away.
The story is full of love, heartache, and hope.
Nothing could ease the heartache he experienced when his dog died.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grief - deep sorrow, especially caused by death
  • Antonym: happiness - state of being happy, joy

Etymology

From 'heart' + 'ache', meaning a pain felt as if in the heart, used metaphorically since the 16th century.
