phenazopyridine

1. NOUN

/ fəˌnæzoʊˈpɪrəˌdin / (AmE), / fəˌnazəʊˈpɪrɪˌdiːn / (BrE)
A drug used as a urinary tract analgesic to relieve pain, burning, and discomfort caused by urinary tract infections.
drug for urinary tract pain relief
medical

Examples

My doctor prescribed phenazopyridine to help relieve the burning sensation during my urinary tract infection.
Taking phenazopyridine caused my urine to turn a bright orange color, as mentioned in the side effects.
The pharmacist explained that phenazopyridine is only for short-term relief of urinary discomfort and not a cure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pyridium - Trade name for phenazopyridine

Etymology

From 'phen-' (phenyl) + 'azo' (nitrogen group) + 'pyridine' (a basic heterocyclic organic compound).
