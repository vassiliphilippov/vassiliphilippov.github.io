Nganasan

1. NOUN

/ ˌŋɑːnəˈsɑːn / (AmE), / ˌŋanəˈsɑːn / (BrE)
A member of a Samoyedic people living on the Taimyr Peninsula in northern Siberia.
member of an indigenous Siberian people

Examples

The Nganasan have traditionally relied on reindeer herding and fishing for their livelihood.
Anthropologists have studied the rituals of the Nganasan for decades.
A small population of Nganasan still lives in remote villages on the Taimyr Peninsula.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Samoyed - a member of a group of related indigenous Siberian peoples

2. NOUN

/ nɑːnˈɡɑːsən / (AmE), / næŋˈɡænəsən / (BrE)
the Uralic language spoken by the Nganasan people of northern Siberia
a Uralic language of northern Siberia

Examples

There are only a few dozen speakers of Nganasan remaining today.
Linguists study Nganasan to learn more about the languages of the Arctic region.
The Nganasan language is considered endangered.

Etymology

From Russian Нганасаны (Nganasany), the self-designation of the Nganasan people.
