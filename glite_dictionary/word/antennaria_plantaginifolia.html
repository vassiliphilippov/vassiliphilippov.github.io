Antennaria plantaginifolia

1. NOUN

/ ænˌtɛːˈnɛəriə ˌplæntəˌdʒɪˈnoʊliə / (AmE), / ænˌtɛnəˈnɛːrɪə ˌplæntɪˌdʒɪˈnəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A North American perennial plant in the daisy family, propagated by runners and known for its woolly flowering heads; also called plantain-leaved pussytoes.
a North American perennial plant

Examples

The woodland floor was dotted with patches of Antennaria plantaginifolia in early spring.
Gardeners sometimes use Antennaria plantaginifolia as a low-maintenance ground cover.
She identified the wildflower as Antennaria plantaginifolia by its woolly white flower heads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pussytoes - common name for plants in the genus Antennaria

Etymology

From genus name Antennaria (from Latin antenna, referring to the flower's tufted pistils) and species epithet plantaginifolia, meaning 'plantain-leaved' in Latin.
