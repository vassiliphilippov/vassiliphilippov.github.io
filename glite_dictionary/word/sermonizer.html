sermonizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜːrməˌnaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈsɜːmənˌaɪzə / (BrE)
a person whose occupation is preaching sermons, especially religious ones
person who preaches sermons

Examples

The sermonizer delivered a powerful message about forgiveness to the congregation.
As a lifelong sermonizer, he traveled from town to town inspiring parishioners with his words.
Many found the sermonizer's passionate style both stirring and thought-provoking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preacher - a person who delivers religious sermons
  • Degree Lower: orator - a public speaker, especially one well versed in rhetoric

Etymology

From 'sermon' (Middle English, from Old French, from Latin 'sermo' meaning discourse) + '-izer' (agent noun suffix).
