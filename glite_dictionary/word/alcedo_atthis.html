Alcedo atthis

1. NOUN

/ ælˈsiːdoʊ ˈæθɪs / (AmE), / ælˈsiːdəʊ ˈæθɪs / (BrE)
a small kingfisher of Europe and Asia, noted for its greenish-blue upperparts and bright orange underparts
a small brightly colored kingfisher

Examples

The vivid colors of the Alcedo atthis make it easy to spot as it darts over rivers.
Researchers observed an Alcedo atthis nesting along the riverbank early in the spring.
Many birdwatchers hope to catch a glimpse of the elusive Alcedo atthis during their outings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kingfisher - a family of brightly colored birds known for catching fish

Etymology

From Latin: Alcedo (kingfisher genus) and atthis (a poetic name from Greek legend).
