banter

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæn.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈbæn.tə / (BrE)
the playful and friendly exchange of teasing remarks
playful, teasing conversation

Examples

There was a lot of banter between the coworkers during their lunch break.
Their friendly banter made everyone in the room smile.
She joined in the banter, not afraid to poke fun at herself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: badinage - light and playful conversation; witty exchange
  • Synonym: raillery - good-humored teasing or ridicule
  • Degree Higher: mockery - teasing and contemptuous language or behavior

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from obsolete French *banter* (to mock, deride).

2. VERB

/ ˈbæn.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈbæn.tə / (BrE)
to speak to someone in a playful and teasing way; to exchange witty remarks with someone in a light-hearted manner
exchange playful teasing remarks

Examples

The two friends bantered back and forth, laughing at each other's jokes.
She loves to banter with her colleagues during lunch breaks.
They bantered playfully about whose team would win the match.

Synset Examples

After we relaxed, we just kidded around

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joke - say something humorous or funny
  • Synonym: tease - make fun of or provoke someone playfully
  • Antonym: insult - to speak to or treat with disrespect (opposite of playful manner)

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from obsolete French 'banter' (to make fun of), meaning developed in English in the 17th century.
