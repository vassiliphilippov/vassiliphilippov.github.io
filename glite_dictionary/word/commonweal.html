commonweal

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.mənˌwil / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ənˌwiːl / (BrE)
the welfare or well-being of the public or the community as a whole
the public good or welfare

Examples

The reforms were enacted for the commonweal, not for individual gain.
She dedicated her life to service and the promotion of the commonweal.
Legislation should prioritize the commonweal over special interests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common good - benefit or interests of all people in a society
  • Synonym: public interest - the welfare or well-being of the general public

Etymology

From Middle English 'commone wele', from 'common' + 'weal' (well-being).
