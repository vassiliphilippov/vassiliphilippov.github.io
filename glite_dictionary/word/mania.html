mania

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪniə / (AmE), / ˈmeɪnɪə / (BrE)
An intense, often irrational enthusiasm or obsession for something, especially one that is overwhelming or irresistible.
an intense obsession or enthusiasm

Examples

His collecting mania led him to fill every room with rare coins and stamps.
Many people develop a mania for fitness and spend hours at the gym every day.
The sudden mania for fidget spinners swept through schools across the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obsession - an idea or thought that continually preoccupies or intrudes on a person's mind

Etymology

From Latin mania, from Greek maniā (madness, frenzy).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪniə / (AmE), / ˈmeɪnɪə / (BrE)
A mental disorder marked by periods of great excitement, euphoria, delusions, and overactivity.
state of extreme mood or excitement
medical

Examples

During a period of mania, he barely slept and spent money recklessly.
Her doctor diagnosed her with bipolar disorder after observing symptoms of both mania and depression.
The patient's sudden burst of energy was a clear sign of mania.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: depression - a mental state characterized by persistent sadness; opposite of mania in mood disorders
  • Degree Lower: hypomania - a mild form of mania, less severe symptoms

Etymology

From Greek *mania* (madness, frenzy).
