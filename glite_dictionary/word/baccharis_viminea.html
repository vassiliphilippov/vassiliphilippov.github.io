Baccharis viminea

1. NOUN

/ bəˈkærɪs vɪˈmiːniə / (AmE), / bəˈkærɪs vɪˈmiːniə / (BrE)
A shrub native to California with slender leafy shoots, important as browse for mule deer.
California shrub important for mule deer

Examples

Baccharis viminea grows along streams and moist slopes throughout California's foothills.
Wildlife experts observed that Baccharis viminea provides essential forage for local mule deer populations.
The slender shoots of Baccharis viminea make it easily identifiable among other native shrubs.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Baccharis' is a genus name, and 'viminea' means 'of twigs/wands', from Latin 'vimen' (twig, pliant branch).
