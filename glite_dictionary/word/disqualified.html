disqualified

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈkwɑlɪˌfaɪd / (AmE), / dɪsˈkwɒlɪˌfaɪd / (BrE)
Not eligible to participate or be chosen because of a violation of law, rule, or provision.
made ineligible by rules or law

Examples

The athlete was disqualified from the competition for breaking the rules.
Any candidate who plagiarizes will be automatically disqualified.
He was found guilty of doping and is now disqualified from all future events.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: eligible - qualified to be chosen or participate
  • Synonym: ineligible - not eligible or qualified

Etymology

From 'disqualify' (verb), from Latin dis- (expressing reversal) + qualify.

2. ADJ

/ dɪsˈkwɑlɪˌfaɪd / (AmE), / dɪsˈkwɒlɪˌfaɪd / (BrE)
barred from taking part in a competition or activity, usually because of breaking rules or failing to meet requirements
barred from competition or activity

Examples

The runner was disqualified for stepping out of her lane during the race.
Any student caught cheating will be disqualified from the exam.
Three teams were disqualified after failing to meet the entry requirements.

Synset Examples

a disqualified player

Related Terms

  • Antonym: eligible - meeting the requirements to participate in something

Etymology

From the prefix 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'qualified' (from Medieval Latin qualificare, to make of a certain quality).
