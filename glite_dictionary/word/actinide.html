actinide

1. NOUN

/ ˈæktɪˌnaɪd / (AmE), / ˈæktɪnʌɪd / (BrE)
any of a series of chemically similar radioactive elements with atomic numbers from 89 (actinium) through 103 (lawrencium), typically noted for their radioactivity.
radioactive element series (atomic numbers 89–103)

Examples

Uranium and plutonium are both classified as actinide elements.
The unique properties of each actinide make them important in nuclear technology.
Researchers are studying actinide compounds to improve waste disposal methods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lanthanide - any of a series of metallic elements from lanthanum to lutetium (atomic numbers 57–71)

Etymology

From ‘actinium’, the first element in the series, plus the suffix ‘-ide’.
