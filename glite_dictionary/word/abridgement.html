abridgement

1. NOUN

/ əˈbrɪdʒ.mənt / (AmE), / əˈbrɪdʒ.mənt / (BrE)
a shortened version of a written work, retaining the essential content
shortened version of a text

Examples

The abridgement of the novel made it more accessible to younger readers.
She read an abridgement of the classic play for her literature class.
The documentary provided an abridgement of the author's life and works.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abbreviation - a shortened form of a word or phrase

Etymology

From Old French 'abregement', from 'abregier' (to shorten), from Latin 'abbreviare'.
