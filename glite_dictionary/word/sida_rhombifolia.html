Sida rhombifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪdə ˌrɒmbəˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˌsiːdə ˌrɒmbɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
a tropical and subtropical herb used for forage and medicinal purposes, known for its strong, fine bast fiber and sometimes considered an aggressive weed
tropical herb with medicinal and fiber uses

Examples

Sida rhombifolia is valued for its fiber, which is stronger than jute and used in making ropes.
Traditional healers often use Sida rhombifolia as a demulcent to soothe irritated tissues.
In some areas, Sida rhombifolia is considered a persistent weed that can invade cultivated fields.

Etymology

From the genus name Sida (Linnaean taxonomy) and Latin rhombifolia meaning 'with rhombus-shaped leaves'.
