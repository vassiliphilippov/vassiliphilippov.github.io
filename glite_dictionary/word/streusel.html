streusel

1. NOUN

/ ˈstruːzəl / (AmE), / ˈʃtruːzəl / (BrE)
A crumbly mixture of flour, butter, and sugar used as a topping for cakes, muffins, and pastries.
crumbly pastry topping

Examples

She sprinkled a generous layer of streusel over the blueberry muffins before baking.
The coffee cake was topped with a sweet streusel that added a delightful crunch.
I love the buttery streusel on top of homemade apple pie.

Etymology

From German 'Streusel', from 'streuen' (to scatter or sprinkle).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstruːzəl / (AmE), / ˈstruːzəl / (BrE)
A crumbly mixture of butter, sugar, and flour used as a topping or filling for cakes and pastries.
crumbly cake or pastry topping

Examples

I made an apple coffee cake with a generous streusel on top.
The streusel gives the pastry a crunchy, sweet finish.
Would you like a slice of blueberry streusel with your coffee?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to crumble - a dessert topped with a crumbly mixture of flour, butter, and sugar

Etymology

From German 'Streusel', from 'streuen' (to scatter, strew).
