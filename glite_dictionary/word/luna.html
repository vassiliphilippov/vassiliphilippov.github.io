Luna

1. PROPN

/ ˈluːnə / (AmE), / ˈluːnə / (BrE)
(in Roman mythology) the goddess of the Moon, counterpart to the Greek Selene.
Roman goddess of the Moon

Examples

In ancient Roman art, Luna is often depicted riding a chariot across the night sky.
The temple of Luna stood on the Aventine Hill in Rome.
Many poets invoke Luna when describing the enchanting beauty of the full moon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Selene - Greek goddess of the Moon

Etymology

From Latin *lūna* (moon), which also gave English the word 'lunar'.
