Lycopodium obscurum

1. NOUN

/ laɪ.kəˈpoʊ.di.əm əbˈskjʊər.əm / (AmE), / lɪ.kəˈpəʊ.di.əm əbˈskjʊə.rəm / (BrE)
A North American species of club moss with evergreen leaves, often called ground pine or princess pine, commonly found in forests.
a species of club moss; ground pine

Examples

The shady forest floor was covered with Lycopodium obscurum, giving the area a lush, green look.
Botanists studied the distribution of Lycopodium obscurum across the northeastern United States.
Many holiday wreaths use Lycopodium obscurum for its attractive, evergreen foliage.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining genus Lycopodium and Latin obscurum (dark, obscure).
