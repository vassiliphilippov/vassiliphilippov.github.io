actinism

1. NOUN

/ ˈæktɪˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈaktɪnɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the property of radiation that enables it to produce photochemical effects, such as causing chemical changes in photographic film or biological tissues
property of radiation producing photochemical effects

Examples

Early photography relied heavily on an understanding of actinism to develop effective plates.
The actinism of ultraviolet light causes certain chemicals in our skin to react.
Researchers measured the actinism of the lamp to assess its impact on biological samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photochemistry - branch of chemistry concerned with chemical effects of light

Etymology

From Greek aktis, aktinos (ray, beam) + -ism.
