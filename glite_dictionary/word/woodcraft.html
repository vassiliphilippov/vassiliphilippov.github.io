woodcraft

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdˌkræft / (AmE), / ˈwʊdˌkrɑːft / (BrE)
skill in carving, shaping, or making objects from wood
skill in woodworking or carving

Examples

His woodcraft was evident in the beautifully carved furniture he made by hand.
She taught her grandson the basics of woodcraft during summer visits to her workshop.
The exhibition highlighted traditional woodcraft from indigenous cultures around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodworking - the activity or skill of making things from wood
  • Synonym: carpentry - the trade or skill of a carpenter

Etymology

From Middle English 'wodecraft', combining 'wood' and 'craft' (skill).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwʊd.kræft / (AmE), / ˈwʊd.krɑːft / (BrE)
Skill and knowledge in dealing with living and surviving in forests or the outdoors, especially in activities like camping, hunting, or fishing.
skill in outdoor and woodland activities

Examples

He relied on his woodcraft to find safe shelter and food in the dense forest.
Her woodcraft impressed even the most experienced outdoor guides.
Children in the camp learned basic woodcraft skills, including building fires and navigating the woods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bushcraft - skill and knowledge of surviving and thriving in wild natural environments

Etymology

Formed from 'wood' + 'craft', meaning the art or skill related to the woods.

Synset POS: n
