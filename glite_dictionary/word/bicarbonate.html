bicarbonate

1. NOUN

/ baɪˈkɑːrbəˌneɪt / (AmE), / baɪˈkɑːbənət / (BrE)
a salt containing the anion HCO₃, formed when one hydrogen atom of carbonic acid is replaced; an acid carbonate
a salt of carbonic acid, HCO₃

Examples

The doctor ordered a test to check the patient's bicarbonate levels in her blood.
Sodium bicarbonate is commonly known as baking soda.
Many antacid tablets contain bicarbonate to help neutralize stomach acid.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: carbonate - a salt containing the anion CO₃₂⁻

Etymology

From French 'bicarbonate', from 'bi-' (indicating two) + 'carbonate'.
