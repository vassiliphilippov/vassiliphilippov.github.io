shoegazing

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃuˌɡeɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃuːˌɡeɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
A style of alternative rock music characterized by heavy use of guitar effects, indistinct vocals, and dense, layered sounds.
alternative rock music subgenre

Examples

The band's sound is heavily influenced by shoegazing, with layers of distorted guitars and subdued vocals.
Many classic shoegazing albums were produced in the UK during the late 1980s and early 1990s.
Fans of shoegazing often appreciate the genre's dreamy, immersive soundscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dream pop - A related genre with ethereal textures and atmospheric sound

Etymology

From 'shoe-gazing', referring to musicians' tendency to look down at their effects pedals while performing.
