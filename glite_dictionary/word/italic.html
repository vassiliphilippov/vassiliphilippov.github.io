Italic

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈtælɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈtælɪk / (BrE)
a branch of the Indo-European language family that includes Latin and related ancient languages such as Oscan and Umbrian
Indo-European language branch including Latin

Examples

Latin is the most well-known member of the Italic language group.
The ancient Italic peoples spoke languages now largely extinct.
Scholars have studied the evolution of Italic languages through inscriptions and historical texts.

Etymology

From Latin 'Italicus' (of Italy).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈtælɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈtalɪk / (BrE)
Relating to the Italic languages, a branch of Indo-European languages once spoken in the Italian Peninsula, including Latin and its relatives.
relating to Italic languages

Examples

The historian specialized in Italic linguistics, focusing on early regional dialects.
Several Italic inscriptions have been discovered in central Italy.
Latin is the most famous member of the Italic language family.

Synset Examples

ancient Italic dialects

Etymology

From Latin *Italicus* (Italian), referring to ancient peoples of Italy.
