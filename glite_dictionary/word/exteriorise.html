to exteriorise

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈstɪriɔˌraɪz / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɪəriəraɪz / (BrE)
to make something external or objective, especially by expressing or manifesting it outwardly
make external or objective

Examples

Language allows us to exteriorise our thoughts and feelings.
Artists often exteriorise their inner emotions through their work.
The therapist encouraged him to exteriorise his anxieties instead of keeping them inside.

Synset Examples

language externalizes our thoughts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to externalize - to make external or give outward form to

Etymology

From French 'extérioriser', from 'extérieur' (exterior) + '-iser' (-ize); see also 'externalize'.
