galangal

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈlæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ɡəˈlæŋɡəl / (BrE)
A Eurasian perennial plant of the sedge family, with aromatic roots used in cooking and herbal medicine.
aromatic plant with spiced roots

Examples

The chef added slices of galangal to give the soup a distinct spicy aroma.
Traditional medicine often uses galangal root to aid digestion.
The thick stems and reddish spikelets help identify a galangal plant in the wild.

Etymology

From Arabic *khalanjan*, from Chinese *gao liang jiang* (高良姜, 'high-quality ginger').

2. NOUN

/ ɡəˈlæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ɡəˈlæŋɡəl / (BrE)
A tropical Southeast Asian perennial plant of the ginger family, with aromatic rhizomes used as spice or medicine.
aromatic root used as spice

Examples

Traditional Thai soups often include galangal as a key ingredient for its distinctive flavor.
Dried galangal can be found in most Asian grocery stores alongside ginger and turmeric.
She pounded fresh galangal with garlic and chili to make a fragrant curry paste.

Etymology

From Middle English 'galingale', from Old French 'galingal', from Arabic 'khalanjān', from Chinese (Cantonese) 'lam keung'.
