argali

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrɡəli / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡəli / (BrE)
a large wild sheep native to the semidesert regions of Central Asia, characterized by massive curved horns in the male
wild Central Asian sheep with large horns

Examples

The argali is well known for its impressive spiral horns, which make the males easily recognizable.
Researchers traveled to Mongolia to study herds of wild argali in their natural habitat.
Conservation efforts are in place to protect the endangered argali from poaching and habitat loss.

Etymology

From Russian 'argali', borrowed from Mongolian 'argali'.
