unresistant

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnrɪˈzɪstənt / (AmE), / ˌʌnrɪˈzɪstənt / (BrE)
Not resistant; likely to be affected or harmed, especially by a particular factor or agent.
lacking resistance to something

Examples

This species of plant is unresistant to mildew, so it requires regular fungicide treatments.
Children are often unresistant to certain common viruses in their first years of life.
The patient was found to be unresistant to the antibiotic, making treatment less effective.

Synset Examples

liable to diabetes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: susceptible - likely to be affected or influenced by something
  • Antonym: resistant - not affected or harmed by something; able to withstand

Etymology

From un- (not) + resistant, formed on the pattern of other 'un-' prefixed adjectives.
