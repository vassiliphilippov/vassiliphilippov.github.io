vena clitoridis

1. NOUN

/ ˈviː.nə klɪˈtɔr.ɪ.dɪs / (AmE), / ˈviː.nə klɪˈtɒr.ɪ.dɪs / (BrE)
a vein or veins that drain blood from the clitoris
veins serving the clitoris
medical

Examples

The vena clitoridis plays an essential role in the blood circulation of the female genitalia.
During surgery, care must be taken not to damage the vena clitoridis.
In medical anatomy, the vena clitoridis refers specifically to the veins associated with the clitoris.

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein) and 'clitoridis,' genitive of 'clitoris'.
