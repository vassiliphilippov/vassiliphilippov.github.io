Melanitta nigra

1. NOUN

/ mɛ.ləˈnɪ.tə ˈnaɪ.ɡrə / (AmE), / mɪ.ləˈnɪ.tə ˈnɪ.ɡrə / (BrE)
A species of sea duck, commonly known as the common scoter, found in northern Europe and Asia.
a species of sea duck (common scoter)

Examples

The Melanitta nigra is often seen diving for food in shallow coastal waters.
Birdwatchers gathered to observe a flock of Melanitta nigra during their annual migration.
Conservation efforts have helped stabilize some populations of Melanitta nigra in Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common scoter - the common English name for Melanitta nigra

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Melanitta' from Greek 'melas' (black) + 'netta' (duck), 'nigra' from Latin (black).
