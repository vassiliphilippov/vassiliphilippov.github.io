myelitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪəˈlaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌmaɪəˈlaɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the spinal cord, often causing neurological symptoms such as pain, weakness, or paralysis
inflammation of the spinal cord
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with myelitis after experiencing sudden weakness in his legs.
Viral infections are among the most common causes of myelitis in young adults.
Early treatment of myelitis can sometimes prevent long-term neurological damage.

Etymology

From Greek myelos (marrow, spinal cord) + -itis (inflammation).
