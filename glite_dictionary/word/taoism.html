Taoism

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈtaʊˌɪzəm / (BrE)
A Chinese philosophical and religious tradition based on the teachings of Lao-tzu, emphasizing living in harmony with the Tao, a fundamental principle underlying the universe. In some sects, it also incorporates pantheism and practices such as alchemy and divination.
Chinese philosophical and religious tradition

Examples

Many people are drawn to Taoism because of its teachings on harmony and simplicity.
The principles of Taoism have influenced Chinese art, medicine, and literature for centuries.
In Taoism, practitioners seek to live in accordance with the Tao, or the natural order of the universe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Taoist - a follower or practitioner of Taoism
  • Antonym: Confucianism - a philosophical and ethical system based on the teachings of Confucius

Etymology

From Chinese 道教 (Dàojiào), from 道 (dào, 'way, path') + 教 (jiào, 'teachings').

3. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈtaʊ.ɪzəm / (BrE)
A Chinese philosophical and religious tradition based on the teachings of Lao-tzu, emphasizing living in harmony with the Tao and often involving practices such as meditation, alchemy, and divination.
Chinese philosophical and religious tradition

Examples

Taoism teaches that a simple life in harmony with nature is the key to happiness.
Many ancient texts on Taoism emphasize the importance of flexibility and effortless action.
She studied Taoism to better understand Eastern philosophies and meditation techniques.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Confucianism - A Chinese philosophical system emphasizing order, harmony, and hierarchy, often contrasted with Taoism
  • Synonym: Buddhism - A major Eastern religion and philosophy focusing on spiritual development and enlightenment, sometimes associated with Taoism in Chinese culture

Etymology

From Chinese 道教 (Dàojiào), with 道 (Dao, meaning 'way' or 'path') and 教 (jiao, meaning 'teaching' or 'doctrine').
