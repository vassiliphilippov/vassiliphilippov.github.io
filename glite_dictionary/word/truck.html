truck

1. NOUN

/ trʌk / (AmE), / trʌk / (BrE)
A large, heavy road vehicle used for transporting goods, materials, or equipment.
large vehicle for carrying goods

Examples

The delivery truck arrived at the warehouse early in the morning.
He loaded all the furniture onto his truck before moving to a new city.
A fire truck sped down the street with its siren blaring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lorry - A large motor vehicle for transporting goods (chiefly British English synonym)

Etymology

From Middle English 'trukke', possibly from Old French 'troque' (exchange, barter), later applied to vehicles for carrying loads.

2. NOUN

/ trʌk / (AmE), / trʌk / (BrE)
a handcart with two low wheels, a bottom ledge, and handles at the top, used for moving heavy objects such as crates
handcart for moving heavy items

Examples

The warehouse worker used a truck to move the heavy boxes across the floor.
Please load the crates onto a truck so we can take them to the back room.
He struggled to balance the stack of chairs on the small truck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dolly - a platform on wheels used for moving heavy objects
  • Synonym: trolley - a wheeled cart used to move items
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03495220-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ trʌk / (AmE), / trʌk / (BrE)
to transport or convey (goods, materials, etc.) by truck
transport by truck

Examples

The company trucks produce from the farm to the city every morning.
They trucked building materials to the remote construction site through the night.
Do you know if we can truck these goods directly to the port?

Synset Examples

truck fresh vegetables across the mountains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haul - to pull or transport something heavy; commonly used for large-scale transport of goods

Etymology

From the noun 'truck', originally meaning a wheeled vehicle for carrying goods.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01958200-v
Synset POS: v
