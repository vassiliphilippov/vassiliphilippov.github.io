bent

1. NOUN

/ bɛnt / (AmE), / bɛnt / (BrE)
a natural talent, inclination, or aptitude for a particular activity or subject
natural talent or inclination

Examples

She has a bent for solving difficult mathematical problems.
Even as a child, he showed a bent for music and the arts.
The company is looking for employees with a bent for innovation and creativity.

Synset Examples

he had a bent for it
he had a special knack for getting into trouble
he couldn't get the hang of it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knack - a special skill or talent for doing something easily

Etymology

From Old English 'bent' (past participle of 'bendan', to bend), suggesting a leaning or inclination toward something.

/ bɛnt / (AmE), / bɛnt / (BrE)

type of grass for lawns and greens

Examples

The golf course is covered in bent to create its smooth putting surface.

This species of bent is especially popular for bowling greens.

Etymology

From Old English 'beonet', meaning a kind of grass.

7. NOUN

/ bɛnt / (AmE), / bɛnt / (BrE)
a natural or habitual inclination or tendency; a disposition or leaning toward a particular thing
natural tendency or inclination

Examples

She has a scientific bent and spends her free time reading about physics.
His artistic bent was apparent even in childhood, when he loved to draw and paint.
With a natural bent for leadership, she quickly rose to a management position.

Synset Examples

the set of his mind was obvious

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inclination - a tendency to do something or feel a certain way
  • Synonym: predilection - a preference or special liking for something
  • Antonym: aversion - a strong dislike or disinclination

Etymology

From Middle English, past participle of 'benden' (to bend); figurative sense of 'inclination' developed in the 16th century.
