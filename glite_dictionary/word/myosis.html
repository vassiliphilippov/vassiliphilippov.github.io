myosis

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈoʊsɪs / (AmE), / maɪˈəʊsɪs / (BrE)
Reflex contraction of the iris sphincter muscle in response to bright light or certain drugs, causing the pupil to become smaller.
contraction of iris shrinking the pupil
medical

Examples

The doctor noted pronounced myosis when she shone the light in his eyes.
Certain medications can induce myosis as a side effect.
Sudden myosis may signal neurological changes that require medical attention.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mydriasis - dilation of the pupil

Etymology

From Greek mysis (a closing of the eyes), related to myein (to close).
