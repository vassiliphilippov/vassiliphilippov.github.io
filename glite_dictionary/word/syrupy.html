syrupy

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪrəpi / (AmE), / ˈsɪrəpi / (BrE)
(of a liquid) having a thick, sticky consistency like syrup; having high viscosity.
thick and sticky like syrup

Examples

The medicine was so syrupy that it coated the spoon.
He poured a syrupy sauce over his pancakes for breakfast.
The drink had a syrupy texture that made it hard to swallow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viscous - having a thick, sticky consistency; high resistance to flow

Etymology

From syrup + -y, indicating the quality or characteristic of syrup.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɪrəpi / (AmE), / ˈsɪrəpi / (BrE)
Thick and sweet like syrup; having the consistency or sweetness of syrup, sometimes because honey or another sweetener has been added.
thick and sweet like syrup

Examples

The pancakes were covered in a syrupy sauce that made them extra sweet.
Her voice took on a strangely syrupy tone when she spoke to the children.
The dessert had a syrupy texture thanks to the added honey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syrup - a thick, sweet liquid made by dissolving sugar in water

Etymology

From syrup + -y, forming an adjective meaning 'like syrup'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02346107-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈsɪrəpi / (AmE), / ˈsɪrəpi / (BrE)
Excessively sweet in taste or sentiment; resembling syrup.
excessively sweet; like syrup

Examples

The dessert was so syrupy that I could barely finish it.
Her syrupy voice made the speech sound insincere.
Despite its syrupy lyrics, the song became an instant hit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sentimental - showing or causing exaggerated emotion
  • Synonym: cloying - disgustingly or sickeningly sweet
  • Antonym: dry - not sweet or sentimental (used figuratively)

Etymology

From syrup + -y, indicating 'like or containing syrup'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02377134-s
Synset POS: s
