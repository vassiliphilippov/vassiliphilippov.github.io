detached

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈtætʃt / (AmE), / dɪˈtætʃt / (BrE)
(of a building) standing alone and separate from others, not joined to another building.
(of a building) not joined to another

Examples

They decided to buy a detached house in the suburbs for more privacy.
The property includes a detached garage large enough for two cars.
In many British towns, detached homes are much more expensive than semi-detached ones.

Synset Examples

detached houses
a detached garage

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: semi-detached - (of a house) joined to another house on one side only
  • Degree Lower: terraced - (of houses) joined in a row sharing walls

Etymology

From French détaché, past participle of détacher, meaning 'to detach'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00161838-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈtætʃt / (AmE), / dɪˈtætʃt / (BrE)
not fixed in position; separated or disconnected from something to which it is usually attached.
not fixed or connected; separated

Examples

The detached shutter dangled precariously from the window frame.
After the storm, several detached wires lay across the road.
He found a detached piece of the model on the floor.

Synset Examples

the detached shutter fell on him
he pulled his arm free and ran

Related Terms

  • Antonym: attached - joined or fastened to something
  • Synonym: disconnected - not joined or attached; separate

Etymology

From attach with the prefix de- meaning 'away or off', from Old French détacher.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01064646-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ dɪˈtætʃt / (AmE), / dɪˈtætʃt / (BrE)
not influenced by emotions or personal interest; showing an absence of emotional involvement
emotionally uninvolved or impartial

Examples

Her response to the bad news was remarkably detached, almost as if she didn't care at all.
As a judge, he tried to remain detached from personal feelings or opinions.
His detached tone made it difficult to know what he was really thinking.

Synset Examples

adopted a degage pose on the arm of the easy chair
she may be detached or even unfeeling but at least she's not hypocritically effusive
an uninvolved bystander

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unemotional - not showing or affected by strong emotion
  • Antonym: involved - emotionally connected or strongly engaged
  • Synonym: impartial - not biased; fair and neutral

Etymology

From French détaché, past participle of détacher ('detach'), ultimately from Old Italian distaccare.

Source: WordNet

5. ADJ

not involved with or influenced by others emotionally; feeling or appearing to be separate or disconnected.
emotionally or physically separate; not involved

Examples

It can be helpful to stay detached when dealing with stressful situations.

6. ADJ

/ dɪˈtætʃt / (AmE), / dɪˈtætʃt / (BrE)
not influenced by emotion or personal interest; showing little or no affection or warmth
emotionally uninvolved or distant

Examples

She remained detached during the heated argument, refusing to take sides.
His detached manner made it difficult for others to connect with him emotionally.
Therapists are trained to be detached so they can offer unbiased support to their clients.

Synset Examples

an uncaring person

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aloof - not friendly or forthcoming; cool and distant
  • Synonym: dispassionate - not influenced by strong emotion; rational and impartial
  • Antonym: engaged - emotionally or personally involved

Etymology

From past participle of 'detach,' derived from French 'détacher' (to unfasten, disengage).
