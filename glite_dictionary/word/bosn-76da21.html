bos'n

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊsən / (AmE), / ˈbəʊsən / (BrE)
a petty officer on a merchant ship responsible for directing the work of other seamen
petty officer supervising crew on a ship

Examples

The bos'n gave instructions to the deckhands before the storm hit.
As bos'n, he made sure all maintenance work was done promptly.
The crew respected the bos'n for his experience and fairness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boatswain - a petty officer in charge of the hull and equipment of a ship

Etymology

Contraction of 'boatswain' from Middle English 'batswayne'.
