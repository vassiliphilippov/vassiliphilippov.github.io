Ambrose Everett Burnside

1. PROPN

/ ˈæm.bɹoʊz ˈɛv.ɚ.ɪt ˈbɝːn.saɪd / (AmE), / ˈæm.brəʊz ˈev.rɪt ˈbɜːn.saɪd / (BrE)
(1824–1881) United States general in the American Civil War, known for his defeat at the Battle of Fredericksburg and for giving his name to 'sideburns'.
US Civil War general (1824–1881); source of 'sideburns'

Examples

General Ambrose Everett Burnside led the Union Army during key battles of the Civil War.
The distinctive sideburns of Ambrose Everett Burnside gave rise to the popular facial hairstyle's name.
Historians often discuss Ambrose Everett Burnside's tactics at the Battle of Fredericksburg.

Etymology

Burnside's distinctive facial hair inspired the term 'sideburns'.
