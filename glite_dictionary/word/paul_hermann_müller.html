Paul Hermann Müller

1. PROPN

/ paʊl hɛrˈmɑːn ˈmʏlɚ / (AmE), / paʊl hɛˈmaːn ˈmʏlɐ / (BrE)
(1899–1965) Swiss chemist who synthesized DDT and discovered its effectiveness as an insecticide.
Swiss chemist, discovered DDT's use as insecticide

Examples

Paul Hermann Müller was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for his work on DDT.
The invention of DDT by Paul Hermann Müller revolutionized pest control worldwide.
Paul Hermann Müller's discovery had a significant impact on public health, especially during World War II.
