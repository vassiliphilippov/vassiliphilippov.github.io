siphonophore

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈfɑnəˌfɔr / (AmE), / saɪˈfɒnəˌfɔː / (BrE)
a marine animal consisting of a colony of polyps and medusoids, often transparent or brightly colored, that float or swim together as a single organism
oceanic colony-forming marine animal
medical

Examples

The Portuguese man o' war is a well-known type of siphonophore found in warm seas.
Scientists discovered a gigantic siphonophore deep in the ocean off the coast of Australia.
Some siphonophores use long stinging tentacles to capture their prey underwater.

Etymology

From Greek 'siphōn' (tube, pipe) and 'phoros' (bearing).
