dun-coloured

1. ADJ

/ dʌn ˈkʌlɚd / (AmE), / dʌn ˈkʌləd / (BrE)
Having a dull grayish-brown color.
dull grayish-brown in color
british

Examples

The horse had a dun-coloured coat that blended with the autumn fields.
She chose a dun-coloured fabric for the sofa to match the rustic decor.
A flock of dun-coloured birds settled quietly on the bare branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dun - dull grayish-brown color

Etymology

From 'dun' (from Middle English, dull brownish-gray) and 'coloured' (from Old French 'colorer', Latin 'colorare').
