larval

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɑr.vəl / (AmE), / ˈlɑː.vəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a larva, especially an immature form of an animal that undergoes transformation before becoming an adult
relating to a larva

Examples

The butterfly is in its larval stage before undergoing metamorphosis.
Scientists studied the larval development of the marine species.
Many diseases can be transmitted during the larval phase of parasites.

Synset Examples

the larval eye

Related Terms

  • Synonym: larva - an immature, wingless, feeding stage of an insect

Etymology

From Latin 'larva' (ghost, mask; later, insect larva) + '-al'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɑːr.vəl / (AmE), / ˈlɑː.vəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the early immature stage of an animal, especially insects, that undergoes metamorphosis
relating to the immature stage of an animal
medical

Examples

The caterpillar is a classic example of a larval stage in butterflies.
Many diseases can affect larval fish more severely than adult ones.
Insects undergo dramatic changes from their larval form to adulthood.

Synset Examples

larval societies
larval crayfishes
the larval stage

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adult - fully developed and mature animal or organism

Etymology

From Latin 'larva' (ghost, mask), used in science for the immature stage of animals.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01493965-s
Synset POS: s
