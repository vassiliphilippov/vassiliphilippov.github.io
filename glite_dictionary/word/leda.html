Leda

1. PROPN

/ ˈliːdə / (AmE), / ˈliːdə / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the queen of Sparta who was raped by Zeus in the form of a swan, and who became the mother of Helen of Troy.
queen of Sparta in Greek mythology; mother of Helen of Troy

Examples

Leda is famously depicted in art with a swan, symbolizing her encounter with Zeus.
The story of Leda has inspired countless paintings, poems, and literary works.
According to myth, Leda gave birth to Helen, who would later become known as Helen of Troy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Helen of Troy - Daughter of Leda and Zeus, central figure whose abduction led to the Trojan War

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Λήδη (Lēdē).
