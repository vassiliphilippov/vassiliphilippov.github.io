ceratozamia

1. NOUN

/ sɛˌrætoʊˈzeɪmiə / (AmE), / sɛˌrætəʊˈzeɪmiə / (BrE)
A small tropical cycad of the genus Ceratozamia, native to Mexico, with a short scaly woody trunk, fernlike leaves, and woody cones.
a genus of small Mexican cycads

Examples

Several species of ceratozamia are cultivated as ornamental plants due to their attractive foliage.
The ceratozamia in the botanical garden is part of a conservation program for endangered cycads.
Native to Mexico, ceratozamia thrives in shaded, humid forest environments.

Etymology

From Greek 'kerato-' (horn) and 'zamia' (a genus of cycads), referring to features of its cones.
