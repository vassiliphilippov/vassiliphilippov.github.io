Ceratozamia

1. NOUN

/ sɛrˌætoʊˈzeɪmiə / (AmE), / sɛrˌatəʊˈzeɪmɪə / (BrE)
a small genus of Mexican cycads, sometimes classified in the family Cycadaceae
genus of Mexican cycads

Examples

Several species of Ceratozamia are cultivated as ornamental plants in botanical gardens.
The conservation of Ceratozamia is important due to its status as a threatened genus.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Ceratozamia genus in the mountains of Mexico.

Etymology

From Greek 'keras, keratos' (horn) and 'zamia' (pine cone), referring to the horn-like projections on some species' cones.
