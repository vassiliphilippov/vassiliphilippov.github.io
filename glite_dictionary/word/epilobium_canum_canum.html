Epilobium canum canum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈloʊbiəm ˈkænəm ˈkænəm / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈləʊbiəm ˈkanəm ˈkanəm / (BrE)
a shrub native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, known for its brilliant scarlet flowers
a scarlet-flowered shrub of southwestern US and Mexico

Examples

The Epilobium canum canum is prized in gardens for its vibrant scarlet blooms.
Botanists often study Epilobium canum canum due to its adaptation to arid climates.
Pollinators are attracted to the bright flowers of Epilobium canum canum during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Epilobium canum - the broader species of which Epilobium canum canum is a subspecies

Etymology

From Latin: Epilobium (a genus of plants) and canum ('gray'), referring to the plant's appearance.
