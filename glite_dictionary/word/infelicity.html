infelicity

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnfəˈlɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌɪnfɪˈlɪsəti / (BrE)
an inappropriate or awkward manner or style of expression, especially in speech or writing
awkward or inappropriate expression

Examples

His speech was marred by a single infelicity that made the audience uncomfortable.
The editor noted several stylistic infelicities in the draft that needed correction.
Her choice of words was an infelicity that distracted from the otherwise strong argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inappropriateness - the quality of being unsuitable for a particular situation
  • Antonym: felicity - pleasing and appropriate expression in language

Etymology

From Latin infelicitas (unhappiness, misfortune), from infelix (unhappy).
