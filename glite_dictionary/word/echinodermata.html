Echinodermata

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌkaɪnəˈdɜrmətə / (AmE), / ɪˌkɪnəʊˈdɜːmətə / (BrE)
a phylum of radially symmetrical marine invertebrates, which includes starfish, sea urchins, and sea cucumbers
phylum of marine invertebrates (starfish, sea urchins, etc.)

Examples

Scientists have discovered several new species within the Echinodermata in the deep sea.
The Echinodermata are known for their unique water vascular system and spiny skin.
Starfish, sea urchins, and sand dollars all belong to the Echinodermata.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: echinoderm - an individual member of the phylum Echinodermata

Etymology

From Greek ekhinos (sea urchin) + derma (skin).
