microcephalic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmaɪkroʊsəˈfælɪk / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊsɪˈfælɪk / (BrE)
having an abnormally small head, especially as a result of a medical condition affecting brain development
having an abnormally small head
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the child was microcephalic, meaning his head size was significantly below average for his age.
Infants born microcephalic often require special medical attention and developmental support.
While rare, a microcephalic condition can be caused by genetic disorders or prenatal exposure to certain infections.

Synset Examples

a nanocephalic dwarf

Related Terms

  • Antonym: macrocephalic - having an abnormally large head
  • Synonym: microcephaly - a medical condition involving abnormal smallness of the head

Etymology

From Greek mikros (small) + kephalē (head).
