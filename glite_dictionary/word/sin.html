sin

1. NOUN

/ sɪn / (AmE), / sɪn / (BrE)
an act regarded as a serious offense against divine law or God's will
offense against divine law

Examples

Many people seek forgiveness for their sins through prayer or confession.
Stealing is considered a sin in many religions.
He struggled with feelings of guilt over his past sins.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: crime - an act punishable by law as forbidden by statute or by common law

Etymology

From Old English 'synn', related to Old High German 'sunta' (sin), probably from a Proto-Germanic root.

2. NOUN

/ sɪn / (AmE), / sɪn / (BrE)
An act or state considered to be a breaking of divine law, resulting in estrangement from God.
violation of divine law; estrangement from God

Examples

The doctrine teaches that sin separates people from God.
She confessed her sins and sought forgiveness.
In many religions, prayer is a way to atone for sin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transgression - an act that goes against a law or code; offense, especially a moral or religious one
  • Antonym: virtue - behavior showing high moral standards

Etymology

From Old English 'synn', related to Old High German 'sunta', meaning wrongdoing or offense.

3. VERB

/ sɪn / (AmE), / sɪn / (BrE)
to commit a moral fault or serious error; to do something that is considered wrong
commit a moral fault or serious mistake

Examples

He knew he would sin if he lied on his tax return.
She felt she had sinned by betraying her friend's trust.
Many religions teach that everyone sins at some point in their lives.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunder - to make a careless or serious mistake
  • Synonym: to err - to make a mistake
  • Antonym: to repent - to feel or express sincere regret

Etymology

From Old English 'syngian', related to 'sin', from Proto-Germanic *sunjō (truth, sin).

5. NOUN

/ sɪn / (AmE), / sɪn / (BrE)
(in trigonometry) the ratio of the length of the side opposite a given angle to the length of the hypotenuse in a right-angled triangle.
trigonometric function (opposite/hypotenuse)

Examples

To find the height of the tree, use the sin of the angle and the distance from the base.
In mathematics, sin 90° is equal to 1.
He explained how the sin function relates angles to ratios in a right-angled triangle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cosine - trigonometric function: ratio of adjacent side to hypotenuse
  • Synonym: tangent - trigonometric function: ratio of opposite side to adjacent side

Etymology

Short for Latin 'sinus' (meaning 'curve, fold'); introduced through translation of Arabic 'jayb'.
