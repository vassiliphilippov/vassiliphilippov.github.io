Sin

1. PROPN

/ sɪn / (AmE), / sɪn / (BrE)
In Akkadian mythology, the god of the Moon, counterpart of the Sumerian deity Nanna.
Akkadian moon god

Examples

Archaeologists discovered ancient tablets referencing Sin as the main lunar deity of Akkadian civilization.
Many myths depict Sin traveling across the night sky in a radiant chariot.
The worship of Sin was especially prominent in the city of Ur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nanna - Sumerian god of the Moon

Etymology

Akkadian origin; related to Sumerian 'Nanna', both moon deities in Mesopotamian religions.

2. NOUN

/ sin / (AmE), / sin / (BrE)
The twenty-first letter of the Hebrew alphabet.
21st letter of the Hebrew alphabet

Examples

In Hebrew script, the letter Sin is distinguished by a dot placed over its left branch.
When studying Hebrew, learning the pronunciation of Sin is essential for correct reading.
The word for 'year' in Hebrew begins with the letter Sin.

Etymology

From Hebrew שִׂין (sin), a letter in the Hebrew alphabet.
