assonance

1. NOUN

/ ˈæs.ə.nəns / (AmE), / ˈæs.ənəns / (BrE)
The repetition of vowel sounds in nearby stressed syllables of successive words, often used in poetry for effect.
repetition of vowel sounds

Examples

The poet employed assonance to give the lines a musical quality.
Careful use of assonance enhances the imagery in many classic poems.
Students are asked to identify instances of assonance in the passage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alliteration - repetition of the same consonant sounds at the beginning of words
  • Synonym: consonance - repetition of identical or similar consonants in neighboring words

Etymology

From French 'assonance', from Latin 'assonare' (to sound in answer).
