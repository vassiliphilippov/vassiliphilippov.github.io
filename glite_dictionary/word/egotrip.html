to egotrip

1. VERB

/ ˈiːɡoʊˌtrɪp / (AmE), / ˈiːɡəʊˌtrɪp / (BrE)
to behave in a way that draws attention to oneself, often to satisfy one's ego
seek attention for oneself
informal

Examples

She tends to egotrip at meetings, making sure everyone hears her accomplishments.
The star athlete was accused of egotripping during the post-game interview.
If you egotrip too much at work, your colleagues might start to resent you.

Synset Examples

This teacher always egotrips and the students don't like him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to show off - to behave in a way intended to attract attention or admiration
  • Synonym: boast - to talk with excessive pride and self-satisfaction
  • Antonym: humble oneself - to act modestly or without seeking attention

Etymology

From 'ego' (Latin for 'I') and 'trip' (slang for experience), popularized in the 1960s.
