whoreson

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔːrˌsʌn / (AmE), / ˈhɔːsən / (BrE)
An illegitimate child; sometimes used as an insulting term.
illegitimate child (archaic, offensive)
archaic offensive

Examples

The old novel described the villain as a whoreson who plotted against the rightful heir.
Shakespeare's plays sometimes use whoreson as a cutting insult between characters.
He was unfairly judged by society for being a whoreson in those times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bastard - an illegitimate child

Etymology

From Middle English 'hore-son', combining 'whore' + 'son'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɔːr.sən / (AmE), / ˈhɔː.sən / (BrE)
an old-fashioned and insulting term for a person regarded with contempt, especially implying stupidity, irritation, or ridiculousness
insulting term for a contemptible person
archaic offensive

Examples

He called the troublesome servant a whoreson during the heated argument.
In the old play, the villain was repeatedly addressed as a whoreson by the protagonist.
The commander dismissed the cowardly soldier as a miserable whoreson in front of the entire troop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bastard - an offensive term for a contemptible person

Etymology

From Middle English (whore + son), literally 'son of a whore'.
