Actinia

1. NOUN

/ ækˈtɪniə / (AmE), / ækˈtɪniə / (BrE)
a genus of brightly colored sea anemones commonly found in coastal rock pools
a genus of sea anemones

Examples

The Actinia attached itself firmly to the rock as the tide receded.
Marine biologists often study Actinia to learn about tidal zone ecosystems.
The vibrant coloration of Actinia makes it a popular subject for underwater photography.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek aktis, aktin- (ray), referring to the radiating tentacles.

2. NOUN

/ ækˈtɪniə / (AmE), / ækˈtɪniə / (BrE)
A genus of sea anemones commonly found in rock pools, known for their brightly colored bodies and tentacles.
genus of sea anemones

Examples

The bright red Actinia stood out among the rocks where the tide had receded.
Marine biologists studied the behavior of Actinia in their natural habitat.
Many species in the genus Actinia are admired for their vivid coloration.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek aktis, aktinos (ray); the suffix -ia forms taxonomic names.
