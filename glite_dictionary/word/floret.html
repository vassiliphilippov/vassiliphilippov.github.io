floret

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɔːrət / (AmE), / ˈflɒrɪt / (BrE)
a small or diminutive flower, especially one forming part of a cluster or composite flower head
a small flower, especially part of a cluster

Examples

Each broccoli head is made up of dozens of tiny florets.
The daisy's central disk is composed of hundreds of individual florets.
She sprinkled a few edible florets over the salad for decoration.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: flower - the reproductive structure in flowering plants
  • Synonym: blossom - a flower or group of flowers, especially of fruit-bearing trees

Etymology

From Latin flos, floris (flower), with diminutive suffix -et.
