delectable

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈlɛktəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈlɛktəbl / (BrE)
(of a person) highly attractive in a way that arouses admiration or sexual desire.
sexually attractive; alluring (of a person)

Examples

The actor was praised for his delectable charm in the romantic comedy.
Rumors spread about the delectable new model featured in the ad campaign.
She became the focus of attention thanks to her delectable looks.

Synset Examples

the delectable Miss Haynes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alluring - powerfully attractive or charming
  • Antonym: repellent - unattractive or causing aversion

Etymology

From Latin delectabilis (delightful), from delectare (to delight).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈlɛktəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈlɛktəbl / (BrE)
(especially of food or drink) extremely pleasing to the taste; delicious.
delicious; extremely tasty

Examples

The chef prepared a delectable meal that everyone enjoyed.
She found the chocolate cake absolutely delectable and went back for seconds.
A plate of delectable pastries was set out for dessert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delicious - highly pleasant tasting or smelling; very tasty

Etymology

From Latin delectabilis (delightful), from delectare (to delight).

Synset POS: s
