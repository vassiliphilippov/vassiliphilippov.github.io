splendid

1. ADJ

/ ˈsplɛn.dɪd / (AmE), / ˈsplɛn.dɪd / (BrE)
impressive and grand in appearance, quality, or style; magnificent
impressive or magnificent

Examples

The palace was decorated in a splendid fashion for the royal wedding.
We attended a splendid ceremony in the ancient cathedral.
The museum houses a splendid collection of Renaissance paintings.

Synset Examples

the brilliant court life at Versailles
a glorious work of art
magnificent cathedrals
the splendid coronation ceremony

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnificent - impressive, grand or splendid in appearance or style
  • Synonym: glorious - having great beauty and splendor
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a low estimate of one's importance; modest

Etymology

From Latin splendĕre (to shine), via French splendide.
