disesteem

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪs.ɪˈstiːm / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.ɪˈstiːm / (BrE)
to have little or no respect for; to hold in low esteem or contempt
regard with little or no respect

Examples

She tends to disesteem any suggestion that challenges her ideas.
Some critics disesteem popular literature as lacking artistic value.
He openly disesteemed his rival's accomplishments in the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to despise - to feel contempt or a deep repugnance for
  • Antonym: esteem - to respect or admire greatly

Etymology

From ‘dis-’ (expressing reversal) + ‘esteem’, originally from Latin aestimare (to value).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.ɪˈstim / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.ɪˈstiːm / (BrE)
The state of being regarded with a lack of respect or approval; loss of esteem.
loss of respect or esteem

Examples

The politician fell into disesteem after the scandal became public.
His dishonest actions led to his disesteem among colleagues.
It took years for the company to recover from the disesteem caused by its unethical practices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disrespect - lack of respect or courtesy
  • Antonym: esteem - respect and admiration

Etymology

From dis- (expressing reversal) + esteem, ultimately from Latin 'aestimare' (to value).
