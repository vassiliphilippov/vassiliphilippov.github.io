mousey

1. ADJ

/ ˈmaʊsi / (AmE), / ˈmaʊsi / (BrE)
(of a place) infested with mice; having many mice present.
infested with mice

Examples

The abandoned warehouse became increasingly mousey as winter set in.
We had to move out because the old apartment was just too mousey for comfort.
That basement smells musty and appears a bit mousey after months of neglect.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmaʊsi / (AmE), / ˈmaʊsi / (BrE)
(of hair or color) having a dull, pale brown or greyish shade, like that of a mouse.
dull pale brown or grey color

Examples

She always felt her mousey hair made her look dull among her more glamorous friends.
He wore a mousey brown sweater that blended into the background.
The paint in the hallway had faded to a mousey shade after years without sunlight.

Synset Examples

a mousy brownish-grey color
mousy hair
mouse-colored hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drab - lacking brightness or interest; dull (especially color)
  • Synonym: brownish - somewhat brown in color
  • Antonym: vivid - producing powerful feelings or strong, clear images in the mind or having intense color

3. ADJ

/ ˈmaʊsi / (AmE), / ˈmaʊsi / (BrE)
(of a person) quiet, timid, and ineffectual in manner or appearance.
quiet, timid, and ineffectual

Examples

The mousey assistant rarely spoke up during meetings and preferred to stay in the background.
She has always been considered the mousey one in her extroverted family.
Despite his mousey demeanor, he had strong opinions that he kept to himself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timid - showing a lack of courage or confidence; easily frightened
  • Antonym: bold - showing willingness to take risks; confident and courageous

Etymology

From 'mouse' (the animal) with the suffix '-y', referring to mouse-like qualities.
