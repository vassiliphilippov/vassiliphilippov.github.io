absurdity

1. NOUN

/ əbˈsɝːdəti / (AmE), / əbˈsɜːdəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being ridiculous or wildly unreasonable; a statement or situation that goes against common sense or logic.
quality of being ridiculous or unreasonable

Examples

The absurdity of his suggestion made everyone laugh in disbelief.
She couldn't believe the absurdity of the rumors circulating in the office.
The play's humor lies in its deliberate absurdity and illogical situations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - speech or writing that has no meaning or makes no sense
  • Antonym: rationality - the quality of being based on reason or logic

Etymology

From Latin absurdus (out of tune, senseless).

2. NOUN

/ əbˈsɝːdəti / (AmE), / əbˈsɜːdəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being ridiculous, illogical, or wildly unreasonable
state of being ridiculous or unreasonable

Examples

The absurdity of his suggestion made everyone laugh.
Many political arguments descend into absurdity when they lack evidence.
She could not believe the absurdity of the situation she found herself in.

Synset Examples

the crowd laughed at the absurdity of the clown's behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - spoken or written words that have no meaning or make no sense
  • Antonym: rationality - the quality of being based on or in accordance with reason or logic

Etymology

From Latin 'absurditas', from 'absurdus' meaning 'out of tune, foolish'.
