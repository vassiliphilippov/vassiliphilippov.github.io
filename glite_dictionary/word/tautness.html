tautness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔtnəs / (AmE), / ˈtɔːtnəs / (BrE)
The state of being pulled or stretched tight; tension.
state of being stretched tight

Examples

The tautness of the rope made it safe to cross the ravine.
After hours of swimming, he could feel the tautness in his muscles.
The artist adjusted the tautness of the canvas before starting to paint.

Synset Examples

it places great tension on the leg muscles
he could feel the tenseness of her body

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tension - the state of being stretched tight
  • Antonym: slackness - the state of being loose or not tight

Etymology

From 'taut' (tight, stretched) + '-ness' (forming nouns of quality or state).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɔtnəs / (AmE), / ˈtɔːtnəs / (BrE)
the state of being stretched tightly, without any slack or looseness
tightness; lack of slack

Examples

The tautness of the rope ensured the tent would not collapse in the wind.
She admired the tautness of the drum skin as she tapped on it.
The dancer's posture conveyed a sense of tautness and control throughout the performance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: slackness - the state of being loose or not taut
  • Synonym: tightness - the quality of being tightly stretched or fitted

Etymology

From 'taut' + '-ness', where 'taut' is of uncertain origin, possibly from Old Norse 'thottr' (tight).
