Boswell

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɑz.wɛl / (AmE), / ˈbɒz.wəl / (BrE)
(1740–1795) A Scottish author best known for his acclaimed biography of Samuel Johnson.
Scottish author and biographer of Samuel Johnson

Examples

Many critics consider Boswell's biography of Samuel Johnson a masterpiece of English literature.
The historian quoted Boswell extensively in her analysis of eighteenth-century London society.
As a writer, Boswell was known for his keen observations and lively narrative style.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːz.wɛl / (AmE), / ˈbɒz.wəl / (BrE)
A devoted admirer and recorder of another person's words and deeds.
devoted follower and chronicler

Examples

She acted as his Boswell, faithfully recording every conversation and remark.
The writer found a Boswell in his assistant, who documented his every move for posterity.
He needed a Boswell to chronicle the behind-the-scenes moments of his campaign.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biographer - a person who writes an account of another person's life

Etymology

From James Boswell (1740–1795), Scottish biographer and diarist known for documenting Samuel Johnson's life.
