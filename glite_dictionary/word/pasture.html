pasture

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæstʃər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːstʃə / (BrE)
An area of land covered with grass or other plants, suitable for the grazing of livestock such as cattle or sheep.
grazing land for livestock

Examples

The cattle spent the afternoon grazing in the pasture near the river.
Farmers rotate their herds from one pasture to another to keep the grass healthy.
The rolling green pasture stretched as far as the eye could see.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meadow - a field of grass, especially one used for hay

Etymology

From Latin 'pastura' (a feeding, grazing), from 'pascere' (to feed, graze).

2. VERB

/ ˈpæstʃər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːstʃə / (BrE)
(of animals) to feed on grass in a meadow or pasture.
(of animals) feed on grass

Examples

Every day, the cows pasture in the fields behind the farmhouse.
In summer, the sheep pasture on the lush green hillsides.
If you pasture your goats here, they will have plenty to eat.

Synset Examples

the herd was grazing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graze - (of animals) to feed on growing grass

Etymology

From Old French *pasture*, from Latin *pastura* (grazing, feeding).

Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ ˈpæstʃər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːstʃə / (BrE)
to put animals in a field or meadow so they can eat the grass
let animals feed in a field

Examples

The farmer pastures his sheep on the hillside every summer.
They decided to pasture the cattle in the southeast meadow this year.
After the storm, the horses were finally pastured again in the green fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graze - to feed on growing grass

Etymology

From Latin 'pastura' (grazing), from 'pastus', past participle of 'pasci' (to feed).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01579309-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ ˈpæs.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːs.tʃə / (BrE)
bulky food such as grass or hay provided for grazing animals like horses or cattle
food for grazing animals (grass/hay)

Examples

The cattle thrived on the rich pasture provided by the farm.
In winter, the lack of fresh pasture made feeding the horses more challenging.
She checked to ensure the sheep had enough pasture to last through the week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forage - food for animals, especially dried grass or hay
  • Synonym: feed - food given to animals
  • Synonym: fodder - coarse food for livestock, such as hay or straw

Etymology

From Latin *pastura* (grazing), from *pastus* (past participle of *pasci*, to feed).
