Edam

1. NOUN

/ ˈiː.dæm / (AmE), / ˈiː.dæm / (BrE)
a mild yellow Dutch cheese, usually formed into balls and encased in a red wax coating.
Dutch mild cheese with red wax rind

Examples

She ordered a sandwich with Edam and tomatoes for lunch.
At the market, we bought a round of Edam for the picnic.
The cheese platter featured brie, stilton, and Edam among others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gouda - a mild Dutch yellow cheese with a smooth texture

Etymology

From Edam, a town in the Netherlands where the cheese originated.
