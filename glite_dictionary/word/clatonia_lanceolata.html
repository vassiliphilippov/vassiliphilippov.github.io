Clatonia lanceolata

1. NOUN

/ kləˈtoʊniə ˌlænsiəˈleɪtə / (AmE), / kləˈtəʊniə ˌlansɪəˈleɪtə / (BrE)
A small, slender North American plant with a pair of succulent leaves at the middle of the stem and a raceme of white, pink, or rose bowl-shaped flowers, bearing an edible corm.
a North American plant with edible corm and bowl-shaped flowers

Examples

The bulbs of Clatonia lanceolata are sometimes foraged as a food source in spring.
Botanists admire the delicate blossoms of Clatonia lanceolata found in woodland meadows.
Clatonia lanceolata prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial shade for optimal growth.
