vestment

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛstmənt / (AmE), / ˈvɛstmənt / (BrE)
a ceremonial garment, especially one worn by clergy during religious services
ceremonial garment worn by clergy

Examples

The bishop donned his elaborate vestment before leading the service.
Ancient manuscripts describe the symbolic meaning of each vestment worn by priests.
She admired the colorful vestment displayed in the cathedral's museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robe - a long, loose outer garment, often worn for ceremonial occasions
  • Synonym: habit - distinctive attire worn by members of a religious order

Etymology

From Latin 'vestimentum' (clothing, garment), from 'vestire' (to clothe).
