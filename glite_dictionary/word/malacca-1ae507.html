malacca

1. NOUN

/ məˈlækə / (AmE), / məˈlækə / (BrE)
the stem of a rattan palm, especially Calamus scipionum, used for making canes and umbrella handles
stem of rattan palm used for canes, handles

Examples

He walked with a malacca cane, which was both sturdy and elegant.
The umbrella had a handle made from polished malacca.
Authentic malacca is prized for its strength and light weight in walking sticks.

Etymology

From the Malacca region in Malaysia, where the rattan palm is found.

2. NOUN

/ məˈlækə / (AmE), / məˈlakə / (BrE)
a walking stick or cane made from the stem of a rattan palm, especially one from Southeast Asia
cane from the stem of a rattan palm

Examples

He leaned on his malacca as he strolled through the park.
The old gentleman prized his antique malacca for its strength and elegance.
She bought a fashionable malacca cane while traveling in Malaysia.

Etymology

From the name Malacca (now Melaka), a region of Malaysia historically associated with the export of rattan canes.
