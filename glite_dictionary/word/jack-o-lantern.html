jack-o'-lantern

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæk əˌlæn tɚn / (AmE), / ˈdʒæk əˌlæn tən / (BrE)
A pale, mysterious light sometimes seen at night over marshy ground, believed in folklore to be caused by spirits or natural phenomena.
mysterious marsh light in folklore

Examples

We caught a glimpse of a jack-o'-lantern flickering above the bog.
Legends say the jack-o'-lantern leads travelers astray at night.
Scientists have tried to explain the jack-o'-lantern as a result of marsh gases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: will-o'-the-wisp - a phosphorescent light seen at night over marshy ground, synonymous with this sense

Etymology

Early 17th century: from 'Jack' (a generic name for a man) and 'lantern', originally referring to a night watchman with a lantern; later transferred to the mysterious light.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæk oʊ ˌlæntɚn / (AmE), / ˈdʒak əʊ ˌlæntən / (BrE)
A lantern made by hollowing out a pumpkin, carving a face into it, and placing a light inside, typically used as a decoration at Halloween.
carved pumpkin lantern for Halloween

Examples

We placed a jack-o'-lantern on our porch for Halloween.
She carefully carved a scary face into the jack-o'-lantern.
The candle inside the jack-o'-lantern flickered in the dark.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: lantern - a portable light source with a protective enclosure

Etymology

From the term 'Jack of the lantern,' originally referring to a night watchman or mysterious light; now used for carved pumpkin lanterns at Halloween.
