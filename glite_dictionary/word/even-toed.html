even-toed

1. ADJ

/ ˈivən toʊd / (AmE), / ˈiːvən təʊd / (BrE)
(of a mammal) belonging to or relating to animals of the order Artiodactyla, characterized by having an even number of functional toes on each foot.
having an even number of toes (esp. mammals, Artiodactyla)

Examples

The giraffe is an even-toed ungulate, sharing this trait with cows and deer.
Scientists studied the adaptation of even-toed animals to different environments.
Unlike horses, which are odd-toed, pigs are classified as even-toed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artiodactyl - a hoofed mammal of the order Artiodactyla
