Cirsium discolor

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜr.si.əm dɪsˈkʌl.ər / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.si.əm dɪsˈkʌl.ə / (BrE)
A stout North American thistle with purplish-pink flower heads, commonly known as the field thistle.
a North American field thistle

Examples

On our hike, we saw several Cirsium discolor growing along the trail near the meadow.
The Cirsium discolor attracts butterflies and bees with its vibrant flower heads.
Gardeners sometimes plant Cirsium discolor to encourage native pollinators in their gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thistle - a prickly plant, typically with purple flowers

Etymology

From Latin: 'Cirsium' (a thistle genus) and 'discolor' meaning 'of different colors'.
