dynamometer

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪnəˈmɑmətər / (AmE), / ˌdʌɪnəˈmɒmɪtə / (BrE)
a device used to measure force, torque, or power, especially in engines or machines
device for measuring force or power

Examples

A dynamometer is often used to test the strength of a car engine.
The technician recorded the vehicle's horsepower using a dynamometer.
To assess grip strength, the doctor asked her to squeeze the dynamometer tightly.

Etymology

From Greek 'dynamis' (power) and 'metron' (measure).
