paging

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpeɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of calling out the name of a person, especially using a loudspeaker system, to summon their attention
calling someone's name over a loudspeaker

Examples

The nurse used paging to call the doctor to the emergency room.
Frequent paging over the intercom kept the staff informed about arrivals.
Please refrain from unnecessary paging unless it is an emergency.

Synset Examples

the public address system in the hospital was used for paging

Related Terms

  • Synonym: announcement - a public or formal statement about a fact or event

Etymology

From the verb 'page' (to summon by name), which possibly derives from the occupation 'page' (a young attendant or messenger).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpeɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
the arrangement or system of assigning numbers to the pages of a book, document, or publication
system for numbering pages

Examples

The paging in this journal follows a continuous sequence throughout the year.
Incorrect paging can cause confusion when referencing sections of a report.
She checked the paging before sending the manuscript to the publisher.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pagination - the process of dividing a document into discrete pages, especially with numbering

Etymology

From 'page' + '-ing', referring to the process or system involving pages.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06269333-n
Synset POS: n
