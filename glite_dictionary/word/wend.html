to wend

1. VERB

/ wɛnd / (AmE), / wɛnd / (BrE)
to go in a particular direction, usually in a slow or purposeful way
travel or proceed in a direction

Examples

They wend their way through the dense forest, following the narrow trail.
After the concert, the crowd slowly wended toward the exit.
She was wending her way home when she saw the beautiful sunset.

Synset Examples

wend your way through the crowds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to proceed - to move forward or continue along a course
  • Degree Lower: meander - to follow a winding or indirect course

Etymology

Old English 'wendan' (to turn, go), related to German 'wenden'.
