B0

1. NOUN

/ bi ˈzɪəroʊ / (AmE), / biː ˈzɪərəʊ / (BrE)
A standard paper size measuring 1000 x 1414 millimeters, used mainly in technical and printing contexts.
standard paper size (1000 x 1414 mm)

Examples

The architectural drawings were printed on B0 sheets for easier viewing.
She ordered a stack of B0 paper for the large-format printer.
Due to its size, B0 is rarely used for regular office documents.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: A0 - standard paper size (841 x 1189 mm) in the A series
  • Degree Lower: B1 - standard paper size (707 x 1000 mm) in the B series, one size smaller than B0

Etymology

From the ISO 216 international paper size standard, where 'B' denotes the series and '0' refers to the largest size in that series.
