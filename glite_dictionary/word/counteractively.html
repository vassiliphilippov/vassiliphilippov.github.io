counteractively

1. ADV

/ ˌkaʊntərˈæktɪvli / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntəˈræktɪvli / (BrE)
In a way that counteracts or works against something else.
in a counteractive manner

Examples

The medication works counteractively to neutralize the effects of the poison.
He spoke counteractively, attempting to reverse the damage done by the earlier speech.
The two policies operate counteractively, reducing the effectiveness of each other.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counteractive - having the effect of counteracting

Etymology

From 'counteractive' + '-ly'. 'Counteractive' ultimately from Latin 'contra' (against) + 'agere' (to do).
