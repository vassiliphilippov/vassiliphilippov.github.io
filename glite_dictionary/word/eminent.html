eminent

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛm.ə.nənt / (AmE), / ˈɛm.ɪ.nənt / (BrE)
(of a person) famous, respected, or distinguished within a particular sphere; prominent and of high rank or quality.
distinguished; of high rank or quality

Examples

She is an eminent scientist known for her groundbreaking research.
Several eminent lawyers spoke at the international conference.
The prize was awarded to an eminent member of the academic community.

Synset Examples

people in high places
the high priest
eminent members of the community

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distinguished - successful, authoritative, and commanding great respect
  • Antonym: obscure - not well known; not prominent or distinguished

Etymology

From Latin *eminentem* (standing out, projecting), present participle of *eminere* (to stand out).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɛm.ə.nənt / (AmE), / ˈɛm.ɪ.nənt / (BrE)
(of a structure or natural feature) rising above others in height; prominent or lofty.
standing out in height; prominent

Examples

The hikers struggled to reach the eminent summit before sunset.
The eminent cliffs provided breathtaking views of the coastline.
From afar, the eminent tower dominated the city's skyline.

Synset Examples

an eminent peak
lofty mountains
the soaring spires of the cathedral
towering icebergs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lofty - of imposing height; tall and impressive
  • Synonym: towering - extremely tall, especially in comparison with surroundings
  • Antonym: low - situated or passing below the normal level or height

Etymology

From Latin eminent- 'jutting, projecting', from the verb eminere.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01208145-s
Synset POS: s
