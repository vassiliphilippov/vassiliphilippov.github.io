Torreya californica

1. NOUN

/ tɔːˈreɪ.ə ˌkæ.ləˈfɔːr.nɪ.kə / (AmE), / tɒˈreɪ.ə ˌkæl.ɪˈfɔː.nɪ.kə / (BrE)
an evergreen tree native to California, with sharp-pointed leaves and a fruit resembling a nutmeg but tasting of turpentine
evergreen tree native to California

Examples

The Torreya californica is often planted as an ornamental tree in western gardens.
Botanists studied the leaves and seeds of Torreya californica to understand its adaptation.
The fruit of Torreya californica is not commonly eaten due to its strong turpentine flavor.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Torreya', named after American botanist John Torrey, and 'californica', meaning 'of California'.
