biform

1. ADJ

/ ˈbaɪ.fɔrm / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.fɔːm / (BrE)
having or combining two distinct forms
having two forms

Examples

The scientist studied a biform fungus that alternates between two shapes.
The statue's biform appearance reflected both human and animal features.
Some minerals crystallize in a biform manner depending on temperature.

Synset Examples

a biform crystal
the biform body of a mermaid

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uniform - having only one form

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'forma' (form, shape).
