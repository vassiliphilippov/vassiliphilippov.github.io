to pacify

1. VERB

/ ˈpæsəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈpasɪfʌɪ / (BrE)
to make someone less angry or hostile; to calm or soothe someone who is upset
to calm or soothe

Examples

The manager tried to pacify the frustrated customers by offering them a discount.
After hearing his side of the story, she was able to pacify her angry friend.
The parent spoke softly to pacify the crying child in the store.

Synset Examples

She managed to mollify the angry customer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mollify - to soothe or calm someone's feelings
  • Antonym: anger - a strong feeling of annoyance or hostility

Etymology

From Latin pacificare, from pax (peace) + facere (to make).

2. VERB

/ ˈpæsɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈpæsɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to bring peace to a place by ending conflict or violence, often through intervention or force
bring peace by ending violence

Examples

The government sent troops to pacify the troubled region after months of unrest.
International organizations often step in to pacify war-torn areas and help rebuild communities.
Efforts to pacify the city included peace talks and humanitarian aid.

Synset Examples

The U.N. troops are working to pacify Bosnia

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calm - to make calm or tranquil
  • Antonym: to agitate - to stir up or provoke (opposite of pacify)

Etymology

From Latin pacificare, from pax (peace) + facere (to make).
