hoosgow

1. NOUN

/ ˈhusˌɡaʊ / (AmE), / ˈhuːz.ɡaʊ / (BrE)
(slang) A jail or prison.
(slang) jail; prison
slang

Examples

After the bar fight, he spent the night in the hoosgow until things cooled down.
She threatened to call the police and have him thrown in the hoosgow.
Local legends say the old hoosgow in town is haunted by former inmates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jail - a place for the confinement of people accused or convicted of a crime
  • Synonym: prison - a building where people are kept as punishment for a crime

Etymology

From Mexican Spanish 'juzgado' (court of law), ultimately from Spanish 'juzgar' (to judge).
