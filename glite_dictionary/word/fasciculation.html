fasciculation

1. NOUN

/ fəˌsɪkjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / fəˌsɪkjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a brief, involuntary twitching of a group of muscle fibers, often visible under the skin
involuntary muscle twitch
medical

Examples

The neurologist noted visible fasciculation in the patient's calf muscles during the examination.
Muscle fasciculation can be a symptom of nerve damage or fatigue.
After intense exercise, some people may experience brief episodes of fasciculation.

Etymology

From Latin 'fasciculus' (a small bundle) + English suffix '-ation'.
