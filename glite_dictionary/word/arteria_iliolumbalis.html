arteria iliolumbalis

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈtɪəriə ˌɪlioʊˈlʌmbəlɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə ˌɪliəʊˈlʌmbəlɪs / (BrE)
An artery that branches from the internal iliac artery and supplies blood to the pelvic muscles and bones.
pelvic artery supplying muscles and bones
medical

Examples

The arteria iliolumbalis is essential for providing blood flow to certain pelvic structures.
Damage to the arteria iliolumbalis during surgery can lead to complications involving the pelvic muscles.
In some cases, the arteria iliolumbalis may give rise to additional branches supplying nearby tissues.

Etymology

From Latin: 'arteria' (artery) + 'ilio-' (ilium, a part of the pelvis) + 'lumbalis' (of the loins).
