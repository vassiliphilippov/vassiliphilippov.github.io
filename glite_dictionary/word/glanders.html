glanders

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlændɚz / (AmE), / ˈɡlændəz / (BrE)
a contagious and often fatal bacterial disease of horses, which can also be transmitted to humans
disease of horses, transmittable to humans

Examples

The veterinary lab confirmed that the horse was suffering from glanders.
Glanders has been virtually eradicated in many countries, but isolated outbreaks still occur.
The stable was quarantined after several animals showed symptoms of glanders.

Etymology

From Middle French 'glandres', plural of 'glandre' (gland, swelling).
