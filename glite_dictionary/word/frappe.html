frappe

1. NOUN

/ fræˈpeɪ / (AmE), / fræˈpeɪ / (BrE)
A drink made by pouring liqueur over shaved ice, sometimes with added flavorings.
liqueur over shaved ice

Examples

After dinner, she enjoyed a frappe with a splash of coffee liqueur.
The bartender served a refreshing frappe topped with orange zest.
They ordered a chocolate frappe as a cool treat on a summer evening.

Etymology

From French frappé, past participle of frapper (to hit or chill), originally referring to a drink served over ice.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfræp / (AmE), / ˈfræpeɪ / (BrE)
A thick milkshake made with ice cream, especially as served in New England, USA.
a thick milkshake with ice cream

Examples

In New England, a frappe always contains ice cream, unlike a regular milkshake.
She ordered a chocolate frappe to cool off on the hot afternoon.
The diner is famous for its thick and creamy frappes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milkshake - a cold drink made of milk, a sweet flavouring such as fruit or chocolate, and usually ice cream, mixed together until it is thick and creamy

Etymology

From French frappé, meaning 'iced' or 'chilled'.

3. NOUN

/ fræˈpeɪ / (AmE), / fraˈpeɪ / (BrE)
A frozen dessert made with fruit flavoring, especially one that contains no milk.
frozen fruit dessert, no milk

Examples

On hot summer days, a cold frappe is the perfect way to cool down.
The restaurant offered a raspberry frappe as a light, refreshing dessert.
Unlike ice cream, a traditional frappe contains no milk or cream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorbet - a frozen dessert made with sweetened fruit juice or puree, no dairy
  • Antonym: ice cream - a sweet frozen food made with milk or cream, sugar, and flavorings

Etymology

From French 'frappé', past participle of 'frapper' (to hit, chill), referring to a drink or dessert that is chilled or iced.
