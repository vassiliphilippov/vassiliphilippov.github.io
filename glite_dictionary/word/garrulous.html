garrulous

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡærələs / (AmE), / ˈɡærʊləs / (BrE)
excessively talkative, especially about trivial or unimportant matters
excessively talkative, about trivial things

Examples

My garrulous aunt could entertain guests for hours with her endless stories.
The garrulous tour guide distracted us from the scenery with constant chatter.
He grew more garrulous after a few drinks at the party.

Synset Examples

kept from her housework by gabby neighbors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talkative - tending to talk a lot
  • Antonym: reticent - not revealing one's thoughts readily; reserved

Etymology

From Latin 'garrulus' (talkative), from 'garrīre' (to chatter).
