lazaret

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæzəˈrɛt / (AmE), / ˌlæzəˈrɛt / (BrE)
a hospital or quarantine station for people with infectious diseases, especially leprosy
hospital for infectious diseases

Examples

The island once housed a lazaret to isolate patients with contagious diseases.
During the outbreak, authorities converted old warehouses into a temporary lazaret.
Historical records describe the harsh conditions inside the lazaret for those suffering from leprosy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leprosarium - hospital for people with leprosy
  • Synonym: quarantine - isolation to prevent spread of disease

Etymology

From Italian 'lazzaretto', ultimately from the biblical figure Lazarus, associated with leprosy.
