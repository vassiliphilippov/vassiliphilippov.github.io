megabyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛɡəˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɛɡəˌbaɪt / (BrE)
a unit of digital information equal to 1,000,000 bytes or 1,000 kilobytes, commonly used to measure computer memory or data size.
unit of digital information (1,000,000 bytes)

Examples

My phone's storage capacity is 64 megabytes.
The document was too large to email because it was over five megabytes.
This photo only takes up two megabytes of space on my computer.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gigabyte - a unit of digital information equal to 1,000,000,000 bytes
  • Degree Lower: kilobyte - a unit of digital information equal to 1,000 bytes

Etymology

From Greek 'mega-' (large) and 'byte' (a unit of digital information).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛɡəˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɛɡəbaɪt / (BrE)
A unit of digital information equal to 1,048,576 bytes (1024 kibibytes), commonly used to measure data size in computing.
unit of digital information (1,048,576 bytes)

Examples

My phone only has 500 megabytes of free storage left.
Downloading the update requires at least 100 megabytes of space on your device.
A single high-resolution photo can take up several megabytes of memory.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gigabyte - a unit of digital information equal to 1,073,741,824 bytes
  • Degree Lower: kilobyte - a unit of digital information equal to 1,024 bytes

Etymology

From mega- (‘million’) + byte, coined in the 1970s alongside computer technology development.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
