Picus viridis

1. PROPN

/ ˈpaɪkəs vəˈraɪdɪs / (AmE), / ˈpɪkʊs vɪˈriːdɪs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the European green woodpecker, a species found in Europe and western Asia.
European green woodpecker (species)

Examples

The Picus viridis is easily identified by its green plumage and red crown.
Unlike other woodpeckers, Picus viridis prefers to forage for ants on the ground.
Researchers tagged several Picus viridis individuals to study their migration patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: green woodpecker - Common English name for Picus viridis

Etymology

From Latin: picus (woodpecker) + viridis (green).
