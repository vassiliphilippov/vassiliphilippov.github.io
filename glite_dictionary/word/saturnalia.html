Saturnalia

1. NOUN

/ ˌsætəˈneɪliə / (AmE), / ˌsatəˈneɪlɪə / (BrE)
An ancient Roman festival held in honor of the god Saturn, characterized by feasting, revelry, and a temporary overturning of social norms.
orgiastic festival in ancient Rome

Examples

During the Saturnalia, slaves and masters would often switch places in a symbolic reversal of roles.
Many historians consider Christmas traditions to have been influenced by the Roman Saturnalia.
The novelist described the party's wild excesses as reminiscent of a modern Saturnalia.

Etymology

From Latin Saturnalia, the name of the festival honoring Saturn.
