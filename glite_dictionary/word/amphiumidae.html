Amphiumidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌæm.fiˈjuː.mɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌam.fɪˈjuː.mɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of aquatic salamanders commonly known as amphiumas or Congo snakes, native to southeastern North America.
family of aquatic salamanders

Examples

The Amphiumidae are a family of eel-like salamanders found in the southeastern United States.
Scientists studying the Amphiumidae have discovered their unique method of respiration in low-oxygen waters.
Herpetologists often debate the evolutionary origins of the Amphiumidae due to their unusual limb structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amphiuma - any species within the Amphiumidae family, aquatic salamanders

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Amphiuma plus the standard zoological suffix -idae for families.

2. NOUN

/ æmˌfjuːmiˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / amˌfjuːmɪˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of aquatic salamanders, commonly known as Congo snakes, native to the southeastern United States
family of aquatic salamanders (Congo snakes)

Examples

The Amphiumidae are distinguished by their elongated bodies and reduced limbs.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Amphiumidae in a southern swamp.
The Amphiumidae are sometimes called Congo eels, though they are actually salamanders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Amphiuma - the type genus of the family Amphiumidae

Etymology

From the genus name Amphiuma, with the New Latin suffix -idae denoting a zoological family.
