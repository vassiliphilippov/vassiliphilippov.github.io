Bryan Donkin

1. PROPN

/ ˈbraɪən ˈdɑnkɪn / (AmE), / ˈbraɪən ˈdɒŋkɪn / (BrE)
(1768–1855) English engineer known for developing a process for preserving food by sterilizing it with heat and sealing it in a steel container, leading to the invention of the first tin can.
English engineer; inventor of the tin can

Examples

Bryan Donkin played a pivotal role in revolutionizing food preservation with his invention of the tin can.
The legacy of Bryan Donkin continues in today's global food packaging industry.
Historians often credit Bryan Donkin as a pioneer of modern canning technology.
