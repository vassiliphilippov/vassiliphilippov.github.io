en passant

1. ADV

/ ɑn pɑːˈsɑ̃ / (AmE), / ɒ̃ pɑːˈsɑ̃ / (BrE)
incidentally or in the course of doing something else; as a side remark.
incidentally; in passing

Examples

She mentioned, en passant, that she had visited Paris last year.
The professor noted en passant that the topic would appear on the exam.
He criticized the policy en passant, without making it the focus of his argument.

Synset Examples

he made this remark in passing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in passing - casually or incidentally during the course of something else

Etymology

From French en passant (in passing).

2. NOUN

/ ɑn pɑˈsɑnt / (AmE), / ɒ̃ paˈsɑ̃ / (BrE)
(in chess) a move in which a pawn capturing an opponent's pawn as it moves two squares forward from its starting position, as if it had moved only one square.
special pawn capture in chess

Examples

His pawn advanced two squares, but I took it en passant on my next move.
Many beginners overlook the en passant rule when learning chess.
She won the game by capturing the opponent's pawn en passant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pawn - a chess piece of lowest value

Etymology

French, meaning 'in passing'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00167686-n
Synset POS: n
