jailor

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒeɪ.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪ.lə / (BrE)
a person in charge of guarding prisoners in a jail; a jailer
person who guards prisoners

Examples

The jailor unlocked the heavy iron door for the morning inspection.
After years of working as a jailor, he became familiar with prison routines.
The jailor was responsible for ensuring no one escaped from the cell block.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jailer - person in charge of guarding prisoners in a jail
  • Antonym: prisoner - a person who is kept in prison

Etymology

From Old French *jaioleor*, from *jaiole* (jail).
