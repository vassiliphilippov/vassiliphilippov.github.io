talc

1. NOUN

/ tælk / (AmE), / tælk / (BrE)
a soft, fine-grained mineral composed of hydrated magnesium silicate, often used in powder form in cosmetics and other products
soft mineral used in powders and cosmetics

Examples

The geologist identified the rock sample as talc due to its soft texture.
Baby powder typically contains talc as a main ingredient.
Because talc is so soft, it can be easily scratched with a fingernail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talcum powder - a cosmetic or medicinal powder containing talc

Etymology

From Arabic 'ṭalq', via Medieval Latin 'talc'.

2. VERB

/ tælk / (AmE), / tælk / (BrE)
to apply talcum powder to the skin, especially to reduce moisture or prevent irritation
apply talcum powder to the skin

Examples

After her shower, she talced her legs to keep them dry.
Some parents prefer to talc their babies after a bath to prevent diaper rash.
He always talcs his feet before putting on his socks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powder - to apply a fine, dry substance, often to the body

Etymology

From the noun 'talc,' ultimately from Arabic 'ṭalq' (a kind of mica), via Medieval Latin and French.
