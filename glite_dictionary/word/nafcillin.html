nafcillin

1. NOUN

/ næfˈsɪlɪn / (AmE), / nafˈsɪlɪn / (BrE)
a penicillinase-resistant antibiotic used to treat infections caused by penicillin-resistant staphylococci
a penicillinase-resistant antibiotic
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed nafcillin to treat the patient's resistant staphylococcal infection.
Patients allergic to penicillin should not take nafcillin without consulting their physician.
Because the bacteria were resistant, nafcillin was chosen as the appropriate antibiotic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penicillin - a group of antibiotics derived from Penicillium fungi

Etymology

From 'na' for sodium and 'ficillin', modeled on penicillin nomenclature.
