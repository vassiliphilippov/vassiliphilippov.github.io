Linum

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪnəm / (AmE), / ˈlɪnəm / (BrE)
a genus of herbaceous plants in the family Linaceae, including the flax, with small sessile leaves
genus of herbaceous plants; includes flax

Examples

The genus Linum includes the cultivated flax, an important source of linen and linseed oil.
Several wild species of Linum are found across Europe and North America.
Botanists have studied Linum to better understand plant fiber production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flax - the common species Linum usitatissimum, source of linen and linseed

Etymology

From Latin Linum (flax, linen), originally from Greek línon (flax).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪnəm / (AmE), / ˈlaɪnəm / (BrE)
A genus of herbaceous plants in the family Linaceae, including plants with small, sessile leaves such as flax.
genus of herbaceous plants; includes flax

Examples

Many species in the Linum genus are cultivated for their attractive flowers.
Common Linum plants such as flax are important for producing linen and linseed oil.
Botanists study the Linum genus to understand its evolutionary relationships within Linaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flax - plant grown for its fiber and seeds, especially Linum usitatissimum

Etymology

From Latin *linum* (flax).
