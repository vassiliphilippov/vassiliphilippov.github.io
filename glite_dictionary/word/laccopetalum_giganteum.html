Laccopetalum giganteum

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæk.oʊ.pəˈtæl.əm dʒaɪˈgæn.ti.əm / (AmE), / ˌlak.əʊ.pəˈtal.əm dʒɪˈɡæn.tɪ.əm / (BrE)
A spectacular perennial plant native to the wet montane grasslands of Peru, previously classified in the genus Ranunculus.
Peruvian perennial plant species

Examples

Researchers discovered a rare specimen of Laccopetalum giganteum high in the Peruvian Andes.
The dazzling flowers of Laccopetalum giganteum make it a sought-after plant among botanical enthusiasts.
Laccopetalum giganteum was previously classified under the genus Ranunculus by early botanists.

Etymology

From Latin roots: 'laccos' (Greek, meaning 'pond' or 'lake') + 'petalum' (petal) and 'giganteum' (gigantic, from Latin 'giganteus').
