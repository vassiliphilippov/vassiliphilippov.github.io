well-found

1. ADJ

/ ˌwɛlˈfaʊnd / (AmE), / ˌwɛlˈfaʊnd / (BrE)
Having all the necessary or suitable equipment, furnishings, or provisions; especially well equipped or fitted out.
having ample suitable equipment or furnishings

Examples

Their yacht was well-found and able to handle the long voyage.
Every classroom in the new building is well-found with modern technology and supplies.
The hotel was well-found, offering its guests every imaginable comfort.

Synset Examples

a well-appointed kitchen
a well-appointed apartment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: well-appointed - having attractive and complete furnishings or equipment

Etymology

Formed from 'well' (satisfactorily) + 'found' (from 'to find', meaning furnished or equipped).
