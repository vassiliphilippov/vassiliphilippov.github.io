Phaseolus vulgaris

1. NOUN

/ fəˈsiː.ə.ləs vʌlˈɡɛər.ɪs / (AmE), / fəˈsiː.ələs vʌlˈɡɑː.rɪs / (BrE)
a species of bean plant, commonly known as the common bean, grown worldwide for its edible seeds and pods
the common bean plant

Examples

Phaseolus vulgaris is the scientific name for the plant that produces common green beans.
Farmers in many countries cultivate Phaseolus vulgaris for its nutritious beans.
Many traditional dishes around the world include varieties of Phaseolus vulgaris as a key ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common bean - another name for Phaseolus vulgaris, or its edible seeds

Etymology

From Latin: Phaseolus (a kind of bean) + vulgaris (common).
