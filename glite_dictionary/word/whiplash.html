whiplash

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪpˌlæʃ / (AmE), / ˈwɪp.læʃ / (BrE)
An injury to the neck caused by a sudden and forceful movement of the head, especially from rapid acceleration or deceleration such as in a car accident.
neck injury from sudden movement
medical

Examples

She suffered a mild case of whiplash after the rear-end collision.
The doctor diagnosed him with whiplash and recommended rest and physical therapy.
Car accidents are a common cause of whiplash injuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neck injury - damage or trauma to the neck area

Etymology

From 'whip' + 'lash', referring to the quick, snapping movement akin to the motion of a whip.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪpˌlæʃ / (AmE), / ˈwɪp.læʃ / (BrE)
a sharp, quick blow delivered with a whip or a whip-like object
a sharp blow from a whip

Examples

The horse flinched as the whiplash struck its side.
You could see the red mark left by the whiplash on his arm.
The cruel whiplash echoed through the silent stable.

Synset Examples

the whip raised a red welt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lash - a sharp blow with a whip or rope

Etymology

From 'whip' + 'lash', indicating the movement and effect of a whip.
