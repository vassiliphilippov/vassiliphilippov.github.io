conclusively

1. ADV

/ kənˈkluːsɪvli / (AmE), / kənˈkluːsɪvli / (BrE)
In a way that proves something and leaves no doubt; decisively and finally.
in a way that leaves no doubt

Examples

The results of the experiment proved conclusively that the theory was correct.
She demonstrated conclusively that she was the rightful heir to the estate.
The investigation ended conclusively, leaving no room for further questions.

Synset Examples

we settled the problem conclusively

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finally - as the last in a series; in a final manner
  • Synonym: decisively - in a way that settles an issue convincingly
  • Antonym: tentatively - in a way that is not certain or fixed; provisionally

Etymology

From 'conclusive' + '-ly'. 'Conclusive' derives from Latin 'concludere' (to close, to conclude).
