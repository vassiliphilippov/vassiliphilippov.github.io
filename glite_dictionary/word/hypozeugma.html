hypozeugma

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpoʊˈzuːɡmə / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəʊˈzjuːɡmə / (BrE)
A rhetorical device in which a series of subjects share a single predicate.
rhetorical device: several subjects, one predicate

Examples

In her speech, she used hypozeugma to connect varying topics under one powerful conclusion.
The teacher explained hypozeugma by writing several subjects leading into a single verb on the board.
Writers sometimes use hypozeugma for stylistic effect, giving clarity and emphasis to the predicate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zeugma - Rhetorical device where a word applies to multiple parts of the sentence

Etymology

From Greek 'hypozeugnumi' (to yoke beneath).
