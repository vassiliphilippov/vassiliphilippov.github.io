Helodermatidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːləʊˌdɜːrməˈtaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌhiːləʊˌdɜːməˈtɪədiː / (BrE)
a family of venomous lizards native to North and Central America, including the Gila monster and the beaded lizard
family of venomous lizards

Examples

The Helodermatidae family includes the Gila monster and the Mexican beaded lizard.
Scientists study the venom properties of Helodermatidae for possible medical applications.
Only members of Helodermatidae are known to be venomous among lizards.

Etymology

From New Latin: Greek hēlos (stud, nail) + derma (skin) + -idae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhiːloʊˌdɜːrməˈtaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌhiːləʊˌdɜːməˈtaɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of venomous lizards that includes the Gila monster and beaded lizards, native to the Americas
family of venomous lizards

Examples

Members of the Helodermatidae family are known for their venomous bite, which is unique among lizards.
The Helodermatidae are native to southwestern North America and Central America.
Scientists study Helodermatidae to better understand the evolution of venom in reptiles.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Heloderma (genus name) and -idae (suffix denoting animal family).
