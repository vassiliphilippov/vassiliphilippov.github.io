coexistent

1. ADJ

/ ˌkoʊ.ɪɡˈzɪs.tənt / (AmE), / ˌkəʊ.ɪɡˈzɪs.tənt / (BrE)
existing or occurring at the same time as something else
existing at the same time

Examples

The two species were coexistent for hundreds of years in the same forest.
Historically, multiple religions have been coexistent within this society.
Several coexistent trends shaped the development of 20th-century art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concurrent - existing, happening, or done at the same time
  • Synonym: simultaneous - occurring, operating, or done at the same time

Etymology

From Latin coexsistere, from co- (together) + exsistere (to exist).
