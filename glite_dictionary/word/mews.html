mews

1. NOUN

/ mjuz / (AmE), / mjuÀêz / (BrE)
A street or row of buildings that were originally private stables, now converted into houses or apartments, especially in Britain.
row of former stables now dwellings
british

Examples

She bought a beautiful cottage in a quiet mews near the city center.
Many old mews in London have been transformed into stylish homes.
Artists are often drawn to the charm and character of a mews dwelling.

Synset Examples

she lives in a Chelsea mews

Etymology

From the Old French *mue* (cage, mew), especially referring to caged birds at the royal stables, later extended in Britain to stables and their conversions.
