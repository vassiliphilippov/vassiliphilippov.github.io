H2O

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪtʃ tuː ˈoʊ / (AmE), / ˌeɪtʃ tjuː ˈəʊ / (BrE)
the chemical formula for water, a clear, colorless, odorless, and tasteless liquid essential for most forms of life
chemical formula for water

Examples

The chemical reaction produces H2O as a byproduct.
Teachers often use H2O to introduce students to chemical formulas.
Drinking enough H2O each day is vital for good health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water - clear, colorless, tasteless liquid; chemical substance H2O

Etymology

Scientific notation: 'H' from hydrogen, '2' (subscript) indicates two hydrogen atoms, 'O' from oxygen.
