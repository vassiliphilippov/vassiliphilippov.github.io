to rend

1. VERB

/ rɛnd / (AmE), / rɛnd / (BrE)
to tear something violently or be torn apart forcefully
tear or be torn violently

Examples

The earthquake caused the ground to rend open, leaving a deep crevice.
She could not bear to rend the old letters, though their words brought only pain.
In ancient rituals, mourners would rend their garments as a sign of grief.

Synset Examples

The curtain ripped from top to bottom
pull the cooked chicken into strips

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tear - to pull apart or damage by force
  • Synonym: rip - to cut or tear quickly or roughly

Etymology

Old English rendan, of Germanic origin; related to Norwegian rande (tear, split).
