Haematopus

1. NOUN

/ hiːˈmætəˌpoʊs / (AmE), / hiːˈmætəˌpɒs / (BrE)
A genus of wading birds known as oystercatchers, found in coastal regions worldwide.
genus of wading birds (oystercatchers)

Examples

The Haematopus genus includes several striking black-and-white wading birds.
Many coastal areas are home to species of Haematopus that feed on shellfish.
Researchers observed nesting behavior in the Haematopus population along the shoreline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oystercatcher - a bird of the genus Haematopus with a long red bill, known for eating shellfish

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + pous (foot), referring to the birds' reddish legs.
