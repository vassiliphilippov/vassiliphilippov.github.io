to emulate

1. VERB

/ ˈɛm.jə.leɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛm.jʊ.leɪt / (BrE)
to try to equal or excel, often by imitating; to compete with successfully
to try to equal or surpass

Examples

Many young athletes emulate their sports heroes in hopes of reaching similar success.
Her company has tried to emulate the achievements of larger, more established firms.
He aspired to emulate the precision and grace of the masters in his piano performances.

Synset Examples

This artist's drawings cannot emulate his water colors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to imitate - to copy the behavior or appearance of someone or something
  • Degree Higher: to surpass - to do or be better than

Etymology

From Latin 'aemulari' (to rival, to strive to equal).

2. VERB

/ ˈɛm.jəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛmjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to try to equal or excel someone or something, especially by imitating them
to try to equal or excel by imitation

Examples

Young artists often emulate the styles of their predecessors when developing their own techniques.
She hoped to emulate her mentor's success in the scientific community.
Many companies try to emulate the innovative culture of leading tech firms.

Synset Examples

He is emulating the skating skills of his older sister

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to imitate - to copy or mimic someone's actions or behavior
  • Degree Higher: to excel - to be exceptionally good at or surpass others

Etymology

From Latin emulari (to rival, strive to equal or excel).

3. VERB

/ ˈɛm.jəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛmjʊleɪt / (BrE)
(computing) to imitate the function of another system, usually by modifying hardware or software, so that one system behaves like another.
imitate the function of another system (computing)

Examples

This software allows you to emulate old video game consoles on your computer.
Engineers developed an application to emulate the original hardware functionality in software.
Many mobile phone apps emulate classic arcade games using modern devices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to simulate - to imitate the conditions of a situation or process
  • Synonym: to replicate - to make an exact copy or reproduction

Etymology

From Latin *aemulari* (to rival, imitate).

Synset POS: v
