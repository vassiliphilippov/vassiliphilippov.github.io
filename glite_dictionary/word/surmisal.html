surmisal

1. NOUN

/ sərˈmaɪzəl / (AmE), / səˈmaɪzəl / (BrE)
An idea or opinion formed without sufficient evidence; a guess or conjecture.
guess based on incomplete evidence

Examples

Her theory about the missing book was pure surmisal without any solid evidence.
All we could offer at this point was surmisal, not fact.
His surmisal turned out to be correct, even though he had little information at the time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surmise - To guess or form an opinion without sufficient evidence
  • Synonym: conjecture - A conclusion formed on the basis of incomplete information

Etymology

From surmise + -al, following French and Latin nominal formation patterns.
