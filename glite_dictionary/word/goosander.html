goosander

1. NOUN

/ ɡuˈsændər / (AmE), / ɡuːˈsændə / (BrE)
a large fish-eating diving duck (the common merganser, Mergus merganser) found in Europe and North America, with a long, slender, serrated bill
large fish-eating duck (common merganser)

Examples

The male goosander has a striking white body and a dark green head.
We watched a pair of goosanders dive beneath the surface of the lake for fish.
During winter, flocks of goosander can be seen on rivers across northern Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merganser - a type of fish-eating duck with a serrated bill

Etymology

From 'goose' + 'ander', possibly influenced by Old Norse 'and' (duck).
