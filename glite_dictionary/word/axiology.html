axiology

1. NOUN

/ ˌæksiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌæksiˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of philosophy that studies values, including ethics and aesthetics, and value judgments
study of values in philosophy

Examples

The professor specialized in axiology and often discussed moral values in class.
A deep understanding of axiology is essential for anyone interested in ethics and philosophy.
Her thesis explored the role of axiology in making decisions about cultural heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethics - philosophical study of moral values and rules
  • Synonym: aesthetics - branch of philosophy concerned with beauty and taste

Etymology

From Greek axios (worthy) + -logia (study or science).
