Albula

1. NOUN

/ ælˈbjuːlə / (AmE), / ælˈbjuːlə / (BrE)
A genus of ray-finned fish, known as bonefishes, which is the only genus in the family Albulidae.
a genus of marine fish (bonefishes)

Examples

The genus Albula is well known among sport fishermen in tropical regions.
Researchers identified a new species within the Albula genus off the coast of Florida.
Unlike many other fish, most Albula are characterized by their slender, torpedo-shaped bodies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bonefish - a silvery game fish of shallow tropical waters; species of Albula

Etymology

From Latin 'albula' meaning 'whitish', referring to the pale color of the fish.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæl.bjʊ.lə / (AmE), / ˈal.bjʊ.lə / (BrE)
A genus of fishes in the family Albulidae, containing the species commonly known as bonefishes.
genus of bonefishes

Examples

The Albula is notable for its importance to sport fishing in tropical regions.
Scientists recently studied the migration patterns of Albula species along the coastline.
Unlike other genera, Albula remains the only genus within its family, Albulidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bonefish - a common name for species in the genus Albula

Etymology

From Latin 'albula', meaning 'little white one', diminutive of 'albus' (white), referring to the silvery color of the fish.
