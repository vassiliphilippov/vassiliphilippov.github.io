anodic

1. ADJ

/ əˈnɑːdɪk / (AmE), / əˈnɒdɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving an anode, especially the positive electrode in a device such as an electrolytic cell or battery.
relating to an anode

Examples

The metal undergoes anodic dissolution during the corrosion process.
The anodic reaction is critical in determining the efficiency of the battery.
Engineers studied the anodic behavior of aluminum alloys under various conditions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cathodic - relating to a cathode, the negative electrode
  • Synonym: anode - the electrode where oxidation occurs in a cell

Etymology

From 'anode' plus the suffix '-ic'. 'Anode' from Greek 'anodos' meaning 'way up'.
