ceratopsian

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛrəˈtɑpsiən / (AmE), / ˌsɛrəˈtɒpsiən / (BrE)
any of a group of four-legged herbivorous dinosaurs characterized by large beaked skulls, often with horns and frills, that lived during the late Cretaceous period
herbivorous dinosaur with beaked skull

Examples

The museum displayed a rare ceratopsian skull discovered in Mongolia.
Fossils show that ceratopsians had impressive frills and horns for defense.
Scientists believe that ceratopsians lived in herds during the late Cretaceous period.

Etymology

From Greek keras (horn) + ops (face), referencing their horned faces.
