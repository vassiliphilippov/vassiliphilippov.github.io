discordant

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈkɔrdənt / (AmE), / dɪsˈkɔːdənt / (BrE)
not in agreement or harmony; conflicting or clashing
not in agreement or harmony

Examples

Their opinions were often discordant, leading to frequent debates.
The colors in this painting seem discordant rather than blended.
He played a discordant note that disrupted the harmony of the piece.

Synset Examples

views discordant with present-day ideas

Related Terms

  • Antonym: harmonious - in agreement or forming a pleasing whole
  • Antonym: concordant - in agreement; consistent

Etymology

From Latin 'discordans', present participle of 'discordare', from 'discordia' (discord).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00556119-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ dɪsˈkɔrdənt / (AmE), / dɪsˈkɔːdənt / (BrE)
lacking harmony; disagreeing or clashing, especially in sound or ideas.
lacking harmony; clashing

Examples

The discordant notes made the music difficult to listen to.
There was a discordant mixture of voices shouting in the hall.
Her views were discordant with the rest of the team.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: harmonious - forming a pleasing or consistent whole; in harmony
  • Synonym: dissonant - lacking harmony; harsh in sound

Etymology

From Latin 'discordant-', present participle of 'discordare' (to be at variance), from 'discordia' (discord).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01167794-s
Synset POS: s
