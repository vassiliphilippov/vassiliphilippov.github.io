unpledged

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈplɛdʒd / (AmE), / ʌnˈplɛdʒd / (BrE)
not promised or committed, especially not engaged to be married.
not promised or engaged in marriage

Examples

She remained unpledged despite several suitors seeking her hand in marriage.
In the novel, the character is described as unpledged and free from any marital ties.
He valued his unpledged status, preferring independence over commitment.

Synset Examples

continued seeing him but on an unengaged basis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unengaged - not formally promised or committed to marriage
  • Antonym: engaged - formally promised to be married

Etymology

From un- (not) + pledged (promised); 'pledge' from Old French 'plege', from Late Latin 'plevium'.
