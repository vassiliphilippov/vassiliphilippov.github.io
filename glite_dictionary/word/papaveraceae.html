Papaveraceae

1. NOUN

/ pəˌpeɪvəˈreɪsiˌi / (AmE), / pəˌpævəˈreɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants, commonly known as the poppy family, consisting of herbs or shrubs with milky or colored sap and capsule-type fruits.
the poppy family of flowering plants

Examples

Many species in the Papaveraceae family contain brightly colored flowers used in gardens worldwide.
Opium poppies, belonging to the Papaveraceae, have been cultivated for their medicinal properties for centuries.
Botanists classify poppies and related plants under the family Papaveraceae.

Etymology

From Papaver, the type genus (Latin for 'poppy'), plus the standard botanical suffix -aceae, denoting a plant family.

2. NOUN

/ pəˌpævəˈreɪsiˌi / (AmE), / pæˌpævəˈriːsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants (herbs or shrubs) with milky, often colored sap and capsular fruits; includes poppies and related species
family of flowering plants including poppies

Examples

The Papaveraceae family includes many well-known ornamental plants, such as poppies.
Botanists study the Papaveraceae to better understand the properties of their milky sap.
Species in the Papaveraceae can be found in temperate and subtropical regions worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Papaver - the type genus of the Papaveraceae family, including poppies

Etymology

From the genus name Papaver (Latin for 'poppy') + standard botanical family suffix -aceae.
