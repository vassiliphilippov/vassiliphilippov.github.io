ravaging

1. ADJ

/ ˈrævɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈravɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
causing severe destruction or devastation; ruinously destructive or wasting
causing severe destruction or wasting

Examples

The ravaging wildfire destroyed hundreds of homes in its path.
He struggled to recover from a ravaging disease that left him weakened for months.
The hurricane swept through the town, leaving behind a ravaging aftermath of damage and loss.

Synset Examples

a ravaging illness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devastating - causing great destruction or damage
  • Antonym: mild - not severe or damaging

Etymology

From the verb 'ravage', which comes from French 'ravager', meaning to devastate.

2. NOUN

/ ˈræv.ɪ.dʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈræv.ɪ.dʒɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of causing excessive damage, destruction, or devastation, often through violent plundering
destructive plundering or devastation

Examples

The ravaging of the town left its streets deserted and its buildings in ruins.
Many species continue to suffer from the ravaging of their habitats by illegal logging.
The ravaging caused by the wildfire was evident across the entire valley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devastation - great destruction or damage
  • Synonym: destruction - the action or process of causing so much damage to something that it no longer exists or cannot be repaired
  • Antonym: rebuilding - the process of constructing again after destruction

Etymology

From the verb 'ravage', ultimately from French 'ravager'.

Synset POS: n
