storminess

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɔr.mi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈstɔː.mi.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having storms or being characterized by violent weather
state of being stormy

Examples

The storminess of the weather made sailing dangerous and unpredictable.
The storminess of the region in autumn attracts adventurous surfers.
After days of storminess, the skies finally cleared and the sun came out.

Synset Examples

he dreaded the storminess of the North Atlantic in winter

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calmness - the quality of being free from storms or disturbance
  • Synonym: stormy - characterized by storms or violent weather

Etymology

From 'stormy' + '-ness', forming nouns indicating a state or quality.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɔrminəs / (AmE), / ˈstɔːminɪs / (BrE)
violent or turbulent passion, especially in speech or behavior; the quality of being marked by intense emotion or outburst.
intense turbulence or passion in behavior

Examples

The storminess of the debate caught everyone by surprise.
She regretted the storminess that had taken over her during the argument.
His storminess in meetings sometimes intimidated his colleagues.

Synset Examples

frightened by the storminess of their argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passion - strong and barely controllable emotion
  • Antonym: calmness - the state of being free from agitation or strong emotion

Etymology

Formed from 'stormy' + '-ness', following the typical pattern for English nouns denoting a state or quality.
