alloantibody

1. NOUN

/ ˌæloʊˈæntɪˌbɒdi / (AmE), / ˌaləʊˈantɪˌbɒdi / (BrE)
An antibody produced by the immune system that reacts against antigens from individuals of the same species but with different genetic makeup, such as in blood transfusions or organ transplants.
antibody against same-species foreign tissue
medical

Examples

The patient developed an alloantibody after receiving multiple blood transfusions.
Detection of an alloantibody is critical before organ transplantation to prevent rejection.
Researchers studied the formation of alloantibody responses in different donor-recipient pairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antibody - a protein produced by the immune system to neutralize pathogens

Etymology

From Greek 'allos' (other) + 'antibody'.
