ravaged

1. ADJ

/ ˈrævɪdʒd / (AmE), / ˈravɪdʒd / (BrE)
having been severely damaged or destroyed by force, violence, or other destructive agency.
severely damaged or destroyed

Examples

The once-beautiful town was ravaged by war and natural disaster.
Years of drought and overgrazing had ravaged the landscape almost beyond recognition.
The ravaged coastline bore the scars of the hurricane's passage.

Synset Examples

the raped countryside

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devastated - extremely damaged or destroyed
  • Antonym: restored - returned to a former, better state

Etymology

From the verb 'ravage', from French 'ravager' (to devastate).
