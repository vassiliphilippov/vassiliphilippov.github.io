touchiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌtʃ.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtʌtʃ.ɪ.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being easily annoyed or offended; sensitivity to slight provocation or criticism
easily irritated or offended

Examples

Her touchiness made it difficult to discuss even minor issues with her.
Due to his increasing touchiness, he often misinterpreted harmless comments as personal attacks.
The manager's touchiness about her decisions discouraged others from offering suggestions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritability - the quality of being easily irritated
  • Antonym: calmness - the state of being calm and not easily disturbed

Etymology

From 'touchy' (easily upset) + '-ness' (state or quality).
