nim

1. NOUN

/ nɪm / (AmE), / nɪm / (BrE)
a game in which objects such as matchsticks are arranged in rows and players take turns removing one or more; the aim is either to take or avoid taking the last object, depending on the variation.
strategy game involving removal of objects

Examples

We spent the afternoon playing nim with colored sticks on the kitchen table.
Mathematicians have analyzed nim for its fascinating strategy and simple rules.
Children often enjoy a game of nim at parties for a quick mental challenge.

Etymology

Origin uncertain, first recorded in the early 20th century in mathematical game theory contexts.
