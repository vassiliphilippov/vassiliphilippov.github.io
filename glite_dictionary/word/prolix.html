prolix

1. ADJ

/ prəˈlɪks / (AmE), / prəˈlɪks / (BrE)
(of speech or writing) using too many words; tediously lengthy or verbose.
tediously wordy or lengthy

Examples

The report was so prolix that it was difficult to find the main points.
His prolix style often left his readers overwhelmed and confused.
Despite a promising introduction, the novel became bogged down by prolix descriptions.

Synset Examples

editing a prolix manuscript
a prolix lecturer telling you more than you want to know
He gave a good verbal description of the suspect

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verbose - using more words than needed; wordy
  • Antonym: concise - giving much information clearly and with few words

Etymology

From Latin prolixus (stretched out, extended, long, verbose).
