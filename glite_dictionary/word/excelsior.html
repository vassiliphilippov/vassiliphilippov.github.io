excelsior

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈsɛl.si.ər / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɛl.si.ɔː / (BrE)
thin, curled wood shavings used for packing or stuffing objects during shipping or storage
thin wood shavings used for packing

Examples

The fragile glassware was carefully packed in excelsior to prevent damage during shipment.
Old trunks were often lined with excelsior to cushion their contents.
They pulled away layer after layer of excelsior from the antique box.

Etymology

From Latin 'excelsior' meaning 'higher', perhaps referring to its lightweight properties.
