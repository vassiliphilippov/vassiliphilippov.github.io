Moctezuma II

1. PROPN

/ ˌmoʊktəˈzuːmə ðə ˈsɛkənd / (AmE), / ˌmɒktəˈzuːmə ðə ˈsɛkənd / (BrE)
the last Aztec emperor of Mexico, overthrown and killed by Hernán Cortés in 1520
Aztec emperor killed by Cortés

Examples

Moctezuma II ruled the Aztec Empire during the arrival of the Spanish conquistadors.
Many historians debate whether Moctezuma II was a strong or weak leader in the face of conquest.
The death of Moctezuma II marked a turning point in the history of Mexico.

Etymology

From Nahuatl *Motēuczōma*, combined with Roman numeral II to distinguish from his predecessor.
