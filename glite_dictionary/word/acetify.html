to acetify

1. VERB

/ əˈsiːtəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / əˈsiːtɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make or become acidic, especially by converting into acetic acid
to turn or make acidic

Examples

If exposed to air for too long, wine can acetify and develop a sharp taste.
The vinegar was produced when the grape juice began to acetify.
Bacteria in the mixture will acetify the alcohol, turning it into vinegar over time.

Synset Examples

the solution acetified

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to acidify - to make or become acidic; to turn into acid

Etymology

From French 'acétifier', from 'acétique' (acetic) and '-fier' (to make), influenced by Latin 'acetum' (vinegar).

2. VERB

/ əˈsiːtɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / əˈsiːtɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make or become sour, especially by turning into vinegar
make or become sour; turn into vinegar

Examples

Certain bacteria can acetify wine, turning it into vinegar over time.
If you leave fruit juice exposed to air, it may eventually acetify.
The winemaker was careful to prevent the wine from starting to acetify during storage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to acidify - to make or become acid

Etymology

From French acétifier, from acétum (vinegar) + -fy (make).
