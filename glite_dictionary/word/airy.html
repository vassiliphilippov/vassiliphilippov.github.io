airy

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛri / (AmE), / ˈeəri / (BrE)
(of a place) having a lot of fresh air; spacious and well-ventilated.
full of fresh air; well-ventilated

Examples

The apartment has airy rooms and large windows overlooking the park.
We enjoyed lunch in an airy café on the corner.
She prefers living in an airy house with lots of natural light.

Synset Examples

airy rooms

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stuffy - (of a room or space) lacking fresh air; poorly ventilated
  • Synonym: ventilated - allowing fresh air to move freely; supplied with airflow

Etymology

From Old French airé (exposed to the air), from air (air).

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈɛri / (AmE), / ˈeəri / (BrE)
very light in weight or texture; almost insubstantial, like air
very light or insubstantial

Examples

The bride wore an airy veil that fluttered in the breeze.
We draped airy fabrics over the windows for a soft, delicate look.
Her airy dress seemed to float as she walked.

Synset Examples

airy gauze curtains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: light - not heavy or weighing little
  • Antonym: heavy - of great weight; difficult to lift or move

Etymology

From air + -y, meaning 'of or pertaining to air'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01190456-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈɛri / (AmE), / ˈeəri / (BrE)
light, delicate, or insubstantial; as intangible or impalpable as air
light or insubstantial; like air

Examples

There was an airy quality to her movements, as if she floated rather than walked.
His speech was filled with airy promises that vanished as quickly as they appeared.
The artist created airy sculptures that seemed to defy gravity.

Synset Examples

figures light and aeriform come unlooked for and melt away
aerial fancies
an airy apparition
physical rather than ethereal forms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethereal - extremely delicate and light; heavenly
  • Antonym: tangible - perceptible by touch; substantial

Etymology

From Middle English 'airy', from Old French 'aire' (air) + '-y'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00628872-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈɛri / (AmE), / ˈeəri / (BrE)
(of ideas, plans, etc.) not practical or realizable; based on speculation rather than reality.
impractical or speculative (idea/plan)

Examples

He dismissed her airy proposal as hopelessly unrealistic.
Too many airy promises were made during the campaign.
The novel is full of airy visions of an ideal future.

Synset Examples

airy theories about socioeconomic improvement
visionary schemes for getting rich

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speculative - based on conjecture rather than knowledge
  • Antonym: practical - concerned with actual doing or use, rather than theory or speculation

Etymology

From Old French *airé* (of the air), from Latin *aer* (air); extended metaphorically to qualities of insubstantiality or unreality.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02508026-s
Synset POS: s
