electric

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈlɛk.trɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛk.trɪk / (BrE)
(of a situation or atmosphere) exceptionally tense or charged with emotion or excitement.
tense or charged with emotion

Examples

The mood in the stadium was electric as the final whistle approached.
She described the meeting as electric with anticipation and nervous energy.
Tension in the room was electric after the controversial announcement.

Synset Examples

an atmosphere electric with suspicion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charged - full of excitement or tension (of atmosphere)

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈlɛktrɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛk.trɪk / (BrE)
full of excitement or intense emotion; thrilling; causing a sudden heightened response as if charged with electricity
exciting or thrilling (figurative)

Examples

The atmosphere in the stadium was absolutely electric after the team's dramatic victory.
His electric performance captivated everyone in the audience.
The tension in the room was electric as the final results were announced.

Synset Examples

gave an electric reading of the play
the new leader had a galvanic effect on morale

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exciting - causing great enthusiasm and eagerness
  • Antonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00924872-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ɪˈlɛk.trɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛk.trɪk / (BrE)
operated by, producing, or relating to electricity
powered by or relating to electricity

Examples

Many modern cars are now fully electric, running solely on batteries.
She bought an electric kettle to make tea more quickly.
During the thunderstorm, an electric wire was damaged and needed repair.

Synset Examples

electric current
electric wiring
electrical appliances
an electrical storm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electrical - relating to electricity (often used interchangeably with 'electric' in some contexts)

Etymology

From Latin 'electricus', meaning 'produced from amber by friction', via New Latin 'ēlectricus' and Greek 'ēlektron' (amber).
