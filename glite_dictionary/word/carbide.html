carbide

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrˌbaɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbaɪd / (BrE)
a compound consisting of carbon combined with a more electropositive element, often used in industry and chemistry
compound of carbon and another element

Examples

Tungsten carbide is often used to make cutting tools because of its hardness.
The reaction produces calcium carbide, which is then used to generate acetylene gas.
Researchers are developing new carbide materials for advanced battery technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silicon carbide - a carbide of silicon, used as an abrasive and in ceramics
  • Synonym: calcium carbide - a compound of calcium and carbon, used to produce acetylene gas

Etymology

From French *carbure* (compound of carbon), from *carbone* (carbon).
