Helsinki

1. PROPN

/ hɛlˈsɪŋki / (AmE), / hɛlˈsɪŋki / (BrE)
the capital and largest city of Finland, located in the south of the country; an important port and cultural center
capital city of Finland

Examples

They spent a week exploring the beautiful city of Helsinki during their trip to Finland.
The 1952 Summer Olympics were held in Helsinki, bringing international attention to the city.
Many direct flights connect London to Helsinki for travelers visiting Scandinavia.

Etymology

From Swedish Helsingfors, originally referring to settlers from Hälsingland at the rapids (fors) of the river.

2. PROPN

/ hɛlˈsɪŋki / (AmE), / hɛlˈsɪŋki / (BrE)
the capital and largest city of Finland, located in the south of the country, known as a major port and center for commerce and culture
capital city of Finland

Examples

Many tourists visit Helsinki each summer to experience its vibrant culture and architecture.
The conference will be held in Helsinki this September, attracting delegates from around the world.
She moved from Stockholm to Helsinki for her new job in technology.

Etymology

From Swedish Helsingfors, the Swedish name for the city.
