redness

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɛdnəs / (BrE)
A physical condition in which the skin or other tissues become red, often as a result of injury, irritation, or inflammation, typically accompanied by pain, swelling, and heat.
red coloration of tissues due to irritation or injury

Examples

The redness around the wound indicated possible infection.
She noticed a slight redness and swelling after bumping her arm.
Prolonged sun exposure can cause redness and discomfort to the skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inflammation - a localized physical condition in which part of the body becomes reddened, swollen, hot, and often painful

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɛdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being red in color; a red hue or pigment resembling that of blood
state of being red in color

Examples

The redness of the sunset made the sky look almost surreal.
After hours in the sun, there was noticeable redness on her cheeks.
He admired the redness of the newly opened roses in the garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red - the color at the end of the visible spectrum, next to orange and opposite violet

Etymology

From Old English 'readness' (state of being red), from 'read' (red) + '-ness' (noun suffix).
