Helichrysum

1. NOUN

/ ˌheɪlɪˈkraɪsəm / (AmE), / ˌhiːlɪˈkraɪsəm / (BrE)
A large genus of mostly African and Australian herbs and shrubs, known for their everlasting flowers.
genus of plants with everlasting flowers

Examples

The garden featured several species of Helichrysum, admired for their brightly colored blooms.
Dried Helichrysum flowers are often used in floral arrangements because they retain their color for months.
Botanists are studying the medicinal properties of plants in the Helichrysum genus.

Etymology

From Greek 'helios' (sun) + 'chrysos' (gold), referring to the flower's color and appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhiːlɪˈkraɪsəm / (AmE), / ˌhiːlɪˈkraɪsəm / (BrE)
A genus of mostly African and Australian herbs and shrubs known for their everlasting flowers, sometimes including the genus Ozothamnus in some classifications.
genus of flowering plants with everlasting flowers

Examples

Many species of Helichrysum are grown in gardens for their attractive, long-lasting blossoms.
The medicinal properties of Helichrysum have been valued in traditional African remedies.
Dried Helichrysum flowers are often used in floral arrangements because they retain their color.

Etymology

From Greek helios (sun) + chrysos (gold), referring to the flower's appearance.
