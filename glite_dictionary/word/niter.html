niter

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtər / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtə / (BrE)
A white crystalline compound, potassium nitrate (KNO3), used especially as a fertilizer and in the manufacture of explosives.
potassium nitrate; used in fertilizer and explosives

Examples

Large quantities of niter were historically used in the production of gunpowder.
The soil was enriched with niter to boost crop growth.
Crystals of niter can be found forming on cave walls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saltpeter - potassium nitrate used for similar purposes

Etymology

From Middle English nitre, from Old French, from Latin nitrum, from Greek nitron.
