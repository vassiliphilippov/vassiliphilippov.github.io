subtraction

1. NOUN

/ səbˈtræk.ʃən / (AmE), / səbˈtrak.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of taking something away from a total or whole, especially in mathematics
the act of taking something away

Examples

The company's budget suffered after the subtraction of several grants.
She mastered addition and subtraction in her first year at school.
After the subtraction of expenses, the profits were much lower than expected.

Synset Examples

he complained about the subtraction of money from their paychecks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deduction - the act of subtracting or taking away
  • Antonym: addition - the process of adding something to something else

Etymology

From Latin 'subtractio' from 'subtrahere' (to draw from beneath, remove).

2. NOUN

/ səbˈtræk.ʃən / (AmE), / səbˈtræk.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of finding the difference between two numbers by taking the smaller away from the larger
the act of subtracting numbers

Examples

She learned how to solve simple subtraction problems in school.
If you use subtraction, what is ten minus four?
The subtraction of expenses from income gives the net profit.

Synset Examples

the subtraction of three from four leaves one
four minus three equals one

Related Terms

  • Antonym: addition - the arithmetic operation of combining two numbers to get their sum
  • Degree Higher: multiplication - the arithmetic operation of finding the product of numbers
  • Degree Higher: division - the arithmetic operation of determining how many times one number is contained in another

Etymology

From Latin subtrahere, meaning 'to draw from beneath'.
