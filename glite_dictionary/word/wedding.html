wedding

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɛdɪŋ / (BrE)
a marriage ceremony, especially including the associated celebrations
marriage ceremony and associated celebrations

Examples

They planned their wedding for early spring in the countryside.
The wedding was a beautiful ceremony attended by friends and family.
After the wedding, the newlyweds traveled to Paris for their honeymoon.

Synset Examples

their marriage was conducted in the chapel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marriage - the legally or formally recognized union of two people as partners
  • Antonym: divorce - the legal dissolution of a marriage

Etymology

From Old English 'weddian' (to pledge oneself, have a wedding), related to 'wedd' (a pledge).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɛdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɛdɪŋ / (BrE)
a ceremony and celebration at which two people are married to each other
marriage ceremony and celebration

Examples

They invited over two hundred guests to their wedding last summer.
Her parents are planning a traditional Indian wedding ceremony next month.
The wedding took place in a beautiful old church outside the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marriage - the legal or formal union of two people as partners in a relationship
  • Antonym: divorce - the legal dissolution of a marriage

Etymology

From Old English 'weddian' (to pledge, to marry).
