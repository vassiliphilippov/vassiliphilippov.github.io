to envenom

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈvɛnəm / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛnəm / (BrE)
to put poison into something; to make poisonous
add poison to

Examples

It is rare for a snake to envenom a human with a dry bite.
The assassin attempted to envenom the king's chalice.
Some spiders envenom their prey before consuming it.

Synset Examples

Her husband poisoned her drink in order to kill her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poison - to administer poison to someone or something

Etymology

From Old French 'envenimer', from 'venim' (poison), from Latin 'venenum' (poison).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00533509-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈvɛnəm / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛnəm / (BrE)
to make (someone or something) bitter or filled with resentment
make bitter or resentful

Examples

Repeated criticism served only to envenom their relationship further.
Harsh words can envenom the feelings between old friends.
Political arguments often envenom otherwise cordial discussions.

Synset Examples

These injustices embittered her even more

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embitter - to make (someone) bitter or resentful

Etymology

From Old French *envenimer*, from Latin *venenum* (poison).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
