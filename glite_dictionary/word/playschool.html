playschool

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪˌskuːl / (AmE), / ˈpleɪˌskuːl / (BrE)
an informal group or place where young children are taught through play, usually for part of the day
informal early childhood education group

Examples

Emma goes to a playschool three mornings a week while her mother works.
We enrolled our son in a playschool to help him socialize before starting kindergarten.
The local community center runs a playschool for toddlers aged two to four.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nursery school - a school for very young children
  • Synonym: kindergarten - a school or class for young children, typically the year before first grade

Etymology

Formed from 'play' + 'school', indicating a school where children learn by playing.
