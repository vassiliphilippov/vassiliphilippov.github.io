to philander

1. VERB

/ fəˈlændər / (AmE), / fɪˈlandə / (BrE)
(of a man) to engage in flirtatious or amorous behavior without serious intentions, often with multiple partners.
behave amorously without serious intent

Examples

He had a reputation for philandering with his co-workers at every office party.
Some men philander openly, caring little for the feelings of their partners.
Rumors spread quickly when he was seen philandering about town with several different women.

Synset Examples

The guys always try to chat up the new secretaries
My husband never flirts with other women

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flirt - to behave as if attracted to someone, without serious intent

Etymology

From Greek philandros (loving men), from philos (loving) + anēr (man).

2. VERB

/ fəˈlændər / (AmE), / fɪˈlændə / (BrE)
(of a man) to have casual romantic or sexual affairs with multiple women; to engage in flirtatious or amorous behavior without commitment.
have casual romantic affairs (of a man)

Examples

Rumors spread that the senator would philander with staffers during campaign trips.
He was known to philander throughout his twenties, never settling down with anyone.
Instead of spending time with his family, Arthur would often philander at parties and social events.

Synset Examples

He has been womanizing for years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to womanize - to have many casual sexual affairs with women
  • Degree Lower: flirt - to behave towards someone as if sexually attracted without serious intentions
  • Degree Higher: to seduce - to lead someone astray into a sexual relationship

Etymology

From Greek 'philandros' (loving men), from 'phil-' (loving) + 'anēr' (man).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02597066-v
Synset POS: v
