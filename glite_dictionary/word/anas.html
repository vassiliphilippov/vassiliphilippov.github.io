Anas

1. NOUN

/ ˈænəs / (AmE), / ˈɑːnəs / (BrE)
The genus of ducks that includes many species of freshwater ducks, such as the mallard.
genus of freshwater ducks

Examples

The mallard belongs to the genus Anas, which includes several familiar duck species.
Scientists have identified new species within the Anas genus based on recent genetic analysis.
Many freshwater ducks in Europe are classified under Anas by ornithologists.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Anatidae - the family of birds that includes ducks, geese, and swans

Etymology

From Latin *anas*, meaning 'duck'.
