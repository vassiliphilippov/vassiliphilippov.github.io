nipping

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
(of weather or air) pleasantly cold and invigorating; bracing.
pleasantly cold and invigorating (of weather)

Examples

A nipping breeze swept across the fields as the sun began to rise.
We enjoyed a long walk outside despite the nipping air.
The nipping weather made her cheeks glow with warmth after coming back inside.

Synset Examples

crisp clear nights and frosty mornings
a nipping wind
a nippy fall day
snappy weather

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crisp - (of weather or air) cool, fresh, and invigorating
  • Synonym: nippy - chilly or pleasantly cold (of weather)
  • Synonym: frosty - very cold, with frost forming

Etymology

Present participle of 'nip', originally from Middle English nippen, meaning to pinch or bite sharply.

2. ADJ

/ ˈnɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
Sharp or caustic in a way that can hurt feelings or inflict emotional pain.
having a sharp, wounding effect

Examples

She couldn't ignore his nipping remarks, which left her feeling embarrassed.
The critic's nipping review spared no one in the cast.
His nipping wit often bordered on cruelty, making people wary of his comments.

Synset Examples

a barbed compliment
a biting aphorism
pungent satire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biting - harshly critical or sarcastic (of remarks or tone)
  • Synonym: caustic - severely critical or sarcastic (of remarks)

Etymology

From the verb 'nip' (to pinch sharply), dating from Middle English 'nippen'.
