Hyphantria cunea

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈfæntriə ˈkjuːniə / (AmE), / hɪˈfantrɪə ˈkjuːnɪə / (BrE)
a species of moth known as the fall webworm, whose larvae build silky webs on the branches of many deciduous trees
moth species, the fall webworm

Examples

The trees in my backyard were covered in silky webs woven by Hyphantria cunea larvae.
Farmers are concerned about Hyphantria cunea because its caterpillars can defoliate crops.
Biologists study the spread of Hyphantria cunea as an invasive pest in new regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fall webworm - the common name for Hyphantria cunea, a moth species

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Hyphantria' + species epithet 'cunea' (Latin for 'wedge-shaped').

2. NOUN

/ haɪˈfantriə ˈkuniə / (AmE), / hɪˈfantrɪə ˈkjuːniə / (BrE)
A species of moth whose larvae are known as fall webworms, found in North America and introduced elsewhere.
a species of moth, the fall webworm

Examples

The destructive caterpillars of Hyphantria cunea are commonly known as fall webworms.
Farmers monitored Hyphantria cunea populations to prevent damage to their crops.
Invasive Hyphantria cunea have spread rapidly in Europe since their introduction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fall webworm - larval stage of Hyphantria cunea; web-making caterpillar
  • Degree Higher: moth - nocturnal insect related to butterflies

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus name 'Hyphantria' and specific epithet 'cunea', from Latin 'cuneus' (wedge), possibly referring to markings or shape.
