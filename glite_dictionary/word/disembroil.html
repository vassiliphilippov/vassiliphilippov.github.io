to disembroil

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪs.ɛmˈbrɔɪl / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.ɛmˈbrɔɪl / (BrE)
to free (someone or something) from involvement in a difficult or complicated situation; to disentangle
free from entanglement or involvement
archaic

Examples

He tried desperately to disembroil himself from the bitter family quarrels.
It took months before she could disembroil her finances from her former business partner.
The diplomat managed to disembroil his country from the escalating international conflict.

Synset Examples

How can I disentangle myself from her personal affairs?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disentangle - to free from entanglement; to unravel
  • Antonym: to embroil - to involve someone in a difficult situation

Etymology

From French 'désembrouiller', from 'dés-' (dis-) + 'embrouiller' (to entangle).
