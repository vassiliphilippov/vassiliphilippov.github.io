Cyclamen neopolitanum

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkləˌmɛn ˌniːoʊpəˈlɪtənəm / (AmE), / ˌsɪkləmən ˌniːəʊpəˈlɪtənəm / (BrE)
A species of wild European cyclamen, noted for its pink flowers, commonly found in woodlands.
wild European cyclamen species

Examples

The woodland path was lined with blooming Cyclamen neopolitanum in the spring.
Gardeners prize Cyclamen neopolitanum for its delicate pink flowers and attractive foliage.
While hiking in Italy, we spotted several patches of Cyclamen neopolitanum beneath the trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cyclamen - a genus of flowering plants with rounded leaves and showy flowers

Etymology

From the genus name Cyclamen (Greek kyklos 'circle') and the specific epithet neopolitanum, meaning 'of Naples' in Latin.
