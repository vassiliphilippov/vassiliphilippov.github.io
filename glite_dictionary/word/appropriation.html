appropriation

1. NOUN

/ əˌproʊpriˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌprəʊprɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of incorporating something by joining or uniting it with something else
incorporation by joining or uniting

Examples

The appropriation of new territory was marked by both celebration and controversy.
Through appropriation, the company merged several smaller firms into its corporate structure.
The city's growth was accelerated by the appropriation of nearby villages.

Etymology

From Latin appropriatio (an assigning), from appropriare (to make one's own), from ad- (to) + proprius (own).

2. NOUN

/ əˌproʊpriˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌprəʊpriˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
an amount of money officially set aside for a specific purpose, especially by a government or legislature
money set aside for a purpose; government budgeting

Examples

The city council approved a special appropriation to renovate the old library.
This year’s appropriation for education was higher than last year’s.
The museum received a federal appropriation to expand its collection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allocation - the process of distributing something, especially official funds
  • Synonym: budget - an estimate of income and expenses for a period; a plan for spending

Etymology

From Latin 'appropriatio', from 'appropriare' (to make one's own).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13310868-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ əˌproʊpriˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌprəʊpriˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of taking something for one's own use, often without permission or legal right
taking something without permission

Examples

The artist was criticized for the appropriation of indigenous designs in her work.
He faced charges following the appropriation of company assets for personal use.
The government's appropriation of private land caused widespread protests.

Synset Examples

the necessary funds were obtained by the government's appropriation of the company's operating unit
a person's appropriation of property belonging to another is dishonest

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: misappropriation - the act of dishonestly or wrongly taking something, especially money
  • Degree Higher: theft - the act of stealing; taking another's property without permission or legal right

Etymology

From Latin appropriatio (a making one's own), from appropriare (to make one's own).
