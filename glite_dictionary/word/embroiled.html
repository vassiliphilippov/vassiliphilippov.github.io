embroiled

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈbrɔɪld / (AmE), / ɪmˈbrɔɪld / (BrE)
deeply involved in a difficult, complicated, or controversial situation
deeply involved in a difficult situation

Examples

Several politicians became embroiled in the corruption scandal that rocked the city.
She found herself embroiled in a heated argument between her two friends.
The company was embroiled in legal disputes for years before reaching a settlement.

Synset Examples

embroiled in the conflict
felt unwilling entangled in their affairs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entangled - involved in difficulties or complicated situations

Etymology

From Old French 'embrouiller' (to tangle, confuse), from 'en-' + 'brouiller' (to mix up).
