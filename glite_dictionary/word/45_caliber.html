.45 caliber

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɔɪnt ˈfɔːrti faɪv ˈkælɪbər / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪnt ˈfɔːti faɪv ˈkælɪbə / (BrE)
(of a firearm or its ammunition) having a bore or bullet diameter of 0.45 inches (about 11.43 mm).
relating to 0.45-inch gun bore size

Examples

He carried a .45 caliber pistol in his holster at all times.
The police retrieved several .45 caliber shells from the crime scene.
Many gun enthusiasts favor the .45 caliber for its stopping power.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caliber - the internal diameter of a gun barrel

Etymology

From the numerical designation of diameter in inches (0.45) plus 'caliber' (from Spanish calibre, from Arabic qālib ‘mold’).
