balata

1. NOUN

/ bəˈlɑtə / (AmE), / bəˈlɑːtə / (BrE)
A tough, rubber-like substance obtained from the latex of certain tropical trees, used in the manufacture of golf balls and machine belts.
rubber-like material from tropical trees

Examples

The covers of early golf balls were often made from balata.
Natural balata is collected from the trees in a way similar to rubber tapping.
Machine belts manufactured from balata are known for their durability and strength.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gutta-percha - a tough plastic substance obtained from the latex of certain trees, used similarly to balata

Etymology

From Spanish 'balata', name for the tree yielding the latex, of unknown origin.

2. NOUN

/ bəˈlɑːtə / (AmE), / bəˈlɑːtə / (BrE)
a tropical hardwood tree (Manilkara bidentata) of tropical America, valued for its heavy red timber and for producing balata gum.
tropical tree yielding balata gum and red timber

Examples

The strong wood of the balata is often used for heavy construction work.
Harvesters collect balata gum by tapping the trees in the rainforest.
Many golf ball covers were once made from balata before synthetic materials became popular.

Etymology

From Spanish 'balata', of Cariban origin.
