spume

1. NOUN

/ spjuːm / (AmE), / spjuːm / (BrE)
Foam or froth, especially on the sea.
sea foam or froth

Examples

The waves crashed onto the shore, leaving behind a layer of spume.
Seabirds skimmed over the spume whipped up by the stormy winds.
After the storm, the beach was covered with white spume and debris.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foam - a mass of small bubbles formed on or in liquid
  • Synonym: froth - a mass of small bubbles formed in or on a liquid

Etymology

From Latin spuma (foam, froth).

2. VERB

/ spjuːm / (AmE), / spjuːm / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to form or produce froth or foam; to become bubbly or frothy.
form or produce foam or froth

Examples

The waves began to spume as the storm intensified near the shore.
Champagne overflowed, spuming from the bottle onto the floor.
Foam spumed around the rocks as the river tumbled downstream.

Synset Examples

Great cliffs loomed above them, and little streams spumed and dashed in rocky gorges below

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foam - to form a mass of small bubbles on the surface of a liquid

Etymology

From Latin spuma (foam, froth).
