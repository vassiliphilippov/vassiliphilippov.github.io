corselet

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːrsəlɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɔːsəlɪt / (BrE)
A piece of body armor covering the trunk, typically consisting of a breastplate and back piece.
piece of body armor for the trunk

Examples

The knight donned his corselet before heading into battle.
Museum visitors admired the ornate corselet displayed in the weaponry section.
His corselet was crafted from polished steel, offering both protection and decoration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breastplate - a piece of armor covering the chest
  • Synonym: cuirass - a piece of armor covering the body from neck to waist

Etymology

From Old French 'corslet', diminutive of 'cors' (body), from Latin 'corpus' (body).
