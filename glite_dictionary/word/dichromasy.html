dichromasy

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈkroʊməsi / (AmE), / daɪˈkrəʊməsi / (BrE)
a deficiency of color vision in which a person can match any color by mixing only two different wavelengths of light, instead of the three required for normal color vision
color vision deficiency involving two wavelengths
medical

Examples

People with dichromasy may have difficulty distinguishing certain colors, such as red and green.
A diagnosis of dichromasy was confirmed after extensive color vision testing.
Research on dichromasy has helped scientists understand how the human eye perceives color.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: trichromacy - normal color vision requiring three wavelengths
  • Degree Lower: monochromasy - color vision with only one wavelength

Etymology

From Greek di- (two) + khrōma (color) + -ia (condition).
