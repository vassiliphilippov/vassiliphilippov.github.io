spectral

1. ADJ

/ ˈspɛk.trəl / (AmE), / ˈspɛk.trəl / (BrE)
relating to or produced by a spectrum, especially of light or electromagnetic radiation.
relating to a spectrum

Examples

The scientist conducted a spectral analysis of the light emitted by the star.
Certain spectral colors are invisible to the human eye.
The spectral data revealed the chemical composition of the gas.

Synset Examples

spectral colors
spectral analysis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spectroscopic - relating to or using a spectroscope or spectroscopy
  • Synonym: optical - relating to sight or light

Etymology

From Latin 'spectrum' (appearance; image; spectrum) + the suffix '-al'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈspɛk.trəl / (AmE), / ˈspɛk.trəl / (BrE)
resembling or characteristic of a ghost or phantom; ghostly or eerie
ghostlike or eerie

Examples

The abandoned house had a spectral atmosphere that made everyone uneasy.
A spectral figure appeared in the mist, vanishing as quickly as it came.
Her pale face looked almost spectral in the moonlight.

Synset Examples

a ghostly face at the window
a phantasmal presence in the room
spectral emanations
spiritual tappings at a seance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ghostly - having the characteristics of a ghost
  • Synonym: phantasmal - resembling a phantom or illusion

Etymology

From Latin 'spectrum' (an appearance, image, or apparition).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01579318-s
Synset POS: s
