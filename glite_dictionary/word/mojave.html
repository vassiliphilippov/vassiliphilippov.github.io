Mojave

1. PROPN

/ moʊˈhɑːvi / (AmE), / məˈhɑːvi / (BrE)
A large desert region in southern California and western Arizona, known for its arid climate and unique landscape.
desert region in southern California and Arizona

Examples

The Mojave is famous for its Joshua trees and striking rock formations.
Temperatures in the Mojave often soar above 100 degrees Fahrenheit during the summer.
Several ghost towns can be found scattered throughout the Mojave Desert region.

Etymology

Named after the Mojave people, indigenous to the area.

2. NOUN

/ moʊˈhɑːvi / (AmE), / məˈhɑːvi / (BrE)
the Yuman language spoken by the Mohave people, an indigenous group from the southwestern United States
a Yuman language of the Mohave people

Examples

The Mojave language is currently spoken by only a few dozen people.
She is studying Mojave to help preserve the traditional culture of the tribe.
Linguists have documented many stories in Mojave over the years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yuman - a family of indigenous languages of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico

Etymology

From the name Mohave (Hamakhaave), the people's own name for themselves and their language.

3. NOUN

/ moʊˈhɑːvi / (AmE), / məˈhɑːvi / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people originally inhabiting the Colorado River valley in Arizona, Nevada, and California
member of a Native American people

Examples

The Mojave have a rich tradition of storytelling and crafts.
Several Mojave elders gathered to share their community's history.
An exhibit on the Mojave people opened at the state museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mohave - alternate spelling for Mojave, member of same group

Etymology

From the Mojave people's own name for themselves, 'Aha Makhav' (people by the river).
