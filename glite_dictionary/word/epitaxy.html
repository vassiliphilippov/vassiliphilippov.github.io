epitaxy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛpɪˌtæksi / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪtaksi / (BrE)
the growth of a crystal layer on the crystalline substrate of another material so that the layer mimics the orientation of the underlying crystal
growth of a crystal layer matching the substrate

Examples

Molecular beam epitaxy allows for precise control of crystal layer thickness.
The scientist studied silicon epitaxy for use in advanced electronics.
Successful epitaxy depends on the compatibility of the crystal lattices.

Etymology

From Greek epi- (upon) + taxis (arrangement, order).
