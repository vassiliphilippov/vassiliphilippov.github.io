inability

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɪnəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the state of being unable to do something; lack of ability, power, or means to accomplish a task.
lack of ability to do something

Examples

His inability to swim prevented him from going on the boat trip.
The company's inability to adapt to new technology led to its decline.
She was frustrated by her inability to remember names at social events.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ability - the power or skill to do something

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (not) + 'habilitas' (ability, aptness).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɪnəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the state of being unable to do something; lack of the necessary ability or power
lack of ability to do something

Examples

His inability to focus made studying for exams very challenging.
The team's inability to score goals cost them the championship.
She was frustrated by her inability to remember names.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ability - the possession of the means or skill to do something
  • Synonym: incapacity - the state of being unable to do something effectively

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (not) + 'habilitas' (ability).
