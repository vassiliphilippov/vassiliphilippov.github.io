Mycetophylidae

1. NOUN

/ maɪˌsiːtəˈfɪlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / maɪˌsiːtəˈfɪlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of small flies, commonly known as fungus gnats, whose larvae typically feed on fungi
a family of small flies (fungus gnats)

Examples

The larvae of the Mycetophylidae typically feed on decaying plant material and fungi.
Scientists discovered several new species within the Mycetophylidae during the rainforest expedition.
Members of the Mycetophylidae are often mistaken for mosquitoes due to their delicate appearance.

Etymology

From Greek 'mykēs' (fungus) and 'phylē' (tribe, race), indicating the group's association with fungi.
