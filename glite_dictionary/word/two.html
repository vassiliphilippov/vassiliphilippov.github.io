two

1. NUM

/ tu / (AmE), / tjuː / (BrE)
The number equivalent to one plus one; 2.
the number 2

Examples

She bought two tickets for the concert next Saturday.
We have two dogs and a cat at home.
Only two people showed up to the meeting.

Synset Examples

he received two messages

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: three - the number equivalent to one plus two; 3
  • Degree Lower: one - the number equivalent to a single unit; 1

Etymology

From Old English 'twā', from Proto-Germanic '*twai', from Proto-Indo-European '*dwóh₁'.

3. NOUN

/ tuː / (AmE), / tuː / (BrE)
A playing card with two spots, being one of four such cards in a standard deck, one from each suit.
playing card with two spots

Examples

He drew the two of hearts from the deck.
The only card that could save her was the two of spades.
I discarded my two because it was the lowest card in my hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deuce - playing card with two spots

4. NUM

/ tuː / (AmE), / tuː / (BrE)
The number equal to one plus one; the cardinal number 2.
the cardinal number 2

Examples

She has two cats and a dog at home.
I will need two tickets for the concert.
Can you give me two reasons why this is important?

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: one - the cardinal number 1
  • Degree Higher: three - the cardinal number 3

Etymology

From Old English 'twā' (feminine/neuter form of 'two'), from Proto-Germanic '*twai', from Proto-Indo-European '*dwóh₁'.
