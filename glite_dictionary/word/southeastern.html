southeastern

1. ADJ

/ ˌsaʊθˈistərn / (AmE), / ˌsaʊθˈiːstən / (BrE)
Situated in, relating to, or oriented toward the southeast.
oriented toward the southeast

Examples

The southeastern part of the island is known for its beautiful beaches.
The southeastern wind brought warm, humid air into the city.
Many rare species are found only in southeastern regions of the country.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: northwestern - situated in or oriented toward the northwest
  • Antonym: southwestern - situated in or oriented toward the southwest
  • Antonym: northeastern - situated in or oriented toward the northeast

2. ADJ

/ ˌsaʊθˈiːstərn / (AmE), / ˌsaʊθˈiːstən / (BrE)
relating to or situated in the southeast, especially of a region such as the southeastern United States, typically including Alabama, Georgia, Florida, Tennessee, South Carolina, and North Carolina.
of or from the southeast region

Examples

Many hurricanes make landfall along the southeastern coast of the United States.
She grew up in a small southeastern town known for its hospitality.
The southeastern region is famous for its warm climate and rich cultural traditions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: northeastern - relating to the northeast region
  • Antonym: southwestern - relating to the southwest region
  • Synonym: southeast - the direction or region midway between south and east

Etymology

Formed from 'south' and 'eastern', meaning 'of the southeast'.
