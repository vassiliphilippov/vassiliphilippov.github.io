Tremella fuciformis

1. NOUN

/ trəˈmɛl.ə ˌfju.səˈfɔr.mɪs / (AmE), / trɪˈmɛl.ə ˌfjuː.sɪˈfɔː.mɪs / (BrE)
a species of gelatinous white mushroom, popular in East Asian cuisine, especially in China, Japan, and Taiwan; typically sold dried
a white, gelatinous mushroom used in Asian cuisine

Examples

Many Chinese desserts feature Tremella fuciformis for its unique texture and health benefits.
After soaking Tremella fuciformis, it expands and becomes translucent and jelly-like.
Cooks often add dried Tremella fuciformis to sweet soups and stews.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snow fungus - the common English name for Tremella fuciformis, an edible gelatinous mushroom
  • Synonym: white fungus - another common name for Tremella fuciformis

Etymology

From Latin: 'tremella' (trembling, jelly-like fungus) and 'fuciformis' (painted, shaped like seaweed).
