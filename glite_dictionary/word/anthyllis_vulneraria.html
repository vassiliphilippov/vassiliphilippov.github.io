Anthyllis vulneraria

1. NOUN

/ ænˈθɪlɪs vʌl.nəˈrɛr.i.ə / (AmE), / ænˈθɪlɪs vʌl.nəˈreə.rɪ.ə / (BrE)
A perennial Eurasian herb with heads of red or yellow flowers, commonly found in meadows and pastures; formerly used in medicine to treat kidney disorders.
Eurasian herb with yellow or red flower heads

Examples

The fields were dotted with the bright yellow heads of Anthyllis vulneraria.
Traditional healers once used Anthyllis vulneraria to treat kidney ailments.
Bees are often attracted to the clusters of flowers produced by Anthyllis vulneraria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kidney vetch - Common name for Anthyllis vulneraria, a flowering herb

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Anthyllis', and species name 'vulneraria' meaning 'wound-healing' from 'vulnus' (wound).
