grouseberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊsˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊsˌbəri / (BrE)
A low shrub of northwestern North America that produces small red berries, commonly found in forests and mountain slopes.
northwestern North American shrub with red berries

Examples

We found a patch of grouseberry growing along the mountain trail.
The grouseberry bush attracts birds who feed on its bright red fruit.
Her research focuses on the habitat of the grouseberry in alpine regions.

Etymology

Formed from 'grouse' (a type of bird) and 'berry,' likely because the berries are eaten by grouse.
