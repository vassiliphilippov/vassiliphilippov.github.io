saute

1. NOUN

/ sɑːˈteɪ / (AmE), / sɔːˈteɪ / (BrE)
a dish made by quickly frying small pieces of food in a small amount of oil or butter over high heat
dish of quickly fried food

Examples

She ordered the chicken saute for her main course.
This seafood saute is cooked with garlic and fresh herbs.
The restaurant's vegetable saute is a popular vegetarian option.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stir-fry - a dish of food quickly fried in a wok with a small amount of oil, often with vegetables and meat

Etymology

From French 'sauté', past participle of 'sauter' meaning 'to jump', referring to how food jumps in the pan when cooked quickly.

2. VERB

/ sɑːˈteɪ / (AmE), / sɔːˈteɪ / (BrE)
To fry food quickly in a small amount of fat over relatively high heat.
fry quickly in a little fat

Examples

First, saute the garlic and onions until they are golden brown.
She likes to saute her vegetables with olive oil for extra flavor.
The recipe says to saute the mushrooms for three minutes before adding the cream.

Synset Examples

saute the onions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fry - to cook food in hot fat or oil, typically in a shallow pan

Etymology

From French 'sauter' (to jump), referencing the tossing motion of food in the pan.

Synset POS: v
