incomprehension

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnˌkɑm.prɪˈhɛn.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnˌkɒm.prɪˈhen.ʃən / (BrE)
The state of being unable to understand something.
inability to understand

Examples

She looked at the maths problem with total incomprehension on her face.
The audience's incomprehension was obvious when the speaker used technical jargon.
His actions were met with incomprehension from his coworkers.

Synset Examples

his incomprehension of the consequences

Related Terms

  • Antonym: understanding - the ability to understand

Etymology

From French 'incompréhension', from Latin 'in-' (not) + 'comprehensio' (grasp).
