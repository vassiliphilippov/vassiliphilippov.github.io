vibration

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈbreɪʃən / (AmE), / vaɪˈbreɪʃən / (BrE)
a feeling or atmosphere that someone senses in a place, situation, or from a person, often perceived instinctively
an atmosphere or feeling sensed instinctively
informal

Examples

There was a tense vibration in the air as the meeting began.
I always get a good vibration from that café; it's so welcoming.
Sometimes you pick up a strange vibration from someone you've just met.

Synset Examples

that place gave me bad vibrations
it gave me a nostalgic vibe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vibe - an atmosphere that a place, event, or person gives you

2. NOUN

/ vaɪˈbreɪʃən / (AmE), / vaɪˈbreɪʃən / (BrE)
(physics) A regular, repeated movement or variation around a central value or position, especially as occurs in sound or other oscillatory phenomena.
regular oscillation around a mean (physics)

Examples

The vibration of the guitar string produced a clear musical note.
Engineers measured the vibration frequency to assess the building's stability.
This instrument detects even slight vibration in the surrounding air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oscillation - swinging or movement back and forth at a regular speed

Etymology

From Latin 'vibrationem' (a shaking, brandishing), from 'vibrare' (to shake, move to and fro).

3. NOUN

/ vaɪˈbreɪʃən / (AmE), / vaɪˈbreɪʃən / (BrE)
the continuous, rapid movement back and forth, or up and down, of an object or substance
rapid back-and-forth movement

Examples

The earthquake caused the entire building to vibrate with a strong vibration.
You can feel a slight vibration when the washing machine is running.
He put his hand on the engine and noticed a strange vibration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oscillation - movement back and forth at a regular speed
  • Antonym: stillness - absence of movement or vibration

Etymology

From Latin 'vibratio', from 'vibrare' meaning 'to shake' or 'sway'.
