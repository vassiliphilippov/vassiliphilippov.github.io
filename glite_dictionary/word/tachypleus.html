Tachypleus

1. NOUN

/ ˌtækiˈpliː.əs / (AmE), / ˌtakɪˈpliː.əs / (BrE)
A genus of horseshoe crabs found in Southeast Asia and surrounding regions.
genus of Asian horseshoe crabs

Examples

The Tachypleus genus includes species commonly used in biomedical research.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Tachypleus genus along the coast of Vietnam.
Several Tachypleus populations are threatened due to habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horseshoe crab - marine arthropod with a hard shell and long tail spine
  • Degree Lower: Limulus - another genus of horseshoe crabs

Etymology

From Greek tachys (swift) + pleus (sailing), referring to the animal's movement.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtækiˈpliːəs / (AmE), / ˌtakɪˈpliːəs / (BrE)
A genus of horseshoe crabs in the family Limulidae, found mainly in Asia.
genus of horseshoe crabs

Examples

The Tachypleus genus comprises several species of Asian horseshoe crabs.
Scientists collected samples of Tachypleus along the coast during their field study.
One of the largest specimens in the region belongs to the Tachypleus genus.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Limulidae - the family of horseshoe crabs

Etymology

From Greek tachys (swift) and pleus (sailer).
