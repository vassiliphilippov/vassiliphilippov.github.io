specification

1. NOUN

/ ˌspɛsəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌspɛsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a detailed and precise description of the requirements, dimensions, materials, or standards needed for a particular job, task, or piece of work
detailed description of requirements or standards

Examples

The architect submitted the specification for the new building to the city council.
Make sure all products meet the specifications before shipping them to customers.
The project was delayed because the initial specification was incomplete.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: requirement - a necessary condition or element
  • Synonym: blueprint - a detailed plan or drawing that describes something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin specificationem, from specificare (to specify).

2. NOUN

/ ˌspɛsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌspɛsɪfɪˈkeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act of stating something clearly and explicitly; the process of naming details or items precisely
explicit naming or clear statement

Examples

The specification of goals helped the team stay focused throughout the project.
Without the specification of the model, the results would have been ambiguous.
Clear specification of requirements is essential in any contract.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to specify - to state something clearly and in detail

Etymology

From Medieval Latin specificationem (a particularizing), from Latin specificare (to specify).

3. NOUN

/ ˌspɛs.ə.fəˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌspɛs.ɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a condition or restriction that is required as part of an agreement or contract
contractual condition or restriction

Examples

The contract included a specification that delivery must be completed within sixty days.
Before signing, she carefully reviewed every specification in the agreement.
Failure to meet the specification could result in legal penalties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: requirement - something that is needed or demanded
  • Synonym: condition - a stipulation or requirement in an agreement

Etymology

From Latin 'specificationem', from 'specificare' (to mention particularly).

4. NOUN

/ ˌspɛsəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌspesɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
(in patent law) a detailed written document describing the nature, design, and intended use of an invention, submitted by an applicant when applying for a patent.
detailed patent application document
legal

Examples

The specification must clearly describe how the invention operates to satisfy the examiner.
After reviewing the specification, the patent office requested more details about the device's mechanism.
The lawyer drafted the specification to ensure every aspect of the process was protected under the patent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patent specification - document setting out the technical details of an invention for patent purposes

Etymology

From Medieval Latin specifīcātiō, from Latin specificare (to specify).

Source: WordNet
