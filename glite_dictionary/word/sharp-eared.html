sharp-eared

1. ADJ

/ ʃɑrp ˈɪrd / (AmE), / ʃɑːp ˈɪəd / (BrE)
having keen or acute hearing; able to hear well or detect sounds easily
having keen hearing

Examples

The sharp-eared detective overheard the suspect's whispered conversation from across the room.
Owls are known for being sharp-eared hunters of the night.
Thanks to her sharp-eared nature, she noticed the faint sound of the phone vibrating upstairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharp-eyed - having keen vision or eyesight
  • Antonym: deaf - unable to hear or having severely impaired hearing
