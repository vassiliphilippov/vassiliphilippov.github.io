precipitancy

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈsɪpɪtənsi / (AmE), / prɪˈsɪpɪtənsi / (BrE)
The quality of acting or occurring with sudden haste or without due consideration; rashness.
sudden or rash haste

Examples

His precipitancy in making the decision led to several unforeseen problems.
The precipitancy of their retreat surprised even their allies.
She blamed the accident on his precipitancy rather than a genuine error.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rashness - The quality of being reckless or acting without consideration
  • Antonym: deliberation - Careful consideration before action

Etymology

From Latin *praecipitāns*, present participle of *praecipitāre* (to throw headlong), via French *précipitant*.
