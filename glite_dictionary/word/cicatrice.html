cicatrice

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪkəˌtris / (AmE), / ˈsɪkəˌtriːs / (BrE)
A mark left on the skin by the healing of a wound; a scar.
scar left by healing of a wound

Examples

He bore a cicatrice on his cheek from a childhood accident.
After surgery, only a faint cicatrice remained on her abdomen.
The old soldier traced the cicatrice on his arm, recalling the battle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scar - a mark left on the skin by a healed wound
  • Degree Higher: wound - an injury to body tissue

Etymology

From Latin cicatrix (scar).
