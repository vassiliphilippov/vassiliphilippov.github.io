durance

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʊrəns / (AmE), / ˈdjʊərəns / (BrE)
imprisonment, especially for a long period; captivity.
imprisonment or captivity
archaic

Examples

After years of durance, he was finally set free by the new government.
The character endured long durance in a remote prison, shaping his outlook forever.
Legends spoke of knights held in cruel durance by their enemies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imprisonment - the state of being imprisoned; confinement
  • Synonym: captivity - the condition of being kept in prison or in a place and not being able to leave or be free

Etymology

From Old French 'durance' (endurance, duration), from 'durer' (to last, to endure), from Latin 'durare' (to harden, last).
