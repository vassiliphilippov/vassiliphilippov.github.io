Bisayan

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈsaɪ.ən / (AmE), / bɪˈsaɪ.ən / (BrE)
a member of the largest indigenous ethnolinguistic group in the Philippines, primarily residing in the Visayas and parts of Mindanao and Luzon
member of the Bisayan people of the Philippines

Examples

Many Bisayan people celebrate vibrant festivals throughout the Visayas region.
After moving to Manila, she missed the traditional food prepared by her Bisayan family.
A group of Bisayan fishermen discussed the latest catch at the harbor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Visayan - member of the Visayan people

Etymology

From Spanish 'Bisaya', derived from the local name 'Bisaya', referring to the Visayan people.
