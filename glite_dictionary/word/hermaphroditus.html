Hermaphroditus

1. PROPN

/ hərˌmæfˈroʊdɪtəs / (AmE), / həˌmæfˈrɒdɪtəs / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the son of Hermes and Aphrodite, who was fused into one body with the nymph Salmacis, thus embodying both male and female characteristics.
mythological figure, son of Hermes and Aphrodite

Examples

In classical art, Hermaphroditus is often portrayed as a figure with both male and female attributes.
A famous myth recounts how Hermaphroditus and Salmacis became one inseparable being.
The statue at the museum depicts Hermaphroditus reclining gracefully.

Etymology

From Latin 'Hermaphroditus', from Ancient Greek 'Ἑρμαφρόδιτος' (Hermaphroditos), named as the son of Hermes and Aphrodite.
