classifier

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæs.ɪ.faɪ.ər / (AmE), / ˈklɑː.sɪ.faɪ.ə / (BrE)
a person who arranges or organizes things into classes or categories
person who organizes into categories

Examples

As a classifier, she developed a new system for organizing the museum's artifacts.
The team hired an expert classifier to sort the biological samples into different species.
Every scientific field needs a skilled classifier to make sense of complex data.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: categorizer - a person who places things in categories

Etymology

From classify + -er, meaning one who classifies.

2. NOUN

/ ˈklæsɪˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈklɑːsɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
A word or morpheme used in some languages, especially in counting, to indicate the semantic class or category of the noun being referred to.
linguistic word class indicator

Examples

In Mandarin Chinese, the classifier 'ge' is used for many common objects.
Languages like Thai and Japanese also use classifier words in counting nouns.
A classifier can specify whether something is flat, round, or long in shape.

Etymology

From classify + -er, following the pattern of forming agent nouns.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06319426-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈklæsɪˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈklɑːsɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
an algorithm or device that automatically assigns items into categories, especially in computing and statistics.
algorithm that assigns categories

Examples

The classifier achieved an accuracy of 95% on the test set.
Choosing the right classifier depends on the nature of your data.
Researchers compared several classifiers to find the best one for image recognition tasks.

Synset Examples

Classifier performance depends greatly on the characteristics of the data to be classified.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: categorizer - something that categorizes items
  • Degree Lower: clusterer - an algorithm that groups unlabeled data

Etymology

From French *classifier*, from Latin *classis* (class) + *-fier* (maker).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-92463236-n
Synset POS: n
