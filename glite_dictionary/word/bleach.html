bleach

1. NOUN

/ bliːtʃ / (AmE), / bliːtʃ / (BrE)
a chemical agent used to make things white or colorless, often for cleaning or disinfecting purposes
chemical for whitening or disinfecting

Examples

She accidentally spilled bleach on her shirt and ruined the color.
It is important to use bleach carefully when cleaning bathrooms.
Many people rely on bleach to disinfect surfaces during flu season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disinfectant - a substance used to destroy bacteria and viruses
  • Antonym: dye - a substance used to add color to materials

Etymology

From Old English 'blǣcan', meaning to whiten.

/ bliːtʃ / (AmE), / bliːtʃ / (BrE)

Examples

The bleach of her new shirt made it stand out among the old ones.
Several applications were needed to achieve a complete bleach of the textile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bleaching - the process of whitening by removing color

4. NOUN

/ bliːtʃ / (AmE), / bliːtʃ / (BrE)
the process of whitening or removing color from something by exposing it to sunlight or using a chemical agent
whitening process using sunlight or chemicals

Examples

The linen went through a thorough bleach before it was considered ready for use.
Sunlight can be an effective natural bleach for stains on white clothing.
The cotton was given a final bleach to achieve the desired brightness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bleaching - the process or act of making something white or whiter, especially by chemical means
  • Antonym: dyeing - the process of adding color to or changing the color of something

Etymology

From Middle English blechen, from Old English blǣċan (to whiten, bleach).
