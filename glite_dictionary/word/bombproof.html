bombproof

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɑːmˌpruːf / (AmE), / ˈbɒmˌpruːf / (BrE)
Strong enough to resist the explosive force of bombs and shells.
able to resist explosive force

Examples

The new military bunker is completely bombproof and designed to withstand direct hits.
They installed a bombproof door at the embassy for added security.
Workers inspected the bombproof shelter after the air raid ended.

Synset Examples

bombproof shelter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bulletproof - resistant to bullets or gunfire

Etymology

Formed from 'bomb' + 'proof', after the pattern of 'bulletproof'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02535891-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːmˌpruf / (AmE), / ˈbɒmˌpruːf / (BrE)
a reinforced shelter, typically underground, designed to withstand bombing and equipped with basic living facilities, used for protection during air raids
reinforced underground shelter against bombing

Examples

During the war, the family spent nights in the bombproof to stay safe from aerial attacks.
The museum preserved an old bombproof as a reminder of the city's wartime history.
Officials inspected the bombproof to ensure it was stocked with enough food and water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bunker - a reinforced underground shelter, especially for military use
  • Synonym: shelter - a place giving temporary protection from bad weather or danger

Etymology

From 'bomb' (explosive device) + 'proof' (protected against), meaning resistant to bombs.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02871978-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈbɑːmˌpruf / (AmE), / ˈbɒmˌpruːf / (BrE)
to make (a building or structure) safe against the effects of bombs or bombing
make safe against bombs

Examples

The engineers worked around the clock to bombproof the government building.
After the attack, officials decided to bombproof key infrastructure in the city.
They spent millions to bombproof the embassy before reopening it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fortify - to strengthen a place with defensive works

Etymology

Formed from 'bomb' and 'proof' in the sense of resistant or protected from.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00166028-v
Synset POS: v
