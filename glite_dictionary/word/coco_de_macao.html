coco de macao

1. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊkoʊ də məˈkaʊ / (AmE), / ˌkəʊkəʊ də məˈkaʊ / (BrE)
a tall feather palm of northern Brazil that produces hard-shelled nuts yielding valuable oil and a type of vegetable ivory
Brazilian palm yielding oil and vegetable ivory

Examples

The coco de macao is valued for its hard nuts, which can be carved like ivory.
Many artisans in Brazil use coco de macao to make jewelry and buttons.
The oil extracted from the coco de macao has a variety of commercial applications.

Etymology

From Portuguese, meaning 'Macau coconut'; 'coco' meaning coconut, 'Macao' referring to a place or the appearance.
