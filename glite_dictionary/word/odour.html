odour

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊdər / (AmE), / ˈəʊdə / (BrE)
a smell, especially one that is distinctive or unpleasant, perceived by the sense of olfaction
a particular smell detected by the nose
british

Examples

The odour of fresh paint lingered in the room for days.
Her kitchen was filled with the odour of baking bread.
A strong odour coming from the garage made them investigate further.

Synset Examples

she loved the smell of roses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: odor - the American English spelling of 'odour'
  • Degree Higher: fragrance - a pleasant, sweet smell
  • Synonym: smell - the sensation or quality detected by olfactory organs

Etymology

From Latin 'odor' (smell).

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊdər / (AmE), / ˈəʊdə / (BrE)
a smell or scent, especially one that is distinctive or unpleasant, detected by the sense of smell
a smell detected by the nose
british

Examples

The strong odour of garlic lingered in the kitchen long after the meal had ended.
She noticed a faint odour of perfume as she walked into the room.
An unpleasant odour coming from the bin made everyone wrinkle their noses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: odor - a smell; American English spelling
  • Degree Higher: fragrance - a pleasant, sweet smell
  • Degree Higher: stench - a very unpleasant smell

Etymology

From Old French 'odour', from Latin 'odor' (smell, scent).
