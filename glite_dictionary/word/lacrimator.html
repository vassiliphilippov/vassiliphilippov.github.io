lacrimator

1. NOUN

/ ˈlækrəˌmeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈlak.rɪ.meɪ.tər / (BrE)
A gas or substance that causes the eyes to produce tears, especially used for crowd control without causing lasting harm.
tear-producing gas or substance

Examples

Police used a lacrimator to disperse the protesters outside the building.
Exposure to a lacrimator can cause intense eye irritation and temporary blindness.
The military stockpiles various types of lacrimator for riot control purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tear gas - a gas that causes severe irritation to the eyes, used for crowd control

Etymology

From Latin 'lacrimare' (to weep), from 'lacrima' (tear).
