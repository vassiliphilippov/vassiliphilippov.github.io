hollyhock

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑliˌhɑk / (AmE), / ˈhɒliˌhɒk / (BrE)
A tall flowering plant of the genus Alcea, grown in gardens for its large, colorful blooms.
tall, flowering garden plant

Examples

The old garden was lined with hollyhock along the fence, their blossoms swaying in the breeze.
She planted several hollyhocks to add height and color to the flower bed.
Each summer, the vibrant hollyhock flowers attract bees and butterflies to the yard.

Etymology

From Middle English 'holi-hoc', combining 'holy' and 'hoc' (mallow).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑliˌhɑk / (AmE), / ˈhɒlɪhɒk / (BrE)
A tall plant of the mallow family, bearing large, showy, white, pink, purple, or yellow flowers, especially of the genus Althaea or Alcea, often grown in gardens.
tall flowering plant of the mallow family

Examples

The hollyhock bloomed brilliantly along the garden fence throughout the summer.
She planted a row of hollyhocks to add some height and color to her flower bed.
Bees buzzed around the pink hollyhock blossoms on the warm July afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mallow - plant of the Malvaceae family with showy flowers

Etymology

From Middle English holihoc, probably from holi (holy) + hoc (mallow).
