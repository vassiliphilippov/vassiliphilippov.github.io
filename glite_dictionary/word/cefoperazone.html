cefoperazone

1. NOUN

/ sɛˌfoʊpəˈræzoʊn / (AmE), / sɛˌfəʊpəˈræzəʊn / (BrE)
a semisynthetic cephalosporin antibiotic used by injection to treat severe bacterial infections
a cephalosporin antibiotic drug
medical

Examples

The hospital prescribed cefoperazone to treat the patient's severe infection.
Doctors sometimes use cefoperazone when other antibiotics have failed.
Allergic reactions to cefoperazone are possible, so patients are monitored closely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cephalosporin - a group of antibiotics related to penicillin

Etymology

From 'cephalosporin', a class of antibiotics, with the suffix -perazone (indicating its chemical structure).
