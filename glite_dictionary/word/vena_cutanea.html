vena cutanea

1. NOUN

/ ˌviːnə kjuːˈteɪniə / (AmE), / ˌviːnə kjuːˈteɪniə / (BrE)
A vein located in the subcutaneous tissue that drains into deeper veins.
subcutaneous vein draining to deep veins
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully avoided the vena cutanea during the procedure.
Damage to a vena cutanea can cause severe bruising under the skin.
Several venae cutaneae converge to empty into deeper veins in the leg.

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein) and 'cutanea' (of the skin).
