parenthetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɛrənˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌparənˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
(of a remark or phrase) qualifying or explaining something, especially as if enclosed in parentheses; not essential to the main point.
of a remark: qualifying, explanatory, in parentheses

Examples

His essay was full of parenthetic asides that distracted from the main argument.
Please ignore the parenthetic comments and focus on the main points of the text.
She added a parenthetic explanation to clarify her statement.

Synset Examples

parenthetical remarks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parenthetical - of, relating to, or inserted as a parenthesis

Etymology

From Greek *parenthetikos*, from *parentithenai* 'to put in beside'.
