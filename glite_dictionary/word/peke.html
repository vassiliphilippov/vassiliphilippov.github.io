Peke

1. NOUN

/ pik / (AmE), / piÀêk / (BrE)
A small, short-legged dog of the Pekingese breed, characterized by a long silky coat and a broad flat muzzle.
small breed of dog; Pekingese

Examples

My grandmother has a Peke that loves to sit on her lap all day.
The Peke requires regular grooming to keep its coat looking beautiful.
She entered her Peke in the local dog show and won first prize.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pekingese - a breed of small dog originally from China, with a long coat and flat face

Etymology

Shortened form of Pekingese, referring to the breed's origin in Peking (now Beijing), China.
