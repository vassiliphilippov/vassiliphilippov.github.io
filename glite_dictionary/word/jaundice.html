jaundice

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒɔndɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːndɪs / (BrE)
to affect with or as if with jaundice; to influence or distort by envy, prejudice, or bitterness
to affect with jaundice or prejudice

Examples

The bitter experience had jaundiced his outlook on life.
Repeated disappointments began to jaundice her perception of her colleagues.
His opinions were jaundiced by years of unresolved conflict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prejudice - to influence (someone) with an unreasonable bias

Etymology

From Old French 'jaunice', from 'jaune' meaning 'yellow', referring to the yellowing of the skin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɔn.dɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːn.dɪs / (BrE)
a medical condition marked by yellowing of the skin and the whites of the eyes, caused by an accumulation of bilirubin in the blood, often indicating liver or bile duct disease
yellowing of skin and eyes; medical condition
medical

Examples

The doctor suspected hepatitis when he noticed the patient's jaundice and other symptoms.
A sudden onset of jaundice can be a sign of a serious liver disorder.
Infants are sometimes born with mild jaundice that usually clears up on its own.

Etymology

From Old French 'jaunisse', from 'jaune' (yellow), ultimately from Latin 'galbinus' (yellowish).

3. VERB

/ ˈdʒɑndɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːndɪs / (BrE)
to affect or influence with prejudice, bitterness, or envy, especially so as to distort judgment
to distort with prejudice or envy

Examples

Long years of isolation had jaundiced his outlook on life.
We should avoid letting personal feelings jaundice our impartiality.
Her recent experiences had jaundiced her views toward the project.

Synset Examples

Jealousy had jaundiced his judgment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bias - to influence in a particular, often unfair, direction
  • Synonym: prejudice - to injure or distort by preconceived opinion or feeling

Etymology

From Old French *jaunice* (yellowness), from *jaune* (yellow). The figurative sense alludes to the yellowing distorting effect of literal jaundice.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
