Cistus albidus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪstəs ælˈbaɪdəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪstəs ælˈbiːdəs / (BrE)
A compact shrub native to southwestern Europe with white hairy leaves and pink flowers, belonging to the rockrose family.
a pink-flowered shrub of southwestern Europe

Examples

Cistus albidus thrives in dry, rocky soils commonly found in Mediterranean regions.
Gardeners prize Cistus albidus for its attractive pink blossoms and drought-resistant qualities.
Botanists noted that Cistus albidus blooms abundantly in late spring.

Etymology

From Latin: Cistus (rockrose) + albidus (whitish), referring to the pale appearance of the plant.
