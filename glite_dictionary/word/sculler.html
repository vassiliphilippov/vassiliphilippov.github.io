sculler

1. NOUN

/ ˈskʌlɚ / (AmE), / ˈskʌlə / (BrE)
a person who propels a boat using a single oar or a pair of sculls
person who rows with sculls

Examples

As a dedicated sculler, she trains every morning on the river.
The sculler swiftly moved ahead of his competitors in the single sculls race.
Each sculler must master balance and technique to succeed in the sport.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rower - a person who rows a boat

Etymology

From 'scull' (to row with a scull), from Middle Low German 'schulle' (oar).
