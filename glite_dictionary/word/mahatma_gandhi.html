Mahatma Gandhi

1. PROPN

/ məˈhɑtmə ˈɡɑndi / (AmE), / məˈhɑːtmə ˈɡandi / (BrE)
(1869–1948) Indian political and spiritual leader who led the country's struggle for independence from British rule through nonviolent resistance.
Indian independence leader, 1869–1948

Examples

Mahatma Gandhi is remembered worldwide for his philosophy of nonviolent protest.
The teachings of Mahatma Gandhi have inspired civil rights movements across the globe.
Statues of Mahatma Gandhi can be found in many major cities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gandhi - the surname or reference to Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'mahatma' meaning 'great soul'; used as an honorific for Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi.
