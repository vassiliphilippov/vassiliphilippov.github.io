welfare

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛlˌfɛr / (AmE), / ˈwɛl.feə / (BrE)
The health, happiness, and fortunes of a person or group; general well-being or prosperity.
well-being; health and happiness

Examples

The charity is committed to improving the welfare of children in developing countries.
Government policies often aim to protect the welfare of their citizens.
The association organized a fundraiser for the welfare of elderly residents.

Synset Examples

for the benefit of all

Related Terms

  • Synonym: well-being - state of being comfortable, healthy, or happy
  • Antonym: misery - a state of great suffering or unhappiness

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old English wel (well) + fare (a going, journey, or condition).

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɛlˌfɛr / (AmE), / ˈwɛl.fɛə / (BrE)
Governmental provision of financial or other assistance to individuals in need, especially through organized programs.
government-provided economic assistance

Examples

After losing her job, she had to rely on welfare to support her family.
The debate over welfare reform dominated last night's political discussion.
He grew up in a neighborhood where many residents depended on welfare.

Synset Examples

she lives on welfare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benefit - a government grant or payment to help those in need
  • Synonym: social security - a government system providing monetary assistance to people with an inadequate or no income

Etymology

From Old English 'wel' (well) + 'faran' (to go, fare).
