regimentation

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛdʒ.ə.mɛn.təˈʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛdʒ.ɪ.men.təˈʃən / (BrE)
the act of imposing strict order, discipline, or uniformity, often in a way that limits individuality or freedom
imposition of strict order or discipline

Examples

The strict regimentation of the army left little room for personal expression.
Some schools avoid excessive regimentation to encourage creativity in students.
Many workers felt stifled by the regimentation of the new management policies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discipline - the practice of training people to obey rules or a code of behavior
  • Antonym: flexibility - the quality of bending easily without breaking; willingness to change or compromise

Etymology

From regiment + -ation, ultimately from Latin 'regimentum' (rule) and '-ation' (action or process).
