Pelycosauria

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛlɪkoʊˈsɔriə / (AmE), / ˌpɛlɪkəˈsɔːrɪə / (BrE)
an extinct order of primitive synapsid reptiles, including species like Edaphosaurus and Dimetrodon, that lived during the late Paleozoic Era
extinct order of prehistoric reptiles

Examples

The Pelycosauria were some of the earliest large terrestrial vertebrates.
Modern mammals share some ancestral traits with the Pelycosauria.
Fossils of Pelycosauria like Dimetrodon have been found in North America and Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synapsid - major group of amniotes including pelycosaurs and mammals
  • Degree Higher: reptile - cold-blooded, scaly vertebrate animal

Etymology

From Greek 'pelyx' (bowl, pelvis) + 'sauros' (lizard), referring to pelvic features; coined in scientific taxonomy.
