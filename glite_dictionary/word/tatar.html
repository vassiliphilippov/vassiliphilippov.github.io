Tatar

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːtɑr / (AmE), / ˈtɑːtɑː / (BrE)
the Turkic language spoken by the Tatar people, primarily in the region between the Volga and Ural Mountains in Russia
a Turkic language of the Tatars

Examples

She is fluent in both Russian and Tatar.
The Tatar language uses a variant of the Cyrillic alphabet in modern times.
Linguists have studied Tatar to explore its Turkic roots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turkish - the main Turkic language spoken in Turkey
  • Antonym: Russian - the Slavic language widely spoken in Russia and surrounding areas

Etymology

From Russian Татар (Tatar), from Persian تاتار (Tātār), the ethnic name.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːtɑr / (AmE), / ˈtɑːtɑː / (BrE)
a member of a Turkic-speaking ethnic group originating primarily from the region between the Volga and Ural Mountains, but the name has also been applied to related groups.
a Turkic ethnic group of Eurasia

Examples

The Tatar community has a rich tradition of literature and music.
Many Tatar people live in the Republic of Tatarstan in Russia.
He learned the Tatar language while traveling through the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turkic - relating to the family of Turkic peoples or languages

Etymology

From Persian Tātār, via Russian Татар (Tatar), possibly ultimately of Mongolian origin.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːtər / (AmE), / ˈtɑːtɑː / (BrE)
a member of a Turkic-speaking people originally from central Asia, known for their invasions of parts of Eastern Europe in the 13th century
a member of a Turkic people of central Asia

Examples

Many Tatar warriors crossed into Europe during the Mongol invasions.
The Tatar community in Russia maintains its cultural identity today.
He was fascinated by the history of the Tatar people and their influence on Eastern Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mongol - a member of an East-Central Asian ethnic group, historically known as conquerors of large parts of Eurasia

Etymology

From Persian Tātār, the name applied to the Mongol and Turkic peoples of the steppes.

Source: WordNet
