unfit

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfɪt / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɪt / (BrE)
not in good physical or mental condition; lacking the fitness or health required for a particular purpose
not in good physical or mental condition

Examples

After months of inactivity, he felt unfit and sluggish.
The doctor declared her unfit for work after the accident.
Many soldiers were found physically unfit during the medical examination.

Synset Examples

fat and very unfit
certified as unfit for army service
drunk and unfit for service

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fit - in good physical or mental condition

Etymology

From the prefix un- (not) + fit.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01021330-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfɪt / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɪt / (BrE)
not suitable or good enough for a particular purpose or situation; below the required standard.
not suitable; below standard

Examples

The building was deemed unfit for habitation after the inspection.
Some foods are considered unfit for infants due to potential allergens.
His lack of experience made him unfit for the leadership role.

Synset Examples

an unfit parent
unfit for human consumption

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fit - suitable or good enough for a particular purpose
  • Synonym: suitable - appropriate for a particular person, purpose, or situation

Etymology

From un- (not) + fit (suitable), first attested in the 16th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01024917-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfɪt / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɪt / (BrE)
(of a person or body part) not in a healthy condition; suffering from disease or physical weakness.
physically unhealthy or diseased

Examples

After the accident, his leg was unfit for running long distances.
The doctor declared him unfit for strenuous activity due to his heart condition.
The neglected horse was found to be unfit because of severe malnutrition.

Synset Examples

has a bad back
a bad heart
bad teeth
an unsound limb
unsound teeth

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fit - in good health; physically healthy and strong
  • Antonym: healthy - in good or sound physical condition

Etymology

From un- (not) + fit (in good health or proper condition).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01177766-s
Synset POS: s

4. VERB

/ ʌnˈfɪt / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɪt / (BrE)
to make unsuitable, incapable, or not qualified for something; to render unfit
make unsuitable or incapable

Examples

The disease may unfit him for military service.
A criminal record can unfit someone for certain jobs.
Harsh working conditions may unfit workers for further employment.

Synset Examples

Your income disqualifies you

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disqualify - to make ineligible or unsuitable for something

Etymology

From un- (not) + fit (suitable).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00302119-v
Synset POS: v
