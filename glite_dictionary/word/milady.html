milady

1. NOUN

/ məˈleɪdi / (AmE), / mɪˈleɪdi / (BrE)
a term of address or reference for an English noblewoman, especially in historical contexts
English noblewoman (term of address)

Examples

The butler bowed deeply and addressed her as milady upon her entrance.
In Victorian novels, it is common for servants to refer to the mistress of the house as milady.
When milady travelled, she expected the highest standards of service throughout her journey.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: milord - a term of address for an English nobleman

Etymology

From French 'ma' (my) + 'lady', modeled after 'milord'.
