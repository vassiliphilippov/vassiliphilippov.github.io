chair

1. NOUN

/ tʃɛr / (AmE), / tʃeə / (BrE)
a particular seat or rank assigned to a musician in an orchestra, often indicating order or responsibility within a section
rank or seat in an orchestra

Examples

After years of practice, she finally earned the first chair in the cello section.
The second chair clarinetist played the solo beautifully.
He was disappointed to be moved from principal to third chair violin.

Synset Examples

he is second chair violin

the person who leads or presides at a meeting, committee, or organization

Examples

/ tʃɛr / (AmE), / tʃɛː / (BrE)
A separate seat for one person, typically having a back and four legs.
seat with a back for one person

Examples

The chair near the window was her favorite spot for reading.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: stool - a seat without a back or arms
  • Degree Higher: armchair - a comfortable chair with side supports for a person's arms
  • Degree Higher: bench - long seat for more than one person

Etymology
