responsive

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈspɑːnsɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈspɒnsɪv / (BrE)
reacting quickly and positively to a stimulus, suggestion, or request
reacting to a stimulus; quick to respond

Examples

The patient's skin was highly responsive to temperature changes during the examination.
Good customer service means being responsive to clients' needs and feedback.
Some animals are more responsive to light and noise than others.

Synset Examples

the skin of old persons is less reactive than that of younger persons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reactive - tending to react to a stimulus or influence

Etymology

From Latin respondēre (to answer).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02112456-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈspɑnsɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈspɒnsɪv / (BrE)
(of an activity or communication) involving an exchange of responses or replies, especially alternately between two parties.
alternating or involving replies; in exchange

Examples

The congregation participated in a responsive reading during the service.
Their laughter had an almost responsive quality, echoing back and forth across the room.
The ceremony included responsive prayers between the leader and the audience.

Synset Examples

responsive reading
antiphonal laughter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiphonal - (especially in music or liturgy) performed responsively or in alternation by two groups

Etymology

From Latin respondēre (to answer) + English suffix -ive.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03122391-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ rɪˈspɑnsɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈspɒnsɪv / (BrE)
Reacting quickly and positively to people, events, or stimuli; showing a willingness to respond or react.
quick or willing to react or reply

Examples

The new customer service team is very responsive to inquiries and feedback.
Her face was responsive, showing every emotion as she listened to the story.
Teachers appreciate students who are responsive and engaged during lessons.

Synset Examples

children are often the quickest and most responsive members of the audience

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unresponsive - not reacting or showing willingness to respond
  • Synonym: reactive - showing a response to a stimulus or situation

Etymology

From Latin responsivus, from respondere (to answer).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02006406-a
Synset POS: a
