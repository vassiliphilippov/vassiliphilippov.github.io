glutton

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlʌtən / (AmE), / ˈɡlʌtən / (BrE)
a person who eats and drinks excessively, often beyond what is reasonable or healthy
person who eats to excess

Examples

He's such a glutton that he finished two whole pizzas by himself.
During the festival, even the most disciplined eater can become a glutton.
She's a real glutton for desserts, never missing a chance to try something sweet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gourmand - person who enjoys eating and often eats too much
  • Antonym: moderate eater - person who eats moderately or with restraint

Etymology

From Old French *gloton*, from Latin *glutto* (one who swallows, glutton).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡlʌtən / (AmE), / ˈɡlʌt(ə)n / (BrE)
a large, carnivorous musteline mammal (Gulo gulo) of northern Eurasia and North America, also known as the wolverine
musteline mammal, wolverine

Examples

The glutton is known for its incredible strength and ferocity for its size.
Despite its name, the glutton is an elusive animal rarely seen by humans in the wild.
Researchers tracked a glutton through the snowy forests using radio collars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wolverine - large musteline mammal, alternative name for glutton

Etymology

From Old French *gloton*, from Latin *glutto* (greedy person), extended to the animal due to its voracious appetite.
