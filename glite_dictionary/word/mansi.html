Mansi

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænsi / (AmE), / ˈmansi / (BrE)
a member of an indigenous, traditionally nomadic people living in the northern Ural region of Russia
member of an indigenous Siberian people

Examples

The Mansi have preserved many aspects of their traditional culture despite modern influences.
Anthropologists have studied the lifestyle of the Mansi for decades.
Some Mansi still live in the taiga forests, practicing reindeer herding and hunting.

Etymology

From Russian Манси (Mansi), the self-designation of the people.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːnsi / (AmE), / ˈmansi / (BrE)
An Ugric language, closely related to Hungarian, spoken by the Mansi people (formerly known as Vogul) in western Siberia.
an Ugric language of western Siberia

Examples

The Mansi language is one of the few remaining Ugric languages still spoken today.
Researchers documented several dialects of Mansi during their expedition to western Siberia.
She wrote her thesis on the grammatical structure of Mansi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hungarian - the Ugric language spoken in Hungary

Etymology

From Russian Манси (Mansi), the self-designation of the Mansi people.
