indemnification

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌdɛm.nə.fəˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌdɛm.nɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a sum of money paid to compensate for loss or injury
compensation for loss or injury
legal

Examples

The contract includes provisions for indemnification in case of property damage.
He received an indemnification from the insurance company after the accident.
The plaintiff is seeking indemnification for the financial losses incurred.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compensation - money awarded to compensate for loss or injury
  • Synonym: damages - money claimed or awarded as compensation for harm or injury

Etymology

From Medieval Latin indemnificatio (compensation for loss), from Latin indemnis (uninjured).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˌdɛm.nə.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌdɛm.nɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of compensating for loss, damage, or inconvenience, especially by payment or other restitution
act of compensating for loss or damage

Examples

The contract requires the company to provide indemnification for any legal costs incurred by its employees.
After the flood, the insurer offered indemnification for the damages sustained to the property.
He sought indemnification from the manufacturer after the product malfunctioned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compensation - something, typically money, awarded to someone as recompense for loss, injury, or suffering
  • Synonym: reimbursement - the act of repaying someone for money spent or lost

Etymology

From Late Latin indemnificatio (securing against loss), from Latin indemnis (unhurt, free from loss).
