isthmus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪsθməs / (AmE), / ˈɪsθməs / (BrE)
A narrow strip of land with sea on either side, forming a link between two larger areas of land.
narrow land bridge between two larger areas

Examples

The Panama isthmus connects Central and South America.
The ancient city was located on a narrow isthmus between two seas.
Geographers studied the unique biodiversity of the isthmus area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peninsula - a piece of land almost surrounded by water or projecting out into a body of water

Etymology

From Greek isthmos (neck of land).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪs.məs / (AmE), / ˈɪs.məs / (BrE)
A narrow strip or band of tissue connecting two larger parts of an organ or structure, especially in anatomy.
narrow tissue connecting two larger anatomical parts
medical

Examples

The thyroid isthmus connects the two lobes of the gland across the front of the trachea.
During surgery, the surgeon had to carefully avoid damaging the isthmus of the brain's corpus callosum.
Some congenital conditions involve abnormal development of the uterine isthmus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bridge - a structure or tissue that connects two parts

Etymology

From Greek *isthmos* (neck of land, narrow passage).

Synset POS: n
