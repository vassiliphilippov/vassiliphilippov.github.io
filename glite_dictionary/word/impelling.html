impelling

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
having a strong or compelling effect that motivates or urges someone emotionally or psychologically.
having a motivating or compelling emotional effect

Examples

Her impelling speech inspired the audience to take immediate action.
There was an impelling sense of urgency in his voice that could not be ignored.
The documentary has an impelling narrative that keeps viewers emotionally invested throughout.

Synset Examples

impelling skill as a teller of tales
an impelling personality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compelling - evoking interest, attention, or admiration in a powerfully irresistible way

Etymology

From the verb 'impel', from Latin 'impellere' meaning 'to drive on'.
