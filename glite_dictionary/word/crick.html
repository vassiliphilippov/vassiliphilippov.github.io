crick

1. NOUN

/ krɪk / (AmE), / krɪk / (BrE)
a sudden, painful muscle spasm, especially in the neck or back, often limiting movement temporarily
painful muscle spasm in neck or back

Examples

He woke up with a crick in his neck after sleeping awkwardly.
A sudden crick in my back made it hard to stand up straight.
She couldn't turn her head because of a painful crick that developed during the drive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spasm - a sudden involuntary muscular contraction
  • Antonym: sprain - an injury to a ligament caused by being stretched

2. VERB

/ krɪk / (AmE), / krɪk / (BrE)
to twist or bend a part of your body, especially your neck, into an awkward or strained position, often causing pain
twist or strain a body part (esp. the neck)

Examples

She accidentally cricked her neck while reaching for a book on a high shelf.
If you crick your back during exercise, stop immediately and stretch gently.
I must have cricked my shoulder sleeping in that awkward position last night.

Synset Examples

crick your neck

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strain - to injure or damage by overexertion or twisting
  • Synonym: sprain - to wrench or twist the ligaments of a joint violently
