commandery

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmændəri / (AmE), / kəˈmændəri / (BrE)
the position, office, or jurisdiction of a commander, especially in military or chivalric orders
position or office of a commander

Examples

He was appointed to the commandery after many years of distinguished service.
The commandery held great influence within the order during the Middle Ages.
Her research focuses on the historical structure of the commandery within knighthood organizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: command - to give orders; have authority over
  • Synonym: commander - a person in authority, especially in the military

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'commanderia' (command, office of a commander).
