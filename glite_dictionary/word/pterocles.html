Pterocles

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛrəˌkliz / (AmE), / ˈtɛrəˌkliːz / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Pteroclididae, comprising several species of sandgrouse found in dry regions of Africa and Asia.
genus of sandgrouse birds

Examples

The genus Pterocles includes several species adapted to arid environments in Africa and Asia.
Researchers observed that Pterocles sandgrouse often travel long distances in search of water.
The unique plumage patterns of Pterocles have intrigued ornithologists for decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pteroclididae - family of birds including sandgrouse

Etymology

From New Latin *Pterocles*, derived from Greek *pteron* (feather) and *klēs* (calling, famous), used in scientific naming.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛrəˌkliːz / (AmE), / ˈtɛrəˌkliːz / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Pteroclididae, comprising the sandgrouse found in arid regions of the Old World.
type genus of sandgrouse

Examples

The genus Pterocles includes several species adapted to dry, open habitats.
Birdwatchers in Africa often seek out members of the Pterocles genus for their striking plumage.
Taxonomists debated whether a new species should be placed within Pterocles or a different genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandgrouse - a bird of the family Pteroclididae

Etymology

From Greek pteron (feather, wing) and -cles (suffix in zoological genus names).
