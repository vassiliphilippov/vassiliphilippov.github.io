unseamed

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsiːmd / (AmE), / ʌnˈsiːmd / (BrE)
(of skin or surfaces) perfectly smooth; without lines or creases.
smooth, without lines or creases

Examples

Her radiant, unseamed complexion made her look years younger than her age.
The statue’s unseamed marble surface glowed softly in the candlelight.
He gazed at the child’s unseamed face, untouched by time or worry.

Synset Examples

his cheeks were unlined
his unseamed face

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lined - marked with lines or wrinkles, especially skin

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsimd / (AmE), / ʌnˈsiːmd / (BrE)
(of a garment or object) having no seams; made in one piece or without seams.
having no seams

Examples

The raincoat was entirely unseamed, keeping water from leaking through the fabric.
She admired the unseamed design of the dress, which gave it a smooth, modern look.
Modern plastics can be molded into unseamed containers, avoiding traditional joints or glue.

Synset Examples

an unseamed garment made of plastic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seamless - having no seams or joints; smooth and continuous
