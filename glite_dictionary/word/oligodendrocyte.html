oligodendrocyte

1. NOUN

/ əˌlɪɡoʊˈdɛndrəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˌɒlɪɡəʊˈdɛndrəsʌɪt / (BrE)
a type of glial cell in the central nervous system that produces myelin, which insulates nerve fibers
type of glial cell in CNS producing myelin
medical

Examples

Damage to oligodendrocytes can result in the loss of myelin in the brain.
An oligodendrocyte can myelinate multiple axons at once in the central nervous system.
The researchers analyzed the role of oligodendrocytes in neurodegenerative diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astrocyte - star-shaped glial cell in the brain and spinal cord

Etymology

From Greek oligos (few), dendron (tree), and kytos (cell).
