catbird

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈkætbɜːd / (BrE)
A North American songbird with a call that resembles a cat's mewing.
North American songbird

Examples

A catbird was singing in the bushes near the riverbank.
The distinctive cry of the catbird made it easy to identify among other birds.
We spotted several catbirds during our morning hike in the forest.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkætˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈkatˌbɜːd / (BrE)
any of various Australasian birds whose males build elaborate, decorated structures (bowers) to attract females, especially in the family Ptilonorhynchidae
Australasian bird known for building bowers

Examples

Male catbirds are famous for their skill in building complex bowers to woo potential mates.
The plumage of the catbird often helps it blend into the dense forests of Australia and New Guinea.
Scientists studied how different decorations affect a catbird's success in attracting females.
