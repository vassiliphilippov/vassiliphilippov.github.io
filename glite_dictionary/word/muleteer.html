muleteer

1. NOUN

/ ˌmjuːləˈtɪr / (AmE), / ˌmjuːlɪˈtɪə / (BrE)
a person who drives and looks after mules, especially as an occupation
person who drives mules

Examples

The muleteer led his animals carefully along the narrow mountain path.
As a young man, he found work as a muleteer in the remote villages of Spain.
The muleteer packed supplies onto the animals before setting out at dawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muleskinner - a person who drives or manages pack mules, especially in the United States

Etymology

From Spanish 'muletero' (driver of mules), from 'mula' (mule).
