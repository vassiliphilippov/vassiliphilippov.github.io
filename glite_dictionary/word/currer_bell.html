Currer Bell

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɜːrər bɛl / (AmE), / ˈkʌr.ər bɛl / (BrE)
the pen name used by English novelist Charlotte Brontë (1816–1855), author of works such as 'Jane Eyre'.
pseudonym of novelist Charlotte Brontë

Examples

The novel 'Jane Eyre' was first published under the pseudonym Currer Bell.
Many critics speculated about the true identity of Currer Bell after the book's release.
Charlotte Brontë used the name Currer Bell to bypass the prejudices against female authors of her time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Charlotte Brontë - English novelist and author of 'Jane Eyre'

Etymology

Pseudonym adopted by Charlotte Brontë to conceal her gender when publishing her works in the 19th century.
