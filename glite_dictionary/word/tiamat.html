Tiamat

1. PROPN

/ ˈtiː.əˌmɑt / (AmE), / ˈtiː.əˌmæt / (BrE)
An ancient Babylonian goddess regarded as the mother of the gods and the consort of Apsu, often associated with chaos and the primordial sea.
Babylonian mother goddess; consort of Apsu

Examples

The myth tells how Tiamat battled Marduk during the creation of the world.
In ancient Mesopotamian stories, Tiamat is depicted as a symbol of chaos and the sea.
Scholars debate whether Tiamat was worshipped as a deity or mainly featured in mythological tales.

Etymology

From Akkadian Tiamat, a primeval deity in Babylonian mythology.
