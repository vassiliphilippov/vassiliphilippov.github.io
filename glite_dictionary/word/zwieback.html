zwieback

1. NOUN

/ ˈzwiˌbæk, ˈswaɪˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈzwiːbæk, ˈswaɪbæk / (BrE)
A slice of sweet, raised bread that is baked a second time until it is brown, hard, and crisp, often eaten as a snack or for infants.
crisp, twice-baked bread slice

Examples

My grandmother always served zwieback with tea in the afternoon.
The baby likes to chew on a piece of zwieback as a snack.
You can crumble zwieback over fruit to add a crunchy topping.

Etymology

From German Zwieback, meaning 'twice baked'.
