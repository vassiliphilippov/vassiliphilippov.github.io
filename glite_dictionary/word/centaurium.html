Centaurium

1. NOUN

/ sɛnˈtɔriəm / (AmE), / sɛnˈtɔːrɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of low-growing herbaceous plants mainly found in the Northern Hemisphere, known for their flowers with protruding, spirally twisted anthers.
genus of low-growing flowering herbs

Examples

The medicinal properties of Centaurium have been documented in various herbal texts.
Several species within the Centaurium genus are valued for their attractive pink flowers.
Botanists often study Centaurium to understand the diversity of anther structures in flowering plants.

Etymology

From Latin 'Centaurium', traditionally linked to the mythical centaur Chiron who was said to have used the herb medicinally.
