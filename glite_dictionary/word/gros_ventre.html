Gros Ventre

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡroʊ ˈvɑːntrə / (AmE), / ˌɡrəʊ ˈvɒntrə / (BrE)
A Siouan language historically spoken by the Hidatsa people of North America.
Siouan language of the Hidatsa people

Examples

The Gros Ventre language is now considered endangered with few fluent speakers remaining.
Linguists have studied Gros Ventre to better understand the structure of Siouan languages.
Efforts are underway to revitalize Gros Ventre through educational programs and community initiatives.

Etymology

From French 'gros ventre' meaning 'big belly', a name given by French explorers.

2. NOUN

/ ɡroʊ ˈvɑːntrə / (AmE), / ɡrəʊ ˈvɒntrə / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people, originally part of the Sioux, who formerly lived along the Missouri River in western North Dakota.
member of a Native American people (North Dakota)

Examples

The Gros Ventre were known for their rich traditions and complex social structure.
A small community of Gros Ventre people still lives in the region today.
Anthropologists have studied the language and customs of the Gros Ventre.

Etymology

From French gros ventre (big belly), a name given by French traders.
