Alcaeus

1. PROPN

/ ælˈsiːəs / (AmE), / ælˈsiːəs / (BrE)
(c. 611–580 BC) An ancient Greek lyric poet from Lesbos, reputed inventor of the Alcaic stanza.
ancient Greek lyric poet of Lesbos
archaic

Examples

Alcaeus is celebrated for his passionate poetry addressing themes of politics and love.
The lyrical innovations of Alcaeus influenced later generations of poets.
Scholars study the surviving fragments of Alcaeus to better understand ancient Greek verse forms.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἀλκαῖος (Alkaîos), a personal name.
