saturated

1. ADJ

/ ˈsætʃəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈsætʃəreɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a solution) containing as much of a dissolved substance as possible at a given temperature, so that no more can be dissolved.
containing the most dissolved substance possible (chemistry)

Examples

At this temperature, the saturated solution cannot dissolve any more sugar.
When salt is added beyond the point of being saturated, the excess will remain at the bottom.
A saturated solution was achieved after stirring continuously for several minutes.

Synset Examples

a saturated solution

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unsaturated - (of a solution) able to dissolve more of a substance at a given temperature

Etymology

From Latin saturatus (filled, glutted), past participle of saturare (to fill).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsætʃəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈsatʃəreɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of an organic compound) having no double or triple bonds between carbon atoms; all available valence bonds are filled, typically with hydrogen.
having all valence bonds filled (chemistry)

Examples

Butter and lard are high in saturated fats.
Many nutritionists advise limiting your intake of saturated fat.
A saturated hydrocarbon contains only single bonds between its carbon atoms.

Synset Examples

saturated fats

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unsaturated - having double or triple bonds between carbon atoms (chemistry)

Etymology

From Latin saturatus (filled, soaked), past participle of saturare (to fill, to saturate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00760781-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈsætʃəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈsætʃəreɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a color) having full intensity and purity, not diluted with white, grey, or black.
chromatically pure color

Examples

The artist chose a saturated blue for the sky to make it stand out.
Some screens display colors that appear overly saturated compared to natural light.
Vivid, saturated colors can evoke a sense of energy and excitement in a painting.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: desaturated - (of a color) reduced in intensity or purity, closer to grey

Etymology

From Latin saturatus (filled, sated), related to 'saturate.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00394569-a
Synset POS: a

4. ADJ

/ ˈsætʃəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈsatʃ(ə)reɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a solution) containing the maximum amount of a substance that can dissolve at a given temperature; any more will remain undissolved.
concentrated to maximum amount dissolved

Examples

The chemist explained that the saturated solution could not dissolve any more salt.
Once the mixture became saturated, excess sugar collected at the bottom of the beaker.
At this temperature, water is saturated with oxygen and cannot absorb additional gas.

a saturated solution
