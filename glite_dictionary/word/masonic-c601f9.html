masonic

1. ADJ

/ məˈsɑnɪk / (AmE), / məˈsɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to stonemasons or masonry, especially their tools, craft, or practices.
relating to stonemasons or masonry

Examples

The museum displayed several masonic tools used in the construction of cathedrals.
He gave a lecture on the evolution of masonic techniques in the Middle Ages.
Some masonic symbols can still be seen carved into the old city walls.

Synset Examples

masonic tools

Related Terms

  • Synonym: masonry - the work of building with stone

Etymology

From Latin 'machina' (machine, device), via Old French 'maçon' (stonemason), plus '-ic' forming adjectives.
