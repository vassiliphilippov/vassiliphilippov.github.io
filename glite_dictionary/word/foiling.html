foiling

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɔɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of preventing someone's plans or efforts from succeeding
an act of hindering someone's plans

Examples

The police's quick foiling of the robbery saved the bank from a major loss.
Thanks to the detective's foiling of the scheme, the criminal plot never came to fruition.
Her foiling of the surprise party was unintentional, but everyone found out too soon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstruction - the act of blocking progress or hindering
  • Synonym: sabotage - the act of deliberately destroying or hindering something
  • Antonym: assistance - the act of helping or supporting someone

Etymology

From the verb 'foil', which comes from Old French 'foiler' (trample down, spoil), from Latin 'folia' (leaves, trampled underfoot).
