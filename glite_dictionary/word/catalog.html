catalog

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæt.əl.ɔg / (AmE), / ˈkæt.əl.ɒg / (BrE)
A complete list of items, especially one that is systematically arranged and often includes details about each item.
systematic list of items

Examples

The museum published a catalog of its entire collection for visitors.
Please refer to the product catalog before placing your order.
His achievements form an impressive catalog of contributions to the field.

Synset Examples

it does not pretend to be a catalog of his achievements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inventory - a detailed list of items, often for business purposes
  • Synonym: list - a number of connected items or names written or printed together

Etymology

From Greek katalogos (a list, register), from kata- (down) + legein (to count, say).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06499734-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæt̬.əl.ɔɡ / (AmE), / ˈkæt.əl.ɒɡ / (BrE)
a book or pamphlet listing a collection of items, such as goods for sale, works in a museum, or library holdings
a book listing items or products

Examples

The museum published a catalog of its new exhibition.
She flipped through the catalog to order her favorite clothes.
Our library's catalog makes it easy to search for any book.

Synset Examples

he found it in the Sears catalog

Related Terms

  • Synonym: directory - a book listing individuals or organizations alphabetically
  • Synonym: inventory - a complete list of items such as goods in stock
  • Synonym: catalogue - British spelling of 'catalog'

Etymology

From Greek 'katalogos' (a list or register) via Late Latin and Old French.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06427849-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈkæt̬.ə.lɔɡ / (AmE), / ˈkæt.əl.ɒɡ / (BrE)
to make a systematic or itemized list of items, often for reference or organization; to classify or group items methodically
make an itemized list; classify

Examples

The librarian will catalog the new arrivals before displaying them on the shelves.
She spent the afternoon cataloging her grandfather's book collection by genre and author.
We need to catalog all the artwork in the gallery for insurance purposes.

Synset Examples

He is cataloguing his photographic negatives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to classify - to arrange or organize according to categories or classes
  • Synonym: list - to make a series of items in a list

Etymology

From Greek 'katalogos' (a list, register) via Late Latin 'catalogus' and Old French 'catalogue'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00654293-v
Synset POS: v

Synset Examples

Related Terms
