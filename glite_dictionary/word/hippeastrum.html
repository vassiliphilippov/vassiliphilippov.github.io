hippeastrum

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪpiˈæstrəm / (AmE), / ˌhɪpiˈæstrəm / (BrE)
A tropical American plant of the amaryllis family, often grown as a houseplant for its large, showy white to red flowers.
flowering plant of the amaryllis family

Examples

The vibrant blooms of the hippeastrum made a stunning centerpiece on the dining table.
She specializes in cultivating different varieties of hippeastrum in her greenhouse.
After winter dormancy, the hippeastrum produced several trumpet-shaped flowers.

Etymology

From modern Latin, from Greek 'hippos' (horse) + 'astron' (star), alluding to the star-shaped flowers.
