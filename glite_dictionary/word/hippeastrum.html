Hippeastrum

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪpiˈæstrəm / (AmE), / ˌhɪpiˈæstrəm / (BrE)
A genus of bulbous flowering plants native to tropical America, known for their large, showy flowers.
genus of bulbous tropical American flowers

Examples

The Hippeastrum produces striking trumpet-shaped blooms indoors during winter.
Gardeners often force Hippeastrum bulbs to flower for the holiday season.
Several species within the Hippeastrum genus are mistaken for amaryllis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amaryllis - bulbous plant often confused with Hippeastrum, especially as a common name

Etymology

From Greek 'hippos' (horse) and 'astron' (star), referring to the star-shaped flowers.
