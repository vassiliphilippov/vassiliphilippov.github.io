gore

1. NOUN

/ ɡɔɹ / (AmE), / ɡɔː / (BrE)
clotted blood, especially as a result of a wound or injury
coagulated blood from a wound

Examples

The scene was covered in gore after the battle.
The movie's special effects included buckets of fake gore.
She shrank away from the sight of gore on his shirt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blood - the red fluid that circulates in the arteries and veins

Etymology

From Old English 'gor' (dirt, dung, filth), later narrowed to refer to clotted blood.

3. NOUN

/ ɡɔr / (AmE), / ɡɔː / (BrE)
a piece of cloth, usually triangular or tapering, that is used in making garments, umbrellas, or sails to provide shape or fullness
triangular piece of cloth used in sewing

Examples

The skirt was made with several gores to give it extra flare.
Sailmakers often add a gore to improve the sail's curve and performance.
She sewed a triangular gore into the sleeve to allow for more movement.

Etymology

From Old English 'gāra' (triangular piece of land or material).

4. VERB

/ ɡɔr / (AmE), / ɡɔː / (BrE)
to wound or pierce with a sharp or penetrating object, especially the horn or tusk of an animal
to wound with a sharp object

Examples

The bull charged at the matador and gored him in the leg.
Several people were injured when the wild boar gored them during the festival.
An antelope's sharp horns can gore its predators in self-defense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pierce - to penetrate or make a hole through something using a sharp object
  • Synonym: stab - to thrust a pointed weapon into someone or something

Etymology

From Old English *gor* (filth, dung, blood), but the verb sense is influenced by Old English *gār* (spear).

/ ɡɔr / (AmE), / ɡɔː / (BrE)
blood that has been shed, especially as the result of violence; graphic scenes of bloodshed and violence.
blood shed through violence

Examples

Many horror movies rely heavily on gore to shock the audience.
The director toned down the gore to make the film suitable for teenagers.
Some viewers found the gore in the video game excessive and disturbing.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bloodshed - the act of killing or injuring people usually in large numbers during a conflict
  • Synonym: violence - behavior involving physical force intended to hurt, damage, or kill

Etymology

From Old English 'gār' (spear, instrument causing bloodshed), later developing to refer to blood shed by violence.
