yaws

1. NOUN

/ jɔz / (AmE), / jɔːz / (BrE)
An infectious tropical disease resembling syphilis in its early stages, characterized by red skin eruptions and ulcerating lesions, primarily affecting children in rural regions.
infectious tropical disease with skin eruptions
medical

Examples

Doctors identified the patient's symptoms as typical of yaws after a thorough examination.
The eradication of yaws has become a public health goal in several tropical countries.
Children living in remote villages are still at risk of contracting yaws.

Etymology

Probably from Carib or African languages, adopted through Spanish and French.
