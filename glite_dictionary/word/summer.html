summer

1. VERB

/ ˈsʌmər / (AmE), / ˈsʌmə / (BrE)
to spend the summer in a particular place or way
spend the summer (somewhere)

Examples

Every year, they summer at their cottage by the lake.
The artists summered in the south of France for inspiration.
Many wealthy families would summer in the mountains to escape the heat.

Synset Examples

We summered in Kashmir

Related Terms

  • Antonym: winter - to spend the winter in a particular place or way

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌmɚ / (AmE), / ˈsʌmə / (BrE)
A period of one's life or development characterized by peak success, happiness, or beauty.
peak period of happiness or success

Examples

She looked back on the summer of her career as a time of joy and fulfillment.
His creativity blossomed during the summer of his youth, when inspiration came easily.
Many regard their college years as the summer of their lives, filled with adventure and optimism.

Synset Examples

the golden summer of his life

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: spring - the period of early growth or youth
  • Degree Higher: autumn - the period of decline or maturity

Etymology

From Old English sumor, of Germanic origin, related to Dutch zomer and German Sommer.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsʌm.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈsʌm.ə / (BrE)
The warmest season of the year, occurring between spring and autumn, typically from the summer solstice to the autumnal equinox in the Northern Hemisphere.
warmest season of the year

Examples

We always go hiking in the summer when the days are long and warm.
Last summer, she traveled through Europe with her friends.
The children spent their summer vacation playing in the park every afternoon.

Synset Examples

they spent a lazy summer at the shore

Related Terms

  • Antonym: winter - the coldest season of the year
  • Antonym: spring - the season before summer, between winter and summer
  • Antonym: autumn - the season after summer, before winter

Etymology

Old English sumor, from Proto-Germanic *sumaraz.
