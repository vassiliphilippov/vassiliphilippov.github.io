Hordeum murinum

1. NOUN

/ hɔrˈdiːəm mjuˈraɪnəm / (AmE), / hɔːˈdiːəm mjʊˈraɪnəm / (BrE)
A species of European annual grass, often found as a weed in waste ground, especially along roadsides and hedgerows.
a species of European annual grass

Examples

Hordeum murinum thrives in urban environments, often growing along the edges of pavements and railway tracks.
Many gardeners regard Hordeum murinum as a troublesome weed due to its rapid spread and resilience.
The seeds of Hordeum murinum can attach to animal fur, aiding in the plant's dispersal.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name *Hordeum* (barley) and species Latin *murinum* (mouse-like), referring to the shape of the awns.
