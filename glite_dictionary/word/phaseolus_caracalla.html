Phaseolus caracalla

1. NOUN

/ fəˈsiːoʊləs kəˌrɑːˈkɑːlə / (AmE), / fəˈsiːələs kəˌrækəˈlæ / (BrE)
A perennial vine native to tropical America, cultivated for its ornamental racemes of showy yellow and purple, snail shell-shaped flowers.
ornamental tropical American vine

Examples

The Phaseolus caracalla is admired for its unique, spiral-shaped flowers that resemble snail shells.
Gardeners often plant Phaseolus caracalla to add color and fragrance to trellises and fences.
In tropical climates, Phaseolus caracalla can bloom throughout much of the year.

Etymology

From Latin 'phaseolus' (a kind of bean) and Spanish 'caracalla' (snail-shaped), referring to the flower's spiral form.
