Echinocactus grusonii

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkɪnoʊˈkæktəs ɡruˈsoʊni.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛkɪnəʊˈkæktəs ɡruːˈsəʊni.aɪ / (BrE)
A species of large, spherical cactus native to east central Mexico, noted for its golden to pale yellow flowers and yellow spines; also known as the golden barrel cactus.
a large spherical cactus, the golden barrel cactus

Examples

The Echinocactus grusonii adds a dramatic focal point to desert-themed gardens.
Many plant enthusiasts appreciate the unique appearance of the Echinocactus grusonii.
The Echinocactus grusonii thrives in sunny, arid environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden barrel cactus - Common name for Echinocactus grusonii

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name Echinocactus from Greek ekhinos (hedgehog, sea urchin) and kaktos (cactus); species named in honor of Hermann Gruson, German engineer and cactus collector.
