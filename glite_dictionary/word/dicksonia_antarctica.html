Dicksonia antarctica

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪkˈsoʊniə ænˌtɑrktɪkə / (AmE), / dɪkˈsəʊnɪə ænˈtɑːktɪkə / (BrE)
A species of large, evergreen tree fern native to Australia and Tasmania, widely cultivated in cool climates for its attractive fronds.
Australian tree fern species

Examples

The Dicksonia antarctica in their garden thrives in the temperate, shaded conditions.
Botanists consider Dicksonia antarctica one of the hardiest tree ferns for cultivation.
After several years, the Dicksonia antarctica developed a tall, textured trunk typical of mature specimens.

Etymology

From genus name Dicksonia, honoring botanist James Dickson, and species epithet 'antarctica' denoting southern distribution.
