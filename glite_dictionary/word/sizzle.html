sizzle

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪzəl / (BrE)
a hissing or crackling sound, especially as of something frying or burning
hissing or crackling sound

Examples

The sizzle of bacon filled the kitchen every morning.
You could hear the sizzle from the hot grill across the patio.
The sizzle died down as the pan cooled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hiss - a sharp, prolonged sibilant sound
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07408519-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈsɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪzəl / (BrE)
to make a hissing or crackling sound like that of fat frying in a pan
make a hissing or crackling sound

Examples

The bacon began to sizzle as soon as it hit the hot pan.
On a summer day, you can hear the pavement sizzle under the blazing sun.
The fireworks sizzled and popped in the night sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hiss - to make a long 's' sound, as of escaping steam or air
  • Synonym: crackle - to make a rapid succession of slight cracking noises

Etymology

Imitative origin, representing the sound produced.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00864255-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈsɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪz(ə)l / (BrE)
to seethe or burn with intense emotion, especially anger or resentment
be seething with emotion, especially anger

Examples

She tried to hide it, but inside she was sizzling with rage.
He sizzled at the unfair decision handed down to his team.
As her colleague spoke over her, she could feel herself sizzling with resentment.

Synset Examples

She was sizzling with anger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to seethe - to be filled with intense but unexpressed emotion
  • Synonym: fume - to feel, show, or express anger intensely
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01771670-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈsɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪzl̩ / (BrE)
to make a hissing or crackling sound when burning or frying; to burn or sear with such a sound
make a hissing sound when frying or burning

Examples

The bacon began to sizzle as soon as it hit the hot pan.
You could hear the onions sizzling in the oil from across the kitchen.
She sizzled the steak quickly to lock in the juices.

Synset Examples

The fat sizzled in the pan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fry - to cook food in hot fat or oil
  • Synonym: sear - to burn or scorch the surface of something with sudden, intense heat
  • Antonym: cool - to make or become less hot

Etymology

Imitative of the sound produced when frying or searing.
