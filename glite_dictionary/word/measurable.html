measurable

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɛʒ.ɚ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈmɛʒ.ər.ə.bəl / (BrE)
(of a quantity, amount, or property) that can be measured; possible to determine the amount, degree, or value of.
able to be measured

Examples

The researchers found a measurable difference between the two groups after the treatment.
There was no measurable improvement in air quality following the new regulations.
The lake's measurable depth increased significantly after the heavy rains.

Synset Examples

measurable depths

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immeasurable - too large, extensive, or extreme to be measured

Etymology

From Middle English *mesurabel*, from Old French *mesurable*, from Late Latin *mensurabilis* (that can be measured).
