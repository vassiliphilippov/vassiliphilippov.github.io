Elaeagnaceae

1. NOUN

/ iˌliːəɡˈneɪsiː / (AmE), / ɛliˌæɡˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of shrubs or small trees, often with thorns, known as the oleaster family.
a family of thorny shrubs or small trees

Examples

Several species within the Elaeagnaceae family are valued for their ornamental qualities.
The Elaeagnaceae include hardy plants that thrive in poor soils.
Botanists have discovered new members of the Elaeagnaceae in central Asia.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Elaeagnus.
