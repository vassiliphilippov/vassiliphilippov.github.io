Haematoxylon

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːməˌtɑːkˈsaɪlɒn / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˌtɒkˈsaɪlɒn / (BrE)
A small genus of spiny, bushy shrubs or trees native to tropical America.
genus of tropical American shrubs or trees

Examples

The dye extracted from Haematoxylon is widely used in histological staining.
Researchers discovered a new medicinal compound in the bark of Haematoxylon trees.
The botanical garden features a rare Haematoxylon specimen from South America.

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) and xylon (wood), referring to the reddish wood of some species in the genus.
