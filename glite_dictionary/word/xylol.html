xylol

1. NOUN

/ ˈzaɪ.lɑl / (AmE), / ˈzaɪ.lɒl / (BrE)
a colorless, flammable, volatile liquid hydrocarbon used mainly as a solvent in industry and laboratories; another name for xylene.
industrial solvent; another name for xylene

Examples

The laboratory was well ventilated to prevent the accumulation of xylol fumes.
Painters commonly use xylol as a solvent for thinning paints and varnishes.
You should wear gloves and a mask when handling xylol due to its toxic vapors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: xylene - another name for xylol, a colorless flammable liquid hydrocarbon used as a solvent

Etymology

From Greek 'xylon' (wood), as it was first obtained from wood tar.
