to lapidate

1. VERB

/ ˈlæpɪˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈlapɪdeɪt / (BrE)
to throw stones at (someone or something), especially as a form of punishment or ritual
throw stones at as punishment or ritual
archaic

Examples

The ancient law permitted crowds to lapidate those accused of severe crimes.
During the festival, participants lapidate large pillars to symbolize casting out evil.
Authorities warned pilgrims not to lapidate animals as part of the ceremony.

Synset Examples

Pilgrims lapidate a stone pillar in commemoration of Abraham's temptation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stone - to throw stones at (someone), usually to injure or kill them

Etymology

From Latin lapidare (to stone), from lapis (stone).

2. VERB

/ ˈlæpɪˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈlæpɪdeɪt / (BrE)
to kill someone by throwing stones at them, usually as a form of execution
kill by throwing stones

Examples

The ancient law permitted a mob to lapidate those convicted of adultery.
In some historical accounts, traitors were lapidated as a public spectacle.
The community refused to lapidate the accused, despite pressure from outside authorities.

Synset Examples

People wanted to stone the woman who had a child out of wedlock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stone - to kill someone by throwing stones at them

Etymology

From Latin lapidare (to stone), from lapis (stone).

3. VERB

/ ˈlæpɪˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈlapɪdeɪt / (BrE)
To attack or criticize harshly, especially in writing or speech; to stone (figuratively or literally).
harshly criticize; stone

Examples

The critics began to lapidate the poorly received film.
She feared her novel would be lapidated by harsh reviewers.
When his decision was announced, the public wasted no time in lapidating him in the press.

Synset Examples

The critics began to lapidate the poorly received film.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stone - to throw stones at someone as a form of punishment or killing
  • Synonym: to criticize - to express disapproval of someone or something

Etymology

From Latin 'lapidare', from 'lapis' meaning 'stone'.
