axillary

1. ADJ

/ ˈæksɪˌleri / (AmE), / ækˈsɪləri / (BrE)
Relating to or situated in the armpit (axilla).
relating to the armpit
medical

Examples

The doctor checked the axillary lymph nodes for signs of swelling.
She applied deodorant to her axillary region after showering.
The patient exhibited an unusual axillary rash that required further examination.

Synset Examples

axillary gland

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axilla - the armpit; the cavity beneath the junction of the arm and the body

Etymology

From Latin 'axillaris', from 'axilla' (armpit).

2. ADJ

/ ˈæksəˌlɛri / (AmE), / ˈæksɪləri / (BrE)
Relating to or situated in the axil, the angle between the upper side of a leaf or branch and the stem from which it grows.
relating to a plant's axil

Examples

The flower clusters are axillary and grow at the junction of leaves and stems.
Some plants produce axillary buds that develop into new branches.
The axillary position of the fruit helps with pollination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axil - the angle between the upper side of a leaf or branch and the stem from which it grows

Etymology

From Latin *axillaris*, from *axilla* (armpit, but also used for 'axil' in botanical sense).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03145334-a
Synset POS: a
