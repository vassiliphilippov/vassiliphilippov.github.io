fossa

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːsə, ˈfoʊsə / (AmE), / ˈfɒsə / (BrE)
a carnivorous mammal (Cryptoprocta ferox) native to Madagascar, closely related to the civets and resembling a small cougar; the only species in its genus.
carnivorous mammal of Madagascar

Examples

The fossa is the largest mammalian carnivore on Madagascar and is an agile forest predator.
Few visitors to Madagascar manage to spot a fossa in the wild due to its elusive nature.
Researchers tracked the movements of the fossa to learn more about its hunting behavior.

Etymology

From Latin fossa (ditch, trench), used in modern taxonomy as a species name.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊ sə / (AmE), / ˈfɒs ə / (BrE)
A large carnivorous mammal (Cryptoprocta ferox) of Madagascar, resembling a small cougar and related to the civets, with a slender body, retractile claws, and a long tail.
carnivorous mammal of Madagascar

Examples

The fossa is the largest predator native to Madagascar.
A fossa can climb trees with great ease due to its flexible ankles.
Zookeepers ensure the fossa has a habitat similar to its natural forest home.

Etymology

From Malagasy fosa, via French fossa.

/ ˈfɑsə / (AmE), / ˈfɒsə / (BrE)

anatomical depression or hollow
medical

Examples

The temporal fossa is an important area of the skull above the cheekbone.
During surgery, the doctor carefully examined the glenoid fossa of the shoulder joint.
Abnormalities may be detected in the cranial fossa on a brain scan.

Etymology

From Latin 'fossa' meaning 'ditch' or 'trench'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːsə / (AmE), / ˈfɒsə / (BrE)
a large carnivorous mammal (Cryptoprocta ferox) native to Madagascar, resembling a small cougar and related to both cats and civets
large carnivorous mammal of Madagascar

Examples

The fossa is the top predator in Madagascar's forests, preying mainly on lemurs.
Few people outside Madagascar have ever seen a wild fossa.
With its slender body and long tail, the fossa is well adapted to chasing prey through trees.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: civet - small, mostly nocturnal mammals with a cat-like appearance
  • Degree Higher: cougar - large, powerful, cat-like carnivorous mammal

Etymology

From Latin 'fossa' (ditch, trench), adopted in scientific taxonomy as the genus name.
