mephenytoin

1. NOUN

/ mɛˈfɛniˌtoɪn / (AmE), / mɛˈfɛnɪˌtɔɪn / (BrE)
A toxic anticonvulsant drug, used in the treatment of epilepsy when less toxic anticonvulsants have been ineffective.
anticonvulsant drug for epilepsy
medical

Examples

The neurologist prescribed mephenytoin after other treatments failed to control the seizures.
Serious side effects may occur when taking mephenytoin, so careful monitoring is required.
Mephenytoin is typically considered only when standard anticonvulsants are not effective.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anticonvulsant - a drug used to prevent or reduce the severity of epileptic fits or other convulsions
