adrenalin

1. NOUN

/ əˈdrɛnəlɪn / (AmE), / əˈdrɛnəlɪn / (BrE)
A hormone and medication produced by the adrenal glands that increases heart rate, blood pressure, and energy supply in response to stress; also known as epinephrine.
hormone produced during stress

Examples

The thrill of skydiving pumps massive amounts of adrenalin into the body.
Doctors sometimes inject adrenalin to treat severe allergic reactions.
Her body was flooded with adrenalin as she prepared for the race.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epinephrine - another name for adrenalin

Etymology

From Latin ad- (toward) + renes (kidneys), referring to the adrenal glands.
