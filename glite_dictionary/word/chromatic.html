chromatic

1. ADJ

/ kroʊˈmætɪk / (AmE), / krəʊˈmætɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by color or hue.
relating to color or hue

Examples

The painting was admired for its chromatic richness and vivid hues.
Many artists study the chromatic spectrum to understand how colors interact.
The designer used a chromatic approach to create a vibrant, engaging space.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: achromatic - without color; lacking hue

Etymology

From Greek *khrōmatikos* (of color), from *khrōma* (color).

2. ADJ

/ kroʊˈmætɪk / (AmE), / krəʊˈmatɪk / (BrE)
relating to or using a musical scale consisting of twelve semitones
relating to a 12-note musical scale

Examples

The piece begins with a chromatic scale that ascends to a high C.
His composition features several chromatic passages that create a sense of tension.
Learning to play chromatic runs is essential for advanced pianists.

Synset Examples

a chromatic scale

Etymology

From Greek *khrōmatikos* (pertaining to color), here extended to musical tones.

3. ADJ

/ kroʊˈmætɪk / (AmE), / krəˈmatɪk / (BrE)
(of a lens or optical device) able to refract light without causing separation of colors into a spectrum.
able to refract light without color separation

Examples

A chromatic lens improves image quality by reducing color fringing in photographs.
The telescope uses a chromatic objective to avoid color distortion.
Advances in optical engineering have made chromatic corrections more efficient.

Synset Examples

chromatic lens

Etymology

From Greek *khrōmatikos* (relating to color), from *khrōma* (color).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02968169-a
Synset POS: a
