bristle

1. VERB

/ ˈbrɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈbrɪsəl / (BrE)
(of an object or surface) to have or be thickly covered with bristles or something resembling bristles.
be thickly covered with bristles

Examples

The cactus bristled with long, sharp spines that deterred animals from eating it.
Her anger bristled on the surface, visible in every tense muscle.
The old paintbrush bristles with dried streaks of color from years of use.

Synset Examples

bristling leaves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throng - to be filled or crowded with something

Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈbrɪsəl / (BrE)
a short, stiff hair, typically one of those on an animal's skin, a plant, or a brush
a stiff hair or hair-like structure

Examples

The pig's bristles were rough to the touch.
A toothbrush has nylon bristles to clean your teeth.
Some caterpillars are covered in tiny bristles that can irritate the skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hair - a fine threadlike strand from animals or humans

Etymology

Old English *brysel*, related to 'brush'.

5. VERB

/ ˈbrɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈbrɪsəl / (BrE)
(of hair or fur) to stand upright away from the skin, especially as a reaction to fear, anger, or excitement.
(of hair/fur) stand upright as in fear or anger

Examples

The cat's fur began to bristle as soon as it saw the dog.
His hair seemed to bristle with fright when the thunder roared overhead.
She could feel the goosebumps form and the tiny hairs on her arms bristle in the cold.

Synset Examples

The dog's fur bristled
It was a sight to make one's hair uprise!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stand on end - (of hair) to become erect, especially in fear or excitement

Etymology

From Old English *brȳstel* (bristle), related to brush.

6. VERB

/ ˈbrɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈbrɪsəl / (BrE)
(of a person) to react suddenly with anger or offense, often showing visible tension.
react angrily or with offense

Examples

He bristled at the implication that he was inexperienced.
She bristled when criticized in front of her colleagues.
At the slightest provocation, he would bristle and defend his actions.

Synset Examples

He bristled at her suggestion that he should teach her how to use the program

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flare up - to suddenly become angry
  • Antonym: to calm down - become less angry or agitated

Etymology

Originally from Old English 'brysel' (bristle), figurative use based on hairs standing up when angry.
