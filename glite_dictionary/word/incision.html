incision

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪʒən / (BrE)
a surgical cut made in body tissue or an organ, especially during an operation
surgical cut in body tissue
medical

Examples

The surgeon made a precise incision in the patient's abdomen.
After the incision healed, she was able to walk comfortably again.
Proper care of the incision site helps prevent infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut - an opening or wound made by a sharp instrument

Etymology

From Latin 'incisio' (a cutting into), from 'incidere' (to cut into).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪʒən / (BrE)
a narrow cut or depression scratched or carved into a surface, especially in stone or metal as decoration or marking
a cut or depression on a surface

Examples

Ancient pottery often features decorative incisions made with simple tools.
The archaeologist examined the faint incisions on the cave wall for clues about early humans.
Artists sometimes use a stylus to create fine incision marks on metal plates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engraving - a design or inscription cut into a hard surface

Etymology

From Latin 'incisio', from 'incidere' (to cut into).
