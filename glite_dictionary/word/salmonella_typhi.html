Salmonella typhi

1. NOUN

/ sælməˈnɛlə ˈtaɪfaɪ / (AmE), / sælməˈnɛlə ˈtɪfiː / (BrE)
a bacterium of the Salmonella genus that causes typhoid fever in humans
bacterium causing typhoid fever
medical

Examples

Salmonella typhi is transmitted through contaminated food and water.
Vaccination can help protect travelers from infection by Salmonella typhi.
Doctors confirmed the presence of Salmonella typhi in the patient's blood sample.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: typhoid fever - a serious infectious bacterial disease causing fever and abdominal pain

Etymology

From the genus name Salmonella, named after American scientist Daniel E. Salmon, and Neo-Latin 'typhi' from 'typhus', relating to fever.
