broiler

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɔɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈbrɔɪlə / (BrE)
An oven or a compartment in a stove used for cooking food with direct radiant heat from above.
oven or stove part for broiling

Examples

She placed the salmon under the broiler for a quick, crispy finish.
The recipe says to use the broiler to melt the cheese evenly.
After preheating the broiler, he cooked the steak for five minutes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grill - a device or part of a stove for cooking by direct heat, usually from below

Etymology

From 'broil' (to cook by direct heat) + '-er' (agent noun suffix), early 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrɔɪlər / (AmE), / ˈbrɔɪlə / (BrE)
the flesh of a small young chicken, typically not over 2 1/2 pounds, suitable for broiling
young chicken for broiling

Examples

The chef recommended grilling the broiler for a tender and juicy result.
At the market, she asked for a fresh broiler to prepare for dinner.
The recipe calls specifically for the meat of a young broiler rather than an older chicken.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: roaster - chicken suitable for roasting; larger than a broiler

Etymology

From 'broil' (to cook by direct heat) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
