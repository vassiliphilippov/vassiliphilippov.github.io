Hypochaeris

1. PROPN

/ haɪpoʊˈkɪərɪs / (AmE), / hɪpəʊˈkɪərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, commonly known as cat's ear, consisting of herbs with milky sap, some of which are widespread weeds.
genus of flowering herbs (cat's ear)

Examples

Hypochaeris species are often found in grasslands and lawns throughout temperate regions.
The flower heads of Hypochaeris resemble those of dandelions but belong to a different genus.
Gardeners sometimes have difficulty removing Hypochaeris because of its deep taproot.

Etymology

From Greek 'hypo-' (under) and 'chaeris' (joy, grace), given as a botanical genus name.

2. NOUN

/ haɪpoʊˈkɪərɪs / (AmE), / hɪˌpɒkəˈɪərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, commonly known as cat's ear, with milky sap and widespread weeds.
a plant genus (cat's ear) in the daisy family

Examples

Some species of Hypochaeris are considered troublesome weeds in agricultural fields.
The leaves of Hypochaeris have a rosette shape similar to those of the dandelion.
Botanists classify several widespread wildflowers under the genus Hypochaeris.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cat's ear - common name for plants in the genus Hypochaeris
  • Synonym: Taraxacum - a related genus including dandelions

Etymology

From Greek 'hypo' (under) and 'chairein' (to rejoice), referring to the plant's low growth.
