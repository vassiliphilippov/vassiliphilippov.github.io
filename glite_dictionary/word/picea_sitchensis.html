Picea sitchensis

1. NOUN

/ paɪˌsiːə sɪˈtʃɛnsɪs / (AmE), / pɪˌsiːə sɪˈtʃɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a large evergreen tree native to the northwestern coast of North America, known as the Sitka spruce, with sharp, stiff needles and valuable lightweight wood
a species of spruce tree (Sitka spruce)

Examples

The wood of the Picea sitchensis is prized for building musical instruments such as guitars and pianos.
Many forests in Alaska and British Columbia are dominated by Picea sitchensis trees.
The needles of Picea sitchensis are very sharp and stiff, distinguishing it from other spruces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sitka spruce - common name for Picea sitchensis

Etymology

From Latin: 'Picea' (spruce) and 'sitchensis', meaning 'of Sitka' (referring to Sitka, Alaska).
