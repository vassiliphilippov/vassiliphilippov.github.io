tourmaline

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʊrməˌliːn / (AmE), / ˈtʊə.mə.liːn / (BrE)
a usually black or variously colored mineral composed of complex borosilicates, used as a gemstone
a mineral used as a gemstone

Examples

The necklace was set with a rare pink tourmaline that sparkled in the light.
Geologists discovered a rich vein of tourmaline crystals deep within the mountain.
Collectors value green tourmaline for its vibrant color and clarity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gemstone - a precious or semi-precious stone used in jewelry

Etymology

From French 'tourmaline', from Sinhalese 'tōramalli', meaning 'mixed gems'.
