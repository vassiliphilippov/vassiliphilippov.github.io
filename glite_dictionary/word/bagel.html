bagel

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪɡəl / (AmE), / ˈbeɪɡl̩ / (BrE)
a dense, ring-shaped bread roll made from yeast dough, typically boiled and then baked, with a chewy interior and a crisp, shiny crust
ring-shaped bread roll

Examples

I bought a freshly baked bagel with cream cheese for breakfast.
The bakery offers bagels in a variety of flavors, including sesame and poppy seed.
She sliced the bagel in half before toasting it.

Etymology

From Yiddish *beygl*, from Middle High German *böugel* (ring, bracelet).
