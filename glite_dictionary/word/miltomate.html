miltomate

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪl.t̬oʊˈmɑː.teɪ / (AmE), / ˌmɪl.təʊˈmɑː.teɪ / (BrE)
A Mexican annual plant, naturalized in parts of North America, bearing yellow to purple edible fruit similar to small tomatoes.
Mexican plant with tomato-like fruit

Examples

The farmers planted rows of miltomate alongside their corn fields for a bountiful harvest.
Many traditional Mexican recipes call for the unique flavor of miltomate in sauces and salsas.
You can find fresh miltomate at local markets from summer to early autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tomatillo - A related plant with small, green or purple edible fruit in a husk

Etymology

From Spanish 'miltomate', a blend of 'mil' (thousand) and 'tomate' (tomato/fruit), originally from Nahuatl 'miltomatl'.
