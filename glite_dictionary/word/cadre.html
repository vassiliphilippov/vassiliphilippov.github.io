cadre

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.drə / (BrE)
a small group of people serving as the core or nucleus of a larger political movement, organization, or military unit
core group in a larger movement or organization

Examples

A dedicated cadre of activists formed the backbone of the political campaign.
The movement relied on a small cadre to spread its message and organize events.
She was recruited into a cadre that coordinated the party’s efforts at the local level.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cell - a small group forming a nucleus within a political movement

Etymology

From French 'cadre' (framework, frame), from Italian 'quadro' (square).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːˌdreɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdri / (BrE)
A nucleus of trained personnel, especially in the military, around which an expanded organization can be built and trained.
core group of trained personnel (military)

Examples

The new unit was formed with a cadre of experienced soldiers as its foundation.
During mobilization, a small cadre trains and leads the influx of recruits.
Each regiment maintained a cadre to ensure readiness for rapid expansion during wartime.

Etymology

From French 'cadre' (framework, framework of a regiment), from Italian 'quadro' (frame).
