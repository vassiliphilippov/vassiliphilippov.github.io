abused

1. ADJ

/ əˈbjuːzd / (AmE), / əˈbjuːzd / (BrE)
subjected to cruel, violent, or wrongful treatment, especially repeatedly or habitually
subjected to cruel treatment

Examples

The shelter provides support for abused children and their families.
She finally left her abused past behind to start a new life.
Counselors often work with abused individuals to help them heal emotionally.

Synset Examples

an abused wife

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistreated - treated badly or cruelly
  • Antonym: protected - kept safe from harm or injury

Etymology

From the verb 'abuse', ultimately from Latin 'abusus', past participle of 'abuti' (to misuse).

2. ADJ

/ əˈbjuzd / (AmE), / əˈbjuːzd / (BrE)
Used in an improper or excessive way, especially referring to substances such as drugs or alcohol.
used excessively or improperly, esp. substances

Examples

The policy targets abused substances like heroin and prescription opioids.
Children are often put at risk in homes where alcohol is abused.
He was arrested for possessing abused prescription drugs.

Synset Examples

an abused substance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misused - Used in a wrong or harmful way
  • Degree Higher: addictive - Tending to cause addiction
