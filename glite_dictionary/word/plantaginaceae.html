Plantaginaceae

1. NOUN

/ plænˌtædʒɪˈneɪsiː / (AmE), / plænˌtadʒɪˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, mainly small herbs and a few shrubs, most commonly known as plantains, many of which are widespread weeds
a family of flowering plants (plantains)

Examples

The Plantaginaceae family includes several species commonly found in lawns and fields.
Botanists have recently reclassified certain genera into the Plantaginaceae based on genetic studies.
Many weeds in the Plantaginaceae are hardy and spread rapidly in disturbed soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Plantago - the type genus of the Plantaginaceae family, including plantains

Etymology

From New Latin Plantaginaceae, from Plantago (the type genus) + -aceae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ plænˌtædʒəˈneɪsiˌi / (AmE), / plænˌtadʒɪˈneɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of chiefly herbaceous plants and some shrubs, commonly known as the plantain family, many of which are widespread weeds
the plantain family of plants (Plantaginaceae)

Examples

Many botanists study the Plantaginaceae because several species are difficult garden weeds.
The Plantaginaceae family includes herbs, shrubs, and some ornamental plants found worldwide.
Recent research has reclassified certain genera into the Plantaginaceae due to genetic studies.

Etymology

From the genus name Plantago, from Latin 'plantago' (plantain).
