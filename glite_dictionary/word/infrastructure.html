infrastructure

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.frəˌstrʌk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈɪn.frəˌstrʌk.tʃə / (BrE)
The basic physical and organizational structures and facilities (e.g. buildings, roads, power supplies) needed for the operation of a society or enterprise.
basic facilities and systems of a society

Examples

The government is investing in new infrastructure to improve transportation and communication.
Much of the country's infrastructure was damaged during the hurricane.
Modern businesses depend on a reliable infrastructure to operate efficiently.

Synset Examples

the industrial base of Japan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: framework - a supporting structure around which something can be built
  • Antonym: superstructure - the part of a building or structure above its foundation

Etymology

From French infrastructure, from Latin infra (below) + structure.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.frəˈstrʌk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˌɪn.frəˈstrʌk.tʃə / (BrE)
The basic physical and organizational structures and facilities needed for the operation of a society, enterprise, or system.
basic structures and facilities of a society or system

Examples

After the earthquake, much of the country's infrastructure was damaged, making recovery efforts difficult.
Investing in public infrastructure like roads and bridges is essential for economic growth.
The company upgraded its IT infrastructure to improve data security and efficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: framework - an essential supporting structure of a building, vehicle, or object
  • Antonym: superstructure - the part of a building or system above its foundation or base

Etymology

From French 'infrastructure,' from Latin 'infra-' (below) + 'structure' (to build).
