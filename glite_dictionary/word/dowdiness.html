dowdiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊdi nəs / (AmE), / ˈdaʊdi nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being unfashionable, dull, or lacking in style or elegance
lack of style or elegance

Examples

Her dowdiness made her stand out in a room full of fashionably dressed people.
Despite her dowdiness, she was respected for her intelligence and kindness.
The film's costumes captured the postwar era's dowdiness perfectly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stylishness - the quality of being fashionable and elegant
  • Antonym: elegance - the quality of being stylish and refined

Etymology

From 'dowdy' + '-ness', where 'dowdy' possibly from Middle English doude (slovenly woman).
