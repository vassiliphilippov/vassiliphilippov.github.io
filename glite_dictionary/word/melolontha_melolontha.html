Melolontha melolontha

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛl.əˈlɒn.θə ˌmɛl.əˈlɒn.θə / (AmE), / ˌmɛl.əˈlɒn.θə ˌmɛl.əˈlɒn.θə / (BrE)
A large European beetle, commonly known as the cockchafer, noted for causing damage to vegetation in both its larval and adult stages.
European beetle damaging to plants

Examples

Gardeners often struggle to control Melolontha melolontha populations in the spring.
The larvae of Melolontha melolontha can cause serious damage to grass roots.
Several plants were destroyed by an infestation of Melolontha melolontha last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockchafer - Large European beetle damaging to plants

Etymology

From Greek 'melolonthē' (a kind of beetle); used in scientific Latin binomial nomenclature.
