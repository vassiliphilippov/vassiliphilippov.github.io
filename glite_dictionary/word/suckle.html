to suckle

1. VERB

/ ˈsʌk.əl / (AmE), / ˈsʌk.əl / (BrE)
to feed by sucking milk from the breast or udder; to allow a baby or young animal to drink milk in this way
feed by sucking milk

Examples

The newborn calf began to suckle minutes after birth.
She sat quietly as her baby suckled at her breast.
The kittens continued to suckle until they were several weeks old.

Synset Examples

the infant was suckling happily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nurse - to feed a baby at the breast
  • Antonym: to wean - to accustom a baby to food other than milk

Etymology

From Old English *sūclian*, related to *sūcan* (to suck), influenced by Latin *succellus*, diminutive of *sucellus* (little cup).

2. VERB

/ ˈsʌk.əl / (AmE), / ˈsʌk.əl / (BrE)
to feed (a baby or young animal) with milk from the breast or udder
to feed a baby or animal with milk

Examples

The ewe gently suckled her newborn lambs in the barn.
She chose to suckle her twins rather than use formula.
In some traditions, wet nurses would suckle the babies of noble families.

Synset Examples

The wetnurse suckled the infant
You cannot nurse your baby in public in some places

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nurse - to feed a baby at the breast (synonym)
  • Antonym: to wean - to stop feeding a baby or animal with milk (antonym)

Etymology

From Middle English 'suklen', frequentative of 'suken' (to suck), from Old English 'sūcan'.
