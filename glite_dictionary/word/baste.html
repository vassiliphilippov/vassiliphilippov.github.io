baste

1. NOUN

/ beɪst / (AmE), / beɪst / (BrE)
A loose, temporary sewing stitch used to hold layers of fabric together before the final stitching.
temporary sewing stitch

Examples

She used a long baste to keep the fabric in place before finishing the seam.
The baste made it easier to adjust the position of the two layers.
After sewing the final seam, he removed the baste carefully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tack - a light or temporary stitch in sewing

2. VERB

/ beɪst / (AmE), / beɪst / (BrE)
to strike someone violently and repeatedly, especially with great force
strike violently and repeatedly
informal

Examples

If you keep insulting him, he might baste you right in the face.
She threatened to baste her noisy neighbor if he didn't quiet down.
During the heated argument, one man tried to baste the other with a rolled-up newspaper.

Synset Examples

She clobbered the man who tried to attack her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clobber - to hit someone hard and repeatedly
  • Synonym: beat - to strike repeatedly; to hit over and over

3. VERB

/ beɪst / (AmE), / beɪst / (BrE)
to sew fabric together temporarily with large, loose stitches, usually to hold it in place before permanent sewing
sew with large temporary stitches

Examples

Before sewing the final seam, she decided to baste the pieces of fabric together.
He quickly basted the dress hem to check the fit before making it permanent.
I'm basting the sleeves onto the bodice so they won't shift as I sew.

Synset Examples

baste a hem

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tack - to fasten temporarily with loose stitches

Etymology

From Old French *bastir* (to build, sew), related to the act of joining or securing fabric.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01334030-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ beɪst / (AmE), / beɪst / (BrE)
To pour liquid, such as melted fat or juices, over food while it is cooking to keep it moist and add flavor.
moisten food with liquid while cooking

Examples

The chef remembered to baste the turkey every thirty minutes for a juicy result.
If you don’t baste the meat while it’s roasting, it could dry out.
During the last hour in the oven, she basted the chicken with melted butter.

Synset Examples

baste a roast

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to marinate - to soak food in a seasoned liquid before cooking
  • Synonym: roast - to cook food, especially meat, by dry heat in an oven or over a fire

Etymology

From Old French *bastir* (to build, prepare, sew), later specialized in cooking.
