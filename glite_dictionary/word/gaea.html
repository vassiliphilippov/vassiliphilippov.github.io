Gaea

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡiː.ə, ˈɡeɪ.ə / (AmE), / ˈɡiː.ə, ˈɡeɪ.ə / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the goddess personifying the earth and the mother of Cronus and the Titans.
Greek earth goddess, mother of Titans

Examples

According to myth, Gaea gave birth to the sky, mountains, and sea.
Many ancient Greeks paid homage to Gaea as the Earth Mother.
The story describes how Gaea helped her son Cronus overthrow Uranus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gaia - alternative spelling; earth goddess in Greek mythology
  • Synonym: Terra - Roman equivalent, goddess of the earth

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Γαῖα (Gaia), meaning 'earth'.
