perambulator

1. NOUN

/ pəˈræmbjəˌleɪtɚ / (AmE), / pəˈræmbjʊleɪtə / (BrE)
a carriage with four wheels used for pushing a baby or young child; a baby carriage
baby carriage, stroller (especially British)
british

Examples

The old-fashioned perambulator stood in the hallway, waiting to be wheeled to the park.
She pushed the baby gently in his perambulator along the quiet country lane.
Back then, every family seemed to have a sturdy perambulator for their infants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stroller - a small carriage for pushing a baby, mainly American use
  • Synonym: baby carriage - a wheeled vehicle for babies, mainly American use

Etymology

From Latin 'perambulare' (to walk through), via French and late 19th century English.
