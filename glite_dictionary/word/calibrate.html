to calibrate

1. VERB

/ ˈkæləˌbreɪt / (AmE), / ˈkælɪbreɪt / (BrE)
to adjust or mark a measuring instrument so that it gives accurate results
adjust measuring instrument for accuracy

Examples

It is important to calibrate your thermometer before taking any scientific measurements.
The technician will calibrate the machine to ensure accurate test results.
You must calibrate the scale regularly to prevent measurement errors.

Synset Examples

calibrate an instrument
graduate a cylinder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graduate - to mark a measuring instrument with units for measurement

Etymology

From Latin 'calibrum' (caliper, gauge), via French 'calibrer'.

WordNet ID: oewn-00296478-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈkæl.ɪˌbreɪt / (AmE), / ˈkæl.ɪ.breɪt / (BrE)
To measure the caliber (internal diameter) of a gun, tube, or similar object.
measure the caliber of a gun or tube

Examples

The engineer was asked to calibrate the new artillery piece before it was put into service.
Before firing, the technician carefully calibrated the barrel using precise tools.
It is essential to calibrate the gun regularly to ensure safety and accuracy.

Synset Examples

calibrate a gun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: measure - determine the size, amount, or degree of something

Etymology

From Latin 'caliber' (means of measuring), via French 'calibrer' (to measure the caliber of a gun).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00741541-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈkæləˌbreɪt / (AmE), / ˈkælɪˌbreɪt / (BrE)
to mark the scale of a measuring instrument so that it can be read in the desired units
mark the scale of an instrument for measurement

Examples

Technicians calibrate each pressure gauge before installation to ensure accuracy.
It is important to calibrate laboratory equipment regularly for reliable results.
The engineer carefully calibrated the scale to read in grams rather than ounces.

Synset Examples

he calibrated the thermometer for the Celsius scale

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adjust - to change something slightly to make it correct or more effective

Etymology

From Latin 'calibrum' (a measuring instrument) via French 'calibrer'.
