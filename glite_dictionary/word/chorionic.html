chorionic

1. ADJ

/ kɔːriˈɑːnɪk / (AmE), / kɒrɪˈɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to the chorion, the outer membrane surrounding an embryo in mammals, birds, and reptiles
relating to the chorion (embryonic membrane)
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that chorionic tissue is essential for nourishing the developing embryo.
A chorionic biopsy can provide valuable genetic information during early pregnancy.
Researchers found differences in chorionic development among various mammalian species.

Synset Examples

a chorionic villus is a minute vascular projection on the fetal chorion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chorion - the outer fetal membrane in mammals, birds, and reptiles

Etymology

From chorion (Greek khórion, membrane enclosing the fetus) + -ic (forming adjectives).
