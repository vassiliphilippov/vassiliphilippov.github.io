headline

1. VERB

/ ˈhɛdˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɛd.laɪn / (BrE)
to provide (a newspaper page or a story) with a headline
to give a headline to

Examples

The Times decided to headline the scandal on its front page.
All major newspapers headlined the election results the following day.
The story was headlined 'A Nation Divided' by the editor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: title - to give a name or title to something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02337441-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈhɛdˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɛdˌlaɪn / (BrE)
to publicize or present (someone or something) in a prominent or highly visible way, as if featuring in a headline.
feature or publicize prominently

Examples

The new policy was headlined in the morning newspapers across the country.
She headlined the festival, drawing crowds from around the region.
The organization hopes to headline its latest achievements at the annual conference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feature - to give prominent presentation or emphasis

Etymology

From the noun 'headline', meaning the heading at the top of a newspaper article.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00978256-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɛdlaɪn / (BrE)
the title or heading at the top of a newspaper article or other published story
title of a newspaper article

Examples

The headline of the article grabbed my attention immediately.
Every newspaper tried to outdo each other with a bold headline.
After reading the headline, she decided to buy the magazine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: title - the name of a book, composition, or other artistic work
  • Synonym: caption - a brief explanation or description accompanying an illustration or photograph

Etymology

From 'head' + 'line', indicating a line at the head (top) of a page or article.
