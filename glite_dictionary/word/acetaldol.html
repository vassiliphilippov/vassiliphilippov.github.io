acetaldol

1. NOUN

/ ˌæsətˈældɔl / (AmE), / ˌasɪtˈaldɒl / (BrE)
A colorless, unsaturated aldol (hydroxyaldehyde) formed by the condensation of two molecules of acetaldehyde, used as an intermediate in organic synthesis.
an unsaturated aldol compound
medical

Examples

Acetaldol is commonly used as an intermediate in the synthesis of various pharmaceuticals.
The condensation of acetaldehyde molecules yields acetaldol as a primary product.
Researchers observed the formation of acetaldol during their study of organic reactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aldol - a compound containing both aldehyde and alcohol groups, formed by aldol condensation

Etymology

From 'acet-' (relating to acetaldehyde) + 'aldol' (from 'aldehyde' and 'alcohol').
