Actiniopteris

1. NOUN

/ ækˌtɪniˈɑptərɪs / (AmE), / ækˌtɪnɪˈɒptərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small terrestrial ferns native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa, characterized by fan-shaped fronds.
genus of tropical ferns

Examples

The delicate fronds of the Actiniopteris make it a popular choice among fern enthusiasts.
Researchers discovered a new species of Actiniopteris in southern India.
Unlike many other ferns, Actiniopteris thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

Etymology

From Greek 'aktis' (ray) and 'pteris' (fern), referring to the radiating fronds.
