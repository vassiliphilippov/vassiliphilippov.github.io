compliment

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmplɪmənt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmplɪmənt / (BrE)
a polite remark or action that expresses praise, admiration, or approval
a remark expressing praise or admiration

Examples

She blushed when he gave her a compliment about her dress.
Receiving a sincere compliment can brighten anyone’s day.
He took her silence as a compliment to his cooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration
  • Antonym: insult - a disrespectful or scornful remark

Etymology

From Latin complimentum (an expression of respect or civility), via French compliment.

2. VERB

/ ˈkɑːmplɪˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmplɪmənt / (BrE)
to politely express praise or admiration to someone
express praise or admiration

Examples

3. VERB

/ ˈkɑːmpləˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmplɪˌmɛnt / (BrE)
to express praise, admiration, or respect for someone; to say something appreciative.
express praise or admiration

Examples

She complimented him on his excellent presentation skills.
The manager complimented the team for their hard work and dedication.
He always knows how to compliment others in a sincere and genuine way.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: praise - to express approval or admiration
  • Antonym: insult - to speak to or treat with disrespect or scorn

Etymology

From Latin 'complimentum' (fulfillment, compliment), from 'complere' (to fill up, complete).
