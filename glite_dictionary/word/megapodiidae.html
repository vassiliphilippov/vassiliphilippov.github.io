Megapodiidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛgəˌpoʊˈdiːɪˌdaɪ / (AmE), / ˌmɛgəˌpəʊˈdɪːɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of medium-sized, ground-dwelling birds known as megapodes, native to Australasia and noted for their unique egg-laying habits
family of ground-dwelling birds (megapodes)

Examples

The Megapodiidae are famous for their mound-building nesting behavior.
Members of the Megapodiidae family can be found throughout Australasia.
Unlike most birds, Megapodiidae use external heat sources to incubate their eggs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: megapode - a chicken-like bird with large feet; member of the Megapodiidae family

Etymology

From Greek mega- (large) + pous/pod- (foot), referring to their large feet, combined into the modern Latin family name.
