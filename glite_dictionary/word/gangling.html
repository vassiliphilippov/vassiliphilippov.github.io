gangling

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡæŋ.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡæŋ.lɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person) tall, thin, and having long, slender limbs, often with an awkward or ungainly appearance.
tall and thin, with long limbs

Examples

The gangling youth ducked under the doorway as he entered the crowded room.
During his teenage years, he was a gangling figure, all arms and legs.
Their son shot up over the summer and transformed into a gangling adolescent.

Synset Examples

a gangling teenager
a lanky kid transformed almost overnight into a handsome young man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lanky - tall and thin, often with long limbs
  • Antonym: stocky - broad, sturdy, and compact in build

Etymology

Probably related to 'gangly,' of uncertain origin, possibly from the word 'gang' (to go, walk).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡæŋ.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡæŋ.lɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person) tall, thin, and awkward in movement or appearance.
tall, thin, and awkward

Examples

He was a gangling teenager who towered over his classmates.
The gangling basketball player moved awkwardly down the court.
She described her brother as a gangling youth with big feet and long arms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lanky - ungracefully tall and thin
  • Antonym: stocky - broad and sturdily built

Etymology

From English 'gang' (to go, walk) + -ling (diminutive or descriptive suffix), originally meaning moving awkwardly.

Synset POS: s
