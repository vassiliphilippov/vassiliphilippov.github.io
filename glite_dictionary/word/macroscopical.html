macroscopical

1. ADJ

/ ˌmæ.krəˈskɑː.pɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌmak.rəˈskɒ.pɪ.kəl / (BrE)
large enough to be visible with the naked eye; not microscopic
visible to the naked eye

Examples

The scientist examined the macroscopical features of the specimen before using a microscope.
Several macroscopical cracks were found on the surface of the old statue.
Unlike bacteria, these organisms are macroscopical and easy to observe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macroscopic - large enough to be visible to the naked eye
  • Antonym: microscopic - too small to be seen except under a microscope

Etymology

From Greek 'makros' (large) + 'skopein' (to look).

2. ADJ

/ ˌmæk.rəˈskɑː.pɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌmæk.rəˈskɒ.pɪ.kəl / (BrE)
visible to the naked eye; large enough to be seen without the use of a microscope
visible to the naked eye

Examples

The plant's macroscopical features include broad leaves and thick stems easily seen without magnification.
No evidence of damage was found upon macroscopical examination of the specimen.
The analysis focused on macroscopical differences between the two mineral samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macroscopic - visible to the naked eye; not microscopic
  • Antonym: microscopic - too small to be seen without a microscope

Etymology

From 'macro-' (large, long) and Greek 'skopein' (to look, to examine), modeled on 'microscopical.'
