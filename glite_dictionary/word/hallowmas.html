Hallowmas

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæloʊˌmæs / (AmE), / ˈhæləʊˌmæs / (BrE)
a Christian feast day honoring all the saints, celebrated on November 1st; also called All Saints' Day
Christian feast day, All Saints' Day

Examples

Many churches hold special services on Hallowmas to honor the saints.
The tradition of Hallowmas dates back to the early centuries of Christianity.
Families gathered to attend mass and celebrate Hallowmas together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: All Saints' Day - Christian festival honoring all saints, celebrated on November 1st

Etymology

From Middle English *halowmasse*, from Old English *hālga mæsse* (mass of the holy [ones]).
