Lyrurus mlokosiewiczi

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈrʊrəs mloʊkəˈʃɛvɪtʃi / (AmE), / lɪˈrʊərəs məˌlɒkəˈʃɛvɪtʃi / (BrE)
A species of black grouse found in western Asia, known for its dark plumage and rounded tail.
a black grouse species of western Asia

Examples

The Lyrurus mlokosiewiczi inhabits forests and mountain slopes in the Caucasus region.
Conservationists are concerned about declines in the population of Lyrurus mlokosiewiczi due to habitat loss.
We observed a male Lyrurus mlokosiewiczi displaying its remarkable lyre-shaped tail feathers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black grouse - a large bird from the grouse family with mostly black plumage

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'lyra', meaning 'lyre', for the tail shape; species named after Polish naturalist Ludwik Młokosiewicz.
