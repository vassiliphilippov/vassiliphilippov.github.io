to confess

1. VERB

/ kənˈfɛs / (AmE), / kənˈfɛs / (BrE)
to admit, often under pressure, that one has committed a crime or done something wrong
admit to a crime or wrongdoing, often under pressure

Examples

After hours of questioning, the suspect finally confessed to the robbery.
She refused to confess her involvement, even when presented with the evidence.
The prisoner confessed under pressure from the authorities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to admit - to acknowledge or disclose something, especially reluctantly
  • Antonym: to deny - to state that something is not true, especially a crime or allegation

Etymology

From Latin 'confessari', meaning ‘to acknowledge’ or ‘admit’.

2. VERB

/ kənˈfɛs / (AmE), / kənˈfɛs / (BrE)
to admit that one has committed a crime, fault, or wrongdoing; to acknowledge something reluctantly or with embarrassment
admit to a wrongdoing or fault

Examples

After hours of questioning, he finally confessed to the crime.
She confessed her mistake in front of the whole team.
I must confess that I was nervous before the interview.

Synset Examples

She confessed that she had taken the money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to admit - to acknowledge or accept as true; often used for admitting faults
  • Antonym: to deny - to state that something is not true; opposite of confess

Etymology

From Latin 'confessus', past participle of 'confiteri' (to acknowledge, confess).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00820214-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kənˈfɛs / (AmE), / kənˈfɛs / (BrE)
(in the Catholic faith) to admit one's sins to God in the presence of a priest, typically as part of a religious sacrament.
admit sins to a priest (Catholic)

Examples

After wrestling with guilt, she decided to confess her sins to the priest at the local church.
Catholics are encouraged to confess regularly as part of their spiritual practice.
He nervously entered the confessional, ready to confess and seek forgiveness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to absolve - to declare someone free from guilt, obligation, or punishment (especially after confession)
  • Synonym: to repent - to feel or express sincere regret about one's wrongdoing or sin

Etymology

From Latin confiteri (to admit, acknowledge).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
