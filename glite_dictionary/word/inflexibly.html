inflexibly

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈflɛksəbli / (AmE), / ɪnˈflɛksəbli / (BrE)
In a way that does not change or adapt; in a rigid or unyielding manner.
in a rigid or unyielding way

Examples

She applied the rules inflexibly, leaving no room for exceptions.
The company's policies are enforced inflexibly across all departments.
He inflexibly refused to change his opinion despite new evidence.

Synset Examples

‘You will — because you must!’ Madam told her inflexibly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rigidly - in a stiff or inflexible manner
  • Antonym: flexibly - in a flexible or adaptable manner

Etymology

From 'inflexible' + adverbial suffix '-ly'; ultimately from Latin 'inflexibilis'.
