antonymy

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.təˈnɪm.i / (AmE), / ˌæn.təˈnɪm.i / (BrE)
the semantic relationship between words that have opposite meanings
opposite word relationship

Examples

Understanding antonymy helps students expand their English vocabulary.
There is a clear case of antonymy between the words 'hot' and 'cold'.
Linguists study antonymy to analyze patterns in language.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: synonymy - the semantic relationship between words with similar meanings

Etymology

From Greek anti- (against) + -onym (name).
