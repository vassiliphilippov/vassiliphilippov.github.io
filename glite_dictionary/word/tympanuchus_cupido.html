Tympanuchus cupido

1. NOUN

/ tɪm.pəˈnuː.kəs ˈkjuː.pɪ.doʊ / (AmE), / tɪm.pəˈnjuː.kəs ˈkjuː.pɪ.dəʊ / (BrE)
a North American game bird, the greater prairie chicken, known for its distinctive mating display and booming calls
species of prairie chicken

Examples

During spring, the Tympanuchus cupido performs elaborate dances on the lek to attract mates.
Habitat loss has caused a steep decline in Tympanuchus cupido populations across the Midwest.
Researchers observed a flock of Tympanuchus cupido booming at sunrise on the prairie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prairie chicken - a North American grouse of open country, including the greater and lesser prairie chicken

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Tympanuchus' from Greek 'tympanon' (drum) + 'nuchos' (neck), and 'cupido' meaning 'desire' in Latin, referring to courtship displays.
