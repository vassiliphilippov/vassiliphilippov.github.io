Chamaecytisus palmensis

1. NOUN

/ kəˌmiːsaɪˈtaɪsəs pælˈmɛnsɪs / (AmE), / kəˌmiːsaɪˈtɪsəs pælˈmɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A shrub native to the Canary Islands, with bristle-tipped oblanceolate leaves, cultivated as cattle fodder.
shrub of Canary Islands used as fodder

Examples

Chamaecytisus palmensis is often grown on farms in the Canary Islands to feed livestock.
Researchers are studying the benefits of planting Chamaecytisus palmensis as a sustainable fodder crop.
Some farmers value Chamaecytisus palmensis for its drought resistance and high nutritional content.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining genus Chamaecytisus (from Greek khamaí 'on the ground' + kytisos, a kind of shrub) and specific epithet palmensis, meaning 'of La Palma'.
