chilblains

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪlˌbleɪnz / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪlˌbleɪnz / (BrE)
Painful, itchy swellings on the skin, usually of the fingers, toes, or ears, caused by exposure to cold and damp conditions.
skin inflammation from cold exposure

Examples

After walking through the snow without gloves, she developed chilblains on her fingers.
Cold, damp weather can increase your chances of suffering from chilblains.
The doctor advised keeping feet dry and warm to prevent chilblains.

Etymology

From chill (cold) + blain (sore), dating to the 16th century.
