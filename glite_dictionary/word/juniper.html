juniper

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːnɪpər / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːnɪpə / (BrE)
A coniferous shrub or small tree with fragrant, needle-like leaves and berrylike cones, often used in landscaping and for flavoring gin.
coniferous shrub or small tree

Examples

The juniper bushes in the garden filled the air with their refreshing scent.
Berries from the juniper are commonly used to flavor gin.
A row of juniper trees created a natural barrier along the driveway.

Etymology

From Latin 'juniperus', the classical name for the plant.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːnəpɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːnɪpə / (BrE)
A desert shrub of Syria and Arabia with small white flowers, mentioned in the Old Testament, sometimes classified in the genus Genista.
desert shrub with small white flowers, biblical reference

Examples

The ancient texts describe travelers resting under the shade of the juniper.
Archaeologists debated whether the juniper mentioned in Scripture is actually a species of Genista.
Desert landscapes in Arabia often feature the hardy juniper shrub.

Etymology

From Latin juniperus (the juniper tree).

the wood of a coniferous shrub or small tree with berrylike cones, used in carpentry, aromatic products, and sometimes for flavoring
wood from the juniper shrub or tree

Examples

The cabinet was crafted from juniper for its distinctive fragrance and durability.
Some cultures use juniper in the production of incense and essential oils.
The soft, pale color of juniper makes it popular for small decorative carvings.

Related Terms
