to deglycerolize

1. VERB

/ diˈɡlɪsəˌroʊˌlaɪz / (AmE), / diˈɡlɪsərəˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to remove glycerol from a substance, especially as part of a laboratory or medical process
remove glycerol from something
medical

Examples

Before transfusion, blood is often deglycerolized to remove the preservative agent.
Laboratory equipment is used to deglycerolize stored red blood cells for safe clinical use.
The team deglycerolized the samples as part of the preparation procedure.

Etymology

From de- (removal) + glycerol + -ize (verb-forming suffix).
