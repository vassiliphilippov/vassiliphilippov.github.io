haslet

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæs.lɪt / (AmE), / ˈhæz.lɪt / (BrE)
the heart, liver, and other edible organs of a hog, usually chopped, formed into a loaf, and cooked by braising; a type of meat loaf made from offal
hog offal meat loaf

Examples

Traditional English butchers often prepare haslet as a specialty for local customers.
She served slices of haslet with warm bread and pickles for lunch.
You can find haslet in some rural markets, especially in the Midlands.

Etymology

From Old French *hastilles* (entrails), further related to Old French *hasteile* (intestine).
