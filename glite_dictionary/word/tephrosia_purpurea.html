Tephrosia purpurea

1. NOUN

/ tɛˈfroʊsiə pɚˈpjʊriə / (AmE), / tɛˈfrəʊsiə pɜːˈpjʊəriə / (BrE)
A tropical shrub of the pea family native to India, with purplish flowers, used in traditional medicine and as green manure.
a tropical Indian shrub with purplish flowers

Examples

Tephrosia purpurea is widely used in Ayurvedic medicine for treating various ailments.
Farmers often grow Tephrosia purpurea to improve soil fertility through green manuring.
The delicate purple blooms of Tephrosia purpurea can be seen across the Indian countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Crotalaria - a related genus of leguminous plants

Etymology

From Greek 'tephros' (ashen) referring to leaf color, and Latin 'purpurea' (purple), describing its flowers.
