Catasetum macrocarpum

1. NOUN

/ ˌkætəˈsɛtəm ˌmækroʊˈkɑrpəm / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈsiːtəm ˌmakrəʊˈkɑːpəm / (BrE)
A tropical orchid species with both male and female flowers on the same raceme, known for its mechanism in which pollen is rapidly ejected when touched.
a tropical orchid species with pollen-ejecting flowers

Examples

The Catasetum macrocarpum displays both male and female flowers on the same flower cluster.
When triggered, the male flower of Catasetum macrocarpum quickly ejects its pollen.
Botanists are fascinated by the unique pollination mechanism of Catasetum macrocarpum.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus name Catasetum + species epithet macrocarpum (from Greek makros 'large' + karpos 'fruit').
