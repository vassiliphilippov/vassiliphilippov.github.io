aecium

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːsiəm / (AmE), / ˈiːsiəm / (BrE)
A cup-shaped fruiting body of certain rust fungi, producing chains of aeciospores.
fungal fruiting body (rust fungi)
medical

Examples

Microscopically, the aecium is identified by its distinctive cup-like shape and arrangement of spores.
The presence of aecium on the leaf undersurface confirmed the rust infection in the barley crop.
Researchers examined the aecium of the fungus to better understand its reproductive cycle.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek aikia (injury), referring to the effects of the fungus on host plants.
