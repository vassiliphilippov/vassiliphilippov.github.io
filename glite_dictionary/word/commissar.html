commissar

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.mɪ.sɑr / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ɪ.sɑː / (BrE)
an official in the Communist Party, especially in the former Soviet Union, assigned to educate and enforce party principles within a military unit or organization.
Communist Party political officer

Examples

The Soviet army assigned a commissar to every regiment to ensure loyalty to the party.
During the revolution, the commissar addressed the troops about communist ideals.
Each division had its own commissar, responsible for overseeing political education.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: political officer - an officer responsible for political education and loyalty in a military unit

Etymology

From Russian 'komissar', from French 'commissaire' (commissioner).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmɪˌsɑːr / (AmE), / ˈkɒmɪsɑː / (BrE)
an official in a position of authority, especially one in a Communist government responsible for political education, control, or enforcement of policy
official in authority, esp. in Communist regimes

Examples

The commissar issued strict orders to the workers in the factory.
During the revolution, the commissar was responsible for enforcing party loyalty among the troops.
He was seen as the commissar of the local community organization.

Synset Examples

He was seen as the commissar of the local community organization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commissioner - an official in charge of a department or organization

Etymology

From Russian 'komissar', from German 'Kommissar', from Latin 'commissarius' (person entrusted with a charge).
