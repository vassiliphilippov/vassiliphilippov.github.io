avulsion

1. NOUN

/ əˈvʌlʃən / (AmE), / əˈvʌlʃən / (BrE)
an abrupt change in the course of a stream or river, especially one that alters property boundaries by transferring land from one owner to another
sudden change in river's course affecting land boundaries
legal

Examples

The farmer lost several acres to his neighbor due to an avulsion caused by last spring’s flood.
Legal disputes often arise after an avulsion modifies the course of a boundary river.
An avulsion can instantly turn one person’s land into another's by shifting the riverbed.

Etymology

From Latin avulsio (a tearing away), from avellere (to tear away).

2. NOUN

/ əˈvʌlʃən / (AmE), / əˈvʌlʃən / (BrE)
the forcible tearing away or surgical separation of a body part from its point of attachment
forcible body part tearing or separation
medical

Examples

The patient suffered an avulsion of the toenail after the accident.
Treatment of a nerve avulsion can be complex and may require surgery.
In dentistry, tooth avulsion occurs when a tooth is completely displaced from its socket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laceration - a deep cut or tear in skin or flesh
  • Synonym: amputation - the removal of a limb or body part by surgery or trauma

Etymology

From Latin 'avulsio' (a tearing away), from 'avellere' (to tear off).
