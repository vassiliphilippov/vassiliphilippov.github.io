combining

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈbaɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / kəmˈbaɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
an instance or process of joining or uniting two or more things into one
joining or uniting things

Examples

The successful combining of their talents led to a groundbreaking project.
Effective research often depends on the combining of data from multiple sources.
A key to his culinary technique is the combining of unusual flavors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: union - the act of joining two or more things together
  • Antonym: separation - the act of moving things apart

2. NOUN

/ kəmˈbaɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / kəmˈbaɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of joining two or more things together to form a single whole
the act of joining things together

Examples

The combining of their resources made the project possible.
A successful recipe often requires the careful combining of flavors.
Through the combining of efforts, the team achieved outstanding results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combination - a result or product of combining

Etymology

From the verb 'combine', from Latin 'combinare' (to unite).
