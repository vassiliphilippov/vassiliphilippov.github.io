ginseng

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪnˌsɛŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪn.sɛŋ / (BrE)
A plant with forked aromatic roots, used especially in Chinese medicine and herbal remedies for its supposed health benefits.
herb with medicinal roots

Examples

Traditional medicine often uses ginseng to boost energy and support the immune system.
She adds a slice of ginseng to her tea every morning for its reputed health benefits.
The roots of ginseng are highly valued in herbal medicine worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Panax - the genus to which ginseng belongs
  • Degree Lower: herb - a plant used for flavoring, food, medicine, or perfume

Etymology

From Chinese rénshēn, meaning 'man root' due to the root’s resembling a human figure.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪnˌsɛŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪn.sɛŋ / (BrE)
The aromatic root of a plant of the genus Panax, valued for its medicinal properties in traditional and herbal medicine.
aromatic medicinal root

Examples

Some people drink tea made from ginseng roots to boost their energy.
The herbalist recommended adding ginseng to the treatment plan for better health.
Asian markets often carry fresh and dried ginseng for use in cooking and medicine.

Etymology

From Chinese rénshēn (人参), meaning 'person root'.
