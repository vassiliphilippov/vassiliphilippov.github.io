seismosaur

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪzmoʊˌsɔr / (AmE), / ˈsaɪzməʊˌsɔː / (BrE)
a huge herbivorous dinosaur of the Cretaceous period, found in western North America
large Cretaceous herbivorous dinosaur

Examples

The seismosaur was one of the longest land animals to have ever lived.
Fossils of the seismosaur were discovered in New Mexico, exciting paleontologists worldwide.
Children are often amazed by the sheer size of a seismosaur at the natural history museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dinosaur - any of various extinct, often large, reptiles of the Mesozoic era

Etymology

From Greek 'seismos' (earthquake) + 'sauros' (lizard).
