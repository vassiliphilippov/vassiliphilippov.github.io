Decumaria

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛkjuˈmɛriə / (AmE), / ˌdɛkjʊˈmɑːriə / (BrE)
A small genus of woody climbing plants with adhesive aerial roots, sometimes classified in the family Saxifragaceae.
genus of woody climbing plants

Examples

The garden featured a rare specimen of Decumaria climbing up the trellis.
Botanists recently revised the classification of Decumaria within flowering plants.
Decumaria is notable for its adhesive aerial roots that enable it to climb surfaces.

Etymology

From Latin 'decumarius', relating to ten, likely referring to the flower parts.
