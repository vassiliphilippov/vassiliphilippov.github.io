archdeaconry

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrtʃˈdiːkənri / (AmE), / ˌɑːtʃˈdiːkənri / (BrE)
A district within a diocese for which an archdeacon is responsible; the area of an archdeacon's ecclesiastical jurisdiction.
territorial jurisdiction of an archdeacon

Examples

The bishop appointed a new leader to oversee the archdeaconry after the previous archdeacon retired.
Historical records show that the archdeaconry played a vital role in administering church affairs in the region.
She conducted a series of visits to parishes throughout the archdeaconry to discuss community programs.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: diocese - the district under the supervision of a bishop
  • Degree Lower: parish - a local church community within a diocese

Etymology

From medieval Latin archidiaconatus, from Greek arkhidiakonos (chief deacon).
