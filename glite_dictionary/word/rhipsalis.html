Rhipsalis

1. NOUN

/ rɪpˈsælɪs / (AmE), / rɪpˈsalɪs / (BrE)
A genus of epiphytic or lithophytic cacti with segmented, often pendulous stems, and small flowers followed by berrylike fruits, native to tropical regions.
a genus of epiphytic cacti

Examples

Rhipsalis is often grown indoors as a decorative plant due to its unusual stem structure.
Many species within the genus Rhipsalis thrive in humid, shaded environments.
The pendulous branches of Rhipsalis can produce small, white flowers followed by bright berries.

Etymology

From Greek 'rhips' (wickerwork, ref. to the stems).

2. PROPN

/ rɪpˈsælɪs / (AmE), / rɪpˈsælɪs / (BrE)
A genus of epiphytic or lithophytic cacti, often with segmented stems and hanging branches, native to tropical forests.
genus of epiphytic cacti

Examples

The greenhouse features a stunning collection of Rhipsalis hanging from the ceiling.
Many Rhipsalis species thrive as houseplants due to their decorative, slender stems.
Rhipsalis is unusual among cacti for being native to rainforests rather than deserts.

Etymology

From Greek *rhíps* (wickerwork, mat), referring to the interwoven stems.
