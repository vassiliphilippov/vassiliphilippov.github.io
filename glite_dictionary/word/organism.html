organism

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɡəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡənɪzəm / (BrE)
a complex system, such as a society or organization, regarded as having interdependent parts functioning together like a living being
complex system behaving like a living body

Examples

In sociology, a nation is often described as a social organism with many interdependent elements.
The company is an organism whose departments must work together to thrive.
Some philosophers consider language an evolving organism shaped by its speakers.

Synset Examples

the social organism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: system - a set of connected things or parts forming a complex whole

Etymology

From Greek organon (instrument, tool) via New Latin organismus.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɡəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡənɪzəm / (BrE)
an individual living thing, such as an animal, plant, or single-celled life form
individual living thing

Examples

A human is a complex organism made up of billions of cells.
Scientists discovered a new organism deep in the ocean.
Every organism requires energy to survive and grow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creature - a living being, especially an animal

Etymology

From Greek *organon* (tool, instrument) and -ism, meaning a being with organs or organized parts.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00004475-n
Synset POS: n
