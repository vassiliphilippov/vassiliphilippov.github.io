gumma

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌmə / (AmE), / ˈɡʌmə / (BrE)
A small, rubbery swelling (granuloma) that occurs in the late stages of syphilis, often causing tissue destruction.
syphilitic swelling (late-stage)
medical

Examples

The patient developed a gumma on his palate, which was later confirmed to be syphilitic in origin.
Untreated syphilis can eventually cause destructive lesions known as gummas in various organs.
Surgical intervention was necessary to remove the gumma that was impairing her mobility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: granuloma - a small area of inflammation in tissue due to infection or immune response

Etymology

From Latin gummi (gum), due to its gum-like consistency.
