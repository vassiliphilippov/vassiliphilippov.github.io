eurhythmics

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈrɪðmɪks / (AmE), / jʊəˈrɪðmɪks / (BrE)
a method of teaching musical understanding through harmonious bodily movements that interpret the rhythm of musical compositions
teaching music through rhythmic movement

Examples

Children learn to appreciate rhythm through eurhythmics classes that combine movement with music.
The school incorporates eurhythmics in its curriculum to enhance students' musical perception.
Many music teachers are trained in eurhythmics to help students develop a sense of timing and expression.

Etymology

From Greek 'eu-' (good, well) and 'rhythmos' (rhythm); popularized by Swiss music educator Émile Jaques-Dalcroze in the early 20th century.
