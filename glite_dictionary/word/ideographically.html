ideographically

1. ADV

/ ˌaɪdiəˈɡræfɪkli / (AmE), / ˌaɪdiəˈɡræfɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to or uses ideograms (symbols or characters representing ideas or concepts rather than sounds).
in an ideogram-based manner

Examples

The ancient script was written ideographically rather than alphabetically.
Some languages record meaning ideographically instead of focusing on phonetic spelling.
Mathematical concepts are often communicated ideographically using universally recognized symbols.

Synset Examples

it's written ideographically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symbolically - in a symbolic manner

Etymology

From 'ideographic' + '-ally', formed from Greek 'idea' (form, pattern) and 'graphein' (to write).
