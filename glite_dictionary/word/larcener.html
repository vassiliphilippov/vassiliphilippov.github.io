larcener

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑr.sə.nər / (AmE), / ˈlɑː.sɪ.nə / (BrE)
a person who commits larceny; a thief
person who commits larceny

Examples

The larcener was quickly apprehended by the police after the theft.
In court, the larcener admitted to stealing the jewelry from the store.
The old law book defines a larcener as anyone guilty of unlawful taking of property.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thief - a person who steals another person's property
  • Synonym: larcenist - a person who commits larceny, especially in criminal law

Etymology

From Old French 'larsinier', from 'larcin' (theft), from Latin 'lātrocinium' (robbery).
