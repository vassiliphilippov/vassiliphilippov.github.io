dryad

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪˌæd / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ.ad / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a nymph or spirit who lives in or is associated with a tree, especially an oak tree, or with forests.
woodland nymph or spirit

Examples

In ancient myths, a dryad was believed to inhabit each oak tree.
The poet described the dryads dancing in the shadowy forest at dusk.
Legends said that harming the woods would anger the dryad who protected them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nymph - a mythological spirit of nature represented as a beautiful maiden

Etymology

From Greek dryas, dryad- (tree nymph), from drys (oak).
