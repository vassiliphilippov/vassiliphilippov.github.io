light-hearted

1. ADJ

/ ˌlaɪtˈhɑrt̬ɪd / (AmE), / ˌlaɪtˈhɑːtɪd / (BrE)
cheerful and carefree; not weighed down by worries or serious concerns.
cheerful and carefree

Examples

She told a light-hearted story that made everyone in the room laugh.
His light-hearted attitude helped ease the tension during the meeting.
The movie has a light-hearted tone, perfect for a relaxing evening.

Synset Examples

was loved for her blithe spirit
a merry blithesome nature
her lighthearted nature
trilling songs with a lightsome heart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic
  • Synonym: carefree - free from worries; having no problems
  • Antonym: serious - solemn or thoughtful in character or manner

Etymology

From 'light' (not heavy) + 'hearted' (having a specified feeling or mood), first used in the late 16th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˌlaɪtˈhɑrtɪd / (AmE), / ˌlaɪtˈhɑːtɪd / (BrE)
cheerful and not intended to be serious; carefree and happy.
cheerful and carefree

Examples

The movie had a light-hearted tone that made everyone smile.
Her light-hearted attitude helped ease the tension in the room.
We enjoyed a light-hearted conversation over coffee.

Synset Examples

The couple entered a light-hearted sex pact for fun.
He joked that the festival featured some light-hearted cornography.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic
  • Antonym: serious - solemn or not light-hearted
  • Synonym: carefree - free from worries or responsibilities

Etymology

From 'light' (not heavy) + 'hearted' (having the specified kind of heart or mood).
