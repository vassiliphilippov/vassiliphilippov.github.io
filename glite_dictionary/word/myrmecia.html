Myrmecia

1. NOUN

/ mɜːrˈmiːʃiə / (AmE), / mɜːˈmiːsɪə / (BrE)
A genus of large, aggressive ants, commonly known as bulldog ants, native to Australia.
a genus of bulldog ants

Examples

The Myrmecia genus includes some of the most aggressive ants found in Australia.
A Myrmecia ant's sting can be extremely painful to humans.
Researchers studied the behavior of Myrmecia colonies in their natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bulldog ant - A common name for ants of the genus Myrmecia, known for their aggression

Etymology

From Greek myrmēk-, meaning 'ant'.
