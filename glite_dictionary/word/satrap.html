satrap

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪ.træp / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.træp / (BrE)
a governor of a province in ancient Persia, responsible for administering the region and collecting taxes on behalf of the central government
governor in ancient Persia

Examples

In the Achaemenid Empire, a satrap ruled over each province with considerable autonomy.
The Persian king appointed a new satrap to restore order in the rebellious region.
Each satrap was responsible for collecting taxes and maintaining security in their territory.

Etymology

From Old Persian *xšaθrapāvan*, via Greek *satrápēs* and Latin *satrapes*, meaning 'protector of the province'.
