indirectness

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.dɪˈrɛkt.nəs / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dɪˈrɛkt.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of not being direct or straightforward; the characteristic of lacking a true or clear course toward a goal
quality of being indirect or not straightforward

Examples

Her indirectness often made conversations more confusing than necessary.
Business negotiations sometimes require a certain level of indirectness to avoid offending the other party.
His indirectness frustrated those who preferred clear instructions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: directness - the quality of being straightforward or clear
  • Synonym: evasiveness - the quality of being evasive or avoiding directness

Etymology

From 'indirect' + '-ness', where 'indirect' is from Latin indirectus (not straight).
