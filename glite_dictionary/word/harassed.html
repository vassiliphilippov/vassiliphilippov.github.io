harassed

1. ADJ

/ həˈræst, ˈhær əst / (AmE), / həˈræst, ˈhær əst / (BrE)
feeling or showing the effects of persistent annoyance, worry, or pressure
persistently troubled or worried

Examples

After weeks of tight deadlines, she looked harassed and exhausted during the meeting.
The harassed teacher struggled to maintain order in the noisy classroom.
Commuters often appear harassed when dealing with crowded trains and daily delays.

Synset Examples

harassed working mothers
a harried expression
her poor pestered father had to endure her constant interruptions
the vexed parents of an unruly teenager

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harried - feeling strained due to persistent demands
  • Antonym: relaxed - free from tension or worry

Etymology

From the verb 'harass', which comes from French 'harasser' (to tire out, vex).
