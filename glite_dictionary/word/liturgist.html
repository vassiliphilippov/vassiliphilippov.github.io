liturgist

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈtɝːdʒɪst / (AmE), / lɪˈtɜːdʒɪst / (BrE)
a person who is an expert in or has specialized knowledge of liturgies, especially in a religious context
expert on liturgies

Examples

The church hired a distinguished liturgist to advise on its worship services.
As a liturgist, she has studied ancient and modern forms of prayer extensively.
Many communities consult a liturgist to help create meaningful rituals and ceremonies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liturgy - a form or formulary according to which public religious worship is conducted

Etymology

From Greek leitourgos (public servant, minister) + -ist (denoting a person).
