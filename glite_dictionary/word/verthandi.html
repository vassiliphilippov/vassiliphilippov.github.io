Verthandi

1. PROPN

/ vɛrˈtændi / (AmE), / vɛˈθændi / (BrE)
In Norse mythology, one of the three Norns, goddesses of fate, who represents the present.
Norse goddess of fate (the present)

Examples

In the myth, Verthandi weaves the events of the present alongside her sisters.
The trio Urd, Verthandi, and Skuld determine the destinies of gods and humans.
Scholars debate how the concept of Verthandi influenced later ideas of fate in literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Urd - Norn representing the past in Norse mythology
  • Synonym: Skuld - Norn representing the future in Norse mythology

Etymology

From Old Norse 'Verðandi', present participle of 'verða' (to become).
