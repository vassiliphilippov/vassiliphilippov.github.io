intercession

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈsɛʃən / (BrE)
a prayer to God made on behalf of another person
prayer for another person

Examples

During the vigil, the priest offered intercession for the victims of the disaster.
She believed in the power of intercession to bring comfort and healing to others.
Many people request intercession from saints when praying for loved ones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supplication - an act of asking or praying for something earnestly

Etymology

From Latin intercessio (intervening, mediation), from intercedere (to go between).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈsɛʃən / (BrE)
the act of intervening on behalf of another, especially to mediate or plead for someone else
the act of intervening or mediating

Examples

His intercession helped resolve the conflict between the two parties.
The priest offered an intercession for the sick and needy during the service.
Through her intercession, the negotiations were able to continue peacefully.

Synset Examples

it occurs without human intervention

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mediation - the process of intervening to resolve a dispute
  • Synonym: intervention - the act of becoming involved in a situation to change what is happening

Etymology

From Latin 'intercessio', from 'intercedere' meaning 'to go between, intervene'.
