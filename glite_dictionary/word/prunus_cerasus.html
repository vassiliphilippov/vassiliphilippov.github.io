Prunus cerasus

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruː.nəs səˈræs.əs / (AmE), / ˈpruː.nəs səˈrɑː.səs / (BrE)
A species of small Eurasian tree that produces red to black sour edible cherries, commonly known as the sour cherry tree.
species of tree yielding sour cherries

Examples

The tart fruit of the Prunus cerasus is prized for making pies and preserves.
Orchards of Prunus cerasus flourish in parts of Europe with cooler climates.
The botanist explained how the Prunus cerasus differs from other cherry species in both taste and appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sour cherry - the edible fruit or tree of Prunus cerasus
  • Degree Higher: sweet cherry - Prunus avium, a related species with sweeter fruit

Etymology

From Latin: prunus (plum tree) + cerasus (cherry tree).
