ecdysteroid

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.dɪˈstɛr.ɔɪd / (AmE), / ˌɛk.dɪˈstɛr.ɔɪd / (BrE)
any of a group of steroid hormones in insects and other arthropods that regulate molting and reproduction, including ecdysone and its derivatives
insect steroid hormone group
medical

Examples

Researchers observed increased levels of ecdysteroid during the larval stage of the butterflies.
Plants sometimes produce substances that mimic ecdysteroid to affect insect growth and survival.
The synthesis of ecdysteroid hormones is crucial for successful insect development.

Synset Examples

Ecdysteroids also occur in other invertebrates where they can play a different role.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecdysone - an ecdysteroid hormone that controls molting in insects

Etymology

From Greek 'ekdysis' (shedding) + 'steroid'.
