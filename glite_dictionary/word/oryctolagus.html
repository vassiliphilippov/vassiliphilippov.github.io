Oryctolagus

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˌrɪktəˈleɪɡəs / (AmE), / ɒˌrɪktəˈleɪɡəs / (BrE)
a genus of Old World rabbits, including the European rabbit
genus of Old World rabbits

Examples

The European rabbit, classified under the genus Oryctolagus, has been introduced to many parts of the world.
Researchers observed several species within the Oryctolagus genus in their natural habitats.
The distinctive burrowing behavior of Oryctolagus makes it an interesting subject for ecological studies.

Etymology

From Greek oryktos (dug up) + lagos (hare), referencing the burrowing habit of these rabbits.
