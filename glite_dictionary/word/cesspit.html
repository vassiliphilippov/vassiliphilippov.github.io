cesspit

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛsˌpɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɛs.pɪt / (BrE)
A covered underground tank or pit for the collection and storage of wastewater and sewage.
underground tank for sewage

Examples

The old farmhouse still uses a cesspit for storing sewage.
A leaking cesspit can contaminate the groundwater nearby.
Regular maintenance of the cesspit is essential to prevent environmental hazards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cesspool - an underground container for the temporary storage of liquid waste and sewage

Etymology

From 'cesspool' and 'pit', modeled on similar terms for waste containment.
