certifiable

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɝː.t̬əˈfaɪ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌsɜː.tɪˈfaɪ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
able to be formally recognized, guaranteed, or certified as meeting a standard or requirement.
able to be certified or guaranteed

Examples

The document must contain a certifiable signature to be accepted by the authorities.
Only certifiable copies of the original certificates will be considered.
The experiment produced certifiable results that could be independently measured.

Synset Examples

a certifiable fact

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verifiable - able to be checked or proved to be true
  • Antonym: uncertifiable - not capable of being guaranteed or certified

Etymology

From certify + -able.

2. ADJ

/ ˌsɝː.təˈfaɪ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌsɜː.tɪˈfaɪ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
suffering from a mental disorder severe enough to meet the criteria for legal commitment to a psychiatric hospital; often used informally to describe someone as insane or crazy.
legally insane or crazy

Examples

If he keeps acting like this, people will think he's certifiable.
She was deemed certifiable and was taken to a psychiatric hospital for treatment.
That idea is so wild, it sounds absolutely certifiable to me.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insane - mentally ill; not sane
  • Antonym: sane - mentally healthy; of sound mind

Etymology

From certify + -able, meaning 'able to be certified.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02082724-s
Synset POS: s
