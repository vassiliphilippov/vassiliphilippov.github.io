companionway

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpænjənˌweɪ / (AmE), / kəmˈpænjənˌweɪ / (BrE)
a stairway or ladder on a ship that provides access between decks
ship's stairway or ladder between decks

Examples

The captain climbed the companionway to reach the bridge.
A sudden wave made it difficult to descend the companionway safely.
Crew members gathered at the top of the companionway before going below deck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ladder - a device for climbing, typically consisting of two sidepieces with rungs between them

Etymology

From 'companion' (ship's officer or passage) + 'way' (path); dates from 18th century nautical usage.
