rebirth

1. NOUN

/ riˈbɝːθ / (AmE), / riːˈbɜːθ / (BrE)
the process of being born again or coming into existence with renewed life, energy, or significance; a second birth
a second or new birth

Examples

The country experienced a rebirth after years of civil unrest.
Spring is often seen as a time of rebirth and renewal.
After the fire, the community underwent a spiritual rebirth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resurrection - the act of rising from the dead or coming back to life
  • Synonym: renewal - the process of making something new again

Etymology

From re- (again) + birth, modeled after earlier terms like 'reborn'.

2. NOUN

/ riˈbɝθ / (AmE), / riˈbɜːθ / (BrE)
the belief or process by which, after death, a soul begins a new life in a different human body; reincarnation
the soul's new life in another body after death

Examples

Many religions teach that rebirth allows the soul to learn and grow over countless lifetimes.
Some people believe that memories of a previous life are possible through rebirth.
The concept of rebirth is central to many Eastern philosophies and spiritual practices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reincarnation - the belief that the soul is born again in a new body after death

Etymology

From re- (again) + birth, meaning 'born again'.

3. NOUN

/ ˌriˈbɝθ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈbɜːθ / (BrE)
a process of spiritual enlightenment resulting in a person beginning a new or transformed life
spiritual renewal; starting a new life

Examples

The retreat led to a profound rebirth that changed his outlook on life.
After battling addiction, she experienced a spiritual rebirth and started helping others.
Many view meditation as a path to personal rebirth and inner peace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: renewal - the process of making something new or stronger
  • Synonym: regeneration - spiritual or moral rebirth

Etymology

from re- (again) + birth; modeled on the idea of being 'born again'

4. NOUN

/ riˈbɜrθ / (AmE), / ˈriːbɜːθ / (BrE)
a period or process in which learning and culture are revived and flourish after a decline or stagnation
renewal of learning and culture

Examples

The city experienced a remarkable rebirth of art and culture during the early 20th century.
Historians often refer to the Renaissance as a rebirth of classical learning.
Her innovative teaching methods contributed greatly to the rebirth of interest in traditional literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: renaissance - the revival of art and literature under the influence of classical models in the 14th–16th centuries

Etymology

From re- (again) and birth; modeled on the literal sense of being born again.
