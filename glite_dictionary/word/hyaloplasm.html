hyaloplasm

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪ.ə.loʊˌplæz.əm / (AmE), / hʌɪˈal.əˌplaz.əm / (BrE)
the clear, non-granular part of a cell's cytoplasm, in which organelles are suspended
clear part of cell cytoplasm
medical

Examples

The organelles float freely in the hyaloplasm of the cell.
Research shows that metabolic reactions occur within the hyaloplasm.
Staining techniques can help distinguish the hyaloplasm from granulated cytoplasmic areas.

Etymology

From Greek 'hyalos' (glass) + 'plasma' (something formed or molded).
