breeze

1. NOUN

/ briːz / (AmE), / briːz / (BrE)
an easy task or undertaking
something very easy to do
informal

Examples

Compared to my last job, this project is a real breeze.
The exam was a breeze for anyone who studied the textbook carefully.
Cooking for just two people is a breeze after hosting large family gatherings.

Synset Examples

marketing this product will be no picnic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinch - something that is very easy to do

2. VERB

/ briːz / (AmE), / briːz / (BrE)
(of the wind) to blow gently and lightly.
blow gently and lightly (of wind)

Examples

A soft wind breezed across the open field at dawn.
Cool air often breezes through the attic windows during the summer.
Palm trees gently breezed in the warm evening air.

Synset Examples

It breezes most evenings at the shore

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gust - a sudden strong rush of wind
  • Synonym: to waft - to move lightly or smoothly through the air

3. NOUN

/ briːz / (AmE), / briːz / (BrE)
a gentle and pleasant wind, usually light and refreshing
a gentle, refreshing wind

Examples

A light breeze rustled the leaves in the garden as she read her book.
The salty breeze from the ocean cooled us as we walked along the shore.
Opening the window let a refreshing breeze into the stuffy room.

Synset Examples

the breeze was cooled by the lake
as he waited he could feel the air on his neck

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gust - a strong, sudden burst of wind

Etymology

Perhaps from Spanish *brisa* (a northeast wind), or Italian *bresa* (cold northeast wind).
