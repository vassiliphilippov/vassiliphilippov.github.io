Victoria Woodhull

1. PROPN

/ vɪkˈtɔːriə ˈwʊdhʌl / (AmE), / vɪkˈtɔːriə ˈwʊdhʌl / (BrE)
(1838–1927) An American activist, publisher, and advocate of women's suffrage, known as the first woman to run for President of the United States in 1872.
U.S. women's rights activist and presidential candidate

Examples

Victoria Woodhull made history as the first woman to campaign for the U.S. presidency in 1872.
Many historians credit Victoria Woodhull with advancing women’s rights and challenging social norms of her era.
The achievements of Victoria Woodhull continue to inspire advocates for gender equality today.
