volleyball

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɑliˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈvɒl.ib.ɔːl / (BrE)
A game in which two teams hit an inflated ball over a high net using their hands, aiming to score points by grounding the ball on the opponent's side.
team sport with ball and net

Examples

The team practiced volleyball every afternoon at the recreation center.
She loves watching beach volleyball matches during the Olympics.
Our school is organizing a volleyball tournament next month.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: beach volleyball - a variation of volleyball played on sand
  • Synonym: basketball - team sport involving shooting a ball into a hoop

Etymology

Formed from 'volley' (to strike and return before hitting the ground) and 'ball' in English, first used in the late 19th century.

4. NOUN

/ ˈvɑliˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈvɒlibɔːl / (BrE)
A game played by two teams who hit an inflated ball over a high net using their hands, with the aim to score points by making the ball land on the opponent's side of the court.
team sport played with an inflated ball and net

Examples

She joined the school's volleyball team in her first year.
We played volleyball on the beach every summer evening.
A standard volleyball match consists of five sets.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: beach volleyball - a variation of volleyball played on sand
  • Synonym: basketball - a game played by two teams trying to throw a ball into the opposing team's basket

Etymology

Formed from 'volley' and 'ball', first used in the United States in the late 19th century.
