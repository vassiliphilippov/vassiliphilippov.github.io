chassis

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃæsi / (AmE), / ˈʃæsi / (BrE)
the base frame of a motor vehicle, onto which the body and other components are mounted
frame of a motor vehicle

Examples

The car's chassis was damaged in the accident, requiring extensive repairs.
The engineer designed a lighter chassis to improve the vehicle's performance.
Before assembly, every chassis undergoes a thorough inspection for structural integrity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frame - the supporting structure of a vehicle or other object

Etymology

From French 'châssis', meaning 'frame'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæsi / (AmE), / ˈʃæsi / (BrE)
a metal frame that supports the circuit components in an electronic device
frame for electronic components

Examples

The radio was repaired by removing the chassis and soldering new wires onto the circuit board.
Dust had accumulated inside the chassis of the old television set.
The technician slid the chassis out of the amplifier to check for damaged components.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frame - a rigid structure that supports or encloses something

Etymology

From French châssis (frame), from châsse (frame).
