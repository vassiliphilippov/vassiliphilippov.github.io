pirouette

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪr.uˈɛt / (AmE), / ˌpɪə.ruˈɛt / (BrE)
A rapid spinning movement performed on one foot, especially in ballet.
rapid spin in ballet

Examples

The dancer executed a flawless pirouette in the center of the stage.
She lost her balance during the second pirouette and had to steady herself.
Mastering the pirouette is a major milestone for aspiring ballet students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spin - a quick turning movement around a central point

Etymology

From French 'pirouette', meaning 'to spin or whirl', from 'pirouetter'.

2. VERB

/ ˌpɪr.uˈɛt / (AmE), / ˌpɪr.uˈɛt / (BrE)
to perform a full turn on the toe or ball of one foot, especially in ballet or dance.
turn on one foot in dance

Examples

The ballerina pirouetted gracefully across the stage.
He pirouettes with remarkable balance during the performance.
As the music swelled, she pirouetted in the spotlight, drawing applause.

Etymology

French, from 'pirouetter' (to whirl, spin).
