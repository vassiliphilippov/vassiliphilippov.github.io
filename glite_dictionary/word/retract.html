to retract

1. VERB

/ rɪˈtrækt / (AmE), / rɪˈtrækt / (BrE)
(of a surgical instrument) to hold open the edges of a wound or an organ during a medical procedure.
hold open wound edges (medical)
medical

Examples

The surgeon used a tool to retract the skin around the incision site.
The assistant carefully retracted the tissue to give the doctor a clear view.
Medical students learn how to retract organs during complex procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hold open - to keep something open

Etymology

From Latin retractare (to draw back).

2. VERB

/ rɪˈtrækt / (AmE), / rɪˈtrækt / (BrE)
to pull something back or inward into a position so that it is hidden or no longer extended
pull back or inward

Examples

The cat instinctively retracted its claws when it was relaxed.
Modern airplanes retract their wheels after takeoff for better aerodynamics.
He watched as the tiny robot arm slowly retracted into its compartment.

Synset Examples

The pilot drew in the landing gear
The cat retracted his claws

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to extend - to make something larger or longer; to stretch out
  • Synonym: to withdraw - to remove or take something back

Etymology

From Latin retractus, past participle of retrahere (to draw back).

3. VERB

/ rɪˈtrækt / (AmE), / rɪˈtrakt / (BrE)
to move or pull back, especially away from something unpleasant, frightening, or undesirable
pull back from something unpleasant or fearsome

Examples

She instinctively retracted her hand from the hot stove.
The cat retracted its claws when it saw the child approach.
He retracted in fear when the snake lunged at him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to withdraw - to move away or remove oneself from a place, situation, or object
  • Antonym: advance - to move forward towards something

Etymology

From Latin 'retractus', past participle of 'retrahere' (to draw back).

4. VERB

/ rɪˈtrækt / (AmE), / rɪˈtrakt / (BrE)
to formally withdraw or disavow a statement, belief, or commitment, especially under pressure or official demand
formally withdraw a statement or belief

Examples

After facing public criticism, the politician decided to retract his earlier comments.
When confronted with new evidence, she had no choice but to retract her accusation.
He was pressured by his superiors to retract his statement about the company's practices.

Synset Examples

He retracted his earlier statements about his religion
She abjured her beliefs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recant - formally withdraw a previous statement or belief
  • Synonym: to abjure - solemnly renounce a belief, cause, or claim
  • Antonym: to affirm - to state something as true firmly and publicly

Etymology

From Latin retractare (draw back, revoke, reconsider), from re- (back) + tractare (to handle, manage).
