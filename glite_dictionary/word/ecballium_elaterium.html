Ecballium elaterium

1. NOUN

/ ɛkˈbæliəm iːˈleɪtɪriəm / (AmE), / ɛkˈbælɪəm iːˈlætɪrɪəm / (BrE)
A Mediterranean plant species in the gourd family, known for its oblong fruit that forcefully ejects seeds and juice when mature.
Mediterranean plant with explosive fruit

Examples

Botanists are fascinated by how the Ecballium elaterium disperses its seeds through explosive force.
The Ecballium elaterium is sometimes called the 'squirting cucumber' due to its remarkable reproduction method.
In Mediterranean regions, the wild Ecballium elaterium grows abundantly along roadsides.

Etymology

From Greek ekballein (to throw out), referring to the fruit's way of expelling seeds.
