demoralized

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈmɔr.əˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒr.ə.laɪzd / (BrE)
having lost confidence, hope, or spirit; discouraged or disheartened.
having lost hope or enthusiasm

Examples

After weeks of unsuccessful job applications, she felt completely demoralized.
The team's demoralized attitude was evident after their crushing defeat.
Many workers became demoralized during the long economic downturn.

Synset Examples

desperate demoralized people looking for work
felt discouraged by the magnitude of the problem
the disheartened instructor tried vainly to arouse their interest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disheartened - having lost confidence or determination
  • Antonym: encouraged - having more confidence or hope

Etymology

From the verb 'demoralize', formed from French 'démoraliser', from 'dé-' (expressing removal) + 'moraliser' (to moralize).
