Sphecidae

1. NOUN

/ sfɛˈsɪdiː / (AmE), / sfɛˈsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of wasps that includes mud daubers and some digger wasps, known for their solitary nesting habits.
family of solitary wasps

Examples

Sphecidae are commonly known as mud daubers due to their habit of building mud nests.
Entomologists study Sphecidae to understand their solitary behaviors and ecological role.
Several species within the Sphecidae family prey on spiders to feed their larvae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wasp - a stinging insect related to bees and ants
  • Degree Higher: Vespidae - family of social wasps and hornets

Etymology

From New Latin 'Sphecidae', based on 'Sphex', type genus of the family, from Greek 'sphex' (wasp).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsfɛsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈsfiːsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of wasps known as mud daubers and digger wasps, characterized by their solitary nesting habits and elongated bodies
family of wasps including mud daubers

Examples

Scientists observed several Sphecidae species constructing mud nests in the laboratory.
The Sphecidae are important pollinators and natural pest controllers in many ecosystems.
Some Sphecidae wasps are commonly known as mud daubers due to their unique nest-building behavior.

Etymology

From New Latin Sphecidae, based on the genus Sphex, from Greek sphēks (wasp).
