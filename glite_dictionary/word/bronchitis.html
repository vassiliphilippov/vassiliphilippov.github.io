bronchitis

1. NOUN

/ brɑnˈkaɪtɪs / (AmE), / brɒŋˈkaɪtɪs / (BrE)
Inflammation of the membranes lining the bronchial tubes, often causing coughing and difficulty breathing.
inflammation of bronchial tubes
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with bronchitis after hearing his persistent cough.
She missed a week of work because her bronchitis made it hard to breathe.
Chronic bronchitis is common among smokers and can cause serious health issues.

Etymology

From Greek bronkhia (bronchial tubes) + -itis (inflammation).
