acapnial

1. ADJ

/ əˈkæpniəl / (AmE), / əˈkapnɪəl / (BrE)
relating to or demonstrating acapnia; characterized by an abnormally low level of carbon dioxide in the blood or tissues.
relating to low carbon dioxide (acapnia)
medical

Examples

The patient displayed acapnial symptoms following prolonged hyperventilation.
Certain medical conditions can create an acapnial environment in the body.
Doctors noted an acapnial state after the use of mechanical ventilation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acapnia - a state of reduced carbon dioxide in the blood

Etymology

From acapnia (‘absence of carbon dioxide’) + -al (adjective suffix).
