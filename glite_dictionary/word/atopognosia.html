atopognosia

1. NOUN

/ əˌtoʊpɑɡˈnoʊziə / (AmE), / əˌtɒpɒɡˈnəʊziə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which a person is unable to identify the location of a touch stimulus on their body.
inability to locate a point of touch
medical

Examples

The neurologist diagnosed the patient with atopognosia after several sensory tests.
Atopognosia can occur following damage to specific areas of the brain.
People with atopognosia may find it difficult to identify where they have been touched.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: topognosia - the ability to recognize the location of a tactile stimulus

Etymology

From Greek a- (not) + topos (place) + gnōsis (knowledge).
