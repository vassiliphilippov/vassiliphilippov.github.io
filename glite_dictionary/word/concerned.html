concerned

1. ADJ

/ kənˈsɝnd / (AmE), / kənˈsɜːnd / (BrE)
involved in something, especially in a way that suggests culpability or responsibility for wrongdoing
involved, especially in wrongdoing

Examples

All those concerned in the fraud have been called for questioning.
The police arrested several men concerned with the drug operation.
Anyone concerned in the incident will be held accountable.

Synset Examples

all those concerned in the bribery case have been identified
named three officials implicated in the plot
an innocent person implicated by circumstances in a crime

Related Terms

  • Synonym: implicated - shown or believed to be involved in something, especially wrongdoing
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01520429-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ kənˈsɝnd / (AmE), / kənˈsɜːnd / (BrE)
worried or anxious about something; showing care or solicitude for someone or something.
worried or showing care

Examples

The teacher was concerned about her student's declining grades.
Her parents looked concerned when she came home late.
Many citizens are concerned about the rising cost of living.

Synset Examples

concerned parents of youthful offenders
was concerned about the future
we feel concerned about accomplishing the task at hand
greatly concerned not to disappoint a small child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxious - worried or uneasy about something with an uncertain outcome
  • Antonym: unconcerned - not worried or not interested

Etymology

From the past participle of 'concern,' which comes from Middle French concerner, from Latin concernere (to sift together, mix up).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00545823-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ kənˈsɝːnd / (AmE), / kənˈsɜːnd / (BrE)
involved in, affected by, or having a connection with something
involved or affected by something

Examples

Please forward this information to all concerned parties as soon as possible.
He is one of the scientists concerned in the environmental study.
Everyone concerned with the project will attend the meeting tomorrow.

Synset Examples

a memorandum to those concerned
an enterprise in which three men are concerned
factors concerned in the rise and fall of epidemics
the interested parties met to discuss the business

Related Terms

  • Synonym: involved - having a part in something; participating
  • Antonym: unconcerned - not involved; not interested or affected

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'concern', from Middle French concerner, from Latin concernere.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01519775-s
Synset POS: s
