Aramus guarauna

1. NOUN

/ əˈræməs ɡwɑːˈraʊnə / (AmE), / əˈræməs ɡwɑːˈraʊnə / (BrE)
the limpkin; a large wading bird native to wetlands and marshes of Central and South America, known for its distinctive loud call
species of wading bird (the limpkin)

Examples

The Aramus guarauna is often seen foraging for snails in wetland habitats across Central and South America.
Unlike most wading birds, the Aramus guarauna is known for its haunting, repetitive call.
Researchers tracked the migration patterns of Aramus guarauna populations during the dry season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limpkin - common English name for Aramus guarauna

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name Aramus and specific epithet guarauna, likely from Tupi language.
