Jenner

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒɛnər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnə / (BrE)
Edward Jenner (1749–1823), English physician who pioneered the practice of vaccination by using cowpox to immunize against smallpox.
English physician who pioneered vaccination

Examples

Edward Jenner is often called the father of immunology for his discovery of the smallpox vaccine.
Many historians credit Jenner with saving countless lives through his work on vaccination.
The pioneering efforts of Jenner led to the eventual eradication of smallpox.
