Atherurus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæθəˈrʊrəs / (AmE), / ˌaθəˈrʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of the Hystricidae family, comprising the brush-tailed porcupines found in parts of Asia and Africa.
genus of brush-tailed porcupines

Examples

The Atherurus genus includes several species known for their distinctive brush-tipped tails.
Brush-tailed porcupines of the genus Atherurus are found in forests across Asia and parts of Africa.
Scientists studied the unique defense mechanisms of Atherurus during their field research.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Hystricidae - the family of Old World porcupines

Etymology

From Greek 'athēr' (brush, ear of corn) + 'oura' (tail), referring to the brush-like tail.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæθəˈrʊrəs / (AmE), / ˌæθəˈrʊərəs / (BrE)
a genus of rodents in the family Hystricidae, known as brush-tailed porcupines, found in Africa and Southeast Asia
genus of brush-tailed porcupines

Examples

The brush-tailed porcupines belong to the genus Atherurus.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Atherurus in the rainforest.
The distribution of Atherurus includes both African and Southeast Asian regions.

Etymology

From Greek athēra (brush, spike) + oura (tail).
