surcoat

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜːrˌkoʊt / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkəʊt / (BrE)
A loose outer coat, typically made of rich material, worn over armor or a tunic in the Middle Ages.
loose outer coat (historical garment)

Examples

The knight's surcoat was emblazoned with his family crest.
He donned a velvet surcoat over his armor before entering the tournament.
Medieval soldiers often wore a brightly colored surcoat to display their allegiance.

Etymology

From Old French 'surcote', from 'sur' (over) + 'cote' (coat).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːˌkoʊt / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.kəʊt / (BrE)
A loose outer garment, typically sleeveless, worn over armor by knights in the Middle Ages.
tunic worn over a knight's armor

Examples

The knight wore a brightly colored surcoat emblazoned with his family crest.
Beneath the battered surcoat, his chainmail glinted in the sunlight.
Her research focused on the symbolism of the designs often displayed on a surcoat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tabard - a short surcoat worn by men in the Middle Ages

Etymology

From Old French *surcote*, from *sur* (over) + *cote* (coat).
