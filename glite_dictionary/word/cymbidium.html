Cymbidium

1. NOUN

/ sɪmˈbɪdiəm / (AmE), / sɪmˈbɪdiəm / (BrE)
A genus of tropical epiphytic or terrestrial orchids, native to Asia and Australia, known for their long-lasting flowers and popularity in cultivation.
a genus of popular tropical orchids

Examples

The greenhouse had several varieties of Cymbidium on display.
Many gardeners choose Cymbidium orchids for their resilience and beauty.
She presented a bouquet featuring vibrant Cymbidium blooms at the flower show.

Etymology

From Latin 'Cymbidium', from Greek 'kymbídion' (a small cup), referring to the shape of the flower's lip.
