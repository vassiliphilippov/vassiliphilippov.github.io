coaxal

1. ADJ

/ koʊˈæksəl / (AmE), / kəʊˈæksəl / (BrE)
(of two or more objects) having a common axis.
sharing a common axis

Examples

The two cylinders in the machine are coaxal, allowing them to rotate together smoothly.
When mounted properly, the optical components are perfectly coaxal for optimal performance.
The experiment required the use of coaxal tubes to ensure precision in measurement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coaxial - (of objects) sharing a common axis

Etymology

From Latin com- (together) + axis (axle), modeled after 'coaxial.'
