LF

1. NOUN

/ ɛl ɛf / (AmE), / ɛl ɛf / (BrE)
an abbreviation for 'low frequency,' referring to radio frequencies in the range of 30 to 300 kilohertz
abbreviation for low frequency (30–300 kHz)

Examples

The navy uses the LF band for some types of underwater communication.
Signals in the LF range are able to travel great distances by ground wave propagation.
LF transmissions are less affected by atmospheric noise than higher frequency bands.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: HF - high frequency (3 to 30 megahertz)
  • Degree Lower: VLF - very low frequency (3 to 30 kilohertz)

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'low frequency'.
