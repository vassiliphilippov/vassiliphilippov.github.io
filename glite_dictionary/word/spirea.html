spirea

1. NOUN

/ spaɪˈriː.ə / (AmE), / spaɪˈrɪə / (BrE)
A shrub, especially of the genus Spiraea, with clusters of small flowers, often cultivated for ornamental use in gardens.
flowering ornamental shrub

Examples

The gardener planted a row of spirea along the path to add spring color.
White spirea blossoms covered the bush in delicate clusters each April.
Many landscapers choose spirea for its hardiness and showy flowers.

Etymology

From New Latin Spiraea, from Greek speiraia, from speira (coil), referring to the coiled inflorescence.

2. NOUN

/ spaɪˈriːə / (AmE), / spaɪˈriːə / (BrE)
a shrub of the genus Spiraea, having sprays of small white or pink flowers, commonly cultivated for ornamental purposes
a flowering shrub of the genus Spiraea

Examples

The spirea in our garden blooms with beautiful white flowers every spring.
She planted several spirea along the fence to create a colorful hedge.
After pruning the old branches, the spirea produced even more vibrant blossoms.

Etymology

From New Latin Spiraea, from Greek speiraia (a plant entwined in wreaths), from speira (coil, spiral).
