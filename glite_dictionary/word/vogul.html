Vogul

1. NOUN

/ ˈvoʊɡəl / (AmE), / ˈvəʊɡʊl / (BrE)
A member of a nomadic people from the northern Ural Mountains, now more commonly known as the Mansi.
member of a nomadic people of the northern Urals

Examples

Anthropologists studied the traditional lifestyle of the Vogul in the northern Urals.
The Vogul were skilled reindeer herders adapting to harsh Arctic environments.
In history books, the Vogul are now often referred to as Mansi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mansi - a member of an indigenous people of the Urals, also called Vogul

Etymology

From Russian 'Vogul', a name for the Mansi people.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvoʊɡəl / (AmE), / ˈvəʊɡʊl / (BrE)
the Ugric language, related to Hungarian, traditionally spoken by the Mansi people of western Siberia.
the Ugric language of the Mansi people

Examples

The Vogul language is primarily spoken in western Siberia.
Linguists have classified Vogul as part of the Ugric branch of the Uralic language family.
Most Vogul speakers today use the term 'Mansi' for their language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mansi - the indigenous people of western Siberia, also the language's modern name

Etymology

From Russian 'Vogul', an exonym for the Mansi people and their language.
