Aztec

1. NOUN

/ ˈæzˌtɛk / (AmE), / ˈaz.tɛk / (BrE)
a member of the indigenous Nahuatl-speaking people who established a powerful empire in central Mexico before the Spanish conquest
indigenous people of central Mexico, empire builders

Examples

The Aztec civilization was renowned for its complex social structure and impressive architectural achievements.
Historians have studied the daily life and rituals of the Aztec people for centuries.
Many of the Aztec temples and monuments still attract visitors from around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Maya - a member of the Mesoamerican people whose civilization flourished in Mexico and Central America before the Spanish conquest
  • Synonym: Inca - a member of the South American people who built an empire in Peru before the Spanish conquest

Etymology

From Nahuatl *Aztecatl*, meaning 'person from Aztlan', a mythical place of origin.
