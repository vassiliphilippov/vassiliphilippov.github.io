convenience

1. NOUN

/ kənˈvinjəns / (AmE), / kənˈviːniəns / (BrE)
a device or control that is very useful for a particular job
useful device or control

Examples

The hotel room is equipped with every modern convenience for travelers.
A dishwasher is a kitchen convenience that saves a lot of time and effort.
Many cars come with added conveniences like heated seats and backup cameras.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appliance - a device or piece of equipment designed for a particular function

Etymology

From Latin 'convenientia' (agreement, suitability).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈvinjəns / (AmE), / kənˈviːniəns / (BrE)
the quality of being useful, easy, or suitable for a particular purpose
the quality of being useful or easy

Examples

For your convenience, the bank is open late on Fridays.
Online shopping offers great convenience for busy people.
They added a bathroom on the first floor for added convenience.

Synset Examples

they offered the convenience of an installment plan

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inconvenience - the quality of being troublesome or difficult

Etymology

From Latin 'convenientia' (agreement, suitableness), from 'conveniens' (suitable).

3. NOUN

/ kənˈvinjəns / (AmE), / kənˈviːniəns / (BrE)
The quality or state of being suitable, easy, or useful for a particular purpose; suitability or usefulness.
suitability or usefulness

Examples

The hotel was chosen for its convenience to the conference center.
Please arrange the meeting at your earliest convenience.
He likes to have everything within easy convenience of his desk.

Synset Examples

chairs arranged for his own convenience

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inconvenience - the state of being inconvenient or causing difficulty

Etymology

From Latin convenientia (agreement, suitability), from convenire (to come together, be suitable).

4. NOUN

/ kənˈviːniəns / (AmE), / kənˈviːniəns / (BrE)
a public toilet; a room or building with a toilet available for use by the public
public toilet or restroom

Examples

The hotel provided a convenience for travelers near the lobby.
She asked a passerby for directions to the nearest public convenience.
Public conveniences are clearly signposted throughout the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restroom - a room with toilet facilities, especially in a public place (US)
  • Synonym: lavatory - a room equipped with a toilet and sink for washing
  • Synonym: toilet - a sanitary fixture for defecation and urination

Etymology

From Latin 'convenientia' (agreement, suitability), later developed in English to include facilities that provide ease or comfort.
