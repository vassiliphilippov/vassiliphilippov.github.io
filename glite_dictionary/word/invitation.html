invitation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.vɪˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.vɪˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a tempting or enticing quality that attracts someone to do something, especially something potentially unwise
enticing quality; allurement

Examples

Her reckless attitude was an invitation for disaster.
Leaving the door unlocked is an invitation to thieves.
His easygoing smile was an invitation to approach and talk.

Synset Examples

she was an invitation to trouble

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allurement - the power to entice or attract through personal charm
  • Synonym: temptation - the desire to do something, especially something wrong or unwise

Etymology

From Latin 'invitatio', from 'invitare' (to invite).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.vɪˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.vɪˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A spoken or written request to someone to attend an event or participate in an activity.
spoken or written request to attend or join

Examples

We received a formal invitation to the company's annual dinner.
She sent out dozens of invitations to her birthday party.
I'm considering accepting his invitation to visit Paris in the spring.

Synset Examples

an invitation to lunch
she threw the invitation away

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summons - an official request to be present, usually legal
  • Synonym: request - an act of asking politely or formally for something
  • Antonym: refusal - the act of declining an offer or request

Etymology

From Latin 'invitatio', from 'invitare' (to invite).
