pathology

1. NOUN

/ pəˈθɑlədʒi / (AmE), / pəˈθɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of diseases and their causes, processes, development, and consequences; also, any deviation from a healthy or normal condition
study of disease or abnormal condition
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the tumor represents a serious pathology requiring immediate treatment.
The study of pathology is essential for understanding how illnesses disrupt normal bodily functions.
Chronic stress can sometimes cause a pathology in otherwise healthy organs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disease - an abnormal condition affecting the body of an organism

Etymology

From Greek pathologia, from pathos (suffering) + -logia (study of).

2. NOUN

/ pəˈθɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / pəˈθɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of medical science that deals with the causes, nature, and effects of diseases
medical study of diseases
medical

Examples

She decided to specialize in pathology after finishing medical school.
Recent advances in pathology have improved our understanding of how cancer develops.
The hospital's pathology department analyzes tissue samples to diagnose disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: histology - the study of the microscopic structure of tissues
  • Synonym: etiology - the study of the causes of diseases
  • Antonym: physiology - the branch of biology that deals with the normal functions of living organisms and their parts

Etymology

From Greek 'pathologia' (study of suffering), from 'pathos' (suffering) + '-logia' (study).
