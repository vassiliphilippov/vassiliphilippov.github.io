to frighten

1. VERB

/ ˈfraɪtən / (AmE), / ˈfraɪtn / (BrE)
to make someone feel afraid; to cause fear in someone
to make someone feel afraid

Examples

The loud thunder frightened the children hiding under the table.
He wears a costume to frighten people on Halloween.
Sudden noises easily frighten my dog.

Synset Examples

The stranger who hangs around the building frightens me
Ghosts could never affright her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scare - to make someone feel sudden fear
  • Degree Higher: to terrify - to fill with intense fear
  • Antonym: calm - to make someone feel less nervous or afraid

Etymology

From Middle English *frightnen*, from *fright* (fear) + *-en* (verb suffix).
