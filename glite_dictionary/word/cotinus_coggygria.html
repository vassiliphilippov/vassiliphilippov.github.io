Cotinus coggygria

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈtaɪnəs ˌkoʊɡiˈɡriːə / (AmE), / kəʊˈtaɪnəs ˌkɒɡɪˈɡriːə / (BrE)
A Eurasian shrub, also known as the smoke tree, with large plumes of yellowish feathery flowers that resemble puffs of smoke.
Eurasian shrub with plume-like flowers

Examples

The Cotinus coggygria in our garden blooms with clouds of smoky flowers every summer.
Gardeners often choose Cotinus coggygria for its decorative foliage and unusual blossoms.
In botanical gardens, Cotinus coggygria is admired for both its autumn colors and its feathery flower plumes.

Etymology

Genus name from Latin; species name from Greek, meaning 'dye-producing.'
