Festuca ovina

1. NOUN

/ fɛˈstjuːkə oʊˈvaɪnə / (AmE), / fɛˈstjuːkə əʊˈviːnə / (BrE)
A species of grass, also known as sheep's fescue, cultivated for sheep pasture in upland areas or used as a lawn grass.
a grass species used for pasture or lawns

Examples

Festuca ovina is frequently chosen for lawns in dry, upland gardens due to its hardiness.
Sheep graze on fields of Festuca ovina in the Scottish Highlands throughout the summer.
Many golf courses in northern Europe feature Festuca ovina as the primary turf grass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheep's fescue - Common name for Festuca ovina

Etymology

From Latin: 'festuca' (straw or blade of grass) and 'ovina' (of sheep).
