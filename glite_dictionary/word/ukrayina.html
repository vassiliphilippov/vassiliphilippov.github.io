Ukrayina

1. PROPN

/ uːˈkraɪnə / (AmE), / juːˈkraɪnə / (BrE)
The name for Ukraine in Ukrainian; a republic in southeastern Europe, once part of the Soviet Union, and the center of the original Russian state dating back to the ninth century.
Ukraine (name in Ukrainian); country in southeastern Europe

Examples

She traveled to Ukrayina to visit her relatives in Kyiv.
Many diplomats are meeting in Ukrayina to discuss European security issues.
The history of Ukrayina dates back to the ninth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ukraine - A country in southeastern Europe

Etymology

Transliteration of the Ukrainian name Україна (Ukrayina).
