hediondilla

1. NOUN

/ ˌheɪdiɔnˈdiːjə / (AmE), / ˌheɪdɪɒnˈdɪlə / (BrE)
A desert shrub native to the southwestern United States and New Mexico, known for its persistent resinous aromatic foliage and small yellow flowers.
desert shrub of southwestern US

Examples

The hediondilla thrives in arid environments and is easily recognized by its yellow flowers.
Botanists study the hediondilla for its aromatic leaves and adaptability to desert climates.
On our hike, we spotted a cluster of hediondilla near the rocky outcroppings.

Etymology

From Spanish 'hediondilla', diminutive of 'hedionda' (fetid), referring to the plant's strong scent.
