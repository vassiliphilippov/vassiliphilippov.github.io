Cypripedium californicum

1. NOUN

/ saɪ.prəˈpiː.di.əm ˌkæl.ɪˈfɔːr.nɪ.kəm / (AmE), / sɪ.prɪˈpiː.dɪ.əm ˌkæl.ɪˈfɔː.nɪ.kəm / (BrE)
A species of lady's slipper orchid native to southwestern Oregon and northern California, often with yellow-green flowers and white pouches, found near streams and seeps.
a species of lady's slipper orchid

Examples

The rare Cypripedium californicum blooms each spring along the shaded streams in northern California.
Botanists trekked through the forest to document a new population of Cypripedium californicum.
Conservationists are working to preserve the wetland habitats that sustain the Cypripedium californicum.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name 'Cypripedium' and the species epithet 'californicum,' meaning 'of California.'
