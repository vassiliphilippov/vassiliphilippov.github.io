starchy

1. ADJ

/ ˈstɑrtʃi / (AmE), / ˈstɑːtʃi / (BrE)
containing or consisting of starch; rich in starch.
containing starch

Examples

Potatoes and bread are examples of starchy foods.
He avoids starchy meals to help control his weight.
The dietitian suggested reducing starchy snacks in the evening.

Synset Examples

starchy foods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbohydrate - organic compounds found in foods and living tissues, including sugars, starch, and cellulose
  • Degree Lower: sugary - containing or resembling sugar; sweet
  • Antonym: fiber-rich - containing a high amount of dietary fiber

Etymology

From 'starch' + -y, denoting having the qualities of starch.

2. ADJ

/ ˈstɑːrtʃi / (AmE), / ˈstɑːtʃi / (BrE)
very formal, stiff, and not relaxed in manner or style
rigidly formal, not relaxed

Examples

Her starchy demeanor made the informal meeting feel uncomfortable for everyone.
The boss's starchy reply to the joke left the whole room silent.
He wrote a starchy letter full of formalities and old-fashioned phrases.

Synset Examples

a starchy manner
the letter was stiff and formal
his prose has a buckram quality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stiff - not relaxed or friendly in manner
  • Synonym: formal - following accepted rules or conventions strictly
  • Antonym: relaxed - easy-going and informal in manner

Etymology

From 'starch' (substance used to stiffen fabric), originally referring to stiffly starched clothing.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01047623-s
Synset POS: s
