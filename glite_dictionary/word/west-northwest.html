west-northwest

1. NOUN

/ wɛst nɔrθˈwɛst / (AmE), / wɛst nɔːθˈwɛst / (BrE)
The direction or compass point halfway between west and northwest, at an angle of 292.5°.
compass point between west and northwest

Examples

The storm was moving toward the west-northwest at twenty miles per hour.
The hiker took a bearing of west-northwest to reach the next campsite.
The ship altered its course to west-northwest to avoid the shallow waters.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: west - the direction opposite to east; one of the four cardinal points
  • Degree Higher: northwest - the direction between north and west; one of the intercardinal points

Etymology

Formed from 'west' and 'northwest', following standard compass naming conventions.
