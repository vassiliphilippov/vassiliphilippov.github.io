chum

1. NOUN

/ tʃʌm / (AmE), / tʃʌm / (BrE)
a large Pacific salmon (Oncorhynchus keta) with small spots on its back, important as a food fish
a species of Pacific salmon

Examples

Fishermen caught several chum during the annual salmon run.
The chum is prized for its firm flesh and mild flavor.
Many canneries depend on the chum for their supply of fish.

Etymology

From Chinook Jargon *tzum*, meaning 'spotted' or 'marked.'

2. NOUN

/ tʃʌm / (AmE), / tʃʌm / (BrE)
bait made of chopped fish and fish oils, thrown into the water to attract fish.
fish bait of chopped fish and oils

Examples

The fisherman prepared a bucket of chum to lure the sharks closer to the boat.
They tossed chum overboard, hoping to start a feeding frenzy.
Many anglers swear by using fresh chum to improve their catch rates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bait - food used to attract fish or other animals

Etymology

Possibly from Scottish chum (food), of obscure origin.

3. NOUN

/ tʃʌm / (AmE), / tʃʌm / (BrE)
a close friend; a companion, especially one with whom one spends a lot of time
close friend; companion

Examples

He went fishing with his old chum from school last weekend.
Sarah and her chum spent hours talking at the café.
It's hard to find a loyal chum these days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pal - a close friend or companion
  • Synonym: buddy - a close friend; companion
  • Antonym: enemy - a person who is actively opposed or hostile

Etymology

Probably from English dialect 'chum' meaning 'roommate', likely short for 'chambermate' (roommate).
