professedly

1. ADV

/ prəˈfɛsɪdli / (AmE), / prəˈfɛsɪdli / (BrE)
by open declaration; as stated or claimed openly, though not necessarily as true
openly declared; stated as

Examples

He is professedly against the plan, yet his actions suggest otherwise.
The group is professedly nonpartisan, though some doubt their neutrality.
She remained professedly optimistic throughout the project, inspiring her team.

Synset Examples

their policy has been avowedly Marxist
Susan Smith was professedly guilty of the murders

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avowedly - by open admission or declaration

Etymology

From professed + -ly, ultimately from Latin professus (declared).

2. ADV

/ prəˈfɛsɪdli / (AmE), / prəˈfɛsɪdli / (BrE)
by declaration, often in a way that is not genuine or is intended to deceive; ostensibly or purportedly.
ostensibly; by declaration (often insincerely)

Examples

He is professedly uninterested in politics, yet he often comments on government policies.
The charity was professedly set up to help the poor, but most of the funds were misused.
She is professedly neutral, but her actions show clear favoritism.

Synset Examples

is only professedly poor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostensibly - in a way that appears or claims to be one thing when it is really something else

Etymology

From 'profess' (to declare or claim) + '-edly' (adverbial suffix).

Source: WordNet
