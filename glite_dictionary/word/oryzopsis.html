Oryzopsis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːrɪˈzɑːpsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɒrɪˈzɒpsɪs / (BrE)
A genus of grasses commonly known as rice grass, native to North America and parts of Asia, recognized for their rice-like seeds.
genus of grasses called rice grass

Examples

Several species of Oryzopsis are found in prairies and open woodlands across North America.
The seeds of Oryzopsis have been used as a food source by indigenous peoples.
Botanists often distinguish Oryzopsis from true rice by its floral characteristics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ricegrass - common name for several grasses with rice-like seeds

Etymology

From Greek oryza (rice) + -opsis (appearance), meaning 'having the appearance of rice'.
