Ligustrum japonicum

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈɡʌstrəm dʒəˈpoʊnɪkəm / (AmE), / lɪˈɡʌstrəm dʒæˈpɒnɪkəm / (BrE)
An evergreen shrub native to Japan and Korea, with small dark leaves and flowers in loose clusters, commonly grown as an ornamental plant and known as Japanese privet.
evergreen shrub native to Japan and Korea

Examples

The Ligustrum japonicum in her garden bloomed beautifully throughout the summer.
Many landscapers recommend Ligustrum japonicum for creating dense, evergreen hedges.
We replaced the old fence with a row of Ligustrum japonicum to enhance the backyard's privacy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: privet - any shrub of the genus Ligustrum
  • Degree Higher: Ligustrum sinense - Chinese privet, a related but larger species

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Ligustrum' meaning privet and 'japonicum' referring to Japan.
