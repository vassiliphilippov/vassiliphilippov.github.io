defalcation

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌfælˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːfælˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of fraudulently taking or misappropriating money or property entrusted to one's care, especially in a professional or fiduciary capacity.
fraudulent misappropriation of entrusted funds
legal

Examples

The accountant was arrested for defalcation after misusing company funds for personal gain.
Years of unnoticed defalcation eventually led to the nonprofit's financial collapse.
Strict auditing procedures are designed to prevent defalcation by employees handling sensitive assets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embezzlement - theft or misappropriation of funds placed in one's trust

Etymology

From Latin defalcatio (diminution), from defalcare (to deduct), from de- (off) + falx (sickle).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdiːfælˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːfælˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
an amount of money that has been misappropriated or embezzled, especially by someone trusted with its charge
sum of money misappropriated
legal

Examples

The audit revealed a substantial defalcation in the company's accounts.
The treasurer was accused of defalcation after several thousand dollars went missing.
Insurance covered the bank's losses due to defalcation by an employee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embezzlement - the act of dishonestly taking money entrusted to one's care
  • Antonym: restitution - the act of restoring something lost or stolen to its proper owner

Etymology

From Latin defalcatio (a subtraction), from defalcare (to deduct).
