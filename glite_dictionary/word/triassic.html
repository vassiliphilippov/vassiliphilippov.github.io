Triassic

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈæsɪk / (AmE), / traɪˈæsɪk / (BrE)
the period of geological time from about 252 to 201 million years ago, marked by the emergence of dinosaurs, marine reptiles, and significant volcanic activity
a geological period from 252 to 201 million years ago

Examples

The Triassic period saw the first appearance of dinosaurs on Earth.
Fossils from the Triassic provide clues about early reptiles.
The separation of continents began during the Triassic era.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Jurassic - the geological period following the Triassic, notable for large dinosaurs
  • Degree Lower: Permian - the geological period preceding the Triassic

Etymology

From Latin 'trias' (triad, three) + '-ic', referring to the division of rocks into three layers in Germany where the system was first studied.

2. ADJ

/ traɪˈæs.ɪk / (AmE), / traɪˈæs.ɪk / (BrE)
of or relating to the first period of the Mesozoic era, spanning from approximately 251 to 199 million years ago
relating to the first period of the Mesozoic era

Examples

The fossils found in this layer date back to the Triassic period.
Some of the earliest dinosaurs first appeared during the Triassic era.
Geologists have discovered a rich deposit of Triassic rocks in that region.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Jurassic - relating to the period following the Triassic in the Mesozoic era
  • Degree Lower: Permian - relating to the geologic period immediately before the Triassic

Etymology

From Latin 'trias' (triad, three) + '-ic'; so called because the rocks of this period were typically in three layers.
