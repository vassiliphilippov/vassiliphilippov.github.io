Toxotes jaculatrix

1. NOUN

/ toʊˈxoʊtiːz ˌdʒækjʊˈleɪtrɪks / (AmE), / tɒkˈsəʊtiːz ˌdʒækjʊˈleɪtrɪks / (BrE)
a species of small freshwater fish, also known as the banded archerfish, that catches insects by squirting water at them to knock them into the water; native to Southeast Asia and Australia
species of archerfish known for shooting insects with water

Examples

The Toxotes jaculatrix can skillfully knock flying insects into the water with a squirt of water from its mouth.
Aquarists often keep Toxotes jaculatrix because of its remarkable hunting behavior.
Researchers have studied the vision and accuracy of Toxotes jaculatrix in laboratory settings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archerfish - fish of the genus Toxotes known for shooting jets of water to catch insects

Etymology

From Latin: Toxotes (meaning 'archer') and jaculatrix (feminine form of 'throwing/darting'), reflecting its method of attacking prey.
