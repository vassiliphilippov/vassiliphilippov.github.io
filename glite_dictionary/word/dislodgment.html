dislodgment

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈlɑːdʒmənt / (AmE), / dɪsˈlɒdʒmənt / (BrE)
the act of forcing someone or something out of a place or position, especially one of advantage.
forced removal from a place or position

Examples

The dislodgment of the occupying forces was celebrated as a major victory.
Heavy rainfall caused the dislodgment of several large boulders blocking the road.
His sudden dislodgment from power surprised both his allies and opponents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: removal - the act of taking something away or off from the position occupied
  • Synonym: eviction - the act of expelling someone from a property

Etymology

From Old French *deslogier* (to dislodge) plus English suffix *-ment*.
