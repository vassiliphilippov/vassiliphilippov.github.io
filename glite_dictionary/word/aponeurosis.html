aponeurosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæpəˌnʊˈroʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌapənjʊˈrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A sheet-like, fibrous tissue similar to a flattened tendon that attaches muscles to the parts they move, such as bones.
sheet-like tendon connecting muscles to bones
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully separated the aponeurosis from the underlying muscle during the procedure.
A tear in the aponeurosis can lead to weakness in the abdominal wall.
The palm of your hand contains an aponeurosis that supports its structure.

Etymology

From Greek aponeurōsis, from apo- (from) + neuron (sinew, tendon).
