anaclitic

1. ADJ

/ ˌænəˈklɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌanəˈklɪtɪk / (BrE)
Relating to a relationship marked by strong emotional dependence of one person on another, especially as described in psychology and psychoanalysis.
emotionally dependent relationship (psychology)

Examples

The psychologist noted the child's anaclitic attachment to his caregiver.
Some adults may develop anaclitic relationships if their early emotional needs were unmet.
The study explored the effects of anaclitic depression in infants separated from their mothers.

Etymology

From Greek 'anaklīnein' (to lean upon).
