Ophioglossum pendulum

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊfi.oʊˈɡlɑsəm ˈpɛndjələm / (AmE), / ˌəʊfi.əʊˈɡlɒsəm ˈpɛndjʊləm / (BrE)
An epiphytic fern with long, straplike, usually twisted fronds, native to tropical regions of Asia, Polynesia, and America.
a species of epiphytic fern

Examples

The Ophioglossum pendulum was found growing epiphytically on an old tree in the rainforest.
Botanists admire the unique twisted fronds characteristic of Ophioglossum pendulum.
Collectors value Ophioglossum pendulum for its unusual straplike leaves and exotic appearance.

Etymology

From Greek *ophis* (snake) + *glossa* (tongue), referring to the shape of the fronds; species epithet from Latin *pendulus* (hanging).
