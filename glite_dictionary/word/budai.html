Budai

1. PROPN

/ buˈdaɪ / (AmE), / buːˈdaɪ / (BrE)
A popular figure in Chinese folklore, often depicted as a cheerful, fat monk, known as one of the Seven Gods of Happiness in East Asian belief.
Chinese folkloric god of happiness

Examples

Many people place a statue of Budai in their homes to attract happiness and good fortune.
The jovial Budai is often wrongly identified as the Buddha in Western cultures.
Tourists often rub the belly of Budai statues in temples for luck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hotei - Japanese name for Budai, also one of the Seven Lucky Gods

Etymology

From Chinese 布袋 (Bùdài) meaning 'cloth sack', referring to the sack he carried.
