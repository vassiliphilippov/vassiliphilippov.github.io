Cortaderia richardii

1. NOUN

/ kɔr.təˈdɛr.iə rɪˈtʃɑrdi.aɪ / (AmE), / kɔː.təˈdɪə.ri.ə rɪˈtʃɑːd.i.aɪ / (BrE)
A tall grass native to New Zealand, cultivated for its plume-like flowering heads.
tall New Zealand grass with plume-like flowers

Examples

Cortaderia richardii is commonly planted in gardens for its striking, feather-like plumes.
The tall stalks of Cortaderia richardii sway beautifully in the New Zealand wind.
Many landscapers value Cortaderia richardii for its decorative appearance and hardiness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pampas grass - a related tall grass with feathery flower heads, genus Cortaderia

Etymology

From the genus name Cortaderia, coined from Spanish 'cortar' (to cut), referencing the grass's sharp leaves; species named after early botanist Richard.
