Pope Innocent III

1. PROPN

/ poʊp ˌɪnəsənt ˈðri / (AmE), / pəʊp ˌɪnəsənt ˈθriː / (BrE)
(1160–1216) Italian pope from 1198 to 1216, known for initiating the Fourth Crusade and expanding papal influence in European politics.
influential pope (1198–1216), led Fourth Crusade

Examples

Pope Innocent III played a crucial role in shaping medieval European politics and the authority of the papacy.
The Fourth Crusade was launched during the reign of Pope Innocent III.
Many historians regard Pope Innocent III as one of the most powerful and influential popes in history.
