Euphorbia ingens

1. NOUN

/ juːˈfɔrbiə ˈɪndʒənz / (AmE), / juːˈfɔːbɪə ˈɪndʒenz / (BrE)
a small tree native to dry, open regions of southern Africa, with tall, angled branches resembling a candelabra
a southern African tree with candelabra-like branches

Examples

The Euphorbia ingens is often planted in gardens for its dramatic, sculptural appearance.
Some animals avoid the Euphorbia ingens because its milky sap is toxic.
We saw a grove of Euphorbia ingens trees on our safari in South Africa.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name 'Euphorbia' (honoring Euphorbus, Greek physician) and Latin 'ingens' (huge, vast).
