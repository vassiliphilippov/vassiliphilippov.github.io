tumescence

1. NOUN

/ tuˈmɛsəns / (AmE), / tjuːˈmɛsəns / (BrE)
the state of being swollen or engorged, especially due to the accumulation of blood or fluid in tissues
swelling or engorgement in tissues
medical

Examples

The surgeon noted significant tumescence in the affected region after the procedure.
As a result of the allergic reaction, tumescence was observed around the injection site.
In medical imaging, tumescence may indicate fluid accumulation or inflammatory processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tumidity - the quality or state of being swollen
  • Antonym: detumescence - the process of subsiding from a swollen or erect state

Etymology

From Latin tumentia (swelling), from tumescere (to begin to swell).
