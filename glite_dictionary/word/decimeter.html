decimeter

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛsɪˌmiːtər / (AmE), / ˈdɛsɪˌmiːtə / (BrE)
a metric unit of length equal to one tenth of a meter (10 centimeters)
metric unit of length, 0.1 meter

Examples

The insect measured almost one decimeter in length, surprising the biologists.
She drew a line exactly five decimeters long on the chart for the experiment.
A decimeter is rarely used in daily measurements, unlike centimeters and meters.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: centimeter - a metric unit of length equal to one hundredth of a meter
  • Degree Higher: meter - the basic metric unit of length

Etymology

From French décimètre, from Latin decimus (tenth) + mètre (meter).
