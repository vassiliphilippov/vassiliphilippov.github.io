chlorpyrifos

1. NOUN

/ klɔrˈpɪrɪˌfɑs / (AmE), / klɔːˈpɪrɪˌfɒs / (BrE)
a toxic organophosphate compound used as a broad-spectrum insecticide in agriculture and pest control
an organophosphate insecticide
medical

Examples

Farmers often use chlorpyrifos to control pests on crops such as corn and soybeans.
Studies have raised concerns about the environmental impact of chlorpyrifos.
The government announced new restrictions on the use of chlorpyrifos in agriculture.
