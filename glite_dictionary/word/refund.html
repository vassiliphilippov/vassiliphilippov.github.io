refund

1. VERB

/ riˈfʌnd / (AmE), / rɪˈfʌnd / (BrE)
to pay back money, especially to someone who is dissatisfied with a product or service or who has paid more than required
pay back money

Examples

The airline agreed to refund the passengers their ticket costs after the flight was cancelled.
If you return the faulty product, we will refund your money within seven days.
They refused to refund the fee, insisting that the service had been delivered as promised.

Synset Examples

Please refund me my money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reimburse - to pay back money spent or lost
  • Synonym: to repay - to pay back money owed

Etymology

From Latin refundere, from re- 'back' + fundere 'pour'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02289796-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈriː.fʌnd / (AmE), / ˈriː.fʌnd / (BrE)
the act of returning money previously paid, or the sum of money returned
the act of returning money

Examples

If the product is faulty, you are entitled to a full refund.
She requested a refund after realizing the event was canceled.
It may take several days for your refund to appear in your account.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reimbursement - the act of repaying someone for expenses
  • Antonym: payment - the action of paying someone or something

Etymology

From Latin 'refundere', meaning to pour back.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01124011-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈriːˌfʌnd / (AmE), / ˈriːˌfʌnd / (BrE)
an amount of money that is paid back to someone because they have returned a product or paid too much
money returned to a payer

Examples

If the item is faulty, you are entitled to a full refund.
She requested a refund after being charged twice for her meal.
The company will issue a refund within five business days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reimbursement - repayment of money already spent
  • Antonym: payment - an amount of money paid

Etymology

From Latin 'refundere', meaning 'to pour back' (re- 'back' + fundere 'to pour').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13303184-n
Synset POS: n
