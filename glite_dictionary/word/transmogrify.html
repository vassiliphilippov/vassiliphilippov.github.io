to transmogrify

1. VERB

/ trænzˈmɑːɡrəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / trænzˈmɒɡrɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
to change or transform the appearance or nature of something, especially in a surprising or magical manner
transform in a surprising or magical way

Examples

The wizard threatened to transmogrify the prince into a toad if he disobeyed.
After months of renovation, the old warehouse was transmogrified into a trendy art gallery.
As the smoke cleared, the magician began transmogrifying objects on stage, astonishing the audience.

Synset Examples

In Kafka's story, a person metamorphoses into a bug
The treatment and diet transfigured her into a beautiful young woman
Jesus was transfigured after his resurrection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to transform - to change in form or appearance
  • Synonym: to metamorphose - to change completely the nature or appearance, particularly in a biological context

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from a fanciful alteration of 'transfigure' or 'metamorphose', first recorded in the 17th century.
