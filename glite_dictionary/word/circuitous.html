circuitous

1. ADJ

/ sɚˈkjuːɾiəs / (AmE), / səˈkjuːɪtəs / (BrE)
(of a route or journey) longer than the most direct way; taking an indirect path.
long and indirect (route or journey)

Examples

The hikers took a circuitous trail through the forest to enjoy the scenery.
His explanation was so circuitous that I struggled to understand his main point.
We drove along a circuitous route to avoid the construction on the main road.

Synset Examples

a scenic but devious route
a long and circuitous journey by train and boat
a roundabout route avoided rush-hour traffic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: direct - moving or extending in a straight line or by the shortest route
  • Synonym: roundabout - not saying something directly; indirect

Etymology

From Latin 'circuitus' (a going around, circuit).

2. ADJ

/ sɚˈkjuːɾiəs / (AmE), / sɜːˈkjuːɪtɪəs / (BrE)
(of speech or conduct) not direct or straightforward; using more words or taking a less direct path than necessary.
indirect in speech or conduct

Examples

The manager's circuitous explanation only confused the team further.
He gave a circuitous answer, avoiding the real issue.
Her circuitous way of telling stories often frustrated listeners.

Synset Examples

the explanation was circuitous and puzzling
a roundabout paragraph
hear in a roundabout way that her ex-husband was marrying her best friend

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roundabout - not saying something directly; indirect in speech or action
  • Antonym: direct - straightforward in speech or conduct

Etymology

From Latin 'circuitus' (a going around), from 'circuire' (to go around).
