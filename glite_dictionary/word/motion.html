motion

1. VERB

/ ˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / ˈməʊʃən / (BrE)
to make a movement with your hand or head to show, express, or direct something
to signal by movement

Examples

She motioned to her friend to come closer.
The teacher motioned for the students to be quiet by raising her hand.
He motioned toward the door, indicating that it was time to leave.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gesture - to make a movement to express an idea or feeling

Etymology

From Latin motio (movement), from movere (to move).
