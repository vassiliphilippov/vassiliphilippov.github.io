Seward

1. PROPN

/ ˈsuːɚd / (AmE), / ˈsjʊəd / (BrE)
William H. Seward (1801–1872), U.S. politician and Secretary of State, who arranged the purchase of Alaska from Russia in 1867.
U.S. Secretary of State who negotiated Alaska purchase

Examples

William H. Seward is best remembered for negotiating the purchase of Alaska.
Many at the time mocked Seward's decision to buy Alaska, calling it 'Seward's Folly.'
Seward served as Secretary of State under President Lincoln and Andrew Johnson.
