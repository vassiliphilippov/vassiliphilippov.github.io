parole

1. NOUN

/ pəˈroʊl / (AmE), / pəˈrəʊl / (BrE)
the conditional release of a prisoner before the end of their sentence, subject to compliance with certain terms
conditional release from prison
legal

Examples

After ten years in prison, he was finally granted parole.
Violating the conditions of parole can result in a return to jail.
The board will review her case to decide if she is eligible for parole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: probation - a period of supervised release as an alternative to imprisonment
  • Antonym: imprisonment - the state of being confined in prison

Etymology

From French 'parole' (word, promise), originally meaning a promise given by a prisoner of war not to escape.

2. VERB

/ pəˈroʊl / (AmE), / pəˈrəʊl / (BrE)
to release a prisoner from detention on the condition of good behavior, typically under supervision, before the end of their sentence
release a prisoner on supervised conditions
legal

Examples

After five years of good behavior, the governor decided to parole the inmate.
The board may parole prisoners if they believe they pose no threat to society.
He was paroled last spring and is now working at a local shelter.

Synset Examples

The prisoner was paroled after serving 10 years in prison

Related Terms

  • Synonym: release - set someone free from confinement or custody
  • Antonym: to imprison - put or keep someone in prison

Etymology

From French 'parole' (word, promise), originally meaning 'word of honor'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
