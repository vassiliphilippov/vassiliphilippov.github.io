subduer

1. NOUN

/ səbˈduːər / (AmE), / səbˈdjuːə / (BrE)
a person who overcomes others and establishes dominance or control, especially by force or persuasion
one who overcomes and controls others

Examples

The early settlers praised the subduer of the wild frontier for his bravery.
As a subduer of opposition, she relied more on persuasion than brute force.
The history book chronicled the rise of the empire under its greatest subduer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conqueror - a person who conquers a place or people

Etymology

From Latin subdūcere (to draw up, lead away); related to 'subdue'.
