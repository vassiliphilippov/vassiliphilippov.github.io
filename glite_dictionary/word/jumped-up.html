jumped-up

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒʌmpt ʌp / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌmpt ʌp / (BrE)
(British, informal) Behaving as if one is more important or influential than one really is, especially after a sudden promotion or rise in status.
(British informal) having exaggerated self-importance
informal british

Examples

The tabloids dismissed him as a jumped-up manager who owed everything to luck.
Some colleagues think she's just a jumped-up assistant who doesn't deserve the promotion.
His jumped-up attitude annoyed those who had worked there for years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upstart - a person who has risen suddenly to wealth or power, especially one who behaves arrogantly
