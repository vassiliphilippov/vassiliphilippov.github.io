television

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛlɪˌvɪʒən / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪˌvɪʒən / (BrE)
A system for transmitting visual images and sound that are reproduced on screens, used for broadcasting programs, information, and entertainment to the public.
system for broadcasting visual images and sound

Examples

The family sat together to watch the news on television every evening.
With the advent of television, people could see events happening around the world in real time.
Modern television systems offer hundreds of channels and high-definition images.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: radio - system for broadcasting sound only

Etymology

Formed in the early 20th century from Greek 'tele-' (far) and Latin 'visio' (sight).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛl.əˌvɪʒ.ən / (AmE), / ˈtɛl.ɪˌvɪʒ.ən / (BrE)
A system for transmitting visual images and sound that are reproduced on screens, used primarily to broadcast programs for entertainment, information, and education.
broadcasting visual images and sound

Examples

Many people watch television to relax after work.
The invention of television changed the way news and entertainment were delivered.
Children should limit the amount of time they spend in front of television.

Synset Examples

she is a star of screen and video
Television is a medium because it is neither rare nor well done

Related Terms

  • Synonym: TV - Abbreviation for television; same meaning
  • Antonym: radio - Transmission of audio signals only, without images

Etymology

From Greek tele- (far) + Latin visio (seeing).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɛləˌvɪʒən / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪˌvɪʒən / (BrE)
an electronic device for receiving television broadcasts and displaying them on a screen
an electronic device for viewing TV programs

Examples

We bought a new television for the living room last weekend.
She spends her evenings watching documentaries on television.
The children gathered around the television to watch their favorite cartoon.

Synset Examples

the British call a tv set a ‘telly’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: TV - common informal abbreviation for 'television'
  • Synonym: telly - British informal term for 'television'

Etymology

From French 'télévision', from Greek 'tēle-' (far) and Latin 'visio' (sight).
