weatherliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛðərlɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈwɛðəlɪnɪs / (BrE)
The ability of a sailing vessel to sail close to the wind with minimal leeward drift, even in strong winds.
ability of a vessel to sail close to the wind

Examples

The captain praised the frigate's weatherliness during the challenging crossing.
Improvements in hull design have greatly increased the weatherliness of modern yachts.
Historical records note that Spanish galleons were admired for their speed and weatherliness.

Synset Examples

the Spanish ships had superior speed and weatherliness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weatherly - (of a sailing vessel) able to sail close to the wind with little leeward drift

Etymology

From 'weatherly' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality of being weatherly.
