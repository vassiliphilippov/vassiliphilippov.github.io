europium

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈroʊpiəm / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiəm / (BrE)
a soft, silvery-white metallic element of the rare earth group, symbol Eu, used in the production of phosphorescent and fluorescent materials
a soft metallic element (symbol Eu)

Examples

Television screens often use europium as a component to create vivid red colors.
The scientist explained the unique properties of europium during her lecture on rare earth elements.
Modern euro banknotes contain europium compounds to help prevent counterfeiting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: element - a substance consisting of atoms all with the same number of protons

Etymology

Named after Europe, the continent.
