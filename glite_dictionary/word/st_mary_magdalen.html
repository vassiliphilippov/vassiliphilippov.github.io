St. Mary Magdalen

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈmɛri ˈmæɡdəˌlən / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈmɛːri ˈmæɡdələn / (BrE)
A follower of Jesus in the New Testament, traditionally believed to have been healed by him of evil spirits and often described as a penitent sinner.
female disciple of Jesus in the New Testament

Examples

Many artists have depicted St. Mary Magdalen as a symbol of repentance in their paintings.
According to the Gospels, St. Mary Magdalen was present at both the crucifixion and the resurrection of Jesus.
Some scholars believe that St. Mary Magdalen played a significant role among the early followers of Jesus.

Etymology

From Hebrew 'Magdala' (a town), referring to Mary from Magdala.
