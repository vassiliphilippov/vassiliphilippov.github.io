haematogenic

1. ADJ

/ himətəˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / hiːmətəˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
relating to the formation of blood or blood cells
pertaining to blood cell formation
medical british

Examples

The haematogenic function of the bone marrow is essential for maintaining healthy blood levels.
Certain diseases can impair haematogenic processes in the body.
Researchers study haematogenic stem cells to understand treatments for blood disorders.

Synset Examples

hemopoietic stem cells in bone marrow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hematopoietic - relating to the formation of blood cells

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' (blood) + 'genēs' (born of or produced by).
