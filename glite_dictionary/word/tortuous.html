tortuous

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɔrtʃuəs / (AmE), / ˈtɔːtʃuəs / (BrE)
Full of twists, turns, or complicated details; highly complex or intricate and sometimes devious.
highly complex or intricate

Examples

The negotiations took a tortuous path, lasting several months before reaching agreement.
The lawyer presented a tortuous argument that left many in the courtroom confused.
The regulations are written in such a tortuous way that even experts struggle to interpret them.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: straightforward - simple and easy to understand or do
  • Synonym: convoluted - extremely complex and difficult to follow

Etymology

From Latin 'tortuosus' (full of twists, winding), from 'torquere' (to twist).

(of a process, route, or argument) not straightforward; full of twists, turns, or complexity; complicated or devious.
