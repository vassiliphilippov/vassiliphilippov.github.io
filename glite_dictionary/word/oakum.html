oakum

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊkəm / (AmE), / ˈəʊkəm / (BrE)
loose hemp or jute fiber obtained by unravelling old ropes, often impregnated with tar, formerly used to caulk seams and pack joints in wooden ships and pipes
fibers used for caulking in ships and pipes

Examples

Sailors spent hours picking apart ropes to make oakum for the ship's repairs.
The caulker forced oakum into the cracks between the planks before sealing them with pitch.
Historically, prisoners were often assigned the laborious task of picking oakum in jails.

Etymology

From Old English *ācumba*, possibly meaning 'tow combings.'
