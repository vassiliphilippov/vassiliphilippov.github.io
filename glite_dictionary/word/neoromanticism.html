neoromanticism

1. NOUN

/ ˌniː.oʊ.roʊˈmæn.tɪ.sɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌniː.əʊ.rəʊˈmæn.tɪ.sɪ.zəm / (BrE)
A movement in art and literature characterized by a revival of Romanticism, often emphasizing emotion, imagination, and individualism.
revival movement of Romanticism in art and literature

Examples

The painter's works are often described as a return to neoromanticism in their themes and styles.
In the early 20th century, neoromanticism influenced many poets seeking to revive emotional expression.
Her novel draws on elements of neoromanticism, blending fantasy with deep personal reflection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: romanticism - an artistic and literary movement focusing on emotion and individualism

Etymology

From neo- (new) + Romanticism; modeled after late 19th and 20th century usage.
