RF

1. NOUN

/ ɑr ɛf / (AmE), / ɑː ɛf / (BrE)
A substance produced by the hypothalamus that stimulates the release of a specific hormone by the anterior pituitary gland; also called releasing factor.
hormone-releasing substance (biology)
medical

Examples

The hypothalamus secretes RF to trigger the release of certain pituitary hormones.
Researchers measured the levels of RF in the subjects' blood samples.
The discovery of RF revolutionized our understanding of hormonal regulation in the body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: releasing hormone - a hormone from the hypothalamus stimulating pituitary hormone release

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'releasing factor'.

2. NOUN

/ ɑr ɛf / (AmE), / ɑː ɛf / (BrE)
Abbreviation for reticular formation; a complex network of neurons in the brainstem involved in regulating arousal, attention, sleep, and muscle tone.
reticular formation in the brainstem
medical

Examples

The neurologist explained that damage to the RF could disrupt consciousness and sleep cycles.
Research on the RF has provided insights into how attention is controlled in the brain.
Abnormalities in the RF may be linked to certain sleep disorders and problems with muscle tone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reticular formation - complex neural structure in the brainstem involved in regulating consciousness and attention

Etymology

Abbreviation of reticular formation, from Latin 'reticula' (net) and 'formation'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05509741-n
Synset POS: n
