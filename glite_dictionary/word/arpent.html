arpent

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈpɑ̃ / (AmE), / ɑːˈpãː / (BrE)
a former French unit of area, approximately equal to an acre, used especially in land measurement in parts of Canada and Louisiana
former French area unit (approx. an acre)

Examples

The land grant was described as twenty arpents wide and forty deep.
Early settlers measured their farms in arpents, following the old French tradition.
He owns several arpents of fertile land along the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acre - a unit of area commonly used in the UK and US, about 4,047 square meters

Etymology

From French 'arpent', from Latin 'arepentem', a measure of land.
