waking

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈweɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the state or condition of being awake, especially as opposed to being asleep
state of being awake

Examples

His long illness was marked by nights of waking and restless thoughts.
During her waking, she listened to the distant sounds of the city.
The medication caused more waking than restful sleep.

Synset Examples

days of danger and nights of waking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wakefulness - the state of being awake and alert
  • Antonym: sleep - the natural periodic state of rest for the body and mind

Etymology

From Old English 'wacian' (to be awake, to stay awake).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05686141-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈweɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈweɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a period of time or state) marked by being fully conscious and alert; not asleep.
fully conscious; not asleep

Examples

During my waking hours, I try to stay productive and focused.
The noise continued through both waking and sleeping moments.
She spent every waking minute thinking about her new project.

Synset Examples

worked every moment of my waking hours

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sleeping - in a state of sleep; not awake

Etymology

Present participle of 'wake', from Old English 'wacan' (to wake, arise).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00189078-s
Synset POS: s
