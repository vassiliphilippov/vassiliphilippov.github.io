lapidator

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæpɪˈdeɪtər / (AmE), / ˌlæpɪˈdeɪtə / (BrE)
a person who attacks someone by pelting them with stones, especially with intent to kill
person who kills or attacks by stoning

Examples

In ancient times, the crime was punished by sending a lapidator into the crowd to enforce the sentence.
The accused claimed he was not a lapidator but was merely an onlooker at the scene.
Historical texts describe the dreaded role of the lapidator in public executions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stoner - a person who stones someone (with stones)
  • Antonym: victim - the person attacked or killed

Etymology

From Latin 'lapidator', from 'lapidare' (to throw stones), from 'lapis' (stone).
