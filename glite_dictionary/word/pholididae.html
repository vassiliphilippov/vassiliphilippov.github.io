Pholididae

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈlɪdɪdiː / (AmE), / fəˈlɪdɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of fish in the suborder Blennioidea, known as gunnels, characterized by their elongated bodies and small scales.
family of elongated marine fish (gunnels)

Examples

The fish in the family Pholididae are commonly called gunnels and are found in temperate marine waters.
Researchers studied several species within Pholididae to better understand their evolutionary history.
Many members of Pholididae have slender, almost eel-like bodies suitable for living in rocky crevices.

Etymology

From New Latin Pholis (a genus of gunnel fish) + -idae (a zoological family suffix from Greek).
