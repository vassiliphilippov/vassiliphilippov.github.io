voiceless

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɔɪsləs / (AmE), / ˈvɔɪslɪs / (BrE)
Deprived of the rights of citizenship, especially the legal right to vote or speak in social or political matters.
deprived of rights, especially voting

Examples

The new policies left many citizens voiceless and excluded from the decision-making process.
Activists hope to give a platform to the voiceless in society.
Throughout history, the voiceless have often resorted to protests to demand basic rights.

Synset Examples

labor was voiceless
disenfranchised masses took to the streets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disenfranchised - deprived of the right to vote or other rights

Etymology

From 'voice' + '-less', with the figurative sense evolving from literal lack of voice to lack of rights.

2. ADJ

/ ˈvɔɪs.ləs / (AmE), / ˈvɔɪs.ləs / (BrE)
(of a speech sound) produced without vibration of the vocal cords.
lacking vocal cord vibration (phonetics)

Examples

The English 'p' is a voiceless consonant, while 'b' is voiced.
Linguists study how voiceless sounds are articulated differently from voiced ones.
She struggled to distinguish between voiceless and voiced consonants in French.

Synset Examples

unvoiced consonants such as ‘p’ and ‘k’ and ‘s’

Related Terms

  • Antonym: voiced - (of a speech sound) produced with vibration of the vocal cords
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02294026-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈvɔɪs.ləs / (AmE), / ˈvɔɪs.lɪs / (BrE)
(of a sound) produced without vibration of the vocal cords; spoken or uttered without voice.
produced without vibration of the vocal cords

Examples

She spoke in a barely voiceless whisper, afraid to wake the baby.
The consonant 'p' in 'pat' is a classic voiceless sound in English phonetics.
His cry was so weak it was almost voiceless and went unheard in the noisy room.

Synset Examples

could hardly hear her breathed plea, ‘Help me’
voiceless whispers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silent - not making or accompanied by any sound
  • Antonym: voiced - (of a speech sound) pronounced with vibration of the vocal cords

Etymology

From 'voice' + '-less', meaning 'without voice'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00175980-s

4. ADJ

/ ˈvɔɪsləs / (AmE), / ˈvɔɪslɪs / (BrE)
(of a person) unable to produce vocal sounds because of injury or illness, able to speak only in whispers.
unable to make vocal sounds due to illness or injury

Examples

After her throat surgery, Maria was voiceless for several weeks.
The accident left him voiceless, able only to communicate in hushed tones.
He felt frustrated being voiceless during his long recovery from the illness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dumb - unable to speak; mute
  • Synonym: mute - unable to speak
  • Degree Lower: speechless - unable to speak, typically temporarily

Etymology

From 'voice' + '-less', meaning 'without voice'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00152929-s
Synset POS: s
