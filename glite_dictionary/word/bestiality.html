bestiality

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɛstiˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌbɛstɪˈalɪti / (BrE)
sexual activity between a human and an animal; sexual intercourse with an animal
sexual activity with an animal

Examples

He was arrested and charged with bestiality under state law.
Some ancient cultures banned bestiality as a form of social taboo.
Modern legal codes in many countries explicitly prohibit bestiality.

Etymology

From Latin bestia ('beast') + -ality, meaning the state or condition.

2. NOUN

/ ˌbɛstiˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌbɛstiˈælɪti / (BrE)
the condition or quality of being brutal, lacking reason or control, resembling that of a beast
brutality; beast-like nature

Examples

The bestiality of the mob shocked the entire nation.
His actions were marked by a certain bestiality that frightened even his allies.
Literature sometimes depicts war as unleashing the bestiality within people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brutality - the quality of being cruel, harsh, or ruthless
  • Antonym: humanity - the quality of being humane, showing compassion

Etymology

From Late Latin *bestialitas* (beastliness), from Latin *bestia* (beast).
