contemporary

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtɛmpəˌrɛri / (AmE), / kənˈtɛmpərəri / (BrE)
a person who lives at the same time or is nearly the same age as another person
person of similar age or era

Examples

Many of Shakespeare's contemporaries admired his plays and poetry.
She has achieved more than most of her contemporaries in the field of science.
Einstein was a contemporary of Marie Curie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peer - a person who is equal to another in age, status, or ability

Etymology

From Latin 'contemporarius', from 'com-' (together) + 'temporarius' (of time), from 'tempus' (time).

5. NOUN

/ kənˈtɛmpəˌrɛri / (AmE), / kənˈtɛmpərəri / (BrE)
a person who lives at the same time as another, especially someone of approximately the same age as another person
person living at the same time as another

Examples

Many of Shakespeare’s contemporaries were also renowned playwrights of their time.
His achievements impressed his contemporaries as much as they continue to inspire people today.
Unlike most of her contemporaries, she traveled the world early in her career.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peer - a person of the same age, status, or ability as another
