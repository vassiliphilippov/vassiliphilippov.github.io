Hendrik Verwoerd

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛn.drɪk vɛrˈvʊərd / (AmE), / ˈhɛn.drɪk vəˈvʊəd / (BrE)
(1901–1966) South African statesman and prime minister, known for instituting and promoting the policy of apartheid.
South African statesman, apartheid architect

Examples

Hendrik Verwoerd played a pivotal role in shaping South Africa's policy of racial segregation.
The assassination of Hendrik Verwoerd marked a significant turning point in South African history.
Many historians consider Hendrik Verwoerd to be the chief architect of apartheid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apartheid - a policy or system of segregation or discrimination on grounds of race, especially as formerly practiced in South Africa
