petroleum

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtroʊliəm / (AmE), / pəˈtrəʊliəm / (BrE)
A thick, dark-colored, flammable liquid consisting mainly of hydrocarbons, found naturally below the earth's surface and processed to produce fuels such as gasoline, kerosene, and diesel oil.
dark flammable liquid oil (crude oil)

Examples

The country relies heavily on petroleum exports for its economic growth.
Environmental concerns have increased over the use of petroleum as a primary energy source.
The refinery converts petroleum into gasoline, diesel, and other useful products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crude oil - unrefined petroleum as extracted from the ground
  • Degree Higher: gasoline - refined fuel derived from petroleum

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'petroleum', from Latin 'petra' (rock) + 'oleum' (oil).
