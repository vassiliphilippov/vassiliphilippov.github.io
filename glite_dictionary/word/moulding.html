moulding

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈməʊldɪŋ / (BrE)
A sculpture or decorative object produced by shaping a material in a mold.
A sculpture made by molding

Examples

The artist displayed a beautiful moulding created from clay in the exhibition.
This antique moulding depicts a scene from Greek mythology.
She specializes in the moulding of figures using traditional techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: casting - An object made by pouring liquid into a mold and allowing it to harden

Etymology

From English 'mold' (to shape) with the suffix '-ing'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈməʊldɪŋ / (BrE)
A decorative strip of material used to adorn or finish the edges of walls, ceilings, doors, or furniture.
decorative finishing strip

Examples

The ornate moulding around the ceiling added elegance to the room.
She painted the moulding above the doorway to match the walls.
Carpenters installed new moulding to give the library a classic look.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trim - decorative edging or strip for finishing
  • Synonym: cornice - horizontal decorative molding at the top of a wall or building

Etymology

From Old French 'moldure', from 'modle' (model), influenced by the act of shaping or forming.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03785925-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈməʊldɪŋ / (BrE)
a decorative strip or surface, often recessed or projected, used to finish or ornament the edge of a structure such as a wall or ceiling
decorative architectural edge feature

Examples

The intricate moulding around the doorway gave the entrance an elegant appearance.
Restoring the original moulding was essential to maintaining the historic charm of the house.
They chose a decorative moulding to add character to the plain ceiling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornice - an ornamental molding around the wall of a room just below the ceiling

Etymology

From Middle English 'molden', related to the verb 'to mould' (to shape); ultimately from Latin 'modulus' (a small measure, standard).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈməʊldɪŋ / (BrE)
A preliminary sculpture made in wax or clay, from which a finished work can be copied.
preliminary sculpture in wax or clay

Examples

The artist created a moulding in clay before casting the final bronze statue.
This moulding will serve as the base for the finished marble sculpture.
A wax moulding allows sculptors to adjust details before making the final piece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: model - A representation of a proposed sculpture for study or copying

Etymology

From Middle English 'moldyng', from 'mold' (form) + '-ing'.
