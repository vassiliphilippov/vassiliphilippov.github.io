pullet

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʊlɪt / (AmE), / ˈpʊlɪt / (BrE)
The meat of a medium-sized young chicken, considered especially suitable for frying.
meat of a young chicken for frying

Examples

The chef recommended using a pullet for the best fried chicken.
Freshly cooked pullet is often more tender than older chicken meats.
They ordered a dish made from pullet at the country restaurant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chicken - flesh of domesticated fowl used as food

Etymology

From Old French *poulette*, diminutive of *poule* (hen), from Latin *pullus* (young animal).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpʊlɪt / (AmE), / ˈpʊlɪt / (BrE)
a young hen, especially one less than a year old and not yet fully mature
young hen (less than a year old)

Examples

The farmer separated the pullets from the older hens to monitor their growth.
At six months old, a pullet starts to lay her first eggs.
Raising pullets requires careful attention to their diet and environment.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: hen - adult female chicken
  • Degree Lower: chick - young chicken, recently hatched

Etymology

From Old French 'poulette', diminutive of 'poule' (hen), from Latin 'pullus' (young animal).
