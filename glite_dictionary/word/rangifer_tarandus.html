Rangifer tarandus

1. NOUN

/ ˈræn.dʒɪ.fər təˈræn.dəs / (AmE), / ˈræn.dʒɪ.fə təˈræn.dəs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the arctic deer species known as the reindeer in Eurasia and the caribou in North America, characterized by large antlers in both sexes.
scientific name for reindeer/caribou

Examples

The migration patterns of Rangifer tarandus are crucial to understanding Arctic ecosystems.
Scientists studied the diet of Rangifer tarandus to assess environmental changes in the tundra.
Both male and female Rangifer tarandus grow large, impressive antlers each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reindeer - arctic deer with large antlers, Eurasian term
  • Synonym: caribou - North American term for Rangifer tarandus

Etymology

From Latin: 'rangifer' (reindeer) and 'tarandus', an ancient name for the reindeer.
