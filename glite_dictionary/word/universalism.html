universalism

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːnɪˈvɜrsəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪˈvɜːsəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the theological doctrine that all people will eventually be saved and attain salvation
doctrine that all will be saved

Examples

The preacher delivered a sermon on universalism and the hope for all humanity's salvation.
Some denominations reject universalism, preferring doctrines with stricter criteria for salvation.
Her beliefs gradually shifted toward universalism after years of theological study.

Etymology

From Latin universalis (universal) + -ism, indicating a doctrine or belief.
