to copulate

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑː.pjə.leɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒp.jʊ.leɪt / (BrE)
to engage in sexual intercourse, especially used of animals or in a biological context
to engage in sexual intercourse

Examples

During the spring, many birds copulate to ensure the survival of their species.
Researchers observed the whales as they copulated in the open ocean.
Some animals perform elaborate rituals before they copulate.

Synset Examples

Birds mate in the spring

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mate - to come together for reproduction (used of animals)

Etymology

From Latin 'copulatus', past participle of 'copulare' (to join together).
