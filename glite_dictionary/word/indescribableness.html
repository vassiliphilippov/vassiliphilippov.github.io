indescribableness

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndɪˈskraɪbəbəlnəs / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈskraɪbəbəlnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being impossible or extremely difficult to describe in words.
state of being impossible to describe

Examples

The sheer indescribableness of the scene left everyone in awe.
He tried to capture the indescribableness of his emotions in his writing, but words failed him.
The artist aimed to convey the indescribableness of beauty through abstract forms and colors.

Synset Examples

Here, humility and prayer are connected directly to Evagrius' apophatic reflection and are subsumed in the indescribability of God's being.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indescribability - The quality of being indescribable
  • Antonym: describability - The quality of being able to be described, the opposite sense

Etymology

From 'indescribable' + '-ness'.
