inconvertibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnkənˌvɝtəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌɪnkənˌvɜːtəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of not being able to be exchanged for something else, especially money or currency.
quality of not being exchangeable

Examples

The inconvertibility of the local currency has severely limited foreign investment in the country.
Owing to the inconvertibility of tickets, refunds or exchanges were not permitted after purchase.
Economic reforms addressed the issue of inconvertibility, allowing citizens to exchange their money freely.

Synset Examples

the inconvertibility of their currency made international trade impossible

Related Terms

  • Antonym: convertibility - the quality of being exchangeable, especially with respect to currency

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (not) + 'convertibilis' (able to be converted).
