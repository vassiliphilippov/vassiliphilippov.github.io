countercharge

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊn.tɚˌtʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.təˌtʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
an accusation made by a person in response to an accusation against them, especially in legal contexts
a responsive accusation, especially in law

Examples

The defendant made a countercharge of harassment against her accuser.
Their argument escalated when each side leveled a new countercharge.
In court, the countercharge was seen as an attempt to shift blame.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accusation - a formal charge or claim that someone has done something wrong
  • Synonym: charge - a formal allegation of wrongdoing or crime

Etymology

Formed from 'counter-' (against) + 'charge' (accusation), in analogy with other compounds like 'counterattack'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntərˌtʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌtʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
an accusation or charge made in response to another; a retaliatory accusation
a retaliatory accusation or charge

Examples

After being accused of negligence, the manager made a countercharge against the complainant.
The heated debate quickly escalated into a series of charges and countercharges.
Her lawyer issued a countercharge claiming the plaintiff had fabricated evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accusation - a statement that someone has done something wrong or illegal
  • Synonym: charge - an official statement accusing someone of a crime or wrongdoing

Etymology

Formed from 'counter-' (against) + 'charge' (accusation).
