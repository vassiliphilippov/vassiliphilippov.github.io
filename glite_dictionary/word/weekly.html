weekly

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːkli / (AmE), / ˈwiːkli / (BrE)
a magazine or newspaper published once a week
periodical published weekly

Examples

She subscribes to a popular weekly focused on world affairs.
Our town has a free weekly delivered to every household.
He wrote a column for the local weekly for over ten years.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: daily - a newspaper published every day
  • Antonym: monthly - a periodical published every month

Etymology

From week + -ly, denoting regular occurrence every week.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06606462-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADV

/ ˈwik.li / (AmE), / ˈwiːk.li / (BrE)
happening once a week; occurring or done every week
once a week; every week (adverb)

Examples

She calls her mother weekly to catch up on family news.
The trash is collected weekly in this neighborhood.
They meet weekly to discuss the progress of the project.

Synset Examples

she visited her aunt weekly

Etymology

From Middle English wekely, formed from 'week' + -ly.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00081941-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADJ

/ ˈwikli / (AmE), / ˈwiːkli / (BrE)
Happening, produced, or appearing once every week.
happening every week

Examples

We have a weekly meeting every Monday at 10 a.m.
She writes a weekly column for the local newspaper.
The gardener makes a weekly visit to tend the plants.

Synset Examples

a weekly visit
weekly paper

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: daily - happening every day
  • Degree Lower: monthly - happening every month

Etymology

From Old English wice (week) + -ly, forming an adjective of frequency.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01975568-s
Synset POS: s
