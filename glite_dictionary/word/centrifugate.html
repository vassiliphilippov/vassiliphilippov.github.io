to centrifugate

1. VERB

/ ˈsɛntrəˌfjuːˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrɪˌfjuːˌɡeɪt / (BrE)
to rotate a mixture at very high speed in order to separate its liquid and solid components, especially in a laboratory setting
separate substances by rapid spinning
medical

Examples

After collecting the blood sample, the technician will centrifugate it to separate the plasma.
The solution was centrifugated for ten minutes to isolate the solid particles.
Researchers often centrifugate samples to prepare them for further analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: centrifuge - a machine that spins substances to separate liquids from solids

Etymology

From Latin 'centrum' (center) and 'fugare' (to drive away); related to centrifuge.
