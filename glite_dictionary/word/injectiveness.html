injectiveness

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdʒɛktɪvnɪs / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒɛktɪvnɪs / (BrE)
(mathematics) The property of a function or mapping being an injection, where each element of the range corresponds to at most one element of the domain.
property of being an injection in mathematics

Examples

The mathematician discussed the injectiveness of the function with her students.
Understanding injectiveness is crucial when studying the properties of different types of mappings.
The theorem depends on the injectiveness of the initial transformation.

Synset Examples

The basic problems of decomposition theory are to formulate necessary and sufficient conditions for injectiveness and to find out about the reconstruction map.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: injective - of a function, mapping distinct inputs to distinct outputs

Etymology

From 'injective' + '-ness', based on Latin 'inicere' (to throw in).
