indestructible

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪndɪˈstrʌktəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈstrʌktəbəl / (BrE)
impossible or very difficult to destroy or damage.
impossible to destroy

Examples

The superhero's shield was said to be indestructible, surviving every attack thrown at it.
These tires are made with an indestructible compound that resists punctures and cuts.
Her spirit proved indestructible in the face of adversity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: destructible - able to be destroyed; not indestructible
  • Synonym: durable - able to withstand wear and pressure; lasting

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + destructibilis (able to be destroyed).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪndɪˈstrʌktəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈstrʌktɪbəl / (BrE)
impossible to destroy or break; extremely durable or long-lasting.
impossible to destroy; very long lasting

Examples

Ancient myths speak of an indestructible shield that could withstand any attack.
Despite years of harsh weather, the statue appeared almost indestructible.
She admired her grandmother's indestructible spirit in the face of adversity.

Synset Examples

less durable rocks were gradually worn away to form valleys
the perdurable granite of the ancient Appalachian spine of the continent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: durable - able to withstand wear, pressure, or damage; hard-wearing
  • Antonym: fragile - easily broken or damaged

Etymology

From Latin 'indestructibilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'destruere' (to destroy).
