stillbirth

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪlˌbɝθ / (AmE), / ˈstɪlˌbɜːθ / (BrE)
the birth of a baby that has died in the womb after having survived through at least the first 20 to 28 weeks of pregnancy
birth of a dead baby (after at least 20-28 weeks)
medical

Examples

The family mourned the stillbirth of their child last year.
She suffered a stillbirth during her third trimester.
Medical staff provided counseling after the tragic stillbirth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miscarriage - loss of a pregnancy before the fetus can survive outside the womb
  • Antonym: live birth - birth of a baby that shows signs of life

Etymology

From 'still' (not moving) + 'birth' (childbirth).
