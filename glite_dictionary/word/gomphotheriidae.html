Gomphotheriidae

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑmfəˈθɪriəˌdi / (AmE), / ɡɒmfəˈθɪəriəˌdiː / (BrE)
an extinct family of elephant-like mammals that lived during the Pleistocene epoch
extinct family of ancient elephants

Examples

Fossils of the Gomphotheriidae have been found on multiple continents, illustrating their widespread range.
The Gomphotheriidae possessed shovel-like lower jaws that distinguished them from modern elephants.
Researchers believe the extinction of Gomphotheriidae may be linked to changing climate and human activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elephant - large herbivorous mammal with a trunk

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek gomphos (peg, bolt) and thērion (beast) with the family suffix -idae.

2. NOUN

/ ɡɒm.fəˈθɪr.i.ɪˌdi / (AmE), / ɡɒm.fəˈθɪə.rɪˌdiː / (BrE)
an extinct family of prehistoric elephants that lived from the Miocene to the Pleistocene epochs
extinct family of prehistoric elephants

Examples

The fossils of Gomphotheriidae have provided key clues about elephant evolution.
Several members of the Gomphotheriidae family had distinctive four-tusked skulls.
Research into Gomphotheriidae reveals their extinction coincided with major climatic changes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: elephant - large, herbivorous mammal with a trunk

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek gomphos (peg, bolt) and therion (beast), plus family suffix -idae.
