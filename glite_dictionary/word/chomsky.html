Chomsky

1. PROPN

/ ˈtʃɑm.ski / (AmE), / ˈtʃɒm.ski / (BrE)
Noam Chomsky (born 1928), an American linguist and philosopher whose theory of generative grammar had a profound impact on linguistics.
famous American linguist and philosopher

Examples

Chomsky's work revolutionized the study of language in the twentieth century.
Many students read Chomsky's books to understand modern linguistic theory.
The influence of Chomsky extends beyond linguistics into philosophy and political discourse.
