supremely

1. ADV

/ suˈprimli / (AmE), / suˈpriːmli / (BrE)
to the highest or greatest degree; extremely
to the highest degree; extremely

Examples

He felt supremely confident as he walked onto the stage.
Her work was supremely important to the success of the project.
The chef prepared a dish that was supremely delicious.

Synset Examples

he was supremely confident
He seemed irritated at Edouard's questions but, apart from that, sublimely unconcerned
she remained sublimely oblivious to the possible havoc she might have caused

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extremely - to a very great degree
  • Antonym: moderately - to a moderate or lesser degree

Etymology

From Latin supremus (highest, supreme) plus the English adverbial suffix -ly.
