storyteller

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɔriˌtɛlɚ / (AmE), / ˈstɔːriˌtɛlə / (BrE)
a person who tells stories, especially as a profession or as a form of entertainment
person who tells stories

Examples

The children sat quietly, captivated by the storyteller's voice.
As a skilled storyteller, she could make even the most ordinary events sound magical.
The festival featured a famous storyteller who shared traditional folk tales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: narrator - a person who narrates or gives an account of something

Etymology

From 'story' + 'teller'; attested since the 18th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɔːriˌtɛlər / (AmE), / ˈstɔːriˌtɛlə / (BrE)
A person who tells lies; someone who invents untrue stories to deceive others.
person who tells lies

Examples

He's such a storyteller—you can never trust a word he says.
Don’t believe her excuses; she’s a skilled storyteller when she’s in trouble.
I could tell by his wild explanation that he was just being a storyteller again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liar - a person who tells lies
  • Antonym: truth-teller - a person who tells the truth

Etymology

From 'story' + 'teller,' originally for narrators; figurative use for liars dates from 18th century.
