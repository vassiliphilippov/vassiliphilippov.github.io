macushla

1. NOUN

/ məˈkuːʃlə / (AmE), / məˈkuːʃlə / (BrE)
(Irish) A term of endearment meaning darling or beloved, used to address someone affectionately.
Irish term of endearment; darling

Examples

He whispered, "Good night, macushla, sleep well," before leaving the room.
In the old song, she is called "macushla", the love of his heart.
"Macushla, will you meet me after work?" he asked with a gentle smile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: darling - a person who is very dear; loved one
  • Synonym: beloved - a much loved person

Etymology

From Irish 'mo chuisle' meaning 'my pulse' or 'my darling'.
