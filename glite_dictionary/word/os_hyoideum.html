os hyoideum

1. NOUN

/ ɑs haɪˈoʊɪdiəm / (AmE), / ɒs hʌɪˈəʊɪdɪəm / (BrE)
the U-shaped bone at the base of the tongue that supports the tongue and its muscles
U-shaped bone at base of tongue
medical

Examples

During the autopsy, the pathologist noted a fracture in the os hyoideum.
The os hyoideum serves as an anchoring structure for the tongue muscles.
Damage to the os hyoideum can be significant in cases of strangulation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyoid bone - U-shaped bone at the base of the tongue that supports tongue muscles

Etymology

From Latin os (bone) + hyoideum (relating to the Greek letter upsilon, due to its shape).
