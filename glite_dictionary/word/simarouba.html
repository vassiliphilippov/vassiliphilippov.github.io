Simarouba

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪməˈruːbə / (AmE), / ˌsɪməˈruːbə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American trees and shrubs with pale, soft wood and bitter bark, forming the type genus of the Simaroubaceae family.
tropical American tree genus

Examples

The Simarouba tree is known for its medicinal bark and seeds.
Botanists studied several species within the Simarouba genus in the rainforest.
The lightweight wood of Simarouba makes it useful for carving and crafts.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the native Carib name for these trees.
