Eleutherodactylus

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌluθəroʊˈdæktɪləs / (AmE), / ɪˌljuːθɛrəˈdæktɪləs / (BrE)
a genus of small, mainly terrestrial frogs, commonly known as robber frogs, found chiefly in the Americas
genus of terrestrial robber frogs

Examples

The Eleutherodactylus genus includes hundreds of distinct species across the Americas.
Many Eleutherodactylus frogs are noted for their ability to thrive far from water.
Biologists often conduct field studies to monitor populations of Eleutherodactylus in tropical forests.

Etymology

From Greek eleutheros (free) and dactylos (finger), referring to the frogs' free digits.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌluθərəˈdæktɪləs / (AmE), / ɪˌljuːθərəˈdæktɪləs / (BrE)
a genus of small, terrestrial frogs found primarily in the Americas, commonly known as robber frogs
genus of terrestrial frogs (robber frogs)

Examples

The genus Eleutherodactylus contains hundreds of species known for their direct development, bypassing the tadpole stage.
Researchers discovered a new species within Eleutherodactylus in the mountains of Hispaniola.
Many Eleutherodactylus species are threatened by habitat loss in Central America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robber frog - common name for frogs in the genus Eleutherodactylus

Etymology

From Greek 'eleutheros' (free) and 'dactylos' (finger), referring to their free fingers.
