shapeless

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃeɪpləs / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪpləs / (BrE)
having no definite or distinct shape or form
without a clear shape or form

Examples

After the heavy rain, the snow had become a shapeless pile on the front lawn.
She wore a shapeless coat that made her look much larger than she was.
The sculpture began as a shapeless lump of clay before the artist molded it.

Synset Examples

amorphous clouds of insects
an aggregate of formless particles
a shapeless mass of protoplasm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amorphous - without a clearly defined shape or form
  • Synonym: formless - having no regular form or shape

Etymology

From 'shape' + '-less', meaning 'without shape'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃeɪpləs / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪpləs / (BrE)
lacking a definite, symmetrical, or attractive shape or form; having no clear structure.
lacking definite or attractive shape

Examples

She wore a shapeless dress that hid her figure completely.
The old sofa had become shapeless after years of use and neglect.
Clouds drifted across the sky in shapeless masses.

Synset Examples

a shapeless hat on his head

Related Terms

  • Synonym: formless - without a clear or distinct form
  • Antonym: shapely - having a well-defined or attractive shape

Etymology

From 'shape' + suffix '-less', meaning 'without shape'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02149721-s
Synset POS: s
