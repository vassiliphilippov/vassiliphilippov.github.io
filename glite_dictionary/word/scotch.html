Scotch

1. NOUN

/ skɑtʃ / (AmE), / skɒtʃ / (BrE)
A type of whisky distilled in Scotland, especially whisky made from malted barley in a pot still.
whisky made in Scotland

Examples

He poured himself a glass of Scotch after a long day at work.
Many collectors seek rare bottles of aged Scotch.
Would you prefer Scotch or bourbon with your meal?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whisky - a distilled alcoholic beverage made from fermented grain mash
  • Antonym: bourbon - a type of American whiskey made primarily from corn

Etymology

From 'Scotch' (an archaic form of 'Scottish'), referring to its origin in Scotland.

2. ADJ

/ skɑtʃ / (AmE), / skɒtʃ / (BrE)
of or relating to Scotland, its people, or its culture; now chiefly used in established phrases or as the name for certain products (such as whisky) and less commonly used to refer to people, often considered outdated or disliked by Scots themselves
relating to Scotland or its culture; often used in product names

Examples

He ordered a bowl of Scotch broth to start his meal.
Many Scottish people dislike being referred to as Scotch.
The traditional pattern of Scotch plaid is recognized around the world.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scottish - relating to Scotland or its people or culture; the current preferred adjective
  • Synonym: Scots - of or relating to Scotland (especially as language or people); also refers to the Scots language

Etymology

From Middle English *Scotish*, from Old English *Scotti* (the Scots people) + -ish.
