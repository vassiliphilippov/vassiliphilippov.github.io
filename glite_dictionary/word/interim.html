interim

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪn.təˌrɪm / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tər.ɪm / (BrE)
serving or intended to serve for a short period until something more permanent is established; temporary.
temporary; for an intervening period

Examples

The company appointed an interim manager while searching for a permanent replacement.
They signed an interim agreement to keep negotiations moving forward.
An interim solution was put in place until the full system was operational.

Synset Examples

an interim agreement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited period of time; not permanent

Etymology

From Latin 'interim' (meanwhile, in the meantime).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01761995-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.tə.rɪm / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tər.ɪm / (BrE)
the period of time between one event, process, or period and another; an intervening interval
period between two events or phases

Examples

The committee appointed an acting manager to lead the company during the interim.
Many students took part-time jobs in the interim before finding permanent employment.
Renovations will start in June, but in the interim the library will remain open.

Synset Examples

meanwhile the socialists are running the government

Etymology

From Latin 'interim', meaning 'meanwhile' or 'in the meantime'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-15297873-n
Synset POS: n
