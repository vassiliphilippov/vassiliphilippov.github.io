troublesomeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrʌbəlˌsəmnəs / (AmE), / ˈtrʌbl̩səmnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of causing difficulty, worry, or annoyance
quality of being troublesome

Examples

The troublesomeness of the new system delayed the project's completion by weeks.
Their persistent complaints only added to the troublesomeness of the situation.
She grew tired of the troublesomeness involved in filing constant reports.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trouble - difficulty or problems
  • Antonym: ease - freedom from difficulty or effort
