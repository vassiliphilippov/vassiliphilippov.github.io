nut

1. NOUN

/ nʌt / (AmE), / nʌt / (BrE)
A unit of space in typesetting equal to half the width of an em.
typographic unit, half an em

Examples

The designer specified a nut space between each column of text.
Old printing manuals mention both the nut and em as standard spacing units.
For precise layout, the typesetter measured every nut carefully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: en - a space in typesetting equal to half the width of an em

Etymology

Possibly from a play on the word 'en', referring to the 'en' space, which is also half the width of an 'em'.
