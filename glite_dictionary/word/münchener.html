Münchener

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʊn.ken.ər / (AmE), / ˈmʏn.tʃə.nə / (BrE)
A dark lager beer produced in Munich, known for its distinctive malty flavor.
dark Munich-style lager beer

Examples

The brewery specializes in traditional Münchener lagers with a rich malt character.
He ordered a cold Münchener to enjoy with his meal.
Many beer enthusiasts appreciate the smoothness of an authentic Münchener.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bock - a strong dark German lager beer
  • Antonym: pilsner - a type of pale lager beer

Etymology

German, from München (Munich), the city of origin.
