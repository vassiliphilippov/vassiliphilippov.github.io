pop-up

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑp ˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈpɒp ˌʌp / (BrE)
a children's book containing pages with three-dimensional structures that rise up when opened
children's book with 3D pages

Examples

She gave her daughter a beautiful pop-up for her birthday.
The pop-up amazed the children as a castle sprang from the page.
Many young readers enjoy pop-ups because of their interactive features.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑp ˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈpɒp ˌʌp / (BrE)
A high, short fly ball in baseball that is easy for fielders to catch.
short high fly ball (baseball)

Examples

The batter hit a pop-up to the infield, and the shortstop made an easy catch.
With two outs, a pop-up ended the inning and stranded the runners on base.
He was frustrated after hitting a pop-up instead of driving the ball to the outfield.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fly ball - a ball hit high into the air in baseball

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːp ˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈpɒp ˌʌp / (BrE)
a shop or store that operates temporarily in a location for a limited period of time, often to take advantage of seasonal demand or promote a product.
temporary retail store

Examples

A new pop-up opened downtown, offering limited-edition sneakers for just one week.
She visited a Christmas pop-up that sold handmade ornaments and gifts.
The art exhibition was hosted in a vacant building as a pop-up shop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temporary store - a store that operates for a short period

Etymology

Formed from 'pop up', indicating something that appears suddenly or unexpectedly.
