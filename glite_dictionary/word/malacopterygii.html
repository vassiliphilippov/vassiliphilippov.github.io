Malacopterygii

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæləkɒptəˈrɪdʒiaɪ / (AmE), / ˌmaləkɒptəˈrɪdʒɪʌɪ / (BrE)
a large group of teleost fishes whose fins are supported by flexible and often cartilaginous rays, rather than spines
group of fishes with soft, flexible fin rays
medical

Examples

The Malacopterygii include many familiar fish such as salmon and cod.
Unlike their relatives, members of the Malacopterygii have soft fins without sharp spines.
Scientists study the diversity of Malacopterygii to understand fish evolution.

Etymology

From Greek 'malakos' (soft) + 'pteryx, pterygos' (fin, wing).
