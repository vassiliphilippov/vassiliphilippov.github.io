Chelydra

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlaɪdrə / (AmE), / kɛˈlɪdrə / (BrE)
A genus of large freshwater snapping turtles native to the Americas, including the common snapping turtle.
genus of snapping turtles

Examples

The common snapping turtle belongs to the genus Chelydra.
Biologists studied the habitat range of several Chelydra species.
The genus Chelydra is distinguished by its powerful jaws and aggressive behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snapping turtle - a large freshwater turtle known for its powerful jaws
  • Synonym: Chelydra serpentina - the common snapping turtle, a species in the Chelydra genus

Etymology

From Greek khelys (tortoise) + hydra (water serpent).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪdrə / (AmE), / kɛˈlɪdrə / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater turtles known as snapping turtles, found in the Americas.
genus of snapping turtles

Examples

The Chelydra genus is well known for its aggressive snapping turtles.
Researchers studied the distribution of Chelydra species in various North American wetlands.
The most recognized member of Chelydra is the common snapping turtle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snapping turtle - Large freshwater turtle known for its powerful jaws

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek khelydros, a kind of water tortoise.
