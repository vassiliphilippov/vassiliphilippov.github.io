dicumarol

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈkjuːməˌrɒl / (AmE), / dɪˈkjuːmərɒl / (BrE)
an anticoagulant drug formerly used to prevent blood clotting, now largely replaced by warfarin
anticoagulant drug, superseded by warfarin
medical

Examples

The use of dicumarol as a blood-thinning agent declined after the introduction of safer alternatives.
Researchers studied the efficacy of dicumarol in treating patients at risk of thrombosis.
Some early anticoagulant therapies included dicumarol, but it was later replaced due to safety concerns.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: warfarin - a commonly used anticoagulant drug

Etymology

From di- (two) + coumarin (a chemical compound found in tonka beans and sweet clover), due to its chemical structure.
