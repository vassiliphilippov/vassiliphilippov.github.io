venison

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛnɪsən / (AmE), / ˈvɛnɪsən / (BrE)
meat from a deer, used as food
meat from a deer

Examples

He cooked a delicious stew using venison he got from the local butcher.
Many people enjoy the rich flavor of venison during hunting season.
The restaurant specializes in wild game, especially venison dishes.

Etymology

From Old French 'veneson', from Latin 'venatio' (a hunt, game hunted).
