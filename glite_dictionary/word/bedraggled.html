bedraggled

1. ADJ

/ bɪˈdræɡəld / (AmE), / bɪˈdræɡəld / (BrE)
looking untidy, dirty, and often wet, as if having been dragged through mud or water
untidy, dirty, and wet appearance

Examples

The kitten appeared at the door looking completely bedraggled after the rainstorm.
After hiking through the muddy field, their clothes were rather bedraggled.
She arrived at the meeting late and bedraggled, her hair and clothes dripping from the sudden downpour.

Synset Examples

the beggar's bedraggled clothes
scarecrows in battered hats or draggled skirts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disheveled - untidy or disordered in appearance
  • Antonym: immaculate - perfectly clean, neat, or tidy

Etymology

From be- (intensive prefix) + draggle (to make dirty and limp by dragging).

2. ADJ

/ bɪˈdræɡəld / (AmE), / bɪˈdræɡəld / (BrE)
dirty, untidy, and in a very poor condition, especially as a result of neglect or exposure to the elements
dirty and untidy, in poor condition

Examples

After walking through the rain, she arrived home bedraggled and exhausted.
The once-grand theater had become a bedraggled shell of its former self.
The bedraggled kitten meowed softly at the doorstep, hoping for food.

Synset Examples

a street of bedraggled tenements
a broken-down fence
a ramshackle old pier
a tumble-down shack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disheveled - (of people or their appearance) untidy; disordered
  • Antonym: immaculate - perfectly clean, neat, or tidy

Etymology

From Middle English, past participle of 'bedraggle' (to make wet and dirty), from 'be-' + 'draggle'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00682984-s
Synset POS: s
