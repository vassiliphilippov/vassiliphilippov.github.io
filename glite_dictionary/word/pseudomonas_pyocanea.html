Pseudomonas pyocanea

1. NOUN

/ suːˌdoʊməˈnæs paɪoʊˈkeɪniə / (AmE), / sjuːˌdəʊməˈnas paɪəʊˈkeɪnɪə / (BrE)
a species of aerobic bacteria belonging to the genus Pseudomonas, known for its blue-green pigment production
species of aerobic bacteria
medical

Examples

Pseudomonas pyocanea is studied for its ability to produce distinctive blue-green pigments.
Researchers isolated Pseudomonas pyocanea from the contaminated water sample.
Infections caused by Pseudomonas pyocanea are rare but can be challenging to treat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pseudomonas aeruginosa - a related species of Pseudomonas bacteria, often pathogenic

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Pseudomonas' from Greek 'pseudo' (false) + 'monas' (unit), and 'pyocanea' from Greek 'pyon' (pus) + 'kyanos' (blue).
