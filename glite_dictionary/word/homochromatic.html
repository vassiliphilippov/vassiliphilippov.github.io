homochromatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhoʊmoʊkroʊˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌhəʊməʊkrəʊˈmatɪk / (BrE)
(of light or other electromagnetic radiation) having only one wavelength; monochromatic.
having only one wavelength (physics)

Examples

A homochromatic beam is used in experiments that require precise wavelength control.
Only homochromatic light passed through the filter, resulting in a pure spectral color.
Lasers are prized for producing highly homochromatic sources of light.

Synset Examples

monochromatic light

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monochromatic - having or consisting of one color or one wavelength only

Etymology

From Greek 'homo-' (same) + 'chroma' (color) + '-ic'.
