triquetral

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈkwɛtrəl / (AmE), / traɪˈkwɛtrəl / (BrE)
a bone in the wrist that articulates with the pisiform, hamate, and lunate bones
wrist bone next to pisiform and lunate
medical

Examples

A fracture of the triquetral is less common than injuries to other carpal bones.
The doctor pointed to the triquetral on the X-ray to explain the pain in her wrist.
Inflammation near the triquetral can cause discomfort during wrist movement.

Etymology

From Latin triquetrus (three-cornered, triangular), referring to the bone's shape.
