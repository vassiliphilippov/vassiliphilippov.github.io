highboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌbɔːd / (BrE)
A raised diving board, usually positioned at a significant height above the water, used for high diving in swimming pools.
raised diving board for diving

Examples

The diver climbed up to the highboard before her spectacular dive.
Children under a certain age are not allowed on the highboard.
Olympic athletes often train for years to perfect their routines on the highboard.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: springboard - a flexible diving board for lower heights
