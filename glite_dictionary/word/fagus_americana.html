Fagus americana

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪɡəs əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnə / (AmE), / ˈfeɪɡəs əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnə / (BrE)
A North American forest tree with light green leaves and edible nuts, also known as the American beech.
North American forest tree (American beech)

Examples

The dense canopy of the Fagus americana provides shade in many eastern forests.
Squirrels often collect the nuts produced by the Fagus americana in autumn.
Many parks preserve old specimens of Fagus americana for their beauty and ecological value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American beech - common name for Fagus americana

Etymology

From Latin: 'Fagus' (beech) + 'americana' (of America).
