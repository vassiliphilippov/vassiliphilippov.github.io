Seriphidium canum

1. NOUN

/ səˌrɪfɪˈdiːəm ˈkænəm / (AmE), / sɛˌrɪfɪˈdiːəm ˈkeɪnəm / (BrE)
A low, much-branched perennial plant of the western United States, with silvery leaves, important for grazing and as shelter for wildlife.
a western US perennial plant with silvery leaves

Examples

Seriphidium canum provides both food and cover for many animals in the western United States.
Ranchers often rely on Seriphidium canum as an important browse plant for their livestock during dry seasons.
The silver leaves of Seriphidium canum help it blend into the arid landscapes where it thrives.

Etymology

From genus name 'Seriphidium,' related to the sagebrush family. Species name 'canum' is Latin for 'gray' or 'hoary,' referring to the leaf color.
