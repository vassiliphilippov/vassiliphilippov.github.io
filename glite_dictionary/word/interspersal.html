interspersal

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈspɜrsəl / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈspɜːsəl / (BrE)
the act or process of placing things at intervals among other things
the act of scattering things among others

Examples

The interspersal of diagrams throughout the manual made the instructions easier to follow.
Frequent interspersal of jokes kept the audience engaged during the lengthy lecture.
The novel achieved greater realism through the interspersal of regional dialects in the characters’ dialogues.

Synset Examples

the interspersion of illustrations in the text

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interspersion - the action of interspersing or the state of being interspersed

Etymology

From 'intersperse' (from Latin interspersus, past participle of interspergere 'to scatter between') plus '-al'.
