Spirochaetales

1. NOUN

/ spaɪroʊˈkiːteɪliːz / (AmE), / spaɪrə(ʊ)ˈkiːteɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of slender, spiral-shaped bacteria distinguished by their flexible, rod-like form
an order of spiral-shaped bacteria
medical

Examples

Several important human pathogens belong to the order Spirochaetales.
The Spirochaetales include bacteria responsible for diseases such as syphilis and Lyme disease.
Under a microscope, members of the Spirochaetales are recognizable by their flexible, spiral shape.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'speira' (coil) and 'chaite' (hair), referencing their spiral shape.
