volva

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɑlvə / (AmE), / ˈvɒlvə / (BrE)
a cuplike structure at the base of the stalk of some fungi, especially mushrooms, that remains after the universal veil bursts
cuplike base of certain mushrooms

Examples

The poisonous mushroom was easily identified by the large volva at its base.
Careful excavation revealed a delicate volva surrounding the stem underground.
Mycologists warn foragers to check for a volva when identifying wild mushrooms.

Etymology

From Latin *volva* (cover, wrapping), related to *volvere* (to roll).
