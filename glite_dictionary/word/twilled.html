twilled

1. ADJ

/ twɪld / (AmE), / twɪld / (BrE)
(of fabric) woven with a pattern of raised diagonal parallel lines or ribs.
having diagonal raised lines (fabric)

Examples

The twilled fabric gave the jacket a subtle textured appearance.
She chose a twilled wool for her winter coat because of its durability.
The tailor recommended a twilled cotton for making trousers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twill - a fabric with a pattern of diagonal parallel ribs

Etymology

From 'twill', which comes from Old English 'twilic', meaning 'woven with a double thread'.
