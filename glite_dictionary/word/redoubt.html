redoubt

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈdaʊt / (AmE), / rɪˈdaʊt / (BrE)
A temporary or supplementary small fort, usually square or polygonal and lacking flanking defenses, used especially in military contexts.
temporary military fortification

Examples

The soldiers quickly constructed a redoubt to defend their position overnight.
During the siege, the old redoubt provided shelter for the outnumbered defenders.
Archaeologists uncovered the remains of a centuries-old redoubt on the battlefield.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fort - a fortified building or military stronghold
  • Synonym: bastion - a projecting part of a fortification

Etymology

From French 'redoute', from Italian 'ridotto', from Medieval Latin 'reductus' (a place of refuge).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈdaʊt / (AmE), / rɪˈdaʊt / (BrE)
a protected place or small fortified enclosure, often used as a defensive stronghold or place of refuge, especially in military contexts
a small defensive fort or stronghold

Examples

The soldiers took shelter in the redoubt when the enemy approached.
The hilltop redoubt guarded the only route into the valley.
Even after the city fell, a small redoubt continued to resist attack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fortress - a large fortified building or place
  • Synonym: citadel - a stronghold protecting or dominating a city

Etymology

From French 'redoute', from Italian 'ridotto' meaning 'a place of retreat', from Medieval Latin 'reductus'.
