halitosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌhælɪˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhælɪˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A medical term for unpleasant or offensive breath.
medical term for bad breath
medical

Examples

Despite his perfect dental hygiene, Mark suffered from chronic halitosis.
The dentist explained that certain foods can exacerbate halitosis.
Many people are embarrassed to discuss their halitosis with others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bad breath - unpleasant odor from the mouth

Etymology

From Latin 'halitus' (breath) + Greek suffix '-osis' (condition).
