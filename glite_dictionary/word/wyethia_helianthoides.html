Wyethia helianthoides

1. NOUN

/ waɪˈiːθiə ˌhiːliˌænθɔɪˈdiːz / (AmE), / waɪˈiːθɪə ˌhiːliˌænθɔɪˈdiːz / (BrE)
A species of plant in the aster family native to the northwestern United States, with basal leaves, hairy stems, and solitary flower heads with white or pale cream-colored rays.
a plant species of the northwestern U.S. with pale flower heads

Examples

The meadows of Idaho were filled with Wyethia helianthoides blooming in late spring.
Botanists documented Wyethia helianthoides as part of the native flora in Washington State.
When hiking in the mountains, we spotted several clumps of Wyethia helianthoides along the trail.

Etymology

Genus 'Wyethia' named after Nathaniel Wyeth, American explorer; species from Greek 'helios' (sun) and 'anthos' (flower), indicating sunflower-like flowers.
