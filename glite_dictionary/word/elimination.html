elimination

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɪməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌlɪmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which waste matter is discharged from the body, especially through urine or feces
the discharge of bodily waste
medical

Examples

The doctor inquired about her regular elimination habits as part of the physical examination.
Proper hydration plays a key role in healthy elimination.
Digestive disorders can interfere with normal elimination of waste from the body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excretion - the process of eliminating waste products from the body

Etymology

From Latin eliminatio (removal), from eliminare (to turn out of doors, expel).

4. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɪməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌlɪmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of removing an unknown variable from a set of mathematical equations by combining them in a way that one variable is cancelled out
removal of a variable in equations

Examples

The method of elimination is commonly used to solve systems of linear equations.
By applying elimination, the algebra students reduced the equations to a single unknown.
Mathematicians often prefer elimination over substitution when dealing with multiple variables.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: substitution - the process of replacing one variable with equivalent expressions in equations

Etymology

From Latin eliminatio (removal, turning out of doors) via eliminate (‘put out, expel’).

5. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɪməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌlɪmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of systematically considering alternative possibilities and rejecting those that are unacceptable to reach a conclusion
process of rejecting alternatives to reach a conclusion

Examples

The detectives used the process of elimination to narrow down the list of suspects.
Through careful elimination of every other option, she arrived at the correct answer.
In competitive quizzes, success often depends on logical elimination rather than memorization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exclusion - the act of keeping something out or the state of being excluded
  • Synonym: deduction - the process of reaching a conclusion based on available facts and logical reasoning

Etymology

From Latin eliminatio (a turning out of doors, expulsion), from eliminare (to turn out of doors).
