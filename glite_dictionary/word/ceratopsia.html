Ceratopsia

1. NOUN

/ sɛrəˈtɒpsiə / (AmE), / sɛrəˈtɒpsiə / (BrE)
a group of herbivorous dinosaurs marked by prominent horns and frills, including Triceratops and its relatives
group of horned dinosaurs

Examples

The Ceratopsia included some of the most recognizable dinosaurs such as Triceratops and Styracosaurus.
Scientists discovered a new member of the Ceratopsia in western Canada.
Fossils of Ceratopsia reveal a spectacular variety of horn arrangements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Triceratops - a large, horned ceratopsian dinosaur

Etymology

From Greek keras (horn) and ops (face), referring to their horned faces.

2. NOUN

/ sɛrəˈtɑpsiə / (AmE), / sɛrəˈtɒpsiə / (BrE)
a suborder of herbivorous dinosaurs known for their large frills and facial horns, including Triceratops and related genera
horned dinosaur suborder

Examples

The suborder Ceratopsia includes dinosaurs such as Triceratops and Styracosaurus.
Paleontologists discovered a new Ceratopsia fossil in the Gobi Desert.
Large frills and facial horns are characteristic features of Ceratopsia dinosaurs.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Triceratops - a well-known ceratopsian dinosaur with three horns
  • Degree Lower: Psittacosaurus - a primitive member of Ceratopsia, lacking large horns

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek keras (horn) and ops (face).
