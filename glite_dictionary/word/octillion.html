octillion

1. NOUN

/ ɑkˈtɪljən / (AmE), / ɒkˈtɪljən / (BrE)
the number represented as 1 followed by 27 zeros (in the short scale, chiefly used in the United States and modern British English), equal to 10^27.
the number 10^27 (1 with 27 zeros)

Examples

A single octillion is a number so large it can barely be imagined.
Scientists estimate there are more than a hundred octillion atoms in a drop of water.
If you tried counting to octillion, it would take trillions of lifetimes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: nonillion - the number represented as 1 with 30 zeros (short scale)
  • Degree Lower: septillion - the number represented as 1 with 24 zeros (short scale)

Etymology

From Latin 'octo' (eight) + '-illion' (patterned after million, billion, etc.).
