babiroussa

1. NOUN

/ ˌbæbɪˈruːsə / (AmE), / ˌbæbɪˈruːsə / (BrE)
A large wild pig native to Indonesia, notable for its long, upward-curving canine teeth.
Indonesian wild pig with curved tusks

Examples

The babiroussa is famous for its remarkable tusks that curve backwards towards its forehead.
We saw a babiroussa at the wildlife park during our trip to Indonesia.
Researchers are studying the habitat loss faced by the babiroussa in Sulawesi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babirusa - Alternative spelling for the same animal, a wild pig with curved tusks from Indonesia

Etymology

From Malay *babi rusa*, meaning 'deer pig'.
