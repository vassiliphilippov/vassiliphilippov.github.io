Pa

1. NOUN

/ pi eɪ / (AmE), / pɑː / (BrE)
the chemical symbol for protactinium, a short-lived radioactive metallic element formed from uranium and decaying into actinium and then into lead
chemical symbol for protactinium

Examples

The element Pa has a high atomic number and is highly radioactive.
Scientists isolated Pa for the first time in 1917.
Samples of Pa must be handled carefully due to their radioactivity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protactinium - a short-lived radioactive metallic element, symbol Pa

Etymology

Abbreviation of protactinium, from Greek 'protos' (first) and 'actinium', as it occurs before actinium in the radioactive decay series.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpæskəl / (AmE), / ˈpaskəl / (BrE)
a unit of pressure equal to one newton per square meter
unit of pressure (pascal)

Examples

The tire pressure is specified in Pa on the label inside the driver's door.
Atmospheric pressure at sea level is about 101,325 Pa.
This material can withstand pressures up to 500,000 Pa without deforming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pascal - the SI unit of pressure equal to one newton per square meter

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'pascal', named after Blaise Pascal, French mathematician and physicist.
