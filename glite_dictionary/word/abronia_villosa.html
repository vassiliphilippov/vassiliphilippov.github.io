Abronia villosa

1. NOUN

/ əˈbroʊniə vɪˈloʊsə / (AmE), / əˈbrəʊniə vɪˈləʊsə / (BrE)
A soft-haired, sticky plant with bright pink, trumpet-shaped flowers, native to sandy deserts of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.
desert plant with pink trumpet-shaped flowers

Examples

Abronia villosa often creates a stunning pink carpet across the desert after a rainy season.
Researchers observed the unique pollination habits of Abronia villosa in the Mojave Desert.
Gardeners interested in native desert plants sometimes cultivate Abronia villosa for its bright flowers.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining genus name Abronia and Latin villosus (hairy)
