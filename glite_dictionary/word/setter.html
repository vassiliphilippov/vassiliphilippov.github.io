setter

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛtər / (AmE), / ˈsetə / (BrE)
a person who sets written material into type for printing
person who sets text for printing

Examples

The setter worked late to prepare the newspaper for the next morning's edition.
During the era of manual printing, a setter needed a great deal of precision and skill.
Modern publishing rarely uses a setter, as much of the work is now digital.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: typesetter - a person who arranges type for printing text
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09969425-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛtə / (BrE)
a breed of long-haired dog once trained to crouch when finding game, but now trained to indicate its location by standing still and pointing
a breed of hunting dog

Examples

The setter moved gracefully through the tall grass, nose to the ground.
She adopted a beautiful Irish setter from the local animal shelter.
During the hunt, the setter froze and pointed to alert the hunters to nearby game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pointer - a breed of hunting dog that indicates game by pointing
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02103051-n
Synset POS: n
