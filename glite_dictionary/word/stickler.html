stickler

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪklɚ / (AmE), / ˈstɪklə / (BrE)
A person who insists on a certain quality or type of behavior, especially uncompromisingly or pedantically.
person who insists on rules or standards

Examples

My manager is a real stickler for meeting deadlines and following procedures.
She's such a stickler for detail that she double-checks everything twice.
As a stickler for grammar, he couldn't ignore even the smallest typo.

Synset Examples

a stickler for promptness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pedant - a person who is excessively concerned with minor details or rules

Etymology

From Middle English *stikeler* (referee), related to 'stickle' (to mediate or referee); root uncertain.
