shield-shaped

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃild ˌʃeɪpt / (AmE), / ˈʃiːld ˌʃeɪpt / (BrE)
(of a leaf) rounded with the stem attached near the center of the lower surface, rather than at the edge, resembling a shield.
leaf with central stem attachment

Examples

The nasturtium's shield-shaped leaves are distinctive and easily recognized.
Researchers noted that several aquatic plants have shield-shaped foliage for optimal sunlight exposure.
The botanist described the new species as having shield-shaped leaves, similar to those of water lilies.

Etymology

From 'shield' (Old English 'scield', a protective device) and 'shaped', describing resemblance to the form of a shield.
