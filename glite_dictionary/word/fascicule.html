fascicule

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæsɪˌkjuːl / (AmE), / ˈfæsɪkjuːl / (BrE)
An individual part or installment of a printed work, issued separately as one in a series of volumes or sections.
installment of a printed work

Examples

The dictionary was published over several years, each fascicule containing a portion of the alphabet.
She eagerly awaited the arrival of the next fascicule to continue her research.
Many academic journals release their volumes as a series of fascicules throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fascicle - another form meaning a separately published part of a book or other printed work

Etymology

From French 'fascicule', from Latin 'fasciculus' (a little bundle).
