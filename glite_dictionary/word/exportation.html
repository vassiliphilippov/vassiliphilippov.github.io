exportation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkspɔrˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛkspɔːˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of sending goods or services to another country for sale
sending goods abroad for sale

Examples

The exportation of wheat increased significantly last year due to favorable trade agreements.
Strict regulations govern the exportation of medical equipment to certain countries.
His company specializes in the exportation of luxury automobiles to Asia.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: importation - the act of bringing goods or services into a country from abroad
  • Synonym: export - to send goods or services to another country for sale

Etymology

From Latin 'exportatio' (carrying away), from 'ex-' (out) + 'portare' (to carry).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛkspɔrˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛkspɔːˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or business of sending goods or services to another country for sale
sending goods abroad for sale

Examples

The government imposed strict regulations on the exportation of weapons.
The exportation of agricultural products is a key part of the nation's economy.
A global demand for textiles has led to increased exportation from local factories.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: importation - the act of bringing goods into a country for sale
  • Synonym: export - to send goods to another country for sale; the act of exporting

Etymology

From Latin exportatio (a carrying out), from exportare (to carry out).
