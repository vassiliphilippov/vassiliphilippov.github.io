al-Qaeda

1. PROPN

/ ælˈkaɪdə / (AmE), / ælˈkaɪdə / (BrE)
A radical Islamist terrorist organization active in many countries, known for organizing and supporting attacks against the United States and its allies.
Islamist terrorist organization

Examples

The United States increased security measures in response to threats from al-Qaeda.
Intelligence agencies tracked the movements of al-Qaeda operatives across multiple countries.
The founding of al-Qaeda in the late 1980s marked a new era in international terrorism.

Etymology

From Arabic القاعدة‎ (al-qāʿida), meaning 'the base' or 'the foundation.'
