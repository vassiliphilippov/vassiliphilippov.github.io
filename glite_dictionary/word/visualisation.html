visualisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɪʒuələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɪʒuəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of forming a mental image of something, especially as an aid to understanding or achieving it
formation of a mental image

Examples

Through regular visualisation, athletes can mentally rehearse their performance before a competition.
Her visualisation of the finished painting helped guide each brushstroke.
Meditation practices often use visualisation techniques to promote relaxation and focus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imagination - the faculty of forming new ideas or images not present to the senses
  • Synonym: visualization - US spelling of visualisation; formation of mental images

Etymology

From Latin 'visualis' (of sight), with '-ation' forming a noun, influenced by 'visualize'.
