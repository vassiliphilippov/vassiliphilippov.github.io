androgeny

1. NOUN

/ ænˈdrɑdʒəni / (AmE), / ænˈdrɒdʒəni / (BrE)
A form of male parthenogenesis in which the embryo develops with only paternal chromosomes because the egg nucleus does not contribute to fertilization.
embryo development from only paternal chromosomes
medical

Examples

Some rare plants can reproduce through androgeny, resulting in offspring with only paternal genes.
Researchers observed androgeny occurring in the laboratory during controlled fertilization experiments.
Cases of androgeny provide insight into genetic inheritance patterns in certain species.

Etymology

Formed from Greek 'andr-' (man) and '-geny' (origin or production).
