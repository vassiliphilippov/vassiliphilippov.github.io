peaceable

1. ADJ

/ ˈpisəbəl / (AmE), / ˈpiːsəbəl / (BrE)
Not involving or inclined to conflict, violence, or war; calm and undisturbed.
calm, not involving conflict or war

Examples

The new leader was determined to maintain a peaceable relationship with neighboring countries.
They enjoyed a peaceable afternoon by the lake, far from the city's noise.
She has a naturally peaceable temperament and avoids arguments whenever possible.

Synset Examples

a peaceful nation
peaceful times
a far from peaceful Christmas
peaceful sleep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peaceful - free from disturbance; tranquil
  • Antonym: hostile - unfriendly; antagonistic

Etymology

From Old French paisible, from pais (peace), from Latin pax (peace).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpiːsəbəl / (AmE), / ˈpiːsəbl / (BrE)
inclined or disposed to avoid conflict or promote peace; not seeking confrontation
inclined to peace; non-confrontational

Examples

The two neighbors reached a peaceable agreement after a long discussion.
She was known for her peaceable nature, avoiding arguments whenever possible.
Despite the tense situation, his peaceable attitude helped calm everyone down.

Synset Examples

they met in a peaceable spirit
peace-loving citizens

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peaceful - free from disturbance; tranquil
  • Antonym: aggressive - ready or likely to attack or confront

Etymology

From Old French paisible, based on 'peace'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈpiːsəbəl / (AmE), / ˈpiːsəbəl / (BrE)
inclined to avoid conflict or disturbance; tending to be calm and peaceful
inclined to avoid conflict

Examples

Despite the loud argument next door, she maintained a peaceable attitude throughout the evening.
The town is known for its peaceable community that rarely experiences conflict.
We hope for a peaceable solution to the dispute rather than resorting to aggression.

Synset Examples

the pacific temper seeks to settle disputes on grounds of justice rather than by force
a quiet and peaceable person
in a peaceable and orderly manner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peaceful - free from disturbance; calm
  • Antonym: hostile - unfriendly; antagonistic

Etymology

From Middle English *pesible*, from Old French *paisible*, from *pais* (peace).
