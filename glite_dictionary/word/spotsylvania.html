Spotsylvania

1. PROPN

/ spɑtsəlˈveɪniə / (AmE), / spɒtsəlˈveɪniə / (BrE)
a village in northeastern Virginia, United States, notable for being the site of major battles during the American Civil War
village in Virginia; American Civil War battle site

Examples

The Battle of Spotsylvania Court House was one of the bloodiest of the Civil War.
Many history enthusiasts visit Spotsylvania each year to tour the battlefield sites.
Her ancestors fought at Spotsylvania, leaving behind letters that tell their story.

2. PROPN

/ spɑtsəlˈveɪniə / (AmE), / spɒtsəlˈveɪniə / (BrE)
a major battle between the Union and Confederate armies, led by Grant and Lee, during the American Civil War's Wilderness Campaign in May 1864
Civil War battle in May 1864

Examples

The Battle of Spotsylvania was one of the bloodiest conflicts of the American Civil War.
Historians often study troop movements at Spotsylvania to understand military strategies of the era.
Many soldiers lost their lives at Spotsylvania, which lasted nearly two weeks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gettysburg - another major Civil War battle in Pennsylvania

Etymology

From Spotsylvania County, Virginia, named after Alexander Spotswood, colonial governor of Virginia.
