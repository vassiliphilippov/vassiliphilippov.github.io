diplomat

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪpləˌmæt / (AmE), / ˈdɪpləmæt / (BrE)
an official person whose job is to represent their country abroad and manage international relations, especially through negotiation
official engaged in international negotiations

Examples

She worked as a diplomat and represented her country in international meetings.
After years of service, he became the senior diplomat at the embassy.
Being a successful diplomat requires tact, patience, and exceptional communication skills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambassador - a diplomatic official of the highest rank sent by one country as its long-term representative to another

Etymology

From French 'diplomate', from Greek 'diplōma' (folded paper, official document).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪpləˌmæt / (AmE), / ˈdɪpləmæt / (BrE)
a person skilled in managing negotiations or maintaining good relations, especially by dealing tactfully with others
person skilled in handling negotiations

Examples

She proved to be a skilled diplomat during the tense business negotiations.
It takes a true diplomat to calm angry parties and find a compromise.
His role as a diplomat helped foster understanding between the two organizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: envoy - a person sent on a diplomatic mission
  • Synonym: mediator - a person who intervenes to resolve conflicts
  • Antonym: antagonist - a person who actively opposes or is hostile

Etymology

From French *diplomate*, from New Latin *diplomata* (diplomat).
