tastebud

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪstˌbʌd / (AmE), / ˈteɪstˌbʌd / (BrE)
A small sensory organ on the surface of the tongue that detects taste.
sensory organ for taste on the tongue
medical

Examples

When you have a cold, your tastebud sensitivity can decrease, making food seem bland.
Each tastebud contains cells that respond to sweet, salty, sour, bitter, and umami flavors.
Burning your tongue can temporarily damage a tastebud, but it usually heals quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: papilla - small rounded protuberance on the tongue containing taste buds

Etymology

From 'taste' + 'bud', referring to its function and small, bud-like shape.
