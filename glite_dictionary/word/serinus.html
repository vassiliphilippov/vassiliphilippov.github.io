Serinus

1. NOUN

/ səˈraɪnəs / (AmE), / səˈriːnəs / (BrE)
A genus of small finches native to Europe, Africa, and Asia, including canaries and serins.
genus of Old World finches

Examples

The genus Serinus contains several species popular in aviculture, such as the canary.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a rare Serinus in the park.
Distinctive songs are one of the key features of many Serinus species.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Latin 'serinus' (canary).
