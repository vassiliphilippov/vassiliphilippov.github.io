anti-semitic

1. ADJ

/ ˌænti səˈmɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌænti səˈmɪtɪk / (BrE)
Showing or involving hostility, prejudice, or discrimination against Jewish people.
hostile or prejudiced against Jews

Examples

The organization was criticized for its anti-semitic remarks.
Many schools teach about the dangers of anti-semitic beliefs.
Several anti-semitic incidents were reported in the city last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antisemitism - hostility to or prejudice against Jews
  • Antonym: philo-semitic - exhibiting appreciation or support for Jews

Etymology

Formed from 'anti-' (against) + 'Semitic' (relating to the Semites, especially Jews).
