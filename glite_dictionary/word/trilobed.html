trilobed

1. ADJ

/ ˈtraɪˌloʊbd / (AmE), / ˈtrʌɪˌləʊbd / (BrE)
(of a leaf or similar object) divided into three distinct lobes.
divided into three lobes (esp. leaves)

Examples

The oak tree has trilobed leaves that are easy to identify in the fall.
A trilobed petal structure sets this flower apart from other varieties.
Scientists studied the trilobed fruit, noting its unusual symmetry.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: lobed - having lobes; divided into rounded or pointed parts

Etymology

From Latin 'tri-' (three) + 'lobus' (lobe).
