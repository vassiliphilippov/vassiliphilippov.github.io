Agastache mexicana

1. NOUN

/ əˌgæˈstɑːki mɛkˈsɪkənə / (AmE), / əˌgæˈstæki mɛkˈsɪkənə / (BrE)
An erect perennial herb native to Mexico, known for its rose to crimson flowers; often cultivated for ornamental use.
Mexican perennial herb with crimson flowers

Examples

Agastache mexicana thrives in sunny gardens and attracts hummingbirds with its vibrant crimson flowers.
The herbal tea was made from dried leaves of Agastache mexicana harvested in late summer.
Many gardeners value Agastache mexicana for its long blooming period and aromatic foliage.

Etymology

Agastache from Greek 'aga' (very much) and 'stachys' (ear of grain); 'mexicana' refers to its origin in Mexico.
