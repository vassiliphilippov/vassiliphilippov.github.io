Plethodon cinereus

1. NOUN

/ ˌplɛθəˌdɑn sɪˈnɪəriəs / (AmE), / ˌpliːθəˌdɒn sɪˈnɪəriəs / (BrE)
A small salamander species native to eastern North America, commonly known as the red-backed salamander.
species of salamander in eastern North America

Examples

The Plethodon cinereus is often found hiding under rocks in moist forests.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Plethodon cinereus to understand its role in the ecosystem.
The distinctive red stripe makes Plethodon cinereus easy to identify among other salamanders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red-backed salamander - Common name for Plethodon cinereus

Etymology

From New Latin: Plethodon (genus name), from Greek plēthōn (full); cinereus from Latin cinereus (ashy, ash-grey).
