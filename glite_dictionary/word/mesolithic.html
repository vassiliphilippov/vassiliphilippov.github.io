Mesolithic

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛzəˈlɪθɪk / (AmE), / ˌmiːzəˈlɪθɪk / (BrE)
the middle period of the Stone Age, between the Paleolithic and Neolithic, beginning about 15,000 years ago
middle Stone Age period

Examples

The Mesolithic period saw the development of more advanced stone tools.
Archaeologists discovered Mesolithic artifacts near the ancient riverbank.
People of the Mesolithic era often lived as hunter-gatherers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Paleolithic - the early phase of the Stone Age
  • Degree Higher: Neolithic - the later part of the Stone Age

Etymology

From Greek mesos (middle) + lithos (stone), referring to the middle stone age.
