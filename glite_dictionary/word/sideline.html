sideline

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a line that marks the side boundary of a playing field, court, or pitch
line marking side of playing field

Examples

The ball went out of bounds over the sideline.
Fans crowded near the sideline to watch the players up close.
He was standing just off the sideline giving instructions to the team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: touchline - the line marking the side boundary of a field, especially in soccer or rugby

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a secondary line of business, merchandise, or activity pursued in addition to a main one
secondary line of business or merchandise

Examples

The shop carries a sideline of handmade jewelry in addition to its usual clothing range.
Many teachers have a sideline in private tutoring to supplement their income.
Their bakery started offering gluten-free cakes as a successful sideline.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to mainline - the principal or primary line of products or activity
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04221938-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪd.laɪn / (AmE), / ˈsaɪd.laɪn / (BrE)
an activity that someone engages in in addition to their main occupation, especially to earn extra income
secondary job or occupation

Examples

She started a sideline selling handmade jewelry online.
Writing freelance articles is just a sideline for him; his main work is teaching.
Many teachers have a sideline to help supplement their income.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: main job - a person's primary occupation
  • Synonym: moonlighting - having a second job in addition to one's regular employment

Etymology

From the phrase 'side line', originally referring to lines on a sports field, later extended to secondary activities.

4. VERB

/ ˈsaɪdˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌlaɪn / (BrE)
to remove someone from the center of activity or importance, or to place them in a position with less influence or involvement
remove from main activity or importance

Examples

She was sidelined after her disagreement with management.
The injuries sidelined several key players for the rest of the season.
The new CEO quickly sidelined his predecessor's closest advisers.

Synset Examples

The outspoken cabinet member was sidelined by the President

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to marginalize - to treat someone or something as unimportant or powerless
  • Synonym: to exclude - to deliberately leave someone out
  • Antonym: to promote - to move someone to a more important or active role

Etymology

Based on the noun 'sideline', originally meaning the boundary line on a playing field, figuratively extended to mean being removed from the main action.
