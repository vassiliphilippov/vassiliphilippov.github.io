normative

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɔːrmətɪv / (AmE), / ˈnɔːmətɪv / (BrE)
establishing, relating to, or deriving from a standard or norm, especially of behavior or conduct
relating to standards or rules

Examples

The report adopts a normative approach, recommending specific policies for ethical conduct.
Many philosophical theories make normative claims about how people ought to behave.
His argument distinguishes between normative and descriptive statements.

Synset Examples

prescriptive grammar is concerned with norms of or rules for correct usage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prescriptive - giving rules or setting norms
  • Antonym: descriptive - describing how things are, not prescribing how they should be

Etymology

From Late Latin 'normativus' (pertaining to a rule).

2. ADJ

/ ˈnɔːrmətɪv / (AmE), / ˈnɔːmətɪv / (BrE)
relating to or establishing standards or norms, especially of behavior; prescribing what ought to be rather than describing what is.
relating to standards or norms

Examples

The committee developed normative guidelines for ethical research practices.
In sociology, a normative approach considers what should be valued by a society.
Her thesis contrasts normative theories of justice with empirical observations.

Synset Examples

normative discipline
normative samples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: descriptive - serving or seeking to describe rather than prescribe

Etymology

From Latin 'norma' (a carpenter’s square, rule) + '-ive'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03007747-a
Synset POS: a
