motorway

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊtərˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈməʊtəweɪ / (BrE)
A wide road specially built for fast traffic, with two or more lanes in each direction and limited access points.
broad high-speed road
british

Examples

The new motorway has reduced travel time between the two cities by almost half.
Heavy traffic was reported on the motorway due to an accident near the junction.
They prefer to take the scenic route rather than driving on the motorway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: highway - a main road, especially in the US, for travel by the public
  • Synonym: freeway - a wide road for fast traffic, especially in the US, with no intersections

Etymology

Formed from 'motor' and 'way', first used in the early 20th century as motor vehicle travel became common.
