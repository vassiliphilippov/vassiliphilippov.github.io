auriscope

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɪˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈɔːrɪskəʊp / (BrE)
a medical instrument with a magnifying lens and light, used to examine the external ear, especially the eardrum
instrument for examining the ear
medical

Examples

The doctor used an auriscope to check the patient’s ear for infection.
Pediatricians often rely on an auriscope to examine children complaining of ear pain.
After sterilizing the auriscope, the nurse handed it to the physician for the next examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: otoscope - a medical instrument for examining the ear, especially the eardrum

Etymology

From Latin 'auris' (ear) and Greek 'skopein' (to look or examine).
