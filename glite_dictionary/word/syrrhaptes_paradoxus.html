Syrrhaptes paradoxus

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈræptiz pəˈræd.ɑk.səs / (AmE), / sɪˈraptiːz pəˈræd.ɒks.əs / (BrE)
A Eurasian species of sandgrouse with a distinctive black patch on its belly, commonly called Pallas's sandgrouse.
Eurasian sandgrouse species with black belly patch

Examples

The sudden arrival of hundreds of Syrrhaptes paradoxus in Britain caused a sensation among birdwatchers.
Wildlife photographers traveled to Mongolia to capture images of the elusive Syrrhaptes paradoxus.
The distinctive black belly patch helps researchers quickly identify Syrrhaptes paradoxus in the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pallas's sandgrouse - Common name for Syrrhaptes paradoxus, a Eurasian sandgrouse species

Etymology

From New Latin, genus Syrrhaptes + species paradoxus (Latin paradoxus, 'strange, unexpected').
