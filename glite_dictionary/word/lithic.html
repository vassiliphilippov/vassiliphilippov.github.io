lithic

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɪθɪk / (AmE), / ˈlɪθɪk / (BrE)
relating to or made of stone, especially in reference to archaeological strata or tools.
relating to or made of stone

Examples

The archaeologist uncovered a lithic implement during the excavation.
This layer is characterized by a high concentration of lithic fragments.
The museum's exhibit displays many lithic artifacts used by early humans.

Synset Examples

lithic sandstone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stone - hard, solid, nonmetallic mineral matter
  • Antonym: metallic - relating to or resembling metal

Etymology

From Greek lithos (stone).
