W. C. Fields

1. PROPN

/ ˌdʌbljuː si ˈfiːldz / (AmE), / ˌdʌbljuː siː ˈfiːldz / (BrE)
(1880–1946) An American comedian and film actor known for his distinctive nasal voice, witty sarcasm, and roles in many classic comedies.
American comedian and film actor (1880–1946)

Examples

W. C. Fields was renowned for his dry wit and distinctive voice in early American cinema.
Many fans consider W. C. Fields one of the greatest comedians of all time.
In the 1930s, W. C. Fields starred in a series of influential comedy films.
