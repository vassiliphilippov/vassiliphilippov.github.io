sewing

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity or process of joining or attaching things by stitches, especially using a needle and thread
joining by stitches, especially with needle and thread

Examples

She spent the afternoon sewing a new dress for her daughter.
His grandmother taught him the basics of sewing when he was a child.
The tear in my shirt was barely noticeable after some careful sewing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stitching - the act of making stitches

Etymology

Derived from the verb 'sew,' from Old English 'siwian,' related to Old High German 'siuwan.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
A piece of needlework being worked on with needle and thread; the specific item or project currently being made by sewing.
piece of needlework being worked on

Examples

She spent the afternoon working on her sewing by the window.
After dinner, she carefully folded her sewing and set it aside for the night.
A small basket held all her sewing in neat, organized piles.

Synset Examples

she put her sewing back in the basket

Related Terms

  • Synonym: needlework - work consisting of sewing, embroidery, etc.
  • Degree Higher: embroidery - decorative needlework

Etymology

From the verb 'sew', from Old English *siwan*, from Proto-Germanic *siwjan*.
