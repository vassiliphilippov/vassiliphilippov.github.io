concavo-convex

1. ADJ

/ kənˌkeɪvoʊ kənˈvɛks / (AmE), / kɒnˌkeɪvəʊ kɒnˈvɛks / (BrE)
Having one side that is concave and the other convex, with the concave curve being more pronounced than the convex.
concave on one side, convex on the other

Examples

The concavo-convex lens focused the light into a precise point.
Microscope slides may use concavo-convex shapes to achieve special magnification effects.
The engineer selected a concavo-convex mirror for the optical system.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: biconvex - having both sides convex, as a lens
  • Antonym: biconcave - having both sides concave, as a lens

Etymology

From Latin concavus (hollow) + convexus (arched, vaulted).
