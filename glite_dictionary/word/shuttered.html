shuttered

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃʌtərd / (AmE), / ˈʃʌtəd / (BrE)
(of a window, building, or structure) fitted with shutters or having shutters closed.
fitted with shutters

Examples

The store remained shuttered for months after the storm.
She admired the quaint, shuttered windows of the cottage.
At night, the mansion's shuttered rooms looked mysterious and uninviting.

Synset Examples

a church with a shuttered belfry and spire
green-shuttered cottages

Related Terms

  • Synonym: closed - not open; shut
  • Antonym: open - not shut or closed

Etymology

From the verb 'shutter' (to provide or close with shutters) + -ed.
