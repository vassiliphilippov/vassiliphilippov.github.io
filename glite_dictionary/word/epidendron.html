epidendron

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈdɛndrən / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdɛndrən / (BrE)
Any of various orchids belonging to the genus Epidendrum, typically found in tropical and subtropical America.
an orchid of the genus Epidendrum

Examples

The epidendron is prized by collectors for its vibrant flowers and unusual growth habits.
Several species of epidendron thrive in the humid rainforests of Central America.
She studied the unique pollination mechanism of the epidendron in her botany class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epidendrum - a genus of tropical American orchids

Etymology

From Greek epi- (upon) + dendron (tree), referring to the plant's habit of growing on trees.
