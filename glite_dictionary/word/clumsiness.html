clumsiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈklʌmzinəs / (AmE), / ˈklʌmzɪnəs / (BrE)
lack of grace or coordination, often resulting in awkward or inelegant movements, sometimes due to embarrassment or nervousness.
lack of grace or coordination

Examples

His clumsiness was obvious when he knocked over the vase in front of everyone.
She tried to hide her clumsiness during the dance performance, but her nervousness made it worse.
Despite his clumsiness, everyone found his awkward charm endearing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awkwardness - the quality of being awkward or not graceful
  • Antonym: grace - elegance and smoothness of movement

Etymology

From clumsy + -ness, with clumsy from Scandinavian origin (cf. Swedish klumsa, 'to stumble')

2. NOUN

/ ˈklʌmzinɪs / (AmE), / ˈklʌmzinɪs / (BrE)
the quality of moving or behaving in an awkward or lacking grace, resulting in ungainly or inelegant movement or posture
awkwardness or lack of grace in movement

Examples

Her clumsiness was evident when she tripped over the rug during the meeting.
Despite his clumsiness, he managed to carry the stack of dishes without dropping any.
People often mistake nervousness for clumsiness in social situations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awkwardness - the state of being awkward, especially physically

Etymology

From 'clumsy' (awkward) + '-ness' (state or quality).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈklʌmzinəs / (AmE), / ˈklʌmzɪnɪs / (BrE)
Unskillfulness or lack of ability, often resulting from insufficient training or practice.
lack of skill or ability

Examples

His clumsiness in handling the delicate equipment resulted in several broken pieces.
Despite her clumsiness, she managed to improve with practice.
The chef laughed at his apprentice's clumsiness while chopping vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awkwardness - the quality of being awkward or lacking grace or skill
  • Antonym: dexterity - skill in performing tasks, especially with the hands

Etymology

From 'clumsy' (awkward, unskillful) + '-ness' (noun suffix).
