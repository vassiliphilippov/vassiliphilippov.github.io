repulsorlift

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈpʌlsərˌlɪft / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌlsəˌlɪft / (BrE)
A fictional anti-gravity propulsion device, especially as featured in the Star Wars universe, allowing vehicles or objects to float or move above the ground.
fictional anti-gravity device (Star Wars)

Examples

The landspeeder hovered above the desert thanks to its repulsorlift system.
Engineers speculated how a real repulsorlift might function outside of science fiction.
Without its repulsorlift, the droid could no longer float across the hangar.

Etymology

Formed from 'repulsor' (from 'repulse') and 'lift', coined within the Star Wars franchise.
