to bespatter

1. VERB

/ bɪˈspætər / (AmE), / bɪˈspætə / (BrE)
to splash or soil (something) with drops or spots of liquid, mud, or other substances.
splash or soil with spots of liquid

Examples

The passing car bespattered her coat with muddy water.
His reputation was bespattered by false accusations in the press.
Raindrops bespattered the windowpane as the storm rolled in.

Synset Examples

The baby spattered the bib with food

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spatter - to scatter small drops of liquid or other substances
  • Synonym: soil - to make dirty, especially with mud or liquid
  • Antonym: clean - to make something free from dirt

Etymology

From be- (intensive prefix) + spatter, late Middle English.

2. VERB

/ bɪˈspætɚ / (AmE), / bɪˈspætə / (BrE)
to splash or soil something by scattering drops of liquid or other substances onto it
splash or soil with drops

Examples

The passing car bespattered the pedestrians with dirty rainwater.
His white shirt was bespattered with flecks of paint after the art class.
Cooking without a lid can bespatter the stove with oil.

Synset Examples

He didn’t notice how the mud would bespatter his clothes.
The artist’s brush began to bespatter paint on the canvas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spatter - to splash small drops of liquid onto something

Etymology

From Middle English bespatren, from be- (intensive prefix) + spatter (to splash).
