hemoglobinemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːməˌɡloʊbɪˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˌɡləʊbɪˈniːmiə / (BrE)
The presence of an abnormally high concentration of hemoglobin in the blood plasma.
excess hemoglobin in blood plasma
medical

Examples

The laboratory tests confirmed hemoglobinemia after the patient exhibited symptoms of acute hemolysis.
Doctors monitor for hemoglobinemia in patients who have undergone major surgery.
Severe hemoglobinemia can indicate extensive red blood cell breakdown.

Etymology

From hemoglobin + -emia (from Greek haima 'blood').
