to tumefy

1. VERB

/ ˈtuːməˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈtjuːmɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
To swell or cause to swell abnormally, especially as a result of inflammation or injury.
swell abnormally; become enlarged
medical

Examples

The tissue began to tumefy after the infection set in.
If untreated, the injury may tumefy and cause discomfort.
Her ankle started to tumefy within hours of the sprain.

Synset Examples

The bellies of the starving children are swelling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swell - increase in size, usually as a result of injury or illness

Etymology

From Latin tumefacere, from tumere (to swell) + facere (to make).

2. VERB

/ ˈtuːməˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈtjuːmɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
to cause to become swollen; to make or become puffed up or enlarged, typically as a result of inflammation or injury
cause to become swollen
medical

Examples

The insect's sting can tumefy the skin, causing discomfort and redness.
Doctors warned that the allergic reaction might tumefy his lips within minutes.
Extreme heat can tumefy the delicate tissue after minor surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swell - to become larger or rounder in size, typically as a result of internal pressure

Etymology

From Latin 'tumefacere', from 'tumēre' (to swell) + 'facere' (to make).
