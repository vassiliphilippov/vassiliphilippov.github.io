Dentaria

1. NOUN

/ dɛnˈtɛriə / (AmE), / dɛnˈtɛəriə / (BrE)
a small genus of perennial herbs in the mustard family, sometimes classified as a separate genus but usually included in Cardamine.
a plant genus similar to Cardamine

Examples

Many botanists consider Dentaria a synonym of Cardamine in modern plant classifications.
The spring wildflower known as cutleaf toothwort belongs to the genus Dentaria.
Some species formerly classified under Dentaria have been reassigned to Cardamine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cardamine - a large genus of plants in the mustard family, including species sometimes placed in Dentaria

Etymology

From Latin 'dentaria', meaning 'of or pertaining to teeth', referring to the tooth-like divisions of the rootstock.

2. NOUN

/ dɛnˈtɛəriə / (AmE), / dɛnˈtɛːrɪə / (BrE)
a genus of perennial herbs in the mustard family, sometimes classified separately but usually included in the genus Cardamine.
genus of perennial herbs (mustard family)

Examples

Some botanists include Dentaria in the larger genus Cardamine.
The woodland wildflower known as toothwort was once classified as Dentaria.
Recent studies suggest that Dentaria should be kept as a distinct genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cardamine - a genus of flowering plants in the mustard family, often including Dentaria

Etymology

From Latin 'dentaria', from 'dens' (tooth), referring to the toothed root or leaves.
