Notonectidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌnoʊtəˈnɛktɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌnəʊtəˈnɛktɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of aquatic insects, commonly called backswimmers, that are carnivorous and swim on their backs.
a family of aquatic carnivorous insects (backswimmers)

Examples

Scientists observed several Notonectidae hunting mosquito larvae in the pond.
The distinctive swimming style of Notonectidae makes them easy to spot in freshwater habitats.
Members of the Notonectidae family are important predators in aquatic ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backswimmer - common name for members of Notonectidae

Etymology

From New Latin: Notonecta (type genus) + -idae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌnoʊtəˈnɛktɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌnɒtəˈnɛktɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of aquatic, carnivorous insects known as backswimmers, characterized by their habit of swimming upside down.
family of aquatic carnivorous insects (backswimmers)

Examples

The pond was teeming with insects from the Notonectidae family, darting beneath the surface.
Notonectidae are known for their unique ability to swim on their backs while hunting prey.
Scientists collected several specimens of Notonectidae to study their predatory behavior in freshwater ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backswimmer - common name for insects in the family Notonectidae

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Notonectidae, based on genus Notonecta ('back swimmer') + -idae (taxonomic suffix for animal families).
