liquefied

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɪkwəˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪfaɪd / (BrE)
(of a substance) that has been made into or changed into a liquid form, often by heating or applying pressure.
turned into liquid form

Examples

The plant stores enormous quantities of liquefied natural gas for export.
Oxygen can be liquefied under low temperatures and high pressure.
She studied a sample of liquefied hydrogen in the laboratory.

Synset Examples

liquefied petroleum gas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liquid - a substance that flows freely but is of constant volume
  • Antonym: solidified - having become solid

Etymology

From 'liquefy' + -ed, from Latin 'liquefacere' (to make liquid).

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɪkwəˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪfaɪd / (BrE)
Turned into a liquid, especially by heating.
turned into liquid by heating

Examples

The tanker was filled with liquefied natural gas for shipment overseas.
Under high heat, the metal became liquefied and began to flow.
During the experiment, the chemist carefully handled the liquefied sulfur.

Synset Examples

a mass of molten rock

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solidified - made or become solid

Etymology

From the verb 'liquefy', from Latin 'liquefacere' (to make liquid), from 'liquere' (to be liquid) + 'facere' (to make).
