luxury

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌkʃəri / (AmE), / ˈlʌkʃəri / (BrE)
A state of great comfort or elegance, often involving expensive or unnecessary items beyond the basic necessities of life.
expensive comfort or indulgence

Examples

For many, owning a private yacht is considered a true luxury.
A hot bath after a long day feels like the ultimate luxury.
Traveling first class is a luxury I can rarely afford.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: necessity - something that is required or indispensable
  • Synonym: extravagance - spending more than is necessary or reasonable

Etymology

From Latin 'luxuria' (excess, extravagance).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlʌɡʒəri, ˈlʌkʃəri / (AmE), / ˈlʌkʃəri, ˈlʌɡʒəri / (BrE)
the state or quality of great comfort and extravagant living, especially when expensive or not necessary
state of great comfort; extravagance

Examples

For many people, owning a private jet is the ultimate luxury.
The hotel room was the height of luxury, with silk sheets and a marble bathroom.
Fine dining in Paris is a luxury few can afford regularly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opulence - great wealth or luxuriousness
  • Antonym: poverty - the state of being extremely poor

Etymology

From Latin luxuria (excess, extravagance), from luxus (excess).

3. NOUN

/ ˈlʌkʃəri / (AmE), / ˈlʌkʃəri / (BrE)
great wealth and comfort, especially as shown by expensive and sumptuous living; a state of extravagant ease or opulence.
state of great wealth and comfort

Examples

After years of hard work, they now live in luxury on their private estate.
For many people in the world, clean running water is still a luxury.
The hotel offers a level of luxury that is hard to find elsewhere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opulence - great wealth or luxuriousness
  • Antonym: poverty - the state of being extremely poor

Etymology

From Latin 'luxuria' (excess, extravagance), from 'luxus' (excess).
