nubby

1. ADJ

/ ˈnʌbi / (AmE), / ˈnʌbi / (BrE)
(of fabric or yarn) having a rough or knobbly surface; characterized by small lumps or tufts.
rough or knobbly-textured (especially fabric)

Examples

She wore a nubby scarf that felt warm against her neck.
The designer preferred nubby fabrics for their tactile appeal.
A nubby wool blanket draped over the chair added a cozy touch to the room.

Synset Examples

a sweater knitted of nubbly homespun yarns

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; opposite of rough or knobbly
