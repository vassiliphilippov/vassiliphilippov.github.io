Bos banteng

1. NOUN

/ boʊs ˈbɑːn.teɪŋ / (AmE), / bɒs ˈbæn.teŋ / (BrE)
A species of wild ox native to Southeast Asia, especially the Malay Archipelago, known for its sturdy build and distinctive markings.
wild ox species of Southeast Asia

Examples

The Bos banteng is considered endangered due to habitat loss and hunting.
Researchers observed a small herd of Bos banteng grazing in the protected reserve.
The Bos banteng plays an important role in the local ecosystem of the Malay Archipelago.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banteng - Another term for Bos banteng, a wild ox species of Southeast Asia

Etymology

From Latin 'Bos' (ox) and Malay 'banteng' (wild cattle).
