interspersion

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈspɜːrʒən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈspɜːʃn / (BrE)
the act of placing or combining things at intervals among other things
act of mixing things at intervals

Examples

The interspersion of photographs throughout the book made it much more engaging.
We admired the interspersion of colorful flowers in the otherwise green landscape.
Her speech was made lively by the interspersion of humorous anecdotes.

Synset Examples

the interspersion of illustrations in the text

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intermingle - to mix or blend together

Etymology

From Latin 'interspersus', past participle of 'interspere' (to strew among).
