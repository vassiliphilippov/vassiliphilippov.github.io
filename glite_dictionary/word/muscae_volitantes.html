muscae volitantes

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌski voʊlɪˈtæntiz / (AmE), / ˌmʌskaɪ vɒlɪˈtɑːnteɪz / (BrE)
spots or threadlike shapes seen drifting in one's field of vision, caused by tiny deposits or cell fragments in the vitreous humor of the eye
floaters; spots seen in vision
medical

Examples

After staring at the bright sky, Jane noticed several muscae volitantes drifting across her vision.
Some people worry when they first see muscae volitantes, but they are usually harmless.
The doctor explained that the muscae volitantes were caused by tiny particles in the eye's vitreous humor.

Synset Examples

floaters seem to drift through the field of vision

Related Terms

  • Synonym: floater - a small spot or thread seen drifting in the eye, often called muscae volitantes

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'flying flies.'
