churchman

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɝːtʃ.mən / (AmE), / ˈtʃɜːtʃ.mən / (BrE)
a male member of the clergy or a man in a position within a Christian church
male member of the clergy

Examples

The churchman delivered a heartfelt sermon on forgiveness.
As a respected churchman, he was often consulted on matters of faith.
In medieval times, a churchman often held great influence over the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clergyman - a male member of the clergy; synonym of churchman
  • Antonym: layman - a non-ordained member of the church; antonym of churchman

Etymology

From 'church' + 'man', attested since the 16th century.
