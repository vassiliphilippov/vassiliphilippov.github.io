pumpkin

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌmp.kɪn / (AmE), / ˈpʌm.pɪn / (BrE)
a large, round, typically deep-yellow or orange fruit of the squash family, maturing in late summer or early autumn, often used as food and decoration
large round yellow-orange squash fruit

Examples

She carved a pumpkin for Halloween and placed it on the porch.
A thick pumpkin soup is perfect for cold autumn nights.
The field was filled with ripe pumpkins ready for harvest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squash - edible fruit belonging to the same plant family as pumpkin

Etymology

From earlier 'pompion', from Middle French 'pompon', from Latin 'peponem' (melon).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpʌmp.kɪn / (AmE), / ˈpʌm.pɪn / (BrE)
A large, round, orange fruit with a thick rind and many seeds, or the plant that produces it, widely cultivated for food and decoration.
large round orange fruit (and plant)

Examples

We carved a pumpkin for Halloween and put a candle inside.
She made a delicious pumpkin pie for Thanksgiving dinner.
A large pumpkin grew in their garden all summer long.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squash - edible fruit or plant of the Cucurbita genus, related to pumpkin

Etymology

From earlier 'pumpion', from Middle French 'pompon', ultimately from Greek 'pepon' (melon).
