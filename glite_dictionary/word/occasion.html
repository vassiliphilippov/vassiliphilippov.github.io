occasion

1. NOUN

/ əˈkeɪʒən / (AmE), / əˈkeɪʒən / (BrE)
a formal or official social gathering or ceremony, often marking a particular event or celebration
formal social gathering or ceremony

Examples

The anniversary dinner was a grand occasion attended by friends and family.
She wore her most elegant dress for the special occasion.
The award ceremony proved to be a memorable occasion for all involved.

Synset Examples

the party was quite an affair
an occasion arranged to honor the president
a seemingly endless round of social functions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: event - a planned public or social occasion
  • Synonym: celebration - a social gathering to mark a special occasion

Etymology

From Old French 'ocasion', from Latin 'occasio' (opportunity, occasion).

2. NOUN

/ əˈkeɪʒən / (AmE), / əˈkeɪʒən / (BrE)
a particular time when something happens or an opportunity to do something
an opportunity or chance for something

Examples

I never had an occasion to visit that part of the city before.
She waited for the right occasion to bring up the topic.
There wasn't an occasion to use my new skills during the project.

Synset Examples

there was never an occasion for her to demonstrate her skill

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opportunity - a set of circumstances that makes it possible to do something

Etymology

From Old French 'ocasion', from Latin 'occāsio' (a falling together, suitable time).

5. VERB

/ əˈkeɪʒən / (AmE), / əˈkeɪʒ(ə)n / (BrE)
to cause something to happen; to give rise to or bring about (an event or situation)
to cause or bring about (verb)

Examples

The delay was occasioned by heavy rainfall in the area.
His careless remark occasioned an awkward silence in the room.
The new policy occasioned much debate among community leaders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cause - to make something happen, especially something bad
  • Synonym: to provoke - to cause a particular reaction or feeling, often undesired

Etymology

From Old French 'ocasion', from Latin 'occasio' (a falling, happening, occasion), related to 'occidere' (to fall down, happen).
