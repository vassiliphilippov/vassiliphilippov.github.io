Pleurotus

1. NOUN

/ pluˈroʊtəs / (AmE), / plʊˈrəʊtəs / (BrE)
A genus of mushrooms with white spores, typically having caps with an eccentric stem; includes species such as the oyster mushroom.
genus of mushrooms, includes oyster mushroom
medical

Examples

The Pleurotus genus includes several edible mushrooms popular in many cuisines worldwide.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Pleurotus under different environmental conditions.
In Japan, Pleurotus species are cultivated for their nutritional and medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oyster mushroom - an edible mushroom, Pleurotus ostreatus

Etymology

From Greek 'pleura' (side, rib) and '-otus' (having ears), referring to the shape of the cap and its attachment.

2. NOUN

/ pluːˈroʊtəs / (AmE), / plʊəˈrəʊtəs / (BrE)
A genus of edible mushrooms with white spores and caps, often having an off-center stem; includes species such as the oyster mushroom.
a genus of edible mushrooms

Examples

Many chefs prefer to cook with Pleurotus species because of their delicate flavor and tender texture.
In biology class, we studied the life cycle of fungi in the Pleurotus genus.
Farmers in Japan cultivate Pleurotus for both local consumption and export markets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oyster mushroom - an edible mushroom from the genus Pleurotus

Etymology

From Greek pleura (side) and ous, ōtos (ear), referring to the shape of the mushroom cap.
