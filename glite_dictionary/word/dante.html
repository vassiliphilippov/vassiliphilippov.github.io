Dante

1. PROPN

/ ˈdɑn.teɪ / (AmE), / ˈdæn.teɪ / (BrE)
An Italian poet (1265–1321), best known for his epic poem the Divine Comedy, which describes a journey through Hell, Purgatory, and Paradise.
Italian poet, author of the Divine Comedy

Examples

Many scholars consider Dante one of the greatest poets of the Middle Ages.
The themes of Dante's Divine Comedy have influenced countless writers and artists.
When studying Italian literature, Dante is an essential figure to explore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Divine Comedy - Epic poem by Dante describing a journey through Hell, Purgatory, and Heaven.

Etymology

From Italian Dante (short for Durante), referring to Dante Alighieri.
