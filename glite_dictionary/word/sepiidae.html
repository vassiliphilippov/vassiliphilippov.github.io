Sepiidae

1. NOUN

/ səˈpaɪ.iˌdi / (AmE), / sɛˈpɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine mollusks commonly known as true cuttlefish, characterized by their internal cuttlebone and ability to rapidly change color.
family of true cuttlefish

Examples

The Sepiidae family includes many species of cuttlefish known for their intelligence and camouflage abilities.
Researchers studied the behavior of Sepiidae in response to environmental changes.
Fossil records show that Sepiidae have existed for millions of years in ocean habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuttlefish - marine mollusk with an internal cuttlebone; member of Sepiidae

Etymology

From modern Latin Sepiidae, based on Greek sēpía (cuttlefish).
