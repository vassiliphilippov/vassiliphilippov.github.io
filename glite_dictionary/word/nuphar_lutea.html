Nuphar lutea

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuː.fɑr ˈluː.ti.ə / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.fɑː ˈluː.tɪ.ə / (BrE)
A species of water lily with broad, floating leaves and large yellow flowers, commonly found in ponds and slow-moving waters in Europe and parts of Asia.
yellow-flowered water lily species

Examples

A patch of Nuphar lutea brightened the surface of the pond with its vibrant yellow blossoms.
Many aquatic birds find shelter among the thick leaves of Nuphar lutea.
The scientist identified the plant as Nuphar lutea during the field survey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water lily - aquatic plant with floating leaves and showy flowers

Etymology

From Latin; 'Nuphar' from Arabic 'nūfar' (water lily), 'lutea' meaning 'yellow' in Latin.
