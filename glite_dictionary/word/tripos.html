tripos

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪ.pɑs / (AmE), / ˈtraɪ.pɒs / (BrE)
any of the final honors degree examinations at Cambridge University, especially as they pertain to different academic subjects
final honors exams at Cambridge University

Examples

She studied Classics and sat the tripos at Cambridge last year.
Earning a first in the mathematical tripos is considered a significant academic achievement.
The tripos system has existed at the University for centuries.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'tripos', meaning 'three-legged stool', originally referring to the stool on which candidates sat for the examination.
