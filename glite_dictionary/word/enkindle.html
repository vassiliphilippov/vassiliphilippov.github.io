to enkindle

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈkɪndl̩ / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɪndl̩ / (BrE)
to arouse or inspire (an emotion, feeling, or response) in someone
arouse or inspire emotions

Examples

The touching speech managed to enkindle hope in the hearts of the audience.
Her generosity enkindled gratitude among all who benefited from her kindness.
A single kind word can sometimes enkindle friendship where there was once only indifference.

Synset Examples

arouse pity
raise a smile
evoke sympathy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to arouse - to evoke or awaken a feeling, emotion, or response
  • Synonym: stir - to provoke or arouse strong feelings
  • Antonym: to quell - to suppress or put an end to (an emotion or feeling)

Etymology

From Old English 'oncyndel', based on 'kindle' (to set alight), ultimately from Old Norse 'kynda'.

2. VERB

/ ɛnˈkɪndəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɪndl / (BrE)
to cause something to start burning; to set alight, often in a vivid or figurative sense
cause to start burning

Examples

The poet's words enkindled a passionate response from the audience.
A single spark can enkindle a forest fire under dry conditions.
As dusk fell, they enkindled a campfire to keep warm.

Synset Examples

The setting sun kindled the sky with oranges and reds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ignite - set on fire or cause to burn
  • Antonym: to extinguish - cause a fire or light to stop burning
  • Synonym: to kindle - to start burning or make something burn

Etymology

From Old English 'on-' (intensive prefix) + 'kindle' (to start a fire).

Source: WordNet
