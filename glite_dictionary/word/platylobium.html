Platylobium

1. NOUN

/ ˌplætɪˈloʊbiəm / (AmE), / ˌplatɪˈləʊbɪəm / (BrE)
A small genus of Australian evergreen shrubs or subshrubs belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.
Australian evergreen leguminous shrub genus

Examples

The Platylobium shrubs added bright color to the Australian bushland.
Botanists discovered several new species within the Platylobium genus.
Gardeners often cultivate Platylobium for its attractive, long-lasting flowers.

Etymology

From Greek 'platys' (broad) and 'lobos' (lobe), referring to the shape of the pod.
