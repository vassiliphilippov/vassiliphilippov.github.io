corkscrew

1. VERB

/ ˈkɔrkˌskru / (AmE), / ˈkɔːkˌskruː / (BrE)
to move or twist in a spiral or zigzag course, like the motion of a corkscrew
move in a spiral or zigzag course

Examples

The fighter jets corkscrewed through the clouds during their complex maneuver.
A trail of smoke corkscrewed skyward from the burning building.
She watched as the river corkscrewed its way through the valley below.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twist - to move in a winding or spiraling course
  • Antonym: to straighten - to make or become straight, lacking curves or bends

Etymology

From the noun 'corkscrew', referring to the tool's spiral motion.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrkˌskru / (AmE), / ˈkɔːk.skruː / (BrE)
a device for pulling corks from bottles, typically consisting of a pointed metal spiral attached to a handle
bottle opener with a metal spiral

Examples

He reached for the corkscrew to open the bottle of wine.
Make sure to pack a corkscrew for the picnic or we won't be able to open anything.
The elegant corkscrew had a wooden handle and a shiny metal spiral.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bottle opener - a device for removing caps from bottles

Etymology

From 'cork' (the material) + 'screw', referring to the spiral mechanism used to remove corks.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03113801-n
Synset POS: n
