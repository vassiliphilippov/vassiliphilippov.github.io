roulette

1. NOUN

/ ruˈlɛt / (AmE), / ruːˈlɛt / (BrE)
a small wheel with sharp teeth, used to make a series of perforations or marks, especially in crafts and bookbinding.
tool for making rows of perforations

Examples

The artisan used a roulette to decorate the edges of the leather cover.
A roulette is essential for creating precise perforations in stamp making.
She rolled the roulette along the paper to make a dotted line.

Etymology

From French 'roulette', diminutive of 'roue' (wheel).

2. NOUN

/ ruːˈlɛt / (AmE), / ruːˈlɛt / (BrE)
A gambling game in which players bet on which numbered section of a revolving wheel a small ball will land.
gambling game with revolving wheel

Examples

He lost a lot of money playing roulette at the casino last night.
Some people prefer blackjack, but others find the excitement of roulette irresistible.
The bright lights and spinning wheel drew a crowd to the roulette table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: casino - a public place where gambling games are played

Etymology

From French 'roulette' meaning 'small wheel', diminutive of 'roule' (wheel, rolling).

3. NOUN

/ ruˈlɛt / (AmE), / ruːˈlɛt / (BrE)
a curve traced by a fixed point on a curve as it rolls without slipping along another curve
curve generated by rolling motion in geometry

Examples

The shape of a perfect gear tooth profile can be derived using the concept of a roulette.
A cycloid is a specific type of roulette generated by a circle rolling along a straight line.
Mathematicians study various forms of roulette curves such as hypocycloids and epicycloids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cycloid - curve traced by a point on a circle as it rolls along a straight line

Etymology

From French *roulette* (little wheel), diminutive of *roule* (wheel), from *rouler* (to roll).
