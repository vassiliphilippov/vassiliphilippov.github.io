to deep-six

1. VERB

/ ˌdipˈsɪks / (AmE), / ˌdiːpˈsɪks / (BrE)
to throw overboard from a boat, especially to get rid of or dispose of something; to discard or eliminate.
discard by throwing overboard
informal slang

Examples

When they upgraded the boat, they decided to deep-six all the old equipment.
The company chose to deep-six its outdated policies in favor of more modern ones.
We had to deep-six those plans after the budget cuts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discard - to get rid of something as no longer useful
  • Antonym: keep - to retain possession of something

Etymology

Originally nautical slang meaning to bury at sea at a depth of six fathoms (36 feet).

2. VERB

/ ˌdiːpˈsɪks / (AmE), / ˌdiːpˈsɪks / (BrE)
to discard, cancel, or dispose of something, especially in a decisive or permanent way
discard or get rid of decisively
informal american

Examples

Facing budget cuts, the company decided to deep-six its plans for a new headquarters.
She threatened to deep-six all the paperwork stacked on his desk if he didn’t organize it soon.
After a disappointing pilot episode, the network deep-sixed the new TV show.

Synset Examples

deep-six these old souvenirs!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discard - to throw away or get rid of something
  • Antonym: keep - to retain or hold onto something

Etymology

Originally U.S. nautical slang, from 'deep six'—the deepest measure on a sounding line, used euphemistically for throwing something overboard.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02227776-v
Synset POS: v
