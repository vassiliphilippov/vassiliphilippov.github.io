costa

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊstə / (AmE), / ˈkɒstə / (BrE)
a riblike structure in a plant or animal, such as the central vein of a leaf or a thickened vein in an insect's wing
riblike structure in plants or animals

Examples

The costa of the insect's wing was easily visible under the microscope.
In botany, the costa refers to the central vein running through the leaf.
Damage to the costa can affect the structural integrity of a plant leaf.

Etymology

From Latin 'costa' (rib, side).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑstə / (AmE), / ˈkɒstə / (BrE)
any of the 12 pairs of curved bones extending from the spine toward or to the sternum in humans; a rib (in anatomy)
anatomical term for a rib
medical

Examples

The seventh costa is classified as a true rib in the human body.
A fractured costa can cause severe chest pain and difficulty breathing.
The anatomy textbook provided detailed diagrams of each costa and their connections to the sternum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rib - curved bone extending from the spine surrounding the chest

Etymology

From Latin costa (rib, side).
