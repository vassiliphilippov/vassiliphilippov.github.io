mastoidale

1. NOUN

/ mæˌstɔɪˈdeɪli / (AmE), / mas.tɔɪˈdeɪ.li / (BrE)
the craniometric point at the lowest point of the mastoid process, used as a reference in anatomical measurements of the skull
craniometric point at lowest mastoid process
medical

Examples

The mastoidale is commonly used as a reference point in cranial measurements for anthropological studies.
During the examination, the doctor located the mastoidale to assess the position of the mastoid process.
Accurate identification of the mastoidale is essential for precise cranial morphometric analysis.

Etymology

From mastoid (referring to the mastoid process of the skull) + Latin suffix -ale, denoting a place or point.
