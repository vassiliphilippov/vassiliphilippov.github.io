office

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑː.fɪs / (AmE), / ˈɒ.fɪs / (BrE)
A room or set of rooms where professional, administrative, or clerical work is conducted.
place for professional or clerical work

Examples

She's looking for a new office closer to the city center.
The lawyer's office was filled with books and legal documents.
He decorated his office with family photos and plants.

Synset Examples

he rented an office in the new building

Related Terms

  • Synonym: workplace - a place where people work
  • Antonym: home - one's place of residence (opposite of workplace)

Etymology

From Latin 'officium' (service, duty).

/ ˈɑː.fɪs / (AmE), / ˈɒf.ɪs / (BrE)

Synset Examples

3. NOUN

/ ˈɑː.fɪs / (AmE), / ˈɒf.ɪs / (BrE)
a religious rite or service prescribed by ecclesiastical authorities, especially as part of a set form of worship
religious rite or service

Examples

The priest performed the morning office with great solemnity.
She attended all the daily offices during Lent.
The office of vespers is one of the most beautiful parts of the liturgy.

Synset Examples

the offices of the mass

Related Terms

  • Synonym: service - a religious ceremony or act of worship
  • Synonym: ceremony - a formal religious or public occasion

Etymology

From Latin 'officium' (service, duty), later used in ecclesiastical Latin for a prescribed religious service.
