Pathan

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtɑn / (AmE), / pəˈtɑːn / (BrE)
a member of an ethnic group speaking Pashto, living mainly in northwestern Pakistan and southeastern Afghanistan
member of a Pashto-speaking ethnic group

Examples

Many Pathan families still uphold their traditional codes of honor and hospitality.
The Pathan tribes have played important roles in the history of the region.
She learned Pashto while living among the Pathan people near Peshawar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pashtun - a member of the Pashto-speaking ethnic group of Afghanistan and Pakistan

Etymology

From Hindi-Urdu पठान (Paṭhān), from Pashto پښتون‎ (Paṣ̌tūn).

2. NOUN

/ pəˈtɑːn / (AmE), / pəˈtɑːn / (BrE)
a member of an ethnic group native to eastern Afghanistan and northwest Pakistan, also known as Pashtuns
member of an ethnic group in Afghanistan/Pakistan

Examples

Many Pathan communities maintain strong traditions of hospitality and honor.
The Pathan tribes have played an important role in the history of Afghanistan.
He traced his ancestry back to a prominent Pathan family.

Synset Examples

Pathans are the predominant ethnic group in Afghanistan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pashtun - member of the Pashtun ethnic group

Etymology

From Hindi/Urdu پٹھان (Paṭhān), likely from Pashto.
