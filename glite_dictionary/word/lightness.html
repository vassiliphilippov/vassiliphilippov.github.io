lightness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having a pale or light color
the state of being a light color

Examples

The lightness of the curtains made the room feel more open and airy.
Painters often use the lightness of a color to create contrast in their work.
He admired the lightness of her hair, which shone in the sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palor - whiteness or paleness of color
  • Antonym: darkness - the quality or state of having a dark color

Etymology

From Old English 'līht', meaning 'not heavy' or 'bright', with the suffix '-ness' forming a noun.
