obliquity

1. NOUN

/ əˈblɪkwəti / (AmE), / əˈblɪkwɪti / (BrE)
the positioning of the fetal head at an abnormal angle during labor
abnormal fetal head angle in labor
medical

Examples

During a difficult delivery, the doctor noticed an obliquity in the baby's head position.
Obstetricians are trained to recognize and manage obliquity during childbirth.
If obliquity is present, additional medical intervention may be necessary.

Etymology

From Latin obliquitas (slant, deviation), from obliquus (slanting).

2. NOUN

/ əˈblɪkwəti / (AmE), / əˈblɪkwɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being morally or intellectually dishonest or misleading
deceptive or dishonest quality

Examples

Some politicians are skilled in obliquity, hiding their true intentions behind clever words.
The novel explores the obliquity of human motives and the complexities of truth.
He was admired for his honesty and shunned for any sign of obliquity in his dealings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dishonesty - lack of honesty or integrity
  • Antonym: straightforwardness - the quality of being honest and direct

Etymology

From Latin obliquitas, from obliquus (slanting, indirect).
