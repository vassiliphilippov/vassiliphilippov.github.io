half-life

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæf ˌlaɪf / (AmE), / ˈhɑːf ˌlaɪf / (BrE)
the time taken for a quantity to decrease to half its original value, especially the time for half the atoms in a radioactive substance to decay.
time for something to halve, esp. radioactive decay

Examples

The half-life of carbon-14 is about 5,730 years.
Scientists measured the half-life of the drug to estimate how long it stays effective in the bloodstream.
Radioactive waste often needs to be stored for thousands of years because of its long half-life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mean life - the average lifetime of a radioactive particle before decay

Etymology

Formed from 'half' + 'life', first used in 1907 in the context of radioactive decay.
