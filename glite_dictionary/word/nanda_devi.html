Nanda Devi

1. PROPN

/ ˈnɑːndə ˈdeɪvi / (AmE), / ˈnændə ˈdiːvi / (BrE)
a mountain in the Himalayas in northern India, standing 25,660 feet (7,816 meters) high; the second highest mountain in India
mountain in the Himalayas, India

Examples

We planned an expedition to climb Nanda Devi during the summer.
Nanda Devi is considered sacred by the local people of Uttarakhand.
Snow covers the peak of Nanda Devi for most of the year, making it a striking sight.

Etymology

From Hindi नन्दा देवी (Nandā Devī), meaning 'Goddess Nanda'.
