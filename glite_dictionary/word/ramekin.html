ramekin

1. NOUN

/ ˈræməkɪn / (AmE), / ˈraməkɪn / (BrE)
a small, round, usually ceramic dish used for baking and serving individual portions of food
small baking dish for individual servings

Examples

She baked chocolate soufflés in a ramekin for each guest.
The chef placed the hot ramekin carefully on the plate.
A custard cooked in a ramekin makes an elegant dessert.

Etymology

From French *ramequin*, possibly of Flemish origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈræm.əkɪn / (AmE), / ˈræm.əkɪn / (BrE)
A small baked dish made with cheese, eggs, and bread crumbs, originally served in an individual heatproof container; also, the dish used to serve it.
small baked cheese dish or its serving vessel

Examples

The chef baked the cheese ramekin until it was golden and puffed.
She served the dessert in a small white ramekin for each guest.
A classic spinach ramekin is perfect as a starter at dinner parties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soufflé - a light, fluffy baked dish made with egg yolks and beaten egg whites

Etymology

From French ramequin, probably of Flemish origin.
