rapist

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪpɪst / (AmE), / ˈreɪpɪst / (BrE)
a person who forces another person to have sexual intercourse without consent, committing the crime of rape
person who commits rape

Examples

The police arrested the rapist after a thorough investigation.
Survivors of rapist attacks often require long-term support and care.
The documentary focused on the rehabilitation of convicted rapists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sexual offender - a person who commits sexual crimes
  • Antonym: victim - person harmed by another’s actions, especially a crime

Etymology

From 'rape' + '-ist', forming an agent noun.
