aphanite

1. NOUN

/ ˈæfəˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈæfəˌnaɪt / (BrE)
A fine-grained, homogeneous igneous rock, such as basalt, in which the mineral crystals are too small to be distinguished with the naked eye.
fine-grained igneous rock with invisible crystals

Examples

Basalt is a common type of aphanite found in volcanic regions.
Under the microscope, aphanite reveals its crystalline structure, which is invisible to the naked eye.
Geologists collected several samples of aphanite for further study in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: phanerite - coarse-grained igneous rock with visible crystals

Etymology

From Greek 'aphanēs' (invisible, unseen), referring to the rock's indistinguishable crystals.
