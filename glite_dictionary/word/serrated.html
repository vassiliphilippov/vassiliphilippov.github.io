serrated

1. ADJ

/ səˈreɪtɪd / (AmE), / səˈreɪtɪd / (BrE)
Having a notched or saw-like edge, with tooth-like projections pointing toward the tip.
having a saw-like notched edge

Examples

The chef used a serrated knife to easily slice through the bread.
Some leaves have serrated edges to deter herbivores from eating them.
The serrated blade made cutting through tough rope much easier.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth - having an even surface without projections or indentations

Etymology

From Latin 'serratus', meaning 'saw-shaped', from 'serra' (saw).
