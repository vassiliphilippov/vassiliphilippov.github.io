goddam

1. ADV

/ ˈɡɑːdæm / (AmE), / ˈɡɒdæm / (BrE)
Used for emphasis, especially to express anger, annoyance, or surprise.
emphatic intensifier, often vulgar
slang vulgar

Examples

I don't give a goddam what you think about my decision.
That was the best goddam burger I've ever had.
He was so late, it made me really goddam angry.

Synset Examples

you are goddamn right!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damn - used for emphasis or to express anger
  • Synonym: goddamn - used for emphasis, expressing strong emotion

Etymology

Shortened form of 'goddamn', from 'God damn'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡɑdˌdæm / (AmE), / ˈɡɒd.dæm / (BrE)
Used informally to express anger or emphasis, especially as an expletive or intensifier.
informal expletive or intensifier
informal vulgar

Examples

Why is there a goddam delay every time I need a cab?
He's just another goddam fool trying to pick a fight.
I lost my goddam keys again this morning.

Synset Examples

he's a blasted idiot
it's a blamed shame
a blame cold winter
not a blessed dime
I'll be damned (or blessed or darned or goddamned) if I'll do any such thing
he's a damn (or goddam or goddamned) fool
a deuced idiot
an infernal nuisance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damn - used to express anger, emphasis, or annoyance
  • Synonym: goddamned - containing or characterized by being damned by God; used informally as an expletive

Etymology

Formed from 'God damn', a mild oath dating to 17th-century English.
