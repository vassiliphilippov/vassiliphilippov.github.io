trapezius

1. NOUN

/ trəˈpiziəs / (AmE), / trəˈpiːzɪəs / (BrE)
either of two large, flat triangular muscles on each side of the upper back and neck, involved in moving the shoulders and supporting the arms
large triangular back and shoulder muscle
medical

Examples

She injured her trapezius while lifting heavy boxes at work.
The massage therapist focused on releasing tension in my trapezius muscles.
Tightness in the trapezius can often lead to neck pain and headaches.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'trapezion' (small table), diminutive of 'trapeza' (table), based on the muscle’s shape.
