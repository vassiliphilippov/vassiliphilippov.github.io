Eurydice

1. PROPN

/ jʊˈrɪdɪsi / (AmE), / jʊəˈrɪdɪsi / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the wife of Orpheus, who died shortly after their wedding and for whom Orpheus descended into the underworld.
wife of Orpheus in Greek mythology

Examples

The myth of Eurydice and Orpheus has inspired countless works of art and literature.
In the story, Orpheus ventures into the underworld to rescue his beloved Eurydice.
Many scholars debate the symbolic meaning of Eurydice's fate in Greek mythology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Orpheus - legendary musician and poet in Greek mythology, husband of Eurydice

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Εὐρυδίκη (Eurydikē), meaning 'wide justice' or 'broad justice'.
