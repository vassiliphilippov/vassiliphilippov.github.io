Sarcoscypha coccinea

1. NOUN

/ sɑr.koʊˈsaɪ.fə kɑːkˈsɪn.i.ə / (AmE), / sɑː.kɒˈsɪ.fə kɒkˈsɪn.i.ə / (BrE)
a species of fungus with saucer-shaped, bright red fruiting bodies and a whitish exterior, often appearing in early spring
a species of fungus known as scarlet elf cup

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with the vivid red cups of Sarcoscypha coccinea after the last frost.
Mycologists collected specimens of Sarcoscypha coccinea to study their role in woodland ecosystems.
The striking appearance of Sarcoscypha coccinea often draws the attention of spring hikers.

Etymology

From Greek 'sarx' (flesh) and 'kyphos' (curved), with 'coccinea' from Latin 'coccineus' (scarlet).
