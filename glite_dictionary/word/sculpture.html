sculpture

1. NOUN

/ ˈskʌlptʃər / (AmE), / ˈskʌlptʃə / (BrE)
a three-dimensional work of art created by shaping or combining materials such as stone, metal, or wood
three-dimensional work of art

Examples

The museum displayed a beautiful sculpture made of marble.
He created a bronze sculpture to honor the city's founder.
Visitors admired the modern sculpture in the center of the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: statue - a carved or cast figure of a person or animal, especially one that is life-size or larger
  • Antonym: painting - a picture made with paint on a surface such as canvas

Etymology

From Latin 'sculptura', from 'sculpere' meaning to carve.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskʌlp.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈskʌlp.tʃə / (BrE)
the art or process of making figures or designs in three dimensions, typically by carving or molding
art of creating three-dimensional figures

Examples

Her passion for sculpture is evident in every piece she creates.
Students studied the history and techniques of sculpture in their art class.
He turned to sculpture after years of working as a painter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sculpt - to create a sculpture
  • Synonym: carving - creating shapes by cutting material away
  • Antonym: painting - art of applying color to a surface

Etymology

From Latin sculptura, from sculpere (to carve).

3. VERB

/ ˈskʌlptʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈskʌlptʃə / (BrE)
to create a figure or object by shaping stone, wood, or another hard material
create by shaping a hard material

Examples

The artist decided to sculpture a magnificent horse from marble.
She learned how to sculpture delicate animals out of wood during her studies.
He sculptured a detailed portrait of his father from a single block of stone.

Synset Examples

sculpt a swan out of a block of ice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sculpt - to shape or carve a figure from a material

Etymology

From Latin sculptura (carving, engraving).

4. VERB

/ ˈskʌlp.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈskʌlp.tʃə / (BrE)
to shape or carve a material such as stone or wood by cutting away parts of it, often to create a work of art
shape or carve material (verb)

Examples

The artist spent months carefully sculpturing the statue from a single block of marble.
Over time, wind and water naturally sculpture the shapes of the rocks along the coastline.
He sculptured a delicate bird out of a piece of driftwood for the exhibition.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to carve - to cut a hard material in order to produce an object, design, or inscription

From Latin 'sculptura', from 'sculpere' (to carve)
