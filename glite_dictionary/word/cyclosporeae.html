Cyclosporeae

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkloʊˈspɔriː / (AmE), / ˌsɪkləʊˈspɔːriː / (BrE)
a historical order of brown algae, now superseded by the order Fucales in more recent scientific classifications
historical order of brown algae (taxonomy)

Examples

The brown algae formerly classified under Cyclosporeae are now placed in the order Fucales.
Researchers often encounter the term Cyclosporeae in older scientific literature on marine botany.
The reclassification of Cyclosporeae reflects advances in our understanding of algal phylogeny.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Fucales - the modern order of brown algae that replaced Cyclosporeae in taxonomy

Etymology

From Greek 'kyklos' (circle) and 'spora' (seed/spore), referring to the round spores of the group.

2. NOUN

/ saɪ.kloʊˈspɔːr.i.iː / (AmE), / saɪ.kləʊˈspɔːr.i.iː / (BrE)
an obsolete class of brown algae, in more recent classifications superseded by the order Fucales
obsolete class of brown algae

Examples

The brown algae once classified as Cyclosporeae are now placed under the order Fucales.
Some historical texts refer to kelp species as members of the class Cyclosporeae.
Biologists no longer use the category Cyclosporeae in modern algal taxonomy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Fucales - an order of brown algae, current classification for species formerly in Cyclosporeae

Etymology

From Greek 'kyklos' (circle) + 'spora' (spore), referring to the round spores produced by these algae.
