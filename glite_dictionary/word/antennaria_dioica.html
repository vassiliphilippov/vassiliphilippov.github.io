Antennaria dioica

1. NOUN

/ ænˌtɛˈnɛr.i.ə daɪˈoʊ.ɪ.kə / (AmE), / anˌtɛnˈnaːrɪə daɪˈəʊ.ɪ.kə / (BrE)
A low-growing perennial herb with leaves covered in whitish down and clusters of small white flowers; commonly known as mountain everlasting or cat's-foot.
species of low-growing perennial herb

Examples

Antennaria dioica is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes across Europe and Asia.
Gardeners appreciate Antennaria dioica for its silvery foliage and delicate white blooms.
The common name 'cat's-foot' refers to the soft, hairy appearance of Antennaria dioica's leaves.

Etymology

From Latin 'antennaria' (referring to the antenna-like pappus of the seeds) and 'dioica' (meaning 'of two houses', indicating separate male and female plants).
