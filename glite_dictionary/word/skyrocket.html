skyrocket

1. NOUN

/ ˈskaɪˌrɑːkɪt / (AmE), / ˈskaɪˌrɒkɪt / (BrE)
A firework that is propelled high into the sky before exploding with a display of light and noise.
firework sent high into the sky

Examples

Children watched in awe as the skyrocket burst into colors above the park.
He lit the fuse and quickly stepped back as the skyrocket shot upwards.
The grand finale included a giant skyrocket that lit up the night sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rocket - a firework propelled through the air that explodes

Etymology

Formed from 'sky' + 'rocket', indicating its flight into the sky.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskaɪˌrɑkɪt / (AmE), / ˈskaɪˌrɒkɪt / (BrE)
a firework that rises high into the air and explodes with a bright display, or a projectile device used to send a line or signal high into the sky
a firework or signal-propelling rocket

Examples

The children watched in awe as a skyrocket burst into a shower of red and gold sparks.
A skyrocket was used to send a lifeline from the shore to the stranded ship.
They set off a skyrocket to celebrate the New Year at midnight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rocket - a cylindrical projectile or engine propelled by combustion gases
  • Synonym: firework - a device that explodes to produce lights and sounds for entertainment

Etymology

From 'sky' + 'rocket', first used in the 18th century for fireworks.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07277721-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈskaɪˌrɑːkɪt / (AmE), / ˈskaɪˌrɒkɪt / (BrE)
to increase very quickly and suddenly to a high level
to rise rapidly and suddenly

Examples

Housing costs in the city have skyrocketed over the last decade.
After the announcement, the company's stock price skyrocketed overnight.
As demand increased, energy prices skyrocketed across the country.

Synset Examples

prices skyrocketed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to soar - to rise rapidly; to increase quickly
  • Antonym: plummet - to fall rapidly and suddenly

Etymology

Derived from the noun 'skyrocket' (a type of firework rocket), reflecting the rapid upward motion.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01947945-v
Synset POS: v
