Cuminum cyminum

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːmɪnəm saɪˈmaɪnəm / (AmE), / ˈkjuːmɪnəm sɪˈmɪnəm / (BrE)
A dwarf annual plant native to the Mediterranean, cultivated for its aromatic seeds used as a spice (cumin).
plant grown for cumin seeds

Examples

Cuminum cyminum is widely cultivated in India for its flavorful seeds.
Many spice blends use seeds harvested from Cuminum cyminum for aroma and taste.
Researchers studied the growth conditions of Cuminum cyminum in different Mediterranean climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cumin - the aromatic seeds of Cuminum cyminum used as a spice

Etymology

From Latin, combining 'Cuminum' (cumin) and 'cyminum' (cumin), both from Greek 'kyminon'.
