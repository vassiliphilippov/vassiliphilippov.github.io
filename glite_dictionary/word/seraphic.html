seraphic

1. ADJ

/ səˈræfɪk / (AmE), / sɪˈræfɪk / (BrE)
Having a sweet, pure, or angelic nature or appearance; resembling a seraph or angel.
angelic or sweet in nature or appearance

Examples

She gave him a seraphic smile that lit up the room.
The baby's seraphic expression melted everyone's heart.
Beneath her seraphic innocence was a sharp intelligence.

Synset Examples

an angelic smile
a cherubic face
looking so seraphic when he slept
a sweet disposition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angelic - resembling or appropriate to an angel; lovely and pure
  • Synonym: cherubic - innocently sweet or childlike, like a cherub

Etymology

From Late Latin 'seraphicus', from 'seraphim', referring to the highest order of angels.

2. ADJ

/ səˈræfɪk / (AmE), / səˈræfɪk / (BrE)
Relating to seraphs or angels of the highest order; characteristic of angelic purity or beauty.
like or relating to the highest order of angels

Examples

The child's seraphic smile filled everyone in the room with peace.
Her singing voice had a seraphic quality that moved the audience to tears.
He gazed at her with a seraphic expression, as if she were an angel descended from heaven.

Synset Examples

he imagined a seraphic presence in the room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angelic - resembling or relating to angels

Etymology

From Medieval Latin seraphicus, from seraphim (the highest order of angels in Christian angelology).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02882150-a
Synset POS: a
