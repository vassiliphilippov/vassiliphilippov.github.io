to overboil

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈbɔɪl / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbɔɪl / (BrE)
(of liquid) to overflow or cause to overflow while boiling.
overflow while boiling

Examples

Be careful not to let the soup overboil or you'll have a mess to clean up.
If you overboil the milk, it will spill onto the stove.
The pot overboiled while I was distracted by a phone call.

Synset Examples

The milk is boiling over

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to boil over - (of liquid) to overflow the sides of its container while boiling

Etymology

From 'over-' (exceeding, beyond) + 'boil' (to reach boiling point).

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊvɚˈbɔɪl / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbɔɪl / (BrE)
to boil something more than necessary, especially so that it is spoiled or loses its intended texture
boil excessively

Examples

Be careful not to overboil the eggs or the yolks will turn gray.
If you overboil vegetables, they can lose their flavor and nutrients.
The soup was ruined because the chef had accidentally overboiled it for too long.

Synset Examples

The peas are overboiled

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undercook - to cook insufficiently
  • Degree Lower: boil - to heat a liquid until it bubbles

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (excessively) + 'boil'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00323520-v
Synset POS: v
