to metastasize

1. VERB

/ məˈtæstəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / mɪˈtæstəsaɪz / (BrE)
(of cancer or disease) to spread from the original site to other parts of the body, often rapidly and uncontrollably.
spread of disease within the body
medical

Examples

Doctors discovered that the tumor had metastasized to her liver.
If left untreated, certain cancers can metastasize very quickly.
The news that his cancer had metastasized was devastating to his family.

Synset Examples

the cancer had metastasized and the patient could not be saved

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spread - to extend or distribute over a larger area or more widely
  • Antonym: remain localized - to stay confined to the original position or site, without spreading

Etymology

From Greek 'metastasis' (removal, migration) + English '-ize' suffix.

2. VERB

/ məˈtæs.təˌsaɪz / (AmE), / mɪˈtæs.tə.saɪz / (BrE)
(of a disease, especially cancer) to spread from one part of the body to another.
spread of disease to other body parts
medical

Examples

Doctors were concerned that the cancer might metastasize if not treated promptly.
When a tumor metastasizes, it can make treatment much more difficult.
Researchers are investigating how cancer cells metastasize to distant organs.

Synset Examples

If left untreated, the cancer could metastasize rapidly.
The tumor began to metastasize to other organs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spread - to move and cover a larger area or affect more people
  • Antonym: to localize - to confine to a particular area or part

Etymology

From Greek metastasis (removal, migration), from meta- (change) + histanai (to place).
