agreeable

1. ADJ

/ əˈɡri.ə.bəl / (AmE), / əˈɡriː.ə.bəl / (BrE)
willing to agree with or accept something; ready to give consent.
willing to agree or consent

Examples

She was agreeable to the idea of meeting next week.
My boss seemed agreeable to changing the project deadline.
If you are agreeable to the terms, please sign the contract.

Synset Examples

agreeable to the plan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consenting - willing to agree or allow something
  • Antonym: unwilling - not willing to do something or allow something to happen

Etymology

From Old French 'agreer' (to please), based on Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'gratus' (pleasing).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00555499-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ əˈɡriəbl̩ / (AmE), / əˈɡriːəbl̩ / (BrE)
pleasant or likable; enjoyable or pleasing to be with or experience
pleasant, likable, pleasing

Examples

The evening was made even more agreeable by good food and lively conversation.
She is such an agreeable colleague that everyone enjoys working with her.
If the weather is agreeable tomorrow, we could have our picnic outside.

Synset Examples

Is the plan agreeable to you?
he's an agreeable fellow
My idea of an agreeable person … is a person who agrees with me
an agreeable manner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pleasant - giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment
  • Antonym: disagreeable - not pleasant or enjoyable; unpleasant

Etymology

From Old French *agreable*, from agreer ‘to please’.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00089736-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ əˈɡriəbəl / (AmE), / əˈɡriːəbl / (BrE)
(formal) in accordance with; consistent or compatible with something else.
in keeping with, compatible

Examples

The company's new policy is agreeable with international regulations.
Her decisions were agreeable with the advice she received.
This interpretation is agreeable with the evidence presented.

Synset Examples

salaries agreeable with current trends
plans conformable with your wishes
expressed views concordant with his background

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conformable - compliant or consistent with something else
  • Synonym: concordant - in agreement or harmony with
  • Antonym: contrary - opposite in nature or in conflict with

Etymology

From Old French *agreable*, from agreer 'to please'.
