nearly

1. ADV

/ ˈnɪrli / (AmE), / ˈnɪəli / (BrE)
In a close or almost exact manner; not completely but very nearly.
almost; closely but not exactly

Examples

He nearly missed the train, but managed to board just in time.
Their solution nearly matches ours, but there are a few minor differences.
She nearly finished the book in one sitting.

Synset Examples

the two phenomena are intimately connected
the person most nearly concerned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: almost - very nearly but not quite
  • Antonym: exactly - completely accurate or correct in every detail

Etymology

From Middle English 'nerly', related to 'near'.

2. ADV

/ ˈnɪrli / (AmE), / ˈnɪəli / (BrE)
Almost but not completely; very close to, but not quite.
almost but not quite

Examples

The storm nearly knocked down the old tree in our yard.
He nearly missed his flight because of the traffic.
I've nearly finished reading the book you lent me.

Synset Examples

the job is (just) about done

we're almost finished
the car all but ran her down

talked for nigh onto 2 hours
the recording is well-nigh perfect
virtually all the parties signed the contract
I was near exhausted by the run
most everyone agrees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: almost - very nearly but not exactly or entirely
  • Synonym: virtually - almost or nearly as described, but not completely or according to strict definition
  • Antonym: completely - entirely; totally

Etymology

From Middle English 'nerely', from 'nere' (near).
