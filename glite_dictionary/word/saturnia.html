Saturnia

1. NOUN

/ səˈtɜːrniə / (AmE), / səˈtɜːniə / (BrE)
A genus of large moths in the family Saturniidae, known as emperor moths.
genus of large moths (emperor moths)

Examples

The Saturnia moth is admired for its striking eye-like patterns on the wings.
Scientists collected several Saturnia specimens during the field study in the forest.
Larvae of Saturnia feed on a wide variety of trees and shrubs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saturniidae - the silk moth family to which Saturnia belongs

Etymology

From Latin Saturnia, after the Roman goddess Saturnia (Juno), named for grandeur or majesty.

2. PROPN

/ səˈtɜːrniə / (AmE), / səˈtɜːniə / (BrE)
A genus of large moths in the family Saturniidae, commonly known as emperor moths.
genus of large emperor moths

Examples

The Saturnia genus includes some of the most visually striking moths in the world.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Saturnia genus in Southeast Asia.
Many Saturnia moths are admired for their large wingspans and colorful eye spots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emperor moth - a large moth of the genus Saturnia, known for its eye-like wing markings

Etymology

From Latin Saturnia, referring to the Roman goddess Saturnia (Juno), possibly for the majestic appearance of the moths.
