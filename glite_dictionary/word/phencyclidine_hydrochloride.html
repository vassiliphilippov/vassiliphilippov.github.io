phencyclidine hydrochloride

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛnsɪˈklaɪdiːn ˌhaɪdrəˈklɔːraɪd / (AmE), / ˌfɛnsɪˈklɪdɪn ˌhaɪdrəˈklɔːraɪd / (BrE)
A synthetic drug used as an anesthetic in veterinary medicine and, illicitly, as a hallucinogen; also known as PCP.
a synthetic anesthetic drug, also PCP
medical

Examples

The substance seized by the police was confirmed to be phencyclidine hydrochloride.
Some patients experienced adverse reactions to phencyclidine hydrochloride during veterinary procedures.
Illicit use of phencyclidine hydrochloride is associated with serious health risks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: PCP - common abbreviation for phencyclidine hydrochloride

Etymology

From phenyl + cyclidine (a chemical ring structure) + hydrochloride (its salt form).
