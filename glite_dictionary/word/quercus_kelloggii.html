Quercus kelloggii

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs kɪˈlɑːɡi.aɪ / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs kɛˈlɒɡi.aɪ / (BrE)
A large deciduous tree native to the Pacific coast of North America, known as the California black oak, with deeply lobed bristle-tipped leaves.
species of California black oak tree

Examples

The dense forests in the Sierra Nevada are home to the majestic Quercus kelloggii.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Quercus kelloggii in various soil types.
The leaves of Quercus kelloggii turn vibrant shades of orange and red in autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: California black oak - Common name of Quercus kelloggii, a tree species native to California and Oregon

Etymology

From Latin 'Quercus' (oak) and named after Albert Kellogg, an American botanist.
