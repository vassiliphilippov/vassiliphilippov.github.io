Iceland

1. PROPN

/ ˈaɪs.lənd / (AmE), / ˈaɪs.lənd / (BrE)
A Nordic island country in the North Atlantic; established as a republic in 1944 after gaining independence from Denmark.
Nordic island country in the North Atlantic

Examples

Tourists travel to Iceland to see the Northern Lights and stunning volcanic landscapes.
After declaring independence in 1944, Iceland became a fully sovereign nation.
The economy of Iceland relies heavily on fishing and renewable energy.

Etymology

From Old Norse Ísland, meaning 'ice land'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈaɪˌslænd / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.slənd / (BrE)
an island country in the North Atlantic Ocean, known for its volcanoes, hot springs, glaciers, and geysers
island country in the North Atlantic

Examples

Many tourists visit Iceland to see the Northern Lights and explore geysers and volcanoes.
The capital of Iceland is Reykjavík, which is known for its vibrant arts scene.
Despite its name, Iceland has a relatively mild climate due to the Gulf Stream.
