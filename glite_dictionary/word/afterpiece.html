afterpiece

1. NOUN

/ ˈæf.tɚ.piːs / (AmE), / ˈɑːf.tə.piːs / (BrE)
A short dramatic performance, typically comic, presented after the main play in a theater to entertain the audience.
short play after the main performance

Examples

The audience stayed for the afterpiece, which was a lighthearted comedy.
During the 18th century, every major playhouse regularly featured an afterpiece to end the evening.
The afterpiece provided comic relief after the tragic main performance.

Etymology

Formed from 'after-' (meaning 'following in time') and 'piece' (a work of art or performance).
