sporophore

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɔr.əˌfɔr / (AmE), / ˈspɔː.rə.fɔː / (BrE)
A branch or organ that bears and produces spores, especially in ferns, mosses, liverworts, and certain fungi.
spore-bearing organ in plants or fungi
medical

Examples

The mushroom's sporophore emerged from the ground after several days of rain.
A fern's sporophore can release thousands of spores into the environment.
Scientists studied the unique shape of the fungus's sporophore under the microscope.

Etymology

From Greek 'spora' (seed, sowing) and 'phoros' (bearing).
