beaver

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiːvər / (AmE), / ˈbiːvə / (BrE)
A full beard that covers the cheeks and chin, especially as worn in former times.
a full beard
archaic

Examples

His thick beaver gave him a distinguished and old-fashioned appearance.
In portraits of early explorers, a beaver was often seen as a sign of maturity.
He prided himself on growing a fine beaver for the winter festivities.

Etymology

From Middle English *bever*, referring to the animal; the association with 'beard' may come from the thick fur of a beaver resembling a full beard.

6. NOUN

/ ˈbiːvər / (AmE), / ˈbiːvə / (BrE)
a large semiaquatic rodent with webbed hind feet and a broad, flat tail, known for building dams and lodges in rivers and streams
large semi-aquatic rodent that builds dams

Examples

The beaver built an impressive dam across the stream near our campsite.
In North America, the beaver population once declined due to the fur trade.
We watched a beaver swim out to collect sticks for its lodge.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: otter - a semiaquatic fish-eating mammal of the weasel family
  • Degree Higher: capybara - the largest living rodent, native to South America

Etymology

From Old English *beofor*, related to Old High German *bibar*, from Proto-Indo-European *bʰébʰrus* (beaver).
