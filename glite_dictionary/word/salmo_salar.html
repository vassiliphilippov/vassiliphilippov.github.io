Salmo salar

1. NOUN

/ ˈsælmoʊ ˈseɪlɑr / (AmE), / ˈsælməʊ ˈseɪlɑː / (BrE)
the scientific name for the Atlantic salmon, a species of fish found in northern coastal Atlantic waters and their tributaries
scientific name for Atlantic salmon

Examples

Fisheries scientists track the migration patterns of Salmo salar to protect its populations.
The decline of Salmo salar in European rivers has raised environmental concerns.
Farmed Salmo salar is a major export for several northern countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Atlantic salmon - common name for Salmo salar, a large migratory fish species

Etymology

From Latin, where 'salmo' means salmon and 'salar' means leaper.
