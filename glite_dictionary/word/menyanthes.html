Menyanthes

1. NOUN

/ mɛnˈjænθiːz / (AmE), / mɛnˈjanθiːz / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the family Menyanthaceae, comprising the bogbean species found in wetlands.
genus of bogbean plants

Examples

The wetland was dominated by Menyanthes and other aquatic plants.
Researchers studied the growth cycle of Menyanthes in different bog habitats.
Menyanthes is recognized for its attractive white flowers and floating leaves.

From Greek menyanthes, a plant mentioned by ancient botanists.

2. NOUN

/ mɛnˈjænθiːz / (AmE), / mɛnˈjanθiːz / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic or marsh plants in the family Menyanthaceae; it includes the bogbean species.
genus of aquatic or marsh plants (bogbean)

Examples

The rare Menyanthes thrives in wetland habitats across northern Europe and North America.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of Menyanthes for potential use in herbal remedies.
The delicate flowers of Menyanthes bloom in late spring along the edges of peat bogs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bogbean - a plant of the species Menyanthes trifoliata, found in wetlands

Etymology

From Greek *Mēnyanthēs*, the name given by Linnaeus, possibly based on Greek roots for 'month' and 'flower'.
