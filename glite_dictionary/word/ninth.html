ninth

1. NOUN

/ naɪnθ / (AmE), / naɪnθ / (BrE)
Each of nine equal parts into which something is divided; one part out of nine.
one of nine equal parts

Examples

She divided the cake into nine pieces and took one ninth for herself.
If you have nine students and one leaves, that's one ninth of the group.
A ninth of the entire budget was allocated to research.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: eighth - one part in eight equal parts
  • Degree Higher: tenth - one part in ten equal parts

Etymology

Formed from "nine" with the ordinal suffix "-th".

2. ADJ

/ naɪnθ / (AmE), / naɪnθ / (BrE)
Coming after the eighth and before the tenth in a sequence; being number nine in order.
number nine in an ordered sequence

Examples

She finished in ninth place during the marathon.
This is the ninth time I've visited that museum.
He was born on the ninth of June.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: eighth - number eight in an ordered sequence
  • Degree Higher: tenth - number ten in an ordered sequence

Etymology

Formed from Middle English "nynthe," from Old English "nigonteoða" (see "nine" + "-th").

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02210918-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ naɪnθ / (AmE), / naɪnθ / (BrE)
The position in a sequence that comes after the eighth and before the tenth; number nine in a series.
position nine in a sequence

Examples

Olivia finished in ninth place out of fifty competitors at the marathon.
The book is divided into chapters, and the ninth chapter discusses climate change.
He was the team's ninth batter in the lineup for that game.

Synset Examples

going into the ninth, they were a run ahead

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: eighth - the position eight in a series
  • Degree Higher: tenth - the position ten in a series

Etymology

Formed from 'nine' + '-th', following the standard formation of ordinal numbers in English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13869912-n
Synset POS: n
