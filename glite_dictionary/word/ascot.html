ascot

1. NOUN

/ ˈæskət / (AmE), / ˈæskət / (BrE)
A type of necktie or cravat with wide square ends, usually fastened with an ornamental pin.
a wide-ended cravat or necktie

Examples

He wore a silk ascot to the wedding for an elegant, vintage look.
The gentleman's ascot was pinned perfectly beneath his collar.
My grandfather still keeps his favorite ascot in the top drawer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cravat - a neckband or scarf, especially a wide one, worn as a necktie

Etymology

Named after the Ascot Racecourse in England, where it was fashionable attire.
