cerebrally

1. ADV

/ səˈriː.brəli / (AmE), / səˈriː.brəli / (BrE)
In an intellectual or mental manner; relating to the intellect rather than emotions or physical aspects.
in an intellectual manner

Examples

She approaches every problem cerebrally, always analyzing before acting.
The book is written cerebrally, with complex arguments and detailed reasoning.
While others reacted emotionally, he responded cerebrally, relying on logic and data.

Synset Examples

cerebrally active

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intellectually - in an intellectual or rational manner

Etymology

From 'cerebral' + '-ly'; 'cerebral' from Latin 'cerebrum' (brain).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00134223-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ səˈriː.brə.li / (AmE), / səˈriː.brəli / (BrE)
In the brain; in a manner relating to or occurring within the brain.
in the brain; relating to the brain

Examples

The injury caused him to bleed cerebrally after the accident.
Some toxins affect the body cerebrally rather than peripherally.
The research focused on how pain is processed cerebrally rather than physically.

Synset Examples

bleeding cerebrally

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cerebral - relating to the brain

Etymology

From 'cerebral', from French cérébral, from Latin cerebrum (brain), with the adverbial suffix '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00134131-r
Synset POS: r
