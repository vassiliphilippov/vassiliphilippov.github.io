abradant

1. NOUN

/ əˈbreɪdənt / (AmE), / əˈbreɪdənt / (BrE)
A tool or material used for wearing down, smoothing, or polishing a surface by friction.
tool for smoothing or polishing surfaces

Examples

The technician used an abradant to smooth the rough edges of the metal piece.
Grinding wheels are a common type of abradant in metalworking shops.
Selecting the right abradant can make polishing surfaces much more efficient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abrasive - a substance used for grinding, polishing, or cleaning a hard surface

Etymology

From Latin abras-, past participle stem of abradere (to scrape off), plus the English suffix -ant.

2. NOUN

/ əˈbreɪdənt / (AmE), / əˈbreɪdənt / (BrE)
A substance used to abrade or wear down surfaces by friction.
substance for abrading surfaces

Examples

The jeweler used an abradant to polish the gemstone to a perfect shine.
Industrial cleaning often involves the use of an abradant to remove surface rust.
Certain abradants can be too harsh and damage delicate materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abrasive - A substance used for grinding, polishing, or cleaning hard surfaces

Etymology

From Latin 'ab' (away) and 'radere' (to scrape), via 'abradere' (to scrape off).
