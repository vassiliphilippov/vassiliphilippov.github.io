wholesomeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊlsəmnəs / (AmE), / ˈhəʊlsəmnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being good for your health or morally beneficial
quality of being healthy or morally good

Examples

The wholesomeness of homemade meals is hard to deny.
She admired the wholesomeness of his lifestyle and values.
Many parents seek out the wholesomeness of rural life for their children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purity - the state of being pure
  • Antonym: corruption - the act of becoming morally bad or impure

Etymology

From wholesome + -ness, with 'wholesome' from Middle English 'holsom', from 'whole' meaning healthy, uninjured.
