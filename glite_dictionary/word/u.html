u

1. NOUN

/ ju / (AmE), / juː / (BrE)
the twenty-first letter of the Roman alphabet; used as a symbol, in spelling, or to represent the spoken sound /juː/ or /ʌ/
twenty-first letter of the Roman alphabet

Examples

The word 'umbrella' begins with the letter u.
In cursive handwriting, u is written with a simple curve.
We learned to pronounce u as both 'you' and 'uh' in class.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: v - the letter immediately preceding 'u' in the Roman alphabet
  • Degree Higher: w - the letter immediately following 'u' in the Roman alphabet

Etymology

From Latin letter U, originating as a variant of V, derived from the Greek upsilon (Υ, υ).
