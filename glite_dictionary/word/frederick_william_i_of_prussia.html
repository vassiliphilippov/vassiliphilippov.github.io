Frederick William I of Prussia

1. PROPN

/ ˈfrɛdərɪk ˈwɪljəm ðə ˈprʌʃə / (AmE), / ˈfrɛdərɪk ˈwɪljəm əv ˈprʌsjə / (BrE)
(1688–1740) King of Prussia from 1713, known for reforming and strengthening the Prussian army and centralizing governmental authority.
King of Prussia (1713–1740), military reformer

Examples

Frederick William I of Prussia is remembered for modernizing the Prussian military and administration.
The reign of Frederick William I of Prussia laid the groundwork for his son's later successes.
Historians often study the reforms initiated by Frederick William I of Prussia to understand Prussian history.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Frederick I of Prussia - king of Prussia, father of Frederick William I
  • Degree Higher: Frederick II of Prussia - son of Frederick William I, also known as Frederick the Great
