interbreeding

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈbriːdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈbriːdɪŋ / (BrE)
the reproduction between individuals from different populations, races, or species, especially those considered distinct
reproduction between different populations or races

Examples

Many biologists study the effects of interbreeding between subspecies in the wild.
The region's diverse populations resulted from centuries of interbreeding.
Concerns about interbreeding have shaped some societies' attitudes toward marriage laws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crossbreeding - breeding animals or plants from different varieties or species
  • Antonym: inbreeding - breeding from closely related individuals

Etymology

From inter- (between) + breed (reproduce).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.tɚˈbriː.dɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈbriː.dɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of breeding together individuals from different species or varieties to produce hybrids, especially in animals or plants
breeding of different species/varieties to create hybrids

Examples

The interbreeding of different dog breeds can produce new traits.
Scientists study the interbreeding of closely related plant species to understand genetic diversity.
Conservationists must monitor interbreeding between wild and captive animal populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hybridization - the process of producing hybrids by cross-breeding
  • Antonym: inbreeding - the mating of closely related individuals

Etymology

Formed from 'inter-' (between) and 'breeding'.
