follicle

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑː.lɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈfɒl.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
a small spherical group of cells containing a cavity, found in various tissues or organs of the body
small group of cells with a cavity
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that a hair follicle is a tiny sac in the skin from which hair grows.
Each ovarian follicle contains an immature egg that develops during the menstrual cycle.
Biologists observed the development of the new follicle under the microscope.

Etymology

From Latin folliculus (a small bag), diminutive of follis (bag).
