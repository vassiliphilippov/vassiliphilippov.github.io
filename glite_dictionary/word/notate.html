to notate

1. VERB

/ ˈnoʊ.teɪt / (AmE), / ˈnəʊ.teɪt / (BrE)
to write something, such as music or dance steps, using a system of notation
write in notation (music, dance, etc.)

Examples

The composer asked her assistant to notate the melody for the entire orchestra.
It is important to notate every step of the new dance routine accurately.
Many musicians notate their improvisations so they can remember them later.

Synset Examples

Nowadays, you can notate an entire ballet; in the old days, the steps had to be memorized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to write - to compose text or symbols on a surface
  • Synonym: record - to set down in writing or other permanent form

Etymology

From Latin nota (mark, sign), via the past participle notatus (marked), related to 'note'.
