to deify

1. VERB

/ ˈdiː.əˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈdiː.ɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to treat or regard someone or something as a god; to worship or idealize excessively
regard as a god; idolize

Examples

Many cultures deify their ancestors and build temples in their honor.
He tends to deify his favorite athletes, overlooking their flaws.
Some people almost deify technology, believing it can solve any problem.

Synset Examples

These young men deify financial success

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to idolize - to admire or revere excessively
  • Antonym: to demonize - to portray as wicked or demonic

Etymology

From Latin 'deificare' (to make a god), from 'deus' (god).

2. VERB

/ ˈdiːəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈdiːɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to treat or regard someone or something as a god; to exalt to divine status
treat as a god; exalt to divine status

Examples

Many ancient cultures would deify their rulers to legitimize their power.
Some fans have a tendency to deify athletes and celebrities, overlooking their flaws.
Throughout history, artists have sometimes been deified for their extraordinary talent and influence.

Synset Examples

the people deified their King

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to idolize - to admire or revere someone or something greatly
  • Antonym: to demonize - to portray someone as wicked or demonic

Etymology

From Latin deificare (to make a god), from deus (god) + facere (to make).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00546220-v
Synset POS: v
