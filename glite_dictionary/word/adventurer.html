adventurer

1. NOUN

/ ədˈvɛn.tʃɚ.ɚ / (AmE), / ədˈvɛn.tʃə.rə / (BrE)
a person who enjoys taking risks and seeking out new, exciting experiences, often in unknown or dangerous situations.
person who seeks adventure or risk

Examples

As an adventurer, Maria traveled alone across the mountains, eager for new challenges.
Every true adventurer enjoys the thrill of exploring uncharted territory.
He lived his life like an adventurer, always taking bold risks for the sake of excitement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daredevil - a person who enjoys doing dangerous things
  • Synonym: risk-taker - a person who is willing to take risks
  • Antonym: cautious person - someone who avoids risks

Etymology

From Old French 'aventureor', from 'aventure' (adventure).

2. NOUN

/ ədˈvɛntʃərər / (AmE), / ədˈvɛntʃərə / (BrE)
a person who travels to unknown or little known places, especially for exploration or scientific discovery
explorer of unknown regions

Examples

The adventurer set out to cross the uncharted desert in search of ancient ruins.
Many famous adventurers have risked their lives to explore the Amazon rainforest.
She dreamed of becoming an adventurer and discovering forgotten lands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explorer - person who investigates unknown places
  • Synonym: pioneer - person who is among the first to explore or settle a new area

Etymology

From French aventurier, from aventure (adventure).
