cosmogony

1. NOUN

/ kɑzˈmɑɡəni / (AmE), / kɒzˈmɒɡəni / (BrE)
the scientific study of the origin and development of the universe, especially as a branch of astrophysics
study of the origin of the universe

Examples

Modern cosmogony seeks to explain how galaxies and stars first formed from primordial matter.
He published several influential papers on cosmogony and the structure of the universe.
Ancient myths often served as early forms of cosmogony before the development of scientific explanations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cosmology - the science of the origin and development of the universe

Etymology

From Greek kosmogonia, from kosmos (world, universe) + -gonia (origin, creation).
