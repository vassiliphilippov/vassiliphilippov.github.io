etiological

1. ADJ

/ ˌiːtiəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌiːtɪəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the philosophical study of causation, or to the causes or origins of phenomena
relating to the study of causation

Examples

Her research focused on the etiological factors that contribute to childhood anxiety disorders.
The philosopher published an etiological analysis of the origins of moral responsibility.
An etiological approach helps identify the root causes behind observed phenomena.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: causal - relating to causation or a cause

Etymology

From Greek *aitiologia* (giving a reason for), from *aitia* (cause) + *-logia* (study).

2. ADJ

/ ˌiːtiəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌiːtɪəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or concerned with the cause or origin of a disease or condition
relating to the cause of a disease
medical

Examples

The researchers are studying the etiological factors contributing to the spread of the virus.
Identifying the etiological agent is crucial for developing effective treatments.
The physician discussed the etiological origin of the patient's symptoms.

Synset Examples

etiological agent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: causal - involving or constituting a cause; causing

Etymology

From Greek 'aitia' (cause) + '-logia' (study of), via New Latin 'ætiologia'.
