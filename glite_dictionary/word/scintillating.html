scintillating

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪntəˌleɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪntɪleɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
brilliantly clever and exciting, especially in conversation or writing.
brilliantly clever or lively

Examples

The professor's scintillating lecture kept the entire audience engaged from start to finish.
She is known for her scintillating sense of humor, which brightens every conversation.
Their scintillating exchange of ideas impressed everyone at the conference.

Synset Examples

scintillating wit
a play full of scintillating dialogue

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement

Etymology

From Latin *scintillare* (to sparkle).

very lively, exciting, and entertaining; brilliantly clever or stimulating.
brilliantly lively or exciting

Examples

His conversation was always scintillating, full of clever observations and lively anecdotes.

3. ADJ

/ ˈsɪn.təlˌeɪ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪn.tɪ.leɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
emitting flashes of light; sparkling or shining brilliantly
sparkling with flashes of light

Examples

The scintillating stars lit up the midnight sky with their brilliant flashes.
Her dress was covered in scintillating sequins that caught the light as she moved.
The diamond's scintillating surface made it the centerpiece of every conversation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dull - lacking brightness; not shiny or sparkling

Etymology

From Latin scintillare (to sparkle), from scintilla (spark).
