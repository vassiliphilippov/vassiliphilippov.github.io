musculus sternocleidomastoideus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌskjələs ˌstɜrnoʊˌklaɪdoʊˌmæsˈtɔɪdiəs / (AmE), / ˈmʌskjʊləs ˌstɜːnəʊˌklaɪdəʊˌmæsˈtɔɪdiːəs / (BrE)
either of the two thick muscles, running from the sternum and clavicle to the mastoid process and occipital bone, that turn the head to the opposite side and help flex or extend the neck
neck muscle turning and flexing the head
medical

Examples

The musculus sternocleidomastoideus is essential for turning and nodding your head.
Injury to the musculus sternocleidomastoideus can cause neck pain and limited movement.
Surgeons must take care not to damage the musculus sternocleidomastoideus during certain procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sternocleidomastoid - the more common English form of the muscle's name

Etymology

From Latin musculus (muscle), combining 'sterno-' (sternum), 'cleido-' (clavicle), and 'mastoideus' (relating to the mastoid process).
