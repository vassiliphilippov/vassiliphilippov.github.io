unsuitable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsuːtəb(ə)l / (AmE), / ʌnˈsuːtəb(ə)l / (BrE)
not appropriate or fitting for a particular purpose, person, or situation; not deserving to be chosen
not appropriate or deserving to be chosen

Examples

She found the apartment unsuitable for raising a family.
His behavior at the dinner was completely unsuitable for the occasion.
They considered him unsuitable as a candidate for the position.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: suitable - appropriate or fitting for a particular purpose or person
  • Synonym: appropriate - suitable or proper in the circumstances

Etymology

From un- (not) + suitable, ultimately from Late Latin *suitabilis* (able to be suited).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsuːtəbl / (AmE), / ʌnˈsuːtəbl / (BrE)
not appropriate or proper for a particular person, purpose, or occasion; not conducive to good moral development or well-being
not appropriate or proper

Examples

The content of this website is unsuitable for young audiences.
He was rejected as an unsuitable candidate for the position due to his lack of experience.
The weather was unsuitable for hiking, so we postponed our trip.

Synset Examples

the movie is unsuitable for children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inappropriate - not suitable or proper in the circumstances
  • Antonym: suitable - right or appropriate for a particular person, purpose, or situation

Etymology

From un- (not) + suitable (from Late Latin *sūtābilis*)

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsuːtəbl̩ / (AmE), / ʌnˈsuːtəbəl / (BrE)
not appropriate or not adapted for a particular purpose or situation; not suitable.
not appropriate or fitting

Examples

This dress is unsuitable for a formal dinner event.
The documentary contained material unsuitable for young children.
He realized the weather was unsuitable for hiking in the mountains.

Synset Examples

a solvent unsuitable for use on wood surfaces

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inappropriate - not suitable or proper in the circumstances
  • Antonym: suitable - right or appropriate for a particular person, purpose, or situation

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' (not) to 'suitable,' which comes from Late Latin *suitabilis* via Old French.
