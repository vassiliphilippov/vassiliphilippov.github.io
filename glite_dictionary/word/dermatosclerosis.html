dermatosclerosis

1. NOUN

/ dɚˌmæt̬oʊsklɚˈoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌdɜːmætəʊsklɪəˈrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A chronic autoimmune disease in which fibrous connective tissue is deposited in the skin, causing hardening and thickening.
autoimmune disease with skin hardening
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with dermatosclerosis after presenting with thickened, tight skin on her hands.
Early detection of dermatosclerosis can improve long-term management of the disease.
Researchers are studying new treatments for dermatosclerosis to slow the progression of skin hardening.

Etymology

From Greek derma (skin) + sclerosis (hardening).
