intense

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtɛns / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛns / (BrE)
(of color) having a very high degree of saturation; vivid or strong in hue.
highly saturated (of color)

Examples

The artist chose an intense blue for the sky in her painting.
Her dress was a striking, intense shade of red that caught everyone's eye.
The intense green of the forest seemed almost unreal in the bright sunlight.

Synset Examples

vivid green
intense blue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vivid - (of color) very bright, distinct, and clear

Etymology

From Latin 'intensus' (stretched, strained, high-strung).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtɛns / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛns / (BrE)
very strong, extreme, or showing a high degree or level of something; existing or felt to a great degree.
very strong or extreme

Examples

The desert was filled with intense heat, making travel difficult.
She felt an intense joy when she heard the good news.
The intense aroma of spices filled the kitchen.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mild - not strong or extreme in degree
  • Degree Lower: moderate - average in amount, intensity, or degree

Etymology

From Latin intensus (stretched, strained), past participle of intendere.

3. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtɛns / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛns / (BrE)
extremely strong, sharp, or severe in degree or quality; very great or acute.
very strong or severe

Examples

The patient complained of intense pain in his lower back after the accident.
She felt an intense burning sensation on her skin after touching the plant.
His intense annoyance was obvious to everyone in the room.

Synset Examples

acute pain
felt acute annoyance
intense itching and burning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acute - sharp or severe in degree; keen
  • Antonym: mild - not severe or strong

Etymology

From Latin intensus (stretched, strained, vehement), past participle of intendere (to stretch out)
