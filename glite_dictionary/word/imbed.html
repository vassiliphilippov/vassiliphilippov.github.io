to imbed

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈbɛd / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɛd / (BrE)
to fix or set something firmly and deeply into a surrounding mass
fix or insert deeply

Examples

The archaeologist carefully imbedded the fragile artifact in protective foam.
Tiny shards of glass were imbedded in his skin after the accident.
The instructions told users to imbed the anchor deeply in the wall before hanging heavy objects.

Synset Examples

He planted a knee in the back of his opponent
The dentist implanted a tooth in the gum

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embed - to fix or set firmly and deeply in a surrounding mass

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'embed', from 'em-' (in) + 'bed'.
