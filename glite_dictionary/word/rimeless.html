rimeless

1. ADJ

/ ˈraɪmləs / (AmE), / ˈraɪmləs / (BrE)
(of poetry or verse) not having rhyme; unrhymed.
having no rhyme

Examples

The poet deliberately chose a rimeless structure for her new collection.
Many translations of classical works prefer a rimeless style to preserve meaning over melody.
Some critics argue that rimeless poetry allows for greater freedom of expression.

Synset Examples

writing unrhymed blank verse is like playing tennis without a net

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unrhymed - not having rhyme
  • Antonym: rhymed - having rhyme

Etymology

Formed from 'rhyme' + '-less' (without).
