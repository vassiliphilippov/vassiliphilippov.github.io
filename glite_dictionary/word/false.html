false

1. ADJ

/ fɔls / (AmE), / fɔːls / (BrE)
wrong because it is based on incorrect information or a mistake.
incorrect; based on error

Examples

He realized he had made a false assumption about her intentions.
The report was based on false information provided by an unreliable source.
Her false impression of the city changed after she visited it in person.

Synset Examples

a false assumption
a mistaken view of the situation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistaken - incorrect or based on a misunderstanding
  • Antonym: correct - free from error; accurate

Etymology

From Old English 'fals', via Old French from Latin 'falsus' (deceived, erroneous).
