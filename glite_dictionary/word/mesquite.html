mesquite

1. NOUN

/ mɛˈskit / (AmE), / mɛˈskiːt / (BrE)
A small spiny tree or shrub of the genus Prosopis, native to arid regions, with cylindrical spikes of flowers and large, sweet seed pods.
spiny tree or shrub of arid regions

Examples

The mesquite provided welcome shade for the hikers in the desert.
Cattle often feed on the pods of the mesquite during the dry season.
Many barbecue enthusiasts use mesquite wood to add a distinctive smoky flavor to grilled meats.

Etymology

From Spanish 'mezquite', from Nahuatl 'mizquitl'.
