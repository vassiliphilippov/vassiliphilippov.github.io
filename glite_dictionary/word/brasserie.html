brasserie

1. NOUN

/ ˌbræsəˈri / (AmE), / ˌbrasəˈriː / (BrE)
a small, informal restaurant or café where beer, wine, and food are served, typically at moderate prices
small informal restaurant serving food and drinks

Examples

They enjoyed a casual lunch at a local brasserie in Paris.
The new brasserie downtown offers a great selection of craft beers and French dishes.
Many tourists love the relaxed atmosphere of a traditional French brasserie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bistro - small, inexpensive restaurant or bar

Etymology

From French 'brasserie' (brewery, restaurant), from 'brasser' (to brew).
