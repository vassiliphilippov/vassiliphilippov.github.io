Midwest

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɪdˈwɛst / (AmE), / ˌmɪdˈwɛst / (BrE)
the north-central region of the United States, often considered the heartland of the country
the north-central region of the United States
american

Examples

Many people in the Midwest experience cold winters and hot summers.
Corn and soybeans are major crops grown throughout the Midwest.
The culture of the Midwest is often described as friendly and down-to-earth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Midwestern - of or relating to the Midwest region

Etymology

From 'mid' + 'west', referring to its position relative to the historical development of the United States.
