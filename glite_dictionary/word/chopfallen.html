chopfallen

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɑpˌfɔl.ən / (AmE), / ˈtʃɒpˌfɔː.lən / (BrE)
Feeling dejected or brought low in spirit; downcast.
downcast or dejected
archaic

Examples

She looked chopfallen after hearing the disappointing news.
The team grew chopfallen as their chances of winning faded.
He entered the room chopfallen and silent, avoiding everyone's gaze.

Synset Examples

left us fatigued and deflated spiritually

Related Terms

  • Synonym: downcast - feeling despondent or dejected
  • Antonym: elated - filled with high spirits, opposite of dejected

Etymology

Possibly from 'chop' (jaw) + 'fallen', referring to a drooping jaw as a sign of dejection.
