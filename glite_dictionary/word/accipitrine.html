accipitrine

1. ADJ

/ ækˈsɪpɪtraɪn / (AmE), / ækˈsɪpɪtrɪn / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of birds of prey in the genus Accipiter, typically hawks.
relating to hawks (genus Accipiter)

Examples

The sharp eyesight and curved beak are typical accipitrine features.
Many accipitrine birds are renowned for their speed and predatory skills.
Researchers observed the nesting behavior of several accipitrine species in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawk - a bird of prey of the family Accipitridae
  • Degree Lower: falconine - relating to or resembling falcons

Etymology

From Latin 'accipiter' (hawk) + '-ine' (adjectival suffix).
