churn

1. NOUN

/ tʃɝːn / (AmE), / tʃɜːn / (BrE)
a container in which cream or milk is shaken or stirred to make butter by separating the butterfat from the buttermilk
container for making butter

Examples

The old wooden churn sat in the farmhouse kitchen, ready for the next batch of butter.
She used a hand churn to turn the fresh cream into butter.
After hours of work, the churn finally yielded a block of homemade butter.

Etymology

Old English cyrin (vessel for churning butter).

2. VERB

/ tʃɝːn / (AmE), / tʃɜːn / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to move about violently or be agitated, especially as a result of turbulence or disturbance.
move violently (of liquid)

Examples

The waves began to churn as the storm intensified.
The river's muddy waters churned after days of heavy rain.
Feelings of anxiety churned within him as he awaited the results.

Synset Examples

the sea was churning in the storm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to agitate - to disturb or stir up (especially a liquid)
  • Antonym: calm - not moving or agitated (of liquid)

Etymology

From Old English cyrin (churn), related to Middle Dutch kern (butter churn).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01889423-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ tʃɜrn / (AmE), / tʃɜːn / (BrE)
to stir or agitate cream vigorously in order to make butter
to make butter by stirring cream

Examples

The farmer used a wooden stick to churn the cream into butter every morning.
You must churn the cream for at least twenty minutes to achieve the right consistency.
Machines now churn butter much faster than people could by hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to agitate - to move or stir something briskly
  • Synonym: stir - to mix a liquid or substance by moving it around with a utensil

Etymology

From Old English *cyrin* (butter churn), possibly of Germanic origin.
