chromolithograph

1. NOUN

/ ˌkroʊməˈlɪθəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˌkrəʊməˈlɪθəɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a print produced by the process of chromolithography, using multiple colors applied in separate layers
a multicolored print made by chromolithography

Examples

The museum displayed a rare chromolithograph from the late nineteenth century.
She purchased a vintage chromolithograph featuring exotic birds and flowers.
Early advertisements often used the chromolithograph technique for bright, eye-catching posters.

Synset Examples

Depending on the number of colours present, a chromolithograph could take months to produce, by very skilled workers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chromolithography - the process of making color prints using lithography

Etymology

From Greek 'chroma' (color) and 'lithos' (stone), referring to printing from colored stones.
