dud

1. ADJ

/ dʌd / (AmE), / dʌd / (BrE)
(of an explosive device) failing to ignite or detonate as intended.
explosive device that fails to detonate

Examples

Luckily, the shell turned out to be a dud and caused no harm.
The bomb squad carefully removed a dud grenade from the construction site.
During training, soldiers sometimes encounter a dud round that doesn't fire.

Synset Examples

he stepped on a dud mine

Related Terms

  • Antonym: live - an explosive device that is charged and ready to detonate

Etymology

From earlier slang meaning 'worthless thing', origin uncertain, possibly imitative or from dud (clothes), from Middle English dudde (cloak).

2. NOUN

/ dʌd / (AmE), / dʌd / (BrE)
an event or thing that is a complete failure or is ineffective
a thing or event that is a failure

Examples

The highly anticipated new product turned out to be a complete dud in the marketplace.
Everyone was disappointed when the fireworks show ended up being a dud.
His presentation was a dud and failed to impress the audience.

Synset Examples

the first experiment was a real turkey
the meeting was a dud as far as new business was concerned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: failure - lack of success in achieving or doing something
  • Antonym: success - the achievement of a desired aim or result

Etymology

Probably from Scottish dud (worthless clothes), later generalized to mean something worthless or useless.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07379793-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ dʌd / (AmE), / dʌd / (BrE)
an explosive device, such as a shell or firework, that fails to detonate or function as intended
explosive device that fails to go off

Examples

The old bomb turned out to be a dud and posed no threat to the workers.
The firework was a complete dud, failing to light up the night sky as expected.
Everyone held their breath, but the grenade was just a dud.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: failure - device or process that does not function as intended
  • Antonym: success - device or event that works as intended

Etymology

Originally British slang, possibly from 'duds' (clothes) or related to 'dead' (nonfunctioning).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07436349-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ dʌd / (AmE), / dʌd / (BrE)
a person who is unsuccessful or disappointing, especially one who fails to meet expectations.
an unsuccessful or disappointing person
informal

Examples

Everyone expected him to do well in the competition, but he turned out to be a real dud.
Her blind date was a dud who barely spoke all night.
The new manager seemed promising, but ended up being a complete dud at the job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: failure - person who does not succeed
  • Antonym: success - person who achieves desired results

Etymology

Probably from Middle English dudde (cloak, rag), later meaning 'worthless thing'.
