concordant

1. ADJ

/ kənˈkɔrdənt / (AmE), / kənˈkɔːdənt / (BrE)
in agreement; having the same opinion or consistent with something else
in agreement; harmonious in opinion

Examples

After a lengthy discussion, the committee members became concordant in their decision.
The findings of the recent study are concordant with previous research.
Their concordant views on the subject made collaboration easy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: discordant - not in agreement; disagreeing in opinion

Etymology

From Latin concordant-, present participle of 'concordare' (to agree), from 'concordia' (harmony).

2. ADJ

/ kənˈkɔrdənt / (AmE), / kənˈkɔːdənt / (BrE)
in agreement; consistent or harmonious with something else
in agreement; harmonious

Examples

Her proposal was concordant with company policies and values.
The two scientists presented concordant findings in their research papers.
Their statements were concordant, showing there was no disagreement between them.

Synset Examples

salaries agreeable with current trends
plans conformable with your wishes
expressed views concordant with his background

Related Terms

  • Antonym: discordant - not in agreement; clashing or conflicting
  • Synonym: agreeable - in keeping with; consistent with
  • Synonym: harmonious - forming a pleasing or consistent whole

Etymology

From Latin 'concordant-', from 'concordare' meaning 'to agree'.
