Haliaeetus albicilla

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæliˈiːtəs ˌælbɪˈsɪlə / (AmE), / ˌhæliˈiːtəs ˌal.bɪˈsɪl.ə / (BrE)
a large, bulky, greyish-brown eagle with a short, wedge-shaped white tail, found in Europe, Greenland, and parts of Asia; also known as the white-tailed eagle.
a large greyish-brown eagle (white-tailed eagle)

Examples

The Haliaeetus albicilla has made a remarkable comeback in several European countries.
Birdwatchers spotted a Haliaeetus albicilla perched near the fjord.
Conservationists are monitoring the nesting sites of the Haliaeetus albicilla to help protect the species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white-tailed eagle - large sea eagle native to Europe and Asia

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Haliaeetus' (from Greek hals, ‘sea’ + aetos, ‘eagle’) and species name 'albicilla' (from Latin albus, 'white' + cilla, 'tail').
