Lonicera albiflora

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈnɪsərə ˌæl.bɪˈflɔrə / (AmE), / lɒˈnɪsərə ˌæl.bɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
A species of bushy honeysuckle with twining branches and white or yellow-white flowers, native to the southern United States.
a species of bushy honeysuckle

Examples

The garden was filled with the delicate aroma of Lonicera albiflora in bloom.
Botanists in Texas often study Lonicera albiflora due to its unique twining branches.
We planted Lonicera albiflora along the fence to attract hummingbirds and bees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeysuckle - a climbing shrub with tubular, fragrant flowers (genus Lonicera)

Etymology

From Latin: 'Lonicera' (the honeysuckle genus, named after botanist Adam Lonicer) and 'albiflora' (white-flowered).
