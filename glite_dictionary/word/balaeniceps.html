Balaeniceps

1. NOUN

/ bəˌliːnəˈsɛps / (AmE), / bəˌliːnɪˈsɛps / (BrE)
A genus of large wading bird in the family Balaenicipitidae, containing the shoebill.
genus of wading birds (shoebills)

Examples

The Balaeniceps is best known for its striking shoe-shaped bill.
Ornithologists classify the shoebill under the genus Balaeniceps.
Members of the Balaeniceps genus are found in East Africa's wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoebill - a large African wading bird with a broad shoe-shaped bill

Etymology

From Latin balaena (whale) and -ceps (headed), referring to the whale-shaped bill.

2. NOUN

/ bəˌliːnəˈsɛps / (AmE), / bəˌliːnɪˈsɛps / (BrE)
A genus of large African birds, comprising the shoebill, and the only genus in the family Balaenicipitidae.
genus of African birds (shoebills)

Examples

The Balaeniceps is recognized as a unique genus due to the distinctive shape of its bill.
Within the family Balaenicipitidae, Balaeniceps contains only one living species, the shoebill.
Ornithologists studied the Balaeniceps in its natural habitat in the wetlands of central Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoebill - a large African bird with a broad bill (Balaeniceps rex)

Etymology

From Latin 'balaena' (whale) + 'caput' (head), referring to the whale-shaped bill.
