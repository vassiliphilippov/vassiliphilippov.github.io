liquified

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɪkwɪˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪfaɪd / (BrE)
(of a substance) reduced to liquid form, especially by heating.
converted to liquid by heating

Examples

The engineers transported liquified natural gas in special insulated containers.
During the process, the plastic was liquified and then molded into shape.
The volcano erupted, releasing a stream of liquified rock down the mountainside.

Synset Examples

a mass of molten rock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liquid - a substance that flows freely but is of constant volume
  • Antonym: solidified - made solid; having become solid from a liquid state

Etymology

From the verb 'liquify,' from Latin 'liquefacere' (to make liquid).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01510205-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɪkwəˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪˌfaɪd / (BrE)
(of a substance) changed from a solid or gas into a liquid state.
turned into a liquid

Examples

The liquified metal was poured into a mold to create new parts.
The tanker transported liquified natural gas across the ocean.
After hours in the sun, the ice cream had completely liquified.

Synset Examples

rivers filled to overflowing by melted snow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melted - changed from solid to liquid by heat

Etymology

From the verb 'liquify', based on Latin 'liquefacere' (to make liquid), from 'liquere' (to be liquid) and 'facere' (to make).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01509670-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈlɪkwəˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪfaɪd / (BrE)
turned into a liquid, especially by heating or pressure.
made into a liquid

Examples

The company specializes in the transport of liquified natural gas across continents.
Some gases are liquified under pressure for easier storage and transportation.
After being heated, the metal was liquified and poured into molds.

Synset Examples

liquefied petroleum gas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liquefied - turned into a liquid (alternate spelling)

Etymology

From Latin liquidus (liquid) + English -fy (to make), via liquefy.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02269929-s
Synset POS: s
