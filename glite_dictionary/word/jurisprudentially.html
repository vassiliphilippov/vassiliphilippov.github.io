jurisprudentially

1. ADV

/ ˌdʒʊr.ɪs.pruːˈdɛn.ʃə.li / (AmE), / ˌdʒʊər.ɪs.pruːˈdɛn.ʃəli / (BrE)
In a manner relating to jurisprudence or the science or philosophy of law.
in a way relating to jurisprudence
legal

Examples

The decision was considered jurisprudentially significant by legal scholars.
The issue must be addressed jurisprudentially rather than simply procedurally.
She approached the case jurisprudentially, focusing on legal theories and philosophical principles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jurisprudence - the science or philosophy of law

Etymology

From 'jurisprudential' + '-ly'; 'jurisprudential' from Latin 'jurisprudentia' (knowledge of law).
