revertible

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈvɜrtəbəl / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːtəbəl / (BrE)
(of property or rights) able to return to a previous possessor, especially to a former owner or that owner's heirs, often by legal process.
able to be returned to a former owner
legal

Examples

The lease was revertible to the landlord upon the tenant's death.
Some historic estates are revertible under special family trusts.
The court found that the land was revertible to the original heirs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reversible - able to be undone or returned to a previous state

Etymology

From Latin 'reverti' (to turn back) + English '-ible' (capable of).
