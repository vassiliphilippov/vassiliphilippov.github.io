Eleusine

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈluːsɪni / (AmE), / ɪˈljuːsɪni / (BrE)
A genus of annual and perennial grasses native to savannas and upland grasslands, including important species such as finger millet.
genus of grasses (including finger millet)

Examples

Eleusine species are often found in tropical and subtropical grasslands.
Farmers cultivate Eleusine for its nutritious grains in many parts of Africa.
The most well-known member of Eleusine is finger millet, valued for its drought resistance.

Etymology

From Greek Eleusis, possibly referring to the ancient Greek city or a local species.
