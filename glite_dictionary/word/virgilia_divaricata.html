Virgilia divaricata

1. NOUN

/ vɜrˈdʒɪliə dɪˌvɛrɪˈkeɪtə / (AmE), / vəˈdʒɪliə dɪˌvɑːrɪˈkeɪtə / (BrE)
a fast-growing round-headed tree native to South Africa, with fragrant white to deep rose flowers, planted as an ornamental
South African ornamental tree with fragrant flowers

Examples

The avenue was lined with Virgilia divaricata, their rosy blossoms creating a stunning display in early summer.
Gardeners favor Virgilia divaricata for its quick growth and attractive fragrant flowers.
We planted a Virgilia divaricata near the patio, and its shade is now a favorite spot in the garden.

Etymology

From Latin: genus Virgilia named after Virgil, species name divaricata meaning 'spreading' or 'straggling'.
