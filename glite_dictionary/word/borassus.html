Borassus

1. NOUN

/ bəˈræsəs / (AmE), / bəˈræsəs / (BrE)
A genus of tall palm trees, commonly known as the palmyra palm, native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and New Guinea.
genus of tall palm trees (palmyra)

Examples

The Borassus palm is an important resource for producing sugar, fruit, and building material.
Botanists traveled to study the unique ecosystem surrounding the Borassus trees.
In some regions, the leaves of Borassus palms are woven into mats and baskets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palmyra - common name for Borassus flabellifer, a species of Borassus palm

Etymology

From Latin 'Borassus', from Greek 'borassos', a kind of palm tree.
