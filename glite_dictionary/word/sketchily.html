sketchily

1. ADV

/ ˈskɛtʃɪli / (AmE), / ˈskɛtʃɪli / (BrE)
In a way that is incomplete, vague, or lacking detail.
in a vague or incomplete way

Examples

He described the plan sketchily, leaving out many important details.
The wall was sketchily painted, with several spots left untouched.
She sketchily recalled the events of that afternoon in her testimony.

Synset Examples

he explained sketchily
the dishes were only sketchily washed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vaguely - in a way that is not clear or definite
  • Antonym: thoroughly - in a way that is complete with attention to detail

Etymology

From 'sketchy' + '-ly', meaning 'in the manner of being sketchy'.
