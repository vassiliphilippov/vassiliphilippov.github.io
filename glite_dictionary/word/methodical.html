methodical

1. ADJ

/ məˈθɑːdɪkəl / (AmE), / mɪˈθɒdɪkəl / (BrE)
done in a careful and systematic way; characterized by method and orderliness.
systematic and orderly

Examples

She tackled the complex project in a methodical manner, making sure not to miss any detail.
A methodical approach is essential when assembling delicate equipment.
Despite the chaos, his methodical nature helped him stay calm and focused.

Synset Examples

a methodical scholar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: systematic - done according to a fixed plan or system
  • Antonym: disorganized - not properly planned or arranged

Etymology

From French ‘méthodique’, from Late Latin ‘methodicus’, from Greek ‘methodikos’ (systematic).
