brattice

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrætɪs / (AmE), / ˈbrætɪs / (BrE)
A temporary partition of planks or cloth used to control ventilation in a mine.
temporary mine partition for ventilation

Examples

The miners installed a brattice to improve airflow in the lower tunnel.
After the explosion, the brattice was quickly replaced to restore proper ventilation.
A wooden brattice separated the main shaft from the working faces.

Etymology

From Middle English 'bratiz' (parapet, wooden screen), from Old French 'bretèche'.

2. VERB

/ ˈbrætɪs / (AmE), / ˈbrætɪs / (BrE)
to equip or supply (a mine) with a brattice in order to improve ventilation
to supply a mine with a brattice

Examples

Work crews were hired to brattice the new mine shaft before winter.
The safety inspector ordered the workers to brattice the tunnel for better airflow.
Properly bratticed passages are essential to prevent gas buildup in coal mines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ventilate - to cause air to enter and circulate freely in a space

Etymology

From Middle English *bretayse* (a temporary wooden structure), from Old French *bretèche* (parapet).
