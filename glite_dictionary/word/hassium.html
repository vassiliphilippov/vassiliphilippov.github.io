hassium

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæsiəm / (AmE), / ˈhasɪəm / (BrE)
a synthetic, radioactive transuranic element with atomic number 108
a radioactive chemical element (atomic number 108)

Examples

The team succeeded in producing hassium by bombarding lead with iron nuclei.
Due to its short half-life, hassium can only be studied in specialized laboratories.
Chemists have synthesized only a few atoms of hassium since its discovery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dubnium - a synthetic element with atomic number 105
  • Degree Higher: meitnerium - a synthetic element with atomic number 109

Etymology

From Latin *Hassia* (Hesse), the German state where it was first synthesized, plus -ium.
