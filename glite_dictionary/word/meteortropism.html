meteortropism

1. NOUN

/ mitiˈɔr.trəˌpɪz.əm / (AmE), / miːtɪˈɔː.trəˌpɪz.əm / (BrE)
the effect of climatic conditions, especially weather changes, on biological processes, such as pain in joints
effect of climate on biological processes
medical

Examples

Some people report worsening joint pain due to meteortropism during rainy weather.
Researchers are studying how meteortropism influences seasonal mood disorders.
Doctors sometimes consider meteortropism when diagnosing recurrent symptoms linked to weather changes.

Etymology

From Greek 'meteōros' (high in the air, weather) and 'tropism' (a turning or change in response to a stimulus).
