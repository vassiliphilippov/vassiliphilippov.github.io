Lavatera

1. NOUN

/ ˌlævəˈtɛrə / (AmE), / ˌlavəˈtɪərə / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the mallow family, comprising herbs or shrubs cultivated for their large, showy flowers
genus of flowering plants (mallows)

Examples

Many gardeners choose Lavatera for its vibrant pink or white blossoms.
The Lavatera shrub grew quickly, filling the garden with color all summer long.
She planted Lavatera seeds along the fence to attract bees and butterflies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mallow - plants in the family Malvaceae, to which Lavatera belongs

Etymology

Named after the Swiss Lavater family of physicians and botanists.

2. NOUN

/ ˌlævəˈtɛrə / (AmE), / ˌlævəˈtɪərə / (BrE)
a genus of herbs or softwood shrubs, widely cultivated for their large, showy flowers; known as tree mallows
genus of flowering shrubs (tree mallows)

Examples

The Lavatera blooms were a highlight in the summer garden, attracting both bees and butterflies.
Gardeners often choose Lavatera for its rapid growth and stunning pink or white flowers.
After planting several Lavatera shrubs, the borders of the park were transformed with color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mallow - herbs or shrubs in the Malvaceae family, including Lavatera

Etymology

Named after the 16th-century Swiss naturalists Johann and Georg Lavater.
