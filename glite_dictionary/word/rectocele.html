rectocele

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛk.toʊˌsiːl / (AmE), / ˈrɛk.təʊˌsiːl / (BrE)
a medical condition in which the rectum bulges or protrudes into the vagina, often due to weakened pelvic muscles, commonly after childbirth
protrusion of rectum into vagina
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that a rectocele can result from weakened pelvic floor muscles.
Surgery may be recommended if a rectocele is causing significant discomfort or difficulty.
Many women experience a rectocele after childbirth, but symptoms can vary widely.

Etymology

From Latin 'rectum' (the rectum) and Greek 'kēlē' (hernia).
