turbinal

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜːrbɪnəl / (AmE), / ˈtɜːbɪnəl / (BrE)
any of the scroll-shaped spongy bones in the nasal passages of humans and other vertebrates, also known as nasal conchae
scrolled bone in nasal passage

Examples

During the examination, the doctor noticed swelling of the turbinal in the patient's left nostril.
The turbinal bones help to humidify and filter the air we breathe.
Damage to a turbinal can affect the airflow through the nasal passages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concha - a spiral or scroll-shaped bone in the nasal cavity (synonym for turbinal)

Etymology

From Latin 'turbinatus' (having a whorled shape), from 'turbo' (spinning top).
