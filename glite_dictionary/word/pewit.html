pewit

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiwɪt / (AmE), / ˈpjuːɪt / (BrE)
A small olive-colored woodland flycatcher native to eastern North America.
small olive-colored woodland flycatcher

Examples

We spotted a pewit flitting among the branches during our hike in the forest.
The distinctive call of the pewit can often be heard in the early mornings of spring.
Birdwatchers consider the pewit a charming addition to the woodlands of eastern North America.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpiːwɪt / (AmE), / ˈpjuːɪt / (BrE)
a large crested Eurasian plover, also known as the lapwing, noted for its distinctive call and appearance
a crested Eurasian plover (lapwing)

Examples

A pewit soared overhead, calling out its familiar, plaintive cry.
The pewit is easily recognized by its distinctive crest and rounded wings.
Farmers often spot pewits nesting in open fields during the spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lapwing - another name for the pewit or crested plover

Etymology

Imitative of the bird’s call.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpiːwɪt / (AmE), / ˈpjuːɪt / (BrE)
a small European bird of the plover family, known for its distinctive call and black head, also called the northern lapwing
small European bird, northern lapwing

Examples

A flock of pewits soared over the wet meadow at dawn.
The distinctive call of the pewit is often heard in springtime fields.
Conservationists are working to protect habitats for pewits across western Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lapwing - another name for the pewit, a wading bird of the plover family

Etymology

Imitative of the bird's call.
