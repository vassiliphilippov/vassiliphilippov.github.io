protistan

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈtɪstən / (AmE), / prəʊˈtɪstən / (BrE)
a eukaryotic organism belonging to the kingdom Protista, typically single-celled, free-living or colonial, with diverse nutritional and reproductive modes
a member of the kingdom Protista; eukaryotic organism
medical

Examples

Many protistan species reproduce asexually by simple cell division.
The diversity of protistan life forms makes classification challenging for scientists.
Algae are an example of protistan organisms found in aquatic environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protozoan - single-celled eukaryotic organism, often considered a type of protist

Etymology

From New Latin Protista (a biological kingdom); influenced by 'proto-' (first, original).
