Greece

1. PROPN

/ ɡris / (AmE), / ɡriːs / (BrE)
A country in southeastern Europe on the southern part of the Balkan Peninsula, known for its ancient history and production of olives and olive oil.
country in southeastern Europe

Examples

Many tourists visit Greece to explore its ancient ruins and beautiful coastlines.
Olive farming has deep roots in Greece's history and economy.
The official language spoken in Greece is Greek.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Greek - the language, culture, or people of Greece

Etymology

From Latin 'Graecia', from Greek 'Graikía', the land of the Greeks.

2. PROPN

/ ɡris / (AmE), / ɡriːs / (BrE)
An ancient country of city-states, especially Athens and Sparta, that reached its peak of civilization in the fifth century BCE.
ancient country of city-states in classical times

Examples

The art and philosophy of Greece have profoundly influenced Western civilization.
In the fifth century BCE, Greece was a center of learning, democracy, and culture.
Many mythological stories originated in ancient Greece.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Athens - chief city-state of ancient Greece
  • Degree Lower: Sparta - prominent city-state in ancient Greece

Etymology

From Greek 'Hellas'; anglicized name via Latin 'Graecia'.
