Leptoglossus

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛp.təˈɡlɑː.səs / (AmE), / ˌlɛp.təˈɡlɒs.əs / (BrE)
A genus of leaf-footed bugs commonly found on various plants, characterized by their flattened, leaf-like hind legs.
genus of leaf-footed bugs

Examples

Gardeners often find Leptoglossus feeding on the fruits and stems of tomato plants.
The distinctive hind legs of Leptoglossus help to identify them among other garden insects.
Scientists studied the impact of Leptoglossus on crop yields in the region.

Etymology

From Greek 'leptos' (slender, thin) + 'glossa' (tongue), referring to features of the insect.

2. NOUN

/ ˌlɛp.təˈɡlɑː.səs / (AmE), / ˌlɛp.təˈɡlɒs.əs / (BrE)
A genus of leaf-footed bugs found mainly in the Americas, known for their enlarged hind legs and plant-feeding habits.
genus of leaf-footed bugs

Examples

The Leptoglossus genus includes several species known to damage crops such as tomatoes and squash.
Researchers have observed that some Leptoglossus bugs can transmit plant diseases.
The distinctive, leaf-like expansions on the hind legs make Leptoglossus easy to identify.

Etymology

From Greek 'leptos' (slender) and 'glossa' (tongue), referring to features of the insect's mouthparts.
