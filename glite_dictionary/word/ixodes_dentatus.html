Ixodes dentatus

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈzoʊdiːz dɛnˈteɪtəs / (AmE), / ɪkˈsəʊdiːz dɛnˈteɪtəs / (BrE)
a species of hard-bodied tick that transmits the Lyme disease spirochete to animals such as cottontail rabbits and wood rats
a tick species that transmits Lyme disease
medical

Examples

Researchers discovered Ixodes dentatus on several wild rabbits in the area.
Lyme disease can be spread to animals by the bite of Ixodes dentatus.
The biologist studied the habitat preferences of Ixodes dentatus in woodland regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tick - any of various small parasitic arachnids

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Ixodes' (tick) and Latin 'dentatus' (toothed).
