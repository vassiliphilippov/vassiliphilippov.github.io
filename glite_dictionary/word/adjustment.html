adjustment

1. NOUN

/ əˈdʒʌstmənt / (AmE), / əˈdʒʌstmənt / (BrE)
the process of adapting or becoming used to a new situation, condition, or environment
process of adapting to something

Examples

Moving to a new country requires a difficult period of adjustment.
After breaking his leg, it took some adjustment to return to normal activities.
The recent changes in the office called for quick adjustment from everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adaptation - the process of adjusting to new conditions

Etymology

From French 'ajustement', from 'ajuster' (to adjust).

3. NOUN

/ əˈdʒʌstmənt / (AmE), / ədˈʒʌstmənt / (BrE)
the act of altering or adapting something to meet a particular standard or condition
action of matching or adapting to a standard

Examples

After installing the new software, a minor adjustment to the settings was required to meet company standards.
The technician made an adjustment to the machine so it would operate within safety limits.
She performed a quick adjustment of the microscope to get a clearer image.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: correction - the act of making something accurate or matching a standard
  • Antonym: distortion - the act of altering something away from a standard or correct form

From French 'ajustement', from 'ajuster' (to adjust).

4. NOUN

/ əˈdʒʌstmənt / (AmE), / əˈdʒʌstmənt / (BrE)
the act of changing something slightly, especially to improve it or make it fit, such as changing the size of a garment
small change for improvement or fit

Examples

She made a small adjustment to the dress so it would fit better.
After installing the new shelves, only minor adjustments were needed to level them.
Getting used to a new job requires some adjustment to your daily routine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alteration - the act of making a change or adjustment, especially in clothing

Etymology

From adjust + -ment, derived from Old French ajuster (to make right) and Latin adjuxtare, from ad- (to) + juxta (near).

5. NOUN

/ əˈdʒʌstmənt / (AmE), / əˈdʒʌstmənt / (BrE)
the process of making something suitable for a new purpose or adapting to new conditions
process of adapting or becoming suitable

Examples

After moving to a new city, making an adjustment to the local culture can take time.
The sudden schedule change required a quick adjustment by all team members.
Her adjustment to the new job was smooth and surprisingly fast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adaptation - the process of adjusting to new conditions
  • Antonym: maladjustment - poor or inadequate adjustment to circumstances

Etymology

From French *ajustement* and Old French *ajuster* (to adjust), from Late Latin *adjuxtare* (to bring near).
