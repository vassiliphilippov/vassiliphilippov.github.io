Old Church Slavonic

1. NOUN

/ oʊld tʃɝtʃ sləˈvɑnɪk / (AmE), / əʊld tʃɜːtʃ sləˈvɒnɪk / (BrE)
the earliest Slavic literary language, used for the translation of the Bible and other religious texts in the 9th century
the earliest Slavic literary language

Examples

The first translation of the Bible into Old Church Slavonic was completed by Saints Cyril and Methodius.
Linguists study Old Church Slavonic to understand the early development of Slavic languages.
Many manuscripts in Old Church Slavonic have been preserved in monasteries.

Etymology

From 'Old' (ancient), 'Church' (Christian religious context), and 'Slavonic' (relating to the Slavs).
