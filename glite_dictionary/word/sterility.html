sterility

1. NOUN

/ stəˈrɪləti / (AmE), / stəˈrɪlɪti / (BrE)
the state or condition of being unable to produce offspring or conceive children
inability to produce offspring
medical

Examples

After years of trying to have a baby, they were devastated to learn about her sterility.
The disease can cause sterility in both men and women if left untreated.
Scientists are studying the causes of male sterility to develop better treatments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infertility - inability to conceive children or produce offspring

Etymology

From Latin sterilitas (barrenness, infertility), from sterilis (unfruitful).

2. NOUN

/ stəˈrɪləti / (AmE), / stəˈrɪləti / (BrE)
the state or condition of being free from living pathogenic microorganisms; complete absence of infectious agents
state of being free from pathogens
medical

Examples

The surgical equipment must maintain sterility throughout the procedure.
A breach in sterility could lead to a serious infection in the patient.
Laboratories use autoclaves to ensure the sterility of glassware and instruments.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: disinfection - the process of eliminating most pathogenic microorganisms
  • Antonym: contamination - the presence of unwanted microorganisms or substances

Etymology

From Latin sterilis (barren, unproductive) + -ity.
