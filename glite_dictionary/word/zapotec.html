Zapotec

1. ADJ

/ ˈzæpəˌtɛk / (AmE), / ˈzap.ə.tɛk / (BrE)
relating to the Zapotec people, their language, or their culture, indigenous to southern Mexico
of the Zapotec people or culture

Examples

The museum features Zapotec textiles from Oaxaca.
They studied Zapotec traditions as part of their anthropology class.
Several Zapotec villages still celebrate ancient festivals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zapotec - the indigenous people of southern Mexico

Etymology

From Spanish Zapoteca, from Nahuatl *zapotēcatl* (Zapotec person).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03157402-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈzæpəˌtɛk / (AmE), / ˈzæpətɛk / (BrE)
the language of the Zapotec people, spoken in parts of southern Mexico
indigenous language of southern Mexico

Examples

Linguists have documented several dialects of Zapotec in Oaxaca.
She is fluent in both Spanish and Zapotec.
The preservation of Zapotec is important for cultural heritage.

Etymology

From Spanish Zapoteco, from Náhuatl *tzapotēcatl* (person from Zapotlán), from *tzapotl* (sapote, a fruit) + the locative suffix *-tecatl*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06931898-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈzæpəˌtɛk / (AmE), / ˈzæpəˌtɛk / (BrE)
a member of an indigenous people of southern Mexico whose civilization flourished from about 300 to 900 CE
member of an indigenous Mexican people

Examples

The ancient Zapotec built impressive cities such as Monte Albán in Oaxaca.
Many modern Zapotec communities still speak their native language today.
Archaeologists have uncovered intricate pottery created by the Zapotec civilization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mixtec - a member of an indigenous people of southern Mexico, culturally related to the Zapotec

Etymology

From Spanish 'zapoteca,' from Nahuatl 'tzapotēcah' (people of Zapote), referencing the region inhabited by the group.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09669077-n
Synset POS: n
