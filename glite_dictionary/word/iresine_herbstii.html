Iresine herbstii

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈriːsiːn hɛrˈbstiː / (AmE), / aɪˈriːsiːn hɛˈbstiː / (BrE)
A South American ornamental plant with green to purple or red branches, decorative foliage, and small woolly flowers.
ornamental South American plant

Examples

Many gardeners choose Iresine herbstii for its striking, colorful foliage in borders and containers.
The Iresine herbstii prefers well-drained soil and thrives in warm, humid climates.
Botanical gardens often display Iresine herbstii alongside other tropical ornamental plants.

Etymology

Named after German botanist Wilhelm Herbst; genus name from Greek 'eirēnē' (peace), possibly in reference to the plant's gentle appearance.
