TRH

1. NOUN

/ ti ɑr eɪtʃ / (AmE), / ti ɑː ɒ tʃ / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the hypothalamus that stimulates the release of thyroid-stimulating hormone from the anterior pituitary gland
hormone controlling thyroid stimulation
medical

Examples

Doctors may order tests to measure TRH levels in patients with suspected thyroid disorders.
Administration of TRH can help diagnose the cause of certain hormone imbalances.
The hypothalamus secretes TRH to regulate thyroid function through the pituitary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thyrotropin-releasing hormone - hormone produced by the hypothalamus stimulating TSH release

Etymology

Abbreviation for thyrotropin-releasing hormone.
