Bittacidae

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈtæsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / bɪˈtæsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of predatory tropical insects belonging to the order Mecoptera, commonly known as hangingflies.
family of predatory insects (Mecoptera)

Examples

Some species within the Bittacidae family are known for their unique mating behaviors.
Researchers discovered a new genus of Bittacidae in the rainforests of South America.
The elongated legs of Bittacidae help them capture prey efficiently while hanging from vegetation.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Bittacidae', based on the type genus 'Bittacus'.
