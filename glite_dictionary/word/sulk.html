sulk

1. NOUN

/ sʌlk / (AmE), / sʌlk / (BrE)
a period or state of being sullenly silent or withdrawn, often because of annoyance or disappointment
a sullen mood or state of withdrawal

Examples

He spent the afternoon in a sulk after losing the game.
When ignored, she often retreats into a sulk and refuses to talk.
Their argument ended with him going off in a sulk.

Synset Examples

stayed home in a sulk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mood - a temporary state of mind or feeling
  • Degree Higher: tantrum - an uncontrolled outburst of anger and frustration

Etymology

From Middle English sulken (to be sullen); related to 'sullen'.

2. VERB

/ sʌlk / (AmE), / sʌlk / (BrE)
to be silently moody or show displeasure, often by withdrawing or refusing to talk
be silently moody; show displeasure

Examples

After being told he couldn't go to the party, he began to sulk in his room.
She would sulk for hours whenever she lost a game.
Rather than argue, he chose to sulk silently in the corner.

Synset Examples

She is pouting because she didn't get what she wanted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pout - to push out one's lips, especially in annoyance or to show displeasure

Etymology

Probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian 'sulka' (to sulk).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02724891-v
Synset POS: v
