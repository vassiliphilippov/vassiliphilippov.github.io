Smilax aspera

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmaɪlæks æsˈpɪrə / (AmE), / ˈsmaɪlæks æsˈpɪərə / (BrE)
A species of climbing or creeping evergreen plant with spiny, zigzag stems, shiny leaves, and racemes of pale-green flowers, native to regions from the Canary Islands and southern Europe to Ethiopia and India.
species of spiny climbing evergreen plant

Examples

The dense thickets of Smilax aspera made it difficult to walk through the Mediterranean forest.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Smilax aspera for its attractive, glossy leaves and unusual flowers.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Smilax aspera commonly found in southern Europe and North Africa.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Smilax' and species epithet 'aspera' meaning 'rough'.
