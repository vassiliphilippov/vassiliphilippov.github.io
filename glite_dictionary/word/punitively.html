punitively

1. ADV

/ pjuˈnɪtɪvli / (AmE), / pjuˈnɪtɪvli / (BrE)
In a way that is intended as punishment.
in a punitive manner; as punishment

Examples

The company was punitively fined for violating environmental laws.
Students found that the school enforced its dress code punitively rather than constructively.
Critics argued that the new tax measures were punitively harsh on low-income families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punitive - intended as punishment

Etymology

From 'punitive' + '-ly'; 'punitive' from Latin punire (to punish).
