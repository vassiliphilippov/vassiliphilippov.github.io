Tantilla

1. NOUN

/ tænˈtɪlə / (AmE), / tanˈtɪlə / (BrE)
A genus of small, harmless colubrid snakes, commonly called black-headed snakes, found in the Americas.
genus of small black-headed snakes

Examples

The Tantilla snakes are often recognized by their distinctive black heads and slender bodies.
Despite their appearance, Tantilla species are harmless and rarely encountered by humans.
Several new Tantilla have been discovered in Central American rainforests in recent years.

Etymology

From New Latin, diminutive form of Latin "tantus" (so much, so great), used in scientific naming.

2. NOUN

/ tænˈtɪlə / (AmE), / tænˈtɪlə / (BrE)
A genus of small, slender snakes known as black-headed snakes, found mainly in the Americas.
genus of black-headed snakes

Examples

The Tantilla snakes are rarely seen due to their secretive habits.
Some species in the genus Tantilla are known for having distinctive black heads.
Researchers discovered a new Tantilla species in Central America last year.

Etymology

Modern Latin, used in scientific classification.
