Sterna

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɝː.nə / (AmE), / ˈstɜː.nə / (BrE)
A genus of seabirds in the family Sternidae, commonly known as terns.
a genus of seabirds (terns)

Examples

The genus Sterna includes several species of graceful, long-winged terns.
Ornithologists studied the migration patterns of birds in the Sterna genus.
Most Sterna species are found in coastal regions around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tern - a seabird of the family Sternidae, related to gulls

Etymology

From Latin *sterna*, used as a genus name for certain seabirds.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɜːrnə / (AmE), / ˈstɜːnə / (BrE)
A genus of seabirds in the family Laridae, commonly known as terns.
genus of seabirds, terns

Examples

The genus Sterna includes several species of graceful, long-winged terns.
Many coastal regions host breeding colonies of birds in the Sterna group.
Ornithologists have studied the migration patterns of Sterna species across continents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tern - a slender, graceful seabird

Etymology

From Latin *sterna*, used in scientific naming for terns.
