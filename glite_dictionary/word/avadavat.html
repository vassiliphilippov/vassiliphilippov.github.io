avadavat

1. NOUN

/ ˌævəˈdəvæt / (AmE), / ˌævəˈdəvæt / (BrE)
A small red weaverbird of Asia, often kept as a cage bird.
small Asian red cage bird

Examples

The avadavat is prized for its beautiful red plumage and lively behavior.
She keeps a pair of avadavats in a spacious aviary in her garden.
In parts of Asia, the avadavat is a popular pet due to its vibrant colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waxbill - small finch-like Old World birds related to the avadavat

Etymology

From the city of Ahmedabad in India, from which the bird was exported.
