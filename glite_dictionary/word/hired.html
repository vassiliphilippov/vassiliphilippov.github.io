hired

1. ADJ

/ ˈhaɪɚd / (AmE), / ˈhaɪəd / (BrE)
(of a person or service) engaged to work or perform for payment rather than voluntarily.
engaged for a fee

Examples

The company brought in several hired consultants to improve efficiency.
We got a hired car for our road trip through the countryside.
She works as a hired assistant during tax season.

Synset Examples

hired hands
a hired gun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: employed - having a paid job
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00867533-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈhaɪɚd / (AmE), / ˈhaɪəd / (BrE)
(of a vehicle or service) hired for the exclusive temporary use of a person or group, especially for transport.
rented or chartered for exclusive use

Examples

The hired bus picked up the tour group at 8 a.m.
We traveled to the mountains in a hired van with other families.
The company arranged for a hired coach to take employees to the event.

Synset Examples

a chartered plane
the chartered buses arrived on time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chartered - rented or hired for exclusive use, especially a vehicle
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00361121-a
Synset POS: a
