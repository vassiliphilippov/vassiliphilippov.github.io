Darmera peltata

1. NOUN

/ dɑrˈmɛrə pɛlˈteɪtə / (AmE), / dɑːˈmɛərə pɛlˈteɪtə / (BrE)
A perennial herbaceous plant native to California's Sierra Nevada, known for its large peltate leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers borne on leafless stems.
species of perennial herbaceous plant

Examples

The bold foliage of Darmera peltata provides stunning contrast in shade gardens.
In spring, Darmera peltata produces pink flower clusters before the leaves fully emerge.
Darmera peltata thrives in moist soil along stream banks in its native habitat.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Darmera' (honoring Karl Darmer, a 19th-century German horticulturist) and Latin 'peltatus' meaning 'shield-shaped'.
