age-old

1. ADJ

/ ˈeɪdʒ oʊld / (AmE), / ˈeɪdʒ əʊld / (BrE)
having existed or continued for a very long time; ancient or longstanding, especially of traditions, beliefs, or customs
very old; longstanding

Examples

These age-old traditions are still observed by villagers today.
The age-old question of nature versus nurture continues to spark debate among scientists.
Many stories explore age-old fears about darkness and the unknown.
