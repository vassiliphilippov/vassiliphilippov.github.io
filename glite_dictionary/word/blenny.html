blenny

1. NOUN

/ ˈblɛni / (AmE), / ˈblɛni / (BrE)
a small marine fish, typically scaleless and with comb-like teeth, that lives among rocky shores and is territorial
a small, rocky shore fish

Examples

There was a small blenny hiding between the rocks at low tide.
Marine biologists observed a colorful blenny defending its territory from intruders.
Several blennies were spotted darting among the crevices during the dive.

Etymology

From Latin 'blennius', from Greek 'blennos' (mucus, slime), referring to its slimy coating.
