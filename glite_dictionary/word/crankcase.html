crankcase

1. NOUN

/ ˈkræŋkˌkeɪs / (AmE), / ˈkraŋkˌkeɪs / (BrE)
The part of an engine housing the crankshaft.
engine housing for a crankshaft

Examples

The mechanic inspected the crankcase for signs of oil leakage.
A cracked crankcase can lead to serious engine damage if not repaired promptly.
Motorcycle engines often have a smaller crankcase than car engines.

Etymology

From 'crank' (mechanical arm) + 'case' (container, enclosure).
