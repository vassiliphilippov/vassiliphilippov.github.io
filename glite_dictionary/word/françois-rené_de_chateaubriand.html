François-René de Chateaubriand

1. PROPN

/ fʁɑ̃ˈswɑ ʁəˈneɪ də ʃa.toʊ.briˈɑ̃ / (AmE), / fʁɑ̃ˈswɑː ʁəˈneɪ də ʃa.təʊ.briˈɒ̃ / (BrE)
(1768–1848) A French writer and statesman, regarded as a forerunner of French Romanticism, known for his influential literary works and political influence.
French writer and Romanticism precursor

Examples

Many regard François-René de Chateaubriand as the father of French Romantic literature.
The political and literary legacy of François-René de Chateaubriand continues to influence French culture today.
His memoirs reveal the complexities of François-René de Chateaubriand's life as both a statesman and author.

Etymology

French personal name; 'Chateaubriand' from the name of his family estate in Brittany.
