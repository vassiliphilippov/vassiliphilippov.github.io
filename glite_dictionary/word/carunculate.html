carunculate

1. ADJ

/ kəˈrʌŋkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / kəˈrʌŋkjʊlət / (BrE)
Having a caruncle; possessing a fleshy growth or protuberance, especially in reference to animals or plants.
having a caruncle (fleshy growth)

Examples

Some species of birds are carunculate, displaying prominent fleshy growths on their heads or necks.
The botanist described the seeds as carunculate due to the appendage attached to their surface.
The veterinarian examined the carunculate tissue near the eye for signs of infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caruncle - a small, fleshy protuberance or outgrowth

Etymology

From Latin 'caruncula' (a small piece of flesh) + English suffix '-ate'.
