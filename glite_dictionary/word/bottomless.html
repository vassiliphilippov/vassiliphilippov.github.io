bottomless

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɑːtəmləs / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəm.ləs / (BrE)
so deep that the bottom cannot be seen or reached; extremely deep
extremely deep; having no visible bottom

Examples

The divers were fascinated by the seemingly bottomless cave beneath the ocean.
Legends spoke of a bottomless chasm that could swallow anything.
They peered into the bottomless pit, awestruck by its darkness and depth.

Synset Examples

a bottomless pit
a bottomless lake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deep - extending far down from the top or surface

Etymology

Formed from 'bottom' + '-less', meaning 'without a bottom'.

/ ˈbɑːt̬əm.ləs / (AmE), / ˈbɒt.əm.ləs / (BrE)
having no bottom or lacking a lower surface or part.
having no bottom

Examples

She wore a pair of bottomless pajamas as described in the catalog.
Urban legends often mention bottomless lakes with mysterious depths.

Synset Examples

3. ADJ

/ ˈbɑːtəmləs / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəmləs / (BrE)
Seemingly without any limit or end; endless or immeasurable.
without limit or end

Examples

They seemed to have a bottomless supply of patience for the relentless demands of toddlerhood.
Rumors spread about his bottomless bank account and extravagant lifestyle.
The museum's collection appears to be bottomless, with new treasures revealed every year.

Synset Examples

a bottomless supply of money
bottomless pockets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limitless - having no limit or boundary
  • Synonym: infinite - having no limits or end; endless
  • Antonym: finite - having limits or bounds

Etymology

Formed from 'bottom' + suffix '-less', meaning 'without a bottom'.
