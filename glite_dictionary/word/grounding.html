grounding

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊndɪŋ / (BrE)
basic training or instruction in the fundamentals of a subject or skill.
basic instruction in a subject

Examples

A solid grounding in grammar is essential for learning a new language.
She received a thorough grounding in classical music at an early age.
His grounding in science helped him understand the experiment quickly.

Synset Examples

he lacks the foundation necessary for advanced study
a good grounding in mathematics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foundation - the basic underlying support or beginning of something, especially knowledge or skill

Etymology

From the verb 'ground' (to base or establish) + -ing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊndɪŋ / (BrE)
The act or process of connecting electrical equipment to the earth to ensure safety by preventing unwanted voltages.
connecting electrical equipment to earth

Examples

Proper grounding is essential to minimize the risk of electric shock in your home.
The electrician checked the grounding of all new outlets during the renovation.
Serious accidents can occur if grounding procedures are not correctly followed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earthing - British term for connecting electrical equipment to earth

Etymology

From 'ground' (the earth) + '-ing', forming a noun denoting the act of grounding.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00149656-n
Synset POS: n
