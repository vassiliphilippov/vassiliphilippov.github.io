South Platte River

1. PROPN

/ saʊθ plæt ˈrɪvɚ / (AmE), / saʊθ plæt ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
A river in the central United States, flowing through Colorado and Nebraska as a tributary of the Platte River.
tributary river of the Platte in the U.S.

Examples

The South Platte River provides water for both agriculture and urban areas in Colorado.
Denver is located near the confluence of the South Platte River and Cherry Creek.
Many people enjoy fishing and kayaking along the South Platte River.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Platte River - major river in Nebraska, USA
