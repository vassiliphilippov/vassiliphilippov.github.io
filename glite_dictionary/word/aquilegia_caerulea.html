Aquilegia caerulea

1. NOUN

/ ˌækwɪˈliːdʒiə sɪˈruːliə / (AmE), / ˌakwɪˈliːdʒɪə sɪˈruːlɪə / (BrE)
A species of columbine native to the Rocky Mountains, characterized by its long-spurred blue and white flowers.
Rocky Mountain columbine species

Examples

The garden featured a vibrant patch of Aquilegia caerulea blooming beside the path.
Many hikers in Colorado admire the delicate beauty of Aquilegia caerulea each spring.
Botanists often study Aquilegia caerulea for its unique flower structure and coloration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: columbine - a plant of the genus Aquilegia, with distinctive spurred flowers

Etymology

From Latin 'Aquilegia' (the genus name, possibly from 'aquila' meaning eagle, due to the flower shape) and 'caerulea' meaning blue.
