Uria lomvia

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʊəriə ˈlɑmviə / (AmE), / ˈjʊərɪə ˈlɒmviə / (BrE)
a species of large, black-and-white seabird, also known as the thick-billed murre, found in Arctic and subarctic regions
thick-billed murre seabird species

Examples

The Uria lomvia nests in large colonies on rocky cliffs in the Arctic.
Researchers observed hundreds of Uria lomvia feeding in the frigid waters near Greenland.
Climate change poses a threat to the breeding grounds of Uria lomvia populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common murre - another species of murre, Uria aalge

Etymology

From the genus name 'Uria' and species 'lomvia', named from scientific Latin.
