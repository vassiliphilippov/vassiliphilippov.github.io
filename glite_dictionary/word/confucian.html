Confucian

1. ADJ

/ kənˈfjuːʃən / (AmE), / kənˈfjuːʃən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of Confucianism, the philosophical and ethical system based on the teachings of Confucius.
of or relating to Confucianism

Examples

The government's policies were influenced by Confucian values of respect for elders.
Her research focuses on Confucian teachings and their impact on East Asian societies.
Many schools in China still teach Confucian principles as part of their curriculum.

Synset Examples

Confucian ethics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Confucianism - the philosophical and ethical system of Confucius

Etymology

From Latin Confucius (the Latinized name of the Chinese philosopher Kong Fuzi) + -an.

2. NOUN

/ kənˈfjuːʃən / (AmE), / kənˈfjuːʃən / (BrE)
A person who believes in or follows the teachings of Confucius.
follower of Confucius

Examples

Many Confucians emphasize the importance of family values in their daily lives.
During the discussion, he described himself as a committed Confucian.
Modern Confucians often seek to apply ancient wisdom to contemporary social issues.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Taoist - a follower of the philosophical tradition of Taoism
  • Synonym: Confucianism - the system of ethical and philosophical teachings founded by Confucius

Etymology

From Confucius, the Latinized form of the Chinese philosopher's name Kong Fuzi, plus the suffix -an.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09974885-n
Synset POS: n
