misunderstanding

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪsʌndərˈstændɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌmɪsʌndəˈstændɪŋ / (BrE)
A failure to understand something correctly; a situation in which someone interprets something wrongly.
failure to understand correctly

Examples

A simple misunderstanding caused them to argue about the meeting time.
There was a misunderstanding between the client and the designer over the project's deadline.
Her apology resolved the misunderstanding that had upset their friendship.

Synset Examples

his misinterpretation of the question caused his error
there was no mistaking her meaning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misinterpretation - the act of interpreting something wrongly
  • Antonym: comprehension - the action or capability of understanding something

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + understanding.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɪsˌʌndərˈstændɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌmɪsʌndəˈstændɪŋ / (BrE)
A failure to understand something correctly; a mistaken belief or interpretation.
incorrect understanding; mistake

Examples

There was a misunderstanding about the time of the meeting, so several people arrived late.
Her anger was based on a simple misunderstanding that could have been avoided with better communication.
I apologize for the misunderstanding—I thought you wanted to cancel the reservation.

Synset Examples

he wasn't going to admit his mistake
make no mistake about his intentions
there must be some misunderstanding — I don't have a sister

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistake - an action or judgement that is wrong
  • Synonym: misinterpretation - an incorrect interpretation
  • Antonym: understanding - comprehension or grasp of something

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + understand + -ing; first recorded in the late 15th century.
