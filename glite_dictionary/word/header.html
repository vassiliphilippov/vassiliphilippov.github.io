header

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɛdə / (BrE)
a machine used in agriculture for cutting the heads off grain crops and moving them into a wagon or receptacle
machine for cutting grain heads

Examples

The old farm still has a header in the barn from the 1930s.
After harvesting wheat, the header was cleaned and stored for the next season.
The mechanic repaired the header to ensure it would cut efficiently during the harvest.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɛdə / (BrE)
a horizontal structural beam or member placed at the end of an opening to support the load from the ends of joists, studs, or rafters, transferring their weight to adjacent parallel members
structural beam supporting ends of joists, etc.

Examples

The carpenter installed a header above the window frame to support the roof load.
When remodeling, it's important not to remove a header without proper structural reinforcement.
A header ensures the weight from the upper floor is distributed to the adjoining wall studs.

7. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdər / (AmE), / ˈhɛdə / (BrE)
a line of text at the beginning of a section or page that indicates the subject of the content below it
heading at the start of a section or page

Examples

The report contained a clear header at the top of each page.
Please add a header to your document with your name and the date.
Without a proper header, readers might not understand what the section is about.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heading - a title at the head of a page or section
  • Antonym: footer - a line of text at the bottom of a page

Etymology

From 'head' + '-er', indicating something at the head or beginning.
