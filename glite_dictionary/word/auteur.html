auteur

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈtɜr / (AmE), / əʊˈtɜː / (BrE)
A filmmaker, usually a director, who has a distinct personal style and maintains strong creative control over their films.
film director with a personal style

Examples

Many critics consider Stanley Kubrick an auteur because of his distinctive vision and control over every aspect of his films.
The French New Wave celebrated the concept of the auteur in cinema.
She aspires to be an auteur, insisting on writing, directing, and editing all her own movies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: director - person who directs the making of a film

Etymology

From French auteur (author), ultimately from Latin auctor (originator, author).
