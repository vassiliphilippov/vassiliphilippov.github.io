Polyodon spathula

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑlioʊˈdɒn ˈspæʧələ / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈəʊdɒn ˈspætjʊlə / (BrE)
a large primitive freshwater fish of the Mississippi Valley, also known as the American paddlefish, noted for its long paddle-shaped snout
primitive freshwater fish with paddle-shaped snout

Examples

The Polyodon spathula is sometimes regarded as a living fossil due to its ancient lineage.
Fishermen in the Mississippi River occasionally catch a Polyodon spathula for scientific study.
The unique snout of the Polyodon spathula helps it detect plankton in murky waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paddlefish - common name for Polyodon spathula, a freshwater fish with a paddle-shaped snout

Etymology

From New Latin Polyodon (genus name, meaning 'many-toothed') + spathula (diminutive of Latin spatha, 'broad blade').
