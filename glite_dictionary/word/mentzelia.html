Mentzelia

1. NOUN

/ mɛnˈtsiːliə / (AmE), / mɛnˈtsiːliə / (BrE)
a genus of bristly herbs or subshrubs native to western America, lacking stinging hairs
genus of bristly herbs of western America

Examples

Several species of Mentzelia are commonly found in dry, open habitats across the American West.
The bristly leaves of Mentzelia distinguish it from many similar-looking wildflowers.
Botanists conducted a survey to catalogue the different Mentzelia species growing in the region.

Etymology

Named after German botanist Christian Mentzel (1622–1701).

2. NOUN

/ mɛnˈtsɛliə / (AmE), / mɛnˈtsɛliə / (BrE)
A genus of bristly herbs or subshrubs native to western America, lacking stinging hairs, commonly known as blazing stars or stickleafs.
genus of bristly herbs native to western America

Examples

Several species within the Mentzelia genus are known as blazing stars and are found in arid regions.
The Mentzelia plants have a distinctive bristly texture but do not possess stinging hairs.
Botanists studied the habitat preferences of various Mentzelia species in the desert.

Etymology

Named after Christian Mentzel, a German botanist (1622–1701).
