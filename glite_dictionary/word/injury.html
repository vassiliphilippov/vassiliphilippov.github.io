injury

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndʒəri / (AmE), / ˈɪndʒəri / (BrE)
Physical harm or damage to someone's body caused by an accident or attack.
physical harm or damage

Examples

He suffered a serious injury during the soccer match and had to leave the field.
The cyclist wore a helmet to reduce the risk of injury.
She returned to work after recovering from her injury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wound - an injury in which the skin or flesh is cut, torn, or broken
  • Synonym: hurt - physical pain or injury
  • Antonym: healing - process of becoming healthy after injury

Etymology

From Latin 'injuria' (a wrong, injustice).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪndʒəri / (AmE), / ˈɪndʒəri / (BrE)
A violation of another person's rights or legal interests, typically regarded as a wrong inflicted unjustly.
violation of rights; legal wrong
legal

Examples

The court found that the company had caused an injury to the plaintiff's rights.
Any injury to his reputation through defamation may result in legal action.
They sought compensation for the injury done to their property by the unlawful decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harm - physical or moral damage or injury

Etymology

From Latin 'injuria' (a wrong, injustice), from 'in-' (not) + 'jus' (law, right).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɪndʒəri / (AmE), / ˈɪndʒəri / (BrE)
An act or instance of causing physical harm or damage to a person or thing.
act causing physical harm or damage

Examples

The cyclist suffered a serious injury when he fell off his bike.
Repeated strain can lead to injury over time, even in seemingly safe activities.
The doctor examined the patient's injury and recommended immediate treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wound - an injury to body involving break of skin or tissue
  • Synonym: damage - physical harm that impairs the value, usefulness, or normal function of something
  • Antonym: healing - the process of becoming healthy again after an injury

Etymology

From Latin 'injuria' (wrong, hurt), from 'in-' (not) + 'jus' (law, right).
