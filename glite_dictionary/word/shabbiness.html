shabbiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃæbɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈʃabɪnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being shabby, especially due to wearing worn-out or dirty clothing; a lack of elegance or neatness.
state of being shabby or run-down

Examples

His shabbiness was obvious in his frayed coat and worn shoes.
Despite the shabbiness of her clothes, she carried herself with dignity.
The shabbiness of the old furniture contrasted with the grandeur of the room.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: elegance - graceful and stylish appearance or manner
  • Synonym: shabbiness - lack of elegance due to worn-out clothing

Etymology

From 'shabby' (probably from dialectal English *shab* ‘scab, shabby person’) + -ness.
