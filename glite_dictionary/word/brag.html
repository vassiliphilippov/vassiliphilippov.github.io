brag

1. NOUN

/ bræɡ / (AmE), / bræɡ / (BrE)
A boastful statement or act; an instance of showing off or talking with excessive pride about something.
boastful statement or behavior

Examples

Despite his brag about winning the competition, no one seemed impressed.
Her constant brag of having the best grades annoyed her classmates.
Every brag he made was met with rolled eyes from the group.

Synset Examples

his brag is worse than his fight
whenever he won we were exposed to his gasconade

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boast - an act of talking with excessive pride
  • Antonym: modesty - lack of boastfulness or humility

Etymology

From Middle English braggen (to boast), of uncertain origin.

2. VERB

/ bræɡ / (AmE), / bræɡ / (BrE)
to talk with excessive pride about oneself or one's achievements, possessions, or abilities; to show off
talk boastfully about achievements

Examples

He loves to brag about his new car whenever he gets the chance.
She couldn't help but brag after winning first place in the competition.
Instead of being modest, he always brags about his accomplishments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boast - to talk with excessive pride about oneself
  • Antonym: humble - modest or not showing pride

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'braggen' (to boast), of uncertain origin.
