molluscum

1. NOUN

/ məˈlʌskəm / (AmE), / məˈlʌskəm / (BrE)
A skin disease marked by soft, pulpy nodules or tumors.
skin disease with soft nodules
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the rash as molluscum and prescribed a topical cream.
Children are more prone to developing molluscum due to their sensitive skin.
The presence of multiple molluscum nodules can be alarming but is usually not serious.

Etymology

From Latin 'molluscum' (a soft growth), from 'mollis' (soft).
