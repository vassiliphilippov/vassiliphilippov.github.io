ignoramus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪɡnəˈreɪməs / (AmE), / ˌɪɡnəˈreɪməs / (BrE)
a person who is ignorant or lacks knowledge in a particular subject or in general
an ignorant person

Examples

Only an ignoramus would believe such obvious misinformation.
The teacher was frustrated by the ignoramus who never paid attention in class.
Though he was called an ignoramus, he proved everyone wrong by solving the problem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool - a person who acts unwisely or lacks sense
  • Antonym: sage - a wise person

Etymology

From Latin 'ignoramus' (we do not know), first used as a legal term.
