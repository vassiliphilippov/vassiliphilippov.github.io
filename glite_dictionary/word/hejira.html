Hejira

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈdʒɪrə / (AmE), / hɪˈdʒɪərə / (BrE)
the migration of Muhammad from Mecca to Medina in 622 AD, marking the beginning of the Islamic calendar.
Muhammad's migration from Mecca to Medina, 622 AD

Examples

The Islamic calendar begins with the Hejira in 622 AD.
Historians often cite the Hejira as a turning point in Islamic history.
Muslims commemorate the Hejira as a symbol of faith and resilience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hegira - another spelling of Hejira referring to the same migration

Etymology

From Arabic *hijra* (migration, emigration), referring to Muhammad’s journey.
