stubbornness

1. NOUN

/ ˈstʌbərnnəs / (AmE), / ˈstʌbənnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being unwilling to change one's attitude or position, often making one difficult to handle or overcome
quality of being difficult to change or persuade

Examples

Her stubbornness made it hard for the team to agree on the best approach.
Despite his stubbornness, she eventually convinced him to try the new restaurant.
Sometimes, stubbornness can be a virtue when standing up for what is right.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstinacy - the quality of being stubborn, refusing to change one's mind
  • Antonym: flexibility - the quality of being adaptable or willing to change

Etymology

From 'stubborn' + '-ness'; 'stubborn' from Middle English 'stibourne'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstʌbərnnəs / (AmE), / ˈstʌbənnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being determined to do or continue with something despite good reasons to stop; resolute refusal to change one's ideas or behavior
quality of being stubborn

Examples

Her stubbornness made it impossible to persuade her to try a new approach.
Despite their stubbornness, the children eventually agreed to share their toys.
His stubbornness was both admired and criticized by his colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstinacy - refusal to change one's behavior or opinion; stubbornness
  • Antonym: flexibility - willingness to change or adapt; opposite of stubbornness

Etymology

From 'stubborn' + '-ness', with 'stubborn' from Old French *estuborne*.
