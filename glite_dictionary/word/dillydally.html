to dillydally

1. VERB

/ ˈdɪliˌdæli / (AmE), / ˈdɪlɪˌdæli / (BrE)
to waste time by being slow or indecisive; to delay taking action, especially because of hesitation or indecision
waste time by delaying or hesitating

Examples

Stop dillydallying and finish your homework before dinner.
He tends to dillydally in the mornings instead of getting ready for work.
They dillydallied so long that they missed their bus.

Synset Examples

He did not want to write the letter and procrastinated for days

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to procrastinate - to delay or postpone action
  • Antonym: to hasten - to move or act quickly

Etymology

Reduplication of 'dally', meaning to waste time, of uncertain origin, first recorded in the 18th century.
