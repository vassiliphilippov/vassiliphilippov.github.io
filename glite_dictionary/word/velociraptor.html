Velociraptor

1. NOUN

/ vəˌlɑːsɪˈræptər / (AmE), / vɪˌlɒsɪˈræptə / (BrE)
A genus of small, fast-moving, carnivorous theropod dinosaurs from the Late Cretaceous period, known for their agility and sickle-shaped claws.
genus of small carnivorous dinosaurs

Examples

The Velociraptor is often depicted as a cunning predator in popular movies.
Scientists discovered new fossils belonging to the Velociraptor genus in Mongolia.
The swift movement of the Velociraptor made it a formidable hunter during the Cretaceous period.

Etymology

From Latin 'velox' (swift) and 'raptor' (robber, plunderer).
