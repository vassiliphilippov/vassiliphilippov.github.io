quayage

1. NOUN

/ ˈkiːɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkiːɪdʒ / (BrE)
a fee charged for the use of a wharf or quay
fee for using a wharf or quay

Examples

The shipping company paid a substantial quayage to dock at the busy port.
Port authorities raised the quayage to cover the cost of new facilities.
Importers must factor quayage into their overall shipping expenses.

Etymology

From 'quay' (French *quai*) + '-age' (fee or charge).
