Agropyron smithii

1. NOUN

/ əˌɡroʊˌpaɪrɒn ˈsmɪθiː / (AmE), / əˌɡrəʊˌpʌɪrɒn ˈsmɪθiː / (BrE)
A species of valuable forage grass, also known as western wheatgrass, native to the western United States and commonly used for grazing and soil conservation.
western U.S. forage grass species

Examples

Cattle ranchers often plant Agropyron smithii to provide nutritious grazing pastures.
Conservation projects use Agropyron smithii to help prevent soil erosion in the Great Plains.
Researchers are studying how Agropyron smithii tolerates drought conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: western wheatgrass - Common name for Agropyron smithii, a forage grass species

Etymology

From genus name Agropyron (Neo-Latin, from Greek agros 'field' + pyros 'wheat'), and species named after botanist J. W. Smith.
