embossment

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈbɑsmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɒsmənt / (BrE)
An impression or raised design produced on a surface by pressing or printing.
raised impression or design

Examples

The certificate featured an official embossment in the lower corner.
He studied the embossment on the book's leather cover.
A decorative embossment gives the invitation a touch of elegance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emboss - to raise or mold into a surface by pressure

Etymology

From Middle French 'embosser' (to bulge out), from Old French 'bos' (hump, boss).

2. NOUN

/ ɛmˈbɔsmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɒsmənt / (BrE)
a raised design or pattern carved, molded, or stamped onto a surface so that it stands out from the background
raised design on a surface

Examples

The ancient temple walls were decorated with intricate embossments depicting historical scenes.
She admired the delicate floral embossment on the silver vase.
The book cover featured a beautiful embossment that made it stand out on the shelf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relief - sculptural technique where elements are raised from the background

Etymology

From 'emboss,' from Old French 'embosser' (to raise a boss on), from 'bosse' (a protuberance).

Synset POS: n
