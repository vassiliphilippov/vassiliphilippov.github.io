to happen

1. VERB

/ ˈhæpən / (AmE), / ˈhæpən / (BrE)
to do or be something by chance, without planning or intention
do or be by chance

Examples

I happen to know the answer, if you'd like some help.
Do you happen to have a pen I can borrow?
She happened to meet an old friend at the supermarket.

Synset Examples

I happen to have just what you need!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to occur - to happen or take place, especially unexpectedly

Etymology

From Middle English 'happenen', from 'hap' (chance, fortune).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02600068-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈhæpən / (AmE), / ˈhap(ə)n / (BrE)
to take place, especially without being planned or intended; to occur or come about by chance
occur or take place

Examples

Strange things can happen when you least expect them.
It just so happened that she was in town that day.
What will happen if we don't finish the project on time?

Synset Examples

It happens that today is my birthday
These things befell

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to occur - to happen or take place
  • Degree Lower: to become - to begin to be; to come into existence

Etymology

From Middle English 'hap' (chance, luck) plus '-en' (verb suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00345297-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈhæpən / (AmE), / ˈhæpən / (BrE)
to take place, especially without being planned; to occur
to take place or occur

Examples

No one expected such a strange event to happen in our small town.
Accidents can happen when you least expect them, so always stay alert.
It really did happen, even if it sounds unbelievable.

Synset Examples

What is happening?
The meeting took place without an incident
Nothing occurred that seemed important

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to occur - to happen, especially unexpectedly or by chance
  • Synonym: to take place - to happen or occur, especially of an event
  • Antonym: cause - to make something happen

Etymology

From Middle English 'happenen', from 'hap' (chance, luck) + -en (verb suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00340744-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈhæpən / (AmE), / ˈhæpən / (BrE)
to come into existence or become a reality; materialize
come into existence; become reality

Examples

After years of planning, their dream to move abroad finally happened.
She worked hard, but never believed her big break would actually happen.
Sometimes, the things we imagine seem impossible until they happen in real life.

Synset Examples

Her dream really materialized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to materialize - to become real or actual
  • Synonym: to occur - to take place or come about
