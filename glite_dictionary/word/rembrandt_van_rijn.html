Rembrandt van Rijn

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɛm.brænt væn ˈraɪn / (AmE), / ˈrɛm.brænt væn ˈraɪn / (BrE)
(1606–1669) A renowned Dutch painter and etcher, widely regarded as one of the greatest visual artists in the history of art.
famous Dutch painter (1606–1669)

Examples

The self-portraits of Rembrandt van Rijn reveal his extraordinary skill and deep psychological insight.
Many art historians consider Rembrandt van Rijn a master of light and shadow.
A painting by Rembrandt van Rijn is displayed in the world’s leading museums.

Etymology

Dutch, from Rembrandt (given name) and van Rijn (of the Rhine, after the Rhine river in the Netherlands).
