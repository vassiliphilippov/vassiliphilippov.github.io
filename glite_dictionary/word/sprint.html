sprint

1. NOUN

/ sprɪnt / (AmE), / sprɪnt / (BrE)
a short, fast run, especially at full speed over a short distance
short fast run

Examples

She finished the race with a final sprint to the line.
After jogging for several minutes, he broke into a sprint.
The team practiced their sprints to improve speed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dash - a short, fast run or movement
  • Antonym: marathon - a long-distance running race of 42.195 kilometers

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *sprenten* (to jump, leap).

WordNet ID: oewn-00295296-n

2. VERB

/ sprɪnt / (AmE), / sprɪnt / (BrE)
to run at full speed over a short distance
run very fast for a short distance

Examples

She decided to sprint the last hundred meters to win the race.
The dog loves to sprint across the open field every morning.
After the whistle blew, all the players began to sprint toward the ball.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dash - run or move quickly and suddenly
  • Degree Lower: jog - run at a steady, moderate pace

Etymology

From Middle English 'sprenten' (to spring, leap), related to 'spring.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01932532-v
Synset POS: v
