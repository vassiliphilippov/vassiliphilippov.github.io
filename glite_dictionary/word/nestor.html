Nestor

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛs.tər / (AmE), / ˈnɛs.tɔː / (BrE)
A genus of parrots native to New Zealand and surrounding islands, including both extant and extinct species.
genus of parrots native to New Zealand

Examples

The Nestor genus includes the well-known kea and kaka parrots of New Zealand.
Ornithologists debate the evolutionary history of Nestor species on nearby islands.
The extinction of several Nestor parrots is attributed to habitat loss and introduced predators.

Etymology

Named after Nestor, a wise king in Greek mythology; used as a scientific genus name.

2. PROPN

/ ˈnɛstər / (AmE), / ˈnɛstə / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a wise old king of Pylos who served as counselor to the Greeks during the Trojan War; by extension, any wise old man or senior advisor.
wise old counselor in Greek mythology

Examples

The advisor was regarded as the Nestor of the company, offering wisdom and guidance to younger executives.
Homer described Nestor as a respected leader who shared his experiences from past battles.
In times of crisis, the community looked to their own Nestor for thoughtful advice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sage - a profoundly wise person

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Νέστωρ (Néstōr), the legendary king of Pylos.
