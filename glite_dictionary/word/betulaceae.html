Betulaceae

1. NOUN

/ bɛtʃuˈleɪsiˌi / (AmE), / bɛtjʊˈleɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of monoecious trees and shrubs, including birches, alders, hornbeams, hazels, and related genera.
family of birches, alders, hazels, etc.
medical

Examples

The Betulaceae family includes familiar trees such as birches and alders.
Several members of the Betulaceae are important for timber and landscaping.
Researchers are studying pollen dispersal within the Betulaceae family to understand allergy trends.

Etymology

From the genus name Betula (birch) plus Latin -aceae, a standard ending for plant family names.
