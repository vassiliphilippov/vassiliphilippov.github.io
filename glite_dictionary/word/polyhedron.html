polyhedron

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈhiːdrən / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈhiːdrən / (BrE)
A three-dimensional solid figure with flat faces, each of which is a polygon.
three-dimensional solid with flat polygonal faces

Examples

A cube is a simple example of a polyhedron with six square faces.
The artist created a sculpture inspired by the shapes of various polyhedra.
Mathematicians often study the properties of a polyhedron to understand geometry in three dimensions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prism - a solid geometric figure with two parallel congruent faces (bases) and other faces that are parallelograms
  • Degree Lower: cube - a polyhedron with six square faces

Etymology

From Greek poly- (many) + hēdra (seat, base).
