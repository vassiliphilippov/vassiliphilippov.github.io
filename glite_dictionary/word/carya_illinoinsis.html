Carya illinoinsis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛəriə ɪˌloʊ.nɔɪˈɛn.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈkaːrɪə ɪˌlɪ.nɔɪˈɛn.sɪs / (BrE)
a large tree native to the southern United States and Mexico, cultivated for its edible nuts known as pecans
pecan tree of North America

Examples

The Carya illinoinsis is prized for producing delicious pecans in southern orchards.
Farmers in Texas often plant Carya illinoinsis for both shade and nut production.
The leaves of Carya illinoinsis are long and pinnate, giving the tree a distinctive appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pecan - the nut produced by the Carya illinoinsis tree

Etymology

From the genus name Carya (from Greek kárya ‘nut tree’) and the species name illinoinsis referring to Illinois.
