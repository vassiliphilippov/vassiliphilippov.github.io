Pelargonium hortorum

1. NOUN

/ pɛlɑːrˈɡoʊniəm hɔːrˈtɔːrəm / (AmE), / pɛlɑːˈɡəʊniəm hɔːˈtɔːrəm / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant commonly known as zonal geranium, featuring scalloped leaves with a broad colored zone and white, pink, or red flowers.
zonal geranium plant

Examples

Many gardeners prefer Pelargonium hortorum for its bright, long-lasting blooms in summer beds.
The distinctive band of color on its leaves makes Pelargonium hortorum easy to identify among geraniums.
She planted several Pelargonium hortorum in pots along her balcony for vivid splashes of color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zonal geranium - common name for Pelargonium hortorum
  • Degree Lower: Pelargonium - the plant genus to which Pelargonium hortorum belongs

Etymology

From genus name Pelargonium (Greek pelargos, stork) + Latin hortorum (of gardens).
