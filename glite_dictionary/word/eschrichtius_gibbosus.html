Eschrichtius gibbosus

1. NOUN

/ ɛsˈkrɪktiˌəs ɡɪˈboʊsəs / (AmE), / ɛsˈkrɪktɪˌʊs ɡɪˈbəʊsəs / (BrE)
A medium-sized greyish-black baleen whale of the northern Pacific, commonly known as the gray whale.
medium-sized whale of the northern Pacific

Examples

Sightings of Eschrichtius gibbosus are common during their annual migration along the Pacific coast.
Conservationists have studied the reproductive habits of Eschrichtius gibbosus to better protect the species.
A pod of Eschrichtius gibbosus was spotted breaching near the shores of Baja California.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gray whale - common name for Eschrichtius gibbosus, a baleen whale of the northern Pacific

Etymology

From Latin; named after Danish zoologist Daniel Eschricht, with 'gibbosus' meaning 'humped' in Latin.
