Iridaceae

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌraɪdəˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪdəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a large family of perennial flowering plants, usually with bulbs, rhizomes, or corms, including irises and related species
family of perennial plants (irises etc.)

Examples

Members of the Iridaceae family include well-known garden irises and crocuses.
Many Iridaceae species thrive in temperate climates around the world.
Botanists study the Iridaceae for their unique underground storage organs and diverse flowers.

Etymology

From Latin *Iris* (the iris) + -aceae (family suffix in plant taxonomy).
