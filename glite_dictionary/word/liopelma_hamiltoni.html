Liopelma hamiltoni

1. NOUN

/ liˌoʊˈpɛlmə ˌhæmɪlˈtoʊaɪ / (AmE), / liˌəʊˈpɛlmə ˌhæmɪlˈtəʊaɪ / (BrE)
A primitive species of frog native to New Zealand, distinguished by four unwebbed toes on its forefeet and five on its hind feet.
species of primitive New Zealand frog

Examples

The endangered Liopelma hamiltoni is rarely seen in the wild due to its limited habitat.
Researchers are studying Liopelma hamiltoni to better understand primitive frog evolution.
A sanctuary was established to help preserve the unique Liopelma hamiltoni population.

Etymology

Named after Hamilton, likely a scientist or place, with genus from Greek 'leios' (smooth) + 'pelma' (sole of the foot).
