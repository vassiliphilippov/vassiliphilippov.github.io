disyllable

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈsɪləbəl / (AmE), / daɪˈsɪləbəl / (BrE)
A word consisting of two syllables.
word with two syllables

Examples

The word "table" is a classic example of a disyllable.
Children often learn to read by clapping out each disyllable they see.
Poets sometimes use disyllable words to create a specific rhythm in their verses.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: monosyllable - word with one syllable
  • Degree Higher: trisyllable - word with three syllables

Etymology

From Greek 'disyllabos', from 'di-' (two) + 'syllabē' (syllable).
