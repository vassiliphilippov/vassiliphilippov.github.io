tusker

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌskər / (AmE), / ˈtʌskə / (BrE)
a large mammal with prominent tusks, especially an elephant or wild boar
animal with prominent tusks

Examples

The old tusker roamed the savanna alone, a legend among local villagers.
Poachers often target the largest tusker for their valuable ivory.
During the safari, we spotted a wild tusker bathing in the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elephant - large land mammal with a trunk and tusks
  • Synonym: boar - a wild pig, especially a male with tusks

Etymology

From 'tusk' + -er, denoting an animal possessing tusks.
