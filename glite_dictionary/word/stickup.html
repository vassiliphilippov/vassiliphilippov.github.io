stickup

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪkˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈstɪkˌʌp / (BrE)
an act of robbery, especially one committed at gunpoint
robbery at gunpoint

Examples

The police arrived too late to stop the stickup at the grocery store last night.
He was arrested for planning a stickup at the local bank.
During the stickup, the robbers demanded all the money from the safe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robbery - the taking of property illegally from a person or place by force or threat

Etymology

From the phrase 'stick up', referring to the command to raise one's hands during a robbery.
