Nostoc

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑstɑk / (AmE), / ˈnɒstɒk / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater blue-green algae that form colonies, often found in moist environments; type genus of the family Nostocaceae.
genus of freshwater blue-green algae

Examples

The scientist studied a sample of Nostoc collected from a local pond.
Some Nostoc species can survive in extreme environmental conditions such as deserts and arctic regions.
Colonies of Nostoc often appear as gelatinous masses in freshwater habitats.

Etymology

From New Latin Nostoc, coined by Paracelsus, likely of unknown origin.
