nostoc

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːstɑk / (AmE), / ˈnɒstɒk / (BrE)
A genus of cyanobacteria that forms rounded, jellylike colonies, typically found in moist places.
a genus of jellylike cyanobacteria

Examples

The pond surface was dotted with colonies of nostoc after the summer rain.
Scientists studied the unique structure of nostoc under a microscope.
Some people once believed that nostoc appeared spontaneously after heavy rain.

Etymology

From New Latin Nostoc, coined by Paracelsus (16th century).
