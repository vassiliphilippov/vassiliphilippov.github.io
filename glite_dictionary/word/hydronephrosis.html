hydronephrosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdroʊnəˈfroʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəʊnɪˈfrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a medical condition in which urine accumulates in the kidney due to an obstruction in the ureter, causing the kidney to swell
swelling of kidney from urine buildup
medical

Examples

The ultrasound revealed hydronephrosis in the patient's right kidney.
Untreated kidney stones can sometimes lead to hydronephrosis and eventual kidney damage.
Early diagnosis of hydronephrosis is important to prevent permanent loss of kidney function.

Etymology

From Greek 'hydro' (water) and 'nephros' (kidney).
