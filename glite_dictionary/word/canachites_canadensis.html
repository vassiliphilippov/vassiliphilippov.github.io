Canachites canadensis

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnæk.ɪ.tiːz kəˌnæˈdɛn.sɪs / (AmE), / kəˈnæk.ɪ.tiːz kəˌnæˈdɛn.sɪs / (BrE)
A North American grouse species that feeds on evergreen buds and needles, commonly known as the spruce grouse.
a North American grouse species (spruce grouse)

Examples

The Canachites canadensis is well adapted to the northern boreal forests of Canada and Alaska.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Canachites canadensis during winter months.
The diet of Canachites canadensis consists primarily of spruce and pine needles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spruce grouse - Common name for Canachites canadensis, a North American grouse species

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name 'Canachites' and Latin 'canadensis' meaning 'of Canada'.
