Liverpool

1. PROPN

/ ˈlɪvərˌpul / (AmE), / ˈlɪvəpuːl / (BrE)
a large city in northwestern England known for its major port, cultural heritage, and history as a center for industrial exports.
major city and port in northwestern England

Examples

The Beatles famously grew up in Liverpool before becoming international stars.
Exports from Liverpool helped fuel Britain's industrial growth during the 19th century.
Many tourists visit Liverpool to see its historic docks and museums.

Etymology

From Old English 'Liferpōl', meaning 'pool or creek of muddy water'.
