bilberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪlˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈbɪl.bəri / (BrE)
A small, dark blue edible berry of a low-growing shrub, found especially in Europe and North America; also the shrub itself.
edible dark blue berry or its shrub

Examples

We picked wild bilberries in the forest for our pie.
The bilberry bush blooms in early spring and produces fruit by midsummer.
Bilberries are often used to make jams, desserts, and herbal medicines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blueberry - an edible blue berry and the related shrub, mainly in North America

Etymology

From Middle English 'bilberie', probably from Scandinavian (compare Danish 'bøllebær').

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɪlˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈbɪl.bəri / (BrE)
A small European shrub bearing blue-black edible berries similar to the American blueberry, or the berry itself.
blue-black berry similar to blueberry

Examples

The hillsides were covered with bilberry bushes laden with ripe fruit.
She added fresh bilberries to the pie for extra flavor.
During our walk, we stopped to pick wild bilberries along the forest path.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blueberry - North American shrub with similar edible berries

Etymology

From Middle English bilberie, of Scandinavian origin, related to Danish 'böllebær' and Swedish 'blåbär' (blue-berry).

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɪlˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈbɪl.bəri / (BrE)
A low-growing shrub native to Europe, bearing solitary flowers and edible blue-black berries similar to blueberries.
European shrub with edible blue-black berries

Examples

The forests in Scandinavia are well known for their abundant bilberry bushes.
She picked a handful of ripe bilberries to add to her breakfast cereal.
Bilberry jam is a traditional treat in many parts of Northern Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blueberry - A similar North American shrub with edible blue berries

Etymology

From Middle English *bilberie*, likely from Scandinavian origin, akin to Danish *böllebær*.
