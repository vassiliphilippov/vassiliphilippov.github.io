highwater

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪˌwɔtɚ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
the state of the tide when the water reaches its highest level
highest level of the tide

Examples

Fishermen waited for highwater before heading out to sea.
The river overflowed its banks at highwater last night.
We scheduled the boat launch for highwater to ensure safe passage.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lowwater - the state of the tide when the water is lowest

Etymology

From 'high' + 'water', referring to the water's greatest height (tide).
