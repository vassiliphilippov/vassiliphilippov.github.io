caret

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛrət / (AmE), / ˈkærət / (BrE)
a mark (‸) used by writers or editors to show where something should be inserted into written or printed text
editing mark for insertions

Examples

Would you please add a caret where the missing word should go?
The editor inserted a caret between the two lines to indicate an addition.
He overlooked the caret and forgot to add the suggested phrase.

Etymology

From Latin caret, meaning 'there is lacking', used in proofreading to indicate omission.
