semi-climbing

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛmiˈklaɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈklaɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a plant) partially climbing; having a growth habit that is somewhat but not fully climbing.
partially climbing (plant)

Examples

The garden featured several semi-climbing roses that needed minimal support to grow upright.
This species is a semi-climbing vine, often sprawling over low shrubs for extra height.
Some beans are semi-climbing, making them easy to train on short trellises or fences.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: climbing - (of a plant) growing upwards by clinging or twining

Etymology

Formed from 'semi-' (meaning 'half' or 'partial', from Latin) and 'climbing'.
