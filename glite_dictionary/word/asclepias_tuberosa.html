Asclepias tuberosa

1. NOUN

/ əˈskliːpiəs tuːˈbəroʊsə / (AmE), / əsˈkliːpiəs tjuːˈbɛrəsə / (BrE)
a perennial plant native to eastern and southern United States, known for its bright orange flowers and attraction to butterflies; commonly called butterfly weed
a North American plant with orange flowers; butterfly weed

Examples

The garden was filled with Asclepias tuberosa, attracting dozens of Monarch butterflies.
Botanists recommend planting Asclepias tuberosa to support local pollinators.
She identified the vibrant orange flowers as Asclepias tuberosa during their nature walk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butterfly weed - common name for Asclepias tuberosa, a North American milkweed species

Etymology

Genus name from Greek Asklēpias (Asclepius, Greek god of medicine); species from Latin tuberosus (full of lumps or swellings).
