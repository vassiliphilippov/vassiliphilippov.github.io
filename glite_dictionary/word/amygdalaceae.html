Amygdalaceae

1. NOUN

/ əˌmɪg.dəˈleɪ.si.i / (AmE), / əˌmɪg.dəˈleɪ.siː.iː / (BrE)
a former botanical family that included plum, peach, and almond trees, now usually classified under the genus Prunus
former botanical family for plum and almond trees

Examples

The genus Prunus was once placed in the Amygdalaceae family in earlier classifications.
Today, taxonomists no longer recognize Amygdalaceae as a valid botanical family.
Historical texts may refer to the plum as a member of Amygdalaceae.

Etymology

From Latin *amygdala* (almond) + -aceae, a suffix used for plant family names.

2. NOUN

/ əˌmɪɡdəˈleɪsiː / (AmE), / əˌmɪɡdəˈleɪsiː / (BrE)
a former botanical family that included plum, peach, and almond trees, now usually classified within the genus Prunus.
former plant family for plums, peaches, and almonds

Examples

Plums and almonds were once classified in the Amygdalaceae family before being moved to Prunus.
Many older botanical texts refer to peaches as members of the Amygdalaceae.
The term Amygdalaceae is now largely historical in plant taxonomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Prunus - the genus including plums, peaches, and almonds

Etymology

From Latin amygdala (almond), from Greek amygdalē, with the botanical suffix -aceae indicating a family.
