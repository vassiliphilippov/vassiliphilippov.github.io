Lammas

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæm əs / (AmE), / ˈlæm əs / (BrE)
A Christian festival held on August 1, formerly observed in England and Scotland to mark the beginning of the harvest, and commemorating Saint Peter's miraculous deliverance from prison.
Christian harvest festival (August 1)

Examples

The villagers celebrated Lammas with a special church service and a communal feast.
In the Middle Ages, farmers brought loaves baked from new wheat to mark Lammas.
Lammas once served as an important division of the farming year in rural England.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harvest festival - Festival celebrating the gathering of crops

Etymology

From Old English hlāfmæsse, meaning 'loaf mass', referring to the offering of the first new bread of the harvest.
