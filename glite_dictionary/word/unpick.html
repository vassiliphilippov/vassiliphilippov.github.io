to unpick

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈpɪk / (AmE), / ʌnˈpɪk / (BrE)
to remove stitches from fabric, or to undo something by separating its threads or fibers
remove stitches or undo by separating threads

Examples

She had to unpick the stitches to correct her mistake.
Carefully unpick the seam so you can add the new panel of fabric.
After making an error, he painstakingly unpicked the hem.

Synset Examples

unravel the thread

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unravel - to undo twisted, knitted, or woven threads

Etymology

From 'un-' (a prefix meaning 'reverse an action') + 'pick' (to pull apart), first attested in the late 16th century.

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈpɪk / (AmE), / ʌnˈpɪk / (BrE)
to undo the stitches of (a piece of sewing) by removing the thread
undo the stitches of sewing

Examples

She had to unpick the hem to shorten the dress.
He patiently unpicked the seams to alter the fit of his shirt.
While unpicking the stitching, she found the fabric was torn underneath.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to sew - to join or repair something by making stitches with a needle and thread
  • Synonym: unstitch - to take out stitches from something that has been sewn

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not, opposite of) + 'pick' (to extract or remove), first recorded in the 1800s.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01332669-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ʌnˈpɪk / (AmE), / ʌnˈpɪk / (BrE)
to carefully examine and separate the parts of a complex problem or situation in order to understand or resolve it
analyze or resolve something complex

Examples

It took the lawyer hours to unpick the complicated legal arguments in the case.
The historian aimed to unpick the causes of the conflict over decades.
She tried to unpick what had gone wrong in the failed experiment.

Synset Examples

He took care to unpick the puzzle pieces gently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to analyze - to examine something in detail to understand it better
  • Synonym: to untangle - to make something less complicated by resolving connections or confusion

Etymology

From un- (reverse) + pick (to pull apart).
