brachiate

1. ADJ

/ ˈbreɪkiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈbrækɪeɪt / (BrE)
having branches that spread widely in pairs from a main axis, especially in plants or animals
having paired spreading branches

Examples

The maple tree is known for its brachiate branching pattern.
Some corals develop brachiate structures to maximize light exposure.
The fossil showed a brachiate form, with limbs branching out in pairs.

Synset Examples

maples are brachiate

Etymology

From Latin brachiatus, from brachium (arm, branch).

2. ADJ

/ ˈbreɪkiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈbrækɪeɪt / (BrE)
(of an animal or plant) having arms or armlike appendages.
having arms or armlike appendages

Examples

Unlike other fish, the brachiate species have developed fin structures resembling arms.
The fossil showed a brachiate form, with elongated limb-like appendages.
Some plants are described as brachiate due to branch structures that look like arms.

Etymology

From Latin brachiatus, from brachium (arm).

3. VERB

/ ˈbreɪkiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈbrækiˌeɪt / (BrE)
(of certain animals, especially primates) to move by swinging from one hold to another using the arms.
swing from hold to hold with the arms

Examples

Gibbons are well known to brachiate through the forest canopy.
Some athletes can brachiate across monkey bars with surprising speed.
Scientists observed how easily the monkeys brachiated from branch to branch.

Synset Examples

the monkeys brachiate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swing - move back and forth or from side to side while suspended or on an axis

Etymology

From Latin 'brachium' meaning 'arm'.
