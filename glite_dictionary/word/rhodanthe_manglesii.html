Rhodanthe manglesii

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈdænθi ˌmæŋˈɡliː.aɪ / (AmE), / rəʊˈdanθiː ˌmæŋˈɡliː.aɪ / (BrE)
An Australian annual everlasting plant with light pink, nodding flower heads, sometimes classified under the genus Helipterum.
Australian annual flowering plant

Examples

Rhodanthe manglesii is often grown in gardens for its delicate pink blooms and long-lasting flowers.
The natural habitats of Rhodanthe manglesii are the sandy soils of Western Australia.
Many florists use dried Rhodanthe manglesii in floral arrangements due to its enduring appearance.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Rhodanthe', Greek *rhodon* (rose) + *anthos* (flower); specific epithet honors English botanist James Mangles.
