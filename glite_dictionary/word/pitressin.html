Pitressin

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈtrɛsɪn / (AmE), / pɪˈtrɛsɪn / (BrE)
a trade name for vasopressin, a hormone secreted by the posterior pituitary gland that increases blood pressure and reduces urine output
trade name for vasopressin, a hormone
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Pitressin to help control the patient's low blood pressure.
High doses of Pitressin may lead to reduced urine output as a side effect.
Pitressin is sometimes administered during surgery to maintain stable blood pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vasopressin - the hormone also known as antidiuretic hormone (ADH), secreted by the pituitary gland
