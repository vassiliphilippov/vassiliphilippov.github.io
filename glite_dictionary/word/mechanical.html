mechanical

1. ADJ

/ məˈkænɪkəl / (AmE), / mɪˈkænɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to machines, machinery, or tools, or being operated by them
relating to machines or machinery

Examples

The engineer developed a mechanical device to perform the task automatically.
She showed excellent mechanical skills when repairing the broken engine.
Many mechanical systems require regular maintenance to function properly.

Synset Examples

mechanical arts
mechanical design
mechanical skills
a technical fault

Related Terms

  • Antonym: manual - done by hand, not by machine

Etymology

From Late Latin mechanicus (pertaining to machines), from Ancient Greek mēkhanikos.

2. ADJ

/ məˈkænɪkəl / (AmE), / mɪˈkænɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or operated by physical forces or principles of mechanics, rather than by human or natural agency; pertaining to mechanics.
relating to mechanics or physical forces

Examples

The engineer designed a mechanical system to automate the process.
Some diseases can be treated with mechanical devices that assist bodily functions.
The mechanical strength of the material is crucial for its use in construction.

Synset Examples

a belief that the universe is a mechanical contrivance
the mechanical pressure of a strong wind

Related Terms

  • Antonym: manual - operated by hand rather than by machine

Etymology

From Late Latin mechanicus (of or pertaining to machines), from Greek mēkhanikos.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02902184-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ məˈkænɪkəl / (AmE), / mɪˈkænɪkəl / (BrE)
operated by or relating to machines or tools; resembling the movement or behavior of a machine rather than a living thing
relating to or operated by machines or mechanisms

Examples

The old clock is powered by a mechanical winding mechanism.
Her response sounded mechanical, as if she were reciting a script.
We were fascinated by the mechanical dinosaur at the science museum.

Synset Examples

a mechanical process
his smile was very mechanical
a mechanical toy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: automatic - working by itself with little or no direct human control

Etymology

From Medieval Latin mechanicus (relating to machines), from Greek mēkhanikos (engineer).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01503365-a
Synset POS: a
