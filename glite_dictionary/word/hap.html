hap

1. NOUN

/ hæp / (AmE), / hæp / (BrE)
an occurrence, especially one that happens by chance; a happening or incident, often accidental
chance happening; occurrence
archaic

Examples

He recorded every little hap and adventure that came his way.
By mere hap, they arrived just as the rain stopped.
In those days, many believed that luck or hap could determine one’s fate.

Synset Examples

he recorded all the little haps and mishaps of his life

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mishap - an unlucky accident
  • Synonym: chance - something that happens unpredictably

Etymology

From Old Norse *happ* (good luck, chance); related to Middle English *hap* (fortune, luck).

2. VERB

/ hæp / (AmE), / hæp / (BrE)
(archaic) to happen; to come to pass.
to happen; come to pass (archaic)
archaic

Examples

It may so hap that fortune favors the bold.
Should any misfortune hap, send word at once.
Accidents will hap when least expected.

Synset Examples

What is happening?
The meeting took place without an incident
Nothing occurred that seemed important

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to happen - to take place; to occur
  • Synonym: to occur - to happen or take place

Etymology

From Middle English *happen* (to occur by chance), from Old Norse *happa* (to chance, to befall).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
