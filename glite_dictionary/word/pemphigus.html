pemphigus

1. NOUN

/ pɛmˈfɪɡəs / (AmE), / pɛmˈfɪɡəs / (BrE)
A rare skin disease marked by large, thin-walled blisters that form on otherwise normal skin or mucous membranes.
skin disease with large blisters
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with pemphigus after developing painful blisters on her skin.
Early treatment of pemphigus can help control flare-ups and reduce complications.
Doctors often use immunosuppressive drugs to treat severe cases of pemphigus.

Etymology

From Greek pemphix, pemphigos (blister, bubble).
