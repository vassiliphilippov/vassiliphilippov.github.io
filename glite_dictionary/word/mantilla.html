mantilla

1. NOUN

/ mænˈtɪl.jə / (AmE), / manˈtɪl.jə / (BrE)
a short light cape or cloak worn by women, often as part of formal or traditional dress
short cape or cloak for women

Examples

She wore a black mantilla over her shoulders at the evening reception.
The museum showcased a beautiful mantilla from the nineteenth century.
Her traditional dress was completed with an embroidered silk mantilla.

Etymology

From Spanish 'mantilla', diminutive of 'manta' (cloak, blanket).

2. NOUN

/ mænˈtiːjə / (AmE), / mænˈtɪl.jə / (BrE)
a woman's scarf of silk or lace, worn over the head and shoulders, especially in Spain and South America
woman's silk or lace head scarf

Examples

She draped a mantilla over her hair before entering the old church.
The black lace mantilla complemented her traditional dress beautifully.
Many women wore a white mantilla during the religious procession.

Etymology

From Spanish 'mantilla', diminutive of 'manta' (cloak, blanket), from Latin 'mantum' (cloak).
