priapism

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪəˌpɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈprʌɪəˌpɪzəm / (BrE)
A persistent and usually painful erection of the penis that occurs without sexual arousal and lasts for several hours.
persistent, painful penile erection
medical

Examples

The patient presented to the emergency department with priapism lasting more than four hours.
Certain medications can sometimes cause priapism as a rare side effect.
If priapism is not treated promptly, it can result in permanent tissue damage.

Etymology

From Greek Priapos (Priapus, a fertility god) + -ism.
