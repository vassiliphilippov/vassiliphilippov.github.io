backwards

1. ADV

/ ˈbækwɚdz / (AmE), / ˈbækwədz / (BrE)
in the direction of the back; toward a position behind
toward the back or rear

Examples

He walked backwards down the corridor to watch the children.
The car rolled backwards down the hill because the brake was off.
She glanced backwards to see if anyone was following her.

Synset Examples

he moved back
tripped when he stepped backward
she looked rearward out the window of the car

Related Terms

  • Antonym: forwards - toward the front or ahead

Etymology

From Middle English 'bakward', from 'bak' (back) + 'ward' (directional suffix), with '-s' added later for adverbial use.

2. ADV

/ ˈbækwɚdz / (AmE), / ˈbækwədz / (BrE)
in a reverse direction, order, or manner to what is usual or expected
in a reverse direction or manner

Examples

She accidentally wore her shirt backwards to the meeting.
If you read the instructions backwards, they won't make any sense.
The video was played backwards as a special effect.

Synset Examples

it's easy to get the ‘i’ and the ‘e’ backward in words like ‘seize’ and ‘siege’
the child put her jersey on backward

Related Terms

  • Antonym: forwards - toward the front; in the normal direction

Etymology

From back + -wards (suffix forming adverbs of direction); -wards is from Old English -weard, meaning ‘toward’.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
