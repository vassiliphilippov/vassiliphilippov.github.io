to attach

1. VERB

/ əˈtætʃ / (AmE), / əˈtatʃ / (BrE)
to develop a strong emotional or social connection with someone or something
form an emotional or social bond

Examples

Children often attach quickly to new caregivers in a supportive environment.
He didn't expect to attach so deeply to his exchange family.
Many people attach sentimental value to objects given by loved ones.

Synset Examples

The grandparents want to bond with the child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bond - to develop a close connection or relationship
  • Antonym: to detach - to remove or separate emotionally or socially

Etymology

From Old French attacher, of Frankish origin, meaning 'to fasten'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02543752-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈtætʃ / (AmE), / əˈtatʃ / (BrE)
to take possession of property by legal authority, especially as a security against a claim
seize property by legal authority
legal

Examples

The court ordered the sheriff to attach the defendant's assets until the trial ended.
The authorities can attach personal property if someone fails to pay their taxes.
Before judgment, the lawyer sought to attach several bank accounts as a precaution.

Synset Examples

The FBI seized the drugs
The customs agents impounded the illegal shipment
The police confiscated the stolen artwork

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to seize - to take hold of something suddenly and forcibly, often by authority
  • Synonym: to impound - to officially seize and hold something, especially by legal authority
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02278138-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ əˈtætʃ / (AmE), / əˈtatʃ / (BrE)
to become fastened or joined to something
become fastened or joined

Examples

The small magnet quickly attached to the refrigerator door.
A sense of excitement attached to the beginning of the expedition.
The label attached to the inside of the shirt after washing.

Synset Examples

The spider's thread attached to the window sill

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to detach - to separate or unfasten something

Etymology

From Old French 'atachier' (to fasten), from a Germanic source.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01293025-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ əˈtætʃ / (AmE), / əˈtatʃ / (BrE)
to join, fasten, or connect one thing to another; to be in physical contact with something
to join or be in contact with

Examples

Please attach the receipt to the application form before submitting it.
The new leaves attach directly to the main stem of the plant.
The cable attached securely to the back of the computer.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to detach - to disconnect or separate something that was joined
  • Synonym: to connect - to join or link together
  • Synonym: to fasten - to fix or join securely

Etymology

From Old French 'atachier' (to attach, fasten), from a Frankish or other Germanic source.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01292858-v
Synset POS: v

5. VERB

/ əˈtætʃ / (AmE), / əˈtatʃ / (BrE)
to fasten or join one thing to another so that they stay together, or to make something part of something else
fasten or join to something

Examples

Please attach the receipts to your expense forms.
He used tape to attach the note to the refrigerator door.
The teacher asked us to attach a photo to our application.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to detach - to separate something that is attached
  • Synonym: to connect - to join two or more things together

Etymology

From Old French *atachier* (to fix, fasten), from Vulgar Latin *attachiāre*.
