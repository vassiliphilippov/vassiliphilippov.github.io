osculation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːskjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒskjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
(mathematics) the point at which two curves or surfaces touch and share a common tangent.
point where curves or surfaces share a tangent

Examples

The osculation of the two circles occurs precisely at the point where they are tangent.
At the point of osculation, the two curves not only meet but also share the same direction.
In differential geometry, studying osculation helps in understanding the local behavior of surfaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tangency - the condition of being tangent, or touching at a single point

Etymology

From Latin osculatio, from osculare 'to kiss'; originally meaning 'kiss', later adopted in mathematics for 'touching at a point'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːskjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒskjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of kissing or caressing with the lips
the act of kissing

Examples

His affectionate osculation surprised everyone at the farewell party.
The poet described their first osculation in exquisite detail.
She greeted her grandmother with a gentle osculation on the cheek.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kiss - to touch with the lips as a sign of love or greeting

Etymology

From Latin osculatio (a kiss), from osculare (to kiss), from osculum (a little mouth, a kiss).
