keynote

1. VERB

/ ˈkiˌnoʊt / (AmE), / ˈkiːnəʊt / (BrE)
to establish or serve as the guiding principle or main theme of something
to set the main theme; to guide

Examples

His optimism keynotes the entire project, inspiring everyone involved.
A flavor of nostalgia keynotes the novel, giving it a reflective tone.
Innovation keynotes the brand's approach to product design every year.

Synset Examples

Comfort keynotes this designer's Fall collection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dominate - to have a commanding influence on

Etymology

From 'keynote' (noun), referring to the principal tone in a musical key, extended metaphorically.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00299969-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈkiˌnoʊt / (AmE), / ˈkiːnəʊt / (BrE)
the central, underlying theme or idea of a speech or literary work
central theme or idea

Examples

The speaker's keynote was the importance of innovation in technology.
Unity was the keynote of her address to the graduates.
The novel's keynote is hope overcoming adversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theme - the subject or main idea in a piece of writing, speech, or work of art

Etymology

From key (in music) + note, originally referring to the fundamental note of a musical scale.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07254938-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈkiˌnoʊt / (AmE), / ˈkiːnəʊt / (BrE)
(music) The first and principal note of a diatonic scale, from which the key of a piece of music is determined.
first note of a musical scale

Examples

The symphony begins and ends on the keynote of C major.
She sang a scale, always returning to the keynote as her anchor.
A strong awareness of the keynote helps with improvisation in jazz music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tonic - the first note of a scale, serving as its tonal center

Etymology

From 'key' (in the musical sense) + 'note'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06870776-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈkiːˌnoʊt / (AmE), / ˈkiːˌnəʊt / (BrE)
to deliver the main address at a conference or event, usually setting the theme or underlying tone.
deliver the main address at an event

Examples

She was invited to keynote the technology conference last year.
Every year a renowned figure is chosen to keynote the international summit.
He will keynote the ceremony, sharing his insights on innovation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: address - a formal speech delivered to an audience
  • Synonym: lecture - an educational talk to an audience

Etymology

From the noun 'keynote', originally in music, later metaphorically extended to speeches.

Source: WordNet
