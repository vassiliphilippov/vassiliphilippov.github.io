Zannichelliaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌzænɪˌkɛliˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌzanɪˌkɛlɪˈiːsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A former family of flowering aquatic plants, now usually included in the family Potamogetonaceae.
former family of aquatic plants

Examples

The genus Zannichellia was formerly classified in the Zannichelliaceae family.
Most modern botanists place species once in Zannichelliaceae into the Potamogetonaceae family.
The aquatic plants once grouped under Zannichelliaceae are found in freshwater habitats worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Potamogetonaceae - family of aquatic plants including pondweeds

Etymology

From the genus name Zannichellia, named after Italian botanist Giovanni Gerolamo Zannichelli, and the botanical family suffix -aceae.
