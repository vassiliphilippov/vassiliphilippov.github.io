stately

1. ADJ

/ ˈsteɪtli / (AmE), / ˈsteɪtli / (BrE)
impressive in size, manner, or appearance; dignified and majestic.
impressive and dignified in appearance

Examples

The stately oak tree stood at the center of the sprawling estate.
She walked up the stately staircase, admiring the ornate banisters and high ceilings.
A row of stately columns lined the entrance to the historic courthouse.

Synset Examples

a baronial mansion
an imposing residence
a noble tree
severe-looking policemen sat astride noble horses
stately columns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: majestic - having or showing impressive beauty or dignity
  • Synonym: grand - impressive and large in scale or appearance
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance; modest in appearance or status

Etymology

From Middle English statly, from state (dignity, condition) + -ly.

3. ADJ

/ ˈsteɪtli / (AmE), / ˈsteɪtli / (BrE)
Impressive and dignified in appearance or manner; having a grand, majestic quality.
imposing and dignified

Examples

The stately columns of the old library towered over the main avenue.
She walked with a stately grace that drew everyone's attention.
The ceremony was held in a stately hall filled with chandeliers and tapestries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: majestic - having majesty; grand and impressive
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance; not imposing

Etymology

From Middle English 'statly', from 'state' (condition, dignity) + '-ly'.
