original

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɪdʒənl̩ / (AmE), / əˈrɪdʒɪnəl / (BrE)
an original creation, especially a document, artwork, or recording from which copies are made
source creation from which copies are made

Examples

The museum displayed the original alongside several copies made over the years.
Please keep the original safe and only work with duplicates.
This tape is the original, so all new recordings must be taken from it.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: copy - a reproduction or duplicate of an original object or document

Etymology

From Old French original, from Latin originalis (“pertaining to origin, source”).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03732765-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ əˈrɪdʒənl / (AmE), / əˈrɪdʒɪnəl / (BrE)
an object from which copies are made; the first form of something before copies or reproductions are produced
object serving as a model for copies

Examples

The museum displays the original alongside several well-made replicas.
Please send me the original document, not a photocopy.
She was thrilled to see the original painting in the gallery.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: copy - a thing made to be similar or identical to another

Etymology

From Latin originalis (of or relating to origin), from origo (beginning, source).

not derived from something else; existing as the first form or source, rather than a copy or adaptation
existing as the first form; not a copy

Examples
