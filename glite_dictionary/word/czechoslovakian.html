Czechoslovakian

1. ADJ

/ ˌtʃɛk.oʊ.sloʊˈvæk.i.ən / (AmE), / ˌtʃɛk.əʊ.sləˈvæk.i.ən / (BrE)
relating to Czechoslovakia, its people, or their language
of or relating to Czechoslovakia

Examples

The museum features a collection of rare Czechoslovakian coins and stamps.
Many families with Czechoslovakian roots celebrate both Czech and Slovak traditions.
She wrote her thesis on the diplomatic history of Czechoslovakian foreign policy.

Synset Examples

The Czech border
Czechoslovak nationalists
The Czechoslovakian population

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Czech - relating to the Czech Republic or its people
  • Synonym: Slovak - relating to Slovakia or its people

Etymology

From Czechoslovakia, combining Czech and Slovak, the principal ethnic groups of the former country.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtʃɛkəsoʊˈlɑːviən / (AmE), / ˌtʃɛkəʊsləˈvɑːkiən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of the former country of Czechoslovakia, which existed in central Europe from 1918 to 1992
person from former Czechoslovakia

Examples

Her grandfather was a proud Czechoslovakian who emigrated to the United States in 1948.
As a Czechoslovakian, he experienced the split into the Czech Republic and Slovakia firsthand.
Many Czechoslovakians contributed significantly to the arts and sciences during the twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Czech - a native or inhabitant of the Czech Republic or formerly Czechoslovakia
  • Synonym: Slovak - a native or inhabitant of Slovakia or formerly Czechoslovakia

Etymology

From Czechoslovakia + -ian, denoting nationality or origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09718596-n
Synset POS: n
