maize

1. NOUN

/ meɪz / (AmE), / meɪz / (BrE)
A tall annual cereal plant (Zea mays) native to the Americas, widely cultivated for its yellow grain, used as food, livestock feed, and in industry.
a cereal plant; corn

Examples

Farmers in this region grow maize as their main crop.
The government is investing in research to improve maize yields.
Many Mexican dishes use maize as a primary ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corn - another common term for maize, especially in American English

Etymology

From Spanish 'maíz', from Taíno 'mahiz'.

2. NOUN

/ meɪz / (AmE), / meɪz / (BrE)
A strong yellow color resembling that of the kernels of ripe maize.
a strong yellow color

Examples

The curtains in the living room are a cheerful maize that brightens the whole space.
She chose a maize dress for the summer party, which complemented her complexion beautifully.
The artist used a shade of maize to evoke warmth and sunlight in the painting.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: yellow - the color between green and orange in the spectrum, resembling that of ripe lemons or gold
  • Degree Higher: golden - a deep, rich yellow color like that of gold

Etymology

From American English 'maize' (corn), ultimately from Spanish 'maíz', from Taíno 'mahiz'.
