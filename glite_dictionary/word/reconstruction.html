Reconstruction

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːkənˈstrʌkʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈstrʌkʃən / (BrE)
the period after the American Civil War (1865–1877) during which the southern states were reorganized and returned to the Union
U.S. historical period after the Civil War
american

Examples

Many historians study Reconstruction to understand the challenges faced by newly freed African Americans.
Laws passed during Reconstruction aimed to reform the South's economy and society.
After the Civil War, Reconstruction policies reshaped political life in the southern states.
