Geochelone

1. NOUN

/ dʒiˈɑːkəloʊni / (AmE), / dʒiːɒˈkɛləʊni / (BrE)
A genus of giant tortoises found primarily in South Asia and Africa.
genus of giant tortoises

Examples

The Geochelone genus includes some of the largest tortoises in the world.
Scientists have studied the migration patterns of Geochelone populations in the wild.
The Aldabra giant tortoise is a famous member of the Geochelone group.

Etymology

From Greek 'geo' (earth) and 'chelone' (tortoise).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒioʊˈkɛloʊni / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəˈkɛləni / (BrE)
A genus of large tortoises native to Africa and Asia, known for their substantial size and longevity.
genus of large tortoises

Examples

The Galápagos giant tortoise was once classified within the genus Geochelone.
Many species in the genus Geochelone are noted for their impressive size and slow movement.
Scientists study the lifespan of Geochelone tortoises to learn more about longevity in reptiles.

Etymology

From Greek 'geo-' (earth) and 'khelōnē' (tortoise).
