Chelonia mydas

1. NOUN

/ kəˈloʊniə ˈmaɪdəs / (AmE), / kɪˈləʊnɪə ˈmaɪdəs / (BrE)
a large species of sea turtle with greenish flesh, found in tropical waters, also known as the green turtle
large tropical sea turtle (green turtle)

Examples

Marine biologists have tracked the migration patterns of the Chelonia mydas across the Pacific Ocean.
The endangered Chelonia mydas is protected under international conservation laws.
Traditional turtle soup was often prepared using the meat of Chelonia mydas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: green turtle - common name for Chelonia mydas

Etymology

From Latin: 'Chelonia' (turtle genus) and 'mydas' (species name, from Greek, possibly meaning 'sea turtle').
