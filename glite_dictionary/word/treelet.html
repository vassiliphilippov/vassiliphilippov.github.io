treelet

1. NOUN

/ ˈtriːlət / (AmE), / ˈtriːlɪt / (BrE)
A small tree, especially one that is not fully grown or is of a naturally small species.
a small tree

Examples

Several rare treelets grow along the forest path, never reaching full height.
The botanist identified the sapling as a unique treelet species.
In the garden, a treelet may take less space than a typical tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sapling - a young tree
  • Antonym: shrub - a woody plant smaller than a tree

Etymology

Formed from 'tree' with diminutive suffix '-let', indicating small size.
