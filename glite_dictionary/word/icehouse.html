icehouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪsˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈaɪsˌhaʊs / (BrE)
a building used for storing ice, traditionally to keep it from melting before the invention of electric refrigeration
building for storing ice

Examples

In the 19th century, wealthy families often had an icehouse on their estates to preserve food through the summer.
The old icehouse near the lake is now a historical landmark.
Before electric refrigerators, ice was stored in an icehouse lined with straw for insulation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: icebox - a container or insulated chest for keeping food cool, traditionally with ice
