Hygrophorus inocybiformis

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌɡrɑːˈfɔːrəs ɪˌnɑːsɪbɪˈfɔːrmɪs / (AmE), / hɪˌɡrɒfəˈrɒːrəs ɪˌnɒsɪbɪˈfɔːmɪs / (BrE)
A species of fungus characterized by a dull, scaly cap, grey-brown scales on a white stalk, and waxy, grey-white gills.
species of fungus with scaly cap and waxy gills

Examples

The Hygrophorus inocybiformis thrives in damp forest environments, often found among leaf litter.
Researchers identified the mushroom as Hygrophorus inocybiformis based on its distinctive scaly cap and waxy gills.
Mushroom enthusiasts spotted several Hygrophorus inocybiformis specimens during their autumn hike.

Etymology

From Greek 'hygros' (wet, moist) and Latinized form of Inocybe (a related genus), with '-formis' meaning 'shaped like'.
