maniraptor

1. NOUN

/ ˌmænɪˈræptər / (AmE), / ˌmænɪˈræptə / (BrE)
an advanced group of carnivorous theropod dinosaurs, believed to include the ancestors of modern birds
group of advanced theropod dinosaurs

Examples

Many paleontologists believe that maniraptors are the evolutionary ancestors of modern birds.
Fossil evidence suggests that some maniraptors had feathers and were capable of flight.
Velociraptor is one of the best-known maniraptors from the late Cretaceous period.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: theropod - a bipedal carnivorous dinosaur
  • Degree Higher: bird - a warm-blooded egg-laying animal with feathers, descended from maniraptors

Etymology

From Latin manus (hand) + raptor (seizer, robber), referring to their grasping hands.
