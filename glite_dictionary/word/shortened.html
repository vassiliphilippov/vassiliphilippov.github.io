shortened

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃɔrtn̩d / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːtənd / (BrE)
made or become shorter in length, duration, or extent.
made shorter in length or duration

Examples

The path was shortened to save time during the hike.
After editing, the story became noticeably shortened and more concise.
Her shortened dress caught everyone's attention at the party.

Synset Examples

a sawed-off shotgun
a sawed-off broomstick
the shortened rope was easier to use

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prolonged - made longer in duration or extent
  • Synonym: abridged - shortened, especially by omission

Etymology

From the verb 'shorten', from Middle English 'shortnen', based on 'short'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃɔrtənd / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːtənd / (BrE)
made shorter in duration or length; cut short.
made shorter in duration

Examples

The meeting was shortened due to technical problems.
Her vacation was shortened because of an unexpected work emergency.
The shortened version of the report is easier to read.

Synset Examples

the abbreviated speech
her shortened life was clearly the result of smoking
an unsatisfactory truncated conversation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abbreviated - made shorter, especially by omitting parts
  • Synonym: truncated - cut short by having a part removed
  • Antonym: extended - made longer or larger in duration or length

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'shorten', from Middle English 'shortnen', from 'short'.

Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ ˈʃɔrtənd / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːtənd / (BrE)
made shorter by having parts removed or reduced in length or duration.
made shorter by removal of parts

Examples

The movie was shortened for its television release to fit the time slot.
He wore a shortened version of the traditional robe for easier movement.
The meeting was shortened due to unexpected technical issues.

Synset Examples

the drastically cut film

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abridged - shortened by omission of parts, especially a text
  • Antonym: lengthened - made longer in length or duration

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'shorten' + past participle ending '-ed'.
