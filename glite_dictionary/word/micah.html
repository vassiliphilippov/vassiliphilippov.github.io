Micah

1. PROPN

/ ˈmaɪkə / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkə / (BrE)
A minor Hebrew prophet of the 8th century BC, known for prophesying the destruction and later restoration of Jerusalem and Samaria.
Hebrew prophet (8th century BC)

Examples

The Book of Micah contains his prophecies about justice and mercy.
Micah warned both Jerusalem and Samaria of impending doom.
Many scholars study the teachings of Micah to understand Hebrew prophecy.

Etymology

From Hebrew Mīkhā, short form of Mikāyāh meaning 'Who is like Yahweh?'

2. PROPN

/ ˈmaɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.kə / (BrE)
A book of the Old Testament that contains the prophecies of Micah, especially concerning the destruction of Jerusalem.
book of the Old Testament; prophecies of Micah

Examples

Many scholars have studied the book of Micah for its social justice themes.
Micah prophesies the destruction and later the restoration of Jerusalem.
In church last week, the pastor quoted from Micah to illustrate his sermon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nahum - another book of the Old Testament by a minor prophet

Etymology

From Hebrew 'Mikha,' a prophet's name, meaning 'Who is like Yahweh?'
