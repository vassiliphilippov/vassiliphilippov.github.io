to automatize

1. VERB

/ əˈtɑːməˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒmətaɪz / (BrE)
to make something operate automatically, especially by using machines or computers instead of people
make automatic; operate automatically

Examples

The company decided to automatize its packaging system to improve efficiency.
New technology allows us to automatize several repetitive tasks that were once done manually.
Efforts to automatize the assembly line have reduced labor costs significantly.

Synset Examples

automatize the production
automate the movement of the robot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to automate - to make a process operate by machines or computers instead of people
  • Antonym: manual - done by hand rather than automatically

Etymology

From French 'automatiser', from 'automatique' (automatic), modeled after 'automatize' in English.

2. VERB

/ əˈtɑː.mə.taɪz / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒm.ə.taɪz / (BrE)
to make something operate automatically or to convert a process, task, or system to automatic operation, especially by using machines or computers
make automatic; convert to automatic operation

Examples

Many companies seek to automatize routine administrative tasks to increase efficiency.
The factory decided to automatize its assembly line using advanced robotics.
As technology evolves, more industries find new ways to automatize complex procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to automate - make a process or system operate automatically

Etymology

From Greek 'automatos' (self-moving) + '-ize' (forming verbs).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00481152-v
Synset POS: v
