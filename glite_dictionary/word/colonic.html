colonic

1. ADJ

/ kəˈlɑnɪk / (AmE), / kəˈlɒnɪk / (BrE)
of or relating to the colon, the part of the large intestine extending from the cecum to the rectum.
relating to the colon (large intestine)
medical

Examples

The doctor noted that the patient had a colonic obstruction requiring immediate attention.
A diet high in fiber can help maintain colonic health.
Researchers are studying the effects of probiotics on colonic bacteria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colon - the main part of the large intestine

Etymology

From Latin colon (colon, large intestine) + -ic (forming adjectives).

WordNet ID: oewn-02711053-a

2. NOUN

/ kəˈlɑnɪk / (AmE), / kəˈlɒnɪk / (BrE)
a procedure in which water is introduced into the colon through the rectum to flush it out, often for purported health benefits
intestinal flushing procedure
medical

Examples

After her colonic, she said she felt lighter and more energetic.
Many spas now offer colonic treatments as part of their detox services.
A colonic should only be performed by a trained professional under sanitary conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enema - fluid injected into the rectum for cleaning or medication

Etymology

From colon (the large intestine) + -ic (adjectival suffix); noun use recorded in 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00697400-n
Synset POS: n
