jug-eared

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒʌɡ ˌɪrd / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌɡ ɪəd / (BrE)
having noticeably large or protruding ears, typically in a way considered unattractive
having large or protruding ears

Examples

The children called him jug-eared when he started at the new school.
A jug-eared man stood nervously at the back of the crowd.
Her jug-eared uncle always made everyone laugh with his stories.

Synset Examples

The character was described as jug-eared and awkward.
He was teased for his jug-eared appearance in school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: big-eared - having large ears

Etymology

From 'jug' (a large vessel) + 'eared', likening protruding ears to the handles of a jug.
