enolic

1. ADJ

/ iˈnoʊlɪk / (AmE), / iˈnɒlɪk / (BrE)
relating to or consisting of an enol (an organic compound containing a hydroxyl group bonded to a carbon atom that is double-bonded to another carbon).
relating to an enol group in chemistry

Examples

The enolic form of the compound is more reactive in some chemical reactions.
Chemists often study the balance between the keto and enolic tautomers.
The enolic hydrogen participates in hydrogen bonding, affecting molecular stability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enol - an organic compound with a hydroxyl group on a carbon atom double-bonded to another carbon atom

Etymology

From 'enol', referring to an ethylene group bonded to an alcohol, combined with the suffix '-ic'.
