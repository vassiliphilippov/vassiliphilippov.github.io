coil

1. NOUN

/ kɔɪl / (AmE), / kɔɪl / (BrE)
a contraceptive device in the shape of a small coil that is placed in a woman's womb to prevent pregnancy; an intrauterine device (IUD)
contraceptive device placed in the womb
medical british

Examples

The doctor explained how the coil works as a long-term contraceptive option.
She decided to have a coil fitted instead of taking daily pills.
Some women may experience side effects after having the coil inserted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: IUD - a device inserted into the uterus for contraception

2. NOUN

/ kɔɪl / (AmE), / kɔɪl / (BrE)
a length of tubing or wire that is wound in a spiral or series of loops, often used in mechanical or electrical devices
tubing or wire wound in a spiral

Examples

The copper coil inside the air conditioner helps transfer heat efficiently.
She carefully wound the hose into a neat coil after watering the garden.
An electric stove heats up quickly because of its metal heating coil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiral - curve that winds around a fixed center point at increasing or decreasing distance

Etymology

From Middle English 'coil' (to twist, curl), perhaps from Old French 'coillir' (to gather) or Latin 'colligere' (to collect).
