exceedance

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈsiːdəns / (AmE), / ɪkˈsiːdən(t)s / (BrE)
(technical) The probability or frequency that a specified value or threshold, such as ground motion, is exceeded within a given time period, especially in environmental science or geology.
probability of exceeding a threshold, esp. in geology/environment

Examples

Scientists use exceedance probabilities to assess seismic hazards for urban areas.
The annual exceedance of pollution levels was calculated for the entire river basin.
Understanding exceedance helps engineers design safer buildings in earthquake-prone regions.

Synset Examples

the concept of exceedance can be applied to any type of environmental risk modeling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exceed - to go beyond a specified limit or amount

Etymology

From 'exceed' (to go beyond a limit) + '-ance' (noun-forming suffix).
