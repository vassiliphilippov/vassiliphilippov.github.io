rockcress

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑkˌkrɛs / (AmE), / ˈrɒkˌkrɛs / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Arabis, typically growing on rocky ground, with small white or pink flowers
a plant of the Arabis genus, found on rocks

Examples

The rockcress thrives in crevices on steep, rocky slopes.
Many gardeners choose rockcress for its delicate, early-spring flowers.
We found patches of wild rockcress along the mountain trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arabis - genus of rockcress and related plants

Etymology

From 'rock' (referring to its habitat) + 'cress' (a term for small, edible or wild herbs).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɑkˌkrɛs / (AmE), / ˈrɒkˌkrɛs / (BrE)
A plant of the cress family with yellow or white flowers, sometimes considered noxious, belonging to certain genera such as Arabis or Sisymbrium.
a plant of the cress family

Examples

The hillside was covered with wild rockcress in full bloom.
Some gardeners consider rockcress a weed because it spreads quickly in rocky soil.
We identified several species of rockcress during the botany field trip.

Etymology

From 'rock' + 'cress', referencing its usual rocky habitats.
