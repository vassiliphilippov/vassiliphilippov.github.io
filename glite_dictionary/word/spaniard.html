Spaniard

1. NOUN

/ ˈspænjɚd / (AmE), / ˈspanjəd / (BrE)
a person who is a native or inhabitant of Spain
a native or inhabitant of Spain

Examples

Christopher Columbus was an Italian, but many of his sailors were Spaniards.
The Spaniard greeted us warmly and offered to show us around Madrid.
Many Spaniards celebrate national holidays with vibrant festivals and music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spanish - relating to Spain, its people, or their language

Etymology

From Middle English Spaignard, from Old French Espaignart, from Espagne (Spain) + -ard.
