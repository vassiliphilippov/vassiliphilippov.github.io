chintzy

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɪntsi / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪntzi / (BrE)
Of very poor quality, cheap or shoddily made; also, stingy or miserly.
of poor quality; cheap

Examples

I don't want to buy that chintzy souvenir; it looks like it will fall apart in a week.
The hotel room felt chintzy with its cheap decor and thin sheets.
She resented the chintzy gifts her boss handed out every year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoddy - badly made or of poor quality
  • Synonym: cheap - inexpensive and often of low quality
  • Antonym: luxurious - very comfortable and expensive; of high quality

Etymology

From chintz, a printed cotton fabric, often regarded as cheap, plus the informal suffix -y.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɪntsi / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪntzi / (BrE)
Embarrassingly stingy or cheap in a way that is noticeable or unpleasant.
embarrassingly stingy or cheap

Examples

His chintzy attitude meant he never contributed to group gifts.
The decorations looked chintzy and poorly made, disappointing the guests.
She refused to buy the chintzy knockoff, opting for something of better quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stingy - unwilling to spend money; mean; close-fisted
  • Antonym: generous - willing to give money, help, or kindness freely

Etymology

Originally from 'chintz,' a printed calico fabric from India, associated with cheapness.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
