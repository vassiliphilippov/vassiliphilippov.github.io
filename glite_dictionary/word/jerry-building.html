jerry-building

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛri ˌbɪldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛri ˌbɪldɪŋ / (BrE)
The practice of constructing buildings using inferior materials or workmanship, typically to maximize quick profit at the expense of quality.
poor-quality building for quick profit

Examples

There was widespread jerry-building in the rush to develop the city's outskirts.
The inspector condemned the apartment complex for its obvious jerry-building.
After years of jerry-building, the neighborhood faced frequent structural problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jerry-built - built cheaply and poorly

Etymology

From 'jerry-build', possibly from the British slang 'jerry' (bad, defective) + 'building'.
