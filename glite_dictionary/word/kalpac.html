kalpac

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑlˌpɑk / (AmE), / ˈkal.pak / (BrE)
a high-crowned black cap, usually made of felt or sheepskin, traditionally worn by men in Turkey, Iran, and the Caucasus
high-crowned black cap from Turkey/Iran/Caucasus

Examples

The diplomat wore a traditional kalpac as part of his formal attire during the ceremony.
Many elderly men in the village still don a kalpac on special occasions.
The museum display included a beautifully embroidered kalpac from the 19th century.

Etymology

From Turkish 'kalpak', referring to a type of cap.
