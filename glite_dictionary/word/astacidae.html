Astacidae

1. NOUN

/ əˈsteɪsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / æˈstæsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of freshwater crayfish found in Europe and western North America.
family of freshwater crayfish

Examples

The Astacidae are common in European and North American rivers.
Scientists studied the decline in populations of Astacidae due to water pollution.
Several species of Astacidae are important in local fisheries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crayfish - common name for freshwater crustaceans of Astacidae and related families

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Astacus and the suffix -idae used for animal families.
