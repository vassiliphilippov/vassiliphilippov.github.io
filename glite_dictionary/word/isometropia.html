isometropia

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsoʊmɪˈtroʊpiə / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəʊmɪˈtrəʊpiə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which both eyes have equal refractive power.
equality of refractive power in both eyes
medical

Examples

The patient's isometropia meant no prescription difference was required between the two lenses.
Routine vision tests confirmed isometropia, so no corrective action was needed.
Uncorrected isometropia usually does not result in any binocular vision problems.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anisometropia - difference in refractive power between the two eyes

Etymology

From Greek 'isos' (equal) + 'metron' (measure) + 'ops' (eye/vision).
