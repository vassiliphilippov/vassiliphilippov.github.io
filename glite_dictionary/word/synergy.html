synergy

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪnɚdʒi / (AmE), / ˈsɪnədʒi / (BrE)
The interaction or cooperation of two or more agents, substances, or forces so that their combined effect is greater than the sum of their individual effects.
combined effect greater than sum of parts

Examples

The synergy between the two departments led to a dramatic increase in productivity.
Certain drug combinations produce a synergy that enhances their effects on patients.
We are seeking synergy in our partnership to deliver better results than we could alone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collaboration - the action of working with someone to produce something

Etymology

From Greek 'synergia' (cooperation), from 'synergos' (working together).
