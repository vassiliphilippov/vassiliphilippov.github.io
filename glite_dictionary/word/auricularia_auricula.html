Auricularia auricula

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˌrɪkjəˈlɛriə ɔːˈjuːrɪkjələ / (AmE), / ɔːˌrɪkjʊˈlɛəriə ɔːˈjuːrɪkjʊlə / (BrE)
A species of edible fungus shaped like a human ear, commonly found on decaying wood and used in cooking, especially in Asian cuisines.
edible ear-shaped fungus

Examples

The chef added dried Auricularia auricula to the soup for a unique texture.
Auricularia auricula is commonly used in Chinese cuisine for its crunchy consistency.
On our hike, we found clusters of Auricularia auricula growing on an old log.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood ear - another name for Auricularia auricula, especially in culinary contexts

Etymology

From Latin 'auricula' meaning 'little ear', referring to its ear-like shape.
