to topple

1. VERB

/ ˈtɑːp.əl / (AmE), / ˈtɒp.əl / (BrE)
to cause something or someone to become unsteady and fall over, especially by pushing
cause to fall over by pushing

Examples

The wind was strong enough to topple several trees in the park.
Protesters tried to topple the statue during the demonstration.
Careless stacking of the boxes caused the pile to topple over.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overturn - to turn something over so that it is upside down
  • Synonym: to upend - to set or turn something on its end or upside down
  • Antonym: erect - to put something in a vertical position (opposite meaning)

Etymology

Probably from frequentative form of 'top', referring to knocking from the top.

2. VERB

/ ˈtɑːpl̩ / (AmE), / ˈtɒpl̩ / (BrE)
(of a person or structure) to fall or make something fall over, especially suddenly or as if collapsing.
fall or cause to fall over suddenly

Examples

Several books began to topple from the overcrowded shelf.
The statue was so heavy that it didn't topple easily in the wind.
After losing its foundations, the old chimney started to topple toward the street.

Synset Examples

The tower of the World Trade Center tumbled after the plane hit it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collapse - to fall down or give way suddenly, often noisily
  • Antonym: stand - to be in an upright position on the feet or a base

Etymology

Origin unknown; perhaps related to 'top'.

Source: WordNet
