sedate

1. VERB

/ sɪˈdeɪt / (AmE), / sɪˈdeɪt / (BrE)
to make someone calm or sleepy by giving them a drug; to calm or quiet using a sedative
to calm or make sleepy, especially with a drug
medical

Examples

The veterinarian had to sedate the dog before performing the procedure.
She was so anxious that the doctor decided to sedate her for the dental surgery.
After the accident, the paramedics chose to sedate the injured passenger to ease his pain.

Synset Examples

The patient must be sedated before the operation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tranquilize - to make someone calm, quiet, or less anxious by giving them a drug

Etymology

From Latin sedatus, past participle of sedare (to calm, settle).

2. ADJ

/ sɪˈdeɪt / (AmE), / sɪˈdeɪt / (BrE)
calm, dignified, and unhurried in manner or character; showing composure and propriety
calm and dignified in manner

Examples

The professor's sedate demeanor commanded respect among the students.
They strolled through the sedate gardens of the old estate.
The ceremony was marked by a sedate and formal atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dignified - having or showing a composed or serious manner worthy of respect
  • Antonym: lively - full of life and energy

Etymology

From Latin sedatus, past participle of sedare (to calm).
