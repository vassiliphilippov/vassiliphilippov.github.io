cha-cha-cha

1. NOUN

/ ˌtʃɑː tʃɑː ˈtʃɑː / (AmE), / ˌtʃɑː tʃɑː ˈtʃɑː / (BrE)
a lively ballroom dance of Cuban origin, characterized by small steps and rhythmic swaying of the hips
Latin American ballroom dance style

Examples

They performed the cha-cha-cha with incredible energy at the competition.
Learning the basic steps of the cha-cha-cha can be fun for beginners.
The music sped up, prompting everyone to join in the cha-cha-cha on the dance floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rumba - a rhythmic Cuban dance style

Etymology

From Cuban Spanish, imitative of the sound of the dancers' feet.
