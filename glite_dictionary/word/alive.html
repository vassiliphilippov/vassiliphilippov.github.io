alive

1. ADJ

/ əˈlaɪv / (AmE), / əˈlaɪv / (BrE)
continuing to exist or function; not extinct, inactive, or dead
still existing or functioning

Examples

Despite many difficulties, the old custom is still alive in the village.
The festival helps keep local traditions alive.
His passionate speech kept everyone's hopes alive during the crisis.

Synset Examples

keep hope alive
the tradition was still alive
an active tradition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: active - functioning or operating; not inactive or dormant
  • Antonym: extinct - no longer existing or in operation

Etymology

From Old English on līfe (in life).

2. ADJ

/ əˈlaɪv / (AmE), / əˈlaɪv / (BrE)
(of a volcano) capable of erupting; not extinct or dormant.
of a volcano, capable of erupting

Examples

The scientists confirmed that the seemingly quiet mountain was still alive.
The region is dangerous because several volcanoes remain alive.
Geologists study the gases to determine if a volcano is alive or extinct.

Synset Examples

a live volcano
the volcano is very much alive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: active - (of a volcano) currently or potentially erupting
  • Antonym: dormant - (of a volcano) not currently active, but could erupt in the future
  • Antonym: extinct - (of a volcano) no longer likely to erupt
Source: WordNet
