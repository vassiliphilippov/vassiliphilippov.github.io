interconnection

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərkəˈnɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəkəˈnɛkʃən / (BrE)
the state or process of being connected to each other, especially in a way that systems, people, or things influence or depend on each other
state of being connected or linked

Examples

The interconnection of the subway lines makes commuting easy across the city.
Scientists studied the interconnection between climate change and extreme weather events.
Modern technology relies on the interconnection of computers and networks worldwide.

Synset Examples

an interconnection between the two buildings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connection - a relationship in which things are linked or associated
  • Antonym: disconnection - the state of not being connected or joined

Etymology

From inter- (mutual, between) + connection; early 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərkəˈnɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəkəˈnɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of connecting or linking together two or more things, especially systems, wires, computers, or theories, so they can work together or affect each other
the act of connecting things together

Examples

The interconnection of different networks allows users to access information globally.
A reliable interconnection between the two computers is essential for data transfer.
Scientists study the interconnection of various ecological systems to understand their impact on the environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connection - the act of connecting or the state of being connected

Etymology

From inter- (between) + connection, modeled on the verb interconnect.

Synset POS: n
