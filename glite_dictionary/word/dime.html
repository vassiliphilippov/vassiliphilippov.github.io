dime

1. NOUN

/ daɪm / (AmE), / daɪm / (BrE)
(slang) A packet of illegal drugs sold for ten dollars.
packet of drugs sold for $10 (slang)
slang american

Examples

The dealer offered me a dime behind the convenience store.
He was caught with a dime of marijuana in his backpack.
On the street, a dime usually refers to a ten-dollar bag of drugs.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: nickel - a packet of drugs sold for five dollars (slang)

Etymology

From the practice of selling drug packets for a 'dime', American slang for ten dollars (from the coin of the same name).

2. NOUN

/ daɪm / (AmE), / daɪm / (BrE)
A United States coin worth one tenth of a dollar (10 cents).
US coin worth 10 cents
american

Examples

He put a dime into the parking meter and waited for the light to change.
Back in the 1960s, a cup of coffee might cost just a dime.
She found a shiny dime lying on the sidewalk.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: nickel - a United States coin worth five cents
  • Degree Higher: quarter - a United States coin worth twenty-five cents

Etymology

From French 'dîme' (tithe, tenth part), ultimately from Latin 'decima'.
