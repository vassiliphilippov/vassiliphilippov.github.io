to descend

1. VERB

/ dɪˈsɛnd / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛnd / (BrE)
to do something that one considers to be beneath one's dignity or standards
do something beneath one's dignity

Examples

He would never descend to lying just to win an argument.
She refused to descend to petty gossip about her colleagues.
Despite criticism, the author would not descend to using sensationalist language in her writing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stoop - to lower one's moral standards or dignity

Etymology

From Latin descendere, from de- ‘down’ + scandere ‘to climb’.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02597468-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ dɪˈsɛnd / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛnd / (BrE)
to move or go down from a higher to a lower position
move downwards

Examples

As the sun began to set, darkness descended over the valley.
The hikers carefully descended the steep mountain trail.
When the curtains descended, the audience burst into applause.

Synset Examples

The temperature is going down
The barometer is falling
The curtain fell on the diva
Her hand went up and then fell again

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to ascend - to move or go up from a lower to a higher position
  • Synonym: fall - to move or drop downward, typically rapidly and freely

Etymology

From Latin descendere, from de- (down) + scandere (to climb).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01974840-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ dɪˈsɛnd / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛnd / (BrE)
to be related to someone who lived in the past; to come from a particular ancestor or family line
be related by ancestry

Examples

She proudly claimed to descend from a line of French aristocrats.
Many Americans can descend from immigrants who arrived over a century ago.
He believed he descended from ancient kings, though there was little evidence.

Synset Examples

She was descended from an old Italian noble family
he comes from humble origins

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to originate - to come from a particular place, time, or situation
  • Synonym: to inherit - to receive (a quality, characteristic, or predisposition) from an ancestor
  • Antonym: to precede - to come before in order or position

Etymology

From Latin 'descendere', from 'de-' (down) + 'scandere' (to climb).

4. VERB

/ dɪˈsɛnd / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛnd / (BrE)
(of something abstract, such as darkness, silence, or emotion) to arrive or spread over a place, as if falling from above.
arrive/spread over as if falling

Examples

As the sun set, a heavy silence descended on the small town.
When night descends, the forest becomes eerily quiet.
A feeling of dread descended upon the spectators as the storm approached.

Synset Examples

Night fell
Silence fell

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fall - to come down freely from a higher position to a lower position
  • Antonym: rise - to move upwards or appear to increase in amount or intensity

Etymology

From Latin 'descendere', from 'de-' (down) + 'scandere' (to climb).

Source: WordNet
