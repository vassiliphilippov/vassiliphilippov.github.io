diablotin

1. NOUN

/ diːˌæbloʊˈtæ̃ / (AmE), / diːˌæbləʊˈtɛ̃ / (BrE)
A thin, round slice of bread toasted or fried, typically served with soup or consomme.
thin round toasted bread for soup

Examples

The chef served the hot consomme with a crisp diablotin on the side.
Add a diablotin to your soup for an authentic French touch.
She topped each diablotin with a bit of grated cheese before toasting them.

Synset Examples

A recipe for consomme diablotins that contains French bread, flour, olive oil, tapioca.

Etymology

From French 'diablotin', meaning 'little devil'.
