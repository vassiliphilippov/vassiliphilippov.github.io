noetic

1. ADJ

/ noʊˈɛtɪk / (AmE), / nəʊˈɛtɪk / (BrE)
Relating to mental activity or the intellect; connected with or requiring the use of reasoning or the mind.
relating to mental activity or intellect

Examples

The professor delivered a noetic analysis of the philosophical text.
Meditation is often considered to foster noetic experiences.
The debate touched upon noetic capacities unique to humans.

Synset Examples

intellectual problems
the triumph of the rational over the animal side of man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intellectual - relating to the intellect or mental activities

Etymology

From Greek noētikos (intellectual), from noein (to think), from nous (mind).
