Adelges abietis

1. NOUN

/ əˈdɛldʒiːz æˈbiːətɪs / (AmE), / əˈdɛldʒiːz æˈbiːətɪs / (BrE)
A species of adelgid, an insect pest affecting Norway spruce and various North American spruces, especially white and black spruce.
insect pest of spruce trees

Examples

The Adelges abietis infestation caused severe damage to the Norway spruces in the park.
Forestry experts are monitoring the spread of Adelges abietis in the affected regions.
Some scientists are researching biological controls to help manage Adelges abietis populations in North American forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spruce aphid - another sap-sucking insect pest of spruce trees

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus Adelges and the species name abietis, from Latin abies (fir tree).
