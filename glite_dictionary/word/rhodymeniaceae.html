Rhodymeniaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌroʊ.dɪˌmiː.niˈeɪ.siː / (AmE), / ˌrəʊ.dɪˌmiː.niˈeɪ.siː / (BrE)
A family of red algae in the division Rhodophyta, comprising several genera of marine algae.
family of red marine algae

Examples

Several genera within the Rhodymeniaceae are important components of coastal marine ecosystems.
The Rhodymeniaceae family includes various types of red algae found worldwide.
Scientists study the Rhodymeniaceae to understand marine biodiversity and algal evolution.

Etymology

From the genus Rhodymenia + Latin -aceae, used for family names in botany.

family of marine red algae

Examples

Many species of the Rhodymeniaceae family are known for their delicate, branched fronds.
The Rhodymeniaceae are important in marine ecosystems as primary producers.
Researchers have discovered several new genera within the Rhodymeniaceae based on genetic analysis.

From the genus name Rhodymenia, derived from Greek 'rhodon' (rose) and 'mēnē' (moon), with the botanical family suffix '-aceae'.
