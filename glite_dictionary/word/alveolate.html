alveolate

1. ADJ

/ ˌælviˈoʊlət / (AmE), / ˌalvɪˈəʊlət / (BrE)
Having cavities or pits resembling those of a honeycomb; marked by small hollow spaces or cells.
having honeycomb-like cavities or pits

Examples

The alveolate surface of the sponge makes it ideal for absorbing water.
Some fossils show alveolate structures where the original tissue contained air cells.
The honeycomb is a classic example of an alveolate pattern found in nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porous - having tiny holes that allow air or liquid to pass through

Etymology

From Latin alveolatus, from alveolus (small cavity, pit, or hollow).
