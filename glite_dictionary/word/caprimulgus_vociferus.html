Caprimulgus vociferus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæprɪˈmʌlɡəs voʊˈsɪfərəs / (AmE), / ˌkæprɪˈmʌlɡəs vəˈsɪfərəs / (BrE)
A North American nocturnal bird, also known as the eastern whip-poor-will, with grey-and-white plumage and a distinctive call.
North American nocturnal bird (whip-poor-will)

Examples

The distinctive call of the Caprimulgus vociferus can be heard on warm summer nights in the countryside.
Researchers tracked the migratory patterns of Caprimulgus vociferus across several eastern states.
Many birdwatchers hope to spot a Caprimulgus vociferus during their nocturnal outings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whip-poor-will - Common name for Caprimulgus vociferus, a nightjar species with a distinctive call

Etymology

From Latin: 'caprimulgus' meaning 'goatsucker' and 'vociferus' meaning 'noisy' or 'clamorous.'
