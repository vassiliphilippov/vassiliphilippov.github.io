hazel

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈheɪzəl / (BrE)
An Australian tree grown for its ornamental value, fine-grained wood, and edible nuts.
Australian tree with edible nuts and fine wood

Examples

The hazel is popular in Australian gardens for its beautiful foliage and edible nuts.
Furniture makers value hazel wood for its fine texture and durability.
We planted a hazel near the patio to enjoy its shade and nuts in summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hazel - shrub or small tree with edible nuts (genus Corylus, common in Northern Hemisphere)

2. NOUN

/ ˈheɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈheɪzəl / (BrE)
A light brown color with a yellowish or reddish tint; particularly a greenish-brown shade when describing eyes.
light brown color, often of eyes

Examples

Her eyes were a beautiful hazel, sparkling in the sunlight.
He wore a shirt in a soft hazel shade that matched the autumn leaves.
The artist mixed yellow and brown to create the perfect hazel color for the portrait's eyes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: brown - a color between red and yellow on the spectrum of visible light
  • Degree Higher: green - the color between blue and yellow in the spectrum

Etymology

From Old English hæsel (hazel tree), originally referring to the color of hazel nuts.

3. NOUN

/ ˈheɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈheɪzəl / (BrE)
a shrub or small tree of the genus Corylus, producing edible nuts (hazelnuts) enclosed in a leafy husk
shrub/tree producing hazelnuts

Examples

The hazel in the garden is covered with clusters of ripening nuts.
Squirrels love to feast on the hazel trees every autumn.
The wood from hazel is often used for making walking sticks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hazelnut - the edible nut of the hazel tree

Etymology

From Old English hæsel, from Proto-Germanic *haslaz.

5. NOUN

/ ˈheɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈheɪzəl / (BrE)
The fine-grained wood obtained from a hazel tree (genus Corylus) or the Australian hazel (genus Pomaderris), used especially for making walking sticks and wickerwork.
wood from a hazel tree

Examples

The artisan carved a beautiful cane from hazel.
Baskets woven from hazel are both strong and flexible.
They gathered branches of hazel for the fireplace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hazelnut - the nut of the hazel tree

Etymology

Old English hæsel, of Germanic origin, related to Dutch hazelaar and German Hasel.
