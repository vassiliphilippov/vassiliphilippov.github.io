Oktoberfest

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑk.toʊ.bɚˌfɛst / (AmE), / ˈɒk.tə.bəˌfɛst / (BrE)
a strong, sweet, copper-colored lager originally brewed in Germany for the Oktoberfest celebration
a type of German lager

Examples

He ordered an Oktoberfest at the pub to enjoy its malty sweetness.
During the festival, the brewery releases a special batch of Oktoberfest.
The rich, coppery taste of this Oktoberfest perfectly complements the autumn season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lager - a type of beer matured at low temperatures

Etymology

From German Oktoberfest (“October festival”), originally referring to the festival for which the beer was created.

2. NOUN

/ oʊkˈtoʊbɚˌfɛst / (AmE), / ɒkˈtəʊbəˌfɛst / (BrE)
an annual German festival held in autumn, celebrated with beer, food, and music, particularly in Munich
German beer festival held in autumn

Examples

Every year, thousands of people travel to Munich to experience the lively Oktoberfest.
The local community hosted its own Oktoberfest, complete with German food and traditional music.
During Oktoberfest, breweries release special seasonal beers just for the occasion.

Etymology

From German 'Oktoberfest' (October festival).
