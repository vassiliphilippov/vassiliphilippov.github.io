aflatoxin

1. NOUN

/ ˌæfləˈtɑksɪn / (AmE), / ˌæfləˈtɒksɪn / (BrE)
a toxic, carcinogenic substance produced by certain molds, especially Aspergillus species, often found in stored food products
carcinogenic mold toxin
medical

Examples

Many developing countries face significant health risks from aflatoxin contamination in staple foods.
The laboratory is testing peanut samples for traces of aflatoxin.
Consuming even small amounts of aflatoxin over time can increase the risk of liver cancer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mycotoxin - toxic compound produced by fungi

Etymology

Blend of Aspergillus flavus (a mold species) and toxin, originally coined in scientific literature.
