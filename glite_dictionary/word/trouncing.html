trouncing

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraʊnsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtraʊnsɪŋ / (BrE)
A decisive or overwhelming defeat.
a decisive defeat

Examples

The team suffered a humiliating trouncing in the championship game.
After the trouncing, the coach promised major changes to the lineup.
The election resulted in a trouncing for the incumbent senator.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defeat - the act of winning against someone in a contest
  • Antonym: victory - success in a contest or struggle

Etymology

From the verb 'trounce', origin uncertain, perhaps from dialectal English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtraʊnsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtraʊnsɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of beating someone severely, especially as punishment; a heavy physical defeat
severe beating or punishment

Examples

After the match, the losing team described the game as a real trouncing.
The headmaster warned that any misbehavior would earn the students a trouncing.
The rebels received a severe trouncing when they tried to storm the fortress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrashing - a severe beating or defeat
  • Synonym: beating - the act of striking someone repeatedly
  • Antonym: victory - an act of defeating an opponent

Etymology

From the verb 'trounce,' possibly of unknown origin, first recorded in the early 19th century.
