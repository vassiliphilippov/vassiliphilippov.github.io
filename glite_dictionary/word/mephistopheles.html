Mephistopheles

1. PROPN

/ məˌfɪstəˈfiliz / (AmE), / mɛˌfɪstəˈfiːliːz / (BrE)
A demon or evil spirit in German legend, notably the one to whom Faust sold his soul.
demon Faust sold his soul to

Examples

The character of Mephistopheles tempts Faust with promises of knowledge and power.
In the play, Mephistopheles serves as both tempter and tormentor to the protagonist.
People often refer to a cunning manipulator as a modern-day Mephistopheles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devil - the supreme spirit of evil; Satan
  • Synonym: demon - an evil spirit or supernatural being

Etymology

From German 'Mephistopheles', name of the demon in Goethe's 'Faust'.
