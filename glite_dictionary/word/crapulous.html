crapulous

1. ADJ

/ ˈkræp.jəl.əs / (AmE), / ˈkrap.jʊ.ləs / (BrE)
Suffering from the effects of excessive eating or drinking; showing the results of heavy indulgence in food or alcohol.
affected by excessive eating or drinking
archaic

Examples

After the feast, he felt distinctly crapulous and regretted his overindulgence.
She awoke with a crapulous headache after the party.
The morning after Thanksgiving was always a crapulous affair for him.

Synset Examples

crapulent sleep
a crapulous stomach

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hungover - suffering from a hangover

Etymology

From Latin crapulosus (very drunk), from crapula (drunkenness).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkræpjələs / (AmE), / ˈkræpjʊləs / (BrE)
Excessively given to eating or drinking; marked by gross intemperance.
excessively indulgent in food or drink
archaic

Examples

After the feast, the crapulous guests lounged on the couches, groaning in discomfort.
His crapulous lifestyle eventually took a toll on his health.
The novel portrays a crapulous nobleman whose excesses scandalize polite society.

Synset Examples

a crapulous old reprobate

Etymology

From Latin 'crapulosus' (drunk, intoxicated), from 'crapula' (intoxication, drunkenness).

Synset POS: s
