Compsognathus

1. NOUN

/ kɒmpˈsɒɡnəθəs / (AmE), / kɒmpˈsɒɡnəθəs / (BrE)
a genus of small, bipedal carnivorous dinosaurs from the late Jurassic period, known for their slender build and fast movement
a genus of small Jurassic carnivorous dinosaurs

Examples

The fossilized remains of a Compsognathus were discovered in southern Germany.
Scientists believe Compsognathus was a swift predator, hunting small vertebrates.
A well-preserved Compsognathus skeleton revealed details about its delicate bone structure.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Theropoda - the suborder of dinosaurs including bipedal carnivores like Compsognathus

Etymology

From Greek 'kompsos' (elegant) and 'gnathos' (jaw), meaning 'elegant jaw'.
