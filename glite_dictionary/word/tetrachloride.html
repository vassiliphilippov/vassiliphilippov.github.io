tetrachloride

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛtrəˈklɔːraɪd / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˈklɔːraɪd / (BrE)
a compound containing four chlorine atoms per molecule.
compound with four chlorine atoms

Examples

Carbon tetrachloride was once widely used as a cleaning solvent.
A tetrachloride can be toxic if inhaled or ingested.
The lab synthesized a new tetrachloride compound for industrial testing.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: trichloride - a compound containing three chlorine atoms per molecule
  • Degree Higher: pentachloride - a compound containing five chlorine atoms per molecule

Etymology

From Greek tetra- (four) and chloride (a compound of chlorine).
