Saurosuchus

1. NOUN

/ saʊroʊˈsuːkəs / (AmE), / sɔːrəˈsuːkəs / (BrE)
a genus of large, extinct, carnivorous archosaur reptiles from the Triassic period, known for their crocodile-like appearance
genus of extinct Triassic archosaur reptiles

Examples

Fossils of the Saurosuchus have been found in Argentina’s Ischigualasto Formation.
The giant Saurosuchus was one of the top predators of the Triassic landscape.
Scientists believe Saurosuchus hunted both large and small animals during its time.

Etymology

From Greek sauros (lizard) + soukhos (crocodile, after the Egyptian crocodile god).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɔːrəˈsuːkəs / (AmE), / ˌsɔːrəˈsuːkəs / (BrE)
A genus of large Triassic predatory archosaur belonging to the group rauisuchians, known for its crocodile-like appearance.
prehistoric predatory rauisuchian archosaur genus

Examples

The fossilized remains of Saurosuchus were discovered in Argentina, revealing a formidable Triassic predator.
Paleontologists believe that Saurosuchus was one of the apex predators of its ecosystem.
Unlike modern crocodiles, Saurosuchus had limbs positioned more directly beneath its body.

Etymology

From Greek 'sauros' (lizard) and 'suchus' (from 'soukhos', crocodile); named for its reptilian appearance.
