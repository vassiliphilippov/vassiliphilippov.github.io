Myrmecophagidae

1. NOUN

/ mɜːrmɛˌkɑːfəˈɡaɪdiː / (AmE), / mɜːrmɪˌkɒfəˈɡeɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of mammals comprising the New World anteaters, known for their elongated snouts and specialized tongues adapted for eating ants and termites.
taxonomic family of New World anteaters

Examples

The family Myrmecophagidae includes several species adapted to feeding on ants and termites.
Researchers discovered a new fossil belonging to the Myrmecophagidae in South America.
Members of Myrmecophagidae are easily recognized by their elongated snouts and tongues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anteater - a mammal that eats ants and termites, especially those in the Myrmecophagidae family

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'myrmex' (ant) + 'phagein' (to eat).

2. NOUN

/ mɜːrmɛkəˈfædʒɪdiː / (AmE), / mɜːmɛkəˈfædʒɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of mammals comprising the New World anteaters, known for their long snouts and tongues adapted for eating ants and termites.
family of New World anteaters

Examples

The Myrmecophagidae are native to Central and South America, where they inhabit forests and grasslands.
Members of the Myrmecophagidae family are specially adapted to feed on ants and termites using their elongated tongues.
The giant anteater is the largest species in the Myrmecophagidae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anteater - a mammal that feeds on ants and termites

Etymology

From Greek 'myrmex' (ant) + 'phagein' (to eat), referencing the ant-eating habits.
