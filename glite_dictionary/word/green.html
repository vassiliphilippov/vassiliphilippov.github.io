Green

1. NOUN

/ ɡrin / (AmE), / ɡriːn / (BrE)
a member or supporter of the Green Party, especially one active in environmental and ecological issues
member of the Green Party

Examples

The Green spoke passionately about renewable energy policies at the town hall meeting.
Many Greens advocate for stricter environmental regulations and sustainable development.
As a Green, she campaigns for climate change initiatives in her community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: environmentalist - a person concerned with or advocating protection of the environment
  • Antonym: conservative - a member or supporter of a Conservative Party

Etymology

Named after the Green Party, whose name refers to its focus on environmental ('green') issues.

2. PROPN

/ ɡriːn / (AmE), / ɡriːn / (BrE)
a river in the western United States that rises in Wyoming and flows south through Utah, joining the Colorado River.
river in Wyoming and Utah, USA

Examples

The Green is a major tributary of the Colorado River in the southwestern United States.
Many adventurers take rafting trips down the Green every summer.
The town of Green River, Wyoming, is named after the nearby Green.
