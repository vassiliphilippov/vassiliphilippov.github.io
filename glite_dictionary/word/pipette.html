pipette

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈpɛt / (AmE), / paɪˈpɛt / (BrE)
a slender, graduated glass or plastic tube used for measuring and transferring precise volumes of liquid in laboratory work
laboratory instrument for measuring/transferring liquid

Examples

The scientist used a pipette to transfer exactly 5 milliliters of solution.
Always rinse the pipette before measuring a different chemical to avoid contamination.
Modern laboratories often use an automatic pipette for greater precision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dropper - a small device for measuring out drops of liquid

Etymology

From French 'pipette', diminutive of 'pipe' (tube, pipe).
