somnific

1. ADJ

/ sɑmˈnɪfɪk / (AmE), / sɒmˈnɪfɪk / (BrE)
Tending to induce sleep; sleep-inducing.
causing sleep

Examples

The somnific effect of the medication made it difficult for her to stay awake.
His voice had a somnific quality that lulled the audience into drowsiness.
Warm milk is sometimes thought to have somnific properties, promoting better sleep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soporific - causing sleep; sleep-inducing

Etymology

From Latin 'somnificus' (sleep-making), from 'somnus' (sleep) + 'facere' (to make).
