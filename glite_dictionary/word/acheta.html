Acheta

1. NOUN

/ əˈkiːtə / (AmE), / əˈkiːtə / (BrE)
a genus of crickets that includes the common house and field crickets
a genus of crickets

Examples

The entomologist identified the specimen as belonging to the genus Acheta.
Many pet stores sell Acheta crickets as food for reptiles and amphibians.
Studies on Acheta have helped scientists understand insect communication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cricket - an insect related to grasshoppers, known for its chirping sound

Etymology

From Latin Acheta, the scientific genus name.

2. NOUN

/ əˈkiːtə / (AmE), / əˈkiːtə / (BrE)
A genus of crickets including the common house and field crickets.
a genus of common crickets

Examples

The common house cricket belongs to the genus Acheta.
Biologists identified the specimen as an Acheta cricket based on its distinctive song.
Many field guides describe the characteristics of Acheta species found worldwide.

Etymology

From Latin *Acheta*, used in biological taxonomy.
