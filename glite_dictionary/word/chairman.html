chairman

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛrˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈtʃeə.mən / (BrE)
The person, typically a man, who presides over the meetings of an organization, committee, or board.
male presiding officer of a meeting or group

Examples

The chairman called the meeting to order promptly at nine o'clock.
As chairman of the board, he was responsible for major decisions.
The chairman thanked everyone for their contributions after the final agenda item.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chairperson - a neutral term for someone who presides over meetings
  • Synonym: chairwoman - female officer who presides at meetings

Etymology

From 'chair' (the seat of authority) + 'man', dating from the 17th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈtʃɛrˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈtʃeə.mæn / (BrE)
to act as the leader or presiding officer of a meeting, committee, or academic department.
to preside over a group or meeting

Examples

She chairmaned the faculty meeting last Friday.
He has chairmaned several important committees during his career.
Next year, I will chairman the annual conference.

Synset Examples

She chaired the department for many years

  • Synonym: chair - to act as the leader or presiding officer of a meeting or committee; verb use
