wiggle

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪɡəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪɡl / (BrE)
a small, quick movement back and forth or from side to side; the act of wiggling
a small, quick movement

Examples

The puppy gave a little wiggle of excitement when it saw its owner.
He got rid of the stubborn tooth with just a gentle wiggle.
After a quick wiggle, the fish slipped off the hook and swam away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wriggle - to twist and turn with quick movements

Etymology

From Middle English *wiglen*, frequentative of *wiggen* (to move, shake).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00350531-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈwɪɡəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪɡ.əl / (BrE)
to move with small, quick movements from side to side or up and down
move with small, quick movements

Examples

The puppy began to wiggle its tail with excitement.
She tried not to wiggle in her seat during the long performance.
If you wiggle the handle, the door might open.

Synset Examples

Don't jiggle your finger while the nurse is putting on the bandage!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jiggle - move repeatedly up and down or from side to side with small rapid movements
  • Synonym: wobble - move unsteadily from side to side

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, possibly imitative or related to "wag" or Middle Low German "wiggeln".

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01902275-v
Synset POS: v
