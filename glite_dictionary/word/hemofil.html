Hemofil

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛməˌfɪl / (AmE), / ˈhɛməˌfɪl / (BrE)
A trademarked preparation of coagulation factor VIII, used in the treatment of hemophilia A.
trademarked coagulation factor VIII for hemophilia A
medical

Examples

Treatment with Hemofil significantly reduces bleeding episodes in patients with hemophilia A.
Before surgery, the hospital ensured an adequate supply of Hemofil was available for the patient's needs.
Some patients develop antibodies to Hemofil, making the treatment less effective over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: factor VIII - a blood clotting protein whose deficiency causes hemophilia A
  • Degree Lower: hemophilia A - a genetic disorder causing impaired blood clotting due to factor VIII deficiency
