gracefulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪsfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪsfəlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of moving in an attractive, smooth, and elegant way
elegant and attractive movement

Examples

The gracefulness of the dancer captivated the entire audience.
She moved with such gracefulness that everyone turned to watch her enter the room.
The swan glided across the water with natural gracefulness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elegance - the quality of being graceful and stylish in appearance or manner
  • Antonym: awkwardness - the quality of being clumsy or lacking grace

Etymology

From 'graceful' + '-ness'; 'graceful' from Old French *gracieux*, from Latin *gratia* (favor, charm).
