hematite

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛməˌtaɪt / (AmE), / ˈhiːməˌtaɪt / (BrE)
a reddish-black mineral, the principal ore of iron, consisting of ferric oxide in crystalline or earthy form
a mineral form of iron ore
medical

Examples

The rusty color of the rocks indicated a high concentration of hematite.
Miners extracted hematite from the site to supply the steel industry.
A sample of hematite displays a striking reddish streak when scratched.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnetite - an iron ore mineral, magnetic form
  • Synonym: limonite - another iron ore mineral, yellow-brown in color

Etymology

From Greek 'haimatitēs', meaning 'blood-like', referring to its color.
