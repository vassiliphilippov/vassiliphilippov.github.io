ayin

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːjiːn / (AmE), / ˈaɪɪn / (BrE)
the sixteenth letter of the Hebrew alphabet (ע), traditionally representing a voiced pharyngeal fricative.
16th letter of the Hebrew alphabet

Examples

The Hebrew word for 'tree' begins with an ayin.
Students practiced writing the character ayin during their Hebrew class.
Unlike some other Hebrew letters, ayin is silent in modern Israeli pronunciation.

Etymology

From Hebrew עַיִן (‘ayin), meaning 'eye'.
