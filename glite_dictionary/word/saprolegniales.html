Saprolegniales

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæproʊˌlɛɡˈnaɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌsæprəʊˌlɛɡˈnaɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of chiefly aquatic fungi, including many water molds, known for their filamentous structures
order of aquatic fungi (water molds)

Examples

Members of the Saprolegniales often infect fish eggs in freshwater environments.
Researchers studied the life cycle of several Saprolegniales species under laboratory conditions.
The spread of Saprolegniales can devastate populations of amphibians and aquatic invertebrates.

Etymology

From the genus name Saprolegnia, derived from Greek 'sapros' (rotten) and 'legnon' (wood), with the taxonomic suffix '-ales'.
