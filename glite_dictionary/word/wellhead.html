wellhead

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛlˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈwɛlˌhɛd / (BrE)
the source or origin of water for a well; the point at which water emerges from the ground to supply a well
the source of water for a well

Examples

The villagers gathered at the wellhead every morning to collect fresh water.
After the earthquake, the wellhead was damaged and needed urgent repair.
They built a small shelter over the wellhead to protect it from contamination.

Etymology

From 'well' (spring, water source) + 'head' (top or origin).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɛlˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈwɛlˌhɛd / (BrE)
a structure built at the top of a well, especially to control the extraction of fluids from the well
structure at the top of a well

Examples

Routine maintenance was being carried out at the wellhead to prevent leaks.
The oil company installed a new safety system at the wellhead after the incident.
Workers in hard hats monitored the pressure gauges attached to the wellhead.

Etymology

From 'well' (as in water or oil well) + 'head' (top or upper part).
