honeysucker

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌniˌsʌkər / (AmE), / ˈhʌniˌsʌkə / (BrE)
An Australasian bird with a tongue and bill especially adapted for extracting nectar from flowers.
Australasian nectar-feeding bird

Examples

The honeysucker darts from flower to flower, extracting nectar with its long tongue.
Early explorers were fascinated by the vibrant plumage of the region's honeysuckers.
Unlike many other birds, the honeysucker relies heavily on flowering shrubs for food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeyeater - Another nectar-feeding Australasian bird

Etymology

Formed from 'honey' and 'sucker', referring to its method of feeding on flower nectar.
