abject

1. ADJ

/ ˈæb.dʒɛkt / (AmE), / ˈab.dʒɛkt / (BrE)
(of a situation or person) showing or experiencing complete resignation or hopelessness; feeling or appearing completely defeated.
completely hopeless or resigned

Examples

After months of unemployment, he finally gave an abject apology to his former boss.
She felt an abject sense of failure after the loss.
The refugees faced abject surrender, with no hope of rescue.

Synset Examples

abject surrender

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hopeless - having no hope; despairing

Etymology

From Latin abjectus, past participle of abicere (to throw away), from ab- (away) + iacere (to throw).

2. ADJ

/ ˈæb.dʒɛkt / (AmE), / ˈæb.dʒɛkt / (BrE)
(of a situation or condition) extremely bad, unpleasant, and degrading; experienced to the maximum degree of misery or humiliation.
extremely miserable or degraded

Examples

He grew up in abject poverty, struggling to afford even basic necessities.
The refugees were living in abject misery after losing their homes to the storm.
Years of neglect had left the building in an abject state of disrepair.
