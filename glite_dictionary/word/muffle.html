muffle

1. VERB

/ ˈmʌf.əl / (AmE), / ˈmʌf.əl / (BrE)
to suppress or conceal an emotion, sound, or reaction, often in order to avoid notice or disturbance
suppress or conceal an emotion or reaction

Examples

She tried to muffle her laughter during the serious meeting.
He muffled a sigh when he heard the disappointing news.
The child managed to muffle his excitement so he wouldn't wake his parents.

Synset Examples

smother a yawn
muffle one's anger
strangle a laugh
repress a cry of fear

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suppress - to put an end to forcibly; subdue
  • Synonym: to repress - to restrain or prevent a feeling or action

Etymology

From Middle English 'moufelen' (wrap up), possibly of imitative origin.

2. VERB

/ ˈmʌf.əl / (AmE), / ˈmʌf.əl / (BrE)
to make a sound quieter, less distinct, or less clear, especially by covering or wrapping something around it
make a sound quieter by covering

Examples

She tried to muffle her laughter by covering her mouth with her hand.
Heavy curtains help muffle the noise from the street outside.
He spoke through a scarf that muffled his voice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dampen - to make a sound less loud or less intense
  • Antonym: to amplify - to increase the strength or volume of sound

Etymology

From Middle English *moflen*, of uncertain origin; possibly related to Old French *moufler* (to cover, muffle).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02195757-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈmʌf.əl / (AmE), / ˈmʌf.əl / (BrE)
a kiln with an inner chamber used for firing objects at a low temperature, often to protect them from direct flame or smoke
kiln with inner chamber for low-temperature firing

Examples

The pottery was placed in the muffle to ensure an even, gentle firing.
She used a muffle to fire delicate glassware that could not withstand high temperatures.
The technician carefully loaded the specimens into the muffle for heat treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kiln - a furnace or oven for firing pottery, bricks, or other materials

Etymology

From French 'moufler', meaning 'to muffle', referring to the chamber isolating objects from direct heat.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03802584-n
Synset POS: n
