Albrecht Durer

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑlˌbrɛkt ˈdʊrər / (AmE), / ˈalbrɛxt ˈdjʊərə / (BrE)
(1471–1528) A leading German painter, printmaker, and theorist of the Renaissance, known for his woodcuts, engravings, and paintings.
German Renaissance painter and engraver

Examples

Albrecht Durer revolutionized printmaking with his detailed engravings and woodcuts.
The works of Albrecht Durer influenced artists across Europe during and after the Renaissance.
A self-portrait by Albrecht Durer is displayed in the Alte Pinakothek in Munich.

Etymology

German name; Albrecht from Old High German *adal* (noble) + *beraht* (bright); Durer is a family name.
