aggregation

1. NOUN

/ ˌæɡ.rəˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌæɡ.rɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a group or collection of things gathered together, or the process of collecting things into a whole
group or collection of things

Examples

The aggregation of data from different sources provided a clearer picture of the company's performance.
An aggregation of small particles formed a much larger rock over time.
The study focused on the aggregation of voters into distinct political groups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collection - a group of things or people gathered together
  • Antonym: dispersal - the process of spreading things over a wide area

Etymology

From Latin aggregatio(n-), from aggregare (to add to, collect).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæɡ.rəˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌæɡ.rɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of collecting or gathering things together into a whole
act of gathering things together

Examples

The aggregation of data from multiple sources creates a comprehensive database.
There has been an aggregation of dust on the shelves over the past year.
Our team focused on the aggregation of all relevant documents before the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collection - the act of collecting things; a group of gathered items
  • Antonym: dispersal - the act of distributing or spreading things over a wide area

Etymology

From Latin aggregatio (a collecting together), from aggregare (to group together).

WordNet ID: oewn-01016008-n
Synset POS: n
