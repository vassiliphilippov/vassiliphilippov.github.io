Geomys bursarius

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːəˌmɪs bɜːrˈsɛəriəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːəmɪs bɜːˈsɑːrɪəs / (BrE)
A species of gopher native to the grasslands of central North America, known for burrowing habits.
plains pocket gopher species

Examples

The Geomys bursarius builds complex tunnel systems in prairie soils.
Farmers sometimes consider Geomys bursarius a pest because it feeds on root crops.
Wildlife researchers track the population of Geomys bursarius to monitor ecosystem health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pocket gopher - burrowing rodent with fur-lined cheek pouches

Etymology

From scientific Latin: Genus 'Geomys' (from Greek 'ge' meaning earth and 'mys' meaning mouse/rat) and species 'bursarius' (Latin, meaning purse-bearing, referring to cheek pouches).
