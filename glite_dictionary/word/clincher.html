clincher

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɪntʃər / (AmE), / ˈklɪntʃə / (BrE)
a tool used to bend over and secure the ends of nails, bolts, or rivets
tool for clinching nails or rivets

Examples

The carpenter used a clincher to secure the rivets on the metal frame.
A clincher is essential when working with horseshoes and nails.
After hammering in the nail, he finished the job with a clincher to bend it over.

Etymology

From 'clinch' (to secure or fasten) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈklɪntʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈklɪntʃə / (BrE)
a fact, argument, or piece of evidence that decisively settles a debate or problem
decisive argument or fact

Examples

Her final point was the clincher that won everyone over to her side.
The lawyer presented a clincher in the form of undeniable DNA evidence.
That photograph served as the clincher and settled the debate for good.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decider - something that decides a contest or question
  • Degree Lower: argument - a statement, reason, or fact for or against a point

Etymology

From 'clinch' (to settle or confirm) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

3. NOUN

/ ˈklɪntʃər / (AmE), / ˈklɪntʃə / (BrE)
a fact, point, or remark that settles an argument or makes a decision certain
decisive point or argument

Examples

Her final argument was the clincher that convinced the jury.
The price was good, but the free warranty was the real clincher.
He was losing the debate until he delivered a clincher that silenced his opponents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decider - something that determines an outcome
  • Degree Lower: argument - a reason given in proof or rebuttal

Etymology

From the verb 'clinch', meaning 'to settle decisively', plus the agent suffix '-er'.
