freetail

1. NOUN

/ ˈfriˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈfriːteɪl / (BrE)
A small, fast-flying insectivorous bat with leathery ears and a long tail extending beyond the tail membrane, commonly found in warm regions.
a type of small bat with a long tail

Examples

The Mexican freetail is one of the most common bats in Texas.
We spotted a freetail swooping through the evening sky, hunting insects.
Researchers tracked the flight patterns of the freetail to learn about its migration habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: free-tailed bat - bat with a tail extending beyond the tail membrane
