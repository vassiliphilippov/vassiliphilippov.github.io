unofficial

1. ADJ

/ ʌnəˈfɪʃəl / (AmE), / ʌnəˈfɪʃəl / (BrE)
not formally recognized or authorized by an official authority; without official approval or status.
not officially recognized or authorized

Examples

He acted as the unofficial spokesperson during the meeting.
People shared an unofficial report before the official announcement was made.
They arranged an unofficial gathering to discuss the project informally.

Synset Examples

a sort of unofficial mayor
an unofficial estimate
he participated in an unofficial capacity

Related Terms

  • Antonym: official - recognized by authority; authorized

Etymology

Formed from the prefix un- (not) + official.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnəˈfɪʃəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnəˈfɪʃəl / (BrE)
not approved, recognized, or established by an official authority; not formal or authorized
not officially recognized or authorized

Examples

The results of the poll are still unofficial and subject to verification.
She attended the meeting in an unofficial capacity, representing her own interests.
There were unofficial reports that the company was planning to expand overseas.

Synset Examples

the early election returns are unofficial

Related Terms

  • Antonym: official - having formal authorization or recognition

Etymology

From un- (not) + official, ultimately from Latin 'officium' (duty, service).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01639061-s
Synset POS: s
