Astrophyton

1. NOUN

/ ˌæstrəˈfaɪtən / (AmE), / ˌastrəˈfʌɪtən / (BrE)
A genus of large basket stars, marine animals with branching arms, found in warm seas.
a genus of basket stars

Examples

The Astrophyton uses its branched arms to catch plankton in the ocean currents.
Marine biologists discovered a new species within the Astrophyton genus off the Caribbean coast.
At the aquarium, visitors were fascinated by the delicate structure of the Astrophyton.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gorgonocephalus - another genus of basket stars

Etymology

From Greek 'astro-' (star) and 'phyton' (plant), referring to the star-like appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæstrəˈfaɪtɑn / (AmE), / ˌæstrəˈfaɪtɒn / (BrE)
A genus of basket stars, marine echinoderms known for their many-branched arms.
genus of basket stars (marine animals)

Examples

The Astrophyton can often be seen extending its branched arms at night to capture food.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Astrophyton genus in the Caribbean Sea.
Because of their intricate arms, Astrophyton are sometimes called basket stars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basket star - a type of brittle star with branching arms

Etymology

From Greek 'astron' (star) + 'phyton' (plant), alluding to the star-like appearance of its arms.
