disqualifying

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈkwɑlɪˌfaɪɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪsˈkwɒlɪˌfaɪɪŋ / (BrE)
making someone or something ineligible or not qualified, especially by taking away a legal right or by violating rules.
rendering ineligible or unqualified

Examples

The candidate made a disqualifying error on the application form.
A criminal record can be a disqualifying factor for some jobs.
There were no disqualifying violations found during the inspection.

Synset Examples

certain disabling restrictions disqualified him for citizenship

Related Terms

  • Antonym: qualifying - making eligible or qualified
  • Synonym: disqualified - having been made ineligible or unqualified
