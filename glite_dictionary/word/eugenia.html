Eugenia

1. NOUN

/ juːˈdʒiːniə / (AmE), / juːˈdʒiːniə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical trees and shrubs with aromatic leaves and often hard, valuable wood, some species producing edible fruits.
genus of tropical trees and shrubs

Examples

The Eugenia in our garden fills the air with a sweet, spicy fragrance each spring.
Many species of Eugenia are valued for their hard timber and attractive foliage.
The bright red fruit of the Eugenia is eaten in some tropical countries.

Etymology

From Latin 'Eugenia', from Greek 'eugenēs', meaning 'well-born' (applied as a plant genus name).
