lechwe

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛtʃˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈlɛtʃwi / (BrE)
a tawny African antelope (Kobus leche) found in wet grassy plains, noted for its long legs and semi-aquatic habits
African antelope of wet plains

Examples

A small herd of lechwe grazed by the marsh's edge as the sun rose.
Wildlife photographers visit the Zambezi floodplains to capture images of lechwe in their natural habitat.
Because lechwe thrive in wetlands, they are rarely found far from water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antelope - a swift-running deerlike animal of Africa and Asia with smooth horns

Etymology

From Lozi (Zambia) 'lečwe', the local name for the animal.
