actinomyxidian

1. NOUN

/ ˌæk.tɪ.noʊ.mɪkˈsɪd.i.ən / (AmE), / ˌæktɪnəʊmɪkˈsɪdiən / (BrE)
a parasitic protozoan of the group Actinomyxidia, which infects worms and other invertebrates
parasitic protozoan infecting worms
medical

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species of actinomyxidian infecting freshwater worms.
The lifecycle of an actinomyxidian often involves multiple hosts, including annelid worms.
Microscopic examination revealed actinomyxidians in the tissue samples from the river sediment.

Etymology

From New Latin Actinomyxidia, combining 'actino-' (ray) and 'myxidia' (slime), referring to the protozoan's form.
