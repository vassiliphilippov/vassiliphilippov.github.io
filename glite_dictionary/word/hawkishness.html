hawkishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔkɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈhɔːkɪʃnəs / (BrE)
a tendency to support aggressive or confrontational policies, especially in foreign relations.
support for aggressive or confrontational policies

Examples

The senator's hawkishness on defense has drawn both criticism and praise.
Some economists argue that the central bank's hawkishness could slow economic growth.
Her hawkishness was evident in her calls for stronger military intervention abroad.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dovishness - preference for peace and diplomacy rather than confrontation, especially in politics
  • Synonym: hawkish - favoring aggressive or warlike policies

Etymology

From 'hawkish' (resembling a hawk; favoring aggressive policies) + '-ness' (forming nouns indicating a state or quality).
