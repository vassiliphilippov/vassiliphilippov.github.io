Platanthera chlorantha

1. NOUN

/ pləˈtænθərə kloʊˈrænθə / (AmE), / pləˈtanθərə kləˈranθə / (BrE)
A species of orchid found in southern Europe, noted for its dark green, larger, and less fragrant flowers compared to Platanthera bifolia.
a species of orchid found in southern Europe

Examples

The rare Platanthera chlorantha thrives in the chalk grasslands of southern Europe.
Unlike its relative, Platanthera chlorantha produces larger but less fragrant green flowers.
Botanists often confuse Platanthera chlorantha with similar orchid species due to overlap in habitat.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Platanthera' (from Greek 'platys' meaning 'broad' and 'anthera' meaning 'anther') and specific epithet 'chlorantha' (from Greek 'chloros' meaning 'green' and 'anthos' meaning 'flower').
