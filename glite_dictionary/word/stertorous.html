stertorous

1. ADJ

/ ˈstɜr.tɚ.əs / (AmE), / ˈstɜː.tər.əs / (BrE)
(of breathing) making a harsh or heavy snoring or gasping sound, typically indicating obstruction of the air passages.
making a harsh snoring sound (breathing)
medical

Examples

The patient's stertorous breathing could be heard throughout the ward.
Doctors immediately recognized the stertorous respiration as a sign of airway obstruction.
His stertorous snoring kept everyone else awake during the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snoring - the act of breathing noisily while sleeping

Etymology

From Latin 'stertor' (snoring).
