harm

1. NOUN

/ hɑrm / (AmE), / hɑːm / (BrE)
Physical injury or damage to a person or thing.
damage or injury

Examples

The company took steps to prevent any further harm to the environment.
He meant no harm by his remark, but she was still offended.
Exposure to chemicals can cause serious harm if proper safety measures are not followed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damage - physical harm that impairs the value, usefulness, or normal function of something
  • Synonym: injury - harm done to a person's body
  • Antonym: benefit - an advantage or positive outcome, the opposite of harm
  • Synonym: hurt - to cause pain or injury

Etymology

From Old English hearm (insult, grief, hurt, pain), of Germanic origin.
