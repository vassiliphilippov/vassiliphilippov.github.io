Tupi

1. NOUN

/ tuˈpi / (AmE), / tjuːˈpiː / (BrE)
the language spoken by the Tupi people of Brazil and Paraguay
language of the Tupi people

Examples

Many traditional stories were originally told in Tupi before being translated into Portuguese.
The study of Tupi provides valuable insights into indigenous Brazilian cultures.
He learned Tupi to communicate with local communities during his field research.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'Tupi', referring to the indigenous Tupi people.

2. NOUN

/ tuːˈpiː / (AmE), / tjuːˈpiː / (BrE)
A member of an indigenous people originally living in Brazil and Paraguay.
a South American indigenous person

Examples

The Tupi are known for their rich cultural traditions and art.
Many modern Brazilians have ancestors who were Tupi.
Historical records describe how the Tupi interacted with Portuguese colonizers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Guarani - a member of another indigenous people from South America, primarily Paraguay

Etymology

From Portuguese Tupi, from the indigenous Tupi language.
