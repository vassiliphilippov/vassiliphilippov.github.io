Nubia

1. PROPN

/ ˈnuːbiə / (AmE), / ˈnjuːbɪə / (BrE)
an ancient region in northeastern Africa, located along the Nile in what is now southern Egypt and northern Sudan.
ancient region in northeastern Africa

Examples

Archaeologists have uncovered ancient artifacts in Nubia dating back thousands of years.
The civilization of Nubia was known for its rich culture and powerful kingdoms.
Much of Nubia now lies beneath Lake Nasser after the construction of the Aswan High Dam.

Etymology

From Latin 'Nubia', possibly from the Egyptian word for gold, 'nub', as the area was known for its gold mines.
