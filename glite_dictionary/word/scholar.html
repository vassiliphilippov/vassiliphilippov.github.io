scholar

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɑːlər / (AmE), / ˈskɒlə / (BrE)
a student who has been awarded a scholarship to support their studies
student with a scholarship

Examples

After winning the award, he became a proud scholar at his university.
The boarding school offers several places each year for talented scholars from disadvantaged backgrounds.
As a scholar, she received free tuition and a stipend for her living expenses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: student - a person who is studying at a school or college

Etymology

From Old French escoler, from Latin scholāris (related to school).

2. NOUN

/ ˈskɑːlər / (AmE), / ˈskɒlə / (BrE)
a person who has acquired deep knowledge in a particular field of study through extensive learning and research
learned person; academic expert

Examples

The university appointed her as a visiting scholar in recognition of her achievements.
Many scholars have written extensively about the origins of language.
To become a leading scholar, one must dedicate years to careful research and study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: academic - a person who teaches or conducts research at a college or university
  • Degree Lower: student - a person formally engaged in learning, especially at a school or university

Etymology

From Old French 'escoler', from Latin 'scholaris' (of a school) and 'schola' (school).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈskɑːlər / (AmE), / ˈskɒlə / (BrE)
a person who is learning, especially a child taught by a teacher; also, a person who studies a particular subject deeply
a person who learns or studies

Examples

The young scholar listened carefully to every word the teacher said.
Each scholar was eager to demonstrate what they had learned during the year.
As a scholar of languages, she quickly mastered French and Spanish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: student - a person who is studying, especially at a school or college

Etymology

From Old English scolere or Latin scholaris (of a school), from schola (school).
