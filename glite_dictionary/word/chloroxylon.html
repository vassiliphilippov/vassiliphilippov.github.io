Chloroxylon

1. NOUN

/ klɔːˈrɑːksaɪˌlɒn / (AmE), / klɒˈrəʊksɪˌlɒn / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous trees found in India and Sri Lanka, known for their durable, aromatic wood.
genus of South Asian deciduous trees

Examples

The wood of Chloroxylon is prized for its strength and durability in fine carpentry.
Botanists discovered a rare species of Chloroxylon growing in the Sri Lankan forest.
Traditional furniture makers often seek Chloroxylon for its aromatic quality and toughness.

Etymology

From Greek 'chloros' (green) and 'xylon' (wood), referring to the greenish wood.

2. NOUN

/ klɔːˈrɑːksɪˌlɒn / (AmE), / klɔːˈrɒksɪˌlɒn / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous trees native to India and Sri Lanka, including the species known as East Indian satinwood.
genus of deciduous trees from South Asia

Examples

The wood of Chloroxylon species is highly valued for fine furniture and veneers.
Botanists studied Chloroxylon trees to better understand their ecological role in Indian forests.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect wild populations of Chloroxylon from overexploitation.

Etymology

From Greek 'chloros' (green) and 'xylon' (wood).
