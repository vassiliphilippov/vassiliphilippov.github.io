peradventure

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrəˈdventʃər / (AmE), / ˌpɛrəˈdventʃə / (BrE)
(archaic) doubt or uncertainty as to whether something is the case.
doubt or uncertainty (archaic)
archaic

Examples

The jury was convinced beyond peradventure of the defendant's innocence.
There can be no peradventure about the importance of this discovery.
She spoke with confidence, leaving no room for peradventure in her conclusion.

Synset Examples

this proves beyond peradventure that he is innocent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doubt - a feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction
  • Antonym: certainty - the quality of being reliably true or sure

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French 'par aventure' meaning 'by chance'.

2. ADV

/ ˌpɛr.ədˈvɛn.tʃər / (AmE), / ˌpɛr.ədˈvɛn.tʃə / (BrE)
(archaic) Perhaps; possibly; by chance.
perhaps; by chance (archaic)
archaic

Examples

It may peradventure be thought that there never was such a time.
Should you, peradventure, encounter him, give him my regards.
The treasure might, peradventure, still be hidden beneath the old oak tree.

Synset Examples

perhaps she will call tomorrow
we may possibly run into them at the concert
it may peradventure be thought that there never was such a time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perhaps - possibly; maybe
  • Synonym: possibly - by chance; it may be the case

Etymology

From Old French 'par aventure' (by chance), from Latin 'adventura' (things about to happen).
