parchment

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrtʃmənt / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtʃmənt / (BrE)
a high-quality, stiff paper made to resemble sheepskin, often used for diplomas, certificates, or special documents
superior paper resembling sheepskin

Examples

The certificate was printed on elegant parchment to give it a traditional feel.
Many wedding invitations are still made using high-quality parchment paper.
She wrote her letter on parchment to make it look more distinguished.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vellum - fine parchment made originally from calfskin

Etymology

From Latin 'pergamentum' (parchment), originally from Pergamum, an ancient city where parchment was reputedly invented.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrtʃmənt / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtʃmənt / (BrE)
a material made from the skin of a sheep or goat, specially prepared for writing or printing on, used especially in the past
prepared animal skin for writing

Examples

The ancient manuscript was preserved on parchment for centuries in the monastery library.
Medieval scribes carefully inscribed texts onto sheets of parchment.
He admired the smooth texture of the parchment before beginning to write.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vellum - fine-quality parchment made from calf skin

Etymology

From Old French *parchemin*, from Latin *pergamenum* (from Pergamum, an ancient city where it was first used extensively).
