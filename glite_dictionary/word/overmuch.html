overmuch

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvərˈmʌtʃ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈmʌtʃ / (BrE)
Too much; excessive in quantity, degree, or extent.
too much; excessive

Examples

She was criticized for showing overmuch enthusiasm during the meeting.
He spends overmuch time worrying about things he can't control.
There isn't overmuch food left after the party.

Synset Examples

showed overmuch affection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excessive - greater than is reasonable or necessary
  • Antonym: insufficient - not enough in amount, degree, or extent

Etymology

From Middle English overmuche, from over- + much.

2. ADV

/ ˌoʊ.vɚˈmʌtʃ / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.vəˈmʌtʃ / (BrE)
to a greater extent than is reasonable or necessary; excessively
excessively; more than necessary

Examples

She avoids going out overmuch during the winter months.
Try not to worry overmuch about minor details.
He wasn't criticized overmuch for his mistake.

Synset Examples

she eats too much
let's not blame them overmuch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: too much - to a greater degree than is desirable or appropriate
  • Synonym: excessively - to too great a degree
  • Antonym: moderately - to a moderate or reasonable degree

Etymology

From Middle English overmuche, from over- (excessively) + much.

3. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərˈmʌtʃ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈmʌtʃ / (BrE)
an excessive or greater amount than is appropriate or necessary
excessive or undue quantity

Examples

He blamed the project’s failure on an overmuch of optimism and too little planning.
There was an overmuch of food at the banquet, more than anyone could possibly eat.
An overmuch of criticism can discourage even the most dedicated students.

Synset Examples

four-year-olds have an overabundance of energy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excess - an amount that is more than necessary or permitted
  • Synonym: surplus - an amount of something left over; more than needed
  • Antonym: deficiency - a lack or shortage of something needed

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (excess) + 'much' (large quantity); attested since Middle English.
