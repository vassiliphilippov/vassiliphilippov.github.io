antheridium

1. NOUN

/ ˌænθəˈrɪdiəm / (AmE), / ˌænθəˈrɪdɪəm / (BrE)
the male reproductive organ of spore-producing plants, such as algae, mosses, and ferns, producing male gametes called antherozoids
male sex organ in spore-producing plants
medical

Examples

In mosses, the antheridium releases sperm that swim toward the egg.
Each antheridium is responsible for producing several motile antherozoids.
Scientists observed the formation of an antheridium under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: archegonium - the female sex organ in spore-producing plants

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek anthera (anther) + -idium (diminutive suffix).
