stocktake

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑkˌteɪk / (AmE), / ˈstɒkˌteɪk / (BrE)
an act of counting and checking the goods and materials that a business has in stock
an act of checking stock in business

Examples

We always schedule a stocktake at the end of each quarter to keep our records accurate.
The annual stocktake revealed several missing items from the warehouse.
Due to the stocktake, the store will be closed for two days next week.

Synset Examples

the auditor did not attend the stocktake or check the valuations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inventory - a complete list of items such as property, goods in stock, or the contents of a building

Etymology

From 'stock' + 'take', originally British English business jargon.
