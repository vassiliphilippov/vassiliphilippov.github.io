Dieffenbachia

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːfənˈbɑːkiə / (AmE), / ˌdiːfənˈbækiə / (BrE)
a genus of evergreen perennial plants native to tropical America, known for their lush foliage and poisonous sap; often grown as ornamental houseplants
a genus of tropical ornamental plants

Examples

The large, decorative leaves of the Dieffenbachia make it a popular choice for indoor gardens.
Be careful when handling Dieffenbachia, as its sap can irritate the skin.
She placed a healthy Dieffenbachia next to her office window.

Etymology

Named after Ernst Dieffenbach, 19th-century German physician and botanist.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdiːfənˈbækiə / (AmE), / ˌdiːfənˈbækiə / (BrE)
a genus of evergreen perennial herbs native to tropical America, known for their large, lush leaves and poisonous sap; often cultivated as houseplants
a genus of tropical houseplants with poisonous sap

Examples

Many people choose Dieffenbachia for their homes because of its attractive, variegated leaves.
You should keep Dieffenbachia out of reach of pets and children, as its sap is toxic.
Several varieties of Dieffenbachia are prized as decorative houseplants throughout the world.

Etymology

Named after Joseph Dieffenbach (1796–1863), Austrian head gardener at the Schönbrunn Palace.
