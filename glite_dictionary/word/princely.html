princely

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɪnsli / (AmE), / ˈprɪnsli / (BrE)
having the rank of or characteristic of a prince; befitting a prince in dignity, magnificence, or generosity
having qualities or status of a prince

Examples

He was known for his princely generosity towards his friends and community.
The guests were treated to a princely banquet in the lavish hall.
Despite losing his crown, he carried himself with a princely dignity.

Synset Examples

a princely bearing
princely manner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: royal - relating to a king or queen; regal
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance

Etymology

From prince + -ly, forming 'princely' to mean 'like a prince'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈprɪnsli / (AmE), / ˈprɪnsli / (BrE)
(of a sum, amount, or thing) impressively large or luxurious; characteristic of wealth and grandeur.
impressively large or luxurious; grand

Examples

She received a princely sum for her memoirs.
They live in a princely mansion overlooking the bay.
The company awarded him a princely bonus for his outstanding performance.

Synset Examples

a princely sum
gilded dining rooms
these architecture magazines are full of the lush interiors of the rich and famous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lavish - sumptuously rich, elaborate, or luxurious
  • Antonym: modest - not large or elaborate; relatively moderate

Etymology

Formed from 'prince' + '-ly', originally meaning 'befitting a prince'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02032205-s
Synset POS: s
