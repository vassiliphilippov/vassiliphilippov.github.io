stormy

1. ADJ

/ ˈstɔrmi / (AmE), / ˈstɔːmi / (BrE)
(especially of weather) characterized by storms, strong winds, and often heavy rain; tumultuous or marked by disturbance.
characterized by storms or turmoil

Examples

We decided to postpone the picnic because the weather was stormy.
The ship struggled through the stormy waters all night.
Their relationship had many stormy moments, but they always made up afterwards.

Synset Examples

a stormy day
wide and stormy seas

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - (of weather or situations) not windy, stormy, or disturbed
  • Synonym: turbulent - characterized by conflict, disorder, or confusion; not controlled or calm

Etymology

From storm +‎ -y, following the typical English adjective-forming suffix.

2. ADJ

/ ˈstɔrmi / (AmE), / ˈstɔːmi / (BrE)
(of a situation, relationship, or person) characterized by strong or violent emotions, conflict, or tumultuous behavior.
characterized by violent emotions or conflict

Examples

The couple endured a stormy marriage with frequent arguments and reconciliations.
Their stormy relationship caused concern among their friends and family.
After a stormy debate, the committee finally reached a decision.

Synset Examples

a stormy argument
a stormy marriage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turbulent - full of conflict, disorder, or confusion; not calm
  • Antonym: peaceful - free from disturbance; calm
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling strong emotions; peaceful

Etymology

From storm + -y, meaning 'full of storms'; figurative use dates from the early 17th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01747161-s
Synset POS: s
