Raphicerus

1. NOUN

/ ræˈfɪsərəs / (AmE), / ræˈfɪsərəs / (BrE)
A genus of small African antelopes, commonly known as steenboks, found in grasslands and savannas.
a genus of small African antelopes

Examples

The Raphicerus genus includes the well-known steenbok of southern Africa.
Scientists studied the habitat preferences of several Raphicerus species.
The Raphicerus antelopes are adapted to open grassland environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steenbok - a small antelope of southern and eastern Africa

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Greek raphis (needle) and keras (horn).

2. NOUN

/ ræˈfɪsərəs / (AmE), / ræˈfɪsərəs / (BrE)
A genus of small antelopes native to sub-Saharan Africa, commonly known as steenboks.
a genus of African small antelopes

Examples

The Raphicerus genus includes the common steenbok found throughout southern Africa.
Biologists study Raphicerus to understand the evolution of small African antelopes.
Three species are recognized within the Raphicerus genus, all adapted to savanna habitats.

Etymology

From modern Latin Raphicerus, from Greek rhaphis (needle, reference to slender shape) + keras (horn).
