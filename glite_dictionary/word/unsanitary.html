unsanitary

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsænəˌtɛri / (AmE), / ʌnˈsænɪtəri / (BrE)
not clean or hygienic; likely to cause disease or harm due to a lack of cleanliness.
not clean or hygienic

Examples

The kitchen was declared unsanitary by the health inspector and shut down.
Living in unsanitary conditions can increase the risk of disease.
She refused to eat at the restaurant after seeing its unsanitary bathrooms.

Synset Examples

unsanitary open sewers
grim and unsanitary conditions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sanitary - clean and hygienic; free from dirt or germs

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' (meaning 'not') to 'sanitary'.
