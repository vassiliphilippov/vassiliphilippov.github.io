cringe

1. NOUN

/ krɪndʒ / (AmE), / krɪndʒ / (BrE)
Something that causes embarrassment or acute discomfort due to awkwardness or shame.
embarrassing or awkward thing
informal

Examples

That joke was such a cringe that the whole room went silent.
She posted a video online, but later deleted it because it was full of cringe.
Watching old talent show auditions always gives me second-hand cringe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embarrassment - feeling of awkwardness, self-consciousness, or shame
  • Synonym: awkwardness - state or feeling of being socially uncomfortable or embarrassed

2. VERB

/ krɪndʒ / (AmE), / krɪndʒ / (BrE)
to draw back or shrink, typically in fear or in anticipation of pain
draw back in fear or pain

Examples

She couldn’t help but cringe at the loud crash behind her.
Whenever he sees needles, he tends to cringe instinctively.
The dog would cringe whenever someone raised their voice.

Synset Examples

she flinched when they showed the slaughtering of the calf

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flinch - to make a quick, nervous movement as an instinctive reaction to fear, pain, or surprise
  • Synonym: recoil - to suddenly spring or flinch back in fear, horror, or disgust

Etymology

From Old English cringan (to fall, yield, give way), related to Old Norse kringla (to bend, turn).

3. VERB

/ krɪndʒ / (AmE), / krɪndʒ / (BrE)
to bend one's body or shrink back in fear or submission
shrink back in fear or submission

Examples

The dog would cringe whenever it heard a loud noise.
She could not help but cringe before the intimidating boss.
He cringed in the corner, afraid to speak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cower - to crouch down in fear
  • Antonym: stand tall - to behave in a confident, unafraid manner

Etymology

From Old English cringan (to yield, fall in battle).

4. NOUN

/ krɪndʒ / (AmE), / krɪndʒ / (BrE)
A posture or gesture of shrinking or recoiling, often from fear, embarrassment, or disgust.
act of recoiling or shrinking

Examples

His obvious lie caused a visible cringe from those in the audience.
With a nervous cringe, she awaited the teacher's reaction to her mistake.
The dog's cringe showed its fear of the loud thunder.

Etymology

From Old English cringan (to yield, fall in battle, collapse).
