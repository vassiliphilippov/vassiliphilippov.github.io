baseball

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪsˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsˌbɔːl / (BrE)
A hard, small ball with a leather cover, used in playing the game of baseball.
ball used in baseball game

Examples

He caught the baseball with his glove during practice.
The pitcher threw the baseball at an incredible speed.
Several baseballs were signed by the famous player and given to fans.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪsˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsˌbɔːl / (BrE)
A game played between two teams of nine players who take turns batting and fielding, using a bat and ball, with the goal of scoring runs.
a team game played with bat and ball

Examples

He has played baseball since he was a child.
The baseball team won the championship last season.
Many families watch baseball together on summer evenings.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cricket - A bat-and-ball game played between two teams, popular in England and Commonwealth countries

Etymology

From base + ball, first recorded in 18th-century England and America.
