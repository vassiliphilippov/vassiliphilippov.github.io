likeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪknəs / (AmE), / ˈlaɪknɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being similar in appearance, character, or nature to someone or something else.
similarity in appearance or character

Examples

The child bore a striking likeness to her grandmother.
There is a clear likeness between the two paintings.
Many myths describe humans as being made in the likeness of the gods.

Synset Examples

man created God in his own likeness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: similarity - the state of being similar
  • Antonym: difference - the state of being unlike or dissimilar

Etymology

From Old English 'gelīcnis', influenced by like + -ness.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪknɪs / (AmE), / ˈlaɪknɪs / (BrE)
a picture, portrait, or graphic image representing a person or thing
portrait or graphic image of a person or thing

Examples

The artist painted a remarkable likeness of the queen.
A bronze likeness of the founder stands in the town square.
The drawing bears a close likeness to the original subject.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: portrait - a painting, photograph, or drawing of a person

Etymology

From Old English līcnes, from līc (body, form) + -nes (ness).
