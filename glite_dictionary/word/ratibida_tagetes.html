Ratibida tagetes

1. NOUN

/ rəˈtɪbɪdə təˈdʒiːtiːz / (AmE), / rəˈtɪbɪdə tɑːˈdʒiːtiːz / (BrE)
A species of coneflower native to central to southwestern United States, known for its yellow or orange-yellow flowers.
a coneflower species native to the US Southwest

Examples

Many gardens in Texas feature the bright yellow blooms of Ratibida tagetes.
Botanists studied the drought resistance of Ratibida tagetes in arid environments.
Ratibida tagetes is a favorite among pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coneflower - a common name for several daisy-like plants in the family Asteraceae

Etymology

From Latin: 'Ratibida' is the genus name; 'tagetes' references the marigold genus Tagetes, due to resemblance.
