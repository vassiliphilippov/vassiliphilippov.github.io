meerkat

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪrˌkæt / (AmE), / ˈmɪə.kæt / (BrE)
a small, social mammal of southern Africa, part of the mongoose family, with a slender body and a pointed face
small, social southern African mammal

Examples

A group of meerkats stood upright, scanning the savannah for predators.
The wildlife documentary followed a family of meerkats living in the Kalahari Desert.
Children watched the meerkat exhibit at the zoo with fascination as they dug tunnels.

Etymology

From South African Dutch 'meerkat', originally from Dutch 'meerkat' (guenon, a kind of monkey).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪrˌkæt / (AmE), / ˈmɪə.kæt / (BrE)
a small, slender mammal of southern Africa, resembling a mongoose, with a pointed face, standing on its hind legs and living in social groups.
small mongoose-like mammal of southern Africa

Examples

The meerkat stood upright, scanning the savanna for any sign of danger.
At the zoo, I watched a family of meerkats darting in and out of their burrow.
A meerkat uses its sharp eyesight to spot predators from far away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mongoose - small carnivorous mammal, related to the meerkat

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'meerkat', from Dutch 'meerkat' meaning 'monkey', though the animal is not a monkey.
