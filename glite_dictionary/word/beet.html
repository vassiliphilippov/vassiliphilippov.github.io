beet

1. NOUN

/ bit / (AmE), / biːt / (BrE)
A plant with a swollen, edible root, often red or purple, that is cultivated as a food crop.
edible root vegetable plant

Examples

She added chopped beet to the salad for extra color and flavor.
Farmers harvest beet in autumn when the roots are at their largest.
The field was filled with rows of red beet ready for picking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beetroot - the root of the beet plant, used as food
  • Synonym: sugar beet - variety of beet grown for its sugar-rich root

Etymology

From Old English *bete*, from Latin *beta* (beet).

2. NOUN

/ bit / (AmE), / biːt / (BrE)
a plant with a round red root that is eaten as a vegetable, or the root itself
round red root vegetable

Examples

I added roasted beet to the salad for extra color and flavor.
Farmers harvest the beet crop in the late summer months.
She prefers her beet sliced thinly and served with goat cheese.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beetroot - the root of the beet plant, especially as used in British English

Etymology

From Old English 'bēte', from Latin 'beta'.
