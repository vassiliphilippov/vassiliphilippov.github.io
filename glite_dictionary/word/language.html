language

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the human ability to acquire and use complex systems of communication, especially spoken or written forms
human faculty for communication

Examples

The development of language is a key factor in human evolution.
Children naturally acquire language through interaction with others.
Researchers study the origins of language to understand how communication began.

Synset Examples

language sets homo sapiens apart from all other animals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speech - the expression of or the ability to express thoughts and feelings by articulate sounds

Etymology

From Latin 'lingua' (tongue, language), through Old French 'langage'.

WordNet ID: oewn-05658665-n

Examples

5. NOUN

/ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a system of communication used by a particular community or country, consisting of spoken or written words arranged according to grammatical rules
system of spoken or written communication

Examples

Chinese is the most widely spoken language in the world.
Learning a new language can open doors to different cultures and opportunities.
English is the official language of many countries across the globe.

Synset Examples

  • Degree Lower: lingo - a foreign language or local dialect
  • Degree Lower: speech - the act or process of speaking as a means of communication
