Volvaria

1. NOUN

/ vɑlˈvɛriə / (AmE), / vɒlˈvɛəriə / (BrE)
A genus of agaric fungi characterized by pink spores and a prominent volva (a cup-shaped structure at the base of the stem).
genus of agaric fungi with a distinct volva

Examples

Several species in the genus Volvaria are distinguished by their pink-colored spores.
The characteristic volva at the base helps mycologists identify Volvaria mushrooms in the field.
Recent studies have examined the ecological role of Volvaria species in forest habitats.

Etymology

From Latin 'volva' (covering or sheath), referring to the cup at the base of the mushroom.

2. NOUN

/ vɑlˈvɛriə / (AmE), / vɒlˈvɛəriə / (BrE)
A genus of agaric fungi characterized by pink spores and a distinct cup-like structure (volva) at the base of the stem.
genus of fungi with pink spores and volva

Examples

The Volvaria genus is easily identified by the presence of a volva around the base of the mushroom stalk.
Researchers collected several specimens from the Volvaria genus for further microscopic study.
Many field guides include illustrations of Volvaria to help differentiate it from other similar fungi.

Etymology

From Latin 'volva,' meaning 'wrapper' or 'covering,' referencing the volva structure.
