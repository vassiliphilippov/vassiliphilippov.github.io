viscera

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪsərə / (AmE), / ˈvɪsərə / (BrE)
the internal organs of the body collectively, especially those within the abdominal cavity
internal organs (esp. abdominal)
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully examined the patient's viscera for signs of disease.
Autopsies often involve a detailed inspection of the viscera.
Certain parasites can infect the viscera, leading to serious health problems.

Synset Examples

‘viscera’ is the plural form of ‘viscus’

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: viscus - a single internal organ, especially in the abdomen

Etymology

From Latin 'viscera', plural of 'viscus' (an internal organ).
