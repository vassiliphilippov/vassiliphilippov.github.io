irrelevant

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈrɛləvənt / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛlɪvənt / (BrE)
Not connected with or not important to what is being discussed or considered.
not connected with the subject

Examples

The lawyer objected to the question, claiming it was irrelevant to the case.
Try to avoid making irrelevant remarks during the meeting.
Most of the information you gave me was irrelevant to my research.

Synset Examples

an irrelevant comment
irrelevant allegations

Related Terms

  • Antonym: relevant - closely connected with the subject at issue

Etymology

From Latin 'relevare' (to raise) with the prefix 'in-' meaning 'not', via French.
