selva

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛlvə / (AmE), / ˈsɛlvə / (BrE)
A dense forest or rainforest, especially in a tropical area such as the Amazon basin.
tropical rainforest, especially in South America

Examples

The Amazon selva is one of the most biodiverse regions on Earth.
Scientists have discovered hundreds of new species in the selva.
The expedition trekked for days through thick selva before reaching the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rainforest - A luxuriant, dense forest found typically in tropical areas with consistently heavy rainfall

Etymology

From Spanish 'selva' (forest, jungle), from Latin 'silva' (forest, wood).
