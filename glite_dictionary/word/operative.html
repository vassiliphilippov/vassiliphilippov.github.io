operative

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑːpəˌreɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈɒpərətɪv / (BrE)
relating to or involving surgical procedures or treatment by surgery, as opposed to non-surgical medicine.
relating to surgical procedures
medical

Examples

The surgeon discussed the operative risks associated with the procedure.
Her condition required operative intervention, as medication alone was insufficient.
Advances in operative techniques have reduced recovery time for patients.

Synset Examples

a surgical appendix
a surgical procedure
operative dentistry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surgical - relating to or used in surgery

Etymology

From Latin operativus (creative, effective); from operari (to work).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01172738-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑp(ə)rətɪv / (AmE), / ˈɒp(ə)rətɪv / (BrE)
a person, especially a detective or agent, employed to gather information and assist in criminal investigations
private detective or agent

Examples

The agency sent an experienced operative to follow the suspect.
As a government operative, she worked undercover in several high-profile cases.
Private detectives and other operatives assisted the police during the investigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detective - a person who investigates crimes and gathers information
  • Synonym: spy - a person who secretly collects and reports information about others

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'operativus' (having the power to produce effect), from Latin 'operari' (to work).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10496256-n
Synset POS: n
