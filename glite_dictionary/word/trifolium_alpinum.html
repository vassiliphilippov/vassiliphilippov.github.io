Trifolium alpinum

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈfoʊliəm ælˈpaɪnəm / (AmE), / trɪˈfəʊlɪəm ælˈpiːnəm / (BrE)
A species of clover native to European mountain regions, known for its fragrant, usually pink flowers.
species of mountain clover

Examples

The bright pink flowers of Trifolium alpinum add color to the alpine meadows in summer.
Botanists often study Trifolium alpinum for its adaptability to harsh mountain climates.
The fragrance of Trifolium alpinum attracts various pollinators high in the European Alps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clover - a plant of the genus Trifolium with three-lobed leaves, often with purple, pink, or white flowers

Etymology

From Latin, with 'trifolium' meaning 'three-leaved' and 'alpinum' referring to mountains or alpine regions.
