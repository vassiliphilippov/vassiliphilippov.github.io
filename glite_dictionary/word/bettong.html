bettong

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛt.ɑŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɛt.ɒŋ / (BrE)
a small Australian marsupial with a short nose, related to the kangaroo and sometimes called a rat kangaroo
a small short-nosed Australian marsupial

Examples

The bettong is an endangered species native to Australia.
Researchers released several bettongs into the protected reserve to help restore the population.
A bettong can hop quickly on its hind legs to escape predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rat kangaroo - any small, hopping Australian marsupial of the family Potoroidae

Etymology

From Dharug (Aboriginal Australian language) 'badang', referring to the animal.
