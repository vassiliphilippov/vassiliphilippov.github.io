myasthenia

1. NOUN

/ maɪ.əsˈθi.ni.ə / (AmE), / maɪ.æsˈθiː.nɪ.ə / (BrE)
A chronic disease characterized by muscular weakness and fatigue, especially in the face and neck, due to a defect in neuromuscular transmission.
muscular weakness disorder
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with myasthenia after experiencing persistent muscle fatigue.
Researchers are studying new treatments for myasthenia to improve patients' quality of life.
Symptoms of myasthenia often worsen with activity and improve after periods of rest.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: myasthenia gravis - severe form of myasthenia with pronounced muscle weakness

Etymology

From Greek 'myastheneia', from 'mys' (muscle) + 'astheneia' (weakness).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.əsˈθiː.ni.ə / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.asˈθiː.nɪ.ə / (BrE)
A condition characterized by muscular weakness, especially one of medical origin.
muscular weakness condition
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with myasthenia after experiencing persistent fatigue in her limbs.
Chronic myasthenia can make everyday tasks challenging for those affected.
Symptoms of myasthenia vary in severity from person to person.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muscle weakness - a general lack of muscle strength
  • Degree Higher: myasthenia gravis - chronic disease causing severe muscle weakness

Etymology

From Greek 'mys' (muscle) and 'astheneia' (weakness).
