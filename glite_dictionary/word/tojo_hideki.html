Tojo Hideki

1. PROPN

/ toʊˈdʒoʊ hɪˈdeɪki / (AmE), / təʊˈdʒəʊ hɪˈdeɪki / (BrE)
(1884–1948) Japanese army officer and Prime Minister during World War II, who authorized the attack on Pearl Harbor and exercised dictatorial control over Japan; executed as a war criminal after the war.
Japanese WWII leader and war criminal

Examples

Tojo Hideki played a key role in shaping Japan's military strategy during World War II.
The trial and execution of Tojo Hideki marked a turning point in international war crime prosecutions.
Many historians debate the extent of Tojo Hideki's responsibility for Japan's involvement in the conflict.
