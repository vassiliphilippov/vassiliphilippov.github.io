psephology

1. NOUN

/ siːˈfɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / siːˈfɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of elections and voting patterns, especially through statistical analysis and opinion polls
study of elections and voting patterns

Examples

Dr. Smith is a leading expert in psephology and has published several books on voting patterns.
The rise of opinion polling has significantly advanced the field of psephology.
Many television networks consult specialists in psephology for election night analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: political science - the systematic study of politics and government

Etymology

From Greek psēphos (pebble, used for voting) + -logy (study of).
