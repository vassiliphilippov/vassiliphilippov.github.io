discredit

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈkrɛdɪt / (AmE), / dɪsˈkrɛdɪt / (BrE)
Loss or lack of respect, honor, or reputation; the state of being regarded with disapproval or suspicion.
loss of respect or honor

Examples

His dishonest actions brought discredit to the entire organization.
The team's repeated losses have put them in a state of discredit within the league.
She worked hard to restore her family's reputation after years of discredit.

Synset Examples

your actions will bring discredit to your name
because of the scandal, the school has fallen into disrepute

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disrepute - the state of being held in low esteem by the public
  • Antonym: honor - high respect or esteem

Etymology

From Latin 'discredere' (to disbelieve), from 'dis-' (not) + 'credere' (to believe).

2. VERB

/ dɪsˈkrɛdɪt / (AmE), / dɪsˈkrɛdɪt / (BrE)
to refuse to accept as true or valid; to consider something as false or untrustworthy
reject as false or untrustworthy

Examples

Many scientists discredit the theory because there is no supporting evidence.
The panel chose to discredit the witness's testimony after finding inconsistencies.
She tried to discredit the rumor, insisting it was completely false.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to believe - to accept as true

Etymology

From Latin discredere (to disbelieve), from dis- (not) + credere (to believe).

3. VERB

/ dɪsˈkrɛdɪt / (AmE), / dɪsˈkrɛdɪt / (BrE)
to harm the reputation of someone or something and cause people to stop believing in them
damage the reputation of

Examples

The scandal threatened to discredit several senior officials.
False information was spread to discredit the scientist's work.
He tried to discredit his rival by revealing old mistakes.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to defame - to damage the reputation of; slander or libel
  • Antonym: to vindicate - to clear someone of blame or suspicion

Etymology

From Latin dis- (expressing reversal) + creditum (belief, loan), from credere (to believe).

to cause people to stop trusting or believing in someone or something; to damage the reputation of
