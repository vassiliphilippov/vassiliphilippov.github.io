Prosauropoda

1. NOUN

/ proʊˌsɔːrəˈpoʊdə / (AmE), / prəʊˌsɔːrəˈpəʊdə / (BrE)
an extinct suborder of early herbivorous dinosaurs that were precursors to the sauropods, known from the Late Triassic and Early Jurassic periods
early group of herbivorous dinosaurs

Examples

The fossils of Prosauropoda provide valuable insight into the evolution of early dinosaurs.
Paleontologists have discovered new specimens of Prosauropoda in South America.
Unlike their larger descendants, Prosauropoda were generally smaller and less massive.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Sauropoda - a suborder of large, long-necked, herbivorous dinosaurs

Etymology

From New Latin: pro- (before) + Sauropoda (the later dinosaur group).

2. NOUN

/ proʊˌsɔːrəˈpoʊdə / (AmE), / prəʊˌsɔːrəˈpəʊdə / (BrE)
an extinct suborder of early herbivorous dinosaurs, among the earliest known, that preceded the sauropods
early suborder of herbivorous dinosaurs

Examples

Fossils of Prosauropoda have been discovered on four continents.
Many paleontologists believe that Prosauropoda were the evolutionary precursors to the giant sauropods.
The Prosauropoda lived during the Late Triassic and Early Jurassic periods.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Sauropoda - suborder of large, long-necked herbivorous dinosaurs descended from prosauropods

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'pro-' (before) and 'Sauropoda' (lizard-footed), indicating their earlier evolutionary placement.
