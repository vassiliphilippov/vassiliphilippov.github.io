leftfield

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛftˌfild / (AmE), / ˈlɛftˌfiːld / (BrE)
The area of the outfield in baseball that is to the left when facing from home plate, or the defensive position covering this area.
outfield area/position in baseball

Examples

The coach moved him to leftfield to take advantage of his strong throwing arm.
A high fly ball sailed into leftfield, where it was caught for the final out.
She has played leftfield for most of her softball career.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rightfield - the area of the outfield to the right from home plate in baseball
  • Degree Higher: centerfield - the area of the outfield directly ahead from home plate in baseball
