Micromeria juliana

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkroʊˈmɪəriə dʒuːliˈænə / (AmE), / ˌmɪkrəˈmɪərɪə dʒuːlɪˈɑːnə / (BrE)
A dwarf aromatic shrub native to Mediterranean regions, belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae).
aromatic Mediterranean shrub

Examples

Micromeria juliana grows in rocky areas along the Mediterranean coast.
Botanists collected samples of Micromeria juliana for a study of aromatic plants.
The tiny leaves of Micromeria juliana emit a pleasant scent when crushed.

Etymology

From the genus name Micromeria (Greek mikros 'small' + meros 'part') and the species name juliana, honoring an individual.
