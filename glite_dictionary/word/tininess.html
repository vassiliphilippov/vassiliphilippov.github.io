tininess

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪni.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtaɪni.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being extremely small in size
the state of being very small

Examples

The tininess of the insect made it hard to spot on the leaf.
She marveled at the tininess of the newborn kitten's paws.
Despite its tininess, the rare jewel was remarkably valuable.

Synset Examples

hence the minuteness of detail in the painting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smallness - the quality of being small
  • Antonym: bigness - the quality of being large

Etymology

From 'tiny' + '-ness', meaning the state of being tiny.
