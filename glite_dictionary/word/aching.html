1. ADJ

/ ˈeɪ.kɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.kɪŋ / (BrE)
causing a continuous, dull pain; experiencing or relating to a persistent pain.
causing dull, continuous pain

Examples

She put her hand on her aching back after lifting the heavy box.
After running the marathon, his legs were aching all night.
I couldn't sleep because of my aching tooth.

Synset Examples

my aching head
her old achy joints

Related Terms

  • Synonym: painful - causing pain
  • Synonym: sore - feeling pain or discomfort

Etymology

Present participle of 'ache', from Old English 'acan' (to ache).

2. NOUN

/ ˈeɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈeɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
a dull, persistent physical pain that is usually not severe but lingers over time
dull, persistent pain

Examples

After running the marathon, he felt an aching in his legs that lasted for days.
The constant aching in her back made it hard for her to sleep.
She complained of a dull aching behind her eyes whenever she stared at the screen too long.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pain - physical suffering or discomfort caused by illness or injury
  • Antonym: sharp pain - sudden, intense pain

Etymology

From the verb 'ache', from Old English *acan* (to ache or pain).

Synset POS: n
