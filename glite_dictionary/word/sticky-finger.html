sticky-finger

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪki ˌfɪŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈstɪki ˌfɪŋɡə / (BrE)
a person who habitually steals; someone with a tendency to steal
person with a tendency to steal
informal

Examples

The sticky-finger kid was caught taking a toy from the shop.
Our office had a sticky-finger last month, and some cash went missing.
It’s wise to keep valuables out of reach if a sticky-finger is around.

Synset Examples

The sticky-finger kid was caught stealing cookies.
His sticky-finger habit landed him in trouble.
Watch out for that sticky-finger thief in the store.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thief - a person who steals

Etymology

From 'sticky' (adhering) + 'finger', referring humorously to hands that 'stick' to what they touch (i.e., take things illicitly).
