FCC

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛf.siːˈsiː / (AmE), / ˌɛf.siːˈsiː / (BrE)
the Federal Communications Commission; a U.S. government agency that regulates interstate and international communications by radio, television, wire, cable, and satellite
U.S. government communications regulator
american

Examples

The FCC issued new rules affecting broadband internet providers last month.
Radio stations must obtain licenses from the FCC to operate legally in the United States.
The FCC fined the company for violating broadcast regulations.

Etymology

Acronym of Federal Communications Commission.
