delicacy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛl.ɪ.kəsi / (AmE), / ˈdɛl.ɪ.kəsi / (BrE)
graceful lightness or gentleness in movement or manner
graceful lightness or gentleness

Examples

Her ballet performance was marked by an extraordinary delicacy of movement.
He handled the fragile sculpture with a delicacy that impressed everyone.
The delicacy of her gestures conveyed the emotion of the scene perfectly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grace - elegance and beauty of movement or manner
  • Antonym: coarseness - lack of refinement or grace in movement or manner

Etymology

From Latin delicatia (pleasure, delight), via Old French delicacie.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛlɪkəsi / (AmE), / ˈdɛlɪkəsi / (BrE)
the ability to handle a situation with subtle skill and sensitivity
subtle skill in handling a situation

Examples

She handled the negotiations with great delicacy to avoid offending either party.
It takes real delicacy to discuss such sensitive topics without causing embarrassment.
The counselor approached the troubled student with admirable delicacy and care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tact - skill and sensitivity in dealing with others or with difficult issues
  • Antonym: insensitivity - lack of concern for the feelings of others; not subtle or tactful

Etymology

From Latin delicatus (giving pleasure, charming, pleasing); via Old French délicatesse.
