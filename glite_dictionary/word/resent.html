to resent

1. VERB

/ rɪˈzɛnt / (AmE), / rɪˈzɛnt / (BrE)
to feel bitter, angry, or indignant about something regarded as unfair or wrong
feel bitterness or indignation

Examples

She resents being paid less than her co-workers.
Many teenagers resent being told what to do by their parents.
He clearly resented the criticism and refused to discuss it further.

Synset Examples

She resents being paid less than her co-workers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to begrudge - to envy or feel resentment at the possession or enjoyment of
  • Antonym: to cherish - to hold dear; to feel love or affection for

Etymology

From French 'ressentir' (to feel deeply), from Latin 'resentire' (to feel again).

2. VERB

/ rɪˈzɛnt / (AmE), / rɪˈzɛnt / (BrE)
to feel bitterness or indignation about something, often because of perceived unfairness or slight
feel bitterness or indignation

Examples

She couldn't help but resent her colleague's constant criticism during meetings.
Many people resent being told what to do without being asked for their opinions.
He secretly resented the extra work assigned to him at the last minute.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to begrudge - to envy or resent the possession or enjoyment of something

Etymology

From French 'ressentir' (to feel deeply), from Latin 'resentire', from 're-' (again) + 'sentire' (to feel).
