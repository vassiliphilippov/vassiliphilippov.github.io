to outmaneuver

1. VERB

/ ˌaʊt.məˈnu.vɚ / (AmE), / ˌaʊt.məˈnjuː.və / (BrE)
to defeat or gain an advantage over someone by using greater skill or cleverness in planning or action
gain advantage by clever action

Examples

Despite being outnumbered, the general managed to outmaneuver the opposing army.
She tried to outmaneuver her colleague to get the promotion, but was unsuccessful.
In negotiations, the company outmaneuvered its competitors by offering a better deal.

Synset Examples

The English troops outmaneuvered the Germans
My new supervisor knows how to outmaneuver the boss in most situations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outwit - to gain an advantage over someone by being more clever
  • Synonym: to outsmart - to defeat or surpass by being more intelligent or cunning
  • Synonym: to outfox - to beat by cleverness or cunning

Etymology

From 'out-' (beyond) + 'maneuver', from French 'manœuvre'.
