bottom-dwelling

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɑːtəm ˌdwɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəm ˌdwɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a fish or marine animal) living on or near the bottom of a body of water.
living at the bottom of a body of water

Examples

Many species of bottom-dwelling fish have adapted to survive in low-light conditions.
The bottom-dwelling crabs burrow into the sand to hide from predators.
Scientists discovered several new bottom-dwelling organisms during the deep-sea expedition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benthic - relating to the bottom of a sea or lake
