nontranslational

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˌtrænzləˈʃɪnəl / (AmE), / nɒnˌtrænzləˈʃɪnəl / (BrE)
relating to movement that is not purely in one direction or involves rotation as well as translation
not involving purely straight-line movement

Examples

The robot's nontranslational movements allowed it to rotate and maneuver in tight spaces.
Engineers analyzed the nontranslational components of the vehicle's motion to improve stability.
In physics, nontranslational motion often refers to spinning or rotating rather than moving straight ahead.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: translational - relating to movement in a straight line, without rotation

Etymology

From non- (not) + translational (from Latin translatio 'carrying across')
