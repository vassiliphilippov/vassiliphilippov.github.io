Maianthemum

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈænθəməm / (AmE), / maɪˈænθɪməm / (BrE)
A genus of woodland plants in the family Asparagaceae, commonly known as false lily of the valley, with small white flowers and broad leaves.
genus of woodland plants (false lily of the valley)

Examples

The moist forest floor was carpeted with Maianthemum in full bloom.
Many gardeners appreciate Maianthemum for its shade tolerance and delicate flowers.
Researchers have observed pollinators visiting Maianthemum during the late spring months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: false lily of the valley - common name for plants in the genus Maianthemum

Etymology

From Greek 'maia' (May) and 'anthemon' (flower), referring to flowering time.

2. NOUN

/ maɪˈænθəməm / (AmE), / maɪˈænθɪməm / (BrE)
A genus of perennial flowering plants in the asparagus family, commonly known as false lily of the valley.
genus of flowering plants (false lily of the valley)

Examples

The forest floor was covered with Maianthemum in full bloom during late spring.
Botanists often study Maianthemum species for their unique flowering patterns.
She identified the delicate white flowers as belonging to the genus Maianthemum.

Etymology

From Greek 'maios' (May) and 'anthemon' (flower), referring to the plant's blooming season.
