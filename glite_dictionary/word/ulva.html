Ulva

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌlvə / (AmE), / ˈʌlvə / (BrE)
A genus of green seaweed with thin, sheet-like thalli, commonly known as sea lettuce.
genus of green seaweed (sea lettuce)

Examples

The shoreline was covered with bright green Ulva after the spring tides.
Marine biologists collected samples of Ulva for their research on coastal ecosystems.
In some cuisines, Ulva is used as an ingredient in salads and soups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea lettuce - common name for Ulva, a green seaweed

Etymology

From Latin 'ulva' meaning seaweed or sedge.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌlvə / (AmE), / ˈʌlvə / (BrE)
a genus of green seaweed in the family Ulvaceae, characterized by thin, flat thalli, commonly known as sea lettuce
a genus of green seaweed (sea lettuce)

Examples

Ulva grows in shallow, coastal waters around the world and is commonly eaten as sea lettuce.
Researchers are studying how Ulva can be used to improve water quality and serve as food.
Some species in the genus Ulva can cause green tides when they proliferate rapidly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea lettuce - a common name for green seaweed of the genus Ulva

Etymology

From Latin 'ulva', meaning 'marsh grass' or 'seaweed'.
