I Maccabees

1. PROPN

/ ˌfaɪrst ˈmækəˌbiːz / (AmE), / ˌfɜːst ˈmækəˌbiːz / (BrE)
an Apocryphal book recounting the revolt of the Jews led by Judas Maccabaeus against the Seleucid Empire under Antiochus IV Epiphanes
book describing Jewish revolt against Seleucids

Examples

Students in religious studies often analyze I Maccabees for its historical context and narrative style.
The story of Judas Maccabaeus's leadership is chronicled in I Maccabees.
Many Christian and Jewish traditions recognize I Maccabees as an important historical document.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: II Maccabees - a later Apocryphal book covering similar events with theological interpretation

Etymology

From Latin 'Machabaeorum liber primus', from Greek 'Makkabaiōn', referring to the Maccabees, a Jewish rebel group.
