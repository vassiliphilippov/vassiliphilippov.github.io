translunar

1. ADJ

/ trænˈsluːnɚ / (AmE), / trænˈsluːnə / (BrE)
(literary) Unworldly or ethereal; existing beyond the earthly or material world.
unworldly or ethereal
archaic

Examples

The poet described her as a translunar spirit untouched by earthly concerns.
His thoughts often wandered into translunar realms far from everyday reality.
The music had a translunar quality that made the audience feel as if they were dreaming.

Synset Examples

high translunary dreams

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethereal - extremely delicate and light in a way that seems not of this world
  • Antonym: earthly - relating to the material world as opposed to the spiritual or ethereal

Etymology

From Latin trans- (across, beyond) + luna (moon).

2. ADJ

/ trænzˈluːnɚ / (AmE), / trænzˈluːnə / (BrE)
Situated or taking place beyond the moon or its orbit around the earth.
beyond the moon or its orbit

Examples

Scientists are studying the possibility of launching a translunar mission in the next decade.
The telescope captured images of a rare translunar comet passing beyond the moon's orbit.
Her science fiction novel is set on a distant translunar outpost far from Earth.

Synset Examples

who can imagine a translunary visitor in Times Square?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cislunar - situated between the Earth and the Moon or within the Moon's orbit

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'trans-' (across, beyond) and 'luna' (moon).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02768371-a
Synset POS: a
