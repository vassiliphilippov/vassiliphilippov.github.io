aegis

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːdʒɪs / (AmE), / ˈiːdʒɪs / (BrE)
support, protection, or guidance, especially as provided by an organization or authority
protection or sponsorship

Examples

The charity operates under the aegis of the United Nations.
The event was organized with the aegis of several international agencies.
The research program is conducted under the aegis of the Ministry of Health.

Synset Examples

the tournament was held under the auspices of the city council

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auspices - protection or backing, often by an organization

Etymology

From Greek aigis (the shield of Zeus or Athena, symbol of protection).

2. NOUN

/ ˈiːdʒɪs / (AmE), / ˈiːdʒɪs / (BrE)
A piece of armor covering the chest; the front part of a cuirass, used for protection.
armor plate protecting the chest

Examples

In the museum, the ancient warrior's aegis gleamed under the display lights.
The hero fastened his aegis as he prepared for battle.
Legends describe the aegis as both a powerful shield and a symbol of divine protection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuirass - a piece of armor covering the body from neck to waist, front and back
  • Synonym: breastplate - a piece of armor covering the chest

Etymology

From Greek aigis (goatskin, shield of Zeus).

Synset POS: n
