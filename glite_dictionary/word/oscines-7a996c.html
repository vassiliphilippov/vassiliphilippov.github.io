Oscines

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːsaɪnz / (AmE), / ˈɒsaɪnz / (BrE)
A suborder of birds known as the typical songbirds, distinguished by their complex vocal organs.
typical songbird suborder

Examples

There are thousands of species in the Oscines suborder, making it the largest group of songbirds.
The Oscines are noted for their sophisticated vocal abilities compared to other birds.
In ornithology class, we studied the anatomical differences between Oscines and Suboscines.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Passerines - order of perching birds, including oscines
  • Degree Lower: Suboscines - suborder of perching birds with simpler songs

Etymology

From Latin oscinis, referring to birds of omen.
