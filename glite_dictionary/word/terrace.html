terrace

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛr.əs / (AmE), / ˈtɛr.əs / (BrE)
a flat, level area of earth formed on a slope, often created naturally or for farming, with steep slopes above and below it
a flat, level area of land on a slope

Examples

The rice was cultivated on narrow terraces carved into the mountainside.
Ancient river terraces can reveal information about past climates and water levels.
Workers constructed terraces to prevent soil erosion on the steep hillside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plateau - an extensive area of relatively flat high ground

Etymology

From Old French *terrasse*, from Old Provençal *terrassa*, from Vulgar Latin *terracia* (of earth), from Latin *terra* (earth).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛr.əs / (AmE), / ˈtɛr.əs / (BrE)
a level, usually paved outdoor area next to a building, often used for sitting or relaxing
paved outdoor area by a building

Examples

The house features a large stone terrace perfect for entertaining guests.
After dinner, they relaxed with coffee on the shaded terrace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patio - a paved outdoor area adjoining a house
  • Antonym: balcony - a platform projecting from the wall of a building, above ground level
