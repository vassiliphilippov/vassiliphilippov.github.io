Flindersia australis

1. NOUN

/ flɪnˈdɜrziə ɔˈstreɪlɪs / (AmE), / flɪnˈdɜːzɪə ɒˈstreɪlɪs / (BrE)
a tall Australian tree with tough, hard wood valued for timber, especially in making staves
Australian timber tree species

Examples

The durable timber of the Flindersia australis is highly prized in furniture making.
Many old staves were crafted from Flindersia australis due to its strength.
The Flindersia australis towers above other trees in the Australian rainforests.

Etymology

Named after Matthew Flinders, English navigator and explorer; 'australis' is Latin for 'southern'.
