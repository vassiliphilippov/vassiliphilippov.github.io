Melospiza georgiana

1. NOUN

/ məˌlɑːˈspaɪzə dʒiːˈɔrdʒiənə / (AmE), / mɛləˌspaɪzə dʒiːˈɔːdʒɪənə / (BrE)
A species of North American sparrow, commonly known as the swamp sparrow, found in marshy areas.
North American swamp sparrow species

Examples

The Melospiza georgiana is known for its subtle plumage and sweet song in wetland habitats.
Researchers observed several Melospiza georgiana singing in the marsh early in the morning.
During migration, the Melospiza georgiana can be found along the edges of ponds and lakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swamp sparrow - Common name for Melospiza georgiana, a North American marshland sparrow

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus name Melospiza meaning 'song finch' and the species from Georgia (US state) where it was first identified.
