protocol

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊt̬əˌkɑl / (AmE), / ˈprəʊtəkɒl / (BrE)
the formal system of rules and procedures governing the conduct of diplomats and heads of state, especially regarding ceremonies and etiquette
diplomatic rules and etiquette

Examples

The ambassador followed strict protocol during the signing ceremony.
Breaking diplomatic protocol, the minister arrived late to the state banquet.
Official protocol required that guests be formally introduced before meeting the president.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: etiquette - the customary code of polite behavior in society or among members of a particular profession or group

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'protocollum' (first leaf of a document, official document), from Greek 'prōtokollon' (first sheet glued onto a manuscript).

2. NOUN

/ ˈproʊtəˌkɑl / (AmE), / ˈprəʊtəkɒl / (BrE)
a set of rules governing the format and transmission of data between electronic devices, especially in computing and telecommunications
rules for data transmission in computing

Examples

The HTTP protocol is used for transferring web pages over the Internet.
To connect the devices, both must support the same protocol.
Engineers are developing a more secure network protocol for encrypted communication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: standard - an accepted model or requirement used for comparison in measurements, quality, or specifications
  • Synonym: procedure - an established way of doing something, especially according to fixed rules

Etymology

From French 'protocole', from Medieval Latin 'protocollum' (first draft of a document).

3. NOUN

/ ˈproʊ.təˌkɑl / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.tə.kɒl / (BrE)
the official rules or system of rules governing affairs of state or diplomatic occasions, or a formal procedure in any context
official rules or formal procedures

Examples

All visitors to the laboratory must follow strict protocol to ensure safety.
In diplomatic meetings, protocol dictates where each official should sit.
The university has a detailed protocol for addressing academic misconduct.

Synset Examples

safety protocols
academic protocol

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procedure - an established or official way of doing something
  • Degree Lower: custom - a traditional and widely accepted way of behaving or doing something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'protocollum' (first leaf of a volume, official record), from Greek 'protokollon' (first glued sheet of a papyrus roll).
