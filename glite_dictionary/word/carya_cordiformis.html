Carya cordiformis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛr.i.ə kɔr.dəˈfɔr.mɪs / (AmE), / ˈkɛː.ri.ə kɔː.dɪˈfɔː.mɪs / (BrE)
A species of hickory tree native to eastern North America, with leaves composed of 7 or 9 leaflets and thin-shelled, very bitter nuts.
species of hickory tree with bitter nuts

Examples

The Carya cordiformis is easily recognized by its smooth gray bark and compound leaves.
Some animals avoid eating the bitter nuts produced by the Carya cordiformis.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Carya cordiformis throughout the eastern United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bitternut hickory - common name for Carya cordiformis
  • Degree Higher: hickory - general term for trees of the genus Carya

Etymology

From Latin: carya (nut tree) and cordiformis (heart-shaped), referring to the nut shape.
