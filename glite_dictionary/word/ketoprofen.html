ketoprofen

1. NOUN

/ kɛˈtoʊprəfɛn / (AmE), / ˌkiːtəˈprəʊfɛn / (BrE)
a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) used to reduce pain and inflammation
a type of pain-relieving medication (NSAID)
medical

Examples

My doctor prescribed ketoprofen to help manage my arthritis pain.
You should not take ketoprofen on an empty stomach, as it may cause irritation.
After her knee surgery, she relied on ketoprofen for several days to reduce swelling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ibuprofen - another common NSAID for pain and inflammation
  • Synonym: aspirin - an older NSAID used for pain and inflammation

Etymology

Formed from chemical roots: keto- (relating to a ketone group) and -profen (as in ibuprofen, from propionic acid derivative).
