footlights

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtˌlaɪts / (AmE), / ˈfʊt.laɪts / (BrE)
the row of lights at the front edge of a theater stage, used to illuminate the set and actors
lights at the front of a theater stage

Examples

The stage was bathed in the soft glow of the footlights.
She waited nervously behind the footlights before her big debut.
After the renovation, new footlights were installed to enhance the actors' visibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotlight - a powerful stage light directed onto a small area or actor

Etymology

From 'foot' + 'light', referring to their placement at actors' feet onstage.
