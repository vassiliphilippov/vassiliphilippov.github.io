unvoiced

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈvɔɪst / (AmE), / ʌnˈvɔɪst / (BrE)
(of a speech sound) produced without vibration of the vocal cords, as in the sounds ‘p’, ‘k’, and ‘s’.
(of a sound) without vocal cord vibration

Examples

The English letter 'p' is an unvoiced consonant.
Some students find it difficult to distinguish unvoiced sounds from voiced ones when learning English.
In phonetics, the difference between 's' and 'z' lies in whether the sound is voiced or unvoiced.

Synset Examples

unvoiced consonants such as ‘p’ and ‘k’ and ‘s’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voiceless - (of a speech sound) produced without vibration of the vocal cords
  • Antonym: voiced - (of a speech sound) produced with vibration of the vocal cords

Etymology

un- (not) + voiced (having a voice, from Latin vocem)

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈvɔɪst / (AmE), / ʌnˈvɔɪst / (BrE)
not expressed in words or not made explicit; left unsaid or unspoken.
not spoken or expressed

Examples

His disappointment was unvoiced, but it was clear in his expression.
There was an unvoiced agreement between them not to discuss the past.
The team's concerns remained unvoiced during the meeting.

Synset Examples

the unexpressed terms of the agreement
things left unsaid
some kind of unspoken agreement
his action is clear but his reason remains unstated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unspoken - not uttered aloud; not stated
  • Antonym: explicit - stated clearly and in detail

Etymology

Prefix 'un-' (not) + 'voiced' (expressed or spoken).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00945649-s
Synset POS: s
