raving

1. ADJ

/ ˈreɪ.vɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈreɪ.vɪŋ / (BrE)
completely irrational or wildly behaving; showing extreme and unreasonable behavior or speech.
behaving or speaking irrationally

Examples

He was acting like a raving maniac after hearing the news.
The suspect was described as a raving lunatic by the police chief.
Everyone thought she was raving when she started shouting nonsense in the street.

Synset Examples

a raving lunatic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delirious - in a state of wild excitement or ecstasy
  • Antonym: sane - having a healthy mind; reasonable and rational

Etymology

Present participle of 'rave', from Middle English raven (to wander, rage).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02084755-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈreɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈreɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
wild, irrational, or frenzied speech or statements, especially when spoken loudly or passionately
wild or frenzied speech

Examples

Her incoherent raving frightened everyone in the room.
The politician’s raving about conspiracies made headline news.
He sat alone, muttering raving to himself in the darkness.

Synset Examples

the raving of maniacs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rant - lengthy, passionate, or angry speech

Etymology

From Old French raver (to rage, be delirious).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07257853-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADV

/ ˈreɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈreɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
In an extremely wild, intense, or frenzied manner.
in an extremely wild manner

Examples

He stormed into the room, raving about the terrible service he had received.
The fans were raving after their team won the championship in overtime.
She was raving mad when she found out someone had scratched her car.

Synset Examples

raving mad

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wildly - in an uncontrolled or frantic manner
  • Antonym: calmly - in a peaceful or controlled manner

Etymology

Present participle of 'rave', meaning to speak or act wildly or irrationally.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00441365-r
Synset POS: r
