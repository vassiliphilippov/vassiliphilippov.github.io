rod

1. NOUN

/ rɑd / (AmE), / rɒd / (BrE)
a type of photoreceptor cell in the retina that is highly sensitive to dim light and allows vision in low-light conditions
light-sensitive cell in the eye
medical

Examples

The human retina contains more rods than cones, allowing us to see in low light.
People with damaged rods often have difficulty seeing at night.
Rods are essential for night vision but do not detect color.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cone - a photoreceptor cell in the retina responsible for color vision

4. NOUN

rod-shaped bacterium
medical

Examples

Staining techniques can help distinguish a rod from other types of bacteria, such as cocci.
E. coli is a commonly studied rod found in the intestines of humans and animals.
When viewed under a microscope, the rod appears as a straight, elongated cell.

Related Terms

5. NOUN

/ rɑd / (AmE), / rɒd / (BrE)
A unit of area, especially in land measurement, equal to a square with sides each one rod (about 5.5 yards or 5.03 meters) in length.
unit of area in land measurement
legal

Examples

The farm covers twenty rods of flat land next to the river.
Each plot was measured at five square rods according to the old survey records.
Under the terms of the lease, the tenant received ten rods of arable ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perch - another name for a square rod (unit of land area)
  • Synonym: pole - alternative term for a square rod in land measurement

Etymology

From Old English 'rodd' (rod, pole, stick), relating to measurement by a rod or pole.

6. NOUN

/ rɑd / (AmE), / rɒd / (BrE)
a former unit of length equal to 16.5 feet (approximately 5.03 meters), used especially in surveying land.
unit of length equal to 16.5 feet

Examples

The land was measured as ten rods wide by twenty rods long.
A fence post was placed every rod along the property line.
Maps from the 1800s sometimes use the rod as a unit of distance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pole - unit of length equal to 16.5 feet; synonym for 'rod' as a measure
  • Synonym: perch - unit of length equal to 16.5 feet; another synonym for 'rod' as a measure

Etymology

From Old English 'rodd' (rod, pole, stick).
