Dicranaceae

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌkreɪnəˈsiː.i / (AmE), / dɪˌkranəˈsiː.i / (BrE)
a family of mosses characterized by costate leaves and long-stalked capsules with a cleft peristome
a family of mosses in botany
medical

Examples

The Dicranaceae family includes several mosses commonly found in temperate forests.
Researchers studied the reproduction methods of plants within the Dicranaceae.
Their field guide described the distinguishing features of Dicranaceae, such as the structure of their capsules.

Etymology

From New Latin Dicranum (a genus of mosses) + -aceae (standard suffix for plant family names).

2. NOUN

/ daɪˌkreɪnəˈsiː / (AmE), / dɪˌkrænəˈsiː / (BrE)
a family of mosses characterized by costate leaves and long-stalked capsules with a cleft peristome
family of mosses with costate leaves

Examples

The Dicranaceae are found mostly in moist, shaded environments around the world.
Botanists study the structural features that distinguish Dicranaceae from other moss families.
Certain members of the Dicranaceae are used as bioindicators of air pollution in forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moss - small, nonvascular, green plants in the division Bryophyta

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Dicranum (from Greek dikranon, meaning 'two-headed fork').
