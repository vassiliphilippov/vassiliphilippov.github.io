Pectinidae

1. NOUN

/ pɛkˈtɪnɪdiː / (AmE), / pɛkˈtɪnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine bivalve molluscs commonly known as scallops, characterized by their fan-shaped shells and ability to swim by rapidly opening and closing their shells.
family of scallops (marine bivalves)

Examples

The Pectinidae family includes many species known for their distinctive fan-shaped shells.
Researchers are studying the behavior and habitat of Pectinidae along the coastal shelf.
Several commercial fisheries harvest members of the Pectinidae for food around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scallop - common name for members of the Pectinidae family
  • Degree Lower: bivalve - class of marine and freshwater molluscs with hinged shells

Etymology

From Latin *pecten* (a comb), referencing the shell's shape.
