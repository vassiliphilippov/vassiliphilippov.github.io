sedimentation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛdəˌmɛnˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛdɪmɛnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which solid particles settle and accumulate at the bottom of a liquid
settling of particles in a liquid

Examples

The river delta was formed over thousands of years by the gradual sedimentation of silt and sand.
Industrial wastewater treatment often relies on sedimentation to remove suspended solids from the water.
The scientist studied the rate of sedimentation in lake beds to understand environmental changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deposition - the act of depositing, especially the laying down of matter by a natural process

Etymology

From French 'sédimentation', based on 'sédiment' (sediment), from Latin 'sedimentum' (settling, residue).
