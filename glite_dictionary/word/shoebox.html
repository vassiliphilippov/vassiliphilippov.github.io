shoebox

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃuˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈʃuːˌbɒks / (BrE)
an oblong rectangular box, usually made of cardboard, designed to hold a pair of shoes
a box for holding shoes

Examples

She kept all her old letters in a shoebox under her bed.
The new sneakers came in a sturdy shoebox with a colorful logo.
He used a shoebox to make a simple diorama for his school project.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃuˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈʃuːˌbɒks / (BrE)
A building, room, or compartment that is rectangular in shape and often considered small or cramped, resembling the shape of a box for shoes.
rectangular, often cramped, structure or room

Examples

The architect designed yet another shoebox apartment building that lacked any distinctive character.
She lives in a tiny shoebox in the city, but she loves the location.
He complained that his office was a cramped shoebox with barely enough space for a desk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cubicle - a small, partitioned space or compartment, often for work

Etymology

Formed by analogy to a box for shoes; 'shoe' + 'box'.
