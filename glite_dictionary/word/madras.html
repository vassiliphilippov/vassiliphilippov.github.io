madras

1. NOUN

/ məˈdræs / (AmE), / ˈmædrəs / (BrE)
A lightweight cotton fabric, typically patterned with colorful checks or stripes, originally made in Madras (now Chennai), India.
light patterned cotton fabric

Examples

He wore a crisp madras shirt to the summer picnic.
The tailor recommended madras for its breathability and vibrant colors.
Patterns of madras became very popular in preppy fashion circles.

Etymology

Named after Madras, former name of Chennai, India, where the fabric originated.
