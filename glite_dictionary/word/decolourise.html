to decolourise

1. VERB

/ dɪˈkʌlɚˌaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈkʌləˌaɪz / (BrE)
to remove or cause to lose color; to make or become lighter or white
remove or lose color

Examples

They used a solution to decolourise the stained fabric before re-dyeing it.
Continuous exposure to sunlight can decolourise brightly painted surfaces over time.
The chemist needed to decolourise the sample for accurate spectroscopic analysis.

Synset Examples

The sun bleached the red shirt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bleach - to make something white or lighter in color, especially by chemical action

Etymology

From de- (removal) + colour + -ise (forming verbs)
