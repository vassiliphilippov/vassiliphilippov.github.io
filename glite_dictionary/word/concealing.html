concealing

1. ADJ

/ kənˈsilɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈsiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
keeping something from being seen, noticed, or known; hiding or covering up
hiding or covering up

Examples

She wore a concealing dress that covered her scar.
The concealing clouds hid the moon from view.
Heavy, concealing makeup was used for the role.

Synset Examples

the concealing darkness
concealing curtains prevented discovery

Related Terms

  • Antonym: revealing - showing more than usual; the opposite of concealing, especially for clothing or information
  • Synonym: hiding - keeping out of sight; similar to concealing

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'conceal', from Latin 'concelare' (to hide).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈsilɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈsiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of keeping something hidden or secret
the act of keeping secret or hidden

Examples

Her concealing of the truth eventually came to light in the investigation.
The concealing of evidence is a serious legal offense.
He became skilled in the art of concealing his true intentions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hiding - the act of concealing something
  • Antonym: revealing - the act of making something known or visible

Etymology

Gerund of 'conceal', from Old French *conceler*, from Latin *concelare* (to hide).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
