poetess

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊ.ə.tɛs / (AmE), / ˈpəʊ.ɪ.tɛs / (BrE)
a female poet; a woman who writes poetry
female poet
archaic

Examples

The Victorian poetess was celebrated for her powerful and emotional verses.
She aspired to be a poetess like her literary idols.
Although the term poetess is rare today, it appears often in nineteenth-century literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poet - a writer of poems

Etymology

Formed from 'poet' + '-ess' (a suffix denoting feminine gender, from Latin -issa via French -esse).
