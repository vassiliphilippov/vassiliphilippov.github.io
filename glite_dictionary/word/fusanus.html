Fusanus

1. NOUN

/ fuˈseɪnəs / (AmE), / fjʊˈseɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of trees native to Australia and Southeast Asia, commonly known as quandongs, with edible fruit.
a genus of quandong trees

Examples

The Fusanus trees produce bright red fruits that are popular in native Australian cuisine.
Many species of Fusanus are valued for their edible seeds and hardwood.
Researchers are studying Fusanus for its potential use in sustainable agriculture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quandong - an edible fruit or tree in the genus Fusanus

Etymology

From Latinized form of an Australian Aboriginal name for the plant.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfuː sən ʌs / (AmE), / ˈfjuː sən ʌs / (BrE)
a genus of trees and shrubs in the sandalwood family, including the quandong trees, native to Australasia and the Pacific
a genus of quandong trees and shrubs

Examples

The Fusanus genus is notable for producing the edible quandong fruit in Australia.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the Fusanus genus.
The wood of Fusanus trees is sometimes used for carving and traditional crafts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quandong - an edible fruit-bearing tree native to Australia

Etymology

From Latinized form of an Australian Aboriginal word, used in botanical nomenclature.
