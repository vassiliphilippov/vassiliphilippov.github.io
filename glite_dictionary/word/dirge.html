dirge

1. NOUN

/ dɝdʒ / (AmE), / dɜːdʒ / (BrE)
a solemn song or piece of music expressing mourning or grief for the dead, typically performed at a funeral or memorial service
mourning song for the dead

Examples

The choir sang a haunting dirge as the mourners gathered in the chapel.
After the tragedy, musicians composed a moving dirge to honor the victims.
The slow notes of the dirge echoed through the old cathedral, deepening the sense of loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elegy - a poem of serious reflection, typically lamenting the dead
  • Synonym: lament - a song, poem, or piece of music expressing grief or sorrow

Etymology

From Latin 'dirige', the first word of an antiphon in the Office for the Dead, meaning 'direct' or 'guide'.
