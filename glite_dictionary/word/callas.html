Callas

1. PROPN

/ ˈkæləs / (AmE), / ˈkal.əs / (BrE)
Maria Callas (1923–1977), a celebrated Greek-American soprano renowned for her dramatic intensity and technical mastery in opera.
world-famous Greek-American opera soprano

Examples

Maria Callas transformed the world of opera with her electrifying performances.
The recordings of Callas remain influential to aspiring sopranos everywhere.
Music critics often cite Callas as one of the most gifted singers of the 20th century.
