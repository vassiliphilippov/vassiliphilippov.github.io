to complain

1. VERB

/ kəmˈpleɪn / (AmE), / kəmˈpleɪn / (BrE)
to make a formal accusation or bring a legal charge against someone
make a formal accusation
legal

Examples

The plaintiff's attorney decided to complain to the judge about the improper evidence.
If you believe a crime has occurred, you must complain to the authorities for them to investigate.
The witness chose not to complain formally, preferring to settle the matter privately.

Synset Examples

The plaintiff's lawyer complained that he defendant had physically abused his client

Etymology

From Old French *complaindre* (to lament), from Latin *complangere* (to bewail).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00845977-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kəmˈpleɪn / (AmE), / kəmˈpleɪn / (BrE)
to say that you are not satisfied or unhappy about something, often in a way that suggests you want it to be changed or improved
express dissatisfaction or unhappiness

Examples

She often complains about the weather when it rains for several days in a row.
If you have any problems with the service, please complain to the manager.
He complained that the food was cold and asked for a replacement.

Synset Examples

My mother complains all day
She has a lot to kick about

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grumble - to complain in a quiet or bad-tempered way
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

From Old French 'complaindre', from Latin 'complangere' (to lament, bewail).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00909100-v
Synset POS: v
