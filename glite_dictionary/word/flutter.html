flutter

1. NOUN

/ ˈflʌtɚ / (AmE), / ˈflʌtə / (BrE)
a quick, light, and repeated movement back and forth or up and down
quick, light back-and-forth movement

Examples

A gentle flutter of leaves caught her attention as she walked through the park.
The flutter of his heartbeat was visible on the monitor.
A sudden flutter of wings startled the group of hikers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vibration - a rapid back-and-forth motion
  • Antonym: stillness - the absence of movement or motion

4. NOUN

/ ˈflʌt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈflʌtə / (BrE)
a medical condition in which the atria of the heart beat rapidly and usually in a regular rhythm, potentially leading to heart block
rapid, regular beating of the heart's atria
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with atrial flutter after the ECG showed abnormally rapid heartbeats.
Atrial flutter can sometimes progress to more serious arrhythmias if left untreated.
Medications are often prescribed to control the heart rate in patients experiencing flutter.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fibrillation - very rapid, irregular contraction of the heart muscle fibers
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14134837-n
