Ivry

1. PROPN

/ ˈɪv.ri / (AmE), / ˈiːv.ri / (BrE)
a decisive battle fought in 1590 near Ivry, France, in which Henry IV led Huguenot forces to victory over the Catholic League.
French battle (1590); Huguenot victory under Henry IV

Examples

The Battle of Ivry marked a turning point in the French Wars of Religion.
Many consider Ivry to be Henry IV's most significant military triumph.
Historians often study Ivry to understand the conflict between Huguenots and Catholics in 16th-century France.

Etymology

From the place name Ivry (Ivry-la-Bataille) in northern France.
