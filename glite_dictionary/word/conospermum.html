Conospermum

1. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊ.nəˈspɜːr.məm / (AmE), / ˌkəʊ.nəˈspɜː.məm / (BrE)
A genus of Australian shrubs and small trees, known as smoke bushes, with dense spikes of small flowers.
genus of Australian smoke bushes

Examples

The Conospermum, or smoke bush, is valued for its soft, cloud-like blooms in Australian gardens.
Several species of Conospermum thrive in sandy heathlands of Western Australia.
Botanists are studying the pollination mechanisms of Conospermum plants.

Etymology

From Greek *kōnos* (cone) + *sperma* (seed), referring to the shape of the seeds.
