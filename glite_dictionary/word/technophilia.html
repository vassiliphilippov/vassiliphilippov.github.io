technophilia

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛk.nəˈfɪl.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌtɛk.nəˈfɪl.i.ə / (BrE)
A strong enthusiasm for or attraction to new technology.
enthusiasm for new technology

Examples

His technophilia drove him to buy every new gadget as soon as it came out.
The museum's new exhibit appealed to visitors with a sense of technophilia.
Her technophilia sometimes made her impatient with people who preferred simpler tools.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: technophobia - fear or dislike of advanced technology

Etymology

From Greek 'techne' (art, skill, craft) + 'philia' (fondness, love). Modeled after words like 'bibliophilia'.
