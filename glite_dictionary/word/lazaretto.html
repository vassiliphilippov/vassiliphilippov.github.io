lazaretto

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæzəˈrɛtoʊ / (AmE), / ˌlæzəˈretəʊ / (BrE)
A small storage locker at the stern of a boat or between the decks of a ship, used for holding equipment or supplies.
locker at the stern of a ship

Examples

The emergency supplies were safely stored in the lazaretto at the back of the yacht.
Before we docked, the captain checked the lazaretto to ensure all safety gear was accounted for.
Fishing rods and spare ropes were neatly arranged in the lazaretto.

Etymology

From Italian 'lazaretto', originally referring to a quarantine station for ships, later applied to shipboard storage spaces.

2. NOUN

/ ˌlæzəˈrɛtoʊ / (AmE), / ˌlæzəˈrɛtəʊ / (BrE)
a hospital or quarantine station for people with infectious diseases, especially leprosy
hospital for infectious diseases

Examples

During the outbreak, the city established a lazaretto on an isolated island.
The ship was required to stop at the lazaretto before passengers could go ashore.
Historical records show that the lazaretto treated thousands of patients with contagious diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leprosarium - hospital specifically for treating people with leprosy
  • Synonym: quarantine - a period or place of isolation to prevent spread of disease

Etymology

From Italian 'lazaretto', named after the church of Santa Maria di Nazaret in Venice, used as a plague hospital.

3. NOUN

/ ˌlæzəˈrɛtoʊ / (AmE), / ˌlæzəˈrɛtəʊ / (BrE)
a hospital or building used for quarantine, especially for isolating people with contagious diseases, historically situated at ports or borders
quarantine hospital or station (historically)

Examples

The old lazaretto stood on an island just outside the city harbor.
Many ships' passengers were required to stay in the lazaretto before entering the country.
The restored lazaretto attracts visitors interested in medical history.

Synset Examples

The historical lazaretto now serves as a museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarantine - a period of isolation to prevent disease spread

Etymology

From Italian 'lazzeretto', originally after the hospital of St. Lazarus, Patron Saint of lepers.
