continence

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑntənəns / (AmE), / ˈkɒntɪnəns / (BrE)
the ability to control the discharge of urine and feces voluntarily
control over urination and defecation
medical

Examples

The patient regained continence after several weeks of physical therapy.
Children usually achieve continence by the age of three.
Loss of continence can greatly affect a person's quality of life.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incontinence - lack of control over urination or defecation

Etymology

From Latin 'continentia', meaning restraint or self-control.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.tən.əns / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.tɪ.nəns / (BrE)
the exercise of self-restraint, especially in sexual activity
self-restraint in sexual activity

Examples

He admired the monk's continence and devotion to his vows.
Many religions encourage continence as a virtue for their followers.
His decision to practice continence was a personal commitment to self-control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chastity - abstention from sexual intercourse or purity in sexual matters

Etymology

From Latin continentia (holding back, abstinence), from continere (to hold together, restrain).
