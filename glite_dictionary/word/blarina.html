Blarina

1. NOUN

/ bləˈraɪnə / (AmE), / bləˈraɪnə / (BrE)
A genus of fairly large shrews with relatively short tails, native to North America.
a genus of large, short-tailed shrews

Examples

The short-tailed shrew belonging to the genus Blarina is common throughout eastern North America.
Researchers observed several Blarina specimens in the wooded area near the river.
Unlike other shrews, Blarina species are known for their toxicity and robust build.
