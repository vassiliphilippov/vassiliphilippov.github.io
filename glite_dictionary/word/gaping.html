gaping

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡeɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
with the mouth wide open, especially in wonder, awe, or surprise
with mouth wide open (in wonder or awe)

Examples

The children listened to the magician with gaping mouths.
She stood gaping at the breathtaking view before her.
The crowd was left gaping in amazement after the announcement.

Synset Examples

the gaping audience
we stood there agape with wonder
with mouth agape

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agape - with the mouth wide open in surprise or wonder

Etymology

Present participle of 'gape', from Old Norse gapa (to gape, yawn).
