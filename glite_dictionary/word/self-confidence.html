self-confidence

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlfˈkɑːn.fə.dəns / (AmE), / ˌselfˈkɒn.fɪ.dəns / (BrE)
a feeling or belief in your own abilities and worth; freedom from doubt about yourself
belief in your own abilities

Examples

She showed remarkable self-confidence during the job interview.
Building self-confidence can help you succeed in many areas of life.
His self-confidence grew as he completed each new challenge.

Synset Examples

his assurance in his superiority did not make him popular
after that failure he lost his confidence
she spoke with authority

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confidence - belief in oneself or in the reliability of something or someone
  • Antonym: self-doubt - lack of confidence in oneself and one's abilities
  • Synonym: self-assurance - confidence in one's own abilities

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' and 'confidence', confidence from Latin 'confidentia' (firmly trusting, bold).
