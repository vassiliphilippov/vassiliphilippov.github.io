bardolatry

1. NOUN

/ bɑrˈdɑlətri / (AmE), / bɑːˈdɒlətri / (BrE)
Excessive admiration or worship of William Shakespeare.
excessive admiration of Shakespeare

Examples

Some literary critics argue that bardolatry can overshadow other important writers of the period.
His passion for Shakespeare bordered on bardolatry, and he visited Stratford-upon-Avon every year.
Debates about bardolatry often arise in university English departments.

Etymology

Coined from 'bard' (a reference to Shakespeare as 'the Bard of Avon') and 'idolatry'.
