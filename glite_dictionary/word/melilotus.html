Melilotus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛlɪˈloʊtəs / (AmE), / ˌmɛlɪˈləʊtəs / (BrE)
A genus of leguminous plants, commonly known as sweet clovers, native to Eurasia and widely cultivated for forage and soil improvement.
genus of plants known as sweet clover

Examples

Several species of Melilotus are widely grown as cover crops to improve soil fertility.
The distinctive fragrance of Melilotus makes it a popular choice for attracting bees.
Researchers studied the tolerance of Melilotus to saline soils in agricultural settings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet clover - common name for species in the genus Melilotus

Etymology

From Latin *melilotus*, from Greek *melilōtos* (honey clover), from *meli* (honey) + *lōtos* (clover).
