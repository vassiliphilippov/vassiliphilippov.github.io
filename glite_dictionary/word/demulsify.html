to demulsify

1. VERB

/ dɪˈmʌlsɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈmʌlsɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
to break up an emulsion into its separate components, especially by chemical means
break up an emulsion

Examples

The technician used a special agent to demulsify the mixture during the experiment.
It is sometimes necessary to demulsify crude oil to separate water before refining.
The chemical treatment helps demulsify wastewater in industrial processes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to emulsify - to form an emulsion or cause to become an emulsion

Etymology

From de- (removal) + emulsion + -ify (forming verbs).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈmʌlsɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈmʌlsɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to break up an emulsion so that its constituent liquids separate
to separate the components of an emulsion

Examples

The chemical additive helped to demulsify the oil-water mixture, making separation easier.
Engineers must demulsify crude oil to remove water before refining.
After treatment, the plant successfully demulsified the solution in less than two hours.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to emulsify - to form an emulsion or cause liquids to mix

Etymology

From de- (remove) + emulsion + -ify (to make or become).
