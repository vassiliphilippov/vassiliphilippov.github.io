junco

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋkəʊ / (BrE)
A small North American finch, typically with gray or brown plumage, that is commonly seen in winter.
small North American finch

Examples

A flock of juncos visited our backyard feeder every morning during the snowy months.
Many birdwatchers look forward to spotting juncos when the weather turns cold.
The junco is easily identified by its slate-gray plumage and white outer tail feathers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finch - a small to medium-sized passerine bird

Etymology

From Spanish 'junco' (rush, reed), possibly in reference to habitat.
