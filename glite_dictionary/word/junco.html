Junco

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒʌŋkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋkəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of small American finches with mainly gray plumage, commonly found in North and Central America.
genus of small American finches

Examples

The Junco is a common sight at winter bird feeders across the northern United States.
Several species within the Junco genus are known for their distinctive white tail feathers.
Scientists have studied the migration patterns of the Junco extensively.

Etymology

From Spanish *junco* (reed), referring to the bird's habitat.
