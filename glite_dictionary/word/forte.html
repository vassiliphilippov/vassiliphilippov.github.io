forte

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔːrteɪ, fɔːrt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːteɪ, ˈfɔːt / (BrE)
a thing at which someone excels; a person's strong point or special talent
a person's strong point or talent

Examples

Management has never been his forte, but he is a brilliant engineer.
Public speaking is truly her forte.
Baking elaborate cakes is my grandmother's forte.

Synset Examples

cooking is his forte

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strength - an area or quality in which someone excels
  • Antonym: weakness - an area lacking in skill or aptitude

Etymology

From French 'fort' meaning 'strong (point)'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɔr.teɪ / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.teɪ / (BrE)
a passage or section in a musical work that is to be played or sung loudly
loud musical passage

Examples

The orchestra played the forte section with impressive energy.
After a gentle introduction, the choir launched into a powerful forte.
The composer indicated a sudden forte to heighten the drama of the piece.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: piano - a musical passage to be performed softly

Etymology

From Italian 'forte', meaning 'loud'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrteɪ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːteɪ / (BrE)
the stronger part of a sword blade between the hilt and the weaker tip (foible)
the strong part of a sword blade near the hilt

Examples

The duelist relied on the forte of his blade to parry the heavy blow.
In fencing, striking with the forte gives you greater control over your opponent's weapon.
The blacksmith explained how the forte must be strong to withstand repeated impacts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: foible - the weaker part of a sword blade near the tip

Etymology

From French 'forte' meaning 'strong (part)', from Latin 'fortis' (strong).

5. ADJ

/ ˈfɔr.teɪ / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.teɪ / (BrE)
(in music) played loudly; used as a direction or indication that a section should be performed at a high volume.
musical direction: loudly

Examples

The conductor signaled that the next movement should be played forte for greater impact.
Look for the forte markings in your sheet music and remember to increase your volume there.
She enjoyed the dramatic shift from piano to forte in the chorus of the song.

Synset Examples

the forte passages in the composition

Related Terms

  • Antonym: piano - (in music) played softly; a musical direction for quiet volume
  • Degree Higher: fortissimo - (in music) very loud; a musical direction for very high volume
  • Degree Lower: mezzo-forte - (in music) moderately loud; a musical direction for medium-high volume

Etymology

From Italian forte meaning 'strong' or 'loud', from Latin fortis (strong).
