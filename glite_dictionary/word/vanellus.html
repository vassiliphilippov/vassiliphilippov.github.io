Vanellus

1. NOUN

/ væˈnɛləs / (AmE), / vəˈnɛləs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the plover family, commonly known as lapwings, native to Eurasia and certain other regions.
a genus of lapwing birds

Examples

The genus Vanellus contains several species of lapwings found across Eurasia.
Researchers studied the migratory patterns of Vanellus in Eastern Europe.
The northern lapwing, or Vanellus vanellus, is known for its distinctive crest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lapwing - a wading bird of the same family, well known in Eurasia

Etymology

From Latin 'vanellus', meaning lapwing.

2. NOUN

/ væˈnɛləs / (AmE), / vəˈniːləs / (BrE)
A genus of wading birds in the plover family, including species such as the Eurasian lapwing.
genus of wading birds (plovers)

Examples

The Eurasian lapwing belongs to the genus Vanellus, which includes several ground-nesting birds.
Many members of the Vanellus genus are easily identified by their distinctive crests and calls.
Researchers studied the migratory patterns of birds in the Vanellus group across Europe and Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lapwing - a bird in the plover family, especially Vanellus vanellus

Etymology

From Latin 'vanellus' (lapwing), diminutive of 'vanus' (empty, referring to the bird's light, waving flight).
