mitigated

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɪt̬.ɪ.ɡeɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈmɪt.ɪ.ɡeɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
made less severe, serious, or painful; lessened in intensity or harshness.
made less severe or intense

Examples

The president offered a mitigated response to the crisis, seeking to ease public concerns.
Thanks to the new safety measures, the risks have been mitigated considerably.
Her punishment was mitigated because of the extenuating circumstances.

Synset Examples

he gladly accepted the mitigated penalty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alleviated - made less severe or burdensome
  • Antonym: exacerbated - made more severe or intense

Etymology

From Latin mitīgātus, past participle of mitīgāre (to soften, alleviate).
