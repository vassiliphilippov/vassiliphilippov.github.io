abrader

1. NOUN

/ əˈbreɪdər / (AmE), / əˈbreɪdə / (BrE)
A tool or machine used for wearing down, smoothing, or polishing a surface by abrasion.
tool for smoothing or polishing surfaces

Examples

The technician used an abrader to prepare the metal surface for painting.
An abrader is essential in the finishing process of woodworking.
Modern manufacturing plants often employ automated abraders to ensure smooth edges on products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grinder - a machine or tool used for grinding surfaces
  • Synonym: polisher - a device or tool that makes something smooth and shiny

Etymology

From Latin 'abradere' (to scrape off), via French 'abrader'.
