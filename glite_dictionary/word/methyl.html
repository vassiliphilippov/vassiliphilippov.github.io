methyl

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːθɪl / (AmE), / ˈmiːθɪl / (BrE)
a univalent chemical group or radical —CH₃, derived from methane, present in many organic compounds
univalent CH₃ radical in chemistry
medical

Examples

Many pharmaceuticals contain a methyl group attached to their molecular structure.
The addition of a methyl group can alter the properties of a compound.
Scientists observed that methyl ions played a crucial role in the reaction.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ethyl - the univalent radical C₂H₅- derived from ethane

Etymology

From French *méthyle*, coined from 'methane' and Greek *hyle* (wood, matter).
