luminism

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːmɪˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈluːmɪnɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
an American artistic movement of the mid-19th century, characterized by realistic landscape paintings that emphasize atmospheric light and aerial perspective
American art movement emphasizing light in landscapes

Examples

The exhibition features works from the luminism movement, highlighting the effects of natural light.
Many art historians consider luminism a uniquely American response to European landscape painting.
Her research focuses on the development of luminism in 19th-century American art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hudson River school - an American art movement focused on landscapes, precursor to luminism

Etymology

From Latin lumen (light) + -ism (indicating a movement or philosophy).
