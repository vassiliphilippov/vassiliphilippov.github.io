kampong

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑm.pɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈkæm.pɒŋ / (BrE)
A traditional village or community of houses in Malaysia, Indonesia, and other Southeast Asian countries, typically inhabited by Malay people.
traditional Southeast Asian village

Examples

The fishermen live in a small kampong near the coast.
She visited a kampong during her travels in Malaysia and learned about local traditions.
Many houses in the kampong are built on stilts to protect against flooding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: village - a group of houses and associated buildings in a rural area

Etymology

From Malay 'kampung' (village).
