planimeter

1. NOUN

/ pləˈnɪmɪtər / (AmE), / pləˈnɪmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument used to measure the area of an irregularly shaped plane figure, typically by tracing its outline
instrument for measuring areas of irregular shapes

Examples

The engineer used a planimeter to determine the area of the irregular plot of land.
By carefully tracing the boundary on the map, the planimeter calculated the enclosed area with precision.
Modern digital tools have largely replaced the traditional planimeter in many fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: measuring instrument - a device used for determining the size, amount, or degree of something

Etymology

From Latin 'planum' (level surface) and Greek 'metron' (measure).
