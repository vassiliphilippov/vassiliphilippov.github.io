Dasypodidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌdæsɪˈpɑdəˌdi / (AmE), / ˌdæsɪˈpɒdɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of mammals comprising the armadillos, known for their leathery armor shell.
armadillo family (zoology)

Examples

Members of the Dasypodidae family are characterized by their unique bony armor.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Dasypodidae in South America.
The Dasypodidae have adapted to diverse habitats, from grasslands to forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armadillo - A burrowing mammal with a bony armor shell, native to the Americas

Etymology

From New Latin Dasypodidae, based on Greek dasys (hairy) + pous, pod- (foot).
