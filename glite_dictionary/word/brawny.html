brawny

1. ADJ

/ ˈbrɔːni / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːni / (BrE)
(of a person) physically strong and muscular; having well-developed muscles.
physically strong and muscular

Examples

The brawny firefighter easily lifted the heavy beam to rescue the trapped man.
Years of training left him with a brawny physique admired by his teammates.
Despite her brawny arms, she moved with remarkable grace.

Synset Examples

a hefty athlete
a muscular boxer
powerful arms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muscular - having well-developed muscles
  • Synonym: powerful - having great strength
  • Antonym: frail - physically weak or delicate

Etymology

From Middle English 'brawny', from Old French 'braon' (fleshy part, muscle).
