harp

1. NOUN

/ hɑrp / (AmE), / hɑːp / (BrE)
A pair of curved metal supports that hold a lampshade in place, typically attaching to the lamp base and fitting around the bulb socket.
curved supports for a lampshade

Examples

The lampshade won't stay up unless you fix the harp.
She replaced the old harp with a sturdier one to fit her new lampshade.
Without a proper harp, the lampshade kept slipping off the lamp.

2. NOUN

/ hɑrp / (AmE), / hɑːp / (BrE)
a large musical instrument with a triangular frame and strings that are plucked with the fingers, producing a gentle, resonant sound
large stringed musical instrument

Examples

She played the harp beautifully during the wedding ceremony.
The orchestra featured a soloist on the harp for the concerto.
Learning to play the harp requires patience and dedication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lyre - an ancient stringed instrument similar to a small harp

Etymology

Old English *hearpe*, related to Old High German *harpa*, a stringed instrument.

4. VERB

/ hɑrp / (AmE), / hɑːp / (BrE)
to talk or complain repeatedly or persistently about something, often in a tiresome or excessive way (usually with 'on')
talk or complain persistently

Examples

He tends to harp on his grievances, even after the issue is resolved.
Please don't harp on my mistakes; I've already apologized several times.
She continues to harp on the need for punctuality at every meeting.

Synset Examples

Don't dwell on the past
She is always harping on the same old things

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dwell - to linger over or keep thinking or talking about something
  • Synonym: brood - to think long and anxiously about something, usually negative
  • Antonym: to move on - to stop focusing on something persistent

Etymology

Figurative sense from the idea of playing repeatedly on the same string of a harp; original from Old English 'hearpan'.
