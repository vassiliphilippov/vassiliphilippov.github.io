stokehole

1. NOUN

/ ˈstoʊkˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈstəʊkˌhəʊl / (BrE)
(Nautical) The chamber or compartment in which a ship's furnaces are stoked or fired, especially on steam-powered vessels.
compartment for ship's furnaces (nautical)

Examples

The stokers labored in the stokehole, shoveling coal into the roaring furnaces.
The unbearable heat of the stokehole made it a dreaded place for many crew members.
During the voyage, he worked in the stokehole to earn his passage across the ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boiler room - room containing the boilers of a ship

Etymology

From 'stoke' (to feed a furnace) + 'hole'.
