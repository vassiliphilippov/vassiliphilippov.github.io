recusation

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛkjəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛkjuːˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Law) An objection raised on the grounds that a judge is biased because of a relationship to one of the parties involved in a case.
legal objection to a judge's impartiality
legal

Examples

The defense filed a recusation against the presiding judge, citing a potential conflict of interest.
Her lawyer explained that a formal recusation was necessary due to the judge's family connection to the plaintiff.
The motion for recusation was reviewed and ultimately denied by the court.

Etymology

From Latin recusatio (refusal, objection).

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛkjəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛkjʊˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Law) The act of disqualifying or challenging a judge or juror because of presumed bias or conflict of interest.
legal disqualification of judge or juror
legal

Examples

The defense attorney filed a motion for recusation of the presiding judge due to a potential conflict of interest.
Any party may request the recusation of a juror if there is evidence of bias.
The court considered the recusation request before proceeding with the trial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recuse - to disqualify oneself as a judge due to conflict of interest or bias

Etymology

From Latin recusatio (refusal), via French récusation.
