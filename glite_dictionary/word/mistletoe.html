mistletoe

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪsəlˌtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɪsl̩təʊ / (BrE)
A plant with white berries that grows as a parasite on trees, especially in North America and Europe, traditionally used as a Christmas decoration.
parasitic plant with white berries, Chrismas tradition

Examples

There is a mistletoe plant growing high in the oak tree outside my window.
At Christmas, people often hang mistletoe in doorways for tradition.
Some American species of mistletoe closely resemble those found in Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holly - evergreen shrub with red berries, also used as Christmas decoration

Etymology

From Old English mistiltān, from mistel (mistletoe) + tān (twig).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪsəlˌtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɪsəlˌtəʊ / (BrE)
A small evergreen shrub with greenish stems, leathery leaves, and white berries, traditionally hung at Christmas and associated with the custom of kissing underneath.
parasitic shrub with white berries, used at Christmas

Examples

He hung a sprig of mistletoe over the doorway before the Christmas party.
According to tradition, you are supposed to kiss beneath the mistletoe.
The waxy white berries of mistletoe are poisonous if eaten.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holly - a plant with spiny leaves and red berries, also used as Christmas decoration

Etymology

From Old English misteltān, from mistel (mistletoe) + tān (twig, sprig).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɪsəlˌtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɪs.əl.təʊ / (BrE)
a plant with white berries that grows as a partial parasite on trees, especially oaks, and is traditionally used as a Christmas decoration
parasitic plant with white berries

Examples

During the holidays, people often hang mistletoe in their doorways as part of the tradition.
The mistletoe grew high up in the branches of the old oak tree.
Legend says couples who kiss under the mistletoe will have good luck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holly - evergreen shrub with red berries, used as Christmas decoration

Etymology

From Old English 'mistiltān', from 'mistel' (mistletoe) + 'tān' (twig, branch).
