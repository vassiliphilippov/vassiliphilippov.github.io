tragopan

1. NOUN

/ ˈtræɡəˌpæn / (AmE), / ˈtræɡəpæn / (BrE)
a brightly colored Asian pheasant of the genus Tragopan, distinguished by fleshy wattles and two horn-like processes on the head, found in forested mountain regions
an Asian pheasant with fleshy head processes

Examples

The male tragopan displays its colorful wattles and horns during courtship rituals.
We spotted a rare tragopan deep within the Himalayan forest.
Their exhibit featured several species, including the satyr tragopan known for its vivid plumage.

Etymology

From Modern Latin Tragopan, the genus name, coined after a mythical horned bird in Roman authors, itself from Greek tragos (goat) + pan (all or Pan, the god).
