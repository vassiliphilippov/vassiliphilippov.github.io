Tragopan

1. NOUN

/ ˈtræɡəˌpæn / (AmE), / ˈtræɡəˌpan / (BrE)
A genus of pheasants in the family Phasianidae, known for the vividly colored birds called tragopans, found in Asia.
a genus of Asian pheasants

Examples

The Satyr Tragopan is one of the most striking members of its genus.
Several species within the genus Tragopan display bright colors during mating season.
Ornithologists observed a rare Tragopan in the forest reserve.

Etymology

From Latin Tragopan, from Ancient Greek tragopán, a mythical horned bird.
