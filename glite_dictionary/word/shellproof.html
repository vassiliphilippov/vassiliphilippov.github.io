shellproof

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃɛlˌpruf / (AmE), / ˈʃɛlˌpruːf / (BrE)
Able to resist the explosive force of bombs and shells; not easily damaged by shells or bombs.
resistant to bombs and shells

Examples

The soldiers took refuge in a shellproof bunker during the artillery barrage.
Engineers designed the command center to be shellproof for maximum safety.
Despite heavy enemy fire, the shellproof walls remained intact.

Synset Examples

bombproof shelter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bombproof - Able to resist bombs or explosions
  • Synonym: bulletproof - Resistant to bullets

Etymology

Formed from 'shell' (explosive projectile) + 'proof' (resistant to).
