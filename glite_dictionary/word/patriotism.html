patriotism

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪtriəˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpætriətɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
Love for or devotion to one's country, often with a willingness to support and defend it.
love and devotion to one's country

Examples

Many citizens expressed their patriotism by volunteering for national service.
Her patriotism was evident in the way she celebrated the country's national holiday.
Public displays of patriotism increased during times of national crisis.

Synset Examples

they rode the same wave of popular patriotism
British nationalism was in the air and patriotic sentiments ran high

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nationalism - identification with and support for one's nation above others
  • Antonym: cosmopolitanism - the ideology that all humans belong to a single community

Etymology

From French 'patriotisme', from 'patriote' (patriot), ultimately from Greek 'patrios' (of one’s fathers).
