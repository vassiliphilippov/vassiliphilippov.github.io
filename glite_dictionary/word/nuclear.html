nuclear

1. ADJ

/ ˈnuː.kli.ɚ, ˈnjuː.kli.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.klɪə / (BrE)
relating to the nucleus of a cell.
relating to a cell nucleus
medical

Examples

The nuclear envelope separates the genetic material from the cytoplasm in eukaryotic cells.
Researchers studied nuclear division during the process of mitosis.
Many diseases are linked to defects in nuclear structure or function.

Synset Examples

nuclear membrane
nuclear division

Etymology

From Latin nucleus (kernel, core), influenced by 'nucleus' in cell biology.

/ ˈnuː.kli.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.klɪ.ə / (BrE)
relating to weapons or energy produced by the release of atomic energy, especially through the splitting or joining of atomic nuclei.
relating to atomic energy or weapons

Examples

Many nations have signed treaties to prevent the spread of nuclear weapons.
The threat of nuclear war influenced international politics throughout the twentieth century.
The country relies on nuclear energy to generate a significant portion of its electricity.

Synset Examples

nuclear weapons

From Latin nucleus (kernel, core), adapted in modern science to refer to the atomic nucleus.
