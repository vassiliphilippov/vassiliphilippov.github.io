ribose

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪboʊs / (AmE), / ˈraɪbəʊz / (BrE)
a pentose sugar that is an important component of ribonucleic acid (RNA)
a sugar in RNA
medical

Examples

Ribose is found in every cell as part of the structure of RNA.
The difference between RNA and DNA is that RNA contains ribose, while DNA contains deoxyribose.
Scientists studied the synthesis of ribose under prebiotic conditions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deoxyribose - a sugar similar to ribose found in DNA

Etymology

From German 'Ribose', coined from 'Ribonsäure' (ribonucleic acid) and the suffix '-ose' indicating a sugar.
