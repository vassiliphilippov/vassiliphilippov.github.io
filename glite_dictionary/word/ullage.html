ullage

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈʌlɪdʒ / (BrE)
the unfilled space in a container, especially the amount by which a container, such as a bottle or tank, falls short of being full
amount container lacks of being full

Examples

The wine bottle's ullage indicated it had lost some liquid over time.
Inspectors measured the ullage in the oil tank to estimate the remaining capacity.
Collectors often check the ullage of old bottles before buying vintage wines.

Etymology

From Old French *ouillage*, from Latin *oculus* (eye), referring to the opening of a container.
