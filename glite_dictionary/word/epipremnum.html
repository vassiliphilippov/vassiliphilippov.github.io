Epipremnum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈprɛmnəm / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈprɛm.nəm / (BrE)
a small genus of evergreen climbing plants (lianas) native to southeastern Asia and western Pacific regions, including some popular as houseplants
genus of evergreen climbing plants

Examples

Epipremnum species are often cultivated indoors for their attractive foliage.
The golden pothos, a popular houseplant, belongs to the Epipremnum genus.
Botanists discovered a new Epipremnum vine in the tropical forests of Borneo.

Etymology

From Greek 'epi-' (upon) and 'premnon' (trunk), referring to the plant's climbing habit.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈprɛmnəm / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈprɛm.nəm / (BrE)
A small genus of evergreen climbing plants (lianas) native to southeastern Asia and the western Pacific region, including popular houseplants such as the pothos.
genus of climbing tropical plants

Examples

The hardy Epipremnum is a favorite among people looking for low-maintenance indoor plants.
Several species of Epipremnum are grown as decorative hanging vines in homes and offices.
The leaves of Epipremnum can be quite varied in shape and color, adding visual interest to interior spaces.

Etymology

From Greek 'epi' (upon) + 'premnon' (trunk), referring to the plant's habit of growing upon trees.
