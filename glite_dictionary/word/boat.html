boat

1. VERB

/ boʊt / (AmE), / bəʊt / (BrE)
to travel or ride in a boat on water.
travel in a boat

Examples

We boated down the river at sunset, enjoying the peaceful scenery.
They boated across the lake to reach the secluded cabin.
She loves to boat every weekend when the weather is nice.

Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ boʊt / (AmE), / bəʊt / (BrE)
a small vessel designed for traveling on water, especially for personal or recreational use
small vessel for travel on water

Examples

We rented a boat and spent the afternoon rowing on the lake.
A small boat can be quickly launched from the shore in case of emergency.
She learned to steer a boat when she was just ten years old.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ship - a large vessel for traveling on water, typically for commercial or military use
  • Degree Lower: raft - a flat structure for floating on water, usually made of planks or logs fastened together

Etymology

Old English *bāt* (boat, ship), of Germanic origin.

3. NOUN

/ boʊt / (AmE), / bəʊt / (BrE)
a dish, often boat-shaped, used for serving gravy or sauce
serving dish for gravy or sauce

Examples

The silver boat was filled with a rich, brown gravy.
Please pass the boat so I can add some sauce to my potatoes.
A porcelain boat sat in the center of the table during dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gravy boat - a vessel with a spout used for serving gravy
