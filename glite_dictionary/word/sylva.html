sylva

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvə / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə / (BrE)
the forest trees growing in a particular country or region
forest trees of a region

Examples

The rich sylva of the region supports a wide diversity of wildlife.
Explorers documented the changes in the native sylva over the centuries.
Protecting the local sylva is crucial for maintaining ecological balance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forest - a large area covered chiefly with trees and undergrowth
  • Synonym: woodland - land covered with trees

Etymology

From Latin 'silva' (forest, wood).
