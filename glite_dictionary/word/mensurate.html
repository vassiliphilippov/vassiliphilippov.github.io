to mensurate

1. VERB

/ ˈmɛn.sjʊˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɛn.sjʊˌreɪt / (BrE)
to determine the measurements of something or somebody; to take measurements of
determine measurements

Examples

The scientist carefully mensurated the specimens before recording the data.
Please mensurate the width of the fabric to ensure accuracy in production.
Engineers mensurating the bridge reported a deviation from the planned dimensions.

Synset Examples

Measure the length of the wall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: measure - to determine the size, amount, or degree of something

Etymology

From Latin 'mensuratus', past participle of 'mensurare' (to measure), from 'mensura' (measure).
