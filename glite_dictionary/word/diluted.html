diluted

1. ADJ

/ daɪˈluːtɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈljuːtɪd / (BrE)
(of a liquid or substance) made thinner or weaker by having had water or another solvent added to it; reduced in strength, concentration, quality, or purity.
made weaker or less concentrated

Examples

She only drinks diluted juice because the regular kind is too sweet for her.
The scientist measured the pH of the diluted acid solution before proceeding with the experiment.
His apology sounded diluted, lacking the sincerity she had hoped for.

Synset Examples

diluted alcohol
a dilute solution
dilute acetic acid

Related Terms

  • Antonym: concentrated - having had water or other diluting agent removed; made stronger
  • Degree Lower: weak - lacking strength or concentration

Etymology

From Latin 'dilutus', past participle of 'diluere' (to wash away, dilute).
