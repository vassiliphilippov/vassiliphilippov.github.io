newly

1. ADV

/ ˈnuːli / (AmE), / ˈnjuːli / (BrE)
Very recently; only a short time ago.
very recently

Examples

The couple moved into their newly renovated apartment last week.
Only newly discovered evidence could change the outcome of the trial.
Many employees are still adjusting to the newly implemented policies.

Synset Examples

they are newly married
newly raised objections
a newly arranged hairdo
grass new washed by the rain
a freshly cleaned floor
we are fresh out of tomatoes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recently - not long ago; in the near past

Etymology

Formed from 'new' + '-ly' (adverbial suffix, Old English and Proto-Germanic roots).
