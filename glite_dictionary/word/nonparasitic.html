nonparasitic

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑnˌpærəˈsɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌpærəˈsɪtɪk / (BrE)
(of an organism or condition) not living as a parasite on another organism.
not living as a parasite

Examples

The disease is caused by a nonparasitic fungus rather than a harmful worm.
Many plants form nonparasitic relationships with the organisms around them.
Some skin conditions result from nonparasitic infections and do not involve any parasites.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: parasitic - (of an organism or condition) living as a parasite on or inside another organism

Etymology

From prefix 'non-' (not) + 'parasitic', from Greek 'parasitos' (person eating at another's table).
