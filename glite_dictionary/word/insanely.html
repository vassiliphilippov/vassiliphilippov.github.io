insanely

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈseɪnli / (AmE), / ɪnˈseɪnli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows insanity; extremely or wildly.
in a wild or extreme manner

Examples

She laughed insanely at the joke, startling everyone in the room.
The roller coaster moved insanely fast down the track.
He worked insanely hard to finish the project on time.

Synset Examples

she behaved insanely
he behaves crazily when he is off his medication
the witch cackled madly
screaming dementedly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crazily - in a crazy or wild manner
  • Synonym: madly - in a mad or frenzied way

Etymology

From insane + -ly.

2. ADV

/ ɪnˈseɪnli / (AmE), / ɪnˈseɪnli / (BrE)
To an extreme or excessive degree; extremely.
extremely; to an excessive degree

Examples

He worked insanely hard to finish the project on time.
The new computer runs insanely fast compared to my old one.
She was insanely jealous of her sister's achievements.

Synset Examples

she was madly in love
deadly dull
deadly earnest
deucedly clever
insanely jealous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: madly - extremely; to a great extent
  • Synonym: extremely - to a very great degree
  • Degree Lower: barely - only just; by a small margin (opposite in degree)

Etymology

From 'insane' + '-ly'. 'Insane' is from Latin 'insanus' (mad, unhealthy).
