Cupid

1. PROPN

/ ˈkjuː.pɪd / (AmE), / ˈkjuː.pɪd / (BrE)
the Roman god of love, often depicted as a winged child with a bow and arrows; the counterpart of the Greek god Eros
Roman god of love in mythology

Examples

Many artists have depicted Cupid as a mischievous child with a bow and arrow.
In Roman mythology, Cupid causes people to fall in love by shooting them with his arrows.
The figure of Cupid frequently appears in classical paintings and Valentine's Day cards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eros - Greek god of love, counterpart to Cupid in Roman mythology

Etymology

From Latin 'Cupido', meaning 'desire', from 'cupere' (to desire).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkjuː.pɪd / (AmE), / ˈkjuː.pɪd / (BrE)
the god of love in Roman mythology, often depicted as a naked, winged boy with a bow and arrows; also used as a symbol for love and affection
Roman god of love; love symbol

Examples

On Valentine’s Day, cards often feature Cupid aiming his arrow at unsuspecting lovers.
In classical paintings, Cupid is shown as a mischievous cherub with little golden wings.
The statue of Cupid in the garden symbolizes eternal love and romance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eros - Greek god of love; counterpart to Cupid

Etymology

From Latin Cupidō, the god of erotic love, from cupere 'to desire'.
