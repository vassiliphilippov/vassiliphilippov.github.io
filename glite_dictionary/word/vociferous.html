vociferous

1. ADJ

/ voʊˈsɪfərəs / (AmE), / vəʊˈsɪfərəs / (BrE)
expressing opinions or feelings loudly and forcefully; characterized by strong vocal outcry or protest
loud and forceful, especially in protest

Examples

The protesters grew increasingly vociferous as their demands were ignored by officials.
A vociferous opponent of the new policy spoke for nearly an hour at the meeting.
Despite his vociferous objections, the decision had already been made.

Synset Examples

blatant radios
a clamorous uproar
strident demands
a vociferous mob

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clamorous - making a loud and confused noise
  • Synonym: blatant - (of behavior) done openly and unashamedly; offensively conspicuous
  • Antonym: quiet - making little or no noise

Etymology

From Latin vociferari (to shout), from vox (voice) + ferre (to carry).
