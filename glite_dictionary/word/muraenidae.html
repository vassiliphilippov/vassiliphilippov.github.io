Muraenidae

1. NOUN

/ mjʊˈriːnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / mjʊəˈriːnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
the family of elongated, predatory fish commonly known as moray eels, found in tropical and subtropical seas
family of moray eels

Examples

The Muraenidae family includes over 200 species of moray eels worldwide.
Marine biologists study Muraenidae to better understand their unique hunting mechanisms.
Most members of the Muraenidae have sharp teeth and are nocturnal predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moray eel - a species of elongated, predatory fish in the family Muraenidae

Etymology

From New Latin Muraenidae, based on Latin muraena (moray eel).
