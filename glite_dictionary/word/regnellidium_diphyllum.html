Regnellidium diphyllum

1. NOUN

/ rɛɡˌnɛlɪˈdiːəm ˈdɪfɪləm / (AmE), / rɛɡˌnɛlɪˈdiːlɪəm ˈdɪfɪləm / (BrE)
A small, latex-containing aquatic fern native to southern Brazil, known for its divided leaves.
aquatic fern of southern Brazil

Examples

The unique floating leaves of Regnellidium diphyllum make it a popular choice for botanical gardens.
Researchers discovered a small population of Regnellidium diphyllum thriving in the wetlands of southern Brazil.
Latex extracted from Regnellidium diphyllum has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.

Etymology

Genus named after Swedish botanist Anders Fredrik Regnell; from Greek 'di-' (two) and 'phyllon' (leaf).
