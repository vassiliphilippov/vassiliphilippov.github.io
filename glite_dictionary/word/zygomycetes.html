Zygomycetes

1. NOUN

/ zaɪɡoʊmaɪˈsiːtiːz / (AmE), / zaɪɡəʊmaɪˈsiːtiːz / (BrE)
a former class of fungi characterized by the production of zygospores, now largely corresponding to the subdivision Zygomycota
class of fungi producing zygospores

Examples

The bread mold is a common example of a Zygomycetes fungus.
Scientists are studying the reproductive cycle of Zygomycetes to better understand fungal evolution.
Many species in the Zygomycetes group can rapidly colonize decaying organic matter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zygomycota - a subdivision of fungi corresponding to Zygomycetes

Etymology

From Greek 'zygōn' (yoke) and 'mykēs' (fungus), referring to the yoked spores produced during sexual reproduction.

2. NOUN

/ zaɪɡoʊmaɪˈsiːtiːz / (AmE), / zaɪɡəʊmaɪˈsiːtiːz / (BrE)
a class of fungi distinguished by the formation of zygospores during sexual reproduction, corresponding to the former subdivision Zygomycota
class of fungi forming zygospores
medical

Examples

Many bread molds, such as Rhizopus stolonifer, belong to the Zygomycetes.
Research on Zygomycetes has provided valuable insight into sexual spore formation in fungi.
The Zygomycetes are known for their ability to decompose organic matter in soil.

Etymology

From Greek 'zygon' (yoke) and 'mykēs' (fungus), referring to the yoke-like joining of cells in reproduction.
