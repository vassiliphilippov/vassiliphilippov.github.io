Clematis tangutica

1. NOUN

/ kləˈmætɪs tɑnˈɡutɪkə / (AmE), / klɪˈmætɪs tɪŋˈɡuːtɪkə / (BrE)
A species of clematis native to China, known for its serrated leaves and large yellow flowers, widely grown as an ornamental climbing plant.
species of clematis with yellow flowers

Examples

The Clematis tangutica in her garden bloomed with bright yellow, lantern-shaped flowers throughout the summer.
Gardeners often train Clematis tangutica to grow along fences and trellises for its striking appearance.
Unlike other varieties, Clematis tangutica is prized for its long-lasting seed heads as well as its blooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clematis - a genus of climbing perennial plants with showy flowers

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name Clematis, and specific epithet tangutica, referring to Tangut (historic region of northwestern China).
