dickey-bird

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪki bɝd / (AmE), / ˈdɪki bɜːd / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly British) A small bird; used especially by adults when talking to children.
small bird (informal, chiefly British, child-directed)
informal british

Examples

Look, there's a dickey-bird singing on the fence!
Grandad pointed to a dickey-bird in the garden to make his granddaughter laugh.
We could hear the dickey-birds chirping in the early morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bird - feathered, winged animal that lays eggs

Etymology

From 'dickey', an affectionate or diminutive form, plus 'bird'.
