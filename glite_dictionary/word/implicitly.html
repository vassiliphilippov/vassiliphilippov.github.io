implicitly

1. ADV

/ ɪmˈplɪsɪtli / (AmE), / ɪmˈplɪsɪtli / (BrE)
in a way that is suggested but not directly expressed; without being stated clearly
in an implied or indirect way

Examples

She implicitly trusted her colleagues to handle the situation professionally.
By agreeing to the terms, you implicitly accept all the conditions outlined in the contract.
The manager implicitly encouraged innovation by rewarding creative thinking.

Synset Examples

he implicitly assumes that you know the answer

Related Terms

  • Antonym: explicitly - in a clear and direct manner

Etymology

From implicit + -ly, with 'implicit' from Latin implicitus (entangled).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00369055-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ɪmˈplɪsɪtli / (AmE), / ɪmˈplɪsɪtli / (BrE)
without doubting or questioning; in a way that is not directly stated but understood or implied
without doubt; not stated directly

Examples

I implicitly trust her judgment in difficult situations.
The contract implicitly requires both parties to act in good faith.
You must implicitly follow the instructions provided to ensure safety.

Synset Examples

I implicitly trust him

Related Terms

  • Antonym: explicitly - in a clear and detailed manner, leaving no room for confusion or doubt

Etymology

From Latin implicitus (entangled, involved) + -ly.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00369364-r
Synset POS: r
