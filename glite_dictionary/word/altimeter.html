altimeter

1. NOUN

/ ælˈtɪmətɚ / (AmE), / ælˈtɪmɪtə / (BrE)
An instrument for measuring altitude, especially the height above the ground or sea level, used in navigation and aviation.
instrument for measuring altitude

Examples

The pilot relied on the altimeter to maintain a safe flying height.
Climbers often carry a altimeter to measure their ascent up the mountain.
A faulty altimeter can lead to serious navigation errors during flight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barometer - instrument measuring atmospheric pressure

Etymology

From French 'altimètre', from Latin 'altus' (high) + French '-mètre' (meter).
