sassaby

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæs.əˌbi / (AmE), / ˈsas.əˌbi / (BrE)
a large African antelope noted for its speed, found in southern Africa
large African antelope

Examples

The herd of sassaby grazed peacefully on the open plains of Botswana.
A sassaby can outrun most predators due to its incredible speed.
Wildlife photographers often travel to Africa to capture images of the elusive sassaby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antelope - a swift-running deer-like animal native to Africa and Asia

Etymology

From Tswana *tsèsèbè*, via Afrikaans *tsessebe*.
