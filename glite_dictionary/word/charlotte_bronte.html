Charlotte Bronte

1. PROPN

/ ʃɑrˈloʊt ˈbrɑnteɪ / (AmE), / ˈʃɑːlət ˈbrɒnteɪ / (BrE)
(1816–1855) An English novelist, the eldest of the three Brontë sisters, best known for works such as 'Jane Eyre'.
English novelist, author of 'Jane Eyre'

Examples

Charlotte Bronte wrote the classic novel 'Jane Eyre', which remains widely read today.
Many scholars consider Charlotte Bronte one of the most influential Victorian authors.
The life of Charlotte Bronte was marked by both personal tragedy and remarkable literary achievement.
