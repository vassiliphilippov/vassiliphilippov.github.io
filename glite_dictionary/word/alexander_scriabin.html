Alexander Scriabin

1. PROPN

/ əˈlɛkzəndər skriˈɑːbɪn / (AmE), / əˈlɛksəndə skriˈɑːbɪn / (BrE)
(1872–1915) Russian composer known for his innovative piano and orchestral works, and for developing a unique musical language.
Russian composer (1872–1915)

Examples

Many music historians consider Alexander Scriabin a pioneer of early twentieth-century classical music.
The piano sonatas of Alexander Scriabin are acclaimed for their expressive harmonies.
Modern composers often cite Alexander Scriabin as an influence on their approach to musical color.
