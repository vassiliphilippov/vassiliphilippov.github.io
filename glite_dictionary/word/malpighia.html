Malpighia

1. NOUN

/ mælˈpɪɡiə / (AmE), / malˈpɪɡɪə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical and subtropical shrubs and trees in the family Malpighiaceae, including the Barbados cherry.
genus of shrubs and trees (Malpighiaceae)

Examples

The Malpighia genus includes species like the Barbados cherry, known for its high vitamin C content.
Botanists studied the leaf structure of Malpighia plants to understand their adaptation to tropical climates.
Several ornamental varieties of Malpighia are popular in landscaping and home gardens.

Etymology

Named after Marcello Malpighi (1628–1694), Italian biologist and physician.
