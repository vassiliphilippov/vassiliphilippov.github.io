stagnant

1. ADJ

/ ˈstæɡ.nənt / (AmE), / ˈstæɡ.nənt / (BrE)
not developing, growing, or moving; lacking activity, force, or vitality.
not moving or developing

Examples

The stagnant economy caused many small businesses to close.
Water in the pond became stagnant after weeks without rain.
His career had grown stagnant, prompting him to look for new opportunities.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: active - engaged in action or movement; energetic
  • Antonym: dynamic - characterized by constant change, activity, or progress
  • Synonym: static - lacking in movement, action, or change

Etymology

From Latin 'stagnantem', present participle of 'stagnare' (to stagnate).

2. ADJ

/ ˈstæɡ.nənt / (AmE), / ˈstæɡ.nənt / (BrE)
not moving or flowing; showing no activity, dull and sluggish
not flowing; showing no movement

Examples

The pond had become stagnant over the summer, attracting mosquitoes.
Financial growth has been stagnant for the past few years in the region.
The air in the closed room felt stagnant and musty.

Synset Examples

dead air
dead water
stagnant water

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flowing - moving smoothly and continuously

Etymology

From Latin 'stagnantem', present participle of 'stagnare' (to stagnate, to stand still), from 'stagnum' (standing water, pool).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01244121-s
Synset POS: s
