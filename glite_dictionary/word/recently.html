recently

1. ADV

/ ˈriːsəntli / (AmE), / ˈriːsntli / (BrE)
at a time not long ago; in the recent past
in the recent past

Examples

I haven’t seen her recently, but I heard she moved to a new city.
Have you watched any good movies recently?
The company has recently launched a new line of products.

Synset Examples

he was in Paris recently
lately the rules have been enforced
as late as yesterday she was fine
feeling better of late
the spelling was first affected, but latterly the meaning also

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lately - recently; in the recent past
  • Antonym: formerly - in the past; previously

Etymology

From recent + -ly, where 'recent' comes from Latin 'recentem' (fresh, new).
