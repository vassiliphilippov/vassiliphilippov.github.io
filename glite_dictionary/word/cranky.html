cranky

1. ADJ

/ ˈkræŋki / (AmE), / ˈkraŋki / (BrE)
easily irritated or annoyed; quick to become upset or complain.
easily annoyed or irritable

Examples

I get a little cranky when I don't get enough sleep.
The cranky old man yelled at the kids for playing near his garden.
She becomes cranky if she skips her morning coffee.

Synset Examples

an incorrigibly fractious young man
not the least nettlesome of his countrymen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritable - easily annoyed or angered
  • Synonym: grumpy - bad-tempered, easily annoyed
  • Antonym: even-tempered - not easily angered or upset

Etymology

Origin uncertain; perhaps from dialectal crank (whimsical, capricious) + -y.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkræŋki / (AmE), / ˈkraŋki / (BrE)
(of a boat) inclined to heel over easily under sail; unstable or lacking balance in the water.
unstable or likely to tip (of a boat)

Examples

The small sailboat was so cranky that it almost capsized in a light breeze.
Experienced sailors avoided using the cranky old dinghy for rough-weather races.
After noticing how cranky the yacht felt, the captain ordered everyone to move to the center.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stable - not likely to tip over or heel under sail (of a boat)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02300153-s
Synset POS: s
