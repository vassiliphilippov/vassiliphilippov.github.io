Moneses uniflora

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈniːsiːz ˌjuːnɪˈflɔːrə / (AmE), / məʊˈniːsiːz ˌjuːnɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
A delicate evergreen dwarf herb of northern temperate regions, with a single white terminal flower.
delicate dwarf herb with single white flower

Examples

The Moneses uniflora is often found growing in moist, shaded forests of North America.
Botanists admire the delicate white flowers of Moneses uniflora.
During our hike, we identified several Moneses uniflora plants among the pine needles.

Etymology

From Greek 'monēsēs' (solitary) and Latin 'uniflora' (one-flowered).
