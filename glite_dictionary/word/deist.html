deist

1. ADJ

/ ˈdiːɪst / (AmE), / ˈdiːɪst / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of deism, the belief in the existence of a supreme being, specifically of a creator who does not intervene in the universe.
relating to deism

Examples

Her deist beliefs distinguish her from traditional religious communities.
The philosopher espoused a deist perspective in his writings on faith and reason.
Some founding fathers of the United States were known for their deist views.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: atheist - not believing in the existence of a god or gods; rejecting theism
  • Antonym: theist - believing in the existence of a god or gods, especially a personal god

Etymology

From French déiste, from Latin deus (god).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdiːɪst / (AmE), / ˈdiːɪst / (BrE)
A person who believes that God created the universe but does not intervene in its workings, having allowed it to run according to natural laws.
follower of deism; believes God created universe but does not intervene

Examples

Thomas Jefferson is often described as a deist rather than a traditional Christian.
A deist argues that the universe operates according to natural laws set by a creator.
Unlike theists, a deist does not believe in divine intervention in daily affairs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: atheist - a person who does not believe in the existence of God
  • Antonym: theist - a person who believes in the existence of a God who intervenes in the universe

Etymology

From French déiste, from Latin Deus (God).

Synset POS: n
