akee

1. NOUN

/ əˈkiː / (AmE), / əˈkiː / (BrE)
A red, pear-shaped tropical fruit native to West Africa, with poisonous seeds and flesh that is toxic unless properly ripened and cooked.
tropical fruit with toxic seeds and flesh

Examples

The national dish of Jamaica is saltfish and akee.
It is important to cook akee properly to avoid poisoning.
Unripe akee contains toxins that can be harmful if ingested.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blighia sapida - the tropical tree that produces the akee fruit

Etymology

From West African Akan language *akyefo*, via Jamaican English.

2. NOUN

/ əˈkiː / (AmE), / əˈkiː / (BrE)
An evergreen tree (Blighia sapida), native to West Africa but widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions for its fragrant flowers and distinctive red fruit, which is used as food after proper preparation.
tropical tree with edible fruit

Examples

The famous Jamaican dish of saltfish and akee is a national favorite.
Before eating, it's important to ensure akee is fully ripe and properly prepared.
We saw a large akee tree blossoming in the tropical garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ackee - Alternative spelling for the same tree and fruit

Etymology

From West African (Twi) *akye* or *aki*; introduced to English via Jamaican usage.
