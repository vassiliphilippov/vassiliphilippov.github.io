googolplex

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡuːɡəlˌplɛks / (AmE), / ˈɡuːɡəlˌplɛks / (BrE)
a cardinal number equal to 1 followed by a googol of zeros (10 to the power of a googol, or 10^(10^100))
number: 1 followed by a googol of zeros

Examples

A googolplex is so large that it cannot be physically written out in standard decimal notation.
Mathematicians use the term googolplex to discuss concepts of very large numbers.
Even the number of atoms in the universe is nowhere near a googolplex.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: googol - a cardinal number represented as 1 followed by 100 zeros (10 to the power of 100)

Etymology

From 'googol', coined in 1938 by Milton Sirotta, plus '-plex' meaning 'woven together' or 'combination'.
