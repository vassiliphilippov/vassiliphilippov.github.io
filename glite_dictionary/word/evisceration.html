evisceration

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌvɪsəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌvɪsəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the removal of internal organs, especially the bowels or viscera, from a body
removal of internal organs
medical

Examples

The surgeon performed an evisceration to remove the infected tissue.
Ancient rituals sometimes involved the evisceration of sacrificial animals.
After the accident, the doctor had to treat a patient with abdominal evisceration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disembowelment - the act of removing the entrails from a body

Etymology

From Latin eviscerare (to disembowel), from e- (out) + viscera (entrails).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌvɪsəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌvɪsəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of weakening or stripping an idea, law, or statement of its essential value or force, often by alteration
weakening or stripping of essential value

Examples

The amendments resulted in the evisceration of the original bill's intent.
Critics argued that the policy underwent evisceration during committee discussions.
Some see the court's decision as an evisceration of civil rights protections.

Synset Examples

the adoption of their amendments would have amounted to an evisceration of the act

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dilution - the process of making something weaker or less effective
  • Antonym: strengthening - the act of making something stronger or more effective

Etymology

From Latin evisceratio, from eviscerare (to disembowel), figuratively meaning to take out the essential parts.

3. NOUN

/ ɪˌvɪsəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌvɪsəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the surgical removal of an organ or the contents of an organ from the body
removal of organ or contents (surgery)
medical

Examples

The patient underwent evisceration of the infected tissue to prevent further complications.
Ocular evisceration is sometimes necessary when the eye is severely damaged.
The surgeon performed an evisceration to remove the diseased abdominal organ.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excision - the act of cutting something out, especially surgically
  • Synonym: enucleation - removal of an organ or tumor, especially the eye, from its socket

Etymology

From Latin eviscerare (to disembowel), from e- (out) + viscera (internal organs).
