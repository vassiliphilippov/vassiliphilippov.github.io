rushing

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrʌʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of moving quickly and often carelessly; hurried movement or action
the act of moving hurriedly and carelessly

Examples

His constant rushing often leads to mistakes at work.
She spilled coffee because she was rushing out the door.
The rushing of students in the hallway created a lot of noise.

Synset Examples

in his haste to leave he forgot his book

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haste - excessive speed or urgency of movement
  • Antonym: leisure - free time and relaxed pace, opposite of hurry

2. NOUN

/ ˈrʌʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrʌʃɪŋ / (BrE)
(in American football) an attempt to advance the ball by running with it, especially into the defensive line.
running play in American football
american

Examples

The team's rushing was particularly effective in the second half of the game.
He broke the school record for most rushing yards in a single season.
Defenders focused on stopping the rushing to force more passing plays.

Synset Examples

the linebackers were ready to stop a rush

Related Terms

  • Antonym: passing - throwing the ball to advance play (American football)
