spirogyra

1. NOUN

/ spaɪrəˈdʒaɪrə / (AmE), / spaɪrəˈdʒʌɪrə / (BrE)
a genus of freshwater green algae consisting of microscopic filaments with characteristic spiral-shaped chlorophyll bands
freshwater algae with spiral chlorophyll bands

Examples

Under the microscope, spirogyra displays beautiful spiral chloroplasts within its cells.
The pond was covered with strands of spirogyra after the spring rains.
Students observed spirogyra in their biology class while learning about algae.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'speira' (coil, spiral) and 'gyra' (ring, circle).
