grasp

1. NOUN

/ ɡræsp / (AmE), / ɡrɑːsp / (BrE)
an act of holding something tightly with your hand or arms
an act of holding firmly

Examples

She tightened her grasp on the suitcase as the train lurched forward.
His grasp on the rope kept him from falling into the river.
The small child's grasp was surprisingly strong for her age.

Synset Examples

he released his clasp on my arm
he has a strong grip for an old man
she kept a firm hold on the railing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grip - an act of holding something tightly
  • Antonym: release - the act of letting go or setting free
  • Synonym: clutch - a tight hold; the act of grasping tightly

Etymology

From Middle English graspen, probably of Germanic origin, related to Old High German graspon (to seize).
