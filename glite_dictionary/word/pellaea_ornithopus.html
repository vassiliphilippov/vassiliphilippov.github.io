Pellaea ornithopus

1. NOUN

/ pəˈliːə ɔːrˈnɪθəpəs / (AmE), / pɛˈliːə ɔːˈnɪθəpəs / (BrE)
A species of fern native to California and Baja California, known as cliff brake, distinguished by its purple-brown leafstalks.
a species of fern with purple-brown leafstalks

Examples

The rocky slopes were covered with Pellaea ornithopus, its distinctive purple-brown stems standing out against the stone.
Botanists documented several colonies of Pellaea ornithopus along the Baja California coastline.
The gardener was excited to add Pellaea ornithopus to the native fern collection.

Etymology

From genus name 'Pellaea' (Greek 'pellos', meaning 'dusky') and species name 'ornithopus' (Greek 'ornithos' for 'bird' and 'pous' for 'foot', referring to foot-shaped structures).
