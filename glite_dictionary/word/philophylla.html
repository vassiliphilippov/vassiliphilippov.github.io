Philophylla

1. NOUN

/ fɪˈlɒfɪlə / (AmE), / fɪˈlɒfɪlə / (BrE)
A genus of fruit flies in the family Tephritidae.
genus of fruit flies

Examples

Researchers identified a new species within the genus Philophylla during the recent expedition.
The larvae of Philophylla are known to feed on the leaves of various plants.
She specializes in the classification of Philophylla and related tephritid flies.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Greek 'philo-' (loving) + 'phylla' (leaves).

2. NOUN

/ fɪˈlɒfɪlə / (AmE), / fɪˈlɒfɪlə / (BrE)
A genus of fruit flies in the family Tephritidae, known for larvae that mine leaves of various plants.
genus of fruit flies (Tephritidae)

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Philophylla during the field expedition.
The larvae of Philophylla feed by mining the leaves of their host plants.
Philophylla is important in studies of plant-insect interactions due to its specialized feeding habits.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Tephritidae - family of fruit flies to which Philophylla belongs

Etymology

From Greek 'philo-' (loving) and 'phylla' (leaves), referring to its leaf-mining larvae.
