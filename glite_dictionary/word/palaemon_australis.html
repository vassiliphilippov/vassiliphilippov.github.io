Palaemon australis

1. NOUN

/ pəˈleɪmən ˌɔːˈstreɪlɪs / (AmE), / pəˈleɪmən ˌɔːˈstreɪlɪs / (BrE)
A large edible freshwater prawn, often exceeding a foot in length, common in Australian rivers.
large Australian freshwater prawn species

Examples

The Palaemon australis is prized by anglers for its impressive size and delicate flavor.
Researchers observed the habitat preferences of Palaemon australis in the upper reaches of the river.
Local fishermen rely on the annual migration of Palaemon australis to sustain their catch.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus Palaemon and Latin australis ('southern').
