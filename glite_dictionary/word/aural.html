aural

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔrəl / (AmE), / ˈɔːrəl / (BrE)
Relating to or characterized by an aura, especially in reference to symptoms or phenomena that precede certain medical conditions, such as migraines or seizures.
relating to an aura (medical)
medical

Examples

Some migraine sufferers notice distinct aural sensations before pain begins.
Epileptic patients sometimes experience aural warnings prior to a seizure.
Doctors noted the patient's aural disturbances when diagnosing his condition.

Synset Examples

various aural effects that precede a migraine headache

Etymology

From Latin 'aura' (breeze, air), used in medical Latin for a supposed subtle emanation.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɔr.əl / (AmE), / ˈɔː.rəl / (BrE)
relating to hearing or the sense of the ear.
relating to hearing or the ear

Examples

The doctor performed an aural examination to check for signs of infection.
Some birds have remarkable aural abilities, enabling them to detect the faintest sounds.
He relied on aural clues rather than visual ones during the test.

Synset Examples

an animal with a very sensitive aural apparatus

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auditory - relating to hearing

Etymology

From Latin 'auris' (ear) + '-al' (forming adjectives).
