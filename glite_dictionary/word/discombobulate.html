to discombobulate

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪskəmˈbɑbjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˌdɪskəmˈbɒbjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to confuse or bewilder someone; to make someone unable to think clearly
to confuse or bewilder

Examples

The sudden change of plans seemed to discombobulate everyone in the office.
Complicated instructions can easily discombobulate new team members.
Jet lag tends to discombobulate me for a couple of days after I travel overseas.

Synset Examples

These questions confuse even the experts
This question completely threw me
This question befuddled even the teacher

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confuse - to cause someone to be unable to think clearly or understand something
  • Synonym: to perplex - to make someone feel completely baffled
  • Antonym: to clarify - to make something less confused and more clearly comprehensible

Etymology

Probably an American humorous coinage from the mid-19th century, based on 'discompose' and similar words.

2. VERB

/ ˌdɪskəmˈbɑbjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˌdɪskəmˈbɒbjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to confuse or disconcert someone; to throw into a state of confusion
to confuse or bewilder
informal american

Examples

The sudden change in schedule seemed to discombobulate everyone in the office.
Traveling to a new country can sometimes discombobulate even the most experienced traveler.
Her unexpected question completely discombobulated him during the interview.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confuse - to make someone unable to think clearly or understand something
  • Antonym: to clarify - to make something clear or easier to understand

Etymology

Probably a fanciful alteration of 'discompose' or 'discomfit,' with humorous effect, first attested in 19th-century American English.
