gusset

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌsɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡʌsɪt / (BrE)
A piece of chain mail used to protect an area that would otherwise be left uncovered by armor plates.
chain mail covering unprotected armor areas

Examples

The knight's gusset protected his underarm, where the plate armor could not reach.
Medieval armorers often sewed a gusset into the armpit area for added protection.
Without a gusset, vulnerable joints could be targeted by enemy weapons.

Etymology

From Old French 'gousset' (armhole; gusset), diminutive of 'gousse' (pod, shell).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌsɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡʌsɪt / (BrE)
a metal plate used to reinforce or connect beams or joists in structures
metal plate for structural reinforcement

Examples

Engineers installed a gusset at each corner to improve the bridge’s stability.
The steel beam was connected to the column with a large gusset.
Before welding, check that every gusset is properly aligned with the joint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plate - a flat piece of metal or another material

Etymology

From Old French gosset (armhole, piece of armor), later extended in meaning.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌsɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡʌsɪt / (BrE)
A piece of material sewn into a garment, bag, or other item to strengthen it or enlarge part of it.
reinforcing or enlarging fabric insert

Examples

The tailor added a gusset under the arms to give the shirt more flexibility.
Yoga pants often have a diamond-shaped gusset for added comfort and movement.
She repaired the tear in her dress by sewing in a small gusset.

Etymology

From Old French *gousset* (a small piece of cloth; a purse), diminutive of *gousse* (pod, shell).
