unrigged

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈrɪɡd / (AmE), / ʌnˈrɪɡd / (BrE)
(of a ship or vessel) stripped of its rigging; having had its ropes, sails, or equipment removed.
stripped of rigging (ships)

Examples

After the storm, the damaged sailboat was quickly unrigged at the harbor.
The museum displays an old unrigged schooner to illustrate its construction.
With the crew's help, the yacht was unrigged and prepared for transport.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rigged - fitted or equipped with rigging (of a ship)

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' to 'rigged', meaning stripped of rigging or equipment.
