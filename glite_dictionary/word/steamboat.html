steamboat

1. NOUN

/ ˈstiːmˌboʊt / (AmE), / ˈstiːmbəʊt / (BrE)
a boat propelled by a steam engine, especially one used on rivers in the 19th and early 20th centuries
boat powered by a steam engine

Examples

The old steamboat chugged upstream, leaving a trail of white smoke in its wake.
Tourists can take a ride on a steamboat to experience life as it was on the Mississippi River.
Mark Twain wrote about his experiences working on a steamboat in the 1800s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steamship - a large ship powered by a steam engine

Etymology

From 'steam' + 'boat', indicating a boat powered by steam.
