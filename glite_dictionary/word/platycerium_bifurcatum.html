Platycerium bifurcatum

1. NOUN

/ ˌplætɪˈsɪriəm baɪˌfɜːrˈkeɪtəm / (AmE), / ˌplætɪˈsɪəriəm baɪˌfɜːkəˈtɑːm / (BrE)
a species of staghorn fern native to Australia, southeastern Asia, and Polynesia, often grown as an ornamental plant
a species of staghorn fern

Examples

The Platycerium bifurcatum makes a striking addition to shady gardens and indoor spaces.
Many plant enthusiasts mount their Platycerium bifurcatum on wooden plaques for display.
Proper humidity is essential for a healthy Platycerium bifurcatum to thrive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: staghorn fern - a fern with antler-like fronds, commonly used as an ornamental plant

Etymology

From Greek 'platys' (broad) and 'kerion' (horn), referring to the fronds, with Latin species epithet 'bifurcatum' (forked).
