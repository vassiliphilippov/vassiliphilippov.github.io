rediffusion

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːdəˈfjuːʒən / (AmE), / ˌriːdɪˈfjuːʒ(ə)n / (BrE)
a system for distributing radio or television programs over a wired network, often to homes or public venues
system for distributing radio or tv broadcasts

Examples

Several cities in the 1950s relied on rediffusion to deliver television signals to neighborhoods.
The museum showcased an old rediffusion set used to broadcast radio programs in colonial times.
Before satellite TV became popular, some remote areas depended on rediffusion services for entertainment.

Etymology

From re- (again) + diffusion (spreading), originally used for rebroadcasting programs via cable or wired systems.
