dissonant

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɪsənənt / (AmE), / ˈdɪsənənt / (BrE)
(of sounds or musical notes) characterized by a lack of harmony; harsh or unresolved in combination.
lacking harmony; musically unresolved

Examples

The composer purposefully used dissonant chords to create tension in the piece.
Some listeners find modern dissonant music uncomfortable, while others appreciate its complexity.
The dissonant sounds at the climax made the audience feel uneasy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consonant - (of music) harmonious and pleasant sounding

Etymology

From Latin dissonans, dissonant-, present participle of dissonare (to sound apart).

WordNet ID: oewn-01168105-s

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɪsənənt / (AmE), / ˈdɪsənənt / (BrE)
lacking harmony; not agreeing or consistent, especially in sound or opinion
lacking harmony; disagreeing

Examples

The dissonant chords in the music created a sense of tension and unrest.
Her dissonant views often clashed with the consensus of the group.
Bright colors can appear dissonant if they do not complement each other well.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consonant - in harmony; harmonious or in agreement

Etymology

From Latin dissonans, dissonant-, present participle of dissonare (to sound differently).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01167794-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈdɪsənənt / (AmE), / ˈdɪsənənt / (BrE)
not in agreement or harmony; conflicting or clashing
not in harmony or agreement

Examples

Her views were dissonant with those of the rest of the team.
The committee was plagued by dissonant opinions that made consensus difficult.
Their dissonant statements confused the public.

Synset Examples

desires at variance with his duty
widely discrepant statements

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consonant - in agreement or harmony
  • Synonym: discordant - lacking harmony; disagreeing

Etymology

From Latin dissonans, dissonant-, present participle of dissonare (to sound apart).
