demonolatry

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːməˈnɑːlətri / (AmE), / ˌdiːməˈnɒlətri / (BrE)
the worship of demons or evil spirits, especially through specific rites or ceremonies
worship of demons or evil spirits

Examples

In the 17th century, accusations of demonolatry were often used to justify witch hunts.
The ancient cult was rumored to engage in secretive demonolatry rites.
Her book explores the history and symbolism of demonolatry in European folklore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demonology - the study of demons or beliefs about demons
  • Degree Lower: idolatry - the worship of idols

Etymology

From Latin 'daemonolatria', from Greek 'daimon' (demon) + 'latreia' (worship).
