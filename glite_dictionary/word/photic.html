photic

1. ADJ

/ ˈfoʊ.tɪk / (AmE), / ˈfəʊ.tɪk / (BrE)
relating to or caused by light, especially as it affects living organisms or environments.
of or relating to light

Examples

Exposure to photic stimuli can trigger migraines in some individuals.
Marine plants thrive best in the photic zone of the ocean where sunlight penetrates.
Researchers studied the photic response of algae under different wavelengths of light.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dark - lacking or devoid of light

Etymology

From Greek phōt-, phōs (light).
