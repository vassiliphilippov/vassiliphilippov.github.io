collage

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɑːʒ / (AmE), / kəˈlɑːʒ / (BrE)
A combination or collection of various items or elements, especially those that are diverse or unrelated, brought together to form a whole.
collection of diverse items or elements

Examples

The article presented a collage of opinions from experts around the world.
At her retirement party, they shared a collage of memories from her long career.
The documentary is essentially a collage of personal stories, interviews, and archival footage.

Synset Examples

a collage of memories

Etymology

From French 'collage', from 'coller' (to glue).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈlɑːʒ / (AmE), / kəˈlɑːʒ / (BrE)
A work of art made by assembling and sticking together pieces of paper, photographs, or other materials to create an artistic composition.
artwork made of assembled materials

Examples

The student created a collage using magazine clippings and colored paper.
Her bedroom wall was covered in a collage of family photos and postcards.
They admired the artist’s latest collage, which combined newspaper headlines with painted images.

Synset Examples

he used his computer to make a collage of pictures superimposed on a map

Related Terms

  • Synonym: montage - an artistic composition made from various elements

Etymology

From French 'coller' meaning 'to glue'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
