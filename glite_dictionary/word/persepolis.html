Persepolis

1. PROPN

/ pərˈsɛpəlɪs / (AmE), / pɜːˈsɛpəlɪs / (BrE)
an ancient city that was the capital of the Persian Empire, now in ruins in present-day Iran
ancient capital of the Persian Empire

Examples

Archaeologists have uncovered intricate carvings at Persepolis that reveal details about Persian royal ceremonies.
The ruins of Persepolis attract thousands of visitors each year interested in ancient history.
Alexander the Great famously burned Persepolis during his conquest of the Persian Empire.

Etymology

From Greek Persépolis, meaning 'city of the Persians.'
