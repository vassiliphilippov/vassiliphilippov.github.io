rubato

1. NOUN

/ ruˈbɑːtoʊ / (AmE), / ruːˈbɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
a musical technique involving flexible tempo, allowing slight speeding up or slowing down of the rhythm for expressive effect
flexible tempo in music

Examples

The pianist played with delicate rubato, giving the piece a deeply emotional character.
Many Romantic-era composers used rubato to enhance the expressiveness of their works.
The conductor encouraged the soloist to use rubato in the slower passages for greater effect.

Etymology

From Italian 'rubato', meaning 'robbed' (from 'rubare', to rob), referring to 'stolen time' in music.
