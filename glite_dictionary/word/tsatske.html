tsatske

1. NOUN

/ ˈtsɑt.skə / (AmE), / ˈtsæts.kə / (BrE)
(informal) An attractive, often unconventional or showy woman.
an attractive, showy woman
informal

Examples

She walked into the party like a real tsatske, turning everyone's heads with her sparkling outfit.
In her younger days, people used to call her quite the tsatske around the neighborhood.
Though she disliked the label, her bold style had earned her the reputation of being a tsatske among her friends.

Etymology

From Yiddish צאַצקע (tsatske), meaning 'trinket' or 'bauble', figuratively used for a lively, attractive woman.
