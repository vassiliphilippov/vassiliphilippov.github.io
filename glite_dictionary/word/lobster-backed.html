lobster-backed

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɑbstər ˌbækt / (AmE), / ˈlɒbstə ˌbækt / (BrE)
(historical, derogatory) Used to describe British soldiers during the American Revolutionary War, referencing their distinctive red coats.
historical term for British soldiers in red coats

Examples

The colonial rebels referred to the British troops as lobster-backed invaders because of their uniforms.
Several lobster-backed soldiers marched through the streets, their red coats bright against the gray morning.
Patriots jeered at the lobster-backed regiments as they advanced into Boston.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redcoat - British soldier, especially during the American Revolutionary War

Etymology

Reference to the red color of lobsters and the red coats worn by British soldiers.
