Ohio

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈhaɪ.oʊ / (AmE), / əʊˈhaɪ.əʊ / (BrE)
A major river in the eastern United States, formed in western Pennsylvania and flowing westward to join the Mississippi River.
river in the eastern US, Mississippi tributary

Examples

The Ohio River forms the southern border of Ohio, Indiana, and Illinois.
Many cities along the Ohio have developed as important river ports.
After heavy rains, the Ohio sometimes floods nearby towns.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Mississippi River - the largest river in North America, the main river into which the Ohio flows

Etymology

From the Seneca word *ohi:yo’*, meaning 'good river' or 'large creek'.

2. PROPN

/ oʊˈhaɪ.oʊ / (AmE), / əʊˈhaɪ.əʊ / (BrE)
A midwestern state in the north-central United States, located in the Great Lakes region; its capital is Columbus.
U.S. state in the Great Lakes region

Examples

The Buckeye State is another nickname for Ohio.
Lake Erie borders the northern edge of Ohio.
James grew up in Ohio before moving to California.

Etymology

From the Seneca word 'ohi:yo’ meaning 'good river' or 'large creek.'
