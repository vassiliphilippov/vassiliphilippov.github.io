impalement

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpeɪlmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpeɪlmənt / (BrE)
the act of piercing a body with a sharpened stake, especially as a form of punishment or torture
punishment by piercing with a stake

Examples

Historical accounts describe impalement as a gruesome method of execution.
The novel includes a harrowing scene of impalement used as punishment for traitors.
Museums sometimes display illustrations depicting impalement in medieval times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to impale - to pierce with a sharp object, especially a stake

Etymology

From French *empalement*, from *empaler* (to impale), from Latin *in* (in) + *palus* (stake).
