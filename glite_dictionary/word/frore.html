frore

1. ADJ

/ frɔːr / (AmE), / frɔːr / (BrE)
(Archaic) Very cold; frosty.
archaic: very cold; frosty
archaic

Examples

The night was frore, with a biting wind howling across the fields.
His hands were numb after walking home on that frore December evening.
A frore silence settled over the frozen pond in the early morning.

Synset Examples

whatever the evenings be — frosty and frore or warm and wet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frosty - very cold, covered with frost
  • Degree Lower: cold - low in temperature
  • Degree Higher: freezing - extremely cold, at or below the freezing point

Etymology

From Old English *froren*, past participle of *frēosan* (to freeze).
