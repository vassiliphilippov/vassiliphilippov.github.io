vaisya

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪʃjə / (AmE), / ˈvaɪsjə / (BrE)
a member of the third of the four traditional Hindu castes, consisting of commoners, farmers, and merchants
third Hindu caste; farmers, merchants

Examples

In traditional Hindu society, a vaisya was responsible for commerce and agriculture.
She learned about the roles of the vaisya during her studies of Indian history.
Many vaisyas became prominent traders in medieval India.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: brahmin - a member of the highest Hindu caste, the priesthood
  • Degree Higher: kshatriya - a member of the second Hindu caste, the warriors and rulers
  • Degree Lower: shudra - a member of the lowest Hindu caste, the laborers and servants

Etymology

From Sanskrit *vaiśya* (member of the third caste).
