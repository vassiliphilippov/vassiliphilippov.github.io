casualness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæʒuəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈkaʒjʊəlnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being casual; lack of regularity, formality, or strictness.
the quality of being casual or irregular

Examples

Her casualness about deadlines sometimes frustrated her colleagues.
There was a certain casualness in his manner that put everyone at ease.
The casualness of the meeting allowed people to speak freely.

Synset Examples

This casualness and irregularity of girls' labour was not the only result of the tacit permission accorded to them to work unapprenticed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: casual - relaxed, unconcerned, or informal

Etymology

Formed from 'casual' + '-ness', indicating the quality or state of being casual.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæʒuəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈkæʒjʊəlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being relaxed and unconcerned, or not formal or serious
the state of being relaxed or informal

Examples

His casualness at the interview made a strong impression on the panel.
She admired the casualness with which he handled the unexpected situation.
The casualness of his attire was inappropriate for such a formal event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: informality - the quality of being informal
  • Antonym: seriousness - the quality of being serious

Etymology

From "casual" + noun suffix "-ness".
