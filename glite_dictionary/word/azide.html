azide

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.zaɪd / (AmE), / ˈæz.aɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound containing the azido group (−N₃) combined with another element or radical
compound containing the azido group
medical

Examples

Sodium azide is commonly used as a propellant in automobile airbags.
Handle azide compounds with care, as some can be highly explosive.
Researchers studied the reaction between copper and an organic azide in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nitride - compound of nitrogen with another element

Etymology

From French *azote* ('nitrogen') + '-ide', indicating a binary compound.
