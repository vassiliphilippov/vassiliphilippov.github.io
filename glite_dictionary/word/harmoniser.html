harmoniser

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːrməˌnaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈhɑːmənaɪzə / (BrE)
a musician who sings or plays musical parts that form harmony with others in a group
musician providing harmony

Examples

Sarah is a talented harmoniser in the school's vocal ensemble.
The band recruited a new harmoniser to improve their sound.
As a harmoniser, Mark adds depth and richness to every performance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: soloist - a musician who performs alone or has a leading part
  • Synonym: harmonist - someone who performs or appreciates musical harmony

Etymology

From 'harmonize' (to bring into harmony) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːrməˌnaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈhɑːmənʌɪzə / (BrE)
a person who mediates to bring different things into harmonious agreement with each other
person who brings things into harmony

Examples

She acted as the harmoniser during the negotiation, ensuring both sides reached a fair agreement.
As a skilled harmoniser, he managed to settle disputes in the team quickly.
Without a harmoniser, the project group remained divided over their approach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mediator - a person who mediates between two parties
  • Synonym: arbitrator - a person who settles a dispute or brings about an agreement

Etymology

From 'harmonize' (to bring into harmony) + '-er' (agent suffix).
