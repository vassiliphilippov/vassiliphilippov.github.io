Saint Beda

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈbiːdə / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈbiːdə / (BrE)
(c. 672–735) An English monk and scholar, also known as the Venerable Bede, renowned for his writings on history and theology.
English monk and scholar (672–735)

Examples

The historical writings of Saint Beda remain influential centuries after his death.
Many regard Saint Beda as the father of English church history.
Scholars still study the works of Saint Beda for insight into early medieval England.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Venerable Bede - another name for Saint Beda, English monk and author

Etymology

From Latin 'Beda', the name of the scholar also known as the Venerable Bede.
