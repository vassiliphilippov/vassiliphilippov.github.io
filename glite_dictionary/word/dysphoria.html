dysphoria

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈfɔriə / (AmE), / dɪsˈfɔːriə / (BrE)
a state of unease or generalized dissatisfaction, often involving anxiety or depression
state of unease or dissatisfaction
medical

Examples

Following the loss of his job, a sense of dysphoria lingered for weeks.
The patient described episodes of dysphoria that interfered with her daily functioning.
Some medications may cause dysphoria as a side effect.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: euphoria - a feeling of great happiness or well-being

Etymology

From Greek δυσφορία (dysphoria), from δυσ- (dys-, 'bad') and φορέω (phoreō, 'to bear').
