patsy

1. NOUN

/ ˈpætsi / (AmE), / ˈpætsi / (BrE)
a person who is easily deceived or taken advantage of; a victim or scapegoat
a person easily deceived or victimized

Examples

He became the patsy in their elaborate scheme, taking the blame for everyone else's mistakes.
Don't be a patsy; always double-check the facts before you agree to anything.
The con artists were looking for a patsy to fall for their latest scam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dupe - a person who is easily tricked or deceived
  • Synonym: scapegoat - a person who is unfairly blamed for something
  • Synonym: victim - a person harmed or tricked by others

Etymology

Possibly from the Italian given name 'Patsy,' a diminutive of 'Patsio', used by American comedians in the early 20th century.
