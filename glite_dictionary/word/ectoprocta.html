Ectoprocta

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.təˈprɑk.tə / (AmE), / ˌɛk.təˈprɒk.tə / (BrE)
a phylum or subphylum of small aquatic invertebrates, commonly known as bryozoans, characterized by having their anus located outside the ring of tentacles
a phylum of aquatic invertebrates (bryozoans)
medical

Examples

The reef was covered with colonies of Ectoprocta, creating a delicate lace-like pattern.
Researchers are studying the evolutionary relationships between Ectoprocta and other aquatic invertebrates.
Specimens of Ectoprocta are commonly found in both marine and freshwater environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bryozoa - another name for the phylum Ectoprocta (moss animals)

Etymology

From Greek ektos (outside) + proktos (anus), referring to the position of the anus outside the tentacular crown.
