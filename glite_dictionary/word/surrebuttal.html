surrebuttal

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɜrɪˈbʌtl̩ / (AmE), / ˌsʌrɪˈbʌtl̩ / (BrE)
(Law) A pleading made by the plaintiff in response to the defendant's rebutter.
plaintiff's response to a rebutter in law
legal

Examples

The attorney filed a surrebuttal to address new arguments presented by the defense.
After reviewing the defendant's rebutter, the judge allowed the plaintiff to submit a surrebuttal.
The court's decision hinged on points made in the plaintiff's final surrebuttal.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: rebutter - the defendant's written response in a legal proceeding

Etymology

Formed from 'sur-' (meaning over or in addition) and 'rebuttal', modeled on legal language patterns.
