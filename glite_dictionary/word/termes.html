Termes

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɜrmiːz / (AmE), / ˈtɜːmiːz / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Termitidae, comprising certain species of termites.
type genus of Termitidae (termites)

Examples

The genus Termes includes some of the best-known termite species studied by entomologists.
Recent research has provided new insights into the social structure of Termes.
Fossils attributed to Termes have been found in ancient sediment layers.

Etymology

From Latin 'termes', meaning 'woodworm' or 'termite'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrmiz / (AmE), / ˈtɜːmiːz / (BrE)
a genus of termites, the type genus of the family Termitidae, comprising various species of social insects known for feeding on wood.
a genus of termites (Termitidae family)
medical

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species belonging to the genus Termes in the rainforest.
Many Termes colonies play an important role in decomposing dead wood in ecosystems.
The soldiers of Termes have strong mandibles for defending their nests.

Etymology

From Latin *termes* (woodworm, termite).
