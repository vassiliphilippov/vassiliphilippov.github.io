two-faced

1. ADJ

/ ˈtuˌfeɪst / (AmE), / ˈtuːˌfeɪst / (BrE)
deliberately deceptive by pretending to have one set of feelings while acting under the influence of another; insincere or hypocritical.
deceptive or hypocritical

Examples

His colleagues discovered he was two-faced, saying kind things in person but criticizing them behind their backs.
I don't trust politicians who seem two-faced during election campaigns.
She acted friendly to everyone but was actually quite two-faced and spread rumors.

Synset Examples

she was a deceitful scheming little thing
a double-dealing double agent
a double-faced infernal traitor and schemer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceitful - deliberately misleading or lying
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful
  • Synonym: hypocritical - pretending to have virtues or beliefs one doesn't possess

Etymology

Formed by analogy from the literal 'having two faces', figuratively meaning duplicitous or deceitful.
