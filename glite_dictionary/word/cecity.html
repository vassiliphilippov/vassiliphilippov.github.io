cecity

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛsəti / (AmE), / ˈsiːsɪti / (BrE)
the state of being blind; loss or lack of sight
blindness; lack of sight

Examples

Prolonged deficiency of vitamin A can lead to cecity in children.
The writer used cecity as a metaphor for ignorance in her novel.
Early intervention may sometimes prevent total cecity after an injury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blindness - the condition of being unable to see

Etymology

From Latin 'caecitas' (blindness), from 'caecus' (blind).
