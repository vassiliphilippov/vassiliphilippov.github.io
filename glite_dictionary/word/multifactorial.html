multifactorial

1. ADJ

/ ˌmʌltiˌfækˈtɔriəl / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪfækˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
(of a condition or process) involving or dependent on several factors or causes, especially when referring to a disease resulting from the interaction of multiple genes or influences.
involving several factors or causes
medical

Examples

The development of heart disease is often multifactorial, involving both genetic predisposition and lifestyle choices.
Autism spectrum disorders are considered to be multifactorial in origin, resulting from complex gene-environment interactions.
Researchers are studying the multifactorial nature of diabetes to better understand its prevention and treatment.

Etymology

From Latin multi- (many) + factor (doer, maker) + -ial (adjectival suffix).
