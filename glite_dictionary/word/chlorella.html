chlorella

1. NOUN

/ kləˈrɛlə / (AmE), / kləˈrɛlə / (BrE)
a genus of single-celled green algae, often used as a food supplement and in scientific research
a genus of green algae

Examples

Many health supplements contain chlorella because of its high nutrient content.
Researchers study chlorella for its potential to purify wastewater.
Some people add powdered chlorella to their smoothies for a nutritional boost.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'chloros' (green) and the diminutive suffix '-ella'.
