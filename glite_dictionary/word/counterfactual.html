counterfactual

1. ADJ

/ ˌkaʊntərˈfæktʃuəl / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntəˈfaktʃuəl / (BrE)
relating to or expressing something that is contrary to known facts or reality, often used to describe hypothetical situations or statements
contrary-to-fact; hypothetical

Examples

The historian posed a counterfactual scenario to explore how events might have unfolded differently.
In philosophy, counterfactual statements are used to discuss what could have happened under different conditions.
Scientists sometimes use counterfactual reasoning when constructing models of climate change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypothetical - based on or serving as a hypothesis
  • Antonym: factual - concerned with what is actually the case

Etymology

From counter- (against) + factual (based on fact).
