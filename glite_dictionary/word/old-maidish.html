old-maidish

1. ADJ

/ ˈoʊld ˌmeɪdɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈəʊld ˌmeɪdɪʃ / (BrE)
Excessively prim, fussy, or concerned with propriety and neatness, in a manner stereotypically associated with a spinster.
primly fastidious; overly fussy

Examples

Her old-maidish insistence on perfectly arranged cutlery amused the younger family members.
He was known for his old-maidish attention to the smallest household chores.
The teacher's old-maidish attitude made her classroom spotless but a little intimidating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prim - very formal and correct in behavior or appearance
  • Synonym: fussy - excessively concerned with details

Etymology

From 'old maid', an expression for an unmarried older woman, plus the suffix '-ish'.
