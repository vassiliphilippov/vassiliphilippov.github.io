Helleborus foetidus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛləˈbɔːrəs ˈfiːtɪdəs / (AmE), / ˌhɛlɪˈbɔːrəs ˈfiːtɪdəs / (BrE)
a species of hellebore with divided leaves, unpleasant odor, and irritant properties if ingested
a species of malodorous hellebore plant

Examples

Gardeners often grow Helleborus foetidus for its unusual foliage and winter blooms.
The leaves of Helleborus foetidus should not be ingested, as they can cause irritation.
I was surprised by the strong scent coming from the Helleborus foetidus in the shade garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stinking hellebore - common name for Helleborus foetidus

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Helleborus' and species epithet 'foetidus', meaning 'foul-smelling'.
