xeric

1. ADJ

/ ˈzɪrɪk / (AmE), / ˈzɪərɪk / (BrE)
(of an environment or organism) characterized by or requiring very little moisture; dry or arid.
dry or moisture-deficient (esp. environment)

Examples

Many cacti thrive in xeric habitats due to their ability to store water.
The xeric landscape made agriculture difficult for early settlers.
Conservationists study xeric grasslands to understand plant adaptation to drought.

Synset Examples

deserts provide xeric environments

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: mesic - characterized by a moderate amount of moisture (of environments)
  • Degree Lower: hydric - containing or relating to an abundance of moisture (of environments)
  • Synonym: arid - extremely dry, especially having little or no rain (of climates or environments)

Etymology

From Greek 'xēros' meaning 'dry'.
