cinder

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪndər / (AmE), / ˈsɪndə / (BrE)
a small piece of partly burned coal, wood, or other fuel that has stopped giving off flames but still has combustible matter in it
fragment left after a fire

Examples

The campfire burned down to glowing cinders by midnight.
After the explosion, only cinders and debris remained on the ground.
He stepped carefully to avoid the hot cinders on the hearth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ash - the powdery residue left after the burning of a substance

Etymology

From Old English *sinder* (dross, slag), from Proto-Germanic *sendra* (slag, cinder).
