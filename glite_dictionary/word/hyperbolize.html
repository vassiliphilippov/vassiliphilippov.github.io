to hyperbolize

1. VERB

/ haɪˈpɜrbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / haɪˈpɜː.bə.laɪz / (BrE)
to represent something as greater, more important, or more extreme than it really is; to exaggerate
exaggerate or overstate

Examples

Politicians often hyperbolize the effects of their policies to gain public support.
Try not to hyperbolize when telling the story; just stick to the facts.
She hyperbolized her vacation experience, making it sound much more exciting than it was.

Synset Examples

tended to romanticize and exaggerate this ‘gracious Old South’ imagery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exaggerate - to magnify beyond the limits of truth; overstate
  • Antonym: to understate - to represent as less significant or important than is the case

Etymology

From Greek hyperbolē (excess, exaggeration) via Late Latin hyperbolizare.
