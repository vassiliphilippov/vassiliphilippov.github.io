engine

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
a wheeled vehicle powered by its own engine, used to pull trains along railway tracks; locomotive
railway vehicle that pulls trains

Examples

The steam engine pulled a long line of freight cars across the countryside.
The train's engine was replaced after it broke down near the station.
Museum visitors admired the restored Victorian engine on display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: locomotive - a powered railway vehicle used for pulling trains

Etymology

From Old French engin (skill, device), from Latin ingenium (natural talent, skill, device).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛndʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
A machine that converts fuel into mechanical energy to perform work, especially to power vehicles or machinery.
machine converting fuel to mechanical work

Examples

The car's engine stopped working suddenly in the middle of the highway.
Steam locomotives were powered by a coal-fired engine.
Our new lawn mower has a more efficient engine than the previous model.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motor - device that converts energy into mechanical motion

Etymology

From Old French 'engin' (skill, cleverness, war machine), from Latin 'ingenium' (natural quality, talent, device).
