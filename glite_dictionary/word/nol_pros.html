nol pros

1. NOUN

/ nɑl ˈprɑs / (AmE), / nɒl ˈprɒs / (BrE)
an entry made in a court record indicating that the plaintiff or prosecutor will not pursue the case further
legal term for dropping a case
legal

Examples

The prosecution entered a nol pros after reviewing new evidence in the case.
Due to lack of witnesses, the judge accepted the nol pros filed by the district attorney.
The defense was relieved when the nol pros was officially recorded in court.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nolle prosequi - formal legal declaration that a case will not be prosecuted further

Etymology

Abbreviation of Latin nolle prosequi ('to be unwilling to pursue').
