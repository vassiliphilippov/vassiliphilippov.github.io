Hesperiphona

1. NOUN

/ hɛsˌpɛrɪˈfoʊnə / (AmE), / hɛsˌpɛrɪˈfəʊnə / (BrE)
A genus of finches known as evening grosbeaks, found in North America and Central America.
genus of evening grosbeaks (bird)

Examples

The flock of Hesperiphona perched high in the pine trees, adding vibrant color to the winter landscape.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a rare Hesperiphona during their early morning walk.
The Hesperiphona is noted for its striking plumage and musical calls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evening grosbeak - a North American songbird with a large bill and yellow plumage

Etymology

From Greek 'hesperos' (evening) and 'phōnē' (voice), referring to their dawn/dusk singing behavior.

/ ˌhɛspəˈrɪfoʊnə / (AmE), / ˌhɛspəˈrɪfɒnə / (BrE)
A genus of North American finches in the family Fringillidae, known as evening grosbeaks.
genus of finches (evening grosbeaks)

Examples

The Hesperiphona genus includes the well-known evening grosbeak found in North America.
Researchers are studying the evolutionary history of Hesperiphona to understand finch diversification.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot several members of Hesperiphona during the winter migration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evening grosbeak - a North American songbird with a thick bill, member of genus Hesperiphona

Etymology

From Greek 'hesperos' (evening) and 'phōnē' (voice), referring to the evening grosbeak.
