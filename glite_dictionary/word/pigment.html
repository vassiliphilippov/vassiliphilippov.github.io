pigment

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪɡmənt / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡmənt / (BrE)
a substance or mixture used as a coating to color, decorate, or protect a surface, typically drying to form a hard layer
substance used to color or coat surfaces

Examples

The artist carefully chose each pigment to achieve the perfect shade for her landscape painting.
Some protective coatings contain a waterproof pigment that extends the life of the surface.
After mixing the pigment with oil, he applied it as a thick paint on the canvas.

Synset Examples

artists use ‘paint’ and ‘pigment’ interchangeably

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paint - a colored substance spread over a surface that dries to leave a thin decorative or protective coating

Etymology

From Latin pigmentum (coloring, paint), from pingere (to paint).

2. VERB

/ ˈpɪɡmənt / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡmənt / (BrE)
(of a substance or tissue) to acquire or develop color, usually by absorbing or depositing pigment.
to acquire or develop color

Examples

Some amphibians can pigment their skin to blend in with their environment.
Iron deposits can pigment certain tissues in the body, leading to visible color changes.
The coral began to pigment after being exposed to stronger sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color - to give color to or become colored

Etymology

From Latin pigmentum (coloring matter).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɪɡmənt / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡmənt / (BrE)
a dry substance, usually a powder, that produces color when mixed with a liquid such as oil or water, and is used in making paints, inks, and dyes
dry substance for coloring (powder for paints, dyes)

Examples

The artist carefully blended the red pigment with oil to create a vibrant color.
Some cosmetics contain natural pigment extracted from plants and minerals.
The restoration process involved matching the original pigment used in the ancient frescoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dye - a substance used to change the color of something (especially textiles)

Etymology

From Latin pigmentum (coloring matter), from pingere (to paint).

/ ˈpɪɡ.mənt / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡ.mənt / (BrE)
to color or dye something with a pigment; to impart color by the addition of pigment
to color or dye with pigment

Examples

Restorers carefully pigmented the faded areas to match the original colors.
Modern techniques allow photographers to digitally pigment old black-and-white images.

5. NOUN

/ ˈpɪɡmənt / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡmənt / (BrE)
a substance that gives color to plant or animal tissues
coloring substance in tissues

Examples

Melanin is the pigment responsible for skin color in humans.
Chlorophyll is the green pigment found in plants.
Some animals can change their pigment to blend into their environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dye - a natural or synthetic substance used to add color, especially in textiles
  • Antonym: bleach - a substance that removes color or whitens

Etymology

From Latin 'pigmentum' (coloring), from 'pingere' (to paint).
