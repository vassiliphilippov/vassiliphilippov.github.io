numerosity

1. NOUN

/ ˌnuːməˈrɑːsəti / (AmE), / ˌnjuːməˈrɒsəti / (BrE)
a large number or quantity; the state of being numerous
a large number or quantity

Examples

A numerosity of stars filled the night sky, making it look almost like day.
Researchers observed a significant numerosity of errors in the experimental data.
The numerosity of choices at the buffet overwhelmed the guests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multiplicity - the state of being multiple or having many parts

Etymology

From Latin 'numerositas' (numerousness), from 'numerosus' (numerous), from 'numerus' (number).
