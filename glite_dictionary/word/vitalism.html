vitalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪtəlˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈvaɪtəlɪzəm / (BrE)
The doctrine that life is governed by a vital principle distinct from physical or chemical processes.
the doctrine that life is due to a vital principle

Examples

Many early biologists supported vitalism before modern science provided chemical explanations for life.
The decline of vitalism marked a shift toward understanding biological processes in physical terms.
His paper discusses the influence of vitalism on 19th-century medical theories.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mechanism - the view that natural processes, including life, are mechanically determined

Etymology

From Latin 'vita' (life) + '-ism'.
