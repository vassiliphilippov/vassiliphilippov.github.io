Trifolium repens

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈfoʊliəm ˈriːpənz / (AmE), / trɪˈfəʊlɪəm ˈriːpɛnz / (BrE)
a species of clover with creeping stems, white or pinkish flowers, and bright green leaves, commonly known as white clover, widely found in lawns and pastures
species of white-flowered clover plant

Examples

Trifolium repens is often used as ground cover in parks and gardens.
Farmers plant Trifolium repens to improve soil nitrogen levels for future crops.
Children sometimes search Trifolium repens patches for rare four-leaf clovers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white clover - common name for Trifolium repens, a widespread clover species

Etymology

From Latin: 'trifolium' meaning clover (literally 'three-leaf'), 'repens' meaning creeping.
