tappa

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːpə / (AmE), / ˈtæpə / (BrE)
The thin, fibrous bark of the paper mulberry or Pipturus albidus, used especially in making bark cloth in the Pacific Islands.
fibrous bark used for bark cloth

Examples

Traditional artisans use tappa to create ceremonial bark cloth garments.
The tappa of the paper mulberry tree is prized for its strength and flexibility.
In Polynesian culture, tappa plays a crucial role in rituals and celebrations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tapa - cloth made from the bark of the paper mulberry

Etymology

From Tahitian and other Polynesian languages, related to tapa (bark cloth).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːpə / (AmE), / ˈtæpə / (BrE)
a paper-like cloth made in the South Pacific by beating the inner bark of certain trees, such as the paper mulberry.
South Pacific bark cloth

Examples

The ceremonial dress was made from finely decorated tappa cloth.
Artisans demonstrated how to create tappa by pounding bark with wooden mallets.
In Fiji, traditional weddings often feature gifts of tappa to the families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tapa - bark cloth made in Oceania

Etymology

From Polynesian languages, especially Samoan and Tongan 'tapa', referring to bark cloth.
