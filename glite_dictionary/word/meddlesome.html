meddlesome

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɛdəlˌsəm / (AmE), / ˈmɛd.əl.səm / (BrE)
Tending to interfere in other people's affairs in an annoying or unwelcome way.
interfering in others' affairs

Examples

Her meddlesome neighbors were always trying to find out what was going on in her house.
He found his aunt's meddlesome questions quite irritating during dinner.
A meddlesome colleague kept involving herself in issues that were none of her business.

Synset Examples

an interfering old woman
bustling about self-importantly making an officious nuisance of himself
busy about other people's business

Related Terms

  • Synonym: officious - assertively interfering in a bothersome way
  • Synonym: interfering - intervening in affairs that do not concern one

Etymology

From 'meddle' (to interfere) + '-some' (having a tendency to).
