chancy

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃænsi / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːnsi / (BrE)
involving or depending on chance; uncertain and unpredictable
uncertain; dependent on chance

Examples

Moving to a new city without a job offer is a chancy decision.
The outcome of the experiment proved more chancy than the researchers expected.
He made a chancy investment that ended up paying off.

Synset Examples

a chancy appeal at best
getting that job was definitely fluky
a fluky wind
an iffy proposition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: risky - involving risk or danger; hazardous
  • Antonym: certain - sure to happen; definite

Etymology

From chance + -y, first recorded in the 16th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtʃænsi / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːnsi / (BrE)
involving uncertainty or risk; likely to have an unpredictable or unfavorable outcome
involving risk or uncertainty

Examples

Investing in start-up companies can be a chancy business.
The weather makes planning an outdoor wedding a bit chancy.
It was a chancy decision to travel without travel insurance.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: risky - involving the possibility of danger or loss
