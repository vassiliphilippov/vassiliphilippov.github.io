eugenics

1. NOUN

/ juˈdʒɛnɪks / (AmE), / juːˈdʒɛnɪks / (BrE)
The study of or belief in improving the genetic quality of a human population, especially by selective breeding or controlled reproduction.
study of improving human genetics by selective breeding

Examples

Controversy surrounds the use of eugenics to control human reproduction.
Many early 20th-century policies were influenced by the principles of eugenics.
Modern genetics has distanced itself from the discredited practices associated with eugenics.

Etymology

From Greek 'eugenēs' (well-born), from 'eu-' (good) + 'genos' (birth, race).
