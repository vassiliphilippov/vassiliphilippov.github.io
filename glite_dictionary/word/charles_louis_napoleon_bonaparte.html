Charles Louis Napoleon Bonaparte

1. PROPN

/ ʃɑrl ˈluːi næˈpoʊliən ˌboʊnəˈpɑrt / (AmE), / ʃɑːlz ˈluːi nəˈpəʊliən ˌbəʊnəˈpɑːt / (BrE)
(1808–1873) Nephew of Napoleon I, who served as Emperor of the French from 1852 to 1871, ruling as Napoleon III.
Emperor of the French, 1852–1871

Examples

Charles Louis Napoleon Bonaparte became Emperor of the French after a successful coup d'état in 1851.
Historians often compare the leadership styles of Napoleon I and Charles Louis Napoleon Bonaparte.
During his reign, Charles Louis Napoleon Bonaparte oversaw major industrial and urban development in France.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Napoleon III - The imperial title assumed by Charles Louis Napoleon Bonaparte
