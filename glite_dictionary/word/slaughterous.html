slaughterous

1. ADJ

/ ˈslɔt̬ərəs / (AmE), / ˈslɔːtərəs / (BrE)
involving or accompanied by great bloodshed or carnage
involving bloodshed or carnage

Examples

The rebels launched a slaughterous attack on the city, leaving devastation in their wake.
Historians recall the slaughterous battles as some of the darkest days of the conflict.
The movie depicted a slaughterous confrontation between the rival gangs.

Synset Examples

this bitter and sanguinary war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanguinary - involving or causing much bloodshed
  • Antonym: peaceful - free from violence or bloodshed

Etymology

From 'slaughter' (to kill in a violent way) + -ous (adjectival suffix).
