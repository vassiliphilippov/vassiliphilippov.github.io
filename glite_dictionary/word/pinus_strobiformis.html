Pinus strobiformis

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪ.nəs stroʊ.bɪˈfɔːr.mɪs / (AmE), / ˈpaɪ.nəs strəʊ.bɪˈfɔː.mɪs / (BrE)
A medium-sized pine tree native to northwestern Mexico, with dark brown, furrowed bark when mature.
medium-sized pine native to northwestern Mexico

Examples

The Pinus strobiformis thrives in the mountainous regions of northwestern Mexico.
Foresters study the growth patterns of Pinus strobiformis to support conservation efforts.
The bark of mature Pinus strobiformis trees is dark brown and deeply furrowed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mexican white pine - common name for Pinus strobiformis

Etymology

From Latin: Pinus (pine) and strobiformis (cone-shaped, from Greek strobilos, pinecone).
