outflow

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌfloʊ / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.fləʊ / (BrE)
a natural movement of water, especially underground water, flowing out from a particular place
natural movement of water flowing outward

Examples

The outflow of groundwater from the hillside created a small stream.
Scientists measured the outflow from the underground spring over several months.
After heavy rains, the outflow from the cave increased dramatically.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inflow - the movement of water or another substance into a place

Etymology

From 'out' + 'flow', meaning to flow outwards.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌfloʊ / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.fləʊ / (BrE)
the movement or process of something flowing out, especially from a place, system, or container
the process of flowing out

Examples

The heavy rain caused a rapid outflow of water from the reservoir.
A sudden outflow of funds worried investors.
The outflow of air from the vents cooled the entire room.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inflow - the process of flowing in

Etymology

From out + flow, modeled after similar compounds.

3. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌfloʊ / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.fləʊ / (BrE)
the act or process of something, especially a liquid or gas, flowing out of a container or place
discharge or flow outward

Examples

An outflow of water was detected from the reservoir after the heavy rains.
Engineers monitored the outflow of gas from the pipeline to prevent any hazards.
The rapid outflow of air sounded an alarm in the laboratory.

Synset Examples

they tried to stop the escape of gas from the damaged pipe
he had to clean up the leak

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inflow - the movement of something into a place, especially a liquid or gas

Etymology

From prefix 'out-' (outside) + 'flow' (move as a fluid), first recorded in the 18th century.
