pedagogy

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛdəˌɡoʊdʒi / (AmE), / ˈpɛdəɡɒdʒi / (BrE)
the method and practice of teaching, especially as an academic subject or theoretical concept
the method and practice of teaching

Examples

His main area of research is pedagogy and teacher development.
Innovative pedagogy can transform the learning experience for students.
Many universities now require courses in pedagogy for aspiring educators.

Synset Examples

he received no formal education
our instruction was carefully programmed
good classroom teaching is seldom rewarded

Related Terms

  • Synonym: education - the process of teaching or learning, especially in a school or college
  • Synonym: instruction - the act of teaching or providing knowledge

Etymology

From Greek *paidagōgia*, from *paidagōgos* (teacher, slave who escorted children to school).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛdəˌɡoʊdʒi / (AmE), / ˈpɛdəɡɒdʒi / (BrE)
the profession or practice of teaching, or the study of methods and principles of education
the profession or practice of teaching

Examples

She has dedicated her career to advancing pedagogy in elementary education.
Modern pedagogy emphasizes student-centered learning over rote memorization.
Many universities now offer degrees in pedagogy to train future teachers.

Synset Examples

he prepared for teaching while still in college
pedagogy is recognized as an important profession

Related Terms

  • Synonym: education - the process of teaching or learning, especially in a school or college
  • Synonym: instruction - the act of teaching or providing knowledge

Etymology

From Greek *paidagōgia* (office of a pedagogue), from *paidagōgos* (slave who led a child to school).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɛdəˌɡɑdʒi / (AmE), / ˈpɛdəɡɒdʒi / (BrE)
the method and practice of teaching, especially as an academic subject or theoretical concept
the method and theory of teaching

Examples

Modern pedagogy emphasizes the importance of student-centered learning.
Her research focuses on innovative approaches to pedagogy in higher education.
Many educators attend workshops to improve their pedagogy and classroom techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teaching - the act or profession of instructing others
  • Synonym: instruction - the process of teaching or being taught

Etymology

From Greek paidagōgia (education, attendance on boys), from paidagōgos (teacher, slave who attended schoolboys).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05668782-n
Synset POS: n
