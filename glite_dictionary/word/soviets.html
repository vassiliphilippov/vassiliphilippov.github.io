Soviets

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊ.vi.ɪts / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ.vi.ɪts / (BrE)
the government or authorities of the former Soviet Union
government of the former Soviet Union

Examples

The Soviets announced a new trade agreement with France in 1976.
During the Cold War, the Soviets were seen as a global superpower.
The Soviets launched the first artificial satellite, Sputnik, in 1957.

Synset Examples

the Soviets said they wanted to increase trade with Europe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Soviet Union - the former federation of communist countries in Eastern Europe and Asia (1922–1991)

Etymology

From Russian 'sovet' (council), used to denote governing councils in the USSR.
