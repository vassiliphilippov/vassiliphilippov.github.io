broadcloth

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɔdˌklɔθ / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːdˌklɒθ / (BrE)
A densely woven woolen or cotton fabric with a smooth, lustrous finish, traditionally used for clothing and uniforms.
densely woven fabric with lustrous finish

Examples

The tailor recommended using broadcloth for the suit because of its luxurious feel and durability.
She prefers broadcloth shirts for their smooth texture and crisp appearance.
The uniforms were made from dark blue broadcloth that held up well under daily use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tweed - rough-surfaced woolen fabric
  • Synonym: flannel - soft woven wool or cotton fabric

Etymology

From Middle English *brode clothe* (broad cloth); 'broad' refers to the original wide loom width.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrɔdˌklɔθ / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːdˌklɒθ / (BrE)
a closely woven fabric with a narrow crosswise rib, traditionally made of silk or synthetic fibers
closely woven ribbed fabric

Examples

The tailor recommended broadcloth for its smooth finish and elegant drape.
She wore a dress made of blue broadcloth on the warm summer evening.
High-quality broadcloth shirts are popular in professional wardrobes due to their comfort and appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poplin - a strong cotton or synthetic fabric with a similar ribbed texture

Etymology

From 'broad' + 'cloth', originally referring to cloth wider than standard loom widths.
