marauding

1. ADJ

/ məˈrɔdɪŋ / (AmE), / məˈrɔːdɪŋ / (BrE)
(especially of people or animals) Going about in search of things to steal or people to attack.
roving in search of things to steal or attack

Examples

After dark, a marauding wolf pack prowled the outskirts of the village.
The marauding soldiers looted every house they passed.
Farmers guarded their crops from marauding animals during harvest season.

Synset Examples

bands of marauding Indians
predatory warfare
a raiding party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predatory - seeking to exploit or oppress others
  • Antonym: peaceful - not involving conflict or aggression

Etymology

From French 'marauder' (to roam in search of plunder).

2. NOUN

/ məˈrɔdɪŋ / (AmE), / məˈrɔːdɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of raiding and pillaging, especially in a violent or aggressive manner.
raiding and pillaging

Examples

The terrified villagers hid from the marauding of the invading soldiers.
Stories of the marauding during the war spread fear throughout the countryside.
With each nightfall, the risk of marauding grew, forcing the community to take extra precautions.

Synset Examples

Set during the last year of the Ugandan dictator Idi Amin's brutal regime, Waiting exposes the fear and courage of a small, close-knit community uncertain of what the edicts of a madman and the marauding of his uncontrollable army will bring with each coming day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plundering - the act of stealing goods, especially by force
  • Synonym: pillaging - the act of robbing a place using violence, especially in war
  • Antonym: protecting - the act of keeping something or someone safe

Etymology

From the verb 'maraud' (French marauder), meaning to roam in search of things to steal or people to attack.
