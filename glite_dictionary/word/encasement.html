encasement

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈkeɪsmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkeɪsmənt / (BrE)
the act of enclosing something in or as if in a case or covering
the act of enclosing in a case

Examples

The fragile artifact was given an encasement to protect it from dust and damage.
During the renovation, workers removed the old encasement surrounding the pipes.
A thick glass encasement was installed to display the crown jewels securely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enclosure - the act of enclosing something

Etymology

From en- (put in) + case + -ment (forming nouns of action or result).
