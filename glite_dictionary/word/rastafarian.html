Rastafarian

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɑːstəˈferiən / (AmE), / ˌrastəˈfɑːrɪən / (BrE)
A follower of Rastafari, a religion originating in Jamaica that regards Haile Selassie I (Ras Tafari) of Ethiopia as divine.
follower of the Rastafari religion

Examples

Many Rastafarians wear their hair in dreadlocks as part of their spiritual practice.
The influence of Rastafarian beliefs is seen in reggae music and its lyrics.
She became a Rastafarian after studying the life of Haile Selassie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rasta - informal term for a Rastafarian
  • Degree Lower: Christian - adherent of Christianity, a major religion

Etymology

From Ras Tafari, birth name of Haile Selassie I, Emperor of Ethiopia.

2. ADJ

/ ˌrɑːstəˈfɛriən / (AmE), / ˌrastəˈfɑːrɪən / (BrE)
of or relating to Rastafarianism or its followers.
relating to Rastafarianism or Rastafarians

Examples

The Rastafarian community has a rich tradition of music and spiritual practice.
She wore her hair in Rastafarian dreadlocks to express her cultural identity.
Many Rastafarian beliefs emphasize peace, equality, and a connection to Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rastafarianism - religious movement originating in Jamaica that venerates Haile Selassie I

Etymology

From Ras Tafari, the pre-coronation title of Haile Selassie I, emperor of Ethiopia, regarded as a messianic figure by Rastafarians.

3. NOUN

/ ˌræstəˈfɛriən / (AmE), / ˌrastəˈfɑːrɪən / (BrE)
a follower of Rastafarianism, a religion and social movement that originated in Jamaica promoting Afrocentric beliefs and the worship of Haile Selassie I
follower of Rastafarianism

Examples

Many Rastafarians believe in the spiritual use of cannabis.
The music of Bob Marley is closely associated with Rastafarian culture.
As a Rastafarian, he observes a strict dietary code known as ital.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rastafari - religion and social movement originating in Jamaica

Etymology

From Rastafari, a title for Haile Selassie I, combined with -an.
