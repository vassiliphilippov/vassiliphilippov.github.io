comatose

1. ADJ

/ ˈkoʊməˌtoʊs / (AmE), / ˈkəʊmətəʊs / (BrE)
in a state of deep and prolonged unconsciousness, especially as a result of injury or illness, and unresponsive to external stimuli
in a deep state of unconsciousness
medical

Examples

After the accident, the driver remained comatose for several weeks.
The patient was comatose and required continuous monitoring in the intensive care unit.
She spoke softly to her comatose brother, hoping he might hear her.

Synset Examples

a comatose patient

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unconscious - not awake and aware of and responding to one's environment
  • Antonym: alert - quick to notice and respond to things

Etymology

From Greek kōma (deep sleep), via French comateux.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkoʊməˌtoʊs / (AmE), / ˈkəʊmətəʊs / (BrE)
relating to or in a state of coma; deeply unconscious or unresponsive as if in a coma.
in or relating to a coma

Examples

The patient was found in a comatose state after the accident.
She remained comatose for several days before regaining consciousness.
Doctors are monitoring his comatose condition closely.

Synset Examples

comatose breathing
comatose state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unconscious - not awake and unaware of and responding to one's environment

Etymology

From French comateux, from coma (deep unconsciousness) + -ose (adjective forming suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02855463-a
Synset POS: a
