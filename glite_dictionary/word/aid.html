aid

1. VERB

/ eɪd / (AmE), / eɪd / (BrE)
to help or support someone or something to achieve a goal or deal with a problem
to help or support

Examples

The paramedics quickly aided the injured passengers at the scene.
Several countries agreed to aid the region after the flood devastated local communities.
Volunteers have been aiding families displaced by the fire.

Synset Examples

Everyone helped out during the earthquake
Can you help me carry this table?
She never helps around the house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: help - to make it easier or possible for someone to do something
  • Synonym: assist - to give support or help

Etymology

From Latin 'adjutare' (to help); influenced by Old French 'aidier' (to help).
