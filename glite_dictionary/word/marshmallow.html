marshmallow

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrʃˌmɛloʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːʃˌmæləʊ / (BrE)
a soft, sweet, spongy confection made from sugar, gelatin, and corn syrup, often dusted with powdered sugar
soft, sweet, spongy confection

Examples

He melted a marshmallow over the campfire for his s'more.
The dessert was topped with mini marshmallows for extra sweetness.
She enjoys adding a marshmallow to her hot chocolate on cold days.

Etymology

Originally referring to a sweet made from the root of the marsh mallow plant (Althaea officinalis); modern confection no longer uses plant extract.
