ergotine

1. NOUN

/ ˈɝːɡəˌtin / (AmE), / ˈɜːɡəˌtiːn / (BrE)
a powerful astringent alkaloid extracted from ergot, formerly used to induce contractions of the uterus
alkaloid from ergot used to contract the uterus
medical

Examples

The physician administered ergotine to help control the patient's postpartum bleeding.
Before safer alternatives were discovered, ergotine was commonly used in obstetrics.
Extraction of ergotine from ergot marked a significant advance in 19th-century pharmacology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ergot - a fungus of the genus Claviceps from which ergotine is derived

Etymology

From ergot (a fungus) + -ine (chemical suffix), indicating a compound derived from ergot.
