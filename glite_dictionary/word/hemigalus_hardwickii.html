Hemigalus hardwickii

1. NOUN

/ hɛˌmɪɡələs hɑrdˈwɪkiː / (AmE), / hɛˌmɪɡaləs hɑːdˈwɪkiː / (BrE)
A small, slender East Indian civet species known as the banded palm civet, native to Southeast Asia.
a species of East Indian civet

Examples

The elusive Hemigalus hardwickii is rarely seen in the wild due to its nocturnal habits.
Researchers in Malaysia documented the behavior of Hemigalus hardwickii in its natural habitat.
Unlike other civets, Hemigalus hardwickii primarily feeds on insects and small vertebrates.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining genus name 'Hemigalus' and species name honoring British naturalist Thomas Hardwicke.
