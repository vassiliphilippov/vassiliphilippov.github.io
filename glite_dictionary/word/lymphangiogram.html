lymphangiogram

1. NOUN

/ lɪmˈfændʒioʊˌɡræm / (AmE), / lɪmˈfændʒiəˌɡræm / (BrE)
an X-ray image of lymph nodes and lymph vessels obtained after injecting a radiopaque substance
medical X-ray of lymph vessels and nodes
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered a lymphangiogram to visualize the patient's lymphatic system.
A lymphangiogram can help diagnose blockages or tumors in lymph vessels.
After receiving the injection, the patient waited for the lymphangiogram procedure to begin.

Etymology

From Greek 'lymph' (clear fluid) + 'angeion' (vessel) + 'gramma' (record, something written).
