trepan

1. NOUN

/ trɪˈpæn / (AmE), / trɪˈpan / (BrE)
a surgical instrument with a circular blade used for removing sections of bone, especially from the skull
surgical tool for cutting bone
medical

Examples

The surgeon used a trepan to create an opening in the patient's skull.
Early neurosurgery often involved the use of a trepan to relieve pressure.
A vintage trepan is displayed in the medical history museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trephine - a similar surgical instrument used to remove a circular section of tissue or bone

Etymology

From French *trépan*, from Medieval Latin *trepanum*, from Greek *trypanon* (borer, auger).

2. VERB

/ trɪˈpæn / (AmE), / trɪˈpan / (BrE)
to cut a hole in something, especially the skull, using a surgical instrument called a trepan
cut a hole with a surgical instrument
medical

Examples

The surgeon had to trepan the patient's skull to relieve the pressure.
In ancient medicine, doctors would sometimes trepan to treat head injuries.
Modern neurosurgery rarely requires doctors to trepan the bone except in severe cases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trephine - to remove a circular section of tissue or bone, especially the skull, with a similar instrument

Etymology

From French 'trépaner', from Medieval Latin 'trepanare', from Greek 'trypanon' (borer, auger).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛpæn / (AmE), / ˈtrɛpan / (BrE)
a tool or drill used for cutting circular holes around a center, especially in surgery or engineering
circular hole-cutting drill

Examples

The surgeon used a trepan to remove a small disc of bone from the patient's skull.
In archaeology, a trepan may be used to take core samples from stone.
The mechanic selected a trepan to drill a perfect circle in the metal plate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trephine - a surgical instrument for cutting out circular sections, especially of bone

Etymology

From French *trépan* and Latin *trepanum*, from Greek *trypanon* (borer, auger).
