volcanology

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɑːlkəˈnɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌvɒlkəˈnɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of volcanoes, lava, magma, and related geological phenomena
study of volcanoes and related processes

Examples

She decided to specialize in volcanology after witnessing a volcanic eruption as a child.
Modern volcanology combines fieldwork, laboratory research, and satellite monitoring to study volcanic activity.
A degree in volcanology can lead to careers in research, education, or hazard management.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: geology - the science dealing with the Earth's physical structure and substance

Etymology

From Latin 'volcanus' (Vulcan, the Roman god of fire) + '-logy' (the study of).
