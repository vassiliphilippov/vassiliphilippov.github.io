remaking

1. NOUN

/ riˈmeɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / riˈmeɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of making something again or creating a new version of something, especially a film, song, or story
the act of creating something again

Examples

The studio announced the remaking of a classic horror movie.
Her remaking of the traditional recipe gave it a modern twist.
The remaking of old musicals has become increasingly popular in recent years.

Synset Examples

it is a remake of an old film

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remake - a new version of something, especially a film or song

Etymology

From 're-' (again) + 'make' + '-ing' (noun-forming suffix).
