Artaxerxes

1. PROPN

/ ˌɑːrtəgˈzɜːrksiːz / (AmE), / ˌɑːtəkˈzɜːksiːz / (BrE)
the name of several kings of ancient Persia, notably Artaxerxes II (died 359 BC), who quelled several revolts and concluded peace with Sparta
king of ancient Persia, especially Artaxerxes II

Examples

Artaxerxes was known for his diplomatic skill in dealing with the Greek states.
Historians often debate the legacy of Artaxerxes in Persian and Greek relations.
Several revolts occurred during Artaxerxes' reign, but he managed to keep control of his empire.

Etymology

From Greek Artaxerxēs, from Old Persian Artaxšaçā (meaning 'whose rule is through truth').

2. PROPN

/ ɑːr.təgˈzɜːrk.siːz / (AmE), / ˌɑː.təgˈzɜːk.siːz / (BrE)
the name of several Persian kings, especially Artaxerxes I (reigned 465–424 BC), who sanctioned the practice of Judaism in Jerusalem
Persian king; especially Artaxerxes I (465–424 BC)

Examples

Artaxerxes played a significant role in the history of the Persian Empire.
According to the Bible, Artaxerxes allowed the Jews to return and rebuild Jerusalem.
The reign of Artaxerxes is often studied by historians interested in ancient Persia.

Etymology

From Greek Artaxerxēs, from Old Persian Artaxšaçā (meaning 'whose reign is through truth').
