Fremontodendron

1. NOUN

/ friˌmɑntoʊˈdɛndrən / (AmE), / friˌmɒntəˈdɛndrən / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen shrubs native to southwestern North America, commonly known as flannelbush, recognized for their large, bright yellow flowers and velvety leaves.
genus of shrubs (flannelbush)

Examples

The Fremontodendron in our backyard is covered in yellow blossoms each spring.
Botanists admire the resilience of Fremontodendron in dry, rocky soils.
Many gardens in California feature Fremontodendron for its showy flowers and drought tolerance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flannelbush - common name for Fremontodendron shrubs

Etymology

Named after John C. Frémont, American explorer, and Greek dendron (tree).

2. NOUN

/ friːˌmɒntəˈdɛndrən / (AmE), / friːˌmɒntəˈdɛndrən / (BrE)
A genus of North American flowering shrubs in the mallow family, known as flannelbush, characterized by their bright yellow flowers and soft, fuzzy leaves.
genus of flowering shrubs (flannelbush)

Examples

The Fremontodendron thrives in dry, rocky soils of California and the American Southwest.
Gardeners admire the showy yellow blooms of the Fremontodendron each spring.
Leaves of the Fremontodendron are soft and fuzzy, giving rise to its common name, flannelbush.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flannelbush - common name for shrubs of the genus Fremontodendron

Etymology

Named after John C. Frémont, American explorer, with -dendron from Greek 'dendron' meaning 'tree'.
