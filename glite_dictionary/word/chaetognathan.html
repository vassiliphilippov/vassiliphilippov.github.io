chaetognathan

1. ADJ

/ kiːtəgˈnæθən / (AmE), / kiːtəgˈneɪθən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of arrowworms (marine worms of the phylum Chaetognatha)
relating to arrowworms

Examples

The chaetognathan species are important predators in planktonic ecosystems.
Researchers observed a decline in chaetognathan populations due to changing water temperatures.
Their unique jaw structures are a defining feature of chaetognathan anatomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrowworm - a marine predatory worm of the phylum Chaetognatha

Etymology

From Greek 'chaite' (hair) + 'gnathos' (jaw), referring to the bristle-jawed worms of the phylum Chaetognatha.
