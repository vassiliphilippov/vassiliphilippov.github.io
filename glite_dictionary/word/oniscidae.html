Oniscidae

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈnɪsɪdiː / (AmE), / əʊˈnɪsɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of isopod crustaceans commonly known as woodlice or pillbugs
family of woodlice (crustaceans)
medical

Examples

Members of the Oniscidae family are commonly known as woodlice.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Oniscidae during their expedition.
The Oniscidae are important for soil ecology due to their role in decomposing organic matter.

Etymology

From New Latin Oniscidae, based on genus Oniscus, from Greek oniskos (woodlouse).

2. NOUN

/ oʊˈnɪsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / əʊˈnɪsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of isopod crustaceans commonly known as woodlice, found in damp habitats and notable for their segmented, oval bodies.
family of woodlice (isopod crustaceans)

Examples

Members of the Oniscidae family are commonly found under rocks and logs in moist areas.
The Oniscidae display a wide variety of shapes and coloration across different genera.
Biologists have classified more than a thousand species within the Oniscidae family.

Etymology

From New Latin Oniscus (a genus name, ultimately from Greek ὀνίσκος 'woodlouse') + -idae (a taxonomic suffix).
