dakoity

1. NOUN

/ dəˈkɔɪti / (AmE), / dəˈkɔɪti / (BrE)
Robbery committed by a gang of armed criminals, especially as formerly prevalent in India and South Asia.
robbery by an armed gang, esp. in South Asia
legal

Examples

The police launched a major operation to prevent dakoity in the rural villages.
He was arrested on charges of dakoity after a gang robbed the jewelry store.
New security measures were introduced to curb dakoity in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robbery - the act of taking property unlawfully from a person or place by force or threat
  • Synonym: burglary - illegal entry into a building to commit a crime, especially theft

Etymology

From Hindi ḍakaitī, from ḍakait (robber, bandit).
