synchroneity

1. NOUN

/ sɪnˈkroʊ.ni.ɪ.ti / (AmE), / sɪnˈkrəʊ.ni.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality or state of occurring at the same time; simultaneousness.
the occurrence of things at the same time

Examples

The synchroneity of the dancers' movements impressed the judges at the competition.
Many scientific experiments depend on the synchroneity of multiple timed processes.
The concert's special effects were carefully arranged for perfect synchroneity with the music.

Synset Examples

the drug produces an increased synchrony of the brain waves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synchrony - the state of being synchronous; simultaneous occurrence
  • Antonym: asynchrony - the state of not occurring at the same time

Etymology

From Greek 'syn' (together) + 'chronos' (time), by analogy with 'synchrony'.
