Caryophyllaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɛriəˌfaɪəˈleɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌkærɪəˌfɪliˈɑːsiː / (BrE)
a large family of herbaceous plants or subshrubs, usually with stems swollen at the nodes, commonly called the pink or carnation family
a family of flowering plants (the pink family)

Examples

The Caryophyllaceae family includes popular garden plants such as carnations and pinks.
Many members of the Caryophyllaceae have swollen nodes on their stems.
Botanists often study the Caryophyllaceae for their diverse floral structures.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Caryophyllus, from Greek karyon (nut) and phyllon (leaf).
