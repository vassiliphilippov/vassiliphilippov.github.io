sideboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌbɔːd / (BrE)
a removable board attached to the side of a wagon or cart to increase its capacity for carrying goods
removable board on wagon to increase capacity

Examples

The farmer added a sideboard to his wagon to carry more hay across the fields.
By raising the sideboard, it was possible to transport larger loads without them spilling out.
Old wooden wagons often featured a detachable sideboard for extra hauling capacity during harvest time.

Etymology

From 'side' + 'board', reflecting its function as a board on the side of a vehicle.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdbɔːd / (BrE)
A piece of furniture, typically placed in a dining room, with shelves and drawers used for storing tableware and serving food.
dining room furniture with shelves and drawers

Examples

The antique sideboard in the dining room was filled with fine china and silverware.
She arranged the glasses neatly on the sideboard before the dinner party began.
We bought a new sideboard to match our oak dining table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buffet - A similar piece of furniture for storing tableware and serving food

3. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdbɔːd / (BrE)
a board forming the side of a bed or crib, enclosing and supporting the structure
board forming side of bed or crib

Examples

The old crib's sideboard was loose and needed repairs.
She sanded the sideboard of the antique bed before repainting it.
For safety, ensure the sideboard is properly attached to the crib.

Etymology

Formed from 'side' + 'board', referring to a board on the side.
