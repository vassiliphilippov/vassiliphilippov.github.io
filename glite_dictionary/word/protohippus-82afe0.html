protohippus

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊtoʊˈhɪpəs / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəˈhɪpəs / (BrE)
an extinct genus of horse from the Pliocene epoch, about the size of a donkey
extinct Pliocene horse genus

Examples

The fossil remains of protohippus suggest it was well-adapted to grassland environments.
Scientists believe that protohippus was significantly smaller than modern horses.
Museums often display skeletons of protohippus to illustrate the evolution of horses.

Etymology

From Greek 'protos' (first) + 'hippos' (horse).
