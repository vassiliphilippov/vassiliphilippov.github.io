Polyhymnia

1. PROPN

/ ˌpɑliˈhɪmniə / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈhɪmniə / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the Muse of sacred poetry, singing, and dance.
the Muse of sacred poetry and dance in Greek mythology

Examples

In ancient art, Polyhymnia is often depicted with a pensive expression, symbolizing deep thought.
According to legend, Polyhymnia inspired poets and musicians with her divine gift.
The mural in the museum shows Polyhymnia holding a finger to her lips, embodying sacred silence.

Etymology

From Greek Πολυύμνια (Poluýmnia), from πολύς (polus, 'many') + ὕμνος (hymnos, 'hymn, song')
