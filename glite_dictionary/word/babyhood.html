babyhood

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪ.biˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈbeɪ.bɪˌhʊd / (BrE)
the period or state of being a baby; the earliest stage in a person's life
the period of being a baby

Examples

She could not remember anything from her babyhood.
The photos of his babyhood were kept in a special album.
During babyhood, children experience rapid growth and development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infancy - the state or period of early childhood or babyhood

Etymology

From 'baby' + 'hood', where 'hood' denotes a state or period.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪ.bi.hʊd / (AmE), / ˈbeɪ.bi.hʊd / (BrE)
the period in a person's life when they are a baby; the earliest stage of childhood.
the period when someone is a baby

Examples

She has no memories of her babyhood but loves seeing her old photographs.
His fascination with music began in babyhood when his parents played lullabies every night.
Modern medicine has greatly improved survival rates during babyhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infancy - the early stage of childhood, especially the period before the ability to walk

Etymology

From 'baby' + '-hood', with '-hood' denoting a state or condition; common in Middle English formation.
