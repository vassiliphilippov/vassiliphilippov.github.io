knotted

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɑːtɪd / (AmE), / ˈnɒtɪd / (BrE)
tied or fastened into a knot or knots.
tied in a knot

Examples

She wore a scarf that was knotted elegantly at her neck.
The sailor checked that all the ropes were securely knotted.
He handed me a knotted handkerchief full of coins.

Synset Examples

his carefully knotted necktie

Related Terms

  • Antonym: untied - not tied or fastened with a knot
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈnɑtɪd / (AmE), / ˈnɒtɪd / (BrE)
having a rough, twisted appearance with knobs or knots, especially when describing old trees or hands.
having knobs or knots

Examples

The old man's knotted hands showed years of hard work in the fields.
A knotted walking stick lay beside the fireplace, its surface worn and uneven.
The ancient oak tree had knotted branches stretching skyward, gnarled by centuries of storms.

Synset Examples

gnarled and knotted hands
a knobbed stick

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gnarled - twisted with a rough, rugged appearance, especially of trees or hands
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface, lacking roughness or knots

Etymology

From knot + -ed, indicating the presence of knots.
