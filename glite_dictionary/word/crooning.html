crooning

1. NOUN

/ ˈkruːnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkruːnɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of singing popular songs in a soft, sentimental, and often intimate manner
sentimental singing style

Examples

The crooning of the jazz singer filled the smoky nightclub with warmth.
He made his mark in music with his smooth crooning and heartfelt lyrics.
Her gentle crooning of the old tune brought back a wave of nostalgia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to croon - to sing in a soft, low voice

Etymology

From 'croon' (to sing softly), probably of Middle Dutch origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkruː.nɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkruː.nɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of singing gently and softly, usually in a low tone
soft, gentle singing

Examples

Her crooning soon put the child to sleep.
The crooning of the singer created a romantic atmosphere in the restaurant.
He listened to the gentle crooning from his grandmother as she rocked him to sleep.

Synset Examples

her crooning soon put the child to sleep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: singing - the act of producing musical tones with the voice

Etymology

From the verb 'croon', which likely derives from Middle Dutch 'kronen' (to lament, mourn).
