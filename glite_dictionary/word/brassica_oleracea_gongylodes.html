Brassica oleracea gongylodes

1. NOUN

/ brəˌsɪkə oʊˌlɛriˈeɪʃə ˌgɒŋɡɪˈloʊdiːz / (AmE), / brəˌsɪkə əˌlɛəriˈeɪʃə ˌgɒŋɡɪˈləʊdiːz / (BrE)
A cultivated variety of cabbage grown for its enlarged, turnip-shaped edible stem, commonly known as kohlrabi.
cultivated plant with edible swollen stem

Examples

Brassica oleracea gongylodes is widely cultivated across Europe and Asia for its crisp, edible stem.
Gardeners appreciate Brassica oleracea gongylodes for its fast growth and resistance to pests.
In culinary uses, Brassica oleracea gongylodes can be eaten raw or cooked in a variety of dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kohlrabi - vegetable; German name for Brassica oleracea gongylodes

Etymology

Latin: Brassica (cabbage genus) + oleracea (vegetable) + gongylodes (turnip-shaped, from Greek 'gongylos', round).
