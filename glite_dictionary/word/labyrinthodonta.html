Labyrinthodonta

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæb.əˌrɪn.θəˈdɒn.tə / (AmE), / ˌlæb.ɪˌrɪn.θəˈdɒn.tə / (BrE)
an extinct group of amphibians, typically resembling large salamanders or crocodiles, characterized by a solid, flattened skull and conical teeth, found from the Devonian to Triassic periods
extinct group of prehistoric amphibians

Examples

The fossil record indicates that Labyrinthodonta flourished from the Devonian to the Triassic period.
Paleontologists recently uncovered a nearly complete skeleton of a Labyrinthodonta species.
The distinctive, maze-like structure of the teeth helped scientists identify the remains as belonging to Labyrinthodonta.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek labyrinthos (labyrinth) + odōn (tooth), referring to the complex tooth structure.

2. NOUN

/ ˌlæbəˌrɪnθəˈdɑntə / (AmE), / ˌlæbərɪnθəˈdɒntə / (BrE)
an extinct order of amphibians, typically large and heavy-bodied, that lived from the Devonian to Triassic periods, characterized by a solid, flattened skull and conical teeth
extinct order of large amphibians

Examples

Scientists have discovered fossils belonging to Labyrinthodonta in rocks dating to the Early Permian.
The teeth of Labyrinthodonta show a complex, maze-like pattern under the microscope.
Many specimens of Labyrinthodonta resembled modern crocodiles in both size and appearance.

Etymology

From Greek 'labýrinthos' (labyrinth) + 'odous, odont-' (tooth), referring to the maze-like tooth structure.
