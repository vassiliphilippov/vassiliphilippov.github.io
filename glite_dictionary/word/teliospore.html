teliospore

1. NOUN

/ təˈlaɪ.əˌspɔr / (AmE), / tɛˈlɪ.əˌspɔː / (BrE)
a thick-walled resting spore formed in the final stage of the life cycle of certain fungi, such as rust fungi
resting spore of rust fungi
medical

Examples

The teliospore stage enables the rust fungus to survive unfavorable environmental conditions.
Researchers examined the teliospores under a microscope to study their structure.
In the fungus's complex life cycle, the teliospore gives rise to basidiospores.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chlamydospore - a thick-walled fungal spore formed by rounding up of a cell

Etymology

From Greek 'telos' (end) + 'spora' (seed, spore); refers to its formation at the end of the fungus's life cycle.
