anima

1. NOUN

/ ˈænɪmə / (AmE), / ˈanɪmə / (BrE)
(in Jungian psychology) the inner self, especially the unconscious feminine aspect in a man's psyche, as opposed to the external persona.
inner self in Jungian psychology

Examples

In Jungian theory, the anima represents the unconscious feminine side of a male's personality.
The exploration of one's anima can lead to greater self-understanding and psychological growth.
Dreams often reveal images of the anima, which may guide a person towards inner balance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: animus - the masculine aspect of the female psyche in Jungian psychology

Etymology

From Latin anima (soul, life, spirit), adopted into psychology by Carl Jung.
