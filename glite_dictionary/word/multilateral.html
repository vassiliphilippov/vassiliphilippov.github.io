multilateral

1. ADJ

/ ˌmʌl.tiˈlæt̬.ɚ.əl / (AmE), / ˌmʌl.tɪˈlæt.ər.əl / (BrE)
involving or having many sides, parts, or participants; especially relating to agreements or actions among several countries or parties
having many sides or participants

Examples

The countries signed a multilateral trade agreement to promote economic cooperation.
A multilateral discussion involves input and negotiation from all participating parties.
The organization encourages multilateral efforts to address climate change.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bilateral - having two sides or involving two parties
  • Degree Lower: unilateral - relating to or involving only one side or party
  • Synonym: polyhedral - having many flat sides (geometry)

Etymology

From Latin multi- (many) + lateralis (of sides).
