oedometer

1. NOUN

/ iˈɑdəmɪtər / (AmE), / iːˈɒdəmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument for measuring the rate and amount of consolidation of a soil specimen under pressure
instrument for measuring soil consolidation

Examples

The geotechnical lab used an oedometer to test the soil sample's consolidation properties.
A malfunction in the oedometer resulted in inconsistent pressure readings during the experiment.
Before constructing the foundation, engineers performed oedometer tests to assess soil stability.

Etymology

From Greek 'oidos' (swelling) + 'meter' (measure).
