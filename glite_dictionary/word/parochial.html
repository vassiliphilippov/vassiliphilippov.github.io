parochial

1. ADJ

/ pəˈroʊkiəl / (AmE), / pəˈrəʊkiəl / (BrE)
having a limited or narrow outlook or scope, often focusing only on local or minor concerns rather than broader or more general issues.
narrow in outlook or scope

Examples

Critics argued that his parochial view prevented the city from adapting to global changes.
The debate was marked by a parochial focus, ignoring the wider national implications.
She was frustrated by the organization's parochial attitude toward innovation.

Synset Examples

little sympathy with parochial mentality
insular attitudes toward foreigners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insular - having a narrow or limited outlook; isolated

Etymology

From Late Latin parochialis (of a parish), from parochia (parish).

WordNet ID: oewn-00640125-s

2. ADJ

/ pəˈroʊkiəl / (AmE), / pəˈrəʊkiəl / (BrE)
relating to or supported by a church parish, especially in the context of churches and their local administrative areas.
relating to a church parish

Examples

Many children in the neighborhood attend the local parochial school.
The church's parochial hall is used for community events.
Their charity work is funded primarily through parochial sources.

Synset Examples

parochial schools

Etymology

From Late Latin parochialis (of a parish), from parochia (parish).
