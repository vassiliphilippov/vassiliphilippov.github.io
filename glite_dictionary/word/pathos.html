pathos

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪθoʊs / (AmE), / ˈpæθɒs / (BrE)
a quality in art, literature, speech, or events that arouses feelings of pity, sympathy, or sorrow
quality that evokes pity or sadness

Examples

The play was full of pathos, moving the audience to tears several times.
Her voice carried such pathos that even the hardest hearts were touched.
The writer relied on pathos to appeal to the readers' emotions.

Synset Examples

the film captured all the pathos of their situation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pity - a feeling of sorrow for someone's misfortune
  • Synonym: empathy - the ability to understand and share another person's feelings
  • Antonym: bathos - (in literature or speech) an effect of anticlimax created by unintentional lapse in mood from the sublime to the trivial

Etymology

From Greek páthos (suffering, feeling, emotion).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪˌθɑs / (AmE), / ˈpæθɒs / (BrE)
a quality in a situation, writing, or art that evokes feelings of pity or compassion
quality evoking pity or compassion

Examples

The film's ending was filled with pathos, leaving the audience in tears.
She spoke with such pathos that everyone felt her pain.
The novel is remarkable for the pathos it conveys about the plight of the poor.

Synset Examples

the blind are too often objects of pity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pity - a feeling of sorrow and compassion caused by the suffering of others
  • Synonym: empathy - the ability to understand and share the feelings of another
  • Antonym: callousness - lack of empathy or compassion for others

Etymology

From Greek páthos (suffering, feeling, emotion).
