pachuco

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtʃukoʊ / (AmE), / pəˈtʃuːkəʊ / (BrE)
(in the United States) a young Mexican-American, especially in the 1940s, known for flamboyant dress, rebellious attitude, and association with urban street gangs.
Mexican-American youth subculture member (1940s)

Examples

The pachuco styled his hair with pomade and wore a bright zoot suit.
During the 1940s, pachucos embodied a distinctive youth culture in Los Angeles.
He was fascinated by the history and fashion of the pachuco.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zoot suiter - a person who wears a zoot suit, often associated with the pachuco subculture

Etymology

From Mexican Spanish 'pachuco', of uncertain origin, popularized in the 1940s.
