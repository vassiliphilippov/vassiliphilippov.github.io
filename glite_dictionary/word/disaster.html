disaster

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈzæstər / (AmE), / dɪˈzɑːstə / (BrE)
A sudden event, such as an accident or natural catastrophe, that causes great damage, loss, or destruction.
event causing great loss or damage

Examples

The hurricane proved to be a major disaster for the entire region.
The company was unprepared, and the product launch turned into a financial disaster.
Fortunately, everyone escaped unharmed from the train derailment, which could have been a much worse disaster.

Synset Examples

the whole city was affected by the irremediable calamity
the earthquake was a disaster

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catastrophe - a sudden and widespread disaster
  • Synonym: calamity - an event causing great and often sudden damage or distress
  • Antonym: blessing - something bringing good luck or happiness

Etymology

From Italian 'disastro', from Greek 'dus-' (bad) + 'astron' (star), originally meaning 'ill-starred event'.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈzæstɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈzɑːstə / (BrE)
A sudden event or situation causing great damage, suffering, or complete failure.
event causing great ruin or failure

Examples

The oil spill was an environmental disaster that affected the coastline for years.
Their attempt to organize a surprise party turned into a complete disaster.
After the failure of the project, the company was left in financial disaster.

Synset Examples

lack of funds has resulted in a catastrophe for our school system
his policies were a disaster

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catastrophe - a sudden and severe disaster or misfortune
  • Synonym: calamity - an event causing great and often sudden damage or distress
  • Antonym: success - the accomplishment of an aim or purpose; opposite of disaster

Etymology

From Italian 'disastro', from Latin 'dis-' (apart, away) + 'astro' (star), originally meaning 'ill-starred event'.

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈzæstɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈzɑːstə / (BrE)
A sudden event, such as an accident or natural catastrophe, that causes great damage, loss, or destruction.
catastrophic event causing damage or loss

Examples

The earthquake was a disaster that left thousands without homes.
Her carefully planned party turned into a disaster when the power went out.
Officials declared the oil spill an environmental disaster of unprecedented scale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catastrophe - a sudden, extensive disaster
  • Synonym: calamity - an event causing great and sudden damage
  • Antonym: miracle - an extraordinary, fortunate event

Etymology

From Italian 'disastro', from Greek 'dis' (bad) + 'astron' (star), originally meaning an ill-starred event.
