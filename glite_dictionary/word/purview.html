purview

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːˌvjuː / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.vjuː / (BrE)
the range or scope of authority, responsibility, influence, or knowledge that someone or something possesses
range or scope of authority or activity

Examples

Matters of foreign policy are outside the purview of the local government.
These decisions fall under the purview of the ethics committee.
Environmental issues are now within the purview of the new ministry.

Synset Examples

It is beyond the horizon of present knowledge

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman purveu, from Old French pourveu, past participle of pourvoir (to provide), used in legal contexts to introduce a clause ('be it provided').
