whitlow

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪtˌloʊ / (AmE), / ˈwɪt.ləʊ / (BrE)
A painful, pus-filled infection occurring at the end of a finger or toe, especially around the nail.
finger or toe infection near the nail
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her painful swelling as a whitlow.
A neglected whitlow can sometimes require medical intervention or drainage.
Frequent handwashing can help prevent a whitlow from developing after minor injuries.

Etymology

From Old English 'hwithlāw', of uncertain origin, possibly related to 'white' (referring to the appearance) and 'lōh' (an inflamed spot).
