dorsoventrally

1. ADV

/ ˌdɔːrsoʊˈvɛntrəli / (AmE), / ˌdɔːsəʊˈvɛntrəli / (BrE)
In a direction from the back (dorsal) to the belly (ventral), or along the back-to-belly axis.
in a back-to-belly direction

Examples

Some fish are dorsoventrally compressed, giving them a flattened shape.
The leaves grew dorsoventrally, lying close to the ground surface.
Certain organs develop dorsoventrally during embryonic growth.

Synset Examples

the ray has a dorsoventrally flattened body

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dorsoventral - relating to or extending along the back-to-belly axis

Etymology

From Latin 'dorsum' (back) and 'venter' (belly) with suffix '-ally' to form an adverb.
