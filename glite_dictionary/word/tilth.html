tilth

1. NOUN

/ tɪlθ / (AmE), / tɪlθ / (BrE)
Land that has been tilled, especially for planting crops; the condition of soil as prepared for sowing.
land prepared for planting

Examples

After months of hard work, the farmer admired the rich tilth of his fields.
Good tilth is essential for healthy plant growth and high crop yields.
Rain can sometimes damage the delicate tilth formed after spring plowing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tillage - the preparation of land for growing crops
  • Antonym: fallow - land left unplanted after plowing

Etymology

From Old English *tilth*, meaning 'cultivation, tillage'.

2. NOUN

/ tɪlθ / (AmE), / tɪlθ / (BrE)
the physical condition of soil, especially in relation to its suitability for supporting plant growth
condition of soil for plant growth

Examples

After plowing and harrowing, the field reached an ideal tilth for planting corn.
Good tilth is essential to successful gardening, as it helps the roots absorb nutrients and water.
Heavy rain can ruin the tilth of freshly prepared seedbeds by compacting the soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soil - upper layer of earth in which plants grow

Etymology

From Old English 'tilth' (act of tilling, cultivation), related to 'till' (to prepare soil for crops).
