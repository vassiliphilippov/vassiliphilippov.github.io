geography

1. NOUN

/ dʒiˈɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / dʒiˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
The study of the Earth's surface, its physical features, climate, soil, vegetation, and how humans interact with these elements.
study of the Earth's surface and human interaction

Examples

She has always been fascinated by geography and loves studying maps of different countries.
Our geography class is learning about the effects of climate on population distribution.
He decided to major in geography at university to better understand the world's landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geology - science concerned with the Earth's physical structure and substance

Etymology

From Greek *geōgraphia* (earth description), from *gē* (earth) + *-graphia* (writing, description).
