pool

1. NOUN

/ pul / (AmE), / puːl / (BrE)
a small area or a mass of something (such as light, liquid, or objects) that collects or is spread on a surface
localized area or mass of something collected

Examples

She found herself standing in a pool of sunlight at the window.
There was a pool of oil beneath the leaking car.
The children played around a pool of scattered toys.

Synset Examples

he stood in a pool of light
his chair sat in a puddle of books and magazines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puddle - a small accumulation of liquid, especially on the ground
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08682839-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ pul / (AmE), / puːl / (BrE)
A small and often shallow body of still water, typically natural or formed in a depression.
a small body of still water

Examples

We found a beautiful pool hidden among the trees in the forest.
After the rain, a shallow pool formed at the bottom of the field.
The children played at the edge of the pool, tossing stones into the calm water.

Synset Examples

the pond was too small for sailing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pond - a small body of still water, usually smaller than a lake

Etymology

From Old English *pōl* (small body of water), related to Dutch *poel*.

11. NOUN

Examples
