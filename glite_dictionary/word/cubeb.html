cubeb

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuː.bɛb / (AmE), / ˈkjuː.bɛb / (BrE)
A tropical Southeast Asian shrubby vine (Piper cubeba) of the pepper family, cultivated for its spicy, berrylike fruits used as a spice and in medicine.
tropical vine with spicy fruits

Examples

The chef added a pinch of cubeb to enhance the sauce's flavor.
In traditional medicine, cubeb has been used for its unique properties.
Aromatic cubeb berries were once popular in spice blends across Europe.

Etymology

From Arabic *kabābah*, via Old French *cubebe*.

2. NOUN

/ kjuˈbɛb / (AmE), / kjuˈbiːb / (BrE)
A cigarette containing cubeb, formerly used especially for treating throat ailments.
a cigarette made with cubeb

Examples

During the 1920s, doctors sometimes recommended cubeb for respiratory problems.
He lit a cubeb after dinner instead of his usual cigarette.
The old advertisement boasted that a cubeb could soothe a sore throat.

Related Terms

3. NOUN

/ ˈkjuː.bɛb / (AmE), / ˈkjuː.bɛb / (BrE)
the dried, spicy fruit of the cubeb vine, used as a spice, in medicine, perfumery, or sometimes smoked in cigarettes
spicy fruit used medicinally and in perfumery

Examples

The chef added cubeb to the sauce for a unique, spicy flavor.
Traditional medicine sometimes uses cubeb as an ingredient for treating certain ailments.
Perfume makers value the aroma of cubeb in creating exotic scents.

Etymology

From Arabic *kubāba*, via Medieval Latin *cubeba*.
