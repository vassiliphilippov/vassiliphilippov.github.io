stint

1. NOUN

/ stɪnt / (AmE), / stɪnt / (BrE)
a very small sandpiper, especially the smallest species found in North America
a small American sandpiper

Examples

The stint foraged for insects along the muddy shoreline.
During migration, flocks of stints can be seen resting on the beaches.
The smallest stint species breeds in northern Alaska and Siberia.

2. NOUN

/ stɪnt / (AmE), / stɪnt / (BrE)
a period of time spent doing a particular job or activity, especially one that is limited or fixed in duration
limited time spent on a task or job

Examples

She completed a six-month stint as a volunteer in South America.
After his stint in the army, he returned to civilian life with new skills.
He did a brief stint working at a law firm before starting his own business.

Synset Examples

there were stretches of boredom
he did a stretch in the federal penitentiary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stretch - a period of time spent engaged in a particular activity, especially in prison

Etymology

Of Old Norse origin; related to 'stinta' meaning 'to restrain or limit'.

3. VERB

/ stɪnt / (AmE), / stɪnt / (BrE)
to supply, give, or use something only in small or restricted amounts; to be sparing with
be sparing with; restrict amount

Examples

She never stints with praise when her children do well in school.
The company had to stint on office supplies to save money this quarter.
During the war, families were forced to stint their use of sugar and butter.

Synset Examples

stint with the allowance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to skimp - to use less of something than is necessary to economize
  • Antonym: lavish - to give or use something in generous or excessive amounts

Etymology

From Old English *styntan* (to blunt, make dull); related to Old Norse *stynta* (to shorten).

4. NOUN

/ stɪnt / (AmE), / stɪnt / (BrE)
a period of time spent doing a particular job or activity, especially as part of one's work duties
a period spent doing a job or task

Examples

During his stint at the factory, he learned valuable skills.
She completed a brief stint as an assistant in a law firm last summer.
After a two-year stint in the military, he returned to university.

Synset Examples

her stint as a lifeguard exhausted her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shift - a period of work during which someone has specific duties
  • Synonym: spell - a period of time spent doing an activity

Etymology

Probably from Old English 'styntan' (to make blunt), later developing the sense of 'limit' or 'allotted portion'.
