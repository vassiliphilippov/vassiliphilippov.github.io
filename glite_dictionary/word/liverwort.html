liverwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪvɚˌwɔrt / (AmE), / ˈlɪvəˌwɔːt / (BrE)
a small green nonvascular plant of the division Marchantiophyta, typically found in moist habitats and resembling mosses
a small nonvascular plant similar to moss

Examples

The damp rocks by the river were covered with liverwort and moss.
A liverwort can often be found in shady, moist forests.
Scientists study liverworts to understand the evolution of land plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moss - a small flowerless plant that typically grows in dense green clumps or mats

Etymology

From Old English 'liferwyrt', meaning 'liver plant', due to its supposed medicinal use for liver ailments.
