sangoma

1. NOUN

/ sɑnˈɡoʊmə / (AmE), / sænˈɡəʊmə / (BrE)
A traditional healer and spiritual guide in southern African cultures, especially among Zulu people.
traditional healer in southern Africa

Examples

The sangoma advised the family on spiritual matters and conducted traditional healing rituals.
As a respected sangoma, she treated many people from nearby villages using herbal remedies.
During the ceremony, the sangoma entered a trance to connect with the ancestors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shaman - a person regarded as having access to the world of spirits
  • Synonym: witch doctor - a traditional healer using magic or folk remedies

Etymology

From Zulu isigq̣i, later sangoma (diviner, healer).
