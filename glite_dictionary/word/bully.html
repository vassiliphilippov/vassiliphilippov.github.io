bully

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊli / (AmE), / ˈbʊli / (BrE)
(archaic) A hired thug or ruffian employed to intimidate or harm others.
hired thug or ruffian (archaic)
archaic

Examples

The wealthy merchant kept a bully nearby to discourage competitors from interfering with his business.
Local politicians sometimes employed a bully to silence their critics in the streets.
In the 18th century, it was not uncommon for a bully to be paid handsomely for his services.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thug - a violent person, especially a criminal hired to intimidate or attack others

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʊli / (AmE), / ˈbʊli / (BrE)
a person who habitually seeks to harm or intimidate those whom they perceive as vulnerable
person who intimidates or harasses others

Examples

The bully at school made several students feel unsafe in the hallway.
She stood up to the bully and refused to let him intimidate her.
Many people remember being targeted by a bully during their childhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tormentor - someone who inflicts pain or suffering on others
  • Synonym: persecutor - one who harasses or mistreats others persistently
  • Antonym: victim - person harmed or bullied by others
  • Antonym: protector - person who defends others from harm

Etymology

Possible origin from Middle Dutch *boele* (lover, brother); related usage in English since the 16th century.

4. VERB

/ ˈbʊli / (AmE), / ˈbʊli / (BrE)
to discourage or frighten someone with threats or a domineering manner; to intimidate
intimidate or threaten

Examples

He used his size to bully the smaller children on the playground.
Some managers bully their employees into working overtime without extra pay.
She refused to be bullied into making a decision she was uncomfortable with.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to coerce - to force someone to do something by using threats or intimidation

5. VERB

/ ˈbʊli / (AmE), / ˈbʊli / (BrE)
to frighten, hurt, or threaten someone who is weaker or less powerful, often repeatedly; to be bossy or intimidating towards someone
intimidate or boss someone around

Examples

He used to bully his classmates at school until the teachers intervened.
The manager was accused of trying to bully the staff into working overtime.
She refused to let anyone bully her into making a decision she wasn't comfortable with.

Synset Examples

Her big brother always bullied her when she was young

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intimidate - to make someone feel timid or fearful, especially to influence their behavior
  • Synonym: to harass - to trouble or annoy someone persistently
  • Antonym: to protect - to keep someone safe from harm or danger

Etymology

Probably from Middle Dutch boel (lover, brother), later meaning 'ruffian.'
