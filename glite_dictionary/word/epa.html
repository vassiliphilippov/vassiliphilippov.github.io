E.P.A.

1. PROPN

/ ˌiː.piːˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌiː.piːˈeɪ / (BrE)
an independent agency of the United States federal government responsible for protecting the environment by regulating pollution.
US government environmental agency
american

Examples

The E.P.A. announced new regulations to limit air pollution from factories.
Scientists from the E.P.A. visited the site to assess the environmental impact.
The report criticized the E.P.A. for delaying action on contaminated water.

Etymology

Abbreviation of Environmental Protection Agency, established in 1970 in the United States.
