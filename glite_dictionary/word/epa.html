EPA

1. NOUN

/ ˌiː piː ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌiː piː ˈeɪ / (BrE)
an independent agency of the United States federal government responsible for coordinating programs to reduce pollution and protect the environment
US federal environmental agency
american

Examples

The EPA announced new regulations to control air pollution from factories.
Local governments often work closely with the EPA to clean up contaminated sites.
Critics argue that recent budget cuts may limit the effectiveness of the EPA.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Environmental Protection Agency - U.S. government agency for environmental protection

Etymology

Acronym of Environmental Protection Agency, established 1970, United States.
