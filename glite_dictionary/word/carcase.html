carcase

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːrkəs / (AmE), / ˈkɑːkɑːs / (BrE)
the dead body of an animal, especially one that has been slaughtered and prepared for food; variant spelling of carcass
dead body of a slaughtered animal (British spelling of carcass)
british

Examples

After the hunt, the carcase was cleaned and prepared for dinner.
Farmers transported the sheep carcases to the market early in the morning.
The butchers hung the beef carcase in the cold room for aging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carcass - the dead body of an animal, especially one prepared for food (American spelling and general term)

Etymology

From Old French 'carcois', variant of 'carcass'. 'Carcase' is chiefly British spelling.
