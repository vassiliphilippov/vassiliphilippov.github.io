schoolma'am

1. NOUN

/ ˈskuːlˌmæm / (AmE), / ˈskuːlˌmɑːm / (BrE)
a woman schoolteacher, especially one who is regarded as strict or old-fashioned
a strict woman schoolteacher
archaic

Examples

The new teacher was a classic schoolma'am with her stern manners and strict rules.
Tales of the old schoolma'am still circulate among the town's oldest residents.
With her hair in a tight bun, she looked every bit the schoolma'am from a bygone era.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schoolmistress - a woman who teaches in a school
  • Antonym: schoolmaster - a man who teaches in a school

Etymology

Formed from 'school' and 'ma'am' (polite form of 'madam').
