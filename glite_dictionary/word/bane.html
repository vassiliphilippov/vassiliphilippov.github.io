bane

1. NOUN

/ beɪn / (AmE), / beɪn / (BrE)
A cause of great distress, misery, or death.
source of distress or death

Examples

Traffic jams are the bane of city commuters.
Mosquitoes are the bane of our summer evenings.
Procrastination has been the bane of his academic life.

Synset Examples

the bane of my life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curse - a cause of harm or misery
  • Antonym: blessing - something that brings happiness or benefit

Etymology

From Old English bana (killer, slayer), related to Old Norse bani (death, killer).
