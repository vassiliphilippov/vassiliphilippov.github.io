glorious

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡlɔriəs / (AmE), / ˈɡlɔːriəs / (BrE)
Having great beauty and splendor; deserving admiration for its appearance or quality.
having great beauty or splendor

Examples

The mountains offered a glorious view at sunrise.
She wore a glorious dress covered in sequins.
They spent the afternoon in a glorious garden filled with roses.

Synset Examples

a glorious spring morning
a glorious sunset
splendid costumes
a kind of splendiferous native simplicity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splendid - impressive in beauty or excellence

Etymology

From Latin 'gloriosus' (full of glory, boasting).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡlɔriəs / (AmE), / ˈɡlɔːriəs / (BrE)
Having or characterized by grandeur, magnificence, or splendor; worthy of admiration or praise.
full of grandeur or splendor

Examples

The palace was decorated in a glorious display of gold and crystal chandeliers.
The museum is known for its glorious collection of Renaissance paintings.
Their wedding was a glorious celebration filled with music and laughter.

Synset Examples

the brilliant court life at Versailles
a glorious work of art
magnificent cathedrals
the splendid coronation ceremony

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splendid - magnificent in appearance or quality
  • Synonym: magnificent - impressively beautiful, elaborate, or extravagant
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance; plain or simple

Etymology

From Latin gloriosus (full of glory), from gloria (glory).
