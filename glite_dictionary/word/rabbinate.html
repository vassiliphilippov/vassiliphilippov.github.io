rabbinate

1. NOUN

/ ˈræbɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈræbɪnət / (BrE)
the office or position of a rabbi, or the collective body of rabbis
the office or position of a rabbi

Examples

He served in the rabbinate for over forty years, guiding his community through many changes.
After years of study, she was officially admitted to the rabbinate.
The conference brought together members of the international rabbinate to discuss religious leadership.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: priesthood - the office or position of a priest

Etymology

From Late Latin rabbīnatus (office of a rabbi), from Hebrew rabbi (teacher).

2. NOUN

/ ˈræbəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈrabɪnət / (BrE)
The collective body of rabbis, or the office or function of a rabbi, especially as a religious authority within Judaism.
the collective body of rabbis; rabbinical office

Examples

The rabbinate has played a significant role in shaping Jewish communal life throughout history.
After years of study, he was welcomed into the rabbinate with a formal ceremony.
The conference brought together members of the rabbinate from around the world to discuss pressing issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clergy - the body of people ordained for religious duties

Etymology

From Medieval Latin rabbīnus (rabbinical) + -ate (denoting office or function).
