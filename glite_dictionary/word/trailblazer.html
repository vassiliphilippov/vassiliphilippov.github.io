trailblazer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪlˌbleɪzɚ / (AmE), / ˈtreɪlˌbleɪzə / (BrE)
a person who marks a trail through wilderness by making blazes or signs, especially on trees, to guide others
person who marks trails with blazes

Examples

The trailblazer carefully marked each tree to help hikers find their way.
Centuries ago, it was common for a trailblazer to use an axe to create pathway signs.
Every team had a designated trailblazer responsible for guiding them through the dense forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pathfinder - someone who finds or makes a path through unexplored territory

Etymology

From 'trail' + 'blaze', where 'blaze' refers to a mark made on trees.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪlˌbleɪzər / (AmE), / ˈtreɪlˌbleɪzə / (BrE)
a person who is the first to develop new methods, ideas, or activities, especially in a particular field
pioneer; innovator in a field

Examples

Marie Curie was a true trailblazer in the field of science.
As a trailblazer, she inspired many to pursue careers in technology.
Many consider him a trailblazer for his groundbreaking work in modern art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pioneer - a person who is among the first to explore or develop a new area
  • Synonym: innovator - a person who introduces new ideas or methods
  • Antonym: follower - a person who comes after and is led by others; not a leader or originator

Etymology

From 'trail' and 'blaze', originally referring to someone who marks a trail through wilderness for others to follow.
