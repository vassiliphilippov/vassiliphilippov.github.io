Cyathea

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈiːθiə / (AmE), / saɪˈiːθɪə / (BrE)
A genus of tree ferns found in tropical and temperate woodlands, belonging to the family Cyatheaceae.
genus of tree ferns

Examples

The Cyathea species can reach impressive heights in their native rainforests.
Several Cyathea ferns were planted in the botanical garden's tropical section.
Botanists studied the unique frond patterns of the Cyathea genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cyatheaceae - The botanical family to which Cyathea belongs

Etymology

From Greek 'kyatheion', diminutive of 'kyathos' (cup), referring to the shape of the sori.
