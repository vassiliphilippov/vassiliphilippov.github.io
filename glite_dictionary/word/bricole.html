bricole

1. NOUN

/ brɪˈkoʊl / (AmE), / brɪˈkəʊl / (BrE)
A type of historical catapult operated by manpower, using cords attached to a lever and sling to launch projectiles.
historical lever-operated catapult

Examples

The army constructed a bricole to hurl stones over the castle walls.
A skilled crew was needed to operate the heavy bricole during the siege.
Medieval engineers improved the power and accuracy of the bricole over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trebuchet - a type of large medieval catapult
  • Synonym: catapult - a device used to hurl objects using tension

Etymology

From French bricole (old term for a military engine), ultimately from Old French.
