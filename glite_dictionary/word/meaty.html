meaty

1. ADJ

/ ˈmiːti / (AmE), / ˈmiːti / (BrE)
containing meat or resembling meat in taste, texture, or substance
like or containing meat

Examples

The restaurant's special is a meaty stew, filled with chunks of beef and savory spices.
After weeks of eating salads, I craved something meaty for dinner.
This burger tastes surprisingly meaty even though it is made from soy protein.

Synset Examples

enough of vegetarianism: let's have a meaty meal!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vegetarian - containing no meat or derived from plants

Etymology

From meat + -y, meaning 'having the qualities of meat.'

Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈmiti / (AmE), / ˈmiːti / (BrE)
(of speech or writing) substantial and thought-provoking; containing a lot of meaningful content.
substantial or thought-provoking

Examples

The professor's lecture was especially meaty, sparking lively debate among the students.
She prefers to read meaty articles that challenge her perspective rather than light entertainment.
We had a meaty discussion about the implications of artificial intelligence.

Synset Examples

a meaty discussion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substantial - important, meaningful, or considerable in amount or value
  • Synonym: weighty - serious, important, or thought-provoking
  • Antonym: trivial - of little value or importance

Etymology

From 'meat' (the flesh of animals used as food), used figuratively to indicate substance or importance.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01500539-s
Synset POS: s
