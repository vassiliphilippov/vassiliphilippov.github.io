dividend

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪvɪˌdɛnd / (AmE), / ˈdɪvɪdɛnd / (BrE)
the number that is to be divided by another number.
number to be divided by another

Examples

In the equation 12 ÷ 3 = 4, the number 12 is called the dividend.
To solve the problem, first identify the dividend and the divisor.
When you divide a dividend by a divisor, you get a quotient.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: divisor - a number by which another number is divided

Etymology

From Latin 'dividendum', meaning 'thing to be divided', from 'dividere' (to divide).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪvɪˌdɛnd / (AmE), / ˈdɪvɪdənd / (BrE)
an extra benefit or bonus, especially a share of surplus or profit
an extra benefit or bonus

Examples

Volunteering in your community can pay dividends in unexpected ways.
Their investment of time brought lasting dividends to the organization.
Taking care of your health now will yield dividends in the future.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bonus - something given in addition to what is usual or expected

Etymology

From Latin 'dividendum', meaning 'thing to be divided'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɪvɪˌdɛnd / (AmE), / ˈdɪvɪdɛnd / (BrE)
A sum of money paid regularly (typically quarterly) by a corporation to its shareholders out of its profits or reserves.
sum paid to shareholders from a company's profits

Examples

Shareholders were pleased when the company announced a higher dividend for the second quarter.
He invested in stocks that provide a steady dividend income.
The board decided to cut the dividend to preserve cash during the downturn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profit - financial gain made in business
  • Antonym: loss - money lost in business, opposite of profit/dividend

Etymology

From Latin 'dividendum' (thing to be divided), from 'dividere' (to divide).
