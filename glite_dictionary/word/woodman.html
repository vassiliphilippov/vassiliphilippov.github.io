woodman

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdmən / (AmE), / ˈwʊdmən / (BrE)
a man who lives in or is closely connected with the woods, especially as a forester, woodsman, or in folklore.
man living in or connected with the woods

Examples

The fairy tale described a brave woodman who rescued lost children in the forest.
In the old village, a solitary woodman was rumored to know every tree for miles around.
He lived simply, like a true woodman in a small, hand-built cabin deep in the woods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodsman - a person who lives or works in the woods

Etymology

From Old English 'wudumann', meaning 'man of the woods'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdmən / (AmE), / ˈwʊdmən / (BrE)
a man whose job is making things out of wood, especially by shaping or carving it
man who works with wood

Examples

The woodman carefully carved a new table from oak he had harvested himself.
In old fairy tales, a brave woodman often helps the main character in the forest.
My grandfather was a skilled woodman who made furniture for the whole village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carpenter - a person who makes and repairs wooden objects and structures
  • Synonym: joiner - a skilled worker who joins pieces of wood, especially as a job

Etymology

From Old English 'wudumann', combining 'wood' and 'man'.
