adenoidal

1. ADJ

/ ˌæd.əˈnɔɪ.dl̩ / (AmE), / ˌæd.ɪˈnɔɪ.dl̩ / (BrE)
Relating to the adenoids, or having a nasal quality of voice characteristic of enlarged adenoids.
relating to adenoids; nasal in speech

Examples

The child's adenoidal voice made her speech sound unusually nasal.
He underwent surgery to remove his adenoidal tissue last year.
Some singers struggle with an adenoidal tone that affects their vocal clarity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nasal - having a quality of sound produced through the nose

Etymology

From adenoid + -al, meaning 'of or pertaining to the adenoids'.

Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌædəˈnɔɪdəl / (AmE), / ˌædɪˈnɔɪdəl / (BrE)
(of a voice) sounding as if the nose is blocked or pinched; nasal in quality.
having a nasal-sounding voice

Examples

Her adenoidal voice made her easy to recognize on the phone.
The comedian exaggerated an adenoidal accent for comedic effect.
He spoke in such an adenoidal tone that it sounded like he had a constant cold.

Synset Examples

a whining nasal voice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nasal - having a quality of sound produced through the nose

Etymology

Formed from 'adenoid' (relating to glands in the throat) + '-al', indicating the quality or relation.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01216222-s
Synset POS: s
