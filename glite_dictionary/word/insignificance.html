insignificance

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.sɪɡˈnɪf.ə.kəns / (AmE), / ˌɪn.sɪɡˈnɪf.ɪ.kəns / (BrE)
The quality of being unimportant or without meaningful effect or influence.
lack of importance or significance

Examples

He felt a sense of insignificance when surrounded by the towering mountains.
The insignificance of her mistake did not warrant such a harsh response.
Political leaders sometimes lose sight of their own insignificance in the grand scheme of history.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: significance - the quality of being important or meaningful
  • Synonym: triviality - the quality of being trivial or unimportant

Etymology

From Latin insignificans (meaning without meaning or unimportant), from in- (not) + significans (significant).
