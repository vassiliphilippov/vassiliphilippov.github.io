Turbellaria

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɜːrbəˈlɛriə / (AmE), / ˌtɜːbɪˈlɛːrɪə / (BrE)
a class of mostly free-living flatworms found in aquatic or moist terrestrial environments
a class of flatworms
medical

Examples

The biologist studied specimens of Turbellaria collected from a freshwater stream.
Unlike parasitic flatworms, Turbellaria are mostly free-living organisms.
Many Turbellaria possess cilia that help them glide smoothly through the water.

Etymology

From Latin 'turbellae' (small disturbances), referring to the movement of water by their cilia.

2. NOUN

/ tɜr.bəˈlɛəri.ə / (AmE), / ˌtɜː.bəˈlɛə.ri.ə / (BrE)
A class of mostly free-living flatworms, found in aquatic and moist terrestrial environments.
class of free-living flatworms
medical

Examples

Biologists observed several species of Turbellaria under the microscope.
Freshwater Turbellaria often feed on small invertebrates.
The diversity of Turbellaria is greatest in tropical aquatic habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flatworm - simple soft-bodied invertebrate of phylum Platyhelminthes

Etymology

From Latin 'turba' (disturbance), referring to their movement in water.
