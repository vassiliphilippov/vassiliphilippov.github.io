behaviorist

1. ADJ

/ bɪˈheɪvjərɪst / (AmE), / bɪˈheɪvjərɪst / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of behaviorism, a theory in psychology focused on observable behavior
relating to behaviorism (in psychology)

Examples

The behaviorist approach emphasizes observable actions over internal thoughts.
Many behaviorist theories are still influential in educational psychology.
Some psychologists prefer behaviorist methods when designing experiments.

Synset Examples

behavioristic psychology

Related Terms

  • Synonym: behavioristic - of or relating to behaviorism

Etymology

Formed from 'behavior' and suffix '-ist', relating to behaviorism.

WordNet ID: oewn-02671356-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ bɪˈheɪvjərɪst / (AmE), / bɪˈheɪvjərɪst / (BrE)
a psychologist who studies and interprets behavior according to the principles of behaviorism, focusing on observable actions rather than internal mental states
psychologist subscribing to behaviorism

Examples

The behaviorist conducted experiments on animal learning using reward and punishment.
As a behaviorist, she believed that people's actions were shaped by their environments.
Modern therapy often incorporates insights from behaviorist approaches alongside other psychological schools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychologist - an expert in psychology
  • Synonym: behaviorism - the school of thought in psychology emphasizing observable behavior

Etymology

From behavior + -ist, after 'behaviorism' (introduced in early 20th-century psychology).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09631550-n
Synset POS: n
