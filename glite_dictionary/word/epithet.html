epithet

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛpəˌθɛt / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪθɛt / (BrE)
a word or phrase used to insult or abuse someone
abusive or insulting word/phrase

Examples

The politician was offended by the epithet hurled at him during the debate.
Using a racial epithet is never acceptable in any context.
Throughout the heated argument, angry epithets were exchanged between the two rivals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slur - an insulting or disparaging remark
  • Synonym: insult - a disrespectful or scornful remark

Etymology

From Greek epitheton (an added word or phrase), from epi- (upon) + tithenai (to put).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛpəˌθɛt / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪθɛt / (BrE)
a word or phrase used to characterize someone or something, often highlighting a notable quality or feature
a descriptive word or phrase

Examples

The poet was often referred to by the epithet 'The Bard of Avon'.
She earned the epithet 'the Iron Lady' because of her strong leadership style.
The author used the epithet 'swift-footed' to describe Achilles in the epic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nickname - an informal, often descriptive name given instead of or in addition to the real name

Etymology

From Greek epitheton (added), from epitithenai (to add on).
