homiletics

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɑːməˈlɛtɪks / (AmE), / ˌhɒmɪˈlɛtɪks / (BrE)
the study and art of preaching, especially the composition and delivery of sermons
the art of sermon writing and preaching

Examples

He studied homiletics at seminary to improve his preaching abilities.
The professor's homiletics lectures focused on effective communication from the pulpit.
Many aspiring ministers attend workshops on homiletics to learn how to connect with their congregation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sermon - a speech given during a religious service

Etymology

From Greek homilētikos (conversational), from homilos (assembly).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɒməˈlɛtɪks / (AmE), / ˌhɒmɪˈlɛtɪks / (BrE)
the art or study of preaching, especially as a formal discipline
the art of preaching

Examples

After years of studying homiletics, she delivered sermons that inspired her congregation.
The seminary requires all students to take courses in homiletics to develop their preaching skills.
His expertise in homiletics made him a sought-after lecturer at theological conferences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preaching - delivering a sermon or religious address

Etymology

From Greek homilētikos (conversational), from homilia (conversation, discourse).
