Salpichroa organifolia

1. NOUN

/ sælˌpɪkroʊ.ə ˌɔrɡəˈnɪfoʊliə / (AmE), / sælˌpɪkrəʊ.ə ˌɔːɡəˈnɪfəʊliə / (BrE)
A weedy vine native to Argentina, bearing solitary white flowers followed by egg-shaped white or yellow fruit.
Argentine weedy vine with white flowers and egg-shaped fruit

Examples

The invasive Salpichroa organifolia has spread across parts of southern Australia, outcompeting native vegetation.
Gardeners often struggle to control Salpichroa organifolia due to its aggressive growth and tolerance to various soils.
The white berries of Salpichroa organifolia can be mistaken for edible fruit, but they are not safe to eat.
