Elbe

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛlbə / (AmE), / ˈɛlbə / (BrE)
A river in central Europe that originates in the northwestern Czech Republic and flows northward through Germany to the North Sea.
river in central Europe

Examples

The Elbe is one of the major rivers flowing through Germany.
After heavy rains, the waters of the Elbe rose above their usual levels.
Many towns along the Elbe thrive due to their proximity to the river.

Etymology

From German 'Elbe', originally from Latin 'Albis', from a pre-Latin European river name.
