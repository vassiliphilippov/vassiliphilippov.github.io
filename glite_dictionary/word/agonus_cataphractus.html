Agonus cataphractus

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡoʊnəs ˌkætəˈfræktəs / (AmE), / əˈɡəʊnəs ˌkatəˈfraktəs / (BrE)
A species of fish, commonly known as the northern Atlantic sea poacher, found in northern Atlantic waters.
species of northern Atlantic sea poacher fish

Examples

The Agonus cataphractus is known for its bony armor plates along its body.
Marine biologists studied the habitat preferences of Agonus cataphractus during winter months.
Fishermen sometimes catch Agonus cataphractus unintentionally while targeting other northern Atlantic species.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Agonus' is the genus name, 'cataphractus' means 'armored' or 'protected'.
