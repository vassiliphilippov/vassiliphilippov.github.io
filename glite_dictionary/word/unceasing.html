unceasing

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsiːsɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈsiːsɪŋ / (BrE)
never stopping; continuing without pause or interruption.
never stopping; continuous

Examples

The unceasing noise from the construction site made it hard to concentrate.
Her unceasing efforts to help others inspired everyone around her.
Despite the unceasing rain, the team continued their work outside.

Synset Examples

the ageless themes of love and revenge
eternal truths
life everlasting
hell's perpetual fires
the unending bliss of heaven

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ceaseless - without an end or pause; incessant
  • Synonym: continuous - without interruption; ongoing, unbroken
  • Antonym: intermittent - occurring at irregular intervals; not continuous

Etymology

From un- (not) + ceasing, from the verb 'cease' (to stop).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsiːsɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈsiːsɪŋ / (BrE)
never stopping; continuing without interruption or end.
never stopping, continuous

Examples

Despite the rain, the unceasing noise from the construction site kept everyone awake all night.
Her unceasing efforts eventually led to a major breakthrough in the research.
He was admired for his unceasing dedication to helping those in need.

Synset Examples

the ceaseless thunder of surf
in constant pain
night and day we live with the incessant noise of the city
the never-ending search for happiness
the perpetual struggle to maintain standards in a democracy
man's unceasing warfare with drought and isolation
unremitting demands of hunger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ceaseless - continuing without stopping or pausing
  • Degree Higher: unceasing - never stopping; continuous (antonym relation would be with 'intermittent')
  • Antonym: intermittent - occurring at irregular intervals; not continuous

Etymology

From un- (not) + cease (from Old French cesser, from Latin cessare, to stop).
