riches

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪtʃɪz / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃɪz / (BrE)
great wealth; an abundance of valuable possessions, money, or resources.
wealth; abundant resources or possessions

Examples

The prospect of sudden riches was enough to attract adventurers from around the world.
She dreamed of traveling the globe and sharing her riches with those in need.
Despite his riches, he remained humble and generous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wealth - an abundance of valuable possessions or money
  • Antonym: poverty - the state of being extremely poor

Etymology

From Old French riches (wealth), from Latin divitiae (riches, wealth).
