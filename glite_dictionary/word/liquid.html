liquid

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɪkwɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪd / (BrE)
existing as or having the characteristics of a liquid; especially tending to flow rather than be solid or gaseous
in a state that flows easily; not solid or gas

Examples

Mercury is unique among metals because it is liquid at room temperature.
The medicine comes in liquid form and should be measured carefully.
Ice cream melts and becomes liquid on a hot day.

Synset Examples

water and milk and blood are liquid substances

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solid - having a definite shape and volume; not liquid or gaseous
  • Antonym: gaseous - relating to or having the form of gas; not solid or liquid

Etymology

From Latin *liquidus* (fluid, liquid), from *liquēre* (to be fluid).

3. NOUN

/ ˈlɪkwɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪd / (BrE)
a substance that is in the state of matter in which it flows freely, has a definite volume but no fixed shape, and is not easily compressible
fluid state of matter

Examples

Water is a liquid at room temperature but turns into ice when frozen.
Mercury is a unique metal because it stays liquid even at very low temperatures.
The scientist poured a clear liquid into the test tube.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solid - state of matter with definite shape and volume, not fluid
  • Antonym: gas - state of matter with no fixed shape or volume, easily compressed

Etymology

From Latin 'liquidus' (fluid, liquid), from 'liquere' (to be liquid or fluid).

Source: WordNet
