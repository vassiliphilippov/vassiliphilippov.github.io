Hirudinea

1. NOUN

/ hɪˌruːdəˈniːə / (AmE), / hɪˌruːdɪˈniːə / (BrE)
a class of hermaphroditic segmented worms, popularly known as leeches, inhabiting aquatic or terrestrial environments, and some are parasitic
a class of segmented worms known as leeches
medical

Examples

Members of the Hirudinea class are commonly known as leeches and inhabit various watery environments.
Biologists discovered a new species in the Hirudinea during their expedition to the remote wetlands.
Some Hirudinea are used in medicine due to their anticoagulant properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leech - a type of bloodsucking parasitic worm

Etymology

From Latin *hirudo* (leech), originally from an Indo-European root.

2. NOUN

/ hɪˌruːdəˈniːə / (AmE), / hɪˌruːdɪˈniːə / (BrE)
a class of hermaphroditic annelid worms, including leeches, that are aquatic, terrestrial, or parasitic
class of annelid worms (leeches)

Examples

Scientists discovered a new freshwater species in the Hirudinea class in the Amazon basin.
Members of Hirudinea can be found in both terrestrial and aquatic environments worldwide.
The study of Hirudinea has contributed greatly to our understanding of parasitism in annelids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leech - a bloodsucking or carnivorous annelid worm; member of class Hirudinea

Etymology

From Latin *hirudo* (leech), with New Latin taxonomic suffix -inea.
