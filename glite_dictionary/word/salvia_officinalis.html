Salvia officinalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæl.vi.ə ˌɑː.fɪˈsɪn.əl.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈsæl.vɪ.ə ˌɒf.ɪˈsɪn.æl.ɪs / (BrE)
a shrubby plant with aromatic greyish-green leaves, commonly known as sage, used as a cooking herb
culinary herb; common sage plant

Examples

Many recipes call for fresh Salvia officinalis to add flavor to meat dishes.
Gardeners often grow Salvia officinalis because its leaves are both attractive and useful in the kitchen.
The leaves of Salvia officinalis are prized for their strong, earthy aroma and culinary uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sage - common name for Salvia officinalis, the cooking herb

Etymology

From Latin: 'salvia' meaning 'sage', and 'officinalis' meaning 'used in medicine or pharmacy'.
