mover

1. NOUN

/ ˈmuːvər / (AmE), / ˈmuːvə / (BrE)
a person who formally proposes a motion at a meeting or in a legislative assembly
person who proposes a formal motion

Examples

The mover of the motion addressed the committee with a clear summary of the proposal.
At the council meeting, the mover explained the reasons for the suggested amendment.
Each resolution needs a mover and a seconder before it can be debated.

Etymology

From the verb 'move', in the sense of 'to propose,' + -er suffix forming agent nouns.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmuːvər / (AmE), / ˈmuːvə / (BrE)
a company that transports the possessions of people or businesses from one location to another
company that moves belongings

Examples

We hired a mover to help us relocate to our new home.
The mover arrived early to start packing up the office furniture.
If you don't have a truck, it's best to call a professional mover.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: removal company - a company that helps people move their household or office belongings

Etymology

From move + -er, indicating one who moves or causes moving.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08495493-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈmuːvər / (AmE), / ˈmuːvə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to transport people's belongings from one place to another, especially as employed by a moving company
workman who moves household goods

Examples

The movers arrived early in the morning to start loading the truck.
We hired professional movers to handle our fragile items during the move.
One of the movers accidentally dropped a box, but nothing was broken.

Synset Examples

the movers were very careful with the grand piano

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remover - person or thing that removes; sometimes used for moving company worker
