neoprene

1. NOUN

/ ˈniːəˌpriːn / (AmE), / ˈniːəʊpriːn / (BrE)
a synthetic rubber that is resistant to oil, heat, and weathering, commonly used in wetsuits, industrial products, and waterproof materials.
synthetic rubber used in waterproof products

Examples

The diver wore a neoprene wetsuit to protect himself from the cold water.
Gloves made of neoprene offer good resistance to chemicals and oils.
Many laptop cases are padded with neoprene for shock protection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rubber - an elastic substance made from the latex of the rubber tree or synthetically

Etymology

Formed from 'neo-' (new) and 'isoprene', the chemical used in its production.
