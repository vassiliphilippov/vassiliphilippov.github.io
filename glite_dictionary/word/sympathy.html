sympathy

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmpəθi / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəθi / (BrE)
A tendency to support or agree with the opinions or cause of a particular person or group.
support for a cause or opinion

Examples

The government expressed strong sympathy for the striking workers.
Her sympathy clearly lay with the environmental activists.
He made it obvious that his sympathy was with those who demanded reform.

Synset Examples

his sympathies were always with the underdog
I knew I could count on his understanding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - agreement with or encouragement of an idea or cause
  • Antonym: opposition - active disagreement or resistance

Etymology

From Greek sympatheia (fellow feeling), via Latin sympathia.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmpəθi / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəθi / (BrE)
A relationship of mutual understanding or harmony between people, where whatever affects one correspondingly affects the other.
mutual understanding or harmony

Examples

There was an immediate sympathy between the two teammates, making their collaboration effortless.
She felt a deep sympathy with her colleague, sharing both his ambitions and frustrations.
Their ideas were in close sympathy, leading to a harmonious partnership.

Synset Examples

the two of them were in close sympathy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affinity - a natural liking or attraction between people
  • Antonym: antipathy - a feeling of strong aversion or opposition

Etymology

From Greek sympatheia (fellow feeling), from syn- (with) + pathos (feeling).

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɪm.pə.θi / (AmE), / ˈsɪm.pə.θi / (BrE)
the feeling of understanding and sharing another person's feelings, especially their sorrow or sadness
shared feeling of sorrow or compassion

Examples

She expressed her sympathy for the family after hearing about their loss.
Many people sent cards and flowers as a gesture of sympathy.
His words were full of sympathy, but they could not ease her pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compassion - deep awareness of another's suffering and desire to alleviate it
  • Synonym: empathy - ability to understand and share the feelings of another
  • Antonym: apathy - lack of feeling or emotion

Etymology

From Greek *sympatheia* (fellow feeling), from *syn-* (together) + *pathos* (feeling).
