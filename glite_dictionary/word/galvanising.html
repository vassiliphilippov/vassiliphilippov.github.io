galvanising

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡæl.vəˌnaɪ.zɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡal.vəˌnaɪ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing someone to feel a sudden strong emotion or excitement; thrilling or electrifying.
exciting or electrifying

Examples

The coach made a galvanising speech that inspired the team to victory.
Her performance had a galvanising effect on the audience.
The new legislation acted as a galvanising force for social change.

Synset Examples

gave an electric reading of the play
the new leader had a galvanic effect on morale

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electric - exciting or thrilling, as if charged with electricity
  • Antonym: uninspiring - not exciting or stimulating; failing to provoke interest

Etymology

From French galvaniser (to stimulate), named after Italian scientist Luigi Galvani.
