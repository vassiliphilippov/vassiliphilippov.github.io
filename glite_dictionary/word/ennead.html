ennead

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛniˌæd / (AmE), / ˈɛniːad / (BrE)
a group or set of nine, especially used in historical or mythological contexts
group of nine

Examples

The ancient Egyptians worshipped the Great Ennead at Heliopolis.
A baseball team can be considered an ennead since it has nine players.
He collected an ennead of rare coins from around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nine - the number 9

Etymology

From Greek ennea (nine) via Late Latin ennead- (group of nine).
