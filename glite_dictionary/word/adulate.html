to adulate

1. VERB

/ ˈædʒəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈad.jʊ.leɪt / (BrE)
To praise or flatter someone excessively, often in an insincere or obsequious way.
flatter someone excessively

Examples

Many employees tend to adulate their manager hoping for a promotion.
The author was adulated by critics for her latest novel.
You shouldn't adulate celebrities just because they're famous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flatter - to praise excessively, especially insincerely
  • Antonym: to criticize - to express disapproval or find fault

Etymology

From Latin 'adulatus', past participle of 'adulari' (to flatter).
