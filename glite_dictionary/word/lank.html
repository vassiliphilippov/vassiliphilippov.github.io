lank

1. ADJ

/ læŋk / (AmE), / læŋk / (BrE)
Long and thin, often limp or lacking stiffness, especially of hair or limbs.
long, thin, and limp (esp. hair)

Examples

His lank hair hung over his eyes after walking in the rain.
The child had grown lank and pale during their long illness.
She brushed her lank locks, hoping to give them some volume.

Synset Examples

grown lank with fasting
lank mousy hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limp - soft or lacking stiffness; not firm
  • Synonym: stringy - thin and resembling string

Etymology

From Middle English lank, from Old English hlanc (slender, lean).

2. ADJ

/ læŋk / (AmE), / læŋk / (BrE)
(especially of hair, limbs, or body) long, thin, and often limp or lacking in vitality.
long, thin, and often limp (hair or body)

Examples

His lank hair hung over his eyes, giving him a disheveled look.
After the rain, the cat's lank fur clung to its thin frame.
She was tall and lank, with long arms and legs that seemed too big for her body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stringy - long, thin, and resembling a string (often of hair or muscles)
  • Synonym: limp - not stiff or firm, often describing hair; lacking vitality
  • Antonym: curly - having curls or a curved shape, especially of hair

Etymology

Probably from Middle Low German *lank* or Old English *lanc* (long, tall).
