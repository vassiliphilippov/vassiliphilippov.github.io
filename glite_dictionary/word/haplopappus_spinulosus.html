Haplopappus spinulosus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæploʊˈpæpəs spaɪˈnjuːləs / (AmE), / ˌhæpləˈpæpəs spɪˈnjuːləs / (BrE)
a slender perennial plant native to western North America, with weakly bristly leaves and yellow flower heads
a North American perennial plant with yellow flowers

Examples

Haplopappus spinulosus is commonly found in dry, rocky areas of the American West.
Botanists use the yellow flower heads of Haplopappus spinulosus to help identify it in the field.
The leaves of Haplopappus spinulosus are weakly bristly, which distinguishes it from similar species.

Etymology

From Greek 'haplo-' (simple) + 'pappos' (down), referring to the plant's simple pappus; 'spinulosus' is Latin for 'spiny.'
