acanthocephalan

1. NOUN

/ əˌkænθoʊˈsɛfələn / (AmE), / əˌkænθəʊˈsɛfələn / (BrE)
a parasitic worm of the phylum Acanthocephala, characterized by a retractable, spiny proboscis used for attachment in the intestines of vertebrate hosts
parasitic worm with spiny proboscis
medical

Examples

The biologist found an acanthocephalan parasite living inside the fish's intestine.
Some species of acanthocephalan are known to infect aquatic birds as their definitive hosts.
Researchers are studying the life cycle of the acanthocephalan to improve parasite control in livestock.

Etymology

From Greek akantha (spine) + kephalē (head), referring to the spiny head of the worm.
