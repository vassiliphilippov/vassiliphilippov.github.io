Scandinavian

1. NOUN

/ ˌskændəˈneɪviən / (AmE), / ˌskændɪˈneɪviən / (BrE)
a person who is from or lives in Scandinavia, typically Denmark, Norway, or Sweden
person from Scandinavia

Examples

Many Scandinavians speak several languages fluently, including English.
The tradition is popular among Scandinavians during the winter months.
As a Scandinavian, she took pride in her country's cultural heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nordic - relating to the northern European countries, especially Scandinavia, Finland, and Iceland

Etymology

From Latin Scandinavia (the region) + English suffix -an.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09749546-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˌskændəˈneɪviən / (AmE), / ˌskændɪˈneɪviən / (BrE)
the group of northern Germanic languages spoken in Scandinavia and Iceland, including Danish, Norwegian, Swedish, Icelandic, and Faroese
northern Germanic language group

Examples

Old Norse is the ancestor of the modern Scandinavian languages.
She is studying the history and grammar of Scandinavian at university.
Icelandic is the most conservative of the Scandinavian languages in terms of grammar and vocabulary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: North Germanic - the branch of the Germanic languages comprising Scandinavian languages

Etymology

From Latin *Scandinavia*, the Roman name for the region, combined with the English suffix -an.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06966471-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˌskændəˈneɪviən / (AmE), / ˌskændɪˈneɪviən / (BrE)
relating to Scandinavia, its people, languages, or culture
of Scandinavia or its people/culture

Examples

The Scandinavian countries are known for their high quality of life and progressive societies.
She decorated her home in a minimalist Scandinavian style, using light colors and natural materials.
Many Scandinavian myths and legends are still popular in modern literature.

Synset Examples

Norse sagas
Norse nomads

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Norse - relating to medieval Scandinavia or its people and culture
  • Degree Lower: Finnish - relating to Finland, its people, or culture

Etymology

From Latin 'Scandinavia,' the region name, with English adjectival suffix '-ian'.
