alms

1. NOUN

/ ɑlmz / (AmE), / ɑːmz / (BrE)
money or goods given to the poor as charity
money or goods given as charity

Examples

The church regularly collects alms for the needy in the community.
She gave alms to the beggars outside the temple every week.
During the festival, people distribute alms to honor tradition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charity - the voluntary giving of help to those in need

Etymology

From Old English ælmesse, from Late Latin eleemosyna, from Greek eleēmosynē (pity, alms).
