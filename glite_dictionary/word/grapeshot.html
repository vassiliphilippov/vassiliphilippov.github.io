grapeshot

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪpˌʃɑt / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪpˌʃɒt / (BrE)
A cluster of small metal balls fired together from a cannon, designed to spread out and inflict damage over a wide area.
cluster of small projectiles fired from a cannon

Examples

During the battle, the cannon fired grapeshot into the advancing infantry.
The use of grapeshot caused devastating injuries on the crowded deck of the enemy ship.
Old cannons and grapeshot were displayed in the military museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canister shot - a type of ammunition similar to grapeshot but containing smaller balls

Etymology

So called because the cluster of balls resembled a bunch of grapes.
