roundel

1. NOUN

/ ˈraʊndəl / (AmE), / ˈraʊndəl / (BrE)
A short poem consisting of three triplets with a refrain after the first and third, based on the French rondeau form.
poetic form with repeated refrain

Examples

The poet composed a roundel to express his feelings of longing and loss.
In literature class, we studied the structure of the roundel and tried writing our own versions.
The roundel, with its recurring refrain, captivated the audience at the poetry reading.

Etymology

From Old French rondeau, diminutive of rond (round).

2. NOUN

/ ˈraʊn.dəl / (AmE), / ˈraʊn.dəl / (BrE)
a round piece of armor plate that protects the armpit, used especially in medieval armor
round piece of armor for armpit protection

Examples

The knight's roundel shielded his armpit from the opponent's sword.
Collectors often display a medieval roundel as part of their armor exhibits.
Without a roundel, that area would be especially vulnerable during battle.

Etymology

From Old French rondel (from rond 'round'), diminutive of ronde ('circle').

3. NOUN

/ ˈraʊndəl / (AmE), / ˈraʊndəl / (BrE)
(heraldry) A charge or symbol in the shape of a filled circle, used as an emblem on a shield or coat of arms.
heraldic filled circle emblem

Examples

The knight's shield displayed a gold roundel in its center.
Many military aircraft use a roundel as a national insignia.
The coat of arms featured a red roundel below the chevron.

Synset Examples

a hollow roundel

Etymology

From Old French *rondel* (little circle), diminutive of *rond* (round).
