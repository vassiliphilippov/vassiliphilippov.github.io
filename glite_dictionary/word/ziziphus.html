Ziziphus

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɪzɪfəs / (AmE), / ˈzɪzɪfəs / (BrE)
A genus of spiny shrubs and small trees native to tropical and subtropical regions, including the jujube.
a genus of spiny shrubs (jujubes)

Examples

The Ziziphus tree is prized for its sweet, edible fruits known as jujubes.
In arid regions, Ziziphus shrubs provide important shelter for wildlife.
Botanists discovered a new species of Ziziphus in the tropical forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jujube - the edible fruit of Ziziphus jujuba, or the tree itself

Etymology

From Latin 'zizyphum', from Ancient Greek 'ζίζυφον' (zízuphon), referring to the jujube tree.
