to cheerlead

1. VERB

/ ˈtʃɪrˌlid / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪəˌliːd / (BrE)
to act as a cheerleader, especially by leading cheers and encouraging a team or crowd at a sports event
act as a cheerleader

Examples

She used to cheerlead for the university football team.
Many students volunteered to cheerlead at the basketball game.
It takes a lot of energy to cheerlead during long matches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheerleader - a person who leads cheers at sports events

Etymology

Formed from 'cheerleader' (noun) + verb-forming usage.
