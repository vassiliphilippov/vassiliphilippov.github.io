module

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.dʒuːl / (AmE), / ˈmɒd.juːl / (BrE)
a self-contained unit, component, or section that forms part of a larger system or structure and can often be used independently or combined with others
self-contained component of a system

Examples

The software consists of several modules that can be installed independently.
Each module of the course covers a different topic.
Space stations are often built by connecting pre-fabricated modules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: component - a part or element of a larger system

Etymology

From Latin 'modulus' (a small measure or unit), diminutive of 'modus' (measure, manner).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːdʒuːl / (AmE), / ˈmɒd.juːl / (BrE)
a detachable compartment of a spacecraft, designed for a specific purpose such as crew living space, experiments, or propulsion
detachable spacecraft compartment

Examples

The lunar module safely landed the astronauts on the Moon's surface.
Engineers designed a new module to be attached to the International Space Station.
During re-entry, the command module separated from the rest of the spacecraft.

Etymology

From Latin modulus (a small measure, diminutive of modus ‘measure’), later adopted in technical English.

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.dʒuːl / (AmE), / ˈmɒd.juːl / (BrE)
a unit of a computer or electronic system consisting of a self-contained assembly of electronic components designed to function as part of a larger system
self-contained electronic unit in computer hardware

Examples

The graphics module can be replaced to upgrade your computer's performance.
Each memory module slots into the motherboard of the server.
Technicians tested the faulty module before installing a new one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: component - a part or element of a larger whole, especially an electronic system

Etymology

From Latin modulus, diminutive of modus (measure, manner).

4. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːdʒuːl / (AmE), / ˈmɒd.juːl / (BrE)
a distinct cognitive or perceptual faculty of the mind, responsible for specific mental processes
cognitive faculty of the mind

Examples

Some theorists propose that the language ability is governed by a specialized module in the brain.
The concept of a cognitive module helps explain how we process information rapidly and unconsciously.
Researchers debate whether the visual system operates as an independent module or is integrated with other mental functions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faculty - an inherent mental or physical power
  • Synonym: capacity - the mental ability to do something

Etymology

From Latin modulus (small measure), diminutive of modus (measure, manner); adopted in technical senses in science and philosophy.
