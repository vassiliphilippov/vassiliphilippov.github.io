adorer

1. NOUN

/ əˈdɔrər / (AmE), / əˈdɔːrə / (BrE)
a person who regards someone with deep love, admiration, or devotion, often in a romantic sense
person who deeply admires someone

Examples

She received countless letters from unknown adorers after her performance.
At the party, he was surrounded by his loyal adorers.
The singer waved to her adorers waiting outside the theater.

Synset Examples

she had many admirers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admirer - a person who has a romantic or respectful attraction for someone
  • Antonym: detractor - a person who criticizes or belittles someone

Etymology

From French 'adorer' (to adore), from Latin 'adorare' (to worship, to adore).
