Alisma

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɪzmə / (AmE), / əˈlɪzmə / (BrE)
A small genus of aquatic or semiaquatic plants in the family Alismataceae, commonly known as water-plantains.
a genus of aquatic or semiaquatic plants

Examples

Alisma is often found growing in shallow lakes and slow-moving streams.
Botanists have classified several species of water-plantains under the genus Alisma.
The leaves of some Alisma species are used in traditional herbal remedies.

Etymology

From Greek alisma, a kind of water plant.
