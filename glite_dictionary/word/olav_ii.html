Olav II

1. PROPN

/ ˈoʊˌlɑːv səˈkɛnd / (AmE), / ˈəʊˌlɑːv sɪˈkənd / (BrE)
(995–1030) King of Norway, also known as Saint Olav, who became the country's patron saint after his death.
King and patron saint of Norway (995–1030)

Examples

The legacy of Olav II is celebrated every year in Norway on St. Olav's Day.
Many churches in Scandinavia are dedicated to Olav II, reflecting his lasting influence.
As Norway's patron saint, Olav II played a key role in the country's conversion to Christianity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saint Olav - Title for Olav II after canonization as Norway's patron saint
