sundown

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈsʌn.daʊn / (BrE)
the time in the evening when the sun disappears below the horizon
time when sun sets

Examples

We should try to get home before sundown to avoid getting lost in the dark.
At sundown, the sky was filled with beautiful shades of orange and red.
Farmers often finish their work at sundown when the fields grow dark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunset - the time in the evening when the sun disappears below the horizon
  • Antonym: sunrise - the time in the morning when the sun appears above the horizon

Etymology

Formed from "sun" + "down," referring to the sun going down below the horizon.
