impenitence

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɛnɪtəns / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛnɪtəns / (BrE)
The quality or state of not feeling regret or remorse for one's actions; unwillingness to repent.
lack of remorse or repentance

Examples

His persistent impenitence frustrated those who hoped he would apologize for his actions.
The judge commented on the defendant's impenitence before passing sentence.
Throughout history, impenitence has often been viewed as a serious moral failing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: remorse - deep regret or guilt for a wrong committed
  • Antonym: penitence - the feeling of sorrow and regret for having done wrong

Etymology

From Latin impenitentia, from in- (not) + paenitentia (repentance).
