bonnie

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɑni / (AmE), / ˈbɒni / (BrE)
Attractive and pleasing to look at; pretty (chiefly Scottish or northern English).
attractive; pretty (Scottish/northern English)
british

Examples

She wore a bonnie blue dress that caught everyone's attention at the party.
They spent the afternoon walking along the bonnie shores of the loch.
His grandmother always called her a bonnie lass when she was little.

Synset Examples

my bonny lass
there's a bonny bay beyond
a comely face
young fair maidens

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comely - attractive and pleasant to look at
  • Synonym: pretty - pleasing or attractive, especially in a delicate way
  • Antonym: plain - not beautiful or attractive

Etymology

From Scots 'bonnie', from Middle French 'bon' (good), influenced by 'bonnie' (pretty).
