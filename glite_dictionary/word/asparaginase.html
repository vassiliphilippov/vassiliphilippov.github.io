asparaginase

1. NOUN

/ əˈspærədʒɪˌneɪs / (AmE), / əˈspærədʒɪˌneɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme used as a medication, especially as an antineoplastic drug to treat certain forms of leukemia
enzyme drug used in leukemia treatment
medical

Examples

Asparaginase is commonly used in combination with other chemotherapy drugs to treat acute lymphoblastic leukemia.
The patient's treatment regimen included asparaginase as a critical component.
Allergic reactions to asparaginase may require adjustment of the medication protocol.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antineoplastic - a drug that inhibits or prevents the growth of tumors

Etymology

From 'asparagine' (an amino acid) + '-ase' (enzyme suffix).
