mango

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæŋɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmaŋɡəʊ / (BrE)
A large, oval-shaped tropical fruit with smooth skin, juicy aromatic flesh, and a large central seed.
large oval tropical fruit

Examples

She sliced a ripe mango for dessert.
The smoothie was made with fresh mango and yogurt.
He bought a basket of mangoes at the market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fruit - the sweet and fleshy product of a tree or other plant that contains seed and can be eaten as food

Etymology

From Portuguese 'manga', from Malayalam 'maanga', referring to the fruit.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæŋɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmæŋɡəʊ / (BrE)
a large evergreen tropical tree of the genus Mangifera, especially Mangifera indica, cultivated for its large oval fruit
large evergreen tropical tree

Examples

The old orchard was filled with towering mango trees that provided shade all summer.
A single mango can grow up to 30 meters tall in the right climate.
Researchers study the growth patterns of the mango to improve fruit yields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mango - the sweet, juicy fruit produced by the mango tree

Etymology

From Portuguese manga, from Malayalam māṅṅa or Tamil māṅgai.
