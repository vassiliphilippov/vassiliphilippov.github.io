chanty

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃæn.ti / (AmE), / ˈʃæn.ti / (BrE)
a rhythmic song sung by sailors while working, especially one used to coordinate labor
sailors' rhythmic work song

Examples

The crew sang a chanty as they hauled the ropes on deck.
A well-known sea chanty echoed across the bay as the ship prepared to leave.
He collected old chanties from sailors around the world for his research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shanty - a sailors' work song; variant spelling

Etymology

Alteration of 'shanty', probably from French 'chanter' (to sing).
