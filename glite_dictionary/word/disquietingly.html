disquietingly

1. ADV

/ dɪsˈkwaɪ.ɪtɪŋli / (AmE), / dɪsˈkwaɪ.ɪtɪŋli / (BrE)
In a way that causes anxiety, uneasiness, or worry.
in an unsettling or disturbing manner

Examples

The storm clouds gathered disquietingly on the horizon, making everyone uneasy about the coming night.
The movie's soundtrack shifted disquietingly as the suspenseful scene unfolded.
He smiled disquietingly, leaving us unsure of his true intentions.

Synset Examples

the disquietingly close sounds of gunfire

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reassuringly - in a way that makes someone feel less worried
  • Synonym: unsettlingly - in an unsettling or disturbing way

Etymology

From 'disquieting' + '-ly', with 'disquieting' deriving from 'disquiet' (from Old French 'desquiet', from Latin 'quietus' meaning 'quiet').
