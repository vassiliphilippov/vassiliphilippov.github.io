Rorippa nasturtium-aquaticum

1. NOUN

/ rəˈrɪpə næˌstɜr.ʃəm əˈkwætɪkəm / (AmE), / rəˈrɪpə næˌstɜːʃəm ɔːˈkwætɪkəm / (BrE)
A perennial Eurasian plant, commonly known as watercress, that grows in running water and has fleshy, pungent leaves used in salads, as a potherb, or as a garnish.
species of watercress plant

Examples

Rorippa nasturtium-aquaticum thrives in clean, flowing streams and is often harvested for culinary use.
Chefs enjoy using Rorippa nasturtium-aquaticum because of its peppery flavor and crisp texture in salads.
The riverbank was covered with patches of Rorippa nasturtium-aquaticum, attracting both foragers and wildlife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watercress - edible aquatic plant with spicy leaves

Etymology

From Latin: Rorippa (genus name), nasturtium-aquaticum meaning 'watercress'.
