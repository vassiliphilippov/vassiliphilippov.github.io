Penstemon rydbergii

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛn.stəˌmɒn rɪdˈbɜːr.ɡi.aɪ / (AmE), / ˈpɛnstɪmən rɪdˈbɜːɡi.aɪ / (BrE)
a perennial plant native to western North America, bearing whorls of small dark blue-violet flowers, found from Washington to Wyoming, and south to California and Colorado
a plant species of western North America

Examples

The meadow was filled with Penstemon rydbergii, their blue-violet flowers brightening the landscape.
Botanists identified Penstemon rydbergii growing along the trails in Colorado.
During the summer, Penstemon rydbergii blooms can be seen from Washington to California.

Etymology

Named after the genus Penstemon and the botanist Per Axel Rydberg.
