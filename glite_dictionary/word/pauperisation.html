pauperisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɔːpərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɔːpəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making someone or a group of people poor
the act of making someone poor

Examples

The pauperisation of rural communities was a major concern after the drought.
Rapid industrialisation sometimes led to the pauperisation of traditional artisans.
He blamed government policies for the pauperisation of the working class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impoverishment - the process or result of making someone poor

Etymology

From French 'paupérisation', based on 'pauper' from Latin 'pauper' (poor).
