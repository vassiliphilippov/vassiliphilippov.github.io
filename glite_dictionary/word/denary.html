denary

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛn.ə.ri / (AmE), / ˈdiː.nə.ri / (BrE)
relating to or based on the number ten; proceeding by tens, especially in reference to the decimal system.
based on the number ten

Examples

Many modern currencies operate on a denary system of units and subunits.
The number 57 is written as 101001 in binary but as 57 in the denary system.
Some students found it easier to convert numbers into denary before performing arithmetic operations.

Synset Examples

the decimal system

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decimal - relating to or based on the number ten

Etymology

From Latin 'denarius' (containing ten), from 'deni' (ten each).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdiːˌner.i / (AmE), / ˈdɛn.əri / (BrE)
relating to or based on the number ten; containing ten parts
relating to the number ten

Examples

The denary system is commonly used in everyday arithmetic.
A denary group consists of exactly ten members.
Roman armies were organized into denary units for certain formations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decimal - relating to the number ten; based on powers of ten

Etymology

From Latin 'deni' (ten each) + '-ary'.
