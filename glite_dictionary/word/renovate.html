to renovate

1. VERB

/ ˈrɛnəˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛnəʊveɪt / (BrE)
to give new life, energy, or freshness to something or someone
give new energy or life to

Examples

A short vacation can renovate your tired mind and body.
Her optimistic attitude renovated the gloomy mood of the team.
A walk in nature always renovates my spirit after a stressful day.

Synset Examples

A hot soup will revive me
This will renovate my spirits
This treatment repaired my health

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revive - restore to life or consciousness; give new strength or energy
  • Synonym: to refresh - make someone feel energetic or reinvigorated

Etymology

From Latin renovare, from re- (again) + novare (make new).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00024272-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈrɛnəˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛnəʊveɪt / (BrE)
to repair and improve something, especially a building, so that it is in good condition again
restore or improve (especially a building)

Examples

They decided to renovate the old farmhouse rather than tear it down.
The museum will close for a year to renovate its main gallery.
She hired a contractor to renovate her kitchen with new cabinets and appliances.

Synset Examples

They renovated the ceiling of the Sistine Chapel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restore - to bring back to a former condition
  • Synonym: to refurbish - to make clean, bright, or new again
  • Antonym: to demolish - to completely destroy a building

Etymology

From Latin renovare (to renew), from re- (again) + novare (make new), from novus (new).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00164009-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈrɛnəˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛnəʊveɪt / (BrE)
to make something look new, better, or more attractive by repairing and improving it
to repair and improve (something)

Examples

They plan to renovate the old library next year.
After buying the house, she decided to renovate the kitchen and bathrooms.
The city is investing millions to renovate its historic buildings downtown.

Synset Examples

we refurbished the guest wing
My wife wants us to renovate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to refurbish - to make clean, bright, or fresh again; renovate
  • Synonym: to restore - to bring back to a former condition
  • Antonym: to dilapidate - to bring into a state of decay or partial ruin

Etymology

From Latin *renovare* (to renew), from *re-* (again) + *novare* (make new).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00163249-v
Synset POS: v
