skim

1. VERB

/ skɪm / (AmE), / skɪm / (BrE)
(of an object or creature) to move lightly and quickly across the surface of water without sinking.
move quickly across water's surface

Examples

The flat stone began to skim across the lake after he threw it.
Water birds are known to skim the surface in search of food.
The small boat seemed to skim over the gentle waves, barely making a sound.
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ skɪm / (AmE), / skɪm / (BrE)
a thin layer or film that forms on the surface of a liquid, often as the result of fat, scum, or impurities rising to the top
thin layer on liquid's surface

Examples

There was a thin skim of oil on the surface of the pond.
A pale skim formed on her tea as it cooled.
He carefully removed the skim from the top of the milk.

Synset Examples

there was a thin skim of oil on the water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: film - a thin layer on a surface
  • Synonym: scum - a layer of dirt or froth on the surface of a liquid
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09459435-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ skɪm / (AmE), / skɪm / (BrE)
to remove a substance from the surface of a liquid, especially by scooping or taking it off carefully
remove from the surface (usually a liquid)

Examples

After boiling the soup, she used a spoon to skim the fat from the top.
It is necessary to skim cream from fresh milk to make butter.
The chef asked his assistant to skim the foam off the broth before serving.

Synset Examples

skim cream from the surface of milk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scoop - take up or remove with a scoop or similar tool

Etymology

From Middle English skimmen, from Old French eskumer (to remove scum), from escume (scum).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01263582-v
Synset POS: v

6. VERB

/ skɪm / (AmE), / skɪm / (BrE)
to move swiftly and lightly over or along the surface of something
move lightly over a surface

Examples

The stone skimmed across the water before sinking.
A bird skimmed the surface of the lake at sunrise.
Clouds skimming the mountain peaks created a beautiful view.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glide - to move smoothly and continuously along, as if without effort
  • Antonym: plunge - to move or fall suddenly and quickly in a different direction (opposite of 'skim' in terms of movement quality)

Etymology

From Old Norse 'skima', meaning to shine or gleam faintly.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

7. ADJ

/ skɪm / (AmE), / skɪm / (BrE)
(Of milk or a milk product) having had the cream removed.
milk with cream removed

Examples

She prefers to drink skim milk in her coffee.
The cafeteria only serves skim milk in small cartons.
Some recipes call for skim yogurt to reduce fat content.

Synset Examples

yogurt made with skim milk
she can drink skimmed milk but should avoid butter

Related Terms

  • Antonym: whole - (Of milk) containing all its natural fat; not skimmed.
  • Synonym: skimmed - (Of milk) same as 'skim' in British English; having had the cream removed.

Etymology

From the verb 'skim', meaning to remove a substance from the surface, originally from Middle English 'skymen', from Old Norse 'skima'.

Source: WordNet
