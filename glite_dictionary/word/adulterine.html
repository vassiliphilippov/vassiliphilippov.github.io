adulterine

1. ADJ

/ əˈdʌltərˌin / (AmE), / əˈdʌltjʊˌriːn / (BrE)
(of a child) conceived as a result of adultery; born to parents not lawfully married to each other, specifically when one or both are married to someone else.
conceived in adultery

Examples

The noble's adulterine son was denied inheritance by the court.
Many historical monarchies did not recognize adulterine children as legitimate heirs.
She grew up unaware of her adulterine origins until adulthood.

Etymology

From Latin adulterinus (illegitimate, spurious), from adulter (adulterer).
