baldric

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔl.drɪk / (AmE), / ˈbɔːl.drɪk / (BrE)
A wide, often ornamented belt worn over one shoulder and across the body to support a sword, bugle, or similar object at the opposite hip.
ornamental shoulder belt for sword or bugle

Examples

The knight adjusted his baldric before heading into battle.
She wore a ceremonial baldric adorned with gold and jewels.
The bugler’s baldric gleamed brightly in the morning sun.

Etymology

From Old French *baudre* or *baldre*, of Germanic origin.
