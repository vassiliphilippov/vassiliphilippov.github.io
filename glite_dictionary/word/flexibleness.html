flexibleness

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɛksɪbəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈflɛksɪblnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being flexible; the ability to bend or be shaped easily without breaking.
the quality of being flexible

Examples

Yoga improves the flexibleness of your joints and muscles.
The flexibleness of the material makes it ideal for crafting sculptures.
Her flexibleness in adapting to new situations was appreciated by her colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flexibility - the quality of being flexible; ability to bend easily

Etymology

From Latin 'flexibilis' (that may be bent) + English suffix '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈflɛksəblnəs / (AmE), / ˈflɛksɪblnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being able to adapt or change easily; adaptability or pliability
quality of being adaptable or pliable

Examples

We value the flexibleness of our new schedule, which allows us more personal time.
The flexibleness of this material makes it ideal for a range of uses.
Her flexibleness in negotiations often led to successful compromises.

Synset Examples

he enjoyed the flexibility of his working arrangement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flexibility - the quality of being able to change or adapt easily

Etymology

Formed from 'flexible' + '-ness', after Latin 'flexibilitas', source of 'flexibility'.
