detonator

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛtəˌneɪtər / (AmE), / ˈdɛtənətə / (BrE)
a device, usually mechanical or electrical, used to set off an explosive or to begin the reaction of a larger explosive charge
device used to initiate an explosion

Examples

The bomb squad carefully removed the detonator before transporting the device.
A remote detonator triggered the fireworks display at exactly midnight.
Engineers use a detonator to safely set off controlled explosions at the mining site.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blasting cap - a small detonator used to trigger a larger explosive charge

Etymology

From Latin 'detonare' (to thunder/explode), via French 'détonateur'.
