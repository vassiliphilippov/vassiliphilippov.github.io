to sermonise

1. VERB

/ ˈsɜːrməˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɜːmənaɪz / (BrE)
to speak to people as if delivering a sermon, especially by expressing moral judgments in a lengthy or tedious way
to speak in a moralizing manner

Examples

Whenever he disagreed, he would sermonise about the right way to live.
She tends to sermonise instead of offering practical advice.
I wish you wouldn't sermonise every time someone makes a mistake.

Synset Examples

This man always sermonizes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to preach - to deliver a sermon or moral advice, often in a formal manner

Etymology

From sermon + -ise, ultimately from Latin 'sermō' (discourse, speech).
