Reynard

1. PROPN

/ ˈreɪnɑrd / (AmE), / ˈreɪnɑːd / (BrE)
A conventional name for a fox, especially as used in fables and medieval tales such as 'Reynard the Fox.'
conventional name for a fox in tales

Examples

In medieval literature, Reynard is celebrated for his cunning wit and clever schemes.
Many European folktales portray Reynard as the ultimate trickster among animals.
The character of Reynard appears in stories across France, Germany, and the Low Countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fox - a small wild animal with reddish fur and a bushy tail

Etymology

From Old French Renart, the name of the fox in the medieval tales.
