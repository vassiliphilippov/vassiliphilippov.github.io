tomography

1. NOUN

/ təˈmɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / təˈmɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
A medical imaging technique that creates detailed pictures or cross-sections of the inside of the body.
medical imaging technique
medical

Examples

CT scans use tomography to create three-dimensional images of internal organs.
Modern hospitals often rely on tomography for accurate diagnosis of diseases.
Thanks to advances in tomography, doctors can now detect tumors much earlier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imaging - the process of creating visual representations of the interior of a body for clinical analysis
  • Synonym: radiography - imaging technique using X-rays

Etymology

From Greek 'tomos' (slice, section) + 'graphia' (writing or recording).
