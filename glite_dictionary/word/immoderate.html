immoderate

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈmɑdərət / (AmE), / ɪˈmɒdərət / (BrE)
excessive or extreme; going beyond what is considered reasonable or acceptable
excessive; beyond reasonable limits

Examples

Her immoderate drinking worried her family and friends.
The immoderate demands from the client delayed the project.
He had an immoderate appetite for sweets, eating entire boxes of chocolates in one sitting.

Synset Examples

immoderate laughter
immoderate spending

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excessive - more than is necessary, normal, or desirable
  • Antonym: moderate - kept within reasonable limits; not excessive

Etymology

From Latin immoderatus, from in- 'not' + moderatus 'moderate'.
