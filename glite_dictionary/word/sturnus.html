Sturnus

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɜrnəs / (AmE), / ˈstɜːnəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Sturnidae, comprising the common starlings.
genus of starlings

Examples

The European starling, scientifically named Sturnus vulgaris, is widespread across Europe and North America.
Researchers studied the breeding habits of several species within the genus Sturnus.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot different members of Sturnus during their spring survey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: starling - a small to medium-sized passerine bird

Etymology

From Latin *sturnus* (starling).
