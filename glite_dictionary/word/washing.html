washing

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɒʃɪŋ / (BrE)
clothes, linens, or other items that are to be washed or have just been washed
clothes or linens for washing

Examples

She hung the washing out to dry in the backyard.
There's a pile of washing waiting to be ironed in the laundry room.
He brought in the washing before it started to rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laundry - clothes or linens that need to be washed or have been washed

Etymology

From Old English 'wæscan', related to the verb 'wash'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɑʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɒʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of cleansing something, usually with soap and water
the act of cleaning with water and soap

Examples

After a long day of hiking, the washing of muddy clothes was a big task.
She let her hands soak in soapy water during the washing of the dishes.
Regular washing of your car helps maintain its appearance and value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cleaning - the act of making something clean

Etymology

From Old English 'wæscan' (to wash) with the suffix '-ing' forming a noun.
