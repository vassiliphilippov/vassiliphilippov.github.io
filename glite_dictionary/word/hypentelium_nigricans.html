Hypentelium nigricans

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌpɛnˈtɛliəm nɪˈɡrɪkænz / (AmE), / hɪˌpɛnˈtiːliəm nɪˈɡrɪkænz / (BrE)
A species of freshwater fish, commonly known as the northern hogsucker, found in warm, clear, shallow streams across North America.
species of freshwater fish (northern hogsucker)

Examples

The Hypentelium nigricans thrives in clear, shallow streams throughout much of North America.
I spotted a Hypentelium nigricans while fishing in the creek near our cabin.
Biologists are studying the habitat preferences of Hypentelium nigricans to understand their distribution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: northern hogsucker - common name for Hypentelium nigricans

Etymology

From Greek 'hypo' (under), 'enteleia' (completeness), and Latin 'nigricans' (blackish), used in scientific taxonomy.
