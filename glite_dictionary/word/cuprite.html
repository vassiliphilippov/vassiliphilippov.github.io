cuprite

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːˌpraɪt / (AmE), / ˈkjuːˌpraɪt / (BrE)
a red or brownish-red mineral consisting of cuprous oxide, and an important source of copper
a copper ore mineral

Examples

The deep red color of the cuprite sample made it easy to identify.
Miners extracted pounds of copper from veins rich in cuprite.
Cuprite is often found alongside minerals such as malachite and azurite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malachite - a green mineral used as a copper ore

Etymology

From Latin 'cuprum' (copper) and '-ite' (used to form names of minerals).
