balky

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɔki / (AmE), / ˈbɔːki / (BrE)
unwilling to proceed, act, or cooperate; stubborn or uncooperative, especially when expected to do so
uncooperative or stubborn

Examples

The old truck was balky and refused to start on cold mornings.
His balky attitude made the negotiations slow and difficult.
She tried to lead the balky horse, but it would not budge.

Synset Examples

a balking
a balky mule
a balky customer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stubborn - unwilling to change one's mind or actions
  • Antonym: compliant - willing to do what is asked

Etymology

From balk (verb, meaning to stop short or refuse), ultimately from Old English *balca* (ridge, bank).
