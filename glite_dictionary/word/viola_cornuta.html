Viola cornuta

1. PROPN

/ vaɪˈoʊlə kɔrˈnuːtə / (AmE), / vaɪˈəʊlə kɔːˈnuːtə / (BrE)
A species of European violet with an unusually long corolla spur, widely cultivated for its ornamental flowers.
a European violet species with long spurred flowers

Examples

The garden was filled with blooming Viola cornuta in shades of purple and blue.
Many gardeners choose Viola cornuta for its delicate fragrance and persistent flowers.
I planted Viola cornuta along the pathway to add color in early spring.

Etymology

From Latin: 'viola' meaning violet, and 'cornuta' meaning horned, referring to its long floral spur.
