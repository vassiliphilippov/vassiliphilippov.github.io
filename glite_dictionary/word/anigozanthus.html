Anigozanthus

1. NOUN

/ ˌænɪɡoʊˈzænθəs / (AmE), / ˌanɪɡəʊˈzanθəs / (BrE)
A genus of Australian perennial plants with woolly flowers on tall stems and sword-shaped leaves, including kangaroo's paw.
genus of Australian flowering plants

Examples

Many gardeners are fascinated by the unique flowers of Anigozanthus in their landscapes.
The most popular species of Anigozanthus is often referred to as kangaroo's paw.
Native to Western Australia, Anigozanthus thrives in well-drained, sunny locations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kangaroo's paw - common name for some species of Anigozanthus with distinctive paw-shaped flowers

Etymology

From Greek 'anisos' (unequal) and 'anthos' (flower), referring to the irregular shape of the flower.
