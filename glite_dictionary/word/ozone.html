ozone

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊzoʊn / (AmE), / ˈəʊzəʊn / (BrE)
A colorless gas (O3) with a distinct, sharp smell, found both in the Earth's lower atmosphere and in the stratosphere, where it forms a layer that protects the planet from harmful ultraviolet radiation.
colorless gas O3, protects from UV radiation

Examples

The ozone layer plays a crucial role in shielding Earth from ultraviolet rays.
After the thunderstorm, the air was filled with the distinctive scent of ozone.
Scientists are concerned about the depletion of ozone in the upper atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oxygen - a colorless, odorless gas (O2) essential for respiration; ozone is an allotrope of oxygen

Etymology

From Greek ozein (to smell), due to its distinctive odor.
