spermatocide

1. NOUN

/ spɚˈmæt̬əˌsaɪd / (AmE), / spɜːˈmatəsʌɪd / (BrE)
A contraceptive substance that kills sperm cells to prevent fertilization.
sperm-killing contraceptive agent
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended a spermatocide as an option for additional contraception.
Some gels and foams that act as spermatocides can be purchased without a prescription.
She researched different spermatocide products before choosing one that suited her needs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spermicide - A contraceptive agent that destroys sperm

Etymology

From spermato- (Greek sperma, spermatos, meaning 'seed') + -cide (Latin caedere, meaning 'to kill').
