senna

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnə / (AmE), / ˈsɛnə / (BrE)
any of various tropical and subtropical plants of the genus Senna, with compound leaves and showy, usually yellow flowers; many species are used in herbal medicine, especially as laxatives.
a genus of medicinal flowering plants

Examples

Senna leaves are commonly used as a natural remedy for constipation.
The garden features a beautiful senna plant in full bloom.
Traditional medicine often includes extracts of senna for their laxative properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cassia - another genus of plants, sometimes overlapping with senna, also used medicinally

Etymology

From Arabic 'sanā', from Greek 'sénna'.
