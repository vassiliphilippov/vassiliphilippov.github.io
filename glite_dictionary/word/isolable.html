isolable

1. ADJ

/ ˈaɪsələbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈaɪsələbl̩ / (BrE)
capable of being separated or isolated from other things; able to be isolated.
capable of being isolated

Examples

The virus is isolable from blood samples in the early stages of infection.
Some minerals are isolable through chemical processes in the laboratory.
This compound is not easily isolable in pure form due to its instability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to isolate - to separate something from others

Etymology

From French 'isolable', from 'isoler' (to isolate), ultimately from Italian 'isolare', from 'isola' (island).
