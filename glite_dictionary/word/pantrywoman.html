pantrywoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæntriˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈpæntriˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a female servant, usually the chief servant in a household, responsible for wines and the table
female servant in charge of wines and table

Examples

The wealthy family employed a pantrywoman to oversee their wine and silverware.
As the pantrywoman, she was entrusted with the keys to the cellar and the plate cupboard.
During the grand dinner, the pantrywoman ensured the table settings were immaculate and the wine was properly served.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pantryman - male servant in charge of the pantry
  • Synonym: butler - chief male servant of a household

Etymology

Formed from 'pantry' (from Old French 'paneterie', a bread room) + 'woman'.
