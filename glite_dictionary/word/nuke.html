nuke

1. NOUN

/ nuːk / (AmE), / njuːk / (BrE)
an informal term for a nuclear warhead or missile designed to deliver an atomic bomb
informal: nuclear warhead or bomb
informal

Examples

The country threatened to launch a nuke if negotiations failed.
They detected a nuke being transported across the border.
The treaty aims to ban the use of any nuke in warfare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuclear bomb - a bomb that derives its destructive power from nuclear reactions

Etymology

Shortened from 'nuclear' (as in nuclear bomb or warhead), from Latin 'nucleus' (kernel).

2. VERB

/ nuk / (AmE), / njuːk / (BrE)
to attack or destroy something using nuclear weapons
attack with nuclear weapons

Examples

Military leaders considered whether to nuke the enemy base during the conflict.
If either side threatens to nuke another country, the consequences could be catastrophic.
In the film, the villains planned to nuke a major city as part of their plot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bomb - attack with explosive devices

Etymology

Shortened and altered from 'nuclear'.
