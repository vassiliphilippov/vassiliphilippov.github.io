Anthriscus

1. NOUN

/ ænˈθrɪskəs / (AmE), / ænˈθrɪskəs / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the parsley family, including chervil, native to Europe, North Africa, and Asia.
a genus of plants including chervil

Examples

The herb Anthriscus is often used in French cuisine for its delicate flavor.
Gardeners recommend planting Anthriscus in partial shade to encourage healthy growth.
Some wild species of Anthriscus can be mistaken for parsley due to their similar leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chervil - an aromatic herb used in cooking

Etymology

From Latin 'anthriscus', from Greek 'anthriskos' meaning chervil.

2. NOUN

/ ænˈθrɪs.kəs / (AmE), / ænˈθrɪs.kəs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the parsley family, known for including chervil and related species, native to Europe, North Africa, and Asia.
genus of plants including chervil

Examples

Anthriscus is commonly found in the wild across Europe and parts of Asia.
The culinary herb chervil belongs to the genus Anthriscus.
Botanists have identified several species of Anthriscus in North Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chervil - an aromatic herb in the parsley family, used in cooking

Etymology

From Greek anthriskos, a type of plant.
