Hedysarum boreale

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛdɪˈsɛrəm bɔˈreɪli / (AmE), / ˌhɛdɪˈsæːrəm bɒˈriːəliː / (BrE)
A perennial herb native to western North America, bearing racemes of pink to purple flowers and distinctive flat, jointed seed pods.
a perennial western North American herb

Examples

The roots of Hedysarum boreale are valued in traditional medicine by some Indigenous peoples.
In summer, Hedysarum boreale produces clusters of showy purple flowers along its stems.
Hedysarum boreale is often planted to restore disturbed rangelands due to its hardiness.

Etymology

From Latin; Hedysarum from Greek *hēdys* (sweet), referencing the sweet taste of some related species, and boreale meaning 'northern'.
