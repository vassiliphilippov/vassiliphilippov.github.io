putrescent

1. ADJ

/ pjuˈtrɛsənt / (AmE), / pjuˈtrɛsənt / (BrE)
beginning to decay or rot; becoming putrid.
beginning to decay or rot

Examples

The warm air was thick with the odor of putrescent garbage left out too long.
Vultures circled above the putrescent remains of the animal in the field.
A putrescent smell indicated that the food in the fridge had spoiled.

Synset Examples

a trail lined by putrescent carcasses

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: putrid - in a state of decomposition; decomposed and foul-smelling

Etymology

From Latin putrescentem, present participle of putrescere (to begin to rot).
