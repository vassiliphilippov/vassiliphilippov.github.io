ingenious

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdʒinjəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒiːniəs / (BrE)
(of a person or a thing) showing originality, inventiveness, and skill in solving problems or creating new things.
showing inventiveness and skill

Examples

Her ingenious design for the water filter helped bring clean water to remote villages.
The detective came up with an ingenious plan to catch the thief red-handed.
It was an ingenious solution that saved both time and money.

Synset Examples

a clever gadget
the cunning maneuvers leading to his success
an ingenious solution to the problem

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clever - quick to understand, learn, and devise or apply ideas
  • Synonym: resourceful - having the ability to find quick and clever ways to overcome difficulties
  • Antonym: unimaginative - lacking originality or inventiveness

Etymology

From Latin ingeniosus (endowed with genius, clever), from ingenium (innate quality, intellect).
