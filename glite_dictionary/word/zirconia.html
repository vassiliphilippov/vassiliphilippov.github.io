zirconia

1. NOUN

/ zɚˈkoʊniə / (AmE), / zɜːˈkəʊniə / (BrE)
a white crystalline oxide of zirconium, used in making ceramics, refractories, insulation, abrasives, enamels, and glazes
white oxide of zirconium used in ceramics and industry

Examples

Industrial furnaces often use zirconia bricks because of their high resistance to heat.
Cubic zirconia is widely used as a diamond substitute in jewelry.
The enamel's durability is enhanced by the addition of zirconia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zirconium - a chemical element, metallic, from which zirconia is derived

Etymology

From zircon + -ia, patterned after 'alumina' (aluminium oxide).
