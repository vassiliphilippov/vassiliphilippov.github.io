Islay

1. PROPN

/ ˈaɪ.lə / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.lə / (BrE)
an island off the west coast of Scotland, at the southern end of the Inner Hebrides, known for its whisky distilleries and rugged scenery.
Scottish island in the Inner Hebrides

Examples

Many famous Scotch whiskies are produced on Islay, making the island a popular destination for whisky enthusiasts.
The dramatic coastline of Islay attracts visitors seeking natural beauty and wildlife.
She spent her summer holiday exploring historic sites and tasting local flavors on Islay.

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic 'Ìle,' the name of the island.
