islay

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪsleɪ / (AmE), / ˈaɪleɪ / (BrE)
a California evergreen wild plum tree with spiny, leathery leaves and white flowers, native to western North America
California evergreen wild plum tree

Examples

The islay thrives in the dry chaparral regions of California.
Clusters of white blossoms appear on the islay each spring.
Local wildlife often feed on the fruit of the islay tree.

Etymology

From Spanish *islay*, likely of indigenous Californian origin, used in regional botanical English.
