musculus temporalis

1. NOUN

/ mʌsˈkjuːləs tɛmpəˈreɪlɪs / (AmE), / mʌsˈkjuːləs tɛmˈpɔːrəlɪs / (BrE)
the broad, fan-shaped muscle on each side of the head that raises the lower jaw and closes the mouth
muscle raising jaw, closing mouth
medical

Examples

The musculus temporalis plays a crucial role in chewing by elevating the mandible.
Damage to the musculus temporalis can result in difficulty closing the mouth.
During surgery, the musculus temporalis is sometimes used for reconstructive procedures in the jaw area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temporal muscle - common English name for musculus temporalis

Etymology

From Latin: 'musculus' (muscle) and 'temporalis' (of the temple, referring to the side of the head).
