Periactin

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛriˈæktɪn / (AmE), / ˌpɛriˈæktɪn / (BrE)
A trademark for an antihistamine drug (cyproheptadine) used to treat allergic reactions.
trademarked antihistamine medication
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Periactin to help relieve his allergy symptoms.
After taking Periactin, she noticed a reduction in her itching and sneezing.
Some patients use Periactin for its appetite-stimulating effects, as recommended by their physician.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antihistamine - a drug used to counteract the effects of histamine and alleviate allergic reactions
