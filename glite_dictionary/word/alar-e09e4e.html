alar

1. ADJ

/ ˈeɪ.lɑr / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.lɑː / (BrE)
having or resembling wings; wing-shaped
having or resembling wings

Examples

The bird's alar appendages stretched wide as it prepared to take flight.
An insect's alar structure makes it easily distinguishable from other arthropods.
The artist sculpted alar motifs onto the sides of the monument.

Etymology

From Latin 'ala' (wing) + '-ar' (adjectival suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈeɪ.lɑr / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.lɑː / (BrE)
Relating to or situated in the axil, especially in a botanical context.
of or relating to the axil (botany)
medical

Examples

The buds emerging from the alar position become new branches in the spring.
Botanists noted the presence of alar shoots in this unusual species.
In some plants, alar flowers appear at the axil of each leaf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axillary - relating to or located in the axil

Etymology

From Latin 'ala' meaning 'wing'; used in botany to describe position at the angle between leaf and stem (axil).
