Gymnophiona

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪm.noʊˈfaɪ.ə.nə / (AmE), / dʒɪm.nəˈfaɪ.ə.nə / (BrE)
An order of amphibians that comprises the limbless, burrowing caecilians.
an order of limbless amphibians (caecilians)

Examples

The order Gymnophiona is distinguished by its members' limbless bodies and burrowing habits.
Researchers have discovered several new species in the Gymnophiona order in South America.
Unlike frogs and salamanders, Gymnophiona are rarely seen due to their subterranean lifestyle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caecilian - a limbless, burrowing amphibian belonging to the order Gymnophiona

Etymology

From Greek 'gymnos' (naked) + 'ophis' (snake), referring to their snake-like appearance.
