enclave

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.kleɪv / (AmE), / ˈɒn.kleɪv / (BrE)
a territory, or part of a territory, surrounded by a foreign territory and culturally distinct from it
territory surrounded by another, culturally distinct

Examples

The city is home to a vibrant enclave of artists with their own distinct traditions.
The small mountain village formed a cultural enclave within the larger region.
Political tensions increased after the government tried to annex the ethnic enclave.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exclave - a portion of a state or territory geographically separated from the main part by surrounding foreign territory

Etymology

From French 'enclave,' from Medieval Latin 'inclavatus' (shut in).
