Asphodeline

1. NOUN

/ æˈsfɑːdəˌlaɪn / (AmE), / æˈsfɒdəliːn / (BrE)
a genus of perennial or biennial herbs with fragrant flowers in long racemes, native to the Mediterranean region and Caucasus
a genus of Mediterranean flowering herbs

Examples

Botanists recently discovered a rare species within the genus Asphodeline in southern Turkey.
The striking yellow blooms of Asphodeline are often used in Mediterranean gardens for their fragrance.
Many gardeners appreciate Asphodeline for its tall, cylindrical flower racemes in spring.

Etymology

From modern Latin Asphodeline, formed from Greek asphodelos (asphodel).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæsfəˈdəˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˌæsfədəˈliːn / (BrE)
A genus of perennial or biennial herbs native to the Mediterranean region and Caucasus, with rhizomatous roots and fragrant flowers arranged in long cylindrical racemes.
genus of Mediterranean perennial herbs

Examples

The garden features several varieties of Asphodeline that bloom in late spring.
Botanists studied samples of Asphodeline collected in the Caucasus region.
The fragrant flowers of Asphodeline attract both bees and butterflies.

Etymology

From Greek asphodelos (asphodel), referring to a type of plant.
