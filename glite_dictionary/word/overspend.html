to overspend

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈspɛnd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈspɛnd / (BrE)
to spend more money than one can afford or than was planned
spend more than can afford

Examples

It's easy to overspend during the holiday season if you don't set a budget.
We must be careful not to overspend on unnecessary equipment for the project.
She realized she had overspent her monthly allowance by the middle of the month.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to underspend - to spend less money than was planned or is usual
  • Synonym: splurge - to spend money freely or extravagantly

Etymology

From over- (excessive) + spend; modeled on Old and Middle English patterns of forming verbs with 'over-' meaning 'beyond, excessively'.

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈspɛnd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈspɛnd / (BrE)
to spend more money than you have or than was planned, especially from a budget
spend more than available or budgeted

Examples

If you overspend on your holiday, you might regret it when the bills arrive.
She tends to overspend every month, especially during the holiday season.
They overspent their budget and had to cut other expenses.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to underspend - to spend less than the amount available or planned

Etymology

From over- (exceeding) + spend (Middle English, from Old English spendan, from Latin expendere ‘pay out’).
