articulator

1. NOUN

/ ɑɹˈtɪkjəˌleɪtɚ / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪkjʊˌleɪtə / (BrE)
a person who pronounces words clearly and distinctly; someone who articulates speech sounds
a person who pronounces words

Examples

She is a skilled articulator who enunciates every syllable clearly.
The speech therapist praised his progress as an articulator of difficult sounds.
Good radio hosts must be clear articulators to reach a broad audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speaker - a person who speaks
  • Antonym: mumbler - a person who speaks unclearly

Etymology

From Latin 'articulare' (to divide into joints, utter distinctly).

2. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈtɪkjəˌleɪtər / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪkjʊleɪtə / (BrE)
a movable organ in the mouth, such as the tongue or lips, used in producing speech sounds
movable organ for speech production
medical

Examples

The tongue is a primary articulator in the production of many speech sounds.
Damage to any articulator can significantly affect a person's ability to speak clearly.
Linguists study how each articulator contributes to the formation of different consonants and vowels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speech organ - any organ involved in producing speech sounds
  • Synonym: articulation - the act of producing speech sounds using speech organs

Etymology

From Latin 'articulare' (to divide into joints), from 'articulus' (a joint).
