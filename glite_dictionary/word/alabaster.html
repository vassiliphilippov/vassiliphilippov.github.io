alabaster

1. NOUN

/ ˈæləˌbæstər / (AmE), / ˈaləbɑːstə / (BrE)
a hard, compact variety of calcite that is often white and translucent, used for carving and ornamental work
compact, white mineral used in carving

Examples

The intricate statue was carved from pure alabaster by skilled artisans.
Sunlight filtered through the thin alabaster window, creating a soft glow inside the chapel.
Museum visitors admired the delicate vases made of polished alabaster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gypsum - a soft white or grey mineral, sometimes called alabaster in other contexts

Etymology

From Old French 'alabastre', from Latin 'alabaster', from Greek 'alábastros' (vase of alabaster).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæləˌbæstər / (AmE), / ˈaləbɑːstə / (BrE)
a very pale white color, often used to describe skin, marble, or other surfaces with a smooth, translucent whiteness
a very light white color

Examples

Her complexion was the color of alabaster, smooth and almost translucent in the morning sun.
The artist chose alabaster for the statue's finish to convey purity and delicacy.
Clouds drifted across an alabaster sky just before dawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ivory - a smooth, creamy-white color like that of elephant tusks
  • Degree Higher: snow - an intensely white color, like that of fresh snow

Etymology

From Old French alabastre, via Latin from Greek alabastros, a type of vessel made from the mineral.

3. NOUN

/ ˈæl.əˌbæs.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈæl.əˌbæs.tə / (BrE)
a smooth, white or lightly tinted mineral (gypsum) that is soft and easily carved, often used for sculpture and decorative objects
soft, white mineral used for carving

Examples

The statue was carved from pure alabaster, giving it a soft, luminous quality.
Antique vases made of alabaster are highly valued by collectors.
Intricate patterns were etched into the alabaster tablet by the skilled artisan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marble - a hard, crystalline form of limestone used for sculpture and building

Etymology

From Old French 'alabastre', from Latin 'alabaster', from Greek 'alabastros' (a kind of vessel or the stone used for it).

/ ˈæləˌbæstər / (AmE), / ˈaləbɑːstə / (BrE)
having a smooth, pale, translucent appearance resembling that of alabaster; often used to describe skin or objects that are white and slightly lustrous.
smooth, pale, like alabaster

Examples

Her alabaster skin seemed to glow in the candlelight.
The sculptor carved an alabaster figurine that looked almost lifelike.
Clouds drifted across an alabaster sky, hiding the sun from view.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
