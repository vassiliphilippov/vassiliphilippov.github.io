Calamintha nepeta

1. NOUN

/ kæl.əˈmɪn.θə nɪˈpiː.tə / (AmE), / ka.ləˈmɪn.θə nɪˈpiː.tə / (BrE)
A low-growing, strongly aromatic perennial herb native to southern Europe and Great Britain, and naturalized in the United States.
aromatic perennial herb (plant species)

Examples

The gardener planted Calamintha nepeta along the walkway for its pleasant fragrance and attractive foliage.
Herbalists often use Calamintha nepeta in teas due to its aromatic leaves.
In some gardens, Calamintha nepeta helps attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus Calamintha and species name nepeta, both from Latin names for mint-like plants.
