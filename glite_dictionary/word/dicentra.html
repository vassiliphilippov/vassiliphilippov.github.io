Dicentra

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈsɛntrə / (AmE), / daɪˈsɛntrə / (BrE)
A genus of North American and Asian herbaceous plants with divided leaves and irregular, pendant flowers, including bleeding heart.
a plant genus of divided-leaved herbs

Examples

The Dicentra in our garden blooms every spring with beautiful pink flowers.
Many species in the Dicentra genus are popular in ornamental horticulture.
She planted a variety of Dicentra to attract pollinators to her yard.

Etymology

From Greek 'di-' (two) + 'kentron' (spur), referring to the two-spurred flowers.

2. NOUN

/ daɪˈsɛntrə / (AmE), / daɪˈsɛntrə / (BrE)
A genus of North American and Asian herbs with divided leaves and irregular, often heart-shaped flowers.
genus of herbs with irregular flowers

Examples

Many gardeners appreciate Dicentra for its delicate, heart-shaped flowers and fernlike foliage.
The bleeding heart is a popular plant in the Dicentra genus, known for its ornamental beauty.
Researchers are studying several Dicentra species native to mountainous regions of Asia.

Etymology

From Modern Latin, combining Greek 'di-' (twice) and 'kentron' (spur), referring to the flower's structure.
