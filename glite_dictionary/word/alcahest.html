alcahest

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.kəˌhɛst / (AmE), / ˈæl.kəˌhɛst / (BrE)
a hypothetical universal solvent once sought by alchemists, believed to be capable of dissolving any substance
universal solvent of alchemy

Examples

For centuries, alchemists pursued the alcahest as the key to unlocking nature's secrets.
The legend of the alcahest reflects humanity's long fascination with transformation and dissolution.
Despite countless experiments, the alcahest remained a myth in the world of early science.

Etymology

From New Latin 'alcahestus', of uncertain origin, possibly an alteration of Arabic 'al-kīmiya' (alchemy).
