Sassenach

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæsəˌnæk / (AmE), / ˈsasənak / (BrE)
(in Scottish English) a term for an English person, especially as used by Scots.
Scottish term for an English person
british

Examples

The old Highlander muttered something about the annoying Sassenach who had just arrived.
She was often teased by her friends for marrying a Sassenach.
The locals didn't seem too fond of the Sassenach tourists visiting their village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Englishman - a man from England
  • Antonym: Scot - a person from Scotland

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic 'Sasannach' (English person), from 'Sasunn' (England).
