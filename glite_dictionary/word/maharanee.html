maharanee

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːhəˈrɑni / (AmE), / ˌmɑːhəˈrɑːni / (BrE)
A queen or princess in India, especially the wife of a maharaja.
Indian queen or princess; wife of a maharaja

Examples

The maharanee hosted a lavish banquet in honor of the visiting dignitaries.
Legends tell of a wise maharanee who ruled alongside her husband with great compassion.
During the festival, the maharanee wore a magnificent silk sari adorned with jewels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maharani - Alternate spelling of maharanee; Indian queen or wife of a maharaja
  • Antonym: maharaja - Indian prince or king

Etymology

From Hindi mahārānī, from Sanskrit mahā (great) + rānī (queen).
