Twinkie

1. NOUN

/ ˈtwɪŋki / (AmE), / ˈtwɪŋki / (BrE)
A small, sweet sponge cake with a synthetic cream filling, often sold as a packaged snack.
small cream-filled sponge cake

Examples

My lunch always included a Twinkie as a special treat.
She bought a box of Twinkies for the road trip.
As a child, he loved the taste of the creamy filling inside a Twinkie.

Etymology

Originally a brand name invented by James Dewar in 1930 for the Hostess snack cake.
