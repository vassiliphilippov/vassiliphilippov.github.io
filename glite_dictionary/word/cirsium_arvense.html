Cirsium arvense

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜr.si.əm ɑrˈvɛn.si / (AmE), / ˈsɪə.si.əm ɑːˈvɛn.siː / (BrE)
A species of perennial thistle native to Europe and western Asia, now widespread and considered an invasive weed in North America.
species of invasive thistle

Examples

Cirsium arvense can quickly take over agricultural fields, making it difficult for crops to thrive.
Farmers often struggle to eliminate Cirsium arvense from their land due to its persistent roots.
Authorities have launched programs to control the spread of Cirsium arvense in rural regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Canada thistle - common name for Cirsium arvense, especially in North America

Etymology

From Latin; 'Cirsium' means thistle, 'arvense' means 'of the field'.
