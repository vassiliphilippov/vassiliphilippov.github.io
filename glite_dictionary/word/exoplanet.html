exoplanet

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛk.soʊˌplæn.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛk.səʊˌplæn.ɪt / (BrE)
A planet that orbits a star outside our solar system.
planet outside our solar system

Examples

Astronomers recently discovered a new exoplanet that may have conditions suitable for life.
The study of exoplanets has revolutionized our understanding of the universe.
Scientists are using powerful telescopes to detect atmospheres on distant exoplanets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: planet - Celestial body orbiting a star

Etymology

From Greek 'exo-' (outside) + English 'planet' (from Greek 'planētēs', wanderer).
