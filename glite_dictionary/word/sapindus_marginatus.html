Sapindus marginatus

1. NOUN

/ sæˈpɪndəs mɑrˈdʒɪnətəs / (AmE), / sæˈpɪndəs mɑːˈdʒɪnətəs / (BrE)
A species of deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States, bearing pulpy fruit that contains saponin.
species of tree in the southwestern US

Examples

The leaves of the Sapindus marginatus turn a brilliant yellow in autumn.
Soap can be made from the saponin-rich fruit of Sapindus marginatus.
The Sapindus marginatus is often planted for its ornamental value in gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soapberry - A common name for trees of the genus Sapindus, which produce saponin-rich fruit.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Sapindus' (soap tree), marginatus (having a margin or edge).
