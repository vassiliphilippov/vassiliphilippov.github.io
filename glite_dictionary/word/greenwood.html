greenwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrinˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnwʊd / (BrE)
woodland in full leaf, especially as a place of rural retreat or adventure in literature and history
woodland in full leaf

Examples

Legends often place Robin Hood and his band in the depths of the greenwood.
In spring, the greenwood comes alive with the sound of birds and rustling leaves.
Poets have long praised the beauty of the greenwood in their verses.

Synset Examples

the greenwood was Robin Hood's home

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forest - a large area covered chiefly with trees and undergrowth
  • Synonym: woodland - land covered with trees

Etymology

From Middle English 'grene' (green) + 'wode' (wood); refers to trees and forests in leafy, verdant state.
