synergist

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪnɚdʒɪst / (AmE), / ˈsɪnədʒɪst / (BrE)
a drug or substance that increases the effectiveness of another drug when used together with it
substance that enhances another drug's effect
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a synergist to increase the painkiller's effectiveness in managing severe pain.
Some pesticides include a synergist to boost the potency of the primary chemical.
In certain therapies, one synergist can reduce the required dose of the main drug.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: antagonist - a drug that counteracts the effects of another drug

Etymology

From Greek 'synergos' (working together), via Medieval Latin.
