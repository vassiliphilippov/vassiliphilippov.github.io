glimpse

1. NOUN

/ ɡlɪmps / (AmE), / ɡlɪmps / (BrE)
a brief or quick look at someone or something
a brief look

Examples

She caught a glimpse of the celebrity as he hurried through the airport.
Through the open window, I had a glimpse of the garden beyond.
He only managed a brief glimpse of the answer before the page turned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glance - a brief look; similar meaning
  • Antonym: stare - to look for a long time; opposite in duration

Etymology

From Middle English 'glyms', from Old English 'glimsian' (to shine faintly); related to 'glimmer'.

2. NOUN

/ ɡlɪmps / (AmE), / ɡlɪmps / (BrE)
A brief or partial view of something.
a brief or partial view

Examples

She caught a glimpse of the actor before he disappeared into the car.
Out of the corner of his eye, he got a glimpse of something moving in the shadows.
The tour offered visitors a rare glimpse into the ancient temple's inner chambers.

Synset Examples

from the window he could catch a glimpse of the lake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peek - a quick or furtive look
  • Synonym: glance - a brief or hurried look
  • Degree Higher: look - to direct one's gaze in a specified direction

Etymology

From Middle English glimsen (to shine faintly), probably of Scandinavian origin; related to Danish glimse (to glimpse).

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ɡlɪmps / (AmE), / ɡlɪmps / (BrE)
a brief or faint indication or suggestion of something
a brief or vague indication

Examples

She caught a glimpse of hope in his words.
There was just a glimpse of understanding in his eyes.
The speech gave only a glimpse of the plan's true intentions.

Synset Examples

he caught only a glimpse of the professor's meaning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hint - a slight or indirect indication or suggestion
  • Synonym: trace - a very small amount or sign of something

Etymology

From Middle English glims, possibly related to 'glimmer.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06815302-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ɡlɪmps / (AmE), / ɡlɪmps / (BrE)
to see someone or something for a very short time or only partly
see briefly or partially

Examples

I managed to glimpse her before she disappeared into the crowd.
They glimpsed the deer as it darted across the path.
She glimpsed a smile on his face before he turned away.

Synset Examples

We glimpsed the Queen as she got into her limousine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spot - to see or notice someone or something suddenly
  • Antonym: miss - to fail to see or notice

Etymology

Probably from Middle English, perhaps from a variant of 'glimmer'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
