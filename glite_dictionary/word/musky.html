musky

1. ADJ

/ ˈmʌski / (AmE), / ˈmʌski / (BrE)
having a strong, sweet, and somewhat animal-like smell similar to musk
having the smell of musk

Examples

The flowers emitted a musky fragrance that filled the room.
His cologne had a musky scent that lingered long after he left.
As the wine aged, it developed a more musky aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: musk - a strong-smelling secretion from the musk deer, used in perfume

Etymology

From 'musk' + '-y', where 'musk' comes from Late Latin 'muscus', based on a Sanskrit word for 'testicle', alluding to the original source of musk.
