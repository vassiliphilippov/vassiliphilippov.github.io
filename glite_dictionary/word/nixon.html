Nixon

1. PROPN

/ ˈnɪksən / (AmE), / ˈnɪksən / (BrE)
(1913–1994) The 37th President of the United States (1969–1974), previously Vice President (1953–1961) under Eisenhower, who resigned following the Watergate scandal.
37th U.S. President; resigned in 1974

Examples

Nixon made a televised address to announce his resignation from the presidency in 1974.
Many historians continue to debate Nixon's lasting impact on American politics.
As Vice President, Nixon was known for his staunch anti-Communist stance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Watergate - political scandal leading to Nixon's resignation
  • Synonym: Eisenhower - 34th President of the United States, under whom Nixon was Vice President
