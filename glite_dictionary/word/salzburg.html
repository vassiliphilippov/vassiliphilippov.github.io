Salzburg

1. PROPN

/ ˈsæltsbɜrɡ, ˈzɑltsbʊrk / (AmE), / ˈzæltsbɜːɡ, ˈzɑːltsbʊːk / (BrE)
a city in western Austria, known for its baroque architecture and as the birthplace of Mozart; a major center for music and the arts
Austrian city, birthplace of Mozart

Examples

She spent a summer in Salzburg attending classical concerts and exploring historic sites.
The breathtaking scenery around Salzburg attracts tourists from around the world.
Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart was born in Salzburg in 1756.

Etymology

From German Salzburg, meaning 'Salt Fortress', referring to salt mines in the region.
