brave

1. ADJ

/ breɪv / (AmE), / breɪv / (BrE)
showing courage and determination when facing danger or pain
showing courage in danger

Examples

The firefighter was brave in rescuing the children from the burning building.
It takes a brave person to admit when they've made a mistake.
Despite the storm, the hikers remained brave and continued along the trail.

Synset Examples

Familiarity with danger makes a brave man braver but less daring
a frank courageous heart … triumphed over pain
set a courageous example by leading them safely into and out of enemy-held territory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courageous - having or showing courage
  • Synonym: fearless - without fear; very brave
  • Antonym: cowardly - lacking courage; easily scared

Etymology

From Old French 'brave' (splendid, valiant), possibly from Italian 'bravo' (bold) or Latin 'barbarus' (foreign, wild).

3. ADJ

/ breɪv / (AmE), / breɪv / (BrE)
willing to face danger, pain, or fear without being intimidated; showing courage
showing courage; not afraid

Examples

The brave firefighter rescued the child from the burning building.
She made a brave decision to speak out against injustice, despite the risks.
Many brave soldiers were honored for their acts of courage during the war.

Synset Examples

audacious explorers
fearless reporters and photographers
intrepid pioneers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courageous - having or showing courage
  • Synonym: fearless - not afraid; lacking fear
  • Antonym: timid - lacking courage or confidence; easily frightened

Etymology

From Middle French *brave* (brave, bold), Italian *bravo* (brave, bold), probably from Latin *barbarus* (foreign, barbarous, wild).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00250985-s
Synset POS: s

4. VERB

/ breɪv / (AmE), / breɪv / (BrE)
to face or deal with something dangerous, difficult, or unpleasant with courage
face with courage

Examples

Despite her fear, she braved the dark alley to get home quickly.
He braved the freezing weather to take his dog for a walk.
Many journalists brave dangerous situations to report the news firsthand.

Synset Examples

She braved the elements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to defy - to openly resist or refuse to obey

Etymology

From Middle French 'brave' (splendid, courageous), from Italian 'bravo' (brave, bold).
