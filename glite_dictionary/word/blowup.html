blowup

1. NOUN

/ ˈbloʊˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈbləʊˌʌp / (BrE)
A sudden and uncontrolled outburst of emotion, especially anger.
an emotional outburst

Examples

There was a serious blowup between the two friends over the misunderstanding.
Her unexpected blowup at the meeting shocked everyone in the room.
After several small arguments, he finally had a blowup and stormed out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outburst - a sudden release of strong emotion

2. NOUN

/ ˈbloʊ.ʌp / (AmE), / ˈbləʊ.ʌp / (BrE)
an explosion or a sudden and violent release of energy, especially as a result of a chemical or nuclear reaction
violent explosion or release of energy

Examples

The factory was destroyed in a massive blowup last year.
Scientists are studying the effects of a nuclear blowup on the environment.
The chemical plant experienced a sudden blowup after a leak went undetected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explosion - a violent bursting with noise and force

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'blow up.'

3. NOUN

/ ˈbloʊˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈbləʊʌp / (BrE)
a photograph that has been enlarged from its original size, typically for greater detail or emphasis
enlarged photographic print

Examples

The detective examined a blowup of the security footage to look for more details.
We hung a large blowup of our wedding photo in the living room.
She requested a blowup of her favorite snapshot so she could frame it.

Etymology

From phrase 'blow up' (to enlarge), adapted as a noun in photography.
