Guallatiri

1. PROPN

/ waː.jaˈti.ɾi / (AmE), / waː.jaˈtiː.ri / (BrE)
a volcano in the Andes of northern Chile, known as one of the world's highest active volcanoes
volcano in the Andes, Chile

Examples

The summit of Guallatiri is often covered in snow and steam vents.
Scientists monitor Guallatiri closely because of its history of eruptions.
We could see Guallatiri looming above the Chilean landscape as we drove through the Andes.

Etymology

From indigenous Aymara or Quechua, local to the region of northern Chile.
