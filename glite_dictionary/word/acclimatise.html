to acclimatise

1. VERB

/ əˈklaɪməˌtaɪz / (AmE), / əˈklaɪmətaɪz / (BrE)
to become accustomed to a new climate or new conditions; to adapt to a different environment
become adapted to a new climate or conditions
british

Examples

After a few days, the team began to acclimatise to the high altitude.
It took her months to fully acclimatise to life in the tropics.
Plants may struggle to acclimatise when moved to a very different environment.

Synset Examples

They never acclimatized in Egypt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to acclimatize - to become accustomed to a new climate or environment (American spelling)

Etymology

From French *acclimatiser*, derived from *climat* (climate).
