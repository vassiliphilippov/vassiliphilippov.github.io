atmoradiograph

1. NOUN

/ ˌætmoʊˈreɪdi.oʊˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˌatmɔːˈreɪdi.əˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a device used for recording or measuring the frequency of atmospheric electrical disturbances (atmospherics)
device measuring atmospheric electrical disturbances

Examples

The scientist calibrated the atmoradiograph before beginning the measurement of radio signals.
With the help of an atmoradiograph, researchers monitored frequent thunderstorms in the area.
The data collected by the atmoradiograph showed an increase in atmospheric disturbances during the night.

Etymology

From Latin 'atmosphaera' (atmosphere) and Greek 'radios' (ray) + '-graph' (instrument for recording).
