whiffletree

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪfəlˌtri / (AmE), / ˈwɪflɪtri / (BrE)
a crossbar attached to the traces of a draft animal and to the vehicle or implement it is pulling, used to distribute the load evenly.
crossbar in draft animal harness

Examples

The old carriage was restored with its original whiffletree still intact.
He examined the whiffletree to make sure it was firmly fixed before harnessing the horses.
A broken whiffletree can make pulling heavy loads much harder for the animal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: singletree - a similar crossbar for a single draft animal
  • Synonym: doubletree - a crossbar for yoking a pair of draft animals

Etymology

Probably an alteration of 'whippletree', from Middle English 'whipel-tree'.
