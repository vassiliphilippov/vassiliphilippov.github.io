frenzy

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɛnzi / (AmE), / ˈfrɛnzi / (BrE)
A state of uncontrolled excitement, agitation, or wild activity, often marked by intense emotion or violence.
violent excitement or agitation

Examples

The crowd was swept into a frenzy as the band began to play.
She worked herself into a frenzy of worry before the exam.
During the sale, shoppers rushed through the store in a frenzy to grab the best deals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hysteria - wildly uncontrolled emotion or excitement
  • Antonym: calm - a state of peacefulness or tranquility

Etymology

From Middle English 'frensie', from Old French 'frenesie', from Latin 'phrenesis', from Greek 'phrenēsis' (delirium).
