shelterbelt

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛltɚˌbɛlt / (AmE), / ˈʃɛltəbɛlt / (BrE)
A row of trees or shrubs planted to protect an area from wind and reduce soil erosion.
row of trees that reduces wind and erosion

Examples

The farmer planted a shelterbelt to protect his crops from strong winds.
Many prairie landscapes include a shelterbelt along the edge of fields.
A well-maintained shelterbelt can significantly reduce soil erosion and increase yield.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windbreak - a barrier of trees or shrubs that reduces wind force

Etymology

From 'shelter' + 'belt', referring to a belt (strip) of sheltering vegetation.
