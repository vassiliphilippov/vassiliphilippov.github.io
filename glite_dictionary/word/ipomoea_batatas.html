Ipomoea batatas

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪpəˈmoʊ.i.ə bəˈteɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌɪpəˈməʊ.i.ə bəˈtɑːtəs / (BrE)
A tropical plant of the morning glory family widely cultivated for its large, sweet, orange-fleshed tuberous root, commonly known as the sweet potato.
species of sweet potato vine

Examples

Scientists studied the growth patterns of Ipomoea batatas under different climate conditions.
Many traditional dishes in the Caribbean use Ipomoea batatas as a main ingredient.
Farmers rotate leguminous crops with Ipomoea batatas to maintain soil fertility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet potato - common name for the tuber of Ipomoea batatas

Etymology

From the scientific Latin name, based on Greek 'ipomoea' (a type of bindweed) and Taíno 'batata' (sweet potato).
