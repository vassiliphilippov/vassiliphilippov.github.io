Cupressaceae

1. NOUN

/ kuːˌprɛˈseɪʃiː / (AmE), / kjuːˌprɛˈsiːsiː / (BrE)
a family of coniferous trees and shrubs that includes cypresses, junipers, and many cedars
family of coniferous trees (cypresses, junipers, cedars)
medical

Examples

Members of the Cupressaceae family can be found on almost every continent.
Many garden hedges are made from species within the Cupressaceae.
The botanist specialized in the study of Cupressaceae trees and their characteristics.

Etymology

From Latin *Cupressus* (cypress) + botanical family suffix *-aceae*.

2. NOUN

/ kuːˌprɛsˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / kjuːˌprɛsˈiːsiː / (BrE)
a family of coniferous trees and shrubs including cypresses, junipers, and many cedars, characterized by scale-like or needle-like leaves and woody cones
family of coniferous trees and shrubs (cypresses, junipers, cedars)

Examples

The Cupressaceae are widespread, occurring on every continent except Antarctica.
Junipers and cypresses, both members of the Cupressaceae, are commonly found in temperate forests.
Many ornamental trees in city parks belong to the Cupressaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conifer - a type of tree that bears cones, such as pines and firs

Etymology

From the botanical Latin family name, derived from *Cupressus* (cypress).
