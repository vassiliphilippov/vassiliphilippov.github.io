Ocimum

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːsɪməm / (AmE), / ˈɒsɪməm / (BrE)
A genus of aromatic herbs in the mint family, including basil and related plants.
genus of aromatic herbs (basil family)

Examples

The genus Ocimum includes several species commonly used in cooking and traditional medicine.
Researchers are investigating the medicinal properties of Ocimum plants.
Sweet basil, a member of the Ocimum genus, is popular in many cuisines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basil - an aromatic herb used as a culinary seasoning, especially in Italian cuisine

Etymology

From Latin Ocimum and Greek ὤκιμον (ōkimon), meaning 'basil'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːsɪməm / (AmE), / ˈɒsɪməm / (BrE)
A genus of aromatic herbaceous plants of the mint family (Lamiaceae), including basil, widely used as culinary and ornamental herbs.
genus of aromatic herbs (basil)

Examples

The species Ocimum basilicum is commonly used to flavor Italian dishes.
Several varieties within the Ocimum genus are cultivated for their aromatic leaves.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of plants in the Ocimum group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basil - an aromatic herb, Ocimum basilicum

Etymology

From Latin *Ocimum*, from Ancient Greek ὤκιμον (ṓkimon), meaning 'basil'.
