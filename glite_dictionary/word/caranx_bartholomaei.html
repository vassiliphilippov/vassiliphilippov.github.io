Caranx bartholomaei

1. NOUN

/ kəˈræŋks bɑrˌθɑləˈmi.aɪ / (AmE), / kəˈræŋks bɑːˌθɒləˈmi.aɪ / (BrE)
A species of jack fish found in the western Atlantic Ocean and Gulf of Mexico, commonly known as the yellow jack or coolihoo.
a jack fish species of the western Atlantic

Examples

Divers often spot the distinctive yellow stripes of the Caranx bartholomaei near coral reefs.
The Caranx bartholomaei is prized by anglers for its fighting spirit when caught on the line.
Research shows that Caranx bartholomaei populations have shifted ranges due to water temperature changes.

Etymology

From Latin. 'Caranx' is a genus of fish, and 'bartholomaei' honors a person named Bartholomaeus.
