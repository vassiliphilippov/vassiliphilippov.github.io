conformity

1. NOUN

/ kənˈfɔrməti / (AmE), / kənˈfɔːməti / (BrE)
agreement or similarity in opinion or attitude; concurrence with others' views or standards.
agreement in opinion or attitude

Examples

The project team acted with remarkable conformity of opinion throughout the discussion.
There was little conformity among committee members on the best course of action.
Changing social values sometimes challenge traditional conformity within communities.

Synset Examples

we are in accord with your proposal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agreement - a situation in which people have the same opinion
  • Antonym: discord - lack of agreement or harmony between people or opinions

Etymology

From Latin 'conformitas' (similarity, likeness), from 'conformis' (similar, of like shape).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈfɔrməti / (AmE), / kənˈfɔːməti / (BrE)
the act of behaving in a way that matches the accepted standards or customs of a group or society, often implying an uncritical acceptance of conventional practices
compliance with accepted standards or norms

Examples

Social pressure often encourages conformity at the expense of individual creativity.
In some environments, conformity is valued more highly than innovation.
The school's strict dress code was intended to promote conformity among students.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonconformity - the refusal to conform to established customs or practices
  • Synonym: convention - a way in which something is usually done, especially within a particular area or activity

Etymology

From Latin 'conformitas', from 'conformare' (to fashion, to conform).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04809012-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ kənˈfɔrməti / (AmE), / kənˈfɔːməti / (BrE)
the practice of aligning one's attitudes, beliefs, or behavior with established standards or societal norms, especially orthodoxy in thoughts and beliefs
adherence to established beliefs or norms

Examples

There was a strong pressure towards conformity in the strict religious community.
The school encourages innovation rather than unquestioning conformity.
His lack of conformity made him stand out in a crowd of traditional thinkers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonconformity - refusal or failure to conform to prevailing rules or practices
  • Synonym: orthodoxy - authorized or generally accepted theory, doctrine, or practice

Etymology

From Latin 'conformitas', from 'conformis' (of the same form).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06221698-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ kənˈfɔːrməti / (AmE), / kənˈfɔːməti / (BrE)
the act of behaving or thinking in accordance with accepted rules, standards, or laws
compliance with accepted standards or norms

Examples

All team members must demonstrate conformity with the company's safety policies.
He questioned whether strict conformity to traditional methods stifled creativity.
There was little conformity among the group's opinions during the debate.

Synset Examples

their financial statements are in conformity with generally accepted accounting practices

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compliance - the act of obeying a wish, rule, or law
  • Antonym: nonconformity - failure or refusal to conform to accepted standards or customs

Etymology

From Old French conformité, from Latin conformitas, from conformare (to form, shape).

5. NOUN

/ kənˈfɔrməti / (AmE), / kənˈfɔːməti / (BrE)
the state of being similar or matching in form, appearance, or character
similarity or correspondence in form

Examples

The architect ensured the new building's conformity with the historic style of the neighborhood.
There must be close conformity between the final product and the approved design.
The uniforms promote a sense of conformity in appearance among the students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: similarity - resemblance; the state of being similar
  • Antonym: difference - the quality of being unlike or dissimilar

Etymology

From Latin 'conformitas', from 'conformis' meaning 'similar in form'.
