septette

1. NOUN

/ sɛpˈtɛt / (AmE), / sɛpˈtɛt / (BrE)
a group or set of seven similar people or things considered as a unit
a group of seven items or people

Examples

The panel was composed of a distinguished septette of scientists.
A rare septette of identical vases was discovered in the antique shop.
The composer wrote a septette for wind instruments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: septet - another term for a group of seven people or things

Etymology

From French 'sept' (seven), with '-ette' as a diminutive suffix.

2. NOUN

/ sɛpˈtɛt / (AmE), / sɛpˈtɛt / (BrE)
A group of seven people regarded as a unit, especially in music or performance.
group of seven people

Examples

The jazz band performed as a septette throughout the summer festival.
She joined a vocal septette that specialized in a cappella arrangements.
A string septette can create a richer sound than smaller ensembles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: septet - a group of seven people, especially musicians
  • Degree Higher: octet - a group of eight people or performers
  • Degree Lower: sextet - a group of six people or performers

Etymology

From French 'septette', from 'sept' meaning 'seven', modeled after words like 'quartette'.

3. NOUN

/ sɛpˈtɛt / (AmE), / sɛpˈtɛt / (BrE)
A group of seven musicians or singers performing together.
group of seven performers

Examples

The renowned septette enchanted the audience with their harmonious performance.
She joined a local jazz septette that plays at weekend festivals.
The composer wrote his new piece specifically for a septette of strings and woodwinds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: septet - group of seven musicians or singers
  • Degree Higher: octet - group of eight musicians or singers
  • Degree Lower: sextet - group of six musicians or singers

Etymology

From French 'septette', based on 'sept' (seven) and patterned after 'quartet'.

4. NOUN

/ sɛpˈtɛt / (AmE), / sɛpˈtɛt / (BrE)
a musical composition written for seven performers or instruments
musical composition for seven performers

Examples

The composer wrote a septette for clarinet, horn, and strings.
Their performance of Beethoven's famous septette received a standing ovation.
She prefers playing chamber works, especially the septette by Saint-Saëns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: septet - a group of seven musicians or a piece written for them

Etymology

From French 'septette', from Latin 'septem' (seven).
