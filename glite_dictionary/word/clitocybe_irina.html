Clitocybe irina

1. NOUN

/ ˌklaɪtəˈsaɪbi ɪˈriːnə / (AmE), / ˌklɪtəsɪˈbiː ɪˈriːnə / (BrE)
A species of edible mushroom with large, silky white caps and thick stalks, belonging to the genus Clitocybe.
an edible mushroom species

Examples

We found several Clitocybe irina mushrooms growing at the forest edge after the rain.
The silky white cap of the Clitocybe irina distinguishes it from other similar fungi.
She prepared a meal using freshly picked Clitocybe irina as the main ingredient.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Clitocybe' meaning 'sloping head', and species name 'irina' from Latin meaning 'peace', though specific epithet origin is unclear.
