artillery

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈtɪləri / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪləri / (BrE)
A branch or unit of the armed forces equipped with large-calibre guns used in warfare.
army unit equipped with big guns

Examples

The artillery moved into position behind the infantry to provide support during the battle.
During the exercise, the artillery coordinated a barrage to cut off enemy advances.
When the artillery unit arrived, their firepower shifted the momentum of the conflict.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infantry - soldiers trained, armed, and equipped to fight on foot

Etymology

From Old French 'artillerie', from 'artiller' (to equip) and ultimately from Latin 'articulum' (small joint).

2. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈtɪləri / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪləri / (BrE)
large-calibre guns, howitzers, or missile launchers used in warfare on land, typically organized as a distinct branch of the armed forces
large military guns or launchers

Examples

The enemy artillery began shelling the town at dawn.
Modern artillery is capable of firing shells over great distances.
During the battle, the advancing troops were supported by heavy artillery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cannon - large, heavy gun typically mounted on wheels or a fixed base
  • Antonym: infantry - soldiers marching or fighting on foot

Etymology

From Old French artillerie, based on Latin artillaria (equipment, implements).
