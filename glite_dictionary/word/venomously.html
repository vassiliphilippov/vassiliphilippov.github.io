venomously

1. ADV

/ ˈvɛnəməsli / (AmE), / ˈvɛnəməsli / (BrE)
In a manner expressing intense malice or spite; with extreme bitterness or hostility.
in a very spiteful or hostile manner

Examples

She spoke venomously about her former business partner during the interview.
His critics accused him of attacking opponents venomously rather than arguing reasonably.
The debate quickly turned personal, with each side responding more venomously than before.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maliciously - in a way that shows a desire to harm someone
  • Antonym: kindly - in a kind or benevolent manner

Etymology

From 'venomous' (containing venom), itself from Old French *venimeus*, from Latin *venenosus* (poisonous).
