cutthroat

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌtˌθroʊt / (AmE), / ˈkʌtˌθrəʊt / (BrE)
a person who murders another by cutting their throat; a ruthless murderer
a person who murders by cutting the throat

Examples

The notorious cutthroat was finally brought to justice after years on the run.
Stories of cutthroats lurking on dangerous roads terrified travelers in olden times.
The detective suspected that the murder was committed by a cutthroat known to the victim.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murderer - a person who commits murder

Etymology

From Middle English 'cut-throte', literally 'cut throat', referring to cutting a person's throat.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkʌtˌθroʊt / (AmE), / ˈkʌtˌθrəʊt / (BrE)
having no consideration for others in order to succeed; ruthlessly competitive
ruthlessly competitive

Examples

The company operates in a cutthroat industry where only the strongest survive.
There is fierce, cutthroat competition among technology startups.
She avoided the cutthroat tactics used by her rivals to gain promotion.

Synset Examples

cutthroat competition
bowelless readiness to take advantage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruthless - having no pity or compassion; merciless
  • Synonym: merciless - showing no mercy; cruel
  • Antonym: fair - treating people equally and not being ruthless

Etymology

Originally referring to a murderer who cut throats; now used figuratively for ruthless behavior.
