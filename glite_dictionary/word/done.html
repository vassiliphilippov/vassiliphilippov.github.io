done

1. ADJ

/ dʌn / (AmE), / dʌn / (BrE)
(of food) cooked thoroughly and ready to eat.
cooked and ready to eat

Examples

The chicken is done when its juices run clear.
Let me check if the cake is done by inserting a toothpick.
The steak was perfectly done to her liking.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: underdone - not cooked enough
  • Antonym: overdone - cooked too much

2. ADJ

/ dʌn / (AmE), / dʌn / (BrE)
having finished or arrived at the end of an activity, process, or task; completed.
finished; completed

Examples

Once the report is done, please send it to the manager.
By the time the guests arrived, dinner was already done.
Let me know when you are done with your homework.

Synset Examples

certain to make history before he's done
it's a done deed
after the treatment, the patient is through except for follow-up
almost through with his studies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finished - having completed something
  • Synonym: completed - brought to an end; finished
  • Antonym: incomplete - not finished or lacking some parts

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'do', from Old English 'don'.
