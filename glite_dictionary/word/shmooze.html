shmooze

1. NOUN

/ ʃmuːz / (AmE), / ʃmuːz / (BrE)
an informal and friendly chat, often intended to gain favor or build a relationship
friendly informal chat

Examples

We enjoyed a pleasant shmooze over coffee after the meeting.
The event was more than just business; it was a chance for a good shmooze.
He always likes a shmooze with old friends when he visits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chat - an informal conversation
  • Synonym: conversation - the act of talking with someone

Etymology

From Yiddish *shmuesn* (to chat), related to Hebrew *shimu'a* (report, rumor).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07151177-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ʃmuːz / (AmE), / ʃmuːz / (BrE)
to engage in casual or friendly conversation, often to gain an advantage or make a social connection
talk casually in a friendly way
informal

Examples

She loves to shmooze with her colleagues at conferences to build new contacts.
He spent the entire evening shmoozing with local politicians at the fundraiser.
It's not enough to do good work—you also have to shmooze a bit to get noticed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chat - to talk in a friendly and informal way
  • Degree Higher: gossip - to talk about other people in a casual way, often spreading rumors

Etymology

From Yiddish *shmuesn* (to chat, converse), ultimately from Hebrew *shəmuʿā* (report, rumor).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01041034-v
Synset POS: v
