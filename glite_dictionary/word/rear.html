rear

1. NOUN

/ rɪr / (AmE), / rɪə / (BrE)
the part of something that is furthest from or opposite to the front; the back part
back part of something

Examples

The delivery entrance is located at the rear of the building.
The group exited quietly through the rear door of the hall.
The car was hit from the rear in the parking lot.

Synset Examples

he stood at the back of the stage
it was hidden in the rear of the store

Related Terms

  • Antonym: front - the part of something that is opposite the rear; the forward part
  • Synonym: back - the part of something that is opposite the front; another term for rear

Etymology

From Latin 'retro' (back, backward), via Old French 'rere'.
