quintupling

1. NOUN

/ kwɪnˈtjuːplɪŋ / (AmE), / kwɪnˈtjuːplɪŋ / (BrE)
an act or instance of increasing something fivefold; multiplying by five
increase by a factor of five

Examples

The quintupling of production led to a surplus of goods in the market.
Over a decade, there was a quintupling in the value of the company's stock.
Environmentalists are concerned about the quintupling of plastic waste in the ocean.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: doubling - an act of increasing by two times
  • Degree Lower: tripling - an act of increasing by three times
  • Degree Lower: quadrupling - an act of increasing by four times
  • Degree Higher: sextupling - an act of increasing by six times

Etymology

From Latin 'quintus' (fifth) + 'uple' as in 'quintuple' + '-ing'.
