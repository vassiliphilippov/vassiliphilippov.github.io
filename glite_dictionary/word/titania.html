Titania

1. PROPN

/ taɪˈteɪniə / (AmE), / tɪˈtɑːnɪə / (BrE)
The queen of the fairies in medieval folklore, notably appearing as a character in Shakespeare's play 'A Midsummer Night's Dream'.
queen of the fairies in folklore

Examples

Titania rules over the fairy realm with grace and wisdom in Shakespeare's play.
The quarrel between Oberon and Titania causes chaos in the enchanted woods.
Many productions depict Titania with a sparkling crown and flowing robes.

Etymology

Coined by Shakespeare in 'A Midsummer Night's Dream', possibly from Latin 'Titania' (daughter of the Titans).
