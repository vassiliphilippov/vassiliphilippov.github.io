verbolatry

1. NOUN

/ vɚˈbɑːlətri / (AmE), / vɜːˈbɒlətri / (BrE)
excessive reverence for words or verbal expression; the worship of words
worship or excessive reverence for words

Examples

Some literary critics accuse certain writers of verbolatry, favoring ornate language over substance.
His tendency toward verbolatry sometimes distracts from the actual message of his speeches.
The poet's verbolatry is evident in his playful manipulation of language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: logolatry - the worship of words

Etymology

Formed from 'verbo-' (word, from Latin 'verbum') + '-latry' (worship, from Greek 'latreia').
