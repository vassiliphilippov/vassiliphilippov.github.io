islander

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.lənd.ər / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.lənd.ə / (BrE)
a person who lives on or comes from an island
inhabitant of an island

Examples

The islander welcomed tourists with warm hospitality every summer.
As an islander, she was accustomed to the rhythms of the tides.
Many islanders rely on fishing as their primary source of income.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mainlander - a person who lives on the main part of a country, not an island

Etymology

Formed from 'island' + '-er', denoting a person from a place.
