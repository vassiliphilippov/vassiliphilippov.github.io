Phaseolus aureus

1. NOUN

/ fəˈsiːoʊləs ɔːˈriːəs / (AmE), / fəˈsiːələs ɔːˈriːəs / (BrE)
a species of leguminous plant widely grown for its edible seeds, commonly known as mung bean, used especially for bean sprouts in Asian cookery
botanical name for the mung bean plant

Examples

Phaseolus aureus is a major source of bean sprouts in Chinese cuisine.
Farmers in Southeast Asia often cultivate Phaseolus aureus for its protein-rich seeds.
Experts recommend soaking Phaseolus aureus seeds overnight before cooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mung bean - the seed of Phaseolus aureus, used in food, especially as bean sprouts

Etymology

From Latin: 'Phaseolus' (a type of bean) and 'aureus' (golden).
