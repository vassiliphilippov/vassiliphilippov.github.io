permissiveness

1. NOUN

/ pərˈmɪs.ɪv.nɪs / (AmE), / pəˈmɪs.ɪv.nɪs / (BrE)
a tendency or willingness to allow freedom of behavior or choices, especially more than is traditional or expected
tolerance of freedom in behavior

Examples

The permissiveness of the new school rules surprised many parents.
Some critics blame social problems on growing permissiveness in society.
Her parents' permissiveness allowed her to make her own decisions from an early age.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tolerance - willingness to allow or accept behavior or beliefs different from one's own
  • Antonym: strictness - the quality of being very limiting or demanding obedience

Etymology

Formed from 'permissive' (Latin permissus, past participle of permittere, to permit) + -ness.
