coke

1. NOUN

/ koʊk / (AmE), / kəʊk / (BrE)
A solid, carbon-rich fuel derived from heating coal in the absence of air, used chiefly in industry, especially for smelting iron ore.
solid fuel made from coal

Examples

The steel plant uses coke to fuel its blast furnaces.
High-quality coke is essential for efficient iron production.
The process of making coke involves heating coal without air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coal - black or brownish-black combustible rock used as fuel
  • Degree Lower: charcoal - a lightweight, black residue of carbon from heating wood

Etymology

From earlier English, possibly related to 'colk', dialectal for core, and connected to coal.

2. VERB

/ koʊk / (AmE), / kəʊk / (BrE)
(of a substance, especially a fuel) to become converted into coke by heating in the absence of air.
to become converted into coke (fuel)

Examples

During the refining process, heavier oils can coke and form hard carbon deposits.
If the residue is heated for too long, it will eventually coke and clog the equipment.
Engineers monitor the temperature closely to prevent the material from coking in the reactor.

Synset Examples

petroleum oils coke after distillation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to carbonize - to convert or be converted into carbon or carbon-containing residue
