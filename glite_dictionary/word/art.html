art

1. NOUN

/ ɑrt / (AmE), / ɑːt / (BrE)
photographs, drawings, or other visual elements included in a publication such as a book, magazine, or newspaper.
visual images in a publication

Examples

The art in this magazine was created by several talented photographers.
She worked on designing the art for the new cookbook.
The publisher requested higher-resolution art for the cover.

Synset Examples

the publisher was responsible for all the artwork in the book

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illustration - a picture or diagram that helps make something clear or attractive, especially in a book or magazine

2. NOUN

/ ɑrt / (AmE), / ɑːt / (BrE)
a skill acquired by study, practice, and observation, often requiring refinement and expertise
skill learned by practice and study

Examples

She has mastered the art of making a perfect soufflé.
Negotiation is truly an art that takes years to perfect.
He demonstrated his art in playing the violin at the concert.

Synset Examples

the art of conversation
it's quite an art

Related Terms

  • Synonym: craft - a skill in making things by hand
  • Antonym: science - systematic knowledge or skill, often contrasted with art

Etymology

From Latin 'ars' (skill, craft, art).

3. NOUN

/ ɑrt / (AmE), / ɑːt / (BrE)
the creative work produced by human imagination, skill, and expression, such as painting, sculpture, and other visual forms, considered collectively
creative works such as painting and sculpture

Examples

This museum has a remarkable collection of contemporary art from around the world.
Her interest in modern art led her to visit galleries throughout the city.
The students studied the history of art in their first semester.

Synset Examples

an art exhibition
a collection of fine art

Related Terms

  • Synonym: craft - an activity involving skill in making things by hand
  • Antonym: science - the intellectual and practical activity involving systematic study of structure and behavior of the physical and natural world

Etymology

From Old French 'art', from Latin 'ars', meaning skill or craft.

Examples

He took an art class to learn how to paint landscapes.
