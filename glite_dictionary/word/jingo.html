jingo

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪŋɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪŋɡəʊ / (BrE)
A person who strongly supports their country and is in favor of aggressive or warlike foreign policy; an extreme nationalist.
aggressive nationalist

Examples

Some politicians have been accused of being a jingo when discussing foreign policy.
The newspaper editorial denounced the jingo attitudes that led to the conflict.
She warned that electing a jingo could increase tensions with neighboring countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chauvinist - an aggressively patriotic or nationalistic person

Etymology

Probably from the phrase 'by Jingo' used in British patriotic songs of the late 19th century.
