marcel

1. NOUN

/ mɑrˈsɛl / (AmE), / mɑːˈsɛl / (BrE)
A hairstyle featuring deep, regular waves, usually created using a heated curling iron.
hairdo with deep waves made by a curling iron

Examples

She wore her hair in a glossy marcel for the vintage-themed party.
The stylist suggested giving my short bob a soft marcel to add some texture.
In the 1920s, the marcel was a fashionable look among Hollywood actresses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wave - a hairstyle featuring hair shaped into waves

Etymology

Named after French hairdresser Marcel Grateau (1852–1936), who invented the style.

2. VERB

/ mɑrˈsɛl / (AmE), / mɑːˈsɛl / (BrE)
to style hair into a wave or series of waves using a special heated curling iron
style hair into waves

Examples

The stylist expertly marcelled her hair for the vintage-themed party.
She decided to marcel her hair to get that classic 1920s look.
Many actresses in old Hollywood films had beautifully marcelled waves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wave - to shape hair into a waving form

Etymology

From French, named after hairdresser Marcel Grateau (1852–1936), who invented the process.
