Cirsium rivulare

1. NOUN

/ sɜːrˈsiːəm rɪˈvjuːlɛər / (AmE), / sɜːˈsɪɪm rɪˈvjʊəleə / (BrE)
a species of thistle native to central and southwestern Europe, known for its tall stature and purple-red flower heads
a European thistle species

Examples

Gardeners often cultivate Cirsium rivulare for its striking purple flowers and attractive appearance.
The wetlands in Poland are a natural habitat for Cirsium rivulare.
Unlike some thistles, Cirsium rivulare is well-behaved and does not aggressively spread in gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thistle - a plant with prickly leaves and purple flowers

Etymology

From Latin: genus Cirsium and species epithet rivulare ('of brooks/streams').
