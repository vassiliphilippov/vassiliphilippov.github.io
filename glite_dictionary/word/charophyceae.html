Charophyceae

1. NOUN

/ kɛˌroʊfɪˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / kəˌrɒfɪˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
A class of green algae in some classifications, often consisting only of the order Charales.
a class of green algae

Examples

The Charophyceae are considered closest relatives to land plants.
Research on Charophyceae provides insight into the evolution of plant life.
Several species within Charophyceae are important for studying freshwater ecosystems.

Etymology

From Greek 'chará' (joy), name of a genus, and Greek '-phyceae' (algae, derived from 'phykos' meaning seaweed).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkæ.rəˈfɪs.i.iː / (AmE), / ˌkæ.rəʊˈfɪs.i.iː / (BrE)
a class of green algae, in some classifications containing only the order Charales, found in freshwater habitats and important in plant evolution studies
a class of freshwater green algae

Examples

Scientists studying freshwater habitats often encounter Charophyceae among other green algae.
The evolutionary significance of Charophyceae has been highlighted in recent botanical research.
Only certain freshwater environments provide suitable conditions for the growth of Charophyceae.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek chara (a type of algae) + -phyceae (from Greek phykos, meaning 'seaweed').
