bomblet

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑm.lət / (AmE), / ˈbɒm.lət / (BrE)
one of the small explosive devices that are released from a cluster bomb and disperse over a wide area
small explosive from a cluster bomb

Examples

The unexploded bomblet was discovered by a farmer in his field.
Many bomblets remain dangerous long after the conflict has ended.
Each cluster bomb can contain dozens of bomblets that scatter upon release.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bomb - an explosive weapon that detonates under specified conditions

Etymology

Formed from 'bomb' + diminutive suffix '-let', indicating small size.
