dickie

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪki / (AmE), / ˈdɪki / (BrE)
a small extra seat at the back of an old-fashioned two-seater car, typically folding out from the bodywork
extra folding seat in old cars

Examples

The vintage roadster had a dickie that folded out from the rear.
She climbed into the dickie while her friends sat in the front seats.
Restoring the old car included repairing its original dickie seat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rumble seat - a folding seat in the back of a car, also known as a dickie

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪki / (AmE), / ˈdɪki / (BrE)
a detachable insert worn at the front of a man's shirt to simulate a shirt front, often starched
detachable false shirt front

Examples

He wore a dickie under his tuxedo to give the appearance of a full dress shirt.
The dickie shifted out of place during the performance, making him look awkward.
For formal occasions, he preferred a dickie instead of buying an expensive new shirt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shirtfront - the part of a shirt covering the chest, often referring to decorative or false fronts
