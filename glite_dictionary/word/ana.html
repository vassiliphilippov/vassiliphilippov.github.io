Ana

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑːnə / (AmE), / ˈɑːnə / (BrE)
In Irish mythology, the mother of the ancient Irish gods, sometimes identified with Danu.
mother goddess in Irish mythology

Examples

In Celtic mythology, Ana is revered as the mother of many deities.
Some legends associate Ana with fertility and the earth.
The hill known as Dá Chich Ana is named after the goddess.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Danu - mother goddess in Irish mythology, sometimes considered the same as Ana

Etymology

Possibly from Old Irish 'Anu', a name for an Irish mother goddess.
