tocainide

1. NOUN

/ toʊˈkeɪnaɪd / (AmE), / təʊˈkeɪnʌɪd / (BrE)
A drug used to treat certain serious irregular heartbeats (ventricular arrhythmias), especially when other medications are ineffective.
antiarrhythmic medication
medical

Examples

After other medications failed, the doctor prescribed tocainide to manage her arrhythmia.
Because of its potential side effects, tocainide is usually used only when safer drugs do not work.
The patient's heart rhythm improved significantly after starting tocainide treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lidocaine - a related local anesthetic and antiarrhythmic drug

Etymology

Derived from the related class of drugs ending in -caine (as in lidocaine) with the suffix -ide.
