in vitro

1. ADV

/ ɪn ˈviːtroʊ / (AmE), / ɪn ˈviːtrəʊ / (BrE)
(of a process or reaction) taking place in an artificial environment outside a living organism, especially in a laboratory setting.
done outside a living organism, in a lab
medical

Examples

The scientists conducted the experiment in vitro to observe the effects without interference from the organism's immune system.
Many drugs are first tested in vitro before being given to patients.
The fertilization of the egg was achieved in vitro using specialized laboratory techniques.

Synset Examples

an egg fertilized in vitro

Related Terms

  • Antonym: in vivo - in a living organism (as opposed to in vitro)

Etymology

From Latin, literally 'in glass', referring to experiments done in glass containers.
