inconceivableness

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnkənˈsiːvəbəlnəs / (AmE), / ˌɪnkənˈsiːvəbəlnəs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being impossible to imagine or comprehend.
state of being impossible to imagine

Examples

The very inconceivableness of the idea made everyone laugh in disbelief.
Philosophers often debate the inconceivableness of an infinite universe.
He marveled at the inconceivableness of time having no beginning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inconceivability - the state of being inconceivable; impossibility to imagine or believe
  • Antonym: conceivableness - the state of being conceivable; possibility to imagine or understand

Etymology

From inconceivable + -ness, patterned after Latin and French equivalents.
