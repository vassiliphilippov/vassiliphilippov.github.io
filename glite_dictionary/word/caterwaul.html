caterwaul

1. VERB

/ ˈkætərˌwɑl / (AmE), / ˈkætəwɔːl / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) to make a shrill, wailing noise like a cat; to utter long, loud, unpleasant sounds.
make a shrill, wailing noise

Examples

The alley cats caterwaul all night, making it hard for anyone to sleep.
She could hear someone caterwauling in the next apartment, probably singing off-key.
As the sirens passed by, the baby began to caterwaul even louder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wail - to make a long, loud, high-pitched cry
  • Synonym: scream - to utter a loud, sharp, piercing cry

Etymology

From Middle English 'caterwawen', possibly imitative or influenced by early Dutch 'kattenwaulen'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkætərˌwɔl / (AmE), / ˈkatəˌwɔːl / (BrE)
a shrill, wailing noise, especially the loud, yowling cry of a cat in heat
loud, wailing cry of a cat

Examples

We couldn't sleep because of the persistent caterwaul coming from the alley behind our house.
At midnight, the caterwaul of stray cats echoed through the neighborhood.
The loud caterwaul startled everyone who was sitting nearby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wail - a prolonged, high-pitched cry of pain, grief, or anger
  • Synonym: howl - a long, loud, doleful cry uttered by an animal such as a dog or wolf

Etymology

From Middle English caterwawen, possibly imitative or from the Dutch 'kat' (cat) + Middle English 'waulen' (to wail).
