UNIX

1. PROPN

/ ˈjuːnɪks / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪks / (BrE)
a powerful multiuser operating system originally developed at Bell Labs, widely used on servers and workstations
multiuser operating system

Examples

The company uses UNIX servers to manage its secure data storage.
Many university computer science courses introduce students to the UNIX operating system.
She prefers working in a UNIX environment for its flexibility and powerful command-line tools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Linux - an open-source operating system inspired by UNIX

Etymology

From 'Uniplexed Information and Computing Service', a play on 'Multics'.
