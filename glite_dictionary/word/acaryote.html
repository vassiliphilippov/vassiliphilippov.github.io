acaryote

1. NOUN

/ əˈkæriˌoʊt / (AmE), / əˈkæriəʊt / (BrE)
a cell that lacks a nucleus, such as a red blood cell (erythrocyte)
cell without a nucleus
medical

Examples

Erythrocytes are considered acaryotes because they lack a nucleus.
Certain ancient single-celled organisms are classified as acaryotes due to their cellular structure.
The presence or absence of a nucleus distinguishes acaryotes from other types of cells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prokaryote - a cell lacking a distinct nucleus but having genetic material not bound by a membrane
  • Antonym: eukaryote - a cell with a nucleus and other organelles enclosed within membranes

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) + 'karyon' (nut, kernel, nucleus).
