Phyllostomus

1. NOUN

/ fɪˈlɒstəˌməs / (AmE), / fɪˈlɒstəməs / (BrE)
A genus of bats in the family Phyllostomatidae, commonly known as leaf-nosed bats, found primarily in Central and South America.
genus of leaf-nosed bats

Examples

The Phyllostomus genus includes bats with distinct leaf-shaped flaps on their noses.
Researchers studied the dietary habits of Phyllostomus to learn more about bat ecology.
A Phyllostomus specimen was collected in the Amazon rainforest for genetic analysis.

Etymology

From Greek 'phyllon' (leaf) and 'stoma' (mouth).
