matrilineally

1. ADV

/ ˌmætrəˈlɪniəli / (AmE), / ˌmatrɪˈlɪnɪəli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to descent through the female line.
by female-line descent

Examples

In some cultures, inheritance is passed matrilineally rather than through the male line.
The royal title was transferred matrilineally from mother to daughter over generations.
Their clan traces its ancestry matrilineally, emphasizing the importance of maternal heritage.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: patrilineally - by descent through the male line

Etymology

From the Latin 'mater' meaning 'mother' and 'linea' meaning 'line'.
