hopeful

1. ADJ

/ ˈhoʊpfəl / (AmE), / ˈhəʊpfʊl / (BrE)
showing signs of being successful or positive in the future; likely to have a good outcome.
promising; likely to succeed

Examples

The director has cast a hopeful newcomer in the lead role.
His early work was seen as hopeful by critics, suggesting great potential.
After a few setbacks, there were some hopeful signs for the company's recovery.

Synset Examples

had a bright future in publishing
the scandal threatened an abrupt end to a promising political career
a hopeful new singer on Broadway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: promising - showing signs of future success
  • Antonym: unpromising - not likely to be successful or positive
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊpfəl / (AmE), / ˈhəʊpfəl / (BrE)
a person who aspires to achieve something, especially in a competition or for a position
an aspiring person, candidate, or competitor

Examples

There were more than fifty hopefuls auditioning for the lead role in the musical.
The party’s presidential hopeful gave a rousing speech to the crowd.
Many young hopefuls move to Hollywood dreaming of becoming stars.

Synset Examples

a lofty aspirant
two executive hopefuls joined the firm
the audience was full of Madonna wannabes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aspirant - a person who has ambitions to achieve something
  • Synonym: candidate - a person applying for a job or competing for election

Etymology

Formed from 'hope' (Old English hopian) + '-ful' (full of), originally as an adjective; noun use dates from the late 19th century.

3. ADJ

/ ˈhoʊpfəl / (AmE), / ˈhəʊpfəl / (BrE)
feeling or showing hope; having a positive expectation about the future or about the outcome of something
feeling or showing hope

Examples

Despite the setbacks, she remained hopeful that things would improve soon.
The doctor gave us a hopeful prognosis after reviewing the test results.
He looked hopeful as he waited for the job interview to start.

Synset Examples

a line of people hopeful of obtaining tickets
found a hopeful way of attacking the problem

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optimistic - expecting the best; having a positive outlook
  • Antonym: hopeless - having no hope; feeling despair

Etymology

From hope + -ful, meaning 'full of hope'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01231403-a
Synset POS: a
