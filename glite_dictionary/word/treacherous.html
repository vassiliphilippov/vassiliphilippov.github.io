treacherous

1. ADJ

/ ˈtrɛtʃərəs / (AmE), / ˈtrɛtʃərəs / (BrE)
(of ground, conditions, etc.) dangerously unstable or unpredictable and likely to cause harm or difficulty.
dangerously unstable or unpredictable

Examples

The hikers avoided the treacherous path, fearing a sudden landslide.
Driving became almost impossible on the treacherous icy roads last night.
The river looks calm but its currents can be surprisingly treacherous at certain times of year.

Synset Examples

treacherous winding roads
an unreliable trestle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dangerous - likely to cause harm or injury
  • Antonym: safe - not likely to cause harm or danger

Etymology

From Old French 'trecherous', from 'trichier' (to cheat), influenced by 'treachery'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtrɛtʃərəs / (AmE), / ˈtrɛtʃərəs / (BrE)
guilty of or involving betrayal or deception; dangerously untrustworthy.
guilty of betrayal or deception

Examples

The hikers were warned about the treacherous paths that could become slippery after rain.
She was shocked by her friend's treacherous behavior during the argument.
Political alliances in ancient times were often treacherous and short-lived.

Synset Examples

Punic faith
the perfidious Judas
the fiercest and most treacherous of foes
treacherous intrigues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perfidious - deceitful and untrustworthy
  • Antonym: faithful - loyal; remaining true to someone or something

Etymology

From Middle English 'trecherous', from Old French 'trecheros', from 'trichier' (to cheat, trick).

Synset POS: s
