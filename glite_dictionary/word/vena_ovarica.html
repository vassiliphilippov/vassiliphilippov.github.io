vena ovarica

1. NOUN

/ ˌveɪnə oʊˈvɛrɪkə / (AmE), / ˌviːnə əʊˈvɑːrɪkə / (BrE)
either of the veins that drain blood from the ovaries, with the right vein opening into the inferior vena cava and the left into the left renal vein
vein draining the ovary
medical

Examples

The vena ovarica plays a vital role in returning blood from the ovaries to the heart.
During the surgery, the doctor carefully identified and preserved the vena ovarica.
An ultrasound can sometimes reveal an enlarged vena ovarica in patients with certain ovarian conditions.

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein) and 'ovarica' (of the ovary).
