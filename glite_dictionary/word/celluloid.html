celluloid

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛl.jə.lɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.jʊ.lɔɪd / (BrE)
a material used as a base for photographic film and, by extension, as a reference to the medium of motion pictures
medium for photographic film or movies

Examples

Many classic Hollywood stories have been immortalized on celluloid.
The director's early experiments with celluloid led to a groundbreaking career in film.
He always dreamed of seeing his novel adapted to celluloid.

Synset Examples

theater pieces transferred to celluloid
this story would be good cinema
film coverage of sporting events

Related Terms

  • Synonym: film - photographic material used for movies
  • Synonym: cinema - the art or industry of making films

Etymology

From cellulose + -oid, first used for the artificial plastic.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɛl.jəˌlɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.jʊ.lɔɪd / (BrE)
(of a character, event, etc.) artificial, stereotypical, or lacking depth, as if depicted in a film.
artificial or stereotypical, like in a film

Examples

The critics complained that the movie was full of celluloid characters with no real depth.
His celluloid charm could not hide his lack of authenticity.
Many readers found the romance between the leads to be purely celluloid and unconvincing.

Synset Examples

a novel with flat celluloid characters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superficial - not thorough, deep, or genuine

Etymology

From the noun 'celluloid' (the early film material), used metaphorically to suggest something filmic or unreal.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɛljəˌlɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɛljʊlɔɪd / (BrE)
A highly flammable, transparent material made from cellulose nitrate and camphor, formerly used in photographic film and other applications.
early plastic, used in film and photography

Examples

Early motion pictures were shot on celluloid film, which was highly flammable.
The old photographs are printed on celluloid and must be handled with care.
With the introduction of safer plastics, celluloid fell out of use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plastic - a synthetic material made from a wide range of organic polymers
  • Degree Higher: thermoplastic - a plastic material that becomes soft when heated and hard when cooled

Etymology

From cellulose + -oid (resembling), coined in the 19th century.
