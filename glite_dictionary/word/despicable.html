despicable

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈspɪkəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈspɪkəbl / (BrE)
Deserving to be despised; morally reprehensible or contemptible.
morally contemptible or deserving hatred

Examples

His despicable actions shocked everyone in the community.
It was despicable of her to spread such cruel rumors.
The villain made a despicable attempt to sabotage their efforts.

Synset Examples

would do something as despicable as murder
ugly crimes
the vile development of slavery appalled them
a slimy little liar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contemptible - deserving contempt or scorn
  • Antonym: admirable - worthy of admiration and respect

Etymology

From Latin despicabilis, from despicari 'to look down on, despise'.
