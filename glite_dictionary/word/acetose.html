acetose

1. ADJ

/ ˈæsɪˌtoʊs / (AmE), / ˈæsɪtəʊs / (BrE)
Having a taste or smell like vinegar; sour or acidic.
tasting or smelling like vinegar

Examples

The sauce had an acetose tang that complemented the sweet vegetables.
Fermentation sometimes gives wine an acetose odor, making it unpleasant to drink.
The acetose smell filled the kitchen after cleaning with strong vinegar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acetic - containing or relating to vinegar or acetic acid
  • Synonym: vinegary - having a sharp, sour taste like vinegar

Etymology

From Latin 'acetosus' (sour, vinegary), from 'acetum' (vinegar).
