starvation

1. NOUN

/ stɑrˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / stɑːˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
a state of extreme hunger caused by not having enough food for a prolonged period
extreme hunger due to prolonged lack of food

Examples

The children showed visible signs of starvation after being trapped for days without food.
Many humanitarian organizations work to prevent starvation in regions affected by famine.
Prolonged starvation can have severe effects on the body's organs and immune system.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hunger - the discomfort or weakness caused by lack of food
  • Synonym: malnutrition - poor condition of health caused by lack of proper nutrients

Etymology

From starve + -ation, modelled after Latin-based nouns.

2. NOUN

/ stɑrˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / stɑːˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of depriving someone of food, or suffering from extreme hunger, often resulting in serious health consequences or death
severe deprivation of food; extreme hunger

Examples

The rebels hoped that starvation would force the city to surrender.
Children in the refugee camp suffered from severe starvation.
Deliberate starvation of prisoners is considered a serious crime under international law.

Synset Examples

the besiegers used starvation to induce surrender
they were charged with the starvation of children in their care

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malnutrition - lack of proper nutrition caused by insufficient or unbalanced diet
  • Synonym: famine - extreme scarcity of food affecting a large population
  • Antonym: feeding - supplying food or nourishment

Etymology

From starve + -ation, ultimately from Old English *steorfan* (to die).
