discourteous

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈkɜrtiəs / (AmE), / dɪsˈkɜːtiəs / (BrE)
showing a lack of courtesy; rude or impolite
rude; lacking courtesy

Examples

His discourteous behavior shocked everyone at the dinner party.
It is discourteous to interrupt someone while they are speaking.
The manager reprimanded the staff for being discourteous to customers.

Synset Examples

a distant and at times discourteous young

Related Terms

  • Antonym: courteous - polite, showing good manners
  • Synonym: rude - offensively impolite or ill-mannered

Etymology

From dis- (expressing reversal) + courteous (from Old French 'courtois').

2. ADJ

/ dɪsˈkɝː.ʃəs / (AmE), / dɪsˈkɜː.ti.əs / (BrE)
showing a lack of courtesy or good manners; rude or impolite.
rude; lacking good manners

Examples

It would be discourteous to interrupt someone while they are speaking.
The waiter was surprisingly discourteous for such an upscale restaurant.
He found her abrupt comments rather discourteous and left the meeting early.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: courteous - polite, respectful, or considerate in manner
  • Synonym: rude - offensively impolite or ill-mannered

Etymology

From Old French 'descortois', from Latin prefix 'dis-' (not) + 'courteous'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00645174-s
Synset POS: s
