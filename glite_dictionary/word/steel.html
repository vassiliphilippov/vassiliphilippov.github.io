2. NOUN

/ stil / (AmE), / stiːl / (BrE)
A ridged rod of steel used to sharpen knives and other cutting tools.
rod for sharpening knives

Examples

He ran the chef's knife along the steel a few times before chopping the vegetables.
A proper steel can keep your kitchen knives sharp for years.
If your steel becomes worn or smooth, it's time to replace it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharpening steel - rod used for honing the edge of a knife
  • Synonym: whetstone - stone for sharpening cutting tools

Etymology

From Old English *stēle*, from Proto-Germanic *stahliją*, referring to hard metal; transferred sense to knife-sharpening rod due to material.

3. NOUN

/ stil / (AmE), / stiːl / (BrE)
A strong, hard alloy of iron with a small amount of carbon, widely used in construction and manufacturing.
strong alloy of iron and carbon

Examples

The beams were made of high-quality steel to support the new bridge.
She polished the steel surfaces until they gleamed.
Modern skyscrapers would not be possible without steel frameworks.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: iron - a strong, hard magnetic metal, elemental form used as base for steel

Etymology

From Old English *stēle*, related to Old High German *stahal* (steel).

4. VERB

/ stil / (AmE), / stiːl / (BrE)
to prepare yourself mentally to face something difficult or unpleasant with determination or courage
prepare oneself mentally for difficulty

Examples

She tried to steel herself before walking into the interview.
You have to steel your nerves when delivering bad news.
He steeled himself for the criticism he knew was coming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brace - prepare for something difficult or unpleasant
  • Synonym: to fortify - strengthen mentally or emotionally

Etymology

From the noun 'steel', referring to the hard metal, used metaphorically to suggest hardening or strengthening oneself, dating from Middle English.
