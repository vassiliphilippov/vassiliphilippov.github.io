sobersides

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊ.bər.saɪdz / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ.bə.saɪdz / (BrE)
A person who is always serious, solemn, or sedate, often to an excessive or habitual degree.
a serious or solemn person

Examples

Everyone at the party was laughing except for sobersides in the corner.
You don't have to be such a sobersides—loosen up and join the fun!
Although he was called sobersides by his friends, he secretly enjoyed a good joke.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stick-in-the-mud - a person who is old-fashioned and resists change

Etymology

Formed from 'sober' (serious, solemn) and 'sides', originally used as a nickname or epithet.
