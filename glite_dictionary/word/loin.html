loin

1. NOUN

/ lɔɪn / (AmE), / lɔɪn / (BrE)
a cut of meat from the side and back of an animal, between the ribs and the rump
cut of meat from an animal's back

Examples

She ordered a grilled loin of pork for dinner.
The butcher recommended the beef loin for roasting.
Lamb loin is especially tender and flavorful.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sirloin - a cut of beef from the back, just above the loin

Etymology

From Old French *loigne*, from Latin *lumbus* (loin, lower back).

2. NOUN

/ lɔɪn / (AmE), / lɔɪn / (BrE)
either side of the lower back between the ribs and the hipbones, in humans and animals
lower back or flank area

Examples

He felt a sharp pain in his loin after lifting the heavy box.
The butcher trimmed the fat from the loin of the lamb.
She placed a heating pad across her lower loin to relieve the tension.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flank - side of the body between the ribs and hip

Etymology

From Old French *loigne*, from Vulgar Latin *lumbea*, from Latin *lumbus* (loin).
