chabazite

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæbəˌzaɪt / (AmE), / ˈkabəzʌɪt / (BrE)
a mineral of the zeolite group, consisting of a hydrous silicate of calcium and aluminum, commonly found in volcanic rocks
a mineral of the zeolite family

Examples

The geologist discovered a vein rich in chabazite while examining the volcanic rocks.
Samples containing chabazite are often studied for their ion-exchange properties in environmental research.
Chabazite crystals can form attractive geometric shapes, making them popular among mineral collectors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zeolite - any of a group of minerals with a similar structure, often used as molecular sieves

Etymology

From French 'chabazite', after Greek 'chabazios', a type of stone mentioned by Pliny the Elder.
