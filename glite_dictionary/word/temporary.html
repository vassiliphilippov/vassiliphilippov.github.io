temporary

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpəˌrɛri / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərəri / (BrE)
a person, especially an office worker, employed for a limited period of time to cover short-term needs
person hired for a short time

Examples

After the regular assistant left, the company brought in a temporary to handle the paperwork.
Many temporaries use agency work as a way to gain office experience.
The temporary was eager to prove her skills during her short stay at the firm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temp - informal term for a temporary worker

2. ADJ

/ ˈtɛmpəˌrɛri / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərəri / (BrE)
Lasting for only a limited period of time; not permanent.
lasting for a limited time

Examples

The company hired a temporary worker to help with the busy season.
Students were moved into temporary classrooms during the construction.
After the flood, many families lived in temporary shelters.

Synset Examples

politics is an impermanent factor of life

a temporary arrangement
temporary housing

Related Terms

  • Antonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last indefinitely
  • Synonym: transient - lasting for a short time

Etymology

From Latin temporarius (of time, temporary), from tempus (time).

3. ADJ

/ ˈtɛmpəˌrɛri / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərəri / (BrE)
not lasting or continuing regularly; lacking continuity or regularity, especially in employment or arrangement
not continuous; lacking regularity or permanence

Examples

She works as a temporary employee during the busy season each year.
We hired a temporary assistant to help with the project.
The company relies on temporary staff whenever demand increases.

Synset Examples

an irregular worker
employed on a temporary basis

Related Terms

  • Antonym: permanent - lasting or expected to last for a long time without change

Etymology

From Latin temporarius (of time, temporary), from tempus (time).
