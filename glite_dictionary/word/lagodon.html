Lagodon

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæɡəˌdɑn / (AmE), / ˈlæɡədɒn / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Sparidae, containing the species known as the pinfish.
genus of marine fish (pinfish)

Examples

Marine biologists studied the population of Lagodon in the coastal waters.
The Lagodon genus is best known for the common pinfish.
Juvenile Lagodon are often found in seagrass beds throughout the Gulf of Mexico.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pinfish - a small marine fish of the western Atlantic, Lagodon rhomboides

Etymology

From Greek ‘lagōs’ (hare) and ‘odous’ (tooth), referring to tooth shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlæɡəˌdɑn / (AmE), / ˈlæɡədɒn / (BrE)
A genus of fish in the family Sparidae, which includes the pinfish.
genus of fish (Sparidae family)

Examples

The Lagodon genus is best known for its only species, the pinfish.
Researchers collected several samples of Lagodon from coastal waters for study.
In marine biology, Lagodon is recognized for its distinctive dental structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pinfish - a small, spiny fish (Lagodon rhomboides) in the Sparidae family

Etymology

From Greek 'lagōs' (hare) and 'odous' (tooth), referring to the shape of the fish's teeth.
