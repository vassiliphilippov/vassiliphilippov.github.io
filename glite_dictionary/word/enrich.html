to enrich

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈrɪtʃ / (AmE), / ɪnˈrɪtʃ / (BrE)
to improve the quality of something, often by adding something to it
improve or enhance the quality of something

Examples

Reading widely can enrich your vocabulary and expand your knowledge.
They use special fertilizers to enrich the soil before planting crops.
Her travels have greatly enriched her perspective on the world.

Synset Examples

The experience enriched her understanding
enriched foods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to improve - to make something better
  • Antonym: to degrade - to lower the quality of something

Etymology

From Old French enrichir, from en- (make) + riche (rich).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00171599-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈrɪtʃ / (AmE), / ɪnˈrɪtʃ / (BrE)
to make someone or something wealthier or richer
make wealthier; increase riches

Examples

The discovery of gold mines helped to enrich the entire region.
Trade with foreign countries can enrich a nation's economy.
Many believe that wise investments will enrich their future.

Synset Examples

the oil boom enriched a lot of local people

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to impoverish - to make someone poor or poorer

Etymology

From Old French *enrichir*, from Latin *in-* (in) + *ricus* (rich).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02323628-v
Synset POS: v
