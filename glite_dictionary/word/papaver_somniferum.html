Papaver somniferum

1. NOUN

/ pəˈpeɪvər sɑmˈnɪfərəm / (AmE), / pəˈpeɪvə sɒmˈnɪfərəm / (BrE)
a species of poppy native to southwestern Asia, with greyish leaves and white or reddish flowers, cultivated as the primary source of opium and poppy seeds
opium poppy species

Examples

The cultivation of Papaver somniferum has a long history in Afghanistan.
Morphine is derived from the milky latex of Papaver somniferum pods.
Gardeners sometimes grow Papaver somniferum for its attractive flowers and edible seeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opium poppy - another name for Papaver somniferum, cultivated for opium and seeds

Etymology

From Latin: 'papaver' meaning 'poppy' and 'somniferum' meaning 'sleep-bringing'.
