stigmatisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌstɪɡməˌtaɪzəˈʃeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌstɪɡmətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of treating someone or something as unworthy of respect or as disgraced
the act of marking as disgraceful

Examples

The stigmatisation of mental illness often prevents people from seeking the help they need.
Social stigmatisation can lead to exclusion and discrimination in the workplace.
Campaigns to reduce the stigmatisation of addiction are vital for public health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stigmatize - to mark someone as disgraceful, to shame
  • Antonym: acceptance - the process of being accepted or approved

Etymology

From English 'stigmatize' + '-ation'; ultimately from Greek 'stigma' (mark, puncture).
