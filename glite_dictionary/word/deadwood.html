deadwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛdˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈdɛdˌwʊd / (BrE)
A branch or part of a tree that is dead.
dead branch or part of a tree

Examples

The forest was filled with deadwood after the harsh winter winds.
Gardeners often remove deadwood to encourage healthy growth in the plants.
Lightning had struck the tree, leaving large sections of deadwood hanging from its branches.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: wood - the hard substance that forms the main structure of the trunk and branches of a tree

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛdwʊd / (AmE), / ˈdɛdwʊd / (BrE)
people or things in an organization that are considered no longer useful or necessary
unneeded people or things; organizational excess

Examples

The company decided to get rid of deadwood to improve efficiency.
If the team is to move forward, we need to clear out any deadwood that holds us back.
During the reorganization, several managers identified as deadwood were let go.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liability - a person or thing whose presence or behavior is likely to cause embarrassment or put one at a disadvantage
  • Antonym: asset - a useful or valuable thing or person

Etymology

Originally referred to dead branches or unproductive wood; figurative sense dates from the 19th century.
