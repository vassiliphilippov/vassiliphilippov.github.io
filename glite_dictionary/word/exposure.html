exposure

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspoʊʒɚ / (AmE), / ɪkˈspəʊʒə / (BrE)
the act of making something secret known to the public or to people who were not previously aware of it
disclosure of something secret

Examples

The exposure of the politician's financial records shocked the public.
They were worried that the exposure of their plans would ruin the deal.
After the exposure of the company's illegal activities, several employees were fired.

Synset Examples

they feared exposure of their campaign plans

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disclosure - the act of making something known or public; revelation
  • Antonym: concealment - the act of keeping something secret or hidden

Etymology

From Latin 'exponere' (to put out, expose) via Old French 'exposicion'.
