Mami

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɑːmi / (AmE), / ˈmɑːmi / (BrE)
A Mesopotamian goddess, worshipped as a mother goddess and associated with childbirth, also known as Ninkhursag.
Mesopotamian goddess of birth

Examples

In ancient Mesopotamia, Mami was honored as the goddess responsible for the creation of humankind.
Many hymns and prayers were dedicated to Mami as the Lady of Birth.
Archaeologists discovered a temple where Mami was worshipped alongside other deities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ninkhursag - Another name for Mami, mother goddess in Sumerian mythology

Etymology

From Akkadian and Sumerian mythology; associated with the term for 'mother'.
