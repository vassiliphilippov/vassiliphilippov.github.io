Gipsy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪp.si / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪp.si / (BrE)
(often offensive) A member of a traditionally itinerant people of Romani origin, especially in Europe and North America.
a member of the Romani people
offensive

Examples

She was fascinated by the colorful wagons of the Gipsy community at the fair.
The old story tells of a mysterious Gipsy who could predict the future.
Many Gipsy artists have contributed to traditional music across Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Romani - a member of the ethnic group originally from northern India
  • Synonym: traveler - a person who travels or moves from place to place, sometimes used specifically for itinerant groups

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, perhaps from the belief that the Romani people came from Egypt.
