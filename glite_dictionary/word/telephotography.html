telephotography

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛləfəˈtɒɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌtɛlɪfəˈtɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the practice of taking photographs using a telephoto lens to capture distant subjects
photography with a telephoto lens

Examples

His passion for wildlife led him to specialize in telephotography of rare birds.
Advancements in telephotography have made it easier to capture sports events from great distances.
She invested in high-quality equipment to improve her telephotography skills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telephoto lens - a lens designed to photograph distant objects with a long focal length

Etymology

From 'tele-' (distant) + 'photography'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtɛləfəˈtɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌtɛlɪfəˈtɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the process of transmitting and reproducing photographs, charts, or pictures over a distance, often by electronic means
transmission of pictures over a distance

Examples

Early experiments in telephotography made it possible to send images over the telegraph.
Newspapers relied on telephotography during the war to receive timely photographs from the front lines.
Advancements in telephotography paved the way for modern fax machines and digital picture transmission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telephoto - pertaining to a lens designed to photograph distant objects

Etymology

From Greek 'tele-' (far) + 'photography' (light drawing).
