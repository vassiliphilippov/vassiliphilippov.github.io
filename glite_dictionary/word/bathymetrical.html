bathymetrical

1. ADJ

/ ˌbæθ.iˈmɛ.trɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌbæθ.ɪˈmɛ.trɪ.kəl / (BrE)
relating to the measurement of the depths of oceans, seas, or lakes
relating to depth measurement in water bodies

Examples

Satellite data are used to create detailed bathymetrical maps of the ocean floor.
The bathymetrical survey revealed previously unknown underwater valleys.
Researchers compiled bathymetrical profiles to study changes in lake depth over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bathymetric - relating to the measurement of water depths; alternative form

Etymology

From Greek bathos (depth) + -metric (pertaining to measurement) + -al (adjective suffix).
