Diodon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪəˌdɒn / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.əʊ.dɒn / (BrE)
a genus of tropical and subtropical marine fish known as porcupinefishes, characterized by their ability to inflate their bodies and presence of spines
genus of spiny marine fish (porcupinefish)

Examples

The Diodon is often studied for its unique defensive adaptations.
Members of the genus Diodon can inflate their bodies when threatened.
Marine biologists discovered several juvenile Diodon near the coral reef.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porcupinefish - spiny marine fish belonging to the genus Diodon

Etymology

From Greek *di-* (two) + *odous* (tooth), referring to their distinctive teeth structure.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪəˌdɑn / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.ə.dɒn / (BrE)
a genus of porcupinefishes, characterized by their spiny bodies and ability to inflate themselves as a defense mechanism
genus of porcupinefishes

Examples

The Diodon is known for its remarkable ability to puff up when threatened.
Scientists discovered a new species within the genus Diodon off the coast of Indonesia.
Juvenile Diodon display shorter spines compared to adults.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Diodontidae - family of fishes known as porcupinefishes

Etymology

From Greek *di-* (two) + *odōn* (tooth), referring to their prominent double teeth.
