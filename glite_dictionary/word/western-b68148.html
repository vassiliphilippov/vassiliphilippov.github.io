western

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɛstərn / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən / (BrE)
(of wind) blowing from the west.
(of wind) from the west

Examples

The western wind brought cooler temperatures to the region.
Farmers welcomed the arrival of a gentle western breeze after weeks of still air.
The captain adjusted the sails to make the most of the western gusts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: eastern - (of wind) from the east

Etymology

From Old English *westerne*, from *west* (the direction) + *-ern*.
