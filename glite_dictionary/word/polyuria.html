polyuria

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈjʊriə / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈjʊəriə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by the production of abnormally large volumes of dilute urine, commonly associated with diabetes or certain kidney disorders.
excessive production of urine
medical

Examples

Patients with uncontrolled diabetes often experience polyuria as one of the first noticeable symptoms.
The doctor asked about polyuria to assess potential kidney issues.
Increased thirst and polyuria can be signs of underlying metabolic disorders.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: oliguria - reduced urine output
  • Synonym: nocturia - excessive urination at night

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'ouron' (urine).
