tetrad

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛˌtræd / (AmE), / ˈtɛt.ræd / (BrE)
A group or set of four; the number four.
group of four; number four

Examples

During meiosis, a tetrad of chromatids is formed.
In our experiment, the subjects were organized into tetrads for the group activity.
The artist arranged the stones in a tetrad with symmetry in mind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quartet - a group of four people or things
  • Degree Lower: triad - a group or set of three
  • Degree Higher: pentad - a group or set of five

Etymology

From Greek 'tetras, tetrad-' (group of four).
