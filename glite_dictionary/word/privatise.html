to privatise

1. VERB

/ ˈpraɪvəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈprʌɪvətʌɪz / (BrE)
to transfer a business, industry, or service from public (governmental) to private ownership and control
transfer to private ownership
british

Examples

The government decided to privatise several major utilities to encourage competition.
Many workers were worried about job security when the company was privatised.
Some argue that it is not always beneficial to privatise public services.

Synset Examples

The oil industry was privatized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to privatize - transfer from public to private ownership (spelling variant, mainly US)
  • Antonym: to nationalise - transfer from private to government ownership
  • Synonym: to publicise - to make public, share with the public (different meaning but similar form)

Etymology

From private + -ise, modelled after 'nationalise'.
