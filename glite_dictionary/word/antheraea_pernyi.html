Antheraea pernyi

1. NOUN

/ ˌænθəˈriːə ˈpɜːrniː / (AmE), / ˌænθəˈriːə ˈpɜːniː / (BrE)
a large Chinese moth that produces a brownish silk commonly used in textile manufacturing
Chinese moth species producing brown silk

Examples

Antheraea pernyi is widely cultivated in China for its valuable tussah silk.
The brown silk spun by Antheraea pernyi is prized for its strength and texture.
Scientists are studying Antheraea pernyi to improve the efficiency of silk production.

Etymology

From Latin: genus Antheraea (from Greek 'anthera', meaning 'flowery') and the species name 'pernyi', after the French missionary A. Perny who studied the species.
