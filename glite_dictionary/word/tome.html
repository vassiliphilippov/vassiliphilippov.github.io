tome

1. NOUN

/ toʊm / (AmE), / təʊm / (BrE)
a large, heavy, and often scholarly book, especially one forming part of a larger work
large, scholarly book

Examples

She spent the afternoon leafing through a tome on medieval art.
The professor wrote a massive tome about European history.
That old tome on the shelf hasn't been opened in years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: volume - a book that is part of a set or series

Etymology

From Latin 'tomus', from Greek 'tomos' (a section, volume), from 'temnein' (to cut).
