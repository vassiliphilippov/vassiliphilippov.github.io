evenness

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːvənnəs / (AmE), / ˈiːvənnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being an even number, especially the property of being divisible by two without a remainder
state of being divisible by two

Examples

The evenness of a number can be determined by checking if it is divisible by two.
In mathematics, evenness and oddness are important properties of integers.
The teacher asked the students to identify the evenness of several numbers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: oddness - the quality or state of being an odd number, not divisible by two

Etymology

From 'even' + noun suffix '-ness', meaning the state or quality of being even.

2. NOUN

/ ˈivənnəs / (AmE), / ˈiːvənnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being uniform, consistent, or free from variation or irregularity
uniformity or lack of variation

Examples

The evenness of the paint coverage gave the wall a professional look.
She admired the evenness of his handwriting throughout the document.
For best results, evenness in baking temperature is essential.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uniformity - the quality of being uniform or consistent
  • Antonym: irregularity - the state of being uneven or lacking uniformity

Etymology

From even + -ness, denoting the state or quality of being even.

3. NOUN

/ ˈiːvənnəs / (AmE), / ˈiːv(ə)nnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being level, balanced, or consistent in degree, amount, or character
quality of being balanced or uniform

Examples

Her calm and evenness in stressful situations inspired confidence in the team.
The evenness of the surface made the floor safe for walking.
He admired the evenness of her tone throughout the debate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unevenness - the quality of being not level or not equal
  • Synonym: uniformity - the quality of being uniform or the same throughout
  • Synonym: balance - a state where different elements are equal or in the correct proportions

Etymology

From 'even' + suffix '-ness', forming nouns of quality or state.
