folly

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑli / (AmE), / ˈfɒli / (BrE)
a foolish action or idea, especially one that results from lack of good judgment; a stupid mistake
a foolish mistake or idea

Examples

It would be utter folly to try climbing that mountain without any experience.
His financial folly left the company in serious trouble.
She soon realized the folly of ignoring the weather warnings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wisdom - the quality of having knowledge and good judgment
  • Synonym: blunder - a careless or stupid mistake

Etymology

From Old French 'folie' (madness, foolishness), from Latin 'follem' (bag, windbag, bellows).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɑli / (AmE), / ˈfɒli / (BrE)
the quality or act of lacking good sense or judgment; foolish or reckless behavior
lack of good sense; foolishness

Examples

His decision to invest everything in the risky scheme was an act of pure folly.
Looking back, she regretted the folly of ignoring her friends' warnings.
The plan was abandoned after its folly became clear to everyone involved.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wisdom - the quality of having experience, knowledge, and good judgment
  • Synonym: foolishness - lack of good sense or judgment

Etymology

From Old French folie (madness, stupidity), from fol (foolish).
