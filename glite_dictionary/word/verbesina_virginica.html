Verbesina virginica

1. NOUN

/ vɜːrˈbiːsɪnə vərˈdʒɪnɪkə / (AmE), / vɜːˈbiːsɪnə vəːˈdʒɪnɪkə / (BrE)
A tall perennial herb native to the eastern United States, with clusters of white flowers; also known as frostweed or white crownbeard.
a tall, white-flowered perennial herb of eastern US

Examples

The garden's wildflower section included several Verbesina virginica plants in full bloom.
Botanists studied the unique frost-forming properties of Verbesina virginica during the winter months.
In the meadow, Verbesina virginica stood out with its tall stems and clusters of white flowers.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Verbesina' (a group of flowering plants in the aster family) and 'virginica', referring to Virginia (the region where the species was first identified).
