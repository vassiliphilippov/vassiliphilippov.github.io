featherbedding

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛðərˌbɛdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɛðəbɛdɪŋ / (BrE)
The practice of requiring an employer, typically under union rules, to hire more workers than are needed for a job.
practice of unnecessary hiring (labor)

Examples

The union was accused of featherbedding when it insisted on retaining outdated job roles.
Critics argue that featherbedding increases costs without benefiting productivity.
New management policies were introduced to eliminate featherbedding in the company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overmanning - the situation of having more workers than necessary

Etymology

From 'featherbed' (to make comfortable or cushioned) + -ing, suggesting artificially easy or protected employment.
