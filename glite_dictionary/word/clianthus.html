Clianthus

1. NOUN

/ kliˈænθəs / (AmE), / klaɪˈænθəs / (BrE)
A genus of semi-prostrate shrubs or vines native to Australasia, known for their striking flowers.
genus of Australasian flowering shrubs or vines

Examples

The Clianthus genus includes plants commonly referred to as kaka beak due to the shape of their flowers.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the Clianthus genus in New Zealand.
Gardeners prize Clianthus for its vibrant red blossoms and attractive foliage.

Etymology

From Greek 'kliaina' (cloak) and 'anthos' (flower), referring to the shape and showiness of the flowers.
