clianthus

1. NOUN

/ kliˈænθəs / (AmE), / klaɪˈænθəs / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs or vines native to New Zealand, with compound leaves and drooping racemes of pea-like red flowers.
genus of New Zealand shrubs or vines with red flowers

Examples

The clianthus is often called the 'kaka beak' due to the distinctive shape of its flowers.
Gardeners prize the clianthus for its striking red blooms and attractive foliage.
Several species of clianthus are endangered and are carefully cultivated in plant conservation projects.

Etymology

From Greek 'kliō' (to lean) and 'anthos' (flower), referring to the shape of the flower clusters.
