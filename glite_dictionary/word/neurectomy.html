neurectomy

1. NOUN

/ nuˈrɛktəmi / (AmE), / njʊəˈrɛktəmi / (BrE)
the surgical removal of all or part of a nerve
surgical removal of a nerve
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended a neurectomy to relieve the patient's chronic pain.
A neurectomy may be performed when less invasive treatments have failed.
After the neurectomy, the patient reported a significant reduction in symptoms.

Etymology

From Greek 'neuron' (nerve) + 'ectomy' (removal).
