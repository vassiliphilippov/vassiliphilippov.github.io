Yana

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɑːnə / (AmE), / ˈjɑːnə / (BrE)
the language formerly spoken by the Yana people of northern California
a Native American language of California

Examples

Few people today are able to speak Yana fluently, as the language is considered extinct.
Linguists have studied old recordings and notes to reconstruct Yana grammar and vocabulary.
The Yana language provides insights into the culture and traditions of the Yana people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yanan - the language of the Yanan people

Etymology

From the name of the Yana people, an indigenous group of northern California.

2. NOUN

/ ˈjɑːnə / (AmE), / ˈjɑːnə / (BrE)
a member of an extinct Native American people who lived in northern California, along the Yuba and Feather rivers
extinct Native American people of northern California

Examples

Anthropologists have studied the culture of the Yana and their adaptation to the environment.
The last known speaker of the Yana language passed away in the early 20th century.
Artifacts from the Yana have been discovered near the Feather River.

Etymology

Name derived from the Yana people's own language.
