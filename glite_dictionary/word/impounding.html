impounding

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpaʊndɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪmˈpaʊndɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of legally placing private property, such as vehicles or animals, into the custody of an official authority
legal seizure or custody of property
legal

Examples

The impounding of the vehicles was ordered by the court after repeated parking violations.
A notice informed residents of the impounding of stray dogs found in the neighborhood.
Her car was subject to impounding because she failed to pay her outstanding fines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seizure - the action of taking something by force or authority
  • Antonym: release - the act of setting something free from custody or restraint

Etymology

From the verb 'impound', from Anglo-French *empounder*, from Old French *en-*, in + *pounde*, pound (enclosure).
