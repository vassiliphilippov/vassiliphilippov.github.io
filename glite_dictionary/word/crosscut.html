crosscut

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔsˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌkʌt / (BrE)
a route that is shorter than the usual or established way
shorter route than usual

Examples

She found a crosscut through the park that saved her ten minutes.
We took a crosscut instead of the main road to avoid traffic.
That hidden alley is a useful crosscut to the shopping district.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shortcut - an alternative route that is shorter than the regular one
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔsˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌkʌt / (BrE)
a path or route that runs diagonally, especially one that cuts across a main direction
a diagonal path or route

Examples

The hikers took a crosscut through the woods to reach the lake more quickly.
The new crosscut across campus saves students at least ten minutes of walking.
A crosscut trail connects the two main paths at an angle.

Etymology

From 'cross' (across) + 'cut' (an opening or path).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08634886-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈkrɔsˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌkʌt / (BrE)
to cut across something, especially at a diagonal or right angle to its main axis or grain
to cut across, usually diagonally

Examples

The carpenter decided to crosscut the plank to fit it into the frame.
You should always measure twice before you crosscut any expensive materials.
Using a sharp saw, she crosscut the logs at an angle for the project.

Etymology

From cross (across) + cut; attested since the 17th century.
