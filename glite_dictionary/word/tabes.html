tabes

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪbiːz / (AmE), / ˈteɪbiːz / (BrE)
progressive wasting of the body, especially as a result of a chronic disease such as tabes dorsalis
progressive wasting due to chronic disease
medical

Examples

The patient suffered from severe tabes as the disease progressed.
Unchecked tuberculosis can sometimes result in tabes and general debility.
Early intervention is crucial to prevent tabes in chronic infections.

Etymology

From Latin tabēs (a wasting away, decay).
