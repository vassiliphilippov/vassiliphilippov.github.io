to consternate

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑn.stɚ.neɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.stə.neɪt / (BrE)
to fill (someone) with anxiety, dread, or sudden confusion; to dismay greatly
to fill with anxiety or dismay

Examples

News of the sudden layoffs consternated everyone in the office.
The unexpected outcome seemed to consternate even the most experienced analysts.
Loud noises during the ceremony consternated the crowd, causing a moment of panic.

Synset Examples

After the terrorist attack, people look consternated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dismay - to cause someone to feel distress or apprehension
  • Synonym: to confound - to cause surprise or confusion in someone

Etymology

From Latin 'consternare' (to throw into confusion, perplex).
