Livistona

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈvɪstəˌnoʊ / (AmE), / lɪˈvɪstəˌnə / (BrE)
A genus of fan palms native to Asia, Australia, and Malaysia, noted for their large, fan-shaped leaves.
a genus of fan palms

Examples

The park's greenhouse boasts a beautiful display of Livistona from Southeast Asia.
A mature Livistona can grow several meters tall with striking, fan-shaped leaves.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Livistona in its native Australian habitat.

Etymology

Named after Patrick Murray, Baron of Livingston, a Scottish botanist.

2. NOUN

/ lɪˈvɪstənə / (AmE), / lɪˈvɪstənə / (BrE)
A genus of fan palms native to Asia, Australia, and Malaysia, characterized by their large, segmented leaves.
genus of fan palms

Examples

Several Livistona species are used as ornamental plants in public gardens.
The Livistona trees provide shade in many parts of Southeast Asia.
Botanists discovered a new Livistona species in northern Australia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palm - a type of tropical tree with large leaves and no branches

Etymology

Named after Patrick Murray, Baron of Livingston, a Scottish botanist.
