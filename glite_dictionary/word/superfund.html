Superfund

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːpərˌfʌnd / (AmE), / ˈsuːpəˌfʌnd / (BrE)
a federal program in the United States for identifying, investigating, and cleaning up hazardous waste sites, administered by the Environmental Protection Agency.
US hazardous waste cleanup program
american

Examples

The government allocated millions of dollars to support the Superfund cleanup of abandoned industrial sites.
Many communities hope that their local landfill will soon be addressed under the Superfund program.
The controversy centered on how Superfund money was spent in different states.

Synset Examples

some have intimated that the Superfund's money may have turned into a political slush fund

Etymology

From 'super' + 'fund', referring to the trust fund established by U.S. law in 1980 to finance cleanup.
