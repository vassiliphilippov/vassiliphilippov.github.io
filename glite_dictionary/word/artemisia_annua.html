Artemisia annua

1. NOUN

/ ɑːr.təˈmɪʒ.i.ə ˈæn.ju.ə / (AmE), / ˌɑː.tɪˈmɪz.i.ə ˈæn.ju.ə / (BrE)
an annual species of wormwood native to southeastern Europe and western Asia, known for its medicinal properties, especially as a source of artemisinin used to treat malaria
annual wormwood plant; malaria treatment source

Examples

Artemisia annua is cultivated widely for its use in producing antimalarial drugs.
Researchers have identified artemisinin, a compound found in Artemisia annua, as highly effective against malaria.
Traditional Chinese medicine has used extracts from Artemisia annua for centuries to treat fevers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wormwood - a common name for various Artemisia species, including A. annua

Etymology

Artemisia from Latin, after Artemis, Greek goddess; annua from Latin 'annuus' (yearly)
