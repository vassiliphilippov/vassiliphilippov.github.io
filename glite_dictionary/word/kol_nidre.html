Kol Nidre

1. NOUN

/ koʊl nɪˈdreɪ / (AmE), / kɒl ˈnɪdreɪ / (BrE)
a solemn Jewish prayer recited at the beginning of the Yom Kippur evening service, asking for annulment of personal vows made during the year
Jewish prayer recited at Yom Kippur

Examples

The cantor began the service with the haunting melody of Kol Nidre on Yom Kippur evening.
For many, the recital of Kol Nidre is the most moving moment of the year.
The synagogue was filled with worshippers gathered for the Kol Nidre prayer.

Etymology

From Aramaic 'kol nidre' meaning 'all vows'.
