Celastrus

1. PROPN

/ səˈlæstrəs / (AmE), / sɪˈlæstrəs / (BrE)
A genus of woody vines and shrubs, chiefly native to Asia and Australia, including species such as bittersweet.
genus of woody vines and shrubs

Examples

Celastrus species are widely cultivated for their colorful fruit and attractive foliage.
The invasive Celastrus orbiculatus can outcompete native plants in some regions.
Botanists collected samples of Celastrus from various parts of Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bittersweet - a climbing woody vine of the genus Celastrus

Etymology

From Greek *kelastros*, name of a plant.

2. NOUN

/ səˈlæstrəs / (AmE), / sɪˈlæstrəs / (BrE)
A genus of woody vines and shrubs, native mainly to Asia and Australia, which includes the bittersweet plant.
genus of woody vines and shrubs

Examples

The Celastrus genus is well known for its attractive orange fruit and climbing stems.
Several species of Celastrus are used in traditional medicine and landscaping.
Botanists have classified bittersweet under the Celastrus genus due to its distinctive characteristics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bittersweet - a common name for Celastrus scandens, a species in this genus

Etymology

From Greek kelastros (a plant name), adopted in New Latin for the genus name.
