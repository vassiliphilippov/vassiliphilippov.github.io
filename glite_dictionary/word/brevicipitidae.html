Brevicipitidae

1. NOUN

/ brɛˌvɪsɪˈpɪtəˌdi / (AmE), / brɛˌvɪsɪˈpɪtɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of narrow-mouthed frogs native to sub-Saharan Africa, known for their burrowing or arboreal habits.
family of narrow-mouthed frogs

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species within the Brevicipitidae family in the rainforests of Tanzania.
Some Brevicipitidae produce unique defensive secretions to deter predators.
The arboreal habits of Brevicipitidae distinguish them from other narrow-mouthed frogs.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Breviceps ('short head'), plus the family suffix '-idae'.
