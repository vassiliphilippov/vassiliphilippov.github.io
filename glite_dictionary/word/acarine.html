acarine

1. NOUN

/ ˈæk.əˌraɪn / (AmE), / ˈæk.əˌriːn / (BrE)
A small arachnid of the order Acari, which includes mites and ticks.
mite or tick (order Acari)

Examples

The veterinary clinic found several acarines on the stray dog's skin.
Some acarine species can transmit diseases to plants and animals.
Researchers are studying how acarines adapt to different environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mite - tiny arachnid; includes many species in the order Acari
  • Synonym: tick - small bloodsucking arachnid; part of Acari

Etymology

From modern Latin Acari (order name), from Greek akari (mite).
