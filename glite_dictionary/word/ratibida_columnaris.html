Ratibida columnaris

1. NOUN

/ rəˈtɪbɪdə kəˈlʌmnərɪs / (AmE), / rəˈtɪbɪdə kəˈlɒmnərɪs / (BrE)
A species of coneflower with flower heads resembling a Mexican hat, having a tall red-brown disk and drooping yellow or yellow and red-brown petals; native to the Great Plains and foothills of the Rocky Mountains.
a coneflower native to North America

Examples

Many wildflower enthusiasts admire the unique shape of the Ratibida columnaris in summer meadows.
The Ratibida columnaris thrives in dry, open prairies across the central United States.
Gardeners often plant Ratibida columnaris for its resilience and striking yellow petals.

Etymology

Genus name from Latin 'ratiba'; species name 'columnaris' from Latin, meaning 'column-like', referring to the tall central disk.
