telophase

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛləˌfeɪz / (AmE), / ˈtiːləˌfeɪz / (BrE)
the final stage of mitosis, during which the chromosomes arrive at the cell poles, the nuclear membrane reforms, and the cell splits into two daughter cells
final stage of mitosis
medical

Examples

During telophase, the two sets of chromosomes are enclosed in new nuclear envelopes.
The cell begins to divide into two separate daughter cells during telophase.
Microscopes can reveal the formation of nuclei during telophase in dividing cells.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: anaphase - the stage of mitosis before telophase, when chromosomes separate

Etymology

From Greek 'telos' (end) + 'phasis' (appearance).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛl.əˌfeɪz / (AmE), / ˈtiːləˌfeɪz / (BrE)
the final stage of cell division, during which chromosomes move to opposite ends of the spindle and two nuclei form
final stage of cell division
medical

Examples

During telophase, the nuclear envelope reforms around the separated chromosomes.
The cell completes mitosis during telophase when the chromosomes decondense.
In biology class, we observed telophase under the microscope as two new nuclei appeared.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: anaphase - the stage before telophase in cell division

Etymology

From Greek 'telos' (end) + 'phasis' (appearance).
