quaveringly

1. ADV

/ ˈkweɪvɚɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈkweɪvərɪŋli / (BrE)
in a trembling or unsteady manner, as if with nervousness or emotion
in a trembling or unsteady way

Examples

Her voice rose quaveringly as she addressed the nervous crowd.
He replied quaveringly, betraying his uncertainty and fear.
The candlelight flickered quaveringly on the old man's wrinkled face.

Synset Examples

the man began quaveringly to question the soldier

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tremulously - in a trembling or quivering manner

Etymology

From 'quaver' (to tremble or shake; Middle English, via Old English 'cwafian'), plus adverbial suffix '-ly'.
