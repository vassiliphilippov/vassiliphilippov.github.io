hygrophyte

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈɡrɑˌfaɪt / (AmE), / hɪˈɡrɒfaɪt / (BrE)
a plant that grows in environments with abundant moisture
plant growing in moist habitat
medical

Examples

Mosses are common hygrophytes found in damp forests.
The study focused on the distribution of hygrophytes in the wetland ecosystem.
Some ferns are classified as hygrophyte species due to their preference for moist soil.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: xerophyte - plant adapted to dry environments
  • Synonym: hydrophyte - plant adapted to grow in water or aquatic environments

Etymology

From Greek 'hygrós' (wet, moist) + 'phyton' (plant).
