laborsaving

1. ADJ

/ ˈleɪbərˌseɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈleɪbəˌseɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a device or method) designed to reduce or eliminate the amount of human effort needed to perform a task, especially manual work.
designed to reduce human labor

Examples

Modern kitchens are equipped with many laborsaving appliances.
The introduction of laborsaving techniques revolutionized the manufacturing industry.
Farmers often invest in laborsaving machinery to improve efficiency and reduce manual work.

Synset Examples

laborsaving devices like washing machines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: time-saving - designed to save time

Etymology

Formed from 'labor' + 'saving,' meaning saving work or effort.
