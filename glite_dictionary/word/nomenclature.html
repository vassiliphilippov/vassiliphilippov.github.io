nomenclature

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊmənˌkleɪtʃər / (AmE), / nəʊˈmɛnklətʃə / (BrE)
a system of names or terms used in a specific field or discipline
system of names in a field

Examples

The scientific community uses a strict nomenclature to classify new species.
Medical nomenclature can be confusing to those not trained in the field.
The committee debated changes to the nomenclature for chemical compounds.

Synset Examples

legal terminology
biological nomenclature
the language of sociology

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terminology - the set of technical terms used in a particular subject

Etymology

From Latin nomenclatura (list of names), from nomen (name) + calare (to call).
