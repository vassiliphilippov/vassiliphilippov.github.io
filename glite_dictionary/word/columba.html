Columba

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈlʌmbə / (AmE), / kəˈlʌmbə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Columbidae, comprising the typical pigeons, including the rock pigeon and related species.
genus of typical pigeons

Examples

The rock pigeon, or Columba livia, is commonly found in cities worldwide.
Ornithologists study several species within the genus Columba to understand their migration patterns.
Many domestic pigeons are descended from wild birds in the Columba genus.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Columbidae - the biological family that includes pigeons and doves

Etymology

From Latin 'columba' meaning 'dove' or 'pigeon'.

2. PROPN

/ koʊˈlʌmbə / (AmE), / kəˈlʌmbə / (BrE)
A constellation in the Southern Hemisphere, located near Puppis and Caelum.
Southern Hemisphere constellation

Examples

The star Alpha Columbae marks the brightest point in the Columba constellation.
Ancient astronomers identified Columba as a dove in the southern sky.
Columba can be seen best from the Southern Hemisphere during the months of February and March.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constellation - a group of stars forming a recognizable pattern

Etymology

From Latin 'columba' meaning 'dove'.

3. PROPN

/ kəˈlʌmbə / (AmE), / kəˈlʌmbə / (BrE)
The genus of birds that includes the typical pigeons, such as the rock dove, and is the type genus of the family Columbidae.
genus of typical pigeons

Examples

The genus Columba includes the common city pigeon found worldwide.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Columba genus in Southeast Asia.
Many birds in the Columba genus have adapted well to urban environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pigeon - a bird in the family Columbidae

Etymology

From Latin *columba* (dove, pigeon).
