Gordian

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡɔr.di.ən / (AmE), / ˈɡɔː.di.ən / (BrE)
extremely complex or difficult to solve, especially referring to a problem or situation; commonly used in the phrase 'Gordian knot.'
extremely complex or intricate

Examples

The company found itself facing a Gordian challenge after the merger.
Resolving the Gordian issues in international diplomacy often requires bold solutions.
Their relationship had become a Gordian knot of misunderstandings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intricate - very complicated or detailed
  • Antonym: simple - easily understood or done; not complicated

Etymology

From Latin Gordius (legendary king of Phrygia), whose knot was cut by Alexander the Great.
