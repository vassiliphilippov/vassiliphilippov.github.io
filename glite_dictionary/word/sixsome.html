sixsome

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪksəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪksəm / (BrE)
A group or set of six people considered together.
group of six people

Examples

The group formed a sixsome for the trivia night competition.
We went out to dinner as a sixsome to celebrate his birthday.
The golf club organized the tournament into pairs, foursomes, and even a sixsome.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: foursome - a group of four people considered together
  • Degree Higher: sevensome - a group of seven people considered together

Etymology

Formed from 'six' and the suffix '-some' meaning 'group', in analogy with 'foursome', 'threesome'.
