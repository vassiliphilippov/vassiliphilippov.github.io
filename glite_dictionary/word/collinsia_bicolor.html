Collinsia bicolor

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪnziə ˈbaɪkəlɔr / (AmE), / kɒˈlɪnziə ˈbaɪkəlɔː / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant native to California, known for its white, lavender, or pale-blue flowers arranged in rings around the stem.
California wildflower with ringed blooms

Examples

The meadow was dotted with Collinsia bicolor, adding splashes of blue and white to the landscape.
Botanists are excited to find Collinsia bicolor thriving in the restored habitat.
In springtime, Collinsia bicolor lines the hiking trails throughout California's coastal regions.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Collinsia,' honoring Zacchaeus Collins, an American botanist, and Latin 'bicolor' meaning 'two-colored.'
