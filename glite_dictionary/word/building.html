building

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɪldɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity or business of constructing or repairing structures, especially as a commercial enterprise
construction activity or business

Examples

He started his career in the building industry as a carpenter.
Local colleges offer courses related to building and construction.
The council introduced new regulations affecting building projects in the city.

Synset Examples

their main business is home construction
workers in the building trades

Related Terms

  • Synonym: construction - the act or process of building something, especially large structures
  • Antonym: demolition - the act of destroying a building or structure

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɪldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɪldɪŋ / (BrE)
The process or act of constructing something, especially structures such as houses or bridges.
the act of constructing; construction

Examples

The building of the new library will begin next year.
She specializes in the building of environmentally friendly homes.
Proper planning is essential for the successful building of large projects.

Synset Examples

during the construction we had to take a detour
his hobby was the building of boats

Related Terms

  • Synonym: construction - the process or business of building things such as houses, bridges, factories, etc.

Etymology

From Old English byldan (to build) + -ing (action noun suffix).

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɪl.dɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɪl.dɪŋ / (BrE)
A structure with a roof and walls, typically standing permanently in one place.
structure with roof and walls

Examples

The new building across the street will open next month.
Fire alarms are installed on every floor of the building.
The old building was renovated into a modern office space.

Synset Examples

there was a three-story building on the corner
it was an imposing edifice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: structure - something that has been built, such as a building or bridge
  • Synonym: edifice - a large, impressive building

Etymology

From Old English 'byldan' (to build), from Proto-Germanic '*bildijaną'.
