bryony

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪəni / (AmE), / ˈbraɪəni / (BrE)
a climbing plant of the genus Bryonia, with large leaves, small flowers, and a juice traditionally used for its emetic and purgative properties
a type of climbing plant (genus Bryonia)

Examples

Legend says that bryony was once used as a medicinal herb despite its toxic properties.
The woods were lush with ferns, nettles, and trailing bryony climbing through the undergrowth.
Gardeners are advised not to confuse black bryony with edible vine species, as it is poisonous.

Etymology

From Latin *bryonia*, from Greek *bruōnia* (related to swelling or burgeoning).
