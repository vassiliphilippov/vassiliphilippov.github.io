worksheet

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːk.ʃiːt / (AmE), / ˈwɜːk.ʃiːt / (BrE)
a sheet of paper with questions, exercises, or spaces to record information, used for planning or completing work
paper for recording work or exercises

Examples

The teacher handed out a worksheet for the students to complete during class.
Before starting the project, we filled in a planning worksheet to organize our tasks.
Each employee must submit a weekly worksheet detailing the hours they have worked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: form - a document with spaces to fill in information
  • Synonym: spreadsheet - a digital document for calculations or data
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06548728-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːkˌʃiːt / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkˌʃiːt / (BrE)
a sheet of paper with columns and rows for accountants to record and organize figures when preparing financial statements
accountant's record sheet for figures

Examples

The accountant filled out the worksheet before finalizing the financial report.
Each column on the worksheet represented a different income category.
She used a detailed worksheet to organize the company's expenses for the month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spreadsheet - an electronic document for organizing data in rows and columns

Etymology

From 'work' + 'sheet', describing a sheet used for working out problems.
