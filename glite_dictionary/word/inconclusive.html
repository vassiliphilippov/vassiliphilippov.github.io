inconclusive

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.kənˈklu.sɪv / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kənˈkluː.sɪv / (BrE)
not leading to a definite or final decision or result; leaving doubt or uncertainty
not resulting in a definite answer

Examples

The jury found the evidence to be inconclusive, so no verdict was reached.
After weeks of testing, the results remained inconclusive and required further analysis.
His statement was so inconclusive that it only added to the confusion.

Synset Examples

an inconclusive reply
inconclusive evidence
the inconclusive committee vote

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conclusive - ending doubt or question; decisive

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (not) + 'concludere' (to conclude).
