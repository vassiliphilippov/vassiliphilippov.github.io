uncompetitiveness

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌn.kəmˈpɛt.ə.tɪv.nəs / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kəmˈpɛt.ɪt.ɪv.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being not able to compete successfully with others.
lack of ability to compete effectively

Examples

The uncompetitiveness of the industry was evident in its declining market share.
Government subsidies were introduced to address the chronic uncompetitiveness of local manufacturers.
Analysts attribute the company's losses to its growing uncompetitiveness in the global marketplace.

Synset Examples

In the separating equilibrium, the domestic government signals the uncompetitiveness of the domestic firm by setting a lower tariff than is optimal under complete information.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: competitiveness - the quality of being able to compete successfully

Etymology

Formed from 'uncompetitive' + '-ness'.
