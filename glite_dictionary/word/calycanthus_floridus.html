Calycanthus floridus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkælɪˈkænθəs ˈflɔrɪdəs / (AmE), / ˌkalɪˈkanθəs ˈflɒrɪdəs / (BrE)
a hardy shrub native to the southeastern United States, noted for its clove-scented wood and fragrant reddish-brown flowers; also known as Carolina allspice or sweetshrub.
a fragrant shrub of the southeastern US

Examples

The Calycanthus floridus in our garden fills the air with its spicy, clove-like fragrance in the spring.
Gardeners often plant Calycanthus floridus for its attractive reddish-brown flowers and fragrant wood.
In the southeastern United States, Calycanthus floridus is a popular choice for natural hedges and ornamental gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Carolina allspice - common name for Calycanthus floridus, a fragrant shrub
  • Synonym: sweetshrub - another common name for Calycanthus floridus

Etymology

From Greek kalux (calyx) and anthos (flower), with floridus from Latin meaning 'flowery'.
