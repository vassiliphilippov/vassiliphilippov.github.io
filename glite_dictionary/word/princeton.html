Princeton

1. PROPN

/ ˈprɪns.tən / (AmE), / ˈprɪns.tən / (BrE)
A town in central New Jersey, USA, known as the location of Princeton University.
university town in New Jersey, USA

Examples

She graduated from Princeton with honors in 2010.
The picturesque streets of Princeton attract many tourists each year.
He moved to Princeton to work at the university's research institute.

Etymology

Named after Prince William, later King William IV of Great Britain.

2. PROPN

/ ˈprɪns.tən / (AmE), / ˈprɪns.tən / (BrE)
a prestigious university located in Princeton, New Jersey, United States
university in New Jersey, US

Examples

She received her degree from Princeton after four years of study.
Many famous scientists have taught at Princeton over the years.
Admission to Princeton is highly competitive and selective.

Etymology

Named after the town of Princeton, New Jersey, where it is located; the town name's origin is likely from "prince's town."
