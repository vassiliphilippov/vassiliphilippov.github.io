Cyclophorus

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈklɑːfəˌrəs / (AmE), / sʌɪˈklɒfərəs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical Old World ferns characterized by closely crowded circular sori and lacking indusia.
genus of tropical Old World ferns

Examples

Many species within the Cyclophorus genus thrive in humid, shaded environments of Southeast Asia.
The absence of indusia distinguishes Cyclophorus from other related fern genera.
Botanists collected several samples of Cyclophorus for their study on tropical flora diversity.

Etymology

From Greek *kyklos* (circle) + *phoros* (bearing), referring to the circular arrangement of sori.
