duskiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌskinəs / (AmE), / ˈdʌskinəs / (BrE)
a dark or swarthy complexion, especially of the skin
darkness of complexion

Examples

Her duskiness set her apart in the small village where most had pale skin.
He wrote of the model's striking duskiness in his journal.
The painter captured the unique duskiness of his subject's complexion in the portrait.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fairness - lightness of skin complexion

Etymology

From English 'dusky' + '-ness', meaning the state of being dusky or dark.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʌskinəs / (AmE), / ˈdʌskinəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being dimly lit or lacking bright illumination
state of being dimly lit

Examples

The duskiness of the old library added to its mysterious charm.
As night fell, the duskiness in the garden grew deeper and shadows lengthened.
Her eyes adjusted slowly to the duskiness inside the abandoned house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dimness - the state of being somewhat dark or lacking light
  • Antonym: brightness - the state of being very well lit or luminous

Etymology

From 'dusky' + '-ness', with 'dusky' from Middle English 'doski', likely from 'dusk'.
