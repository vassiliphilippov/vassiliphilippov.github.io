Bromus

1. NOUN

/ ˈbroʊməs / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊməs / (BrE)
A genus of grasses, commonly known as brome grasses, belonging to the family Gramineae (Poaceae).
a genus of grasses (brome grasses)

Examples

Some species of Bromus are important forage crops for animals.
The Bromus genus includes both annual and perennial species.
Researchers studied the distribution of Bromus in different habitats.

Etymology

From Latin 'Bromus', from Greek 'brōmos' (oats).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbroʊməs / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊməs / (BrE)
a genus of grasses in the family Gramineae (Poaceae), commonly known as brome grasses, found in temperate regions worldwide
genus of grasses (brome)

Examples

Several species in the Bromus genus are commonly referred to as brome grasses.
The botanist collected samples of Bromus from the meadow for study.
Invasive species of Bromus can pose a threat to local ecosystems.

Etymology

From Latin 'bromos', meaning wild oat.
