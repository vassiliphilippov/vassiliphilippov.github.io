cowl

1. NOUN

/ kaʊl / (AmE), / kaʊl / (BrE)
a loose hood or hooded robe, especially one worn by a monk
a loose hooded robe, esp. worn by monks

Examples

The monk pulled his cowl over his head as he entered the dim chapel.
A heavy woolen cowl protected him from the mountain wind.
She admired the intricate embroidery on the cowl of the old religious robe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hood - a covering for the head and neck with an opening for the face

Etymology

From Latin 'cucullus' (hood, cap).

WordNet ID: oewn-03129125-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ kaʊl / (AmE), / kaʊl / (BrE)
to cover with or as if with a cowl; to wrap or envelop in a covering, especially around the head.
cover with or as with a cowl

Examples

The statue was cowled in heavy cloth for protection during the storm.
Mist cowled the mountain peaks, hiding them from view.
She cowled her head with a dark shawl before stepping into the cold night.

Synset Examples

cowl the boys and veil the girls

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloak - to cover or conceal with a cloak
  • Antonym: to uncover - to remove a cover or not be covered

Etymology

From Old English *cugele* (hood or cowl), influenced by Latin *cucullus* (hood, cowl).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01339906-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ kaʊl / (AmE), / kaʊl / (BrE)
a protective covering, usually metal, that covers the engine of a vehicle, especially an aircraft
metal covering over an engine

Examples

The mechanic lifted the cowl to inspect the aircraft’s engine.
Older motorcycles often have a prominent cowl covering their engines for safety reasons.
After the accident, the cowl had to be replaced before the plane could fly again.

Synset Examples

there are powerful engines under the hoods of new cars
the mechanic removed the cowling in order to repair the plane's engine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowling - removable cover for an engine, especially in aircraft
  • Synonym: hood - cover over the engine compartment of a car

Etymology

From Old English 'cūle', from Latin 'cucullus' (hood, covering).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03536090-n
Synset POS: n
