isochronism

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ.səˈkroʊ.nɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.səˈkrɒn.ɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the property of occurring at equal time intervals; regular periodicity, especially under varying conditions
occurrence at equal time intervals

Examples

The isochronism of the planet's orbit ensures that it returns to the same position at regular intervals.
Galileo demonstrated the isochronism of pendulum swings, which was key to developing accurate clocks.
The engineer adjusted the mechanism to maintain its isochronism despite temperature fluctuations.

Synset Examples

Galileo's discovery was that the period of swing of a pendulum is independent of its amplitude — the arc of the swing — the isochronism of the pendulum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: periodicity - the quality of recurring at intervals

Etymology

From Greek 'iso-' (equal) and 'chronos' (time), via French 'isochronisme'.
