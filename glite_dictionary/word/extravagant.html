extravagant

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈstrævəɡənt / (AmE), / ɪkˈstrævəɡənt / (BrE)
Unrestrained or excessive, especially in expression of feelings, actions, or praise.
excessive; unrestrained, especially with feelings

Examples

The governor's extravagant praise for the mayor surprised the audience.
His extravagant gestures made his excitement clear to everyone.
She was known for her extravagant displays of affection.

Synset Examples

extravagant praise
exuberant compliments
overweening ambition
overweening greed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excessive - Exceeding a normal, usual, or proper limit
  • Antonym: restrained - Characterized by moderation or self-control

Etymology

From Latin extravagari (to wander outside or beyond), via Medieval Latin 'extravagans'.

2. ADJ

/ ɪkˈstrævəɡənt / (AmE), / ɪkˈstrævəɡənt / (BrE)
spending money or using resources in a reckless or wasteful way, far exceeding what is reasonable or necessary
recklessly wasteful; excessive

Examples

Despite earning a modest salary, she maintained an extravagant lifestyle that soon led to debt.
He received criticism for his extravagant spending on luxury items.
The wedding was beautiful but far too extravagant for their simple tastes.

Synset Examples

prodigal in their expenditures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prodigal - spending money or resources freely and recklessly
  • Antonym: frugal - using money and resources carefully and not wastefully
  • Synonym: lavish - sumptuously rich, elaborate, or luxurious

Etymology

From Latin 'extravagari' (to wander outside bounds), via French 'extravagant'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02431016-s
Synset POS: s
