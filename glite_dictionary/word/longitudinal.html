longitudinal

1. ADJ

/ ˌlɑːndʒɪˈtuːdɪnəl / (AmE), / ˌlɒndʒɪˈtjuːdɪnəl / (BrE)
Relating to or situated along lines of longitude, especially in a geographical context.
relating to lines of longitude

Examples

The navigator relied on longitudinal coordinates to chart their position at sea.
Maps often display longitudinal lines running from the North Pole to the South Pole.
Accurate longitudinal measurements are essential for determining time zones.

Synset Examples

longitudinal reckoning by the navigator

Etymology

From Latin 'longitudo' (length).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02841230-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌlɑːn.dʒəˈtuː.dɪ.nəl / (AmE), / ˌlɒn.dʒɪˈtjuː.dɪ.nəl / (BrE)
(of research or data) involving repeated observations or measurements of the same subjects over an extended period of time.
measured over a long period of time

Examples

The longitudinal study tracked thousands of participants for more than twenty years.
Researchers collected data from the same subjects at several points in this longitudinal project.
A longitudinal analysis often reveals trends that cross-sectional studies may miss.

Synset Examples

a longitudinal study of twins

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cross-sectional - Observations or measurements at a single point in time (research design)

Etymology

From Latin 'longitudo' (length), from 'longus' (long).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01443650-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌlɔndʒəˈtudənəl / (AmE), / ˌlɒndʒɪˈtjuːdɪnəl / (BrE)
running or extending lengthwise rather than across
running or extending lengthwise

Examples

The researchers observed changes by taking longitudinal measurements over several years.
The car's frame is reinforced by a longitudinal beam running down its center.
The spinal cord is protected by longitudinal grooves in the vertebrae.

Synset Examples

a thin longitudinal strip
longitudinal measurements of the hull

Related Terms

  • Antonym: transverse - running or lying across, not lengthwise

Etymology

From Late Latin longitudinalis, from Latin longitudo (length).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01448449-s
Synset POS: s
