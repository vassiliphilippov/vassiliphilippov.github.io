enough

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnʌf / (AmE), / ɪˈnʌf / (BrE)
An adequate quantity to satisfy a need or purpose.
adequate quantity; sufficiency

Examples

Do we have enough to feed everyone at the party?
For me, a cup of tea and a good book is enough.
She took just enough to get by and left the rest for others.

Synset Examples

enough is as good as a feast
there is more than a sufficiency of lawyers in this country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sufficiency - an amount that is adequate for a purpose
  • Antonym: shortage - an inadequate amount; insufficiency
  • Antonym: insufficiency - not having enough

Etymology

From Middle English 'enogh', from Old English 'genōg' (sufficient, enough).

2. ADV

/ ɪˈnʌf / (AmE), / ɪˈnʌf / (BrE)
to the degree or quantity needed; as much or as many as required
as much as necessary (adverb)

Examples

Have you slept enough to feel rested today?
She didn't practice enough to pass the audition.
Is there enough food for everyone at the party?

Synset Examples

have I eaten enough?
I've had plenty, thanks (‘plenty’ is nonstandard)

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sufficiently - to an adequate degree
  • Antonym: insufficiently - not to an adequate degree

Etymology

From Middle English 'inough', from Old English 'genōg' (sufficient).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00146749-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADJ

/ ɪˈnʌf / (AmE), / ɪˈnʌf / (BrE)
as much or as many as required for a particular purpose; sufficient.
sufficient; as much as needed

Examples

Do we have enough chairs for everyone at the meeting?
She didn’t have enough money to buy the ticket.
Make sure there is enough food for all the guests.

Synset Examples

an adequate income
the food was adequate
enough food
food enough

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sufficient - enough for a particular purpose; adequate
  • Antonym: insufficient - not enough; lacking in what is necessary
  • Degree Higher: too much - more than is needed or required
  • Degree Lower: insufficient - not enough; inadequate

Etymology

From Middle English enogh, from Old English genōg (sufficient, enough).
