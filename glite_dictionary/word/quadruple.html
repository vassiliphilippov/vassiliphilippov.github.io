quadruple

1. NOUN

/ kwɑːˈdruːpəl / (AmE), / kwɒˈdruːpəl / (BrE)
A quantity that is four times as great as another.
four times the quantity

Examples

The company reported a quadruple in profits compared to last year's earnings.
Her workload became a quadruple after taking on several new projects.
The investment yielded a quadruple of the initial amount in just two years.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: double - a quantity twice as great as another
  • Degree Lower: triple - a quantity three times as great as another
  • Degree Higher: quintuple - a quantity five times as great as another

Etymology

From Latin 'quadruplus', from 'quattuor' (four) + 'plus' (more).
