Hexapoda

1. NOUN

/ hɛkˈsæpədə / (AmE), / hɛkˈsapədə / (BrE)
a large class of arthropods characterized by having six legs, including all insects and their close relatives
a class of six-legged arthropods

Examples

Hexapoda includes familiar creatures like ants, beetles, and butterflies.
Scientists estimate that Hexapoda comprises more species than any other animal group.
The evolutionary history of Hexapoda is a subject of great interest to biologists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insect - a small arthropod animal with six legs and generally one or two pairs of wings

Etymology

From Greek 'hexa' (six) + 'pous, podos' (foot).

2. NOUN

/ hɛkˈsæpədə / (AmE), / hɛkˈsæpədə / (BrE)
A class of arthropods that includes insects, characterized by having six legs.
class of arthropods with six legs (insects)

Examples

The vast majority of known animal species belong to the Hexapoda.
Scientists study the evolutionary history of the Hexapoda to understand insect diversity.
The class Hexapoda includes butterflies, ants, beetles, and millions of other insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insect - small arthropod animal with six legs, typically with wings

Etymology

From Greek 'hexa' (six) + 'pous, podos' (foot).
