junkyard

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋk.jɑrd / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋk.jɑːd / (BrE)
a place where discarded or scrap materials, especially metal or vehicles, are collected and stored for resale or recycling
place for collecting and storing scrap or junk
american

Examples

The old car sat in the junkyard for years before it was finally recycled.
We found a replacement part for the engine at a local junkyard.
Children love to explore the junkyard in search of hidden treasures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrapyard - a place where scrap metal is stored and processed

Etymology

From 'junk' (discarded items) + 'yard' (enclosed area); common in American English.
