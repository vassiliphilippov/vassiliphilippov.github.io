ante

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.ti / (AmE), / ˈæn.ti / (BrE)
an amount of money that each player must put into the pot before a hand is dealt in a game of poker or similar gambling games
initial stake in poker or gambling game

Examples

Each player must place an ante before the cards are dealt.
She tossed her ante into the pot without hesitation.
If you don't have enough to cover the ante, you can't join the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stake - an amount of money risked on the outcome of a game or contest

Etymology

From Latin ante (before), used in English since the 19th century in card games.

2. VERB

/ ˈæn.ti / (AmE), / ˈæn.ti / (BrE)
to put up a stake or bet, especially at the beginning of a card game
put up a stake in betting

Examples

Each player must ante five dollars before the cards are dealt.
He forgot to ante and was reminded by the dealer.
If you want to stay in the game, you have to ante up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bet - to risk money on the outcome of an event or game
  • Antonym: fold - to withdraw from a round of betting in a card game

Etymology

From Latin ante (before); the noun sense in card-playing first appeared in American English in 19th century.
