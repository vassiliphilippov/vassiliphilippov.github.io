finiteness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪnətnəs / (AmE), / ˈfaɪnɪtnəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of having limits or being restricted in size, amount, or extent
state of being finite or limited

Examples

The finiteness of natural resources compels us to use them wisely.
Some mathematicians study the finiteness or infiniteness of mathematical sets.
Her awareness of life's finiteness inspired her to live more fully.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infiniteness - the state of being infinite or unlimited
  • Synonym: finite - having limits or bounds

Etymology

From Latin finitus (limited, finite) plus English -ness (forming nouns of quality or state).
