palsy

1. NOUN

/ ˈpælzi / (AmE), / ˈpɔːlzi / (BrE)
A condition characterized by uncontrollable tremors or paralysis, often caused by disease or injury to the nervous system.
condition with uncontrollable tremors or paralysis
medical

Examples

The patient suffered from palsy, which caused his hands to shake constantly.
After the accident, she developed facial palsy that affected her ability to smile.
In the past, doctors used the term 'palsy' to describe several nervous disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paralysis - loss of the ability to move body parts

Etymology

From Old French *paralisie* (paralysis), later shortened to *palsy*; related to Latin *paralysis*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpælzi / (AmE), / ˈpɔːlzi / (BrE)
Loss of the ability to move part or all of the body, often resulting from disease or injury.
loss of movement of a body part
medical

Examples

After the accident, he suffered from palsy in his right arm.
Doctors are researching new treatments for facial palsy.
Her grandfather developed a severe palsy that made walking difficult.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paralysis - complete loss of ability to move a part or most of the body

Etymology

From Old French *paralisie*, from Latin *paralysis*, from Greek *parálusis* (paralysis).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈpælzi / (AmE), / ˈpælzi / (BrE)
To affect with palsy; to cause a loss of muscle control or paralysis.
to cause paralysis

Examples

The sudden illness began to palsy the left side of his face.
A rare complication can palsy the muscles used for speech.
Doctors feared the disease would eventually palsy his limbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to paralyze - to make unable to move or act

Etymology

From Old French *paralisie*, from Medieval Latin *paralysis*.
