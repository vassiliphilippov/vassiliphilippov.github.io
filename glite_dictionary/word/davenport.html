davenport

1. NOUN

/ ˈdævənˌpɔrt / (AmE), / ˈdævənpɔːt / (BrE)
A small decorative writing desk with a sloping top and drawers, used especially in the 19th century.
small decorative writing desk

Examples

The antique davenport in the corner was once used for writing correspondence.
She placed her papers on the davenport before beginning her letter.
Collectors often seek out a well-preserved davenport for its historical value.

Etymology

Named after Captain Davenport, for whom one was supposedly made in the late 18th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdævənˌpɔrt / (AmE), / ˈdævənpɔːt / (BrE)
A large sofa, especially one that can be converted into a bed.
large sofa, sometimes convertible to a bed

Examples

She unfolded the davenport for her guests to sleep on.
The room was furnished with a sturdy oak table and a large davenport.
After a long day, he stretched out on the davenport to watch television.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sofa - a long, upholstered seat with a back and arms, for two or more people
  • Synonym: couch - a piece of furniture for seating two or more people
  • Synonym: settee - a long upholstered seat for more than one person, typically with a back and arms

Etymology

Originally from the name of a 19th-century American furniture manufacturer, the A.H. Davenport Company.
