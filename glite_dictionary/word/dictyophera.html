Dictyophera

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪktioʊˈfɛrə / (AmE), / ˌdɪktɪəˈfɪərə / (BrE)
A genus of fungi closely related to Phallus, distinguished by a net-like skirt (indusium) hanging from below the cap (pileus).
genus of fungi with a net-like skirt

Examples

The Dictyophera species are renowned for their striking net-like skirts.
Researchers discovered a new Dictyophera in the tropical rainforest.
The delicate indusium of the Dictyophera attracts considerable interest from botanists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Phallus - genus of fungi to which Dictyophera is closely related

Etymology

From Greek 'diktyon' (net) and 'phero' (to bear), referring to the net-like skirt.
