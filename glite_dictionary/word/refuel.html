to refuel

1. VERB

/ riˈfjuəl / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfjʊəl / (BrE)
to put more fuel into a vehicle, aircraft, ship, or machine so that it can continue operating
put more fuel into something

Examples

We stopped at the service station to refuel before continuing our journey.
The plane will need to refuel halfway through its flight to New York.
After hours at sea, the ship docked to refuel and take on supplies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuel - supply with fuel; add fuel to something

Etymology

From re- (again) + fuel (material used to produce energy).

2. VERB

/ ˌriːˈfjuːəl / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfjʊəl / (BrE)
to fill or supply again with fuel, especially to aircraft, ships, or vehicles
to supply with more fuel

Examples

The pilot had to refuel the plane before continuing the flight.
After a long drive, we stopped at a service station to refuel.
Ships often refuel at ports during long voyages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuel - material such as coal, gas, or oil that is burned to produce heat or power

Etymology

From re- (again) + fuel (from Old French 'fouaille', from Latin 'focus', hearth).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
