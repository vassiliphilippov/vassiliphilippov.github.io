lecanora

1. NOUN

/ lɛkəˈnɔrə / (AmE), / lɛkəˈnɔːrə / (BrE)
any lichen of the genus Lecanora, some species of which are used in dyeing and as food.
genus of lichens

Examples

The pale green lecanora growing on the old stone wall caught the botanist's attention.
Some traditional dyes are made from species of lecanora collected in the wild.
In Arctic regions, local people sometimes eat lecanora during times of scarcity.

Etymology

From modern Latin Lecanora, the name of the genus, based on Greek lekanē (dish, basin), referring to the shape of its fruiting body.
