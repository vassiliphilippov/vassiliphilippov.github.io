Daubentonia madagascariensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌdoʊbɛnˈtoʊniə ˌmædəˌɡæsɑːrˈjɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌdɔːbɛnˈtəʊnɪə ˌmædəˌɡæsɑːrˈjɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a species of nocturnal lemur from Madagascar, known for its long bony fingers and rodent-like incisor teeth; commonly called the aye-aye
a species of nocturnal lemur (aye-aye)

Examples

The Daubentonia madagascariensis is notable for its unusual method of finding insects inside tree bark.
Conservationists are working to protect Daubentonia madagascariensis from habitat loss in Madagascar.
Many tourists to Madagascar hope to spot a Daubentonia madagascariensis during night safaris.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aye-aye - common name for Daubentonia madagascariensis, a nocturnal lemur

Etymology

Named after French naturalist Louis-Jean-Marie Daubenton and the island of Madagascar.
