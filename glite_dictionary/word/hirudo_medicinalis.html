Hirudo medicinalis

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈruːdoʊ ˌmɛdɪˈsɪnəlɪs / (AmE), / hɪˈruːdəʊ ˌmɛdɪˈsɪnəlɪs / (BrE)
a large European freshwater leech, formerly used in medicine especially for bloodletting
species of medicinal leech

Examples

In the 19th century, Hirudo medicinalis was widely used by doctors for bloodletting procedures.
Today, Hirudo medicinalis is still bred for certain medical applications such as microsurgery.
Researchers study Hirudo medicinalis to better understand the properties of its anticoagulant saliva.

Etymology

From Latin: 'hirudo' meaning 'leech' and 'medicinalis' meaning 'medicinal'.
