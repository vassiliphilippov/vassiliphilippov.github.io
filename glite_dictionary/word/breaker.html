breaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪkər / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkə / (BrE)
a quarry worker whose job is to split off blocks of stone from larger rock.
quarry worker splitting stone blocks

Examples

The breaker worked steadily, carefully shaping each block of marble.
Many breakers in the quarry use traditional hand tools rather than machines.
As a skilled breaker, she could split huge stones with remarkable precision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarrier - a person who works in a quarry
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪkər / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkə / (BrE)
a wave that breaks into foam as it approaches the shore or a shallow area
a wave breaking on the shore

Examples

We watched the breakers crash against the rocks at sunset.
The beach was deserted except for the breakers rolling in from the ocean.
Seagulls hovered above the foaming breakers looking for fish.

Etymology

From break + -er, denoting something that breaks, here referring to a wave breaking.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪkər / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkə / (BrE)
a wave that breaks into foam when it reaches the shore or a reef
a wave breaking on the shore

Examples

The boat struggled to get through the breakers crashing on the reef.
Children played at the edge of the breakers, letting the water rush over their feet.
Surfers waited patiently beyond the breakers for the perfect wave.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wave - water moving along the surface, especially toward the shore

Etymology

From the verb ‘break’ + -er, meaning something that breaks (in this case, waves).

4. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪkər / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkə / (BrE)
a device that automatically stops the flow of electricity in a circuit as a safety measure when the circuit becomes overloaded
electrical safety device for circuit overloads

Examples

If the toaster trips the breaker again, you'll need to check for an electrical fault.
She flipped the main breaker off before replacing the light fixture.
A power surge caused the breaker to shut off the electricity in the kitchen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circuit breaker - a device that automatically interrupts an electric circuit
  • Synonym: fuse - a safety device that melts to break an overloaded circuit

Etymology

From 'break' + '-er', denoting something that breaks (in this case, an electrical circuit).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03038229-n
Synset POS: n
