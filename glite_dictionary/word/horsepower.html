horsepower

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrsˌpaʊər / (AmE), / ˈhɔːsˌpaʊə / (BrE)
a unit of power equal to 746 watts, commonly used to measure the power of engines.
a unit of power (746 watts)

Examples

The car's engine produces over 300 horsepower at peak performance.
This generator has a maximum output of 10 horsepower.
Farm machinery used to be rated in terms of horsepower.

Etymology

From 'horse' + 'power', coined in the late 18th century by engineer James Watt to compare the output of steam engines to horse work.
