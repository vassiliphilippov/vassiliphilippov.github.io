apadana

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑː.pəˈdɑː.nə / (AmE), / ˌæp.əˈdɑː.nə / (BrE)
the large audience hall found in ancient Persian palaces, especially at Persepolis and Susa
great hall in ancient Persian palaces

Examples

The ruins of the apadana at Persepolis display a forest of massive stone columns.
Visitors marveled at the intricate reliefs that once adorned the walls of the apadana.
Archaeologists have studied the apadana to better understand ancient Persian architecture.

Etymology

From Old Persian *apadāna* (palace; hall with columns).
