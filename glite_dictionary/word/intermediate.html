intermediate

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.tɚˈmiː.di.ət / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈmiː.di.ət / (BrE)
a substance formed during a chemical reaction that exists temporarily before converting into the final product
temporary substance in a chemical reaction

Examples

During the synthesis of aspirin, an intermediate is produced before the final product is formed.
Scientists identified a new intermediate that helps explain the reaction's unusual speed.
The presence of a stable intermediate can sometimes be detected by spectroscopic methods.

Etymology

From Latin 'intermedius' meaning 'lying between'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪntɚˈmiːdiət / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmiːdiət / (BrE)
being between two other related things, levels, or points in a range or scale; neither at the one extreme nor the other
in the middle of a range or scale

Examples

She signed up for the intermediate Spanish class because she wasn't a beginner but not yet advanced.
This job requires an intermediate level of computer skills to complete the tasks effectively.
They purchased a bike that was suitable for intermediate riders, not for absolute beginners.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: average - typical or in the middle of a range
  • Synonym: medium - of middle size, level, or amount
  • Antonym: extreme - at either end of a range, not intermediate

Etymology

From Latin 'intermedius', from 'inter-' (between) + 'medius' (middle).

4. VERB

/ ˌɪntərˈmiːdiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmiːdiˌeɪt / (BrE)
to act as a mediator between people or groups in order to help resolve a dispute or reach an agreement

Examples

The lawyer was asked to intermediate between the two companies to help them reach a compromise.
She often intermediates in arguments at work, helping her colleagues resolve their issues.
An independent third party intermediated during the heated negotiations to ensure fairness.
