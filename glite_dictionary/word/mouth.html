mouth

1. NOUN

/ maʊθ / (AmE), / maʊθ / (BrE)
the place where a river or stream flows into a larger body of water, such as a lake, sea, or ocean
place where river meets larger body of water

Examples

The Mississippi River has a vast delta at its mouth on the Gulf of Mexico.
A small fishing village lies near the mouth of the river.
They built a port at the mouth of the Thames.

Synset Examples

New York is at the mouth of the Hudson

Related Terms

  • Antonym: source - the place where a river or stream begins

2. NOUN

/ maʊθ / (AmE), / maʊθ / (BrE)
The opening in the lower part of the face through which food is taken in and sounds are produced.
opening for eating and speaking

Examples

She put the spoon in her mouth and tasted the soup.
The dentist told him to open his mouth wide during the examination.
He covered his mouth when he yawned.

Synset Examples

he stuffed his mouth with candy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oral cavity - the cavity of the mouth, technically referred to in anatomy

Etymology

From Old English *mūþ*, related to German *Mund* and Dutch *mond*.

10. NOUN

/ maʊθ / (AmE), / maʊθ / (BrE)

Examples

The hikers stopped at the mouth of the cave to rest and admire the view.
The river slows down as it approaches the mouth where it meets the sea.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entrance - an opening that allows entry to a place
  • Antonym: exit - an opening through which one can leave a place; antonym for entrance

Etymology
