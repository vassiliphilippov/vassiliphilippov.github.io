horseweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrsˌwid / (AmE), / ˈhɔːswɪd / (BrE)
A tall, strong-scented North American plant with pointed, serrated leaves and small yellowish flowers, commonly found in fields and disturbed areas.
tall, strong-scented North American plant

Examples

Farmers often struggle to control horseweed in their soybean fields.
After the fire, horseweed was among the first plants to colonize the area.
The roadside was lined with tall horseweed swaying in the breeze.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrsˌwid / (AmE), / ˈhɔːswɪd / (BrE)
a common North American weed with narrow leaves and small clusters of yellowish flowers, often found in temperate regions.
a widespread North American weed

Examples

Horseweed is often the first plant to colonize disturbed soil in gardens and fields.
Farmers find horseweed difficult to control because it quickly becomes resistant to herbicides.
The tall stalks of horseweed can reach up to five feet in height.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fleabane - a group of related flowering plants in the same family
  • Synonym: conyza - the genus in which horseweed is sometimes placed
