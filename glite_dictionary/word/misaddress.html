to misaddress

1. VERB

/ ˌmɪs.əˈdrɛs / (AmE), / ˌmɪs.əˈdrɛs / (BrE)
to put the wrong address on (a letter, package, etc.)
put the wrong address on

Examples

He accidentally misaddressed the package, so it was returned to the sender.
The letter was misaddressed and ended up at the wrong house.
Always double-check to avoid misaddressing important documents.

Synset Examples

misdirect the letter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to misdirect - to send (something) to the wrong place or person

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'mis-' (wrongly) and 'address'.
