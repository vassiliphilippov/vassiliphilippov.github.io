Lepidodendrales

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛpɪdoʊˈdɛndrəliːz / (AmE), / ˌlɛpɪdəʊˈdɛndrəliːz / (BrE)
an extinct order of large, tree-like plants that lived from the early Devonian to the Carboniferous period
extinct order of ancient tree-like plants

Examples

Fossil remains of Lepidodendrales are commonly found in Carboniferous coal beds.
Some paleobotanists specialize in studying the structure and evolution of Lepidodendrales.
The massive trunks of Lepidodendrales sometimes exceeded a meter in diameter.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Lepidodendrales', from 'Lepidodendron' (the type genus) + '-ales' (order).

2. NOUN

/ ˌlɛpɪˌdoʊdɛnˈdreɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌlɛpɪˌdɒdɛnˈdreɪliːz / (BrE)
an extinct order of large, tree-like, spore-bearing plants that first appeared in the early Devonian period and were widespread during the Carboniferous
extinct order of fossil tree-like plants

Examples

The fossilized remains of Lepidodendrales can still be found in coal deposits around the world.
Many scientists study Lepidodendrales to understand the evolution of ancient forests.
During the Carboniferous period, Lepidodendrales dominated the swampy landscapes.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Lepidodendron (a genus name) and the suffix -ales, used in botanical taxonomy.
