haematohiston

1. NOUN

/ hiˌmætəˈhɪstɑn / (AmE), / hiˌmætəˈhɪstɒn / (BrE)
A colorless protein obtained by removing the heme group from hemoglobin in red blood cells.
colorless protein from hemoglobin
medical

Examples

Researchers studied haematohiston to understand its role in the breakdown of hemoglobin.
Once the heme is removed from hemoglobin, the remaining protein is called haematohiston.
Early studies suggested that haematohiston plays no direct role in oxygen transport.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemoglobin - the iron-containing oxygen-transport metalloprotein in red blood cells

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' (blood) and 'histos' (tissue), with suffix '-on'.
