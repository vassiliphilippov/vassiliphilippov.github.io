tackiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæk.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtæk.i.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being sticky or adhesive to the touch
stickiness; adhesive quality

Examples

The tackiness of the glue made it difficult to separate the two sheets of paper.
She tested the paint for tackiness before adding a second coat.
High humidity increased the tackiness of the adhesive labels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stickiness - the quality of being sticky

Etymology

From 'tacky' + '-ness', where 'tacky' likely from Middle English 'takel' (gear; hook).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtæki.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtæk.i.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being cheap, vulgar, or lacking in taste
quality of being tasteless or vulgar

Examples

The tackiness of the decorations made the party feel less elegant than intended.
There was a certain tackiness to the souvenir shop’s merchandise, with glitter and plastic on everything.
Critics complained about the tackiness of the film’s special effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vulgarity - the state or quality of being vulgar
  • Antonym: elegance - the quality of being graceful and stylish in appearance or manner

Etymology

From 'tacky' (cheap, tasteless) + '-ness'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04825691-n
Synset POS: n
