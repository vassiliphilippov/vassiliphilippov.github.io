Ben Sira

1. NOUN

/ bɛn ˈsi.rə / (AmE), / bɛn ˈsiː.rə / (BrE)
an Apocryphal book of wisdom teachings, also known as Sirach or Ecclesiasticus, resembling Proverbs in style and content
book of maxims in the Apocrypha

Examples

Many scholars study Ben Sira to understand Jewish wisdom traditions.
The teachings in Ben Sira are often compared to those in Proverbs.
Ancient manuscripts of Ben Sira have been discovered among the Dead Sea Scrolls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ecclesiasticus - another name for the Book of Ben Sira
  • Degree Lower: Proverbs - a biblical book of wisdom sayings, similar in style to Ben Sira

Etymology

From Hebrew 'Ben Sira', meaning 'son of Sira', referring to its attributed author.
