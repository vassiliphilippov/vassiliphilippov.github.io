argonaut

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrɡəˌnɔt / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡənɔːt / (BrE)
a person engaged in a risky but potentially rewarding adventure, often used to refer to daring explorers or seekers of fortune
adventurous person; daring explorer

Examples

The young entrepreneur was called an argonaut for his bold entry into the emerging tech market.
Drawn by the promise of gold, thousands of argonauts flocked to California in 1849.
She considered herself a modern-day argonaut, always chasing the next big challenge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adventurer - a person who enjoys or seeks adventure
  • Synonym: explorer - a person who investigates unknown regions

Etymology

From Greek 'Argonautēs', meaning sailor of the Argo, the ship in Greek mythology.
