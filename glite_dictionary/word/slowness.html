slowness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsloʊnəs / (AmE), / ˈsləʊnəs / (BrE)
a condition in which intellectual capacities develop less quickly or efficiently than what is considered typical
slow intellectual development
medical

Examples

The teacher recognized signs of slowness in cognitive development and recommended testing.
Early intervention can help children who show slowness in intellectual growth reach their potential.
Families often seek support when a child displays slowness in learning basic skills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retardation - delay or hindrance in mental development

Etymology

From slow + -ness; 'slow' from Old English 'slāw' (sluggish).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsloʊnəs / (AmE), / ˈsləʊnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of moving, happening, or being done at a slow rate; absence of haste or speed.
lack of speed or quickness

Examples

Her slowness in responding made everyone anxious about the decision.
The slowness of the internet frustrated all the users in the office.
He was criticized for his slowness on the job, despite his attention to detail.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: speed - the rate at which someone or something moves or operates
  • Synonym: leisureliness - the quality of being relaxed and unhurried
  • Synonym: laziness - the quality of being unwilling to work or use energy, sometimes confused with slowness
  • Antonym: quickness - the quality of being fast or prompt

Etymology

From slow + -ness, forming a noun from the adjective.
