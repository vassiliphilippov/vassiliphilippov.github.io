authenticator

1. NOUN

/ əˈθɛntɪˌkeɪtər / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɛntɪkeɪtə / (BrE)
a person or device that determines the authenticity of something or guarantees its validity
one who verifies or guarantees authenticity

Examples

The art collector hired an authenticator to examine the painting before purchasing it.
Many online services now require a digital authenticator for extra security.
The authenticator confirmed that the signature was genuine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verifier - a person or device that checks the truth or validity of something

Etymology

From 'authenticate' + '-or', influenced by 'author' and Latin 'authenticus'.
