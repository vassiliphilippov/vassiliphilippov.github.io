clincher-built

1. ADJ

/ ˈklɪntʃər bɪlt / (AmE), / ˈklɪntʃə bɪlt / (BrE)
(of a boat or ship) having overlapping hull planks, typically as a method of construction.
having overlapping hull planks (boat/ship)

Examples

The old fishing boat was clincher-built, making it sturdy against rough seas.
Many traditional Viking ships were clincher-built for added strength and flexibility.
He admired the craftsmanship required for a clincher-built hull at the maritime museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clinker-built - having overlapping hull planks (traditional term)
  • Antonym: carvel-built - having planks laid edge to edge rather than overlapping

Etymology

From 'clincher', an alternative form of 'clinker', relating to the method of fastening planks, plus 'built'.
