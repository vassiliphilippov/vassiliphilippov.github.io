narrowed

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɛroʊd / (AmE), / ˈnærəʊd / (BrE)
reduced in width or extent; made or become narrower or more limited
reduced in width or scope

Examples

The patient's narrowed arteries required immediate medical attention.
His narrowed perspective limited his understanding of the issue.
A narrowed road made it difficult for two cars to pass each other.

Synset Examples

narrowed arteries impair blood circulation
a narrowed view of the world

Related Terms

  • Antonym: widened - made wider; increased in breadth or scope

Etymology

From the verb "narrow" plus the past participle suffix "-ed".

2. ADJ

/ ˈnæroʊd / (AmE), / ˈnærəʊd / (BrE)
made or become smaller in width or size, especially by being compressed together
made smaller in width by compression

Examples

She looked at him with narrowed eyes, clearly suspicious of his motives.
The road passes through a narrowed section between two tall cliffs.
His narrowed focus allowed him to excel in his studies.

Synset Examples

his narrowed eyes

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: narrow - having a small distance from one side to the other
