beggarly

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɛɡərli / (AmE), / ˈbɛɡəli / (BrE)
very poor; characteristic of or suitable for a beggar.
very poor; like a beggar

Examples

After losing his job, he was forced to live a beggarly existence for several years.
The organization provided food and blankets to those trapped in beggarly conditions.
Her beggarly salary barely covered the cost of rent and basic food.

Synset Examples

a beggarly existence in the slums
a mean hut

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impoverished - lacking money or material possessions
  • Synonym: destitute - extremely poor; without the basic necessities of life
  • Antonym: wealthy - having a great deal of money, resources, or assets

Etymology

From Middle English *beggere* (beggar) + *-ly*.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbɛɡ.ər.li / (AmE), / ˈbɛɡ.əli / (BrE)
(of an amount) so small or meagre as to be contemptible; pitifully inadequate.
contemptibly small; pitifully meagre

Examples

The workers protested against the beggarly wages offered by the factory.
He made a beggarly donation that barely covered the cost of a meal.
They were forced to accept a beggarly sum for their valuable land.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meagre - lacking in quantity or quality
  • Synonym: paltry - small or meagre in amount, and not worth considering
  • Antonym: generous - (of a sum or amount) larger or more plentiful than strictly necessary

Etymology

From Middle English 'beggerly', from 'beggar' + '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01116711-s
Synset POS: s
