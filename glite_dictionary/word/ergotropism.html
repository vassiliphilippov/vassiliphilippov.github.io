ergotropism

1. NOUN

/ ˌɝːɡəˈtroʊpɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɜːɡəˈtrəʊpɪzəm / (BrE)
a tendency or preference for activity, especially work or effort; an affinity for work.
affinity or drive for work

Examples

The manager admired his employee’s ergotropism and dedication to every task.
Researchers studied the ergotropism observed in certain personality types who thrive in highly structured environments.
Her natural ergotropism made her excel in demanding, fast-paced jobs.

Etymology

From Greek 'ergon' (work) + 'tropism' (turning toward).
