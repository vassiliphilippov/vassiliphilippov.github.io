Bombycilla garrulus

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɑːmbɪˈsɪlə ɡəˈruːləs / (AmE), / ˌbɒmbɪˈsɪlə ɡəˈruːləs / (BrE)
a large species of waxwing native to northern North America and Eurasia, known for its silky plumage and distinctive crested head
large waxwing bird species

Examples

The Bombycilla garrulus is often seen feeding on berries during the winter months.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a flock of Bombycilla garrulus near the northern forest edge.
Unlike the cedar waxwing, the Bombycilla garrulus has striking chestnut colors on its face and wings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waxwing - a bird of the Bombycillidae family with silky plumage and a short crest

Etymology

From Latin: 'Bombycilla' meaning 'silk-tail', and 'garrulus' meaning 'noisy' or 'chattering'.
