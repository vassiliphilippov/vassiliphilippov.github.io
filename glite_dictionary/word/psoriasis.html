psoriasis

1. NOUN

/ səˈraɪəsɪs / (AmE), / səˈraɪəsɪs / (BrE)
a chronic skin disease causing red, scaly patches, especially on the scalp, elbows, knees, and lower back
chronic skin disease with red, scaly patches
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with psoriasis after examining the red, scaly rash on her scalp.
People with psoriasis may experience flare-ups triggered by stress or cold weather.
New treatments have helped many patients better manage their psoriasis symptoms.

Etymology

From Greek psōriasis, from psōra (itch).
