cordwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrdˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdˌwʊd / (BrE)
Firewood that is cut and stacked in cords, typically sold by the cord as a unit of measurement.
firewood sold in measured cords

Examples

The farmer stacked the cordwood neatly behind the barn for the winter.
We bought two cords of cordwood to heat the cabin through the cold months.
Prices for cordwood have risen due to increased demand for firewood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firewood - wood burned as fuel, especially in a fireplace or stove

Etymology

From 'cord' (a unit of measurement for stacked firewood) + 'wood'.
