Thielavia basicola

1. NOUN

/ θaɪˈleɪ.vi.ə ˌbæsɪˈkoʊlə / (AmE), / θaɪˈleɪ.vi.ə ˌbæsɪˈkəʊlə / (BrE)
a species of fungus that causes brown root rot in pea, potato, cucumber, and related plants
fungus causing brown root rot in plants
medical

Examples

Thielavia basicola is a major cause of brown root rot in commercial pea crops.
Farmers use crop rotation to reduce the impact of Thielavia basicola infestations.
Root lesions caused by Thielavia basicola can stunt plant growth and decrease yields.

Etymology

From genus name 'Thielavia' and species name 'basicola', used in mycological taxonomy.
