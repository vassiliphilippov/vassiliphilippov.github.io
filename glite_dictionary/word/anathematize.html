to anathematize

1. VERB

/ əˈnæθəməˌtaɪz / (AmE), / əˈnæθəmətaɪz / (BrE)
to formally curse or condemn as evil, often with a threat of divine punishment
to curse or condemn as evil

Examples

The church leaders chose to anathematize the heretical teachings during the council.
In his speech, the preacher anathematized those who would lead others astray.
Some critics anathematize modern art for abandoning traditional values.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to excommunicate - to officially exclude from participation in the sacraments and services of the Christian Church
  • Antonym: to bless - to invoke or confer divine favor upon; opposite of curse

Etymology

From Medieval Latin anathematizare, from Greek anathematizein (to devote, to curse)
