ribald

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪb.əld, rɪˈbɔld / (AmE), / ˈrɪb.əld, rɪˈbɔːld / (BrE)
a person who makes coarse jokes or uses vulgar and offensive language, typically in a humorous way
person making coarse, vulgar jokes

Examples

The king's court always included at least one ribald to keep the nobles laughing.
His reputation as a ribald made him unwelcome at formal gatherings.
Medieval plays often featured a ribald for comic relief.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buffoon - a person who amuses others by ridiculous behavior
  • Antonym: prude - a person easily shocked by rude things

Etymology

From Old French *ribaut*, meaning 'rogue' or 'profligate'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɪb.əld / (AmE), / ˈrɪb.əld / (BrE)
referring to speech, behavior, or humor that is coarse, vulgar, or indecent, especially in a humorous way
coarse or vulgar humor

Examples

His ribald jokes had everyone in the room laughing and blushing at the same time.
The comedian's ribald humor wasn't suitable for a family audience, but many adults enjoyed it.
She whispered a ribald comment that made her friends burst out laughing.

Synset Examples

bawdy songs
off-color jokes
ribald language

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bawdy - indecent or lewd, especially in a humorous way
  • Synonym: off-color - slightly indecent or improper, usually for humor

Etymology

From Old French *ribaut* (rogue, scoundrel), from earlier *riber* (to be wanton).
