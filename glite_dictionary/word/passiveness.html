passiveness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæs.ɪv.nəs / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.sɪv.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of accepting what happens or what others do without active response or resistance; submissiveness.
submissiveness; lack of active response

Examples

Her passiveness made it difficult for her to stand up for herself in the meeting.
The child's passiveness in class concerned his teachers, who encouraged more active participation.
In relationships, excessive passiveness can lead to feelings of frustration and resentment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submissiveness - readiness to conform to the authority or will of others; meekness
  • Antonym: assertiveness - the quality of being self-assured and confident without being aggressive

Etymology

From 'passive' (from Latin passivus, meaning 'capable of feeling or suffering') + '-ness', forming a noun indicating state or quality.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpæsɪvnɪs / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of not acting or not taking initiative; a tendency to accept or allow things to happen rather than actively responding
lack of activity or initiative

Examples

His passiveness in meetings disappointed his managers, who expected more engagement from him.
Some people mistake passiveness for patience, but they are not the same thing.
The film subtly critiqued the passiveness of ordinary citizens in the face of injustice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passivity - the state of being passive; acceptance of what happens without active response
  • Antonym: activity - the condition of doing something; taking action

Etymology

From 'passive' + '-ness', modeled after Latin passive and English suffix '-ness'.
