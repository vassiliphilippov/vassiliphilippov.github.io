oleo

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊlioʊ / (AmE), / ˈəʊliəʊ / (BrE)
A spread made mainly from vegetable oils, used as a substitute for butter.
butter substitute spread

Examples

We used oleo instead of butter in the cookie recipe.
During the war, oleo became a popular and affordable spread for families.
You can melt oleo to use in baking if you don't have real butter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: margarine - a substitute for butter made from vegetable oils or animal fats

Etymology

Short for 'oleomargarine', from Latin 'oleum' (oil) and 'margarine' (from Greek 'margaron', meaning pearl).
