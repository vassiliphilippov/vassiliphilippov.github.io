Lobelia siphilitica

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈbiliə sɪfəˈlɪtɪkə / (AmE), / ləʊˈbiːliə sɪfɪˈlɪtɪkə / (BrE)
A tall, erect perennial herb native to eastern North America, with dense spikes of blue flowers.
a perennial North American herb with blue flowers

Examples

Lobelia siphilitica thrives in moist meadows and along stream banks in its native range.
Gardeners prize Lobelia siphilitica for its striking blue flower spikes and hardiness.
Pollinators such as bees and butterflies are often attracted to Lobelia siphilitica when it blooms in late summer.

Etymology

From genus name Lobelia (after botanist Matthias de l’Obel) and species name siphilitica, referring to past use against syphilis.
