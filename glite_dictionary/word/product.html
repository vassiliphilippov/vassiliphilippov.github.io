product

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.dʌkt / (AmE), / ˈprɒd.ʌkt / (BrE)
an item or substance that is created or manufactured as a result of a process
created or manufactured item

Examples

The new product quickly became popular with consumers worldwide.
Our company launches several innovative products every year.
Quality control ensures that each product meets strict safety standards.

Synset Examples

they improve their product every year
they export most of their agricultural production

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goods - items produced for sale or use
  • Antonym: service - an action performed for someone; the opposite of a material product

Etymology

From Latin 'productum', meaning 'something produced'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04014270-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːdəkt / (AmE), / ˈprɒdʌkt / (BrE)
a thing that results from a particular action, process, or set of circumstances
result of an action or circumstances

Examples

Her confidence is the product of years of experience in the field.
The argument was the product of a misunderstanding between the two friends.
This feeling of satisfaction is often the product of hard work and perseverance.

Synset Examples

skill is the product of hours of practice
his reaction was the product of hunger and fatigue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: result - something that happens because of something else
  • Antonym: cause - the reason something happens

Etymology

From Latin 'productum', past participle of 'producere' meaning 'to bring forth'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-11435956-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈprɑdʌkt / (AmE), / ˈprɒdʌkt / (BrE)
A chemical substance that is formed as the result of a chemical reaction.
chemical formed in a reaction

Examples

The product formed in this experiment is a white crystalline solid.
Water is a common product of many combustion reactions.
The teacher asked the students to identify the product of mixing hydrochloric acid with sodium hydroxide.

Synset Examples

a product of lime and nitric acid

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reactant - a substance that takes part in and undergoes change during a reaction

Etymology

From Latin productum, meaning 'something produced'.

5. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.dʌkt / (AmE), / ˈprɒd.ʌkt / (BrE)
the number or quantity resulting from multiplying two or more numbers or amounts together
result of multiplication

Examples

The product of 4 and 5 is 20.
In algebra, you often need to find the product of variables.
The teacher asked them to write down the product for each pair of numbers.

Synset Examples

the product of 2 and 3 is 6

Related Terms

  • Synonym: factor - a number that is multiplied to obtain a product
  • Antonym: sum - the result of addition
