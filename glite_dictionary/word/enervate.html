to enervate

1. VERB

/ ˈɛnərˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛnəˌveɪt / (BrE)
to make someone feel drained of energy or vitality; to weaken mentally or morally
weaken or drain energy

Examples

The oppressive heat can enervate even the most active people.
Her long illness had enervated her, leaving her unable to concentrate.
A sense of hopelessness began to enervate the team after repeated losses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to weaken - to make or become weaker
  • Antonym: to strengthen - to make or become stronger

Etymology

From Latin enervatus, past participle of enervare (to weaken), from e- (out) + nervus (sinew).
