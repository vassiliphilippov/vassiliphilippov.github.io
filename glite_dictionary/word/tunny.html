tunny

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌni / (AmE), / ˈtʌni / (BrE)
a large marine fish of the genus Thunnus, related to the mackerel, found mainly in warm waters and valued for food and sport
a large marine fish (genus Thunnus)

Examples

Fishermen set out early in the morning hoping to catch a giant tunny.
The tunny is prized both as a game fish and for its delicious meat.
Populations of tunny have declined in some regions due to overfishing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuna - a large marine fish of the genus Thunnus

Etymology

From Italian tonno, from Latin thunnus, from Greek thynnos.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʌni / (AmE), / ˈtʌni / (BrE)
a large, warm-water, fatty fish of the genus Thunnus, commonly known as tuna, often served as steaks
a large edible fish (tuna)

Examples

The fishermen were hoping to catch a tunny large enough to feed the entire village.
Seared tunny steaks are a specialty at the coastal restaurant.
The migration patterns of the tunny have fascinated marine biologists for decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuna - another name for the tunny, a large edible fish

Etymology

From Old Provençal *ton*, from Latin *thunnus*, from Greek *thynnos* (tuna).
