Astragalus danicus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæstrəˈɡeɪləs dəˈnaɪkəs / (AmE), / ˌastrəˈɡaləs dəˈnaɪkəs / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to southern and western Europe, characterized by dense clusters of purple or violet flowers.
European perennial plant with violet flowers

Examples

The rare Astragalus danicus was found growing in the limestone meadows of southern France.
Botanists study Astragalus danicus for its attractive purple racemes and role in European grasslands.
Gardeners value Astragalus danicus for its hardiness and striking violet flowers.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus name 'Astragalus' and species Latinized from 'danicus' meaning 'Danish'.
