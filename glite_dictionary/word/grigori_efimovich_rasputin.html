Grigori Efimovich Rasputin

1. PROPN

/ ɡrɪˈɡɔri ɛˈfiməvɪtʃ ræˈspjutɪn / (AmE), / ɡrɪˈɡɔːri ɪˈfiːməvɪtʃ ræsˈpjuːtɪn / (BrE)
A Russian mystic and self-proclaimed holy man who became a close adviser to the family of Nicholas II, the last Emperor of Russia, and was assassinated by nobles who believed he threatened the monarchy (1872–1916).
Russian mystic and royal adviser (1872–1916)

Examples

Many historians continue to debate the influence Grigori Efimovich Rasputin had over the Russian royal family.
A musical about Grigori Efimovich Rasputin was a surprise success on Broadway.
Legends about Grigori Efimovich Rasputin's alleged supernatural powers continue to fascinate people today.

Etymology

Russian: Григо́рий Ефи́мович Распу́тин (Grigórij Jefímovič Rasputín).
