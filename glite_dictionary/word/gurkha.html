Gurkha

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɜr.kə / (AmE), / ˈɡɜː.kə / (BrE)
a member of a Hindu ethnic group from Nepal, descended from Brahmins and Rajputs, traditionally known for serving as soldiers
member of a Hindu ethnic group from Nepal

Examples

Many British regiments have recruited Gurkhas for their renowned bravery.
The Gurkha soldiers are highly respected for their loyalty and discipline.
She studied the history of the Gurkha people and their contributions to world wars.

Etymology

From Nepali 'Gorkhā', the name of a region and former kingdom in Nepal.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɜrkə / (AmE), / ˈɡɜːkə / (BrE)
a member of a people from Nepal renowned for serving as soldiers, especially within the British army, known for courage and skill in combat
Nepalese soldier in British army

Examples

The Gurkha regiments have long been celebrated for their bravery and loyalty.
A Gurkha soldier received a medal for his exceptional courage in battle.
Many Gurkhas have chosen to settle in the United Kingdom after their military service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soldier - a person who serves in an army

Etymology

From Nepali 'Gorkhāli', meaning 'inhabitant of Gorkha', a region in Nepal.
