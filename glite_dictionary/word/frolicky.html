frolicky

1. ADJ

/ ˈfrɑːlɪki / (AmE), / ˈfrɒlɪki / (BrE)
Full of playful and lively merriment; fond of frolicking.
playful; full of lively merriment

Examples

The children became especially frolicky after the snow started falling.
Her frolicky laughter echoed through the garden on a sunny afternoon.
During the spring picnic, a frolicky mood spread among the entire group.

Synset Examples

frolicsome students celebrated their graduation with parties and practical jokes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frolicsome - full of high-spirited fun; playful
  • Synonym: playful - fond of games, fun, or amusement; lighthearted
  • Antonym: serious - grave in manner or disposition; not playful

Etymology

From 'frolic' (to play and move about cheerfully) plus the adjective suffix '-y'.
