winding

1. ADJ

/ ˈwaɪndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪndɪŋ / (BrE)
having many bends or turns; not straight
having many bends or turns

Examples

The car made its way down the winding road that hugged the side of the mountain.
We followed a winding path through the dense forest, unsure of where it would lead.
The river carved a winding course across the valley, glittering in the afternoon sun.

Synset Examples

a tortuous road up the mountain
winding roads are full of surprises

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twisty - having many twists and turns; not straight
  • Antonym: straight - without bends or curves; direct
  • Synonym: tortuous - full of twists and turns

2. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪndɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of turning or twisting something around to coil it or to tighten it, especially as with a key or string.
the act of twisting or coiling

Examples

The winding of the old clock required a special key.
With each careful winding, the yarn became more tangled.
You can hear the clicking sound during the winding of the music box.

Synset Examples

he put the key in the old clock and gave it a good wind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twisting - the act of turning something in a spiral or circular motion

Etymology

From the verb 'wind', Old English 'windan' (to twist, turn).

3. ADJ

/ ˈwaɪn.dɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪn.dɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a road, path, or river) having many bends or turns; repeatedly curving or twisting.
having many bends or turns

Examples

The winding road through the mountains offered breathtaking views at every turn.
We hiked along a winding path that disappeared into the forest.
A winding stream flows behind the old farmhouse.

Synset Examples

meandering streams
rambling forest paths
the river followed its wandering course
a winding country road

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meandering - following a winding and turning course, especially of a river or road
  • Antonym: straight - extending or moving in one direction without bends or curves

Etymology

From the verb 'wind' (to turn repeatedly), ultimately from Old English 'windan'.
