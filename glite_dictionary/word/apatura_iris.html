Apatura iris

1. NOUN

/ əˈpætʊrə ˈaɪrɪs / (AmE), / apəˈtjʊərə ˈaɪrɪs / (BrE)
a large European butterfly, the purple emperor, distinguished by the male's wings shaded with purple
large European butterfly, the purple emperor

Examples

The male Apatura iris displays striking iridescent purple markings on its wings.
Many butterfly enthusiasts travel to forests to observe the elusive Apatura iris in its natural habitat.
The caterpillar of the Apatura iris feeds primarily on the leaves of willows.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name Apatura and species name iris, both from Latin.
