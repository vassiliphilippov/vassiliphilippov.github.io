Carcharhinus leucas

1. NOUN

/ kɑːr.kəˈraɪ.nəs ˈluː.kəs / (AmE), / kɑː.kəˈraɪ.nəs ˈljuː.kəs / (BrE)
A large and aggressive species of shark, also known as the bull shark, found in warm, shallow coastal waters worldwide.
species of shark (bull shark)

Examples

The Carcharhinus leucas is well-known for its ability to swim in both saltwater and freshwater rivers.
Researchers tagged several Carcharhinus leucas to track their migration patterns along the coast.
Due to its aggressive nature, the Carcharhinus leucas is considered dangerous to humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bull shark - Common name of Carcharhinus leucas

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, with Carcharhinus from Greek 'karcharos' (sharp) + 'rhinos' (nose), and 'leucas' from Greek 'leukos' (white).
