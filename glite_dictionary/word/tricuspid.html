tricuspid

1. ADJ

/ traɪˈkʌs.pɪd / (AmE), / traɪˈkʌs.pɪd / (BrE)
having three cusps or points, especially referring to a tooth or a heart valve
having three cusps or points

Examples

The dentist noticed that her tricuspid molar was healthy and intact.
The tricuspid valve helps separate the right atrium from the right ventricle in the heart.
Researchers studied the unique structure of the tricuspid teeth in certain mammals.

Synset Examples

tricuspid molar
tricuspid valve

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bicuspid - having two cusps or points (esp. a tooth or valve)

Etymology

From Latin 'tricuspidalis', from 'tri-' (three) + 'cuspis' (point, cusp).
