silicate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪlɪkət / (BrE)
a salt or ester derived from silicic acid, commonly found in minerals and rocks
a salt or ester of silicic acid

Examples

Many minerals in the Earth's crust are forms of silicate.
Glass is often made by melting sand, which is primarily composed of silicate minerals.
The scientist examined the silicate structure under a microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silica - a hard, unreactive, colorless compound that occurs as the mineral quartz

Etymology

From Latin 'silica' (flint, hard stone) + '-ate' (denoting salts or esters).
