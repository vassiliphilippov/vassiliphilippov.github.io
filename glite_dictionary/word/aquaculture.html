aquaculture

1. NOUN

/ ˈæ.kwəˌkʌl.tʃər / (AmE), / ˈakwəˌkʌltʃə / (BrE)
the cultivation of aquatic animals or plants for food, such as fish, shellfish, or seaweed
cultivation of aquatic animals or plants for food

Examples

The demand for seafood has led to rapid expansion of aquaculture in many countries.
Sustainable aquaculture practices are needed to protect marine ecosystems.
She is conducting research on disease control in aquaculture systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mariculture - the cultivation of marine organisms in the sea

Etymology

From Latin 'aqua' (water) + 'culture' (cultivation).
