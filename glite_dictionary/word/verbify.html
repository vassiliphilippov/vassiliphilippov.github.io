to verbify

1. VERB

/ ˈvɜrbəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈvɜːbɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to turn a noun or another part of speech into a verb, especially by creating a new verbal form
make into a verb

Examples

Linguists sometimes verbify nouns to illustrate changes in language.
The word 'Google' was quickly verbified as people began to 'Google' things online.
Students often try to verbify technical terms in casual speech.

Synset Examples

‘mouse’ has been verbified by computer users

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to verbalize - to express in words or make verbal; sometimes used as a synonym when meaning to make into a verb

Etymology

Formed from 'verb' + '-ify' (to make into); patterned after similar formations like 'personify'.
