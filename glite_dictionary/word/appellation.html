appellation

1. NOUN

/ ˌæpəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌapəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a name, title, or designation by which a person or thing is identified or distinguished
name or title used for identification

Examples

The region is famous for wine produced under the appellation Bordeaux.
He earned the appellation of 'The Great' after his many victories.
The committee debated which appellation should be officially adopted for the new project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: name - a word or set of words by which a person or thing is known, addressed, or referred to
  • Synonym: title - a name that describes someone's position or job

Etymology

From Latin 'appellatio' (a naming or calling), from 'appellare' (to call upon).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæpəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌapəˈleɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
A legally defined and protected geographical name used to identify where the grapes for a wine are grown.
geographical name for wine origin

Examples

The wine label indicated that it came from the Bordeaux appellation.
Producers must follow strict regulations to use the Champagne appellation.
This region was recently granted its own official appellation status.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: denomination - a name or designation, especially one for a class of things
  • Synonym: brand - a type of product manufactured by a particular company under a particular name

Etymology

From French *appellation* (name, designation), from Latin *appellatio*.
