jaunty

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɑnti / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːnti / (BrE)
having a lively, cheerful, and self-confident manner or appearance
lively and cheerful in manner

Examples

He strolled into the room with a jaunty smile and energetic steps.
Her jaunty hat added a playful touch to her outfit.
Despite the bad news, he maintained a jaunty attitude throughout the day.

Synset Examples

looking chipper, like a man … diverted by his own wit
life that is gay, brisk, and debonair
walked with a jaunty step
a jaunty optimist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheerful - happy and showing positive feelings
  • Synonym: lively - full of life and energy
  • Antonym: gloomy - sad or lacking in cheer

Etymology

Perhaps from French gentil (polite, elegant), influenced by English dialect jaunty (elegant, fashionable).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɑːnti / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːnti / (BrE)
having a lively, cheerful, and stylish appearance or manner; showing confidence and energy in one's dress or behavior
lively and stylish in appearance or manner

Examples

He wore a jaunty fedora, which made him stand out in the crowd.
Her jaunty walk and big smile brightened everyone's day at the office.
The jaunty scarf added a cheerful touch to her outfit.

Synset Examples

a dapper young man
a jaunty red hat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dapper - neat and stylish in appearance (especially of a man)

Etymology

Probably from French *gentil* (nice, stylish, elegant), influenced by English 'jaunce' (to prance about).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00975112-s
Synset POS: s
