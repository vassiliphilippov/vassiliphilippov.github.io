Domingo de Guzman

1. PROPN

/ doʊˈmiŋɡoʊ deɪ ɡuˈzmɑːn / (AmE), / dəˈmɪŋɡəʊ deɪ ɡʊzˈmæn / (BrE)
A Spanish priest who founded the Dominican Order, also known as the Black Friars, around 1170–1221.
Spanish priest; founder of the Dominican Order

Examples

Domingo de Guzman was canonized as a saint for his role in founding the Dominican Order.
Many historical texts detail the influence Domingo de Guzman had on the spread of the Catholic faith.
The Black Friars trace their origins back to Domingo de Guzman in the early thirteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dominican - a member of the religious order founded by Domingo de Guzman
