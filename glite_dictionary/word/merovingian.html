Merovingian

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛrəˈvɪndʒiən / (AmE), / ˌmɛrəˈvɪndʒiən / (BrE)
a member of a Frankish dynasty founded by Clovis I, which ruled parts of Gaul and Germany from about 500 to 750 AD
member of early Frankish ruling dynasty

Examples

The Merovingian kings were known for their long hair, which distinguished them from their subjects.
Historians study the rise and fall of the Merovingian dynasty to understand early medieval Europe.
Artifacts from the Merovingian period have been discovered in northern France.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Carolingian - member of the Frankish dynasty succeeding the Merovingians

Etymology

From French Mérovingien, from medieval Latin Merovingi, plural of Merovech, the dynasty's semi-legendary founder.

2. ADJ

/ ˌmɛrəˈvɪndʒiən / (AmE), / ˌmɛrəˈvɪndʒɪən / (BrE)
of or relating to the Merovingian dynasty, a Frankish ruling family in Gaul from the 5th to 8th centuries.
relating to the Merovingian dynasty

Examples

The Merovingian kings played a crucial role in the early history of France.
Artifacts from the Merovingian period reveal much about Frankish society.
Historians often debate the true power held by Merovingian rulers.

Etymology

From French 'Mérovingien', from Mérovée or Merovech, a semi-legendary Frankish leader.

3. NOUN

/ ˌmɛrəˈvɪndʒiən / (AmE), / ˌmɛrəˈvɪndʒiən / (BrE)
a member of the Merovingian dynasty, which ruled the Franks in parts of present-day France and Germany from the 5th to 8th centuries
member of the Frankish Merovingian dynasty

Examples

A Merovingian king ruled the Frankish people during the early Middle Ages.
Researchers discovered the tomb of a powerful Merovingian noble near the ancient town.
The art and architecture of the Merovingian period remain influential in European history.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Carolingian - a member of the Carolingian dynasty, which succeeded the Merovingians

Etymology

From French Mérovingien, from Medieval Latin Merovingi, referring to the descendants of Merovech, a semi-legendary leader of the Salian Franks.

WordNet ID: oewn-10330071-n
Synset POS: n
