foodstuff

1. NOUN

/ ˈfuːd.stʌf / (AmE), / ˈfuːd.stʌf / (BrE)
a substance that is used or prepared as food, especially one of the basic substances that forms part of people's diet
substance used or prepared as food

Examples

The government imposed strict controls on imported foodstuffs.
Many basic foodstuffs such as rice and wheat have become more expensive.
Dried fruits and nuts are important foodstuffs in Middle Eastern cuisine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: food - what people and animals eat to live

Etymology

From 'food' + 'stuff', dating to the 18th century in English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfuːdˌstʌf / (AmE), / ˈfuːd.stʌf / (BrE)
a substance used as food, especially when considered as a product sold or processed commercially
substance used as food

Examples

The government imposed strict quality controls on imported foodstuffs.
Rising prices of basic foodstuffs have put many families under financial strain.
Supermarkets offer a wide variety of organic foodstuffs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: food - any nutritious substance that people or animals eat or drink

Etymology

Formed from 'food' + 'stuff', first recorded in the early 17th century.
