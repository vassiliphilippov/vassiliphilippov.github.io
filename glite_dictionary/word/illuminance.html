illuminance

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈluːmɪˌneɪns / (AmE), / ɪˈljuːmɪnəns / (BrE)
the amount of luminous flux falling on a unit area; a measure of how much light is received by a surface
measure of light received by a surface
medical

Examples

The illuminance in the laboratory was measured to ensure the lighting met health and safety standards.
Proper illuminance is crucial for reducing eye strain in office environments.
A light meter can be used to check the illuminance on a desk to ensure it's adequate for reading.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illumination - the process or result of lighting something up

Etymology

From Latin 'illuminare' (to light up), with the scientific suffix '-ance.'
