idealisation

1. NOUN

/ aɪˌdiələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / aɪˌdɪəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of portraying or regarding something as perfect or better than reality.
depiction as perfect or better than reality
british

Examples

The idealisation of childhood can overlook the real challenges many children face.
Her book is criticized for its idealisation of ancient Greece.
Romantic poets were known for their idealisation of nature, often depicting it as pure and harmonious.

Synset Examples

the idealization of rural life was very misleading

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idealization - portrayal of something as ideal (American spelling)

Etymology

From the French 'idéalisation', based on 'ideal'.

2. NOUN

/ aɪˌdiələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / aɪˌdiəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
something that exists only as an idea and not in reality; a conception or representation that is perfect or better than reality.
thing existing only as an idea

Examples

The notion of a perfect democracy is often just an idealisation rather than an achievable goal.
His theory was based on an idealisation of human behavior, ignoring practical limitations.
For simplicity, we use an idealisation in which all surfaces are perfectly smooth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abstraction - a general idea not associated with any specific instance
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

From Medieval Latin idealis (ideal) + -ation.

3. NOUN

/ aɪˌdiəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / aɪˌdiːəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(psychiatry) A defense mechanism in which a person regards someone or something they feel ambivalent about as either entirely good or entirely bad, splitting their perception into two extreme representations.
psychiatric defense mechanism of perceiving extremes
medical

Examples

The therapist explained that idealisation made it hard for Clara to accept flaws in her partner.
Children often resort to idealisation when they have mixed feelings about their parents.
In clinical settings, idealisation is seen as a way to manage conflicting emotions toward significant others.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: devaluation - defense mechanism involving viewing someone as all bad

Etymology

From Medieval Latin idealis (relating to an idea) + -ation; see also idealize.
