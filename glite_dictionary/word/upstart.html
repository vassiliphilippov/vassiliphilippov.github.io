upstart

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌpˌstɑrt / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌstɑːt / (BrE)
a gymnastic movement in which the body starts with the legs above the upper body, then moves to an upright position by arching the back and swinging the legs downward while forcing the chest upright
gymnastic movement from inverted to upright position

Examples

The gymnast performed a flawless upstart on the uneven bars.
Mastering the upstart requires both strength and coordination.
Her coach praised her technique during the upstart phase of the routine.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌpˌstɑrt / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌstɑːt / (BrE)
a person who has risen suddenly to wealth or power and behaves arrogantly or presumptuously
arrogant person risen to power

Examples

Many viewed the young entrepreneur as an upstart who didn't respect tradition.
The established politicians dismissed her as an upstart with no real experience.
Despite being called an upstart, he quickly gained the admiration of his peers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parvenu - a person who has recently risen to a higher social or economic class, often regarded as lacking refinement
  • Antonym: nobody - a person of no importance or authority

Etymology

From Middle English 'upsterten' (to spring up), from 'up' + 'start'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈʌpˌstɑrt / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌstɑːt / (BrE)
a person who has risen suddenly to wealth or high position, especially one who is regarded as presumptuous or lacking the social graces of the established class
person newly risen to wealth or position

Examples

The upstart billionaire quickly made enemies among the city's old-money families.
Many saw him as an upstart who did not understand the traditions of the company.
Her critics dismissed her as an upstart, but she proved her worth through hard work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parvenu - a person of obscure origin who has gained wealth, influence, or celebrity

Etymology

From Middle English, combining 'up' and 'start', referring to someone who has started up suddenly.

Source: WordNet

4. ADJ

/ ˈʌpˌstɑrt / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌstɑːt / (BrE)
(of a person) having risen suddenly to wealth or position, often regarded as lacking the necessary manners or social skills for the new status.
newly rich; lacking social grace

Examples

The party was full of upstart millionaires unfamiliar with traditional etiquette.
Many saw the new CEO as an upstart who didn't understand the company's culture.
His upstart attitude alienated those who valued tradition and respect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parvenu - a person who has recently acquired wealth or status but lacks the manners and social skills of the new class

Etymology

From Middle English upstarten, meaning ‘to rise or jump up.’
