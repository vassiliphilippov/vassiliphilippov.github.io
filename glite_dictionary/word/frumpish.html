frumpish

1. ADJ

/ ˈfrʌmpɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈfrʌmpɪʃ / (BrE)
(of a person, thing, or style) primly old-fashioned or dowdy, lacking in style or fashionableness.
old-fashioned or dowdy; lacking style

Examples

Her frumpish outfit made her look much older than she actually was.
The rooms were decorated in a rather frumpish manner, with faded curtains and heavy furniture.
Despite her frumpish demeanor, she had a sharp wit and lively sense of humor.

Synset Examples

nothing so frumpish as last year's gambling game

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dowdy - unfashionable and without style in appearance
  • Antonym: stylish - fashionable and elegant

Etymology

From 'frump' (dowdy, old-fashioned person) + '-ish', English adjective suffix.
