Buckleya distichophylla

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌk.li.ə ˌdɪs.tɪ.kəˈfɪl.i.ə / (AmE), / ˈbʌk.li.ə ˌdɪs.tɪ.kəˈfɪl.i.ə / (BrE)
a parasitic shrub native to the eastern United States, with opposite leaves, small greenish flowers, and dull green, olive-like fruits
a parasitic shrub of eastern US

Examples

We spotted a rare Buckleya distichophylla while hiking in North Carolina last spring.
Because Buckleya distichophylla is a parasitic plant, it depends on the roots of nearby trees for nutrients.
Botanists are concerned about the declining population of Buckleya distichophylla due to habitat loss.

Etymology

From the genus name Buckleya, named after botanist Samuel Botsford Buckley, and species epithet distichophylla, from Greek roots meaning 'two-ranked leaves'.
