to depilate

1. VERB

/ ˈdɛpɪleɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛpɪleɪt / (BrE)
to remove hair from the body or a part of the body, especially by using a chemical or mechanical method
remove body hair

Examples

Many athletes depilate their legs to reduce resistance when competing.
She decided to depilate her arms before the wedding for a smoother look.
Some people experience skin irritation after they depilate frequently.

Synset Examples

epilate her legs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to epilate - to remove hair by pulling it out from the root

Etymology

From Latin 'depilare', from 'de-' (removal) + 'pilus' (hair).
