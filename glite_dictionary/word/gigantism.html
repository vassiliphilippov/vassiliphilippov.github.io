gigantism

1. NOUN

/ dʒaɪˈɡænˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / dʒɪˈɡæn.tɪ.zəm / (BrE)
Abnormal largeness of stature due to excessive growth, especially resulting from overproduction of growth hormone.
abnormal large stature
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the young patient with gigantism after noticing his unusual growth rate.
People with untreated gigantism may experience significant health challenges as they age.
Gigantism is often caused by a tumor affecting the pituitary gland.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dwarfism - Abnormally short stature due to medical condition

Etymology

From Greek 'gigas', 'gigantos' (giant) + '-ism'.

2. NOUN

/ dʒaɪˈgænˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / dʒɪˈɡæn.tɪ.zəm / (BrE)
A medical condition in which abnormal growth results in excessive physical size, usually caused by overproduction of growth hormone by the pituitary gland.
abnormal overgrowth due to excess growth hormone
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the child with gigantism after noticing abnormal growth patterns.
Unchecked gigantism can lead to further health complications if not treated early.
Gigantism is often distinguished from acromegaly by the age of onset.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acromegaly - enlargement of extremities due to excessive growth hormone in adults

Etymology

From Greek gigas, gigant- (giant) + -ism.
