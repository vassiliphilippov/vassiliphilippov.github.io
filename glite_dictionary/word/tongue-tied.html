tongue-tied

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʌŋˌtaɪd / (AmE), / ˈtʌŋ.taɪd / (BrE)
Unable to speak easily or express yourself clearly, usually because of nervousness or embarrassment.
unable to speak clearly due to nerves or embarrassment

Examples

She felt tongue-tied when asked to speak in front of the class.
He became tongue-tied whenever he tried to talk to his crush.
During the interview, I was so nervous that I was completely tongue-tied.

Synset Examples

felt tongue-tied with embarrassment
incoherent with grief

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inarticulate - unable to express oneself clearly; not able to speak clearly or coherently
  • Antonym: eloquent - able to express oneself clearly and effectively

Etymology

Metaphorically refers to the tongue being 'tied' or restricted, dating from the 16th century.
