norgestrel

1. NOUN

/ nɔːrˈɡɛstrəl / (AmE), / nɔːˈdʒɛstrəl / (BrE)
a synthetic progestin used chiefly in oral contraceptives to prevent pregnancy
synthetic progestin in contraceptives
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed norgestrel to help regulate her menstrual cycle.
Most modern oral contraceptives contain synthetic hormones like norgestrel.
Some patients reported mild side effects after taking norgestrel as part of their birth control regimen.

Etymology

Blend of 'nor' (a chemical prefix indicating absence of a group) and 'gestrel' from 'gestagen' (progestogen) and 'estr-' (estrogen).
