Arbutus unedo

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈbjuːtəs juːˈniːdoʊ / (AmE), / ɑːˈbjuːtəs juːˈniːdəʊ / (BrE)
A small evergreen European shrub or tree, also known as the strawberry tree, bearing many-seeded scarlet berries that are edible but bland; native to Ireland, southern Europe, and Asia Minor.
European evergreen shrub/tree with edible red berries

Examples

The Arbutus unedo adds a splash of color to gardens with its distinctive scarlet berries.
Although the fruits of Arbutus unedo are edible, they are not particularly sweet or flavorful.
Botanists in southern Europe often study the Arbutus unedo for its unique ecological adaptations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strawberry tree - Common name for Arbutus unedo

Etymology

From Latin 'arbutus' (strawberry tree) and 'unedo' ('I eat one', alluding to the fruit's puny taste).
