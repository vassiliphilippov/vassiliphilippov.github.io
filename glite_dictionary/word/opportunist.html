opportunist

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːpərˈtunɪst / (AmE), / ˌɒpəˈtjuːnɪst / (BrE)
a person who seeks to gain personal advantage by taking immediate opportunities, often without regard for principles or ethics
person who exploits opportunities for gain

Examples

The opportunist took credit for the team's work whenever he could.
As an opportunist, she changed her stance whenever it suited her advancement.
Some consider him an opportunist rather than a true leader.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opportunistic - taking advantage of opportunities as they arise, often unethically

Etymology

From French 'opportuniste', from 'opportun' (opportune), ultimately from Latin 'opportunus'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɑːpərˈtuːnɪst / (AmE), / ˌɒpəˈtjuːnɪst / (BrE)
Taking immediate advantage of any opportunity, often without considering principles or ethics.
taking advantage of opportunities, often unethically

Examples

The opportunist approach of the company led to several questionable business decisions.
In politics, an opportunist strategy often attracts criticism for disregarding ethics.
Some viewed his opportunist behavior as shrewd, while others saw it as dishonest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opportunistic - using opportunities to gain personal advantage, often at the expense of ethics
  • Antonym: principled - acting according to moral values or principles

Etymology

From Latin 'opportunus' (favorable, convenient).
