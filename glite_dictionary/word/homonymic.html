homonymic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhɑː.məˈnɪm.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɒ.məˈnɪm.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving homonyms; having the nature of a homonym.
relating to homonyms

Examples

The two words 'right' and 'write' are homonymic because they sound alike but have different meanings.
Linguists often study homonymic relationships to understand the complexity of language.
Misunderstandings in communication can arise from homonymic ambiguity in speech or writing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homonymous - relating to or having the nature of a homonym

Etymology

From French homonymique, from Greek homōnymos (having the same name).
