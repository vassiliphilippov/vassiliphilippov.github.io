six

1. NUM

/ sɪks / (AmE), / sɪks / (BrE)
the cardinal number equal to five plus one; 6.
the number 6

Examples

He has six sisters and two brothers.
There are six apples on the table.
The movie starts at six o'clock.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: seven - the cardinal number after six; 7
  • Degree Lower: five - the cardinal number before six; 5

Etymology

From Old English siex, from Proto-Germanic *seks, from Proto-Indo-European *seks.
