prerequisite

1. ADJ

/ priˈrɛkwəzɪt / (AmE), / prɪˈrɛkwɪzɪt / (BrE)
Required as a prior condition for something else to happen or be done.
required as a prior condition

Examples

Completion of the introductory course is a prerequisite for enrollment in advanced classes.
A valid driver's license is prerequisite to renting a car in most countries.
Certification in basic first aid is often prerequisite for working as a camp counselor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mandatory - required by law or rules; compulsory
  • Antonym: optional - not required; left to choice

Etymology

From Medieval Latin praerequisitus, from prae- (before) + requisitus (required).

2. NOUN

/ priˈrɛkwɪˌzɪt / (AmE), / priˈrɛkwɪzɪt / (BrE)
a thing that is required as a prior condition for something else to happen or exist
something required in advance

Examples

Completion of Biology 101 is a prerequisite for enrolling in the advanced course.
A valid passport is a prerequisite for international travel.
Strong computer skills have become a prerequisite for many office jobs.

Synset Examples

Latin was a prerequisite for admission

Related Terms

  • Synonym: requirement - something that is needed or demanded
  • Antonym: obstacle - something that blocks progress or is required to be overcome

Etymology

From Latin 'praerequisitus', past participle of 'praerequirere' (to require beforehand).

Synset POS: n
