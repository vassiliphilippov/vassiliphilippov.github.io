to disincarnate

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪsɪnˈkɑrneɪt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈkɑːneɪt / (BrE)
to make immaterial or remove the real, physical essence of something or someone; to free from bodily form.
make immaterial; remove physical essence

Examples

Some mystics claim they can disincarnate their consciousness and travel beyond the physical world.
The poet attempted to disincarnate his ideas, expressing them in a purely abstract form.
According to ancient legends, powerful shamans could disincarnate a spirit from its earthly vessel.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incarnate - to give bodily form to; to embody in flesh

Etymology

From Latin prefix dis- (opposite of) + incarnate (made flesh), from caro (flesh).
