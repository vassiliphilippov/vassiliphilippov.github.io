timber-framed

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɪmbɚ freɪmd / (AmE), / ˈtɪmbə freɪmd / (BrE)
(of a building or structure) having a frame made of exposed timbers, typically as part of traditional construction.
having a frame made of exposed timbers

Examples

Many historic houses in England are timber-framed and have stood for centuries.
The museum displays a beautifully restored timber-framed barn from the seventeenth century.
Some new eco-homes use the timber-framed method to combine tradition with sustainability.

Synset Examples

two timber-framed buildings dating to the 3rd - 5th century
