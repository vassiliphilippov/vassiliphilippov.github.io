Pt

1. NOUN

/ pi ti / (AmE), / piː tiː / (BrE)
the chemical symbol for platinum, a heavy, precious, silver-white metallic element resistant to corrosion
chemical symbol for platinum

Examples

The scientist measured the amount of Pt present in the alloy.
Pure Pt is often used in laboratory equipment due to its inertness.
Jewelry marked with Pt indicates it is made of platinum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: platinum - a heavy, precious, grey-white metallic element

Etymology

Abbreviation of New Latin platinum, from Spanish platina ("little silver"). Symbol from the Latin name.
