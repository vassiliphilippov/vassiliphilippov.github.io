yardstick

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɑrdˌstɪk / (AmE), / ˈjɑːdˌstɪk / (BrE)
A measuring stick or tape, usually three feet (one yard) long, used for measuring length.
three-foot measuring stick

Examples

The carpenter measured the board with a yardstick before cutting it.
Our teacher asked us to bring a yardstick for the math project.
He keeps a yardstick in his workshop to measure fabric accurately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meter stick - a measuring stick one meter long, used for measuring length

Etymology

Formed from 'yard' (unit of length) + 'stick' (rod).

2. NOUN

/ ˈjɑːrdˌstɪk / (AmE), / ˈjɑːdˌstɪk / (BrE)
a standard or criterion by which something is judged or measured
a standard for comparison

Examples

Profits alone should not be the only yardstick for evaluating a company's success.
Educational achievement is often used as a yardstick for intelligence, though this is debatable.
The committee lacked a clear yardstick to judge the effectiveness of the new policy.

Synset Examples

on what kind of yardstick is he basing his judgment?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benchmark - a standard or point of reference for comparison
  • Synonym: standard - something serving as a measure, norm, or model

Etymology

Originally from the literal 'yardstick,' a stick a yard long used for measuring; 'yard' from Old English 'gerd' (rod, stick), 'stick' from Old English 'sticca'.
