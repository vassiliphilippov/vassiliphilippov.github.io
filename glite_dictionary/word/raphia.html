raphia

1. NOUN

/ ˈræfiə / (AmE), / ˈræfiə / (BrE)
the strong, flexible fiber obtained from the leaves of the raffia palm, used for making baskets, mats, and other woven goods
fiber from the raffia palm used in weaving

Examples

Artisans use raphia to weave beautiful baskets for sale at the local market.
The children made hats from dyed raphia during the craft workshop.
Traditional floor mats in the region are often crafted using raphia fibers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raffia - fiber from the raffia palm used in weaving and crafts

Etymology

From New Latin Raphia, the genus name, from Greek rhaphis, rhaphidos (needle; referring to the shape of the leaves).
