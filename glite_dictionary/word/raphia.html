Raphia

1. NOUN

/ ˈræfiə / (AmE), / ˈræfiə / (BrE)
a genus of feather palms found in tropical Africa, Madagascar, and Central and South America, cultivated commercially for their strong leaf fibers
a tropical feather palm genus producing raffia fiber

Examples

The Raphia palm is highly valued for the long, strong fibers it produces.
In some regions, products made from Raphia leaves are a major export.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Raphia palms in different climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raffia - the fiber obtained from Raphia palm leaves

Etymology

From New Latin Raphia, the genus name, from Greek rhaphis (needle), referring to the needle-like leaf segments.

2. NOUN

/ ˈræfiə / (AmE), / ˈreɪfiə / (BrE)
a genus of feather palms native to tropical Africa, Madagascar, and Central and South America, widely cultivated for commercial purposes
genus of feather palms

Examples

The Raphia genus is valued for its long, strong fibers used in making mats and baskets.
Many species within Raphia are cultivated for palm wine and building materials in Africa.
Researchers studied the ecological importance of Raphia palms in tropical wetland habitats.

Etymology

From New Latin Raphia, from Greek 'raphis' (needle), referring to the shape of the palm's leaflets.
