Gregory the Great

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡrɛɡəri ði ɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡrɛɡəri ðə ˈɡreɪt / (BrE)
(c. 540–604) An Italian pope recognized as a saint and Doctor of the Church, noted for his spiritual and administrative leadership in the early Roman Catholic Church.
pope and saint, early Church leader

Examples

Gregory the Great implemented important reforms in the early Church that influenced Christianity for centuries.
Many historians credit Gregory the Great with the development of Gregorian chant.
The writings of Gregory the Great are still studied by theologians today.
