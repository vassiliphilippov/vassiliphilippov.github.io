twit

1. NOUN

/ twɪt / (AmE), / twɪt / (BrE)
a silly or annoying person; someone regarded with mild contempt or derision
a foolish or annoying person

Examples

Stop being such a twit and pay attention to what you're doing.
He made a complete twit of himself at the meeting by forgetting everyone's name.
Only a real twit would wear sandals in the snow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool - a person lacking good sense or judgement
  • Synonym: idiot - a very foolish or stupid person
  • Antonym: genius - a person of great intelligence

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *twiten* (to reproach, blame).

2. VERB

/ twɪt / (AmE), / twɪt / (BrE)
to harass or reproach someone with persistent criticism, teasing, or carping remarks
to harass with persistent criticism

Examples

He would twit his colleagues about their mistakes in front of everyone.
She was twitted endlessly for her unusual accent at school.
Rather than offering help, he just kept twitting his friend about the error.

Synset Examples

The children teased the new teacher
Don't ride me so hard over my failure
His fellow workers razzed him when he wore a jacket and tie

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tease - to make fun of or provoke in a playful or unkind way
  • Synonym: razz - to make fun of, mock or tease
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

From Middle English *twiten* (to reproach), from Old English *twītan* (to reproach, blame).
