overflow

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvɚˈfloʊ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈfləʊ / (BrE)
(of a container or space) to flow or run over the rim, edge, or limit, typically because it is too full.
to flow or run over the edge or limit

Examples

If the river keeps rising, it will overflow its banks and flood the nearby fields.
The sink began to overflow because someone left the tap running.
Crowds would often overflow from the stadium into the surrounding streets after a big match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spill - to cause or allow liquid to flow over the edge of its container
  • Synonym: flood - to cover or submerge with water

Etymology

From Old English 'oferflōwan' (to flow over).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02076087-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvɚˌfloʊ / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌfləʊ / (BrE)
the occurrence of liquid spilling or flowing beyond the limits of a container or place due to excess volume
liquid spilling over due to excess

Examples

The heavy rain caused an overflow in the river, flooding nearby fields.
Please check the tank's overflow to make sure it isn't blocked.
A sudden overflow of water soaked the basement after the pipe burst.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spill - the act of liquid accidentally flowing out of a container

Etymology

From Old English 'oferflōwan', from 'ofer' (over) + 'flōwan' (to flow).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07421872-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvɚˌfloʊ / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌfləʊ / (BrE)
a large or excessive flow of liquid, especially beyond the limits or capacity of a container or place
an excessive or large flow

Examples

The heavy rain caused an overflow from the river onto the streets.
The dam's overflow threatened to flood the nearby village.
The sink couldn't handle the sudden overflow of water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flood - an overflow of a large amount of water beyond its normal limits

Etymology

From Old English oferlēogan (to overflow), from ofer (over) + flōwan (to flow).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00330645-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˌoʊ.vɚˈfloʊ / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.vəˈfləʊ / (BrE)
(of a person or their feelings) to be so full of a particular quality or emotion that it is obvious to others.
be extremely full of emotion or quality

Examples

Her heart seemed to overflow with gratitude after hearing the news.
The room overflowed with excitement as the band took the stage.
He is always overflowing with new ideas and enthusiasm.

Synset Examples

The children bubbled over with joy
My boss was bubbling over with anger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burst - to be filled with emotion so that it is very obvious
  • Synonym: brim - to be full to the point of spilling over, often with emotion
Source: WordNet
