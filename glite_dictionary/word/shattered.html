shattered

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃædɚd / (AmE), / ˈʃætəd / (BrE)
completely ruined or violently disrupted; deeply damaged or destroyed, especially in a non-physical or emotional sense
completely ruined; violently disrupted

Examples

The news of the accident left the whole family shattered and unable to speak.
After years of hard work, her shattered hopes were difficult to bear.
The team's shattered morale was evident after their unexpected defeat.

Synset Examples

our shattered dreams of peace and prosperity
a tattered remnant of its former strength
my torn and tattered past

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broken - damaged or no longer whole; destroyed in spirit or function
  • Antonym: intact - remaining whole or untouched; not damaged

Etymology

Past participle of 'shatter', from Middle English 'schateren', of imitative origin.
