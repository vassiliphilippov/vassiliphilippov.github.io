Orchis spectabilis

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrkɪs spɛkˈteɪbɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˈɔːkɪs spɛkˈteɪbɪlɪs / (BrE)
A North American orchid, also called the showy orchid, with spikes of violet-purple and white flowers; sepals and petals form a hood.
North American orchid species

Examples

The Orchis spectabilis blooms in early spring in shaded woodlands across eastern North America.
Botanists admire the vibrant colors of Orchis spectabilis during their field studies.
The hood formed by the sepals and petals is a distinguishing feature of the Orchis spectabilis.

Etymology

From Latin: 'orchis' (orchid) and 'spectabilis' (notable, showy).
