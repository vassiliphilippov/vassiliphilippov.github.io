sip

1. NOUN

/ sɪp / (AmE), / sɪp / (BrE)
a small amount of liquid taken into the mouth and swallowed
a small drink

Examples

He took a cautious sip of the hot coffee before speaking.
After a sip of water, she continued her run.
Would you like to have a sip of my juice?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gulp - a large and hurried swallow of liquid

Etymology

Probably from Middle Dutch sippen (to sip).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00845155-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ sɪp / (AmE), / sɪp / (BrE)
to drink (a liquid) in small quantities with small mouthfuls, usually slowly
drink in small mouthfuls

Examples

She sipped her coffee slowly, enjoying the quiet morning.
He likes to sip his favorite wine while reading a book.
The child was sipping juice through a straw at the picnic.

Synset Examples

She was sipping her tea

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gulp - to swallow or drink something quickly and in large mouthfuls

Etymology

Perhaps imitative of the sound of sipping, or related to Middle English 'sipen' (to soak).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01172967-v
Synset POS: v
