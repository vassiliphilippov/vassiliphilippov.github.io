inclusion

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkluʒən / (AmE), / ɪnˈkluːʒən / (BrE)
the act of including someone or something as part of a group or whole
the act of including

Examples

Everyone appreciated the team's commitment to inclusion and respect for diversity.
The inclusion of new features in the update made the app more useful.
Her sense of inclusion made all the newcomers feel welcome.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exclusion - the act of not including

Etymology

From Latin inclusio (a shutting in), from includere (to shut in, include).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkluʒən / (AmE), / ɪnˈkluːʒən / (BrE)
a small body or particle present inside a cell, often observed in certain diseases or under abnormal conditions
a small body within a cell
medical

Examples

The pathologist observed a large inclusion in the infected liver cell.
Certain viral infections cause the formation of unusual inclusions within cell nuclei.
One hallmark of the disease is the presence of cytoplasmic inclusions seen under the microscope.

Synset Examples

an inclusion in the cytoplasm of the cell

Etymology

From Latin inclusio (enclosing, shutting in), from includere (to shut in).

Our society is moving toward greater inclusion of people with disabilities.
