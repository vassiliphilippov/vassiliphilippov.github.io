neuromarketing

1. NOUN

/ ˌnʊroʊˈmɑrkətɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəʊˈmɑːkɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
The study and application of marketing techniques based on neuroscientific insights into consumers' sensorimotor, cognitive, and emotional responses.
marketing based on neuroscientific consumer insights
medical

Examples

Many major brands are investing in neuromarketing to better understand consumer preferences.
The neuromarketing study revealed surprising reactions to the new product packaging.
Advancements in neuromarketing have changed the way companies design their advertisements.

Etymology

Formed from 'neuro-' (relating to nerves or the nervous system, from Greek 'neuron') and 'marketing'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌnuːroʊˈmɑrkətɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəʊˈmɑːkɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
A field of marketing that uses insights from neuroscience to study how consumers' brains respond to marketing stimuli, with the aim of improving advertising and product strategies.
marketing using neuroscience insights

Examples

Many companies are investing in neuromarketing to better understand their customers' subconscious reactions to advertisements.
Some critics argue that neuromarketing raises ethical questions about consumer manipulation.
The conference featured a workshop on how neuromarketing can enhance brand loyalty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marketing - the activity of promoting and selling products or services

Etymology

Formed from 'neuro-' (relating to nerves or the nervous system) and 'marketing'.
