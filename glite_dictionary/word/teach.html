to teach

1. VERB

/ titʃ / (AmE), / tiːtʃ / (BrE)
to accustom someone gradually to a particular action, habit, or attitude
accustom someone to an action or attitude

Examples

Parents often teach their children to say 'please' and 'thank you' from a young age.
It's important to teach pets not to jump on visitors.
She patiently teaches her students to take responsibility for their work.

Synset Examples

The child is taught to obey her parents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: train - to teach someone the skills for a particular task or job
  • Synonym: condition - to accustom someone gradually to a pattern of behavior, often by repeated reinforcement

Etymology

Old English *tǣcan* (to show, instruct), from Proto-Germanic *taikijaną*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00274533-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ titʃ / (AmE), / tiːtʃ / (BrE)
to give instruction to someone; to impart skills or knowledge to another person
impart knowledge or skills

Examples

She loves to teach young children how to read and write.
My grandfather used to teach me how to fish every summer.
Many volunteers teach English as a second language around the world.

Synset Examples

I taught them French
He instructed me in building a boat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to instruct - to teach or train in a formal way
  • Antonym: to learn - to acquire knowledge or skill (as the receiver)

Etymology

Old English *tæcan* (to show, declare, instruct).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00830768-v
Synset POS: v
