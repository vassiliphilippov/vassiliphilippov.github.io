fez

1. NOUN

/ fɛz / (AmE), / fɛz / (BrE)
A brimless, usually red felt cap in the shape of a truncated cone, often with a tassel, traditionally worn by men in some Middle Eastern and North African cultures.
brimless felt cap with tassel

Examples

He wore a bright red fez during the ceremony, making him easy to spot.
Many tourists buy a fez as a souvenir when visiting Morocco.
The traditional costume included a silk robe and a fez with a long black tassel.

Etymology

From Turkish 'fes', from Arabic 'fās', named after the city of Fes in Morocco.
