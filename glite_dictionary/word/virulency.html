virulency

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪr.jə.lən.si / (AmE), / ˈvɪr.jʊ.lən.si / (BrE)
the degree of harmfulness or severity, especially in relation to a microorganism's ability to cause disease
capacity to cause harm or disease
medical

Examples

The virulency of some strains of influenza makes them particularly dangerous during outbreaks.
Research is ongoing to understand how bacteria develop increased virulency over time.
Doctors monitored the virulency of the infection to determine the appropriate treatment.

Synset Examples

the virulence of the plague

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virulence - the quality of being extremely harmful or deadly, especially in pathogens

Etymology

From Latin 'virulentia' (full of poison).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɪr.ə.lən.si / (AmE), / ˈvɪr.ʊ.lən.si / (BrE)
the quality of being extremely hostile, harmful, or bitter
extreme hostility or harmfulness
archaic

Examples

The virulency of her remarks left everyone in the room stunned and uncomfortable.
Historians noted the virulency of the pamphlet's attack on the king.
He was shocked by the virulency of the debate, which quickly turned personal.

Synset Examples

the virulence of the malicious old man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virulence - the quality of being extremely severe or harmful

Etymology

From Latin *virulentia* (poisonous quality), relating to 'virulence.'
