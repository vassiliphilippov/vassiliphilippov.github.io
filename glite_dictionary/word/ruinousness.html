ruinousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈruː.ə.nəs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈruː.ɪ.nəs.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being likely to cause great damage or destruction.
quality of being ruinous

Examples

The ruinousness of the war left the country in economic hardship for decades.
He underestimated the ruinousness of taking out so many loans at once.
Environmentalists warned about the ruinousness of unchecked industrial expansion.

Synset Examples

The masses had not as yet formed such an idea of the ruinousness of the policy of the administration as to seriously threaten the power of the Republicans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destructiveness - the quality of causing destruction
  • Antonym: beneficiality - the quality of being beneficial

Etymology

From 'ruinous' + '-ness'; 'ruinous' from Latin 'ruinosus', from 'ruina' (ruin).
