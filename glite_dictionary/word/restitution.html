restitution

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛstəˈtuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛstɪˈtjuːʃən / (BrE)
the act of returning something to its original condition or restoring it to its former state
restoring to original state

Examples

After the fire, the museum focused on the restitution of damaged artwork.
The court ordered restitution of the park to its original, undeveloped state.
Efforts at environmental restitution are vital after an oil spill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restoration - the act of bringing something back to its former condition

Etymology

From Latin restitutio (restoration), from restituere (to restore).

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛstəˈtuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛstɪˈtjuːʃən / (BrE)
the act of paying money or giving back something lost or stolen to compensate for loss or injury
compensation for loss or injury

Examples

The judge ordered the defendant to pay restitution to the victims of the fraud.
After the fire, the company was required to make restitution for the damages caused.
Part of his sentence included community service and financial restitution to the affected families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compensation - payment given to make up for loss or injury
  • Synonym: damages - money awarded by court for loss or injury

Etymology

From Latin restitutio (a restoring), from restituere (to restore).

3. NOUN

/ ˌrɛstəˈtuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛstɪˈtjuːʃən / (BrE)
the act of returning something that was lost or stolen to its rightful owner
returning something to its owner

Examples

The museum celebrated the restitution of the stolen paintings to their original owners.
After a lengthy legal battle, restitution was finally made to the victims of the scam.
The judge ordered the restitution of all property taken during the burglary.

Synset Examples

upon the restitution of the book to its rightful owner the child was given a tongue lashing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restoration - the act of bringing something back to a former condition
  • Antonym: confiscation - the action of taking or seizing someone's property with authority

Etymology

From Latin 'restitutio', from 'restituere' meaning 'restore, return'.
