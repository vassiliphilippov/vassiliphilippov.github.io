remiss

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈmɪs / (AmE), / rɪˈmɪs / (BrE)
not giving enough care or attention to a duty or responsibility; negligent or careless in performing a task.
neglectful of duty or responsibility

Examples

It would be remiss of us not to thank everyone who contributed to the project.
The manager was criticized for being remiss in handling customer complaints.
She admitted she had been remiss in her duties as a team leader.

Synset Examples

derelict (or delinquent) in his duty
neglectful of his duties
remiss of you not to pay your bills

Related Terms

  • Synonym: negligent - failing to take proper care over something; neglectful
  • Antonym: diligent - showing care and effort in one's work or duties

Etymology

From Latin remissus (slack, negligent), past participle of remittere (to remit).
