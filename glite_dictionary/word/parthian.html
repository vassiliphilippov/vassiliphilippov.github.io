Parthian

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrθiən / (AmE), / ˈpɑːθiən / (BrE)
an extinct Iranian language spoken in the Parthian kingdom from about 250 BC to AD 226
an extinct Iranian language of the Parthian kingdom

Examples

Scholars have preserved many ancient Parthian texts on clay tablets.
The Parthian language influenced the vocabulary of modern Persian.
Few people today can read or understand Parthian.

Etymology

From Parthia, an ancient region in north-eastern Iran, and the Latin 'Parthianus.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrθiən / (AmE), / ˈpɑːθiən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Parthia, an ancient region located in northeastern Iran
native or inhabitant of ancient Parthia

Examples

The ancient Parthian archers were famous for their skill in battle.
Archaeologists discovered artifacts belonging to a noble Parthian family.
Many Roman historians wrote about the customs of the Parthian people.

Etymology

From Latin Parthius, from Greek Parthos, ultimately of Old Persian origin.
