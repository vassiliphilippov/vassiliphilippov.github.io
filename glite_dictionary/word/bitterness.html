bitterness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪtərnəs / (AmE), / ˈbɪtənəs / (BrE)
A sharp, pungent taste that is often unpleasant, as found in coffee or quinine.
sharp, pungent taste (coffee, quinine)

Examples

The bitterness of the dark chocolate lingered on her tongue after each bite.
Some people enjoy the bitterness of black coffee, while others prefer it sweetened.
The bitterness in the grapefruit made it less appealing to children.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sweetness - the quality of tasting sweet
  • Antonym: sourness - the quality of tasting sour
  • Synonym: astringency - the taste sensation that causes the mouth to pucker

Etymology

From Old English *biter* (bitter) + *-ness* (noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɪtərnəs / (AmE), / ˈbɪtənəs / (BrE)
A feeling of deep anger and resentment towards someone or something.
deep anger and resentment

Examples

Her bitterness toward her former employer was obvious in every conversation.
Years of injustice fueled his bitterness and mistrust of authority.
He struggled to let go of the bitterness that had built up over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resentment - a feeling of persistent ill will or anger at being treated unfairly
  • Antonym: forgiveness - the act of ceasing to feel resentment or anger against someone

Etymology

From Old English 'biter' (bitter) + '-ness' (state or quality).

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɪtɚnəs / (AmE), / ˈbɪtənəs / (BrE)
The quality or property of having a harsh, unpleasant taste.
harsh, unpleasant taste quality

Examples

The bitterness of the medicine made it hard to swallow.
Some people enjoy the bitterness of dark chocolate in desserts.
The coffee had a strong bitterness that lingered on his tongue.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sweetness - the property of having a pleasant, sugary taste

Etymology

From Old English 'biternes', from 'biter' (bitter) + '-ness'.
