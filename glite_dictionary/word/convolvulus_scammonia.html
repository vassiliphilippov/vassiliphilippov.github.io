Convolvulus scammonia

1. NOUN

/ kənˌvɑːlvjʊləs skəˈmoʊniə / (AmE), / kənˌvɒlvjʊləs skəˈməʊnɪə / (BrE)
A twining plant native to Asia Minor, with cream-colored to purple flowers and thick roots that yield a resin used as a cathartic.
plant yielding cathartic resin
medical

Examples

Convolvulus scammonia has been traditionally used in medicine for its strong laxative properties.
The thick roots of Convolvulus scammonia are harvested to extract scammony resin.
Gardeners rarely cultivate Convolvulus scammonia due to its potent medicinal effects.

Etymology

From Latin 'convolvulus', meaning 'to entwine', and late Latin 'scammonia', from Greek 'skammonia', the plant.
