Gastromycetes

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡæstroʊmaɪˈsiːtiːz / (AmE), / ˌɡæstrəʊmɪˈsiːtiːz / (BrE)
A former classification of fungi in which the spore-producing layer is enclosed until the spores mature, including puffballs, earth stars, and stinkhorn fungi.
group of fungi with enclosed spore layer

Examples

Members of the Gastromycetes are known for producing puffball-like fruiting bodies.
Mycology students studied the unique reproductive methods of the Gastromycetes.
Earth stars belong to the Gastromycetes, a group distinguished by their enclosed hymenium.

Etymology

From Greek 'gastro-' (stomach) + 'mycetes' (fungi), referring to the internal development of spores.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɡæstroʊmaɪˈsiːtiːz / (AmE), / ˌɡastrəʊmʌɪˈsiːtiːz / (BrE)
a former class of fungi in which the spore-bearing tissue (hymenium) is enclosed until the spores have matured, including puffballs, earth stars, and stinkhorns
a former class of fungi (e.g. puffballs, earth stars)
medical

Examples

The Gastromycetes are known for species like puffballs and stinkhorn fungi.
Researchers found several unique structures among the Gastromycetes collected from the forest.
Many Gastromycetes release their spores only when the fruiting body ruptures.

Etymology

From Greek *gaster* (stomach) + *mykēs* (fungus).
