Passiflora

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæsəˈflɔrə / (AmE), / ˌpasɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the family Passifloraceae, commonly known as passionflowers or passion vines
genus of flowering plants, passionflowers

Examples

The Passiflora genus includes over 500 species of passionflowers found mainly in tropical regions.
Many species of Passiflora are cultivated for their exotic flowers and edible fruit.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Passiflora in treating anxiety and insomnia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passionflower - a common name for plants of the genus Passiflora

Etymology

From Latin 'passio' (passion) + 'flos' (flower), referring to the passionflower.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpæsɪˈflɔrə / (AmE), / ˌpasɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants known as passionflowers or passion vines, native chiefly to tropical and subtropical regions.
genus of passionflowers

Examples

Many exotic fruits come from the Passiflora genus, including the passion fruit.
Several species in the Passiflora genus are cultivated for their striking flowers.
The botanist specialized in studying members of the Passiflora genus found in South America.

Etymology

From Latin 'passio' (passion) + 'flora' (flower), referring to Christian symbolism attributed to the flower's parts.
