decurved

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈkɜrvd / (AmE), / dɪˈkɜːvd / (BrE)
bent or curved downward, especially referring to a beak, bill, or other structure.
bent or curved downward

Examples

The curlew is known for its long, decurved bill.
He studied the decurved petals of the rare tropical flower.
The bird’s decurved beak allows it to forage for food in the mud.

Synset Examples

the decurved bill of a curlew

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curved - having a smoothly bending shape without angles
  • Antonym: incurved - curved inward

Etymology

From Latin 'de-' (down) + 'curvus' (curved).
