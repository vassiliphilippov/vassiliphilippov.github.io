Brachychiton populneus

1. NOUN

/ brəˌkaɪkɪˌtɑn pɑːˈpʌlniəs / (AmE), / brəˌkɪkɪˌtɒn pɒˈpʌlniːəs / (BrE)
a tree native to eastern Australia, known for its tough fiber, soft light wood, and use as emergency cattle fodder
Australian tree with tough fiber and soft wood

Examples

The Brachychiton populneus provides emergency fodder for cattle in drought-prone parts of Australia.
Farmers often harvest the fiber from Brachychiton populneus to make ropes and twine.
The wood of Brachychiton populneus is prized for its attractive grain and lightweight properties.

Etymology

From Greek 'brachys' (short) + 'chiton' (tunic), referring to the short tunic-like covering of the seed; 'populneus' referring to its poplar-like leaves.
