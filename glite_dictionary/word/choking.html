choking

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃoʊkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃəʊkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of suffocating someone by constricting the windpipe
suffocation by constricting the windpipe

Examples

There was no evidence that the choking had been accidental.
The defendant denied any involvement in the choking of the victim.
Emergency responders arrived quickly after the reported choking incident.

Synset Examples

no evidence that the choking was done by the accused

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strangulation - the act of killing or injuring by squeezing the throat

2. ADJ

/ ˈtʃoʊkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃəʊkɪŋ / (BrE)
(of air or an environment) making breathing difficult, especially because of a lack of fresh air or the presence of heat, dust, or smoke.
making breathing difficult (of air or environment)

Examples

The choking heat in the crowded subway left everyone drenched in sweat.
A thick, choking haze filled the valley after the wildfire.
She gasped for breath in the choking atmosphere of the basement.

Synset Examples

the choking June dust
the smothering soft voices
smothering heat
the room was suffocating — hot and airless

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suffocating - making it difficult to breathe due to lack of fresh air
  • Synonym: stifling - oppressively hot and close, making breathing difficult
Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ˈtʃoʊkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃəʊkɪŋ / (BrE)
a condition in which the airway to the lungs is blocked, often by food or swelling, making it difficult or impossible to breathe.
airway blockage; difficulty breathing

Examples

He started choking when a piece of steak got stuck in his throat.
Immediate action is necessary in cases of choking to restore normal breathing.
The teacher demonstrated how to help someone who is experiencing choking during lunch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suffocation - when breathing stops due to lack of air
  • Synonym: asphyxiation - a condition caused by lack of oxygen due to blocked breathing
  • Antonym: breathing - the process of taking air into and expelling it from the lungs
