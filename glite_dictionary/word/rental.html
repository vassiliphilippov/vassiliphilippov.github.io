rental

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛntəl / (AmE), / ˈrɛntəl / (BrE)
relating to or available for rent; involving the payment of rent
relating to rent or renting

Examples

We just signed a rental agreement for the new apartment.
The rental price for this car is quite affordable.
Many families rely on rental housing in large cities.

Synset Examples

rental agreement
rental charges

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lease - a contract granting use or occupation of property during a specified period in exchange for a specified rent

Etymology

From rent (noun) + -al (suffix forming adjectives and nouns).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02903285-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɛn.təl / (AmE), / ˈrɛnt.əl / (BrE)
(of a product, especially property or a vehicle) available to be hired or leased for a period of time.
available to hire or lease

Examples

We reserved a rental car for our trip across the country.
A rental property can provide extra income to homeowners.
He needed a rental bike because he was visiting only for the weekend.

Synset Examples

a rental car

Etymology

From the noun 'rent' + suffix '-al'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02903416-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ ˈrɛntəl / (AmE), / ˈrɛntəl / (BrE)
a property, such as a house, flat, or car, that is leased or available for renting
property that is leased or rented out

Examples

The family moved into a rental while their new house was being built.
All the rentals on the beach were booked for the summer holidays.
She decided to invest in a rental property as a source of income.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lease - a contract granting use of property for a specified time
  • Antonym: ownership - the state of owning something instead of leasing

Etymology

From rent + -al, shaped by Late Latin and French influences.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13269292-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈrɛn.təl / (AmE), / ˈrɛn.təl / (BrE)
the act of paying for the temporary use of something, such as property, a vehicle, or equipment.
the act of paying for use of something

Examples

The rental of the apartment includes utilities and internet access.
She paid a weekly rental fee for the car while on vacation.
Bike rental is available near the train station for commuters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lease - a contract granting use or occupation of property for a specified period in exchange for rent
  • Antonym: purchase - the act of buying something and gaining ownership

Etymology

From rent (to pay for use) + -al (forming nouns).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01113280-n
Synset POS: n
