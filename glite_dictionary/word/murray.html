Murray

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɜri / (AmE), / ˈmʌri / (BrE)
A major river in southeastern Australia, flowing west and then south to the Indian Ocean near Adelaide.
river in southeastern Australia

Examples

The Murray is the longest river in Australia, stretching over 2,500 kilometers.
Farmers rely on water from the Murray for irrigation during dry seasons.
Many species of fish can be found along the Murray and its tributaries.

Etymology

Named after Sir George Murray (1772–1846), a British politician and soldier.
