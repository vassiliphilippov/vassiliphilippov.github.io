palilalia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæləˈleɪliə / (AmE), / ˌpalɪˈleɪlɪə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which a person involuntarily and rapidly repeats words or phrases, especially their own speech.
pathological repetition of words
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with palilalia after observing his repetitive speech patterns.
Palilalia is commonly seen in some neurological disorders, including Tourette syndrome.
Her therapist explained that palilalia can be challenging to manage, but there are strategies to help.

Etymology

From Greek 'palin' (again) + 'lalia' (speech, talk).
