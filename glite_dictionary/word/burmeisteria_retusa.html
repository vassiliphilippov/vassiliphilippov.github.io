Burmeisteria retusa

1. PROPN

/ bɝːˌmaɪˈstɪəriə rɪˈtjuːsə / (AmE), / bɜːˌmaɪˈstɪəriə rɪˈtjuːsə / (BrE)
A species of trilobite formerly found in southern South America, known from the fossil record.
species of trilobite (fossil) from South America

Examples

The fossilized remains of Burmeisteria retusa have been studied to understand Devonian trilobite diversity.
Scientists discovered several Burmeisteria retusa specimens in southern Argentina's shale deposits.
The unique features of Burmeisteria retusa make it valuable for paleontological research.

Etymology

Genus named after the German zoologist Hermann Burmeister; 'retusa' from Latin meaning 'blunt' or 'notched'.
