hatchel

1. NOUN

/ ˈhætʃəl / (AmE), / ˈhætʃəl / (BrE)
a tool with a comb-like structure used for separating flax fibers by drawing them through its teeth
comb for separating flax fibers

Examples

The worker used a hatchel to prepare the flax fibers for spinning.
Vintage textile museums often display a wooden hatchel among their exhibits.
Before spinning, the flax was carefully pulled through the hatchel to align the fibers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hackle - a comb for flax or hemp; also the act of separating fibers with such a comb

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *hekel*, meaning a (flax-) comb.

2. VERB

/ ˈhætʃəl / (AmE), / ˈhætʃəl / (BrE)
to comb flax or hemp with a heckle in order to separate the fibers
comb flax or hemp with a heckle

Examples

The workers carefully hatcheled the flax to remove the coarse fibers.
Before spinning, the hemp is thoroughly hatcheled to separate the long fibers.
Old mills once used to hatchel flax in preparation for weaving linen.

Synset Examples

heckle hemp or flax

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heckle - to comb flax or hemp with a heckle

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *hekele* (a comb for flax) and related to the noun 'hatchel'.
