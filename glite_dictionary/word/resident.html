resident

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛzɪdənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛzɪdənt / (BrE)
living in a particular place; having one's home or dwelling in a specified location.
living in a particular place

Examples

Only resident students are allowed to park in this lot.
The country provides healthcare for all resident children.
He has been a resident expert in the field for many years.

Synset Examples

resident aliens

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonresident - not living in a particular place
  • Synonym: inhabitant - someone who lives in a particular place

Etymology

From Latin 'resident-', present participle of 'residere' (to remain, stay).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02132790-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɛzɪdənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛzɪdənt / (BrE)
(of animals) not migrating; remaining in a particular area throughout the year.
animals that do not migrate

Examples

Many birds in the region are resident and can be seen year-round.
Some butterflies are resident species while others migrate long distances each season.
The resident fish populations remained stable throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: migratory - moving from one place to another at different times of the year (of animals)

Etymology

From Latin resident-, from residere (to remain, to stay).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02136516-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ ˈrɛzɪdənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛzɪdənt / (BrE)
a physician in training who lives at and works in a hospital, caring for patients under the supervision of senior staff
physician in hospital-based training
medical

Examples

After graduating from medical school, she became a resident at a large urban hospital.
The resident was called in the middle of the night to assess a new patient.
He completed his training as a surgical resident before moving on to a fellowship.

Synset Examples

the resident was receiving special clinical training at the hospital

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intern - a student or recent graduate undergoing supervised practical training, especially in medicine

Etymology

From Latin resident-, 'remaining, dwelling', from residere 'to remain'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10208897-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈrɛzɪdənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛzɪdənt / (BrE)
a person who lives in a particular place for a long time or permanently, especially as distinguished from a visitor or transient
person who lives in a particular place

Examples

The city has over a million residents who come from many different backgrounds.
Only residents with valid parking permits can leave their cars on this street overnight.
As a longtime resident of the area, she knows all the local shops and restaurants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inhabitant - a person or animal that lives in or occupies a place
  • Antonym: visitor - a person who visits a place but does not live there

Etymology

From Latin 'resident-', present participle of 'residere' meaning 'to remain, stay'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10543112-n
Synset POS: n
