Benedict of Nursia

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɛnədɪkt əv ˈnɜrsiə / (AmE), / ˈbɛnədɪkt əv ˈnɜːsiə / (BrE)
(c. 480–547) An Italian monk regarded as the founder of Western monasticism, who established the Benedictine order around 540.
Italian monk, founder of Benedictine order

Examples

Many consider Benedict of Nursia the father of Western monastic life.
The Rule of Benedict of Nursia continues to influence Christian monasticism today.
Historians study how Benedict of Nursia shaped religious practices in the early Middle Ages.
