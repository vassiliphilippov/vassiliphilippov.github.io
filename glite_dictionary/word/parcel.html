parcel

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrsəl / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsəl / (BrE)
a package or collection of items wrapped or boxed together, especially for transport or delivery
a package of wrapped items

Examples

She sent a parcel to her parents for their anniversary.
The parcel arrived earlier than expected and was neatly wrapped.
He opened the parcel to find several books inside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: package - a wrapped or boxed object for transportation

Etymology

From Old French 'parcela' meaning 'a small part or portion', later extended to 'package'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08024575-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrsəl / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsəl / (BrE)
a piece or section of land, especially one considered as part of an estate or for sale
a piece of land

Examples

The developer purchased a large parcel near the highway for the new shopping center.
They own a parcel of land by the river where they plan to build a cabin.
Each parcel was clearly marked with its boundaries and lot number.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plot - a small marked-out area of land for a specific purpose
  • Synonym: tract - a large area of land

Etymology

From Old French *parcelle* (a small part), from Latin *particella* (a small part).

Source: WordNet

4. VERB

/ ˈpɑrsəl / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsəl / (BrE)
to divide something into separate parts, especially with the intention of distributing them
divide into parts for distribution

Examples

After acquiring the property, they parceled it into five smaller lots for sale.
The estate was parceled among the siblings according to the will.
In preparation for development, the city council decided to parcel the vacant land.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divide - to separate into parts or groups
  • Synonym: to apportion - to divide and allocate proportionally

Etymology

From Old French *parceller* (to divide into portions), from *parcel* (a part, division).

to cover something, especially rope, with strips of canvas, typically as a protective wrapping

6. VERB

/ ˈpɑrsəl / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsəl / (BrE)
to wrap or package something into a container, typically for transport or delivery
wrap or package something

Examples

She parceled the gifts carefully before sending them to her friends overseas.
The factory workers parcel the fragile items to ensure they arrive safely.
He parceled up the old books and donated them to charity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrap - to cover or enclose in paper or soft material
  • Antonym: to unwrap - to remove the wrapping from something

Etymology

From Old French 'parcelle', from Medieval Latin 'particella', diminutive of Latin 'pars' (part, portion).
