palatal

1. ADJ

/ ˈpælətl̩ / (AmE), / ˈpalətəl / (BrE)
relating to or situated near the palate, the roof of the mouth
of or near the palate (roof of mouth)
medical

Examples

The dentist examined the palatal surface of my upper teeth.
A palatal abscess can cause significant discomfort if untreated.
Certain consonants, like 'sh' in 'ship', are classified as palatal sounds in linguistics.

Synset Examples

palatal index
the palatine tonsils

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palatine - of or relating to the palate

Etymology

From Latin 'palatum' (palate) with the suffix '-al' forming adjectives.

2. ADJ

/ ˈpælət̬l / (AmE), / ˈpælətl / (BrE)
(of a speech sound) produced with the front or blade of the tongue near or touching the hard palate, as in the sounds 'y' in 'yes', 'ch' in 'chin', or 'j' in 'gin'.
produced with the tongue near the hard palate

Examples

The English 'y' is an example of a palatal consonant.
Some languages have more palatal sounds than English does.
Linguists classify 'ch' in 'chin' as a palatal affricate.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: velar - sound produced with the back of the tongue near the soft palate

Etymology

From Latin *palatum* (palate) + -al (forming adjectives).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01161001-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˈpælətl / (AmE), / ˈpalətl / (BrE)
A speech sound, such as a semivowel or consonant, articulated with the body of the tongue near or touching the hard palate, as in the initial sound of 'yeast.'
speech sound made near the hard palate

Examples

The English 'y' in 'yeast' is a palatal semivowel.
Some languages have more than one palatal consonant in their phonetic inventory.
The linguist demonstrated how to pronounce a palatal by raising his tongue toward the hard palate.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: velar - a sound produced with the back of the tongue against the soft palate

Etymology

From Latin 'palatum' (palate) + '-al' (adjectival suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07129243-n
Synset POS: n
