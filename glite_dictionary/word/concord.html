Concord

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɑŋ.kərd / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋ.kɔːd / (BrE)
The capital city of the state of New Hampshire, located in the south-central part of the state on the Merrimack River.
capital of New Hampshire, US
american

Examples

The governor gave a speech in Concord addressing the citizens of New Hampshire.
Many historical buildings in Concord attract visitors each year.
She moved from Boston to Concord for a new job opportunity.

Etymology

Named after the English word 'concord,' meaning 'agreement,' reflecting a resolution of territorial disputes.

2. PROPN

/ ˈkɑːŋ.kərd / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋ.kɔːd / (BrE)
The site of the first battle of the American Revolution, fought on April 19, 1775, in Concord, Massachusetts.
site of first American Revolution battle, 1775

Examples

The battle of Concord signaled the start of the American Revolution.
Many tourists visit Concord each year to see the historic battlefield.
The events at Concord inspired generations in the fight for independence.

Etymology

From Latin *concordia* (agreement, harmony); the town was named to symbolize harmony among the settlers.
