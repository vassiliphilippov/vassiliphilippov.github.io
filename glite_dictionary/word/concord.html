concord

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːnˌkɔrd / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.kɔːd / (BrE)
formal agreement or harmony between people or groups, especially in opinion or feeling.
agreement; harmony (esp. of opinions)

Examples

There was a rare sense of concord among the team members after the meeting.
The treaty established lasting concord between the two nations.
Debates often threaten concord, but mutual respect can restore it.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: discord - disagreement or lack of harmony
  • Synonym: agreement - harmony or consensus among people

Etymology

From Latin 'concordia' (agreement, harmony), from 'con-' (together) + 'cor' (heart).

2. VERB

/ ˈkɑːn.kɔrd / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.kɔːd / (BrE)
(formal) To be in agreement or harmony; to go together well.
to be in agreement; to harmonize

Examples

The new law does not concord with existing regulations.
Their interests rarely concord, leading to frequent disagreements.
The two theories concorded surprisingly well during the experiment.

Synset Examples

The colors don't harmonize
Their ideas concorded

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to harmonize - to be in harmony; to go well together
  • Antonym: to disagree - to not agree; to be in conflict or contrast

Etymology

From Latin 'concordare' (to agree), from 'concordia' (agreement).

harmony; agreement among parts

Examples

Their voices blended in such concord that the audience was moved to tears.
In his paintings, the artist seeks a concord of form and emotion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: discord - lack of harmony or agreement
