wager

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪdʒər / (AmE), / ˈweɪdʒə / (BrE)
an amount of money that is risked on the outcome of a game, race, or other uncertain event; a bet
money risked on a bet

Examples

He placed a wager of $50 on the outcome of the match.
Her wager paid off when her horse won the race.
Many casinos limit the maximum wager that players can make on a single hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bet - an act of risking money on the outcome of an event

Etymology

From Middle English wager, probably from Anglo-Norman wageure (a pledge or stake), related to wage (a pledge).

2. NOUN

/ ˈweɪdʒər / (AmE), / ˈweɪdʒə / (BrE)
the act of risking money or something of value on the outcome of an event; a bet
the act of betting; a bet

Examples

He placed a large wager on the outcome of the football match.
She lost her wager at the casino and left empty-handed.
The friends made a friendly wager on who could finish the puzzle first.

Synset Examples

he did it on a bet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bet - an agreement in which people risk money on the outcome of an event

Etymology

From Middle English 'wagen', from Old North French 'wagier' (to pledge, stake).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00507669-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈweɪdʒər / (AmE), / ˈweɪdʒə / (BrE)
to state confidently or assert that something will happen, as though making a bet
assert confidently, as in a bet

Examples

I wager he will be late, as usual.
She wagered that the project would finish ahead of schedule.
You can wager that this new policy will face criticism.

Synset Examples

I bet she will be there!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bet - to risk something, usually money, on the outcome of an event; to assert with confidence

Etymology

From Middle English wageren, from Anglo-Norman wageure (a pledge, a wager).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00920551-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈweɪ.dʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈweɪ.dʒə / (BrE)
to risk money or something valuable on the result of an event or a contest; to bet
to bet money or valuables

Examples

He decided to wager a large sum on his favorite football team.
She often wagers on the outcome of reality TV competitions.
They wagered everything they had and won the jackpot.

Synset Examples

I bet $100 on that new horse
She played all her money on the dark horse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bet - to risk money or valuables on an uncertain outcome

Etymology

From Middle English *wag(i)eren*, from Anglo-Norman *wagerer* (to pledge, to bet).
