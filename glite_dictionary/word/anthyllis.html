Anthyllis

1. NOUN

/ ænˈθɪlɪs / (AmE), / ænˈθɪlɪs / (BrE)
a genus of Mediterranean herbs and shrubs belonging to the pea family, Fabaceae
Mediterranean plant genus (Fabaceae)

Examples

The botanist discovered a rare species of Anthyllis on the rocky Mediterranean coast.
Several gardens feature Anthyllis for its attractive yellow flowers and drought resistance.
Research has shown that Anthyllis species play an important role in local ecosystems.

Etymology

From Latin genus name *Anthyllis*, from Greek *anthýllis* (a kind of plant), from *ánthos* (flower).

2. NOUN

/ ænˈθɪlɪs / (AmE), / ænˈθɪlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of herbs and shrubs native to the Mediterranean region, belonging to the pea family (Fabaceae).
genus of Mediterranean herbs and shrubs

Examples

The species Anthyllis vulneraria is commonly known as kidney vetch.
Several plants in the Anthyllis genus have medicinal properties.
Botanists studied the distribution of Anthyllis in coastal habitats.

Etymology

From Greek *anthos* (flower), referring to the floral nature of the genus.
