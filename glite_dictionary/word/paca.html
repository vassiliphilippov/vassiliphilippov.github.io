paca

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːkə / (AmE), / ˈpækə / (BrE)
a large burrowing rodent native to Central and South America, valued as food and known for its spotted coat
large burrowing rodent of Central and South America

Examples

The local guides pointed out a paca darting into the undergrowth near the riverbank.
Many communities in the Amazon region hunt pacas for their tender meat.
A paca typically excavates complex burrows in forested areas to avoid predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agouti - a rodent related to the paca, native to Central and South America

Etymology

From Portuguese *paca*, from Tupi *paka*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːkə / (AmE), / ˈpækə / (BrE)
a large, burrowing rodent native to South and Central America, prized for its meat and known for its spotted coat
large burrowing rodent of South and Central America

Examples

The paca is often hunted for its tender and flavorful meat.
During our jungle trek, we spotted a paca darting into its burrow.
Local farmers sometimes raise pacas as a sustainable source of protein.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agouti - rodent similar to a paca, found in Central and South America

Etymology

From Spanish 'paca', possibly from Tupi 'paka'.
