Chosen

1. PROPN

/ tʃoʊˈsɛn / (AmE), / tʃəʊˈsɛn / (BrE)
The name used by Japan for Korea when it was a Japanese province from 1910 to 1945.
historical name for Korea under Japanese rule

Examples

During the Japanese occupation, Korea was officially referred to as Chosen.
Historical records from 1910 to 1945 often mention Chosen as the name for Korea.
The governor-general of Chosen implemented colonial policies throughout the peninsula.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Korea - country in East Asia

Etymology

From Japanese 朝鮮 (Chōsen), the name for Korea.
