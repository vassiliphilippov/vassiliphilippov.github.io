chosen

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃoʊzən / (AmE), / ˈtʃəʊzən / (BrE)
a person who is selected or preferred over others; one who is the object of choice
one who is selected or preferred

Examples

Throughout the competition, it was clear that Maria was the chosen of the judges.
He was treated like the chosen among his classmates after winning the scholarship.
In the ancient story, only the chosen could enter the sacred temple.

Synset Examples

she was Mama's chosen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: favorite - a person or thing preferred over others
  • Antonym: reject - a person who is not chosen or preferred

Etymology

From Middle English 'chosen', past participle of 'choose', from Old English 'ceosan'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃoʊzən / (AmE), / ˈtʃəʊzən / (BrE)
a group of people who are selected or regarded as specially privileged or set apart from others; the elect
an exclusive or privileged group

Examples

Only a handful of the chosen were invited to the private ceremony.
Throughout history, rulers often believed they ruled as part of a divine chosen.
Access to the inner circle was granted solely to the political chosen.

Synset Examples

one of the elect who have power inside the government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elect - a group of people specially chosen or favored

Etymology

From Old English 'ceosan' (to choose), past participle 'gecoren', through Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08403584-n
Synset POS: n
