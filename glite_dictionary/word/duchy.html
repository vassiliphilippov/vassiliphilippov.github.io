duchy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌtʃi / (AmE), / ˈdʌtʃi / (BrE)
a territory or domain ruled by a duke or duchess
territory ruled by a duke or duchess

Examples

The grand duchy of Luxembourg is a small, landlocked country in Western Europe.
After the war, the duchy was incorporated into the larger kingdom.
She inherited the duchy upon her father's death, becoming the first female ruler of that region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: principality - territory ruled by a prince or princess
  • Degree Higher: kingdom - territory ruled by a king or queen
  • Degree Lower: county - territory governed by a count or earl

Etymology

From Old French 'duché', from 'duc' (duke), from Latin 'dux'.
