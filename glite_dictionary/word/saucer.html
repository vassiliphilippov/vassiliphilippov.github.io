saucer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔsər / (AmE), / ˈsɔːsə / (BrE)
A flat, circular disk used in throwing competitions, resembling a plate in shape.
disk for throwing competitions

Examples

Each athlete took a turn hurling the saucer across the open field.
The competition uses a bright orange saucer for increased visibility.
She set a new record for distance with her first throw of the saucer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frisbee - a brand of plastic flying disk used in games and sports

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɔsɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɔːsə / (BrE)
a directional antenna consisting of a parabolic reflector designed to focus microwave or radio frequency signals, often used for satellite communication
parabolic antenna for satellite or radio signals

Examples

The television signal was received by a large saucer on the roof.
Many homes have a saucer in their backyard for satellite TV.
Technicians adjusted the angle of the saucer to improve reception.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dish - a parabolic antenna for receiving or transmitting signals

4. NOUN

/ ˈsɔsɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɔːsə / (BrE)
A small, shallow dish, typically placed under a cup to catch drips or hold a spoon.
small dish under a cup

Examples

She set the teacup carefully onto the saucer to avoid spilling.
A matching cup and saucer were part of the antique dinner set.
He placed a sugar cube on the saucer next to his espresso.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plate - a flat dish for serving or eating food

Etymology

From Old French 'saucier' (sauce dish), from 'sauce' (sauce).
