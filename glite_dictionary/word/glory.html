glory

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɔri / (AmE), / ˈɡlɔːri / (BrE)
A halo or radiant light depicted around the head of a holy figure, especially in religious art, to signify sanctity.
halo or radiant light around a holy figure's head

Examples

The painting featured a saint with a bright glory encircling her head.
In medieval icons, the glory signifies the divine nature of the figure.
Artists often use gold leaf to depict the glory in religious portraits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halo - a circle of light around the head of a saint or holy person in art

Etymology

From Latin 'gloria' (fame, renown, glory).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɔri / (AmE), / ˈɡlɔːri / (BrE)
Brilliant, radiant beauty or splendor, especially that which inspires admiration or awe.
radiant beauty; splendor

Examples

The garden was at the height of its glory in spring, bursting with color and life.
We stood in awe as the mountain revealed its snow-covered glory at sunrise.
The cathedral's stained-glass windows shimmered in all their glory in the afternoon light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beauty - a combination of qualities that pleases the senses
