on-license

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑn ˌlaɪ.səns / (AmE), / ˈɒn ˌlaɪ.səns / (BrE)
A license granted to sell alcoholic drinks for consumption on the premises, such as in a bar or restaurant.
permit to sell alcohol for on-site consumption
legal british

Examples

The pub applied for an on-license to serve beer to its customers.
Restaurants must have an on-license to legally offer wine with meals.
He invested in a bar after obtaining the required on-license.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: off-license - retail license to sell alcohol for off-premises consumption
