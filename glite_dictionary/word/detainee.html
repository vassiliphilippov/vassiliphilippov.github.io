detainee

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːtəˈniː / (AmE), / ˌdiːteɪˈniː / (BrE)
a person who is held in custody, especially for political or legal reasons
person held in custody

Examples

The camp held hundreds of detainees awaiting trial on various charges.
International observers visited the detainee to assess his condition in custody.
The rights of each detainee must be respected according to international law.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prisoner - a person legally held in prison as a punishment for a crime or while awaiting trial
  • Antonym: captor - a person who takes or holds someone captive

Etymology

From French détenir (to detain), from Latin detinere (to hold back).
