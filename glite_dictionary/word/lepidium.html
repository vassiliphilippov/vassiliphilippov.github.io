Lepidium

1. NOUN

/ ləˈpɪdiəm / (AmE), / lɛˈpɪdɪəm / (BrE)
a genus of annual, biennial, and perennial herbs in the mustard family, commonly known as cress
genus of herbs known as cress

Examples

Lepidium species are often grown for their edible spicy leaves, commonly called garden cress.
Botanists studied the distribution and adaptation of Lepidium across various continents.
The genus Lepidium includes several plants valued both for culinary and medicinal purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cress - any of several plants of the genus Lepidium, used in salads for their peppery leaves

Etymology

From Latin lepidium, borrowed from Greek lepidion, meaning 'little scale', referring to the shape of the seeds.

2. NOUN

/ ləˈpɪdiəm / (AmE), / lɪˈpɪdɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of annual, biennial, and perennial herbs in the mustard family, including cress plants, found worldwide.
a genus of herbs (cress plants) worldwide

Examples

The scientist studied several species within the genus Lepidium for their medicinal properties.
Many salad greens, such as garden cress, belong to the Lepidium genus.
We found wild Lepidium growing in the open fields near the coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cress - a plant of the genus Lepidium; edible herb used in salads

Etymology

From Latin *lepidium* (a kind of plant), from Greek *lepidion* (a diminutive of *lepis*, 'scale'), referring to the fruit's shape.
