Eruca vesicaria sativa

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈruːkə vɛˈsɪkəriə səˈtiːvə / (AmE), / ɪˈruːkə vɛˈsɪkəriə səˈtɪvə / (BrE)
An erect annual plant of European origin, often cultivated for its young, tender leaves used in salads; also known as arugula or rocket.
annual salad plant; arugula/rocket

Examples

The chef garnished the dish with fresh Eruca vesicaria sativa leaves for a peppery flavor.
Many Mediterranean dishes feature Eruca vesicaria sativa as a primary salad ingredient.
Gardeners appreciate how quickly Eruca vesicaria sativa grows from seed to harvest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arugula - young leaves of Eruca vesicaria sativa used as a salad green
  • Synonym: rocket - another common name for Eruca vesicaria sativa, especially in British English

Etymology

From Latin: Eruca (a type of cabbage), vesicaria (bladder-like), sativa (cultivated).
