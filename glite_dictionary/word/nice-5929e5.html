nice

1. ADJ

/ naɪs / (AmE), / naɪs / (BrE)
behaving in a courteous and polite manner; showing kindness or consideration toward others
courteous and polite

Examples

He always makes a nice gesture by holding the door open for others.
It was nice of her to send a thank-you note after the interview.
The staff were extremely nice and made us feel welcome throughout our stay.

Synset Examples

a nice gesture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polite - having or showing good manners and respect for others
  • Antonym: rude - offensive or impolite in manner or speech

Etymology

From Latin 'nescius' (ignorant), later shifting in meaning through Old French and Middle English to 'pleasant, agreeable'.

2. ADJ

/ naɪs / (AmE), / naɪs / (BrE)
(of a person) behaving in a morally proper or socially acceptable way; respectable and virtuous.
morally proper; respectable

Examples

She comes from a very nice family and always behaves politely.
Her parents wanted her to marry a nice young man from their community.
In those days, a nice girl was expected to be home before dark.

Synset Examples

from a decent family
a nice girl

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virtuous - having or showing high moral standards
  • Antonym: improper - not in keeping with accepted standards of morality or propriety

Etymology

From Latin 'nescius' (ignorant), later influenced by Old French 'nice' (silly, simple), evolved to current meanings in English.

Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ naɪs / (AmE), / naɪs / (BrE)
very particular and easily disgusted about cleanliness, propriety, or minor details; excessively fastidious or squeamish
excessively fastidious; easily disgusted

Examples

He was too nice to eat anything that wasn't perfectly clean and well-prepared.
She is so nice about tidiness that even a smudge will bother her all day.
Some critics are nice to the point of rejecting any artwork that isn't meticulously executed.
