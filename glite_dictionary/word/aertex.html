Aertex

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrˌtɛks / (AmE), / ˈɛəˌtɛks / (BrE)
A type of lightweight, loosely woven cotton fabric, originally a trademark, used especially for making shirts and underwear.
lightweight woven cotton fabric

Examples

He wore a white Aertex shirt on the tennis court.
School uniforms often included blue Aertex blouses during the summer.
The breathable quality of Aertex makes it ideal for sportswear.

Etymology

Originally a trademark, from 'aero-' (air) and 'textile'.
