Polanisia dodecandra

1. NOUN

/ poʊˌleɪˈnɪziə ˌdoʊdɪˈkændrə / (AmE), / pɒˌlæˈnɪziə ˌdəʊdɪˈkændrə / (BrE)
a strong-scented herb native to the southern United States, covered with intermixed glands and hairs; also known as redwhisker clammyweed
a strong-scented herb of the southern US

Examples

Botanists often study Polanisia dodecandra to understand adaptations to dry environments.
Polanisia dodecandra is commonly found in sandy soils across the southern United States.
The strong scent of Polanisia dodecandra helps attract pollinators to its small, white flowers.

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name Polanisia, species epithet dodecandra (from Greek dodeka 'twelve' and anēr 'male', referring to twelve stamens).
