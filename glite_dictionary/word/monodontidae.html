Monodontidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmoʊnəˈdɒntɪdi / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈdɒntɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of Arctic and sub-Arctic whales that includes the narwhal and the beluga.
family of whales (narwhal, beluga)

Examples

The Monodontidae family includes both the narwhal and the beluga whale.
Scientists study Monodontidae to better understand Arctic marine life.
Members of the Monodontidae are well adapted to cold northern waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: narwhal - an Arctic whale with a long spiral tusk
  • Synonym: beluga - a white Arctic whale, also called 'white whale'

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek monos (single) + odous/odont- (tooth), referring to the dentition of these whales.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɒnəˈdɒntɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəʊˈdɒntɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of Arctic and subarctic toothed whales that includes the narwhal and the beluga whale
family of toothed whales (narwhal, beluga)

Examples

The narwhal is a fascinating member of the Monodontidae family.
Researchers study the migration patterns of Monodontidae in the Arctic Ocean.
Beluga whales, classified within Monodontidae, are known for their distinctive white color.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: narwhal - a type of Arctic whale with a long spiral tusk
  • Degree Higher: beluga - a species of white whale found in Arctic and subarctic regions

Etymology

From New Latin Monodontidae, based on genus name Monodon (meaning 'one tooth') and Greek -idae (family suffix).
