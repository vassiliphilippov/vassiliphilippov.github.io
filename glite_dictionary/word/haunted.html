haunted

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɑntɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɔːntɪd / (BrE)
(of a place) inhabited or frequented by ghosts or spirits.
inhabited by ghosts or spirits

Examples

Many tourists visit the old haunted castle during Halloween to hear spine-chilling stories.
She swore she saw a shadow in the haunted mansion late at night.
The haunted hotel is famous for its unexplained noises and mysterious sightings.

Synset Examples

a haunted house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spooky - strangely frightening; eerie; suggestive of ghosts
  • Synonym: possessed - (of a place or person) controlled by a spirit
  • Antonym: ordinary - having no supernatural qualities; not haunted

2. ADJ

/ ˈhɔntɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɔːntɪd / (BrE)
having an anxious, troubled, or preoccupied appearance, as if disturbed by persistent thoughts or worries
preoccupied or troubled in appearance

Examples

Her haunted expression suggested she had been worrying about the accident for days.
He gave a haunted look whenever someone mentioned his former job.
After months of stress, her face appeared drawn and haunted.

Synset Examples

he was taken up in worry for the old woman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obsessed - having persistent disturbing thoughts or feelings about something
  • Synonym: preoccupied - deep in thought about something to the exclusion of other concerns

3. ADJ

/ ˈhɑntɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɔːntɪd / (BrE)
(of a person's appearance or expression) showing signs of deep emotional distress or worry.
showing deep emotional distress

Examples

Her eyes looked haunted after the accident, as if she hadn’t slept in days.
He wore a haunted expression that made everyone worry about him.
The haunted look on her face revealed a pain she couldn't put into words.

Synset Examples

her expression became progressively more haunted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: troubled - showing distress or worry
  • Antonym: serene - calm and untroubled in appearance

Etymology

From the verb 'haunt', originally from Old French 'hanter' (to frequent, visit regularly).
