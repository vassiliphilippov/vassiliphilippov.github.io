safflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈsæflaʊə / (BrE)
a thistlelike plant of Eurasian origin with red or orange flower heads, grown for its seeds that yield a valuable oil
plant with red or orange flower heads used for oil

Examples

The oil extracted from safflower seeds is often used in cooking and salad dressings.
Farmers planted rows of safflower alongside sunflowers to harvest both oil-rich crops.
She purchased a bag of safflower petals to use as a natural dye for fabric.

Etymology

From Arabic "as-sufr" (yellow), via Persian "gol-e safa" (flower of saffron).
