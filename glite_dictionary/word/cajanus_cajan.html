Cajanus cajan

1. NOUN

/ kəˈdʒɑːnəs kəˈdʒɑːn / (AmE), / kəˈdʒɑːnəs kəˈdʒɑːn / (BrE)
a tropical woody herb with showy yellow flowers and flat pods, widely cultivated for its edible seeds as pigeon pea
tropical plant, source of pigeon pea

Examples

Cajanus cajan is commonly grown in India for its protein-rich seeds.
Farmers rotate Cajanus cajan with other crops to improve soil fertility.
The lush foliage and yellow flowers of Cajanus cajan make it a distinctive tropical plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pigeon pea - edible seed and common name for Cajanus cajan

Etymology

From the genus Cajanus in Latin, ultimately from Malay 'kacang' meaning 'bean.'
