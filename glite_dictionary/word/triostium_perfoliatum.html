Triostium perfoliatum

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈɒstiəm pɜrˌfoʊliˈeɪtəm / (AmE), / traɪˈɒstiəm pəːˌfɔːlɪˈɑːtəm / (BrE)
A coarse, weedy American perennial herb with large, usually perfoliate leaves and purple or dull red flowers.
American perennial herb with perfoliate leaves

Examples

Triostium perfoliatum is commonly found in moist woodland areas across eastern North America.
Botanists studied the purple flowers of Triostium perfoliatum to understand its pollination mechanisms.
The large, perfoliate leaves make Triostium perfoliatum easy to identify during field surveys.

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name 'Triostium' + Latin 'perfoliatum' meaning 'with leaves surrounding the stem'.
