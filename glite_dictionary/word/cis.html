CIS

1. PROPN

/ si aɪ ɛs / (AmE), / siː aɪ ɛs / (BrE)
an association formed by several former Soviet republics after the breakup of the Soviet Union in 1991 to facilitate cooperation in political, economic, and military matters
Commonwealth of Independent States; post-Soviet regional organization

Examples

After the collapse of the Soviet Union, several countries joined the CIS to maintain cooperation.
The CIS summit addressed issues of trade and border security among member states.
Belarus and Kazakhstan are among the prominent members of the CIS.

Etymology

Acronym for Commonwealth of Independent States.
