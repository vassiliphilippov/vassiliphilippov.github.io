miler

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪlər / (AmE), / ˈmaɪlə / (BrE)
an athlete who competes in one-mile races, especially in track and field events
runner in a one-mile race

Examples

The defending champion is a seasoned miler with several national titles to her name.
As a young miler, he trains six days a week to improve his speed and endurance.
Many milers transition to longer races as their careers progress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sprinter - an athlete who runs short-distance races
  • Antonym: marathoner - an athlete who runs in marathon races (26.2 miles)

Etymology

From 'mile' + '-er', denoting a person associated with the activity.
