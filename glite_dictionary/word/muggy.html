muggy

1. ADJ

/ ˈmʌɡi / (AmE), / ˈmʌɡi / (BrE)
(of the weather or air) unpleasantly warm and humid.
warm and humid (weather)

Examples

It was so muggy last night that I could hardly sleep.
The air feels particularly muggy after the summer rainstorm.
She complained about the muggy conditions in the crowded subway.

Synset Examples

muggy weather
the steamy tropics
sticky weather

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humid - (of the air or weather) containing a high amount of water vapor
  • Synonym: steamy - (of the weather or air) hot and filled with moisture
  • Antonym: dry - (of weather or air) not having much moisture
