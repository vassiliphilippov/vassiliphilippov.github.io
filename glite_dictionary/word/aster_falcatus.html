Aster falcatus

1. NOUN

/ ˈæstər fælˈkeɪtəs / (AmE), / ˈæstə fælˈkeɪtəs / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to western North America, with white flowers.
perennial North American plant with white flowers

Examples

Aster falcatus blooms in late spring and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.
Gardeners appreciate Aster falcatus for its resilience and attractive white blossoms.
Aster falcatus can add diversity to native plant gardens in western regions.

Etymology

From Latin 'aster' (star, plant genus) and 'falcatus' (sickle-shaped).
