Rig-Veda

1. PROPN

/ rɪɡ ˈveɪdə / (AmE), / rɪɡ ˈveɪdə / (BrE)
the oldest of the four Vedas, consisting of a collection of ancient Hindu hymns and poems composed in Sanskrit before 2000 BC
ancient Hindu collection of hymns; oldest Veda

Examples

The Rig-Veda is considered the earliest literary work in the Indo-European languages.
Many hymns from the Rig-Veda praise the natural forces and deities of early Vedic society.
Scholars have extensively studied the Rig-Veda to understand ancient Hindu philosophy and ritual.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Veda - any of the oldest sacred writings of Hinduism in Sanskrit

Etymology

From Sanskrit Ṛgveda, from ṛc (verse, hymn) + veda (knowledge, sacred knowledge).
