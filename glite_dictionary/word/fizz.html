fizz

1. NOUN

/ fɪz / (AmE), / fɪz / (BrE)
a drink that is bubbly with effervescence, especially a type of cocktail made with carbonated water and usually alcoholic spirits
effervescent cocktail or beverage

Examples

The bartender prepared a refreshing gin fizz for the customer.
Her favorite drink is a lemon fizz on a warm afternoon.
Many classic cocktails, like the Ramos fizz, are known for their frothy texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cocktail - an alcoholic mixed drink

Etymology

Imitative of the sound produced by effervescence; first used in the mid-19th century.

2. VERB

/ fɪz / (AmE), / fɪz / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to become bubbly or produce bubbles of gas, often making a hissing or spluttering sound.
to become bubbly or foamy

Examples

When you open a can of soda, it will fizz loudly as the gas escapes.
The champagne began to fizz as soon as it was poured into the glass.
As the tablet dissolved in water, it started to fizz and bubble energetically.

Synset Examples

The boiling soup was frothing
The river was foaming
sparkling water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bubble - to form bubbles or to rise in bubbles; to effervesce
  • Synonym: foam - to produce a mass of small bubbles on the surface of a liquid
  • Antonym: flat - (of a drink) to have lost its bubbles or effervescence

Etymology

Imitative of the sound produced by effervescent liquids; related to 'fizzle'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00520443-v
Synset POS: v
