to bollocks

1. VERB

/ ˈbɑːləks / (AmE), / ˈbɒləks / (BrE)
(British, slang) To ruin, spoil, or make a mess of something through incompetence or carelessness.
mess up; ruin; botch (slang)
slang british

Examples

He completely bollocksed the presentation by forgetting his notes and losing his place.
You’ll bollocks the whole project if you don’t pay more attention to the details.
They realized they had bollocksed up the order and sent the wrong items to customers.

Synset Examples

I botched the dinner and we had to eat out
the pianist screwed up the difficult passage in the second movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botch - to carry out a task badly or carelessly
  • Synonym: to screw up - to make a mistake or mess up

Etymology

Originally British slang, from the noun 'bollocks' (testicles), later extended to mean 'nonsense' or 'rubbish', and used as a verb in the 20th century.
