to revalue

1. VERB

/ riˈvælju / (AmE), / riˈvaljuː / (BrE)
to assess or set a new value on something, especially a currency or asset, often resulting in an increase of its official worth
assign a new value, often higher

Examples

The central bank decided to revalue the national currency to strengthen the economy.
After the renovation, the house was revalued at a significantly higher price.
The company regularly revalues its assets to reflect current market conditions.

Synset Examples

revalue the German Mark

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to devalue - to reduce the official value of something, especially currency

Etymology

From re- (again) + value (to estimate worth).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00316611-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ riˈvæl.ju / (AmE), / riˈval.juː / (BrE)
to increase in value, especially in terms of exchange rate or currency worth
increase in value, especially in finance

Examples

Many investors hope the currency will revalue against the dollar this year.
If the central bank intervenes, it might revalue the local currency.
After years of stagnation, real estate prices began to revalue dramatically.

Synset Examples

The yen appreciated again!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to devalue - to reduce the value of something, especially a currency
  • Synonym: to appreciate - to increase in value over time
  • Antonym: to depreciate - to lose value over time

Etymology

From prefix re- (again) + value. Used in financial contexts from the early 20th century.
