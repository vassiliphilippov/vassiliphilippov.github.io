crith

1. NOUN

/ krɪθ / (AmE), / krɪθ / (BrE)
a unit of mass in chemistry equal to the weight of one liter of hydrogen gas at 0°C and standard atmospheric pressure
unit of mass for hydrogen gas

Examples

The calculation required converting the mass into criths for accuracy in the experiment.
One crith is used as a reference standard in determining gas densities.
Chemists once measured gases in criths before the use of the mole became standard.

Etymology

From Greek 'krithē' (barleycorn), used as a measure of weight.
