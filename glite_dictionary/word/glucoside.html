glucoside

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡluːkoʊˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈɡluːkəsʌɪd / (BrE)
A glycoside compound in which the sugar component is glucose.
glycoside with glucose as the sugar part
medical

Examples

Certain plants produce glucoside compounds as a defense mechanism against herbivores.
The bitter flavor of almonds is due to a glucoside that breaks down during digestion.
Scientists isolated a new glucoside from the bark extract for further study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glycoside - a compound of a sugar and another substance

Etymology

From glucose + -ide, indicating a derivative or compound.
