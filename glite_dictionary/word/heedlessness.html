heedlessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːdləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈhiːdləsnəs / (BrE)
The quality of not paying attention to possible dangers or to what is important; lack of care or attention.
lack of attention or care

Examples

Her heedlessness when crossing the street put her in danger.
The company's heedlessness of safety regulations resulted in several accidents.
His heedlessness toward his friends' feelings eventually led to conflicts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carelessness - lack of care or attention
  • Antonym: attentiveness - the quality of paying attention

Etymology

Formed from 'heedless' + '-ness'; 'heedless' from Middle English 'heden' (to heed) + '-less'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhidl̩snɪs / (AmE), / ˈhiːdləsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of acting carelessly or without paying attention to possible dangers or consequences
careless lack of attention or caution

Examples

His heedlessness while driving nearly caused an accident.
She regretted her heedlessness after realizing the risks she had ignored.
The explorers' heedlessness of the weather warnings led to trouble.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carelessness - lack of attention and consideration
  • Antonym: caution - careful attention to danger

Etymology

From 'heedless' + '-ness', where 'heed' comes from Old English 'hēdan' (to observe, pay attention).

3. NOUN

/ ˈhidləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈhiːdləsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of not paying attention to something, especially neglecting responsibilities or warnings
lack of attention or care

Examples

Her heedlessness of the warning signs led to unnecessary problems.
Many accidents can be traced back to the heedlessness of basic safety rules.
His heedlessness towards deadlines caused frustration among his coworkers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carelessness - lack of attention or consideration; similar to heedlessness
  • Antonym: attentiveness - paying close attention to something; opposite of heedlessness

Etymology

From 'heedless' + '-ness'; 'heedless' from Middle English 'heden' (to pay attention) + '-less'.
