Pagophila eburnea

1. PROPN

/ ˌpeɪɡəˈfaɪlə ɛˈbɜrniə / (AmE), / ˌpaɪɡəˈfɪlə ɪˈbɜːniə / (BrE)
A species of white Arctic gull that migrates as far south as England and New Brunswick; also known as the ivory gull.
white Arctic gull species

Examples

The Pagophila eburnea is increasingly rare due to climate change affecting its Arctic habitat.
Birdwatchers in Newfoundland were excited to spot a Pagophila eburnea during the winter migration.
The pristine white plumage makes Pagophila eburnea easily identifiable among Arctic gulls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ivory gull - common name for Pagophila eburnea

Etymology

From Greek 'pagos' (ice) and 'philos' (loving); 'eburnea' from Latin for 'ivory', referencing its white plumage.
