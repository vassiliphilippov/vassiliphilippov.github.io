Isère

1. PROPN

/ iˈzɛər / (AmE), / iˈzɛər / (BrE)
a river in southeastern France that flows into the Rhône
river in southeastern France

Examples

The Isère valley is famous for its beautiful alpine scenery.
Several towns along the Isère rely on the river for water and irrigation.
After heavy rains, the Isère occasionally floods its banks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rhône - major river in southeastern France and Switzerland

Etymology

From the French 'Isère', ultimately from Latin 'Isara', of pre-Latin origin.
