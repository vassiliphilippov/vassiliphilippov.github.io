Glyceria grandis

1. NOUN

/ ɡlaɪˈsɪəriə ˈɡrændɪs / (AmE), / ɡlɪˈsɪəriə ˈɡrandɪs / (BrE)
A tall North American grass found in moist areas, commonly known as American mannagrass, used as pasture grass.
a tall pasture grass of North America

Examples

The wetland was filled with lush Glyceria grandis, providing pasture for grazing animals.
Botanists surveyed the area and identified Glyceria grandis among other native grasses.
Farmers often plant Glyceria grandis to improve the quality of their livestock pastures.

Etymology

From the genus name Glyceria (from Greek glukeros, 'sweet') and Latin grandis, 'large'.
