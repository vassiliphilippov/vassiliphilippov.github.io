adjustor

1. NOUN

/ əˈdʒʌstər / (AmE), / əˈdʒʌstə / (BrE)
a person who investigates insurance claims or claims for damages and recommends an effective settlement
insurance claims investigator

Examples

After the storm, the insurance company sent an adjustor to assess the damage to the house.
The role of an adjustor involves negotiating settlements with claimants.
If you disagree with the settlement, you can request a different adjustor to review your claim.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adjuster - alternative spelling for 'adjustor' with the same meaning

Etymology

From 'adjust' + '-or', denoting a person who adjusts.
