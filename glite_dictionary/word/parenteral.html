parenteral

1. ADJ

/ pəˈrɛntərəl / (AmE), / pəˈrɛntərəl / (BrE)
(of medication or nutrition) administered or occurring by injection through routes other than the digestive tract, such as by intravenous or intramuscular injection.
administered by injection, not orally
medical

Examples

The patient received parenteral nutrition due to a severe intestinal disorder.
Some antibiotics can only be given parenterally to ensure proper absorption.
In emergency situations, parenteral administration of fluids may be essential.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: oral - administered through the mouth or digestive tract

Etymology

From Greek 'para' (beside) + 'enteron' (intestine), meaning 'outside the intestine'.

2. ADJ

/ pəˈrɛntərəl / (AmE), / pəˈrɛntərəl / (BrE)
Located or occurring outside the alimentary (digestive) tract, especially referring to administration of medication by injection rather than by mouth.
outside the digestive tract (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a parenteral nutrition plan for the patient who could not eat by mouth.
Certain antibiotics are only effective when given in a parenteral form.
When oral medication is not possible, parenteral administration becomes essential.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: enteral - administered through the digestive tract

Etymology

From Greek para- (beside) + enteron (intestine) + -al.
