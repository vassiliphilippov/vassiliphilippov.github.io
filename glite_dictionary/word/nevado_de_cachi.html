Nevado de Cachi

1. PROPN

/ neˈvɑːdoʊ deɪ ˈkɑːtʃi / (AmE), / neˈvɑːdəʊ deɪ ˈkætʃi / (BrE)
a mountain in the Andes of northwestern Argentina, rising to 20,932 feet (6,384 meters) above sea level.
mountain in the Andes, Argentina

Examples

Climbers from around the world attempt to summit Nevado de Cachi every year.
The breathtaking views from the peak of Nevado de Cachi attract many adventurers.
At over 20,000 feet high, Nevado de Cachi dominates the Salta Province landscape.

Etymology

From Spanish, literally ‘snow-covered (mountain) of Cachi’.
