Jesse Jackson

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒɛsi ˈdʒæk.sən / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛsi ˈdʒæk.sən / (BrE)
(born 1941) A prominent American civil rights leader, Baptist minister, and politician known for his activism against racial discrimination and his presidential campaigns in the 1980s.
American civil rights leader and politician

Examples

Jesse Jackson played an influential role in the civil rights movement of the 1960s and 1970s.
In 1984, Jesse Jackson ran for the Democratic presidential nomination.
Many students study the speeches of Jesse Jackson for their powerful messages on equality and justice.
