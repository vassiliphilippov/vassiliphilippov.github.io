helicon

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛlɪˌkɑn / (AmE), / ˈhɛlɪkən / (BrE)
a large brass musical instrument of the tuba family, coiled so it fits around the player's shoulders and is used especially in marching bands
tuba that coils over the shoulder

Examples

The brass band featured a shining helicon wrapped around the musician's body.
His grandfather played the helicon in parades throughout the city every summer.
Marching with a helicon requires skill because of its size and coiling shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuba - a large brass wind instrument with a deep sound

Etymology

From Greek helix, helikos (spiral, something twisted).
