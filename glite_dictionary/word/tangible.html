tangible

1. ADJ

/ ˈtændʒəbəl / (AmE), / ˈtændʒɪbəl / (BrE)
perceptible by touch; able to be physically felt or clearly seen and understood.
able to be touched or perceived

Examples

The museum displayed several tangible artifacts that visitors could touch.
There was a tangible sense of excitement in the air before the concert began.
Her fear was almost tangible, as if you could reach out and feel it.

Synset Examples

skin with a tangible roughness

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intangible - unable to be touched or grasped; not having a physical presence

Etymology

From Late Latin tangibilis, from Latin tangere (to touch).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02400023-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈtændʒəbl / (AmE), / ˈtan.dʒɪ.bl̩ / (BrE)
(of something, especially an asset) having physical substance and intrinsic monetary value; capable of being touched or physically measured.
having physical substance; material

Examples

The company owns several tangible assets, including buildings and equipment.
Only tangible property can be insured against fire or theft.
Their tangible resources give them a significant advantage over rival firms.

Synset Examples

tangible property like real estate
tangible assets such as machinery

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intangible - not having physical substance; incapable of being touched; lacking material existence

Etymology

From Latin tangibilis (that may be touched), from tangere (to touch).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02400702-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈtændʒəbəl / (AmE), / ˈtændʒɪbəl / (BrE)
able to be touched or felt; real and concrete rather than imaginary or abstract.
able to be touched; real or concrete

Examples

4. ADJ

/ ˈtændʒəbəl / (AmE), / ˈtændʒɪbəl / (BrE)
(of evidence, facts, or results) able to be clearly shown, proved, or recognized; real and definite.
able to be proved or shown as fact

Examples

The lawyer presented tangible evidence that convinced the jury of his client's innocence.
We need tangible results before we invest more money in this project.
There was no tangible proof linking him to the crime.

Synset Examples

his brief time as Prime Minister brought few real benefits to the poor

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intangible - not able to be touched or easily defined; not having physical presence
