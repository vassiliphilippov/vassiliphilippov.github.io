wrinkled

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɪŋkəld / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋkəld / (BrE)
having small lines or folds, usually caused by creasing or aging; marked by wrinkles.
having lines or folds; marked by wrinkles

Examples

His shirt looked very wrinkled after a long day of travel.
The old man's wrinkled hands shook as he wrote his letter.
She tried to iron the wrinkled napkins before setting the table.

Synset Examples

tired travelers in wrinkled clothes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; without wrinkles

Etymology

From 'wrinkle' + '-ed', dating to Middle English.

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɪŋkəld / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋkəld / (BrE)
(of fabric or clothing) having creases or lines, typically from not being ironed or folded neatly.
with creases; not ironed (fabric)

Examples

She wore a wrinkled shirt to the meeting after pulling it from the laundry basket.
The wrinkled bed sheets made the room look untidy.
His pants were wrinkled because he forgot to iron them before leaving.

Synset Examples

a pile of unironed laundry
wore unironed jeans

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creased - having lines or folds, especially from being pressed or crumpled
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even or flat surface, not creased or wrinkled

Etymology

From wrinkle + -ed, meaning 'having wrinkles.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01362777-a
Synset POS: a
