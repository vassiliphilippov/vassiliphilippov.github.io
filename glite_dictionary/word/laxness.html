laxness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæks.nəs / (AmE), / ˈlæks.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being lacking in strictness, care, or attention; negligence or slackness.
lack of strictness or care

Examples

The laxness of the security measures made the museum vulnerable to theft.
His laxness in meeting deadlines caused frequent problems at work.
The judge criticized the laxness of the company's safety protocols.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: negligence - failure to take proper care, neglect
  • Antonym: strictness - quality of being strict or exacting

Etymology

From 'lax' + '-ness', with 'lax' from Latin laxus (loose, slack).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlæks.nəs / (AmE), / ˈlæks.nəs / (BrE)
The condition of being physiologically loose, lacking firmness or normal tone, especially in muscles or bodily tissues.
state of physiological looseness or lack of tone
medical

Examples

Muscle laxness can result in poor posture and back pain.
Dietary changes may reduce the laxness of the intestines.
Physiotherapists often work to correct joint laxness after injury.

Synset Examples

baths can help the laxness of the bowels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flaccidity - the state of being soft or lacking firmness, especially in muscles
  • Antonym: firmness - the quality of being solid or taut, opposite of laxness in this context

Etymology

From Latin 'laxus' (loose, slack) + English suffix '-ness'.
