wheelwork

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːlˌwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈwiːlˌwɜːk / (BrE)
the interconnected wheels and gears in a machine, especially as part of a mechanical device
system of wheels and gears in a machine

Examples

The delicate wheelwork of the old clock was restored by a skilled horologist.
He examined the wheelwork inside the watch to determine why it had stopped.
The inventor designed intricate wheelwork to power his mechanical device.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gear train - a connected series of gears that transmits motion

Etymology

Formed from 'wheel' + 'work', indicating a mechanism that works with wheels.
