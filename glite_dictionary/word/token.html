token

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊkən / (AmE), / ˈtəʊkən / (BrE)
an individual occurrence of a symbol, character, or element, especially in linguistic, mathematical, or computing contexts
individual instance of a symbol or element

Examples

Each letter in the string is treated as a separate token by the program.
The sentence was split into several tokens for analysis.
You must count the number of token occurrences of each digit separately.

Synset Examples

the word ‘error’ contains three tokens of ‘r’

Etymology

From Old English *tācen* (sign, symbol), related to German *Zeichen* (sign).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06808603-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊ.kən / (AmE), / ˈtəʊ.kən / (BrE)
something that represents or is a sign of a fact, feeling, event, or idea
something that serves as a sign or symbol

Examples

She gave him a token of her appreciation in the form of a small gift.
The medal was presented as a token of bravery by the community.
Please accept this card as a token of my gratitude for your help.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symbol - something that stands for or represents something else
  • Synonym: emblem - an object or image that represents an idea or quality

Etymology

From Old English *tāc(e)n* (sign, symbol), related to German *Zeichen* (sign).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06659294-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊkən / (AmE), / ˈtəʊkən / (BrE)
an object kept or given to remind someone of a person, place, or event; something kept for its sentimental value
object kept for sentimental value

Examples

She still keeps the small token he gave her on their first date.
As a token of our friendship, I want you to have this necklace.
The old photograph was a precious token that reminded him of his childhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: memento - an object kept as a reminder of a person or event
  • Synonym: souvenir - an object kept as a reminder of a place or event
  • Synonym: keepsake - an object kept in memory of the giver

Etymology

From Old English *tāc(e)n* (sign, symbol), related to German *Zeichen* (sign).

4. ADJ

/ ˈtoʊkən / (AmE), / ˈtəʊkən / (BrE)
(of an action, gesture, or amount) done for the sake of appearance or as a symbolic gesture, rather than as something meaningful or substantial.
symbolic; minimal or for appearance only

Examples

Her apology was merely a token gesture, not a sincere attempt to make amends.
The company made a token donation to charity, but it was clear their priorities lay elsewhere.
Only a token number of changes were introduced, so the system stayed much the same.

Synset Examples

the fee was nominal
a token gesture of resistance
a tokenish gesture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nominal - very small in amount; existing in name only

Etymology

From Old English *tācen* (sign, symbol), related to German *Zeichen* (sign).

Source: WordNet

5. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊkən / (AmE), / ˈtəʊkən / (BrE)
a metal or plastic disk used as a substitute for money, often for use in slot machines, public transportation, or games.
disk used in place of money in certain machines or systems

Examples

She inserted a token into the subway turnstile to pay her fare.
The arcade only accepts tokens instead of cash in its slot machines.
He found an old token from a long-closed amusement park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coin - piece of metal issued by a government as money

Etymology

From Old English *tāc(e)n* (sign, symbol), later extended to mean a coin-like disk used as a substitute for currency.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
