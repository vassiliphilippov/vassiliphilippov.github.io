felon

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛlən / (AmE), / ˈfɛlən / (BrE)
a person who has been found guilty of a serious crime, especially a felony
person convicted of a serious crime

Examples

After serving his sentence, the felon struggled to find employment.
The law prohibits felons from possessing firearms.
She was labeled a felon after her conviction for grand theft.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criminal - a person who has committed a crime
  • Antonym: innocent - a person not guilty of a crime

Etymology

From Old French felon (wicked person, criminal), from Medieval Latin felonem.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɛlən / (AmE), / ˈfɛlən / (BrE)
a painful, pus-filled infection around the soft tissue at the end of a finger or toe, especially near the nail
pus-filled finger or toe infection
medical

Examples

He had a painful felon on his index finger that required medical attention.
A felon can develop quickly when bacteria enter a small cut near the fingernail.
Proper care and early treatment can prevent a felon from becoming severe.

Etymology

From Old French *felon* (wicked person), later used in medical Latin for a finger infection (possibly from the pain being seen as 'malignant').
