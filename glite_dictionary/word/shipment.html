shipment

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪpmənt / (AmE), / ˈʃɪpmənt / (BrE)
the act of sending goods or cargo to a particular place, especially by ship, train, truck, or aircraft
the act of sending goods or cargo

Examples

The shipment of medical supplies is scheduled to arrive tomorrow.
They tracked the shipment online to ensure it reached the customer.
A delay in the shipment caused problems for the company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delivery - the action of delivering goods; the bringing of parcels or goods to a recipient
  • Synonym: dispatch - the sending of goods to a destination

Etymology

From ship + -ment, denoting the action or result of shipping.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪpmənt / (AmE), / ˈʃɪpmənt / (BrE)
a quantity of goods or cargo that is transported together, typically by a large vehicle, aircraft, or vessel
goods transported together

Examples

The shipment of electronics arrived at the warehouse on Friday afternoon.
Customs officials inspected the shipment before it was cleared for delivery.
Our company handles every shipment with care to ensure the products reach customers safely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consignment - a batch of goods destined for or delivered to someone

Etymology

From the verb 'ship' + -ment; 'ship' from Old English 'scip' (ship).
