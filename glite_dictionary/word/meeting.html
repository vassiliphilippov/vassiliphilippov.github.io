meeting

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmiːtɪŋ / (BrE)
a small informal social gathering
informal social gathering

Examples

We hosted a meeting at our house with close friends to catch up over snacks and coffee.
After work, a small meeting formed in the break room to celebrate a colleague’s birthday.
She enjoyed the informal meeting with neighbors that turned into a lively chat about local events.

Synset Examples

there was an informal meeting in my living room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gathering - an assembly or meeting, especially a social or informal one
  • Degree Higher: party - a social gathering of invited guests

Etymology

From Old English *mētan* (to meet) + -ing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmitɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmiːtɪŋ / (BrE)
An occasion when people come together for a particular purpose, especially to discuss something.
gathering for a purpose

Examples

The weekly staff meeting is scheduled for 10 a.m. every Monday.
She arranged a meeting with her lawyer to discuss the contract.
Do you have time for a quick meeting this afternoon?

Synset Examples

his meeting with the salesmen was the high point of his day
the lovers met discreetly for the purposes of sexual congress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assembly - a group of people gathered together in one place for a common purpose
  • Synonym: gathering - an instance of coming together as a group
  • Synonym: conference - a formal meeting for discussion

Etymology

From Old English 'mētan' (to meet) + '-ing'.

Examples
