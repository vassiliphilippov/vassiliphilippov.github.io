thermoelectricity

1. NOUN

/ ˌθɜːrmoʊɪˌlɛkˈtrɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌθɜːməʊɪˌlɛkˈtrɪsəti / (BrE)
electricity produced directly from heat, especially by means of a thermocouple or similar device
electricity generated by heat

Examples

The sensor is powered by thermoelectricity generated from the heat of the engine.
Scientists studied thermoelectricity to improve the efficiency of heat-to-electricity conversion.
Some remote space probes rely on thermoelectricity for generating power from radioactive decay.

Etymology

From thermo- (relating to heat) + electricity, combining Greek 'thermē' (heat) and 'ēlektron' (amber, source of 'electricity').
