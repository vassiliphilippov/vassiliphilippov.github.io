autobiographic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɔːtəˌbaɪəˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəʊˌbaɪəˈɡræfɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of an autobiography or one's own life story.
relating to an autobiography

Examples

Her latest novel contains several autobiographic elements based on her own experiences.
The artist's paintings are often autobiographic, reflecting his journey through life.
He wrote an autobiographic essay detailing his struggles and achievements.

Synset Examples

they compiled an autobiographical history of the movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autobiographical - relating to or in the style of an autobiography

Etymology

Formed from 'autobiography' + '-ic', modeled after 'biographic'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɔːtoʊˌbaɪəˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəʊˌbaɪəˈɡræfɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of an autobiography or autobiographer; based on the writer's own life.
relating to autobiography or autobiographers

Examples

Her novel has a distinctly autobiographic quality, drawing heavily on her own experiences.
The autobiographic nature of his writing makes it deeply personal and compelling.
Critics praised the autobiographic elements woven subtly into his poetry.

Synset Examples

he seldom suppressed his autobiographical tendencies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autobiographical - relating to or characteristic of autobiography; based on one's own life

Etymology

From 'autobiography' + '-ic', formed after the pattern of similar adjectival forms.
