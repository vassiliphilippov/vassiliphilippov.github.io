mastic

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˈmæs.tɪk / (BrE)
a soft, pasty cement used as an adhesive or filler in building and repair work
a pasty adhesive or filler

Examples

After applying the mastic, the tiles adhered firmly to the wall.
The plumber sealed the joints with a waterproof mastic to prevent leaks.
Some types of mastic can also be used to fill small cracks in concrete.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adhesive - a substance used for sticking objects or materials together
  • Synonym: sealant - a material used for sealing something to make it airtight or watertight

Etymology

From Greek *mastikha*, referring to chewing gum or resin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˈmæs.tɪk / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region, cultivated for its aromatic resin used in food and industry
Mediterranean shrub cultivated for resin

Examples

The mastic is prized for its aromatic resin, which has been used since ancient times.
In Greece, mastic trees are grown on the island of Chios for commercial production.
The leaves of the mastic shrub are small, glossy, and evergreen throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mastic tree - shrub cultivated for its resin, also known as 'Pistacia lentiscus'

Etymology

From Latin mastiche, from Greek mastikhe (resin of the mastic tree).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmæstɪk / (AmE), / ˈmæstɪk / (BrE)
an aromatic gum or resin obtained from the mastic tree, used especially in varnishes and as a flavoring
aromatic gum from the mastic tree

Examples

The painter applied a thin layer of mastic to seal the wood surface.
Authentic Greek desserts sometimes use mastic as a flavoring agent.
Harvesting mastic from the tree is still a traditional craft in some Mediterranean regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gum - a sticky, resinous substance from plants

Etymology

From Greek mastikhē (to chew), referring to its use as chewing gum in antiquity.
