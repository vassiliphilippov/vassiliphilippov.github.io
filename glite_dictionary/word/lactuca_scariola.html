Lactuca scariola

1. NOUN

/ lækˈtjuːkə skəˈraɪ.ələ / (AmE), / lækˈtjuːkə skəˈraɪ.ələ / (BrE)
A species of wild lettuce native to Europe, known for its prickly stems and regarded as a troublesome weed in parts of the United States.
a species of prickly wild lettuce

Examples

Lactuca scariola can be found growing alongside roads and in disturbed soils across many regions.
Farmers often struggle to control the spread of Lactuca scariola in their fields due to its rapid growth.
The prickly stems are a distinguishing feature of Lactuca scariola, making it easy to identify among wild plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prickly lettuce - common name for Lactuca scariola, an annual wild lettuce

Etymology

From Latin: 'Lactuca' meaning 'lettuce', and 'scariola', a diminutive of the Latin 'scarion' (a kind of plant).
