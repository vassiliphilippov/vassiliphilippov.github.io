hydrocephalic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪdrəsɪˈfælɪk / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪdrə(ʊ)sɪˈfalɪk / (BrE)
relating to or affected by hydrocephalus, a condition characterized by an abnormal accumulation of fluid in the brain.
relating to hydrocephalus
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the infant as hydrocephalic after noticing the unusual swelling of the head.
Researchers studied the neurological effects seen in hydrocephalic patients.
Surgery is sometimes required to relieve fluid pressure in hydrocephalic children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrocephalus - condition involving excess fluid in the brain

Etymology

From hydrocephalus (from Greek 'hydro-' water + 'kephalē' head) + -ic.
