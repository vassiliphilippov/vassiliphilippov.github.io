Marya Sklodowska

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɑriə skloˈdɔfska / (AmE), / ˈmɑːriə sklɒˈdɒfska / (BrE)
The birth name of Marie Curie (1867–1934), a French chemist and physicist born in Poland, celebrated for her pioneering research on radioactivity and for being awarded two Nobel Prizes.
birth name of Marie Curie, Nobel-winning chemist

Examples

Before she became famous as Marie Curie, she was known as Marya Sklodowska.
Many biographies discuss the early life of Marya Sklodowska in Poland.
The museum in Warsaw honors Marya Sklodowska and her contributions to science.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Marie Curie - French-Polish chemist and physicist, major figure in radioactivity research

Etymology

Polish: Marya is a variant of Maria; Sklodowska is her maiden surname.
