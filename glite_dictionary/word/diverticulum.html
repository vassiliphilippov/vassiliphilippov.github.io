diverticulum

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌvɜrˈtɪkjələm / (AmE), / dɪˌvɜːˈtɪkjʊləm / (BrE)
An abnormal pouch or sac forming in the wall of a hollow organ, especially the intestine or colon.
abnormal pouch in organ wall
medical

Examples

During the colonoscopy, the doctor found a diverticulum in the patient's large intestine.
A ruptured diverticulum can lead to serious infection in the abdominal cavity.
The presence of a diverticulum often requires dietary changes to prevent inflammation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polyp - an abnormal growth projecting from a mucous membrane

Etymology

From Latin 'diverticulum' (byway, side road), diminutive of 'divertere' (to turn aside).
