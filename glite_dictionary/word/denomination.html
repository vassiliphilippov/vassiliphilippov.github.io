denomination

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌnɑməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌnɒmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
A recognized branch or division within a religion, especially a Christian church, with its own organization and distinctive beliefs.
religious branch or group

Examples

The church belongs to a large Christian denomination with millions of members worldwide.
Several different denominations are represented in the city's vibrant faith community.
She changed her religious denomination after moving to a new country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sect - A smaller religious group, often with differing beliefs from the main group
  • Antonym: non-denominational - Not restricted or affiliated with any particular religious denomination

Etymology

From Latin 'denominatio' (naming, designation), from 'denominare' (to name).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˌnɑməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌnɒmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a class of units in a system, especially a specified value of currency, weight, or measure
a unit or value, especially of money

Examples

The cashier didn't have enough bills in the ten-dollar denomination to give change.
Coins are available in several denominations, from one cent to one dollar.
You need to specify the denomination of notes you would like when withdrawing cash.

Synset Examples

he flashed a fistful of bills of large denominations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: value - the numerical amount of a unit or item
  • Synonym: face value - the nominal value printed on currency or a security

Etymology

From Latin denominatio (naming, designation), from denominare (to name)

3. NOUN

/ ˌdɛnəˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛnəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
a name or designation that identifies and distinguishes a person, group, or thing
identifying name or designation

Examples

The official denomination of the currency changed after the country's independence.
The plant's scientific denomination helps botanists differentiate it from similar species.
Each coin has its value clearly marked by the denomination on its face.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: designation - a name or title that identifies
  • Synonym: epithet - a descriptive phrase expressing a quality of a person or thing
  • Antonym: anonymity - the state of being anonymous, without a name or identity

Etymology

From Latin 'denominatio' (naming, designation), from 'denominare' (to name) + '-tion'.
