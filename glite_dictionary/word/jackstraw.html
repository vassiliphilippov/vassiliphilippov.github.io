jackstraw

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒækˌstrɔ / (AmE), / ˈdʒakˌstrɔː / (BrE)
A thin strip of wood or plastic used in the game of jackstraws, where players try to remove pieces from a pile without disturbing others.
thin stick used in the game jackstraws

Examples

She carefully picked up the jackstraw without moving any others in the pile.
The old game set includes dozens of wooden jackstraws and a storage box.
Children enjoyed playing with brightly colored jackstraws on rainy afternoons.
