Cercis occidentalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜːrsɪs ˌɑksɪˈdɛntəlɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɜːsɪs ˌɒksɪˈdɛntəlɪs / (BrE)
a shrub native to the western United States, known for its pink or crimson flowers and tendency to form thickets
western US flowering shrub

Examples

The Cercis occidentalis blooms with bright pink flowers every spring in California's hills.
Gardeners often use Cercis occidentalis to create natural thickets in drought-prone landscapes.
The vibrant flowers of Cercis occidentalis attract bees and butterflies to the garden.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Cercis' (the redbud genus), 'occidentalis' meaning 'western'.
