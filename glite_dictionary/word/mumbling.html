mumbling

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌm.blɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmʌm.blɪŋ / (BrE)
speech or words spoken in a way that is not clear enough to be easily understood; indistinct enunciation.
unclear or indistinct speech

Examples

His constant mumbling made it difficult to understand what he was saying.
During the ceremony, there was a lot of nervous mumbling from the participants.
I couldn't follow her instructions because of her quiet mumbling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutter - to speak in a low, unclear voice
  • Antonym: articulation - clear and distinct speech

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'mumble', from Middle English *mommelen*, probably of imitative origin.
