stockholding

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑkˌhoʊldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstɒkˌhəʊldɪŋ / (BrE)
the stocks or shares owned by an individual or organization at a particular time
stocks or shares owned

Examples

He decided to sell some of his stockholding in the company before the merger.
The new regulations require firms to disclose their stockholdings annually.
Her stockholding makes her one of the largest investors in the business.

Synset Examples

sell stockholdings he has in corporations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shareholding - the shares owned by an individual or organization
  • Synonym: stake - a share or interest in a business or investment

Etymology

Formed from 'stock' (ownership shares in a company) + 'holding' (something owned or held).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɑːkˌhoʊldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstɒkˌhəʊldɪŋ / (BrE)
the state or fact of owning shares (stock) in a company or corporation
ownership of shares in a company

Examples

His stockholding in the company increased after the merger.
The law limits foreign stockholding in certain industries.
Institutional stockholding can influence company decisions significantly.

Synset Examples

prohibition of unrestricted intercorporate stockholding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shareholding - the state of owning shares in a company

Etymology

From 'stock' (share in a company) + 'holding' (the act of having or owning).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
