czworniak

1. NOUN

/ t͡svɔrˈɲak / (AmE), / t͡svɔːrˈɲak / (BrE)
A Polish mead made with three parts water to one part honey.
type of Polish mead

Examples

The historical drink known as czworniak is enjoyed at many Polish festivals.
You can find czworniak alongside other traditional meads in specialty liquor stores.
He explained that czworniak is milder and less sweet than stronger varieties of mead.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: dwójniak - Polish mead with one part honey to one part water
  • Degree Higher: trójniak - Polish mead with two parts water to one part honey

Etymology

From Polish 'czworniak', derived from 'czworo' (fourfold), referring to the four-part mix (one honey, three water).
