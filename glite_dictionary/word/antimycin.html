antimycin

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tiˈmaɪ.sɪn / (AmE), / ˌan.tɪˈmʌɪ.sɪn / (BrE)
a crystalline antibiotic substance active against various fungi, used especially in scientific research and as a fungicide
antibiotic substance active against fungi
medical

Examples

The laboratory uses antimycin to study mitochondrial function in cells.
Exposure to antimycin inhibits the growth of certain fungal species.
Researchers treated the samples with antimycin to prevent fungal contamination.

Etymology

From anti- (against) + mycin (from Greek mykes, meaning fungus, via 'mycin' used for antibiotics derived from Streptomyces bacteria).
