filet

1. NOUN

/ fɪˈleɪ / (AmE), / ˈfɪlɪt / (BrE)
A thick slice of boneless meat, especially beef, cut from the tenderloin.
boneless steak from beef tenderloin

Examples

The chef recommended the filet for its tenderness and rich flavor.
He ordered a grilled filet with a side of vegetables at the restaurant.
Many high-end steakhouses feature filet as a signature dish on their menu.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fillet - Alternative spelling for a boneless cut of meat or fish

Etymology

From French 'filet' meaning 'small thread' or 'strip'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07675810-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ fɪˈleɪ / (AmE), / ˈfɪl.eɪ / (BrE)
a boneless slice or side of a fish, typically cut lengthwise from the backbone
a boneless slice of fish

Examples

The chef carefully removed the bones to produce a perfect filet of salmon.
She pan-fried the trout filet with a squeeze of lemon for extra flavor.
He ordered a grilled filet of sole for his main course at the restaurant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fillet - Alternate spelling with same culinary meaning

Etymology

From French *filet* (a thread, strip), diminutive of *fil* (thread).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07670946-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ fɪˈleɪ / (AmE), / ˈfɪlɪt / (BrE)
to cut meat or fish into boneless strips or pieces called filets
cut meat/fish into filets

Examples

He skillfully fileted the trout before cooking it.
It takes practice to filet a fish cleanly without wasting any meat.
She watched the chef filet the salmon into perfect strips.

Synset Examples

filet the fish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bone - to remove the bones from meat or fish

Etymology

From French *filet* (a thread, strip, or filet of meat).

lace with a square mesh

Examples

She learned how to make filet by following her grandmother's patterns.
