woolgathering

1. ADJ

/ ˈwʊlˌɡæðərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwʊlˌɡæðərɪŋ / (BrE)
in a dreamy or absent-minded state, often lost in one's own thoughts; given to daydreaming
absent-minded or dreamy

Examples

She spent the afternoon woolgathering instead of doing her work.
His woolgathering expression told me he wasn't listening.
During meetings, he is often lost in woolgathering and misses key points.

Synset Examples

a woolgathering moment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absent-minded - lacking attention; lost in thought
  • Synonym: daydreaming - lost in pleasant thoughts
  • Antonym: focused - concentrating on a task or thought

Etymology

Originally from the literal act of gathering loose wool from bushes; figuratively, from the 16th century, referring to aimless wandering of thought.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwʊlˌɡæðərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwʊlˌɡæðərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of indulging in idle daydreaming or fanciful thoughts, often with a lack of practical purpose
idle daydreaming or fantasy

Examples

During the long meeting, his woolgathering made him miss several important points.
She was caught woolgathering and didn't hear the teacher call her name.
Instead of working on his assignment, he spent hours in woolgathering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daydreaming - the activity of being absorbed in pleasant thoughts that distract from the present
  • Antonym: concentration - focus of mental effort on a particular task or idea

Etymology

From the literal activity of gathering stray bits of wool in fields, later metaphorically applied to wandering thoughts.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05777723-n
Synset POS: n
