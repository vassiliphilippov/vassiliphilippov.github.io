frown

1. NOUN

/ fraʊn / (AmE), / fraʊn / (BrE)
A facial expression made by bringing the eyebrows together and wrinkling the forehead, showing dislike, displeasure, or concern.
facial expression of displeasure

Examples

A deep frown appeared on his face when he heard the news.
She gave him a puzzled frown before answering the question.
The teacher's frown made the students fall silent.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smile - a pleased, kind, or amused facial expression
  • Synonym: scowl - an angry or bad-tempered expression

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English 'frounen' (to wrinkle the forehead).

2. VERB

/ fraʊn / (AmE), / fraʊn / (BrE)
to wrinkle one's forehead and bring the eyebrows together, often as an expression of anger, displeasure, or concentration
wrinkle forehead to show displeasure or anger

Examples

She began to frown when she heard the disappointing news.
He frowned in concentration as he tried to solve the puzzle.
Please don't frown at me just because I made a mistake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scowl - to look at someone in an angry or disapproving way
  • Antonym: smile - to make a happy or pleased facial expression by curving the lips upward

Etymology

From Middle English 'frounen', probably from Old French 'frognier' (to snort, scowl).

Synset POS: v
