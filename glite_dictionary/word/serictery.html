serictery

1. NOUN

/ səˈrɪk.tə.ri / (AmE), / səˈrɪk.təri / (BrE)
a silk-producing gland in insects (especially silkworms) or spiders
silk-producing gland in insects/spiders

Examples

The silkworm secretes threads of silk from its serictery during cocoon formation.
Scientists studied the structure of the spider’s serictery to better understand silk production.
Damage to the serictery can inhibit an insect’s ability to spin silk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silk gland - gland in arthropods that produces silk

Etymology

From Latin 'sericum' (silk) + Greek 'thēra' (gland).
