quaver

1. NOUN

/ ˈkweɪvər / (AmE), / ˈkweɪvə / (BrE)
a musical note with the time value of an eighth of a whole note
eighth note in music
british

Examples

The melody began with a sharp quaver followed by a series of longer notes.
She counted each quaver carefully as she practiced her scales on the piano.
The sheet music showed a passage filled with rapid quavers requiring nimble fingers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eighth note - a musical note having the time value of an eighth of a whole note (US term)

2. NOUN

/ ˈkweɪvər / (AmE), / ˈkweɪvə / (BrE)
A tremulous or wavering sound, especially in a person's voice, caused by nervousness or emotion.
tremulous, wavering sound

Examples

There was a noticeable quaver in her voice as she spoke to the crowd.
The old man's quaver made his story even more moving.
With a quaver in his tone, he began his emotional speech.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tremor - a slight shaking or trembling movement or sound
  • Synonym: vibration - a rapid rhythmic movement or sound

Etymology

From Middle English 'quaven', probably of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07405457-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈkweɪvər / (AmE), / ˈkweɪvə / (BrE)
to sing or play with a trembling, trilling, or wavering sound, often by alternating rapidly with the note above or below
sing or play with a trembling or trilling sound

Examples

The soprano began to quaver on the high note, giving the melody a haunting quality.
He tried to quaver the last note, but his voice was too weak.
With skilled fingers, the violinist could quaver between notes, adding expressive depth to the music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to vibrate - to move or sound with slight rapid motion or variation
  • Synonym: tremble - to shake involuntarily, often used for sound or voice

Etymology

From Middle English 'quaven' (to shake, tremble).

4. VERB

/ ˈkweɪvər / (AmE), / ˈkweɪvə / (BrE)
(of a voice or sound) to produce a trembling, unsteady, or wavering tone, especially due to emotion or weakness.
produce an unsteady or trembling sound

Examples

Her voice began to quaver with emotion as she spoke about her childhood.
The old man's words quavered as he struggled to remember the details of the story.
The note quavered in the cold night air, barely audible above the wind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tremble - to shake involuntarily, especially from emotion or weakness
  • Synonym: wobble - to move or sound unsteadily from side to side
  • Antonym: steady - firmly fixed, supported, or balanced; not shaking or moving

Etymology

From Middle English quaven (to tremble), probably of imitative origin.
