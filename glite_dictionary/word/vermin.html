vermin

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɜrmɪn / (AmE), / ˈvɜːmɪn / (BrE)
Small animals or insects that are harmful or troublesome, especially those that carry disease or cause damage, such as rats or cockroaches.
harmful small animals or insects (pests)

Examples

Cereals must be stored carefully to protect them from vermin.
The farmer set traps to keep vermin out of the barn.
Authorities launched a campaign to control vermin in the city.

Synset Examples

cereals must be protected from mice and other vermin
he examined the child's head for vermin
boys in the village have probably been shooting vermin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pest - an insect or small animal harmful to crops or property

Etymology

From Old French vermin (worms, vermin), from Latin vermis (worm).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɜrmɪn / (AmE), / ˈvɜːmɪn / (BrE)
an irritating, obnoxious, or despicable person (used as an insult)
obnoxious or despicable person (derogatory)
offensive

Examples

After what he did to his friends, some people now consider him vermin.
She called her cheating ex-boyfriend vermin and never spoke to him again.
The politician was denounced as vermin by his harshest critics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scoundrel - a dishonest or unscrupulous person
  • Synonym: villain - a cruelly malicious person

Etymology

From Middle English 'vermin', from Old French, from Latin 'vermis' meaning 'worm'.
