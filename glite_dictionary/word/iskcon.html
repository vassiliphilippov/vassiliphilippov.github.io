ISKCON

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪs.kɑn / (AmE), / ˈaɪs.kɒn / (BrE)
The International Society for Krishna Consciousness, a Hindu religious movement founded in the United States in 1966, known for its devotion to Krishna, public chanting of mantras, and distinctive lifestyle practices such as vegetarianism and meditation.
Hindu religious movement founded in 1966

Examples

After joining ISKCON, she adopted a vegetarian lifestyle and participated in daily devotional practices.
The influence of ISKCON can be seen in many temples and cultural events around the world.
Many ISKCON members engage in joyful chanting and distribute religious literature in city centers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hare Krishna - A member of ISKCON or the religious movement itself

Etymology

Acronym of International Society for Krishna Consciousness, founded in 1966 in New York.
