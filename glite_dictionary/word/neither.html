neither

1. PRON

/ ˈniðər / (AmE), / ˈnaɪðə / (BrE)
Not one nor the other of two people or things; not either.
not one or the other; not either

Examples

Neither of the answers was correct, so the teacher explained the solution again.
Neither movie appealed to me, so I stayed home instead.
Neither of them wanted to take responsibility for the broken window.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: either - one or the other of two
  • Antonym: both - the two together; all of the two

Etymology

From Old English nāhwæther (not either), from nā (not, no) + hwæther (which of two, whether).
