pentose

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnˌtoʊs / (AmE), / ˈpɛntəʊs / (BrE)
a monosaccharide sugar composed of five carbon atoms in its molecule
a five-carbon sugar

Examples

Ribose is a pentose sugar that forms part of the backbone of RNA.
Many nucleic acids contain a pentose as their sugar component.
Scientists analyzed the metabolic pathway involving several pentoses in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: hexose - a monosaccharide with six carbon atoms
  • Synonym: monosaccharide - the simplest form of sugar; a single sugar molecule

Etymology

From Greek 'pente' (five) + '-ose' (sugar).
