boll

1. NOUN

/ boʊl / (AmE), / bəʊl / (BrE)
the rounded seed capsule of a cotton or flax plant, containing seeds and fiber
seed capsule of a cotton/flax plant

Examples

The cotton boll is harvested when it bursts open in late summer.
Farmers inspected the bolls for signs of insect damage.
A single flax boll can contain several seeds ready for harvesting.

Etymology

From Middle English *bolle* (a round object, bowl), possibly from Old Norse *bolla* (a round vessel).
