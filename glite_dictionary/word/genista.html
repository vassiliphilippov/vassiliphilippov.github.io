Genista

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈnɪstə / (AmE), / dʒɪˈnɪstə / (BrE)
A genus of mainly deciduous shrubs or small trees native to the Mediterranean region and western Asia, commonly known as broom.
genus of Mediterranean shrubs (broom)

Examples

Several species of Genista are cultivated for their vibrant yellow flowers.
The hills were covered with wild Genista shrubs in full bloom.
Researchers studied the drought resistance of various Genista species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broom - a common name for several shrubs of the genus Genista and related genera

Etymology

From Latin 'genista' (broom plant).

2. NOUN

/ dʒəˈnɪstə / (AmE), / dʒɪˈnɪstə / (BrE)
a genus of chiefly deciduous shrubs or small trees of the Mediterranean region and western Asia, commonly known as brooms
genus of Mediterranean brooms

Examples

The Genista shrubs bloomed with bright yellow flowers in early spring.
Several species within the Genista genus are cultivated for ornamental purposes.
Botanists studied the distribution of Genista in the Mediterranean region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broom - a shrub belonging to the genus Genista or related genera; also, the type of brush made from its twigs

Etymology

From Latin 'genista' (broom), the name for shrubs in this genus.
