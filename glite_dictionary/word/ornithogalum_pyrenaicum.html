Ornithogalum pyrenaicum

1. NOUN

/ ɔrˌnɪθəˈɡæləm paɪˌrɛnˈeɪɪkəm / (AmE), / ɔːˌnɪθəˈɡæləm paɪˌrɛˈneɪɪkəm / (BrE)
A species of star of Bethlehem native to Europe, known for its edible young shoots.
species of star of Bethlehem plant

Examples

The young shoots of Ornithogalum pyrenaicum are often harvested and eaten as a delicacy.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Ornithogalum pyrenaicum for its attractive flowers and edible stems.
You can find Ornithogalum pyrenaicum growing wild in woodlands across parts of Western Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bath asparagus - an alternative common name for Ornithogalum pyrenaicum

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Ornithogalum' meaning 'bird's milk' and species 'pyrenaicum' referring to the Pyrenees mountains.
