Prunus nigra

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruː.nəs ˈnaɪ.ɡrə / (AmE), / ˈpruː.nəs ˈnɪɡ.rə / (BrE)
A small deciduous tree native to northeastern North America, valued for its oblong, orange-red fruit.
species of North American plum tree

Examples

The Prunus nigra blooms early in the spring, producing fragrant white flowers.
Orchards in Canada often include Prunus nigra because of its hardy nature.
The oblong fruit of the Prunus nigra is prized for making jams and jellies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Canadian plum - common name for Prunus nigra
  • Degree Higher: plum - a fruit produced by trees of the genus Prunus

Etymology

From Latin: 'prunus' (plum tree) + 'nigra' (black).
