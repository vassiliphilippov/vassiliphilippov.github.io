claxon

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæksən / (AmE), / ˈklæksən / (BrE)
a type of loud electric horn formerly used on motor vehicles, known for its distinctive sound
loud automobile horn (historical)

Examples

The antique car still had its original claxon mounted on the front.
He pressed the claxon, and its blaring sound startled everyone nearby.
Old films often feature cars with the unmistakable wail of a claxon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horn - a device that makes a loud warning sound, especially on vehicles

Etymology

From Klaxon, a trademark name for a brand of horn popular in early automobiles.
