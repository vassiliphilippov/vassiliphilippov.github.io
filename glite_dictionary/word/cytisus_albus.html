Cytisus albus

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈtaɪsəs ˈælbəs / (AmE), / sɪˈtɪsəs ˈal.bəs / (BrE)
A low European shrub of the broom family, with trifoliate leaves and yellowish-white flowers.
a low European broom shrub

Examples

The garden featured a beautiful specimen of Cytisus albus near the stone path.
Cytisus albus is well-suited to sandy, well-drained soils.
Botanists studied the flowering patterns of Cytisus albus in its native habitat.

Etymology

From Latin: Cytisus, a genus of shrubs, and albus, meaning 'white'.
