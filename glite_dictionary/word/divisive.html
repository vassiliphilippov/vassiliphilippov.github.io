divisive

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈvaɪsɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈvɪsɪv / (BrE)
Tending to cause disagreement, hostility, or division among people.
causing disagreement or division

Examples

The politician's divisive remarks fueled further debate within the community.
Social media can sometimes play a divisive role in public opinion.
Her divisive tactics alienated many potential supporters.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unifying - bringing people together or creating unity
  • Synonym: contentious - causing or likely to cause an argument; controversial

Etymology

From Latin 'dividere' meaning 'to divide'.
