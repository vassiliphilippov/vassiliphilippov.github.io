Grewia asiatica

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡriːwiə eɪˈʃiːətɪkə / (AmE), / ˈɡriːwiə æˈsiːatɪkə / (BrE)
A drought-resistant Asiatic shrub or small tree bearing small, pleasantly acidic, red edible fruits, often used in making sherbets.
drought-resistant shrub with edible red fruits

Examples

The Grewia asiatica fruit is prized in India for its refreshing flavor and health benefits.
Farmers appreciate Grewia asiatica for its resilience to drought and its productive harvests.
During the summer, locals enjoy chilled sherbets made from Grewia asiatica berries.

Etymology

Genus name 'Grewia' from botanist Nehemiah Grew; 'asiatica' indicates Asian origin.
