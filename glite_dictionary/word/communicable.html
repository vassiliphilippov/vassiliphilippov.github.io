communicable

1. ADJ

/ kəˈmjunɪkəbəl / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːnɪkəbl / (BrE)
(of ideas, information, or qualities) able to be easily transmitted, shared, or imparted to others.
easily transmitted or shared

Examples

The professor presented complex theories in a highly communicable manner.
Her enthusiasm made even abstract concepts communicable to the audience.
Clear language ensures that your message is communicable across cultures.

Synset Examples

communicable ideas

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incommunicable - not able to be communicated or shared

Etymology

From Latin communicabilis, from communicare (to share).

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ kəˈmjunɪkəbəl / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːnɪkəbl / (BrE)
(of a disease) able to be transmitted from one person or organism to another, usually by infection.
(of disease) able to be transmitted by infection

Examples

Measles is a highly communicable disease that can spread quickly in unvaccinated populations.
The hospital has strict procedures for handling patients with communicable infections.
Some illnesses are not communicable and cannot be passed from person to person.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infectious - (of a disease) liable to be transmitted to people, organisms, etc.; contagious.
  • Synonym: contagious - (of a disease) spread by contact; infectious.
  • Antonym: noncommunicable - (of a disease) not transmissible directly from one person to another.

Etymology

From Latin communicabilis (that can be communicated, imparted).
