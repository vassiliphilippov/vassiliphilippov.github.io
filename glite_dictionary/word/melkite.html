Melkite

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛlˌkaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɛlkaɪt / (BrE)
A member of an Eastern Christian church, particularly associated with the patriarchates of Alexandria, Antioch, or Jerusalem, who accepts the authority of the Byzantine emperor and patriarch.
member of an Eastern Christian church

Examples

The Syrian Melkite community celebrates Easter according to the Julian calendar.
Many Melkites emigrated to the Americas during the 19th century.
He studied the liturgy of the Melkite Church in detail.

Etymology

From Medieval Greek Melkitēs, from Syriac malkāyā (royalist), referring to those who accepted the authority of the Byzantine emperor (the 'king').

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛlkaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɛlkaɪt / (BrE)
a member of an Eastern Christian church, especially in Egypt or Syria, who follows the Orthodox faith as defined by the Council of Chalcedon in 451 and accepted by the Byzantine emperor.
Eastern Christian adhering to Orthodox Chalcedonian tradition

Examples

The Melkite community in Syria celebrates its unique liturgical traditions.
Many Melkites maintained loyalty to the Byzantine emperor after the schism.
She studied the history of Melkite Christians in the Middle East.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eastern Orthodox - a member of the Eastern Orthodox Christian Church

Etymology

From Late Greek *Melkī́tēs*, from Syriac *malkāyā* ('imperial'), referring to loyalty to the Byzantine emperor.
