Phoenician

1. ADJ

/ fəˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / fəˈniːʃən / (BrE)
relating to Phoenicia, its people, or their language, culture, or history
of or relating to ancient Phoenicia

Examples

The museum displayed several Phoenician artifacts dating back to the first millennium BCE.
She studied Phoenician trade routes during her time as an archaeology student.
The Phoenician alphabet greatly influenced later writing systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Phoenicia - ancient region on the eastern Mediterranean

Etymology

From Latin Phoeniceus, from Greek Phoiníkē (Phoenicia).

2. NOUN

/ fəˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / fəˈniːʃən / (BrE)
an extinct Semitic language spoken by the ancient Phoenicians, who dominated trade in the Mediterranean world from about 1200 to 300 BCE.
extinct Semitic language of Phoenicians

Examples

Scholars have studied Phoenician inscriptions to better understand ancient Mediterranean trade.
The Phoenician language used one of the earliest known alphabets.
Very few people today can read Phoenician texts without specialized training.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hebrew - an ancient Semitic language of the Hebrews
  • Synonym: Aramaic - a Semitic language closely related to Phoenician and Hebrew

Etymology

From Latin Phoenīcius, from Greek Phoiníkēs (the Phoenicians).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07002133-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ fəˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / fəˈniːʃən / (BrE)
a member of an ancient Semitic people who lived in the coastal region of the eastern Mediterranean and dominated trade during the first millennium B.C.
member of an ancient Mediterranean trading people

Examples

The Phoenician civilization was renowned for its seafaring and trade networks.
Archaeologists have recently discovered Phoenician artifacts along the coast of Spain.
Many Phoenician settlements later became important cities in the Mediterranean region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Carthaginian - a member of the ancient people of Carthage, descended from Phoenician settlers

Etymology

From Latin Phoenicianus, from Greek Phoiníkēs, referring to the ancient people of Phoenicia.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09661838-n
Synset POS: n
