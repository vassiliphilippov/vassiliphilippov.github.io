neoclassicist

1. ADJ

/ ˌnioʊˈklæsɪsɪst / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊˈklæsɪsɪst / (BrE)
relating to or advocating neoclassicism, especially in art, literature, or architecture
of or advocating neoclassicism

Examples

Her neoclassicist approach to sculpture emphasized harmony and balance over modern abstraction.
The gallery's new exhibit highlights neoclassicist trends in contemporary architecture.
Many art critics described the painting as having a distinctly neoclassicist style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neoclassical - relating to neoclassicism
  • Antonym: romanticist - relating to or advocating romanticism

Etymology

Formed from 'neo-' (new) and 'classicist' (adherent of classicism), referencing revival of classical styles.

2. NOUN

/ ˌniː.oʊˈklæs.ə.sɪst / (AmE), / ˌniː.əʊˈklæs.ɪ.sɪst / (BrE)
A person who supports or practices neoclassicism, especially in art, literature, or architecture.
an advocate of neoclassicism

Examples

As a prominent neoclassicist, she promoted a return to classical simplicity in design.
Many 18th-century artists were influenced by the ideas of the neoclassicist movement.
The museum featured works from several noted neoclassicists of the period.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: classicist - a person who studies or admires classical antiquity

Etymology

Formed from 'neo-' (new) + 'classicist', relating to neoclassicism.
