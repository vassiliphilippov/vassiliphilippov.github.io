ambulant

1. ADJ

/ ˈæmbjələnt / (AmE), / ˈæmbjʊlənt / (BrE)
able to walk about; not confined to bed, especially due to illness or injury.
able to walk about (esp. in medicine)
medical

Examples

After surgery, the doctor was pleased to see the patient was ambulant within a few days.
Most ambulant patients were discharged from the clinic by the end of the week.
Access ramps are essential for both wheelchair users and ambulant individuals with limited mobility.

Synset Examples

the patient is ambulatory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambulatory - able to walk; not bedridden

Etymology

From Latin ambulans, ambulant-, present participle of ambulare (to walk).
