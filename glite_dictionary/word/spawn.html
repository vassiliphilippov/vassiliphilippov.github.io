spawn

1. NOUN

/ spɑn / (AmE), / spɔːn / (BrE)
the mass of eggs laid by fish, amphibians, or molluscs, often in water
eggs of fish, amphibians, or molluscs

Examples

Each spring, the river is filled with the spawn of salmon returning to their birthplace.
Scientists collected samples of frog spawn from the pond for their research.
The water's surface shimmered with the fresh spawn laid by the molluscs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roe - eggs of fish, especially when used as food

Etymology

From Middle English 'spawn', perhaps from Anglo-Norman *espaundre, alteration of Old French 'espandre' (to spread).

2. VERB

/ spɔn / (AmE), / spɔːn / (BrE)
(of fish, amphibians, etc.) to lay eggs in water.
to lay eggs (fish, amphibians)

Examples

Every spring, the trout swim upstream to spawn in the shallow gravel beds.
Scientists observed hundreds of frogs returning to the pond to spawn after the first rainfall.
If water conditions are right, goldfish may spawn several times in a season.

Synset Examples

The salmon swims upstream to spawn

Etymology

Of unknown origin, possibly related to Old French *espandre* (to spread).

3. VERB

/ spɑn / (AmE), / spɔːn / (BrE)
to produce or generate something, especially suddenly or in large numbers; to give rise to.
produce or give rise to

Examples

The success of the film spawned numerous sequels and spin-offs.
Rumors quickly spawned panic throughout the small town.
Her unique approach spawns innovative solutions to complex problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to generate - to produce or bring into existence
  • Synonym: trigger - to cause something to begin or happen
  • Antonym: to destroy - to put an end to something's existence

Etymology

Originally referring to the eggs of fish; from Middle French 'espaundre', from Latin 'expandere' (to spread out).
