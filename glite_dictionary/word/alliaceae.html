Alliaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌæl.iˈeɪ.si.i / (AmE), / ˌæl.iˈeɪ.siː.iː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants that includes the onion, garlic, leek, and related genera, sometimes considered a subfamily of Liliaceae
family of flowering plants (onions, garlic, etc.)

Examples

The Alliaceae family includes many familiar vegetables such as onions and garlic.
Several botanists argue that Alliaceae should be recognized as distinct from Liliaceae.
Chives and shallots are also members of the Alliaceae.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Allium (onion) + standard botanical suffix -aceae (family).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæliˈeɪsiːˌiː / (AmE), / ˌæliˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, formerly classified as a subfamily within Liliaceae, including the genus Allium (onions, garlic, etc.), but now often recognized as a separate family in some taxonomies
plant family including onions and garlic

Examples

The Alliaceae family is known for its characteristic pungent-smelling bulbs.
Botanists once included garlic in Liliaceae, but it is now placed in Alliaceae by some classifications.
Several common vegetables, such as onions and chives, belong to the Alliaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Allium - genus including onions, garlic, leeks, and shallots

Etymology

From Latin *Allium* (genus name for garlic), with the suffix –aceae indicating a plant family.
