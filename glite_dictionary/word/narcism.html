narcism

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːrˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈnɑː.sɪ.zəm / (BrE)
Excessive self-interest and admiration of oneself; self-love that shuts out others.
excessive self-admiration

Examples

His narcism made it difficult for him to form genuine relationships with others.
The artist's narcism was evident in his many self-portraits.
Many people confuse confidence with narcism, but there is an important difference.

Synset Examples

self-love that shut out everyone else

Related Terms

  • Synonym: narcissism - Excessive self-love and self-centeredness

Etymology

From German 'Narzissmus' and Greek myth of Narcissus; variant of 'narcissism'.
