acknowledged

1. ADJ

/ əkˈnɑlɪdʒd / (AmE), / əkˈnɒlɪdʒd / (BrE)
recognized as being true, accepted, or officially known; generally admitted or accepted by others.
recognized or accepted as true

Examples

She is the acknowledged expert on Italian art in the museum.
His acknowledged mistake helped resolve the longstanding dispute.
The team won the award as the acknowledged leader in innovation.

Synset Examples

the acknowledged leader of the community
a woman of acknowledged accomplishments
his acknowledged error

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admitted - accepted or acknowledged as true
  • Synonym: recognized - officially identified as something or someone
  • Antonym: disputed - questioned or called into doubt

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'acknowledge', from Middle English 'acnowlechen', from Old English 'oncnāwan' (to recognize).

2. ADJ

/ əkˈnɑlɪdʒd / (AmE), / əkˈnɒlɪdʒd / (BrE)
Recognized or accepted as true, valid, or existing by most people or by general agreement.
generally accepted or recognized

Examples

She is an acknowledged expert in her field.
His contribution to the project is widely acknowledged.
The novel became an acknowledged classic over the years.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unacknowledged - not recognized or accepted by people
  • Synonym: recognized - officially accepted or approved as valid or existing

Etymology

From the verb 'acknowledge', which is from Old English 'oncnāwan' (to recognize, know).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01924863-s
Synset POS: s
