Palatinate

1. PROPN

/ pæˈlætɪnˌeɪt / (AmE), / pəˈlætɪnət / (BrE)
a historical territory in southwestern Germany that was formerly ruled by counts palatine; also, the region associated with this rule.
historic region of southwestern Germany

Examples

The Palatinate played a crucial role in the Thirty Years' War.
He traced his ancestry back to the Palatinate region of Germany.
Many families emigrated from the Palatinate in the eighteenth century.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'palatinatus', from 'palatinus' (palatine, relating to a palace or official rank).
