Liquidambar

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪkwɪdˈæmbɑr / (AmE), / ˌlɪkwɪdˈæmbɑː / (BrE)
a genus of deciduous trees known as sweet gum, with star-shaped leaves and spiny fruit clusters, native to warm regions of the Americas and Asia
a genus of trees; sweet gum

Examples

The leaves of the Liquidambar turn brilliant colors in autumn.
A large Liquidambar was the centerpiece of the botanical garden exhibit.
Gardeners often plant Liquidambar trees for their vibrant fall foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet gum - a common name for trees of the Liquidambar genus

Etymology

From Latin 'liquidus' (liquid) + 'ambar' (amber), referring to the aromatic resin produced by the tree.

2. NOUN

/ ˌlɪkwɪdˈæmbɑr / (AmE), / ˌlɪkwɪdˈæmbɑː / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous trees known as sweet gum, with star-shaped leaves and spiky fruits, native to warm regions of the Americas and Asia.
genus of deciduous sweet gum trees

Examples

The Liquidambar trees along the avenue turn brilliant shades of red and orange in autumn.
A botanist identified several Liquidambar species during her survey of the wetland forest.
The hardened sap of the Liquidambar, often called storax, has been used in traditional medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet gum - common name for trees of the genus Liquidambar

Etymology

From Latin 'liquidus' (liquid) and 'ambar' (amber), referring to the aromatic resin they produce.
