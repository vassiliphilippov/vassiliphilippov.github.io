continuo

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtɪn.ju.oʊ / (AmE), / kənˈtɪn.ju.əʊ / (BrE)
(in Baroque music) a continuous bass part, usually played by a keyboard or other chordal instrument along with a bass instrument, with numbers indicating the chords to be played.
continuous bass part in Baroque music

Examples

The harpsichordist played the continuo part throughout the entire cantata.
A cellist and organist together performed the continuo in the Baroque ensemble.
Understanding how to read figured bass is essential for any musician handling the continuo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basso continuo - continuous bass part in Baroque music with figures indicating chords

Etymology

From Italian "continuo" (continuous); short for "basso continuo".
