freshness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɛʃ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfrɛʃ.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being new, pure, or clean, and not stale, spoiled, or deteriorated
quality of being fresh or not stale

Examples

The freshness of the morning dew made the garden look beautiful.
Many chefs value the freshness of their ingredients above all else.
She opened the window to enjoy the freshness of the cool breeze.

Synset Examples

she loved the freshness of newly baked bread
the freshness of the air revived him

Related Terms

  • Antonym: staleness - the quality of being old or no longer fresh

Etymology

From fresh + -ness, forming a noun indicating the state or quality of being fresh.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfrɛʃ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfrɛʃ.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being new, original, or surprising; originality or novelty
originality; quality of being new or novel

Examples

The freshness of her ideas impressed everyone at the meeting.
A writer's greatest challenge is to maintain freshness in every story.
The exhibition was praised for the freshness of its approach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: novelty - the quality of being new, original, or unusual
  • Antonym: staleness - the quality of being unoriginal or lacking freshness

Etymology

From fresh + -ness, based on Middle English 'fressh', from Old French 'fraîche', from Latin 'friscus'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈfrɛʃ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfrɛʃ.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being alert, lively, and full of energy; feeling refreshed
the state of being alert and refreshed

Examples

After a full night of sleep, she awoke with remarkable freshness and enthusiasm for the day.
The mountain air gave him a feeling of freshness and renewed energy.
Meditation helped restore his mental freshness after a long day at work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alertness - the quality of being quick to notice and respond
  • Antonym: weariness - the state of being very tired

Etymology

From 'fresh' + '-ness', old French 'frais' (cool, fresh), ultimately from Latin 'friscus' (fresh).
