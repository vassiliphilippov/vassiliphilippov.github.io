Vespula maculata

1. NOUN

/ vɛsˈpjuːlə ˌmækjʊˈleɪtə / (AmE), / vɛsˈpjuːlə ˌmakjʊˈleɪtə / (BrE)
A species of hornet native to North America, also known as the bald-faced hornet, noted for its black and white coloring and large paper nests.
North American hornet species

Examples

A large Vespula maculata nest hung from the tree near the edge of the forest.
Some people refer to the Vespula maculata as the bald-faced hornet due to its distinctive markings.
If you disturb a Vespula maculata nest, the hornets can become very aggressive.

Etymology

From Latin: 'vespula' (a diminutive of 'vespa', meaning 'wasp') and 'maculata' (spotted).
