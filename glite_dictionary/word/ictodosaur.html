ictodosaur

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈtoʊdəˌsɔr / (AmE), / ɪkˈtəʊdəˌsɔː / (BrE)
a member of an extinct group of mammal-like reptiles that are intermediate in form between the therapsids and the most primitive true mammals.
extinct reptile between therapsids and primitive mammals

Examples

Fossils of the ictodosaur provide crucial evidence for the evolutionary link between reptiles and mammals.
Some paleontologists believe that the ictodosaur was among the earliest creatures with transitional mammalian features.
The discovery of a complete ictodosaur skeleton helped to clarify the relationships among prehistoric vertebrates.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek 'iktis' (weasel) + 'sauros' (lizard), used in scientific taxonomy.
