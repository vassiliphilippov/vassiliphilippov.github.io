al-Ma'unah

1. NOUN

/ æl məˈuːnə / (AmE), / æl məˈuːnə / (BrE)
a radical Islamist group in Malaysia, formed by disaffected middle-class professionals, aiming to overthrow the government by violent means and establish an Islamic state.
Malaysian radical Islamist group

Examples

The Malaysian authorities launched a crackdown on al-Ma'unah after their failed raid in 2000.
Membership in al-Ma'unah was restricted to individuals who shared the group's radical ideology.
Security forces foiled an attack planned by al-Ma'unah operatives in the northern region.
