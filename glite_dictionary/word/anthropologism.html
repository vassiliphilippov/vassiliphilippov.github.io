anthropologism

1. NOUN

/ ˌænθrəˈpɑlədʒɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌanθrəˈpɒlədʒɪzəm / (BrE)
The viewpoint that human nature is entirely shaped and determined by the surrounding culture, allowing no independent human agency.
belief that culture fully shapes human nature

Examples

Many critics oppose anthropologism because it denies any universal aspects of human nature.
The philosopher's essay is a defense of anthropologism in explaining societal behaviors.
Debates about anthropologism highlight tensions between cultural determinism and the idea of individual freedom.

Synset Examples

This is where he locates his anthropologism in a wider naturalism and materialism in response (in part) to criticism that it was not sufficiently distinguished from the immanentist Hegelianism that was a dominant feature of Left Hegelianism.

Etymology

From anthropology + -ism, patterned after philosophical terms denoting viewpoints or doctrines.
