encyclopaedic

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˌsaɪkləˈpiːdɪk / (AmE), / ɪnˌsʌɪkləˈpiːdɪk / (BrE)
containing comprehensive information; covering many subjects in great detail, like an encyclopedia
comprehensive; covering many subjects

Examples

She has an encyclopaedic knowledge of American history.
The guide is encyclopaedic in its coverage of world art.
His answers were so detailed and encyclopaedic that everyone was impressed.

Synset Examples

encyclopedic knowledge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comprehensive - including all or nearly all elements or aspects of something
  • Antonym: narrow - covering a limited area; not broad or wide in scope

Etymology

From encyclopedia + -ic, patterned after French encyclopédique or Modern Latin encyclopaedicus.
