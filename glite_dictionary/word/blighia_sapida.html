Blighia sapida

1. NOUN

/ ˈblaɪjiə səˈpidə / (AmE), / ˈblɪɡiə səˈpiːdə / (BrE)
A tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa, widely cultivated for its fragrant flowers and edible, colorful fruit known as ackee, introduced to the Caribbean in the 18th century.
tropical tree known for ackee fruit

Examples

The Blighia sapida tree is prized both for its shade and its culinary value in Caribbean cuisine.
Harvesting the fruit of Blighia sapida requires knowledge of when it is safe to eat.
Botanists studied the spread of Blighia sapida from West Africa to Jamaica.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ackee - the edible fruit of Blighia sapida

Etymology

Named after Captain William Bligh, who introduced the species to Jamaica; from genus name Blighia, species sapida meaning 'savory, edible'.
