bobtail

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːbˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈbɒbˌteɪl / (BrE)
A short or shortened tail, especially of certain animals, either naturally or as a result of docking.
short or shortened animal tail

Examples

The dog was born with a bobtail rather than a long tail.
Some breeds of cattle are easily recognized by their bobtail.
He adopted a cat with a unique bobtail after visiting the shelter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dock - to cut short an animal's tail

Etymology

From 'bob' (to cut short) + 'tail'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːb.teɪl / (AmE), / ˈbɒb.teɪl / (BrE)
a large sheepdog, especially the Old English Sheepdog, with a shaggy bluish-grey and white coat and a short tail
a sheepdog with a short tail and shaggy coat

Examples

The farm was guarded by a friendly bobtail that loved to play with children.
Her bobtail won first prize at the dog show for its beautiful coat.
They adopted a bobtail because of its gentle nature and intelligence.

Etymology

From 'bob' (to cut short) + 'tail', referring to the dog’s docked or naturally short tail.
