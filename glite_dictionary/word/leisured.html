leisured

1. ADJ

/ ˈliːʒərd / (AmE), / ˈlɛʒəd / (BrE)
having a lot of free time and not burdened by work or responsibilities, especially as characteristic of a social class or lifestyle.
having much free time; not burdened by work

Examples

The leisured classes enjoyed long holidays in the countryside.
In his leisured retirement, he took up painting and gardening.
The novel explores the daily routines of leisured aristocrats in the 19th century.

Synset Examples

he writes in his leisure hours
life as it ought to be for the leisure classes
even the artist and the sculptor were not regarded … as leisured men

Related Terms

  • Antonym: busy - having a lot to do; not having much free time

Etymology

From 'leisure' + '-ed', meaning possessing leisure.
