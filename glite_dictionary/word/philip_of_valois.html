Philip of Valois

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɪlɪp əv vælˈwɑ / (AmE), / ˈfɪlɪp əv ˈvælwɑː / (BrE)
King of France (reigned 1328–1350), founder of the Valois dynasty, whose claim to the throne led to the Hundred Years' War.
King of France and founder of the Valois dynasty

Examples

Philip of Valois became king of France after the death of Charles IV.
The Hundred Years' War began when Philip of Valois claimed the French throne.
Historians often discuss the policies of Philip of Valois and their impact on medieval France.

Etymology

From Old French Philippe de Valois; Valois is a region in northern France.
