sensuality

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛnʃuˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌsɛnʃuˈælɪti / (BrE)
the enjoyment, expression, or pursuit of physical, especially sexual, pleasure
desire for sensual pleasures

Examples

The novel is filled with sensuality, exploring characters' passions and desires in great detail.
Her art often celebrates the sensuality of the human body and movement.
He was criticized for living a life devoted to pleasure and sensuality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensualism - the doctrine that pleasure or sensation is the chief good
  • Antonym: asceticism - severe self-discipline and avoidance of all forms of indulgence

Etymology

From Old French sensualite, from Late Latin sensualitas (capacity for sensation), from Latin sensualis (endowed with feeling).
