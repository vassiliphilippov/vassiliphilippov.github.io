reeler

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːlər / (AmE), / ˈriːlə / (BrE)
a person who dances reels, especially in Scottish or Irish folk dance
person who dances reels

Examples

The skilled reeler led the troupe with lively steps and infectious energy.
At the ceilidh, every enthusiastic reeler took their turn on the dance floor.
She has been a passionate reeler since her childhood in the Highlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reel - a lively Scottish or Irish folk dance

Etymology

From 'reel' (a type of folk dance) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈriːlər / (AmE), / ˈɹiːlə / (BrE)
a person who walks unsteadily, as if about to fall
person who walks unsteadily

Examples

The old man looked like a reeler after his long illness.
After several drinks, she became a reeler on the dance floor.
The intoxicated reeler was escorted safely outside by security.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: staggerer - someone who staggers or walks with difficulty
  • Degree Lower: walker - a person who walks

Etymology

From 'reel' (to move unsteadily) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
