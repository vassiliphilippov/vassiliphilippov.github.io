barnyard

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːrnˌjɑrd / (AmE), / ˈbɑːnˌjɑːd / (BrE)
An area of ground adjoining a barn, typically used for storing farm equipment or penning livestock.
yard next to a barn

Examples

The chickens wandered freely around the barnyard every morning.
Children love to visit the barnyard to see the animals up close.
Old tools and equipment were scattered across the muddy barnyard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farmyard - the yard surrounding a farmhouse or farm buildings

Etymology

Formed from the combination of 'barn' and 'yard'.
