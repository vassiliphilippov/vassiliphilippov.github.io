ebbing

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɛbɪŋ / (BrE)
a gradual decrease or decline in strength, power, quantity, or intensity
gradual decline or decrease

Examples

There was an ebbing of enthusiasm as the meeting dragged on.
The ebbing of public support forced the politician to change tactics.
His strength was ebbing fast after hours of intense labor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decline - a gradual and continuous loss of strength, numbers, quality, or value
  • Synonym: waning - a gradual decrease in visibly illuminated portion (of the moon) or in strength

Etymology

From the verb 'ebb', meaning to recede or diminish, from Old English 'ebbian'.
