Terrapene ornata

1. NOUN

/ tɛˈræpəni ɔːrˈneɪtə / (AmE), / tɛˈrapəni ɔːˈnɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of box turtle native to the prairies of the western United States and northern Mexico, known for its ornate shell patterns.
a species of box turtle

Examples

The Terrapene ornata is recognized by the detailed lines on its shell.
Researchers studied the habitat of the Terrapene ornata to improve its conservation.
The Terrapene ornata is active mostly in the early morning and late afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: box turtle - a North American turtle with a domed shell that can be closed tightly

Etymology

From Latin: 'Terrapene' (genus name for box turtles) and 'ornata' meaning 'ornate' or 'decorated'.
