zaire

1. NOUN

/ zaɪˈɪr / (AmE), / zaɪˈɪə / (BrE)
the former basic unit of currency in Zaire, used from 1967 to 1997 before being replaced by the Congolese franc
former currency of Zaire

Examples

In 1978, a loaf of bread cost five zaires in the capital city.
The collector had an old zaire banknote from the early 1980s in his album.
After hyperinflation, the zaire was replaced by a new currency.

Etymology

Named after Zaire, the former name of the Democratic Republic of the Congo; itself from Portuguese adaptation of African Kongo 'nzadi o nzere' (the river that swallows all rivers).
