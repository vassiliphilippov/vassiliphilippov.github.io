Zaire

1. PROPN

/ zaɪˈɪr / (AmE), / zaɪˈɪə / (BrE)
the name of a former country in central Africa, known today as the Democratic Republic of the Congo; it was called Zaire from 1971 to 1997.
former name of Democratic Republic of the Congo

Examples

In 1975, Zaire hosted the famous 'Rumble in the Jungle' boxing match between Muhammad Ali and George Foreman.
During the 1980s, Zaire was ruled by President Mobutu Sese Seko.
After years of political turmoil, Zaire was renamed the Democratic Republic of the Congo in 1997.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Democratic Republic of the Congo - country in central Africa, formerly known as Zaire

Etymology

From Portuguese 'Zaire', an adaptation of the Kikongo word 'nzadi' (river), referring to the Congo River.
