sweetbreads

1. NOUN

/ ˈswitˌbrɛdz / (AmE), / ˈswiːtbriːdz / (BrE)
The thymus or pancreas glands of a calf or lamb, used as food.
edible animal glands used as food

Examples

The chef prepared sweetbreads sautéed in butter and herbs.
Some people are surprised to learn that sweetbreads are actually animal glands.
At the gourmet restaurant, sweetbreads are served as a delicacy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offal - the entrails and internal organs of an animal used as food

Etymology

From sweet + bread (“flesh, meat”), referring to their mild taste and soft texture.
