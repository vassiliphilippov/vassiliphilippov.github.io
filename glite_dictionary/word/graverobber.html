graverobber

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪvˌrɑbər / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪvˌrɒbə / (BrE)
a person who steals valuables or bodies from graves or tombs
one who steals from graves or tombs

Examples

The police arrested a graverobber who had disturbed several ancient tombs.
Stories about the notorious graverobber spread fear throughout the small village.
In the nineteenth century, a graverobber could sell stolen corpses to medical schools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grave robber - a thief who steals from graves or tombs
  • Synonym: thief - a person who steals, especially secretly

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪvˌrɑbər / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪvˌrɒbə / (BrE)
a person who illegally removes corpses from graves, typically to sell them for anatomical dissection or other purposes
someone who steals bodies from graves

Examples

The notorious graverobber was finally apprehended by the police last night.
In the 19th century, medical schools sometimes relied on graverobbers to supply bodies for dissection.
Stories of the graverobber spread fear throughout the small village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: body snatcher - a person who steals corpses from graves
  • Antonym: grave digger - a person who digs graves (not a thief)

Etymology

Formed from 'grave' (burial place) + 'robber' (thief).
