tactics

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæk.tɪks / (AmE), / ˈtak.tɪks / (BrE)
the branch of military science concerned with the planning and execution of maneuvers to achieve objectives set by strategy
military planning of maneuvers

Examples

The general was an expert in battlefield tactics and often outmaneuvered his opponents.
Modern tactics incorporate both conventional and unconventional maneuvers to achieve victory.
Understanding enemy movements is essential for developing effective tactics in combat.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: strategy - the art of planning and directing overall military operations

Etymology

From Greek taktikē (art of arrangement or tactics), from taktos (ordered, arranged).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtæk.tɪks / (AmE), / ˈtæk.tɪks / (BrE)
the actions or strategies carefully planned to achieve a specific goal, often as part of a larger plan
planned actions to achieve a goal

Examples

The company's marketing tactics helped increase sales during the holiday season.
She considered various tactics before making her next move in the game.
His negotiation tactics were aggressive but ultimately effective.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strategy - a plan of action designed to achieve a long-term or overall aim
  • Synonym: plan - a detailed proposal for achieving something

Etymology

From Greek taktikē (art of arrangement), via Latin tactica.
