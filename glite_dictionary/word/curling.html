curling

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɝːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɜːlɪŋ / (BrE)
a game played on ice in which players slide heavy, handled stones toward a target area, aiming for precision and strategy
ice game using sliding stones

Examples

The Winter Olympics always feature curling as one of the main sports.
She joined a local curling club to learn the basics of the game.
The strategy in curling is often compared to chess on ice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ice hockey - a fast contact sport played on ice with sticks and a puck

Etymology

From Scots 'curl', referring to motion with a twist, and -ing forming the action noun. First recorded as the name of the game in the early 17th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɝlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɜːlɪŋ / (BrE)
(of hair) forming curls or having been made to curl; characterized by the presence of curls.
hair that forms curls

Examples

She admired her daughter's curling hair as it gleamed in the sunlight.
Over time, his curling locks became his most distinctive feature.
The stylist used a hot iron to create curling waves in her hair.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: straight - (of hair) not curly or wavy; having no curls
  • Degree Lower: wavy - (of hair) forming slight curves or waves, not straight or tightly curled
