Madagascar

1. PROPN

/ ˌmæd.əˈɡæskər / (AmE), / ˌmæd.əˈɡæskə / (BrE)
A republic consisting of the island of Madagascar, located off the southeastern coast of Africa; it gained independence from France in 1960.
island nation off southeastern Africa

Examples

The biodiversity of Madagascar attracts scientists and tourists from around the world.
After gaining independence in 1960, Madagascar developed its own cultural and political identity.
Vanilla is one of the major exports produced in Madagascar.

Etymology

From Malagasy Madagasikara, from Portuguese Madagascar, ultimately from Arabic جزيرة مدغشقر (jazīrat maḏaġashqar).

2. PROPN

/ ˌmædəˈɡæskər / (AmE), / ˌmædəˈɡæskə / (BrE)
a large island country in the Indian Ocean, off the southeastern coast of Africa, known as the world's fourth largest island
large island nation off southeastern Africa

Examples

Lemurs are native only to Madagascar, making it a unique destination for biologists.
The rainforests of Madagascar are home to many rare and endangered species.
She plans to travel to Madagascar to study its diverse ecosystems.

Etymology

From Portuguese *Madagascar*, based on Arabic *Madqascar* or similar forms, popularized in Europe by Marco Polo.
