Warlpiri

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːrlˌpɪri / (AmE), / ˈwɑːlˌpiːri / (BrE)
A language spoken by the Warlpiri people, an Indigenous Australian group, primarily in Australia's Northern Territory.
Australian Aboriginal language

Examples

Researchers have documented the Warlpiri language for its unique grammatical structures.
Many children in the Tanami Desert still speak Warlpiri at home.
The novel contains several phrases in Warlpiri to reflect the culture of the region.

Etymology

From the name of the Warlpiri people, the Indigenous group who speak the language.
