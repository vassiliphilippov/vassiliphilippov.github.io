Hydrocharitaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdroʊˌkerəˈteɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪdrəʊˌkarɪˈteɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of simple, nearly stemless freshwater aquatic plants, widely distributed in various regions.
family of aquatic plants

Examples

The family Hydrocharitaceae includes many common freshwater plants such as tape grass.
Researchers studied the ecological impact of Hydrocharitaceae in local lakes.
Several invasive species in the Hydrocharitaceae family have spread to new waterways.

Etymology

Formed from Neo-Latin: Hydro- (relating to water) + Charitaceae (typical family ending in botanical Latin).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪ.droʊˌkær.ɪˈteɪ.siː / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.drəʊˌkær.ɪˈteɪ.siː / (BrE)
a family of simple, nearly stemless freshwater aquatic plants distributed widely around the world
family of aquatic freshwater plants

Examples

The Hydrocharitaceae are important in aquatic habitats for providing food and shelter for wildlife.
Many invasive species, like the water hyacinth, belong to the Hydrocharitaceae family.
Scientists studied the reproductive strategies of several Hydrocharitaceae in freshwater lakes.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Hydrocharis plus the family suffix -aceae.
