oligodontia

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːlɪɡoʊˈdɑːnʃə / (AmE), / ˌɒlɪɡɒnˈtiːə / (BrE)
A congenital condition in which several permanent teeth are missing from the normal complement.
congenital absence of several teeth
medical

Examples

The dentist diagnosed the child with oligodontia after noticing several missing permanent teeth.
Due to her oligodontia, she required extensive dental treatment from a young age.
Genetic factors are thought to play a role in the development of oligodontia.

Etymology

From Greek oligos (few) + odous (tooth).
