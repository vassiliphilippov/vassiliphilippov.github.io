acrogenous

1. ADJ

/ əˈkrɑdʒənəs / (AmE), / əˈkrɒdʒɪnəs / (BrE)
(of certain non-flowering plants) growing only at the apex or tip of the main stem, as in ferns or mosses.
growing at the tip (botany)

Examples

Ferns are classified as acrogenous plants because they grow from the tip of the main stem.
The botanist explained that mosses display acrogenous growth patterns, different from seed plants.
Many acrogenous species have a distinctive appearance, with all new growth emerging from their uppermost point.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acrocarpous - growing at the apex (botanical term, especially for mosses)

Etymology

From Greek 'akros' (highest) + '-genous' (producing, forming).
