molecule

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑlɪˌkjul / (AmE), / ˈmɒlɪˌkjuːl / (BrE)
A group of atoms bonded together, representing the smallest fundamental unit of a chemical compound that can take part in a chemical reaction.
smallest unit of a compound

Examples

A water molecule consists of two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom.
Scientists can now observe the behavior of a single molecule using advanced microscopes.
Each molecule in the gas moves independently of the others.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: atom - the basic unit of a chemical element
  • Degree Higher: compound - a substance formed from two or more elements chemically united in fixed proportions

Etymology

From French *molécule* or Latin *molecula* (little mass), diminutive of *moles* (mass).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.lɪ.kjuːl / (AmE), / ˈmɒl.ɪ.kjuːl / (BrE)
(nontechnical) A very tiny piece or particle of anything; a minute amount.
a tiny piece or particle of anything

Examples

There wasn't a molecule of doubt in her mind about his honesty.
Not a molecule of evidence was left after the cleanup.
I don’t have a molecule of interest in their argument.
