Moses

1. PROPN

/ ˈmoʊzɪz / (AmE), / ˈməʊzɪz / (BrE)
(in the Bible) the Hebrew prophet who led the Israelites out of Egypt and received the Ten Commandments from God on Mount Sinai.
Hebrew prophet, leader in Exodus

Examples

Many people regard Moses as one of the most influential figures in religious history.
According to tradition, Moses parted the Red Sea to help the Israelites escape from Egypt.
The story of Moses receiving the Ten Commandments is found in the Book of Exodus.

Etymology

From Hebrew 'Mosheh', possibly from Egyptian meaning 'born (of)' or 'son'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈmoʊzɪz / (AmE), / ˈməʊzɪz / (BrE)
(Grandma Moses) United States painter known for her colorful, primitive rural scenes, active from 1860 to 1961.
U.S. folk artist (1860–1961) known as Grandma Moses

Examples

Many art collectors admire Moses for her unique style of depicting rural life.
The museum showcased several works by Moses, drawing visitors from around the country.
Although she started painting late in life, Moses became an icon of American folk art.
