pantomimer

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæn.təˌmaɪ.mər / (AmE), / ˈpæn.təˌmiː.mə / (BrE)
an actor who performs or communicates entirely by gesture and facial expression, without speaking
actor using gesture, no speech

Examples

The pantomimer captivated the audience with his expressive gestures and silent storytelling.
At the festival, a pantomimer performed on the street, delighting children and adults alike.
She trained for years to become a skilled pantomimer in the tradition of Marcel Marceau.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mime - actor performing using gestures without speech

Etymology

From 'pantomime' (from Greek 'pantomimos'), plus the agent suffix '-er'.
