to desquamate

1. VERB

/ ˈdɛskwəˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛskwəmeɪt / (BrE)
(of skin or other tissue) to shed, peel, or come off in thin scales or flakes.
to shed or peel off in scales
medical

Examples

After a sunburn, the affected skin may desquamate over several days.
Some diseases cause the palms of the hands to desquamate noticeably.
As the rash healed, patches of her skin started to desquamate in fine flakes.

Synset Examples

dry skin desquamates

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exfoliate - to remove or shed skin in flakes or scales
  • Synonym: slough - to shed or remove a layer of dead skin or tissue

Etymology

From Latin desquamare, from de- (off) + squama (scale).
