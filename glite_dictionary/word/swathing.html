swathing

1. NOUN

/ ˈsweɪðɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈswɔːðɪŋ / (BrE)
A length of cloth used to wrap around something, especially for protection or support, such as a wound or a baby.
cloth wrapping or covering

Examples

The nurse secured the wound with a clean swathing of gauze.
The baby lay quietly, protected by the soft swathing around her.
Layers of swathing were used to stabilize the injured arm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bandage - a strip of material used to bind a wound or protect an injured part of the body
  • Synonym: wrapping - material used to cover or enclose something

Etymology

From the verb 'swathe', from Old English 'swæþian' (to bind or wrap).
