tranquillising

1. ADJ

/ træŋˈkwɪlaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / træŋˈkwɪlʌɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
having a calming or soothing effect; tending to make someone calm or relaxed
having a calming or soothing effect

Examples

The doctor prescribed a tranquillising medication to help her sleep through the night.
The sound of the gentle rain had a tranquillising effect on everyone in the room.
He drank a cup of tranquillising herbal tea before going to bed.

Synset Examples

valium has a tranquilizing effect
took a hot drink with sedative properties before going to bed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sedative - having a calming, soothing, or tranquilizing effect, especially as a drug
  • Antonym: stimulating - increasing alertness or activity, having the opposite effect to calming

Etymology

From Latin 'tranquillus' (calm, quiet) + English suffix '-ize' + '-ing'.
