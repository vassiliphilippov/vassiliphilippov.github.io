to niggle

1. VERB

/ ˈnɪɡəl / (AmE), / ˈnɪɡəl / (BrE)
to argue or worry over trivial or minor details; to engage in petty criticism or dispute
argue or fuss over petty details

Examples

We shouldn't niggle over such minor details when the deadline is so close.
Instead of appreciating the effort, they kept niggling about the tiniest mistakes.
She tends to niggle with her coworkers about things that don't really matter.

Synset Examples

Let's not quibble over pennies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quibble - to argue or raise objections about a trivial matter
  • Synonym: to bicker - to argue about petty and trivial matters

Etymology

Perhaps from earlier dialectal 'niggle' meaning 'to trifle', possibly of Scandinavian origin.

2. VERB

/ ˈnɪɡəl / (AmE), / ˈnɪɡl / (BrE)
to worry or fuss over something in a way that is excessive or unnecessary
worry or fuss unnecessarily

Examples

She tends to niggle over every small detail, even when it's not important.
It started to niggle at him that he might have forgotten to lock the door.
Stop letting minor issues niggle you so much and try to relax.

Synset Examples

don't fuss too much over the grandchildren — they are quite big now

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuss - to show unnecessary concern or excitement
  • Synonym: to dwell - to linger over or worry about something repeatedly

Etymology

Possibly from Old English *gnæg(e)lan* (to gnaw).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01797944-v
Synset POS: v
