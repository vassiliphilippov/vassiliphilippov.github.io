batch

1. NOUN

/ bætʃ / (AmE), / bætʃ / (BrE)
a group of things or people dealt with at one time or in one process
a group handled together

Examples

The bakery produced a fresh batch of cookies this morning.
She sorted the documents into one large batch for review.
A new batch of students will join the program next semester.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: group - a number of people or things that are located, gathered, or classed together

Etymology

Middle English, from Old French 'bache', meaning 'something baked', later generalized to a quantity made at one time.

2. VERB

/ bætʃ / (AmE), / bætʃ / (BrE)
to group or process things together as a single batch
group or process together as a batch

Examples

The warehouse staff will batch the orders before shipment to increase efficiency.
To save time, the system batches incoming requests for processing.
They batched all similar documents together during the afternoon scan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assemble - to bring things together to form a group or set
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01388727-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ bætʃ / (AmE), / bætʃ / (BrE)
a group of items, especially loaves of bread, baked or produced at the same time
group baked or produced together

Examples

The baker placed a fresh batch of bread on the cooling rack.
This batch of rolls smells especially delicious right out of the oven.
Each morning, they sell out of their first batch within an hour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lot - a set or group of things dealt with at the same time

Etymology

From Middle English bache, from Old French bache, possibly from Vulgar Latin *bacca (something baked).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08417729-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ bætʃ / (AmE), / bætʃ / (BrE)
a quantity or group of things or people dealt with or produced together at one time, often followed by 'of' to indicate a large number or amount
a group or quantity dealt with at one time

Examples

We received a new batch of applications for the scholarship yesterday.
The bakery produces a fresh batch of bread every morning.
A large batch of emails awaited her after the weekend.
