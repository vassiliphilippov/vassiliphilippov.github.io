surmounted

1. ADJ

/ sɚˈmaʊntɪd / (AmE), / səˈmaʊntɪd / (BrE)
having something placed on top; topped by something else.
having something on top

Examples

The entrance was flanked by impressive gates surmounted with ornate lamps.
Each pillar was surmounted by a figure representing a different virtue.
The building's roof was surmounted by a golden dome.

Synset Examples

columns surmounted by statues

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'surmount', from Old French 'surmonter', from Latin 'supermontare' (to rise above).
