kaput

1. ADJ

/ kəˈpʊt / (AmE), / kəˈpʊt / (BrE)
no longer working, effective, or in a usable condition; broken or destroyed
no longer working or ruined

Examples

After the lightning strike, the old television was completely kaput and wouldn't turn on.
My car went kaput halfway to work, so I had to take the bus instead.
If this washing machine goes kaput again, I'm finally replacing it.

Synset Examples

we are gone geese

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broken - damaged and not working
  • Synonym: destroyed - completely ruined or demolished
  • Antonym: intact - undamaged, whole, not broken

Etymology

From German 'kaputt' (broken, destroyed), itself from French 'capot' (defeated).
