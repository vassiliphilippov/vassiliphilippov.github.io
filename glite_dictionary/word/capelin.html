capelin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæpəlɪn / (AmE), / ˈkapəlɪn / (BrE)
a small northern fish (Mallotus villosus) from cold Arctic and northern Atlantic waters, important as food for marine animals
a small northern fish species

Examples

The seabirds gathered in large numbers to feed on the spawning capelin along the coast.
Many marine mammals rely on capelin as a primary food source during the summer months.
Fishermen reported an exceptionally large capelin run this spring near coastal Newfoundland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smelt - a small silvery fish of northern waters, related in size and ecological niche

Etymology

From Canadian French capelan, from Old Provençal capelan (mullet), influenced by Medieval Latin capellanus (chaplain).
