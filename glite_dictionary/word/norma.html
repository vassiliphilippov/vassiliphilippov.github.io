Norma

1. PROPN

/ ˈnɔrmə / (AmE), / ˈnɔːmə / (BrE)
a small constellation in the Southern Hemisphere, located near Lupus and Ara in the Milky Way
constellation in the southern sky

Examples

The constellation Norma is visible in the Southern Hemisphere during the winter months.
Astronomers often use Norma as a reference point to locate nearby constellations like Lupus and Ara.
Many stars in Norma are part of the Milky Way's rich star fields.

Etymology

From Latin 'norma' meaning 'carpenter's square', which the constellation represents.
