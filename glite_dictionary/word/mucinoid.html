mucinoid

1. ADJ

/ ˈmjuːsəˌnɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈmjuːsɪnɔɪd / (BrE)
Resembling mucin in appearance or properties.
resembling mucin
medical

Examples

Under the microscope, the tumor displayed a mucinoid texture distinct from healthy tissue.
Some pathological samples reveal mucinoid changes due to excess mucin production.
The secretion was described as mucinoid because of its viscous, gelatinous appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mucinous - relating to, resembling, or containing mucin

Etymology

From 'mucin' (a protein produced by epithelial tissues) + '-oid' (resembling or like).
