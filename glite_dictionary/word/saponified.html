saponified

1. ADJ

/ səˈpɑː.nəˌfaɪd / (AmE), / səˈpɒn.ɪ.faɪd / (BrE)
(of a fat or oil) converted into soap by treatment with an alkali.
converted into soap

Examples

The saponified oils are often used in making natural soaps and detergents.
Only saponified fats will react with the dye in this test.
During the process, the fat is saponified by being boiled with sodium hydroxide.

Synset Examples

saponified oils

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unsaponified - not converted into soap; opposite of saponified
  • Synonym: to saponify - to convert (a fat or oil) into soap by reaction with an alkali

Etymology

From 'saponify', from French 'saponifier', from Latin 'sapo' (soap).
