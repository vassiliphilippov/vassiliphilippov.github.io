indisputable

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪndɪˈspjutəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈspjuːtəbəl / (BrE)
impossible to doubt or question; certain and not open to dispute
impossible to question

Examples

There is indisputable evidence linking him to the crime.
Her dedication to the cause is indisputable among her colleagues.
It is indisputable that climate change poses a serious threat to our planet.

Synset Examples

indisputable (or sure) proof

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undeniable - impossible to deny or argue against
  • Antonym: disputable - open to question or debate

Etymology

From Late Latin indisputabilis, from in- ('not') + disputabilis ('able to be disputed').

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪndɪˈspjutəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈspjuːtəb(ə)l / (BrE)
impossible to doubt or question; clearly true or certain
impossible to question; obviously true

Examples

The scientist presented indisputable facts during the conference.
There is indisputable proof that the painting is genuine.
Her influence on the team’s success is indisputable.

Synset Examples

indisputable evidence of a witness

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disputable - able to be questioned or challenged
  • Synonym: unquestionable - not able to be doubted; certain

Etymology

From Latin 'indisputabilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'disputabilis' (able to be disputed).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00725080-s
Synset POS: s
