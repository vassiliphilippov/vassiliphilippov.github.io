unstructured

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈstrʌktʃɚd / (AmE), / ʌnˈstrʌktʃəd / (BrE)
not organized or arranged in a specific or systematic way; lacking a definite structure or form.
not organized or lacking structure

Examples

The team performed well in the unstructured environment, quickly adapting to change.
His essay was criticized for being too unstructured and lacking a clear argument.
Children often benefit from unstructured playtime, where they can use their imagination freely.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: structured - organized or arranged in a particular way; having structure

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' to 'structured', meaning not structured.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈstrʌktʃɚd / (AmE), / ʌnˈstrʌktʃəd / (BrE)
Not organized or arranged in a definite or systematic way; lacking a clear structure.
lacking definite structure or organization

Examples

The children enjoyed playing in the unstructured environment of the park.
Her unstructured schedule allowed for a lot of creativity and spontaneity.
In unstructured interviews, candidates are encouraged to speak freely.

Synset Examples

an unstructured situation with no one in authority
a neighborhood gang with a relatively unstructured system
children in an unstructured environment often feel insecure
unstructured inkblots

Related Terms

  • Antonym: structured - organized or arranged in a definite or systematic way

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'structured', from Latin 'structura' (a fitting together, building).
