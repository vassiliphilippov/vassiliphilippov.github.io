hawk-eyed

1. ADJ

/ hɔk-aɪd / (AmE), / hɔːk-aɪd / (BrE)
Having very keen or sharp vision; able to notice or spot things quickly and easily.
having very keen vision

Examples

With her hawk-eyed attention to detail, nothing ever escaped her notice.
Security guards remained hawk-eyed throughout the event, watching for any suspicious behavior.
My grandmother is hawk-eyed and can spot a lost button from across the room.

Synset Examples

quick-sighted as a cat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eagle-eyed - having extremely sharp eyesight
  • Synonym: sharp-sighted - having very good vision
  • Antonym: short-sighted - unable to see distant objects clearly; lacking foresight

Etymology

Formed by analogy with the keen eyesight associated with hawks.
