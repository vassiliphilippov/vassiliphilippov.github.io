Cynocephalus variegatus

1. NOUN

/ saɪ.noʊˈsɛf.ə.ləs vəˌriː.əˈɡeɪ.təs / (AmE), / sɪ.nəʊˈsɛf.ə.ləs vəˌrɪəˈɡeɪ.təs / (BrE)
A species of flying lemur native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its ability to glide between trees using a membrane of skin.
a species of flying lemur

Examples

The Cynocephalus variegatus spends most of its life gliding from tree to tree in the rainforest canopy.
Researchers observed that Cynocephalus variegatus is primarily nocturnal, feeding on leaves and fruits at night.
Deforestation has threatened the natural habitat of the Cynocephalus variegatus in Southeast Asia.

Etymology

From Greek 'kyon' (dog) and 'kephalē' (head); Latin species epithet meaning 'variegated'.
