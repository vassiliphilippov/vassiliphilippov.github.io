Maricopa

1. NOUN

/ ˌmærɪˈkoʊpə / (AmE), / ˌmærɪˈkəʊpə / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people traditionally living along the Gila River in Arizona.
member of a Native American people of Arizona

Examples

The Maricopa have a rich tradition of basket weaving passed down through generations.
Several Maricopa attended the cultural heritage conference in Phoenix last year.
He studied the language spoken by the Maricopa to better understand their history.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmærɪˈkoʊpə / (AmE), / ˌmærɪˈkəʊpə / (BrE)
the Yuman language traditionally spoken by the Maricopa and Halchidhoma peoples of the southwestern United States
Yuman language of the Maricopa people

Examples

Few people still speak Maricopa today, making it an endangered language.
She is studying Maricopa to help preserve the tribe's cultural heritage.
The Maricopa language has several unique features compared to other Yuman languages.

Etymology

From the name Maricopa, applied by outsiders to the indigenous Piipaash people.
