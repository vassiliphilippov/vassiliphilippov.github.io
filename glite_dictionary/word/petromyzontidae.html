Petromyzontidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛtroʊˌmaɪˈzɒntɪdi / (AmE), / ˌpɛtrəʊˌmɪˈzɒntɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of jawless fish known as lampreys, characterized by their eel-like bodies and circular sucking mouths.
family of lampreys (jawless fish)

Examples

The Petromyzontidae are distinguished by their lack of jaws and paired fins.
Scientists study Petromyzontidae to learn about early vertebrate evolution.
The river is home to several species within the Petromyzontidae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lamprey - parasitic jawless fish with a funnel-like sucking mouth

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'petra' (rock) + 'myzo' (to suck), referring to their habit of attaching to rocks and fish.

family of jawless fish (lampreys)

Examples

There are over thirty species in the Petromyzontidae family found around the world.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of Petromyzontidae in the river ecosystem.
Lampreys of the Petromyzontidae family are known for their parasitic feeding habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lamprey - a jawless fish with a toothed, funnel-like sucking mouth

Etymology

From New Latin Petromyzon (the type genus) and Greek 'petra' (rock) + 'myzo' (to suck).
