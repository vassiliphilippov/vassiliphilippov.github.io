puritanical

1. ADJ

/ ˌpjʊrɪˈtænɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌpjʊərɪˈtænɪkəl / (BrE)
Having or showing very strict moral attitudes, especially about self-indulgence or sexual matters.
very strict in moral attitudes

Examples

His puritanical attitude made parties at his house rather dull.
The school's puritanical dress code forbade even modest accessories.
Some people found her views on dating to be overly puritanical.

Synset Examples

my straitlaced Aunt Anna doesn't approve of my miniskirts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: straitlaced - having very strict moral standards
  • Antonym: lenient - permissive, not strict or severe

Etymology

From Puritan, a member of a strict Protestant group in the 16th–17th century, plus -ical.

2. ADJ

/ ˌpjʊr.ɪˈtæn.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌpjʊə.rɪˈtæn.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
having or displaying extremely strict or rigid moral beliefs and practices, often discouraging pleasure or indulgence
strict in moral or religious matters

Examples

Her puritanical attitude made her critical of the slightest breach of etiquette.
The school's puritanical rules prohibited any form of dancing or dating.
He grew up in a puritanical household where even lighthearted jokes were frowned upon.

Synset Examples

puritanic distaste for alcohol
she was anything but puritanical in her behavior
blue laws

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strict - enforcing rigid rules or discipline
  • Antonym: lenient - not strict; more forgiving

Etymology

From 'Puritan' (a member of a group of English Protestants), with the suffix '-ical'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01303318-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌpjʊr.ɪˈtæn.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌpjʊə.rɪˈtæn.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
strictly adhering to moral or religious principles, often with a tendency to be austere or severe; relating to the Puritans or their practices.
strictly moral or austere; relating to Puritans

Examples

The new principal imposed puritanical rules regarding dress and behavior at the school.
She has a puritanical attitude toward alcohol and parties.
His puritanical upbringing influenced his conservative views on relationships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: austere - strict or severe in manner, attitude, or appearance
  • Antonym: liberal - open to new behavior or opinions; willing to discard traditional values

Etymology

From Puritan, influenced by French *puritain*, ultimately from Latin *puritas* (purity).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03116753-a
Synset POS: a
