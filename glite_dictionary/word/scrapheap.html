scrapheap

1. NOUN

/ ˈskræpˌhip / (AmE), / ˈskrapˌhiːp / (BrE)
a pile or place where waste or discarded material is collected
a heap of discarded material or junk

Examples

An old bicycle was thrown onto the scrapheap behind the factory.
Many broken machines end up rusting on the scrapheap.
What you see as useless junk, he sees as treasure on the scrapheap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: junkyard - a place where scrap is collected and stored
  • Synonym: landfill - a site for the disposal of waste materials by burial

Etymology

Formed from 'scrap' (waste material) + 'heap' (a pile).

2. NOUN

/ ˈskræpˌhip / (AmE), / ˈskrap.hiːp / (BrE)
a pile where scrap metal or other discarded items are collected, especially for disposal or recycling
a pile of discarded metal

Examples

He found an old bicycle frame on the scrapheap behind the factory.
Some inventions belong on the scrapheap of history, never to be used again.
The company recycles metal that would otherwise end up on the scrapheap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: junkyard - a place where discarded or wrecked items, especially cars and metal, are collected

Etymology

From 'scrap' (discarded metal or waste) + 'heap' (a pile).
