fricandeau

1. NOUN

/ frɪˌkɒn.doʊ / (AmE), / frɪˈkɒn.dəʊ / (BrE)
A dish of veal, typically larded, braised, and glazed in its own juices.
larded, braised veal dish

Examples

The chef prepared a delicate fricandeau for the main course, impressing all the guests.
Traditional French cookbooks often include recipes for fricandeau, showcasing classic techniques.
Her favorite dish at the restaurant was fricandeau served with seasonal vegetables.

Etymology

From French 'fricandeau', referring to a larded, braised veal dish.
