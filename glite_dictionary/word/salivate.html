to salivate

1. VERB

/ ˈsælɪˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈsælɪveɪt / (BrE)
(informal) To feel eager or excited for something, especially to the point of strong anticipation or desire.
to feel strong desire or anticipation
informal

Examples

The tech fans salivated over the latest smartphone release.
Investors are salivating at the prospect of huge profits.
He salivated when he saw all the new cars on display.

Synset Examples

She was salivating over the raise she anticipated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to crave - to feel a powerful desire for something
  • Synonym: desire - to strongly wish for something
  • Antonym: disdain - to feel contempt or lack of desire for something

2. VERB

/ ˈsæləˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈsælɪveɪt / (BrE)
to produce saliva, especially as a result of seeing or smelling food, or in eager anticipation
produce saliva, often in anticipation

Examples

The aroma of fresh bread made everyone salivate instantly.
He began to salivate as soon as he saw the juicy steak on the grill.
Pavlov's experiment showed that dogs would salivate at the sound of a bell.

Synset Examples

We salivated when he described the great meal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drool - let saliva flow from the mouth; show eager anticipation

Etymology

From French 'saliver', from Latin 'saliva' (saliva).
