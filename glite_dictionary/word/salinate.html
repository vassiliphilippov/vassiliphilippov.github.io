to salinate

1. VERB

/ ˈsælɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈsalɪneɪt / (BrE)
to make something salty or to add salt to it, especially in chemical or scientific contexts
make or become salty

Examples

The laboratory technician will salinate the solution before beginning the next phase of the experiment.
Over time, irrigation can unintentionally salinate the soil, making it less suitable for agriculture.
Engineers need to monitor the process carefully to ensure they do not salinate the water supply.

Synset Examples

salinated solution

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to desalinate - remove salt from something, especially water

Etymology

From Latin 'salinatus', past participle of 'salinare' (to salt), from 'sal' (salt).
