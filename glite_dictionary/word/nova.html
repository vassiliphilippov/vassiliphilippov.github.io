nova

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊ.və / (AmE), / ˈnəʊ.və / (BrE)
A star that suddenly increases in brightness by expelling material in a cloud, becoming temporarily much more luminous.
star that suddenly becomes much brighter

Examples

Astronomers observed a nova appearing unexpectedly in the night sky.
The brightness of the nova gradually faded after a few weeks.
She studied the process by which a nova expels material into space.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: supernova - a stellar explosion much more powerful than a nova

Etymology

From Latin 'nova' (new), from 'nova stella' meaning 'new star'.
