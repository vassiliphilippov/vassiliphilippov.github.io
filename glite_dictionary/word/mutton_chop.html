mutton chop

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌtn tʃɑp / (AmE), / ˈmʌtn tʃɒp / (BrE)
A style of facial hair in which whiskers grow down the sides of a man's face in front of the ears, with the rest of the beard usually shaved.
style of facial hair growing down the face

Examples

The actor wore distinctive mutton chops for his role in the historical drama.
He decided to grow mutton chops to change up his appearance.
Victorian gentlemen were often famous for their impressive mutton chop whiskers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sideburn - facial hair grown down the sides of the face in front of the ears

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to the shape of a cooked mutton chop (a cut of sheep meat).
