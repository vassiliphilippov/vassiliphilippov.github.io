severed

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛvɚd / (AmE), / ˈsɛvəd / (BrE)
cut off or detached by cutting; separated, especially by a sharp instrument
cut off or detached by cutting

Examples

The hikers stumbled upon a severed branch lying across the trail after the storm.
Archaeologists found a severed hand buried beneath the ancient ruins.
The power was out after a severed cable disrupted the electricity supply.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detached - separated or disconnected from something
  • Antonym: attached - joined or fastened to something

Etymology

Past participle of 'sever', from Old French 'sevrer', from Latin 'separare' (to separate).
