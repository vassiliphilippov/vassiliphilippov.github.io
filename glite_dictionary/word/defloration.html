defloration

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛfləˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːflɔːˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of depriving a woman of her virginity, especially by rupturing the hymen through sexual intercourse
loss of virginity (by intercourse)

Examples

The novel alluded subtly to the defloration of its young protagonist.
In some cultures, defloration rituals have significant social importance.
She spoke openly about the myths surrounding defloration and virginity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deflower - to deprive a woman of her virginity

Etymology

From Latin defloratio, from deflorare (to deflower).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdiːfləˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːfləˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of spoiling or destroying the innocence or beauty of something
spoiling innocence or beauty

Examples

Many critics argued that the construction of highways through the park was an act of defloration of a natural treasure.
The artist mourned the defloration of the old city center by modern development.
Some saw the graffiti as a defloration of the building’s historic façade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to despoil - to strip of value or beauty
  • Antonym: preserve - to maintain the purity or beauty of something

Etymology

From Latin defloratio, from deflorare 'to pluck the flower from', meaning to take away the bloom or virginity.
