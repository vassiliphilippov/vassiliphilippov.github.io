valetudinarian

1. ADJ

/ ˌvælətjudəˈnɛriən / (AmE), / ˌvælətjuːdɪˈneəriən / (BrE)
showing excessive concern for one's own health or suffering from constant health worries; characteristic of a valetudinarian.
excessively concerned about health

Examples

His valetudinarian habits kept him from enjoying outdoor activities with friends.
She developed a valetudinarian attitude after a series of minor illnesses.
The protagonist is portrayed as a valetudinarian old man who worries endlessly about his health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypochondriac - a person abnormally anxious about their health

Etymology

From Latin 'valetudo' (health, illness) plus '-arian'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌvælətjudɪˈneriən / (AmE), / ˌvalɪtjuːdɪˈneəriən / (BrE)
a person who is weak, sickly, or excessively concerned about their own health
person overly concerned with health

Examples

Despite feeling well, Tom became a valetudinarian, constantly worrying about every minor ache or pain.
The elderly woman was dismissed as a valetudinarian by her less sympathetic relatives.
Some believe that too much time spent reading about illnesses can turn someone into a valetudinarian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypochondriac - person with an abnormal worry about their health

Etymology

From Latin 'valetudinarius' (sickly), from 'valetudo' (health, illness).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10765281-n
Synset POS: n
