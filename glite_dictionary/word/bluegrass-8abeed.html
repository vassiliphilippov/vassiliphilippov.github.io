bluegrass

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A style of country music played at a fast tempo, typically featuring banjos, guitars, and other stringed instruments.
fast-tempo country music genre

Examples

Many musicians travel to Kentucky to experience authentic bluegrass performances.
She learned to play the banjo by practicing classic bluegrass songs.
The festival featured several top bluegrass bands from around the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: country music - a style of popular music from the rural southern US

Etymology

Named after the Blue Grass Boys, the band of Bill Monroe, considered the founder of the genre; the band's name refers to the bluegrass region of Kentucky.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbluːˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
any of several grasses of the genus Poa, commonly found in temperate regions, noted for their bluish-green stems or leaves.
a type of grass with bluish-green leaves

Examples

The lawn was covered in lush bluegrass that stayed green throughout the summer.
Farmers often plant bluegrass to prevent soil erosion on slopes.
He identified the plant as bluegrass by its narrow, bluish leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kentucky bluegrass - a common species of bluegrass (Poa pratensis)
