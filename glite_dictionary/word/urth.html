Urth

1. PROPN

/ ʊrθ / (AmE), / ɜːθ / (BrE)
in Norse mythology, a goddess of fate who personifies the past, one of the three Norns.
Norse goddess of fate (the past)

Examples

In Norse legends, Urth sits by the Well of Fate and weaves the destinies of men.
The role of Urth in mythology is to represent all events that have already occurred.
Many ancient stories mention Urth as a powerful force shaping the past.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Norn - in Norse mythology, one of the three goddesses determining fate

Etymology

From Old Norse *Urðr*, meaning 'fate' or 'that which has come to pass'.
