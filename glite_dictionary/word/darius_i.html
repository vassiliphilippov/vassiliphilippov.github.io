Darius I

1. PROPN

/ dəˈraɪəs ðə ˈfɜrst / (AmE), / dəˈraɪəs ðə ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
(c. 550–486 BC) King of Persia who expanded the Achaemenid Empire and led the first invasion of Greece, being defeated at the Battle of Marathon.
King of Persia, defeated at Marathon

Examples

Darius I reorganized the Persian Empire into provinces governed by satraps.
The defeat of Darius I at the Battle of Marathon marked a turning point in Greek history.
Darius I commissioned the construction of the famous Royal Road to improve communication across his empire.

Etymology

From Latin 'Darius', from Ancient Greek 'Δαρεῖος' (Dareîos), from Old Persian 'Dārayavahuš' (holding firm the good).
