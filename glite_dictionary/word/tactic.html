tactic

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæk.tɪk / (AmE), / ˈtæk.tɪk / (BrE)
an action or strategy carefully planned to achieve a specific end
a planned action or strategy

Examples

The coach devised a new tactic to surprise the opposing team.
Using a clever tactic, she managed to negotiate a better deal.
Changing our marketing tactic helped boost sales significantly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strategy - a plan of action designed to achieve a long-term aim
  • Synonym: maneuver - a movement or series of moves requiring skill and care
  • Antonym: blunder - a careless or serious mistake

Etymology

From Greek taktike (art of arrangement), from tassein (to arrange).
