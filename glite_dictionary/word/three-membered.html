three-membered

1. ADJ

/ θriː ˈmɛmbɚd / (AmE), / θriː ˈmɛmbəd / (BrE)
(of a chemical compound) having a ring structure composed of three atoms or groups.
having a three-atom ring (chemistry)

Examples

Cyclopropane is a three-membered ring compound commonly studied in organic chemistry.
The strain in three-membered rings makes them highly reactive compared to larger rings.
Aziridine contains a three-membered ring with one nitrogen atom replacing a carbon.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: four-membered - having a ring with four members (chemistry)
  • Degree Higher: five-membered - having a ring with five members (chemistry)
