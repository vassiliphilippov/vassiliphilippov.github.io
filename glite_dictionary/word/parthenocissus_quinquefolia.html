Parthenocissus quinquefolia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːrθənoʊˈsɪsəs kwɪŋkwɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˌpɑːθənəˈsɪsəs kwɪŋkwɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A vigorous North American climbing vine with palmately compound leaves and clusters of small bluish-black fruit, commonly known as Virginia creeper.
North American climbing plant (Virginia creeper)

Examples

The Parthenocissus quinquefolia quickly covered the old stone wall in just a few years.
Gardeners value Parthenocissus quinquefolia for its brilliant red foliage in autumn.
Birds are attracted to the berries produced by Parthenocissus quinquefolia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Virginia creeper - Common name for Parthenocissus quinquefolia

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, with 'Parthenocissus' meaning 'virgin ivy' and 'quinquefolia' meaning 'five-leaved'.
