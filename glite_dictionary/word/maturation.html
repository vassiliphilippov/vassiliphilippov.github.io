maturation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmætʃəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmatʃəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
(Medicine) The formation of pus in an abscess or vesicle, especially as part of the process leading to discharge.
formation of pus in an abscess
medical

Examples

The doctor observed the maturation of the abscess before performing the procedure.
Proper maturation of the infected area can be crucial for effective drainage.
Antibiotics were prescribed while monitoring the maturation of the pus-filled vesicle.

Etymology

From Latin maturatio (ripening), from maturare (to ripen).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmætʃəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmatjʊˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of growing or developing to full maturity or adulthood
process of becoming mature

Examples

The maturation of the wine takes place over several years in oak barrels.
Physical maturation in adolescents is marked by significant growth spurts.
Her emotional maturation became evident as she learned to handle complex situations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: development - the process of growing or causing something to grow or become larger or more advanced
  • Antonym: immaturity - the state of not being fully grown or developed

Etymology

From Latin 'maturatio', from 'maturare' (to ripen).

3. NOUN

/ ˌmætʃəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmatjʊˈreɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the process by which an individual organism grows and develops, especially as it gradually changes from a simple to a more complex form
biological growth and development
medical

Examples

The maturation of the butterfly involves dramatic changes from larva to adult.
Bone maturation is often used to estimate a child's growth potential.
Researchers study the maturation process to better understand childhood development.
