symbiotically

1. ADV

/ ˌsɪm.biˈɑː.tɪ.kli / (AmE), / ˌsɪm.bɪˈɒ.tɪ.kli / (BrE)
In a way that involves or relates to a close and mutually beneficial relationship between different organisms or groups.
in a symbiotic manner

Examples

The two species live symbiotically, each providing resources the other needs to survive.
Our organizations work symbiotically to achieve greater impact than either could alone.
Certain fungi and plants interact symbiotically to promote growth and nutrient absorption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutually - in a way that is shared by two or more parties

Etymology

From 'symbiotic' + '-ally', ultimately from Greek 'sumbiōsis' (living together).
