commission

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / kəˈmɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the act of committing a crime or an illegal act
the act of committing a crime

Examples

The commission of a crime carries serious consequences under the law.
He was found guilty of the commission of burglary after a lengthy trial.
Laws are designed to prevent the commission of acts that harm others.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: omission - the act of neglecting to do something, especially something required by law

Etymology

From Latin commissio (entrusting, committing), from committere (to commit).
