Dusicyon cancrivorus

1. NOUN

/ duːˈsɪsiːˌɒn ˌkæŋkrɪˈvɔrəs / (AmE), / duːˈsɪsiɒn ˌkaŋkrɪˈvɔːrəs / (BrE)
A species of wild canid, also known as the crab-eating fox, native to northern and central South America.
species of wild canid in South America

Examples

The Dusicyon cancrivorus inhabits forests and grasslands throughout northern South America.
Researchers studied the diet of Dusicyon cancrivorus to understand its role in local ecosystems.
Unlike many other canids, the Dusicyon cancrivorus feeds extensively on crabs and other small animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crab-eating fox - A South American wild canid, common name for Dusicyon cancrivorus

Etymology

From Greek 'dysis' (dusk, twilight) + 'kyon' (dog) and Latin 'cancrivorus' (crab-eating).
