gnome

1. NOUN

/ noʊm / (AmE), / nəʊm / (BrE)
a short, pithy saying expressing a general truth or principle
a concise saying of general truth

Examples

The old philosopher was fond of quoting a Latin gnome to illustrate his point.
Her speech was filled with memorable gnomes that resonated with the audience.
"Know thyself" is a famous gnome found in ancient Greek writings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maxim - a short statement expressing a general truth or rule of conduct
  • Synonym: aphorism - a concise statement of a principle or truth
  • Synonym: proverb - a simple and concrete saying, popularly known and repeated

Etymology

From Greek *gnōmē* (opinion, maxim).

2. NOUN

/ noʊm / (AmE), / nəʊm / (BrE)
a legendary creature resembling a small, elderly man, said to live underground and guard hidden treasures
mythical tiny old man; underground guardian

Examples

In old fairy tales, a gnome is often pictured as a bearded man who lives underground.
The children imagined a gnome guarding a treasure chest beneath the ancient oak tree.
Collectors enjoy placing a gnome statue in their garden for good luck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dwarf - a small mythical humanlike creature, often associated with earth and mining
  • Synonym: elf - a small, mischievous or magical creature from folklore

Etymology

From Renaissance Latin 'gnomus,' introduced in 16th-century alchemical texts.
