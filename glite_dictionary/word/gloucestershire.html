Gloucestershire

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡlɑstərˌʃɪr / (AmE), / ˈɡlɒstəʃə / (BrE)
A county in southwestern England, located in the lower Severn valley, known for its rural landscapes and historic towns.
county in southwestern England

Examples

She spent her childhood in Gloucestershire, surrounded by the beauty of the English countryside.
Many tourists visit Gloucestershire each year to explore its historic villages and rolling hills.
The Cotswolds, a popular scenic area, are partly located within Gloucestershire.

Etymology

From Old English 'Glowecestrescīr', meaning 'shire of Gloucester'.
