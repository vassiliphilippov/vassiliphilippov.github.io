mesodermal

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɛsəˈdɝːməl / (AmE), / ˌmɛsəˈdɜːməl / (BrE)
relating to or derived from the mesoderm, the middle germ layer of an embryo in early development
relating to the mesoderm (embryonic layer)
medical

Examples

The mesodermal layer gives rise to muscles, bones, and the cardiovascular system.
Certain tumors originate from mesodermal tissues within the embryo.
Researchers studied the mesodermal derivatives to understand organ development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mesodermic - relating to the mesoderm

Etymology

From 'mesoderm' + '-al', with 'mesoderm' from Greek 'mesos' (middle) + 'derma' (skin).
