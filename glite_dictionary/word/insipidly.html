insipidly

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈsɪpɪdli / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪpɪdli / (BrE)
In a way that lacks flavor, interest, or liveliness.
in a dull or uninteresting manner

Examples

Her speech was delivered insipidly, failing to energize the audience.
The story was insipidly written, lacking any engaging details or emotions.
He answered the question insipidly, showing little enthusiasm or interest.

Synset Examples

insipidly expressed thoughts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dully - in a dull or uninteresting manner
  • Antonym: vividly - in a way that is lively or full of life

Etymology

From 'insipid' + '-ly', with 'insipid' from Latin 'insipidus' (tasteless).
