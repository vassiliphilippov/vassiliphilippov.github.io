Dunkerque

1. NOUN

/ dʌŋˈkɜrk / (AmE), / dʌŋˈkɜːk / (BrE)
An amphibious evacuation in 1940 during World War II, when 330,000 Allied troops were rescued from the beaches of northern France under fire from German forces.
major WWII Allied evacuation (France, 1940)

Examples

The evacuation at Dunkerque was a turning point in World War II.
Many soldiers owe their lives to the successful retreat from Dunkerque.
The events at Dunkerque continue to inspire stories of courage and resilience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dunkirk - British name for the 1940 WWII evacuation at Dunkerque

Etymology

French name for Dunkirk, from Middle Dutch 'dune' (dune) + 'kerke' (church).

2. PROPN

/ dʌŋˈkɜrk / (AmE), / dʌŋˈkɛək / (BrE)
a seaport in northern France on the North Sea, famous as the site of the evacuation of British forces in 1940 during World War II
French seaport; WWII evacuation site

Examples

The evacuation from Dunkerque was a pivotal moment in World War II history.
Many war veterans have vivid memories of the beaches of Dunkerque.
Tourists visit Dunkerque to learn about its role during the 1940 evacuation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dunkirk - English name for Dunkerque, referring to the same French seaport and historic site

Etymology

French: from Middle Dutch *dune* (dune) + *kerke* (church), meaning 'dune church'.
