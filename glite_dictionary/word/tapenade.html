tapenade

1. NOUN

/ ˌtæpəˈnɑd / (AmE), / ˌtapəˈnɑːd / (BrE)
A savory spread or paste made from finely chopped or pureed capers, black olives, and anchovies mixed with olive oil, popular in Mediterranean cuisine.
olive, caper, and anchovy spread

Examples

We served fresh bread with a bowl of homemade tapenade before dinner.
The salty, savory flavor of tapenade makes it a perfect appetizer for a summer picnic.
She bought a jar of tapenade from the local farmers' market to use on sandwiches.

Etymology

From Provençal French *tapéno* (caper), entered English via French.
