indebtedness

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɛtɪdnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛtɪdnəs / (BrE)
the state of being under an obligation to someone for a service or favor received
state of being obligated to someone

Examples

She felt a deep sense of indebtedness to her mentor, who had guided her career.
His indebtedness to his friends was clear after they helped him through tough times.
He expressed his indebtedness by writing a heartfelt thank you letter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obligation - a duty or commitment one is bound to carry out

Etymology

From Latin 'indebitus' (not owed properly), via 'indebted'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɛtɪdnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛtɪdnəs / (BrE)
the condition of owing money to someone else; the state of being in debt
the state of owing money

Examples

Rising levels of indebtedness have become a concern for many households.
The company's indebtedness forced it to restructure its finances.
He managed to clear his indebtedness after years of careful budgeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debt - a sum of money that is owed
  • Antonym: solvency - the ability to pay all debts

Etymology

From 'indebted' + '-ness', ultimately from Latin 'debitum' (something owed).
