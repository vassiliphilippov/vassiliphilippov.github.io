Tetragonia tetragonioides

1. NOUN

/ tɛtrəˈɡoʊniə tɛtrəˌɡoʊniˈɔɪdiz / (AmE), / tɛtrəˈɡəʊniə tɛtrəˌɡəʊniˈɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
a coarse, sprawling plant native to Australasia, with red or yellow flowers, cultivated for its edible young shoots and succulent leaves; also known as New Zealand spinach
Australasian plant grown for edible leaves

Examples

Tetragonia tetragonioides is often grown as a warm-weather substitute for true spinach in vegetable gardens.
The leaves of Tetragonia tetragonioides are high in nutrients and can be used in salads or cooked dishes.
Botanists recommend planting Tetragonia tetragonioides in well-drained soil for best growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: New Zealand spinach - common name for Tetragonia tetragonioides, used as a synonym

Etymology

From Latin genus name Tetragonia, referring to the plant's four-angled fruit; species epithet from Greek 'tetragonos' (four-angled) and '-oides' (resembling).
