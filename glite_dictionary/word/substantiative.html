substantiative

1. ADJ

/ səbˈstænʃətɪv / (AmE), / səbˈstanʃətɪv / (BrE)
serving to support or corroborate something; providing additional evidence or confirmation.
supporting or corroborating

Examples

The attorney presented substantiative evidence to strengthen her client's case.
Her report offered substantiative data that supported the research findings.
We need substantiative details to corroborate your account of the event.

Synset Examples

collateral evidence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corroborative - serving to confirm or support a statement, finding, or theory
  • Synonym: substantive - having a firm basis in reality; significant

Etymology

From Latin 'substantiatus' (endowed with substance), influenced by English 'substantiate'.
