to discerp

1. VERB

/ dɪˈsɜrp / (AmE), / dɪˈsɜːp / (BrE)
to divide or tear into parts or pieces; to dismember
tear or divide into pieces
archaic

Examples

Historians believe the ancient kingdom was discerped into several smaller states after the king's death.
The storm discerped the ship, leaving its pieces scattered along the shore.
Centuries of conflict discerped the once-united region into warring factions.

Synset Examples

our department was dismembered when our funding dried up
The Empire was discerped after the war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismember - to cut or tear the limbs from; divide up in parts
  • Synonym: fragment - to break into pieces; to split apart

Etymology

From Latin discerpo, discerptum (to tear apart).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈsɝːp / (AmE), / dɪˈsɜːp / (BrE)
to cut off or separate something from a whole, especially by force
cut off from a whole
archaic

Examples

In ancient texts, it was believed that the soul could discerp from the mortal body at death.
The violent storm seemed to discerp the tree's branches from its trunk.
Scholars once theorized how emotions might discerp reason from decision.

Synset Examples

His head was severed from his body
The soul discerped from the body

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sever - to cut off or separate, especially by cutting
  • Antonym: to connect - to join or link together

Etymology

From Latin discerpre (to pluck or tear asunder), from dis- (apart) + carpere (to pluck).
