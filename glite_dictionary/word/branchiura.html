Branchiura

1. NOUN

/ brænˈkiːjʊrə / (AmE), / brænˈkiːjʊərə / (BrE)
an order of copepod crustaceans with suctorial mouthparts, parasitic on fishes
order of parasitic copepods on fish

Examples

The Branchiura are known for their parasitic lifestyle on freshwater and marine fish.
Researchers studied how Branchiura attach to their hosts using specialized suctorial mouthparts.
Certain species within Branchiura can cause significant problems in aquaculture environments.

Etymology

From Greek 'branchia' (gills) + 'oura' (tail).

2. NOUN

/ brænˈkaɪjʊrə / (AmE), / braŋˈkɪjʊərə / (BrE)
An order of parasitic copepod crustaceans that possess suctorial mouthparts and are primarily parasitic on fishes.
order of parasitic copepods

Examples

Members of the Branchiura are known for attaching themselves to the skin or gills of freshwater fish.
The Branchiura order includes several species that can impact fish populations in aquaculture.
Researchers have studied the anatomy of Branchiura to understand how their suctorial mouthparts evolved.

Etymology

From Greek 'branchia' (gills) + 'oura' (tail), referring to the physical features of these crustaceans.
