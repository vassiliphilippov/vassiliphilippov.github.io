Polymonium caeruleum van-bruntiae

1. NOUN

/ pəˌlɪˈmoʊniəm sɛˌruːliəm vænˈbrʌntaɪ.i / (AmE), / pɒˌlɪˈməʊniəm sɪˌruːliəm vænˈbrʌntaɪ.i / (BrE)
A perennial European plant with pinnate leaves and bright blue or white flowers, classified as a subspecies of Jacob's ladder.
a perennial plant with blue or white flowers

Examples

The Polymonium caeruleum van-bruntiae thrives in moist, wooded habitats across parts of Europe.
Gardeners admire Polymonium caeruleum van-bruntiae for its delicate blue flowers in late spring.
Conservationists are working to protect wild populations of Polymonium caeruleum van-bruntiae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jacob's ladder - common name for Polymonium caeruleum, a related plant

Etymology

From genus name Polymonium + Latin 'caeruleum' (blue) + named for Mary Van Brunt, an American botanist.
