wholesale

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊlˌseɪl / (AmE), / ˈhəʊlseɪl / (BrE)
the sale of goods in large quantities, typically to retailers rather than to end consumers
sale of goods in bulk to retailers

Examples

The company deals primarily in wholesale and supplies small retailers throughout the region.
Changes in wholesale prices can affect how much consumers pay in stores.
They buy fruit at wholesale and sell it at local markets.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: retail - the sale of goods directly to consumers

Etymology

From Middle English, from the phrase 'whole sale', meaning 'the entire quantity sold at one time'.

2. ADV

/ ˈhoʊlˌseɪl / (AmE), / ˈhəʊlseɪl / (BrE)
In a way that involves large quantities or affects many things or people without careful selection.
in large quantities; without discrimination

Examples

After the scandal, the company made wholesale changes to its policies.
Rumors of downsizing led to wholesale layoffs in the department.
Critics condemned the wholesale destruction of the historic neighborhood.

Synset Examples

I buy food wholesale

Related Terms

  • Antonym: retail - selling in small quantities to the public

Etymology

From the noun 'wholesale', combining 'whole' and 'sale', originating from Middle English.

/ ˈhoʊlˌseɪl / (AmE), / ˈhəʊlseɪl / (BrE)
At a wholesale price; in a manner involving the sale of goods in large quantities to be retailed by others.
at a wholesale price; in bulk for resale

Examples

The supplier offered to sell the goods wholesale to small businesses.
If you buy these shirts wholesale, you'll get a much better price.
Many stores purchase products wholesale and then mark them up for retail sale.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: retail - at a retail price; in small quantities to consumers

Etymology

4. ADJ

/ ˈhoʊlˌseɪl / (AmE), / ˈhəʊlseɪl / (BrE)
(of an action) done on a large scale without regard for distinctions or exceptions; indiscriminate or sweeping.
indiscriminate; undifferentiated

Examples

The reforms led to the wholesale closure of small businesses across the city.
Critics condemned the wholesale destruction of historic buildings to make way for new developments.
He rejected the idea of making wholesale changes to the policy without proper review.

5. VERB

/ ˈhoʊlˌseɪl / (AmE), / ˈhəʊlseɪl / (BrE)
To sell goods in large quantities, typically to retailers rather than to individual consumers.
sell in large quantities (to retailers)

Examples

The company decided to wholesale its products to a network of small retailers.
Some manufacturers prefer to wholesale directly rather than work with distributors.
As demand increased, they began wholesaling to supermarkets across the region.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: retail - To sell goods in small quantities to consumers

Etymology

From whole + sale, influenced by Middle Dutch wholesale (in bulk).
