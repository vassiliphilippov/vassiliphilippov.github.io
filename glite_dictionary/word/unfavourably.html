unfavourably

1. ADV

/ ʌnˈfeɪvərəbli / (AmE), / ʌnˈfeɪvərəbli / (BrE)
in a way that shows disapproval or a negative opinion
with disapproval or negativity
british

Examples

The committee responded unfavourably to his proposal.
Her work was judged unfavourably compared to her colleagues'.
The policy has been described unfavourably in several recent reports.

Synset Examples

he reviewed the play unfavorably

Related Terms

  • Antonym: favorably - in a way that shows approval or a positive opinion

Etymology

Formed by adding the suffix -ly to the adjective 'unfavourable', itself from Latin 'favorem' (favor) with the negative prefix 'un-'.
