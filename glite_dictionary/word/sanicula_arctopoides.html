Sanicula arctopoides

1. NOUN

/ səˈnɪkjʊlə ɑːrktoʊˈpaɪdiːz / (AmE), / səˈnɪkjʊlə ɑːktəʊˈpɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A species of sanicle native to the northwestern United States and British Columbia, known for its yellow flowers.
a sanicle plant with yellow flowers, native to northwest North America

Examples

The coastal grasslands are home to Sanicula arctopoides, recognized by its distinct yellow blooms.
Researchers studied the population decline of Sanicula arctopoides along the Oregon coast.
Sanicula arctopoides is valued for its unique foliage and contribution to native plant diversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanicle - any plant of the genus Sanicula

Etymology

Sanicula from Latin 'sanus' (healthy); species name combines Greek 'arktos' (bear) and 'pous' (foot), referring to leaf shape.
