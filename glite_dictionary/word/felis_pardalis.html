Felis pardalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːlɪs pɑːrˈdælɪs / (AmE), / ˈfiːlɪs pɑːˈdalɪs / (BrE)
A nocturnal wildcat of Central and South America, also known as the ocelot, with a dark-spotted buff-brown coat.
nocturnal wildcat of Central/South America

Examples

The Felis pardalis uses its keen senses to hunt small mammals and birds at night.
Researchers have tracked the movement of Felis pardalis populations in the Amazon rainforest.
The striking coat pattern of Felis pardalis makes it a target for illegal wildlife trade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ocelot - another name for Felis pardalis, a spotted wildcat of the Americas

Etymology

From Latin 'felis' (cat) and 'pardalis' (leopard-like).
