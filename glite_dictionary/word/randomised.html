randomised

1. ADJ

/ ˈræn.dəˌmaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈræn.dəˌmaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a process, experiment, or distribution) set up or assigned in a deliberately random way.
arranged or assigned randomly

Examples

The study was a randomised controlled trial involving 200 participants.
Patients were randomised into two groups to ensure fairness in the experiment.
A randomised sample was selected to avoid selection bias.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: randomized - Same as 'randomised'; US spelling

Etymology

From 'random' + '-ise' (verb-forming suffix) + '-ed' (past participle ending).
