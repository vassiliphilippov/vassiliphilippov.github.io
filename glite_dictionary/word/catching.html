catching

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkætʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of noticing or detecting something, such as catching sight of an object or event
act of noticing or detecting

Examples

The sudden catching of the shadow made her pause in fear.
His catching of the thief was pure luck and quick observation.
The catching of her name in the conversation drew his attention.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkætʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkatʃɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of playing the position of catcher in a baseball team.
playing the catcher position in baseball

Examples

He excelled at catching during his years on the high school baseball team.
After years of catching, he moved to the outfield for a new challenge.
Her knowledge of catching made her a valuable coach for young players.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catcher - the defensive player positioned behind home plate in baseball

3. ADJ

/ ˈkætʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkatʃɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a disease) able to be passed from one person to another; contagious.
contagious, easily spread disease

Examples

The flu is highly catching, so be sure to wash your hands frequently.
He stayed home from work, worried that his cold might be catching.
Chickenpox is a catching disease, particularly in schools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contagious - (of a disease) able to be spread directly from one person or organism to another

Etymology

From the verb 'catch' + -ing, meaning a disease that can be 'caught' from another person.
