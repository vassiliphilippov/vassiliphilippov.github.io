wai

1. NOUN

/ waɪ / (AmE), / waɪ / (BrE)
a traditional Thai greeting in which the palms are pressed together in front of the chest, often accompanied by a slight bow
traditional Thai greeting with pressed palms

Examples

When meeting someone older in Thailand, it is customary to give a respectful wai.
She greeted the monk with a wai, showing deep respect for his position.
Tourists are often taught how to perform the wai correctly when visiting temples.

Synset Examples

The traditional greeting known as the wai, where you press your hands together as is in prayer and bow slightly, is derived from the Hindu cultural influence from India, and still widely practised.
Among Thais, there are strict rules of hierarchy that dictate how and when the wai should be given.

Etymology

From Thai ไหว้ (wâi), meaning 'to pay respect' or 'to greet'.

2. VERB

/ waɪ / (AmE), / waɪ / (BrE)
to perform the traditional Thai greeting by placing the palms together in a prayer-like gesture, usually accompanied by a small bow
perform a Thai greeting with palms together

Examples

When entering a Thai temple, it is customary to wai as a sign of respect.
She waied politely to the elders at the beginning of the ceremony.
Newcomers are often unsure when and how to wai during formal events.

Synset Examples

You should not wai service people or street vendors.
As a foreign visitor, you are not expected to know how to wai, nor to reciprocate when wai'd to

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bow - to bend forward as a gesture of respect or greeting

Etymology

From Thai ไหว้ (wâi), meaning 'to pay respect.'
