Marcus Aurelius

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɑrkəs ɔːˈriːliəs / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkəs ɔːˈriːliəs / (BrE)
(121–180) Roman emperor, Stoic philosopher, and author whose reign marked the beginning of the decline of the Roman Empire.
Roman emperor and Stoic philosopher

Examples

Marcus Aurelius is remembered both as a powerful ruler and as a thoughtful philosopher.
In his book 'Meditations', Marcus Aurelius discusses themes of duty and resilience.
Many students of philosophy admire Marcus Aurelius for his practical approach to Stoicism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Antoninus Pius - Roman emperor and adoptive father of Marcus Aurelius

2. PROPN

/ ˈmɑrkəs ɔːˈriliəs / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkəs ɔːˈriːliəs / (BrE)
(121–180 AD) Roman emperor and Stoic philosopher, known for his leadership during the decline of the Roman Empire and for his philosophical writings.
Roman emperor and Stoic philosopher

Examples

Marcus Aurelius was both a Roman emperor and a prominent figure in the Stoic philosophical tradition.
Many people are inspired by the teachings found in Marcus Aurelius's 'Meditations'.
The reign of Marcus Aurelius is often seen as the last period of the Pax Romana.

Etymology

Latin name; 'Marcus' from the Roman praenomen, 'Aurelius' from his adoptive family name.
