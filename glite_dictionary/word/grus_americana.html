Grus americana

1. NOUN

/ ɡruːs əˌmɛrɪˈkænə / (AmE), / ɡruːs əˌmɛrɪˈkɑːnɪə / (BrE)
A rare North American crane with striking black-and-white plumage and a loud trumpeting call; commonly known as the whooping crane.
a rare North American crane (whooping crane)

Examples

Conservationists are working hard to protect the endangered Grus americana in its natural wetland habitat.
The distinctive trumpeting call of the Grus americana can be heard during its migration.
Researchers track the nests of Grus americana to monitor the population's recovery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whooping crane - common name for Grus americana, a rare North American crane

Etymology

From the genus name 'Grus' (Latin for 'crane') and the species epithet 'americana', indicating American origin.
