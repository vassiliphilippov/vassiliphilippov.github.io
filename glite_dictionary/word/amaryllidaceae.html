Amaryllidaceae

1. NOUN

/ əˌmærɪlɪˈdeɪsiː / (AmE), / əˌmærɪlɪˈdeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants that includes snowdrops, daffodils, and narcissus, sometimes treated as a subfamily of Liliaceae in some classification systems.
family of flowering plants (snowdrops, daffodils, etc.)

Examples

The Amaryllidaceae family includes popular garden plants like daffodils and snowdrops.
Some botanists consider Amaryllidaceae to be a subfamily within the Liliaceae.
Members of the Amaryllidaceae are known for their beautiful, often fragrant flowers.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Amaryllis (a genus in the family) + -aceae (a standard botanical family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæm.əˌrɪl.ɪˈdeɪ.siː / (AmE), / ˌæm.ərɪ.lɪˈdeɪ.siː / (BrE)
a family of perennial, bulbous flowering plants including snowdrops, narcissus, and daffodils, sometimes considered a subfamily of the Liliaceae in older classification systems.
a family of bulbous flowering plants

Examples

Many popular garden plants, such as the daffodil, belong to the Amaryllidaceae family.
The Amaryllidaceae includes well-known genera like snowdrop and narcissus.
Botanists once classified Amaryllidaceae as a subfamily of the lily family.

Etymology

From New Latin Amaryllidaceae, based on Amaryllis, the type genus, named after a shepherdess in Virgil’s Eclogues.
