to summate

1. VERB

/ sʌˈmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌmeɪt / (BrE)
to form or constitute a cumulative effect by adding together parts or elements
form a cumulative effect

Examples

The minor incidents summate to create significant stress over time.
In neurons, electrical signals summate to trigger an action potential.
Small errors in calculation can summate into a major discrepancy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accumulate - gather together or build up over time
  • Synonym: aggregate - combine into a whole

Etymology

From Latin 'summatus', past participle of 'summare' (to sum up), from 'summa' (sum, total).

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ səˈmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌmeɪt / (BrE)
to find or determine the total sum of a set of numbers, amounts, or items
to add up; to total

Examples

The results from each experiment were summated to calculate the overall effect.
She carefully summated the expenses to ensure nothing was left out.
When the points were summated, our team won by a narrow margin.

Synset Examples

Add all the people in this town to those of the neighboring town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to add - to combine numbers or quantities to get a total
  • Antonym: to subtract - to take a number or amount away from another

Etymology

From Latin 'summatus', past participle of 'summare' (to sum up), from 'summa' (sum, total).
