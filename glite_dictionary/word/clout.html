clout

1. NOUN

/ klaʊt / (AmE), / klaʊt / (BrE)
a target placed on the ground used in archery, often as a mark for long-distance shooting
archery target on the ground

Examples

The archers aimed their arrows at the distant clout set up on the field.
During the competition, hitting the clout scored more points than any other target.
He proudly retrieved his arrow from the center of the clout after a perfect shot.

Etymology

From Old English *clūt* (a piece of cloth), originally referring to a piece of cloth used as a target.
