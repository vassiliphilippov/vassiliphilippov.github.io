mortician

1. NOUN

/ mɔrˈtɪʃən / (AmE), / mɔːˈtɪʃən / (BrE)
a person whose job is to arrange and manage funerals, including the preparation of the deceased for burial or cremation
funeral director or undertaker

Examples

The mortician handled all of the funeral arrangements with great sensitivity.
After the ceremony, the mortician led the family to the graveside for the final farewell.
She decided to become a mortician after volunteering at a hospice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undertaker - a person whose job is to arrange funerals and prepare the dead for burial or cremation
  • Synonym: funeral director - a person who manages funerals and related services

Etymology

Formed in the United States in the early 20th century, from Latin 'mort-' (death) and the suffix '-ician' (one skilled in).
