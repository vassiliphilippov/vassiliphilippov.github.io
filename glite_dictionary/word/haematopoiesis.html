haematopoiesis

1. NOUN

/ hiˌmætəpoʊˈiːsɪs / (AmE), / hiːˌmatəʊpɔɪˈiːsɪs / (BrE)
The process by which blood cells are formed in the living body, especially in the bone marrow.
formation of blood cells
medical

Examples

The study focused on how haematopoiesis is regulated within the bone marrow.
Disorders of haematopoiesis can lead to various blood diseases.
Understanding haematopoiesis is essential for developing new treatments for anemia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hematopoiesis - American English spelling of haematopoiesis; blood cell formation

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + poiesis (making, formation).
