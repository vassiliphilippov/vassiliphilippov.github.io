gynecology

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡaɪnəˈkɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌɡɪnɪˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of medicine concerned with the health, diseases, and hygiene of the female reproductive system.
branch of medicine for women's reproductive health
medical

Examples

She decided to specialize in gynecology because of her interest in women's health.
After her medical degree, she completed a residency in gynecology and obstetrics.
The hospital has an entire department dedicated to gynecology and reproductive care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstetrics - the branch of medicine and surgery concerned with childbirth and the care of women giving birth

Etymology

From Greek gynaik-, gunē (woman) + -logia (study).
