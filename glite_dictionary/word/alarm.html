alarm

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɑrm / (AmE), / əˈlɑːm / (BrE)
a clock designed to wake someone at a set time by making a sound or signal
clock that wakes a person at a set time

Examples

My alarm went off at six o'clock this morning, waking me up for work.
He set his alarm to ensure he wouldn't oversleep for the meeting.
The loud ring of the alarm startled everyone in the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alarm clock - a clock with a mechanism that triggers a sound or signal at a set time
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02697521-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ əˈlɑrm / (AmE), / əˈlɑːm / (BrE)
a sudden feeling of fear or anxiety caused by the realization of danger or threat
sudden fear or anxiety from danger

Examples

There was a sense of alarm in the city after the earthquake.
She could not hide her alarm when she heard the unexpected news.
His face showed visible alarm as the car skidded on the icy road.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: panic - sudden uncontrollable fear or anxiety
  • Antonym: calm - state of being peaceful and untroubled

Etymology

From Italian 'all'arme' (to arms), a call to alert or warn.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07535012-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ əˈlɑrm / (AmE), / əˈlɑːm / (BrE)
a device that makes a loud sound or signal to warn people that something bad or dangerous may happen
device that gives warning signal

Examples

When the fire alarm sounded, everyone left the building immediately.
She set an alarm to wake her up at 6 a.m. every morning.
The house's security alarm was triggered by a stray cat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alert - a warning signal, especially one announcing danger

Etymology

From Italian all'arme ('to arms!'), a warning cry.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02697285-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ əˈlɑrm / (AmE), / əˈlɑːm / (BrE)
to make someone feel frightened or worried about a possible danger or problem
make someone afraid or concerned

Examples

The news of the approaching storm alarmed local residents, prompting evacuations.
The sudden drop in temperature alarmed scientists monitoring climate changes in the area.
She didn't want to alarm her family by calling them late at night.

Synset Examples

The empty house alarmed him
We alerted the new neighbors to the high rate of burglaries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alert - to warn someone of danger or put on guard
  • Antonym: to reassure - to say or do something to remove someone's fears

Etymology

From Italian all'arme (to arms), a call to prepare for battle.

Source: WordNet

/ əˈlɑrm / (AmE), / əˈlɑːm / (BrE)
to fill someone with fear, anxiety, or sudden worry; to cause to be unpleasantly surprised or concerned
to cause fear or sudden worry

Examples

News of the sudden policy change alarmed many employees.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to frighten - to make someone feel afraid or anxious
  • Antonym: to reassure - to make someone feel less worried

From Italian all'arme (to arms), originally a call to action in battle, later extended to general warning and fear.

6. NOUN

/ əˈlɑrm / (AmE), / əˈlɑːm / (BrE)
a device or signal that automatically warns of danger, typically by making a loud noise or sound
automatic warning signal or device

Examples

The fire alarm rang loudly throughout the building, signaling everyone to evacuate.
She installed a burglar alarm to protect her house from intruders at night.
We heard the car alarm go off in the middle of the night and looked outside to check.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alert - an immediate warning of danger or need for action
  • Synonym: warning - a statement or event that indicates possible danger or problem

Etymology

From Italian 'allarme', from 'all’arme!' meaning 'to arms!', used to alert of danger.

Source: WordNet
