however

1. ADV

/ haʊˈɛvɚ / (AmE), / haʊˈɛvə / (BrE)
In whatever way or manner; by whatever means.
in whatever way or manner

Examples

However you solve the problem, be sure to check your work at the end.
They promised to help, however they could.
However it happened, we are glad you arrived safely.

Synset Examples

Victory, however it was brought about, was sweet
however he did it, it was very clever

Etymology

From Middle English, from how + ever.

2. ADV

/ haʊˈɛvər / (AmE), / haʊˈɛvə / (BrE)
To whatever degree or extent; no matter how.
to whatever degree or extent

Examples

However difficult the task may seem, we must continue to try.
He remained positive, however challenging the situation became.
However hard you work, there will always be more to learn.

Synset Examples

The results, however general, are important
they have begun, however reluctantly, to acknowledge the legitimacy of some of the opposition's concerns

Etymology

From Middle English 'how ever', meaning 'in whatever way'.

3. ADV

/ haʊˈɛvɚ / (AmE), / haʊˈɛvə / (BrE)
Used to introduce a statement that contrasts with or seems to contradict something that has been said previously; nevertheless; in spite of that.
to introduce a contrast or concession

Examples

I was tired after a long day; however, I decided to go for a run.
The new rules are strict. However, most employees appreciate the clarity they bring.
She didn’t like the movie; however, she enjoyed spending time with her friends.

Synset Examples

although I'm a little afraid, however I'd like to try it
while we disliked each other, nevertheless we agreed
he was a stern yet fair master
granted that it is dangerous, all the same I still want to go

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nevertheless - in spite of that; notwithstanding
  • Synonym: nonetheless - in spite of that; nevertheless
  • Synonym: yet - used to introduce something contrasting with what has already been mentioned

Etymology

From Middle English, from 'how' + 'ever'.

4. ADV

/ haʊˈɛvɚ / (AmE), / haʊˈɛvə / (BrE)
Used to introduce a statement that contrasts with or seems to contradict something that has been said previously.
contrasting conjunction/adverb

Examples

The report is finished; however, we still need to check the data for errors.
I wanted to go for a walk. However, it started raining heavily.
The meal looked delicious. However, it was disappointingly bland.

Synset Examples

the first part was easy; the second, however, took hours

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nevertheless - in spite of that; notwithstanding
  • Synonym: but - used to introduce a statement that contrasts with or seems to contradict something that has been said previously
  • Antonym: moreover - used to add information or emphasize what is being said

Etymology

From Middle English 'hou ever', from 'how' + 'ever'
