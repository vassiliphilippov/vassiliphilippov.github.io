totality

1. NOUN

/ toʊˈtæləti / (AmE), / təʊˈtælɪti / (BrE)
the state of being complete or whole, without exception or exclusion
the state of being complete or whole

Examples

The philosopher argued that only by understanding the totality of human experience can we approach the truth.
The totality of the evidence convinced the jury of the defendant's innocence.
He admired the totality of her commitment to the cause, leaving nothing undone.

Synset Examples

the totality of war and its consequences
the all-embracing totality of the state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wholeness - the state of being unbroken or undivided
  • Antonym: partiality - the state of being partial or incomplete

Etymology

From Medieval Latin totalitas, from Latin totus (whole, entire).

2. NOUN

/ toʊˈtæləti / (AmE), / təʊˈtælɪti / (BrE)
the whole of something; the state of being total or complete
the whole; completeness

Examples

The totality of the evidence convinced the jury of his guilt.
We must consider the totality of circumstances before making a decision.
During the eclipse, the moon entered the totality phase, completely blocking the sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entirety - the whole of something; the state of being entire
  • Antonym: part - a portion or section; not the whole

Etymology

From Late Latin totalitas, from Latin totus (whole, entire).
