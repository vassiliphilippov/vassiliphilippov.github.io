Quercus montana

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs mɒnˈteɪnə / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs mɒnˈteɪnə / (BrE)
A species of oak tree native to the eastern United States, valued for its durable wood used in timber, baskets, and chair seats.
oak tree species of eastern USA

Examples

The wood of the Quercus montana is highly prized for its strength and durability.
Baskets crafted from split Quercus montana are a tradition in some Appalachian communities.
Many forests in the eastern U.S. are home to the majestic Quercus montana.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a deciduous tree or shrub of the genus Quercus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Quercus' meaning 'oak', 'montana' meaning 'of the mountains'.
