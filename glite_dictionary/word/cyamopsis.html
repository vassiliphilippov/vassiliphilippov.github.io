Cyamopsis

1. NOUN

/ saɪ.əˈmɑp.sɪs / (AmE), / saɪ.əˈmɒp.sɪs / (BrE)
A small genus of annual, usually hairy herbs native to tropical Africa and Arabia.
genus of annual herbs in Africa and Arabia

Examples

Researchers studied how Cyamopsis adapts to arid climates in northern Africa.
The most well-known species within Cyamopsis is the guar plant, used for its gum.
Botanists have identified several medicinal uses for members of the Cyamopsis genus.

Etymology

From Greek 'kyamos' (bean) and the suffix '-opsis' (appearance), referring to the plant's bean-like appearance.
