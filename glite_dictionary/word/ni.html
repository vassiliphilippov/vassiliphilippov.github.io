Ni

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈnɪkəl / (BrE)
the chemical symbol for nickel, a hard, silvery-white metallic element resistant to corrosion, used in alloys.
chemical symbol for nickel

Examples

Stainless steel contains Ni to improve its resistance to corrosion.
The periodic table lists Ni as element number 28.
Engineers selected an alloy high in Ni for its durability in harsh environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nickel - a hard, silvery-white metallic element, symbol Ni, atomic number 28.

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'nickel,' from German 'Nickel,' short for 'Kupfernickel' (false copper).
