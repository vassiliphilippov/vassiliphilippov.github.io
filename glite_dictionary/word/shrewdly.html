shrewdly

1. ADV

/ ˈʃruːdli / (AmE), / ˈʃruːdli / (BrE)
In a way that shows clever judgment, sharp insight, or astuteness, especially in practical matters.
in a clever or astute manner

Examples

She shrewdly negotiated the contract to ensure the best possible terms for her company.
By shrewdly observing the market trends, he made several profitable investments.
The detective shrewdly deduced who committed the crime from the smallest details.

Synset Examples

he invested his fortune astutely
he was acutely insightful

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astutely - in a clever or perceptive manner

Etymology

From 'shrewd', originally meaning 'wicked' or 'cunning', later 'clever'; ultimately related to 'shrew'.
