columella

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɒl.juˈmɛl.ə / (AmE), / ˌkɒl.juˈmɛl.ə / (BrE)
a small column or column-like structure found as a part of certain plants or animals, especially in biological anatomy
small column-like structure in biology
medical

Examples

The nasal columella separates the nostrils and can vary in shape.
In gastropods, the columella is the central spiral axis of the shell.
Botanists observed the columella cells within the root cap of the plant.

Etymology

From Latin columella, diminutive of columna (column).
