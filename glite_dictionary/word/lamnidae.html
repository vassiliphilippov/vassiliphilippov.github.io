Lamnidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæm.nɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈlam.nɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, fast-swimming oceanic sharks known for their pointed snouts and robust bodies, including the great white shark and mako sharks.
family of oceanic sharks

Examples

The Lamnidae family includes some of the most formidable predators in the ocean.
Scientists study the migration patterns of Lamnidae to learn about shark ecology.
Several species within Lamnidae are threatened due to overfishing and habitat loss.

Etymology

From New Latin Lamnidae, based on Lamna (a genus of sharks) + -idae, a zoological family suffix.
