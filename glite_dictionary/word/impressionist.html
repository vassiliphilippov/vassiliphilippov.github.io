impressionist

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈprɛʃənɪst / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɛʃənɪst / (BrE)
an artist who practices or follows the style and theories of Impressionism, a 19th-century movement characterized by the depiction of light and color in painting.
artist practicing Impressionism

Examples

Many art historians consider Monet the greatest impressionist of his generation.
The gallery features several works by French impressionists from the late 1800s.
She admired the vibrant colors used by the impressionist in his paintings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Impressionism - 19th-century art movement focusing on light and color

Etymology

From French impressionniste, coined in the 19th century from 'impression' (influence from Monet’s painting 'Impression, soleil levant').

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈprɛʃənɪst / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɛʃənɪst / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of Impressionism, especially in art, music, or literature
relating to Impressionism

Examples

The gallery is hosting an exhibit of impressionist paintings this spring.
She prefers impressionist music for its light, evocative qualities.
Critics praised his impressionist writing style for its vivid imagery.

Synset Examples

impressionist music

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impressionistic - pertaining to the Impressionist style or movement

Etymology

From French 'impressionniste', originally referring to artists influenced by the painting 'Impression, Sunrise' by Monet.
