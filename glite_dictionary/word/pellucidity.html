pellucidity

1. NOUN

/ pəˌluːˈsɪdədi / (AmE), / pɛlˌjuːˈsɪdɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being clear and easy to understand; clarity of expression or thought
clarity; clearness of expression

Examples

The pellucidity of the professor's lectures made complex topics accessible to all students.
Her writing is admired for its pellucidity and precision, avoiding unnecessary jargon.
Critics praised the novel for the pellucidity of its narrative style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to understand
  • Antonym: obscurity - the state of being difficult to understand

Etymology

From Latin pellucidus (transparent, clear), from per- (through) + lucidus (bright, clear).

2. NOUN

/ pəˌluːˈsɪdəti / (AmE), / pɛljuːˈsɪdəti / (BrE)
the quality of allowing light to pass through without diffusion or distortion; transparency or clearness
the quality of being transparent or clear

Examples

The pellucidity of the crystal allowed us to see right through to the other side.
Scientists measured the pellucidity of the water to determine its purity.
The painting's colors were enhanced by the pellucidity of the glass overlay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transparency - the quality of being able to see through something; lack of opacity
  • Antonym: opacity - the quality of not allowing light to pass through; lack of transparency

Etymology

From Latin 'pellucidus' (transparent, clear), from 'per-' (through) + 'lucidus' (clear, bright).
