Inchon

1. PROPN

/ ɪnˈtʃɑn / (AmE), / ɪnˈtʃɒn / (BrE)
A port city in western South Korea on the Yellow Sea, known for its strategic significance and international airport.
port city in South Korea

Examples

The flight from San Francisco arrived in Inchon late in the evening.
Many historical events took place in Inchon during the Korean War.
She plans to study abroad at a university near Inchon next year.

2. PROPN

/ ˈɪn.tʃɒn / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tʃɒn / (BrE)
a major battle during the Korean War in 1950, in which United States forces landed at Inchon, turning the tide of the conflict
battle in the Korean War, 1950

Examples

The Battle of Inchon was a turning point in the Korean War.
Historians credit the success at Inchon with shifting the momentum in favor of United Nations forces.
General MacArthur masterminded the daring landing at Inchon in September 1950.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Korean War - war fought on the Korean Peninsula from 1950 to 1953

Etymology

From the name of the city of Incheon, South Korea, near where the battle occurred.
