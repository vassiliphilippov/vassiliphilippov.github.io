Momordica balsamina

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈmɔrdɪkə bælˈsæmɪnə / (AmE), / məˈmɔːdɪkə bælˈsæmɪnə / (BrE)
A tropical Old World flowering vine known for its red or orange warty fruit, commonly called balsam apple.
tropical vine with warty fruit

Examples

The Momordica balsamina vine climbs over fences in many tropical gardens.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of Momordica balsamina fruit.
The bright orange fruit of Momordica balsamina is often used in traditional remedies.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, from Latin 'balsamum' (balsam) and 'Momordica' (a genus name from Latin 'momordicus', meaning 'to bite', referencing the bitten appearance of the seed).
