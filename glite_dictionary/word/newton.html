Newton

1. PROPN

/ ˈnuːtən / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtən / (BrE)
Sir Isaac Newton (1642–1727), English mathematician, physicist, and astronomer, known for formulating the laws of motion and universal gravitation.
English mathematician and physicist (1642–1727)

Examples

Newton revolutionized physics with his formulation of the laws of motion.
Many students learn about Newton and his laws in high school science classes.
The work of Newton laid the foundation for modern physics.
