Schomburgkia

1. NOUN

/ ʃɒmˈbɜrɡkiə / (AmE), / ʃɒmˈbɜːɡkiə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American epiphytic orchids known for their showy cluster-like racemes of flowers.
genus of tropical American orchids

Examples

The vibrant blooms of the Schomburgkia make it a favorite among orchid collectors.
Botanists discovered a rare species belonging to the Schomburgkia genus in the Amazon rainforest.
She carefully pollinated her Schomburgkia to encourage more flowers next season.

Etymology

Named after Richard Schomburgk, a German botanist and explorer.

2. NOUN

/ ʃɒmˈbɜːrɡkiə / (AmE), / ʃɒmˈbɜːɡkiə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American epiphytic orchids with showy racemose flowers, formerly classified as distinct but now often included in Laelia.
genus of tropical American orchids

Examples

Several species formerly belonging to Schomburgkia are now classified under other genera.
The vibrant blooms of Schomburgkia orchids are prized by collectors.
Botanists debated the correct classification of Schomburgkia for many years.

Etymology

Named for Sir Robert Hermann Schomburgk, a German-born British explorer and botanist.
