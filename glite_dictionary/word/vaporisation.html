vaporisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌveɪpərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌveɪpəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process by which a substance changes from a liquid or solid into a vapor.
process of becoming vapor

Examples

The vaporisation of water begins to occur rapidly at 100°C.
During the experiment, the vaporisation of the chemical was carefully measured.
Efficient vaporisation is crucial in the operation of steam engines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evaporation - the process of a liquid turning into vapor, usually at a temperature below its boiling point
  • Antonym: condensation - the process of vapor turning back into liquid

Etymology

From Latin 'vapor' (steam) and '-isation', after 'vaporize'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌveɪpɚəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌveɪpərəˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The complete destruction or annihilation of something by turning it into vapor; the process of making something vanish by vaporizing it.
total destruction by vaporizing

Examples

The intense heat from the explosion caused the vaporisation of the entire building.
Scientists studied the vaporisation of metals under extreme laboratory conditions.
Any threat of invasion would meet with immediate vaporisation by advanced weaponry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vaporization - the act of converting into vapor, especially by heat

Etymology

From vapor + -isation; modelled after Latin vapor (steam) and based on the verb 'to vaporize.'
