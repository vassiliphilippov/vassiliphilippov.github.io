exode

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛkˌsoʊd / (AmE), / ˈɛksəʊd / (BrE)
a farcical short play performed as an afterpiece in ancient Roman theater, typically following the main performance.
farcical afterpiece in Roman theater

Examples

The troupe concluded their performance with a lively exode that had the audience roaring with laughter.
In Roman theater, the exode often provided comic relief after a serious tragedy.
Scholars have uncovered scripts of several ancient exodes that hint at their popularity among Roman audiences.

Etymology

From Latin 'exodus', meaning a going out or departure, influenced by Greek theatrical terms.
