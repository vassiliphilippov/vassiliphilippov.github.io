Teiidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪ.i.ɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈteɪ.ɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of chiefly tropical and subtropical American lizards, commonly known as whiptails, with long slender bodies and tails
family of tropical lizards (whiptails)

Examples

The Teiidae family includes many species of active, fast-moving lizards.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of several Teiidae in South America.
Whiptails, members of the Teiidae, can sometimes reproduce without males.

Etymology

From New Latin Teiidae, derived from the genus name Teius + -idae (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).

2. NOUN

/ teɪˈɪɪ.diˌaɪ / (AmE), / teɪˈɪɪ.diˌaɪ / (BrE)
A family of mainly New World lizards known as whiptails and their relatives, characterized by their long slender bodies and active behavior.
family of New World lizards (whiptails)
medical

Examples

Many species in the Teiidae family are fast-moving and diurnal.
Researchers discovered a new member of the Teiidae in the Amazon Rainforest.
The Teiidae are commonly referred to as whiptails due to their long tails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whiptail - A type of slender, active lizard often found in the Americas

Etymology

From New Latin Teiidae, from Teius (a genus of lizards) + -idae (denoting a zoological family).
