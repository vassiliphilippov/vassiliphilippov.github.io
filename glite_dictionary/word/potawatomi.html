Potawatomi

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊtəˈwɑːtəmi / (AmE), / ˌpɒtəˈwætəmi / (BrE)
the Algonquian language traditionally spoken by the Potawatomi people of North America
the Potawatomi language

Examples

Few people today are fluent speakers of Potawatomi.
He is learning to translate stories into Potawatomi.
The dictionary contains over five thousand Potawatomi words.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquian - a family of North American indigenous languages

Etymology

From Ojibwe 'Bodéwadmi(g)', meaning 'keepers of the fire.', referring to the native tribe and their language.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːtəˈwɑːtəmi / (AmE), / ˌpɒtəˈwɒtəmi / (BrE)
a member of an Algonquian Native American people originally from Michigan and Wisconsin
member of an Algonquian Native American people

Examples

Sarah is a proud Potawatomi and often participates in tribal cultural events.
The Potawatomi were historically located around the Great Lakes region.
Many Potawatomi now live in Oklahoma, Kansas, and other states due to historic relocation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquian - a member of a family of Native American peoples

Etymology

From Potawatomi self-designation 'Bodéwadmi,' meaning 'keepers of the fire.'
