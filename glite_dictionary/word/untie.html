to untie

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈtaɪ / (AmE), / ʌnˈtaɪ / (BrE)
to make something become loose or no longer fastened, especially by undoing a knot, string, or fastening
make loose by undoing

Examples

She stopped to untie her shoelaces before entering the house.
It took him several minutes to untie the stubborn knot in the rope.
Can you help me untie this package?

Synset Examples

undo the shoelace
untie the knot
loosen the necktie

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tie - to fasten or attach with a knot or string
  • Synonym: to loosen - to make or become less tight or firm

Etymology

From un- (a prefix meaning 'not' or 'reverse of') + tie (from Old English *tīgan*, meaning 'to tie').

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈtaɪ / (AmE), / ʌnˈtaɪ / (BrE)
to undo or release something that has been fastened or tied, especially by unfastening a knot
undo or release by untying

Examples

She carefully untied the ribbon on the gift box.
After sailing, we untied the ropes that secured the boat.
He struggled for a moment but finally untied the knot in his shoelace.

Synset Examples

They untied the prisoner

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tie - to fasten or bind with a rope, string, or cord

Etymology

From un- (prefix meaning 'not') + tie (from Old English tīegan, related to fastening).
