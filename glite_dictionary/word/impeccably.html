impeccably

1. ADV

/ ɪmˈpɛkəbli / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛkəbli / (BrE)
in a flawless or faultless manner; perfectly.
in a flawless manner

Examples

She delivered her speech impeccably, with every detail perfectly in place.
The chef's kitchen was impeccably clean after a long night of cooking.
He dressed impeccably for the award ceremony, impressing everyone with his style.

Synset Examples

the film was impeccably authentic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perfectly - in a perfect or flawless manner
  • Synonym: flawlessly - without any imperfections or errors

Etymology

From Latin 'impeccabilis' (not liable to sin); from 'im-' (not) + 'peccare' (to sin).
