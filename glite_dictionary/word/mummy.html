mummy

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌmi / (AmE), / ˈmʌmi / (BrE)
(especially British English, informal) a child's word for mother.
informal British word for mother
informal british

Examples

When I fell over, I ran straight to my mummy for comfort.
Can I have some juice, mummy?
Little children often call their mother mummy when they're young.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mommy - U.S. informal word for mother
  • Synonym: mother - female parent
  • Antonym: daddy - informal word for father

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌmi / (AmE), / ˈmʌmi / (BrE)
a body that has been embalmed, dried, and wrapped in cloth for preservation, especially as practiced in ancient Egypt
a preserved, wrapped body from ancient Egypt

Examples

The mummy was carefully placed in a stone sarcophagus for the tomb.
Tourists were fascinated by the mummy displayed in the museum's ancient Egypt section.
Scientists analyzed the mummy to learn more about Egyptian burial rituals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embalmed body - a body preserved with chemicals

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'mumia', from Arabic 'mūmiyāʼ' (bitumen, embalmed body).
