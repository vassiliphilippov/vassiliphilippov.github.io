elitism

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈliːtɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˈliːtɪzəm / (BrE)
The belief that society should be led or governed by an elite group of people considered to be superior in ability or qualities.
belief in rule by an elite

Examples

The politician's views were criticized as elitism by her opponents.
Some argue that elitism can undermine democracy by ignoring the needs of ordinary citizens.
He defended elitism as necessary for maintaining high standards in government.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meritocracy - a system where advancement is based on ability and talent
  • Antonym: populism - belief in the power of ordinary people over elites

Etymology

From 'elite' (from French élite, selected group) + '-ism'.
