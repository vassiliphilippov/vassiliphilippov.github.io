whimsicality

1. NOUN

/ ˌwɪmzɪˈkæləti / (AmE), / ˌwɪmzɪˈkælɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being playfully unusual, imaginative, or unpredictable, often in a way that is appealing or amusing
quality of being fanciful or playful

Examples

Her whimsicality made every family gathering feel like a small adventure.
The artist's whimsicality is evident in the bright colors and dreamlike scenes of her paintings.
He was loved by his students for the whimsicality he brought to daily lessons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capriciousness - the quality of being prone to sudden, unpredictable changes
  • Antonym: seriousness - the quality of being serious and lacking playfulness

Etymology

From 'whimsical' + '-ity', patterned after Latin-derived nouns.

2. NOUN

/ ˌwɪmzɪˈkæləti / (AmE), / ˌwɪmzɪˈkælɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being unpredictable, fanciful, or acting on a whim rather than reason
the quality of being whimsical or unpredictable

Examples

The artist's whimsicality shone through in every painting, making each one a delightful surprise.
Her whimsicality often led her friends to expect the unexpected during their outings.
The story charmed readers with its sense of whimsicality and playful imagination.

Synset Examples

I despair at the flightiness and whimsicality of my memory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capriciousness - the quality of being subject to sudden changes or whims
  • Antonym: predictability - the quality of being able to be predicted or expected

Etymology

From 'whimsical' + '-ity'; 'whimsical' from 'whim' (fanciful notion) + '-ical'.
