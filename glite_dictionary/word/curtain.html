curtain

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɝːtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈkɜːtn̩ / (BrE)
something that acts as a barrier to communication or vision, especially in a figurative or metaphorical sense
a figurative barrier to communication or vision

Examples

The country maintained a curtain of secrecy around its military operations.
A dense curtain of fog made driving difficult that morning.
Negotiations were hampered by a curtain of silence from the other side.

Synset Examples

a curtain of secrecy
a curtain of trees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screen - something that shelters, protects, or hides; a similar figurative barrier
  • Synonym: veil - something that conceals, disguises, or obscures
  • Synonym: barrier - something that blocks movement, access, or communication

Etymology

From Old French cortine, from Latin cortina (curtain, covering).

2. VERB

/ ˈkɝː.tn̩ / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.tən / (BrE)
to provide with or enclose by a curtain or drapery
to provide with curtains

Examples

After moving in, they decided to curtain all the front windows for privacy.
The stagehands will curtain the theater before the performance begins.
She quickly curtained the open shelves to hide the clutter.

Synset Examples

curtain the bedrooms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drape - to cover or decorate with cloth in loose folds
Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɝː.tn̩ / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.tən / (BrE)
A piece of hanging cloth, often used to cover a window or divide a room, that can be opened or closed to block light or provide privacy.
hanging cloth for window or division

Examples

She pulled the curtain aside to let in some morning sunlight.
Heavy velvet curtains kept the room dark and quiet.
We hung a thick curtain between the two areas for extra privacy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drape - a long curtain or piece of fabric for covering or decorating a surface, especially in American English

Etymology

From Old French *courtine*, from Late Latin *cortina* (curtain, screen).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
