to cross-dress

1. VERB

/ krɑs drɛs / (AmE), / krɒs drɛs / (BrE)
to wear clothing that is typically associated with the opposite sex
to wear clothes of the opposite sex

Examples

Some actors cross-dress for comedic effect in plays and movies.
He decided to cross-dress for the costume party and wore his sister's dress.
People may cross-dress for personal expression or social reasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transvestite - person who cross-dresses

Etymology

From 'cross' (across) + 'dress', first used in the 20th century.
