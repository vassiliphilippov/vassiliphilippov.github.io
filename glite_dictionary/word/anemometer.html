anemometer

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈmɑːmətər / (AmE), / ˌænɪˈmɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument for measuring the speed and sometimes the direction of the wind
instrument measuring wind speed and direction

Examples

The weather station uses an anemometer to track wind speed throughout the day.
After the storm, technicians checked the anemometer to see how strong the winds had been.
The spinning cups of the anemometer caught the breeze on the rooftop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wind gauge - instrument for measuring wind speed

Etymology

From Greek anemos (wind) + -meter (measuring instrument).
