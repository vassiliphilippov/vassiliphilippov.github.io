Susiana

1. PROPN

/ suːˈsaɪənə / (AmE), / suːˈsaɪənə / (BrE)
an ancient region in southwestern Asia, to the east of the Tigris River, known in antiquity for its warlike people; located in what is now southwestern Iran.
ancient region in southwestern Asia (now Iran)

Examples

The ancient civilization of Susiana played a significant role in the development of early urban culture.
Archaeologists have discovered impressive ruins in the region once known as Susiana.
Historians believe Susiana was famed for both its rich resources and its formidable warriors.

Etymology

From Latin Susiana, from Greek Sousiana, after the ancient city of Susa.
