platyrrhinian

1. ADJ

/ ˌplætiˈrɪniən / (AmE), / ˌplætɪˈrɪnɪən / (BrE)
Relating to New World monkeys with widely spaced nostrils or to people with broad noses.
relating to broad-nosed primates or people

Examples

The platyrrhinian monkeys are native to South and Central America.
Researchers found that the ancient skull displayed distinctive platyrrhinian features.
Heritage studies often classify certain populations as platyrrhinian based on nasal structure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: catarrhine - relating to Old World monkeys and apes with close-set downward-facing nostrils

Etymology

From Greek 'platys' (broad, flat) + 'rhis, rhinos' (nose).

2. NOUN

/ ˌplætɪˈrɪniən / (AmE), / ˌplætɪˈrɪniən / (BrE)
A monkey of a group native to Central and South America, characterized by widely separated nostrils and usually a prehensile tail; a New World monkey.
New World monkey with wide nostrils

Examples

Spider monkeys are a well-known type of platyrrhinian found in South America.
The main difference between a platyrrhinian and a catarrhine monkey lies in the shape and position of their nostrils.
Researchers observed the feeding habits of various platyrrhinians in the rainforest canopy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: catarrhine - Old World monkeys with narrow, downward-facing nostrils
  • Synonym: New World monkey - A monkey native to Central and South America; synonym for platyrrhinian

Etymology

From Greek 'platys' (broad, flat) + 'rhis, rhinos' (nose), referring to the broad-nosed characteristic.
