Caulophyllum

1. NOUN

/ kɔl.əˈfɪl.əm / (AmE), / ˌkɔː.ləˈfɪl.əm / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbs, commonly known as blue cohosh, native to North America and valued for its medicinal roots.
genus of herbs known as blue cohosh

Examples

The roots of Caulophyllum have been used in herbal medicine for centuries.
Caulophyllum flowers bloom in early spring in the forests of North America.
Herbalists sometimes recommend Caulophyllum as a traditional remedy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue cohosh - common name for Caulophyllum species

Etymology

From Greek 'kaulos' (stem) + 'phyllon' (leaf), referring to the plant's stem-leaved appearance.

2. NOUN

/ kɔːloʊˈfɪləm / (AmE), / kɔːlɒˈfɪləm / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbs in the family Berberidaceae, commonly known as blue cohosh, native to eastern North America and eastern Asia.
genus of flowering herbs (blue cohosh)

Examples

The plant known as Caulophyllum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
Botanists have identified several species within the Caulophyllum genus, including C. thalictroides.
You can find Caulophyllum growing in the woodlands of eastern North America.

Etymology

From New Latin Caulophyllum, from Greek 'kaulos' (stem) and 'phyllon' (leaf).
