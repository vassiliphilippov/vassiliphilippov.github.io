weevil

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːvəl / (AmE), / ˈwiːvəl / (BrE)
A small beetle with an elongated snout, belonging to several families, that typically feeds on plants and plant products.
a small plant-eating beetle

Examples

A weevil infestation destroyed the farmer's entire grain supply.
Scientists studied the damage caused by the cotton boll weevil to crops.
I found a small weevil crawling inside a bag of flour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beetle - an insect of the order Coleoptera, with a hard shell-like back

Etymology

From Old English *wifel* (beetle, weevil).
