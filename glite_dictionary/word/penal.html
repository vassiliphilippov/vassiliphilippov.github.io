penal

1. ADJ

/ ˈpiː.nəl / (AmE), / ˈpiː.nəl / (BrE)
relating to punishment given by law or used for punishing people who break the law
relating to legal punishment

Examples

He spent several years in a penal colony after his conviction.
Certain laws carry penal consequences for violations.
The judge ordered him to a penal institution for young offenders.

Synset Examples

penal servitude
the juvenile was sentenced to six months in a penal institution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punitive - inflicting or intended as punishment

Etymology

From Latin 'poenalis', from 'poena' (penalty, punishment).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpiːnəl / (AmE), / ˈpiːnəl / (BrE)
(of an act or offense) liable to punishment by law; relating to legal penalties.
subject to legal punishment

Examples

Committing tax fraud is considered a penal offense in many countries.
The court discussed the penal consequences of violating the new regulation.
They are advocating for reforms to the current penal code.

Synset Examples

a penal offense

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criminal - involving or related to crime or punishment by law

Etymology

From Latin poenalis, from poena (penalty, punishment).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈpiːnəl / (AmE), / ˈpiːnəl / (BrE)
relating to or prescribed for legal punishment or penalties, especially under the law
relating to legal punishment
legal

Examples

The government introduced stricter penal codes to address financial crimes.
Prison sentences are a common penal measure for serious offenses.
He focused his research on penal systems and their effectiveness in deterring crime.

Synset Examples

penal reform
penal code

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punitive - inflicting or intended as punishment
  • Antonym: rewarding - providing a reward rather than a punishment

Etymology

From Latin poenalis (pertaining to punishment), from poena (penalty, punishment).

Source: WordNet
