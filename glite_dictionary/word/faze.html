to faze

1. VERB

/ feɪz / (AmE), / feɪz / (BrE)
to disturb, upset, or disconcert someone, especially by making them lose confidence or composure
to disturb or unsettle

Examples

The sudden noise did not faze the experienced pilot.
Even criticism from her rivals didn't faze her.
He wasn't fazed by the tough questions during the interview.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unnerve - to make someone lose courage or confidence
  • Synonym: dismay - to cause someone to feel concern and distress
  • Antonym: to reassure - to restore confidence or calmness to someone

Etymology

Probably a variant of the dialectal 'feeze', from Old English 'fēsian' (to drive away).
