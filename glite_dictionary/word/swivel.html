swivel

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɪvəl / (AmE), / ˈswɪvəl / (BrE)
a device consisting of two parts joined so that one or both can rotate freely, typically used to connect parts that need to turn independently, such as in a chain or on machinery
device that allows rotation at a joint

Examples

The lamp is mounted on a swivel so it can be directed where needed.
He attached the chain to a swivel to prevent it from twisting.
The chair's swivel allows the seat to rotate 360 degrees.

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'swivel' (a pivot or turning pin), related to 'swive' (to turn).

2. VERB

/ ˈswɪvəl / (AmE), / ˈswɪvəl / (BrE)
to turn or rotate on or as if on a pivot
turn or rotate on a pivot

Examples

She swiveled around in her chair to face the window.
The security camera swivels to cover the entire entrance.
He swiveled his head quickly to see who had called his name.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rotate - to turn around a central point
  • Synonym: spin - to turn around rapidly on an axis

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'swivel', related to 'swive' (to move) and the noun suffix '-el'.
