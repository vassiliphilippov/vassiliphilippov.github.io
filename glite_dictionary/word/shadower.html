shadower

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃædoʊər / (AmE), / ˈʃædəʊə / (BrE)
a person, especially a spy, employed to follow someone secretly and report on their movements
person who secretly follows someone

Examples

The detective assigned a shadower to keep an eye on the suspect day and night.
A skilled shadower must blend in with crowds and avoid drawing attention.
The company suspected industrial espionage and hired a shadower to track their ex-employee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shadow - to follow and observe someone in secret
