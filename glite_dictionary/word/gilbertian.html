Gilbertian

1. ADJ

/ gɪlˈbɜrtiən / (AmE), / gɪlˈbɜːtiən / (BrE)
Having the characteristics of the works of W. S. Gilbert, especially in being absurdly comic, satirical, or improbable.
absurdly comic or satirical (like Gilbert and Sullivan)

Examples

The novel unfolds in a Gilbertian manner, full of improbable twists and witty dialogue.
Critics described the film’s plot as delightfully Gilbertian, with an array of eccentric characters and wild coincidences.
Her Gilbertian humor made even the most bizarre situations seem perfectly natural.

Synset Examples

a Gilbertian world people with foundlings and changelings

Etymology

From the name of W. S. Gilbert, English dramatist and librettist (1836–1911).

2. ADJ

/ gɪlˈbɜrtiən / (AmE), / gɪlˈbɜːtiən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of the satirical, witty, and sometimes absurd style of William S. Gilbert, especially as seen in the Gilbert and Sullivan operas.
in the style of William S. Gilbert

Examples

The play's Gilbertian humor delighted both critics and audiences.
Her Gilbertian approach to satire made the operetta both clever and farcical.
Some critics describe his writing style as distinctly Gilbertian, full of witty rhymes and topsy-turvy logic.

Synset Examples

Gilbertian libretti

Etymology

From the name of William S. Gilbert (1836–1911), English dramatist and librettist.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03080337-a
Synset POS: a
