Certhia americana

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɜrθi.ə ˌæməˈrɪkənə / (AmE), / ˌsɜːθɪə ˌæməˈrɪkənə / (BrE)
A North American bird species, commonly known as the brown creeper, with a slender down-curved bill, that moves up tree trunks searching for insects.
North American brown creeper species

Examples

The Certhia americana is often spotted spiraling up tree trunks in search of insects.
During our hike, we observed a Certhia americana blending in perfectly with the bark.
Naturalists often rely on the distinctive call of Certhia americana to identify it in dense forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brown creeper - Common name for Certhia americana, a North American bird

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus name Certhia from Greek *kerthios* (a small tree-creeping bird), and americana, meaning 'of America'.
