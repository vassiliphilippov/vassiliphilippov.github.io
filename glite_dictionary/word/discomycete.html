discomycete

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.kəˈmaɪ.siːt / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.kəˈmʌɪ.siːt / (BrE)
A fungus belonging to the subclass Discomycetes, characterized by disc-shaped fruiting bodies.
fungus of the Discomycetes group
medical

Examples

Many discomycetes produce noticeable cup-shaped fruiting bodies in woodlands.
The scientist discovered a rare discomycete while studying decaying leaves.
Edible species among the discomycetes include some popular types of mushrooms.

Etymology

From New Latin Discomycetae, from Greek diskos (disc) + mykēs (fungus).
