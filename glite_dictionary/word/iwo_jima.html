Iwo Jima

1. PROPN

/ ˌiːwoʊ ˈdʒiːmə / (AmE), / ˌiːwəʊ ˈdʒiːmə / (BrE)
an island in the western Pacific Ocean, the largest of the Volcano Islands of Japan, known for the WWII battle fought there.
island in the western Pacific, site of WWII battle

Examples

The Battle of Iwo Jima was a pivotal moment in World War II.
Many films and books have depicted the events on Iwo Jima.
Today, Iwo Jima remains uninhabited except for military personnel.

Etymology

Japanese, literally 'Sulfur Island'.

2. PROPN

/ ˌiːwoʊ ˈdʒiːmə / (AmE), / ˌiːwəʊ ˈdʒiːmə / (BrE)
the site of a major World War II battle in 1945, where American forces captured the island of Iwo Jima from Japanese defenders after intense fighting
World War II battle site and island

Examples

The Battle of Iwo Jima was a turning point in the Pacific campaign of World War II.
Photographs of the flag raising on Iwo Jima became iconic symbols of American determination.
Many veterans still recall their experiences fighting on Iwo Jima during 1945.

Etymology

From Japanese 硫黄島 (Iō-tō), meaning 'Sulphur Island'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
