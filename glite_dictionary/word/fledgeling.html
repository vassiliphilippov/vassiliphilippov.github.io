fledgeling

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɛdʒlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflɛdʒlɪŋ / (BrE)
A young bird that has just acquired the feathers necessary for flight and has recently left the nest.
young bird able to fly

Examples

The robin fledgeling perched on the branch, hesitating before its first flight.
After a week, the fledgeling left the safety of its nest to explore the world.
A mother bird was seen feeding her fledgeling in the early morning.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: nestling - a young bird too young to leave the nest

Etymology

From 'fledge' (to acquire feathers necessary for flight) + '-ling' (diminutive suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈflɛdʒlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflɛdʒlɪŋ / (BrE)
a person, organization, or system that is new, inexperienced, or just beginning in a particular activity or field
new or inexperienced participant

Examples

The company hired a fledgeling designer who quickly learned the ropes.
As a fledgeling in the industry, she eagerly absorbed advice from her mentors.
Our band was once only a fledgeling, but now we play to sold-out crowds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: novice - a person new to and inexperienced in a job or situation
  • Antonym: veteran - a person with much experience in a particular field

Etymology

From Middle English 'flegeling', from 'fledge' (having grown feathers needed for flight) + diminutive suffix '-ling'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10375148-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈflɛdʒ.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflɛdʒ.lɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a young bird) having just developed wing feathers large enough for flight.
young bird with developed flight feathers

Examples

The fledgeling sparrows hopped awkwardly along the garden wall.
A fledgeling owl peered curiously from the nest's edge.
We watched as the fledgeling robins took their first flights.

Synset Examples

a fledgling robin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fledgling - a young bird that has just acquired its flight feathers (noun)

Etymology

From 'fledge' (to acquire feathers) + '-ling' (diminutive suffix).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
