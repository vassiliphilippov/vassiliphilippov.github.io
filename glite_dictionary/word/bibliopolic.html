bibliopolic

1. ADJ

/ ˌbɪbliəˈpɑlɪk / (AmE), / ˌbɪbliəˈpɒlɪk / (BrE)
of or relating to booksellers, especially dealers in rare or antiquarian books
relating to booksellers or book dealing

Examples

The bibliopolic trade has flourished in the city for centuries.
He was known for his deep involvement in bibliopolic circles throughout Europe.
Their shop offered a remarkably bibliopolic selection of rare manuscripts and first editions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bibliopole - a bookseller, especially in rare or antique books

Etymology

From 'bibliopole' (bookseller) plus the suffix '-ic' (forming adjectives).
