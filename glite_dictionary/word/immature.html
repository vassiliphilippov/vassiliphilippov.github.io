immature

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪməˈtʃʊr / (AmE), / ˌɪməˈtjʊə / (BrE)
not fully developed or mature; not ripe
not fully developed; not ripe

Examples

The immature apples tasted sour and were not ready for picking.
Because the wood was immature, it burned poorly and produced too much smoke.
The scientists studied the immature frogs before they transformed into adults.

Synset Examples

unripe fruit
fried green tomatoes
green wood

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mature - fully developed or ripe

Etymology

From Latin immaturus, from in- (not) + maturus (ripe, mature).

WordNet ID: oewn-01497045-a

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪməˈtʃʊr / (AmE), / ˌɪməˈtʃʊə / (BrE)
behaving in a way that shows a lack of emotional or mental maturity; not fully developed in attitude or behavior
showing lack of maturity

Examples

His immature behavior embarrassed everyone at the meeting.
Many immature comments were made during the heated discussion.
She realizes now that her actions were immature and regrets them.

Synset Examples

immature behavior

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mature - showing the mental, emotional, or physical qualities of an adult
  • Synonym: childish - typical of a child; immature

Etymology

From Latin immaturus, from in- (not) + maturus (ripe, mature).

Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ ˌɪməˈtʃʊr / (AmE), / ˌɪməˈtjʊə / (BrE)
not fully developed physically, emotionally, or mentally; not yet mature.
not fully developed or mature

Examples

Her behavior at the meeting was considered immature by her colleagues.
Green bananas are still immature and should not be eaten yet.
It's important to let children make mistakes; they're still immature and learning.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mature - fully developed or grown

Etymology

From Latin 'immaturus' meaning 'untimely, unripe', from 'in-' (not) + 'maturus' (mature, ripe).

(of a bird) not yet having developed feathers; not fully fledged.

Examples
