Panthera uncia

1. NOUN

/ pænˈθɪrə ˈʌnʃə / (AmE), / pænˈθɪərə ˈʌnsiə / (BrE)
the scientific name for the snow leopard, a large wild cat native to the mountain ranges of Central and South Asia, known for its long, thick, whitish fur
the snow leopard's scientific name

Examples

The endangered Panthera uncia inhabits the high mountains of Central Asia.
Research on Panthera uncia has helped conservationists understand its elusive behavior.
Photographers traveled to track the beautiful Panthera uncia in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snow leopard - common name for Panthera uncia
  • Degree Lower: leopard - a large wild cat, Panthera pardus

Etymology

From Latin; 'Panthera' is the genus for large cats, 'uncia' derives from Latin for 'inch' or 'twelfth part', possibly referencing the animal's size.
