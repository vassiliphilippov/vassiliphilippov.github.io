used

1. ADJ

/ juːzd / (AmE), / juːzd / (BrE)
having been owned or utilized by someone else before; secondhand.
previously owned or utilized

Examples

We decided to buy a used sofa instead of spending money on a new one.
She found several used textbooks online at a fraction of the price.
Many used cars now come with certified warranties.

Synset Examples

bought a secondhand (or used) car

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secondhand - previously owned by someone else; not new
  • Antonym: new - not previously used or owned
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01644709-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ juːzd / (AmE), / juːzd / (BrE)
(of something) having been put to a purpose or employed in a particular activity or process.
having been employed or utilized

Examples

The most used textbook in the class quickly became worn at the edges.
This is a heavily used trail, so it is well-marked and maintained.
After years of service, the car looked distinctly used compared to the newer models.

Synset Examples

the principle of surprise is the most used and misused of all the principles of war

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unused - not having been used or employed
  • Synonym: utilized - put to practical use
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02504601-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ just juːzd / (AmE), / just juːzd / (BrE)
(of a person) regarded or treated as a means to someone else's end, especially without appreciation or respect; taken advantage of.
treated exploitatively; taken advantage of

Examples

She confided in her colleague, only to end up feeling used and betrayed.
After lending money to his friends repeatedly, Mark began to realize he was being used.
It's important in any relationship not to make the other person feel used.

Synset Examples

after going out of his way to help his friend get the job he felt not appreciated but used

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exploited - treated unfairly for another's benefit
  • Antonym: valued - appreciated or considered important
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02505376-s
Synset POS: s
