Marsilea

1. NOUN

/ mɑːrˈsɪliə / (AmE), / mɑːˈsɪliə / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic ferns with clover-like leaves, commonly known as water clover.
a genus of aquatic ferns

Examples

Marsilea can often be found growing in shallow ponds and marshes worldwide.
Some aquarists use Marsilea as a decorative plant in freshwater aquariums.
The leaves of Marsilea resemble those of a four-leaf clover.

Etymology

Named after Italian naturalist Luigi Ferdinando Marsili (1656–1730).
