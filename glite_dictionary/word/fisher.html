fisher

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪʃər / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃə / (BrE)
a person whose occupation is to catch fish, especially for a living
person who catches fish for a living

Examples

As a skilled fisher, he knows the best spots along the river.
Local fishers depend on the lake for their livelihoods.
The government introduced new regulations to protect fishers and marine life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fisherman - a person who catches fish as an occupation, especially a man

Etymology

From Old English 'fiscere' (fisherman), from 'fiscian' (to fish).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪʃər / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃə / (BrE)
a large dark brown North American mammal of the weasel family, with an arboreal and carnivorous lifestyle
large North American carnivorous mammal

Examples

The dense forests of Canada are home to the elusive fisher.
A fisher will prey on porcupines and other small mammals in its territory.
Wildlife researchers tracked the movement of a fisher using a radio collar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marten - a small carnivorous mammal of the weasel family (especially in relation to similar species)

Etymology

From Middle English 'fisher', of uncertain origin, unrelated to 'fish'; possibly from French 'fichet' (a polecat).
