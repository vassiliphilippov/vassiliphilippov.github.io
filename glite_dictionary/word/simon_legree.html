Simon Legree

1. PROPN

/ ˈsaɪmən ləˈɡri / (AmE), / ˈsaɪmən ləˈɡriː / (BrE)
A cruel and brutal slave owner in Harriet Beecher Stowe's novel 'Uncle Tom's Cabin', often used allusively to describe a harsh or tyrannical person.
cruel slave owner; harsh taskmaster (literary)

Examples

Many employees nicknamed their boss Simon Legree for his unreasonable demands and stern demeanor.
In literary criticism, the character of Simon Legree represents the embodiment of cruelty and oppression.
Her father, though strict, was no Simon Legree and always acted out of genuine concern.

Etymology

Name of a character in Harriet Beecher Stowe's 1852 novel 'Uncle Tom's Cabin'.
