Hypericum prolificum

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈpɛrɪkəm proʊˈlɪfɪkəm / (AmE), / hɪˈpɛrɪkəm prəʊˈlɪfɪkəm / (BrE)
A species of stiff shrub in the St. John's wort family, with oblong leaves and clusters of yellow flowers, native to North America.
a shrub with yellow flowers, St. John's wort family

Examples

The native Hypericum prolificum grows well in wetland gardens across eastern North America.
Botanists studied the blooming cycle of Hypericum prolificum to better understand its pollination.
Landscape designers often choose Hypericum prolificum for its dense yellow flower clusters and attractive foliage.

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Hypericum' from Greek 'hyperikon' (St. John's wort), 'prolificum' from Latin 'prolificus' (prolific).
