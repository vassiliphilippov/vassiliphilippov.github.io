archaeological

1. ADJ

/ ˌɑr.ki.əˈlɑ.dʒɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌɑː.ki.əˈlɒ.dʒɪ.kəl / (BrE)
relating to the study of past human life and culture by examining material remains
relating to archaeology

Examples

The team made an archaeological survey of the ancient site before construction began.
Recent archaeological findings have shed new light on the city's history.
She is pursuing an archaeological career and hopes to work on excavations in Egypt.

Synset Examples

an archaeological dig
a dramatic archaeological discovery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archaeology - the study of human history through excavation and analysis of artifacts

Etymology

From archaeology + -ical, ultimately from Greek arkhaiologia (study of ancient things).
