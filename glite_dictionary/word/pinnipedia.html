Pinnipedia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪnɪˈpidiə / (AmE), / ˌpɪnɪˈpiːdɪə / (BrE)
A group of marine mammals including seals, sea lions, and walruses, characterized by fin-like limbs.
group of marine mammals (seals, sea lions, walruses)

Examples

The Pinnipedia include animals that are well adapted to marine life, such as seals and walruses.
Marine biologists study Pinnipedia to understand how these mammals evolved from land ancestors.
All members of the Pinnipedia have streamlined bodies suited for swimming and diving.

Etymology

From modern Latin, from Latin 'pinna' (fin) + 'pes, pedis' (foot).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɪnəˈpɪdiə / (AmE), / ˌpɪnɪˈpiːdɪə / (BrE)
a suborder of aquatic, fin-footed mammals including seals, sea lions, and walruses
group of fin-footed aquatic mammals

Examples

The fossil record of Pinnipedia helps scientists trace the evolution of modern seals.
All species in Pinnipedia share adaptations for swimming in cold marine environments.
Research on Pinnipedia populations has revealed the effects of climate change on marine mammals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seal - aquatic, fin-footed mammal of the family Phocidae
  • Synonym: sea lion - large eared seal of the family Otariidae
  • Synonym: walrus - large marine mammal with tusks, Odobenidae family

Etymology

From Latin 'pinna' (fin, feather) + 'pes, pedis' (foot).
