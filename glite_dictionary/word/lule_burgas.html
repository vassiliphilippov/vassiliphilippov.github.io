Lule Burgas

1. PROPN

/ ˌluːleɪ bʊrˈɡɑːs / (AmE), / ˌluːleɪ bʊəˈɡɑːs / (BrE)
a major battle in the Balkan Wars of 1912, where Bulgarian forces defeated the Ottoman army.
battle in the Balkan Wars (1912)

Examples

The Battle of Lule Burgas was a turning point in the Balkan Wars.
Many historians study Lule Burgas to understand the decline of Ottoman influence in Europe.
The memorial at Lule Burgas attracts visitors interested in military history.

Etymology

From the name of the town Lule Burgas in modern-day Bulgaria, where the battle took place.
