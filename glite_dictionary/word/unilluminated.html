unilluminated

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪˈluːməˌneɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪˈljuːməˌneɪtɪd / (BrE)
not lit up; lacking illumination or light
not illuminated; without light

Examples

The unilluminated side of the building was shrouded in shadows.
Astronomers observed the unilluminated hemisphere of the moon during the eclipse.
Her path home led through several unilluminated alleyways, making her feel uneasy.

Synset Examples

came up the lightless stairs
the unilluminated side of Mars
through dark unlighted (or unlit) streets

Related Terms

  • Antonym: illuminated - lit up; provided with light
  • Synonym: dark - with little or no light; not illuminated

Etymology

Prefix un- (not) + illuminate (from Latin illuminare, to light up) + -ed (past participle suffix).
