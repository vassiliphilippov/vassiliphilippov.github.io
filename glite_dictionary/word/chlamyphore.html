chlamyphore

1. NOUN

/ kləˈmɪfəˌfɔr / (AmE), / kləˈmɪfəˌfɔː / (BrE)
A very small Argentine armadillo with pale, silky hair and distinctive pink plates on its head and neck.
small Argentine armadillo species
medical

Examples

The pink fairy armadillo is a type of chlamyphore found in central Argentina.
Scientists studied a chlamyphore to better understand its adaptation to arid environments.
A chlamyphore's shell is soft and covered with fine, pale fur.

Etymology

From Greek 'chlamys' (cloak) + 'phoros' (bearing), referring to the animal's protective plates.
