Monardella

1. NOUN

/ moʊnɑrˈdɛlə / (AmE), / məʊnɑːˈdɛlə / (BrE)
a genus of fragrant herbs in the mint family, found chiefly in western North America
genus of fragrant herbs

Examples

The aromatic leaves of Monardella are often used in herbal teas.
Several species of Monardella are popular in native plant gardens for their attractive flowers.
Botanists discovered a new variety of Monardella growing in the foothills.

Etymology

Named after Spanish botanist Nicolás Monardes (1493–1588).

2. NOUN

/ moʊ.nɑːrˈdɛl.ə / (AmE), / məʊ.nɑːˈdɛl.ə / (BrE)
A genus of fragrant herbs in the mint family (Lamiaceae) native to the western United States.
genus of fragrant herbs in mint family

Examples

Several species of Monardella are cultivated for their aromatic leaves and attractive flowers.
Monardella plants thrive in dry, rocky soils typical of their native western U.S. habitats.
Botanists are currently studying the pollination habits of different Monardella species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mint - aromatic herb in the Lamiaceae family

Etymology

Named after the Spanish botanist Nicolás Monardes, with Latin botanical suffix -ella.
