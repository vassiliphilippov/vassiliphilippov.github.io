gherkin

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɝː.kɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡɜː.kɪn / (BrE)
a small variety of cucumber, especially one that is pickled whole and eaten as a condiment
a small pickled cucumber

Examples

She added a gherkin to her sandwich for a tangy flavor.
A jar of gherkins sat on the shelf next to the other pickles.
Some people like to snack on a gherkin straight from the jar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pickle - a food, especially a vegetable, preserved in vinegar or brine

Etymology

From early modern Dutch *gurken*, plural of *gurk* (cucumber), influenced by German *Gurke*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɜr.kɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡɜː.kɪn / (BrE)
a small variety of cucumber, typically with a prickly skin, often used for pickling
small prickly cucumber variety

Examples

I like to add a gherkin to my sandwiches for an extra crunch.
She grew small gherkins in her garden to make homemade pickles.
The recipe calls for chopped gherkin to enhance the flavor of the potato salad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cucumber - a long, green-skinned fruit with watery flesh, eaten as a vegetable

Etymology

From early modern Dutch 'gurken', ultimately from Slavic '*ogurek*' (cucumber).
