Tetraneuris acaulis

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛtrəˈnjʊrɪs əˈkɔlɪs / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˈnjʊərɪs əˈkɔːlɪs / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to west central North America, with tufted basal leaves and short, leafless stalks each bearing a single yellow flower head.
a North American perennial plant with yellow flowers

Examples

On the prairie, Tetraneuris acaulis blooms in early spring, brightening the landscape with its yellow flower heads.
Botanists studied the adaptation of Tetraneuris acaulis to dry, rocky soils in the region.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Tetraneuris acaulis for its drought resistance and attractive blossoms.

Etymology

From Greek 'tetra-' (four), 'neuron' (nerve), and Latin 'acaulis' (stemless), referring to the plant's characteristics.
