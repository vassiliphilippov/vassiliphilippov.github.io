pallbearer

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔlˌbɛrər / (AmE), / ˈpɔːlˌbeərə / (BrE)
a person who helps carry the coffin at a funeral
person who carries the coffin at a funeral

Examples

The pallbearer walked solemnly beside the hearse, holding the coffin steady.
Her brother was chosen as a pallbearer at their grandfather's funeral.
Each pallbearer wore a dark suit in respect for the occasion.

Etymology

From 'pall', meaning a cloth covering a coffin, and 'bearer', from Old English 'beran' (to bear or carry).
