to sectionalise

1. VERB

/ ˈsɛkʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɛkʃənəlaɪz / (BrE)
to divide something into sections, especially along geographic or administrative lines
divide into sections
british

Examples

The government decided to sectionalise the territory for administrative purposes.
Some historians argue that colonial borders served to sectionalise communities that had once lived together.
The company chose to sectionalise its market strategy by region to better address local needs.

Synset Examples

sectionalize a country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sectionalize - divide into sections, especially geographically (American spelling)
  • Antonym: to unify - make a whole; bring together into a single unit

Etymology

From 'section' + '-al' + '-ise', following formation patterns with the suffix '-ise'.
