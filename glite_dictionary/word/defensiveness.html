defensiveness

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfɛnsɪvnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛnsɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being excessively sensitive to criticism or perceived attack, often resulting in self-protective reactions
excessive sensitivity to criticism

Examples

Her defensiveness made it difficult to have an honest conversation about her mistakes.
When receiving feedback, excessive defensiveness can prevent personal growth.
The team's defensiveness increased after several harsh reviews from the manager.

Synset Examples

his defensiveness was manifested in hurt silence
the fear of being sued for malpractice has magnified physicians' defensiveness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensitivity - the quality of being easily affected or hurt by criticism or comments
  • Antonym: openness - the quality of being receptive to feedback or criticism

Etymology

From 'defensive' + '-ness', with 'defensive' from Latin 'defendere' (to defend).
