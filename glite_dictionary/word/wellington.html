Wellington

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɛlɪŋtən / (AmE), / ˈwɛlɪŋtən / (BrE)
the capital city of New Zealand, located at the southern tip of the North Island
capital city of New Zealand

Examples

Many government offices are based in Wellington, making it the political heart of New Zealand.
She moved to Wellington to study at Victoria University.
The strong winds in Wellington have earned it the nickname 'Windy Welly'.

Etymology

Named after Arthur Wellesley, 1st Duke of Wellington (1769–1852).

3. PROPN

/ ˈwɛlɪŋtən / (AmE), / ˈwɛlɪŋtən / (BrE)
(1769–1852) The 1st Duke of Wellington, a British general and statesman known for defeating Napoleon at Waterloo and later serving as Prime Minister.
British general and Prime Minister (1769–1852)

Examples

Wellington led the Allied forces to victory at the Battle of Waterloo in 1815.
British history often refers to Wellington both as a military hero and a Prime Minister.
The leadership style of Wellington is still studied at military academies worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Napoleon - French military leader defeated at Waterloo by Wellington

Etymology

Title from Wellington, a town in Somerset, England, given to Arthur Wellesley upon his elevation to the peerage.
