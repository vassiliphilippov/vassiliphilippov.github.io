frenzied

1. ADJ

/ ˈfrɛn.ziːd / (AmE), / ˈfrɛn.zid / (BrE)
in a state of uncontrolled excitement or wild behavior; affected by frenzy or mania
wildly excited or uncontrolled

Examples

The fans gave a frenzied cheer as the team entered the stadium.
During the sale, shoppers raced through the store in a frenzied search for bargains.
She worked at a frenzied pace to meet the tight deadline.

Synset Examples

a frenzied attack
a frenzied mob
the prosecutor's frenzied denunciation of the accused
outbursts of drunken violence and manic activity and creativity

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions

Etymology

From Middle English 'frenesied', based on Old French 'frenesie', from Latin 'phrenesis' (delirium).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfrɛnziːd / (AmE), / ˈfrɛnziːd / (BrE)
Wildly excited or uncontrolled, usually as a result of strong emotion such as fear or anger.
wildly excited; uncontrolled

Examples

After the announcement, a frenzied crowd rushed through the doors in a panic.
Her frenzied attempts to finish the project only made things worse.
The dog barked in a frenzied manner when it saw the stranger.

Synset Examples

frantic with anger and frustration
frenetic screams followed the accident
a frenzied look in his eye

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frantic - wild or distraught with fear, anxiety, or other emotion
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions

Etymology

From Middle French *frénésie*, from Latin *phrenesis*, from Greek *phrenēsis* (delirium).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
