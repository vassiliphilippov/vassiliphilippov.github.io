polypody

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑl.əˌpoʊ.di / (AmE), / ˈpɒl.ɪˌpəʊ.di / (BrE)
Any of numerous ferns of the genus Polypodium, typically having creeping rhizomes and deeply divided leaves.
a type of fern (genus Polypodium)

Examples

The shady woodland was dotted with polypody growing among the mossy rocks.
Gardeners often choose polypody for shaded areas due to its hardiness.
She studied the structure of the polypody fronds for her botany class.

Etymology

From Latin polypodium, from Greek polypodion (literally 'many-footed'), referring to the root structure.
