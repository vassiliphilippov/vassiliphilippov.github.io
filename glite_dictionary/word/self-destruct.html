to self-destruct

1. VERB

/ ˌsɛlf.dɪˈstrʌkt / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf.dɪˈstrʌkt / (BrE)
to destroy oneself or itself deliberately, often by an automatic process
destroy oneself or itself

Examples

The spy's message will self-destruct five seconds after being read.
If the system detects a threat, it will automatically self-destruct.
His inability to control his anger caused him to self-destruct professionally.

Synset Examples

The machine will self-destruct if you tamper with it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to destroy - to put an end to the existence of something

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' + 'destruct', based on Latin 'destruere' (to unbuild, demolish).

2. VERB

/ ˌsɛlf.dɪˈstrʌkt / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf.dɪˈstrʌkt / (BrE)
(of a device or system) to destroy itself automatically, often programmed to do so under certain conditions.
destroy itself automatically

Examples

If the computer detects a serious fault, it will self-destruct to prevent data theft.
The note read: 'This message will self-destruct after five minutes.'
He warned that the robot might self-destruct if tampered with.

Synset Examples

He joked that his phone would self-destruct if dropped.
The device is programmed to self-destruct if tampered with.
The spy's message will self-destruct in five seconds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to explode - burst or shatter violently and noisily
  • Antonym: to deactivate - make (something) inactive or disabled

Etymology

Self- (oneself) + destruct (destroy), modeled after 'self-destructive.'
