to propose

1. VERB

/ prəˈpoʊz / (AmE), / prəˈpəʊz / (BrE)
to intend or plan to do something; to have as one's aim
intend or plan to do

Examples

He proposed to start his own business after graduation.
We propose to visit Europe next summer if our finances allow.
The committee proposes to examine the issue in greater detail.

Synset Examples

I aim to arrive at noon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intend - to have a plan or purpose in mind
  • Synonym: plan - to decide on and arrange in advance

Etymology

From Latin proponere, from pro- (forward) + ponere (to put).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00710809-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ prəˈpoʊz / (AmE), / prəˈpəʊz / (BrE)
to suggest a plan, idea, or course of action for others to consider
suggest something for consideration

Examples

At the meeting, she proposed that the policy be revised to address new challenges.
Several experts proposed alternative solutions to the issue of climate change.
He is proposing a new method to make the process more efficient.

Synset Examples

He proposed a new plan for dealing with terrorism
She proposed a new theory of relativity
The candidate projects himself as a moderate and a reformer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suggest - to put forward an idea or plan for consideration
  • Synonym: to recommend - to suggest something as being good or suitable

Etymology

From Latin proponere, from pro- 'forward' + ponere 'put'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00708009-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ prəˈpoʊz / (AmE), / prəˈpəʊz / (BrE)
to suggest someone for appointment to an office, job, or honor; to nominate
nominate for office or position

Examples

The committee decided to propose her as a candidate for the board of directors.
They plan to propose Dr. Wilson for a national science award next year.
At the meeting, someone proposed him for the position of club president.

Synset Examples

The President nominated her as head of the Civil Rights Commission

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to nominate - to formally suggest someone for a job or position

Etymology

From Latin proponere (set forth), through Old French proposer.

Source: WordNet

5. VERB

/ prəˈpoʊz / (AmE), / prəˈpəʊz / (BrE)
to suggest a plan, idea, or course of action for others to consider
to suggest an idea or plan

Examples

The committee will propose new guidelines at the next meeting.
She proposed that we take a break and resume work in the morning.
If you have a better solution, please propose it now.

Synset Examples

the senator proposed to abolish the sales tax

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suggest - to mention an idea, possible plan or action for other people to consider
  • Synonym: to recommend - to advise something as a course of action
  • Antonym: to oppose - to disagree with or resist a plan or suggestion

Etymology

From Latin proponere (to put forward).
