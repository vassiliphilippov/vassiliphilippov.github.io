rapprochement

1. NOUN

/ ˌræproʊʃˈmɑ̃ / (AmE), / ˌraprɒʃˈmɒ̃ / (BrE)
the development or restoration of friendly relations, especially between countries or groups
restoring friendly relations

Examples

The leaders agreed to meet in hopes of achieving a rapprochement between their two countries.
After years of tension, there was finally a rapprochement between management and the union.
Historians regard the treaty as the start of a new era of rapprochement in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reconciliation - the restoration of friendly relations after a conflict
  • Antonym: estrangement - the state of being alienated or separated

Etymology

From French 'rapprochement', from 'rapprocher' (to bring together again), from 're-' (again) + 'approcher' (to approach).
