legitimation

1. NOUN

/ lɪˌdʒɪtəˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / lɪˌdʒɪtəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making something lawful or giving it legal status
making lawful; granting legal status

Examples

The legitimation of the new government required constitutional amendments.
Marriage resulted in the legitimation of their children under the law.
He campaigned for the legitimation of certain previously prohibited activities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legalization - the process of making something legal
  • Antonym: delegitimation - the process of removing legitimacy or legal status

Etymology

From Medieval Latin legitimationem, from Latin legitimare ('to make lawful').

2. NOUN

/ lɪˌdʒɪtəˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / lɪˌdʒɪtɪˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making someone or something legitimate, especially in law; the action of granting lawful status or rights, often used in the context of legitimizing a person's status at birth
the act of making legitimate, often legally
legal

Examples

He obtained letters of legitimation from the king and secured his inheritance rights.
The legitimation of the child was completed after the parents married.
In some legal systems, legitimation confers all the rights of a legitimate child.

Synset Examples

he has filial rights because he obtained letters of legitimation from the king
his parents' subsequent marriage resulted in his legitimation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legitimacy - the state of being legitimate or lawful

Etymology

From Latin 'legitimatio', from 'legitimare' (to make lawful, legitimate).
