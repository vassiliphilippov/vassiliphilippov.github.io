Borges

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɔr heɪz / (AmE), / ˈbɔː hɛz / (BrE)
(1899–1986) Argentinian writer known for his influential short stories, essays, and poems, especially for exploring philosophical and fantastical themes.
Argentinian writer (1899–1986)

Examples

Many literary critics regard Borges as a pioneer of modernist fiction.
The stories of Borges often blur the line between reality and imagination.
I've read several works by Borges in translation.
