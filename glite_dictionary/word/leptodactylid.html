leptodactylid

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛptəˈdæktɪlɪd / (AmE), / ˌlɛptəˈdæktɪlɪd / (BrE)
A frog of the family Leptodactylidae, which includes various terrestrial, aquatic, or arboreal toothed frogs found mainly in the Americas.
frog of the family Leptodactylidae

Examples

A leptodactylid can be distinguished by its slender fingers and terrestrial habits.
Scientists have discovered a new species of leptodactylid in the Amazon rain forest.
The leptodactylid exhibits a remarkable variety of reproductive behaviors among frog families.

Etymology

From New Latin Leptodactylidae, from Greek leptos (slender) + daktylos (finger).
