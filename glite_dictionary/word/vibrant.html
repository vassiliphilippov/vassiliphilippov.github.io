vibrant

1. ADJ

/ ˈvaɪ.brənt / (AmE), / ˈvaɪ.brənt / (BrE)
(of colors) bright and striking in appearance; full of energy and vividness.
bright and striking in color

Examples

The artist chose vibrant colors to make the painting stand out.
She wore a vibrant red dress to the party, catching everyone's attention.
The garden was filled with vibrant flowers in every shade imaginable.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dull - lacking brightness or vividness in color
  • Synonym: brilliant - very bright and radiant in color

Etymology

From Latin 'vibrāre' meaning 'to move rapidly to and fro'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈvaɪ.brənt / (AmE), / ˈvaɪ.brənt / (BrE)
(of a sound) strong, resonant, and full of energy.
strong and resonant (of sound)

Examples

The singer's vibrant voice filled the concert hall and moved the audience.
He recalled the vibrant laughter echoing throughout his childhood home.
A vibrant note from the cello set the tone for the entire orchestra.

Synset Examples

the men's vibrant voices

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resonant - (of sound) deep, clear, and continuing to sound or ring
  • Degree Lower: mellow - (of sound) pleasantly smooth or soft; free from harshness
  • Degree Higher: booming - (of sound) loud, deep, and resonant

Etymology

From Latin 'vibrāre' (to move to and fro, shake).

3. ADJ

/ ˈvaɪ.brənt / (AmE), / ˈvaɪ.brənt / (BrE)
full of energy, enthusiasm, and life; lively and animated.
full of energy and life

Examples

The city's vibrant nightlife attracts tourists from all over the world.
Her vibrant personality makes everyone feel welcome at the party.
The artist used vibrant colors to convey energy and excitement in his painting.

Synset Examples

a vibrant group that challenged the system
a charming and vivacious hostess
a vivacious folk dance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lively - full of energy and enthusiasm
  • Antonym: dull - lacking energy or excitement
  • Synonym: dynamic - characterized by constant change, activity, or progress

Etymology

From Latin vibrant-, stem of vibrare (to vibrate, move quickly to and fro).
