amrinone

1. NOUN

/ ˈæm.rɪˌnoʊn / (AmE), / ˈæm.rɪ.nəʊn / (BrE)
A drug used intravenously to treat heart failure by increasing the strength of the heart's contractions.
heart failure medication (increases heart contraction)
medical

Examples

The cardiologist administered amrinone to improve the patient's heart function during the acute episode.
Recent studies show that amrinone can increase cardiac output in patients with severe heart failure.
Nurses must monitor for side effects when amrinone is given intravenously.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milrinone - a related drug used in heart failure with similar mechanism
