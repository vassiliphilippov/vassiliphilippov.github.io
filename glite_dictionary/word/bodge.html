to bodge

1. VERB

/ bɑdʒ / (AmE), / bɒdʒ / (BrE)
to do something badly or clumsily; to make a mess of a task
to do something badly or clumsily
british

Examples

He completely bodged the installation of the new shelf, so it fell down within an hour.
She tried to repair her bike but only bodged it and made things worse.
If you bodge this assignment, you'll have to redo it from scratch.

Synset Examples

I botched the dinner and we had to eat out
the pianist screwed up the difficult passage in the second movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botch - to carry out a task badly or carelessly
  • Antonym: fix - to repair or mend something in a satisfactory way

Etymology

Origin obscure; possibly British dialect, influenced by 'botch.'
