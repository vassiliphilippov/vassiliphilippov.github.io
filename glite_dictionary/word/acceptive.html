acceptive

1. ADJ

/ əkˈsɛptɪv / (AmE), / əkˈsɛptɪv / (BrE)
willing or ready to accept; receptive to new ideas or things.
willing to accept; receptive

Examples

She has an acceptive attitude toward criticism, always trying to learn from feedback.
The company is known for its acceptive environment for innovative ideas.
His acceptive nature made it easy for him to adapt to different cultures.

Synset Examples

acceptive of every new idea
an acceptant type of mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: receptive - willing to consider new suggestions or ideas
  • Antonym: resistant - not accepting or opposed to something

Etymology

From Latin 'acceptivus' (fit for receiving), from 'acceptus', past participle of 'accipere' (to receive).

2. ADJ

/ əkˈsɛptɪv / (AmE), / əkˈsɛptɪv / (BrE)
willing or inclined to accept things, rather than to reject them
inclined to accept

Examples

Her acceptive nature made her easy to work with on collaborative projects.
The committee was not acceptive of the new proposal, preferring to stick with tradition.
He found her attitude refreshingly acceptive compared to the skepticism of others.

Synset Examples

she was seldom acceptive of my suggestions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: receptive - willing to receive new ideas or suggestions

Etymology

From Latin accipere (to accept) + -ive (forming adjectives).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00076560-a
Synset POS: a
