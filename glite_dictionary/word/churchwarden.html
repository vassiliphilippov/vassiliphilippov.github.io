churchwarden

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɝːtʃˌwɔrdn̩ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɜːtʃˌwɔːdn̩ / (BrE)
an officer in some Anglican or Episcopal churches who assists the parish priest with non-religious matters such as administration and maintenance
church officer aiding priest with secular matters

Examples

The churchwarden ensured that the church grounds were well maintained throughout the year.
She was elected churchwarden after years of volunteering in the parish.
During the meeting, the churchwarden presented the annual financial report to the congregation.

Etymology

From 'church' + 'warden', warden from Old Northern French *wardein* (guardian).
