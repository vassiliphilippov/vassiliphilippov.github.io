aioli

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈoʊli / (AmE), / aɪˈəʊli / (BrE)
A sauce made of garlic, olive oil, and usually egg yolk, similar to mayonnaise and often used as a condiment or dip.
garlic-flavored mayonnaise sauce

Examples

The grilled vegetables were served with a side of aioli for dipping.
Many restaurants offer aioli as a flavorful substitute for mayonnaise on sandwiches.
She whisked fresh garlic into the aioli to enhance its taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mayonnaise - a thick, creamy sauce made of egg yolks, oil, and vinegar or lemon juice

Etymology

From Provençal *aiòli*, from *ai* (garlic) and *òli* (oil), reflecting its primary ingredients.

2. NOUN

/ aɪˈoʊli / (AmE), / aɪˈəʊli / (BrE)
A sauce made of garlic and olive oil, often emulsified with egg yolk and sometimes regarded as garlic mayonnaise.
garlic and olive oil sauce

Examples

He served roasted potatoes with a side of aioli for dipping.
The grilled fish was topped with a generous dollop of aioli.
For extra flavor, try adding some lemon juice to your homemade aioli.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mayonnaise - a thick, creamy dressing made from egg yolks, oil, and vinegar or lemon juice

Etymology

From Provençal French *aiòli*, from Latin *allium* (garlic) + *oleum* (oil).
