kitambilla

1. NOUN

/ kɪˌtɑːmˈbɪlə / (AmE), / kɪˌtæmˈbɪlə / (BrE)
A small shrubby spiny tree native to Sri Lanka and India, cultivated for its maroon-purple fruit with sweet, gooseberry-like pulp.
tropical fruit tree of Sri Lanka and India

Examples

The kitambilla is prized for its unique, sweet-tart fruit in local Sri Lankan cuisine.
Farmers cultivate kitambillas in parts of southern India for their edible berries.
I tasted a kitambilla for the first time at a market in Colombo.

2. NOUN

/ kɪˈtæmbɪlə / (AmE), / kɪˈtæmbɪlə / (BrE)
A maroon-purple fruit native to India, similar to a gooseberry, with tart-sweet purple pulp often used in preserves.
Indian maroon-purple gooseberry-like fruit

Examples

The market had a fresh batch of kitambilla for making traditional Indian jams.
She tasted the tart-sweet pulp of the kitambilla and decided to use it in her preserves.
Dried kitambillas are a popular ingredient in some regional Indian desserts.
