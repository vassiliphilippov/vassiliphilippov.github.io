hyphenation

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪfəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhaɪfəˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of dividing or connecting syllables or words with hyphens, especially at the end of a line of text
division or connection with hyphens

Examples

Modern word processors automatically handle hyphenation at the ends of lines.
Incorrect hyphenation can make text difficult to read and understand.
She adjusted the hyphenation settings to improve the document's layout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyphen - the symbol '-' used to join words or separate syllables

Etymology

From 'hyphen' (Greek huphen, meaning 'together') + '-ation', denoting action or process.

2. NOUN

/ haɪfəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / haɪfəˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of dividing a word at the end of a line by inserting a hyphen, or the way in which such division is applied
division of a word by hyphens

Examples

Modern word processors often handle hyphenation automatically to improve text layout.
Incorrect hyphenation can make a document difficult to read and understand.
The editor adjusted the hyphenation settings for better alignment of the columns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyphen - the sign (-) used to join words or split them at the end of a line

Etymology

From 'hyphen' + '-ation', based on Greek 'hyphēn' (together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00391543-n
Synset POS: n
