Ursus arctos horribilis

1. NOUN

/ ˈɝː.səs ˈɑːrk.toʊs hɔːˈrɪ.bɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / ˈɜː.səs ˈɑːk.tɒs hɒˈrɪ.bɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the grizzly bear, a powerful brownish-yellow bear native to the uplands of western North America
grizzly bear scientific name

Examples

The Ursus arctos horribilis is more commonly known as the grizzly bear in North America.
Biologists studied the habitat of Ursus arctos horribilis to better understand its behavior.
Encounters with Ursus arctos horribilis are rare but potentially dangerous for hikers in Montana.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grizzly bear - common English name for Ursus arctos horribilis

Etymology

From Latin: 'ursus' (bear), 'arctos' (Greek for bear), 'horribilis' (frightful or terrible).
