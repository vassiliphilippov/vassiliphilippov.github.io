alerting

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɝtɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈlɜːtɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of warning someone so that they become alert to danger or potential problems
process of making someone alert to danger

Examples

The company has an alerting system in place for fire emergencies.
Early alerting can save lives during natural disasters.
Proper alerting is crucial in preventing workplace accidents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warning - a statement or signal that something bad may happen

Etymology

From the verb 'alert' + -ing, meaning the action or process of alerting.

2. NOUN

/ əˈlɝːtɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈlɜːtɪŋ / (BrE)
the state of being ready to respond quickly to a stimulus or situation
state of readiness to respond

Examples

During overnight shifts, alerting is crucial for air traffic controllers to maintain safety.
Caffeine consumption has been shown to improve alerting in many sleep-deprived individuals.
Researchers measured participants' alerting using reaction time to sudden signals.

Synset Examples

alerting was indicated by the desynchronization of the EEG

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vigilance - the action or state of keeping careful watch for possible danger or difficulties
  • Antonym: inattention - lack of attention; not being alert or aware
