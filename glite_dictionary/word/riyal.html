riyal

1. NOUN

/ riˈjɑl / (AmE), / riˈɑːl / (BrE)
the basic unit of currency in Qatar.
Qatari currency unit

Examples

The price of the souvenir was fifty riyals at the market in Doha.
She exchanged her dollars for riyal at the airport upon arriving in Qatar.
Using a riyal coin, he paid for a bottle of water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dirham - the monetary subunit of the Qatari riyal (1 riyal = 100 dirhams)
  • Synonym: riyal (Saudi Arabia) - the currency of Saudi Arabia, also called riyal

Etymology

From Arabic riyāl (real), ultimately from Spanish real (royal).

2. NOUN

/ riˈjɑl / (AmE), / riˈjɑːl / (BrE)
the basic unit of currency in Saudi Arabia, equal to 100 halalas
Saudi Arabian currency unit

Examples

The price of the souvenir was 50 riyals at the market.
She exchanged her dollars for riyals before traveling to Saudi Arabia.
The company's capital is valued at several million riyals.

Etymology

From Arabic رِيَال (riyāl), borrowed from Spanish real (royal).
