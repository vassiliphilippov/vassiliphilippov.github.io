wakefulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪkfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈweɪkfəlnəs / (BrE)
the state of being awake, especially when alert and conscious of one's surroundings
state of being awake and alert

Examples

Periods of wakefulness during the night can make you feel tired the next day.
Caffeine can increase wakefulness and help you stay focused.
Meditation is sometimes practiced to enhance wakefulness and awareness.

Synset Examples

consciousness during wakefulness in a sane person is pretty well ordered and familiar

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sleep - a natural periodic state of rest for the mind and body
  • Synonym: alertness - the quality of being alert and attentive

Etymology

From 'wakeful' + '-ness'; 'wakeful' from Old English wæcnan (to wake).

2. NOUN

/ ˈweɪk.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈweɪk.f(ə)l.nəs / (BrE)
the state of being awake, especially when unable or unwilling to sleep
state of being awake; inability to sleep

Examples

After several nights of wakefulness, she finally fell asleep just before dawn.
Excessive caffeine can increase wakefulness and make it harder to fall asleep.
Periods of wakefulness are common as we age, often interrupting sleep throughout the night.

Synset Examples

accept your wakefulness and sleep in its own contrary way is more likely to come

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sleep - a natural periodic state of rest for the mind and body

Etymology

From 'wakeful' + '-ness', based on 'wake' (to become awake).

3. NOUN

/ ˈweɪk.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈweɪk.fəl.nəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being alert and paying close and continuous attention
alert attentiveness; vigilance

Examples

Her wakefulness during the night shift allowed her to spot any unusual activity.
Learned wakefulness is essential for anyone working as a security guard.
In the meeting, his wakefulness was noticed by his supervisor.

Synset Examples

wakefulness, watchfulness, and bellicosity make a good hunter
vigilance is especially susceptible to fatigue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vigilance - the action or state of keeping careful watch for possible danger or difficulties
  • Synonym: watchfulness - the action or state of being alert and ready to notice things
  • Antonym: inattention - lack of attention; disregard

Etymology

From 'wakeful' + '-ness', patterned after words denoting states or qualities.

Source: WordNet
