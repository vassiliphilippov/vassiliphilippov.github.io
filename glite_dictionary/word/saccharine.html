saccharine

1. ADJ

/ ˈsækərɪn / (AmE), / ˈsak.ər.iːn / (BrE)
Excessively sweet, sentimental, or ingratiating, to the point of being unpleasant or insincere.
overly sweet or sentimental

Examples

Her saccharine smile made it clear she was just trying to win favor.
The movie's ending was criticized as saccharine and unrealistic by most reviewers.
He spoke with such saccharine sincerity that nobody believed a word he said.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloying - excessively sweet or sentimental, to the point of disgust
  • Synonym: sentimental - showing or causing excessive emotion, especially in an insincere way
  • Antonym: bitter - having a sharp, pungent taste or emotion, the opposite of sweet

Etymology

From Latin saccharinum, from saccharum (sugar), from Greek sakkharon (sugar).
