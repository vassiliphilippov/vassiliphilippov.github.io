The Dagda

1. PROPN

/ ðə ˈdɑːɡdə / (AmE), / ðə ˈdaɡdə / (BrE)
A chief god in Irish mythology, leader of the Tuatha Dé Danann, associated with strength, abundance, and wisdom.
chief god in Irish mythology

Examples

In Irish mythology, The Dagda was known for his magical harp and immense strength.
Legends say that The Dagda could control life and death with his staff.
Artists often depict The Dagda as a giant with a cauldron of plenty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Odin - chief Norse god, associated with wisdom and war
  • Synonym: Jupiter - chief Roman god, equivalent to Zeus in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Old Irish *Dagda*, meaning 'the good god'.
