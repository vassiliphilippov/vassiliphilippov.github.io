Ulmus serotina

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌlməs səˈroʊtɪnə / (AmE), / ˈʌlməs səˈrəʊtɪnə / (BrE)
A species of elm tree native to the southeastern United States, notable for flowering in autumn.
a species of autumn-flowering elm tree

Examples

The Ulmus serotina is valued in some gardens for its late blooming season.
Botanists studied the Ulmus serotina in various habitats across the southeastern United States.
Unlike most elms, Ulmus serotina flowers in autumn rather than in spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elm - a type of deciduous tree of the genus Ulmus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Ulmus' meaning elm, and 'serotina' meaning late (referencing late flowering).
