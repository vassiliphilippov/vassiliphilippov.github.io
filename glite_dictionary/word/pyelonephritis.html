pyelonephritis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪəloʊnɪˈfraɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌpaɪələʊnɪˈfraɪtɪs / (BrE)
A bacterial infection and inflammation of the kidney and its renal pelvis, usually caused by the spread of a urinary tract infection.
kidney and renal pelvis infection
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with pyelonephritis after she developed a high fever and back pain.
If left untreated, pyelonephritis can lead to serious complications affecting kidney function.
Patients with recurrent pyelonephritis often require further investigation for underlying urinary tract abnormalities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nephritis - inflammation of the kidneys

Etymology

From Greek pyelos (trough, pelvis) + nephros (kidney) + -itis (inflammation).
