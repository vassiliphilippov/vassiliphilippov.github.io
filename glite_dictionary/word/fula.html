Fula

1. NOUN

/ ˈfuːlə / (AmE), / ˈfuːlə / (BrE)
A member of a pastoral and nomadic people of western Africa, traditionally cattle herders and predominantly Muslim.
member of a pastoral people of western Africa

Examples

Many Fula migrated seasonally in search of grazing land for their cattle.
The Fula have a rich tradition of oral poetry and music.
A group of Fula women gathered to celebrate a local festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fulani - another term for a member of the Fula people

Etymology

From Fula self-designation Pullo (singular), Fulɓe (plural).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfuːlə / (AmE), / ˈfuːlə / (BrE)
a group of related languages spoken by the Fulani people across West Africa, widely used as a lingua franca from Senegal to Chad
a West African language spoken by the Fulani people

Examples

Many people in Senegal speak Fula as their first or second language.
The Fula language is known for its complex system of noun classes.
He studied Fula to communicate more easily while traveling in West Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fulani - an ethnic group in West Africa closely associated with the Fula language

Etymology

From Fulani *Fulɓe*, the name of the people.
