turnabout

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːnəˌbaʊt / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnəˌbaʊt / (BrE)
an act or decision that reverses a previous position or ruling
a reversal of a previous decision

Examples

The judge's turnabout surprised both the defense and prosecution.
There was a sudden turnabout in the company's policy on remote work.
After much debate, the committee announced a complete turnabout on the issue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reversal - an act of changing to the opposite direction, position, or course of action

Etymology

Formed from 'turn' + 'about'; first attested in 1800s America.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːnəˌbaʊt / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnəˌbaʊt / (BrE)
an act or instance of turning in the opposite direction or a complete reversal of position or opinion
a change to the opposite direction or position

Examples

The sudden turnabout in traffic caused some confusion among the drivers.
After the turnabout, the boat started heading back toward the shore.
Her opinion underwent a complete turnabout following the new evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reversal - act of reversing direction or position
  • Synonym: about-face - a complete change of direction or attitude

Etymology

From 'turn' + 'about', indicating reversal or change of direction.
