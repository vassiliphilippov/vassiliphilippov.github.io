sargassum

1. NOUN

/ sɑrˈɡæsəm / (AmE), / sɑːˈɡasəm / (BrE)
A type of brown seaweed with rounded air bladders, often forming dense floating mats in warm Atlantic waters, especially in the Sargasso Sea.
brown seaweed found in floating mats

Examples

Large patches of sargassum drifted past the boat during our trip across the Atlantic.
The beaches were covered in sargassum after the recent storm, making swimming difficult.
Marine life often thrives in the floating mats of sargassum in the open ocean.

Etymology

From Portuguese sargaço (a kind of seaweed), possibly from Latin sargassum.
