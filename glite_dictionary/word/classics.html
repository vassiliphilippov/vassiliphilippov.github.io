classics

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæsɪks / (AmE), / ˈklæsɪks / (BrE)
the study of the languages, literature, and culture of ancient Greece and Rome
study of ancient Greek and Roman culture

Examples

She majored in classics at university and became a professor of ancient history.
Many universities around the world offer degrees in classics.
His passion for classics led him to translate Homer’s Iliad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classical studies - the study of classical antiquity

Etymology

From Latin classicus (of the highest class), originally referring to first-class Roman citizens or authors.
