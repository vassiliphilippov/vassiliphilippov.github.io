banana

1. NOUN

/ bəˈnænə / (AmE), / bəˈnɑːnə / (BrE)
Any of several tropical and subtropical plants of the genus Musa, with large leaves and clusters of elongated edible fruit.
tropical plant yielding edible fruit

Examples

The banana plant grows quickly in warm, humid climates.
Large banana leaves are often used to wrap food for cooking in Southeast Asia.
The banana is one of the world's most important fruit crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plantain - a plant of the genus Musa with starchy fruit, often cooked before eating

Etymology

From Portuguese or Spanish 'banana,' likely of West African origin.

2. NOUN

/ bəˈnænə / (AmE), / bəˈnɑːnə / (BrE)
A long, curved fruit with yellow skin and soft, sweet flesh, growing in clusters on tropical plants.
long curved yellow fruit

Examples

She peeled a banana for her breakfast smoothie.
The banana ripened quickly in the warm kitchen.
Monkeys are often shown eating a banana in cartoons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plantain - a starchy banana variety, often cooked rather than eaten raw

Etymology

From Portuguese or Spanish 'banana', originally from Wolof (West African language).
