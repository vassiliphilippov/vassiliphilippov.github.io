alienee

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ.li.əˈni / (AmE), / ˌeɪ.li.əˈniː / (BrE)
a person to whom the title of property is legally transferred
person receiving transferred property title
legal

Examples

The alienee became the rightful owner of the estate after the deed was signed.
The contract outlines the rights and obligations of the alienee in the transaction.
Property taxes must be paid by the alienee after the transfer of ownership.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: alienor - the person who transfers the title of property

Etymology

From Anglo-French 'alienee', from Old French 'aliener' (to transfer ownership), from Latin 'alienare'.
