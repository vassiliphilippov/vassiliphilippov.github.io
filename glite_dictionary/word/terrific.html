terrific

1. ADJ

/ təˈrɪfɪk / (AmE), / təˈrɪfɪk / (BrE)
Extremely great in amount, intensity, or degree; very intense.
very great or intense

Examples

A terrific noise woke everyone in the middle of the night.
The storm caused terrific damage to the coastal town.
They fought a terrific battle that lasted for hours.

Synset Examples

a terrific noise
a terrific thunderstorm
fought a terrific battle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intense - very strong or extreme in force or degree

Etymology

From Latin 'terrificus' (causing terror), from 'terrere' (to frighten).

2. ADJ

/ təˈrɪfɪk / (AmE), / təˈrɪfɪk / (BrE)
Extremely good, excellent, or impressive; very great in amount, intensity, or degree.
extremely good or impressive

Examples

She did a terrific job organizing the event, and everyone was impressed.
The view from the top of the mountain was absolutely terrific.
We had a terrific time at the concert last night.

Synset Examples

a fantastic trip to the Orient
the film was fantastic!
a howling success
a marvelous collection of rare books
had a rattling conversation about politics
a tremendous achievement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fantastic - extraordinarily good or attractive
  • Antonym: awful - very bad or unpleasant
  • Synonym: tremendous - very great in amount, scale, or intensity

Etymology

From Latin 'terrificus' (causing terror), from 'terrere' (to frighten). The modern positive sense developed in the 19th century.
