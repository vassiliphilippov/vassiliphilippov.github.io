oxidation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːksɪˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
a chemical process in which a substance combines with oxygen or loses electrons, often resulting in the formation of a new compound
chemical process involving oxygen or electron loss

Examples

The oxidation of iron results in the formation of rust.
Food can spoil quickly due to the oxidation of fats.
During respiration, glucose undergoes oxidation to produce energy in cells.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reduction - a chemical reaction involving the gain of electrons or removal of oxygen

Etymology

From French 'oxydation', from 'oxyde' (oxide), from Greek 'oxys' (sharp, acid)
