eptatretus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛptəˈtriːtəs / (AmE), / ˌɛptəˈtriːtəs / (BrE)
A genus of hagfish, including both fossil and living species, found in deep-sea environments.
genus of hagfish (marine animals)
medical

Examples

Scientists discovered a well-preserved eptatretus fossil in the sediment layers of the ancient seabed.
The eptatretus is notable for its multiple gill slits, a characteristic feature of hagfish.
Modern relatives of eptatretus still inhabit deep ocean waters around the world.

Etymology

From Greek 'hepta' (seven) and 'treta' (holes), referring to the number of gill openings in some species.
