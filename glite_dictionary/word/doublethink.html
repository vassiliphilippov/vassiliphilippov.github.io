doublethink

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌbl̩ˌθɪŋk / (AmE), / ˈdʌbl̩ˌθɪŋk / (BrE)
The act of simultaneously accepting two mutually contradictory beliefs as correct, often used to describe conflicting thoughts encouraged by political or ideological systems.
simultaneous belief in contradictory ideas

Examples

The regime maintained power partly by encouraging doublethink among its citizens.
His ability to engage in doublethink allowed him to ignore obvious contradictions in the policy.
Critics accused the government of promoting doublethink to justify its conflicting statements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cognitive dissonance - psychological discomfort of holding conflicting beliefs

Etymology

Coined by George Orwell in his novel 'Nineteen Eighty-Four' (1949), from 'double' + 'think'.
