Kosteletzya virginica

1. NOUN

/ koʊstəˈlɛtsiə vərˈdʒɪnɪkə / (AmE), / kɒstəˈlɛtsiə vəˈdʒɪnɪkə / (BrE)
A perennial subshrub found in the southeastern United States to New York, commonly known as seashore mallow.
a perennial subshrub (seashore mallow) of southeastern US

Examples

Kosteletzya virginica is often found growing in salt marshes along the Atlantic coast.
Ecologists study Kosteletzya virginica for its role in coastal wetland restoration.
The pink flowers of Kosteletzya virginica attract a variety of pollinators during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seashore mallow - common name for Kosteletzya virginica

Etymology

Genus named after Czech botanist Vincenz Franz Kosteletzky; species name refers to Virginia, USA.
