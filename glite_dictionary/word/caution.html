caution

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔʃən / (AmE), / ˈkɔːʃən / (BrE)
the quality of being careful to avoid danger or mistakes; circumspect and prudent behavior
carefulness to avoid danger or mistakes

Examples

You should always show caution when walking alone at night.
Her caution saved the company from a risky investment.
Too much caution can sometimes prevent people from taking necessary risks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carefulness - the quality of being cautious or attentive to avoid harm or mistakes
  • Antonym: recklessness - the quality of being careless and taking unnecessary risks

Etymology

From Latin 'cautio' (care, caution), from 'cavere' (to beware).
