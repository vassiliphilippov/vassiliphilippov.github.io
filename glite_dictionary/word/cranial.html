cranial

1. ADJ

/ ˈkreɪniəl / (AmE), / ˈkreɪniəl / (BrE)
relating to the cranium, the part of the skull that encloses the brain
relating to the cranium or skull
medical

Examples

The patient was experiencing increased cranial pressure after the injury.
Doctors monitored cranial development in the infant to ensure normal growth.
The surgery required a detailed understanding of cranial anatomy.

Synset Examples

cranial pressure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cranial - situated in or affecting the cranium
  • Antonym: caudal - relating to the tail or posterior part of the body

Etymology

From Late Latin cranialis, from cranium (skull) + -al.
