opprobrium

1. NOUN

/ əˈproʊbriəm / (AmE), / əˈprəʊbrɪəm / (BrE)
severe public disgrace or harsh criticism arising from someone's shameful conduct
public disgrace or harsh criticism

Examples

After the scandal, the politician became the target of widespread opprobrium in the media.
His dishonest actions brought opprobrium upon the entire organization.
The company worked hard to recover from the opprobrium following the safety violations.

Synset Examples

a date which will live in infamy
the name was a by-word of scorn and opprobrium throughout the city

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgrace - loss of reputation or respect
  • Synonym: infamy - the state of being well known for a bad quality or deed
  • Antonym: honor - high respect; great esteem

Etymology

From Latin opprobrium (disgrace, reproach), from opprobrare (to reproach).

2. NOUN

/ əˈproʊbriəm / (AmE), / əˈprəʊbriəm / (BrE)
harsh criticism or public disgrace arising from someone's actions or conduct
public disgrace or condemnation

Examples

The scandal brought lasting opprobrium to the politician's career.
After the incident, the company was subject to public opprobrium and widespread calls for reform.
He sought to atone for the opprobrium caused by his irresponsible behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgrace - loss of reputation or respect as the result of a dishonorable action
  • Antonym: honor - high respect as shown for special merit

Etymology

From Latin 'opprobrium' (disgrace, infamy), from 'opprobrare' (to reproach).
