muddy

1. ADJ

/ ˈmʌdi / (AmE), / ˈmʌdi / (BrE)
covered with or full of mud; dirty and messy
covered with mud; dirty

Examples

The children came home with muddy shoes after playing in the rain.
After the storm, the path was so muddy that it was hard to walk on.
She washed the muddy dog before letting it inside.

Synset Examples

muddy boots
a mucky stable

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clean - free from dirt or impurities

2. VERB

/ ˈmʌdi / (AmE), / ˈmʌdi / (BrE)
To make (a liquid) cloudy or turbid, especially by stirring up sediment.
make cloudy or turbid

Examples

Heavy rains can muddy the river, making it unsafe for swimming.
Political debates sometimes muddy the issues instead of clarifying them.
Please don't muddy the water with unnecessary details.

Synset Examples

muddy the water

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to clarify - to make (a liquid) clear
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈmʌdi / (AmE), / ˈmʌdi / (BrE)
to make dirty by covering or filling with mud
make dirty with mud

Examples

After the rainstorm, the children muddied their shoes playing in the yard.
The heavy truck muddied the road as it drove through the construction site.
Be careful not to muddy the floor with your boots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dirty - to make something unclean
  • Antonym: clean - to make something clean; opposite of dirty

Etymology

From Middle English 'mudi', from 'mud' + '-y'.

5. ADJ

/ ˈmʌdi / (AmE), / ˈmʌdi / (BrE)
(of soil or ground) soft, wet, and often covered or mixed with mud; saturated with water to the point of being difficult to walk on.
soft and wet with mud

Examples

After the heavy rain, the field became so muddy that it was impossible to walk across.
The children came home with muddy shoes after playing near the stream.
Tractors often get stuck in the muddy farmland during the spring thaw.

7. ADJ

/ ˈmʌdi / (AmE), / ˈmʌdi / (BrE)
(of liquids) not clear; clouded by suspended sediment or particles.
not clear, clouded with sediment (liquids)

Examples

After the storm, the river water became muddy and unsafe to drink.
He poured out a cup of muddy coffee filled with grounds.
Visibility was poor because the pond was unusually muddy after the rain.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloudy - (of liquids) not clear or transparent
  • Antonym: clear - (of liquids) transparent, free from cloudiness or sediment
  • Synonym: murky - dark or cloudy, especially due to sediment
