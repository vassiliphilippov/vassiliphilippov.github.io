synaeresis

1. NOUN

/ sɪnˈɛrəsɪs / (AmE), / sɪnˈɪərəsɪs / (BrE)
the process by which two adjacent vowels within a word are pronounced together as a single syllable, often forming a diphthong
contraction of two vowels into a diphthong

Examples

The poet uses synaeresis to compress the syllables and fit the poetic meter.
In linguistics, synaeresis often leads to the creation of diphthongs in everyday speech.
Shakespeare’s verse contains several instances of synaeresis, especially in rapid dialogue.

Etymology

From Greek synaíresis (a taking or drawing together), from syn- (together) + hairein (to take).

2. NOUN

/ sɪˈnɛrəsɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈnaɪərəsɪs / (BrE)
the separation of liquid from a gel caused by contraction, as in cheese making
separation of liquid from a gel
medical

Examples

During the production of yogurt, synaeresis can cause whey to pool on the surface.
The cheese maker monitored the synaeresis process to achieve the desired texture.
Excessive synaeresis may indicate a problem with the stability of the gel.

Etymology

From Greek synairesis (a taking or drawing together).
