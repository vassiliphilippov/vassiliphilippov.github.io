Tridacna

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈdæknə / (AmE), / trɪˈdaknə / (BrE)
A genus of very large marine bivalve mollusks known as giant clams, found in coral reefs of the Indo-Pacific region.
genus of giant clams

Examples

Marine biologists discovered a rare Tridacna species on the coral reef.
The Tridacna can reach enormous sizes compared to other clams.
Divers are often fascinated by the colorful mantles of the Tridacna.

Etymology

From Greek 'tridákna' (a kind of shellfish).

2. NOUN

/ traɪˈdæknə / (AmE), / trɪˈdaknə / (BrE)
A genus of large saltwater clams, known as giant clams, belonging to the family Tridacnidae.
type genus of giant clams

Examples

The Tridacna genus includes some of the largest living bivalve mollusks.
Marine biologists often study Tridacna species to understand reef ecosystems.
The shells of Tridacna clams are prized for their impressive size and beauty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant clam - A very large clam found in tropical waters, especially species of Tridacna.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek *tridaknos* (a kind of shellfish).
