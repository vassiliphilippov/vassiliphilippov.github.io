Khirghiz

1. NOUN

/ kɪrˈɡiz / (AmE), / kɪəˈɡiːz / (BrE)
the Turkic language spoken by the Kyrgyz people, chiefly in Kyrgyzstan
Turkic language of the Kyrgyz

Examples

She is fluent in both Russian and Khirghiz.
The Khirghiz language belongs to the Turkic family of languages.
Many official documents in Kyrgyzstan are translated into Khirghiz.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kyrgyz - the language of the Kyrgyz people

Etymology

From Russian Киргиз (Kirgiz), from Kyrgyz Кыргыз (Kyrgyz).

2. NOUN

/ kɪrˈɡiːz / (AmE), / kɪəˈɡiːz / (BrE)
A member of a Turkic ethnic group traditionally inhabiting vast regions of Central Asia and Siberia, especially Kyrgyzstan.
member of a Central Asian ethnic group

Examples

The Khirghiz have a rich tradition of oral poetry passed down through generations.
Many Khirghiz people still practice nomadic herding in the mountainous regions.
During the festival, Khirghiz dancers performed in traditional costumes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kyrgyz - a member of the Kyrgyz ethnic group

Etymology

From Russian 'Kirghiz', from Turkic self-designation 'Kyrgyz'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09755744-n
Synset POS: n
