ravenous

1. ADJ

/ ˈrævənəs / (AmE), / ˈravənəs / (BrE)
extremely hungry; having a great desire for food
extremely hungry

Examples

After skipping breakfast, I was absolutely ravenous by lunchtime.
The ravenous wolves devoured their prey in minutes.
She looked at the food with a ravenous expression after her marathon run.

Synset Examples

they were tired and famished for food and sleep
a ravenous boy
the family was starved and ragged
fell into the esurient embrace of a predatory enemy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: famished - extremely hungry
  • Synonym: starving - suffering or dying from hunger
  • Antonym: satiated - fully satisfied, especially with food

Etymology

From Old French *ravineux* (greedy, voracious), from *raviner* (to ravage or devour), based on Latin *rapina* (plundering).

2. ADJ

/ ˈrævənəs / (AmE), / ˈrævənəs / (BrE)
Extremely hungry; having or showing a great desire for food.
extremely hungry

Examples

After skipping lunch, she was absolutely ravenous by dinner time.
The ravenous wolves tore into their prey without hesitation.
He greeted the banquet with a ravenous appetite, barely pausing to breathe between bites.

Synset Examples

edacious vultures
a rapacious appetite
ravenous as wolves
voracious sharks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voracious - having a very eager approach to an activity, especially eating
  • Degree Lower: hungry - feeling the need for food
  • Antonym: satiated - fully satisfied, especially with food

Etymology

From Old French *ravineux*, from Latin *rapina* (plundering), from *rapere* (to seize).
