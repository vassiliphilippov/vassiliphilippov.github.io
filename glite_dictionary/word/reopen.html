to reopen

1. VERB

/ ˌrioʊˈpɛn / (AmE), / ˌriːˈəʊpən / (BrE)
to open (something) again after it has been closed
to open again after being closed

Examples

The museum will reopen next week after extensive renovations.
After the investigation, the police decided to reopen the case.
The shop was forced to close due to flooding but plans to reopen soon.

Synset Examples

They reopened the theater

Related Terms

  • Antonym: close - to shut or stop access to something
  • Synonym: open - to make or become open or accessible

Etymology

From re- (again) + open, following the standard English prefixation pattern.
