chemotherapy

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɛmoʊˈθɛrəpi / (AmE), / ˌkɛməʊˈθɛrəpi / (BrE)
The treatment of disease, especially cancer, by the use of chemical substances, especially cytotoxic drugs.
treatment of disease using chemical substances
medical

Examples

Many cancer patients undergo chemotherapy to help shrink tumors before surgery.
Side effects of chemotherapy can include hair loss and fatigue.
Doctors explained that chemotherapy was the best option to control the disease's progression.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: radiotherapy - treatment of disease using radiation, typically to treat cancer

Etymology

From chemo- (relating to chemicals) + therapy (treatment).
