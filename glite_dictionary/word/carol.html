carol

1. NOUN

/ ˈkærəl / (AmE), / ˈkærəl / (BrE)
a joyful religious song, especially one celebrating the birth of Christ and typically sung at Christmas
Christmas religious song

Examples

The choir performed a beautiful carol during the Christmas service.
Every year, children go door-to-door singing their favorite carols for neighbors.
She composed an original carol to celebrate the holiday season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hymn - a religious song of praise to God
  • Synonym: anthem - a song of devotion or praise

Etymology

From Old French carole (a circle dance, round song), from Late Latin choraula (choral song, dance accompanied by flutes).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkerəl / (AmE), / ˈkærəl / (BrE)
a joyful song, especially a religious song sung at Christmas to celebrate the birth of Christ
joyful Christmas song

Examples

The children sang a beautiful carol at the Christmas service.
Every year, the choir performs traditional carols in the town square.
She wrote her own carol to celebrate the holiday season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hymn - a religious song of praise
  • Synonym: anthem - a song of praise or celebration

Etymology

From Old French carole (a kind of dance, round dance accompanied by singing), from Late Latin choraula (choral song).

3. VERB

/ ˈkærəl / (AmE), / ˈkærəl / (BrE)
to sing carols, especially Christmas songs, in a group or to people, usually in celebration
sing Christmas songs in a group

Examples

The children love to carol through the streets on Christmas Eve.
Every December, our choir carols at the nursing home to spread holiday cheer.
They caroled in front of each house, delighting the neighbors with their festive songs.

Synset Examples

They went caroling on Christmas Day

Etymology

From Old French *carole* (a dance), from Late Latin *choraula* (chorus leader).
