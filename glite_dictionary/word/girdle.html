girdle

1. VERB

/ ˈɡɝːdəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɜːdl / (BrE)
to cut a ring around the bark of a tree or plant, typically to kill it by stopping the flow of water and nutrients
cut a ring around a plant to kill it

Examples

If you girdle a tree, it will eventually die as it can no longer transport nutrients.
The forester decided to girdle several invasive species to control their spread.
Improperly girdling the plant can harm it without killing it outright.

Synset Examples

girdle the plant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ring-bark - to remove a ring of bark from a tree, thereby killing it
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01611026-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɝːdəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɜː.dəl / (BrE)
an encircling or ringlike structure, especially one found in anatomy or biology
anatomical ringlike structure
medical

Examples

The pelvic girdle supports the weight of the upper body.
Fish possess a pectoral girdle that helps connect their fins to the skeleton.
Nerves that pass through the shoulder girdle can affect arm movement.

Etymology

From Old English *gyrdel*; related to Latin *cingulum* (belt).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05586542-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈɡɝːdəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɜːdəl / (BrE)
to encircle with or as if with a girdle; to put a girdle on or around something
to encircle with or as if with a girdle

Examples

The ancient warriors would girdle their waists with leather belts before battle.
A silver ribbon girdled the base of the elegant hat.
The city was girdled by a protective wall for centuries.

Synset Examples

gird your loins

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to encircle - to form a circle around; to surround

Etymology

From Old English 'gyrdel', related to 'gird' and German 'Gürtel' (belt).

4. NOUN

/ ˈɡɝːdəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɜː.dl̩ / (BrE)
A close-fitting undergarment worn especially by women to shape or support the waist and hips.
woman's foundation garment for shaping the waist and hips

Examples

She wore a girdle under her dress to create a smooth silhouette.
The vintage shop still sells traditional girdles and corsets.
My grandmother used to say that a good girdle was essential for special occasions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corset - a tightly fitting undergarment worn to shape the figure

Etymology

From Old English 'gyrdel', from Proto-Germanic *gurdilaz, related to 'gird'.

5. NOUN

/ ˈɡɜrdl̩ / (AmE), / ˈɡɜːdl̩ / (BrE)
A band of material sewn around the waist of a skirt or trousers to provide strength or shape.
waist band supporting garments

Examples

The dress had a tightly sewn girdle to keep its shape.
A strong girdle around the trousers prevented them from slipping.
He noticed the girdle made the skirt fit snugly at the waist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belt - a band worn around the waist, often to support clothing

Etymology

From Old English gyrdel (belt, band), related to gird (to encircle).
