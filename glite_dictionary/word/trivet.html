trivet

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪvət / (AmE), / ˈtrɪvɪt / (BrE)
a small metal stand, typically with three legs, used for supporting a cooking vessel over a fire or to protect a surface from hot dishes
three-legged metal stand for hot pots or pans

Examples

The cast iron trivet kept the kettle steady above the open flame.
She placed the hot casserole dish on a trivet to protect the table.
In old kitchens, a trivet was essential for cooking over the hearth.

Etymology

From Latin 'tripēs' (three-footed), via Old French and Middle English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪvɪt / (AmE), / ˈtrɪvɪt / (BrE)
a small stand, typically with short feet, placed under a hot dish to protect a table surface
stand for hot dishes on a table

Examples

She placed the casserole on a trivet to protect the table from the heat.
We need a trivet before setting down that hot pan.
The ornate metal trivet matched the kitchen decor perfectly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hot pad - a thick pad for placing hot dishes on

Etymology

From Latin 'tripes, tripodis' meaning 'three-footed'.
