syrinx

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪrɪŋks / (AmE), / ˈsɪrɪŋks / (BrE)
the vocal organ of birds, situated at the base of the trachea, responsible for producing their sounds
the vocal organ of a bird

Examples

The complexity of a songbird's syrinx allows it to produce a wide variety of calls.
Scientists studied the syrinx to better understand how birds communicate.
Damage to a parrot's syrinx can result in a loss of vocal ability.

Etymology

From Greek sūrinx (pipe, tube).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪr.ɪŋks / (AmE), / ˈsɪr.ɪŋks / (BrE)
A primitive wind instrument consisting of several parallel pipes of varying length bound together, also known as panpipes.
primitive wind instrument of bound pipes

Examples

The shepherd played a beautiful melody on his syrinx at sunset.
Ancient Greek art often depicts Pan holding a syrinx.
The museum displayed a reconstructed syrinx alongside other traditional instruments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panpipes - a musical instrument made of several pipes bound together and played by blowing across the tops

Etymology

From Greek syrinx (pipe, panpipes, channel).
