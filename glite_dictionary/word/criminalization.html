criminalization

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪmɪnələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkrɪmɪnələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making an activity illegal by law or statute
making something illegal by law

Examples

The criminalization of certain drugs remains a controversial issue in many countries.
Many activists oppose the criminalization of homelessness, arguing it punishes vulnerable individuals.
Advocates called for the criminalization of animal cruelty to protect pets and wildlife.

Synset Examples

the criminalization of marijuana

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decriminalization - the process of ceasing to treat something as illegal

Etymology

From 'criminalize' (to make illegal) + '-ation'.
