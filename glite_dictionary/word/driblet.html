driblet

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɪblət / (AmE), / ˈdrɪblɪt / (BrE)
a very small quantity, especially of a liquid; a small amount or portion
a very small quantity or portion

Examples

Only a driblet of rain managed to fall from the dark clouds overhead.
She receives her inheritance in occasional driblets rather than in a lump sum.
There was just a driblet of milk left at the bottom of the bottle.

Synset Examples

he had a drop too much to drink
a drop of each sample was analyzed
there is not a drop of pity in that man
years afterward, they would pay the blood-money, driblet by driblet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drop - a small round or pear-shaped portion of liquid
  • Synonym: dribble - a small trickling flow of liquid

Etymology

Diminutive of 'dribble', possibly from Middle English 'dribben', meaning to fall in drops.
