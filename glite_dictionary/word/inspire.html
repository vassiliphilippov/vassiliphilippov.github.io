to inspire

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈspaɪɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈspaɪə / (BrE)
to fill someone with revolutionary or transformative ideas and enthusiasm for change
fill with revolutionary ideas

Examples

The leader's speech inspired the crowd with visions of a better future.
Her words inspired young activists to challenge the status quo.
History teachers often inspire students to think critically about social change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to motivate - to provide someone with a reason or incentive to act
  • Antonym: to dissuade - to persuade someone not to take a particular course of action

Etymology

From Latin inspirare (to breathe into), from in- (into) + spirare (breathe).
