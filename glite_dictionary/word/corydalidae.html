Corydalidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːrɪˈdælɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌkɒrɪˈdalɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of large aquatic insects known as dobsonflies, typically found near streams and rivers.
family of insects known as dobsonflies

Examples

Members of the Corydalidae family are commonly found near freshwater streams.
The larvae of Corydalidae are also known as hellgrammites and are used as fishing bait.
Researchers discovered several new species within the Corydalidae during the expedition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dobsonfly - a large aquatic insect in the family Corydalidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Corydalus and the standard family suffix -idae.
