sunstone

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈsʌnstəʊn / (BrE)
a translucent variety of feldspar or quartz, typically spangled with tiny reflections from inclusions of mica or other minerals, giving it a glittering appearance
a glittering mineral, often feldspar or quartz

Examples

The museum displayed a beautiful sunstone specimen with shimmering golden flakes.
She wore a pendant made from polished sunstone that sparkled in the light.
Geologists examined the sunstone and noted its unique, glittering inclusions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feldspar - a group of rock-forming minerals, commonly in sunstones

Etymology

Named for its sparkling, sun-like appearance; attested since the 18th century.
