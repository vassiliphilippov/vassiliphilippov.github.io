to revolutionize

1. VERB

/ ˌrɛvəˈluːʃəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˌrɛvəˈluːʃənaɪz / (BrE)
to completely change something or fill with new, revolutionary ideas; to effect a radical transformation
to effect a radical change

Examples

The Internet has revolutionized the way we communicate and access information.
Her new teaching method aims to revolutionize the classroom by encouraging independent thinking.
Social media platforms are said to have revolutionized political activism in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to transform - to make a thorough or dramatic change
  • Antonym: conserve - to keep unchanged; preserve

Etymology

From French révolutionner (to bring about a revolution), from révolution (revolution).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00606705-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌrɛvəˈluːʃəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˌrɛvəˈluːʃənaɪz / (BrE)
to overthrow (a government or system) by means of a revolution
overthrow by revolution

Examples

The rebels hoped to revolutionize the country's leadership through an armed uprising.
The movement aimed to revolutionize the existing regime by rallying popular support.
Throughout history, many have attempted to revolutionize oppressive governments without success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overthrow - to remove forcibly from power; to topple
  • Synonym: upheaval - a violent disturbance or change, especially in politics

Etymology

From French révolutionner, from révolution (revolution) + -ize.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02407837-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌrɛvəˈluːʃəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˌrɛvəˈluːʃənaɪz / (BrE)
to completely change something so that it is much better or very different
radically change or transform

Examples

The invention of the smartphone helped revolutionize how we access information every day.
New medical techniques continue to revolutionize the treatment of many diseases.
Online shopping has revolutionized the retail industry over the past decade.

Synset Examples

E-mail revolutionized communication in academe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to transform - make a thorough or dramatic change in form, appearance, or character
  • Synonym: to modernize - make something more modern or up to date
  • Antonym: preserve - maintain something in its original or existing state

Etymology

From French 'révolutionner' (to bring about a revolution), from 'révolution' (revolution).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00125441-v
Synset POS: v
