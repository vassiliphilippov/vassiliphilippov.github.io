Uigur

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːɡər / (AmE), / ˈwiːɡə / (BrE)
the script, derived from Aramaic, used for writing the Uighur language
script for the Uighur language

Examples

The ancient manuscripts were written in Uigur script, making them invaluable to historians.
Linguists study Uigur to learn more about the evolution of Central Asian writing systems.
The museum displayed several religious texts composed in Uigur.

Etymology

From Old Turkic and ultimately from Aramaic, reflecting the script's historical origins.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwiːˌɡʊr / (AmE), / ˈwiːˌɡʊə / (BrE)
a member of a Turkic ethnic group native to Xinjiang and surrounding regions, primarily speaking the Uighur language
member of a Turkic ethnic group in Xinjiang

Examples

Many Uigur communities reside in the Xinjiang region of China.
The Uigur people have a rich cultural heritage and unique traditions.
She studied the history of the Uigur minority and their language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Uighur - alternative spelling for a member of the Uighur ethnic group

Etymology

From Turkish 'Uygur', possibly from Old Turkic 'Uyghur' meaning 'united' or 'allied'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09756018-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈwiːɡər / (AmE), / ˈwiːɡə / (BrE)
the Turkic language spoken by the Uighur people, primarily in northwestern China
Turkic language of the Uighur people

Examples

She is fluent in both Mandarin and Uigur.
A translation of the document was provided in Uigur for local residents.
The Uigur language uses an Arabic-based script.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Uighur - alternative spelling for the Turkic language spoken by the Uighur people

Etymology

From Turkish 'Uygur', from Old Turkic 'Uygur', the name of the people and their language.

Source: WordNet
