Homarus vulgaris

1. NOUN

/ hoʊˈmærəs vʌlˈgɛərɪs / (AmE), / həʊˈmærəs vʌlˈɡɑːrɪs / (BrE)
A species of lobster native to the Atlantic coast of Europe, commonly known as the European lobster.
European lobster species

Examples

The Homarus vulgaris is highly prized in European cuisine for its delicate flavor and texture.
Marine biologists are studying the habitat of Homarus vulgaris along the French coast.
Overfishing has led to a decline in Homarus vulgaris populations in some regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobster - a large marine crustacean with a muscular tail and pincers

Etymology

From Latin: Homarus (a Latinized form of the lobster genus) + vulgaris ('common').
