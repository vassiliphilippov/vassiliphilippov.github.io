meditation

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛdəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɛdɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
Deep and focused contemplation, especially on spiritual or philosophical matters, often practised as a religious or self-improvement activity.
contemplation on spiritual or philosophical matters

Examples

She practices meditation every morning to calm her mind and find inner peace.
During the retreat, participants engaged in silent meditation on spiritual questions.
Buddhist monks are renowned for their long hours of meditation in search of enlightenment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contemplation - deep reflective thought, especially on spiritual matters

Etymology

From Latin 'meditatio' (thinking over, contemplation), from 'meditari' (to think, ponder).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛdəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɛdɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of engaging in deep and continuous thought, usually on a serious or philosophical subject
deep or prolonged contemplation

Examples

She fell into a state of meditation while pondering the meaning of life.
Regular meditation helps her maintain clarity and peace of mind.
His meditation on the poem revealed layers of hidden meaning.

Synset Examples

the habit of meditation is the basis for all real knowledge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reflection - serious thought or consideration
  • Synonym: contemplation - long and thoughtful observation or consideration

Etymology

From Latin 'meditatio' (thinking, contemplation), from 'meditari' (to think, reflect).
