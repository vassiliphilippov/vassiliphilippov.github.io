concretely

1. ADV

/ kənˈkriːtli / (AmE), / kɒnˈkriːtli / (BrE)
In a way that is clear and specific, rather than abstract or general; in concrete terms.
in a clear and specific way

Examples

Concretely, this policy will result in lower taxes for middle-income families.
She explained her ideas concretely so that everyone could understand the plan.
Instead of theorizing, try to think concretely about how to solve the actual problem.

Synset Examples

concretely, this meant that he was broke

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abstractly - in an abstract way; not concretely or specifically

Etymology

From concrete + -ly, formed in analogy to 'concrete' meaning 'real, specific.'
