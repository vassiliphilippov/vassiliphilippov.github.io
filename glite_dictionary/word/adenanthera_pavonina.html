Adenanthera pavonina

1. NOUN

/ ˌædɪˈnænθərə ˌpævəˈnaɪnə / (AmE), / ˌædɪˈnænθərə ˌpævəˈniːnə / (BrE)
An East Indian tree with clusters of yellow-white flowers, grown as an ornamental plant.
East Indian ornamental tree species

Examples

The brilliant red seeds of the Adenanthera pavonina are often used in jewelry.
Gardeners admire the delicate appearance of the Adenanthera pavonina's flowers in spring.
The Adenanthera pavonina can be found lining the streets in many tropical cities.

Etymology

From New Latin, genus and species name; 'Adenanthera' from Greek 'aden' (gland) and 'anthera' (anther), 'pavonina' from Latin 'pavo' (peacock), referring to the seeds' appearance.
