emergency

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɝːdʒənsi / (AmE), / ɪˈmɜːdʒənsi / (BrE)
a sudden, unexpected situation that requires immediate action, often because it involves danger or risk
urgent, unexpected situation needing swift action

Examples

The fire alarm was sounded because of an emergency in the building.
Please use the emergency exit in case of fire or other danger.
She always keeps a first-aid kit ready for any emergency at home.

Synset Examples

he never knew what to do in an emergency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crisis - a time of intense difficulty or danger
  • Antonym: routine - something regular or ordinary, not an emergency

Etymology

From Latin 'emergentia' (an unforeseen occurrence), from 'emergere' meaning 'to arise, to come forth'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɜrdʒənsi / (AmE), / ɪˈmɜːdʒənsi / (BrE)
An official state in which normal procedures are suspended and extraordinary powers, such as martial law, may be enacted to deal with a crisis.
state of crisis with special powers invoked

Examples

After the earthquake, the president declared an emergency to restore order and deploy the military.
During the emergency, civil liberties were temporarily suspended in the worst-affected areas.
The city remained under emergency rule until the crisis was fully resolved.

Synset Examples

the governor declared a state of emergency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: martial law - military control of normal civilian functions

Etymology

From Latin emergentia (an unforeseen occurrence).
