unctuously

1. ADV

/ ˈʌŋk.tʃu.əs.li / (AmE), / ˈʌŋk.tjʊ.əs.li / (BrE)
in a way that is excessively or ingratiatingly flattering or oily, often used to describe insincere behavior or speech
in an overly flattering, insincere manner

Examples

He smiled unctuously, hoping to win favor with the new manager.
The host greeted us unctuously, but his sincerity was questionable.
She apologized unctuously, making the entire exchange feel insincere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unctuous - excessively flattering or insincerely earnest (adj.)

Etymology

From Latin *unctuosus* (oily), from *unguere* (to anoint).
