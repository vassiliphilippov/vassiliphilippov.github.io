Styrax texana

1. NOUN

/ staɪˌræks tɛkˈseɪnə / (AmE), / staɪˌræks tɛkˈsɑːnə / (BrE)
A species of styrax tree native to the southwestern United States and classified as a threatened species.
a threatened styrax tree species of southwestern U.S.

Examples

Styrax texana is increasingly rare and has been listed as a threatened species.
Botanists are working to preserve Styrax texana in its natural habitat.
The fragrant flowers of Styrax texana make it a unique find in southwestern woodlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Styrax - genus of ornamental tree or shrub with fragrant white flowers

Etymology

From Latin 'Styrax' (genus name) and Latinized form of 'Texana' referring to Texas.
