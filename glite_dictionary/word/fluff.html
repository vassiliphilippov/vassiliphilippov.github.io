fluff

1. NOUN

/ flʌf / (AmE), / flʌf / (BrE)
soft, light, and loosely clinging material such as down or fibers from fabric or plants.
soft, light downy material

Examples

The kitten was covered in soft fluff after rolling around in the blanket.
She brushed away the fluff from her sweater before leaving the house.
A bit of cotton fluff floated through the air on the breeze.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lint - small fibers that come off cloth, often collecting on fabrics
  • Synonym: down - soft fine feathers from birds, especially young birds
  • Synonym: stuffing - soft material used to fill cushions or toys

Etymology

From Old French flue (down), of Germanic origin.
