artichoke

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrtɪˌtʃoʊk / (AmE), / ˈɑːtɪtʃəʊk / (BrE)
the thick flower head of a Mediterranean plant with fleshy, edible bracts and heart, often eaten as a vegetable
edible thistlelike flower head

Examples

She served the artichoke with a garlic butter dipping sauce.
The chef demonstrated how to trim an artichoke before cooking it.
I have never tasted roasted artichoke until I visited Rome.

Etymology

From Italian 'articiocco', from Spanish 'alcachofa', from Arabic 'al-ḵuršūf'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːr.t̬ɪˌtʃoʊk / (AmE), / ˈɑː.tɪ.tʃəʊk / (BrE)
A Mediterranean plant with a large, edible flower head, widely cultivated as a vegetable.
edible Mediterranean plant

Examples

The recipe calls for trimming the outer leaves of the artichoke before cooking.
Fresh artichokes are available at the farmer's market in spring.
He dipped the artichoke hearts in melted butter as a starter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: globe artichoke - variety of artichoke with large flower head, typical in cuisine
  • Synonym: cardoon - closely related thistle plant, sometimes eaten

Etymology

From Italian 'articiocco', from Spanish 'alcachofa', from Arabic 'al-ḵuršūf'.
