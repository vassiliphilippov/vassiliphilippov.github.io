advisability

1. NOUN

/ ədˌvaɪzəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ədˌvaɪzəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being advisable; the extent to which something is recommended or prudent to do
quality of being advisable

Examples

The board debated the advisability of expanding into new markets during the meeting.
Many experts questioned the advisability of lifting the restrictions so soon.
She considered the advisability of postponing the trip due to the weather forecast.

Synset Examples

they questioned the advisability of our policy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inadvisability - the quality of not being advisable
  • Synonym: advisable - worthy of being recommended or suggested

Etymology

From 'advisable' + '-ity', following the pattern of abstract noun formation in English.
