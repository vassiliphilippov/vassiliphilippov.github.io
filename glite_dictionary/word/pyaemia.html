pyaemia

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈiːmiə / (AmE), / paɪˈiːmɪə / (BrE)
A type of septicemia caused by pus-forming bacteria entering the bloodstream, often originating from an abscess.
septicemia by pus-forming bacteria
medical

Examples

The patient developed pyaemia after the untreated abscess burst.
19th-century surgeons often reported pyaemia as a serious postoperative complication.
If left unchecked, pyaemia can spread infection rapidly throughout the body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: septicemia - infection of the blood by pathogenic microorganisms

Etymology

From Greek 'puon' (pus) + 'haima' (blood).
