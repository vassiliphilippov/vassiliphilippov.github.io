Spartina

1. NOUN

/ spɑːrˈtaɪnə / (AmE), / spɑːˈtiːnə / (BrE)
A genus of grasses found in freshwater swamps and salt marshes of Europe, Africa, America, and South Atlantic islands.
a grass genus in salt marshes and swamps

Examples

Spartina is commonly used in coastal restoration projects to help stabilize salt marshes.
Researchers found that the spread of Spartina was affecting the habitat of native marsh birds.
The distinctive blades of Spartina grasses are adapted to survive in salty, waterlogged soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cordgrass - common name for several species of Spartina

Etymology

From New Latin, named after Spartina, a region in Greece.

2. NOUN

/ spɑːrˈtiːnə / (AmE), / spɑːˈtiːnə / (BrE)
A genus of grasses found in freshwater swamps and salt marshes of Europe, Africa, America, and South Atlantic islands, commonly known as cordgrasses.
genus of marsh grasses (cordgrasses)

Examples

Salt marshes along the Atlantic coast are often dominated by Spartina species.
Biologists studied the spread of Spartina in the wetlands to monitor ecosystem health.
The dense roots of Spartina help prevent coastal erosion.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'spartē' meaning 'cord' or 'rope', referencing the tough stems.
