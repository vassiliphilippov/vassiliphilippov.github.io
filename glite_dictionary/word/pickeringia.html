Pickeringia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪkəˈrɪndiə / (AmE), / ˌpɪkəˈrɪndɪə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plant in the pea family (Fabaceae), consisting of a single species, the chaparral pea, native to California and Baja California.
genus of plant: chaparral pea

Examples

The Pickeringia is well adapted to dry environments and often found in chaparral habitats.
Botanists studied Pickeringia to understand its fire-resistant qualities.
Pickeringia produces bright pink flowers in late spring, attracting many pollinators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaparral pea - a shrub native to California and Baja California, sole species of Pickeringia

Etymology

Named after Charles Pickering, an American naturalist.

2. NOUN

/ pɪˌkɛrəŋˈgiə / (AmE), / pɪˌkɛərɪŋˈɡiːə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the pea family Fabaceae, containing only one species, the chaparral pea, native to California.
genus of flowering plant (chaparral pea)

Examples

Pickeringia is known for its resilience in the dry chaparral environments of California.
Botanists often study Pickeringia because it is the sole species in its genus.
The chaparral pea, or Pickeringia montana, produces vibrant purple flowers in spring.

Etymology

Named after Charles Pickering, American naturalist.
