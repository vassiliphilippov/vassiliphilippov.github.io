hardening

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrdənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdənɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of becoming hard or solid, especially through cooling, drying, or crystallization
process of becoming hard or solid

Examples

The hardening of concrete requires careful temperature control.
Rapid hardening can affect the strength of certain materials.
The glue undergoes hardening after being exposed to air.

Synset Examples

the hardening of concrete
he tested the set of the glue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solidification - process of becoming solid
  • Antonym: softening - process of becoming softer

Etymology

From the verb 'harden' + -ing.

/ ˈhɑrdn̩ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdnɪŋ / (BrE)

abnormal thickening of body tissue
medical

Examples

Liver hardening, or cirrhosis, is often linked to chronic alcohol abuse.
