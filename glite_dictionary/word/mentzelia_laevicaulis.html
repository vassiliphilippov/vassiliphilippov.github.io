Mentzelia laevicaulis

1. NOUN

/ mɛnˈzɛliə ˌleɪviˈkoʊlɪs / (AmE), / mɛnˈzɛliə ˌliːvɪˈkaʊlɪs / (BrE)
A biennial plant native to the southwestern United States, with white stems, toothed leaves, and large pale yellow morning-blooming flowers.
species of biennial wildflower from SW US

Examples

Mentzelia laevicaulis produces large, pale yellow flowers that open early in the morning during summer.
Gardeners in arid climates often cultivate Mentzelia laevicaulis for its dramatic blooms and low water needs.
The white stems and toothed leaves help to distinguish Mentzelia laevicaulis from similar wildflowers found in the Southwest.

Etymology

Genus name from botanist Christian Mentzel; species name from Latin 'laevis' (smooth) and 'caulis' (stem).
