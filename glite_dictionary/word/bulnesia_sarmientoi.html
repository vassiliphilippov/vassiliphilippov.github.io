Bulnesia sarmientoi

1. NOUN

/ bulˈniːzi.ə sɑːrmiˈɛntoi / (AmE), / bʌlˈniːzɪə sɑːmɪˈɛntɔɪ / (BrE)
A South American tree of dry interior regions of Argentina and Paraguay, known for its resinous heartwood used for incense.
a South American tree with resinous heartwood

Examples

The resin from the Bulnesia sarmientoi tree is often used in making incense.
Many artisans prize the wood of Bulnesia sarmientoi for its durability and fragrance.
Efforts are being made to conserve Bulnesia sarmientoi due to overharvesting in its native habitats.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Bulnesia' and specific epithet honoring Pedro Sarmiento, Argentine explorer.
