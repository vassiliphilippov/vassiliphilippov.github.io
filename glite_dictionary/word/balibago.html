balibago

1. NOUN

/ bɑːliˈbɑːgoʊ / (AmE), / bæliˈbɑːgəʊ / (BrE)
A shrubby tropical tree (Hibiscus tiliaceus) widely found along tropical coasts, known for its light, tough wood used in canoe outriggers and its fibers used for cordage and caulking; also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
tropical coastal tree with tough wood and useful fiber

Examples

The villagers used the strong wood of the balibago to construct new outriggers for their fishing boats.
Coastal gardens often feature the balibago for its attractive yellow flowers and resilience to salt spray.
The fibrous bark of the balibago is valued in traditional crafts for making durable ropes.

Etymology

From Tagalog 'balibago,' the local name for Hibiscus tiliaceus.
