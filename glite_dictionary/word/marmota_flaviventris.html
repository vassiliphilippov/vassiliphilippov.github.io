Marmota flaviventris

1. NOUN

/ mɑrˈmoʊtə flævɪˈvɛntrɪs / (AmE), / mɑːˈməʊtə flævɪˈvɛntrɪs / (BrE)
a species of heavy-bodied yellowish-brown marmot found in rocky areas of western North America, commonly known as the yellow-bellied marmot
species of marmot native to western North America

Examples

During our hike, we spotted a Marmota flaviventris sunning itself on a rock.
Researchers studied the hibernation patterns of Marmota flaviventris in the Sierra Nevada mountains.
The Marmota flaviventris is well adapted to the alpine climate of western North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellow-bellied marmot - common name for Marmota flaviventris, a species of marmot

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Marmota (marmot) and species name flaviventris, from 'flavus' (yellow) and 'venter' (belly).
