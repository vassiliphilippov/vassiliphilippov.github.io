president

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛz.ɪ.dənt / (AmE), / ˈprɛz.ɪ.dənt / (BrE)
the elected head of state and chief executive of a republic
the chief executive of a republic

Examples

The president addressed the nation in a televised speech last night.
After being elected, the new president vowed to implement major reforms.
In many countries, the president serves as both head of state and head of government.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prime minister - the head of government in parliamentary systems

Etymology

From Latin praesident-, present participle of praesidēre (to preside).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɛzəˌdɛnt / (AmE), / ˈprɛzɪdənt / (BrE)
the head administrative officer of a college or university, responsible for overall management and leadership
chief officer of a college or university

Examples

The president of the university announced a new scholarship program for students.
After serving as president for ten years, she decided to retire and focus on research.
The faculty met with the college president to discuss changes to the academic calendar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chancellor - the head of a university in some countries, often ceremonial in the UK
  • Synonym: principal - the head of a school or college, especially in the UK

Etymology

From Latin praesidens, praesident-, present participle of praesidēre ‘preside’.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10488335-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈprɛzɪdənt / (AmE), / ˈprɛzɪdənt / (BrE)
the executive officer of a firm or corporation, typically responsible for day-to-day management and operations
executive officer of a company

Examples

The company's president announced a new strategic plan during the annual meeting.
After twenty years with the firm, she was promoted to president in recognition of her leadership.
The president oversees all major business operations and reports directly to the board of directors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chief executive officer - the highest-ranking person in a company or organization, ultimately responsible for managerial decisions
  • Degree Lower: vice president - an officer next in rank to a president who serves as their deputy

Etymology

From Latin praesidens, present participle of praesidēre (to preside).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈprɛz.ɪ.dənt / (AmE), / ˈprɛz.ɪ.dənt / (BrE)
the person who leads and presides over the meetings of an organization, society, or committee
officer presiding over meetings

Examples

The president called the meeting to order and outlined the agenda for the evening.
As president of the chess club, she ensures every voice is heard during discussions.
After being elected president, he chaired all committee meetings throughout the year.

Synset Examples

address your remarks to the chairperson

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chairperson - person who leads or presides over meetings, often used as a gender-neutral term
  • Antonym: secretary - officer responsible for records and correspondence in an organization

Etymology

From Latin praesidens, present participle of praesidēre (to preside).
