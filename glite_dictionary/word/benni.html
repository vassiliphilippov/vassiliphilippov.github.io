benni

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛni / (AmE), / ˈbɛni / (BrE)
an East Indian annual plant (Sesamum indicum) cultivated for its seeds, which are used to produce sesame oil and as food
East Indian plant yielding sesame

Examples

The benni plant thrives in warm, well-drained soils.
Farmers harvested benni seeds to produce oil used in cooking.
Traditional medicines sometimes include extracts from the benni herb.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sesame - a tropical plant grown for its edible seeds and oil

Etymology

Variant of 'benniseed,' from West African languages (compare Mandingo 'bini-si').
