approaching

1. NOUN

/ əˈproʊtʃɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈprəʊtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or state of becoming nearer in time; the act of something drawing closer in time
becoming nearer in time

Examples

With the approaching of autumn, the days grew shorter and cooler.
The team prepared for the approaching of the deadline with increased urgency.
He sensed the approaching of a significant change in his life.

Synset Examples

the approach of winter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approach - the act of drawing near in time or space

2. NOUN

/ əˈproʊtʃɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈprəʊtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of coming closer in distance or time
the act of coming closer

Examples

With the approaching of winter, birds begin to migrate south.

/ əˈproʊtʃɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈprəʊtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of coming nearer in space to someone or something; the process of drawing closer.
the act of coming nearer

Examples

His approaching frightened the shy deer away from the clearing.
With the approaching of winter, the days became noticeably shorter.

4. ADJ

/ əˈproʊtʃɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈprəʊtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
(of an event or time) happening or likely to happen soon; imminent.
happening soon; imminent

Examples

The company is preparing for the approaching deadline.
We are excited about the approaching holidays with our families.
Everyone was nervous about the approaching storm.

Synset Examples

the approaching election
this coming Thursday
the forthcoming holidays
the upcoming spring fashions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imminent - about to happen
  • Synonym: forthcoming - about to happen or appear
  • Antonym: past - having already happened

Etymology

Present participle of 'approach', from Old French approchier, from Latin appropiare (to draw near).
