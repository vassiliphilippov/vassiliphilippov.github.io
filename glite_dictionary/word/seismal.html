seismal

1. ADJ

/ ˈsaɪzməl / (AmE), / ˈsaɪzməl / (BrE)
relating to or caused by an earthquake or earth vibration
of or relating to earthquakes

Examples

The city implemented new building codes to withstand potential seismal disturbances.
Geologists recorded a series of seismal events along the fault line.
After the seismal activity, residents were cautious about returning to their homes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seismic - relating to or caused by earthquakes or earth vibrations

Etymology

From Greek 'seismos' (earthquake) + English suffix '-al'.
