to engrave

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈɡreɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡreɪv / (BrE)
to cut or carve a design, letters, or words into the surface of something
cut a design or letters into a surface

Examples

She decided to engrave her initials on the inside of her wedding ring.
The jeweler can engrave any message you want on the trophy.
Ancient craftsmen would engrave detailed scenes onto the surface of bronze vases.

Synset Examples

engrave the pen with the owner's name

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to etch - to cut a design into a surface using acid or a sharp tool
  • Synonym: to carve - to cut a design or shape into a hard material

Etymology

From Middle French *engraver*, from en- (in) + graver (to grave, carve).

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈɡreɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡreɪv / (BrE)
to carve or cut a design or letters into a surface, especially a printing block, or to make a print from such a block
carve or cut into a surface (printing)

Examples

The artist carefully engraved the design into the wooden block.
She used special tools to engrave the image for the printing process.
Old books sometimes feature illustrations engraved on metal plates.

Synset Examples

engrave a letter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to etch - to cut or corrode a design onto a surface using acid or other means
  • Synonym: to inscribe - to write or carve words or symbols on something

Etymology

From Middle French *engraver*, from en- (in) + graver (to carve).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01753598-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈɡreɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡreɪv / (BrE)
(figurative) To impress or fix deeply in the mind or memory.
impress deeply in memory or mind (figurative)

Examples

The loss of his childhood home engraved a lasting sorrow in his heart.
Her father's words were engraved in her memory and guided her decisions for years.
That day will always be engraved on my mind as one of the happiest of my life.

Synset Examples

The event engraved itself into her memory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imprint - to impress or fix firmly (especially on the memory)
  • Antonym: to erase - to remove from memory or consciousness

Etymology

From Middle English engraven, from Anglo-Norman engraver, from en- (in) + graver (to grave, carve).

cut or carve into a surface

Examples

They decided to engrave a message inside their wedding rings.
The artist engraved a beautiful scene onto the copper plate.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

Etymology

From Dutch *engraven*, from en- (in) + graven (to dig).
