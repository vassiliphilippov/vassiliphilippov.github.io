alacrity

1. NOUN

/ əˈlækrəti / (AmE), / əˈlakrɪti / (BrE)
brisk and cheerful readiness; eager willingness or enthusiasm
eager readiness; enthusiasm

Examples

She accepted the invitation with alacrity, eager to join the celebration.
The volunteers performed their duties with remarkable alacrity.
He responded to every request with alacrity, impressing his supervisors.

Synset Examples

he accepted with alacrity
the smartness of the pace soon exhausted him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eagerness - keen or enthusiastic willingness
  • Antonym: reluctance - unwillingness or lack of enthusiasm

Etymology

From Latin alacritas (eagerness, liveliness), from alacer (brisk, lively).
