mild

1. ADJ

/ maɪld / (AmE), / maɪld / (BrE)
humble in spirit or manner; showing a gentle, unassertive character, sometimes to the point of submissiveness
gentle or unassuming in manner

Examples

Despite the criticism, he remained mild and refused to argue back.
Her mild answers reflected a personality more inclined to listen than to lead.
In group discussions, his mild manner contrasted with the enthusiasm of others.

Synset Examples

meek and self-effacing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meek - quiet, gentle, and easily imposed on; submissive
  • Synonym: submissive - willing to submit to others; unassertive
  • Antonym: assertive - having or showing a confident and forceful personality

Etymology

From Old English milde (gentle, merciful), of Germanic origin.

2. ADJ

/ maɪld / (AmE), / maɪld / (BrE)
(of weather, climate, or conditions) gentle, not severe, and pleasantly moderate.
gentle or pleasantly moderate (especially of weather)

Examples

We enjoyed a mild autumn with plenty of sunshine and little rain.
The island is known for its mild winters and gentle breezes.
After a week of storms, a mild evening was a welcome change.

Synset Examples

balmy days and nights
the climate was mild and conducive to life or growth
a soft breeze

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balmy - pleasantly warm or mild (especially of weather)
  • Antonym: harsh - unpleasantly rough or severe (especially of weather)

Etymology

From Old English milde (gentle, merciful).

3. ADJ

/ maɪld / (AmE), / maɪld / (BrE)
not severe or extreme in degree, effect, or force; moderate
not severe or extreme; moderate

Examples

The doctor assured me that the symptoms were mild and should pass quickly.
We were lucky that the winter was unusually mild this year.
Her criticism was mild compared to others, and I appreciated her gentle tone.

Synset Examples

a mild winter storm
a mild fever
fortunately the pain was mild
a mild rebuke
mild criticism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gentle - having or showing a mild, kind, or tender temperament or character
  • Synonym: moderate - average in amount, intensity, quality, or degree
  • Antonym: severe - very great; intense; opposite of mild
  • Antonym: harsh - unpleasantly rough or jarring to the senses; opposite of mild

Etymology

From Old English 'milde' (gentle, merciful), of Germanic origin.
