squelcher

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwɛltʃər / (AmE), / ˈskwɛltʃə / (BrE)
a remark or response that completely silences or overwhelms someone, especially by being sharp or conclusive
crushing or silencing remark

Examples

Her comeback was such a squelcher that nobody dared to reply.
He delivered a squelcher that left his opponent speechless during the debate.
Sometimes a well-timed squelcher is more effective than a lengthy argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: put-down - an insulting or dismissive remark
  • Synonym: retort - a sharp, quick, or witty reply

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'squelch' (to suppress or silence), with the agent noun suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskwɛltʃər / (AmE), / ˈskwɛltʃə / (BrE)
an electronic circuit that automatically suppresses the audio output of a receiver when the received signal is weaker than a preset threshold, usually to eliminate noise
electronic circuit suppressing weak signals

Examples

A squelcher is essential in walkie-talkies to prevent constant static noise.
The technician adjusted the squelcher to improve radio reception quality.
Without a properly set squelcher, the receiver picked up a lot of background noise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squelch - a circuit that suppresses background noise in a radio receiver

Etymology

From 'squelch' (to suppress or silence) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04300396-n
Synset POS: n
