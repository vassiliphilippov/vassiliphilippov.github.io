languor

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈlæŋɡə / (BrE)
an oppressive stillness or heaviness in the air, often making one feel sluggish or lethargic
oppressive stillness in the air

Examples

The air hung heavy with languor during the humid summer afternoon.
By noon, a sense of languor filled the silent, sun-drenched streets.
After the storm, the city was cloaked in a deep languor that stilled every movement.

Synset Examples

the afternoon was hot, quiet, and heavy with languor
Summer shows all the languor of a hot, breezeless day as the dancer lazily brushes her hand over her brow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torpor - a state of physical or mental inactivity; lethargy
  • Antonym: vigor - physical strength and good health; energy

Etymology

From Latin 'languor' (faintness, weakness).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlæŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈlaŋɡə / (BrE)
a pleasant feeling of relaxation and lack of energy or activity
pleasant, relaxed lack of energy

Examples

She stretched out in the hammock, overwhelmed by a delicious languor in the afternoon heat.
After the long meal, a sense of languor settled over the guests, making conversation slow and quiet.
The honeyed air and gentle breeze filled him with a dreamy languor as he sat by the lake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: listlessness - lack of energy or enthusiasm
  • Antonym: vitality - liveliness and energy

Etymology

From Latin 'languor' (faintness, weakness), from 'languere' (to be weak or faint).

3. NOUN

/ ˈlæŋɚ / (AmE), / ˈlaŋɡə / (BrE)
a state of tiredness or lack of physical or mental energy; lethargy or sluggishness
tiredness or lack of energy

Examples

After a long day in the sun, she was overcome by a feeling of languor and wanted to rest.
A sense of languor filled the room as the afternoon heat wore on.
He tried to shake off his morning languor with a brisk walk in the fresh air.

Synset Examples

the general appearance of sluggishness alarmed his friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lethargy - a lack of energy and enthusiasm
  • Antonym: vigor - physical strength and good health; energetic activity

Etymology

From Latin languor (faintness, weakness, sluggishness).

4. NOUN

/ ˈlæŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈlaŋɡə / (BrE)
a state of feeling tired or lacking energy and interest; a pleasant or dreamy fatigue
a feeling of tiredness or lack of energy

Examples

She felt a strange languor after the long, hot afternoon in the sun.
A gentle languor settled over the village as dusk approached, making everything seem slower.
Despite his illness, he savored the languor that allowed him to rest peacefully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: listlessness - lack of energy or enthusiasm
  • Antonym: vigor - physical strength and good health; energy

Etymology

From Latin 'languor' (faintness, weariness), from 'languere' (to be weak or faint).
