apnea

1. NOUN

/ ˈæpniə / (AmE), / ˈapniə / (BrE)
A temporary stopping of breathing, especially during sleep or due to certain health conditions.
temporary stopping of breathing
medical

Examples

The patient experienced a brief episode of apnea during the surgery.
Sleep apnea can lead to serious health problems if left untreated.
Doctors monitored her for signs of apnea after she was sedated.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dyspnea - difficult or labored breathing

Etymology

From Greek apnoia, from a- (without) + pnoē (breathing).
