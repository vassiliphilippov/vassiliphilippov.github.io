delavirdine

1. NOUN

/ dɛləˈvɜrdin / (AmE), / dɛləˈvɜːdiːn / (BrE)
a non-nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor used in the treatment of HIV and AIDS, marketed under the trade name Rescriptor
drug for HIV/AIDS treatment
medical

Examples

Doctors may prescribe delavirdine as part of a combination therapy for HIV infection.
Patients should take delavirdine exactly as directed by their healthcare provider.
The development of delavirdine provided new hope for people living with AIDS.
