Clotho

1. PROPN

/ ˈkloʊθoʊ / (AmE), / ˈkləʊθəʊ / (BrE)
in Greek mythology, one of the three Fates, the goddess who spins the thread of life, determining its beginning.
Greek goddess of fate who spins life's thread

Examples

According to Greek mythology, Clotho was responsible for spinning the thread of each person's life.
The ancient Greeks believed that Clotho controlled the beginnings of destiny.
In classic art, Clotho is often depicted with a spindle in her hands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lachesis - the Fate who measures the thread of life in Greek mythology
  • Synonym: Atropos - the Fate who cuts the thread of life in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Κλωθώ (Klo̱thṓ), meaning 'spinner'.
