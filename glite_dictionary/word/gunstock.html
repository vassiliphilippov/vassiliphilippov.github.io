gunstock

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌnˌstɑk / (AmE), / ˈɡʌnˌstɒk / (BrE)
the handle or butt of a rifle, shotgun, or similar firearm, held against the shoulder when firing
butt or handle of a firearm

Examples

He replaced the old wooden gunstock on his rifle with a synthetic one.
The soldier braced the gunstock firmly against his shoulder before firing.
Carving intricate designs into a gunstock was a popular tradition among hunters.

Synset Examples

the rifle had been fitted with a special stock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stock - the part of a rifle or similar firearm to which the barrel and firing mechanism are attached

Etymology

From 'gun' + 'stock', where 'stock' refers to the supporting part of a tool or weapon.
