amebic

1. ADJ

/ əˈmiːbɪk / (AmE), / əˈmiːbɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or resembling amoebae, especially in reference to certain infections or characteristics.
pertaining to or like an amoeba
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with amebic dysentery after identifying the parasite in his stool sample.
Some amebic infections can be serious if not treated promptly with medication.
Researchers are studying amebic movement to better understand cell motility in humans.

Synset Examples

amoebic dysentery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amoeboid - resembling an amoeba in form or movement
  • Antonym: non-amebic - not related to or caused by amoebae

Etymology

From amoeba + -ic, denoting relating to amoebae.
