Peter the Great

1. PROPN

/ ˈpiːtər ðə ɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˈpiːtə ðə ɡreɪt / (BrE)
(1672–1725) Czar of Russia who modernized the country, expanded its territory, and founded St. Petersburg.
czar of Russia, modernizer (1672–1725)

Examples

Peter the Great is credited with transforming Russia into a major European power.
Many historians study the reforms implemented by Peter the Great in the early 18th century.
Peter the Great founded the city of St. Petersburg as Russia's new capital.
