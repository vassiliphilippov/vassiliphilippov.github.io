Azaleastrum

1. NOUN

/ əˌzeɪliˈæstrəm / (AmE), / əˌzeɪliˈæstrəm / (BrE)
a former genus of evergreen or deciduous shrubs now classified under the genus Rhododendron
former genus of shrubs; now in Rhododendron

Examples

Botanists once classified these shrubs under the genus Azaleastrum before moving them to Rhododendron.
The plant labeled as Azaleastrum is now correctly identified as a rhododendron.
Historically, Azaleastrum included both evergreen and deciduous species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rhododendron - a genus of flowering shrubs including azaleas

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Azalea' and Latin 'astrum' (star, used in taxonomy to indicate resemblance).
