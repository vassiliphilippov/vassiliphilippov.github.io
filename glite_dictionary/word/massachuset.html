Massachuset

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæsəˈtʃuːsɪt / (AmE), / ˌmasəˈtʃuːsɪt / (BrE)
A member of the Algonquian people who formerly lived around Massachusetts Bay.
member of an Algonquian people of Massachusetts Bay

Examples

The Massachuset played a significant role in the early history of New England.
Artifacts belonging to the Massachuset have been found near the coast.
Many descendants of the Massachuset continue to honor their heritage today.

Etymology

From Massachusett, the name of the people, likely from an Algonquian word meaning 'at or about the great hill'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmæsəˈtʃuːsɪt / (AmE), / ˌmæsəˈtʃuːsɪt / (BrE)
the Algonquian language formerly spoken by the Massachuset people of eastern Massachusetts.
Algonquian language of the Massachuset people

Examples

Few people today can speak Massachuset, but efforts to revive the language are ongoing.
Some early colonial documents were translated into Massachuset to communicate with native peoples.
The grammar of Massachuset is distinct from that of English.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wampanoag - a closely related Algonquian language spoken in Massachusetts
  • Synonym: Narragansett - another Algonquian language related to Massachuset

Etymology

From the name of the Massachuset people, possibly from an Algonquian word meaning 'at the large hill.'
