antimony

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.tɪˌmoʊ.ni / (AmE), / ænˈtɪməni / (BrE)
a silvery-white brittle metallic element (symbol Sb) used in alloys and found in minerals such as stibnite
a metallic chemical element (Sb)

Examples

The alloy contains antimony to increase its hardness.
Researchers discovered a rich deposit of antimony in the mountain region.
Exposure to antimony compounds can cause health problems if not handled carefully.

Etymology

From Latin antimonium, of obscure origin; possibly from Arabic al-ithmid (stibnite).
