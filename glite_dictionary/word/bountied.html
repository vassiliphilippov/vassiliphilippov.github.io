bountied

1. ADJ

/ ˈbaʊntiːd / (AmE), / ˈbaʊntid / (BrE)
(of an animal or object) eligible to receive a reward or payment, especially in the form of a bounty.
eligible for a bounty or reward

Examples

The organization paid hunters for each bountied wolf they brought in.
He displayed a bountied animal pelt as proof of his successful hunt.
Some invasive species are now considered bountied due to their impact on local ecosystems.

Synset Examples

a bountied animal pelt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bounty - a reward offered, especially by a government, for the capture or killing of a person or animal

Etymology

From 'bounty' (reward, premium) + '-ed' (adjective-forming suffix).
