plastically

1. ADV

/ ˈplæstɪkli / (AmE), / ˈplæstɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner that is capable of being shaped or molded, especially without breaking; in a way that relates to plasticity.
in a manner capable of being shaped or molded

Examples

The metal deformed plastically under the high pressure, creating a permanent bend.
Sculptors often work the clay plastically to achieve fine details in their pieces.
Rock layers can behave plastically deep underground when subjected to intense heat and stress.

Synset Examples

he used the clay plastically

Related Terms

  • Antonym: elastically - in a manner that returns to original shape after deformation

Etymology

From 'plastic' + '-ally'; 'plastic' from Greek plastikos (fit for molding).
