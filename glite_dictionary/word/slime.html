slime

1. NOUN

/ slaɪm / (AmE), / slaɪm / (BrE)
a thick, slippery or sticky substance, often unpleasant, that is viscous in nature
thick, sticky substance

Examples

The pond was covered in a thick layer of green slime.
After the storm, slime coated the sidewalk and made it slippery.
The children played with homemade slime in bright, vibrant colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ooze - soft, slimy mud or a similar viscous substance
  • Synonym: mucus - a slimy substance produced in the body, especially by mucous membranes
  • Degree Lower: grime - dirt that forms a layer on the surface of something, often sticky or greasy

Etymology

From Old English *slīm* (slime, mucus), of Germanic origin.

2. VERB

/ slaɪm / (AmE), / slaɪm / (BrE)
to cover or stain with a slippery, viscous substance
cover with a viscous substance

Examples

The giant snail slimed the garden path as it moved slowly along.
She accidentally slimed her hand while cleaning the fish tank.
The children laughed when the host slimed the contestant with green goo on live TV.

Synset Examples

The snake slimed his victim

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coat - to cover something with a layer of a substance
  • Antonym: clean - to make something free from dirt or substances
