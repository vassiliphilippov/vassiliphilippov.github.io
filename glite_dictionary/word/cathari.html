Cathari

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæθəˌraɪ / (AmE), / ˈkæθəˌriː / (BrE)
members of a Christian religious sect in southern France during the 12th and 13th centuries who practiced Albigensianism, a dualist and ascetic form of Christianity.
Christian sect in medieval southern France

Examples

The Cathari were persecuted as heretics by the medieval Catholic Church.
Many towns in Languedoc were known strongholds of the Cathari in the 12th century.
The beliefs of the Cathari included a strict dualism that contrasted the material and spiritual worlds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Albigensian - a member of a related religious sect in southern France

Etymology

From Medieval Latin Cathari, from Greek katharoi (the pure ones).
