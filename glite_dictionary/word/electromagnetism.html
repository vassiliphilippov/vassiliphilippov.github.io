electromagnetism

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktroʊˈmæɡnəˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˈmæɡnətɪzəm / (BrE)
The branch of physics dealing with the study of electromagnetic phenomena, including electricity and magnetism and their interactions.
branch of physics on electromagnetic phenomena

Examples

She is studying electromagnetism as part of her physics degree.
Understanding electromagnetism is fundamental to designing modern electronic devices.
The course covers the principles of electromagnetism and their applications in engineering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electrostatics - the study of stationary electric charges
  • Synonym: magnetism - physics dealing with magnetic forces and properties

Etymology

From electro- (relating to electricity) and magnetism.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktroʊˈmæɡnɪˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˈmæɡnɪtɪzəm / (BrE)
Magnetism that is produced by an electric current, or the branch of physics dealing with the interaction of electric and magnetic fields.
magnetism from electric currents; physics field

Examples

James Clerk Maxwell made groundbreaking contributions to electromagnetism in the 19th century.
The principles of electromagnetism are essential for understanding how electric motors work.
Many everyday devices, such as transformers and generators, rely on electromagnetism.

Synset Examples

electromagnetism was discovered when it was observed that a copper wire carrying an electric current can magnetize pieces of iron or steel near it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnetism - the physical phenomenon produced by magnets or moving electric charges

Etymology

From electro- (relating to electricity) and magnetism (from Greek 'magnetis' meaning 'magnet stone').
