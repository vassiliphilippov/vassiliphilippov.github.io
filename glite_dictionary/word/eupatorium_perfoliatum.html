Eupatorium perfoliatum

1. NOUN

/ juːpəˈtɔriəm pɚˌfɔliˈeɪtəm / (AmE), / juːpəˈtɔːriəm pɜːˌfəʊliˈætəm / (BrE)
A North American perennial herb with clusters of small white flower heads, formerly used in folk medicine; also known as boneset.
a North American perennial herb (boneset)

Examples

Traditional healers sometimes used Eupatorium perfoliatum to treat flu-like symptoms.
Clusters of white flowers make Eupatorium perfoliatum a distinctive sight in wetlands.
The dried leaves of Eupatorium perfoliatum were brewed into teas by Native Americans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boneset - common name for Eupatorium perfoliatum, a medicinal herb

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining genus Eupatorium (honoring King Mithridates Eupator) and species perfoliatum referring to the shape of the leaves.
