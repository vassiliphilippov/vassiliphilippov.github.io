impalpably

1. ADV

/ ɪmˈpælpəbli / (AmE), / ɪmˈpælpəbli / (BrE)
In a way that is not perceptible or cannot be felt; in a manner that lacks physical substance or clear expression.
in a way that cannot be felt or perceived

Examples

The boundaries between reality and dream seemed to shift impalpably throughout the night.
His mood changed impalpably, making it hard to notice the moment it shifted.
The mist grew impalpably denser, blurring the outlines of the distant trees.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tangibly - in a way that can be touched or perceived physically

Etymology

From 'impalpable', from Latin 'palpare' (to touch or feel), with prefix 'im-' (not) and suffix '-ly'.
