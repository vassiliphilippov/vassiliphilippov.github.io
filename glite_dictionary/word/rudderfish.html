rudderfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌdərˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈrʌdəˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A fish found in North and South American coastal waters, noted for its habit of following ships.
a fish known for following ships

Examples

While sailing off the Atlantic coast, we spotted a group of rudderfish trailing the boat.
The rudderfish is often seen near large vessels, attracted by the movement of the ships.
Researchers observed the rudderfish closely to understand why it follows ships so persistently.

Etymology

Named for its tendency to follow ships, especially near the rudder.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrʌdərˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈrʌdəˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a food and game fish found around Bermuda and Florida, often known for following ships.
a game fish around Bermuda and Florida

Examples

The rudderfish is prized by local fishermen for its firm white flesh.
While sailing off the coast, we spotted a school of rudderfish following our boat.
Many tourists enjoy catching rudderfish on fishing trips near Bermuda.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amberjack - a large marine fish of warm seas; sometimes confused with rudderfish

Etymology

Named for its behavior of following ships' rudders.
