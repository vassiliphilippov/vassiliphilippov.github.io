pointillism

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔɪntɪˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪntɪlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
a technique of painting in which small, distinct dots of color are applied in patterns to form an image, developed by Georges Seurat and his followers in late 19th-century France
painting technique using dots of color

Examples

The museum's new exhibit features several masterpieces of pointillism by Georges Seurat.
She studied pointillism to better understand how painters use color and perception.
Modern artists sometimes adapt pointillism techniques in digital art projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impressionism - a related art movement focusing on light and color

Etymology

From French pointillisme, from pointiller 'to mark with dots', from point 'dot, point'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpwɑɪn.tɪˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪn.tɪˌlɪz.əm / (BrE)
a technique of painting in which small, distinct dots of pure color are applied in patterns to form an image, especially associated with a school of painters led by Georges Seurat in late 19th-century France
painting with dots of pure color

Examples

Georges Seurat is famous for developing pointillism, a technique using tiny dots of color to create images.
The museum's new exhibit features several masterpieces of pointillism from the late 19th century.
Her artwork showcases modern pointillism, blending vibrant dots into stunning landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divisionism - a painting technique where color is separated into individual dots or patches

Etymology

From French 'pointillisme', from 'pointiller' (to mark with dots), from 'point' (dot, point).
