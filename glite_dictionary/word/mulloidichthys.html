Mulloidichthys

1. NOUN

/ mʌˌlɔɪdɪkˈθɪs / (AmE), / mʌˌlɔɪdɪkˈθɪs / (BrE)
A genus of goatfishes found in tropical and subtropical marine waters.
a genus of tropical marine goatfishes

Examples

Marine biologists discovered a new species in the Mulloidichthys genus off the Hawaiian coast.
The aquarium's display includes several brilliantly colored Mulloidichthys swimming among coral reefs.
Researchers studied the feeding habits of Mulloidichthys in different Pacific habitats.

Etymology

From Greek 'mulloidēs' (like a mullet) + '-ichthys' (fish).

2. NOUN

/ mʌˌlɔɪ.dɪkˈθaɪz / (AmE), / mʌˌlɔɪ.dɪkˈθaɪz / (BrE)
A genus of tropical goatfishes in the family Mullidae, found primarily in the Indo-Pacific oceans.
genus of tropical goatfishes

Examples

The waters around Hawaii are home to several species in the genus Mulloidichthys.
Researchers studied the feeding habits of Mulloidichthys in coral reef environments.
A bright yellow stripe is characteristic of many fishes in Mulloidichthys.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'mulloidēs' (mullet-like) + 'ichthys' (fish).
