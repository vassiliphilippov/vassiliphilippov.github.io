carpellate

1. ADJ

/ kɑːrˈpɛl.eɪt / (AmE), / kɑːˈpɛl.eɪt / (BrE)
(of a plant or flower) bearing or consisting of carpels.
having carpels (botany)

Examples

The carpellate flowers develop into fruit after pollination.
Botanists examined the carpellate structures under a microscope.
Some species produce only carpellate flowers, while others have both male and female types.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carpellar - relating to a carpel
  • Antonym: staminate - having stamens but no carpels

Etymology

From carpel + -ate, used in botanical terminology.
