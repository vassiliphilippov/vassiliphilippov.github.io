memorialization

1. NOUN

/ məˌmɔriələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / mɪˌmɔːriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of commemorating someone or something, especially through a ceremony or formal event
commemoration ceremony or act

Examples

The community gathered for a memorialization of the fallen soldiers.
His painting served as a lasting memorialization of his grandfather's achievements.
The annual memorialization included speeches, music, and the laying of wreaths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commemoration - the act of remembering and showing respect for someone or something in a ceremony

Etymology

From 'memorialize' + '-ation', based on Latin 'memoria' (memory).
