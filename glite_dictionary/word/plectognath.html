plectognath

1. NOUN

/ plɛkˈtɑɡˌnæθ / (AmE), / plɛkˈtɒɡ.næθ / (BrE)
a tropical marine fish of the order Tetraodontiformes, with teeth fused into a beak and tough skin covered with bony plates or spines
tropical fish with beak-like teeth and bony skin

Examples

A plectognath is easily recognized by its beak-like mouth and rough armored skin.
Scientists studied the defensive adaptations of the plectognath in coral reef environments.
The aquarium features several exotic plectognaths among its tropical fish collection.

Etymology

From Greek *plektos* (twisted) + *gnathos* (jaw).
