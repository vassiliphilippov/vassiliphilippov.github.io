tearful

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɪr.fəl / (AmE), / ˈtɪə.fəl / (BrE)
full of tears or showing sorrow; appearing as if about to cry.
full of tears or showing sorrow

Examples

She gave a tearful farewell at the airport, unable to hide her emotion.
The children listened with tearful eyes as the story reached its sad ending.
His tearful apology moved everyone in the room.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: joyful - feeling or expressing great happiness
  • Synonym: weepy - inclined to cry easily; sentimental

Etymology

From Middle English, from 'teare' (tear) + '-ful'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtɪrfl̩ / (AmE), / ˈtɪəfl̩ / (BrE)
showing or expressing tears, especially as a result of emotion such as sadness or joy.
full of or marked by tears

Examples

She gave a tearful goodbye to her childhood friends at the airport.
His tearful confession moved everyone in the room to sympathy.
With tearful eyes, she accepted the award for her years of service.

Synset Examples

tearful eyes
tearful entreaties

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tearless - not shedding or marked by tears
  • Synonym: weeping - shedding tears; crying

Etymology

From Middle English, from 'tear' (a drop of saline fluid from the eye) + '-ful' (full of).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02482616-a
Synset POS: a
