fuller

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊlər / (AmE), / ˈfʊlə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to cleanse and thicken newly woven cloth by a process known as fulling
person who cleans and thickens cloth

Examples

The medieval town had a famous fuller whose work was highly prized by merchants.
As a fuller, he spent hours stomping on wet cloth to clean and thicken it.
A fuller would use water and soap to treat the raw woolen fabric.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milliner - a person who makes or sells women's hats

Etymology

From Old English 'fullere', from Latin 'fullo' (a person who cleans cloth).
