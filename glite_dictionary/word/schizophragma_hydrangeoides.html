Schizophragma hydrangeoides

1. NOUN

/ skɪˌzɒfræɡmə haɪˌdreɪndʒiˈɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / skɪˌzɒfræɡmə haɪˌdrændʒɪˈɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A climbing shrub native to East Asia, with adhesive aerial roots, opposite leaves, and small white flowers in terminal clusters; found from the Himalayas to Taiwan and Japan.
species of climbing shrub native to East Asia

Examples

The garden features a mature Schizophragma hydrangeoides climbing the old stone wall.
Botanists documented the distribution of Schizophragma hydrangeoides throughout the mountainous regions of Japan.
When in bloom, Schizophragma hydrangeoides produces clusters of delicate white flowers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hydrangea - a related genus of flowering plants

Etymology

From Greek 'schizo' (split) + 'phragma' (fence), and Latinized Greek 'hydrangea' (water vessel) + '-oides' (resembling).
