victualler

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪtʃuələr / (AmE), / ˈvɪtjʊələ / (BrE)
a person who keeps an inn, especially in British English; an innkeeper or provisions supplier
innkeeper or supplier of provisions
british

Examples

The local victualler welcomed weary travelers to his inn each evening.
She inherited the title of victualler when her parents retired from running the old tavern.
As a respected victualler, he was known for both excellent ale and hearty meals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innkeeper - person who manages an inn
  • Synonym: publican - person who owns or manages a pub or tavern

Etymology

From Middle English viteler, from Old French vitailleor, from Latin victualia (provisions).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɪtələr / (AmE), / ˈvɪtələ / (BrE)
a person or company that supplies food and other provisions, especially to an army or a ship
supplier of food or provisions

Examples

During the campaign, the army depended on a victualler for fresh bread and meat.
The victualler delivered barrels of salted pork to the navy ship every week.
Centuries ago, being a victualler was a vital trade in port towns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purveyor - a person or company that supplies goods, especially food

Etymology

From Middle English vitayler, from Anglo-Norman vitailer, from Old French vitaillier, based on Latin victualia (provisions).

Synset POS: n
