camise

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmiz / (AmE), / kəˈmiːz / (BrE)
a loose-fitting shirt or tunic, especially one worn in the Middle Ages
loose medieval shirt or tunic

Examples

The knight wore a linen camise under his armor for comfort.
During the play, the actress appeared on stage dressed in a flowing white camise.
Historians believe the camise was a common garment among both men and women in medieval Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tunic - a loose garment, typically reaching to the knees
  • Synonym: shirt - a garment for the upper body

Etymology

From Old French 'camise', from Latin 'camisia' (shirt, tunic).
