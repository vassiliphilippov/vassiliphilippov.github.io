plentitude

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɛn.tɪ.tud / (AmE), / ˈplɛn.tɪ.tjuːd / (BrE)
an abundance or a full supply of something
abundance; full supply

Examples

After years of hardship, the family finally enjoyed a plentitude of food and comfort.
The plentitude of natural resources on the island attracted many settlers.
Her garden is admired for the plentitude of colorful flowers throughout the spring.

Synset Examples

there was plenty of food for everyone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something
  • Antonym: scarcity - the state of being scarce or in short supply

Etymology

From Latin 'plentitudo' (fullness, abundance), from 'plenus' (full).
