conclusiveness

1. NOUN

/ kənˈkluːsɪvnəs / (AmE), / kənˈkluːsɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being final, decisive, or leaving no doubt
quality of being final or decisive

Examples

The conclusiveness of the evidence left no room for doubt in the jury’s mind.
Scientists praised the conclusiveness of the study’s results after years of uncertainty.
There was a certain conclusiveness to her argument that ended the discussion.

Synset Examples

the finality of death

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finality - the state of being final or settled
  • Synonym: decisiveness - the quality of being able to decide quickly and confidently

Etymology

From Latin 'concludere' meaning 'to shut up, finish', via 'conclusive'.
