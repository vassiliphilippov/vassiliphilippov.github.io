daisy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪzi / (AmE), / ˈdeɪzi / (BrE)
a plant of the family Asteraceae, with a flower head that has a central disc surrounded by well-developed ray petals, typically arranged in a single whorl
a plant with round white and yellow flowers

Examples

The garden was filled with daisy blooms in the spring.
She wore a crown made of fresh daisies for the festival.
A single daisy stood out among the green grass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aster - plant of the same family with star-shaped flower heads

Etymology

From Old English 'dæges ēage' (day's eye), referring to the flower opening in the morning.
