meiotic

1. ADJ

/ maɪˈɑtɪk / (AmE), / maɪˈɒtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving meiosis, the process of cell division that halves the number of chromosomes, producing gametes or spores
relating to meiosis (cell division)
medical

Examples

During meiotic division, genetic material is exchanged between chromosomes.
Errors in meiotic processes can result in genetic disorders.
The study focused on meiotic spindle formation in oocytes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mitotic - relating to mitosis, another form of cell division

Etymology

From 'meiosis' (Greek meiōsis, a lessening) + -ic (forming adjectives).
