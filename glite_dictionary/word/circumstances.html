circumstances

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜrkəmˌstænsɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkəmstənsɪz / (BrE)
a person's financial or material state, especially with regard to wealth or poverty
a person's financial situation

Examples

After losing his job, he tried to conceal his difficult circumstances from friends.
She was able to travel only because of her improved circumstances.
In straitened circumstances, people often have to make tough choices.

Synset Examples

he found himself in straitened circumstances

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɜrkəmˌstænsɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkəmstɑːnsɪz / (BrE)
the conditions or facts affecting a person's life, including everything that happens to them
overall conditions in a person's life

Examples

She found herself in difficult circumstances after losing her job.
His circumstances improved when he received the inheritance.
Many young people try to succeed regardless of their circumstances.

Synset Examples

whatever my fortune may be
deserved a better fate
has a happy lot
the luck of the Irish
a victim of circumstances
success that was her portion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: situation - a person's current state or set of circumstances
  • Synonym: condition - the particular state someone or something is in

Etymology

From Latin 'circumstantia' (surrounding condition, situation).
