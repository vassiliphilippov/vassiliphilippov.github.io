Geum triflorum

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiəm traɪˈflɔrəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːəm traɪˈflɔːrəm / (BrE)
A North American perennial plant with hairy, pinnate basal leaves, purple flowers, and plume-tipped fruits.
North American perennial plant with purple flowers

Examples

The prairie was dotted with the delicate purple flowers of Geum triflorum.
Many gardeners admire Geum triflorum for its attractive plume-tipped fruits.
Botanists study Geum triflorum to understand its adaptation to North American grasslands.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Geum', the genus name, and 'triflorum', meaning 'three-flowered'.
