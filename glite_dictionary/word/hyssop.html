hyssop

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪsəp / (AmE), / ˈhɪsəp / (BrE)
An aromatic plant with bitter leaves and small blue flowers, used in cooking and herbal medicine.
aromatic plant used in cooking and medicine

Examples

The chef added a few leaves of hyssop to enhance the salad's flavor.
Dried hyssop is sometimes steeped in hot water to make a herbal tisane.
They grew hyssop alongside other herbs in the kitchen garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oregano - aromatic herb used in cooking

Etymology

From Latin *hyssopus*, from Greek *hyssōpos*, of Semitic origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɪsəp / (AmE), / ˈhɪsəp / (BrE)
a small aromatic Eurasian plant of the mint family, with pungent leaves used in cooking, perfumery, and traditional medicine
aromatic Eurasian plant used in cooking and medicine

Examples

The herbal tea was flavored with hyssop and lemon.
She grows hyssop in her garden for both cooking and medicinal use.
Hyssop oil is sometimes added to perfumes for its fresh scent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mint - aromatic herb plants of the genus Mentha

Etymology

From Latin 'hyssopus', from Greek 'hyssopos', ultimately of Semitic origin.
