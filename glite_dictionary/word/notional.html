notional

1. ADJ

/ ˈnoʊʃənl / (AmE), / ˈnəʊʃənl / (BrE)
Existing only in the imagination; not based on fact or real events.
imaginary; not based in fact

Examples

The budget includes several notional expenses that have not actually been incurred yet.
Philosophers often debate notional worlds that differ from our own.
His authority in the group was largely notional, with little real influence.

Synset Examples

the falsehood about some fanciful secret treaties
a small child's imaginary friends
to create a notional world for oneself

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imaginary - existing only in the mind; not real
  • Synonym: fanciful - over-imaginative and unrealistic
  • Antonym: real - actually existing as a thing or occurring in fact

Etymology

From Latin notio (idea, conception) via French notionnel.
