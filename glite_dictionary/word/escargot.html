escargot

1. NOUN

/ ɛs.kɑrˈɡoʊ / (AmE), / ɛs.kɑːˈɡəʊ / (BrE)
an edible land snail, especially one prepared as a delicacy in French cuisine, typically served in its shell with garlic butter
edible land snail dish

Examples

He ordered escargot as an appetizer at the French restaurant.
Many people are surprised to learn that escargot is considered a delicacy.
The chef prepared the escargot with a rich garlic butter sauce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snail - any of various gastropod mollusks with a spiral shell

Etymology

From French 'escargot' (snail), from Late Latin 'coculium'.
