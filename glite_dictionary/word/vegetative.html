vegetative

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɛdʒ.ə.teɪ.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈvɛdʒ.ɪ.tə.tɪv / (BrE)
Relating to involuntary bodily functions such as growth, digestion, or circulation, rather than to conscious activity.
relating to involuntary bodily functions
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that breathing and heartbeat are controlled by vegetative functions of the nervous system.
During a coma, only vegetative processes like circulation and digestion continue to operate.
Damage to the brainstem can disrupt vegetative activities essential for survival.

Synset Examples

vegetative functions such as digestion or growth or circulation

Etymology

From Latin vegetativus (enlivening), from vegetare (to enliven).

2. ADJ

/ ˈvɛdʒətətɪv / (AmE), / ˈvɛdʒɪtətɪv / (BrE)
(of a layer, cover, or region) consisting of or relating to vegetation or plants.
consisting of vegetation or plants

Examples

The forest floor was covered by a thick vegetative layer that nourished the soil.
Our study focuses on vegetative growth in alpine regions.
The vegetative cover helps prevent soil erosion on the hillside.

Synset Examples

regions rich in vegetal products
vegetational cover
the decaying vegetative layer covering a forest floor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vegetal - relating to plants

Etymology

From Latin vegetativus (enlivening), from vegetare (to enliven, grow).

3. ADJ

/ ˈvɛdʒ.ə.teɪ.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈvɛdʒ.ɪ.tə.tɪv / (BrE)
(of an activity or lifestyle) passive, monotonous, and lacking in energy or excitement.
passive or monotonous (activity/lifestyle)

Examples

After his retirement, he slipped into a vegetative routine of watching television all day.
She worried that her son's summer had become too vegetative and unproductive.
Trapped in a vegetative cycle, he felt his days lacked challenge or purpose.

Synset Examples

a dull vegetative lifestyle

Related Terms

  • Antonym: active - characterized by energetic movement or participation
  • Antonym: dynamic - marked by continuous activity or change

Etymology

From Latin 'vegetativus', meaning enlivening or growing.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02844216-a
Synset POS: a

4. ADJ

/ ˈvɛdʒəˌteɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈvɛdʒ.ɪ.tə.tɪv / (BrE)
(of reproduction) relating to asexual reproduction, especially in plants, involving processes such as budding, fragmentation, or runners rather than the union of gametes.
of asexual reproduction (esp. in plants)

Examples

Strawberry plants can multiply through vegetative reproduction by sending out runners.
Many gardeners use vegetative propagation to grow new plants from cuttings.
Mosses often employ vegetative means of reproduction when conditions aren't favorable for spores.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sexual - relating to reproduction involving the union of gametes

Etymology

From Latin vegetativus (enlivening), from vegetare (to enliven).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02144077-s
Synset POS: s
