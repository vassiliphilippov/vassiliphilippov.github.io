Casuarina equisetifolia

1. NOUN

/ kæʒuˌæriːnə iːkwəˌsetəˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / kæsjʊˌærɪnə iːkwɪˌsetɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A tropical Australian tree, often grown ornamentally, known for its slender, needle-like leaves and hard, reddish wood.
Australian ornamental tree; hard red wood

Examples

The Casuarina equisetifolia thrives along tropical coastlines, providing shade and windbreaks.
Wood from the Casuarina equisetifolia is valued for its hardness and reddish colour.
In many parks, Casuarina equisetifolia is planted as an ornamental tree for its graceful foliage.

Etymology

From genus name 'Casuarina,' named for the resemblance of its needle-like foliage to a cassowary's feathers, and species epithet from Latin 'equisetifolia' (with leaves like Equisetum).
