aurally

1. ADV

/ ˈɔr.ə.li / (AmE), / ˈɔː.rə.li / (BrE)
in a way that relates to hearing or the sense of sound
in relation to hearing or sound

Examples

The instructions were provided aurally for those who could not see the screen.
Students were tested both aurally and visually to assess different learning styles.
Some people process information better aurally than through reading.

Synset Examples

the new musical was visually and aurally appealing

Related Terms

  • Antonym: visually - in a way that relates to sight or seeing

Etymology

From Latin 'auris' (ear) + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.
