ebracteate

1. ADJ

/ iˈbræk.ti.eɪt / (AmE), / iˈbræk.ti.eɪt / (BrE)
(of a plant, flower, or inflorescence) lacking bracts.
lacking bracts (botany)

Examples

Many desert plants are ebracteate, producing their flowers directly from the stem.
The ebracteate nature of the species helps botanists distinguish it from its relatives.
Under the microscope, the flower appeared completely ebracteate and symmetrical.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bracteate - having bracts (in botany)

Etymology

From Latin e- (without) + bractea (thin plate, bract).
