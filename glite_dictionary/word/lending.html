lending

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɛndɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of giving money or property to someone with the expectation that it will be returned, often with interest
the act of giving for later return

Examples

Lending money to friends can sometimes put a strain on your relationship.
The bank specializes in lending to small and medium-sized businesses.
Modern technology has made peer-to-peer lending more accessible to everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loan - an amount of money that is borrowed, often with interest
  • Antonym: borrowing - the act of receiving or taking something with the intention of returning it

Etymology

From the verb 'lend', which is of Old English origin (lǣnan, to grant, permit), cognate with Dutch lenen and German leihen.
