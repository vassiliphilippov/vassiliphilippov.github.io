seaborgium

1. NOUN

/ siˈbɔrɡiəm / (AmE), / siˈbɔːɡiəm / (BrE)
a synthetic, radioactive chemical element with the symbol Sg and atomic number 106
synthetic transuranic chemical element (Sg)

Examples

Scientists created seaborgium in a laboratory by bombarding californium with oxygen ions.
Because seaborgium is highly unstable, it cannot be found in nature and must be synthesized.
Researchers study the chemical properties of seaborgium to understand its place in the periodic table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: element 106 - element with atomic number 106 (alternative name for seaborgium)

Etymology

Named after Glenn T. Seaborg, American chemist.
