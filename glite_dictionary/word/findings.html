findings

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪndɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈfaɪndɪŋz / (BrE)
small articles or tools used by artisans, especially for making jewelry, clothing, or shoes, such as clasps, hooks, or fasteners
small parts used by artisans in crafts

Examples

The jeweler selected gold findings to finish her latest collection of necklaces.
A designer needs a variety of findings like zippers and buttons for constructing garments.
I ordered silver findings online to use in my handmade earrings.

Etymology

From 'finding' in the sense of that which is found or provided; specialized use from 19th century crafts and trade.
