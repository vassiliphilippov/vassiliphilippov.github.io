recurved

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈkɝvd / (AmE), / rɪˈkɜːvd / (BrE)
curved backward or inward
curved backward or inward

Examples

The bird's beak is recurved, allowing it to extract insects from tree bark.
Some antelope species have recurved horns, which help them in fighting for territory.
The flower's petals are recurved gracefully at the tips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curved - bent in a smooth, continuous way
  • Antonym: straight - having no curve; not bent

Etymology

From Latin 'recurvus', from 're-' (back) + 'curvus' (bent, curved).
