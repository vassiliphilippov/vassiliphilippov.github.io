Fauvist

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊ.vɪst / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.vɪst / (BrE)
a member of a group of early 20th-century French painters who followed the fauvism movement, known for the use of bold colors and expressive brushwork
member of the Fauvism art movement

Examples

Henri Matisse was a leading Fauvist who revolutionized modern art with his vivid palette.
The exhibition featured works by several notable Fauvists from the early 1900s.
As a young painter, she was inspired by the bold techniques used by the Fauvists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fauvism - art movement characterized by vivid colors and bold brushwork

Etymology

From French *fauviste*, from *fauve* (wild beast), a term used by critics to describe the artists' style.
