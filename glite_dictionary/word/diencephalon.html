diencephalon

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪənˈsɛfəlˌɒn / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.ənˈsef.ə.lɒn / (BrE)
the part of the forebrain situated between the cerebral hemispheres and the midbrain, including structures such as the thalamus and hypothalamus
posterior division of the forebrain
medical

Examples

The diencephalon contains major structures such as the thalamus and hypothalamus.
Damage to the diencephalon can affect a variety of bodily functions, including hormone regulation.
Medical students must memorize the functions of the diencephalon in neuroanatomy.

Etymology

From Greek 'dia-' (through, across) + 'enkephalos' (brain).
