mandril

1. NOUN

/ ˈmændrɪl / (AmE), / ˈmændrɪl / (BrE)
a rotating shaft that acts as an axis for larger rotating parts, especially in machinery
rotating shaft in machinery

Examples

The technician inserted the mandril into the center of the disc to hold it steady during rotation.
A worn-out mandril can cause vibration in the machine, affecting precision.
To change the grinding wheel, first remove the mandril from its housing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mandrel - a shaft or spindle in a machine used to hold or support material being worked

Etymology

Alteration of 'mandrel', from French 'mandrin', of uncertain origin.
