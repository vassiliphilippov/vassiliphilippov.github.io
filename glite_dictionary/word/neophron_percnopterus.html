Neophron percnopterus

1. NOUN

/ ˌniːəˈfrɒn ˌpɜːrkˈnɒptʃərəs / (AmE), / ˌniːɒˈfrɒn ˌpɜːkˈnɒptjʊərəs / (BrE)
a small mostly white vulture of Africa and southern Eurasia, also known as the Egyptian vulture
species of vulture (Egyptian vulture)

Examples

The Neophron percnopterus is easily recognized by its white plumage and yellow face.
Conservationists are working to protect the declining populations of Neophron percnopterus in southern Europe.
We were fortunate to spot a Neophron percnopterus soaring above the cliffs during our safari.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Egyptian vulture - common name for Neophron percnopterus

Etymology

From Greek 'neōs' (new) + 'phron' (mind), and Greek 'perknos' (dusky) + 'pteros' (winged).
