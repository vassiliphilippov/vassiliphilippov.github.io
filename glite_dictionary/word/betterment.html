betterment

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛtərmənt / (AmE), / ˈbɛtəmənt / (BrE)
the action of improving or making something better, especially the improvement of a situation or condition
the process of making something better

Examples

The community center was built for the betterment of local residents.
She dedicated her life to the betterment of society through education.
The charity's efforts led to the betterment of living conditions in the village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improvement - the act or process of making something better

Etymology

From Middle English 'betrement', from 'better' + '-ment'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛtərmənt / (AmE), / ˈbɛtəmənt / (BrE)
the process of becoming or making something better; improvement
improvement; progress for the better

Examples

He dedicated his life to the betterment of society through education and charity work.
The redevelopment project promises significant betterment of living conditions for local residents.
Innovations in technology have contributed to the betterment of healthcare around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improvement - the act of making something better
  • Antonym: deterioration - the process of becoming worse

Etymology

Formed from 'better' + '-ment', modeled after French 'améliorment'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07371988-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɛtərmənt / (AmE), / ˈbɛtəmənt / (BrE)
an improvement that increases the value of a property or facility, especially one that is permanent or structural
an improvement increasing property value

Examples

The city funded betterment projects to revitalize the downtown area.
A new roof is considered a betterment rather than just a repair.
Owners must pay a portion of the costs for any betterment made to the public facilities near their property.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improvement - the act of making something better

Etymology

From the verb 'better' + -ment, meaning the act of making better.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
