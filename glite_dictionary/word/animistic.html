animistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌænɪˈmɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌænɪˈmɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the belief that natural objects, phenomena, and the universe itself possess souls or consciousness (animism)
relating to animism or the doctrine of animism

Examples

The tribe maintained an animistic reverence for the spirits inhabiting the forest.
Certain rituals in the village are deeply rooted in animistic traditions.
Anthropologists study animistic beliefs found in many ancient cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: animism - the belief that objects, places, and creatures all possess a distinct spiritual essence

Etymology

From animism + -istic; animism from Latin anima (soul, life).
