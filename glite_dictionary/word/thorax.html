thorax

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɔːrˌæks / (AmE), / ˈθɔː.ræks / (BrE)
the part of the body between the neck and the diaphragm, forming the chest in humans and other vertebrates
chest area between neck and diaphragm

Examples

The doctor examined his thorax to check for any rib injuries.
Pain in the thorax can sometimes indicate heart or lung problems.
Insects have a thorax that connects their head and abdomen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chest - the front surface of a person's or animal's body between the neck and abdomen

Etymology

From Greek thōrax (breastplate, chest).

2. NOUN

/ ˈθɔːr.æks / (AmE), / ˈθɔː.ræks / (BrE)
the middle section of an arthropod's body, located between the head and the abdomen
middle section of an arthropod's body

Examples

The butterfly’s thorax is covered with tiny scales that help regulate its body temperature.
Insects have six legs attached to the thorax, while their wings may also be found in this segment.
The spider’s head and thorax are fused into a single body part called the cephalothorax.

Etymology

From Greek thorax (breastplate, chest).

3. NOUN

/ ˈθɔˌræks / (AmE), / ˈθɔː.ræks / (BrE)
the middle section of an insect's body between the head and abdomen, bearing the wings and legs
middle part of an insect's body

Examples

The butterfly's thorax is covered in tiny scales that shimmer in sunlight.
Damage to the thorax can prevent an insect from moving its wings properly.
Scientists examined the thorax to identify the species of the captured beetle.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: abdomen - the posterior part of an insect's body
  • Degree Lower: head - the anterior part of an insect's body

Etymology

From Greek thōrax (breastplate, chest).
