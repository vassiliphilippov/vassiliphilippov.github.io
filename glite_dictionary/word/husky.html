husky

1. ADJ

/ ˈhʌski / (AmE), / ˈhʌski / (BrE)
(of a voice or sound) deep, rough, and slightly hoarse, often because of emotion, illness, or shouting.
deep, rough, or hoarse (voice/sound)

Examples

After shouting all night, her husky voice was barely audible.
He greeted them with a deep, husky whisper that caught everyone's attention.
The singer's husky tone added emotion to the performance.

Synset Examples

gruff voices
the dog's gruff barking
hoarse cries
makes all the instruments sound powerful but husky

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoarse - (of a voice) sounding rough and harsh, typically as the result of a sore throat or of shouting
  • Synonym: gruff - rough or harsh in manner, speech, or voice

2. NOUN

/ ˈhʌski / (AmE), / ˈhʌski / (BrE)
a breed of strong, thick-coated dog originally developed to pull sleds in Arctic regions
Arctic sled dog breed

Examples

The husky is well known for its stamina and ability to work in harsh cold climates.
She adopted a husky because she loved their wolf-like appearance and friendly nature.
A team of huskies pulled the sled swiftly across the snowy fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malamute - a breed of large sled dog from Alaska

Etymology

Originated from Russian "uskiy" (related to the Chukchi people and their sled dogs).
