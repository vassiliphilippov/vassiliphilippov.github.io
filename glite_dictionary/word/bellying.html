bellying

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɛliɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɛliɪŋ / (BrE)
curving or swelling outward like a belly.
curving or swelling outward

Examples

The sails were bellying out in the strong wind, straining against the ropes.
The bellying sides of the barrel indicated it was overfilled.
Heavy rain had caused the tent’s canvas to become bellying with pooled water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bulging - protruding outward in a rounded shape
  • Antonym: concave - curved inward, opposite of convex or bulging

Etymology

From the verb 'belly' (to swell or bulge), from 'belly' (stomach, abdomen), of Old English origin.
