/ ˈkænən / (AmE), / ˈkænən / (BrE)
the lower part of the leg in hoofed mammals, extending from the hock to the fetlock, especially the large metacarpal or metatarsal bone
lower leg part of hoofed mammals

Examples

The horse injured its cannon during the race, requiring immediate veterinary attention.
A thick cannon is often considered desirable in racehorses for added strength and support.

/ ˈkænən / (AmE), / ˈkanən / (BrE)
(historical) A cylindrical piece of armor plate designed to protect the arm, especially during the Middle Ages.
cylindrical arm armor plate (historical)

Examples

The knight wore a cannon over his forearm to shield against sword blows.
Restorers discovered a rusted cannon among the remains of the medieval suit of armor.

Etymology

From Old French canon (tube, pipe, rule), ultimately from Latin canna (reed, tube).

5. NOUN

/ ˈkænən / (AmE), / ˈkanən / (BrE)
A large, heavy gun, typically mounted on wheels, used in warfare to fire heavy projectiles.
large artillery gun on wheels

Examples

The ancient fortress was guarded by a row of cannon facing the sea.
A single cannon fired the signal that started the battle.
Historians discovered several rusted cannon buried beneath the old battlefield.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artillery - large-caliber guns used in warfare
  • Degree Lower: howitzer - a type of artillery with a short barrel for firing shells at high angles
  • Degree Lower: gun - a weapon incorporating a metal tube from which bullets or shells are discharged

Etymology

From Old French *canon* (tube, rule), from Italian *cannone* (large tube), from Latin *canna* (reed, tube).
