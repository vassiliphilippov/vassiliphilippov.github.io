narcotising

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɑːr.kə.taɪ.zɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnɑː.kə.taɪ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
making someone insensible or inducing a state of stupor or unconsciousness, especially with a drug; causing narcosis.
inducing stupor or narcosis
medical

Examples

The doctor administered a narcotising injection before the surgery began.
Some plants contain narcotising compounds that can have powerful effects on the nervous system.
The narcotising properties of the medication caused the patient to fall into a deep sleep.

Synset Examples

narcotic drugs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anesthetizing - causing loss of sensation, especially by drugs
  • Antonym: stimulating - making active or more alert, opposite of narcotising

Etymology

From 'narcotize', based on Greek narkoun (make numb), from narkē (numbness).
