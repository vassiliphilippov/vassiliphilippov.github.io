Chauna

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑː.nə / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑː.nə / (BrE)
A genus of South American birds in the family Anhimidae, known as the crested screamers.
genus of crested screamers (birds)

Examples

The Chauna are well known for their loud, trumpet-like calls that carry over great distances.
Researchers observed a flock of Chauna feeding near the wetland's edge.
Unlike other waterfowl, the Chauna have partial webbing on their feet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screamer - large South American bird with a loud call

Etymology

From Latin, used in scientific taxonomy for this bird genus.
