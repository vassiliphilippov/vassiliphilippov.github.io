fellah

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlɑ / (AmE), / fəˈlɑː / (BrE)
A peasant or agricultural laborer, especially in Arab countries such as Egypt or Syria.
agricultural laborer in Arab countries

Examples

The fellah worked from sunrise to sunset in the fields along the Nile.
During the 19th century, every Egyptian fellah was required to pay a heavy tax on his crops.
The village was made up mostly of fellahin who depended on the annual floods for irrigation.

Etymology

From Arabic 'fallāḥ' (ploughman, farmer).
