to magnetise

1. VERB

/ ˈmæɡ.nəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmæɡ.nɪ.taɪz / (BrE)
to attract or captivate strongly as if by a magnet
attract or captivate strongly

Examples

Her performance magnetised everyone in the room from the first moment.
He has a unique ability to magnetise crowds with his speeches.
The novel’s intriguing plot magnetised me and I couldn’t put it down.

Synset Examples

She magnetized the audience with her tricks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to captivate - to attract and hold the interest of
  • Synonym: to mesmerize - to hold the attention of someone to the exclusion of all else
  • Antonym: to repel - to drive or force something away

Etymology

From French 'magnétiser', ultimately from Latin 'magnes' (magnet).

2. VERB

/ ˈmæɡ.nə.taɪz / (AmE), / ˈmæɡ.nɪ.taɪz / (BrE)
to make (an object or material) magnetic by exposing it to a magnetic field
make magnetic

Examples

Scientists can magnetise certain metals by exposing them to a strong magnetic field.
You can magnetise a screwdriver so it picks up small screws easily.
Repeatedly rubbing the needle with a magnet will eventually magnetise it.

Synset Examples

The strong magnet magnetized the iron shavings

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to demagnetise - to remove magnetic properties from

Etymology

From magnet + -ise, ultimately from Greek 'magnētis' (magnet stone).
