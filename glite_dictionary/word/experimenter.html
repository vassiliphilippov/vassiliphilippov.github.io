experimenter

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspɛrəməntər / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛrɪmɛntə / (BrE)
a person who tries out new methods or ideas, often by conducting experiments or testing innovations
person who tests new ideas or methods

Examples

As a true experimenter, she is always willing to try creative recipes in the kitchen.
The art class encouraged each experimenter to explore unconventional materials.
Many scientific breakthroughs have come from a determined experimenter who refused to give up.

Synset Examples

she was an experimenter in new forms of poetry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innovator - person who introduces new methods or ideas

Etymology

From 'experiment' (from Latin 'experimentum', meaning a trial or test) + '-er', a person who does something.

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspɛr.əˌmɛn.tɚ / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛr.ɪ.men.tə / (BrE)
a person who conducts scientific tests or investigations to discover something or test a hypothesis
person who carries out experiments

Examples

The experimenter recorded the results carefully to avoid any errors.
As an experimenter, she always ensured her tests were fair and unbiased.
The experimenter explained the procedure to the participants before beginning the trial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: researcher - a person who carries out academic or scientific inquiry
  • Antonym: subject - a person or animal that is part of an experiment, not conducting it

Etymology

From Latin *experimentum* (a trial, test).
