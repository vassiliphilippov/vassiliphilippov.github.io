doughy

1. ADJ

/ ˈdoʊ.i / (AmE), / ˈdəʊ.i / (BrE)
having a soft, thick, and sticky consistency like dough, often due to insufficient cooking or leavening
having the consistency of uncooked dough

Examples

The bread turned out doughy in the center, so we put it back in the oven.
No one liked the doughy texture of the underbaked pizza crust.
Her pancakes were doughy, lacking the fluffy lightness we expected.

Synset Examples

the cake fell: it's a doughy mess

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gummy - having a sticky, chewy texture that is unpleasant in food
  • Synonym: undercooked - not cooked enough

Etymology

From dough + -y, meaning "like or resembling dough".
