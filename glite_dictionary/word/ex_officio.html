ex officio

1. ADV

/ ɛks əˈfɪʃioʊ / (AmE), / ɛks ɒˈfɪsiəʊ / (BrE)
by virtue of one's office, position, or official role, rather than by being elected or appointed separately
by virtue of office or position
legal

Examples

The vice president serves as an ex officio member of several committees.
As mayor, she is ex officio the head of the local emergency management board.
The treasurer attended the meeting ex officio without voting rights.

Synset Examples

the head of the department serves as an ex officio member of the board

Etymology

From Latin ex (from) + officio (duty, office); literally, 'from the office'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01637031-s

2. ADV

/ ɛks əˈfɪʃioʊ / (AmE), / ɛks əˈfɪʃiəʊ / (BrE)
by virtue of one's position or office; because of the office held, rather than by specific appointment
by virtue of one's office or position

Examples

As chairperson, she serves ex officio on all subcommittees.
The treasurer is an ex officio member of the board.
He became a voting member ex officio when he was elected president.

Synset Examples

the president sat on the committee ex officio

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'from the office'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00253968-r
Synset POS: r
