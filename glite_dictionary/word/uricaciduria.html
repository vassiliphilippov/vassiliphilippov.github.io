uricaciduria

1. NOUN

/ ˌjʊrɪkˌæsɪdˈjʊriə / (AmE), / ˌjʊərɪkˌæsɪdˈjʊəriə / (BrE)
The presence of an abnormally high amount of uric acid in the urine, often associated with gout or other metabolic disorders.
excess uric acid in urine
medical

Examples

Elevated uricaciduria may indicate an increased risk for gout or kidney stones.
The doctor ordered tests to confirm the presence of uricaciduria in the patient's urine.
In cases of severe uricaciduria, dietary changes may help reduce uric acid levels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gout - A disease with painful inflammation of the joints caused by uric acid buildup.

Etymology

From uric acid + -uria (condition of urine), the latter from Greek ouron (urine).
