laudableness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔːdəblnɪs / (AmE), / ˈlɔːdəblnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being deserving of praise or commendation
worthiness of praise

Examples

Her laudableness in volunteering for difficult projects inspired others to do the same.
The laudableness of his actions was recognized by the entire community.
Their commitment to honesty and integrity demonstrated the laudableness of their character.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laudability - the quality of deserving praise
  • Antonym: blameworthiness - the quality of deserving blame

Etymology

From Latin 'laudabilis' (praiseworthy) + English suffix '-ness'.
