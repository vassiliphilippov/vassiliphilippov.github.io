Rosh Hashanah

1. NOUN

/ roʊʃ həˈʃɑːnə / (AmE), / ˌrɒʃ həˈʃɑːnə / (BrE)
The Jewish New Year festival, celebrated on the first or first two days of Tishri, marked by prayer, the blowing of the shofar, and reflection.
Jewish New Year festival

Examples

People all over the world gather in synagogues to celebrate Rosh Hashanah every autumn.
During Rosh Hashanah, it is customary to eat apples dipped in honey for a sweet year.
The blowing of the shofar is a central tradition of Rosh Hashanah.

Etymology

From Hebrew *rosh hashshānāh* (literally 'head of the year').
