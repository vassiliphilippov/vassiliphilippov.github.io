Nevi'im

1. NOUN

/ nɛˈvi.im / (AmE), / nɛˈviː.ɪm / (BrE)
The second of the three divisions of the Hebrew Scriptures, containing the books of the prophets.
second division of the Hebrew Scriptures

Examples

The Nevi'im includes books such as Joshua, Judges, and Samuel.
During his studies, he learned to recite passages from the Nevi'im.
Scholars often compare themes found in the Nevi'im to those in the Torah.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Torah - the first division of the Hebrew Scriptures (the Law)
  • Degree Higher: Ketuvim - the third division of the Hebrew Scriptures (the Writings)

Etymology

From Hebrew נְבִיאִים (nəvīʼīm), meaning 'prophets'.
