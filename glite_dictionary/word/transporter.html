transporter

1. NOUN

/ trænˈspɔrtər / (AmE), / trænˈspɔːtə / (BrE)
a large truck or trailer designed to carry motor vehicles from one place to another
truck for carrying cars

Examples

The transporter arrived early to load the new cars for delivery to dealerships.
Due to its size, the transporter requires an experienced driver and special permits.
Several luxury vehicles were damaged when the transporter was involved in an accident on the highway.
Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ trænˈspɔrtər / (AmE), / trænˈspɔːtə / (BrE)
a conveyor belt or similar device used for moving objects from one place to another, especially in factories
a conveyor belt for moving objects

Examples

The boxes were carried down the assembly line by a transporter.
Safety guards must be installed on every transporter to prevent accidents.
Workers loaded the packages onto the transporter for sorting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conveyor belt - a continuous moving band used for transporting objects from one place to another

Etymology

From transport + -er; transport from Latin transportare (to carry across).

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ trænˈspɔrtər / (AmE), / trænˈspɔːtə / (BrE)
a crane used to move materials quickly, especially for loading and unloading ships
crane for moving cargo at docks

Examples

The dock workers operated the transporter to move heavy containers from the ship to the warehouse.
A malfunction in the transporter delayed the unloading of cargo on the pier.
Modern transporters are capable of lifting much heavier loads than earlier models.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crane - a machine for lifting and moving heavy objects

Etymology

From Latin 'transportare' (to carry across).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
