constitutionalism

1. NOUN

/ kənˌstɪtʃuˈʃənəlɪzəm / (AmE), / kɒnˌstɪtjuːˈʃɪənəlɪzəm / (BrE)
The principle or practice of government according to a constitution, especially one that limits the powers of government and guarantees rights to citizens.
principle of government by constitution

Examples

Many countries strive to uphold constitutionalism to protect the rights of their citizens.
The rise of constitutionalism marked a shift toward limited government power.
Debates about constitutionalism often center on the separation of powers within a state.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: democracy - a system of government by the whole population, typically through elected representatives
  • Antonym: authoritarianism - the enforcement or advocacy of strict obedience to authority at the expense of personal freedom

Etymology

From 'constitution' + '-alism'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑn.stəˈtu.ʃə.nəˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.stɪˈtjuː.ʃə.nə.lɪz.əm / (BrE)
the belief in or advocacy of government according to an established constitution, with limits on powers and protection of citizens' rights
support for government by constitution

Examples

Constitutionalism is fundamental to many modern democracies, ensuring that the government operates within established legal limits.
Many legal scholars have written extensively about the history and virtues of constitutionalism.
After years of dictatorship, the country embraced constitutionalism to guarantee civil liberties and restrict arbitrary power.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rule of law - the principle that everyone is subject to the law, including lawmakers

Etymology

From constitution + -alism; first used in the 19th century.
