vegetation

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɛdʒəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɛdʒɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
all the plants growing in a particular area or during a particular period, especially considered as a collective group
plant life in a region or period

Examples

The tropical rainforest is known for its dense and diverse vegetation.
Arctic regions have sparse vegetation due to the extreme cold and short growing season.
The rapid spread of invasive species is threatening native vegetation in the wetlands.

Synset Examples

Pleistocene vegetation
the flora of southern California
the botany of China

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flora - the plants of a particular region, habitat, or geological period
  • Antonym: fauna - the animals of a particular region, habitat, or geological period

Etymology

From Latin vegetatio (growth, enlivenment), from vegetare (to enliven, animate).

2. NOUN

/ ˌvɛdʒɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɛdʒɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
an abnormal growth or excrescence of tissue, especially a warty growth on the valves of the heart often associated with infection or disease
abnormal tissue growth (medical); esp. on heart valves
medical

Examples

The echocardiogram revealed a vegetation attached to the mitral valve.
Doctors treated the patient for endocarditis caused by a bacterial vegetation on the heart valve.
A large vegetation was found during the surgical examination, requiring immediate intervention.

Etymology

From Latin vegetatio (growth, enlivenment) from vegetare (to enliven or quicken).
