provisional

1. ADJ

/ prəˈvɪʒənl / (AmE), / prəˈvɪʒənəl / (BrE)
arranged or existing for the present, possibly to be changed later; not yet permanent or final
not final; temporary

Examples

The committee made a provisional decision pending further information.
A provisional government was set up after the revolution.
She accepted a provisional offer of employment until her contract was finalized.

Synset Examples

probationary employees
a provisional government
just a tentative schedule

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temporary - lasting or meant to last only for a short time; not permanent
  • Antonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last indefinitely; not temporary

Etymology

From Latin provisio (a foreseeing, provision) via French provisionnel.
