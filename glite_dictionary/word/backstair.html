backstair

1. ADJ

/ ˈbækˌstɛr / (AmE), / ˈbak.stɛə / (BrE)
(of actions or behavior) secret, sly, or sordid, often with the suggestion of being underhanded or unethical.
secret or underhanded; sly

Examples

Rumors about the CEO's resignation quickly led to backstair discussions among the staff.
The politician was accused of backstair dealings to secure his nomination.
As a journalist, she refuses to participate in backstair gossip or speculation.

Synset Examples

backstairs gossip
his low backstairs cunning
backstairs intimacies
furtive behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furtive - attempting to avoid notice or attention, typically because of guilt
  • Antonym: open - honest and not secretive or concealed

Etymology

From 'back stairs,' referring to the private stairs used by servants or to conceal one's movements.
