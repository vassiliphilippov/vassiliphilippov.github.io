to brandmark

1. VERB

/ ˈbrændˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈbrændˌmɑːk / (BrE)
to mark a product or item with a brand name, logo, or trademark to indicate its origin or manufacturer
mark with a brand or trademark

Examples

The company decided to brandmark each new product with its updated logo.
Counterfeiters attempted to brandmark their goods to make them look genuine.
We will brandmark the packaging before the products are shipped.

Synset Examples

when this product is not branded it sells for a lower price

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brand - to mark (an animal, product, etc.) with a distinctive symbol of ownership

Etymology

From 'brand' (mark of ownership) and 'mark' (sign or symbol).
