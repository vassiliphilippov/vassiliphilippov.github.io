arteria temporalis anterior

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈtɪəriə tɛmˈpɔːrəlɪs ænˈtɪriər / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə tɛmˈpɒrəlɪs anˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
the temporal artery supplying the anterior part of the cerebral cortex of the temporal lobe
artery in anterior temporal lobe
medical

Examples

The arteria temporalis anterior provides blood to the front part of the temporal cortex.
Damage to the arteria temporalis anterior may affect speech and memory functions.
Surgeons must carefully navigate around the arteria temporalis anterior during temporal lobe procedures.

Etymology

From Latin: 'arteria' (artery), 'temporalis' (temporal), 'anterior' (before, in front).
