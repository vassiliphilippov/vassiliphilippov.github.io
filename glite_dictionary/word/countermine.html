countermine

1. VERB

/ ˈkaʊntərˌmaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəmaɪn / (BrE)
to destroy or neutralize enemy mines by detonating one's own mines, typically in a military context
destroy enemy mines with own mines

Examples

Engineers worked through the night to countermine the enemy's mining operations.
The soldiers were ordered to countermine the approach to the bridge.
Our unit successfully countermined the area, making it safer for advancing troops.

Synset Examples

We countermined the banks of the river

Etymology

From counter- (against) + mine (explosive device), originally used in military engineering.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01128308-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntərˌmaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəmaɪn / (BrE)
(military) A tunnel dug to prevent or disrupt similar tunneling activities by the enemy, often used to thwart enemy mines or saps.
military tunnel to defeat enemy mines

Examples

The engineers dug a countermine beneath the fortress walls to intercept the enemy tunnellers.
During the siege, a complex network of countermines was constructed by the defenders.
Their countermine successfully collapsed the invading army’s tunnel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mine - a tunnel dug to place explosives under enemy positions

Etymology

From counter- (against) + mine (tunnel to destroy fortifications).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
