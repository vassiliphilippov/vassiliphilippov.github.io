to unloosen

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈluːsən / (AmE), / ʌnˈluːsən / (BrE)
to release or set free from confinement or restraint; to make less tight or strict
release or set free from restraint

Examples

The guard decided to unloosen the prisoner's chains after the hearing.
She carefully unloosened the knots holding the sail in place.
Pressure from the public helped to unloosen the government's strict policies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to loosen - to make less tight or restrictive; to set free
  • Antonym: to tighten - to make or become tight or tighter

Etymology

Formed by prefixing 'un-' to 'loosen,' patterned after analogies with other verbs though often redundant.

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈluːsən / (AmE), / ʌnˈluːsən / (BrE)
to make something less tight; to loosen or release
make less tight; loosen

Examples

Could you unloosen the knot for me?
He stopped to unloosen his tie after a long meeting.
It took a while to unloosen the bolts on the old bike.

Synset Examples

unloose your sneakers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to loosen - make less tight

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' to 'loosen,' though the meaning duplicates 'loosen.'
