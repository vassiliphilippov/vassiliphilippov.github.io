reportable

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈpɔrtəbl̩ / (AmE), / rɪˈpɔːtəbəl / (BrE)
(of income, incidents, etc.) required by law or regulations to be officially reported to an authority.
required to be officially reported

Examples

Your reportable income must be disclosed on your tax return.
Only reportable incidents are recorded in the official log.
The accountant identified all reportable expenses related to the project.

Synset Examples

reportable income

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taxable - subject to tax by law (especially of income)

Etymology

From 'report' + '-able'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00474047-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈpɔrtəbl̩ / (AmE), / rɪˈpɔːtəbl̩ / (BrE)
worthy of being reported or required to be reported, especially to authorities or in a formal context.
worthy or required to be reported

Examples

The error was considered reportable according to company policy.
There are certain diseases that are legally reportable to public health agencies.
The investigation found no reportable incidents during the project period.

Synset Examples

years of research produced no reportable results

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notifiable - required to be reported to the authorities

Etymology

From 'report' + '-able', formed by compounding the verb 'report' (from Latin 'reportare', to bring back) with the adjectival suffix '-able'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00473832-s
Synset POS: s
