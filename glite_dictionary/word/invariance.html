invariance

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈveriəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛərɪəns / (BrE)
the quality or property of remaining unchanged when subjected to a transformation, especially in mathematics, physics, or logic
property of remaining unchanged under transformation

Examples

The scientist discussed the invariance of certain physical laws across reference frames.
Rotational invariance is important in computer vision for recognizing objects from different angles.
The equation exhibits invariance under a change of variables.

Synset Examples

the invariance of the configuration under translation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constancy - the quality of being unchanging over time

Etymology

From Latin 'invariant-', from 'in-' (not) + 'variant-' (changing).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvɛriəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈveəriəns / (BrE)
the quality of remaining unchanged or unaffected by variation or outside influences
quality of remaining unchanged

Examples

The laws of physics often demonstrate invariance under certain transformations.
Her research focuses on the invariance of biological processes despite environmental changes.
Understanding invariance is crucial to developing robust machine learning algorithms.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: variability - the quality of being subject to variation or change
  • Synonym: constancy - the quality of staying the same over time

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + varians, variant- (changing).
