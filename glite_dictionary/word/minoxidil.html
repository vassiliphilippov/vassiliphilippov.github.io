minoxidil

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈnɑksɪˌdɪl / (AmE), / mɪˈnɒksɪˌdɪl / (BrE)
A medication that is used as a vasodilator to treat high blood pressure and, as a topical solution, to promote hair growth in cases of male-pattern baldness.
medicine for hypertension and hair loss
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed minoxidil for his severe hypertension when other medications failed.
Many people use topical minoxidil to help regrow hair on thinning areas of their scalp.
Studies have shown that minoxidil can be effective in treating certain forms of hair loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rogaine - brand name for topical minoxidil used for hair growth
