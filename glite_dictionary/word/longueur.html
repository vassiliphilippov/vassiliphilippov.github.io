longueur

1. NOUN

/ lɔ̃ˈɡɜr / (AmE), / lɒŋˈɡɜː / (BrE)
A dull or tedious passage in a work of literature, music, or performing art.
dull or tedious passage in a work

Examples

The novel was engaging, but it did have the occasional longueur that slowed the pace.
Despite the play's brilliance, some critics pointed out its longueur, especially in the second act.
Reading through the longueur of the essay, she found it hard to keep her attention focused.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boredom - a feeling of being bored or weary
  • Antonym: highlight - the most exciting or interesting part of something

Etymology

From French 'longueur' (length, tedious passage), from Latin 'longus' (long).
