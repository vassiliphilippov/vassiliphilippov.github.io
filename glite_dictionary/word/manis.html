Manis

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænɪs / (AmE), / ˈmænɪs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Manidae, comprising the various species of pangolins found in Asia and Africa.
genus of pangolins

Examples

The genus Manis includes several species of pangolins native to Southeast Asia.
Scientists have recently discovered a new species within the Manis genus.
Conservation efforts focus on protecting all members of Manis due to their endangered status.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pangolin - Nocturnal mammal covered in scales, known for curling into a ball for protection

Etymology

From Latin 'manis', a genus name assigned in modern taxonomy.
