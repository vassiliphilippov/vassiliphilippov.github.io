atonicity

1. NOUN

/ ˌæ.təˈnɪs.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌat.əˈnɪs.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
lack of normal muscular tension or tone; the absence of muscle tonus
lack of normal muscle tone
medical

Examples

Loss of bladder control can occur when there is atonicity of the bladder muscles.
Physical therapists look for signs of atonicity during muscle examinations.
Certain conditions can cause atonicity in the digestive tract, affecting normal movement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tonicity - the normal tension or tonus in a muscle

Etymology

From Greek a- (without) + tonos (tone) + -ity (quality).
