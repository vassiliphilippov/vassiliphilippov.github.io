stripped

1. ADJ

/ strɪpt / (AmE), / strɪpt / (BrE)
lacking unnecessary features; reduced to only the essential or basic elements
having only basic or essential features

Examples

The stripped version of the software costs less but lacks advanced functions.
They bought a stripped model of the car to save money.
Many airlines now offer stripped fares with no checked baggage or meal included.

Synset Examples

a stripped new car
a stripped-down budget

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basic - having only the simplest or most essential features
  • Antonym: elaborate - having many details or features; very complex or ornate

2. ADJ

/ strɪpt / (AmE), / strɪpt / (BrE)
not wearing any or much clothing; with clothing removed.
without clothing; undressed

Examples

The model appeared stripped for the charity calendar photo shoot.
A stripped man was found swimming in the river, attracting a crowd.
She felt vulnerable and stripped on the stage, exposed to everyone's gaze.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naked - without clothing covering the body
  • Antonym: clothed - wearing clothes
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00462109-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ strɪpt / (AmE), / strɪpt / (BrE)
having had everything extraneous or contents removed; left bare or empty
having contents or covering removed

Examples

After the move, the once cozy living room was completely stripped of furniture.
The abandoned storefront had stripped walls, revealing patches of old paint.
When the house was repossessed, it was left stripped and empty.

Synset Examples

the bare walls
the cupboard was bare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bare - lacking covering or contents; empty

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'strip', from Old English *strypan* (to plunder, despoil).
