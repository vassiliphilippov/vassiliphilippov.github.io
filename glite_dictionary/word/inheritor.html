inheritor

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈhɛrɪtər / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɛrɪtə / (BrE)
a person who is legally entitled to receive property or assets from another after their death, especially through a will or by law
person entitled to inherit property

Examples

After the reading of the will, it was clear that John was the primary inheritor of his aunt's estate.
The law determined that she would be the rightful inheritor of the family property.
As the sole inheritor, he was responsible for settling all outstanding debts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heir - a person legally entitled to the property or rank of another upon that person's death

Etymology

From Medieval Latin inheritor (heir); from Latin hereditare (to inherit).
