philhellenism

1. NOUN

/ fɪlˈhɛləˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / fɪlˈhɛlɪnɪzəm / (BrE)
Admiration for Greece, Greeks, or Greek culture and customs.
admiration for Greece or Greek culture

Examples

The scholar's philhellenism was evident in his passion for Greek art and literature.
Nineteenth-century philhellenism played a significant role in the Greek War of Independence.
Her philhellenism led her to study both the ancient and modern Greek languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hellenism - devotion to or imitation of ancient Greek thought, customs, or styles

Etymology

From Greek 'philos' (loving) + 'Hellēn' (Greek).
