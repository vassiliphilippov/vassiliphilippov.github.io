aerofoil

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛroʊˌfɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈeərəʊˌfɔɪl / (BrE)
A shaped surface or structure, such as a wing or blade, that produces lift or another aerodynamic force when moving through air.
surface that generates lift in air

Examples

The shape of the aerofoil is crucial in determining how much lift the wing generates.
Engineers tested the new aerofoil design in a wind tunnel before installing it on the aircraft.
A helicopter's rotor blades act as aerofoils, allowing it to rise vertically.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: airfoil - a surface that produces lift or control in flight (American English spelling)

Etymology

From Greek 'aēr' (air) + 'foil' (thin leaf or sheet).
