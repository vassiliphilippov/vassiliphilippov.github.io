leukoma

1. NOUN

/ luˈkoʊmə / (AmE), / luːˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
a white opaque spot or scar on the cornea of the eye, often resulting from injury or disease
white opaque spot on the cornea
medical

Examples

After the injury, a leukoma developed on her cornea, affecting her vision.
The doctor explained that the leukoma could be the result of an untreated eye infection.
Surgery may be necessary to remove a leukoma if it interferes with sight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cataract - clouding of the lens of the eye

Etymology

From Greek *leukos* (white) + *-oma* (tumor, swelling).
