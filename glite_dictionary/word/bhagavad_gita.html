Bhagavad Gita

1. PROPN

/ ˌbɑɡəˌvɑːd ˈɡitə / (AmE), / ˌbʌɡəˌvɑːd ˈɡiːtə / (BrE)
a sacred Hindu scripture, part of the Mahabharata, consisting of a dialogue between Krishna and Arjuna on duty and spirituality
sacred Hindu scripture; dialogue between Krishna and Arjuna

Examples

Many scholars have studied the Bhagavad Gita for its philosophical insights on life and duty.
The teachings of the Bhagavad Gita continue to inspire millions of people worldwide.
In his speech, he quoted a verse from the Bhagavad Gita to emphasize the importance of selflessness.

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Bhagavad-gītā' meaning 'Song of the Lord' or 'Song of God'.
