longshot

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔŋˌʃɑt / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋˌʃɒt / (BrE)
A photograph or film shot taken from a considerable distance, showing a broad view of the scene or subject.
photograph taken from a distance

Examples

The director chose to begin the scene with a longshot of the city skyline.
Photographers often use a longshot to establish the setting for the audience.
The longshot captured the entire football field and thousands of spectators.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: close-up - a photograph taken at very close range
