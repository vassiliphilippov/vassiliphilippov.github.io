sassafras

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæsəˌfræs / (AmE), / ˈsasəfrəs / (BrE)
A North American tree with aromatic leaves and bark, used as a source of sassafras oil and traditionally for flavoring and medicine.
aromatic North American tree

Examples

The leaves and bark of the sassafras are known for their distinctive aroma.
Early settlers in North America used sassafras as a medicinal plant.
Bottles of old-fashioned root beer often contained extracts from sassafras roots.

Etymology

From French 'sassafras', probably derived from Spanish 'saxifrage' or related to Latin 'saxifraga'.

the dried root bark of the sassafras tree, formerly used as a flavoring and in medicine
dried root bark of sassafras tree

Examples

The pharmacist used sassafras to make herbal remedies.
Old-fashioned root beer was once flavored with sassafras.
She added a piece of sassafras bark to her tea for a unique taste.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Spanish 'sasafrás', from French 'sassafras', origin uncertain, possibly from a Native American language.
