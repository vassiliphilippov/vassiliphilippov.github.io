Scutigera

1. PROPN

/ skuːˈtɪdʒərə / (AmE), / skjuːˈtɪdʒərə / (BrE)
A genus of centipedes in the family Scutigeridae, known for their long legs and fast movements.
genus of centipedes (Scutigeridae)

Examples

The common house centipede belongs to the genus Scutigera.
Researchers have studied the movement patterns of several Scutigera species.
A specimen of Scutigera was discovered in the basement during renovations.

Etymology

From Latin 'scutigera', meaning 'shield-bearing', referring to the shape of the exoskeleton.

2. NOUN

/ skuːˈtɪdʒərə / (AmE), / skjuːˈtɪdʒərə / (BrE)
A genus of centipedes in the family Scutigeridae, including species such as the house centipede.
genus of centipedes (Scutigeridae)

Examples

The common house centipede belongs to the genus Scutigera.
Several species in the Scutigera genus are found in temperate regions around the world.
Scientists have studied the movement of Scutigera centipedes for insights into insect locomotion.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Scutigeridae - family of centipedes to which Scutigera belongs

Etymology

From Latin 'scutigera', from 'scutum' (shield) and '-ger' (bearing), referring to the shield-like plates on its body.
