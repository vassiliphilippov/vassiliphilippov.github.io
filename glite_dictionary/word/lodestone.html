lodestone

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊdˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈləʊd.stəʊn / (BrE)
a naturally magnetized piece of magnetite that can attract iron or steel
naturally magnetized mineral

Examples

Ancient sailors used a lodestone to help navigate across the seas.
A piece of lodestone can attract iron filings in a simple science demonstration.
He found a lodestone in the field and was fascinated by its magnetic properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnetite - a mineral and the main constituent of lodestone
  • Synonym: magnet - an object that produces a magnetic field

Etymology

From Middle English 'lodestone', from 'lode' (way, journey) + 'stone', referring to its ability to guide (as in a compass).
