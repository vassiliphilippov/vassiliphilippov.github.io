Amentiferae

1. NOUN

/ əˌmɛntəˈfɪri / (AmE), / əˌmɛntɪˈfɪəriː / (BrE)
a former taxonomic group in botanical classification, comprising plants that bear catkins, such as willows and birches
plants that bear catkins (old classification)

Examples

Many trees once classified in the Amentiferae group are now distributed among various families.
The Amentiferae included plants like alder, oak, and birch.
Botanists rarely use the term Amentiferae in modern plant taxonomy.

Etymology

From Latin *amentum* (catkin) + -ferae (bearing).

2. NOUN

/ əˌmɛntəˈfɪri / (AmE), / əˌmɛntɪˈfɪəriː / (BrE)
a group of plants, formerly used in classification, characterized by bearing catkins (amentiferous inflorescences)
group of plants with catkins (old classification)

Examples

The family of Amentiferae includes trees that are commonly found in temperate forests.
Many botanists no longer use the term Amentiferae in modern classification systems.
Willows and birches were once grouped together under Amentiferae due to their similar flowering structures.

Etymology

From Latin *amentum* (strap, thong) referring to a catkin, plus Latin *-ferae* (bearing).
