Acrostichum aureum

1. NOUN

/ əˌkɹɑːˈstɪkəm ɔˈɹiəm / (AmE), / əˌkɹɒˈstɪkəm ɔˈriːəm / (BrE)
A stout tropical fern found in swampy areas, especially in tropical America, with large fronds and golden yellow sporangia on the underside.
a tropical swamp fern species

Examples

The large fronds of Acrostichum aureum are easily recognized in tropical wetland areas.
Local communities use the leaves of Acrostichum aureum for weaving mats and roofs.
Botanists were excited to discover a thriving patch of Acrostichum aureum in the coastal swamp.

Etymology

From Greek 'akrostichon' (acrostic) and Latin 'aureum' (golden), referring to the golden sporangia.
