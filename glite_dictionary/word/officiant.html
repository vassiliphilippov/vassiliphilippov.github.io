officiant

1. NOUN

/ əˈfɪʃ.i.ənt / (AmE), / əˈfɪʃ.i.ənt / (BrE)
a person, often a clergy member, who leads a religious ceremony or service
person conducting a religious ceremony

Examples

The officiant welcomed everyone to the wedding ceremony with a warm smile.
Each officiant has their own style of leading religious services.
We chose a close friend to serve as the officiant at our outdoor ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celebrant - person who performs a religious ceremony

Etymology

From Latin 'officiens' (performing, executing), present participle of 'officere' (to perform duty).
