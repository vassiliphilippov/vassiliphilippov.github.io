finance

1. NOUN

/ faɪˈnæns, fəˈnæns / (AmE), / ˈfaɪnæns, fɪˈnæns / (BrE)
the management of large amounts of money, especially by governments or large organizations, including banking, investments, and credit
management of money, banking, and investments

Examples

She decided to study finance at university because she was interested in investments and banking.
A strong background in finance is essential for any successful entrepreneur.
The workshop covered basic finance topics such as budgeting, credit, and managing investments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: economics - the branch of knowledge concerned with the production, consumption, and transfer of wealth

Etymology

From Old French 'finance' (a payment, settlement), from finer (to end, settle a debt), from Latin 'finis' (end).

3. NOUN

/ fəˈnæns / (AmE), / ˈfaɪnɑːns / (BrE)
the commercial activity of providing funds or capital for businesses, projects, or individuals
provision of funds or capital

Examples

The company sought external finance to expand its operations.
Government finance plays a crucial role in infrastructure development.
Banks are the main providers of finance to small and medium-sized enterprises.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: funding - the act of providing money for a particular purpose
  • Synonym: investment - the action or process of investing money for profit
  • Antonym: debt - money owed or due

Etymology

From Old French *finance* (payment, settlement), from finer (to end, settle a debt), from Latin *finire* (to end).
