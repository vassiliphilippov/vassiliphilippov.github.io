casbah

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæz.bɑ / (AmE), / ˈkæzbɑː / (BrE)
the old part of a city in North Africa, typically containing the citadel or fortress
old district with citadel in North African cities

Examples

The winding streets of the casbah were filled with vibrant markets and lively crowds.
Tourists flocked to the casbah to admire its historic architecture and bustling atmosphere.
From the hilltop casbah, you could see the entire city spread out below.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medina - the old city or historic core of a North African town

Etymology

From Arabic "qaṣba(h)" (fortress, citadel).
