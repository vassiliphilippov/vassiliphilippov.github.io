Chishona

1. NOUN

/ tʃiˈʃoʊnə / (AmE), / tʃɪˈʃɒnə / (BrE)
a Bantu language that is one of the two major languages spoken in Zimbabwe
Bantu language of Zimbabwe

Examples

She is fluent in both English and Chishona.
The government publishes official documents in Chishona and English.
He learned Chishona during his stay in Zimbabwe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shona - another name for the Chishona language

Etymology

From Shona 'chiShona', meaning 'the Shona language'.

2. NOUN

/ tʃɪˈʃoʊnə / (AmE), / tʃɪˈʃəʊnə / (BrE)
A Bantu language spoken in Zimbabwe, one of the country's two major languages.
Bantu language of Zimbabwe

Examples

Many schools in Zimbabwe teach in Chishona alongside English.
He is fluent in both Chishona and English, which helps him communicate in the region.
The novel was originally written in Chishona before being translated into English.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shona - the major ethnic group of Zimbabwe and the language they speak

Etymology

From Shona 'chiShona', where 'chi-' denotes language and 'Shona' the people or group.
