idolised

1. ADJ

/ ˈaɪdəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈaɪdəˌlaɪzd / (BrE)
Regarded with deep admiration, love, or almost worship, often to an excessive degree.
regarded with deep admiration or worship
british

Examples

As a child, he idolised his older brother and tried to copy everything he did.
For years, the actor was idolised by fans around the world.
She grew up in a family where her grandfather was idolised as the wisest man.

Synset Examples

adored grandchildren
an idolized wife

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adored - regarded with deep love and respect

Etymology

Past participle of 'idolise', from French 'idolâtrer', from Latin 'idolum' (idol).
