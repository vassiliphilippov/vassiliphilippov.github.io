stagnancy

1. NOUN

/ ˈstæɡnənsi / (AmE), / ˈstaɡnənsi / (BrE)
The state or quality of being stagnant, especially in relation to liquids; a lack of movement or flow, resulting in inactivity.
state of being stagnant, especially in liquids

Examples

The stagnancy of the pond encouraged the growth of algae and mosquitoes.
Economic stagnancy can harm a country's development and prosperity.
The water's stagnancy after the flood posed a health risk to residents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stagnation - the state of not flowing or moving; lack of activity or growth

Etymology

From Latin 'stagnantia' (standing water), from 'stagnare' (to stagnate).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstæɡ.nən.si / (AmE), / ˈstæɡ.nən.si / (BrE)
A state or condition in which activity, growth, or development has stopped or slowed significantly.
state of inactivity or lack of growth

Examples

After years of stagnancy, the town finally saw a burst of new business.
The stagnancy in the art market worried gallery owners and artists alike.
Periods of economic stagnancy can lead to increased unemployment and frustration.

Synset Examples

economic growth of less than 1% per year is considered to be economic stagnation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stagnation - prolonged period of little or no growth or activity
  • Antonym: activity - the state of being active, energetic action

Etymology

From Latin stagnantia (standing water), from stagnare (to stand still).
