Acanthophis antarcticus

1. NOUN

/ əˌkænθəfɪs ænˌtɑːrkˈtɪkəs / (AmE), / əˌkanθəʊfɪs anˌtɑːktɪkəs / (BrE)
A species of highly venomous Australian snake, commonly known as the common death adder, which closely resembles an adder in appearance.
venomous Australian snake (common death adder)

Examples

The Acanthophis antarcticus is known for its potent venom and distinctive ambush hunting style.
Researchers carefully observed the Acanthophis antarcticus in its natural Australian habitat.
Unlike other snakes, the Acanthophis antarcticus uses its tail as a lure to attract prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: death adder - venomous Australian snake, common name for Acanthophis antarcticus

Etymology

From Greek akantha (thorn, spine) and ophis (snake); 'antarcticus' referring to southern distribution.
