Duma

1. NOUN

/ ˈduːmə / (AmE), / ˈduːmə / (BrE)
the elected legislative body in Russia and some other republics that were part of the former USSR
legislative body in Russia

Examples

The Russian Duma passed a new law on economic reform last week.
Debates in the Duma often reflect the political tensions within Russia.
After the 1905 revolution, Tsar Nicholas II allowed the formation of a Duma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parliament - the highest legislative authority in a state, especially in the UK
  • Synonym: congress - the national legislative body of the US, consisting of the Senate and House of Representatives

Etymology

From Russian Дума (Duma), meaning 'council' or 'deliberation'.
