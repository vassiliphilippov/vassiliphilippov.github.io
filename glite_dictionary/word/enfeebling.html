enfeebling

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈfiːblɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈfiːblɪŋ / (BrE)
making someone or something weak; causing a loss of strength or power.
causing weakness or debilitation

Examples

The disease has an enfeebling effect on the body over time.
The long drought had an enfeebling impact on the once-lush vegetation.
His enfeebling illness left him unable to walk without assistance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weakening - making something weaker
  • Antonym: strengthening - making stronger

Etymology

From 'enfeeble', from Old French 'enfeblir', from feble (weak).
