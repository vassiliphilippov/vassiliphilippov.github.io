one

1. NUM

/ wʌn / (AmE), / wʌn / (BrE)
the number equivalent to the sum of zero and one; the smallest positive whole number
the number 1

Examples

She has only one brother, who lives abroad.
There is just one answer to this question.
He arrived at one o'clock for the meeting.

Synset Examples

he has the one but will need a two and three to go with it
they had lunch at one

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: two - the number after one
  • Degree Lower: zero - the number before one

Etymology

Old English *ān*, from Proto-Germanic *ainaz* (one) and related to Dutch *een*, German *ein*, and Latin *unus*.
