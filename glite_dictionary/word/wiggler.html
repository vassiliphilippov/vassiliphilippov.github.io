wiggler

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪɡlər / (AmE), / ˈwɪɡlə / (BrE)
the larva of a mosquito, often seen moving with wiggle-like motions in standing water
mosquito larva

Examples

A microscope revealed hundreds of tiny wigglers swimming in the water sample.
The pond was full of wigglers right after the rainy season began.
Birds feed on wigglers before they develop into adult mosquitoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: larva - an immature form of an insect that undergoes metamorphosis

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪɡlər / (AmE), / ˈwɪɡlə / (BrE)
A common name for a terrestrial worm, especially one used as bait by anglers and known for burrowing into and aerating soil.
common worm used as fishing bait

Examples

The fisherman dug in the garden to find a wiggler for his bait box.
After the rain, dozens of wigglers appeared on the wet pavement.
Children watched a wiggler crawl through the loose soil in the backyard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worm - a limbless, soft-bodied invertebrate animal
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01938036-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɪɡ.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɪɡ.lə / (BrE)
a person, especially a child, who cannot sit or stay still; someone who fidgets or squirms repeatedly
someone who can't stay still, especially a child

Examples

The toddler was such a wiggler during dinner that he couldn't stay in his seat.
My niece, always a wiggler, kept jumping up and down while waiting in line.
It's hard for a wiggler to sit through a long movie without moving around.

Synset Examples

the toddler was a real wiggler on plane trips

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fidget - a person who fidgets or has trouble staying still

Etymology

From the verb 'wiggle' (to move with short, quick movements) + -er (agent noun suffix)
