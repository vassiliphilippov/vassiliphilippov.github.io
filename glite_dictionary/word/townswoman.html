townswoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaʊnˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈtaʊnˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman who comes from the same town as another person or from a particular town
a female resident of a town

Examples

She became close friends with a townswoman who had lived there all her life.
A townswoman welcomed us and showed us around the local market.
Many townswomen gathered to organize the annual festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: townsman - a man from the same town as yourself

2. NOUN

/ ˈtaʊnzˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈtaʊnzˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman who lives in a town or city
female resident of a town or city

Examples

She is a proud townswoman who has lived here all her life.
As a townswoman, she participated in many local community events.
The mayor praised every townswoman who contributed to the project.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: townsman - a man who lives in a town or city

Etymology

Formed by combining 'towns' and 'woman', patterned after 'townsman'.
