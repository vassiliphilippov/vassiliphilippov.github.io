distribution

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪstrəˈbjuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪstrɪˈbjuːʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the way in which something is spread or arranged over an area or among a group
the act or result of spreading over an area

Examples

The distribution of rainfall varies greatly across the country.
Biologists study the distribution of species in different habitats.
We analyzed the distribution of income in the urban area.

Synset Examples

worldwide in distribution
the distribution of nerve fibers
in complementary distribution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dispersion - the process or result of being spread over a wide area

Etymology

From Latin 'distributio' (division, distribution), from 'distribuere' (to divide).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪstrɪˈbjuʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪstrɪˈbjuːʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of supplying goods from producers to consumers, especially by transporting and selling them
the supplying and selling of goods

Examples

The company improved its distribution network to ensure faster deliveries to customers.
Efficient distribution is essential for getting products from factories to stores.
She works in distribution, coordinating the shipment and sale of imported goods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delivery - the act of bringing goods to a place
  • Synonym: supply - the provision of goods needed

Etymology

From Latin distributio (a portioning out), from distribuere (to divide, distribute).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01114977-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌdɪstrəˈbjuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪstrɪˈbjuːʃən / (BrE)
the act of giving or spreading things to a number of people or places, or the way in which something is shared out
the act of giving or spreading out

Examples

The distribution of food and supplies was organized by the local government.
There is a fair distribution of wealth in that country compared to others.
Mail distribution has been delayed due to the strike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allocation - the process of giving out resources or duties for a particular purpose
  • Antonym: collection - the act of gathering things into a group (contrasts with distributing)

Etymology

From Latin distributio (a division, distribution), from distribuere (to divide, distribute).

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ ˌdɪstrɪˈbjuʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪstrɪˈbjuːʃən / (BrE)
(Statistics) The way in which the values of a variable are spread or arranged, often shown as frequencies or probabilities.
statistical arrangement of variable values

Examples

The normal distribution is commonly used to model real-world data in statistics.
Researchers analyzed the distribution of test scores across different schools.
A skewed distribution indicates that the data are not evenly spread around the mean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frequency distribution - a table or graph showing the frequency of values in intervals
  • Degree Higher: probability distribution - a function describing the probabilities of possible values

Etymology

From Latin distributio, from distribuere (to distribute).
