Lota lota

1. NOUN

/ ˌloʊtə ˈloʊtə / (AmE), / ˌləʊtə ˈləʊtə / (BrE)
a species of freshwater cod found in northern Europe, Asia, and North America, also known as the burbot, with barbels around its mouth
freshwater cod species (burbot)

Examples

The Lota lota is the only freshwater member of the cod family.
Anglers in Alaska often seek out Lota lota during the winter months.
Research on the population of Lota lota has increased due to environmental concerns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burbot - common name for Lota lota

Etymology

From scientific Latin *Lota lota*, the species name given by Linnaeus; related to French "lotte".
