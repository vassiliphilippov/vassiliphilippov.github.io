illogically

1. ADV

/ ɪˈlɑdʒɪkli / (AmE), / ɪˈlɒdʒɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that is not logical or does not follow clear reasoning.
in an illogical manner

Examples

He reacted illogically to the criticism, refusing to listen to any reason.
The plan failed because it was illogically structured from the start.
She sometimes acts illogically when she feels stressed.

Synset Examples

she acted illogically under the pressure

Related Terms

  • Antonym: logically - in a logical manner

Etymology

From 'illogical' + '-ly'. 'Illogical' from Late Latin 'illogicus' (not logical).
