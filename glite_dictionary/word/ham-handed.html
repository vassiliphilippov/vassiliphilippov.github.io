ham-handed

1. ADJ

/ ˈhæmˌhændɪd / (AmE), / ˈhæmˌhændɪd / (BrE)
clumsy or lacking dexterity, especially with the hands; awkward in movement or action
clumsy or awkward, especially with hands

Examples

His ham-handed attempt to fix the lamp only made things worse.
The ham-handed handling of the negotiations offended many participants.
She was too ham-handed to tie the delicate bow properly.

Synset Examples

a bumbling mechanic
a bungling performance
ham-handed governmental interference
could scarcely empty a scuttle of ashes, so handless was the poor creature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clumsy - lacking coordination or skill in movement
  • Antonym: deft - skillful and quick in movement

Etymology

From 'ham' (meat from the upper part of a pig's leg), used figuratively for something large and clumsy, plus 'handed' (having hands).
