Alligator mississipiensis

1. NOUN

/ əˌlɪɡeɪtər mɪˌsɪsɪˈpiːɛnsɪs / (AmE), / əˌlɪɡeɪtə mɪˌsɪsɪˈpiːɛnsɪs / (BrE)
the large alligator species native to the southeastern United States, commonly known as the American alligator
American alligator species

Examples

The Alligator mississipiensis is often spotted sunning itself along riverbanks in Florida.
Unlike its cousin in China, the Alligator mississipiensis is much larger and more widespread.
Biologists study the Alligator mississipiensis to monitor wetland health in Louisiana.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alligator - large semiaquatic reptile of the family Alligatoridae

Etymology

From Latin, combining 'alligator' with 'mississipiensis' meaning 'from Mississippi'.
