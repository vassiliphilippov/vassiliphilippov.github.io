sternocleidomastoid

1. NOUN

/ ˌstɜr.noʊˌklaɪ.dəˈmæs.tɔɪd / (AmE), / ˌstɜː.nəʊˌklaɪ.dəʊˈmæs.tɔɪd / (BrE)
Either of two thick muscles on each side of the neck that function to rotate and flex the head.
neck muscle that rotates and flexes the head
medical

Examples

Tightness in the sternocleidomastoid can cause pain and limit neck movement.
The doctor palpated the patient's sternocleidomastoid to check for any abnormalities.
Injuries to the sternocleidomastoid are common in whiplash accidents.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'sterno-' (sternum), 'cleido-' (clavicle), and 'mastoid' (mastoid process of the skull).

2. NOUN

/ stɝː.noʊˌklaɪ.doʊˈmæs.tɔɪd / (AmE), / stɜː.nəʊˌklaɪ.dəˈmæs.tɔɪd / (BrE)
either of the two thick muscles on each side of the neck, running from the sternum and clavicle to the mastoid process behind the ear, which rotate the head and flex the neck
neck muscle that turns and flexes the head
medical

Examples

The sternocleidomastoid muscle is often massaged to relieve tension headaches.
Injuring your sternocleidomastoid can make it painful to turn your head.
Doctors check the sternocleidomastoid during a physical examination for signs of swelling.

Etymology

From Greek 'sternon' (chest), 'kleis' (clavicle), and 'mastoides' (breast-shaped, referring to the mastoid process).
