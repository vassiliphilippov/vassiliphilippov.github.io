Felis yagouaroundi

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːlɪs ˌjɑːɡwəˌraʊˈandi / (AmE), / ˈfiːlɪs ˌjæɡjuːəˈrændɪ / (BrE)
A long-bodied, long-tailed wildcat native to tropical regions of Central and South America, commonly known as the jaguarundi.
tropical American wildcat species

Examples

The Felis yagouaroundi is rarely seen in the wild due to its elusive behavior.
Researchers spotted a young Felis yagouaroundi hunting along the riverbank.
Deforestation threatens the natural habitat of the Felis yagouaroundi throughout South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jaguarundi - common name for Felis yagouaroundi, a tropical American wildcat

Etymology

From Latin 'Felis' (cat genus) and the Tupi-Guarani 'yaguarundí' (name for the animal).
