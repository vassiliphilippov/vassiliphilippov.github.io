Thalictrum

1. NOUN

/ θəˈlɪktrəm / (AmE), / θəˈlɪktrəm / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbs, commonly known as meadow rue, widely distributed in temperate regions.
genus of meadow rue plants

Examples

Several species of Thalictrum are native to North America and prized for their delicate foliage.
Gardeners appreciate the airy blooms of Thalictrum in shaded borders.
Botanists have classified meadow rue under the genus Thalictrum for centuries.

Etymology

From Greek tháliktros, an ancient herb name.
