fouled

1. ADJ

/ faʊld / (AmE), / faʊld / (BrE)
made dirty, polluted, or contaminated, especially with waste or unwanted material
made dirty or contaminated

Examples

The crew worked quickly to repair the fouled machinery before production resumed.
Swimming in fouled water can be hazardous to your health.
The filters were replaced every month to avoid fouled air in the building.

Synset Examples

a building befouled with soot
breathing air fouled and darkened with factory soot

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clean - free from dirt or impurities
  • Synonym: contaminated - made impure by exposure to or addition of a poisonous or polluting substance
Source: WordNet

2. ADJ

/ faʊld / (AmE), / faʊld / (BrE)
(especially of ropes, lines, or anchors) entangled or caught so as to impede movement, especially in a nautical context.
entangled, especially of lines or anchors

Examples

The crew worked quickly to free the fouled anchor from the seaweed.
A fouled line delayed the departure of the fishing boat.
Divers were sent down to clear the fouled propeller before the vessel could proceed.

Synset Examples

with its sails afoul
a foul anchor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tangled - twisted together in a confused mass
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
