loafer

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊfər / (AmE), / ˈləʊfə / (BrE)
a low, slip-on leather shoe with a moccasin-like upper and a broad, flat heel, typically without laces
a slip-on leather shoe with flat heel

Examples

He slipped on his favorite pair of loafers before leaving for work.
These black leather loafers are both stylish and comfortable for all-day wear.
Many students prefer loafers over lace-up shoes for their convenience and classic look.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moccasin - a soft leather slipper or shoe without a heel or separate sole
  • Antonym: oxford - a traditional shoe with laces and a low heel

2. NOUN

/ ˈloʊfər / (AmE), / ˈləʊfə / (BrE)
a person who avoids work or is habitually lazy
habitually lazy person

Examples

He's such a loafer that he never helps out around the house.
The manager fired the most obvious loafer in the team.
Rather than being a loafer, she works hard every day.

Synset Examples

a lazy bum

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slacker - a person who avoids work or responsibility
  • Synonym: idler - a person who is habitually lazy or does no work
  • Antonym: workaholic - a person who compulsively works long and hard hours

Etymology

From 'loaf' (to idle), possibly related to German 'Landläufer' (vagabond).
