conodont

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊ.nəˌdɑnt / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.nəˌdɒnt / (BrE)
an extinct, small, eel-like marine animal of the Cambrian to Triassic periods, known for its cone-shaped, tooth-like fossils; considered a possible ancestor of jawless fishes
extinct eel-like marine animal with cone-shaped fossils

Examples

The fossilized conodont elements provide valuable clues about ancient marine environments.
Scientists debated the classification of the mysterious conodont for many years.
A well-preserved conodont fossil was recently discovered in the limestone deposits.

Etymology

From Greek 'kōnos' (cone) + 'odous, odont-' (tooth), referring to the shape of their fossilized elements.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.nə.dɑnt / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.nə.dɒnt / (BrE)
a tiny, cone-shaped fossil tooth belonging to an extinct primitive vertebrate of the order Conodonta, used to study geological time periods
tiny fossil tooth of extinct vertebrate

Examples

Paleontologists discovered several conodont elements in the limestone deposit.
The age of the rock layer was determined by analyzing conodont fossils found within it.
Because of their abundance and diversity, conodonts are useful for dating Paleozoic strata.

Etymology

From Greek kōnos (cone) + odont- (tooth).
