Passiflora ligularis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæsəˈflɔrə lɪˌɡjuːˈlærɪs / (AmE), / ˌpæsɪˈflɔːrə lɪˌɡjuːˈlærɪs / (BrE)
a species of passionflower native to South America, cultivated for its sweet edible fruit, also known as sweet granadilla
a type of passionflower with edible fruit

Examples

The fruit of the Passiflora ligularis is prized for its sweet, aromatic pulp.
Farmers in Colombia cultivate Passiflora ligularis for local and international markets.
The Passiflora ligularis vine thrives in subtropical climates at high altitudes.

Etymology

From Latin: 'passiflora' (passionflower) + 'ligularis' (bearing a small strap, referring to the shape of some floral parts).
