reward

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈwɔrd / (AmE), / rɪˈwɔːd / (BrE)
a payment or benefit given in return for a service or achievement
payment for a service or achievement

Examples

The police offered a reward for information leading to the arrest of the suspect.
She received a generous reward for returning the lost dog to its owner.
All volunteers were given a small reward as a token of appreciation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: punishment - a penalty given in response to wrongdoing
  • Synonym: prize - something won as a result of achieving something

Etymology

From Old French 'reguarde' (regard, consideration), from regarder (to take notice of), influenced by Middle English 'rewarden' (to regard or reward).

From Old French 'reguard', from regarder (to regard, to look at), later associated with 'reward' in sense of recompense.
