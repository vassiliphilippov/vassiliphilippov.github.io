teazel

1. NOUN

/ ˈtiːzəl / (AmE), / ˈtiːzəl / (BrE)
any of several prickly herbs of the genus Dipsacus, native to the Old World, with flower heads surrounded by spiny bracts
prickly Old World plant (genus Dipsacus)

Examples

The field was filled with teazel, their prickly heads swaying in the breeze.
Gardeners sometimes remove teazel to prevent it from spreading among other wildflowers.
Dried teazel is often used in floral arrangements for its unique texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teasel - alternative spelling; same meaning, more common

Etymology

From Middle English *tesel*, from Old English *tæsel* (thistle, teasel).
