hypochlorite

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪ.poʊˈklɔːr.aɪt / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪ.pəˈklɔː.rʌɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester of hypochlorous acid, commonly used as a disinfectant or bleaching agent
salt or ester of hypochlorous acid

Examples

Sodium hypochlorite is commonly found in household bleach and is used to disinfect surfaces.
The chemist explained that calcium hypochlorite is frequently used to sanitize swimming pools.
Exposure to hypochlorite solutions can cause irritation to the skin and eyes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: chlorite - a salt of chlorous acid

Etymology

From hypo- (meaning 'under' or 'less') and chlorite, referring to compounds with a lower proportion of oxygen; ultimately from Greek 'chloros' (green-yellow).
