yardwoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɑrdˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈjɑːdˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman who works in a railway yard, typically involved in tasks such as organizing trains, shunting, or handling freight
female railway yard worker

Examples

The yardwoman coordinated the shunting of freight cars in the early morning.
As the first yardwoman at her station, she paved the way for others to follow.
Every yardwoman attended a safety briefing before starting her shift.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yardman - a male worker in a railway yard

2. NOUN

/ ˈjɑrdˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈjɑːdˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman employed to do outdoor work such as mowing lawns and gardening
female outdoor laborer

Examples

The school hired a yardwoman to take care of the playground and gardens.
As a yardwoman, she spent her mornings mowing the lawn and pruning bushes.
The estate's yardwoman keeps the grounds tidy throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yardman - a man employed to do outdoor work such as landscaping or gardening

Etymology

Formed by combining 'yard' (outdoor area) and 'woman', parallel to 'yardman'.
