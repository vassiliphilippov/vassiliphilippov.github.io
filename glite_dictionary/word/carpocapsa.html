Carpocapsa

1. NOUN

/ kɑːrpəˈkæpsə / (AmE), / kɑːpəˈkæpsə / (BrE)
A former genus name of codling moths, now typically classified as Cydia; moths whose larvae are known pests of apples and other fruit.
former genus name for codling moths

Examples

While reading an old entomology text, I came across several references to Carpocapsa.
The Carpocapsa larvae burrow into apples, causing extensive damage to orchards.
Taxonomic revisions have moved species once called Carpocapsa into the genus Cydia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: codling moth - A moth whose larvae attack apples, Cydia pomonella
  • Synonym: Cydia - Genus of moths, the current classification for codling moths

Etymology

From Greek karpos (fruit) + kaptein (to swallow), referring to the larva's fruit-eating habit.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːr.pəˈkæp.sə / (AmE), / ˌkɑː.pəˈkæp.sə / (BrE)
A genus of moths including the codling moth, known for its larvae that infest fruits such as apples and pears.
genus of moths (codling moths)

Examples

The Carpocapsa genus includes several important fruit pests, notably the codling moth.
Scientists are studying Carpocapsa species to develop better pest control methods for orchards.
Damage caused by the larvae of Carpocapsa can significantly reduce apple crop yields.

Etymology

From Greek karpos (fruit) + kapsa (box), referring to the moth's association with fruit.
