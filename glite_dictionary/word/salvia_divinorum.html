Salvia divinorum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæl.vi.ə dɪˌvɪn.əˈrʊm / (AmE), / ˈsal.vi.ə dɪˌvɪn.əˈrʊm / (BrE)
a plant native to Oaxaca, Mexico, known for its powerful hallucinogenic effects due to the active compound salvinorin
hallucinogenic Mexican plant

Examples

The Mazatec people traditionally use Salvia divinorum in shamanic rituals for spiritual healing.
She studied the effects of Salvia divinorum on human perception and consciousness.
Possession of Salvia divinorum is restricted in some countries due to its hallucinogenic properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sage - aromatic herb of the genus Salvia

Etymology

Latin: 'salvia' meaning sage, 'divinorum' meaning of the diviners.
