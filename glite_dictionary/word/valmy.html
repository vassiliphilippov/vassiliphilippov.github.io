Valmy

1. PROPN

/ ˈvæl.mi / (AmE), / ˈval.mi / (BrE)
the site of a decisive battle in 1792 where French forces defeated Austrian and Prussian troops during the French Revolutionary Wars
site of a battle (1792, French victory)

Examples

The Battle of Valmy marked a turning point in the French Revolutionary Wars.
Historians often cite Valmy as the first major victory for the revolutionary army.
Many consider Valmy the moment when the French nation was defended against foreign intervention.

Etymology

Named after the village of Valmy in northeastern France, where the battle took place.
