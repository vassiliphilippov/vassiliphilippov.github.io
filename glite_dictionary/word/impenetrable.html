impenetrable

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɛnɪtrəbəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛnɪtrəbl / (BrE)
extremely difficult or impossible to understand; incomprehensible.
impossible to understand

Examples

The report was filled with impenetrable legal language that confused most readers.
Her writing style is so dense and impenetrable that few people finish her books.
For outsiders, the technical discussion remained largely impenetrable.

Synset Examples

impenetrable jargon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incomprehensible - impossible to understand; unintelligible

Etymology

From Latin impenetrabilis, from in- (not) + penetrabilis (penetrable).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00537141-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɛnɪtrəbəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛnɪtrəbl / (BrE)
impossible to pass through or enter; not allowing access, passage, or penetration.
impossible to pass through or enter

Examples

The thick fog this morning was so impenetrable that I could barely see a few feet ahead.
Security forces guarded the facility, making it virtually impenetrable to intruders.
The ancient castle sits atop a hill, surrounded by impenetrable walls.

Synset Examples

an impenetrable fortress
impenetrable rain forests

Related Terms

  • Antonym: penetrable - able to be entered or passed through

Etymology

From Latin 'impenetrabilis' (not to be penetrated).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01776177-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɛnɪtrəbəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛnɪtrəbl / (BrE)
so dense that little or no light can pass through; impossible to see through
very dense, allowing no light through

Examples

The thick morning fog was so impenetrable that we could barely see the street ahead.
An impenetrable forest blocked out the sunlight, casting deep shadows on the ground.
The smoke from the fire formed an impenetrable barrier that made it impossible to find the exit.

Synset Examples

dense smoke
heavy fog
impenetrable gloom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opaque - not able to be seen through; not transparent
  • Antonym: translucent - allowing light, but not detailed shapes, to pass through
  • Antonym: transparent - allowing light to pass through so objects behind can be seen clearly

Etymology

From Latin impenetrabilis, from in- (not) + penetrabilis (penetrable).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02425186-s
Synset POS: s
