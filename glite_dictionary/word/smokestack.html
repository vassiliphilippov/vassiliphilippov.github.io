smokestack

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmoʊkˌstæk / (AmE), / ˈsməʊkˌstak / (BrE)
a tall, vertical chimney on a factory, ship, or power plant for releasing smoke and gases from combustion into the atmosphere
tall industrial chimney

Examples

The factory's smokestack released a steady stream of grey smoke into the sky.
They could see the old ship's smokestack from across the harbor.
Strict regulations now limit what can be emitted from a smokestack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chimney - a vertical structure that provides ventilation for smoke or gases

Etymology

From 'smoke' + 'stack', first used in the 19th century with the rise of steam-powered machinery.
