cetacean

1. ADJ

/ sɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / sɪˈteɪsɪən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of whales, dolphins, and porpoises
relating to whales and dolphins

Examples

The museum features a vast collection of cetacean skeletons, including those of whales and dolphins.
Recent studies have revealed new information about cetacean intelligence and social behaviors.
Many cetacean species are protected due to concerns about their declining populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whale - a large marine mammal in the order Cetacea
  • Synonym: dolphin - a small toothed whale in the family Delphinidae

Etymology

From Latin 'cetus' (whale), via French 'cétacé'.

2. NOUN

/ sɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / sɪˈteɪsiən / (BrE)
A large aquatic mammal of the order Cetacea, such as a whale, dolphin, or porpoise, characterized by fin-like forelimbs, no hind limbs, and carnivorous habits.
large aquatic mammal (whale, dolphin, etc.)

Examples

Baleen whales are a type of cetacean found in oceans worldwide.
Scientists are studying the social behavior of cetaceans, including dolphins and porpoises.
The blue whale, the largest animal on Earth, is a marine cetacean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whale - large marine mammal of the order Cetacea
  • Synonym: dolphin - small toothed whale with a beaklike snout
  • Synonym: porpoise - small toothed whale with a blunt snout

Etymology

From Latin 'Cetacea' (neuter plural of 'cētāceus', 'pertaining to a whale'), from Greek 'kētos' (sea monster, whale).

3. NOUN

/ sɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / sɪˈteɪsɪən / (BrE)
a large aquatic mammal of the order Cetacea, including whales, dolphins, and porpoises, with fin-like forelimbs and no hind limbs
marine mammal (whale, dolphin, porpoise)

Examples

The blue whale is the largest cetacean and the largest animal on Earth.
Scientists study the migration patterns of cetaceans to monitor ocean health.
Many cetacean species are protected by international law.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whale - a very large marine mammal of the order Cetacea
  • Synonym: dolphin - a small toothed whale with a beaklike snout
  • Synonym: porpoise - a small toothed whale distinct from dolphins

Etymology

From Latin cetaceus (pertaining to a whale), from cetus (whale).

Synset POS: n
