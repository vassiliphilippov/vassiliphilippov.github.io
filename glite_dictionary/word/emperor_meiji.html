Emperor Meiji

1. PROPN

/ ɛmˈpɪrər meɪˈdʒi / (AmE), / ɛmˈpɪərə ˈmeɪdʒi / (BrE)
the emperor of Japan from 1867 to 1912, known for leading the country's modernization and westernization during the Meiji period
emperor of Japan (1867–1912) who led modernization

Examples

Emperor Meiji played a crucial role in transforming Japan into a modern state.
The reforms initiated by Emperor Meiji brought significant changes to Japanese society.
Many historians credit Emperor Meiji with opening Japan to the world.

Etymology

Meiji is Japanese for 'enlightened rule', the name of the era during his reign.
