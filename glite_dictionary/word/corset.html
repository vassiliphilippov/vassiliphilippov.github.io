corset

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔr.sɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.sɪt / (BrE)
a close-fitting undergarment worn by women to shape and support the torso, often extending from below the bust to the hips
close-fitting women's foundation garment

Examples

Victorian women often wore a corset to achieve a fashionable silhouette.
She laced the corset tightly before putting on her dress for the costume party.
Modern designers sometimes incorporate the corset style into evening wear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: girdle - a similar type of undergarment used for shaping the waist
  • Degree Lower: bra - an undergarment supporting the breasts

Etymology

From Old French *corset* (diminutive of *cors*, meaning 'body'), from Latin *corpus* (body).

2. VERB

/ ˈkɔːr.sət / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.sɪt / (BrE)
to dress or fit someone with a corset, or to tightly restrict with or as if with a corset.
to dress or fit with a corset

Examples

The actress was corseted for her role in the period drama.
Victorian fashion often corseted women to create an hourglass figure.
She complained that being corseted all day made it hard to breathe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lace - to fasten or tighten a garment by lacing

Etymology

From French 'corset', diminutive of 'cors' (body).
