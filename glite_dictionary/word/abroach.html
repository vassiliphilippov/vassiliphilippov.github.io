abroach

1. ADJ

/ əˈbroʊtʃ / (AmE), / əˈbrəʊtʃ / (BrE)
(of a cask or barrel) opened so that its contents can be drawn off; broached.
of a cask: opened for use
archaic

Examples

When the wine barrel was set abroach, the guests eagerly awaited a taste.
The innkeeper kept several casks abroach during the festival.
Once the keg was abroach, the ale flowed freely into mugs.

Synset Examples

the cask was set abroach

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broached - (of a cask) opened so contents can be drawn

Etymology

From Old French *abrochier* (to broach, to pierce), from *à* (to) + *broche* (spit, pointed rod).
