stagnation

1. NOUN

/ stæɡˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / stæɡˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a period or state in which activity or growth stops, especially in business, the economy, or art
a period of inactivity or lack of growth

Examples

After years of rapid expansion, the company entered a period of stagnation that lasted nearly a decade.
Many experts fear that prolonged stagnation could lead to higher unemployment rates.
Cultural stagnation often occurs when societies resist innovation and change.

Synset Examples

economic growth of less than 1% per year is considered to be economic stagnation

Related Terms

  • Antonym: boom - a period of rapid economic growth or activity
  • Degree Lower: recession - a period of economic decline
  • Antonym: prosperity - a period of successful, flourishing, or thriving condition

Etymology

From Latin stagnatio (standing water), from stagnum (pool, standing water).

2. NOUN

/ stægˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / stæɡˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
The state of being still or inactive, especially of a liquid, without movement or flow.
state of being still or inactive (liquid)

Examples

The stagnation of water in the ditch created a breeding ground for mosquitoes.
Pipes blocked by debris can lead to stagnation in the plumbing system.
During droughts, lakes can suffer from stagnation, resulting in poor water quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stagnancy - the state of being stagnant
  • Antonym: flow - continuous movement, especially of a liquid

Etymology

From Latin stagnatio (standing water, stagnation), from stagnum (standing water, pool).
