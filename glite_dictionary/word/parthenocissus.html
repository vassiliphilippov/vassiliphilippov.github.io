Parthenocissus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑrθənoʊˈsɪsəs / (AmE), / ˌpɑːθɪnəˈsɪsəs / (BrE)
a genus of woody climbing vines having tendrils with small, disklike adhesive tips, including species such as Virginia creeper and Boston ivy
genus of woody climbing vines

Examples

Parthenocissus is commonly used to cover the exterior walls of buildings for its attractive foliage.
Some species within the Parthenocissus genus have leaves that turn brilliant red in autumn.
Gardeners appreciate Parthenocissus for its ability to rapidly climb and provide dense cover.

Etymology

From Greek 'parthenos' (virgin) and 'kissos' (ivy), referring to the Virginia creeper species.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːrθənoʊˈsɪsəs / (AmE), / ˌpɑːθənəʊˈsɪsəs / (BrE)
a genus of woody vines with disklike tips on their tendrils, including species such as Virginia creeper and Boston ivy
genus of woody vines (Virginia creeper, Boston ivy)

Examples

The Parthenocissus genus includes the well-known Virginia creeper often seen climbing garden walls.
Gardeners value Parthenocissus for its vibrant autumn foliage and vigorous growth.
Boston ivy, a species of Parthenocissus, is commonly used to cover building facades.

Etymology

From Greek 'parthenos' (virgin) and 'kissos' (ivy).
