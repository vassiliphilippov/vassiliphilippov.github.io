Campanula

1. NOUN

/ kæmˈpæn.ju.lə / (AmE), / kæmˈpæn.jʊ.lə / (BrE)
a large genus of flowering plants, known as bellflowers, with bell-shaped blossoms often grown in gardens
genus of bellflowers

Examples

The Campanula in our garden bloom in a variety of purple and blue hues each summer.
She planted several Campanula species along the stone pathway.
The delicate Campanula blossoms attract bees and butterflies throughout the season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bellflower - common name for plants in the Campanula genus

Etymology

From Latin 'campanula', diminutive of 'campana' (bell), referring to the bell-shaped flowers.
