campanula

1. NOUN

/ kæmˈpæn.ju.lə / (AmE), / kamˈpan.jʊ.lə / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Campanula, typically with blue or white bell-shaped flowers
plant with bell-shaped blue or white flowers

Examples

We planted a row of campanula along the garden path for their delicate blue blooms.
The campanula in her bouquet added a lovely touch of color and elegance.
Many gardeners appreciate campanula for its long flowering season and easy care.

Etymology

From Late Latin 'campanula' (little bell), diminutive of Latin 'campana' (bell), referring to the shape of the flower.
