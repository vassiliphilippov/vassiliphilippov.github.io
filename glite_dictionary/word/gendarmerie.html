gendarmerie

1. NOUN

/ ʒɑnˈdɑrməri / (AmE), / ʒɒnˈdɑːməri / (BrE)
A police force, especially in France and other countries, organized along military lines and responsible for policing rural areas or acting as a national police.
military-style police force (esp. in France)

Examples

The French gendarmerie patrols rural areas as well as highways.
A division of the gendarmerie was called in to restore order after the protests.
Many countries modelled their own national police after the French gendarmerie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: police - the civil force of a state responsible for maintaining public order

Etymology

From French 'gendarmerie', from 'gendarme' (armed man) + '-erie' (collective noun suffix).
