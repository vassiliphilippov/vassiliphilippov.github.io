acroanesthesia

1. NOUN

/ ˌækroʊˌænəsˈθiːʒə / (AmE), / ˌakrəʊˌanɪsˈθiːzɪə / (BrE)
Loss of sensation in the extremities, such as the hands or feet, typically due to nerve impairment.
loss of sensation in extremities
medical

Examples

The patient reported acroanesthesia in his fingers and toes after exposure to extreme cold.
Long-term diabetes can sometimes lead to acroanesthesia as a result of nerve damage.
Doctors monitored the progression of acroanesthesia to assess the severity of the patient's neuropathy.

Etymology

From Greek 'akron' (extremity) + 'anesthesia' (loss of sensation).
