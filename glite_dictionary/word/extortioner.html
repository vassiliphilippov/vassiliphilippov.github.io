extortioner

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstɔrʃənər / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɔːʃənə / (BrE)
a person who obtains money or other benefits from someone by threatening to reveal embarrassing or damaging information about them or by other means of coercion
person who coerces money by threats

Examples

The extortioner sent several threatening letters demanding money in exchange for silence.
Police arrested the extortioner after he tried to blackmail a business executive.
Many victims are too afraid to report an extortioner for fear of further threats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackmailer - someone who demands payment by threatening to reveal secret information
  • Antonym: victim - a person harmed or exploited, especially by a crime

Etymology

From Latin extorquere (to wrench out) via Medieval Latin extortionem.
