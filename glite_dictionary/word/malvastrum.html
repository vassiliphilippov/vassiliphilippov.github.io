Malvastrum

1. NOUN

/ mælˈvæstrəm / (AmE), / malˈvastrəm / (BrE)
A genus of mallows with red and yellow flowers, sometimes classified in other genera.
genus of mallows with red and yellow flowers

Examples

Several species of Malvastrum are cultivated for their ornamental red and yellow flowers.
The botanist studied the leaf structure unique to the Malvastrum genus.
Some taxonomists debate whether Malvastrum should be merged with other closely related genera.

Etymology

From Latin 'malva' (mallow) and '-astrum' (a diminutive or pejorative suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmælvəˈstrʌm / (AmE), / ˌmalvəˈstrʌm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the mallow family, characterized by red and yellow flowers, often reclassified into other genera.
genus of flowering plants in the mallow family

Examples

The Malvastrum genus is notable for its brightly colored flowers.
Botanists often debate whether certain species belong in Malvastrum or another mallow genus.
Gardeners value Malvastrum for its resilience and ornamental appeal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mallow - A plant of the Malvaceae family, known for its mucilaginous properties

Etymology

From Latin, combining "malva" (mallow) and "astrum" (star).
