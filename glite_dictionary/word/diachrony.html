diachrony

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈɑːkrəni / (AmE), / daɪˈækrəni / (BrE)
The study of language change over time.
study of language change over time

Examples

The field of historical linguistics is devoted to the diachrony of languages.
A complete analysis of language must consider both diachrony and synchrony.
Experts debate how diachrony influences the grammatical structures we see today.

Synset Examples

the synchrony and diachrony of language

Related Terms

  • Antonym: synchrony - the study of language at a particular point in time

Etymology

From Greek 'dia' (through) + 'chronos' (time).
