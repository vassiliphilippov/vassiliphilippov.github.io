diabetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌdaɪəˈbɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəˈbiːtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or affected by diabetes, or causing diabetes.
relating to diabetes

Examples

The diabetic patient needs to monitor their blood sugar regularly.
A special diabetic diet was recommended by the nutritionist.
Some symptoms may indicate an underlying diabetic condition.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: non-diabetic - not related to or affected by diabetes

Etymology

From Greek diabētikos (relating to diabetes).

WordNet ID: oewn-03072376-a

2. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪəˈbɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəˈbɛtɪk / (BrE)
a person who has diabetes, a medical condition that affects blood sugar regulation
person with diabetes
medical

Examples

The hospital has a support group for diabetics to share advice and experiences.
As a diabetic, she monitors her blood sugar levels daily.
Many diabetics benefit from following a special diet.

Etymology

From Greek 'diabētēs' (diabetes) via Medieval Latin 'diabēticus'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10030631-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ daɪ.əˈbɛt̬.ɪk / (AmE), / daɪ.əˈbɛt.ɪk / (BrE)
Having, relating to, or suffering from diabetes.
having diabetes
medical

Examples

The diabetic patient must monitor her blood sugar levels regularly.
Some foods are specifically designed for diabetic individuals to help manage their condition.
A diabetic person should always carry emergency glucose in case of hypoglycemia.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nondiabetic - not having diabetes
  • Degree Higher: hyperglycemic - having high blood sugar, often relating to diabetes
  • Degree Lower: hypoglycemic - having low blood sugar, often relating to diabetes

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'diabēticus', from Greek 'diabētikos', from 'diabētēs' (diabetes).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02554704-s
Synset POS: s
