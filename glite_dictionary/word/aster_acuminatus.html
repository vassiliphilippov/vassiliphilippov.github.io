Aster acuminatus

1. NOUN

/ ˈæstər əˌkjuːməˈneɪtəs / (AmE), / ˈæstə əˌkjuːmɪˈneɪtəs / (BrE)
A North American perennial plant with whorled leaves and showy white, purple-tinged flowers.
a North American flowering plant species

Examples

The shady woodland was filled with blooming Aster acuminatus in early summer.
Gardeners admire Aster acuminatus for its delicate, purple-tinged blossoms and hardy nature.
During our hike we found a patch of Aster acuminatus growing near the creek.

Etymology

From Latin *Aster* (star) and *acuminatus* (pointed), referring to the shape of the plant's parts.
