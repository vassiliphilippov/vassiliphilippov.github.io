Pyrus communis

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪɹəs kəˈmjunɪs / (AmE), / ˈpaɪərəs kəˈmjuːnɪs / (BrE)
The common pear tree of Eurasia, widely cultivated for its sweet, gritty-textured, juicy fruit.
the common pear tree

Examples

The orchard was filled with mature Pyrus communis trees laden with ripe fruit.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Pyrus communis in different climates.
She planted a Pyrus communis in her backyard to enjoy fresh pears each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pear - the fruit of the pear tree

Etymology

From Latin: 'pyrus' (pear tree) + 'communis' (common).
