yellowtail

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊˌteɪl / (BrE)
a tropical Atlantic and Caribbean food fish with a broad yellow stripe along its sides and tail
a tropical fish with a yellow stripe and tail

Examples

Local fishermen are famous for catching yellowtail in these waters.
We ordered grilled yellowtail at the seaside restaurant.
The chef recommended the fresh yellowtail for tonight’s special.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snapper - a type of fish related to the yellowtail

2. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊˌteɪl / (BrE)
a game fish of southern California and Mexico, noted for its yellow tail fin
a fish with a yellow tail fin

Examples

Many anglers travel to Baja California hoping to catch a giant yellowtail.
The grilled yellowtail was the highlight of the seafood platter.
Schools of yellowtail can often be seen near rocky reefs during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amberjack - a large game fish, related species to yellowtail

Etymology

From 'yellow' + 'tail', referring to the species' distinctive tail color.
