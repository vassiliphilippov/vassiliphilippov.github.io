cut-through

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌtˌθru / (AmE), / ˈkʌtˌθruː / (BrE)
a route that provides a shortcut through an area, typically to avoid congestion or reduce travel time
a shortcut route through an area

Examples

There is a cut-through behind the shops that saves me ten minutes of walking.
Local residents oppose the new cut-through because it increases traffic in their neighborhood.
The taxi driver used a cut-through to get us to the airport on time.

Synset Examples

That path is a popular cut-through for commuters.
The new road is a great cut-through to avoid traffic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shortcut - a quicker route than the usual one
