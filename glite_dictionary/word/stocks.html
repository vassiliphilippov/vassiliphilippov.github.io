stocks

1. NOUN

/ stɑks / (AmE), / stɒks / (BrE)
a framework that supports a boat or ship while it is being built or repaired
frame supporting a boat during construction

Examples

The workers positioned the keel on the stocks before beginning the hull assembly.
The old stocks were worn from years of supporting fishing boats in the shipyard.
Before launching, the vessel rested safely on the stocks for several months.

2. NOUN

/ stɑks / (AmE), / stɒks / (BrE)
a strong wooden frame with movable barriers, used to restrain an animal, especially a horse, during veterinary procedures or while being shod
frame for restraining animals during care

Examples

The veterinarian secured the horse in the stocks before starting the examination.
The blacksmith used stocks to safely shoe the restless animal.
Without stocks, treating large animals would be dangerous for both the animal and the handler.

3. NOUN

/ stɑks / (AmE), / stɒks / (BrE)
a device consisting of a heavy wooden frame with holes for securing the feet (and sometimes the hands), formerly used to punish offenders by public confinement
wooden device for punishing offenders
archaic

Examples

In medieval times, criminals were often placed in the stocks in the town square for public humiliation.
Tourists can see a restored set of stocks in front of the old courthouse.
The villagers laughed and jeered as the thief sat helplessly in the stocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pillory - a device for punishment with holes for the head and hands

Etymology

Plural of 'stock' (wooden block, tree trunk), in this context referring to the wooden frame used for punishment.
