conceivableness

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsiː.vəb.əl.nəs / (AmE), / kənˈsiː.vəb.l̩.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being conceivable; the capacity to be imagined or understood
quality of being conceivable

Examples

Many people questioned the conceivableness of life on distant planets.
The conceivableness of such a theory depends on current technological advances.
He discussed the conceivableness of infinite space in his lecture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conceivability - the quality of being conceivable; synonym often used in modern contexts

Etymology

From conceivable + -ness, following standard English word formation.
