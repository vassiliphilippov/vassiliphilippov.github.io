acanthotic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæk.ænˈθɑ.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌæk.ænˈθɒ.tɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by acanthosis (thickening of the skin's epidermis)
relating to acanthosis (epidermal thickening)
medical

Examples

The biopsy revealed acanthotic changes consistent with chronic skin inflammation.
A dermatologist noted acanthotic alterations in the patient's epidermis.
The presence of acanthotic skin can indicate underlying endocrine disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acanthosis - abnormal thickening of the skin's epidermis

Etymology

From Greek akantha (thorn, spine) + -otic (relating to); based on acanthosis.
