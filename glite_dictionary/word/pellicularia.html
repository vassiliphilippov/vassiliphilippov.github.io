Pellicularia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛlɪkjəˈlɛriə / (AmE), / ˌpɛlɪkjʊˈlɑːrɪə / (BrE)
a genus of fungi characterized by having a crust-like hymenium; some species were previously classified under the form genus Rhizoctinia
a genus of crust-forming fungi
medical

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the genus Pellicularia in tropical soils.
The unique crust-like structures of Pellicularia distinguish it from other fungi.
Some plant pathologists have reclassified certain fungi from Rhizoctinia to Pellicularia.

Etymology

From Latin 'pellicula' meaning 'a little skin or film', referring to the crust-like form of the fungi.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɛlɪkjəˈlɛriə / (AmE), / ˌpɛlɪkjʊˈlɑːrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of fungi with the hymenium in the form of a crust, some species of which were formerly classified in the form genus Rhizoctinia.
a genus of crust-like fungi

Examples

The genus Pellicularia includes several species that form crust-like structures on decaying wood.
Researchers recently reclassified a number of fungal isolates under the genus Pellicularia.
Many members of Pellicularia are important in ecological decomposition processes.

Etymology

From Latin pellicula (small skin or film), referring to the crust-like appearance.
