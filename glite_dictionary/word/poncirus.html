Poncirus

1. NOUN

/ pɒnˈsaɪrəs / (AmE), / pɒnˈsɪərəs / (BrE)
A small genus of deciduous thorny shrubs or small trees in the family Rutaceae, chiefly known for the trifoliate orange (Poncirus trifoliata), commonly used as a rootstock for citrus species.
genus of thorny shrubs; trifoliate orange

Examples

The Poncirus genus is closely related to true citrus plants.
Horticulturists often use Poncirus as a rootstock for grafting oranges and lemons.
Poncirus trifoliata is valued for its cold hardiness and ornamental qualities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trifoliate orange - deciduous, thorny shrub or small tree of Poncirus genus

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek 'ponos' (labor, hardship) combined with 'Citrus.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɒn.sɪ.rəs / (AmE), / ˈpɒn.sɪ.rəs / (BrE)
A small genus of thorny Asian trees in the rue family, including the trifoliate orange.
genus of thorny trees; trifoliate orange

Examples

The Poncirus genus is best known for the trifoliate orange, valued as a hardy rootstock.
Some gardeners plant Poncirus trees as ornamental barriers due to their dense thorns.
Botanists sometimes debate whether Poncirus should be merged with the Citrus genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Citrus - a genus of flowering plants, including oranges, lemons, and related fruits

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, possibly an altered form of the Latin name for citron (Citrus medica poncirus).
