Sebastiana

1. NOUN

/ sɛˌbæstiˈænə / (AmE), / sɛˌbæstiˈɑːnə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical American plants in the spurge family, known as Mexican spurges, with small greenish flowers and often found in dry regions
genus of tropical American spurges

Examples

Sebastiana species are common in the dry forests of Mexico and Central America.
Researchers discovered a new medicinal compound in a plant of the Sebastiana genus.
The leaves of Sebastiana are used in some regions for traditional herbal remedies.

Etymology

Modern Latin, created as a genus name in botanical classification.

2. NOUN

/ sɛˌbæstiˈænə / (AmE), / sɛˌbæstiˈɑːnə / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the spurge family (Euphorbiaceae), native to Mexico and Central America, known as Mexican spurges.
genus of Mexican spurges

Examples

The botanist specialized in plants belonging to the genus Sebastiana.
Several species in the Sebastiana genus are found in arid Mexican regions.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Sebastiana species in traditional remedies.

Etymology

Named after a person named Sebastian or derived from Latin "Sebastianus".
