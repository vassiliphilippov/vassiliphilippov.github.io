Cortaderia selloana

1. NOUN

/ kɔɹ.təˈdɛɹ.i.ə sɛˈloʊ.ə.nə / (AmE), / kɔː.təˈdɛə.ri.ə sɛˈlɒə.nə / (BrE)
a tall perennial grass native to the Pampas of South America, with silvery feathery plumes, often grown as an ornamental plant
tall grass with silvery plumes; Pampas grass

Examples

The garden featured a striking display of Cortaderia selloana swaying in the breeze.
Many landscapers use Cortaderia selloana for its tall, showy plumes and dramatic effect.
Because Cortaderia selloana spreads quickly, it can become invasive in some regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pampas grass - common name for Cortaderia selloana, a tall grass with silvery plumes

Etymology

From Spanish cortadera (from cortar, to cut) + Latinized specific epithet selloana, after botanist Friedrich Sellow.
