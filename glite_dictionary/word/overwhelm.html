to overwhelm

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvɚˈwɛlm / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈwɛlm / (BrE)
to give someone too many tasks, responsibilities, or problems so that they feel unable to cope
give too many tasks or responsibilities

Examples

The team was overwhelmed by the number of new projects handed to them this month.
She feels overwhelmed at work because her boss keeps assigning her extra duties.
It's easy to overwhelm employees if you don't manage workloads carefully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overburden - to give someone more work or responsibilities than they can handle
  • Antonym: underwhelm - to fail to impress or excite someone

Etymology

From Old English *oferhwhelman* (to overturn, overthrow), from 'over' + 'whelm' (to submerge).

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈhwɛlm, ˌoʊvərˈwɛlm / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈwɛlm / (BrE)
to overpower or greatly affect someone emotionally or mentally, often to the point of being unable to cope
overpower emotionally or mentally

Examples

Grief can sometimes overwhelm us when we least expect it.
She was so overwhelmed by the kindness of strangers that she began to cry.
The sheer amount of work threatened to overwhelm him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overpower - to defeat or overcome by superior force
  • Synonym: to subdue - to bring under control
  • Antonym: to encourage - give support or confidence (opposite of discourage or overwhelm)

Etymology

From Middle English 'overwhelmen', from 'over-' + 'whelmen' (to cover up, submerge).

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈwɛlm / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈwɛlm / (BrE)
to defeat someone or something completely, especially by using greater strength or force
defeat or overcome completely by force

Examples

The army was overwhelmed by enemy forces and forced to surrender.
Floodwaters can quickly overwhelm a poorly constructed dam during heavy rains.
The sheer number of applications overwhelmed the admissions staff this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overpower - to defeat or overcome with superior strength
  • Synonym: to subdue - to bring under control, especially by force
  • Antonym: to succumb - to yield or give in to a force or influence

Etymology

From Middle English 'overwhelmen', from 'over-' + 'whelmen' (to overturn or engulf).

4. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈwɛlm / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈwɛlm / (BrE)
to cover or bury completely, making something impossible to see or perceive
cover or make imperceptible

Examples

The rising tide began to overwhelm the small boat, making it vanish from sight.
The city was overwhelmed by a thick blanket of fog, hiding the skyline.
Her words were overwhelmed by the roar of applause from the audience.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to expose - to leave something uncovered or visible

5. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈwɛlm / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈwɛlm / (BrE)
to affect someone very strongly, so that they feel unable to deal with it; to make someone feel intensely stressed or burdened
to affect deeply; make unable to cope

Examples

She felt overwhelmed by the number of tasks she had to complete.
The news was so shocking that it completely overwhelmed him.
During the busy season at work, many employees feel overwhelmed by their responsibilities.

Synset Examples

Life can be overwhelming at times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overpower - to defeat or subdue with superior force
  • Synonym: crush - to put great pressure on; to subdue or destroy completely
  • Antonym: to relieve - to make less severe or burdensome

Etymology

From Middle English 'overwhelmen' (to overturn), from 'over-' + 'whelmen' (to overturn or overwhelm).
