nonintersecting

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑnˌɪntərˈsɛktɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌɪntəˈsɛktɪŋ / (BrE)
(Of lines, planes, or surfaces) never meeting or crossing at any point.
never meeting or crossing (geometry)

Examples

The two roads are nonintersecting, so they run parallel to each other indefinitely.
In geometry, nonintersecting circles never touch or cross at any point.
The architect designed nonintersecting beams to support the open structure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intersecting - (Of lines, planes, or surfaces) meeting or crossing at a point

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' meaning 'not' and 'intersect', from Latin 'intersecare' (to cut between).
