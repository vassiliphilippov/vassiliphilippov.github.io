deafness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛfnəs / (AmE), / ˈdɛfnɪs / (BrE)
the partial or complete inability to hear
loss or lack of hearing
medical

Examples

Her deafness developed gradually, making conversation increasingly difficult over the years.
Many people adapt remarkably well to deafness by learning sign language and reading lips.
After the accident, his sudden deafness was a challenging adjustment for the whole family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hearing loss - partial or total inability to hear
  • Antonym: hearing - the ability to perceive sound

Etymology

From Old English 'dēafness', from 'dēaf' (deaf) + '-ness'.
