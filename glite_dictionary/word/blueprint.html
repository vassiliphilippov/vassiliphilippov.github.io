blueprint

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluˌprɪnt / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌprɪnt / (BrE)
a photographic print of a technical plan or drawing, typically with white lines on a blue background, used especially in engineering and architecture
photographic copy of technical plans

Examples

The architect unrolled the blueprint to show the proposed layout of the building.
Before construction began, the engineers carefully reviewed every blueprint to ensure accuracy.
Old blueprints of the bridge were discovered in the city archives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plan - detailed proposal for doing or achieving something

Etymology

From the process by which technical drawings were reproduced as white lines on blue paper (invented in the 19th century).

2. VERB

/ ˈbluˌprɪnt / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌprɪnt / (BrE)
to make a detailed plan or design of something, especially as a blueprint or technical drawing
make a detailed plan or technical drawing

Examples

The architect will blueprint the new campus before construction begins.
City planners blueprinted the entire road network for the new suburb.
The engineering team is blueprinting a more efficient cooling system for the facility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plan - to decide or arrange the details of something in advance
  • Synonym: sketch - to make a quick, rough drawing of something

Etymology

From 'blueprint', the noun, referring originally to blue-coloured technical drawings.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01687477-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈbluːˌprɪnt / (AmE), / ˈbluː.prɪnt / (BrE)
a detailed plan or model that serves as a guide for making or achieving something
detailed plan or guide

Examples

The architect showed us the blueprint for the new school building.
Her business proposal provided a blueprint for expanding into international markets.
They used his notes as a blueprint to develop the innovative product.

Synset Examples

a blueprint for a house
a pattern for a skirt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plan - a detailed proposal for achieving or making something
  • Synonym: template - a model or pattern used as a guide for something to be made

Etymology

Originally refers to a reproduction of a technical drawing made using a contact print process on light-sensitive paper, producing white lines on a blue background.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05910921-n
Synset POS: n
