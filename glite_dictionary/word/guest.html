guest

1. NOUN

/ ɡɛst / (AmE), / ɡɛst / (BrE)
a person who is invited to visit or stay in someone else's home or attend an event, and to whom hospitality is shown
person invited and shown hospitality

Examples

We invited several guests to our anniversary party.
As a hotel guest, you are entitled to complimentary breakfast.
The guest arrived with a bottle of wine as a thank you for the invitation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: host - person who receives or entertains guests
  • Synonym: visitor - someone who visits a person or place

Etymology

From Old Norse 'gestr'; related to Old English 'gest' (visitor, stranger).

2. NOUN

/ ɡɛst / (AmE), / ɡɛst / (BrE)
(computing) A computer that is connected to a network, especially one that is not an integral part of the system or is given temporary access.
computer connected to a network (computing)

Examples

The network administrator assigned the guest computer a temporary IP address.
If your laptop is connected as a guest, you may have limited access to shared resources.
The new security policy restricts what a guest can do on the company network.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: host - main computer that provides services to others on a network

3. NOUN

/ ɡɛst / (AmE), / ɡɛst / (BrE)
a person who is staying at a hotel, eating at a restaurant, or otherwise receiving hospitality as a customer or visitor
customer at a hotel or restaurant

Examples

The hotel welcomed every guest with a complimentary drink at check-in.
As a guest at the restaurant, she was treated to excellent service.
The guest checked out early after enjoying his stay.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: host - person who receives or entertains guests

Etymology

From Old Norse 'gestr', related to Old English 'gæst' (guest, stranger).
