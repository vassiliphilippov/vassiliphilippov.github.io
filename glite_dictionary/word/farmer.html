farmer

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑrmər / (AmE), / ˈfɑːmə / (BrE)
a person who owns or manages a farm, especially as a principal occupation
person who operates a farm

Examples

My grandfather was a farmer who raised cattle and grew corn on his land.
Many farmers wake up before dawn to tend to their crops and animals.
The farmer decided to invest in new equipment to improve his harvest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agriculturist - a person who studies or practices agriculture
  • Synonym: rancher - a person who owns or manages a ranch

Etymology

From Middle English fermour, from Anglo-Norman fermer, from Old French fermer (to lease, farm), from Medieval Latin firma (fixed payment), referring originally to tax or rent collectors who 'farmed' the land for income.
