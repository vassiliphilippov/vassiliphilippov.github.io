goldmine

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldˌmaɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldˌmaɪn / (BrE)
A mine from which gold ore is extracted.
mine where gold is found

Examples

The discovery of a new goldmine sparked a rush of hopeful prospectors.
This region is famous for its historic goldmine that attracted thousands in the 19th century.
After months of searching, they finally found a productive goldmine deep in the mountains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mine - an excavation from which minerals are extracted

Etymology

From 'gold' + 'mine', denoting a place where gold is mined.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊldˌmaɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldˌmaɪn / (BrE)
A place, source, or thing that yields a plentiful supply of something valuable or desirable.
source of great value or abundance

Examples

The library was a goldmine of information for her research project.
When the website went viral, it turned into a goldmine for its creators.
His grandfather's attic proved to be a goldmine of old photographs and letters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treasure trove - a collection or source of valuable things
  • Synonym: bonanza - a situation or event that creates a sudden increase in wealth or good fortune

Etymology

From 'gold' + 'mine', originally referring to a mine where gold is extracted; later used figuratively.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03450150-n
Synset POS: n
