tribalisation

1. NOUN

/ traɪbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / traɪbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something tribal in character or uniting people on the basis of tribal affiliations
the process of making tribal or uniting on tribal basis
british

Examples

The tribalisation of politics can lead to deep societal divisions.
Many scholars study the tribalisation of urban communities after civil unrest.
Efforts at tribalisation often undermine national unity and promote factionalism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tribalism - strong loyalty to one's tribe, emphasizing group identity

Etymology

From 'tribal' + '-isation', influenced by British English formation pattern.
