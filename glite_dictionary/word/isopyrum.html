Isopyrum

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsəˈpaɪrəm / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈpɪərəm / (BrE)
A genus of tufted perennial herbs native to the Northern Hemisphere, belonging to the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae).
a genus of perennial herbs (Ranunculaceae)

Examples

Isopyrum can often be found in moist woodlands across temperate regions.
Botanists distinguish Isopyrum by its delicate, white or yellow flowers.
The leaves of Isopyrum resemble those of its relatives in the buttercup family.

Etymology

From Greek iso- (equal) + pyrum, from Latin 'pyrus' (pear), referring to the shape of some parts of the plant.
