clambake

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæmˌbeɪk / (AmE), / ˈklæmˌbeɪk / (BrE)
a party or cookout at the beach where clams, fish, and other foods are traditionally cooked on heated stones covered with seaweed
beach cookout where clams are cooked
american

Examples

We're planning a clambake on the beach this weekend with friends.
Everyone brought something to share at the annual family clambake.
The aroma of seafood and seaweed filled the air during the traditional clambake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barbecue - an outdoor meal where food is cooked over an open fire or grill

Etymology

From 'clam' (the mollusk) + 'bake' (to cook by dry heat). Popular in New England, USA.
