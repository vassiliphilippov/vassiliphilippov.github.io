finocchio

1. NOUN

/ fɪˈnoʊkiˌoʊ / (AmE), / fɪˈnɒkiːəʊ / (BrE)
a cultivated variety of fennel with a thick, bulbous, aromatic stem base, eaten cooked or raw in salads and other dishes
an edible fennel bulb used in cooking

Examples

The salad was garnished with thin slices of finocchio for a refreshing crunch.
You can roast finocchio to bring out its natural sweetness and aroma.
Many Italian recipes call for finocchio as a key ingredient, especially in winter dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fennel - a plant with feathery leaves and yellow flowers, used as a herb or vegetable

Etymology

From Italian 'finocchio' meaning 'fennel', ultimately from Latin 'feniculum'.
