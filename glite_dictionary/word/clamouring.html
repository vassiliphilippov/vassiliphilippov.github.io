clamouring

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæm.ɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklæm.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
A loud and persistent outcry or demand from many people, often in protest or excitement.
loud public outcry or demand
british

Examples

The clamouring of the protesters could be heard outside the gates all night.
Amidst the clamouring for change, the government was forced to respond.
The judge struggled to speak over the clamouring in the packed courtroom.

Synset Examples

he ignored the clamor of the crowd

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clamor - loud and persistent outcry or demand (American spelling)
  • Synonym: uproar - a state of noisy confusion and excitement

Etymology

From Latin 'clamor' (shout, cry), via Old French 'clamour'.
