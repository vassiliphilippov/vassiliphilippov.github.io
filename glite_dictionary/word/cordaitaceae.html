Cordaitaceae

1. NOUN

/ kɔrˌdaɪəˈtæsi.i / (AmE), / kɔːˌdaɪəˈtæsiː.iː / (BrE)
an extinct family of chiefly Paleozoic gymnospermous plants, with Cordaites as the chief genus
extinct family of Paleozoic gymnosperm plants

Examples

Fossils of Cordaitaceae provide important clues about prehistoric plant evolution.
Plants in the Cordaitaceae family were widespread during the Carboniferous period.
The extinction of Cordaitaceae marked a turning point in gymnosperm history.

Etymology

From the genus name Cordaites, which honors botanist Augustin Pyrame de Candolle, with the standard botanical family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ kɔrˌdaɪəˈteɪsiˌi / (AmE), / kɔːˌdaɪəˈtiːsiːiː / (BrE)
an extinct family of chiefly Paleozoic plants, with Cordaites as the chief and typical genus
extinct family of Paleozoic plants

Examples

The fossil record for Cordaitaceae helps paleobotanists reconstruct ancient forest ecosystems.
Most species within Cordaitaceae are known only from Paleozoic strata.
Researchers discovered new reproductive structures belonging to the extinct Cordaitaceae family.

Etymology

From New Latin Cordaitaceae, named after the genus Cordaites, itself named for Czech botanist August Carl Joseph Corda.
