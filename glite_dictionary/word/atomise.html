to atomise

1. VERB

/ ˈæ.təˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈat.ə.maɪz / (BrE)
to break up or reduce something into very small particles or fine droplets, often using a spray or other mechanical process
break up into small particles

Examples

Modern spray bottles atomise liquids to create a fine mist.
When fuel is atomised before combustion, it burns more efficiently.
The perfume was atomised into the air, filling the room with a pleasant scent.

Synset Examples

the fine powder had been atomized by air

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disperse - to scatter or spread over a wide area
  • Antonym: coagulate - to change from a fluid into a thickened mass

Etymology

From atom + -ise, modeled after French atomiser, based on 'atom'.

2. VERB

/ ˈætəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈætəmaɪz / (BrE)
to destroy something completely by using bombs or other powerful weapons, reducing it to small particles or fragments
destroy with bombs or great force

Examples

The airstrike atomised the entire warehouse, leaving nothing behind.
If the order is given, the military can atomise the enemy position within seconds.
The old building was atomised by a controlled demolition, sending dust and debris into the sky.

Synset Examples

zap the enemy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to annihilate - destroy utterly; obliterate
  • Synonym: to vaporize - destroy instantly by turning into vapor, e.g., via explosion

Etymology

From atom + -ise, meaning to reduce to atoms or small particles.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01135714-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈæ.tə.maɪz / (AmE), / ˈat.ə.maɪz / (BrE)
to convert a substance into very fine particles or spray; to spray very finely
to spray very finely or into tiny particles
british

Examples

The device is designed to atomise water into a fine mist for humidifying the air.
She atomised her favourite perfume onto her wrists before leaving the house.
Modern inhalers work by atomising the medicine so it can be easily breathed in.

Synset Examples

atomize perfume

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to atomize - to spray very finely (American spelling)

Etymology

From atom + -ise (from Greek atomos ‘indivisible’ and English suffix -ise).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
