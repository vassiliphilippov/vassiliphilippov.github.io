manor

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænɚ / (AmE), / ˈmanə / (BrE)
A large country house with lands, especially one that was historically the home of a lord of the manor.
large estate and house of a lord

Examples

They restored the old manor to its former grandeur.
The village grew up around the manor in medieval times.
Each autumn, a festival is held on the grounds of the manor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mansion - a large, impressive house
  • Synonym: estate - an extensive area of land in the country, usually with a large house

Etymology

From Old French 'manoir', from Latin 'manēre' (to remain, dwell).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmænər / (AmE), / ˈmanə / (BrE)
A large house or mansion, especially one owned by a lord or wealthy person in the countryside.
large country house of a lord or wealthy person

Examples

The wealthy family owned a grand manor in the English countryside.
The old manor is now a museum open to the public.
They spent the weekend at a historic manor surrounded by gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mansion - a large, impressive house
  • Antonym: cottage - a small, simple house, usually in the countryside

Etymology

From Old French 'manoir' (dwelling), from Latin 'manere' (to remain, dwell).
