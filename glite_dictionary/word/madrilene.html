madrilene

1. NOUN

/ ˌmædrəˈlin / (AmE), / ˌmædrɪˈliːn / (BrE)
A clear, tomato-flavored consommé, often served chilled as a soup appetizer.
chilled tomato-flavored consommé

Examples

The chef served a refreshing madrilene as the first course on a hot summer day.
A bowl of chilled madrilene is perfect for a light lunch.
She learned how to make madrilene during her culinary studies in Paris.

Etymology

From French 'madrilène', meaning 'from Madrid'.
