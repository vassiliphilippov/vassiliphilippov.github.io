Juncus inflexus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋ.kəs ɪnˈflɛk.səs / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋ.kəs ɪnˈflɛk.səs / (BrE)
A species of tall, slender rush native to temperate regions, often found in wetlands and marshes.
a tall rush plant of temperate regions

Examples

The pond's edges were lined with Juncus inflexus, swaying gently in the breeze.
Ecologists identified Juncus inflexus as a key species in the wetland restoration project.
Many gardeners appreciate Juncus inflexus for its graceful, upright stems and ability to thrive in damp soils.

Etymology

From Latin: genus Juncus (rush) and specific epithet inflexus (bent inward).
