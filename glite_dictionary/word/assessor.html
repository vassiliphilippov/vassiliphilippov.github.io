assessor

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɛsər / (AmE), / əˈsɛsə / (BrE)
an official whose job is to evaluate the value of property, especially for the purposes of taxation
official who values property for taxes

Examples

The county assessor determined the new value of our home for property tax purposes.
As an assessor, she is responsible for ensuring fair taxation across the district.
Residents can appeal the decision of the assessor if they believe their property has been overvalued.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appraiser - a person who estimates the value of something, especially property

Etymology

From Medieval Latin assessōr (one who sits beside, assistant), from Latin assidēre (to sit beside).
