Perisoreus canadensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrɪˈsɔːriəs ˌkænəˈdɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˈsɔːrɪəs ˌkænəˈdɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a species of jay native to northern North America, known for its black-capped head, lack of a crest, and noted boldness in pilfering food
North American jay species, also known as Canada jay

Examples

The Perisoreus canadensis, or Canada jay, is often spotted in northern forests sneaking food from campsites.
Researchers have documented the Perisoreus canadensis demonstrating remarkable memory skills when hiding food.
Because of its fearless nature, the Perisoreus canadensis is a favorite subject for wildlife photographers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Canada jay - common name for Perisoreus canadensis

Etymology

From Latin; Perisoreus, the genus name, and canadensis meaning 'of Canada'.
