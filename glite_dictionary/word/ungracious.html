ungracious

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɡreɪʃəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡreɪʃəs / (BrE)
not polite, kind, or showing good manners; lacking social graces or courtesy.
not polite or courteous

Examples

It was ungracious of him not to thank his hosts for dinner.
Her ungracious remarks made everyone uncomfortable at the meeting.
Despite winning, his ungracious attitude disappointed the audience.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gracious - courteous, kind, and pleasant
  • Synonym: rude - offensively impolite or ill-mannered

Etymology

From un- (not) + gracious, from Latin 'gratia' (favor, kindness).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɡreɪʃəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡreɪʃəs / (BrE)
lacking charm, good taste, or courtesy; not gracious
lacking charm or courtesy

Examples

She thought it would be ungracious to decline their invitation without a good reason.
He made an ungracious remark about the city's appearance, offending the locals.
The host's ungracious behavior left a negative impression on the guests.

Synset Examples

an ungracious industrial city
this curt summary is not meant to be ungracious
ungracious behavior

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gracious - showing kindness and elegance; courteous and pleasant
  • Synonym: rude - impolite; lacking respect or manners

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (meaning 'not') + 'gracious'.
