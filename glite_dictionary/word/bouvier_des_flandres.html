Bouvier des Flandres

1. NOUN

/ buːˈvjeɪ deɪ ˈflændrəz / (AmE), / buːˈvjeɪ deɪ ˈflɑːndrəs / (BrE)
a large, rough-coated breed of dog originating in Belgium, traditionally used for herding and guarding cattle
Belgian herding and guard dog breed

Examples

The Bouvier des Flandres is known for its intelligence and strong work ethic.
Many farmers still use the Bouvier des Flandres to help manage livestock.
Her family adopted a Bouvier des Flandres because of its gentle temperament and loyalty.

Etymology

From French, meaning 'cowherd of Flanders'.

2. NOUN

/ buːˈvjeɪ deɪ ˈflɑːndrəz / (AmE), / buːˈvjeɪ deɪ ˈflɒndrəz / (BrE)
a large, rough-coated breed of dog originally from Belgium, developed for herding and guarding cattle
a Belgian cattle-herding dog breed

Examples

The Bouvier des Flandres is known for its intelligence and hardworking nature.
She trains her Bouvier des Flandres to compete in agility events every year.
Farmers in Belgium once relied on the Bouvier des Flandres to guard their livestock.

Related Terms
