philhellenic

1. ADJ

/ ˌfɪl.hɛˈlɛn.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌfɪl.hɛˈlɛn.ɪk / (BrE)
Having or showing a strong admiration or love for Greece, its culture, or its people.
admiring Greek culture or Greece

Examples

His philhellenic interests led him to study ancient Greek literature and philosophy.
During the 19th century, philhellenic societies were established across Europe to support Greek independence.
She decorated her home in a distinctly philhellenic style, with statues and columns evoking ancient Greece.

Synset Examples

the Philhellenic Society

Related Terms

  • Synonym: philhellenism - the admiration or love of Greek culture, people, or history

Etymology

From Greek philo- (loving) + Hellenic (relating to Greece).
