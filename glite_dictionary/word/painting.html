painting

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪn.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpeɪn.tɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity or art of creating pictures using paint
the art of creating pictures with paint

Examples

She studied painting at the Academy of Fine Arts for four years.
He found relaxation and inspiration in painting landscapes on weekends.
The course covers both basic drawing and painting techniques.

Synset Examples

he studied painting and sculpture for many years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drawing - the art of creating pictures with pen, pencil, or similar tools instead of paint
  • Synonym: sculpture - the art of shaping materials such as stone or wood

Etymology

From Old French *peint* (painted) and -ing (verbal noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpeɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
A picture or artistic composition created by applying paints to a surface such as canvas, paper, or a wall.
a work of art made with paint

Examples

The museum is famous for its impressive collection of paintings by European masters.
She spent the afternoon working on a landscape painting in her studio.
They admired the colorful painting hanging above the fireplace.

Synset Examples

a small painting by Picasso
he bought the painting as an investment
his pictures hang in the Louvre

Related Terms

  • Synonym: picture - a painting, drawing, or photograph; a visual representation
  • Synonym: drawing - a picture made with pen, pencil, or another writing instrument

Etymology

From Old French *peint*, past participle of *peindre* (to paint), from Latin *pingere* (to paint).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03882197-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpeɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
the occupation or trade of applying paint to buildings or other surfaces, especially as a job
occupation of a house or building painter

Examples

He found steady work in painting after moving to the city.
She started a business specializing in exterior painting of homes and offices.
Many people hire professionals for painting because it requires skill and careful preparation.

Synset Examples

house painting was the only craft he knew

Related Terms

  • Synonym: house painting - the activity or occupation of painting the interior or exterior of houses

Etymology

From Middle English painten (to paint) + -ing (forming nouns denoting action or process).

4. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpeɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of applying paint to a surface, especially as a process or task
the act of applying paint

Examples

The painting of the kitchen took the whole afternoon.
She hired a professional to handle the painting of the office walls.
After the repairs were finished, he began the painting of the fence.

Synset Examples

you can finish the job of painting faster with a roller than with a brush

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decorating - the activity of making something look more attractive by putting things on it or painting

Etymology

From Latin pingere (to paint), via Old French peintier and Middle English peinten.
