Gnaphalium

1. NOUN

/ næˈfeɪliəm / (AmE), / nəˈfaliəm / (BrE)
A genus of coarse, hairy herbs in the family Asteraceae, known for whitish flower heads, widely distributed and often called cudweed.
genus of coarse, hairy herbs (cudweed)

Examples

Several species in the Gnaphalium genus are commonly known as cudweed in North America.
Botanists have studied Gnaphalium for its medicinal properties and distinctive woolly foliage.
You can often find Gnaphalium growing in dry, open fields and along roadsides.

Etymology

From Greek *gnaphalion*, diminutive of *gnaphalon* (lock of wool), referring to the woolly texture of the plants.

2. NOUN

/ nəˈfeɪliəm / (AmE), / nəˈfælɪəm / (BrE)
a genus of coarse, hairy herbs with whitish floral bracts, widely distributed in temperate regions; commonly known as cudweeds
plant genus of coarse hairy herbs (cudweeds)

Examples

Several species within Gnaphalium are commonly called cudweeds and are found in temperate climates.
The leaves of Gnaphalium are often woolly and greyish-green in color.
Some herbal remedies use extracts from the Gnaphalium genus for their purported medicinal benefits.

Etymology

From Greek 'gnaphalon' (a lock of wool), referring to the woolly appearance of the plants.
