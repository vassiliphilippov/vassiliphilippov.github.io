ribonucleinase

1. NOUN

/ ˌraɪboʊnjuːˈkliːneɪs / (AmE), / ˌraɪbəʊnjuːˈkliːneɪz / (BrE)
An enzyme that catalyzes the hydrolysis of ribonucleic acid (RNA).
enzyme catalyzing RNA hydrolysis
medical

Examples

The activity of ribonucleinase is crucial for the breakdown of RNA in cells.
Researchers measured the levels of ribonucleinase during the experiment.
A deficiency in ribonucleinase can disrupt normal RNA metabolism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ribonuclease - enzyme that catalyzes the degradation of RNA

Etymology

From 'ribonucleic' (as in ribonucleic acid) + '-nase', a suffix used for enzymes.
