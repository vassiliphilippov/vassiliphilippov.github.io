computerization

1. NOUN

/ kəmˌpjuːtəɹəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / kəmˌpjuːtəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of controlling or operating something by means of computers
the use of computers to control processes

Examples

The computerization of banking has transformed the way we manage money.
Rapid computerization has led to greater efficiency in manufacturing plants.
The government invested heavily in the computerization of public records.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: automation - the use of technology to control and monitor the production and delivery of various goods and services

Etymology

From 'computer' + '-ization', modeled after 'computerize'.
