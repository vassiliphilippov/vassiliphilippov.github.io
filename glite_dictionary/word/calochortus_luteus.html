Calochortus luteus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæloʊˈkɔrtəs ˈluːtiəs / (AmE), / ˌkaləˈkɔːtəs ˈluːtɪəs / (BrE)
a species of mariposa lily with clusters of large, deep yellow, bell-shaped flowers found in California coastal ranges
a yellow-flowered mariposa lily of California

Examples

The Calochortus luteus blooms in vibrant yellow across the California grasslands each spring.
Botanists studied the habitat preferences of Calochortus luteus in various coastal regions.
Gardeners often cultivate Calochortus luteus for its striking bell-shaped flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mariposa lily - a group of western North American lilies in the genus Calochortus

Etymology

From Greek kalos (beautiful) + chortos (grass); species name from Latin luteus (yellow).
