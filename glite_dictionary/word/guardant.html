guardant

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡɑrdənt / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːdənt / (BrE)
(heraldry) depicted as having the head turned to face the viewer, especially of an animal shown in profile with the head facing forward.
(heraldry) facing forward; head toward viewer

Examples

On the family crest, a lion guardant stands out, staring straight ahead.
The heraldic term 'leopard guardant' describes a walking lion whose head faces the viewer.
The shield features three wolves guardant, each gazing forward with watchful eyes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: regardant - (heraldry) with head turned backward; looking back

Etymology

From French *gardant*, present participle of *garder* (to guard, to look at).
