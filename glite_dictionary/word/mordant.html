mordant

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɔrdnt / (AmE), / ˈmɔːdnt / (BrE)
(of a substance, especially a strong acid) capable of destroying or eating away by chemical action.
able to eat away by chemical action

Examples

The mordant acid corroded the metal surface within hours.
Always wear protective gloves when handling mordant chemicals in the laboratory.
The statue was damaged by years of exposure to mordant substances in acid rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrosive - having the quality of eating away or destroying by chemical action

Etymology

From Latin 'mordere' (to bite), via French 'mordant'.

expressing harsh criticism, especially in a sharply ironic or biting way
sarcastically critical or biting

Examples

She wrote a mordant review of the restaurant, pulling no punches about the service.
His mordant comments often left his colleagues feeling uncomfortable.

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrdənt / (AmE), / ˈmɔːdənt / (BrE)
a substance used to treat materials such as textiles or leather before dyeing, to help fix the dye or make it more intense
substance aiding dyeing process

Examples

Alum is a common mordant used in natural dyeing to help colors set on fabric.
A mordant must be applied to the wool before immersing it in the dye bath.
They experimented with different mordants to achieve brighter and longer-lasting colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fixative - a substance that fixes colors in dyeing or photography

Etymology

From French 'mordant', from Latin 'mordere' (to bite), referring to its function of fixing dyes by 'biting' into the material.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
