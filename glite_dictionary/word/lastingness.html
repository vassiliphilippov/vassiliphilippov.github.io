lastingness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæstɪŋnəs / (AmE), / ˈlɑːstɪŋnəs / (BrE)
the quality of lasting for a long time or resisting wear, stress, or force
quality of lasting or endurance

Examples

The lastingness of the ancient monument amazed archaeologists.
She admired the lastingness of their friendship throughout the years.
The company's products are known for their lastingness and high quality.

Synset Examples

they advertised the durability of their products

Related Terms

  • Synonym: durability - the quality of being durable; ability to withstand wear or pressure
  • Synonym: permanence - the state or quality of lasting or remaining unchanged
  • Antonym: impermanence - the quality of not lasting forever; transience

Etymology

From 'lasting' + '-ness', where 'lasting' comes from Old English 'lǣstan' (to last, continue), and '-ness' forms nouns indicating a state or quality.
