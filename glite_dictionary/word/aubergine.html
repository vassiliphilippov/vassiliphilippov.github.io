aubergine

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊbərˌʒiːn / (AmE), / ˈəʊbəʒiːn / (BrE)
A hairy, upright plant native to Southeast Asia, widely cultivated for its large, glossy, edible fruit, which is commonly used as a vegetable.
plant and its edible fruit (eggplant)
british

Examples

She roasted slices of aubergine with olive oil and herbs for dinner.
The recipe calls for a large aubergine to be cut into cubes and fried.
In the UK, people often refer to eggplant as aubergine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eggplant - the fruit of the same plant; common in American English

Etymology

From French 'aubergine', from Catalan 'albergínia', from Arabic 'al-bāḏinjān'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊbərˌʒin / (AmE), / ˈəʊbəʒiːn / (BrE)
An egg-shaped vegetable with smooth, typically dark purple skin, though sometimes white or yellow, also known as eggplant.
egg-shaped purple vegetable; eggplant
british

Examples

She cooked a delicious curry with chopped aubergine and spices.
Grilled aubergine is a popular Mediterranean dish.
The recipe calls for two medium aubergines, sliced thinly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eggplant - an egg-shaped vegetable, especially in American English

Etymology

From French *aubergine*, originally from Arabic *al-bāḏinjān*.
