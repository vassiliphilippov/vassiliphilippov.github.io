Parochetus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæ.roʊˈkiː.təs / (AmE), / ˌpæ.rəˈkiː.təs / (BrE)
A small genus of flowering plants in the pea family, Fabaceae, consisting of a single species, the shamrock pea (Parochetus communis), native to the Himalayas and parts of Africa.
genus of plants, shamrock pea

Examples

The flower of the Parochetus is a striking blue, making it a popular ornamental plant.
Botanists discovered a thriving patch of Parochetus in the Himalayan foothills.
The leaves of Parochetus resemble those of a shamrock, hence its common name.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Greek 'parokhetos' meaning bordered or trimmed.
