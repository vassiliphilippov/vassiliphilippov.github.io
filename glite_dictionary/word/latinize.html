to Latinize

1. VERB

/ ˈlæt.əˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈlæt.ɪ.naɪz / (BrE)
to translate (a word, phrase, or text) into Latin
translate into Latin

Examples

The scholars decided to Latinize the manuscript for use in the Vatican.
He struggled to Latinize Greek scientific terms during the translation process.
Many medieval texts were Latinized to reach a broader scholarly audience.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Anglicize - to make English in form or character
  • Synonym: romanize - to write a language in the Roman alphabet; sometimes a synonym for Latinize

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'latinizare', from Latin 'Latinus' (Latin) + '-ize'.

2. VERB

/ ˈlæt.əˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈlæt.ɪˌnaɪz / (BrE)
to write or represent (a word, text, or language) using the Latin alphabet instead of another script
write in the Latin alphabet

Examples

The Japanese word was Latinized so that English speakers could read it.
Historians often Latinize names from ancient Greek manuscripts for easier study.
It is common to Latinize foreign brand names for international markets.

Synset Examples

many shops in Japan now carry neon signs with Romanized names

Related Terms

  • Synonym: romanize - to write using the Roman (Latin) alphabet

Etymology

From Latin 'latinus' (Latin) + English '-ize'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
