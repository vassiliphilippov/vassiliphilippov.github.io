Cruciferae

1. NOUN

/ kruːˈsɪfɚˌi / (AmE), / kruːˈsɪfəˌriː / (BrE)
a large family of flowering plants, including mustards, cabbages, broccoli, turnips, and cresses, known for their four-petaled flowers
plant family including mustards and cabbages

Examples

Broccoli, kale, and cabbage all belong to the Cruciferae family of plants.
Many vegetables in the Cruciferae are rich in vitamins and fiber.
Scientists are studying compounds found in Cruciferae for their health benefits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Brassicaceae - the more modern botanical name for the Cruciferae family

Etymology

From Latin *crucifer* ('cross-bearing'), referring to the cross-shaped arrangement of the flower petals.

2. NOUN

/ kruːˈsɪfəˌri / (AmE), / kruːˈsɪfəriː / (BrE)
a large family of plants with four-petaled flowers, including mustards, cabbages, broccoli, turnips, and cresses
plant family including mustards and cabbages

Examples

Many vegetables we eat, such as cabbage and broccoli, belong to the Cruciferae family.
The flowers of the Cruciferae are notable for their cross-shaped petals.
Scientists study Cruciferae to learn more about plant evolution and disease resistance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Brassicaceae - alternative family name for Cruciferae

Etymology

From Latin 'crucifer' (cross-bearing), referring to the cross-shaped arrangement of the flower's petals.
