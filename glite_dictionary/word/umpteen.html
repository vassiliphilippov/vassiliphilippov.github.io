umpteen

1. ADJ

/ ˈʌmpˌtin / (AmE), / ˈʌmptiːn / (BrE)
very many; a large but unspecified number, especially when the exact figure is not important
very many, a large indefinite number
informal

Examples

I've told you umpteen times to close the door behind you!
They came up with umpteen excuses for being late to the meeting.
After umpteen attempts, she finally solved the puzzle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: countless - too many to be counted
  • Synonym: numerous - existing in large number
  • Antonym: few - a small number of things

Etymology

Formed in early 20th century, modelled on 'teen' numbers (thirteen, fourteen, etc.) and playful alteration of 'umpty' (dash in Morse code slang for an indefinite number).
