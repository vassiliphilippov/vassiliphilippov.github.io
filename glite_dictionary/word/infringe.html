to infringe

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈfrɪndʒ / (AmE), / ɪnˈfrɪndʒ / (BrE)
to break or act contrary to a law, rule, or agreement; to violate or encroach upon something, especially rights or regulations
break or violate a law or rule

Examples

Any use of the company's trademark without permission would infringe on their intellectual property rights.
The new law protects workers' rights against employers who might infringe them.
You must not infringe upon other people's privacy, even if you disagree with their choices.

Synset Examples

He ran afoul of the law
This behavior conflicts with our rules

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to violate - to break or fail to comply with (a rule or law)
  • Antonym: to comply - to act in accordance with a rule, command, or wish

Etymology

From Latin 'infringere' (to break, weaken), from 'in-' (in) + 'frangere' (to break).

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈfrɪndʒ / (AmE), / ɪnˈfrɪndʒ / (BrE)
to go beyond the accepted or legal limits of something; to encroach or trespass
go beyond limits; encroach

Examples

The company was found to infringe on environmental regulations by expanding its operations beyond permitted boundaries.
When you infringe upon another country’s territory, it can lead to serious conflict.
Some neighbors felt the new fence infringed beyond the agreed property line.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to encroach - to gradually go beyond usual or accepted limits
  • Synonym: to violate - to break or fail to comply with (a rule or formal agreement)
  • Antonym: respect - to not go beyond limits or boundaries

Etymology

From Latin 'infringere' (to break, weaken), from 'in-' (in) + 'frangere' (to break).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
