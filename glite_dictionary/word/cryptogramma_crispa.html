Cryptogramma crispa

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪptoʊˈɡræmə ˈkrɪspə / (AmE), / ˌkrɪptəˈɡræmə ˈkrɪspə / (BrE)
a fern species of Europe and Asia Minor with short slender rhizome and densely tufted bright green fronds resembling parsley
a fern species with parsley-like fronds

Examples

The shady slopes were covered in Cryptogramma crispa, lending the rocks a fresh, green look.
Botanists studied the adaptation strategies of Cryptogramma crispa in mountainous terrain.
Hikers were delighted by the parsley-like fronds of Cryptogramma crispa growing along the trail.

Etymology

From Greek kryptos (hidden) + gramma (letter), and Latin crispus (curled) describing its frond margins.
