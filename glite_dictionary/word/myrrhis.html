Myrrhis

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɜr.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.rɪs / (BrE)
A genus of European perennial herbs with pinnate leaves and clusters of white flowers, belonging to the family Apiaceae.
a genus of European perennial herbs

Examples

The leaves of Myrrhis are sometimes used as a flavoring in traditional European dishes.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Myrrhis in shaded woodlands.
Myrrhis is known for its sweet scent and delicate white flowers.

Etymology

From Latin *myrrhis*, from Ancient Greek μύρρις (múrrhis), a kind of aromatic plant.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɜr.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈmɪr.ɪs / (BrE)
A genus of European perennial herbs characterized by pinnate leaves and umbels of white flowers.
a genus of European perennial herbs

Examples

The garden's collection included Myrrhis, with its fragrant leaves.
Botanists often study Myrrhis for its distinctive umbels of white flowers.
Myrrhis is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic qualities.

Etymology

From Latin *myrrhis*, from Greek *murrhís* (sweet cicely).
