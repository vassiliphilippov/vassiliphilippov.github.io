disenfranchisement

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsɪnˈfræn.tʃaɪz.mənt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈfræn.tʃaɪz.mənt / (BrE)
the act of withdrawing the right to vote or terminating someone's rights, privileges, or certification, especially in a legal or organizational context
act of withdrawing rights or certification

Examples

The new law resulted in the disenfranchisement of thousands of eligible voters.
His disenfranchisement from the professional society followed repeated breaches of the code of conduct.
Widespread disenfranchisement threatened the legitimacy of the election process.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: enfranchisement - the act of giving the right or privilege, especially to vote

Etymology

From dis- (expressing reversal) + enfranchise (from French 'enfranchir', from 'franc' meaning free) + -ment.
