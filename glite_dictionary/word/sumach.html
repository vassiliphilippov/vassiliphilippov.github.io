sumach

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːmæk / (AmE), / ˈsjuːmæk / (BrE)
a shrub or small tree of the genus Rhus, typically with compound leaves and clusters of red or white berries, found in warm regions; often refers to the non-poisonous varieties
shrub or tree of the genus Rhus

Examples

The bright red berries of the sumach can be used to make a tangy spice.
They planted a row of sumach trees along the driveway for their striking autumn colors.
Some species of sumach are popular in landscaping for their ornamental foliage.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: poison ivy - a toxic plant of the genus Toxicodendron, closely related to poisonous Rhus
  • Synonym: staghorn sumac - a common species of sumach (Rhus typhina) with distinctive fuzzy branches

Etymology

From Arabic *summāq* (red dye), via Old French and Latin.
