Albion

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.bi.ən / (AmE), / ˈæl.bi.ən / (BrE)
A literary or archaic name for England or Great Britain, especially used in poetry.
poetic or archaic name for England or Great Britain
archaic

Examples

The poet described the rolling green hills of Albion with reverence.
Legends from ancient Albion have inspired generations of storytellers.
The term Albion often appears in romantic poetry about England.

Etymology

From Latin 'Albion', possibly from the Proto-Indo-European root ‘albho-’ meaning 'white', referring to the white cliffs of Dover.
