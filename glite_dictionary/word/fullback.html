fullback

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊlˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈfʊlbæk / (BrE)
(in American football) a player on the offensive team who lines up behind the quarterback and is primarily responsible for blocking and short-yardage running plays.
offensive football position; blocking running back
american

Examples

The team's fullback led an effective block for the running back to score a touchdown.
As a fullback, he was known for his strength and reliable short-yardage gains.
She earned a scholarship to play fullback at a top college football program.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halfback - offensive football position, typically faster and plays different running/receiving roles

Etymology

Formed from 'full' + 'back'; originally used in rugby and association football for a defensive position, later adapted for American football.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfʊl.bæk / (AmE), / ˈfʊl.bæk / (BrE)
(in American football) a player on the offensive team who lines up behind the quarterback and primarily blocks for the running back, but may also run with the ball or catch passes.
offensive football position (blocking/running)

Examples

The team's fullback excelled at blocking defenders to open lanes for the running back.
Our coach decided to use the fullback on short-yardage plays.
As a rookie fullback, he quickly learned the importance of protecting the quarterback.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halfback - running back positioned closer to the line of scrimmage; carries the ball more often

Etymology

From 'full' + 'back', originally referring to a deep defensive position in early forms of football.
