Casuarius

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæz.juˈɛəri.əs / (AmE), / ˌkæz.juˈɛər.i.əs / (BrE)
The genus consisting of the large, flightless birds known as cassowaries, native to Australia and New Guinea.
genus of cassowary birds

Examples

The genus Casuarius includes several species of large, flightless birds.
Biologists have studied the diet and behavior of Casuarius in tropical rainforests.
Members of the Casuarius genus are known for their helmet-like casques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cassowary - A large, flightless bird native to Australia and New Guinea

Etymology

From Latin casuarius (cassowary), from Malay kasuari.
