Peromyscus eremicus

1. NOUN

/ pɛˌrɒmɪskəs ɪˈrɛmɪkəs / (AmE), / pɛˌrɒmɪskəs ɪˈrɛmɪkəs / (BrE)
a species of burrowing mouse found in the desert areas of the southwestern United States
desert-dwelling burrowing mouse species

Examples

The Peromyscus eremicus is specially adapted to survive in arid desert environments.
Researchers tracked the movements of several Peromyscus eremicus to study their burrowing habits.
Unlike other mice, Peromyscus eremicus rarely drinks water, relying mainly on seeds for hydration.

Etymology

From the genus name Peromyscus (from Greek 'pēros', meaning 'maimed', and 'mys', meaning 'mouse') and the Latin 'eremicus', meaning 'of the desert'.
