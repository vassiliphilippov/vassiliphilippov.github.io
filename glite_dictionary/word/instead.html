instead

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈstɛd / (AmE), / ɪnˈstɛd / (BrE)
as an alternative or substitute; rather than what was originally intended or expected
as an alternative; rather than

Examples

I was going to go for a walk, but I stayed home instead.
She didn't buy the dress; she chose a skirt instead.
You could take the bus, or you could drive instead.

Synset Examples

rather than disappoint the children, he did two quick tricks before he left
he didn't call; rather (or instead), he wrote her a letter
used English terms instead of Latin ones

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rather - used to indicate preference or an alternative

Etymology

From Middle English 'in stead' (in place), from Old English 'stede' (place).

2. ADV

/ ɪnˈstɛd / (AmE), / ɪnˈstɛd / (BrE)
as a substitute or alternative; in place of someone or something else
as a substitute or alternative

Examples

If you don't have milk, you can use water instead.
She wanted to go for a walk, but it started raining, so they watched a movie instead.
Why not email me instead of calling?

Synset Examples

Felix became a herpetologist instead
alternatively we could buy a used car

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alternatively - another way or possibility; as another option
  • Synonym: rather - used to indicate preference or choice

Etymology

From Middle English 'in stede' (in place), influenced by Old English 'stede' (place).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00063710-r
Synset POS: r
