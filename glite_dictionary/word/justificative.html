justificative

1. ADJ

/ ˌdʒʌstɪˈfɪkətɪv / (AmE), / ˌdʒʌstɪˈfɪkətɪv / (BrE)
intended to justify or defend something in speech or writing
serving to justify or defend

Examples

His justificative remarks tried to explain the controversial decision.
The president’s justificative letter aimed to persuade critics of the new law.
She adopted a justificative tone throughout her article to defend her actions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: justificatory - intended to justify; serving as justification

Etymology

From Late Latin justificativus (making just), from justificare (to justify).

2. ADJ

/ ˌdʒʌstəˈfɪkətɪv / (AmE), / ˌdʒʌstɪˈfɪkətɪv / (BrE)
serving to justify or provide a reason for something; giving justification
serving to justify

Examples

The lawyer presented a justificative statement to defend her client’s actions.
His report was more justificative than explanatory, focusing on reasons rather than details.
A justificative document must be submitted to support the expense claim.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: justificatory - serving to justify or as a justification

Etymology

From Late Latin justificativus (making just), from justificare (to justify).
