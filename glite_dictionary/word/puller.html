puller

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʊlər / (AmE), / ˈpʊlə / (BrE)
a person or thing that pulls, tugs, or drags something, especially as part of a task or process
person or thing that pulls or drags

Examples

The strongest puller on the team helped move the heavy cart up the hill.
A horse was once the primary puller of plows on many farms.
The device acts as a cable puller to assist with wiring installations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pusher - a person or thing that pushes

2. NOUN

/ ˈpʊlɚ / (AmE), / ˈpʊlə / (BrE)
a person or thing that pulls something, especially with force or in a specific role
a person or thing that pulls

Examples

The strongest puller on the team helped us win the tug-of-war match.
This device acts as a puller to remove gears from shafts.
He’s a fast weed puller when working in the garden.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pusher - a person or thing that pushes

Etymology

From 'pull' + '-er', a suffix forming agent nouns.
