banal

1. ADJ

/ bəˈnæl / (AmE), / bəˈnɑːl / (BrE)
So lacking in originality as to be obvious and boring; repeated too often to be interesting or fresh.
lacking originality or freshness

Examples

Many romantic comedies suffer from banal plots that audiences have seen countless times before.
Her speech was well-intentioned but filled with banal clichés and predictable phrases.
Critics dismissed the novel as banal, saying it offered nothing new to the genre.

Synset Examples

his remarks were trite and commonplace
hackneyed phrases
a stock answer
repeating threadbare jokes
parroting some timeworn axiom
the trite metaphor ‘hard as nails’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trite - lacking originality or freshness
  • Synonym: hackneyed - overused and unoriginal
  • Antonym: original - fresh and inventive; not derived from something else

Etymology

From French banal, meaning 'commonplace' or 'banal,' itself from 'ban' (proclamation, summons to public use).
