confectionery

1. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛkʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / kənˈfɛkʃənəri / (BrE)
the occupation or business of making sweets and other confections
profession of making sweets; confectioner's trade

Examples

His family was known for their confectionery skills, passed down through generations.
She attended a special school to study confectionery and cake design.
Mastering the art of confectionery requires both creativity and precision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confectioner - a person who makes or sells sweets and confections

Etymology

From French *confiserie* (confectionery), from *confire* (to preserve), ultimately from Latin *conficere* (to prepare, accomplish).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛk.ʃəˌnɛr.i / (AmE), / kənˈfɛk.ʃən.ər.i / (BrE)
(chiefly British) A shop that sells sweets, chocolate, and other sweet foods.
shop selling sweets and chocolates
british

Examples

I stopped by the confectionery to buy some chocolates for my friend.
The village confectionery was famous for its handmade sweets and fudge.
After school, children often head straight to the local confectionery for treats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confectioner's - a shop selling sweets and chocolates

Etymology

From French *confiserie*; ultimately from Latin *confectio* (a making, preparing).

sweets and candies collectively, or a shop that sells them.
sweets and candies collectively

Examples

The shop mainly sells homemade confectionery and gourmet chocolates.
She has a weakness for French confectionery, especially macarons and truffles.
After dinner, a selection of confectionery was served with coffee.

Synset Examples

the business decided to concentrate on confectionery and soft drinks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candy - sweet food made primarily from sugar; especially in American English
  • Synonym: pastry - baked food made with dough, often filled or topped with sweet or savory ingredients

Etymology

From French *confiserie*, from Latin *confectio* (something prepared).
