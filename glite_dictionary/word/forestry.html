forestry

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔr.ɪ.stri / (AmE), / ˈfɒr.ɪ.stri / (BrE)
the science and practice of planting, caring for, and managing forests, especially for the production of timber
science and practice of managing forests

Examples

She studied forestry at university to learn how to manage woodlands sustainably.
Advances in forestry have contributed to improved conservation practices around the world.
The government invested in modern forestry techniques to boost timber production and protect habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silviculture - the science of cultivating forests

Etymology

From Latin 'foresta' (open wood), via Old French 'foresterie'.
