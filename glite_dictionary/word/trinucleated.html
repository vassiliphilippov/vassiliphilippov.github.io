trinucleated

1. ADJ

/ traɪˈnuː.kli.eɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / traɪˈnjuː.kli.eɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of a cell) having three nuclei.
having three nuclei (biology)
medical

Examples

The pathologist observed a trinucleated cell under the microscope.
Some protozoa may exhibit trinucleated stages during their life cycle.
Researchers studied the effects that produced trinucleated hepatocytes in liver tissue samples.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: binucleated - having two nuclei (biology)

Etymology

From tri- (three) + nucleated (having nuclei); based on Latin nucleus (kernel, core).
