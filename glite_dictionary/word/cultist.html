cultist

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌl.tɪst / (AmE), / ˈkʌl.tɪst / (BrE)
a member of a religious cult
member of a religious cult

Examples

Police arrested several cultists in connection with the illegal ceremonies.
The novel's villain was portrayed as a devoted cultist willing to do anything for his leader.
The cultist refused to answer any questions about the group's inner workings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cult - a system of religious veneration and devotion directed toward a particular figure or object

Etymology

From 'cult' + '-ist', the suffix denoting someone who practices or is concerned with something.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌltɪst / (AmE), / ˈkʌltɪst / (BrE)
a member of a cult, especially one regarded as unorthodox or extreme, often living outside conventional society under the guidance of a charismatic leader
member of a cult

Examples

The police raided the compound where the cultists had been living in isolation.
A charismatic leader managed to convince several cultists to donate their possessions to the group.
Journalists struggled to get information from the cultists who rarely spoke to outsiders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: follower - a person who supports and admires a particular person or set of ideas
  • Antonym: leader - a person who leads or commands a group, organization, or country

Etymology

From 'cult' + '-ist', denoting a follower or adherent.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10002055-n
Synset POS: n
