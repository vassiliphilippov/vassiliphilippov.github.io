dormy

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɔːr.mi / (AmE), / ˈdɔː.mi / (BrE)
(in golf match play) having as many holes ahead as remain to be played, so that at worst a tie can result.
ahead in golf match play so a tie is the worst result

Examples

He was dormy three but still managed to lose the match.
When a player is dormy two, the best the opponent can hope for is a tie.
Reaching the seventeenth hole, she realized she was dormy and could not lose the match outright.

Synset Examples

he was dormie three and still lost the match

Etymology

Probably from obsolete French 'dormir' (to sleep), suggesting security or inability to lose.
