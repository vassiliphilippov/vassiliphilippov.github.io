brigandine

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪɡ.ənˌdiːn / (AmE), / ˈbrɪɡ.ənˌdiːn / (BrE)
a medieval armored jacket made of metal plates or rings sewn onto cloth or leather, used as protection in battle
medieval armored jacket

Examples

The knight wore a brigandine over his tunic as he prepared for battle.
Museum visitors admired the intricately decorated brigandine displayed in the medieval arms section.
Though less protective than full plate armor, a brigandine allowed for greater movement during combat.

Etymology

From Old French 'brigandine', related to 'brigand' (foot soldier, bandit).
