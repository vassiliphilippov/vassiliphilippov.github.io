hiss

1. NOUN

/ hɪs / (AmE), / hɪs / (BrE)
a sharp, sibilant sound made to express disapproval or contempt
sharp sound showing disapproval

Examples

The crowd responded to the poor performance with a loud hiss that echoed through the hall.
A sudden hiss from the audience silenced the speaker for a moment.
His attempt at humor was met with a hiss of disapproval from the back row.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boo - an exclamation expressing disapproval or contempt
  • Antonym: cheer - a shout of approval, support, or praise

Etymology

Imitative; from Middle English hissen, of imitative origin.

3. VERB

/ hɪs / (AmE), / hɪs / (BrE)
to move, flow, or pass with a sharp sibilant or whooshing sound
move with a sharp sibilant or whooshing sound

Examples

The train began to hiss as it pulled into the station.
Winds hissed through the gaps in the old wooden door.
The rockets hissed overhead, leaving trails of smoke in the sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whiz - to move quickly with a buzzing or hissing sound
  • Synonym: whistle - to move or make a sound like a whistle

Etymology

Imitative; from Old English 'hysian', echoing the sound produced.
