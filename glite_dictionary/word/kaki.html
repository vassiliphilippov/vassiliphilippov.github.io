kaki

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːki / (AmE), / ˈkɑːki / (BrE)
A small deciduous tree native to Asia, especially Japan and China, bearing large red or orange edible astringent fruit; also called Japanese persimmon.
Asian tree with edible orange fruit

Examples

The kaki tree in their garden was heavy with bright orange fruit in the autumn.
He bought a box of ripe kakis from the market to make a traditional dessert.
Dried kaki slices are popular snacks in East Asian countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: persimmon - A tree or its sweet edible fruit; commonly used synonym

Etymology

From Japanese *kaki*, meaning 'persimmon'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːki / (AmE), / ˈkɑːki / (BrE)
a blackish stilt (Himantopus novaezelandiae) native to New Zealand, sometimes regarded as a color phase of the white-headed stilt
blackish New Zealand stilt (bird)

Examples

The population of the kaki has declined sharply due to habitat loss and introduced predators.
Wildlife rangers track the movements of the rare kaki in New Zealand wetlands.
Seeing a kaki in the wild is considered a memorable experience for birdwatchers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: white-headed stilt - a wader species with white head, related to the kaki

Etymology

From Maori 'kākī', meaning 'stilt' or 'neck'.
