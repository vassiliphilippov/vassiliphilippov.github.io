Amsinckia

1. NOUN

/ æmˈsɪŋkiə / (AmE), / æmˈsɪŋkiə / (BrE)
A genus of rough annual herbs, known as fiddlenecks, native to Europe and the Americas.
genus of rough annual herbs (fiddlenecks)

Examples

The fields were dotted with yellow-flowering Amsinckia during the spring.
Amsinckia species are often called fiddlenecks due to the shape of their flower stems.
In California, Amsinckia is a common sight in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiddleneck - Common name for plants in the genus Amsinckia

Etymology

Named in honor of German banker Wilhelm Amsinck (1752–1831).
