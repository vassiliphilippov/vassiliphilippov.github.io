disciplined

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɪs.ə.plɪnd / (AmE), / ˈdɪs.ɪ.plɪnd / (BrE)
obeying rules or showing controlled and orderly behavior, especially as a result of training or self-control.
obeying rules; self-controlled

Examples

The most disciplined students tend to achieve the best results in school.
A disciplined athlete follows a strict training schedule every day.
Her disciplined approach to work earns her respect from colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orderly - well-organized and well-behaved
  • Antonym: unruly - not obedient or hard to control

Etymology

From Latin disciplina (instruction, knowledge), via Old French discipline.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɪsəplɪnd / (AmE), / ˈdɪsɪplɪnd / (BrE)
trained and controlled through instruction or exercise, especially to behave or perform in a particular way.
trained or controlled by instruction

Examples

His disciplined approach to studying helped him excel at university.
It takes a disciplined mind to focus for long hours without distraction.
The soldiers were highly disciplined and worked together with remarkable precision.

Synset Examples

the beautiful coordination of his disciplined muscles
a disciplined mind

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unruly - badly behaved; difficult to control
  • Synonym: trained - having been taught a particular skill or discipline

Etymology

From Latin disciplina (instruction, knowledge), from discipulus (pupil).
