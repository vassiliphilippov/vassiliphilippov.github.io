tetrasporangium

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛtrəspəˈrændʒiəm / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəspɒˈrandʒiəm / (BrE)
A sporangium that produces or contains four asexual spores, especially in algae and fungi.
sporangium with four asexual spores
medical

Examples

In some red algae, the tetrasporangium divides to form four identical spores.
After fertilization, the tetrasporangium matures and releases its spores into the water.
Under the microscope, the tetrasporangium appears as a tiny sac filled with four spores.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sporangium - a structure in which spores are produced

Etymology

From Greek tetra- (four) + sporangium (spore case).
