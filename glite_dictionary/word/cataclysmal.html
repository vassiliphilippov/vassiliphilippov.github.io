cataclysmal

1. ADJ

/ ˌkæt.əˈklɪz.məl / (AmE), / ˌkat.əˈklɪz.məl / (BrE)
Causing great destruction or upheaval; severely destructive.
severely destructive

Examples

The city struggled to recover after a cataclysmal storm left thousands homeless.
Scientists warn that cataclysmal events could reshape the earth's surface dramatically.
A cataclysmal volcanic eruption devastated the region in a matter of hours.

Synset Examples

cataclysmic nuclear war
a cataclysmic earthquake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cataclysmic - causing sudden and violent upheaval or destruction

Etymology

From 'cataclysm', patterned after 'cataclysmic', from Greek kataklysmos (deluge, flood).
