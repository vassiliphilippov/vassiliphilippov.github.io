alumroot

1. NOUN

/ ˈælmˌrut / (AmE), / ˈaləmˌruːt / (BrE)
any of several perennial herbs of the genus Heuchera, native to North America and grown for their attractive foliage and small flowers
a plant of the genus Heuchera

Examples

The shade garden featured coral bells and alumroot among its many perennials.
Early settlers used the root of alumroot for its astringent properties in home remedies.
In spring, the alumroot produces delicate sprays of white or pink flowers.

Etymology

Name refers to the plant's astringent roots, which were sometimes used like alum.
