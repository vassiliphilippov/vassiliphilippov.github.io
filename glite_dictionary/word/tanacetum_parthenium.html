Tanacetum parthenium

1. NOUN

/ ˌtænəˈsitəm ˌpɑːrˈθiːniəm / (AmE), / ˌtænəˈsiːtəm ˌpɑːˈθiːniəm / (BrE)
A bushy, aromatic perennial herb native to Europe, with clusters of white-rayed, button-like flowers, traditionally used for medicinal purposes; also known as feverfew.
an aromatic medicinal herb (feverfew)

Examples

Tanacetum parthenium is often grown in herb gardens for its medicinal properties.
Many people use dried Tanacetum parthenium leaves as a remedy for headaches.
The daisy-like flowers of Tanacetum parthenium bloom in early summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feverfew - common name for Tanacetum parthenium

Etymology

From Latin: 'Tanacetum' meaning 'tansy' and 'parthenium', from Greek 'parthenion', an ancient name of the plant.
