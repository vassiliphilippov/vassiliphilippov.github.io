normotensive

1. ADJ

/ nɔr.moʊˈtɛn.sɪv / (AmE), / nɔː.məʊˈtɛn.sɪv / (BrE)
having normal blood pressure; not affected by abnormally high or low blood pressure
having normal blood pressure
medical

Examples

The patient's blood pressure remained normotensive throughout the procedure.
Most healthy adults are considered normotensive if their readings are within the standard range.
Regular exercise can help individuals maintain a normotensive state.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypertensive - having abnormally high blood pressure
  • Antonym: hypotensive - having abnormally low blood pressure

Etymology

From Latin 'norma' (standard, rule) + 'tensive' from 'tendere' (to stretch), modeled after 'hypertensive'.
