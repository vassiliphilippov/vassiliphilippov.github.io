acetaminophen

1. NOUN

/ əˌsitəˈmɪnəˌfɛn / (AmE), / ˌæsɪtiːˈmɪnəˌfiːn / (BrE)
A common drug used to relieve mild pain and reduce fever, not effective against inflammation.
mild pain reliever and fever reducer
medical american

Examples

My doctor recommended acetaminophen to help reduce my fever.
If you have a headache, you can take acetaminophen for relief.
The label warns not to exceed the recommended dosage of acetaminophen in a 24-hour period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paracetamol - alternative name for acetaminophen (primarily in British English)

Etymology

From 'acetyl' + 'amino' + 'phenol,' describing its chemical structure.
