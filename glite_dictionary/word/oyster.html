oyster

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔɪstɚ / (AmE), / ˈɔɪstə / (BrE)
a small, tender, rounded piece of dark meat located on each side of the backbone of a fowl, considered a delicacy
small tender dark meat on a fowl's back

Examples

The chef carefully removed the oysters from the chicken before serving.
Many consider the oyster to be the tastiest part of a roasted bird.
He always saves the oysters for last when carving a turkey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wishbone - the forked bone in the chest of a bird

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔɪstər / (AmE), / ˈɔɪstə / (BrE)
an edible bivalve mollusc with a rough irregular shell, found on the seabed and farmed or harvested for food
edible marine mollusc with a rough shell

Examples

She ordered a dozen oysters on the half shell at the seafood restaurant.
The oyster yields pearls as well as being prized for its meat.
Fresh oysters are often served with lemon and a dash of hot sauce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clam - a similar edible bivalve mollusc

Etymology

From Old French *oistre* and Latin *ostrea* (oyster).

4. NOUN

/ ˈɔɪstər / (AmE), / ˈɔɪstə / (BrE)
a marine mollusk with a rough, irregular shell, usually found on the sea bed in coastal waters; some species are edible and may produce pearls
edible marine mollusk with rough shell

Examples

We ordered a dozen oysters on the half shell at the seaside restaurant.
The oyster produces pearls as a natural defense against irritants.
She carefully pried open the oyster to find if it held a pearl.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clam - a marine mollusk with a hinged shell, similar to an oyster
  • Synonym: scallop - a marine mollusk with a fan-shaped shell
  • Synonym: mussel - a marine bivalve mollusk with an elongated shell

Etymology

From Old French *oistre*, from Latin *ostrea* (oyster).
