graduality

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡrædʒuˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌɡrædjuːˈælɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being gradual, or of occurring in slow, progressive stages
quality of being gradual

Examples

The graduality of the healing process made her appreciate every small improvement.
Policy changes were implemented with remarkable graduality, avoiding abrupt disruptions.
His writing style is marked by the graduality with which ideas unfold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gradualness - the quality of happening slowly and steadily
  • Antonym: suddenness - the quality of happening quickly and unexpectedly

Etymology

From 'gradual' + '-ity', influenced by Latin root 'gradus' (step) and '-italitas' (quality or state).
