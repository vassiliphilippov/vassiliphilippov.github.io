startup

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑːrtˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈstɑːtˌʌp / (BrE)
the act of beginning a new operation, process, or activity
beginning of a new operation or activity

Examples

The startup of the new power plant was attended by local officials.
We experienced several technical issues during the startup phase of the software.
Safety checks are essential before the startup of any new equipment.

Synset Examples

he opposed the inauguration of fluoridation
the startup of the new factory was delayed by strikes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commencement - the beginning of something
  • Antonym: shutdown - the act of stopping an operation or business

Etymology

From 'start' + 'up', first used in mid-20th century.

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɑrtˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈstɑːtʌp / (BrE)
the act of starting something, especially a machine or a system
the act of starting something up

Examples

The startup of the equipment must be monitored by qualified staff.
Frequent startups and shutdowns can shorten the lifespan of a machine.
After the startup procedure, the system operates automatically.

Synset Examples

repeated shutdowns and startups are expensive

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shutdown - the act of closing or ceasing operation

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'start up', meaning to begin operation.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00243298-n
Synset POS: n
