Panthera

1. NOUN

/ pænˈθɛrə / (AmE), / panˈθɪərə / (BrE)
A genus of large cats that includes the lion, tiger, leopard, jaguar, and snow leopard.
genus of big cats (lion, tiger, etc.)

Examples

The lion is classified in the genus Panthera along with tigers and leopards.
All members of Panthera are capable of roaring except the snow leopard.
Conservationists study how habitat loss impacts different Panthera species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: big cat - any of the large wild cats such as lion, tiger, or leopard

Etymology

From Latin Panthera, from Ancient Greek panthēra (panther).

2. NOUN

/ pænˈθɪrə / (AmE), / pænˈθɪərə / (BrE)
A genus of large cats that includes lions, tigers, leopards, and jaguars, known for their ability to roar.
genus of large roaring cats

Examples

The Panthera genus includes majestic animals such as lions and tigers.
Scientists have studied Panthera to better understand the evolution of big cats.
A leopard is a member of the Panthera genus, known for its adaptability to different habitats.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Felidae - the biological family of cats
  • Synonym: lion - large wild cat, species Panthera leo
  • Synonym: tiger - large striped wild cat, species Panthera tigris

Etymology

From Latin, from Greek panthēra (panther).
