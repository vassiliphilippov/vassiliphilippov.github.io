zeppelin

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɛpəlɪn / (AmE), / ˈzɛpəlɪn / (BrE)
a large rigid airship with a metal framework, originally used for passenger transport and as a bomber, especially in early 20th-century Europe
large rigid airship

Examples

The German army used a zeppelin to bomb London during World War I.
We took a tour of a zeppelin museum in Friedrichshafen.
The sight of a zeppelin floating above the city amazed the spectators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dirigible - a lighter-than-air aircraft that can be steered and propelled forward

Etymology

From German Zeppelin, named after Count Ferdinand von Zeppelin (1838–1917), its inventor.
