to perish

1. VERB

/ ˈpɛrɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɛrɪʃ / (BrE)
to die, especially in a sudden, violent, or untimely manner
to die, often suddenly or violently

Examples

Thousands of people perished in the earthquake that struck the city.
If left without water, these plants will quickly perish in the heat.
Many species have perished due to rapid climate change.

Synset Examples

She died from cancer
The children perished in the fire
The patient went peacefully
The old guy kicked the bucket at the age of 102

Related Terms

  • Synonym: die - to stop living; to cease to exist
  • Antonym: to survive - to continue to live, especially after coming close to dying

Etymology

From Old French *perir*, from Latin *perire* meaning 'to pass away, die' (from *per* 'through, completely' + *ire* 'to go').
