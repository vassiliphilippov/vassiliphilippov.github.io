dependence

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈpɛndəns / (AmE), / dɪˈpɛndəns / (BrE)
a condition in which a person is abnormally tolerant to and reliant upon a habit-forming substance, especially alcohol or drugs
reliance on a habit-forming substance
medical

Examples

His dependence on painkillers made recovery from surgery much more complicated.
Treating alcohol dependence often requires a combination of therapy and medication.
Years of drug dependence affected her health and personal relationships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: addiction - compulsive need for and use of a habit-forming substance

Etymology

From Latin 'dependēre', meaning 'to hang down or depend'.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈpɛndəns / (AmE), / dɪˈpɛndəns / (BrE)
The state of relying on or being controlled by someone or something else.
state of relying on someone or something

Examples

The patient's dependence on medication increased over time.
Economic dependence on a single export can make a country vulnerable.
His sense of dependence on his friends decreased as he grew more confident.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: independence - the state of not relying on or being controlled by others
  • Synonym: reliance - the act or state of depending on something or someone

Etymology

From Latin 'dependere' (to hang down), via French 'dépendance'.
