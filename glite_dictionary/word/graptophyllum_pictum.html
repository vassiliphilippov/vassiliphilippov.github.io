Graptophyllum pictum

1. NOUN

/ ɡræp.toʊˈfɪl.əm ˈpɪk.təm / (AmE), / ɡræp.təˈfɪl.əm ˈpɪk.təm / (BrE)
a tropical Old World shrub with purple or red tubular flowers and leaves often marked with patterns resembling a human face
a tropical shrub with patterned leaves and tubular flowers

Examples

The gardener planted Graptophyllum pictum along the fence for its striking foliage.
Botanists are fascinated by the leaf patterns of Graptophyllum pictum.
In tropical gardens, Graptophyllum pictum is valued for both its flowers and decorative leaves.

Etymology

From Greek 'graptos' (painted) and 'phyllon' (leaf), referring to the distinctive markings on the leaves.
