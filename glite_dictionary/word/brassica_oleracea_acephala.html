Brassica oleracea acephala

1. NOUN

/ ˌbræsɪkə oʊˈlɛriːəsiə æˈsɛfələ / (AmE), / ˌbrasɪkə əʊˌlɛːriːˈeɪsiə æˈsɛfələ / (BrE)
A hardy vegetable of the cabbage family with coarse, curly leaves that do not form a dense head; commonly known as kale or collard greens.
type of cabbage with loose, curly leaves

Examples

Brassica oleracea acephala is often grown for its edible, nutrient-rich leaves.
Farmers prefer Brassica oleracea acephala because it tolerates cold weather very well.
Many traditional dishes in Portugal use Brassica oleracea acephala as a main ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kale - A hardy, leafy cabbage without a dense head
  • Synonym: collard greens - Variety of loose-leaf cabbage similar to kale

Etymology

From Latin: 'Brassica' (cabbage), 'oleracea' (vegetable), 'acephala' (headless).
