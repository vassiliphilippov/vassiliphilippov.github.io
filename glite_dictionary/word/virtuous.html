virtuous

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɝːtʃuəs / (AmE), / ˈvɜːtʃuəs / (BrE)
having or showing high moral standards; morally good or righteous.
morally good or righteous

Examples

The novel's virtuous heroine sacrifices her happiness for the good of others.
He was respected as a virtuous leader who always tried to do what was right.
It is not always easy to remain virtuous when faced with difficult choices.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wicked - morally bad or evil
  • Synonym: righteous - morally right or justifiable

Etymology

From Latin 'virtuosus' (full of virtue, good, virtuous), from 'virtus' (virtue).

2. ADJ

/ ˈvɝːtʃuəs / (AmE), / ˈvɜːtʃuəs / (BrE)
(of a woman, sometimes archaic) remaining in a state of sexual virginity; chaste.
remaining sexually pure (archaic, of a woman)
archaic

Examples

In Victorian novels, a truly virtuous heroine was often expected to remain pure until marriage.
She was praised as a virtuous woman who guarded her chastity above all else.
The story emphasized that only the virtuous maidens were chosen for the sacred ceremony.

Synset Examples

pure and vestal modesty
a spinster or virgin lady
men have decreed that their women must be pure and virginal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaste - abstaining from sexual activity, especially for moral reasons
  • Synonym: virginal - relating to being a virgin or sexually pure
  • Antonym: promiscuous - having or characterized by many transient sexual relationships

Etymology

From Latin virtuosus (full of manly qualities, valiant, virtuous).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
