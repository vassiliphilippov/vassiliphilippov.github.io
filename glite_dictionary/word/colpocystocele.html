colpocystocele

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑl.poʊˈsɪstəˌsil / (AmE), / ˌkɒl.pəʊˈsɪstəˌsiːl / (BrE)
A hernia in which the urinary bladder protrudes through the wall of the vagina, sometimes occurring after childbirth.
hernia of the bladder into the vagina
medical

Examples

After a difficult delivery, she was diagnosed with a colpocystocele requiring surgical repair.
A colpocystocele can cause symptoms such as urinary incontinence and pelvic pressure.
Doctors often check for colpocystocele during a pelvic examination, especially in women with urinary complaints.

Etymology

From Greek 'kolpos' (vagina) + 'kystis' (bladder) + '-cele' (hernia).
