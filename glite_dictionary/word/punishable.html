punishable

1. ADJ

/ ˈpʌnɪʃəbəl / (AmE), / ˈpʌnɪʃəbl / (BrE)
Liable to or deserving punishment according to law or rules.
deserving punishment; liable to penalty

Examples

The crime was considered punishable by a heavy fine under state law.
Certain actions in the workplace are punishable offenses and may result in termination.
Failure to report income can be a punishable violation of tax regulations.

Synset Examples

punishable offenses

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unpardonable - unable to be forgiven; not excusable

Etymology

From punish + -able, from Old French *punissable*, from Latin *punire* (to punish) + *-abilis* (able).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpʌnɪʃəbəl / (AmE), / ˈpʌnɪʃəbl / (BrE)
(of an act or offense) subject to legal punishment; liable to be punished by law.
liable to legal punishment
legal

Examples

The theft was considered a punishable offense under the new law.
Failure to file taxes on time is punishable by a fine or imprisonment.
Certain actions are only punishable if committed with criminal intent.

Synset Examples

a penal offense

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unpardonable - impossible to forgive or excuse (not deserving pardon)
  • Antonym: blameless - free from guilt; not deserving blame or punishment

Etymology

From punish + -able, modeled on French 'punissable'.

WordNet ID: oewn-01407374-s
Synset POS: s
