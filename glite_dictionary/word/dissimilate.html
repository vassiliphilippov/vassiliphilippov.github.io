to dissimilate

1. VERB

/ dɪˈsɪməˌleɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɪmɪleɪt / (BrE)
to make or become less similar; to cause something to diverge in form or character
make or become less similar

Examples

During language evolution, certain speech sounds dissimilate to make words easier to pronounce.
The artist chose to dissimilate the two styles within his new painting.
If the twins start dressing differently, they will dissimilate their appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to differentiate - to recognize or make distinct differences
  • Antonym: to assimilate - to become similar; to absorb and make alike

Etymology

From Latin dissimilare (to make unlike), from dis- (apart) + similis (like).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈsɪməˌleɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɪmɪleɪt / (BrE)
to become dissimilar or less similar to something else; to undergo a process of growing apart in resemblance or characteristics
become less similar

Examples

Over centuries, the languages spoken in this region began to dissimilate due to isolation.
After their separation, the two companies started to dissimilate in terms of culture and strategy.
Some linguists study how consonant sounds may dissimilate within a word over time.

Synset Examples

These two related tribes of people gradually dissimilated over time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to differentiate - to become different or develop differences
  • Antonym: to resemble - to be similar to

Etymology

From Latin 'dissimilat-', past participle of 'dissimilare' (to render unlike), from 'dis-' (apart) + 'similis' (like).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00160461-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ dɪˈsɪməˌleɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɪmɪleɪt / (BrE)
(of sounds, especially in a word) to become less similar to each other in pronunciation, usually through a phonetic change.
to become less similar in sound (linguistics)

Examples

In some dialects, certain consonants dissimilate over time to reduce similarity.
When placed together, linguistic elements may dissimilate to ease pronunciation.
Phonologists have observed that the two 'r' sounds in 'February' often dissimilate.

Synset Examples

These consonants dissimilate

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to assimilate - (linguistics) to make sounds more similar in pronunciation

Etymology

From Latin dissimilatus, past participle of dissimilare (to make unlike).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
