footnote

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtˌnoʊt / (AmE), / ˈfʊt.nəʊt / (BrE)
A note at the bottom of a page in a book or document, providing additional information or references related to the main text.
note at bottom of a page

Examples

Each chapter includes at least one footnote with further reading suggestions.
He clarified his argument in a footnote at the bottom of the page.
If you look at the footnote, you'll find the source of this quote.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: endnote - a note placed at the end of a text or section instead of at the bottom of the page

Etymology

Formed from 'foot' + 'note', referring to a note at the 'foot' (bottom) of a page.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06776955-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈfʊtˌnoʊt / (AmE), / ˈfʊt.nəʊt / (BrE)
to add explanatory notes or critical comments to a text, typically at the bottom of a page or at the end of a document.
add explanatory notes to text

Examples

The editor decided to footnote all rare terms in the manuscript for clarity.
She footnoted every claim in her research to provide additional context.
While reviewing the draft, Mark noticed which sections still needed to be footnoted.

Synset Examples

The scholar annotated the early edition of a famous novel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to annotate - to add explanatory notes to a text

Etymology

From 'footnote' (noun), combining 'foot' + 'note', reflecting notes placed at the foot of a page.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01708812-v
Synset POS: v
