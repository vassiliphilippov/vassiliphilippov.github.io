Astarte

1. PROPN

/ əˈstɑːrti / (AmE), / əˈstɑːti / (BrE)
an ancient Phoenician goddess of love and fertility, regarded as the Phoenician counterpart to Ishtar.
Phoenician goddess of love and fertility

Examples

Many ancient temples were dedicated to Astarte throughout the Phoenician world.
The worship of Astarte was associated with rituals celebrating fertility and renewal.
Archaeologists discovered artifacts linked to the cult of Astarte near the Mediterranean coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ishtar - Mesopotamian goddess of love, beauty, and fertility

Etymology

From Greek Astarte, from Phoenician Ashtart.
