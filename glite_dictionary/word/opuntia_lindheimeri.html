Opuntia lindheimeri

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈpʌn.ti.ə lɪndˈhaɪ.mə.raɪ / (AmE), / əˈpʌn.tɪə lɪndˈhaɪ.məri / (BrE)
A species of prickly pear cactus with yellow flowers and purple fruits, native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.
a species of prickly pear cactus

Examples

The Opuntia lindheimeri thrives in arid regions and is well-known for its colorful fruits.
Many gardeners admire the striking yellow flowers of Opuntia lindheimeri during the summer months.
Wildlife often feed on the purple fruits produced by Opuntia lindheimeri in its native habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prickly pear - common name for species of the genus Opuntia, cacti with spiny fruit

Etymology

Opuntia: from Latin, after Opus in Greece; lindheimeri: named after German-American botanist Ferdinand Lindheimer.
