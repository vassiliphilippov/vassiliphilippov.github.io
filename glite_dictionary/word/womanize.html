to womanize

1. VERB

/ ˈwʊməˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈwʊmənʌɪz / (BrE)
to give a more feminine or womanly quality or appearance to someone or something
make (more) feminine

Examples

The costume designer decided to womanize the character's appearance for the new play.
Advocates argued to womanize the language of the document to promote inclusivity.
He attempted to womanize the brand by introducing softer colors and curved designs.

Synset Examples

This hairdo feminizes the man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to feminize - to give a feminine quality or appearance to

Etymology

Formed from 'woman' + '-ize', following the pattern of similar verbs like 'feminize'.

(of a man) to pursue casual sexual relationships with many women.
have amorous affairs with many women

Examples

Some celebrities are criticized for their tendency to womanize.
His marriage ended because he continued to womanize despite promising to change.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

3. VERB

/ ˈwʊm.əˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈwʊm.ə.naɪz / (BrE)
(of a man) to repeatedly engage in casual or indiscriminate sexual relationships with women.
to have casual sexual relationships with women

Examples

He was known to womanize in his youth, often bragging about his conquests.
Rumors spread quickly when the politician was caught womanizing at bars downtown.
She refused to date anyone who seemed likely to womanize or play games with her feelings.

Synset Examples

Many warned her not to date him; he had a reputation to womanize.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: womanizer - a man who habitually seeks casual sexual encounters with women

Etymology

Formed from 'woman' + '-ize' suffix, modeled after 'womanizer.'
