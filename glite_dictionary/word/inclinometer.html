inclinometer

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌklɪˈnɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / ɪnˌklɪˈnɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument for measuring angles of slope, elevation, or magnetic dip, typically used in engineering and navigation.
measuring instrument for slope or dip angle

Examples

The engineer used an inclinometer to check the angle of the hillside before construction began.
Pilots often rely on an inclinometer to measure the aircraft's angle of climb or descent.
The magnetic inclinometer provided accurate readings during the geophysical survey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clinometer - an instrument for measuring angles of slope (clinical use)

Etymology

From Latin inclinare (to lean) + -meter (measuring device) from Greek metron (measure).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnkləˈnɑːmədər / (AmE), / ˌɪnklɪˈnɒmɪtə / (BrE)
An instrument used for measuring the angle of inclination or tilt with respect to the horizontal, especially in aircraft or construction.
instrument for measuring angle of tilt

Examples

The pilot used the inclinometer to ensure the aircraft was level during flight.
Engineers checked the bridge's stability with a digital inclinometer.
A faulty inclinometer can result in inaccurate tilt measurements in construction projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clinometer - an instrument for measuring angles of slope

Etymology

From Latin 'inclinare' (to lean) + '-meter' (measuring device).
