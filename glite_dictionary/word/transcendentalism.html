transcendentalism

1. NOUN

/ ˌtrænˌsɛnˈdɛn(t)əlˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌtræn.sɛnˈdɛn.təl.ɪ.zəm / (BrE)
a philosophical system that emphasizes intuition and spiritual experience over empirical and material evidence
philosophy emphasizing intuition and the spiritual

Examples

The essay explores how transcendentalism values intuition over observable facts.
Many American writers in the 19th century were influenced by transcendentalism and its focus on the inner spirit.
Ralph Waldo Emerson is often regarded as a central figure in the movement of transcendentalism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: empiricism - the theory that all knowledge is derived from sense-experience

Etymology

From 'transcendental' (Latin transcendere 'to climb over, surpass') + '-ism'.
