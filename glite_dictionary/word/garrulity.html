garrulity

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈruːlɪti / (AmE), / ɡəˈrjuːlɪti / (BrE)
Excessive talkativeness, especially about trivial matters.
excessive talkativeness

Examples

Her garrulity made meetings drag on much longer than necessary.
Despite his garrulity, he often failed to say anything truly meaningful.
At family gatherings, her garrulity is both amusing and exasperating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loquacity - the quality of talking a lot; talkativeness
  • Antonym: taciturnity - the quality of being reserved or saying little

Etymology

From Latin 'garrulitas', from 'garrulus' (talkative).
