awry

1. ADV

/ əˈraɪ / (AmE), / əˈraɪ / (BrE)
Twisted or turned to one side; not in the correct position or arrangement.
twisted or turned to one side

Examples

The picture on the wall was hanging awry after the earthquake.
She smiled with her hat sitting slightly awry on her head.
His glasses were knocked awry in the scuffle but he quickly fixed them.

Synset Examples

rugs lying askew
with his necktie twisted awry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: askew - not in a straight or level position

Etymology

From Middle English awry (turned away).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00273600-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADJ

/ əˈraɪ / (AmE), / əˈraɪ / (BrE)
not in the correct position; turned or twisted to one side.
twisted or out of proper position

Examples

His tie was awry after he rushed to get dressed for the meeting.
The picture frame hung awry on the wall, bothering her sense of order.
After the strong wind, many hats sat awry on the heads of the wedding guests.

Synset Examples

a … youth with a gorgeous red necktie all awry
his wig was, as the British say, skew-whiff

Related Terms

  • Synonym: askew - not in a straight or level position; twisted to one side
  • Antonym: straight - not turned or twisted; in proper position

Etymology

From Middle English 'awry', from a- (on) + wry (twisted).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02320130-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ əˈraɪ / (AmE), / əˈraɪ / (BrE)
not working as intended; in a state of disorder or failure
not working properly; out of order

Examples

The negotiations went awry after a series of misunderstandings.
When the plan went awry, the team had to improvise quickly.
Something must be awry with the computer because it keeps restarting on its own.

Synset Examples

something is amiss
has gone completely haywire
something is wrong with the engine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amiss - not quite right; out of place or wrong
  • Synonym: wrong - not functioning properly; incorrect or faulty

Etymology

From Middle English 'awry', equivalent to a- (on, in, to) + wry (twisted).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01096064-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADV

/ əˈraɪ / (AmE), / əˈraɪ / (BrE)
away from the correct, expected, or normal course; not as planned or intended
not as planned; wrong direction

Examples

When the weather suddenly changed, our camping trip plans went awry.
If anything goes awry during the event, please let me know immediately.
Despite careful preparation, the project still went awry in the final stages.

Synset Examples

something has gone awry in our plans
something went badly amiss in the preparations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amiss - not quite right; wrong or out of place

Etymology

Probably from Middle English awry (a- 'on' + wry, 'twisted').
