lined

1. ADJ

/ laɪnd / (AmE), / laɪnd / (BrE)
having visible lines or wrinkles, especially referring to skin marked by age or emotion
having visible lines or wrinkles

Examples

Her grandfather's lined face told the story of a long and eventful life.
The mirror reflected his lined forehead as he frowned in concentration.
After years in the sun, the farmer's hands became rough and lined.

Synset Examples

their lined faces were immeasurably sad
a seamed face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrinkled - having lines or wrinkles, especially in skin
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even surface without lines or wrinkles

2. ADJ

/ laɪnd / (AmE), / laɪnd / (BrE)
having a border or edge formed by a row of objects, especially trees or similar items.
bordered by a row of objects

Examples

The city is famous for its tree-lined avenues and beautiful parks.
We walked down a lined pathway, with flowers on each side.
Many historic towns have shop-lined streets bustling with visitors.

Synset Examples

tree lined streets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bordered - having a border or edge
Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ laɪnd / (AmE), / laɪnd / (BrE)
having a lining or liner, often used in combination to describe something with an inner layer of material
having a lining or inner layer

Examples

She wore a lined coat to stay extra warm in winter.
These curtains are lined to help block out sunlight.
My favorite notebook has lined pages for easy note-taking.

Synset Examples

a lined skirt
a silk-lined jacket

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unlined - not having a lining or inner layer
