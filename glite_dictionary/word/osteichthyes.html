Osteichthyes

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːstiˈɪkθiˌiz / (AmE), / ˌɒstiˈɪkθiːz / (BrE)
A class of fish having a skeleton composed mainly of bone as well as cartilage, including most modern fish.
class of bony fish
medical

Examples

The Osteichthyes represent the largest class of vertebrates in the animal kingdom.
Unlike sharks, which have cartilage skeletons, members of the Osteichthyes have skeletons made mostly of bone.
Scientists study the evolution of Osteichthyes to understand how modern bony fishes developed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Chondrichthyes - class of cartilaginous fishes (sharks and rays)

Etymology

From Greek osteon (bone) + ikhthys (fish), referring to bony fish.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːstiˈɪkθiˌiz / (AmE), / ˌɒstiˈɪkθiˌiːz / (BrE)
a class of fish characterized by having a skeleton made mainly of bone rather than cartilage
class of bony fish
medical

Examples

The Osteichthyes include the vast majority of modern fish species.
Salmon, trout, and tuna are all classified within the Osteichthyes.
Unlike sharks, which have cartilage skeletons, the Osteichthyes possess true bone.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Chondrichthyes - a class of fish having skeletons made primarily of cartilage (cartilaginous fish)

Etymology

From Greek osteon (bone) + ichthys (fish).
