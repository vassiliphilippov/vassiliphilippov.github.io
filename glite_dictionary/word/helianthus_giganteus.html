Helianthus giganteus

1. NOUN

/ hiːliˈænθəs ʤaɪˈgæntiəs / (AmE), / hiːlɪˈænθəs dʒɪˈgantiːəs / (BrE)
A very tall perennial plant native to central and eastern North America, with edible tuberous roots; also known as the giant sunflower.
giant North American sunflower species

Examples

The Helianthus giganteus thrives in moist soils along riverbanks and wetlands.
Botanists studied Helianthus giganteus for its potential as a source of edible tubers.
Tall stalks of Helianthus giganteus made a striking display in the summer prairie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunflower - a tall plant with large yellow flowers and edible seeds

Etymology

From Latin: 'Helianthus' meaning 'sunflower' (from Greek 'hēlios' sun + 'anthos' flower); 'giganteus' meaning 'giant, huge'.
