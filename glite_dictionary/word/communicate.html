to communicate

1. VERB

/ kəˈmjunɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːnɪkeɪt / (BrE)
(of a Christian) to receive Holy Communion, particularly in the Catholic Church.
receive Holy Communion (Catholic context)

Examples

On Easter Sunday, many parishioners choose to communicate during the special Mass.
She communicated for the first time after her confirmation.
Those who had properly confessed were able to communicate at the service.

Etymology

From Latin communicare (to share, to make common), influenced by Ecclesiastical Latin communicare (to partake of the Eucharist).

/ kəˈmjunɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːnɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to share or exchange information, news, or ideas, especially by speaking, writing, or using some other medium
share or exchange information

Examples

to transmit (especially a disease or feeling) from one person or organism to another

Examples
