plunk

1. NOUN

/ plʌŋk / (AmE), / plʌŋk / (BrE)
a short, hollow, twanging sound, typically produced by plucking a string or striking a similar object
a hollow twanging sound

Examples

A sudden plunk rang out as she released the guitar string.
We heard the plunk of a pebble dropping into the pond.
Every time the ball hit the metal bucket, it made a distinct plunk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twang - a sharp, vibrating sound like that of a plucked string
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07404907-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ plʌŋk / (AmE), / plʌŋk / (BrE)
to pull or pluck lightly but sharply, especially a string on a musical instrument
pull or pluck sharply (musical context)

Examples

She plunked the bass guitar strings, letting the deep notes ring out.
He absentmindedly plunked on his ukulele while thinking of a new melody.
Each child took turns plunking a single note on the old piano.

Synset Examples

he plucked the strings of his mandolin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pluck - to pull something quickly and sharply, especially a string on an instrument
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01455157-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ plʌŋk / (AmE), / plʌŋk / (BrE)
to move or make a sound with a sharp, dull, or hollow thud, as a horse's hooves striking the ground
make a thudding sound or movement

Examples

The horse plunked along the dirt road, each hoof hitting the ground with a muffled thud.
She could hear the sound of hooves plunking outside her window just before dawn.
We watched as the carriage plunked steadily over the cobblestones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clop - make a sound like horses' hooves
  • Synonym: gallop - to run fast with a regular beating sound (of a horse)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02189411-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ plʌŋk / (AmE), / plʌŋk / (BrE)
(Baseball) A hit in which the ball drops suddenly after being struck, often softly.
baseball hit that drops suddenly

Examples

The batter managed a plunk that landed just beyond the pitcher's mound.
With a gentle plunk, the ball fell between the infielders and allowed a runner to advance.
Sometimes a well-placed plunk can be just as effective as a powerful drive.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00133039-n
Synset POS: n

5. VERB

/ plʌŋk / (AmE), / plʌŋk / (BrE)
to put or drop something or oneself down heavily and often with a loud sound
put down heavily with a noise

Examples

He plunked his suitcase on the floor and let out a sigh.
Sarah plunked herself down in the nearest chair after the long walk.
The kid plunked a handful of coins onto the counter.

Synset Examples

He planked the money on the table
He planked himself into the sofa

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plonk - to put something down heavily and noisily

Etymology

Imitative of the sound produced when something is dropped or struck; first recorded in the late 18th century.
