aggressor

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡrɛsər / (AmE), / əˈɡresə / (BrE)
a person, group, or country that attacks first or initiates hostile actions against another
person or group that starts an attack

Examples

The country acted as the aggressor in the conflict, launching attacks without provocation.
In playground disputes, it's important to distinguish the aggressor from the victim.
The treaty condemned any nation acting as an aggressor against its neighbors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attacker - a person who attacks
  • Antonym: defender - a person who defends against attack

Etymology

From Latin 'aggredi' meaning 'to attack' or 'to approach'.

2. NOUN

/ əˈɡrɛsər / (AmE), / əˈɡrɛsə / (BrE)
a person who initiates conflict or is the first to attack or act in a forceful, assertive way
person who initiates conflict or attack

Examples

The country claimed it was merely defending itself against a foreign aggressor.
In the debate, Mark played the role of the aggressor, always pushing his points forcefully.
The playground monitor intervened when one child became an aggressor towards the others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attacker - a person who attacks
  • Antonym: defender - a person who defends or protects

Etymology

From Latin 'aggredi' (to attack), via Middle French 'agresseur'.
