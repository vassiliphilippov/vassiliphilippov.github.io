Cinnamomum zeylanicum

1. NOUN

/ sɪˌnæməməm zeɪˈlæˌnɪkəm / (AmE), / sɪˌnæməməm zeɪˈlæˌnɪkəm / (BrE)
a tropical Asian evergreen tree with aromatic yellowish-brown bark, cultivated as the primary source of the spice cinnamon
tropical tree; main source of cinnamon

Examples

The bark of Cinnamomum zeylanicum is harvested to produce high-quality cinnamon.
Farmers in Sri Lanka grow Cinnamomum zeylanicum for its valuable aromatic bark.
Researchers studied the essential oils derived from Cinnamomum zeylanicum leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinnamon - the aromatic spice obtained from the bark of Cinnamomum zeylanicum

Etymology

From Latin Cinnamomum (genus name), from Greek kinnamōmon, and zeylanicum from New Latin, meaning 'of Ceylon' (Sri Lanka).
