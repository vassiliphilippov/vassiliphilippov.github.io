to certify

1. VERB

/ ˈsɝː.tə.faɪ / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.tɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
(of an official body) to officially declare someone to be legally insane.
to officially declare insane
legal

Examples

The court may certify a person as legally insane if warranted by medical evidence.
A psychiatrist must assess the patient before authorities can certify him.
She was officially certified after a lengthy legal process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to declare - To state something clearly and officially

Etymology

From Old French *certifier*, from Late Latin *certificare* (to make certain).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00823923-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈsɝː.tə.faɪ / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.tɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
(of a bank) to guarantee the payment of a check by verifying that sufficient funds are available and stamping the check as certified.
guarantee payment of a check

Examples

The bank will certify your check if you request it at the counter.
Before mailing the payment, he asked the teller to certify the check to reassure the landlord.
Once the bank certifies a check, the funds are set aside until it is cashed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guarantee - to promise or assure the fulfillment of a condition
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02354809-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈsɝː.tə.faɪ / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.tɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to officially guarantee that something meets a particular standard or has certain qualities
guarantee as meeting a standard

Examples

This product is certified organic by an independent agency.
The company can certify that all their electronics meet safety standards.
Only a licensed inspector can certify the building as earthquake-resistant.

Synset Examples

certified grade AAA meat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to verify - to make sure or demonstrate that something is true or accurate

Etymology

From Old French certifier, from Latin certificare, from certus (certain) + facere (to make).

5. VERB

/ ˈsɝː.təˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.tɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to provide evidence for or formally attest to something; to show or prove through one's behavior, attitude, or external attributes
attest or provide evidence for

Examples

The accountant will certify these financial statements as accurate and complete.
Her composure in the crisis seemed to certify her leadership abilities.
The diploma will certify that you have successfully completed the course.

  • Synonym: to demonstrate - to clearly show the existence or truth of something by giving proof or evidence
