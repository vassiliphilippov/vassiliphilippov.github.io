Sterope

1. PROPN

/ ˈstɛrəˌpi / (AmE), / ˈstɛrəʊpi / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, one of the seven Pleiades, the daughters of Atlas and Pleione.
one of the seven Pleiades in Greek mythology

Examples

In ancient stories, Sterope was considered one of the seven celestial sisters in Greek mythology.
The star associated with Sterope can be found in the constellation Taurus.
Many artists have depicted Sterope alongside her sisters, the Pleiades, in classical paintings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pleiades - the seven daughters of Atlas and Pleione in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Στερόπη (Sterópē), meaning 'lightning'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈstɛrəˌpi / (AmE), / ˈstɛrəʊpi / (BrE)
one of the stars in the Pleiades star cluster, also known as Asterope.
star in the Pleiades cluster

Examples

Ancient astronomers often referred to Sterope as one of the seven sisters in the night sky.
Through the telescope, Sterope appeared as a faint, shimmering point among the Pleiades.
Mythology names Sterope as one of Atlas's daughters, immortalized as a star.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pleiades - a star cluster in the constellation Taurus
  • Synonym: Asterope - alternative name for Sterope, a star in the Pleiades

Etymology

From Greek 'Steropē', meaning 'lightning', a figure in Greek mythology and one of the Pleiades sisters.
