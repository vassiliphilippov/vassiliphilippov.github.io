Vincetoxicum negrum

1. NOUN

/ vɪnsɛˈtɑksɪkəm ˈnɛɡrəm / (AmE), / vɪnsɪˈtɒksɪkəm ˈniːɡrəm / (BrE)
A species of twining vine with hairy leaves and dark purplish-brown flowers, belonging to the genus Vincetoxicum.
species of twining vine with dark flowers

Examples

The garden features a rare specimen of Vincetoxicum negrum climbing the trellis.
Botanists recorded the habitat preferences of Vincetoxicum negrum in the region.
The dark purplish-brown flowers of Vincetoxicum negrum make it easy to identify among similar vines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vincetoxicum - a genus of twining vines in the dogbane family

Etymology

From Latin: 'vincetoxicum' meaning 'poison conqueror'; 'negrum' meaning 'black', referring to the flower color.
