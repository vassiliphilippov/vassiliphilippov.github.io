fortunate

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɔrtʃənət / (AmE), / ˈfɔːtʃənət / (BrE)
having or resulting from unexpected good luck or fortune
having unexpected good luck

Examples

She was fortunate to find her lost keys just before leaving.
Only a few fortunate individuals won tickets to the concert.
We were fortunate that the storm missed our town.

Synset Examples

other, less fortunate, children died
a fortunate choice

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unlucky - having bad luck
  • Synonym: lucky - having good luck

Etymology

From Latin 'fortunatus' (prospered, blessed), from 'fortuna' (fortune).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɔrtʃənət / (AmE), / ˈfɔːtʃənət / (BrE)
having exceptionally good luck or being supremely favored by circumstances; very lucky
supremely lucky; extremely favored

Examples

She considered herself fortunate to have survived the accident without injury.
Only a fortunate few were able to attend the exclusive gala this year.
He was fortunate enough to be in the right place at the right time.

Synset Examples

golden lads and girls all must / like chimney sweepers come to dust
fortunate son

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lucky - having good luck or being favored by chance
  • Antonym: unlucky - not favored by luck; experiencing misfortune

Etymology

From Latin fortunatus (prospered, lucky), from fortuna (chance, luck).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00674174-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈfɔrtʃənət / (AmE), / ˈfɔːtʃənət / (BrE)
indicating or bringing a chance of good luck or a favorable outcome; presaging good fortune
indicating good luck; favorable

Examples

It was a fortunate choice that she invested early in the company.
The championship team's win was the result of several fortunate events.
It seemed like a fortunate sign when the rain stopped just before the ceremony.

Synset Examples

she made a fortunate decision to go to medical school
rosy predictions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lucky - having good luck or favorable circumstances
  • Antonym: unlucky - having bad luck

Etymology

From Latin fortunatus (prospered, lucky), past participle of fortunare (to make lucky), from fortuna (fortune).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
