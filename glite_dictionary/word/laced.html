laced

1. ADJ

/ leɪst / (AmE), / leɪst / (BrE)
fastened or closed with a lace, especially referring to shoes, boots, or garments
fastened with a lace

Examples

She wore a pair of laced boots that reached up to her knees.
Make sure your shoes are properly laced before running.
His laced sneakers provided a secure and comfortable fit.

Synset Examples

snugly laced shoes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unlaced - not fastened with a lace
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00255382-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ leɪst / (AmE), / leɪst / (BrE)
having an edge or streak of a different color woven or running through; edged or streaked with color.
having an edge or streak of color

Examples

The white tablecloth was laced with delicate threads of gold.
Many birds have feathers laced with shades of blue along the edges.
The designer chose curtains laced with silver to complement the room's decor.

Synset Examples

white blossoms with purple-laced petals

Etymology

From 'lace', ultimately from Old French 'laz', from Vulgar Latin 'lassus' (a cord or tie).
