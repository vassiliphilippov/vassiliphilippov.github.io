itch

1. VERB

/ ɪtʃ / (AmE), / ɪtʃ / (BrE)
to cause a tingling or irritating sensation on the skin that makes you want to scratch
to cause an itching sensation

Examples

My arm began to itch after I touched the plant.
Wool sweaters always itch me in the winter.
If your skin continues to itch, you should see a doctor.

Synset Examples

his skin itched

Related Terms

  • Antonym: scratch - to rub your skin with your nails because it itches

Etymology

From Old English 'giccan' (to itch).

2. NOUN

/ ɪtʃ / (AmE), / ɪtʃ / (BrE)
A contagious skin disease caused by the itch mite, characterized by persistent itching and skin irritation; also known as scabies.
contagious skin infection (scabies)
medical

Examples

Doctors diagnosed his rash as itch, which requires specific treatment.
The old medical texts warned sailors about outbreaks of itch during long voyages.
She suffered from severe itch after being exposed to contaminated bedding.

Synset Examples

he has a bad case of the itch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scabies - a contagious skin disease marked by itching and small raised red spots, caused by the itch mite

Etymology

From Old English *gicce* (itch), related to the verb 'itch'.
