pacha

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑː.tʃə / (AmE), / ˈpæ.tʃə / (BrE)
A high-ranking civil or military official in the former Ottoman Empire, particularly in Turkey or Egypt.
Ottoman civil or military official

Examples

The sultan appointed a new pacha to govern the province.
Several pachas played significant roles during the Ottoman Empire's expansion.
A pacha was present at the ceremonial event in Cairo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pasha - A high-ranking official in the Ottoman Empire

Etymology

Variant of 'pasha', from Turkish *paşa*, possibly from Persian *paadshah* (king or ruler).
