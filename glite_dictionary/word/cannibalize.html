to cannibalize

1. VERB

/ ˈkæn.ɪ.bəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkæn.ɪ.bə.laɪz / (BrE)
to eat human flesh; to consume members of one's own kind as food
eat human flesh

Examples

Some starving survivors were forced to cannibalize in order to stay alive.
There are historical accounts of sailors who had to cannibalize after a shipwreck.
The movie depicts a group of people who cannibalize in the face of extreme hunger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to devour - eat something eagerly and in large amounts

Etymology

From 'cannibal' (from Spanish 'caníbal', from Carib people reputed to eat human flesh) + '-ize'.

2. VERB

/ ˈkæn.ɪ.bəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkæn.ɪ.bə.laɪz / (BrE)
to take parts from one item, especially a machine or vehicle, to use them in repairing or building another similar item
take parts from one for use in another

Examples

The mechanic had to cannibalize some old cars to find the right parts for the engine.
They decided to cannibalize the broken computer to repair another one in the office.
During the restoration, she cannibalized a vintage radio for working components.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salvage - to rescue or save, especially parts from damaged goods

Etymology

From 'cannibal' + '-ize', originally referring metaphorically to people, later extended to objects.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01164459-v
Synset POS: v
