dentine

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛnˌtin / (AmE), / ˈdɛntiːn / (BrE)
The hard, dense, calcareous material beneath the enamel of a tooth that forms the main bulk of the tooth.
hard tissue forming bulk of a tooth
medical

Examples

The dentine was exposed after the enamel was worn away.
Fluoride helps protect the dentine as well as the enamel of teeth.
Cavities that are left untreated can eventually damage the dentine.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: enamel - the hard, outermost layer of a tooth
  • Degree Lower: pulp - the soft tissue at the center of a tooth

Etymology

From Latin 'dens, dentis' meaning 'tooth', via modern Latin 'dentina'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛnˌtin / (AmE), / ˈdɛntiːn / (BrE)
The hard, dense, calcified tissue forming the bulk of a tooth, beneath the enamel and surrounding the pulp cavity.
calcified tissue beneath tooth enamel
medical

Examples

The dentist explained that the cavity had reached the dentine of my tooth.
Exposure of the dentine can cause sensitivity to hot and cold foods.
Unlike enamel, dentine is capable of some repair if damaged.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: enamel - the hard, outer surface layer of a tooth

Etymology

From Latin 'dens, dentis' meaning 'tooth'.
