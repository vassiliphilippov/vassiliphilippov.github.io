Pityrogramma

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪtiəˈɡræmə / (AmE), / ˌpɪtiəˈɡræmə / (BrE)
a genus of terrestrial tropical ferns with fronds that have powdery yellowish or white undersides, sometimes classified in the families Polypodiaceae or Adiantaceae
genus of tropical ferns with powdery frond undersides

Examples

The Pityrogramma fern is easily recognized by the pale powder on the underside of its fronds.
Botanists have debated whether Pityrogramma should belong to the Polypodiaceae or Adiantaceae family.
In tropical gardens, Pityrogramma is prized for its decorative, silvery foliage.

Etymology

From Greek pitys (pine) + gramma (line, letter), referring to the fern's frond markings.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɪtiəɡroʊˈɡræmə / (AmE), / ˌpɪtɪəɡrəˈɡræmə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical terrestrial ferns characterized by fronds with powdery yellowish or white undersides, sometimes classified within the families Polypodiaceae or Adiantaceae.
a genus of tropical ferns

Examples

The Pityrogramma ferns are easily identified by the pale powdery coating on the underside of their leaves.
Botanists discovered a new species belonging to the Pityrogramma genus in the rainforests of South America.
Some gardeners appreciate Pityrogramma for its distinctive, silvery fronds in shaded areas.

Etymology

From Greek pitys (pine) and gramma (line, letter); denotes the lined or marked appearance of the fronds.
