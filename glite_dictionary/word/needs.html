needs

1. ADV

/ nidz / (AmE), / niːdz / (BrE)
Used with 'must' or similar verbs to indicate that something is necessary or unavoidable; necessarily (now somewhat archaic or literary).
necessarily; unavoidably (archaic/literary use)
archaic

Examples

We needs must accept the results, whether we like them or not.
If you needs go, be sure to return before dark.
Change needs come when the situation is desperate.

Synset Examples

it is necessarily so
we must needs be objective

Related Terms

  • Synonym: necessarily - in such a manner as could not be otherwise

Etymology

From Old English nēdes (of necessity), genitive of nēd (need).
