supercomputer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuː.pɚ.kəmˌpjuː.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈsuː.pə.kəmˌpjuː.tə / (BrE)
a very powerful computer that can process extremely large amounts of data at very high speeds, used especially for scientific and engineering calculations
extremely powerful computer

Examples

The new supercomputer will help scientists analyze massive amounts of climate data.
Universities compete for access to the world's fastest supercomputer.
A supercomputer can perform trillions of calculations per second, far outpacing typical computers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mainframe - a large, powerful computer used primarily by large organizations

Etymology

Formed from 'super-' (meaning 'above, beyond') and 'computer'.
