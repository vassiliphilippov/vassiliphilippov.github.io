tamed

1. ADJ

/ teɪmd / (AmE), / teɪmd / (BrE)
domesticated, controlled, or changed from a wild state to a manageable or less dangerous one
domesticated or made less wild

Examples

The formerly wild horses have been tamed by experienced trainers.
Decades of cultivation have tamed the rough countryside into productive farmland.
He was impressed by how the river was tamed with dams and canals.

Synset Examples

the once inhospitable landscape is now tamed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wild - living or growing in a natural state; not domesticated or controlled
  • Synonym: domesticated - tamed and kept as a pet or on a farm

2. ADJ

/ teɪmd / (AmE), / teɪmd / (BrE)
(of an animal, plant, or sometimes a person) brought from a wild or natural state into one that is domesticated or controlled.
brought from wildness into a domesticated state

Examples

After years of patient training, the tamed elephant now obeys simple commands.
These tamed foxes are comfortable around humans and live safely on the sanctuary grounds.
The tamed blueberry bushes produce more fruit than their wild counterparts.

Synset Examples

tame animals
fields of tame blueberries

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wild - living or growing in a natural state, not domesticated or controlled
  • Synonym: domesticated - (of an animal or plant) tamed and kept by humans for use or companionship
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02396713-a
Synset POS: a
