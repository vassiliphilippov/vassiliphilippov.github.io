Cyperaceae

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈpɛr.əˌsiː / (AmE), / sɪˌpɛə.rəˈsiː / (BrE)
a large family of grass-like monocotyledonous flowering plants, known as sedges, which includes bulrushes, papyrus, cotton grass, and chufa
family of grass-like sedge plants

Examples

Many wetland habitats are dominated by members of the Cyperaceae family.
Botanists often study the Cyperaceae to better understand marshland ecosystems.
The Cyperaceae include economically important plants such as papyrus and chufa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sedge - a grass-like plant in the family Cyperaceae
  • Synonym: Cyperus - a large genus of plants in the Cyperaceae family
  • Antonym: Poaceae - the botanical family of true grasses, as distinct from sedges

Etymology

From modern Latin Cyperaceae, based on the genus Cyperus (from ancient Greek kupeiros, meaning 'sedge').

2. NOUN

/ saɪpəˈreɪsiˌi / (AmE), / sɪˌpɛrəˈsiːiː / (BrE)
A large family of grass-like, monocotyledonous flowering plants commonly known as the sedge family, including bulrush, chufa, cotton grass, papyrus, and umbrella plant.
botanical family of sedges and similar plants

Examples

The Cyperaceae family includes many species found in wetlands and marshes worldwide.
Several important crops, such as chufa and papyrus, belong to the Cyperaceae.
Botanists studied the unique features of Cyperaceae to understand their ecological roles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sedge - any grass-like plant of the family Cyperaceae

Etymology

From New Latin *Cyperaceae*, based on the type genus *Cyperus*.
