razor

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪzər / (AmE), / ˈreɪzə / (BrE)
a sharp-edged tool used for shaving hair from the skin, especially the face or body
tool for shaving hair

Examples

He used a razor to carefully shave his beard in the morning.
Disposable razors are popular for travel and convenience.
She bought a new razor with multiple blades for a closer shave.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shaver - a person or device that removes hair by shaving

Etymology

From Middle English rasour, from Old French raseor, from raser (to scrape, shave).

2. VERB

/ ˈreɪ.zɚ / (AmE), / ˈreɪ.zə / (BrE)
to shave with a razor or to remove hair using a razor
shave with a razor

Examples

He razored his beard before the interview to look more professional.
She carefully razors her legs every morning as part of her routine.
The barber razored the back of his neck for a clean finish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shave - to remove hair from the body using a razor or another sharp tool
