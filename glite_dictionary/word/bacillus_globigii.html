Bacillus globigii

1. NOUN

/ bəˈsɪl.əs gloʊˈbɪdʒ.aɪ / (AmE), / bəˈsɪl.əs ˌɡləʊˈbɪdʒ.iː / (BrE)
a species of bacterium found in soil and decomposing organic matter, with some strains known to produce antibiotics
a species of soil-dwelling bacterium

Examples

Bacillus globigii is often used as a model organism in microbiology research.
Some strains of Bacillus globigii have been studied for their ability to produce antibiotics.
The team isolated Bacillus globigii from decaying plant material in the forest.

Etymology

From Latin 'bacillus' (small rod) and the species epithet 'globigii,' honoring German botanist Ferdinand Julius Cohn (Globig was his birthplace).
