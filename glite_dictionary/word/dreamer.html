dreamer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdriːmər / (AmE), / ˈdriːmə / (BrE)
a person who is experiencing a dream while asleep
person dreaming while asleep

Examples

The dreamer awoke suddenly, startled by a vivid nightmare.
Scientists study the brain activity of a dreamer during rapid eye movement sleep.
She watched her brother, the peaceful dreamer, sleeping soundly in his bed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleeper - a person who is sleeping

Etymology

From Middle English 'dremer', from 'dream' + '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdriːmər / (AmE), / ˈdriːmə / (BrE)
a person who lives in an imaginary world or who tends to escape reality by indulging in fantasy.
person who escapes into fantasy

Examples

As a dreamer, she often lost herself in thoughts of magical worlds far away.
He is a dreamer who sees possibilities where others see obstacles.
Many inventors begin as dreamers, imagining the impossible before making it real.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: realist - a person who accepts a situation as it is and prepares to deal with it accordingly

Etymology

From Middle English 'dremer', from 'dreamen' (to dream).

3. NOUN

/ ˈdriːmər / (AmE), / ˈdriːmə / (BrE)
a person who is guided more by ideals or imagination than by practical realities
person guided by ideals rather than practicality

Examples

She has always been a dreamer, imagining a world without conflict or pain.
Some call him a dreamer, but his bold ideas inspire those around him.
While his friends pursued steady jobs, he remained a dreamer chasing artistic visions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idealist - a person who is guided more by ideals than by practical considerations
  • Antonym: realist - a person who accepts a situation as it is and is prepared to deal with it accordingly

Etymology

From Old English 'drēam' (joy, music, mirth), later evolving to mean 'vision experienced in sleep'.
