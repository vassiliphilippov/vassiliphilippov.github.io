Formicariidae

1. NOUN

/ fɔrˌmɪkəˈraɪɪˌdiː / (AmE), / fɔːˌmɪkərɪˈɪːdɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of small to medium-sized insectivorous birds, commonly known as antbirds, native to Central and South America.
family of birds known as antbirds

Examples

The Formicariidae are well known for their association with army ants in tropical forests.
Many species within the Formicariidae feed by following swarms of ants to catch fleeing insects.
Ornithologists often debate the classification of members of the Formicariidae due to recent genetic studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antbird - Common name for a bird of the family Formicariidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Latin 'formica' (ant) plus -idae (zoological family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ fɔr.mɪˌkɛr.iˈaɪ.ə.di / (AmE), / fɔː.mɪˌkæɹ.ɪˈiː.diː / (BrE)
A family of small to medium-sized passerine birds found in Central and South America, commonly known as antbirds.
family of birds known as antbirds

Examples

The Formicariidae are predominantly insectivorous birds found in the understory of tropical forests.
Ornithologists have discovered many new species within the Formicariidae family.
Unlike some related families, members of the Formicariidae typically follow army ants to find prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antbird - common name for birds of the Formicariidae family

Etymology

From New Latin Formicariidae, derived from Formicarius (a genus of antbirds), from Latin formica (ant).
