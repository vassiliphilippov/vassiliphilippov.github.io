discordance

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈkɔrdəns / (AmE), / dɪsˈkɔːdəns / (BrE)
a harsh, unpleasant combination of sounds; lack of harmony
harsh mixture of sounds

Examples

The discordance of the orchestra tuning their instruments filled the hall.
There was a discordance in the sounds coming from the busy construction site.
As the band played out of sync, a harsh discordance overwhelmed the melody.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cacophony - a harsh, discordant mixture of sounds
  • Antonym: consonance - a combination of sounds that are harmonious

Etymology

From Old French 'discordance', from Latin 'discordare' (to disagree).

2. NOUN

/ dɪsˈkɔrdəns / (AmE), / dɪsˈkɔːdəns / (BrE)
strife or conflict resulting from a lack of agreement or harmony
lack of agreement; conflict

Examples

Political discordance between the two parties led to delays in passing vital legislation.
The discordance in their opinions caused ongoing tension during team meetings.
In families, discordance can arise when expectations and values clash.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: harmony - agreement or concord in situations or relationships
  • Synonym: discord - lack of agreement or harmony

Etymology

From Medieval Latin discordantia (disagreement), from Latin discordare.
