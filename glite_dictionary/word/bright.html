bright

1. ADJ

/ braɪt / (AmE), / braɪt / (BrE)
(of a person) quick and intelligent in learning; able to understand things easily.
quick to learn; intelligent

Examples

She is a bright child who always grasps new ideas quickly.
The bright students finished the assignment in half the time.
His teachers noticed how bright he was from a very young age.

Synset Examples

some children are brighter in one subject than another
smart children talk earlier than the average

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smart - having or showing a quick-witted intelligence
  • Antonym: dull - slow to learn or understand; not intelligent
