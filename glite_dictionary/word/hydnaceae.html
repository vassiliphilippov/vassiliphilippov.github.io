Hydnaceae

1. NOUN

/ hɪdˈneɪsiaɪ / (AmE), / hɪdˈneɪsiːiː / (BrE)
A family of tooth fungi characterized by fruiting bodies with spiny or toothed surfaces, belonging to the order Cantharellales.
family of tooth fungi
medical

Examples

Scientists collected several samples of Hydnaceae in the forest for further study.
The Hydnaceae are easily distinguished by the spiny structures on their fruiting bodies.
Some edible wild mushrooms belong to the Hydnaceae family.

Etymology

From scientific Latin, based on the type genus Hydnum and the suffix -aceae (family).

2. NOUN

/ hɪdˈneɪsi.iː / (AmE), / hɪdˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi known as tooth fungi, characterized by spore-bearing structures resembling teeth or spines rather than gills or pores.
a family of tooth fungi

Examples

The Hydnaceae are known for their unique spiny hymenophores instead of gills.
Several edible mushrooms belong to the Hydnaceae family.
Studies of the Hydnaceae have led to new insights in fungal taxonomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tooth fungus - fungus with tooth-like spore-bearing structures

Etymology

From the genus name Hydnum, ultimately from Greek 'hydnon' (truffle).
