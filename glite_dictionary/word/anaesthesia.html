anaesthesia

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəsˈθiʒə / (AmE), / ˌænəsˈθiːziə / (BrE)
Loss of sensation in all or part of the body, sometimes accompanied by loss of consciousness, especially as induced by medical means before surgery or other procedures.
loss of bodily sensation, often medically induced
medical british

Examples

Local anaesthesia was used during the dental procedure to numb the area.
The patient remained under anaesthesia for the entire duration of the surgery.
General anaesthesia can sometimes cause side effects such as nausea or dizziness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anesthesia - American spelling of 'anaesthesia'
  • Degree Lower: analgesia - absence of pain but not necessarily loss of all sensation

Etymology

From Greek anaisthetos (without sensation), from an- (without) + aisthēsis (sensation).
