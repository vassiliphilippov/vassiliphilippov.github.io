sinus paranasales

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪnəs ˌpærəˌneɪˈseɪliːz / (AmE), / ˈsaɪnəs ˌpærəˌneɪˈseɪliːz / (BrE)
Any of the paired air-filled cavities in the bones of the face, adjacent to the nasal cavity, lined with mucous membrane continuous with the lining of the nasal cavities.
paired air-filled sinuses near nasal cavity
medical

Examples

The sinus paranasales are susceptible to infection in cases of sinusitis.
Doctors examined the patient's sinus paranasales for signs of inflammation.
Computed tomography can help visualize abnormalities in the sinus paranasales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paranasal sinus - a sinuses located near the nasal cavity

Etymology

From Latin sinus paranasales, meaning 'paranasal sinuses'.
