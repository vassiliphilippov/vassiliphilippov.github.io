dwindling

1. NOUN

/ ˈdwɪndlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdwɪndlɪŋ / (BrE)
The process of gradually decreasing in size, amount, or strength.
gradual decrease or decline

Examples

The dwindling of the town's population worried the community leaders.
Many environmentalists are concerned about the dwindling of natural resources.
She felt a sense of loss at the dwindling of family traditions.

Synset Examples

there is no greater sadness that the dwindling away of a family

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decline - a gradual and continuous loss of strength, numbers, quality, or value
  • Antonym: increase - the process of becoming larger or greater over time

Etymology

From the verb 'dwindle', of obscure origin, possibly related to 'dwind' (dialectal variant).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdwɪndlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdwɪndlɪŋ / (BrE)
becoming gradually smaller, fewer, or less; diminishing steadily over time
gradually becoming less

Examples

The company is facing a dwindling supply of raw materials, threatening its production.
Attendance at the club has been dwindling for the past few months, causing concern among its members.
With their dwindling savings, they started looking for new sources of income.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dwindle - to become steadily less
  • Antonym: increasing - becoming greater in number or amount
  • Synonym: shrinking - becoming smaller in size or amount

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'dwindle', of uncertain origin, perhaps from Scots.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
