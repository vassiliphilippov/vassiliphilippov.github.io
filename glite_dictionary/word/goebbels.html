Goebbels

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡɜrbəlz / (AmE), / ˈɡɜːbəlz / (BrE)
(1897–1945) German politician; Minister of Propaganda in Nazi Germany, known for orchestrating propaganda campaigns and persecuting Jews.
Nazi German propaganda minister

Examples

Historians have studied Goebbels' strategies to understand the power of propaganda.
The museum exhibit featured speeches written by Goebbels for the Nazi regime.
Many books analyze how Goebbels manipulated media and public opinion in Germany.
