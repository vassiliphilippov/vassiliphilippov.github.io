Cuscuta gronovii

1. NOUN

/ kuːˈskuːtə ɡrəˈnoʊviːaɪ / (AmE), / kuːˈskuːtə ɡrəˈnəʊvɪaɪ / (BrE)
A species of leafless parasitic vine with dense clusters of small white bell-shaped flowers on orange-yellow stems, commonly found twining around plants such as clover or flax.
leafless parasitic vine with white flowers

Examples

Cuscuta gronovii can often be seen winding around wildflowers in wet meadows.
Scientists study the interactions between Cuscuta gronovii and its host plants to better understand parasitism in ecosystems.
The orange-yellow stems of Cuscuta gronovii make it easy to spot among the green vegetation.

Etymology

From the genus name Cuscuta (dodder), and the specific epithet honoring Dutch botanist Jan Frederik Gronovius.
