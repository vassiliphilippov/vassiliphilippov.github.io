apprentice

1. NOUN

/ əˈprɛn.tɪs / (AmE), / əˈprɛn.tɪs / (BrE)
a person who learns a trade or craft by working for a skilled employer over a fixed period
person learning a trade from an expert

Examples

After finishing school, she became an apprentice at a local bakery.
The apprentice watched carefully as the carpenter demonstrated the correct technique.
In medieval times, it was common for young people to work as an apprentice before becoming a master.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trainee - a person undergoing training for a particular job or profession

Etymology

From Old French 'aprentis', from late Latin 'apprenticium', from 'apprehendere' (to apprehend, take hold of).

2. VERB

/ əˈprɛn.tɪs / (AmE), / əˈprɛn.tɪs / (BrE)
to work as an apprentice to learn a trade or profession, usually under the guidance of a skilled practitioner
work as an apprentice

Examples

After college, she apprenticed with a famous sculptor to refine her craft.
He apprenticed as a carpenter before starting his own business.
Many young chefs apprentice at top restaurants to gain experience.

Synset Examples

She apprenticed with the great master

Related Terms

  • Synonym: train - to teach or learn a particular skill or type of behavior through instruction and practice
  • Synonym: mentor - to advise or train someone, especially a younger colleague

Etymology

From Old French aprentis (learner), from apprendre (to learn), from Latin apprehendere (to seize, grasp).
