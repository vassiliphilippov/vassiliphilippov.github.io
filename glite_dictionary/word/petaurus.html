Petaurus

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈtɔrəs / (AmE), / pɪˈtɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of small, gliding marsupials found in Australia and New Guinea, known as flying phalangers or wrist-winged gliders.
genus of gliding marsupials

Examples

The sugar glider is a member of the genus Petaurus and is native to Australia.
Researchers have studied the unique gliding membranes of Petaurus species.
Several animals in the Petaurus genus are popular as exotic pets due to their playful nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glider - an animal able to glide, especially small mammals that use flaps of skin to glide between trees

Etymology

From Latin *petaurus* (rope dancer, acrobat), referring to their gliding ability.

2. NOUN

/ pɪˈtɔːrəs / (AmE), / pɪˈtɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of small marsupial mammals in the family Phalangeridae, known for their gliding ability using membranes stretching from their forelegs to hindlegs.
a genus of gliding marsupials

Examples

The Petaurus genus includes the well-known sugar glider, popular as an exotic pet.
Several species within Petaurus can glide between trees using membranes along their limbs.
Scientists have studied the ecology and behavior of Petaurus species in Australian forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sugar glider - a small, nocturnal gliding marsupial (Petaurus breviceps)

Etymology

From New Latin Petaurus, from Latin petaurus (a rope-dancer's stage, leaping board), from Greek petauros (springboard).
