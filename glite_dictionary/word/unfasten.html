to unfasten

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈfæsn̩ / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɑːsn̩ / (BrE)
(of something that is tied, attached, or secured) to become undone or open; to lose its fastening.
to become undone or untied

Examples

After walking for a while, her necklace began to unfasten and eventually fell off.
If the straps on your backpack unfasten, your belongings might spill out.
The curtains started to unfasten from the rod during the storm.

Synset Examples

The shoelaces unfastened

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to fasten - to make something closed or secure

Etymology

From un- (not) + fasten (to make secure), originally from Old English fæstnian.

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈfæsn̩ / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɑːsn̩ / (BrE)
to make something become undone or not fixed, especially by releasing a fastening or closure
to make something undone or loose

Examples

Please unfasten your seat belt before leaving your seat.
She quickly unfastened the buttons on her coat as she entered the warm room.
He paused to unfasten the clasp of the necklace.

Synset Examples

unfasten your belt

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to fasten - to attach or secure something firmly
  • Synonym: to undo - to open or loosen something that was fastened

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'fasten' (to make secure), originally from Old English 'fæstnian'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01346975-v
Synset POS: v
