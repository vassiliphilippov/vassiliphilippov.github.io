exudate

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛɡ.zjəˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛks.juː.deɪt / (BrE)
a substance, such as fluid or sap, that oozes out from pores or cells of plants or animals, typically as a result of injury or inflammation
fluid or substance oozed from cells
medical

Examples

The tree's bark was covered in a sticky exudate after it was damaged.
Doctors collected the exudate from the infected wound for analysis.
Some plants release a milky exudate when their leaves are cut.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secretion - a substance produced and discharged by a cell or gland

Etymology

From Latin exsudatum (something exuded), from exsudare ‘to sweat out’.
