low-tension

1. ADJ

/ ˌloʊˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˌləʊˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
(of electrical equipment or lines) designed to operate or operating at a relatively low voltage.
operating at low voltage (electricity)

Examples

The electrician installed low-tension wiring throughout the building for safety reasons.
Most household appliances are designed to work with low-tension circuits.
The diagram clearly marked the low-tension cables in blue and the high-tension ones in red.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: high-tension - designed to operate at high voltage (electricity)

Etymology

Formed from 'low' (of small degree) + 'tension', an old term for 'voltage'.
