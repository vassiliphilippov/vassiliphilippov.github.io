weighty

1. ADJ

/ ˈweɪti / (AmE), / ˈweɪti / (BrE)
causing serious worry or anxiety; important and difficult to deal with
causing anxiety or concern

Examples

She faced several weighty decisions that would impact her future.
The committee discussed a number of weighty issues during the meeting.
His mind was occupied by weighty concerns about his family's wellbeing.

Synset Examples

weighty problems

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serious - important and requiring careful consideration
  • Antonym: trivial - of little value or importance, not serious
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01193711-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈweɪti / (AmE), / ˈweɪti / (BrE)
having great influence, power, or force; strongly persuasive or important
having strong influence or persuasiveness

Examples

The lawyer made a weighty case in favor of his client.
Her weighty arguments eventually convinced the entire board.
It was a weighty decision that influenced the company’s future.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cogent - clear, logical, and convincing
  • Synonym: compelling - evoking interest, attention, or admiration in a powerfully irresistible way
  • Antonym: weak - lacking force or influence

Etymology
