Syringa josikaea

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈrɪŋɡə joʊˈsɪkiˌiːə / (AmE), / sɪˈrɪŋɡə jɒˈsɪkiːə / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to central Europe, with elliptic leaves and upright clusters of lilac or deep violet flowers; also known as the Hungarian lilac.
a central European lilac shrub

Examples

The garden featured a lovely Syringa josikaea that bloomed every spring.
Botanists identified the shrub as Syringa josikaea because of its upright clusters of violet flowers.
They planted a Syringa josikaea to add color and fragrance to their backyard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hungarian lilac - Another common name for Syringa josikaea

Etymology

From modern Latin; genus name 'Syringa' from Greek 'syrinx' (pipe), possibly referring to its hollow stems; 'josikaea' honors the Hungarian botanist Margit Jósika.
