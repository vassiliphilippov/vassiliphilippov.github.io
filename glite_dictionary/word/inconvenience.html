inconvenience

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnkənˈviːniəns / (AmE), / ˌɪnkənˈviːniəns / (BrE)
the quality or state of causing difficulty, discomfort, or lack of convenience
being difficult or not convenient

Examples

The closure of the main road was a major inconvenience to local residents.
We apologize for any inconvenience this delay may have caused.
Although it was only a minor inconvenience, it still disrupted my day.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: convenience - the quality of being useful or convenient

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (not) + 'convenientia' (agreement, suitability).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.kənˈviː.ni.əns / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kənˈviː.ni.əns / (BrE)
A difficulty or problem that causes discomfort, trouble, or anxiety.
a difficulty or trouble

Examples

We apologize for any inconvenience this delay may have caused you.
The construction outside his apartment was a constant inconvenience to residents.
She decided to help despite the inconvenience to her own schedule.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: convenience - the state of being able to proceed easily or with little effort
  • Synonym: discomfort - a state of physical or mental unease
  • Synonym: nuisance - something that causes inconvenience or annoyance

Etymology

From Latin 'inconveniens' (unsuitable, inappropriate), from 'in-' (not) + 'conveniens' (suitable).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04718711-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪnkənˈviːniəns / (AmE), / ˌɪnkənˈviːniəns / (BrE)
A state or fact of causing trouble, discomfort, or difficulty; something that is inconvenient.
trouble or discomfort caused to someone

Examples

We apologize for any inconvenience this delay may have caused.
Due to maintenance work, some inconvenience is expected for passengers.
He considered it a minor inconvenience compared to the benefits gained.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: convenience - the state of being able to proceed with ease or little effort
  • Degree Higher: hardship - a severe suffering or discomfort
  • Degree Lower: annoyance - a mild irritation or inconvenience

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + convenientia (agreement, suitability).

Source: WordNet

4. VERB

/ ˌɪnkənˈviːniəns / (AmE), / ˌɪnkənˈviːniəns / (BrE)
to cause trouble or difficulty to someone; to make things less convenient or more difficult for someone
cause trouble or difficulty to

Examples

I don't want to inconvenience you, so please let me know what time works best.
Due to the repairs, some residents were inconvenienced by a temporary loss of water service.
She apologized for inconveniencing her guests with the last-minute change of plans.

Synset Examples

Sorry to trouble you, but …

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trouble - to cause distress or inconvenience to
  • Synonym: bother - to take the trouble to do something, often causing inconvenience
  • Antonym: help - to make it easier or more convenient for someone

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (not) + 'convenientia' (agreeableness, suitability).
