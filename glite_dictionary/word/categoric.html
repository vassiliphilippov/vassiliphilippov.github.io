categoric

1. ADJ

/ ˌkætəˈɡɔrɪk / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈɡɒrɪk / (BrE)
expressed in a clear and definite way, without any possibility of doubt or qualification; unconditional.
unconditional; without qualification

Examples

She issued a categoric statement denying all allegations made against her.
The witness gave a categoric answer, leaving no room for misinterpretation.
He made a categoric refusal to participate in the scheme.

Synset Examples

a categorical denial
a flat refusal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: categorical - absolute, without qualifications or reservations
  • Antonym: qualified - limited or modified in some way

Etymology

From Greek katēgorikos (accusatory), via Late Latin categoricus. See also 'categorical'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌkætəˈɡɔrɪk / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈɡɒrɪk / (BrE)
relating to or included in a category or categories.
relating to a category or categories

Examples

The survey results were organized into categoric groups for analysis.
Only categoric distinctions were considered in this classification.
Her research focuses on categoric variables in statistical models.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: categorical - unambiguously explicit and direct; also relating to a category

Etymology

From Greek kategorikos, from kategoria (category).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02694742-a
Synset POS: a
