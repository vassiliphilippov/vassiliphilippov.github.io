proconsular

1. ADJ

/ proʊˈkɑːn.səl.ɚ / (AmE), / prəʊˈkɒn.sjʊ.lə / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a proconsul, especially in administrative or governmental roles
relating to a proconsul

Examples

The governor wielded proconsular powers throughout the province.
A proconsular edict was issued to enforce Roman law in the region.
His proconsular office was headquartered in the city’s administrative quarter.

Synset Examples

proconsular offices

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proconsul - a governor of a province in ancient Rome, with the authority of a consul

Etymology

From Latin proconsularis, from proconsul (proconsul).
