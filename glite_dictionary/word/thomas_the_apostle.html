Thomas the Apostle

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɑməs ði əˈpɑsəl / (AmE), / ˈtɒməs ði əˈpɒsl̩ / (BrE)
one of the twelve Apostles of Jesus, known for doubting Jesus' resurrection until he saw Jesus for himself
Apostle known for doubting the resurrection

Examples

Many people refer to Thomas the Apostle when talking about doubt and faith.
In paintings, Thomas the Apostle is often shown touching the wounds of the risen Jesus.
The story of Thomas the Apostle is found in the Gospel of John.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Doubting Thomas - a person who refuses to believe something without direct evidence; based on Thomas the Apostle
