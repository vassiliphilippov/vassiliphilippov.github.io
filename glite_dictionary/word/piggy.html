piggy

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɪɡi / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡi / (BrE)
Resembling a pig in appearance or manner, especially by being gluttonous, greedy, or coarse.
resembling a pig; gluttonous or greedy

Examples

He grabbed the last slice of pizza with a piggy grin.
The children were scolded for their piggy behavior at the buffet.
Her piggy appetite amazed everyone at the family dinner.

Synset Examples

piggish table manners
the piggy fat-cheeked little boy and his porcine pot-bellied father
swinish slavering over food

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piggish - resembling a pig; greedy or coarse
  • Synonym: porcine - of, relating to, or resembling a pig
  • Synonym: swinish - of or like swine; coarse or brutish

Etymology

From 'pig' + diminutive suffix '-y'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪɡi / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡi / (BrE)
A young pig, often used as a term of endearment or in children's language.
a young pig

Examples

The children fed the little piggy some scraps from the kitchen.
Each piggy in the pen had a different color spot on its back.
She watched as the mother pig cared for her newborn piggy in the barn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piglet - a young pig

Etymology

Diminutive form of 'pig', with '-y' or '-ie' suffix used for affectionate or childlike terms.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02398346-n
Synset POS: n
