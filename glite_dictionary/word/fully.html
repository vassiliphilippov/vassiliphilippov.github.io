fully

1. ADV

/ ˈfʊli / (AmE), / ˈfʊli / (BrE)
completely or to the greatest extent; more than adequately or sufficiently.
completely; more than adequately

Examples

The instructions must be fully understood before you begin the experiment.
She was fully recovered after two weeks of rest.
I can't fully agree with your assessment of the situation.

Synset Examples

the evidence amply (or fully) confirms our suspicions
they were fully (or amply) fed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: completely - totally; to the full extent
  • Antonym: partly - not completely; to some extent but not whole

Etymology

From Old French 'follement' (foolishly, madly), from 'fol' (foolish, mad); sense shifted to 'completely' in Middle English.

2. ADV

/ ˈfʊli / (AmE), / ˈfʊli / (BrE)
completely or entirely; to the greatest extent or degree possible.
completely; entirely

Examples

She didn't fully understand the instructions, so she asked for clarification.
The house was fully furnished and ready for the new tenants.
Make sure the project is fully completed before the deadline.

Synset Examples

fully grown
he didn't fully understand
knew full well
full-grown
full-fledged

Related Terms

  • Synonym: completely - to the greatest extent; totally
  • Antonym: partially - in part; not completely

Etymology

From Middle English 'fulliche', from Old English 'fullīċe' (in full manner).
