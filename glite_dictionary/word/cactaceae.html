Cactaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌkækˈteɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌkækˈtæsiː / (BrE)
the family of flowering plants commonly known as cacti, characterized by succulent stems adapted to arid environments
the botanical family of cacti

Examples

The Cactaceae family includes a wide variety of drought-resistant plants native to the Americas.
Botanists are continually discovering new genera within the Cactaceae family.
Many members of the Cactaceae have spines instead of true leaves as an adaptation to their environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cactus - a plant belonging to the family Cactaceae, typically with thick, fleshy stems that store water

Etymology

From the Latin plural of 'Cactus', from Greek 'kaktos', a spiny plant.
