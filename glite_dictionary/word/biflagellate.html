biflagellate

1. ADJ

/ ˌbaɪfləˈdʒɛlət / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪfləˈdʒɛlət / (BrE)
(of a cell or organism) having two flagella.
having two flagella

Examples

The algae produced a biflagellate zoospore during reproduction.
Certain protozoa are biflagellate, possessing exactly two flagella on their cell body.
Under the microscope, the biflagellate nature of the swimming cell was clearly visible.

Synset Examples

a biflagellate zoospore

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'bi-' (two) + 'flagellum' (whip, flagellum).
