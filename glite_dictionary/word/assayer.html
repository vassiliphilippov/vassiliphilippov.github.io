assayer

1. NOUN

/ əˈseɪ.ər / (AmE), / əˈseɪ.ə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to analyze and test metals, especially to determine their quality and composition
person who tests and analyzes metals

Examples

The gold was sent to an assayer for testing before it was sold.
As an experienced assayer, she could quickly determine the purity of precious metals.
The mining company relied on the assayer's report to price its silver ore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analyst - a person who conducts detailed examination of something, often in a laboratory

Etymology

From Middle French 'assaier', from Old French 'assaier' (to try, test), from Late Latin 'exagiare'.
