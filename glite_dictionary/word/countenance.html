countenance

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntənəns / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntənəns / (BrE)
a person's face or facial expression, especially as an indication of mood or character
a person's face or facial expression

Examples

She entered the room with a calm countenance, hiding her true feelings.
His angry countenance discouraged further questions from the staff.
There was a solemn look on her countenance during the ceremony.

Synset Examples

a pleasant countenance
a stern visage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: visage - a person's face, with reference to the form or proportions of the features

Etymology

From Old French 'contenance' (behavior, bearing), from Latin 'continentia' (restraint).

4. VERB

/ ˈkaʊn.tənəns / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.tɪ.nəns / (BrE)
to permit, approve of, or give support to something, especially in a formal or official way
to permit or approve

Examples

I cannot countenance such reckless behavior in the workplace.
The board refused to countenance any further delay in the project.
She would never countenance lying, not even to protect a friend.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permit - to allow something to happen
  • Synonym: to allow - to let someone do something

Etymology
