keloid

1. NOUN

/ ˈkiː.lɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈkiː.lɔɪd / (BrE)
A raised, thickened area of scar tissue that forms at the site of an injury due to excessive tissue repair.
raised, thick scar at injury site
medical

Examples

The surgeon warned that a keloid might form after the procedure.
She consulted a dermatologist to treat the keloid on her shoulder.
People with darker skin are more likely to develop keloids after injuries.

Etymology

From Greek 'kheloeidēs' (tumor resembling a crab's claw), from 'khelē' (claw).
