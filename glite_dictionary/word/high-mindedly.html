high-mindedly

1. ADV

/ ˌhaɪˈmaɪndɪdli / (AmE), / ˌhaɪˈmaɪndɪdli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows strong moral principles or idealism.
in a morally principled or idealistic manner

Examples

She spoke high-mindedly about justice and fairness.
They high-mindedly rejected all forms of corruption in their organization.
The judge delivered his verdict high-mindedly, focusing on ethics above all else.

Synset Examples

he talks high-mindedly, but we don't know whether he acts according to his principles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: high-minded - having strong moral principles; lofty in ideals
  • Antonym: cynically - in a distrustful or skeptical manner about human motives
