ophthalmoscopy

1. NOUN

/ ɑfˌθælˈmɑskəpi / (AmE), / ɒfˌθælˈmɒskəpi / (BrE)
the examination of the interior of the eye using an ophthalmoscope, often to diagnose eye diseases
examination of the eye's interior
medical

Examples

The doctor performed ophthalmoscopy to check for signs of retinal damage.
With regular ophthalmoscopy, early symptoms of glaucoma can often be detected.
During the eye examination, ophthalmoscopy revealed swelling of the optic nerve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ophthalmoscope - an instrument for examining the interior of the eye

Etymology

From Greek 'ophthalmos' (eye) + 'skopein' (to look or examine).
