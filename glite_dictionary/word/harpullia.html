harpullia

1. NOUN

/ hɑrˈpuːliə / (AmE), / hɑːˈpjuːliə / (BrE)

fast-growing tree from India/East Indies

The wood from the harpullia is prized for its strength and durability in construction.

2. NOUN

/ hɑːrˈpʊl.i.ə / (AmE), / hɑːˈpʊl.i.ə / (BrE)
Any of various trees of the genus Harpullia, native to tropical Asia and Australia, known for their ornamental foliage and often colorful fruit.
genus of tropical trees

Examples

The harpullia is prized in gardens for its attractive orange seed capsules.
Several species of harpullia are cultivated in Australia as ornamental street trees.
The leaves of the harpullia are glossy and provide dense shade.

Etymology

From New Latin Harpullia, the scientific name of the genus.
