fretwork

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɛtˌwɝk / (AmE), / ˈfrɛtˌwɜːk / (BrE)
ornamental design made by cutting patterns into thin strips of wood, metal, or other materials, often used for decoration in architecture and furniture
ornamental design of cut wood or metal

Examples

The porch was adorned with intricate fretwork along its railings.
You can see fine Victorian fretwork on the old library's facade.
She carefully traced the pattern of the wooden fretwork with her fingers.

Etymology

From French 'freter' (to interlace), influenced by Old French 'frete' (thin strip, lattice).
