Catholic

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæθəlɪk / (AmE), / ˈkæθlɪk / (BrE)
a member of the Roman Catholic Church, or sometimes of any Catholic church
member of the Roman Catholic Church

Examples

She has been a devout Catholic all her life.
Many Catholics attend Mass every Sunday.
The new bishop addressed the concerns of the local Catholic community.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Protestant - a member of the Protestant churches; religious antonym
  • Antonym: Orthodox - a member of the Eastern Orthodox churches

Etymology

From Greek *katholikos* (universal), via Latin *catholicus*.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkæθəlɪk / (AmE), / ˈkæθlɪk / (BrE)
Relating to the Roman Catholic Church or its members; supporting or characteristic of Catholicism.
relating to the Roman Catholic Church

Examples

She was raised in a Catholic family and attends Mass every Sunday.
Many schools with a Catholic ethos emphasize religious education.
The new pope introduced reforms to modernize the Catholic Church.

Synset Examples

the Catholic Church

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Protestant - relating to Protestantism or its members
  • Synonym: Catholicism - the faith, practice, and church order of the Roman Catholic Church

Etymology

From Greek *katholikos* (universal), later adopted in Latin as *catholicus*; used to denote universal Christian doctrine, and later, specifically, the Roman Catholic Church.
