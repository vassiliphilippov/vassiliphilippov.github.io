phragmocone

1. NOUN

/ ˈfræɡ.məˌkoʊn / (AmE), / ˈfræɡ.məkəʊn / (BrE)
the thin, conical, chambered internal shell of a belemnite or other certain extinct cephalopods, either straight or curved in form
chambered shell of a belemnite

Examples

The fossilized phragmocone of the belemnite revealed its internal chambered structure.
Paleontologists examined the curvature of the phragmocone to identify the species.
A well-preserved phragmocone can provide insights into the habitat of ancient cephalopods.

Etymology

From Greek *phragma* (fence, partition) + *konos* (cone).
