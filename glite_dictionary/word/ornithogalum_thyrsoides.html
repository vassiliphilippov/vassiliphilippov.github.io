Ornithogalum thyrsoides

1. NOUN

/ ɔrˌnɪθəˌɡæləm θɪrˈsɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / ɔːˌnɪθəˌɡæləm θɜːˈsɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A South African perennial plant with long spikes of white flowers, commonly grown for use as cut flowers.
a South African perennial with white flower spikes

Examples

Ornithogalum thyrsoides is popular in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.
Gardeners value Ornithogalum thyrsoides for its ability to produce flowers through the winter months.
Many florists import Ornithogalum thyrsoides bulbs from South Africa to cultivate as cut flowers.

Etymology

From Greek 'ornitho-' (bird) and 'galum' (milk), forming the genus name, with the species name 'thyrsoides' meaning 'thyrsus-like' or 'having clustered flowers'.
