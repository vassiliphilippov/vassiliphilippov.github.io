Citellus lateralis

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈtɛləs ləˈtɛrəlɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈtɛləs ləˈtɛərəlɪs / (BrE)
a common black-striped, reddish-brown ground squirrel of western North America, resembling a large chipmunk
a North American ground squirrel species

Examples

The Citellus lateralis is often mistaken for a chipmunk because of its black stripes.
Researchers observed a group of Citellus lateralis gathering food before winter.
Hikers in the Rockies frequently spot the Citellus lateralis darting between rocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden-mantled ground squirrel - North American ground squirrel with golden band above its stripes, common name for Citellus lateralis
  • Degree Lower: chipmunk - small striped squirrel-like rodent of North America, often confused with Citellus lateralis

Etymology

From Latin: 'citellus' meaning ground squirrel and 'lateralis' meaning on the side, referring to the side stripes.
