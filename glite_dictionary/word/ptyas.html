Ptyas

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪ.əs / (AmE), / ˈtaɪ.əs / (BrE)
A genus of non-venomous snakes, commonly known as Asian rat snakes, found in Asia.
genus of Asian rat snakes

Examples

The Ptyas species are among the largest rat snakes found in Asia.
Researchers observed a Ptyas climbing a tree in search of prey.
Unlike some snakes, the Ptyas is not venomous and poses little threat to humans.

Etymology

From Greek 'ptyas' (spitter), previously believed some species spit venom.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪ.əs / (AmE), / ˈtiː.as / (BrE)
a genus of Asian rat snakes known for their slender bodies and swift movements, including species such as the Oriental rat snake
a genus of Asian rat snakes

Examples

The Ptyas genus includes some of the largest rat snakes found in Asia.
Scientists observed the long, agile body of a Ptyas slithering through the grasslands.
The Oriental rat snake, a member of the Ptyas genus, is common in India.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rat snake - a common name for nonvenomous snakes of the subfamily Colubrinae

Etymology

From Greek *ptyas* (spitter), referring to spit or spitting, though members of this genus do not actually spit.
