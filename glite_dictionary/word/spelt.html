spelt

1. NOUN

/ spɛlt / (AmE), / spɛlt / (BrE)
a species of hardy wheat with a tough husk, grown chiefly in Europe for food and animal feed
a type of hardy wheat

Examples

Spelt flour is often used as an alternative to regular wheat flour in baking.
Many organic farmers choose to grow spelt because it is more disease-resistant.
Livestock are sometimes fed with spelt rather than common wheat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wheat - a cereal plant cultivated for its grain

Etymology

From Old English 'spelt', from Proto-Germanic *spiltō.

2. NOUN

/ spɛlt / (AmE), / spɛlt / (BrE)
a hardy variety of wheat with a tough husk, grown mainly in Europe as a food crop and for livestock feed
a variety of wheat, used as food and feed

Examples

Some artisan bakeries use spelt flour to produce rustic breads with a nutty flavor.
The farmer planted a field of spelt to feed his livestock during the winter months.
People with certain wheat sensitivities sometimes find spelt easier to digest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wheat - a cereal plant cultivated for its grain, a staple food worldwide

Etymology

From Old English 'spelt', from Late Latin 'spelta', of Germanic origin.

3. NOUN

/ spɛlt / (AmE), / spɛlt / (BrE)
a type of hardy wheat (Triticum spelta) grown mainly in Europe for livestock feed and also used for human consumption
a type of wheat grain

Examples

Ancient farmers in Europe often grew spelt because of its resilience to harsh climates.
More bakeries are using spelt flour due to its distinct nutty flavor.
Most of the spelt harvested in the region is used as livestock feed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wheat - a cereal plant cultivated for its grain

Etymology

From Old English spelt, from Proto-Germanic *spiltiz, related to German 'Spelz'.
