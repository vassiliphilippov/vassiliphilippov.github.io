GIS

1. NOUN

/ dʒi aɪ ɛs / (AmE), / dʒiː aɪ ɛs / (BrE)
An information system designed to capture, store, manipulate, analyze, manage, and present geographical or spatial data.
system for geographic data analysis

Examples

Many cities now rely on a GIS to plan transportation networks and manage resources.
The environmental study used a GIS to map habitat changes over the past decade.
Learning how to use a GIS has become essential for careers in urban planning.

Synset Examples

This operator was applied to a mobile technology GIS application.
We have defined new operators specific to the GIS domain and a way to apply them to a SUT.
In addition, we have tested the operators defined in two GIS applications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geographic information system - full form of GIS; system for handling geographic data

Etymology

Acronym for Geographic Information System.
