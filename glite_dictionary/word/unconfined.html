unconfined

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnkənˈfaɪnd / (AmE), / ˌʌnkənˈfaɪnd / (BrE)
not restricted, restrained, or kept within limits; free or open.
not restricted or limited

Examples

The river flowed unconfined across the plains after the heavy rains.
He valued an unconfined space for his creative work.
The prisoners enjoyed a brief period of unconfined movement during the exercise hour.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: confined - kept within limits; restricted
  • Synonym: free - not confined, controlled, or limited

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'confined' (past participle of confine; from Latin 'confinare', to border).

2. ADJ

/ ʌn.kənˈfaɪnd / (AmE), / ʌn.kənˈfaɪnd / (BrE)
not restricted, enclosed, or limited; free from confinement or restraint.
not confined or restrained

Examples

The rescued birds were finally unconfined and released into the wild.
She felt unconfined joy during her long walk on the beach.
The gas spreads rapidly in an unconfined space.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: free - not under physical restraint; able to move or act as desired
  • Antonym: confined - restricted in movement or enclosed within bounds

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + confined, from Latin confīnare (to border, limit).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01067327-s
Synset POS: s
