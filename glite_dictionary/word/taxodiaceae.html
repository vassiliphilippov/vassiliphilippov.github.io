Taxodiaceae

1. NOUN

/ tækˌsoʊdiˈeɪsiˌaɪ / (AmE), / takˌsəʊdiˈeɪsiːˌaɪ / (BrE)
a family of coniferous trees, formerly classified as a separate family, now often included in Cupressaceae in modern botanical classification
family of coniferous trees

Examples

The Taxodiaceae include several notable species such as the bald cypress and redwood.
Recent studies reclassified Taxodiaceae as part of the Cupressaceae family.
Botanists once regarded Taxodiaceae as a distinct family of coniferous trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cupressaceae - the cypress family, in which Taxodiaceae is now often included

Etymology

From modern Latin, formed from the genus Taxodium plus the family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ tækˌsoʊdiˈeɪsi.i / (AmE), / tækˌsəʊdɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a group of coniferous trees formerly regarded as a family but now usually treated as a subfamily (Taxodioideae) within the Cupressaceae; includes genera such as Taxodium and Metasequoia
group of coniferous trees (taxonomic subfamily)

Examples

The Taxodiaceae include well-known members like the bald cypress and dawn redwood.
Recent classifications place Taxodiaceae as a subfamily within Cupressaceae rather than an independent family.
Botanists debated for decades whether Taxodiaceae should be a separate family or part of another group.

Etymology

From genus Taxodium + Latin suffix -aceae, denoting a family in botanical taxonomy.
