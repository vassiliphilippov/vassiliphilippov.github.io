Blattidae

1. NOUN

/ blæˈtɪdiː / (AmE), / blæˈtɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of cockroaches that includes many domestic species commonly found in human dwellings.
family of domestic cockroaches
medical

Examples

Several species within the Blattidae family are common household pests worldwide.
Researchers studied the behavior of Blattidae under different environmental conditions.
The exterminator identified the infestation as belonging to the Blattidae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockroach - an insect of the order Blattodea, known for infesting human dwellings

Etymology

From New Latin Blattidae, from 'Blatta', type genus, + suffix '-idae' (denoting animal families).

2. NOUN

/ blæˈtɪdiː / (AmE), / bləˈtɪdiː / (BrE)

family of domestic cockroaches

Examples

The insect specimens collected from the kitchen all belonged to the Blattidae family.
Several species in the Blattidae group are common household pests found worldwide.
Entomologists study the Blattidae family to understand domestic cockroach behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockroach - a common insect; several species belong to Blattidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Blattidae', based on 'Blatta' (cockroach genus).
