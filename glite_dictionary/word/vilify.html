to vilify

1. VERB

/ ˈvɪl.əˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈvɪl.ɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to speak or write about someone or something in an abusively disparaging manner; to spread negative or damaging information about someone
to speak or write abusively about someone

Examples

Many tabloids vilify celebrities to attract more readers.
The politician claimed that his opponents tried to vilify his character during the campaign.
It is wrong to vilify someone without knowing the full story.

Synset Examples

The Nazi propaganda vilified the Jews

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to defame - to damage the reputation of someone by false statements
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration of someone or something

Etymology

From Latin *vilificare*, from *vilis* (cheap, base) + *facere* (to make).
