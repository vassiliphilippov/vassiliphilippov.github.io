to affirm

1. VERB

/ əˈfɜrm / (AmE), / əˈfɜːm / (BrE)
to state something positively; to declare support for or agreement with something
to state or confirm positively

Examples

The witness chose to affirm the statement under oath.
She affirmed her support for the new policy during the meeting.
He repeatedly affirms his commitment to honesty in all his work.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to deny - to state that something is not true
  • Synonym: to confirm - to establish the truth or correctness of something

Etymology

From Latin affirmare, meaning 'to make steady, strengthen, confirm'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01013752-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈfɝːm / (AmE), / əˈfɜːm / (BrE)
to establish or strengthen something, especially a belief or statement, by providing new evidence or facts
establish by providing evidence

Examples

The new documents affirm his claims about the meeting.
The witness's testimony affirmed that the suspect was at the scene.
Recent research affirms the effectiveness of the proposed treatment.

Synset Examples

his story confirmed my doubts
The evidence supports the defendant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confirm - to prove or establish the truth or validity of something
  • Antonym: to deny - to state that something is not true

Etymology

From Latin affirmare (to make steady, to assert); ad- (to) + firmare (to make firm).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00667804-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ əˈfɜrm / (AmE), / əˈfɜːm / (BrE)
to state firmly and publicly that something is true, especially in a formal or official way
declare something as true formally or firmly

Examples

The witness affirmed that he had seen the accident occur.
She affirmed her commitment to upholding the company's values.
He affirmed under oath that the evidence was genuine.

Synset Examples

Before God I swear I am innocent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assert - to state something confidently and forcefully
  • Antonym: to deny - to say that something is not true

Etymology

From Latin affirmare (to strengthen, assert).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01013058-v
Synset POS: v
