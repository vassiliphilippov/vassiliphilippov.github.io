intensifier

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɛnsɪˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛnsɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a word, phrase, or particle used to increase the strength or force of another word, especially an adjective or adverb
word or particle that adds emphasis

Examples

In the sentence 'She was very happy,' the word 'very' is an intensifier.
He finished up all his work before the deadline, where 'up' acts as an intensifier.
Some people use multiple intensifiers in speech, such as 'really,' 'so,' or 'totally.'

Synset Examples

‘up’ in ‘finished up’ is an intensifier
‘honestly’ in ‘I honestly don't know’ is an intensifier

Related Terms

  • Antonym: downtoner - a word or phrase that reduces the force of another word
  • Synonym: modifier - a word or phrase that qualifies or describes another word

Etymology

From Latin 'intensificare' (to intensify) + English '-er'.
