/ ɑr / (AmE), / ɑː / (BrE)
a unit of ionizing radiation exposure; the amount that produces one electrostatic unit of charge in one cubic centimeter of dry air
unit of radiation exposure

Examples

The patient's exposure was measured at 2 R during the X-ray procedure.
Federal guidelines limit occupational exposure to a maximum of 5 R per year.
The scientist recorded how many R were detected in the laboratory test.

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'roentgen', named after Wilhelm Röntgen, the discoverer of X-rays.

3. NOUN

/ ɑr / (AmE), / ɑː / (BrE)
the universal constant in the ideal gas law, equal to 8.3143 joules per kelvin per mole, relating pressure, volume, and temperature of gases
ideal gas law constant

Examples

In the ideal gas law, the value of R is essential for converting between different units.
The constant R appears in many equations in physical chemistry, especially those concerning gases.
Remember to use the appropriate value for R depending on the units in your calculation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gas constant - another name for the universal constant in the gas equation
  • Degree Lower: Boltzmann constant - fundamental constant relating the average kinetic energy for particles in a gas with temperature

Etymology

Used as a symbol for 'constant of proportionality' in the ideal gas law, from the initial letter of German 'Regnault', after Henri Victor Regnault, who determined its value.
