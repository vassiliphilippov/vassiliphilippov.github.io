Eucalypt grandis

1. NOUN

/ juːˈkæ.lɪpt ˈɡræn.dɪs / (AmE), / juːˈkælɪpt ˈɡrændɪs / (BrE)
a very tall eucalyptus tree native to Queensland and New South Wales, known for its smooth bark and valuable timber
tall eucalyptus tree of Australia

Examples

The Eucalypt grandis towers above other species in the forests of Queensland.
Timber from Eucalypt grandis is highly valued for its strength and durability.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Eucalypt grandis in various environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flooded gum - common name for Eucalypt grandis, same species
  • Antonym: mountain ash - a different tall Australian eucalypt species (Eucalyptus regnans)

Etymology

From Latinized scientific binomial; 'Eucalypt' from Greek eu- (well) + kalyptos (covered), referring to the flower buds; 'grandis' means 'large' in Latin.
