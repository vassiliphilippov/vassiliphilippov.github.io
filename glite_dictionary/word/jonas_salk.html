Jonas Salk

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒoʊnəs sɔːlk / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊnəs sɒlk / (BrE)
(1914–1995) An American virologist who developed the first successful polio vaccine in 1955.
American virologist; developer of polio vaccine

Examples

Jonas Salk was celebrated worldwide for his groundbreaking work on the polio vaccine.
Many medical students study the achievements of Jonas Salk in their coursework.
After creating the vaccine, Jonas Salk became an advocate for public health initiatives.
