bailee

1. NOUN

/ beɪˈli / (AmE), / beɪˈliː / (BrE)
A person or agent to whom property is temporarily entrusted under a bailment agreement.
person entrusted with property in bailment
legal

Examples

As a bailee, the warehouse must take reasonable care of the goods in its possession.
The law requires a bailee to return the property to the bailor after the contract ends.
If the bailee damages the item, they may be liable for compensation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bailor - the person who delivers property in a bailment

Etymology

From bail (in legal sense) + -ee, modelled after similar legal agent nouns.
