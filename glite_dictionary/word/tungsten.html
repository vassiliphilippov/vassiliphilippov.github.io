tungsten

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌŋstən / (AmE), / ˈtʌŋstən / (BrE)
a heavy, hard, grey-white metallic element (symbol W) used mainly in electrical applications and found in ores such as wolframite and scheelite
a heavy grey-white metallic element

Examples

The filament in this light bulb is made of tungsten for its high melting point.
She studied samples of tungsten taken from local mineral deposits.
Because of its density, tungsten is used in specialized industrial machinery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wolfram - another name for the element tungsten

Etymology

From Swedish 'tung sten' meaning 'heavy stone'.
