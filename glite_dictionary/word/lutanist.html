lutanist

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːtənɪst / (AmE), / ˈluːtənɪst / (BrE)
A musician who plays the lute, especially in early music.
a lute player

Examples

The famous lutanist delighted the audience with an intricate Renaissance melody.
Many paintings from the Baroque period feature a lutanist performing in a courtly setting.
She trained as a lutanist before becoming known for her work with early music ensembles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lutenist - a person who plays the lute

Etymology

From lute + -an + -ist, modeled after 'lutenist' from Old French 'luth' (lute).
