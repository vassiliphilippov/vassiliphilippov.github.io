Rudbeckia hirta

1. NOUN

/ ruːdˈbɛkiə ˈhɜːrtə / (AmE), / ruːdˈbɛkiə ˈhɜːtə / (BrE)
a North American flowering plant of the daisy family, with yellow to orange ray flowers and dark centers, known as the black-eyed Susan and the state flower of Maryland
a North American daisy-like flower; black-eyed Susan

Examples

The Rudbeckia hirta blooms abundantly in Maryland's fields every summer.
Gardeners often choose Rudbeckia hirta for its bright, cheerful flowers and hardiness.
Many pollinators are attracted to Rudbeckia hirta in native prairie gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black-eyed Susan - common name for Rudbeckia hirta

Etymology

Named after Olof Rudbeck, Swedish botanist, and from Latin 'hirtus' meaning 'hairy'.
