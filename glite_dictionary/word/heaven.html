heaven

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛvən / (AmE), / ˈhɛvən / (BrE)
In many religions, the place believed to be the home of God or the gods, and where good people go after death; a paradise.
the dwelling place of God/gods or the blessed

Examples

Many people believe that after you die, your soul goes to heaven if you have lived a good life.
In some paintings, angels are shown descending from heaven to guide the faithful.
He said the view from the mountain peak was so beautiful it felt like being in heaven.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paradise - an idealized place of happiness or perfection after death
  • Antonym: hell - the place believed to be the afterlife of suffering or punishment

Etymology

From Old English heofon (sky, heaven).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛvən / (AmE), / ˈhɛv(ə)n / (BrE)
A place or state of complete happiness, delight, and peace.
place or state of perfect happiness

Examples

Relaxing in the mountain cabin felt like heaven after months of hard work.
The garden was so beautiful and peaceful, it was a little piece of heaven on earth.
For a chocolate lover, a dessert buffet is pure heaven.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paradise - a place or state of perfect happiness; synonym for 'heaven'

Etymology

From Old English heofon (sky, home of God), related to Old Norse himinn.
