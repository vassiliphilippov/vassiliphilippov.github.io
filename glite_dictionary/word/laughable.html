laughable

1. ADJ

/ ˈlæfəbəl / (AmE), / ˈlɑːfəbəl / (BrE)
so absurd or funny that it causes laughter; deserving to be laughed at.
provoking laughter; absurd or funny

Examples

The suggestion that cats can do your taxes is simply laughable.
His explanation for being late was so laughable that nobody believed him.
Some of the movie's special effects were unintentionally laughable.

Synset Examples

an amusing film with a steady stream of pranks and pratfalls
an amusing fellow
a comic hat
a comical look of surprise
funny stories that made everybody laugh
a very funny writer
it would have been laughable if it hadn't hurt so much
a mirthful experience
risible courtroom antics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: funny - amusing; causing laughter
  • Antonym: serious - not funny or laughable; solemn or earnest

Etymology

From laugh + -able; influenced by French risible (laughable).

2. ADJ

/ ˈlæfəbəl / (AmE), / ˈlɑːfəbl̩ / (BrE)
so absurd or unreasonable that it deserves ridicule; ridiculous or comically absurd.
ridiculously absurd; deserving ridicule

Examples

The proposal was so laughable that no one at the meeting could take it seriously.
His claim to have finished all the work overnight was simply laughable.
Some of the excuses he gave were too laughable to believe.

Synset Examples

the absurd excuse that the dog ate his homework
that's a cockeyed idea
ask a nonsensical question and get a nonsensical answer
a contribution so small as to be laughable
it is ludicrous to call a cottage a mansion
a preposterous attempt to turn back the pages of history
her conceited assumption of universal interest in her rather dull children was ridiculous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ridiculous - deserving or inviting derision or mockery
  • Antonym: serious - not intended to be funny or ridiculous

Etymology

From laugh + -able, meaning 'capable of being laughed at'.
