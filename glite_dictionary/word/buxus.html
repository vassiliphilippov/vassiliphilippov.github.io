Buxus

1. NOUN

/ ˈbjuːksəs / (AmE), / ˈbʌksəs / (BrE)
a genus of evergreen shrubs and small trees, commonly known as boxwood, native to Europe, Asia, Africa, and Central America
genus of boxwood shrubs or trees

Examples

Many gardens feature Buxus hedges due to their dense foliage and easy shaping.
The landscaper recommended planting Buxus along the walkway for a classic look.
English Buxus is often used for making topiary sculptures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boxwood - common name for shrubs or small trees of the genus Buxus

Etymology

From Latin *buxus*, meaning box tree, from Ancient Greek *pyxos* (box tree).
