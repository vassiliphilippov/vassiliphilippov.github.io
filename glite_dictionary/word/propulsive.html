propulsive

1. ADJ

/ prəˈpʌl.sɪv / (AmE), / prəˈpʌl.sɪv / (BrE)
Having the ability or tendency to propel or drive forward.
having the power to propel

Examples

The development of propulsive systems made space travel possible.
Her propulsive energy helped the team finish the project ahead of schedule.
Jet engines provide a strong propulsive force to lift the aircraft off the ground.

Synset Examples

propellant fuel for submarines
the faster a jet plane goes the greater its propulsive efficiency
universities … the seats of propulsive thought

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propellant - a substance that propels, especially in engines or weaponry

Etymology

From Latin 'propulsus', past participle of 'propellere' (to drive forward).

2. ADJ

/ prəˈpʌlsɪv / (AmE), / prəˈpʌlsɪv / (BrE)
having the power or tendency to propel or drive forward
having the power to drive forward

Examples

The jet engine provides a propulsive force that moves the aircraft forward.
Their ideas had a propulsive effect on the movement for reform.
The swimmer used a powerful kick to generate propulsive energy in the water.

Synset Examples

propulsive coefficient

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propelling - causing to move or push forward
  • Antonym: static - not moving or having power to move

Etymology

From Latin 'propulsus', past participle of 'propellere' (to drive forward).
