Mbit

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛm bɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛm bɪt / (BrE)
a unit of information equal to one million (1,000,000) bits, commonly used for measuring data transfer rates
a unit of digital information (1,000,000 bits)

Examples

The internet speed is advertised as 100 Mbit per second.
A file size of 8 Mbit equals one megabyte.
Streaming high-definition video may require a minimum of 5 Mbit per second.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: megabit - a unit of information equal to one million bits
  • Synonym: Mb - alternative abbreviation for megabit

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'megabit,' from Greek 'mega' meaning 'large' and 'bit,' the smallest unit of data in computing.
