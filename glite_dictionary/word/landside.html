landside

1. NOUN

/ ˈlænd.saɪd / (AmE), / ˈlan.saɪd / (BrE)
The side of a plow or similar implement that lies opposite the moldboard, helping to guide the tool and resist lateral pressure during use.
side part of a plow opposite the moldboard

Examples

The landside of the plow showed signs of heavy wear after a season in the fields.
Careful adjustment of the landside is essential to keep the plow running straight.
He replaced the damaged landside to improve the tool's effectiveness.

Etymology

From 'land' + 'side', referring to the side facing the unplowed land.
