asphalt

1. VERB

/ ˈæsfəlt / (AmE), / ˈæs.fælt / (BrE)
to cover or pave (a surface) with asphalt.
to cover with asphalt

Examples

They decided to asphalt the old dirt road to reduce dust.
After months of work, the crew finally asphalted the parking lot.
Workers will be asphalting Main Street next week, so expect some delays.

Synset Examples

asphalt the driveway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pave - to cover a surface with a hard material, such as stones, bricks, or concrete

Etymology

From Late Latin 'asphaltum', from Greek 'asphaltos'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæsfɔlt / (AmE), / ˈasfɑːlt / (BrE)
A mixture of bitumen and crushed stone, gravel, or sand, used especially for paving roads and also for roofing.
mixture for paving roads, roofing, etc.

Examples

The construction crew resurfaced the highway using asphalt to create a smoother drive.
Hot asphalt was poured onto the street to repair the damaged section.
The roof was coated with a layer of asphalt to protect it from the rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bitumen - the black, sticky substance used in making asphalt

Etymology

From Greek *asphaltos*, meaning 'secure' or 'to make firm.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14793644-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈæsfəlt / (AmE), / ˈasfɑːlt / (BrE)
a dark, sticky substance, consisting mainly of hydrocarbons, used to make roads and as a waterproofing material
dark road surfacing material

Examples

The workers poured asphalt onto the new highway to create a smooth driving surface.
Cracks began to appear in the old asphalt after a harsh winter.
Freshly laid asphalt has a strong, distinctive smell that lingers for days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bitumen - a mixture of hydrocarbons similar to asphalt, used in road surfacing and roofing

Etymology

From Late Latin 'asphaltum', from Greek 'asphaltos' (pitch).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14691634-n
Synset POS: n
