sectional

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛkʃənl̩ / (AmE), / ˈsɛkʃənəl / (BrE)
consisting of or divided into sections; made up of distinct parts that can be arranged or used separately or together
made of sections; divided into parts

Examples

We decided to buy a sectional sofa for the living room because it can be rearranged easily.
The architect drew a sectional view of the building to show the interior structure.
The museum displayed a sectional model of the ship, allowing visitors to see the inside.

Synset Examples

a sectional sofa
sectioned plates

Etymology

From section + -al (forming adjectives); section from Latin sectio (a cutting).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02492322-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛk.ʃə.nəl / (AmE), / ˈsek.ʃə.nəl / (BrE)
a large sofa or other piece of furniture made up of separate sections that can be arranged in different configurations
modular sofa or furniture unit

Examples

We just bought a new sectional for the living room to maximize seating.
The sectional can be rearranged to fit into smaller spaces.
She placed a blanket over one side of the sectional to make it more inviting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sofa - a long, comfortable seat for more than one person
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04172470-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈsɛkʃənəl / (AmE), / ˈsɛkʃənəl / (BrE)
Relating to or limited to a particular region, subdivision, or group within a larger community or territory.
relating to a region or subdivision

Examples

The debate quickly took on a sectional character, dividing north and south.
She criticized the legislation for serving sectional interests rather than the whole country.
Sectional differences sometimes make it difficult to reach a national consensus.

Synset Examples

sectional tensions arose over slavery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regional - relating to a particular region
  • Antonym: national - relating to an entire nation rather than a section

Etymology

From 'section' + '-al', from Latin 'sectio' (a cutting, division).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02417891-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈsɛkʃənəl / (AmE), / ˈsɛkʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or based on a section, especially as if cut through by an intersecting plane; showing a view of the inside as if exposed by a cut.
relating to a section, as if cut through

Examples

The engineer studied the sectional drawing of the machine to understand its internal structure.
A sectional view of the building revealed hidden support beams.
The textbook includes several sectional diagrams to illustrate how the device works.

Synset Examples

a sectional view
sectional drawings

Etymology

From section + -al, meaning pertaining to a section.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02955108-a
Synset POS: a
