Dagda

1. PROPN

/ ˈdæɡdə / (AmE), / ˈdaɡdə / (BrE)
(in Irish mythology) the chief Celtic god of the Tuatha Dé Danann, regarded as a father-figure and associated with fertility, wisdom, and strength.
chief god in Irish mythology

Examples

Many ancient Irish tales depict the Dagda wielding a magic club that could both kill and revive.
The Dagda was known for his great appetite, often portrayed as a jovial and powerful figure.
In mythology, the Dagda is said to be the father of Brigit and Angus Og.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Odin - chief god in Norse mythology
  • Synonym: Zeus - chief god in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Old Irish Dagda, meaning 'the good god'.
