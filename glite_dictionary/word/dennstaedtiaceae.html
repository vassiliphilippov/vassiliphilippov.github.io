Dennstaedtiaceae

1. NOUN

/ dɛnˌstiːdiˈeɪsiˌaɪ / (AmE), / dɛnˌstiːdiˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of ferns distinguished by their large, often finely divided fronds, including species such as bracken and hay-scented fern
family of ferns (including bracken)

Examples

The Dennstaedtiaceae family is known for ferns with creeping rhizomes and delicate fronds.
Bracken, a common member of the Dennstaedtiaceae, can be found in woodlands worldwide.
Recent studies have revised the classification of the Dennstaedtiaceae family within the order Polypodiales.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Dennstaedtia (named after August W. Dennstaedt, German botanist) + -aceae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛnˌstiːdiˈeɪsi.iː / (AmE), / ˌdɛn.stiːd.iˈeɪ.siː.iː / (BrE)
a family of ferns, including bracken and other genera, recognized in some classification systems as distinct from Polypodiaceae
a family of ferns (bracken family)

Examples

The Dennstaedtiaceae family includes the well-known bracken fern found in many temperate regions.
Botanists have debated whether certain genera should be classified within Dennstaedtiaceae or in other fern families.
Many members of the Dennstaedtiaceae are identified by their finely divided leaves and creeping rhizomes.

Etymology

From the genus name Dennstaedtia, after German botanist August W. Dennstaedt, combined with the standard botanical suffix -aceae for plant families.
