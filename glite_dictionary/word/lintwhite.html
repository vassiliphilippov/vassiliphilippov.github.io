lintwhite

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪntˌwaɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪntˌwaɪt / (BrE)
a small Old World finch, especially the male, which has a red breast and forehead; also known as the linnet.
Old World finch with red breast

Examples

The male lintwhite is easily recognized by its distinctive red forehead.
Farmers often saw flocks of lintwhites among the fields in spring.
She listened to the sweet song of a lintwhite perched on the garden fence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linnet - a small Old World finch with a red breast; common name for the species

Etymology

Variant of 'linnet', likely from Middle English *lint*, related to flax, referencing the bird's fondness for flax seeds.
