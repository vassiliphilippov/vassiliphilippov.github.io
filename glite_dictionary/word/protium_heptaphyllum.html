Protium heptaphyllum

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊtiəm ˌhɛptəˈfɪləm / (AmE), / ˈprəʊtiəm ˌhɛptəˈfɪləm / (BrE)
A tropical American tree of the genus Protium, known for its fragrant resin and used for timber and traditional medicine.
tropical American tree species

Examples

The Protium heptaphyllum is valued for its aromatic resin, which is used in incense and traditional remedies.
Botanists discovered a grove of Protium heptaphyllum thriving in the rainforest.
Local communities harvest the wood and resin from Protium heptaphyllum for various uses.

Etymology

Protium from Greek 'protos' (first); heptaphyllum from Greek 'hepta' (seven) and 'phyllon' (leaf), indicating seven leaflets.
