Aphrophora

1. PROPN

/ ˌæf.rəˈfɔːr.ə / (AmE), / ˌæf.rəˈfɔː.rə / (BrE)
A genus of spittlebugs belonging to the family Cercopidae, found in various regions and known for producing frothy spittle masses as nymphs.
a genus of spittlebugs

Examples

The nymphs of Aphrophora produce protective foam on plant stems during development.
Several species of Aphrophora are found in North America, where they feed on sap from various plants.
Entomologists study Aphrophora to understand the life cycle and behavior of spittlebugs.

Etymology

From Greek 'aphros' (foam) + '-phora' (bearing), referring to the foam produced by the nymphs.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæf.rəˈfɔːr.ə / (AmE), / ˌæf.rəˈfɔː.rə / (BrE)
A genus of froghoppers (family Cercopidae), insects known for producing spittle-like foam as nymphs.
a genus of froghopper insects

Examples

The Aphrophora species are often observed in meadows during the summer months.
Researchers discovered a new Aphrophora specimen in the rainforests of Borneo.
Juvenile Aphrophora insects produce protective spittle masses on plant stems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: froghopper - a common name for insects of the family Cercopidae, which includes Aphrophora

Etymology

From Greek 'aphros' (foam) and '-phora' (bearing), referring to the spittle-producing nature of the insects.
