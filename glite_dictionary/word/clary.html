clary

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɛri / (AmE), / ˈklɑːri / (BrE)
an aromatic herb of southern Europe, cultivated in Britain as a potherb and often grown as an ornamental plant
aromatic herb grown as a potherb or ornamental

Examples

The gardener planted clary in the herb garden for its fragrant leaves.
Dried clary is sometimes used to add flavor to soups and stews.
The vibrant flowers of clary make it a favorite among ornamental plants.

Etymology

From Old French *clerc*, related to the plant's historical use in clarifying liquids.
