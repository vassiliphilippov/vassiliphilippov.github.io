diethylmalonylurea

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪˌɛθilˌmæˌlɒnɪlˈjʊriə / (AmE), / ˌdaɪˌɛθɪlˌmælɒnɪlˈjʊəriə / (BrE)
A chemical compound of the barbiturate class used as a hypnotic drug to induce sleep.
barbiturate hypnotic drug
medical

Examples

The patient was prescribed diethylmalonylurea to help manage his insomnia.
Researchers studied the effects of diethylmalonylurea on sleep duration in laboratory animals.
Long-term use of diethylmalonylurea may result in tolerance and dependence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barbiturate - any of a class of sedative and sleep-inducing drugs derived from barbituric acid

Etymology

From diethyl- (containing two ethyl groups) + malonylurea (malonic acid derivative with urea).
