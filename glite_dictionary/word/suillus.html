Suillus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːɪləs / (AmE), / ˈsjuːɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Boletaceae, associated with pine trees and mainly found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
genus of fungi associated with pines

Examples

Several species of Suillus form symbiotic relationships with the roots of pine trees.
The mushroom she collected belonged to the Suillus genus, noted for its slimy cap.
Researchers discovered a new Suillus species in the forests of northern Europe.

Etymology

From Latin 'suillus' meaning 'of swine', originally relating to the porcino (pig-like) appearance of some species.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsuːɪləs / (AmE), / ˈsjuːɪləs / (BrE)
a genus of fungi in the family Boletaceae, with species mainly found in temperate northern regions and typically associated with pine trees
a genus of fungi associated with pines

Examples

Several species of Suillus form symbiotic relationships with pine trees in North America.
Mushroom foragers often seek out Suillus species in forests during autumn.
The cap of a Suillus fungus is typically sticky or slimy when wet.

Etymology

From Latin *suillus* meaning 'of swine' (possibly referring to the greasy appearance of some species).
