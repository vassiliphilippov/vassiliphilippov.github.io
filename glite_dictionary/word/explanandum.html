explanandum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkspləˈnændəm / (AmE), / ˌɛkspləˈnændəm / (BrE)
(Logic) A fact, event, or statement that is to be explained, especially in the context of logical or scientific inquiry.
something to be explained (in logic/philosophy)

Examples

The scientist specified the explanandum before formulating a hypothesis.
Every scientific theory must address some explanandum that calls for explanation.
In the discussion, the explanandum was the observed anomaly in the data.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: explanans - a statement or set of statements that provides an explanation

Etymology

From Latin 'explanandum' (something to be explained), from 'explanare' (to explain).
