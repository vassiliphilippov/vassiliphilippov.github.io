Oriolidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔriˈɑlɨˌdi / (AmE), / ˌɔːrɪˈɒlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of passerine birds known as Old World orioles, native to Africa, Europe, Asia, and Australia.
family of Old World orioles (birds)

Examples

The Oriolidae family includes many brightly colored oriole species found across Africa and Asia.
Researchers observed nesting behavior unique to Oriolidae during their field studies in Australia.
Unlike New World orioles, the Oriolidae are native exclusively to the Old World tropics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Oriole - any bird of the Oriolidae family, especially of the genus Oriolus

Etymology

From scientific Latin Oriolidae, based on the type genus Oriolus.
