Maupassant

1. PROPN

/ moʊˈpæsɒnt / (AmE), / məʊˈpæsɒ̃ / (BrE)
Guy de Maupassant (1850–1893), a French writer known especially for his short stories.
French short story writer (1850–1893)

Examples

Many readers consider Maupassant one of the masters of the short story.
The works of Maupassant are frequently studied in literature classes worldwide.
I recently finished a collection of Maupassant's most famous stories.

Etymology

From the name of Guy de Maupassant, a French surname.
