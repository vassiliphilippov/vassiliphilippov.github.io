Greenland

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡriːnˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnlənd / (BrE)
the largest island in the world, situated between the North Atlantic and the Arctic Ocean, and an autonomous territory within the Kingdom of Denmark
the world's largest island; Danish autonomous territory

Examples

She has always dreamed of visiting Greenland to see its vast glaciers and unique wildlife.
After years of planning, the scientists conducted their climate research in Greenland.
Despite its name, much of Greenland is covered with ice.

Etymology

Named by Norse settlers, from Old Norse 'Grœnland' meaning 'green land'.
