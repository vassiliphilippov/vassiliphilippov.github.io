paynim

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪnɪm / (AmE), / ˈpeɪnɪm / (BrE)
(Archaic) A non-Christian, especially a Muslim, typically used in medieval literature to refer to perceived heathens.
archaic term for a non-Christian, esp. a Muslim
archaic

Examples

In the medieval romance, the hero battled against the paynim invaders to defend his homeland.
Many old poems speak of crusaders fighting the paynim armies in the Holy Land.
The knight refused to bow to the customs of the paynim, staying true to his own faith.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heathen - a person who does not belong to a widely held religion (especially non-Christian)
  • Synonym: pagan - a person holding religious beliefs other than those of the main world religions

Etymology

From Old French paienime (pagan), ultimately from Latin paganus (villager, pagan).
