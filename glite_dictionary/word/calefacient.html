calefacient

1. ADJ

/ ˌkæləˈfeɪʃənt / (AmE), / ˌkæləˈfeɪʃənt / (BrE)
(of a substance) producing the sensation of heat when applied to the body.
producing a sensation of heat (med.)
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended a calefacient ointment to relieve the sore muscle.
Applying a calefacient cream can help improve blood circulation in cold limbs.
She used a calefacient patch to ease the tension in her neck.

Synset Examples

a mustard plaster is calefacient

Etymology

From Latin calor (heat) + facere (to make).
