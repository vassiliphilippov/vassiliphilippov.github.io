pallor

1. NOUN

/ ˈpælɚ / (AmE), / ˈpælə / (BrE)
an unnatural or unhealthy paleness of the skin, often caused by illness, shock, or emotional distress
unnatural paleness of the skin

Examples

The nurse noticed the pallor on his face and immediately checked his vital signs.
After receiving the shocking news, a sudden pallor overtook her complexion.
Her pallor made it clear that she was not feeling well.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paleness - the quality of being pale in color

Etymology

From Latin 'pallor' (paleness), from 'pallere' (to be pale).
