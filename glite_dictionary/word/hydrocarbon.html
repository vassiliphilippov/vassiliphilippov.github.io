hydrocarbon

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdroʊˈkɑrbən / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəʊˈkɑːbən / (BrE)
an organic compound consisting entirely of hydrogen and carbon atoms
organic compound of hydrogen and carbon

Examples

Gasoline is a hydrocarbon commonly used as a fuel for cars.
The burning of hydrocarbons in engines releases carbon dioxide into the atmosphere.
Methane is the simplest hydrocarbon in nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alkane - a type of hydrocarbon with only single bonds
  • Synonym: organic compound - a compound containing carbon, especially found in living things

Etymology

From hydro- (relating to hydrogen) and carbon, denoting its composition.
