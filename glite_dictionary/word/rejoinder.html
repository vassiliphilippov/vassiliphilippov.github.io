rejoinder

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈdʒɔɪndər / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɔɪndə / (BrE)
(Law) A pleading submitted by a defendant in response to a plaintiff's replication.
legal pleading in response to a replication
legal

Examples

The defendant filed a detailed rejoinder challenging each point made in the replication.
Under common law, a rejoinder must address the specific allegations raised by the plaintiff.
The judge reviewed both the replication and the rejoinder before making a ruling.

Etymology

From Old French 'rejoindre' (to join again), from Latin 're-' (again) + 'iungere' (to join).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈdʒɔɪndɚ / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɔɪndə / (BrE)
a quick or sharp reply, especially one that is witty or critical
quick, witty or critical reply

Examples

Her clever rejoinder left everyone in the room laughing.
When criticized, he always had a well-prepared rejoinder.
The debate became heated with each side offering a swift rejoinder.

Synset Examples

it brought a sharp rejoinder from the teacher

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retort - a quick, incisive, or witty reply to a remark
  • Synonym: response - something said or done as an answer or reaction
  • Antonym: question - a sentence, phrase, or word used to find out information

Etymology

From Old French 'rejoindre' (to reply), based on Latin 're-' (again) + 'iungere' (to join).
