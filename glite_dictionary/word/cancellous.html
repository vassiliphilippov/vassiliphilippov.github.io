cancellous

1. ADJ

/ ˈkænsələs / (AmE), / ˈkænsɪləs / (BrE)
having a structure that is open, latticed, or porous, like a network; especially referring to bone with a spongy, porous texture
having an open, latticed, or porous structure
medical

Examples

The ends of long bones are made of cancellous bone, which is lighter and less dense than compact bone.
Surgeons prefer to use cancellous grafts because they integrate more rapidly with existing bone.
The X-ray revealed an area of cancellous tissue within the fractured vertebra.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compact - having a dense, solid structure (as in compact bone)

Etymology

From Latin 'cancelli' (lattice) and '-ous' suffix.
