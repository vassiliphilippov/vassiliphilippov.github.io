inarticulate

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnˌɑrˈtɪkjələt / (AmE), / ɪnˌɑːˈtɪkjʊlət / (BrE)
unable to express oneself clearly or easily in speech; lacking the power of speech or words.
unable to express oneself clearly

Examples

She became so angry that she was inarticulate with frustration.
The witness remained inarticulate, unable to answer the lawyer's questions.
His inarticulate cry echoed through the silent night.

Synset Examples

inarticulate beasts
remained stupidly inarticulate and saying something noncommittal
inarticulate with rage
an inarticulate cry

Related Terms

  • Antonym: articulate - able to express thoughts and feelings clearly in speech or writing
  • Degree Higher: eloquent - fluent or persuasive in speaking or writing
  • Synonym: mute - unable to speak

Etymology

From Latin inarticulatus (not distinct, not uttered distinctly).
