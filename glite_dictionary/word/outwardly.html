outwardly

1. ADV

/ ˈaʊtwɚdli / (AmE), / ˈaʊtwədli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to or affects only the external or visible aspect of something.
relating to the outside or surface

Examples

Outwardly, the building looked unremarkable, but inside it was beautifully decorated.
She appeared calm outwardly, though she was extremely nervous.
The company is outwardly successful, but it has internal problems.

Synset Examples

outwardly, the figure is smooth

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inwardly - in a way that relates to the inside or inner feelings

Etymology

Formed from 'outward' + -ly.

2. ADV

/ ˈaʊt.wɚd.li / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.wəd.li / (BrE)
in a way that relates to how someone or something appears on the outside, rather than how they really are
on the outside; in appearance

Examples

Outwardly, he seemed calm, but inside he was very nervous.
The building is outwardly plain, but the interior is beautifully decorated.
She was outwardly friendly to her colleagues, though she didn't trust them.

Synset Examples

outwardly, she appeared composed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apparently - as far as appears or can be perceived
  • Antonym: inwardly - on the inside; in one's mind or emotions

Etymology

Formed from 'outward' + '-ly', with 'outward' from Old English 'ūtweard' (toward the outside).
