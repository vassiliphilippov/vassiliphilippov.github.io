xenogenesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌziːnəˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌzɛnəˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (BrE)
the alternation of two or more different forms in the life cycle of a plant or animal, especially as observed in certain insects and algae
alternation of forms in a life cycle

Examples

Certain jellyfish display xenogenesis as their body form changes during their life cycle.
Algae often undergo xenogenesis, alternating between different reproductive stages.
Scientists studied xenogenesis in the insect's development for clues about evolutionary adaptation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metagenesis - the alternation of sexual and asexual generations in the life cycle of an organism

Etymology

From Greek xenos (strange, foreign) + genesis (origin, creation).
