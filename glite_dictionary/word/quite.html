quite

1. ADV

/ kwaɪt / (AmE), / kwaɪt / (BrE)
to the greatest extent; completely or entirely.
completely; to the greatest extent

Examples

You are quite correct in your assessment of the situation.
After the meeting ended, she was quite alone in the office.
He was quite mistaken about the date of the event.

Synset Examples

you're quite right
she was quite alone
was quite mistaken
quite the opposite
not quite finished
did not quite make it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entirely - completely; fully
  • Antonym: partly - to some extent; not completely

Etymology

From Middle English 'quite' (completely), from Old French 'quite' (free, clear), from Latin 'quietus' (calm, at rest).

2. ADV

/ kwaɪt / (AmE), / kwaɪt / (BrE)
actually; really; to a significant or extreme degree.
to a significant or extreme degree

Examples

The results were quite surprising, exceeding even our most optimistic expectations.
Her decision took me quite by surprise; I never thought she would leave so suddenly.
That was quite a performance—everyone in the audience was blown away.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: very - to a high degree
  • Degree Lower: somewhat - to a moderate degree; not completely
  • Degree Higher: entirely - completely, fully

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French *quit* (free, clear), later influenced by 'quite' in the sense of 'completely'.

Examples

to a certain or significant degree; fairly or rather (not used with negatives)
to a certain or significant degree

Examples

It's quite late, so we should probably head home soon.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
