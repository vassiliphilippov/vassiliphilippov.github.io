twirl

1. NOUN

/ twɝl / (AmE), / twɜːl / (BrE)
a quick or rapid turning or spinning movement
rapid spinning movement

Examples

She gave the ribbon a graceful twirl before letting it go.
With a twirl of his umbrella, he signaled for the music to start.
The dancer finished her routine with a final twirl on stage.

Synset Examples

he gave the crank a spin
it broke off after much twisting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spin - the act of rotating rapidly
  • Synonym: twist - the act of turning or twisting
  • Synonym: whirl - an instance of spinning or turning rapidly
Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ twɜrl / (AmE), / twɜːl / (BrE)
to turn around or spin quickly with a twisting motion
spin or turn with a twist

Examples

The dancers twirled gracefully across the stage.
She loves to twirl her hair around her finger when she's thinking.
Autumn leaves twirl through the air before touching the ground.

Synset Examples

The leaves swirled in the autumn wind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spin - to turn around quickly
  • Synonym: whirl - to move or cause to move rapidly around and around
  • Synonym: twist - to turn something, especially repeatedly, so that it forms a spiral shape
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02052840-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ twɝl / (AmE), / twɜːl / (BrE)
to turn or spin something quickly around and around, especially repeatedly or lightly
turn or spin something around quickly

Examples

She twirled the pen between her fingers absentmindedly during the meeting.
The magician twirled a coin on the table to impress the audience.
He picked up a lock of her hair and gently twirled it around his finger.

Synset Examples

spin a coin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spin - to turn around quickly; to cause to turn around rapidly
  • Synonym: to rotate - to turn around a central point or axis

Etymology

Probably a frequentative of 'twirl,' altered from 'twire' (to peep or twinkle); influenced by 'whirl.'
