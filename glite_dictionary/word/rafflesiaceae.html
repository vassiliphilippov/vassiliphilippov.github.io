Rafflesiaceae

1. NOUN

/ rəˌfliːziˈeɪsiˌaɪ / (AmE), / rəˌfliːzɪˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of parasitic flowering plants, including Rafflesia, known for producing the world's largest flowers
family of parasitic plants with giant flowers

Examples

The Rafflesiaceae are best known for their enormous, foul-smelling flowers.
Scientists studying Rafflesiaceae have discovered new insights into plant parasitism.
Many members of the Rafflesiaceae family are native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

Etymology

Named after the genus Rafflesia, itself named after Sir Stamford Raffles, British statesman and founder of Singapore.

2. NOUN

/ ræˌfliːsiˈeɪsiˌaɪ / (AmE), / ræfˌliːsɪˈeɪsiːˌaɪ / (BrE)
a family of parasitic flowering plants, notably including the genus Rafflesia, known for producing some of the largest flowers in the world
family of parasitic flowering plants

Examples

The Rafflesiaceae are unique for their enormous and malodorous flowers.
Scientists discovered a new species in the Rafflesiaceae family deep within the rainforest.
Members of the Rafflesiaceae lack leaves and stems, relying entirely on their host plants.

Etymology

Named after the genus Rafflesia, itself named for Sir Stamford Raffles, British colonial administrator.
