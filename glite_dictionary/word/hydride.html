hydride

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪˌdraɪd / (AmE), / ˈhʌɪˌdrʌɪd / (BrE)
a binary chemical compound in which hydrogen is combined with another element
a compound of hydrogen and another element
medical

Examples

Lithium hydride is commonly used as a hydrogen storage material.
The chemist explained that sodium hydride reacts violently with water.
Scientists are developing metal hydrides for use in clean energy technologies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oxide - a binary compound of oxygen and another element

Etymology

From Greek 'hydro-' (water) and '-ide' (used in chemistry for binary compounds).
