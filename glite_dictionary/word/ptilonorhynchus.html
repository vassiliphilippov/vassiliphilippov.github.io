Ptilonorhynchus

1. NOUN

/ taɪlɵnɔˈrɪŋkəs / (AmE), / taɪlənɒˈrɪŋkəs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Ptilonorhynchidae, known for species such as the satin bowerbird.
genus of bowerbirds

Examples

The Ptilonorhynchus genus includes several species noted for their courtship displays.
Researchers observed male Ptilonorhynchus constructing elaborate bowers to attract females.
The satin bowerbird belongs to the Ptilonorhynchus genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bowerbird - bird known for building elaborate structures to attract mates

Etymology

From Greek 'ptilon' (feather) + 'rhynchos' (beak).

2. NOUN

/ tɪləˌnɔːˈrɪŋkəs / (AmE), / tɪləˌnɒˈrɪŋkəs / (BrE)
A genus of bowerbirds native to Australia, known for the elaborate bowers built by males to attract mates.
genus of Australian bowerbirds

Examples

The male Ptilonorhynchus constructs elaborate structures decorated with brightly colored objects.
One species in the genus Ptilonorhynchus is particularly famous for its complex courtship displays.
Researchers have studied Ptilonorhynchus to understand the evolution of mating behaviors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bowerbird - any bird of the family Ptilonorhynchidae, including Ptilonorhynchus

Etymology

From Ancient Greek 'ptilon' (feather) and 'rhynchos' (beak).
