cargo

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːɡəʊ / (BrE)
goods transported by a ship, aircraft, or other large vehicle
goods carried by a vehicle

Examples

The ship was loaded with cargo bound for South America.
Workers carefully unloaded the cargo from the airplane after it landed.
The value of the stolen cargo was estimated at several million dollars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freight - goods transported in bulk, especially by truck or train
  • Antonym: passenger - a person who is traveling in a vehicle but is not operating it

Etymology

From Spanish 'cargo' (load), from cargar (to load), from Late Latin 'carricare' (to load a wagon).
