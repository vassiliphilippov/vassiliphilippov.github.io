protozoa

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊtəˈzoʊə / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəˈzəʊə / (BrE)
A group of single-celled, microscopic organisms that are typically motile and feed on organic matter, including flagellates, ciliates, sporozoans, and amoebas.
single-celled microscopic organisms

Examples

Scientists observed the protozoa moving rapidly under the microscope.
Diseases such as malaria are caused by certain types of protozoa.
The pond water sample contained several species of protozoa and bacteria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protozoon - a single protozoan organism
  • Antonym: algae - simple, typically aquatic photosynthetic organisms

Etymology

From New Latin, plural of 'protozoon', from Greek prōto- (first) + zōon (animal).
