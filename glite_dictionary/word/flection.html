flection

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈflɛkʃən / (BrE)
the state of being bent or flexed, especially of a joint
state of being bent (of a joint)
medical

Examples

The flection of the elbow allowed her to lift the object easily.
Limited flection in his knee made walking difficult after the injury.
During the exam, the doctor checked the flection and extension of each joint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flexion - the action of bending or the condition of being bent (especially of a joint)

Etymology

From Latin 'flexio' meaning bending, via French and Late Latin influence.

2. NOUN

/ ˈflɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈflɛkʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
The act or state of bending or deviation from a straight or normal course.
deviation from a straight or normal course

Examples

The flection of the river's path was caused by the rocky terrain.
Engineers measured the flection in the bridge's support beams after the earthquake.
The doctor's report noted an abnormal flection in the patient's spine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flexion - the act of bending or condition of being bent

Etymology

From Latin 'flexio' (a bending), from 'flectere' (to bend).
