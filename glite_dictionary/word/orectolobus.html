Orectolobus

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˌrɛk.təˈloʊ.bəs / (AmE), / ɒˌrɛk.təˈləʊ.bəs / (BrE)
a genus of carpet sharks known as wobbegongs, native to the western Pacific and Indian Oceans, characterized by flattened bodies and fringed mouths
genus of wobbegong carpet sharks

Examples

Several species within the genus Orectolobus are commonly referred to as wobbegongs.
Researchers studied the camouflage patterns of Orectolobus in Australian waters.
The aquarium recently acquired a new Orectolobus specimen for its shark exhibit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wobbegong - a common name for sharks in the genus Orectolobus

Etymology

From Greek 'orektos' (stretched out) and 'lobos' (lobe), referring to the fringed lobes around the mouth.
