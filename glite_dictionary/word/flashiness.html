flashiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈflæʃi.nɪs / (AmE), / ˈflæʃɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being bright, showy, or attention-grabbing, often in a way that is tasteless or lacks subtlety
tasteless or showy display

Examples

The flashiness of his gold jewelry made him stand out in the crowd.
Some people value elegance over flashiness when choosing their clothing.
Critics complained about the flashiness of the production, calling it gaudy and excessive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostentation - excessive or pretentious display; showing off
  • Antonym: modesty - lack of showiness; being reserved or unpretentious

Etymology

From 'flashy' + '-ness'; 'flashy' meaning ostentatious or showy.
