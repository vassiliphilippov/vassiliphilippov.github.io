2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌtəri / (AmE), / ˈbʌtəri / (BrE)
a small room or storeroom in a house or institution, used for storing food, drink, or wine, especially in former times
storeroom for food or wine
archaic

Examples

The servants fetched more wine from the buttery during the feast.
The old manor house still has its original buttery next to the kitchen.
In medieval colleges, the buttery was stocked with ale and bread for the students.

Etymology

From Old French botelerie, from botel (bottle), originally meaning a place where bottles were kept.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbʌt̬əri / (AmE), / ˈbʌtəri / (BrE)
A room or shop in a British university, especially at Oxford or Cambridge, where students can buy tea, snacks, or light meals.
university tearoom or snack bar (Britain)
british

Examples

He met his friends daily in the buttery for tea and sandwiches.
During the exam period, the buttery stayed open late for hungry students.
The old college buttery is famous for its homemade cakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canteen - a place where food and drinks are served, often in institutions

Etymology

From Middle English, originally meaning 'storeroom for butter', later evolving to mean a refreshment room in colleges.
