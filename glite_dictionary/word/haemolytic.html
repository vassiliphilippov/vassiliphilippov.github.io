haemolytic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhiːməˈlɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˈlɪtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or causing the destruction of red blood cells (hemolysis).
relating to the destruction of red blood cells
medical british

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with haemolytic anemia after extensive blood tests.
Certain bacteria can produce haemolytic toxins that damage red blood cells.
Researchers studied the haemolytic activity of the new drug in the laboratory.

Synset Examples

hemolytic anemia

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemolytic - relating to the destruction of red blood cells; American spelling

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' (blood) + 'lytikos' (able to loosen or dissolve).
