Felis silvestris

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːlɪs sɪlˈvɛstrɪs / (AmE), / ˈfiːlɪs sɪlˈvɛstrɪs / (BrE)
A bushy-tailed wildcat native to Europe, resembling the domestic cat, and considered the ancestor of the domestic cat.
European wildcat species

Examples

The Felis silvestris is rarely seen in the dense forests where it lives.
Some scientists believe the domestic cat descended from the Felis silvestris.
Habitat loss has made the Felis silvestris population increasingly vulnerable in parts of Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wildcat - a small wild feline resembling a domestic cat

Etymology

From Latin: 'felis' (cat) and 'silvestris' (of the woods/forest).
