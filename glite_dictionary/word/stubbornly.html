stubbornly

1. ADV

/ ˈstʌbərnli / (AmE), / ˈstʌbənli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows refusal to change one's attitude or position, despite good reasons to do so.
in a stubborn manner

Examples

She stubbornly refused to apologize, even after realizing her mistake.
The old dog stubbornly stayed outside in the rain despite repeated calls.
They stubbornly clung to their beliefs, unwilling to consider new evidence.

Synset Examples

she remained stubbornly in the same position

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstinately - in a stubborn or unyielding manner
  • Antonym: yieldingly - in a way that yields or gives in

Etymology

From 'stubborn' (Middle English), from Old French 'estoborn', of unknown ultimate origin; + -ly.
