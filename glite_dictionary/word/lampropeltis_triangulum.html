Lampropeltis triangulum

1. NOUN

/ læmˈprɑːpɛltɪs traɪˈæŋgjələm / (AmE), / læmˈprɒpəltɪs traɪˈæŋgjʊləm / (BrE)
a nonvenomous species of king snake found in North and Central America, known for its varied color patterns, often mimicking those of venomous coral snakes
species of nonvenomous king snake (milk snake)
medical

Examples

The Lampropeltis triangulum is often mistaken for the venomous coral snake because of its red and black bands.
Researchers studied the diet of the Lampropeltis triangulum in its natural habitat.
A Lampropeltis triangulum can make a good pet due to its docile temperament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milk snake - another common name for Lampropeltis triangulum
  • Antonym: coral snake - venomous snake with similar coloration, often mimicked by the milk snake

Etymology

From Greek 'lampros' (bright, shining) and 'peltis' (shield), with 'triangulum' from Latin meaning 'triangle', referring to markings.
