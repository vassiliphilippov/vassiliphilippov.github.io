snipefish

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnaɪpˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈsnaɪpˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A small bottom-dwelling marine fish with a compressed body and a long, slender, toothless snout, found in warm seas.
small fish with a long snout in warm seas

Examples

The snipefish uses its elongated snout to search for small prey in sandy seabeds.
Divers spotted a group of snipefish near the coral reef last summer.
Because of its delicate appearance, the snipefish is often admired by marine biologists.

Etymology

Named for its long snout, resembling that of a snipe (a wading bird).
