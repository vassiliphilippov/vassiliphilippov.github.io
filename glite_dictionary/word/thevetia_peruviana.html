Thevetia peruviana

1. NOUN

/ θəˈviː.ti.ə pəˌruː.viˈɑː.nə / (AmE), / θəˈviː.ti.ə pəˌruː.viˈɑː.nə / (BrE)
A tropical American shrub or small tree with glossy dark green leaves and fragrant yellow, orange, or peach-colored flowers; all parts of the plant are highly poisonous.
poisonous tropical shrub or small tree

Examples

The gardener warned that Thevetia peruviana is extremely toxic if ingested by children or pets.
In tropical gardens, Thevetia peruviana is valued for its beautiful, trumpet-shaped flowers.
Locals avoid planting Thevetia peruviana near schools due to its poisonous nature.

Etymology

From New Latin, named after André Thévet (French explorer) and Peru, indicating the region of origin.
