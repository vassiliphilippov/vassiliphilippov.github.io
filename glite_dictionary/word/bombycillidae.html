Bombycillidae

1. NOUN

/ bɒm.bɪˈsɪl.ɪ.di / (AmE), / bɒm.bɪˈsɪl.ɪ.di / (BrE)
A family of birds in the suborder Oscines, comprising the waxwings, silky flycatchers, and the gray hypocolius of Southwest Asia.
family of birds including waxwings

Examples

The Bombycillidae are known for their soft, silky plumage and are found across the Northern Hemisphere.
Scientists recently reviewed the evolutionary relationships within the Bombycillidae family.
Members of the Bombycillidae display unique social and feeding behaviors among songbirds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waxwing - songbird of the Bombycillidae family with soft, silky plumage

Etymology

From modern Latin Bombycilla (genus of waxwings) + -idae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌbɒmbɪˈsɪlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌbɒmbɪˈsɪlɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of birds in the suborder Oscines, including waxwings, silky flycatchers, and the gray hypocolius, found in North America, Eurasia, and Southwest Asia
family of birds including waxwings and silky flycatchers

Examples

The Bombycillidae are known for their sleek plumage and distinctive waxy tips on their wings.
Scientists recently studied the migration patterns of the Bombycillidae across Europe and Asia.
The gray hypocolius is a rare member of the Bombycillidae family found in Southwest Asia.

Etymology

From New Latin Bombycilla (a genus of birds) + -idae (denoting family in zoological taxonomy).
