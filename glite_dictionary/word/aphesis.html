aphesis

1. NOUN

/ ˈæfəsɪs / (AmE), / ˈæfəsɪs / (BrE)
the gradual loss of an initial unstressed vowel or syllable in a word, as in 'squire' from 'esquire'
gradual loss of a word's initial unstressed vowel

Examples

The word 'lone' is a result of aphesis from 'alone'.
Some English words have shorter forms due to aphesis over time.
Linguists study aphesis to understand changes in word origins and pronunciations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elision - the omission of a sound or syllable in speech
  • Synonym: apheresis - the loss of a sound or syllable at the beginning of a word

Etymology

From Greek aphesis 'a letting go', from apheinein, 'to let go'.
