nagger

1. NOUN

/ ˈnæɡər / (AmE), / ˈnaɡə / (BrE)
a person who constantly complains or finds fault, especially in an annoying or persistent way
person who constantly complains or finds fault
informal

Examples

My brother says I'm a nagger because I keep reminding him to clean his room.
He was tired of being called a nagger just for caring about the details.
Nobody likes a nagger, so I try not to repeat myself too often.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complainer - a person who expresses dissatisfaction or annoyance frequently
  • Antonym: praiser - a person who expresses approval or admiration

Etymology

From the verb 'nag' (to complain or find fault persistently) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
