atropine

1. NOUN

/ ˈætɹəˌpiːn / (AmE), / ˈat.rə.piːn / (BrE)
a poisonous crystalline substance obtained from the nightshade family, used as a medicine to dilate the pupils and as an antidote to certain toxins
anticholinergic drug from nightshade used in medicine
medical

Examples

The doctor administered atropine to counteract the effects of nerve agent poisoning.
During the eye exam, the nurse used atropine drops to dilate my pupils.
Atropine is sometimes prescribed as an antispasmodic to relieve gastrointestinal discomfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scopolamine - alkaloid from nightshade, used as an anticholinergic drug

Etymology

From Latin Atropa (the genus of deadly nightshade), named after Atropos, one of the Fates in Greek mythology, and the chemical suffix -ine.
