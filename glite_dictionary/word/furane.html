furane

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjʊr.eɪn / (AmE), / ˈfjʊə.reɪn / (BrE)
A colorless, toxic, and flammable liquid organic compound used in the synthesis of nylon and other chemicals.
toxic, flammable organic compound
medical

Examples

Furane is used as a chemical intermediate in the production of adhesives and nylon.
The laboratory stored furane in tightly sealed containers due to its volatility.
Exposure to furane should be minimized because it is highly toxic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furan - organic compound; alternative spelling for furane

Etymology

From Latin 'furfur' (bran), as the compound can be derived from bran.
