ascomycetous

1. ADJ

/ ˌæskəˈmaɪsətəs / (AmE), / ˌaskəˈmʌɪsɪtəs / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of fungi belonging to the class Ascomycetes, which produce spores in a sac-like structure called an ascus.
relating to Ascomycetes fungi
medical

Examples

The structure of ascomycetous fungi allows them to thrive in diverse environments.
Researchers identified the mold as an ascomycetous species based on its spore formation.
Many ascomycetous fungi play important roles in decomposing organic matter.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: basidiomycetous - relating to fungi of the Basidiomycetes class

Etymology

From New Latin Ascomycetes (type of fungi) + English -ous (forming adjectives).
