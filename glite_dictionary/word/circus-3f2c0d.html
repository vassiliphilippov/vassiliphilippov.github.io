circus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.kəs / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.kəs / (BrE)
A traveling show consisting of performances by acrobats, clowns, and trained animals, often held in a large tent.
traveling show with acts and animals

Examples

The children were thrilled when they saw the circus coming to town.
We bought tickets to the circus to watch the amazing acrobats and clowns.
During her travels in Europe, she visited a famous old circus in Paris.

Synset Examples

the children always love to go to the circus

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carnival - A traveling amusement show with rides, games, and sideshows
  • Synonym: theater - A building or outdoor area for dramatic performances

Etymology

From Latin 'circus' (circle, ring), referring to Roman arenas for public shows.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.kəs / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.kəs / (BrE)
In ancient Rome, a large open-air stadium used for public events such as chariot races and gladiatorial games.
ancient Roman open-air stadium
archaic

Examples

The ancient Romans gathered in the circus to watch thrilling chariot races.
Much of the excitement in the circus centered on the gladiators' brutal contests.
Archaeologists discovered the remains of a grand circus outside the city walls.

Etymology

From Latin 'circus' meaning 'circle, ring'; referring to the circular arenas of ancient Rome.
