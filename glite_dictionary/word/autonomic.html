autonomic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɑː.təˈnɑː.mɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔː.təˈnɒm.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to or controlled by the autonomic nervous system, which regulates involuntary body functions such as heartbeat and digestion.
relating to the autonomic nervous system
medical

Examples

The autonomic nervous system regulates involuntary functions like breathing and heart rate.
Some medications can affect autonomic responses, causing changes in blood pressure.
His doctor explained that his fainting episodes were related to an autonomic dysfunction.

Synset Examples

autonomic reflexes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: voluntary - controlled by conscious will rather than by the autonomic nervous system

Etymology

From Greek 'autonomos' (having its own laws), from 'autos' (self) + 'nomos' (law), via 'autonomy'.
