reeve

1. NOUN

/ riv / (AmE), / riːv / (BrE)
a female ruff, a species of wading bird in the sandpiper family
female ruff (wading bird)

Examples

The reeve is less colorful than the male ruff during the breeding season.
Several reeves were observed feeding by the lakeshore.
Unlike the male, the reeve does not develop elaborate plumage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruff - a large Eurasian wading bird, the male of which has a distinctive ornamental ruff of feathers

Etymology

From Middle English *reve*, probably related to the bird name 'ruff'.

2. VERB

/ riv / (AmE), / riːv / (BrE)
to fasten or thread a rope or line by passing it through a hole or around something, especially in a pulley or block
pass a rope through a hole or around something

Examples

To lift the cargo, the crew needed to reeve the rope through the block.
Before raising the sail, you must reeve the halyard properly.
He carefully reeved the line around the pulley to secure the load.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thread - to pass something (such as a string) through a hole or narrow opening

Etymology

From Middle English reven, from Old English rēofan (to tear, split).
