Owen Glendower

1. PROPN

/ ˈoʊ.ɪn ˈɡlɛnˌdaʊ.ər / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.ɪn ˈɡlɛn.daʊ.ə / (BrE)
(1359–1416) A Welsh chieftain who led a major revolt against the rule of Henry IV in Wales, becoming a national hero.
Welsh rebel leader against Henry IV

Examples

Many Welsh people regard Owen Glendower as a symbol of national pride.
In his play 'Henry IV, Part 1', Shakespeare included the character of Owen Glendower.
The uprising led by Owen Glendower had a lasting impact on Welsh history.
