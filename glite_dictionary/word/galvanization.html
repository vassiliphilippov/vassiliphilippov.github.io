galvanization

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡælvənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡal.və.naɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The act of stimulating someone or something into sudden activity or heightened energy.
stimulation into action or energy

Examples

The surprise announcement led to an immediate galvanization of public opinion.
The leader's speech was a galvanization for the movement.
There was a noticeable galvanization of the team after their early setback.

Synset Examples

the unexpected news produced a kind of galvanization of the whole team

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stimulation - the act of arousing activity or energy in someone or something

Etymology

From Italian 'galvanismo', after Luigi Galvani, Italian physician, + English '-ation'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɡæv.lə.nəˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡal.və.naɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the stimulation of nerves or muscles using a direct electric (galvanic) current, especially for therapeutic or experimental purposes
electrical stimulation with galvanic current
medical

Examples

Physical therapists sometimes use galvanization to help stimulate muscle contractions in patients with nerve injuries.
Early experiments in galvanization demonstrated how electrical currents could activate frog legs in the laboratory.
With advances in technology, galvanization has become a more precise and controlled form of electrotherapy.

Etymology

From galvanize, after Luigi Galvani, Italian scientist (1737–1798), noted for his study of electricity's effects on animal tissue.

3. NOUN

/ ˌɡæl.və.nɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡæl.və.naɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of coating iron or steel with a layer of zinc to protect it from rusting, typically using a galvanic current
coating metal with zinc to prevent rust

Examples

The steel beams were protected from corrosion by galvanization.
Many outdoor structures undergo galvanization to extend their lifespan.
Galvanization helps prevent iron fences from rusting, even in harsh weather conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electroplating - the process of coating a metal with another metal using an electric current
  • Antonym: rust - the formation of iron oxide due to the reaction of iron with oxygen and moisture

Etymology

From French 'galvanisation', after Luigi Galvani, Italian scientist.
