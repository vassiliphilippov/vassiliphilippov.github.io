sheepskin

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃipˌskɪn / (AmE), / ˈʃiːp.skɪn / (BrE)
the tanned skin of a sheep with the fleece left on, often used for clothing or coverings
tanned fleece-covered sheep skin

Examples

She wore a warm sheepskin coat during the cold winter months.
The chair was covered with a soft sheepskin for extra comfort.
Traditional boots made from sheepskin keep your feet cozy in winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lambskin - the skin of a young sheep, often softer and finer than adult sheepskin

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃipˌskɪn / (AmE), / ˈʃiːp.skɪn / (BrE)
a diploma or certificate, especially one given for completing a course of study at a college or university
diploma or graduation certificate

Examples

He proudly displayed his sheepskin on the wall of his office.
After four years of hard work, she finally earned her sheepskin.
Many graduates frame their sheepskin as a memento of their achievement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diploma - an official document certifying graduation or completion of a course of study

Etymology

Originally, diplomas were written on parchment made from sheepskin.

3. NOUN

/ ˈʃipˌskɪn / (AmE), / ˈʃiːpskɪn / (BrE)
The skin of a sheep or goat prepared for use as a material to write on, especially before the invention of paper.
skin of a sheep or goat used for writing

Examples

The ancient manuscript was written on sheepskin rather than paper.
Medieval scribes often used sheepskin because it was durable and easy to prepare.
The museum displayed a rare sheepskin document from the twelfth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parchment - stiff, flat, thin material made from animal skin used in writing
