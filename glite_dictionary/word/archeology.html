archeology

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrkiˈɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌɑːkiˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of human history and prehistory through the excavation and analysis of artifacts, structures, and other physical remains
study of ancient human cultures

Examples

She majored in archeology to learn about ancient civilizations.
New discoveries in archeology have shed light on prehistoric cultures.
The museum's new exhibition showcases findings from recent archeology digs.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: anthropology - the study of human societies and cultures and their development
  • Synonym: history - the study of past events, particularly in human affairs
  • Degree Lower: paleontology - the study of fossils and ancient life forms

Etymology

From Greek 'arkhaiologia' meaning 'ancient history'; 'arkhaios' (ancient) + 'logia' (study).
