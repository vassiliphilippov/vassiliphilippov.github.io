Calluna

1. NOUN

/ kəˈluːnə / (AmE), / kəˈluːnə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Ericaceae, commonly known as heather, containing the single species Calluna vulgaris.
a genus of heath plants (heather)

Examples

Calluna is a prevalent genus found in moorland habitats throughout northern Europe.
The purple flowers of Calluna provide food for many pollinators in late summer.
Many gardeners plant Calluna for its hardiness and colorful blooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heather - common name of Calluna vulgaris, a small shrub with pink or purple flowers

Etymology

From Latin 'calluna', from Greek 'kalluno' (to clean or sweep), in reference to the use of the plant for making brooms.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈluːnə / (AmE), / kəˈluːnə / (BrE)
A small genus of flowering plants in the heath family, containing the common heather (Calluna vulgaris).
genus of flowering plants (heather)

Examples

The moors were covered in blooming Calluna during late summer.
Gardeners often choose Calluna for its hardiness and vivid purple flowers.
Native bees are attracted to the nectar of Calluna plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heather - common name for Calluna vulgaris, a flowering plant in the heath family

Etymology

From Greek 'kallunein' (to sweep clean), because heather was used for making brooms.
