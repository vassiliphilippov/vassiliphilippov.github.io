botheration

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɑːðəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌbɒðəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
A state of being irritated, annoyed, or troubled.
state of annoyance or irritation

Examples

The constant noise outside was a real botheration during my online meeting.
She spoke of the botheration caused by the never-ending paperwork.
His missing keys were a minor botheration but nothing he couldn't handle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annoyance - a feeling or state of being annoyed
  • Synonym: irritation - a feeling of being irritated

Etymology

Formed from "bother" (to annoy) with the suffix "-ation" indicating a state or condition.

2. NOUN

/ ˌbɑːðəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌbɒðəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
A person or thing that causes annoyance, trouble, or inconvenience.
an annoying or troublesome thing or person

Examples

The broken printer was a real botheration during the busiest week of the year.
For some people, minor delays are more than a botheration; they can ruin the whole day.
Replacing the old pipes turned into a major botheration for the home owners.

Synset Examples

a bit of a bother
he's not a friend, he's an infliction
washing dishes was a pain in the neck before we got a dish washer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annoyance - something that causes irritation
  • Synonym: nuisance - an annoying or troublesome person, thing, or situation
  • Antonym: relief - the lessening or removal of something painful or troubling

Etymology

Formed from 'bother' + '-ation', imitating other nouns of action.

Source: WordNet
