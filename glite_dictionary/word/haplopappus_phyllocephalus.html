Haplopappus phyllocephalus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæpləˈpæpəs ˌfɪloʊˈsɛfələs / (AmE), / ˌhæpləʊˈpæpəs ˌfɪlɪəʊˈsɛfələs / (BrE)
An annual plant native to the southern United States and Mexico, with bristly leaves and pale yellow flowers.
a plant with bristly leaves, yellow flowers

Examples

Haplopappus phyllocephalus thrives in dry, sandy soils across parts of Texas and northern Mexico.
The bristly leaves of Haplopappus phyllocephalus make it easy to distinguish from similar species.
Botanists collected several specimens of Haplopappus phyllocephalus during their fieldwork in southern Arizona.

Etymology

From Greek: haplo- (single, simple) + pappus (down, fluff on seeds); species name from Greek phyllo- (leaf) and kephale (head).
