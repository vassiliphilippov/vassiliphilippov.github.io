Cornus mas

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrnəs mæs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnəs mæs / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia, known for its bright red, cherry-like fruit.
European shrub with bright red fruit

Examples

The fruit of the Cornus mas is often used to make jams and syrups in Eastern Europe.
Botanists observed that Cornus mas blooms early each spring, brightening gardens before many other shrubs.
Our neighbor planted a Cornus mas tree for its showy yellow flowers and edible red berries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornelian cherry - another common name for Cornus mas

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'male cornel', referring to the tree's hard wood.
