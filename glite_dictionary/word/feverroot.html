feverroot

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːvərˌrut / (AmE), / ˈfiːvəˌruːt / (BrE)
A coarse, weedy American perennial herb with large, usually perfoliate leaves and purple or dull red flowers.
American perennial herb with large leaves and reddish flowers

Examples

The traditional healer brewed a tea from feverroot to help treat colds and fevers.
Clusters of feverroot grew wild along the edge of the marshy field.
The distinctive purple flowers of the feverroot attracted a variety of pollinators in spring.
