prodrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.drəʊm / (BrE)
an early symptom indicating the onset of a disease or an attack.
early symptom before disease or attack
medical

Examples

A headache is often the prodrome of a migraine attack.
The patient recognized the prodrome and took medication to prevent the seizure.
Doctors monitor for any prodrome to intervene before serious symptoms develop.

Etymology

From Greek prodromos (forerunner), from pro- (before) + dromos (running, course).
