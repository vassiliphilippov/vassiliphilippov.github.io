feloniousness

1. NOUN

/ fəˈloʊniəsnəs / (AmE), / fɪˈləʊniəsnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being felonious; relating to or involving a serious crime.
state of being felonious or criminal
legal

Examples

The prosecutor argued convincingly about the feloniousness of the defendant's actions.
Many laws differentiate between degrees of feloniousness and lesser offenses.
The report focused on reducing the feloniousness prevalent in certain neighborhoods.

Synset Examples

Big John might give you a smack, but that required an extraordinary circumstance, feloniousness of a type that was beyond Joey and Linwood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criminality - the state of being criminal
  • Antonym: innocence - the state of being not guilty of a crime

Etymology

From 'felonious' + '-ness'; 'felonious' from Anglo-Norman *felonius*, from Old French *felon* (criminal).
