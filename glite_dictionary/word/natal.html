Natal

1. PROPN

/ nəˈtɑl / (AmE), / nəˈtɑːl / (BrE)
a former province in eastern South Africa along the Indian Ocean, now known as KwaZulu-Natal since 1994.
region in eastern South Africa

Examples

The British colonized Natal in the 19th century, leading to significant cultural changes in the region.
After 1994, Natal officially became known as KwaZulu-Natal.
Many tourists visit Natal for its beautiful beaches and rich history.

Synset Examples

Natal was renamed KwaZulu-Natal in 1994

Etymology

From Portuguese 'natal' (relating to birth), originally named after Christmas Day (Portuguese: Dia de Natal) when it was first sighted by European sailors.
