abysmal

1. ADJ

/ əˈbɪzməl / (AmE), / əˈbɪzməl / (BrE)
extremely deep or immeasurable, like an abyss
immeasurably deep

Examples

The abysmal chasm seemed to have no visible bottom.
Researchers sent a probe into the abysmal trenches of the Pacific Ocean.
He stared down into the abysmal darkness, unable to see anything below.

Synset Examples

the abyssal depths of the ocean

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bottomless - of such great depth as to be unfathomable

Etymology

From late Latin *abyssimus* (deepest), from Greek *ábyssos* (bottomless).

2. ADJ

/ əˈbɪzməl / (AmE), / əˈbɪzməl / (BrE)
Extremely bad or severe; immeasurably great or appalling in extent, degree, or quality.
extremely bad or severe

Examples

The team delivered an abysmal performance that disappointed both fans and critics.
His lack of knowledge about basic history facts was truly abysmal.
The living conditions in the camp were described as abysmal by aid workers.

Synset Examples

abysmal misery
abysmal stupidity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atrocious - extremely bad or unpleasant
  • Antonym: excellent - extremely good; of the highest quality

Etymology

From late Latin abysmalis, from abyssus (abyss).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01502652-s
Synset POS: s
