Menopon palladum

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛnəˈpɒn ˈpælədəm / (AmE), / ˌmɛnəˈpɒn ˈpalədəm / (BrE)
a species of louse that is parasitic on poultry, feeding on their skin and feathers
a louse species parasitic on poultry
medical

Examples

Chickens infested with Menopon palladum may exhibit excessive preening and feather loss.
Veterinarians recommend regular checks for Menopon palladum in commercial poultry farms.
Effective treatment is essential to prevent Menopon palladum from spreading among birds.

Etymology

From genus name Menopon (from Greek 'meno-' meaning 'to remain' and 'ponos' meaning 'toil, work') and species name from Latin 'palladum', of uncertain derivation.
