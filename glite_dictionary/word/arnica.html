arnica

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrnɪkə / (AmE), / ˈɑːnɪkə / (BrE)
An ointment or preparation made from the arnica plant, used to treat bruises and reduce inflammation.
ointment for treating bruises

Examples

She applied arnica to her swollen ankle to help reduce the bruising.
Many athletes carry arnica in their first aid kits for quick relief after an injury.
The herbalist suggested trying arnica ointment for his bruised knee.

Etymology

From Latin *Arnica*, taken from Greek *arnikē* (lamb's ear), referring to the plant's soft leaves.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑrnɪkə / (AmE), / ˈɑːnɪkə / (BrE)
a perennial plant of the genus Arnica, with yellow, daisy-like flowers, often used in herbal medicine
a plant used in herbal medicine

Examples

Arnica is widely used in topical creams to help reduce bruises and inflammation.
The yellow flowers of arnica are easily recognizable in alpine meadows.
She prepares a tincture using dried arnica leaves from her garden.

Etymology

From New Latin *Arnica*, the genus name, possibly from Greek *arni* (lamb), from the soft, hairy leaves.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɑrnɪkə / (AmE), / ˈɑːnɪkə / (BrE)
A plant of the daisy family, especially Arnica montana, whose flowers are used as a herbal remedy, particularly for treating bruises and minor injuries.
plant used for treating bruises
medical

Examples

She applied arnica cream to the bruise to reduce swelling.
Many athletes use arnica gel after intense physical activity.
The herbalist recommended arnica for minor bumps and sprains.

Etymology

From New Latin Arnica, from Spanish arnica, probably from Latin ptarmica (sneezing plant), from Ancient Greek ptarmikos (causing sneezing).
