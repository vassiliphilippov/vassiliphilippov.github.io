processed

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɑː.sɛst / (AmE), / ˈprɒ.sɛst / (BrE)
(of food, materials, etc.) prepared or changed from its natural state, especially by using chemicals or machines.
prepared or altered from natural state

Examples

Many breakfast cereals contain a high percentage of processed ingredients.
The factory produces processed cheese by blending natural cheese with emulsifiers.
You should try to eat fewer processed foods for a healthier diet.

Synset Examples

processed ores

Related Terms

  • Antonym: raw - (of food or materials) in a natural or unprocessed state

Etymology

From the verb 'process', ultimately from Latin 'processus' (advance, progression).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01958506-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈprɑː.sɛst / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.sɛst / (BrE)
having been subjected to a special process or treatment, especially to preserve it or make it ready for use
subjected to special treatment

Examples

Many breakfast cereals are made from processed grains.
She prefers to eat fresh vegetables rather than processed foods.
The processed meat had a much longer shelf life than raw meat.

Synset Examples

processed cheeses are easy to spread

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unprocessed - not subjected to a special process or treatment

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'process', from Latin 'processus' (advance, progression).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01850509-s
Synset POS: s
