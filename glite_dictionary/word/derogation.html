derogation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛrəˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛrəˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
(law) The partial removal or reduction of the force or effect of a law, typically by subsequent legislation.
partial repeal or reduction of a law
legal

Examples

The new statute provided a derogation from the earlier law affecting environmental regulation.
Any derogation of constitutional rights is subject to strict scrutiny by the courts.
There was significant debate about whether the amendment constituted a derogation or a full repeal.

Synset Examples

any derogation of the common law is to be strictly construed

Etymology

From Latin derogatio (partial repeal), from derogare (to take away).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛrəˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛrəˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
an expression or act that belittles or disparages someone or something
act of belittling or disparaging

Examples

The article's derogation of her achievements was both unfair and unfounded.
Such constant derogation can damage a person's self-esteem over time.
His speech was filled with subtle derogation rather than open criticism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disparagement - the act of speaking about someone in a derogatory or belittling manner
  • Antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration

Etymology

From Latin derogatio (partial repeal, taking away), from derogare (to take away, detract).
