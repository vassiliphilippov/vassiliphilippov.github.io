Neoceratodus

1. NOUN

/ ˌnioʊˌsɛrəˈtoʊdəs / (AmE), / ˌniːə(ʊ)ˌsɛrəˈtəʊdəs / (BrE)
A genus of Australian lungfishes, including extant species such as the Australian lungfish (Neoceratodus forsteri).
genus of Australian lungfishes

Examples

The Neoceratodus is notable for its ability to breathe both air and water.
Scientists have studied Neoceratodus to better understand the evolution of lungs in vertebrates.
The survival of Neoceratodus in Australian rivers has made it a living fossil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lungfish - a freshwater fish that can breathe air

Etymology

From Greek 'neos' (new) + 'keras' (horn) + 'odous' (tooth), referring to the structure of the fish's teeth.
