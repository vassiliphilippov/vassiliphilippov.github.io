Porte

1. NOUN

/ pɔːrt / (AmE), / pɔːt / (BrE)
the central government or court of the Ottoman Empire in Constantinople, especially as referred to in European diplomatic contexts
the Ottoman imperial government in Constantinople

Examples

European ambassadors were received at the Porte during important diplomatic negotiations.
The Porte played a key role in shaping the fate of the Eastern Mediterranean in the 19th century.
After years of tension, the Porte finally agreed to grant autonomy to several Balkan provinces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sublime Porte - another name for the government of the Ottoman Empire

Etymology

From French 'la Porte' (the Gate), referring to the High Gate (Bab-ı Ali) of the Ottoman imperial palace in Constantinople, the seat of government.
