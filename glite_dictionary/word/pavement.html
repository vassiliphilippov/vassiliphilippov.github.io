pavement

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪvmənt / (AmE), / ˈpeɪvmənt / (BrE)
A paved surface or path, especially one at the side of a road for people to walk on.
paved walkway for pedestrians
british

Examples

We walked along the pavement to avoid the busy road.
The rain made the pavement slippery and dangerous to walk on.
Children were drawing pictures with chalk on the pavement outside their house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sidewalk - a paved path for pedestrians, especially beside a street (US usage)

Etymology

From Latin 'pavimentum', meaning a floor beaten or rammed down.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪvmənt / (AmE), / ˈpeɪvmənt / (BrE)
the hard, paved surface of a road or street, intended for vehicles or, in some regions, pedestrians
surface of a road or walkway

Examples

The workers needed to repair the pavement before the road could reopen to traffic.
Rainwater collected in puddles on the cracked pavement after the storm.
Freshly painted lines marked the center of the new pavement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sidewalk - a path beside a street for people to walk on (US usage)

Etymology

From Old French *pavement*, from Latin *pavimentum* (a beaten firm surface).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪvmənt / (AmE), / ˈpeɪvmənt / (BrE)
A surface layer of hard material, such as concrete, asphalt, or stone, used for paving roads, walkways, or other outdoor areas.
hard surface material for paving

Examples

The crew poured fresh pavement onto the highway to repair the damaged section.
Good quality pavement ensures a smoother and safer driving experience.
The old pavement was cracked, so the parking lot needed resurfacing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asphalt - mixture used for paving roads
  • Synonym: concrete - building material used for paving surfaces

Etymology

From Old French 'pavement', from Latin 'pavimentum' (a floor beaten or rammed down).

Source: WordNet
