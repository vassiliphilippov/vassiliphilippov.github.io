inferiority

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌfɪriˈɔrəti / (AmE), / ɪnˌfɪəriˈɒrɪti / (BrE)
the state of being lower in quality, status, or ability compared to others; a competitive disadvantage
state of being lower in quality or status

Examples

The company's inferiority in technology made it difficult to compete in the market.
Many students struggle with feelings of inferiority when comparing themselves to their peers.
The team's consistent losses were blamed on their inferiority in strategy compared to their rivals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: superiority - the state of being higher in quality or status
  • Synonym: disadvantage - an unfavorable condition or position

Etymology

From Latin 'inferior' (lower) + '-ity' (state or condition).
