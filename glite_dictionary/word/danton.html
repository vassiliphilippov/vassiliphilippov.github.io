Danton

1. PROPN

/ dænˈtɒn / (AmE), / dænˈtɒn / (BrE)
(1759–1794) A French revolutionary leader who played a key role in the early stages of the French Revolution and was executed for opposing the Reign of Terror.
French revolutionary leader (1759–1794)

Examples

Danton was instrumental in overthrowing the monarchy during the French Revolution.
Many historians debate whether Danton's execution was justified or a tragic excess of the Reign of Terror.
The fiery speeches of Danton inspired many Parisians to join the revolutionary cause.

Etymology

From French, the surname of Georges Jacques Danton.
