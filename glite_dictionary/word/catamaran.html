catamaran

1. NOUN

/ ˌkætəməˈræn / (AmE), / ˌkatəməˈran / (BrE)
A boat with two parallel hulls joined by a single deck, designed for stability and speed.
boat with two parallel hulls

Examples

They sailed across the bay in a sleek catamaran.
Renting a catamaran for the afternoon was the highlight of our vacation.
The catamaran glided smoothly over the waves, faster than the other sailboats.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monohull - a boat with a single hull

Etymology

From Tamil *kaṭṭumaram* (tied wood or tree).
