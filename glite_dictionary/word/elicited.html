elicited

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈlɪsɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪsɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a response, feeling, or reaction) called forth or drawn out by a stimulus or provocation.
drawn out by a stimulus

Examples

The experiment measured the elicited reactions of participants to different sound stimuli.
Her elicited laughter suggested she was genuinely amused by the joke.
An elicited response is often used to assess nervous system function in neurological exams.

Synset Examples

evoked potentials
an elicited response

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evoked - (of a response) brought out or produced by a stimulus

Etymology

Past participle of 'elicit', from Latin 'elicitus', past participle of 'elicere' (to draw out).
