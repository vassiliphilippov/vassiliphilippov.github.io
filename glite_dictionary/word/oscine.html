oscine

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑː.saɪn / (AmE), / ˈɒ.sɪn / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of songbirds (members of the suborder Passeri).
relating to songbirds

Examples

The remarkable vocal abilities of oscine birds have fascinated researchers for decades.
Most oscine species learn their songs from adult tutors during early development.
The ornithologist specializes in the study of oscine populations across South America.

Etymology

From Latin 'oscinus' (a bird given to singing), from 'os' (mouth) and 'canere' (to sing).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑː.saɪn / (AmE), / ˈɒ.sɪn / (BrE)
A songbird; any passerine bird belonging to a suborder characterized by a specialized vocal apparatus, enabling complex singing.
songbird with specialized vocal apparatus

Examples

The melodious call of the oscine filled the forest at dawn.
Scientists study the brains of oscines to understand the evolution of vocal learning.
Many common garden birds, such as the robin and finch, are classified as oscines.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: passerine - bird of the order Passeriformes, perching bird
  • Synonym: songbird - a bird known for singing complex vocalizations

Etymology

From Latin 'oscinus', relating to birds of omen, from 'os' (mouth).
