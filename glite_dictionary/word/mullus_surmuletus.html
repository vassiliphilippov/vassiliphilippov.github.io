Mullus surmuletus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌləs sɜrˈmjuːlɪtəs / (AmE), / ˈmʌləs sɜːˈmjuːlɪtəs / (BrE)
A species of brightly colored marine fish, commonly known as the striped red mullet, found in the Mediterranean and eastern Atlantic, notable for its chin barbels.
species of striped red mullet fish

Examples

The Mediterranean coast is home to the Mullus surmuletus, prized for its delicate flavor.
Marine biologists studied the feeding habits of Mullus surmuletus in shallow waters.
Fishermen often catch Mullus surmuletus using nets near rocky reefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: striped red mullet - common name for Mullus surmuletus, a species of fish

Etymology

From Latin; 'Mullus' is the genus name, and 'surmuletus' is the species epithet derived from Latin.
