James Naismith

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒeɪmz ˈneɪˌsmɪθ / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪmz ˈneɪsmɪθ / (BrE)
(1861–1939) Canadian-American educator and physician who invented the game of basketball.
inventor of basketball

Examples

James Naismith invented basketball in 1891 while working as a physical education instructor.
The legacy of James Naismith lives on in the sport of basketball worldwide.
Many basketball halls of fame honor James Naismith as the game's creator.
