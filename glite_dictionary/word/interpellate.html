to interpellate

1. VERB

/ ˌɪn.tərˈpɛ.leɪt / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈpɛ.leɪt / (BrE)
to formally question a member of a government or legislative body about official policy or actions
formally question in government

Examples

The opposition party decided to interpellate the minister about the suspected misuse of public funds.
During yesterday's session, several members interpellated the prime minister on issues of national security.
The senator plans to interpellate the cabinet tomorrow regarding the proposed environmental regulations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: question - to ask someone for information

Etymology

From Latin *interpellare* (to interrupt, to hinder), from *inter-* (between) + *pellere* (to push).
