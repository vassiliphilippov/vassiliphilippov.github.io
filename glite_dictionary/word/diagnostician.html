diagnostician

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪəɡˌnɑːˈstɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəɡˌnɒˈstɪʃən / (BrE)
a doctor or specialist skilled in identifying diseases or conditions by examining symptoms and signs
doctor specializing in medical diagnosis
medical

Examples

The patient was referred to a diagnostician for a thorough evaluation of her symptoms.
As an experienced diagnostician, she quickly identified the rare condition.
Hospitals employ skilled diagnosticians to handle complex medical cases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diagnosis - the act of identifying a disease from its symptoms

Etymology

From French 'diagnosticien', from 'diagnostic' + '-ian'.
