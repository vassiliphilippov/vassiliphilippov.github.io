relation

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a person who is connected to another by blood or marriage; a relative
a person connected by blood or marriage

Examples

She visited her relations in Ireland every summer.
Many of his relations attended the wedding.
After the accident, the hospital contacted the relations of the injured.

Synset Examples

police are searching for relatives of the deceased
he has distant relations back in New Jersey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relative - a person connected by blood or marriage

Etymology

From Latin 'relatio', from 'referre' meaning 'to bring back'.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
(Law) The principle that an act done at a later date is considered by law to have occurred at an earlier specified time.
legal principle deeming acts retroactively effective
legal

Examples

The judge applied the doctrine of relation to backdate the legal filing.
Their lawyer argued that the amendment's relation ensured it was valid from the original date.
Understanding relation is important when determining the effect of certain legal documents.

Synset Examples

his attorney argued for the relation back of the amended complaint to the time the initial complaint was filed

Etymology

From Latin relatio (a bringing back), relating to the legal concept of retroactive effect.
