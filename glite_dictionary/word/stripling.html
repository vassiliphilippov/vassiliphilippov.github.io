stripling

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɪplɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstrɪplɪŋ / (BrE)
a young man or youth who is not yet fully mature
a young man; youth
archaic

Examples

When he arrived at the farm, he was merely a stripling of sixteen.
The old fisherman spoke of his days as a stripling learning the trade.
Though still a stripling, he showed remarkable courage on the battlefield.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: youth - a young person, especially a young man
  • Antonym: adult - a fully grown and mature person

Etymology

From Middle English stripling (youth), from 'strip' (a slender person) + -ling (diminutive suffix).
