Monarda pectinata

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈnɑːrdə pɛkˈtaɪnətə / (AmE), / məˈnɑːdə pɛkˈtɪnətə / (BrE)
An annual herb native to the southern United States, belonging to the mint family and known for its fragrant leaves and flowers.
annual herb of the southern U.S.

Examples

Monarda pectinata is often cultivated in gardens for its aromatic leaves and attractive flowers.
Native to arid regions, Monarda pectinata thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Monarda pectinata in relation to traditional herbal remedies.

Etymology

From the genus name Monarda, after Nicolás Monardes, a Spanish botanist, and Latin 'pectinata' meaning 'comb-like', referring to the plant's inflorescence.
