whaleboat

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪlˌboʊt / (AmE), / ˈweɪl.bəʊt / (BrE)
a long, narrow, and sturdy boat originally designed for hunting whales, capable of quick turning and suitable for use in rough seas.
long, narrow, sturdy boat for rough seas

Examples

The sailors quickly lowered the whaleboat into the turbulent water to reach the stranded vessel.
A traditional whaleboat can handle rough seas better than many modern boats its size.
During the expedition, the historian examined a restored 19th-century whaleboat at the maritime museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lifeboat - a boat kept on a ship for use in an emergency

Etymology

Named for its original use in whale hunting.
