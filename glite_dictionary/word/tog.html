to tog

1. VERB

/ tɑg / (AmE), / tɒg / (BrE)
to dress or provide someone with clothes, often used with 'up' for dressing in a particular style
to dress or clothe someone

Examples

Before the party, she togged her son in his best suit.
They togged up in formal attire for the gala event.
You should tog the children warmly before they go outside.

Synset Examples

Parents must feed and dress their child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dress - to put clothes on oneself or another

Etymology

From Latin *toga* (cloak), by way of English slang.
