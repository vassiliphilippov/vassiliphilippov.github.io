howl

1. VERB

/ haʊl / (AmE), / haʊl / (BrE)
to laugh loudly and uncontrollably
laugh loudly and uncontrollably

Examples

The comedian's jokes made the entire audience howl with laughter.
When he heard the news, he began to howl so hard he could barely breathe.
We all howled at her hilarious impersonation of the teacher.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roar - to laugh very loudly
  • Degree Lower: chuckle - to laugh quietly or to oneself
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00029829-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ haʊl / (AmE), / haʊl / (BrE)
a long, loud, emotional cry or sound, such as one made from pain or strong feeling
loud emotional cry or sound

Examples

The movie audience erupted in howls of laughter at the joke.
A sudden howl of pain broke the silence of the night.
Their protests were met with a howl of outrage from the crowd.

Synset Examples

he gave a howl of pain
howls of laughter
their howling had no effect

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yell - a loud, sharp cry or shout
  • Synonym: shriek - a high-pitched loud scream
  • Antonym: whisper - a soft or quiet way of speaking

Etymology

From Middle English houlen, from Old English hōlian (to yell or howl).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ haʊl / (AmE), / haʊl / (BrE)
a long, loud, and plaintive cry, especially that of a dog, wolf, or other wild animal
long plaintive cry of a dog or wolf

Examples

We heard the howl of a wolf echoing through the night.
The howl of the hound sent chills down my spine.
A distant howl signaled the arrival of the approaching storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yowl - a loud wailing cry, usually of a cat or person

Etymology

From Old Norse *ȳla* ‘howl’ or Middle Dutch *hūlen*.

7. VERB

/ haʊl / (AmE), / haʊl / (BrE)
(of wind, water, or vehicles) to make a long, loud, mournful sound; to produce a noise similar to an animal's howl.
make a loud, mournful noise (wind, water, vehicles)

Examples

During the storm, the wind began to howl through the narrow streets.
The train howled as it sped over the mountain pass.
All night long, the ocean waves howled against the rocky cliffs.

Synset Examples

The wind was howling in the trees
The water roared down the chute

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roar - to make a loud, deep noise, as of a lion or water
  • Antonym: whisper - to make a soft, rustling noise, as of wind

Etymology

From Old Norse *holva* or Middle English *howlen*, imitative in origin.

Source: WordNet
