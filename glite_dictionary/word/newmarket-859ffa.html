newmarket

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːˌmɑrkɪt / (AmE), / ˈnjuːˌmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
A long, close-fitting coat worn for riding, especially fashionable in the 19th century.
long 19th-century riding coat

Examples

The horseman wore a tailored newmarket as he rode out in the morning sun.
In Victorian novels, characters often don a newmarket before a day at the stables.
The museum's exhibit included a beautifully preserved newmarket from the 1840s.

Etymology

Named after Newmarket, a town in England noted for horse racing.
