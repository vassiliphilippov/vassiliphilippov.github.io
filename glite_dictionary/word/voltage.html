voltage

1. NOUN

/ ˈvoʊltɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈvəʊltɪdʒ / (BrE)
the amount of electric potential difference, measured in volts, that drives current between two points in a circuit
electric potential difference, measured in volts

Examples

The device can be damaged if the voltage supplied exceeds its maximum rating.
We need to measure the voltage across the battery terminals using a voltmeter.
Different countries use different standard voltage levels for their electrical outlets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potential difference - the difference in electric potential between two points
  • Antonym: current - flow of electric charge in a circuit

Etymology

From 'volt' (after Alessandro Volta, Italian physicist) + -age (suffix denoting action or result).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvoʊltɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈvəʊltɪdʒ / (BrE)
the difference in electrical potential between two points in a circuit, measured in volts
difference in electrical potential

Examples

The technician measured the voltage across the battery terminals.
High voltage lines carry electricity over long distances.
A drop in voltage can cause electronic devices to malfunction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potential difference - the difference in electric potential between two points; another term for voltage
  • Antonym: current - the flow of electric charge; a related electrical property

Etymology

From 'volt' (unit named after Alessandro Volta) + '-age' (forming nouns indicating a related action or process).
