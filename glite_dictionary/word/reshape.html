to reshape

1. VERB

/ riˈʃeɪp / (AmE), / riːˈʃeɪp / (BrE)
to shape something again or in a different way
to shape again or differently

Examples

The company decided to reshape its marketing strategy to attract younger customers.
After the sculptor dropped the clay, she had to reshape the entire statue.
Technological advances continue to reshape the way we communicate.

Synset Examples

The new foreign minister reshaped the foreign policy of his country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reform - to make changes in order to improve (something)
  • Synonym: to alter - to change or make different

Etymology

From re- (again) + shape.
