innumerable

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈnuːmərəbəl / (AmE), / ɪˈnjuːmərəbl / (BrE)
Too many to be counted; countless.
too many to be counted

Examples

The museum houses innumerable works of art from the Renaissance period.
She faced innumerable obstacles on her journey to success.
There are innumerable reasons why people love traveling abroad.

Synset Examples

countless hours
an infinite number of reasons
innumerable difficulties
the multitudinous seas
myriad stars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: countless - too many to be counted
  • Antonym: numerable - able to be counted

Etymology

From Latin 'innumerabilis' (countless), from 'in-' (not) + 'numerabilis' (able to be counted).
