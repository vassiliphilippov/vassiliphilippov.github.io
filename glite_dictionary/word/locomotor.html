locomotor

1. ADJ

/ ˈloʊ.kəˌmoʊ.tər / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.kəˌməʊ.tə / (BrE)
relating to movement or the ability to move from one place to another, especially in biological or medical contexts.
relating to movement or locomotion
medical

Examples

Damage to the spinal cord can severely impair locomotor function.
The researchers studied the locomotor activity of the mice during the experiment.
Physical therapy is essential for patients recovering their locomotor abilities after an accident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: locomotion - the act or ability of moving from place to place

Etymology

From Latin 'locomotorius', from 'locus' (place) + 'motus' (motion).
