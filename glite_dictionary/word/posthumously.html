posthumously

1. ADV

/ ˈpɑːstjʊməsli / (AmE), / ˈpɒstjʊməsli / (BrE)
After the death of the person in question, especially referring to something done, received, or published.
after someone's death

Examples

The author was awarded the Nobel Prize posthumously for his life's work.
Her memoir was published posthumously, gaining widespread acclaim.
The soldier received a medal of honor posthumously for his bravery in battle.

Synset Examples

these piano pieces were published posthumously
he was honored posthumously

Etymology

From Latin 'posthumus' (after burial, last), altered from 'postumus' (last-born).
