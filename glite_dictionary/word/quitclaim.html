quitclaim

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪtˌkleɪm / (AmE), / ˈkwɪtˌkleɪm / (BrE)
A legal document by which a person transfers their interest, title, or claim in a property to another without making any guarantee of validity.
legal document transferring property claim
legal

Examples

He signed a quitclaim to transfer his rights to the property.
The lawyer prepared a quitclaim for the disputed land.
Without a quitclaim, there could be issues with the title transfer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deed - a legal document transferring ownership of property

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman 'quitclamer', from Old French 'quiter' (to release) + 'clamer' (to claim).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪtˌkleɪm / (AmE), / ˈkwɪtˌkleɪm / (BrE)
a formal legal act of giving up one's right, title, or claim to property and transferring it to another; commonly used in real estate transactions
a legal act of giving up a claim to property
legal

Examples

The property was transferred to her by a quitclaim from her mother.
After the divorce, he signed a quitclaim so that his ex-wife would have the house.
A quitclaim is often used when property is transferred without warranties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: release - the act of giving up a claim, right, or title

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *quiteklaim*, from Old French *quite clamer* (to renounce a claim).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01110546-n
Synset POS: n
