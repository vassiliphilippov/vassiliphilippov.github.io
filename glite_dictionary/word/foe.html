foe

1. NOUN

/ foʊ / (AmE), / fəʊ / (BrE)
an enemy or opponent, especially one that is personal or defined by strong opposition
enemy; opponent

Examples

After years of rivalry, the two foes finally shook hands and ended their conflict.
She realized too late that her greatest foe was not someone else, but her own doubts.
In the story, the hero must confront his foe in a decisive battle.

Synset Examples

they had been political foes for years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enemy - a person who is actively opposed or hostile to someone or something
  • Antonym: friend - a person with whom one has a bond of mutual affection

Etymology

From Old English 'fā', meaning enemy or hostile.

2. NOUN

/ foʊ / (AmE), / fəʊ / (BrE)
an armed adversary or enemy, especially a member of an opposing military force
armed enemy; military adversary

Examples

The soldiers advanced bravely, showing no fear of the foe before them.
During the conflict, he never forgot that his foe was also a human being.
Legends tell of heroes who defeated a much stronger foe against all odds.

Synset Examples

a soldier must be prepared to kill his enemies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enemy - a person who actively opposes or is hostile to someone or something
  • Antonym: ally - a person or organization that cooperates with or helps another

Etymology

From Old English 'fāh' (hostile, enemy).
