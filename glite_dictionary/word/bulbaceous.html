bulbaceous

1. ADJ

/ bʌlˈbeɪʃəs / (AmE), / bʌlˈbeɪʃəs / (BrE)
(of a plant) producing or growing from bulbs.
producing or growing from bulbs

Examples

The tulip is a classic example of a bulbaceous plant.
Many gardeners prefer bulbaceous flowers for their vibrant spring colors.
Some bulbaceous species require specific soil conditions to thrive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bulbous - having bulbs or resembling a bulb

Etymology

From Latin 'bulbus' (bulb) + '-aceous' (having the nature of).
