culprit

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌl.prɪt / (AmE), / ˈkʌl.prɪt / (BrE)
a person who is responsible for a crime or other wrongdoing
person responsible for wrongdoing

Examples

The police quickly found the culprit and arrested him.
After hours of investigation, the true culprit was finally revealed.
The missing sandwiches were traced to a hungry culprit in the kitchen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offender - a person who commits an illegal act
  • Antonym: victim - a person harmed by a crime or wrongdoing

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman 'culprit,' probably from 'Culpable: prest' meaning 'guilty: ready (to answer the charge).'
