ERA

1. NOUN

/ ˌiː ˌɑːr ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌiː ˌɑː ˈɑː / (BrE)
(in baseball) a statistic that measures a pitcher's effectiveness, calculated as the average number of earned runs allowed per nine innings pitched.
baseball statistic for pitchers
american

Examples

The pitcher's impressive ERA has attracted attention across the league.
With a low ERA, she was considered one of the best pitchers on the team.
His ERA climbed after allowing several home runs during the last two games.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earned run average - full form of ERA; baseball pitching statistic

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'earned run average', first used in baseball statistics in late 19th century.
