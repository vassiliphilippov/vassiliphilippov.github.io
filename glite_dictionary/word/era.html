era

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪrə / (AmE), / ˈɪərə / (BrE)
A major division of geological time, typically divided into two or more periods.
major division of geological time

Examples

The extinction of the dinosaurs marked the end of a geological era.
The Mesozoic era is often called the age of reptiles.
Geologists study the different eras to understand Earth’s history.

Etymology

From Latin 'aera' (a number, a chronological epoch), from Greek 'hēra' (era, year).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛrə / (AmE), / ˈɪərə / (BrE)
A period of time marked by distinctive events, characteristics, or conditions, or reckoned from a particular point or event.
distinctive period of time

Examples

The invention of the internet marked the beginning of a new era in communication.
Historians often debate when the medieval era truly ended.
The company is entering an exciting era of innovation and growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: age - a particular period in history marked by notable events or circumstances
  • Synonym: epoch - a notable or distinctive period in history
  • Synonym: period - an interval of time characterized by a particular feature or condition

Etymology

From Late Latin 'aera' (number, era; basis for calculation of time), via Latin and Greek origins.
