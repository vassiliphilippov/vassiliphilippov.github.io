enlistment

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈlɪstmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈlɪstmənt / (BrE)
the act of enrolling or being enrolled, especially in the armed forces
the act of joining the military or other organization

Examples

After his enlistment, he was sent to basic training.
Her enlistment in the army lasted for six years.
The enlistment process can take several weeks to complete.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recruitment - the process of seeking and attracting new people to join an organization, especially the military
  • Antonym: discharge - the act of officially allowing someone to leave the military

Etymology

From enlist + -ment; 'enlist' from French 'enlister'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈlɪstmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈlɪstmənt / (BrE)
A period of time that someone spends serving in the military after officially joining.
period of military service

Examples

After completing his enlistment, he decided to pursue a career in engineering.
During her second enlistment, she was promoted to sergeant.
Many benefits are available to veterans after their enlistment ends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: service - the period during which someone serves in the armed forces

Etymology

From 'enlist' + '-ment', modeled after similar nouns in English.
