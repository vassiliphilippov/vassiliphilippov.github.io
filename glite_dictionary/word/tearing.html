tearing

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
(of emotions or convictions) extremely intense or violent; marked by fervor or vehemence.
extremely intense or violent (emotion, conviction)

Examples

He was in a tearing rage when he heard the news.
The team showed tearing determination to win the championship.
She felt a tearing passion for justice that could not be contained.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of shedding tears, typically as an expression of emotion
the act of shedding tears

Examples

Constant tearing made her eyes red and puffy by the end of the movie.
Allergies often cause itching and unexpected tearing of the eyes.
There was a sudden tearing when he received the sad news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crying - the act of making tears come from your eyes, usually because of emotion or pain
  • Synonym: weeping - shedding tears, usually silently, as an expression of sorrow

Etymology

From the verb 'tear' (to weep), related to Old English 'tēarian' (to tear) and 'tēar' (tear, as in a drop from the eye).
