wag

1. NOUN

/ wæɡ / (AmE), / wæɡ / (BrE)
a witty and amusing person who enjoys making jokes
witty person who tells jokes

Examples

The wag at the dinner party kept everyone laughing all night.
She was known as the office wag for her clever one-liners and jokes.
Every classroom seems to have a resident wag who can't resist making everyone laugh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joker - a person who jokes or jest; a jester
  • Synonym: wit - a person known for their clever humour

2. VERB

/ wæɡ / (AmE), / wæɡ / (BrE)
to move or cause to move rapidly from side to side or up and down, typically repeatedly
move from side to side repeatedly

Examples

The little dog's tail would wag excitedly whenever he saw his owner.
She scolded her son while wagging her finger at him.
The teacher noticed the students' tongues wagging as they gossiped after class.

Synset Examples

The happy dog wagged his tail

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shake - move quickly back and forth or up and down
  • Antonym: still - not moving, stationary

Etymology

From Old English 'wagian' (to shake, move to and fro).
