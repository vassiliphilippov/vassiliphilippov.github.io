introit

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.troʊ.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.trəʊ.ɪt / (BrE)
a piece of music sung or played at the beginning of a church service, especially in the Christian liturgy
opening church service music

Examples

The choir sang a beautiful introit as the congregation arrived.
At the start of the mass, the introit filled the cathedral with solemn music.
The organist practiced the introit to ensure a smooth opening to the service.

Etymology

From Latin 'introitus' (a going in, entrance), from 'introire' (to enter).
