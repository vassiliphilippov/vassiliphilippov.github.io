snooker

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnuk.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈsnuː.kə / (BrE)
A game played on a large rectangular table with pockets, in which players use cues to pot colored balls in a set order, especially popular in Britain.
a cue sport played on a large table
british

Examples

He spent the afternoon playing snooker with his friends at the local club.
The World Snooker Championship is watched by millions of fans each year.
Learning the rules of snooker can be challenging for beginners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: billiards - a cue sport played on a table without pockets or with different rules
  • Synonym: pool - a popular cue sport with simpler rules

Etymology

Perhaps from English dialect 'snook' (to sniff), later used as military slang to mean a beginner or simpleton, then applied to the game.

2. VERB

/ ˈsnʊkər / (AmE), / ˈsnuːkə / (BrE)
to trick, deceive, or dupe someone, especially so they are put at a disadvantage or prevented from succeeding
to deceive or trick (informal)
informal

Examples

I can't believe I was snookered into buying such an expensive ticket.
The team felt snookered by the referee's unexpected call.
She snookered her rivals by leaking false information about the project.

Synset Examples

He was snookered by the con-man's smooth talk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool - to trick or deceive someone
  • Synonym: dupe - to deceive or trick someone, often so that they do something for you

Etymology

From the game of snooker, where a player is left unable to make a clear shot; figurative use from late 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02584469-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈsnʊkər / (AmE), / ˈsnuːkə / (BrE)
(in snooker or similar cue sports) to leave your opponent unable to make a direct shot at the object ball by positioning the cue ball behind another ball.
prevent opponent's direct shot (cue sports)

Examples

He managed to snooker his opponent with a clever safety shot.
If you snooker your rival, they have little chance to score on their next turn.
She snookered me so well that I couldn't even see the object ball.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: block - to obstruct or impede the movement or progress of something or someone

Etymology

From the game 'snooker', which originated as a billiards game among British army officers in India, late 19th century.
