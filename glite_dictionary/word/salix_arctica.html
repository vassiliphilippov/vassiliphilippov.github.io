Salix arctica

1. NOUN

/ ˈsælɪks ɑːrˈktɪkə / (AmE), / ˈsælɪks ɑːkˈtɪkə / (BrE)
a low, creeping shrub found in Arctic Europe and North America, commonly known as the Arctic willow
Arctic creeping shrub

Examples

The Salix arctica thrives in the harsh climate of the Arctic tundra.
Researchers studied the adaptations of Salix arctica to extreme cold conditions.
The small, creeping stems of Salix arctica make it well suited to windy, open landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arctic willow - common name for Salix arctica

Etymology

From Latin: Salix (willow) + arctica (of the Arctic).
