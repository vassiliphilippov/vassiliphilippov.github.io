infeasible

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈfiːzəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈfiːzəbl / (BrE)
not possible to achieve or carry out; impracticable.
not possible; impracticable

Examples

The project became infeasible when funding was cut by half.
Building a bridge across the canyon would be infeasible with the available technology.
Due to time constraints, it was infeasible to complete all the tests before the deadline.

Synset Examples

refloating the sunken ship proved impracticable because of its fragility
a suggested reform that was unfeasible in the prevailing circumstances

Related Terms

  • Antonym: feasible - possible and practical to do easily or conveniently
  • Synonym: impracticable - not able to be carried out or put into practice

Etymology

From prefix in- (not) + feasible, from Old French faisable, from Latin facere (to do).
