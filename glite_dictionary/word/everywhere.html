everywhere

1. ADV

/ ˈɛvriˌwɛr / (AmE), / ˈɛvriwɛə / (BrE)
In or to all places; in every place.
in all places

Examples

Fast food restaurants can be found everywhere in the city.
She looked everywhere for her lost keys, but couldn't find them.
After the announcement, people everywhere started talking about the news.

Synset Examples

You find fast food stores everywhere
people everywhere are becoming aware of the problem
he carried a gun everywhere he went
looked all over for a suitable gift

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nowhere - not in or to any place
  • Degree Lower: somewhere - in or to some place
  • Degree Lower: anywhere - in or to any place (but not necessarily all)

Etymology

From Middle English, combining 'every' and 'where'.
