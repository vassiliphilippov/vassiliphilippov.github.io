Erica

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrɪkə / (AmE), / ˈɛrɪkə / (BrE)
a genus of low, much-branched evergreen shrubs or small trees, commonly known as heaths, native mainly to Europe and South Africa
a genus of evergreen shrubs (heaths)

Examples

The garden was filled with vibrant Erica species that bloomed in late summer.
Many horticulturists cultivate Erica for its hardiness and delicate flowers.
Several varieties of Erica grow wild on the heathland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heath - an evergreen shrub of the genus Erica

Etymology

From Latin *Erica* (heath), from Ancient Greek *erikē* (heather, heath).
