extemporisation

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˌstɛmpəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˌstɛmpərʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of performing or speaking spontaneously without prior preparation or planning
spontaneous performance without preparation
british

Examples

The jazz pianist's extemporisation captivated the audience with its creativity and spontaneity.
During her lecture, she relied on extemporisation to answer unexpected questions.
Shakespearean actors often practice extemporisation during improv workshops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improvisation - the act of creating or performing spontaneously without preparation

Etymology

From Latin 'ex tempore' (out of the moment) + English '-isation'.
