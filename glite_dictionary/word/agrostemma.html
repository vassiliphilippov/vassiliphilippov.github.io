Agrostemma

1. NOUN

/ ˌæɡroʊˈstɛmə / (AmE), / ˌæɡrɒˈstɛmə / (BrE)
A small genus of flowering plants in the family Caryophyllaceae, including the corn cockle, known for its pink or purple flowers.
genus of flowering plants including corn cockle

Examples

Agrostemma is most famously represented by the corn cockle, once common in European grain fields.
Gardeners sometimes plant Agrostemma for its delicate, showy flowers.
Researchers studied various species within the Agrostemma genus for their ecological impact.

Etymology

From Greek agros (field) + stemma (garland).

/ ˌæɡrəˈstɛmə / (AmE), / ˌaɡrəˈstɛmə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Caryophyllaceae, which includes the corn cockles.
plant genus including corn cockles

Examples

The fields were dotted with Agrostemma, adding bright spots of pink among the wheat.
Botanists conducted a study on various species within the Agrostemma genus.
The decline of Agrostemma populations has been noted in several regions due to changes in agriculture.
