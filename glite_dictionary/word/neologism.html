neologism

1. NOUN

/ niˈɑlədʒɪzəm / (AmE), / niˈɒlədʒɪzəm / (BrE)
the act of inventing a new word or phrase
act of inventing a new word

Examples

The neologism of 'googling' has become widely accepted in everyday language.
Language evolves thanks to the constant neologism by creative writers and speakers.
The conference will feature a workshop on neologism and the evolution of language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coinage - the invention of a new word or phrase

Etymology

From French 'néologisme', from Greek 'neos' (new) + 'logos' (word).

2. NOUN

/ niˈɑːləˌdʒɪzəm / (AmE), / niˈɒlədʒɪzəm / (BrE)
a newly coined word or expression, or the process of inventing new words or expressions
newly coined word or expression

Examples

The term 'selfie' is a modern neologism that gained widespread popularity with the rise of social media.
Writers often use neologisms to capture new ideas or trends.
Some people find it hard to keep up with all the neologisms introduced each year in technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coinage - the invention of a new word or phrase

Etymology

From French néologisme, from Greek neos (new) + logos (word).
