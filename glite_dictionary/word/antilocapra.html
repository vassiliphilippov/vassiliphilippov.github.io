Antilocapra

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tɪ.ləˈkæp.rə / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪ.ləˈkæp.rə / (BrE)
A genus of mammals in the family Antilocapridae, containing only one species: the pronghorn of North America.
genus of mammals (pronghorn)

Examples

The Antilocapra is notable as the only surviving member of the Antilocapridae family.
North America's fastest land mammal, the pronghorn, belongs to the genus Antilocapra.
Zoologists studied the evolution of Antilocapra to understand adaptations to open grasslands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pronghorn - the only species in the genus Antilocapra

Etymology

From Greek 'antílo-/ἀντίλοος' (antelope) and Latin 'capra' (goat).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæntɪloʊˈkæprə / (AmE), / ˌæntɪləʊˈkæprə / (BrE)
The genus of mammals in the family Antilocapridae that contains the pronghorn, native to North America.
genus containing the pronghorn

Examples

The only member of the genus Antilocapra is the pronghorn, found in western North America.
Scientists study the characteristics unique to Antilocapra to better understand its evolutionary history.
Unlike other ruminants, Antilocapra retains certain primitive features in its anatomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pronghorn - a North American mammal, sole species in Antilocapra genus

Etymology

From Greek 'antí-' (against, resembling) + 'lókos' (a type of animal) + 'kápros' (he-goat), referring to resemblance to both goat and antelope.
