hypervelocity

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpərvəˈlɑsəti / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəvəˈlɒsəti / (BrE)
a very high velocity, especially one great enough to cause unique physical effects in collisions, typically above 3,000 meters per second
extremely high velocity (esp. of impacts)

Examples

The spacecraft was designed to withstand hypervelocity impacts from micrometeoroids.
Scientists studied the crater formed by the hypervelocity collision.
Protective shielding is used to guard against hypervelocity debris in orbit.

Synset Examples

the meteorites struck the earth with hypervelocity impacts

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: velocity - speed in a given direction

Etymology

From hyper- (meaning 'over, beyond') + velocity.
