Hibiscus heterophyllus

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈbɪskəs ˌhɛt̬əroʊˈfɪləs / (AmE), / hɪˈbɪskəs ˌhɛtərəʊˈfɪləs / (BrE)
An Australian tree or shrub in the mallow family (Malvaceae), known for its acid-tasting leaves and large flowers.
Australian tree with acid leaves

Examples

The leaves of the Hibiscus heterophyllus are noticeably acidic when tasted.
Many Australian gardens include a Hibiscus heterophyllus for its striking flowers and foliage.
Researchers studied how the Hibiscus heterophyllus adapts to different soil types.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rose mallow - a common name for various Hibiscus species

Etymology

From Latin: 'Hibiscus' (the genus), from Greek hibiskos (mallow); 'heterophyllus' from Greek hetero- (different) and -phyllus (leaf).
