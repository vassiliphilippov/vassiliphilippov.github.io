Dermochelys

1. NOUN

/ dɜrˈmoʊkɪliz / (AmE), / dɜːˈməʊkɪliːz / (BrE)
The genus of large marine turtles known as leatherback turtles, belonging to the family Dermochelyidae.
genus of leatherback turtles

Examples

The Dermochelys genus is represented today by only one living species, the leatherback turtle.
Researchers are studying the migratory patterns of Dermochelys in the Atlantic Ocean.
Fossils of Dermochelys have been found in several parts of the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leatherback turtle - the largest species of sea turtle, known for its leathery shell

Etymology

From Greek 'derma' (skin) + 'chelys' (turtle), referring to the leathery shell.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɜːr.məˈkiː.lɪs / (AmE), / ˌdɜː.məˈkiː.lɪs / (BrE)
A genus of very large sea turtles, including only the leatherback turtle, and the type genus of the family Dermochelyidae.
genus of large sea turtles (leatherbacks)

Examples

The leatherback turtle is the only living species in the genus Dermochelys.
Scientists have studied Dermochelys populations to understand their migratory patterns.
Fossil evidence suggests the ancient Dermochelys lineage dates back millions of years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leatherback turtle - a very large marine turtle of the genus Dermochelys

Etymology

From Greek derma (skin) + chelys (turtle).
