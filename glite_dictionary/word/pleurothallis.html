Pleurothallis

1. NOUN

/ pluːr.oʊˈθæl.ɪs / (AmE), / plʊə.rəʊˈθæl.ɪs / (BrE)
A large genus of epiphytic or lithophytic orchids native to tropical America.
genus of tropical American orchids

Examples

The Pleurothallis genus is renowned for its diverse and unusual orchid flowers.
Many collectors value Pleurothallis species for their adaptability to terrarium environments.
The tiny blooms of Pleurothallis are often overlooked due to their small size.

Etymology

From Greek 'pleura' (side, rib) and 'thallos' (branch, shoot), referring to aspects of the plant's morphology.

2. NOUN

/ pluːrəˈθælɪs / (AmE), / plʊə.rəˈθæl.ɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small, tufted orchids with leathery or fleshy leaves and racemes bearing one or many small flowers.
a genus of small tufted orchids

Examples

The greenhouse was filled with a variety of Pleurothallis species collected from South America.
He specialized in cultivating rare orchids, especially those in the Pleurothallis genus.
Botanists were excited to discover a new Pleurothallis species growing in the remote cloud forests.

Etymology

From Greek 'pleura' (side/rib) + 'thallos' (a sprout), referring to aspects of the plant's appearance.
