vomit

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɑmɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɒmɪt / (BrE)
the act of ejecting the contents of the stomach through the mouth, usually as a reflex
reflex act of ejecting stomach contents

Examples

He ran to the bathroom to avoid making a mess with his vomit.
The strong smell of vomit lingered in the air after the party.
Doctors collected a sample of the patient's vomit for analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emesis - technical term for vomiting
  • Degree Lower: retching - the act of making efforts to vomit without ejecting anything

Etymology

From Latin vomitus (act of vomiting), from vomere (to vomit).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɑː.mɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɒm.ɪt / (BrE)
the matter that is expelled from the stomach through the mouth when someone vomits
matter expelled in vomiting

Examples

There was so much vomit on the floor that they had to call for help to clean it up.
The child was embarrassed when he got vomit on his shirt at school.
The smell of vomit lingered in the room long after the party ended.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emesis - the act of vomiting, or the matter vomited (medical)

Etymology

From Latin vomitus, from vomere (to vomit).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14880143-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈvɑː.mɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɒm.ɪt / (BrE)
to force the contents of the stomach up through the mouth; to throw up
eject stomach contents; throw up

Examples

He felt so sick after the ride that he had to vomit behind the bushes.
The smell of the spoiled food almost made her vomit.
Doctors advised her to see a specialist after she began to vomit frequently.

Synset Examples

After drinking too much, the students vomited
He purged continuously
The patient regurgitated the food we gave him last night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to throw up - to bring food or drink up from your stomach out through your mouth; to vomit
  • Synonym: to regurgitate - to bring swallowed food back up into the mouth

Etymology

From Latin 'vomitare', frequentative of 'vomere' (to vomit).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00076153-v
Synset POS: v
