verbosity

1. NOUN

/ vərˈbɑːsəti / (AmE), / vəːˈbɒsəti / (BrE)
the quality of using more words than needed, especially when expressing something in a lengthy or wordy way
use of too many words

Examples

The professor was criticized for the verbosity of his lectures, which often confused the students.
Despite the verbosity of the report, it failed to provide any clear conclusions.
Editors often try to reduce verbosity to make articles easier to read.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conciseness - the quality of being brief and to the point
  • Synonym: prolixity - excessive length or wordiness in speech or writing

Etymology

From Latin 'verbositas', from 'verbosus' (wordy).
