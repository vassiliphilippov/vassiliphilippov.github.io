piping

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpaɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of playing a pipe or bagpipes, especially as a performance or ceremonial activity
playing a pipe or bagpipes

Examples

The sound of piping echoed across the valley during the festival.
She learned traditional Scottish piping from her grandfather.
The wedding procession was led by piping and drums.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipe playing - the act of playing a pipe instrument
  • Antonym: drumming - the act of playing drums

Etymology

From pipe + -ing, denoting the action of playing a pipe.

Synset POS: n

2. ADV

/ ˈpaɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpaɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
(adverb, usually in 'piping hot') to a very great or extreme degree (of heat); extremely.
extremely (of heat); very hot

Examples

Be careful—the soup is piping hot and might burn your mouth.
She served the coffee piping hot, just the way he liked it.
The bread came out of the oven piping hot and smelled delicious.

Synset Examples

the casserole was piping hot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scalding - extremely hot; able to burn
  • Antonym: lukewarm - moderately warm; not hot

Etymology

From the verb 'pipe' (to play on a pipe), with reference to the hot steam from a pipe.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r

3. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpaɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
Long tubes made of metal or plastic used to carry water, oil, gas, etc., especially as part of a system.
tubing that carries fluids or gases

Examples

The building was fitted with new piping to improve its water supply.
Engineers inspected the piping for signs of corrosion.
A leak in the piping caused water damage in the basement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pipe - A tube for conveying liquids or gases

Etymology

From 'pipe' + '-ing', denoting material or system of pipes.

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpaɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
a narrow, corded strip of fabric sewn into the seams of clothing or upholstery as an edging or decoration
strip of fabric cord for edging seams

Examples

The dress was finished with a delicate piping along the collar and sleeves.
She carefully stitched piping into the cushion's seams for a professional look.
Adding piping can help highlight the shape and structure of a garment.

Etymology

From 'pipe' + '-ing', referring to the tube-like effect when inserted between seams.
