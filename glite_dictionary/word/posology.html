posology

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈsɑlədʒi / (AmE), / pɒˈsɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of pharmacology concerned with the determination and regulation of drug dosages
study of drug dosage determination
medical

Examples

The pharmacist specialized in posology to ensure patients received safe and effective drug dosages.
Advances in posology have improved the accuracy of medication administration in hospitals.
Studying posology is essential for those developing new pharmaceutical treatments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pharmacology - the branch of medicine concerned with the uses, effects, and modes of action of drugs

Etymology

From Greek *posos* (how much) + *-logy* (study of).
