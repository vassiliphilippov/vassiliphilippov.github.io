wholeheartedly

1. ADV

/ ˌhoʊlˈhɑrtɪdli / (AmE), / ˌhəʊlˈhɑːtɪdli / (BrE)
in a way that shows complete sincerity, enthusiasm, or commitment; without any reservation or holding back
with complete sincerity or commitment

Examples

She wholeheartedly supports her team's decision and has no doubts about it.
They wholeheartedly embraced the new project, eager to see it succeed.
I wholeheartedly agree with your view on education reform.

Synset Examples

he adopted wholeheartedly some of the policies that he had previously criticized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wholehearted - showing complete sincerity and commitment

Etymology

From 'wholehearted' + '-ly', where 'wholehearted' dates from the mid 19th century, meaning 'with one's whole heart'.
