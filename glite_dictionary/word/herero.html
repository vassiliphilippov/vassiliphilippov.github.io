Herero

1. NOUN

/ həˈreɪroʊ / (AmE), / həˈrɛərəʊ / (BrE)
A Bantu language spoken by the Herero people in Namibia, Botswana, and Angola.
a Bantu language of southern Africa

Examples

He is studying Herero as part of his research on African languages.
Few young people in the city still speak Herero fluently.
The translation was provided in both English and Herero for the local community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bantu - a large group of African languages and related peoples

2. NOUN

/ həˈrɛroʊ / (AmE), / həˈrɛərəʊ / (BrE)
a member of a Bantu-speaking pastoral people living mainly in Namibia, Botswana, and Angola
a member of a Bantu ethnic group in southwestern Africa

Examples

The Herero maintained their traditional way of life even after colonial rule was imposed.
Many Herero live in the central region of Namibia.
The annual festival celebrates the vibrant culture of the Herero.

Etymology

From Herero, the self-designation of the people.
