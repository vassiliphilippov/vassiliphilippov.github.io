thornless

1. ADJ

/ ˈθɔrn.ləs / (AmE), / ˈθɔːn.ləs / (BrE)
(of a plant or stem) having no thorns.
having no thorns

Examples

We bought a thornless variety of rose for the children’s garden.
The thornless stems make pruning much easier.
I prefer thornless blackberries because they are safer to pick.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: thorny - having many thorns

Etymology

Formed from 'thorn' + '-less' (Old English þorn + suffix -less, meaning 'without').
