suborbital

1. ADJ

/ ˌsʌbˈɔrbɪtəl / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˈɔːbɪtəl / (BrE)
situated on or below the floor of the eye socket, especially referring to anatomical structures.
below or near the floor of the eye socket (anatomy)
medical

Examples

A fracture of the suborbital bone can affect vision and eye movement.
Some fish have a distinctive suborbital ridge below their eyes.
The suborbital area is vulnerable in certain facial injuries.

Synset Examples

a suborbital bone

Etymology

from Latin 'sub-' (under) + 'orbita' (orbit, track)

2. ADJ

/ sʌbˈɔrbɪtl̩ / (AmE), / sʌbˈɔːbɪtəl / (BrE)
(of a trajectory, flight, or vehicle) reaching space but not completing one full orbit around a celestial body.
trajectory not completing a full orbit

Examples

The company conducted its first suborbital test flight last year.
A suborbital rocket briefly entered space before returning to Earth.
This suborbital mission will gather data for future orbital launches.

Synset Examples

the first manned suborbital flight

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: orbital - relating to or occurring in an orbit

Etymology

From the prefix 'sub-' (meaning 'under' or 'less than') and 'orbital', based on 'orbit'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02990988-a
Synset POS: a
