to nationalize

1. VERB

/ ˈnæʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnæʃənəˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something national in character, scope, or relevance
make national in character or scope

Examples

The media nationalized the achievements of the athletes, celebrating them as symbols of national pride.
Efforts were made to nationalize local traditions during the centenary celebrations.
The organization aimed to nationalize education standards across all regions.

Synset Examples

His heroic deeds were nationalized by the press

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to internationalize - to make international in character or scope

Etymology

From French 'nationaliser' or late Latin 'natio' (nation) + '-ize'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00579372-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈnæʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnaʃ(ə)nəlaɪz / (BrE)
(of a government) to bring an industry, company, or asset under state control or ownership.
bring under state ownership

Examples

The government decided to nationalize the failing railway system to improve its management.
Several industries were nationalized after the revolution to ensure public welfare.
There was a heated debate about whether to nationalize major energy companies.

Synset Examples

Mitterand nationalized the banks

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to privatize - to transfer ownership from the public sector to private individuals or organizations

Etymology

From nation + -alize, modeled after French nationaliser.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00412472-v
Synset POS: v
