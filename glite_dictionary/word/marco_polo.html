Marco Polo

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɑːrkoʊ ˈpoʊloʊ / (AmE), / ˌmɑːkəʊ ˈpəʊləʊ / (BrE)
A Venetian explorer and traveler of the 13th century, known for his extensive journeys through Asia and his service to Kublai Khan.
Venetian explorer and traveler of Asia

Examples

Marco Polo wrote detailed accounts of his travels in China that fascinated readers across Europe.
Many historians regard Marco Polo as one of the greatest explorers of all time.
The journey of Marco Polo influenced later explorers such as Christopher Columbus.

Etymology

Italian name; Marco is the Italian form of Mark. Polo is a Venetian family name.
