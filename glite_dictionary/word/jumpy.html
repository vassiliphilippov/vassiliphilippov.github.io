jumpy

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒʌmpi / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌmpi / (BrE)
causing or characterized by sudden jolts and irregular movements; not moving smoothly.
marked by jolts and irregular movement

Examples

The old car has a very jumpy ride on rough roads.
After the repairs, the washing machine no longer felt so jumpy during the spin cycle.
The video playback was jumpy, making it difficult to follow the action.

Synset Examples

a rough ride

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth - moving in a continuous and even manner, without sudden movements
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02251311-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʒʌmpi / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌmpi / (BrE)
anxious or nervous, especially so as to be easily startled or agitated
nervous; easily startled

Examples

She gets jumpy whenever she hears footsteps outside her window at night.
The loud noises from the construction site made him feel especially jumpy all morning.
After watching the horror movie, the children were so jumpy that they jumped at every little sound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nervous - easily agitated or alarmed; tending to be anxious
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions

Synset POS: s
