galactosis

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˌlækˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ɡəˌlækˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
the secretion or production of milk, especially by the mammary glands
the secretion of milk
medical

Examples

After childbirth, galactosis occurs naturally as the body prepares to feed the newborn.
Certain hormonal changes can stimulate galactosis in mammals.
Researchers studied the process of galactosis in dairy cows to improve milk production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lactation - the secretion of milk by the mammary glands

Etymology

From Greek 'galaktos' (milk) and '-osis' (process or condition).
