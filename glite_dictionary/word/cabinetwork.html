cabinetwork

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæbɪˌnɛtwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈkæbɪnɛtwɜːk / (BrE)
the craft or activity of making high-quality furniture, especially from wood
the craft of making fine furniture

Examples

He learned the art of cabinetwork from a master craftsman.
The museum displayed several examples of eighteenth-century cabinetwork.
Precision and skill are essential in the field of cabinetwork.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabinetmaking - the craft of making cabinets and other fine woodworking furniture
  • Degree Lower: carpentry - the trade or activity of making and repairing wooden objects and structures

Etymology

Formed from 'cabinet' (a type of furniture) + 'work' (meaning craft or activity).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæbɪˌnɛtwɜːrk / (AmE), / ˈkabɪnɛtwɜːk / (BrE)
woodwork that is finely finished and constructed by a cabinetmaker, typically by hand
finely made woodwork by a cabinetmaker

Examples

The antique desk is a masterpiece of Victorian cabinetwork and craftsmanship.
He spent years perfecting his skills in cabinetwork before opening his own workshop.
Many historic homes feature exquisite cabinetwork in their doors and built-in furniture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joinery - woodworking joints and construction
  • Synonym: carpentry - the trade of cutting and constructing wood

Etymology

From 'cabinet' (furniture) + 'work', referring to the craft of making cabinets and similar items.
