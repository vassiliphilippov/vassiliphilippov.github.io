assize

1. NOUN

/ əˈsaɪz / (AmE), / əˈsaɪz / (BrE)
a writ formerly issued by a court to the sheriff for the recovery of property
historic legal writ for property recovery
legal archaic

Examples

The court issued an assize to help the landowner reclaim his property.
Medieval sheriffs were often tasked with enforcing an assize after disputes over land ownership.
The concept of the assize became obsolete as modern legal systems developed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: writ - a formal written order issued by a body with administrative or judicial jurisdiction

Etymology

From Old French 'assise', from Medieval Latin 'assisa', meaning a session or sitting.

2. NOUN

/ əˈsaɪz / (AmE), / əˈsaɪz / (BrE)
an official regulation or ordinance that determines the weights and measures of items offered for sale, especially as issued by authorities in medieval England.
official regulation of weights and measures
legal archaic

Examples

In medieval England, an assize fixed the price of bread and ale to ensure fairness in trade.
The king issued a new assize to regulate the standard weight for loaves of bread.
Merchants who violated the assize of weights could face heavy fines or public punishment.

Etymology

From Old French 'assise' (session, sitting, decree), from Medieval Latin 'assisa' (session), from Latin 'assidere' (to sit beside).
