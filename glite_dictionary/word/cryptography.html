cryptography

1. NOUN

/ krɪpˈtɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / krɪpˈtɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
The practice or study of writing or solving codes and ciphers to conceal information.
the practice of writing codes and ciphers

Examples

The field of cryptography has become crucial for securing online transactions.
He studied cryptography to learn how to protect sensitive information from hackers.
During the war, experts in cryptography developed new ways to send secret messages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encryption - the process of converting information into a code
  • Antonym: decryption - the process of converting encoded data back into its original form

Etymology

From Greek kryptos (hidden) + -graphia (writing).

2. NOUN

/ krɪpˈtɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / krɪpˈtɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the science or practice of designing, analyzing, and deciphering codes and ciphers to secure communication
science of codes and ciphers

Examples

Cryptography is essential for protecting sensitive data in online transactions.
The ancient art of cryptography has evolved significantly with the advent of computers.
By studying cryptography, she learned how to both encode and decode secret messages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encryption - the process of converting information into code
  • Antonym: decryption - the process of converting coded data back into plain text

Etymology

From Greek kryptos (hidden) + -graphia (writing).
