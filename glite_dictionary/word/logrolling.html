logrolling

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔɡˌroʊlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɒɡˌrəʊlɪŋ / (BrE)
the sport of balancing and rapidly rotating a floating log in the water, often as a competition between two people
sport of balancing on floating logs

Examples

Professional lumberjacks demonstrated incredible balance and agility during the logrolling competition at the fair.
Many outdoor festivals in North America feature logrolling as a highlight event for spectators.
He trained every summer to improve his logrolling skills and win local tournaments.

Etymology

From 'log' (tree trunk) and 'rolling', indicating the action involved.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɔɡˌroʊlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɒɡˌrəʊlɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of exchanging favors, especially by legislators voting for each other's proposed legislation to achieve mutual benefits
exchange of favors among legislators

Examples

The senator was accused of logrolling to get support for the new highway project.
Political analysts argued that logrolling undermined transparent lawmaking in Congress.
Through strategic logrolling, several unpopular bills managed to pass the legislature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reciprocity - mutual exchange of privileges
  • Synonym: pork-barrel - government spending for localized projects to please voters

Etymology

Originally referred to the practice of neighbors helping each other roll logs, later used figuratively in U.S. politics.
