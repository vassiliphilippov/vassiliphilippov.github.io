Caulophyllum thalictroides

1. NOUN

/ kɔləˈfɪləm θəˌlɪktrəˈdiz / (AmE), / kɔːlɒˈfɪləm θəˌlɪktrɔɪˈdiːz / (BrE)
A tall herb native to eastern North America and Asia, with blue berrylike fruit and a thick, knotty rootstock formerly used medicinally; also known as blue cohosh.
species of plant, blue cohosh

Examples

The roots of Caulophyllum thalictroides were once used in traditional medicine by Native Americans.
Gardeners appreciate Caulophyllum thalictroides for its ornamental blue berries and lush foliage.
Researchers are studying potential compounds found in Caulophyllum thalictroides for new medicinal applications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue cohosh - common name for Caulophyllum thalictroides

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek *kaulos* (stem) and *phyllon* (leaf); *thalictroides* refers to a resemblance to the genus Thalictrum.
