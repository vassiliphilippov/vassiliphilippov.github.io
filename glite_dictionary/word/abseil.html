abseil

1. VERB

/ ˈæb.seɪl / (AmE), / ˈæb.seɪl / (BrE)
to descend a steep slope or vertical surface by sliding down a rope that is anchored at the top, usually with the aid of a harness and friction device
descend using a rope (climbing)

Examples

The team decided to abseil down the cliff to reach the ledge below.
Before you abseil, double-check that your harness and rope are secure.
She had never abseiled before, but the instructor guided her through each step.

Synset Examples

The ascent was easy — roping down the mountain would be much more difficult and dangerous
You have to learn how to abseil when you want to do technical climbing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rappel - descend a vertical surface using a rope; chiefly US English

Etymology

From German abseilen, from ab- (down) + Seil (rope).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæbˌseɪl / (AmE), / ˈabseɪl / (BrE)
a controlled descent down a vertical surface, such as a cliff or wall, using a rope secured at a higher point
controlled descent down a rock face using a rope

Examples

The climbers prepared to abseil down the cliff face after reaching the summit.
She completed her first abseil with the help of an experienced instructor.
A rope was anchored at the top to ensure a safe abseil for all participants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rappel - to descend a rock face by using a rope (chiefly American English equivalent)

Etymology

From German *abseilen* (to rope down), from *ab-* (down, off) + *Seil* (rope).
