revise

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈvaɪz / (AmE), / rɪˈvʌɪz / (BrE)
a version of written work that has been rewritten or revised; the act of editing or rewriting something, especially in preparation for publication or review
a rewritten or edited version; act of rewriting
british

Examples

The author made a third revise of the manuscript before submission.
Please turn in your revise by Friday’s deadline.
The professor asked for a major revise after reading the initial report.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revision - the action of revising, especially for exams or written material

Etymology

From Latin 'revidēre' (to look at again) via French 'reviser'.

WordNet ID: oewn-06440646-n

2. VERB

/ rɪˈvaɪz / (AmE), / rɪˈvaɪz / (BrE)
to change something, especially a piece of writing, in order to correct or improve it
change to correct or improve

Examples

She decided to revise her essay after receiving feedback from her professor.
The publisher asked the author to revise several chapters before printing the book.
Before submitting your application, make sure to revise any spelling or grammatical errors.

Synset Examples

revise a thesis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to edit - to prepare written material for publication by correcting or modifying it
  • Synonym: to amend - to make minor changes to a text or document

Etymology

From Latin 'revidēre' (to look at again), from 're-' (again) + 'vidēre' (to see).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00998147-v
Synset POS: v
