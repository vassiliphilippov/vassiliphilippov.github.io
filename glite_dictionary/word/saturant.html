saturant

1. NOUN

/ ˈsætʃəˌrənt / (AmE), / ˈsætʃər(ə)nt / (BrE)
a substance used to saturate or impregnate another material, often to improve its properties or performance
substance for saturation or impregnation

Examples

The engineer applied a saturant to the fabric to make it water-resistant.
A special saturant was added to enhance the durability of the rubber compound.
Some artists use a saturant to intensify the colors in their canvas.

Synset Examples

I have to coat my synthetic fiber jacket with a saturant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to saturate - to soak or impregnate thoroughly

Etymology

From 'saturate' + '-ant', patterned after other agent nouns.
