Ladino

1. NOUN

/ ləˈdiːnoʊ / (AmE), / ləˈdiːnəʊ / (BrE)
a Spanish-derived language traditionally spoken by Sephardic Jews, especially using Hebrew script
Judaeo-Spanish language of Sephardic Jews

Examples

My grandmother still speaks Ladino at home with her friends from the community.
The ancient melodies were sung in Ladino, preserving centuries-old Sephardic traditions.
There are efforts underway to document and revitalize the Ladino language among younger generations.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Ladino', originally meaning 'Latin' or 'Latinized', referring to language or culture.
