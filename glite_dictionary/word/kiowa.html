Kiowa

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaɪ.oʊ.wə / (AmE), / ˈkaɪ.əʊ.wə / (BrE)
the Tanoan language spoken by the Kiowa people, native to parts of the southern United States
a Tanoan language of the Kiowa people

Examples

She is studying Kiowa to help preserve her heritage.
There are few fluent speakers of Kiowa left today.
The university offers a course in Kiowa language and culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kiowa - a member of a Native American people of the southern US

2. NOUN

/ ˈkaɪ.oʊ.wə / (AmE), / ˈkaɪ.əʊ.wə / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people originally from the southwestern United States, especially Oklahoma, Texas, and New Mexico
member of Southwestern Native American people

Examples

Many Kiowa still maintain traditional ceremonies and crafts.
The history of the Kiowa people is deeply connected to the Great Plains.
She interviewed a prominent Kiowa elder for her research on Native American cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kiowa - the language of the Kiowa people

Etymology

From the Kiowa language for 'principal people' (Káʼigù) or possibly via other tribes' names for them.
