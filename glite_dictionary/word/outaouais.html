Outaouais

1. PROPN

/ ˌuːtəˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˌuːtəˈweɪ / (BrE)
A river in southeastern Canada that forms part of the boundary between Quebec and Ontario, flowing into the Saint Lawrence River near Montreal.
river on the Quebec-Ontario border, Canada

Examples

The Outaouais is a vital source of hydroelectric power for the surrounding regions.
Many communities along the Outaouais rely on fishing and tourism.
The city of Gatineau lies on the north bank of the Outaouais River.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ottawa River - another name for the Outaouais, used commonly in English

Etymology

From French 'Outaouais,' itself from Algonquin 'Odàwà,' referring to the Ottawa people.
