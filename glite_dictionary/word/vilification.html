vilification

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɪlɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɪlɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
rude or abusive speech intended to offend, insult, or defame someone
abusive or insulting speech

Examples

The constant vilification of public officials undermines trust in government.
She grew tired of the vilification directed at her online.
Their campaign relied heavily on the vilification of their opponents.

Synset Examples

when a student made a stupid mistake he spared them no abuse
they yelled insults at the visiting team

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abuse - rude or insulting language or behavior
  • Synonym: insult - an offensive remark or action

Etymology

From Latin 'vilificare' (to make vile, despise), via Medieval Latin 'vilificatio'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌvɪl.ə.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɪl.ɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of saying or writing very harsh and critical things about someone or something; slanderous defamation
slander; harsh criticism

Examples

The politician's reputation suffered from constant vilification in the media.
Online vilification can have serious consequences on a person's mental health.
She took legal action against the tabloid for their repeated vilification of her character.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defamation - the act of damaging someone's reputation by making false statements
  • Synonym: slander - the action or crime of making a false spoken statement damaging to a person's reputation
  • Antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration

Etymology

From Latin 'vilificare' (to make vile), via Late Latin 'vilificatio'.
