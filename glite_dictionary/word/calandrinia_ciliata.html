Calandrinia ciliata

1. NOUN

/ kəˌlænˈdrɪniə sɪˈlaɪətə / (AmE), / kəˌlanˈdrɪnɪə sɪˈlaɪətə / (BrE)
A succulent, carpet-forming plant with small, brilliant reddish-pink flowers, native to the southwestern United States.
succulent plant with reddish-pink flowers

Examples

The sandy hillside was covered in a dense mat of Calandrinia ciliata.
Botanists often admire the vibrant color of Calandrinia ciliata in its native habitat.
After the winter rains, Calandrinia ciliata blooms in abundance across desert landscapes.

Etymology

From New Latin, genus name Calandrinia, named after botanist Jean Louis Calandrini; species epithet from Latin 'ciliata' meaning fringed with fine hairs.
