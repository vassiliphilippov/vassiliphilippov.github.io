Mahonia nervosa

1. NOUN

/ məˈhoʊniə nɚˈvoʊsə / (AmE), / məˈhəʊniə nɜːˈvəʊsə / (BrE)
A small evergreen shrub with grey-green leaves, yellow flowers, and blue berries, native to western North America.
species of small evergreen shrub

Examples

The shady garden bed is filled with Mahonia nervosa that bloom beautifully each spring.
Hikers sometimes spot Mahonia nervosa growing in the understory of Pacific Northwest forests.
The berries of Mahonia nervosa attract many birds during late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Oregon grape - common name for shrubs of the Mahonia genus
  • Degree Higher: Mahonia aquifolium - a related species of Mahonia with holly-like leaves

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus Mahonia named after Bernard McMahon, and 'nervosa' from Latin meaning 'veined' or 'marked with nerves'.
