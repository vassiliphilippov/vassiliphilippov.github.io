demodulator

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈmɑdʒəˌleɪtər / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒdjʊˌleɪtə / (BrE)
an electronic device that extracts the original information from a modulated carrier wave, typically in radio or television receivers
device that extracts information from a carrier wave

Examples

The radio's demodulator converts the broadcast signal into audible sound.
Without a working demodulator, the television cannot display any channels.
Modern smartphones contain a built-in demodulator for wireless communication.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: modulator - device that imposes a signal onto a carrier wave
  • Synonym: detector - electronic device that detects signals such as radio waves

Etymology

From de- (removal of) + modulator, referring to undoing modulation.
