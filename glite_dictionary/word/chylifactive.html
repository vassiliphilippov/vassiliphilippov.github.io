chylifactive

1. ADJ

/ kaɪlɪˈfæktɪv / (AmE), / kaɪlɪˈfæktɪv / (BrE)
Producing or converting into chyle, especially in reference to digestive processes.
producing or converting into chyle
medical

Examples

The chylifactive properties of the small intestine play a crucial role in fat absorption.
Researchers examined the chylifactive response after administering a high-fat diet.
A chylifactive process is essential for the formation and transport of chyle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chylous - relating to or containing chyle

Etymology

From chyle (a milky digestive fluid) + -factive (from Latin facere 'to make').
