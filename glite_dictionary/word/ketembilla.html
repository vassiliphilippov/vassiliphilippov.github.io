ketembilla

1. NOUN

/ kɛˌtɛmˈbɪlə / (AmE), / kɛˌtɛmˈbɪlə / (BrE)
A small, spiny shrub native to Sri Lanka and India, cultivated for its maroon-purple fruit with sweet, gooseberry-like pulp.
spiny shrub with edible purple fruit

Examples

The farmer decided to plant a row of ketembilla shrubs along the garden fence.
Children enjoy the sweet, tangy taste of fresh ketembilla picked straight from the shrub.
The market was selling homemade jams made from ripe ketembillas.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɛtəmˈbɪlə / (AmE), / ˌkɛtəmˈbɪlə / (BrE)
A maroon-purple fruit from India, similar to a gooseberry, with tart-sweet purple pulp used especially for preserves.
Indian maroon-purple fruit like a gooseberry

Examples

The ketembilla is prized in India for making delicious preserves and jams.
She tasted the tart-sweet pulp of the ripe ketembilla for the first time.
Local markets often sell fresh ketembillas during the peak harvest season.
