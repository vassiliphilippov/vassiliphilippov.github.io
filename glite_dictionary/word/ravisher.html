ravisher

1. NOUN

/ ˈrævɪʃər / (AmE), / ˈravɪʃə / (BrE)
a person who sexually assaults someone, especially by rape
person who commits sexual assault
offensive archaic

Examples

The victim identified her ravisher in court.
In the novel, the ravisher was eventually brought to justice for his crimes.
The term ravisher is rarely used in modern legal contexts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rapist - someone who commits rape

Etymology

From Middle French 'ravisseur', from ravir ('to ravish').

2. NOUN

/ ˈrævɪʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈravɪʃə / (BrE)
a woman who is extremely attractive or seductively beautiful
very attractive or seductive woman

Examples

The artist captured the essence of a ravisher in his portrait, stunning everyone with her beauty.
At the gala, all eyes turned to the ravisher in the red dress, her presence impossible to ignore.
Her reputation as a ravisher preceded her, and she commanded attention wherever she went.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beauty - a beautiful woman
  • Synonym: enchantress - a woman who attracts or fascinates

Etymology

From 'ravish' (to seize or carry off, to delight), ultimately from Latin *rapere* (to seize).
