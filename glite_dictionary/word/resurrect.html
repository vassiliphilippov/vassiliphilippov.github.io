to resurrect

1. VERB

/ ˌrɛzəˈrɛkt / (AmE), / ˌrɛzəˈrɛkt / (BrE)
to bring (someone or something) back to life or to active use; to revive from death, extinction, or disappearance
bring back to life or revive

Examples

The scientist claimed he could resurrect extinct species in his laboratory.
The committee hopes to resurrect the festival after a five-year absence.
Many legends describe heroes who are miraculously resurrected from the dead.

Synset Examples

raise from the dead
Slavery is already dead, and cannot be resurrected
Upraising ghosts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revive - restore to life or consciousness
  • Antonym: kill - cause death; end the life of

Etymology

From Latin resurrectus, past participle of resurgere (to rise again).

3. VERB

/ ˌrɛzəˈrɛkt / (AmE), / ˌrɛzəˈrɛkt / (BrE)
to bring something back into use, practice, or attention after it has been forgotten or inactive
bring back into use

Examples

The museum plans to resurrect an old tradition of public art lectures next year.
She managed to resurrect several recipes that had fallen out of favor for decades.
The company hopes to resurrect its brand by introducing new and innovative products.

Synset Examples

He revived this style of opera
He resurrected the tango in this remote part of Argentina

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revive - restore to life, consciousness, or activity
  • Synonym: to restore - bring back to a former state or condition

Etymology

From Latin 'resurrectus', past participle of 'resurgere' meaning 'to rise again'.
