uncomfortable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkʌmfɚtəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈkʌmftəbəl / (BrE)
causing or experiencing physical discomfort or uneasiness.
causing or feeling discomfort

Examples

The old sofa was so uncomfortable that my back started to ache after just ten minutes.
She spent an uncomfortable night trying to sleep on the hard airport floor.
Wearing wet clothes all day made me feel extremely uncomfortable.

Synset Examples

an uncomfortable chair
an uncomfortable day in the hot sun

Related Terms

  • Antonym: comfortable - providing or experiencing physical ease or comfort

Etymology

From un- (not) + comfortable, from Latin 'comfortare' (to strengthen).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00480005-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkʌmfərtəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈkʌmftəbəl / (BrE)
feeling or causing slight pain, embarrassment, or mental distress; not at ease or relaxed.
feeling mental or physical discomfort

Examples

She felt uncomfortable when everyone stared at her during the meeting.
It's an uncomfortable topic, but we need to discuss it openly.
He grew increasingly uncomfortable as the interview progressed.

Synset Examples

this kind of life can prove disruptive and uncomfortable
the uncomfortable truth
grew uncomfortable beneath his appraising eye
an uncomfortable way of surprising me just when I felt surest
the teacher's presence at the conference made the child very uncomfortable

Related Terms

  • Antonym: comfortable - free from pain, stress, or mental discomfort; at ease

Etymology

From un- (not) + comfortable, from Latin confortare (to strengthen), via Old French conforter.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00481923-a
Synset POS: a
