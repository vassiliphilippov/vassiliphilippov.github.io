adsorber

1. NOUN

/ ædˈzɔrbɚ / (AmE), / adˈzɔːbə / (BrE)
A device or apparatus designed to remove substances from a gas or liquid by causing them to adhere to the surface of a solid material.
device that removes substances by adsorption

Examples

The chemical plant installed a new adsorber to reduce airborne pollutants emitted during the process.
Activated carbon is often used in an adsorber to capture volatile organic compounds from exhaust gases.
Routine maintenance is essential to ensure the adsorber operates efficiently and continues to remove contaminants.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: absorber - a device that absorbs substances
  • Synonym: adsorption - the process by which atoms, ions, or molecules adhere to a surface

Etymology

From 'adsorb', patterned after 'absorb', based on the Latin 'ad-' (to) and 'sorbere' (to suck in).
