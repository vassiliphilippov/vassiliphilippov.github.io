waterborne

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɔːtərˌbɔrn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəbɔːn / (BrE)
(of a craft, vehicle, or object) supported by or floating on water.
floating or supported by water

Examples

The harbor was filled with waterborne vessels of all sizes.
Safety measures are crucial for waterborne transport operations.
The parade featured a spectacular display of waterborne floats.

Synset Examples

waterborne craft

Related Terms

  • Antonym: airborne - carried or transported by air
  • Antonym: landborne - carried or supported by land

Etymology

Formed from 'water' + 'borne' (carried or transported).

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɔtərˌbɔrn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəbɔːn / (BrE)
carried or transported by water.
carried or conveyed by water

Examples

Many waterborne diseases are caused by contaminated drinking water.
The port city depends on waterborne freight for its economic growth.
Flooding increased the risk of waterborne pathogens affecting residents.

Synset Examples

waterborne freight

Related Terms

  • Antonym: airborne - carried or transported by air

Etymology

From 'water' + 'borne' (from Old English 'beran', to bear or carry).
