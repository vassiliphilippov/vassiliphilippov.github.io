inward

1. ADV

/ ˈɪnwɚd / (AmE), / ˈɪnwəd / (BrE)
Toward the center or interior of something.
toward the center or interior

Examples

She drew the curtains inward to let in more light.
When you breathe deeply, your chest moves inward and outward.
The crowd gradually moved inward toward the stage.

Synset Examples

move the needle further inwards!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outward - away from the center or interior

Etymology

From Old English 'inweard', from 'in' + 'weard' (toward).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪnwɚd / (AmE), / ˈɪnwəd / (BrE)
(of feelings, thoughts, etc.) existing in the mind; mental or spiritual rather than external or physical.
existing in the mind; mental

Examples

She felt an inward sense of relief when she heard the news.
His response was calm, but his inward emotions were in turmoil.
Meditation helps people explore their inward thoughts and feelings.

Synset Examples

a concern with inward reflections

Related Terms

  • Synonym: internal - existing or occurring inside the mind or body
  • Antonym: outward - relating to things external to the mind; external or visible

Etymology

From Old English 'inweard', from 'in' (in) + 'weard' (ward, toward).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00954437-a
Synset POS: a

going or directed toward the inside

Examples
