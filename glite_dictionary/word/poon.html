poon

1. NOUN

/ pun / (AmE), / puːn / (BrE)
The wood of any poon tree, especially valued for making masts and spars in shipbuilding.
wood from the poon tree

Examples

The shipbuilder selected poon for the mast due to its strength and flexibility.
Traditional boats in the region often feature hulls made from poon.
The carpenter inspected the quality of the poon before construction began.

2. NOUN

/ pun / (AmE), / puːn / (BrE)
Any of several East Indian trees, especially of the genus Calophyllum, with shiny leathery leaves and lightweight, hard wood.
a tropical tree of the genus Calophyllum

Examples

The furniture maker used poon wood for its strength and attractive finish.
Along the coast, the towering poon trees provided welcome shade for villagers.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of leaves from the poon tree.

Etymology

Probably from Hindi 'pun', referring to Calophyllum trees.
