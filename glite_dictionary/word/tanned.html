tanned

1. ADJ

/ tænd / (AmE), / tænd / (BrE)
(of animal skin or hide) converted into leather by treatment with a tanning agent.
made into leather by tanning

Examples

The craftsman used tanned hides to produce durable saddles and boots.
Much of the furniture was made from tanned leather for added longevity.
Only tanned skins are suitable for making high-quality handbags.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: untanned - not converted into leather by tanning

Etymology

From the verb 'tan', from Old French 'tanner' (to tan), from Medieval Latin 'tannare'.

2. ADJ

/ tænd / (AmE), / tænd / (BrE)
(of skin) made darker in color as a result of exposure to the sun.
having darker skin from sun exposure

Examples

After a two-week vacation at the beach, Maria returned looking noticeably tanned.
He admired his tanned arms in the mirror after spending the summer outdoors.
She wore a white dress to show off her tanned skin.

Synset Examples

a young bronzed Apollo

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sunburned - having skin burned by overexposure to the sun
  • Antonym: pale - having light-colored or very fair skin

Etymology

Past participle of 'tan', from Middle English 'tannen', from Old French 'tanner', from Medieval Latin 'tannāre' (to tan, make brown).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00245814-s
Synset POS: s
