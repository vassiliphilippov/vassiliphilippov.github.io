longlegs

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔŋˌlɛɡz / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋˌlɛɡz / (BrE)
a long-legged, three-toed black-and-white wading bird found in inland ponds, marshes, or brackish lagoons; also known as a stilt
a type of wading bird (stilt)

Examples

The longlegs waded gracefully through the shallow marsh in search of food.
Birdwatchers spotted several longlegs near the edge of the brackish lagoon.
A solitary longlegs stood on its long, thin legs in the morning mist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stilt - a long-legged wading bird found in marshes and lagoons
