authorization

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːθəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the official power or right to give orders, make decisions, or take action
the right or power to make decisions

Examples

Only senior managers have authorization to approve these expenses.
The security guard asked for authorization before letting anyone enter the restricted area.
Without proper authorization, employees are not allowed to access confidential documents.

Synset Examples

he has the authority to issue warrants
deputies are given authorization to make arrests
a place of potency in the state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authority - the power or right to give orders and enforce obedience
  • Degree Lower: permission - consent to do something
  • Degree Higher: power - the ability or capacity to do something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'auctorizatio', from Latin 'auctorizare' (to authorize).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɔːθəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the official permission or power given to do something; the act of conferring legal or formal approval
official permission or approval

Examples

The company requires written authorization before releasing any confidential information.
Without proper authorization, you cannot access the secured area.
He showed the document as proof of authorization for the transaction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permission - the act of allowing someone to do something
  • Antonym: prohibition - the act of forbidding something by law

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'auctorizatio', from Latin 'auctor' (author, originator).

4. NOUN

/ ˌɔːθəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
an official document that grants permission or gives an instruction or command
official document giving permission

Examples

You cannot access the restricted area without an authorization from the supervisor.
The committee issued a written authorization allowing the project to proceed.
Please present your travel authorization at the checkpoint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permit - an official document giving someone authorization to do something
  • Synonym: licence - an official document giving you permission to do something
  • Antonym: prohibition - the act of forbidding something, especially by law

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'auctorizatio', from 'auctorizare' (to authorize).
