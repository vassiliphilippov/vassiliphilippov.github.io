liveried

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɪvərid / (AmE), / ˈlɪvərid / (BrE)
wearing a distinctive uniform or livery, often as a servant or member of a particular group
wearing a special uniform or livery

Examples

The liveried doorman held open the door with a formal bow.
Several liveried chauffeurs waited outside the concert hall for their employers.
The liveried staff added a sense of grandeur to the historic hotel.

Synset Examples

liveried footmen stood on the palace steps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uniformed - wearing a uniform

Etymology

From 'livery' (Middle English liveree, from Old French livrée, meaning 'dispensed, delivered'), plus the suffix -ed.
