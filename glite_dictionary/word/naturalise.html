to naturalise

1. VERB

/ ˈnætʃɚəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnætʃrəlʌɪz / (BrE)
to make something more natural or lifelike in appearance, behavior, or characteristics
make more natural or lifelike

Examples

The designer tried to naturalise the artificial landscape by adding real plants and water features.
Efforts were made to naturalise the actor's movements so the character would seem more authentic.
The painting technique helps naturalise the appearance of the prosthetic limb.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: artificialize - to make less natural or more artificial

Etymology

From French 'naturaliser', from Latin 'naturalis' meaning 'natural'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00413621-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈnætʃrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnatʃərəlaɪz / (BrE)
to grant someone citizenship of a country; to make a foreigner become a legal citizen
grant citizenship to a foreigner

Examples

After living in Canada for five years, she decided to naturalise as a citizen.
The government voted to naturalise refugees who have settled in the country long-term.
He was officially naturalised, allowing him to vote in national elections.

Synset Examples

The French family was naturalized last year

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to denaturalise - to deprive someone of citizenship

Etymology

From French *naturaliser* and Medieval Latin *naturalizare*, meaning 'to make natural'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00413217-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈnætʃrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnatʃərəlaɪz / (BrE)
To cause a wild plant or piece of unclaimed land to adapt and thrive in a particular environment as if native.
adapt a wild plant or land to an environment

Examples

Botanists hope to naturalise several exotic species in the local forest.
With careful management, she was able to naturalise the hillside and restore native flora.
Over time, some non-native plants may naturalise in the new environment and flourish.

Synset Examples

domesticate oats
tame the soil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to domesticate - to adapt a plant or animal to live with humans
  • Synonym: tame - to make a wild animal or plant less wild or easier to control

Etymology

From French *naturaliser*, from Latin *natura* (nature).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00303245-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈnætʃərəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnatʃ(ə)rəlʌɪz / (BrE)
to make something or someone native to a new place or environment; to adapt or become established in a different country or context
make adaptable to a new place

Examples

Many European plant species have been naturalised in North America over the past century.
The fairy tales were naturalised into the local culture, making them familiar to children everywhere.
After years abroad, he finally began to naturalise to his new home.

Synset Examples

The stories had become naturalized into an American setting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to introduce - to bring something into use or operation for the first time in a place
  • Synonym: to assimilate - to absorb and integrate into a wider society or culture

Etymology

From French 'naturaliser', from Medieval Latin 'naturalizare', from Latin 'nātūrālis' (natural).
