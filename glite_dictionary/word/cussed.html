cussed

1. ADJ

/ ˈkʌsɪd / (AmE), / ˈkʌsɪd / (BrE)
stubborn and unwilling to change one's mind or behavior, especially in a way that is unreasonable or annoying.
stubbornly uncooperative or contrary
informal

Examples

Even as a child, he was so cussed that no one could convince him otherwise.
Her cussed refusal to admit defeat frustrated everyone involved in the project.
It was his cussed determination that got him through the most difficult times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stubborn - unwilling to change opinion or attitude
  • Synonym: obstinate - refusing to change behavior or ideas
  • Antonym: agreeable - willing to agree or consent

Etymology

From dialectal past participle of 'cuss' (curse), influenced by 'accursed'.
