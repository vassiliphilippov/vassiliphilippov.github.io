Helios

1. PROPN

/ ˈhiːliˌɑs / (AmE), / ˈhiːlɪɒs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the god of the sun who drove his chariot across the sky each day; associated with the Roman god Sol.
Greek sun god in mythology

Examples

According to myth, Helios rode his fiery chariot across the sky every day.
The ancient Greeks believed that Helios watched over oaths and witnessed important events.
A beautiful mosaic depicting Helios was found in the ruins of a Roman villa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sol - Roman personification of the sun; equivalent of Helios

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἥλιος (Hēlios), meaning 'sun'.
