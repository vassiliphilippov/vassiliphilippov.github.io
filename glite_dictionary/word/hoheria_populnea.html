Hoheria populnea

1. NOUN

/ hoʊˈhɪəriə pɒˈpʊlniə / (AmE), / həʊˈhɪəriə pɒˈpʊlniə / (BrE)
A small tree or shrub native to New Zealand, known for clusters of fragrant, white flowers and bark used for making cordage.
New Zealand tree with white flowers and useful bark

Examples

The bark of Hoheria populnea was traditionally used by the Māori for making ropes and textiles.
In spring, Hoheria populnea produces clusters of beautiful, paper-white flowers.
Gardeners in New Zealand often plant Hoheria populnea to attract bees with its fragrant blooms.

Etymology

From Maori 'hoheria' and Latin 'populnea' meaning 'poplar-like'.
