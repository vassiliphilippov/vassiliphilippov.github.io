Sheol

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃiː.oʊl / (AmE), / ˈʃiː.ɒl / (BrE)
(in ancient Hebrew belief) the place or abode of the dead; the underworld.
the underworld in ancient Hebrew belief

Examples

In ancient texts, souls were said to descend to Sheol after death.
The prophet spoke of deliverance from Sheol as a sign of hope.
Discussions of the afterlife in theology often reference Sheol and its meaning.

Synset Examples

No one goes to Hades with all his immense wealth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hades - the underworld in Greek mythology; the abode of the dead
  • Synonym: hell - a place regarded in various religions as a spiritual realm of evil and suffering

Etymology

From Hebrew 'She'ōl', meaning 'the grave' or 'the abode of the dead'.
