indirection

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.dəˈrɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dɪˈrɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
the use of indirect methods or actions, rather than being straightforward or direct
use of indirect methods or actions

Examples

He tried to uncover the truth through indirection rather than by asking outright.
In business negotiations, a little indirection can sometimes achieve better results than confrontation.
Her way of giving feedback relies on indirection, making suggestions instead of direct criticism.

Synset Examples

he tried to find out by indirection

Related Terms

  • Antonym: directness - the quality of being straightforward and clear
  • Synonym: subtlety - the quality of being indirect or not obvious

Etymology

From Latin 'indirectus' (not straight) via Middle French and English formation.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪndəˈrɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈrɛkʃən / (BrE)
Deceitful or evasive action; behavior that is not straightforward or direct.
deceitful or evasive action

Examples

She disliked his habit of speaking in indirection rather than answering plainly.
The negotiations were marked by indirection and subtle manipulation.
Critics accused the minister of using indirection to avoid the real issue.

Synset Examples

he could see through the indirections of diplomats

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceit - the act of deceiving; misrepresentation, trickery
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest and straightforward

Etymology

From Latin 'indirectus' (not straight), influenced by 'direction'.
