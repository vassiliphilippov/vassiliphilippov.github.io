retail

1. VERB

/ ˈriː.teɪl / (AmE), / ˈriː.teɪl / (BrE)
to be sold to the public, especially in shops or stores, rather than to wholesalers or businesses
to be sold to the public

Examples

The new phone models retail for less than $500 at most stores.
Organic produce often retails at higher prices than conventional alternatives.
These limited-edition sneakers retailed quickly when they first launched.

Synset Examples

These gems retail at thousands of dollars each

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wholesale - to be sold in large quantities, often to be retailed by others

Etymology

From Old French 'retaillier' (to cut off, divide), from 're-' + 'tailler' (to cut).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02734445-v
Synset POS: v

2. ADV

/ ˈriː.teɪl / (AmE), / ˈriː.teɪl / (BrE)
at a retail price or by retail; in the manner of selling to individual customers rather than wholesale.
at a retail price or by retail

Examples

He prefers to sell his handmade goods retail rather than in bulk.
Many items are available both wholesale and retail.
We can supply the product retail to individual customers.

Synset Examples

I'll sell it to you retail only

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wholesale - at a wholesale price; in the manner of selling to retailers rather than customers

Etymology

From Old French retaille, from retailler 'to cut off, trim'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00444562-r
Synset POS: r

3. NOUN

/ ˈriːˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈriːteɪl / (BrE)
the sale of goods to the public in relatively small quantities for use or consumption rather than for resale
the sale of goods to the public

Examples

She works in retail and enjoys interacting with customers every day.
Online retail has dramatically changed the way people shop for goods.
The store specializes in both retail and wholesale sales.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wholesale - the sale of goods in large quantities for resale by others

Etymology

From Old French 'retaillier' (to cut off, pare, divide up), from re- (again) + taillier (to cut).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01116568-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈriːˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈriːteɪl / (BrE)
to sell goods directly to the public, usually in small quantities for personal use rather than for resale.
sell to the public in small quantities

Examples

The company plans to retail its new line of shoes in selected stores this fall.
Many farmers prefer to retail their produce directly at local markets.
The goods are imported from abroad and then retailed at competitive prices.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wholesale - sell goods in large quantities, typically to be retailed by others

Etymology

From Old French 'retaillier' (to cut off, pare, divide up), from re- (again) + tailler (to cut).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02251810-v
Synset POS: v
