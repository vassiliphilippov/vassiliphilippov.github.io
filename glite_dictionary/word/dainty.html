dainty

1. ADJ

/ ˈdeɪnti / (AmE), / ˈdeɪnti / (BrE)
(of food) especially pleasing and delicate in flavor or appearance.
pleasing, delicate food

Examples

The chef prepared a dainty dessert that delighted all the guests.
Each plate was arranged with a dainty assortment of pastries and fruit.
She served a dainty appetizer before the main course.

Synset Examples

a dainty dish to set before a kind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delicacy - a choice or expensive food, especially one regarded as a luxury
  • Synonym: tasty - having a pleasant, distinct flavor

Etymology

Middle English, from Old French *deintie* (delicacy, pleasure), from Latin *dignitatem* (worthiness).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdeɪnti / (AmE), / ˈdeɪnti / (BrE)
(of a person) excessively fastidious or easily disgusted, especially about cleanliness or eating.
overly fastidious or easily disgusted (of a person)

Examples

He was too dainty to eat the food served at camp.
She grew up so dainty that even the smallest mess bothered her.
Their dainty habits made family dinners a challenge.

Synset Examples

too nice about his food to take to camp cooking
so squeamish he would only touch the toilet handle with his elbow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squeamish - easily made to feel sick or disgusted

Etymology

From Middle English 'deinte', from Old French 'deintie' (delicacy, pleasure).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00987524-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈdeɪnti / (AmE), / ˈdeɪnti / (BrE)
delicately small, attractive, and beautiful; pleasingly graceful or refined in appearance
delicately beautiful or attractive

Examples

She admired the dainty lacework on the old handkerchief.
A dainty china dish sat on the windowsill, decorated with tiny flowers.
The bride wore a dainty silver bracelet on her wrist.

Synset Examples

a dainty teacup
an exquisite cameo

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delicate - delicately beautiful or fragile
  • Synonym: elegant - pleasingly graceful and stylish in appearance

Etymology

From Middle English 'deinte', from Old French 'deintie' (delight, pleasure), from Latin 'dignitas' (worthiness).

4. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪnti / (AmE), / ˈdeɪnti / (BrE)

delicate, tasty piece of food

Examples

Related Terms
