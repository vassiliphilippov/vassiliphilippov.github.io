steering

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of guiding or directing the course of something, especially a vehicle or organization.
the act of guiding or directing

Examples

The steering of the ship required great skill during the storm.
She took charge of the steering of the committee through a difficult period.
Good steering is essential for keeping the project on track.

Synset Examples

a new council was installed under the direction of the king

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guidance - the process of directing or leading
  • Synonym: direction - the management or guidance of something

Etymology

From 'steer' (to guide), Old English *stēran* (to guide a ship).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
the action or process of controlling the direction of a ship or other vehicle
action of steering (esp. a ship)

Examples

The captain took over the steering during the storm to ensure the ship stayed on course.
Effective steering is critical in narrow harbors and during docking procedures.
He praised the crew's skillful steering as they maneuvered through the treacherous waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: navigation - the process of planning and directing the route of a ship, aircraft, or other vehicle

Etymology

From the verb 'steer,' from Old English 'stēran' (to guide).
