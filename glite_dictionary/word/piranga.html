Piranga

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈræŋɡə / (AmE), / pɪˈræŋɡə / (BrE)
A genus of brightly colored birds in the tanager family, including species such as the scarlet tanager.
genus of birds (tanagers)

Examples

The scarlet tanager belongs to the genus Piranga and is known for its vivid red plumage.
Scientists observed several Piranga species during their expedition in South America.
Many birdwatchers hope to see a member of the Piranga genus, such as the summer tanager.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tanager - a small brightly colored American bird of the family Thraupidae

Etymology

From Latin 'piranga', used as a scientific designation in ornithology.

2. NOUN

/ pɪˈræŋɡə / (AmE), / pɪˈraŋɡə / (BrE)
A genus of brightly colored birds in the family Thraupidae, commonly known as tanagers, found mainly in the Americas.
a genus of tanager birds

Examples

The Piranga genus includes species such as the scarlet tanager and summer tanager.
Observers spotted several Piranga birds during the spring migration.
Researchers are studying the migration patterns of Piranga species in Central America.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Piranga, based on a Tupi word.
