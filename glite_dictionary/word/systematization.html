systematization

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪstəmətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪstəmətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of arranging or organizing something in a methodical and systematic way
methodical organization or arrangement

Examples

The systematization of company procedures improved productivity and consistency.
Modern science has benefited greatly from the systematization of knowledge.
The systematization of library resources made information retrieval much more efficient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organization - the arrangement of parts for effective function
  • Antonym: disorder - a lack of order or organization

Etymology

From Greek 'systema' (organised whole) via French 'systématisation'.
