suppressive

1. ADJ

/ səˈprɛsɪv / (AmE), / səˈprɛsɪv / (BrE)
tending or able to suppress or restrain something, especially undesirable activity or symptoms
tending to suppress

Examples

The government implemented suppressive laws to curb public dissent.
Certain medications have suppressive effects on the immune system.
The regime's suppressive tactics drew international criticism.

Synset Examples

the government used suppressive measures to control the protest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inhibitory - serving to inhibit or restrain
  • Antonym: stimulative - tending to encourage or stimulate

Etymology

From Latin 'supprimere' (to press down, suppress) plus the English suffix '-ive'.
