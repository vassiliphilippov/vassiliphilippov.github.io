butterflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌt̬ɚˌflaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈbʌtəˌflaʊə / (BrE)
any of various plants of the genus Ranunculus, commonly known for their bright yellow flowers
a plant of the genus Ranunculus

Examples

The children gathered butterflowers from the meadow in the afternoon sun.
A single butterflower brightened the corner of the wild garden.
Many butterflowers bloom every spring along the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buttercup - another common name for plants of the genus Ranunculus
  • Synonym: Ranunculus - the scientific name of the plant genus including butterflowers and buttercups

Etymology

Formed from 'butter' and 'flower', likely alluding to the plant's yellow color.
