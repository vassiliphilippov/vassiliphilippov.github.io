kiley

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaɪli / (AmE), / ˈkaɪli / (BrE)
an Australian boomerang with one side flat and the other convex, traditionally used as a hunting weapon
an Australian type of boomerang

Examples

The hunter skillfully threw his kiley to catch a passing bird.
Traditional Aboriginal art sometimes depicts the use of the kiley in hunting scenes.
He brought back an authentic kiley as a souvenir from his trip to Western Australia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boomerang - a curved throwing stick that returns to the thrower

Etymology

From an Aboriginal Australian language (possibly Noongar), referring to a returning boomerang.
