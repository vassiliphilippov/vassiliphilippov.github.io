nog

1. NOUN

/ nɑɡ / (AmE), / nɒɡ / (BrE)
a wooden block built into a masonry wall to provide a surface for fixing joinery or nails
wooden block in masonry wall for fixing joinery

Examples

The carpenter nailed the door frame to the nog embedded in the brick wall.
When renovating old houses, you often find original nogs hidden behind the plaster.
Proper placement of each nog ensures that shelves can be securely fastened later.

2. NOUN

/ nɑɡ / (AmE), / nɒɡ / (BrE)
a wooden pin driven into a surface, especially used in construction or carpentry
wooden pin in construction

Examples

The carpenter hammered a nog into the beam to secure the joist.
Each shelf was held in place by a single nog at each end.
You may need to remove the old nog before replacing the damaged section of wood.
