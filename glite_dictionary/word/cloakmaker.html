cloakmaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊkˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈkləʊkˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
a person whose job is making or repairing cloaks or fur garments
person who makes or repairs cloaks

Examples

Her grandmother was a skilled cloakmaker in the early 1900s, working long hours in a busy workshop.
The cloakmaker carefully selected the finest fabrics for her next creation.
During the winter, the demand for a talented cloakmaker increased as people sought warm, stylish outerwear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tailor - a person whose occupation is making fitted clothes

Etymology

From 'cloak' (a loose outer garment) + 'maker' (one who makes).
