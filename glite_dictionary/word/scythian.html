Scythian

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪθiən / (AmE), / ˈsɪθiən / (BrE)
the Iranian language once spoken by the ancient Scythians, a nomadic people of Eurasia
ancient Iranian language of the Scythians

Examples

Most knowledge of the Scythian language comes from names and rare inscriptions.
Linguists study Scythian to understand the spread of ancient Iranian peoples.
Few written records of Scythian have survived to the present day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iranian - language family including Persian, Kurdish, and others

Etymology

From Latin Scythia, from Greek Skythia, referring to the Scythians, an ancient people.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06988129-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪθiən / (AmE), / ˈsɪθiən / (BrE)
a member of an ancient nomadic people who lived in the region known as Scythia, primarily north of the Black Sea, from about the 8th century BC to the 2nd century AD
member of an ancient nomadic people of Scythia

Examples

Archaeologists discovered artifacts believed to have been crafted by a Scythian warrior.
The Scythian tribes were renowned for their horsemanship and archery skills.
Legends describe the Scythian people as fierce nomadic warriors roaming the Eurasian steppes.

Etymology

From Latin Scytha (a Scythian), from Greek Skythēs.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10586394-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈsɪθiən / (AmE), / ˈsɪθiən / (BrE)
relating to the ancient Scythians, their culture, or their language
of the ancient Scythians or their culture

Examples

Several Scythian artifacts were discovered in the burial mound by archaeologists.
The Scythian language belonged to the Iranian branch of the Indo-European family.
The museum exhibited a collection of Scythian weapons and horse trappings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scythian - a member of the ancient Scythians

Etymology

From Latin Scytha (Scythian), from Greek Skythēs (Σκύθης).
