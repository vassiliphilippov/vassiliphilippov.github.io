cork

1. NOUN

/ kɔrk / (AmE), / kɔːk / (BrE)
(Botany) The protective outer layer of dead cells in the bark of trees and woody plants.
botanical outer layer of bark

Examples

In some trees, the cork layer thickens to provide extra protection against disease.
Wine bottles are sometimes closed with stoppers made from the cork harvested from certain trees.
The cork in oak bark is commercially valuable for various uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bark - the outer covering of the stem of a woody plant

Etymology

From Spanish 'corcho', from Latin 'cortex' (bark).

2. NOUN

/ kɔrk / (AmE), / kɔːk / (BrE)
The outer bark of the cork oak tree, used for making stoppers for bottles and for other products.
bark of cork oak used as stopper

Examples

Wine bottles are traditionally sealed with a cork to preserve their flavor.
The bulletin board was made from a sheet of cork attached to the wall.
Harvesting cork from trees in Portugal is a centuries-old tradition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stopper - device used to seal a bottle or container

Etymology

From Spanish *alcorque*, ultimately from Latin *quercus* (oak).
