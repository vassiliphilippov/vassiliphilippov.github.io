cop

1. NOUN

/ kɑp / (AmE), / kɒp / (BrE)
an informal term for a police officer, often used with an uncomplimentary or casual tone
informal term for police officer
informal

Examples

The cop pulled me over for speeding.
Some people feel nervous when a cop is nearby.
He grew up dreaming of becoming a cop one day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: policeman - a member of a police force
  • Synonym: officer - another word for a police officer

Etymology

Probably from 'copper', 19th-century slang for policeman, itself from the verb 'to cop' (to catch, seize).

2. VERB

/ kɑp / (AmE), / kɒp / (BrE)
(slang) To steal or take unlawfully; to pilfer.
steal; take unlawfully (slang)
slang

Examples

Someone tried to cop my bag while I was distracted by the street performer.
He got caught trying to cop a phone from the electronics store.
Thieves frequently cop wallets in crowded markets.

Synset Examples

Someone snitched my wallet!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steal - to take something without permission or legal right
  • Synonym: to rob - to take property illegally from a person or place
  • Antonym: return - to give back something that was taken or used
