to wean

1. VERB

/ wiːn / (AmE), / wiːn / (BrE)
to gradually stop feeding an infant or young animal its mother's milk and introduce other foods, or to accustom (someone) to doing without something they are dependent on
gradually stop feeding mother's milk

Examples

Many mothers choose to wean their babies after six months.
It's important to wean kittens slowly to ensure their health.
After she weaned her child, he started eating solid food.

Synset Examples

she weaned her baby when he was 3 months old and started him on powdered milk
The kitten was weaned and fed by its owner with a bottle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weaning - the process of accustoming an infant or animal to food other than its mother's milk

Etymology

From Old English wenian (to accustom, train).

2. VERB

/ win / (AmE), / wiːn / (BrE)
to gradually detach someone's affection or dependence from something or someone to which they have become attached
gradually detach affection or dependence

Examples

After years of devotion, it was hard to wean him from his old habits.
She tried to wean herself off her attachment to social media.
Parents often struggle to wean their children from their dependence on television.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to detach - to separate or disengage something
  • Synonym: to accustom - to make someone used to something, often by gradual exposure

Etymology

From Old English wenian (to accustom, train), related to German gewöhnen.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
