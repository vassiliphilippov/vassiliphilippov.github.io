toner

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊnər / (AmE), / ˈtəʊnə / (BrE)
a liquid cosmetic or lotion applied to the skin to cleanse and tighten the pores
skin-cleansing and pore-tightening lotion

Examples

After washing her face, she applied toner to refresh her skin.
This toner helps to tighten pores and remove any leftover makeup.
Using a gentle toner can improve your daily skincare routine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astringent - a substance that contracts body tissues, often used on skin

2. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊnər / (AmE), / ˈtəʊnə / (BrE)
a black or colored powder used in laser printers and photocopiers to form printed text and images on paper
powder for printers or copiers

Examples

I had to replace the toner in the office printer after the pages started to come out blank.
The technician recommended using only high-quality toner for best printing results.
Many modern printers have an indicator to show when the toner is low.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ink - colored fluid used for writing, printing, or drawing
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-15100434-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊnər / (AmE), / ˈtəʊnə / (BrE)
a chemical solution used to alter the color of a photographic print during processing
chemical for changing photo print color

Examples

The photographer used a toner to give the print a sepia tone.
You can enhance the mood of your photos by applying a toner during development.
Some traditional darkroom artists still prefer to experiment with different types of toner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: developer - a chemical used to make an image visible in photographic processing
