jetty

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛti / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛti / (BrE)
a long structure built from the shore into the water to protect a harbor or coastline from the force of waves or to prevent erosion
structure built from shore to protect coast

Examples

Waves crashed against the jetty as the wind picked up during the storm.
Fishermen stood casting their lines from the end of the jetty.
The city rebuilt the jetty to prevent further erosion of the beach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pier - a structure built out into the water for use as a landing place or promenade
  • Synonym: breakwater - a barrier built out into the sea to protect a coast or harbor from the force of waves

Etymology

From Old French 'jetee' (a thrown-out structure), from 'jeter' (to throw).
