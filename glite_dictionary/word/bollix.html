to bollix

1. VERB

/ ˈbɑːlɪks / (AmE), / ˈbɒlɪks / (BrE)
to ruin or spoil something through clumsiness or incompetence; to make a mess of
ruin or mess up
informal slang

Examples

He completely bollixed the presentation and lost the client.
If you bollix the recipe, dinner will be ruined.
The new software update bollixed my computer and caused it to crash.

Synset Examples

I botched the dinner and we had to eat out
the pianist screwed up the difficult passage in the second movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botch - to carry out a task badly or carelessly
  • Synonym: to mess up - to do something badly or ruin something
  • Antonym: to succeed - to achieve the desired result; to do something successfully

Etymology

Variant of 'bollocks', likely from British slang meaning nonsense or mess.

2. VERB

/ ˈbɑːlɪks / (AmE), / ˈbɒlɪks / (BrE)
to make a mess of something; to spoil or ruin by clumsy or careless action
to mess up; to ruin through mistakes
informal

Examples

If you bollix the instructions, we might fail the task.
The chef managed to bollix the recipe by forgetting an ingredient.
Try not to bollix everything up at the last minute.

Synset Examples

If you bollix the instructions, we might fail the task.
Don't bollix the project by missing the deadline.
He tends to bollix things up when he's rushed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botch - to carry out a task badly or carelessly
  • Synonym: bungle - to do something clumsily or incompetently

Etymology

Variant of 'bollocks', British slang for 'nonsense' or 'mistake'.
