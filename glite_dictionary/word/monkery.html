monkery

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌŋkəri / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋkəri / (BrE)
the practices or lifestyle characteristic of monks, especially living simply and ascetically
life or practices of monks
archaic

Examples

She practiced monkery in the countryside, shunning all modern comforts.
After losing everything, he chose to practice monkery and live with only the bare necessities.
The author described her year of practiced monkery as both challenging and enlightening.

Synset Examples

They practiced monkery by living simply and humbly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asceticism - severe self-discipline and avoidance of all forms of indulgence
  • Antonym: luxury - great comfort and extravagant living

Etymology

From 'monk' + English suffix '-ery', denoting a place or practice related to monks.
