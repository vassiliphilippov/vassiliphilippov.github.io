Enhydra lutris

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈhaɪdrə ˈluːtrɪs / (AmE), / ɛnˈhɪdrə ˈluːtrɪs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the sea otter, a large marine mammal of the northern Pacific coasts with very thick dark brown fur
sea otter (scientific name)

Examples

The population of Enhydra lutris has made a remarkable recovery along certain stretches of the Pacific coast.
Researchers observed Enhydra lutris using rocks to crack open shellfish.
Conservationists are working to protect Enhydra lutris from oil spills and habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea otter - large marine otter of the northern Pacific coasts

Etymology

From Greek 'enhydra' (in water) and Latin 'lutris' (otter).
