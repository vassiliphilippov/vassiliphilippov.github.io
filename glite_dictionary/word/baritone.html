baritone

1. ADJ

/ ˈbærɪˌtoʊn / (AmE), / ˈbærɪtəʊn / (BrE)
(of a voice or instrument) lower in range than tenor and higher than bass.
(of voice/instrument) between tenor and bass

Examples

His baritone voice filled the concert hall with warmth and depth.
The baritone saxophone adds a rich sound to the jazz band.
She was looking for a singer with a strong baritone quality for the main role.

Synset Examples

a baritone voice
baritone oboe

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tenor - higher male singing voice
  • Degree Lower: bass - lowest male singing voice

Etymology

From Italian baritono, from Greek barytonos (deep-sounding), from barys (heavy) + tonos (tone).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01218468-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈbærɪˌtoʊn / (AmE), / ˈbærɪtəʊn / (BrE)
a male singer with a voice range between tenor and bass, or the range itself
male singer with middle voice range

Examples

The baritone impressed the audience with his powerful yet smooth voice.
He trained for years to master the baritone range for opera performances.
As a baritone, he often performed parts written for both higher and lower voices.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tenor - a male singer with a high voice
  • Degree Lower: bass - a male singer with the lowest adult male voice

Etymology

From Italian 'baritono', from Greek 'barytonos' (deep-sounding).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09858824-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɛrəˌtoʊn / (AmE), / ˈbær.ɪ.təʊn / (BrE)
a brass wind instrument with a range lower than a tenor horn and higher than a tuba, also known as the baritone horn
a low-pitched brass wind instrument

Examples

The marching band featured a talented baritone player in the brass section.
She switched from trumpet to baritone for the school concert.
The warm sound of the baritone filled the auditorium during the symphony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: euphonium - a similar brass instrument with a slightly different tone and range

Etymology

From Italian baritono, originally from Greek barytonos (deep-sounding).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02796112-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈbær.ɪˌtoʊn / (AmE), / ˈbær.ɪ.təʊn / (BrE)
the second lowest adult male singing voice, lower than tenor and higher than bass
second lowest adult male singing voice

Examples

He sings as a baritone in the local choir.
Most operatic heroes are written for baritone rather than tenor voices.
The choir needs a new baritone to balance the harmony.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tenor - the highest adult male singing voice
  • Degree Lower: bass - the lowest adult male singing voice

Etymology

From Italian 'baritono', from Greek 'barytonos' meaning 'deep-sounding'.
