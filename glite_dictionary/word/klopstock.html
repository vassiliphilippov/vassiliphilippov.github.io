Klopstock

1. PROPN

/ ˈklɑːpˌstɑk / (AmE), / ˈklɒpˌstɒk / (BrE)
(1724–1803) A German poet noted for his epic poems and influence on early German Romanticism, particularly known for 'The Messiah.'
German poet (1724–1803)

Examples

Klopstock is best remembered for his religious epic poem, 'The Messiah.'
As a major figure of the 18th century, Klopstock helped shape German literature.
Many scholars study Klopstock's works to understand early Romanticism in Europe.
