Chartist

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑrtɪst / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːtɪst / (BrE)
a member of the 19th-century British movement seeking political and social reforms for working people, especially through the People's Charter of 1838
19th-century British political reformer

Examples

As a dedicated Chartist, he campaigned tirelessly for workers' rights and universal suffrage.
The factory workers admired the bravery of the Chartists who spoke at public meetings.
She read a book about the Chartist movement and its impact on British democracy.

Etymology

From 'charter' + '-ist', referring to the People's Charter of 1838.
