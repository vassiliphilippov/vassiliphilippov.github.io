Presbyterian

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɛzbɪˈtɪriən / (AmE), / ˌprɛzbɪˈtɪəriən / (BrE)
a member or follower of the Presbyterian Church, which is based on Calvinist principles and characterized by governance by elders.
member of the Presbyterian Church

Examples

Her grandparents are devout Presbyterians who attend church every Sunday.
As a Presbyterian, he believes in a church governed by elders rather than by bishops.
The Presbyterian community played a significant role in the town's history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Calvinist - a follower of the teachings of John Calvin
  • Antonym: Catholic - a member of the Roman Catholic Church

Etymology

From Latin 'presbyter' (elder), from Greek 'presbyteros', combined with '-ian' to denote a follower.
