Andreaeales

1. NOUN

/ ˌændriˈiːəlz / (AmE), / ˌandrɪˈiːəliːz / (BrE)
An order of mosses in the class Bryopsida, containing the single genus Andreaea.
an order of mosses (Andreaea)

Examples

The Andreaeales are commonly known as granite mosses due to their preferred rocky habitats.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Andreaeales during their expedition in the Alps.
Unlike most mosses, Andreaeales do not possess true peristome teeth.

Etymology

From New Latin, named after the genus Andreaea.
