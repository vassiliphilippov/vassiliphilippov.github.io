thiodiphenylamine

1. NOUN

/ ˌθaɪoʊdaɪˌfɛnɪlˈæmin / (AmE), / ˌθaɪəʊdɪˌfɛnɪlˈæmiːn / (BrE)
a chemical compound used mainly in veterinary medicine to eliminate internal parasites in farm animals
veterinary anti-parasitic compound
medical

Examples

Veterinarians often prescribe thiodiphenylamine to treat livestock infested with intestinal worms.
The farm switched to using thiodiphenylamine for parasite control in their cattle.
Safe handling of thiodiphenylamine is important due to its potent effects on animal health.

Etymology

From thio- (containing sulfur) + diphenylamine (an organic compound class); scientific nomenclature.
