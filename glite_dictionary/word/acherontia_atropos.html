Acherontia atropos

1. NOUN

/ əˈkɛr.ən.ti.ə ˈæ.trə.pɒs / (AmE), / əˈkɛr.ən.ti.ə ˈæ.trə.pɒs / (BrE)
A large European hawkmoth with markings on its thorax resembling a human skull, commonly known as the 'death's-head hawkmoth'.
European moth with skull-like markings

Examples

The distinctive skull-like pattern on the back of the Acherontia atropos has inspired many myths and legends.
During our trip, we saw an Acherontia atropos resting on the garden wall at dusk.
The Acherontia atropos, or death's-head hawkmoth, is often associated with superstition in European folklore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: death's-head hawkmoth - Common name for Acherontia atropos, noted for skull-like marking

Etymology

From Latin, combining 'Acherontia' (referring to Acheron, a river of the underworld in Greek mythology) and 'Atropos' (one of the three Fates in Greek mythology).
