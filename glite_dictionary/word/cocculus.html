Cocculus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːkjʊləs / (AmE), / ˈkɒkjʊləs / (BrE)
a genus of climbing shrubs or woody vines in the family Menispermaceae, native to tropical and subtropical regions.
a genus of climbing shrubs or vines

Examples

Cocculus species are often found in tropical Asian rainforests, where they climb up trees for sunlight.
The dried berries of Cocculus are used in traditional herbal remedies in some cultures.
Botanists collected several varieties of Cocculus during their expedition in Southeast Asia.

Etymology

From Latin *cocculus* (little berry), diminutive of *coccus* (berry).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːkjʊləs / (AmE), / ˈkɒkjʊləs / (BrE)
A genus of climbing plants or shrubs in the family Menispermaceae, found primarily in tropical regions.
genus of climbing plants or shrubs

Examples

The Cocculus genus includes several species used in traditional medicine.
Leaves and berries from the Cocculus plant have been studied for their chemical properties.
Researchers discovered a new member of the Cocculus genus in Southeast Asia.

Etymology

From Latin *cocculus* (little berry), referring to the fruit of plants in this genus.
