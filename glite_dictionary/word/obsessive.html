obsessive

1. ADJ

/ əbˈsɛsɪv / (AmE), / əbˈsɛsɪv / (BrE)
thinking about something or someone too much or all the time, often in a way that is unhealthy or excessive.
excessively preoccupied or obsessed

Examples

Her obsessive attention to detail sometimes slows down the project.
He became obsessive about keeping his house spotlessly clean.
The detective's obsessive pursuit of the truth impressed everyone.

Synset Examples

the obsessional character of his response
obsessive gambling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compulsive - involving an irresistible urge, especially one that is against one's conscious wishes

Etymology

From Latin 'obsessus', past participle of 'obsidere' (to besiege, occupy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01589430-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ əbˈsɛsɪv / (AmE), / əbˈsɛsɪv / (BrE)
a person who is preoccupied or excessively concerned with a particular idea, activity, or thing
person with an obsession

Examples

As an obsessive, he spent hours organizing his book collection alphabetically every week.
Some obsessives devote their lives to collecting rare stamps or coins.
Her friends teased her for being an obsessive about cleanliness and tidiness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fanatic - a person filled with excessive or single-minded zeal

Etymology

From Latin 'obsessus', past participle of 'obsidere' (to besiege).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10368720-n
Synset POS: n
