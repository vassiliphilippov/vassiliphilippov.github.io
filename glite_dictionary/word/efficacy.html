efficacy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛf.ɪ.kə.si / (AmE), / ˈɛf.ɪ.kə.si / (BrE)
The ability to produce the intended or desired result; effectiveness.
the ability to produce a desired result

Examples

The new policy was evaluated for its efficacy in reducing energy consumption.
Clinical trials are necessary to demonstrate the efficacy of the treatment.
Questions have been raised about the efficacy of the proposed measures.

Synset Examples

concern about the safety and efficacy of the vaccine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effectiveness - the degree to which something is successful in producing a desired result
  • Antonym: inefficacy - lack of power to produce the desired effect

Etymology

From Latin efficacia, from efficax (effectual, powerful).
