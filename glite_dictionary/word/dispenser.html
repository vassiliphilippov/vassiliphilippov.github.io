dispenser

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈspɛnsɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈspɛnsə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to give out or supply something, especially medicines or equipment
person who gives out or supplies something

Examples

The hospital dispenser filled the prescription quickly.
A dispenser of justice must be fair and impartial at all times.
The clinic employs a dispenser to manage medical supplies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to distribute - to give things to several people; to supply broadly

Etymology

From Latin 'dispensare' (to distribute).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈspɛnsɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈspensə / (BrE)
A container or device designed to release its contents in measured or prescribed amounts.
container releasing measured amounts

Examples

The office has a water dispenser near the entrance for employees to use throughout the day.
He refilled the soap dispenser in the bathroom.
A hand sanitizer dispenser is now installed at every doorway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to distribute - to give out or deliver something to people

Etymology

From Old French 'dispensier', from Latin 'dispensator' (distributor).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03215404-n
Synset POS: n
