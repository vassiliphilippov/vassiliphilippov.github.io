distressingly

1. ADV

/ dɪˈstrɛsɪŋli / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɛsɪŋli / (BrE)
In a way that causes distress, anxiety, or unhappiness; unpleasantly.
in a way that causes distress or discomfort

Examples

The city's pollution was distressingly apparent during our visit.
Her memory loss became distressingly frequent over the past year.
It was distressingly clear that the project would not be finished on time.

Synset Examples

his ignorance was painfully obvious

Related Terms

  • Synonym: painfully - in a way that causes pain or distress
  • Antonym: pleasantly - in a way that is pleasing or enjoyable

Etymology

From the adjective 'distressing' + '-ly', ultimately from 'distress' (Middle English, from Old French 'destresce', from Latin 'districtia').
