Marrano

1. NOUN

/ məˈrɑnoʊ / (AmE), / məˈrɑːnəʊ / (BrE)
(historical, offensive) A Jew in medieval Spain or Portugal who converted to Christianity to avoid persecution but continued to practice Judaism secretly; a term used disparagingly.
Jew who secretly practiced Judaism after forced conversion
offensive

Examples

The term Marrano was used pejoratively in medieval Spain to refer to Jews who secretly observed their faith.
Many Marranos faced severe risks if their religious practices were discovered.
Historians study the lives of the Marrano community to understand religious persecution in Iberian history.

Etymology

From Spanish 'marrano' (literally 'swine'), used as a slur for Jews forced to convert.
