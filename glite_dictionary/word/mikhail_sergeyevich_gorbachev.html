Mikhail Sergeyevich Gorbachev

1. PROPN

/ mɪˈkaɪl sərˈɡeɪəvɪtʃ ˈɡɔrbətʃɛf / (AmE), / mɪˈkaɪl sɜːˈɡeɪəvɪtʃ ˈɡɔːbətʃɒf / (BrE)
(1931–2022) Soviet statesman who served as the last leader of the Soviet Union, introducing major political and economic reforms and helping to end the Cold War.
Soviet leader who ended the Cold War

Examples

Mikhail Sergeyevich Gorbachev played a pivotal role in ending the Cold War through his policy of openness and reform.
In 1985, Mikhail Sergeyevich Gorbachev became the General Secretary of the Soviet Communist Party.
Many historians credit Mikhail Sergeyevich Gorbachev with initiating political and economic changes that led to the dissolution of the Soviet Union.
