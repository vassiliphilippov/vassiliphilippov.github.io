de-Nazification

1. NOUN

/ diːˌnɑːtsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / diːˌnæt.sɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of removing Nazis from positions of authority and eliminating influence of Nazi ideology, especially after World War II
removal of Nazis and Nazi influence

Examples

The Allies implemented de-Nazification policies in Germany after the war.
De-Nazification involved screening officials for links to the Nazi Party.
Historians still debate the effectiveness of de-Nazification in postwar Europe.

Synset Examples

denazification was a slow process

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to denazify - to remove Nazis or Nazi influence

Etymology

From the prefix de- (removal) and Nazi, modeled after German Entnazifizierung.
