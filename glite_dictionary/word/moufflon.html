moufflon

1. NOUN

/ ˈmuːflɑn / (AmE), / ˈmʌflɒn / (BrE)
a wild mountain sheep native to Corsica and Sardinia, having short curved horns and a brown coat
wild mountain sheep native to Corsica and Sardinia

Examples

The moufflon is one of the wild ancestors of today's domesticated sheep.
During our hike in Sardinia, we spotted a herd of moufflon grazing on the rocky slopes.
Conservationists are working to protect the remaining moufflon populations in Corsica.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheep - a domesticated ruminant mammal with a thick woolly coat

Etymology

From French 'mouflon', itself from Italian 'muflone'.
