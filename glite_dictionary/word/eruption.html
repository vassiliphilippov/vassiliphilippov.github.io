eruption

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈrʌpʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈrʌpʃən / (BrE)
the process of a tooth emerging through the gum
emergence of a tooth through the gum
medical

Examples

The dentist explained that the eruption of the wisdom teeth could cause discomfort.
Delayed eruption of permanent teeth sometimes indicates an underlying health issue.
A baby's first eruption usually occurs around six months of age.

Etymology

From Latin eruptio (a breaking out), from erumpere (to break out).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈrʌpʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈrʌpʃən / (BrE)
a sudden and violent ejection of steam, ash, and other material from a volcano
violent volcanic discharge

Examples

In 1980, the eruption of Mount St. Helens was one of the most significant in U.S. history.
Scientists monitor seismic activity to predict when a volcanic eruption might occur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explosion - a violent burst or blowing apart

Etymology

From Latin eruptio (a bursting forth), from erumpere (to burst out).

6. NOUN

/ ɪˈrʌpʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈrʌpʃən / (BrE)
A sudden occurrence in which a volcano expels fumes, lava, or ash from its crater.
volcanic expulsion of lava or fumes

Examples

The volcanic eruption sent ash and smoke high into the atmosphere.
Geologists studied the layers formed by the eruption thousands of years ago.
The residents evacuated the island before the eruption became dangerous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explosion - a violent and sudden release of energy, as in a blast
  • Antonym: quiescence - a state of being inactive or dormant, especially of a volcano

Etymology

From Latin eruptionem (a breaking out), from erumpere (to burst forth).
