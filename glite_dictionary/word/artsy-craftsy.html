artsy-craftsy

1. ADJ

/ ˌɑːrtsi ˈkræftsi / (AmE), / ˌɑːtsi ˈkrɑːfti / (BrE)
(informal) trying to be artistic in a pretentious or overly amateurish way; cloyingly charming or affectedly creative.
pretentiously artistic or overly crafty (informal)
informal

Examples

The decor in their living room is a little too artsy-craftsy for my taste.
She brought some artsy-craftsy homemade gifts to the party.
His artsy-craftsy style is not really appreciated in more traditional galleries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artsy - showing an interest or pretense in art

Etymology

Formed from 'artsy' (imitating the style of artists) and 'crafty' or 'crafts', with reduplication for emphasis.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɑːrt.si ˈkræft.si / (AmE), / ˈɑːtsi ˈkrɑːftsi / (BrE)
Showing or relating to arts and crafts, often in a way that seems amateurish or overly decorative; characterized by creative or handmade qualities, sometimes with a dismissive or playful nuance.
relating to arts and crafts, often informal
informal american

Examples

She decorated her kitchen with artsy-craftsy touches like handmade ceramic tiles.
The summer camp offers all kinds of artsy-craftsy activities for kids.
I'm not really into artsy-craftsy gifts, but I appreciated the effort she made.

Synset Examples

They attended an artsy-craftsy fair to find unique gifts.
She loves artsy-craftsy projects on the weekends.
His home is filled with artsy-craftsy decorations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artsy - showing artistic style or interest, sometimes affectedly so
  • Synonym: crafty - relating to or skilled in crafts (but also means clever/sly)

Etymology

Blend of 'artsy' (imitative of 'artistic') and 'craftsy' (from 'craft', with -sy added for playfulness); both American English coinages from the mid-20th century.
