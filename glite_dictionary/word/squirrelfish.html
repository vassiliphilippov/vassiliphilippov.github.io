squirrelfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwɝːrəlˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈskwɪrəlˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a small, spiny-finned marine fish found in bays along the southeastern coast of the United States, similar in appearance to a sea bream
a type of small, spiny-finned marine fish

Examples

The diver spotted a brightly colored squirrelfish hiding among the coral.
Squirrelfish are known for their large eyes and red hues.
In the aquarium, the squirrelfish darted quickly behind the rocks when startled.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskwɝːrəlˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈskwɪrəlˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a small, brightly colored tropical marine fish with large eyes, known for making noises reminiscent of a squirrel's bark; found mainly in shallow reef waters and active at night
a brightly colored tropical reef fish

Examples

The diver spotted a squirrelfish hiding among the coral at dusk.
Many squirrelfish are caught by underwater photographers for their striking red color.
At night, you can hear the chirping noises of the squirrelfish on the reef.

Etymology

Named for their barking-like noises, reminiscent of a squirrel.
