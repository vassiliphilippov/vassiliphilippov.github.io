federal

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɛdərəl / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəl / (BrE)
relating to a system of government in which power is divided between a central authority and regional governments, such as states or provinces.
of a system dividing power between central and regional government

Examples

The United States has a federal system in which both national and state governments have authority.
Germany is organized as a federal republic with several powerful states.
The constitution set up a federal government to share powers with regional authorities.

Synset Examples

a federal system like that of the United States
federal governments often evolved out of confederations

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unitary - centralized government without division of power between central and regional authorities

Etymology

From Latin 'foedus' (treaty, agreement), via French 'fédéral'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɛdərəl / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəl / (BrE)
Relating to the central government of a federation, especially the United States, as distinct from state or local governments.
relating to a central/national government

Examples

The federal government passed new regulations on environmental protection.
Funding for highways often comes from federal sources rather than state budgets.
Many federal agencies have their headquarters in Washington, D.C.

Synset Examples

the Federal Bureau of Investigation
federal courts
the federal highway program
federal property

Related Terms

  • Synonym: national - relating to an entire nation or country, often in governmental context

Etymology

From Latin 'foedus' (covenant, treaty), via French 'fédéral'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01109822-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈfɛdərəl / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəl / (BrE)
relating to the central government of a federation, as distinct from the governments of its member states.
relating to a central government in a federation

Examples

The federal government passed a new law affecting all states.
Education is managed at both state and federal levels in the United States.
The agency is subject to federal regulations rather than local ordinances.

Synset Examples

a federal district is one set aside as the seat of the national government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: national - relating to or typical of a nation; often used to contrast with 'federal' in countries with a federal system
  • Antonym: state - relating to a state as part of a federal system; often contrasted with 'federal' powers or agencies

Etymology

From Latin 'foedus' (covenant, league), via French 'fédéral'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02736565-a
Synset POS: a
