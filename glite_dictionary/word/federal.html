Federal

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛdərəl / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəl / (BrE)
(historical) A soldier who fought for the Union Army during the American Civil War.
Union Army soldier (US Civil War)

Examples

The Federals advanced across the river to engage the Confederate troops.
Many young men from the North enlisted as Federals during the American Civil War.
After the battle, the wounded Federal was taken to the field hospital.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Union soldier - A member of the US Army fighting for the North in the American Civil War
  • Antonym: Confederate - A soldier in the army of the Confederate States during the American Civil War

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɛd.ɚ.əl / (AmE), / ˈfed.ər.əl / (BrE)
relating to the northern United States and those supporting the Union during the American Civil War.
of the Union in the Civil War (US history)
american

Examples

The Federal army advanced quickly to reclaim the city from Confederate forces.
He wore a blue Federal uniform, marking him as a soldier for the Union.
Many Federal generals later became prominent figures in American politics.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Union - the northern states supporting the federal government during the Civil War
  • Antonym: Confederate - relating to the southern states during the American Civil War

Etymology

From Latin 'foedus' (league, covenant), adopted in American English for Union government or forces during the Civil War.
