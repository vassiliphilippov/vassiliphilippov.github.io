lochia

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊkiə / (AmE), / ˈlɒkiə / (BrE)
a vaginal discharge consisting of blood, mucus, and uterine tissue, occurring for several weeks after childbirth
postpartum vaginal discharge
medical

Examples

After giving birth, the doctor explained that lochia was a normal part of recovery.
Nurses monitor the amount and color of lochia to ensure there are no complications.
The patient was concerned because the lochia had an unusual odor, so she contacted her physician.

Etymology

From Greek *lokheia* (childbirth), related to *lokhos* (childbed).
