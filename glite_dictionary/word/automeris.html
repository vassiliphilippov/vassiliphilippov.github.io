Automeris

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔtəˈmɪrɪs / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈmɪərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of large, showy moths in the family Saturniidae, known for distinctive eyespots on the hindwings, including the io moth.
a genus of large moths (Saturniidae)

Examples

The caterpillar of the Automeris io moth has distinctive spines that can cause skin irritation.
Researchers studied several species within the genus Automeris found in Central America.
The vivid eyespots on the hindwings help Automeris moths deter potential predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saturniidae - a family of large, silk-producing moths to which Automeris belongs

Etymology

From Greek 'auto-' (self) + 'meris' (part), referring to morphological features.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɔtəˈmɛrɪs / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈmɛərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of moths in the family Saturniidae, known for species such as the io moth, found chiefly in the Americas.
genus of moths including the io moth

Examples

The caterpillars of Automeris species can sometimes cause skin irritation if handled.
Entomologists discovered a new Automeris in the Amazon rainforest last year.
The striking eyespots on the wings are characteristic of the Automeris genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: io moth - a species of moth in the genus Automeris, notable for its eyespots

Etymology

From Greek auto- (self) and meros (part).
