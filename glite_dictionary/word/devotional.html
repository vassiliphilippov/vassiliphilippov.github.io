devotional

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈvoʊʃənəl / (AmE), / dɪˈvəʊʃənəl / (BrE)
a short religious service or exercise, especially one for private worship
short religious service

Examples

He attends a morning devotional at his church every day before work.
The students began their day with a brief devotional led by the principal.
A devotional was held before the main ceremony to offer prayers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prayer meeting - a meeting for prayer and worship, especially among Christians

Etymology

From Medieval Latin devotionalis, from Latin devotio (devotion).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈvoʊʃənl̩ / (AmE), / dɪˈvəʊʃənl / (BrE)
relating to the worship of a deity or the expression of religious devotion.
relating to worship or religious devotion

Examples

She attended a devotional service at the church every morning.
Many people keep a devotional book for daily meditation and prayer.
Chanting is a common devotional practice in many religions.

Synset Examples

a devotional exercise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: religious - relating to religion; having to do with faith or worship
  • Antonym: secular - not connected with religion or spiritual matters

Etymology

From Latin devotio (devotion), via Middle French dévotion and English 'devotion'.
