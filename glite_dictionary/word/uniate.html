Uniate

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːniˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪeɪt / (BrE)
A member of an Eastern Christian church in communion with the Roman Catholic Church but retaining its own rites and traditions.
member of an Eastern Catholic Church

Examples

The Uniate community celebrates several unique religious festivals.
Many Uniate churches still follow the Byzantine liturgical traditions.
Her grandparents were Uniates from Eastern Europe.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'uniatus', from Latin 'unitas' meaning unity.

2. ADJ

/ ˈjuːniˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪeɪt / (BrE)
relating to an Eastern Christian church or community that has entered into communion with the Roman Catholic Church but retains its own liturgy and traditions.
of an Eastern church united with Rome, retaining own rites

Examples

The Uniate churches preserve ancient Byzantine rites within Roman Catholicism.
Historians have studied the Uniate movement in Eastern Europe extensively.
Some communities identify as Uniate, maintaining a distinct identity from both Orthodox and Roman Catholic churches.

Etymology

From Ukrainian or Polish *unia* 'union', referring to the ecclesiastical union with Rome.
