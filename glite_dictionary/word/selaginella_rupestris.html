Selaginella rupestris

1. NOUN

/ sɛləˌdʒɪnələ ruˈpɛstrɪs / (AmE), / sɛləˌdʒɪnələ ruˈpɛstrɪs / (BrE)
A species of tufted spikemoss that forms loose, spreading mats, native to eastern North America.
tufted spikemoss of eastern North America

Examples

Selaginella rupestris is commonly found growing on rocky outcrops in eastern North America.
Botanists often study Selaginella rupestris for its ability to survive dry conditions.
The loose mats formed by Selaginella rupestris provide important microhabitats for small insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spikemoss - any plant of the genus Selaginella

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Selaginella' and species epithet 'rupestris' meaning 'of rocks'.
