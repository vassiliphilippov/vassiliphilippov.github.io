Listera cordata

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈstɛrə kɔrˈdeɪtə / (AmE), / lɪˈstɪərə kɔːˈdeɪtə / (BrE)
A species of small orchid with two heart-shaped leaves and a loose cluster of green to rust-colored flowers, found in Europe, Asia, North America, and Greenland.
a species of small orchid

Examples

The rare Listera cordata grows in moist woodlands and mossy bogs in northern regions.
Botanists admire the delicate flowers of Listera cordata for their unique shape and subtle coloration.
During our hike, we spotted several Listera cordata orchids nestled among the undergrowth.

Etymology

From the genus name Listera, honoring British botanist Martin Lister, and Latin cordata (heart-shaped) referring to the leaves.
