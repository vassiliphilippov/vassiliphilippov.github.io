iontophoresis

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ.ɑn.tə.fəˈriː.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.ɒn.tə.fəˈriː.sɪs / (BrE)
a medical treatment that uses an electric current to deliver ions of medication through the skin into body tissues
delivery of drugs through skin using electric current
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended iontophoresis to treat her excessive sweating on the hands.
A physical therapist may use iontophoresis to reduce localized inflammation in a joint.
Recent advances in iontophoresis allow for more efficient drug delivery during therapy sessions.

Etymology

From Greek 'ion' (going) + 'phoresis' (carrying).
