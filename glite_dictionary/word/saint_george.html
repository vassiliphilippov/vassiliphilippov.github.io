Saint George

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈdʒɔrdʒ / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈdʒɔːdʒ / (BrE)
A Christian martyr and the patron saint of England, famed for the legend in which he slew a dragon and saved a princess.
patron saint of England; dragon-slaying legend

Examples

Many English people celebrate Saint George's Day every April 23rd with parades and festivities.
The story of Saint George slaying the dragon is depicted in countless medieval paintings and sculptures.
In some countries, Saint George is honored as a protector of soldiers and a symbol of bravery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saint Andrew - patron saint of Scotland
  • Synonym: Saint David - patron saint of Wales
  • Synonym: dragon - legendary serpent slain by Saint George in the legend
