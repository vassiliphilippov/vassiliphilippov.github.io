to sleepwalk

1. VERB

/ ˈsliːp.wɑk / (AmE), / ˈsliːp.wɔːk / (BrE)
to walk about while asleep, usually unconsciously and without memory of the activity
walk while asleep

Examples

He used to sleepwalk as a child, sometimes wandering around the house at night.
Doctors warned her parents to lock the doors because she might sleepwalk into danger.
After a stressful day, he found himself sleepwalking through the hotel corridors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to somnambulate - walk while asleep (formal term)
  • Antonym: wake - to cease to sleep

Etymology

Formed from 'sleep' + 'walk', first recorded in the 19th century.
