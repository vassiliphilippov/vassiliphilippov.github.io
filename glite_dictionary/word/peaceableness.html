peaceableness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːsəblnəs / (AmE), / ˈpiːsəblnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being calm, tranquil, or inclined to avoid conflict.
state of being peaceful or calm

Examples

Her peaceableness in stressful situations is admired by everyone at work.
The peaceableness of the countryside was a welcome escape from city life.
Their peaceableness made them easy to get along with in group projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peacefulness - the state of being peaceful or calm
  • Antonym: aggressiveness - the quality of being aggressive or inclined to hostility

Etymology

From 'peaceable' (from Middle English, from Old French 'paisible'), plus the suffix '-ness'.
