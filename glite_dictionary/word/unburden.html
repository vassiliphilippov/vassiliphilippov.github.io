to unburden

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈbɝːdən / (AmE), / ʌnˈbɜːdn̩ / (BrE)
to relieve (someone or something) of a burden or load; to free from something that is weighing down
remove a burden or load

Examples

After work, he liked to unburden himself by talking to a close friend.
The farmer stopped to unburden the exhausted horse before sunset.
She decided to unburden her heart in a long letter to her sister.

Synset Examples

unburden the donkey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to relieve - free (someone) from a burden or distress
  • Antonym: burden - to load heavily or to trouble

Etymology

From un- (not, remove) + burden, Middle English from Old English *byrthen* (something carried).

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈbɝːdn̩ / (AmE), / ʌnˈbɜːdn̩ / (BrE)
to free or relieve someone of a burden, problem, or responsibility, often by expressing feelings or disclosing something
free or relieve of a burden

Examples

After a long day, she needed to unburden herself by talking to a friend.
He decided to unburden his heart and confess his worries to his sister.
Writing in his journal helped him unburden his mind of stress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to relieve - to remove or reduce someone's pain or burden
  • Antonym: burden - to cause someone to carry or endure a heavy load or responsibility

Etymology

From un- (reverse, remove) + burden (from Old English 'byrthen').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02365170-v
Synset POS: v
