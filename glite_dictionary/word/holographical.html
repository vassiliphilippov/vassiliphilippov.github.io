holographical

1. ADJ

/ ˌhoʊ.ləˈɡræ.fɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌhɒl.əˈɡræf.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
(of a document) written entirely in the author's own handwriting.
written in one's own hand

Examples

The will was deemed valid because it was a holographical document.
He presented a holographical letter believed to be from the author herself.
In some jurisdictions, a holographical testament is accepted as a legal will.

Synset Examples

holographic document

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holographic - written entirely by the person in whose name it appears

Etymology

From holograph + -ical, relating to Greek holos (whole) and -graphos (written).
