spastic

1. ADJ

/ ˈspæstɪk / (AmE), / ˈspastɪk / (BrE)
relating to or affected by muscle spasms, especially as a result of neurological conditions
affected by muscular spasms
medical

Examples

The patient suffered from spastic movements due to a neurological disorder.
Doctors diagnosed her with a spastic colon after several tests.
Physical therapy was recommended to help reduce spastic symptoms in his legs.

Synset Examples

a spastic colon
spastic paralysis is a spastic form of cerebral palsy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flaccid - lacking muscle tone; opposite of spastic in muscle tone

Etymology

From Middle French spastique, from Latin spasticus, from Greek spastikos (drawing in, convulsive).

2. ADJ

/ ˈspæstɪk / (AmE), / ˈspæstɪk / (BrE)
relating to or affected by involuntary jerky muscular contractions; resembling a spasm.
affected by muscular spasms

Examples

Some patients experience spastic movements in their limbs after a stroke.
The spastic contractions made it difficult for him to control his muscles.
The doctor noted a spastic response during the neurological exam.

Synset Examples

convulsive motions
his body made a spasmodic jerk
spastic movements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spasmodic - having the characteristics of a spasm; convulsive
  • Synonym: convulsive - producing or consisting of convulsions

Etymology

From Latin spasticus, from Greek spastikos (drawing in, pulling tight), from span (to draw, pull).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02311435-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈspæstɪk / (AmE), / ˈspæstɪk / (BrE)
suffering from or relating to muscle stiffness or involuntary muscle spasms, especially as a result of spastic paralysis.
having or related to spastic paralysis
medical

Examples

The doctor specializes in treating spastic children with cerebral palsy.
A spastic gait can make walking difficult for those affected.
The clinic offers therapy for spastic muscles caused by neurological conditions.

Synset Examples

a spastic child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paraplegic - affected by paralysis of the lower body
  • Antonym: flaccid - lacking firmness or muscle tone, opposite of spastic

Etymology

From Latin 'spasticus', from Greek 'spastikos' (drawing in, convulsive).

4. NOUN

/ ˈspæstɪk / (AmE), / ˈspæstɪk / (BrE)
(dated, offensive) A person suffering from spastic paralysis, a condition marked by stiff or uncontrollable muscles.
person with spastic paralysis (dated, offensive)
offensive medical

Examples

In the past, children with cerebral palsy were sometimes referred to as spastics.
The use of the term spastic to describe patients is now considered outdated and offensive.
Medical professionals no longer use spastic as a noun in clinical settings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spastic paralysis - a condition marked by stiff or uncontrollable muscles

Etymology

From Latin spasticus, from Greek spastikos (‘drawing in’), from span (‘to draw, pull’).

Source: WordNet
