tarantula

1. NOUN

/ təˈræntʃələ / (AmE), / təˈræntjʊlə / (BrE)
a large hairy spider, especially native to southern Europe, once believed to cause tarantism; also refers to related large spiders found elsewhere
large hairy spider, esp. of southern Europe

Examples

A tarantula crawled slowly across the forest floor, its hairy legs moving silently.
Many people believe the bite of a tarantula is deadly, but most species are harmless to humans.
She kept a pet tarantula in a glass terrarium on her desk.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin tarantula, named after Taranto, a town in southern Italy.

2. NOUN

/ təˈræntʃələ / (AmE), / təˈræntjʊlə / (BrE)
A large, hairy tropical spider of the family Theraphosidae, known for its fangs and painful but rarely dangerous bite.
large hairy tropical spider

Examples

A tarantula crawled slowly across the forest floor, its hairy legs brushing aside dry leaves.
Many people are afraid of the tarantula, but its bite is rarely dangerous to humans.
He kept a tarantula as a pet in a glass terrarium in his bedroom.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: spider - an arachnid with eight legs and the ability to spin silk

Etymology

From Medieval Latin tarantula, after Taranto, a town in southern Italy where the species was first recorded.
