Cardamine rotundifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːr.dəˈmaɪn roʊˌtʌn.dəˈfoʊ.li.ə / (AmE), / ˌkɑː.dəˈmiːn rəʊˌtʌn.dɪˈfəʊ.li.ə / (BrE)
A mat-forming perennial plant species found in cold springs of the eastern United States, belonging to the mustard family.
mat-forming perennial plant of eastern U.S.

Examples

The cold stream bank was covered in a lush patch of Cardamine rotundifolia.
Researchers noted that Cardamine rotundifolia thrives best in cool, shaded springs.
Botanists often seek Cardamine rotundifolia when surveying plant diversity in Appalachian wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: round-leaved bittercress - Common name for Cardamine rotundifolia

Etymology

From Latin: 'Cardamine' (a genus of plants in the mustard family) + 'rotundifolia' (round-leaved).
