dragnet

1. NOUN

/ ˈdræɡˌnɛt / (AmE), / ˈdræɡ.nɛt / (BrE)
a system of coordinated actions organized by police or other authorities in order to catch criminals or wanted persons
systematic search by authorities

Examples

The fugitive was finally caught in the FBI's dragnet after weeks on the run.
Authorities set up a dragnet to capture suspects trying to leave the city.
News of the dragnet spread quickly, warning others to stay away from the area.

Synset Examples

caught in the police dragnet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manhunt - an organized search for a person, usually a fugitive

Etymology

Originally referred to a fishing net drawn through water, from 'drag' + 'net'; later applied metaphorically to police operations.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdræɡˌnɛt / (AmE), / ˈdræɡ.nɛt / (BrE)
A large conical fishing net that is dragged through the water to catch fish, especially at great depths.
large fishing net dragged through water

Examples

Fishermen lowered the dragnet into the deep sea to maximize their catch.
The trawler pulled up its heavy dragnet, full of various fish and debris.
Using a dragnet allows crews to harvest large amounts of seafood at once.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seine - a fishing net that hangs vertically in the water with floats at the top and weights at the bottom edge

Etymology

From 'drag' + 'net', referring to the action of dragging the net through water.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
