covetously

1. ADV

/ ˈkʌvətəsli / (AmE), / ˈkʌvɪtəsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows a strong desire for something belonging to someone else; in an envious or jealous manner.
in an envious or jealous way

Examples

The child gazed covetously at the candy behind the glass.
She watched covetously as her colleague accepted the award.
He eyed his neighbor’s garden covetously, wishing for such beautiful flowers.

Synset Examples

he looked at his friend's new car jealously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enviously - in a way that shows envy or jealousy
  • Synonym: jealously - in a manner showing jealousy

Etymology

From Latin 'cupidus' (desirous), via 'covetous' (Middle English).

2. ADV

/ ˈkʌv.ə.t̬əs.li / (AmE), / ˈkʌv.ɪ.təs.li / (BrE)
In a greedy or envious manner; with a strong desire for what belongs to others.
in a greedy or envious way

Examples

She looked covetously at her neighbor's new car, longing to own one herself.
The children eyed the last slice of cake covetously, each hoping to claim it.
He watched covetously as his colleague received the prestigious award.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greedily - in a greedy manner
  • Antonym: generously - in a generous manner

Etymology

From Latin 'cupidus' (desirous), via Old French 'coveitus.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00277821-r
Synset POS: r
