brassie

1. NOUN

/ ˈbræsi / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːsi / (BrE)
(historical) A type of wooden golf club with a loft higher than a driver but lower than a spoon, used especially before the advent of modern clubs.
old type of golf club
archaic

Examples

In his grandfather's day, a brassie was essential for long fairway shots.
He displayed a vintage brassie alongside other antique golf clubs in the clubhouse.
Golfers no longer carry a brassie, as modern woods have taken its place.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: driver - golf club used for long-distance shots, lowest loft
  • Degree Higher: spoon - old golf club with more loft than a brassie

Etymology

From 'brass', referring to the brass plate fitted to the sole of the club head.
