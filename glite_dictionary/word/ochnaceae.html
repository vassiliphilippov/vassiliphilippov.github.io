Ochnaceae

1. NOUN

/ ɑkˈneɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ɒkˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical evergreen trees and shrubs characterized by thick, shiny, parallel-veined leaves.
family of tropical evergreen trees and shrubs

Examples

The Ochnaceae are easily recognized by their parallel-veined leaves and shiny appearance.
Several species within Ochnaceae are valued for ornamental landscaping in tropical regions.
Botanists discovered a new genus of the Ochnaceae during their expedition.

Etymology

From modern Latin Ochnaceae, based on the genus Ochna.
