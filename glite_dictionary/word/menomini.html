Menomini

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛnəˈmiːni / (AmE), / ˌmɛnəˈmiːni / (BrE)
the Algonquian language traditionally spoken by the Menomini people of Wisconsin.
Algonquian language of the Menomini people

Examples

Few people today are fluent speakers of Menomini.
Linguists are working to document and preserve the Menomini language.
He took a class to learn basic Menomini phrases and greetings.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Algonquian - a family of North American indigenous languages

Etymology

From the name of the Menomini people, an Algonquian-speaking Indigenous group of Wisconsin.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛnəˈmiːni / (AmE), / ˌmɛnəˈmiːni / (BrE)
a member of the federally recognized Algonquian people native to central Wisconsin
member of an Algonquian Native American tribe in Wisconsin

Examples

The Menomini have lived in Wisconsin for centuries, maintaining rich cultural traditions.
A delegation of Menomini leaders visited the museum to share their history with local students.
The documentary features the daily life on the Menomini reservation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Menominee - alternate spelling for the same Algonquian people

Etymology

From Menominee (Ojibwe manoominii, meaning 'wild rice people').
