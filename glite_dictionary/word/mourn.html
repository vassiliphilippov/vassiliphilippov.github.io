to mourn

1. VERB

/ mɔrn / (AmE), / mɔːn / (BrE)
to feel or show deep sadness, especially because someone has died
feel or show deep sadness

Examples

Many people mourn the loss of a loved one in different ways.
She continues to mourn her childhood friend, even after many years have passed.
It is natural to mourn after experiencing a significant loss.

Synset Examples

She is mourning her dead child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to grieve - to feel deep sorrow, especially for a death
  • Antonym: to rejoice - to feel or show great joy or delight

Etymology

From Old English murnan (to grieve), related to Old High German mornōn.

2. VERB

/ mɔrn / (AmE), / mɔːn / (BrE)
to observe traditional customs or express sadness after the death of someone.
express grief after a death

Examples

After his grandmother died, the whole family gathered to mourn according to tradition.
Many cultures mourn the loss of loved ones with special ceremonies and rites.
She continued to mourn her late husband for several years, wearing black as a sign of respect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to grieve - to feel or show deep sadness, especially after a loss
  • Antonym: to celebrate - to observe with rejoicing; opposite in the context of mourning a death

Etymology

From Old English *murnan* (to grieve, lament).
