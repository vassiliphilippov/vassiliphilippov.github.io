Canicula

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnɪkjʊlə / (AmE), / kəˈnɪkjʊlə / (BrE)
an old term for Sirius, the brightest star in the sky, located in the constellation Canis Major
Sirius, brightest star in Canis Major
archaic

Examples

Ancient astronomers used the rising of the Canicula to mark the start of summer.
The brilliant glow of Canicula can be seen just above the horizon before dawn.
Early texts often refer to Sirius as the Canicula or Dog Star.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sirius - the brightest star in the night sky, also known as the Dog Star

Etymology

From Latin 'canicula' (small dog), referring to the constellation Canis Major.
