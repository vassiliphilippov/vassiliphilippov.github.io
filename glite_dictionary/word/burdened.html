burdened

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɝːdənd / (AmE), / ˈbɜːdənd / (BrE)
carrying a heavy load of work, difficulties, or responsibilities; feeling weighed down by something.
weighed down by work or difficulties

Examples

She felt burdened by the weight of her responsibilities at work.
After the financial crisis, many families were burdened with debt.
He walked out of the meeting looking burdened and worried.

Synset Examples

she always felt burdened by the load of paper work

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unburdened - not weighed down by burdens or responsibilities

Etymology

From 'burden' (Middle English, from Old English 'byrthen'), plus the past participle suffix '-ed'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbɝːdnd / (AmE), / ˈbɜːdnd / (BrE)
Carrying a heavy weight or load; weighed down physically.
carrying a heavy weight or load

Examples

After the snowstorm, the branches were burdened with thick layers of ice.
She walked slowly, burdened by the heavy backpack on her shoulders.
The donkey was burdened with sacks of grain for the long journey.

Synset Examples

tree limbs burdened with ice
a heavy-laden cart
loaded down with packages

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loaded - bearing a load; carrying a weight
  • Antonym: unburdened - not carrying a burden or load

Etymology

From the verb 'burden' (from Old English 'byrthen', meaning load or weight).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00871549-s
Synset POS: s
