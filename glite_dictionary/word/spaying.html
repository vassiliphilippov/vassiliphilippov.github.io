spaying

1. NOUN

/ ˈspeɪ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspeɪ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the surgical removal of the ovaries (and usually the uterus) of a female animal to prevent reproduction
removal of female animal's ovaries
medical

Examples

Many veterinarians recommend spaying cats to help control the pet population.
The shelter offered spaying services for female dogs at a reduced cost.
After spaying her rabbit, Jane noticed she was much calmer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neutering - removal of reproductive organs to prevent animal breeding
  • Antonym: castration - removal of testicles in a male animal

Etymology

From 'spay', from Old French *espeer* (to cut with a sword)
