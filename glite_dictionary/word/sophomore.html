sophomore

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːf.moʊr / (AmE), / ˈsɒf.mə / (BrE)
A student in the second year of a course at a college or university, especially in the US.
second-year student (college/university, US)
american

Examples

After completing her freshman year, Maria returned in the fall as a sophomore.
The university organized a special event for all sophomore students.
He became president of his club during his sophomore year at college.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: freshman - a first-year undergraduate student, especially in the US
  • Degree Higher: junior - a third-year undergraduate student, especially in the US

Etymology

From Greek 'sophos' (wise) and 'moros' (foolish); originally American college slang.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɑːfmoʊr / (AmE), / ˈsɒfəmɔː / (BrE)
relating to the second year at an American high school or college.
second-year (US high school or college)
american

Examples

Many students find that their sophomore year is more challenging than their freshman year.
The university requires all sophomore students to complete a writing course.
She joined the debate team during her sophomore semester.

Synset Examples

the sophomore class
his sophomore year

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: freshman - first-year student at high school or college (US)
  • Degree Higher: junior - third-year student at high school or college (US)

Etymology

From Greek sophos (wise) + moros (foolish), originally denoting a wise fool; adopted into American English for students in their second year.
