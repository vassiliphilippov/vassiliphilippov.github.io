jassid

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈdʒæsɪd / (BrE)
Any of various small plant-sucking insects of the family Cicadellidae (leafhoppers), known for feeding on plant sap and sometimes causing damage to crops.
a type of leafhopper insect

Examples

The cotton plants were infested with jassids that damaged the leaves.
Farmers monitor their crops regularly to detect the presence of jassids.
The biologist identified the insect sample as a species of jassid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leafhopper - a small jumping insect of the family Cicadellidae
