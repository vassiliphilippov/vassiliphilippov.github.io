buttermilk

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌt̬ərˌmɪlk / (AmE), / ˈbʌtəˌmɪlk / (BrE)
The liquid left after churning butter from cream, traditionally from sour raw milk; or pasteurized milk that has been cultured to create a slightly sour taste.
slightly sour liquid left after churning butter

Examples

She made fluffy pancakes using buttermilk instead of regular milk.
A glass of chilled buttermilk is refreshing on a hot day.
Traditional recipes often call for buttermilk to add a tangy flavor to baked goods.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: milk - the white liquid produced by the mammary glands of mammals, especially cows, used as food by humans
  • Degree Higher: cream - the thick, fatty part of milk that rises to the top; used to make butter

Etymology

Formed from 'butter' + 'milk', referring to the liquid byproduct of butter-making.
