lack

1. NOUN

/ læk / (AmE), / læk / (BrE)
The state of not having or being without something that is needed or desired.
state of being without something needed

Examples

There was a notable lack of enthusiasm among the attendees at the conference.
Many developing countries suffer from a lack of access to clean water.
The project failed due to a lack of funding.

Synset Examples

there is a serious lack of insight into the problem
water is the critical deficiency in desert regions
for want of a nail the shoe was lost

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shortage - a situation in which there is not enough of something
  • Synonym: deficiency - a lack or shortage of something necessary
  • Antonym: abundance - a large quantity of something, more than enough
  • Synonym: scarcity - the state of being scarce, in short supply

Etymology

From Old Norse *laka* (to lack, be wanting).

2. VERB

/ læk / (AmE), / læk / (BrE)
to be without something that is needed or wanted
be without; not have

Examples

She realized her meal did lack flavor, so she added more spices.
Many remote villages still lack access to clean water.
His proposal lacks sufficient detail to be approved.

Synset Examples

This soup lacks salt
There is something missing in my jewelry box!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miss - to not have or experience something; to be without
  • Antonym: to possess - to have or own something

Etymology

From Middle English laken (to lack), from Old Norse lak (deficiency or fault).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02638434-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ læk / (AmE), / læk / (BrE)
to be without or not have enough of something, especially something necessary or desirable
to be without something needed

Examples

She lacks confidence when speaking in public.
Despite his efforts, he lacked the necessary experience for the job.
Many students lack adequate preparation for the exam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: need - to require or be in want of something
  • Antonym: to possess - to have or own something

Etymology

From Old Northern French *lac* (deficiency) and Middle English *lacken*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-90012531-v
Synset POS: v
