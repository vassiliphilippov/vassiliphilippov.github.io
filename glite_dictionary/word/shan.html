Shan

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑn / (AmE), / ʃan / (BrE)
A Tai language spoken mainly in the Louangphabang Province of Laos, and by Shan people in Myanmar and surrounding regions.
a Tai language of Laos and Myanmar

Examples

She is fluent in Shan, as well as Burmese and Mandarin.
The Shan language has its own unique script and literary tradition.
Linguists have documented the grammar and sounds of Shan in several studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tai - a branch of the Tai-Kadai language family

Etymology

From the name 'Shan', which derives from Burmese 'Shan', itself from 'Siam'.
