regional

1. ADJ

/ ˈriːdʒənl̩ / (AmE), / ˈriːdʒənəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a particular region or area, especially as distinguished from others.
of or relating to a region

Examples

The festival features a wide range of regional foods from around the country.
Her research focuses on regional differences in language usage.
The museum exhibits highlight the regional history and culture of the area.

Synset Examples

regional flora

Related Terms

  • Synonym: local - relating to a particular area or neighborhood
  • Antonym: national - relating to a whole nation rather than a local area

Etymology

From Latin regio (region) + -al (adjectival suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈriːdʒənl / (AmE), / ˈriːdʒənəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a particular region or area, rather than the whole country or world.
relating to a specific region

Examples

The competition only features regional teams from the northern states.
There are significant regional differences in climate across the country.
The company has several regional offices to serve its customers more efficiently.

Synset Examples

a regional dialect

Related Terms

  • Synonym: local - relating to a particular area, especially nearby
  • Antonym: national - relating to a whole nation or country
  • Antonym: global - relating to the whole world

Etymology

From Latin regio (region) + -al (adjective suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02417773-s
Synset POS: s
