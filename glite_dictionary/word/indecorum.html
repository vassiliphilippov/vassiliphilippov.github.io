indecorum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.dɪˈkɔɹ.əm / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dɪˈkɔː.rəm / (BrE)
Lack of propriety or good manners; behavior that is improper or inappropriate.
lack of propriety or good manners

Examples

The teacher was shocked by the indecorum displayed during the assembly.
His constant interruptions and rude remarks were acts of indecorum.
Critics accused the official of indecorum during the televised debate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decorum - behavior in keeping with good taste and propriety

Etymology

From Latin 'indecorum,' from 'in-' (not) + 'decorum' (propriety).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.dɪˈkɔɹ.əm / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dɪˈkɔː.rəm / (BrE)
behavior or actions that violate accepted standards of propriety or good taste; lack of decorum.
lack of propriety; improper behavior

Examples

The guests found his indecorum at the formal dinner quite shocking.
Such indecorum is not tolerated in prestigious institutions.
Her public display of affection was regarded as an indecorum by traditionalists.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decorum - behavior in keeping with good taste and propriety
  • Synonym: impropriety - failure to observe standards of honesty or modesty; improper behavior

Etymology

From Latin 'indecorum', from 'in-' (not) + 'decorum' (propriety, seemliness).
