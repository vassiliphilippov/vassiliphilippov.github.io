to retranslate

1. VERB

/ ˌriːtrænzˈleɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːtrænzˈleɪt / (BrE)
to translate something again, usually to improve accuracy or adapt to a new context
translate again

Examples

After reading the new manuscript, the translator decided to retranslate several key passages for clarity.
The director asked her team to retranslate the subtitles to better match the original tone of the film.
Sometimes scholars will retranslate ancient texts as language and interpretation evolve over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to translate - to render text or speech in another language

Etymology

From re- (again) + translate, modeled on Latin-based word formation.
