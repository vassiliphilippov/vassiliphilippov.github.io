ammoniuria

1. NOUN

/ əˌmoʊniˈjʊriə / (AmE), / əˌmɒniˈjʊəriə / (BrE)
The presence of an unusually high level of ammonia in the urine, often indicating a metabolic or renal disorder.
excess ammonia in urine (medical)
medical

Examples

Ammoniuria can be a sign of a metabolic imbalance or a kidney disorder.
Doctors may order further tests if ammoniuria is detected on a urinalysis.
The treatment for ammoniuria depends on the underlying cause of the excessive ammonia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: albuminuria - presence of albumin in the urine
  • Synonym: hematuria - presence of blood in the urine

Etymology

From New Latin: 'ammonia' + '-uria' (Greek 'ouron' meaning urine).
