public

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌblɪk / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk / (BrE)
the people in general; ordinary people considered as a group
the general population

Examples

The mayor made an announcement to the public about the upcoming changes.
Health officials urged the public to follow new safety guidelines.
Her paintings have never been shown to the public before.

Synset Examples

he is a hero in the eyes of the public

Related Terms

  • Synonym: society - people living together in organized communities
  • Antonym: private - not open to the general public; belonging to an individual

Etymology

From Latin 'publicus', meaning 'of the people'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈpʌblɪk / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk / (BrE)
relating to or affecting all the people or the whole community; not private or exclusive
relating to the people as a whole

Examples

She works in public health, focusing on improving community wellness.
The mayor called for more public investment in local schools and parks.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: private - relating to an individual person or limited group, not public

Etymology

3. NOUN

/ ˈpʌblɪk / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk / (BrE)
a group of people with a shared interest or characteristic, especially considered as a target audience or community
a group sharing a common interest

Examples

The public was invited to attend the museum's grand opening.
She has written several bestselling novels for the reading public.
Politicians must always consider the interests of the general public when making decisions.

Synset Examples

the reading public

Related Terms

  • Synonym: audience - a group of people assembled to watch or listen to something
  • Synonym: community - a group of people living in the same place or sharing a particular interest

Etymology

From Latin 'publicus' (of the people, public), related to 'populus' (people).
