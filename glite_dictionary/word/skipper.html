skipper

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɪpər / (AmE), / ˈskɪpə / (BrE)
the officer in command of a ship, especially in a navy; the captain of a naval vessel
officer commanding a ship, especially in the navy

Examples

The skipper gave the order to change course immediately.
All the crew respected the skipper for his calm leadership during the storm.
After years at sea, she finally became the skipper of her own ship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: captain - the person in command of a ship
  • Antonym: crew member - a member of a ship's crew, subordinate to the skipper

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *schipper* (ship's captain), from *schip* (ship).

2. NOUN

/ ˈskɪpər / (AmE), / ˈskɪpə / (BrE)
the officer in charge of a ship, especially a merchant vessel; the captain or master of a ship
commander of a ship or captain

Examples

The skipper of the cargo ship gave orders to the crew before departure.
When the weather turned rough, the skipper remained calm and navigated safely to port.
As skipper, she was responsible for the safety of everyone on board.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: captain - the person in ultimate command of a vessel
  • Antonym: crewman - a member of the crew, not the commanding officer

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *schipper*, from *schip* (ship).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈskɪpɚ / (AmE), / ˈskɪpə / (BrE)
to act as the captain or person in command of a vessel or boat
act as captain of a vessel

Examples

She skippered the yacht across the Atlantic last summer.
He has skippered several fishing trawlers over the past decade.
They asked her to skipper the team in the upcoming regatta.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: captain - to act as the person in command of a ship

Etymology

From Middle Dutch 'schipper' (ship's captain), from 'schip' (ship).
