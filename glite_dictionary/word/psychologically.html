psychologically

1. ADV

/ ˌsaɪkəˈlɑdʒɪkli / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəˈlɒdʒɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to the mind or mental processes; with regard to psychology.
in terms of the mind or psychology

Examples

The team was psychologically prepared for the difficult challenge ahead.
She felt exhausted both physically and psychologically after the long ordeal.
Children may be psychologically affected by constant exposure to conflict.

Synset Examples

war that caught them in its toils either psychologically or physically
the event was very damaging to the child psychologically

Related Terms

  • Antonym: physically - in a way that relates to the body or physical condition

Etymology

Formed from 'psychological' (from Greek 'psyche' meaning 'mind') + '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ saɪˌkɑlədʒɪkli / (AmE), / saɪˌkɒlədʒɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to psychology or the functioning of the mind.
in terms of psychology or the mind

Examples

He was psychologically prepared for the challenges ahead.
Some people are psychologically affected by traumatic events.
The film explores how characters change psychologically over time.

Synset Examples

classify poetry psychologically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emotionally - in a way that relates to emotions
  • Antonym: physically - in a way that relates to the body

Etymology

Formed from 'psychological' + '-ly'; 'psychological' from Greek 'psyche' (soul, mind) + 'logos' (study).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00437035-r
Synset POS: r
