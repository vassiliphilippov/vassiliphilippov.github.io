Saxifraga hypnoides

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæksɪˈfreɪɡə hɪpˈnɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˌsæksɪˈfreɪɡə hɪpˈnɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A mat-forming perennial plant native to European mountains, cultivated for its small white flowers.
mountain plant with white flowers

Examples

The Saxifraga hypnoides adds delicate beauty to alpine rock gardens.
Botanists often study Saxifraga hypnoides for its adaptation to mountainous environments.
She planted Saxifraga hypnoides alongside other shade-loving perennials.

Etymology

From Latin: 'saxifraga' meaning 'stone-breaker' and 'hypnoides' meaning 'moss-like'.
