Abyssinian

1. NOUN

/ ˌæbɪˈsɪniən / (AmE), / ˌæbɪˈsɪniən / (BrE)
a breed of domestic cat of African origin, having a slender body, short brownish fur, and a reddish undercoat
breed of slender short-haired cat

Examples

The Abyssinian is known for its lively personality and striking appearance.
Many people admire the soft, ticked coat of the Abyssinian breed.
Her family adopted a playful Abyssinian from the local animal shelter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cat - domesticated feline animal (Felis catus)

Etymology

Named after Abyssinia (now Ethiopia), the region from which the breed was believed to have originated.
