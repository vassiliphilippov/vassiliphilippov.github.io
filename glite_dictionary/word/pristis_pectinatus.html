Pristis pectinatus

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪstɪs pɛkˈtɪnətəs / (AmE), / ˈprɪstɪs pɛkˈtɪnətəs / (BrE)
A species of sawfish found in tropical bays and estuaries, known for its long, toothed snout and generally non-aggressive behavior.
species of sawfish with toothed snout

Examples

The Pristis pectinatus is now endangered due to habitat loss and overfishing.
Researchers tagged several Pristis pectinatus to study their movement in coastal waters.
Local fishermen recognize the distinctive toothed snout of the Pristis pectinatus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sawfish - a type of large, cartilaginous fish with a long, flattened, toothed snout

Etymology

From Latin 'pristis' (shark or sawfish) and 'pectinatus' (comb-like, referring to the snout teeth).
