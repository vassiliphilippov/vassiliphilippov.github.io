coulomb

1. NOUN

/ ˈkuːˌlɑm, ˈkuːˌloʊm, kuːˈlɒm / (AmE), / ˈkuːlɒm / (BrE)
the SI unit of electric charge, equal to the quantity of electricity transferred by a current of one ampere in one second
the SI unit of electric charge

Examples

A single coulomb is the amount of electric charge transferred in one second by a current of one ampere.
The capacitor can store up to five coulombs of electrical charge.
When you measure the flow of electrons, the coulomb is an essential unit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ampere - the SI unit of electric current

Etymology

Named after Charles-Augustin de Coulomb, French physicist (1736–1806).
