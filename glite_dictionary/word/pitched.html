pitched

1. ADJ

/ pɪtʃt / (AmE), / pɪtʃt / (BrE)
(of a roof or surface) set at a slant or angle rather than flat or level.
set at a slant or angle

Examples

The house had a traditional pitched roof covered with red tiles.
A pitched driveway allows rainwater to drain easily.
You must install special gutters for a pitched roof.

Synset Examples

a pitched rather than a flat roof

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flat - having a level surface with no slope

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'pitch' (to set at a particular angle or slope) + '-ed'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01238207-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ pɪtʃt / (AmE), / pɪtʃt / (BrE)
(of sound) set at a particular pitch or key.
set at a particular pitch (of sound)

Examples

The alarm emitted a pitched tone that quickly drew everyone's attention.
They preferred voices with a lower pitched quality for the narration.
The pitched whine gradually faded as the machine powered down.

Synset Examples

high-pitched

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: high-pitched - (of sound) having a high pitch
  • Degree Lower: low-pitched - (of sound) having a low pitch
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03163882-a
Synset POS: a
