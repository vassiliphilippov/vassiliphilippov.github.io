paleography

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪliˈɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌpeɪliˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the study of ancient writing systems and the deciphering and dating of historical manuscripts
study of ancient writing

Examples

She is an expert in paleography and spends her days analyzing medieval manuscripts.
A background in paleography is essential for historians studying ancient texts.
He took a course on paleography to learn how to read documents written in obsolete scripts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epigraphy - the study of inscriptions or epigraphs as writing
  • Synonym: calligraphy - the art of beautiful handwriting

Etymology

From Greek palaiós (ancient) + -graphia (writing).
