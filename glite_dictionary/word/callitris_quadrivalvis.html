Callitris quadrivalvis

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪtrɪs ˌkwɒdrɪˈvælvɪs / (AmE), / kəˈlɪtrɪs ˌkwɒdrɪˈvalvɪs / (BrE)
A large evergreen conifer native to North Africa and Spain, characterized by flattened branches, scalelike leaves, and fragrant, hard wood; its bark produces a resin used in varnishes.
coniferous tree of North Africa and Spain

Examples

The timber from Callitris quadrivalvis is highly valued for its durability and fragrance.
Resin extracted from Callitris quadrivalvis bark is used in making high-quality varnishes.
Botanists study Callitris quadrivalvis to understand its adaptation to arid climates.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, genus name 'Callitris' from Greek 'kallos' (beauty) and 'tri' (three), referring to tree features; 'quadrivalvis' means 'four-valved' in Latin.
