orotund

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔːrəˌtʌnd / (AmE), / ˈɒrətʌnd / (BrE)
(of writing or speech) pompous or overly grand and impressive in style or tone
pompous or grand style (of speech or writing)

Examples

The mayor's orotund style made his speeches sound more dramatic than substantial.
She mocked his orotund pronouncements, preferring straightforward explanations to flowery language.
The novel's opening chapter is written in an orotund tone, full of elaborate metaphors and grand statements.

Synset Examples

a man given to large talk
tumid political prose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bombastic - high-sounding but with little meaning; inflated
  • Synonym: grandiloquent - pompous or extravagant in style, especially in a way that is intended to impress
  • Antonym: plain - simple or lacking embellishment in speech or writing

Etymology

From Latin 'ore rotundo' (with round mouth), referring to round, full speech.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɔːrəˌtʌnd / (AmE), / ˈɒrətʌnd / (BrE)
(of a sound or a voice) full, round, and imposing in quality.
full and resonant (of sound)

Examples

The actor's orotund voice filled every corner of the theater.
Her orotund tones gave the speech a powerful and commanding presence.
He practiced until his vowels were perfectly orotund for the performance.

Synset Examples

orotund tones
the rotund and reverberating phrase
pear-shaped vowels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sonorous - (of a sound) imposingly deep and full
  • Synonym: resonant - (of sound) deep, clear, and continuing to sound or ring

Etymology

From Latin orotundus, from os, oris 'mouth' + rotundus 'round'.
