puckerbush

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌk.ərˌbʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈpʌk.əˌbʊʃ / (BrE)
An evergreen aromatic shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States, bearing small hard berries coated with white wax, which is used for making candles.
aromatic shrub with waxy berries of southeastern US

Examples

The old farm was bordered by a dense line of puckerbush along the road.
Early settlers collected berries from the puckerbush to make candles for their homes.
The distinctive aroma of puckerbush trees filled the southern woodland air.
