pitiful

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɪtɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈpɪtɪfəl / (BrE)
deserving or arousing a mixture of pity and contempt; so inadequate or miserable as to inspire scornful pity.
deserving contemptuous pity

Examples

The team's pitiful performance left the fans both disappointed and embarrassed.
He offered a pitiful excuse for missing the important deadline.
She gave a pitiful cry for help that barely reached the neighbors.

Synset Examples

their efforts were pathetic
pitiable lack of character
pitiful exhibition of cowardice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pathetic - arousing pity, especially through vulnerability or sadness
  • Synonym: contemptible - deserving contempt; despicable
  • Antonym: admirable - deserving respect or approval

Etymology

From Middle English *pitiful*, from Old French *pitefel*, from *pite* (pity) + *-ful* (full of).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɪdɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈpɪtɪfəl / (BrE)
deserving or causing feelings of pity; arousing compassion because of distress or inadequacy
deserving or causing pity

Examples

The abandoned puppy was in such a pitiful state that everyone wanted to help.
Her pitiful attempt at an apology only made things worse.
We donated to the charity because the conditions in the refugee camp were truly pitiful.

Synset Examples

a hapless victim
miserable victims of war
the shabby room struck her as extraordinarily pathetic
piteous appeals for help
pitiable homeless children
a pitiful fate
Oh, you poor thing
his poor distorted limbs
a wretched life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitiable - deserving pity, usually because of misfortune
  • Synonym: pathetic - arousing pity, especially through vulnerability or sadness
  • Synonym: wretched - in a very unhappy or unfortunate state
  • Antonym: admirable - inspiring approval or respect, the opposite of pitiful

Etymology

From Middle English pitiful, from Old French pitiful, from pitié (pity) + -ful (full of).

3. ADJ

/ ˈpɪtəfəl / (AmE), / ˈpɪtɪfəl / (BrE)
Very bad or unfortunate; deserving disapproval or sympathy because of poor quality or condition.
very poor or unfortunate

Examples

The team gave a pitiful performance and lost the match by a wide margin.
She looked at the pitiful state of the abandoned house and felt sorry for its past inhabitants.
After the accident, his excuse was so pitiful that no one believed him.

Synset Examples

my finances were in a deplorable state
a lamentable decision
her clothes were in sad shape
a sorry state of affairs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deplorable - shockingly bad in quality
  • Synonym: sorry - bad in quality or condition; deserving pity
  • Synonym: lamentable - bad or unfortunate; worthy of complaint
  • Antonym: excellent - of the highest quality

Etymology

From Middle English pitous, pitiful, from Old French pitiful (compassionate), from pitié (pity).

Source: WordNet
