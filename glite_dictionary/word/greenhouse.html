greenhouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrinˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnhaʊs / (BrE)
a building with glass walls and roof, used for growing and displaying plants in controlled environmental conditions
building for growing plants under glass

Examples

Tom built a small greenhouse in his backyard to grow tomatoes year-round.
The botanical garden's greenhouse is filled with rare tropical plants.
Maintaining a greenhouse requires careful control of temperature and humidity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hothouse - a heated building for growing plants

Etymology

From 'green' (the color of plants) + 'house' (building for shelter).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03462844-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡrinˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnhaʊs / (BrE)
(of gases or related phenomena) relating to or resulting from the greenhouse effect, especially in causing global warming.
relating to the greenhouse effect

Examples

Carbon dioxide is a major greenhouse gas contributing to climate change.
Scientists are studying the rise in greenhouse emissions from vehicles.
Greenhouse warming is a serious concern for the future of the planet.

Synset Examples

greenhouse gases

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greenhouse effect - the trapping of the sun's warmth in a planet's lower atmosphere

Etymology

From the analogy to the glass greenhouse, where heat is trapped inside, leading to warming.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03081529-a
Synset POS: a
