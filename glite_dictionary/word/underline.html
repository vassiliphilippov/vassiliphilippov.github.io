underline

1. VERB

/ ˈʌn.dɚˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈʌn.dəˌlaɪn / (BrE)
to draw a line or lines under a word or sentence to emphasize it or indicate its importance
draw a line underneath for emphasis

Examples

Please underline the key words in each sentence.
The teacher asked us to underline any unfamiliar vocabulary.
He underlined her name twice to make sure he wouldn't forget.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: highlight - mark text with a background color or emphasis

Etymology

From under + line, meaning literally ‘draw a line under’.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01006280-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌndərˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈʌndəlaɪn / (BrE)
a line drawn underneath a word or phrase in text, often to emphasize it or indicate its importance
a line below text for emphasis or indication

Examples

The teacher asked us to draw an underline beneath each title.
The underline in red made the word stand out on the page.
Please use a single underline, not a double, when filling out the form.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: underscore - a line drawn under a word or space in text
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06814276-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈʌndərˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌlaɪn / (BrE)
to emphasize or give extra weight to something, especially in speech or writing
emphasize or stress (figurative)

Examples

She wanted to underline the importance of honesty in her speech.
The director's tone underlined how serious the issue was.
His facial expression clearly underlines his disappointment.

Synset Examples

Her gesture emphasized her words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emphasize - to give special importance or focus to something
  • Synonym: highlight - to make something stand out or attract attention
  • Antonym: to minimize - to reduce the importance of something

Etymology

From under + line, originally referring to drawing a line beneath text to show emphasis.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01016618-v
Synset POS: v
