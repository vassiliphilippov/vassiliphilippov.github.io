shrike

1. NOUN

/ ʃraɪk / (AmE), / ʃraɪk / (BrE)
a passerine bird found chiefly in the Old World, known for its strong hooked bill and habit of feeding on insects and small animals, sometimes impaling its prey on thorns.
a predatory songbird with a hooked beak

Examples

A shrike perched on the wire, scanning the field for movement.
The butcherbird is another name for the shrike because it impales its prey on thorns.
We spotted a shrike during our walk in the countryside.

Etymology

From Old English scrīc (shriek, thrush), related to the bird's harsh call.
