Paul Bunyan

1. PROPN

/ pɔl ˈbʌnjən / (AmE), / pɔːl ˈbʌnjən / (BrE)
a legendary giant lumberjack in North American folklore, especially associated with the northern United States and Canada
legendary giant lumberjack of folklore

Examples

The story of Paul Bunyan is popular among children in the Midwest.
Some say that Paul Bunyan and Babe created the Great Lakes with their footprints.
At the festival, a giant statue of Paul Bunyan stood at the entrance.

Synset Examples

Paul Bunyan had a blue ox named Babe
the lakes of Minnesota began when Paul Bunyan and Babe's footprints filled with water

Etymology

Folklore character originating in North American oral traditions from the late 19th to early 20th century.
