theologiser

1. NOUN

/ θiˈɑlədʒaɪzər / (AmE), / θiˈɒlədʒaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who is learned in theology or who theorizes about theological matters
person who speculates about theology
british

Examples

The conference was attended by every prominent theologiser in Europe.
As a young theologiser, she presented many fresh perspectives on old doctrines.
He began his career as a mere preacher but became known as a distinguished theologiser.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theologian - an expert in theology
  • Antonym: layman - a person without specialized knowledge in theology

Etymology

From theology (from Greek 'theologia') + -iser (British variant of -izer, from Greek 'izein').
