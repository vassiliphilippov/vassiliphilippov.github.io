vacant

1. ADJ

/ ˈveɪkənt / (AmE), / ˈveɪkənt / (BrE)
(of a position, seat, or place) not occupied; having no current occupant or incumbent.
not occupied; without a current occupant

Examples

The company announced that the manager's position was vacant after the resignation.
After months of searching, they finally filled the vacant seat on the council.
That house on the corner has been vacant for years.

Synset Examples

the throne is never vacant

Related Terms

  • Antonym: occupied - being used or held by someone

Etymology

From Latin 'vacans', present participle of 'vacare' (to be empty).

WordNet ID: oewn-01091666-s

2. ADJ

/ ˈveɪkənt / (AmE), / ˈveɪkənt / (BrE)
(of a person's expression or mind) showing no sign of intelligence, thought, or interest.
showing no thought or intelligence

Examples

She stared at the painting with a vacant expression on her face.
His vacant gaze made it clear he wasn't paying attention.
The actor was able to perfectly portray a vacant look of confusion.

Synset Examples

a vacuous mind
a vacant expression

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vacuous - showing a lack of thought or intelligence
  • Antonym: thoughtful - showing careful thought or consideration

Etymology

From Latin vacans, vacant-, present participle of vacare (to be empty).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02581841-s
Synset POS: s
