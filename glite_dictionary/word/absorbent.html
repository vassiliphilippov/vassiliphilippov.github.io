absorbent

1. ADJ

/ əbˈzɔrbənt, æbˈzɔrbənt / (AmE), / əbˈzɔːbənt, æbˈzɔːbənt / (BrE)
able to take in and hold liquid or other substances easily; capable of absorbing.
able to absorb liquid or substances

Examples

This paper towel is highly absorbent and perfect for cleaning up spills.
Cotton is an absorbent material often used in clothing and bandages.
These sponges are so absorbent that they soak up water instantly.

Synset Examples

as absorbent as a sponge

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonabsorbent - not able to absorb liquids or substances
  • Synonym: porous - having minute spaces or holes through which liquid or air may pass

Etymology

From Latin 'absorbent-' (swallowing up), from 'absorbere'.

2. NOUN

/ əbˈzɔrbənt, æbˈzɔrbənt / (AmE), / əbˈzɔːbənt, æbˈzɔːbənt / (BrE)
a material that is able to soak up liquids or gases easily
material that soaks up liquids or gases

Examples

Paper towels are a common absorbent found in most kitchens.
Activated charcoal acts as an absorbent for toxins in some medical treatments.
The laboratory technician used an absorbent to collect the spilled liquid.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adsorbent - a material that accumulates substances on its surface rather than absorbing them

Etymology

From Latin 'absorbens', present participle of 'absorbere' (to suck in).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
