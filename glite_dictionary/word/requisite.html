requisite

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛkwəzɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛkwɪzɪt / (BrE)
necessary or required for a particular purpose; essential.
necessary; required

Examples

Candidates must have the requisite experience for this job.
Please submit all requisite documents before the deadline.
He lacked the requisite skills to complete the project successfully.

Synset Examples

provided them with all things needful

Related Terms

  • Synonym: necessary - needed or essential for a purpose
  • Antonym: optional - not required or compulsory

Etymology

From Latin 'requisitus', past participle of 'requirere' (to seek, require).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01585183-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛkwəzɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛkwɪzɪt / (BrE)
a thing that is necessary or indispensable for achieving a particular purpose
something necessary or indispensable

Examples

A passport is a requisite for international travel.
Clean water is a basic requisite for healthy living.
The project could not succeed without the proper requisites.

Synset Examples

food and shelter are necessities of life
the essentials of the good life
allow farmers to buy their requirements under favorable conditions
a place where the requisites of water fuel and fodder can be obtained

Related Terms

  • Synonym: necessity - a thing that is essential or indispensable
  • Synonym: requirement - something that is needed or demanded
  • Antonym: luxury - a thing that is not essential but desirable

Etymology

From Latin 'requisitus' (required, needed), past participle of 'requirere' (to require).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09390100-n
Synset POS: n
