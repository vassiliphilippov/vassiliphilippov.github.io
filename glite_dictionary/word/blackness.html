blackness

1. NOUN

/ ˈblæknəs / (AmE), / ˈblaknəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being black; the degree to which something is black in color
the state or quality of being black in color

Examples

The blackness of the night made it hard to see anything.
She admired the blackness of the artist's ink against the white paper.
After the fire, the walls were covered in a deep blackness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: whiteness - the quality or state of being white

Etymology

From 'black' + suffix '-ness', denoting a state or quality.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblæknəs / (AmE), / ˈblaknɪs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being completely without light; the total absence of light
total absence of light

Examples

After the lights went out, the children sat in complete blackness until help arrived.
The blackness of the cave was overwhelming, making it impossible to see anything.
A thick fog descended, adding to the blackness of the night.

Synset Examples

they fumbled around in total darkness
in the black of night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: darkness - the partial or total absence of light

Etymology

From Middle English ‘blakenes’, from ‘blak’ (black) + ‘-ness’.
