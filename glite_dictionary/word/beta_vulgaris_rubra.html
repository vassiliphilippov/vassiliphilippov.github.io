Beta vulgaris rubra

1. NOUN

/ ˌbeɪtə vʌlˈɡɛrɪs ˈruːbrə / (AmE), / ˌbiːtə vʌlˈɡɛːrɪs ˈruːbrə / (BrE)
A beet with a large, swollen red root, commonly grown for human consumption.
red beet with edible root

Examples

Beta vulgaris rubra is commonly used in salads and soups due to its sweet flavor.
Farmers cultivate Beta vulgaris rubra for both fresh market sales and processing into pickles.
Research has shown that Beta vulgaris rubra is rich in vitamins and minerals beneficial to health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beetroot - the edible reddish-purple root of a beet plant
  • Degree Higher: beet - general name for plants of species Beta vulgaris

Etymology

From Latin: 'Beta' (beet), 'vulgaris' (common), 'rubra' (red).
