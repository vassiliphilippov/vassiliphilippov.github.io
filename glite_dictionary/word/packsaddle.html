packsaddle

1. NOUN

/ ˈpækˌsædəl / (AmE), / ˈpækˌsædəl / (BrE)
a special saddle designed for pack animals, used to secure and support loads for transportation
saddle for carrying loads on animals

Examples

The mule wore a packsaddle loaded with supplies for the long journey.
It takes skill to properly secure bags on a packsaddle so they don't slip off.
Early explorers often relied on packsaddles to transport their equipment across rugged terrain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saddle - a seat fastened on an animal's back for riding

Etymology

From pack + saddle, combining 'pack' (to carry goods) and 'saddle'.
