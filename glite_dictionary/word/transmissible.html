transmissible

1. ADJ

/ trænˈmɪsəbl̩ / (AmE), / trænˈmɪsəbəl / (BrE)
(of a disease or condition) capable of being transmitted from one person or organism to another, especially by infection.
able to be transmitted, especially by infection

Examples

Measles is a highly transmissible viral disease, especially among children.
The researchers are studying how transmissible the new variant is.
Some illnesses are transmissible through direct contact, while others require a vector.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infectious - (of a disease) able to be passed from one person, animal, or organism to another
  • Synonym: contagious - (of a disease) spread by close contact between people
  • Antonym: noncommunicable - (of a disease) not capable of being passed from person to person

Etymology

From Latin 'transmissibilis', from 'transmittere' (to transmit).

2. ADJ

/ trænˈmɪsəbəl / (AmE), / trænzˈmɪsɪbəl / (BrE)
(of rights, property, or qualities) able to be inherited or passed on according to established or legal rules of descent.
able to be inherited or passed on

Examples

There are certain titles that are transmissible only through the male line.
The law specified which rights were transmissible to the next generation.
This ancient ritual is a transmissible tradition, handed down through families for centuries.

Synset Examples

ancestral home
ancestral lore
hereditary monarchy
patrimonial estate
transmissible tradition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heritable - able to be inherited, especially by legal right
  • Antonym: nontransferable - not able to be transferred or passed on to another person

Etymology

From Latin transmittere (to send across) via French transmissible.
