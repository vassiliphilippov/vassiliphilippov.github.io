adjunction

1. NOUN

/ æˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ædˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
the act of joining or adding something to another; the state of being joined or added.
the act of joining or adding

Examples

The adjunction of a new wing to the building increased its capacity.
In mathematics, the process of adjunction allows for combining different sets.
The document mentions the adjunction of two companies through a merger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: addition - the act of adding something
  • Antonym: separation - the act of moving things apart or disconnecting

Etymology

From Latin 'adjunctio', from 'adjungere' meaning 'to join to'.
