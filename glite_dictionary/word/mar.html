mar

1. VERB

/ mɑr / (AmE), / mɑː / (BrE)
to damage or spoil the perfection or appearance of something
to spoil or damage

Examples

The graffiti marred the walls of the historic building.
A loud crash marred the peaceful silence of the night.
He accidentally marred the table's surface while moving his keys.

Synset Examples

nothing marred her beauty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spoil - to diminish or destroy the value or quality of
  • Synonym: ruin - to cause great and often irreparable damage to
  • Antonym: repair - to restore something to a good condition

Etymology

From Old English *merran* or *merian* (to hinder, damage).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ mɑr / (AmE), / mɑː / (BrE)
to damage or spoil the appearance or quality of something; to injure or scar severely
damage or spoil severely

Examples

The graffiti marred the side of the historic building, spoiling its appearance.
A deep scratch marred the polished surface of the table.
Their argument marred what should have been a joyful evening.

Synset Examples

mutilated bodies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damage - to harm or spoil the condition of something
  • Synonym: ruin - to destroy or severely impair the quality of something
  • Antonym: repair - to restore something that has been marred or damaged

Etymology

From Old English *merran*, meaning 'to hinder or obstruct'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00090460-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ mɑːr / (AmE), / mɑː / (BrE)
a mark, blemish, or flaw that spoils the appearance of something, especially on a person's body
blemish or flaw on something

Examples

The antique vase had a small mar on its surface that reduced its value.
A single mar on her otherwise flawless complexion drew his attention.
Despite the mar on the painting, it was still considered a masterpiece.

Synset Examples

a facial blemish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blemish - a small mark or flaw that spoils the appearance of something
  • Synonym: scar - a mark left on the skin after an injury has healed

Etymology

From Old English *merran*, related to Old Norse *merja* (to bruise, mark).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04699505-n
Synset POS: n
