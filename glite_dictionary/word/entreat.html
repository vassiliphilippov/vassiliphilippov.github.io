to entreat

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈtriːt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtriːt / (BrE)
to ask or beg for something earnestly or urgently
ask or beg earnestly

Examples

She entreated him to stay a little longer, hoping he would change his mind.
The villagers entreated the mayor for more time to pay their taxes.
He is entreating his friends not to reveal the secret until tomorrow.

Synset Examples

The prophet bid all people to become good persons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to beg - to ask someone earnestly for something
  • Synonym: to implore - to beg someone earnestly or desperately to do something
  • Antonym: demand - to ask for something forcefully, not earnestly

Etymology

From Old French 'entraiter', from Latin 'intricare' (to entangle, perplex), later influenced by English 'treat'.
