Village

1. PROPN

/ ˈvɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈvɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
a residential district in Manhattan, New York, also known as Greenwich Village, noted for its cultural and artistic history
residential Manhattan district (Greenwich Village)

Examples

During the 1960s, the Village was a center for countercultural movements and artistic experimentation.
Many famous musicians began their careers performing in coffeehouses in the Village.
Renting an apartment in the Village has become increasingly expensive over the years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Greenwich Village - the common full name for the Village district in Manhattan
