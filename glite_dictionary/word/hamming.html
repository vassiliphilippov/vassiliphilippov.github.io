hamming

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhæmɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of performing in an exaggerated or overdramatic manner, especially in acting
overacting or exaggerated acting

Examples

The critic dismissed the play for its endless hamming and lack of subtlety.
Some actors have a tendency for hamming when they're nervous.
Her hamming during the audition made the role seem less believable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overacting - performing with exaggerated emotion or expression
  • Antonym: underacting - performing with too little emotion or expression

Etymology

From 'ham' (slang for an overacting performer), with -ing to form the gerund/participle.
