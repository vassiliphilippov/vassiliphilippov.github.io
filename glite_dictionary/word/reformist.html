reformist

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈfɔrmɪst / (AmE), / rɪˈfɔːmɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or advocates reform, especially in political or social systems
person who advocates reform

Examples

The new government appointed a prominent reformist as Minister of Education.
As a known reformist, she often clashed with conservative members of the board.
During the election, the reformist candidate promised sweeping changes to the tax system.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conservative - a person who is averse to change and holds traditional values
  • Degree Higher: radical - a person who advocates thorough or complete political or social reform
  • Degree Lower: moderate - a person who holds moderate views, especially in politics

Etymology

From reform + -ist, patterned after other agent nouns.

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈfɔrmɪst / (AmE), / rɪˈfɔːmɪst / (BrE)
Favouring or promoting reform, especially through changes in laws or government policies.
supporting reform or change

Examples

Many reformist politicians push for changes to improve social welfare systems.
Her reformist ideas challenged the traditional leadership of the party.
The government's reformist agenda met with both support and resistance from voters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: progressive - favoring or promoting progressive change or reform
  • Antonym: conservative - resistant to change or reform; favoring tradition

Etymology

From 'reform' + '-ist' (denoting a person who practices or is concerned with something).
