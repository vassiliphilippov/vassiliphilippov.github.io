Charadrius melodus

1. NOUN

/ kəˈreɪdriəs məˈloʊdəs / (AmE), / kəˈreɪdrɪəs mɪˈləʊdəs / (BrE)
A small plover native to eastern North America, commonly known as the piping plover, with pale plumage and a distinctive call.
species of small North American plover

Examples

The Charadrius melodus faces threats from habitat loss along Atlantic coastlines.
Conservation programs have been launched to protect nesting sites of the Charadrius melodus.
Researchers spent the summer banding Charadrius melodus chicks to monitor their movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piping plover - common name for Charadrius melodus, a small North American plover

Etymology

From the genus name Charadrius (a group of plovers) and species epithet melodus, Latin for 'melodious,' referring to its call.
