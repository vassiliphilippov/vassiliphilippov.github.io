faveolate

1. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪviəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈfeɪvɪəˌleɪt / (BrE)
having a surface marked by small, cell-like cavities, resembling a honeycomb.
pitted with tiny, honeycomb-like cavities

Examples

The faveolate structure of the coral adds to its unique appearance.
Under the microscope, the ceramic material showed a distinct faveolate texture.
Certain types of bone are described as faveolate due to their small, cavity-like spaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeycombed - having a structure resembling a honeycomb; full of small holes or cavities
  • Synonym: alveolate - having deep pits or cavities on the surface, like a honeycomb

Etymology

From Latin 'faveolatus', from 'faveola' (small honeycomb cell).
