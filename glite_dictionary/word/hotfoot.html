hotfoot

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑtˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈhɒtˌfʊt / (BrE)
A practical joke in which a match is secretly inserted between the sole and upper of someone's shoe and then lit.
practical joke with a lit match in shoe

Examples

At summer camp, someone played a hotfoot on my brother while he was sleeping.
The mischievous boys planned to give their teacher a hotfoot as a prank.
He laughed as he recounted the time he fell victim to a classic hotfoot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prank - a playful trick or practical joke

2. ADV

/ ˈhɑtˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈhɒtˌfʊt / (BrE)
without delay; quickly and directly
quickly; without delay

Examples

She left the meeting hotfoot to catch her train.
We traveled hotfoot to the emergency room as soon as we heard the news.
He went hotfoot down the stairs when he smelled smoke.

Synset Examples

sent ambassadors hotfoot to the Turks
drove hotfoot for Boston

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hurriedly - in a quick or rushed manner
  • Antonym: slowly - at a low speed; not quickly

Etymology

From 'hot' + 'foot', originally referring to running with burning or painful feet, later figuratively meaning moving quickly.

3. VERB

/ ˈhɑtˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈhɒtˌfʊt / (BrE)
to move quickly or hurriedly, often by running or walking fast
move hurriedly, often by running

Examples

She hotfooted it to the store when she realized she was late.
The reporter hotfooted across the festival grounds to catch the interview.
We saw him hotfooting down the street after the last bus.

Synset Examples

He rushed down the hall to receive his guests
The cars raced down the street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush - move with urgent haste
  • Antonym: to dawdle - move slowly or waste time

Etymology

Originally from the phrase "hot foot," referring to moving as if one's foot were hot; dates from the late 19th century.
