though

1. ADV

/ ðoʊ / (AmE), / ðəʊ / (BrE)
Used as an adverb, especially at the end of a sentence, to mean 'however' or 'nevertheless'.
adverb meaning 'however' or 'nevertheless'

Examples

He was tired; he kept working, though.
The weather’s nice. It might rain later, though.
It’s expensive. I think it’s worth it, though.

Synset Examples

it might be unpleasant, though

Related Terms

  • Synonym: however - on the other hand; nevertheless
  • Synonym: although - used to introduce a contrast; even though

Etymology

From Old English 'þēah', related to Dutch 'doch' and German 'doch'.
