Avicennia officinalis

1. NOUN

/ ˌævɪˈsɛniə ˌɑfɪsɪˈnælɪs / (AmE), / ˌævɪˈsiːniə ˌɒfɪsɪˈneɪlɪs / (BrE)
a small to medium-sized mangrove tree found in brackish water, especially along the shores of the southwestern Pacific region
a species of mangrove tree

Examples

The extensive mangrove forests are dominated by Avicennia officinalis and support diverse wildlife.
Researchers studied the salt tolerance mechanisms in Avicennia officinalis along the estuarine coast.
Local communities rely on Avicennia officinalis for firewood and traditional medicine.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Avicennia honors Persian physician Avicenna; officinalis denotes medicinal use.
