Kogia breviceps

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊdʒiə brɛˈvɪsɛps / (AmE), / ˈkəʊdʒiə brɛˈvɪsɛps / (BrE)
A species of small sperm whale found in warm waters worldwide, especially along the coasts of North America, known as the pygmy sperm whale.
species of small sperm whale

Examples

Marine biologists recently spotted a Kogia breviceps off the California coast.
The Kogia breviceps is distinguished by its short, blunt head and small size compared to other whales.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitats of Kogia breviceps worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pygmy sperm whale - Common name for Kogia breviceps

Etymology

From Latin: 'Kogia' is the genus name; 'breviceps' means 'short-headed' (from Latin brevis 'short' + -ceps 'headed').
