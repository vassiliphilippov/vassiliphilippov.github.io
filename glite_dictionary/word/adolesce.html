to adolesce

1. VERB

/ ˌædəˈlɛs / (AmE), / ˌadəˈlɛs / (BrE)
to undergo the process of becoming an adolescent; to pass through adolescence
to become an adolescent

Examples

The children are starting to adolesce as they approach their teenage years.
Many physical and emotional changes occur when young people adolesce.
Researchers study how children adolesce in different cultural environments.

Synset Examples

The children are beginning to adolesce around the age of 12

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: mature - to become fully developed or grown
  • Degree Lower: childhood - the state of being a child, before adolescence

Etymology

From Latin adolescent-, present participle stem of adolescere (to grow up).
