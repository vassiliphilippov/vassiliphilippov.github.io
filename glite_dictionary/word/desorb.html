to desorb

1. VERB

/ dɪˈsɔrb / (AmE), / dɪˈzɔːb / (BrE)
(of a substance) to be released from or cause to be released from a surface to which it was previously adsorbed.
release from a surface (chemistry/physics)
medical

Examples

The gas molecules desorb from the activated carbon filter at higher temperatures.
The sample was heated to allow the pollutant to desorb from the surface.
Scientists watched as the chemicals desorbed during the experimental procedure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to adsorb - to collect and hold molecules on a surface

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'de-' (away from) + 'absorb'.

2. VERB

/ dɪˈsɔrb / (AmE), / dɪˈzɔːb / (BrE)
to release (a substance) from a surface on which it was previously adsorbed
release from an adsorbed surface

Examples

When heated, the carbon filter can desorb the captured chemicals into the air.
The laboratory process requires the catalyst to desorb the product molecules efficiently.
Some gases will desorb from the soil when the temperature increases.

Synset Examples

the substance was desorbed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to adsorb - to collect and hold (a substance) on a surface

Etymology

From 'de-' (removal) and 'absorb' (from Latin 'sorbere', to suck in).
