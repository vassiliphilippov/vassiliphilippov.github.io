intermission

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntɚˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
a short period of time between parts of a play, concert, or other performance during which people can rest or leave the room
break during a performance

Examples

The audience rushed to the lobby during the intermission to buy refreshments.
There was a brief intermission between the two acts of the play.
Please return to your seats when the intermission ends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interval - a pause or period between events
  • Synonym: pause - a short stop in an activity

Etymology

From Latin 'intermissio' (a leaving off, interruption), from 'intermittere' (to leave off, interrupt).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪntɚˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
a pause or temporary break in an activity or event, especially in a performance or meeting
a pause or break in activity

Examples

The play will continue after a brief intermission.
We took a short intermission during our meeting to grab some coffee.
The concert had an intermission halfway through so the audience could stretch their legs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pause - a temporary stop in an activity or speech
  • Antonym: resumption - the act of beginning again after a pause

Etymology

From Latin 'intermissio' (a break, interruption), from 'intermittere' (to leave off, discontinue).
