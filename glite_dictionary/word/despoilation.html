despoilation

1. NOUN

/ dɛˌspɔɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / dɛˌspɔɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of violently stripping or robbing someone or something; plundering or spoliation
the act of violent stripping or robbing

Examples

The despoilation of the ancient city left it in ruins for centuries.
Protesters condemned the despoilation of natural resources by illegal logging.
Historical records detail the despoilation of art treasures during the occupation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plunder - the act of stealing goods by force, especially in war
  • Synonym: spoliation - the act of spoiling or robbing, especially in legal or historical contexts

Etymology

From Latin 'despoliare' (to strip, rob), via Old French 'despoiller'.
