overuse

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈjus / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈjuːz / (BrE)
to use something too often or too much, often to the point of causing harm or reducing effectiveness
use something too much or too often

Examples

You shouldn't overuse antibiotics, as it can make them less effective.
If you overuse certain phrases in your writing, it may become repetitive and dull.
Many athletes overuse specific muscles, leading to injury.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: underuse - to use something insufficiently

Etymology

Formed from the prefix over- (excessively) + use.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01164011-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərˈjus / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈjuːs / (BrE)
the act or process of using something too much, especially to the point where it becomes less effective or causes harm
the excessive use of something

Examples

Physical overuse of joints can lead to chronic injuries.
The overuse of antibiotics has contributed to drug-resistant bacteria.
Teachers warn against the overuse of smartphones in the classroom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misuse - using something in the wrong way or for the wrong purpose
  • Synonym: abuse - improper or harmful use

Etymology

From over- (excessive) + use, first recorded in the late 19th century.

Synset POS: n
