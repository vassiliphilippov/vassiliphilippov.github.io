Richmondena cardinalis

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɪtʃ.mənˈdiː.nə ˌkɑːr.dɪˈneɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / ˌrɪtʃ.mənˈdiː.nə ˌkɑː.dɪˈneɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
A North American finch species with a distinctive crest and a thick bill, where the male has bright red plumage; commonly known as the northern cardinal.
North American finch (northern cardinal)

Examples

The Richmondena cardinalis is often seen at bird feeders throughout eastern North America.
Male Richmondena cardinalis are easily recognized by their brilliant red coloration and prominent crest.
Many bird watchers hope to spot the distinctive Richmondena cardinalis during the spring migration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: northern cardinal - popular name for Richmondena cardinalis, a North American songbird

Etymology

From the former genus name Richmondena (honoring Charles Wallace Richmond) and cardinalis, Latin for 'cardinal'.
