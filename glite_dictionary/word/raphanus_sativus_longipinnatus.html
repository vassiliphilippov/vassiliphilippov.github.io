Raphanus sativus longipinnatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌræf.ə.nəs səˈtɪv.əs ˌlɒn.dʒɪˈpɪn.ə.təs / (AmE), / ˌræf.ənəs səˈtɪv.əs ˌlɒn.dɪˈpɪn.ətəs / (BrE)
a variety of radish native to East Asia, known for its long, white, hard root that is eaten raw or cooked
East Asian radish with long white root

Examples

Many Japanese dishes feature Raphanus sativus longipinnatus as a crunchy, flavorful ingredient.
The chef sliced some Raphanus sativus longipinnatus to garnish the salad with its refreshing taste.
Farmers in Asia often cultivate Raphanus sativus longipinnatus for both its culinary and health benefits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daikon - white East Asian radish, commonly known as daikon
  • Degree Higher: radish - edible root vegetable of the mustard family

Etymology

From Latin: 'Raphanus' (radish), 'sativus' (cultivated), 'longipinnatus' (long-pinnate, referring to leaf shape).
