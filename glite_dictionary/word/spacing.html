spacing

1. NOUN

/ ˈspeɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspeɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
the interval of time between occurrences of a repeating event
interval between repeating events

Examples

The spacing between the two meetings was exactly one week.
Proper spacing of medication doses is important for effective treatment.
Teachers recommend regular spacing of study sessions to improve memory retention.

Synset Examples

some women do not control the spacing of their children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interval - a period of time between events

Etymology

From 'space' + '-ing'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-15307752-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈspeɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspeɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
the arrangement or amount of space between objects, lines, or elements in a group
arrangement of space between things

Examples

The designer adjusted the spacing between the letters to make the text easier to read.
Good spacing between the plants allows them to grow healthily.
Please check the spacing of the items on the shelf so they are evenly distributed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gap - a break or space between objects; something missing
  • Antonym: grouping - arrangement of objects into groups

Etymology

From 'space' + '-ing', indicating the act or result of creating space.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05090535-n
Synset POS: n
