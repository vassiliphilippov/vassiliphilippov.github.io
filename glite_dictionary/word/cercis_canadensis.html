Cercis canadensis

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜrsɪs ˌkænəˈdɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɜːsɪs ˌkanəˈdɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a small deciduous tree native to eastern North America, with pink flowers, commonly known as the eastern redbud
eastern North American redbud tree

Examples

The Cercis canadensis is often planted in gardens for its vibrant spring blossoms.
In early spring, the branches of Cercis canadensis are covered with clusters of pink flowers.
Wildlife such as bees and butterflies are attracted to the Cercis canadensis during its flowering season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redbud - deciduous tree with rosy-pink flowers, especially those of the genus Cercis

Etymology

From Latin genus name *Cercis* and species epithet *canadensis*, meaning 'of Canada'.
