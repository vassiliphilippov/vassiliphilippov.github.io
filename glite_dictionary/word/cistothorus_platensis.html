Cistothorus platensis

1. NOUN

/ sɪs.təˈθɔːr.əs pləˈtɛn.sɪs / (AmE), / sɪstəˈθɔːrəs plæˈtɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A small American wren species that inhabits wet, sedgy meadows, commonly known as the sedge wren.
a small American wren (sedge wren)

Examples

Scientists observed the Cistothorus platensis nesting in the marshlands during their spring survey.
Wet meadows provide essential habitat for the Cistothorus platensis throughout much of North America.
Birdwatchers hope to spot a Cistothorus platensis during their early morning excursions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sedge wren - a small North American wren found in wet meadows; common name for Cistothorus platensis

Etymology

From Latin: 'Cistothorus' (genus of small wrens), 'platensis' (from the Río de la Plata region).
