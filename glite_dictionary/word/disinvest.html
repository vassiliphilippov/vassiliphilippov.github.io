to disinvest

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪsɪnˈvɛst / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈvɛst / (BrE)
to withdraw or reduce investment in a business, asset, or country, often by selling shares or assets
withdraw investment; sell assets

Examples

The university voted to disinvest from companies involved in fossil fuels.
Several shareholders chose to disinvest their stakes during the financial downturn.
Activists urged the city council to disinvest in corporations tied to deforestation.

Synset Examples

The company decided to divest
the board of trustees divested $20 million in real estate property
There was pressure on the university to disinvest in South Africa

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to invest - to put money into something in order to earn a profit
  • Synonym: to divest - to sell off assets, especially for ethical reasons

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'dis-' (opposite of) and 'invest' (to put money into something); first used in the 20th century.

2. VERB

/ dɪsɪnˈvɛst / (AmE), / dɪsɪnˈvɛst / (BrE)
to withdraw or reduce financial investment from a business, asset, or sector
withdraw financial investment

Examples

Following the new regulations, the company chose to disinvest from the fossil fuel sector.
Some pension funds are pressured to disinvest in companies that do not meet environmental standards.
After a detailed review, they decided to disinvest their money from underperforming stocks.

Synset Examples

They decided to disinvest their resources in outdated technology.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to invest - to put money into financial schemes or assets for profit

Etymology

Formed from the prefix dis- (expressing reversal) + invest.
