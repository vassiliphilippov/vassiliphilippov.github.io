sabbat

1. NOUN

/ səˈbæt / (AmE), / səˈbæt / (BrE)
(in folklore) a meeting of witches held at night to practice witchcraft and sorcery, often described as a demonic gathering or orgy, especially in stories from the Middle Ages.
meeting of witches for witchcraft rites

Examples

The villagers feared that a sabbat was being held deep in the forest.
During the witch trials, many confessed to attending a sabbat under duress.
Legends describe the sabbat as a wild gathering of witches and demons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witches' sabbath - a similar term for a gathering of witches for rituals

Etymology

From French 'sabbat', originally from Hebrew 'shabbat' (Sabbath), referring to the supposed gathering of witches.
