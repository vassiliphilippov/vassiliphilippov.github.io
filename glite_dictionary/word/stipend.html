stipend

1. NOUN

/ staɪ.pɛnd / (AmE), / ˈstʌɪ.pɛnd / (BrE)
a fixed regular sum paid, often for services or to cover living expenses, typically for scholars, clergy, or interns.
regular fixed payment, often for work or expenses

Examples

The graduate students receive a modest stipend to assist with their living expenses.
Each intern will be given a monthly stipend as compensation for their work.
She relied on her church stipend to support her during the year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salary - fixed regular payment, typically paid monthly by an employer
  • Synonym: wage - payment, usually hourly or weekly, for work done

Etymology

From Latin 'stipendium' (soldier's pay), from 'stips' (wages, contribution) + 'pendere' (to pay).
