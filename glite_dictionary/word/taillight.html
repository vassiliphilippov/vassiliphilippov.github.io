taillight

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪlˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈteɪlˌlaɪt / (BrE)
A red safety lamp mounted at the rear of a motor vehicle, used to make the vehicle visible at night or in poor visibility.
rear safety lamp on a vehicle

Examples

The car's taillight was broken after the accident, making it harder to see at night.
Please check if the taillight is working before you start driving in the dark.
When he pressed the brakes, the taillight and brake light both illuminated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brake light - a light at the rear of a vehicle that illuminates when the brakes are applied

Etymology

From 'tail' + 'light', indicating a light at the rear end of a vehicle.
