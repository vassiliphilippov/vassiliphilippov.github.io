infidelity

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnfəˈdɛləti / (AmE), / ˌɪnfɪˈdɛlɪti / (BrE)
the act or state of being unfaithful to a spouse or partner; unfaithfulness in a relationship or obligation
the act of being unfaithful to a partner

Examples

The marriage ended after years of infidelity on both sides.
Trust was broken when his infidelity was discovered.
Rumors of infidelity can damage even the strongest relationships.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fidelity - faithfulness to a person, cause, or belief

Etymology

From Latin 'infidelitas', from 'in-' (not) + 'fidelitas' (faithfulness).
