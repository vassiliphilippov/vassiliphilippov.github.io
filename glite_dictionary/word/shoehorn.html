shoehorn

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃuˌhɔrn / (AmE), / ˈʃuːˌhɔːn / (BrE)
A curved device used to help slide the heel into a shoe.
curved tool for putting on shoes

Examples

He used a shoehorn to slide his foot easily into the tight new shoes.
My grandfather always keeps a shoehorn by the door for his leather boots.
Without a shoehorn, it was nearly impossible to get into those narrow shoes.

Etymology

From 'shoe' + 'horn', originally made from animal horn.

2. VERB

/ ˈʃuˌhɔrn / (AmE), / ˈʃuːˌhɔːn / (BrE)
to force or squeeze someone or something into a place, position, or situation where they do not easily fit or belong
force or squeeze into unsuitable place

Examples

He managed to shoehorn an extra guest into the crowded dining room.
The director tried to shoehorn a love story into the action movie.
She shoehorned her belongings into the small suitcase.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squeeze - to fit something tightly into a space
  • Synonym: wedge - to force into a narrow or tight space
  • Antonym: to accommodate - to provide sufficient space for

Etymology

From 'shoehorn', originally a tool for putting on a shoe, later used metaphorically as a verb.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
