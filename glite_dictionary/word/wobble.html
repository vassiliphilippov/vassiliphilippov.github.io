wobble

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑbəl / (AmE), / ˈwɒbəl / (BrE)
an unsteady or rocking movement back and forth or from side to side
an unsteady rocking movement

Examples

There was a noticeable wobble in the table after it lost a leg.
Her bike developed a wobble when she hit the gravel road.
The plane's wings had a slight wobble during the turbulence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rock - a rocking or oscillating movement
  • Antonym: stability - the state of being steady or stable
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈwɑːbəl / (AmE), / ˈwɒbəl / (BrE)
to move or cause to move unsteadily from side to side
move unsteadily; shake or sway

Examples

The table began to wobble when I leaned on it.
She tried to balance on the beam, but her legs continued to wobble.
The child's first steps caused his knees to wobble slightly.

Synset Examples

His knees wobbled
The old cart wobbled down the street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shake - to move with quick, short movements up and down or from side to side
  • Antonym: steady - to make or become firm, stable, or fixed

Etymology

Probably of Low German origin; related to Low German 'wabeln' (to shake, totter).

3. VERB

/ ˈwɑbəl / (AmE), / ˈwɒbəl / (BrE)
to move unsteadily from side to side, or to shake with such movement
move unsteadily from side to side

Examples

The glass began to wobble on the edge of the table before falling.
He tried to ride the bike, but the wheels started to wobble dangerously.
After spinning too fast, the top would wobble and eventually tip over.

Synset Examples

The ship careened out of control

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teeter - to move unsteadily, as if about to fall
  • Antonym: to stabilize - make or become stable
