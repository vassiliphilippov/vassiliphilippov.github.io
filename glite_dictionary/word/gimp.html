gimp

1. NOUN

/ ɡɪmp / (AmE), / ɡɪmp / (BrE)
A difficulty or disability in walking, typically due to injury or impairment of the legs or feet; a limp.
disability affecting walking; limp

Examples

After the accident, he walked with a noticeable gimp that never fully went away.
Her gimp made long walks difficult, but she refused to let it slow her down.
You could see the gimp in his step as he crossed the crowded street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limp - a tendency to walk with difficulty, typically due to injury
  • Degree Higher: stagger - to walk or move unsteadily, as if about to fall

2. VERB

/ ɡɪmp / (AmE), / ɡɪmp / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly North American) To walk with difficulty, typically because of a physical injury or limitation; to limp or hobble.
to walk with a limp or hobble
informal american

Examples

After twisting his ankle, he had to gimp all the way home from the gym.
The runner managed to gimp across the finish line despite her injury.
She gimped down the hallway, wincing with every step.

Synset Examples

The old woman hobbles down to the store every day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limp - walk with difficulty, especially because of a damaged leg or foot
  • Synonym: hobble - to walk in an awkward way, usually because of injury
