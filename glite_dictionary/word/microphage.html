microphage

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪkroʊˌfeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmʌɪkrə(ʊ)ˌfeɪdʒ / (BrE)
a white blood cell, such as a neutrophil, that engulfs and digests small particles or bacteria
a type of white blood cell
medical

Examples

The microphage promptly surrounded and ingested the invading bacteria.
Under the microscope, the activity of the microphage is clearly observable.
An infection triggers the body's microphage response as part of the immune defense.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: macrophage - a large white blood cell that engulfs and digests larger particles

Etymology

From micro- (small) and Greek phagein (to eat).
