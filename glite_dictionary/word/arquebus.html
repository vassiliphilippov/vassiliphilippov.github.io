arquebus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrkəˌbʌs / (AmE), / ˈɑːkɪbəs / (BrE)
an early type of portable firearm with a long barrel, used from the 15th to 17th centuries, operated by a matchlock or similar mechanism
early firearm with a long barrel
archaic

Examples

The soldiers loaded their arquebus and took aim at the charging cavalry.
During the reenactment, he demonstrated how to fire an arquebus from the shoulder.
The arquebus played a significant role in early modern European warfare.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: musket - a later, more advanced long-barreled firearm

Etymology

From Old French 'harquebuse', from Dutch 'haakbus' (hook-gun), from 'haak' (hook) + 'bus' (gun, tube).
