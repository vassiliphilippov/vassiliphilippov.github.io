sawn-off

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɔn ˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈsɔːn ˌɒf / (BrE)
(of a person) very short; well below average height, especially in a stocky or stunted way.
very short in stature (person)
informal

Examples

He was a sawn-off goalkeeper but made up for it with agility.
The sawn-off kid stood his ground against taller classmates.
They always joked about Sam being a sawn-off dynamo on the football pitch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: short - of little height; not tall

Etymology

Figurative use based on 'sawn off', likening short stature to something cut short with a saw.
