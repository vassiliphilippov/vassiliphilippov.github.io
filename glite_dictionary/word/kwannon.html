Kwannon

1. PROPN

/ ˈkwɑːn ən / (AmE), / ˈkwæn ən / (BrE)
The Japanese name for the Buddhist bodhisattva of compassion, corresponding to the Chinese Kuan Yin.
Japanese Buddhist goddess of mercy

Examples

The temple is dedicated to Kwannon, the goddess of mercy in Japanese Buddhism.
Many statues of Kwannon have a serene, compassionate expression.
In Japanese art, Kwannon is often depicted holding a lotus flower.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kuan Yin - Chinese bodhisattva of compassion in Buddhism

Etymology

From Japanese 観音 (Kannon), adapted in English as Kwannon, corresponding to Chinese Guanyin (Kuan Yin).
