Mantispidae

1. NOUN

/ mænˈtɪspɪˌdiː / (AmE), / manˈtɪspɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of predatory insects known as mantidflies, characterized by raptorial front legs and a superficial resemblance to mantises.
family of insects known as mantidflies

Examples

The Mantispidae are easily recognized by their mantis-like forelegs and delicate wings.
Many entomologists study Mantispidae to learn about predatory behaviors among insect families.
A specimen of Mantispidae was found in the rainforest, perched on a leaf.

Etymology

From modern Latin Mantispidae, from the genus Mantispa (a type of mantidfly), influenced by the form of mantis (praying mantis).

2. NOUN

/ mænˈtɪspɪˌdiː / (AmE), / mænˈtɪspɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of insects, known as mantispids, characterized by raptorial forelegs and a resemblance to mantises, belonging to the order Neuroptera
family of mantispid insects

Examples

The Mantispidae are often mistaken for praying mantises due to their striking forelegs.
Researchers have discovered new species within the Mantispidae family in tropical rainforests.
Characteristics of Mantispidae include transparent wings and a predatory lifestyle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mantispid - an insect of the family Mantispidae

Etymology

From Mantisp- (type genus Mantispa) + Latin -idae (denoting animal families).
