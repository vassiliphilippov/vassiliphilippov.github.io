snowplough

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnoʊˌplaʊ / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊˌplaʊ / (BrE)
a vehicle or device for clearing snow from roads, usually equipped with a broad blade in front
vehicle for clearing snow
british

Examples

The snowplough cleared the mountain pass early in the morning.
After the snowstorm, a snowplough worked through the night to keep the roads open.
Drivers waited patiently as the snowplough moved slowly ahead of them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snowplow - American English term for a vehicle used to clear snow

Etymology

From 'snow' + 'plough', meaning to plough or clear snow.
