apparition

1. NOUN

/ ˌæpəˈrɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌapəˈrɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act of appearing or becoming visible, especially in a sudden or unexpected way
an unexpected appearance

Examples

The sudden apparition of a deer startled the hikers on the quiet trail.
The villagers spoke for years about the apparition that appeared at dusk near the old mill.
The magician's trick relied on the apparition of objects seemingly out of nowhere.

Synset Examples

natives were amazed at the apparition of this white stranger

Etymology

From Latin 'apparitio(n-)' meaning 'appearance', from 'apparere' (to appear).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæpəˈɹɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌapəˈɾɪʃən / (BrE)
a supernatural or ghostly figure that appears unexpectedly, often imagined or thought to be real
appearance of a ghost or spirit

Examples

Late at night, he claimed to see an apparition in the hallway.
The sudden apparition of the old woman startled everyone in the room.
Some people believe that an apparition foretells important events.

Synset Examples

I was recalled to the present by the apparition of a frightening specter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ghost - the spirit of a dead person that is believed to appear to the living
  • Synonym: phantom - something that has appearance but no reality; a ghost
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

From Latin apparitio (appearance), from apparere (to appear).

ghostly or supernatural figure

Examples

  • Synonym: phantom - something seen or imagined but having no physical reality

4. NOUN

/ ˌæpəˈrɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌæpəˈrɪʃən / (BrE)
a ghost or ghostlike figure that suddenly appears, especially one that is unexpected or startling
ghostly or supernatural figure

Examples

The children claimed to see an apparition in the old, abandoned house.
He awoke in the night and thought he saw an apparition standing at the foot of his bed.
The sudden apparition of a robed figure startled everyone in the church.

Synset Examples

we were unprepared for the apparition that confronted us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phantom - a ghost or illusionary figure
  • Synonym: ghost - the spirit of a dead person, often appearing to the living
  • Synonym: specter - a ghost; something widely feared as a possible unpleasant occurrence

Etymology

From Latin 'apparitio' (appearance), from 'apparere' (to appear).
