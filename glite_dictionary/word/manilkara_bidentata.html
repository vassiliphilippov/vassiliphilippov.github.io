Manilkara bidentata

1. NOUN

/ ˌmænɪlˈkɑːrə ˌbaɪdɛnˈteɪtə / (AmE), / ˌmænɪlˈkɑːrə ˌbaɪdɛnˈtɑːtə / (BrE)
A tropical hardwood tree native to the Americas, known for producing balata gum and heavy red timber.
tropical tree yielding balata gum and red timber

Examples

The dense wood of the Manilkara bidentata tree is highly valued in construction.
In some regions, the gum extracted from Manilkara bidentata is used as a natural latex alternative.
Researchers study Manilkara bidentata for its ecological role in tropical forests.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining the genus Manilkara and species name bidentata (Latin: 'two-toothed').
