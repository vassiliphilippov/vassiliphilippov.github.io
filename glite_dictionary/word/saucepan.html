saucepan

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔsˌpæn / (AmE), / ˈsɔːsˌpæn / (BrE)
A deep pan with a long handle, used for boiling or stewing foods on the stove.
deep cooking pan with a handle

Examples

She filled the saucepan with water and placed it on the stove to boil.
Please stir the soup in the saucepan so it doesn't burn.
He used a small saucepan to melt the butter for the recipe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pot - a deep round container used for cooking
  • Antonym: frying pan - a shallow pan with a long handle, used for frying foods

Etymology

From French 'sauce' (sauce) + English 'pan' (container for cooking), originally denoting a pan for making sauces.
