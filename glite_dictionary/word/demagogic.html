demagogic

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɛməˈɡɑdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌdiːməˈɡɒdʒɪk / (BrE)
typical of a demagogue; appealing to emotions, prejudices, or popular desires rather than rational argument
characteristic of a demagogue

Examples

The candidate's demagogic speeches stirred up strong emotions among the crowd.
Critics accused the leader of adopting demagogic tactics to win support.
Instead of addressing real issues, the debate was filled with demagogic rhetoric.

Synset Examples

demagogic speeches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demagogue - a political leader who seeks support by appealing to popular desires and prejudices rather than rational argument

Etymology

From 'demagogue' + '-ic', ultimately from Greek 'dēmagōgos' (leader of the people).
