cyberwar

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪbərˌwɔr / (AmE), / ˈsaɪbəˌwɔː / (BrE)
the use of digital attacks by a nation or organization to disrupt or damage another's computer systems or networks, typically as a form of warfare
digital warfare; attack on computer networks

Examples

The government increased its defenses against cyberwar after several attacks on critical infrastructure.
Experts warn that cyberwar could become as common as traditional warfare in the future.
During the summit, leaders discussed how to prevent an escalation into cyberwar between rival nations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cyberwarfare - the engagement in cyberwar; warfare conducted in cyberspace

Etymology

Blend of 'cyber-' (from 'cybernetics') and 'war'.
