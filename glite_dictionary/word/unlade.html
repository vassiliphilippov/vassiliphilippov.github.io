to unlade

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈleɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈleɪd / (BrE)
to remove the load or cargo from (a container or vehicle)
remove a load from a vehicle or container

Examples

The workers began to unlade the ship as soon as it docked.
It took several hours to unlade all the boxes from the freight car.
Before entering customs, drivers must unlade their cargo for inspection.

Synset Examples

unload the truck
offload the van

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unload - to remove goods from a vehicle or container
  • Antonym: load - to put a load or cargo onto something

Etymology

From Old English ' unlǣdan', from 'un-' (opposite of) + 'lǣdan' (to load).
