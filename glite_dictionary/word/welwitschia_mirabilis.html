Welwitschia mirabilis

1. NOUN

/ wɛlˈwɪtʃiə mɪˈræbɪlɪs / (AmE), / wɛlˈwɪtʃɪə mɪˈrabɪlɪs / (BrE)
a rare, long-lived plant native to arid regions of southwestern Africa, with a thick trunk and two large, persistent, strap-shaped leaves, considered a living fossil
ancient plant of southwestern Africa

Examples

The Welwitschia mirabilis is renowned for its ability to survive in extreme desert environments for thousands of years.
Botanists consider Welwitschia mirabilis a living fossil due to its ancient lineage.
Visitors to Namibia are often amazed by the unusual appearance of Welwitschia mirabilis in the desert landscape.

Etymology

Named after Moritz Welwitsch, the Austrian botanist who discovered it, with 'mirabilis' from Latin meaning 'wonderful' or 'remarkable'.
