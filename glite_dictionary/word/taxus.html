Taxus

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæksəs / (AmE), / ˈtaksəs / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen coniferous trees and shrubs known as yews, native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
genus of yew trees and shrubs

Examples

The Taxus genus includes several species commonly used in landscaping.
Many parts of the Taxus plant are toxic if ingested by humans or animals.
Researchers study Taxus for its medical uses, especially in the development of cancer drugs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yew - common name for trees of the genus Taxus

Etymology

From Latin *taxus* (yew tree).

2. PROPN

/ ˈtæksəs / (AmE), / ˈtaksəs / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen trees and shrubs known as yews, having flat needle-like leaves and red berry-like fruits.
genus of yew trees and shrubs

Examples

Taxus is widely cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and hardy nature.
Several species in the Taxus genus are sources of the anti-cancer drug paclitaxel.
The English yew, or Taxus baccata, is common in churchyards across Britain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yew - an evergreen tree or shrub of the genus Taxus

Etymology

From Latin 'taxus' (yew).
