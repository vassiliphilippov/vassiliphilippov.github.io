retentiveness

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈtɛntɪvnəs / (AmE), / rɪˈtɛntɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being able to retain liquid or moisture
ability to retain liquid or moisture

Examples

The clay soil's retentiveness causes water to remain near the roots for a long time.
Gardeners prefer soil with low retentiveness to avoid waterlogged plants.
Sandy soil typically has poor retentiveness and dries out quickly after rain.

Synset Examples

moisture retentivity of soil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absorbency - the ability to soak up liquids easily

Etymology

From Latin retinere (to retain), via retentivus (having the power of holding fast) + -ness.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈtɛntɪvnəs / (AmE), / rɪˈtɛntɪvnəs / (BrE)
The quality or ability to retain or hold on to things, especially possessions or acquired items.
the quality of retaining possessions

Examples

Her retentiveness made her reluctant to throw away anything, even old newspapers.
The retentiveness of the collector's habit filled the house with memorabilia from decades past.
Some people are known for their retentiveness when it comes to cherished possessions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: forgetfulness - tendency to forget or fail to keep things

Etymology

From retentive + -ness; ultimately from Latin retentus (held back, retained).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04953577-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈtɛntɪvnəs / (AmE), / rɪˈtɛntɪvnɪs / (BrE)
The ability to retain and recall information or past experiences.
ability to retain and recall information

Examples

Her retentiveness made her an excellent student who rarely forgot any facts.
He envied the retentiveness of his colleague's memory during meetings.
A good level of retentiveness is especially useful when learning new languages.

Synset Examples

he had a good memory when he was younger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: memory - the ability to remember information, experiences, and people
  • Antonym: forgetfulness - the quality of being unable to remember things

Etymology

Formed from 'retentive' (from Latin retentivus, from retinere 'to retain') + suffix '-ness'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
