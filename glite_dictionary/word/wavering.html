wavering

1. ADJ

/ ˈweɪvərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈweɪvərɪŋ / (BrE)
uncertain, indecisive, or hesitant in purpose or action; not steady or firm
uncertain or indecisive

Examples

Her wavering voice revealed how unsure she felt about her decision.
He remained wavering in his commitment until the very last minute.
Despite his wavering thoughts, he finally made a choice.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decisive - having or showing the ability to make decisions quickly and effectively
  • Synonym: hesitant - unsure or slow in acting or speaking; lacking certainty

Etymology

Present participle of 'waver', from Middle English 'waveren' (to sway, totter).

2. NOUN

/ ˈweɪvərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈweɪvərɪŋ / (BrE)
A state of doubt or indecision in speech or action.
indecision; hesitation

Examples

Her wavering during the negotiations made others doubt her decision-making abilities.
The judge's wavering was evident as he paused before delivering the verdict.
Despite moments of wavering, she finally chose the path that felt right.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hesitation - the action of pausing or being uncertain before acting or speaking
  • Antonym: certainty - being completely confident or sure about something; the opposite of wavering

3. NOUN

/ ˈweɪvərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈweɪvərɪŋ / (BrE)
the quality of being unsteady, indecisive, or subject to change
state of being unsteady or indecisive

Examples

There was a clear wavering in his voice as he delivered the news.
The stock market's wavering made investors nervous all week.
Her wavering between two options delayed the decision for hours.

Synset Examples

he kept a record of price fluctuations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instability - the quality of being unstable
  • Antonym: steadiness - the quality of being steady or stable

Etymology

From the verb 'waver', which comes from Middle English 'waveren', meaning 'to move unsteadily'.
