gobble

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑbəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɒbəl / (BrE)
The characteristic clucking sound made by a male turkey (turkey cock).
the sound made by a male turkey

Examples

The loud gobble echoed through the farmyard at dawn.
During mating season, the tom's gobble can be heard from far away.
The child laughed when he heard the turkey's gobble at the petting zoo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gobble - to make the characteristic sound of a turkey
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07398727-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈɡɑːbəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɒbəl / (BrE)
to eat food quickly and often noisily, without proper chewing
eat quickly without chewing properly

Examples

The children gobbled their breakfast before running outside to play.
He was so hungry that he gobbled his lunch in just a few minutes.
Please don't gobble your food; try to eat more slowly.

Synset Examples

Don't bolt your food!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bolt - to eat food quickly, often without chewing properly
  • Synonym: to devour - to eat something quickly and eagerly
  • Antonym: nibble - to eat something by taking small bites

Etymology

Perhaps imitative of the sound of eating.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01176230-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈɡɑbəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɒbəl / (BrE)
(of a turkey) to make a loud gurgling or bubbling call, typically as part of its mating display.
make a gurgling call (of turkeys)

Examples

The male turkey began to gobble loudly at dawn, echoing across the farmyard.
We listened as the wild turkeys gobbled in the woods during mating season.
Every time someone approached the enclosure, the big tom would gobble in response.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cackle - emit a harsh, shrill cry (as a hen or goose)

Etymology

Imitative of the sound made by a turkey.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01060298-v
Synset POS: v
