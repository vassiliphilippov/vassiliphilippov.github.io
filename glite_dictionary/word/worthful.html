worthful

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɝːθ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈwɜːθ.fəl / (BrE)
having worth, merit, or value; valuable or deserving of esteem
having worth or value
archaic

Examples

The charity received a worthful donation that made a real difference to their cause.
Her years of worthful service earned her much respect in the community.
He hoped to live a worthful life that contributed to others' happiness.

Synset Examples

a valuable friend
a good and worthful man

Related Terms

  • Antonym: worthless - having no worth or value
  • Synonym: valuable - having great value or importance

Etymology

From 'worth' + '-ful', following the pattern of adjectives formed to mean 'full of worth'.
