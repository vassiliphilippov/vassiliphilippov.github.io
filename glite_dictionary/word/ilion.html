Ilion

1. PROPN

/ ˈɪliən / (AmE), / ˈɪliən / (BrE)
an ancient city in Asia Minor, also known as Troy, famed as the site of the Trojan War in Greek mythology.
ancient city, site of the Trojan War

Examples

Homer's epic poems recount the siege of Ilion by Greek forces.
Archaeologists have suggested that the site near Hisarlik may be the location of ancient Ilion.
The legend of Ilion has inspired countless works of literature and art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Troy - ancient city in Asia Minor, also known as Ilion

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἴλιον (Ílion), the Greek name for the city of Troy.
