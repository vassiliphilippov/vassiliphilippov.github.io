wrothful

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɔθ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈrɒθ.fəl / (BrE)
vehemently angry; full of wrath; incensed and condemnatory (archaic or literary)
vehemently angry; full of wrath (archaic)
archaic

Examples

The wrothful king banished those who disobeyed his command.
She delivered a wrothful speech that left everyone silent in the hall.
His wrothful gaze made the soldiers tremble with fear.

Synset Examples

they trembled before the wrathful queen
but wroth as he was, a short struggle ended in reconciliation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrathful - feeling or showing strong anger
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling anger

Etymology

Formed from 'wroth' (Old English *wrāth*, angry) and '-ful', meaning 'full of'.
