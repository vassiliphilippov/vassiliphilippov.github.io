Abul-Walid Mohammed ibn-Ahmad Ibn-Mohammed ibn-Roshd

1. PROPN

/ ˌæbəl wɑːˈliːd moʊˈhæməd ɪb ən ɑːˈmæd ɪb ən moʊˈhæməd ɪb ən ˈrɔːʃd / (AmE), / ˌæbjuːl wəˈliːd məʊˈhæməd ɪb ən ˈæhməd ɪb ən məʊˈhæməd ɪb ən ˈrɒʃd / (BrE)
(1126–1198) An influential Arabian philosopher, jurist, and physician born in Spain, renowned for his detailed commentaries on Aristotle that greatly influenced medieval European philosophy.
Arabian philosopher and commentator on Aristotle

Examples

The works of Abul-Walid Mohammed ibn-Ahmad Ibn-Mohammed ibn-Roshd influenced both Islamic and Christian philosophers.
Medieval scholars studied the commentaries written by Abul-Walid Mohammed ibn-Ahmad Ibn-Mohammed ibn-Roshd on Aristotle's philosophy.
Many historians regard Abul-Walid Mohammed ibn-Ahmad Ibn-Mohammed ibn-Roshd as a key bridge between ancient and modern thought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Averroes - Latinized name of Abul-Walid Mohammed ibn-Ahmad Ibn-Mohammed ibn-Roshd, Arabian philosopher
