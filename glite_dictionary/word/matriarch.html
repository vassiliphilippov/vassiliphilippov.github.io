matriarch

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ.tri.ɑrk / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.tri.ɑːk / (BrE)
a woman who is the head of a family or tribe
female head of a family or tribe

Examples

After her husband's death, the family looked to their matriarch for guidance and stability.
As the matriarch of the clan, she presided over all major decisions and celebrations.
The film explores the powerful role played by a strong matriarch in a traditional village.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: patriarch - male head of a family or tribe

Etymology

From Latin 'mater' (mother) + Greek 'archē' (rule).
