chorister

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːr.ɪstər / (AmE), / ˈkɒr.ɪstə / (BrE)
a person, especially a child, who sings in a choir, often in a church or cathedral
singer in a choir

Examples

The young chorister had a beautiful, clear voice that stood out during the performance.
Each chorister wore a white robe and sang in perfect harmony with the rest of the choir.
After years as a chorister, he eventually became the choir's director.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choir member - a person who belongs to and sings in a choir
  • Antonym: soloist - a singer who performs alone, not in a choir

Etymology

From Middle English chorister, from Anglo-Norman *choriste* (singer in a choir), from Medieval Latin *chorista*, from Greek *khoros* (chorus).
