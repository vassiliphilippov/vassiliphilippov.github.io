fortification

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɔrt̬əfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɔːtɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the art or science of designing, building, and maintaining military defenses or fortified structures to protect against attack
the art of building military defenses

Examples

The engineer specialized in the fortification of cities threatened by invasion.
Medieval fortification techniques involved building thick walls and deep moats.
She studied fortification in military school to better understand historic battles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defense - the action of protecting from harm or attack
  • Synonym: siegecraft - the art of conducting a siege

Etymology

From Latin 'fortificatio', from 'fortificare' (to strengthen).

2. NOUN

/ ˌfɔːrt̬əfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɔːtɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of adding an ingredient to food or drink to improve its nutritional value or strength, such as adding vitamins to food or alcohol to wine.
enrichment of food or drink by adding substance

Examples

The fortification of milk with vitamin D has helped prevent deficiencies in children.
Some wines are made stronger through the fortification process, resulting in products like port and sherry.
Cereal manufacturers often advertise the fortification of their products with essential vitamins and minerals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enrichment - the process of improving the quality or value of something, especially in food

Etymology

From Latin fortificatio (strengthening), from fortificare (to strengthen).

3. NOUN

/ ˌfɔrtɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɔːtɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a defensive structure, such as walls or mounds, built to protect a place or strengthen its defenses
defensive structure built for protection

Examples

The ancient city was protected by massive fortifications that still stand today.
Engineers worked tirelessly to repair the damaged fortification before the enemy could attack again.
During the tour, we walked along the old fortifications and learned about their history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bastion - a projecting part of a fortification
  • Synonym: rampart - a defensive wall of a castle or walled city
  • Antonym: siege - a military blockade of a fortified place

Etymology

From Latin fortificatio (strengthening), from fortificare (to strengthen).
