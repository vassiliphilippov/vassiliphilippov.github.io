Skagit

1. NOUN

/ ˈskædʒɪt / (AmE), / ˈskædʒɪt / (BrE)
a Salishan language formerly spoken by the Skagit people of western Washington State
a Salishan language of the Skagit people

Examples

The Skagit language has been the subject of recent linguistic preservation efforts.
Few fluent speakers of Skagit remain today, making documentation vital.
She studied Skagit as part of her research on Salishan dialects.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskædʒɪt / (AmE), / ˈskædʒɪt / (BrE)
A member of a Salish Native American people native to northwestern Washington State, USA.
member of a Salish people in Washington State

Examples

The Skagit have a rich tradition of salmon fishing and canoe building.
Historians have studied the migration patterns of the Skagit in the Puget Sound region.
A Skagit elder spoke at the cultural heritage event last weekend.

Etymology

From the group's self-designation or regional river/area name in the Salishan languages.
