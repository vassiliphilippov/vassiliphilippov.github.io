flogging

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɑɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflɒɡɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of beating someone with a whip, strap, or rope as a form of punishment
beating with a whip for punishment

Examples

The soldier received a severe flogging for disobeying orders.
In the past, flogging was a common punishment in the navy.
Human rights activists have condemned the use of flogging as inhumane.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whipping - the act of striking with a whip as punishment
  • Synonym: caning - punishment by beating with a cane

Etymology

From verb 'flog' (18th century, origin uncertain—possibly from Latin 'flagellare' meaning to whip).
