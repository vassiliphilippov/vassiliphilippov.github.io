chowder

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaʊdər / (AmE), / ˈtʃaʊdə / (BrE)
A thick soup or stew typically made with milk, potatoes, onions, and sometimes bacon or seafood.
a thick soup or stew

Examples

She prepared a hearty bowl of chowder for dinner on the cold evening.
The seafood chowder at the coastal restaurant was filled with clams and potatoes.
Many New England towns hold annual chowder festivals to celebrate local recipes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soup - a liquid dish, typically savory and made by boiling meat, fish, or vegetables
  • Synonym: stew - a dish of meat and vegetables cooked slowly in liquid

Etymology

From French *chaudrée*, from *chaudron* (cauldron).
