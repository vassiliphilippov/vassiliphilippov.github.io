brome

1. NOUN

/ broʊm / (AmE), / brəʊm / (BrE)
any of several woodland and meadow grasses of the genus Bromus, native to temperate regions.
a type of temperate grass (genus Bromus)

Examples

The field was covered in tall brome and wildflowers swaying in the breeze.
Farmers sometimes plant brome as a forage crop for livestock.
Researchers studied how brome competes with native plant species in grasslands.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Bromus', from Greek 'brōmos' (a kind of oat).
