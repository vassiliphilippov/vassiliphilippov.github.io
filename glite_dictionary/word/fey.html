fey

1. ADJ

/ feɪ / (AmE), / feɪ / (BrE)
appearing strange or otherworldly, often suggesting a slightly insane or eccentric manner
slightly insane or eccentric

Examples

Her fey behavior at the party made everyone wonder what she was thinking.
The character in the novel was described as fey, with odd habits and a distant look in his eyes.
His fey remarks left the listeners both amused and bewildered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eccentric - unconventional and slightly strange
  • Antonym: sane - mentally healthy and not strange

Etymology

From Old English *fǣge* (doomed to die), later associated with strange or magical qualities.

2. ADJ

/ feɪ / (AmE), / feɪ / (BrE)
Having an otherworldly, mysterious, or strange quality, often in a way that suggests magic or elf-like mysteriousness.
having an otherworldly or magical quality

Examples

She spoke in a fey voice, as if her mind were somewhere far away.
The garden had a fey atmosphere, filled with strange flowers and shimmering light.
His fey gaze unsettled everyone, as though he could see things hidden from the rest of us.

Synset Examples

thunderbolts quivered with elfin flares of heat lightning
the fey quality was there, the ability to see the moon at midday

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elfin - having qualities reminiscent of an elf, especially strangeness or otherworldly charm

Etymology

From Old English fæge (fated to die, accursed), later influenced by Scottish usage meaning strange or visionary.
