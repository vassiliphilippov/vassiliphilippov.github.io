Clavariaceae

1. NOUN

/ kləˌvɛriˈeɪsi.i / (AmE), / kləˌvɑːriˈeɪsi.i / (BrE)
a family of fleshy fungi known as coral fungi, characterized by their branched, club-shaped fruiting bodies
a family of coral fungi

Examples

The Clavariaceae family includes many colorful species of coral fungi found in forests.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Clavariaceae during their field study.
Members of the Clavariaceae are often mistaken for underwater coral due to their appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coral fungus - a fungus with branched, coral-like fruiting bodies

Etymology

From modern Latin, from Clavaria (the type genus) + -aceae, the standard suffix for plant and fungal families.

a family of fleshy fungi known as coral fungi, characterized by their branching, often coral-like fruiting bodies
a family of coral fungi
medical

Examples

The Clavariaceae are commonly referred to as coral fungi due to their branching appearance.
Several species in the Clavariaceae can be found on woodland floors, often forming colorful clusters.
Researchers are studying the ecological role of Clavariaceae in forest nutrient cycles.

Etymology

From the genus Clavaria, from Latin 'clava' (club) + scientific suffix '-aceae' for plant/fungal families.
