ionic

1. ADJ

/ aɪˈɑnɪk / (AmE), / aɪˈɒnɪk / (BrE)
containing, involving, or occurring in the form of ions; relating to or denoting chemical compounds consisting of ions held together by electrostatic forces.
relating to ions or ionic compounds
medical

Examples

Sodium chloride is an ionic compound composed of sodium and chloride ions.
The ionic strength of a solution can affect biochemical reactions.
Calcium ions play a crucial role in ionic transport in biological cells.

Synset Examples

ionic charge
ionic crystals
ionic hydrogen

Related Terms

  • Antonym: covalent - relating to a type of chemical bond involving the sharing of electron pairs between atoms
  • Antonym: molecular - relating to or consisting of molecules

Etymology

From 'ion' + suffix '-ic', after Greek 'ionikos' (relating to going). Modern use from 'ion' (charged particle) in 19th-century chemistry.
