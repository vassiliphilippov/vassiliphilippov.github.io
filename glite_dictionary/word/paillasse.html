paillasse

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈlɑːs / (AmE), / paɪˈlæs / (BrE)
A mattress consisting of a thin pad filled with straw or sawdust, used especially in historical or rustic settings.
thin mattress filled with straw or sawdust

Examples

The prisoner slept on a paillasse in the corner of his cell.
During the harvest, workers rested on a simple paillasse under the barn roof.
The inn offered no real beds, only an old paillasse for travelers.

Etymology

From French 'paillasse', from 'paille' meaning 'straw'.
