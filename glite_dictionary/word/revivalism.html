revivalism

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈvaɪ.vəˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / rɪˈvaɪ.vəl.ɪ.zəm / (BrE)
belief in or the practice of reviving religious faith, especially through evangelistic meetings or efforts to reawaken spiritual fervor.
promotion of religious revivals

Examples

The rise of revivalism in the 19th century led to a surge of religious enthusiasm across the country.
Many churches embraced revivalism to attract new members and reawaken spiritual commitment.
Critics argued that revivalism sometimes prioritized emotion over doctrine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evangelism - zealous preaching or spreading of the Christian gospel

Etymology

From 'revival' + '-ism'; 'revival' from Latin 'revivalis', relating to coming back to life.
