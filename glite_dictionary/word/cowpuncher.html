cowpuncher

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊˌpʌntʃər / (AmE), / ˈkaʊˌpʌntʃə / (BrE)
A person employed to tend cattle and do ranch work, typically on horseback; a cowboy.
ranch worker who tends cattle on horseback
american

Examples

The seasoned cowpuncher guided the herd across the wide, open plains.
As a young cowpuncher, he spent long days riding and rounding up strays.
She listened to tales of old-time cowpunchers around the campfire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowboy - person who tends cattle and does ranch work, especially in the western US

Etymology

American English, from 'cow' + 'puncher' (one who drives or herds animals).
