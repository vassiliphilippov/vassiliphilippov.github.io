tragacanth

1. NOUN

/ ˈtræɡəˌkænθ / (AmE), / ˈtræɡəkænθ / (BrE)
a natural gum obtained from various Middle Eastern plants, used in pharmacy, adhesives, and textile printing as a thickening or stabilizing agent
natural gum used in pharmacy and industry

Examples

The artist used tragacanth to prepare the pigments for her illuminating work.
In pharmacy, tragacanth serves as a binder for pills and lozenges.
Traditional textile printers relied on tragacanth as a thickener for their dyes.

Etymology

From Greek *tragakantha*, from *tragos* (goat) + *akantha* (thorn), referencing the plant source.
