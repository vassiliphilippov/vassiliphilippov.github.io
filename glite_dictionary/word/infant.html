infant

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.fənt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fənt / (BrE)
a very young child, especially one under one year old who has not yet begun to walk or talk
very young child (under one year)

Examples

The hospital provides special care for premature infants.
An infant requires constant attention and care from adults.
She enrolled her infant in a parenting class for new mothers.

Synset Examples

the baby began to cry again
she held the baby in her arms
it sounds simple, but when you have your own baby it is all so different

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baby - a very young child, especially one newly or recently born

Etymology

From Latin 'infans', meaning 'unable to speak'.
