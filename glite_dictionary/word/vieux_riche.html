vieux riche

1. NOUN

/ vju ˈriʃ / (AmE), / vjøː ˈriːʃ / (BrE)
people or families who have inherited wealth over generations, especially as contrasted with those who are newly rich
people with old inherited wealth

Examples

The town was dominated by the vieux riche whose families had lived there for centuries.
Unlike the flashy newcomers, the vieux riche preferred understated elegance and tradition.
She married into a family known among locals as part of the vieux riche.

Synset Examples

His attitude screamed vieux riche, distasteful yet intriguing.
The party was full of vieux riche flaunting their wealth.
The nouveau riche often envy the vieux riche's sophistication.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nouveau riche - people who have recently acquired wealth

Etymology

From French 'vieux riche', meaning 'old rich'.
