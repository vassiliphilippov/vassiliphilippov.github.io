to kibbitz

1. VERB

/ ˈkɪbɪts / (AmE), / ˈkɪbɪts / (BrE)
to make unwanted, intrusive, or meddlesome comments, especially as an onlooker or bystander
make unwanted, intrusive comments
informal

Examples

Please don’t kibbitz when I’m trying to solve the puzzle on my own.
He tends to kibbitz during meetings, offering advice no one asked for.
While we played chess, my uncle couldn’t help but kibbitz from the sidelines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to interfere - to get involved in a situation where you are not wanted or needed
  • Synonym: to butt in - to interrupt or involve oneself, often in an unwelcome way
  • Antonym: to mind one's own business - to refrain from interfering or commenting

Etymology

From Yiddish קיבוץ‎ (kibitsn), influenced by German 'Kiebitz' (a meddler, busybody, or an onlooker at card games).
