congruent

1. ADJ

/ kənˈɡruː.ənt / (AmE), / kənˈɡruː.ənt / (BrE)
Having the same shape and size, so that they exactly coincide when superimposed.
having the same shape and size

Examples

The two triangles are congruent because all their sides and angles are equal.
These patterns are congruent to each other when overlaid.
For the puzzle to fit, the pieces must be congruent in shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: identical - being exactly the same
  • Synonym: corresponding - having a direct relationship or equivalence

Etymology

From Latin 'congruent-', from 'congruere' meaning 'to agree, coincide'.

2. ADJ

/ kənˈgruːənt / (AmE), / kənˈgruːənt / (BrE)
in agreement or harmony; corresponding in character, form, or kind
matching or in agreement

Examples

Her actions were congruent with her words, demonstrating true integrity.
The architect ensured the new extension was congruent with the building's original style.
Their goals are congruent, so working together makes perfect sense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compatible - able to exist or work together without conflict
  • Synonym: consistent - acting or done in the same way over time; compatible or in agreement
  • Antonym: incongruent - not in agreement or harmony

Etymology

From Latin congruent-, congruens, present participle of congruere (to agree, coincide).
