wishful

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɪʃfəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪʃfəl / (BrE)
having or showing a strong desire or longing for something to happen or to be true
having or expressing a desire or longing

Examples

He cast a wishful glance at the dessert menu, hoping for an extra treat.
Despite her wishful thinking, the weather did not improve for the picnic.
The child's wishful eyes were fixed on the bicycle in the shop window.

Synset Examples

desirous of high office
desirous of finding a quick solution to the problem

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desirous - having or showing desire for something
  • Degree Higher: hopeful - feeling or inspiring hope for a particular outcome

Etymology

From English 'wish' + '-ful', meaning full of wishing or desire.

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɪʃfəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪʃfəl / (BrE)
having or showing a desire for something to happen or be true, especially when it is unlikely; yearning or aspiring, particularly for recognition or advancement.
having or showing a strong desire or wish

Examples

After years of hard work, the wishful young artist finally exhibited her paintings in a gallery.
His wishful gaze reflected his dream of becoming the team's captain someday.
Many wishful candidates applied for the prestigious scholarship, hoping for a chance to advance their careers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aspiring - having ambitions to achieve something, especially a career or recognition
  • Synonym: ambitious - having a strong desire for success or achievement

Etymology

From wish + -ful (suffix meaning 'full of').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00105332-s
Synset POS: s
