Euonymus

1. NOUN

/ juˈɑnɪməs / (AmE), / juˈɒnɪməs / (BrE)
a genus of chiefly evergreen shrubs, small trees, or vines, widely cultivated for their ornamental foliage and fruit
a genus of ornamental shrubs or trees

Examples

The garden featured several Euonymus bushes along the walkway.
She planted a Euonymus to add year-round greenery to her yard.
Gardeners often choose Euonymus for its attractive, variegated leaves.

Etymology

From Greek euōnymos meaning 'of good name'.

2. NOUN

/ juːˈɑːnəməs / (AmE), / juːˈɒnɪməs / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly evergreen shrubs, small trees, or vines that are widely distributed in temperate regions.
genus of evergreen shrubs or small trees

Examples

Many gardens feature at least one Euonymus for its year-round foliage and decorative berries.
The Euonymus hedge provided a neat, evergreen border along the walkway.
Several varieties of Euonymus are popular choices for landscaping due to their hardiness.

Etymology

From Latin 'euonymus', from Ancient Greek 'euōnymos', meaning 'of good name' or 'lucky'.
