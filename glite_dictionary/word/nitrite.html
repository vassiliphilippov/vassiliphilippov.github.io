nitrite

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪˌtraɪt / (AmE), / ˈnɪtraɪt / (BrE)
a chemical compound containing the nitrite ion (NO2−), or the nitrite ion itself; commonly found as a salt or ester of nitrous acid.
a compound or ion containing NO2−
medical

Examples

The water sample was found to contain a high level of nitrite ions, which can be harmful to fish.
Sodium nitrite is used as a food preservative and color fixative in processed meats.
Researchers monitored nitrite concentrations in the river to assess the impact of agricultural runoff.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: nitrate - a chemical compound, often a salt or ester, containing the nitrate ion (NO3−)

Etymology

From nitro- (relating to nitrogen) and -ite (denoting a salt or ester).
