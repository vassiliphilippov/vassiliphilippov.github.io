scratchy

1. ADJ

/ ˈskrætʃi / (AmE), / ˈskrætʃi / (BrE)
(of a sound or voice) unpleasantly harsh or grating.
harsh or grating (especially of sound)

Examples

Her scratchy voice made the announcement difficult to understand.
The old record produced a scratchy sound that filled the room with static.
After cheering loudly all night, his throat was sore and his words came out scratchy.

Synset Examples

a gravelly voice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gravelly - (of a voice) deep and rough-sounding
  • Synonym: raspy - hoarse or harsh-sounding (of a voice)
  • Antonym: smooth - (of a voice or sound) pleasant, not harsh or grating

Etymology

Formed from 'scratch' + '-y', denoting a quality, originally describing something like a surface that scratches, later extended to sound.

2. ADJ

/ ˈskrætʃi / (AmE), / ˈskrætʃi / (BrE)
(of a person or temperament) easily irritated or annoyed; quick to take offense.
easily annoyed or irritable (of a person)

Examples

After a long and stressful day, he can become quite scratchy with everyone around him.
Her scratchy mood made it difficult for the team to approach her for feedback.
Try not to be so scratchy; we are all doing our best.

Synset Examples

an incorrigibly fractious young man
not the least nettlesome of his countrymen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritable - easily annoyed or angered
  • Antonym: good-natured - naturally friendly and easygoing

Etymology

From 'scratch' + '-y'; extended metaphorical sense from the feeling of physical irritation.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈskrætʃi / (AmE), / ˈskrætʃi / (BrE)
(of a performance or result) inconsistent or uneven in quality or execution.
inconsistent or uneven in quality

Examples

His pitching was scratchy throughout the game, making it hard for the team to win.
The actor gave a scratchy performance on opening night, missing several lines.
Her recent work has been scratchy, showing flashes of brilliance mixed with careless mistakes.

Synset Examples

the golfer hit the ball well but his putting was spotty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotty - inconsistent in quality or performance
  • Antonym: consistent - always behaving or performing in a similar way

4. ADJ

/ ˈskrætʃi / (AmE), / ˈskrætʃi / (BrE)
(of a surface or material) causing slight abrasion or irritation when touched or rubbed against the skin.
causing abrasion or irritation

Examples

The wool sweater felt scratchy against my skin, so I wore a T-shirt underneath.
She complained that the new labels inside her shirt were scratchy and uncomfortable.
After washing, the towels became noticeably scratchy to the touch.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; not rough or scratchy

Etymology

From 'scratch' + '-y', indicating a tendency to cause scratches.
