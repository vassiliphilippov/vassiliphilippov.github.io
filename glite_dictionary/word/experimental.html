experimental

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˌspɛrɪˈmɛntəl / (AmE), / ɪkˌspɛrɪˈmɛntəl / (BrE)
based on, relating to, or involving observation or experiment rather than theory or pure logic
based on experiment or observation

Examples

The scientist published experimental findings in a peer-reviewed journal.
Their conclusions are based on experimental evidence rather than mere speculation.
Before launching a new drug, it must go through several experimental phases.

Synset Examples

experimental results that supported the hypothesis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empirical - based on observation or experience rather than theory or pure logic

Etymology

From Latin experimentalis (pertaining to trial or experience); from experimentum (a trial, test)

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00863136-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ɪkˌspɛrɪˈmɛntəl / (AmE), / ɪkˌspɛrɪˈmentəl / (BrE)
involving or based on new ideas, methods, or scientific tests rather than established practice
based on experiment; new or innovative

Examples

The researchers conducted experimental studies to test their hypothesis.
She is known for her experimental approach to film-making.
These drugs are still at an experimental stage and not widely available.

Synset Examples

experimental physics

Related Terms

  • Antonym: theoretical - concerned with theories rather than practice or experiments
  • Synonym: empirical - based on observation or experience rather than theory or pure logic

Etymology

From Latin 'experimentum' (trial, test) via late Middle English and Old French.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02951729-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ɪkˌspɛrəˈmɛntəl / (AmE), / ɪkˌspɛrɪˈmɛntl̩ / (BrE)
involving or used for experiments; based on untested ideas or procedures
relating to or involving experiments

Examples

The new medicine is still an experimental drug and not yet widely available.
She participated in an experimental program to test behavioral therapy techniques.
Many technological advances began as experimental ideas in university laboratories.

Synset Examples

an experimental drug

Related Terms

  • Antonym: theoretical - concerned with theories rather than experiments

Etymology

From Latin experimentum (trial, test) via Old French and late Middle English.
