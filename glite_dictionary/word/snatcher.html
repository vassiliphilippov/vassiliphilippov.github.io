snatcher

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnætʃər / (AmE), / ˈsnætʃə / (BrE)
a thief who quickly seizes something, especially by grabbing and running away
a thief who grabs and runs

Examples

The snatcher darted into the crowd, disappearing with her handbag.
Police arrested a notorious snatcher who had been targeting tourists in the park.
After the snatcher grabbed his phone, he gave chase but couldn't catch him.

Synset Examples

a purse snatcher

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thief - a person who steals

Etymology

From the verb 'snatch' (to seize quickly) + agent suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsnætʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈsnætʃə / (BrE)
a person who seizes or kidnaps someone unlawfully, often holding them for ransom
person who unlawfully kidnaps someone

Examples

The police arrested the snatcher just minutes after the incident occurred.
Parents warned their children to stay alert in areas known for snatcher activity.
The snatcher demanded a large sum of money in exchange for the victim's release.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kidnapper - a person who takes someone by force, often for ransom
  • Synonym: abductor - a person who carries off or leads away by force; kidnapper

Etymology

From the verb 'snatch' (to seize quickly), first recorded in English in the 16th century, combined with the agent suffix '-er'.

Synset POS: n
