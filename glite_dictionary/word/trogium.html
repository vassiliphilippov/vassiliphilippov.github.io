Trogium

1. NOUN

/ ˈtroʊdʒiəm / (AmE), / ˈtrəʊdʒiəm / (BrE)
A genus of small insects in the family Psocidae, which includes species of booklice.
a genus of insects (Psocidae)

Examples

The Trogium genus includes several species of booklice commonly found in stored products.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Trogium genus during their fieldwork in Asia.
Pest control experts identified the insects as members of the Trogium genus infesting the warehouse.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek *trōgion* (a small eatable thing, from *trōgein* ‘to gnaw’).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtroʊdʒiəm / (AmE), / ˈtrəʊdʒiəm / (BrE)
a genus of small, primitive insects in the family Trogiidae, commonly known as booklice or barklice.
a genus of booklice or barklice

Examples

The species Trogium pulsatorium is commonly found in old paper and books.
Researchers identified several members of the genus Trogium in the laboratory samples.
Trogium insects can sometimes be a nuisance in archival collections.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'trōgion' (a kind of small insect).
