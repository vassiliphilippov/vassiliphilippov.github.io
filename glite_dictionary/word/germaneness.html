germaneness

1. NOUN

/ dʒɜrˈmeɪn.nɪs / (AmE), / dʒɜːˈmeɪn.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being closely relevant or pertinent to the subject under consideration
relevance or pertinence to a subject

Examples

The judge questioned the germaneness of the witness's testimony to the case.
Her comments had little germaneness to the subject under discussion.
Lawyers often debate the germaneness of certain pieces of evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relevance - the quality of being connected to the matter at hand
  • Antonym: irrelevance - lack of relation to the matter at hand

Etymology

From 'germane', which originates from Middle English via Old French germain, from Latin germanus (having the same parents, related).
