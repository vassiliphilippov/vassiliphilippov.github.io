Tectaria macrodonta

1. NOUN

/ tɛkˈtɛriə ˌmækrəˈdɑntə / (AmE), / tɛkˈtɛːriə ˌmakrəˈdɒntə / (BrE)
a fern of tropical Asia characterized by round, buttonlike bulbils
Asian fern with buttonlike bulbils

Examples

Scientists discovered a rare Tectaria macrodonta growing near the edge of the rainforest.
The Tectaria macrodonta can be easily recognized by its distinctive round bulbils.
Gardeners appreciate Tectaria macrodonta for its unique appearance and adaptability to tropical climates.

Etymology

From the genus name Tectaria (Latinized from Greek 'tektōn', builder or carpenter) and the Latin 'macrodonta' (macro- 'large' + -donta 'toothed').
