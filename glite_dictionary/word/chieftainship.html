chieftainship

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃiːf.tənˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈtʃiːf.tɪn.ʃɪp / (BrE)
the position or authority of a chieftain; leadership of a tribe or clan
position of a chieftain

Examples

After the old leader's death, his son assumed the chieftainship of the tribe.
Her chieftainship was marked by years of peace and prosperity.
The rules of succession to the chieftainship were clearly outlined in their tradition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chieftain - leader of a tribe or clan

Etymology

Formed from 'chieftain' + '-ship', denoting a state or condition.
