androgyne

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.drəˌdʒaɪn / (AmE), / ˈan.drə.dʒʌɪn / (BrE)
a person who has both male and female sexual characteristics and organs, making gender assignment at birth ambiguous
person with both male and female sexual characteristics

Examples

The ancient legend spoke of an androgyne who united both genders within one body.
Medical experts discussed the challenges faced when a child is born as an androgyne.
Some ancient societies regarded the androgyne as sacred and endowed with special wisdom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hermaphrodite - a person or animal having both male and female reproductive organs

Etymology

From Greek 'andro-' (man) and 'gynē' (woman).
