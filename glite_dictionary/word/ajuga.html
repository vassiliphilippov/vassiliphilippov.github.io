Ajuga

1. NOUN

/ əˈdʒuːɡə / (AmE), / əˈdʒuːɡə / (BrE)
A genus of low-growing herbaceous plants in the mint family, commonly grown for their decorative foliage and spikes of blue flowers.
a genus of low-growing herbaceous plants

Examples

Ajuga is often used as a ground cover in shady gardens.
The vivid blue flowers of Ajuga attract bees and other pollinators in the spring.
Gardeners appreciate Ajuga for its ability to spread quickly and suppress weeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bugleweed - another name for plants in the Ajuga genus

Etymology

From Latin 'ajuga', meaning bugleweed.

Examples
