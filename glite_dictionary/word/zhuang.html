Zhuang

1. NOUN

/ ʒwɑŋ / (AmE), / ʒwɑːŋ / (BrE)
the language of the Zhuang people, belonging to the Tai language family and spoken mainly in southern China
Tai language spoken in southern China

Examples

Many people in Guangxi speak Zhuang as their first language.
The Zhuang language uses a modified Latin alphabet for writing.
Linguists study Zhuang to understand the diversity of Tai languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tai languages - a language family to which Zhuang belongs

Etymology

From Chinese Zhuàng (壮/壯) referring to the Zhuang ethnic group.
