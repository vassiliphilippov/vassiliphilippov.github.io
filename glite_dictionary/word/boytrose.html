boytrose

1. ADJ

/ bɔɪˈtroʊs / (AmE), / bɔɪˈtrəʊz / (BrE)
Resembling a cluster of grapes in form or appearance.
grapelike in form
archaic

Examples

The flower clusters on the vine had a distinctly boytrose arrangement.
Under the microscope, the bacteria appeared in boytrose groups.
The artist admired the boytrose quality of the glass beads.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek βοτρυώδης (botryōdēs), from βοτρύς (botrys) meaning 'bunch of grapes.'
