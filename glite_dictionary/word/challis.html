challis

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæli / (AmE), / ˈʃæli / (BrE)
a soft, lightweight fabric, often printed, typically used for dresses or scarves
soft lightweight printed fabric

Examples

She wore a summer dress made of delicate challis patterned with small flowers.
The shop sells various fabrics, including cotton, silk, and challis.
Challis is prized for its softness and flowing drape, making it ideal for scarves.

Etymology

From French 'challis', of uncertain origin.
