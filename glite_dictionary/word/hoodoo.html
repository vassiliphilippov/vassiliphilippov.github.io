hoodoo

1. NOUN

/ ˈhuːduː / (AmE), / ˈhuːduː / (BrE)
A charm or object believed to have magical powers, especially for bringing good or bad luck.
superstitious magical charm

Examples

She carried a small hoodoo in her pocket for good luck.
The old woman claimed her hoodoo would protect the house from harm.
He blamed his misfortune on a cursed hoodoo found in his garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talisman - an object thought to have magical powers and bring good luck
  • Synonym: amulet - an ornament or small piece of jewelry thought to give protection against evil, danger, or disease

Etymology

Probably from Louisiana Creole *houdou*, from West African languages such as Ewe and Fon *vodu* (spirit).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhuː.duː / (AmE), / ˈhuː.duː / (BrE)
a person, thing, or action believed to bring bad luck or misfortune
something believed to cause bad luck

Examples

Some believe that breaking a mirror is a hoodoo that brings seven years of bad luck.
He kept losing at cards and blamed a hoodoo for his misfortune.
The abandoned house had a reputation as a hoodoo among the locals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jinx - a person or thing that is believed to bring bad luck
  • Antonym: charm - something believed to bring good luck or protection

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from African-American English, influenced by West African spiritual practices.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03537260-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈhuːduː / (AmE), / ˈhuːduː / (BrE)
a tall, thin spire of rock that protrudes from the bottom of an arid basin or badland, formed by erosion and weathering
unusually shaped rock column

Examples

The trail wound past a towering hoodoo that cast a long shadow across the desert floor.
Geologists studied the layers of rock exposed in the hoodoo formations of Bryce Canyon.
After centuries of erosion, only a single slender hoodoo remained standing near the cliff edge.

Synset Examples

a tall sandstone hoodoo

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pillar - a tall, vertical structure of stone; may refer to a natural rock formation or a man-made column

Etymology

Originally a term of West African origin, brought to North America; in geology, probably adopted due to the peculiar appearance of the formations.

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ ˈhuː.duː / (AmE), / ˈhuː.duː / (BrE)
a person who practices voodoo, especially in the southern United States
practitioner of voodoo

Examples

The old woman was known as a powerful hoodoo in her community.
Stories say that a hoodoo can cure ailments with mysterious rituals.
He sought the advice of a hoodoo to lift the curse on his family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voodoo - a religion involving magic and spirit worship, chiefly in the Caribbean and southern US

Etymology

Possibly from West African, related to Ewe *hudu* (spirit), or variant of 'voodoo'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10203909-n
Synset POS: n

5. VERB

/ ˈhuː.duː / (AmE), / ˈhuː.duː / (BrE)
to bring bad luck to; to cause someone or something to have misfortune
bring bad luck to

Examples

Some superstitious players believe that the new mascot will hoodoo the team.
The broken mirror seemed to hoodoo the production, as accidents kept happening.
They worried the old ship was hoodooed after so many mishaps at sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jinx - to bring bad luck to; to cast an evil spell on

Etymology

Probably from African American English, ultimately from West African spiritual traditions.
