Leiopelmatidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌlaɪ.oʊˌpɛlˈmætɪdi / (AmE), / ˌlaɪ.əʊˌpɛlˈmætɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of primitive frogs native to New Zealand, known for their ancient evolutionary characteristics.
family of primitive New Zealand frogs

Examples

The Leiopelmatidae are considered some of the most ancient frog families in the world.
Due to habitat loss, several species within the Leiopelmatidae are now critically endangered.
Scientists study Leiopelmatidae to learn about the evolutionary history of amphibians.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Leiopelma, from Greek leios (smooth) + pelma (sole of the foot) + -idae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌliː.oʊˌpɛlˈmætɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌliː.əʊˌpɛlˈmætɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of primitive frogs native to New Zealand, notable for their ancient evolutionary lineage.
a family of primitive New Zealand frogs

Examples

Scientists are studying the endangered Leiopelmatidae to understand amphibian evolution in New Zealand.
The Leiopelmatidae are one of the most ancient groups of frogs still living today.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitats of the Leiopelmatidae from invasive species.

Etymology

From Greek 'leios' (smooth) + 'pelma' (sole of the foot); used in zoological nomenclature.
