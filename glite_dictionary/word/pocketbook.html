pocketbook

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑkɪtˌbʊk / (AmE), / ˈpɒkɪtbʊk / (BrE)
A small, portable paperback book designed to fit in a pocket.
small portable paperback book

Examples

She took a pocketbook with her to read on the train.
These pocketbooks are perfect for travel because they fit easily in your bag.
He collects classic novels in pocketbook editions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paperback - a book with a flexible paper cover
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03979528-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑk.ɪtˌbʊk / (AmE), / ˈpɒk.ɪtˌbʊk / (BrE)
a small bag or purse used for carrying money and small personal items, especially by women
small handbag or purse (esp. for women)
american

Examples

She rummaged through her pocketbook searching for her car keys.
He bought his mother a new pocketbook for her birthday.
Emily's pocketbook was full of receipts and loose change.

Synset Examples

she reached into her bag and found a comb

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purse - a small bag for carrying money, especially by a woman
  • Synonym: handbag - a bag carried by women to hold personal items

Etymology

From 'pocket' + 'book'; originally referred to a small book that could fit in a pocket, later to a pouch for carrying valuables.

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɑkɪtbʊk / (AmE), / ˈpɒkɪtbʊk / (BrE)
a person's financial resources or ability to pay for something; one's budget or available money
a person's financial means

Examples

Higher taxes will certainly affect every family's pocketbook.
College tuition is a big strain on my pocketbook this year.
She always considers her pocketbook before making any major purchases.

Synset Examples

that car is too expensive for my pocketbook

Related Terms

  • Synonym: budget - the amount of money available to spend

Etymology

Originally from the literal meaning of a small booklike case for money, expanding to denote financial resources.

4. NOUN

a small, flat case that fits into a pocket, used for carrying money, papers, and other small personal items
small case for holding money and papers
american

Examples

She reached into her pocketbook to pay for the coffee.
He lost his pocketbook on the subway and had to cancel all his cards.
My grandmother keeps old photographs in her pocketbook along with her cash.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wallet - a flat, folding case for carrying paper money, cards, etc.
  • Synonym: purse - a small bag used for carrying money, especially by women

Etymology

From 'pocket' + 'book', referring to a book that fits in the pocket or a small carrying case.
