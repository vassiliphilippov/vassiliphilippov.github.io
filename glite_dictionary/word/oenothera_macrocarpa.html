Oenothera macrocarpa

1. NOUN

/ iːˈnɒθərə ˌmækroʊˈkɑːrpə / (AmE), / iːˈnɒθərə ˌmakrəʊˈkɑːpə / (BrE)
A species of evening primrose native to the south central United States, known for its large yellow flowers that open in the evening.
a species of evening primrose

Examples

The garden featured several beautiful Oenothera macrocarpa blooming at dusk.
Botanists studied the distribution of Oenothera macrocarpa in its native habitats.
Many gardeners prize Oenothera macrocarpa for its showy evening flowers.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus name Oenothera + Latinized Greek makros (large) + karpos (fruit).
