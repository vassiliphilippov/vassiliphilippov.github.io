boater

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊtər / (AmE), / ˈbəʊtə / (BrE)
a person who drives or rides in a boat, especially for pleasure
person who drives or rides in a boat

Examples

Every summer, hundreds of boaters visit the lake to enjoy water sports.
The river was busy with boaters of all ages during the sunny weekend.
As an experienced boater, he always wears a life jacket while on the water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sailor - a person who works on or operates a boat or ship

2. NOUN

/ ˈboʊtər / (AmE), / ˈbəʊtə / (BrE)
a stiff hat made of straw with a flat crown, typically worn as part of summer attire or at certain traditional events
stiff straw hat with a flat crown

Examples

He wore a crisp white suit and a boater to the garden party.
The old photograph showed a group of men in boaters along the riverside.
At the regatta, many spectators donned their traditional boater hats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: straw hat - a hat made from woven straw or similar material

Etymology

From the word 'boat', referring to its popularity among boating enthusiasts in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.
