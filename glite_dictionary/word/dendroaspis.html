Dendroaspis

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛndroʊˈæspɪs / (AmE), / ˌdɛndrəʊˈaspɪs / (BrE)
A genus of large, highly venomous snakes known as mambas, native to Africa.
genus of venomous African snakes (mambas)

Examples

The black mamba, a member of the Dendroaspis genus, is one of Africa's most feared snakes.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Dendroaspis genus in Central Africa.
All members of the Dendroaspis genus are known for their potent venom and agility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mamba - any of several highly venomous African snakes of the genus Dendroaspis

Etymology

From Greek dendron (tree) + aspis (asp, snake).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛndroʊˈæspɪs / (AmE), / ˌdɛndrəʊˈaspɪs / (BrE)
A genus of highly venomous snakes known as mambas, native to sub-Saharan Africa.
genus of venomous African snakes (mambas)

Examples

The highly venomous snakes of the genus Dendroaspis are commonly known as mambas.
Researchers documented the behavior of green mambas, a species within the Dendroaspis genus.
The black mamba, the largest member of Dendroaspis, is renowned for its speed and potent venom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mamba - A highly venomous African snake of the genus Dendroaspis

Etymology

From Greek dendron (tree) + aspis (shield, snake).
