Lewisia

1. NOUN

/ luˈɪʃiə / (AmE), / luˈɪsiə / (BrE)
A genus of low-growing flowering plants native to western North America, characterized by linear, woolly leaves and large pink flowers.
genus of North American flowering plants

Examples

Many gardeners in the western United States grow Lewisia for its vibrant spring flowers.
The alpine rock garden featured a rare species of Lewisia in full bloom.
With its drought tolerance, Lewisia is becoming a popular choice for native plant landscaping.

Etymology

Named after Meriwether Lewis, American explorer of the Lewis and Clark Expedition.

2. NOUN

/ luˈɪziə / (AmE), / luˈɪziə / (BrE)
A genus of low-growing, western North American herbs with linear, woolly leaves and large pink flowers.
genus of North American herbs with pink flowers

Examples

The mountain garden featured a beautiful display of Lewisia in full bloom.
Many horticulturists prize Lewisia for its resilience and striking flowers.
Lewisia species are well adapted to rocky, well-drained soils.

Etymology

Named after Meriwether Lewis (1774–1809), American explorer.
