to guttle

1. VERB

/ ˈɡʌtəl / (AmE), / ˈɡʌtəl / (BrE)
to eat food greedily; to devour voraciously
eat greedily; devour voraciously

Examples

The children began to guttle the cookies as soon as they were out of the oven.
He would often guttle his food after skipping lunch at work.
Despite the fine surroundings, she didn't hesitate to guttle her dinner.

Synset Examples

he devoured three sandwiches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to devour - to eat something eagerly and in large amounts so that nothing is left
  • Synonym: gorge - to eat a large amount greedily
  • Antonym: nibble - to eat something by taking small bites

Etymology

Perhaps an alteration of 'gut' or from Middle English 'gotelen' (to gulp)
