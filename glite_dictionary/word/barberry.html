barberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːrˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈbɑːbəri / (BrE)
a prickly shrub of the genus Berberis, with yellow flowers and small red or blue berries
prickly shrub with red berries

Examples

The bright red berries of the barberry bush attract birds in the autumn.
Gardeners often plant barberry hedges because they are dense and thorny.
Traditional medicine sometimes uses barberry root for its antibacterial properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Berberis - the genus of shrubs known as barberries

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'berberis', from Arabic 'barbārīs'.
