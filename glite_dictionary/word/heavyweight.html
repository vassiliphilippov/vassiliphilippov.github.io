heavyweight

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛviˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈhɛviweɪt / (BrE)
a person of great size, strength, or influence, either physically or in terms of their abilities or status
a large, powerful, or influential person

Examples

The company hired a heavyweight to lead its new research division.
As a political heavyweight, she commands respect across party lines.
He may not seem like a heavyweight, but his ideas have a huge impact.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lightweight - a person of relatively small size or influence; opposite of heavyweight
  • Synonym: giant - a very large or powerful person

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛviˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈhɛviweɪt / (BrE)
An amateur boxer whose body weight does not exceed 201 pounds (91 kilograms), forming a specific weight classification in the sport.
amateur boxer of up to 201 pounds

Examples

He qualified for the national championships as a heavyweight after winning his regional bout.
The heavyweight division often attracts boxers with powerful punches and strong endurance.
As an amateur heavyweight, he maintained a strict diet to stay within the 201-pound limit.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: light heavyweight - an amateur boxer weighing between 165 and 178 pounds
  • Degree Higher: super heavyweight - an amateur boxer weighing more than 201 pounds

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɛviˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈhɛviweɪt / (BrE)
A competitor in the heaviest weight division, especially in boxing, wrestling, or other combat sports; also, the weight class itself for these competitors.
heaviest combat sports weight class

Examples

The heavyweight champion successfully defended his title last night.
She trains in the women's heavyweight division for judo.
Several legendary heavyweights have made history in the sport of boxing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lightweight - a competitor in a lower weight class in combat sports

Etymology

From 'heavy' + 'weight', originally used in boxing to categorize fighters by weight class.

/ ˈhɛviˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈhɛviweɪt / (BrE)
a professional boxer, wrestler, or other athlete who competes in a category for those weighing more than a specified limit, typically more than 190 pounds in boxing.
boxer or athlete over a weight limit

Examples

The heavyweight stepped into the ring amid loud cheers from the crowd.
He won the national heavyweight title after a dramatic twelve-round fight.
Several famous heavyweights have become legends in boxing history.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lightweight - a boxer or athlete who competes in a lighter weight class
  • Degree Lower: middleweight - a boxer or athlete in an intermediate weight class

Etymology

From 'heavy' + 'weight'; refers to those of greater body mass.
