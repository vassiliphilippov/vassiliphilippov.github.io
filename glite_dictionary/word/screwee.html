screwee

1. NOUN

/ skruˈi / (AmE), / skruˈi / (BrE)
a person who is cheated, exploited, or taken advantage of in a situation or deal
person who is victimized or exploited
informal

Examples

Nobody wants to be the screwee in a business partnership.
Unfortunately, she ended up as the screwee in the apartment contract dispute.
Every scam needs both a scammer and a screwee to work.

Synset Examples

Nobody wants to be the screwee in a bad investment.
In this deal, he was the screwee and lost everything.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: victim - a person harmed or exploited in an event or situation
  • Antonym: screwer - a person who cheats or takes advantage of another

Etymology

Formed from 'screw' (slang sense: to cheat or treat unfairly) + suffix '-ee' (denoting the person affected).
