suricate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʊrɪˌkæt / (AmE), / ˈsjʊərɪˌkæt / (BrE)
A small, diurnal, burrowing mammal of southern Africa, also known as the meerkat, often kept as a pet or observed in the wild.
small burrowing mammal of southern Africa; meerkat

Examples

The suricate is well adapted to life in the arid regions of southern Africa.
At the zoo, the children enjoyed watching a playful suricate stand upright on its hind legs.
Unlike some pets, a suricate requires a special environment to thrive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meerkat - small, social mongoose native to southern Africa

Etymology

From French 'suricate', from Latin 'suricatta', of uncertain origin; also related to Afrikaans 'meerkat'.
