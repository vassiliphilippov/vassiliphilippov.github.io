Nahuatl

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːwɑːtəl / (AmE), / ˈnɑːwɑːtəl / (BrE)
a Uto-Aztecan language spoken by the Nahua people of central Mexico and surrounding regions
the language of the Nahua people

Examples

Many Aztec codices were originally written in Nahuatl.
She is studying Nahuatl to better understand ancient Mesoamerican cultures.
Some modern Mexican communities still speak Nahuatl as their primary language.

Etymology

From Nahuatl *nāhuatl* (clear speech).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːwɑːtəl / (AmE), / ˈnɑːwətəl / (BrE)
A member of any of the indigenous peoples of central Mexico, especially those descended from the Aztecs.
indigenous people of central Mexico

Examples

Many Nahuatl still live in villages throughout central Mexico.
A Nahuatl can trace their ancestry to the ancient Aztec civilization.
Some Nahuatl have preserved their traditional customs and language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aztec - a member of the indigenous people dominant in central Mexico before the Spanish conquest

Etymology

From Nahuatl *nāhuatl*, the name of the language and people.
