binate

1. ADJ

/ ˈbaɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
(of a leaf or other plant structure) arranged or growing in pairs; consisting of two parts.
in pairs; in twos (botany)

Examples

The plant displays binate leaves, each pair emerging from the stem at the same level.
Many ferns have binate fronds as a characteristic morphological feature.
The botanist noted the binate arrangement of flowers along the stalk.

Synset Examples

binate leaves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paired - existing or arranged in pairs

Etymology

From Latin 'binatus' (double or paired), from 'bini' (two by two).
