perfidy

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrfədi / (AmE), / ˈpɜːfɪdi / (BrE)
The act of deliberately betraying someone's trust; treachery or deceitfulness.
deliberate betrayal or treachery

Examples

The soldier's perfidy shocked even his enemies, who had expected some measure of honor in battle.
His reputation was forever tarnished by an act of perfidy against his closest friend.
History is filled with tales of perfidy that changed the fate of nations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treachery - betrayal of trust; deceptive action or nature
  • Antonym: loyalty - faithful adherence to a person, cause, or institution

Etymology

From Latin perfidia (faithlessness, treachery), from per- (to ill effect) + fides (faith).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrfɪdi / (AmE), / ˈpɜːfɪdi / (BrE)
The act of betraying someone or showing deliberate breach of trust; treachery.
betrayal of trust; treachery

Examples

Her act of perfidy left her friends feeling deeply wounded and betrayed.
History is full of tales of perfidy committed by those trusted with power.
Such blatant perfidy cannot go unpunished in a court of law.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treachery - betrayal of trust or faith
  • Antonym: fidelity - faithfulness or loyalty

Etymology

From Latin perfidia (faithlessness), from perfidus (treacherous), from per- (to ill effect) + fides (faith).
