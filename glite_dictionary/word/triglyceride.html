triglyceride

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈɡlɪs.əˌraɪd / (AmE), / traɪˈɡlɪs.ər.aɪd / (BrE)
a type of fat molecule consisting of three fatty acids bonded to a glycerol, found in animal and plant tissues, and serving as a major energy source in the body
type of fat molecule; major energy source
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that high triglyceride levels can increase the risk of heart disease.
Eating a balanced diet helped lower her triglyceride concentration in the blood.
Vegetable oils are rich in triglyceride molecules, which are important sources of energy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fat - a natural oily substance found in animal bodies
  • Synonym: cholesterol - a fatty substance in the blood related to triglyceride

Etymology

From tri- (three) + glyceride (compound derived from glycerol)
