aristocracy

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛrɪˈstɑːkrəsi / (AmE), / ˌærɪˈstɒkrəsi / (BrE)
The class of people in a society who hold hereditary titles or offices, often possessing special privileges and influence.
privileged hereditary class

Examples

During the revolution, the aristocracy was stripped of its privileges and influence.
Members of the aristocracy often owned large estates and held political power.
He came from the aristocracy, but chose to live a modest life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nobility - the group of people belonging to the noble class in a country

Etymology

From Greek aristokratia, from aristos (best) + kratos (power, rule).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛrəˈstɑkrəsi / (AmE), / ˌærɪˈstɒkrəsi / (BrE)
the highest social class in a particular society, usually made up of people who have titles or inherited wealth and power
highest social class; nobility

Examples

The aristocracy often held vast estates and wielded significant political power.
Members of the aristocracy were invited to the exclusive royal ball.
Despite his humble origins, he managed to marry into the aristocracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nobility - the group of people belonging to the highest social class
  • Antonym: commoners - the people who are not members of the aristocracy or nobility

Etymology

From Greek aristokratia, from aristos (best) + -kratia (power, rule).
