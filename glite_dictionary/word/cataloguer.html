cataloguer

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæt.əˌlɔɡ.ər / (AmE), / ˈkat.əl.ɒɡ.ə / (BrE)
a person, especially a librarian, who classifies and organizes publications or items according to a system of categories
person who classifies items in a system

Examples

The cataloguer spent hours organizing books into the new digital system.
As a junior cataloguer, she learned to assign subject headings accurately.
Every library needs a skilled cataloguer to ensure easy access to resources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: librarian - a person who works in or is in charge of a library

Etymology

From 'catalogue' + '-er', where 'catalogue' is from French 'catalogue' and Latin 'catalogus', from Greek 'katalogos' (a list, register).
