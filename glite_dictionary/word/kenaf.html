kenaf

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnæf / (AmE), / kəˈnɑːf / (BrE)
A tall annual plant (Hibiscus cannabinus) native to the East Indies and cultivated for its valuable fibrous stalks, used in making cordage, textiles, and paper.
fiber plant (Hibiscus cannabinus)

Examples

Kenaf is increasingly being used as a sustainable source of fiber for paper production.
Farmers in many countries have started growing kenaf because of its fast growth and versatility.
The stalks of the kenaf plant are harvested and processed to extract strong natural fibers.

Etymology

From Arabic *kanāb*, meaning 'hemp'.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈnæf / (AmE), / kəˈnæf / (BrE)
A valuable fiber obtained from the stalks of the tropical plant Hibiscus cannabinus, used in rope, twine, and other products.
fiber plant (Hibiscus cannabinus)

Examples

The paper was made from kenaf, a sustainable alternative to wood pulp.
Farmers in the region have begun cultivating kenaf as a cash crop.
Ropes made from kenaf are strong and environmentally friendly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jute - a long, soft, shiny vegetable fiber used for making twine and rope

Etymology

From Arabic 'kanaf', referring to Hibiscus cannabinus.
