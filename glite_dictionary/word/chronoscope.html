chronoscope

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊ.nəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈkrɒn.əˌskəʊp / (BrE)
an instrument used for precise measurement of very short time intervals
instrument for measuring brief time intervals

Examples

Scientists used a chronoscope to record the reaction times during the experiment.
The laboratory invested in a new chronoscope to improve the accuracy of their measurements.
Historically, the chronoscope was essential for studying the speed of nerve impulses.

Etymology

From Greek 'chronos' (time) + 'skopein' (to look at).
