offspring

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔfˌsprɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌsprɪŋ / (BrE)
A person's children or an animal's young; the immediate descendants of an individual.
a person's children or descendants

Examples

All three offspring attended their mother's birthday party together.
The lioness fiercely protected her offspring from danger.
Genetic traits can be inherited by an organism's offspring.

Synset Examples

she was the mother of many offspring
he died without issue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: descendant - a person descended from a particular ancestor
  • Antonym: ancestor - a person from whom one is descended

Etymology

Old English ofspring, from of (away, off) + springan (to spring).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːfˌsprɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌsprɪŋ / (BrE)
the young of a person, animal, or plant; a child or animal's young
an animal's or person's young

Examples

The lioness carefully protected her offspring from predators.
Tadpoles are the aquatic offspring of frogs before they become adults.
Farmers often keep the offspring of their best livestock to improve breeding stock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: progeny - descendants or offspring, especially collectively
  • Antonym: parent - an animal or person's father or mother

Etymology

Old English ofspring (descendant, offspring), from of- (away, off) + springan (to spring).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɑfˌsprɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɒfsprɪŋ / (BrE)
something that is produced as a result of a particular process, idea, or activity; a product or outcome.
product or result of something

Examples

The nation's constitution is the offspring of years of struggle and compromise.
Many modern genres of music are the creative offspring of earlier traditions.
This school was the offspring of a community effort to improve education.

Synset Examples

industrialism prepared the way for acceptance of the French Revolution's various socialistic offspring
this skyscraper is the solid materialization of his efforts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: product - something that is produced as a result of a process or action
  • Synonym: result - something that happens or exists because of something else
  • Antonym: cause - a person or thing that gives rise to an action, phenomenon, or condition

Etymology

Old English 'ofspring', from 'of' (off) + 'springan' (to spring).
