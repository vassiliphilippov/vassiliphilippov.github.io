ripened

1. ADJ

/ ˈraɪpənd / (AmE), / ˈraɪpənd / (BrE)
(of fruit, cheese, wine, etc.) having reached the right stage of development or maturity; fully developed or matured.
fully matured; developed to desired state

Examples

The ripened cheese had a much deeper and more complex flavor than the fresh variety.
He picked the ripened tomatoes directly off the vine for dinner.
Only ripened grapes are used in the production of this premium wine.

Synset Examples

mature well-aged cheeses

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unripe - not fully grown or developed
  • Synonym: mature - fully developed physically; ripe

Etymology

Past participle of 'ripen,' from Middle English ripen, related to 'ripe'.
