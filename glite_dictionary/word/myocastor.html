Myocastor

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈɑːkəstɔr / (AmE), / mʌɪˈɒkastɔː / (BrE)
A genus of large, semiaquatic rodents in the family Capromyidae, best known for the species nutria (Myocastor coypus).
genus of semiaquatic rodents including the nutria

Examples

The Myocastor genus includes the well-known nutria, which is native to South America.
Scientists are studying the habitat preferences of Myocastor populations in wetlands.
A member of Myocastor is often referred to as a coypu or nutria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nutria - large, semiaquatic rodent also known as coypu, member of Myocastor genus

Etymology

From Greek 'mys' (mouse) + 'kastor' (beaver).

2. NOUN

/ maɪ.oʊˈkæstɔr / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.əˈkæs.tɔː / (BrE)
A genus of large semiaquatic rodents, native to South America, formerly placed in the family Capromyidae; includes the coypu or nutria.
genus of South American rodents

Examples

The Myocastor genus includes only one living species, the coypu.
Scientists have studied Myocastor for its ecological impact as an invasive species.
The Myocastor skeleton is adapted to a semi-aquatic lifestyle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coypu - a large semiaquatic rodent (nutria), member of Myocastor

Etymology

From Greek myō (mouse) + kastōr (beaver), referring to its beaver-like appearance.
