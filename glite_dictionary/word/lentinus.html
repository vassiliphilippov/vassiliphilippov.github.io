Lentinus

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛn.tɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈlɛn.tɪ.nəs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Tricholomataceae, which includes several species of mushrooms found in woodlands.
genus of fungi (mushrooms)

Examples

The common shiitake mushroom was formerly classified under the genus Lentinus.
Many species within Lentinus decompose wood, playing an important role in forest ecosystems.
Researchers discovered a new medicinal compound in a recently described Lentinus species.

Etymology

From Latin 'lentinus', meaning pliant or flexible; used in scientific naming.

2. NOUN

/ lɛnˈtaɪnəs / (AmE), / lɛnˈtɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Tricholomataceae, which includes species such as the shiitake mushroom.
genus of fungi (mushrooms)

Examples

The species Lentinus edodes is commonly known as the shiitake mushroom.
Mycologists have discovered new species within the Lentinus genus in tropical regions.
The fruiting bodies of Lentinus are often cultivated for culinary purposes.

Etymology

From Latin 'lentinus' (pliable), used as the genus name in scientific classification.
