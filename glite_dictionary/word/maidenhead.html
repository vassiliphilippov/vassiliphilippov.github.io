maidenhead

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪdənˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈmeɪd(ə)nhɛd / (BrE)
the fold of tissue (hymen) that partly covers the entrance to the vagina, especially in a virgin; now chiefly archaic
the hymen; a vaginal fold in virgins (archaic)
archaic

Examples

In ancient literature, a woman's maidenhead was often regarded as a symbol of purity.
The physician explained that the maidenhead could be broken in various non-sexual ways.
She read a historical novel describing the significance society placed on a girl's maidenhead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hymen - the membrane partially closing the vaginal opening

Etymology

From Middle English *maidenhed*, derived from Old English *mægdenhād* (‘maidenhood, virginity’).
