Oriolus oriolus

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˈraɪələs ɔːˈraɪələs / (AmE), / ˌɔːrɪˈəʊləs ˌɔːrɪˈəʊləs / (BrE)
A brightly colored songbird native to Europe and western Asia, noted for its golden-yellow plumage and black wings; also known as the Eurasian golden oriole.
a brightly colored Eurasian songbird

Examples

During migration season, birdwatchers hope to catch a glimpse of the elusive Oriolus oriolus among the trees.
The male Oriolus oriolus is recognized by its vivid yellow body and melodious song.
Conservationists are studying the habitat changes affecting populations of Oriolus oriolus across Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden oriole - common name for Oriolus oriolus, the Eurasian golden oriole

Etymology

From Latin, the scientific name given by Linnaeus for the Eurasian golden oriole.
