vibratory

1. ADJ

/ ˈvaɪbrəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈvɪbrətəri / (BrE)
moving rapidly back and forth or up and down; relating to vibration
relating to vibration or rapid movement

Examples

The engineer adjusted the vibratory mechanism to ensure smoother operation of the machine.
Many musical instruments produce sound through vibratory motion of strings or membranes.
Researchers observed the vibratory patterns created by the tuning fork in the experiment.

Synset Examples

the vibrating piano strings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vibrating - moving continuously and rapidly back and forth
  • Antonym: still - not moving; motionless

Etymology

From Latin vibrāre (to shake, move quickly) + -ory (relating to).
