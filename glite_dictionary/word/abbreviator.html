abbreviator

1. NOUN

/ əˈbriviˌeɪtər / (AmE), / əˈbriːvɪeɪtə / (BrE)
a person who shortens, abridges, or condenses a written work
someone who condenses or shortens texts

Examples

As the official abbreviator, she condensed lengthy reports into concise summaries.
The book's abbreviator skillfully captured the essence of the original work.
Before publishing, the manuscript was reviewed by an experienced abbreviator to ensure clarity and brevity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abridger - person who makes abridgments, especially of books

Etymology

From Latin abbreviātor, from abbreviāre (to shorten).
