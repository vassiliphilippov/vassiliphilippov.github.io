conspecific

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːn.spəˈsɪf.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.spɪˈsɪf.ɪk / (BrE)
belonging to or coming from the same species; of the same species.
belonging to the same species

Examples

The two populations of birds are considered conspecific despite their different feather colors.
Researchers studied the interactions between conspecific individuals in the primate group.
Cabbage and its wild relatives are conspecific, sharing the same species classification.

Synset Examples

cultivated cabbage and wild cabbage are conspecific

Related Terms

  • Antonym: heterospecific - belonging to a different species

Etymology

From Latin con- (together) + specificus (of a kind, species).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnsəˈspɪsɪk / (AmE), / ˌkɒnsɪˈspɪsɪk / (BrE)
an organism belonging to the same species as another organism
organism of the same species

Examples

Male birds often compete more fiercely with conspecifics than with members of other species.
The researchers observed grooming behavior among conspecifics in the primate group.
Aggressive interactions between conspecific individuals were recorded during the study.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: heterospecific - an organism belonging to a different species

Etymology

From Latin 'con-' (together) + 'specificus' (of a kind/species).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01316517-n
Synset POS: n
