archosaurian

1. ADJ

/ ˌɑːr.kəˈsɔːr.i.ən / (AmE), / ˌɑː.kəˈsɔː.ri.ən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of reptiles belonging to the subclass Archosauria, which includes crocodiles, dinosaurs, and pterosaurs.
relating to archosaur reptiles

Examples

Many paleontologists specialize in the study of archosaurian fossils to understand prehistoric life.
Modern crocodiles are considered to be archosaurian reptiles, closely related to ancient species.
The archosaurian lineage includes both flying pterosaurs and giant dinosaurs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archosaur - a reptile of the subclass Archosauria

Etymology

From Greek 'archos' (ruler) + 'sauros' (lizard).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrkəˈsɔriən / (AmE), / ˌɑːkəˈsɔːriən / (BrE)
Any member of a large group of extinct reptiles that includes dinosaurs, plesiosaurs, pterosaurs, ichthyosaurs, and thecodonts.
member of a group of extinct reptiles

Examples

Fossil evidence suggests that archosaurians were the dominant terrestrial vertebrates during the Mesozoic Era.
Scientists believe the first birds evolved from small, feathered archosaurians.
The study compared the bone structures of modern crocodiles and their ancient archosaurian relatives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archosaur - Any reptile of the group Archosauria, which includes crocodiles, pterosaurs, and dinosaurs.

Etymology

From Greek arkhos (chief, ruler) + sauros (lizard).
