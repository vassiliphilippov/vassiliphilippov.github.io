nopal

1. NOUN

/ noʊˈpɑl / (AmE), / ˈnəʊpal / (BrE)
A type of cactus with yellow flowers and purple edible fruits, widely used in Mexican cuisine and agriculture.
a cactus with edible fruits and pads

Examples

The nopal is a staple ingredient in many traditional Mexican dishes.
She bought fresh nopal from the market to make a healthy salad.
Farmers cultivate nopal both for its nutritious pads and colorful fruits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prickly pear - a cactus bearing sweet, flat, edible pads and fruit

Etymology

From Spanish 'nopal', from Nahuatl 'nohpalli' (prickly pear cactus pad).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊpæl / (AmE), / ˈnəʊpal / (BrE)
A type of cactus of the genus Nopalea, especially one resembling the prickly pear, native to Mexico and the southwestern United States, often used as a food.
a cactus of the genus Nopalea

Examples

The chef added slices of nopal to the salad for a unique texture and flavor.
Farmers in Mexico often cultivate nopal as both a crop and a natural fence.
In many markets, you can find fresh nopal pads for traditional dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prickly pear - a cactus with edible fruit and pads, genus Opuntia

Etymology

From Spanish 'nopal', from Nahuatl 'nohpalli' (prickly pear cactus pad).
