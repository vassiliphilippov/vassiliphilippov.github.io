Blenniidae

1. NOUN

/ blɛˈnɪ.iːˌdeɪ / (AmE), / blɛˈnɪ.iːˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of small marine and freshwater fish, commonly known as combtooth blennies, characterized by elongated bodies and comb-like teeth
family of combtooth blenny fish

Examples

The Blenniidae are known for their unique comb-like teeth and ability to live in rocky coastal environments.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Blenniidae family in the tropical Pacific.
Many members of the Blenniidae display vibrant colors to attract mates or warn predators.

Etymology

From Greek blennos (mucus, referring to their slimy coating) + -idae, the zoological family suffix.
