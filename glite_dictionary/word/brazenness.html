brazenness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪznɪs / (AmE), / ˈbreɪznɪs / (BrE)
shameless boldness or defiant behavior without regard for propriety
shameless boldness; lack of shame

Examples

Her brazenness during the interrogation shocked even the experienced detectives.
The brazenness of his lie left everyone speechless.
She admired his brazenness in confronting authority without fear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boldness - willingness to take risks or act innovatively; confidence or courage
  • Antonym: timidity - lack of courage or confidence; shyness

Etymology

From 'brazen' (old English origin, meaning 'made of brass' and later 'shameless').
