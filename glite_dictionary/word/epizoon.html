epizoon

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈzuːɑn / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈzuːɒn / (BrE)
an external parasitic organism that lives on the surface of another animal, such as a flea or louse
external parasitic organism

Examples

Fleas are a common type of epizoon found on domestic animals.
The veterinarian explained that the rash was caused by an epizoon living on the cat's skin.
Many birds preen themselves to remove epizoon that may inhabit their feathers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: endoparasite - a parasite that lives inside the body of its host
  • Synonym: ectoparasite - a parasite that lives on the external surface of the host

Etymology

From Greek epi- (upon) + zōon (animal).
