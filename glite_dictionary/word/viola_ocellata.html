Viola ocellata

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈoʊlə oʊˈsɛlətə / (AmE), / vaɪˈəʊlə əʊˈsɛlətə / (BrE)
A violet plant native to the Pacific coast of North America, with white petals tinged with yellow and deep violet.
violet native to Pacific coast of North America

Examples

The Viola ocellata blooms in early spring along shaded woodland trails.
Botanists studied the unique markings on the petals of Viola ocellata.
Gardeners appreciate Viola ocellata for its delicate white and yellow-tinged flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violet - a small plant with purple, blue, or white flowers

Etymology

From Latin: 'Viola' meaning violet, 'ocellata' meaning 'with little eyes' (referring to petal markings).
