allegorical

1. ADJ

/ ˌæləˈɡɔrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌalɪˈɡɒrɪkəl / (BrE)
(of a work of art, story, or idea) containing or using allegory; having a hidden moral or political meaning.
containing or using allegory

Examples

The painting is highly allegorical, representing justice as a blindfolded woman.
Her novel takes an allegorical approach to the theme of freedom.
Many fables are allegorical, using animals to convey moral lessons.

Synset Examples

allegorical stories
an allegorical painting of Victory leading an army

Related Terms

  • Antonym: literal - Being the primary or explicit meaning; not allegorical or figurative
  • Synonym: symbolic - Using symbols to represent ideas or qualities

Etymology

From Medieval Latin allegoricus, from Greek allēgorikos (allegorical).
