centrism

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrɪzəm / (BrE)
a political philosophy that seeks to avoid extremes by adopting moderate positions between the left and right
moderate political philosophy

Examples

Many voters are attracted to centrism because they prefer balance over ideological extremes.
The party's new leader emphasized centrism to appeal to a broader electorate.
Critics argue that centrism sometimes results in indecisive policies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moderation - the quality of avoiding extremes, especially in politics
  • Antonym: extremism - the holding of extreme political or religious views

Etymology

From centre + -ism, modelled on words like socialism and conservatism.
