samarium

1. NOUN

/ səˈmɛriəm / (AmE), / səˈmeəriəm / (BrE)
a grey metallic element of the rare earth group, used in special alloys and found in minerals such as monazite and bastnasite
a rare earth metallic element

Examples

Scientists added samarium to the alloy to improve its magnetic properties.
Monazite is an important source of samarium and other rare earth elements.
The samarium sample was stored in a sealed container to prevent oxidation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lanthanum - a soft, silvery-white rare earth metal element
  • Synonym: europium - a rare metallic element of the lanthanide series

Etymology

Named after Russian mine official Vasili Samarsky-Bykhovets.
