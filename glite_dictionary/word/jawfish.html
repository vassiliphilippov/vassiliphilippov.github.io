jawfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɔˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A small tropical marine fish with a large mouth, found along sandy bottoms, where the male carries eggs in its mouth until they hatch; popular in aquariums.
tropical marine fish with large mouth; males brood eggs in mouth

Examples

The aquarium display featured a colorful jawfish building its burrow in the sand.
Male jawfish can be seen carrying eggs in their mouths until they hatch.
Many enthusiasts keep jawfish because of their fascinating brooding behavior.
