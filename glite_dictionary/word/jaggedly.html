jaggedly

1. ADV

/ ˈdʒæɡɪdli / (AmE), / ˈdʒæɡɪdli / (BrE)
in a manner that is rough, uneven, or with sharp points
in a rough or uneven way

Examples

The mountain peaks rose jaggedly against the evening sky, silhouetted in sharp contrast.
She tore the paper jaggedly, leaving uneven edges all around.
Icicles hung jaggedly from the roof after the winter storm.

Synset Examples

a long beard, raggedly cut

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smoothly - in a way that is even and without roughness
  • Synonym: raggedly - in a rough, irregular, or uneven manner

Etymology

From 'jagged' (with sharp projections) + '-ly' (adverbial suffix).
