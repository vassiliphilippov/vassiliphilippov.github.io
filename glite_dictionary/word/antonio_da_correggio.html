Antonio da Correggio

1. PROPN

/ ænˈtoʊnioʊ də kɔːrˈɛdʒioʊ / (AmE), / ænˈtəʊniəʊ də kɒrˈɛdʒiəʊ / (BrE)
(1489–1534) Italian Renaissance painter noted for his use of chiaroscuro and perspective.
Italian Renaissance painter (1489–1534)

Examples

Antonio da Correggio is celebrated for his luminous frescoes in the Cathedral of Parma.
Art historians often compare the use of light in Antonio da Correggio's works to that of his contemporaries.
The emotional intensity in Antonio da Correggio's paintings influenced many later Baroque artists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Leonardo da Vinci - Renowned Italian Renaissance artist and inventor
  • Synonym: Raphael - Italian Renaissance painter and architect

Etymology

From Italian, referring to the painter Antonio Allegri da Correggio, named after his birthplace, Correggio.
