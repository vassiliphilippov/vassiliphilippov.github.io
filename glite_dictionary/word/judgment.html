judgment

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌdʒ.mənt / (BrE)
the ability to make considered decisions or form sensible opinions, especially by understanding and distinguishing between different options or relations
the ability to make considered decisions

Examples

Her judgment in choosing the best candidate was highly respected by her colleagues.
Good judgment is essential when making important life decisions.
He regretted his poor judgment in trusting someone he barely knew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discernment - the ability to judge well, especially in matters of taste or perception
  • Synonym: wisdom - the quality of having experience, knowledge, and good judgment
  • Antonym: foolishness - lack of good sense or judgment

Etymology

From Old French jugement, from Latin judicium (judgment, opinion).
