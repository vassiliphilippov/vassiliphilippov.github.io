Postimpressionist

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊst.ɪmˈprɛʃ.ə.nɪst / (AmE), / ˌpəʊst.ɪmˈprɛʃ.ə.nɪst / (BrE)
an artist who belonged to the Postimpressionist movement and reacted against the methods and aims of Impressionism, typically in late 19th-century France
artist of the Postimpressionist movement

Examples

Vincent van Gogh is widely considered a leading Postimpressionist.
The exhibition presents works by several notable Postimpressionists from across Europe.
After studying Impressionism, she became inspired by the bold colors of the Postimpressionists.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Impressionist - an artist of the Impressionist movement

Etymology

From post- (after) + Impressionist, referring to the movement following Impressionism in art.
