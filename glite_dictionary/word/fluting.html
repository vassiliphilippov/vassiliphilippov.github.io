fluting

1. NOUN

/ ˈfluːtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfluːtɪŋ / (BrE)
A series of shallow, concave grooves running vertically along a surface, especially as a decorative feature on the shaft of a column or in fabric.
shallow grooves on columns or fabric

Examples

The marble columns were ornamented with fluting that ran their entire length.
Careful fluting on the dress gave it a sophisticated texture and appearance.
The architect chose a simple design with minimal fluting for a modern aesthetic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: groove - a long, narrow cut or depression in a surface

Etymology

From 'flute', referring to a grooved pipe or channel; architectural usage dates to the 17th century.
