diarrhoea

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈɛriə / (AmE), / daɪəˈriːə / (BrE)
A condition in which a person has frequent and watery bowel movements, often as a symptom of infection or digestive disturbance.
frequent, watery bowel movements
medical british

Examples

If you experience diarrhoea for more than two days, you should consult a doctor.
Travellers are often advised about the risk of diarrhoea when visiting certain countries.
The child developed diarrhoea after eating contaminated food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diarrhea - frequent and watery bowel movements (American spelling)
  • Antonym: constipation - difficulty in emptying the bowels, usually associated with hardened feces

Etymology

From Greek *diarrhoia*, from *dia-* ‘through’ + *rhein* ‘to flow’, via Late Latin and Middle English.
