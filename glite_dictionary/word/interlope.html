to interlope

1. VERB

/ ˌɪntərˈloʊp / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈləʊp / (BrE)
to intrude or encroach on the rights or privileges of others, especially by engaging unlawfully in business or trade
to intrude or encroach, especially in business

Examples

Unauthorized traders would often interlope in restricted markets, undermining official merchants.
She warned him not to interlope in their family's affairs.
The government imposed strict penalties on those who interloped without a license.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intrude - to push oneself into a situation or place without invitation or permission
  • Synonym: to encroach - to gradually take or use someone else's rights or possessions

Etymology

From interloper (person who intrudes), from Dutch *interlooper* (intruder).
