methionine

1. NOUN

/ məˈθaɪ.əˌnin / (AmE), / məˈθaɪ.əniːn / (BrE)
a sulfur-containing essential amino acid found in most proteins, necessary for human nutrition
essential sulfur-containing amino acid
medical

Examples

Foods rich in methionine support healthy growth and tissue repair.
Scientists study the role of methionine in various metabolic processes.
Eggs and fish are good dietary sources of methionine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amino acid - an organic compound forming the building blocks of proteins

Etymology

From French méthionine, from methyl (referring to its chemical group) and Greek theíōn (sulfur).
