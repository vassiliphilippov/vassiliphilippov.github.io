nevertheless

1. ADV

/ ˌnɛvərðəˈlɛs / (AmE), / ˌnɛvəðəˈlɛs / (BrE)
in spite of what has just been said; despite that
in spite of that; despite that

Examples

The weather was cold and rainy; nevertheless, the event continued as planned.
She was feeling exhausted; nevertheless, she finished the project on time.
The plan was risky. Nevertheless, everyone agreed to proceed.

Synset Examples

although I'm a little afraid, however I'd like to try it
while we disliked each other, nevertheless we agreed
he was a stern yet fair master
granted that it is dangerous, all the same I still want to go

Related Terms

  • Synonym: however - used to introduce a statement that contrasts with or seems to contradict something that has been said previously
  • Synonym: nonetheless - in spite of that; nevertheless

Etymology

From Middle English 'never the less', a phrase meaning 'notwithstanding'.
