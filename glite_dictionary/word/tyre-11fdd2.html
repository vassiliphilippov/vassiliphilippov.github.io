tyre

1. NOUN

/ taɪər / (AmE), / taɪə / (BrE)
A thick rubber covering, typically filled with air, that fits around the outer edge of a wheel on a vehicle to provide traction and cushion the ride.
rubber covering on a wheel
british

Examples

She noticed that the front tyre of her bicycle was flat after riding over a sharp stone.
It's important to check your car's tyre pressure regularly for safe driving.
The mechanic replaced the worn tyre with a new one before the long journey.

Synset Examples

automobile tires are usually made of rubber and filled with compressed air

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tire - rubber covering on a wheel (American English spelling)

Etymology

From Middle English tire (equipment, attire), later specialized to mean 'wheel covering.'
