Pseudotsuga menziesii

1. NOUN

/ suːdoʊtˈsuːɡə mɛnˈziːzi.aɪ / (AmE), / sjuːdəʊtˈsuːɡə mɛnˈziːzi.aɪ / (BrE)
A tall evergreen conifer native to northwestern North America, known as the Douglas fir, with short needles and distinctive egg-shaped cones.
species of Douglas fir tree

Examples

The forests of the Pacific Northwest are dominated by Pseudotsuga menziesii, also known as Douglas fir.
Botanists often study the growth rings of Pseudotsuga menziesii to understand climate patterns.
Mature Pseudotsuga menziesii trees can reach impressive heights in their natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Douglas fir - the common name for Pseudotsuga menziesii, a coniferous tree species

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'pseudes' (false) + 'Tsouga' (Japanese for hemlock) and honoring Scottish botanist Archibald Menzies.
