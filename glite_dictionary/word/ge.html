Ge

1. PROPN

/ dʒi / (AmE), / dʒiː / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the goddess of the earth and mother of Cronus and the Titans.
goddess of the earth in Greek mythology

Examples

Many ancient myths describe Ge as the primeval mother of all gods and creatures.
In classical artwork, Ge is often depicted emerging from the earth.
The worship of Ge highlights the importance of nature in ancient Greek religion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gaia - Greek goddess of the earth, another name for Ge

Etymology

From Greek Γῆ (Gē), meaning 'earth'.

2. NOUN

/ dʒi / (AmE), / dʒiː / (BrE)
the chemical symbol for germanium, a brittle grey semiconducting metalloid used in transistors and electronics
chemical symbol for germanium

Examples

High-purity Ge is an essential component in some types of infrared optics.
Scientists use Ge wafers to manufacture certain transistors and diodes.
The mineral argyrodite contains significant amounts of Ge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: germanium - a brittle grey crystalline element (symbol Ge), used as a semiconductor

Etymology

Abbreviation of Germanium, named after Germany (Latin: Germania).
