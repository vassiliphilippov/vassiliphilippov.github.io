cothromboplastin

1. NOUN

/ koʊˌθrɒmboʊˈplæstɪn / (AmE), / kəʊˌθrɒmbəʊˈplæstɪn / (BrE)
a coagulation factor produced in the kidney that plays a role in blood clotting and is formed under the influence of vitamin K
kidney-derived blood clotting factor
medical

Examples

Researchers studied the effect of vitamin K on cothromboplastin levels in patients with kidney disorders.
A deficiency in cothromboplastin can lead to abnormal blood clotting.
The kidney releases cothromboplastin to help maintain proper coagulation.

Etymology

From co- (together) + thromboplastin (a substance involved in blood clotting).
