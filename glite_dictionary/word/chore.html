chore

1. NOUN

/ tʃɔr / (AmE), / tʃɔː / (BrE)
a task, especially one that is routine or unpleasant, that must be done as part of one’s duties at work or at home
routine or necessary task, especially a duty

Examples

Every Saturday morning, the children are expected to complete their chores before watching television.
She found the daily chore of washing dishes to be monotonous but necessary.
Mowing the lawn is a chore I usually put off until the weekend.

Synset Examples

estimates of the city's loss on that job ranged as high as a million dollars
the job of repairing the engine took several hours
the endless task of classifying the samples
the farmer's morning chores

Related Terms

  • Synonym: task - an assigned piece of work to be finished within a certain time
  • Synonym: job - a piece of work, especially a specific task to be done
  • Antonym: pleasure - something done for enjoyment rather than duty

Etymology

From mid-18th century English, dialect variant of 'char', meaning a turn of work.
