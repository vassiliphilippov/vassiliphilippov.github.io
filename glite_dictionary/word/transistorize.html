to transistorize

1. VERB

/ trænˈzɪstɚˌaɪz / (AmE), / trænˈzɪstəˌaɪz / (BrE)
to equip (an electronic circuit or device) with transistors, often in place of earlier components such as vacuum tubes
equip with transistors

Examples

Many radio manufacturers chose to transistorize their products in the 1960s for better performance.
The engineer plans to transistorize the old amplifier to improve its efficiency.
After they transistorized the circuit, it became much smaller and more reliable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transistor - a semiconductor device used to amplify or switch electronic signals

Etymology

From 'transistor' and the suffix '-ize', meaning 'to make or become.'
