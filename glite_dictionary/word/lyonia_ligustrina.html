Lyonia ligustrina

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈoʊniə lɪɡʌsˈtriːnə / (AmE), / laɪˈəʊniə lɪɡʌsˈtriːnə / (BrE)
A species of deciduous shrub in the heath family, bearing dense clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers.
species of deciduous shrub with white flowers

Examples

Lyonia ligustrina produces dense clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring.
The wetland restoration plan included planting Lyonia ligustrina to support native wildlife.
Botanists studied the leaf structure of Lyonia ligustrina to better understand its adaptation to acidic soils.

Etymology

From the genus name Lyonia, named after John Lyon, a Scottish botanist, and the species epithet ligustrina, meaning 'privet-like' in Latin.
