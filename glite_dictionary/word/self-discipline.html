self-discipline

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlfˈdɪsəplɪn / (AmE), / ˌselfˈdɪsəplɪn / (BrE)
The ability to control one's feelings and overcome one's weaknesses; the capacity to pursue what one thinks is right despite temptations to abandon it.
ability to control one's impulses or desires

Examples

It takes a great deal of self-discipline to stick to a strict diet.
She attributes her success to her self-discipline and determination.
Without self-discipline, it's easy to be distracted from your long-term goals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-control - ability to restrain one's impulses or actions

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' + 'discipline'; 'discipline' from Latin disciplina (instruction, training).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlfˈdɪsəplɪn / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈdɪsɪplɪn / (BrE)
the ability to control one's own behavior and feelings, especially in difficult situations, without needing help from others
ability to control oneself

Examples

She admired his self-discipline when he refused the cake at the party.
Training for a marathon takes a lot of self-discipline and commitment.
With enough self-discipline, you can stick to your goals even when it's difficult.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-control - the ability to control one's emotions and desires

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' and 'discipline', with 'discipline' from Latin 'disciplina' (instruction, knowledge).

Synset POS: n
