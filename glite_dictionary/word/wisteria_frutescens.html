Wisteria frutescens

1. NOUN

/ wɪˈstɪəriə fruˈtɛsəns / (AmE), / wɪˈstɪəriə fruˈtesəns / (BrE)
A species of wisteria native to the eastern United States, known for its pale purple-lilac flowers.
North American wisteria species

Examples

The Wisteria frutescens in her garden bloomed with clusters of delicate, pale purple flowers.
Unlike the Asian wisteria, Wisteria frutescens is less invasive and better suited to North American climates.
Botanists recommend Wisteria frutescens for native plant gardens because of its resilience and beauty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wisteria - a genus of climbing shrubs with pendulous clusters of flowers

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Wisteria' after American anatomist Caspar Wistar; 'frutescens' from Latin fruticosus (shrubby).
