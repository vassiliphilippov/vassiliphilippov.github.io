connoisseurship

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnəˈsɜːrʃɪp / (AmE), / ˌkɒnəˈsɜːʃɪp / (BrE)
Expertise or refined appreciation and judgment in matters of art, taste, or fine objects.
expertise in art or taste

Examples

Her connoisseurship of modern art impressed everyone at the gallery opening.
Years of travel in Europe deepened his connoisseurship in fine wines and rare antiques.
The museum values staff members with real connoisseurship in Asian ceramics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expertise - Expert skill or knowledge in a particular field
  • Antonym: amateurism - Lack of professional skill or expertise

Etymology

From French 'connoisseur' (expert, judge) + '-ship' (state or condition).
