Pyxis

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɪk.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈpɪk.sɪs / (BrE)
a faint constellation in the southern sky, located near Puppis and Antlia
constellation in the southern sky

Examples

The constellation Pyxis is best seen in the Southern Hemisphere during late winter.
Astronomers identified Pyxis close to the constellations Puppis and Antlia.
She used a star chart to locate Pyxis among the other faint constellations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Puppis - constellation in the southern sky adjacent to Pyxis
  • Synonym: Antlia - constellation in the southern sky near Pyxis

Etymology

From Latin pyxis (box), referring to a mariner's compass box.
