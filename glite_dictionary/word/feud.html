feud

1. NOUN

/ fjud / (AmE), / fjuːd / (BrE)
A long and bitter quarrel or conflict between two people, families, or groups.
a long-standing bitter quarrel

Examples

The family feud lasted for generations and caused a deep rift in the community.
They tried to end the bitter feud through mediation, but neither side would compromise.
The two companies have been locked in a legal feud over the patent for years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conflict - a serious disagreement or argument
  • Antonym: reconciliation - the restoration of friendly relations

Etymology

From Old French *feide* (hostility, enmity), from Medieval Latin *faida*.

2. VERB

/ fjud / (AmE), / fjuːd / (BrE)
to have a long and bitter quarrel or dispute with someone
engage in a lengthy quarrel

Examples

The two families have been feuding over the land for decades.
Rival politicians often feud in public, fueling media headlines.
They feuded so fiercely that their friends started taking sides.

Synset Examples

The two professors have been feuding for years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarrel - to have an argument or disagreement
  • Antonym: to reconcile - to restore friendly relations

Etymology

From Old French *feide* (hostility, animosity), from Latin *fides* (faith, trust), later associated with Old English *fæhth* (enmity).
