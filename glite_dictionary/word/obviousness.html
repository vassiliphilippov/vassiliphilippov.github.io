obviousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑb.vi.əs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɒb.vi.əs.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being easy to see, recognize, or understand; the condition of being obvious.
the quality of being obvious

Examples

The obviousness of the solution surprised the entire research team.
She was frustrated by the obviousness of the error, which everyone else had missed.
His presentation lacked obviousness, making it difficult for the audience to follow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clarity - the quality of being clear; easy to understand
  • Antonym: subtlety - the quality of being subtle, not obvious or easily noticed

Etymology

From 'obvious' + '-ness'; 'obvious' from Latin 'obvius' (in the way).
