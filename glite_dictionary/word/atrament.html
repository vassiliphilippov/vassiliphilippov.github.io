atrament

1. NOUN

/ ˈætrəˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈatrəmənt / (BrE)
A very black or deep ink used in writing or drawing.
deep black ink for writing
archaic

Examples

The ancient manuscript was written in atrament on fine parchment.
The artist insisted on using traditional atrament for his calligraphy.
She marveled at the rich hue of the atrament covering the old documents.

Synset Examples

He admired the atrament of the ink used in the calligraphy.

Etymology

From Latin 'atramentum' (black liquid, ink), from 'ater' (black, dark).
