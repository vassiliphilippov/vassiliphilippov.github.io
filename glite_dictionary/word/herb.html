herb

1. NOUN

/ hɝːb / (AmE), / hɜːb / (BrE)
a plant that does not have a permanent woody stem and often dies down to the ground after flowering; many herbs are cultivated for their flowers, leaves, or medicinal properties.
non-woody flowering plant

Examples

A dandelion is a common herb found in many gardens.
Some gardeners grow herbs for their medicinal benefits as well as their beauty.
This region's native plants include a variety of flowering herbs and wildflowers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shrub - a woody plant smaller than a tree, often with multiple stems

Etymology

From Latin 'herba' (grass, green crops, herb).

2. NOUN

/ hɜrb / (AmE), / hɜːb / (BrE)
A plant or part of a plant, such as leaves or stems, valued for its flavor, scent, or medicinal properties, especially one used to add flavor to food.
aromatic plant used in cooking

Examples

She sprinkled fresh herbs like basil and parsley over the pasta before serving.
This soup is flavored with a mixture of dried herbs and spices.
Growing your own herbs at home is both practical and enjoyable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spice - aromatic substance from plants used to flavor food

Etymology

From Old French 'erbe', from Latin 'herba' (grass, herb).

Synset POS: n
