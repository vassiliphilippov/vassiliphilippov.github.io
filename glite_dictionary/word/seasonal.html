seasonal

1. ADJ

/ ˈsiːzənəl / (AmE), / ˈsiːzənəl / (BrE)
happening or needed only at a particular time of year; relating to or dependent on a season.
occurring at or relating to a season

Examples

The hotel offers seasonal discounts during the off-peak months.
Many farm workers rely on seasonal employment to earn a living.
There was a seasonal increase in tourism during the summer.

Synset Examples

seasonal labor
a seasonal rise in unemployment

Related Terms

  • Antonym: perennial - lasting or existing throughout the year or for a long time
  • Degree Lower: temporary - lasting for only a limited period; not permanent

Etymology

From 'season' + -al, first recorded in the 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsiːzənl̩ / (AmE), / ˈsiːzənəl / (BrE)
a worker employed only during a particular season or part of the year, especially in industries such as agriculture or tourism
worker employed for part of the year

Examples

The farm relies on a team of seasonals each harvest to pick the crops.
Each summer, the resort hires dozens of seasonals to accommodate the influx of tourists.
Many seasonals return year after year for temporary work in the vineyards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seasonal worker - a worker who is employed only during certain seasons
