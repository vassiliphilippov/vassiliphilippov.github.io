deponent

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈpoʊnənt / (AmE), / dɪˈpəʊnənt / (BrE)
a person who gives sworn evidence, especially in the form of a written statement (deposition), typically in legal proceedings.
person giving sworn legal testimony
legal

Examples

The deponent answered all of the lawyer's questions during the deposition.
Counsel reminded the deponent to tell the truth under oath.
As a deponent, she provided critical information about the contract dispute.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witness - person who gives evidence in court or a legal proceeding

Etymology

From Latin 'deponens', present participle of 'deponere' (to put down, declare).
