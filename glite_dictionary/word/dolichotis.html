Dolichotis

1. NOUN

/ ˌdoʊlɪˈkoʊtɪs / (AmE), / ˌdɒlɪˈkəʊtɪs / (BrE)
A genus of large South American rodents commonly known as maras, resembling hares.
genus of South American rodents (maras)

Examples

The Dolichotis is found throughout the grasslands of Argentina.
Zookeepers introduced a new pair of Dolichotis to the South American exhibit.
Unlike most rodents, Dolichotis have long legs suited for running.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mara - a large South American rodent resembling a rabbit or hare

Etymology

From Greek dolikhos (long) + ous, ōtos (ear) for their long ears.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdoʊlɪˈkoʊtɪs / (AmE), / ˌdɒlɪˈkəʊtɪs / (BrE)
A genus of large rodents native to South America, commonly known as maras.
a genus of South American rodents (maras)

Examples

The Dolichotis genus includes the Patagonian mara, a large rodent found in Argentina.
Scientists observed a group of Dolichotis feeding in the grasslands.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect Dolichotis from habitat loss.

Etymology

From Greek 'dolikhos' (long) and 'ous, otos' (ear), referring to their long ears.
