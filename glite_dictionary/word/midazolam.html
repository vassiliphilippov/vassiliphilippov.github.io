midazolam

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈdæzəlæm / (AmE), / mɪˈdazəlæm / (BrE)
a sedative drug of the benzodiazepine class, used especially for sedation before or during medical procedures
sedative drug used for medical sedation
medical

Examples

The doctor administered midazolam before the procedure to help the patient relax.
During minor surgery, midazolam is commonly used for conscious sedation.
Patients receiving midazolam are carefully monitored for possible side effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Versed - trade name for midazolam, a sedative drug
  • Synonym: diazepam - another benzodiazepine sedative drug

Etymology

From the chemical name: 'imidazole' (a chemical structure) plus 'benzodiazepine.'
