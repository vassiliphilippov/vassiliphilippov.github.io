Convallaria

1. NOUN

/ kənˌvæl.əˈɹiː.ə / (AmE), / kɒnˌvæl.əˈɹɪə / (BrE)
a genus of perennial flowering plants, commonly known as lily of the valley, sometimes placed in the family Convallariaceae
genus of plants; lily of the valley

Examples

Convallaria is best known for its fragrant bell-shaped flowers commonly called lily of the valley.
The shady woodland garden was filled with clusters of Convallaria in spring.
Botanists debated whether Convallaria should remain in the Convallariaceae or be placed in another family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lily of the valley - a woodland flowering plant with bell-shaped white flowers in the Convallaria genus

Etymology

From Latin *convallaria* (plant growing in a valley), from *convallis* (valley).

2. NOUN

/ kənˌvæləˈlɛriə / (AmE), / kɒnˌvæləˈlɪəriə / (BrE)
A genus of perennial flowering plants in the asparagus family, commonly known for the lily of the valley.
plant genus containing lily of the valley

Examples

The delicate scent of Convallaria filled the springtime garden.
Botanists have classified the lily of the valley under the genus Convallaria.
My grandmother grew Convallaria in the shady part of her yard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lily of the valley - a fragrant flowering plant of the genus Convallaria

Etymology

From Latin *convallis* (valley), referring to the plant's typical habitat.
