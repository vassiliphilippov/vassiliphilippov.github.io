ill-famed

1. ADJ

/ ɪl feɪmd / (AmE), / ɪl feɪmd / (BrE)
Having a bad reputation; widely known for negative reasons; notorious or infamous.
having a bad reputation

Examples

The old saloon was once ill-famed for its rowdy crowds and illegal activities.
She avoided the ill-famed parts of town after dark for her own safety.
The pirate became ill-famed throughout the colonies for his ruthless raids.

Synset Examples

a notorious gangster
the tenderloin district was notorious for vice
the infamous Benedict Arnold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notorious - well known for some bad quality or deed
  • Synonym: infamous - well known for a shocking, wicked, or disgraceful act

Etymology

Formed by combining 'ill' (bad) and 'famed' (well-known).
