weeping

1. ADJ

/ ˈwiːpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwiːpɪŋ / (BrE)
showing sorrow, especially through tears or a sorrowful expression.
showing sorrow or sadness

Examples

Her weeping eyes told the story of her heartache.
We passed a weeping mother in the hospital corridor.
He stood there, a weeping figure barely able to speak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crying - showing or shedding tears, usually as an expression of emotion

Etymology

Present participle of 'weep', from Old English 'wēpan' (to weep).

2. ADJ

/ ˈwiːpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwiːpɪŋ / (BrE)
(of branches, flowers, or plants) having parts that hang or bend downward, often gracefully.
with branches or flowers hanging downward

Examples

The garden features a beautiful weeping cherry tree near the pond.
Many parks plant weeping willows for their elegant, trailing branches.
This variety of maple is popular for its weeping habit and graceful appearance.

Synset Examples

nodding daffodils
the pendulous branches of a weeping willow
lilacs with drooping panicles of fragrant flowers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drooping - hanging or bending downward
  • Synonym: pendulous - hanging down loosely, especially in a way that suggests heaviness or fullness

Etymology

Derived from the verb 'weep', in reference to the drooping form resembling the appearance of weeping.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01241359-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˈwiːpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwiːpɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of shedding tears, often accompanied by sobs or other vocal expressions of emotion
the act of shedding tears

Examples

Her uncontrollable weeping filled the quiet room with a sense of sadness.
The movie was so touching that it left many audience members in weeping.
He struggled to hide his weeping during the emotional farewell.

Synset Examples

I hate to hear the crying of a child
she was in tears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crying - the act of producing tears and sounds of distress
  • Antonym: laughter - the action or sound of laughing

Etymology

From Middle English wepen (to weep) + -ing.
