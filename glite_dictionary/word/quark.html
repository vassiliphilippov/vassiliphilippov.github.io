quark

1. NOUN

/ kwɑrk / (AmE), / kwɑːk / (BrE)
A soft, fresh, unripened cheese with a smooth texture, made from pasteurized milk, a starter, and rennet.
fresh, soft cheese

Examples

I spread some quark on my toast for a healthy breakfast.
Many German desserts use quark as a main ingredient for their creamy texture.
The recipe calls for 200 grams of quark mixed with herbs and spices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cottage cheese - a soft, lumpy cheese made from curds of slightly soured milk

Etymology

2. NOUN

/ kwɑrk / (AmE), / kwɑːk / (BrE)
A hypothetical fundamental particle that is a constituent of hadrons such as protons and neutrons; six types, or 'flavors', are known in physics, each with a fractional electric charge.
fundamental particle in physics

Examples

Some physicists spend their careers studying the behavior of quarks inside matter.
Each quark possesses a fractional electric charge, unlike particles such as electrons.
The theory predicts that protons and neutrons are made of different types of quarks.

Synset Examples

quarks have not been observed directly but theoretical predictions based on their existence have been confirmed experimentally

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lepton - a fundamental particle not subject to the strong nuclear force
  • Degree Higher: baryon - a hadron composed of three quarks, such as a proton or neutron

Etymology

Coined by physicist Murray Gell-Mann, inspired by a phrase in James Joyce's 'Finnegans Wake'.
