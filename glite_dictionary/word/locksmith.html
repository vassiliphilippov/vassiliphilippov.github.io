locksmith

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːkˌsmɪθ / (AmE), / ˈlɒk.smɪθ / (BrE)
a person whose job is to make, repair, or open locks, and to cut keys
a person who makes or repairs locks

Examples

We had to call a locksmith when we lost our house keys.
The locksmith quickly repaired the broken lock on the front door.
Many locksmiths now offer emergency services late at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: key cutter - a person who cuts keys
  • Antonym: burglar - someone who breaks into buildings, often by defeating locks

Etymology

From Middle English 'loksmith', from 'lock' + 'smith'.
