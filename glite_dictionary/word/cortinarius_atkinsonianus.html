Cortinarius atkinsonianus

1. NOUN

/ kɔːr.tɪˈnɛəri.əs ˌæt.kɪnˈsoʊ.ni.ən.əs / (AmE), / kɔː.tɪˈnɛː.ri.əs ˌæt.kɪnˈsəʊ.ni.ə.nəs / (BrE)
A species of edible mushroom with a slimy yellow cap that changes to olive and tawny, and lavender-colored flesh.
a species of edible mushroom

Examples

The distinctive lavender flesh helps identify Cortinarius atkinsonianus in the wild.
Some mushroom hunters seek Cortinarius atkinsonianus for its unique coloration and edible properties.
Care should be taken when collecting Cortinarius atkinsonianus, as some similar species are not edible.

Etymology

Genus name from Latin 'cortina' (curtain), alluding to the cobweb-like partial veil; species named after mycologist George F. Atkinson.
