baronial

1. ADJ

/ bəˈroʊniəl / (AmE), / bəˈrəʊniəl / (BrE)
Impressive in size, manner, or appearance; grand and stately, resembling the style or grandeur associated with a baron.
grand or impressive; stately

Examples

They hosted the gala in a baronial hall adorned with enormous chandeliers.
The baronial entrance to the estate made a powerful first impression on visitors.
He dreamed of living in a baronial mansion surrounded by vast gardens.

Synset Examples

a baronial mansion
an imposing residence
a noble tree
severe-looking policemen sat astride noble horses
stately columns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stately - impressive and dignified in appearance or manner
  • Synonym: grand - magnificent or imposing in appearance, size, or style

Etymology

From Old French baronial, from 'baron' (a nobleman of certain rank).
