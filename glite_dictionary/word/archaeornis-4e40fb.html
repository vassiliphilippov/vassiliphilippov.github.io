Archaeornis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrkiˈɔrnɪs / (AmE), / ˌɑːkiˈɔːnɪs / (BrE)
a genus of extinct primitive birds known from Jurassic fossils, considered an early link between dinosaurs and modern birds.
genus of extinct primitive birds (Jurassic)

Examples

The discovery of Archaeornis fossils provided important evidence for the evolutionary link between reptiles and birds.
Scientists compared the skeletal structure of Archaeornis with that of modern birds.
The Jurassic rock formation contained remains of several ancient creatures, including Archaeornis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Archaeopteryx - another genus of early fossil birds from the Jurassic period

Etymology

From Greek 'arkhaios' (ancient) + 'ornis' (bird).
