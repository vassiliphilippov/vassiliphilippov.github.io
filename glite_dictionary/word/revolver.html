revolver

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈvɑːlvər / (AmE), / rɪˈvɒlvə / (BrE)
a type of pistol with a revolving cylinder that typically holds several cartridges, allowing repeated firing without reloading after each shot
pistol with revolving cylinder for bullets

Examples

The detective drew his revolver and cautiously entered the abandoned warehouse.
He collected antique revolvers and proudly displayed them in his study.
After cleaning the revolver, she reloaded it and put it back in the drawer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pistol - a small firearm designed to be held in one hand
  • Antonym: rifle - a firearm with a long barrel, typically fired from the shoulder

Etymology

From revolve, referring to the rotating cylinder, plus the agent suffix -er.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈvɑlvər / (AmE), / rɪˈvɒlvə / (BrE)
a door consisting of several panels that rotate around a central axis, allowing people to enter or leave while minimizing airflow between inside and outside
a rotating door with several panels

Examples

The hotel lobby was accessed through a large revolver that kept out the cold wind.
Many office buildings install a revolver to help maintain indoor climate control.
She pushed the heavy glass revolver and stepped into the busy entrance hall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revolving door - a door with panels that rotate about a central axis

Etymology

From revolve, by analogy with the rotating motion of the panels; originally influenced by the firearm's name.
