Nymphalis antiopa

1. NOUN

/ nɪmˈfælɪs ænˈtaɪ.ə.pə / (AmE), / nɪmˈfalɪs anˈtɪ.ə.pə / (BrE)
A large butterfly of temperate regions, with dark purple wings edged with yellow, commonly known as the mourning cloak.
a butterfly species with dark wings and yellow borders

Examples

We spotted a beautiful Nymphalis antiopa resting on the tree trunk in early spring.
The coloring of the Nymphalis antiopa makes it easy to identify compared to other butterflies.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of the Nymphalis antiopa across North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mourning cloak - common English name for Nymphalis antiopa, a butterfly with dark wings and yellow edges

Etymology

From Latin; 'Nymphalis' is a genus name, 'antiopa' is a species epithet possibly of mythological origin.
