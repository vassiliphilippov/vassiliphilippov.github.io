inquisitiveness

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkwɪzətɪvnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈkwɪzɪtɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being eager to learn or curious about things
the quality of being curious or eager to learn

Examples

Her inquisitiveness led her to explore new cultures whenever she traveled.
The scientist's inquisitiveness drove him to conduct dozens of experiments.
Children often display natural inquisitiveness about the world around them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curiosity - a strong desire to know or learn something
  • Antonym: indifference - lack of interest or concern

Etymology

From 'inquisitive' + '-ness'; 'inquisitive' from Latin 'inquisitivus' (searching).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkwɪzətɪvnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈkwɪzɪtɪvnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being curious and eager to acquire knowledge.
curiosity; eagerness to learn

Examples

Her natural inquisitiveness led her to read every book she could find on the subject.
The scientist’s inquisitiveness drove him to make many important discoveries.
Teachers often encourage inquisitiveness in their students to promote active learning.

Synset Examples

The penetration and the inquisitiveness of his glance troubled me: I felt embarrassed and guilty in his presence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curiosity - A strong desire to know or learn something
  • Antonym: indifference - Lack of interest or concern

Etymology

From 'inquisitive' + '-ness'. Ultimately from Latin 'inquisitivus' (eager to seek or inquire).
