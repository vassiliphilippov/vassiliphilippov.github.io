chthonian

1. ADJ

/ ˈθoʊniən / (AmE), / ˈkθəʊniən / (BrE)
(chiefly literary or mythological) Relating to or inhabiting the underworld or beneath the surface of the earth.
relating to the underworld; subterranean

Examples

In Greek mythology, Hades is a chthonian deity ruling the underworld.
Ancient rituals were sometimes held to honor chthonian gods associated with the earth.
The writer described the cave’s eerie silence as a chthonian presence lurking below.

Synset Examples

nether regions

Etymology

From Greek *khthonios* (in or under the earth), from *khthōn* (earth).
