mathematical

1. ADJ

/ ˌmæθəˈmætɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌmæθˈmətɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or having the ability to think in or work with numbers; possessing numerical aptitude or skill.
relating to numerical ability or skill

Examples

Her mathematical skills impressed everyone at the science fair.
The job requires a mathematical mind capable of solving complex problems.
Even at a young age, he showed a mathematical talent far beyond his peers.

Synset Examples

tests for rating numerical aptitude
a mathematical whiz

Related Terms

  • Synonym: numerate - able to work with numbers; having numerical skill
  • Antonym: innumerate - lacking numerical ability or skill

Etymology

From Medieval Latin mathematicus (mathematical), from Greek mathematikos.

3. ADJ

/ ˌmæθəˈmætɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌmæθəˈmætɪkəl / (BrE)
characterized by or demonstrating the exactness or precision of mathematics
showing mathematical precision

Examples

The engineer followed the instructions with mathematical precision to ensure perfect results.
With mathematical accuracy, the clock maintained time down to the millisecond.
Her approach to organizing the data was almost mathematical in its thoroughness.

Synset Examples

mathematical precision

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precise - exact; accurate and careful about details
  • Synonym: exact - completely correct or accurate
  • Antonym: approximate - not quite exact or accurate

Etymology

From Latin 'mathematicus', from Greek 'mathēmatikos' (fond of learning), from 'mathēma' (science, knowledge, learning).

4. ADJ

/ ˌmæθəˈmætɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌmæθəˈmætɪkəl / (BrE)
(of a chance or possibility) existing in theory or by calculation, but extremely unlikely to happen; statistically possible though highly improbable.
statistically possible but highly unlikely

Examples

Despite their recent losses, the team still has a mathematical chance of reaching the finals.
There's only a mathematical possibility that the comet will collide with Earth.
They have a mathematical shot at winning, but it would require every other team to lose.

Synset Examples

have a mathematical chance of making the playoffs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theoretical - existing in theory rather than practice
  • Antonym: practical - likely to happen or actually feasible

Etymology

From Latin mathematicus, from Greek mathēmatikos (fond of learning).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01828419-s
Synset POS: s
