Saul Bellow

1. PROPN

/ sɔl ˈbɛloʊ / (AmE), / sɔːl ˈbɛləʊ / (BrE)
(1915–2005) a Canadian-born American author whose novels profoundly influenced post-World War II American literature.
Canadian-born American novelist (1915–2005)

Examples

Saul Bellow won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1976 for his powerful novels.
Many critics consider Saul Bellow one of the greatest American writers of the twentieth century.
Her thesis explored the influence of Saul Bellow on modern American fiction.
