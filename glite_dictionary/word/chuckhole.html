chuckhole

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃʌkˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈtʃʌkˌhəʊl / (BrE)
a hole or depression in a road surface caused by wear or weathering, especially one that is hazardous to vehicles
hole in a road surface

Examples

Be careful when driving on that country road; there's a deep chuckhole near the bridge.
After the heavy rain, several chuckholes appeared along Main Street, making the commute rough.
The city sent a crew to repair the massive chuckhole that damaged several cars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pothole - a hole in a road surface caused by wear or subsidence
