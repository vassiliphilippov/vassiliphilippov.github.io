impotence

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪmpətəns / (AmE), / ˈɪmpətəns / (BrE)
The inability, especially in men, to achieve or maintain an erection or to copulate.
inability to copulate (in men)
medical

Examples

Medical treatments have been developed for men suffering from impotence.
The stigma associated with impotence can make it difficult for men to seek help.
Impotence can have psychological as well as physical causes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erectile dysfunction - difficulty achieving or maintaining an erection
  • Antonym: fertility - ability to reproduce or conceive offspring

Etymology

From Latin impotentia (lack of power, weakness), from in- (not) + potentia (power).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪmpətəns / (AmE), / ˈɪmpətəns / (BrE)
The state of lacking power or strength; weakness or feebleness.
weakness; lack of power

Examples

The government’s impotence in dealing with corruption frustrated the public.
She was angered by the sense of impotence that came with being ignored.
The impotence of the opposition became clear after the vote.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powerlessness - lack of ability or means to act
  • Antonym: strength - the quality of being strong

Etymology

From Latin *impotentia* (lack of power), from *im-* (not) + *potens* (powerful).
