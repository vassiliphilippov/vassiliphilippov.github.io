Ctenizidae

1. NOUN

/ təˈnɪzɪˌdiː / (AmE), / tɛˈnɪzɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of large burrowing spiders, known for constructing trapdoor burrows
family of large burrowing spiders

Examples

The Ctenizidae family includes several species of trapdoor spiders found in warm climates.
Scientists discovered a new burrow belonging to the Ctenizidae during their expedition.
Members of the Ctenizidae are known for their unique method of constructing trapdoor-like entrances to their burrows.

Etymology

From Greek ktenizein (to comb), referring to the spiders' comb-like structures, combined with the zoological suffix -idae.
