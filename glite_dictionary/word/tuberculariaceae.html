Tuberculariaceae

1. NOUN

/ tuːbɜːrkjəˌlɛriˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / tjuːbɜːkjʊləˌriːˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
A large family of mainly saprophytic imperfect fungi that do not have a known sexual stage.
family of imperfect fungi

Examples

Many species in the Tuberculariaceae family are important decomposers in their habitats.
The Tuberculariaceae are classified as imperfect fungi because their sexual reproductive stage is unknown.
Researchers have discovered new fungi within the Tuberculariaceae in decaying wood samples.

Etymology

From New Latin Tubercularia (type genus) + -aceae (family suffix).
