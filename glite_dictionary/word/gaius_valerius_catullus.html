Gaius Valerius Catullus

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡaɪ.əs vəˈlɪəriəs kəˈtʌləs / (AmE), / ˈɡaɪ.əs vəˈlɪəriəs kəˈtʌləs / (BrE)
(c. 84–c. 54 BC) A Roman poet known for his passionate lyric poetry, particularly love poems addressed to an aristocratic woman he called Lesbia.
Roman lyric poet (84–54 BC)

Examples

Many students of Latin literature read the poems of Gaius Valerius Catullus in their original language.
Gaius Valerius Catullus is celebrated for his emotionally charged verses dedicated to Lesbia.
The works of Gaius Valerius Catullus provide valuable insight into Roman society and personal relationships.

Etymology

Latin personal name: Gaius (praenomen) Valerius (gens name) Catullus (cognomen).
