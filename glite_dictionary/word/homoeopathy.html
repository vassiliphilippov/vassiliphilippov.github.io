homoeopathy

1. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊ.miˈɑː.pə.θi / (AmE), / ˌhəʊ.miˈɒp.ə.θi / (BrE)
A system of alternative medicine in which diseases are treated with small doses of natural substances that, in larger amounts, would produce symptoms of the disease in healthy people.
system of alternative medicine
medical

Examples

Many people turn to homoeopathy for relief from chronic health conditions.
The principles of homoeopathy differ greatly from those of conventional medicine.
She decided to try homoeopathy after standard treatments failed to help her allergies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homeopathy - alternative spelling of homoeopathy

Etymology

From Greek homoios (similar) + pathos (suffering).
