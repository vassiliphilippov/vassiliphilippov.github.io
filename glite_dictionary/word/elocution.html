elocution

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛləˈkjuʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛl.əˈkjuː.ʃən / (BrE)
the skill of clear and expressive speech, especially of distinct pronunciation and articulation, often with controlled voice and gestures
the art of clear and expressive speech

Examples

The actor trained in elocution to improve his performance on stage.
Proper elocution helps speakers communicate their message clearly to the audience.
Her lessons in elocution made her speech both articulate and engaging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oratory - the art of public speaking, especially in a formal and eloquent manner

Etymology

From Latin 'elocutio' (speaking out), from 'eloqui' (to speak out).
