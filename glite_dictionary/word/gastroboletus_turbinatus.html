Gastroboletus turbinatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡæstroʊboʊˈliːtəs tɜːrˈbɪnətəs / (AmE), / ˌɡastrəʊbəʊˈliːtəs tɜːˈbɪnətəs / (BrE)
A species of fungus with a cap that can range in color from red to dark brown, superficially resembling a bolete.
species of fungus with dark or red-brown cap

Examples

The distinctive red cap of the Gastroboletus turbinatus makes it easy for mycologists to identify in the wild.
During the mushroom survey, a single Gastroboletus turbinatus specimen was found growing under pine trees.
Unlike other boletes, Gastroboletus turbinatus has a unique shape and color variation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bolete - a type of mushroom with a thick, soft cap and pores beneath instead of gills

Etymology

From New Latin: 'gastro-' (stomach) + 'Boletus' (genus of fungi), 'turbinatus' referring to its top-shaped cap.
