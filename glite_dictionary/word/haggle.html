haggle

1. VERB

/ ˈhæɡəl / (AmE), / ˈhæɡəl / (BrE)
to argue or negotiate persistently, especially over the cost of something or the terms of an agreement
argue or bargain, especially over price

Examples

They spent over an hour trying to haggle with the car dealer for a better price.
It's common to haggle at the market to get a good deal.
She dislikes having to haggle when she buys souvenirs abroad.

Synset Examples

Let's not haggle over a few dollars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bargain - to negotiate the price of something
  • Antonym: to agree - to reach a mutual arrangement without argument

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *haggen* (to chop, hack), related to bargaining as chopping away at a price.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhæɡəl / (AmE), / ˈhæɡəl / (BrE)
an intense argument or negotiation, especially about the price or terms of a purchase or agreement
intense bargaining or argument

Examples

The negotiations turned into a prolonged haggle over the final price.
They had a fierce haggle with the vendor at the market.
After much haggle, both sides agreed on the contract terms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bargain - an agreement between two or more parties as to what each will do for the other
  • Degree Higher: quarrel - an angry argument or disagreement

Etymology

From Dutch *haggelen* (to chop, hack), influenced by Low German *haggelen*.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
