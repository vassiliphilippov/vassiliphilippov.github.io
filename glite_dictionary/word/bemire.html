to bemire

1. VERB

/ bəˈmaɪr / (AmE), / bɪˈmaɪə / (BrE)
to make (someone or something) dirty or muddy
make dirty or muddy

Examples

The rain and mud bemired the runners' shoes during the marathon.
The old truck was bemired after driving through the flooded field.
Please remove your boots before you bemire the clean kitchen floor.

Synset Examples

don't soil your clothes when you play outside!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soil - make dirty or stained
  • Antonym: clean - make clean or remove dirt from

Etymology

From be- (prefix) + mire (mud, dirt), originally Middle English *mir* from Old Norse *myrr* (bog).

2. VERB

/ bəˈmaɪr / (AmE), / bɪˈmaɪə / (BrE)
to make dirty with mud or filth; to soil or stain something, especially by covering it with mire or dirt
make dirty or muddy

Examples

The children bemired their shoes while playing in the soggy field.
He warned the workers not to bemire the hallway with their boots.
After the rainy hike, her trousers were completely bemired.

Synset Examples

Don't bemire the clean floor with muddy shoes.
She tried to avoid bemiring her dress at the picnic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soil - to make dirty or unclean, particularly by contact with something
  • Antonym: to cleanse - to make something clean; to remove dirt or impurities

Etymology

From be- (intensive prefix) + mire (mud or filth), dating from late Middle English.
