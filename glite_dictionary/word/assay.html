assay

1. NOUN

/ ˈæˌseɪ / (AmE), / ˈæseɪ / (BrE)
A sample or substance that is undergoing analysis to determine its composition or quality.
substance being analyzed

Examples

The assay was carefully labeled before it was sent for chemical analysis.
Each assay was tested twice to confirm the accuracy of the results.
Researchers stored the assay at low temperature to preserve its integrity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sample - a small part or quantity intended to show what the whole is like

Etymology

From Old French 'assai', from Late Latin 'exagium' (a weighing, a testing).
