appropriable

1. ADJ

/ əˈproʊpri.ə.bəl / (AmE), / əˈprəʊpri.ə.bəl / (BrE)
able to be appropriated; capable of being taken for one's own use, especially by authority or for a specific purpose.
able to be appropriated

Examples

The committee allocated only the appropriable funds to new projects.
Water rights are granted for only that portion of the river judged appropriable under current law.
Only appropriable assets can be transferred to the development fund.

Synset Examples

appropriable funds

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inappropriate - not suitable or proper in the circumstances; not able to be appropriated

Etymology

From Latin 'appropriabilis', from 'appropriare' (to make one's own).
