phonation

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / fəʊˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of producing vocal sound by vibration of the vocal folds, modified by resonances in the vocal tract
production of vocal sound
medical

Examples

The quality of a singer's phonation can greatly influence the beauty of their voice.
Researchers studied how various medical conditions affect phonation in patients with vocal disorders.
Proper phonation is essential for clear and healthy speech.

Synset Examples

a singer takes good care of his voice
the giraffe cannot make any vocalizations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: articulation - the physical production of particular speech sounds
  • Synonym: vocalization - the act or process of producing sounds with the voice

Etymology

From Greek phōnē (voice, sound) + -ation.
