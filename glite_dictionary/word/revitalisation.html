revitalisation

1. NOUN

/ riˌvaɪtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / riˌvaɪtələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something active, successful, or prominent again after a period of decline or inactivity
making something active or prominent again
british

Examples

The new urban park project led to the revitalisation of the once-neglected city center.
After years of decline, the theatre underwent major revitalisation and attracted new audiences.
The government's investment policy focused on the revitalisation of local industries.

Synset Examples

the revival of trade
a revival of a neglected play by Moliere
the Gothic revival in architecture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revitalization - making something active or prominent again (American spelling)
  • Synonym: revival - an instance of bringing something back into use, attention, or popularity

Etymology

From re- (again) + vital (life, energy) + -isation (process), patterned after the verb 'revitalise.'
