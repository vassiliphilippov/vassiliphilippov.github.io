grasping

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræspɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡrɑːspɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of taking or holding something firmly with the hands or another organ
the act of gripping something firmly

Examples

The grasping of the rope kept her from falling into the river.
With a sudden grasping of the ledge, the climber stabilized himself.
The octopus's grasping of prey is both powerful and precise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grip - the act of holding something tightly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00813935-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡræspɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡrɑːspɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of understanding something, especially something difficult to comprehend
an attempt to understand

Examples

Achieving a firm grasping of quantum mechanics can take years of study.
Her grasping of advanced mathematics improved steadily with practice.
Despite repeated grasping at the theory's meaning, he still felt confused.

Synset Examples

the lecture was beyond his most strenuous graspings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: understanding - the ability to understand; comprehension
  • Antonym: misunderstanding - failure to comprehend correctly

Etymology

From the verb 'grasp' (to seize or understand); from Old Norse *grapsa* (to snatch).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05814863-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈɡræspɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡrɑːspɪŋ / (BrE)
Always eager to acquire more, especially money; greedy or acquisitive.
greedy; acquisitive; eager for wealth

Examples

The grasping landlord raised the rent again despite the tenants' protests.
Her grasping nature made her unpopular among her colleagues.
The novel depicts a grasping aristocrat determined to increase his fortune at any cost.

Synset Examples

they are avaricious and will do anything for money
casting covetous eyes on his neighbor's fields
a grasping old miser
grasping commercialism
greedy for money and power
grew richer and greedier
prehensile employers stingy with raises for their employees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greedy - having or showing an intense and selfish desire for something, especially wealth or power
  • Synonym: avaricious - having an extreme greed for wealth or material gain
  • Antonym: generous - showing a readiness to give more of something, especially money, than is strictly necessary or expected

Etymology

From the verb 'grasp' (to seize or clutch) + -ing, figuratively extended to mean 'seizing greedily'.
