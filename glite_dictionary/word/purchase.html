purchase

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝː.tʃəs / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.tʃəs / (BrE)
an item, service, or property that has been bought; something acquired by buying
something acquired by buying

Examples

She showed me her latest purchase: a vintage leather handbag.
Online purchases have increased significantly in the past year.
Please keep your receipt as proof of purchase.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acquisition - something gained or acquired, especially as an asset
  • Antonym: sale - the act or process of selling or a sold item (opposite action)

Etymology

From Old French 'pourchacier' (to pursue, procure), from Latin 'purchasare'.

2. VERB

/ ˈpɝːtʃəs / (AmE), / ˈpɜːtʃəs / (BrE)
to obtain something by paying money for it; to buy
buy by paying money

Examples

He decided to purchase a laptop instead of renting one for the project.
You must be at least 18 years old to purchase alcohol in this country.
They purchased their first home after saving for several years.

Synset Examples

The family purchased a new car
The conglomerate acquired a new company
She buys for the big department store

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buy - to get something by paying money for it
  • Antonym: sell - to give something in exchange for money

Etymology

From Old French 'pourchacier' (to pursue, seek to obtain), from Latin 'purchasare'.
