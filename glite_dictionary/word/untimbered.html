untimbered

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtɪmbɚd / (AmE), / ʌnˈtɪmbəd / (BrE)
lacking timbers; not made with or reinforced by wooden beams or supports.
not having timbers

Examples

The barn was completely untimbered, relying entirely on steel supports.
They launched an untimbered canoe onto the calm lake at dawn.
Engineers inspected the untimbered shaft for structural safety concerns.

Synset Examples

an untimbered boat

Related Terms

  • Antonym: timbered - having timbers; made or reinforced with wooden beams
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02438814-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtɪmbɚd / (AmE), / ʌnˈtɪmbəd / (BrE)
(of land or an area) having no trees; not covered with forest.
without trees; not forested

Examples

The vast untimbered plains stretched for miles in every direction.
We camped in an untimbered valley, far from the dense forests.
The region's untimbered landscape made it ideal for large-scale farming.

Synset Examples

an untimbered area

Related Terms

  • Antonym: forested - (of land) covered with trees or forest

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'timbered' (provided with trees or timber).
