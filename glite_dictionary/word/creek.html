Creek

1. NOUN

/ kɹik / (AmE), / kriːk / (BrE)
a member of the Creek Confederacy, especially the Muskogee people, originally from Georgia and Alabama but now chiefly in Oklahoma
a Native American people of the southeastern US

Examples

Many Creek were relocated from their ancestral lands during the 19th century.
He studied the traditions of the Creek people for his anthropology thesis.
A delegation of Creek leaders visited Washington to discuss their tribal rights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Muskogee - a member of the Muskogee people, a principal group within the Creek Confederacy

Etymology

From Muscogee (Mvskoke) Creek/Muskogee-language ethnonym.
