frictional

1. ADJ

/ ˈfrɪkʃənl̩ / (AmE), / ˈfrɪkʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to, produced by, or resulting from friction.
relating to friction

Examples

The frictional forces between the wheels and the road help the car to stop safely.
Scientists measured the frictional heat generated by rubbing two surfaces together.
The design uses frictional gearing to transfer power efficiently.

Synset Examples

frictional electricity
frictional heat
frictional gearing

Related Terms

  • Antonym: frictionless - lacking or free from friction

Etymology

From friction + -al, with friction from Latin 'frictio' (a rubbing).
