lisle

1. NOUN

/ laɪl / (AmE), / laɪl / (BrE)
A fine, smooth, strong cotton fabric, often used for hosiery and gloves, made from lisle thread.
a fine cotton fabric made from lisle thread

Examples

The socks are made of lisle, making them both durable and comfortable.
She preferred lisle gloves for their smooth texture and elegant look.
These fine lisle stockings were once considered a luxury item.

Etymology

From French 'Lisle', referring to the city of Lille in northern France, known for producing fine thread.

2. NOUN

/ laɪl / (AmE), / laɪl / (BrE)
a strong, tightly twisted cotton thread, often made from long-staple cotton, used especially for hosiery and underwear
strong twisted cotton thread

Examples

High-quality socks are often made from lisle for added comfort and durability.
She preferred stockings made with lisle because of their smooth finish.
Lisle is valued in the textile industry for its strength and sheen.

Etymology

From the name of Lille (formerly Lisle) in northern France, known for textile manufacture.
