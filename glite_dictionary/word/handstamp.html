handstamp

1. NOUN

/ ˈhændˌstæmp / (AmE), / ˈhændˌstæmp / (BrE)
a stamp, typically made of rubber, used by hand to imprint a mark or design
stamp for imprinting by hand

Examples

The officer pressed a handstamp onto each visitor's wrist as they entered.
You need a handstamp to re-enter the event later in the day.
He bought a custom handstamp to label his correspondence with his address.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rubber stamp - a stamp made of rubber used for imprinting a design
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03493970-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈhændˌstæmp / (AmE), / ˈhændˌstæmp / (BrE)
to mark (a document or item) by impressing a rubber stamp, often to show official approval.
mark with a rubber stamp

Examples

She handstamped each ticket at the entrance to the event.
The clerk handstamped the document before returning it to me.
Every outgoing parcel must be handstamped to confirm it has been checked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stamp - to impress a mark or seal on something with a tool or die
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
