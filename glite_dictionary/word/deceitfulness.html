deceitfulness

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsitfəlnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈsiːtfʊlnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being deceitful; the tendency or ability to mislead or deceive others.
the quality of being deceitful

Examples

His deceitfulness eventually destroyed the trust others had in him.
People were shocked by the deceitfulness displayed in her testimony.
The novel explores the deceitfulness of political leaders during the crisis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest
  • Synonym: deception - the act of deceiving or tricking
  • Synonym: insincerity - not expressing genuine feelings

Etymology

From Middle English 'deceitful' + '-ness', with 'deceit' from Old French 'deceite', from Latin 'decipere' (to deceive).
