Litocranius

1. NOUN

/ lɪtəˈkreɪniəs / (AmE), / lɪtəˈkreɪnɪəs / (BrE)
A genus of African antelopes known as gerenuks, characterized by their long necks and found in eastern Africa.
genus of antelopes (gerenuks) from eastern Africa

Examples

The Litocranius is noted for its remarkable ability to stand on its hind legs while browsing for food.
Researchers observed a group of Litocranius grazing among the acacia trees in the savanna.
Unlike many other antelopes, the Litocranius can reach foliage that is out of reach for most herbivores.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gerenuk - a long-necked species of antelope from eastern Africa, member of the genus Litocranius

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Greek 'lito-' (plain) and 'kranion' (skull), referring to skull features.

2. NOUN

/ laɪ.təˈkreɪ.ni.əs / (AmE), / lɪ.təˈkrɑː.nɪ.əs / (BrE)
A genus of antelope native to eastern Africa, including the gerenuk.
genus of antelopes (gerenuks) in eastern Africa

Examples

The Litocranius genus is best known for the uniquely long-necked gerenuk antelope.
Scientists recently observed behavioral differences among female Litocranius in the wild.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect Litocranius populations from habitat loss in eastern Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gerenuk - a long-necked antelope of eastern Africa

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, likely combining Greek 'litos' (simple) and 'kranion' (skull).
