Juncaginaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒʌŋkəˌdʒɪˈneɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌdʒʌŋkəˌdʒɪˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of monocotyledonous bog herbs in the order Naiadales, commonly known as arrowgrass family.
family of monocotyledonous bog herbs

Examples

The Juncaginaceae are found primarily in marshy or boggy environments across temperate regions.
Many botanists classify arrowgrasses under the family Juncaginaceae.
Research on the Juncaginaceae family has provided insights into wetland plant evolution.

Etymology

From the genus name Juncagin(a) + Latin -aceae (family suffix in botanical names).

Examples

Several marshes in the region are dominated by members of the Juncaginaceae family.
The Juncaginaceae are characterized by narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers.
Botanists study the distribution and habitat preferences of Juncaginaceae around freshwater wetlands.

Etymology

From the type genus Juncaginum, from Latin 'juncus' (rush) + '-aceae', a standard suffix for plant family names.
