to necessitate

1. VERB

/ nəˈsɛsɪteɪt / (AmE), / nɪˈsɛsɪteɪt / (BrE)
to make something necessary as a result or consequence
make necessary; require

Examples

The complexity of the new software necessitates further staff training.
Changing climate conditions necessitate adjustments in farming practices.
The surgery was necessitated by a severe injury to his knee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to require - to need something for a particular purpose
  • Antonym: to dispense with - to do without something; to not require

Etymology

From Latin necessitatus, past participle of necessitare (to compel, make necessary), from necessitas (necessity).
