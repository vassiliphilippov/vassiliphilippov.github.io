mediant

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːdiənt / (AmE), / ˈmiːdiənt / (BrE)
(music) the third note of a diatonic scale, lying midway between the tonic and the dominant.
third note of a diatonic scale (music)

Examples

In the key of C major, the mediant is the note E.
Composers sometimes build melodies around the mediant to add variety to a piece.
The teacher explained how the mediant functions within different musical keys.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tonic - the first note of a scale, the tonal center
  • Degree Higher: dominant - the fifth note of a scale, just above the mediant

Etymology

From Latin mediant-, mediāns (present participle of mediāre 'to be in the middle'), referring to its position in the scale.
