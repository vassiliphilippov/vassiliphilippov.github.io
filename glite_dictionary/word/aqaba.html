Aqaba

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑː.kə.bə / (AmE), / ˈæk.ə.bə / (BrE)
A port city in southwestern Jordan, located on the Gulf of Aqaba, known for its historical significance and as Jordan's only coastal city.
Jordanian port city on the Gulf of Aqaba

Examples

Many tourists visit Aqaba each year for its beautiful beaches and diving spots.
The Red Sea port of Aqaba is Jordan’s gateway for trade and travel.
From Aqaba, you can easily explore both Petra and Wadi Rum.

Etymology

From Arabic العقبة (al-ʿAqaba), meaning 'the pass' or 'the steep road'.
