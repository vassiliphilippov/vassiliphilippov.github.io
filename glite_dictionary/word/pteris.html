Pteris

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛrɪs / (AmE), / ˈtɪərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of terrestrial ferns found chiefly in tropical and subtropical regions, sometimes classified in the family Polypodiaceae.
genus of ferns in the tropics and subtropics

Examples

The leaves of Pteris species are often attractively patterned and make popular houseplants.
Botanists collected several samples of Pteris during the expedition to the tropical rainforest.
Many gardeners grow Pteris for their delicate, fern-like fronds.

Etymology

From Greek *pteris* (fern), related to *pteron* (wing, feather), referencing the feathery shape of the fronds.
