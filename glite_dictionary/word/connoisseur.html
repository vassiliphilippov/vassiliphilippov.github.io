connoisseur

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnəˈsɜr / (AmE), / ˌkɒnəˈsɜː / (BrE)
a person with expert knowledge and keen discrimination, especially in the fine arts, food, or matters of taste
expert judge in matters of taste

Examples

She is a connoisseur of Italian Renaissance art and can instantly identify the work of different painters.
Only a true connoisseur would appreciate the subtle flavors in this wine.
The restaurant's menu was carefully curated by a connoisseur of French cuisine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expert - person with extensive knowledge or skill in a particular area
  • Antonym: novice - a person new to and inexperienced in a field

Etymology

From French 'connaisseur', from Old French 'conoisseor', from 'conoistre' (to know), from Latin 'cognoscere'.
