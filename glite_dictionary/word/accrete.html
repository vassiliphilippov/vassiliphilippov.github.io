to accrete

1. VERB

/ əˈkrit / (AmE), / əˈkriːt / (BrE)
to grow or increase by gradual accumulation, or to become attached by accretion
to grow by gradual accumulation

Examples

Over millions of years, planets can accrete material from surrounding dust and gas.
A new narrative began to accrete around the historical events as more stories were told.
Mineral deposits accrete on the surface of the cave walls over time.

Synset Examples

The story accreted emotion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accumulate - to gather or collect gradually

Etymology

From Latin accretus, past participle of accrescere (to grow, to increase).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00159176-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈkrit / (AmE), / əˈkriːt / (BrE)
(of plants and organs) to grow together or fuse during development, so as to form a single structure.
(plants/organs) to grow together or fuse

Examples

Over time, the roots of neighboring trees can accrete into a single tangled mass.
In some flowers, the petals accrete with the stamens as they develop.
The two wounds may accrete during the healing process, resulting in a scar.

Synset Examples

After many years the rose bushes grew together

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuse - to join or blend to form a single entity
  • Antonym: separate - to move or be moved apart; to stop being joined

Etymology

From Latin accretus, past participle of accrescere (to grow together, to increase).

Synset POS: v
