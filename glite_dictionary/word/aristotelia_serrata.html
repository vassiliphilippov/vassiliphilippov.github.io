Aristotelia serrata

1. NOUN

/ ˌærəstoʊˈtiːliə səˈreɪtə / (AmE), / ˌærɪstəʊˈtiːliə səˈreɪtə / (BrE)
a graceful deciduous shrub or small tree native to New Zealand, with finely serrated leaves and small red berries that turn black when ripe and are sometimes used for making wine
New Zealand shrub or small tree with red-to-black berries

Examples

The Aristotelia serrata in the garden attracts many native birds with its fruit.
Scientists studied the medicinal properties of the bark from Aristotelia serrata.
Local winemakers occasionally use berries from Aristotelia serrata to create unique wines.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Aristotelia', named after Aristotle, and Latin 'serrata' meaning 'serrated', referring to its leaf edges.
