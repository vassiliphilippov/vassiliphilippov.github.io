goy

1. NOUN

/ ɡɔɪ / (AmE), / ɡɔɪ / (BrE)
A person who is not Jewish, especially a Christian.
non-Jewish person

Examples

Most of the guests at the Seder were Jewish, but there was also one goy present.
He sometimes felt out of place as the only goy among his friends.
She explained the traditions to her goy neighbor with patience and warmth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gentile - a person who is not Jewish

Etymology

From Yiddish 'goy' (nation, non-Jew), from Hebrew 'goy' (nation, people).
