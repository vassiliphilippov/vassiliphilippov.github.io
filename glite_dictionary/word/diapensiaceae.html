Diapensiaceae

1. NOUN

/ daɪ.əˌpɛn.siˈeɪ.si.i / (AmE), / daɪ.əˌpɛn.siˈiː.si.iː / (BrE)
a family of small, north temperate, evergreen plants, sometimes placed in its own order (Diapensiales) in classifications; includes several genera of low-growing perennial herbs and shrubs.
family of evergreen plants (botany)

Examples

The Diapensiaceae family includes several genera of low evergreen herbs native to temperate regions.
Botanists sometimes classify Diapensiaceae in a separate order called Diapensiales.
Members of the Diapensiaceae are often found in alpine and subarctic habitats.

Etymology

From the type genus *Diapensia* + Latin family suffix *-aceae*.

2. NOUN

/ daɪəˌpɛnsiˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / daɪəˌpensiˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of small, low, evergreen plants native to northern temperate regions, sometimes classified in its own order (Diapensiales) in botany
family of small evergreen plants (botany)

Examples

The Diapensiaceae family includes several species adapted to rocky mountain slopes.
Botanists debated whether Diapensiaceae should be placed in its own order, Diapensiales.
Many plants in the Diapensiaceae are prized for their hardiness in cold climates.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Diapensia, itself of uncertain origin.
