garroter

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈroʊtər / (AmE), / ɡəˈrɒtə / (BrE)
A person who kills someone by strangling, especially with a garrote.
someone who kills by strangling

Examples

The notorious garroter left panic in the streets during the late 19th century.
Police suspected that the victim had been attacked by a garroter lurking in the alleyway.
The story told of a garroter who targeted wealthy travelers after dark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strangler - someone who kills by strangulation

Etymology

From Spanish 'garrote' (a stick or iron collar used for execution).
