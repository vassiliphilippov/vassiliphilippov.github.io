slumgullion

1. NOUN

/ slʌmˈɡʌliən / (AmE), / slʌmˈɡʌliən / (BrE)
a thin and often unappetizing stew of meat and vegetables, sometimes with other inexpensive ingredients
thin stew of meat and vegetables

Examples

The pioneers made a pot of slumgullion with whatever scraps they could find.
After a long day, he served up some slumgullion for dinner.
The recipe for slumgullion calls for leftover meat and plenty of vegetables.

Etymology

Origin uncertain; first recorded in American English in the mid-19th century.
