denivelation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːˌnɪvəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːnɪvəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the difference in height or depth between two points, especially the highest and lowest parts of a cave system
difference in height or depth between two points

Examples

The denivelation of the cave makes exploration challenging for inexperienced spelunkers.
Geologists measured the denivelation between the entrance and the deepest chamber of the system.
The record for the greatest denivelation in a single cave system is held by Krubera Cave.

Synset Examples

Denivelation is always less than or equal to depth.

Etymology

From French 'dénivelation', from 'dé-' (down, removal) + 'niveler' (to level).
