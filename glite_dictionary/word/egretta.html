Egretta

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈɡrɛtə / (AmE), / ɪˈɡretə / (BrE)
A genus of small herons found in both the Old and New Worlds, known for their slender bodies and long legs.
genus of small herons

Examples

The Egretta species are commonly found in wetlands across various continents.
Researchers observed several Egretta individuals nesting near the riverbank.
The identification of the bird was confirmed as belonging to the Egretta genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: egret - a type of heron with long plumes

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, diminutive of French 'aigrette' meaning 'egret'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈɡrɛtə / (AmE), / ɪˈɡrɛtə / (BrE)
a genus of small herons found in both the Old World and New World, known for their slender bodies and long legs; includes several species commonly called egrets
genus of small herons (egrets)

Examples

The snowy egret belongs to the genus Egretta and is admired for its graceful appearance.
Many species in the genus Egretta can be found in wetlands around the world.
Biologists observed several members of the genus Egretta nesting near the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heron - large wading bird with long legs and neck

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, derived from French 'aigrette' (egret).
