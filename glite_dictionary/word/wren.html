wren

1. NOUN

/ rɛn / (AmE), / rɛn / (BrE)
a small, active songbird with brown plumage and a short upright tail, commonly found in the Northern Hemisphere
small brown songbird with upright tail

Examples

A small wren perched on the edge of the garden fence, singing loudly.
We often see a family of wrens searching for insects in the hedges.
The distinctive call of the wren can be heard early in the morning.

Etymology

From Old English *wrenna*, of Germanic origin.
