pause

1. VERB

/ pɔz / (AmE), / pɔːz / (BrE)
to stop doing something for a short time before continuing
stop briefly before continuing

Examples

She paused to think before answering the question.
Let's pause the movie and get some snacks.
He paused briefly at the door before entering the room.

Synset Examples

We pause for station identification
let's break for lunch

Related Terms

  • Antonym: resume - to begin again after a pause or interruption
  • Degree Higher: halt - to come or bring to a complete stop
  • Synonym: break - to interrupt an activity for a period

Etymology

From Latin 'pausa' (a stopping, a pause), via Old French and Middle English.

2. NOUN

/ pɔz / (AmE), / pɔːz / (BrE)
A temporary stop or period of inactivity in an action or speech.
temporary stop or inactivity

Examples

There was a long pause before she answered the question.
The orchestra took a brief pause between movements.
After a short pause, he resumed speaking to the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break - a short interruption or rest
  • Synonym: interruption - an act of temporarily stopping something
  • Antonym: continuation - the act of continuing without stopping

Etymology

From Old French 'pauser', from Latin 'pausa' (a stopping, pause), from Greek 'pausis' (a stopping).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break - an interruption or pause in activity
  • Synonym: interruption - a break in continuity or uniformity
  • Antonym: continuation - the act of carrying on or resuming after a pause

Etymology

From Old French *pauser*, from Latin *pausare* (to cease, pause).

4. VERB

/ pɔz / (AmE), / pɔːz / (BrE)
to interrupt an action, speech, or process temporarily before continuing
temporarily stop before continuing

Examples

She had to pause the movie to answer the phone.
The teacher paused before explaining the next section of the lesson.
Please pause for a moment and think about your answer.

Synset Examples

The speaker paused

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: stop - to cease moving or operating
  • Antonym: resume - to begin again after a pause or interruption

Etymology

From Latin pausa (a stopping, a pause), from Ancient Greek pausis (stopping), from pauein (to stop).
