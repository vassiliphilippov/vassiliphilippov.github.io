Cowpens

1. PROPN

/ ˈkaʊˌpɛnz / (AmE), / ˈkaʊˌpɛnz / (BrE)
a battle during the American Revolution in 1781 in which American forces under Daniel Morgan decisively defeated the British army.
American Revolutionary War battle (1781)

Examples

The Battle of Cowpens marked a turning point in the Southern campaign of the American Revolution.
Historians often study Cowpens to understand tactics used by General Daniel Morgan.
Many reenactments of Cowpens are held to honor the American victory against the British troops.

Etymology

Named after the site 'The Cowpens' in South Carolina, which was used for pasturing cattle.
