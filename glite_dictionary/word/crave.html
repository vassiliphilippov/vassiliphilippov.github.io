to crave

1. VERB

/ kreɪv / (AmE), / kreɪv / (BrE)
to plead for or ask for something urgently or earnestly
to ask for urgently or earnestly

Examples

He craved forgiveness from his parents after his mistake.
She craved the audience's approval during her speech.
They crave peace and understanding in their community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to beg - to ask for something earnestly or desperately
  • Degree Higher: demand - to ask for forcefully or insistently

Etymology

From Old English 'crafian' (to demand, ask), related to Old Norse 'krefja' (to demand).

2. VERB

/ kreɪv / (AmE), / kreɪv / (BrE)
to have a strong desire for something, especially food or affection
have a strong desire for something

Examples

Pregnant women often crave unusual combinations of food.
After a week in the desert, they craved cold water more than anything.
He craves acceptance and recognition from his peers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desire - to want something strongly
  • Synonym: long - to have a strong desire or yearning
  • Antonym: to loathe - to feel intense dislike or disgust for

Etymology

From Old English *crafian* (to demand, ask for), related to Middle Low German *kraven* (to demand).

Synset POS: v
