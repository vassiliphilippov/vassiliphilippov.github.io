Orchidales

1. NOUN

/ ɔːrˈkɪdeɪliːz / (AmE), / ɔːˈkɪd.eɪliːz / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of flowering plants characterized by irregular flowers with minute seeds, including orchids and related families
order of flowering plants, including orchids

Examples

The Orchidales include some of the most diverse and beautiful flowering plants in the world.
Many horticulturists specialize in cultivating plants from the Orchidales order, particularly orchids.
Botanists have studied the evolutionary relationships within Orchidales to better understand plant diversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Orchidaceae - the botanical family of orchids

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on 'Orchidaceae' (the orchid family) and the suffix '-ales' used for plant orders.
