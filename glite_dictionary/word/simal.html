simal

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪməl / (AmE), / ˈsɪməl / (BrE)
An East Indian silk cotton tree (Bombax ceiba) whose fibers are used as a stuffing material, considered inferior to kapok.
East Indian silk cotton tree

Examples

The villagers stuffed their mattresses with fibers from the simal tree.
Unlike kapok, simal is considered less resilient for pillow filling.
The tall simal stood out among the palm trees of the tropical forest.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: kapok - fibrous material from the kapok tree, used as stuffing

Etymology

From Hindi simal and Sanskrit śimbal, referring to the silk-cotton tree.
