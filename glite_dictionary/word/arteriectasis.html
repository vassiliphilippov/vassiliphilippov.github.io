arteriectasis

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˌtɪriˈɛktəsɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˌtɪəriˈɛktəsɪs / (BrE)
An abnormal distension or dilation of an artery.
abnormal artery dilation
medical

Examples

The scan revealed significant arteriectasis in the patient's abdominal region.
Chronic hypertension can sometimes lead to arteriectasis in elderly patients.
The doctor explained that arteriectasis could result in weakened arterial walls.

Etymology

From Greek 'arteria' (artery) + 'ektasis' (dilation, expansion).
