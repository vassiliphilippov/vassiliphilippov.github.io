tor

1. NOUN

/ tɔr / (AmE), / tɔː / (BrE)
a high, rocky hill or a large outcrop of rocks, especially on the top of a hill
rocky hill or outcrop

Examples

The granite tor stood out against the rolling moorland.
We climbed to the top of the tor to get a better view of the valley.
Many hikers enjoy exploring the ancient tors scattered throughout Dartmoor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outcrop - an exposed portion of bedrock or rock formation
  • Degree Lower: hill - a naturally raised area of land, not as high as a mountain

Etymology

From Old English torr (rock or tower), possibly of Celtic origin.

2. NOUN

/ tɔr / (AmE), / tɔː / (BrE)
A high, rocky hill or peak, especially one that stands out from the surrounding landscape.
high rocky hill or peak

Examples

From the summit of the tor, you could see for miles in every direction.
The hikers stopped to rest at the base of the ancient granite tor.
Several rare wildflowers grow among the rocks of the windswept tor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hill - a naturally raised area of land, smaller than a mountain
  • Degree Higher: mountain - a very high, steep hill, larger than a hill or tor

Etymology

From Old English torr (rock, tower), possibly of Celtic origin.
