pericementoclasia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛr.i.sɪˌmɛn.toʊˈkleɪ.zi.ə / (AmE), / ˌpɛr.i.sɪˌmɛn.təʊˈkleɪ.zi.ə / (BrE)
The formation of a pus pocket around a tooth, often due to infection of the tissues surrounding the tooth's root.
pus formation around a tooth
medical

Examples

The dentist diagnosed the painful swelling as pericementoclasia and prescribed antibiotics.
Untreated pericementoclasia can lead to severe dental complications and even bone loss.
X-rays revealed that pericementoclasia had developed around the patient's lower molar.

Etymology

From peri- (around) + cemento (relating to cementum, the tooth root covering) + -clasia (breaking, destruction), from Greek.
