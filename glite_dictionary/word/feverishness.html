feverishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːvərɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈfiːvərɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
The condition of having a raised body temperature, often as a symptom of illness or infection.
state of having a fever
medical

Examples

His persistent feverishness led the doctor to suspect an infection.
The feverishness subsided after she took the medication.
Children often experience feverishness when they catch the flu.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fever - an abnormally high body temperature

Etymology

From 'feverish' + '-ness'; 'feverish' from Old French *fievreus*, from Latin *febris* (fever).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfiːvərɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈfiːvərɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
A state of intense excitement, restlessness, or agitation.
intense excitement or agitation

Examples

The feverishness of the crowd grew as the band entered the stage.
There was a certain feverishness in her voice as she described the day's events.
With mounting feverishness, the staff prepared for the grand opening.

Synset Examples

That amid the turmoil of the time and the feverishness of our days it is always easy I do not pretend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excitement - a feeling of great enthusiasm and eagerness
  • Antonym: calmness - the state of being free from agitation or excitement

Etymology

From 'feverish' + '-ness', modelled after 'fever'.
