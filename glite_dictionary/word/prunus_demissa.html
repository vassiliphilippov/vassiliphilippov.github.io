Prunus demissa

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs dɪˈmɪsə / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs dɪˈmɪsə / (BrE)
A species of wild chokecherry native to the western United States, known for its edible but astringent fruit.
species of chokecherry native to western US

Examples

The berries of the Prunus demissa can be used to make jams and jellies despite their tartness.
In California, Prunus demissa often grows along stream banks and mountain slopes.
Wildlife frequently feed on the fruit of Prunus demissa during late summer and early autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chokecherry - the common name for several North American wild cherry species

Etymology

From the genus name Prunus (Latin for 'plum tree'), and demissa (from Latin 'demissus', meaning 'low' or 'drooping').
