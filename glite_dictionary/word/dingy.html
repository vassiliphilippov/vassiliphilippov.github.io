dingy

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɪndʒi / (AmE), / ˈdɪndʒi / (BrE)
thickly covered with ingrained dirt or soot; dirty and dull in appearance
covered with ingrained dirt or soot

Examples

The once-white curtains looked dingy after years of collecting dust and smoke.
He changed out of his dingy work clothes before coming into the living room.
Shelly scrubbed the dingy tiles, hoping to restore their original shine.

Synset Examples

a miner's begrimed face
dingy linen
grimy hands
grubby little fingers
a grungy kitchen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grimy - covered with or characterized by grime; dirty
  • Antonym: immaculate - perfectly clean, free from dirt or stains

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English 'dinge', related to 'ding' meaning 'to strike, batter', or of uncertain origin.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɪndʒi / (AmE), / ˈdɪndʒi / (BrE)
(of a color or surface) discolored by impurities so that it appears dull, dirty, or not bright; lacking brightness or clarity.
dull or dirty in color; not bright

Examples

The dingy curtains let very little sunlight into the room.
Her coat was once white but now looked dingy after months of wear.
The apartment had dingy yellow walls that made it seem gloomy.

Synset Examples

a dirty (or dingy) white
the muddied grey of the sea
muddy colors
dirty-green walls
dirty-blonde hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dirty - covered or marked with dirt; not clean
  • Antonym: bright - full of light, strong in color

Etymology

Perhaps from Old English *dingy*, related to 'dung', meaning filth.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01915458-s
Synset POS: s
