lithops

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪθɑps / (AmE), / ˈlɪθɒps / (BrE)
a small African succulent plant of the genus Lithops, having thick leaves that resemble stones and solitary yellow or white flowers
stone-like African succulent plant

Examples

Many collectors are fascinated by lithops because their stone-like leaves offer unique camouflage.
I bought three new lithops for my succulent garden last weekend.
The lithops bloomed with a single yellow flower in early autumn.

Etymology

From Greek lithos (stone) + ops (face), referring to the stone-like appearance of the plant.
