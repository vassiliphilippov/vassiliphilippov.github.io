Lithops

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪθɑps / (AmE), / ˈlɪθɒps / (BrE)
A genus of small, stemless South African succulents, commonly called 'living stones', known for their stone-like appearance and ability to survive in arid environments.
genus of South African succulents

Examples

Many plant enthusiasts collect Lithops because of their unusual appearance and resilience.
The Lithops in my greenhouse have just started to bloom with tiny yellow flowers.
Researchers study Lithops to understand how plants adapt to extreme drought.

Etymology

From Greek 'lithos' (stone) and 'ops' (face), referring to the plant's stone-like appearance.
