echolalia

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkoʊˈleɪliə / (AmE), / ˌiːkəʊˈleɪlɪə / (BrE)
(psychiatry) The involuntary and repetitive repetition of another person's spoken words, often observed in certain mental health conditions such as schizophrenia or autism.
involuntary repetition of others' words
medical

Examples

The child's echolalia made communication challenging for the teachers.
In patients with autism, echolalia can be a way of processing language.
The doctor noted the presence of echolalia during the psychiatric evaluation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palilalia - involuntary repetition of one's own words or phrases

Etymology

From Greek echo (sound) + lalia (speech).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛkoʊˈleɪliə / (AmE), / ˌɛkəʊˈleɪlɪə / (BrE)
The automatic repetition of the words or sounds made by another person, especially as observed in infants or in certain medical conditions.
repetition of others' sounds or words
medical

Examples

The speech therapist observed echolalia in several children during the session.
Infants often display echolalia as they learn to imitate the sounds of adults.
In some developmental disorders, persistent echolalia can be a symptom to watch for.

Etymology

From Greek echo (sound) + lalia (speech, talk).
