dicloxacillin

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌklɑksəˈsɪlɪn / (AmE), / daɪˌklɒksəˈsɪlɪn / (BrE)
a semi-synthetic penicillin antibiotic used to treat infections caused by staphylococci, particularly those resistant to penicillin
antibiotic for staph infections
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed dicloxacillin to treat her resistant staph infection.
Patients allergic to penicillin should avoid taking dicloxacillin.
After a week on dicloxacillin, his symptoms had significantly improved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloxacillin - a related penicillin antibiotic
  • Synonym: penicillin - a group of antibiotics including dicloxacillin

Etymology

From 'di-' (two) + 'cloxa(cillin)' referencing its chemical structure related to cloxacillin.
