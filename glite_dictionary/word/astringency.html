astringency

1. NOUN

/ əˈstrɪndʒənsi / (AmE), / əˈstrɪndʒənsi / (BrE)
the property of causing body tissues to contract, reducing bleeding or the flow of secretions
property of contracting tissues
medical

Examples

The astringency of the medication helped stop the bleeding quickly.
Doctors often use substances with astringency to treat minor cuts and wounds.
The astringency of this lotion can dry out oily skin effectively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astringent - a substance that contracts body tissues

Etymology

From Latin 'astringentia', from 'astringere' (to bind fast, tighten).

2. NOUN

/ əˈstrɪndʒənsi / (AmE), / əˈstrɪndʒənsi / (BrE)
a sharp, puckering taste caused by substances that contract body tissues, often experienced in certain foods and drinks
sharp, puckering taste sensation

Examples

The astringency of the unripe persimmon made my mouth pucker instantly.
Red wines with high astringency can leave a dry feeling on the tongue.
Some people enjoy the astringency of strong black tea, while others prefer it milder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bitterness - a sharp, pungent taste often considered unpleasant

Etymology

From Latin 'astringere' meaning 'to bind fast'.
