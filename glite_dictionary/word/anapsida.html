Anapsida

1. NOUN

/ əˈnæpsɪdə / (AmE), / əˈnapsɪdə / (BrE)
a group of reptiles characterized by skulls without temporal openings, including turtles and extinct Permian species; formerly considered a subclass.
reptile group with solid skulls (e.g. turtles)

Examples

The fossil record provides insight into the evolution of Anapsida and their relationship to modern turtles.
Scientists debate whether turtles truly belong in Anapsida based on recent genetic evidence.
Many Permian reptiles once classified under Anapsida are now thought to be more closely related to other groups.

Etymology

From Greek ana- (without) + apsis (arch, loop), referring to the lack of temporal openings in the skull.

2. NOUN

/ əˈnæpsɪdə / (AmE), / əˈnapsɪdə / (BrE)
a subclass of reptiles that includes turtles and various extinct Permian forms, characterized by skulls without temporal openings
subclass of reptiles including turtles and extinct forms

Examples

The turtle is the only living representative of the Anapsida.
Many paleontologists study fossil records to understand the evolution of Anapsida.
The skull structure of Anapsida distinguishes them from other reptilian groups.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'an-' (without) + 'apsis' (arch, loop), referring to the absence of temporal openings in the skull.
