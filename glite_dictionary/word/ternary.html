ternary

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɜrnɛri / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnəri / (BrE)
consisting of three parts, elements, or components; having or involving three units
composed of three parts or elements

Examples

The new encoding uses a ternary logic instead of the usual binary system.
Chemists classified the compound as a ternary mixture due to its three chemical components.
Each row in the puzzle follows a distinct ternary pattern.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: binary - consisting of two parts or elements
  • Degree Higher: quaternary - consisting of four parts or elements

Etymology

From Latin ternarius, from terni meaning 'three each'.
