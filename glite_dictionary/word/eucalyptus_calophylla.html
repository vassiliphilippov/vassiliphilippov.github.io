Eucalyptus calophylla

1. NOUN

/ juːˌkælɪptəs ˌkæləˈfɪlə / (AmE), / juːˌkælɪptəs ˌkæləˈfɪlə / (BrE)
A very large Australian red gum tree, also known as the marri, valued for its timber and ornamental use.
large Australian red gum tree

Examples

The majestic Eucalyptus calophylla can be found across the south-west of Western Australia.
Local carpenters highly value the wood from the Eucalyptus calophylla for its durability.
The park is known for its ancient Eucalyptus calophylla specimens towering above the rest of the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marri - Common name for Eucalyptus calophylla, a large red gum tree

Etymology

From Greek 'eu-' (well), 'kalyptos' (covered), and 'phyllon' (leaf); scientific Latin naming.
