Hindenburg

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɪndənˌbɜrɡ / (AmE), / ˈhɪndənbɜːɡ / (BrE)
Paul von Hindenburg (1847–1934), German field marshal and statesman, president of the Weimar Republic (1925–1934), who appointed Adolf Hitler as chancellor in 1933.
German field marshal; president of Weimar Republic

Examples

Hindenburg's decision to appoint Hitler as chancellor had a profound impact on German history.
Many historians debate Hindenburg's role in the collapse of the Weimar Republic.
As president, Hindenburg struggled to maintain stability during a time of political turmoil.

Etymology

From the German surname Hindenburg.
