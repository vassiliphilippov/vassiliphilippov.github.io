jawbone

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑˌboʊn / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːbəʊn / (BrE)
The bone that forms the structure of the jaw and is hinged to open and close the mouth in vertebrates.
bone forming the jaw

Examples

The fossilized jawbone of a dinosaur was discovered by paleontologists in the desert.
He fractured his jawbone in a biking accident and needed surgery.
The dentist examined my lower jawbone before deciding on the treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mandible - the lower jawbone in vertebrates

2. VERB

/ ˈdʒɔˌboʊn / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːbəʊn / (BrE)
to talk idly or casually and in a friendly way; to chat or gossip informally
talk idly or casually; chat
informal

Examples

They sat on the porch and jawboned for hours about old times.
It's common for coworkers to jawbone around the water cooler during breaks.
We spent the afternoon jawboning instead of finishing our work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chat - to talk in a friendly, informal way
  • Synonym: gossip - to talk about other people's private affairs

Etymology

Derived from the noun 'jawbone,' referencing the movement of the jaw while talking.
