onomastics

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːnəˈmæstɪks / (AmE), / ˌɒnəˈmæstɪks / (BrE)
the branch of linguistics concerned with the study of the origins, forms, and meanings of proper names
study of proper names

Examples

She published a book on onomastics that explores the origins of European place names.
The field of onomastics is essential for understanding how languages evolve.
My professor specializes in onomastics and frequently lectures on the history of surnames.

Etymology

From Greek onomastikos (of or belonging to naming), from onoma (name).
