sinus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈsaɪ.nəs / (BrE)
An abnormal channel or passage leading from a pus-filled cavity to the surface of the body, usually resulting from infection or injury.
abnormal passage from a pus-filled cavity to the body surface
medical

Examples

The surgeon cleaned the infected sinus to promote healing.
After the abscess drained, a sinus remained that needed treatment.
The patient developed a sinus between the tooth socket and the gum surface.

Etymology

From Latin 'sinus' (a curve, fold, or hollow).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsaɪ.nəs / (BrE)
an anatomical channel or cavity, especially a wide vessel or passage containing blood and lacking the typical wall structure of blood vessels
blood-filled anatomical channel or cavity
medical

Examples

The hepatic sinus allows blood to flow through the liver for filtration.
Damage to a dural venous sinus can lead to severe complications.
In some animals, a large sinus replaces the typical blood vessels in certain organs.

Etymology

From Latin 'sinus' (a curve, fold, or cavity).

3. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈsaɪ.nəs / (BrE)
any of various air-filled cavities, especially those in the bones of the skull that connect with the nasal cavities
air-filled cavity in a bone, especially the skull
medical

Examples

He suffered from headaches caused by an infection in his sinus.
The doctor examined her X-ray and noted inflammation in the maxillary sinus.
Seasonal allergies can lead to pressure and pain in the sinuses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cavity - a hollow space within a body part

Etymology

From Latin sinus (a curve, recess, cavity).
