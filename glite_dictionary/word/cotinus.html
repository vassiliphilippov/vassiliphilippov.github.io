Cotinus

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈtaɪnəs / (AmE), / kəʊˈtiːnəs / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous shrubs or small trees, commonly known as smoke trees, cultivated for their ornamental foliage and feathery flower clusters.
genus of shrubs known as smoke trees

Examples

The Cotinus in the garden adds beautiful color in early summer.
Many gardeners prize Cotinus for its unique, plume-like flower clusters.
Our local nursery stocks both European and American species of Cotinus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smoke tree - a common name for species in the Cotinus genus

Etymology

From Latin Cotinus (the old Latin name for the plant).

2. NOUN

/ koʊˈtaɪnəs / (AmE), / kəʊˈtiːnəs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering shrubs or small trees in the cashew family, commonly known as smoke trees, valued for their feathery flower clusters.
genus of smoke trees or shrubs

Examples

The leaves of the Cotinus turn vibrant shades of orange and red in the autumn.
Many gardeners plant Cotinus for its dramatic, smoky-looking flower plumes.
A mature Cotinus can be a striking focal point in a landscape design.

Etymology

From Latin Cotinus, meaning a kind of wild olive or sumac.
