doggedly

1. ADV

/ ˈdɑɡɪdli / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡɪdli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows stubborn determination or persistence.
with stubborn determination

Examples

He worked doggedly to complete the project, refusing to give up despite the setbacks.
The detective doggedly pursued every lead in the case until the mystery was solved.
She doggedly continued her studies, even when others doubted her success.

Synset Examples

he pursued her doggedly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: persistently - in a persistent manner
  • Synonym: obstinately - in a stubborn or unyielding manner
  • Antonym: reluctantly - in an unwilling or hesitant manner

Etymology

From 'dogged' + '-ly'; 'dogged' derives from 'dog', referencing the tenacious qualities attributed to dogs.
