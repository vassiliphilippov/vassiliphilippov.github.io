whimper

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪmpɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɪmpə / (BrE)
a low, plaintive, whining sound or complaint, often expressing discomfort or dissatisfaction
a plaintive, whining sound or complaint

Examples

The puppy let out a soft whimper as thunder rumbled outside.
Her only response to the criticism was a quiet whimper.
He tried to hide his disappointment, but a small whimper escaped his lips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whine - a long, high-pitched complaining cry or sound

Etymology

Imitative; related to Middle Low German wimpern (to whine, moan).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07226412-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈwɪmpər / (AmE), / ˈwɪmpə / (BrE)
to make low, weak, or soft crying sounds, often from pain or distress
cry softly or weakly

Examples

The puppy began to whimper when it was left alone in the dark.
She could hear her little brother whimper after he fell down.
John tried not to whimper, but the pain was too much.

Synset Examples

she wailed with pain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sob - cry noisily, making convulsive gasps
  • Degree Higher: wail - cry out loudly in pain or grief

Etymology

Imitative origin, representing the sound of weak crying.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00065796-v
Synset POS: v
