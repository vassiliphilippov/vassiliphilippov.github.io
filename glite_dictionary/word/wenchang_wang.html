Wenchang Wang

1. PROPN

/ wɛnˈtʃɑŋ wɑŋ / (AmE), / wɛnˈtʃɑːŋ wæŋ / (BrE)
In Chinese mythology, the god of literature and scholarly success, worshipped as a patron of writers and students.
Chinese god of literature

Examples

Many students pray to Wenchang Wang before important exams for guidance and success.
The temple dedicated to Wenchang Wang is a popular site during exam season.
Writers and scholars have honored Wenchang Wang for centuries in Chinese culture.

Etymology

From Chinese 文昌王 (Wénchāng Wáng), meaning 'King Wenchang'.
