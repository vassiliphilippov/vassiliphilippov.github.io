wainscot

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪnskət / (AmE), / ˈweɪnskət / (BrE)
Wooden panels used to line the lower part of the walls of a room as a decorative or protective feature.
wooden wall panels

Examples

The dining room was beautifully finished with a dark oak wainscot.
They decided to install a wainscot to protect the walls from scuffs and scratches.
The old house featured original wainscot dating back to the early twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paneling - wooden boards or panels fixed to walls

Etymology

From Middle Low German 'wagenschot', meaning 'wallboard'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈweɪnsˌkɑt / (AmE), / ˈweɪnskət / (BrE)
Wooden paneling or lining that covers the lower part of an interior wall, usually finished differently from the upper part.
wood paneling on lower part of a wall

Examples

The dining room featured elegant wainscot along its lower walls, adding a touch of traditional charm.
They decided to install painted wainscot to protect the walls from scuffs and marks.
The wainscot complemented the wallpaper above, creating a classic look in the hallway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paneling - wooden boards or panels used to line the walls of a room

Etymology

From Middle Low German *wagenschot* (boards for a wall or wagon), via Dutch *wanschot*.
