adynamic

1. ADJ

/ ˌeɪ.daɪˈnæm.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌeɪ.dɪˈnæm.ɪk / (BrE)
lacking energy, strength, or vitality; characterized by physical or functional weakness, especially in medical contexts.
lacking energy or vitality
medical

Examples

The patient appeared adynamic, unable to perform basic daily activities without assistance.
After the long illness, she remained adynamic for several weeks.
Doctors noted an adynamic state that required careful monitoring and supportive treatment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dynamic - full of energy, enthusiasm, and a strong personality

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'dynamis' (power, strength).

2. ADJ

/ ˌeɪdaɪˈnæmɪk / (AmE), / ˌeɪdɪˈnæmɪk / (BrE)
lacking strength or vitality; characterized by an absence of force or activity, especially in a medical or physiological context
lacking strength or force; inactive
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed as adynamic following a severe illness.
An adynamic bowel can result in serious gastrointestinal complications.
After surgery, he remained adynamic and required careful monitoring.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dynamic - characterized by constant change, activity, or progress; energetic
  • Synonym: weak - lacking physical strength or power

Etymology

From French 'adynamique', from Greek 'a-' (without) + 'dynamis' (power).

Synset POS: a
