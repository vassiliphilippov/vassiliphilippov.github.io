acanthous

1. ADJ

/ əˈkænθəs / (AmE), / əˈkanθəs / (BrE)
having the shape or characteristics of a spine or thorn; spiny or thorn-like in appearance.
shaped like a spine or thorn

Examples

The cactus is easily recognized by its acanthous stems and leaves.
Under the microscope, the plant's acanthous surface became obvious due to its tiny spines.
Some sea creatures have developed acanthous structures for protection against predators.

Etymology

From Greek akanthos (thorn, spine).
