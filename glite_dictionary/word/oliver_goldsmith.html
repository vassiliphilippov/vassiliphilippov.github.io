Oliver Goldsmith

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑːlɪvər ˈɡoʊldˌsmɪθ / (AmE), / ˈɒlɪvə ˈɡəʊldsmɪθ / (BrE)
(1728–1774) An Irish writer known for his novels, poetry, plays, and essays, including 'The Vicar of Wakefield' and 'She Stoops to Conquer'.
Irish writer, poet, and playwright

Examples

Oliver Goldsmith wrote 'The Vicar of Wakefield', a novel that remains a classic of English literature.
The witty comedy 'She Stoops to Conquer' was penned by Oliver Goldsmith.
Students of English literature often study the poetry and essays of Oliver Goldsmith.
