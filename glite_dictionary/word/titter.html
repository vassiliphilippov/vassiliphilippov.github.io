titter

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪtər / (AmE), / ˈtɪtə / (BrE)
a short, nervous or restrained laugh, often in response to something only mildly amusing or socially awkward
nervous or restrained laugh

Examples

She gave a nervous titter when her name was unexpectedly called.
His awkward joke drew a titter from the audience rather than real laughter.
There was a sudden titter at the back of the classroom when the teacher tripped.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giggle - a light, silly, repeated laugh
  • Synonym: chuckle - a quiet or suppressed laugh
  • Degree Higher: guffaw - a loud and boisterous laugh

Etymology

Of imitative origin, representing the sound of such laughter (possibly influenced by similar words like 'twitter').

2. VERB

/ ˈtɪtər / (AmE), / ˈtɪtə / (BrE)
to laugh in a nervous, restrained, or partly suppressed way
laugh nervously or quietly

Examples

The students began to titter when the teacher mispronounced a difficult word.
She tried not to titter during the awkward silence in the meeting.
A few people at the back of the room tittered at the comedian's joke.

Synset Examples

The girls giggled when the rock star came into the classroom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giggle - to laugh lightly and repeatedly in a silly or nervous way

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, dating from the late 17th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00030135-v
Synset POS: v
