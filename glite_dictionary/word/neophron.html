Neophron

1. NOUN

/ niˈɑːfrɒn / (AmE), / niːˈɒfrɒn / (BrE)
A genus of vultures in the family Accipitridae, containing the Egyptian vulture, also called the white scavenger vulture or pharaoh's chicken.
genus of vultures including the Egyptian vulture

Examples

The Neophron genus includes only one extant species, the Egyptian vulture.
Biologists studied the feeding habits of Neophron in their natural habitat.
The unique appearance of Neophron makes it easily distinguishable from other vultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Egyptian vulture - a species of vulture in the genus Neophron

Etymology

From Greek 'neōphron', a kind of vulture.

2. NOUN

/ niˈɒfrɑn / (AmE), / niˈɒfrɒn / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Accipitridae, containing the Egyptian vulture.
genus of birds (includes Egyptian vulture)

Examples

The genus Neophron contains only a single living species, the Egyptian vulture.
Ornithologists have studied the feeding habits of birds in the Neophron genus.
Most conservation efforts for Neophron focus on protecting the endangered Egyptian vulture.

Etymology

From Greek *neophros* (kidney), used as a classical genus name in ornithology.
