rambutan

1. NOUN

/ ræmˈbuːtən / (AmE), / ræmˈbuːtən / (BrE)
A tropical tree native to Southeast Asia that produces a bright red, hairy fruit with sweet, juicy flesh.
a tropical tree or its hairy red fruit

Examples

We bought fresh rambutan at the market during our trip to Malaysia.
The rambutan tree thrives in warm, humid climates and produces fruit twice a year.
Peeling a rambutan reveals a translucent, sweet interior similar to lychee.

Etymology

From Malay 'rambut' meaning 'hair', referring to the fruit's hairy skin.

2. NOUN

/ ræmˈbuːtɑn / (AmE), / ræmˈbuːtæn / (BrE)
A bright red oval tropical fruit from Southeast Asia, covered with soft spines and having pleasantly acidic flesh.
Malayan fruit with soft spines

Examples

At the market, I tried a rambutan for the first time and loved its juicy, tangy flavor.
The rambutan tree grows well in tropical climates and produces fruit twice a year.
She peeled the spiny skin off the rambutan before enjoying the sweet, white flesh inside.

Etymology

From Malay 'rambutan', from 'rambut' (hair), referring to the fruit's hairy appearance.
