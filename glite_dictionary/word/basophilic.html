basophilic

1. ADJ

/ ˌbeɪsəˈfɪlɪk / (AmE), / ˌbeɪsəˈfɪlɪk / (BrE)
(of cells or tissues) staining readily with basic (alkaline) dyes, especially due to the presence of certain chemical groups.
readily stained by basic dyes
medical

Examples

The cell's cytoplasm appeared basophilic under the light microscope, indicating a high RNA content.
Pathologists often identify basophilic structures when examining tissue samples.
Many white blood cells show basophilic granules, which aid in diagnostic procedures.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acidophilic - staining readily with acidic dyes

Etymology

From base (referring to alkaline dyes) + -philic (from Greek 'philos', loving).
