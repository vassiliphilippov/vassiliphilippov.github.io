Coraciiformes

1. NOUN

/ kɔːˌreɪsiˈɪfɔːrmiːz / (AmE), / kɒˌræsɪˈɪfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of usually colorful birds, including rollers, kingfishers, hornbills, hoopoes, motmots, bee-eaters, and todies
order of colorful birds (rollers, kingfishers, etc.)

Examples

The Coraciiformes include some of the most vividly colored birds in the world.
Many species within the Coraciiformes order are found in tropical regions.
Kingfishers, part of the Coraciiformes, are renowned for their bright plumage and swift hunting skills.

Etymology

From modern Latin Coraciiformes, from Coracias (a genus of roller birds) + -formes (having the form of).
