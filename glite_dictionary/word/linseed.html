linseed

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪn.siːd / (AmE), / ˈlɪn.siːd / (BrE)
The seed of the flax plant, used as a source of oil and sometimes as food.
seed of the flax plant, source of oil

Examples

Linseed oil is commonly used as a finish for wood furniture.
The recipe calls for ground linseed as a healthy ingredient.
Farmers grew linseed for both its oil and its fiber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flaxseed - the seed of flax, also called linseed

Etymology

From Old English 'līn' (flax) + 'sæd' (seed).
