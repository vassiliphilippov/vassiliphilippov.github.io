one-step

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʌn.stɛp / (AmE), / ˈwʌn.stɛp / (BrE)
an early ballroom dance that was a precursor to the fox-trot, typically characterized by a single step per beat in 2/4 time
early ballroom dance, precursor to fox-trot

Examples

In the early 1900s, couples would dance the one-step at elegant social gatherings.
The one-step was popular before the fox-trot became fashionable.
She learned both the one-step and the waltz at her grandmother's dance class.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: fox-trot - a ballroom dance with a rhythm similar to that of the one-step

2. VERB

/ ˈwʌn ˌstɛp / (AmE), / ˈwʌn ˌstɛp / (BrE)
to dance the one-step, a popular ballroom dance from the early 20th century characterized by quick walking steps.
to dance the one-step

Examples

The couple one-stepped across the ballroom with graceful precision.
During the party, everyone was invited to one-step to the lively music.
He had never one-stepped before, but he decided to join the dance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foxtrot - type of ballroom dance originating in the same era
  • Synonym: waltz - ballroom dance in triple time, different style

Etymology

From the name of the dance 'one-step,' which originated in the United States in the early 1900s.
