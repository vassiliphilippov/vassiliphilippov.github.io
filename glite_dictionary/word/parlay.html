parlay

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːr.leɪ / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.leɪ / (BrE)
A series of bets in which the winnings from each bet are used as the stake for the next.
series of linked bets with winnings reinvested

Examples

He placed a parlay on three different football games to increase his potential winnings.
After winning the first round, she decided to use her earnings for a parlay on horse racing.
The casino offers higher payouts for a successful parlay than for individual bets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bet - an act of risking money on the outcome of a future event

Etymology

From French "paroli" (a gambling term), likely via Italian "paroli".

WordNet ID: oewn-00508282-n

2. VERB

/ ˈpɑːr.leɪ / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.leɪ / (BrE)
to use winnings or an initial amount and place it as a stake on a subsequent bet or investment, especially in the hope of increasing returns
use winnings to make a further bet

Examples

She decided to parlay her casino winnings into a bigger bet on the next horse race.
He managed to parlay a small investment into a multi-million dollar fortune.
After winning the first round, he chose to parlay his earnings instead of cashing out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gamble - to bet money on the outcome of an event
  • Degree Lower: to invest - to put money into something in order to make a profit

Etymology

From French 'paroli', used in gambling, possibly from Italian 'paroli' (double stakes).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01142049-v
Synset POS: v
