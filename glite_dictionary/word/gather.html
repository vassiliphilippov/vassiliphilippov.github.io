gather

1. VERB

/ ˈɡæðɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡæðə / (BrE)
to bring or collect people or things together in one place
bring or collect together

Examples

We should gather all the documents before the meeting starts.
Families usually gather around the table for Sunday dinner.
A crowd began to gather in the park to listen to the concert.

Synset Examples

We assembled in the church basement
Let's gather in the dining room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assemble - to come or bring together in a group
  • Antonym: to disperse - to spread or move apart in different directions

Etymology

From Old English gadrian (to assemble, unite).
