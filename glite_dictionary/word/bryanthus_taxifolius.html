Bryanthus taxifolius

1. NOUN

/ braɪˈænθəs ˌtæksɪˈfoʊliəs / (AmE), / braɪˈænθəs ˌtæksɪˈfəʊlɪəs / (BrE)
A small evergreen shrub with tiny leaves and pink or purple flowers, found on alpine summits and high elevations in Asia, Europe, and the United States.
alpine shrub with pink or purple flowers

Examples

The rare Bryanthus taxifolius thrives in the harsh climate of alpine summits.
Botanists discovered several specimens of Bryanthus taxifolius during their mountain expedition.
Gardeners appreciate Bryanthus taxifolius for its attractive pink flowers and evergreen foliage.

Etymology

From Greek 'bryon' (moss) and 'anthos' (flower), and Latin 'taxifolius' (yew-leaved).
