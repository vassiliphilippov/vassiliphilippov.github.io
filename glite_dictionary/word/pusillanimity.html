pusillanimity

1. NOUN

/ ˌpjuːsɪləˈnɪməti / (AmE), / ˌpjuːsɪləˈnɪmɪti / (BrE)
lack of courage or determination; timidity or cowardliness, especially regarded as contemptible
lack of courage; cowardliness

Examples

His pusillanimity in the face of adversity disappointed his colleagues.
The general's pusillanimity was blamed for the disaster that befell his troops.
Some leaders mistake caution for pusillanimity, but the difference is significant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowardice - lack of bravery; cowardly behavior
  • Antonym: courage - the ability to do something that frightens one; bravery

Etymology

From Latin 'pusillanimitas', from 'pusillus' (very small) + 'animus' (spirit).
