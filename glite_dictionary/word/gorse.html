gorse

1. NOUN

/ ɡɔrs / (AmE), / ɡɔːs / (BrE)
A very spiny and dense evergreen shrub with fragrant golden-yellow flowers, common throughout western Europe.
spiny shrub with yellow flowers

Examples

The hillside was covered with bright yellow gorse, adding color to the landscape.
Farmers often find it difficult to clear gorse from their fields because of its sharp spines.
On their walk, they admired the fragrance of the gorse blooming beside the path.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furze - another name for gorse, used especially in British English

Etymology

From Old English *gors* (furze, gorse).
