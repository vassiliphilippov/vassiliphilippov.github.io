Elephas

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛlɪfæs / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪfas / (BrE)
A genus of elephants, including the Asian elephant, and the type genus of the family Elephantidae.
genus of elephants (type genus of Elephantidae)

Examples

The Asian elephant, classified as Elephas maximus, is native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia.
Fossils from the genus Elephas reveal much about elephant evolution.
Researchers compared the skull structures of Elephas and Loxodonta to understand their differences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Loxodonta - Genus of African elephants

Etymology

From Latin *Elephas*, from Greek *elephas* meaning 'elephant'.
