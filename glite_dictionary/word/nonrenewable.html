nonrenewable

1. ADJ

/ nɑn.rɪˈnuː.ə.bəl / (AmE), / nɒn.rɪˈnjuː.ə.bəl / (BrE)
(of a resource or contract) not able to be renewed or replaced after use or expiration.
not able to be renewed or replaced

Examples

Coal is a nonrenewable resource, so its supply will eventually run out.
Many governments promote alternative energy to reduce dependence on nonrenewable fuels.
The contract was strictly nonrenewable, expiring after a single term.

Synset Examples

books on that shelf are unrenewable
gas and oil are nonrenewable resources

Related Terms

  • Antonym: renewable - able to be renewed or replaced

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' (not) and 'renewable', meaning not capable of being renewed.
