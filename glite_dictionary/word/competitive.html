competitive

1. ADJ

/ kəmˈpɛt̬.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / kəmˈpɛt.ɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
Characterized by or involving competition, especially in the context of capitalism or market economies.
relating to or involving competition

Examples

The company operates in a highly competitive market where only the strongest survive.
Many people thrive in competitive environments that reward individual achievement.
A competitive economy often encourages innovation and efficiency.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cooperative - involving mutual assistance rather than competition
  • Antonym: monopolistic - characterized by or relating to monopoly and lack of competition

Etymology

From Latin 'competitivus', from 'competere' (to strive for).

2. ADJ

/ kəmˈpɛt̬.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / kəmˈpɛt.ɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
having a strong desire to compete or succeed, often showing a determined and aggressive attitude toward others
determined to compete or win

Examples

Our manager is extremely competitive and always pushes the team to outperform others.
She adopted a competitive attitude in every meeting, eager to lead the project.
The company's culture is so competitive that even small achievements are fiercely contested.

Synset Examples

highly competitive sales representative
militant in fighting for better wages for workers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aggressive - behaving in a forceful or determined way to succeed or win
  • Antonym: uncompetitive - not inclined to compete or lacking a desire to win

Etymology

From compete + -ive, based on Latin competere 'strive together'.

3. ADJ

/ kəmˈpɛt̬.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / kəmˈpɛt.ɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
relating to or involving competition; having or showing a strong desire to compete or succeed against others.
relating to competition

Examples

Our company operates in a highly competitive market, so we must always innovate.
She has a competitive streak and hates to lose, even in friendly games.
The competitive spirit among the teams made the tournament exciting.

Synset Examples

competitive games
to improve one's competitive position

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cooperative - working together toward a common goal rather than competing
  • Synonym: ambitious - having a strong desire for success or achievement

Etymology

From Late Latin competītīvus, from competere (to compete).
