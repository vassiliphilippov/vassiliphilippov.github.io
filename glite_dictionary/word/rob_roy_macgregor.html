Rob Roy MacGregor

1. PROPN

/ rɑb rɔɪ məˈɡrɛɡər / (AmE), / rɒb rɔɪ məˈɡrɛɡə / (BrE)
A famous Scottish clan leader and outlaw (1671–1734), renowned for his resistance against authorities, and the subject of literary and folk legends.
Scottish outlaw and clan leader (1671–1734)

Examples

Sir Walter Scott's novel immortalized Rob Roy MacGregor as a symbol of Scottish resistance.
Many Scottish folk tales celebrate the exploits of Rob Roy MacGregor.
The legend of Rob Roy MacGregor continues to capture the imagination of visitors to the Highlands.

Etymology

From his Gaelic name Raibeart Ruadh MacGriogair; 'Rob Roy' means 'Red Rob' in Scots due to his hair color.
