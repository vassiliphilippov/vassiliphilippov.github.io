crudely

1. ADV

/ ˈkrudli / (AmE), / ˈkruːdli / (BrE)
In a way that lacks refinement, tact, or sophistication; in a rough or raw manner.
in a rough or unrefined manner

Examples

The carpenter quickly and crudely built a shelter out of scrap wood.
She crudely interrupted the meeting without any regard for etiquette.
Some early cave paintings were crudely drawn but still powerful.

Synset Examples

he was crudely bold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roughly - in a rough or crude manner

Etymology

From 'crude' + suffix '-ly'; 'crude' from Latin 'crudus' (raw, rough).

2. ADV

/ ˈkruːdli / (AmE), / ˈkruːdli / (BrE)
In a way that lacks finesse, skill, or polish; done roughly or without sophistication.
without finesse or skill

Examples

She painted the sign crudely, with uneven letters and splashes of paint everywhere.
The furniture was crudely made, with visible nails and rough edges.
Although his jokes were crudely delivered, the audience still laughed.

Synset Examples

an inexpertly constructed lean-to

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roughly - in a rough or approximate way; without detail or precision
  • Antonym: finely - in a refined or skillful way

Etymology

From 'crude' (Latin 'crudus' meaning raw) + '-ly' forming an adverb.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00275957-r
Synset POS: r
