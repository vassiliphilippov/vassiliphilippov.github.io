Rhus vernix

1. NOUN

/ ɹʌs vɜrˈnɪks / (AmE), / ɹʌs ˈvɜːnɪks / (BrE)
A North American swamp shrub, also known as poison sumac, with pinnate leaves, greenish flowers, and greenish-white berries, which produces an irritating oil that can cause skin rashes.
North American shrub known as poison sumac
medical

Examples

Hikers should learn to identify Rhus vernix to avoid painful rashes caused by its oil.
The wetlands are home to Rhus vernix, which thrives in the swampy soil.
After brushing against Rhus vernix, she developed an itchy rash on her arm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poison sumac - common name for Rhus vernix, a shrub that causes skin irritation

Etymology

From Latin 'rhus' (sumac) and Latinized species name 'vernix', meaning 'varnish' or 'shellac'.
