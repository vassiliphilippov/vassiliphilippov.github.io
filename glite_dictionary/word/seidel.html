seidel

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdəl / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdəl / (BrE)
a large glass tankard or mug for serving beer, especially in German or Central European contexts
large beer glass or mug

Examples

He lifted his seidel and toasted the group with a hearty grin.
The bartender poured beer into a frosty seidel and slid it across the counter.
Collectors often seek out antique seidels from old German breweries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stein - a large, usually earthenware beer mug with a hinged lid

Etymology

From German Seidel (beer mug), ultimately from Middle High German 'sidel' (drinking vessel).
