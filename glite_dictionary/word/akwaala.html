Akwa'ala

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːkwəˈɑːlə / (AmE), / ˌækwəˈɑːlə / (BrE)
a member of the indigenous Akwa'ala community, native to Baja California in Mexico
an indigenous person of Baja California, Mexico

Examples

The Akwa'ala maintain a rich cultural heritage in Baja California.
Researchers have studied the traditions of the Akwa'ala for decades.
She traveled to Mexico to learn the language of the Akwa'ala.

2. PROPN

/ ɑːˈkwɑː.ɑː.lə / (AmE), / ɑːˈkwɑː.ɑː.lə / (BrE)
a Native American community living in Baja California, Mexico, who speak a Hokan language
indigenous community of Baja California

Examples

The Akwa'ala are known for their traditional hunting and gathering practices.
Linguists have studied the Akwa'ala language to learn more about the Hokan family.
Many Akwa'ala still live in small settlements in northern Baja California.

3. NOUN

/ ˌɑːkwəˈɑːlə / (AmE), / ˌɑːkwəˈɑːlə / (BrE)
the Yuman language spoken by the Akwa'ala people of northern Baja California.
Yuman language of the Akwa'ala people

Examples

Linguists have documented the structure of Akwa'ala to help preserve the language.
He is fluent in both Spanish and Akwa'ala.
The Akwa'ala language has unique features distinguishing it from other Yuman languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yuman - a family of Native American languages spoken in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico

Etymology

From the name of the Akwa'ala people, an indigenous group of northern Baja California.
