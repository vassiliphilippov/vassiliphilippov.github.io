Nicotiana alata

1. NOUN

/ nɪˌkoʊʃiˈænə əˈleɪtə / (AmE), / nɪˌkəʊʃɪˈɑːnə əˈlɑːtə / (BrE)
A South American ornamental perennial plant with nocturnally fragrant greenish-white flowers, commonly grown in gardens.
ornamental plant with fragrant white flowers

Examples

Many gardeners choose Nicotiana alata for its fragrant flowers that bloom at night.
The white blossoms of Nicotiana alata attract moths after sunset.
She planted Nicotiana alata along the path to enjoy its sweet scent in the evenings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flowering tobacco - another common name for Nicotiana alata

Etymology

From Latin: Nicotiana (the tobacco genus, named after Jean Nicot), alata meaning 'winged'.
