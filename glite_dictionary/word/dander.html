dander

1. NOUN

/ ˈdændər / (AmE), / ˈdændə / (BrE)
Tiny flakes or particles from animal skin, hair, or feathers, which can trigger allergic reactions in some people.
tiny skin flakes causing allergies

Examples

People with pet allergies often react to dander rather than to the animal itself.
Regular cleaning can help reduce the amount of dander in your home.
Cat dander is one of the most common indoor allergens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allergen - something that causes an allergic reaction

2. NOUN

/ ˈdændɚ / (AmE), / ˈdændə / (BrE)
A feeling or state of anger or irritation, especially as expressed in the phrase 'get one's dander up'.
anger; irritation (esp. in idioms)

Examples

Her dander was clearly up after hearing the unfair criticism.
Don't let them get your dander up—they're just teasing you.
Whenever he feels slighted, his dander rises quickly.

Synset Examples

having one's hackles or dander up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anger - a strong feeling of displeasure or hostility
  • Antonym: calm - the absence of anger or agitation
