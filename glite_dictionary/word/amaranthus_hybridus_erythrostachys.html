Amaranthus hybridus erythrostachys

1. NOUN

/ ˌæm.əˈræn.θəs ˈhaɪ.brɪ.dəs ˌɪr.ɪ.θroʊˈstæ.kɪs / (AmE), / ˌæm.əˈræn.θəs ˈhɪ.brɪ.dəs ˌɛ.rɪ.θrɒˈsta.kɪs / (BrE)
A tall, showy tropical American annual plant with hairy stems and long spikes of usually red flowers, whose seeds are often used as cereal.
tropical American annual plant; red flower spikes, cereal seeds

Examples

The leaves of Amaranthus hybridus erythrostachys are notable for their deep purple flush.
Farmers in some regions cultivate Amaranthus hybridus erythrostachys for its nutritious seeds.
The tall spikes of red flowers make Amaranthus hybridus erythrostachys a striking plant in tropical gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amaranth - any plant of the genus Amaranthus, known for its edible seeds and colorful flowers

Etymology

From Latin: 'Amaranthus' meaning 'unfading flower', 'hybridus' meaning 'hybrid', 'erythrostachys' from Greek 'erythros' (red) and 'stachys' (spike).
