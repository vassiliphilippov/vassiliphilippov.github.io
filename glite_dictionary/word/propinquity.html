propinquity

1. NOUN

/ prəˈpɪŋkwəti / (AmE), / prəˈpɪŋkwɪti / (BrE)
the state of being near in space, time, or relationship
nearness in space or relationship

Examples

The propinquity of the two villages has led to a close relationship between their residents.
Their frequent meetings are due to the propinquity of their offices in the same building.
He was drawn to her not only by affection, but also by the propinquity created by working together every day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proximity - nearness in space, time, or relationship
  • Antonym: distance - the amount of space between two points; lack of closeness

Etymology

From Latin propinquitas (nearness, kinship), from propinquus (near).
