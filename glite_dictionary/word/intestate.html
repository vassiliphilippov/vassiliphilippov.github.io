intestate

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtɛsteɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛsteɪt / (BrE)
not having made a legally valid will before death; or (of property) not disposed of by a legal will
without a legal will at death
legal

Examples

The man died intestate, so the courts had to divide his estate.
It is important to make a will to avoid intestate succession.
The property remained intestate until a distant relative claimed it.

Synset Examples

he died intestate
intestate property

Related Terms

  • Antonym: testate - having left a legally valid will at death

Etymology

From Latin intestatus, from in- 'not' + testatus 'having left a will'.
