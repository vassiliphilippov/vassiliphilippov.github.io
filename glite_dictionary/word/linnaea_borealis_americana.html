Linnaea borealis americana

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈniːə bɔːˈraɪlɪs əˌmɛrɪˈkænə / (AmE), / lɪˈniːə bɔːˈraɪlɪs əˌmɛrɪˈkænə / (BrE)
a North American subspecies of the twinflower, similar to the species found in northern Europe and Asia
North American twinflower subspecies

Examples

The Linnaea borealis americana blooms in northern forests during late spring and early summer.
Botanists identified the plant as Linnaea borealis americana due to its distinctive paired flowers.
Hikers admired the delicate beauty of Linnaea borealis americana growing along the woodland trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twinflower - a small, fragrant woodland plant with paired bell-shaped flowers

Etymology

From Linnaea borealis, named after Carl Linnaeus, Swedish botanist, with americana denoting its distribution in America.
