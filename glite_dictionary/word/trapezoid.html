trapezoid

1. NOUN

/ ˈtræp.əˌzɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈtræp.ɪ.zɔɪd / (BrE)
A quadrilateral with exactly one pair of parallel sides.
quadrilateral with one pair of parallel sides

Examples

The teacher drew a trapezoid on the board to explain parallel sides.
Each trapezoid in the pattern was colored differently to help students distinguish them.
In geometry class, we learned how to calculate the area of a trapezoid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trapezium - quadrilateral with at least one pair of parallel sides (British definition) or no parallel sides (American definition)

Etymology

From Greek *trapezoeidēs*, meaning 'table-shaped', from *trapeza* (table).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtræp.əˌzɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈtræp.ɪ.zɔɪd / (BrE)
a small wrist bone located between the trapezium and capitate bones in the human hand
wrist bone between trapezium and capitate
medical

Examples

The trapezoid connects with the second metacarpal bone in the hand.
Fractures of the trapezoid are rare due to its protected location.
The surgeon carefully examined the trapezoid for signs of injury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trapezium - the wrist bone adjacent to the trapezoid bone
  • Synonym: capitate - the largest wrist bone next to the trapezoid

Etymology

From Greek *trapezion* (little table), diminutive of *trapeza* (table).

Source: WordNet
