combustive

1. ADJ

/ kəmˈbʌstɪv / (AmE), / kəmˈbʌstɪv / (BrE)
supporting or causing combustion; capable of igniting or promoting the burning process.
relating to causing or supporting combustion

Examples

Oxygen is a highly combustive element, crucial for keeping fires burning.
The laboratory stored all combustive chemicals in flameproof cabinets for safety.
Proper ventilation reduces the risk posed by combustive gases accumulating indoors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combustible - capable of catching fire and burning easily

Etymology

From Latin 'comburere' (to burn).
