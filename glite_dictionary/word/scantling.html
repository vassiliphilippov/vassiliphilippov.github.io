scantling

1. NOUN

/ ˈskæntlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskæntlɪŋ / (BrE)
an upright piece of timber, usually of small cross section, used in the framework of a building
upright timber in house framing

Examples

The carpenter measured each scantling before attaching it to the frame.
During renovations, several old scantlings were found behind the plaster walls.
The strength of the roof depends on how well the scantlings are installed.

Etymology

From Middle English scantlon, of Old French origin, meaning a small sample or piece.
