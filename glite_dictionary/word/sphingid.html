sphingid

1. NOUN

/ ˈsfɪŋɡɪd / (AmE), / ˈsfɪŋɡɪd / (BrE)
Any of various strong-flying moths of the family Sphingidae, typically with long, narrow forewings, capable of hovering over flowers to feed.
a family of strong-flying moths

Examples

The sphingid hovered in place as it fed from the night-blooming jasmine.
Scientists studied the migratory patterns of several sphingid species across continents.
Many gardeners welcome the appearance of a sphingid moth in their evening flower beds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawk moth - a moth of the family Sphingidae, known for strong flight
  • Antonym: butterfly - an insect related to moths but with clubbed antennae and brighter colors

Etymology

From New Latin Sphingidae, based on Greek sphinx (a mythical creature), referring to the posture of some caterpillars.
