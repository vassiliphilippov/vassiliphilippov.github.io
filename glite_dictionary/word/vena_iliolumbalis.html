vena iliolumbalis

1. NOUN

/ ˌviːnə ˌɪli.oʊˈlʌmbəlɪs / (AmE), / ˌviːnə ˌɪli.əʊˈlʌmbəlɪs / (BrE)
a vein that is a branch of the internal iliac vein, draining blood from the iliac and lumbar regions.
branch of the internal iliac vein
medical

Examples

During the operation, the surgeon carefully identified the vena iliolumbalis to prevent unnecessary bleeding.
The anatomy textbook includes a detailed illustration of the vena iliolumbalis and its neighboring blood vessels.
Damage to the vena iliolumbalis can result in significant blood loss due to its location near the internal iliac vein.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: internal iliac vein - large vein collecting blood from the pelvis

Etymology

From Latin: 'vena' (vein) + 'ilio-' (referring to the ilium) + 'lumbalis' (pertaining to the lumbar region).
