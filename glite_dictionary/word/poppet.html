poppet

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑpɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɒpɪt / (BrE)
A valve shaped like a mushroom that moves perpendicularly from its seat, typically used in internal-combustion engines to control the flow of gases.
valve in engines; mushroom-shaped

Examples

The mechanic inspected the poppet for signs of wear before reassembling the engine.
A damaged poppet can lead to poor engine performance and reduced fuel efficiency.
Modern internal-combustion engines rely on the poppet to regulate the intake and exhaust of gases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valve - a device that regulates the flow of liquids or gases
