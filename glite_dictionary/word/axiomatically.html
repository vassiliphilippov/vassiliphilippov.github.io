axiomatically

1. ADV

/ ˌæk.si.əˈmæt.ɪ.kli / (AmE), / ˌæk.si.əˈmæt.ɪ.kli / (BrE)
In a manner based on or relating to axioms; as something taken to be self-evident or universally accepted.
in a way based on axioms

Examples

The theorem was axiomatically true within the established mathematical framework.
She argued that freedom of speech should be axiomatically protected in any democracy.
Their approach was axiomatically flawed, as it assumed facts not in evidence.

Synset Examples

this is axiomatically given

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axiomatic - self-evident or unquestionable; relating to an axiom

Etymology

From 'axiomatic,' which is derived from Greek 'axiōmatikos' (fit to be considered axiomatic).
