rehash

1. NOUN

/ riˈhæʃ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈhæʃ / (BrE)
something that consists of old material that has been reworked and presented as if new, often with only minor changes.
reused material with minor changes

Examples

Critics dismissed the movie as a rehash of last year's blockbuster.
The author's latest book is just a rehash of old ideas with little innovation.
Their presentation felt like a rehash of material we've already seen.

Synset Examples

merely a dull rehash of his first novel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remake - a new version of something, such as a film, based on an earlier work
  • Antonym: original - something created or produced for the first time rather than copied or reworked

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 're-' (again) + 'hash' (from French 'hacher', to chop up).

2. VERB

/ ˌriˈhæʃ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈhæʃ / (BrE)
to present or use something again with little or no change, especially when regarded as lacking originality or freshness
present or use again with little change

Examples

The director was criticized for choosing to rehash old plotlines instead of creating something new.
Rather than rehashing the same arguments, let's try to find a new approach.
He simply rehashed his last report with only a few minor edits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repeat - to say or do something again
  • Synonym: to revamp - to revise or improve something significantly

Etymology

From re- (again) + hash (to chop up); originally relating to food, later extended to ideas or material.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01164945-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ riˈhæʃ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈhæʃ / (BrE)
to discuss, repeat, or present something again with little or no change; to reuse old ideas or material without substantial alteration
reuse or repeat without much change

Examples

The author tends to rehash the same arguments in every book he writes.
There is no need to rehash yesterday’s meeting; let’s focus on new solutions.
He simply rehashed the old proposals instead of offering anything novel.

Synset Examples

retrograde arguments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repeat - to say or do something again; to reiterate
  • Synonym: to recycle - to process and use again, especially materials or ideas
  • Antonym: to innovate - to introduce something new or original

Etymology

From re- (again) + hash (to chop up or mix, from French 'hacher').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01011124-v
Synset POS: v
