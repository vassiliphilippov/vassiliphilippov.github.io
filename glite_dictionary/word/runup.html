runup

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌnˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈrʌnʌp / (BrE)
a sudden and significant increase in price, value, or amount over a short period of time
rapid increase in value or amount

Examples

There was a dramatic runup in oil prices last summer.
The company benefited from the recent runup in technology stocks.
Analysts are concerned about a runup in inflation rates this quarter.

Synset Examples

a runup in interest rates
market runups are followed by corrections

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surge - a sudden large increase, especially in quantity or value
  • Antonym: drop - a decrease or reduction in value or amount
