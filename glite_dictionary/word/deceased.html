deceased

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsist / (AmE), / dɪˈsiːst / (BrE)
a person who has died
a person who has died

Examples

The deceased was remembered fondly by everyone at the memorial service.
The will specified how the property of the deceased should be divided.
The police contacted the relatives of the deceased after the accident.

Synset Examples

I wonder what the dead person would have done

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dead - no longer alive, lifeless
  • Antonym: living - currently alive, existing

Etymology

From Latin 'decedere' meaning 'to depart' or 'die'.

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈsist / (AmE), / dɪˈsiːst / (BrE)
(of a person) no longer living; dead, especially used formally or respectfully.
no longer living; dead (formal)

Examples

The insurance policy is payable to the beneficiaries of the deceased.
The deceased man's family gathered for the funeral.
According to the will, the deceased wished to be buried beside his wife.

Synset Examples

he is deceased
our dear departed friend

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dead - no longer alive
  • Antonym: alive - living, not dead

Etymology

From past participle of Latin 'decedere' (to depart, die), from 'de-' (away) + 'cedere' (to go).
