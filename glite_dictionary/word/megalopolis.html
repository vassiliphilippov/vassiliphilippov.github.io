megalopolis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəˈlɑːpəlɪs / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡəˈlɒpəlɪs / (BrE)
a very large, heavily populated urban area that consists of several connected cities and towns
very large urban area of connected cities

Examples

New York, Boston, and Washington, D.C. form a continuous megalopolis along the East Coast.
As industries grew, the area merged into a vast megalopolis stretching for miles.
Living in a megalopolis offers both exciting opportunities and significant challenges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metropolis - a very large and important city
  • Synonym: conurbation - an extended urban area formed by merging towns or cities

Etymology

From Greek mega- (large, great) + polis (city).
