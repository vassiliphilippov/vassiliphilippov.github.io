chaetognathous

1. ADJ

/ kiːtəgˈnæθəs / (AmE), / kiːtəgˈnɑːθəs / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of arrowworms (marine worms of the phylum Chaetognatha).
relating to arrowworms

Examples

The chaetognathous species are common in plankton samples collected from coastal waters.
Researchers observed distinctive feeding mechanisms in chaetognathous organisms.
The morphology of chaetognathous animals aids in their predatory behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrowworm - a small marine predatory worm of the phylum Chaetognatha

Etymology

From Greek khaite (hair) + gnathos (jaw), referencing the bristly jaws of these animals.
