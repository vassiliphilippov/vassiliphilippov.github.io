snowy

1. ADJ

/ ˈsnoʊ.i / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊ.i / (BrE)
covered with, or characterized by, the presence of snow.
full of snow; covered with snow

Examples

The snowy landscape looked beautiful under the moonlight.
Children built a fort on the snowy hill after school.
The forecast predicts a snowy weekend in the mountains.

Synset Examples

a white Christmas
the white hills of a northern winter

Related Terms

  • Antonym: snowless - without snow; lacking snow
  • Synonym: frosty - characterized by frost; very cold

2. ADJ

/ ˈsnoʊ.i / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊ.i / (BrE)
Having the white color of snow; pure white.
white like snow

Examples

She wore a snowy white dress to the ceremony.
The chef placed a snowy layer of icing on the cake.
Her snowy hair gleamed under the sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white - of the color of milk or fresh snow

Etymology

From snow + -y, resembling or pertaining to snow.

3. ADJ

/ ˈsnoʊ.i / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊ.i / (BrE)
covered with or characterized by snow.
covered with snow

Examples

We decided to go skiing on the snowy slopes of the mountain.
The children built a snowman in their snowy backyard.
Driving conditions are dangerous on snowy roads during the storm.

Synset Examples

snow-clad hills
snow-covered roads
a long snowy winter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frosty - covered with frost or very cold
  • Synonym: icy - covered with or consisting of ice
  • Antonym: rainy - marked by rain

Etymology

From 'snow' + '-y', Old English 'snāwig'.
