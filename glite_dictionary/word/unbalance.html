unbalance

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈbæl.əns / (AmE), / ʌnˈbæl.əns / (BrE)
A state of mental disturbance or disorientation; a mental state in which normal balance of emotion or perception is lost.
mental disturbance or instability

Examples

Periods of extreme unbalance can interfere with daily life and relationships.
The trauma caused a temporary unbalance in her mental health.
After months of stress, he felt a sense of unbalance that he couldn't shake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insanity - severe mental illness or derangement
  • Antonym: sanity - the condition of having a healthy mind

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'balance', patterned after similar uses indicating loss or lack of balance.

3. VERB

to throw out of balance or equilibrium; to cause something to become unstable or uneven

Adding so many new features at once could unbalance the entire software system.

4. VERB

/ ʌnˈbæl.əns / (AmE), / ʌnˈbæl.əns / (BrE)
to cause someone to lose their mental stability or become insane
make mentally unstable or insane

Examples

The tragic accident seemed to unbalance her for years afterward.
Constant stress can unbalance even the strongest minds.
It was feared that the shock might unbalance him permanently.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to derange - to disturb the order or arrangement of; to make insane
  • Synonym: to unhinge - to make mentally unbalanced or unstable
  • Antonym: to stabilize - to make stable or restore to normal mental balance

Etymology

From the prefix 'un-' meaning 'not' or 'reverse of' and 'balance,' originally from French 'balance' (scales).
