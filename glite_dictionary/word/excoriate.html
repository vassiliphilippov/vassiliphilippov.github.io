to excoriate

1. VERB

/ ɛkˈskɔriˌeɪt / (AmE), / ɪkˈskɔːrɪeɪt / (BrE)
to damage or remove part of the surface of the skin by abrasion or chafing
to scrape off skin; abrade

Examples

The rough surface of the rope can excoriate a child's hands if used carelessly.
Allergic reactions may cause patients to excoriate their own skin by scratching.
After slipping on the gravel, she badly excoriated her knees and elbows.

Synset Examples

This leash chafes the dog's neck

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to abrade - to scrape or wear away by friction or erosion
  • Synonym: chafe - to make or become sore by rubbing

Etymology

From Latin ex (out) + corium (skin, hide).

2. VERB

/ ɪkˈskɔriˌeɪt / (AmE), / ɪkˈskɔːrɪeɪt / (BrE)
to criticize someone or something very severely; to express strong disapproval of publicly
criticize severely; denounce

Examples

The editorial excoriated the government's response to the crisis.
She excoriated the company for its unethical business practices.
Many critics excoriated the film for its lack of originality.

Synset Examples

We condemn the racism in South Africa
These ideas were reprobated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to condemn - to express complete disapproval of, typically in public
  • Synonym: to denounce - to publicly declare to be wrong or evil
  • Antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration

Etymology

From Latin excoriare (to strip the skin), from ex- (out) + corium (skin, hide).
