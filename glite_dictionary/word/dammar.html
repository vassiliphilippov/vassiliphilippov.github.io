dammar

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæmɚ / (AmE), / ˈdæmə / (BrE)
A hard, translucent resin obtained from trees of the family Dipterocarpaceae and the genus Agathis, used in varnishes and other products.
resin from certain trees used in varnish

Examples

The furniture was polished with dammar to give it a glossy finish.
Artists sometimes use dammar as a component in varnish for oil paintings.
Harvesting dammar from Agathis trees is an important source of income in some regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resin - a sticky substance produced by some trees and plants

Etymology

From Malay 'damar', meaning resin.
