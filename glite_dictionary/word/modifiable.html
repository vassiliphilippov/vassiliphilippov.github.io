modifiable

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɑːdəˈfaɪəbəl / (AmE), / ˌmɒdɪˈfaɪəbəl / (BrE)
able to be changed or altered in form, character, or extent.
able to be changed or altered

Examples

All of the settings in this program are modifiable according to user preferences.
The design must be modifiable to accommodate new safety standards in the future.
Her suggestions are modifiable depending on the needs of the project.

Synset Examples

the rhythm of physiological time is not modifiable except by interference with certain fundamental processes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immutable - unable to be changed or modified

Etymology

From modify + -able, originally from Latin modificare (to measure or regulate).
