bargee

1. NOUN

/ bɑrˈdʒi / (AmE), / bɑːˈdʒiː / (BrE)
a person who operates or works on a barge, especially on inland waterways
person who operates a barge

Examples

The bargee skillfully guided the vessel through the narrow canal.
Many bargees live on the water during the summer months.
As a young man, his father worked as a bargee on the Thames.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bargeman - a man who operates or works on a barge
  • Synonym: boatman - a person who works or travels in a boat, especially as an occupation

Etymology

From 'barge' (a flat-bottomed boat) + '-ee' (denoting person associated with).
