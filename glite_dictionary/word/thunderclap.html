thunderclap

1. NOUN

/ ˈθʌndɚˌklæp / (AmE), / ˈθʌndəˌklæp / (BrE)
a sudden and startling event or announcement; a shocking surprise
sudden shocking event or news

Examples

The resignation of the CEO came as a thunderclap to the entire industry.
His sudden confession was a thunderclap that shocked everyone present.
For many, the economic collapse struck with the force of a thunderclap.

Synset Examples

news of the attack came like a bombshell

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bombshell - an event or piece of news that is unexpected and shocking

Etymology

From 'thunder' + 'clap', referring originally to the sudden sound of thunder.

2. NOUN

/ ˈθʌndɚˌklæp / (AmE), / ˈθʌndəˌklæp / (BrE)
a single sudden and loud crash of thunder
a loud crash of thunder

Examples

The sudden thunderclap startled everyone at the picnic.
A loud thunderclap echoed across the valley just as rain began to fall.
She woke with a jolt to the sound of a thunderclap outside her window.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thunderbolt - a flash of lightning with a simultaneous crash of thunder
  • Degree Lower: rumble - a continuous deep sound, often of thunder

Etymology

Formed from 'thunder' + 'clap', first recorded in the 16th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07412152-n
Synset POS: n
