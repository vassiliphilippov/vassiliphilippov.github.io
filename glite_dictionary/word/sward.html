sward

1. NOUN

/ swɔrd / (AmE), / swɔːd / (BrE)
A surface layer of ground covered with grass and grass roots.
surface layer of ground with grass

Examples

The sheep grazed peacefully on the lush sward beneath the trees.
A well-kept sward stretched across the estate's lawns.
Their picnic blanket lay on a gentle sward near the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turf - A piece or layer of grass-covered ground held together by grass roots

Etymology

From Old English sweard (skin, rind, layer of earth).
