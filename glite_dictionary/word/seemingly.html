seemingly

1. ADV

/ ˈsiːmɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈsiːmɪŋli / (BrE)
appearing to be true based on what is seen or known, though not necessarily so; as far as one can tell from appearances alone
appearing to be true; by appearances only

Examples

The project is seemingly simple, but there are many hidden challenges.
She was seemingly calm during the interview, though her hands were shaking.
The house is seemingly abandoned, although lights sometimes appear in the windows.

Synset Examples

irrigation often produces bumper crops from apparently desert land
the child is seemingly healthy but the doctor is concerned
had been ostensibly frank as to his purpose while really concealing it
on the face of it the problem seems minor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apparently - seemingly; as far as one knows or can see
  • Antonym: actually - in fact; really (contrast to appearances)

Etymology

From verb 'seem' + adverbial suffix '-ly'.
