Magna Mater

1. PROPN

/ ˌmæɡ.nə ˈmeɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˌmæɡ.nə ˈmɑː.tə / (BrE)
the great mother goddess of ancient Phrygia, later identified with Greek Rhea and Roman Ops
ancient mother goddess of Phrygia

Examples

The cult of Magna Mater was introduced to Rome in the third century BCE.
In ancient art, Magna Mater is often depicted enthroned and surrounded by lions.
Priests of the Magna Mater played a key role in Phrygian religious festivals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rhea - Greek mother goddess, counterpart of Magna Mater
  • Synonym: Ops - Roman goddess of fertility and earth, counterpart of Magna Mater

Etymology

Latin for 'Great Mother' (Magna Mater); title used in Roman religion for this goddess.
