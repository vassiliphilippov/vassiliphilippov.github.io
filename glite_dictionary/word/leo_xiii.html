Leo XIII

1. PROPN

/ ˌleɪ.oʊ ðə ˌθɜrˈtiːnθ / (AmE), / ˌliː.əʊ ðə ˌθɜːˈtiːnθ / (BrE)
(1810–1903) Italian pope from 1878 to 1903, known for promoting learning and opening the Vatican secret archives to scholars.
Italian pope (1878–1903) who advanced learning

Examples

Leo XIII was renowned for his efforts to promote scholarly access to Vatican documents.
Many historians regard the papacy of Leo XIII as a turning point for modern Catholic education.
Leo XIII's encyclicals had a lasting impact on the Church's social teachings.
