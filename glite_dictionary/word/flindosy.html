flindosy

1. NOUN

/ flɪnˈdoʊzi / (AmE), / flɪnˈdəʊzi / (BrE)
A tall Australian timber tree yielding tough, hard wood used especially for making staves.
Australian timber tree (hard wood)

Examples

The durable wood of the flindosy is highly valued for constructing flooring and tool handles.
Many old barrels in the region were made from flindosy due to its strength and resistance to splitting.
The botanist carefully recorded data on the growth patterns of the flindosy in the wild.
