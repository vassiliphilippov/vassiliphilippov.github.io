quadrumvirate

1. NOUN

/ kwɑːˈdrʌm.vəˌrət / (AmE), / kwɒˈdrʌm.vɪ.rət / (BrE)
A group or association of four men, especially in a political or administrative context.
group of four men

Examples

The board was governed by a quadrumvirate during the transitional period.
A quadrumvirate of generals seized control after the coup.
Leadership of the organization shifted to a quadrumvirate in 1920.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: triumvirate - a group of three men

Etymology

From Latin 'quadrumviri' (four men in office), from 'quadri-' (four) + 'viri' (men).
