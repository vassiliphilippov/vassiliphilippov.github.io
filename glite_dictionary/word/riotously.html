riotously

1. ADV

/ ˈraɪ.ə.təs.li / (AmE), / ˈraɪ.ət.əs.li / (BrE)
In a lively, exuberant, or unrestrained manner.
in an exuberant or unrestrained way

Examples

The garden was riotously colorful in the spring sunlight.
Guests laughed riotously as the comedian took the stage.
The room was riotously decorated for the festive celebration.

Synset Examples

the exuberantly baroque decoration of the church

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exuberantly - in a very energetic and lively way
  • Antonym: calmly - in a quiet and peaceful manner

Etymology

From 'riotous' + '-ly', where 'riotous' derives from Old French 'rioteus' (disorderly), from 'riote' (dispute, quarrel).

2. ADV

/ ˈraɪ.ə.təs.li / (AmE), / ˈraɪ.ə.təs.li / (BrE)
In a loud, wild, and uncontrolled manner; in a way that shows great excitement or disorder.
in a wild or tumultuous manner

Examples

The fans cheered riotously as the winning goal was scored in the final seconds.
After the announcement, the room erupted riotously with laughter and applause.
The parade moved riotously through the streets, with music and colorful costumes everywhere.

Synset Examples

the crowd was demonstrating tumultuously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tumultuously - in a way that involves a lot of noise, confusion, or disorder
  • Antonym: calmly - in a quiet and peaceful manner

Etymology

From 'riotous' + '-ly'; ultimately from Old French 'riote' (quarrel, dispute).
