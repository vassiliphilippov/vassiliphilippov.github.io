Gossypium hirsutum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡɒsɪˈpiːəm hɪrˈsuːtəm / (AmE), / ˌɡɒsɪˈpiːəm hɪəˈsuːtəm / (BrE)
A species of cotton plant native to tropical America, now widely cultivated in the United States for its short-staple cotton.
species of cotton plant

Examples

Gossypium hirsutum is the most widely grown cotton species in the United States today.
Researchers studied the resistance of Gossypium hirsutum to various pests and diseases.
The fibers produced by Gossypium hirsutum are used in a wide range of textile products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cotton plant - a plant that yields cotton fibre

Etymology

From Latin: 'gossypium' (cotton) and 'hirsutum' (hairy, shaggy).
