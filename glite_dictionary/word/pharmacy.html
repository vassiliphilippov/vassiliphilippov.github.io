pharmacy

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːrməsi / (AmE), / ˈfɑːməsi / (BrE)
the science and practice of preparing, dispensing, and reviewing drugs and medicines
science of preparing and dispensing medicines

Examples

After studying pharmacy, she began working at a large hospital.
Modern pharmacy requires knowledge of chemistry and biology.
He chose to pursue a career in pharmacy because he wanted to help people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pharmacology - the branch of medicine concerned with the uses, effects, and modes of action of drugs
  • Synonym: pharmaceutics - the discipline of pharmacy that deals with the process of turning a new chemical entity into a medication

Etymology

From Greek pharmakeia (use of drugs, medicine).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɑrməsi / (AmE), / ˈfɑːməsi / (BrE)
A shop or store where medicinal drugs are prepared or sold, and often other health-related products are available.
shop selling medicinal drugs

Examples

I went to the pharmacy to pick up my prescription.
Most pharmacies also sell toiletries and personal care items.
There's a 24-hour pharmacy near the hospital if you need medicine late at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drugstore - American term for a shop selling medicine and related goods
  • Synonym: chemist - British term for a shop selling medicine and toiletries

Etymology

From Greek 'pharmakeia' (use of drugs, medicine), via Old French and medieval Latin.
