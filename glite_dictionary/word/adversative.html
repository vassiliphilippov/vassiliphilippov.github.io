adversative

1. ADJ

/ ædˈvɜrsətɪv / (AmE), / ədˈvɜːsətɪv / (BrE)
(of a word or construction) expressing contrast or opposition; indicating an antithesis.
expressing contrast or opposition

Examples

In the sentence 'She is tired but happy,' 'but' is an adversative conjunction.
The adversative meaning in this phrase highlights the contrast between the two ideas.
Linguists analyze how adversative relationships function in different types of clauses.

Synset Examples

the adversative conjunction ‘but’ in ‘poor but happy’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrastive - expressing or involving contrast

Etymology

From Latin adversativus, from adversari (to oppose), from adversus (opposite).
