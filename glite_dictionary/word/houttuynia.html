Houttuynia

1. NOUN

/ haʊtˈtuːniə / (AmE), / haʊtˈtuːnɪə / (BrE)
a genus of low-growing plants native to East Asia, typically found in wet places, with heart-shaped leaves and often cultivated as ground cover or for culinary use.
East Asian wetland plant genus

Examples

The pond was surrounded by lush Houttuynia plants with their broad, colorful leaves.
In Vietnamese cuisine, Houttuynia leaves are often used as a fresh herb in salads and soups.
Gardeners appreciate Houttuynia for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded areas.

Etymology

Named after the Dutch botanist Martinus Houttuyn.
