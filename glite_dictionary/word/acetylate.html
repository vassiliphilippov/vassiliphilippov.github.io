to acetylate

1. VERB

/ əˈsiːtɪˌleɪt / (AmE), / əˈsiːtɪleɪt / (BrE)
to introduce an acetyl group into (a chemical compound)
add an acetyl group chemically
medical

Examples

Many enzymes can acetylate proteins as part of the regulation process.
Scientists acetylated the compound to study its reactivity.
The researchers observed that the drug acetylates certain amino acids in the cell.

Etymology

From acetyl (from French acétyle, coined from acide acétique and Greek hylē 'material') + -ate (verb suffix).

2. VERB

/ əˈsiːtəˌleɪt / (AmE), / əˈsiːtɪleɪt / (BrE)
to introduce or substitute an acetyl group into a compound, especially in a chemical reaction
add an acetyl group (chemistry)
medical

Examples

Researchers commonly acetylate proteins to study changes in function.
The laboratory acetylated the compound to improve its stability.
Certain enzymes can acetylate DNA, affecting gene expression.

Synset Examples

the compounds acetylated

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deacetylate - to remove an acetyl group from a molecule (opposite process)

Etymology

From 'acetyl' (from Latin 'acetum' meaning vinegar) + '-ate' (as a verb-forming suffix).
