sump

1. NOUN

/ sʌmp / (AmE), / sʌmp / (BrE)
An oil reservoir at the bottom of an internal combustion engine where oil collects before being recirculated.
oil reservoir in an engine

Examples

The mechanic drained the old oil from the sump before replacing the filter.
A cracked sump can cause oil to leak from the engine.
Most cars have a plug at the bottom of the sump for easy oil changing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oil pan - the removable lower part of an engine's sump

Etymology

From Middle English *sompe* (marsh, swamp), related to Low German *sump* (swamp).

2. NOUN

/ sʌmp / (AmE), / sʌmp / (BrE)
a pit or reservoir, typically beneath the ground, where liquid collects, especially in drainage or mechanical systems
pit for liquid collection

Examples

The maintenance team checked the sump to ensure it was not clogged with debris.
Heavy rains caused water to accumulate in the basement sump, which had to be pumped out.
Oil collected at the bottom of the sump in the engine needs to be drained during servicing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cistern - a tank for storing water
  • Synonym: reservoir - a place where fluid is collected and stored

Etymology

From Middle English *sompe* (swamp or marsh), possibly of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ sʌmp / (AmE), / sʌmp / (BrE)
a covered pit or cistern for collecting waste water and sewage, often as part of a drainage or sewage system
covered pit for waste water or sewage

Examples

The basement sump needs to be checked regularly to prevent flooding.
When it rains heavily, water collects in the sump before being pumped out.
The plumber inspected the sump for blockages caused by debris.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cistern - a tank for storing water, especially one supplying taps or as part of a flushing toilet

Etymology

From Middle English *sompe*, possibly from Middle Dutch *sompe* (swamp, marsh).
