cloistered

1. ADJ

/ ˈklɔɪstɚd / (AmE), / ˈklɔɪstəd / (BrE)
kept away from the outside world; providing or characterized by privacy and seclusion, often sheltered from ordinary life
sheltered; providing privacy or seclusion

Examples

She spent her childhood in a cloistered boarding school far from the city.
The university was criticized for its cloistered and insular approach to academic life.
They enjoyed a cloistered afternoon together in the quiet of the garden.

Synset Examples

the cloistered academic world of books
sat close together in the sequestered pergola
sitting under the reclusive calm of a shade tree
a secluded romantic spot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secluded - providing seclusion or privacy
  • Synonym: isolated - far away from other places or people; remote
  • Antonym: exposed - not protected or sheltered

Etymology

From 'cloister', from Latin 'claustrum' (enclosure, barrier).

2. ADJ

/ ˈklɔɪstərd / (AmE), / ˈklɔɪstəd / (BrE)
living in a way that is secluded from the outside world, especially as part of a religious community under vows
secluded or sheltered, especially in a religious context

Examples

The monks led a cloistered existence within the monastery walls.
Raised in a cloistered community, she knew little of the outside world.
He chose a cloistered life devoted to prayer and study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secluded - kept apart from others; isolated
  • Synonym: isolated - far away from other places or people
  • Antonym: open - not shut or secluded; exposed to view or social contact

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'clausura' (enclosure); related to 'cloister'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02589332-s
Synset POS: s
