rebellious

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈbɛliəs / (AmE), / rɪˈbɛlɪəs / (BrE)
showing a desire to resist authority, control, or tradition; discontented or defiant toward established order or rules.
showing resistance to authority

Examples

The teenager became increasingly rebellious after his parents set strict curfews.
Her rebellious attitude often got her into trouble at school.
Many artists have a rebellious spirit that challenges conventional ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defiant - showing resistance or opposition
  • Antonym: obedient - willing to do what one is told by authority

Etymology

From Latin rebellis ('rebelling'), from re- ('again') + bellum ('war').

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈbɛliəs / (AmE), / rɪˈbɛliəs / (BrE)
involved in or supporting active resistance or opposition, especially against authority or a constituted government
resisting authority or government

Examples

During the civil war, the rebellious provinces declared independence from the central government.
The army was sent to suppress the rebellious uprising in the capital region.
Many songs from that era reflect the spirit of a rebellious youth unwilling to accept authority.

Synset Examples

the rebellious confederacy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defiant - showing resistance or disobedience
  • Antonym: obedient - willing to comply with orders or requests

Etymology

From Latin rebellis (waging war again, insurgent), from re- (again) + bellum (war).

3. ADJ

/ rɪˈbɛliəs / (AmE), / rɪˈbɛliəs / (BrE)
unwilling to obey authority or follow control; showing a desire to resist rules or restrictions
resisting authority or control

Examples

The rebellious teenager often questioned her parents' rules and refused to follow them.
After a rebellious outburst, the student was sent to the principal's office.
The government struggled to control the rebellious province during the unrest.

Synset Examples

temperamentally rebellious
a rebellious crew

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defiant - openly resisting authority or rules
  • Antonym: obedient - willing to comply with orders or requests

Etymology

From Old French rebellieus, from Latin rebellis (waging war again, insurgent).
