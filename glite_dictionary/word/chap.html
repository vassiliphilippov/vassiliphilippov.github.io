chap

1. NOUN

/ tʃæp / (AmE), / tʃap / (BrE)
A crack or split in the skin, especially on the lips or hands, typically caused by cold or dry weather.
crack in skin, especially from cold weather

Examples

Wearing lip balm can help prevent your lips from developing a painful chap in winter.
She touched the chap on her lip, wincing as it stung from the cold air.
After a week of hiking in the mountains, he noticed a chap forming on his hands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fissure - a long, narrow crack or opening

Etymology

From Middle English chappen, probably from Old English cēapian (to bargain, trade), later developing the sense of 'crack' in skin.

2. VERB

/ tʃæp / (AmE), / tʃap / (BrE)
(of the skin or lips) to crack, roughen, or become sore, especially as a result of exposure to cold or dry weather.
to crack, especially of skin or lips

Examples

My hands chap quickly in the cold winter air.
If you don’t use lip balm, your lips might chap during the dry season.
Wind and sun can easily chap unprotected skin.

Synset Examples

My lips chap in this dry weather

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crack - to break partially without dividing into separate parts; to develop a narrow opening
  • Antonym: to heal - to become healthy or well again; to repair a wound or damage

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *chappen*, of uncertain origin, perhaps related to Dutch *kappen* (to cut).

3. NOUN

/ tʃæp / (AmE), / tʃap / (BrE)
A protective leather leg covering, usually worn in pairs (chaps), without a seat, secured by a belt, and typically worn by cowboys over trousers to protect the legs.
leather leg covering worn by cowboys
american

Examples

The cowboy strapped on a chap before heading out to tend the cattle.
One chap was torn during his ride through the thorny brush.
He examined each chap for signs of wear before the rodeo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaps - leather leggings worn over trousers by cowboys

Etymology

From Mexican Spanish 'chaparreras', derived from 'chaparro' (a type of low scrub), referring to the brushy terrain where such protection was useful.
