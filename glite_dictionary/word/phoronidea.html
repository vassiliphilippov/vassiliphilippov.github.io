Phoronidea

1. NOUN

/ foʊˌroʊnɪˈdiːə / (AmE), / fəˌrɒnɪˈdiːə / (BrE)
A small phylum of wormlike marine animals characterized by a tubular structure and a crown of tentacles surrounding the mouth.
phylum of wormlike marine animals

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the Phoronidea phylum near the Pacific coast.
Members of the Phoronidea are known for their distinctive lophophore feeding structures.
The biology class examined the tubular bodies of marine worms in the phylum Phoronidea.

Etymology

From genus name Phoronis, ultimately from Greek Phorṓn (a mythological figure) + -idea, a taxonomic suffix.
