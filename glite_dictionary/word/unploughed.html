unploughed

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈplaʊd / (AmE), / ʌnˈplaʊd / (BrE)
(of land) not ploughed or turned over with a plough, especially for farming.
not ploughed (of land)

Examples

The farmer left several acres of unploughed land for wildflowers to grow naturally.
After a harsh winter, parts of the field remained unploughed until spring.
Local wildlife found shelter among the unploughed hedgerows bordering the property.

Synset Examples

unplowed fields
unbroken land

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ploughed - of land, turned over with a plough
