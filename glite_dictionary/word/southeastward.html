southeastward

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaʊtˈiːstwɚd / (AmE), / ˌsaʊθˈiːstwəd / (BrE)
The compass point between south and east, corresponding to 135 degrees.
direction midway between south and east

Examples

The ship altered its course to a southeastward direction to avoid the storm.
Strong winds blew from the southeastward throughout the afternoon.
Migratory birds followed a southeastward route during the autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: southeast - the direction halfway between south and east

Etymology

From 'south' + 'east' + 'ward', indicating direction toward the southeast.

2. ADV

/ ˌsaʊθˈistwərd / (AmE), / ˌsaʊθˈiːstwəd / (BrE)
In or toward the southeast.
in a southeast direction

Examples

The storm system drifted southeastward overnight, bringing heavy rain to the coastal areas.
Migrating birds tend to travel southeastward each autumn to reach warmer climates.
The explorers set off southeastward, hoping to reach the distant valley by sunrise.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
