millerite

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlɚˌaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɪləraɪt / (BrE)
a yellow mineral composed of nickel sulfide, serving as a minor source of nickel
a yellow nickel sulfide mineral

Examples

The geologist identified the bright yellow streak as millerite embedded in the rock.
Rare specimens of millerite are prized by mineral collectors for their distinctive color and crystal form.
Mining companies extract millerite as one of the minor sources of nickel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nickel - a silvery-white metallic element

Etymology

Named after British mineralogist William Hallowes Miller (1801–1880).
