vena cerebri

1. NOUN

/ ˈviːnə səˈriːbraɪ / (AmE), / ˈviːnə sɛˈriːbrɪ / (BrE)
any of several veins that drain blood from the cerebral hemispheres of the brain.
veins serving the brain's cerebral hemispheres
medical

Examples

The vena cerebri transport deoxygenated blood away from the brain's cortex.
Damage to a vena cerebri can lead to neurological complications.
During brain surgery, surgeons must identify the position of each vena cerebri.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cerebral vein - a vein that drains blood from the cerebrum
  • Antonym: arteria cerebri - an artery serving the cerebral hemispheres of the brain

Etymology

From Latin vena (vein) and cerebri (of the brain).
