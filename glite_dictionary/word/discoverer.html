discoverer

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈskʌvɚɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈskʌvərə / (BrE)
a person who is the first to find, observe, or identify something that was previously unknown or unrecognized
person who finds something first

Examples

Christopher Columbus is often called the discoverer of America, although others arrived before him.
The discoverer of the new species was awarded a prestigious prize.
As the discoverer of the hidden cave, she was invited to speak at the conference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explorer - a person who travels to new places to learn about them

Etymology

From discover + -er.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈskʌvərər / (AmE), / dɪˈskʌvərə / (BrE)
a person who finds or invents something for the first time
a person who discovers something

Examples

Marie Curie is celebrated as the discoverer of radium.
The discoverer of the new plant species received international fame.
Every great invention has a discoverer behind it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inventor - a person who creates something for the first time
  • Synonym: explorer - a person who travels to new places to learn about them
  • Antonym: follower - someone who comes after or imitates another

Etymology

From Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'cooperire' (to cover), meaning 'to uncover'.
