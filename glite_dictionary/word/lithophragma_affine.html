Lithophragma affine

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪθoʊˈfræɡmə əˈfaɪn / (AmE), / ˌlɪθəˈfræɡmə əˈfiːn / (BrE)
A perennial herb native to California, cultivated for its racemose white flowers with widely spreading petals; sometimes classified in the genus Tellima.
a California perennial herb with white flowers

Examples

The delicate white flowers of Lithophragma affine bloom each spring in California’s coastal hills.
Gardeners sometimes choose Lithophragma affine for its attractive racemose flower clusters.
Botanists occasionally place Lithophragma affine in the genus Tellima due to its similarities.

Etymology

From Greek 'lithos' (stone) + 'phragma' (fence) and Latin 'affinis' (related).
