dickey-seat

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪki sit / (AmE), / ˈdɪki siːt / (BrE)
A small extra seat in the back of some old-fashioned two-seater cars, often folding and exposed to the weather.
small rear seat in old cars
british

Examples

The vintage roadster still has its original dickey-seat at the back, perfect for an extra passenger.
On sunny days, children loved to ride in the dickey-seat despite its exposure to the wind.
He restored the old car, including the rare dickey-seat that folded out from the trunk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rumble seat - folding seat in the rear of an early automobile

Etymology

From British English 'dickey', meaning a folding seat at the back of a carriage or car.
