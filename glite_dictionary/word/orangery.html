orangery

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrəndʒəri / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪndʒəri / (BrE)
a building or greenhouse, especially one from the 17th to 19th centuries, where orange trees and other citrus plants are grown, typically in cooler climates
greenhouse for orange trees

Examples

The restored orangery in the palace gardens is open to visitors all year round.
In the eighteenth century, wealthy Europeans built grand orangeries to house their exotic fruit trees.
The estate boasts an elegant orangery filled with lemon and orange trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greenhouse - a glass building for growing plants

Etymology

From French orangerie, from orange (the fruit).
