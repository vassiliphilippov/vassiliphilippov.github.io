smaltite

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmæl.taɪt / (AmE), / ˈsmɔːl.taɪt / (BrE)
a grey mineral consisting chiefly of cobalt arsenide with some nickel and iron, used as an ore of cobalt and nickel
a mineral of cobalt arsenide, source of cobalt and nickel

Examples

The geologist identified the shiny grey mineral sample as smaltite.
Rich deposits of smaltite were once an important source of cobalt for industrial uses.
Miners extracted smaltite from the vein to supply cobalt for making pigments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skutterudite - a related cobalt arsenide mineral, often containing nickel and iron

Etymology

From German Smaltit, related to 'smalt' (a cobalt-containing blue glass pigment) + German '-it' (mineral suffix).
