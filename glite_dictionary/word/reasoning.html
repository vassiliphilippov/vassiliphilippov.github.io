reasoning

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːzənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈriːz(ə)nɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of thinking about something in a logical and coherent way in order to form a conclusion or judgment
logical thinking process

Examples

Her reasoning was clear and persuasive, convincing everyone in the meeting.
We could not follow the judge's line of reasoning in the final verdict.
Critical thinking relies heavily on the ability for logical reasoning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: logic - reasoning conducted or assessed according to strict principles of validity
  • Antonym: intuition - the ability to understand something instinctively, without the need for conscious reasoning

Etymology

From 'reason' + -ing, with 'reason' from Old French 'raison', from Latin 'ratio' (reckoning, understanding).
