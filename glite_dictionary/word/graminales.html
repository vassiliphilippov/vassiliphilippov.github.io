Graminales

1. NOUN

/ ɡræmɪˈneɪliːz / (AmE), / ɡræmɪˈneɪliːz / (BrE)
An order of flowering plants that includes grasses, sedges, and rushes.
order of grasses, sedges, and rushes

Examples

The order Graminales contains some of the world’s most important cereal crops.
Botanists classify both wheat and rice in the Graminales.
Many prairies are dominated by species from the Graminales order.

Etymology

From Latin 'gramen' (grass) + '-ales' (order), used in botanical nomenclature.
