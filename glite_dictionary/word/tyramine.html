tyramine

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪrəˌmiːn / (AmE), / ˈtɪərəmiːn / (BrE)
an amino acid derived from tyrosine, acting as a sympathomimetic agent and found in certain foods such as cheese, chocolate, and beer
an amino acid found in some foods
medical

Examples

Foods high in tyramine can trigger headaches in some people.
Chocolate and aged cheese are common sources of tyramine.
Patients taking MAO inhibitors must monitor their tyramine intake carefully.

Synset Examples

patients taking MAOIs should avoid foods containing tyramine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tyrosine - an amino acid from which tyramine is derived

Etymology

From Greek 'tyros' (cheese) + '-amine' (chemical suffix).
