uric

1. ADJ

/ ˈjʊrɪk / (AmE), / ˈjʊərɪk / (BrE)
relating to or derived from urine, especially used to describe substances found in urine.
relating to urine or its substances
medical

Examples

High levels of uric acid in the blood can lead to gout.
Doctors tested for uric crystals in the joint fluid to confirm the diagnosis.
Certain foods can increase uric acid production in the body.

Synset Examples

uric acid

Etymology

From French urique, from Greek ouron (urine).
