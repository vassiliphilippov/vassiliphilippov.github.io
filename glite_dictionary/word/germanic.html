Germanic

1. ADJ

/ dʒɚˈmænɪk / (AmE), / dʒəˈmænɪk / (BrE)
relating to the ancient Teutons or to the group of languages they spoke, which includes German, English, Dutch, and the Scandinavian languages
relating to ancient Teutons or their languages

Examples

The Germanic tribes played a major role in European history.
English is classified as a Germanic language.
Many Germanic legends have influenced modern storytelling.

Synset Examples

Teutonic peoples such as Germans and Scandinavians and British
Germanic mythology

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Teutonic - relating to the Teutons or their languages

Etymology

From Latin 'Germanicus', from 'Germani' (the Germanic people).

2. NOUN

/ dʒərˈmænɪk / (AmE), / dʒəˈmænɪk / (BrE)
a branch of the Indo-European family of languages, including the Scandinavian and West Germanic groups, such as English, German, and Dutch
branch of Indo-European languages

Examples

English, German, and Dutch all belong to the Germanic branch of languages.
Linguists have traced many English words back to their Germanic roots.
Swedish and Danish are both classified as Germanic languages.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Romance - branch of Indo-European languages derived from Latin
  • Antonym: Slavic - branch of Indo-European languages spoken in Eastern Europe

Etymology

From Latin 'Germanicus', relating to 'Germani', a group of peoples living in Northern Europe.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06959259-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ dʒərˈmænɪk / (AmE), / dʒɜːˈmænɪk / (BrE)
relating to the German language or to the group of closely related languages that includes German, English, Dutch, and the Scandinavian languages
relating to the Germanic language family

Examples

Philologists have long studied the Germanic sound shifts that distinguish English from its relatives.
The Germanic languages share many common roots and grammatical features.
Old English is classified as a Germanic language due to its structure and vocabulary.

Synset Examples

the Germanic sound shifts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Teutonic - relating to the Germanic peoples or their languages

Etymology

From Latin 'Germanicus', from 'Germani' (a people of central Europe).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03079758-a
Synset POS: a
