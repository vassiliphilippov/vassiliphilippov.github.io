Prostigmin

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈstɪɡmɪn / (AmE), / prəʊˈstɪɡmɪn / (BrE)
A cholinergic drug (trade name for neostigmine) used to treat certain eye conditions and myasthenia gravis.
trade name for a drug (neostigmine)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Prostigmin to help manage the patient's myasthenia gravis symptoms.
Some ophthalmologists use Prostigmin in the treatment of certain eye disorders.
Before surgery, the nurse checked if the patient had any allergies to Prostigmin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neostigmine - a cholinesterase inhibitor used in medicine

Etymology

Trade name coined in the 1930s for neostigmine.
