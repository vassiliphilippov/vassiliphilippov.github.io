flagon

1. NOUN

/ ˈflæɡ.ən / (AmE), / ˈflæɡ.ən / (BrE)
a large container, usually with a handle and spout, made of metal or pottery, used for holding alcoholic drinks, especially wine
large vessel for alcoholic drinks

Examples

The innkeeper brought a flagon of wine to the weary travelers.
A silver flagon stood at the center of the banquet table, glinting in the candlelight.
He poured ale from the heavy flagon into each guest's mug.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jug - a large container with a handle and spout for holding liquids

Etymology

From Old French flacon, from Late Latin *flasco(n-)* (bottle, flask).
