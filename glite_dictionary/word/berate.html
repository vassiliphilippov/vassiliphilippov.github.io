to berate

1. VERB

/ bəˈreɪt / (AmE), / bɪˈreɪt / (BrE)
to scold or criticize (someone) angrily and severely
criticize angrily; scold severely

Examples

The teacher began to berate the student for not turning in his homework on time.
She didn't deserve to be berated in front of her colleagues for a minor mistake.
His boss would often berate him for every small error, making the workplace stressful.

Synset Examples

The mother scolded the child for entering a stranger's car
The deputy ragged the Prime Minister
The customer dressed down the waiter for bringing cold soup

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scold - to reprimand or criticize angrily
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration for someone

Etymology

From Latin 'beratare', influenced by Old French 'berater' (to accuse, blame).
