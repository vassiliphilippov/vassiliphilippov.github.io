rascal

1. NOUN

/ ˈræskəl / (AmE), / ˈrɑːskəl / (BrE)
a person who behaves dishonestly or mischievously, often in a playful or harmless way
dishonest or mischievous person

Examples

The young rascal played a prank on his teacher, but everyone laughed at his harmless mischief.
That rascal stole my sandwich when I wasn't looking!
She caught the little rascal sneaking cookies from the kitchen again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scoundrel - a dishonest or unscrupulous person
  • Synonym: rogue - a dishonest or unprincipled man
  • Synonym: villain - a person who is cruelly malicious

Etymology

Middle English, from Old French 'rascaille' meaning 'rabble, mob'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈræs.kəl / (AmE), / ˈrɑː.skəl / (BrE)
a person, especially a child, who behaves in a playfully mischievous or cheeky way
playfully mischievous person

Examples

My little brother is a real rascal—he’s always getting into some kind of trouble.
The puppy acted like a rascal, stealing socks and hiding them under the sofa.
Everyone called her a rascal for playing harmless pranks on her classmates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scamp - a person, especially a child, who is mischievous in a likable or amusing way
  • Antonym: villain - a wicked or evil person; someone who does bad things

Etymology

From Middle English rascal (a wretched person), from Old French rascaille (rabble, mob).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10219666-n
Synset POS: n
