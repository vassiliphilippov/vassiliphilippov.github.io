zebibit

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɛbɪˌbɪt / (AmE), / ˈzɛbɪˌbɪt / (BrE)
a unit of information equal to 1024 exbibits, or 2^70 (1,180,591,620,717,411,303,424) bits
unit of information (2^70 bits)

Examples

One zebibit is much larger than any amount of information most computers process today.
Data storage capacity is sometimes measured in zebibits for extremely large-scale systems.
The theoretical network could handle up to half a zebibit of traffic per second.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: exbibit - a unit of information equal to 2^60 bits
  • Degree Higher: yobibit - a unit of information equal to 1024 zebibits or 2^80 bits

Etymology

Formed from 'zebi-' (binary prefix for 2^70, from 'zetta-' and 'binary') and 'bit' (binary digit).
