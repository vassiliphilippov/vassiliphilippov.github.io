prospectus

1. NOUN

/ prəˈspɛktəs / (AmE), / prəˈspɛktəs / (BrE)
a formal written document offering securities for sale, providing details about a proposed business enterprise for investors
formal document offering securities for sale

Examples

The company issued a prospectus outlining the terms of its upcoming stock offering.
Every investor should read the prospectus carefully before committing any money to the fund.
According to the prospectus, the funds raised will be used to expand operations and research new products.

Synset Examples

a prospectus should contain the facts that an investor needs to make an informed decision

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brochure - informational booklet, often for marketing or advertising

Etymology

From Latin prospectus (view, outlook) via Late Latin in the sense of 'statement of expectations'.

2. NOUN

/ prəˈspɛktəs / (AmE), / prəˈspɛktəs / (BrE)
a booklet or document providing details about an institution, especially a college or university, and its courses, facilities, and services
booklet outlining courses and information about an institution

Examples

The university sent me a prospectus outlining all its engineering courses.
Before applying, she read through the prospectus to learn more about student life and facilities.
Most students request a prospectus to compare different colleges before making a decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brochure - a small booklet or pamphlet containing information

Etymology

From Latin 'prospectus' (view, prospect).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06687692-n
Synset POS: n
