payment

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪmənt / (AmE), / ˈpeɪmənt / (BrE)
An amount of money that is paid, or the act of settling a financial obligation.
money paid or act of settling a debt

Examples

She received the final payment for the car yesterday.
Late payment will result in an additional fee.
He arranged monthly payments to settle his loan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repayment - the act of paying back money
  • Antonym: receipt - the act of receiving money or goods

Etymology

From Old French *paiment* (payment), from Latin *pacare* (to pacify, to pay).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪmənt / (AmE), / ˈpeɪmənt / (BrE)
the act of giving money that is owed for goods or services; the process of paying
the act of giving money; the process of paying

Examples

Your payment is due by the end of the month.
She made the final payment on her car last week.
Online payment is available through our website.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remittance - the sending of money to someone at a distance
  • Antonym: receipt - the act of receiving money that has been paid

Etymology

From Old French 'paiement', from 'payer' meaning 'to pay', from Latin 'pacare' (to pacify, satisfy a debt).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01122769-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪmənt / (AmE), / ˈpeɪmənt / (BrE)
the act of repaying or returning something in kind as a response or recompense
an act of returning in kind or recompense

Examples

As a payment for his kindness, she helped him with his work.
He saw the trouble as a payment for past mistakes.
They viewed the apology as a just payment for the harm done.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: requital - the act of returning or recompensing
  • Synonym: retaliation - the act of returning a like for a like

Etymology

From Old French *paiment*, from Latin *pacare* (to pacify, appease).
