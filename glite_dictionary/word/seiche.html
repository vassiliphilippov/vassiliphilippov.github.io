seiche

1. NOUN

/ seɪʃ / (AmE), / seɪʃ / (BrE)
A wave oscillation on the surface of an enclosed or partially enclosed body of water, such as a lake or bay, caused by atmospheric or seismic disturbances.
oscillating wave in a lake or bay

Examples

The earthquake triggered a seiche that lasted several hours in the lake.
Scientists measured the height of the seiche following the severe thunderstorm.
A sudden change in wind direction can induce a noticeable seiche in large reservoirs.

Etymology

From French 'seiche', of unknown origin, possibly from a Swiss French dialect.
