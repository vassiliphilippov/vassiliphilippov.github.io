Arisarum

1. NOUN

/ ˌærɪˈsɑːrəm / (AmE), / ˌærɪˈsɑːrəm / (BrE)
A genus of tuberous or rhizomatous perennial herbs native mainly to the Mediterranean area, classified in the family Araceae.
genus of Mediterranean perennial herbs

Examples

The unique flowers of the Arisarum can be found in shaded Mediterranean woodlands.
Botanists have identified several species within the Arisarum genus.
Gardeners appreciate Arisarum for its unusual appearance and ability to thrive in difficult soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arum - a plant of the family Araceae, commonly called arum or aroid

Etymology

From New Latin, derived from Greek 'arisaron', a type of arum.

2. NOUN

/ ˌærəˈsɑːrəm / (AmE), / ˌærɪˈsɑːrəm / (BrE)
a genus of tuberous or rhizomatous perennial herbs native mainly to the Mediterranean region
genus of Mediterranean perennial herbs

Examples

The Arisarum species are known for their unique hooded flowers.
Botanists have documented several new varieties within the Arisarum genus.
Gardeners in southern Europe often cultivate Arisarum for its ornamental foliage.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Arisarum,' the botanical genus name, which is derived from ancient Greek 'arisaron,' a plant name used by Dioscorides.
