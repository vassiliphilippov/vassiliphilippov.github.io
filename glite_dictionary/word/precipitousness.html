precipitousness

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈsɪp.ə.tʃuːs.nəs / (AmE), / prɪˈsɪp.ɪ.tjuːs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being extremely steep, especially with reference to slopes, cliffs, or rises
state of being very steep

Examples

The precipitousness of the mountain path made it dangerous for inexperienced hikers.
Photographers were amazed by the precipitousness of the seaside cliffs.
Engineers had to find a way to reduce the precipitousness of the slope before constructing the road.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steepness - the quality of being steep

Etymology

From 'precipitous' (steep), from Latin praeceps (headlong, steep) + -ness (state or quality).

2. NOUN

/ prɪˈsɪp.ɪ.tʃuːs.nəs / (AmE), / prɪˈsɪp.ɪ.tjuːs.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of happening suddenly or with great haste, often without warning or preparation.
quality of suddenness or haste

Examples

The precipitousness of his resignation left everyone shocked and unprepared.
She was alarmed by the precipitousness with which the storm arrived.
Business leaders criticized the precipitousness of the government's sudden policy change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suddenness - The quality of occurring quickly or unexpectedly
  • Synonym: abruptness - The quality of being sudden or unexpected

Etymology

From the adjective 'precipitous', ultimately from Latin 'praeceps' (headlong).
