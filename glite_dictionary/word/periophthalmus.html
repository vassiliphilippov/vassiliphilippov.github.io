Periophthalmus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛriˌɑfˈθælməs / (AmE), / ˌpɛriˌɒfˈθælməs / (BrE)
A genus of amphibious fish in the family Gobiidae, commonly known as mudskippers, noted for their ability to move on land as well as in water.
a genus of amphibious fish (mudskipper)

Examples

The genus Periophthalmus includes fish that can breathe air and move about on land.
Researchers observed the unique locomotion of Periophthalmus species along the muddy banks.
Periophthalmus is often cited as an example of evolutionary adaptation to amphibious life.

Etymology

From Greek 'peri-' (around) and 'ophthalmos' (eye), referring to the fish’s prominent eyes.

2. NOUN

/ ˌperiˌɑfˈθælməs / (AmE), / ˌpɛriɒfˈθælməs / (BrE)
A genus of gobies known for their ability to live both in water and on land, commonly called mudskippers.
genus of amphibious gobies (mudskippers)

Examples

The Periophthalmus genus includes several species that are famous for their amphibious lifestyle.
Researchers observed Periophthalmus moving between tidal pools during low tide.
The unique adaptations of Periophthalmus have made them a subject of evolutionary studies.

Etymology

From Greek 'peri-' (around) and 'ophthalmos' (eye), referring to fishes with prominent eyes.
