Vespa crabro

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛspə ˈkreɪbroʊ / (AmE), / ˈvɛspə ˈkrɑːbrəʊ / (BrE)
A large species of hornet native to Europe and parts of Asia, commonly known as the European hornet, and introduced to North America.
European hornet species

Examples

The Vespa crabro is known for its characteristic yellow and brown markings.
In some regions, the Vespa crabro has become established as an invasive species.
Many beekeepers take precautions to protect their hives from Vespa crabro attacks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hornet - a large stinging wasp of the genus Vespa

Etymology

From Latin: vespa (wasp, hornet) and crabro (hornet).
