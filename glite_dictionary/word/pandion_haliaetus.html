Pandion haliaetus

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæn.di.ɑn ˌhæ.liˈeɪ.təs / (AmE), / ˈpæn.dɪ.ɒn ˌhæ.liˈeɪ.təs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the osprey, a large fish-eating bird of prey found worldwide, known for building bulky nests often used for years
osprey; large fish-eating bird of prey

Examples

The Pandion haliaetus can often be seen hovering over lakes and rivers in search of fish.
Many conservation programs focus on protecting the nesting sites of Pandion haliaetus.
A detailed study of the migration patterns of Pandion haliaetus was published last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: osprey - common English name for Pandion haliaetus, a large fish-eating bird of prey

Etymology

From Latin *Pandion haliaetus*, the taxonomic name for the osprey, from Greek 'pandion' (mythical king) and 'haliaetos' (sea eagle).
