unvitrified

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈvɪtrɪˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈvɪtrɪˌfaɪd / (BrE)
(of ceramics) not having a glass-like, hard, shiny surface; lacking a vitreous finish.
not having a vitreous (glass-like) finish

Examples

The archaeologist identified several unvitrified pottery fragments at the excavation site.
Unlike porcelain, earthenware is often unvitrified and can absorb water.
The unvitrified surface of the plate gave it a natural, rustic appearance.

Synset Examples

unvitrified pottery

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vitrified - converted into glass or a glass-like substance, usually by heat

Etymology

From the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'vitrified', from Latin vitreus (glass).
