Scombridae

1. NOUN

/ skɑmˈbrɪdiː / (AmE), / skɒmˈbrɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of marine fish including mackerels, chub mackerels, and tuna, found widely in ocean waters and valued as food
family of fish: mackerels and tuna

Examples

Species in the Scombridae family are important for commercial fisheries worldwide.
The diet of dolphins often consists of members of the Scombridae, such as mackerel and tuna.
Environmental changes can greatly affect populations within the Scombridae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mackerel - an edible marine fish in the Scombridae family
  • Synonym: tuna - a large marine fish in the Scombridae family

Etymology

From New Latin Scombridae, based on Latin scomber (mackerel).

2. NOUN

/ skɑmˈbrɪdi / (AmE), / skɒmˈbrɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of marine fishes that includes mackerels, chub mackerels, and tuna, known for their streamlined bodies and importance as food fish
a family of fishes including mackerels and tuna

Examples

The Scombridae family includes some of the most commercially valuable fish, such as tuna and mackerel.
Many species in the Scombridae family are prized for their speed and agility in open water.
Overfishing is a major concern for members of the Scombridae family due to their popularity as seafood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mackerel - a marine fish belonging to the family Scombridae
  • Synonym: tuna - a large marine fish of the family Scombridae

Etymology

From New Latin *Scombridae*, based on *Scomber* (mackerel genus), from Greek *skómbrōs* (mackerel).
