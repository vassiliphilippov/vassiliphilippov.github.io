William Penn

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪljəm pɛn / (AmE), / ˈwɪljəm pɛn / (BrE)
(1644–1718) An English Quaker and founder of the American colony of Pennsylvania.
English Quaker; founder of Pennsylvania

Examples

William Penn established Pennsylvania as a place of religious freedom for Quakers and others.
Many American cities still honor William Penn with statues and street names.
The ideals of William Penn had a lasting impact on religious tolerance in America.
