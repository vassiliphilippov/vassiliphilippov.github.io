predaceous

1. ADJ

/ prɪˈdeɪʃəs / (AmE), / prɪˈdeɪʃəs / (BrE)
(of an animal) hunting and killing other animals for food; predatory.
hunting and killing for food

Examples

The predaceous instincts of wolves make them effective hunters in the wild.
Many beetle species are predaceous, feeding on other insects.
Some birds develop predaceous habits, preying on smaller animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predatory - hunting and feeding on other animals

Etymology

From Latin praeda (booty, prey) + -aceous (resembling or pertaining to).

2. ADJ

/ prɪˈdeɪʃəs / (AmE), / prɪˈdeɪʃəs / (BrE)
(of animals or people) living by preying on others or victimizing others for personal gain.
living by preying on others

Examples

The lions are predaceous hunters that stalk their prey in the savanna.
Some companies have developed predaceous business strategies to eliminate competitors.
He warned against trusting predaceous individuals who exploit the vulnerable.

Synset Examples

predatory capitalists
a predatory, insensate society in which innocence and decency can prove fatal
a predacious kind of animal — the early geological gangster

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predatory - living by preying on others

Etymology

From Latin praeda (prey) + -aceous (having the nature of).
