curie

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjʊri / (AmE), / ˈkjʊəri / (BrE)
a unit of radioactivity equal to the activity of a substance undergoing 3.7 × 10¹⁰ disintegrations per second
unit of radioactivity

Examples

The laboratory measured the sample's radioactivity in curies to ensure safety standards were met.
The nuclear plant released a small fraction of a curie into the environment.
Medical isotopes are often expressed in curies to indicate their strength.

Etymology

Named after Marie and Pierre Curie, the French physicists known for their work on radioactivity.
