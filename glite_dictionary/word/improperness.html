improperness

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈprɑpɚnəs / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɒpənəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being improper, especially in manners or behavior.
state of being improper

Examples

Her improperness at the formal dinner shocked many of the attendees.
The committee discussed the improperness of his remarks during the meeting.
She was criticized for the improperness of her response to the serious situation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impropriety - the quality of being improper; an improper act or remark
  • Antonym: properness - the quality of being proper; appropriateness

Etymology

Formed from 'improper' + '-ness'.
