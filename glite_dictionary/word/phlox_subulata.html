Phlox subulata

1. NOUN

/ ˈfloʊks ˌsʌbjʊˈleɪtə / (AmE), / ˈflɒks ˌsʌbjʊˈleɪtə / (BrE)
A low, tufted perennial plant with needlelike evergreen leaves and pink or white flowers, native to the United States and often grown as a ground cover.
a perennial flowering ground cover plant

Examples

In early spring, the Phlox subulata bursts into a vibrant carpet of flowers in our garden.
Gardeners often choose Phlox subulata for rock gardens because of its dense, mat-forming growth.
We planted Phlox subulata along the walkway to create a colorful and hardy ground cover.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moss phlox - common name for Phlox subulata, a flowering plant used as ground cover

Etymology

From modern Latin; genus name 'Phlox' from Greek phlox (flame), referring to the bright colors; 'subulata' from Latin subula (awl), referencing the needlelike leaves.
