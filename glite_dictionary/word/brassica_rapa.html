Brassica rapa

1. NOUN

/ brəˈsɪkə ˈreɪpə / (AmE), / brəˈsɪkə ˈrapə / (BrE)
A plant species cultivated for its large, fleshy, edible white or yellow root, known to include varieties such as turnip.
species of plant with edible root (e.g., turnip)

Examples

Farmers often grow Brassica rapa for its nutritious and versatile roots.
Some subspecies of Brassica rapa are popular in Asian cuisine.
The yellow root of Brassica rapa is commonly used in soups and stews.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turnip - a round root vegetable, variety of Brassica rapa

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Brassica' means cabbage and 'rapa' means turnip.
