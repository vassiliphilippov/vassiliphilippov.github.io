Sylviinae

1. NOUN

/ sɪlˈvaɪəˌni / (AmE), / sɪlˈvɪɪniː / (BrE)
a subfamily of Old World warblers, small insectivorous birds found mainly in Eurasia and Africa
subfamily of Old World warblers (birds)

Examples

The Sylviinae comprise several genera of Old World warblers.
Scientists recently revised the classification of birds in the Sylviinae subfamily.
Many members of the Sylviinae are known for their melodic songs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warbler - a small songbird, often with a trilling or warbling song

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on the type genus *Sylvia*, meaning 'woodland' or 'warbler.'

2. NOUN

/ sɪlˈviː.ɪˌniː / (AmE), / sɪlˈviː.ɪˌniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of birds, commonly known as the Old World warblers, within the family Sylviidae.
subfamily of Old World warblers (birds)

Examples

The Sylviinae include many of the small, insectivorous warblers found across Europe and Asia.
Recent taxonomic studies have led to the reclassification of certain species into the Sylviinae.
Birdwatchers often refer to the Sylviinae when identifying Old World warblers in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warbler - a small songbird from the family Sylviidae or related families

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from genus name Sylvia and the suffix -inae (denoting a zoological subfamily).
