co-respondent

1. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊ.rɪˈspɑːn.dənt / (AmE), / ˌkəʊ.rɪˈspɒn.dənt / (BrE)
a person who is named with a defendant in a divorce case, especially as the alleged partner in an adulterous relationship with the estranged spouse
person named in divorce case as adultery partner
legal

Examples

The court summoned the co-respondent to testify regarding the alleged affair.
In her petition for divorce, she named her husband's colleague as the co-respondent.
The judge considered evidence from both the defendant and the co-respondent before making a ruling.

Etymology

co- (together) + respondent; first used in English legal context in the 19th century.
