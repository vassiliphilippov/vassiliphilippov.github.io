tutsan

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌt.sən / (AmE), / ˈtʌt.sən / (BrE)
A deciduous Eurasian shrub (Hypericum androsaemum) with golden yellow flowers and reddish-purple fruits, sometimes used to make a soothing salve.
a Eurasian shrub with yellow flowers and red-purple fruit

Examples

The tutsan glowed with golden blossoms along the garden path.
Her grandmother always kept a jar of tutsan ointment for cuts and bruises.
Wild tutsans can be found growing abundantly in parts of western Europe.

Etymology

From French 'toute-saine', meaning 'all-heal', referring to the plant's medicinal properties.
