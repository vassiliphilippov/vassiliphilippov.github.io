Kazakhstan

1. PROPN

/ kæzəkˈstæn / (AmE), / kazækˈstɑːn / (BrE)
A large landlocked country in Central Asia, south of Russia and northeast of the Caspian Sea, formerly part of the Soviet Union.
country in Central Asia

Examples

She traveled to Kazakhstan for an international business conference last year.
The vast steppes of Kazakhstan are home to a diverse range of wildlife.
After the breakup of the Soviet Union, Kazakhstan declared its independence in 1991.

Etymology

From Russian 'Казахстан' (Kazakhstan), from Kazakh 'Қазақстан'.
