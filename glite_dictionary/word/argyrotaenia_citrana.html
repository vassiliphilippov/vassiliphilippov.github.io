Argyrotaenia citrana

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˌɡaɪroʊˌtiːniə sɪˈtreɪnə / (AmE), / ɑːˌɡaɪrəʊˌtiːniə sɪˈtreɪnə / (BrE)
A moth native to California whose larvae commonly infest oranges and other citrus fruits.
California moth; larvae infest oranges

Examples

The Argyrotaenia citrana is known to cause significant damage to orange crops in California.
Farmers monitor their orchards for signs of Argyrotaenia citrana larvae to protect their fruit.
Researchers studied the behavior of Argyrotaenia citrana to develop better pest control methods.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'argyros' (silver) and 'tainia' (band/ribbon), and Latin 'citrana' (relating to citrus).
