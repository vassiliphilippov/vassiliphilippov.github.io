VI

1. NUM

/ viː aɪ / (AmE), / viː aɪ / (BrE)
the Roman numeral representing the number six
Roman numeral for six

Examples

The clock struck VI as the sun began to set.
His favorite Super Bowl was VI, played in 1972.
Chapters I through VI were assigned for homework.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: six - the cardinal number equal to VI

Etymology

From Latin 'VI', combining 'V' (5) and 'I' (1) to signify 6.

2. NUM

/ viː aɪ / (AmE), / viː aɪ / (BrE)
the Roman numeral for the number six
Roman numeral for six

Examples

The clock struck VI, marking six in the evening.
The book's chapter was labeled as Chapter VI.
The ancient coin was inscribed with the number VI.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: six - the number 6
  • Degree Higher: VII - the Roman numeral for seven
  • Degree Lower: V - the Roman numeral for five

Etymology

From Latin numerals, in which 'V' represents five and 'I' represents one; together 'VI' signifies six.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02194472-s
Synset POS: s
