grin

1. VERB

/ ɡrɪn / (AmE), / ɡrɪn / (BrE)
to smile broadly, often by showing the teeth, sometimes as a sign of amusement, pleasure, or embarrassment
smile broadly, showing the teeth

Examples

She couldn't help but grin when she saw the surprise party waiting for her.
He grinned sheepishly as he admitted his mistake.
The children were grinning from ear to ear after receiving their gifts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smile - to make a facial expression of pleasure or amusement, often without showing teeth
  • Antonym: frown - to make a facial expression showing disapproval or displeasure

Etymology

Probably from Old English *grennian* (to show the teeth in pain or anger).

2. NOUN

/ ɡrɪn / (AmE), / ɡrɪn / (BrE)
A broad smile in which the corners of the mouth are turned up, usually showing pleasure or amusement.
broad, often joyful smile

Examples

He greeted everyone with a wide grin on his face.
Her mischievous grin made everyone laugh.
A nervous grin spread across his lips as he stepped onto the stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smile - a pleasant facial expression with the corners of the mouth turned up
  • Antonym: frown - a facial expression showing displeasure or worry

Etymology

From Old English *grennian* (to snarl, show the teeth), related to Middle Low German *grinnen*.

Synset POS: n
