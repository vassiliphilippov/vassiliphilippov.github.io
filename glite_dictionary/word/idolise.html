to idolise

1. VERB

/ ˈaɪdəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈaɪdəlaɪz / (BrE)
to admire, love, or revere someone or something greatly, often to an excessive or unquestioning degree
admire or revere greatly
british

Examples

As a child, she used to idolise her older brother, following him everywhere.
Many fans idolise pop stars, treating them as if they can do no wrong.
He was idolised by the public for his humanitarian work.

Synset Examples

Many teenagers idolized the Beatles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to idolize - American English spelling of 'idolise'
  • Synonym: to adore - to regard with deep, often excessive, love or respect
  • Antonym: to despise - to feel contempt or a strong dislike for someone or something

Etymology

From French *idolâtrer*, based on Latin *idolum* (idol) and Greek *eidolon* (image, idol).
