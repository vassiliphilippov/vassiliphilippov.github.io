Hideki Tojo

1. PROPN

/ hiˈdeɪki ˈtoʊdʒoʊ / (AmE), / hɪˈdeɪki ˈtəʊdʒəʊ / (BrE)
(1884–1948) Japanese army officer and political leader who served as Prime Minister of Japan during much of World War II, ordered the attack on Pearl Harbor, and was later executed as a war criminal.
Japanese leader and Prime Minister during WWII

Examples

Hideki Tojo was Japan's Prime Minister during the attack on Pearl Harbor in 1941.
After World War II, Hideki Tojo was tried by an international tribunal and found guilty of war crimes.
Many historians debate the extent of Hideki Tojo's responsibility for Japan's wartime actions.
