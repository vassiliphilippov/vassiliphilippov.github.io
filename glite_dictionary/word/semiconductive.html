semiconductive

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛmɪkənˈdʌktɪv / (AmE), / ˌsɛmɪkənˈdʌktɪv / (BrE)
Having properties characteristic of a semiconductor; exhibiting electrical conductivity greater than an insulator but less than a good conductor.
having semiconductor-like electrical properties

Examples

Silicon is a semiconductive material widely used in electronics.
The device relies on the semiconductive layer to control energy flow.
Researchers studied the semiconductive properties of the newly discovered compound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: semiconductor - a substance with intermediate electrical conductivity

Etymology

Formed from 'semiconductor' + '-ive', indicating possessing or pertaining to the properties of a semiconductor.
