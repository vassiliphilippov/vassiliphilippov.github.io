to interlace

1. VERB

/ ˌɪntərˈleɪs / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈleɪs / (BrE)
to entwine or lock together, especially by crossing parts such as fingers or hands
to entwine or lock together

Examples

She interlaced her fingers nervously as she waited for the interview to begin.
He interlaced his hands behind his head and leaned back in his chair.
The couple interlaced their arms as they strolled through the park.

Synset Examples

He locked his hands around her neck

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intertwine - to twist or twine together
  • Antonym: to unlace - to untie or loosen laced or interwoven things

Etymology

From Old French entrelacer, from entre- (between) + lacer (to lace).

2. VERB

/ ˌɪntərˈleɪs / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈleɪs / (BrE)
to twist, weave, or wind together two or more things so that they cross each other repeatedly; to entwine
weave or twist together

Examples

The artist carefully interlaced strands of gold into the fabric for a shimmering effect.
Their fingers interlaced as they walked down the street, hand in hand.
The branches of the old trees interlace above the path, creating a natural tunnel.

Synset Examples

intertwine the ribbons
Twine the threads into a rope
intertwined hearts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intertwine - to twist or twine together
  • Synonym: to entwine - to twist together; to wrap or wind around

Etymology

From Old French entrelacer, from entre- (between) + lacer (to lace).
