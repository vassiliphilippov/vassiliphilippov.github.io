to pour

1. VERB

/ pɔr / (AmE), / pɔː / (BrE)
to cause a liquid or other substance to flow from or into a container in a steady stream, especially by tipping the container
cause liquid to flow in a stream

Examples

She poured the coffee into two mugs and handed one to her guest.
It's easy to pour too much detergent if you're not careful.
Water poured over the edge of the sink onto the floor.

Synset Examples

pour water over the floor

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spill - cause or allow liquid to flow over the edge and be lost

Etymology

From Old French 'pourer', meaning 'to sift, pour out, decant'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02073479-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pɔr / (AmE), / pɔː / (BrE)
(of people or animals) to move somewhere in large numbers, especially quickly and in a steady stream.
move in large numbers

Examples

Crowds began to pour into the stadium as soon as the gates opened.
After the announcement, people poured out of the building in excitement.
Refugees poured across the border seeking safety.

Synset Examples

people were pouring out of the theater
beggars pullulated in the plaza

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stream - to move in a continuous flow
  • Synonym: flood - to arrive or move in overwhelming numbers
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02032212-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ pɔr / (AmE), / pɔː / (BrE)
(of rain) to fall heavily and in large amounts.
rain heavily

Examples

It's supposed to pour all day tomorrow, so bring an umbrella.
We got soaked because it started to pour just as we left the building.
As we drove through the countryside, it began to pour with rain.

Synset Examples

Put on your rain coat — it's pouring outside!

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: drizzle - to rain lightly in very small drops

4. VERB

/ pɔr / (AmE), / pɔː / (BrE)
to supply or invest something, especially money or resources, in large quantities
supply or invest in large amounts

Examples

After the hurricane, charitable organizations poured food and supplies into the affected region.
The government poured billions of dollars into the new infrastructure project.
Investors poured resources into the startup, hoping for rapid growth.

Synset Examples

We poured money into the education of our children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to invest - to put money, effort, or time into something to make a profit or achieve a result
  • Antonym: to withdraw - to remove or take away resources or money from a place or activity

5. VERB

/ pɔr / (AmE), / pɔː / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to flow rapidly or in a continuous stream, especially in large amounts or with force.
to flow or cause to flow rapidly

Examples

Rain began to pour down on the city, drenching everyone in minutes.
She accidentally tilted the jug and juice poured onto the table.
Tears poured from his eyes as he listened to the sad news.

Synset Examples

Water poured all over the floor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spill - to cause a liquid to flow over the edge of a container, usually by accident
  • Degree Lower: trickle - (of a liquid) to flow slowly in a thin stream

Etymology

From Old French 'pourer', meaning to sift or strain.
