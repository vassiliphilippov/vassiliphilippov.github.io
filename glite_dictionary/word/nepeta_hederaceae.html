Nepeta hederaceae

1. NOUN

/ nəˈpitə ˌhedəˈreɪsiː / (AmE), / nɪˈpiːtə ˌhɛdəˈreɪsiː / (BrE)
A trailing European aromatic plant of the mint family with rounded leaves and small purplish flowers, often cultivated in hanging baskets and naturalized in North America.
a species of trailing mint plant

Examples

The fragrant Nepeta hederaceae draped beautifully over the edge of the hanging basket.
Gardeners value Nepeta hederaceae for its hardy nature and attractive purple flowers.
Native to Europe, Nepeta hederaceae has adapted well in North American gardens.

Etymology

From Latin genus name Nepeta + specific epithet hederaceae, meaning 'ivy-like'.
