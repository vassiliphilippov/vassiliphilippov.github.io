Catalan

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætəlæn / (AmE), / ˈkatəlæn / (BrE)
the Romance language spoken in Catalonia, eastern Spain, and surrounding regions, closely related to Spanish and Occitan
a Romance language of Catalonia

Examples

She is fluent in both Spanish and Catalan.
The novel was originally written in Catalan before being translated.
Many schools in Barcelona teach Catalan as a primary language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spanish - the Romance language spoken in Spain and many countries worldwide
  • Synonym: Occitan - a Romance language spoken in southern France and parts of Italy and Spain

Etymology

From the native name 'català', via French 'catalan'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkætəˌlæn / (AmE), / ˈkætələn / (BrE)
A native or inhabitant of Catalonia, an autonomous community in northeastern Spain.
a person from Catalonia (Spain)

Examples

The Catalan greeted us warmly and offered to show us around Barcelona.
Many Catalans are proud of their distinct language and culture.
As a Catalan, she celebrates traditional festivals unique to the region.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Spaniard - a native or inhabitant of Spain
  • Synonym: Andalusian - a native or inhabitant of Andalusia, Spain

Etymology

From Catalonia, a region in Spain; ultimately from Latin 'Catalonia'.
