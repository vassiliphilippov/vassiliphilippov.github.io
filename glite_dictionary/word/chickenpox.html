chickenpox

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪk.ɪnˌpɑks / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪk.ɪnˌpɒks / (BrE)
an infectious disease causing an itchy rash of red spots and blisters, mainly affecting children, and caused by the varicella-zoster virus
infectious disease with itchy rash
medical

Examples

Many children catch chickenpox before they start school.
The doctor confirmed that the red spots were a symptom of chickenpox.
After recovering from chickenpox, a person usually develops immunity to the disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: varicella - medical term for chickenpox

Etymology

From 'chicken' + 'pox', possibly referring to the mildness of the disease compared to smallpox.
