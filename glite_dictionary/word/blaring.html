blaring

1. ADJ

/ ˈblɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbleərɪŋ / (BrE)
(of sound) extremely loud and harsh; unpleasantly loud and penetrating.
unpleasantly loud and harsh (of sound)

Examples

The blaring music from the nightclub kept us awake all night.
He covered his ears against the blaring siren of the ambulance as it passed by.
The blaring announcements at the train station made conversation almost impossible.

Synset Examples

the blaring noise of trumpets
shut our ears against the blasting music from his car radio

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deafening - so loud as to make hearing impossible
  • Antonym: soft - not loud; gentle in sound

Etymology

From the verb 'blare' (imitative of loud sound), with adjectival suffix -ing.
