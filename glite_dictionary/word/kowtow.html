kowtow

1. NOUN

/ kaʊˈtaʊ / (AmE), / kaʊˈtaʊ / (BrE)
an act of kneeling and touching the forehead to the ground as a sign of deep respect, worship, or submission, especially in former Chinese custom
act of deep respect or submission (Chinese custom)

Examples

The emperor expected every visitor to perform the kowtow upon entering the throne room.
Historians discussed the role of the kowtow in diplomatic ceremonies.
The painting depicts envoys performing the kowtow before the emperor.

Etymology

From Chinese kētóu, literally 'knock head'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07289980-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈkaʊˌtaʊ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊtaʊ / (BrE)
to kneel and touch the ground with the forehead in an act of worship or submission, or to act in an excessively subservient manner
act in a very submissive way

Examples

He refused to kowtow to the new management, even if it meant losing his job.
The ambassador was criticized for appearing to kowtow to foreign leaders in negotiations.
Some employees feel pressured to kowtow to their boss to get ahead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to grovel - to act in an excessively humble or servile manner

Etymology

From Chinese kǒu tóu (叩头), meaning 'to knock head' (as in a gesture of respect or submission).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02044554-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈkaʊˌtaʊ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊ.taʊ / (BrE)
to act in an excessively obedient or servile manner in order to gain favor or approval
act in an overly submissive manner

Examples

She refused to kowtow to the unreasonable demands of her manager.
In some companies, employees feel pressured to kowtow to upper management.
The young politician chose not to kowtow to party leaders, earning respect for his independence.

Synset Examples

He is always kowtowing to his boss

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fawn - to try to gain favor by acting overly submissive
  • Synonym: to grovel - to behave in a way that shows too much willingness to serve or please someone
  • Antonym: to defy - to openly resist or refuse to obey

Etymology

From Chinese 'kòu tóu' (叩头), literally 'knock head', a traditional act of deep respect involving kneeling and bowing the head to the ground.

4. VERB

/ ˌkaʊˈtaʊ / (AmE), / ˌkaʊˈtaʊ / (BrE)
to act in an excessively submissive manner in order to gain favor or approval; to flatter or be obsequious
act in an excessively submissive way

Examples

She refused to kowtow to the unreasonable demands of her superiors.
There is no need to kowtow to every suggestion just to be liked.
The new manager expects everyone to kowtow, but most employees resist his authority.

Synset Examples

He is always kowtowing to his boss

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fawn - to try to gain favor by flattering or acting submissively
  • Synonym: to flatter - to praise excessively, often to gain advantage

Etymology

From Mandarin Chinese 'kètou', literally 'knock head', referring to the act of kneeling and touching one's head to the ground.
