micromillimetre

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.kroʊˈmɪl.əˌmiː.tər / (AmE), / ˌmʌɪ.krəʊˈmɪl.ɪˌmiː.tə / (BrE)
a metric unit of length equal to one billionth (10⁻⁹) of a meter
one billionth of a meter (10⁻⁹ m)

Examples

Scientists once suggested measuring extremely small wavelengths in micromillimetres.
The micromillimetre is not commonly used in modern scientific literature, with nanometre being preferred.
Some conversion tables still list the micromillimetre, although it is now mostly obsolete.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nanometre - a metric unit of length equal to one billionth of a meter (10⁻⁹ m)

Etymology

From micro- (meaning 'one millionth') + millimetre, but technically redundant; rarely used in practice.
