Campeche

1. PROPN

/ kæmˈpeɪtʃi / (AmE), / kamˈpɛtʃeɪ / (BrE)
A port city in southeastern Mexico, located on the Bay of Campeche in the state of Campeche.
port city in southeastern Mexico

Examples

We visited Campeche last summer and admired its historic fortifications.
Many cruise ships dock at Campeche on their way through the Gulf of Mexico.
The colorful colonial buildings in Campeche are a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Campeche', likely from the Mayan 'Ah Kin Pech', meaning 'place of snakes and ticks'.

2. PROPN

/ kæmˈpeɪtʃeɪ / (AmE), / kamˈpɛtʃeɪ / (BrE)
a state in southeastern Mexico, located on the western side of the Yucatán Peninsula, bordering the Gulf of Mexico.
state in southeastern Mexico

Examples

We spent our vacation exploring the ancient ruins and beaches of Campeche.
Campeche is known for its well-preserved colonial architecture and historic fortifications.
The cuisine of Campeche features a rich blend of Mayan and Spanish influences.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Campeche', possibly from the Maya word 'Ah-Kin-Pech' meaning 'place of serpents and ticks.'
