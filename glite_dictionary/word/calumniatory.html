calumniatory

1. ADJ

/ kəˈlʌmniˌɛːtɔri / (AmE), / kəˈlʌmnɪətəri / (BrE)
(of statements) false and intended to harm someone’s reputation; slanderous or defamatory.
false and defamatory; slanderous

Examples

The article contained several calumniatory remarks that defamed the politician.
She sued the magazine for its calumniatory allegations about her personal life.
Such calumniatory statements can irreparably harm someone's reputation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defamatory - intended to damage someone's reputation; slanderous
  • Synonym: libelous - containing or constituting a written statement that is damaging to a person's reputation
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration; the opposite of defame

Etymology

From Latin calumniatorius, from calumnia (false accusation).
