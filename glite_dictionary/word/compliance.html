compliance

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈplaɪəns / (AmE), / kəmˈplaɪəns / (BrE)
the act of submitting or yielding to another's authority or control
act of submitting; yielding to authority

Examples

She acted in compliance with the director's requests, even when she disagreed.
The king demanded compliance from the rebellious provinces.
Their immediate compliance surprised those who expected resistance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obedience - the act of following orders or rules
  • Antonym: defiance - open resistance or bold disobedience

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'compliantia,' from 'compli-' (complied), from Latin 'complēre' (to fill up, accomplish).

2. NOUN

/ kəmˈplaɪəns / (AmE), / kəmˈplaɪəns / (BrE)
the act of obeying a law, rule, or request, or conforming to accepted standards
following rules or accepted standards

Examples

The company's strict compliance with environmental regulations earned it an industry award.
All employees are responsible for ensuring compliance with workplace safety rules.
Compliance with the new tax law is mandatory for all businesses.

Synset Examples

their financial statements are in conformity with generally accepted accounting practices

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conformity - behavior that matches socially accepted standards, rules, or laws
  • Antonym: defiance - open resistance to authority or rules

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *compliancia*, from Latin *compliēre* (to fill up, accomplish).

3. NOUN

/ kəmˈplaɪəns / (AmE), / kəmˈplaɪəns / (BrE)
the tendency or willingness to yield to the wishes or demands of others
willingness to yield to others

Examples

Her compliance made it easy for her coworkers to delegate tasks to her.
The manager was impressed by the team's compliance with his instructions.
He showed little compliance when asked to change his behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obedience - the act of obeying; willing to do what one is told
  • Antonym: defiance - open resistance or bold disobedience

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'compliantia', from Latin 'compliāre' (to fill up, accomplish), influenced by 'comply'.
