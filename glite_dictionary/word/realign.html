to realign

1. VERB

/ ˌriˈə.laɪn / (AmE), / ˌriːˈə.laɪn / (BrE)
to put something into a new or better alignment or position
put into new or improved alignment

Examples

After the repairs, the mechanic had to realign the wheels of my car.
Management decided to realign the company's priorities to better match market demands.
The physical therapist will realign your spine during the session.

Synset Examples

The surgeon realigned my jaw after the accident

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to align - to place in a straight line or proper position

Etymology

From re- (again) + align (from French aligner, from à ‘to’ + ligne ‘line’).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00466058-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌriːəˈlaɪn / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈlaɪn / (BrE)
to change the position or direction of something so that it is in a correct or better position; to align again.
align again or in a new way

Examples

After the printer jammed, I had to realign the paper trays to get it working again.
The company decided to realign its marketing strategy to better reach younger audiences.
The orthopedist will realign your broken bone so it heals properly.

Synset Examples

The surgeon realigned my jaw after the accident

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to align - to place or arrange things in a straight line or correct relative positions

Etymology

From re- (again) + align (from French 'aligner', from 'à ligne' meaning 'to the line').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00466058-v
Synset POS: v
