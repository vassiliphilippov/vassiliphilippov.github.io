sacrifice

1. VERB

/ ˈsækrɪˌfaɪs / (AmE), / ˈsækrɪfaɪs / (BrE)
to offer something valuable, especially an animal, to a deity or higher power as part of a religious ceremony
offer as a religious ritual

Examples

Many ancient civilizations would sacrifice animals during their religious festivals.
The priest sacrificed a lamb as an offering to the gods.
Some cultures sacrifice food to seek blessings for a good harvest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offer - present or give something, especially as a religious act

Etymology

From Latin sacrificare, from sacer (sacred) + facere (to make).

personnel or assets deliberately given up or lost in order to achieve a strategic objective, such as in military operations or competitive situations.

She refused to regard her fallen team as mere sacrifice for political gain.

5. NOUN

/ ˈsækrɪˌfaɪs / (AmE), / ˈsækrɪˌfaɪs / (BrE)
In baseball, an out made by a batter to advance one or more base runners, especially through a bunt or fly ball.
baseball out that advances runners

Examples

The coach signaled for a sacrifice to move the runner to third base.
He laid down a perfect sacrifice to help his team score.
After the sacrifice, both runners were in scoring position.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sacrifice bunt - a bunt in baseball intended to advance runners at the expense of being put out
  • Synonym: sacrifice fly - a fly ball in baseball resulting in a run scored after a catch

Etymology

From Latin sacrificium, meaning 'an offering to a deity', later applied metaphorically to sports actions involving giving up something for a team advantage.
