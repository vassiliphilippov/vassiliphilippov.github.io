Cassiope

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæsiˈoʊpi / (AmE), / ˌkæsiˈəʊpiː / (BrE)
A genus of small, low-growing evergreen shrubs found in cold, north temperate regions, with moss-like leaves and nodding white or pink flowers.
genus of small, cold-region evergreen shrubs

Examples

The Cassiope grows abundantly in the alpine tundra, forming dense mats.
Botanists often admire the delicate flowers of Cassiope in northern mountain regions.
During the summer, the Cassiope shrubs display small, bell-shaped blossoms.

Etymology

From Greek mythology: Cassiopeia, a queen; used in botanical nomenclature.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkæsiˈoʊpi / (AmE), / ˌkæsiˈəʊpiː / (BrE)
A genus of small, tufted evergreen shrubs found in cold northern temperate regions, with moss-like leaves and nodding white or pink flowers.
genus of low evergreen shrubs

Examples

The alpine meadows were dotted with Cassiope in full bloom.
Botanists collected several specimens of Cassiope from the Arctic slopes.
The delicate white bells of Cassiope stood out against the mossy ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heather - a similar low shrub found in moorland and heath

Etymology

Named after Cassiope, a queen in Greek mythology.
