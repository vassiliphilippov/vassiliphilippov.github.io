Myrica gale

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈraɪkə ˈɡeɪl / (AmE), / mɪˈrɪkə ˈɡeɪl / (BrE)
A small aromatic shrub of northern temperate regions, with bitter-tasting, fragrant leaves, found in boggy areas.
aromatic bog shrub of temperate regions

Examples

The wetlands are home to Myrica gale, which gives off a distinctive fragrance when touched.
Local brewers sometimes use Myrica gale as a substitute for hops in ale-making.
Botanists identified Myrica gale growing in the peat bog near the river.

Etymology

From Latin *Myrica* (the genus name) and specific epithet *gale* from Greek *galē*, perhaps referring to its historical uses.
