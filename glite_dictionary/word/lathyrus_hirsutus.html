Lathyrus hirsutus

1. NOUN

/ ləˈθaɪrəs hɪrˈsuːtəs / (AmE), / ləˈθaɪrəs hɪəˈsjuːtəs / (BrE)
A weak-stemmed annual plant of the pea family, native to the Mediterranean region and established in the southern United States, cultivated as a cover and pasture crop.
annual pea plant, cover crop

Examples

Lathyrus hirsutus is often planted in southern regions as a cover crop to improve soil fertility.
Farmers value Lathyrus hirsutus for its hardiness and ability to provide good pasture for livestock.
Although native to the Mediterranean, Lathyrus hirsutus has adapted well to the climate of the United States.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Lathyrus' (pea genus) and species epithet 'hirsutus' meaning 'hairy'.
