lousiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaʊzinəs / (AmE), / ˈlaʊzinəs / (BrE)
the condition of being infested with lice, typically causing severe itching
infestation with lice

Examples

The child's persistent scratching was due to lousiness picked up at school.
Poor hygiene can sometimes lead to lousiness in crowded living conditions.
After treatment, the symptoms of lousiness quickly disappeared.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pediculosis - infestation with lice (medical term)

Etymology

From louse + -iness, modeled on 'louse' with the noun-forming suffix '-iness.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaʊzinəs / (AmE), / ˈlaʊzinəs / (BrE)
the quality of being unpleasant or disgusting to the senses or emotions.
the state of being disgusting or unpleasant

Examples

After days of rain, the lousiness of the damp apartment became unbearable.
He complained about the lousiness of the service at the restaurant last night.
The lousiness of their attitudes made working there a miserable experience.

Synset Examples

the vileness of his language surprised us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vileness - the quality of being extremely unpleasant or morally bad
  • Synonym: filthiness - the state of being very dirty or disgusting

Etymology

From 'lousy' + '-ness', indicating the quality or state.
