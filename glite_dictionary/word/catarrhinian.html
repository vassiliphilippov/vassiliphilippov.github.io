catarrhinian

1. ADJ

/ ˌkætəˈrɪniən / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈrɪnɪən / (BrE)
relating to Old World monkeys or apes whose nostrils are close together and point downward.
relating to Old World monkeys and apes

Examples

The catarrhinian monkeys of Africa are distinguished by their downward-facing nostrils.
Anthropologists studied the differences between platyrrhine and catarrhinian primates.
Gibbons and macaques are both examples of catarrhinian species.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: platyrrhine - relating to New World monkeys with widely spaced, side-facing nostrils

Etymology

From Greek 'katarrhin', meaning 'with a downward nose' (kata- 'down' + rhis 'nose').
