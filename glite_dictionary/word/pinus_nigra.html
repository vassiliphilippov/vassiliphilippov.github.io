Pinus nigra

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪ.nəs ˈnaɪ.ɡrə / (AmE), / ˈpʌɪ.nəs ˈnɪɡ.rə / (BrE)
A large pine tree species native to southeastern Europe, characterized by its dark bark and two-needled leaves, valued for its timber.
European black pine species

Examples

The forest reserve is primarily composed of Pinus nigra, which provides valuable timber for the region.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Pinus nigra in its native southeastern European habitat.
The Pinus nigra planted along the street offers both shade and ornamental value.

Etymology

From Latin: pinus (pine) + nigra (black), referring to its dark bark.
