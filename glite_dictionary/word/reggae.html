reggae

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛɡeɪ / (AmE), / ˈreɡeɪ / (BrE)
A style of popular music that originated in Jamaica, characterized by a strong, syncopated rhythm and repetitive bass lines.
Jamaican style of popular music

Examples

Bob Marley is one of the most famous artists in reggae history.
The concert featured several bands playing classic reggae songs.
She enjoys listening to reggae because of its relaxed rhythms and uplifting melodies.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ska - a precursor genre to reggae with a faster tempo
  • Degree Higher: dub - a subgenre of reggae with heavy use of remix and effects

Etymology

From Jamaican English, possibly of imitative origin, referring to a style of music.
