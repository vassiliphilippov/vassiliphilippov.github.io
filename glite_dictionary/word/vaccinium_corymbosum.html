Vaccinium corymbosum

1. NOUN

/ vækˈsɪniəm kəˌrɪmbəˈsʌm / (AmE), / vækˈsɪniəm kəˌrɪmbəˈsʌm / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, known for producing edible bluish-black berries and being the primary source of cultivated blueberries.
species of blueberry shrub

Examples

Vaccinium corymbosum is commonly grown in commercial blueberry farms across North America.
With proper care, Vaccinium corymbosum can thrive in acidic garden soils and produce abundant fruit.
The berries of Vaccinium corymbosum are rich in antioxidants and enjoyed fresh or in baked goods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blueberry - A fruit produced by shrubs of the genus Vaccinium, typically blue to black in color.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Vaccinium' (a genus of berry-producing shrubs), 'corymbosum' (having flowers in clusters called corymbs).
