balkiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːki.nəs / (AmE), / ˈbɔːki.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being difficult to start, continue working, or likely to stop unexpectedly
tendency to stop or refuse to continue

Examples

The balkiness of the old lawn mower made yard work frustrating every spring.
Software updates were delayed due to the balkiness of the outdated computers.
Her horse's balkiness was well known among the trainers at the stable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balky - likely to stop or refuse to proceed

Etymology

From balky + -ness; 'balky' from 'balk' (to stop short), Middle English, from Old English 'balca' (ridge, bank).
