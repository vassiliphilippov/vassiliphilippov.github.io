Ramanavami

1. NOUN

/ rɑːməˈnɑːvi / (AmE), / ˌrɑːməˈnʌːvi / (BrE)
A Hindu festival celebrated on the ninth day of the Hindu month of Chaitra, marking the birth of the deity Rama.
Hindu festival celebrating Rama's birth

Examples

Many devotees observe Ramanavami by fasting and reciting the Ramayana.
The streets were filled with processions celebrating Ramanavami across several Indian cities.
Special prayers and religious events are held in temples during Ramanavami each year.

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Rāmanavamī', meaning 'the ninth day (navami) of Rama'.
