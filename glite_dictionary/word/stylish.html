stylish

1. ADJ

/ ˈstaɪlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈstaɪlɪʃ / (BrE)
Following or characteristic of current fashion and style; fashionable or elegant.
fashionable; elegant in style

Examples

She always wears stylish clothes that make her stand out at every event.
The restaurant has a stylish interior with modern decor and elegant lighting.
His apartment is both comfortable and stylish, reflecting his keen sense of design.

Synset Examples

fashionable clothing
the fashionable side of town
a fashionable cafe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fashionable - following the latest fashion; popular at a particular time
  • Antonym: unfashionable - not in accordance with current fashion; not stylish

Etymology

From English 'style' + '-ish' (having the qualities of).

2. ADJ

/ ˈstaɪlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈstaɪlɪʃ / (BrE)
having elegance or good taste in manner, appearance, or dress; fashionable and sophisticated
fashionable and elegant

Examples

She looked stylish in her new black dress at the party.
The restaurant's décor is both modern and stylish.
He always manages to appear stylish without trying too hard.

Synset Examples

a little less posh but every bit as stylish as Lord Peter Wimsey
the stylish resort of Gstadd

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fashionable - popular or in fashion at a particular time (synonym)
  • Synonym: elegant - graceful and stylish in appearance or manner (synonym)
  • Antonym: unfashionable - not in accordance with current fashion (antonym)

Etymology

From style + -ish, formed in English in the 18th century.
