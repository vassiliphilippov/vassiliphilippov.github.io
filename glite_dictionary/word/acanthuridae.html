Acanthuridae

1. NOUN

/ əˌkænˈθjʊrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / əˌkænˈθjʊərɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fishes known as surgeonfishes, characterized by a blade-shaped spine on either side of the tail.
family of marine fishes (surgeonfishes)

Examples

Several species within the Acanthuridae family are popular in tropical aquariums.
The bright colors and distinctive tail spines make Acanthuridae easily recognizable among marine biologists.
Researchers observed that members of Acanthuridae play a vital role in maintaining reef health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surgeonfish - a common name for fish in the Acanthuridae family

Etymology

From New Latin Acanthuridae, based on Greek akantha (thorn) and oura (tail), referring to the spine by the tail.

2. NOUN

/ əˌkænθjʊˈrɪdiː / (AmE), / əˌkanθjʊˈrɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of marine fish, commonly known as surgeonfishes, characterized by a laterally compressed body and sharp spines on the caudal peduncle
family of surgeonfishes

Examples

Surgeonfishes belong to the family Acanthuridae, well known for their colorful appearance and sharp spines.
The Acanthuridae are widely distributed in tropical marine waters.
Marine biologists study the habits and habitats of fish in the Acanthuridae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surgeonfish - common name for fish of the family Acanthuridae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Acanthuridae', based on genus 'Acanthurus' (from Greek 'akantha' meaning spine and 'oura' meaning tail).
