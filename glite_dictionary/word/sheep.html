sheep

1. NOUN

/ ʃip / (AmE), / ʃiːp / (BrE)
A person who is timid or easily influenced and lacks initiative, often following others without question.
timid or easily led person

Examples

He acts like a sheep in meetings, never giving his own opinion.
Many people blindly follow trends like sheep without questioning.
She was tired of being treated like a sheep and wanted to think for herself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: follower - a person who accepts the leadership of another
  • Antonym: leader - a person who leads or commands others

2. NOUN

/ ʃip / (AmE), / ʃiːp / (BrE)
A domesticated, typically woolly ruminant mammal (Ovis aries) kept for its wool, meat, and milk, often with horns.
domesticated woolly mammal

Examples

The farmer led the sheep into the barn before the storm began.
A flock of sheep grazed peacefully in the green meadow.
She spun yarn from the sheep's wool she collected last spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goat - a hardy domesticated ruminant mammal related to the sheep

Etymology

Old English sceap; related to Old High German scāf (sheep).

3. NOUN

/ ʃip / (AmE), / ʃiːp / (BrE)
A person who is docile, easily influenced, and follows others without independent thought or decision.
person who follows others blindly

Examples

Many people acted like sheep during the campaign, never questioning what they were told.
Don't be a sheep—stand up for your own beliefs!
The cult members followed their charismatic leader like obedient sheep.

Synset Examples

his students followed him like sheep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: follower - a person who follows the opinions or teachings of another
  • Antonym: leader - a person who leads or guides others
