suavity

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɑːvəti / (AmE), / ˈswɑːvɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being smooth, gracious, and polite in manner, often in a way that seems charming or ingratiating
smooth and gracious manner

Examples

He spoke to the guests with such suavity that everyone felt instantly at ease.
The diplomat's suavity made even the most difficult negotiations seem pleasant.
Behind his apparent suavity, there was a sharp, calculating mind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urbanity - polished and courteous manner
  • Antonym: rudeness - lack of courtesy or politeness; roughness of manner

Etymology

From Latin 'suavitas' (sweetness, agreeableness), via French 'suavité'.
