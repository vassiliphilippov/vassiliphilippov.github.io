mongoloid

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑŋ.ɡə.lɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈmɒŋ.ɡə.lɔɪd / (BrE)
(offensive, dated) A person with Down syndrome; this term is no longer used in technical or polite contexts.
dated, offensive term for a person with Down syndrome
offensive archaic

Examples

The use of the word mongoloid to describe people with Down syndrome is offensive and outdated.
Decades ago, medical literature sometimes incorrectly referred to children with Down syndrome as mongoloid.
Modern advocacy groups strongly discourage the term mongoloid due to its pejorative and inaccurate nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Down syndrome - A genetic disorder causing developmental and intellectual delays

Etymology

From 'Mongol,' referencing perceived facial similarities; now recognized as inaccurate and offensive.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɑŋ.ɡə.lɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈmɒŋ.ɡə.lɔɪd / (BrE)
(Offensive) Of or relating to Down syndrome, especially as an outdated and offensive term once used to describe people with the condition.
offensive, outdated term for Down syndrome
offensive

Examples

The term mongoloid is now recognized as highly offensive and is not used in modern medical practice.
He referred to the child as mongoloid, a usage that is both outdated and hurtful.
Medical literature from decades ago sometimes used mongoloid to describe people with Down syndrome, but this is no longer acceptable.

Etymology

From 'Mongol' plus the suffix '-oid', originally referring to perceived facial similarities; later used offensively for Down syndrome.
