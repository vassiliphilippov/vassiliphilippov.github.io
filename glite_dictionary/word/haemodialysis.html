haemodialysis

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːmoʊˌdaɪˈæləsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhiːməʊˌdaɪˈæləsɪs / (BrE)
A medical procedure in which a machine filters waste products and excess fluids from the blood, used especially when the kidneys are not functioning properly.
blood-filtering procedure for kidney failure
medical british

Examples

The patient underwent haemodialysis three times a week to manage his kidney failure.
Advances in haemodialysis technology have greatly improved the quality of life for those with chronic renal disease.
Her doctor explained that haemodialysis would help remove toxins from her bloodstream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dialysis - process of separating waste from the blood
  • Antonym: peritoneal dialysis - dialysis using the lining of the abdomen rather than a machine

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + dialysis (separation).
