prokaryotic

1. ADJ

/ proʊˌkæriˈɑːtɪk / (AmE), / prəʊˌkæriˈɒtɪk / (BrE)
(of a cell or organism) having cells that lack membrane-bound nuclei or other membrane-bound organelles.
lacking membrane-bound nuclei (of cells)
medical

Examples

Bacteria are prokaryotic organisms, meaning their cells do not contain a nucleus.
Unlike animal or plant cells, prokaryotic cells have no membrane-bound organelles.
Many antibiotics target unique features of prokaryotic cell structure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: eukaryotic - having cells with membrane-bound nuclei

Etymology

From pro- (before) + karyon (nut, referring to the nucleus) in Greek.
