Calochortus nuttallii

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæləˈkɔrtəs nʌˈtælaɪ / (AmE), / ˌkæləˈkɔːtəs nʌˈtalɪaɪ / (BrE)
a perennial plant of the lily family with clusters of one to four showy white, bell-shaped flowers, native to western North America; its edible bulbs were historically used as food during scarcity
a North American flowering plant, also known as sego lily

Examples

The Calochortus nuttallii blooms in spring, transforming the meadows with its striking white flowers.
Early settlers relied on the bulbs of Calochortus nuttallii for food during times of famine.
The Calochortus nuttallii can be found in the rocky soils of Utah and neighboring states.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sego lily - common name for Calochortus nuttallii

Etymology

Named after botanist Thomas Nuttall; genus from Greek 'kalos' (beautiful) and 'chortos' (grass).
