16 PF

1. NOUN

/ sɪkstiːn pi ɛf / (AmE), / sɪkstiːn piː ɛf / (BrE)
a self-report questionnaire designed by Raymond B. Cattell to assess sixteen distinct personality traits through factor analysis
personality test assessing 16 traits
medical

Examples

The psychologist recommended taking the 16 PF to gain insight into potential career paths.
Researchers often use the 16 PF to compare personality traits across different groups.
Completing the 16 PF helped me understand my strengths and areas for development.

Etymology

Short for 'Sixteen Personality Factor Questionnaire', developed by Raymond B. Cattell in the 1940s.
