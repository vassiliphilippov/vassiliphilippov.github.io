benzoate

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛnzoʊˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɛnzəʊeɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester derived from benzoic acid, used in food preservation and in various chemical applications
salt or ester of benzoic acid
medical

Examples

Sodium benzoate is commonly used as a preservative in carbonated beverages.
The laboratory synthesized a new benzoate compound for their research.
Some individuals may be sensitive to benzoate additives in processed foods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benzoic acid - a white crystalline aromatic acid used as a food preservative

Etymology

From benzoic acid, itself from benzoin (a balsamic resin) and -ate, a suffix for salts and esters.
