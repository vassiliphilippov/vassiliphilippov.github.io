senescence

1. NOUN

/ səˈnɛsəns / (AmE), / sɪˈnɛsəns / (BrE)
the process of becoming old or the state of being old; the deterioration associated with advancing age.
process or condition of aging

Examples

The senescence of cells leads to a gradual decline in tissue function over time.
Researchers study senescence to understand the biological processes that cause aging.
In many animals, visible senescence appears as reduced mobility and grey hair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aging - the process of growing old or showing the effects of increasing age

Etymology

From Latin senescere (to grow old).

2. NOUN

/ səˈnɛsəns / (AmE), / sɪˈnɛsəns / (BrE)
the process of aging and showing the physical or biological effects of advancing age
the process of aging

Examples

Scientists study senescence to understand the biological mechanisms behind aging.
The visible senescence of the trees in autumn is a natural part of their life cycle.
Modern medicine aims not just to extend life but to delay senescence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aging - the process of becoming older
  • Antonym: rejuvenation - the process of making someone or something look or feel younger

Etymology

From Latin 'senescere' (to grow old), from 'senex' (old).
