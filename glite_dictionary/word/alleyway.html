alleyway

1. NOUN

/ ˈæliˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈæliˌweɪ / (BrE)
a narrow passage or street between or behind buildings, often with high walls on both sides
narrow street between buildings

Examples

The children ran down the alleyway behind their house to play.
A dimly lit alleyway separated the two apartment blocks.
She lost her way and wandered into a deserted alleyway at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alley - a narrow passage between or behind buildings

Etymology

From 'alley' (from Old French 'alee', meaning 'walk' or 'passage') + 'way'.
