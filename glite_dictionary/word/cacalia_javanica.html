Cacalia javanica

1. NOUN

/ kəˈkeɪliə dʒəˈvænɪkə / (AmE), / kəˈkeɪlɪə dʒəˈvɑːnɪkə / (BrE)
A tropical African annual plant with scarlet, tassel-shaped flower heads; sometimes classified in the genus Cacalia.
tropical African flowering annual plant

Examples

The gardener planted Cacalia javanica to add vibrant color to the tropical borders.
Researchers studied the medicinal potential of Cacalia javanica in local communities.
The scarlet flowers of Cacalia javanica attract numerous pollinators during the rainy season.

Etymology

From the genus name Cacalia, assigned in botanical Latin, with 'javanica' referring to Java, where it was first described.
