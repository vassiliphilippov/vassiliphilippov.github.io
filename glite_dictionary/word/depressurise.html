to depressurise

1. VERB

/ dɪˈprɛʃəˌraɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈprɛʃəraɪz / (BrE)
to reduce the air pressure in a container, vehicle, or other enclosed space
reduce air pressure
british

Examples

If the pilot accidentally depressurises the cabin, oxygen masks will drop automatically.
Technicians must depressurise the tank before they can safely enter for maintenance.
The crew depressurised the module to simulate conditions in outer space.

Synset Examples

depressurize the cabin in the air plane

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depressurize - to reduce the air pressure in a container; American spelling
  • Antonym: to pressurise - to increase or maintain air pressure in a container

Etymology

From de- (expressing removal) + pressurise, modelled after pressurise/pressurize.
