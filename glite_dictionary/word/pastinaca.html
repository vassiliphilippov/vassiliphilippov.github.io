Pastinaca

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæstəˈneɪkə / (AmE), / ˌpæstɪˈneɪkə / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the family Apiaceae (Umbelliferae) that includes the parsnip.
plant genus including parsnips

Examples

The genus Pastinaca is well known for its edible root, the parsnip.
Several weeds belonging to Pastinaca can be found in temperate regions around the world.
Scientists studied the chemical compounds in Pastinaca to understand plant defense mechanisms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parsnip - an edible root vegetable, Pastinaca sativa, in this genus

Etymology

From Latin *pastinaca*, meaning parsnip.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpæstəˈneɪkə / (AmE), / ˌpastɪˈneɪkə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Apiaceae, which includes the parsnip.
genus of plants including parsnip

Examples

The genus Pastinaca contains several wild species as well as the common parsnip.
Botanists study Pastinaca to learn more about root vegetable domestication.
Gardeners often plant Pastinaca sativa for its edible roots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parsnip - an edible root vegetable from the genus Pastinaca

Etymology

From Latin 'pastinaca' (parsnip), the root vegetable.
