granadillo

1. NOUN

/ ɡrəˌnæˈdiːjoʊ / (AmE), / ɡrəˌnæˈdiːjəʊ / (BrE)
A West Indian tree (Brya ebenus) that yields a fine grade of green ebony wood, often used in musical instruments and fine woodworking.
West Indian tree producing green ebony wood

Examples

The artisan crafted a flute from granadillo because of its excellent acoustic properties.
Furniture made from granadillo wood is prized for its durability and rich color.
The dense, greenish-black wood of granadillo is highly valued by luthiers.

Etymology

From Spanish 'granadillo', possibly from 'granada' (pomegranate), referring to resemblance of the fruit.
