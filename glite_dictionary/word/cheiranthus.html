Cheiranthus

1. NOUN

/ kaɪˈrænnθəs / (AmE), / kaɪˈranθəs / (BrE)
A genus of Old World perennial plants, commonly known as wallflowers, grown for their showy flowers.
a genus of flowering perennial plants (wallflowers)

Examples

Botanists have classified the Cheiranthus species as belonging to the mustard family.
Gardeners prize Cheiranthus for their brilliant yellow and orange blooms in early spring.
The old stone wall was covered with wild Cheiranthus in full flower.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wallflower - a common name for plants of the genus Cheiranthus, grown for their fragrant, colorful flowers

Etymology

From Greek kheir (hand) and anthos (flower), referring to the use of the flowers in nosegays held in the hand.
