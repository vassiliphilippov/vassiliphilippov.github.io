dishonored

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈsɑnərd / (AmE), / dɪsˈɒnəd / (BrE)
Having lost respect or suffered shame; no longer regarded with honor.
having lost respect or suffered shame

Examples

After the scandal, the politician was a dishonored figure in his community.
The knight returned from exile, a dishonored man seeking redemption.
She felt dishonored by the false accusations made against her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgraced - having lost respect or honor
  • Antonym: honored - respected or held in high esteem

Etymology

From the verb 'dishonor', ultimately from Old French 'deshonorer', from Latin 'dishonorare'.
