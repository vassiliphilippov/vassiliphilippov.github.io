Paiute

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈjuːt / (AmE), / paɪˈjuːt / (BrE)
A member of either of two closely related Native American peoples (Northern Paiute and Southern Paiute) originally inhabiting the southwestern United States.
a Native American people of the southwestern U.S.

Examples

The Paiute have a rich tradition of storytelling passed down through generations.
A delegation of Paiute elders attended the tribal council meeting.
Historians have studied the migration patterns of the Paiute across the Nevada desert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ute - a Native American people originally living in the western United States

Etymology

From Southern Paiute *Payɨpɨ* ('true Ute').

2. NOUN

/ paɪˈjuːt / (AmE), / paɪˈjuːt / (BrE)
the Uto-Aztecan language spoken by the Paiute people of the western United States
language of the Paiute people

Examples

She is studying Paiute as part of her research on indigenous languages.
The elders preserve traditional stories by telling them in Paiute.
Efforts to teach Paiute in local schools are helping to revitalize the language.

Etymology

From the name of the Paiute people, originally deriving from their autonym.
