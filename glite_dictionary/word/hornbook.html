hornbook

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrnˌbʊk / (AmE), / ˈhɔːn.bʊk / (BrE)
A basic primer used historically to teach children the rudiments of reading or a branch of knowledge, often consisting of a single sheet of text mounted on a wooden tablet and covered with transparent horn.
historical reading primer for children
archaic

Examples

The child learned his letters from a hornbook in the village school.
A hornbook was once a common sight in English classrooms of the seventeenth century.
Collectors of antique educational tools often seek out a well-preserved hornbook.

Etymology

From the use of thin, transparent sheets of horn to protect the lesson sheet on the tablet.
