Sisymbrium

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈsɪmbriəm / (AmE), / sɪˈsɪmbriəm / (BrE)
A genus of annual, biennial, or perennial herbs with racemose flowers, native to the Old World, many of which are considered weeds.
a plant genus of flowering herbs, including some weeds

Examples

The weed growing in the garden was identified as a species of Sisymbrium.
Many Sisymbrium plants thrive in disturbed soils and quickly colonize open spaces.
Several species within the genus Sisymbrium are common in fields across Europe and Asia.

Etymology

From Latin 'sisymbrium', from Greek 'sisymbrion', an ancient name for a kind of cress.
