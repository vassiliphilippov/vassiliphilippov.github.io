hemstitch

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛmˌstɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈhɛm.stɪtʃ / (BrE)
an embroidery technique that produces a decorative openwork border along the edge of a hem by drawing out threads and grouping the remaining threads with stitches
decorative openwork embroidery on hems

Examples

She carefully added a hemstitch to the edge of the linen tablecloth for a delicate finish.
The antique handkerchiefs featured intricate hemstitch patterns along their borders.
Learning how to hemstitch takes patience, but the elegant results are worth the effort.

Etymology

Formed from 'hem' (the edge of a piece of cloth) and 'stitch' (a loop of thread in sewing or knitting).

2. VERB

/ ˈhɛmˌstɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈhɛmˌstɪtʃ / (BrE)
to sew (the border of a piece of fabric) using a decorative stitch that draws out threads along the edge, creating a delicate openwork pattern
sew with decorative openwork stitches

Examples

She carefully hemstitched the edges of the tablecloth for a refined look.
He learned to hemstitch a handkerchief as part of a sewing class.
The artisan hemstitches decorative borders on linen napkins for her clients.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɛmˌstɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈhɛmˌstɪtʃ / (BrE)
A decorative sewing stitch in which parallel threads are drawn out and the remaining threads are grouped together, often used to embellish hems.
a decorative stitch grouping threads in drawn fabric

Examples

She used a hemstitch to add a delicate touch to the handkerchief's border.
The vintage tablecloth was edged with a lovely hemstitch design.
Learning the hemstitch technique took patience, but the results were beautiful.

Etymology

From 'hem' (edge of cloth) + 'stitch'; the technique developed in Europe, especially in fine linens.
