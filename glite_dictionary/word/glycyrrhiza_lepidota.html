Glycyrrhiza lepidota

1. NOUN

/ ɡlaɪˌsɪrəˈhaɪzə lɛˈpɪdoʊtə / (AmE), / ɡlɪˌsɪrɪˈhʌɪzə lɛˈpɪdəʊtə / (BrE)
A North American plant in the pea family, similar to true licorice, with a root that has similar flavor and medicinal properties.
North American plant similar to licorice

Examples

Glycyrrhiza lepidota grows widely across prairies and streambanks throughout North America.
Native Americans traditionally used the root of Glycyrrhiza lepidota for its sweet flavor and medicinal benefits.
The dried root of Glycyrrhiza lepidota can serve as a substitute for true licorice in herbal remedies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: licorice - Sweet root of Glycyrrhiza glabra, used in flavoring and medicine

Etymology

From Greek *glykys* (sweet) and *rhiza* (root); 'lepidota' from Greek *lepidōtos* (scaly).
