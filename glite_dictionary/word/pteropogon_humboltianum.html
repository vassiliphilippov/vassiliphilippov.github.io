Pteropogon humboltianum

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛroʊˈpoʊɡɑn hʌmˌbɔltiˈɑnəm / (AmE), / ˌtɛrəˈpəʊɡən hʌmˌbɒltiˈɑːnəm / (BrE)
A southern Australian plant species characterized by feathery hairs surrounding its fruit, belonging to the grass family.
southern Australian grass with feathery fruit hairs

Examples

The feathery fruits of Pteropogon humboltianum are easily dispersed by the wind.
Botanists discovered a new population of Pteropogon humboltianum in southern Australia.
Farmers sometimes mistake Pteropogon humboltianum for a common weed due to its grass-like appearance.

Etymology

From Greek 'pteron' (feather) + 'pogon' (beard), and honoring explorer Alexander von Humboldt.
