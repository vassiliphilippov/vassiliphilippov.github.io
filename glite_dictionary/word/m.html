M

1. NOUN

/ ɛm / (AmE), / ɛm / (BrE)
the Roman numeral for one thousand (1000)
Roman numeral for 1000

Examples

The marathon had more than 5M participants this year.
Prices often reach several M dollars in that neighborhood.
The population of the city exceeded 2M people last year.

Etymology

From Latin 'M', abbreviation of 'mille' meaning 'thousand'.

3. NOUN

/ ɛm / (AmE), / ɛm / (BrE)
a unit of concentration, equal to one mole of solute per liter of solution; used to express molarity in chemistry
unit for molarity (concentration)
medical

Examples

The solution was prepared to a final concentration of 0.5 M.
Chemists often use M to indicate molarity in their calculations.
A 1 M NaCl solution contains one mole of sodium chloride per liter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: molar - concentration measured as moles per liter (molarity)

Etymology

Abbreviation for molarity, from mole (a unit in chemistry) and -arity.

5. NOUN

/ ɛm / (AmE), / ɛm / (BrE)
A unit of information in computing equal to 1024 kibibytes or 1,048,576 bytes (2^20 bytes), sometimes used to denote a mebibyte.
computing unit equal to 1024 kibibytes

Examples

Each file on this server is less than 1 M in size.
This video will take up about 450 M on your hard drive.
The database has grown to over 800 M since last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mebibyte - A unit of digital information equal to 2^20 (1,048,576) bytes
  • Degree Lower: kilobyte - A unit of digital information equal to 1024 bytes
  • Degree Higher: gigabyte - A unit of digital information equal to 1,073,741,824 (2^30) bytes

Etymology

Used as an abbreviation for 'mebibyte,' which combines the metric prefix 'mega-' with 'binary' (from 'bi').

Source: WordNet
