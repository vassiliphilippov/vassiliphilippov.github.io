philosophizer

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlɑːsəˌfaɪzər / (AmE), / fɪˈlɒsəˌfaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who considers situations from a philosophical perspective or thinks deeply about fundamental questions
someone who thinks philosophically

Examples

As a philosophizer, she often paused to reflect on the deeper meaning of everyday events.
The discussion was dominated by a self-proclaimed philosophizer who questioned the purpose of every action.
He was known in his group of friends as the resident philosophizer, always pushing conversations into abstract territory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: philosopher - a person engaged or learned in philosophy, especially as an academic discipline

Etymology

From English 'philosophy' + '-izer', modeled after 'philosophize'.
