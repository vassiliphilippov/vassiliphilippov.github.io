magician

1. NOUN

/ məˈdʒɪʃən / (AmE), / məˈdʒɪʃən / (BrE)
a person who performs magic tricks as entertainment or who is believed to have supernatural powers
person who performs magic or sorcery

Examples

The famous magician amazed the audience with his disappearing act.
Some stories feature a powerful magician able to control the forces of nature.
As a child, he dreamed of becoming a magician and performing at grand theaters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorcerer - a person who claims or is believed to have magical powers
  • Synonym: wizard - a man who is supposed to have magical powers
  • Synonym: witch - a woman who is believed to have magical powers

Etymology

From Latin 'magus' (magus, sorcerer) via Old French 'magicien'.

2. NOUN

/ məˈdʒɪʃən / (AmE), / məˈdʒɪʃən / (BrE)
a person who entertains an audience by performing magic tricks and illusions
person who performs magic tricks

Examples

The magician pulled a rabbit out of his hat, amazing the entire audience.
Children watched in awe as the magician made the card disappear.
At the birthday party, a magician entertained guests with incredible tricks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illusionist - a performer who creates illusions of seemingly impossible feats
  • Synonym: conjurer - a person who performs magic tricks, especially by sleight of hand

Etymology

From Old French magicien, from Latin magus (magus, sorcerer).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10300371-n
Synset POS: n
