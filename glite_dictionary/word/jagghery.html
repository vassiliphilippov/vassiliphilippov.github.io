jagghery

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæɡəri / (AmE), / ˈdʒæɡəri / (BrE)
unrefined brown sugar made from the sap of palm trees or sugarcane, commonly used in South Asian and African cuisines
unrefined brown sugar from palm sap

Examples

The recipe calls for a tablespoon of jagghery to sweeten the curry.
Traditional sweets in India often use jagghery instead of refined sugar.
He bought a block of jagghery at the market to make desserts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jaggery - unrefined brown sugar made from sugarcane or palm sap (common spelling)

Etymology

From Portuguese *jágar*, from Malayalam *śarkkara* (sugar), via Hindi *gur* and related South Asian languages.
