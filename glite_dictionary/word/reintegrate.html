to reintegrate

1. VERB

/ ˌriːˈɪntəˌɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɪntɪɡreɪt / (BrE)
to integrate someone or something again into a group, society, or system after having been removed or separated
integrate again into a group or system

Examples

After the war, it took years to reintegrate the displaced families into their communities.
Programs are in place to help former prisoners reintegrate into society.
The company worked hard to reintegrate remote employees back into the office culture.

Synset Examples

Russia must be reintegrated into Europe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to integrate - to combine or put together to become part of a whole
  • Antonym: to segregate - to separate or set apart from others

Etymology

From Latin re- (again) + integrare (to make whole).
