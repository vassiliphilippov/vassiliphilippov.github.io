Iberia

1. PROPN

/ aɪˈbɪriə / (AmE), / aɪˈbɪəriə / (BrE)
an ancient region south of the Caucasus Mountains, roughly corresponding to present-day Georgia
ancient region in the Caucasus (now Georgia)

Examples

The ancient kingdom of Iberia played a crucial role in the history of the Caucasus region.
Archaeologists have discovered artifacts from Iberia dating back to the early medieval period.
The rulers of Iberia were instrumental in the spread of Christianity in the area now known as Georgia.

Etymology

From Latin 'Iberia', used by Greek and Roman writers to refer to this Caucasian kingdom.

2. PROPN

/ aɪˈbɪriə / (AmE), / aɪˈbɪəriə / (BrE)
the peninsula in southwestern Europe comprising Spain and Portugal, known as the Iberian Peninsula.
peninsula in southwestern Europe (Spain and Portugal)

Examples

The Pyrenees mountains form a natural border between Iberia and the rest of Europe.
Ancient cultures once thrived in Iberia long before the rise of Rome.
Climate patterns in Iberia can differ significantly from those in northern Europe.

Etymology

From Latin Iberia, from Ancient Greek Ἰβηρία (Ibēría), referring to the region inhabited by the Iberians.
