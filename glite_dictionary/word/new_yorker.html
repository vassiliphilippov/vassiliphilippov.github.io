New Yorker

1. NOUN

/ nuː ˈjɔrkər / (AmE), / njuː ˈjɔːkə / (BrE)
a person who was born in or lives in New York, especially New York City.
a native or resident of New York

Examples

She has lived in Manhattan for years and truly considers herself a New Yorker.
Many New Yorkers rely on the subway to get around the city quickly.
As a New Yorker, he's used to the fast-paced lifestyle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: New York - a state in the northeastern US; also a city in that state

2. NOUN

/ nuː ˈjɔrkər / (AmE), / njuː ˈjɔːkə / (BrE)
a person who lives in or comes from New York City
a resident of New York City

Examples

She considers herself a true New Yorker after living there for ten years.
It takes a New Yorker to navigate the subway during rush hour.
Many New Yorkers are proud of their city's energy and diversity.

Synset Examples

Being a Jew Yorker means embracing diversity and history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: New York - a city in the United States
