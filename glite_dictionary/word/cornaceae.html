Cornaceae

1. NOUN

/ kɔːrˈneɪsiˌi / (AmE), / kɔːˈneɪsiːiː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants in the order Cornales, commonly known as the dogwood family, which includes the genus Cornus and several others.
family of flowering plants (dogwoods)

Examples

The Cornaceae family includes well-known species such as the flowering dogwood.
Botanists recently reclassified some plants previously thought to be in the Cornaceae.
Members of the Cornaceae can be found in temperate regions across the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogwood - common name for trees and shrubs of the Cornus genus

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Cornus (the type genus) + -aceae (a standard suffix for plant family names).

2. NOUN

/ kɔːrˈneɪsiː / (AmE), / kɔːˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants of the order Cornales, including genera such as Cornus (dogwoods) and related plants
dogwood family of flowering plants

Examples

Many species within the Cornaceae family are cultivated for their attractive flowers and foliage.
The Cornaceae are widespread in temperate regions, especially known for the dogwood genus.
Research into Cornaceae has contributed to our understanding of plant evolution and systematics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cornus - type genus of the Cornaceae, commonly called dogwoods

Etymology

From the genus name Cornus, with the standard botanical suffix -aceae indicating a family.
