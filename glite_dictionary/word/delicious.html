delicious

1. ADJ

/ dəˈlɪʃəs / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪʃəs / (BrE)
extremely pleasing to the taste; having a very pleasant flavor
extremely pleasing in taste

Examples

The homemade apple pie was absolutely delicious and disappeared within minutes.
She prepared a delicious meal for her guests, who loved every bite.
The strawberries were so delicious that I ate the whole bowl.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tasty - having a pleasant, distinct flavor
  • Antonym: bland - lacking strong flavor

Etymology

From Old French *delicious*, from Latin *deliciosus* (dainty, delightful).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈlɪʃəs / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪʃ.əs / (BrE)
Extremely pleasing or delightful, especially in a way that entertains or brings joy.
highly pleasing or delightful

Examples

The morning air was delicious with the scent of blooming flowers.
She shared a delicious secret that made everyone smile.
His performance had a delicious sense of humor that kept the audience laughing.

Synset Examples

a delightful surprise
the comedy was delightful
a delicious joke

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delightful - giving great pleasure or delight

Etymology

From Latin 'deliciosus' (delightful, charming) via Old French.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01813229-s
Synset POS: s
