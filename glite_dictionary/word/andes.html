Andes

1. PROPN

/ ˈændiz / (AmE), / ˈandiːz / (BrE)
a major mountain range in western South America, extending along the Pacific coast for about 5,000 miles (8,000 kilometers)
mountain range in South America

Examples

The snow-capped peaks of the Andes are visible from miles away.
Many rare species inhabit the unique ecosystems of the Andes.
Machu Picchu is located high in the Andes of Peru.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rocky Mountains - major mountain range in western North America
  • Synonym: Himalayas - major mountain range in Asia

Etymology

From Spanish 'Andes', of uncertain origin, possibly from Quechua 'anti' (east).
