to opalise

1. VERB

/ ˈoʊpəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈəʊpəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make opalescent; to give a milky iridescence similar to that of an opal
make opalescent
british

Examples

The artist managed to opalise the glass, creating a beautiful shimmer in the sunlight.
Certain minerals will opalise over time when exposed to specific chemical conditions.
Manufacturers sometimes opalise glassware for decorative effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to opalize - to make opalescent; to give an opal-like iridescence (American spelling)

Etymology

From opal + -ise, modeled after opalescent.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00117038-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈoʊ.pə.laɪz / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.pə.laɪz / (BrE)
(of a material) to replace or convert into opal, often through a natural process over time.
convert into opal

Examples

Over millions of years, the fossil began to opalise, resulting in a dazzling array of colors.
Certain volcanic ashes can opalise when exposed to the right chemical conditions.
Scientists discovered several opalised dinosaur bones in the outback region.

Synset Examples

opalized tree trunks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to petrify - to change organic material into stone or a stony substance over time

Etymology

From opal + -ise, modeled after related verbs like 'fossilise.' Variant spelling of American 'opalize.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00116893-v
Synset POS: v
