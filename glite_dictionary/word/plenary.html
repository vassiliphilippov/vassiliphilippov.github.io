plenary

1. ADJ

/ ˈpliːnɛri / (AmE), / ˈplɛnəri / (BrE)
(of a meeting or power) complete; attended or given in full, with no restrictions or limitations.
complete; with full authority or attendance

Examples

The board granted him plenary authority to act on its behalf.
All members are expected to attend the plenary session tomorrow morning.
The committee met in plenary each afternoon to discuss the proposals.

Synset Examples

a plenary session of the legislature
a diplomat with plenary powers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: partial - not complete; existing only in part

Etymology

From Latin plenarius (complete, full), from plenus (full).
