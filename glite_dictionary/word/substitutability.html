substitutability

1. NOUN

/ sʌb.stəˌtu.təˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / sʌb.stɪˌtjuː.təˈbɪl.ə.ti / (BrE)
The quality of being able to be replaced by another person or thing without significant difference or loss of function.
ability to be substituted or replaced

Examples

Engineers considered the substitutability of various materials for building the new bridge.
The substitutability of one brand for another makes competition stronger in a free market.
High substitutability among products often leads to lower prices for consumers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: replaceability - the capacity to be replaced
  • Antonym: irreplaceability - the quality of not being replaceable

Etymology

From 'substitute' + '-ability'; ultimately from Latin 'substituere' (to put in place of) and the suffix '-ability'.
