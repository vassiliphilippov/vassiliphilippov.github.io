Synentognathi

1. NOUN

/ saɪˌnɛntəgˈneɪθaɪ / (AmE), / sɪˌnɛntɒgˈneɪθɪ / (BrE)
an order of fishes characterized by spineless fins, including species such as needlefishes, sauries, flying fishes, and halfbeaks
order of spineless-finned fishes

Examples

The Synentognathi are easily distinguished by their long, slender bodies and spineless fins.
Researchers studied the evolutionary adaptations of Synentognathi in open ocean habitats.
Needlefishes and flying fishes both belong to the order Synentognathi.

Etymology

From Greek syn- (together) + entos (within) + gnathos (jaw), referring to jaw structure.
