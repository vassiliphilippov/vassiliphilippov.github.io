Entomophthoraceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.toʊ.mɒfˌθɔː.rəˈsiː.i / (AmE), / ˌɛn.təʊ.mɒfˌθɔː.rəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of mostly parasitic fungi that typically develop inside the bodies of insects
family of parasitic fungi affecting insects

Examples

Some species within the Entomophthoraceae are studied for their potential in controlling insect pests.
The fungus belonging to Entomophthoraceae infects a wide range of insect hosts.
Researchers have identified new members of Entomophthoraceae in tropical forest environments.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'entomo-' (insect), 'phthora' (destruction in Greek), and standard botanical suffix '-aceae' (denoting a family).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛntəˌmɑfθərəˈsiːi / (AmE), / ˌɛntəmɒfˌθɔːrəˈsiːi / (BrE)
A family of mostly parasitic lower fungi that typically grow inside the bodies of insects.
family of parasitic fungi infecting insects

Examples

The Entomophthoraceae play an important role in controlling insect populations.
Scientists study the Entomophthoraceae to develop new biological pest control methods.
Many species within the Entomophthoraceae infect agricultural pests.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Greek 'entomon' (insect) + 'phthoros' (destruction).
