gangsaw

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæŋˌsɔ / (AmE), / ˈɡaŋˌsɔː / (BrE)
a power saw with multiple parallel blades that make several simultaneous cuts, typically used for cutting large blocks of material such as stone or wood
power saw with multiple blades making parallel cuts

Examples

The marble blocks were sliced into slabs using a gangsaw at the factory.
A modern gangsaw can cut multiple boards from a log in a single pass.
Proper maintenance is crucial to ensure the blades of a gangsaw remain aligned during operation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bandsaw - a saw with a long, sharp blade consisting of a continuous band of toothed metal

Etymology

From 'gang' (a set or group) + 'saw', indicating a set of saw blades working together.
