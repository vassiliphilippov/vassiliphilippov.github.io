to feudalize

1. VERB

/ ˈfjudəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfjʊːdəlaɪz / (BrE)
To bring (a country or people) under the system or influence of feudalism.
bring under feudalism

Examples

The conqueror sought to feudalize the newly acquired territories by granting land to his followers.
Efforts to feudalize the region met with resistance from the local population.
During the Middle Ages, rulers would often feudalize their lands to maintain control through a hierarchy of lords.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to modernize - make modern or adopt modern methods
  • Synonym: feudalism - the social system in medieval Europe, based on lords, vassals, and fiefs

Etymology

From 'feudal' + suffix '-ize', patterned after similar verbs formed from nouns.
