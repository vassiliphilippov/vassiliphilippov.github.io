Crataegus oxycantha

1. NOUN

/ krəˈtiːɡəs ɒksɪˈkænθə / (AmE), / krəˈtiːɡəs ɒksɪˈkanθə / (BrE)
A thorny Eurasian shrub or small tree of the hawthorn genus, bearing dense clusters of white to scarlet flowers and deep red berries, established in parts of eastern North America.
a Eurasian hawthorn shrub or small tree

Examples

The garden in spring was alive with the blossoms of Crataegus oxycantha.
In eastern North America, Crataegus oxycantha has established itself as an ornamental species.
The bright red berries of Crataegus oxycantha attract many birds during autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawthorn - A shrub or tree of the genus Crataegus producing small fruits and thorny branches

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Crataegus from Greek krataigos (hawthorn), and species name oxycantha from Greek oxys (sharp) + akantha (thorn).
