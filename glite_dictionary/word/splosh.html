to splosh

1. VERB

/ splɑʃ / (AmE), / splɒʃ / (BrE)
to cause a liquid to splash or spatter forcefully
cause liquid to splash with force

Examples

The child sploshed paint across the canvas, making a colorful mess.
He sploshed water onto the floor while washing the dishes.
She loves to splosh through puddles after it rains.

Synset Examples

She splashed the water around her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splash - to cause liquid to scatter in drops or particles
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01377432-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ splɑʃ / (AmE), / splɒʃ / (BrE)
to make a loud, wet, splashing sound, or to move noisily through water or mud
make a splashing sound

Examples

The children ran through the puddles, their boots sploshing with every step.
She accidentally sploshed water onto the kitchen floor while washing the dishes.
Heavy rain sploshed against the window all night.

Synset Examples

water was splashing on the floor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splash - to cause liquid to scatter in drops or make such a sound
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02191314-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ splɑʃ / (AmE), / splɒʃ / (BrE)
to walk with a splattering or sloshing sound through water, mud, or mire
walk noisily through mud or water

Examples

We had to splosh across the wet meadow to get to the farmhouse.
The children sploshed through puddles on their way home from school.
She sploshed along the muddy path, her boots caked with dirt.

Synset Examples

We had to splosh across the wet meadow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splash - to cause liquid to scatter and make a sound or mess
  • Synonym: trudge - to walk slowly and with heavy steps, usually because of tiredness or harsh conditions

Etymology

Imitative; from the sound of splashing in mud or water.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01925584-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ splɑʃ / (AmE), / splɒʃ / (BrE)
to splash or dash a liquid onto or against something, often making a sound or mess
splash liquid onto something

Examples

The children love to splosh around in the puddles after a rainstorm.
She accidentally sploshed milk all over the kitchen counter.
Try not to splosh the paint when you're cleaning the brushes.

Synset Examples

The mother splashed the baby's face with water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splash - to cause liquid to scatter or fall; to dash liquid over

Etymology

Imitative of the sound made by liquid splashing.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01376685-v
