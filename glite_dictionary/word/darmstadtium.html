darmstadtium

1. NOUN

/ dɑrmˈstæt.i.əm / (AmE), / dɑːmˈstad.ti.əm / (BrE)
a synthetic radioactive chemical element with the symbol Ds and atomic number 110
synthetic radioactive element (atomic number 110)

Examples

Scientists first synthesized darmstadtium in a laboratory in Germany.
Because darmstadtium is highly unstable, it is mainly used in scientific research.
The team detected the presence of darmstadtium for only a fraction of a second.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: element - a substance consisting of atoms with the same number of protons

Etymology

Named after Darmstadt, Germany, where it was first discovered, with '-ium' as a common suffix for metallic elements.
