caseful

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪs.fəl / (AmE), / ˈkeɪs.fʊl / (BrE)
The quantity that fills a case; as much as a case can hold.
quantity that fills a case

Examples

She carried a caseful of wine up to the kitchen.
The shop stocks at least a caseful of every item in storage.
After the event, we had a caseful of leftover brochures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boxful - the quantity that fills a box

Etymology

Formed from 'case' + the suffix '-ful', meaning 'full of'.
