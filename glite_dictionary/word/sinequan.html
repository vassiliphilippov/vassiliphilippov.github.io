Sinequan

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪnɪˌkwæn / (AmE), / ˈsɪnɪˌkwæn / (BrE)
A trade name for doxepin, a tricyclic antidepressant prescribed for depression and anxiety, often associated with side effects such as dry mouth, sedation, and gastrointestinal disturbances.
trade name for a tricyclic antidepressant (doxepin)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Sinequan to help manage her symptoms of depression.
Some patients experience drowsiness as a side effect of Sinequan.
He switched from Sinequan to another antidepressant due to persistent dry mouth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Adapin - another trade name for doxepin, a tricyclic antidepressant
  • Synonym: doxepin - the generic name for Sinequan; a tricyclic antidepressant
