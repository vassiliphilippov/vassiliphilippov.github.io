pigfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪɡˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A marine fish of the western Atlantic, noted for its pig-like snout, found from Long Island southward.
a western Atlantic marine fish

Examples

Fishermen often catch pigfish along the Atlantic coast during the summer months.
The pigfish is recognizable by its distinctive snout and silvery body.
Local restaurants sometimes serve fresh pigfish as a seasonal delicacy.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪɡˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡ.fɪʃ / (BrE)
a species of fish found in coastal waters, especially around the Great Barrier Reef, known for its pig-like snout
a coastal fish with a pig-like snout

Examples

While snorkeling, we spotted a colorful pigfish darting among the coral.
The pigfish is often studied by marine biologists for its unique features.
Many divers hope to catch a glimpse of the elusive pigfish near the Great Barrier Reef.
