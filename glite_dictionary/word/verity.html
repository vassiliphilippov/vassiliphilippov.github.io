verity

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛrəti / (AmE), / ˈvɛrɪti / (BrE)
a true principle or belief, especially one of fundamental importance in ethics, religion, or aesthetics
a fundamental or enduring truth

Examples

The philosopher devoted his life to the search for universal verity in human existence.
Many religious texts claim to express eternal verities that guide their followers' lives.
Artists often strive to reveal some deeper verity through their creative work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: truth - that which is true or in accordance with fact or reality
  • Antonym: falsehood - the state of being untrue; a lie or inaccuracy

Etymology

From Old French verite, from Latin veritas (truth), from verus (true).
