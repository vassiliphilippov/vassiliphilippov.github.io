Calamus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæləˌmʌs / (AmE), / ˈkæləməs / (BrE)
A genus of fish in the family Sparidae, commonly known as porgies, found chiefly in the western Atlantic Ocean.
a genus of porgies (fish)

Examples

The species within Calamus are popular among anglers for their tasty flesh.
Several types of Calamus inhabit the reefs along the Florida coast.
Scientists recently discovered a new species in the genus Calamus during their expedition.

Etymology

From Latin *calamus* (reed), adopted as a genus name in zoological taxonomy.
