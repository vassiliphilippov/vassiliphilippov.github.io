Iapetus

1. PROPN

/ aɪˈæpətəs / (AmE), / aɪˈæpɪtəs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a Titan who was the father of Atlas, Epimetheus, and Prometheus.
Titan in Greek mythology, father of Atlas

Examples

According to legend, Iapetus was considered one of the original Titans.
Many myths describe Iapetus as the father of Prometheus and Atlas.
Some scholars believe that Iapetus played a crucial role in the genealogy of Greek gods.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἰαπετός (Iapetos), a Titan in Greek mythology.
