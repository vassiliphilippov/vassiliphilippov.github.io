gaseous

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡæsiəs / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪsiəs / (BrE)
existing as or relating to gas; having the form or characteristics of a gas rather than a liquid or solid
having the form of gas

Examples

Oxygen is gaseous at room temperature, but it becomes liquid when cooled.
Steam is simply water in its gaseous phase.
The planet Jupiter is mostly composed of gaseous elements like hydrogen and helium.

Synset Examples

steam is water in the gaseous state

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solid - substance with definite shape and volume; not liquid or gas
  • Antonym: liquid - substance that flows freely but is of constant volume; not solid or gas

Etymology

From 'gas' + '-eous', patterning after similar Latin-based adjectives like 'aqueous'.
