catheterization

1. NOUN

/ kæθətərɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / kəˌθiːtəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the medical procedure of inserting a catheter into the body to allow drainage, deliver fluids, or access internal organs
insertion of a catheter for medical purposes
medical

Examples

The patient required catheterization during surgery to monitor urine output.
Doctors performed a cardiac catheterization to check for blocked arteries.
Following her operation, catheterization was necessary for several days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catheter - a flexible tube inserted into the body to remove or deliver fluids

Etymology

From catheter + -ization; 'catheter' from Greek 'kathetēr' (a thing let down).
