fluctuation

1. NOUN

/ ˌflʌk.tʃuˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌflʌk.tjʊˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the quality or state of being unsteady or likely to change frequently or irregularly
unsteady or irregular change

Examples

Stock markets are known for their fluctuation in response to global events.
The fluctuation of temperatures made it difficult to plan outdoor activities.
Scientists are studying the fluctuation in animal populations over the past decade.

Synset Examples

he kept a record of price fluctuations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: variation - the act or process of changing
  • Antonym: stability - the quality of being stable or steady

Etymology

From Latin 'fluctuatio' (a wave, fluctuation), from 'fluctuare' (to undulate, fluctuate).

2. NOUN

/ ˌflʌk.tʃuˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌflʌk.tjʊˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an instance of change, especially a continuous and irregular variation in level, amount, or value
irregular change or variation

Examples

The stock market is known for its unpredictable fluctuation over short periods.
Temperature fluctuation throughout the year can affect crop yields.
The doctor monitored the patient's blood pressure for any sudden fluctuation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: variation - a change or slight difference in condition, amount, or level
  • Antonym: stability - the state of being stable and unchanging

Etymology

From Latin 'fluctuatio' (a surging, fluctuating), from 'fluctuare' (to undulate or wave)

3. NOUN

/ ˌflʌk.tʃuˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌflʌk.tjʊˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a continuous motion of rising and falling or moving up and down in a wave-like manner, especially in liquids or surfaces.
wave-like motion, especially in liquids

Examples

The fluctuation of the ocean made the boat rise and fall gently.
We could see the fluctuation of the water's surface as the wind picked up.
The ship was designed to withstand the fluctuation of the waves during storms.

Synset Examples

the fluctuations of the sea

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undulation - a smooth, wave-like motion

Etymology

From Latin fluctuationem (a wavering, undulation), from fluctuare (to wave).
