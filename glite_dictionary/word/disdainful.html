disdainful

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈdeɪnfəl / (AmE), / dɪsˈdeɪnfʊl / (BrE)
showing contempt or lack of respect for someone or something considered unworthy
showing contempt or lack of respect

Examples

She gave him a disdainful look and walked away without saying a word.
His disdainful attitude made it clear that he thought the project was beneath him.
Reviewers were disdainful of the new movie, calling it unoriginal and boring.

Synset Examples

some economists are disdainful of their colleagues in other social disciplines
haughty aristocrats
his lordly manners were offensive
walked with a prideful swagger
very sniffy about breaches of etiquette
his mother eyed my clothes with a supercilious air
a more swaggering mood than usual

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contemptuous - showing contempt; scornful
  • Antonym: respectful - showing respect or esteem

Etymology

From Old French 'desdaignier' (to scorn), from Latin 'dēdignārī' (to disdain).

2. ADJ

/ dɪsˈdeɪnfəl / (AmE), / dɪsˈdeɪnfʊl / (BrE)
showing contempt or lack of respect
showing contempt or scorn

Examples

She gave him a disdainful glance as she walked past.
His disdainful attitude made him few friends in the office.
The critic's review was written in a disdainful tone that upset the artist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contemptuous - showing deep dislike or disdain
  • Antonym: respectful - showing respect or admiration

Etymology

From Old French *desdaignier* (to scorn), from Latin *dignari* (to deem worthy) with prefix *dis-* (opposite of).
