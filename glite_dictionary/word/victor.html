victor

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪktər / (AmE), / ˈvɪktə / (BrE)
a person who defeats an opponent or enemy in a battle, contest, or competition
winner of a contest or competition

Examples

After a long and grueling match, Serena emerged as the victor.
The army returned home as victors after defeating their rivals in battle.
He shook hands with the victor and congratulated her on her achievement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winner - a person who wins something
  • Antonym: loser - a person who is defeated in a contest or competition

Etymology

From Latin 'victor' (conqueror), from 'vincere' (to conquer).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɪktər / (AmE), / ˈvɪktə / (BrE)
a person who defeats others in a competition, battle, or contest; a winner
winner in a contest or battle

Examples

The victor emerged from the final round of the boxing match, cheered by the crowd.
History often forgets those who lost, but remembers the victor of great battles.
She trained for months and was overjoyed to be declared the victor in the marathon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winner - a person who wins a contest or competition
  • Antonym: loser - a person who is defeated in a contest or competition

Etymology

From Latin 'victor' (winner, conqueror), from 'vincere' (to conquer).
