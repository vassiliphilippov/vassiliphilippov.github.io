directed

1. ADJ

/ dəˈrɛktɪd, daɪˈrɛktɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛktɪd, daɪˈrɛktɪd / (BrE)
(often used in combination) having a specified or definite direction or purpose.
having a specified direction or purpose

Examples

The experiment used a directed flow of air to create specific conditions.
These animals display directed movement toward sources of food.
Her actions seemed very directed and purposeful throughout the project.

Synset Examples

a positively directed vector
goal-directed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undirected - not having a specified or definite direction

Etymology

Past participle of 'direct', from Latin 'directus' (straight, guided).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01687721-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈrɛktɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛktɪd / (BrE)
(of an activity or program) managed, guided, or controlled by a supervising person or agent.
managed or supervised by someone

Examples

The students enrolled in a directed study program to receive individual guidance from a professor.
The company initiated a directed campaign focused on improving employee wellness.
All participants will take part in a directed exercise regimen designed by fitness experts.

Synset Examples

a directed program of study

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supervised - overseen or managed by someone

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'direct', from Latin 'dirigere' (to guide, align).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01478184-s
Synset POS: s
