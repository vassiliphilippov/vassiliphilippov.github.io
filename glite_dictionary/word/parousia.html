Parousia

1. NOUN

/ pəˈruːziə / (AmE), / ˌpærʊˈsiːə / (BrE)
(in Christian theology) the Second Coming of Christ, when Jesus is believed to return to judge the living and the dead at the Last Judgment.
Second Coming of Christ (Christianity)

Examples

The doctrine of the Parousia has played a central role in Christian eschatology.
Some believers await the imminent Parousia and prepare for the return of Christ.
The New Testament contains several references to the Parousia throughout its texts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Second Coming - the anticipated return of Jesus Christ to Earth

Etymology

From Greek parousia (presence, arrival), especially used in the New Testament for Christ's return.
