Camptosorus rhizophyllus

1. NOUN

/ kæmpˌtəsɔɹəs raɪzəˈfɪləs / (AmE), / kæmpˌtəsɔːrəs raɪzəˈfɪləs / (BrE)
A species of fern with lance-shaped fronds that often root at the tip, commonly known as walking fern.
a species of fern (walking fern)

Examples

The unique rooting behavior of Camptosorus rhizophyllus allows it to spread efficiently across shaded forest floors.
Botanists studied the adaptation strategies of Camptosorus rhizophyllus in its native habitat.
Gardeners admire Camptosorus rhizophyllus for its graceful, arching fronds that root wherever the tips touch the soil.

Etymology

From Greek 'kamptos' (bent) + 'sōros' (heap) and 'rhiza' (root) + 'phyllon' (leaf), referring to the fern's growth habit.
