to discern

1. VERB

/ dɪˈsɜrn / (AmE), / dɪˈsɜːn / (BrE)
to detect or recognize something, especially with difficulty, by using the senses
detect or recognize with the senses

Examples

In the thick fog, it was nearly impossible to discern the outline of the building.
She could just discern a faint sound coming from the alleyway.
With his keen eyesight, he was able to discern the tiny movements of the butterfly.

Synset Examples

The fleeing convicts were picked out of the darkness by the watchful prison guards
I can't make out the faces in this photograph

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to detect - discover or identify the presence of something
  • Synonym: to perceive - become aware or conscious of something
  • Antonym: miss - fail to notice or detect something

Etymology

From Latin discernere, from dis- (apart) + cernere (to separate, sift).
