to devastate

1. VERB

/ ˈdɛvəˌsteɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəsteɪt / (BrE)
to overwhelm or overpower, especially with intense shock, grief, or distress
to overwhelm with shock or grief

Examples

She was devastated by the news of her friend's sudden passing.
The entire community felt devastated after the hurricane hit their town.
He looked so devastated that no one knew how to comfort him.

Synset Examples

He was devastated by his grief when his son died

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overwhelm - to overpower or overcome completely, especially with emotion

Etymology

From Latin devastare (to lay waste), from de- (completely) + vastare (to lay waste).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00261197-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈdɛvəˌsteɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəsteɪt / (BrE)
to cause great destruction or damage to something; to ruin utterly
to destroy or ruin completely

Examples

The hurricane devastated the coastal city, leaving thousands without homes.
She was devastated by the news of her friend's sudden passing.
Wildfires have devastated large areas of forest this summer.

Synset Examples

The enemy lay waste to the countryside after the invasion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to destroy - to damage something so badly that it no longer exists or cannot be used
  • Synonym: ruin - to severely harm or damage; to spoil completely
  • Antonym: repair - to restore something that is damaged to a good condition

Etymology

From Latin devastare, from de- (intensive prefix) + vastare (to lay waste).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
