stogy

1. NOUN

/ ˈstoʊ.ɡi / (AmE), / ˈstəʊ.ɡi / (BrE)
a cheap, thin, and usually long cigar, typically of inferior quality
cheap, thin cigar

Examples

He pulled a stogy from his pocket and lit it with a match.
The old man was always seen with a stogy clenched between his teeth.
Instead of an expensive cigar, he preferred a simple stogy after dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cigar - a cylindrical roll of tobacco for smoking

Etymology

Originally slang for 'Conestoga' wagons, from Pennsylvania, where such cigars were smoked.
