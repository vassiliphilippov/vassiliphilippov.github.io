brow

1. NOUN

/ braʊ / (AmE), / braʊ / (BrE)
the part of the face above the eyes, including the forehead or the ridge over the eyes
part of face above the eyes

Examples

He frowned and a deep line appeared on his brow.
Sweat trickled down his brow after the long run.
She raised her brow in surprise at the news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forehead - the part of the face above the eyebrows

Etymology

From Old English 'brū' (eyebrow, eyelid), related to Old High German 'brā' (eyebrow).

2. NOUN

/ braʊ / (AmE), / braʊ / (BrE)
the arch of hair growing above each eye; an eyebrow
arch of hair above the eye

Examples

She raised her brow in surprise, not sure what he meant.
The actor's thick, dark brow gave him an intense look on stage.
Sweat dripped from his brow as he ran the last mile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eyebrow - the ridge of hair growing above the eye

Etymology

From Old English 'brū' (eyebrow, eyelid, forehead), of Germanic origin.

3. NOUN

/ braʊ / (AmE), / braʊ / (BrE)
the summit, edge, or upper part of a hill or slope
top or edge of a hill

Examples

We reached the brow of the hill just as the sun began to rise.
A house stood on the brow of the slope, overlooking the valley below.
She paused at the brow before descending to the village on the other side.

Synset Examples

the sun set behind the brow of distant hills

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summit - the highest point of a hill or mountain
  • Antonym: base - the lowest part or edge of something, especially a hill or mountain

Etymology

From Old English brū (eyebrow, eyelid, edge, or brim), later extended metaphorically to hills.
