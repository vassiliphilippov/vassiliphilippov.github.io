encomium

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈkoʊ.mi.əm / (AmE), / ɛnˈkəʊ.mɪ.əm / (BrE)
A formal expression of high praise; a laudatory statement or tribute.
formal expression of praise

Examples

The retiring professor received an encomium from her colleagues at the farewell banquet.
The mayor's speech was an encomium to the volunteers who rebuilt the community center.
He wrote a heartfelt encomium celebrating her many accomplishments in literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eulogy - a speech or piece of writing praising someone, especially one who has died
  • Synonym: panegyric - a public speech or published text in praise of someone or something
  • Antonym: criticism - the expression of disapproval of someone or something

Etymology

From Latin 'encomium', from Greek 'enkṓmion' (laudatory ode), from 'en-' (in) + 'komos' (revel).
