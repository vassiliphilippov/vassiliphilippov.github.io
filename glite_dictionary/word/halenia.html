Halenia

1. NOUN

/ həˈliːniə / (AmE), / həˈliːniə / (BrE)
A genus of herbs found in Eurasia and the Americas, known commonly as spurred gentians.
a genus of Eurasian and American spurred gentian herbs

Examples

The alpine meadow was dotted with several rare species of Halenia.
Botanists conducted a survey to study the distribution of Halenia across North America.
Many flowers in the genus Halenia are characterized by their distinctive spurred petals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gentian - A plant in the Gentianaceae family, known for its blue flowers

Etymology

Named after German botanist Franz Xaver von Halen; standard formation of botanical Latin.
