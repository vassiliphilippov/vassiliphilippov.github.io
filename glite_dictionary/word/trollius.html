Trollius

1. NOUN

/ ˈtroʊli.əs / (AmE), / ˈtrɒlɪ.əs / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbs of the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae), native to north temperate regions, commonly known as globeflowers.
genus of globeflowers

Examples

The garden's pond was bordered by a cluster of brilliant yellow Trollius.
Botanists observed several species of Trollius thriving in the alpine meadows.
She planted Trollius in the shade to complement her collection of perennials.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on a Germanic root, probably related to German *Trollblume* (globeflower).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtroʊli.əs / (AmE), / ˈtrɒlɪəs / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbaceous plants in the buttercup family, known as globeflowers, native to north temperate regions.
genus of globeflower plants

Examples

Many gardeners cultivate Trollius for their bright, globe-shaped yellow flowers.
Several species in the Trollius genus thrive in moist, cool climates.
The early summer bloom of Trollius adds vibrant color to garden borders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: globeflower - common name for Trollius species, round yellow flower

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, adopted as the botanical genus name.
