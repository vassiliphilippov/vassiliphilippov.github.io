Alosa alosa

1. NOUN

/ əˈloʊsə əˈloʊsə / (AmE), / əˈləʊsə əˈləʊsə / (BrE)
The scientific name for the European shad, a large migratory fish found in European rivers and coastal waters.
species of European shad fish

Examples

The Alosa alosa migrates upstream to spawn in freshwater rivers each spring.
Conservationists are studying the declining populations of Alosa alosa in Western Europe.
The Alosa alosa is distinguished by its silvery body and forked tail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: European shad - a species of shad fish found in European waters

Etymology

From Latin genus name *Alosa*, used for shad fish.
