obverse

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑbˌvɜrs / (AmE), / ˈɒbvɜːs / (BrE)
the front or more prominent side of an object, especially a coin or medal; the opposite or counterpart of something contrasted
front or principal side; opposite aspect

Examples

The obverse of the coin displays a portrait of the reigning monarch.
His kindness is only the obverse of his tendency to be overly generous.
Climate change is often discussed without considering its obverse: the measures needed for mitigation.

Synset Examples

the obverse of this issue

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reverse - the back or less prominent side, especially of a coin or medal

Etymology

From Latin 'obversus', past participle of 'obvertere' (to turn toward).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑbˌvɜrs / (AmE), / ˈɒbvɜːs / (BrE)
the side of a coin or medal that bears the principal design or inscription, usually the front or 'heads' side
front side of a coin or medal

Examples

The obverse of the coin features a portrait of the ancient ruler.
Collectors inspected the obverse for signs of wear and authenticity.
Every medal shows an emblem on its obverse and a motto on the reverse.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reverse - the opposite side of a coin or medal, typically the back or 'tails' side

Etymology

From Latin 'obversus', past participle of 'obvertere' meaning 'to turn toward'.
