payola

1. NOUN

/ peɪˈoʊlə / (AmE), / peɪˈəʊlə / (BrE)
the illegal practice of paying a disc jockey or broadcaster to promote certain recordings in order to influence their popularity
illegal payment to promote records

Examples

The record company was accused of offering payola to radio stations to boost their latest single.
He lost his job when the scandal over payola payments was exposed in the press.
Laws were tightened after several payola cases rocked the music industry in the 1950s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bribe - money or favor given to influence someone illegally
  • Synonym: kickback - an illicit payment for promoting or facilitating something

Etymology

Blend of 'pay' and 'Victrola' (trademark for a brand of phonograph), originating in the U.S. music industry.
