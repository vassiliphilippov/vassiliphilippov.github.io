negotiatress

1. NOUN

/ nɪˈɡoʊʃiəˌtrɛs / (AmE), / nɪˈɡəʊʃɪəˌtrɛs / (BrE)
a female negotiator, especially used in older or historical contexts
female negotiator (archaic)
archaic

Examples

In the peace talks, the skilled negotiatress managed to bridge the gap between the two parties.
Writers of the 19th century sometimes referred to a female envoy as a negotiatress.
She made history as the first negotiatress to lead international trade discussions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: negotiator - a person who negotiates agreements or deals

Etymology

Formed from 'negotiate' plus the suffix '-ess', used to denote female agents.
