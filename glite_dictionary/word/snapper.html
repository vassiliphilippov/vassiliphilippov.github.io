3. NOUN

/ ˈsnæpər / (AmE), / ˈsnæpə / (BrE)
a large, sharp-toothed marine fish of the family Lutjanidae, found mainly in tropical coastal waters, prized as food and sport fish
a type of tropical marine fish

Examples

We ordered grilled snapper at the seaside restaurant.
The fisherman proudly displayed his catch of a large snapper.
Snappers are popular among anglers in tropical regions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: red snapper - a common name for several species of snapper with red coloring

Etymology

Perhaps from the verb 'snap' due to the fish's quick biting action.
