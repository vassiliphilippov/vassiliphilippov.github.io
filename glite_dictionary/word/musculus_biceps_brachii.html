musculus biceps brachii

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌs.kjə.ləs ˈbaɪ.sɛps ˈbræki.aɪ / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kjʊ.ləs ˈbaɪ.sɛps ˈbræki.aɪ / (BrE)
a muscle in the upper arm that flexes the elbow and turns the forearm so the palm faces upward
muscle of the upper arm
medical

Examples

The musculus biceps brachii is responsible for flexing your elbow when you lift a weight.
Injury to the musculus biceps brachii can result in decreased arm strength and limited movement.
During the curl exercise, the musculus biceps brachii contracts to raise the forearm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biceps - common name for the musculus biceps brachii

Etymology

From Latin: 'musculus' (muscle), 'biceps' (two-headed), 'brachii' (of the arm).
