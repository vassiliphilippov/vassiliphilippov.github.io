remora

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛməˌrɑ / (AmE), / ˈrɛməˌrə / (BrE)
a small marine fish with a flattened, elongated body and a suction disk on its head, used to attach itself to larger fish or objects
fish that attaches to larger fish with suction disk

Examples

The remora clung tightly to the shark as it swam through the reef.
Fishermen sometimes spot remoras attached to the sides of large sea turtles.
By using its suction disk, a remora can hitch a ride on passing whales.

Etymology

From Latin remora (delay, hindrance), from re- (again) + mora (delay), originally referring to the fish's supposed ability to slow ships.
