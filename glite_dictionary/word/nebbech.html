nebbech

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛbək / (AmE), / ˈnɛbək / (BrE)
A person regarded as timid, unlucky, or ineffectual; a simpleton or unfortunate individual (originally from Yiddish).
timid or unfortunate simpleton
informal

Examples

He always seems like such a nebbech, afraid to speak up for himself at meetings.
In the story, the nebbech tries his best but luck is never on his side.
They hired him out of pity, thinking he was just a harmless nebbech.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schlemiel - an inept, clumsy person
  • Synonym: loser - an unsuccessful or ineffectual person

Etymology

From Yiddish נֶבֶּךְ (nebekh), meaning 'poor thing, simpleton', ultimately from Hebrew נֶבֶךְ (nebekh, 'poor wretch').
