nominalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːmənəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈnɒmɪnəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
(Philosophy) the doctrine that universals or abstract concepts have no independent existence but exist only as names for groups of individual objects.
philosophical doctrine denying universals

Examples

Many medieval philosophers debated the merits of nominalism versus realism.
According to nominalism, words like 'redness' do not correspond to independent entities.
Her studies focus on the history of nominalism in early modern philosophy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: realism - The doctrine that universals have real existence independent of objects
  • Synonym: conceptualism - The doctrine that universals exist only within the mind

Etymology

From Medieval Latin nominalismus, from Latin nomen (name).
