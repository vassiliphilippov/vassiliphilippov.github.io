bioassay

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊˈæseɪ / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.əʊˈæseɪ / (BrE)
A scientific test to measure the biological activity of a substance by examining its effect on living organisms and comparing the results with a standard.
test measuring a substance's biological effects
medical

Examples

Researchers performed a bioassay to determine the potency of the new drug.
The lab uses bioassays to detect toxic substances in samples.
A mosquito bioassay was conducted to evaluate the effectiveness of the insecticide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assay - determination of the content or quality of a substance

Etymology

From Greek 'bios' (life) + 'assay' (to test or analyze).

2. VERB

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊˈæ.seɪ / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪ.əʊˈæ.seɪ / (BrE)
to determine the biological activity or concentration of a substance by testing its effects on living organisms or cells.
test substance's effect on living organisms
medical

Examples

The laboratory will bioassay the new drug to determine its potency.
Each sample was bioassayed for its toxic effects on the bacterial culture.
They are currently bioassaying the compounds for antiviral properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: test - to examine or try something to find out its nature or quality
  • Synonym: to analyze - to examine in detail to discover meaning or essential features

Etymology

From 'bio-' (life) + 'assay' (analysis or test).
