claustrophobia

1. NOUN

/ klɔːstrəˈfoʊbiə / (AmE), / klɔːstrəˈfəʊbiə / (BrE)
an abnormal or irrational fear of being in enclosed or confined spaces
irrational fear of confined spaces
medical

Examples

She suffers from claustrophobia and avoids elevators whenever possible.
His claustrophobia made flying in small airplanes particularly stressful.
Many people with claustrophobia experience anxiety in crowded rooms or tight spaces.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agoraphobia - fear of open or public spaces
  • Degree Lower: fear - an unpleasant emotion caused by threat of danger or pain

Etymology

From Latin 'claustrum' (a closed space) and Greek 'phobos' (fear).
