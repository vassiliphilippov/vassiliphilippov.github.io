set-to

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛtˌtu / (AmE), / ˈsɛtˌtuː / (BrE)
a brief but energetic fight or argument
brief, vigorous fight or argument

Examples

After a minor misunderstanding, the two players had a set-to on the field.
There was a loud set-to between the neighbors last night.
The debate quickly turned into a heated set-to in front of the committee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarrel - an angry argument or disagreement
  • Synonym: scuffle - a short, confused fight or struggle

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛtˌtu / (AmE), / ˈsɛtˌtuː / (BrE)
a brief and often noisy quarrel or argument
brief quarrel or argument

Examples

There was a set-to between the neighbors about the parking space.
After a short set-to, they managed to resolve their differences.
The heated set-to left everyone at the table uncomfortable.

Synset Examples

The team had a set-to over project deadlines.
There was a set-to at the meeting about budget cuts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: argument - a reasoned or heated exchange of views
  • Synonym: fight - a physical or verbal conflict

Etymology

Probably from the idiomatic phrase 'set to', meaning to begin or engage in something energetically, first used in the early 19th century.
