magnetization

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæɡ.nə.təˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌmæɡ.nɪ.taɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The physical property of being magnetic, or the degree to which a material can be magnetized.
the property of being magnetic

Examples

The magnetization of the material increases when exposed to a strong magnetic field.
Scientists measured the magnetization of the rock samples to study Earth's magnetic history.
When heated above a certain temperature, a magnet loses its magnetization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnetism - the force exerted by magnets when they attract or repel each other

Etymology

From magnet + -ization, after French 'magnétisation'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmæɡnɪtəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmaɡnɪtaɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the process by which a material becomes magnetic, either temporarily or permanently
the process of becoming magnetic

Examples

The magnetization of iron can be achieved by rubbing it with a strong magnet.
After exposure to a magnetic field, the rod showed a clear increase in magnetization.
Demagnetizing a material involves reversing its magnetization process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnetism - the physical phenomenon produced by the motion of electric charge, resulting in attractive and repulsive forces between objects

Etymology

From 'magnetize' + '-ation', modeled after similar scientific formations.

3. NOUN

/ ˌmæɡ.nə.t̬əˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌmæɡ.nɪ.taɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the extent to which a material is magnetized, typically measured as the magnetic moment per unit volume
degree to which something is magnetized

Examples

The magnetization of the iron bar increased when it was placed in a strong magnetic field.
Researchers measured the magnetization to determine the properties of the new material.
A sudden drop in magnetization can indicate a critical change in the material's structure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: demagnetization - the process of removing magnetization from a material

Etymology

From magnet + -ization, after French 'magnétisation'.
