beguiler

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈɡaɪlər / (AmE), / bɪˈɡaɪlə / (BrE)
a person who charms or captivates others, often by personal attractiveness or charisma
person who charms or captivates

Examples

The beguiler enchanted everyone at the party with her witty conversation and effortless grace.
He was known as a beguiler, always able to win people over with his charm.
Despite his reputation as a beguiler, few could resist his warm smile and friendly demeanor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charmer - a person with an engaging personality who attracts or fascinates others
  • Antonym: deceiver - a person who misleads or tricks others

Etymology

From Middle English 'beguile' (to deceive or amuse).

2. NOUN

/ bɪˈɡaɪlər / (AmE), / bɪˈɡaɪlə / (BrE)
a person who deceives or charms someone, often by leading them to believe something that is untrue
a deceiver or charmer

Examples

The master beguiler convinced everyone with his charming stories.
She called him a beguiler for the way he twisted the truth.
Beware of the beguiler who promises more than he can deliver.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceiver - a person who causes someone to believe something that is not true
  • Synonym: trickster - a person who deceives or cheats people
  • Antonym: honest person - someone who is truthful and does not deceive others

Etymology

From the verb 'beguile', from Middle English 'begilen', from be- (prefix) + guile (deceit).
