unbelievable

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.bɪˈliv.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌʌn.bɪˈliː.və.bəl / (BrE)
so extraordinary or unlikely as to be difficult or impossible to believe.
extraordinary; difficult to believe

Examples

The magician's final trick was unbelievable and left the audience speechless.
Her story sounded so unbelievable that no one thought it could be true.
The team's comeback victory was simply unbelievable after they were trailing by so much.

Synset Examples

at incredible speed
the book's plot is simply incredible

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incredible - so extraordinary as to be difficult to believe; astonishing
  • Antonym: plausible - seeming reasonable or probable

Etymology

From un- (not) + believable.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.bɪˈliː.və.bəl / (AmE), / ˌʌn.bɪˈliː.və.bəl / (BrE)
so unlikely or improbable as to be difficult or impossible to believe
too improbable to be believed

Examples

The magician's final trick was so unbelievable that the audience gasped in amazement.
Her story about winning the lottery twice in one year sounded unbelievable.
It is unbelievable how quickly technology is advancing these days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incredible - so extraordinary as to seem impossible
  • Antonym: plausible - seeming reasonable or probable

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'believable' (able to be believed).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01805119-s
Synset POS: s
