Tabernaemontana divaricata

1. NOUN

/ təˌbɜrnəˌmɒnˈtɑːnə dɪˌværɪˈkeɪtə / (AmE), / təˌbɜːnəməˌnɒnˈtɑːnə dɪˌvɑːrɪˈkeɪtə / (BrE)
A tropical shrub from northern India to Thailand with glossy leaves and fragrant, night-blooming white flowers that have crimped or wavy corollas.
species of tropical shrub with fragrant nocturnal flowers

Examples

The garden featured several Tabernaemontana divaricata shrubs blooming with fragrant white flowers at dusk.
Many traditional medicines in Southeast Asia use parts of Tabernaemontana divaricata for their healing properties.
He was captivated by the glossy foliage and crimped petals of Tabernaemontana divaricata during his botanical expedition.

Etymology

From Latin, with genus name honoring 16th-century botanist Jakob Theodor von Bergzabern (Tabernaemontanus).
