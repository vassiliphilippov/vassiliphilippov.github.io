atom

1. NOUN

/ ˈætəm / (AmE), / ˈætəm / (BrE)
the smallest unit of a chemical element that retains the properties of that element, consisting of a nucleus surrounded by electrons
smallest unit of an element

Examples

An atom of hydrogen consists of one proton and one electron.
Chemists study how atoms combine to form molecules.
Every object in the universe is made up of atoms.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: molecule - a group of atoms bonded together
  • Synonym: ion - an atom or molecule with an electric charge due to loss or gain of electrons
  • Degree Higher: element - a substance made of only one kind of atom

Etymology

From Greek 'atomos' meaning 'indivisible', from 'a-' (not) + 'temnein' (to cut).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæt̬.əm / (AmE), / ˈæt.əm / (BrE)
A very small amount or particle of something; the smallest possible part.
tiny particle or amount

Examples

She doesn't have an atom of doubt about her decision.
Not an atom of evidence was found at the scene.
There wasn't an atom of truth in his story.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: molecule - the smallest particle of a substance that retains its chemical properties
  • Synonym: speck - a small particle or spot

Etymology

From Greek atomos, meaning 'uncuttable' or 'indivisible', referring to something that cannot be divided further.
