Martin Luther King Jr.

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɑrtɪn ˈluːθər kɪŋ ˈdʒuːniɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtɪn ˈluːθə kɪŋ ˈdʒuːniə / (BrE)
(1929–1968) United States civil rights leader and Baptist minister known for advocating nonviolent resistance to racial segregation and injustice.
U.S. civil rights leader (1929–1968)

Examples

Every January, Americans celebrate the legacy of Martin Luther King Jr. on a national holiday.
Martin Luther King Jr. delivered his iconic 'I Have a Dream' speech in 1963.
The teachings of Martin Luther King Jr. continue to inspire movements for equality around the world.
