mercifulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɜrsɪfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈmɜːsɪfəlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of showing compassion or leniency towards someone, especially an offender or someone subject to justice
compassion or leniency towards offenders

Examples

The judge's mercifulness toward first-time offenders earned her widespread respect.
Showing mercifulness in difficult situations is a sign of true strength.
Their leader was known for his mercifulness, often granting pardons instead of punishment.

Synset Examples

he threw himself on the mercy of the court

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mercy - compassion or forgiveness shown toward someone
  • Antonym: ruthlessness - lack of compassion or pity for others

Etymology

From merciful + -ness, modeled on Middle English mercifulnesse.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɜrsɪfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈmɜːsɪfʊlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of showing mercy or compassion towards others
quality of showing mercy or compassion

Examples

Her mercifulness was evident in the way she forgave those who wronged her.
The judge's mercifulness led to a lighter sentence for the defendant.
Acts of mercifulness are often remembered long after harsh judgments are forgotten.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compassion - sympathetic pity and concern for the sufferings of others
  • Antonym: cruelty - callous indifference or pleasure in causing pain or suffering

Etymology

From 'merciful' + '-ness', with 'merciful' from Old French *mercif* (merciful), ultimately from Latin *mercēs* (reward, wages, mercy).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɜrsɪflnəs / (AmE), / ˈmɜːsɪfəlnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of showing compassion or forgiveness to others, especially when it is within one's power to punish or harm.
quality of being merciful or forgiving

Examples

Her mercifulness in the face of betrayal earned her great respect.
The leader's mercifulness prevented a harsh punishment for the offenders.
Many admired the mercifulness he showed during difficult times.

Synset Examples

in those days a wife had to depend on the mercifulness of her husband

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mercy - compassion or forgiveness shown toward someone
  • Antonym: ruthlessness - the quality of having no pity or compassion

Etymology

From 'merciful' + '-ness'; ultimately from Latin 'mercifulis' (merciful).
