hassle

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæsəl / (AmE), / ˈhæsəl / (BrE)
a situation causing difficulty, trouble, or disagreement, often involving argument or annoyance
argument or troublesome situation

Examples

I don't want to deal with the hassle of returning this item to the store.
After a long day, the last thing she needed was more hassle at home.
There was a lot of hassle at the meeting over the new proposal.

Synset Examples

he didn't want to make a fuss
they had labor trouble
a spot of bother

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuss - unnecessary or excessive excitement or activity, especially about something unimportant
  • Synonym: bother - trouble or worry caused by a difficult situation
  • Antonym: peace - a state or period in which there is no war, or a disturbance

Etymology

Originated in American English in the 20th century, possibly an alteration of 'haggle'.

2. VERB

/ ˈhæsəl / (AmE), / ˈhæsəl / (BrE)
to annoy or trouble someone repeatedly or persistently
annoy or bother persistently
informal

Examples

Stop hassling me about my homework—I said I'd get it done.
She felt hassled by constant calls from telemarketers.
The manager hassles his team over minor mistakes every day.

Synset Examples

He is known to harry his staff when he is overworked
This man harasses his female co-workers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to harass - to annoy or trouble someone repeatedly
  • Synonym: bother - to cause minor annoyance to someone
