Sundacarpus amara

1. NOUN

/ sʌn.dəˈkɑːr.pəs əˈmɑː.rə / (AmE), / sʌn.dəˈkɑː.pəs əˈmɑː.rə / (BrE)
A large, fast-growing tropical evergreen tree with large glossy lanceolate leaves, native to rainforests from Sumatra and the Philippines to northern Queensland.
species of tropical evergreen tree

Examples

The Sundacarpus amara thrives in the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia and Australia.
Botanists admire the Sundacarpus amara for its unusually large lance-shaped leaves.
Many conservation programs aim to protect the habitat of Sundacarpus amara due to its ecological importance.

Etymology

From the genus name Sundacarpus and species name amara, used in botanical Latin.
