keystone

1. NOUN

/ ˈkiːstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈkiːstəʊn / (BrE)
the most important part of a system, organization, or plan, supporting or holding together all the other parts
central, supporting element

Examples

Trust is the keystone of any successful partnership.
Education has always been the keystone of their community’s progress.
Her leadership became the keystone that held the project together.

Synset Examples

faith is his anchor
the keystone of campaign reform was the ban on soft money
he is the linchpin of this firm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linchpin - a person or thing vital to an enterprise or organization
  • Synonym: cornerstone - an important quality or feature on which a particular thing depends or is based

Etymology

Originally from architecture: the central stone at the summit of an arch, from 'key' + 'stone'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkiːstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈkiːstəʊn / (BrE)
the central, wedge-shaped stone at the apex of an arch or vault, locking the whole together
central stone at top of an arch

Examples

The mason carefully placed the keystone to complete the archway.
Without the keystone, the entire structure would collapse.
Architects admired the intricately carved keystone above the entrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capstone - the top stone of a building or structure
