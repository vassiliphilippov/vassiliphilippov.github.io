Umayyad

1. NOUN

/ uˈmaɪjəd / (AmE), / uˈmaɪjəd / (BrE)
a member of the first dynasty of Arab caliphs, which ruled from Damascus from 661 to 750 CE
member of early Arab caliph dynasty

Examples

The Umayyad dynasty established Damascus as the capital of the Islamic empire.
Many architectural achievements from the Umayyad period still stand today.
After the fall of the Umayyad caliphate, the Abbasids rose to power.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Abbasid - the dynasty that succeeded the Umayyads as caliphs

Etymology

From Arabic Banū Umayya, meaning 'sons of Umayya.'
