erythrite

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛr.ɪˌθraɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛr.ɪˌθraɪt / (BrE)
a reddish mineral composed of hydrated cobalt arsenate, occurring in monoclinic crystals and used especially in coloring glass
reddish mineral (hydrated cobalt arsenate)

Examples

The bright pink crystals of erythrite were easily visible under the microscope.
Miners discovered a vein rich in erythrite alongside cobalt ore.
Samples of erythrite are often prized by mineral collectors for their striking color.

Etymology

From Greek 'erythros' (red), referring to its color.
