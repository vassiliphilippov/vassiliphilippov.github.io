Clupeidae

1. NOUN

/ kluːˈpiːɪˌdiː / (AmE), / kluːˈpiːɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, silvery marine fish, including herrings, shad, and sardines.
family of fish including herrings and sardines

Examples

Sardines belong to the Clupeidae family, which also includes herrings and shad.
Marine biologists study the population dynamics of various Clupeidae species in the Atlantic Ocean.
The Clupeidae are known for forming large schools near the surface of the water.

Etymology

From modern Latin Clupeidae, based on Clupea (the type genus, herring).

2. NOUN

/ kluːˈpiːɪˌdiː / (AmE), / kluːˈpiːɪdiː / (BrE)
a large family of mainly marine fish, including herrings, shad, and sardines, typically having a compressed body and silvery scales
family of fish including herrings and sardines

Examples

The Clupeidae family includes commercially important fish such as sardines and herrings.
Many species within Clupeidae are vital for marine food webs and human consumption.
Researchers are studying the migration patterns of Clupeidae in the Atlantic Ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herring - a small, silvery fish of the Clupeidae family
  • Synonym: sardine - a small, oily fish of the Clupeidae family, often canned

Etymology

From modern Latin Clupeidae, from Clupea (the herring genus), from Latin clupea (herring).
