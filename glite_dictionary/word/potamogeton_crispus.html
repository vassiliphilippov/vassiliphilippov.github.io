Potamogeton crispus

1. NOUN

/ poʊˌtæməˈdʒiːtən ˈkrɪspəs / (AmE), / pəˌtæməˈdʒiːtən ˈkrɪspəs / (BrE)
A species of aquatic plant, commonly known as curly-leaf pondweed, native to Europe and naturalized in North America.
species of aquatic plant (curly-leaf pondweed)

Examples

Potamogeton crispus is often found in ponds and slow-moving streams throughout the United States.
The rapid growth of Potamogeton crispus can sometimes cause problems for native aquatic plants.
Researchers studied how Potamogeton crispus affects oxygen levels in freshwater ecosystems.

Etymology

From Greek 'potamos' (river) + 'geiton' (neighbor), with the species epithet from Latin 'crispus' (curled).
