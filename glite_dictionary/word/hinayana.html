Hinayana

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪniˈjɑːnə / (AmE), / ˌhɪniˈjɑːnə / (BrE)
a historical school of Buddhism emphasizing personal salvation through individual effort, contrasted with the Mahayana tradition
school of Buddhism focused on personal salvation

Examples

The term Hinayana is often considered pejorative by some Buddhist communities.
Scholars debate the relevance of Hinayana in contemporary Buddhist studies.
Early Buddhist scriptures formed the doctrinal basis of what came to be known as Hinayana.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Mahayana - a major school of Buddhism teaching universal salvation, contrasted with Hinayana

Etymology

From Sanskrit hīnayāna (literally 'lesser vehicle'), from hīna (inferior) + yāna (vehicle).

2. NOUN

/ hiːnəˈjɑːnə / (AmE), / hɪnəˈjɑːnə / (BrE)
an offensive term for the early, conservative branch of Buddhism, often identified with Theravada; no longer used by practitioners and considered disrespectful
offensive term for early conservative Buddhism
offensive

Examples

The term Hinayana is now considered pejorative and is avoided in academic writing.
Many scholars prefer to use 'early Buddhism' instead of Hinayana to avoid offense.
Theravada Buddhism, sometimes labeled Hinayana by its critics, is practiced mainly in Sri Lanka and Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Theravada - the earliest surviving Buddhist school, emphasizing personal enlightenment

Etymology

From Sanskrit hīna (less, inferior) + yāna (vehicle), meaning 'the lesser vehicle'.
