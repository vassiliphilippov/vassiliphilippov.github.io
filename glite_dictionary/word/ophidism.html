ophidism

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑfɪˌdɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɒfɪˌdɪzəm / (BrE)
poisoning resulting from the bite or venom of a snake.
snake venom poisoning
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with ophidism after being bitten by a venomous viper.
Effective treatment of ophidism often requires prompt administration of antivenom.
Researchers are studying new ways to prevent ophidism in areas prone to snakebites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: envenomation - the process of being poisoned by venom; especially via animal bite or sting

Etymology

From Greek 'ophis' (snake) + '-ism'.
