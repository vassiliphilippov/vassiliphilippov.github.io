anorak

1. NOUN

/ ˈænəˌræk / (AmE), / ˈanərak / (BrE)
A waterproof jacket, typically with a hood, often worn in cold or wet weather.
a hooded waterproof jacket

Examples

I wore my anorak when hiking in the Scottish Highlands.
She pulled up the hood of her anorak as the rain started to fall.
His red anorak kept him warm and dry during the storm.

Etymology

From Greenlandic 'annoraaq' (anorak, windproof jacket).
