Augmentin

1. NOUN

/ ɔgˈmɛntɪn / (AmE), / ɔːɡˈmɛntɪn / (BrE)
A trademarked antibiotic combining amoxicillin and clavulanic acid, used to treat bacterial infections.
branded antibiotic (amoxicillin and clavulanic acid)
medical

Examples

My doctor prescribed Augmentin for my sinus infection after the first antibiotic didn't work.
Children with ear infections are often treated with Augmentin to combat resistant bacteria.
If you experience a rash while taking Augmentin, contact your physician immediately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amoxicillin - a penicillin antibiotic used to treat infections
