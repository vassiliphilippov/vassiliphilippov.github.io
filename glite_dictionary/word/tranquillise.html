to tranquillise

1. VERB

/ træŋˈkwɪlaɪz / (AmE), / træŋˈkwɪlaɪz / (BrE)
to make a person or animal calm or quiet, especially by giving them a drug
calm or quiet with a drug

Examples

The vet needed to tranquillise the wild dog before approaching it.
Doctors sometimes tranquillise patients who are extremely anxious or agitated.
It took only a few minutes for the medication to tranquillise the animal.

Synset Examples

The patient must be sedated before the operation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sedate - to calm or quiet with a sedative
  • Antonym: to stimulate - to make more active or alert

Etymology

From Latin 'tranquillus' (calm, quiet) + English suffix '-ise'.

2. VERB

/ træŋˈkwɪlaɪz / (AmE), / træŋˈkwɪlʌɪz / (BrE)
to make someone or something calm or still, especially by administering a calming drug
make calm or still

Examples

The veterinarian tried to tranquillise the wild animal before performing the procedure.
She listened to soft music to tranquillise her nerves before the exam.
Police had to tranquillise the escaped tiger to safely return it to the sanctuary.

Synset Examples

quiet the dragons of worry and fear

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calm - make (someone) tranquil or quiet
  • Antonym: to agitate - make (someone) nervous or uneasy

Etymology

From Latin tranquillus ('calm, quiet') + English suffix -ise.
