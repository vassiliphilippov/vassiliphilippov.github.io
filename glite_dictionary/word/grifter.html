grifter

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɪftɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪftə / (BrE)
a person who deceives or cheats others out of money or possessions, typically through fraud or trickery
person who swindles or deceives
informal american

Examples

The police arrested the grifter after he tried to sell fake tickets to a concert.
She realized too late that the charming stranger was actually a skilled grifter.
In the movie, the main character starts as a grifter before turning his life around.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swindler - person who cheats or defrauds others
  • Synonym: con artist - person who deceives people by persuading them to believe something false
  • Antonym: honest person - person who acts honestly and does not cheat

Etymology

Probably from 'grift', American slang for petty swindling, of uncertain origin.
