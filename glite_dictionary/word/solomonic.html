Solomonic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɑːləˈmɑːnɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɒləˈmɒnɪk / (BrE)
showing or requiring great wisdom and fairness, especially in making difficult decisions, like the wisdom attributed to King Solomon in the Bible.
showing great wisdom in decision-making

Examples

The judge made a Solomonic decision to split the inheritance equally between the siblings.
Faced with a complex dilemma, the committee relied on a Solomonic solution that pleased both parties.
Her Solomonic wisdom helped resolve the conflict without anyone feeling cheated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wise - having or showing experience, knowledge, and good judgment
  • Antonym: foolish - lacking good sense or judgment

Etymology

From Solomon, the biblical king famed for his wisdom, plus the suffix -ic.
