auxiliary

1. ADJ

/ ɔɡˈzɪljəri / (AmE), / ɔːɡˈzɪljəri / (BrE)
(of equipment, staff, or institutions) providing additional help or support; supplementary.
providing additional help or support

Examples

The hospital used its auxiliary staff to handle the increased number of patients.
A generator can serve as an auxiliary power source during electrical outages.
Many schools have auxiliary buildings for sports and extracurricular activities.

Synset Examples

the main library and its auxiliary branches

Related Terms

  • Antonym: main - chief or principal; primary
  • Synonym: supplementary - providing additional or extra support

Etymology

From Latin auxiliarius, from auxilium (help).

3. NOUN

/ ɔgˈzɪljəri / (AmE), / ɔːgˈzɪljəri / (BrE)
a person who provides help or support, especially as an assistant in a particular organization or activity
assistant; helper

Examples

The hospital relies on auxiliaries to support nursing staff during busy periods.
Several auxiliary members helped organize the charity event last weekend.
He served as an auxiliary in the volunteer fire department.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assistant - a person who helps someone else in their work
  • Synonym: helper - someone who helps another person or group

Etymology

From Latin auxiliarius (helping), from auxilium (help, aid).
