mullioned

1. ADJ

/ ˈmʌliənd / (AmE), / ˈmʌliənd / (BrE)
(of a window) having vertical bars or piers dividing the panes, typically made of stone or wood.
having windows divided by vertical bars

Examples

The castle's great hall was filled with light from its mullioned windows.
They restored the old manor, keeping the distinctive mullioned frames for historical accuracy.
The church features beautiful mullioned arches along its nave.

Synset Examples

mullioned windows

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mullion - vertical bar between panes of glass in a window

Etymology

From mullion + -ed, where mullion is from Middle French mouillon (vertical division in a window).
