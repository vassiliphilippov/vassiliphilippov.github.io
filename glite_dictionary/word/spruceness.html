spruceness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsprusnəs / (AmE), / ˈspruːsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being neat, smart, and well-groomed, especially in appearance or dress
state of being neat and well-dressed

Examples

His spruceness was evident in the way he dressed for the interview.
She admired the spruceness of his office, with everything arranged perfectly.
Despite the early hour, the hotel's staff greeted guests with their usual spruceness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neatness - the quality of being clean, tidy, and orderly
  • Antonym: sloppiness - lack of neatness or order

Etymology

From 'spruce' (neat, tidy), itself possibly from Spruce, an old term for Prussia.
