sneeze

1. NOUN

/ sniz / (AmE), / sniːz / (BrE)
An involuntary expulsion of air from the nose and mouth, usually caused by irritation of the nasal passages.
involuntary expulsion of air from the nose

Examples

He let out a loud sneeze during the meeting, startling everyone.
If you have a cold, you may get a sneeze every few minutes.
A sudden sneeze can spread germs in the air.

Etymology

Probably imitative in origin, first recorded in English in the Middle Ages.

2. VERB

/ sniːz / (AmE), / sniːz / (BrE)
to suddenly force air out of your nose and mouth in a way that you cannot control, usually because of irritation inside your nose
force air out of the nose/mouth suddenly

Examples

Whenever I have allergies, I tend to sneeze many times in a row.
She tried to hold back a sneeze, but it burst out suddenly during the meeting.
If you sneeze without covering your mouth, you might spread germs to others.

Synset Examples

Pepper makes me sneeze

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cough - to expel air abruptly from the lungs, usually to clear the throat or airways
  • Synonym: sniffle - to make a repeated sniffing sound, often when crying or with a cold

Etymology

Probably imitative; related to Middle English 'nesen' and Old English 'fnēosan' (to sneeze).
