sulfur

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌl.fɚ / (AmE), / ˈsʌl.fə / (BrE)
A yellow, nonmetallic chemical element (symbol S), occurring widely in minerals and in pure form, used in industry and essential for life.
yellow, nonmetallic chemical element

Examples

Pure sulfur is bright yellow and found near volcanoes.
The chemical industry uses a lot of sulfur to produce fertilizers.
They studied the presence of sulfur in mineral samples from the mine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulphur - British English spelling of 'sulfur'

Etymology

From Latin 'sulphur' or 'sulfur', meaning 'brimstone'.

2. VERB

/ ˈsʌl.fɚ / (AmE), / ˈsʌl.fə / (BrE)
to treat (something), especially food, with sulfur, usually to preserve it or prevent discoloration
treat with sulfur to preserve

Examples

The packers sulfur the dried apricots to maintain their bright color.
Many wineries sulfur the barrels before use to prevent spoilage.
The peaches were carefully sulfured before being stored for the winter.

Synset Examples

These dried fruits are sulphured

Etymology

From Latin 'sulphur' (brimstone), via Old French and Middle English, with modern spelling influenced by scientific conventions.
