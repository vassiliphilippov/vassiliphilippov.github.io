to enthrone

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈθroʊn / (AmE), / ɪnˈθrəʊn / (BrE)
to give someone power and authority, often in a formal or ceremonial way
install in a position of authority

Examples

The new king was enthroned in a grand ceremony attended by heads of state.
With her popular support, many believed democracy had finally been enthroned in the nation.
The board decided to enthrone him as the company's next CEO after months of deliberation.

Synset Examples

They vested the council with special rights

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to dethrone - to remove from a position of power or authority

Etymology

From Old French *enthroner*, from Medieval Latin *entronare* – en- (put in) + throne.

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈθroʊn / (AmE), / ɪnˈθrəʊn / (BrE)
to put a monarch or ruler formally on the throne or in a position of authority, especially in a ceremonial manner
install a monarch in authority

Examples

The new king was formally enthroned during an elaborate state ceremony.
It took several months to enthrone the rightful heir after the king's death.
Historians note that she was enthroned at a very young age.

Synset Examples

The Queen was enthroned more than 50 years ago

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to dethrone - remove a monarch from the throne
  • Synonym: crown - officially declare someone to be king or queen, often with a ceremony

Etymology

From Old French enthroner, based on Latin in- (in) + thronus (throne).

3. VERB

/ ɛnˈθroʊn / (AmE), / ɪnˈθrəʊn / (BrE)
to formally install someone in a position of authority, especially as a monarch, bishop, or other leader
formally install in authority

Examples

The board chose to enthrone the new CEO after months of searching for the right candidate.
In ancient traditions, priests would enthrone the king with elaborate rituals and blessings.
She hopes to enthrone a sense of justice and fairness as her guiding principles.

Synset Examples

Tradition dictates that we enthrone the leader with a ceremony.
They planned to enthrone the new king at dawn.
The council decided to enthrone the queen next month.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to dethrone - to remove someone from a position of authority, especially a monarch
  • Synonym: crown - to formally invest someone, especially a monarch, with regal power

Etymology

From Old French 'en-' (in) + 'trone' (throne), from Latin 'thronus'.
