teratogenesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrətoʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌtɛrətəˈdʒɛnɪsɪs / (BrE)
the process by which congenital defects develop in an embryo or fetus
development of birth defects in an embryo
medical

Examples

Exposure to certain chemicals during pregnancy can result in teratogenesis in the developing embryo.
Researchers are studying the mechanisms of teratogenesis to better understand how birth defects occur.
The risk of teratogenesis increases when harmful substances are encountered in early pregnancy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teratogen - an agent that causes malformations in an embryo

Etymology

From Greek 'teras' (monster, marvel) + 'genesis' (origin, creation).
