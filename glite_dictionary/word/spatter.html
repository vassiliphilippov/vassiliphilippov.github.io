spatter

1. VERB

/ ˈspæt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈspætə / (BrE)
(of rain or another liquid) to fall or be scattered in small drops or spots, often lightly or intermittently.
to fall or scatter in small drops

Examples

A light drizzle began to spatter the pavement as we walked home.
It may only spatter today, but streets can still become slippery.
The windows spattered with the soft, intermittent rain.

Synset Examples

It has only sprinkled, but the roads are slick

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sprinkle - to rain very lightly

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, related to 'spit' and 'splatter.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02763786-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈspæt̬ər / (AmE), / ˈspætə / (BrE)
a short, sharp noise made when a liquid or small particles hit a surface, or when a series of explosive sounds occur
sharp, explosive noise or splashing sound

Examples

There was a sudden spatter of rain against the window.
She was startled by the spatter of gravel as the car sped away.
A spatter of applause followed the speaker’s remarks.

Synset Examples

he heard a spatter of gunfire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sputter - make a series of soft explosive or spitting sounds
  • Synonym: burst - a sudden, brief outbreak or spurt, especially of sound or activity

Etymology

From Middle English spateren, of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07409414-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈspætər / (AmE), / ˈspætə / (BrE)
to splash or dash a liquid or small particles onto a surface, often in scattered drops or spots
splash liquid or particles in scattered drops

Examples

Rain began to spatter the windows during the storm.
She accidentally spattered paint on her jeans while working on the mural.
Oil from the pan spatters onto the stove when cooking bacon.

Synset Examples

The mother splashed the baby's face with water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splash - to cause liquid to fly or scatter in drops
  • Degree Lower: dribble - to let liquid fall in drops or a thin stream

Etymology

From Middle English spateren, probably of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01376685-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈspæt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈspætə / (BrE)
to scatter drops or small spots of a liquid or substance over a surface, especially so as to soil it slightly
scatter spots or drops over a surface

Examples

The rain spattered the window with tiny drops.
Paint spattered onto his shirt while he was working.
She spattered the apron with flour as she baked the bread.

Synset Examples

The baby spattered the bib with food

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splash - to cause liquid to fall or be thrown in drops
  • Synonym: soil - to make dirty, especially by spotting or staining

Etymology

Probably frequentative of 'spat', imitative of the sound made by a splashing liquid.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01541417-v
Synset POS: v

5. NOUN

/ ˈspætər / (AmE), / ˈspætə / (BrE)
an act or instance of splashing a liquid onto a surface or in small drops
an instance of liquid being splashed

Examples

There was a spatter of paint on the floor near the canvas.
Rain left a spatter of droplets across the windshield.
He cleaned up the spatter of sauce that landed on the counter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splash - an act of liquid striking a surface and scattering
  • Degree Lower: dribble - to fall in small drops or a small stream

Etymology

Probably imitative; related to 'spat' (to splash).

Source: WordNet
