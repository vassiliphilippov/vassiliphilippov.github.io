to demilitarize

1. VERB

/ diˈmɪlɪtəˌraɪz / (AmE), / diˈmɪlɪtəraɪz / (BrE)
to remove all military forces, equipment, or capability from (a region, organization, or group)
remove military capability

Examples

The two countries agreed to demilitarize the border area as part of the peace treaty.
Following the conflict, international forces helped demilitarize the region.
The government announced plans to demilitarize several strategic zones.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to militarize - to equip with military forces or character

Etymology

From de- (removal) + militarize, ultimately from Latin 'militaris' (of soldiers or war).

2. VERB

/ diˈmɪlɪtəˌraɪz / (AmE), / diˈmɪlɪtəˌraɪz / (BrE)
to remove all military forces or military potential from (an area, organization, etc.)
remove military forces or potential

Examples

After the peace agreement, both sides agreed to demilitarize the border region.
The United Nations sent inspectors to ensure the country would demilitarize its chemical weapons program.
They hope to eventually demilitarize the entire zone to promote lasting stability.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to militarize - to equip or supply with military forces

Etymology

From de- (removal) + militarize (to equip with military forces), patterned after demilitarisé in French.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01101801-v
Synset POS: v
