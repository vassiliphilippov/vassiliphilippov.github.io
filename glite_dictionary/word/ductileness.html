ductileness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌktɪl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdʌktɪl.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being able to be drawn out into a thin wire or hammered into thin sheets without breaking; malleability.
quality of being able to be drawn into wire

Examples

The ductileness of copper makes it ideal for electrical wiring.
Engineers tested the ductileness of the new metal alloy in the laboratory.
Due to its high ductileness, gold can be fashioned into extremely thin sheets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malleability - The ability of a substance to be hammered or rolled into thin sheets without breaking
  • Antonym: brittleness - The property of a material that fractures when subjected to stress

Etymology

From Latin ductilis ‘that may be led or drawn’, from ducere ‘to lead’.
