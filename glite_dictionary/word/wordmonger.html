wordmonger

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːdˌmʌŋɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɜːdˌmʌŋɡə / (BrE)
a person, especially a writer, who uses words carelessly or pretentiously with little regard for meaning
person who uses words pretentiously or carelessly

Examples

The novel was filled with pretentious language, revealing the author as a true wordmonger.
Critics accused the politician of being a wordmonger who disguised empty promises with grandiose speeches.
She dismissed his argument as little more than the ramblings of a wordmonger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windbag - a person who talks too much without saying anything important
  • Synonym: pedant - someone overly concerned with formal rules and minor details

Etymology

Formed from 'word' + 'monger' (dealer or trader).
