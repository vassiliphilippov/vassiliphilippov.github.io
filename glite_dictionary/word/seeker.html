seeker

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːkər / (AmE), / ˈsiːkə / (BrE)
a missile guidance device that detects and follows a target by being attracted to its emissions of heat, light, sound, or radio waves
device in a missile that tracks target emissions

Examples

The missile's seeker locked onto the enemy aircraft's heat signature.
Modern infrared seekers enable missiles to accurately track moving targets.
Radar-guided seeker systems are used in many anti-aircraft missiles.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsiːkər / (AmE), / ˈsiːkə / (BrE)
a person who is searching for or trying to obtain something, especially knowledge, truth, or a particular goal
a person searching or pursuing something

Examples

As a lifelong seeker of truth, she traveled the world to broaden her perspective.
Many seekers come to the city with hopes of finding spiritual guidance.
He is a seeker of rare books, always searching old bookstores for hidden gems.

Synset Examples

they are seekers after truth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: searcher - a person who searches for something
  • Degree Higher: finder - a person who finds something after searching

Etymology

From Old English *sēcan* (to seek) with agent suffix '-er'.
