bale

1. VERB

/ beɪl / (AmE), / beɪl / (BrE)
to make something into a bale, especially by compressing and binding it tightly for storage or transport
make into a tightly packed bundle

Examples

Farmers often bale hay in late summer to prepare for winter feeding.
After cutting the cotton, workers began to bale it using the machine.
We need to bale the straw before the rain starts.

Synset Examples

bale hay

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bundle - to tie or wrap things together into a bundle

Etymology

From Middle English bal (bundle), from Old French bale.

2. NOUN

/ beɪl / (AmE), / beɪl / (BrE)
A large bundle of material, such as hay, cotton, or paper, tightly bound with cords or wrapping for storage or transport.
large bundle of material

Examples

The farmer stacked each bale of hay neatly in the barn.
A bale of recycled paper was delivered to the factory.
They loaded the cotton bales onto the truck for shipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bundle - a collection of things tied or wrapped together

Etymology

From Old French *bale* (bundle) and Old English *bǣl* (fire, funeral pile), but likely influenced in meaning by Old French.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02781461-n
Synset POS: n
