technological

1. ADJ

/ ˌtɛk.nəˈlɑ.dʒɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌtek.nəˈlɒdʒ.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
Relating to or involving technology, especially scientific or industrial progress or developments.
relating to technology or technical progress

Examples

The rapid spread of technological innovations has changed the way we communicate.
Some countries are global leaders in technological development and research.
The job requires someone with strong technological expertise in software and electronics.

Synset Examples

a technological civilization

Related Terms

  • Synonym: technical - relating to practical skills or techniques, often in a specific field

Etymology

From Greek 'tekhnē' (art, skill) + '-logical' from 'logos' (study, word).

2. ADJ

/ ˌtɛknəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌtɛknəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or involving technology or its practical applications, especially as organized by scientific principles
relating to technology or scientific applications

Examples

The company is investing in technological innovation to remain competitive in the market.
The exhibition showcases the technological advances made during the past decade.
Many modern conveniences rely on technological developments to function efficiently.

Synset Examples

technical college
technological development

Related Terms

  • Synonym: technical - having to do with a particular subject, art, or craft, or its techniques

Etymology

From technology + -ical, technology from Greek tekhnologia (systematic treatment).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02820431-a
Synset POS: a
