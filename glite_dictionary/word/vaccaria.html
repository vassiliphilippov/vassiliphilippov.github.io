Vaccaria

1. NOUN

/ vækˈkɛəriə / (AmE), / vakˈkeə.rɪ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Caryophyllaceae, commonly known as cow-cockle.
a genus of flowering plants (cow-cockle)

Examples

Vaccaria is often found in grain fields across Europe and Asia.
The dried seeds of Vaccaria are sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
Farmers have learned to identify Vaccaria to prevent it from spreading among crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cow-cockle - a common name for plants of the genus Vaccaria

Etymology

From Latin 'vacca' (cow), referring to the plant's association with fields grazed by cows.
