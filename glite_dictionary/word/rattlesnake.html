rattlesnake

1. NOUN

/ ˈrætlˌsneɪk / (AmE), / ˈrætlˌsneɪk / (BrE)
a venomous American snake with a series of horny segments at the end of its tail that make a rattling sound when shaken
a venomous snake with a rattling tail

Examples

The distinctive rattle of a rattlesnake warns predators to keep their distance.
Hikers spotted a rattlesnake sunning itself near the trail in the desert.
Rattlesnakes use their tails to create a warning sound when they feel threatened.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viper - a type of venomous snake
  • Synonym: cobra - a large, venomous snake known for its hood

Etymology

From 'rattle' + 'snake', referring to its distinctive tail sound.
