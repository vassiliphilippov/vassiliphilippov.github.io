Tomistoma

1. NOUN

/ toʊˈmɪstəmə / (AmE), / təˈmɪstəmə / (BrE)
A genus of large freshwater crocodilians native to Southeast Asia, known as the false gharial.
genus of Malayan crocodiles

Examples

The Tomistoma is sometimes called the false gharial due to its long, narrow snout.
Researchers are studying Tomistoma populations in the wild to understand their habitat needs.
Unlike other crocodilians, Tomistoma is rarely seen outside Southeast Asian rivers.

Etymology

From Greek 'tomos' (a cutting, section) and 'stoma' (mouth), referring to the creature's snout shape.

2. NOUN

/ toʊˈmɪstoʊmə / (AmE), / təˈmɪstəmə / (BrE)
A genus of crocodiles native to Malaysia and surrounding regions, known for their long, narrow snouts.
genus of Malayan crocodiles

Examples

The Tomistoma is sometimes called the false gharial due to the shape of its snout.
Conservationists are working to protect the endangered Tomistoma in Southeast Asia.
A Tomistoma can grow to over five meters long in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crocodile - large aquatic reptile with a long snout

Etymology

From Greek tomos (a cut, section) + stoma (mouth).
