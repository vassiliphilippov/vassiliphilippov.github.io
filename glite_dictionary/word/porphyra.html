Porphyra

1. PROPN

/ pɔrˈfaɪrə / (AmE), / pɔːˈfʌɪrə / (BrE)
A genus of red algae, commonly known as laver, used in food and found in marine environments.
genus of edible red algae (laver)

Examples

Porphyra is widely harvested for making nori, a popular ingredient in Japanese cuisine.
Scientists study Porphyra to better understand its ecological role in coastal environments.
Dried Porphyra sheets can be found in many Asian grocery stores around the world.

Etymology

From Greek 'porphyra' (purple), referencing the color of some species.

2. PROPN

/ ˈpɔr.fɪ.rə / (AmE), / ˈpɔː.fɪ.rə / (BrE)
A genus of red algae comprising edible seaweeds, commonly known as laver, used especially in Asian cuisine.
genus of edible red algae (laver)

Examples

Dried Porphyra sheets are an essential ingredient in making sushi rolls.
Marine biologists often study Porphyra because of its economic and ecological importance.
In Wales, traditional laverbread is made from cooked Porphyra seaweed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laver - an edible seaweed harvested for food, especially in East Asia and Wales

Etymology

From Ancient Greek porphyra (purple), referring to the color of the algae.
