scrutinizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈskruːt̬n̩ˌaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈskruːtəˌnaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who examines or inspects something very carefully
person who examines very carefully

Examples

As a scrutinizer, she left no detail unnoticed in the financial report.
Every election, a scrutinizer is appointed to ensure the voting process is fair.
The museum relies on a skilled scrutinizer to authenticate rare artifacts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inspector - person who inspects or examines something, especially as a job
  • Synonym: overseer - a person who supervises others, especially workers
  • Synonym: to scrutinize - to examine or inspect closely and thoroughly

Etymology

From the verb "scrutinize" + -er, ultimately from Latin "scrutari" (to search) via French.
