emergent

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈmɝːdʒənt / (AmE), / ɪˈmɜːdʒənt / (BrE)
In the process of coming into being or becoming important.
coming into existence

Examples

The country is an emergent power in the technology industry.
Several emergent trends are shaping the future of education.
The emergent company quickly gained a reputation for innovation.

Synset Examples

an emergent republic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nascent - just coming into existence and beginning to display signs of future potential
  • Antonym: established - having been in existence for a long time and therefore recognized and generally accepted

Etymology

From Latin emergere (to arise, to come forth).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00003552-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈmɜrdʒənt / (AmE), / ɪˈmɜːdʒənt / (BrE)
(of a situation or event) occurring unexpectedly and requiring urgent action, especially in a medical or emergency context.
requiring urgent action; unexpected (esp. medical)
medical

Examples

The patient was taken to surgery for an emergent procedure after a sudden collapse.
An emergent situation in the ward required all doctors to respond quickly.
The hospital staff is trained to handle emergent cases efficiently and calmly.

Synset Examples

emergent repair of an aorta

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urgent - requiring immediate action or attention
  • Antonym: routine - regular and not urgent or unexpected

Etymology

From Latin 'emergentem', present participle of 'emergere' (to arise, come forth).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01147340-s
Synset POS: s
