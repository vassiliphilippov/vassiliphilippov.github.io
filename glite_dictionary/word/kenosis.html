kenosis

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / kɪˈnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
(in Christian theology) the act of emptying oneself of personal will and embracing God's will, especially as exemplified by Christ in his incarnation.
theological self-emptying of one's will

Examples

The doctrine of kenosis is central to certain interpretations of Christ's nature in Christian theology.
Through kenosis, believers are called to surrender their desires and follow a higher spiritual path.
Some theologians debate how kenosis influences the understanding of Jesus' humanity and divinity.

Etymology

From Greek kenōsis (emptying), from kenoun (to empty), from kenos (empty).
