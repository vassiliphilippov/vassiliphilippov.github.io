sickliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪk.li.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪk.li.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being frequently ill, weak, or in poor health, especially over a long period.
state of being often ill or weak

Examples

Despite her sickliness, she remained determined to finish her studies.
The child's sickliness worried his parents and required frequent visits to the doctor.
Years of poverty contributed to his sickliness and lack of energy.

Synset Examples

He did well in school, despite his sickliness and despite the fact that Mama had become increasingly agitated with him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frailty - state of being physically weak and delicate
  • Antonym: robustness - state of being strong and healthy

Etymology

From "sickly" (Middle English sikly, from Old English sēoclic) + -ness.
