phrenologist

1. NOUN

/ frəˈnɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / frəˈnɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a person who claims to determine character and mental abilities from the shape of the skull, especially according to the theory of phrenology
person who studies skull shapes for character
archaic

Examples

In the 19th century, a phrenologist might have measured your skull to draw conclusions about your personality.
The phrenologist insisted that lumps on the head revealed secrets about one's temperament.
Even today, some people are fascinated by the strange history of the phrenologist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phrenology - the study of skull shapes to determine character

Etymology

From phrenology + -ist, with phrenology from Greek *phren* (mind) + *-logia* (study).
