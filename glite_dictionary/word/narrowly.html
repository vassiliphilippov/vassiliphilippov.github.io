narrowly

1. ADV

/ ˈnæroʊli / (AmE), / ˈnærəli / (BrE)
In a manner that is limited, strict, or not allowing for exceptions.
in a limited or strict way

Examples

The new regulation will be interpreted narrowly to avoid unintended consequences.
She narrowly defined the scope of the project to ensure focus.
Their policy narrowly limits assistance to a small group of eligible people.

Synset Examples

he interprets the law narrowly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: broadly - in a broad or general way

Etymology

From 'narrow' + '-ly', denoting manner.
