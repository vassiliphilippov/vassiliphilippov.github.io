burg

1. NOUN

/ bɝɡ / (AmE), / bɜːɡ / (BrE)
A town or city, especially a small one, used chiefly in informal American English.
informal term for a town or city
informal american

Examples

This little burg gets quieter every winter.
He grew up in a sleepy burg far from the big cities.
Our burg finally got its own coffee shop this year.

Synset Examples

I've lived in this burg all my life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: town - a human settlement larger than a village but smaller than a city
  • Synonym: city - a large town or urban area

Etymology

From German 'Burg' meaning 'castle' or 'fortified town', adopted into American English slang.
