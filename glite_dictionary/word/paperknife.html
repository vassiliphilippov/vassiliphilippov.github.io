paperknife

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪpərˌnaɪf / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpəˌnaɪf / (BrE)
A thin, dull-edged knife used for opening envelopes or cutting uncut pages of books.
knife for opening envelopes or books

Examples

He used a paperknife to neatly open the sealed envelope.
The antique paperknife on her desk had an ornate ivory handle.
Before starting the new novel, she needed a paperknife to separate the uncut pages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: letter opener - a tool for opening envelopes

Etymology

Formed from 'paper' + 'knife', originally describing a tool for cutting paper.
