Sian

1. PROPN

/ siˈɑːn / (AmE), / siˈɑːn / (BrE)
an older English spelling for Xi'an, a city in central China, known as the capital of ancient Chinese dynasties
former English spelling for Xi'an, China

Examples

The ancient Silk Road began in Sian and stretched across Asia to the Mediterranean.
During our travels in China, we visited Sian to see the Terracotta Army.
Historians often refer to Sian when discussing the early capitals of the Chinese empire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Xi'an - modern spelling of Sian, capital of Shaanxi, China

Etymology

Former romanization of Chinese 西安 (Xi'an).
