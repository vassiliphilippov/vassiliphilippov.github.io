olympian

1. ADJ

/ əˈlɪmpiən / (AmE), / əˈlɪmpiən / (BrE)
extremely great in size, degree, or achievement; surpassing what is usual or ordinary
exceptionally great; extraordinary

Examples

She made an olympian effort to complete the project on time despite all obstacles.
His olympian achievements in science changed the course of history.
Facing the disaster, the volunteers showed olympian courage and determination.

Synset Examples

a night of exceeding darkness
an exceptional memory
olympian efforts to save the city from bankruptcy
the young Mozart's prodigious talents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exceptional - unusually excellent; much greater than usual
  • Synonym: prodigious - remarkably or impressively great in extent, size, or degree

Etymology

From Latin Olympianus, from Greek Olympios, relating to Mount Olympus, home of the gods.

2. ADJ

/ əˈlɪmpiən / (AmE), / əˈlɪmpiən / (BrE)
Majestic in manner or bearing; showing great detachment or superiority to ordinary concerns.
majestic, aloof; superior to ordinary concerns

Examples

Her olympian detachment during the heated debate made her stand out among her peers.
He remained olympian in the face of personal attacks, never stooping to petty arguments.
The artist's olympian beauty and poise captivated everyone in the gallery.

Synset Examples

his majestic presence
olympian detachment
olympian beauty and serene composure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: majestic - having or showing impressive beauty or dignity
  • Synonym: aloof - not friendly or forthcoming; cool and distant

Etymology

From Latin 'Olympius', from Greek 'Olympios', relating to Mount Olympus, home of the gods in Greek mythology.
