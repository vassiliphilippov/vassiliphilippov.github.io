see-through

1. ADJ

/ ˈsiˌθru / (AmE), / ˈsiːˌθruː / (BrE)
(of cloth or material) so thin that it allows light to pass through and things can be seen through it.
thin enough to be transparent

Examples

She wore a see-through blouse that revealed the outline of her camisole.
The curtains were so see-through that you could easily see outside.
He preferred see-through packaging so customers could view the product inside.

Synset Examples

a hat with a diaphanous veil
filmy wings of a moth
gauzy clouds of dandelion down
gossamer cobwebs
sheer silk stockings
transparent chiffon
vaporous silks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transparent - allowing light to pass through so that objects can be seen clearly
  • Antonym: opaque - not able to be seen through; not transparent
  • Degree Lower: translucent - allowing light, but not detailed shapes, to pass through
