Dhu al-Hijjah

1. NOUN

/ duː ɑlˈhɪdʒə / (AmE), / duː ælˈhɪdʒə / (BrE)
the twelfth and final month of the Islamic calendar, marked by the annual pilgrimage (hajj) to Mecca
twelfth month of the Islamic calendar; hajj season

Examples

Muslims from around the world travel to Mecca during Dhu al-Hijjah to perform the hajj.
The festival of Eid al-Adha falls in the month of Dhu al-Hijjah each year.
In the Islamic calendar, Dhu al-Hijjah is known for its religious significance and rituals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hajj - the Muslim pilgrimage to Mecca, performed in Dhu al-Hijjah

Etymology

From Arabic Ḏū al-Ḥijja, meaning 'possessor of the pilgrimage'.
