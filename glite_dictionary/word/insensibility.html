insensibility

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌsɛnsəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪnˌsɛnsɪˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
a lack of awareness, responsiveness, or concern for physical or emotional sensations; the state of being insensible.
lack of awareness or sensation

Examples

After the accident, he lay in a state of insensibility for several hours.
Her insensibility to criticism made her a resilient leader.
The prolonged insensibility to their suffering was both shocking and disappointing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insensitivity - lack of sensitivity to feelings or situations

Etymology

From Latin 'insensibilis' (incapable of feeling), via Old French.

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˌsɛnsəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪnˌsɛnsɪˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality or state of lacking emotion, compassion, or sensitivity; hardness of heart.
lack of emotion or compassion

Examples

Despite the tragedy, her insensibility to others' pain was shocking.
His insensibility made it difficult for him to form close relationships.
Some leaders are criticized for their apparent insensibility to public suffering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apathy - lack of interest, enthusiasm, or emotion
  • Synonym: heartlessness - lack of compassion or sympathy
  • Antonym: compassion - sympathetic pity and concern for the suffering of others

Etymology

From Latin insensibilis (not perceivable by the senses) via French insensibilité.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
