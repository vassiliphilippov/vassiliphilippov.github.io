Sterculia rupestris

1. NOUN

/ stɚˈkjuːliə ruːˈpɛstrɪs / (AmE), / stɜːˈkjuːlɪə ruːˈpɛstrɪs / (BrE)
a large tree native to Queensland, Australia, with cream-colored flowers blotched with red on the inside; sometimes classified within the genus Sterculia
tree species native to Queensland, Australia

Examples

The Sterculia rupestris is often called the Queensland bottle tree due to its distinctive trunk.
Botanists are studying how Sterculia rupestris adapts to arid environments in Australia.
A mature Sterculia rupestris can reach impressive heights, making it popular in large gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bottle tree - another common name for Sterculia rupestris, especially in Australia

Etymology

From Latin genus name Sterculia and species epithet rupestris, meaning 'rocky' or 'growing among rocks.'
