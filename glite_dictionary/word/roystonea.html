Roystonea

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɔɪˈstɔːniə / (AmE), / ˌrɔɪˈstəniə / (BrE)
A genus of tall feather palms native to the West Indies, known for their smooth trunks and ornamental use.
genus of West Indian feather palms

Examples

The Roystonea is commonly planted as an ornamental tree in tropical regions.
Many city streets in Havana are lined with majestic Roystonea palms.
Botanists have classified several species within the Roystonea genus.

Etymology

Named after Roy Stone, a U.S. Army engineer.
