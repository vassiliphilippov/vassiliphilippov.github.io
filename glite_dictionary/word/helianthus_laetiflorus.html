Helianthus laetiflorus

1. PROPN

/ hiːliˈænθəs laɪˈtɪflɔːrəs / (AmE), / hiːlɪˈænθəs liːˈtɪflərəs / (BrE)
A tall perennial sunflower with rough leaves and a few large flower heads, native to the central United States.
a tall American sunflower species

Examples

Many prairies in Kansas feature the vibrant Helianthus laetiflorus in late summer.
The Helianthus laetiflorus can grow over six feet tall and attracts butterflies.
Botanists studied the distribution of Helianthus laetiflorus across the Midwest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunflower - common name for the genus Helianthus

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Helianthus' (sunflower) and species name 'laetiflorus' (bearing cheerful flowers).
