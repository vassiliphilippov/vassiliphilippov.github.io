banking

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbæŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
The business of managing money at or with a bank, such as depositing, withdrawing, or borrowing funds.
managing money at or with a bank

Examples

She has worked in banking for over twenty years, specializing in loans and investments.
Online banking has made it easier for customers to manage their accounts from home.
Many students learn the basics of personal banking before opening their first account.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finance - management of large amounts of money, especially by governments or large companies
  • Synonym: banking institution - an institution for receiving, lending, exchanging, and safeguarding funds

Etymology

From 'bank' (financial institution) + '-ing' (suffix forming nouns from verbs).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbæŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
the business activity of operating a bank, including keeping money for savings and checking accounts, exchanging currency, and issuing loans and credit
the business of operating a bank

Examples

She has worked in banking for over twenty years, specializing in corporate finance.
Modern banking offers customers a range of services beyond just saving and borrowing money.
The rise of online banking has transformed the way people manage their finances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finance - management of large sums of money
  • Synonym: investment - the action or process of investing money for profit

Etymology

From 'bank' (financial institution) + -ing, dating from the late 17th century.
