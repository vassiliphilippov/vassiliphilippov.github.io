beta-carotene

1. NOUN

/ ˌbeɪtə kəˈroʊtin / (AmE), / ˌbiːtə kəˈrəʊtiːn / (BrE)
a naturally occurring pigment found in dark green and orange-yellow fruits and vegetables, which the body can convert into vitamin A
a pigment and vitamin A precursor in plants

Examples

Spinach and carrots are rich sources of beta-carotene.
Some nutritional supplements contain beta-carotene for its antioxidant properties.
A deficiency in beta-carotene can lead to vitamin A deficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carotene - a group of orange pigments found in plants
  • Degree Higher: vitamin A - a fat-soluble vitamin important for vision and growth

Etymology

From Greek beta (the second letter of the Greek alphabet) and carotene (from carrot, due to its discovery in carrots).
