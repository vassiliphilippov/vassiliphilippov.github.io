Satureia montana

1. NOUN

/ sæˈtʊər.i.ə mɒnˈteɪ.nə / (AmE), / sæˈtjʊə.ri.ə mɒnˈtɑː.nə / (BrE)
A perennial subshrub native to southern Europe, with pink or white flowers and leathery, thyme-flavored leaves, known as winter savory.
species of herb, winter savory

Examples

The chef garnished the roast with fresh Satureia montana for a hint of thyme flavor.
Gardeners in southern Europe often cultivate Satureia montana for both its flowers and culinary uses.
Dried Satureia montana is a common ingredient in traditional herb blends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winter savory - common English name for Satureia montana

Etymology

From Latin: Satureia (genus name) + montana ('of the mountains').
