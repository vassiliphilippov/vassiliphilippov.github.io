Gymnorhina

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪm.nɔːˈraɪ.nə / (AmE), / dʒɪm.nɔːˈraɪ.nə / (BrE)
A genus of Australian birds known as piping crows, sometimes placed in the family Laniidae, including the Australian magpie.
genus of Australian piping crows (Australian magpie)

Examples

The Gymnorhina is best known for its melodious calls across the Australian countryside.
Researchers observed several species within the genus Gymnorhina during their bird survey.
The Australian magpie, a member of the genus Gymnorhina, is highly territorial during breeding season.

Etymology

From Greek 'gymnos' (bare) + 'rhis, rhinos' (nose), referring to the bird's bare facial region.

2. NOUN

/ dʒɪm.nɔːrˈhaɪ.nə / (AmE), / dʒɪm.nɔːˈriː.nə / (BrE)
A genus of birds native to Australia, commonly known as piping crows or Australian magpies, sometimes classified in the family Laniidae.
Australian bird genus; piping crows

Examples

The unique call of the Gymnorhina is a familiar sound across much of Australia.
Ornithologists have long debated the correct classification of the Gymnorhina.
In suburban gardens, the Gymnorhina is often seen foraging for insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magpie - a type of bird in the crow family, including the Australian magpie

Etymology

From Greek 'gymnos' (bare) + 'rhis, rhinos' (nose), referring to the bird's bare nasal area.
