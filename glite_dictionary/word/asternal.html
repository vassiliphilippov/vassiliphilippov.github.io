asternal

1. ADJ

/ əˈstɜr.nəl / (AmE), / əˈstɜː.nəl / (BrE)
(of bones or ribs) not connected to the sternum or breastbone.
not connected to the sternum
medical

Examples

Some animals have asternal ribs that do not attach directly to the breastbone.
The asternal region can be found between the ends of the ribs and the sternum.
In anatomy class, we discussed how certain mammals possess asternal costal cartilage.

Synset Examples

asternal ribs

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sternal - relating to the sternum or breastbone

Etymology

From prefix 'a-' meaning 'not' and 'sternal', relating to the sternum.
