proselytism

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːsələˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈprɒsəlɪˌtɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice of attempting to convert people to another religion or belief.
attempt to convert others to one's faith or beliefs

Examples

Many religious leaders condemned the aggressive proselytism practiced by the newcomers.
The country has strict laws against proselytism and religious conversions.
He viewed proselytism as a threat to the cultural traditions of his community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to proselytize - to try to convert someone to a different religion or belief

Etymology

From Late Latin proselytismus, from Ancient Greek prosēlytismos (conversion).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.sə.lɪˌtɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˈprɒs.əl.ɪˌtɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the practice of seeking to convert others to one's religion, beliefs, or cause
the act of seeking converts to a belief

Examples

Some religious groups were accused of proselytism in the community.
The government passed laws to restrict proselytism in public schools.
She saw her efforts not as proselytism but as sharing her faith respectfully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evangelism - the enthusiastic preaching or spreading of the gospel
  • Antonym: apostasy - the abandonment or renunciation of a religious or political belief

Etymology

From Late Latin proselytismus, from Greek prosēlytismos (conversion).
