Kwakiutl

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑːkiˌuːtəl / (AmE), / ˈkwækɪˌjuːtəl / (BrE)
the Wakashan language traditionally spoken by the Kwakiutl people of British Columbia
Wakashan language of the Kwakiutl people

Examples

Linguists have documented the intricate grammar of Kwakiutl for over a century.
She studied Kwakiutl to better understand the cultural traditions of the Pacific Northwest.
Many elders are working to preserve the Kwakiutl language for future generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wakashan - a family of Native American languages, including Kwakiutl

Etymology

From the name of the Kwakiutl people, an Anglicized form of their own ethnonym Kwakwakaʼwakw.

2. NOUN

/ kwɑːkiˈjuːtəl / (AmE), / kwɑːkɪˈjuːtəl / (BrE)
a member of an Indigenous people of the Pacific Northwest Coast of Canada, living mainly around Queen Charlotte Sound and northern Vancouver Island
Indigenous people of Pacific Northwest Coast Canada

Examples

The Kwakiutl have a rich tradition of ceremonial art and totem pole carving.
Many Kwakiutl still live in their ancestral territories on northern Vancouver Island.
A museum exhibit featured pottery and masks made by the Kwakiutl.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wakashan - a group of Indigenous peoples; language family

Etymology

From the Kwakʼwala language, name for the people themselves.
