to invigorate

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈvɪɡəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɪɡəreɪt / (BrE)
to give strength, energy, or vitality to someone or something
give strength or energy

Examples

A brisk walk in the fresh air can invigorate both body and mind.
The new coach's enthusiasm invigorated the team before the big match.
Drinking cold water in the morning always invigorates me for the day ahead.

Synset Examples

Exercise is invigorating

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to energize - to give energy or vitality to
  • Antonym: to weaken - to make weaker or less strong

Etymology

From Latin 'vigor' (liveliness, force) + English suffix '-ate'.

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈvɪɡəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɪɡəreɪt / (BrE)
to make someone or something feel more lively, energetic, or refreshed
make more lively or energetic

Examples

A brisk walk in the crisp morning air can invigorate you for the rest of the day.
The new CEO's enthusiasm helped to invigorate the entire company.
Adding fresh paint and plants will invigorate this old room.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to energize - to give energy or vitality to
  • Synonym: to refresh - to make feel less tired or more lively
  • Antonym: to weaken - to make less strong or lively

Etymology

From Latin 'invigorare', from 'in-' (in) + 'vigorem' (liveliness, vigor).
