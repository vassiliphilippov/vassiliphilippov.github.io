Soave

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈɑːveɪ / (AmE), / səʊˈɑːveɪ / (BrE)
a dry white Italian wine from the Verona region, made primarily from Garganega grapes.
a dry white wine from Italy

Examples

He ordered a bottle of Soave to pair with the seafood pasta.
A chilled glass of Soave is perfect on a summer evening.
The wine menu features Soave among its selection of Italian whites.

Etymology

From Italian 'Soave,' a town in the Veneto region of Italy.
