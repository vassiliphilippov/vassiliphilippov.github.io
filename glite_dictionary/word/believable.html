believable

1. ADJ

/ bɪˈlivəbəl / (AmE), / bɪˈliːvəbl / (BrE)
Able to be accepted as true or real; plausible.
able to be believed; plausible

Examples

Her explanation was so detailed that it sounded completely believable.
The movie's plot was more believable than most science fiction stories I've seen.
He told a believable lie that almost fooled everyone.

Synset Examples

completely credible testimony
credible information

Related Terms

  • Synonym: credible - able to be believed; convincing
  • Antonym: unbelievable - not able to be believed; incredible

Etymology

From 'believe' + '-able', patterned after Old French 'croiable'.
