theatrically

1. ADV

/ θiˈætrɪkli / (AmE), / θɪˈætrɪkli / (BrE)
In an exaggerated or overly dramatic manner, as if performing in a theatre.
in a dramatic or stage-like manner

Examples

He sighed theatrically and threw his hands in the air for effect.
She entered the room theatrically, as if stepping onto a stage.
The children theatrically recited their lines during the school play.

Synset Examples

‘I cannot show my face at her house,’ he declared theatrically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dramatically - in a way that is exaggerated or theatrical

Etymology

From "theatrical" + -ly; ultimately from Greek theatron (theatre).

2. ADV

/ θiˈætrɪkli / (AmE), / θɪˈætrɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner that is exaggerated, dramatic, or characteristic of the theater.
in a dramatic or theatrical manner

Examples

She entered the room theatrically, drawing everyone's attention with her grand gestures.
He sighed theatrically to show his annoyance at the situation.
The actor theatrically removed his hat and bowed to the audience.

Synset Examples

theatrically dressed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dramatically - in a way that is exaggerated or intended to create an effect

Etymology

From 'theatrical' + '-ly'. 'Theatrical' from late Latin 'theatricalis', from Greek 'theatron' (theater).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
