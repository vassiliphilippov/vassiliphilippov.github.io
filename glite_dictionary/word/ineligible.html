ineligible

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈnɛlədʒəbl̩ / (AmE), / ɪˈnɛlɪdʒəbl̩ / (BrE)
not having the right or qualifications to participate, be chosen, or receive something
not qualified or not permitted

Examples

She was ineligible to receive the scholarship due to her grades.
Foreign nationals are ineligible for jury duty in this country.
Because he missed the deadline, he is ineligible to enter the contest.

Synset Examples

ineligible to vote
ineligible for retirement benefits

Related Terms

  • Antonym: eligible - having the right to do or obtain something; qualified

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (not) + 'eligibilis' (that may be chosen).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00856167-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈnɛlədʒəbl̩ / (AmE), / ɪˈnɛlɪdʒəbl̩ / (BrE)
not allowed to do or receive something by official rules or requirements
not permitted by rules or requirements

Examples

The player was declared ineligible for the match after breaking the rules.
Several applicants were found ineligible due to missing documents.
An ineligible pass receiver resulted in a penalty for the team.

Synset Examples

an ineligible pass receiver

Related Terms

  • Antonym: eligible - having the right to do or receive something; qualified

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (not) + 'eligibilis' (that may be chosen).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01407046-s
Synset POS: s
