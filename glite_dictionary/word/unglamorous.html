unglamorous

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɡlæmərəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡlæmərəs / (BrE)
not attractive, exciting, or interesting; dull or lacking in prestige
not attractive or exciting

Examples

He found the unglamorous task of data entry unexpectedly satisfying.
Working overnight shifts at the warehouse is an unglamorous but necessary job.
Despite the unglamorous nature of cleaning, it is essential to public health.

Synset Examples

an unglamorous job greasing engines

Related Terms

  • Antonym: glamorous - attractive, exciting, or prestigious

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + glamorous (from glamour, influenced by French 'gramaire' and Scots 'glamer').
