Harpia

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːr.pi.ə / (AmE), / ˈhɑː.pi.ə / (BrE)
A genus of large eagles, including the harpy eagle, native to Central and South America.
genus of large eagles

Examples

The Harpia is best known for its sole species, the powerful harpy eagle.
Researchers observed the breeding habits of Harpia in the Brazilian rainforest.
Members of the genus Harpia possess impressive talons and are apex predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harpy eagle - a large eagle belonging to the genus Harpia

Etymology

From Latin *harpyia*, from Ancient Greek *harpuia* (harpy).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑr.pi.ə / (AmE), / ˈhɑː.pi.ə / (BrE)
A genus of large eagles in the family Accipitridae, which includes the harpy eagle.
genus of large eagles

Examples

The Harpia genus includes one of the world's largest and most powerful eagles.
Researchers observed a Harpia specimen in the Amazon rainforest.
The harpy eagle, known scientifically as Harpia harpyja, is native to Central and South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harpy eagle - large powerful eagle of tropical South America, the sole species in this genus

Etymology

From Latin, based on Greek 'harpyia' (mythological creature).
