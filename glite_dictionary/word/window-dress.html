to window-dress

1. VERB

/ ˈwɪndoʊˌdrɛs / (AmE), / ˈwɪndəʊˌdrɛs / (BrE)
to make something appear more attractive or appealing than it really is, especially in order to deceive or mislead
make superficially attractive

Examples

The company tried to window-dress its financial reports to impress investors.
Politicians often window-dress their achievements before elections.
We shouldn't window-dress the facts; honesty is more important.

Synset Examples

The researcher tried to dress up the uninteresting data
Don't try to dress up the unpleasant truth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dress up - to make something seem more attractive or better than it is

Etymology

From 'window dressing', referring to arranging goods in a shop window to attract customers.
