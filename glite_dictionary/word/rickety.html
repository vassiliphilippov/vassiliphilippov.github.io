rickety

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɪk.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈrɪk.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
(of a person or part of the body) affected with or suffering from rickets, making the bones weak or deformed.
affected by or characteristic of rickets
medical

Examples

The boy's rickety legs made it difficult for him to walk without support.
Doctors noted several rickety features, including bowed legs and enlarged joints.
A rickety appearance in the limbs often indicates a vitamin D deficiency in children.

Synset Examples

rickety limbs and joints
a rachitic patient

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rachitic - affected with rickets

Etymology

From rickets (disease, origin uncertain) + -y.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02557086-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɪk.ɪ.ti / (AmE), / ˈrɪk.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
physically weak or lacking in strength or vitality; feeble or frail, especially as a result of age or illness.
physically weak or feeble

Examples

After his long illness, he felt too rickety to leave his bed unassisted.
The rickety old man struggled to walk without his cane.
She emerged from the hospital looking pale and rickety, having lost much of her strength.

Synset Examples

a feeble old woman
her body looked sapless

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feeble - lacking physical strength, especially as a result of age or illness

Etymology

Probably from 'rick', variant of 'wrick' (to twist), with -ety suffix indicating a state or quality.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02047640-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈrɪkɪti / (AmE), / ˈrɪkɪti / (BrE)
(of a structure or object) likely to collapse or shake because of weakness or poor construction.
unstable or shaky due to weakness

Examples

The old wooden bridge looked rickety and unsafe to cross.
She sat on a rickety bench that creaked every time she moved.
After years of use, the barn’s door was barely hanging on its rickety hinges.

Synset Examples

a rickety table
a wobbly chair with shaky legs
the ladder felt a little wobbly
the bridge still stands though one of the arches is wonky

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unstable - not firmly fixed; liable to give way or change position
  • Antonym: sturdy - strongly and solidly built

Etymology

From English dialect rickety (weak, feeble), perhaps from rickets (the disease), of unknown origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02300529-s
Synset POS: s
