Vitis labrusca

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪtɪs ləˈbrʌskə / (AmE), / ˈvaɪtɪs ləˈbrʌskə / (BrE)
A species of grape native to the northeastern United States, known as the origin of many cultivated varieties such as Concord grapes.
species of American grape

Examples

The juice from Vitis labrusca is commonly used to make Concord grape jelly.
Researchers are studying Vitis labrusca for its resistance to certain plant diseases.
Many American vineyards cultivate Vitis labrusca for its unique flavor profile.

Etymology

From Latin: 'vitis' meaning 'vine' and 'labrusca', meaning 'wild vine'.
