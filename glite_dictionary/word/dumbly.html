dumbly

1. ADV

/ ˈdʌmli / (AmE), / ˈdʌmli / (BrE)
In a stupid, unthinking, or insensitive manner.
in a stupid or insensitive way

Examples

He stared dumbly at the exam paper, unable to answer the first question.
She responded dumbly to the criticism, making no attempt to defend herself.
The child followed the instructions dumbly, not really understanding what to do.

Synset Examples

he had so rapaciously desired and so obtusely expected to find her alone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stupidly - in a stupid manner
  • Synonym: insensitively - without sensitivity or awareness
  • Antonym: thoughtfully - showing careful consideration

Etymology

From 'dumb' + '-ly', where 'dumb' originally meant 'unable to speak', but also came to mean 'stupid'.

2. ADV

/ ˈdʌmli / (AmE), / ˈdʌmli / (BrE)
In a way that shows an inability or unwillingness to speak; without saying anything.
without speaking; silently

Examples

He stared dumbly at the broken vase, unable to find words.
She nodded dumbly in agreement, not trusting herself to speak.
The children waited dumbly for instructions, their faces full of uncertainty.

Synset Examples

I nodded dumbly and he slit the envelope

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silently - in a quiet or noiseless manner
  • Antonym: vocally - with the use of one's voice

Etymology

From "dumb" + "-ly"; "dumb" meaning 'unable to speak'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00324586-r
Synset POS: r
