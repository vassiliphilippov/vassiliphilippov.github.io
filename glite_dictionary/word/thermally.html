thermally

1. ADV

/ ˈθɜrməli / (AmE), / ˈθɜːməli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to heat or thermal properties
in relation to heat or thermal properties

Examples

The material is thermally conductive, making it ideal for heat sinks.
The building is thermally insulated to reduce energy costs.
Some rocks expand and contract thermally over daily temperature cycles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thermal - relating to heat

Etymology

From 'thermal' + '-ly', ultimately from Greek thermē (heat).
