aide

1. NOUN

/ eɪd / (AmE), / eɪd / (BrE)
a person who assists another, especially in a professional or official capacity
assistant or helper, often in a professional role

Examples

The senator's aide helped organize the press conference.
She worked as a teacher's aide in the local elementary school.
His aide handed him the documents he needed for the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assistant - a person who helps someone, especially in an office or professional setting

Etymology

From French 'aide' (help, helper), from Old French, from Latin 'adiutare' (to help).

2. NOUN

/ eɪd / (AmE), / eɪd / (BrE)
an officer who acts as a military assistant to a more senior officer
military assistant to a senior officer

Examples

The general's aide handed him the confidential report before the meeting.
As an aide to the colonel, she often coordinated communications between units.
The young captain served as an aide during the overseas mission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assistant - a person who helps someone, typically in a job or office

Etymology

From French 'aide' (help, assistance), from Latin 'adiutare' (to help).
