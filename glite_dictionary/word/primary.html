primary

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪˌmɛri / (AmE), / ˈpraɪ.məri / (BrE)
one of the main flight feathers projecting along the outer edge of a bird's wing
main flight feather of a bird's wing

Examples

The eagle lost a primary during its last molt.
Scientists measured the length of each primary on the bird's wing.
A broken primary can affect a bird's ability to fly efficiently.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: secondary - a flight feather nearer to the body than the primary

2. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪˌmɛri / (AmE), / ˈprʌɪməri / (BrE)
(astronomy) A celestial body, especially a star, around which another object or objects revolve.
celestial body orbited by another

Examples

The Earth acts as the primary for the Moon, which orbits around it.
In our solar system, the Sun is the primary for all the planets.
Each planet is a primary to its natural satellites.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: secondary - a celestial body orbiting a primary

Etymology

From Latin primarius (of the first rank), from primus (first).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪˌmɛri / (AmE), / ˈprʌɪməri / (BrE)
the coil in an electrical circuit through which a changing current induces a current in a secondary coil
the main coil in a transformer

Examples

The voltage applied to the primary determines the operation of the entire transformer.
Engineers tested the primary to ensure it was generating the correct magnetic field.
If the primary is damaged, the transformer cannot function properly.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: secondary - the coil in which current is induced by the primary coil in a transformer
