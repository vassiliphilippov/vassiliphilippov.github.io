epizoan

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛpɪˈzoʊən / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈzəʊən / (BrE)
Relating to epizoa; living on the surface of another animal.
relating to organisms living on animals

Examples

The researcher studied several epizoan communities on marine turtles.
Some barnacles are considered epizoan organisms because they attach to whales.
The presence of epizoan algae can indicate changes in water quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epizoic - relating to or living on the surface of an animal

Etymology

From Greek epi- (upon) + zōion (animal).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛpəˈzoʊ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈzəʊ.ən / (BrE)
an organism that lives on the surface of another animal as an external parasite
external parasitic organism
medical

Examples

The tick is a common epizoan that feeds on the blood of mammals.
Some epizoans, such as lice and fleas, can transmit diseases to their hosts.
Marine biologists studied the barnacles as epizoans living on the whale's skin.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: endoparasite - a parasite that lives inside the host's body
  • Synonym: ectoparasite - a parasite that lives on the surface of its host

Etymology

From Greek epi- (upon) + zōion (animal).

Synset POS: n
