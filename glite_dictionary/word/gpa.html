GPA

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒi pi ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː piː ˈeɪ / (BrE)
an average that represents the overall academic performance of a student, calculated by dividing total grade points earned by the number of credits attempted
grade point average measure
american

Examples

Her GPA improved significantly after she started studying with a tutor.
A minimum GPA is required to apply for the scholarship.
He was proud to graduate with the highest GPA in his class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grade point average - academic average calculated from grades

Etymology

Acronym of 'grade point average.'
