Emydidae

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɪdɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ɛˈmɪdɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of turtles, including box turtles and water turtles, found mostly in the Americas and parts of Europe
family of turtles (box and water turtles)

Examples

The Emydidae family includes some of the most recognizable turtles in North America.
Many species in the Emydidae family are adapted to aquatic environments.
Researchers are studying the decline of Emydidae populations due to habitat loss.

Etymology

From modern Latin Emydidae, from Greek emys (freshwater turtle).

2. NOUN

/ ɛˈmɪdəˌdi / (AmE), / ɛˈmɪdɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of turtles that includes box turtles and various water turtles, commonly found in North America.
family of box and water turtles

Examples

Box turtles belong to the Emydidae family, along with many aquatic turtles.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Emydidae in North America.
The Emydidae are known for their distinctive domed shells and adaptability to varied habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turtle - a reptile with a hard shell that lives in water or land

Etymology

From New Latin *Emydidae*, based on the type genus *Emys*.
