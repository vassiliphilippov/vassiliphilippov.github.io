Jean-Philippe Rameau

1. PROPN

/ ʒɑ̃ fiˈlip raˈmoʊ / (AmE), / ʒɑ̃ fiˈliːp raˈmoʊ / (BrE)
(1683–1764) French composer and music theorist, renowned for his operas and for laying the foundation of modern harmony theory.
French composer and music theorist

Examples

The music of Jean-Philippe Rameau is celebrated for its intricate harmonies and innovative orchestration.
Many music students study the treatises of Jean-Philippe Rameau when learning about Baroque harmony.
A production of Jean-Philippe Rameau's opera was featured at the city's summer festival.
