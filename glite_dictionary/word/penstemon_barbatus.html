Penstemon barbatus

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛn.stə.mən bɑrˈbeɪ.təs / (AmE), / ˈpɛn.stɪ.mən bɑːˈbeɪ.təs / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States, known for its long clusters of scarlet flowers with yellow hairs on the lower lip.
plant with scarlet flowers native to southwestern US

Examples

The garden's centerpiece was a blooming Penstemon barbatus attracting dozens of hummingbirds.
Botanists study the adaptation of Penstemon barbatus to arid environments in the southwest.
I planted Penstemon barbatus along the walkway for a splash of vibrant color.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Penstemon' is a genus name, and 'barbatus' means bearded.
