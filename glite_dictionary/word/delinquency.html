delinquency

1. NOUN

/ dəˈlɪŋkwənsi / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪŋkwənsi / (BrE)
failure to pay a debt or financial obligation by its due date.
nonpayment of a debt when due

Examples

The homeowner's mortgage delinquency resulted in a warning from the bank.
High rates of credit card delinquency can indicate economic difficulties.
Any prolonged delinquency on your loan may affect your credit score.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: default - failure to fulfill a financial obligation, especially to repay a loan
  • Antonym: repayment - the act of paying back money that is owed

Etymology

From Latin delinquentia (fault, offence), from delinquere (to fail, be lacking).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈlɪŋkwənsi / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪŋkwənsi / (BrE)
A minor crime or antisocial act, especially one committed by a young person in violation of the law.
minor crime or antisocial act by a youth

Examples

The city is taking steps to reduce youth delinquency in the neighborhoods most affected by crime.
Concern over rising delinquency led to the introduction of after-school programs for teenagers.
His early delinquency was attributed to a lack of positive role models at home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crime - an act that is against the law
  • Synonym: offense - a breach of a law or rule; an illegal act
  • Antonym: obedience - compliance with laws or rules

Etymology

From Latin delinquentia (offence, fault), from delinquere (to fail, to offend).

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈlɪŋkwənsi / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪŋkwənsi / (BrE)
a tendency to neglect duties or responsibilities; negligent or careless behavior
neglectful or careless behavior

Examples

The student's delinquency in completing assignments concerned his parents and teachers.
A pattern of delinquency among the staff led to a decline in productivity.
Repeated delinquency at work resulted in a formal warning from management.

Synset Examples

he inherited his delinquency from his father
his derelictions were not really intended as crimes
his adolescent protest consisted of willful neglect of all his responsibilities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: negligence - failure to take proper care in doing something
  • Synonym: carelessness - lack of attention or concern
  • Antonym: diligence - careful and persistent effort or work

Etymology

From Latin delinquentia (offence, fault), from delinquere (to fail, to offend).
