Spilogale putorius

1. NOUN

/ ˌspaɪloʊˈɡeɪli pjuːˈtɔːriəs / (AmE), / ˌspɪləˈɡɑːli pjuːˈtɔːriəs / (BrE)
a small skunk species with a marbled black and white coat, native to the United States and Mexico
a small North American spotted skunk

Examples

The Spilogale putorius is noted for its unique pattern of white spots and stripes.
Due to habitat loss, populations of Spilogale putorius have declined in many regions.
Researchers studied the behavior of Spilogale putorius in grassland environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skunk - a small North American mammal known for its strong odor

Etymology

From Greek 'spilos' (spot) + 'gale' (weasel) and Latin 'putorius' (pertaining to odor).
