Retama

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈtɑːmə / (AmE), / rɪˈtɑːmə / (BrE)
A small genus of Mediterranean shrubs in the pea family, sometimes included in the genus Genista.
genus of Mediterranean shrubs

Examples

The Retama bushes bloom beautifully along the Mediterranean coast in spring.
Gardeners often plant Retama for its tolerance of dry, sandy soils.
Some botanists classify Retama as part of the genus Genista due to their similarities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Genista - a genus of flowering plants in the pea family, closely related to Retama

Etymology

From Spanish retama (broom shrub), ultimately from Latin retama.
