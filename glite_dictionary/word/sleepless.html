sleepless

1. ADJ

/ ˈsliːpləs / (AmE), / ˈsliːpləs / (BrE)
unable to sleep or having difficulty sleeping; marked by wakefulness during usual sleeping hours.
unable to sleep; awake at night

Examples

After drinking too much coffee, I spent a sleepless night staring at the ceiling.
The worries of the coming exam left her feeling sleepless and anxious.
He wrote poetry during his long, sleepless hours until morning.

Synset Examples

insomniac old people
insomniac nights
lay sleepless all night
twenty watchful, weary, tedious nights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wakeful - remaining awake; not sleeping
  • Synonym: restless - unable to rest, relax, or remain still
  • Antonym: rested - having had enough sleep or rest

Etymology

From Old English 'slǣpless' meaning 'without sleep'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsliːpləs / (AmE), / ˈsliːpləs / (BrE)
(literary) Always watchful or vigilant; never inattentive.
always watchful; vigilant

Examples

The sleepless guards remained alert throughout the long night.
With a sleepless sense of duty, the lighthouse keeper watched for ships in distress.
Her sleepless attention to her children's safety brought her peace of mind.

Synset Examples

to an eye like mine, a lidless watcher of the public weal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vigilant - keeping careful watch for possible danger or difficulties

Etymology

Formed from 'sleep' + '-less', meaning 'without sleep'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00093245-s
Synset POS: s
