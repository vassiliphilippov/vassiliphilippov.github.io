possessed

1. ADJ

/ pəˈzɛst / (AmE), / pəˈzɛst / (BrE)
(of a person) controlled or strongly influenced by a powerful force, such as an emotion, idea, or external power.
controlled by a strong force or emotion

Examples

She was possessed by an overwhelming desire to succeed, letting nothing stand in her way.
During the performance, he acted as if possessed by the spirit of the character.
The artist painted with a possessed intensity, lost in a world of his own emotions.

Synset Examples

by love possessed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obsessed - preoccupied or dominated by a particular thought or feeling

Etymology

Past participle of 'possess', from Latin 'possidēre' (to have, hold, own).

2. ADJ

/ pəˈzɛst / (AmE), / pəˈzɛst / (BrE)
(of a person) influenced or controlled by an evil spirit, demon, or supernatural force.
controlled by an evil spirit or demon

Examples

Some villagers believed the girl was possessed by a wicked spirit and in need of an exorcism.
During the ritual, he acted as though he were possessed, uttering strange words in a deep voice.
The horror film tells the story of a possessed doll that terrorizes a family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haunted - frequently visited or troubled by ghosts
  • Antonym: to exorcise - to drive out an evil spirit

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'possess', from Latin possidere (to have, hold, occupy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02873340-a
Synset POS: a
