axe

1. VERB

/ æks / (AmE), / æks / (BrE)
to chop or split something with an axe

Examples

She skillfully axed the firewood into neat, manageable pieces.
He spent the morning axing logs for the campfire.

2. NOUN

/ æks / (AmE), / æks / (BrE)
a tool with a heavy, sharp-edged metal head mounted at a right angle to a long handle, used for chopping wood and other materials
tool for chopping wood or materials

Examples

He swung the axe skillfully to split the firewood into manageable pieces.
The old axe hanging in the shed had belonged to his grandfather.
She used a sharp axe to chop down the dead tree in the garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hatchet - a small axe with a short handle, used with one hand
  • Synonym: adze - a cutting tool similar to an axe, but with the blade set at a right angle to the handle

Etymology

From Old English 'æx', related to Old High German 'Ahs(a)', Latin 'ascia'.

3. VERB

/ æks / (AmE), / æks / (BrE)
to abruptly terminate or cancel something, especially a job, project, or service
abruptly terminate or cancel

Examples

After years of declining sales, the company decided to axe several product lines.
When the budget was cut, the university had to axe some of its research projects.
The editor was suddenly axed after a controversial article was published.

Synset Examples

they will ax the research program

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismiss - to remove someone from their job; to let go
  • Synonym: fire - to dismiss from employment
  • Antonym: to retain - to keep or continue to have something

Etymology

From Old English 'æx', meaning axe; figurative use dates from the 20th century.
