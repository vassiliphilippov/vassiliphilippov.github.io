Ophiurida

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊ.fiˈjʊr.ɪ.də / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.fɪˈjʊə.rɪ.də / (BrE)
an order of echinoderms commonly known as brittle stars, characterized by their slender, flexible arms radiating from a central disc
an order of brittle stars

Examples

Many species within the Ophiurida group inhabit deep-sea environments around the world.
Marine biologists recently discovered a new member of the Ophiurida near the Great Barrier Reef.
The fossil record shows that Ophiurida have existed since the Early Ordovician period.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'ophis' (snake) and 'oura' (tail), referring to the appearance of the arms.

2. NOUN

/ ˌoʊ.fi.jʊˈraɪ.də / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.fɪ.jʊˈrɪ.də / (BrE)
A subclass of echinoderms comprising the brittle stars, characterized by their long, slender arms and central disc-shaped body.
a subclass of brittle stars

Examples

Recent studies have revealed the diversity within Ophiurida across deep ocean habitats.
Fossil records indicate that Ophiurida have existed since the early Jurassic period.
As members of Ophiurida, brittle stars display remarkable regenerative abilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brittle star - An echinoderm of the subclass Ophiurida with slender, flexible arms

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'ophis' (snake) and 'oura' (tail), referring to the slender arms.
