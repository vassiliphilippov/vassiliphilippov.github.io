deposit

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈpɑzɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈpɒzɪt / (BrE)
an amount of money paid as the first part of a total payment, with the rest due later
initial part-payment of total price

Examples

You need to pay a deposit to reserve the apartment.
A $200 deposit is required before we process your order.
She left a deposit at the hotel and paid the rest at checkout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: down payment - first installment paid when something is bought on credit

Etymology

From Latin *depositum* (something laid aside), via Old French *deposer* (to put down).
