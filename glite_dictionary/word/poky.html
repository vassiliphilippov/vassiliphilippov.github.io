poky

1. ADJ

/ ˈpoʊki / (AmE), / ˈpəʊki / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) slow or moving at a sluggish pace, especially so as to waste time.
moving or working slowly; sluggish

Examples

She was so poky getting ready that we missed the train.
This poky computer makes even simple tasks take forever.
The servers were a bit poky tonight, so dinner took much longer than usual.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slow - moving or acting at a low speed
  • Synonym: sluggish - lacking energy; moving slowly
  • Antonym: speedy - moving or happening quickly

Etymology

Originally from the word 'poke' (to prod, move slowly), likely of dialect origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊki / (AmE), / ˈpəʊki / (BrE)
(informal) A prison or jail, especially one that is small, unpleasant, or poorly maintained.
(informal) prison or jail
informal

Examples

After the fight, he spent the night in the poky for disturbing the peace.
She dreaded the thought of ending up in a dirty poky like that.
The suspects were held in the local poky until their court appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jail - a place for the confinement of people accused or convicted of a crime
  • Synonym: prison - an institution for the confinement of persons convicted of serious crimes

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly a variant of 'pogie', also slang for jail.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈpoʊ.ki / (AmE), / ˈpəʊ.ki / (BrE)
(of a place) small, remote, and insignificant; lacking importance or interest.
small and unimportant (of a place)

Examples

The town she grew up in was a poky little village in the countryside.
We stopped at a poky café that hardly had any customers.
He felt stuck in his poky hometown, longing for a more exciting life.

Synset Examples

a jerkwater college
passed a series of poky little one-horse towns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insignificant - not important or lacking significance
  • Antonym: important - of great significance or value

Etymology

Perhaps from English dialect poke (a small bag), originally meaning 'enclosed or confined'.
