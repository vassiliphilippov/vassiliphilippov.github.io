acetylcholine

1. NOUN

/ əˌsitəlˈkoʊlin / (AmE), / əˌsiːtilˈkəʊliːn / (BrE)
A neurotransmitter derived from choline, released at the ends of nerve fibers in the somatic and parasympathetic nervous systems, where it transmits nerve impulses.
neurotransmitter in nerves and muscles
medical

Examples

The neurotransmitter acetylcholine plays a critical role in muscle contraction.
Alzheimer’s disease is associated with a deficiency of acetylcholine in the brain.
Many nerve agents interfere with the action of acetylcholine, causing paralysis.

Etymology

From acetyl + choline, reflecting its chemical composition.
