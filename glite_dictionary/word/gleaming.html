gleaming

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡliːmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡliːmɪŋ / (BrE)
a bright or shining appearance produced by reflected light
a reflected bright or shining appearance

Examples

A faint gleaming rose from the silver candlesticks in the corner.
The gleaming of polished marble was visible even at dusk.
He noticed a curious gleaming in her eyes as she told the story.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gleam - a flash or beam of light
Source: WordNet

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡlimɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡliːmɪŋ / (BrE)
shining brightly with a steady, often subdued, light; polished or glossy in appearance.
brightly shining or polished

Examples

The sun reflected off the gleaming surfaces of the skyscrapers.
Her gleaming white teeth shone as she smiled.
The antique car's gleaming chrome caught everyone's eye at the show.

Synset Examples

from the plane we saw the city below agleam with lights
the gleaming brass on the altar
Nereids beneath the nitid moon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glossy - shiny and smooth; having a surface that reflects light
  • Antonym: dull - not shiny; lacking brightness or gloss

Etymology

Present participle of 'gleam,' from Middle English *glemen* (to shine).
