oriel

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔriəl / (AmE), / ˈɔːriəl / (BrE)
a projecting bay window that is supported by brackets or corbels and extends from the wall of a building
bay window projecting from a wall

Examples

The old library features an oriel overlooking the garden below.
Renovators preserved the original oriel to maintain the building's historic charm.
She admired the sunset through the oriel in the upstairs sitting room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bay window - a window space projecting outward from the main walls of a building

Etymology

From Old French oriol (gallery, porch), of unknown origin.
