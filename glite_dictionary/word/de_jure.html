de jure

1. ADJ

/ deɪ ˈdʒʊri / (AmE), / di ˈdʒʊəreɪ / (BrE)
existing or holding a position according to law or by right, as opposed to in practice (de facto).
according to law; by right
legal

Examples

The country had a de jure government, but the military held power in reality.
Slavery was abolished de jure in the 19th century, but discrimination persisted.
He is the de jure owner of the property, though others reside there.

Synset Examples

de jure recognition of the new government

Related Terms

  • Antonym: de facto - existing in fact, whether legally recognized or not

Etymology

From Latin 'de jure' (by law).

2. ADV

/ deɪ ˈdʒʊri / (AmE), / deɪ ˈdʒʊəri / (BrE)
According to law; by right; legally recognized, regardless of whether the situation exists in fact.
by law; legally recognized
legal

Examples

Although he was the de jure owner of the company, his partner managed all its operations.
Racial segregation was abolished de jure, but remained in practice for many years.
Their marriage was not recognized de jure until new laws were passed.

Synset Examples

we are lawfully wedded now

Related Terms

  • Antonym: de facto - in fact; in reality, even if not legally recognized

Etymology

From Latin 'de jure' meaning 'from the law' or 'by right'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00253289-r
Synset POS: r
