haggard

1. ADJ

/ ˈhæɡɚd / (AmE), / ˈhæɡəd / (BrE)
looking pale and exhausted, especially because of illness, worry, or lack of food
looking thin and worn; gaunt

Examples

After weeks of worry, his face appeared haggard and drawn.
The refugees arrived looking haggard after their long journey.
She gave him a haggard smile, showing the toll the ordeal had taken on her.

Synset Examples

a nightmare population of gaunt men and skeletal boys
eyes were haggard and cavernous
small pinched faces
kept life in his wasted frame only by grim concentration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaunt - very thin and bony, especially from suffering, hunger, or age
  • Synonym: emaciated - abnormally thin or weak, especially because of illness or lack of food

Etymology

Probably from Old French *hagard* (wild, wild-looking).

2. ADJ

/ ˈhæɡərd / (AmE), / ˈhæɡəd / (BrE)
looking tired, pale, and thin because of worry, suffering, or overwork
looking exhausted from worry or hardship

Examples

After weeks of overtime at the hospital, she looked haggard and pale.
His haggard features revealed the toll the long journey had taken on him.
The haggard man at the door asked for food, his face marked by exhaustion.

Synset Examples

looking careworn as she bent over her mending
her face was drawn and haggard from sleeplessness
that raddled but still noble face
shocked to see the worn look of his handsome young face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drawn - looking thin and tired, often due to illness or worry
  • Antonym: fresh - looking healthy, rested, and energetic

Etymology

Originally, from Old French 'hagard' (wild, untamed), later used for people appearing wild or gaunt from hardship.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
