uncorrelated

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˈkɔr.əˌleɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈkɒr.ə.leɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of two or more variables, facts, or events) not related to or affecting each other; showing no tendency to vary together.
not related; not varying together

Examples

The two sets of results were found to be uncorrelated in multiple analyses.
Noise and signal in this measurement appear to be uncorrelated, which simplifies the data interpretation.
Her interests in art and science are largely uncorrelated, but equally strong.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: correlated - having a mutual relationship or connection; tending to vary together

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + correlated, from Latin correlatus, past participle of correlare (to relate together).
