edentulous

1. ADJ

/ iˈdɛntʃələs / (AmE), / iˈdɛntjʊləs / (BrE)
having lost all natural teeth; toothless, especially as a result of aging or disease
lacking natural teeth
medical

Examples

The dentist recommended dentures for the edentulous patient.
Researchers studied the eating habits of edentulous individuals in nursing homes.
An edentulous jaw often requires special care during oral surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toothless - lacking teeth

Etymology

From Latin 'edentulus', from 'e-' (without) + 'dens' (tooth) + '-ulus' (diminutive suffix).
