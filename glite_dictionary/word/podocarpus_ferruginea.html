Podocarpus ferruginea

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊdəˈkɑːrpəs ˌfɛrəˈdʒɪniə / (AmE), / ˌpɒdəʊˈkɑːpəs ˌfɛrəˈdʒɪniə / (BrE)
a New Zealand coniferous tree whose dark wood is used for lumber and interior carpentry
New Zealand coniferous tree used for lumber

Examples

The dense wood of the Podocarpus ferruginea is prized for making furniture in New Zealand.
Botanists studied the distribution of Podocarpus ferruginea in native forests across the South Island.
Many traditional homes feature paneling crafted from Podocarpus ferruginea due to its rich color and durability.

Etymology

From the genus name Podocarpus (Greek 'pous', foot, and 'karpos', fruit), and the species epithet 'ferruginea' (Latin, rust-coloured).
