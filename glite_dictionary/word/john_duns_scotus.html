John Duns Scotus

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn dʌnz ˈskoʊtəs / (AmE), / dʒɒn dʌnz ˈskəʊtəs / (BrE)
(c. 1265–1308) A Scottish theologian and philosopher influential in medieval scholastic thought, known for his complex arguments on metaphysics and theology.
Scottish medieval theologian and philosopher

Examples

John Duns Scotus was renowned for his intricate philosophical arguments during the Middle Ages.
Many scholars still study the writings of John Duns Scotus today.
The teachings of John Duns Scotus greatly influenced medieval theology and philosophy.
