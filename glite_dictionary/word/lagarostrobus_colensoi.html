Lagarostrobus colensoi

1. NOUN

/ ləˌgæroʊˈstroʊbəs koʊˈlɛnˌsoʊ.aɪ / (AmE), / ləˌɡærəˈstrəʊbəs kəˈlɛnsəʊ.aɪ / (BrE)
A tall timber tree native to New Zealand, valued for its shiny white wood; also known as New Zealand silver pine.
New Zealand timber tree with white wood

Examples

The Lagarostrobus colensoi is highly prized for its durable and attractive timber.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitats of Lagarostrobus colensoi in New Zealand's forests.
Furniture made from Lagarostrobus colensoi wood is sought after for its smooth finish and pale color.

Etymology

From Greek 'lagaros' (slack) and 'strobos' (cone); species epithet honoring W. Colenso, a New Zealand botanist.
