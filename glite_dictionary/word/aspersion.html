aspersion

1. NOUN

/ əˈspɝːʒən / (AmE), / əˈspɜːʃən / (BrE)
the act of sprinkling water, especially as part of a baptismal ceremony in some Christian traditions (rare)
act of sprinkling water in baptism (rare)

Examples

The priest performed the aspersion as part of the infant's baptism.
During the ceremony, aspersion replaced the more common practice of immersion.
Some Christian denominations prefer aspersion to full-body baptism.

Etymology

From Latin 'aspersio' (sprinkling), from 'aspergere' (to sprinkle).

2. NOUN

/ əˈspɝːʒən / (AmE), / əˈspɜːʃən / (BrE)
an insult or damaging remark directed at someone's character or reputation
insult to someone's character

Examples

She cast an aspersion on his honesty during the heated debate.
It is unfair to throw aspersions at someone without evidence.
Politicians often endure aspersions from their opponents during campaigns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slander - spoken false statement damaging to a person's reputation
  • Synonym: calumny - the making of false and defamatory statements to damage someone's reputation
  • Antonym: praise - expression of approval; opposite of insult or defamation

Etymology

From Latin 'aspersio' (a sprinkling), figuratively extended to mean slander or damaging remark.

3. NOUN

/ əˈspɝːʒən / (AmE), / əˈspɜːʃən / (BrE)
an insulting or damaging remark about someone or something; a slur or criticism intended to harm reputation
disparaging or damaging remark

Examples

He cast an aspersion on her honesty during the meeting.
Throwing aspersions without evidence can seriously damage someone's reputation.
Despite the aspersions against him, he maintained his composure and dignity.

Synset Examples

in the 19th century any reference to female sexuality was considered a vile aspersion
it is difficult for a woman to understand a man's sensitivity to any slur on his virility

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slur - an insulting or damaging remark about someone
  • Synonym: calumny - false and defamatory statement
  • Antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration

Etymology

From Latin aspergere (to sprinkle), later used figuratively in English for casting damaging remarks.
