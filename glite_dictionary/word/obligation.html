obligation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːbləˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒblɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
a feeling of indebtedness or duty towards someone, often because of a service or favor received
sense of duty due to being indebted

Examples

He felt a strong obligation to help her after all she had done for him.
You shouldn't feel any obligation to return the favor, but it's appreciated if you do.
Their kindness created an obligation in her to support them in times of need.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duty - something that one is required to do by moral or legal obligation
  • Synonym: debt - a feeling of owing something, especially gratitude or a favor, to someone
  • Synonym: gratitude - the feeling of being thankful for a service or favor

Etymology

From Latin obligatio (a binding), from obligare (to bind).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɑːblɪˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒblɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
A legal agreement that requires a person or organization to pay money or do something, with a penalty for failing to comply.
legal agreement requiring action or payment
legal

Examples

The company is under a legal obligation to provide safe working conditions.
She failed to meet her payment obligation by the agreed deadline.
If you break the terms of the obligation, you may have to pay a penalty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contract - a formal agreement between two or more parties
  • Synonym: liability - the state of being responsible for something, especially by law
  • Antonym: right - a legal entitlement or claim

Etymology

From Latin obligatio (a binding), from obligare (to bind).

Examples

Social obligation often guides people's behavior even when there is no legal requirement.

5. NOUN

/ ˌɑːbləˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒblɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
A written promise, especially a formal contract or bond, by which a person or organization undertakes to pay a specified sum of money, often as a debt.
written promise to repay a debt

Examples

The company issued an obligation to investors, promising repayment with interest in five years.
A government obligation typically guarantees regular payments to the bondholder.
He purchased a municipal obligation as a way to invest his savings safely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bond - a certificate issued by a government or corporation promising to repay borrowed money with interest
  • Synonym: debt - something that is owed or that one is bound to pay to or perform for another

Etymology

From Latin obligātiō (a binding, pledge), from obligare (to bind).
