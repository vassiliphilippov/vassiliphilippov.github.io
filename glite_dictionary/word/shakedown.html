shakedown

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪkˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪk.daʊn / (BrE)
a period or process of testing and adjustment to improve the functioning or efficiency of something, especially when new
initial adjustment period or process

Examples

The company went through a shakedown phase before becoming fully operational.
Engineers performed a shakedown of the new equipment to ensure all systems worked properly.
After launch, the software will undergo a shakedown to identify any issues and optimize performance.

Synset Examples

the new industry's economic shakedown

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'shake down', originally meaning 'to force down by shaking'; later extended to 'trial run' or 'test period'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07384522-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃeɪkˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪk.daʊn / (BrE)
(of a test, event, or period) intended to assess the performance of a new system or piece of equipment under real operating conditions and to familiarize users with it.
intended as a test under real conditions

Examples

The engineers planned a shakedown voyage to identify any problems with the new ship.
Before the factory opened, a shakedown period ensured the machines operated smoothly.
The company conducted a shakedown test of its new software before public release.

Synset Examples

a shakedown cruise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trial - a test of something new to assess its performance

Etymology

From the verb 'shakedown', meaning to test or make sure something works as intended.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03156214-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪkˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪkdaʊn / (BrE)
a thorough search of a person or place, especially by authorities such as police or prison staff
thorough search or inspection

Examples

The prison guards conducted a shakedown of all the cells after the incident.
A surprise shakedown at the border revealed a stash of illegal goods.
After reports of theft, the school administration did a shakedown of the students’ lockers.

Synset Examples

a shakedown by the police uncovered the drugs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: search - an act of looking for something carefully
  • Synonym: pat-down - a search where a person's body is felt for hidden items

Etymology

From the verb 'shake down', meaning to shake in order to dislodge something; later extended metaphorically to mean an intense search.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00995873-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪkˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪk.daʊn / (BrE)
an act of obtaining money or favors from someone by using threats or blackmail
extortion, especially by blackmail

Examples

The police launched an investigation into the shakedown at several local businesses.
He was the victim of a shakedown after receiving anonymous threats demanding money.
Prosecutors alleged that the payments were part of an organized shakedown scheme.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extortion - the practice of obtaining something, especially money, through force or threats
  • Synonym: blackmail - the act of demanding money from someone in return for not revealing compromising information

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'shake down,' meaning to extort money, used in criminal slang from the early 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00786588-n
Synset POS: n
