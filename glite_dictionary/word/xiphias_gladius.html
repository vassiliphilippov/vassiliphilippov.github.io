Xiphias gladius

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɪfi.əs ˈɡlædi.əs / (AmE), / ˈzɪfi.as ˈɡlædi.as / (BrE)
the scientific name for the swordfish, a large marine fish with a long, sword-like upper jaw, found in warm and temperate oceans worldwide
scientific name for swordfish

Examples

Xiphias gladius is prized by anglers for its speed and power.
The diet of Xiphias gladius includes smaller fish and squid.
Researchers study Xiphias gladius to understand how it warms its brain and eyes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swordfish - a large marine fish with a long, flat bill, known for its speed and agility

Etymology

From Greek 'xiphias' (swordfish), from 'xiphos' (sword), and Latin 'gladius' (sword).
