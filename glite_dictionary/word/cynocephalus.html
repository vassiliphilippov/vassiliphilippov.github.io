Cynocephalus

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪnoʊˈsɛfələs / (AmE), / ˌsɪnəʊˈsɛfələs / (BrE)
a genus of mammals in the family Cynocephalidae, comprising the flying lemurs or colugos
genus of flying lemurs (colugos)

Examples

The genus Cynocephalus is represented by just two species of flying lemur in Southeast Asia.
Biologists debated the classification of Cynocephalus after new fossil discoveries.
Members of Cynocephalus are sometimes called colugos or flying lemurs.

Etymology

From Greek 'kynokephalos' (dog-headed), from 'kyon' (dog) + 'kephalē' (head).

2. NOUN

/ saɪˌnoʊsɪˈfæləs / (AmE), / sɪˌnəʊsɪˈfæləs / (BrE)
A genus of mammals in the family Cynocephalidae, comprising the colugos or flying lemurs.
genus of mammals (colugos)

Examples

The Cynocephalus genus includes species known for their ability to glide between trees.
Research on Cynocephalus has expanded our knowledge of mammalian evolution.
The Philippine colugo, classified as Cynocephalus volans, is native to Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colugo - arboreal gliding mammal; member of genus Cynocephalus

Etymology

From Greek kynokephalos, meaning 'dog-headed', from kyon (dog) + kephalē (head).
