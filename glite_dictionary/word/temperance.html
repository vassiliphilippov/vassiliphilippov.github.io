temperance

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpərəns / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərəns / (BrE)
the practice of avoiding excess; moderation or self-restraint, especially in eating or drinking
moderation; avoidance of excess

Examples

He admired her temperance in both speech and behavior.
The movement promoted temperance as a way to improve society.
Exercising temperance at the dinner table helped him stay healthy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moderation - the avoidance of excess or extremes
  • Antonym: intemperance - lack of moderation or restraint

Etymology

From Latin temperantia (moderation), from temperare (to moderate).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmp(ə)rəns / (AmE), / ˈtɛmp(ə)rəns / (BrE)
moderation and self-restraint, especially in avoiding excess or overindulgence
moderation; self-restraint

Examples

Her temperance in eating and drinking contributed to her good health.
The movement promoting temperance gained significant momentum in the nineteenth century.
He showed admirable temperance in the face of temptation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moderation - avoiding extremes or excesses
  • Antonym: intemperance - lack of moderation or restraint, especially with alcohol or appetites

Etymology

From Latin 'temperantia' (moderation) via Old French 'temperaunce'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpɚəns / (AmE), / ˈtɛmp(ə)rəns / (BrE)
the act of moderating or counterbalancing something to achieve a desired condition or quality
the act of moderating or counterbalancing

Examples

The temperance of heat and cold was crucial to the success of the experiment.
Metalworkers rely on temperance to create the right balance in their alloys.
Careful temperance of ingredients led to a remarkably smooth texture in the dish.

Etymology

From Latin *temperantia* (moderation), from *temperare* (to moderate, blend).
