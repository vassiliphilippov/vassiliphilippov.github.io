odiousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊdiəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈəʊdiəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality or fact of being extremely unpleasant or repulsive; the state of being hateful or detestable
state of being hateful or repulsive

Examples

The sheer odiousness of the crime shocked the entire community.
She recoiled at the odiousness of his remarks during the meeting.
Historians have written about the odiousness of certain outdated social practices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offensiveness - the quality of being likely to upset or hurt someone
  • Antonym: agreeableness - the quality of being pleasant or likable

Etymology

From Latin odiosus (hateful) + -ness.
