family Coraciidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli kəˌreɪsiˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli kɒˌræsɪˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of brightly colored, medium-sized birds known as rollers, found in warm regions of the Old World.
bird family: rollers

Examples

The family Coraciidae includes several species renowned for their vibrant plumage.
Many ornithologists travel to Africa to observe members of the family Coraciidae in their natural habitat.
Unlike other bird groups, the family Coraciidae is known for spectacular courtship flights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roller - a brightly colored bird of the family Coraciidae

Etymology

From Latin 'Coracias', the type genus, and taxonomic suffix '-idae' for animal families.
