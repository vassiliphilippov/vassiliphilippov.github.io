knowingness

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊɪŋnəs / (AmE), / ˈnəʊɪŋnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having knowledge or being aware; consciousness or awareness of something
the state of being knowledgeable or aware

Examples

Her knowingness about the situation helped everyone stay calm.
A certain knowingness flashed in his eyes when he heard the news.
The novel is written with a subtle knowingness that appeals to experienced readers.

Synset Examples

he had no awareness of his mistakes
his sudden consciousness of the problem he faced
their intelligence and general knowingness was impressive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awareness - the state or ability to perceive, to feel, or to be conscious of events
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or awareness

Etymology

From 'knowing' (from Old English 'cnāwan', to know) + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting a state or condition).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊɪŋnəs / (AmE), / ˈnəʊɪŋnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being shrewd, perceptive, or aware, especially as shown by possessing knowledge or understanding
shrewd or perceptive awareness

Examples

Her knowingness during the negotiations gave her a clear advantage over the competitors.
There was a subtle knowingness in his smile, as if he understood more than he let on.
The film's script was praised for its wit and knowingness about modern relationships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrewdness - the quality of having sharp powers of judgment
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or awareness

Etymology

From 'knowing' + '-ness', forming a noun denoting a state or quality.

Synset POS: n
