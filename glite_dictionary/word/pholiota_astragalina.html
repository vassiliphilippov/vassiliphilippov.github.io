Pholiota astragalina

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈlaɪ.ə tə æs.trəˈɡæ.lɪ.nə / (AmE), / fəˈlɪ.ə tə æs.trəˈɡæ.lɪ.nə / (BrE)
A species of fungus with a smooth orange cap, yellow gills, and a pale yellow stalk, found in woodlands.
species of orange-capped fungus

Examples

We spotted a cluster of Pholiota astragalina growing on a fallen log in the forest.
The smooth orange cap of Pholiota astragalina makes it quite distinctive among woodland fungi.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Pholiota astragalina throughout the autumn months.

Etymology

From genus name Pholiota (Greek pholis, 'scale') and species astragalina, referring to the color resemblance to the mineral astragaline.
