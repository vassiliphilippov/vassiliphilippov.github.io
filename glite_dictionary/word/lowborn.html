lowborn

1. ADJ

/ ˈloʊˌbɔrn / (AmE), / ˈləʊˌbɔːn / (BrE)
of humble or low social birth or origin
of humble birth or origin

Examples

Despite her lowborn status, she quickly won the respect of the court.
In that era, lowborn individuals rarely had access to education.
He tried to conceal his lowborn origins from the aristocrats.

Synset Examples

a topsy-turvy society of lowborn rich and blue-blooded poor

Related Terms

  • Antonym: highborn - of noble or aristocratic birth
  • Antonym: noble - having high social rank by birth or title

Etymology

Formed from 'low' (referring to low rank) + 'born' (from birth).
