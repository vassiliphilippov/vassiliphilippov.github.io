Sidalcea

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈdælsiə / (AmE), / sɪˈdalsɪə / (BrE)
a genus of showy flowering plants native to western North America, with palmate leaves and racemes of variously colored flowers
genus of flowering plants native to western North America

Examples

Many gardeners value Sidalcea for its attractive, long-lasting blooms in summer.
The wildflower field was dotted with pink and purple Sidalcea blossoms.
She decided to plant several varieties of Sidalcea to attract pollinators to her yard.

Etymology

From the botanical Latin combining Sidal- (from Sida, a related genus) and -cea, from Alcea (hollyhock genus).

2. NOUN

/ saɪˈdæl.si.ə / (AmE), / saɪˈdal.sɪ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants native to western North America, known for palmate leaves and racemose clusters of variously colored flowers.
genus of North American flowering plants

Examples

The Sidalcea species flourish in the foothills of California each spring.
Gardeners often choose Sidalcea for its attractive, colorful flower spikes.
Recent fieldwork identified three new Sidalcea populations in Oregon.

Etymology

From the combination of the genera Sida and Alcea, as coined in scientific Latin.
