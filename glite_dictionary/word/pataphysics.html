pataphysics

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæt̬əˈfɪzɪks / (AmE), / ˌpætəˈfɪzɪks / (BrE)
a philosophy or branch of thought concerned with what lies beyond metaphysics, often dealing with imaginary solutions and the science of exceptions, originally conceived as a mock-science by the French writer Alfred Jarry.
the study of what lies beyond metaphysics; science of imaginary solutions

Examples

The artist's work is deeply influenced by pataphysics, exploring imaginary solutions and absurd realities.
During the lecture, she explained how pataphysics differs from traditional metaphysics or science.
Some critics view pataphysics as a playful satire rather than a serious field of study.

Synset Examples

Of all the French cultural exports over the last 150 years or so, ‘pataphysics’ — the science of imaginary solutions and the laws governing exceptions — has proven to be one of the most durable.

Etymology

From French 'pataphysique', coined by Alfred Jarry, as a parody of 'metaphysics'.
