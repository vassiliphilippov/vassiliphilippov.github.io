Gallinula chloropus

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈlɪn(j)ələ ˈklɔrəˌpʊs / (AmE), / ɡalɪˈnjuːlə ˈklɔːrəpəs / (BrE)
A species of water bird, also known as the common moorhen, with a mainly blackish plumage, found in ponds and lakes.
species of water bird; common moorhen

Examples

The Gallinula chloropus is commonly seen darting among the reeds of freshwater lakes.
Scientists observed several Gallinula chloropus nesting along the riverbank during the spring survey.
Unlike the coot, the Gallinula chloropus has a bright red facial shield above its bill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moorhen - another name for Gallinula chloropus, a type of water bird
  • Degree Lower: coot - a similar species of water bird

Etymology

From Latin: 'gallinula' meaning 'little hen', and 'chloropus' meaning 'green foot'.
