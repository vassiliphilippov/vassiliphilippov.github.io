patriciate

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtrɪʃiət / (AmE), / pəˈtrɪʃɪət / (BrE)
The collective body or class of patricians, especially in ancient Rome or in other societies with hereditary aristocracy.
the patrician class or its members

Examples

The Venetian patriciate wielded significant political power in the city's government.
During the Renaissance, the Florentine patriciate was a prominent supporter of the arts.
He traced his lineage back to the Roman patriciate, a source of family pride.

Synset Examples

the patriciate regarded patronage of the arts as a moral and social duty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aristocracy - the highest class in certain societies, especially those holding hereditary titles
  • Antonym: plebeian - a commoner in ancient Rome

Etymology

From Latin *patriciatus* (the rank or office of a patrician), from *patricius* (patrician).
