ganglion

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæŋɡliən / (AmE), / ˈɡaŋɡlɪən / (BrE)
An encapsulated mass of nerve cell bodies located outside the central nervous system.
cluster of nerve cell bodies
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that a ganglion is a cluster of nerve cells outside the brain or spinal cord.
Damage to a ganglion can disrupt the transmission of nerve signals in the body.
Scientists studied the structure of the dorsal root ganglion in laboratory animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nerve - a bundle of fibers that transmits electrical impulses between brain or spinal cord and body

Etymology

From Greek 'ganglion' (tumor, knot).
