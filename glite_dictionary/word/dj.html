DJ

1. NOUN

/ diː ˈdʒeɪ / (AmE), / diː ˈdʒeɪ / (BrE)
a person whose job is to select, announce, and play recorded popular music, especially at clubs, parties, or on the radio
person who plays music at events or on radio

Examples

The DJ kept the crowd dancing all night with a great mix of songs.
She became a popular DJ on the local radio station last year.
At the wedding, a professional DJ played all the couple's favorite tracks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disc jockey - a person who plays recorded music for an audience

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'disc jockey', originally from the era of vinyl records.

2. VERB

/ diː ˈdʒeɪ / (AmE), / diː ˈdʒeɪ / (BrE)
to act as a disc jockey by selecting, mixing, and playing recorded music for an audience, often at events or on radio.
perform as a disc jockey

Examples

He loves to DJ at local clubs on Saturday nights.
She started DJing at college parties before moving to larger venues.
Several famous musicians have DJed at that festival over the years.

Synset Examples

3. NOUN

/ diː dʒeɪ / (AmE), / diː dʒeɪ / (BrE)
a person who selects, announces, and plays recorded popular music, especially at a club or on the radio
person who plays recorded music

Examples

The DJ played my favorite song at the party, and everyone started dancing.
Our local radio DJ has a great sense of humor and introduces new music every week.
She worked as a DJ in several nightclubs before producing her own albums.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disc jockey - a person who plays and introduces popular recorded music

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'disc jockey'.
