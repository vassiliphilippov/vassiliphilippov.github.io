Cephalotaxaceae

1. NOUN

/ sɛfələˌtæksəˈsiː / (AmE), / sɛfəʊləˌtæksɪˈsiː / (BrE)
a family of evergreen coniferous shrubs and small trees, including the genus Cephalotaxus, native to East Asia.
a family of coniferous plants
medical

Examples

The Cephalotaxaceae include several species used in traditional medicine.
Botanists have reclassified some members of the Cephalotaxaceae based on genetic studies.
Only a few genera are recognized within the Cephalotaxaceae family.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Cephalotaxus ('head-yew').

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛfəloʊˌtæksəˈsiː / (AmE), / ˌsɛfələʊˌtæksəˈsiː / (BrE)
a family of coniferous plants, commonly known as the plum yew family, native to East Asia and the Himalayas
a family of coniferous plants (plum yew family)

Examples

The Cephalotaxaceae are primarily found in East Asia and the Himalayan region.
Scientists have studied the unique seeds produced by members of the Cephalotaxaceae.
The Cephalotaxaceae family includes slow-growing evergreen shrubs and small trees.

Etymology

From the genus name Cephalotaxus (from Greek 'kephalē' meaning head and 'taxis' meaning arrangement) + standard botanical family ending -aceae.
