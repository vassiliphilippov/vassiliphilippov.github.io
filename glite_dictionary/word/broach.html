broach

1. NOUN

/ broʊtʃ / (AmE), / brəʊtʃ / (BrE)
a decorative pin, typically worn by women, that is attached to clothing for ornamentation
decorative pin worn on clothing

Examples

She pinned a sparkling broach to her jacket before leaving for the gala.
The antique broach was passed down from her grandmother.
He bought a beautiful silver broach for his mother's birthday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brooch - an ornamental pin, often spelled as 'brooch'

Etymology

From Old French *broche* (long needle, spit); originally referred to a pointed instrument.

2. VERB

/ broʊtʃ / (AmE), / brəʊtʃ / (BrE)
to begin to discuss or mention a subject, especially one that may be sensitive or difficult
bring up a subject for discussion

Examples

He was nervous to broach the subject of moving to a new city with his parents.
During the meeting, Sarah decided to broach the idea of flexible working hours.
It can be awkward to broach difficult topics with close friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raise - to bring up for consideration or discussion
  • Synonym: to introduce - to bring a topic into discussion for the first time

Etymology

From Old French *brocher* (to pierce), originally referring to opening a cask, later extended metaphorically to opening a subject.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
