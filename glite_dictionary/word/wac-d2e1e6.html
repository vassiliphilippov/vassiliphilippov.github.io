Wac

1. NOUN

/ wæk / (AmE), / wæk / (BrE)
A member of the Women's Army Corps, the women's branch of the United States Army, especially during World War II.
member of the Women's Army Corps (US Army, WWII)
american

Examples

During World War II, my grandmother served as a proud Wac.
The Wacs played a crucial role in supporting the soldiers overseas.
She wore her Wac uniform to the Veterans Day parade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: WAVES - A member of the Women Accepted for Volunteer Emergency Service (US Navy, WWII)
  • Synonym: soldier - A person who serves in an army

Etymology

Acronym from Women's Army Corps, formed during World War II.
