Njord

1. PROPN

/ ˈnjɔrd / (AmE), / ˈnjɔːd / (BrE)
(in Norse mythology) the chief of the Vanir; god of the sea, winds, and prosperity, and the father of Frey and Freya.
Norse god of sea, winds, prosperity

Examples

In Norse mythology, Njord is honored as the god of the sea and winds.
The fishermen prayed to Njord for safe passage and abundant catches.
As the father of Frey and Freya, Njord played a central role among the Vanir gods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Frey - Norse god of fertility and sunlight, son of Njord
  • Synonym: Freya - Norse goddess of love and beauty, daughter of Njord

Etymology

From Old Norse Njǫrðr, related to the Proto-Germanic *Nerþuz, goddess of fertility and peace.
