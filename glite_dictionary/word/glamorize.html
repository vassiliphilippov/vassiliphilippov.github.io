to glamorize

1. VERB

/ ˈɡlæməˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈɡlaməraɪz / (BrE)
to make something or someone appear more glamorous or attractive than they really are
make glamorous or attractive

Examples

The movie glamorizes the lives of criminals, presenting them as exciting and stylish.
She tried to glamorize the job to make it more appealing to applicants.
The photoshoot glamorized a simple kitchen, turning it into a dreamy space.

Synset Examples

This new wallpaper really glamorizes the living room!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embellish - to make something more attractive by adding details
  • Synonym: to romanticize - to make something seem more attractive or ideal than it really is
  • Antonym: to downplay - to make something seem less important or attractive

Etymology

From 'glamour' + '-ize', meaning to make glamorous.

2. VERB

/ ˈɡlæm.əˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈɡlæm.ə.raɪz / (BrE)
to make something appear more attractive, exciting, or appealing than it really is, often in a romanticized or exaggerated way
make appear more attractive or exciting

Examples

Many movies tend to glamorize the lives of criminals, ignoring the harsh realities.
He didn't want to glamorize war, preferring to show its tragic consequences.
The article glamorized the fast-paced lifestyle but failed to mention its drawbacks.

Synset Examples

Don't romanticize this uninteresting and hard work!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to romanticize - to make something seem more romantic or appealing than it really is
  • Antonym: to downplay - to make something seem less important or attractive

Etymology

From 'glamor' (variant of 'glamour') + '-ize'. 'Glamour' comes from Scots, originally meaning a magic spell, later meaning charm or allure.
