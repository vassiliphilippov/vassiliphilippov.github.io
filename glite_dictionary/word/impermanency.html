impermanency

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɝː.mə.nən.si / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜː.mə.nən.si / (BrE)
The quality or state of not lasting forever; the property of being temporary or transitory.
the quality of not lasting forever

Examples

The impermanency of life is a central idea in many philosophies.
They spoke about the impermanency of fame and fortune in the entertainment industry.
This old photograph serves as a reminder of the impermanency of our memories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impermanence - the state or fact of lasting only for a limited period of time
  • Antonym: permanency - the state or quality of lasting or remaining unchanged

Etymology

From Latin 'im-' (not) + 'permanens' (lasting).
