briar-patch

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪər ˌpætʃ / (AmE), / ˈbraɪə ˌpatʃ / (BrE)
a difficult or troublesome situation, often tangled or confusing and hard to escape from
troublesome or confusing situation

Examples

The new manager inherited a briar-patch of conflicting policies and unclear procedures.
After years of poor investments, they found themselves in a financial briar-patch.
Negotiating the contract became a briar-patch of legal and technical issues.

Synset Examples

She managed to escape the briar-patch of bad decisions.
The project turned into a briar-patch of complications.
He felt trapped in a briar-patch of confusion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predicament - a difficult, unpleasant, or embarrassing situation
  • Synonym: quagmire - a difficult or hazardous situation
  • Synonym: mess - a situation that is confused or full of problems

Etymology

From the literal meaning of 'briar patch' (a dense, tangled area of thorny plants), popularized in American folklore such as the Br'er Rabbit stories.
