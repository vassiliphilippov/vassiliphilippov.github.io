juggernaut

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌɡərˌnɔt / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌɡənɑːt / (BrE)
(historical) A large, crude idol of the Hindu god Krishna, formerly carried in processions at Puri, India.
crude idol of Krishna (historical)

Examples

The immense juggernaut was paraded through the streets during the annual festival.
Many early travelers wrote about the huge juggernaut and the crowds it attracted.
The wood carvings on the juggernaut depicted various scenes from Hindu mythology.

Etymology

From Hindi *Jagannāth* (lord of the world), a form of Krishna worshipped at Puri.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌɡɚˌnɔt / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌɡənɔːt / (BrE)
A massive and unstoppable force or institution that crushes everything in its path.
an overwhelming, crushing force

Examples

The new tech company quickly became a juggernaut in the industry, eclipsing all competitors.
Critics warn that the project is a bureaucratic juggernaut that is impossible to stop once it gains momentum.
The movie was a box office juggernaut, dominating ticket sales for months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steamroller - force or institution that crushes opposition

Etymology

From Hindi 'Jagannāth', a title of the Hindu god Krishna, whose idol was reputedly drawn on a giant cart under which devotees sometimes threw themselves.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌɡərˌnɔt / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌɡənɔːt / (BrE)
A powerful, overwhelming force or institution that cannot be stopped.
an unstoppable force or institution

Examples

The new smartphone launch quickly became a juggernaut in the tech industry.
No one could stand in the way of the political juggernaut sweeping the nation.
The film franchise grew into a financial juggernaut unmatched by any other studio.

Synset Examples

The company's new product became a juggernaut in sales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: force - a strength or energy as an attribute of movement or action
  • Synonym: powerhouse - a person or thing of great energy, strength, or power
  • Antonym: pushover - something easily defeated or overcome

Etymology

From Hindi 'Jagannāth', title of a form of the Hindu god Vishnu, and the name of a massive chariot reportedly crushing devotees during ritual processions.
