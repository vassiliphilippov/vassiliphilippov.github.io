commonwealth

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmənˌwɛlθ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmənˌwɛlθ / (BrE)
A political system in which the supreme power lies with the citizens, who elect representatives to govern on their behalf; a republic.
a political system ruled by the people

Examples

In history class, we learned how the Roman commonwealth gave citizens the power to elect leaders.
Many nations today strive for a commonwealth where the people participate in government.
The philosopher argued that a commonwealth best protects individual rights and freedoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: republic - a political system with elected representatives and no monarchy

Etymology

From Middle English 'commoun welthe', meaning 'common well-being' or 'public welfare.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.mənˌwɛlθ / (AmE), / ˈkɒ.mənˌwɛlθ / (BrE)
(in the US) the official title of certain states (Massachusetts, Pennsylvania, Virginia, Kentucky) and territories (such as Puerto Rico), having no legal distinction from other states or territories but reflecting historical tradition.
official title of some US states/territories
american

Examples

Kentucky is officially known as the Commonwealth of Kentucky.
Puerto Rico is an unincorporated territory of the United States with the status of commonwealth.
Virginia uses the term commonwealth in its full official name, the Commonwealth of Virginia.

Etymology

From Middle English 'commenwelthe', from common + wealth, meaning public welfare; political usage adopted in the US in 18th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɑm.ənˌwɛlθ / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ənˌwɛlθ / (BrE)
an association of autonomous states or countries that are united by a shared allegiance but are not subordinate to a central authority or to each other, especially referring to the Commonwealth of Nations
association of autonomous states

Examples

After gaining independence, several countries chose to remain part of the commonwealth for economic and cultural cooperation.
The principles of equality among member states are central to the commonwealth structure.
They attended the annual commonwealth meeting to discuss trade and human rights issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confederation - an organization of states united for a common purpose
  • Synonym: federation - a group of states with a central government but independence in internal affairs

Etymology

From Middle English 'common welthe', meaning common well-being or public welfare.
