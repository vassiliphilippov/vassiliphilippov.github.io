inextricable

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈɛk.strɪ.kə.bəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈɛk.strɪ.kə.bəl / (BrE)
impossible to disentangle, separate, or escape from; not permitting extrication.
impossible to disentangle or escape from

Examples

The cables were twisted together in an inextricable mess after the move.
Her life became inextricable from his once they married.
There is an inextricable link between history and culture in this region.

Synset Examples

an inextricable knot
inextricable unity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inseparable - unable to be separated or treated separately
  • Antonym: extricable - able to be disentangled or freed

Etymology

From Latin inextricabilis, from in- (not) + extricabilis (able to be disentangled).
