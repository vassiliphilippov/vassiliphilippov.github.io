Crepis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɛpɪs / (AmE), / ˈkriːpɪs / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the daisy family, commonly known as hawk's-beard, native to the Northern Hemisphere.
genus of plants (hawk's-beard)

Examples

The Crepis species can often be found growing wild in meadows across Europe.
Several varieties of Crepis are cultivated for their yellow, dandelion-like flowers.
Botanists examined the unique seed structure of Crepis during their field study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawk's-beard - Common name for plants of the genus Crepis

Etymology

From Greek krēpis (shoe or base), referring to the plant's form.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɛpɪs / (AmE), / ˈkriːpɪs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the daisy family (Asteraceae), known as hawk's-beard, widely distributed in the Northern Hemisphere.
genus of flowering plants (hawk's-beard)

Examples

The genus Crepis is commonly known as hawk's-beard due to the shape of its flower.
Botanists have identified over 200 species within the Crepis genus.
Many members of Crepis thrive in meadows and open woodlands in temperate regions.

Etymology

From Greek *krēpis* (boot, shoe), referencing the form of the fruit.
