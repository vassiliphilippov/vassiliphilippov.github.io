carbonyl

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑr.bə.nɪl / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.bə.nɪl / (BrE)
relating to or containing the carbonyl group, which consists of a carbon atom double-bonded to an oxygen atom.
relating to the carbonyl group in chemistry
medical

Examples

The carbonyl compound drew immediate interest from the research team.
Aldehydes and ketones are known for their carbonyl functionality.
Chemists often test for carbonyl groups to distinguish between different types of organic molecules.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: carboxyl - relating to or containing the carboxyl group

Etymology

From French carbone (carbon) + -yl (chemical suffix indicating radical or group).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːr.bəˌnil / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.bə.nɪl / (BrE)
a chemical compound in which a metal is bonded to carbon monoxide groups; often used to refer to such complexes in inorganic chemistry
metal compound bonded with carbon monoxide
medical

Examples

Nickel forms a carbonyl compound used in refining processes.
The toxicity of some metal carbonyls makes them hazardous to handle without precautions.
Iron pentacarbonyl is a well-known example of a metal carbonyl.

Etymology

From carbon + -yl, with -yl from French -yle (from Greek hylē, meaning matter or substance), used in chemical nomenclature.
