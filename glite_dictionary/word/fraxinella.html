fraxinella

1. NOUN

/ frækˈsɪnələ / (AmE), / frækˈsɪnələ / (BrE)
A Eurasian perennial herb (Dictamnus albus) with white or pink flowers that emit a flammable vapor in hot weather.
Eurasian herb with flammable vapor

Examples

The fraxinella in our garden releases a faint lemon scent on hot days.
When touched with a match, the vapor from the fraxinella can briefly ignite without harming the plant.
Botanists are fascinated by the flammable properties of fraxinella flowers.

Etymology

From Latin 'fraxinella', diminutive of 'fraxinus' (ash tree), referring to the resemblance of its leaves to those of an ash.
