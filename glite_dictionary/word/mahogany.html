mahogany

1. NOUN

/ məˈhɑɡəni / (AmE), / məˈhɒɡəni / (BrE)
A strong, reddish-brown wood from several tropical trees, especially of the genus Swietenia, valued for use in high-quality furniture and cabinetry.
reddish-brown hardwood used in furniture

Examples

The desk was crafted from solid mahogany and polished to a high shine.
Antique dealers often prize furniture made of mahogany for its durability and rich color.
The ship's cabin was lined with mahogany panels, adding a touch of elegance.

Etymology

From Spanish 'mahogany' (madera de caoba), from Taíno 'mahogani', referring to the tree.

2. NOUN

/ məˈhɑɡəni / (AmE), / məˈhɒɡəni / (BrE)
a deep reddish-brown color, resembling the wood of the mahogany tree
a deep reddish-brown color

Examples

She chose a mahogany paint for the living room walls to add warmth to the space.
The table had a rich mahogany finish that complemented the vintage decor.
His hair had a natural mahogany hue in the sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chestnut - a medium reddish-brown color
  • Synonym: auburn - a reddish-brown color, especially of hair

From the name of the mahogany tree, originally from Spanish 'mahogany', possibly of Caribbean origin.

3. NOUN

/ məˈhɑɡəni / (AmE), / məˈhɒɡəni / (BrE)
a tropical hardwood tree of the genus Swietenia or its hard, reddish-brown wood, valued for furniture and fine woodworking
tropical hardwood tree or its wood

Examples

The dining table was made from solid mahogany and gleamed in the sunlight.
Carpenters prize mahogany for its durability and reddish color.
The antique desk features intricate carvings in mahogany wood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teak - a tropical hardwood tree and its durable wood
  • Synonym: oak - a hardwood tree commonly used in making furniture

Etymology

From Spanish 'mahogany', likely from Taíno 'mahagani'.
