plotter

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɑːtər / (AmE), / ˈplɒtə / (BrE)
a clerk whose job is to record or mark data on a chart, especially in fields such as navigation or meteorology
a clerk marking data on a chart

Examples

The plotter updated the weather chart every hour with new data.
Before computers, a plotter played an essential role in ship navigation.
As a plotter, she carefully recorded each position on the map.

Etymology

From 'plot' (to mark on a chart) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈplɑːtər / (AmE), / ˈplɒtə / (BrE)
a person who secretly plans a harmful or illegal act, especially as part of a conspiracy
person involved in a conspiracy

Examples

The police discovered that he was the main plotter behind the attempted coup.
As soon as the meeting ended, the plotter began to contact his accomplices.
The authorities arrested several suspected plotters in connection with the assassination plot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conspirator - person who is part of a conspiracy
  • Synonym: accomplice - person who helps another in a crime
  • Antonym: victim - person harmed by a crime or conspiracy

Etymology

From 'plot' (to plan secretly), + -er (agent noun suffix).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈplɑːt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈplɒtə / (BrE)
a computer-controlled device that draws graphs, diagrams, or pictures on paper, typically used in engineering or design applications
computerized drawing device

Examples

The engineer used a plotter to produce detailed building plans.
Our office upgraded to a new plotter that prints architectural drawings much faster.
Before digital screens, a plotter was essential for creating large technical diagrams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: printer - a device for printing text or pictures on paper

4. NOUN

/ ˈplɑːtər / (AmE), / ˈplɒtə / (BrE)
a person who secretly makes plans, especially to achieve something unlawful or harmful
a person who secretly plans (often something bad)

Examples

The detective suspected that the plotter was still hiding within the organization.
Historians uncovered letters revealing that she was a plotter in the failed rebellion.
Every plotter eventually makes a mistake that exposes their plan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conspirator - a person who takes part in a conspiracy
  • Synonym: schemer - someone who makes secret plans, often for their own advantage
  • Antonym: victim - person who is harmed by a plot or scheme

Etymology

From 'plot' (to devise a secret plan) + '-er' (agent suffix), attested since the late 16th century.
