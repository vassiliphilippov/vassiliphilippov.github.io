artefactual

1. ADJ

/ ˌɑːrtəˈfæktʃuəl / (AmE), / ˌɑːtɪˈfæktʃuəl / (BrE)
relating to or resulting from artifacts, especially as opposed to something naturally occurring; often used to describe things that arise from human activity or intervention rather than by natural processes.
relating to artifacts or artificial origin

Examples

The archaeologists debated whether the marks on the bones were artefactual or naturally caused.
Some studies have found that the results are artefactual rather than reflecting a true biological effect.
The museum display focused on the artefactual remains left by ancient cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artifactual - relating to an artifact or artificial origin (alternate spelling, especially American)

Etymology

From 'artifact' + '-ual', based on Latin 'arte factum' (something made by skill).
