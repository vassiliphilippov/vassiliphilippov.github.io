Uintatherium

1. NOUN

/ juːˌɪn.təˈθɪr.i.əm / (AmE), / juːˌɪn.təˈθɪə.ri.əm / (BrE)
an extinct genus of large, herbivorous prehistoric mammals resembling elephants, found in Eocene deposits, especially in Wyoming.
extinct genus of large prehistoric mammals

Examples

Fossils of the Uintatherium have been discovered in Eocene strata in Wyoming.
The large, horned skull of Uintatherium fascinated paleontologists in the nineteenth century.
In museums, reconstructions of Uintatherium help illustrate the diversity of prehistoric mammals.

Etymology

From Uinta (mountain range in Utah and Wyoming) + Greek therion (beast).

2. NOUN

/ juˌaɪn.təˈθɪr.i.əm / (AmE), / juːˌaɪn.təˈθɪə.ri.əm / (BrE)
a genus of extinct large herbivorous mammals resembling elephants, native to Eocene-era Wyoming, and the type genus of the family Uintatheriidae
genus of extinct large Eocene mammals

Examples

Fossils of Uintatherium have been discovered in the Eocene deposits of Wyoming.
Scientists believe Uintatherium used its large skull outgrowths for display or defense.
The museum's exhibit features a reconstructed skeleton of a Uintatherium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Uintatheriidae - family of extinct, elephant-like herbivorous mammals

Etymology

From Uinta Mountains (Utah, USA) + Ancient Greek therion (beast).
