anabiotic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.i.baɪˈɑː.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.i.baɪˈɒ.tɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or denoting a state of suspended animation or dormancy, especially as seen in certain organisms under extreme conditions.
relating to a suspended or dormant state

Examples

Tardigrades can survive extreme conditions by entering an anabiotic state.
Research on anabiotic organisms may lead to advances in cryopreservation.
The species remains anabiotic during harsh environmental changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anabiosis - a temporary state of suspended animation in organisms

Etymology

From Greek anabiosis (revival), based on ana- (up, again) and bios (life).
