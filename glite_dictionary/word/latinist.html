Latinist

1. NOUN

/ ˈlætənɪst / (AmE), / ˈlætɪnɪst / (BrE)
A scholar or expert in the Latin language and literature.
scholar of the Latin language

Examples

She is a renowned Latinist who has translated many ancient texts.
The university is seeking a Latinist to teach its advanced language courses.
As a Latinist, he contributed to several scholarly journals on Roman literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classicist - a scholar of classical languages and literature

Etymology

From Latin 'Latinus' (Latin), with '-ist' denoting someone who practices or is concerned with something.
