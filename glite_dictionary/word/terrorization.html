terrorization

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtɛrəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of causing someone to feel intense fear or terror
the act of causing fear or terror

Examples

The terrorization of the villagers forced many to flee their homes.
Acts of terrorization are condemned by international law.
The constant terrorization made it impossible for the group to relax.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intimidation - the act of frightening or threatening someone
  • Antonym: comforting - the act of making someone feel less afraid or anxious

Etymology

From 'terrorize' + '-ation', modeled after Latin-based noun formations.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtɛrəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of deliberately causing fear or terror, especially for political purposes
the act of causing fear or terror

Examples

The terrorization of the local community led many to flee their homes.
International laws condemn the terrorization of civilians during armed conflict.
She spoke about her experiences of terrorization at the hands of extremist groups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrorism - the unlawful use of violence and intimidation, especially against civilians, in the pursuit of political aims

Etymology

From 'terrorize' + '-ation', formed on the pattern of other nouns in '-ation'.
