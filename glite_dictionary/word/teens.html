teens

1. NOUN

/ tiːnz / (AmE), / tiːnz / (BrE)
the period in a person's life between the ages of 13 and 19
period of life from 13 to 19

Examples

She learned to play the guitar in her teens and joined a band shortly after.
During their teens, many people experience significant changes both physically and emotionally.
He lived abroad for most of his teens and returned home at twenty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adolescence - the period in human development after childhood and before adulthood

2. NOUN

/ tinz / (AmE), / tiːnz / (BrE)
the numbers from thirteen to nineteen, each ending with '-teen'
numbers 13 to 19

Examples

Children often find it hard to read numbers in the teens when first learning mathematics.
The numbers in the teens are between twelve and twenty.
She wrote all the teens on the board: thirteen, fourteen, fifteen, and so on.

Etymology

Formed from the suffix '-teen', derived from Old English 'tēne', used in the numbers thirteen to nineteen.
