Leopoldville

1. PROPN

/ liˈoʊpəldˌvɪl / (AmE), / liˈəʊpɒldˌvɪl / (BrE)
the former name of Kinshasa, the capital city of the Democratic Republic of the Congo, located on the Congo River across from Brazzaville
former name for Kinshasa, DRC

Examples

The city of Leopoldville was renamed Kinshasa in 1966.
During colonial times, Leopoldville was a major center of administration in the Congo.
Many historical documents refer to the capital as Leopoldville rather than Kinshasa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kinshasa - the current name for the capital of the Democratic Republic of the Congo

Etymology

Named after King Leopold II of Belgium, under whose rule the Congo Free State was established.
