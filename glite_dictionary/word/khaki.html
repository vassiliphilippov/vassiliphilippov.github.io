khaki

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæki / (AmE), / ˈkɑːki / (BrE)
A sturdy, twilled fabric of yellowish-brown color, especially used in military uniforms.
a sturdy yellowish-brown cloth

Examples

The soldiers wore khaki uniforms during the parade.
His trousers were made of durable khaki fabric.
She bought a new khaki jacket for hiking trips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: olive drab - a dull olive-green color often used for military uniforms
  • Degree Higher: camouflage - fabric or clothing designed to blend into the environment

Etymology

From Urdu *khākī* (dust-colored), from Persian *khāk* (dust).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkæki / (AmE), / ˈkɑːki / (BrE)
of a yellowish brown color, typically used to describe fabric or clothing
yellowish brown color

Examples

She wore a khaki jacket during the hiking trip.
The uniforms were all the same dull khaki color.
A khaki shade suits you better than bright colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tan - light yellowish brown color
  • Degree Lower: olive - a grayish-green color

Etymology

From Urdu *khākī* (dust-colored), from Persian *khāk* (dust).
