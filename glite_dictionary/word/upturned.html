upturned

1. ADJ

/ ˈʌpˌtɜrnd / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌtɜːnd / (BrE)
turned so that the bottom is facing upwards; upside-down or inverted.
having the bottom facing upwards

Examples

She found an upturned chair in the middle of the room.
The child used an upturned bucket as a drum.
During the storm, several boats were left upturned on the shore.

Synset Examples

an overturned car
the upset pitcher of milk
sat on an upturned bucket

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overturned - turned over so the bottom is now on top
  • Antonym: upright - in a vertical position, not overturned
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌpˈtɝnd / (AmE), / ˌʌpˈtɜːnd / (BrE)
(of an object, especially a nose) having the end turned upward.
having the end turned upward (esp. nose)

Examples

She had an upturned nose that gave her a playful look.
The sculpture featured an upturned beak, adding a whimsical touch.
His upturned nose was the first thing people noticed about him.

Synset Examples

a retrousse nose
a small upturned nose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retroussé - (of the nose) turned up at the tip

Synset POS: s
