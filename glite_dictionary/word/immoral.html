immoral

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈmɔrəl / (AmE), / ɪˈmɒrəl / (BrE)
Not conforming to accepted standards of morality; not ethical or morally right.
not conforming to moral principles

Examples

Many people consider lying to be immoral, regardless of the circumstances.
The company was fined for engaging in immoral business practices.
She refused to participate in anything she believed was immoral.

Synset Examples

base and unpatriotic motives
a base, degrading way of life
cheating is dishonorable
they considered colonialism immoral
unethical practices in handling public funds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unethical - not morally correct
  • Degree Lower: amoral - lacking a moral sense; unconcerned with morality
  • Antonym: moral - conforming to accepted standards of morality

Etymology

From Latin immoralis (not moral), from in- (not) + moralis (moral).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈmɔrəl / (AmE), / ɪˈmɒrəl / (BrE)
not conforming to accepted standards of morality; deliberately violating principles of right and wrong.
deliberately violating moral principles

Examples

Many people considered the politician's actions to be immoral and dishonest.
He refused to participate in what he felt was an immoral business scheme.
Some argue that lying is always immoral, no matter the circumstances.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: amoral - lacking a moral sense; not concerned with right or wrong
  • Antonym: moral - concerned with principles of right and wrong behavior
  • Synonym: unethical - not morally correct; not following accepted moral standards

Etymology

From Latin immoralis, from in- (not) + moralis (moral).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01553334-a
Synset POS: a
