assonant

1. ADJ

/ ˈæsənənt / (AmE), / ˈæsənənt / (BrE)
having the same or similar vowel sounds, especially in successive stressed syllables; relating to or containing assonance
having similar vowel sounds; related to assonance

Examples

The poet used assonant rhymes to create a melodic effect in the verse.
Many children's songs contain assonant phrases that are easy to remember.
Her analysis highlighted several assonant lines within the stanza.

Synset Examples

note the assonant words and syllables in ‘tilting at windmills’

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consonant - having similar consonant sounds; relating to consonance

Etymology

From Latin 'assonare' (to sound in answer to), from 'ad-' (to) + 'sonare' (to sound).

2. ADJ

/ ˈæs.ə.nənt / (AmE), / ˈæs.ənənt / (BrE)
(of words or syllables) having identical or very similar vowel sounds but different consonants, especially in successive words or stressed syllables, as in poetic effects.
having the same vowel sound with different consonants

Examples

The poem uses several assonant pairs to create a musical quality.
In the line 'rise high in the bright sky', the words are assonant.
Many tongue twisters rely on assonant patterns for their effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assonance - the resemblance of vowel sounds in nearby words or syllables
  • Antonym: consonant - (in sound) having a similar consonant sound

Etymology

From Latin 'assonans' (sounding in response), from 'ad-' (to) + 'sonare' (to sound).
