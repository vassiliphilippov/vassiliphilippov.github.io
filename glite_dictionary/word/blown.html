blown

1. ADJ

/ bloʊn / (AmE), / bləʊn / (BrE)
moved or acted upon by wind or air; carried, formed, or shaped by blowing.
moved or shaped by wind or air

Examples

The road was covered with blown sand after the storm.
They admired the intricate shapes of the blown glass sculptures.
During the drought, blown soil accumulated in the corners of the fields.

Synset Examples

blown clouds of dust choked the riders
blown soil mounded on the window sill

Etymology

Past participle of 'blow', from Old English *bláwan* (to blow, as of wind).
