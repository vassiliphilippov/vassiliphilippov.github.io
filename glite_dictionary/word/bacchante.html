bacchante

1. NOUN

/ bækˈænti, bəˈkæn.ti / (AmE), / bakˈkanteɪ, bəˈkænti / (BrE)
(in classical mythology) a female priestess or devotee of Bacchus, the Roman god of wine; a maenad.
female devotee of Bacchus (mythology)

Examples

The sculpture depicts a bacchante dancing joyfully in a forest clearing.
In Roman mythology, a bacchante would participate in secret rites celebrating Bacchus.
She dressed as a bacchante for the masquerade ball, complete with vines and a thyrsus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maenad - female follower of Dionysus in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Latin 'bacchante', from 'Bacchus' (the god of wine).
