rigourousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪɡərəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡ(ə)rəznəs / (BrE)
the quality of being extremely strict, severe, or stern, especially in enforcing rules or discipline
extreme sternness or severity

Examples

The rigourousness of the new regulations surprised even seasoned professionals in the field.
Boot camp is known for the rigourousness of its training, pushing recruits to their limits.
Some critics argued that the rigourousness of the punishment did not fit the crime.

Synset Examples

severity of character
the harshness of his punishment was inhuman
the rigors of boot camp

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rigor - strictness or severity
  • Antonym: leniency - quality of being merciful or less strict

Etymology

From rigorous + -ness; 'rigorous' from Latin rigor (stiffness, hardness).
