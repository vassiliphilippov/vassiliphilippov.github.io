unimpeachable

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.ɪmˈpitʃ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌʌn.ɪmˈpiː.tʃə.bəl / (BrE)
so honest or good that nobody could doubt or criticize it; not able to be doubted or questioned
not able to be doubted or criticized

Examples

The judge was known for her unimpeachable integrity and commitment to justice.
His unimpeachable honesty earned him the trust of colleagues and friends alike.
Their evidence was considered unimpeachable by the court, leaving no room for doubt.

Synset Examples

has lived a blameless life
of irreproachable character
an unimpeachable reputation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blameless - without blame; free from guilt or fault
  • Synonym: irreproachable - so good or blameless as to be beyond criticism

Etymology

From un- (not) + impeachable, from Anglo-Norman empecher (to hinder), ultimately from Latin impedicare (to entangle).

not able to be criticized; trustworthy

Examples

He maintained an unimpeachable standard of integrity in all his business dealings.

Synset Examples

  • Antonym: impeachable - able to be doubted or criticized; open to accusation

Etymology

From un- (not) + impeachable, from impeach (to accuse or charge), ultimately from Latin impedicare (to entangle).

3. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.ɪmˈpitʃ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌʌn.ɪmˈpiː.tʃə.bəl / (BrE)
so good or blameless as to be beyond doubt or criticism; not able to be doubted or questioned
impossible to doubt or criticize

Examples

Everyone trusted the committee's findings because they were based on unimpeachable evidence.
She has an unimpeachable reputation for honesty in the industry.
According to an unimpeachable source, the story is true.

Synset Examples

an unimpeachable source

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impeccable - in accordance with the highest standards; faultless
  • Antonym: questionable - not certain, not reliable, or open to doubt

Etymology

From un- (not) + impeachable (from 'impeach' meaning to challenge or accuse).
