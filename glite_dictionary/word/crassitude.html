crassitude

1. NOUN

/ ˈkræs.ɪˌtuːd / (AmE), / ˈkræs.ɪ.tjuːd / (BrE)
the state or quality of being crude, coarse, or lacking in refinement or sensitivity
crudeness; lack of refinement

Examples

His blatant crassitude during the meeting offended everyone present.
The film's humor leans heavily on crassitude rather than wit or subtlety.
She was appalled by the crassitude of their comments about the guests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crassness - the quality of being crass; lack of refinement or sensitivity
  • Antonym: refinement - the process or quality of being refined or cultured

Etymology

From French 'crassitude', based on Latin 'crassitudo' (thickness, coarseness).
