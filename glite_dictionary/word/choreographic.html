choreographic

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɔːriəˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌkɒːriəˈɡræfɪk / (BrE)
relating to choreography or the composition of dance movements and sequences
relating to choreography

Examples

The ballet showcased impressive choreographic complexity that captivated the audience.
Her choreographic style blends contemporary and classical elements seamlessly.
They received an award for outstanding choreographic achievement in modern dance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choreography - the art or practice of designing dance sequences

Etymology

From French 'chorégraphique', ultimately from Greek 'khoreia' (dance) + '-graphic' (relating to writing/description).
