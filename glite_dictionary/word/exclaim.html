to exclaim

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈskleɪm / (AmE), / ɪkˈskleɪm / (BrE)
to say something suddenly or loudly, often because of surprise, strong emotion, or excitement
to say suddenly or loudly with emotion

Examples

"What a beautiful view!" she exclaimed as they reached the mountaintop.
He exclaimed in surprise when he opened the unexpected gift.
The teacher exclaimed that the class had done an excellent job on the project.

Synset Examples

‘I am not a Communist,’ he exclaimed
The King will proclaim an amnesty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shout - to speak very loudly, often due to anger or excitement
  • Synonym: to proclaim - to declare something publicly or officially
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak very softly or quietly, the opposite of exclaim

Etymology

From Latin exclamare, from ex- (out) + clamare (to cry out).

2. VERB

/ ɪkˈskleɪm / (AmE), / ɪkˈskleɪm / (BrE)
to say something suddenly and loudly, often because of surprise, strong emotion, or excitement
say suddenly and loudly with emotion

Examples

"What a beautiful view!" she exclaimed as they reached the top of the hill.
He exclaimed in surprise when he opened the door and saw the balloons.
The children exclaimed with joy when they saw the puppy.

Synset Examples

‘I won!’ he exclaimed
‘Help!’ she cried
‘I'm here,’ the mother shouted when she saw her child looking lost

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shout - to say something very loudly
  • Synonym: cry - to call out loudly, especially in emotion or pain

Etymology

From Latin exclamare (to cry out), from ex- (out) + clamare (to shout).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00914001-v
Synset POS: v
