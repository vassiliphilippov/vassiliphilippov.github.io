seminarian

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛm.əˈnɛr.i.ən / (AmE), / ˌsɛm.ɪˈneə.ri.ən / (BrE)
a student at a seminary, especially one training for the priesthood in the Roman Catholic Church
student at a seminary

Examples

After four years as a seminarian, he was ordained as a priest.
The young seminarian studied theology, philosophy, and pastoral care.
Many seminarians attend retreats to reflect on their spiritual journey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seminary - a college for training people to become priests, ministers, or rabbis

Etymology

From Latin seminarium (seed plot, nursery) + -an (forming nouns).
