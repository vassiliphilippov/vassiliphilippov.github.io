palindrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæl.ɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈpæl.ɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
a word, phrase, or sequence of characters that reads the same backward as forward
word or phrase reading the same backward

Examples

"Racecar" is a famous palindrome that is spelled the same way backward and forward.
The teacher challenged the students to come up with a new palindrome for the contest.
Some people enjoy finding hidden palindromes in sentences and names.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anagram - word or phrase formed by rearranging letters of another

Etymology

From Greek palindromos, from palin (again) + dromos (running).
