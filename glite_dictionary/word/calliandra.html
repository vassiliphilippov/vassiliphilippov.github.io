calliandra

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæliˈændrə / (AmE), / ˌkælɪˈændrə / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs and small trees valued for their finely divided leaves and clusters of white, pink, or red flowers.
genus of flowering shrubs or small trees

Examples

The garden features several calliandra bushes, their pink blossoms attracting hummingbirds.
Among the tropical plants, the calliandra stands out for its finely divided foliage and vibrant flowers.
She planted a calliandra tree along the walkway to add color and texture to the landscape.

Etymology

From Greek kallos (beauty) + andros (man, referring to the stamens), referring to the showy stamens of the flowers.
