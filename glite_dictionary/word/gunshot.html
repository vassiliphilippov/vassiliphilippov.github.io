gunshot

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌnˌʃɑt / (AmE), / ˈɡʌnʃɒt / (BrE)
the act of firing a gun; a shot discharged from a firearm
the act of shooting a gun

Examples

A single gunshot echoed through the empty street.
Witnesses said they heard multiple gunshots during the incident.
The sound of a distant gunshot alarmed the campers.

Synset Examples

the gunfire endangered innocent bystanders
they retreated in the face of withering enemy fire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gunfire - the repeated firing of guns
  • Synonym: gunshot wound - an injury caused by a bullet fired from a gun

Etymology

From 'gun' + 'shot', first recorded in English in the late 16th century.
