stinker

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪŋkɚ / (AmE), / ˈstɪŋkə / (BrE)
an artifact, especially a car, that is defective or unsatisfactory
defective artifact, especially a car
informal

Examples

That old car of his is a real stinker—it breaks down every week.
Even after the repairs, the washing machine was still a stinker.
She traded her stinker of a laptop for a better model.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemon - an automobile regarded as a bad buy; a defective or unsatisfactory car
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɪŋkər / (AmE), / ˈstɪŋkə / (BrE)
an informal term for a person who is regarded as despicable or contemptible
despicable or contemptible person (informal)
informal

Examples

I can't believe he lied to us—what a stinker!
The kids called their classmate a stinker after he ratted them out to the teacher.
Don't trust him; he's a real stinker when it comes to keeping secrets.

Synset Examples

only a rotter would do that
kill the rat
throw the bum out
you cowardly little pukes!
the British call a contemptible person a ‘git’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rotter - an unpleasant or contemptible person (chiefly British)
  • Synonym: scoundrel - a dishonest or unscrupulous person
  • Antonym: hero - a person admired for brave or noble qualities

Etymology

From 'stink' + '-er', originally meaning someone or something that smells bad, later extended figuratively.

3. NOUN

/ ˈstɪŋkər / (AmE), / ˈstɪŋkə / (BrE)
something that has a strong, unpleasant smell, especially a cheap cigar.
thing with an offensive odor
informal

Examples

The locker room was such a stinker after the game that no one wanted to go inside.
He lit up a stinker of a cigar that quickly filled the room with smoke.
That old trash pile is a real stinker in the hot summer sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stink - to have a strong, unpleasant smell

Etymology

From the verb 'stink' + '-er', originally meaning 'one who stinks'.
