dully

1. ADV

/ ˈdʌli / (AmE), / ˈdʌli / (BrE)
In a manner that lacks brightness, shine, or luster.
in a dull, not shiny way

Examples

The silver candlesticks gleamed dully in the dim room.
Under the thick clouds, the sun shone dully and offered little warmth.
Her eyes reflected the light dully, lacking any sparkle.

Synset Examples

the light shone dully through the haze
unpolished buttons glinted dully

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brightly - in a manner showing brightness or shine

Etymology

From 'dull' + '-ly', forming an adverb from the adjective.

2. ADV

/ ˈdʌli / (AmE), / ˈdʌli / (BrE)
In a manner that lacks liveliness, interest, or brightness.
in a dull, lifeless way

Examples

She stared out of the window dully, not reacting to anything around her.
His voice droned on dully, putting the whole class to sleep.
The lights flickered dully in the foggy evening, barely illuminating the street.

Synset Examples

she nodded her head dully

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lively - full of life or energy
  • Synonym: listlessly - in a way that shows a lack of energy or enthusiasm

Etymology

From 'dull' + '-ly', standard adverbial suffix.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00238021-r
Synset POS: r
