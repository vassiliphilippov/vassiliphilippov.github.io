Abronia fragrans

1. NOUN

/ əˈbroʊniə ˈfreɪɡrənz / (AmE), / əˈbrəʊniə ˈfreɪɡrænz / (BrE)
A species of wildflower native to North America, known for its tall growth and night-blooming fragrant white flowers.
species of night-blooming wildflower

Examples

The garden was filled with the sweet scent of Abronia fragrans blooming at dusk.
Researchers studied the pollination patterns of Abronia fragrans to learn more about nocturnal insects.
Many native plant enthusiasts cultivate Abronia fragrans for its attractive and fragrant flowers.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Abronia' is the genus name, 'fragrans' means 'fragrant'.
