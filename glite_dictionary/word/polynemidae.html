Polynemidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊlɪˈnɛməˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈnɛməˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical and subtropical marine fishes known as threadfins, characterized by long, filamentous rays on their pectoral fins.
family of fishes called threadfins

Examples

The Polynemidae family is distinguished by separate threadlike rays extending from their pectoral fins.
Several species within Polynemidae are important for commercial and subsistence fishing.
Members of Polynemidae are commonly found in warm, coastal waters around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: threadfin - a common name for fishes in the Polynemidae family

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek polynēmos (many-threaded) + -idae (denoting a family).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊlɪˈnɛməˌdaɪ / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈnɛməˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of chiefly tropical marine fishes known as threadfins, characterized by long, threadlike pectoral fin rays.
family of tropical fishes (threadfins)
medical

Examples

The Polynemidae are found in warm coastal waters worldwide.
Many members of the Polynemidae family are prized as food fish in Southeast Asia.
Scientists studied the unique feeding habits of Polynemidae in coral reef ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: threadfin - Any fish of the family Polynemidae, known for their long, filamentous fin rays.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'poly-' (many) and 'nēma' (thread), referring to the long fin rays.
