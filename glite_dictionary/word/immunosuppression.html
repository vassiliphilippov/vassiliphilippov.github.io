immunosuppression

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌmjuːnoʊsəˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌmjuːnəsəˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
reduction of the body's natural immune response to foreign substances, either intentionally (as before organ transplants) or as an unintended side effect (such as from chemotherapy)
reduction of immune response
medical

Examples

Organ transplant patients often require immunosuppression to prevent rejection of the new organ.
Prolonged immunosuppression can increase the risk of infections in cancer patients.
Some medications can cause immunosuppression as an unintended side effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immunosuppressant - a drug that suppresses the immune response
  • Antonym: immunostimulation - enhancement of the immune response

Etymology

From Latin 'immunis' (exempt, immune) + English 'suppression'.
