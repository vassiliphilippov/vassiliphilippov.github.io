Caproidae

1. NOUN

/ kəˈprɔɪdiː / (AmE), / kəˈprɔɪdɪi / (BrE)
A family of marine fishes known as boarfishes, characterized by deep, laterally compressed bodies and typically found in deep waters.
family of marine fishes (boarfish)

Examples

The Caproidae are distinguished by their deep, laterally compressed bodies, which set them apart from other fish families.
Marine biologists discovered a new species within the Caproidae in the Pacific Ocean.
Detailed studies of the Caproidae have contributed to our understanding of deep-sea fish adaptation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boarfish - a common name for fish in the Caproidae family

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Capros and the suffix -idae (used for zoological family names).
