Swainsona galegifolia

1. NOUN

/ sweɪnˈsoʊnə gæˌlɛgɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / sweɪnˈsəʊnə ˌɡalɪˈɡfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
An erect or trailing perennial plant native to eastern Australia, characterized by racemes of blue, purple, or red flowers.
Australian perennial plant with colorful flowers

Examples

The Swainsona galegifolia blooms in late spring, brightening the fields with its purple and red flowers.
Botanists are studying Swainsona galegifolia for its potential uses in horticulture.
I found a patch of Swainsona galegifolia along the riverbank during my trip to Australia.

Etymology

Named for Isaac Swainson, English botanist, and the resemblance to Galega (another plant genus).
