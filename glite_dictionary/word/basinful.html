basinful

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪsɪnˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsɪnfʊl / (BrE)
An amount that fills a basin.
amount that fills a basin

Examples

She carried a basinful of water from the well to the kitchen.
After washing the vegetables, the basinful of dirty water was poured outside.
He returned with a basinful of soapy water to clean the floors.

Synset Examples

a basinful of water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bowlful - the amount that fills a bowl
  • Synonym: spoonful - the amount that fills a spoon

Etymology

Formed from 'basin' + 'ful', in the sense of 'as much as will fill'.
