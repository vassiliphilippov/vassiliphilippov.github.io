austereness

1. NOUN

/ ɔˈstɪr.nəs / (AmE), / ɒˈstɪə.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being extremely plain, simple, or lacking in decoration or luxury
extreme plainness or simplicity

Examples

The austereness of the room was striking—bare walls, simple furniture, and no decoration.
Some people find peace in the austereness of minimalist design.
Her wardrobe reflected a certain austereness, with simple cuts and muted colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: austerity - the quality of being austere, or extreme simplicity or severity

Etymology

From 'austere' (via Old French, from Latin austerus, from Greek austēros meaning 'severe').
