Psilotum nudum

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈloʊtəm ˈnuːdəm / (AmE), / saɪˈləʊtəm ˈnjuːdəm / (BrE)
A species of whisk fern found in pantropical regions, often growing on other plants or on the ground, characterized by dull yellow branches and tiny leaves.
species of pantropical whisk fern

Examples

The rare Psilotum nudum is often spotted growing on tree trunks in tropical forests.
Botanists studied the reproductive cycles of Psilotum nudum in its native habitats.
Psilotum nudum is sometimes called the 'whisk fern' because of its branched, leafless stems.

Etymology

From Latin: 'psilotum' meaning bare or naked, and 'nudum' meaning naked, referring to the plant's simple structure.
