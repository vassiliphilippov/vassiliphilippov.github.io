Mahonia

1. NOUN

/ məˈhoʊniə / (AmE), / məˈhəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen shrubs and small trees native to North and Central America and Asia, known for their pinnate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers.
a genus of evergreen shrubs and small trees

Examples

The garden was filled with the bright yellow blossoms of Mahonia in early spring.
Mahonia is valued by gardeners for its hardiness and striking foliage.
Birds are attracted to the berries that appear on the Mahonia after flowering.

Etymology

Named after Bernard McMahon, an Irish-American horticulturist.
