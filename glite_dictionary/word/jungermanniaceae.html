Jungermanniaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌjʌŋərˌmæn.iˈeɪ.si.iː / (AmE), / ˌjʌŋəˌmæn.iˈeɪ.siːˌiː / (BrE)
A family of leafy liverworts in the order Jungermanniales.
family of leafy liverworts

Examples

The Jungermanniaceae family includes many species of leafy liverworts found in temperate forests.
Researchers studied the morphology of several Jungermanniaceae genera to clarify their classification.
Members of Jungermanniaceae play an important role in forest ecosystems as decomposers.

Etymology

From New Latin Jungermanniaceae, after German botanist Ludwig Jungermann (1572–1653).
