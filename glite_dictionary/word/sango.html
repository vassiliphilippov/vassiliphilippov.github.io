Sango

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑŋˌɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsæŋɡəʊ / (BrE)
a trade language widely spoken in the Central African Republic and parts of Chad, used as a lingua franca between speakers of different native languages
Central African trade language

Examples

Many people in Chad use Sango to communicate with others who speak different native languages.
She learned Sango after moving to the Central African Republic for her work.
Linguists have studied the spread of Sango as a major trade language across central Africa.

Etymology

From Sango (autonym), named after the Sango people of the Central African Republic.
