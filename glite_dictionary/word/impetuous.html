impetuous

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɛtʃuəs / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛtjuːəs / (BrE)
Acting or done quickly and without careful thought or consideration.
acting without due thought

Examples

Her impetuous decision to quit her job surprised everyone in the office.
He regretted his impetuous words once he saw the hurt they caused.
Driven by an impetuous urge, she booked a ticket and left for Paris that night.

Synset Examples

a hotheaded decision
liable to such impulsive acts as hugging strangers
an impetuous display of spending and gambling
madcap escapade

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rash - acting without thinking about the consequences
  • Antonym: thoughtful - considering things carefully before acting

Etymology

From Late Latin impetuosus (violent, headlong), from impetus (attack).

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɛtʃuəs / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛtjʊəs / (BrE)
(of movement, force, or action) moving with great energy or violence; forceful or rapid.
moving with great force or violence

Examples

The impetuous river rushed through the narrow gorge, churning up debris in its path.
During the storm, impetuous gusts of wind battered the coastal village.
He watched the impetuous waves crash relentlessly against the rocks.

Synset Examples

impetuous heaving waves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violent - using or involving physical force intended to hurt, damage, or kill
  • Antonym: gentle - having or showing a mild, kind, or tender temperament or character

Etymology

From Late Latin impetuosus (violent, rapid), from impetus (attack, force).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00846243-s
Synset POS: s
