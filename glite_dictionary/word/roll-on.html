roll-on

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊl ɒn / (AmE), / ˈrəʊl ɒn / (BrE)
A close-fitting women's undergarment that is rolled on over the hips, designed to shape and support the body.
woman's supportive undergarment

Examples

She wore a roll-on under her dress to create a smooth silhouette.
In the 1950s, the roll-on was a popular choice for women seeking shapewear.
My grandmother still prefers to wear a roll-on for extra support.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: girdle - a close-fitting undergarment for women, worn to shape and support the waist and hips

2. NOUN

/ ˈroʊl ɑn / (AmE), / ˈrəʊl ɒn / (BrE)
a container for liquid cosmetics or medications, especially deodorant, that dispenses through a revolving ball at the tip
liquid cosmetic dispenser with a revolving ball

Examples

She always carries a roll-on deodorant in her gym bag.
For sensitive skin, try a fragrance-free roll-on instead of a spray.
The new roll-on applies smoothly and dries quickly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spray - a dispenser that disperses liquid in the form of a spray (e.g. spray deodorant)
