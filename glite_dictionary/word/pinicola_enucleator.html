Pinicola enucleator

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈnɪkələ ˌɛn.juːˈkliː.eɪ.tər / (AmE), / pɪˈnɪkələ ˌɛn.juːˈkliː.eɪ.tə / (BrE)
A large grosbeak species found in coniferous forests of both the Old and New Worlds, known commonly as the pine grosbeak.
large grosbeak of northern coniferous forests

Examples

The bright red male Pinicola enucleator is a stunning sight against the winter snow.
Scientists observed several Pinicola enucleator feeding on berries in the boreal forest.
Birdwatchers in Scandinavia often hope to spot a Pinicola enucleator during the colder months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pine grosbeak - common name for Pinicola enucleator

Etymology

From Latin: 'Pinicola' meaning 'pine dweller,' and 'enucleator' meaning 'one who removes the kernel.'
