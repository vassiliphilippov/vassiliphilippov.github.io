Pieris floribunda

1. NOUN

/ piˈɛrɪs flɔːˈrɪbəndə / (AmE), / paɪˈɛərɪs flɒˈrɪbəndə / (BrE)
an ornamental evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States, bearing clusters of small white, bell-shaped flowers
ornamental evergreen shrub with white flowers

Examples

The garden featured a beautiful Pieris floribunda blooming in early spring.
Landscapers often choose Pieris floribunda for its attractive evergreen foliage and clusters of white flowers.
When planting Pieris floribunda, make sure to provide acidic, well-drained soil for best results.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Pieris; floribunda from Latin 'flos' (flower) + 'abundo' (to abound), meaning 'abundantly flowering'.
