Lupinus perennis

1. NOUN

/ luˈpaɪnəs pəˈrɛnɪs / (AmE), / luːˈpiːnəs pəˈrɛnɪs / (BrE)
A perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern and central North America, known for its palmate leaves and showy blue flowers; commonly called wild lupine.
a North American perennial plant with blue flowers

Examples

The sandy soils of the prairie are ideal for growing Lupinus perennis.
Monarch butterflies often lay their eggs on patches of Lupinus perennis.
Conservationists are working to restore Lupinus perennis habitats to support endangered species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild lupine - common English name for Lupinus perennis

Etymology

From Latin Lupinus (the genus, meaning 'wolf-like') and perennis ('lasting through the year', perennial).
