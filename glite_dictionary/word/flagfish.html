flagfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈflæɡˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈflæɡˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a small North American fish with a dark-blue back and pale sides marked with red stripes, native to swamps and streams of Florida
small striped fish native to Florida

Examples

The colorful flagfish is a popular choice among aquarium enthusiasts.
We spotted a group of flagfish swimming in a shallow Florida stream.
He studied the distinctive markings on a flagfish during his field research.

Etymology

Named for the flag-like appearance of its striped pattern.
