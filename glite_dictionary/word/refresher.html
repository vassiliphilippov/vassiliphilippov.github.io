refresher

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈfrɛʃər / (AmE), / rɪˈfrɛʃə / (BrE)
an additional fee paid to a barrister in a case that continues beyond a single day
additional legal fee for prolonged cases
legal british

Examples

The barrister received a refresher after the trial extended into a second week.
If the hearing lasts more than one day, a refresher is usually agreed upon in advance.
The cost of a refresher may be significant for lengthy court proceedings.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈfrɛʃər / (AmE), / rɪˈfrɛʃə / (BrE)
a drink, especially a non-alcoholic one, taken to refresh oneself
refreshing drink

Examples

After the long hike, we all enjoyed a cold refresher at the nearby café.
Would you like a refresher before we continue our meeting?
He paused for a quick refresher at the bar before heading home.

Synset Examples

he stopped at the bar for a quick refresher

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beverage - a drink, especially one other than water

Etymology

From refresh + -er, first used in the sense of someone or something that refreshes.

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈfrɛʃər / (AmE), / rɪˈfrɛʃə / (BrE)
a course that reviews and updates knowledge or skills for people who have not kept up with recent developments
course to update knowledge or skills

Examples

The company offered a refresher to help employees learn the latest safety protocols.
Teachers are encouraged to attend a refresher every few years to stay current.
I took a refresher on CPR because I hadn't practiced it in years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refresher course - a course to review and update existing knowledge in a subject
