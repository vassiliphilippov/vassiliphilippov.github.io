supervised

1. ADJ

/ ˈsuː.pɚ.vaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈsuː.pə.vaɪzd / (BrE)
under the observation or control of someone responsible, such as a supervisor or overseer
under observation or control

Examples

The children were allowed to swim only in the supervised area of the pool.
During the experiment, all participants remained in a supervised environment for their safety.
Many after-school programs offer supervised activities to ensure kids are looked after.

Synset Examples

supervised play

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unattended - not supervised or watched over
  • Synonym: monitored - watched or checked regularly for a purpose

Etymology

From the verb 'supervise', from Latin supervidere (to oversee), from super (over) + videre (to see).
