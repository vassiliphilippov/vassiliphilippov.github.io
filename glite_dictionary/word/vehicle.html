vehicle

1. NOUN

/ ˈviːɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈviːɪkl̩ / (BrE)
An inanimate object that can transmit infectious agents from one person to another.
object transmitting infection
medical

Examples

Contaminated towels acted as a vehicle for the spread of the disease in the hospital.
Money can serve as a vehicle for transmitting bacteria between people.
Dishes not properly cleaned may become a vehicle of infection in restaurants.

Etymology

From Latin vehiculum (means of transport), from vehere (to carry).

2. NOUN

/ ˈviːɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈviːɪkl̩ / (BrE)
A substance used as a medium to convey or distribute a drug, pigment, or other material with which it is mixed.
substance that serves as a medium for another

Examples

In this ointment, an oil-based vehicle is used to carry the medication into the skin.
Water often serves as the vehicle for dyes in solutions used for scientific experiments.
The choice of vehicle can affect how evenly the pigment spreads on the surface.

Etymology

From Latin vehiculum (means of conveyance), from vehere (to carry).

4. NOUN

/ ˈviː.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈviː.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
A machine, usually with wheels, used for transporting people or goods from one place to another.
means of transport

Examples

The city has made it easier for electric vehicles to get charging stations.
She loaded all her camping gear into the back of the vehicle and set off for the mountains.
Using public vehicles like buses and trains can help reduce air pollution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conveyance - a means of transporting something
  • Degree Lower: car - a road vehicle, typically with four wheels, powered by an engine
  • Degree Higher: aircraft - a vehicle that can fly, such as an airplane or helicopter

Etymology

From Latin vehiculum (means of transport), from vehere (to carry).
