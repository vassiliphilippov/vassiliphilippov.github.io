orthopaedist

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːrθəˈpiːdɪst / (AmE), / ˌɔː.θəˈpiː.dɪst / (BrE)
A medical doctor who specializes in the diagnosis and treatment of deformities or disorders of the bones, joints, and related structures, especially in children.
specialist in skeletal disorders
medical british

Examples

The orthopaedist recommended surgery to correct the spinal curvature.
After the accident, she was referred to an orthopaedist for treatment of her broken leg.
As a leading orthopaedist, he has published numerous papers on pediatric bone disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orthopedist - specialist in skeletal disorders (American spelling)

Etymology

From Greek orthos (straight) + paideia (rearing of children); influenced by French orthopédiste.
