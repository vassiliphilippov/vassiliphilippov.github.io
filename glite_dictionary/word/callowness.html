callowness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæloʊnəs / (AmE), / ˈkaləʊnəs / (BrE)
the quality of lacking experience, maturity, or sophistication.
inexperience or immaturity

Examples

His callowness in business matters led to several avoidable mistakes.
The teacher recognized the callowness of her new students and guided them patiently.
Despite his talent, his callowness was evident during the high-pressure negotiations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: maturity - the quality of being mature; having full development or experience
  • Synonym: inexperience - lack of experience; lack of skill or knowledge

Etymology

From Middle English 'calowe' (bare, unfledged), influenced by 'callow' meaning immature or inexperienced.
