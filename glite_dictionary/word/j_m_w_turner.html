J. M. W. Turner

1. PROPN

/ ˌdʒeɪ ˌɛm ˌdʌbəl.juː ˈtɜrnər / (AmE), / ˌdʒeɪ ˌɛm ˌdʌb.ljuː ˈtɜː.nə / (BrE)
(1775–1851) English landscape painter noted for his innovative use of light and color, whose work influenced the Impressionist movement.
English landscape painter (1775–1851)

Examples

J. M. W. Turner revolutionized landscape painting with his depiction of light and atmosphere.
The works of J. M. W. Turner are displayed in major galleries around the world.
Many art historians consider J. M. W. Turner a precursor to the Impressionist movement.
