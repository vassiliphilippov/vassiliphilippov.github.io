stockpile

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑkˌpaɪl / (AmE), / ˈstɒkˌpaɪl / (BrE)
A large supply of something that is stored and kept for future use.
accumulated supply kept for future use

Examples

The government maintains a stockpile of medical supplies for emergencies.
Farmers created a stockpile of grain to prepare for the winter months.
The army's stockpile of weapons was inspected by international observers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reserve - a supply of something kept for future use or emergencies
  • Synonym: hoard - an accumulated store of something valuable, often secretively kept

Etymology

From 'stock' (supply) + 'pile' (heap, accumulation), first used in the early 20th century.

3. VERB

/ ˈstɑkˌpaɪl / (AmE), / ˈstɒkˌpaɪl / (BrE)
to keep a large supply of something for future use or emergencies
keep a large supply for future use

Examples

During the winter, many families stockpile extra food and water in case of emergencies.
Pharmacies often stockpile medicines before a flu outbreak to meet increased demand.
The company stockpiled raw materials to avoid shortages during the strike.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoard - to accumulate and hide a supply of something
  • Antonym: to deplete - to use up the supply of something
