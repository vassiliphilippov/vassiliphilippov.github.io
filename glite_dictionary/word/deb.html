deb

1. NOUN

/ dɛb / (AmE), / dɛb / (BrE)
a young woman of upper-class background making her formal entrance into society; a debutante
young woman entering high society

Examples

Every spring, the city hosts a grand ball for each deb to be presented.
Her grandmother was a celebrated deb in the 1950s, attending many society parties.
The young deb wore a white gown and long gloves at her coming-out ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debutante - a young woman making her first appearance in society

Etymology

Shortened form of 'debutante', from French 'débutante' (female beginner).
