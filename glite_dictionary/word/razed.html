razed

1. ADJ

/ reɪzd / (AmE), / reɪzd / (BrE)
(of a building, structure, or place) destroyed completely; demolished to the ground.
destroyed or demolished completely

Examples

The old factory was razed to make way for a new shopping mall.
After the fire, several homes in the neighborhood were completely razed.
Most of the historic city center was razed during the conflict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demolished - completely destroyed, especially a building
  • Antonym: intact - untouched or not damaged, remaining whole

Etymology

Past tense and past participle of 'raze', from Middle French 'raser' (to scrape, shave off, erase).
