scission

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪʒ.ən / (AmE), / ˈsɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the act of cutting, splitting, or dividing something into parts
the act of cutting or splitting

Examples

The scission of the company resulted in two independent businesses.
Polymer scission occurs when the chains are broken during chemical reactions.
The treaty marked the official scission between the two formerly allied nations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: division - the act of separating something into parts
  • Synonym: cleavage - the act of splitting or dividing, especially along a natural line

Etymology

From Latin 'scissio' (a cutting, cleaving), from 'scindere' (to cut, split).
