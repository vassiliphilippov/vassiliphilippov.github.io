to skreak

1. VERB

/ skrik / (AmE), / skriːk / (BrE)
to utter a harsh, abrupt scream or cry
emit a harsh, abrupt scream

Examples

The bird skreaked loudly as it flew past the window.
Children skreaking in fear could be heard throughout the old house.
Suddenly, he skreaked in pain as the door slammed on his finger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screech - to make a loud, harsh, piercing sound
  • Synonym: shriek - to utter a loud, sharp, high-pitched cry

2. VERB

/ skriːk / (AmE), / skriːk / (BrE)
(archaic or dialect) To make a high-pitched, screeching noise.
make a screeching or shrill noise
archaic

Examples

The iron gate skreaked as it swung open in the wind.
Her voice skreaked with fear during the storm.
Crows skreaked in the leafless trees at dusk.

Synset Examples

The door creaked when I opened it slowly
My car engine makes a whining noise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screech - to make a loud, harsh, piercing cry
  • Synonym: shriek - to utter a high-pitched piercing sound

Etymology

Probably an alteration of Scots "skreigh" (to shriek, screech), of imitative origin.
