basting

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbeɪstɪŋ / (BrE)
A loose, temporary stitch used to hold layers of fabric together before the final sewing.
temporary sewing stitch

Examples

She used basting to hold the pieces of fabric together before sewing the seam.
The tailor removed the basting stitches after finishing the dress.
Good basting makes it easier to assemble a complex garment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tacking - temporary stitching to hold fabric in place

Etymology

From the verb 'baste,' meaning to sew with long, loose stitches; of unknown origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbeɪstɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of regularly moisten a roasting food with fat, juices, or other liquid to prevent it from drying out during cooking
moistening food during roasting

Examples

Frequent basting is the key to keeping the turkey juicy and full of flavor.
The recipe recommends basting every thirty minutes for best results.
After each basting, the chicken developed a beautiful golden color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baste - to pour juices or melted fat over food while cooking

Etymology

From Middle English *basten* (to moisten or pour fat over roasting meat).
