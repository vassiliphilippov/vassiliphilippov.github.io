Symplocos paniculata

1. NOUN

/ sɪmˈploʊkəs ˌpænɪˈkjuːlɑːtə / (AmE), / sɪmˈpləʊkɒs ˌpænɪˈkjuːlətə / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub of eastern Asia, known for its decorative bright blue fruit.
Asian shrub with bright blue fruit

Examples

The Symplocos paniculata adds a striking splash of color to gardens with its bright blue berries.
Botanists often study Symplocos paniculata for its ornamental value and ecological role in eastern Asia.
In autumn, the leaves of Symplocos paniculata turn yellow while the fruit remains vividly blue.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus Symplocos and the species name from Latin 'panicula' (a tuft or panicle).
