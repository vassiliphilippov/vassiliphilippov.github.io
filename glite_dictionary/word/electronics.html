electronics

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑnɪks / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒnɪks / (BrE)
the branch of physics and technology concerned with the emission, behavior, and effects of electrons, as well as the design and application of electronic devices
branch of physics dealing with electrons and electronic devices

Examples

She decided to study electronics at university because she was fascinated by how devices work.
Advances in electronics have made it possible to create smaller and more powerful computers.
The laboratory is equipped with the latest electronics for research and development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electrical engineering - the branch of engineering dealing with electricity and its applications

Etymology

From electron + -ics, modeled after physics.
