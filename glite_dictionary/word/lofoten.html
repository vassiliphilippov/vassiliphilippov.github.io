Lofoten

1. PROPN

/ ˈloʊˌfoʊtən / (AmE), / ˈlɒfəʊtən / (BrE)
a group of islands off the northwestern coast of Norway in the Norwegian Sea, known for dramatic scenery and fishing villages.
archipelago off Norway's northwest coast

Examples

Many photographers travel to Lofoten to capture its stunning Arctic landscapes.
Fishing has been central to the economy of Lofoten for centuries.
During the summer, the sun does not set in Lofoten, creating the phenomenon known as the midnight sun.

Etymology

From Norwegian 'Lofoten', possibly from Old Norse 'ló', lynx, and 'fótr', foot, referring to the shape of the islands.
