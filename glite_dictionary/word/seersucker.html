seersucker

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪrˌsʌkər / (AmE), / ˈsɪəˌsʌkə / (BrE)
a light fabric with a puckered or crinkled surface, often featuring stripes, commonly used in warm-weather clothing
light puckered striped fabric

Examples

He wore a seersucker suit to the summer wedding, looking both stylish and comfortable.
The lightweight seersucker fabric is perfect for hot, humid weather.
Classic seersucker jackets often feature a blue and white striped pattern.

Etymology

From Hindi *śīrśakar*, from Persian *shīr o shakar* meaning 'milk and sugar', describing the fabric's smooth and rough stripes.
