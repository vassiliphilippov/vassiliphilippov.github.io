chrysomelid

1. NOUN

/ krɪˈsɑːməlɪd / (AmE), / krɪˈsɒmɪlɪd / (BrE)
A brightly colored beetle of the family Chrysomelidae, many of which feed on plant leaves or infest stems and roots as larvae.
beetle of the Chrysomelidae family

Examples

The chrysomelid larvae caused severe damage to the crops by feeding on the roots.
Entomologists discovered several new species of chrysomelid in the rainforest.
Many gardeners recognize the chrysomelid by its vivid, metallic coloration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leaf beetle - Any beetle of the family Chrysomelidae, commonly known as chrysomelids

Etymology

From scientific Latin Chrysomelidae, based on Greek khrusos (gold) + melē (limb).
