domesticity

1. NOUN

/ dəˌmɛˈstɪsəti / (AmE), / dəˌmɛˈstɪsɪti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being domestic, especially relating to home life or household affairs
quality of being domestic; home life

Examples

The author explored themes of domesticity and family life in her latest novel.
After years of travel, he found comfort in the routines of domesticity.
Their home radiated a sense of domesticity that made guests feel welcome.

Synset Examples

a royal family living in unpretentious domesticity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: domestication - the process of taming or adapting to domestic life

Etymology

From Latin domesticus (of the home) + -ity.

2. NOUN

/ dəˌmɛˈsɪtəti / (AmE), / dəʊˌmɛˈsɪsɪti / (BrE)
life at home or the activities and atmosphere associated with the household or family.
life or activities in the home

Examples

After years of traveling, she longed for the comfort and routine of domesticity.
Some people find domesticity fulfilling, while others prefer an adventurous lifestyle.
Their weekends were devoted to domesticity, from cooking together to tending the garden.

Synset Examples

making a hobby of domesticity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: home life - the way people live at home, especially in terms of family routines and atmosphere

Etymology

From Latin 'domesticus' (pertaining to the home) + English '-ity'.
