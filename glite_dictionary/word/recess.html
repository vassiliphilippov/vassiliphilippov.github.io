recess

1. VERB

/ rɪˈsɛs / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛs / (BrE)
to make a hollow space or indentation in a surface or object
create an indentation or hollow space

Examples

The carpenter will recess the door hinges into the frame for a flush fit.
You need to recess the light fixture into the ceiling so it doesn't stick out.
After measuring carefully, they recessed the shelf into the wall.

Synset Examples

recess the piece of wood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indent - to make a dent or notch in something
  • Antonym: to protrude - to stick out from a surface, the opposite of making an indentation

Etymology

From Latin recessus (a going back, retreat), from recedo (to go back, withdraw).
