pelecypodous

1. ADJ

/ pəˌlɛsɪˈpoʊdəs / (AmE), / pɛlɪˈsɪpədəs / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of bivalve mollusks (such as clams, oysters, or mussels).
relating to bivalve mollusks
medical

Examples

Many fossils display pelecypodous shells, indicating they belonged to ancient bivalves.
The pelecypodous structure of the clam distinguishes it from other mollusks.
Scientists examined the pelecypodous fauna found within the riverbed sediment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bivalve - an aquatic mollusk with a compressed body enclosed within a hinged shell

Etymology

From New Latin Pelecypoda (a class of mollusks), from Greek pelekys (axe) + pous (foot), referring to the shape of the mollusk's foot.
