carious

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɛəriəs / (AmE), / ˈkeəriəs / (BrE)
(of teeth or bone) affected with decay or cavities.
affected with decay (esp. teeth or bone)
medical

Examples

The dentist discovered several carious teeth during the examination.
Proper oral hygiene can help prevent carious lesions from developing.
He needed treatment for the carious molar that was causing him pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decayed - having undergone decomposition or rot
  • Antonym: sound - not decayed or damaged

Etymology

From Latin 'cariosus', from 'caries' (decay).
