reordering

1. NOUN

/ riˈɔrdərɪŋ / (AmE), / riːˈɔːdərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of arranging something in a different order
the act of rearranging order

Examples

The reordering of the chapters improved the flow of the book.
After the reordering of the list, my name appeared at the top.
Inventory reordering is necessary to keep the warehouse organized.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rearrangement - the act of changing the arrangement of something

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 're-' and 'ordering', from 'order' (Latin 'ordo').
