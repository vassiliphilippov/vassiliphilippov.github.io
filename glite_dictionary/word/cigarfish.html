cigarfish

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈɡɑrˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / sɪˈɡɑːˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A small, spindle-shaped fish found in the western Atlantic Ocean.
small fish of the western Atlantic

Examples

The cigarfish is often found near coral reefs along the western Atlantic coast.
Marine biologists studied the migration patterns of the cigarfish last season.
A fisherman caught a rare cigarfish during his deep-sea expedition.

Etymology

From 'cigar' (the shape) + 'fish'.
