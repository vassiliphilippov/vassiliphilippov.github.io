vibrio

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪbrioʊ / (AmE), / ˈvɪbrɪəʊ / (BrE)
a genus of motile, curved rod-shaped bacteria, some species of which can cause diseases in humans and animals
curved rod-shaped bacterium
medical

Examples

Several diseases, including cholera, are caused by the vibrio genus.
Scientists discovered a new strain of vibrio in the contaminated water sample.
Proper food handling can prevent infections from vibrio bacteria found in seafood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacterium - single-celled microorganism

Etymology

From Latin 'vibrare' (to vibrate) + New Latin '-io' (forming nouns).
