cd

1. NOUN

/ si di / (AmE), / siː diː / (BrE)
The SI unit of luminous intensity, equal to one candela.
SI unit of luminous intensity
medical

Examples

The light source emitted an intensity of 50 cd in the forward direction.
Regulations require emergency exit signs to have at least 10 cd of luminous intensity.
The laboratory measured the lamp's output and found it to be 120 cd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candela - SI base unit of luminous intensity, symbol: cd

Etymology

Abbreviation of candela, from Latin 'candela' (candle).
