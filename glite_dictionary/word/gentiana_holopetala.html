Gentiana holopetala

1. NOUN

/ dʒɛnˈtaɪənə ˌhoʊloʊˈpɛtələ / (AmE), / dʒɛnˈtɪənə ˌhɒləʊˈpɛtələ / (BrE)
A small blue-flowered fringed gentian native to the Sierra Nevada mountains.
small blue-flowered gentian of Sierra Nevada

Examples

The delicate Gentiana holopetala blooms along alpine meadows in early summer.
Botanists admire the resilience of Gentiana holopetala in high-altitude conditions.
We spotted several Gentiana holopetala nestled among the rocks during our hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fringed gentian - a species of gentian with fringed blue petals

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Gentiana' named after King Gentius of Illyria, and 'holopetala' meaning 'whole petal'.
