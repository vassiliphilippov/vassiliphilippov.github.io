morosoph

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔr.əˌsɑf / (AmE), / ˈmɔː.rəˌsɒf / (BrE)
A person who is foolish despite being learned or having knowledge.
a learned fool
archaic

Examples

Despite his many degrees, the professor was often called a morosoph by his colleagues.
The play featured a classic morosoph who quoted Latin while making obvious blunders.
In her novel, the author painted the nobleman as a morosoph, lost in theory and blind to reality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool - a person lacking good sense or judgment

Etymology

From Greek mōros (foolish) + sophos (wise, learned).
