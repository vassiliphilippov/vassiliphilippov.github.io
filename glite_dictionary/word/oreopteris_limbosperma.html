Oreopteris limbosperma

1. NOUN

/ ɔːriˈɑːptərɪs ˌlɪmboʊˈspɜːrmə / (AmE), / ɔːrɪˈɒptərɪs ˌlɪmbəʊˈspɜːmə / (BrE)
A species of European mountain fern with fragrant lemon- or balsam-scented fronds.
a species of mountain fern

Examples

The Oreopteris limbosperma is known for its fragrant, lemon-scented fronds in alpine meadows.
Botanists found several thriving Oreopteris limbosperma specimens along the shaded mountain stream.
Gardeners appreciate Oreopteris limbosperma for its decorative and aromatic fronds.

Etymology

From Greek 'oros' (mountain) + 'pteris' (fern); species name from Latin 'limbus' (border) + 'sperma' (seed).
