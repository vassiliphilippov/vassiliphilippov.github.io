to miscegenate

1. VERB

/ mɪˈsɛdʒəˌneɪt / (AmE), / mɪˈsɛdʒɪneɪt / (BrE)
to marry or cohabit with a person of another race, especially used in historical, legal, or sociological contexts
marry or cohabit with a person of another race

Examples

Some states once banned couples from legally miscegenating to enforce racial segregation.
Scholars studied how anti-miscegenation laws discouraged people from miscegenating.
She argued that society should not dictate whom one chooses to miscegenate with.

Etymology

From Latin 'miscere' (to mix) and 'genus' (race, kind).
