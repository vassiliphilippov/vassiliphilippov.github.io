Makaira

1. NOUN

/ məˈkaɪrə / (AmE), / məˈkaɪərə / (BrE)
A genus of large marine fish known as marlins, characterized by an elongated body and a spear-like snout.
genus of marlins (large marine fish)

Examples

The Makaira genus includes some of the largest species of marlins in the world.
A blue marlin, classified under Makaira, is highly prized by sport fishermen.
Scientists studied the migratory patterns of Makaira in the Atlantic Ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marlin - a large marine fish with a spear-like upper jaw

Etymology

From Greek makaira (a kind of sword), referring to the fish's long bill.

2. NOUN

/ məˈkaɪrə / (AmE), / məˈkaɪərə / (BrE)
A genus of large oceanic fish in the family Istiophoridae, including specimens commonly known as marlins.
genus of marlins (fish)

Examples

The blue marlin, classified under the genus Makaira, is a prized catch among sport fishers.
Researchers studied the migratory patterns of several Makaira species in the Atlantic Ocean.
Products made from the meat of Makaira are popular in some coastal cuisines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marlin - A large game fish with a long sharp bill, found in tropical seas

Etymology

From Greek makaira (a kind of sword or knife), referencing the elongated bill of the marlin.
