opponent

1. ADJ

/ əˈpoʊnənt / (AmE), / əˈpəʊnənt / (BrE)
Opposing or actively hostile, especially as characteristic of a rival or adversary side.
actively hostile; opposing

Examples

The opponent factions refused to reach any compromise during the talks.
During the battle, the two opponent armies clashed with intense ferocity.
The debate featured opponent viewpoints on climate policy.

Synset Examples

opponent (or opposing) armies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opposing - Actively resisting, standing in opposition
  • Antonym: supporting - Taking the side of or aiding

Etymology

From Latin opponens, present participle of opponere (to oppose).

2. NOUN

/ əˈpoʊ.nənt / (AmE), / əˈpəʊ.nənt / (BrE)
a person who competes against or disagrees with another in a contest, argument, or conflict.
a person who opposes or competes

Examples

She defeated her strongest opponent in the final round.
During the debate, he remained calm even when his opponent argued forcefully.
The football team trained hard to face their opponent this weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adversary - a person, group, or force that opposes or attacks; enemy, foe
  • Antonym: ally - a person or group that cooperates with or helps another

Etymology

From Latin opponens, present participle of opponere (to set against).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09792668-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ əˈpoʊ.nənt / (AmE), / əˈpəʊ.nənt / (BrE)
a person who takes the opposite side in a contest, competition, or argument; someone you compete against
person you compete against

Examples

She faced her toughest opponent in the final round of the tournament.
During the debate, each opponent presented strong arguments.
His opponents respected him for his fair play and determination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rival - a person or thing competing with another for the same objective
  • Antonym: ally - a person who supports or cooperates with another

Etymology

From Latin opponere (to set against, oppose).
