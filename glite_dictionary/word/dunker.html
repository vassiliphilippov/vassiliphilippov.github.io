dunker

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌŋkər / (AmE), / ˈdʌŋkə / (BrE)
a person who dips food into a drink or other liquid before eating it
a person who dips food before eating

Examples

My grandfather is a true dunker, always dipping his biscuits into tea.
As a habitual dunker, she never eats cookies without milk.
You could tell he was a dunker by the crumbs left in his coffee.

Synset Examples

he was a dunker — he couldn't eat a doughnut without a cup of coffee to dunk it in

Etymology

From the verb 'dunk' (to dip), plus the agent suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʌŋkər / (AmE), / ˈdʌŋkə / (BrE)
a basketball player who is able to make dunk shots, especially with skill or frequency
basketball player skilled at dunking

Examples

He became famous as an incredible dunker during his college basketball career.
The team relies on their star dunker to energize the crowd with spectacular plays.
Every aspiring player hopes to be a great dunker like their favorite NBA stars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dunk - to score by putting the ball directly through the basket with one or both hands

Etymology

From 'dunk' (to score by putting the ball directly through the basket with one or both hands) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
