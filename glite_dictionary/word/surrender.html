surrender

1. NOUN

/ səˈrɛndɚ / (AmE), / səˈrɛndə / (BrE)
the act of declaring that you give up or admit defeat, especially in a conflict or competition
admitting defeat; giving up

Examples

The enemy signaled their surrender by raising a white flag.
Her speech was an open surrender to her opponent's superior argument.
Negotiations followed the army's surrender after months of conflict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capitulation - an act of surrendering or ceasing to resist; act of giving up resistance
  • Antonym: resistance - refusal to accept or comply; attempt to prevent something by action or argument

Etymology

From Old French 'surrendre' (to give up), from Latin 'reddere' (to give back).

/ səˈrɛndɚ / (AmE), / səˈrɛndə / (BrE)
the act of giving up control or possession to another, typically under agreed conditions or as a result of defeat
the act of giving up control to another

Examples

The general announced the surrender of his troops after a long and costly siege.
After weeks of negotiations, the company agreed to the surrender of its assets.
The surrender was formalized in a treaty signed by both sides.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: resistance - refusal to accept or comply; the act of resisting

Etymology

From Old French *surrendre* (to give up), from sur- (over) + rendre (to give back).

acceptance of despair; giving up hope

Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: defiance - open or bold resistance; refusal to yield

Examples

6. NOUN

/ səˈrɛndɚ / (AmE), / səˈrɛndə / (BrE)
the act of delivering a person (such as a suspect or defendant) into lawful custody, especially to authorities or the police
delivery of a person into lawful custody
legal

Examples

The fugitive agreed to surrender himself to the authorities to resolve the charges.
A formal surrender of the suspect was arranged between the two countries.
His lawyer negotiated the terms of his surrender with the prosecution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extradition - the act of handing over an accused person from one jurisdiction to another

Etymology

From Old French *surrendre*, from Vulgar Latin *surrendere* (to give up).
