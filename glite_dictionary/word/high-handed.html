high-handed

1. ADJ

/ haɪ ˈhændɪd / (AmE), / haɪ ˈhændɪd / (BrE)
using power or authority in a way that is overbearing and ignores others' opinions or rights; arrogantly or oppressively assertive.
overbearing; arrogantly assertive

Examples

The manager's high-handed actions angered the entire staff.
She resented his high-handed way of making decisions without consulting anyone.
The new policy was imposed in a high-handed manner, leaving employees frustrated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrogant - having an exaggerated sense of one's own importance
  • Synonym: authoritarian - favoring or enforcing strict obedience to authority
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a low estimate of one's importance

Etymology

Formed from 'high' + 'handed', metaphorically referring to acting from a superior position.
