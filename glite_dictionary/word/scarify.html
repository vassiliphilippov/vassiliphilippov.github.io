to scarify

1. VERB

/ ˈsker.əˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈskær.ɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
To break up the surface of (soil) to improve its condition for planting or aeration.
break up the surface of soil

Examples

Farmers often scarify the soil before planting new crops.
To encourage grass growth, you should scarify your lawn in early spring.
The gardener used a tool to scarify the compacted earth.

Synset Examples

scarify soil

Etymology

From French *scarifier*, from Late Latin *scarificare* (to scratch open), from Greek *skariphasthai* (to scratch lightly).

3. VERB

/ ˈskær.əˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈskar.ɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to scratch or make shallow cuts in the surface of something, especially to improve growth or absorption
make shallow cuts in a surface

Examples

Before planting, it is often necessary to scarify the seeds to promote germination.
Gardeners recommend that you scarify your lawn every spring to allow air and water to reach the roots.
The technician scarified the surface of the soil to help the new grass take root.

Synset Examples

scarify seeds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scratch - to make a mark or cut with something sharp

Etymology

From Old French *escarifier*, from Late Latin *scarificare* (to cut with a lancet), from Latin *scarificus* (cutting), from Greek *skariphasthai* (to carve lightly).
