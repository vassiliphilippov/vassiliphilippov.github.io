demon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiːmən / (AmE), / ˈdiːmən / (BrE)
A supernatural being, often considered evil, that is believed to possess or torment people or to bring about harm.
evil supernatural being

Examples

The villagers believed a demon was haunting the old church.
In many stories, a demon tries to tempt heroes into committing evil acts.
Some people claimed they saw a demon disappear into the shadows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devil - supreme evil spirit; Satan
  • Antonym: angel - benevolent supernatural being

Etymology

From Greek daimōn (spirit, divine power), via Latin daemon, originally meaning a guiding spirit, later given negative connotations.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdimən / (AmE), / ˈdiːmən / (BrE)
A cruel, wicked, and inhuman person.
cruel or wicked person

Examples

After the scandal, many saw the politician as a demon who had no regard for others.
The warlord's demon-like cruelty made him feared by the whole region.
Despite his charming exterior, some say a demon lurked beneath his smile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiend - an extremely cruel or wicked person
  • Antonym: angel - a kind, compassionate, or virtuous person

Etymology

From Greek daimōn (spirit, lesser god).

3. NOUN

/ ˈdiːmən / (AmE), / ˈdiːmən / (BrE)
a person who is extremely diligent or skillful, especially in a particular activity
extremely diligent or skillful person

Examples

He's a demon on the basketball court, scoring over thirty points every game.
Maria is a demon when it comes to organizing events, handling every detail perfectly.
After just a few lessons, Jake became a computer demon and could fix almost any problem.

Synset Examples

he worked like a demon to finish the job on time
she's a demon at math

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whiz - a person who is very skilled at something
  • Synonym: expert - a person with a high level of knowledge or skill

Etymology

From Latin daemon, from Greek daimōn (spirit; supernatural being); figurative use for energetic person dates from the 19th century.
