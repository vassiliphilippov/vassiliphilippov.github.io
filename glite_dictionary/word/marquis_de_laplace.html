Marquis de Laplace

1. PROPN

/ mɑrˌki də lɑˈplɑs / (AmE), / mɑːˌkiː də laˈplas / (BrE)
(1749–1827) French mathematician and astronomer noted for formulating the nebular hypothesis of the solar system's origin and for developing the theory of probability.
French mathematician and astronomer (1749–1827)

Examples

The contributions of Marquis de Laplace revolutionized our understanding of probability and celestial mechanics.
When studying the origins of the solar system, scientists often reference the Marquis de Laplace and his nebular hypothesis.
Many consider the Marquis de Laplace one of the founding fathers of modern astronomy and mathematics.
