trigger

1. VERB

/ ˈtrɪɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrɪɡə / (BrE)
to release or pull the trigger of a gun or other device, causing it to discharge or operate
pull the trigger of a gun or device

Examples

The officer triggered the rifle during the training exercise.
You should never trigger a weapon unless you are certain it is safe.
She accidentally triggered the alarm by pulling the safety lever.

Synset Examples

Trigger a gun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire - to discharge a gun or other firearm

Etymology

From the noun 'trigger', itself from Dutch 'trekken' (to pull).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01137797-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈtrɪɡə / (BrE)
an event or action that initiates a process or sets off a series of events
event or action that starts something

Examples

The politician's speech was the trigger for a series of nationwide protests.
A sudden drop in temperature acted as a trigger for the alarm system to activate.
Losing his job became the trigger for making major changes in his life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catalyst - something that causes an event or change to happen
  • Synonym: precursor - something that comes before and leads to another event
  • Antonym: consequence - a result or effect of an action or event
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrɪɡə / (BrE)
A small lever that is pressed to activate the firing mechanism of a gun or weapon.
lever that fires a gun

Examples

He squeezed the trigger and the gun fired.
Always keep your finger off the trigger until you are ready to shoot.
The detective examined the trigger for fingerprints.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lever - a rigid bar used to exert a force
  • Antonym: safety catch - a device that prevents accidental firing

Etymology

Perhaps from Dutch 'trekken' (to pull) or Middle Dutch 'trecken', relating to the idea of pulling.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03475752-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrɪɡə / (BrE)
a small lever or device that, when pressed or pulled, causes a mechanism or process to start, especially the firing of a gun or the activation of a machine
mechanism that initiates an action

Examples

The safety mechanism prevents the trigger from being pulled accidentally.
Smoke set off the alarm's trigger, causing the system to activate.
Squeezing the trigger releases the spring and fires the projectile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: switch - a device for making and breaking the connection in an electrical circuit

Etymology

From Dutch 'trekken' (to pull), related to the act of pulling a gun mechanism.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

5. VERB

/ ˈtrɪɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrɪɡə / (BrE)
to cause something to start or happen; to set something in motion
to cause something to happen

Examples

The recent announcement triggered widespread protests across the city.
Exposure to the chemical can trigger an allergic reaction in some people.
Her remarks triggered a heated debate among the panel members.

Synset Examples

trigger a reaction
actuate the circuits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: initiate - to start something
  • Synonym: cause - to make something happen
  • Antonym: to prevent - to stop something from happening

Etymology

From Middle English 'trygger', related to the Dutch 'trekken' (to pull), originally referring to the trigger of a gun.
