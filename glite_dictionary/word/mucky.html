mucky

1. ADJ

/ ˈmʌki / (AmE), / ˈmʌki / (BrE)
dirty or messy, especially covered with mud or filth
dirty or messy (especially with mud)

Examples

The children came home with their clothes all mucky from playing outside.
Please take off those mucky boots before coming into the house.
After a day in the garden, her hands were completely mucky.

Synset Examples

muddy boots
a mucky stable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muddy - covered or smeared with mud; dirty with mud
  • Antonym: clean - free from dirt or impurities

2. ADJ

/ ˈmʌki / (AmE), / ˈmʌki / (BrE)
(of ground or soil) soft, wet, and muddy; dirty and difficult to walk on.
soft, wet, muddy (soil)

Examples

The field became mucky after several days of rain and was difficult to cross.
Her boots were covered in mucky soil after she walked through the marsh.
We avoided the mucky path that ran alongside the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muddy - covered or filled with mud; soft and wet
  • Synonym: boggy - (of ground) too soft and wet to support a heavy body
