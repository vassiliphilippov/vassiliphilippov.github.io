Macedonia

1. PROPN

/ ˌmæsɪˈdoʊniə / (AmE), / ˌmæsɪˈdəʊniə / (BrE)
an ancient kingdom in the southeastern Balkans, ruled by Philip II and Alexander the Great, now divided among parts of modern North Macedonia, Greece, and Bulgaria
ancient kingdom in the Balkans

Examples

The ancient kingdom of Macedonia played a pivotal role in the spread of Greek culture.
Historians often discuss the conquests of Alexander the Great, who ruled Macedonia.
Archaeological finds from Macedonia reveal its rich and complex history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Alexander the Great - king of ancient Macedonia and one of history's greatest military leaders

Etymology

From Greek Makedonía, named after the ancient Macedonians.

2. PROPN

/ ˌmæsɪˈdoʊniə / (AmE), / ˌmæsɪˈdəʊniə / (BrE)
A landlocked republic in the Balkan Peninsula in southeastern Europe, officially known as North Macedonia since 2019.
country in southeastern Europe; North Macedonia

Examples

She traveled through Macedonia during her tour of the Balkans.
Macedonia became an independent country after the breakup of Yugoslavia in 1991.
The dispute over the name Macedonia was resolved in 2019 when it became North Macedonia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: North Macedonia - official short name of Macedonia since 2019
  • Antonym: Greece - country bordering Macedonia to the south

Etymology

From Latin 'Macedonia', from Ancient Greek 'Μακεδονία' (Makedonía), the name of an ancient kingdom.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08981403-n
Synset POS: n
