to terrorize

1. VERB

/ ˈtɛrəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈtɛrəraɪz / (BrE)
to frighten or intimidate someone, especially by using threats or violence, often to achieve a particular aim
intimidate by threats or violence

Examples

The gang used threats to terrorize the neighborhood for months.
Some dictators terrorize their citizens to maintain control.
She felt terrorized by the constant phone calls and threats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intimidate - to frighten or threaten someone, especially in order to make them do what one wants
  • Antonym: to reassure - to say or do something to remove someone's doubts or fears

Etymology

From French 'terroriser', from Latin 'terror' (fear, dread).

2. VERB

/ ˈtɛrəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈtɛrəraɪz / (BrE)
to deliberately frighten someone very much; to fill with intense fear
to frighten greatly; to fill with terror

Examples

The villagers were terrorized by the bandits for months.
Bullies often terrorize younger students on the playground.
Police arrested a man who had terrorized the neighborhood with threats and violence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intimidate - to frighten or threaten someone into doing something
  • Antonym: to reassure - to say or do something to remove someone's fears

Etymology

From French 'terroriser', from 'terreur' (terror), from Latin 'terror' (fear, dread).

Synset POS: v
