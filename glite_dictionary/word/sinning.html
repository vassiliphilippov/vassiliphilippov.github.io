sinning

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
Engaged in transgressing moral or divine law; committing sin.
committing sin

Examples

He confessed to being a sinning man in need of forgiveness.
The preacher warned the sinning congregation to repent their ways.
She felt guilty, believing herself to be a sinning soul.

Synset Examples

if it be a sin to covet honor, I am the most sinning soul alive

Related Terms

  • Antonym: virtuous - having or showing high moral standards
  • Synonym: wicked - evil or morally wrong

Etymology

Present participle of "sin," from Old English "syngian" (to sin).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01635278-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of committing a sin or a transgression considered against divine law or God's will
the act of committing a sin

Examples

He sought forgiveness after years of sinning and living a reckless life.
According to her beliefs, sinning leads to spiritual separation from God.
The preacher warned the congregation against sinning and encouraged them to repent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sin - an immoral act considered to be a transgression against divine law

Etymology

From Old English 'syngian' (to sin), from 'syn' (sin).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00758433-n
Synset POS: n
