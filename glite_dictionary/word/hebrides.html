Hebrides

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛb.rəˌdiz / (AmE), / ˈhɛb.rɪˌdiːz / (BrE)
a group of more than 500 islands off the western coast of Scotland, divided into the Inner and Outer Hebrides
island group off west coast of Scotland

Examples

We spent a week exploring the beautiful Hebrides during our trip to Scotland.
Many rare bird species can be found throughout the Hebrides.
The climate of the Hebrides is milder than on the Scottish mainland.

Etymology

From Latin 'Hebrides', of uncertain origin, possibly from a pre-Celtic language.
