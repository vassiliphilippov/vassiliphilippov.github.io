Selaginella lepidophylla

1. NOUN

/ səˌlædʒɪˈnɛlə lɛpɪˈdɒfɪlə / (AmE), / sɛˌlædʒɪˈnɛlə lɛpɪˈdɒfɪlə / (BrE)
A species of spikemoss native to arid regions from the southwestern United States to Peru, known for curling into a tight ball when dry and uncurling to grow when moisture is present.
a spikemoss; resurrection plant

Examples

Selaginella lepidophylla can survive for years without water, reviving quickly when it becomes moist.
The unique ability of Selaginella lepidophylla to curl into a ball has earned it the name 'resurrection plant.'
Botanists often study Selaginella lepidophylla to learn about drought resistance in plants.

Etymology

Genus name from Latin 'Selago' (mistletoe) + Greek 'lepis' (scale) and 'phyllon' (leaf).
