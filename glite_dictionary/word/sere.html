sere

1. ADJ

/ sɪr / (AmE), / sɪə / (BrE)
(especially of vegetation) dry or withered, having lost all moisture.
dry and withered (often vegetation)

Examples

The sere branches snapped easily under my feet as I walked through the autumn woods.
In late summer, the fields became sere after weeks without rain.
Only a few sere leaves clung to the otherwise bare tree.

Synset Examples

dried-up grass
the desert was edged with sere vegetation
shriveled leaves on the unwatered seedlings
withered vines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: withered - (of plants) dry and shriveled, often from lack of moisture
  • Antonym: lush - (of vegetation) rich and healthy, especially with lots of moisture

Etymology

From Old English *sēar* (dry, withered).
