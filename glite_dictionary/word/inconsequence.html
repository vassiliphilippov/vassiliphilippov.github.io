inconsequence

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnˈkɑːnsɪkwəns / (AmE), / ˌɪnˈkɒnsɪkwəns / (BrE)
the quality or state of having no important effects or influence; lack of significance or importance.
lack of importance or significance

Examples

The trivial matter was dismissed due to its inconsequence in the overall decision.
She worried about the inconsequence of her daily efforts, feeling they made no difference.
The film's inconsequence disappointed critics who expected a more impactful story.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insignificance - the quality of being unimportant
  • Antonym: consequence - the effect or result of an action or condition

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (not) + 'consequentia' (following).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnˈkɑːnsəkwəns / (AmE), / ˌɪnˈkɒnsɪkwəns / (BrE)
the state or quality of being illogical or lacking valid reasoning; a conclusion or argument that does not follow correctly from its premises.
invalid or illogical reasoning

Examples

The essay was criticized for its inconsequence and frequent logical gaps.
His argument descended into inconsequence when he ignored the main evidence.
A hasty conclusion often leads to inconsequence in one’s reasoning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illogicality - lack of logic or sound reasoning
  • Antonym: consequence - a result or effect following logically from something else

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (not) + 'consequentia' (sequence, consequence).
