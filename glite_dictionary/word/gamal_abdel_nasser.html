Gamal Abdel Nasser

1. PROPN

/ ɡəˈmɑːl ˌæbdɛl ˈnɑːsər / (AmE), / ɡəˈmɑːl ˌæbdɛl ˈnɑːsə / (BrE)
(1918–1970) Egyptian statesman and president, known for nationalizing the Suez Canal and leading Egypt from 1956 to 1970.
Egyptian president, nationalized Suez Canal

Examples

Gamal Abdel Nasser was a central figure in the Arab world during the mid-20th century.
The nationalization of the Suez Canal by Gamal Abdel Nasser led to an international crisis in 1956.
Many Egyptians still admire Gamal Abdel Nasser for his vision of Arab unity and independence.
