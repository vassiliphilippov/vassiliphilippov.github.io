Chang Kuo-lao

1. PROPN

/ tʃɑŋ ˌɡwoʊ ˈlaʊ / (AmE), / tʃɑːŋ ˌɡwəʊ ˈlaʊ / (BrE)
One of the Eight Immortals in Taoist mythology, often depicted as a wise old man riding a donkey backwards.
one of the Eight Immortals in Taoism

Examples

Chang Kuo-lao is often shown riding a donkey backwards in traditional Chinese paintings.
The tales of the Eight Immortals, including Chang Kuo-lao, are well known in Chinese folklore.
Many temples in China have statues depicting Chang Kuo-lao as an old man with a bamboo drum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eight Immortals - legendary group of Taoist immortals

Etymology

From Chinese 常 (Cháng) 国老 (Guó-lǎo), meaning 'Chang, the Elder.'
