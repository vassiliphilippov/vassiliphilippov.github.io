Leo the Great

1. PROPN

/ ˌliːoʊ ðə ˈɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˌliːəʊ ðə ˈɡreɪt / (BrE)
(c. 400–461) Pope of the Roman Catholic Church, notable for extending papal authority in the West and persuading Attila the Hun not to attack Rome.
Pope and influential church leader (c. 400–461)

Examples

Many historians regard Leo the Great as one of the most influential early popes.
The intervention of Leo the Great prevented Attila the Hun from sacking Rome.
Leo the Great's writings shaped church doctrine for centuries.
