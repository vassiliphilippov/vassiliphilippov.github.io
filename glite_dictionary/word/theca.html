theca

1. NOUN

/ ˈθiːkə / (AmE), / ˈθiːkə / (BrE)
a sheath or case, especially the outer covering of the pupa of certain insects
outer sheath of an insect pupa

Examples

The butterfly's pupa is protected by a hard theca during metamorphosis.
Scientists studied the theca to understand how the insect develops within.
The removal of the theca revealed the next stage of the wasp's life cycle.

Etymology

From Greek thekē (case, box, sheath).

2. NOUN

/ ˈθiːkə / (AmE), / ˈθiːkə / (BrE)
A case or sheath, especially a pollen sac in plants or a capsule in mosses.
a case or sheath in biology
medical

Examples

Each anther contains two thecae, which are responsible for producing pollen.
The spore capsule of many mosses is known as the theca.
Damage to the theca can hinder proper development of pollen grains.

Etymology

From Greek thēkē (case, box).
