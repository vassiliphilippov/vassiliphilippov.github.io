General de Gaulle

1. PROPN

/ ʒəˈnɛrəl də ɡoʊl / (AmE), / dʒəˈnɛrəl də ˈɡəʊl / (BrE)
Charles de Gaulle (1890–1970), French general and statesman, leader of the Free French during World War II and later president of France.
French general and statesman (1890–1970)

Examples

During World War II, General de Gaulle led the Free French Forces in their resistance against Nazi Germany.
Many streets and airports in France are named after General de Gaulle to honor his legacy.
As president, General de Gaulle played a crucial role in shaping modern French politics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Charles de Gaulle - French general and statesman, same individual as General de Gaulle

Etymology

From French 'général' (general), title, and 'de Gaulle,' family name.
