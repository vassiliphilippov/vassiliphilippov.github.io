spiral

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪ.rəl / (AmE), / ˈspaɪ.rəl / (BrE)
a downward movement or flight in a helical path with a large radius, especially in the air
downward helical flight path

Examples

The hawk went into a spiral as it dived toward its prey.
The pilot executed a controlled spiral to lose altitude safely.
Smoke rose in a slow spiral as the wind began to swirl.

Etymology

From Latin 'spiralis' (coiled), from 'spira' (coil).

2. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪrəl / (AmE), / ˈspaɪərəl / (BrE)
a curve that winds around a fixed central point or axis and gradually moves away from or toward it, especially one that lies on the surface of a cylinder or cone and cuts the element at a constant angle
curve winding around an axis at a constant angle

Examples

The staircase in the old lighthouse has a beautiful spiral that winds to the top.
She drew a perfect spiral on the chalkboard during the geometry lesson.
The shell's spiral pattern fascinated the biologist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: helix - a type of smooth space curve, like a corkscrew or spiral staircase

Etymology

From Latin 'spiralis', from Greek 'speira' meaning coil.
