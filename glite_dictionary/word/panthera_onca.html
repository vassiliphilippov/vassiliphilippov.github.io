Panthera onca

1. NOUN

/ pænˈθɪrə ˈɑŋkə / (AmE), / pænˈθɪərə ˈɒŋkə / (BrE)
the scientific name for the jaguar, a large, spotted cat native to Central and South America, closely related to the leopard
jaguar's scientific name

Examples

The Panthera onca is the only big cat species native to the Americas.
Scientists monitor the habitat of Panthera onca to protect it from deforestation.
In zoos, Panthera onca is displayed alongside other large felines for educational purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jaguar - large, spotted cat native to the Americas

Etymology

From Latin: 'Panthera' (panther) + 'onca' (lynx/jaguar in Portuguese, from Tupi 'onça').
