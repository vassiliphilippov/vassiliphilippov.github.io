sarcophagus

1. NOUN

/ sɑɹˈkɑfəɡəs / (AmE), / sɑːˈkɒfəɡəs / (BrE)
a stone coffin, often decorated with sculpture or inscriptions, used especially in ancient times
stone coffin, often ancient and decorated

Examples

The ancient pharaoh was buried inside a massive sarcophagus carved from granite.
Archaeologists discovered a Roman sarcophagus covered in intricate reliefs.
Museum visitors admired the hieroglyphs on the sarcophagus displayed in the main gallery.

Etymology

From Latin 'sarcophagus', from Greek 'sarkophagos' (flesh-eating stone), originally describing limestone thought to decompose flesh quickly.
