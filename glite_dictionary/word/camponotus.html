Camponotus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæm.pəˈnoʊ.təs / (AmE), / ˌkæm.pəˈnəʊ.təs / (BrE)
A genus of large ants, known as carpenter ants, that nest in wood and can damage structures by excavating galleries.
genus of carpenter ants

Examples

Camponotus species are commonly found nesting in dead wood and tree trunks.
The infestation was identified as a colony of Camponotus damaging the wooden beams.
Many North American homes are vulnerable to Camponotus because these ants excavate wood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carpenter ant - large ant in the genus Camponotus that nests in wood

Etymology

From Greek 'kampē' (bend) and 'notos' (back), referring to the bent shape of some parts of the ant's body.
