shoal

1. NOUN

/ ʃoʊl / (AmE), / ʃəʊl / (BrE)
a large group of fish swimming together
large group of fish

Examples

A shoal of silver fish darted past the divers.
We observed a huge shoal moving gracefully through the clear water.
Predators often target the edges of a shoal.

Synset Examples

a school of small glittering fish swam by

Related Terms

  • Synonym: school - a group of fish swimming together

Etymology

From Middle English 'shole', of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ ʃoʊl / (AmE), / ʃəʊl / (BrE)
a shallow area in a body of water, especially a sandbank that is visible at low tide
sandbank visible at low tide

Examples

We avoided the shoal as we navigated the river in our boat.
At low tide, the shoal was teeming with birds searching for food.
The ship ran aground on a shoal just outside the harbor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandbank - a ridge of sand built up by currents in a river or sea

Etymology

From Old English 'sceald' (shallow), related to Middle Dutch 'schole' (shoal).

5. NOUN

/ ʃoʊl / (AmE), / ʃəʊl / (BrE)
an area of shallow water, especially one that is a navigational hazard for ships
area of shallow water

Examples

The boat ran aground on a hidden shoal near the coast.
A shoal formed at the mouth of the river, making navigation difficult for large vessels.
Fishermen often find plentiful fish around the edges of a shoal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandbank - a ridge of sand in shallow water, often exposed at low tide
  • Synonym: reef - a ridge of rock, coral, or sand in a body of water, just above or below the surface
  • Antonym: deep - an area of deep water

Etymology

Probably from Middle Dutch *schole* (school of fish, shallow place), related to Old English *sceald* (shallow).
