lubricious

1. ADJ

/ luˈbrɪʃəs / (AmE), / ljuːˈbrɪʃəs / (BrE)
having a smooth, slippery, or oily surface or quality
smooth and slippery

Examples

The chef handled the lubricious eel with care as it slipped across the cutting board.
The road became lubricious after the rain, making driving hazardous.
The lubricious surface of the new slide made it especially popular with the children.

Synset Examples

the skin of cephalopods is thin and lubricious

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slippery - difficult to hold or stand on because smooth, wet, or slimy

Etymology

From Latin lubricus (slippery) + -ious.

2. ADJ

/ luˈbrɪʃəs / (AmE), / luːˈbrɪʃəs / (BrE)
(formal) displaying or intended to arouse sexual desire; lewd or lustful.
sexually lustful or lewd

Examples

The novel was banned for its lubricious scenes and explicit language.
His lubricious remarks made everyone at the meeting uncomfortable.
Critics denounced the film as a lubricious attempt to provoke controversy.

Synset Examples

eluding lubricious embraces
prurient literature
prurient thoughts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lewd - showing or intended to arouse sexual desire
  • Synonym: prurient - having or encouraging an excessive interest in sexual matters
  • Antonym: modest - not sexual or indecent; showing reserve in sexual matters

Etymology

From Latin *lubricus* (slippery, wanton) + English suffix -ious.
