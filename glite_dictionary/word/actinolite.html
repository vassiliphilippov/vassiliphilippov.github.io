actinolite

1. NOUN

/ ækˈtɪnəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ækˈtɪnəlaɪt / (BrE)
a green mineral of the amphibole group, consisting of a calcium magnesium iron silicate
a green mineral; amphibole group

Examples

The geologist identified the shiny green crystals as actinolite under the microscope.
Many metamorphic rocks contain minerals such as hornblende, biotite, and actinolite.
Actinolite occurs in fibrous form in some asbestos deposits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amphibole - a group of inosilicate minerals forming prism or needle-like crystals

Etymology

From Greek 'aktis' (ray) and 'lithos' (stone), referring to its radiate crystal habit.
