whirlpool

1. VERB

/ ˈwɝːlˌpuːl / (AmE), / ˈwɜːlˌpuːl / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to move or flow in a rapid, circular motion like that of a whirlpool.
flow in a rapid, circular motion

Examples

The river would whirlpool around the rocks after a heavy rain.
Colored dye was poured into the water so we could watch it whirlpool down the drain.
As the bath drained, I watched the last of the soap bubbles whirlpool away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eddy - to move in a circular motion, especially of water or air

Etymology

From the noun 'whirlpool', originally Middle English 'whirle-poole', meaning a swirling body of water.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɜrˌpul / (AmE), / ˈwɜːlpuːl / (BrE)
a powerful circular current of water, especially formed by opposing tides or currents, drawing objects towards its center
powerful circular water current

Examples

The boat was pulled into the whirlpool and spun around rapidly by the current.
Swimmers need to beware of the natural whirlpool near the base of the falls.
A sudden whirlpool formed where the two rivers met, creating a dangerous situation for kayakers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vortex - a mass of whirling fluid or air, especially a whirlpool or whirlwind
  • Synonym: eddy - a circular movement of water causing a small whirlpool

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *werlpool*, from *wervelen* (to whirl) + *poel* (pool).
