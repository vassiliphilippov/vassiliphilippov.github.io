impolitely

1. ADV

/ ˌɪm.pəˈlaɪt.li / (AmE), / ˌɪm.pəˈlaɪt.li / (BrE)
In a way that shows a lack of respect or good manners; rudely.
in a rude or disrespectful way

Examples

He spoke impolitely to the waiter, making everyone uncomfortable.
She impolitely interrupted the speaker during the presentation.
Children should be taught not to behave impolitely towards their elders.

Synset Examples

he treated her impolitely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rudely - in a rude or disrespectful manner
  • Antonym: politely - in a polite or courteous manner

Etymology

From "impolite" + "-ly", where "impolite" is from Latin "impolitus" (unpolished, rude).
