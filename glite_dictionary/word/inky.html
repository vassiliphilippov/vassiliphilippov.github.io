inky

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪŋki / (AmE), / ˈɪŋki / (BrE)
Very dark or black in color, like black ink.
very dark or black in color

Examples

The sea looked inky under the night sky, reflecting no light at all.
She wore an inky dress that shimmered in the darkness.
The artist dipped his brush into the inky paint before starting his masterpiece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black - having the darkest color; the opposite of white
  • Antonym: white - having the lightest color; the opposite of black

Etymology

From 'ink' + '-y', forming an adjective from the noun 'ink'.
