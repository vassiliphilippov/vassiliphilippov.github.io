salinometer

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɪn.əˌmɪt̬.ɚ / (AmE), / sæˈlɪn.əˌmɪt.ə / (BrE)
an instrument used to determine the concentration of salt in a solution by measuring its density
instrument for measuring salt concentration

Examples

The scientist used a salinometer to check the salt levels in the seawater sample.
A salinometer is essential equipment on ships to monitor the salinity of boiler feedwater.
By using a salinometer, the technician ensured the brine solution had the correct concentration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrometer - an instrument for measuring the density of liquids

Etymology

From Latin 'salina' (salt works) + '-meter' (measuring device).
