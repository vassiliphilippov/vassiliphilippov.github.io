mafia

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.fi.ə / (AmE), / ˈmæf.i.ə / (BrE)
A group of people who are closely connected and act together, often to promote their own interests, sometimes excluding outsiders.
tightly knit group of associates

Examples

The academic mafia controls most of the prestigious research journals in the field.
There’s a small mafia that runs the local art scene and keeps newcomers at bay.
He felt that an unofficial mafia of senior staff made all the important decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clique - a small, close group of people, especially one that does not welcome outsiders
  • Synonym: cabal - a secret political clique or faction

Etymology

From Italian 'mafia,' referring originally to the Sicilian criminal organization.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.fi.ə / (AmE), / ˈmæf.i.ə / (BrE)
A closely connected group of people with significant control or influence, often seen as operating in a secretive or exclusive manner.
exclusive influential group

Examples

The research mafia controls access to the most prestigious journals.
She felt excluded by the old school mafia in the company.
A literary mafia dominated the awards for years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabal - a secret political clique or faction
  • Synonym: clique - a small, exclusive group of people

Etymology

From Italian *mafia*, originally referring to a criminal organization in Sicily.
