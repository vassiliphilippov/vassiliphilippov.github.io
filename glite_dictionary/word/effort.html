effort

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛfərt / (AmE), / ˈefət / (BrE)
earnest and determined attempt to accomplish a goal or task; the use of physical or mental energy to do something
earnest attempt to accomplish something

Examples

She made a tremendous effort to finish the project on time.
Winning the championship was the result of years of effort and dedication.
Even a small effort can make a big difference in someone's life.

Synset Examples

made an effort to cover all the reading material
wished him luck in his endeavor
she gave it a good try

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endeavor - an attempt to achieve something, especially a long or difficult one
  • Synonym: try - an attempt or effort to do something
  • Antonym: idleness - the state of being inactive or not making any effort

Etymology

From Old French 'esfort', from Vulgar Latin *exfortiare, based on Latin 'ex-' (out) + 'fortis' (strong).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛfərt / (AmE), / ˈɛfət / (BrE)
a work or achievement that is particularly notable, especially as a result of considerable exertion
notable achievement; accomplishment

Examples

Her new sculpture is widely regarded as her greatest effort.
Winning the championship was an extraordinary effort by the team.
This novel is a remarkable effort that redefined modern fiction.

Synset Examples

he performed a great feat
the book was her finest effort

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feat - a remarkable or impressive achievement
  • Synonym: accomplishment - something that has been achieved successfully

Etymology

From Old French *esforz* (effort, force, strength), from the verb *esforcier* (to force, exert oneself).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛfərt / (AmE), / ˈɛfət / (BrE)
an organized activity or series of actions aimed at achieving a particular objective or supporting a cause
organized activity toward a goal

Examples

The relief effort after the earthquake involved hundreds of volunteers and organizations.
Their fundraising efforts resulted in enough money to build a new community center.
Environmental efforts have led to cleaner air and water in many cities.

Synset Examples

the movement to end slavery
contributed to the war effort

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campaign - a planned series of activities to achieve a particular result
  • Synonym: movement - a group of people working together to advance an idea or cause

Etymology

From Old French *esfort* (force, exertion), from Latin *exfortis* (out of strong).

4. NOUN

/ ˈɛfɚt / (AmE), / ˈɛfət / (BrE)
The physical or mental energy needed to do something; hard work.
physical or mental energy; hard work

Examples

Winning the championship took a lot of effort from the entire team.
Despite her best effort, she could not solve the complex puzzle.
The project was a group effort that demanded everyone's dedication.

Synset Examples

he got an A for effort
they managed only with great exertion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exertion - vigorous physical or mental effort
  • Antonym: laziness - the quality of being unwilling to work or use energy

Etymology

From French *effort*, from Old French *esforz*, from *esforcier* (to force, exert oneself), ultimately from Latin *efforciare*.
