allotrope

1. NOUN

/ ˈæləˌtroʊp / (AmE), / ˈalətrəʊp / (BrE)
a form of a chemical element that has a different physical structure or molecular arrangement from other forms of the same element
structurally different form of an element
medical

Examples

Diamond is an allotrope of carbon that has a crystalline structure.
Oxygen gas (O2) and ozone (O3) are two allotropes of the element oxygen.
Scientists study each allotrope to understand its unique properties.

Synset Examples

graphite and diamond are allotropes of carbon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isotope - forms of the same element with different numbers of neutrons

Etymology

From Greek 'allo-' (other) + 'tropos' (turn, way), meaning 'different form.'
