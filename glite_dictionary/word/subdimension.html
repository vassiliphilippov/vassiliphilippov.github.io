subdimension

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌb.dɪˈmɛn.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsʌb.dɪˈmɛn.ʃən / (BrE)
a component or secondary aspect that forms part of an overall dimension, especially in technical or academic contexts
a component part of a dimension

Examples

Each personality trait was measured along several subdimensions to capture its complexity.
Researchers identified a key subdimension that contributed most to overall job satisfaction.
The model divides intelligence into multiple subdimensions for a more nuanced analysis.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: dimension - a measurable extent or aspect of a phenomenon

Etymology

Formed from Latin prefix 'sub-' (under, below) and 'dimension' (from Latin 'dimensio','a measuring')
