angiosperm

1. NOUN

/ ˈændʒiəˌspɜrm / (AmE), / ˈan(d)ʒɪəˌspɜːm / (BrE)
a plant belonging to the group characterized by having seeds enclosed within an ovary, commonly known as flowering plants
flowering plant with seeds enclosed in an ovary
medical

Examples

The rose is an example of an angiosperm because its seeds are enclosed within fruit.
Most plants in your garden are angiosperms, producing flowers during their life cycle.
Scientists discovered a new species of angiosperm growing in the rainforest.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gymnosperm - plant with unenclosed or 'naked' seeds

Etymology

From Greek angeion (vessel) + sperma (seed), meaning 'vessel seed'.
