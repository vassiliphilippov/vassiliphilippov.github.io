Asclepias meadii

1. NOUN

/ əˈskliːpiəs ˈmiːdiːaɪ / (AmE), / əˈskliːpɪəs ˈmiːdiːaɪ / (BrE)
A species of milkweed native to central North America, considered threatened due to habitat loss.
a threatened North American milkweed species

Examples

The prairies are home to the rare Asclepias meadii, which plays a crucial role in supporting pollinators.
Conservationists are working hard to restore habitats where Asclepias meadii once thrived.
Loss of native grasslands has led to a significant decline in Asclepias meadii populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milkweed - any plant of the genus Asclepias

Etymology

From Latin genus name Asclepias, after Asclepius, the Greek god of healing, and specific epithet meadii, honoring botanist Samuel Barnum Mead.
