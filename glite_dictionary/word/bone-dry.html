bone-dry

1. ADJ

/ boʊn ˈdraɪ / (AmE), / bəʊn ˈdraɪ / (BrE)
Completely dry; lacking any trace of moisture.
completely dry, no moisture

Examples

After hours in the sun, the towels were bone-dry and warm to the touch.
Make sure the wood is bone-dry before you use it for building a fire.
The air in the desert felt bone-dry, making it hard to stay hydrated.

Synset Examples

bone-dry leaves are a fire hazard
a drier to get the clothes bone dry

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dry - free from moisture or liquid; not wet or moist
  • Degree Higher: parched - extremely dry, especially because of heat

2. ADJ

/ boʊnˈdraɪ / (AmE), / bəʊnˈdraɪ / (BrE)
completely dry; lacking any moisture at all.
completely dry; without moisture

Examples

After weeks without rain, the fields were bone-dry and cracked.
He opened the oven and found the turkey was bone-dry inside.
She took a sip from her water bottle, but it was bone-dry after the long hike.

Synset Examples

The desert air felt bone-dry and unforgiving.
Her humor was bone-dry, leaving everyone puzzled.
The river was bone-dry after the summer heat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parched - very dry, especially from heat
  • Antonym: soaking - extremely wet

Etymology

From 'bone' (the dry solid tissue of the skeleton) + 'dry'.
