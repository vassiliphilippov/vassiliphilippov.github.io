contractor

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtræktər / (AmE), / kənˈtræktə / (BrE)
(in contract bridge) the player who wins the bidding and declares which suit is to be trumps.
the player who declares trumps in bridge

Examples

After the auction, the contractor announced that spades would be trumps.
As the contractor, she planned her play carefully to make the contract.
The contractor must fulfil the contract bid during the play.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: declarer - the player who plays the hand for their side in bridge

2. NOUN

/ kənˈtræktər / (AmE), / kənˈtræktə / (BrE)
a person or company that enters into a contract to provide goods or services; a party to a contract, especially in legal contexts
party to a contract
legal

Examples

The contractor must fulfill all obligations specified in the agreement.
Before starting the project, the contractor and the client signed a detailed contract.
If the contractor breaches the contract, they may be liable for damages.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: client - the recipient in a contractual agreement

Etymology

From contract + -or, originally from Latin contrahere (to draw together, to make a contract).

3. NOUN

/ kənˈtræktər / (AmE), / kənˈtræktə / (BrE)
a person or company that undertakes a contract to provide materials or labor to perform a service or do a job, especially in construction
person or company hired to build or perform a service

Examples

The homeowner hired a contractor to renovate the kitchen and bathroom.
That company is a well-known contractor specializing in commercial construction projects.
As a general contractor, she manages several crews on each job site.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: subcontractor - a person or company hired by a contractor to fulfill part of a contract
  • Synonym: builder - a person who constructs buildings

Etymology

From Latin 'contractus', past participle of 'contrahere' (to draw together, to make a contract).
