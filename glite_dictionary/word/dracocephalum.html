Dracocephalum

1. NOUN

/ ˌdreɪkoʊˈkɛfələm / (AmE), / ˌdrækəʊˈsɛfæləm / (BrE)
A genus of American herbs and dwarf shrubs in the mint family, commonly known as dragonheads.
genus of herbs/shrubs (dragonheads) in the mint family

Examples

Several species in the genus Dracocephalum are prized for their ornamental flowers.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Dracocephalum in different climates.
The aromatic leaves of Dracocephalum attract bees and butterflies to gardens.

Etymology

From Greek drakōn (dragon) + kephalē (head), referring to the flower shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdreɪkoʊˈkɛfələm / (AmE), / ˌdrækəʊˈkɛfələm / (BrE)
A genus of American herbs and dwarf shrubs in the mint family, commonly known as dragonheads.
genus of herbs; dragonheads

Examples

Several species in the Dracocephalum genus are prized for their ornamental flowers.
The Dracocephalum plants are often called dragonheads due to the shape of their blooms.
Botanists have identified new varieties within the Dracocephalum genus in North America.

Etymology

From Latin Dracocephalum, from Greek drakōkephalos, meaning 'dragon-headed'.
