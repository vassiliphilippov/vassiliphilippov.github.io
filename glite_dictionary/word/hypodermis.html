hypodermis

1. NOUN

/ haɪpəˈdɜrmɪs / (AmE), / haɪpəˈdɜːmɪs / (BrE)
A layer of cells beneath the outer covering of arthropods that secretes the chitinous cuticle.
cell layer in arthropods forming the cuticle
medical

Examples

The hypodermis secretes the tough exoskeleton that protects insects.
Damage to the hypodermis can affect an arthropod's ability to molt properly.
Researchers studied the role of the hypodermis in the development of new cuticle layers.

Etymology

From Greek 'hypo-' (under) + 'derma' (skin).
