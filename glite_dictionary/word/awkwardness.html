awkwardness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːkwərdnəs / (AmE), / ˈɔːkwədnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being ungainly or lacking grace in movement or posture
lack of grace or ease in movement

Examples

Her awkwardness on the dance floor was obvious to everyone watching.
He tried to hide his awkwardness by moving slowly and carefully across the room.
Teenagers often struggle with physical awkwardness as their bodies change and grow.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gracefulness - the quality of moving in a smooth, attractive, or elegant way

Etymology

From 'awkward' + '-ness', originally from Middle English 'awk' (backward, wrong way).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːkwərdnəs / (AmE), / ˈɔːkwədnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being embarrassing, uncomfortable, or lacking in social grace.
embarrassing or uncomfortable situation

Examples

There was a noticeable awkwardness in the room after he made his comment.
She tried to laugh off the awkwardness when she realized her mistake.
The silence grew, amplifying the awkwardness between the two friends.

Synset Examples

he sensed the awkwardness of his proposal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embarrassment - the state of feeling awkward or uncomfortable
  • Antonym: ease - the state of being comfortable and relaxed

Etymology

From 'awkward' + '-ness', where 'awkward' is from Middle English 'awkeward' (turned the wrong way).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɔːkwərdnəs / (AmE), / ˈɔːkwədnəs / (BrE)
difficulty in carrying or managing something due to its large size, shape, or weight.
difficulty caused by size or shape

Examples

The awkwardness of the large sofa made it difficult to carry upstairs.
They struggled with the awkwardness of the heavy box as they passed through the doorway.
Despite its beauty, the awkwardness of the sculpture made it hard to move without assistance.

Synset Examples

the movers cursed the unwieldiness of the big piano

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unwieldiness - the fact of being difficult to handle because of size or shape

Etymology

From awkward + -ness, where 'awkward' comes from Middle English awk (turned the wrong way) + -ward.

4. NOUN

/ ˈɔːkwərd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɔːkwəd.nəs / (BrE)
unskillfulness or lack of dexterity, often due to a lack of experience or training

Examples

There was a certain awkwardness in the way he handled the paintbrush for the first time.
The awkwardness of the new dancers was obvious during their first performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clumsiness - lack of physical coordination or skill
  • Antonym: gracefulness - elegance or skill in movement

Etymology

From awkward + -ness; awkward from Middle English awk 'turned the wrong way' + -ward.
