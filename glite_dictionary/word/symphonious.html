symphonious

1. ADJ

/ sɪmˈfoʊniəs / (AmE), / sɪmˈfəʊniəs / (BrE)
having a harmonious or pleasing sound; characterized by harmony
having a harmonious sound

Examples

The symphonious melodies of the orchestra filled the concert hall with warmth.
Her voice was unusually symphonious, calming everyone around her.
In the forest, the symphonious chorus of birds greeted the morning sun.

Synset Examples

the symphonic hum of a million insects

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harmonious - having a pleasant and consistent arrangement of sounds
  • Antonym: discordant - lacking harmony or agreement in sound

Etymology

From Greek 'symphōnia' (agreement or concord of sound) via Late Latin 'symphonia'.
