debit

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛbɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛbɪt / (BrE)
An entry on the left side of an account in bookkeeping, representing an amount owed or the deduction of funds.
accounting entry for money owed or deducted

Examples

The accountant recorded a debit of $500 to the company's cash account.
Each debit must be balanced by a corresponding credit in double-entry bookkeeping.
My bank statement showed a debit for the online purchase.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: credit - an accounting entry recording a sum received or added

Etymology

From Latin 'debitum' (something owed).

2. VERB

/ ˈdɛbɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛbɪt / (BrE)
to record or charge an amount of money to the debit side of an account
record an amount as a debit

Examples

The bank will debit your account for the monthly subscription fee.
She debited the amount to the correct ledger as required.
Before processing the payment, please ensure you have sufficient funds to debit the account.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: credit - to record or enter as credit in an account

Etymology

From Latin 'debitum' (something owed), via Old French 'debiter' and Middle English.
