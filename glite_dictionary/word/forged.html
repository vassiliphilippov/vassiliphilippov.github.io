forged

1. ADJ

/ fɔrdʒd / (AmE), / fɔːdʒd / (BrE)
copied or produced fraudulently, especially in order to deceive
fraudulently reproduced or copied

Examples

The art expert quickly spotted the forged painting among the originals.
He was arrested for using a forged passport at the border.
Several employees were found to have submitted forged documents to obtain benefits.

Synset Examples

like a bad penny …
a forged twenty dollar bill

Related Terms

  • Antonym: authentic - genuine; not a copy or forgery

Etymology

From Old French *forgier* (to forge), from Latin *fabricare* (to make).
