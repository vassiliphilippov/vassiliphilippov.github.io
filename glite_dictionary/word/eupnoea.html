eupnoea

1. NOUN

/ juːpˈniːə / (AmE), / juːpˈnɪə / (BrE)
normal and unlabored breathing, especially as opposed to abnormal or difficult breathing
normal, relaxed breathing
medical

Examples

During sleep, healthy individuals usually experience eupnoea, allowing the body to rest and repair.
The patient's eupnoea was noted after the successful removal of the airway obstruction.
In medical assessment, eupnoea can indicate normal lung and heart function.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dyspnoea - difficult or labored breathing

Etymology

From Greek 'eupnoia', from 'eu-' (well) + 'pnoē' (breath).
