uncompensated

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˈkɑːm.pənˌseɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈkɒm.pənˌseɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of a job or position) not receiving payment or a salary for work done.
not receiving payment or salary

Examples

He accepted an uncompensated position on the board because he cared about the charity's mission.
Many internships in the past were uncompensated, but there is now growing criticism of this practice.
Serving as an uncompensated advisor gave her valuable experience despite the lack of a paycheck.

Synset Examples

an uncompensated federal post

Related Terms

  • Antonym: paid - receiving payment for work

Etymology

From un- (not) + compensate + -ed.
