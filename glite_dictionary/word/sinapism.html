sinapism

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈneɪ.pɪzəm / (AmE), / sɪˈneɪ.pɪzəm / (BrE)
A plaster containing powdered black mustard, applied to the skin as a counterirritant or rubefacient.
mustard plaster used as a counterirritant
medical archaic

Examples

In the past, doctors often applied a sinapism to relieve chest congestion.
The nurse prepared a sinapism to treat the patient’s aching muscles.
A sinapism was traditionally used to draw blood to the surface and ease pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mustard plaster - A traditional remedy using mustard paste on cloth applied to the skin

Etymology

From Latin 'sinapismus', from Greek 'sinapismos', from 'sinapi' meaning 'mustard'.
