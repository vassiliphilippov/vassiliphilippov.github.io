Penstemon davidsonii

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛnstəˌmɒn ˌdævɪdˈsoʊni.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌpɛnstɪˌmɒn ˌdævɪdˈsəʊni.aɪ / (BrE)
A mat-forming perennial plant with blue and lavender flowers, native to regions from British Columbia to northern California.
species of flowering plant native to western North America

Examples

Penstemon davidsonii is often found growing in rocky alpine environments.
Gardeners value Penstemon davidsonii for its vibrant blue and lavender blooms.
The distribution of Penstemon davidsonii ranges from British Columbia to northern California.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'pente' (five) and 'stemon' (stamen); species named after botanist George Davidson.
