gleaner

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡliːnər / (AmE), / ˈɡliːnə / (BrE)
a person who collects leftover grain or crops from fields after the main harvest has been gathered
person collecting leftover crops after harvest

Examples

During the afternoon, several gleaners combed the fields looking for stray ears of wheat.
The painting depicted gleaners bending low to gather the grain left behind.
In some rural communities, gleaner groups are allowed to enter fields after harvest to collect food for the needy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harvester - person who gathers crops at harvest

Etymology

From Old French *glener* (to glean) and Latin *glennare* (to glean).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡliːnər / (AmE), / ˈɡliːnə / (BrE)
a person who gathers or collects things, especially information or resources, bit by bit and with care
one who gathers carefully bit by bit

Examples

As a gleaner of historical facts, she left no detail overlooked.
The journalist was a patient gleaner of information, interviewing many sources for the story.
Early in the morning, a gleaner searched the field for any remaining grain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collector - a person who collects things

Etymology

From Old French glener, from Late Latin glennare (to glean).

Synset POS: n
