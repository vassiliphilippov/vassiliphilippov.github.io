aerogram

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈɛːrəˌɡram / (BrE)
A letter specially designed for sending by airmail, usually a lightweight sheet that folds into its own envelope.
letter sent by airmail

Examples

He sent an aerogram to his parents while traveling abroad.
In the past, sending an aerogram was the most affordable way to communicate internationally.
I found an old aerogram in my grandmother's collection of letters.

Etymology

From French 'aérogramme', from Greek 'aēr' (air) + 'gramma' (letter).
