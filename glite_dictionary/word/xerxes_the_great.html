Xerxes the Great

1. PROPN

/ ˈzɜrksiːz ðə ɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˈzɜːksiːz ðə ɡreɪt / (BrE)
King of Persia (reigned 486–465 BC) who led a major invasion of Greece, winning at Thermopylae but ultimately defeated.
Persian king, led invasion of Greece

Examples

Historians still debate the strategic mistakes made by Xerxes the Great during his campaign against Greece.
Xerxes the Great is remembered for his ambitious construction projects as well as his military endeavors.
The story of Xerxes the Great and the battle at Thermopylae has inspired countless works of literature and film.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Darius I - Father of Xerxes the Great and previous king of Persia

Etymology

From Greek Xerxēs, from Old Persian Xšayār̥šā (ruling over heroes), Latinized as Xerxes.
