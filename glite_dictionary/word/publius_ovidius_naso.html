Publius Ovidius Naso

1. PROPN

/ ˈpʌbli.əs oʊˈvɪdi.əs ˈneɪsoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪ.əs əʊˈvɪdi.əs ˈnɑːsəʊ / (BrE)
the Roman poet known as Ovid, celebrated for his elegiac poetry on love and mythology, active from 43 BC to AD 17
Roman poet known as Ovid

Examples

Many scholars regard Publius Ovidius Naso as one of the greatest poets of classical antiquity.
The works of Publius Ovidius Naso have influenced Western literature for centuries.
Students in the Latin class translated passages from Publius Ovidius Naso's Metamorphoses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ovid - the pen name of Publius Ovidius Naso, Roman poet

Etymology

Publius and Naso are Roman personal and family names; Ovidius is the Latin form of Ovid.
