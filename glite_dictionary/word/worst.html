worst

1. NOUN

/ wɜrst / (AmE), / wɜːst / (BrE)
the weakest effort or poorest achievement that a person can produce
poorest achievement or effort

Examples

Even on his worst, he outperformed most of his classmates.
That was the worst I've ever done in a competition.
She knew she had given her worst in the interview and regretted it immediately.

Synset Examples

it was the worst he had ever done on a test

Related Terms

  • Antonym: best - the most excellent effort or achievement one can produce

Etymology

From Old English 'wyrst', superlative of 'yfel' (bad).

/ wɝst / (AmE), / wɜːst / (BrE)
to defeat someone completely or thoroughly in a competition or conflict

archaic

Examples

The young challenger worsted the reigning champion in a stunning upset.
They were worsted in every debate throughout the competition.
No one expected the small startup to worst such a large corporation.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defeat - to win a victory over someone in a contest
  • Synonym: to overcome - to succeed in dealing with or gaining control over something difficult
  • Antonym: to lose - to be defeated in a contest or struggle

Etymology

From Old English 'wyrst', superlative of 'wiers' (bad). Verb use from the sense of making someone 'the worst' (i.e., outdo or defeat them).
