weighbridge

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪˌbrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈweɪ.brɪdʒ / (BrE)
A large platform scale, typically set flush with the surface of a road, used for weighing vehicles and their loads.
platform for weighing vehicles

Examples

The truck was directed onto the weighbridge to check its load before leaving the factory.
Every lorry must pass over the weighbridge at the entrance to the distribution center.
During inspections, officials use a weighbridge to ensure that vehicles do not exceed legal weight limits.

Etymology

From 'weigh' + 'bridge', referring to a platform (bridge) for weighing.
