John Luther Jones

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn ˈluːðɚ dʒoʊnz / (AmE), / dʒɒn ˈluːθə ˈdʒəʊnz / (BrE)
(1864–1900) United States railroad engineer, famously known as "Casey Jones," who died trying to stop his train from crashing into another, inspiring a well-known ballad.
famous U.S. railroad engineer, subject of a ballad

Examples

Many people know the legend of John Luther Jones as the heroic engineer Casey Jones.
A ballad immortalized the bravery of John Luther Jones after his tragic train accident.
The story of John Luther Jones has become a part of American folklore.
