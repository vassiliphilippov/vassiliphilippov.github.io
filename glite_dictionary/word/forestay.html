forestay

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔːrˌsteɪ / (AmE), / ˈfɒr.steɪ / (BrE)
A rope, wire, or rod running from the front of a ship's mast to the deck or bowsprit, used to support and control the mast.
supporting cable from mast to bow on a ship

Examples

The crew tightened the forestay to improve the sail's shape before the race.
A broken forestay can cause serious stability issues for the mast of a sailing yacht.
The rigging includes both a forestay and a backstay to keep the mast upright.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: backstay - a rope or wire from the mast to the stern, providing backward support

Etymology

From fore- (meaning 'front') + stay (meaning 'supporting rope or wire').
