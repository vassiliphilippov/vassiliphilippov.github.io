Mallotus

1. NOUN

/ məˈloʊtəs / (AmE), / məˈləʊtəs / (BrE)
A genus of small fish, including the capelin, found in northern seas.
genus of fish (capelin)

Examples

The Arctic seabird population relies heavily on the presence of Mallotus in the ecosystem.
Scientists observed spawning Mallotus near the coastal shelf last spring.
Researchers studied the diet of Mallotus to understand its role in marine food webs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capelin - a small fish found in northern waters, member of Mallotus genus

Etymology

From New Latin Mallotus, the genus name.
