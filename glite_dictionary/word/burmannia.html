Burmannia

1. NOUN

/ bɜːrˈmæniə / (AmE), / bɜːˈmænɪə / (BrE)
A genus of slender herbaceous plants found in warm regions, with scale-like leaves and flowers having a three-angled or three-winged perianth, belonging to the family Burmanniaceae.
genus of slender tropical herbs

Examples

Many species in the genus Burmannia are found in tropical wetlands and rainforests.
The distinctive three-angled perianth helps botanists identify Burmannia among other similar herbs.
Some Burmannia plants rely on fungi to obtain nutrients instead of photosynthesis.

Etymology

Named after Johannes Burman (1707–1779), Dutch botanist.
