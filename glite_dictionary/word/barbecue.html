barbecue

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːr.bɪ.kjuː / (AmE), / ˈbɑː.bɪ.kjuː / (BrE)
a metal rack for cooking meat or other food over hot charcoal, usually outdoors
a metal rack for outdoor cooking

Examples

We cooked steaks on the barbecue last weekend.
The barbecue was set up in the backyard for the family picnic.
Please clean the barbecue before we use it again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grill - a metal framework used for cooking food over fire

Etymology

From Spanish 'barbacoa', originally from Taíno 'barabicu' (sacred fire pit).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02793692-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːr.bɪ.kjuː / (AmE), / ˈbɑː.bɪ.kjuː / (BrE)
Meat that has been cooked on a grill or over an open fire, typically flavored with a highly seasoned sauce.
meat cooked with seasoned sauce on a grill

Examples

We served the barbecue with cornbread and coleslaw at the family picnic.
The aroma of spicy barbecue drifted through the backyard during the party.
She ordered a plate of smoked barbecue for lunch at the restaurant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barbecued meat - meat that has been cooked on a barbecue

Etymology

From Spanish 'barbacoa', possibly from Taino 'barabicu' meaning 'framework of sticks'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07669757-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈbɑːrbɪˌkjuː / (AmE), / ˈbɑːbɪˌkjuː / (BrE)
to cook food, especially meat, outdoors over an open fire or on a special grill
cook food outdoors over a grill

Examples

We decided to barbecue some chicken for the party last night.
They love to barbecue outdoors every weekend during summer.
She barbecued the steaks to perfection over charcoal.

Synset Examples

let's barbecue that meat
We cooked out in the forest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grill - to cook food over direct heat, usually on a grill

Etymology

From Spanish 'barbacoa', probably from Taíno, referring to a framework for smoking or drying meat.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00325607-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːr.bɪ.kjuː / (AmE), / ˈbɑː.bɪ.kjuː / (BrE)
an outdoor meal or gathering where food, especially meat, is cooked over an open fire or grill, often featuring whole animals roasted on a spit
outdoor meal with food cooked over fire

Examples

We hosted a barbecue in the backyard last weekend for our friends.
Every summer, the community organizes a huge barbecue featuring roasted lamb on a spit.
The smell of the barbecue drifted through the neighborhood, making everyone hungry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cookout - an outdoor meal or gathering where food is cooked over an open fire or grill

Etymology

From Spanish *barbacoa*, from Arawak (Taino) word meaning 'a framework of sticks for roasting meat.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07592346-n
Synset POS: n

5. VERB

/ ˈbɑːr.bɪ.kjuː / (AmE), / ˈbɑː.bɪ.kjuː / (BrE)
to cook food outdoors over an open fire or on a barbecue grill, often with a smoky flavor
cook food outdoors on a grill

Examples

We're planning to barbecue chicken and vegetables in the backyard this weekend.
They barbecued sausages over a wood fire at the campsite.
She enjoys barbecuing whenever the weather is warm and sunny.

Synset Examples

let's barbecue that meat
We cooked out in the forest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grill - to cook food over direct heat, usually on a grill or in an oven
  • Synonym: roast - to cook food, especially meat, in an oven or over a fire

Etymology

From Spanish 'barbacoa', which in turn comes from Taíno, referring to a framework of sticks for cooking meat over a fire.
