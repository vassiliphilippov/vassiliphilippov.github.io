capuchin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæp.jə.tʃɪn / (AmE), / ˈkap.jʊ.tʃɪn / (BrE)
A woman's cloak with a hood, especially popular in the 18th century.
woman's hooded cloak

Examples

The museum displayed an ornate capuchin from the 18th century.
She wore a velvet capuchin to protect herself from the cold night air.
The costume designer recreated a capuchin for the historical drama.

Etymology

From French 'capuchin', from Italian 'cappuccino', diminutive of 'cappuccio' (hood).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæp.ju.tʃɪn / (AmE), / ˈkæp.ju.tʃɪn / (BrE)
A small monkey of Central and South America, known for its thick hair on the head resembling a monk's cowl.
a small Central/South American monkey

Examples

The capuchin is known for its intelligence and ability to use tools in the wild.
At the zoo, we watched a playful capuchin swing from branch to branch.
Many documentaries feature the capuchin because of its expressive face and social behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monkey - a primate of the suborder Simian, typically with a long tail

Etymology

From French 'capucin', from Italian 'capuccino', referring to the Capuchin friars, because of the monkey's cowl-like head hair.
