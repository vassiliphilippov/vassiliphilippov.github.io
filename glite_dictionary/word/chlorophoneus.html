Chlorophoneus

1. NOUN

/ klɔːˌroʊfəˈniːəs / (AmE), / klɔːˌrəʊfəˈniːəs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the subfamily Malaconotinae, found in Africa, comprising bush-shrikes known for their colorful plumage.
genus of African bush-shrikes

Examples

The Chlorophoneus genus includes several brightly colored species native to sub-Saharan Africa.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Chlorophoneus genus.
Birdwatchers often hope to spot members of Chlorophoneus due to their distinctive calls.

Etymology

From Greek khlōros (green) + phōnē (sound), referring to the bird's call and color.

2. NOUN

/ kloʊˌroʊfəˈniːəs / (AmE), / klɒˌrəʊfəˈniːəs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Malaconotidae, commonly known as bushshrikes, found in sub-Saharan Africa.
genus of African bushshrikes (birds)

Examples

Several species in the Chlorophoneus genus are known for their distinctive vocalizations.
Ornithologists have recently reclassified some birds under the genus Chlorophoneus.
Birdwatchers in Africa often try to spot members of the Chlorophoneus genus.

Etymology

From Greek 'chloros' (green) and 'phōneus' (killer), referring to coloration and predatory nature.
