to delve

1. VERB

/ dɛlv / (AmE), / dɛlv / (BrE)
to dig, turn up, or loosen earth, especially with a spade or tool
dig or turn up earth

Examples

The archaeologists began to delve into the ground, searching for ancient artifacts.
Every spring, she would delve the garden to prepare it for planting.
Farmers must often delve the soil deeply to ensure healthy crops.

Synset Examples

Dig we must
turn over the soil for aeration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dig - to break up and move earth with a tool or machine

Etymology

From Old English 'delfan' (to dig).
