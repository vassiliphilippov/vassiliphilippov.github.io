de-iodinase

1. NOUN

/ diˈaɪ.ə.dɪˌneɪs / (AmE), / diˈaɪ.ə.dɪˌneɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that removes an iodine atom from a molecule, particularly from thyroid hormones
enzyme that removes iodine from molecules
medical

Examples

Researchers studied the role of de-iodinase in regulating thyroid hormone activity.
A deficiency in de-iodinase enzymes can disrupt normal metabolism.
The presence of de-iodinase in the liver helps control hormone levels in the body.

Etymology

de- (removal) + iodine + -ase (enzyme suffix)
