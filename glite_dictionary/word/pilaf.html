pilaf

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈlɑf / (AmE), / ˈpiːlɑːf / (BrE)
A dish of rice cooked in seasoned broth, often with onions, vegetables, and sometimes meat or seafood.
rice dish cooked in seasoned broth

Examples

She served chicken pilaf garnished with fresh herbs for dinner.
This restaurant is famous for its seafood pilaf recipe.
Vegetable pilaf makes a hearty yet light lunch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paella - Spanish rice dish often containing seafood, meat, and vegetables
  • Synonym: risotto - Italian rice dish cooked with broth to a creamy consistency

Etymology

From Turkish *pilav*, from Persian *pilāw*.
