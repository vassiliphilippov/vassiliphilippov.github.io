blackcock

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌkɑk / (AmE), / ˈblækˌkɒk / (BrE)
The male of the black grouse, a bird found in Europe and Asia known for its dark plumage and lyre-shaped tail.
male black grouse

Examples

The blackcock displays impressive courtship dances during the breeding season.
We spotted a blackcock on the edge of the moor at dawn.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the blackcock in Scotland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black grouse - a large Eurasian grouse with dark plumage

2. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌkɑk / (AmE), / ˈblækˌkɒk / (BrE)
the male of the black grouse, a large bird found in Europe and Asia, known for its dark plumage and distinctive courtship display
male black grouse

Examples

The woods were alive with the booming calls of the blackcock at dawn.
During the lek, a single blackcock may compete with several rivals for attention.
Hunters in Scotland often seek the elusive blackcock during the shooting season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black grouse - a large bird native to Europe and Asia, notable for its elaborate mating display

Etymology

From 'black' (color) + 'cock' (male bird).
