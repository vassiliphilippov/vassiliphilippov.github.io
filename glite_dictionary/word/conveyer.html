conveyer

1. NOUN

/ kənˈveɪ.ər / (AmE), / kənˈveɪ.ə / (BrE)
a person who carries or transmits something from one place, person, or group to another
person who carries or transmits

Examples

She was the conveyer of the committee's final decision to the staff.
As the conveyer of tragic news, he struggled to find the right words.
The conveyer of gifts smiled as she handed out presents to the children.

Synset Examples

the conveyer of good tidings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: messenger - a person who carries messages or documents
  • Synonym: bearer - a person who carries or brings something

Etymology

From convey + -er, denoting a person who conveys.

2. NOUN

/ kənˈveɪ.ər / (AmE), / kənˈveɪ.ə / (BrE)
a continuous moving belt used to transport objects, especially in a factory
a moving belt for transporting objects

Examples

The packages were loaded onto the conveyer and carried to the next station.
Workers stood beside the conveyer inspecting products as they moved by.
A malfunction in the conveyer halted production for nearly an hour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conveyor belt - a continuous moving band used to transport objects, especially in a factory

Etymology

From the verb 'convey', based on Middle English 'conveien', ultimately from Latin 'conviare' (to accompany). The agentive '-er' suffix denotes 'one that conveys'.
