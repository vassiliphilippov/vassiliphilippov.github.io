hangbird

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæŋ.bɝd / (AmE), / ˈhæŋ.bɜːd / (BrE)
an eastern subspecies of the northern oriole, a songbird known for its brightly colored plumage and hanging nest.
eastern subspecies of the northern oriole

Examples

The bright plumage of the hangbird made it easy to spot in the treetops.
A hangbird was busy weaving its nest from fine grasses and fibers.
Many birdwatchers are excited to see the arrival of the hangbird each spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oriole - a colorful songbird in the Old World and New World families

Etymology

From the bird's characteristic habit of hanging its nest from tree branches.
