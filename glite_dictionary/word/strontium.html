strontium

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɑn.ti.əm / (AmE), / ˈstrɒn.ti.əm / (BrE)
a soft, silver-white or yellowish metallic chemical element of the alkaline earth metal group, occurring naturally in minerals like celestite and strontianite
a soft metallic chemical element

Examples

The scientist analyzed the mineral sample for traces of strontium and other elements.
Modern fireworks often contain strontium compounds to produce a vivid red color.
Exposure to radioactive strontium can pose health risks if not properly controlled.

Etymology

From New Latin 'strontia' (strontianite), named after Strontian, a village in Scotland where it was first discovered, plus '-ium'.
