server

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːvər / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvə / (BrE)
A computer that manages access to resources, such as files or printers, for other computers on a network.
computer managing network resources

Examples

Our company upgraded its server to improve network performance and handle more users.
If the server crashes, employees lose access to shared files until it's fixed.
You need the correct password to connect to the database server.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: client - a computer or program that accesses services provided by a server

Etymology

From serve + -er, indicating something that serves; used in computing since the 1960s.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04182208-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːvɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvə / (BrE)
the player who serves to start a point in court games such as tennis or volleyball
player serving in a court game

Examples

In tennis, the server must stand behind the baseline before hitting the ball.
After losing a point, she switched roles and became the server for the next game.
The server delivered a powerful serve that scored an ace.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: receiver - the player who receives the serve in court games
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10602056-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːvɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to serve food and drinks to customers at their tables in a restaurant or similar establishment
person serving food in a restaurant

Examples

The server brought our drinks quickly and with a smile.
As a server, she often works double shifts at the busy bistro.
We thanked our server for the excellent service and left a generous tip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waiter - a man whose job is to serve customers at their tables in a restaurant
  • Synonym: waitress - a woman whose job is to serve customers at their tables in a restaurant

Etymology

From Latin 'servire' (to serve), via Old French 'servir' and Middle English.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːvər / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvə / (BrE)
a flat or shaped utensil used for serving food or drink, such as a cake server or pie server
utensil for serving food or drink

Examples

She used a server to neatly distribute slices of cake to the guests.
The silver server matched the rest of the cutlery on the table.
Please pass me the server so I can dish out the lasagna.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spatula - a broad, flat utensil used for lifting and turning food

Etymology

From Old French 'servir' (to serve), ultimately from Latin 'servire'.
