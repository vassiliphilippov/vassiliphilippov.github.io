Trichoglossus

1. NOUN

/ trɪkoʊˈɡlɑːsəs / (AmE), / trɪkəˈɡlɒsəs / (BrE)
A genus of brightly colored parrots known as lorikeets, native to Australasia and distinguished by their brush-tipped tongues for feeding on nectar.
genus of nectar-feeding lorikeets

Examples

The rainbow lorikeet, belonging to the genus Trichoglossus, is famous for its vibrant plumage.
Recent studies have examined how Trichoglossus species adapt to urban environments in Australia.
Conservationists are concerned about declining numbers of Trichoglossus in certain regions.

Etymology

From Greek 'tricho-' (hair) + 'glōssa' (tongue), referring to their brush-tipped tongues.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtrɪkoʊˈɡlɑːsəs / (AmE), / ˌtrɪkəˈɡlɒsəs / (BrE)
A genus of brightly colored parrots known as lorikeets, found mainly in Australasia and Southeast Asia.
a genus of lorikeets (parrots)

Examples

The rainbow lorikeet is a species within the genus Trichoglossus.
Scientists have discovered several new species belonging to Trichoglossus in recent years.
Members of the Trichoglossus genus are famous for their vivid coloration and playful behavior.

Etymology

From Greek 'tricho-' (hair) + 'glossa' (tongue), referring to their brush-tipped tongues.
