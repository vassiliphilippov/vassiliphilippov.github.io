takahe

1. NOUN

/ tɑːˈkɑːheɪ / (AmE), / tæˈkɑːheɪ / (BrE)
a large, flightless bird native to New Zealand, similar in appearance to gallinules, with bright blue and green plumage
flightless New Zealand bird

Examples

The takahe was once thought extinct until its rediscovery in 1948.
New Zealand conservationists have worked hard to protect the endangered takahe population.
We saw several takahes grazing in the Fiordland National Park.

Etymology

From Māori 'takake' or 'takahe', the native name for the bird.
