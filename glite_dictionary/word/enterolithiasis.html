enterolithiasis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.tə.roʊ.lɪˈθaɪ.ə.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛn.tə.rəʊ.lɪˈθaɪ.ə.sɪs / (BrE)
the formation or presence of calculi (stones) in the intestines
intestinal stones (medical condition)
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with enterolithiasis after abdominal imaging revealed intestinal stones.
Surgical intervention may be necessary in severe cases of enterolithiasis.
Chronic abdominal pain can sometimes be attributed to underlying enterolithiasis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gallstone - a hard deposit in the gallbladder
  • Synonym: urolithiasis - the formation of stones in the urinary tract

Etymology

From Greek 'enteron' (intestine) + 'lithos' (stone) + '-iasis' (diseased condition).
