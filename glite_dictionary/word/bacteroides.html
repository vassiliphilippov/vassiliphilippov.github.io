Bacteroides

1. NOUN

/ bækˈtɛrɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / bækˈtɛə.rɔɪ.diːz / (BrE)
A genus of Gram-negative, rod-shaped, anaerobic bacteria that do not produce endospores or pigment, commonly found in the intestines of humans and animals.
genus of gut bacteria
medical

Examples

Bacteroides species are among the most abundant bacteria in the human gut microbiome.
Researchers studied how Bacteroides help digest complex carbohydrates in the intestine.
An imbalance in Bacteroides populations can be linked to gastrointestinal diseases.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining 'bacterium' (bacterium) and Greek '-oeidēs' (resembling).
