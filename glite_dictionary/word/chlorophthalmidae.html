Chlorophthalmidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌklɔːrəfˌθælmɪˈdiː / (AmE), / ˌklɒrəfˌθælˌmɪˈdiː / (BrE)
A small family of marine fish with soft fins and large eyes, related to lizardfishes and typically found near the sea bottom.
family of deep-sea fish with large eyes

Examples

Members of the Chlorophthalmidae family are commonly known as greeneyes due to their distinctive coloration.
The biologist collected several Chlorophthalmidae specimens from the deep-sea expedition.
Chlorophthalmidae are often found on the continental slopes at considerable depths.

Etymology

From Greek 'chloros' (green) and 'ophthalmos' (eye), referring to the fish's distinctive eye color.

2. NOUN

/ klɔːrɔfˌθælmɪˈdiː / (AmE), / klɒrəfˌθælmɪˈdiː / (BrE)
A small family of soft-finned, bottom-dwelling fishes with large eyes, related to lizardfishes.
family of bottom-dwelling fishes with large eyes

Examples

The Chlorophthalmidae are distinguished by their large, reflective eyes specialized for life at great depths.
Several species in the Chlorophthalmidae family are studied for their adaptations to deep-sea environments.
Marine biologists recently discovered a new member of the Chlorophthalmidae in the Indian Ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lizardfish - a member of the fish family Synodontidae, related to Chlorophthalmidae

Etymology

From Greek 'chloros' (green) + 'ophthalmos' (eye), referring to the greenish color and prominent eyes of these fishes.
