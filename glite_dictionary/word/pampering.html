pampering

1. ADJ

/ ˈpæmpərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpæmpərɪŋ / (BrE)
indulging or gratifying someone's tastes, desires, or needs, often to an excessive or luxurious degree.
indulging or gratifying desires or needs

Examples

The hotel offers a pampering spa package to help guests unwind.
She enjoyed a pampering afternoon with massages and facials at the salon.
A pampering breakfast in bed made her birthday feel extra special.

Synset Examples

pampering parents often have spoilt children
a very pampering cruise experience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indulgent - showing a tendency to be overly generous or lenient
  • Synonym: spoiling - overindulging or giving someone too much of what they desire

Etymology

From the verb 'pamper', of uncertain origin, possibly imitative or of Dutch origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpæmpərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpæmpərɪŋ / (BrE)
the action or process of indulging someone's desires or giving them special treatment, often involving comfort or luxury
the act of indulging someone

Examples

A weekend at a luxury spa is the ultimate pampering experience.
She enjoys a bit of pampering after a long week at work.
The hotel offers pampering services such as massages and gourmet meals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indulgence - the act of allowing oneself or another to enjoy pleasure
  • Antonym: neglect - failure to care for or attend to someone

Etymology

From verb 'pamper' (to spoil by excessive indulgence), likely of Dutch origin, possibly from 'pamperen' (to cram with food).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01075165-n
Synset POS: n
