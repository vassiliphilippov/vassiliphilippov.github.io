Dolichos lablab

1. NOUN

/ doʊˈlaɪkəs læˈblæb / (AmE), / dəˈlɪkəs ˈlæblæb / (BrE)
A perennial climbing plant of the Old World tropics, with trifoliate leaves and racemes of fragrant purple flowers, followed by maroon pods containing edible seeds, cultivated as an ornamental and a vegetable, especially in the Indian subcontinent; also known as hyacinth bean.
tropical vine grown for edible seeds and ornament

Examples

In many Indian gardens, the Dolichos lablab is valued for both its beauty and edible beans.
The purple flowers and maroon pods of Dolichos lablab add color to the landscape.
Farmers often cultivate Dolichos lablab as a drought-resistant crop.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek *dolichos* (long), referring to the vine's growth, and possibly from Arabic or Sanskrit for the species epithet.
