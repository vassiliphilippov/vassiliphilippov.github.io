slum

1. VERB

/ slʌm / (AmE), / slʌm / (BrE)
(of someone from a higher social class) to spend time in impoverished areas or among people of lower socio-economic status, often out of curiosity or a sense of adventure, sometimes in a condescending or insensitive manner.
to visit poor areas out of curiosity or novelty

Examples

The wealthy tourists decided to slum in the city's rundown neighborhood for the evening.
She sometimes likes to slum by eating at fast-food restaurants instead of her usual fancy spots.
Critics accuse celebrities who slum at charity events of being patronizing.

Synset Examples

attending a motion picture show by the upper class was considered slumming in the early 20th century

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slum it - to put up with uncomfortable or less luxurious conditions, usually temporarily

2. NOUN

/ slʌm / (AmE), / slʌm / (BrE)
an area of a city where living conditions are very poor and where many people live in poverty
urban area with poor living conditions

Examples

The city government plans to renovate the old slum to provide better housing for its residents.
Many families living in the slum struggle to access clean water and electricity.
She grew up in a slum but went on to achieve great academic success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ghetto - part of a city, especially a slum area, occupied by a minority group or groups
  • Antonym: suburb - an outlying district of a city, especially a residential one (contrasts with slum)

Etymology

Probably from British dialect 'slum' meaning 'room' or 'back room'; origin uncertain, first used in 19th century.

3. NOUN

/ slʌm / (AmE), / slʌm / (BrE)
a densely populated urban area marked by poor housing, squalor, and social deprivation
run-down urban area with poor housing

Examples

Many children growing up in the slum face challenges impacting their education.
The government launched a program to improve conditions in the city's largest slum.
She dedicated her career to volunteering in slums around the world.

Synset Examples

Many families live in the slum with limited resources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ghetto - a part of a city in which members of a minority group live, especially because of social, legal, or economic pressure
  • Antonym: suburb - an outlying residential area of a city typically with better living conditions

Etymology

Probably from British English slang of uncertain origin, first attested early 19th century.
