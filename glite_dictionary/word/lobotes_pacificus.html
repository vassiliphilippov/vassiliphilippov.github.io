Lobotes pacificus

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈboʊtiːz pəˈsɪfɪkəs / (AmE), / ləʊˈbəʊtiːz pəˈsɪfɪkəs / (BrE)
A species of tripletail fish found in the Pacific Ocean.
a Pacific tripletail fish species

Examples

The Lobotes pacificus is commonly found in coastal waters of the eastern Pacific Ocean.
Fishermen often catch Lobotes pacificus when casting near floating debris.
Researchers are studying the migratory patterns of Lobotes pacificus to better understand its habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tripletail - a common name for fish of the genus Lobotes

Etymology

From Latin: 'Lobotes' (the genus), and 'pacificus' meaning 'of the Pacific'.
