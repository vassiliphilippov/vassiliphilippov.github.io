artificial

1. ADJ

/ ˌɑːrtɪˈfɪʃəl / (AmE), / ˌɑːtɪˈfɪʃəl / (BrE)
Made by human beings rather than occurring naturally, especially as a copy of something natural.
made by humans, not natural

Examples

The garden was filled with artificial flowers that looked almost real.
He prefers artificial light to natural sunlight for painting.
An artificial lake was created to supply water to the city.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: natural - existing in or caused by nature; not made by humans

Etymology

From Latin artificialis, from artificium (art, craft).

3. ADJ

/ ˌɑːr.tɪˈfɪʃ.əl / (AmE), / ˌɑː.tɪˈfɪʃ.əl / (BrE)
(of behaviour or style) not natural or spontaneous; contrived or affected, especially in a way that is overly formal.
affectedly formal or contrived

Examples

Her apology sounded so artificial that nobody believed she meant it.
He greeted us with an artificial smile that seemed forced and insincere.
Many people find formal dinners awkward because of the artificial atmosphere.

Synset Examples

that artificial humility that her husband hated
contrived coyness
a stilted letter of acknowledgment
when people try to correct their speech they develop a stilted pronunciation

Related Terms

  • Antonym: natural - not made or caused by humankind; genuine or spontaneous
  • Synonym: contrived - deliberately created rather than arising naturally; artificial
  • Synonym: affected - artificial, pretentious, or designed to impress

Etymology

From Latin artificialis (of art, not natural), from artificium (craftsmanship).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00074073-s
Synset POS: s
