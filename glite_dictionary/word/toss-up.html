toss-up

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑs ˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈtɒs ˌʌp / (BrE)
A situation in which the outcome is uncertain or either result is equally likely.
uncertain situation; equal chance outcome

Examples

It's a toss-up who will win the championship game this year.
Whether we'll get rain or sunshine tomorrow is really a toss-up.
The election was such a toss-up that the outcome surprised everyone.

Synset Examples

it's a toss-up whether he will win or lose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coin toss - the act of throwing a coin to decide something randomly
  • Antonym: certainty - something that is sure to happen

Etymology

Originally from the act of tossing a coin to decide between two outcomes.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɑs.ʌp / (AmE), / ˈtɒs.ʌp / (BrE)
a situation in which either of two outcomes is equally likely or uncertain; something unpredictable.
an unpredictable or uncertain outcome

Examples

It's a toss-up who will become the next chairman after the debates.
Whether the weather will clear up in time for the game is still a toss-up.
The election was such a toss-up that no one could confidently predict the winner.

Synset Examples

it's a toss-up whether he will win or lose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coin flip - the action of tossing a coin to decide between two possibilities
  • Antonym: certainty - state of being sure; absence of doubt or unpredictability

Etymology

From the idea of tossing a coin to decide an outcome, originating in 19th-century American English.
