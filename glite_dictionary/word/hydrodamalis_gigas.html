Hydrodamalis gigas

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdroʊdəˈmeɪləs ˈɡaɪɡəs / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪdrəʊdəˈmɑːlɪs ˈɡaɪɡəs / (BrE)
an extinct, very large aquatic mammal known as Steller's sea cow, once found near the Bering Sea
extinct large marine mammal (Steller's sea cow)

Examples

The extinction of Hydrodamalis gigas is considered one of the earliest documented cases of human-caused marine extinction.
Researchers discovered bones belonging to Hydrodamalis gigas on a remote Bering Sea island.
Unlike other sirenians, Hydrodamalis gigas had no true teeth, only keratinous plates for feeding on kelp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Steller's sea cow - common name for Hydrodamalis gigas, the extinct marine mammal

Etymology

From New Latin: genus Hydrodamalis (from Greek hydro- 'water' + damalis 'heifer, young cow') and Latin gigas 'giant'.
