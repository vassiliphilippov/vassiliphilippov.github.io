slough

1. NOUN

/ slʌf / (AmE), / slʌf / (BrE)
A mass of dead or necrotic tissue separated from living tissue, especially in a wound; a mortified or gangrenous part.
dead or necrotic tissue in a wound
medical

Examples

The doctor removed the slough from the patient's ulcer to promote healing.
A thick slough had formed over the infected wound, restricting recovery.
Debridement may be necessary when slough accumulates in chronic wounds.

2. NOUN

/ slʌf / (AmE), / slʌf / (BrE)
an outer layer or covering of an animal, such as skin or shell, that is shed or cast off
outer layer that is shed (e.g. snake skin)

Examples

The snake left its slough behind on the forest floor.
Some amphibians periodically shed their slough as they grow.
Finding the slough of a lizard is a sign that it lives nearby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shedding - the process of losing or discarding something, especially skin or outer covering

Etymology

From Old English *slōh*, related to the sense of shedding or separating.

3. VERB

/ slʌf / (AmE), / slʌf / (BrE)
(of an animal) to shed or cast off a layer of skin, hair, horn, or feathers, especially as part of a natural process of renewal or growth.
shed or cast off skin, hair, or feathers

Examples

Every spring, the snake will slough its old skin in one continuous piece.
As the wound began to heal, dead tissue started to slough off gradually.
During molting, birds slough their feathers to make way for fresh growth.

Synset Examples

our dog sheds every Spring

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shed - to lose or cast off hair, skin, leaves, etc., naturally

Etymology

From Old English *slōh* (a skin or husk stripped off).

4. NOUN

/ sluː, slaʊ / (AmE), / sluː, slʌf / (BrE)
A stagnant or slow-moving swamp or muddy area, often found as part of a bayou or low-lying region.
a stagnant swamp or marsh

Examples

The alligators watched from the slough as fishermen passed in their boats.
Heavy rains turned the field into a muddy slough that was difficult to cross.
Birds flocked to the slough each spring, drawn by the abundance of water and shelter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swamp - an area of low-lying, uncultivated ground where water collects
  • Synonym: bog - wet, spongy ground with decaying vegetation
  • Synonym: fen - a low and marshy or frequently flooded area of land

Etymology

From Old English *slōh* (muddy place), related to Middle Low German *slu* (marsh).

5. NOUN

/ slʌf / (AmE), / slʌf / (BrE)
a hollow or depression in the ground that is filled with mud or mire, often forming a swampy area
muddy hollow or swampy depression

Examples

The hikers struggled to cross the slough after heavy rains turned the ground to muck.
Wildlife is abundant near the slough, where the water pools in the muddy depression.
In spring, the slough floods, creating a temporary wetland for migrating birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swamp - an area of low-lying, uncultivated ground where water collects; a bog or marsh
  • Synonym: quagmire - a soft boggy area of land that gives way underfoot

Etymology

From Old English *slōh* (a muddy place or bog).
