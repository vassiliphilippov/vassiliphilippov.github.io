Pseudemys

1. NOUN

/ suːˈdɛmɪs / (AmE), / sjuːˈdɛmɪs / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater turtles known commonly as sliders, including red-bellied terrapins, native to North America.
a genus of freshwater turtles (sliders)

Examples

The river is home to several species of Pseudemys that bask on logs during the day.
Researchers are studying the population decline of Pseudemys in southeastern wetlands.
The red-bellied terrapin, a type of Pseudemys, is listed as a threatened species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slider - common name for turtles of the genus Pseudemys

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'pseudes' (false) and 'emys' (freshwater turtle).

2. NOUN

/ suːˈdɛmɪs / (AmE), / sjuːˈdɛmɪs / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater turtles commonly known as sliders or red-bellied terrapins, native to North America.
a genus of freshwater turtles

Examples

The freshwater turtle Pseudemys can be found in rivers and ponds throughout the southeastern United States.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the genus Pseudemys.
Many people keep Pseudemys as pets because of their colorful shells and calm nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slider - a common name for turtles of the genus Pseudemys and related genera
  • Synonym: red-bellied terrapin - a common name for some species in the genus Pseudemys

Etymology

From Greek 'pseudes' (false) and 'emys' (freshwater turtle).
