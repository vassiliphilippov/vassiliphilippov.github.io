monochromasy

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑː.nəˈkroʊ.mə.si / (AmE), / ˌmɒn.əˈkrəʊ.mə.si / (BrE)
A condition of complete color blindness in which colors can only be distinguished based on brightness, not hue.
complete color blindness
medical

Examples

People with monochromasy experience the world in shades of gray rather than in color.
Researchers developed special lenses to help those with monochromasy navigate daily life.
Because of his monochromasy, he couldn't tell the difference between red and green apples except by their brightness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color blindness - inability to distinguish certain colors

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) + 'chrōma' (color).
