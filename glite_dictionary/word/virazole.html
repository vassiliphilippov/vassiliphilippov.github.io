Virazole

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪrəˌzoʊl / (AmE), / ˈvɪərəˌzəʊl / (BrE)
a trade name for ribavirin, an inhaled antiviral medication used to treat serious virus infections
trade name for an antiviral drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Virazole to help treat the patient's severe respiratory infection.
Children hospitalized with RSV may receive Virazole therapy via inhalation.
Some patients report mild side effects when using Virazole for antiviral treatment.
