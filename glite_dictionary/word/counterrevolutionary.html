counterrevolutionary

1. ADJ

/ ˌkaʊntərˌrɛvəˈluʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntəˌrɛvəˈluːʃənəri / (BrE)
Opposed to or actively working against a revolution or revolutionary changes.
opposing a revolution

Examples

The government cracked down on counterrevolutionary activities during the unrest.
His counterrevolutionary stance made him unpopular with the revolutionary committee.
They were accused of spreading counterrevolutionary propaganda.

Synset Examples

ostracized for his counterrevolutionary tendencies

Related Terms

  • Antonym: revolutionary - favoring or supporting revolution

Etymology

From 'counter-' (against) + 'revolutionary', modeled after 'counterrevolution'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌkaʊntərˌrɛvəˈluːʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntəˌrɛvəˈluːʃənəri / (BrE)
Intended to oppose or reverse a political revolution; relating to a counterrevolution.
opposing a revolution

Examples

The new government enacted counterrevolutionary laws to suppress dissent.
Her views were considered dangerously counterrevolutionary by the ruling party.
He was accused of spreading counterrevolutionary propaganda after the regime change.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: revolutionary - supporting or engaged in revolution

Etymology

From 'counter-' (against) + 'revolutionary'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02856756-a

3. NOUN

/ ˌkaʊntərˌrɛvəˈluːʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntəˌrɛvəˈluːʃənəri / (BrE)
a person who seeks to overturn or reverse the changes introduced by a previous revolution
person opposing a previous revolution

Examples

The government arrested several suspected counterrevolutionaries after the coup attempt.
During the aftermath, former officials were branded as counterrevolutionaries and put on trial.
He was accused of being a counterrevolutionary for opposing the new regime.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: revolutionary - a person who supports or participates in a revolution

Etymology

From counter- (against) + revolutionary, modelled after revolution.
