treadmill

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛdˌmɪl / (AmE), / ˈtrɛd.mɪl / (BrE)
an exercise machine with a moving belt on which a person walks or runs in place
exercise machine for walking or running in place

Examples

Many people prefer to use a treadmill at the gym when the weather is bad.
After injuring her knee, she started walking on the treadmill for rehabilitation.
He set the treadmill to a steep incline for a more challenging workout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elliptical - an exercise machine for simulated stair climbing, walking, or running

Etymology

From 'tread' + 'mill'; originally referred to a mill powered by people treading steps.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛdˌmɪl / (AmE), / ˈtrɛd.mɪl / (BrE)
a type of mill driven by people or animals walking on a large wheel, belt, or set of steps, especially one used in the past to generate power or perform labor
mill powered by walking or climbing

Examples

In the 19th century, prisoners were often forced to work on the treadmill for hours as punishment.
The old treadmill in the museum shows how energy was generated by human labor.
Horses walked on the treadmill to power the farm's grain mill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mill - a machine for grinding grain or generating mechanical power

Etymology

From tread (to step) + mill (grinding device).

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛdˌmɪl / (AmE), / ˈtrɛd.mɪl / (BrE)
a job or situation that is boring, repetitive, and seems never-ending, involving routine drudgery and little freedom
monotonous, repetitive job or routine

Examples

After years in the same office treadmill, she longed for a more exciting career.
He felt trapped on a work treadmill, repeating the same tasks every day.
For many, daily life can sometimes feel like a never-ending treadmill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drudgery - hard, monotonous, and dull work
  • Synonym: routine - a regular way of doing things, often dull and repetitive
  • Antonym: variety - the quality of being different; the opposite of monotony

Etymology

Originally from the literal treadmill device used for punishment or labor; extended figuratively to monotonous work.
