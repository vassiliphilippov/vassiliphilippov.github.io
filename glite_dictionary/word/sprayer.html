sprayer

1. NOUN

/ ˈspreɪ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈspreɪ.ə / (BrE)
a worker whose job is to apply liquid in the form of a spray to a surface, such as with paint or pesticides
worker who applies liquids by spraying

Examples

The sprayer carefully coated the car with a flawless layer of paint.
All workers must wear protective gear while working as a sprayer in the field.
The company is hiring experienced sprayers for its agricultural division.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: painter - a worker whose job is to paint surfaces

2. NOUN

/ ˈspreɪ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈspreɪ.ə / (BrE)
a device or container for turning a liquid, such as water or perfume, into a fine mist or spray
device that sprays liquid as a mist

Examples

She filled the sprayer with water to mist her plants every morning.
The perfume sprayer released a fine, pleasant mist with each press.
After cleaning, he used a disinfectant sprayer to sanitize the kitchen surfaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atomizer - a device that turns liquid into a fine spray, especially used for perfume

Etymology

From 'spray' (to scatter liquid in fine particles) + '-er' (agent suffix).
