toleration

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɑːləˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtɒləˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
The willingness or practice of allowing people, beliefs, or practices different from one's own.
willingness to allow different beliefs or practices

Examples

Promoting toleration of diverse religious beliefs is essential in multicultural societies.
Her toleration of different lifestyles set an example for everyone in the community.
The school's policy encourages toleration rather than discrimination.

Synset Examples

all people should practice toleration and live together in peace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tolerance - the ability or willingness to tolerate the existence of opinions or behavior that one dislikes or disagrees with
  • Antonym: intolerance - unwillingness to accept views, beliefs, or behavior that differ from one's own

Etymology

From Latin 'toleratio', from 'tolerare' (to endure, to tolerate).

2. NOUN

/ ˌtɑːləˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtɒləˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
official acceptance of people or practices differing from one's own, especially recognition of the right to hold different religious or political views.
official acceptance of dissenting opinions

Examples

The principle of toleration was fundamental to the new constitution.
Religious toleration allowed people of different faiths to live together peacefully.
Debates about toleration shaped early modern European politics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tolerance - the ability or willingness to tolerate something, especially opinions or behavior that one does not agree with

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *toleratio* (endurance, forbearance), from Latin *tolerare* (to bear, endure).
