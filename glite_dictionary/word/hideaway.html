hideaway

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdəˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdəˌweɪ / (BrE)
a place where someone can go to be alone and not be found by other people
a secluded place for privacy or escape

Examples

He kept a hideaway in the mountains to escape city life.
The old cabin served as their secret hideaway during weekends.
Everyone needs a hideaway where they can relax and be themselves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retreat - a quiet place for relaxation or privacy
  • Synonym: sanctuary - a place of refuge or safety

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪ.dəˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ.dəˌweɪ / (BrE)
a place where someone can go to be alone or to avoid detection, often a remote or secret place used as a refuge
secret or remote place for hiding

Examples

The thieves escaped to their mountain hideaway after robbing the bank.
She bought a small cabin as a secret hideaway where she could write in peace.
Police found the fugitives' hideaway deep in the forest, far from any town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retreat - a place of refuge, seclusion, or privacy
  • Synonym: sanctuary - a place of refuge or safety
  • Synonym: refuge - a place offering protection or shelter

Etymology

From 'hide' + 'away', first recorded in the 19th century.
