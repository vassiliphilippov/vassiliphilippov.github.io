BLitt

1. NOUN

/ bi ˈlɪt / (AmE), / bi ˈlɪt / (BrE)
A bachelor's degree in literature, typically awarded by universities.
bachelor's degree in literature

Examples

After completing his BLitt, he decided to pursue postgraduate research in English poetry.
The university offers a BLitt for students interested in literature and critical theory.
She proudly listed her BLitt alongside her other academic qualifications on her CV.

Etymology

Abbreviation of Bachelor of Letters; 'Litt' from Latin 'litterarum' (of letters).
