guava

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡwɑvə / (AmE), / ˈɡwɑːvə / (BrE)
A small tropical tree native to Central and South America, widely grown for its sweet, round, yellow fruit.
tropical tree with sweet yellow fruit

Examples

The guava tree in her backyard produces fruit all year round.
He made a refreshing smoothie with fresh guava and pineapple.
At the market, they sold ripe guava alongside papayas and mangos.

Etymology

From Spanish 'guayaba', from Taíno 'guayabo'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡwɑvə / (AmE), / ˈɡwɑːvə / (BrE)
A tropical fruit with yellow skin and pink or white pulp, eaten fresh or used to make jellies and preserves.
tropical fruit with yellow skin and pulp

Examples

She sliced a ripe guava for breakfast and sprinkled it with a pinch of salt.
The market was filled with the sweet aroma of fresh guavas.
They used guava to make a delicious homemade jelly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: papaya - A tropical fruit with orange-colored flesh and black seeds

Etymology

From Spanish 'guayaba', from Taíno 'guayabo'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡwɑvə / (AmE), / ˈɡwɑːvə / (BrE)
A small tropical tree of the myrtle family, native to Central and South America, that bears edible yellowish or greenish fruit with sweet flesh.
tropical tree with edible fruit

Examples

The guava tree in our backyard is loaded with ripe fruit every summer.
She made a delicious jam from fresh guava.
Guava is a popular ingredient in many tropical juices and desserts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psidium - the botanical genus to which guava belongs

Etymology

From Spanish guayaba, from Arawak (probably Taíno) guayabo.
