Acer saccharinum

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪsər ˌsækəˈraɪnəm / (AmE), / ˈeɪsə ˌsakəˈraɪnəm / (BrE)
a North American maple tree with five-lobed leaves that are green above and silvery white below, valued for its wood and ornamental use; commonly known as the silver maple
a North American silver maple tree

Examples

The fast growth of the Acer saccharinum makes it popular for urban landscaping.
Leaves of the Acer saccharinum are easily recognized by their silvery undersides.
The wood of Acer saccharinum is often used for furniture and musical instruments, despite being somewhat brittle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silver maple - the common name for Acer saccharinum, a North American maple tree
  • Degree Lower: Acer rubrum - a related North American maple tree known as the red maple

Etymology

From Latin: 'Acer' meaning 'maple', and 'saccharinum' meaning 'sugary' or 'containing sugar', referring to the sap.
