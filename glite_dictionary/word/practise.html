to practise

1. VERB

/ ˈpræk.tɪs / (AmE), / ˈpræk.tɪs / (BrE)
to work in a particular profession, especially law or medicine
work professionally (e.g. in law)
british

Examples

After graduating, she returned to her hometown to practise law.
Doctors must be licensed to practise medicine in the UK.
He chose not to practise as a barrister after qualifying.

Synset Examples

practice law

Related Terms

  • Synonym: practice - to work in a profession, especially law or medicine (US spelling)

Etymology

From Old French 'practiser', from Medieval Latin 'practicare', from Greek 'praktikos' (practical).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02574587-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈpræk.tɪs / (AmE), / ˈpræk.tɪs / (BrE)
to do an activity or exercise repeatedly in order to improve one's skill at it
do something repeatedly to improve skill
british

Examples

You should practise every day if you want to master the guitar.
Before the big game, the team practised their passing and shooting skills for hours.
She spends an hour each morning practising her French pronunciation.

Synset Examples

We drilled French verbs every day
Pianists practice scales

Related Terms

  • Synonym: practice - to do something repeatedly to improve skill (American English spelling)

Etymology

From Old French *practiser*, from Medieval Latin *practicare* (to do, perform), from Greek *praktikos* (practical).

3. VERB

/ ˈpræk.tɪs / (AmE), / ˈpræk.tɪs / (BrE)
to do an activity or exercise repeatedly or regularly in order to improve at it, especially a skill or performance
do something repeatedly to improve skill
british

Examples

She has to practise the piano for at least an hour every day.
The actors practised their lines before the show started.
If you want to get better at chess, you need to practise regularly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: practice - to do something repeatedly to get better at it; American English spelling
  • Synonym: to rehearse - to practise an activity, especially for a performance

Etymology

From Old French pratiquer, from Medieval Latin practicare, from Late Latin practicus (practical), from Greek praktikós.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01727101-v
Synset POS: v
