Messidor

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛsɪdɔr / (AmE), / ˈmɛsɪdɔː / (BrE)
the tenth month of the French Revolutionary calendar, corresponding roughly to June and July, traditionally known as the month of harvest
tenth month of the French Revolutionary calendar

Examples

During the French Revolution, the law was passed in Messidor of the Year II.
The harvest festival took place every Messidor, celebrating the season's abundance.
Historians use the term Messidor when dating events from the Revolutionary era.

Etymology

From French 'Messidor', from Latin 'messis' (harvest).
