beneficiary

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɛnəˈfɪʃiˌɛri / (AmE), / ˌbɛnɪˈfɪʃəri / (BrE)
In linguistics, the person or entity who receives the benefit from an action, as described by the verb in a sentence.
person/entity benefiting from an action (linguistics)

Examples

In the sentence 'She baked him a cake,' 'him' is the beneficiary of the action.
Linguists often analyze who acts as the beneficiary in different sentence structures.
Identifying the beneficiary helps clarify for whom an action is performed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agent - the doer of an action in linguistics

Etymology

From Latin beneficiarius (enjoying a favor), from beneficium (benefit).

2. ADJ

/ ˌbɛnɪˈfɪʃiˌɛri / (AmE), / ˌbɛnɪˈfɪʃiəri / (BrE)
possessing, or relating to, a benefice; having rights or privileges arising from the holding of a benefice, especially in ecclesiastical or historical contexts.
relating to a benefice; having rights from a benefice
archaic

Examples

Only those of beneficiary rank were entitled to income from the church lands.
He wore robes reserved for members of the beneficiary clergy.
During the Middle Ages, a beneficiary knight could support himself through his granted lands.

Synset Examples

a beneficiary baron

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'beneficiarius' (holder of a benefice), from Latin 'beneficium' (benefit, favor).

3. NOUN

/ ˌbɛnəˈfɪʃiˌɛri / (AmE), / ˌbɛnɪˈfɪʃəri / (BrE)
A person or organization that receives money or other benefits from something such as a will, trust, insurance policy, or financial arrangement.
person receiving benefits or funds

Examples

The primary beneficiary of the trust will receive monthly payments for life.
Each policyholder must name at least one beneficiary when signing up for insurance.
The orphanage is the main beneficiary of the charity's annual fundraiser.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recipient - a person or thing that receives something
  • Antonym: donor - a person who gives something

Etymology

From Latin beneficiarius (enjoying a favor or privilege), from beneficium (benefit).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
