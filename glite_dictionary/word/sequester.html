to sequester

1. VERB

/ sɪˈkwɛstər / (AmE), / sɪˈkwɛstə / (BrE)
to isolate or set something or someone apart from others, especially for a specific purpose
to set apart; to isolate

Examples

During the trial, the judge decided to sequester the jury to prevent outside influence.
Researchers sequester specimens in a controlled environment for study.
To stop the spread of infection, doctors may sequester affected patients from others.

Synset Examples

The dentist sequesters the tooth he is working on

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to isolate - to separate something or someone from others
  • Antonym: release - to allow something or someone to return to the group or public

Etymology

From Latin sequestrare (to hand over to a trustee), from sequester (trustee).

/ sɪˈkwɛstər / (AmE), / sɪˈkwɛstə / (BrE)
(of a legal authority) to take temporary possession of property as security or by order of the court.
take possession by legal authority
legal

Examples

The court may sequester the defendant's assets during the trial.
Authorities decided to sequester the disputed land until the ownership is resolved.
The government can sequester property that is involved in criminal investigations.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: to impound - to take legal custody of something, especially by law enforcement

Etymology

to take legal possession of someone's property, especially by force or authority; to confiscate temporarily, often during a legal dispute or as a penalty

legal

Examples

The judge ordered the court to sequester the disputed funds while the case was under review.

5. VERB

/ sɪˈkwɛstər / (AmE), / sɪˈkwɛstə / (BrE)
(of a substance) to undergo sequestration by forming a stable compound with an ion, often to remove it from participation in other reactions.
form a stable compound with an ion (chemistry)
medical

Examples

Certain molecules can sequester calcium ions, preventing them from interfering with other reactions.
Chelating agents are added to sequester metal ions in water treatment processes.
The drug is designed to sequester iron, reducing its bioavailability in the bloodstream.

Synset Examples

The cations were sequestered

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chelate - to form a complex compound with a metal ion

Etymology

From Latin sequestrare (to set aside, surrender).
