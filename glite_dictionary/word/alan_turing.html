Alan Turing

1. PROPN

/ ˈælən ˈtʊrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈælən ˈtjʊərɪŋ / (BrE)
(1912–1954) An English mathematician and computer scientist known for devising the concept of the Turing machine and for breaking German codes during World War II.
English mathematician and codebreaker

Examples

Alan Turing is celebrated as the father of theoretical computer science and artificial intelligence.
The team credited Alan Turing with shortening World War II by years through his codebreaking work.
Many universities commemorate Alan Turing through lectures and scholarships in his name.
