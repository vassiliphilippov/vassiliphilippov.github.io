Yugoslav

1. ADJ

/ ˈjuːɡəˌslɑːv / (AmE), / ˈjuːɡəˌslɑːv / (BrE)
of or relating to the former country of Yugoslavia, or its people, culture, or languages
relating to Yugoslavia or its people

Examples

The exhibit features Yugoslav art from the mid-twentieth century.
He was one of the most prominent Yugoslav filmmakers of his generation.
They enjoyed a traditional dish from Yugoslav cuisine at the festival.

Synset Examples

Yugoslavian wine

Etymology

From Yugoslavia, itself from Serbo-Croatian 'Jugoslavija' ('jug' meaning 'south' and 'slavija' meaning 'land of the Slavs').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02973558-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈjuːɡəˌslɑːv / (AmE), / ˈjuːɡəˌslɑːv / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Yugoslavia, a former country in southeastern Europe
person from former Yugoslavia

Examples

During the 1980s, many Yugoslavs competed in international sporting events together.
He grew up as a proud Yugoslav before the country dissolved.
Several Yugoslavs immigrated to the United States in the 1970s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Serb - a native or inhabitant of Serbia
  • Synonym: Croat - a native or inhabitant of Croatia

Etymology

From Yugoslavia, the former country in southeastern Europe; ultimately from Serbo-Croatian 'Jugoslavija' (South Slavia).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
