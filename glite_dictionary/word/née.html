née

1. ADJ

/ neɪ / (AmE), / neɪ / (BrE)
used after a married woman's name to indicate her maiden or family name
indicating maiden name

Examples

The biography was about Queen Elizabeth II, née Elizabeth Alexandra Mary Windsor.
Her full name was listed as Margaret Johnson, née Smith, in the announcement.
The novel's protagonist is Anna Peterson, née Ivanova, a Russian immigrant.

Synset Examples

Hillary Clinton nee Rodham

Etymology

From French 'née', feminine past participle of 'naître' (to be born), from Latin 'natus'.
