shebang

1. NOUN

/ ʃəˈbæŋ / (AmE), / ʃəˈbæŋ / (BrE)
the whole of something; the entire system, thing, or situation.
the whole thing; entire system
informal

Examples

We're moving to a new house, pets and all—the whole shebang.
The company is updating the shebang, including all its old software systems.
He sold his business, the building, the equipment, the shebang, for a tidy sum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: everything - all things; the whole lot
  • Antonym: part - a piece or portion of a whole

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly American slang from the mid-19th century.
