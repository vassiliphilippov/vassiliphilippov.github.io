to roughen

1. VERB

/ ˈrʌfən / (AmE), / ˈrʌfən / (BrE)
to make or become rough or rougher in texture or surface
make or become rough

Examples

You may need to roughen the wood before painting it for better adhesion.
Sandpaper is often used to roughen a surface before gluing pieces together.
Weather and time will naturally roughen the once-smooth stones over the years.

Synset Examples

roughen the surfaces so they will stick to each other

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth - to make smooth or smoother

Etymology

Formed from 'rough' (Middle English, from Old English rūh) + '-en' (a verb-forming suffix).
