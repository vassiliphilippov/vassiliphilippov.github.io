randomisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌrændəməˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrandəməˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act or process of arranging things or assigning subjects in a study in a way that is based entirely on chance, in order to avoid bias
arrangement based entirely on chance to avoid bias
british

Examples

The researchers used randomisation to assign participants to treatment and control groups.
Proper randomisation ensures that the results of the experiment are not biased.
A lack of randomisation in the sampling process can lead to inaccurate conclusions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: randomization - the act or process of making something random (American spelling)

Etymology

From random +‎ -isation, following standard formation for nouns denoting processes; chiefly British spelling of 'randomization'.
