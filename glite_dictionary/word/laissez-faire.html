laissez-faire

1. ADJ

/ ˌlæs.eɪˈfer / (AmE), / ˌlɛseɪˈfeə / (BrE)
Relating to or advocating a policy of minimal governmental intervention in economic affairs; allowing businesses and markets to operate with very little regulation or restriction.
policy of minimal government interference in the economy

Examples

The government adopted a laissez-faire approach to the tech industry, allowing innovation to flourish.
Many economists debate whether laissez-faire policies lead to greater prosperity or increased inequality.
Her laissez-faire management style gave employees a lot of autonomy to make their own decisions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interventionist - favoring government intervention in economic affairs

Etymology

From French 'laissez faire' meaning 'let do, let go.'
