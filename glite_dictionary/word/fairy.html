2. NOUN

/ ˈfɛri / (AmE), / ˈfeəri / (BrE)
a small imaginary being in human form with magical powers, often depicted as playful and delicate
mythical magical being in human form

Examples

The children listened in awe as their grandmother spoke of a fairy who lived in the garden.
According to legend, leaving milk out at night would please the resident fairy.
She dressed as a fairy for the costume party, complete with wings and a wand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elf - a small, mischievous supernatural being from folklore
  • Synonym: sprite - a small or elusive supernatural being; a spirit
  • Antonym: goblin - a mischievous, ugly creature in folklore

Etymology

From Old French *faerie* (enchantment, fairyland), from Latin *fata* (fates, goddesses of destiny).
