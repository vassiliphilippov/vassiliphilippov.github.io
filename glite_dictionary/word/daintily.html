daintily

1. ADV

/ ˈdeɪntɪli / (AmE), / ˈdeɪntɪli / (BrE)
In a delicate, refined, and fastidious manner.
in a delicate or refined way

Examples

She ate her dessert daintily, barely making a sound with her fork.
The kitten daintily licked its paws after finishing its meal.
He placed the flowers daintily into the vase, arranging them with care.

Synset Examples

she nibbled daintily at her cake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delicately - in a gentle and careful manner
  • Antonym: roughly - in a coarse or unrefined manner

Etymology

From 'dainty' + '-ly', ultimately from Old French 'deintie' (delicacy).

2. ADV

/ ˈdeɪntɪli / (AmE), / ˈdeɪntɪli / (BrE)
in a manner that is delicate, graceful, or refined
in a delicate or graceful way

Examples

She nibbled her sandwich daintily, careful not to make a mess.
The kitten walked daintily along the windowsill, barely making a sound.
Each dessert was arranged daintily on the plate to impress the guests.

Synset Examples

the invitation cards were written up daintily in white and gold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gracefully - in an elegant or graceful manner
  • Antonym: clumsily - in a way lacking grace or delicacy

Etymology

From 'dainty' + '-ly'. 'Dainty' from Old French 'deintie' (delicacy, pleasure).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00305669-r
Synset POS: r
