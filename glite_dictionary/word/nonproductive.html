nonproductive

1. ADJ

/ nɑn.prəˈdʌk.tɪv / (AmE), / nɒn.prəˈdʌk.tɪv / (BrE)
not producing useful results or not directly contributing to production; unproductive.
not yielding results or output

Examples

The meeting turned out to be entirely nonproductive and did not resolve any outstanding issues.
Many companies try to minimize nonproductive time to improve efficiency.
Some tasks are considered nonproductive because they do not contribute directly to finished goods.

Synset Examples

nonproductive labor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unproductive - not productive; not producing results or output
  • Antonym: productive - producing or able to produce large amounts; yielding results or output

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' meaning 'not' and 'productive', from medieval Latin productivus.
