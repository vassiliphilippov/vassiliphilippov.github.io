Cornwall

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɔrnˌwɑl / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnwəl / (BrE)
a county in southwestern England, known for its hills, coastline, and distinct cultural heritage
county in southwestern England

Examples

We spent our summer holiday exploring the rugged coastline of Cornwall.
Cornwall is famous for its picturesque fishing villages and scenic countryside.
Many legends about King Arthur are associated with Cornwall.

Etymology

From Old English 'Cornwealas', meaning 'the Cornish people'.
