usufruct

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːzəˌfrʌkt / (AmE), / ˈjuːzjuːˌfrʌkt / (BrE)
the legal right to use and benefit from property owned by another, provided the property itself is not harmed
right to use another's property without damaging it
legal

Examples

The widow was granted a usufruct of the estate until her death.
He held a usufruct on the farmland, enabling him to harvest crops but not sell the land.
Local law acknowledges a usufruct, allowing one party to benefit from property without changing its substance.

Etymology

From Latin 'usus' (use) and 'fructus' (enjoyment, fruit).
