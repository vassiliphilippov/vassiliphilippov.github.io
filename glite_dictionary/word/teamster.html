teamster

1. NOUN

/ ˈtiːm.stɚ / (AmE), / ˈtiːm.stə / (BrE)
a person who drives a team of draft animals, especially horses, to haul goods or perform other work
driver of a team of draft animals

Examples

During the nineteenth century, a teamster was vital for transporting goods across long distances.
The old photograph showed a teamster guiding his horses through a muddy road.
Before the advent of trucks, every mining company hired at least one experienced teamster.

Etymology

From 'team' + '-ster', a suffix for persons, from Old English '-estre'; originally denoted one who drives a team of draft animals.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtiːmstɚ / (AmE), / ˈtiːmstə / (BrE)
a person who drives a truck, especially as an occupation; in North America, often refers to a member of the Teamsters Union.
truck driver (esp. North America)
american

Examples

He spent most of his life working as a teamster hauling goods across the Midwest.
Many teamsters are members of one of the largest labor unions in the United States.
After years on the road, the seasoned teamster had driven more miles than he could count.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: truck driver - a person whose job is to drive a truck

Etymology

From 'team' (a group of draft animals) + '-ster' (agent suffix); originally referred to someone who drove teams of draft animals.
