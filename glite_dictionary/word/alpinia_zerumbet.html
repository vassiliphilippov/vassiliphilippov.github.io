Alpinia zerumbet

1. NOUN

/ ælˈpɪniə zəˈrʌmbɛt / (AmE), / ælˈpɪniə zəˈrʌmbɛt / (BrE)
A tropical plant species cultivated for its shiny oblong leaves and arching clusters of white and pink flowers with yellow and magenta markings, also known as shell ginger.
tropical plant with showy flowers, also called shell ginger

Examples

The lush garden featured several Alpinia zerumbet plants, their fragrant flowers attracting butterflies.
After planting Alpinia zerumbet near the patio, we enjoyed its decorative leaves year-round.
Florists often use Alpinia zerumbet stems in tropical floral arrangements due to their unique appearance.

Etymology

From New Latin, genus name Alpinia (named after Prospero Alpini, Italian botanist) and specific epithet zerumbet (possibly from Arabic "zarambād" for ginger).
