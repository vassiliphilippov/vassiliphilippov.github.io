Miao

1. NOUN

/ miˈaʊ / (AmE), / miˈaʊ / (BrE)
a member of an ethnic group traditionally living in southern China and neighboring regions, known for their distinct culture and language
member of an ethnic group in southern China

Examples

Many Miao communities continue to practice traditional crafts such as embroidery and silverwork.
The Miao have a rich tradition of folk music and colorful festivals.
Some members of the Miao ethnic group have emigrated to the United States in recent decades.

Etymology

From Mandarin 苗 (Miáo), referring to the ethnic group.

2. NOUN

/ miˈaʊ / (AmE), / miˈaʊ / (BrE)
the language spoken by the Hmong people, belonging to the Hmong-Mien (Miao-Yao) family, primarily in southern China and Southeast Asia
Hmong language of southern China and Southeast Asia

Examples

Many Hmong people continue to speak Miao in their communities in Laos and Vietnam.
Linguists are studying the different dialects of Miao and their historical development.
Efforts are being made to preserve the Miao language among younger generations.

Etymology

Chinese 苗 (Miáo), referring to the ethnic group and their languages.
