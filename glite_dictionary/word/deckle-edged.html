deckle-edged

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛkəl ɛdʒd / (AmE), / ˈdɛkəl ɛdʒd / (BrE)
(of paper) having a rough, irregular edge, typically produced to resemble handmade paper.
having a rough, irregular edge (paper)

Examples

The collector prized books with deckle-edged pages for their artisanal appearance.
Handmade invitations are often printed on deckle-edged paper to give a rustic feel.
You can recognize older luxury books by their deckle-edged sheets.

Etymology

From 'deckle', a frame used in paper-making, plus 'edged'.
