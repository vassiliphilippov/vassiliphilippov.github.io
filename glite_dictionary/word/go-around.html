go-around

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊ əˌraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊ əˌraʊnd / (BrE)
an instance of abandoning an approach or attempt in order to try again, especially in aviation when an aircraft discontinues its landing and prepares for another approach.
a failed approach followed by another attempt

Examples

The pilot performed a go-around after encountering turbulence near the runway.
Due to the unexpected obstacle, the team had to do a go-around before completing the task.
Several go-arounds were necessary before the plane could land safely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to retry - an act of trying something again; a new attempt
