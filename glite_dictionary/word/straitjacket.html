straitjacket

1. NOUN

/ ˈstreɪtˌdʒækɪt / (AmE), / ˈstreɪtˌdʒækɪt / (BrE)
a strong garment with long sleeves that can be tied together to restrain a person's arms, typically used to prevent violent behavior
garment for restraining arms

Examples

The patient was placed in a straitjacket to keep him from hurting himself.
Some people use the term straitjacket metaphorically to describe restrictive rules or regulations.
The old asylum displayed an antique straitjacket as part of its history exhibit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restraint - a device or means of restraining someone or something

Etymology

From 'strait' (meaning 'tight' or 'confining') + 'jacket'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstreɪtˌdʒækət / (AmE), / ˈstreɪtˌdʒækɪt / (BrE)
a restriction or limitation that severely hinders or confines progress, development, or free action, especially in a figurative sense.
severe restriction or limitation

Examples

The company's outdated policies put innovation in a straitjacket.
Creative thinking often struggles within the straitjacket of rigid rules.
Rising costs have placed a financial straitjacket on the project.

Synset Examples

they defected because Russian dance was in a straitjacket
the government is operating in an economic straitjacket

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constraint - something that limits or restricts actions or freedom
  • Synonym: limitation - a restriction on progress or ability
  • Antonym: freedom - the state of being free or at liberty

Etymology

From 'strait' (narrow, tight) + 'jacket'; originally referring to a garment used to restrain mentally ill patients.

3. NOUN

/ ˈstreɪtˌdʒækɪt / (AmE), / ˈstreɪtˌdʒækɪt / (BrE)
A situation, rule, or set of conditions that severely restricts freedom of action, thought, or development.
something that severely restricts freedom

Examples

The bureaucratic procedures acted as a straitjacket on innovation within the company.
Some artists feel that too many rules can become a creative straitjacket.
She longed to break free from the straitjacket of tradition.

Synset Examples

He was trapped in a straitjacket of his own making.
The new regulations felt like a straitjacket on our creativity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constraint - a limitation or restriction
  • Synonym: restriction - a limiting condition or measure
  • Antonym: freedom - the state of being free; absence of restraint

Etymology

From 'strait' (narrow, strict) + 'jacket', originally referring to a restraining garment; later used figuratively.
