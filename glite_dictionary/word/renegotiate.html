to renegotiate

1. VERB

/ ˌriːnəˈɡoʊʃieɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːnɪˈɡəʊʃieɪt / (BrE)
to discuss and change the terms of an agreement, contract, or arrangement, typically to reach a new or improved deal
discuss and change terms again

Examples

After the merger, the company decided to renegotiate all vendor contracts.
The union leaders want to renegotiate the terms of the workers' employment.
Due to unforeseen circumstances, we had to renegotiate our agreement with the landlord.

Synset Examples

The two warring parties will have to renegociate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to negotiate - to discuss something to reach an agreement

Etymology

From re- (again) + negotiate; first appeared in the early 20th century.

2. VERB

/ ˌriːnɪˈɡoʊʃieɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːnɪˈɡəʊʃieɪt / (BrE)
to discuss and change the terms of an agreement or contract, especially to make them more favorable or fair
revise terms of an agreement or contract

Examples

The company plans to renegotiate its contracts with suppliers to reduce costs.
After losing his job, he tried to renegotiate the terms of his loan.
We decided to renegotiate our lease when the initial period ended.

Synset Examples

We renegociated our old mortgage now that the interest rates have come down

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to negotiate - discuss with others to reach an agreement

Etymology

From re- (again) + negotiate (from Latin negociari, to do business).

3. VERB

/ ˌriː.niˈɡoʊ.ʃi.eɪt / (AmE), / ˌriː.nɪˈɡəʊ.ʃi.eɪt / (BrE)
to discuss and change the terms of an agreement, contract, or arrangement, usually to reach a new deal or improve conditions
revise terms of an agreement or contract

Examples

After the company merger, we had to renegotiate our supplier contracts to get better rates.
The tenants asked to renegotiate the lease due to changes in the building's conditions.
The coach decided to renegotiate his contract after leading the team to victory.

Synset Examples

We renegociated our old mortgage now that the interest rates have come down

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to negotiate - discuss something formally to reach an agreement

Etymology

Formed from re- (again) and negotiate (discuss terms), modelled after Latin roots.
