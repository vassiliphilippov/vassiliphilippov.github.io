Cheilanthes eatonii

1. NOUN

/ kiːˈlæn.θiːz ˌiːˈtoʊ.ni.aɪ / (AmE), / kiːˈlæn.θiːz ˌiːˈtəʊ.ni.aɪ / (BrE)
A species of lip fern native to Texas, Oklahoma, Colorado, Arizona, and Mexico, with tall, erect, tufted fronds.
a North American species of lip fern

Examples

The rocky slopes were covered with Cheilanthes eatonii, lending a delicate green hue to the landscape.
Botanists documented the presence of Cheilanthes eatonii in several protected areas of Arizona.
In her garden, she cultivated Cheilanthes eatonii for its distinctive, tufted fronds.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, naming after botanist Daniel Cady Eaton.
