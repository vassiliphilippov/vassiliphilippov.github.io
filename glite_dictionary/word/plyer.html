plyer

1. NOUN

/ ˈplaɪ.ər / (AmE), / ˈplaɪ.ə / (BrE)
a person who engages in or carries on a particular trade or activity, especially regularly or skillfully
person who practices a trade or activity

Examples

As a seasoned plyer of his trade, he was trusted by everyone in the market.
The town was well known for its many skilled plyers of different crafts.
She became a recognized plyer of legal services in her community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: practitioner - a person actively engaged in an art, discipline, or profession
  • Antonym: amateur - a person who engages in an activity for pleasure rather than professionally

Etymology

From the verb 'ply', from Middle English 'plien' (to bend, occupy oneself), from Old French 'plier'.
