Euryalida

1. NOUN

/ jʊriˈælɪdə / (AmE), / jʊəriˈælɪdə / (BrE)
A subclass of echinoderms that includes basket stars, characterized by highly branched, flexible arms.
subclass of basket stars (echinoderms)

Examples

The deep-sea habitat is home to various Euryalida, including several rare basket star species.
Researchers discovered a new species within the subclass Euryalida in the South Pacific Ocean.
Unlike brittle stars, members of Euryalida possess highly branched arms for filter-feeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basket star - type of brittle star with branched arms; member of Euryalida

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek eurys (broad, wide) + -ida (suffix denoting taxonomic groups).

2. NOUN

/ jʊˈraɪ.ə.lɪdə / (AmE), / jʊəˈraɪ.ə.lɪdə / (BrE)
A subclass of echinoderms known as basket stars, characterized by their branching arms and found mainly in deep sea environments.
subclass of basket stars (echinoderms)
medical

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species belonging to the Euryalida subclass in the Pacific Ocean.
The arms of most members of Euryalida are highly branched, resembling a delicate bush.
Marine biologists study the feeding habits of Euryalida to understand their ecological role.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'eurys' (broad, wide) and '-ida', a standard zoological suffix.
