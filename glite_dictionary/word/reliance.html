reliance

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈlaɪəns / (AmE), / rɪˈlaɪəns / (BrE)
firm trust or confidence in someone or something, often based on past experience
firm trust or confidence

Examples

After years of working together, she had great reliance on her colleague's judgment.
The company's reliance on past success made it slow to adapt to new technology.
Farmers in the region have a heavy reliance on rainfall for their crops.

Synset Examples

he wrote the paper with considerable reliance on the work of other scientists
he put more trust in his own two legs than in the gun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dependence - the state of relying on something or someone for support
  • Synonym: trust - firm belief in the reliability of someone or something
  • Antonym: distrust - lack of trust or confidence

Etymology

From Old French *reliance* (‘confidence, trust’), from *reliant*, present participle of *relier* (‘to bind’).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈlaɪəns / (AmE), / rɪˈlaɪəns / (BrE)
Dependence on or trust in someone or something.
dependence or trust in something

Examples

Her reliance on public transportation makes her morning commute much easier.
The country's reliance on imported oil has decreased in recent years.
Too much reliance on technology can create problems when systems fail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dependence - the state of relying on or being controlled by something else
  • Antonym: independence - the state of not relying on or being controlled by others

Etymology

From Old French 'reliance' and relyer (to rely), from Latin 'religare' (to bind fast).
