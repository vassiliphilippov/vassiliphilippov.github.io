spyhole

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈspaɪhəʊl / (BrE)
a small hole in a door, wall, or other surface that allows a person to look through secretly from one side
hole for secret viewing

Examples

He looked through the spyhole before opening the door to make sure it was safe.
The old oven has a spyhole so you can check the food without letting out heat.
She installed a spyhole in her front door for added security.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peephole - a small hole in a door for peeping through, usually fitted with a lens

Etymology

Formed from 'spy' (observe secretly) and 'hole'.
