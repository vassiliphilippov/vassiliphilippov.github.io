to misgrab

1. VERB

/ mɪsˈɡræb / (AmE), / mɪsˈɡræb / (BrE)
to grasp or take something incorrectly, usually by accident
grasp something incorrectly

Examples

He reached for his phone but gave a misgrab and knocked it off the table.
Her misgrab of the kettle nearly spilled boiling water everywhere.
During the relay, a runner's misgrab of the baton cost the team valuable seconds.

Synset Examples

His misgrab of the steering wheel caused the accident.
She misgrabbed the wrong file and sent it to everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grab - to seize suddenly or quickly

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'mis-' (wrongly) and 'grab' (to seize).
