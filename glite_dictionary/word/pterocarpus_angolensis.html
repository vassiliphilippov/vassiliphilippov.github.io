Pterocarpus angolensis

1. NOUN

/ təˌrɑːkɑːrˌpəs ænˌgoʊˈlɛnsɪs / (AmE), / tɛˌrɒkɑːˌpʊs æŋˌgəʊˈlɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to southern Africa, known for its large compound leaves, fragrant orange-yellow flowers, red sap, and strong durable wood.
South African tree with fragrant flowers and strong wood

Examples

The furniture was crafted from Pterocarpus angolensis, prized for its beauty and strength.
Conservationists are concerned about the overharvesting of Pterocarpus angolensis in southern Africa.
The striking orange-yellow blossoms of Pterocarpus angolensis are a common sight in the African savannah.

Etymology

From Greek pteron (wing) + karpos (fruit), referring to its winged seeds; species name from Angola, a region in Africa.
