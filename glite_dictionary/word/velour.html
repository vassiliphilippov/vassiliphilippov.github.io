velour

1. NOUN

/ vəˈlʊr / (AmE), / vəˈlʊə / (BrE)
a plush, knitted or woven fabric that resembles velvet, typically having a soft, dense pile
plush fabric similar to velvet

Examples

The curtains were made from a rich velour that blocked out the light.
He wore a comfortable velour tracksuit to the gym.
The seats in the theater were upholstered in deep red velour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: velvet - a soft, luxurious fabric with a dense pile

Etymology

From French 'velours' meaning 'velvet'.
