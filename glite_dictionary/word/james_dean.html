James Dean

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒeɪmz ˈdin / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪmz ˈdiːn / (BrE)
An American film actor (1931–1955) famous for his moody and rebellious roles, becoming a symbol of youthful angst and a cultural icon.
American actor, symbol of youthful rebellion

Examples

Many teenagers in the 1950s aspired to look and act like James Dean.
The tragic death of James Dean only increased his legendary status in Hollywood.
People still see James Dean as the epitome of cool and rebellion.

2. PROPN

/ ˈdʒeɪmz ˈdiːn / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪmz ˈdiːn / (BrE)
(1931–1955) An American film actor known for his moody, rebellious roles, especially in 'Rebel Without a Cause', and regarded as a cultural icon of teenage disillusionment.
American film actor, cultural icon

Examples

Many critics believe that James Dean's performances redefined American movie acting.
Young fans were drawn to James Dean because of his brooding presence and style.
'Rebel Without a Cause' cemented James Dean's reputation as a symbol of youthful rebellion.
