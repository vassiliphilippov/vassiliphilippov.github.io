microgliacyte

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkroʊˈɡlaɪ.əˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˌmʌɪ.krəʊˈɡlʌɪ.ə.sʌɪt / (BrE)
a cell of the microglia in the nervous system that may become phagocytic, collecting waste products of nerve tissue
microglial cell in the nervous system
medical

Examples

Under certain pathological conditions, a microgliacyte may transform into an active phagocyte, engulfing cellular debris.
Researchers found an increase in microgliacytes in the affected brain tissue of the patients.
The role of the microgliacyte in neuroinflammation is a subject of ongoing research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: microglia - type of glial cell in the central nervous system that acts as the main form of active immune defense

Etymology

From microglia (a type of glial cell of the nervous system) + -cyte (from Greek 'kytos', meaning 'cell').
