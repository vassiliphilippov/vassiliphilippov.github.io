hand-me-down

1. NOUN

/ ˈhænd mi daʊn / (AmE), / ˈhænd mi daʊn / (BrE)
a piece of clothing or other item that is passed from one person to another after being used, especially within a family
used item passed to another person

Examples

When I was a child, most of my clothes were hand-me-downs from my older sister.
He didn't mind wearing a hand-me-down jacket as long as it kept him warm.
The family gratefully accepted a box of hand-me-downs for their newborn baby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: castoff - an item, especially clothing, that is no longer wanted and given away

2. ADJ

/ ˈhænd mi ˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈhænd mi ˌdaʊn / (BrE)
an object, especially a piece of clothing, that is passed on from one person to another after being used
secondhand item passed from one person to another

Examples

Many of my childhood toys were hand-me-downs from my older cousins.
She appreciated the vintage look of hand-me-down dresses.
We try to reduce waste by giving hand-me-down clothes to friends and family.

Synset Examples

not too proud to wear hand-me-down clothes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secondhand - previously owned or used by someone else

Etymology

From the phrase 'hand me down,' describing the action of passing something from one person to another.
