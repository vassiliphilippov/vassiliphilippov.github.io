outgo

1. VERB

/ ˌaʊtˈɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˌaʊtˈɡəʊ / (BrE)
to do, perform, or be better than someone or something; to surpass or exceed
surpass; do better than

Examples

Her determination allows her to outgo all her competitors in the annual race.
In times of challenge, his kindness always seems to outgo that of others.
The company's new product aims to outgo all existing models in efficiency.

Synset Examples

her performance surpasses that of any other student I know
She outdoes all other athletes
This exceeds all my expectations
This car outperforms all others in its class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outdo - to do better than someone else
  • Synonym: to exceed - to surpass a level or limit
  • Antonym: to fall short - to fail to reach a standard or expectation

Etymology

Formed from 'out-' (surpassing) + 'go', analogous to similar verbs like 'outdo' and 'outrun'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌgoʊ / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌgəʊ / (BrE)
An amount of money spent or paid out; expenditure.
money paid out; expenditure

Examples

The company's outgo has increased steadily over the past year.
We need to cut down our outgo if we want to save more money.
Her monthly outgo far exceeds her income, creating financial difficulties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expenditure - the act of spending money; an amount spent
  • Antonym: income - money received, especially on a regular basis

Etymology

From out- + go, influenced by analogy with words like 'income'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13296311-n
Synset POS: n
