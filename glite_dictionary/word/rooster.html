rooster

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːstər / (AmE), / ˈruːstə / (BrE)
An adult male chicken, especially one kept for breeding or crowing.
adult male chicken
american

Examples

The rooster crowed loudly at dawn, waking the entire farm.
That rooster is particularly proud of his colorful feathers.
We saw a rooster strutting around the barnyard yesterday.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hen - adult female chicken
  • Synonym: cock - adult male chicken, chiefly British term

Etymology

From early 18th-century American English, from 'roost' (where chickens rest) + '-er'.
