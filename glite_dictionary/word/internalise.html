to internalise

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈtɜrnəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːnəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make an attitude, belief, or value a part of your own character, so that it influences the way you think or behave
make personal; absorb into one's character

Examples

It took time for her to internalise the company's core values.
Children often internalise what they see in their parents' behaviour.
He gradually internalised the lessons he learned from his failures.

Synset Examples

internalize a belief

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to externalise - express or show (a feeling or thought) outwardly
  • Synonym: to absorb - take in or assimilate (information, ideas, or experience)

Etymology

From internal + -ise, after French 'internaliser'.
