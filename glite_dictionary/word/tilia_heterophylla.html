Tilia heterophylla

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪliə ˌhɛtərəˈfaɪlə / (AmE), / ˈtɪlia ˌhɛtərəˈfɪlə / (BrE)
A species of basswood tree native to the Allegheny region of the United States, also known as white basswood or American basswood.
a basswood tree of the Allegheny region

Examples

The forested slopes were dotted with stately Tilia heterophylla trees, their leaves shimmering in the morning light.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Tilia heterophylla in the Allegheny region.
The soft wood and large leaves make Tilia heterophylla a recognizable species among North American basswoods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American basswood - A common name for Tilia americana, a closely related or synonymous species

Etymology

From Latin: Tilia (genus name for linden or lime trees) and heterophylla (from Greek heteros 'different' and phyllon 'leaf', referring to its variable leaves).
