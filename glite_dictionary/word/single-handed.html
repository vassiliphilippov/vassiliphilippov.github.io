single-handed

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɪŋɡəlˈhændɪd / (AmE), / ˌsɪŋɡlˈhændɪd / (BrE)
Done without help from others; carried out or achieved alone.
done alone, without help

Examples

She built her business single-handed, facing many obstacles along the way.
The sailor completed the long voyage single-handed, with no crew or companions.
It was a single-handed effort that transformed the dilapidated house into a beautiful home.

Synset Examples

a single-handed accomplishment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unaided - done without assistance from others
  • Antonym: assisted - done with help or support from others

2. ADV

/ ˌsɪŋɡəlˈhændəd / (AmE), / ˌsɪŋɡəlˈhændɪd / (BrE)
(adverb) Without help from anyone else; alone.
without help from others

Examples

He managed to finish the entire project single-handed.
She raised her three children single-handed after her husband passed away.
They restored the old farmhouse single-handed in just two years.

Synset Examples

I built this house single-handedly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alone - without other people or help
  • Synonym: unaided - without any help or support from others

Etymology

Formed from 'single' (alone) + 'handed' (having or using a hand), originally referring to using one hand, later extended to acting alone.

3. ADJ

/ ˌsɪŋɡəlˈhændɪd / (AmE), / ˌsɪŋɡlˈhændɪd / (BrE)
Without help or support from others; done alone.
done without help from others

Examples

She climbed the mountain single-handed, without any assistance or guides.
He managed to organize the entire event single-handed, which impressed everyone.
The artist restored the old painting single-handed over several months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alone - without any other people; by oneself
  • Synonym: unaided - without help or support; unassisted
  • Antonym: assisted - helped or supported by others

Etymology

From 'single' (one, alone) + 'handed' (having or using hands); originally in reference to using one hand, later extended to mean 'unassisted'.
