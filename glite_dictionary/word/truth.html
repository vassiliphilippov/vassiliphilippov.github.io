truth

1. NOUN

/ truθ / (AmE), / truːθ / (BrE)
the quality or state of being in accordance with fact or reality
conformity to fact or reality

Examples

Sometimes the truth is difficult to accept, but it is essential to face it.
The journalist worked tirelessly to uncover the truth behind the rumors.
Children are taught the importance of always telling the truth.

Synset Examples

they debated the truth of the proposition
the situation brought home to us the blunt truth of the military threat
he was famous for the truth of his portraits
he turned to religion in his search for eternal verities

Related Terms

  • Antonym: falsehood - the quality of being untrue or not factual
  • Synonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

From Old English 'trēowþ' (faith, fidelity, truth), related to 'trēowe' (faithful, trustworthy).

2. NOUN

/ truθ / (AmE), / truːθ / (BrE)
A fact or statement that is accepted as true or has been proven to correspond to reality.
fact that has been verified

Examples

The scientist presented the truth about climate change based on years of research.
It took time, but eventually the truth came out in court.
Sometimes the truth is more complicated than people expect.

Synset Examples

at last he knew the truth
the truth is that he didn't want to do it

Related Terms

  • Antonym: falsehood - a statement or belief that is not true
  • Antonym: lie - a deliberately false statement
  • Synonym: fact - something known or proved to be true

Etymology

From Old English trēowth (faith, fidelity, loyalty), related to trēowe (faithful, true).
