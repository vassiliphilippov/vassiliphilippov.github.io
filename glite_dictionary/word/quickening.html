quickening

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪkənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkwɪkənɪŋ / (BrE)
the moment during pregnancy when a mother first feels movement of the fetus in her womb
first sensation of fetal movement (pregnancy)

Examples

Many women eagerly anticipate the quickening as a sign that their pregnancy is progressing normally.
The doctor explained that the quickening usually occurs between 16 and 20 weeks of pregnancy.
After experiencing her first quickening, she felt a stronger emotional connection to her unborn child.

Etymology

From Middle English 'quikening', from 'quicken' (to come to life, be alive).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪkənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkwɪkənɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of coming to life, stirring, or showing the first signs of vital activity, especially in seeds, plants, or unborn offspring
the process of showing life or vitality

Examples

The spring rain brought a sudden quickening of growth to the fields.
Many poets have described the quickening of nature at the start of spring.
She felt a quickening in her heart as excitement grew.

Synset Examples

the quickening of seed that will become ripe grain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revival - the act of bringing back to life or consciousness

Etymology

From Middle English 'quikening', from 'quiken' (to come to life, revive). Related to 'quick' meaning 'alive'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪkənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkwɪkənɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of becoming faster or increasing in speed
the act of becoming faster

Examples

There was a noticeable quickening of his pace as he saw the bus approaching.
The economic report predicts a quickening in the growth rate next year.
A sudden quickening of excitement ran through the crowd as the concert began.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acceleration - an increase in speed or rate

Etymology

From the verb 'quicken' + -ing.
