Psidium guineense

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈdɪdiəm ɡwiːˈɛnseɪ / (AmE), / psɪˈdɪdɪəm ɡɪˈɛnsiː / (BrE)
A South American tree of the myrtle family, bearing fruit similar to the common guava.
South American tree with guava-like fruit

Examples

The Psidium guineense thrives in subtropical regions of South America.
Researchers studied the nutritional content of Psidium guineense fruit compared to common guavas.
Farmers in Brazil are cultivating Psidium guineense for both its fruit and ornamental value.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: guava - A tropical fruit from the genus Psidium, especially Psidium guajava.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Psidium' (the guava genus) and 'guineense' (from Guinea, referring to the region or appearance).
