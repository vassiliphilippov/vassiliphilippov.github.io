huskiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌskinɪs / (AmE), / ˈhʌskinɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being big and strong in build; sturdiness or robustness
quality of being big and strong

Examples

His huskiness made him well-suited for the position of a security guard.
The football player's huskiness gave him an advantage on the field.
Despite his huskiness, he moved with surprising agility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robustness - the quality of being strong and healthy
  • Antonym: frailness - the quality of being weak or delicate

Etymology

From 'husky' (big and strong) + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhʌskinəs / (AmE), / ˈhʌskinəs / (BrE)
a throaty or harsh vocal quality, often characterized by a slightly rough or hoarse sound
throaty, harsh vocal quality

Examples

There was a charming huskiness to her voice that made her singing unique.
After shouting at the concert, he woke up with a noticeable huskiness in his throat.
The actor's distinctive huskiness lent depth to his character's dialogue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoarseness - a rough or harsh-sounding voice, often due to illness
  • Synonym: raspiness - a voice quality that is harsh and scratchy

Etymology

From 'husky' + '-ness', where 'husky' means having a hoarse or rough voice. 'Husky' may be of Scandinavian origin, possibly from Swedish 'hes' (hoarse).
