canicular

1. ADJ

/ kəˈnɪkjələr / (AmE), / kəˈnɪkjʊlə / (BrE)
Relating to or occurring during the period around the heliacal rising of the Dog Star (Sirius); especially, pertaining to the hottest days of summer.
relating to the Dog Star or hottest summer days

Examples

The oppressive heat during the canicular days made it difficult to sleep at night.
Ancient Romans associated the canicular period with increased fevers and storms.
The festival was scheduled for the canicular part of the summer, when Sirius appeared in the dawn sky.

Synset Examples

canicular days

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dog days - the hottest period of the year, traditionally associated with the rising of Sirius

Etymology

From Latin canicularis, from canicula (the Dog Star, Sirius), diminutive of canis (dog).

2. ADJ

/ kəˈnɪkjələr / (AmE), / kəˈnɪkjʊlə / (BrE)
relating to the dog days of summer; characterized by the hottest period of summer, typically associated with sweltering heat.
relating to the hottest part of summer

Examples

The canicular sun beat down relentlessly on the parched fields.
We sought shelter from the canicular afternoons by staying indoors.
Writers often describe the mood of August as slow and heavy with canicular heat.

Synset Examples

the canicular heat of the Deep South

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dog days - the hottest period of the summer, often linked to lethargy or discomfort

Etymology

From Latin canicularis, from canicula (little dog), referencing Sirius, the Dog Star, whose rising marks the dog days.
