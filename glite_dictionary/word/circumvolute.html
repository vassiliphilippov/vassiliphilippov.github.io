to circumvolute

1. VERB

/ ˌsɜr.kəmˈvɑː.lut / (AmE), / ˌsɜː.kəmˈvɒ.ljuːt / (BrE)
to wind or twist in spirals or coils, especially inward, as in the way a snail's shell forms
wind or twist in inward spirals

Examples

The vine began to circumvolute around the wooden pole as it grew.
The snail’s shell circumvolutes inward, forming a perfect spiral.
The ribbon circumvoluted gracefully around the gift box, creating an intricate pattern.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coil - to wind into a series of loops or spirals

Etymology

From Latin circumvolutus, past participle of circumvolvere (‘to wind around’), from circum (‘around’) + volvere (‘to roll’).
