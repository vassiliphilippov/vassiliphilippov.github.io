to imagine

1. VERB

/ ɪˈmædʒɪn / (AmE), / ɪˈmadʒɪn / (BrE)
to form a mental image or concept of something that is not present or not real
form a mental image or concept

Examples

Can you imagine living in a world without electricity?
She tried to imagine what life would have been like a hundred years ago.
It's hard to imagine him as anything other than a teacher.

Synset Examples

Can you conceive of him as the president?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to conceive - to form an idea or notion in the mind
  • Synonym: to visualize - to form a clear mental image of something
  • Antonym: to forget - to fail to remember or be unable to bring to mind

Etymology

From Latin 'imaginari' (to form an image), from 'imago' (image).

2. VERB

/ ɪˈmædʒɪn / (AmE), / ɪˈmadʒɪn / (BrE)
to think or believe something is likely or true, often without certain evidence
to suppose or believe

Examples

I imagine she won't be very pleased to see us arriving late.
Do you imagine he'll finish the work by tomorrow?
I can't imagine why anyone would refuse such a generous offer.

Synset Examples

I imagine she earned a lot of money with her new novel
I thought to find her in a bad state
he didn't think to find her in the kitchen
I guess she is angry at me for standing her up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suppose - to believe to be true or probable
  • Synonym: to assume - to accept something as true without proof
  • Antonym: doubt - to feel uncertain about; to not believe

Etymology

From Latin imaginari (to form a mental picture), from imago (image).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00633399-v
Synset POS: v
