anguished

1. ADJ

/ ˈæŋɡwɪʃt / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡwɪʃt / (BrE)
experiencing or showing severe mental or emotional pain or distress.
severely distressed or in great pain

Examples

She gave an anguished cry when she heard the terrible news.
His anguished expression showed how deeply the decision affected him.
After months of waiting, the anguished parents finally received word about their missing son.

Synset Examples

an anguished conscience
a small tormented schoolboy
a tortured witness to another's humiliation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distressed - feeling extreme anxiety, sorrow, or pain
  • Synonym: tormented - experiencing great mental suffering
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions

Etymology

From verb 'anguish' (from Old French 'angoisse', from Latin 'angustia' meaning tightness, distress).
