liturgics

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈtɝːdʒɪks / (AmE), / lɪˈtɜːdʒɪks / (BrE)
the academic study or systematic analysis of liturgies and forms of worship
study of liturgies

Examples

She has published several influential papers on liturgics and church ceremonies.
His graduate studies focused on liturgics within the Anglican tradition.
The college offers a course in liturgics for theology students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liturgy - a form or formulary according to which public religious worship is conducted

Etymology

From liturgy, from Greek leitourgia (public service, worship), with the academic suffix -ics.
