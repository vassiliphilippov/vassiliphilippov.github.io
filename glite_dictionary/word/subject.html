subject

1. ADJ

/ ˈsʌbdʒɪkt / (AmE), / ˈsʌbdʒɪkt / (BrE)
likely to be affected by or to experience something, especially something unpleasant or restrictive
likely to be affected by something

Examples

All food imports are subject to health and safety checks at the border.
Admission to the event is subject to availability of seats.
These prices are subject to change without prior notice.

Synset Examples

the bond is subject to taxation
he is subject to fits of depression

Related Terms

  • Synonym: susceptible - likely or liable to be influenced or harmed by a particular thing

Etymology

From Latin subjectus (brought under), from subicere (to subject; bring under).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbdʒɛkt / (AmE), / ˈsʌbdʒɪkt / (BrE)

Examples

The subject of the statement is what the proposition asserts something about.
In formal logic, the subject and predicate play distinct roles.

4. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbdʒɛkt / (AmE), / ˈsʌbdʒɪkt / (BrE)
(Grammar) The part of a sentence or clause that indicates what it is about, typically the person, place, thing, or idea that is doing or being something.

Examples

Related Terms
