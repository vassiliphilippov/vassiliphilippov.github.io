macula

1. NOUN

/ ˈmækjələ / (AmE), / ˈmakjʊlə / (BrE)
A cooler, darker area that appears periodically on the sun's photosphere and is associated with a strong magnetic field; also called a sunspot.
darker area on the sun's surface (sunspot)

Examples

Astronomers observed a large macula forming near the sun's equator.
The appearance of a macula often signals increased solar activity.
Early scientists struggled to explain the cause of each macula seen through their telescopes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunspot - a dark, cooler patch on the sun's surface caused by magnetic activity

Etymology

From Latin 'macula' meaning 'spot' or 'stain'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmækjələ / (AmE), / ˈmakjʊlə / (BrE)
A patch of skin that is discolored but not usually raised, often occurring as a result of disease.
flat, discolored skin patch
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the macula on her arm to determine the cause of discoloration.
A flat red macula appeared on his cheek after he developed the rash.
Multiple maculae across the patient's back indicated a possible skin infection.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: papule - a small, raised, solid pimple or swelling on the skin

Etymology

From Latin *macula* (spot, stain).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmækjələ / (AmE), / ˈmakjʊlə / (BrE)
a small, yellowish area near the center of the retina, responsible for sharp and detailed central vision
central area of retina for clear vision
medical

Examples

Age-related degeneration of the macula can lead to a loss of central vision.
The macula allows us to read fine print and recognize faces clearly.
An ophthalmologist examined her macula for signs of disease.

Etymology

From Latin macula (spot, stain).
