to celebrate

1. VERB

/ ˈsɛləˌbreɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɛlɪˌbreɪt / (BrE)
to observe or honor a special day, event, or religious rite with ceremonies or festivities
observe or honor a special occasion

Examples

Every year, our family gathers to celebrate Thanksgiving with a big dinner.
Many people celebrate Diwali by lighting lamps and sharing sweets.
She loves to celebrate her birthday with friends and music.

Synset Examples

Keep the commandments
celebrate Christmas
Observe Yom Kippur

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to commemorate - to honor the memory of with a ceremony, especially for events or people
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to a day or occasion

Etymology

From Latin celebratus, past participle of celebrare (to frequent, honor, or celebrate).
