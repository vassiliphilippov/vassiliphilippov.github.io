to hornswoggle

1. VERB

/ ˈhɔːrnˌswɑːɡəl / (AmE), / ˈhɔːnˌswɒɡəl / (BrE)
to cheat or deceive someone, especially by trickery
cheat or deceive by trickery
informal american

Examples

The salesman tried to hornswoggle us into buying a fake watch.
She was hornswoggled by a clever scam on the internet.
Don't let yourself get hornswoggled by empty promises.

Synset Examples

He swindled me out of my inheritance
She defrauded the customers who trusted her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swindle - to cheat someone out of money or property
  • Synonym: to defraud - to illegally obtain money from someone by deception
  • Synonym: cheat - to act dishonestly or unfairly to gain an advantage

Etymology

American English, probably of fanciful origin in the 19th century; precise origin unknown.
