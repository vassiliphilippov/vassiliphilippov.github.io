to discountenance

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪskaʊnˈtɛnəns / (AmE), / ˌdɪskaʊnˈtɛnəns / (BrE)
to express disapproval of something, often by discouraging it
express disapproval; discourage

Examples

The school principal discountenanced the students' proposal for a longer lunch break.
Historically, certain fashions were discountenanced by leaders who preferred more modest attire.
The company will openly discountenance any attempts at discrimination in the workplace.

Synset Examples

any measure tending to fuse invalids into a class with special privileges should be discountenanced

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disapprove - to have or express an unfavorable opinion
  • Antonym: to encourage - to give support or confidence; to urge on

Etymology

From dis- (expressing reversal) + countenance (from Old French, from Latin *continentia*, meaning bearing).

2. VERB

/ dɪsˈkaʊntənəns / (AmE), / dɪsˈkaʊntɪnəns / (BrE)
to look with disfavor on; to disapprove of or discourage
to look on with disfavor, disapprove

Examples

Many teachers discountenance the use of mobile phones in class.
The organization publicly discountenanced any form of corruption.
His proposal was discountenanced by senior management.

Synset Examples

The republic soon discountenanced its few friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disapprove - to have or express an unfavorable opinion
  • Antonym: to approve - to consider something good or acceptable

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French 'descontenancer' meaning to put out of countenance, from 'des-' (dis-) + 'contenance' (countenance).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01796442-v
Synset POS: v
