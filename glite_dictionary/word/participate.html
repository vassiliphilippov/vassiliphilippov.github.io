to participate

1. VERB

/ pɑrˈtɪsɪˌpeɪt / (AmE), / pɑːˈtɪsɪpeɪt / (BrE)
to take part or share in an activity, event, or experience with others
take part in an activity or event

Examples

All employees are encouraged to participate in the company's wellness program.
She refused to participate in the discussion, remaining silent throughout the meeting.
Students must participate actively for the class to be engaging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: join - to become involved in an activity or group
  • Antonym: to withdraw - to stop participating or remove oneself from an activity

Etymology

From Latin participatus, past participle of participare (to share in).

2. VERB

/ pɑrˈtɪsɪˌpeɪt / (AmE), / pɑːˈtɪsɪpeɪt / (BrE)
to take part or become actively involved in an activity or event with others
to take part in an activity or event

Examples

All employees are encouraged to participate in the annual charity event.
She has participated in several international conferences this year.
They enjoyed participating in the group discussion.

Synset Examples

enter a race
enter an agreement
enter a drug treatment program
enter negotiations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: join - to become a member of a group or a participant in an activity
  • Antonym: to withdraw - to leave or stop taking part in an activity or event

Etymology

From Latin participare (to share in), from pars (part) + capere (to take).
