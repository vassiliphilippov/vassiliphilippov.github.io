to date-mark

1. VERB

/ ˈdeɪtˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈdeɪtˌmɑːk / (BrE)
to mark something with the date and sometimes the place at which it was created or received
mark with date and place

Examples

All correspondence should be date-marked before filing.
The laboratory samples were date-marked to track their collection times.
Before publishing, the articles are always date-marked for record-keeping purposes.

Synset Examples

dateline a newspaper article

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dateline - to attach the date and place of origin to a document or article
