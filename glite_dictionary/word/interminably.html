interminably

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈtɜrmənəbli / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːmɪnəbli / (BrE)
In a way that seems to have no end; endlessly or ceaselessly.
endlessly; without stopping

Examples

The meeting dragged on interminably, with no conclusion in sight.
She waited interminably for the results to be announced.
He spoke interminably about subjects that bored everyone else.

Synset Examples

a theological student with whom I argued interminably
her nagging went on endlessly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endlessly - In a way that has no end; without stopping

Etymology

From Latin 'interminabilis' (unending), via French 'interminable' and English suffix '-ly'.
