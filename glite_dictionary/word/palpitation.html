palpitation

1. NOUN

/ ˌpælpəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpalpɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a noticeably rapid, strong, or irregular heartbeat, often felt by a person
rapid or irregular heartbeat
medical

Examples

She went to the doctor because she was experiencing frequent palpitations.
Anxiety attacks can sometimes cause a sensation of palpitation in the chest.
After drinking too much coffee, he noticed a strange palpitation when he tried to relax.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrhythmia - an irregular heart rhythm
  • Degree Higher: tachycardia - abnormally rapid heart rate

Etymology

From Latin 'palpitare' (to throb, flutter).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpælpəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpælpɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a rapid or trembling motion, especially a rhythmic trembling or quivering
a shaky or trembling motion

Examples

The palpitation in her hands was noticeable when she tried to hold the cup steady.
He struck a match with a palpitation that betrayed his nervousness.
A slight palpitation of the curtain revealed someone hiding behind it.

Synset Examples

the shaking of his fingers as he lit his pipe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tremor - a slight shaking movement or sound, especially in the body
  • Synonym: quiver - a slight, rapid shaking or trembling movement

Etymology

From Latin palpitare (to tremble, to throb).
