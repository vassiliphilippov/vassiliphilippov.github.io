epergne

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈpɜrn / (AmE), / ɪˈpɜːn / (BrE)
an ornamental centerpiece for a dining table, typically with several branching holders for fruit, sweets, or flowers
ornamental table centerpiece with holders

Examples

The ornate epergne at the center of the table held fresh flowers and ripe fruit.
She inherited a silver epergne that had been in her family for generations.
The wedding feast was made more elegant by a crystal epergne displaying candies and roses.

Etymology

From French 'épergne', possibly from 'épargner' meaning to save.
