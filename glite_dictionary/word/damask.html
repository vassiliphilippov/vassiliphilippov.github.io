damask

1. ADJ

/ ˈdæm.æsk / (AmE), / ˈdam.əsk / (BrE)
(of fabric) having a woven pattern, typically with a subtle sheen, often used for tablecloths or upholstery.
having a woven patterned fabric

Examples

The table was elegantly set with damask napkins and polished silverware.
She admired the intricate damask curtains hanging in the old manor house.
His jacket was lined with a luxurious, red damask fabric.

Synset Examples

damask table linens

Etymology

From Middle English damask, from Old French damas, from Medieval Latin damascus, from Damascus, the Syrian city where such fabric was originally made.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdæməsk / (AmE), / ˈdaməsk / (BrE)
a rich, woven fabric, typically with a pattern visible on both sides, often used for table linen and made from linen, silk, or cotton
rich patterned fabric for table linen

Examples

The dinner table was beautifully set with white damask and fine china.
She inherited her grandmother’s collection of damask tablecloths.
The hotel dining room featured heavy damask linens on every table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brocade - a rich fabric woven with a raised pattern, often of gold or silver thread

Etymology

From Medieval Latin damascus, from Damascus (the city in Syria where such fabrics were originally produced).

3. NOUN

/ ˈdæməsk / (AmE), / ˈdaməsk / (BrE)
A richly patterned, reversible fabric of linen, cotton, silk, or wool, often used for table linen and upholstery.
reversible patterned fabric

Examples

The dining room table was covered with a beautiful damask tablecloth for the holiday meal.
She chose pale blue damask curtains to add elegance to the living room.
Antique armchairs upholstered in damask gave the room a sophisticated look.

Etymology

From French 'damas', from Italian 'damasco', from Damascus, the city where the fabric was originally made.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
