caranday

1. NOUN

/ ˌkær.ənˈdeɪ / (AmE), / ˌkær.ənˈdeɪ / (BrE)
A South American palm (Copernicia alba) that produces a wax similar to carnauba wax.
South American palm yielding wax

Examples

The caranday thrives in the wetlands of South America, where it is valued for its durable wax.
Farmers collect the wax from caranday leaves to use in polishes and cosmetics.
The habitat of the caranday is threatened by agricultural expansion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carnauba - A Brazilian palm tree known for its wax

Etymology

From Spanish 'caranday', from Guaraní 'karanda’y', referring to the palm.
