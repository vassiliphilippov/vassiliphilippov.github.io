graffiti

1. NOUN

/ ɡrəˈfiti / (AmE), / ɡrəˈfiːti / (BrE)
Writing or drawings scribbled, scratched, or sprayed illicitly on a wall or other surface in a public place.
illicit writing or drawings on surfaces

Examples

The walls of the abandoned warehouse were covered in colorful graffiti.
City officials launched a campaign to remove graffiti from public spaces.
He was fined for spray-painting graffiti on a historic monument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vandalism - deliberate destruction or damage to property, especially public property

Etymology

From Italian 'graffiti', plural of 'graffito', meaning 'a scratch'.
