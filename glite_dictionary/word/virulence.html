virulence

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪrjələns / (AmE), / ˈvɪərʊləns / (BrE)
the degree of harmfulness or severity of a disease or the ability of a microorganism to cause disease
degree of a microorganism's harmfulness

Examples

The virulence of the new strain of the virus has alarmed scientists across the globe.
Researchers are studying the virulence factors that make certain bacteria more dangerous.
The decline in virulence over time made the disease less deadly.

Synset Examples

the virulence of the plague

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pathogenicity - the ability of an organism to cause disease

Etymology

From Latin 'virulentia' (poisonous quality), from 'virus' (poison).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɪr.jə.ləns / (AmE), / ˈvɪə.rʊ.ləns / (BrE)
the quality of being extremely hostile or harmful in attitude, expression, or action
extreme hostility or harmfulness

Examples

The virulence of his response shocked everyone at the meeting.
She was surprised by the virulence of the criticism directed at her proposal.
Online debates can quickly take on a tone of virulence if not moderated.

Synset Examples

the virulence of the malicious old man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hostility - unfriendly or antagonistic behavior
  • Antonym: benignity - kindness; opposite of hostility

Etymology

From Latin 'virulentia' (full of poison), from 'virus' (poison).

WordNet ID: oewn-04665718-n
Synset POS: n
