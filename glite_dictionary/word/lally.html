lally

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæli / (AmE), / ˈlæli / (BrE)
A support column made from a steel cylinder filled with concrete, commonly used in construction to provide structural support.
steel-and-concrete support column

Examples

The basement renovation included installing a new lally to support the upper floor.
Workers secured the beam with a lally during the construction process.
Inspectors checked the lally columns for rust and structural integrity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pillar - an upright support for a structure
  • Synonym: column - a vertical structural element, often used for support

Etymology

From the name of U.S. inventor John Lally, who patented this type of column in the late 19th century.
