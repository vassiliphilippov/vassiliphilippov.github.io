spareness

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛr.nəs / (AmE), / ˈspɛə.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of having little body fat; leanness or thinness of physique
leanness; little body fat

Examples

Her spareness made her appear taller and more graceful.
The model's spareness was noted by the fashion critics during the show.
Recent health trends often emphasize spareness over a more muscular build.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leanness - the quality of being thin or having little fat
  • Synonym: thinness - the state of being thin in form or appearance

Etymology

From 'spare' (lean, thin) + '-ness', Middle English and Old English roots.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɛr.nəs / (AmE), / ˈspɛə.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being scanty, bare, or lacking in density or fullness.
quality of being scanty or sparse

Examples

The spareness of the furniture accentuated the room’s quiet elegance.
Many admired the spareness of the poet’s writing style, which left nothing unnecessary.
The artist’s work is known for its spareness and subtle use of space.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sparsity - the state of being sparse or in short supply
  • Antonym: density - the degree of compactness, opposite of spareness

Etymology

From 'spare' + '-ness', where 'spare' comes from Old English 'spǣr' (scarce, frugal).
