clogged

1. ADJ

/ klɑgd / (AmE), / klɒgd / (BrE)
blocked or filled so as to hinder movement or flow.
blocked or obstructed

Examples

The drain became clogged with hair and soap scum over time.
Traffic was clogged on the highway after the accident.
Her nose felt clogged due to her cold.

Synset Examples

The wings of birds were clogged with ice and snow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blocked - unable to pass through because of an obstruction
  • Antonym: unclogged - free from obstruction or blockage

Etymology

From Middle English 'clog' (a lump or block), origin uncertain.

2. ADJ

/ klɑgd / (AmE), / klɒgd / (BrE)
(of a substance) thickened or stuck together in soft, thick lumps; coalesced into a mass or clot.
thickened or stuck together in lumps

Examples

The drain became clogged with wet leaves after the storm.
After standing for hours, the gravy was clogged and unappetizing.
The printer jammed because the ink nozzles were clogged with dried pigment.

Synset Examples

clotted blood
seeds clogged together

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clotted - having formed into clots or lumps

Etymology

From the verb 'clog', meaning to block or become thickened, of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02424560-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ klɑgd / (AmE), / klɒgd / (BrE)
blocked or obstructed so that movement or flow is made difficult or impossible
blocked or obstructed

Examples

The kitchen sink was clogged with grease after the big family dinner.
Traffic was clogged on the highway due to the accident.
My nose is clogged because of this terrible cold.

Synset Examples

clogged pipes
clogged up freeways
streets choked with traffic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blocked - not allowing movement or flow; obstructed
  • Antonym: unclogged - no longer blocked or obstructed

Etymology

Past participle of 'clog,' from Middle English 'clogge' (block, hindrance).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01625449-s
Synset POS: s
