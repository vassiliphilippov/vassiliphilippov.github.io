Antlia

1. PROPN

/ ˈæntliə / (AmE), / ˈantliə / (BrE)
a faint constellation in the Southern Hemisphere located near Hydra and Vela
a southern constellation

Examples

The constellation Antlia is barely visible due to its faint stars.
Astronomers have identified several deep-sky objects within Antlia.
On a clear night in the Southern Hemisphere, you might catch a glimpse of Antlia.

Etymology

From Latin 'antlia' (pump), the constellation was named by French astronomer Nicolas Louis de Lacaille in the 18th century.
