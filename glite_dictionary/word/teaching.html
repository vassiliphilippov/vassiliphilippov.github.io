teaching

1. NOUN

/ ˈtiː.tʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtiː.tʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the profession or practice of being a teacher, especially as a career
the profession of a teacher

Examples

She decided to pursue a career in teaching after graduating from university.
Many people believe that teaching is one of the most rewarding professions.
During his years in teaching, he influenced the lives of countless students.

Synset Examples

he prepared for teaching while still in college
pedagogy is recognized as an important profession

Related Terms

  • Synonym: education - the process of giving or receiving systematic instruction
  • Synonym: pedagogy - the theory and practice of teaching

Etymology

From Old English *tǣċan* (to show, teach).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtiːtʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtiːtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
a principle or doctrine that is taught, especially as part of a religion or philosophy
principle or doctrine taught

Examples

Buddhist teachings emphasize compassion and mindfulness.
She devoted her life to spreading the teachings of her faith throughout the community.
Many people find inspiration in the teachings of ancient philosophers.

Synset Examples

the teachings of religion
he believed all the Christian precepts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doctrine - a belief or set of beliefs taught by a church, political party, or other group
  • Synonym: precept - a general rule or principle intended to regulate behavior or thought

3. NOUN

/ ˈtiːt͡ʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtiːtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or profession of imparting knowledge or instructing others, especially in an educational setting
the act of imparting knowledge

Examples

Teaching requires patience, creativity, and a deep understanding of the subject matter.
She has dedicated her entire career to teaching children in underprivileged areas.
Modern technology has introduced new tools and methods into classroom teaching.

Synset Examples

he received no formal education
our instruction was carefully programmed
good classroom teaching is seldom rewarded

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instruction - the act of teaching or providing knowledge
  • Antonym: learning - the process of acquiring knowledge or skills

Etymology

From Old English *tǣcan* (to show, instruct) + -ing, denoting action or process.
