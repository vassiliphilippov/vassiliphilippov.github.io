beanstalk

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiːnˌstɑːk / (AmE), / ˈbiːnˌstɔːk / (BrE)
The tall, slender stem of a bean plant, especially one that is rapidly growing.
stem of a bean plant

Examples

The children gazed up at the towering beanstalk in the school garden.
In the story, Jack climbed a magical beanstalk that reached the clouds.
After a week of rain, the beanstalk seemed to double in height.

Etymology

bean (from Old English 'bēan') + stalk (from Old English 'stælca', 'stolca', meaning 'stem' or 'support').
