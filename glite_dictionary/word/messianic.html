messianic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɛsiˈænɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛsiˈanɪk / (BrE)
Relating to a messiah or to the hope of deliverance or salvation, especially in a religious or spiritual context.
relating to a messiah or savior

Examples

The leader was seen by his followers as having a messianic role in their community.
The movement was fueled by a messianic belief in a coming savior.
Her speeches had a messianic tone that inspired great hope.

Synset Examples

messianic cult

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prophetic - relating to prophecy or prediction of the future
  • Antonym: secular - not connected with religious or spiritual matters

Etymology

From Hebrew 'mashiach' (anointed one), via Greek 'messias' and Late Latin 'Messianicus'.
