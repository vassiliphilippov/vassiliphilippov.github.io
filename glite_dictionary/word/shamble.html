shamble

1. VERB

/ ˈʃæm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈʃæm.bəl / (BrE)
to walk in an awkward or unsteady way, dragging the feet
walk dragging feet; move awkwardly

Examples

The old man shambled across the park, barely lifting his feet.
After the long flight, she shambled off the plane looking exhausted.
Wounded and weary, he shambled back to camp at sunset.

Synset Examples

he shuffled out of the room
We heard his feet shuffling down the hall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shuffle - to walk by dragging one's feet

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly related to earlier noun 'shamble', from Old English 'scamel' (stool, footstool), associated with a shuffling gait.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01921542-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃæmbəl / (AmE), / ˈʃæmbəl / (BrE)
a slow, awkward way of walking in which you drag your feet along the ground without lifting them fully
a slow, dragging walk

Examples

After hours of hiking, his shamble revealed his exhaustion.
The old man's shamble across the street drew concerned looks from passersby.
His characteristic shamble was easy to spot from a distance.

Synset Examples

from his shambling I assumed he was very old

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shuffle - to walk by dragging one's feet along the ground

Etymology

Probably from earlier sense 'scene of carnage', influenced by obsolete verb 'to shamble' (move awkwardly).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00293556-n
Synset POS: n
