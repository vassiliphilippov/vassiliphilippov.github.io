Dalmatian

1. NOUN

/ dælˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / dælˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
A native or inhabitant of Dalmatia, a region along the Adriatic coast in Croatia.
native or inhabitant of Dalmatia (Croatia)

Examples

The museum featured artifacts collected by a prominent Dalmatian during the 19th century.
As a proud Dalmatian, she often shared stories about her homeland's culture.
Several Dalmatians immigrated to the United States in the early 20th century.

Etymology

From Latin Dalmatia, referring to the region along the Adriatic coast.

2. ADJ

/ dælˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / dælˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
of or relating to Dalmatia or its inhabitants
relating to Dalmatia or its people

Examples

Many Dalmatian cities have a rich, multicultural heritage.
She is proud of her Dalmatian ancestry.
The Dalmatian coastline is famous for its clear waters and stunning scenery.

Etymology

From Dalmatia, a historical region along the Adriatic coast (now part of Croatia), with the suffix -ian.

3. NOUN

/ dælˈmeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / dælˈmeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a large breed of dog with a smooth white coat and distinctive black or brown spots, originally from Dalmatia
a spotted breed of dog

Examples

The family adopted a playful Dalmatian from the animal shelter.
A Dalmatian is easily recognized by its unique black spots.
Their Dalmatian loves to run alongside them during morning jogs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dog - a domesticated carnivorous mammal commonly kept as a pet

Etymology

Named after Dalmatia, a historical region on the Adriatic coast (now part of Croatia), where the breed originated.
