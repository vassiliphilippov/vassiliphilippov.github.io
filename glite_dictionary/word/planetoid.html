planetoid

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæn.ɪ.tɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈplæn.ɪ.tɔɪd / (BrE)
a small celestial body that orbits the sun, especially one resembling a small planet; an asteroid
small celestial body orbiting the sun

Examples

The telescope revealed a distant planetoid moving slowly beyond Neptune.
Scientists discovered a new planetoid with an unusual orbit in the asteroid belt.
Many planetoids are composed of rock, ice, and metal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asteroid - small rocky body orbiting the sun, especially between Mars and Jupiter

Etymology

From Greek 'planētēs' (wanderer) and English '-oid' (resembling).
