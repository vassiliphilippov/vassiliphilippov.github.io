inflexibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.flɛk.səˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɪn.flɛk.sɪˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality of being rigid, unyielding, or unwilling to change; extreme severity or lack of adaptability
rigid or unyielding quality

Examples

His inflexibility made it impossible to negotiate a compromise with the committee.
The inflexibility of the new policy caused frustration among staff members.
She was admired for her principles but sometimes criticized for her inflexibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rigidity - the quality of being stiff or unyielding
  • Antonym: flexibility - the quality of bending easily without breaking and being adaptable

Etymology

From Latin prefix in- (not) + flexibility.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnflɛkˈsɪbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌɪnflɛkˈsɪbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being unable to bend easily; a lack of physical flexibility
lack of ability to bend or flex

Examples

Her inflexibility made it difficult for her to touch her toes during yoga class.
The patient complained of inflexibility in his knees after the injury.
Aging can often lead to increased inflexibility in the muscles and joints.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flexibility - the ability to bend easily or adapt

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + flexibilis (flexible).
