tamanoir

1. NOUN

/ təˌmɑː.nwɑːr / (AmE), / təˌmæn.wɑː / (BrE)
A large, shaggy-haired, toothless anteater of South America, with a long tongue and powerful claws; also known as the giant anteater.
giant anteater of South America

Examples

The tamanoir is native to the grasslands and forests of Central and South America.
A tamanoir uses its long tongue to extract ants and termites from their nests.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the tamanoir in Brazil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant anteater - a large, toothless, long-snouted anteater of South America

Etymology

From French 'tamanoir', ultimately from Tupi via French, referring to the giant anteater.
