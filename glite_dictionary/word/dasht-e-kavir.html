Dasht-e-Kavir

1. PROPN

/ dɑːʃt eɪ kəˈvɪr / (AmE), / dæʃt eɪ kəˈvɪə / (BrE)
a large salt desert in north central Iran, known for its harsh climate and salt flats
salt desert in Iran

Examples

The Dasht-e-Kavir is famous for its vast stretches of salt and sand dunes.
Scientists study the unique climate of the Dasht-e-Kavir to learn about desert ecosystems.
Few plants or animals can survive the extreme conditions of the Dasht-e-Kavir.

Etymology

From Persian دشتِ كوير (Dašt-e Kavīr), meaning 'Great Salt Desert'.
