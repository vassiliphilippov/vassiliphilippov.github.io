heterotaxy

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛt̬.ə.roʊˌtæk.si / (AmE), / ˈhɛt.ər.əʊˌtæk.si / (BrE)
A condition in which the internal organs of the body are abnormally arranged.
abnormal arrangement of internal organs
medical

Examples

The surgeon diagnosed the infant with heterotaxy after noticing the unusual positions of the heart and liver.
Patients with heterotaxy may require complex surgical procedures to correct their organ positions.
Genetic testing is often recommended for families affected by heterotaxy.

Etymology

From Greek hetero- (different) and taxis (arrangement).
