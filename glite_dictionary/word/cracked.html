cracked

1. ADJ

/ krækt / (AmE), / krækt / (BrE)
(of skin) roughened and split as a result of dryness, cold, or exposure to the elements.
skin roughened and split by cold or dryness

Examples

After working outside all winter, her cracked hands needed extra moisturizer.
The cold wind left his lips dry and cracked.
Her heels became painfully cracked after walking barefoot all summer.

2. ADJ

/ krækt / (AmE), / krækt / (BrE)
(of paint or varnish) having a surface marked by many small cracks, resembling alligator skin.
paint/varnish marked with small cracks

Examples

The old dresser's cracked paint gave it a distinctive antique look.
Restorers carefully examined the cracked surface of the oil painting before starting their work.
The sunlight revealed how cracked the varnish on the wooden table had become over the years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: craquelure - fine pattern of cracks in paint or varnish, especially on paintings
