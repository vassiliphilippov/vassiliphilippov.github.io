civic

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪvɪk / (AmE), / ˈsɪvɪk / (BrE)
relating to a city or to the duties or activities of people in relation to their town, city, or local area
relating to a city or its citizens

Examples

The city council launched a civic campaign to encourage recycling among residents.
She felt a strong sense of civic responsibility to volunteer in her community.
The new civic center will host concerts, exhibitions, and public meetings.

Synset Examples

civic center
civic problems

Related Terms

  • Synonym: municipal - relating to a town or city and its governing body
  • Antonym: rural - relating to the countryside rather than the city

Etymology

From Latin 'civicus' (relating to a citizen), from 'civis' (citizen).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɪvɪk / (AmE), / ˈsɪvɪk / (BrE)
relating to a city, citizens, or the duties and activities of people in relation to their town or community
relating to citizens or city affairs

Examples

The mayor encouraged civic engagement by inviting residents to participate in local decision-making.
Voting in elections is considered a fundamental civic responsibility.
She was honored for her civic contributions to the community.

Synset Examples

civil rights
civil liberty
civic duties
civic pride

Related Terms

  • Synonym: civil - relating to ordinary citizens and their concerns

Etymology

From Latin 'civicus' (relating to a citizen), from 'civis' (citizen).

Synset POS: a
