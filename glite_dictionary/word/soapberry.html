soapberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊpˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈsəʊpˌbɛri / (BrE)
a tree of the genus Sapindus, or its fruit, which contains saponin and is used as a natural soap
tree or fruit yielding natural soap

Examples

The dried soapberry fruit can be used as a natural detergent for washing clothes.
Many species of soapberry are native to tropical and subtropical regions.
She used soapberry extract in her homemade cleaning products.

Etymology

From 'soap' + 'berry', referring to the fruit's soap-like properties.
