Aloeaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌæloʊˈiːsiːˌiː / (AmE), / ˌaləʊˈiːsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, including aloes, formerly recognized in some botanical classification systems as distinct from the Liliaceae, but not widely accepted
a plant family including aloes (botanical)
medical

Examples

The Aloeaceae include many succulent species adapted to dry climates.
Older botanical texts often refer to the Aloeaceae as a separate family from the Liliaceae.
Some scientists no longer recognize Aloeaceae as a valid plant family.

Etymology

From genus name Aloe + Latin -aceae (denoting plant families).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæloʊˈiːsiːˌiː / (AmE), / ˌaləʊˈiːsiːˌiː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants, formerly recognized in some classification systems as distinct from Liliaceae, but not widely accepted.
plant family (Aloeaceae)

Examples

Some taxonomists still recognize the Aloeaceae as a distinct plant family.
The Aloeaceae includes the well-known genus Aloe, famous for its medicinal properties.
Modern classification often merges Aloeaceae into the larger family Asphodelaceae.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Liliaceae - a family of flowering plants known as the lily family

Etymology

From the genus name Aloe, from Greek aloē (a kind of plant).
