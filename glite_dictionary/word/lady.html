lady

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪdi / (AmE), / ˈleɪdi / (BrE)
A woman who holds the rank of peerage in Britain, such as the wife or female equivalent of a lord, or a woman who holds the title in her own right.
woman of the British nobility
british

Examples

As a lady of the realm, she was entitled to attend royal ceremonies.
Lady Diana became internationally famous as a lady in the British peerage.
The title of lady was passed to her after her father's death.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lord - a man of the British peerage

2. NOUN

/ ˈleɪdi / (AmE), / ˈleɪdi / (BrE)
A polite or respectful way of referring to a woman.
polite term for a woman

Examples

The lady at the front desk answered all my questions politely.
Please allow the lady to go ahead of you in line.
A kind lady offered to help me carry the groceries.

Synset Examples

a nice lady at the library helped me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woman - An adult female human being.

Etymology

From Old English *hlǣfdīge* (female head of a household, noblewoman).

3. NOUN

/ ˈleɪdi / (AmE), / ˈleɪdi / (BrE)
A woman of refinement, good manners, or high social position.
woman of refinement or high social position

Examples

She was considered a true lady in both her behavior and her dress.
At the gala, a distinguished lady greeted the guests with grace.
The lady at the front of the room spoke with quiet authority and elegance.

Synset Examples

a chauffeur opened the door of the limousine for the grand lady

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gentleman - a man of refinement or good manners

Etymology

From Old English hlǣfdīge, originally meaning 'bread kneader' or 'mistress of the house.'
