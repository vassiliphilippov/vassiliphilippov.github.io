unsavoury

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈseɪvəri / (AmE), / ʌnˈseɪvəri / (BrE)
having an unpleasant smell or taste; not pleasing to the senses of smell or taste.
unpleasant in smell or taste

Examples

The refrigerator contained some unsavoury leftovers that needed to be thrown out.
She grimaced at the unsavoury odor coming from the garbage bin.
The soup had an unsavoury taste that made everyone avoid it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unpleasant - not pleasing or enjoyable
  • Synonym: disagreeable - not pleasant or likable, especially in taste or smell

Etymology

From un- (not) + savoury (from Old French savouré, from Latin sapor, taste).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈseɪvəri / (AmE), / ʌnˈseɪvəri / (BrE)
Unpleasant or morally offensive, especially in relation to character or reputation.
morally offensive or unpleasant
british

Examples

The company avoided any unsavoury dealings that could harm its reputation.
He was dismissed from his job due to an unsavoury incident in his past.
They were concerned about the unsavoury characters hanging around the neighborhood.

Synset Examples

an unsavory reputation
an unsavory scandal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repugnant - extremely distasteful or unacceptable
  • Synonym: disreputable - not considered to be respectable in character or appearance
  • Antonym: respectable - regarded by society to be good, proper, or correct

Etymology

From un- (not) + savoury, where 'savoury' comes from Old French 'savoré' (tasty, agreeable), itself from Latin 'sapor' (taste).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01632146-a
Synset POS: a
