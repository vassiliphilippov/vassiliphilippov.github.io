Anarhichas

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈrɪkəs / (AmE), / ˌænəˈrɪkəs / (BrE)
A genus of large marine fishes, commonly known as wolffish, found in northern seas.
genus of marine wolffish

Examples

The Anarhichas genus includes several species commonly referred to as wolffish.
Fishermen often encounter Anarhichas in the cold waters of the North Atlantic.
Biologists study the teeth of Anarhichas to understand their feeding habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wolffish - common name for fishes of the Anarhichas genus

Etymology

From Greek anarikhos, a type of fish.
