guarantor

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡɛrənˈtɔr / (AmE), / ˌɡærənˈtɔː / (BrE)
a person or organization that promises to pay a debt or fulfill an obligation if another person fails to do so.
person who guarantees a debt or obligation

Examples

The bank required a guarantor before approving the loan application.
If the tenant defaults on payment, the guarantor must cover the outstanding rent.
Her father agreed to act as a guarantor for her college loan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surety - a person who takes responsibility for another's performance of an undertaking
  • Antonym: debtor - a person who owes a debt

Etymology

From Old French garant (warrant, pledge), from Germanic origin, related to 'warrant'.
