specter

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛktər / (AmE), / ˈspɛktə / (BrE)
a ghost or visible spirit, especially one of a terrifying nature
a ghostly apparition

Examples

The old mansion was rumored to be haunted by a specter that appeared at midnight.
She claimed to see a specter drifting down the hallway.
The unexpected sight of a specter sent chills down his spine.

Synset Examples

we were unprepared for the apparition that confronted us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ghost - the spirit of a dead person that appears to the living
  • Synonym: apparition - a ghost or ghostlike image of a person

Etymology

From Latin 'spectrum' (appearance, image, apparition), via French 'spectre'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɛktər / (AmE), / ˈspɛktə / (BrE)
a haunting or troubling memory, thought, or fear that preoccupies the mind
haunting memory or fear

Examples

The specter of failure haunted him throughout his career.
Every time she returned home, the specter of her childhood trauma resurfaced.
The memories of the war cast a long specter over the community.

Synset Examples

he looked like he had seen a ghost
it aroused specters from his past

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ghost - the spirit of a dead person; apparition
  • Synonym: phantom - something seen, heard, or sensed, but having no physical reality

Etymology

From Latin 'spectrum' (appearance, apparition), through French 'spectre'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05906778-n
Synset POS: n
