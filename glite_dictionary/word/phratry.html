phratry

1. NOUN

/ ˈfreɪ.tri / (AmE), / ˈfreɪ.tri / (BrE)
(in anthropology) a social group or division in some societies, consisting of several clans or families claiming descent from a common ancestor.
social group of related clans

Examples

The tribe was divided into two phratries, each with its own traditions and responsibilities.
Membership in a phratry determined marriage rules and social status in the community.
Anthropologists studied how the phratry maintained unity among the various clans.

Synset Examples

his family has lived in Massachusetts since the Mayflower

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clan - a group of close-knit and interrelated families

Etymology

From Greek 'phratra' (brotherhood, clan).
