enterobacteria

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.təroʊ.bækˈtɪr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌɛn.tərəʊ.bækˈtɪə.ri.ə / (BrE)
Rod-shaped, Gram-negative bacteria that occur normally or pathogenically in the intestines of humans and other animals.
bacteria found in the intestines
medical

Examples

Some enterobacteria are harmless, while others can cause serious intestinal infections.
The doctor explained that enterobacteria are often found in the gut of healthy humans.
Researchers are studying how different enterobacteria contribute to antibiotic resistance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Escherichia coli - a common species of enterobacteria found in the intestines
  • Synonym: pathogen - a bacterium, virus, or other microorganism that can cause disease

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'entero-' (intestine) and 'bacteria'.
