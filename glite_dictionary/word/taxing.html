taxing

1. ADJ

/ ˈtæksɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtæksɪŋ / (BrE)
Demanding great effort or endurance; difficult and tiring.
requiring much effort; exhausting

Examples

Climbing the steep mountain trail was especially taxing for the inexperienced hikers.
The long and taxing negotiations lasted late into the night.
Preparing for final exams can be both mentally and physically taxing.

Synset Examples

the burdensome task of preparing the income tax return
my duties weren't onerous: I only had to greet the guests
a taxing schedule

Related Terms

  • Synonym: onerous - involving a great deal of effort or difficulty
  • Synonym: burdensome - causing difficulty or worry
  • Antonym: easy - not difficult; requiring little effort

Etymology

From the verb 'tax' meaning to impose a burden, with the present participle suffix '-ing'.
