china

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪnə / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪnə / (BrE)
high-quality porcelain, especially tableware, originally made in China and noted for its fineness and translucency
high-quality porcelain tableware

Examples

We only use the china for special occasions, like weddings and holidays.
Carefully washing the delicate china, she admired its intricate blue pattern.
The tea set was made of fine china imported from England.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porcelain - a white, translucent ceramic material

Etymology

From China, the country where porcelain was first made and exported to Europe.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪnə / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪnə / (BrE)
Delicate, high-quality dishware made of porcelain, often used for formal occasions.
porcelain dishware

Examples

We only use the china when guests come over for dinner.
She accidentally broke one of the antique china plates.
The china set was passed down through generations in her family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porcelain - a white, vitrified, translucent ceramic

Etymology

From the country name China, because fine porcelain was originally imported from China.
