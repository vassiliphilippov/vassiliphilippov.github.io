ancient

1. ADJ

/ ˈeɪn.tʃənt / (AmE), / ˈeɪn.tʃənt / (BrE)
very old; having existed for a very long time
very old; existing for a long time

Examples

The museum houses several ancient artifacts from the Bronze Age.
She told stories about her ancient childhood home, which still stands today.
The ancient trees in the forest have survived for over a thousand years.

Synset Examples

an ancient mariner

Related Terms

  • Antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times
  • Degree Lower: old - having lived or existed for a long time

Etymology

From Latin 'antianus' (former, ancient), from 'ante' (before).

2. ADJ

/ ˈeɪnʃənt / (AmE), / ˈeɪnʃənt / (BrE)
belonging to a very distant past, especially before the end of the Western Roman Empire
very old; from distant historical times

Examples

The ruins are a reminder of ancient civilizations that once flourished here.
Many legends have their roots in ancient history.
The city is famous for its ancient temples and monuments.

Synset Examples

ancient history
ancient civilizations such as those of the Etruscans and Sumerians
ancient Greece

Related Terms

  • Antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times

Etymology

From Latin 'ancientem', from 'ante' meaning 'before'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01732846-s
Synset POS: s
