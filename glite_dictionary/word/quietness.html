quietness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwaɪətnəs / (AmE), / ˈkwaɪətnɪs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being quiet; absence of noise or disturbance.
state of being quiet

Examples

She enjoyed the quietness of the early morning before the city woke up.
After the guests had left, a soothing quietness filled the house.
Many people seek quietness in nature to escape the noise of daily life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silence - complete absence of sound

Etymology

From quiet + -ness, based on Latin 'quietus' (rest, calm).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwaɪətnəs / (AmE), / ˈkwaɪətnəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being quiet; absence of noise or sound
absence of noise or sound

Examples

The quietness of the library made it easy to concentrate on my reading.
She was comforted by the quietness that filled the house after everyone had left.
As night fell, a deep quietness settled over the village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silence - absence of any sound or noise
  • Antonym: noisiness - the quality of making much noise

Etymology

From Latin 'quietus' (calm, at rest) via Old French 'quiet' and Middle English.
