thyrse

1. NOUN

/ θɜrs / (AmE), / ˈθɜːs / (BrE)
a dense flower cluster, such as that of the lilac or horse chestnut, in which the main axis has a racemose structure and the branches are cymose
type of dense flower cluster

Examples

The lilac bush was covered in purple thyrses during the spring bloom.
Under the microscope, the botanist identified the flower arrangement as a classic thyrse.
Certain species of horse chestnut produce impressive thyrses that attract many pollinators.

Etymology

From Latin thyrsus (stalk, staff), from Greek thyrsos (a stalk, staff).
