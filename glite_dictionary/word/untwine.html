to untwine

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈtwaɪn / (AmE), / ʌnˈtwaɪn / (BrE)
to separate things that have been twisted or wound together
separate twisted or wound things

Examples

She carefully began to untwine the vines that had wrapped around the garden gate.
It took him an hour to untwine all the cables under his desk.
The kitten tried to untwine the ball of yarn with its tiny paws.

Synset Examples

untwine the strings

Related Terms

  • Antonym: twine - to twist together

Etymology

From un- (reversing action) + twine (from Old English twīnan, to twist, wind).
