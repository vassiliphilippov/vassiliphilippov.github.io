Haliotidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæliˈɑːtɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌhæliˈəʊtɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine gastropod mollusks known commonly as abalones, characterized by their ear-shaped shells with a row of respiratory pores.
family of abalones (marine mollusks)

Examples

The Haliotidae family includes species valued for their colorful shells and edible flesh.
Marine biologists study Haliotidae to understand their ecological role in coastal environments.
Collectors often seek out rare shells from the Haliotidae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abalone - a type of marine mollusk with an ear-shaped shell, edible

Etymology

From Modern Latin Haliotidae, based on genus Haliotis (from Greek halios 'of the sea' + ōtos 'ear').
