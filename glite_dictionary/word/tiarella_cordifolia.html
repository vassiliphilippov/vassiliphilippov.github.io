Tiarella cordifolia

1. NOUN

/ tiˈɛrələ kɔrˈdɪfoʊliə / (AmE), / taɪˈærələ kɔːˈdɪfəʊliə / (BrE)
A North American woodland plant with white flowers that blooms in spring and spreads by stolons; also known as foamflower.
North American woodland plant (foamflower)

Examples

The shady garden was filled with clusters of Tiarella cordifolia in full bloom.
Many gardeners appreciate Tiarella cordifolia for its delicate white flowers and attractive foliage.
Tiarella cordifolia spreads easily, making it an excellent ground cover for woodland areas.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Tiarella' (diminutive of 'tiara', a type of crown) and 'cordifolia' meaning 'heart-leaved'.
