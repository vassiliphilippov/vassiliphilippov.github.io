Saki

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɑːki / (AmE), / ˈsɑːki / (BrE)
The pen name of Hector Hugh Munro (1870–1916), a British writer known for his witty, mischievous, and sometimes macabre short stories.
British short story writer (1870–1916)

Examples

Many of Saki's stories feature unexpected and darkly humorous twists.
In English literature classes, students often read Saki for his sharp wit and satire.
Despite his light tone, Saki often exposed the darker side of Edwardian society.

Etymology

Pen name of Hector Hugh Munro, possibly chosen after a character in the Rubáiyát of Omar Khayyám.
