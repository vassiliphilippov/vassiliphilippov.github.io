palette

1. NOUN

/ ˈpælət / (AmE), / ˈpælɪt / (BrE)
one of the rounded armor plates located at the armpits of a suit of armor, designed to protect the wearer
armpit armor plate on a suit of armor

Examples

The knight's palette deflected the blade away from his vulnerable armpit.
Each palette was carefully shaped to allow for arm movement while offering protection.
Conservators restored the dented palette from the 15th-century suit of armor.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpælət / (AmE), / ˈpælət / (BrE)
the range of colors characteristic of a particular artist, painting, or school of art
range of colors used by an artist or school

Examples

The artist’s use of a vivid palette brought the landscape to life.
Impressionist painters are known for their distinctive palette of soft pastels and bright hues.
Her new collection features a darker palette than her earlier work, reflecting a change in mood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color scheme - an arrangement or combination of colors

Etymology

From French *palette*, diminutive of *pale* (spade, blade), originally referring to the painter’s board.

4. NOUN

/ ˈpælət / (AmE), / ˈpælət / (BrE)
a thin, usually oval or rectangular board with a thumb hole at one end, used by artists to mix colors of paint; also, the range of colors typically used by a particular artist, painting, or school of art
artist's paint-mixing board or color range

Examples

The artist squeezed new paint onto his palette before starting the portrait.
Her use of a muted palette gave the painting a dreamy, nostalgic feel.
Digital illustrators can choose from a wide palette of colors on their screens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color scheme - a planned selection of colors used in a design or artwork

Etymology

From French 'palette' (small shovel, palette), diminutive of 'pale' (shovel), from Latin 'pala' (spade, shovel).
