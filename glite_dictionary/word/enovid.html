Enovid

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛnəˌvɪd / (AmE), / ˈɛnəˌvɪd / (BrE)
A trade name for a pioneering oral contraceptive drug containing mestranol and norethynodrel, first marketed in the 1960s.
oral contraceptive trade name (historical)

Examples

The introduction of Enovid revolutionized birth control methods in the United States.
Doctors began prescribing Enovid as one of the first oral contraceptives in the early 1960s.
Due to evolving medical standards, Enovid is no longer available on the market.
