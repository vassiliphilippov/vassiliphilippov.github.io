cadet

1. NOUN

/ kəˈdɛt / (AmE), / kəˈdɛt / (BrE)
a young person undergoing training to become an officer in the armed forces, especially at a military academy
military trainee at an academy

Examples

After graduating from high school, he applied to become a cadet at West Point.
The cadet marched smartly across the parade ground during the morning drills.
Each cadet must maintain excellent grades and physical fitness throughout their training.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: officer - a person with a position of authority in the armed forces

Etymology

From French 'cadet' (younger son or junior), originally Gascon dialect.
