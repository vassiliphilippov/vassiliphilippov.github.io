falsifying

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔl.sɪˌfaɪ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːl.sɪˌfaɪ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of proving or making something false, especially by presenting false evidence or information
the act of making or proving false

Examples

The scientist was accused of falsifying results to fit his theory.
The falsifying of official records is a serious offense.
He spent hours falsifying documents to cover up the fraud.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: falsification - the act of falsifying information or the condition of being falsified
  • Synonym: fabrication - the act of inventing false information
  • Antonym: verifying - the act of confirming the truth of something

Etymology

From Latin 'falsificare' (to make false), via 'falsify.'
