stayer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈsteɪ.ə / (BrE)
a person or animal who has stamina and endurance, especially one able to persist or last to the end of a demanding activity
person/animal with endurance

Examples

The marathon winner proved she was a real stayer by keeping up her pace throughout the race.
Among all the runners, John was the only stayer who didn't tire as the event went on.
That horse is known to be a strong stayer over long distances.

Synset Examples

the horse that won the race is a good stayer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finisher - a person or animal that completes a race or task
  • Antonym: quitter - a person who gives up easily or does not finish

Etymology

From the verb 'stay' (to remain or endure) with agentive suffix '-er'.
