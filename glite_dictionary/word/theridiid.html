theridiid

1. NOUN

/ θɛˈrɪdiˌɪd / (AmE), / θɛˈrɪdɪɪd / (BrE)
A spider belonging to the family Theridiidae, characterized by a comb-like row of bristles on each hind foot.
spider of the Theridiidae family

Examples

The black widow is a well-known species of theridiid found in many regions.
Researchers discovered a new theridiid with unusually long bristles on its hind legs.
Some theridiids build tangled webs in dark corners of houses.

Etymology

From modern Latin Theridiidae, the scientific family name, from Greek *thēridion* (a kind of spider).
