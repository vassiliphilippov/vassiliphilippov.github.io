murk

1. NOUN

/ mɝk / (AmE), / mɜːk / (BrE)
a thick, dark, or cloudy atmosphere that makes it difficult to see
thick, cloudy atmosphere

Examples

A heavy murk settled over the harbor, hiding the ships from view.
Through the evening murk, only a few dim lights could be seen in the distance.
The forest was shrouded in murk after the thunderstorm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mist - fine spray or light fog reducing visibility
  • Synonym: fog - thick cloud of tiny water droplets reducing visibility
  • Antonym: clarity - the state of being clear or easy to see through

Etymology

From Middle English 'murke', variant of 'mirke', from Old Norse 'myrkr' (darkness).

2. VERB

/ mɜːrk / (AmE), / mɜːk / (BrE)
to make dark, dim, or gloomy; to cloud or obscure with darkness or murkiness
to make dark or gloomy

Examples

Low fog had begun to murk the city streets, making the lamps glow dimly against the gloom.
As the evening wore on, heavy clouds murked the sky, blocking out the stars.
The smoke from the campfire slowly murked the forest, creating an eerie atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to darken - to make or become dark or darker

Etymology

From Middle English 'mirke', from Old Norse 'myrk' (darkness, dark).
