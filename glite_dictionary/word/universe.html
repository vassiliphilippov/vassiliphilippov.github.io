universe

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːnɪvɝs / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪvɜːs / (BrE)
(Statistics) the total set of items or individuals about which information is sought, from which samples may be drawn.
statistical population; total set for sampling

Examples

The study surveyed a universe of 10,000 households to gather demographic data.
Researchers selected a random sample from the entire universe of applicants.
Accurate results depend on correctly defining the universe from which the data are drawn.

Synset Examples

it is an estimate of the mean of the population

Related Terms

  • Synonym: population - the complete set of individuals or items being studied

Etymology

From Latin universus (all together, whole).

2. NOUN

/ ˈjuːnɪˌvɝːs / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪvɜːs / (BrE)
all of space and everything in it, including all matter and energy; everything that exists anywhere.
all existing matter and space

Examples

Scientists are trying to understand how the universe began and what its ultimate fate might be.
Many philosophers have pondered whether the universe is infinite or has an edge.
The universe contains billions of galaxies, each with millions of stars.

Synset Examples

they study the evolution of the universe
the biggest tree in existence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cosmos - the universe regarded as an orderly system
  • Degree Higher: multiverse - a hypothetical collection of multiple universes

Etymology

From Latin 'universum' (the whole, entire), from 'universus' (whole, entire).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09489410-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈjuːnɪˌvɝːs / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪˌvɜːs / (BrE)
all that is considered or assumed in a particular discussion, context, or domain.
everything relevant in a context or discussion

Examples

Within the universe of this experiment, only the listed variables are measured.
The universe of possible answers depends on how you interpret the question.
In logic, the universe often refers to the set of all objects being discussed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: domain - area or set under discussion or analysis
  • Synonym: context - the circumstances or factors relevant to a particular event, statement, or idea

Etymology

From Latin 'universum' (the whole, entire), from 'universus' (all together).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
