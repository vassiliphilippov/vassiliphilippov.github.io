Simchas Torah

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪm.xəs ˈtɔɹ.ə / (AmE), / ˌsɪm.xəs ˈtɔː.rə / (BrE)
(in Judaism) a Jewish holy day celebrated at the end of Sukkot, marking the completion of the annual cycle of Torah readings.
Jewish festival marking end of Torah readings

Examples

Jewish communities around the world celebrate Simchas Torah with joyous dancing and singing.
On Simchas Torah, the congregation completes the yearly cycle of Torah readings and immediately begins again from Genesis.
Children often carry flags and dance during the festivities of Simchas Torah.

Etymology

From Hebrew 'Simḥat Torah', meaning 'rejoicing of the Torah'.
