ti

1. NOUN

/ ti / (AmE), / ti / (BrE)
a tropical shrub (Cordyline fruticosa) with tufted elongated leaves, used for thatching, clothing, and food in southeastern Asia, Australia, and Hawaii
tropical shrub with elongated leaves, used for thatching and food

Examples

In Hawaii, the ti plant is often grown in gardens for its beautiful foliage.
The roots of the ti are sometimes baked and eaten as a sweet treat.
Leaves from the ti plant can be woven into skirts and traditional garments.

Etymology

From Hawaiian 'kī', referring to the plant.

2. NOUN

/ ti / (AmE), / tiː / (BrE)
the syllable naming the seventh note of the solfège (do-re-mi) scale in music
seventh note of the musical scale (solfège)

Examples

In the song, each child sang the note ti before moving on to 'do'.
The sequence do, re, mi, fa, so, la, ti, do is known by many music students.
Practicing scales with solfège syllables, she made sure to pronounce ti clearly.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sol - the syllable naming the fifth note of the solfège scale
  • Antonym: do - the syllable naming the first note of the solfège scale

Etymology

From Italian 'si', later altered to 'ti' for clarity in English-speaking regions.
