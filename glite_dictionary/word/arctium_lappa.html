Arctium lappa

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrk.ti.əm ˈlæp.ə / (AmE), / ˈɑːk.ti.əm ˈlæp.ə / (BrE)
A species of burdock native to Europe and Asia, with heart-shaped leaves, sometimes cultivated for culinary and medicinal purposes.
species of burdock plant

Examples

The roots of Arctium lappa are often used in traditional medicine across Asia.
In the wild, Arctium lappa can be found growing along woodland edges and hedgerows.
Many chefs incorporate Arctium lappa into their recipes for its earthy flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burdock - a large herbaceous plant with prickly burrs, genus Arctium

Etymology

From Latin: Arctium (meaning 'bear', referring to the plant’s burrs) and lappa (Latin for 'burdock').
