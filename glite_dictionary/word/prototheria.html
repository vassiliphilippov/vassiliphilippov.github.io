Prototheria

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊtəˈθɪriə / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəˈθɪəriə / (BrE)
A subclass or order of egg-laying mammals, including the monotremes such as echidnas and the platypus.
egg-laying mammals (echidnas, platypus)

Examples

The platypus and echidnas are classified in Prototheria because they lay eggs instead of giving birth to live young.
Scientists study the unique features of Prototheria to understand early mammalian evolution.
Unlike marsupials and placental mammals, Prototheria species have a cloaca for reproduction and excretion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monotreme - Egg-laying mammal characterized by a single opening for excretion and reproduction

Etymology

From Greek 'prōtos' (first) + 'thērion' (beast).

2. NOUN

/ ˌproʊtəˈθɪriə / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəˈθɪəriə / (BrE)
a subclass of primitive egg-laying mammals that includes the platypus and echidnas
subclass of egg-laying mammals

Examples

The platypus and echidnas are members of the subclass Prototheria within the animal kingdom.
Scientists study Prototheria to understand the evolution of mammals.
Prototheria are unique among mammals because they lay eggs rather than give birth to live young.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monotreme - egg-laying mammal (platypus or echidna)

Etymology

From Greek 'prōtos' (first) + 'thērion' (beast), referring to primitive mammals.
