faucal

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɔːkəl / (AmE), / ˈfɔːkəl / (BrE)
relating to the fauces, the opening at the back of the mouth leading to the pharynx
relating to the fauces (back of mouth)
medical

Examples

The doctor observed a faucal inflammation during the examination.
A faucal abscess requires prompt medical attention to avoid complications.
There was noticeable redness in the faucal region of the patient's throat.

Etymology

From Latin *fauces* (throat, jaws) + -al (adjective suffix).
