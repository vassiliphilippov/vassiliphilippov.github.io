jejunum

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪˈdʒunəm / (AmE), / dʒɪˈdʒuːnəm / (BrE)
the middle section of the small intestine, located between the duodenum and the ileum
middle section of the small intestine
medical

Examples

The surgeon removed a section of the jejunum during the operation.
Absorption of many nutrients takes place in the jejunum.
Diseases affecting the jejunum can lead to malnutrition.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ileum - the third and final part of the small intestine
  • Degree Lower: duodenum - the first part of the small intestine

Etymology

From Latin 'jejunus' (empty), referring to the organ often being found empty after death.
