po-po

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊ ˌpoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpəʊ ˌpəʊ / (BrE)
(slang) The police, especially as a collective group of law enforcement officers.
(slang) the police
slang

Examples

As soon as they saw the po-po approaching, everyone left the park.
He told me to keep an eye out for the po-po while he parked the car.
Some neighborhoods have a complicated relationship with the po-po.

Synset Examples

The po-po were called to handle the noisy party.
The po-po showed up quickly after the disturbance.
We saw the po-po checking for speeding cars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: police - the official organization that enforces laws and maintains order

Etymology

U.S. slang, possibly from reduplication of 'police' or from the phrase 'police officer'.
