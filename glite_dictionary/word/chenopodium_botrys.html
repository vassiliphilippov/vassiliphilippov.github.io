Chenopodium botrys

1. NOUN

/ tʃiːnoʊˈpoʊdiəm ˈbɑtrɪs / (AmE), / tʃiːnəˈpəʊdiəm ˈbɒtrɪs / (BrE)
A Eurasian aromatic plant of the goosefoot family with oak-shaped leaves and many yellow-green flowers, naturalized in North America.
species of aromatic goosefoot plant

Examples

The leaves of Chenopodium botrys are aromatic and resemble those of an oak.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Chenopodium botrys for its distinctive scent and foliage.
In some regions, Chenopodium botrys has become naturalized outside its native range.

Etymology

From Greek *chen* (goose) + *pous* (foot), referring to the shape of the leaves; 'botrys' from Greek meaning 'cluster' or 'bunch'.
