artificer

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrtɪˌfɪsər / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪfɪsə / (BrE)
a member of the armed forces responsible for the maintenance and repair of weapons and equipment, especially small arms and machine guns
military specialist in weapon maintenance

Examples

The army assigned an experienced artificer to service the battalion's weapons.
As an artificer, she was responsible for keeping all firearms in working condition.
During training, the artificer demonstrated how to disassemble and clean a machine gun.

Etymology

From Old French 'artificier', from Latin 'artificium' (craft, skill).

2. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈtɪfəsər / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪfɪsə / (BrE)
a person who invents or skilfully makes things, especially using creativity or ingenuity
inventor or skilled maker

Examples

Leonardo da Vinci was a remarkable artificer whose inventions were far ahead of his time.
The artificer designed a clever mechanism to solve the problem.
In ancient workshops, every artificer took pride in his unique creations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inventor - a person who invents new things
  • Synonym: craftsman - a person skilled in a particular craft

Etymology

From Old French 'artificier', from Latin 'artificium' (craft, trade), from 'artifex' (craftsman).

3. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈtɪfɪsər / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪfɪsə / (BrE)
a person who is skilled at making things, especially by hand; a craftsman or artisan
skilled craftsperson; artisan

Examples

The master artificer worked tirelessly to finish the ornate gate for the palace.
Medieval castles often employed a resident artificer skilled in both weaponry and building.
Her great-grandfather was an artificer who carved beautiful furniture by hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artisan - a worker in a skilled trade, especially making things by hand
  • Synonym: craftsman - a person skilled in a particular craft
  • Antonym: novice - a person new to or inexperienced in a field or situation

Etymology

From Old French *artificier*, from Latin *artificium* (craftsmanship), from *artifex* (craftsman).
