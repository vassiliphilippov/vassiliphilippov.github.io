desirable

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈzaɪrəbl / (AmE), / dɪˈzaɪərəbl / (BrE)
attractive or worth having, especially as a choice for a partner or spouse
worth having or choosing; attractive

Examples

In traditional societies, financial stability made a man highly desirable as a husband.
Many colleges look for students with desirable qualities beyond academic performance.
The apartment's central location made it extremely desirable to young professionals.

Synset Examples

the parents found the girl suitable for their son

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attractive - having qualities that appeal to others
  • Antonym: undesirable - not wanted or not attractive as a choice

Etymology

From Latin 'desiderabilis', from 'desiderare' (to long for, desire).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈzaɪɹəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈzaɪərəbl / (BrE)
worth having, seeking, or achieving; attractive or beneficial to possess
worth having or seeking

Examples

A flexible work schedule is a highly desirable benefit for many employees.
The car comes with several desirable safety features that are now standard in newer models.
Living near the beach is considered very desirable by people who enjoy the ocean.

Synset Examples

a desirable job
computer with many desirable features
a desirable outcome

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undesirable - not wanted or not attractive

Etymology

From Old French *desirabler*, from Latin *desiderabilis* (to be desired), from *desiderare* (to desire).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00736491-a
Synset POS: a
