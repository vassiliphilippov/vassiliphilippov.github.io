unworkable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈwɜrkəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɜːkəbəl / (BrE)
not able to be put into effect or carried out successfully
not able to be put into practice

Examples

The committee rejected the proposal as unworkable given the current budget constraints.
Many critics argue that the new guidelines are simply unworkable in a real-world classroom.
His plan sounded good in theory but turned out to be unworkable in practice.

Synset Examples

refloating the sunken ship proved impracticable because of its fragility
a suggested reform that was unfeasible in the prevailing circumstances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impracticable - not able to be carried out in practice
  • Antonym: feasible - possible and practical to do

Etymology

From un- (not) + workable (able to be worked), first used in the late 19th century.
