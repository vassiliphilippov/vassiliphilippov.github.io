tussock

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌsək / (AmE), / ˈtʌsək / (BrE)
A small, thick clump or tuft of grass, hair, or similar material that stands out from its surroundings.
clump or tuft of grass or hair

Examples

A lone deer grazed near a tussock of tall grass at the edge of the field.
She noticed a tussock of hair sticking out from under his cap.
The land was dotted with tussocks, making walking uneven and difficult.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuft - a bunch of something such as hair, grass, or threads, held or growing together

Etymology

From early 16th century; of unknown origin.
