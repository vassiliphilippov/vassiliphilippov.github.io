genuineness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛnjuɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnjʊɪnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being genuine; the state of being authentic and undisputedly credible
quality of being genuine or authentic

Examples

Many customers appreciate the genuineness of her concern for their satisfaction.
It was hard to doubt the genuineness of his smile after hearing his heartfelt words.
The genuineness of the signature was confirmed by the art expert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authenticity - the quality of being authentic or real
  • Antonym: falseness - the quality of not being genuine or authentic; the state of being fake

Etymology

From genuine + -ness, ultimately from Latin genuinus (native, natural) + English suffix -ness.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛnjuɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnjʊɪnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being authentic, real, or sincere; the state of being genuine.
the quality of being genuine

Examples

Her genuineness was evident in every word she spoke.
The genuineness of the signature was later confirmed by experts.
People appreciated the genuineness he brought to the conversation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authenticity - the quality of being authentic or genuine
  • Antonym: falseness - the quality of not being genuine; being fake or insincere

Etymology

Formed from 'genuine' + '-ness'. 'Genuine' from Latin 'genuinus' (innate, natural, authentic).
