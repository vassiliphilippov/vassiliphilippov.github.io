thyronine

1. NOUN

/ ˈθaɪrəˌniːn / (AmE), / ˈθaɪrǝniːn / (BrE)
a phenolic amino acid that forms the structural basis of thyroid hormones such as thyroxine
amino acid; basis of thyroid hormones
medical

Examples

Researchers studied how thyronine derivatives affect thyroid function in mammals.
The core structure within thyroxine is based on thyronine.
Many compounds synthesized in the lab share the thyronine skeleton.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: thyroxine - a hormone derived from thyronine, produced by the thyroid gland

Etymology

From 'thyroxine' (a thyroid hormone) and the chemical suffix '-ine' (used for amino acids).
