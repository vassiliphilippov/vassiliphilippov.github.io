pithead

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪtˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈpɪthɛd / (BrE)
the area at the entrance to a coal mine, including buildings and equipment immediately outside the mine shaft
entrance area of a coal mine

Examples

The miners gathered at the pithead to discuss the day’s work before descending underground.
A small village had developed around the pithead, supporting those who worked in the mine.
Safety inspections were carried out regularly at the pithead to protect the workers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mine entrance - entrance to any mine

Etymology

From 'pit' (mine shaft) + 'head' (top or end of something).
