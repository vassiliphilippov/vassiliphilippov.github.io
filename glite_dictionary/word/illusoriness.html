illusoriness

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈluːsəriˌnɪs / (AmE), / ɪˈljuːsəriˌnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being based on illusion; the condition of appearing real or true while actually being false or unreal.
quality of being an illusion

Examples

Philosophers often discuss the illusoriness of free will in human behavior.
The illusoriness of success can be disappointing when it does not bring real happiness.
Her novel explores the illusoriness of appearances in modern society.

Synset Examples

Modern physics has revealed the illusoriness of these beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illusion - something that deceives by producing a false or misleading impression of reality
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

From 'illusory' + '-ness', where 'illusory' comes from Latin 'illusorius' (mocking, illusory).
