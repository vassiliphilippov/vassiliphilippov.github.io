Natrix maura

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪ.trɪks ˈmɔː.rə / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.trɪks ˈmɔː.rə / (BrE)
A small, non-venomous snake found in Europe and North Africa, commonly known as the viperine snake.
species of non-venomous European snake

Examples

The Natrix maura is often mistaken for a venomous snake due to its similar coloration.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Natrix maura in southern France.
While hiking, we spotted a Natrix maura basking on a rock beside the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viperine snake - common name for Natrix maura

Etymology

From Latin *natrix* (water snake) and likely from Latin *maurus* (Moorish, referring to the region of North Africa).
