squeamishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwiːmɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈskwiːmɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
a feeling of nausea or discomfort caused by something unpleasant, such as blood, dirt, or bad smells
state of mild nausea or discomfort

Examples

Her squeamishness prevented her from watching the surgery on TV.
He apologized for his squeamishness at the sight of blood.
The movie is not for those with any squeamishness about violence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nausea - a feeling of sickness with an inclination to vomit

Etymology

From 'squeamish' + '-ness'; 'squeamish' from Middle English 'squames' (fastidious).

2. NOUN

/ ˈskwiːmɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈskwiːmɪʃnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being easily made to feel sick, faint, or disgusted, especially by unpleasant images, such as blood or injury, or by matters of taste and propriety; excessive fastidiousness or sensitivity
easily disgusted or shocked

Examples

His squeamishness prevented him from watching horror movies with his friends.
The surgeon showed no trace of squeamishness during the operation.
Some critics accused the editor of squeamishness for refusing to publish controversial artwork.

Synset Examples

the program was withdrawn because of the squeamishness of some viewers
he refused to allow squeamishness to deter him from his duty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensitivity - the quality of being sensitive to stimuli
  • Antonym: stoicism - the quality of being able to endure pain or hardship without showing feelings or complaining

Etymology

From 'squeamish' + '-ness'; 'squeamish' from Middle English 'squaimous,' ultimately from Old French 'escoymous' (fastidious, squeamish).
