Phyllocladus

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɪləˈkleɪdəs / (AmE), / ˌfɪləkˈleɪdəs / (BrE)
a genus of evergreen coniferous trees known as celery pines, native to Australasia and Southeast Asia, with flattened stems that resemble leaves.
genus of evergreen trees (celery pines)

Examples

The rare Phyllocladus thrives in the temperate rainforests of New Zealand.
Botanists are studying Phyllocladus species for their unique leaf-like stems.
Several gardens feature Phyllocladus as an ornamental conifer due to its unusual appearance.

Etymology

From Greek phyllon (leaf) + klados (branch), referring to leaf-like branches.
