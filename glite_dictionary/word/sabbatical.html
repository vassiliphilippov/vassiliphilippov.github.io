sabbatical

1. ADJ

/ səˈbætɪkəl / (AmE), / səˈbætɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a sabbatical leave, typically a period away from work for study or travel
relating to sabbatical leave

Examples

She spent her sabbatical year conducting research in Italy.
Many professors apply for sabbatical leave to focus on their studies.
The university offers sabbatical opportunities for faculty every seventh year.

Synset Examples

sabbatical research project

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sabbatical leave - a period of paid or unpaid leave from work for study or travel

Etymology

From Latin *sabbaticus*, from Greek *sabbatikós*, relating to the Sabbath.

2. ADJ

/ səˈbæt̬ɪkəl / (AmE), / səˈbætɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the Sabbath or a day of religious observance and rest in various faiths
relating to the Sabbath day

Examples

Friday is considered a sabbatical day for Muslims around the world.
Many Jewish families gather for a sabbatical meal every Saturday.
Certain communities observe strict sabbatical rules that prohibit work on designated days of worship.

Synset Examples

Friday is a sabbatical day for Muslims

Etymology

From Latin 'sabbaticus', from Greek 'sabbatikós', from 'sabbaton' (Sabbath, Saturday).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03124657-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ səˈbætɪkəl / (AmE), / səˈbætɪkəl / (BrE)
a period of paid leave, especially for academics, traditionally every seventh year, to study or travel for professional development
period of paid leave for study or travel

Examples

After seven years at the university, she was granted a sabbatical to conduct research in Europe.
Many professors use their sabbatical to write books and attend conferences abroad.
During his sabbatical, he developed a new curriculum for his department.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leave of absence - an officially approved period away from work

Etymology

From Latin 'sabbaticus', from Greek 'sabbatikos', meaning 'of the Sabbath', referring to rest every seventh year.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-15164809-n
Synset POS: n
