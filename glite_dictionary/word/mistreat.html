to mistreat

1. VERB

/ mɪsˈtrit / (AmE), / mɪsˈtriːt / (BrE)
to treat a person or animal badly or cruelly
treat badly or cruelly

Examples

The animal shelter rescues pets that people mistreat.
It is wrong to mistreat anyone, regardless of the circumstances.
Some workers claimed their employer repeatedly mistreated them.

Synset Examples

This boss abuses his workers
She is always stepping on others to get ahead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abuse - use or treat in a harmful, injurious, or offensive way
  • Synonym: to ill-treat - treat someone or something in a cruel or unkind way
  • Antonym: to pamper - treat with excessive care and attention

Etymology

From prefix 'mis-' (badly, wrongly) + 'treat'; first recorded in the late 17th century.
