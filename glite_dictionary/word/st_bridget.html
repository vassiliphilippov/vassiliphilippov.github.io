St. Bridget

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈbrɪdʒɪt / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈbrɪdʒɪt / (BrE)
an Irish abbess and one of the patron saints of Ireland, venerated for her piety and influence in early Irish Christianity (c. 453–523)
Irish patron saint and abbess

Examples

Many Irish girls are named after St. Bridget in honor of her legacy.
The feast day of St. Bridget is celebrated on February 1st in Ireland.
Legends say that St. Bridget performed many miracles during her lifetime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: St. Patrick - the primary patron saint of Ireland
  • Synonym: St. Columba - another patron saint of Ireland

Etymology

St. Bridget, also known as Brigid, from Old Irish Brigid; 'Saint' from Latin 'sanctus'.
