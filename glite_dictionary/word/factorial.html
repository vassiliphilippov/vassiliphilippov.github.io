factorial

1. ADJ

/ fækˈtɔriəl / (AmE), / fækˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
relating to or involving a factorial (the product of all positive integers up to a given number)
relating to a factorial

Examples

The factorial growth of the sequence quickly produces very large numbers.
Each row in the table corresponds to a factorial value.
The algorithm has factorial time complexity, making it inefficient for large inputs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: factorial - the product of all positive integers up to a number (the noun)

Etymology

From Latin 'factor' (maker, doer) with the suffix '-ial'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02736086-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ fækˈtɔriəl / (AmE), / fækˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
the product of all the positive integers from 1 up to and including a given number, used especially in mathematics
the product of all positive integers up to a number

Examples

To find the number of ways the books can be arranged, you must calculate the factorial of five.
In mathematics, the exclamation mark after a number indicates its factorial.
Four factorial is 24, since 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 equals 24.

Synset Examples

1, 2, 6, 24, and 120 are factorials

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permutation - an arrangement of items or values in a particular order

Etymology

From Latin 'factor' (maker, performer) with the suffix '-ial', referring to factors in multiplication.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05868395-n
Synset POS: n
