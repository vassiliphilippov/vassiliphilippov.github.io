megaspore

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛɡəˌspɔr / (AmE), / ˈmɛɡəˌspɔː / (BrE)
the larger of the two types of spores produced by some plants, which develops into a female gametophyte
large spore developing into female gametophyte

Examples

In flowering plants, the megaspore undergoes cell division to become the embryo sac.
Scientists studied the development of the megaspore within the ovule.
Each ovule typically produces only one functional megaspore that can develop further.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: microspore - the smaller of the two types of spore produced in heterosporous plants

Etymology

From Greek mega- (large) + spora (seed, sowing).
