keming

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛ.mɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɛ.mɪŋ / (BrE)
Poor letter spacing in typography, especially when it results in characters appearing visually merged or difficult to distinguish.
poor letter spacing in typography

Examples

The heading on the flyer suffered from keming, making it difficult to read.
Graphic designers often laugh about accidental keming in amateur posters.
Excessive keming can ruin the clarity of even the best-designed text.

Synset Examples

The poster had a few instances of keming that made it hard to read.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: kerning - the adjustment of spacing between characters in typography

Etymology

Humorous alteration of 'kerning' (typographic adjustment of letter spacing), imitating the appearance of poor kerning where 'r' and 'n' blend into 'm'.
