Formosa

1. PROPN

/ fɔrˈmoʊsə / (AmE), / fɔːˈməʊsə / (BrE)
a historical name for the island of Taiwan, located off the southeastern coast of China in the South China Sea
historical name for Taiwan

Examples

The Portuguese sailors called the island Formosa because of its beauty.
Many historical maps label Taiwan as Formosa.
The export label still read 'Made in Formosa' well into the 20th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Taiwan - East Asian island nation and the current name of Formosa

Etymology

From Portuguese 'formosa' meaning 'beautiful', given by Portuguese sailors in the 16th century.
