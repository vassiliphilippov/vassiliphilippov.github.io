limpkin

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪmp.kɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɪmp.kɪn / (BrE)
A large brown wading bird (Aramus guarauna) native to Florida, Cuba, and Jamaica, known for its long, slightly curved bill and distinctive wailing call.
large wading bird of Florida, Cuba, and Jamaica

Examples

We spotted a limpkin beside the marsh, searching for snails in the shallow water.
The eerie call of the limpkin echoed across the Florida wetlands at dusk.
Unlike herons, the limpkin has a distinctive, drooping bill and a louder, mournful cry.
