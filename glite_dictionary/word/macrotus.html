macrotus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæk.ɹə.təs / (AmE), / ˈmak.rə.təs / (BrE)
A genus of large-eared, greyish bats found in southern California and northwestern Mexico.
genus of large-eared bats

Examples

The macrotus is easily identified by its prominent ears and grayish fur.
Researchers discovered a new roosting site for the macrotus in the desert canyon.
Unlike most bats, the macrotus prefers dry, rocky habitats.

Etymology

From Greek 'makros' (large) and 'ous, ōtos' (ear).
