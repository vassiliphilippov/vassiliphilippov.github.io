Macrotus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmækrəˌtəs / (AmE), / ˈmæk.rə.təs / (BrE)
A genus of leafnose bats native to the Americas, characterized by large ears and a distinctive noseleaf.
genus of leafnose bats

Examples

The Macrotus genus includes several distinctive leafnose bat species found in Central America.
Researchers studied the echolocation abilities of Macrotus bats in the wild.
Macrotus is notable for its large ears and keen sense of hearing.

Etymology

From Greek makros (large) and ous, otos (ear), referring to the bat's large ears.
