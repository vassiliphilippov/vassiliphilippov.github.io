hexose

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛkˌsoʊs / (AmE), / ˈhɛksəʊs / (BrE)
a monosaccharide sugar containing six carbon atoms per molecule, such as glucose or fructose
sugar with six carbon atoms

Examples

Glucose is a well-known hexose commonly found in foods and used by the body for energy.
The scientist studied the biochemical properties of each hexose in the sample.
Fructose and galactose are both examples of a hexose sugar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monosaccharide - a simple sugar that cannot be hydrolyzed to a simpler sugar
  • Degree Lower: pentose - monosaccharide with five carbon atoms
  • Degree Higher: heptose - monosaccharide with seven carbon atoms

Etymology

From Greek hex (six) + -ose (indicating a sugar).
