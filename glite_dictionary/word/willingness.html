willingness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪlɪŋnəs / (AmE), / ˈwɪlɪŋnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being ready and eager to do something without reluctance or complaint
readiness or eagerness to do something

Examples

Her willingness to volunteer impressed everyone on the team.
He showed great willingness to adapt to new circumstances.
The success of the project depended on their willingness to cooperate.

Synset Examples

he expressed his willingness to help

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reluctance - unwillingness or hesitation to do something

Etymology

From 'willing' + '-ness', formed in English.
