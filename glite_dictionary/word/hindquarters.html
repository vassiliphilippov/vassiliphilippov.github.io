hindquarters

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪndˌkwɔrtɚz / (AmE), / ˈhaɪndˌkwɔːtəz / (BrE)
the rear part of an animal's body, including the hips, thighs, and buttocks
rear part of an animal's body

Examples

The horse suffered an injury to its hindquarters during the race.
Lions often target the hindquarters of larger prey during a hunt.
The dog wagged its tail energetically, making its hindquarters wiggle.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: forequarters - the front part of an animal's body

Etymology

From 'hind' (back) + 'quarters' (sections of the body), first used in the 17th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪndˌkwɔrtərz / (AmE), / ˈhaɪndˌkwɔːtəz / (BrE)
the fleshy part of the body that you sit on; the buttocks
the buttocks; the part you sit on

Examples

The child slipped and landed hard on his hindquarters.
After a long ride, my hindquarters were sore from sitting in the saddle.
The dog sat obediently on its hindquarters while waiting for a treat.

Synset Examples

he deserves a good kick in the butt
are you going to sit on your fanny and do nothing?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buttocks - the two fleshy parts of the body you sit on
  • Synonym: rump - the back part of the body on which you sit

Etymology

From 'hind' meaning 'rear' and 'quarters' meaning 'parts of the body'.
