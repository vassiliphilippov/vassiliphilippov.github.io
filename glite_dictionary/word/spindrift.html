spindrift

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɪnˌdrɪft / (AmE), / ˈspɪn.drɪft / (BrE)
Spray blown from the surface of the sea by the wind.
spray blown from the sea by wind

Examples

A gust of wind sent the spindrift swirling across the deck.
The lighthouse stood firm against waves and spindrift during the storm.
Through the thick spindrift, the sailors struggled to see the shore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea spray - water droplets blown from the surface of the sea

Etymology

From Scots 'spen-drift', probably related to 'spen' (to drive before the wind) and 'drift'.
