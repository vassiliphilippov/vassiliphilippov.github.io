Octopodidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːk.təˈpɑː.dɪ.diː / (AmE), / ˌɒk.təˈpɒ.dɪ.diː / (BrE)
A family of cephalopod molluscs that includes most species of octopuses.
family of octopuses
medical

Examples

The family Octopodidae comprises the majority of known octopus species found worldwide.
Recent studies have revealed new information about the evolutionary history of Octopodidae.
Marine biologists observed remarkable camouflage behaviors among members of the Octopodidae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: octopus - a marine mollusc of the order Octopoda with eight arms

Etymology

From Modern Latin, based on Greek 'oktōpous' (octopus) + '-idae' (a zoological family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑktəˈpɑdɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌɒktəˈpɒdɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of cephalopod mollusks that includes all octopuses, characterized by soft bodies and eight arms.
family of octopuses (cephalopods)

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the Octopodidae family in the deep sea.
All true octopuses belong to the family Octopodidae, which is distinguished by certain anatomical features.
Research on the Octopodidae has provided important insights into cephalopod behavior and evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: octopus - a soft-bodied, eight-armed marine animal

Etymology

From modern Latin Octopodidae, based on Greek oktōpous (octopus).
