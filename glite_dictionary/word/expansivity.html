expansivity

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˌspænˈsɪvəti / (AmE), / ɪkˌspænˈsɪvɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being expansive, either in scale, generosity, or the tendency to expand.
quality of being expansive

Examples

The expansivity of the mansion impressed all the visitors at the gala.
She was known for the expansivity of her hospitality and generosity.
Engineers must account for the expansivity of materials when designing large structures.

Synset Examples

the expansiveness of their extravagant life style was soon curtailed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expansiveness - the quality of being expansive

Etymology

From expansive + -ity, modeled after nouns formed with the suffix -ity.

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˌspænˈsɪvɪti / (AmE), / ɪkˌspænˈsɪvɪti / (BrE)
the rate at which a material's length, area, or volume changes in response to a change in temperature at constant pressure
rate of expansion with temperature

Examples

The expansivity of metals must be considered when constructing bridges to prevent structural damage from temperature changes.
Scientists measured the expansivity of the new polymer to determine its suitability for use in electronics.
Knowledge of water's expansivity explains why pipes can burst in freezing weather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thermal expansion - an increase in the size of a material as its temperature increases

Etymology

From Latin 'expansus', past participle of 'expandere', plus '-ivity' denoting a quality or condition.

3. NOUN

/ ɪkˌspænˈsɪvəti / (AmE), / ɪkˌspansˈɪvɪti / (BrE)
a quality of being open, friendly, and talkative, often making conversation freely and warmly
friendliness and openness in speech

Examples

Her natural expansivity made everyone around her feel instantly comfortable and welcomed.
Despite his usual expansivity, he remained quiet during the tense meeting.
The expansivity of the host brought life to the otherwise dull gathering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expansiveness - quality of being open and communicative
  • Antonym: reserve - quality of being restrained and not openly communicative

Etymology

From French *expansivité*, from Latin *expansivus* (expansive).
