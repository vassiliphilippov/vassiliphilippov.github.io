hydrazine

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪ.drəˌzin / (AmE), / ˈhʌɪ.drəˌziːn / (BrE)
a colorless, fuming, and highly corrosive liquid used mainly as a rocket fuel and a reducing agent in chemical processes
corrosive liquid used in rocket fuel
medical

Examples

The laboratory stored hydrazine in special containers due to its reactive nature.
Modern spacecraft often use hydrazine as a propellant for maneuvering thrusters.
Exposure to hydrazine requires caution because of its toxic and corrosive properties.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ammonia - colorless gas with pungent smell, chemical relative of hydrazine

Etymology

From German Hydrazin (hydro- + azin, referring to nitrogen groups).
