Keynesian

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪnziən / (AmE), / ˈkeɪnziən / (BrE)
A person who supports or follows the economic theories of John Maynard Keynes, which advocate government intervention in the economy to achieve full employment and stable growth.
supporter of Keynesian economic theory

Examples

As a proud Keynesian, she advocated for increased government spending during economic downturns.
The policies were heavily influenced by Keynesian economists within the administration.
His critics accused him of being a die-hard Keynesian unwilling to consider alternative theories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Keynesianism - the economic theories and policy prescriptions advocated by John Maynard Keynes
  • Antonym: monetarist - a follower of the economic theories emphasizing the role of money supply in economic stability

Etymology

From the name of British economist John Maynard Keynes (1883–1946) + -ian.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkeɪnziən / (AmE), / ˈkeɪnzɪən / (BrE)
Relating to the economic theories of John Maynard Keynes, particularly those advocating government intervention to stabilize the economy.
relating to Keynes's economic theories

Examples

The government introduced Keynesian policies during the recession to boost demand.
Many economists debate the effectiveness of Keynesian versus monetarist approaches.
Her research focuses on the development of Keynesian thought in modern macroeconomics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Keynesianism - the economic theories and policies based on Keynes's ideas
  • Antonym: monetarist - relating to the economic theories emphasizing control of a country's money supply

Etymology

From the name of British economist John Maynard Keynes (1883–1946) + -ian (adjective-forming suffix).
