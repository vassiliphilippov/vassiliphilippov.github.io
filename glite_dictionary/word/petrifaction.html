petrifaction

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛtrɪˈfækʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɛtrɪˈfækʃən / (BrE)
The process by which organic material, especially plant matter, becomes fossilized through the infiltration of mineral-rich water, turning it into stone while preserving its original structure.
process of turning organic material into stone

Examples

The petrifaction of trees has resulted in beautiful stone logs in the Petrified Forest National Park.
Scientists studied the petrifaction process to understand how ancient plants were preserved for millions of years.
The display included samples of petrifaction showing detailed patterns of the original wood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fossilization - the process of becoming a fossil

Etymology

From Latin 'petrificatio' (making into stone), from 'petra' (rock) + 'facere' (to make).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɛtrəˈfækʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɛtrɪˈfækʃən / (BrE)
a fossilized object created when organic material is infiltrated by minerals and preserved in stone; the process of turning to stone
fossilized object formed by mineralization

Examples

The museum displayed a petrifaction of an ancient tree trunk that turned to stone millions of years ago.
Geologists study petrifaction to understand the mineralization process in fossil formation.
The desert walk revealed a rare petrifaction, once a piece of wood, now solid rock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fossil - the remains or impression of a prehistoric organism preserved in petrified form or as a mold or cast in rock

Etymology

From Latin 'petrificare' (to turn into stone), from 'petra' (rock) + 'facere' (to make).
