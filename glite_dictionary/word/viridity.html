viridity

1. NOUN

/ vɪˈrɪdəti / (AmE), / vɪˈrɪdəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being green; greenness, especially the color of growing grass
greenness; green color or pigment

Examples

The lush viridity of the forest was breathtaking after the spring rains.
Painters often seek to capture the viridity of meadows in their landscapes.
In summer, the viridity of the fields signals the land's fertility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greenness - the quality of being green in color

Etymology

From Latin 'viriditas' (greenness), from 'viridis' (green).
