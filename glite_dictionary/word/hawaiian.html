Hawaiian

1. NOUN

/ həˈwaɪ.ən / (AmE), / həˈwaɪ.ɪ.ən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Hawaii, or a person of Hawaiian ancestry
a person from Hawaii

Examples

Many Hawaiians celebrate their cultural heritage through traditional music and dance.
She met several Hawaiians during her trip to the islands last summer.
The Hawaiian community organized a festival to showcase local cuisine and crafts.

Etymology

From Hawaii, the name of the islands; the suffix -an forms the demonym.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09761900-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ həˈwaɪ.ən / (AmE), / həˈwaɪ.i.ən / (BrE)
of or relating to Hawaii, its people, culture, or language
relating to Hawaii

Examples

The Hawaiian sunset is famous for its breathtaking colors.
She loves Hawaiian music and often plays it at home.
Traditional Hawaiian food features fresh fish and tropical fruits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hawaii - a state and group of islands in the Pacific Ocean, part of the United States
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02749514-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ həˈwaɪ.i.ən / (AmE), / həˈwaɪ.i.ən / (BrE)
the Polynesian language native to Hawaii
language native to Hawaii

Examples

Many people in Hawaii are working to preserve the Hawaiian language as an important part of their culture.
She studied Hawaiian at the university to better understand the region's history and traditions.
Several street names in Honolulu are written in both English and Hawaiian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Maori - the Polynesian language of the Maori people of New Zealand

Etymology

From Hawaii, the name of the islands, itself from Hawaiian Hawaiʻi.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06951270-n
Synset POS: n
