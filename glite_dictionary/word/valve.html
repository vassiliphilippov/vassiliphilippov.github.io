valve

1. NOUN

/ vælv / (AmE), / vɑːlv / (BrE)
the entire one-piece shell of a snail or certain other molluscs
the shell of a snail or similar mollusc

Examples

The valve of the snail protects its soft body from predators.
A conch has a beautifully spiraled valve that is prized by collectors.
Marine biologists examine the valve structure to classify different mollusc species.

Etymology

From Latin 'valva,' meaning 'leaf of a door' or 'folding door.'
