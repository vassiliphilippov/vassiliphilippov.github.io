delectation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːlɛkˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːlɛkˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
pleasure or delight, especially one that is very great or derived from enjoyment of something
great pleasure or delight

Examples

The chef prepared a five-course meal for our delectation.
Musical performances were arranged for the delectation of the guests.
These rare books are placed here for the delectation of serious collectors.

Synset Examples

his delight to see her was obvious to all

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delight - great pleasure or joy

Etymology

From Latin delectatio (delight, pleasure), from delectare (to delight).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛlɛkˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːlɛkˈteɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
Pleasure or delight, especially when derived from something enjoyed.
pleasure; delight; enjoyment

Examples

The chef prepared a stunning meal for our delectation.
He read out the poem for the delectation of the guests.
A box of fine chocolates was brought forth for everyone's delectation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delight - great pleasure or joy
  • Antonym: displeasure - a feeling of annoyance or dissatisfaction

Etymology

From Latin delectatio (delight), from delectare (to please or charm).
