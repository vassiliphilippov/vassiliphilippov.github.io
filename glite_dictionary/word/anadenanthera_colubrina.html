Anadenanthera colubrina

1. NOUN

/ ænəˌdɛnəˈnθɛrə koʊˌlʌˈbriːnə / (AmE), / anəˌdɛnəˈnθɛərə kɒˌlʊˈbriːnə / (BrE)
A tropical South American tree or shrub with twice-pinnate leaves, small spike-like flowers, and flat or irregularly constricted pods; sometimes classified in the genus Piptadenia.
a South American tree or shrub species

Examples

Many indigenous groups in Brazil use the seeds of Anadenanthera colubrina in traditional ceremonies.
The Anadenanthera colubrina tree produces pods that contain psychoactive compounds.
Researchers study Anadenanthera colubrina for its medicinal and ecological value.

Etymology

From Latinized form of indigenous South American plant name; genus name from Greek 'anadenai' (to climb) and species name from Latin 'colubrinus' (serpentine, likely referring to pod shape).
