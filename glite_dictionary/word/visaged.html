visaged

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɪzɪdʒd / (AmE), / ˈvɪzɪdʒd / (BrE)
(in combinations) having a face or appearance of the type specified.
having a specified type of face or look

Examples

The leader was a stern-visaged woman who rarely smiled.
A gentle-visaged old man greeted us at the gate.
The painting depicted a noble-visaged knight in shining armor.

Synset Examples

gloomy-visaged funeral directors

Etymology

Formed from 'visage' (from Old French 'visage', from Latin 'visus' meaning sight).
