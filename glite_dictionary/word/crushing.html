crushing

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrʌʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkrʌʃɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing overwhelming distress or destruction; devastating in effect, often used of emotional or physical blows.
devastating; overwhelmingly destructive

Examples

The team suffered a crushing defeat in the final minutes of the game.
Her confidence was dealt a crushing blow when she was not selected for the role.
He received the crushing news of the company's bankruptcy yesterday.

Synset Examples

a crushing blow
a crushing rejection
bone-crushing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devastating - causing severe shock, distress, or destruction
  • Antonym: mild - not severe or strong; gentle or moderate

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrʌʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkrʌʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of forcefully suppressing or defeating something or someone, especially by authority or with overwhelming power
forceful suppression or defeat

Examples

The government responded to the protests with a swift and decisive crushing of dissent.
Her novel describes the crushing of freedom during the dictatorship era.
There was a brutal crushing of the workers' strike in the early 20th century.

Synset Examples

the suppression of heresy
the quelling of the rebellion
the stifling of all dissent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppression - the act of preventing something from continuing or being expressed
  • Synonym: quelling - the act of putting an end to something, typically by force
  • Antonym: encouragement - the act of giving support, confidence, or hope

Etymology

From the verb 'crush', Middle English *crusshen*, probably of Old French origin.
