Picea engelmannii

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈsiːə ɛŋɡəlˈmæniaɪ / (AmE), / paɪˈsiːə ɛŋɡəlˈmaniːaɪ / (BrE)
a tall spruce tree native to the Rocky Mountains and British Columbia, noted for its blue-green needles and sharply conical crown; its wood is used for rough lumber and boxes.
a North American species of spruce tree

Examples

The dense forests of Colorado are home to many Picea engelmannii trees.
Lumber companies value Picea engelmannii for its strong yet lightweight wood.
Researchers study Picea engelmannii to understand its adaptation to cold mountain climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spruce - a type of evergreen tree of the genus Picea

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Picea' (spruce), species name honoring US botanist George Engelmann.
