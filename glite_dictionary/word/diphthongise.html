to diphthongise

1. VERB

/ ˈdɪf.θɑŋ.aɪz / (AmE), / ˈdɪf.θɒŋ.aɪz / (BrE)
to change (a simple vowel) into a diphthong, or to become a diphthong
change a vowel into a diphthong

Examples

Linguists observed that Old English vowels often diphthongise before certain consonants.
Some regional accents diphthongise the vowel in 'go' more distinctly than others.
The long e sound began to diphthongise during the Great Vowel Shift.

Synset Examples

This vowel diphthongized in Germanic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monophthongise - change a diphthong into a simple vowel

Etymology

From 'diphthong' (from Greek diphthongos, 'double sound') + '-ise'.
