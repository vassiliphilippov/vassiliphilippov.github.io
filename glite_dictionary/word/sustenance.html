sustenance

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌstənəns / (AmE), / ˈsʌstənəns / (BrE)
food and drink regarded as a source of strength; nourishment.
food or nourishment

Examples

The villagers depended on the river for their daily sustenance.
During the hike, we brought only enough sustenance for two days.
Many plants and animals serve as sustenance for local communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nourishment - the food necessary for growth, health, and good condition

Etymology

From Latin sustinēre (to hold up, sustain), via Old French sustenir.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌstənəns / (AmE), / ˈsʌstənəns / (BrE)
food and other necessities that support and maintain life.
food or means of support for life

Examples

When the crops failed, the villagers struggled to find enough sustenance to survive the winter.
Many animals migrate in search of sustenance during harsh seasons.
Fresh fruits and vegetables provide vital sustenance for a healthy diet.

Synset Examples

they were in want of sustenance
fishing was their main sustainment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nutriment - something that nourishes or feeds
  • Antonym: starvation - the condition of suffering severely from lack of food

Etymology

From Latin sustentare (to uphold, support).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01218681-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈsʌstənəns / (AmE), / ˈsʌstənəns / (BrE)
the money or means needed to support oneself financially; a livelihood
means of financial support

Examples

Farming was his main source of sustenance after he lost his factory job.
Many refugees struggle to find enough sustenance to survive in their new country.
Without regular work, her family depended on government aid for sustenance.

Synset Examples

each child was expected to pay for their keep
he applied to the state for support
he could no longer earn his own livelihood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: livelihood - a means of securing the necessities of life
  • Synonym: support - financial assistance for living expenses
  • Antonym: poverty - the state of having little or no money and few or no material possessions

Etymology

From Latin sustinere (to hold up, support); via Old French sustenance.
