antitussive

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tɪˈtʌs.ɪv / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈtʌs.ɪv / (BrE)
a medicine or drug that suppresses or relieves coughing
medicine to stop coughing
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an antitussive to help ease his persistent cough.
You should not take an antitussive if you have a productive cough with mucus.
This syrup contains an antitussive that works quickly to stop coughing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cough suppressant - a medicine that suppresses coughing
  • Antonym: expectorant - a medicine that helps to expel mucus from the lungs

Etymology

From Latin 'anti-' (against) and 'tussis' (cough).
