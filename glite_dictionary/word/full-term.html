full-term

1. ADJ

/ fʊl ˈtɝm / (AmE), / fʊl ˈtɜːm / (BrE)
(of a pregnancy or baby) carried or developed for the complete normal duration, typically about 37 to 42 weeks.
carried for the normal pregnancy duration

Examples

The doctor said the baby was full-term and healthy at birth.
She delivered a full-term infant after a normal pregnancy.
Most complications decrease when babies are born full-term.

Synset Examples

a healthy full-term baby

Related Terms

  • Antonym: premature - (of a baby) born before the full period of pregnancy is completed
