liturgical

1. ADJ

/ lɪˈtɝːdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / lɪˈtɜːdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to liturgy or public forms of worship, especially in a religious context.
relating to public worship or liturgy

Examples

The priest wore special liturgical vestments for the Easter service.
Gregorian chant is a form of liturgical music.
The church organizes several liturgical events during the year.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: secular - not connected with religious or spiritual matters
  • Synonym: ceremonial - relating to or used in a ceremony or ritual

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'liturgicus', from Greek 'leitourgikos' (of public service, of liturgy).
