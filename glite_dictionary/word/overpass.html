overpass

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvərˌpæs / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌpɑːs / (BrE)
A bridge that allows one road or railway to cross over another at a different level.
bridge over another road or railway

Examples

Traffic was heavy on the overpass during the morning commute.
The city built a new overpass to reduce congestion at the busy intersection.
We could see the river below as we drove across the overpass.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: underpass - a passage running under a road or railway

Etymology

From 'over' + 'pass', indicating a structure allowing passage over something.
