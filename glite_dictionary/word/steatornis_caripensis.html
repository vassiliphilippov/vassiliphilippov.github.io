Steatornis caripensis

1. NOUN

/ stiːəˈtɔrnɪs kɑːˈrɪpɛnsɪs / (AmE), / stiːəˈtɔːnɪs kəˈrɪpɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A nocturnal, fruit-eating bird native to northern South America, known for its fatty young which produce oil traditionally used as a butter substitute.
nocturnal oil-yielding bird of S. America

Examples

The Steatornis caripensis is famous for its ability to navigate caves using echolocation.
Locals have traditionally harvested oil from young Steatornis caripensis as a butter alternative.
The unique diet of Steatornis caripensis consists mainly of fatty fruits found in South American forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oilbird - common name for Steatornis caripensis

Etymology

From Greek 'stea' (fat) + 'ornis' (bird) and the species name referring to Cueva del Caripe, Venezuela.
