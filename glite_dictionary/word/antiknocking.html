antiknocking

1. ADJ

/ ˌæntiˈnɑkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈnɒkɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a substance or property) suppressing or eliminating engine knock in combustion engines.
reducing engine knock in combustion engines

Examples

The fuel's antiknocking properties make it ideal for high-performance engines.
Engineers developed an antiknocking additive to improve fuel quality.
The company advertises their new gasoline as highly antiknocking for modern vehicles.

Synset Examples

antiknock properties
antiknock rating

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiknock - reducing engine knock in combustion engines

Etymology

Formed from 'anti-' (against) and 'knocking' (engine knock).
