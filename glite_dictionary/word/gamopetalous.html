gamopetalous

1. ADJ

/ ˌɡæməˈpɛtələs / (AmE), / ˌɡaməˈpɛtələs / (BrE)
(of a corolla) having petals that are partially or wholly fused to form a tube or funnel shape.
having fused petals forming a tube or funnel
medical

Examples

Many members of the bellflower family are gamopetalous, displaying tubular corollas.
In gamopetalous flowers, the petals are joined together, unlike in polypetalous species.
The morning glory's gamopetalous structure makes its funnel-shaped bloom distinctive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sympetalous - having united petals; an equivalent term in botany
  • Antonym: polypetalous - having separate, unjoined petals

Etymology

From Greek 'gamos' (marriage, union) + 'petalon' (petal).
