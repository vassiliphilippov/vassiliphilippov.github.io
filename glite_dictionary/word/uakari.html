uakari

1. NOUN

/ wɑːˈkɑːri / (AmE), / wɑːˈkɑːri / (BrE)
a medium-sized, tree-dwelling monkey native to the Amazon basin, notable for its short tail and reddish face
Amazonian monkey with a short tail

Examples

The red-faced uakari is known for its striking appearance in the Amazon rainforest.
Researchers observed a group of uakaris feeding on fruit in the treetops.
Unlike most monkeys, a uakari has a remarkably short tail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monkey - a primate of the suborder Simian, typically with a long tail and agile movement

Etymology

From Portuguese 'uakari', from indigenous languages of the Amazon region.
