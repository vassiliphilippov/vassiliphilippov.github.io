coenzyme

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈɛnˌzaɪm / (AmE), / kəʊˈɛnzaɪm / (BrE)
a small non-protein molecule that is necessary for the activity of certain enzymes, often serving as a carrier of chemical groups or electrons
non-protein molecule assisting enzymes
medical

Examples

The vitamin B12 molecule acts as a coenzyme in many metabolic reactions.
NAD+ serves as a key coenzyme in cellular respiration.
Without the necessary coenzyme, the enzyme cannot catalyze the reaction effectively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enzyme - a protein that acts as a catalyst in biological reactions
  • Synonym: cofactor - a non-protein chemical compound that is required for a protein's biological activity

Etymology

From 'co-' (together) + 'enzyme', coined in early 20th century scientific English.
