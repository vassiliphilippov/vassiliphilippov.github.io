Erica tetralix

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛr.ɪ.kə tɛˈtrælɪks / (AmE), / ˌɪər.ɪ.kə tɛˈtrælɪks / (BrE)
A low-growing European shrub with small, rose-colored flowers, commonly found in wet heaths and bogs; also known as the cross-leaved heath.
dwarf European shrub with rose flowers

Examples

The wet heath was dotted with the pale pink blooms of Erica tetralix in midsummer.
Botanists often study Erica tetralix to understand adaptation in bog environments.
We saw several clumps of Erica tetralix along the trail during our hike.

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Erica' meaning 'heath' and 'tetralix' referring to the four-fold arrangement of leaves.
