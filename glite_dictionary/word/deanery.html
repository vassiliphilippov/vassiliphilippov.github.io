deanery

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiːnəri / (AmE), / ˈdiːnəri / (BrE)
the official residence of a dean, especially in a university or church context
the residence of a dean

Examples

The annual reception was held at the deanery, drawing faculty from across the college.
After the meeting, the bishop visited the deanery to discuss diocesan affairs.
The deanery boasts a beautiful garden that is open for community events.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'decanaria', related to 'decanus' (dean).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdiːnəri / (AmE), / ˈdiːnəri / (BrE)
the position or office held by a dean, especially in a college, university, or church
the office or position of a dean

Examples

After his promotion, he assumed the deanery of the college.
She was honored to accept the deanery after years of dedicated teaching.
His retirement marked the end of his long deanery at the cathedral.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deanship - the position of being a dean
  • Degree Lower: rectorship - the office or position of a rector

Etymology

From Medieval Latin decanatus (office of a dean), from decanus (dean).
