nifedipine

1. NOUN

/ naɪˈfɛdəˌpin / (AmE), / nɪˈfɛdɪpiːn / (BrE)
a drug used to treat high blood pressure and angina by relaxing the blood vessels (a calcium channel blocker)
drug for high blood pressure and angina
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed nifedipine to help control her blood pressure.
Patients taking nifedipine should be monitored for potential side effects.
Research shows that nifedipine may be effective in managing certain heart conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amlodipine - a similar calcium channel blocker used to treat high blood pressure

Etymology

From International Nonproprietary Name (INN), patterned on chemical structure nomenclature.
