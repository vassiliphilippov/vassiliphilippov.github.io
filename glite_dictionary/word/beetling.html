beetling

1. ADJ

/ ˈbiːtəlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbiːtlɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a part of a structure or body) jutting or overhanging, especially in a threatening or prominent way.
jutting or overhanging

Examples

The castle was perched on a cliff, with beetling rocks below.
His beetling brows gave him a fierce appearance.
Overhead, beetling crags cast deep shadows across the valley.

Synset Examples

beetle brows

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jutting - extending out over or beyond the main body
  • Antonym: receding - moving back or away, not projecting

Etymology

From the verb 'beetle' (to jut), likely imitative of projecting form.
