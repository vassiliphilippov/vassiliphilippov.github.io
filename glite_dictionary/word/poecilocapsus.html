Poecilocapsus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpiːsɪloʊˈkæpsəs / (AmE), / ˌpiːsɪləʊˈkæpsəs / (BrE)
A genus of plant bugs in the family Miridae, which are small insects known for feeding on plants.
genus of plant bugs (Miridae)

Examples

Poecilocapsus is frequently researched by entomologists studying plant-insect interactions.
The most studied species in the genus Poecilocapsus is Poecilocapsus lineatus, known as the four-lined plant bug.
Gardeners sometimes encounter Poecilocapsus species feeding on the leaves of ornamental plants.

Etymology

From Greek 'poikilos' (variegated) + 'kapsa' (box), used as a scientific genus name.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpiːsɪloʊˈkæpsəs / (AmE), / ˌpiːsɪləˈkæpsəs / (BrE)
A genus of true bugs in the family Miridae, known for certain plant-feeding species.
genus of plant bugs (Miridae)

Examples

The Poecilocapsus genus includes several species that are commonly found on cultivated plants.
Researchers observed the behavior of Poecilocapsus in summer meadows.
One species within Poecilocapsus is a well-known pest of garden flowers.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek poikilos (variegated) and kapsos (box, case) as a standard genus form.
