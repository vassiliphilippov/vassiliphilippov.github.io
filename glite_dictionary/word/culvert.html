culvert

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌl.vɚt / (AmE), / ˈkʌl.vət / (BrE)
a pipe or channel that allows water to flow under a road, railway, or similar obstruction
enclosed drain under a road or railway

Examples

The construction crew installed a new culvert to prevent flooding beneath the highway.
Heavy rains overwhelmed the old culvert, causing water to spill onto the road above.
Inspectors checked the culvert regularly to ensure it was not blocked by debris.
