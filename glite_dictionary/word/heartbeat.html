heartbeat

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːrtˌbiːt / (AmE), / ˈhɑːtˌbiːt / (BrE)
the regular movement or sound of the heart as it pumps blood, especially as felt in the arteries
regular movement of the heart

Examples

The doctor listened carefully to the baby's heartbeat during the examination.
Her heartbeat quickened as she waited for the results.
After running up the stairs, he could feel his heartbeat pounding in his chest.

Synset Examples

he could feel the beat of her heart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pulse - the regular throbbing of the arteries caused by heart contractions

Etymology

From Middle English 'herte' (heart) + 'beat', formed in English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrtˌbit / (AmE), / ˈhɑːtˌbiːt / (BrE)
the essential core or vital force of something, especially as the main source of activity or energy
vital force or core of something

Examples

Silicon Valley is often described as the heartbeat of the tech industry.
The volunteers are the heartbeat of our community organization.
London is considered the cultural heartbeat of the country.

Synset Examples

New York is the commercial heartbeat of America

Related Terms

  • Synonym: center - the central or most important part
  • Synonym: lifeblood - the indispensable factor or influence; the sustaining element

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrtˌbit / (AmE), / ˈhɑːtˌbiːt / (BrE)
a very short space of time; an instant
a very brief moment

Examples

If you need my help, I’ll be there in a heartbeat.
She accepted the offer in a heartbeat and moved to Paris.
The scenery changed in a heartbeat as the storm rolled in.

Synset Examples

if I had the chance, I'd do it in a flash

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moment - a very brief period of time
  • Synonym: instant - an extremely short period of time
