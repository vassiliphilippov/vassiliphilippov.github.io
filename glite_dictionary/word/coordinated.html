coordinated

1. ADJ

/ ˌkoʊˈɔrdɪˌneɪtɪd / (AmE), / kəʊˈɔːdɪneɪtɪd / (BrE)
able to use different parts of the body smoothly and efficiently; dexterous in physical movements.
physically dexterous and controlled

Examples

As a dancer, she is extremely coordinated and can learn complex routines quickly.
Young children are still developing the skills to become more coordinated in their movements.
His coordinated actions allowed him to juggle six balls at once.

Synset Examples

she was usually good with her hands and well coordinated

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clumsy - awkward in movement; lacking dexterity

Etymology

From Latin com- (together) + ordinatus (arranged).

2. ADJ

/ koʊˈɔr.dɪˌneɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / kəʊˈɔː.dɪ.neɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
organized so that different elements or people work well together as a unit or in harmony
organized to work together effectively

Examples

The team developed a coordinated strategy to address the city's traffic problems.
A coordinated response from all departments is essential during emergencies.
Their outfits are perfectly coordinated for the wedding ceremony.

Synset Examples

a unified utility system
a coordinated program

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organized - arranged or planned in a structured way

Etymology

From Latin 'coordinatus', past participle of 'coordinare' (to arrange in order).

3. ADJ

/ koʊˈɔrdɪˌneɪtɪd / (AmE), / kəʊˈɔːdɪneɪtɪd / (BrE)
able to use different parts of the body together smoothly and effectively; dexterous in physical movements.
able to use body parts smoothly together

Examples

The dancer was so coordinated that every movement looked effortless.
After his injury, he didn’t feel as coordinated as before when playing sports.
Children usually become more coordinated as they grow older and practice new skills.

Synset Examples

she was usually good with her hands and well coordinated

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clumsy - awkward in movement, lacking coordination

Etymology

From coordinate + -ed, meaning brought into proper relation or working order.

4. ADJ

/ ˌkoʊ.ɔːr.dəˈneɪ.t̬ɪd / (AmE), / kəʊˈɔː.dɪ.neɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of things) deliberately arranged or matched to look harmonious or complementary, especially in color, style, or design.
intentionally matched or harmonized

Examples

Her living room has coordinated furniture and curtains in shades of blue and gray.
All the dancers wore coordinated outfits to create a visually appealing performance.
He prefers a coordinated wardrobe, making sure his clothes always match well.

Synset Examples

curtains and walls were color coordinated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: matching - having the same color, pattern, or style as something else
  • Antonym: contrasting - showing differences when compared

Etymology

From coordinate + -ed, after Latin 'coordinatus', meaning 'arranged in order'.
