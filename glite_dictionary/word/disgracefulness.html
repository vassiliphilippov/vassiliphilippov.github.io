disgracefulness

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈɡreɪsfʊlnəs / (AmE), / dɪsˈɡreɪsfʊlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being deserving of public disgrace or dishonor
quality of being disgraceful

Examples

The sheer disgracefulness of his actions shocked everyone in the community.
She could not ignore the disgracefulness of their behavior during the ceremony.
Historians still debate the disgracefulness of the government's decision in that scandal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shamefulness - the quality of being deserving of shame
  • Antonym: honor - high respect or esteem

Etymology

Formed from 'disgraceful' + '-ness', where 'disgraceful' derives from 'disgrace' (from Old French 'desgrace').
