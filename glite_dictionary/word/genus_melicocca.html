genus Melicocca

1. NOUN

/ ˈd͡ʒiːnəs ˌmɛlɪˈkɑːkə / (AmE), / ˈd͡ʒiːnəs ˌmɛlɪˈkɒkə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American trees and shrubs that bear berries, belonging to the soapberry family.
genus of tropical American berry-bearing trees

Examples

The genus Melicocca includes several tropical trees admired for their edible berries.
Botanists often study the genus Melicocca to understand its diversity in South America.
Fruit from plants in the genus Melicocca is popular in local markets in the Caribbean.

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and 'Melicocca', the genus name.
