ambidextrous

1. ADJ

/ ˌæmbɪˈdɛkstrəs / (AmE), / ˌæmbɪˈdɛkstrəs / (BrE)
able to use both hands with equal skill or ease.
able to use both hands equally well

Examples

Though naturally ambidextrous, she usually writes with her right hand.
The baseball player was so ambidextrous that he could pitch with either arm.
An ambidextrous surgeon can operate with high precision in challenging situations.

Synset Examples

an ambidextrous surgeon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dexterous - skillful with the hands
  • Antonym: right-handed - using the right hand more skillfully
  • Antonym: left-handed - using the left hand more skillfully

Etymology

From Latin 'ambidexter' (right-handed on both sides), from 'ambi-' (both) and 'dexter' (right hand).
