Pesah

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪ.sɑ / (AmE), / ˈpeɪ.sɑː / (BrE)
The Jewish festival of Passover, traditionally lasting eight days from the 15th of Nissan, commemorating the exodus of the Israelites from Egypt.
Jewish festival of Passover

Examples

The family gathered together every year to celebrate Pesah with a traditional Seder meal.
She explained the significance of Pesah to her classmates during a cultural presentation.
During Pesah, observant Jews refrain from eating leavened bread.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Passover - Jewish festival commemorating the exodus from Egypt

Etymology

From Hebrew 'Pesaḥ' meaning 'pass over'.
