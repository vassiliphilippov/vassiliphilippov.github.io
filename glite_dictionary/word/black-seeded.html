black-seeded

1. ADJ

/ ˈblæk ˌsiːdɪd / (AmE), / ˈblæk ˌsiːdɪd / (BrE)
(of a plant or seed variety) having black seeds.
having black seeds

Examples

The black-seeded variety of watermelon is becoming less common in stores.
You can identify this species by its black-seeded pods, which stand out against the foliage.
We planted several black-seeded beans in the garden this spring.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: white-seeded - having white seeds
