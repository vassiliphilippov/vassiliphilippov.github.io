benniseed

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛniˌsiːd / (AmE), / ˈbɛniˌsiːd / (BrE)
the small oval seeds of the sesame plant, used as food or for oil extraction
sesame seeds

Examples

Traditional African dishes often include benniseed as a main ingredient.
The chef sprinkled benniseed over the bread before baking it.
Farmers harvested the benniseed at the end of the rainy season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sesame seed - the edible seed of the sesame plant

Etymology

Variant of 'benne', from Portuguese 'bene', from Arabic 'benne', referring to the sesame plant.
