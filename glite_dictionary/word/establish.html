to establish

1. VERB

/ ɪˈstæblɪʃ / (AmE), / ɪˈstæblɪʃ / (BrE)
to prove or show the truth or validity of something, especially through evidence, reasoning, or demonstration
prove or demonstrate the truth of something

Examples

The study helped establish the effectiveness of the new drug.
She used several experiments to establish her theory beyond doubt.
It took years to establish that the painting was indeed a genuine work by Van Gogh.

Synset Examples

The experiment demonstrated the instability of the compound
The mathematician showed the validity of the conjecture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prove - to demonstrate the truth or existence of something by evidence or argument
  • Antonym: to disprove - to show that something is false or incorrect

Etymology

From Old French establir, from Latin stabilire (make stable).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00666706-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈstæblɪʃ / (AmE), / ɪˈstæblɪʃ / (BrE)
to start or create an organization, system, or set of rules that is meant to last for a long time
to set up something, especially an organization

Examples

The committee decided to establish a new set of guidelines for the project.
Many universities were established in the nineteenth century.
The company established its headquarters in London in 1998.

Synset Examples

She set up a literacy program

Related Terms

  • Synonym: found - to establish or originate an institution or organization
  • Antonym: dissolve - to formally end or break up an organization or institution

Etymology

From Old French 'establir', from Latin 'stabilire' (to make firm), from 'stabilis' (stable).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
