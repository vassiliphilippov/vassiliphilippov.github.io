poseuse

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈzɜz / (AmE), / pəʊˈzɜːz / (BrE)
A woman who pretends to be something she is not, especially to impress others; a female poseur.
a woman who affects an attitude to impress

Examples

The critics dismissed her as a mere poseuse in the fashionable art scene of Paris.
Throughout the novel, the character is portrayed as a charming yet transparent poseuse.
Some accused her of being a poseuse because of her sudden change in style and friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poseur - A person who pretends to be what they are not, to impress others

Etymology

From French 'poseuse', feminine form of 'poseur'.
