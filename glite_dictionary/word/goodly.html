goodly

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡʊdli / (AmE), / ˈɡʊdli / (BrE)
considerable in size, amount, or extent; large or substantial
large in amount or extent

Examples

They raised a goodly sum for the charity during the fundraiser.
A goodly portion of the cake was eaten before I arrived.
The project required a goodly amount of time and effort to complete.

Synset Examples

it cost a considerable amount
a goodly amount
received a hefty bonus
a respectable sum
a tidy sum of money
a sizable fortune

Related Terms

  • Synonym: considerable - large in amount or degree
  • Synonym: substantial - of considerable importance, size, or worth
  • Antonym: small - of limited size or amount

Etymology

Old English 'gōdlīc', from 'gōd' (good) + '-līc' (like).
