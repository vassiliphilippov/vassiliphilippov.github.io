pediculosis

1. NOUN

/ pɛˌdɪkjəˈloʊsɪs / (AmE), / pɛˌdɪkjʊˈləʊsɪs / (BrE)
An infestation of lice on the body, especially by Pediculus humanus, causing severe itching.
lice infestation causing itching
medical

Examples

The doctor confirmed that the severe itching was due to pediculosis.
Proper hygiene can help prevent pediculosis outbreaks in schools.
Treatment for pediculosis typically involves using medicated shampoos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lice - small wingless parasitic insects

Etymology

From Latin pediculus (louse) + -osis (condition).
