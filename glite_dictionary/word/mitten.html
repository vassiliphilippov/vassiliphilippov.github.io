mitten

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈmɪtən / (BrE)
a type of glove with one section for the thumb and a single section that covers the other four fingers together
glove covering thumb separately, fingers together

Examples

She wore red mittens to keep her hands warm in the snow.
Children often lose a mitten while playing outside in winter.
These wool mittens were knitted by my grandmother.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glove - a piece of clothing that covers the hand with separate parts for each finger

Etymology

From Old French *mite* (glove), probably of Germanic origin.
