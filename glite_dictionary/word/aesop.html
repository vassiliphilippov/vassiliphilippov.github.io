Aesop

1. PROPN

/ ˈiːsɑp / (AmE), / ˈiːsɒp / (BrE)
an ancient Greek storyteller believed to be the author of a collection of fables, known as Aesop's Fables, around 620–560 BC
ancient Greek author of fables

Examples

Many of Aesop's fables feature animals to teach moral lessons.
The story of the tortoise and the hare was first told by Aesop.
Aesop is often credited with creating timeless stories that are still read today.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Αἴσωπος (Aísōpos), the reputed author of fables.
