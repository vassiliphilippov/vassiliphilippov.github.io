Sezession

1. NOUN

/ zəˈzɛs.i.ˌɔn / (AmE), / zɛˈzɛs.i.ˌɔn / (BrE)
an Austrian movement in art and architecture around the 1890s, parallel to Art Nouveau, emphasizing decorative and modern design
Austrian art movement like Art Nouveau

Examples

The Vienna Sezession produced some of the most innovative art of its era.
Gustav Klimt was a founding member of the Sezession movement in Austria.
The influence of the Sezession can be seen in many modern architectural designs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Art Nouveau - an ornamental style of art popular in Europe around the turn of the 20th century

Etymology

From German 'Sezession', literally 'secession', referring to artists' break from traditional institutions.
