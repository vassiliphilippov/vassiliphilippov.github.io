obduracy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑb.djəˌreɪ.si / (AmE), / ˈɒb.djʊ.rə.si / (BrE)
the quality of being stubbornly persistent and unwilling to change one's opinion or course of action
stubborn resistance to change

Examples

Despite numerous appeals, his obduracy prevented any compromise from being reached.
The company's obduracy on the issue frustrated both employees and customers.
Her obduracy in the face of criticism was both admirable and exasperating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stubbornness - the quality of being stubborn
  • Antonym: flexibility - the quality of being easily changed or adapted

Etymology

From Latin 'obduratus', past participle of 'obdurare' (to harden).
