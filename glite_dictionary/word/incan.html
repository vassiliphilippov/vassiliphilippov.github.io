Incan

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪŋkən / (AmE), / ˈɪŋkən / (BrE)
relating to the Incas, their culture, or their empire in South America
of or relating to the Incas or their empire

Examples

The museum has a remarkable collection of Incan artifacts dating back centuries.
Many Incan ruins can still be found in the mountains of Peru.
The Incan civilization was known for its advanced engineering and agricultural techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Inca - member of the South American indigenous people and their rulers

Etymology

From 'Inca', referring to the dynasty and empire of the Inca people of Peru, plus English '-an'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.kən / (AmE), / ˈɪŋ.kən / (BrE)
A member of the Quechuan people who lived in the Cuzco valley in Peru, known for founding the Inca Empire before the Spanish conquest.
member of ancient Quechuan people of Peru

Examples

Archaeologists discovered new artifacts belonging to an ancient Incan settlement near Cuzco.
The Incan were skilled engineers, known for building Machu Picchu high in the Andes.
Many traditions of the Incan people still influence Peruvian culture today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Inca - Emperor or member of the royal family of the Inca Empire

Etymology

From Spanish 'inca', from Quechua 'inka' (lord, king).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09746533-n
Synset POS: n
