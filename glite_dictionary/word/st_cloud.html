St. Cloud

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt klaʊd / (AmE), / seɪnt klaʊd / (BrE)
A town in central Minnesota, United States, located on the Mississippi River, known for its granite quarries.
town in Minnesota, U.S.

Examples

He grew up in St. Cloud and often visited the nearby Mississippi River.
St. Cloud is famous for its historic granite quarries and scenic parks.
The university in St. Cloud attracts students from across the region.

Etymology

Named after Saint Cloud, a suburb of Paris, itself named for Clodoald (Saint Cloud), a 6th-century Frankish saint.
