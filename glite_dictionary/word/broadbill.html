broadbill

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɔdˌbɪl / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːdˌbɪl / (BrE)
A type of diving duck found in North America, known for its bluish-grey bill.
North American diving duck

Examples

The broadbill is often seen in large flocks on northern lakes in early autumn.
Hunters commonly refer to the lesser scaup as a broadbill in North America.
The distinct bluish-grey bill helps to identify the male broadbill during the breeding season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scaup - another diving duck of similar size and shape

4. NOUN

/ ˈbrɔdˌbɪl / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːdbɪl / (BrE)
a small tropical bird of the Old World, known for its bright plumage and wide, short bill; a member of the family Eurylaimidae
small bright-plumaged tropical bird with a wide bill

Examples

The colorful broadbill perched quietly among the dense tropical foliage.
Many birdwatchers travel to Southeast Asia to catch a glimpse of a rare broadbill.
Scientists study the unique beak adaptations found in broadbills across different habitats.

Etymology

From 'broad' + 'bill', describing the wide shape of the bird's beak.
