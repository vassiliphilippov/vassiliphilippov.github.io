navigator

1. NOUN

/ ˈnævəˌɡeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈnævɪɡeɪtə / (BrE)
a member of an aircrew responsible for planning and directing the route or course of an aircraft during flight
aircrew member who directs aircraft's course

Examples

The navigator carefully plotted their position to ensure a safe journey across the Atlantic.
As a navigator, she was vital to the bomber crew during the mission.
The new training program helps aspiring navigators learn both traditional and digital mapping skills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pilot - the person who controls an aircraft during flight

Etymology

From Latin navigator (sailor; from navigare, to sail).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnævəˌɡeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈnævɪɡeɪtə / (BrE)
a person who is skilled in planning and directing the course of a ship, especially one who explores unfamiliar seas
person who directs and explores ships

Examples

Famous navigators like Magellan helped map the world during the Age of Exploration.
The navigator used the stars to chart a safe course across the ocean.
Many early navigators faced great dangers as they sailed into uncharted waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explorer - a person who investigates unknown regions

Etymology

From Latin 'navigator' (sailor), from 'navigare' (to sail).

3. NOUN

/ ˈnævɪˌɡeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈnavɪɡeɪtə / (BrE)
a person on a ship or aircraft who is responsible for planning and directing the course or route of travel.
officer in charge of navigation on a ship or aircraft

Examples

The navigator plotted a new course to avoid the approaching storm.
During the journey, the navigator worked closely with the captain to ensure their destination was reached safely.
He trained as a navigator before serving on the merchant ship during the war.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pilot - person who operates the flying controls of an aircraft
  • Synonym: captain - the person in overall command of a ship

Etymology

From Latin navigator (one who sails), from navigare (to sail).
