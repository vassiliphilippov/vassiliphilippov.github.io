buzzard

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌz.ɚd / (AmE), / ˈbʌz.əd / (BrE)
a large bird of prey found in Europe, belonging to the hawk family, typically with broad wings and a short tail
a large European bird of prey

Examples

A buzzard soared above the fields, searching for its next meal.
During the hike, we spotted a buzzard perched on a fence post.
The conservation area is known for its large population of buzzards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawk - a bird of prey with sharp talons and a hooked beak
  • Degree Higher: eagle - a large bird of prey with a massive hooked bill and long broad wings

Etymology

From Old French 'busart', from Latin *buteonem* (buzzard, a kind of hawk).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌz.ɚd / (AmE), / ˈbʌz.əd / (BrE)
A large scavenging bird, especially a New World vulture common in South and Central America and the southern United States.
a New World vulture; large scavenging bird

Examples

We saw a buzzard circling high above the desert looking for prey.
The buzzard is often mistaken for other birds of prey in the region.
A buzzard landed on the fence post, scanning the area for food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vulture - a large bird of prey feeding mainly on carrion

Etymology

From Old French busard, from Latin buteō (a kind of hawk).
