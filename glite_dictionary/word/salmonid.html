salmonid

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæmənɪd / (AmE), / ˈsæmənɪd / (BrE)
a fish of the family Salmonidae, which includes salmon, trout, and related soft-finned species typically found in cold and temperate waters
a fish of the salmon family

Examples

The river is home to several species of salmonid, including brown trout and Atlantic salmon.
Pollution has reduced salmonid populations in many cold-water streams.
Scientists studied the migration patterns of each salmonid in the hatchery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salmon - a large edible fish found in northern waters
  • Synonym: trout - a common freshwater fish related to salmon

Etymology

From modern Latin Salmonidae, the family name, based on Latin salmo, salmonis (salmon).
