treadle

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛdəl / (AmE), / ˈtrɛd(ə)l / (BrE)
a lever operated by the foot, typically used to drive a machine such as a sewing machine or loom
foot-operated lever

Examples

The old sewing machine was powered by a treadle that moved up and down under her foot.
He pressed the treadle to keep the loom working smoothly.
After years of use, the treadle on the antique lathe needed repair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pedal - a foot-operated lever or control

Etymology

From Old English 'tredan' (to tread).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03909502-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈtrɛdəl / (AmE), / ˈtrɛdəl / (BrE)
to operate (a machine or mechanism) by means of a treadle
operate by means of a treadle

Examples

She expertly treadled the old sewing machine to mend the dress.
The artisan treadled the loom to weave a beautiful pattern into the fabric.
He treadled steadily, keeping the machinery in smooth motion throughout the day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pedal - to operate by means of a foot pedal

Etymology

From Middle English 'tredil', from 'treden' (to tread).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01228025-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈtrɛd.l̩ / (AmE), / ˈtrɛd.l̩ / (BrE)
to press or step repeatedly on something, especially as part of a mechanical process or to shape material by foot
press or step repeatedly, especially by foot

Examples

The traditional potter treadles the clay to prepare it for shaping.
As part of the process, the worker must treadle over the mixture to remove lumps.
Each morning, she treadled the damp earth, readying it for the bricks to be molded.

Synset Examples

the brick maker treadles over clay to pick out the stones

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tread - to step or walk on or over something

Etymology

From Middle English tredel (step, rung), from Old English tredan (to tread).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01933641-v
Synset POS: v
