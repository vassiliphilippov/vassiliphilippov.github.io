cull

1. NOUN

/ kʌl / (AmE), / kʌl / (BrE)
a person or thing that is rejected or set aside as being of inferior quality
person or thing rejected as inferior

Examples

The culls from the orchard were sold at a discount for juicing.
Only the best apples were kept, while the culls were thrown away.
In breeding programs, culls are animals that do not meet the desired standards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reject - a person or thing dismissed as inadequate or unsuitable

Etymology

From Middle English 'cullen', from Old French 'cuillir' (to gather), from Latin 'colligere'.

2. VERB

/ kʌl / (AmE), / kʌl / (BrE)
to select and gather things, especially from a larger group, often for removal or use
select and gather items from a group

Examples

They set out early to cull the ripest berries from the fields.
The researchers culled specimens from several locations for their study.
Workers were instructed to cull only the best flowers for export.

Synset Examples

pick mushrooms
pick flowers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pick - to gather or collect, especially crops or flowers
  • Synonym: gather - to collect things from different places or sources

Etymology

From Old French 'culer', from Latin 'colligere' (to gather together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01384737-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kʌl / (AmE), / kʌl / (BrE)
to remove or select out individuals that are unwanted or rejected, especially to control the size or improve the quality of a group, such as animals in a population
remove unwanted individuals, especially animals

Examples

Wildlife managers cull deer to maintain ecological balance in the park.
The company decided to cull outdated products from its inventory.
Each year, farmers cull weaker animals to strengthen the herd.

Synset Examples

cull the sick members of the herd

Related Terms

  • Synonym: select - to choose from a group as being the best or most suitable
  • Synonym: to eliminate - to completely remove or get rid of something

Etymology

From Middle English *culle* (to pick out), probably from Old French *cuillir* (to gather), from Latin *colligere* (to collect).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
