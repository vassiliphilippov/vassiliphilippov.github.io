hallucinogen

1. NOUN

/ həˈluːsəˌnoʊd͡ʒən / (AmE), / həˈluːsɪnədʒən / (BrE)
a substance, especially a drug, that causes hallucinations or significantly alters perception, mood, and cognitive processes
drug causing hallucinations
medical

Examples

The researcher studied how a hallucinogen affects brain activity in volunteers.
He accidentally ingested a hallucinogen during a trip to South America.
Some plants contain natural hallucinogens used in traditional rituals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychedelic - a drug producing mind-altering effects similar to hallucinogens
  • Antonym: stimulant - a substance that raises levels of physiological or nervous activity

Etymology

From Latin 'hallucinari' (to wander in mind, dream) + '-gen' (producer).
