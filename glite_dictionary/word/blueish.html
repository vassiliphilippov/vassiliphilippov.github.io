blueish

1. ADJ

/ ˈbluːɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbluːɪʃ / (BrE)
having a tinge or shade of blue; somewhat blue in color.
somewhat blue in color

Examples

Her eyes had a blueish tint that was especially noticeable in sunlight.
The sky remained blueish even as the sun began to set.
The artist mixed white and blue to achieve a blueish shade for the painting.

Synset Examples

October's bright blue weather
a blue flame
blue haze of tobacco smoke

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluish - having a bluish color or tinge; variant spelling
  • Degree Higher: blue - pure spectral color between green and violet
  • Degree Lower: grayish - somewhat gray in color

Etymology

From 'blue' + '-ish', a suffix denoting 'somewhat' or 'like'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbluːɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbluːɪʃ / (BrE)
somewhat blue in color; having a tinge or shade of blue
somewhat blue in color

Examples

The sky this morning was a gentle, blueish shade that hinted at rain.
Her dress looked more blueish than green under the dim lights.
A blueish bruise appeared on his arm after he bumped into the table.

Synset Examples

The color is blue-ish rather than pure blue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluish - somewhat blue in color (standard spelling variant)

Etymology

Formed from 'blue' + adjective suffix '-ish' (meaning 'somewhat' or 'having a touch of').
