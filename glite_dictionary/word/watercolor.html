watercolor

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚˌkʌlɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌkʌlə / (BrE)
A type of paint in which the pigments are suspended in water, commonly used for art, especially on paper.
paint with water-soluble pigments

Examples

She painted a beautiful landscape with watercolor on textured paper.
Many artists prefer watercolors for their delicate transparency and layering effects.
The museum displayed several watercolor sketches from the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gouache - opaque water-based paint used by artists

Etymology

From 'water' + 'color', referring to coloring using water as the solvent.
