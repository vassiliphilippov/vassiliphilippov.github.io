wax

/ wæks / (AmE), / wæks / (BrE)

Examples

2. NOUN

/ wæks / (AmE), / wæks / (BrE)
a substance of animal, vegetable, or mineral origin that is solid at room temperature and insoluble in water, used for making candles, polishes, and other products
solid, water-insoluble substance (like candle wax)

Examples

The candle is made of pure wax and burns with a steady flame.
She used wax to polish her wooden dining table until it shone.
Bees produce wax to build the honeycomb inside their hive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paraffin - a white or colorless soft solid substance resembling wax, used to make candles, etc.
  • Synonym: beeswax - a natural wax produced by honey bees

Etymology

Old English *weax*, related to German *Wachs*, Dutch *was*

3. VERB

/ wæks / (AmE), / wæks / (BrE)
(of the moon) to increase in the visible illuminated portion; to move from new moon toward full moon.
(of the moon) to increase in visible size

Examples

Each night this week, the waxing moon appeared larger in the sky.
As the days passed, we could see the moon wax from a thin crescent to nearly full.
In folklore, certain rituals were performed only when the moon was waxing toward fullness.

Synset Examples

the moon is waxing

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wane - (of the moon) to decrease in the visible illuminated portion

Etymology

From Old English 'weaxan' (to grow), related to German 'wachsen'.
