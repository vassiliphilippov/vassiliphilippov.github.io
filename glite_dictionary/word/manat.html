manat

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːnɑt / (AmE), / ˈmænæt / (BrE)
the basic monetary unit of Azerbaijan, used as its official currency
Azerbaijan's official currency

Examples

The price of bread has risen to two manats in recent months.
She exchanged her dollars for manat before traveling to Baku.
Tourists are advised to carry some manat for local purchases.

Etymology

From Russian 'манат', likely via Persian 'munāt', itself from the Russian word 'монета' (coin).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.nɑːt / (AmE), / ˈmæn.æt / (BrE)
the basic monetary unit of Turkmenistan, equal to 100 tenge.
currency of Turkmenistan

Examples

The price of a loaf of bread is one manat in Ashgabat.
She exchanged dollars for manats at the bank before her trip to Turkmenistan.
The manat has fluctuated in value over the past year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruble - basic monetary unit of Russia and former Soviet states

Etymology

From Russian 'манат' (manat), ultimately from Persian 'منات' (manāt), meaning 'coin' or 'money'.
