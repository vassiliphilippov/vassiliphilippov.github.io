Pieris

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪərɪs / (AmE), / ˈpɪərɪs / (BrE)
a genus of decorative evergreen shrubs or woody vines, often grown as ornamental plants, including species such as the Japanese andromeda.
genus of evergreen ornamental shrubs

Examples

The Pieris in our garden blooms with white flowers every spring.
Gardeners often choose Pieris for its year-round foliage and attractive blossoms.
We planted a row of Pieris along the pathway for visual interest and privacy.

Etymology

From Greek *Pieris*, a name from mythology, used in scientific Latin for this plant genus.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪ.ə.rɪs / (AmE), / ˈpaɪər.ɪs / (BrE)
A genus of butterflies in the family Pieridae, including several common white butterflies, or a genus of evergreen shrubs in the family Ericaceae, known for their ornamental value.
genus of butterflies or shrubs

Examples

The cabbage white butterfly, a member of the Pieris genus, is common in gardens across Europe.
Gardeners often plant Pieris shrubs for their showy spring flowers and evergreen foliage.
Several species within Pieris are studied for their adaptability to different environments.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Pieridae - the family of butterflies including Pieris

Etymology

From Modern Latin, used in Linnaean taxonomy; ultimately from Greek Pieris, a name for one of the Muses.

/ ˈpaɪ.ə.rɪs / (AmE), / ˈpaɪərɪs / (BrE)
The type genus of the Pieridae family, comprising butterflies commonly known as whites.
genus of butterflies (Pieridae family)

Examples

The cabbage white butterfly belongs to the genus Pieris.
Some species of Pieris are agricultural pests.
Researchers observed the life cycle of several Pieris species in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Pieridae - a family of butterflies that includes the whites and sulphurs

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'pieris', a name of a mountain or region in Macedonia.

4. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪərɪs / (AmE), / ˈpɪərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen shrubs in the heath family, often cultivated for their decorative foliage and flowers.
genus of decorative evergreen shrubs

Examples

The Pieris in our garden produces delicate white flowers every spring.
Gardeners often choose Pieris for its year-round green leaves and attractive blossoms.
Japanese Pieris is a popular choice for landscaping due to its ornamental appeal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Andromeda - A closely related genus of shrubs in the heath family with similar ornamental value

Etymology

From Modern Latin, from Greek 'Pieris', one of the Muses, applied as a plant genus name in botanical Latin.
