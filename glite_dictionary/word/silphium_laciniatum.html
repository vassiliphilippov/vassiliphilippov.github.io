Silphium laciniatum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlfiəm læˌsɪniˈeɪtəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪlfiəm ləˌsɪnɪˈɑːtəm / (BrE)
A North American perennial plant with a resinous odor and yellow flowers, commonly known as compass plant.
North American perennial plant

Examples

The Silphium laciniatum thrives in prairies and is often called the compass plant due to its leaf orientation.
Gardeners admire the tall yellow blooms of Silphium laciniatum in late summer.
Native Americans reportedly used parts of Silphium laciniatum for medicinal purposes.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name Silphium and Latin 'laciniatum' (fringed or slashed).
