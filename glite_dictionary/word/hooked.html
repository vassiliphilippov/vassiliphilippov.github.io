hooked

1. ADJ

/ hʊkt / (AmE), / hʊkt / (BrE)
unable to stop taking a drug or doing something because you are addicted to it.
addicted, especially to a drug

Examples

He's been hooked on heroin since his early twenties.
After just one cigarette, she was already hooked.
Some people become hooked on painkillers after surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: addicted - physically or mentally dependent on a substance or activity

Etymology

Formed from 'hook' + '-ed', originally metaphorical for being caught as by a fishhook (late 19th century); later applied to addiction.

2. ADJ

/ hʊkt / (AmE), / hʊkt / (BrE)
having a shape that curves sharply, resembling a hook, or able to grasp and hold like a hook
having a hook-like shape

Examples

The eagle's hooked beak is perfect for tearing flesh.
She noticed the hooked end of the cane hanging by the door.
Some plants have hooked thorns to help them cling onto surfaces.

Synset Examples

hooklike thorns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curved - having a smooth, rounded shape

Etymology

From 'hook' + '-ed', meaning 'having a hook', from Middle English 'hok'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02324046-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ hʊkt / (AmE), / hʊkt / (BrE)
having a sharply curved or bent shape, especially resembling a hook or an eagle's beak.
curved like a hook or an eagle's beak

Examples

The bird had a hooked beak perfect for tearing meat.
He noticed the old man's hooked nose as soon as he entered the room.
The bridge was supported by hooked metal rods at each end.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: straight - not curved or bent; lacking curvature

Etymology

From 'hook' + -ed, indicating the state or quality of being like a hook.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02320034-s
Synset POS: s
