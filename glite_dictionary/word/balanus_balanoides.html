Balanus balanoides

1. NOUN

/ bəˈleɪnəs ˌbæləˈnɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / bəˈleɪnəs ˌbæləˈnɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
a species of barnacle commonly found attached to rocks in intertidal zones of northern seas
species of barnacle in northern seas

Examples

During low tide, the Balanus balanoides become clearly visible on the exposed rocks.
Researchers studied how Balanus balanoides survived harsh conditions in the intertidal zone.
Seagulls often feed on Balanus balanoides found along the rocky shores.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barnacle - a marine crustacean that attaches to surfaces

Etymology

From Latin; 'Balanus' (barnacle) and species epithet 'balanoides' meaning 'barnacle-like'.
