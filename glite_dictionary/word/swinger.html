swinger

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɪŋər / (AmE), / ˈswɪŋə / (BrE)
a person who swings a bat, racket, club, or similar implement in a sport
someone who swings a sports implement

Examples

The baseball swinger stepped up to the plate, ready to face the next pitch.
As a skilled swinger, she could hit both forehand and backhand with equal power.
The coach praised the young swinger for his quick reaction at the golf tee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: batter - a player who hits the ball in baseball or cricket

2. NOUN

/ ˈswɪŋər / (AmE), / ˈswɪŋə / (BrE)
a person who engages freely in sexual activities with different partners, often as part of a lifestyle that rejects traditional monogamy
person engaging in promiscuous sex

Examples

The club was known as a gathering place for swingers looking to meet like-minded people.
He admitted that he and his wife were swingers, participating in partner-swapping events.
Documentaries have explored the lives of swingers and how they navigate relationships.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monogamist - a person who practices or advocates monogamy

Etymology

From 'swing', referencing moving freely between partners; popularized in the 1960s.
