smirch

1. VERB

/ smɝtʃ / (AmE), / smɜːtʃ / (BrE)
to make something dirty or stained by smearing; to soil or tarnish
make dirty or stained by smearing

Examples

The muddy footprints smirched the once spotless carpet.
His reputation was smirched by false rumors spread online.
She accidentally smirched her dress with greasy hands while cooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smear - to spread or apply a substance messily; to stain
  • Antonym: to cleanse - to make clean or remove dirt from something

Etymology

Probably an alteration of 'smutch,' related to 'smudge.'

2. VERB

/ smɝːtʃ / (AmE), / smɜːtʃ / (BrE)
to falsely accuse or damage the reputation of someone, especially with malicious intent
damage someone's reputation

Examples

His rivals tried to smirch his name during the campaign.
Rumors were spread to smirch the integrity of the organization.
She accused the tabloids of attempting to smirch her reputation with false stories.

Synset Examples

The journalists have defamed me!
The article in the paper sullied my reputation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sully - to damage or tarnish the purity or integrity of something
  • Synonym: to defame - to damage the good reputation of someone
  • Antonym: honor - to show great respect for someone or something

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English 'smerch', related to the verb 'smirch' (to stain) of uncertain origin.

4. NOUN

/ smɝːtʃ / (AmE), / smɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a mark or stain, especially one made by dirt, that spoils the appearance of something
a dirt mark or stain

Examples

The fresh shirt was ruined by a smirch of ink on the sleeve.
She tried to wipe the smirch from her child's face before the photo was taken.
Years of neglect had left a visible smirch on the once-beautiful painting.

Synset Examples

he had a smudge on his cheek

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smudge - a blurred or smeared mark, often left by rubbing
  • Synonym: stain - a colored patch or dirty mark that is difficult to remove
  • Antonym: cleanliness - the state of being clean; absence of dirt or stains

Etymology

From Middle English smirch (to soil), probably related to Old English smearcian (to smile) or from smirk, influenced by 'smudge.'

5. VERB

/ smɝtʃ / (AmE), / smɜːtʃ / (BrE)
to stain or soil, especially in a way that damages the appearance or reputation of someone or something
to stain or tarnish

Examples

The politician's rivals tried to smirch her reputation with false rumors.
Be careful not to smirch your clothes while painting the fence.
The mistake was minor, but it threatened to smirch his professional image.

Synset Examples

She was careful not to smirch her makeup.
The scandal threatened to smirch his career.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sully - to damage or tarnish, especially a reputation
  • Synonym: taint - to contaminate or spoil, especially morally
  • Antonym: to cleanse - to make clean or pure

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *smirch*, from Old French *esmircher* (to daub, to mark).
