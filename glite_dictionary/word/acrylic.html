acrylic

1. NOUN

/ əˈkrɪlɪk / (AmE), / əˈkrɪlɪk / (BrE)
a synthetic fabric made from polyacrylonitrile, often used in clothing and textiles as a lightweight, warm alternative to wool
synthetic fabric made from polyacrylonitrile

Examples

This sweater is made from acrylic, so it's both soft and easy to wash.
Many artists prefer wool over acrylic for winter wear, but the latter is more affordable.
The blanket's acrylic fibers make it lightweight and warm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polyester - a synthetic fabric made from polyester fibers

Etymology

From acrylic acid, derived from Latin 'acer' (sharp) due to the acid’s pungent smell, plus -yl (chemical suffix).

2. NOUN

/ əˈkrɪlɪk / (AmE), / əˈkrɪlɪk / (BrE)
A synthetic resin or fiber made from a polymer of acrylonitrile, used in paints, textiles, and plastics.
synthetic resin or fiber

Examples

The sweater is made from soft acrylic that feels like wool.
Many modern paints use acrylic as a binder for their pigments.
She prefers acrylic because it is lightweight and dries quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nylon - a type of synthetic polymer fiber
  • Synonym: polyester - a category of polymers used in fibers

Etymology

From acrylic acid, itself from acrolein (Latin 'acer' meaning sharp) + '-yl' + '-ic'.

3. NOUN

/ əˈkrɪlɪk / (AmE), / əˈkrɪlɪk / (BrE)
a synthetic thermoplastic material that is transparent, lightweight, and can be molded or used in coatings and adhesives
transparent thermoplastic material

Examples

The window panes were replaced with acrylic to make them more shatter-resistant.
She used clear acrylic sheets to build the model display case.
Many artists prefer acrylic paint because it dries quickly and is easy to layer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polycarbonate - a durable, transparent thermoplastic used as a substitute for glass

Etymology

From acrylic acid, which comes from Latin *acetum* (vinegar) and Greek *akron* (tip, extremity), originally describing the acid used to make these plastics.

4. NOUN

/ əˈkrɪlɪk / (AmE), / əˈkrɪlɪk / (BrE)
A paint, fiber, or plastic made from acrylic acid or its derivatives, commonly used in art and industry.
synthetic resin paint/fiber/plastic

Examples

The artist preferred to use acrylic for its quick-drying properties.
She bought several tubes of acrylic to complete her painting.
Unlike oil paints, acrylic allows for easy clean-up with water.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: oil paint - a traditional paint made with oil, used by artists
  • Antonym: watercolor - paint made with pigment suspended in water, used by artists

Etymology

From acrylic acid, which derives from Latin 'acrum' (sharp) via French 'acrylique'.
