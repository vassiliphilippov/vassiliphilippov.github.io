brigadier

1. NOUN

/ ˌbrɪɡ.əˈdɪr / (AmE), / ˌbrɪɡ.əˈdɪə / (BrE)
a senior army officer ranking below a major general and above a colonel
senior military officer below major general

Examples

The president promoted her to the rank of brigadier after years of distinguished service.
A brigadier commands a brigade and plays a crucial role in military operations.
As a newly appointed brigadier, he was given authority over several thousand troops.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: major general - an army officer ranking above brigadier/general
  • Degree Lower: colonel - an army officer ranking below brigadier

Etymology

From French 'brigadier', originally meaning 'commander of a brigade'.
