coloratura

1. NOUN

/ ˌkʌl.ər.əˈtʊr.ə / (AmE), / ˌkɒl.ər.əˈtjʊər.ə / (BrE)
ornate vocal music or singing characterized by elaborate runs, trills, and rapid passages, especially in operatic works
elaborate vocal ornamentation in singing

Examples

The soprano amazed the audience with her brilliant coloratura during the aria.
Critics praised her flawless technique and effortless coloratura passages in the opera.
He specializes in coloratura, adding vibrancy and complexity to every performance.

Etymology

From Italian 'coloratura' (ornamentation), from 'colorare' (to colour).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkʌl.ər.əˈtʊr.ə / (AmE), / ˌkɒl.ər.əˈtjʊə.rə / (BrE)
A lyric soprano singer who specializes in performing elaborate vocal music with florid, ornamented passages.
lyric soprano specializing in ornate vocal music

Examples

The soprano's brilliance as a coloratura earned her roles in several major opera houses.
As a gifted coloratura, she captivated audiences with her agile and expressive singing.
Many coloratura sopranos master the complex trills and runs required in Mozart's operas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soprano - a woman or boy with a high singing voice

Etymology

From Italian 'coloratura' (ornamentation), from 'colorare' (to color).
