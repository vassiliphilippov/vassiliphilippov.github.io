downshift

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊnˌʃɪft / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnʃɪft / (BrE)
a change to a lower gear in a car, bicycle, or similar vehicle, typically to gain more power or control
change to a lower gear

Examples

The sudden downshift helped the car accelerate as it climbed the hill.
After the downshift, her bike was much easier to pedal up the steep road.
He heard the engine rev louder after the downshift on the highway.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: upshift - a change to a higher gear in a car, bicycle, or similar vehicle

Etymology

Formed from 'down' + 'shift', following the pattern of mechanical or automotive terminology.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊnˌʃɪft / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnʃɪft / (BrE)
a change from a financially rewarding but stressful career to a less well paid but more fulfilling one
change to a less stressful, lower-paid career

Examples

After years working as an executive, he decided to downshift for a better work-life balance.
Many professionals consider a downshift when they realize their high salaries do not bring happiness.
A dramatic downshift in her career led her to find genuine satisfaction in a new field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: career change - the process of changing from one profession or occupation to another

Etymology

From 'down-' (indicating reduction) + 'shift' (a change in position or activity); originally from motoring, later extended metaphorically.
