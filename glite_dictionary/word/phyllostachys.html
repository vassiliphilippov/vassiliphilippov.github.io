Phyllostachys

1. NOUN

/ fɪl.oʊˈstæ.kɪs / (AmE), / fɪl.ə(ʊ)ˈstæ.kɪs / (BrE)
A genus of medium to large bamboos native to East Asia, often used ornamentally or for timber.
a genus of medium to large bamboos

Examples

Many gardeners cultivate Phyllostachys in their landscapes for its striking appearance and rapid growth.
The timber produced by Phyllostachys is highly valued for its strength and versatility.
Several species within the genus Phyllostachys can tolerate cold climates, making them popular outside Asia.

Etymology

From Greek 'phyllon' (leaf) and 'stachys' (spike).
