instigator

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnstɪˌɡeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɪnstɪɡeɪtə / (BrE)
a person who deliberately initiates trouble, conflict, or an action, especially one that causes others to act
person who initiates trouble or action

Examples

He was widely regarded as the instigator of the protest that disrupted the meeting.
The principal called in the instigator of the prank to discuss the consequences.
Rumors spread that an unknown instigator was encouraging the workers to go on strike.

Synset Examples

she was the instigator of their quarrel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agitator - a person who stirs up others for a cause, often political
  • Antonym: peacemaker - a person who tries to create harmony or stop conflict

Etymology

From Latin instigātor (one who incites), from instigāre (to urge on).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪnstəˌɡeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɪnstɪɡeɪtə / (BrE)
a person who initiates or provokes an action, especially one that causes trouble or leads to controversy
person who initiates an action

Examples

The police arrested the instigator of the protest for inciting unrest.
He was identified as the main instigator behind the heated argument.
Without an instigator, the disagreement might never have escalated into a fight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provocateur - a person who provokes others, especially to cause trouble
  • Antonym: peacemaker - a person who tries to create peace and prevent conflict

Etymology

From Latin 'instigare' (to urge on, incite).

Synset POS: n
