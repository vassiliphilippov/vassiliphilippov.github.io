Oxyuranus scutellatus

1. NOUN

/ ɑksiˈjʊrənəs skuːˈtɛlətəs / (AmE), / ɒksɪˈjʊərənəs skuːˈtɛlətəs / (BrE)
A large and highly venomous snake native to northeastern Australia, also known as the coastal taipan.
venomous Australian snake species

Examples

The Oxyuranus scutellatus is responsible for several dangerous encounters in northern Australia each year.
Venom from the Oxyuranus scutellatus acts quickly, making immediate medical attention essential.
Researchers captured an Oxyuranus scutellatus to study its behavior and venom composition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taipan - common name for Oxyuranus scutellatus, a highly venomous Australian snake

Etymology

From Greek 'oxys' (sharp) + 'ouranos' (vault, sky) and Latin 'scutellatus' (having a small shield), referring to its scale patterns.
