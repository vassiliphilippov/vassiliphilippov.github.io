to diversify

1. VERB

/ daɪˈvɜr.sɪ.faɪ / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜː.sɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to make or become more varied or diverse, especially by adding different types or elements
make or become more diverse

Examples

The company decided to diversify its product line to reach more customers.
In order to reduce risk, investors often diversify their portfolios.
Many universities seek to diversify their course offerings to attract a wider range of students.

Synset Examples

diversify a course of study

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to vary - change or alter, make different

Etymology

From Latin 'diversificare', from 'diversus' (different) + '-ficare' (to make).

Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ daɪˈvɜːrsəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜːsɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
to make or become more varied, especially in order to reduce risk or increase opportunities in business, investment, or other contexts
make or become more varied; spread risk or expand

Examples

The company plans to diversify its product offerings next year.
Investors are encouraged to diversify their portfolios to minimize risk.
After years of focusing on software, she decided to diversify her business into consulting.

Synset Examples

The company diversified

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to consolidate - to combine into a single more effective or coherent whole

Etymology

From Latin 'diversificare' (to make diverse).

3. VERB

/ dəˈvɜrsɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜːsɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to develop a wider range of products, interests, habitats, or forms; to become more varied or to make something more varied
become or make more varied

Examples

The company decided to diversify its product range to reach a broader market.
Over time, the ecosystem began to diversify as new species arrived.
Farmers may diversify their crops to reduce risk and improve yields.

Synset Examples

The plants on this island diversified

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to variegate - to make diverse or varied in appearance
  • Antonym: to standardize - to make things conform to a normal standard

Etymology

From Medieval Latin diversificare, from Latin diversus (different) + facere (to make).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
