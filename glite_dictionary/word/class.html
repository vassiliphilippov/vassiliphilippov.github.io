class

1. NOUN

/ klæs / (AmE), / klɑːs / (BrE)
a group into which people or things are ranked, often by quality, ability, or merit, as in sports or competitions
a rank or league by quality

Examples

After winning the tournament, the team moved up to a higher class next season.
She competed in the lightweight class at the world championships.
The baseball player was promoted from Class C to a higher class after an impressive season.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: division - a group or level in a sports competition

Etymology

From Latin 'classis' (a division, group, or class of people).

9. NOUN

/ klæs / (AmE), / klɑːs / (BrE)
A group in biological classification ranking above order and below phylum, containing one or more related orders.
biological taxonomic group above order

Examples

Mammals belong to the class Mammalia within the animal kingdom.
Insects are grouped under the class Insecta, which contains several different orders.
The class Reptilia includes turtles, snakes, and lizards.

Related Terms
