carpal

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑr.pəl / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.pəl / (BrE)
relating to the wrist or the bones forming the wrist
of or relating to the wrist
medical

Examples

Carpal tunnel syndrome is a common condition affecting the hands and wrists.
The doctor explained that the pain was due to inflammation of the carpal bones.
X-rays revealed a fracture in one of the carpal bones after the accident.

Synset Examples

Carpal tunnel syndrome

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tarsal - relating to the ankle or bones forming the ankle

Etymology

From Medieval Latin carpus (wrist), from Greek karpos (wrist).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑr.pəl / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.pəl / (BrE)
any of the eight small bones forming the wrist in primates
bone of the wrist
medical

Examples

An X-ray revealed a fracture in the carpal bone of her left hand.
The carpal bones allow for the wrist’s flexibility and range of motion.
Athletes are sometimes at risk for injuries to the carpal region after a fall.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tarsal - any of the bones in the ankle of vertebrates

Etymology

From New Latin 'carpalis', from Greek 'karpos' (wrist).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05279313-n
Synset POS: n
