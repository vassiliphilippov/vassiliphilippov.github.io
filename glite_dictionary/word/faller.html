faller

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔlər / (AmE), / ˈfɔːlə / (BrE)
a person who falls, especially accidentally or as the result of a mishap
person who falls (accident, mishap)

Examples

The mountain rescue team struggled to reach the faller before nightfall.
In the video, you can see the faller quickly regaining his composure after the stumble.
Paramedics treated the faller at the scene for minor injuries.

Synset Examples

one of them was safe but they were unable to save the faller
a faller among thieves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: victim - a person harmed, injured, or killed as a result of an accident or crime
  • Synonym: jumper - a person who jumps (sometimes intentionally from a height)

Etymology

From the verb 'fall' + agent suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɑlɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɒlə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to cut down trees, especially as part of logging operations
person who fells trees

Examples

The experienced faller made precise cuts to ensure the tree would drop safely.
Each faller on the crew wore protective gear while working in the forest.
Many fallers attend special training programs before starting their jobs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: logger - person who cuts down trees and works in logging
  • Synonym: forester - person managing or working in forests

Etymology

From 'fell' (to cut down trees) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10295742-n
Synset POS: n
