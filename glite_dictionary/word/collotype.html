collotype

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːləˌtaɪp / (AmE), / ˈkɒləˌtaɪp / (BrE)
a photomechanical printing process in which an image is transferred from a gelatin-coated glass plate; used for producing high-quality reproductions, often in one or more colors
photomechanical printing process using a gelatin plate

Examples

The museum's catalogue includes several rare collotype prints from the early 20th century.
He specialized in creating collotype reproductions of historic photographs for art books.
Advances in digital imaging have largely replaced the labor-intensive collotype process in modern publishing.

Etymology

From Greek 'kolla' (glue) + 'type' (impression), referring to the use of glue (gelatin) in the process.
