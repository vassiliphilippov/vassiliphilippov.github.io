tailwind

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪlˌwɪnd / (AmE), / ˈteɪlˌwɪnd / (BrE)
a wind that blows in the same direction as the course of a vehicle, especially a ship or aircraft, helping it to move faster
wind blowing with the motion of a vehicle

Examples

The plane arrived earlier than expected because of a strong tailwind.
Cyclists can increase their speed significantly when a tailwind is behind them.
Sailing with a tailwind made the journey much smoother.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: headwind - a wind blowing in the opposite direction to the one in which a vehicle is traveling

Etymology

From 'tail' (the rear part) + 'wind', indicating wind from behind.
