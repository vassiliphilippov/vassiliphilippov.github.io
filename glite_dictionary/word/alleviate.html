to alleviate

1. VERB

/ əˈliː.vi.eɪt / (AmE), / əˈliː.vi.eɪt / (BrE)
to make a problem or suffering less severe or more bearable
make less severe or more bearable

Examples

The new medicine helped to alleviate her migraines.
He donated money to alleviate poverty in the community.
This policy aims to alleviate traffic congestion in the city.

Synset Examples

you could facilitate the process by sharing your knowledge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to relieve - to reduce pain or discomfort
  • Antonym: to exacerbate - to make a problem or situation worse

Etymology

From Latin 'alleviatus', past participle of 'alleviare' (to lighten, relieve), from 'ad-' (to) + 'levis' (light).

2. VERB

/ əˈli.vi.eɪt / (AmE), / əˈliː.vi.eɪt / (BrE)
to make pain, suffering, or a problem less severe or more bearable
make pain or a problem less severe

Examples

The doctor prescribed medication to alleviate the patient's chronic back pain.
Her kind words helped to alleviate my anxiety about the interview.
This new law aims to alleviate poverty in rural areas.

Synset Examples

This pill will relieve your headaches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to relieve - to reduce or remove pain or difficulty
  • Antonym: to exacerbate - to make a problem worse

Etymology

From Late Latin alleviatus, past participle of alleviare (to lighten).
