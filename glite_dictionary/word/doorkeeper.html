doorkeeper

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔrˌkiːpər / (AmE), / ˈdɔːˌkiːpə / (BrE)
A person whose job is to guard or supervise the entrance to a building or room, and control who is allowed to enter.
person who guards an entrance

Examples

The doorkeeper checked everyone's identification before allowing them to enter the building.
As a doorkeeper at the museum, he greeted hundreds of visitors every day.
The parliament's doorkeeper ensures only authorized personnel access the chamber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doorman - person whose job is to attend or guard the entrance to a building
  • Synonym: guard - person who keeps watch, especially at an entrance

Etymology

From door + keeper; 'keeper' from Old English 'cēapere' (one who keeps).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɔrˌkiːpər / (AmE), / ˈdɔːˌkiːpə / (BrE)
the lowest of the minor Holy Orders in the unreformed Western Church, formerly responsible for the doors of the church, now suppressed by the Roman Catholic Church
lowest minor ecclesiastical order (historical)

Examples

In the early Church, only the doorkeeper was permitted to open the doors during services.
The doorkeeper was a minor order, responsible for guarding the doors of the sanctuary.
The role of doorkeeper has been suppressed in the modern Roman Catholic Church.

Etymology

From door + keeper, meaning one who keeps (guards) the door; ecclesiastical use dates to early Christian Church terminology.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɔrˌkipər / (AmE), / ˈdɔːˌkiːpə / (BrE)
an official whose job is to control entrance to a courtroom, legislative chamber, or similar important room.
official controlling entrance to a chamber

Examples

The doorkeeper checked everyone's credentials before allowing them into the council chamber.
As a doorkeeper, she was responsible for maintaining order outside the courtroom.
The parliament doorkeeper announced the arrival of the members to the assembly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: usher - an official who shows people to their seats or maintains order at events

Etymology

From door + keeper, dating to Middle English, meaning one who keeps the door.
