wetter

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛtər / (AmE), / ˈwɛtə / (BrE)
a chemical agent that reduces the surface tension of a liquid in which it is dissolved, making it spread more easily
chemical that reduces surface tension

Examples

Adding a wetter helps the paint to spread evenly on the surface.
The laboratory tested several wetters to improve the efficiency of the solution.
In agriculture, a wetter is sometimes mixed with pesticides to increase their effectiveness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surfactant - a substance that reduces the surface tension of a liquid; synonym in scientific context

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɛtər / (AmE), / ˈwɛtə / (BrE)
a worker responsible for moistening materials during a manufacturing process
person who wets materials in manufacturing

Examples

The wetter made sure the fabric was dampened evenly before dyeing.
In the old match factories, the wetter played a crucial safety role.
Each wetter wore protective gear to handle the chemical solutions.

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɛtər / (AmE), / ˈwɛtə / (BrE)
a person, especially a child, who urinates involuntarily while asleep in bed; a bed-wetter.
person who urinates in bed while asleep

Examples

The doctor explained that being a wetter at his age was not unusual.
She worried about her son being labeled a wetter by his classmates.
Support groups can help both children and adults who are chronic wetters.
