Ovis montana dalli

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.vɪs mɑnˈteɪnə ˈdæli / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.vɪs mɒnˈtɑːnə ˈdæli / (BrE)
a large white wild sheep native to northwestern Canada and Alaska, also known as Dall sheep
large wild sheep of northwestern Canada and Alaska

Examples

The Ovis montana dalli is well adapted to the rocky terrain and harsh climate of Alaska.
Wildlife photographers travel from around the world to capture images of Ovis montana dalli in its natural habitat.
Conservation efforts have helped increase the population of Ovis montana dalli in northwestern Canada.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dall sheep - common name for Ovis montana dalli

Etymology

From Latin 'Ovis' (sheep), 'montana' (of the mountains); 'dalli' honors American naturalist William Healey Dall.
