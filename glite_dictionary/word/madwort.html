madwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈmædˌwɝt / (AmE), / ˈmadˌwɜːt / (BrE)
any plant of the genus Alyssum, typically with clusters of small yellow or white flowers, often grown in gardens
flowering plant of the genus Alyssum

Examples

The gardener chose madwort for its delicate clusters of yellow flowers.
In early spring, madwort brightens the flower beds with its cheerful blooms.
Some species of madwort are known for their drought tolerance and hardiness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alyssum - any plant of the genus Alyssum

Etymology

From 'mad' and 'wort' (Old English 'wyrt', meaning plant or herb); historically believed to cure madness in animals.
