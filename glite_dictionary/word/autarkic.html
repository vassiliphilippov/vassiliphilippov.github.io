autarkic

1. ADJ

/ ɔːˈtɑːrkɪk / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɑːkɪk / (BrE)
(of a country or economy) self-sufficient and not reliant on imports from other countries.
economically self-sufficient; not reliant on imports

Examples

The country's autarkic policies limited international trade and discouraged imports.
North Korea is often cited as the most autarkic nation in the modern world.
Pursuing an autarkic system can protect against external shocks but may restrict economic growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autarky - economic independence or self-sufficiency; the policy of self-sufficiency
  • Antonym: dependent - relying on others, especially for economic needs

Etymology

From Greek autarkēs (self-sufficient), via French autarcique.
