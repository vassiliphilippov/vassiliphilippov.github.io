Hazardia

1. NOUN

/ həˈzɑrdiə / (AmE), / həˈzɑːdiə / (BrE)
A small genus of shrubs and subshrubs native to the western United States, noted for their flowers that change color as they mature.
genus of western U.S. shrubs

Examples

Botanists have identified several species within the Hazardia genus along the Californian coast.
The unique flowers of Hazardia are admired for changing color as they mature.
Restoration projects often include Hazardia to support native plant communities in the region.

2. NOUN

/ həˈzɑːrdiə / (AmE), / həˈzɑːdɪə / (BrE)
A small genus of shrubs and subshrubs native to the western United States, known for flowers that change color as they mature.
genus of western US shrubs with color-changing flowers

Examples

The Hazardia genus includes several species found along the California coast.
Botanists were fascinated by the way Hazardia flowers change color as they mature.
Some Hazardia shrubs are important components of chaparral ecosystems.

Etymology

Named in honor of Barclay Hazard, an American botanist.
