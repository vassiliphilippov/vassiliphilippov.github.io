tears

1. NOUN

/ tɪrz / (AmE), / tɪəz / (BrE)
A state or process of shedding tears, usually as an expression of emotion such as sadness or joy.
state or process of crying

Examples

Her voice broke as she spoke, and within seconds she was in tears.
He tried to hide his tears during the emotional speech.
Everyone could see the tears streaming down her cheeks after the news.

Synset Examples

I hate to hear the crying of a child
she was in tears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crying - the act of crying or shedding tears as an emotional expression
  • Antonym: laughter - the action or sound of laughing, often contrasted with crying

Etymology

From Old English tēar (tear, drop from the eye), related to German Träne and Latin lacrima.
