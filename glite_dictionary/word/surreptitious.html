surreptitious

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɝː.rəpˈtɪʃ.əs / (AmE), / ˌsʌrəpˈtɪʃəs / (BrE)
kept secret, especially because it would not be approved of; done by stealth or in a concealed way
secret or stealthy

Examples

He cast a surreptitious glance at the answer sheet during the exam.
Her surreptitious meetings with the journalist were eventually discovered.
The employee made a surreptitious copy of the confidential report.

Synset Examples

clandestine intelligence operations
cloak-and-dagger activities behind enemy lines
hole-and-corner intrigue
secret missions
a secret agent
secret sales of arms
surreptitious mobilization of troops
an undercover investigation
underground resistance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clandestine - kept secret or done secretively, especially for an illicit purpose
  • Antonym: overt - done or shown openly; not secret or hidden

Etymology

From Latin 'surrepticius' (stolen, secret), from 'surripere', meaning 'to seize secretly'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌsɝː.rəpˈtɪʃ.əs / (AmE), / ˌsʌr.əpˈtɪʃ.əs / (BrE)
kept secret, especially because it would not be approved of; done in a hidden or stealthy way
kept secret; stealthy

Examples

With a surreptitious glance, she checked her phone during the meeting.
They held surreptitious meetings to discuss their plans in secret.
He gave her a surreptitious smile, hoping no one else would notice.

Synset Examples

a furtive manner
a sneak attack
stealthy footsteps
a surreptitious glance at his watch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furtive - attempting to avoid notice or attention, typically because of guilt or a belief that discovery would lead to trouble
  • Antonym: overt - done or shown openly; not secret or hidden

Etymology

From Latin 'surrepticius' (stolen, secret), from 'surripere' (to seize secretly).
