Hypericaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪ.pə.rɪˈkeɪ.siˌi / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.pɛr.ɪˈkeɪ.siː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, commonly known as the St. John's wort family, sometimes included in the family Guttiferae in certain classification systems
family of flowering plants (St. John's wort family)

Examples

The Hypericaceae family includes plants with medicinal properties, such as St. John's wort.
Some taxonomists group Hypericaceae together with the Guttiferae family.
A botanist specializing in Hypericaceae studies the unique characteristics of these flowering plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Guttiferae - former name for the plant family now called Clusiaceae; sometimes includes Hypericaceae

Etymology

From the genus Hypericum, type genus of the family, with the Latin suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ haɪˌpɛrɪˈkeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / hʌɪˌpɛrɪˈkeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants commonly known as the St. John's wort family, sometimes included within the Guttiferae in certain classifications.
plant family; St. John's wort family

Examples

The Hypericaceae family includes many medicinal plants such as St. John's wort.
Scientists debated whether Hypericaceae should be classified within the Guttiferae family.
Members of Hypericaceae are found throughout temperate and tropical regions globally.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Guttiferae - plant family sometimes including Hypericaceae

Etymology

From the genus Hypericum (St. John's wort) + -aceae, the standard suffix for plant family names in Latin.
