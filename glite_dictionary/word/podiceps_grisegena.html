Podiceps grisegena

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈdaɪsɛps ˌɡraɪsəˈdʒiːnə / (AmE), / pə(ʊ)ˈdɪsɛps ˌɡrɪsɪˈdʒiːnə / (BrE)
A large stocky species of grebe, found in circumpolar regions, notable for its dark (rufous) neck.
species of large northern grebe

Examples

The Podiceps grisegena builds its nest in shallow lakes in northern Europe and North America.
Birdwatchers spotted a Podiceps grisegena swimming gracefully across the reservoir.
During migration, flocks of Podiceps grisegena are sometimes seen along coastal waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red-necked grebe - common name for Podiceps grisegena

Etymology

From Latin: 'Podiceps' meaning 'rump-footed' (referring to the placement of the legs), and 'grisegena' meaning 'with grey cheeks'.
