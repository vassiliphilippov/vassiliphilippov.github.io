Urania

1. PROPN

/ jʊˈreɪniə / (AmE), / jʊəˈreɪnɪə / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the Muse of astronomy, one of the nine Muses who inspired the arts and sciences.
the Muse of astronomy in Greek mythology

Examples

In ancient texts, Urania is often depicted holding a globe to symbolize her connection to the stars.
The observatory was named after Urania, honoring her role as the Muse of astronomy.
Classical poets frequently invoked Urania when writing about the heavens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Muse - any of the nine goddesses who inspire the arts and sciences in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Greek Ourania (Οὐρανία), meaning 'heavenly', from ouranos ('sky, heaven').
