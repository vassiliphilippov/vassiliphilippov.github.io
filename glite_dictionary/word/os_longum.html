os longum

1. NOUN

/ ɑs ˈlɒŋɡəm / (AmE), / ɒs ˈlɒŋɡʌm / (BrE)
A long, cylindrical bone found in the limbs of vertebrate animals, typically containing marrow.
long bone of a vertebrate limb
medical

Examples

The femur is an example of an os longum in the human body.
During growth, an os longum increases in length at the epiphyseal plate.
X-rays revealed a fracture in the patient’s os longum of the arm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long bone - a bone that is longer than it is wide, such as the femur or humerus

Etymology

From Latin os (bone) + longum (long), referring to the bone's shape.
