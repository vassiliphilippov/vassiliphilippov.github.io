shnook

1. NOUN

/ ʃnʊk / (AmE), / ʃnʊk / (BrE)
A gullible or naive person, regarded with pity rather than contempt.
gullible, pitiable person
informal

Examples

My cousin is such a shnook that he always falls for obvious scams.
Don't be a shnook and believe every advertisement you see.
She pitied the poor shnook who was blamed for the office mishap.

Synset Examples

don't be such an apologetic shnook

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simpleton - a person lacking in common sense or intelligence
  • Synonym: fool - a person who acts unwisely or imprudently
  • Antonym: scoundrel - a dishonest or unscrupulous person, sometimes an antonym by social standing

Etymology

From Yiddish *shnuk* (fool, simpleton).
