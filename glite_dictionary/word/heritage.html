heritage

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛr.ə.tɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈhɛr.ɪ.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
an attribute, tradition, or possession inherited from previous generations, especially from one's ancestors or culture
something inherited from ancestors or tradition

Examples

The ancient city is famous for its rich heritage and historic buildings.
Many traditions in the festival are part of our cultural heritage.
Education is an important heritage that we pass on to future generations.

Synset Examples

my only inheritance was my mother's blessing
the world's heritage of knowledge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inheritance - something received from a predecessor, especially property or money

Etymology

From Old French *heritage*, from heriter (to inherit), from Latin *hereditare* (to inherit).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛrɪtɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈhɛrɪtɪdʒ / (BrE)
property, title, or estate that is passed down to an heir by law when the owner dies
property or title inherited by law

Examples

The large house became part of his heritage after his grandfather passed away.
The estate will form your legal heritage according to the will.
She fought in court to claim the heritage left by her ancestors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inheritance - the act of receiving property, a title, or position after the death of someone

Etymology

From Old French *eritage*, based on Latin *hereditare* (to inherit).

4. NOUN

/ ˈhɛr.ɪ.t̬ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈher.ɪ.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
the traditions, achievements, beliefs, and practices that are passed down from previous generations within a society, family, or group
cultural traditions handed down

Examples

The country's rich heritage is visible in its architecture, music, and cuisine.
She feels proud of her Native American heritage and participates in traditional ceremonies.
Efforts are being made to preserve the cultural heritage of the region for future generations.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tradition - customs or beliefs passed down within a group or society

Etymology

From Old French *heritage* (inheritance), from *heriter* (to inherit), from Latin *hereditare*.
