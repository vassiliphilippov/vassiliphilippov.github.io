sting

1. NOUN

/ stɪŋ / (AmE), / stɪŋ / (BrE)
a sudden, sharp pain or an emotionally painful feeling, like being stung
a sudden, sharp pain (physical or emotional)

Examples

He winced at the sting of the needle during the injection.
The sting of her criticism stayed with him all day.
There was a sting in his foot after he stepped on the thorn.

Synset Examples

the sting of death
he felt the stinging of nettles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prick - a sharp, sudden pain from a small puncture

Etymology

Old English sting (a pricking, stinging, or puncture), from the verb 'stingan' (to stab).
