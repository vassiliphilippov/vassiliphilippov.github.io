valued

1. ADJ

/ ˈvæl.juːd / (AmE), / ˈvæl.juːd / (BrE)
regarded as important, useful, or held in high esteem, especially for intrinsic qualities
regarded as important or esteemed

Examples

He became a valued member of the team through his dedication and skill.
Your advice is always valued and appreciated by everyone here.
She gave me her most valued possession as a sign of trust.

Synset Examples

a valued friend
precious memories

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prized - highly valued or esteemed
  • Synonym: treasured - regarded as precious or cherished
  • Antonym: worthless - having no value or worth

Etymology

From verb 'value' + -ed, meaning 'having value or worth'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈvæl.juːd / (AmE), / ˈvæl.juːd / (BrE)
(in combination) having a value of a particular kind, amount, or specified property, often used as a combining form (e.g., 'double-valued', 'highly valued').
having a specified kind or amount of value (combining form)

Examples

The new sensor provides multi-valued output for each measurement.
In mathematics, a triple-valued function can have three possible results for each input.
He invested in a rare, highly valued collection of stamps.

Synset Examples

triple-valued

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prized - highly valued or esteemed
  • Antonym: worthless - having no value
