rummy

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌmi / (AmE), / ˈrʌmi / (BrE)
A card game in which the objective is to form sets or sequences, with the winner being the first to meld all their cards.
a card game based on sets and sequences

Examples

Every Sunday evening, we play rummy with the family.
He taught his friends the rules of rummy during their camping trip.
After dinner, they relaxed with a few rounds of rummy at the kitchen table.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gin rummy - a popular variant of rummy
  • Degree Higher: canasta - a variation of rummy played with two decks

2. NOUN

/ ˈrʌmi / (AmE), / ˈrʌmi / (BrE)
a person who drinks alcohol habitually or excessively, especially one addicted to alcohol
habitual or chronic drinker
informal

Examples

His uncle was something of a rummy who spent every night at the pub.
She finally left her husband, unable to live with a rummy any longer.
The old rummy on the corner always had a story to tell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alcoholic - a person suffering from alcoholism; addicted to alcohol

Etymology

Derived from 'rum', a type of alcoholic drink, with '-y' as a suffix meaning 'characterized by'.

There's something a bit rummy about the old house at the end of the lane.
