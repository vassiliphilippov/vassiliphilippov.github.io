to transfigure

1. VERB

/ trænsˈfɪɡjɚ / (AmE), / trænsˈfɪɡjə / (BrE)
to transform the appearance or nature of someone or something, especially so as to elevate or idealize it; to change in a way that glorifies or exalts, in reference to the biblical Transfiguration.
transform and elevate gloriously

Examples

The artist's vision seemed to transfigure even the most ordinary scene into something magical.
Her kindness could transfigure a room, filling it with warmth and light.
In the story, suffering and faith transfigure his character, leading to a profound sense of peace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to transform - to undergo a marked change in form, appearance, or character
  • Synonym: to idealize - to regard or represent as perfect or better than in reality
  • Antonym: to debase - to lower in quality, value, or character

Etymology

From Latin transfigurare (to change the shape of), from trans- (across) + figura (form, shape).

2. VERB

/ trænsˈfɪɡjɚ / (AmE), / trænsˈfɪɡjə / (BrE)
to change the appearance or nature of someone or something completely, especially in a way that makes them more beautiful or spiritual
change appearance or nature completely

Examples

The artist's vision seemed to transfigure the ordinary landscape into a realm of wonder.
The new leader hoped to transfigure the organization with fresh ideas and energy.
The legend tells how the hero was transfigured by a touch of divine light.

Synset Examples

In Kafka's story, a person metamorphoses into a bug
The treatment and diet transfigured her into a beautiful young woman
Jesus was transfigured after his resurrection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to transform - make a thorough or dramatic change in form, appearance, or character
  • Synonym: to metamorphose - change completely in form or nature
  • Antonym: to disfigure - spoil the appearance of

Etymology

From Latin transfigurare, from trans- 'across' + figurare 'to form, shape'.
