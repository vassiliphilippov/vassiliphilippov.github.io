Bacillaceae

1. NOUN

/ bəˌsɪləˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / bəˌsɪlɪˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
A family of typically rod-shaped, usually Gram-positive bacteria that produce endospores.
family of endospore-forming rod-shaped bacteria
medical

Examples

The Bacillaceae are known for their ability to survive extreme conditions by forming spores.
Identifying bacteria within the Bacillaceae family requires Gram staining and microscopic examination.
Some species in the Bacillaceae family are used industrially for enzyme production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bacillus - genus of rod-shaped endospore-producing bacteria

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, from genus name Bacillus + Latin -aceae (a family suffix in taxonomy).
