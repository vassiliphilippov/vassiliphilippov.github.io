Chrysanthemum parthenium

1. NOUN

/ krɪˌsænθəˌmjum pɑːrˈθiːniəm / (AmE), / krɪˌsænθəˌmiːəm pɑːˈθiːniəm / (BrE)
a bushy, aromatic European perennial herb with white-rayed flower heads, traditionally valued for medicinal uses, also known as feverfew
herbaceous plant; also called feverfew
medical

Examples

Many gardeners plant Chrysanthemum parthenium to repel insects naturally within their flower beds.
The herbal remedy made from Chrysanthemum parthenium has been used to treat migraines for centuries.
I read that Chrysanthemum parthenium is sometimes added to teas for its calming effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feverfew - common name for Chrysanthemum parthenium

Etymology

From Greek khrusánthemon (golden flower) and parthénion, the plant’s ancient name.
