androglossia

1. NOUN

/ ˌændroʊˈɡlɑsiə / (AmE), / ˌandrəʊˈɡlɒsiə / (BrE)
A woman's voice that exhibits male vocal qualities or characteristics.
female voice with male qualities

Examples

Her androglossia made her singing voice stand out among the other soprano performers.
The speech therapist noted a subtle androglossia in her client's tone.
Some actors train deliberately to develop androglossia for certain roles.

Etymology

From Greek 'andr-' (man) and 'glōssa' (tongue, language), referring to male-like vocal features in women.
