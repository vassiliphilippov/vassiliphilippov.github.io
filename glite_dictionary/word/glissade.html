glissade

1. NOUN

/ ɡlɪˈsɑd / (AmE), / ɡlɪˈsɑːd / (BrE)
A gliding or sliding step used in ballet, where one foot slides along the floor before both feet come together in the air or in landing.
gliding step in ballet

Examples

The dancer performed a perfect glissade across the stage, earning applause from the audience.
During rehearsals, students practiced their glissade to improve their transitions between movements.
She incorporated a quick glissade into her routine to seamlessly link the two sequences.

Etymology

From French 'glissade', from 'glisser' meaning 'to slip, to slide'.

2. VERB

/ ɡlɪˈsɑd / (AmE), / ɡlɪˈsɑːd / (BrE)
To perform a glissade, a gliding ballet step in which one foot slides along the floor to the other.
perform a gliding ballet step

Examples

The dancer began to glissade gracefully across the polished stage.
Before the leap, you should glissade to build momentum.
He learned to glissade elegantly by practicing every day in ballet class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glide - to move smoothly and continuously; general motion related to glissade

Etymology

From French 'glissade' (a glide, sliding), from 'glisser' (to glide).
