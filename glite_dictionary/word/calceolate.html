calceolate

1. ADJ

/ kælˈsiː.əˌleɪt / (AmE), / kælˈsiː.əleɪt / (BrE)
(of a flower or blossom) shaped like a slipper; having a pouch-like or slipper-like form.
slipper-shaped (botanical)

Examples

The orchid's calceolate lip is its most distinctive feature.
This species is known for its calceolate flowers, which resemble tiny slippers.
Botanists admire the unique calceolate structure of certain orchid blossoms.

Synset Examples

the calceiform lip of certain orchids

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calceiform - having the shape of a slipper or shoe (especially in flowers)

Etymology

From Latin 'calceolus' (a little shoe), diminutive of 'calceus' (shoe).
