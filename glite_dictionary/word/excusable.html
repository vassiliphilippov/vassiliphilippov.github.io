excusable

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈskjuːzəbəl / (AmE), / ɪkˈskjuːzəbəl / (BrE)
easily forgiven or justified; able to be excused because it is not serious or is understandable
able to be forgiven or justified

Examples

The student’s late arrival was excusable due to the severe weather conditions.
It was an honest mistake and therefore excusable in this situation.
Some errors are simply not excusable, no matter the circumstances.

Synset Examples

a venial error

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inexcusable - not able to be excused or justified
  • Synonym: forgivable - able to be forgiven; pardonable
  • Synonym: venial - (of a fault or offense) slight and pardonable

Etymology

From Latin excusabilis (that may be excused), from excusare (to excuse).

2. ADJ

/ ɪkˈskjuːzəbəl / (AmE), / ɪkˈskjuːzəbəl / (BrE)
Able to be forgiven or justified; deserving to be overlooked or pardoned.
able to be forgiven or justified

Examples

His late arrival was excusable given the terrible weather conditions.
A minor mistake is often excusable in a complex process like this.
Her anger might be excusable considering the circumstances she was facing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forgivable - able to be forgiven; deserving pardon
  • Antonym: inexcusable - impossible to justify or forgive

Etymology

From Latin excusabilis, from excusare (to excuse).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01726274-a
Synset POS: a
