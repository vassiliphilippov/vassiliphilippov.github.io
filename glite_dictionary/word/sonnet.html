sonnet

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑː.nət / (AmE), / ˈsɒn.ɪt / (BrE)
A poem of 14 lines with a fixed rhyme scheme, typically written in iambic pentameter.
14-line poem with fixed rhyme scheme

Examples

Shakespeare is renowned for writing many beautiful sonnets during his lifetime.
The poet spent weeks refining his latest sonnet to perfect its rhythm and rhyme.
In class, we analyzed a classic Petrarchan sonnet and discussed its structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ode - lyric poem with elaborate structure and style
  • Degree Lower: haiku - traditional Japanese three-line poem

Etymology

From Italian 'sonetto', from Latin 'sonus' (sound).
