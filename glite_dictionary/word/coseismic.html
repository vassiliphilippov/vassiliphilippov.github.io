coseismic

1. ADJ

/ koʊˈsaɪzmɪk / (AmE), / kəʊˈsaɪzmɪk / (BrE)
Occurring or being associated with the simultaneous arrival of earthquake waves at a location.
relating to simultaneous arrival of earthquake waves
medical

Examples

The researchers observed a coseismic displacement between two distant locations during the earthquake.
A coseismic rupture can change the landscape immediately as the seismic waves pass through.
Some buildings experienced coseismic failure when the main shock hit the city.

Etymology

From prefix 'co-' (together) and 'seismic' (relating to earthquakes).
