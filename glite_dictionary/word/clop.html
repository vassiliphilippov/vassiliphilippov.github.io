clop

1. NOUN

/ klɑp / (AmE), / klɒp / (BrE)
a short, loud sound made by a horse's hoof hitting a hard surface
sound of horse's hoof on hard ground

Examples

The clop of the horse's hooves echoed down the cobblestone street.
From the stable came the steady clop of horses early in the morning.
We listened to the gentle clop as the carriage passed by.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clip-clop - repetitive sound of horse's hooves
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07395534-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ klɑp / (AmE), / klɒp / (BrE)
(of a horse or similar animal) to make a sound like hooves striking the ground; to move with such a sound.
move or sound like striking hooves

Examples

The horse clopped down the cobblestone street, echoing through the morning air.
She could hear boots clopping on the wooden floor upstairs.
Donkeys clop past our window every morning as the village wakes up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clip-clop - move with a sound of horse's hooves (reduplicative form)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02189411-v
Synset POS: v
