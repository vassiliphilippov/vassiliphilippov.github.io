critic

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˈkrɪtɪk / (BrE)
a person who habitually finds fault or makes harsh and often unfair judgments
person who frequently finds fault

Examples

She's her own worst critic and rarely appreciates her achievements.
Instead of offering advice, he became a harsh critic of every decision.
The constant critic in the meeting made progress difficult for everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faultfinder - a person who finds excessive faults
  • Antonym: praiser - a person who expresses approval or admiration

Etymology

From Greek kritikós (able to discern), via Latin criticus (a judge, critic).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˈkrɪtɪk / (BrE)
a person who expresses reasoned opinions or judgments about something, especially the arts, literature, or public affairs
a person who judges or reviews something

Examples

As a respected critic, her opinions were highly valued by the theater community.
The film received poor reviews from almost every critic in the country.
He became a fierce critic of government policies after leaving office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reviewer - a person who writes critical articles or reports, especially for publication

Etymology

From Greek kritikos (able to discern or judge), from kritēs (judge).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪt̬ɪk / (AmE), / ˈkrɪtɪk / (BrE)
a person whose job is to analyze, interpret, and judge works of art, literature, music, or film especially in published reviews
person analyzing and judging art, literature, etc.

Examples

The film critic gave the movie a glowing review in the newspaper.
As a respected art critic, she frequently writes essays for major magazines.
Some critics argue that the novel fails to capture the complexity of modern life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reviewer - a person who reviews works, especially for publication
  • Antonym: praiser - a person who expresses approval or admiration

Etymology

From Greek kritikós (able to discern, judge), via Latin criticus.

Source: WordNet
