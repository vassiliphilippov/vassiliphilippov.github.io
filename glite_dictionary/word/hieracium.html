Hieracium

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈræsiəm / (AmE), / hɪəˈræsɪəm / (BrE)
a large genus of perennial hairy herbs, commonly known as hawkweeds, found in Europe, Asia, Africa, and North America
genus of perennial herbs (hawkweeds)

Examples

The meadow was dotted with bright yellow Hieracium flowers throughout the summer.
Researchers collected several specimens of Hieracium for the herbarium study.
Some gardeners plant Hieracium as ground cover despite its invasive reputation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawkweed - common name for plants in the Hieracium genus

Etymology

From Latin *hieracium*, from Greek *hierax* (hawk), referring to the belief that hawks ate these plants to improve their eyesight.
