phonological

1. ADJ

/ ˌfoʊ.nəˈlɑ.dʒɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌfəʊ.nəˈlɒ.dʒɪ.kəl / (BrE)
relating to the study of the sound system of a language or languages.
relating to phonology or sounds of language

Examples

The professor explained the phonological rules that govern sound changes in English.
Children with phonological disorders may struggle to pronounce certain sounds correctly.
Linguists conduct phonological analysis to understand how sounds function in a particular language.

Synset Examples

the phonological component of language

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phonology - the study of the sound system of languages

Etymology

From phonology + -ical. Ultimately derived from Greek phōnē (sound, voice).
