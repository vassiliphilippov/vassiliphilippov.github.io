Salishan

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈseɪlɪʃən / (BrE)
a family of indigenous languages spoken in the northwestern United States and western Canada
indigenous language family of Pacific Northwest

Examples

Many linguists have studied the structure of Salishan languages to understand their unique features.
Few fluent speakers of Salishan languages remain, leading to concerns about language preservation.
The Salishan language family includes several distinct dialects across different regions.

Etymology

From Salish, the autonym of the Interior Salish people, with the suffix -an indicating relation.
