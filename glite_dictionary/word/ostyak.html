Ostyak

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːstiˌæk / (AmE), / ˈɒstiːˌak / (BrE)
a member of a nomadic Ugrian people traditionally living in northwestern Siberia, east of the Ural Mountains
member of a Siberian Ugrian people

Examples

The Ostyak have a long history of fishing and reindeer herding in Siberia.
Anthropologists have studied Ostyak traditions and beliefs for over a century.
Many Ostyak people now prefer to be known as Khanty, reflecting their own name for themselves.

Etymology

From Russian Остяк (Ostyak), referring to various indigenous peoples of western Siberia.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːstiˌæk / (AmE), / ˈɒstɪˌak / (BrE)
A Ugric language related to Hungarian, spoken by the Khanty people of western Siberia.
Ugric language of western Siberia

Examples

The Ostyak language has several dialects spoken in western Siberia.
Linguists have studied Ostyak to understand its relationship with Hungarian.
Few young people now speak Ostyak, leading to concerns about the language's survival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Khanty - Modern name for the people and the language formerly called Ostyak

Etymology

From Russian Остяк (Ostjak), used historically for the Khanty people.
