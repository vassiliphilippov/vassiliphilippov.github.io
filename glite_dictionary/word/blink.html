blink

1. NOUN

/ blɪŋk / (AmE), / blɪŋk / (BrE)
a quick closing and opening of the eyelids, usually as a reflex
rapid closing and opening of the eyelids

Examples

She gave a startled blink when the bright light flashed in her eyes.
After staring at the computer for hours, he felt his blink rate slow down.
A sudden blink can protect your eyes from small debris or dust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wink - a closing and opening of one eye, often as a signal
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00118779-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ blɪŋk / (AmE), / blɪŋk / (BrE)
to quickly close and open the eyes, often involuntarily or briefly
quickly close and open the eyes

Examples

She tried not to blink during the staring contest.
The nurse asked him to blink several times to test his reflexes.
He didn't blink once throughout the shocking announcement.

Synset Examples

The TV announcer never seems to blink

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wink - to close and open one eye quickly

Etymology

From Middle English 'blenken' (to shine, dazzle, wink), of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00007739-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ blɪŋk / (AmE), / blɪŋk / (BrE)
to try to prevent or suppress (tears or emotion) by blinking.
suppress tears or emotion by blinking

Examples

She managed to blink away her tears before anyone noticed.
He tried to blink back his emotions during the speech.
She quickly blinked away any sign of sadness and smiled.

Synset Examples

blink away tears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hold back - to restrain or suppress (especially emotion or tears)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00008435-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ blɪŋk / (AmE), / blɪŋk / (BrE)
(of a light) to shine with a quick, intermittent gleam; to flash on and off repeatedly.
shine or flash intermittently (light)

Examples

The distant lighthouse began to blink as night fell.
The warning light will blink rapidly if there is a problem.
Red and blue lights blinked in the darkness as the police car approached.

Synset Examples

The lights were flashing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flash - shine with a sudden bright light
  • Synonym: flicker - burn or shine with an unsteady or wavering light

Etymology

From Middle English blenken, probably of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
