endogenic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛndəˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛndəˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
Originating or produced within an organism, system, or process; having an internal origin.
originating internally

Examples

Most volcanic activity is considered endogenic because it originates beneath the Earth's surface.
The researchers studied endogenic factors influencing cell growth.
In geology, endogenic processes such as earthquakes and mountain building occur within the planet.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exogenic - originating externally

Etymology

From Greek 'endon' (within) + 'genēs' (born).

WordNet ID: oewn-00876421-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌɛn.doʊˈdʒɛn.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛn.dəʊˈdʒɛn.ɪk / (BrE)
(of rocks or geological processes) formed or occurring beneath the Earth's surface.
formed beneath Earth's surface (geology)

Examples

Granite is considered an endogenic rock because it forms from magma beneath the Earth's surface.
Many endogenic processes contribute to mountain building and geological uplift.
Unlike sedimentary rocks, endogenic rocks undergo formation deep within the Earth.

Synset Examples

endogenic rocks are not clastic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exogenic - relating to rocks or processes formed on or near the Earth's surface

Etymology

From Greek endo- (within) + -genic (produced by).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01334627-s
Synset POS: s
