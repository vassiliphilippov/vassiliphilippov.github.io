to enlist

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈlɪst / (AmE), / ɪnˈlɪst / (BrE)
to secure the help, support, or services of a person or group for a particular purpose
secure help or services

Examples

The company decided to enlist a consultant to improve their marketing strategy.
We should enlist the support of local volunteers for the event.
The school hopes to enlist parents in efforts to raise funds for new equipment.

Synset Examples

engage aid, help, services, or support

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recruit - to persuade someone to join or help, especially in a job or organization

Etymology

From French 'enlister', from 'en-' (in) + 'liste' (list, roll).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02244933-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈlɪst / (AmE), / ɪnˈlɪst / (BrE)
to persuade or engage someone to join the armed forces or another organization.
engage someone to join the army

Examples

The army hopes to enlist thousands of new recruits this year.
They tried to enlist her help in organizing the event.
After graduation, he decided to enlist in the Marines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recruit - to enlist someone for military service
  • Antonym: discharge - to officially release someone from military service

Etymology

From French 'enlister', literally 'to put on a list'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01099911-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈlɪst / (AmE), / ɪnˈlɪst / (BrE)
to join the armed forces or arrange to become a member of the military
join the military

Examples

After high school, he decided to enlist in the navy.
Many young men chose to enlist during times of war.
She plans to enlist as soon as she turns eighteen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enroll - to register or sign up formally, especially for military service
  • Antonym: discharge - to release someone from military service

Etymology

From French *enlister*, from 'en-' (in) + 'liste' (list).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01099603-v
Synset POS: v
