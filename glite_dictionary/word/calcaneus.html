calcaneus

1. NOUN

/ kælˈkeɪniəs / (AmE), / kælˈkeɪnɪəs / (BrE)
the large bone forming the heel of the human foot
heel bone
medical

Examples

The surgeon explained that the patient's calcaneus was fractured in the accident.
Pain in the calcaneus can make walking very difficult.
X-ray images revealed a small tumor on her right calcaneus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heel bone - common term for calcaneus; bone forming the heel
  • Degree Higher: talus - the bone of the foot that sits on top of the calcaneus

Etymology

From Latin 'calcaneus' (pertaining to the heel).
