paid

1. ADJ

/ peɪd / (AmE), / peɪd / (BrE)
(of work, officials, services, etc.) receiving payment for service or completed with payment given.
receiving or involving payment

Examples

She finally found a paid internship after months of searching.
The paid staff members receive benefits, but volunteers do not.
Any paid advertisements must be clearly labeled as such.

Synset Examples

paid work
a paid official
a paid announcement
a paid check

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unpaid - not receiving payment for work or service

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'pay', from Old French 'paier', from Latin 'pacare' (to appease, to pacify, to pay).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01712702-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ peɪd / (AmE), / peɪd / (BrE)
(of work, a position, etc.) involving payment for employment, especially in contrast to voluntary or hobby activities.
involving payment for work

Examples

She finally found a paid position after years of volunteering.
Many artists struggle to turn their hobby into paid work.
He prefers to keep photography as a paid side job rather than a career.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unpaid - (of work, a position, etc.) not involving payment; done without financial compensation

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'pay', from Old French 'paier', from Latin 'pacare' (to pacify, satisfy a debt).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01875076-s
Synset POS: s
