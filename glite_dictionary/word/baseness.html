baseness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪsnəs / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being morally low, dishonorable, or lacking higher values
moral lowness or dishonor

Examples

The baseness of his actions shocked everyone in the room.
Her novel explores the baseness and nobility that coexist within human nature.
They were appalled by the baseness of the corruption exposed during the investigation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nobility - the quality of being noble in character or spirit
  • Synonym: depravity - moral corruption; wickedness

Etymology

From base (meaning 'morally low') + -ness (forming nouns of quality).
