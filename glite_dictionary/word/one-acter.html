one-acter

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʌn ˌæktər / (AmE), / ˈwʌn ˌæktə / (BrE)
a short play that consists of a single act
a short play in one act

Examples

The drama club decided to perform a one-acter for the school's spring festival.
She wrote a one-acter that won her first prize in the playwriting contest.
We enjoyed several funny one-acters during the evening showcase.

Synset Examples

The origin of the one-act play may be traced to the very beginning of drama: in ancient Greece, Cyclops, a satyr play by Euripides, is an early example.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: one-act play - a play in only one act

Etymology

Formed from 'one' + 'act' + '-er', denoting a play having a single act.
