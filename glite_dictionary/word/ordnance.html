ordnance

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrd.nəns / (AmE), / ˈɔːd.nəns / (BrE)
military weapons, ammunition, and equipment supplied to armed forces
military weapons and ammunition

Examples

The army's supply officers are responsible for distributing ordnance to the front lines.
Large amounts of ordnance were stored in underground bunkers during the war.
Experts were called in to safely remove unexploded ordnance from the battlefield.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: munitions - military weapons, ammunition, and stores; a synonym for ordnance

Etymology

From Old French 'ordenance' (an order or arrangement), from Medieval Latin 'ordinantia', related to 'ordinare' (to put in order).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrd.nəns / (AmE), / ˈɔːd.nəns / (BrE)
military weapons and equipment, especially large guns, artillery, and ammunition that can be moved and used in battle
military weapons and artillery

Examples

The army transported heavy ordnance to the front lines during the conflict.
Old military ordnance was discovered and safely detonated by experts.
Modern warships are equipped with advanced ordnance for defense and attack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artillery - large-caliber guns used in warfare on land
  • Synonym: munitions - military weapons, ammunition, and equipment

Etymology

From Middle English ordinance (“order, arrangement”), influenced by Old French ordener (“to arrange, to order”). Sense evolution from 'order' to 'military equipment'.
