hawkshaw

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑkˌʃɔ / (AmE), / ˈhɔːkʃɔː / (BrE)
A detective, especially one who is observant or clever.
detective (informal, dated)

Examples

The hawkshaw followed the suspect through the crowded station, blending in with the crowd.
As a rookie hawkshaw, she quickly developed a reputation for her keen intuition.
Old novels often feature a clever hawkshaw who always gets his man.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detective - a person whose job is to investigate crimes and find information

Etymology

From the name of Hawkshaw, a detective character in the 1863 play 'The Ticket-of-Leave Man' by Tom Taylor.
