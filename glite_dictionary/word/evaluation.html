evaluation

1. NOUN

/ ˌiˌvæljuˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌvæljuˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of judging or determining the value, quality, or importance of something
assessment or judgment of value

Examples

The teacher's evaluation of the student's work was detailed and fair.
We requested an independent evaluation to determine the property's market value.
Her performance evaluation showed significant improvement over the year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assessment - the process of making a judgment about something's quality or importance
  • Synonym: appraisal - the act of evaluating something or someone
  • Synonym: valuation - an estimation of something's worth or value
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention; disregard value

Etymology

From Latin 'valere' (to be worth) via French 'évaluer' and English 'evaluate'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌvæljuˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌvæljuːˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of judging or assessing the value, quality, or importance of something
assessment of value or quality

Examples

The teacher's evaluation of the students' essays was detailed and constructive.
We are waiting for an official evaluation before making a decision about the property.
The performance review included a thorough evaluation of her leadership skills.

Synset Examples

he set a high valuation on friendship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assessment - the process of evaluating or estimating the nature or quality of something
  • Synonym: appraisal - the act of estimating or judging the nature or value of something
  • Synonym: valuation - an estimation of something's worth, especially by a professional

Etymology

From Latin 'valere' (to be worth), via French 'évaluer' (to assess).
