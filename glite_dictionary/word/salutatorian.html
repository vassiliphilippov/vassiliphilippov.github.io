salutatorian

1. NOUN

/ səˌluːtəˈtɔriən / (AmE), / səˌljuːtəˈtɔːriən / (BrE)
a student who has the second highest academic rank in a graduating class and often delivers the opening address at the graduation ceremony
student with second highest academic rank

Examples

When she was named salutatorian, her parents were incredibly proud.
The salutatorian gave a memorable speech welcoming everyone to the ceremony.
He worked hard all four years and graduated as the class salutatorian.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: valedictorian - student with the highest academic rank in a graduating class

Etymology

From Latin 'salutatio' (a greeting) plus English '-ian'.
