Calidris ferruginea

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlaɪdrɪs ˌfɛrəˈdʒiːniə / (AmE), / kəˈlaɪdrɪs ˌfɛrəˈdʒiːniə / (BrE)
a species of Old World sandpiper with a curved bill resembling that of a curlew, commonly known as the curlew sandpiper
a species of Old World sandpiper

Examples

The Calidris ferruginea migrates long distances between Eurasia and Africa every year.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a rare Calidris ferruginea on the mudflats this spring.
Unlike many other sandpipers, the Calidris ferruginea has a distinctively downcurved bill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curlew sandpiper - common name for Calidris ferruginea, a sandpiper with a downcurved bill

Etymology

From Latin: 'calidris', a term used by Aristotle for a kind of waterside bird, and 'ferruginea' meaning 'rust-colored.'
