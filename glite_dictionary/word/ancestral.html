ancestral

1. ADJ

/ ænˈsɛstrəl / (AmE), / ænˈsestrəl / (BrE)
connected with or inherited from ancestors; belonging to or characteristic of former generations of a family or group.
related to ancestors or inheritance

Examples

She wore her ancestral necklace at the wedding, honoring her family's heritage.
The villagers gather annually at the ancestral home to celebrate their traditions.
They uncovered ancestral artifacts during the archaeological dig.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hereditary - transmitted from parent to offspring

Etymology

From Latin 'ancestralis', from 'antecessor' (predecessor, ancestor).

2. ADJ

/ ænˈsɛstrəl / (AmE), / ænˈsɛstrəl / (BrE)
relating to, or inherited from, one's ancestors; passed down from previous generations by established rules of descent
inherited from ancestors

Examples

Her family still lives in their ancestral home in the countryside.
The ancestral traditions are carefully preserved by each new generation.
He inherited an ancestral land that has belonged to his family for centuries.

Synset Examples

ancestral home
ancestral lore
hereditary monarchy
patrimonial estate
transmissible tradition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hereditary - passed from parent to offspring, especially by genetic means or legal inheritance

Etymology

From Latin 'ancestralis', from 'ancestor'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01317151-s
Synset POS: s
