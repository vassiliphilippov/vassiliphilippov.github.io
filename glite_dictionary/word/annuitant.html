annuitant

1. NOUN

/ əˈnuː.ɪ.tənt / (AmE), / əˈnjuː.ɪ.tənt / (BrE)
a person who receives payments from an annuity, often at regular intervals
recipient of an annuity

Examples

After retiring, she became an annuitant and received monthly income from her pension.
The insurance company tracks each annuitant to ensure timely payments.
As an annuitant, he relies on regular disbursements for his living expenses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beneficiary - person who receives benefits or payments, especially from a will, trust, or insurance

Etymology

From French 'annuitant', from 'annuité' (annuity) plus '-ant' (agent suffix).
