bulkiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌlkinəs / (AmE), / ˈbʌlkinɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being large and difficult to handle, often due to size or weight
the quality of being bulky

Examples

The bulkiness of the package made it hard to fit into the car.
She complained about the bulkiness of her winter coat when traveling.
Despite its bulkiness, the machine was surprisingly easy to operate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bulky - large and difficult to handle; taking up much space
  • Antonym: compactness - the quality of being closely packed together; small in size

Etymology

From 'bulky' + '-ness', where 'bulky' comes from 'bulk' (Middle English, possibly from Old Norse 'bulki' meaning 'cargo').
