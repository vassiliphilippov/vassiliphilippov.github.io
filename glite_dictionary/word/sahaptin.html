Sahaptin

1. NOUN

/ səˈhæptɪn / (AmE), / səˈhæptɪn / (BrE)
a Penutian language spoken by various Native American tribes in the interior Pacific Northwest of the United States
Native American language of the Pacific Northwest

Examples

The Sahaptin language is still spoken by some elders of the tribe.
A linguist published a grammar of Sahaptin in the 1970s.
Efforts are underway to revitalize Sahaptin among younger generations.

Etymology

From Salishan (Coast Salish) or related tribal name

2. NOUN

/ səˈhæptɪn / (AmE), / səˈhæptɪn / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people originally inhabiting regions along the Columbia River in Oregon, Washington, and northern Idaho.
member of a Native American people of the Pacific Northwest

Examples

Several Sahaptin families attended the annual tribal gathering along the river.
Anthropologists have studied the traditions of the Sahaptin for over a century.
The Sahaptin were skilled fishers and traders in the Pacific Northwest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nez Perce - a member of another indigenous group from the Pacific Northwest

Etymology

From Salishan roots, used as a community self-identifier; adopted into English in the 19th century.
