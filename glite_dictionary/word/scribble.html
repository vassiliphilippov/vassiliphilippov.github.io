scribble

1. NOUN

/ ˈskrɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈskrɪbəl / (BrE)
a careless or aimlessly drawn mark or drawing, often done quickly and without purpose
careless, aimless drawing

Examples

She absentmindedly filled the margin with a scribble of stars and circles.
The child's scribble covered the entire page in colorful loops.
What looked like a simple scribble was actually the start of her next masterpiece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doodle - a rough, absent-minded drawing

Etymology

From Latin scribillare, diminutive of scribere (to write).

2. NOUN

/ ˈskrɪb.əl / (AmE), / ˈskrɪb.əl / (BrE)
a piece of writing that is messy or difficult to read, especially due to poor handwriting
messy or illegible handwriting

Examples

I couldn't read his scribble on the note he left me.
Her hurried scribble made the shopping list hard to decipher.
The teacher struggled to make sense of the scribble on the homework assignment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrawl - carelessly written or drawn writing
  • Degree Higher: handwriting - writing by hand, style of writing

Etymology

From Latin 'scribere' (to write) via Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06415790-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈskrɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈskrɪbəl / (BrE)
to write something quickly and carelessly, making it difficult to read
write carelessly or untidily

Examples

She scribbled a quick note before leaving the house.
He tends to scribble his ideas on napkins during meetings.
The teacher could barely read the homework because it was scribbled so messily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrawl - to write in a hurried, careless, or untidy way

Etymology

Probably from Latin scribillare, frequentative of scribere (to write).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01751011-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈskrɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈskrɪbəl / (BrE)
to write or draw something quickly and carelessly, often with little attention to detail or neatness
write or draw carelessly

Examples

She grabbed a pen and scribbled a note before rushing out the door.
Stop scribbling on your homework and try to write more neatly.
He scribbled his phone number on a scrap of paper for me.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrawl - to write in a hurried, careless way
  • Antonym: neatly - in a tidy or orderly manner

Etymology

Probably from Latin 'scribillare', from 'scribere' (to write).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01008284-v
Synset POS: v
