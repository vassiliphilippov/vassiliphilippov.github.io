tichodrome

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɪkəˈdroʊm / (AmE), / ˌtɪkəˈdrəʊm / (BrE)
a small crimson-and-grey songbird (Tichodroma muraria) that lives on cliffs and town walls in southern Eurasia and northern Africa
crimson-and-grey cliff-dwelling songbird

Examples

We caught a glimpse of the rare tichodrome as it searched for insects on the old stone wall.
The distinctive coloring makes the tichodrome easy to spot against the cliffs.
Birdwatchers travel far to see a tichodrome in its natural rocky habitat.

Etymology

From Greek tikhodromos, meaning 'wall runner', from tikhē (wall) + dromos (running).
