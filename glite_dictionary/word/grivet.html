grivet

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɪvɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪvɪt / (BrE)
a white and olive green monkey of East Africa with long white tufts of hair beside its face
an East African monkey with white face tufts

Examples

The grivet is known for its striking white whiskers that stand out against its olive green fur.
Scientists observed a group of grivets foraging in the acacia trees near the river.
Unlike some other monkeys, the grivet has distinctive long tufts of hair beside its face.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vervet - a closely related African monkey also called the green monkey

Etymology

From French *grivet*, likely from an African language.
