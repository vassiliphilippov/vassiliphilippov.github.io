peerage

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɪərɪdʒ / (BrE)
the collective body of peers in a kingdom, especially in the United Kingdom
the peers of a kingdom; nobility

Examples

The British peerage has influenced the country’s political history for centuries.
Several members of the peerage attended the royal wedding.
He was recently elevated to the peerage by the Queen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nobility - the aristocratic class; people privileged by birth or title

Etymology

From Old French 'perage', from 'per' (peer) + '-age' (suffix forming nouns).
