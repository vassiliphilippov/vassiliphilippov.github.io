Aethionema

1. NOUN

/ iˌθiəˈniːmə / (AmE), / iˌθiːəˈniːmə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Brassicaceae (formerly Cruciferae), native to the Old World, especially the Mediterranean region.
genus of Old World cruciferous plants

Examples

Many alpine gardens feature species of Aethionema for their delicate pink flowers.
Aethionema thrives in well-drained, rocky soils found in the Mediterranean.
The botanist discovered a new species within the genus Aethionema during her expedition.

Etymology

From Greek aithōn (shining) and nema (thread), referring to the plant's bright filaments.

2. NOUN

/ ˌiːθi.oʊˈniːmə / (AmE), / ˌiːθi.əˈniːmə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Brassicaceae, native to the Old World, commonly known as stonecresses.
genus of flowering plants (stonecresses)

Examples

Several species belonging to the genus Aethionema are valued for their ornamental flowers.
Researchers studied how Aethionema plants adapt to rocky mountain habitats.
The pink blooms of Aethionema brighten many Mediterranean gardens in spring.

Etymology

From Greek 'aithō' (to burn) and 'nēma' (thread), referring to the thread-like stamens.
