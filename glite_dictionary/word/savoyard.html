Savoyard

1. NOUN

/ səˈvɔɪ.ɑrd / (AmE), / səˈvɔɪ.ɑːd / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Savoy, a historical region in southeastern France and western Italy
a person from Savoy

Examples

The museum had a special exhibit dedicated to Savoyard culture and traditions.
As a proud Savoyard, she loved to share stories of her mountain homeland.
Many Savoyards emigrated to different parts of Europe during the 19th century.

Etymology

From French 'savoyard', from Savoy (Savoie), a region in France and Italy.

2. NOUN

/ səˈvɔɪˌɑrd / (AmE), / səˈvɔɪ.ɑːd / (BrE)
a performer in the operettas of Gilbert and Sullivan, particularly those staged at the Savoy Theatre in London
performer in Gilbert and Sullivan operettas

Examples

After years of performing, she proudly identified herself as a Savoyard.
The Savoyard entertained the audience with clever comic songs from The Mikado.
Many Savoyards travel internationally to appear in Gilbert and Sullivan productions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gilbertian - relating to the works or style of W. S. Gilbert, especially the operettas with Sullivan

Etymology

From Savoy Theatre in London, where Gilbert and Sullivan's operettas were performed, combined with the suffix -ard.
