amphitheatre

1. NOUN

/ ˈæm.fəˌθi.ə.t̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈam.fɪˌθɪə.tə / (BrE)
A large, round or oval building with tiers of seats, typically used in ancient times for public spectacles, games, and performances.
large round or oval building for spectacles

Examples

The ancient Romans built the Colosseum as a grand amphitheatre for public spectacles.
Several concerts are held each summer in the open-air amphitheatre by the lake.
From the top tier of the amphitheatre, the audience had a perfect view of the stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arena - a level area surrounded by seats for sports or entertainment
  • Degree Lower: theatre - a building where performances are given

Etymology

From Greek amphitheatron, from amphi- (on both sides) + theatron (theatre).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæm.fəˌθi.ə.t̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈam.fɪˌθiː.ə.tə / (BrE)
a sloping, semicircular gallery with rising seats for spectators, especially in a theater or operating room
sloping gallery of seats for spectators

Examples

The doctors sat in the amphitheatre and observed the surgical procedure.
Hundreds of students filled the amphitheatre to watch the guest lecturer’s demonstration.
The hospital’s old amphitheatre is still used for training medical students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auditorium - the part of a theater, concert hall, or other public building where the audience sits

Etymology

From Greek 'amphitheatron', from 'amphi-' (on both sides) + 'theatron' (theater).
