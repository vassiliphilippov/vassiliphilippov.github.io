Microstrobos

1. NOUN

/ maɪ.kroʊˈstroʊ.bəs / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.krəʊˈstrəʊ.bɒs / (BrE)
A genus of small evergreen shrubs native to Australia and Tasmania, consisting of two species.
genus of small evergreen Australian shrubs

Examples

The Microstrobos genus is native to the cool, moist regions of Tasmania.
Botanists have classified two species under the Microstrobos genus.
The leaves of Microstrobos shrubs are tiny and scale-like, making them distinctive among evergreens.

Etymology

From Greek 'mikros' (small) + 'strobos' (cone), referring to the plant's small cones.
