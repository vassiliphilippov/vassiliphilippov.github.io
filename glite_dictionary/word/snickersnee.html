snickersnee

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnɪkərˌsni / (AmE), / ˈsnɪkəˌsniː / (BrE)
(archaic) Fighting with knives or a knife fight.
knife fight (archaic)
archaic

Examples

The two rivals settled their dispute with a snickersnee in the back alley.
In old tales, pirates were often depicted engaging in snickersnee over hidden treasure.
Fearing a snickersnee, the townspeople avoided the raucous tavern at night.

Etymology

From Dutch *snickersnee*, from *snijden* (to cut).
