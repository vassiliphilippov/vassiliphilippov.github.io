alright

1. ADV

/ ɔlˈraɪt / (AmE), / ɔːlˈraɪt / (BrE)
In a satisfactory or adequate manner; well enough, though not exceptionally so.
in a satisfactory manner; well enough
informal

Examples

He was nervous about the speech, but everything turned out alright in the end.
The engine sounded strange, but the car ran alright for the rest of the trip.
If you just follow the instructions, things should go alright.

Synset Examples

she'll do okay on her own
held up all right under pressure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: all right - in a satisfactory or adequate manner (standard spelling)
  • Degree Higher: excellent - extremely good or outstanding
  • Antonym: badly - in an unsatisfactory or poor manner

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'all right', likely influenced by analogy to already, altogether.

2. ADV

/ ɔlˈraɪt / (AmE), / ɔːlˈraɪt / (BrE)
Used to emphasize that there is no doubt; certainly or indeed.
used to reinforce an assertion
informal

Examples

He's talented, alright, but he's not disciplined.
The movie was scary, alright, so don't watch it alone.
It's a tough job, alright, but someone has to do it.

Synset Examples

it's expensive all right

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certainly - without any doubt; used for emphasis
  • Synonym: surely - used to emphasize the speaker’s firm belief in the truth of their statement
  • Antonym: possibly - indicating a lower degree of certainty
Source: WordNet

used to express agreement or understanding, often at the beginning of a sentence
expression of agreement or understanding
informal
