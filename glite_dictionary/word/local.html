local

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊkəl / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəl / (BrE)
a train or bus that stops at all stations or stops along its route, rather than only at major ones
train or bus that stops at all stations

Examples

We took the local because the express train wasn't running that day.
The local is slower, but it’s the only option late at night.
He always rides the local to avoid missing any stops near his office.

Synset Examples

the local seemed to take forever to get to New York

Related Terms

  • Antonym: express - a train or bus that stops only at major stations or stops
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03686268-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈloʊ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.kəl / (BrE)
relating to or affecting only a particular part or area, especially of the body.
affecting a restricted area

Examples

The dentist administered a local anesthetic before filling the cavity.
The infection appears to be local and hasn't spread to other organs.
She experienced only local side effects around the injection site.

Synset Examples

local anesthesia

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: regional - affecting a larger but still limited area
  • Antonym: general - affecting the whole body or area

Etymology

From Latin 'localis', from 'locus' (place).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01428402-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ ˈloʊkəl / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəl / (BrE)
a drug or injection that causes numbness in a specific part of the body, used especially in minor medical procedures
local anesthetic drug or injection
medical

Examples

The dentist gave me a local before filling the cavity, so I didn't feel any pain.
For the minor surgery, only a local was needed to numb my arm.
The doctor asked if I wanted a general or just a local for the procedure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: general anesthetic - an anesthetic that affects the whole body and induces unconsciousness
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03686474-n
Synset POS: n

4. ADJ

/ ˈloʊkəl / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəl / (BrE)
relating to the administration, affairs, or interests of a particular city, town, or district, rather than a larger area.
relating to a city, town, or district administration

Examples

The city council is responsible for local regulations and policies affecting residents.
Many local businesses benefit from government-funded improvement projects in the town.
The mayor addressed concerns about rising local taxes during the meeting.

Synset Examples

local taxes
local authorities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: municipal - relating to a town or city or its government
  • Degree Higher: regional - relating to a larger geographic area

Etymology

From Latin localis, from locus (place).

Source: WordNet

5. ADJ

/ ˈloʊkəl / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a particular place, area, or community, especially as distinct from a larger or wider area.
relating to a particular area or place

Examples

Many local residents support the new school project.
We enjoy trying different dishes at local restaurants when we travel.
The local newspaper covers events happening in our community.

Synset Examples

local customs
local schools
the local citizens
a local point of view
local outbreaks of flu
a local bus line

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: regional - relating to a large area or region; broader in scope than local
  • Degree Higher: national - relating to a whole country
  • Degree Higher: global - relating to the whole world

Etymology

From Latin 'localis', from 'locus' (place).
