tenacity

1. NOUN

/ təˈnæsəti / (AmE), / tɪˈnæsɪti / (BrE)
the quality or fact of being very determined and persistent in achieving something
persistent determination

Examples

Her tenacity in pursuing her goals inspired everyone around her.
Despite repeated failures, his tenacity eventually led to success.
The athlete's tenacity was evident as she completed the race with an injured leg.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: determination - the quality of being firmly committed to a purpose
  • Synonym: perseverance - continued effort to do or achieve something despite difficulties
  • Synonym: resoluteness - firmness or resolve in carrying out a course of action
  • Antonym: indecision - inability to make decisions quickly
  • Antonym: hesitation - the action of pausing due to uncertainty or reluctance

Etymology

From Latin tenacitas, from tenax (holding fast) and tenere (to hold).
