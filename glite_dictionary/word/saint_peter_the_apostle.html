Saint Peter the Apostle

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈpiːtər ði əˈpɑːsəl / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈpiːtə ði əˈpɒsl̩ / (BrE)
the disciple of Jesus who became the leader of the Apostles, regarded as the first Pope and the vicar of Christ on earth by Catholics
disciple of Jesus and first Pope

Examples

Many churches around the world are dedicated to Saint Peter the Apostle.
According to tradition, Saint Peter the Apostle was crucified upside down in Rome.
Saint Peter the Apostle is often depicted holding keys, symbolizing his authority in the early Church.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Apostle - one of the twelve chief disciples of Jesus
  • Synonym: Saint Paul the Apostle - another prominent apostle in early Christianity

Etymology

From Latin Petrus, from Greek Petros meaning 'rock', reflecting Jesus' naming of Peter as the 'rock' on which his church would be built.
