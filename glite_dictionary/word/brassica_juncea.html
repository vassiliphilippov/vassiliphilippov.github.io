Brassica juncea

1. NOUN

/ brəˈsɪkə ˈdʒuːnsiə / (AmE), / brəˈsɪkə ˈdʒʌnsiə / (BrE)
a plant species of the mustard family, commonly known as Indian mustard or mustard greens, used as a leafy vegetable and for its seeds
a leafy vegetable, Indian mustard

Examples

The spicy leaves of Brassica juncea are often stir-fried in Asian cuisine.
Many gardeners grow Brassica juncea for its nutritious and flavorful greens.
The seeds of Brassica juncea are also used to make a pungent mustard oil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mustard greens - leafy greens of various mustard plants, used as a vegetable
  • Synonym: mustard plant - any plant in the genus Brassica used for seeds or as a vegetable

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: 'Brassica' (Latin for 'cabbage'); 'juncea' (Latin for 'rush-like', referring to appearance).
