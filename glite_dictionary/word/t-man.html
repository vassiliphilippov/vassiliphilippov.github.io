T-man

1. NOUN

/ ˈtiː mæn / (AmE), / ˈtiː mæn / (BrE)
A male law-enforcement agent of the United States Treasury Department, especially one involved in enforcing federal laws related to finance and tax.
male U.S. Treasury law-enforcement agent
american

Examples

The T-man tracked down counterfeiters across state lines.
In 1930s films, a T-man was often portrayed as a determined investigator.
After years on the job, the experienced T-man retired with honor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: G-man - a male law-enforcement agent of the U.S. federal government, especially the FBI

Etymology

Formed from 'T' (an abbreviation for 'Treasury') and 'man'.
