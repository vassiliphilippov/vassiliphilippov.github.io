pachouli

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtʃoʊli / (AmE), / pəˈtʃəʊli / (BrE)
A small East Indian shrub of the mint family with fragrant leaves, from which a strongly scented oil used in perfumes is obtained.
fragrant tropical shrub; oil used in perfumes

Examples

The pachouli plant is prized for its aromatic leaves used in traditional incense.
Many perfumes contain pachouli oil to add a rich, earthy scent.
She bought a bottle of pachouli essential oil at the herbal shop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patchouli - fragrant East Indian shrub and its oil (alternative spelling)

Etymology

From Hindi paccūli, from Tamil paccilai (green leaf).

2. NOUN

/ pəˈtʃoʊli / (AmE), / pəˈtʃəʊli / (BrE)
A strong, heavy perfume made from the oil of the patchouli plant.
perfume from the patchouli plant

Examples

She dabbed a little pachouli on her wrists before leaving the house.
The shop was filled with the unmistakable scent of pachouli.
Some people find the smell of pachouli too strong, while others love its earthy aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patchouli - a perennial Southeast Asian plant or the essential oil/perfume from it

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'patchouli', from Tamil *pacci* (green) + *ilai* (leaf).
