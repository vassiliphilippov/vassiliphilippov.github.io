communalism

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmjunəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːnəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the practice or principle of living together and sharing property, resources, and responsibilities within a community
communal living and shared ownership

Examples

Some religious groups practice communalism as a way of promoting equality and cooperation.
The founders of the settlement were inspired by ideals of communalism and mutual support.
Communalism encourages people to pool their resources and share daily responsibilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collectivism - the ownership of property and resources by the whole community
  • Antonym: individualism - the belief in the importance of the individual and personal independence

Etymology

From French 'communalisme', from 'commun' meaning 'common'.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈmjunəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːnəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the principle or practice of giving priority loyalty and commitment to one's own minority or ethnic group rather than to society as a whole
group loyalty over broader society

Examples

The rise of communalism in the region has contributed to political tensions.
Critics argue that communalism can hinder national unity and integration.
Many leaders called for an end to communalism and discrimination among different groups.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: individualism - the habit or principle of being independent and self-reliant; prioritizing the individual over the group

Etymology

From 'commune' + '-alism'; related to the idea of group or community interests.
