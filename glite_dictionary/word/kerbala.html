Kerbala

1. PROPN

/ kərˈbɑːlə / (AmE), / kəˈbɑːlə / (BrE)
A city in central Iraq, south of Baghdad, regarded as a holy city by Shiite Muslims because it is the site of the tomb of Husayn ibn Ali, the grandson of the Prophet Muhammad, who was killed there in 680.
holy Shiite city in Iraq

Examples

Pilgrims travel to Kerbala each year to commemorate the martyrdom of Husayn ibn Ali.
The city of Kerbala holds immense significance for millions of Shiite Muslims around the world.
Fierce battles have taken place near Kerbala throughout its long history.

Etymology

From Arabic كَرْبَلَاء‎ (Karbalaʾ).
