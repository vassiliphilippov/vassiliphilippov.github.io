can-kicking

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæn ˌkɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkæn ˌkɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the action of delaying or avoiding dealing with a difficult issue or making a decision, especially by postponing it for later
postponing a decision or problem
informal

Examples

The company’s repeated can-kicking only made the financial crisis worse.
After months of can-kicking, the committee was forced to confront the issue.
We need leaders who solve problems, not ones who resort to can-kicking.

Synset Examples

The government has been can-kicking on the budget issue.
Instead of solving the problem, they keep can-kicking it down the road.
We can't afford more can-kicking; it's time to act.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kick the can down the road - to postpone dealing with a problem
  • Synonym: procrastination - the act of delaying or postponing something

Etymology

Based on the metaphor 'kick the can down the road,' meaning to defer action or avoid making a decision.
