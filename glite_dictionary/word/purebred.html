purebred

1. ADJ

/ ˈpjʊrˌbrɛd / (AmE), / ˈpjʊəˌbrɛd / (BrE)
(of an animal) bred for many generations from members of a recognized breed or strain, without outside crossbreeding.
bred from a recognized breed (animals)

Examples

The kennel only accepts purebred dogs for registration in the show.
She bought a purebred Siamese cat from a reputable breeder.
Many horse owners prefer purebred animals for their predictable traits and lineage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pedigree - having a documented ancestry or lineage
  • Antonym: mongrel - an animal resulting from the crossbreeding of different breeds or strains

Etymology

Formed from 'pure' and 'bred', referring to unadulterated lineage in breeding.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpjʊrˌbrɛd / (AmE), / ˈpjʊəˌbrɛd / (BrE)
an animal whose parents are both of the same recognized breed, especially one with a recorded pedigree
animal of unmixed breed

Examples

The kennel club held a show exclusively for purebred dogs.
Raising a purebred horse requires careful attention to its lineage.
She prefers to adopt rescue animals rather than buy purebred pets from breeders.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: crossbreed - an animal resulting from the crossing of different breeds
  • Synonym: pedigree - the record of descent of an animal, showing it to be purebred

Etymology

from 'pure' + 'bred', indicating unmixed breeding origin (first attested early 20th century)

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
