to glorify

1. VERB

/ ˈɡlɔːrɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡlɒrɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
to give glory, honor, or great praise to someone or something; to make something seem admirable or magnificent
give glory or great praise to

Examples

The documentary glorifies the achievements of the explorers.
They were accused of trying to glorify war in their speeches.
The artist's work glorifies the beauty of nature in every detail.

Synset Examples

The victory over the enemy glorified the Republic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: praise - to express approval or admiration
  • Antonym: to demean - to lower in dignity or status

Etymology

From Latin 'glorificare', from 'gloria' (glory) + 'facere' (to make).

2. VERB

/ ˈɡlɔːrɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡlɔːrɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to praise or honor someone or something highly, often as more impressive or important than they really are
to praise or honor highly

Examples

The coach would often glorify the team's achievements in his interviews.
Many films tend to glorify violence, drawing criticism from concerned groups.
The biography tries to glorify the leader's role in the nation's history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: praise - to express approval or admiration
  • Antonym: to demean - to lower in dignity or standing

Etymology

From Latin glorificare, from gloria (glory) + facere (to make).

3. VERB

/ ˈɡlɔːrɪfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡlɔːrɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to describe or present something as more splendid or praiseworthy than it really is; to exaggerate its positive qualities
make something seem more splendid than it is

Examples

The article tries to glorify the achievements of the company, glossing over its failures.
Some films tend to glorify violence, making it seem exciting or heroic.
They often glorify their hometown's history, even when the facts are less impressive.

Synset Examples

You are glorifying a rather mediocre building

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exaggerate - to represent something as better or worse than it really is

Etymology

From Old French glorifier, from Late Latin glorificare (to glorify), from Latin gloria (glory).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00490432-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈɡlɔːr.əˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡlɔː.rɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to praise or honor something or someone highly, often by making them seem better or more admirable than they truly are; to elevate or idealize in reputation or status.
to praise or idealize highly

Examples

The statue was built to glorify the achievements of the nation's founders.
Some films tend to glorify violence rather than show its consequences.
In their speeches, they glorified their leader as a visionary and savior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exalt - to raise in rank, honor, or power; to praise highly
  • Synonym: praise - to express admiration or approval for someone or something
  • Antonym: to demean - to lower in dignity, honor, or standing

Etymology

From Latin glorificare (to glorify), from gloria (glory) + facere (to make).

Synset POS: v
