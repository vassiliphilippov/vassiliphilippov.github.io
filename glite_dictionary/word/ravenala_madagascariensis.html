Ravenala madagascariensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌrævəˈnɑːlə ˌmædəˌɡæskəˈriːənˌsɪs / (AmE), / ˌrævənˈɑːlə ˌmædəˌɡæskəˈriːənˌsɪs / (BrE)
A tall, tree-like plant native to Madagascar, known as the traveler's palm, with edible nuts and leafstalks that produce drinkable sap.
tropical plant, traveler's palm of Madagascar

Examples

The Ravenala madagascariensis, or traveler's palm, is a common sight in Madagascar's rainforests.
Early explorers used the sap from Ravenala madagascariensis as a source of drinking water.
Gardeners prize Ravenala madagascariensis for its dramatic, fan-shaped leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traveler's palm - common name for Ravenala madagascariensis, famous for water-holding leaf bases

Etymology

From scientific Latin; genus name Ravenala from Malagasy, species epithet refers to Madagascar.
