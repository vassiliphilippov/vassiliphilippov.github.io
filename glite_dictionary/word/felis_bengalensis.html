Felis bengalensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌfiːlɪs bɛnˈɡælɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌfiːlɪs bɛnˈɡalɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A small wildcat species of southern Asia and Malaysia, with a spotted coat.
small wildcat of southern Asia, Malaysia

Examples

The Felis bengalensis is known for its distinctive spotted fur and agile climbing ability.
Researchers tracked a population of Felis bengalensis in the forests of Malaysia.
Habitat loss poses a serious threat to the survival of Felis bengalensis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leopard cat - common name for Felis bengalensis

Etymology

From Latin: 'Felis' (cat) and 'bengalensis' (of Bengal).
