Caucasus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔkəsəs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːkəsəs / (BrE)
a mountain range and region in Eurasia between the Black Sea and the Caspian Sea, forming part of the traditional border between Europe and Asia
mountain range between Black and Caspian Seas

Examples

The Caucasus has long been considered a crossroads of various cultures and empires.
Mount Elbrus, the highest peak in the Caucasus, attracts climbers from all over the world.
Tensions in the Caucasus region have shaped much of its modern political landscape.

Etymology

From Latin Caucasus, from Ancient Greek Kaukasis.

2. PROPN

/ ˈkɔːkəsəs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːkəsəs / (BrE)
a large region situated between the Black Sea and the Caspian Sea, known for the Caucasus Mountains and rich oil resources
region between Black and Caspian Seas

Examples

Many ancient cultures developed in the Caucasus due to its strategic location and diverse landscape.
The Caucasus is home to a wide variety of ethnic groups and languages.
Geologists are interested in the Caucasus for both its mountains and its significant oil reserves.

Etymology

From Latin 'Caucasus', from Ancient Greek 'Καύκασος' (Kaukásos), the name of the mountain region.
