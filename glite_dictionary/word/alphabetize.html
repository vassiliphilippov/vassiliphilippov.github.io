to alphabetize

1. VERB

/ ˈæl.fə.bəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈæl.fə.bɪ.taɪz / (BrE)
to arrange words or items in the order of the letters of the alphabet
arrange in alphabetical order

Examples

Please alphabetize the names before submitting the roster.
The librarian had to alphabetize hundreds of books on the new shelf.
We decided to alphabetize our DVDs so we could find movies more easily.

Synset Examples

Alphabetize the list

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sort - to arrange in a particular order

Etymology

From alphabet + -ize, modeled after French alphabétiser.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00280009-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈæl.fə.bəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈæl.fə.bə.taɪz / (BrE)
To provide (a language or group) with an alphabet; to create or introduce a writing system based on an alphabet.
to provide with an alphabet

Examples

Linguists worked to alphabetize the indigenous language, making it easier to read and write.
The missionaries sought to alphabetize the spoken dialects they encountered.
After years of study, she helped alphabetize the local language by developing a suitable script.

Synset Examples

Cyril and Method alphabetized the Slavic languages

Etymology

From 'alphabet' + '-ize'; modeled after the French 'alphabétiser'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02335963-v
Synset POS: v
