Sialis

1. PROPN

/ saɪˈælɪs / (AmE), / saɪˈɑːlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of insects in the family Sialidae, commonly known as alderflies.
genus of alderflies

Examples

The larvae of Sialis are aquatic and play a role in freshwater ecosystems.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Sialis genus in northern Europe.
Adult Sialis are often seen resting on leaves near streams in spring.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Sialidae - family of insects including alderflies

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek sialis (a kind of bird or fly).
