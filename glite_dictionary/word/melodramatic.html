melodramatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɛlədrəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛlədrəˈmatɪk / (BrE)
behaving or presented in an exaggerated, emotional, or theatrical manner, often more than seems appropriate for the situation
overly dramatic or emotional

Examples

Her melodramatic reaction to the news drew puzzled looks from everyone in the room.
The actor's melodramatic gestures distracted from the seriousness of the scene.
He tends to become melodramatic when telling stories about his childhood.

Synset Examples

histrionic gestures
an attitude of melodramatic despair
a theatrical pose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: histrionic - overly theatrical or dramatic in character or style
  • Synonym: theatrical - exaggerated and excessively dramatic
  • Antonym: restrained - showing control of emotion or behavior

Etymology

From French *mélodramatique*, from *mélodrame* (melodrama).

2. ADJ

/ ˌmɛlədrəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛlədrəˈmatɪk / (BrE)
characterized by exaggerated emotions and excitement, typical of melodrama
overly emotional or exaggerated

Examples

Her reaction to the news was so melodramatic that everyone in the office stopped and stared.
He tends to become melodramatic about minor setbacks, making them seem like major disasters.
The movie's plot was criticized for being too melodramatic and unrealistic.

Synset Examples

a melodramatic account of two perilous days at sea

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dramatic - relating to drama or the performance of drama; exciting
  • Synonym: histrionic - excessively theatrical or dramatic in character or style
  • Antonym: restrained - characterized by moderation or reserve; not excessively emotional

Etymology

From French mélodramatique, from mélodrame (melodrama), ultimately from Greek melos (song) + drama (action, play).
