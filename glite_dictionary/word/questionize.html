to questionize

1. VERB

/ ˈkwɛstʃəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkwɛstʃəˌnaɪz / (BrE)
to inquire into or challenge something, especially by critically examining ideas or beliefs
to critically challenge or examine beliefs or ideas

Examples

It's important to questionize deeply held traditions in order to understand their value.
She often questionizes company policies she disagrees with.
At the seminar, attendees were encouraged to questionize every statement presented.

Synset Examples

Don't hesitate to questionize anything that seems off.
It's time to questionize the assumptions we've made.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: question - to ask questions about something; to doubt
  • Synonym: challenge - to dispute or question the truth or validity of; demand justification
  • Antonym: to accept - to believe or take as true without questioning

Etymology

Formed from 'question' (from Latin 'quaestionem', question) + '-ize', a productive English verb-forming suffix.
