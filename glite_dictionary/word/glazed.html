glazed

1. ADJ

/ ɡleɪzd / (AmE), / ɡleɪzd / (BrE)
fitted or covered with glass, especially in reference to windows, doors, or surfaces.
fitted or covered with glass

Examples

The sunroom features fully glazed doors that overlook the garden.
Modern office buildings often have glazed walls to maximize natural light.
Each display case was glazed to protect the artifacts inside.

Synset Examples

four glazed walls

Etymology

From Middle English glasen (to fit with glass), from glass.

2. ADJ

/ ɡleɪzd / (AmE), / ɡleɪzd / (BrE)
(of eyes) showing no emotion or liveliness, often appearing dull or unfocused.
(of eyes) dull, lacking liveliness

Examples

Her glazed eyes stared blankly at the wall, lost in thought.
After hours of studying, his expression became glazed and unfocused.
During the dull lecture, many students sat with glazed eyes, barely listening.

Synset Examples

empty eyes
a glassy stare
his eyes were glazed over with boredom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glassy - (of eyes) having a shiny, fixed appearance, lacking expression
  • Antonym: lively - (of eyes) full of life and energy

/ ɡleɪzd / (AmE), / ɡleɪzd / (BrE)

coated with a shiny layer (food)

Examples

She brought a box of glazed doughnuts for the office breakfast.
The chef prepared a beautifully glazed ham for the holiday dinner.
The pastry had a glazed surface that caught the light.
