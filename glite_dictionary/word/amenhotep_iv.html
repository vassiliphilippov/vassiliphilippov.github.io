Amenhotep IV

1. PROPN

/ əˌmɛnhoʊˈtɛp ðə ˈfɔrθ / (AmE), / əˌmɛnˈhəʊtɛp ðə ˈfɔːθ / (BrE)
an early ruler of Egypt (reigned c.1353–1336 BC) who rejected traditional Egyptian gods and promoted the worship of the sun disc, Aten; also known as Akhenaten.
Egyptian pharaoh who introduced sun worship

Examples

Amenhotep IV is notable for introducing monotheistic worship of Aten during his reign in Egypt.
Many scholars debate whether Amenhotep IV's religious reforms influenced later religious movements.
The art and architecture from the period of Amenhotep IV reflect major cultural changes in ancient Egypt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Akhenaten - Egyptian pharaoh, originally known as Amenhotep IV, who introduced worship of Aten

Etymology

From Egyptian, meaning 'Amun is satisfied.'
