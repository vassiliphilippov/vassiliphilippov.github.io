elderberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛldərˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈɛldəˌbɛri / (BrE)
A small tree or shrub of Europe and Asia with clusters of small black or dark purple berries, used for making wines and jellies.
a shrub/tree with edible dark berries

Examples

She made a delicious homemade jam from fresh elderberries.
The elderberry bush in our garden attracts many birds every summer.
Many people use elderberry syrup as a natural remedy for colds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sambucus - the genus of flowering plants known as elders or elderberries

Etymology

From 'elder' (the tree) + 'berry'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛldɚˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈɛldəˌbɛri / (BrE)
A small, dark purple fruit from the elder tree, often used to make wines, jellies, and syrups.
small dark berry from the elder tree

Examples

She picked a basket of elderberries to make homemade jam.
The wine was made from fermented elderberries gathered in late summer.
Many people believe elderberry syrup can help support the immune system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elder - the shrub or tree that produces elderberries

Etymology

From 'elder' (the tree) + 'berry'; 'elder' from Old English 'ellærn'.
