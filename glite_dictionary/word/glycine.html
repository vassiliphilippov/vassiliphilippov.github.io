Glycine

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlaɪˌsin / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪsiːn / (BrE)
A genus of Asiatic erect or sprawling herbs in the pea family, including the soybean.
genus of herbs including soybean

Examples

The genus Glycine includes species important for agriculture, such as the soybean.
Botanists have studied the growth habits of various Glycine species.
Many plant breeders are interested in the genetic diversity within Glycine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soybean - An edible bean from plants of the genus Glycine, used as food and in industry

Etymology

From Greek 'glykys' (sweet), referring to the sweet taste of some species.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡlaɪˌsin / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪsiːn / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the pea family, including the soya bean and other similar Asiatic herbs.
genus of plants including the soya bean

Examples

The genus Glycine includes the commercially important soya bean.
Botanists debated the classification of certain species within Glycine.
The agricultural value of Glycine has increased due to the demand for soy products.

Etymology

From Greek *glykys* (sweet), alluding to the sweet roots of some species.
