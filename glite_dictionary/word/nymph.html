nymph

1. NOUN

/ nɪmf / (AmE), / nɪmf / (BrE)
the immature form of some insects, such as dragonflies or mayflies, that undergoes incomplete metamorphosis and usually resembles the adult except for size and lack of wings
immature stage of some insects

Examples

The mayfly spends most of its life as a nymph before emerging as an adult.
The dragonfly nymph lives underwater and preys on small aquatic animals.
Scientists collected several nymphs to study their development.

Etymology

From Greek nymphē (bride, maiden, nymph), referring to mythological nature spirits and later used in zoology.

2. NOUN

/ nɪmf / (AmE), / nɪmf / (BrE)
a young woman who is very beautiful and attractive, often depicted as charming and alluring, especially in literary or poetic contexts
beautiful and attractive young woman (literary)

Examples

The actress played the role of a mysterious nymph in the fantasy film.
In the poetry, she is described as a delicate nymph dancing by the river.
Many artists have painted the image of a nymph to capture youthful beauty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: siren - a woman regarded as alluring but potentially dangerous
  • Synonym: enchantress - a woman of great charm or fascination

Etymology

From Greek nymphē (young woman, bride, beautiful maiden).

3. NOUN

/ nɪmf / (AmE), / nɪmf / (BrE)
(in classical mythology) a minor female deity or spirit of nature, typically depicted as a beautiful maiden inhabiting rivers, woods, or other natural places.
minor female nature deity in mythology

Examples

In ancient myths, a nymph would often be found living near a spring or in a lush forest.
Many Renaissance paintings portray a nymph as a graceful and enchanting young woman.
The poet described the river as being guarded by a gentle nymph.

Synset Examples

the ancient Greeks believed that nymphs inhabited forests and bodies of water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fairy - a small imaginary being of human form with magical powers
  • Synonym: dryad - a tree or wood nymph in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Greek numphē (bride, young woman, nymph).
