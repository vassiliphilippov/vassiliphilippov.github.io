distraint

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈstreɪnt / (AmE), / dɪˈstreɪnt / (BrE)
the legal seizure of someone's property in order to obtain payment of money owed, especially rent
legal seizure of property for debt
legal

Examples

The court issued a warrant of distraint on his belongings after he failed to pay the debt.
Distraint was used historically by landlords to reclaim unpaid rent from tenants.
They feared the threat of distraint if the loan remained unpaid.

Synset Examples

Originally distress was a landlord's remedy against a tenant for unpaid rents or property damage but now the landlord is given a landlord's lien

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distress - the act of seizing property to obtain payment or fulfilment of an obligation
  • Degree Higher: foreclosure - taking possession of mortgaged property for failure to pay a debt

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *distraint*, from Old French *destreinte*, past participle of *destreindre* (to constrain, to seize).
