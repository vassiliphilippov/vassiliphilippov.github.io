yellowlegs

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɛloʊˌlɛɡz / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊˌlɛɡz / (BrE)
either of two species of North American shorebirds, the greater yellowlegs and the lesser yellowlegs, both characterized by their long yellow legs and spotted plumage.
North American shorebird with yellow legs

Examples

We spotted a flock of yellowlegs feeding along the muddy shoreline.
The yellowlegs is easy to identify by its bright yellow legs and slender bill.
During migration, thousands of yellowlegs can be seen at coastal wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandpiper - a wading bird of the family Scolopacidae, related to yellowlegs

Etymology

From 'yellow' + 'legs', describing the bird's distinctive coloration.
