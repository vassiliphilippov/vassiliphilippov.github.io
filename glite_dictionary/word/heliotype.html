heliotype

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːliəˌtaɪp / (AmE), / ˈhiːlɪəʊtaɪp / (BrE)
a process for producing photographic prints using a gelatin plate, or a print made by this method
photographic printing process or its print

Examples

The museum displayed a rare heliotype of the original manuscript.
A heliotype was used to reproduce detailed images in early books.
She collected 19th-century prints, including an exquisite heliotype portrait.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photogravure - a similar photographic printing process

Etymology

From Greek hēlios (sun) + type, reflecting its use of light in printing.
