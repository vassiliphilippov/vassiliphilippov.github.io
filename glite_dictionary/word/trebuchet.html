trebuchet

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛb.juːˌʃɛt / (AmE), / ˈtrɛb.jʊˌʃɛt / (BrE)
A large medieval catapult with a swinging arm used to hurl projectiles at enemy fortifications.
medieval siege catapult

Examples

During the siege, the army launched boulders over the city walls using a trebuchet.
Building a small model trebuchet can help students understand medieval warfare technology.
The museum had a full-size trebuchet on display in its medieval weapons section.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catapult - a device for launching projectiles

Etymology

From Old French *trebuchet*, meaning 'war engine', from *trebucher* (to overthrow).
