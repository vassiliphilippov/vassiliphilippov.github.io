atopognosis

1. NOUN

/ əˌtoʊpɑɡˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / əˌtəʊpɒɡˈnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
the inability to correctly identify the location on the body where a touch stimulus is applied, due to neurological impairment
inability to localize touch (medical)
medical

Examples

Patients with atopognosis may not be able to indicate where they have been touched on their skin.
The neurologist tested for atopognosis by lightly tapping different parts of the patient's arm.
Loss of sensation and atopognosis can indicate damage to the parietal lobe.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: topognosia - the ability to localize sensation or touch on the body

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'topos' (place) + 'gnosis' (knowledge).
