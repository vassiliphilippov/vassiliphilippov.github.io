anthropopathism

1. NOUN

/ ˌænθrəˈpɑːθɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌanθrəˈpɒθɪzəm / (BrE)
The attribution of human emotions or feelings to non-human entities, such as animals, objects, or deities.
attributing human feelings to non-humans

Examples

The poet's reference to the angry sea is an example of anthropopathism.
Some critics argue that describing the wind as 'jealous' is a form of anthropopathism.
In ancient myths, anthropopathism was used to explain the actions of gods and nature.

Synset Examples

Moreover, when hate is attributed to God in scriptural translations, it is most likely an anthropopathism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthropomorphism - attributing human characteristics or behavior to non-humans

Etymology

From Greek 'anthrōpos' (human) and 'pathos' (feeling), patterned after analogous terms like 'anthropomorphism'.
