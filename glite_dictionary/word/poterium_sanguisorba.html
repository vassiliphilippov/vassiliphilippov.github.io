Poterium sanguisorba

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈtɪriəm sæŋˌɡwɪˈsɔrbə / (AmE), / pəʊˈtɪəriəm sæŋˌɡwɪˈsɔːbə / (BrE)
A European perennial herb with purple-tinged flowers and pinnate leaves, sometimes used in salads.
European herb used in salads

Examples

The leaves of Poterium sanguisorba add a fresh, slightly nutty flavor to mixed salads.
Many gardeners cultivate Poterium sanguisorba as a decorative and edible border plant.
Herbalists have long valued Poterium sanguisorba for its traditional medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salad burnet - common name for Poterium sanguisorba, a leafy salad herb

Etymology

From Latin 'Poterium', ancient name for burnet, and 'sanguisorba', from Latin 'sanguis' (blood) and 'sorbere' (to absorb), referring to traditional medicinal uses.
