suppleness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌplənəs / (AmE), / ˈsʌplɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being able to adapt easily to new situations or changes, especially in mind or character
adaptability; mental or character flexibility

Examples

Her suppleness allowed her to navigate complex negotiations with ease.
A leader's suppleness of mind helps a team adapt to change.
The company's suppleness in strategy was key to surviving the crisis.

Synset Examples

he was valued for his reliability and pliability
he increased the leanness and suppleness of the organization

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flexibility - the quality of bending easily or being easily adapted
  • Antonym: rigidity - inability or unwillingness to change or adapt

Etymology

From Old French 'souplesse', from Latin 'supplex' (submissive, flexible).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌplənəs / (AmE), / ˈsʌplɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being flexible and bending easily without breaking, especially in a graceful manner
flexible and graceful quality

Examples

The dancer's suppleness was evident in the way she moved across the stage.
Yoga can improve the suppleness of your joints and muscles.
Tigers are admired for the suppleness and speed of their bodies in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flexibility - the ability to bend easily without breaking
  • Antonym: stiffness - the quality of being rigid, not flexible

Etymology

From Old French souplesse, from souple (supple), ultimately from Latin supplex (submissive, bending under).

3. NOUN

/ ˈsʌplənəs / (AmE), / ˈsʌplɪnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being easily bent or flexible without breaking; flexibility or pliancy
quality of being flexible or pliant

Examples

Her yoga practice greatly improved the suppleness of her muscles.
The suppleness of the leather made it ideal for crafting gloves.
A dancer needs both strength and suppleness to perform fluid movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flexibility - the ability to bend easily without breaking
  • Antonym: rigidity - the quality of being stiff and inflexible

Etymology

From Old French 'soupless', from Latin 'supplex' meaning 'submissive, bending under'.
