unhappy

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈhæpi / (AmE), / ʌnˈhæpi / (BrE)
Causing discomfort or distress; unpleasant or unfortunate.
causing discomfort or distress

Examples

The unhappy outcome of the negotiations disappointed everyone involved.
She had to deliver the unhappy news to the staff this morning.
It is an unhappy truth that not all dreams come true.

Synset Examples

the unhappy truth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unpleasant - not pleasant; causing discomfort or unhappiness
  • Antonym: happy - feeling or showing pleasure or contentment

Etymology

From un- (not) + happy, originally meaning 'not happy' and later developing related senses.
