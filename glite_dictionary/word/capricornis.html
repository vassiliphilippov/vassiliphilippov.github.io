Capricornis

1. PROPN

/ ˌkæprɪˈkɔrnɪs / (AmE), / ˌkæprɪˈkɔːnɪs / (BrE)
A genus of goat-antelopes, known as serows, native to eastern Asia.
genus of goat-antelope (serow)

Examples

The genus Capricornis includes several species of serows native to Asia.
Biologists studied the habits of Capricornis in the mountainous regions of Japan.
Capricornis is notable for its distinctive backward-curving horns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serow - a goat-antelope found in eastern Asia

Etymology

From Latin capricornus (goat-horned), referring to its goat-like appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkæprəˈkɔrnɪs / (AmE), / ˌkæprɪˈkɔːnɪs / (BrE)
A genus of goat-antelopes known as serows, found in Asia, characterized by short, sharp horns and a stocky build.
a genus of goat-antelopes (serows)

Examples

The endangered Capricornis species are found in mountainous regions across Asia.
Researchers observed a herd of Capricornis grazing on the steep hillside.
Conservation efforts focus on preserving the habitat of Capricornis in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serow - A medium-sized goat-antelope native to Asia, member of Capricornis genus

Etymology

From Latin 'capricornus', meaning 'goat horn', used in zoological nomenclature.
