oath

1. NOUN

/ oʊθ / (AmE), / əʊθ / (BrE)
a solemn promise, often made in the presence of a divine witness, about one's future actions or behavior
solemn promise; declaration of intention

Examples

The president swore an oath to uphold the Constitution.
Every witness in court must take an oath before testifying.
Doctors often recite the Hippocratic oath at their graduation ceremonies.

Synset Examples

they took an oath of allegiance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vow - a serious promise, often to do something or behave in a certain way

Etymology

From Old English āþ, from Proto-Germanic *aiþaz, related to Old Norse eiðr (oath).

2. NOUN

/ oʊθ / (AmE), / əʊθ / (BrE)
a profane or obscene word or expression, typically used to express strong emotion such as surprise or anger
profane or obscene expression

Examples

Unable to control himself, he let out a loud oath when he hit his thumb with the hammer.
She muttered an oath under her breath as the door slammed shut.
His angry oath echoed through the empty hallway.

Synset Examples

expletives were deleted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curse - a profane or obscene word, especially as an expression of anger
  • Synonym: expletive - an oath or swear word expressing strong emotion

Etymology

From Old English āth, related to Old Norse eithr (oath), from Proto-Germanic *aiþaz.

3. NOUN

/ oʊθ / (AmE), / əʊθ / (BrE)
a formal promise to tell the truth or to do something, especially made in a court of law or on a solemn occasion
formal promise, often legal

Examples

He took an oath to tell the truth before giving his testimony in court.
Doctors often swear an oath to uphold ethical standards before beginning their practice.
Lying under oath is considered a serious crime known as perjury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affirmation - a solemn declaration allowed to those who object to taking oaths
  • Antonym: perjury - the offense of lying under oath
  • Synonym: vow - a solemn promise, often religious or personal

Etymology

From Old English āð, related to Old Norse eiðr (oath), and German Eid (oath).

Synset POS: n
