Persia

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɜrʒə / (AmE), / ˈpɜːʃə / (BrE)
the former name for Iran, especially in reference to its history and culture before 1935
former name for Iran; historical region

Examples

The art and architecture of Persia have influenced cultures across the world.
Many classical writers referred to the empire of Persia in their works.
Trade routes through Persia connected the East and West for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iran - modern nation formerly called Persia; country in the Middle East

Etymology

From Latin 'Persia', from Ancient Greek 'Persis', from Old Persian 'Pārsa', referring to the region around modern Fars.

2. PROPN

/ ˈpɜrʒə / (AmE), / ˈpɜːʃə / (BrE)
a historic empire in southwestern Asia, founded by Cyrus the Great in the 6th century BC and conquered by Alexander the Great in the 4th century BC, known for its influential civilization and extensive territory
ancient empire in southwestern Asia

Examples

The art and architecture of Persia have influenced many later civilizations.
Cyrus the Great expanded Persia into one of the largest empires of the ancient world.
The conquest of Persia by Alexander the Great marked the end of its imperial era.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iran - modern country occupying territory once known as Persia

Etymology

From Latin 'Persia', from Ancient Greek 'Persís', ultimately from Old Persian 'Pārsa'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
