parsley

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑr.sli / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.sli / (BrE)
an aromatic herb with flat or curly green leaves that are used, especially chopped, to garnish or flavor food
a type of herb used in cooking

Examples

Chop the parsley finely to sprinkle over the cooked potatoes.
A sprig of parsley adds a fresh touch to the dish.
She grows basil and parsley in her kitchen garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cilantro - herb also known as coriander, often used as garnish or flavoring

Etymology

From Old French 'persil', from Latin 'petroselinum', from Greek 'petroselinon' (rock celery).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrsli / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsli / (BrE)
a plant with aromatic, usually curly or flat leaves, commonly used as a culinary herb or garnish
aromatic herb used in cooking

Examples

She added chopped parsley to the soup for extra flavor.
A sprig of parsley was used as a garnish on the plate.
Fresh parsley can brighten up the taste of many dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cilantro - herb with similar leaves, often used in Latin American and Asian cooking

Etymology

From Old French 'persil', from Latin 'petroselinum', from Greek 'petroselinon' (rock celery).
