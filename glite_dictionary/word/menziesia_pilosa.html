Menziesia pilosa

1. NOUN

/ mɛnˈziːziə pɪˈloʊsə / (AmE), / mɛnˈziːziə pɪˈləʊsə / (BrE)
A low shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by downy twigs; commonly known as Minnie bush.
a low shrub of the eastern US with downy twigs

Examples

Botanists identified the shrub as Menziesia pilosa growing along the forest edge.
The Menziesia pilosa thrives in moist, acidic soils of the Appalachian Mountains.
Gardeners appreciate Menziesia pilosa for its delicate flowers and hairy stems.

Etymology

Genus named after Scottish botanist Archibald Menzies, species name from Latin 'pilosa' (hairy).
