ethnarch

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛθˌnɑrk / (AmE), / ˈɛθ.nɑːk / (BrE)
the ruler or governor of a province or people, especially in the Roman and Byzantine Empires, or a religious leader holding secular authority
ruler or governor of a province or people

Examples

Makarios III was regarded as the ethnarch of Cyprus after his election as archbishop.
During the Byzantine Empire, an ethnarch would often administer both civil and religious affairs.
The title of ethnarch was sometimes granted to leaders of minority communities in the Ottoman Empire.

Synset Examples

the election of Makarios III to archbishop gave him the status of the ethnarch of Cyprus

Related Terms

  • Synonym: governor - official in charge of governing a province or region
  • Synonym: viceroy - a ruler exercising authority in a colony on behalf of a sovereign

Etymology

From Greek ethnarches, from ethnos (nation, people) + archein (to rule).
