confect

1. VERB

/ kənˈfɛkt / (AmE), / kənˈfɛkt / (BrE)
to make or construct something, especially with skill or care, often used in reference to preparing food or creating something elaborate
make or construct skillfully

Examples

The chef skillfully confected a series of delicate pastries for the dessert table.
She managed to confect a plausible excuse for being late to the meeting.
Engineers will confect a new solution to deal with the recurring traffic problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prepare - to make ready for use or consideration
  • Synonym: construct - to build or put together

Etymology

From Latin 'confectus', past participle of 'conficere' meaning 'to prepare, make, put together'.

2. VERB

/ kənˈfɛkt / (AmE), / kənˈfɛkt / (BrE)
to make or prepare something, especially a sweet food or confection
make or prepare, esp. a sweet

Examples

The chef will confect a special dessert for the event.
Local artisans still confect traditional sweets using old recipes.
The pharmacist used to confect remedies by hand in the apothecary.

Related Terms

3. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛkt / (AmE), / kənˈfɛkt / (BrE)
a rich sweet made of flavored sugar, often combined with fruit or nuts; a confection.
a sweet confection made with sugar and fruits or nuts

Examples

She placed a confect filled with almonds and honey on the dessert plate.
Each confect in the box had a different fruit flavor and texture.
Visitors sampled a traditional confect made from rosewater and pistachios at the festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confection - a sweet food made with sugar or syrup

Etymology

From Latin 'confectum', past participle of 'conficere' (to prepare, make).
