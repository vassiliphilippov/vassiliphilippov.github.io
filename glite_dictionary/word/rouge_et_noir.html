rouge et noir

1. NOUN

/ ruːʒ eɪ nwɑːr / (AmE), / ruːʒ eɪ nwɑː / (BrE)
A card game where two rows of cards are dealt and players bet on the color of the cards or which row's total will be closer to a specified number.
card game involving two rows and color betting

Examples

Many gamblers enjoyed playing rouge et noir at the casino tables.
The rules of rouge et noir made it a popular game among 18th-century French nobility.
He placed his bet on the top row in rouge et noir, hoping to win big.

Etymology

French, literally 'red and black', referring to the colors of the cards.
