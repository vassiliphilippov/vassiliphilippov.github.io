employable

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈplɔɪəbəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈplɔɪəbəl / (BrE)
(of a person) having the skills, ability, and qualifications to be able to get or hold a regular job.
able to be employed; fit for work

Examples

After completing the training course, she felt much more employable in the job market.
The government runs programs to help young people become employable.
A strong work ethic and good communication skills make candidates more employable.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unemployable - not able to be employed

Etymology

From 'employ' + '-able', dating to the early 19th century.
