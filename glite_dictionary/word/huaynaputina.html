Huaynaputina

1. PROPN

/ waɪnəpuːˈtiːnə / (AmE), / waɪnəpuːˈtiːnə / (BrE)
an inactive volcano in the Andes mountains of southern Peru, known for its significant eruption in 1600
inactive volcano in southern Peru

Examples

The eruption of Huaynaputina in 1600 was one of the largest volcanic events in South America.
Scientists study Huaynaputina to better understand the effects of volcanic activity on climate.
Many tourists hike near Huaynaputina to see the remnants of its historic eruption.

Etymology

From Quechua, meaning 'young volcano'.
