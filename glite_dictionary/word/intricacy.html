intricacy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪntrɪkəsi / (AmE), / ˈɪntrɪkəsi / (BrE)
the quality of being intricate; the state or quality of having many complex and connected parts or details
complexity; elaborate detail

Examples

The intricacy of the machine's design impressed everyone in the engineering team.
She marveled at the intricacy of the lacework on the wedding dress.
The plot's intricacy required careful attention to every detail to fully understand the story.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complexity - the state of being complex; having many parts
  • Antonym: simplicity - the quality of being simple or easy to understand

Etymology

From Latin intricatus (entangled, perplexed), from intricāre (to entangle).
