snarl

1. NOUN

/ snɑrl / (AmE), / snɑːl / (BrE)
an angry or threatening facial expression, showing hostility or aggression
angry or threatening facial expression

Examples

He greeted me with a snarl instead of a smile.
The dog bared its teeth in a threatening snarl.
Her snarl warned everyone to stay away from her desk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scowl - an angry or bad-tempered expression
  • Antonym: smile - a happy or friendly facial expression

Etymology

Early 17th century, from the verb 'snarl', imitative of a growling sound.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ snɑrl / (AmE), / snɑːl / (BrE)
to make a low, harsh sound or to move while making such a sound, like an animal showing anger or aggression
make or move with a snarling noise

Examples

The wolves began to snarl when they sensed danger nearby.
Angry engines snarled through the dark tunnel, startling the hikers.
In the silence, we heard something snarling behind the trees.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: growl - make a low, guttural sound, often expressing hostility
  • Antonym: whisper - speak very softly using one's breath without vocal cords

4. NOUN

/ snɑːrl / (AmE), / snɑːl / (BrE)
a complicated or confused situation, especially one that is difficult to unravel or understand
a confused or tangled situation

Examples

Years of bureaucratic mismanagement resulted in a financial snarl that took months to untangle.
Traffic was at a standstill because of a snarl at the main intersection downtown.
The new law is meant to simplify the legal snarl facing small business owners.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tangle - a confused mass or muddle

Etymology

6. VERB

/ snɑrl / (AmE), / snɑːl / (BrE)
to make something more complicated, confused, or tangled, especially by entangling things together
make complicated or tangled

Examples

Rush hour always tends to snarl traffic in the city center.
Careless wiring can snarl up even the most organized workspace.
Negotiations were snarled by conflicting demands from the parties involved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tangle - to twist together in a confused mass
  • Antonym: to untangle - to free from tangles or complications

Etymology

Probably from Middle English snarlen, frequentative of snar (to snare or entangle).
