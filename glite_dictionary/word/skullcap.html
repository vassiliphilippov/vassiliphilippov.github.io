/ ˈskʌlˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈskʌlˌkæp / (BrE)

2. NOUN

/ ˈskʌlˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈskʌlˌkæp / (BrE)
A small, rounded, close-fitting cap that covers the crown of the head and lacks a brim, often worn for religious or ceremonial purposes.
rounded brimless cap for the head

Examples

The rabbi placed his skullcap on his head before the ceremony.
A skullcap is often worn by clerics as part of their religious dress.
He bought a black velvet skullcap at the market.

Etymology

Formed from 'skull' + 'cap', referring to its fit on the crown of the head.

3. NOUN

/ ˈskʌlˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈskʌl.kæp / (BrE)
a herbaceous plant of the genus Scutellaria, bearing small blue or purple flowers, and having a calyx shaped like a helmet
herbaceous plant of the genus Scutellaria

Examples

The herbal tea contained dried skullcap, which is known for its calming properties.
Botanists identified the wildflower as a species of skullcap growing by the riverbank.
She planted American skullcap alongside other medicinal herbs in her garden.

Etymology

Named for the helmet-like shape of the flower's calyx, from the words 'skull' and 'cap'.
