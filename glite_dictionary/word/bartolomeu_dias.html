Bartolomeu Dias

1. PROPN

/ ˌbɑrtəloʊˈmeɪoʊ ˈdiːəs / (AmE), / ˌbɑːtələˈmeɪu ˈdiːas / (BrE)
(c. 1450–1500) A Portuguese explorer who, in 1488, was the first European to sail around the Cape of Good Hope, opening the sea route from Europe to Asia.
Portuguese explorer; first to round Cape of Good Hope

Examples

Bartolomeu Dias was the first European explorer to round the Cape of Good Hope in 1488.
Many historians credit Bartolomeu Dias with paving the way for European sea trade with Asia.
The voyage of Bartolomeu Dias marked a turning point in maritime exploration.
