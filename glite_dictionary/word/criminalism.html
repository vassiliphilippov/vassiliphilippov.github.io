criminalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪmənlˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈkrɪmɪnəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the condition or state of being a criminal; involvement in criminal behavior or activities
state of being a criminal

Examples

Sociologists have studied the roots of criminalism in urban communities for decades.
Some believe that poverty and lack of education contribute to cycles of criminalism.
The documentary explores how systemic issues can perpetuate criminalism in certain populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criminality - the state of being criminal or engaging in crime

Etymology

Formed from 'criminal' + '-ism' (a suffix denoting a state or condition).
