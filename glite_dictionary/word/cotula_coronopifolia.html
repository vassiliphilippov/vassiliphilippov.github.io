Cotula coronopifolia

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈtuːlə ˌkɔːrəˌnoʊpɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / kəˈtjuːlə ˌkɒrənəʊpɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A South African herb with golden-yellow, rounded flower heads, often grown as an ornamental and naturalized in coastal California.
a South African ornamental herb

Examples

The bright yellow flowers of Cotula coronopifolia brighten many California wetlands in the spring.
Botanists are studying how Cotula coronopifolia adapts to moist coastal environments outside its native range.
Gardeners prize Cotula coronopifolia for its ornamental value in water features and borders.

Etymology

From Latin: Cotula (genus name) + coronopifolia, referring to leaves resembling those of the plant Coronopus.
