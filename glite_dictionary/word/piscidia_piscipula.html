Piscidia piscipula

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈsɪdiə pɪˈsɪpjʊlə / (AmE), / pɪˈsɪdiə pɪˈsɪpjʊlə / (BrE)
A small tree native to the West Indies and Florida, known for its pinnate leaves, showy flowers, curly seedpods, and use as a source of fish poison.
tropical tree, known as Jamaican dogwood

Examples

The bark of Piscidia piscipula has been traditionally used to stun fish in Caribbean waters.
A Piscidia piscipula tree produces beautiful clusters of purple to white flowers each spring.
Scientists are researching the medicinal properties of chemicals found in Piscidia piscipula.

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Piscidia', from Latin 'piscis' (fish), relating to its traditional use in fishing; specific epithet 'piscipula' is diminutive of 'piscis'.
