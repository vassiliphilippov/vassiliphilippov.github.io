identical

1. ADJ

/ aɪˈdɛntɪkəl / (AmE), / aɪˈdɛntɪkəl / (BrE)
exactly alike in every detail; having properties that are the same in all respects
exactly alike; matching in every detail

Examples

The two documents are identical in content and format.
After the renovation, the rooms looked almost identical to one another.
The twins wore identical outfits to school.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: different - not the same; unlike in nature, form, or quality
  • Degree Lower: similar - resembling without being identical; having resemblance in appearance or character

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'identicus', from Latin 'idem' (the same).

2. ADJ

/ aɪˈdɛntɪkəl / (AmE), / aɪˈdɛntɪkəl / (BrE)
(of twins) developed from a single fertilized egg and therefore genetically identical; monozygotic.
(of twins) from a single egg
medical

Examples

Identical twins share the same genetic makeup because they come from a single egg.
The doctor explained that identical twins are always the same sex.
Scientists study identical twins to understand how genetics influence behavior.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fraternal - (of twins) developed from two separate eggs; dizygotic

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'identicus', from Latin 'idem' (the same).
