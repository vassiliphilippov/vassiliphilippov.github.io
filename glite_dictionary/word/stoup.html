stoup

1. NOUN

/ stuːp / (AmE), / stuːp / (BrE)
an old-fashioned drinking vessel, typically a cup or tankard, used in former times
archaic drinking vessel
archaic

Examples

He raised the stoup to his lips, savoring the strong ale.
The ancient tavern displayed a battered stoup among its relics.
Shakespeare's characters often called for a stoup of wine in their revelry.

Etymology

From Middle English *stowpe*, probably from Old Norse *staup* (drinking cup).

2. NOUN

/ stuːp / (AmE), / stuːp / (BrE)
A basin, usually at the entrance of a church, containing holy water for worshippers to dip their fingers in.
basin for holy water in a church

Examples

The old church had a marble stoup by the entrance, filled with holy water.
He dipped his fingers into the stoup and crossed himself before entering the sanctuary.
During renovations, the ancient stoup was carefully restored to its original condition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: font - large basin for holding water for baptism in a church

Etymology

From Old French *estoupe*, likely of Germanic origin.
