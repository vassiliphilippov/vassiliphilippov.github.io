Auriculariales

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˌrɪkjəˌlɛrˈiːəˌliːz / (AmE), / ɔːˌrɪkjʊəˌlɪəˈliːz / (BrE)
An order of fungi that is coextensive with the family Auriculariaceae, sometimes included in the order Tremellales.
an order of fungi

Examples

The Auriculariales include several species of edible jelly fungi.
Scientists studied the evolutionary relationships within the Auriculariales using genetic analysis.
Members of the Auriculariales are often found growing on decaying wood in damp forests.

Etymology

From New Latin Auricularia (a genus name) + -ales (denoting orders in taxonomy).
