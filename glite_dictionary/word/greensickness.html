greensickness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡriːnˌsɪk.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnˌsɪk.nəs / (BrE)
an old term for a form of anemia in young women, marked by weakness, menstrual problems, and a pale or greenish skin tint
archaic term for anemia in young women
archaic medical

Examples

The symptoms of greensickness were often misunderstood by physicians in the 18th century.
In Victorian novels, young ladies sometimes fainted due to greensickness.
Medical texts once described greensickness as a malady of unmarried women.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anemia - a medical condition marked by a deficiency of red blood cells or hemoglobin, causing weakness and pallor

Etymology

From Middle English, literally 'green' + 'sickness', first used in the 16th century to describe a form of anemia.
