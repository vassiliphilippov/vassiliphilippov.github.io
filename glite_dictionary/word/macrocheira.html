Macrocheira

1. NOUN

/ ˌmækroʊˈkaɪrə / (AmE), / ˌmakrəʊˈkʌɪrə / (BrE)
A genus of giant crabs native to Japanese waters, including the Japanese spider crab, which has the largest leg span of any arthropod.
genus of giant Japanese crabs

Examples

The Macrocheira can reach a leg span of up to 4 meters, making it the largest crab in the world.
Scientists study Macrocheira to understand more about deep-sea adaptation.
A newly discovered specimen of Macrocheira was displayed at the marine research center.

Etymology

From Greek makros (large) + cheir (hand, claw).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmækroʊˈkaɪrə / (AmE), / ˌmakrəʊˈkʌɪrə / (BrE)
a genus of giant spider crabs native to the waters of Japan, including the Japanese spider crab
genus of giant Japanese spider crabs

Examples

The largest member of the genus Macrocheira is the Japanese spider crab, known for its long legs.
Marine biologists study Macrocheira to understand the growth patterns of deep-sea crustaceans.
The aquarium features a rare specimen from the genus Macrocheira on display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Macrocheira kaempferi - Japanese spider crab, largest member of genus Macrocheira

Etymology

From Greek 'makros' (large) and 'cheir' (hand), referring to the crab's large claws.
