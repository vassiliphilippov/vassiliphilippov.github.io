recursive

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈkɝːsɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈkɜːsɪv / (BrE)
relating to or involving the process of recursion, in which a procedure, definition, or algorithm refers to itself or is applied repeatedly
involving recursion or self-reference

Examples

The function is recursive, calling itself with a smaller input each time.
A recursive definition explains a term in terms of itself.
Many computer algorithms use recursive methods to solve complex problems.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: iterative - repeatedly applying a process without self-reference

Etymology

From Latin 'recurrere' (to run back), via recursion + -ive.
