dimorphic

1. ADJ

/ daɪˈmɔɹ.fɪk / (AmE), / daɪˈmɔː.fɪk / (BrE)
existing or occurring in two distinct forms, especially in reference to individuals of the same species or to crystals.
existing in two distinct forms

Examples

In some bird species, males and females are dimorphic, with distinct coloration or size differences.
The fungus exhibits a dimorphic life cycle, alternating between yeast and mold forms.
Mineralogists are studying dimorphic crystals that can grow in two different shapes under varying conditions.

Synset Examples

dimorphic crystals
dimorphous organisms

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: polymorphic - existing in many different forms
  • Degree Lower: monomorphic - existing in only one form

Etymology

From Greek 'di-' (two) + 'morphē' (form).
