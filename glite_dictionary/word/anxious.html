anxious

1. ADJ

/ ˈæŋkʃəs / (AmE), / ˈaŋkʃəs / (BrE)
very eager or desirous to do or have something, sometimes to the point of impatience; strongly wishing for something to happen.
eager or strongly desirous

Examples

She was anxious to meet her favorite author at the signing event.
The students were anxious to find out their exam results.
He's anxious for the concert to start, checking his watch every few minutes.

Synset Examples

anxious to see the new show at the museum
dying to hear who won

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eager - keenly or impatiently wanting to do or have something

Etymology

From Latin anxius (troubled in mind, uneasy), from angere (to choke, distress).

2. ADJ

/ ˈæŋkʃəs / (AmE), / ˈæŋkʃəs / (BrE)
feeling or showing worry, nervousness, or unease about something with an uncertain outcome
experiencing or showing worry or nervousness

Examples

She spent an anxious hour waiting for her son to call.
He cast anxious glances at the clock as the deadline approached.
Everyone became anxious when the storm clouds gathered overhead.

Synset Examples

spent an anxious night waiting for the test results
cast anxious glances behind her
those nervous moments before takeoff
an unquiet mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nervous - easily agitated or alarmed; tending to be anxious or highly strung
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions

Etymology

From Latin anxius (troubled, uneasy), from angere (to choke, squeeze).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
