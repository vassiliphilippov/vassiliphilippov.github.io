chromite

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊˌmaɪt / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊˌmaɪt / (BrE)
A brownish-black mineral that is the principal ore of chromium.
a mineral; main source of chromium

Examples

The geologist collected samples of chromite to analyze their chromium content.
South Africa has some of the largest chromite deposits in the world.
The extraction of chromium metal begins with mining chromite.

Etymology

From Greek *chrōma* (color), referring to its use as a source of color in compounds.
