Oxalis acetosella

1. NOUN

/ ɑkˈsælɪs ˌæsɪˈtosələ / (AmE), / ɒkˈsælɪs ˌæsɪˈtəʊsɪlə / (BrE)
A small Eurasian woodland plant with heart-shaped, three-part leaves and white flowers marked with purple veins; commonly known as wood sorrel.
Eurasian plant with trifoliate leaves and white, purple-veined flowers

Examples

The shady forest floor was covered with the delicate leaves of Oxalis acetosella.
Children sometimes nibble the tart leaves of Oxalis acetosella during nature walks.
Botanists identified the white, purple-veined blooms as belonging to Oxalis acetosella.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood sorrel - Common English name for Oxalis acetosella

Etymology

From Latin: oxalis (sorrel) + acetosella (a type of sorrel, from 'acetum', meaning vinegar, for the sour taste).
