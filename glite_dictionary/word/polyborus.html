Polyborus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈbɔrəs / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈbɔːrəs / (BrE)
a genus of birds in the family Falconidae, including the typical caracaras or long-winged hawks
genus of birds; typical caracaras

Examples

The South American Polyborus is known for its aggressive scavenging behavior.
The crested caracara, classified as Polyborus plancus, inhabits open and semi-open areas.
Taxonomists debate the precise species included in the genus Polyborus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caracara - a bird of prey in the family Falconidae, closely related to Polyborus

Etymology

From Greek 'polys' (much, many) + 'boros' (voracious), referring to the birds' feeding habits.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈbɔːrəs / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈbɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Falconidae, commonly known as caracaras, native to the Americas.
genus of caracara birds

Examples

The crested Polyborus is often seen scavenging in open grasslands.
Scientists have observed distinct behaviors in members of the Polyborus genus.
The distribution of Polyborus species extends throughout Central and South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Caracara - another genus of birds of prey closely related to Polyborus

Etymology

From Greek polus (many) + boros (ravenous), referring to their feeding habits.
