domine

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːməˌneɪ / (AmE), / ˈdɒmɪni / (BrE)
(archaic) a clergyman, especially a minister or parson in a Christian church.
archaic term for clergyman or minister
archaic

Examples

The villagers sought advice from the wise old domine who lived near the church.
In many stories of old Scotland, a domine was the central figure in the parish.
The young boy was inspired by the sermons of the domine every Sunday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parson - a beneficed member of the clergy; a minister in charge of a parish
  • Synonym: minister - a member of the clergy, especially in Protestant churches

Etymology

From Latin 'domine' (vocative of 'dominus', meaning 'lord' or 'master'), used as a title for clergymen.
