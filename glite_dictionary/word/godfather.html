godfather

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑdˌfɑðɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡɒdˌfɑːðə / (BrE)
a man who sponsors a child at baptism and undertakes to help guide their religious upbringing
male baptismal sponsor

Examples

Michael was proud to be asked to serve as godfather to his niece.
At the christening, her godfather made a heartfelt promise to support her throughout her life.
They invited their oldest friend to be the baby's godfather.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: godmother - a woman who sponsors a child at baptism

Etymology

From Old English 'godfæder', literally 'god' + 'father'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑdˌfɑðɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡɒdˌfɑːðə / (BrE)
A man who acts as a sponsor or mentor, especially one who has a relationship similar to that of a male sponsor to his godchild.
male sponsor or mentor

Examples

He has always considered Mr. Smith his godfather in the business world.
As a political godfather, she guided many young leaders to success.
The artist acknowledged his godfather for inspiring his creative journey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mentor - an experienced and trusted adviser

Etymology

From Old English 'godfæder', meaning 'spiritual father', literally 'god' + 'father'.

Synset POS: n
