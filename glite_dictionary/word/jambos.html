jambos

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæmˌboʊs / (AmE), / ˈdʒambɒs / (BrE)
a tropical tree (Eugenia jambos) of the myrtle family, cultivated for its rose-scented edible fruit, also known as the rose apple.
tropical tree; rose apple

Examples

The jambos tree grows well in warm, humid climates.
Farmers cultivate jambos for its crisp, aromatic fruit.
The flowers of the jambos are fragrant and attract many bees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rose apple - a tropical fruit from Eugenia jambos, or the tree that bears it

Etymology

From Portuguese 'jambos', from Malay 'jambu' (guava or rose apple).
