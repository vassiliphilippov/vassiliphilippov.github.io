stonecress

1. NOUN

/ ˈstoʊn.krɛs / (AmE), / ˈstəʊn.krɛs / (BrE)
any of several small herbs of the genus Aethionema, native to sunny limestone habitats in the Old World.
a herbaceous plant of the genus Aethionema

Examples

The rocky hillside was dotted with clusters of stonecress in full bloom.
Gardeners value stonecress for its delicate pink flowers and ability to thrive in poor, stony soil.
Botanists discovered a rare variety of stonecress growing near the ancient ruins.

Etymology

From 'stone' + 'cress', referring to its habitat and resemblance to cress plants.
