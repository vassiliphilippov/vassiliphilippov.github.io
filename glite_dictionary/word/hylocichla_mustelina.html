Hylocichla mustelina

1. NOUN

/ haɪ.loʊˈsɪk.lə ˌmʌs.təˈlaɪ.nə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪ.lə(ʊ)ˈkɪk.lə ˌmʌs.tɪˈliː.nə / (BrE)
a large North American thrush with a reddish-brown back, spotted breast, and a distinctive, melodious song, found in eastern American woodlands
North American woodland thrush

Examples

Birdwatchers delight in hearing the Hylocichla mustelina during early morning walks in the forest.
The Hylocichla mustelina is experiencing population decline due to habitat loss.
One of the best bird songs in North America is attributed to Hylocichla mustelina.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood thrush - common name for Hylocichla mustelina

Etymology

From Greek hylē (wood) + sikhlē (thrush) and Latin mustelinus (weasel-like, from appearance or color).
