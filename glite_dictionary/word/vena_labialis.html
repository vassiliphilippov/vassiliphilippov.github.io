vena labialis

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪ.nə læˈbeɪ.li.əs / (AmE), / ˈviː.nə læˈbeɪ.li.æs / (BrE)
a vein that drains blood from the lips of the mouth
vein draining the lips
medical

Examples

The vena labialis can be observed during certain surgical procedures involving the lips.
Swelling of the vena labialis may indicate an underlying circulatory issue.
An injury to the vena labialis can cause excessive bleeding of the lip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: labial vein - vein of the lips

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein) and 'labialis' (of the lip).

vein draining vulvar lips
medical

Examples

The vena labialis plays a role in draining blood from the external genitalia.
Inflammation of the vena labialis can cause local swelling and discomfort.
A detailed anatomical study was conducted on the vena labialis in human specimens.
