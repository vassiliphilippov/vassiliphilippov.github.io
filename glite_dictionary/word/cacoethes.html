cacoethes

1. NOUN

/ kækoʊˈiːθiːz / (AmE), / kakəʊˈɛθiːz / (BrE)
An irresistible and usually irrational urge or impulse to do something, especially something inadvisable or strange.
an uncontrollable urge or impulse

Examples

He struggled daily with a cacoethes for gambling, unable to resist the lure of the casino.
Writers sometimes confess to a cacoethes for putting their thoughts on paper, regardless of the hour.
Her cacoethes for adventure led her to embark on reckless journeys around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mania - an excessive enthusiasm or desire; an obsession
  • Synonym: compulsion - the action or state of forcing or being forced to do something; an irresistible urge
  • Synonym: impulse - a sudden strong and unreflective urge or desire to act

Etymology

From Greek kakoēthes (bad habit), from kakos (bad) + ēthos (habit).
