Sir Geraint

1. PROPN

/ sɜr dʒəˈraɪnt / (AmE), / sə dʒəˈraɪnt / (BrE)
A knight of the Round Table in Arthurian legend, noted for his courage and loyalty.
Arthurian knight of the Round Table

Examples

In the ancient tales, Sir Geraint undertakes a perilous quest to defend his wife's honor.
Many scholars believe the story of Sir Geraint reflects values of medieval chivalry and romance.
The poems of Alfred, Lord Tennyson, often feature Sir Geraint as a brave and faithful knight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sir Lancelot - A prominent knight of the Round Table famed for his prowess
