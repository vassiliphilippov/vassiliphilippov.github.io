chevalier

1. NOUN

/ ʃəˈvæl.jɪr / (AmE), / ˌʃɛ.vəˈlɪə / (BrE)
a gallant or courtly gentleman, especially one who acts with chivalry
gallant or courtly gentleman

Examples

He was the very image of a chevalier, charming and impeccably mannered.
At the ball, she was escorted by a chevalier who captivated everyone with his grace.
Stories of the chevalier's bravery spread throughout the kingdom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knight - a man awarded a nonhereditary title, often for service or chivalry
  • Antonym: churl - a rude, boorish or surly person; the opposite of a gentleman

Etymology

From French 'chevalier' (knight), from Late Latin 'caballarius' (horseman).
