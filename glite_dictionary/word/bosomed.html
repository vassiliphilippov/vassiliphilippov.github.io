bosomed

1. ADJ

/ ˈboʊzəmd / (AmE), / ˈbəʊzəmd / (BrE)
having a bosom of a specified kind or having something resembling a bosom; chiefly used in compounds and literary contexts.
having a specified bosom, often in compounds

Examples

She portrayed a full-bosomed heroine in the historical drama.
The valley was described as a green-bosomed haven by the poet.
In Victorian art, ample-bosomed figures were a common motif.

Synset Examples

full-bosomed women
the green-bosomed earth

Etymology

From 'bosom' + '-ed' suffix, forming an adjective.
