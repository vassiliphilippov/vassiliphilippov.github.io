Morus nigra

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔɹəs ˈnaɪɡɹə / (AmE), / ˈmɔːrəs ˈnɪɡrə / (BrE)
A species of mulberry tree native to western Asia, known for its dark leaves and blackish fruit.
species of mulberry tree with black fruit

Examples

The Morus nigra tree produces sweet, dark-colored mulberries in late summer.
Botanists planted Morus nigra in the arboretum to compare its fruit with other mulberry species.
The leaves of Morus nigra are broad and deeply veined, adding beauty to any garden.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: white mulberry - a species of mulberry tree with pale fruit
  • Synonym: red mulberry - a North American species of mulberry with red to purple fruit

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'black mulberry'.
