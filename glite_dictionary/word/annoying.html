annoying

1. ADJ

/ əˈnɔɪ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈnɔɪ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
causing irritation or making someone feel bothered or disturbed
causing irritation or bother

Examples

The constant buzzing of the mosquito was extremely annoying while I was trying to sleep.
She found his habit of interrupting conversations very annoying.
It is annoying when people use their phones during a movie.

Synset Examples

tapping an annoying rhythm on his glass with his fork

swarms of pestering gnats

a vexatious child
it is vexing to have to admit you are wrong

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritating - causing annoyance or mild anger
  • Degree Higher: enraging - causing intense anger
  • Antonym: boring - not interesting; dull

Etymology

From the verb 'annoy,' which comes from Old French 'anoier', from Late Latin 'inodiare' (to make loathsome).
