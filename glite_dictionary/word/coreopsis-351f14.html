Coreopsis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːr.iˈɑːp.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌkɒr.iˈɒp.sɪs / (BrE)
A genus of American flowering plants in the daisy family, widely cultivated for their bright, showy flowers.
genus of American flowering plants

Examples

Coreopsis is often grown in gardens for its vibrant yellow blooms.
Several species of Coreopsis are native to North America.
The borders of the park were lined with colorful Coreopsis flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tickseed - common name for plants of the genus Coreopsis

Etymology

From Greek 'koris' (bug) and 'opsis' (appearance), referring to the resemblance of the seeds to bugs.
