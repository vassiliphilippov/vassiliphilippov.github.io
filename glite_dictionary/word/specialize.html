to specialize

1. VERB

/ ˈspɛʃəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
to become an expert in a specific area of activity or field of study, concentrating one's efforts on a particular subject
focus on a specific field or activity

Examples

After medical school, she decided to specialize in cardiology.
Many law firms specialize in intellectual property cases.
As the tech industry grew, more companies began to specialize in cybersecurity solutions.

Synset Examples

She specializes in Near Eastern history

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to generalize - to make less specific or more general

Etymology

From Medieval Latin specializare, from Latin specialis (specific, particular).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00438402-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈspɛʃəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
(of an organism or species) to evolve or develop characteristics that make it particularly suited to a specific environment or way of life, sometimes resulting in the formation of a new species.
evolve to suit a specific environment

Examples

Over millions of years, certain birds specialized to survive in desert habitats.
The island's isolated population began to specialize, eventually forming a new species.
Amphibians often specialize to exploit specific ecological niches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adapt - change or adjust to fit an environment or new conditions

Etymology

From Medieval Latin specializare (to render special), from Latin specialis (special).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00119074-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈspɛʃəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
to state or describe something in detail; to specify or make more specific
to make or state more specific

Examples

Before we proceed, could you specialize which aspect of the project concerns you most?
The report did not specialize the reasons for the delay, leaving the issue unclear.
Can you specialize your feedback on the proposal to help us make improvements?

Synset Examples

Could you please specify your criticism of my paper?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to specify - to state explicitly or in detail

Etymology

From Medieval Latin specializare, from Latin specialis (special).

5. VERB

/ ˈspɛʃəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
to focus on a particular area of study, work, or activity; to devote oneself or one's efforts to a specific subject or skill
focus on a particular area of work or study

Examples

Dr. Lee specializes in pediatric medicine at the local hospital.
Our law firm specializes in environmental issues and litigation.
After several years, she specialized in French cuisine and became a renowned chef.

Synset Examples

This baker specializes in French bread

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concentrate - to focus all one's attention or efforts on a particular task or subject
  • Antonym: to generalize - to make less specialized; to consider in a more general way

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'specializare' (to make special), from Latin 'specialis' (special).
