soundboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaʊndˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈsaʊndˌbɔːd / (BrE)
A thin, typically wooden board in a musical instrument that vibrates to amplify and enrich the sound produced by the instrument.
vibrating board in an instrument for sound amplification

Examples

The piano's soundboard is crucial for producing its rich, resonant tone.
A crack in the soundboard can greatly affect the quality of a guitar's sound.
Instrument makers carefully select the wood for a violin's soundboard to achieve the desired acoustics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resonator - a device or part that increases and enriches sound vibrations
