privateness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪvət.nəs / (AmE), / ˈpraɪvət.nɪs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being secluded from the presence or view of others; the condition of being private
state of being private or secluded

Examples

He cherished the privateness of his study, where he could think without interruptions.
The lush gardens offered an appealing sense of privateness in the heart of the city.
She valued the privateness of her new apartment overlooking the quiet street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: privacy - the state of being free from public attention or unsanctioned intrusion

Etymology

Formed from 'private' + '-ness' (noun-forming suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪvət.nəs / (AmE), / ˈpraɪvət.nɪs / (BrE)
the state or condition of being concealed, hidden, or not open to observation or access; privacy
state of being hidden or private

Examples

She cherished the privateness of her secluded garden, away from prying eyes.
The privateness of the meeting was ensured by locking all the doors.
Journals were once treasured for the privateness they granted to one's innermost thoughts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: privacy - the state of being free from public attention
  • Antonym: publicity - the condition of being in public view or widely known

Etymology

From private + -ness, following standard noun formation in English.

Synset POS: n
