oldwife

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊldˌwaɪf / (AmE), / ˈəʊldˌwaɪf / (BrE)
a species of tropical Atlantic fish, Lagodon rhomboides, known for its laterally compressed body and strong dorsal spines.
tropical Atlantic fish species

Examples

The fisherman caught an oldwife while trawling off the Caribbean coast.
Snorkelers in the Bahamas often spot the brightly colored oldwife among coral reefs.
Many local recipes highlight the firm flesh of the oldwife for grilling or baking.

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊldˌwaɪf / (AmE), / ˈəʊldˌwaɪf / (BrE)
a species of long-tailed sea duck (Clangula hyemalis) commonly found in the northern United States, also known as the long-tailed duck
long-tailed sea duck of North America

Examples

The oldwife is easily recognized by its long tail and striking plumage.
Flocks of oldwives migrate southward as winter approaches.
Birdwatchers spotted an oldwife feeding near the rocky shoreline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long-tailed duck - another name for the oldwife, Clangula hyemalis
