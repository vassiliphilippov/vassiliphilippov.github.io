to reprize

1. VERB

/ rɪˈpraɪz / (AmE), / rɪˈprʌɪz / (BrE)
to repeat an earlier theme or section of a musical or literary composition
repeat a theme in music or literature

Examples

The composer decided to reprize the opening melody in the final movement.
The novel reprizes its central motif in the concluding chapters.
During the concert, the orchestra reprized the main theme for dramatic effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repeat - to do or say something again
  • Synonym: to reprise - to repeat a part of a musical composition or literary work

Etymology

From French 'reprise' (a taking up again), from Old French 'reprendre' (to take back), from Latin 'reprendere'.
