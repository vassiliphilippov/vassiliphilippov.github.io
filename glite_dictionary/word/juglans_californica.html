Juglans californica

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒʌɡlænz ˌkæləˈfɔrnɪkə / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌɡlænz ˌkælɪˈfɔːnɪkə / (BrE)
A medium-sized tree native to California, with aromatic compound leaves and edible nuts, commonly known as the California black walnut.
California walnut tree species

Examples

The Juglans californica is an important native tree species in southern California woodlands.
Botanists studied the leaf structure of Juglans californica to understand its adaptation to dry climates.
Edible nuts produced by Juglans californica are a food source for local wildlife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: California black walnut - Common name for Juglans californica, a walnut species native to California

Etymology

From Latin: 'Juglans' meaning walnut, and 'californica' indicating its origin in California.
