to reseat

1. VERB

/ riˈsit / (AmE), / riːˈsiːt / (BrE)
to show or move someone to a different seat
show to a different seat

Examples

The theater staff had to reseat all the guests after the spillage in the front row.
After checking our tickets, the usher decided to reseat us closer to the stage.
Due to a last-minute change in the seating plan, we were kindly reseated by the attendant.

Synset Examples

The usher insisted on reseating us

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: seat - to show to a seat or cause to be seated

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 're-' (again) + 'seat'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01547340-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ riˈsit / (AmE), / riˈsiːt / (BrE)
to provide with a new seat or seating surface
to provide with a new seat

Examples

The carpenter will reseat the antique chair with new fabric.
We had to reseat the cushion because it was torn.
After years of use, the stool was finally reseated by a professional upholsterer.

Synset Examples

reseat the old broken chair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to replace - to put something new in the place of something old

Etymology

From re- (again) + seat (to install a seat or seating).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02339454-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ riˈsit / (AmE), / riːˈsiːt / (BrE)
to provide (a place, such as a hall or theater) with new seats; to install new seating
provide with new seats

Examples

The city plans to reseat the old opera house next year.
After decades of use, it was necessary to reseat the entire stadium.
The renovation project includes funds to reseat the main auditorium with modern chairs.

Synset Examples

reseat Carnegie Hall

Etymology

From re- (again) + seat (to furnish with seats)

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02339169-v
Synset POS: v
