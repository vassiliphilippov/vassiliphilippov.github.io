statin

1. NOUN

/ ˈstætɪn / (AmE), / ˈstætɪn / (BrE)
any of a class of drugs that lower cholesterol levels in the blood by inhibiting the enzyme HMG-CoA reductase
cholesterol-lowering drug
medical

Examples

My doctor prescribed a statin to help control my cholesterol levels.
Some patients experience muscle pain after starting a statin.
Research shows that taking a statin can reduce the risk of heart attacks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cholesterol-lowering drug - a medication used to decrease blood cholesterol

Etymology

From the suffix -stat (meaning 'inhibiting') + -in (used in naming proteins and drugs).
