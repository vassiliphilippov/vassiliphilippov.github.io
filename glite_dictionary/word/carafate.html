Carafate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkærəˌfeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkærəˌfeɪt / (BrE)
A trademarked medicine in tablet form used to treat peptic ulcers by forming a protective coating over the ulcer site.
medicine for peptic ulcers
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Carafate to help heal her stomach ulcer.
If you take Carafate on an empty stomach, it works more effectively.
Some patients experience constipation when using Carafate for ulcer treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sucralfate - the generic name for Carafate, a medication for ulcers
