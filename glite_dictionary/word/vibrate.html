to vibrate

1. VERB

/ ˈvaɪˌbreɪt / (AmE), / ˈvaɪ.breɪt / (BrE)
to move or swing back and forth or from side to side in a regular, rapid motion
move back and forth rapidly

Examples

When the phone is on silent, it will vibrate to alert you of new messages.
The bridge began to vibrate slightly as the heavy truck drove across it.
If you pluck the guitar string, you can see it vibrate from side to side.

Synset Examples

the needle on the meter was oscillating

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to oscillate - to move or swing back and forth at a regular speed
  • Synonym: quiver - to shake with a slight, rapid motion

Etymology

From Latin 'vibrare' (to shake, move to and fro).

2. VERB

/ ˈvaɪˌbreɪt / (AmE), / ˈvaɪ.breɪt / (BrE)
to move rapidly back and forth with small, quick movements, typically in an uncontrolled or continuous way
move quickly back and forth; shake

Examples

The alarm on my phone began to vibrate loudly on the nightstand.
Her voice seemed to vibrate with excitement as she shared the news.
You could feel the walls vibrate during the thunderstorm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quiver - move with slight rapid motions
  • Synonym: shake - move irregularly or quickly back and forth

Etymology

From Latin 'vibrare' (to shake, brandish).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01895242-v
Synset POS: v
