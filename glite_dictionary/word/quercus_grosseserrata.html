Quercus grosseserrata

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs ɡroʊsˌɛrəˈrɑːtə / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs ɡrɒsˌɛrəˈrɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of oak native to Japan, known for its moderately light, fine-grained wood.
Japanese oak species

Examples

The forest in northern Japan is home to the Quercus grosseserrata among other native trees.
Timber from the Quercus grosseserrata is prized for its fine grain and durability.
Researchers are studying how Quercus grosseserrata adapts to changes in the climate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a tree of the genus Quercus, bearing acorns

Etymology

From Latin: 'Quercus' meaning 'oak' and 'grosseserrata' meaning 'coarsely serrated'.
