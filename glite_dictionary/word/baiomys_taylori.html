Baiomys taylori

1. NOUN

/ baɪˈoʊmɪs ˈteɪlɔri / (AmE), / baɪˈəʊmɪs ˈteɪlɔːri / (BrE)
A very small dark greyish brown mouse resembling a house mouse, native to Texas and Mexico; also known as the northern pygmy mouse.
species of tiny mouse from Texas and Mexico

Examples

The Baiomys taylori can be found in grasslands and scrubland areas of southern Texas.
Researchers studied the behavior of the Baiomys taylori in its natural habitat.
Unlike some other rodents, Baiomys taylori is active both day and night.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: mouse - small rodent with a pointed snout and long tail

Etymology

From the genus name Baiomys and species epithet taylori, named in honor of naturalist William Taylor.
