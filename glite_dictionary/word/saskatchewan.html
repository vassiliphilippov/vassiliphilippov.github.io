Saskatchewan

1. PROPN

/ səˈskætʃəwɑn / (AmE), / səˈskatʃəwən / (BrE)
a province in west central Canada, one of the three Prairie Provinces, known for its vast farmland and prairies
a Canadian prairie province

Examples

Every summer, tourists travel to Saskatchewan to see its beautiful landscapes and open skies.
The majority of Canada's lentils are grown in Saskatchewan due to its fertile soil.
After moving from Toronto, she settled in a small town in Saskatchewan.

Synset Examples

vast fields of wheat grow on Saskatchewan's prairies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Manitoba - a Canadian prairie province east of Saskatchewan
  • Synonym: Alberta - a Canadian prairie province west of Saskatchewan

Etymology

From the Saskatchewan River, which derives its name from the Cree word kisiskāciwani-sīpiy meaning 'swift-flowing river'.
