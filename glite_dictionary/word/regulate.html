to regulate

1. VERB

/ ˈrɛɡjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to control or adjust the time, amount, degree, or rate of something
control or adjust (time, amount, degree, etc.)

Examples

Engineers constantly regulate the airflow to ensure safety in the laboratory.
Parents may need to regulate their children's screen time for healthy development.
The thermostat automatically regulates the temperature in the house throughout the day.

Synset Examples

regulate the temperature
modulate the pitch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: control - to exercise restraint or direction over; to command
  • Synonym: to adjust - to change something slightly in order to make it better or more suitable
  • Antonym: to deregulate - to remove regulations or restrictions from something

Etymology

From Latin 'regulatus', past participle of 'regulare' (to control by rule).

2. VERB

/ ˈrɛɡjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to control or restrain the emission or release of something, such as sound, gas, fluid, etc.
control the emission of something

Examples

Authorities have taken steps to regulate the release of harmful chemicals into the river.
The device is designed to regulate the flow of water in the pipes.
New rules require factories to regulate their noise emissions at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: control - to exercise restraint or direction over something
  • Antonym: release - to allow something to flow out or be emitted

Etymology

From Latin 'regulare', from 'regula' (rule).

3. VERB

/ ˈrɛɡjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to control or direct something so that it develops or behaves in a particular way
to control or give direction to

Examples

The government aims to regulate the financial markets to prevent fraud and abuse.
Parents often regulate their children’s screen time to ensure a healthy balance.
A teacher’s role is to regulate classroom behavior for a positive learning environment.

Synset Examples

experience often determines ability
mold public opinion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: control - to exercise restraint or direction over; dominate
  • Synonym: direct - to cause to move in a particular direction or influence development
  • Synonym: influence - to have an effect on the character or behavior of someone or something

Etymology

From Latin 'regulare' (to control, rule), from 'regula' (rule).

4. VERB

/ ˈrɛɡjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to control something by means of rules or laws; to make something operate according to a system or standard
control by rules or laws

Examples

The government plans to regulate the use of pesticides to protect the environment.
Schools often regulate students' behavior through a strict code of conduct.
It's important to regulate financial markets to prevent abuse and ensure stability.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to deregulate - to remove regulations or rules from something
  • Synonym: control - to exercise power over something or someone
