Codiaeum variegatum

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈdiː.i.əm ˌvær.iˈɡeɪ.təm / (AmE), / kəʊˈdiː.i.əm ˌvær.iˈɡeɪ.təm / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub grown in many varieties for its brightly colored, variegated foliage, commonly cultivated as a houseplant and ornamental plant
evergreen shrub with colorful foliage, grown as houseplant

Examples

Many people choose Codiaeum variegatum for its vibrant, multicolored leaves to brighten their living rooms.
She placed a potted Codiaeum variegatum near the window to make the most of the sunlight.
Gardeners appreciate Codiaeum variegatum for its tolerance of indoor conditions and striking appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: croton - common name for Codiaeum variegatum, an ornamental plant with brightly colored leaves

Etymology

From genus name Codiaeum (from Greek kodeia, meaning 'head of poppy') plus Latin variegatum, meaning 'variegated' or 'having different colors.'
