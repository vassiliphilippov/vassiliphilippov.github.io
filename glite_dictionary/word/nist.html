NIST

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪst / (AmE), / ˈnɪst / (BrE)
the National Institute of Standards and Technology, a U.S. government agency that develops measurement standards and provides guidance for industry and government programs
U.S. agency for standards and measurement

Examples

NIST develops standards that are used in a wide range of industries across the United States.
The cybersecurity framework created by NIST is widely adopted by companies to enhance their defenses.
Government agencies often rely on NIST guidelines when implementing new technologies.

Etymology

Acronym of National Institute of Standards and Technology, established in 1988 (previously National Bureau of Standards).
