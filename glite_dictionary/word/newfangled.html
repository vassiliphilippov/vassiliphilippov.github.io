newfangled

1. ADJ

/ ˈnuˌfæŋɡəld / (AmE), / ˈnjuːˌfæŋɡəld / (BrE)
recently invented or introduced, often regarded as unnecessarily or suspiciously modern
unnecessarily or suspiciously modern

Examples

He refuses to use any newfangled technology in his kitchen.
Many people are skeptical of newfangled health trends that promise quick results.
My grandmother doesn't trust these newfangled gadgets and prefers traditional tools.

Synset Examples

newfangled ideas
she buys all these new-fangled machines and never uses them

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times
  • Antonym: old-fashioned - not modern; belonging to or typical of a time in the past

Etymology

From Middle English 'newfangel', from 'new' + 'fangel' (inclined to take), influenced by Old English 'fangol' (inclined to take, grasping).

2. ADJ

/ ˈnuːˌfæŋɡəld / (AmE), / ˈnjuːˌfæŋɡəld / (BrE)
(often disapproving) recently invented or introduced and regarded as unnecessary or overly modern.
recently invented, needlessly novel

Examples

Many people are suspicious of newfangled technology until it becomes commonplace.
My grandmother always complains about newfangled kitchen gadgets cluttering the shelves.
He refused to download the newfangled app, preferring his old flip phone.

Synset Examples

newfangled ideas
she buys all these new-fangled machines and never uses them

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times, as opposed to the remote past
  • Antonym: old-fashioned - not modern; belonging to a former or past era

Etymology

From Middle English 'newfanglyd', from 'new' + 'fangled' (from 'fang', an old word for 'to take').

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
