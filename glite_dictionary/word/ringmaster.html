ringmaster

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪŋˌmæstər / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋˌmɑːstə / (BrE)
the person who directs and announces the performances in a circus ring
circus show director

Examples

The ringmaster welcomed the excited crowd as the circus show began.
As the ringmaster cracked his whip, the acrobats entered the ring with a flourish.
Her dream was to become a famous ringmaster and run her own circus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emcee - a person who introduces performers at a show
  • Degree Lower: performer - someone who entertains an audience

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪŋˌmæstər / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋˌmɑːstə / (BrE)
the person who introduces the performers and directs the proceedings in a circus
person in charge of a circus show

Examples

The ringmaster introduced the acrobats with a flourish.
As a child, she dreamed of becoming a ringmaster and leading the circus parade.
The ringmaster wore a bright red tailcoat and top hat.

Synset Examples

The ringmaster announced the next act.

Etymology

From 'ring' (circus performance area) + 'master' (person in charge)
