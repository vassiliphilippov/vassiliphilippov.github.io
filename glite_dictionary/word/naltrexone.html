naltrexone

1. NOUN

/ nælˈtrɛksoʊn / (AmE), / nælˈtrɛksəʊn / (BrE)
a synthetic drug used as an oral medication to block the effects of opiates, often prescribed to help treat opioid or alcohol dependence
drug blocking effects of opiates
medical

Examples

After completing detoxification, the doctor prescribed naltrexone to help prevent relapse.
The clinic offers naltrexone as part of a comprehensive treatment program for opioid addiction.
Research has shown that naltrexone can reduce cravings for both alcohol and opioids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naloxone - a drug that reverses the effects of opioid overdose
  • Degree Lower: opioid - a class of drugs that includes opiates

Etymology

From the chemical name N-allylnoroxymorphone, with -trex- and -one indicating structural features.
