Jew's-ear

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːz ɪr / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːz ɪə / (BrE)
A widely distributed edible fungus (Auricularia auricula-judae), shaped like a human ear and found growing on decaying wood.
edible ear-shaped fungus

Examples

The Jew's-ear fungus is often used in Asian dishes for its unique texture.
After heavy rain, clusters of Jew's-ear appeared on the old willow tree.
She collected fresh Jew's-ear during her morning walk in the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood ear - another name for Jew's-ear fungus, especially in Asian cuisine

Etymology

Named for its traditional association with the Judas tree (supposed tree of Judas's suicide), from the resemblance of the fungus's shape to a human ear.
