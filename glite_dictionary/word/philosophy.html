philosophy

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlɑːsəfi / (AmE), / fəˈlɒsəfi / (BrE)
a set of personal beliefs or guiding principles about how to live or how to approach particular situations
personal belief or guiding principle

Examples

Her philosophy is to treat everyone with respect, no matter their background.
Steve's parenting philosophy emphasizes independence and responsibility.
The company adopted a new philosophy of putting customers first in all decisions.

Synset Examples

self-indulgence was his only philosophy
my father's philosophy of child-rearing was to let mother do it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: principle - a fundamental truth or proposition that serves as the foundation for a system of belief or behavior
  • Synonym: doctrine - a belief or set of beliefs held and taught by a group

Etymology

From Greek 'philosophia' meaning 'love of wisdom'.

2. NOUN

/ fəˈlɑːsəfi / (AmE), / fɪˈlɒsəfi / (BrE)
A set of beliefs or principles that is accepted by a group, school, or individual as authoritative and guides their actions or thinking.
system of accepted beliefs or principles

Examples

The company has a strict philosophy of putting the customer first in all situations.
Many schools have their own unique educational philosophy that shapes their teaching methods.
His personal philosophy emphasizes honesty, hard work, and respect for others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doctrine - a belief or set of beliefs held and taught by a group
  • Synonym: creed - a set of beliefs or aims that guide someone's actions
  • Synonym: dogma - a principle or set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true

Etymology

From Greek philosophia (love of wisdom).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05952149-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ fəˈlɑːsəfi / (AmE), / fəˈlɒsəfi / (BrE)
the study of the fundamental nature of knowledge, existence, and reality, especially when considered as an academic discipline
study of knowledge, existence, and ethics

Examples

She studied philosophy at university, focusing on ancient Greek thinkers.
The meaning of life is a question often explored in philosophy.
His approach to business is influenced by his personal philosophy of fairness and transparency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metaphysics - branch of philosophy concerned with existence and reality
  • Synonym: ethics - branch of philosophy dealing with moral principles

Etymology

From Greek philosophia, meaning 'love of wisdom'.
