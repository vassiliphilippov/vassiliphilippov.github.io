proviso

1. NOUN

/ prəˈvaɪzoʊ / (AmE), / prəˈvaɪzəʊ / (BrE)
a statement in a legal document that makes a particular condition or restriction
condition or restriction in a legal document
legal

Examples

The contract was accepted with the proviso that all work must be completed by June.
He agreed to testify, but only with a proviso ensuring his identity would be protected.
The new rule includes a proviso allowing exceptions in special circumstances.

Synset Examples

he accepted subject to one provision

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provision - a clause in a legal document that sets out a requirement or condition

Etymology

From Latin proviso, literally 'it being provided', from providere (to provide).
