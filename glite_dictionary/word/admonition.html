admonition

1. NOUN

/ ˌæd.məˈnɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌæd.məˈnɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a firm warning or reprimand, especially one intended to correct or caution
firm warning or reprimand

Examples

The teacher's admonition left the class silent and attentive for the rest of the period.
Despite her mother's admonition, she stayed out past midnight.
He received a gentle admonition rather than a severe punishment for his mistake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebuke - an expression of sharp disapproval or criticism
  • Synonym: warning - a statement or event that warns of something or acts as a caution
  • Antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration

Etymology

From Latin admonitio (a warning), from admonere (to warn).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæd.məˈnɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌæd.məˈnɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a warning or piece of advice, especially about something dangerous or unpleasant that is likely to happen
warning or cautionary advice

Examples

Despite his teacher's admonition, he continued to procrastinate on his assignments.
The hikers ignored the ranger's admonition and ventured into the dangerous area.
In her speech, she gave an admonition about the consequences of reckless driving.

Synset Examples

a letter of admonition about the dangers of immorality
the warning was to beware of surprises
his final word of advice was not to play with matches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warning - a statement or event that tells of possible danger
  • Synonym: advice - guidance or recommendations about future action

Etymology

From Latin admonitio, from admonere (to warn, advise).
