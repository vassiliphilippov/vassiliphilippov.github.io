precision

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈsɪʒən / (AmE), / prɪˈsɪʒ.ən / (BrE)
the quality of being exact, accurate, and able to be consistently reproduced in amount or performance
exactness; ability to be consistently accurate

Examples

The scientist emphasized the importance of precision when recording experimental data.
Her precision in hitting the target impressed the entire team.
Modern manufacturing relies on a high degree of precision to ensure product quality.

Synset Examples

he handled it with the preciseness of an automaton
note the meticulous precision of his measurements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accuracy - the quality of being correct or true

Etymology

From Latin 'precisio', meaning 'cutting off', from 'praecidere' (to cut off).
