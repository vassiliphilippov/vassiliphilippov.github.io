psycholinguistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsaɪkəʊlɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊlɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk / (BrE)
Relating to the study of the psychological processes involved in the acquisition and use of language.
relating to the psychology of language

Examples

The psycholinguistic approach examines how children acquire their first language.
Researchers use psycholinguistic experiments to study sentence processing in the brain.
Her psycholinguistic research focuses on the relationship between memory and vocabulary learning.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: linguistic - relating to language or linguistics

Etymology

From psycho- (relating to the mind) + linguistic (relating to language).
