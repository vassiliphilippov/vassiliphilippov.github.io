effacement

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈfeɪsmənt / (AmE), / ɪˈfeɪsmənt / (BrE)
the process by which the cervix becomes shorter and thinner during labor, preparing for childbirth
thinning of cervix during labor
medical

Examples

During active labor, cervical effacement must reach nearly 100% before delivery is possible.
The doctor monitored the patient's effacement and dilation during her contractions.
Early effacement of the cervix can be an indicator that labor is progressing.

Etymology

From French 'effacement', from 'effacer' (to erase).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈfeɪsmənt / (AmE), / ɪˈfeɪsmənt / (BrE)
the act of making oneself less noticeable or withdrawing into the background; the reduction of one's prominence or visibility
the act of making oneself inconspicuous

Examples

Her effacement in meetings made her contributions less visible, even though she had valuable ideas to share.
In the busy restaurant, he moved with such quiet effacement that few patrons even noticed his presence.
The artist's effacement was so complete that hardly anyone recognized her at the gallery opening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-effacement - modesty; the quality of not claiming attention for oneself
  • Antonym: ostentation - showy display intending to attract notice

Etymology

From French effacement, from effacer (to erase), from Latin ex- (out) + facies (face, form).
