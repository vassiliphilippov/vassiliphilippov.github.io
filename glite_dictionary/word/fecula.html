fecula

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːkjʊlə / (AmE), / ˈfiːkjʊlə / (BrE)
Excreta, particularly the waste matter produced by insects.
insect excreta; waste matter

Examples

The scientist examined the fecula left behind by the caterpillars on the leaves.
Keepers noticed small piles of fecula in the insect enclosure each morning.
Under the microscope, the fecula provided important clues about the beetle's diet.

Etymology

From Latin *faecula* (dregs, sediment), a diminutive of *faex* (dregs, sediment).
