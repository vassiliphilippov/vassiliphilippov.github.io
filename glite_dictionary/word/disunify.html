to disunify

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈjuːnɪfaɪ / (AmE), / dɪsˈjuːnɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to cause to become disunited or to break up into parts; to separate or divide
cause to be separated or divided

Examples

Leaders worry that proposed reforms might disunify the organization and weaken its influence.
Conflicting interests can disunify a team that once worked well together.
The policy changes threatened to disunify the previously united federation.

Synset Examples

The country is disunifying
Yugoslavia broke apart after 1989

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to unify - bring together or combine into a single entity
  • Synonym: divide - to separate into parts or groups

Etymology

From Latin prefix dis- (apart, not) + unify (to make one).
