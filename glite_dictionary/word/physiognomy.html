physiognomy

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɪziˈɑːnəmi / (AmE), / ˌfɪziˈɒnəmi / (BrE)
the features or appearance of a person's face, especially as an indication of character
facial features or appearance

Examples

The prisoner's stern physiognomy revealed little about his emotions during the trial.
She could read a great deal from the physiognomy of her friends when they entered the room.
In the portrait, the king's regal physiognomy dominated the canvas, demanding attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: face - the front part of a person's head
  • Synonym: countenance - a person's face or facial expression
  • Synonym: mug - informal term for face

Etymology

From Greek physiognōmia, from physis (nature) + gnomon (judge, interpreter).
