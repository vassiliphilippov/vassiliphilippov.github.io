Zoysia matrella

1. NOUN

/ ˈzoʊziə məˈtrɛlə / (AmE), / ˈzɔɪziə məˈtrɛlə / (BrE)
a species of warm-season grass native to Southeast Asia, commonly used for lawns and turf, especially in tropical and subtropical regions
a species of warm-season lawn grass

Examples

The golf course used Zoysia matrella for its fairways to ensure a dense, fine-bladed turf.
Homeowners in warmer climates often prefer Zoysia matrella because of its heat and drought tolerance.
Landscapers recommended planting Zoysia matrella in the new park for its lush appearance and durability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zoysia japonica - another species of the genus Zoysia, commonly known as Japanese lawngrass

Etymology

From the genus name Zoysia, named after botanist Karl von Zois, and species name matrella, from Latin.
