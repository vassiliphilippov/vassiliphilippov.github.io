vesicatory

1. NOUN

/ vəˈsɪkəˌtɔri / (AmE), / vəˈsɪkətəri / (BrE)
a chemical or substance that causes blistering of the skin, especially used in medicine or referenced in toxicology
blister-causing agent
medical

Examples

The laboratory handled the vesicatory with extreme caution due to its severe effects on the skin.
Mustard gas is a notorious vesicatory used in chemical warfare.
Doctors once used a vesicatory to treat certain skin conditions, but this practice is now outdated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blistering agent - a substance that causes blisters

Etymology

From Latin vesica (bladder) + -tory (producing or pertaining to).

2. ADJ

/ vəˈsɪkəˌtɔri / (AmE), / vɪˈsɪkət(ə)ri / (BrE)
(of a substance or agent) causing blisters on the skin.
causing blisters (medical term)
medical

Examples

The doctor warned that the ointment could be vesicatory if used improperly.
Certain chemicals act as vesicatory agents when they contact the skin.
He developed a severe reaction after exposure to a vesicatory substance in the lab.

Etymology

From Latin 'vesicatorius', from 'vesica' meaning 'bladder'.
