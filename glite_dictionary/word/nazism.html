Nazism

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑː.tsi.ɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈnæts.i.ɪzəm / (BrE)
The political principles and practices of the Nazi Party, characterized by totalitarianism, racist ideology, and the belief in the supremacy of a strong leader.
Nazi political ideology; totalitarian racism

Examples

The rise of Nazism in Germany had devastating effects on Europe and the world.
Many historians study how economic hardship contributed to the spread of Nazism.
Rejecting all forms of Nazism is crucial to promoting human rights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fascism - an authoritarian and nationalistic right-wing system of government and social organization
  • Antonym: democracy - a system of government by the whole population, typically through elected representatives

Etymology

From German Nazismus, from Nazi (shortened form of Nationalsozialist, ‘National Socialist’).
