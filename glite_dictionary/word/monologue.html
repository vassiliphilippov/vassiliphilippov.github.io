monologue

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːnəˌlɔɡ / (AmE), / ˈmɒnəlɒɡ / (BrE)
a long speech by a single actor in a play, film, or as part of a performance; a dramatic speech delivered by one person
long dramatic speech by one person

Examples

The actor delivered a powerful monologue that moved the entire audience.
Her audition consisted of a dramatic monologue from Shakespeare.
The play opens with a lengthy monologue that sets the stage for the story.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soliloquy - speech in a play in which a character speaks thoughts aloud, usually alone

Etymology

From Greek monológos, from mónos (single) + lógos (speech).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.nə.lɔːɡ / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.ə.lɒɡ / (BrE)
a long speech by one person, especially when expressing their thoughts aloud or addressing themselves
long speech by one person

Examples

She delivered a powerful monologue about her struggles and dreams.
During the play, the actor's monologue revealed the character's deepest fears.
He often drifts into a monologue when he's lost in thought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soliloquy - an act of speaking one's thoughts aloud when alone, especially in a play

Etymology

From French 'monologue', from Greek 'monologos' (speaking alone).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːnəˌlɔg / (AmE), / ˈmɒnəlɒg / (BrE)
a long speech by one person during a conversation, or a dramatic speech by a single actor on stage
long speech by one person

Examples

The actor delivered a powerful monologue in the play's final scene.
Her father launched into a long monologue about the importance of hard work.
Tired of his monologue, the team members struggled to join the discussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soliloquy - an act of speaking one's thoughts aloud when by oneself, especially in a play

Etymology

From French 'monologue', from Greek 'monologos' (speaking alone) — 'mono' (alone) + 'logos' (speech).
