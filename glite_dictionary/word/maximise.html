to maximise

1. VERB

/ ˈmæk.səˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmæk.sɪ.maɪz / (BrE)
to make something as large, great, or effective as possible
make as large as possible
british

Examples

The company introduced new strategies to maximise efficiency across all departments.
She worked overtime to maximise her earnings before the holidays.
Farmers use technology to maximise crop yields during the growing season.

Synset Examples

Maximize your profits!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to maximize - to make something as great or large as possible
  • Antonym: to minimise - to make something as small or unimportant as possible

Etymology

From Latin 'maximus' (greatest) via 'maximum'; patterned after 'minimise'.

2. VERB

/ ˈmæk.səˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmak.sɪ.maɪz / (BrE)
to make as great as possible or to use to the fullest extent
make as great as possible

Examples

We need to maximise the use of our resources during this project.
She always tries to maximise her learning opportunities at work.
The new system is designed to maximise efficiency and reduce costs.

Synset Examples

He maximized his role

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to minimise - to make as small as possible

Etymology

From Latin 'maximus' (greatest), via French 'maximiser'.
