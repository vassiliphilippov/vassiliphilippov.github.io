Viverridae

1. NOUN

/ vɪˈvɛrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / vɪˈvɛrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small to medium-sized mammals, including genets, civets, and related species, native primarily to Africa and Asia.
family of mammals including civets and genets

Examples

The Viverridae family includes animals like civets and genets, which are found across Africa and Asia.
Scientists are studying the evolutionary history of the Viverridae to understand their adaptation to diverse habitats.
Members of Viverridae are primarily carnivorous and are known for their distinct scent glands.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Viverridae', from genus name 'Viverra' (civet) + '-idae' (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ vaɪˈvɛr.ɪˌdi / (AmE), / vɪˈvɛr.ɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small to medium-sized, mostly nocturnal mammals including civets, genets, and related species, native to the Old World.
family of civets, genets, and related mammals

Examples

The Viverridae family includes both civets and genets, which are found in Africa and Asia.
Scientists continue to debate the evolutionary relationships within the Viverridae.
Some members of the Viverridae, like the African civet, have distinctive scent glands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mongoose - small carnivorous mammal in the family Herpestidae, sometimes previously classed in Viverridae
  • Synonym: civet - small, lean, mostly nocturnal mammals of the Viverridae family

Etymology

From modern Latin Viverridae, from Latin viverra (ferret).
