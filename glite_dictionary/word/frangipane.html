frangipane

1. NOUN

/ ˈfræn.dʒəˌpæn / (AmE), / ˈfræn.dʒɪˌpæn / (BrE)
A sweet, creamy filling made from ground almonds, butter, sugar, and eggs, typically used in tarts and pastries.
almond-flavored pastry filling

Examples

The pear tart was filled with a rich frangipane that complemented the fruit perfectly.
She learned to make frangipane from her grandmother’s old recipe book.
Many classic French pastries include frangipane as a key component.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marzipan - sweet almond paste, often sculpted or used in desserts

Etymology

From French 'frangipane', possibly named after Marquis Muzio Frangipani, an Italian nobleman.
