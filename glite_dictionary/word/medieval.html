medieval

1. ADJ

/ ˌmiːdiˈivəl / (AmE), / ˌmiːdɪˈiːvəl / (BrE)
relating to the Middle Ages, the period of European history from about AD 500 to 1500.
of the Middle Ages

Examples

The castle's architecture reflects a medieval style that has fascinated historians for centuries.
During the medieval period, illuminated manuscripts were painstakingly created by hand.
The museum exhibit features weapons and armor from the medieval era.

Synset Examples

Medieval scholars
Medieval times

Related Terms

  • Antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times, especially as opposed to the past; antonym to 'medieval'
  • Degree Lower: ancient - belonging to the very distant past; further removed in time than 'medieval'

Etymology

From Latin 'medium aevum' (the middle age), via Medieval Latin 'medievalis'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03001222-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌmiːdɪˈivəl / (AmE), / ˌmɛdɪˈiːvəl / (BrE)
Relating to the qualities, customs, or atmosphere of chivalry, knighthood, or feudal society in the Middle Ages.
characteristic of Middle Ages chivalry and knighthood

Examples

The castle's medieval architecture features towers and battlements reminiscent of knighthood.
He studied the medieval codes of chivalry that governed the behavior of knights.
Their reenactment group wears medieval armor and participates in jousting tournaments.

Synset Examples

chivalric rites
the knightly years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chivalric - relating to chivalry, knighthood, and medieval ideals
  • Antonym: modern - relating to the current or recent times (opposite of medieval)

Etymology

From Latin 'medium aevum' (the Middle Ages) via medieval Latin 'medievalis'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01733389-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ məˈdiːviəl / (AmE), / mɪˈdiːvɪəl / (BrE)
Very old-fashioned in attitudes or ideas, especially in a way considered unenlightened; relating to the Middle Ages.
old-fashioned or related to the Middle Ages

Examples

He still holds medieval views about gender roles.
Her parents considered dating apps to be entirely too medieval.
Many people see the practice as medieval and out of touch with the times.

Synset Examples

a medieval attitude toward dating

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiquated - old-fashioned; out of date
  • Antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times

Etymology

From Latin 'medium aevum' (the Middle Ages) via Medieval Latin 'mediaevalis'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01541543-s
Synset POS: s
