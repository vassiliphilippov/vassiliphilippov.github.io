to pollute

1. VERB

/ pəˈlut / (AmE), / pəˈluːt / (BrE)
to make water, air, soil, etc. dirty or harmful by adding waste or poisonous substances
to make impure or dirty, especially the environment

Examples

Factories that dump chemicals into rivers pollute the water and harm wildlife.
It is illegal to pollute the air with hazardous fumes.
Careless disposal of trash can pollute the local environment.

Synset Examples

The industrial wastes polluted the lake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to contaminate - to make something impure or harmful by adding something undesirable
  • Antonym: to purify - to remove impurities from; to make pure

Etymology

From Latin polluere (to defile, to soil).
