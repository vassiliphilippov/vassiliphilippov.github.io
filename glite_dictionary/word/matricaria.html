Matricaria

1. NOUN

/ ˌmætrɪˈkɛriə / (AmE), / ˌmætrɪˈkeəriə / (BrE)
A genus of strong-smelling, weedy plants in the daisy family, including species often called chamomile.
genus of plants including chamomile

Examples

Matricaria is widely used for its medicinal properties, especially in teas.
Some species of Matricaria are common wildflowers found in Europe and Asia.
The botanist identified the plant as belonging to the Matricaria genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chamomile - common name for several species in Matricaria

Etymology

From Latin 'matricaria', meaning 'womb herb', from 'matrix' (womb), due to its historical medicinal uses.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmætrɪˈkɛriə / (AmE), / ˌmætrɪˈkɑːrɪə / (BrE)
a genus of strong-smelling weedy herbs in the daisy family, native chiefly to the Old World, including plants sometimes grouped under other genera such as Tanacetum and Tripleurospermum
genus of aromatic weedy herbs (chamomile)

Examples

Many herbal teas contain dried flowers of Matricaria for their calming properties.
Researchers studied the chemical compounds found in Matricaria to understand its medicinal effects.
Wild Matricaria often grows abundantly along roadsides and in uncultivated fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chamomile - aromatic herb of the genus Matricaria, often used for tea

Etymology

From Latin *matricaria* (chamomile), from *matrix* (womb), referring to traditional medicinal use.
