spattering

1. NOUN

/ ˈspætərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspatərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of splashing small drops of liquid onto a surface
act of splashing drops of liquid

Examples

A sudden spattering of rain hit the window as the storm approached.
The spattering of paint on the wall suggested a messy renovation.
He wiped away the spattering of mud left by the passing car.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splashing - the act of causing liquid to scatter in drops

Etymology

From the verb 'spatter', dating from the late 15th century, possibly imitative in origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspæt̬.ɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspæt.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
a short, sudden noise made by something striking or splashing in small drops or bursts, usually in a rapid or explosive manner
sudden noise from splattering or bursting

Examples

There was a sudden spattering of rain against the window.
The spattering of grease hit the stove as he fried the bacon.
A distant spattering of applause broke out after her performance.

Synset Examples

he heard a spatter of gunfire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spatter - the act or sound of splashing in small drops
  • Synonym: sputter - a series of explosive spitting or popping sounds

Etymology

From the verb 'spatter', likely imitative of the sound, with '-ing' forming the noun.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07409414-n
Synset POS: n
