lumper

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌmpər / (AmE), / ˈlʌmpə / (BrE)
a taxonomist who groups organisms into large categories based on major characteristics, rather than distinguishing small differences
taxonomist who uses broad classifications

Examples

In the debate between lumpers and splitters, she firmly sided with the former.
Some lumpers argue that the two species should be combined due to their similarities.
The conference included a session on the history of lumpers in taxonomy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: splitter - a taxonomist who favors dividing organisms into smaller, more specific groups

2. NOUN

/ ˈlʌmpər / (AmE), / ˈlʌmpə / (BrE)
a laborer employed to load and unload ships, trucks, or other freight at a port or warehouse
dockworker who loads/unloads ships or trucks

Examples

The port hired a lumper to help unload the cargo from the ship.
Several lumpers worked together to empty the truck by noon.
As a lumper, her job was physically demanding but vital to the shipping industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stevedore - a person employed at a dock to load and unload ships
