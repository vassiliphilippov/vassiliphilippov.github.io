clue

1. NOUN

/ kluː / (AmE), / kluː / (BrE)
A slight indication or piece of information that helps to solve a problem or mystery.
a hint or slight indication

Examples

The detective found a clue that pointed to the suspect's identity.
There was no clue as to where she had gone.
A faint smell of perfume was the only clue left at the scene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hint - a slight or indirect indication or suggestion
  • Synonym: evidence - something that gives a sign or proof
  • Antonym: answer - a solution or response to a problem

Etymology

Originally spelled 'clew', from Old English *cliewen* (a ball of thread), later influenced by the idea of following a thread to solve a mystery.

2. NOUN

/ klu / (AmE), / kluː / (BrE)
a piece of information or evidence that helps to solve a problem, question, or mystery
evidence or information to help solve a problem

Examples

The detective searched the room for any clue that could explain the disappearance.
She found a clue in the old photograph that led her to the answer.
Without a single clue, solving the puzzle seemed impossible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hint - a small piece of information to help understand or solve something
  • Antonym: solution - the answer to a problem or mystery

Etymology

Variant of 'clew', meaning a ball of thread, from Old English 'cliow', once used to guide through a maze.
