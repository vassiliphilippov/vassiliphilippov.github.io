grackle

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrækəl / (AmE), / ˈɡrækəl / (BrE)
a long-tailed American blackbird with iridescent black plumage, belonging to the family Icteridae
a North American blackbird

Examples

A noisy grackle perched on the fence, its feathers gleaming in the sunlight.
In the park, flocks of grackles can often be seen scavenging for food.
The distinctive call of the grackle filled the air at dawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackbird - a common European or American songbird with black plumage

Etymology

From Latin *graculus* (a jackdaw).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡrækəl / (AmE), / ˈɡrækl̩ / (BrE)
a glossy black starling of Asia, often able to mimic speech
Asian glossy black starling

Examples

The grackle in the aviary could imitate several words with surprising clarity.
Many bird enthusiasts try to teach their pet grackle to mimic phrases.
A grackle perched on the wire, its feathers shining in the sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mynah - starling native to Asia, known for its ability to mimic human speech

Etymology

From Latin *graculus* (a jackdaw, a type of bird).
