Veratrum viride

1. NOUN

/ vəˈreɪtrəm ˈvaɪ.riːd / (AmE), / vəˈrætrəm ˈvɪr.ɪdiː / (BrE)
A North American plant with large leaves and yellowish-green flowers, known to yield a toxic alkaloid used medicinally.
North American medicinal plant with toxic alkaloid
medical

Examples

The roots of Veratrum viride have been used in traditional medicine, but they are highly toxic.
Botanists studied the flower structure of Veratrum viride in its native habitat.
Veratrum viride should be handled with care due to the dangerous alkaloids it contains.

Etymology

From Latin: 'veratrum' (hellebore) + 'viride' (green).
