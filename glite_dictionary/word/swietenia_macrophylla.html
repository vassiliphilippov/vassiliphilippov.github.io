Swietenia macrophylla

1. NOUN

/ swiˌɛtiniə ˌmækrəˈfɪlə / (AmE), / swɪˌɛtɪniə ˌmakrəˈfɪlə / (BrE)
A species of mahogany tree native to Central and South America, valued for its high-quality timber.
a Central/South American mahogany tree

Examples

The wood of Swietenia macrophylla is prized for making fine furniture and musical instruments.
Conservation efforts aim to protect Swietenia macrophylla from illegal logging.
Large plantations of Swietenia macrophylla are found in parts of Brazil and Honduras.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mahogany - hard reddish-brown wood from several tropical trees

Etymology

From genus name Swietenia (named after Gerard van Swieten, an Austrian botanist) and Greek makros (large) + phyllon (leaf).
