brute

1. NOUN

/ brut / (AmE), / bruːt / (BrE)
a person who is cruel, violent, or lacks compassion
a cruel or violent person

Examples

The prison guard was known as a brute who showed no mercy to the inmates.
Even his friends admitted that he could be a brute when he was angry.
He acted like a brute towards anyone who disagreed with him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beast - an animal, especially a large or dangerous one; also used metaphorically for a cruel person
  • Synonym: monster - a person who is inhumanly cruel
  • Antonym: gentleman - a polite or kind man, in contrast to a brute

Etymology

From Latin brutus (heavy, dull, stupid).

2. ADJ

/ bruːt / (AmE), / bruːt / (BrE)
Resembling a beast; lacking human sensibility, compassion, or reasoning.
lacking human sensibility; beast-like

Examples

The soldiers used brute force to break down the door.
His brute behavior shocked everyone at the dinner party.
Survival in the wild often comes down to brute strength rather than intelligence.

Synset Examples

beastly desires
a bestial nature
brute force
a dull and brutish man
bestial treatment of prisoners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bestial - like a beast; lacking reason or intelligence
  • Synonym: beastly - like or characteristic of a beast
  • Antonym: humane - showing compassion or benevolence

Etymology

From Middle French *brut* (coarse, animal-like), from Latin *brutus* (dull, stupid, insensible).
