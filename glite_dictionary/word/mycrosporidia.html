Mycrosporidia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.kroʊ.spəˈrɪd.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌmɪ.krə.spɒˈrɪd.i.ə / (BrE)
an order of microscopic, spore-forming parasites belonging to the subclass Cnidosporidia, infecting various hosts.
order of microscopic spore-forming parasites
medical

Examples

Infectious diseases in fish are sometimes caused by Mycrosporidia.
Researchers study Mycrosporidia to understand their effects on invertebrate hosts.
The classification of Mycrosporidia has been revised in recent years based on genetic data.

Etymology

From Greek 'mykēs' (fungus) and 'spora' (seed, spore).
