Tilia americana

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪliə əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnə / (AmE), / ˈtɪliə əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnə / (BrE)
A large American deciduous tree (the American basswood) with a rounded crown and large, dark green leaves, valued as a shade tree.
American shade tree with large leaves

Examples

The Tilia americana in the park provides excellent shade on hot summer days.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Tilia americana in various climates.
In some regions, Tilia americana is preferred for its attractive foliage and fast growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basswood - another name for Tilia americana
  • Synonym: linden - another common name for trees of the Tilia genus

Etymology

From Latin genus name Tilia, and Latin americana, meaning 'of America'.
