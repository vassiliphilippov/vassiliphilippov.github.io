bloodthirsty

1. ADJ

/ ˈblʌdˌθɜːrsti / (AmE), / ˈblʌdˌθɜːsti / (BrE)
eager to cause violence, bloodshed, or killing; showing a desire for blood or cruelty.
eager for violence or bloodshed

Examples

The dictator's bloodthirsty regime was responsible for countless atrocities.
Crowds cheered with bloodthirsty excitement during the gladiatorial games.
His bloodthirsty threats frightened everyone in the room.

Synset Examples

bloody-minded tyrants
bloodthirsty yells
went after the collaborators with a sanguinary fury that drenched the land with blood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanguinary - involving or causing much bloodshed; bloodthirsty
  • Antonym: merciful - showing compassion or forgiveness instead of harshness

Etymology

Formed from 'blood' + 'thirsty,' originally meaning 'having a thirst for blood.'

2. ADJ

/ ˈblʌdˌθɝsti / (AmE), / ˈblʌdˌθɜːsti / (BrE)
Having or showing a desire for violence, killing, or cruelty.
eager for violence or cruelty

Examples

The bloodthirsty crowd cheered wildly as the gladiators entered the arena.
The novel describes a bloodthirsty warlord who rules through fear and violence.
She refused to watch bloodthirsty movies because she hated scenes of cruelty.

Synset Examples

The bloodthirsty crowd demanded justice after the incident.
The film featured a bloodthirsty monster terrorizing the town.
His bloodthirsty nature emerged during the heated debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruel - willing to cause pain or suffering
  • Antonym: merciful - showing mercy or compassion

Etymology

Formed from 'blood' + 'thirsty', with 'thirsty' figuratively meaning eager or craving.
