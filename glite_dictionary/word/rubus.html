Rubus

1. PROPN

/ ˈruːbəs / (AmE), / ˈruːbəs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the rose family, including blackberries, raspberries, and similar brambles, known for their edible aggregate berries and thorny stems.
genus of brambles (blackberries, raspberries, etc.)

Examples

Many species within the genus Rubus produce edible fruits, such as blackberries and raspberries.
Gardeners often cultivate Rubus plants for their attractive flowers and delicious berries.
Wild Rubus bushes can quickly spread and form dense thickets in wooded areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bramble - any rough, tangled, prickly shrub, especially of the genus Rubus

Etymology

From Latin *rūbus* meaning 'bramble' or 'blackberry bush'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈruːbəs / (AmE), / ˈruːbəs / (BrE)
a large genus of bramble plants in the rose family (Rosaceae) that bear aggregate berries, including raspberries, blackberries, and dewberries
genus of bramble plants including raspberries and blackberries

Examples

The fruits produced by Rubus species are an important food source for wildlife.
Gardeners often cultivate varieties of Rubus for their delicious berries.
Many wild Rubus plants can be found growing along country roadsides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bramble - a rough, prickly shrub, especially a blackberry or raspberry bush

Etymology

From Latin rubus (bramble, blackberry bush).
