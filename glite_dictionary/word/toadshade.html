toadshade

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊdˌʃeɪd / (AmE), / ˈtəʊdʃeɪd / (BrE)
A woodland plant of the northeastern United States, a type of trillium with sessile leaves and red or purple, pungent-smelling flowers.
a woodland trillium with reddish flowers

Examples

We spotted a cluster of toadshade growing along the shaded woodland trail.
The toadshade is known for its reddish flowers and pungent scent in early spring.
Botanists often study the ecology of toadshade because of its unique adaptation to forest understories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trillium - a genus of flowering plants with three-petaled flowers
