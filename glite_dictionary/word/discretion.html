discretion

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈskrɛʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɛʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
The ability to make careful judgments and to behave with refined taste and tact.
refined taste; tact

Examples

She handled the delicate situation with remarkable discretion and grace.
A diplomat must possess both intelligence and discretion in their interactions.
His comments were marked by the discretion expected of someone in his position.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tact - Skill and sensitivity in dealing with others or with difficult issues

Etymology

From Latin 'discretio' (separation, distinction, discernment), from 'discernere' (to discern).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈskrɛʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɛʃən / (BrE)
the ability to make careful and wise decisions, especially by respecting privacy or handling sensitive matters objectively
wise and careful judgment

Examples

The manager handled the situation with great discretion to avoid unnecessary conflict.
Teachers are trusted to use their discretion when dealing with sensitive student issues.
Due to her sense of discretion, she was often chosen to manage confidential information.

Synset Examples

a man of discernment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judgement - the ability to make considered decisions or come to sensible conclusions
  • Antonym: indiscretion - lack of good judgment or care in behavior or speech

Etymology

From Latin 'discretio' (separation, discernment), from 'discernere' (to separate, distinguish).
