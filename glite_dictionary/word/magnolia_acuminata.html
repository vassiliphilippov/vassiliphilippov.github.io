Magnolia acuminata

1. NOUN

/ mæɡˌnoʊliə əˌkjuːmɪˈneɪtə / (AmE), / mæɡˌnəʊliə əˌkjuːmɪˈneɪtə / (BrE)
A species of American deciduous magnolia with large leaves and cucumber-like fruit, also known as the cucumber tree.
a North American deciduous magnolia tree species

Examples

The Magnolia acuminata is often called the cucumber tree due to the shape of its fruit.
A mature Magnolia acuminata can reach heights of up to 30 meters in the wild.
In spring, the Magnolia acuminata produces yellow-green flowers among its large, broad leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cucumber tree - Another name for Magnolia acuminata

Etymology

From Latin, combining the genus Magnolia and 'acuminata' meaning 'pointed' or 'tapering to a point'.
