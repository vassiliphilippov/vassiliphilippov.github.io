deliberateness

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈlɪbərɪtnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪbərətnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being careful, thoughtful, and intentional in one's actions or decisions.
thoughtfulness and intention in action or decision

Examples

Her deliberateness in making major decisions earned her the respect of her colleagues.
He spoke with a deliberateness that made everyone pay attention to his words.
The deliberateness of his actions showed he had considered every possible outcome.

Synset Examples

he was a man of judicial deliberation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deliberation - careful consideration before a decision
  • Antonym: impulsiveness - acting or done without forethought

Etymology

From deliberate + -ness; 'deliberate' from Latin deliberatus, past participle of deliberare (to weigh well, consider).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈlɪbərətnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪbərətnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being slow, careful, and unhurried, showing an absence of haste or hurry.
absence of haste; careful unhurriedness

Examples

His deliberateness allowed him to avoid making careless mistakes under pressure.
She chose her words with deliberateness, making sure not to offend anyone.
The deliberateness of his movements gave him an air of calm authority.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: haste - excessive speed or urgency
  • Synonym: carefulness - the quality of being cautious and attentive

Etymology

From deliberate + -ness.
