Peromyscus

1. NOUN

/ pəˈrɑːmɪskəs / (AmE), / pɛˈrɒmɪskəs / (BrE)
A genus of small rodents known as New World wood mice, native to North and Central America, often referred to as deer mice.
genus of New World wood mice

Examples

Researchers studied the population dynamics of Peromyscus in various North American habitats.
The Peromyscus is noted for its agility and adaptability in forest environments.
Several species within the genus Peromyscus are important subjects in ecological and genetic research.

Etymology

From Greek 'pera' (pouch, wallet) and 'mys' (mouse), referring to the pocket-like cheek pouches of some rodents.
