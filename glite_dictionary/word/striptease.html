striptease

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɪpˌtiz / (AmE), / ˈstrɪpˌtiːz / (BrE)
A form of entertainment in which a performer gradually removes their clothing in a sexually provocative manner, typically to music.
erotic dance involving gradual undressing

Examples

The performer captivated the audience with her skilful striptease routine.
He had never attended a live striptease before his friend's bachelor party.
Classic movies from the 1950s sometimes featured elaborate striptease scenes.

Synset Examples

she did a strip right in front of everyone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burlesque - a variety show featuring striptease acts and comedy

Etymology

From 'strip' (undress) + 'tease' (to tantalize), first used in the early 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstrɪpˌtiz / (AmE), / ˈstrɪptiːz / (BrE)
a performance in which a person gradually undresses to music, especially as a form of erotic entertainment
erotic dance performance involving undressing

Examples

The club advertised a live striptease show every Friday night.
She perfected her striptease routine with careful choreography and practiced moves.
Many popular movies have featured a dramatic striptease scene for comedic or sensual effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burlesque - a form of variety show often including striptease and comedy acts
  • Degree Higher: lap dance - an erotic dance performed closely in front of a seated person

Etymology

Blend of 'strip' and 'tease', first used in the early 20th century.
