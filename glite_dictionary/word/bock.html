bock

1. NOUN

/ bɑk / (AmE), / bɒk / (BrE)
a very strong lager, traditionally brewed in autumn and aged through winter for drinking in spring
a strong lager beer

Examples

He ordered a glass of bock to go with his meal.
The brewery is famous for its seasonal bock each spring.
Many beer enthusiasts look forward to the release of the new bock every year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lager - a type of pale or dark beer brewed and conditioned at low temperature

Etymology

From German 'Bock' (male goat), used to name the beer style.
