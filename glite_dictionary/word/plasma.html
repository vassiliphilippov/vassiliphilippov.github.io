plasma

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæz.mə / (AmE), / ˈplɑːz.mə / (BrE)
the pale, liquid component of blood and lymph, in which blood cells are suspended and which does not contain cells itself
liquid part of blood or lymph
medical

Examples

The doctor collected a sample of plasma to test for antibodies.
After donating blood, only the plasma is separated for medical use.
Plasma makes up more than half of the blood's volume in the human body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serum - the clear yellowish fluid that remains from blood plasma after clotting factors have been removed

Etymology

From Greek *plasma* (something molded or formed), related to *plassein* (to mold).

2. NOUN

/ ˈplæz.mə / (AmE), / ˈplɑːz.mə / (BrE)
A green, slightly translucent variety of chalcedony used as a gemstone.
green translucent gemstone variety of chalcedony

Examples

The ancient Egyptians often carved plasma into ornamental scarabs and amulets.
She wore a unique necklace made from polished plasma stones.
Because of its green hue, plasma is sometimes mistaken for jade.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Late Latin *plasma* (something molded or formed), relating to its appearance.

3. NOUN

/ ˈplæz.mə / (AmE), / ˈplɑːz.mə / (BrE)
A state of matter distinct from solid, liquid, or gas, consisting of an ionized gas with free electrons and ions, found in stars and fusion reactors.
ionized gas; the fourth state of matter

Examples

The sun, like other stars, is made almost entirely of plasma.
Scientists study plasma to learn more about fusion energy.
In fusion reactors, hydrogen gas is heated until it becomes a plasma.

Synset Examples

particles in space exist in the form of a plasma

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solid - state of matter with fixed shape and volume
  • Antonym: liquid - state of matter with fixed volume but no fixed shape
  • Antonym: gas - state of matter with no fixed shape or volume

Etymology

From Greek 'plásma' (something molded or formed).
