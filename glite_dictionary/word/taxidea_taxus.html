Taxidea taxus

1. NOUN

/ tækˈsaɪdi.ə ˈteɪksəs / (AmE), / tækˈsɪdi.ə ˈtaksəs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the American badger, a burrowing mammal native to North America, known for its distinctive appearance and digging habits
American badger (scientific name)

Examples

The Taxidea taxus is well adapted to digging in the grasslands of North America.
Researchers observed a family of Taxidea taxus creating an extensive network of burrows.
Farmers sometimes encounter Taxidea taxus while working the fields in the Midwest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: badger - a burrowing mammal with a broad body and short legs

Etymology

From Latin; 'Taxidea' from taxonomy conventions for genus, 'taxus' meaning badger.
