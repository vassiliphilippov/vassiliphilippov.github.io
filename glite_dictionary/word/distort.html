to distort

1. VERB

/ dɪˈstɔrt / (AmE), / dɪˈstɔːt / (BrE)
to twist or press something out of its normal shape so that it becomes misshapen
twist out of shape

Examples

Heat can distort plastic containers, making them unusable.
The funhouse mirrors distort your reflection in hilarious ways.
When pressed too tightly, the soft metal will distort and lose its original shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deform - to change the shape of something, especially in a damaging way

Etymology

From Latin distortus, past participle of distorquere (to twist apart).

WordNet ID: oewn-01353381-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ dɪˈstɔrt / (AmE), / dɪˈstɔːt / (BrE)
to change the original form or meaning of something, such as a message or story, so that it becomes false or misleading
alter so as to mislead

Examples

The newspaper was accused of distorting the facts to suit its agenda.
He often distorts the truth to make his stories more interesting.
The report had been distorted by repeated retelling until it was hardly recognizable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to misrepresent - to give a false or misleading account of something
  • Synonym: to falsify - to alter information or evidence so as to mislead
  • Degree Lower: to exaggerate - to represent something as being larger or more important than it really is

Etymology

From Latin 'distortus', past participle of 'distorquere' (to twist apart).

3. VERB

/ dɪˈstɔrt / (AmE), / dɪˈstɔːt / (BrE)
to change the shape or form of something so that it is no longer correct or natural, often by applying pressure or stress
alter the shape of something by stress

Examples

The heat began to distort the metal frame, causing it to bend unnaturally.
She accidentally distorted the plastic lid when she pressed down too hard.
Prolonged pressure can gradually distort the shape of a soft object.

Synset Examples

His body was deformed by leprosy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deform - to spoil the shape or form of something
  • Synonym: warp - to bend or twist out of shape, typically by heat or damp
  • Antonym: to straighten - to make or become straight or normal in shape

Etymology

From Latin distortus, past participle of distorquere, meaning 'to twist apart'.
