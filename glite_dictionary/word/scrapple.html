scrapple

1. NOUN

/ ˈskræpəl / (AmE), / ˈskræpəl / (BrE)
a food made from pork scraps mixed with cornmeal, formed into loaves, and sliced and fried
pork and cornmeal loaf, sliced and fried

Examples

Many Pennsylvania Dutch families serve scrapple for breakfast.
You can buy scrapple at local markets in the Mid-Atlantic region.
She fried slices of scrapple until they were golden and crispy.

Etymology

From 'scrap' plus diminutive suffix '-le', originating in early American English.
