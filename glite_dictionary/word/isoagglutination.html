isoagglutination

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsoʊəˌɡluːtəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəʊəˌɡluːtɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
The agglutination of red blood cells or other antigens of one individual by the serum of another individual of the same species.
agglutination between same-species individuals' cells/serum
medical

Examples

Isoagglutination can occur during incompatible blood transfusions within the same species.
Blood typing relies on the principles of isoagglutination to determine compatibility.
Researchers observed isoagglutination in the samples taken from the experimental group.

Etymology

From iso- (equal, same) and agglutination, from Latin 'agglutinare' (to glue to).
