Macleaya cordata

1. NOUN

/ məˈkleɪ.ə kɔːrˈdeɪ.tə / (AmE), / məˈkleɪ.ə kɔːˈdɑːtə / (BrE)
A tall herb native to China and Japan, widely cultivated for its plume-like panicles of creamy white flowers.
Asian herb with plume-like white flowers

Examples

Macleaya cordata can add a dramatic flair to any garden with its tall white flower plumes.
Many gardeners appreciate the striking foliage of Macleaya cordata in mixed borders.
Macleaya cordata is known for thriving in rich, moist soils and partial sunlight.

Etymology

Named after Scottish botanist Alexander Macleay (1767–1848); 'cordata' from Latin cordatus, meaning 'heart-shaped,' referring to the leaves.
