Trichosurus vulpecula

1. NOUN

/ trɪˈkoʊsərəs vʌlˈpɛkjʊlə / (AmE), / trɪˈkɒsərəs vʊlˈpɛkjʊlə / (BrE)
a species of possum native to Australia, commonly known as the common brushtail possum, with a bushy tail and adapted to various habitats
species of Australian possum

Examples

The Trichosurus vulpecula is one of the most widespread marsupials in Australia.
Scientists studied the urban adaptation of Trichosurus vulpecula populations in New Zealand.
Conservationists are monitoring Trichosurus vulpecula numbers to assess their impact on native forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common brushtail possum - the common English name for Trichosurus vulpecula

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Trichosurus, from Greek 'thrix' (hair) + 'oura' (tail); species name vulpecula, Latin for 'little fox'.
