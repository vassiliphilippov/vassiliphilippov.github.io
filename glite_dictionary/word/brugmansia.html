Brugmansia

1. NOUN

/ bruɡˈmænziə / (AmE), / bruːɡˈmænziə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical South American plants known for their large, trumpet-shaped flowers, commonly called angel's trumpets
tropical plant genus; angel's trumpets

Examples

The garden was filled with the fragrant blooms of Brugmansia every summer night.
Many horticulturists admire Brugmansia for its impressive trumpet-shaped flowers.
Some species of Brugmansia are highly toxic if ingested.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Datura - a related genus of plants with similar trumpet-shaped flowers

Etymology

Named after Dutch botanist Sebald Justinus Brugmans (1763–1819).

2. NOUN

/ bruɡˈmænziə / (AmE), / bruːɡˈmansɪə / (BrE)
A genus of South American plants in the nightshade family, bearing large, trumpet-shaped flowers commonly called angel's trumpets.
genus of flowering plants, angel's trumpets

Examples

The Brugmansia bloomed profusely in the evening, filling the garden with its sweet scent.
Horticulturists often debate whether Brugmansia should be classified separately from Datura.
The large, pendulous flowers of Brugmansia are sometimes used as ornamentals, but all parts are toxic if ingested.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Datura - a related genus of plants also known for trumpet-shaped flowers and psychoactive properties

Etymology

Named after Dutch botanist Sebald Justinus Brugmans (1763–1819).
