Sylviidae

1. NOUN

/ sɪlˈvaɪɪdiː / (AmE), / sɪlˈvɪɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small passerine birds known as Old World warblers, typically found in Europe, Asia, and Africa
family of Old World warblers (birds)

Examples

The Sylviidae include many species with distinctive songs and calls.
For decades, ornithologists debated whether Sylviidae should be considered its own family or a subfamily.
Several members of Sylviidae migrate long distances between Europe and Africa each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warbler - a small songbird known for its melodious singing
  • Degree Higher: Muscicapidae - the Old World flycatcher family, sometimes including Sylviidae

Etymology

From New Latin Sylviidae, based on the type genus Sylvia.
