Vitidaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɪtəˈdeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌvɪtɪˈdeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of climbing plants (vines), including the grapevines, belonging to the order Rhamnales.
family of climbing plants (grape family)

Examples

The Vitidaceae family comprises several species of plants commonly known as grapevines.
Scientists studied the distribution of Vitidaceae in tropical forests around the world.
Many economically important fruits, such as grapes, belong to the Vitidaceae family.

Etymology

From Latin 'Vitis' (vine) + the family suffix '-aceae'.
