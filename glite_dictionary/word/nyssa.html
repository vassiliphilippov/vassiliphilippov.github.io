Nyssa

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪsə / (AmE), / ˈnɪsə / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous trees found in moist habitats, especially swamps and near ponds, commonly known as tupelos.
a genus of deciduous trees (tupelos)

Examples

The Nyssa trees in the swamp attract many species of birds every season.
Botanists catalogued several Nyssa specimens near the pond's edge.
Nyssa is valued for its resistance to waterlogged soil conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tupelo - a deciduous tree of moist habitats in North America, especially Nyssa sylvatica

Etymology

From Greek Nýs(s)a, used as a proper name in classical texts.
