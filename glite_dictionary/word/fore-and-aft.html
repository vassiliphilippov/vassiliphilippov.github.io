fore-and-aft

1. ADJ

/ ˌfɔr ən ˈæft / (AmE), / ˌfɔːr ən ˈɑːft / (BrE)
(of a direction or arrangement) parallel with the keel of a boat or ship; lengthwise from front to back.
parallel to a ship's keel (lengthwise)

Examples

The boat was damaged on the fore-and-aft axis during the storm.
They installed new ropes running fore-and-aft along the deck.
The cabin features two fore-and-aft berths for sleeping.

Etymology

From nautical use of 'fore' (front) and 'aft' (rear) to indicate directions or positions on a vessel.
