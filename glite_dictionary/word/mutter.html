mutter

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈmʌtə / (BrE)
a complaint or remark spoken in a low and indistinct voice, often expressing discontent or annoyance
low, indistinct complaint or remark

Examples

She ignored his mutter about the cold food.
A quiet mutter spread through the crowd as the news was announced.
His only response was a disgruntled mutter under his breath.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grumble - a low, indistinct complaint or protest

Etymology

From Middle High German 'muteren', meaning to mumble, probably imitative of the sound.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07225752-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈmʌt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈmʌtə / (BrE)
to speak or make sounds in a low voice, especially when complaining or expressing dissatisfaction, so that it is difficult for others to hear clearly
speak quietly in a complaining way

Examples

She tends to mutter complaints about her boss when no one is listening.
He began to mutter under his breath after reading the email.
The students would mutter quietly about the difficult exam as they left the room.

Synset Examples

she grumbles when she feels overworked

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grumble - to complain about something in a quiet but slightly angry way
  • Synonym: to complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance
  • Antonym: shout - to speak loudly and with force

Etymology

Probably from Middle High German 'muteren' (to mumble).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00911172-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈmʌtər / (AmE), / ˈmʌtə / (BrE)
a low, continuous, and indistinct sound, often made when someone speaks quietly or murmurs without clear articulation
low, indistinct murmuring sound

Examples

There was a mutter of discontent among the crowd as the decision was announced.
The constant mutter of voices in the café made it hard to concentrate.
She could hear the faint mutter of the rain against the window.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murmur - a soft, indistinct, continuous sound

Etymology

From Middle English muteren, from Old French mutter, of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07401520-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈmʌtɚ / (AmE), / ˈmʌtə / (BrE)
to speak in a low, indistinct voice, often as a complaint or when not wanting to be heard clearly
speak in a low, indistinct voice

Examples

She began to mutter under her breath as the meeting dragged on.
He muttered something I couldn't quite catch before leaving the room.
Stop muttering and speak more clearly if you want me to understand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murmur - to say something in a soft, quiet voice; often suggesting continued low sound
  • Synonym: grumble - to complain or protest about something in a bad-tempered but typically muted way
  • Antonym: shout - to say something very loudly

Etymology

From Middle English 'muten' or 'mouten', probably of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet
