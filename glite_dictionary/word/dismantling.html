dismantling

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈmæntlɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪsˈmæntlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of taking something apart or removing the parts of something, especially machinery or a complex system
act of taking something apart

Examples

The dismantling of the factory was completed within two weeks.
They announced the dismantling of outdated computer systems across all offices.
The international treaty required the dismantling of certain missile sites.

Synset Examples

Russia and the United States discussed the dismantling of their nuclear weapons

Related Terms

  • Antonym: assembly - the action of putting something together
  • Synonym: disassembly - the process of taking something apart into its pieces

Etymology

From 'dismantle' + '-ing', with 'dismantle' from Old French 'desmanteler' (to tear down fortifications).
