to quash

1. VERB

/ kwɑʃ / (AmE), / kwɒʃ / (BrE)
to officially reject or declare something, especially a decision or ruling, as invalid
formally declare invalid
legal

Examples

The judge decided to quash the previous conviction due to new evidence.
The defense team filed a motion to quash the subpoena.
The committee voted to quash the proposal, citing serious procedural flaws.

Synset Examples

The contract was annulled
void a plea

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to annul - to make a legal agreement invalid
  • Antonym: to uphold - to confirm or support a decision or law

Etymology

From Old French 'casser, quasser' (to break), from Latin 'quassare' (to shatter).

2. VERB

/ kwɑʃ / (AmE), / kwɒʃ / (BrE)
to suppress something forcibly or put an end to it, especially by the use of authority or force
suppress or put down by force

Examples

The military was ordered to quash the rebellion before it spread to other regions.
The company tried to quash rumors about the new product's failure.
Protesters feared the government would quash their movement with strict new laws.

Synset Examples

The government quashes any attempt of an uprising
China keeps down her dissidents very efficiently
The rich landowners subjugated the peasants working the land

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suppress - to put an end to something by force
  • Synonym: to subdue - to overcome or bring under control, especially by force
  • Antonym: to encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to; to foster

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *quasser*, from Latin *quassare* (to shake violently).

Source: WordNet
