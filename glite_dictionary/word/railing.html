railing

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈreɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the materials for making rails or a structure consisting of rails collectively, typically serving as a barrier or support
material or structure made of rails

Examples

The iron railing around the park was recently painted black.
Please don't lean on the wooden railing; it hasn't been secured yet.
They replaced the old balcony railing with a modern glass one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rail - a bar of metal or wood used as part of a fence or barrier

Etymology

From 'rail' + '-ing', denoting an object or material consisting of rails.

2. NOUN

/ ˈreɪ.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈreɪ.lɪŋ / (BrE)
a barrier made of a horizontal bar supported by posts, often used at the edge of stairs, balconies, or platforms to prevent falls or mark boundaries
barrier made of bar and supports

Examples

She leaned over the railing to get a better view of the river below.
The old wooden railing along the stairs needed to be replaced for safety reasons.
Children are warned not to climb on the railing of the balcony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fence - a structure serving as an enclosure or barrier, typically outdoors
  • Synonym: parapet - a low protective wall along the edge of a roof, bridge, or balcony

Etymology

From rail (the bar forming the barrier) + -ing; 'rail' from Old French reille (bar), from Latin regula (straight stick).
