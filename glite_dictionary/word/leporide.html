leporide

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛpəˌraɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɛpəˌraɪd / (BrE)
A red breed of domestic rabbit produced as a hybrid between the Old World rabbit and a hare.
hybrid breed of domestic rabbit

Examples

The scientist studied the genetics of the leporide to understand hybrid vigor.
Many breeders value the leporide for its unique red coat and docile nature.
The exhibition featured a rare leporide among other unusual rabbit breeds.

Etymology

From Latin *lepus* (hare) + -ide (denoting descendants or hybrids in taxonomy).
