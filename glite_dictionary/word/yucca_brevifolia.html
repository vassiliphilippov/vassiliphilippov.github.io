Yucca brevifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʌkə ˌbrɛvəˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈjʌkə ˌbrɛvɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A large, branched yucca plant native to the southwestern United States, with short leaves and clusters of greenish-white flowers; commonly known as the Joshua tree.
species of yucca, the Joshua tree

Examples

The Yucca brevifolia is an iconic sight in the Mojave Desert.
We stopped to photograph a flowering Yucca brevifolia along the hiking trail.
Many animals depend on the Yucca brevifolia for shelter and food in the desert ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Joshua tree - common name for Yucca brevifolia, a tree-like yucca of the southwestern US

Etymology

From Latin: 'yucca' (a plant genus) and 'brevifolia' (short-leafed), referring to its short leaves.
