sockeye

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑˌkaɪ / (AmE), / ˈsɒkaɪ / (BrE)
the edible red flesh of a Pacific salmon (Oncorhynchus nerka), valued for its rich flavor
edible red flesh of a Pacific salmon

Examples

We grilled fresh sockeye for dinner, enjoying its rich, flavorful texture.
The chef recommended using sockeye in the sushi for its vibrant color.
Canned sockeye can be an excellent addition to salads and pastas.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: salmon - the flesh of salmon fish, an edible pink fish often eaten as food

Etymology

From Halkomelem (a Salishan language) 'sθə́qəy̓', referring to this salmon species.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːkaɪ / (AmE), / ˈsɒkaɪ / (BrE)
a species of Pacific salmon with red flesh, valued as food, found in northern Pacific rivers; adults die after spawning
a type of Pacific salmon

Examples

Every summer, thousands of sockeye migrate upstream to their spawning grounds.
We had grilled sockeye for dinner, and its flavor was rich and distinctive.
The population of sockeye in this river has been steadily recovering in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salmon - a large edible fish found in the Atlantic and Pacific

Etymology

From Halkomelem (Salishan language) *sθə́qəy̓* (sockeye salmon).
