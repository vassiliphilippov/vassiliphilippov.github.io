lanceolate

1. ADJ

/ ˈlænsiˌoʊlət / (AmE), / ˈlɑːnsiəˌleɪt / (BrE)
(of a leaf) shaped like the head of a lance; long and narrow with both ends tapering to a point, especially the apex.
narrow, tapering leaf shape

Examples

The willow tree has lanceolate leaves that flutter in the wind.
Botanists identified the plant by its narrow, lanceolate foliage.
Many grasses display lanceolate leaf blades, aiding in water runoff.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ovate - egg-shaped leaf, broader at the base
  • Degree Lower: linear - long and very narrow leaf shape

Etymology

From Latin 'lanceolatus', from 'lancea' (lance, spear).
