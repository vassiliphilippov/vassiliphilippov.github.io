makeweight

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪkˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈmeɪkweɪt / (BrE)
a weight that is added to something, especially on a scale, to reach a required total weight
a weight added to reach a required total

Examples

The jeweler placed a makeweight on the scale to ensure an accurate measurement.
He used a small makeweight to bring the balance to exactly five kilograms.
A makeweight was necessary because the goods did not reach the minimum shipping weight.

Etymology

Formed from 'make' and 'weight'.

WordNet ID: oewn-03720149-n

2. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪkˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈmeɪkˌweɪt / (BrE)
an item or person added to complete the required amount or to fill out something, often considered of little importance compared to the rest
something added to complete a whole

Examples

Several unimportant facts were included in the report as makeweights.
She added some unnecessary items to the basket as makeweights to reach the minimum order.
A few of the interviewees were only there as makeweights to fill out the panel.

Synset Examples

some of the items in the collection are mere makeweights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filler - something used to fill space or make up a deficiency

Etymology

Formed from 'make' + 'weight' to indicate something added to reach a desired weight or total.
