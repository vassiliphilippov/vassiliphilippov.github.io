bundle

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌn.dl̩ / (AmE), / ˈbʌn.dl̩ / (BrE)
a large sum of money, especially as pay or profit
large sum of money (informal)
informal

Examples

She lost a bundle at the casino last weekend.
They spent a bundle renovating their old farmhouse.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fortune - a very large amount of money

3. NOUN

/ ˈbʌn.dl̩ / (AmE), / ˈbʌn.dl̩ / (BrE)
a collection of things tied or wrapped together for easy carrying or storage
a collection of items tied together

Examples

She carried a heavy bundle of newspapers under her arm.
The old man tied his clothes into a neat bundle before leaving the house.
Please leave the bundle of sticks by the door.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: package - an object or group of objects wrapped in paper or packed in a box
  • Synonym: parcel - an object or collection of objects wrapped in paper, usually to be sent by mail

Etymology

From Middle English 'bundel', probably from Middle Dutch 'bondel', diminutive of 'bonde' (bundle).

6. VERB

/ ˈbʌndəl / (AmE), / ˈbʌnd(ə)l / (BrE)
(historical) To sleep fully clothed in the same bed with one's betrothed, especially as a part of a courtship custom.
to sleep clothed with one's betrothed
archaic

Examples

In some Puritan communities, it was acceptable for couples to bundle before marriage as part of the courting ritual.
The novel describes a scene where the young couple bundles as their families look on.
Though considered old-fashioned now, the tradition of bundling once signified trust between betrothed partners.

Etymology

Related to the custom called 'bundling' in colonial America and Europe, from 'bundle' (noun).
