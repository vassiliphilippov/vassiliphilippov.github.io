Jutish

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːtɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːtɪʃ / (BrE)
the southern dialect of Old English spoken in the Anglo-Saxon kingdom of Kent
southern Old English dialect of Kent

Examples

The Jutish dialect contributed several unique words to the Kentish area.
Linguists study Jutish to better understand the development of Old English dialects.
The influences of Jutish can still be detected in some local Kent vocabulary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Old English - the earliest form of the English language, used from the 5th to 12th centuries

Etymology

From Jute, referring to the Jutes, a Germanic people who settled in Kent, plus the suffix -ish.
