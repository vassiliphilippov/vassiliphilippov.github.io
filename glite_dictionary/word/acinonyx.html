Acinonyx

1. NOUN

/ æsɪˈnoʊnɪks / (AmE), / ˌæsɪˈnɒnɪks / (BrE)
A genus of large cats that includes the cheetah, known for its speed and slender build.
genus of large cats (cheetahs)

Examples

The genus Acinonyx includes only one living species: the cheetah.
Researchers studied the evolutionary history of Acinonyx to understand its unique adaptations.
Fossils of extinct Acinonyx species have been discovered across Eurasia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheetah - a large, slender, spotted cat known for its speed

Etymology

From Greek akis (point) + onyx (claw), referring to the cheetah's semi-retractile claws.
