Ayapana triplinervis

1. NOUN

/ aɪ.əˈpɑː.nə trɪplɪnˈɛr.vɪs / (AmE), / aɪ.əˈpɑː.nə trɪplɪnˈɜː.vɪs / (BrE)
A low, spreading tropical American shrub with long slender leaves, sometimes used to make a mildly stimulating drink resembling tea.
tropical American shrub, source of herbal drink

Examples

The leaves of Ayapana triplinervis are often brewed to create a herbal tea in some South American regions.
Some botanists classify Ayapana triplinervis within the genus Eupatorium.
The mildly stimulating properties of Ayapana triplinervis make its infusion popular among locals.

Etymology

From the genus name Ayapana and the Latin species name triplinervis ('with three-nerved leaves').
