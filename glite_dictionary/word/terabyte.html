terabyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛrəˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɛərəˌbaɪt / (BrE)
A unit of digital information storage equal to 1,024 gigabytes or approximately one trillion bytes (2^40 bytes).
unit of digital information (about 1 trillion bytes)

Examples

The new server has a storage capacity of 12 terabytes for all our data.
A single terabyte can store hundreds of thousands of high-resolution photos.
Cloud services often offer plans with multiple terabytes of storage for users.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: gigabyte - a unit of information equal to 1,024 megabytes
  • Degree Higher: petabyte - a unit of information equal to 1,024 terabytes

Etymology

From 'tera-' (denoting a factor of one trillion) + 'byte', modelled after 'megabyte' and 'gigabyte'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛrəˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɛərəˌbaɪt / (BrE)
a unit of digital information equal to 1,000 gigabytes, or 1,000,000,000,000 bytes (10^12 bytes)
unit of digital information (1,000 gigabytes)

Examples

The new hard drive has a storage capacity of two terabytes.
Many modern laptops now come with at least one terabyte of storage.
Cloud services often offer several terabytes for backing up personal files.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: gigabyte - a unit of digital information equal to 1,000 megabytes
  • Degree Higher: petabyte - a unit of digital information equal to 1,000 terabytes

Etymology

From 'tera-' (prefix denoting one trillion) + 'byte'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13651137-n
Synset POS: n
