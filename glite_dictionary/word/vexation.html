vexation

1. NOUN

/ vɛkˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / vɛkˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
the state or act of being annoyed, frustrated, or worried; something that causes annoyance or distress
annoyance or irritation

Examples

To her great vexation, her luggage was lost at the airport.
He could not hide his vexation at being interrupted during the meeting.
The constant noise from the street became a source of vexation for the residents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annoyance - the feeling or state of being annoyed
  • Synonym: irritation - the state of feeling irritated
  • Antonym: delight - great pleasure, opposite of vexation

Etymology

From Latin 'vexatio' (harassment), from 'vexare' (to shake, disturb).
