declension

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈklɛnʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈklɛnʃən / (BrE)
a category of nouns, pronouns, or adjectives in certain languages that share the same pattern of inflectional endings
inflectional category of nouns/pronouns/adjectives

Examples

In Latin, nouns are grouped according to their declension patterns.
The first declension in Greek typically ends with -a or -ē.
Understanding the declension of a German noun helps determine its case.

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈklɛnʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈklɛnʃən / (BrE)
the set of grammatical changes (inflections) that nouns, pronouns, and adjectives undergo to indicate case, number, and gender, especially in Indo-European languages
grammatical inflection of nouns, adjectives, pronouns

Examples

Latin nouns follow a system of declension to indicate their grammatical role in a sentence.
You need to memorize the declension of this pronoun for your Greek exam.
The textbook includes a table of the noun declensions in Russian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conjugation - the inflection of verbs to show tense, person, number, etc.

Etymology

From Latin declinatio (a bending, inflection), from declinare (to bend away, turn aside).
