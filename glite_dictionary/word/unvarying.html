unvarying

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈvɛriɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈvɛːrɪɪŋ / (BrE)
not changing; always staying the same in nature, appearance, or degree
not changing; constant

Examples

The region enjoys an unvarying climate with little difference between seasons.
Her support for the project remained unvarying despite the challenges.
He performed his daily routines in an unvarying sequence every morning.

Synset Examples

maintained a constant temperature
principles of unvarying validity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constant - remaining the same over a period of time
  • Antonym: variable - likely to change; not consistent

Etymology

From un- (not) + vary (to change) + -ing; 19th century formation.

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈvɛriɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈvɛːrɪɪŋ / (BrE)
not changing or not capable of changing; always staying the same in form, character, or degree.
not changing; always the same

Examples

Despite the seasons, the region's climate remained unvarying throughout the year.
She spoke in an unvarying tone that made her lecture very monotonous.
The company's unvarying procedures ensured consistent quality in every product.

Synset Examples

a street of uniform tall white buildings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constant - unchanging over time or in effect
  • Antonym: variable - apt or liable to change; not constant

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'varying' (changing), from Latin 'variāre'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01973553-a
Synset POS: a
