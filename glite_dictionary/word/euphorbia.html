Euphorbia

1. NOUN

/ juˈfɔrbiə / (AmE), / juːˈfɔːbɪə / (BrE)
a large genus of flowering plants in the spurge family (Euphorbiaceae), characterized by varied forms and milky sap
genus of spurge plants with milky sap

Examples

Many gardeners appreciate Euphorbia for its unique foliage and drought tolerance.
The milky sap found in Euphorbia species can cause skin irritation if handled without gloves.
Some varieties of Euphorbia produce striking yellow-green flowers in the spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spurge - common name for plants in the genus Euphorbia

Etymology

Named after Euphorbus, a Greek physician of King Juba II of Mauritania.

2. NOUN

/ juˈfɔr.bi.ə / (AmE), / juːˈfɔː.bɪ.ə / (BrE)
A very large genus of diverse, mostly herbaceous plants in the spurge family (Euphorbiaceae), characterized by milky sap.
genus of diverse plants in the spurge family

Examples

Many gardeners value Euphorbia species for their striking foliage and drought tolerance.
The milky sap of Euphorbia plants can irritate the skin if touched.
Botanists classify poinsettias in the Euphorbia genus due to their similar floral structures.

Etymology

From Latin 'Euphorbia', named after Euphorbus, a Greek physician.
