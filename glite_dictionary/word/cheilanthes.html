Cheilanthes

1. NOUN

/ kiːˈænθiːz / (AmE), / kiːˈænθiːz / (BrE)
A genus of small evergreen ferns commonly known as lipferns, native to warm, dry regions.
genus of small evergreen ferns (lipferns)

Examples

Many species in the genus Cheilanthes are adapted to grow in rocky, arid environments.
Botanists recently discovered a new Cheilanthes species in the southwestern United States.
The delicate fronds of Cheilanthes make it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Etymology

From Greek 'cheilos' (lip) and 'anthos' (flower), referring to the lip-shaped indusia of the ferns.

2. NOUN

/ kiːˈlænθiːz / (AmE), / kiːˈlænθiːz / (BrE)
a genus of small evergreen ferns known as lipferns, often found in dry habitats, sometimes placed in family Polypodiaceae or Adiantaceae.
a genus of small evergreen ferns (lipferns)

Examples

The rocky hillside was covered with Cheilanthes, providing green cover even in the dry season.
Botanists discovered a rare species of Cheilanthes during their field research in the canyon.
In her greenhouse, she cultivated several varieties of Cheilanthes for educational purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - a non-flowering vascular plant with fronds
  • Degree Higher: Polypodiaceae - the family of ferns Polypodiaceae to which Cheilanthes is sometimes assigned
  • Degree Higher: Adiantaceae - the family of ferns Adiantaceae to which Cheilanthes is sometimes assigned

Etymology

From Greek kheilos (lip) + anthos (flower), referring to the lip-like shape of some frond parts.
