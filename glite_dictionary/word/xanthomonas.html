Xanthomonas

1. NOUN

/ zænˌθoʊˈmoʊnəs / (AmE), / zanˌθəˈməʊnəs / (BrE)
A genus of bacteria that produce a yellow pigment not soluble in water, similar to Pseudomonas, and known to affect plants.
genus of yellow-pigmented bacteria
medical

Examples

Some plant diseases are caused by Xanthomonas species that infect crops.
Laboratories often culture Xanthomonas to study its yellow pigment and agricultural impact.
The yellow color of the colonies suggested the presence of Xanthomonas in the soil sample.

Etymology

From Greek xanthos (yellow) + -monas (unit, single).

2. NOUN

/ zænˌθoʊˈmoʊnəs / (AmE), / zanˌθəˈməʊnəs / (BrE)
A genus of bacteria similar to Pseudomonas, characterized by the production of a yellow pigment that is not soluble in water.
genus of yellow-pigmented bacteria

Examples

Xanthomonas bacteria are known to cause leaf spots and blights in a variety of plants.
Researchers isolated a strain of Xanthomonas from the infected beans for further study.
The yellow pigment produced by Xanthomonas distinguishes it from other bacterial genera.

Etymology

From Greek 'xanthos' (yellow) and 'monas' (unit, monad), referring to the yellow pigment and single-celled nature.
