luscious

1. ADJ

/ ˈlʌʃəs / (AmE), / ˈlʌʃəs / (BrE)
having a rich, sweet, and pleasant taste or smell that is very pleasing to the senses, especially taste
richly pleasing to the taste or smell

Examples

The strawberries were so luscious that I couldn't stop eating them.
Her chocolate cake was famous for its luscious flavor and creamy texture.
We enjoyed a luscious meal made with fresh local ingredients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delicious - very pleasant to taste or smell
  • Antonym: bland - lacking strong flavor or taste

Etymology

From Late Latin *lūssus*, alteration of Latin *luxuriosus* (luxurious, wanton).

2. ADJ

/ ˈlʌʃəs / (AmE), / ˈlʌʃ.əs / (BrE)
sexually attractive in a way that is pleasing or appealing
sexually attractive; appealing

Examples

The actress played a luscious femme fatale who charmed everyone she met.
He couldn't take his eyes off the luscious model on the billboard.
The novel describes a luscious beauty who turns heads wherever she goes.

Synset Examples

juicy barmaids

a voluptuous woman
a toothsome blonde in a tight dress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voluptuous - curvaceous and sexually attractive (especially of a woman)

Etymology

From Middle English lucius, from Old French lusius (delicious), from Latin luxus, meaning 'luxury'.
