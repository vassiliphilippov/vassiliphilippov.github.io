to atone

1. VERB

/ əˈtoʊn / (AmE), / əˈtəʊn / (BrE)
to do something to show that you are sorry for having done something wrong; make amends or reparation
make amends; seek forgiveness

Examples

He tried to atone for his mistakes by volunteering at the shelter.
The company issued a public apology in an effort to atone for the data breach.
She spent years trying to atone for the hurt she caused her family.

Synset Examples

expiate one's sins

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expiate - to make amends or reparation for (guilt or wrongdoing)
  • Antonym: to condone - to accept or allow morally wrong behavior (antonym in sense of not making amends)

Etymology

From Middle English 'atonen' (to be in harmony, be at one), from the phrase 'at one.'

2. VERB

/ əˈtoʊn / (AmE), / əˈtəʊn / (BrE)
to make amends or show remorse for a wrongdoing, especially by performing a penance or seeking forgiveness
make amends or seek forgiveness

Examples

He tried to atone for his mistakes by volunteering at the shelter.
The criminal sought to atone through years of community service and restitution.
On Yom Kippur, many people fast to atone for their sins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to repent - feel or express sincere regret or remorse about one's wrongdoing or sin
  • Synonym: to expiate - make amends or reparation for wrongdoing or guilt
  • Antonym: sin - an immoral act considered to be a transgression against divine law

Etymology

From Middle English 'atonen', from the phrase 'at one'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01800811-v
Synset POS: v
