ferrimagnetism

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛr.iˈmæɡ.nəˌtɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌfɛr.ɪˈmæɡ.nɪ.tɪ.zəm / (BrE)
A form of magnetism in which the magnetic moments of atoms in a material are opposed, as in antiferromagnetism, but unequal so that a net magnetic moment remains.
form of magnetism with unequal opposing moments
medical

Examples

Many ferrites display ferrimagnetism and are used in electronic components.
The discovery of ferrimagnetism helped scientists develop better magnetic materials.
Yttrium iron garnet is a classic example of a material that exhibits ferrimagnetism.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: antiferromagnetism - magnetism where opposing moments cancel out completely
  • Degree Higher: ferromagnetism - magnetism where all moments align and reinforce each other

Etymology

From Latin 'ferrum' (iron) + 'magnetism'.
