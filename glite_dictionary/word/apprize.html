to apprize

1. VERB

/ əˈpraɪz / (AmE), / əˈprʌɪz / (BrE)
to inform or notify someone of something
inform; notify

Examples

Please apprize me of any changes to the meeting schedule.
The manager apprized the staff of the new safety protocols during today's briefing.
You should apprize your supervisor if you need to leave early.

Synset Examples

I advised him that the rent was due

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inform - to give someone facts or information
  • Synonym: to notify - to formally tell someone about something
  • Antonym: to conceal - to keep something secret or hidden

Etymology

From French 'appriser', from Latin 'appretiare' (to value, estimate; also associated with 'inform').

2. VERB

/ əˈpraɪz / (AmE), / əˈprʌɪz / (BrE)
to inform someone of something; to make aware
inform, make aware

Examples

The students were apprized of the changes to the curriculum via email.
Could you apprize your manager of the latest developments?
We must ensure all parties are apprized of the schedule before the event.

Synset Examples

Have the students been apprised of the tuition hike?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inform - to give knowledge or information to someone
  • Synonym: to notify - to formally tell someone about something
  • Antonym: to conceal - to keep something secret; to not inform

Etymology

From French *appriser*, from Late Latin *appretiare* (to value, to appraise). Spelling influenced by 'apprise'.
