Francophobe

1. NOUN

/ ˈfræŋ.kəˌfoʊb / (AmE), / ˈfræŋ.kəfəʊb / (BrE)
A person who has a strong dislike or hatred of France or French people and culture.
person who hates France or things French

Examples

As a self-declared Francophobe, he refused to visit Paris despite his friends' insistence.
The columnist was accused of being a Francophobe after repeatedly criticizing French culture.
She never eats French food, claiming she's something of a Francophobe.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Francophile - a person who admires France or French people and culture

Etymology

Formed from French 'franco-' (France, French) and Greek '-phobe' (one who fears or dislikes).
