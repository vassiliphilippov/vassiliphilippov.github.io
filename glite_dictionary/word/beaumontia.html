Beaumontia

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈmɔːntiə / (AmE), / bəʊˈmɔːntiə / (BrE)
A small genus of evergreen woody vines native to the East Indies and Asia, noted for their large white, trumpet-shaped flowers.
genus of woody vines, East Indies and Asia

Examples

The Beaumontia climbs quickly, covering walls with its striking white blossoms.
Gardeners often plant Beaumontia in tropical landscapes for its decorative appearance.
A mature Beaumontia can provide dense shade and a beautiful floral display in spring.

Etymology

Named after Diana Beaumont, an English botanist and horticulturist (c. 1765–1831).
