illumination

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌluːməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌljuːmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
A painting or drawing used to decorate a book, especially in medieval manuscripts.
decorative painting in manuscripts

Examples

The medieval manuscript features an exquisite illumination of a saint surrounded by gold leaf.
Scholars examined the illuminations to understand the symbolism in the ancient texts.
Many libraries preserve books with colorful illumination adorning the margins and initials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illustration - a picture or diagram that helps make something clear

Etymology

From Latin 'illuminatio' (a lighting up), related to the practice of decorating manuscripts with bright colors and gold.

Examples

The poet wrote about his search for illumination through contemplation and prayer.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

4. NOUN

Examples
