factorization

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæk.tɚ.ɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌfæk.tər.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
(Mathematics) the process of breaking down an expression into a product of simpler factors that, when multiplied together, yield the original expression.
decomposition of an expression into factors
medical

Examples

The factorization of 12 is 2 × 2 × 3.
Understanding factorization is crucial for solving quadratic equations.
Prime factorization is often used in cryptography.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decomposition - the process of breaking down into simpler elements
  • Antonym: multiplication - the mathematical operation of combining two or more numbers to get a product; inverse of factorization

Etymology

From factor + -ization, formed from Latin 'factor' (maker, doer) and suffix '-ization'.
