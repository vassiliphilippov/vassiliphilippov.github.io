beggarweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛɡərˌwid / (AmE), / ˈbɛɡəwiːd / (BrE)
A West Indian plant, cultivated in the southern United States as forage and as a soil improver.
West Indian forage and soil-improving plant

Examples

Farmers often plant beggarweed to enrich the soil and feed their livestock.
The field was covered with lush beggarweed after last summer's planting.
Beggarweed thrives in warm climates and is commonly used as green manure.
