Dagon

1. PROPN

/ ˈdeɪɡɒn / (AmE), / ˈdeɪɡɒn / (BrE)
an ancient god of agriculture and the earth, worshipped as the principal deity of the Philistines.
Philistine god of agriculture and earth

Examples

The temple of Dagon was central to the religious life of the Philistines.
In the ancient texts, Dagon is described as a god of agriculture and fertility.
Samson brought down the pillars of the Dagon temple as told in the Bible.

Etymology

From Hebrew Dāgōn, possibly related to Hebrew 'dāg' meaning 'fish' or 'grain'.
