uratemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌjʊrəˈtiːmiə / (AmE), / ˌjʊərəˈtiːmiə / (BrE)
The presence of abnormally high levels of uric acid salts in the blood, often associated with gout.
high uric acid in the blood
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered blood tests to check for uratemia as part of the patient's gout diagnosis.
Untreated uratemia can lead to painful joint inflammation and kidney problems.
Medications and dietary changes are often prescribed to manage uratemia in patients with chronic gout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyperuricemia - excess of uric acid in the blood
  • Synonym: gout - disease caused by deposition of uric acid crystals in joints

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'uric' (from urine) and Greek 'haima' (blood).
