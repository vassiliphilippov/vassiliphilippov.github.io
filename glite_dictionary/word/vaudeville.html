vaudeville

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɔd(ə)ˌvɪl / (AmE), / ˈvɔːd(ə)vɪl / (BrE)
a type of entertainment show featuring a variety of acts such as songs, comedy, and dance, popular chiefly in the early 20th century
a variety entertainment show with songs and comedy

Examples

Many entertainers in the early 1900s got their start performing in vaudeville shows.
The museum features rare posters from the golden age of vaudeville.
Some modern comedians trace their performance style back to the traditions of vaudeville.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: variety show - a performance with a variety of acts, such as singing, dancing, and comedy

Etymology

From French vaudeville, originally a popular ballad or satirical song, later referring to theatrical variety entertainment.
