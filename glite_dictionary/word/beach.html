beach

1. VERB

/ biːtʃ / (AmE), / biːtʃ / (BrE)
(of a ship, boat, animal, or object) to come or be brought onto a beach or shore; to run aground on land beside water.
come or be brought onto a beach

Examples

The fishermen decided to beach their boat before the storm arrived.
A large whale was found beached on the shore early this morning.
During the evacuation, several landing craft beached along the coast.

Synset Examples

the ship beached near the port

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strand - to drive or leave (a ship, animal, etc.) ashore or on land

2. NOUN

/ biːtʃ / (AmE), / biːtʃ / (BrE)
An area of sand or small stones beside a sea or lake.
sandy area by sea or lake

Examples

The children spent all afternoon building sandcastles on the beach.
After a long walk, we sat on the beach and watched the sunset.
Swimming at the beach is my favorite summer activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shore - the land along the edge of a sea, lake, or other large body of water
  • Synonym: coast - the land next to the sea; the seashore
  • Antonym: inland - away from the coast, toward the interior of a country

Etymology

From Old English 'bæce', perhaps related to 'beck' (stream).
