Trichechus manatus

1. NOUN

/ trɪˈkiːkəs məˈneɪtəs / (AmE), / trɪˈkiːkəs məˈneɪtəs / (BrE)
A large herbivorous aquatic mammal found in the tropical coastal waters of the Americas, commonly known as the West Indian manatee, with a rounded, flat tail.
West Indian manatee species

Examples

The Trichechus manatus is protected by law in many countries due to its endangered status.
We spotted a Trichechus manatus swimming peacefully near the mangroves during our boat tour.
Marine biologists study the habitat needs of the Trichechus manatus to help conserve its population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manatee - A large aquatic mammal of the genus Trichechus

Etymology

From New Latin, genus name 'Trichechus' (from Greek 'trikhekhos' meaning 'hair-lipped') and species name 'manatus' (Latin for 'having hands', referring to flippers).
