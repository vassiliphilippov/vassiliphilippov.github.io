blare

1. NOUN

/ blɛr / (AmE), / bleə / (BrE)
a loud, harsh, and often unpleasant noise
loud harsh noise

Examples

The blare of horns filled the busy street during rush hour.
She woke to the blare of her alarm clock early in the morning.
The blare from the loudspeakers made it hard to concentrate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blast - a sudden loud sound or noise
  • Synonym: din - a loud, unpleasant, and prolonged noise
  • Antonym: murmur - a soft, low, and continuous sound

2. VERB

/ blɛr / (AmE), / blɛə / (BrE)
(of a sound) to make a loud, harsh, and usually unpleasant noise.
make a loud, harsh noise

Examples

Sirens blared through the city as the fire trucks raced to the scene.
Music blared from the open windows of passing cars.
The loudspeaker blared announcements throughout the busy station.

Synset Examples

The horns of the taxis blared

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blast - to make a sudden, loud noise

3. VERB

/ blɛr / (AmE), / blɛə / (BrE)
to make a loud and harsh or strident sound
make a loud, harsh sound

Examples

The car horns blared in the traffic jam.
Music blared from the loudspeakers all afternoon.
The alarm blared suddenly, startling everyone in the building.

Synset Examples

She tended to blast when speaking into a microphone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blast - to make a loud, forceful sound or noise
  • Antonym: murmur - to make a soft, low, and indistinct sound

Etymology

Of imitative origin, representing a harsh noise.
