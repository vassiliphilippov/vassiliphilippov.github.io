viverrine

1. NOUN

/ vɪˈvɛrˌin / (AmE), / vɪˈvɛr.iːn / (BrE)
A small, cat-like, predatory mammal of the Old World, belonging to the family Viverridae, such as civets and genets.
small cat-like Old World mammal

Examples

The viverrine is an agile predator found in the dense forests of Southeast Asia.
Scientists believe the viverrine plays an important role in controlling insect populations.
Many viverrines resemble domestic cats but have longer bodies and bushy tails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: civet - a small, lean, mostly nocturnal mammal of the family Viverridae

Etymology

From Latin 'viverrinus', from 'viverra' (ferret).
