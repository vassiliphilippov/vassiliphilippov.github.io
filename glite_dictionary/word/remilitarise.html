to remilitarise

1. VERB

/ riːˈmɪlɪtəˌraɪz / (AmE), / riːˈmɪlɪtəraɪz / (BrE)
To equip or prepare a country or region with military forces again, after they have been disbanded or removed.
restore military forces to

Examples

After decades of peace, the country decided to remilitarise its borders in response to rising tensions.
Some policymakers opposed the effort to remilitarise the region, fearing it would escalate the conflict.
The treaty strictly prohibited any attempt to remilitarise the area following the war.

Synset Examples

Should Japan be remilitarized?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to demilitarise - to remove military forces from an area
  • Synonym: to militarise - to equip or prepare with military forces

Etymology

From re- (again) + militarise, based on Latin militaris (military).
