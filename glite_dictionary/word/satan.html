Satan

1. PROPN

/ ˈseɪtən / (AmE), / ˈseɪtən / (BrE)
In Judeo-Christian and Islamic religions, the chief spirit of evil and adversary of God; the tempter of humankind and master of Hell.
chief evil spirit; adversary of God

Examples

Many people believe that Satan is responsible for leading people into temptation and sin.
In literature, Satan is often depicted as a cunning and manipulative figure.
The preacher warned his congregation about the dangers of allowing Satan into their lives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devil - the supreme spirit of evil; synonym for Satan
  • Antonym: God - the supreme being in monotheistic religions; opposite spiritual force to Satan

Etymology

From Hebrew śāṭān (adversary, accuser), via Greek Satanas and Latin Satan.
