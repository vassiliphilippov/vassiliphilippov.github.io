Epilachna varivestis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈlæknə ˌvɛrɪˈvɛstɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈlæknə ˌvɛrɪˈvɛstɪs / (BrE)
A species of beetle, commonly known as the Mexican bean beetle, originally from Mexico and introduced into the United States, which feeds on the foliage of bean plants.
species of beetle attacking bean plants

Examples

The Epilachna varivestis is a notorious pest among North American bean farmers.
Scientists have studied Epilachna varivestis to develop better pest control methods for crops.
Infestation by Epilachna varivestis can dramatically reduce the yield of bean plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mexican bean beetle - common name for Epilachna varivestis, a beetle species feeding on bean plants

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Epilachna' (genus of lady beetles) and specific epithet 'varivestis'.
