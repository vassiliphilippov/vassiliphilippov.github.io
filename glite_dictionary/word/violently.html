violently

1. ADV

/ ˈvaɪ.ələnt.li / (AmE), / ˈvaɪ.ə.lənt.li / (BrE)
In a way that involves the use of physical force, strong action, or intensity; in a violent manner.
in a violent manner

Examples

The protest ended abruptly when some participants began shouting and acting violently.
He shook the bottle violently before opening it, causing the drink to spray everywhere.
The storm struck violently overnight, damaging several houses in the town.

Synset Examples

they attacked violently

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vigorously - with great force or energy
  • Antonym: gently - in a mild or soft manner

Etymology

From 'violent', from Latin 'violentus' (vehement, forceful), with adverbial -ly suffix.
