rabidly

1. ADV

/ ˈræbɪdli / (AmE), / ˈræbɪdli / (BrE)
In an extremely passionate or fanatical way; with intense or excessive enthusiasm.
in an extreme or fanatical way

Examples

The fans cheered rabidly for their team throughout the entire game.
Some people are rabidly opposed to any changes in tradition.
He defended his opinion rabidly, refusing to listen to anyone else's arguments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fanatically - in a way that shows extreme enthusiasm or devotion
  • Antonym: moderately - in a way that shows moderate or restrained enthusiasm

Etymology

Formed from 'rabid' (from Latin rabidus, 'raging, mad') + adverbial suffix '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ ˈræbɪdli / (AmE), / ˈræbɪdli / (BrE)
In an extremely aggressive, fanatical, or intense manner, like that of an animal affected by rabies.
in an extremely aggressive or fanatical manner

Examples

The fans cheered rabidly for their team after each goal was scored.
He argued rabidly against any changes to the policy, unwilling to listen to other viewpoints.
Rumors spread rabidly through the office, causing unnecessary panic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fanatically - in a fanatical manner
  • Antonym: calmly - in a calm manner

Etymology

From 'rabid' + '-ly', where 'rabid' originates from Latin 'rabidus' (raging, mad).
