courbaril

1. NOUN

/ kʊr.bəˈril / (AmE), / ˌkɔː.bəˈriːl / (BrE)
A West Indian locust tree (Hymenaea courbaril) with pinnate leaves and large white or purplish flowers, valued for its very hard wood.
West Indian locust tree; source of hard wood

Examples

The wood of the courbaril is prized for its strength and durability in construction.
Botanists studied the flowering cycles of the courbaril in the Caribbean rainforest.
A piece of furniture made from courbaril can last for generations.

Etymology

From French 'courbaril', from Cariban (Kalina) origin.
