Parkinsonia florida

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːrkɪnˈsoʊniə ˈflɔːrɪdə / (AmE), / ˌpɑːkɪnˈsəʊniə ˈflɒrɪdə / (BrE)
A densely branched spiny tree of the southwestern United States, known for its showy yellow flowers and blue-green bark; sometimes placed in the genus Cercidium.
spiny tree with yellow flowers, U.S. Southwest

Examples

The Parkinsonia florida is easily recognized by its blue-green bark and vibrant yellow blooms in spring.
Many desert gardens feature Parkinsonia florida for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
In the Sonoran Desert, the Parkinsonia florida serves as an important habitat for local wildlife.

Etymology

From the genus name Parkinsonia, named after English botanist John Parkinson, and the Latin 'florida' meaning 'flowery'.
