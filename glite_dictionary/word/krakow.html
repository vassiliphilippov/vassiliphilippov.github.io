Krakow

1. PROPN

/ ˈkrɑːˌkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈkræk.aʊ / (BrE)
A major city in southern Poland, situated on the Vistula River, known for its historic architecture and cultural significance.
city in southern Poland

Examples

Many tourists visit Krakow each year to see its medieval Old Town and the Wawel Castle.
She moved to Krakow for university and quickly fell in love with the city.
The conference will be held in Krakow this September, attracting experts from around the world.

Etymology

From Polish 'Kraków,' named after the legendary founder Krakus.
