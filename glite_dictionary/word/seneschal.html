seneschal

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnɪʃəl / (AmE), / ˈsɛnɪʃəl / (BrE)
the chief steward or officer in charge of domestic arrangements in a great household, especially in medieval times
chief steward of a great household
archaic

Examples

The seneschal was responsible for overseeing the vast estate and its many servants.
As seneschal, he took pride in organizing grand feasts for the lord and his guests.
In the absence of the duke, the seneschal governed the castle and its affairs efficiently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steward - a person who manages another's property or affairs
  • Synonym: butler - the chief male servant of a household

Etymology

From Old French 'seneschal', from Frankish *siniskalk (senior servant).
