lollipop

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːliˌpɑp / (AmE), / ˈlɒliˌpɒp / (BrE)
a hard, flavored candy mounted on a small stick and eaten by licking or sucking
hard candy on a stick

Examples

She gave her son a cherry lollipop as a reward for good behavior.
The children lined up to get a free lollipop from the booth at the fair.
He unwrapped his favorite grape lollipop and started to suck on it slowly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candy - sweet, confectionery treat made mainly from sugar

Etymology

Possibly from English dialect 'lolly' (tongue) + 'pop' (slap, hit); pop referencing the sound of licking.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːliˌpɑp / (AmE), / ˈlɒlɪpɒp / (BrE)
A piece of flavored ice cream or water ice on a small stick, eaten as a sweet treat.
ice cream or water ice on a stick

Examples

After school, the children bought a lollipop from the ice cream cart.
On hot days, I love sucking on a cold lollipop in the park.
In England, what Americans call a popsicle is usually called an ice lollipop.

Synset Examples

in England a popsicle is called an ‘ice lolly’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: popsicle - a flavored ice on a stick, chiefly US
  • Synonym: ice lolly - British term for a frozen flavored ice on a stick
