hakim

1. NOUN

/ hɑːˈkiːm / (AmE), / hɑːˈkiːm / (BrE)
(in Islamic societies) a Muslim ruler, governor, or judge.
Muslim ruler, governor, or judge

Examples

The hakim settled disputes in the village according to Islamic law.
After the appointment of a new hakim, local governance improved significantly.
Many people sought the hakim's advice on matters both legal and personal.

Etymology

From Arabic حاكم (*ḥākim*) meaning 'ruler' or 'judge'.

2. NOUN

/ hɑˈkim / (AmE), / hɑːˈkiːm / (BrE)
A Muslim physician, especially one practicing traditional medicine such as Unani.
Muslim physician (often Unani medicine)

Examples

The hakim prescribed herbal remedies for the patient's illness.
In traditional communities, a hakim holds great respect as a healer and advisor.
She visited a renowned hakim to seek treatment using Unani medicine.

Etymology

From Arabic ḥakīm (wise, physician).
