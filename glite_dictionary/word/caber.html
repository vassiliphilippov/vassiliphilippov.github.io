caber

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪbər / (AmE), / ˈkeɪbə / (BrE)
a heavy, trimmed wooden pole, especially the trunk of a young tree, that is tossed as a test of strength in Scottish Highland games
wooden pole used in Scottish Highland games

Examples

The athlete skillfully tossed the caber during the Highland games.
Tossing the caber requires both strength and technique.
Spectators watched as the caber flipped end over end through the air.

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic 'cabar' (pole, beam).
