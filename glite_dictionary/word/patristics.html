patristics

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtrɪstɪks / (AmE), / pəˈtrɪstɪks / (BrE)
the branch of theology concerned with the study of the lives, writings, and doctrines of the Church Fathers
study of the early Church Fathers

Examples

She specialized in patristics as part of her theological studies at university.
The seminar will cover major themes in patristics including Augustine's work.
His research into patristics shed new light on early Christian doctrine.

Etymology

From Latin 'pater' (father) plus '-istics' (study of).

2. NOUN

/ pəˈtrɪstɪks / (AmE), / pəˈtrɪstɪks / (BrE)
the study of the writings and teachings of the early Christian Church Fathers
study of early Church Fathers' writings

Examples

She decided to specialize in patristics for her graduate studies in theology.
Many theological debates are rooted in patristics and the interpretation of early Church writings.
The university offers a seminar on patristics focusing on the thoughts of Augustine and Jerome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patrology - the study of early Christian writers and their works

Etymology

From Latin 'pater' (father) with the academic suffix '-istics', denoting a field of study.
