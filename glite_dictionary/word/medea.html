Medea

1. PROPN

/ məˈdiːə / (AmE), / mɪˈdiːə / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a princess of Colchis who aided Jason in obtaining the Golden Fleece and is renowned for her subsequent tragic story.
mythical princess of Colchis

Examples

The story of Medea has been retold by countless playwrights and writers throughout history.
Medea is remembered for her complex character and her dramatic role in the legend of Jason and the Argonauts.
Many scholars analyze Medea's actions in the myth to explore themes of betrayal and revenge.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Μήδεια (Mēdeia), meaning 'cunning' or 'crafty'.
