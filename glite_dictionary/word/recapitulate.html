to recapitulate

1. VERB

/ ˌriːkəˈpɪtʃəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːkəˈpɪtjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to state the main points of something again in a brief way; to summarize briefly
summarize briefly

Examples

To conclude, let me recapitulate the essential arguments presented today.
The professor will recapitulate the previous lecture at the beginning of class.
She recapitulated the project goals for the new team members.

Synset Examples

Let's recapitulate the main ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to summarize - to give a brief statement of the main points

Etymology

From Latin recapitulare, from re- (again) + capitulum (chapter, heading).

2. VERB

/ ˌriːkəˈpɪtʃəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːkəˈpɪtjʊleɪt / (BrE)
(Biology) To repeat stages of evolutionary development during the embryonic phase of life.
repeat evolutionary stages in embryo (biology)

Examples

According to the outdated theory, embryos recapitulate the evolutionary stages of their species.
Some biologists once claimed that human embryos recapitulate traits seen in other animals during development.
The notion that embryonic growth recapitulates evolutionary history has been largely discredited in modern science.

Etymology

From Latin recapitulare, from re- (again) + capitulum (chapter, main part).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01739025-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌriː.kəˈpɪt͡ʃ.ə.leɪt / (AmE), / ˌriː.kəˈpɪt.jʊ.leɪt / (BrE)
(in music or writing) to repeat or restate the main theme or point in a composition, often towards the end.
repeat or restate main theme

Examples

The composer chose to recapitulate the main motif in the final movement.
She decided to recapitulate her main arguments before concluding the article.
In the final section, the piece recapitulates the opening melody with greater intensity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to summarize - to give a brief statement of the main points

Etymology

From Late Latin recapitulari, from Latin re- (again) + capitulum (chapter, heading).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01731369-v
Synset POS: v
