creamy

1. ADJ

/ ˈkriːmi / (AmE), / ˈkriːmi / (BrE)
having the pale yellowish-white color of cream
pale cream color

Examples

The walls were painted a creamy shade that brightened the room.
She chose a creamy dress for the summer wedding.
The cat's fur had a soft, creamy hue.

Synset Examples

creamy translucent pebbles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: off-white - a white color with a slight tint, not pure white

Etymology

From cream + -y, meaning 'like cream' in color or texture.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkriːmi / (AmE), / ˈkriːmi / (BrE)
having a smooth, thick, and rich texture like that of cream
smooth and thick like cream

Examples

This soup has a creamy texture that makes it very satisfying.
She prefers creamy desserts like cheesecake and mousse.
The lotion felt creamy as I rubbed it into my skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rich - having a pleasingly smooth, dense quality in food or beverage
  • Antonym: watery - thin or diluted in texture or taste

Etymology

From 'cream' + '-y', meaning 'full of' or 'having the qualities of'.

Synset POS: s
