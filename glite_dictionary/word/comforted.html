comforted

1. ADJ

/ ˈkʌm.fɚ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈkʌm.fət.ɪd / (BrE)
feeling less distressed because someone or something has given support or relief
feeling less distressed; reassured

Examples

She felt comforted by her friend's kind words after the bad news.
He was comforted knowing that his family was safe during the storm.
The child's tears dried as she was comforted by her mother's embrace.

Synset Examples

the news make her feel comforted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reassured - made less worried or anxious
  • Antonym: distressed - feeling extreme anxiety, sorrow, or pain

Etymology

Past participle of 'comfort', from Old French conforter (to strengthen).
