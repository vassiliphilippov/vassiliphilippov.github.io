to destruct

1. VERB

/ dɪˈstrʌkt / (AmE), / dɪˈstrʌkt / (BrE)
to deliberately destroy or cause the destruction of something, especially by an internal or controlled process
deliberately destroy or cause destruction

Examples

The engineers decided to destruct the old bridge using explosives.
When the missile failed mid-flight, it was remotely destructed for safety reasons.
Some files are programmed to destruct automatically after being read.

Synset Examples

The fire destroyed the house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to destroy - to put an end to the existence of something by damaging it or attacking it

Etymology

Back-formation from 'destruction', influenced by Latin 'destructus' (pulled down, destroyed).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈstrʌkt / (AmE), / dɪˈstrʌkt / (BrE)
(especially in aerospace and military contexts) to deliberately destroy one's own missile, rocket, or similar object, often for safety or security reasons.
deliberately destroy one's own missile or rocket

Examples

After the malfunction, engineers had to destruct the rocket to prevent it from crashing into a populated area.
Mission control decided to destruct the missile remotely due to a loss of guidance.
If an unauthorized course is detected, protocols require the operator to destruct the launch vehicle.

Synset Examples

The engineers had to destruct the rocket for safety reasons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to destroy - to put an end to the existence of something by damaging or attacking it

Etymology

Back-formation from 'destruction,' likely influenced by military jargon.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01623725-v
Synset POS: v
