Richard III

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɪtʃərd ðə θɜrd / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃəd ðə θɜːd / (BrE)
(1452–1485) King of England from 1483 to 1485, known for seizing the throne from his nephew Edward V and for being defeated by Henry Tudor at the Battle of Bosworth Field.
King of England (1483–1485)

Examples

William Shakespeare wrote a famous play about Richard III that explores his rise and fall.
Historians continue to debate whether Richard III was truly responsible for the disappearance of the princes in the Tower.
The remains of Richard III were discovered beneath a parking lot in Leicester in 2012.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Richard II - King of England from 1377 to 1399
  • Antonym: Henry VII - King who defeated Richard III at Bosworth Field
