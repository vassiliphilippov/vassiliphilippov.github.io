multi-colour

1. ADJ

/ ˌmʌltiˈkʌlɚ / (AmE), / ˌmʌltiˈkʌlə / (BrE)
having several colors or patches of different colors, often bright or contrasting
having several different colors

Examples

The children wore multi-colour hats to the festival, making the crowd look like a rainbow.
She bought a multi-colour rug to brighten up the living room.
A flock of multi-colour birds gathered in the garden, attracting everyone's attention.

Synset Examples

a jester dressed in motley
the painted desert
a particolored dress
a piebald horse
pied daisies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multicolored - having many colors or differently colored sections
  • Antonym: monochrome - having or consisting of only one color

Etymology

From Latin 'multus' (many) and Old French 'colour' (color).
