Fraxinus caroliniana

1. NOUN

/ ˈfræksɪnəs ˌkærəˌlaɪniˈænə / (AmE), / ˈfræksɪnəs ˌkærəˌlɪniˈɑːnə / (BrE)
A species of small ash tree native to swampy areas of the southeastern United States, also known as the Carolina ash.
species of ash tree (Carolina ash)

Examples

The Fraxinus caroliniana is commonly found in wet woodlands and swampy regions of Florida.
Botanists conducted a study on how Fraxinus caroliniana adapts to periodic flooding.
Landscapers sometimes use Fraxinus caroliniana in restoration projects for its tolerance of moist soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Carolina ash - Common name for Fraxinus caroliniana

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Fraxinus (ash) and species Latinized from Carolina, referring to the region.
