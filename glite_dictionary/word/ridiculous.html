ridiculous

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈdɪkjələs / (AmE), / rɪˈdɪkjʊləs / (BrE)
deserving to be laughed at or regarded with scorn because of being absurd or unreasonable
absurd or laughable; deserving scorn

Examples

It was ridiculous to expect him to finish the project in one evening.
Her ridiculous hat drew laughter from everyone at the party.
He looked ridiculous trying to dance in those oversized shoes.

Synset Examples

how silly an ardent and unsuccessful wooer can be especially if he is getting on in years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absurd - wildly unreasonable, illogical, or inappropriate
  • Synonym: ludicrous - so foolish or unreasonable as to be amusing
  • Antonym: reasonable - having sound judgment; fair and sensible

Etymology

From Latin 'ridiculosus' (laughable), from 'ridiculus', from 'ridere' (to laugh).

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈdɪkjələs / (AmE), / rɪˈdɪkjʊləs / (BrE)
deserving or inviting mockery or derision because of being absurd or unreasonable
deserving mockery; absurd or unreasonable

Examples

  • Antonym: serious - acting or spoken sincerely and in an earnest way

3. ADJ

/ rɪˈdɪkjələs / (AmE), / rɪˈdɪkjʊləs / (BrE)
Extremely silly, unreasonable, or absurd, often so much so as to be funny or laughable.
extremely silly or absurd

Examples

His explanation was so ridiculous that everyone burst out laughing.
The idea of wearing pajamas to a formal dinner seems ridiculous to me.
She made a ridiculous face and the children giggled.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ludicrous - so foolish, unreasonable, or out of place as to be amusing

Etymology

From Latin ridiculus (laughable), from ridere (to laugh).
