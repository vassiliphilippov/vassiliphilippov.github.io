Ramayana

1. PROPN

/ rɑːˈmɑːjənə / (AmE), / rɑːˈmɑːjənə / (BrE)
an ancient Hindu epic poem recounting the story of Rama's banishment, the abduction of his wife Sita by the demon Ravana, and his eventual return and restoration to the throne
ancient Hindu epic poem

Examples

Many scholars have analyzed the moral lessons found in the Ramayana.
The Ramayana continues to influence art, literature, and drama throughout South Asia.
He read a translation of the Ramayana during his world religions course.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mahabharata - another major ancient Hindu epic

Etymology

From Sanskrit Rāmāyaṇa, meaning 'Rama's journey.'
