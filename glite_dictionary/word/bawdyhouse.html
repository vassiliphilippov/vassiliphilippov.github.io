bawdyhouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔdiˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈbɔːdiˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A house or building where prostitutes are available; a brothel.
house where prostitutes work; brothel

Examples

The building was rumored to have once been a bawdyhouse during the Victorian era.
The police raided the bawdyhouse after complaints from local residents.
Literature from the 18th century often references the bawdyhouse as a common setting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brothel - a house of prostitution

Etymology

From 'bawdy' (obscene, lewd) + 'house', originating in Middle English.
