to delimit

1. VERB

/ dɪˈlɪmɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪmɪt / (BrE)
to set the boundaries or limits of something; to define or determine the essential qualities or extent of something
set the boundaries or define the limits of

Examples

The committee was asked to delimit the scope of the new project.
It can be difficult to delimit the essential qualities of abstract concepts.
Clear rules help to delimit the responsibilities of each team member.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to define - to state or describe exactly the nature, scope, or meaning of
  • Synonym: bound - to form the boundary or limit of something
  • Antonym: to expand - to make or become larger or more extensive

Etymology

From Late Latin 'delimitare' (to mark out the limits), from Latin 'de-' (off) + 'limitare' (to limit).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈlɪmɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪmɪt / (BrE)
to set, mark, or define the limits or boundaries of something
set or mark boundaries

Examples

The property was delimited by a wooden fence on all sides.
We need to delimit the area where construction is permitted.
Clearly delimiting each department's responsibilities helped to avoid confusion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to define - state or describe exactly the nature, scope, or meaning of
  • Synonym: outline - draw or describe the outer edge or shape of something
  • Antonym: to expand - make or become larger or more extensive; remove boundaries

Etymology

From Latin delimitare, from de- (away, off) + limitare (to limit).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00732286-v
Synset POS: v
