shortstop

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɔrtˌstɑp / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːtstɒp / (BrE)
the fielding position in baseball between second and third base, or the player who occupies this position
baseball position between second and third base

Examples

The team's new shortstop made an incredible double play in the seventh inning.
After practice, the shortstop worked on his throws to first base.
She earned a reputation as the best shortstop in the league.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: second baseman - player at the base between first and second
  • Synonym: third baseman - player at the base between second and third

Etymology

From the combination of 'short', meaning near, and 'stop', referring to stopping the ball; originally denoted a player positioned close to the infield to 'stop' short balls.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɔrtˌstɑp / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːtˌstɒp / (BrE)
(Baseball) the player who defends the area between second and third base.
baseball position between second and third base

Examples

The team's shortstop made an incredible diving catch to end the inning.
As a shortstop, she has lightning-fast reflexes and a strong throwing arm.
Many aspiring young players dream of playing shortstop in the major leagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: second baseman - the player who plays second base in baseball
  • Synonym: third baseman - the player who plays third base in baseball
