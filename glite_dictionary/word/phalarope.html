phalarope

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæl.əˌroʊp / (AmE), / ˈfal.ə.rəʊp / (BrE)
A small shorebird of the genus Phalaropus, known for its lobed toes and strong swimming ability, which breeds in the Arctic and winters in the tropics.
small swimming shorebird with lobed toes

Examples

The red-necked phalarope is admired for its unique feeding behavior and migratory patterns.
We spotted a flock of phalaropes swimming gracefully in the shallow lagoon.
During migration, the phalarope can travel thousands of miles between its breeding and wintering grounds.

Etymology

From French phalarope, from Greek phalaris (coot) + pous (foot).
