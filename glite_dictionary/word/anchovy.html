anchovy

1. NOUN

/ ˈænˌtʃoʊvi / (AmE), / ˈæn.tʃəʊ.vi / (BrE)
A small, silvery fish of the herring family, often preserved and used as a seasoning or appetizer.
small, salty fish used in cooking

Examples

She sprinkled anchovy fillets over the pizza for an extra burst of flavor.
The chef added a touch of anchovy paste to enhance the sauce.
Canned anchovy is a popular ingredient in Mediterranean cuisine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sardine - a small, oily fish used as food, often canned

Etymology

From Spanish 'anchoa', possibly from Basque 'antzua' (dry, salted fish).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæn.tʃoʊ.vi / (AmE), / ˈæn.tʃəʊ.vi / (BrE)
a small, herring-like fish found in tropical and temperate waters, often used as food and typically canned or made into paste.
small, edible fish often canned or in paste

Examples

She sprinkled chopped anchovy over the salad for a salty flavor.
The pizza was topped with olives, capers, and anchovy fillets.
Cooks often use anchovy paste to add depth to sauces and dressings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sardine - a small, oily fish often canned for food

Etymology

From Spanish 'anchoa', likely related to Basque 'antzua', meaning 'dry' or 'salted'.
