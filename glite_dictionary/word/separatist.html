separatist

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛp(ə)rətɪst / (AmE), / ˈsɛp(ə)rətɪst / (BrE)
Advocating or supporting separation from a larger group, organization, or country.
advocating separation from a group or country

Examples

The separatist movement gained momentum after the country's economic crisis.
He holds strongly separatist views and favors independence for the region.
A separatist group demanded a new policy of autonomy for its members.

Synset Examples

a breakaway faction

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unionist - opposing separation, supporting political or organizational unity

Etymology

From French séparatiste, from séparer (to separate), ultimately from Latin separare.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛpərətɪst / (AmE), / ˈsɛpərətɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports the separation of a group of people from a larger body on the basis of ethnicity, religion, or nationality
person who advocates separation from a larger group

Examples

The separatist movement in the region gained significant international attention.
Some separatist groups demand complete independence from the central government.
He was labeled a separatist after openly supporting the province's secession.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unionist - person who supports unity, especially political union
  • Synonym: secessionist - person who supports withdrawal, especially from a political entity

Etymology

From Late Latin separatus (separated), from Latin separare (to separate) + -ist.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10599482-n
Synset POS: n
