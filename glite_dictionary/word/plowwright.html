plowwright

1. NOUN

/ ˈplaʊˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈplaʊˌraɪt / (BrE)
a person who makes and repairs plows, especially as a traditional occupation
person who makes or repairs plows

Examples

The village plowwright was renowned for his expertly crafted farming tools.
In the past, every rural town relied on a skilled plowwright to maintain their equipment.
He apprenticed with a plowwright to learn the trade of making plows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blacksmith - a person who makes and repairs things in iron by hand

Etymology

From 'plow' + 'wright' (from Old English 'wyrhta', meaning worker or maker).
