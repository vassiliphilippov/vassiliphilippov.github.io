alternative

1. NOUN

/ əlˈtɝː.nə.t̬ɪv / (AmE), / ɒlˈtɜː.nə.tɪv / (BrE)
one of a number of different possibilities or choices from which only one can be selected
one of several possible choices

Examples

We need to consider every alternative before making a final decision.
With no other alternative available, she accepted the job offer.
Carpooling is an eco-friendly alternative to driving alone.

Synset Examples

what option did I have?
there is no other alternative
my only choice is to refuse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: option - a thing that may be chosen
  • Synonym: choice - an act of choosing between alternatives
  • Antonym: necessity - something that is essential or unavoidable

Etymology

From Medieval Latin alternativus (offering a choice), from Latin alternare (to do by turns).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05798949-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ɔlˈtɜrnətɪv / (AmE), / ɒlˈtɜːnətɪv / (BrE)
(of something) serving or used in place of another; available as another choice or option.
serving as another choice; substitute

Examples

We need an alternative solution if this plan fails.
Some people choose an alternative medicine approach to treat illness.
The company offered an alternative venue when the original site became unavailable.

Synset Examples

an alternative plan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substitute - serving in place of another; replacement
  • Antonym: main - primary; not substitute or alternative

Etymology

From Medieval Latin alternativus, from Latin alternare (to do by turns).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01859862-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ɔlˈtɝnətɪv / (AmE), / ɔːlˈtɜːnətɪv / (BrE)
(of ideas, activities, or ways of life) departing from or not conforming to the usual or established options; unconventional.
not conforming; unconventional

Examples

She chose to pursue an alternative career in art rather than a corporate job.
Their club promotes alternative forms of education outside the mainstream school system.
He prefers alternative remedies over standard Western medicine.

Synset Examples

an alternative life style

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conventional - following traditional or commonly accepted ideas or practices

Etymology

From Medieval Latin alternativus (offering one thing after another), from Latin alternare (to do by turns).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00611045-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ɔlˈtɝː.nə.tɪv / (AmE), / ɒlˈtɜː.nə.tɪv / (BrE)
(of two or more things) involving or necessitating a choice between mutually exclusive possibilities.
involving a choice between mutually exclusive options

Examples

The committee faced an alternative choice: either cut spending or raise taxes.
In such cases, there are only two alternative solutions to consider.
Their proposal offered several alternative methods for completing the project.

Synset Examples

alternative possibilities were neutrality or war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optional - available as a choice but not required
  • Antonym: mandatory - required by rule or law; not optional

Etymology

From Latin alternativus (offering one or the other of two) via French alternatif.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00566932-s
Synset POS: s
