retaliatory

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈtæliəˌtɔri / (AmE), / rɪˈtæl.i.ə.t(ə)r.i / (BrE)
done as a response to a previous attack or harmful action; intended as revenge or retribution.
relating to revenge or reprisal

Examples

The government imposed retaliatory tariffs on goods in response to the sanctions.
She sent a retaliatory email after receiving a harsh message from her supervisor.
Military officials warned that any attack would trigger a swift retaliatory response.

Synset Examples

retributive justice demands an eye for an eye

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retributive - relating to or involving retribution
  • Synonym: vengeful - seeking to harm someone in return for a perceived injury

Etymology

From Latin retaliare (to return like for like), from re- (back) + talis (such).
