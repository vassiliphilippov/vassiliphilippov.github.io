pacifist

1. ADJ

/ ˈpæsɪfɪst / (AmE), / ˈpæsɪfɪst / (BrE)
opposed to war or violence; favoring peaceful solutions.
opposed to war or violence

Examples

Her pacifist beliefs led her to protest against military action.
Even in the face of aggression, he maintained a pacifist stance.
The country's new leader is known for his pacifist policies and commitment to diplomacy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: militaristic - favoring war or military solutions
  • Synonym: peaceful - favoring peace; not violent or warlike

Etymology

From Latin 'pacificus' (making peace) via French 'pacifiste.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈpæsəfɪst / (AmE), / ˈpæsɪfɪst / (BrE)
A person who believes that violence, especially war, is unjustifiable and should be avoided in resolving disputes.
person opposed to violence or war

Examples

As a pacifist, she refused to support the military intervention.
The pacifist argued that dialogue, not violence, was the only way to bring lasting peace.
Throughout his life, Martin Luther King Jr. remained a committed pacifist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peace activist - person advocating peaceful methods to achieve goals
  • Antonym: warmonger - person who encourages or advocates war

Etymology

From Latin 'pacificus' (making peace), via French 'pacifiste'.

Synset POS: n
