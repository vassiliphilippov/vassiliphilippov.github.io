multiple

1. ADJ

/ ˈmʌltəpəl / (AmE), / ˈmʌltɪp(ə)l / (BrE)
involving or consisting of more than one part, person, or thing
more than one part or element

Examples

She suffered injuries to multiple parts of her body after the accident.
The company owns multiple office buildings across the city.
Scientists have identified multiple factors that contribute to climate change.

Synset Examples

multiple birth
multiple ownership
made multiple copies of the speech
his multiple achievements in public life
her multiple personalities
a pineapple is a multiple fruit

Related Terms

  • Antonym: single - one only; not multiple

Etymology

From Latin 'multiplex' (having many folds), from 'multi-' (many) + 'plex' (fold).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌltəpəl / (AmE), / ˈmʌltɪpl / (BrE)
A number that can be divided by another number without a remainder; the product of a given number and an integer.
number divisible by another

Examples

Twelve is a multiple of three, as twelve divided by three equals four.
When checking your answer, ask if the number is a multiple of five.
In the times tables, each result is a multiple of the starting number.

Synset Examples

36 is a multiple of 9

Related Terms

  • Antonym: factor - a number that divides another number exactly
  • Antonym: divisor - a number by which another number is divided

Etymology

From Latin 'multiplex' (having many folds), from 'multus' (much, many) + 'plex' (fold).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05868575-n
Synset POS: n
