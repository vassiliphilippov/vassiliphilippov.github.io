capitate

1. ADJ

/ ˈkæpɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈkæpɪteɪt / (BrE)
(of a part or organ) abruptly enlarged and rounded at the tip; having a head-like, globular end.
abruptly enlarged and rounded at the tip

Examples

The flower cluster had a capitate form, resembling a small rounded head.
Certain fungi develop a capitate structure at the end of their stalks.
The capitate gland is easily recognized by its swollen, rounded tip.

Etymology

From Latin 'capitatus', from 'caput' (head).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæpɪteɪt / (AmE), / ˈkapɪteɪt / (BrE)
the largest bone in the wrist, located in the center of the carpal bones, with a rounded head that articulates with the third metacarpal bone
largest wrist bone; central carpal bone
medical

Examples

The capitate is the largest of the carpals and lies at the center of the wrist.
Fractures of the capitate are rare compared to other carpal bones.
An X-ray revealed an injury to the capitate following the fall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carpal bone - any of the eight small bones that make up the wrist

Etymology

From Latin 'capitatus' (having a head), from 'caput' (head).
