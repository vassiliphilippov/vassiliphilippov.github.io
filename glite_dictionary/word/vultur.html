Vultur

1. NOUN

/ ˈvʌltʃər / (AmE), / ˈvʌltʃə / (BrE)
a genus of large birds of prey in the family Cathartidae, chiefly represented by the Andean condor
genus of large birds, including the Andean condor

Examples

The Vultur genus is noted for its impressive wingspan, especially in the Andean condor.
Research on the Vultur has contributed to conservation efforts for large birds of prey.
Members of the Vultur are generally scavengers, feeding primarily on carrion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Andean condor - a large South American vulture, the best-known species of genus Vultur
  • Synonym: vulture - any of several large birds of prey that feed chiefly on carrion

Etymology

From Latin 'vultur' meaning 'vulture'.
