rant

1. NOUN

/ rænt / (AmE), / rɑːnt / (BrE)
a loud, angry, or impassioned speech, especially one that is lengthy and delivered with strong emotion
loud, impassioned speech or tirade

Examples

The politician launched into a lengthy rant about government corruption.
After losing the game, he gave a furious rant in the locker room.
Her rant against the new policy was met with silence from the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tirade - a long, angry speech of criticism or accusation
  • Synonym: diatribe - a forceful and bitter verbal attack

Etymology

Origin unknown, possibly from Dutch 'randten' (to talk foolishly).

2. NOUN

/ rænt / (AmE), / rɑːnt / (BrE)
pompous, loud, or pretentious speech or writing, often characterized by anger or long-windedness.
pompous or angry speech or writing

Examples

His speech quickly turned into a lengthy rant against the government.
She launched into a rant about the poor service at the restaurant.
The article read more like a bitter rant than a reasoned argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tirade - a long, angry speech
  • Synonym: diatribe - a forceful and bitter verbal attack
  • Antonym: eulogy - a speech or writing in praise of someone

Etymology

Probably from Dutch 'ranten' (to talk nonsense, rave).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07101969-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ rænt / (AmE), / rɑːnt / (BrE)
to speak or shout at length in an angry, impassioned, or loud way
speak angrily or loudly at length

Examples

The coach began to rant about the team's poor performance during halftime.
She tends to rant when she's frustrated with work.
He was ranting on social media about the latest political scandal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rave - to speak or write in a wildly enthusiastic or extravagant way
  • Antonym: calm - to speak or behave in a quiet, peaceful way

Etymology

Probably from Dutch 'ranten' (to talk foolishly, rave).
