poultry

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊltri / (AmE), / ˈpəʊltri / (BrE)
the meat or flesh of chickens, turkeys, ducks, or geese that are raised for food
meat of domesticated birds

Examples

We bought fresh poultry from the farmer's market for dinner.
There are strict regulations on the storage and handling of poultry in restaurants.
Consuming undercooked poultry can lead to foodborne illnesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meat - the flesh of animals used as food

Etymology

From Old French 'pouleterie', from 'poulet' (young fowl), from Latin 'pullus' (young animal, chicken).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊltri / (AmE), / ˈpəʊltri / (BrE)
domesticated birds such as chickens, turkeys, ducks, and geese raised for their eggs or meat
domesticated birds for food

Examples

The farm specializes in raising poultry such as chickens and ducks.
Fresh poultry is available daily at the local farmers' market.
Many people prefer organic poultry for its taste and quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fowl - a bird, especially one used for food or hunted as game

Etymology

From Old French pouleterie, from poulet (young fowl), from Latin pullus (young animal).
