great-grandmother

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡreɪtˈɡrænˌmʌðər / (AmE), / ˌɡreɪtˈɡrænˌmʌðə / (BrE)
the mother of one's grandparent
mother of a grandparent

Examples

My great-grandmother was born in 1892 and lived to be one hundred years old.
Sarah shared stories her great-grandmother used to tell about life in the old country.
He keeps a photograph of his great-grandmother on the mantelpiece.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: great-grandfather - father of one's grandparent
  • Degree Lower: grandmother - mother of one's parent
  • Degree Higher: great-great-grandmother - mother of one's great-grandparent
