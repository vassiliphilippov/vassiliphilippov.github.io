Clupea harengus pallasii

1. NOUN

/ kluˈpiːə həˈrɛŋɡəs pæˈlasiː / (AmE), / kluˈpiːə həˈrɛŋɡəs pæˈlasiː / (BrE)
a subspecies of herring found in the northern Pacific Ocean, commonly known as Pacific herring, important as a food fish
Pacific herring subspecies; important food fish

Examples

The annual migration of Clupea harengus pallasii attracts commercial fishing fleets from around the world.
Researchers are studying the decline of Clupea harengus pallasii populations due to environmental changes.
Smoked Clupea harengus pallasii is a traditional delicacy in several Pacific coastal communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pacific herring - common name for Clupea harengus pallasii, an important food fish of the northern Pacific
  • Antonym: Atlantic herring - a related herring species in the North Atlantic

Etymology

From Latin: Clupea (herring genus), harengus (herring), and pallasii (after zoologist Peter Simon Pallas).
