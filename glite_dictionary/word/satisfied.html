satisfied

1. ADJ

/ ˈsætɪsˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈsætɪsfaɪd / (BrE)
pleased because you have achieved something or because something you wanted has happened
feeling pleasure from fulfillment or achievement

Examples

The manager was satisfied with the team's performance in the project.
She felt completely satisfied after finishing her meal.
Are you satisfied with the outcome of the negotiations?

Synset Examples

a satisfied customer

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dissatisfied - not pleased or content; feeling disappointment or unhappiness
  • Synonym: content - in a state of peaceful happiness; satisfied

Etymology

Past participle of 'satisfy', from Old French satisfaire, from Latin satisfacere ('to do enough').

2. ADJ

/ ˈsæt̬.ɪs.faɪd / (AmE), / ˈsæt.ɪs.faɪd / (BrE)
Having a need, desire, or appetite fully fulfilled; having one’s hunger, thirst, or curiosity allayed.
having a need or desire fulfilled

Examples

After a long drink of water, he finally felt satisfied.
A small bowl of soup was enough to leave her satisfied for hours.
Once his hunger was satisfied, he returned to work with renewed energy.

Synset Examples

his thirst quenched, he was able to continue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fulfilled - feeling that a need or desire has been met
  • Antonym: unsatisfied - not having one's needs or desires fulfilled

Etymology

From satisfy + -ed; 'satisfy' from Latin 'satisfacere' meaning to do enough.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01523756-s
Synset POS: s
