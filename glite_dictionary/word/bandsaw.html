bandsaw

1. NOUN

/ ˈbændˌsɔ / (AmE), / ˈbændˌsɔː / (BrE)
A power saw with a long, continuous toothed metal band stretched between two wheels, used to cut wood, metal, or other materials.
power saw with a continuous toothed band

Examples

He used a bandsaw to cut precise curves in the wooden panel.
The metal shop invested in a new industrial bandsaw for complex fabrication jobs.
Always wear safety goggles when operating the bandsaw.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jigsaw - a power tool with a reciprocating blade for cutting irregular curves
  • Antonym: handsaw - a saw with a straight blade operated by hand

Etymology

From 'band' (as in a loop or strip) + 'saw'.
