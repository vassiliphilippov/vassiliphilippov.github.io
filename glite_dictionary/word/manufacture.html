manufacture

1. VERB

/ ˌmæn.jəˈfæk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˌmæn.jʊˈfæk.tʃə / (BrE)
to make or produce something in large quantities, especially using machinery; to create in a mechanical or deliberate way
produce or create, often mechanically

Examples

The factory manufactures thousands of cars every year for export.
After his debut novel became a bestseller, the author began to manufacture plotlines to meet demand.
Some companies manufacture evidence to support their claims, which is both unethical and illegal.

Synset Examples

This novelist has been manufacturing his books following his initial success

Related Terms

  • Synonym: produce - to make or create something
  • Synonym: to fabricate - to construct or manufacture an industrial product

Etymology

From Latin 'manu factus', meaning 'made by hand'.

6. VERB

/ ˌmæn.jəˈfæk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˌmæn.jəˈfæk.tʃə / (BrE)
(of a living body or part) to produce (a substance) naturally within an organism.
produce (a substance) naturally in the body
medical

Examples

The liver manufactures several important proteins essential for blood clotting.
This plant can manufacture its own food through photosynthesis.
Some hormones are manufactured by specialized cells in the pancreas.

Synset Examples

this gland manufactures a specific substance only

Related Terms

  • Synonym: produce - to make or generate within the body
  • Synonym: to secrete - (of a cell or gland) to produce and release a substance

Etymology

From Latin 'manufactura' (a making by hand), from 'manus' (hand) + 'facere' (to make).
