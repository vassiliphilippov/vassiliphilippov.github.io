Actinomeris alternifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæktɪˈnɑːmərɪs ɔˌltɜrnɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˌæktɪˈnɒmərɪs ɔːlˌtɜːnɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A perennial herb with showy yellow flowers, native to the eastern United States.
perennial herb with yellow flowers

Examples

Many wildflower enthusiasts admire the Actinomeris alternifolia for its bright yellow blooms in late summer.
The Actinomeris alternifolia can often be found growing in moist meadows and along streams in the eastern United States.
Botanists sometimes refer to Actinomeris alternifolia by its common name, wingstem.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Actinomeris' + species epithet 'alternifolia' meaning 'alternate leaves' in Latin.
