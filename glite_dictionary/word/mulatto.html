mulatto

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɑːtoʊ / (AmE), / mjʊˈlætəʊ / (BrE)
a person with one Black parent and one White parent; this term is now regarded as offensive or outdated.
person of mixed Black and White parentage
offensive archaic

Examples

In 19th-century literature, the term mulatto appears frequently, though its use is now considered inappropriate.
The census once included a category for mulatto but it has since been removed.
He disliked the word mulatto because of its offensive and outdated connotations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biracial - having parents of two different races
  • Synonym: mixed-race - of mixed racial heritage, especially Black and White

Etymology

From Spanish 'mulato', probably from 'mulo' (mule), reflecting mixed heritage.
