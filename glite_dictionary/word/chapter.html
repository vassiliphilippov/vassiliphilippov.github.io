chapter

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæptɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃæptə / (BrE)
a distinct period or episode in history or in a person's life, often marking a significant change or development
distinct period in history or someone's life

Examples

Moving to a new city marked an exciting chapter in her life.
The country's independence was a significant chapter in its history.
They closed the chapter on their failed business and started again.

Synset Examples

the industrial revolution opened a new chapter in British history
the divorce was an ugly chapter in their relationship

Etymology

From Latin 'capitulum' (small head, chapter), diminutive of 'caput' (head).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæp.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃæp.tə / (BrE)
a main division of a book or other written work, usually numbered or titled
main division of a written work

Examples

She finished the first chapter of her novel before going to sleep.
Each chapter begins with a short quote from a famous author.
The textbook includes review questions at the end of every chapter.

Synset Examples

Examples

  • Synonym: episode - a distinct event or period in a sequence
  • Synonym: phase - a stage in a process or period of development
