clangor

1. VERB

/ ˈklæŋər / (AmE), / ˈklaŋə / (BrE)
(literary or rare) To make a loud, resonant, metallic noise; to clang.
make a loud, metallic noise

Examples

The cathedral bells clangor through the night, echoing across the silent town.
In the factory, the machines clangor with deafening regularity.
As the workers hammered the steel, their tools clangored against the metal beams.

Synset Examples

clanging metal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clang - produce a loud, resonant metallic sound

Etymology

From Latin 'clangor' (a ringing sound).

2. NOUN

/ ˈklæŋər / (AmE), / ˈklæŋə / (BrE)
a loud, continuous, and resonant noise, often a repeated or metallic clang
loud resonant noise

Examples

The clangor of the church bells echoed throughout the valley on Sunday morning.
Factories used to fill the town with a constant clangor as machines pounded metal day and night.
Amidst the clangor of the construction site, it was difficult to hear the supervisor's instructions.

Synset Examples

he could hear the clang of distant bells

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clamor - a loud and confused noise, especially that of people shouting
  • Synonym: din - a loud, unpleasant, and prolonged noise
  • Antonym: silence - absence of any sound or noise

Etymology

From Latin clangor (a ringing sound, noise), from clangere (to resound).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈklæŋɚ / (AmE), / ˈklæŋə / (BrE)
(of a bell or similar object) to make a loud, resonant, and often metallic noise.
make a loud, metallic noise

Examples

The church bells clangor in the quiet morning, waking the nearby residents.
The old elevator clangored as it reached the top floor, startling everyone.
As the machinery started up, it clangored loudly through the empty warehouse.

Synset Examples

the alarm clangored throughout the building

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clang - to make a loud, resonant metallic sound
  • Degree Lower: chime - to make a musical, ringing sound (like a bell)

Etymology

From Latin 'clangor' (a ringing, a resounding noise), from 'clangere' (to ring).

Synset POS: v
