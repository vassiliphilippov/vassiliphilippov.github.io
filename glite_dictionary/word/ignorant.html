ignorant

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪɡnərənt / (AmE), / ˈɪɡnərənt / (BrE)
lacking knowledge, information, or awareness about a particular thing
lacking knowledge or awareness

Examples

She remained ignorant of the changes until it was too late to respond.
Many people are ignorant about the environmental impact of their actions.
He admitted that he was ignorant of the local customs when he first arrived.

Synset Examples

he was completely ignorant of the circumstances
an unknowledgeable assistant
his rudeness was unwitting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unaware - not knowing about something
  • Antonym: knowledgeable - having knowledge; well-informed

Etymology

From Latin 'ignorant-', present participle of 'ignorare' (not know, be ignorant).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪɡnərənt / (AmE), / ˈɪɡnərənt / (BrE)
lacking knowledge or awareness in a particular subject or field; uneducated in the fundamentals of a given art or branch of learning.
lacking knowledge in a specific area

Examples

Many people remain ignorant of the basic principles of nutrition.
She admitted she was ignorant of the local customs before moving to the country.
Although he is talented at sports, he is completely ignorant of classical music.

Synset Examples

she is ignorant of quantum mechanics
he is musically illiterate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illiterate - unable to read or write; lacking knowledge in a particular subject

Etymology

From Latin ignorans, ignorant-, present participle of ignorare (not to know).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00834520-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈɪɡnɚənt / (AmE), / ˈɪɡnərənt / (BrE)
lacking knowledge, education, or awareness in general; uneducated or unsophisticated.
lacking knowledge or education

Examples

It is difficult to have a serious discussion with someone who is ignorant of basic facts.
Many ignorant people believed the superstition because they had little access to education.
She felt ignorant about art until she took a history class at university.

Synset Examples

an ignorant man
nescient of contemporary literature
an unlearned group incapable of understanding complex issues
exhibiting contempt for his unlettered companions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uneducated - lacking formal education or schooling
  • Antonym: knowledgeable - having or showing knowledge, especially through experience or education

Etymology

From Latin *ignorantem* (not knowing), from *ignorare* (to not know).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
