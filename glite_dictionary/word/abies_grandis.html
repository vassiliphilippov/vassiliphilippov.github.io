Abies grandis

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.bi.iːz ˈɡræn.dɪs / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.bi.iːz ˈɡræn.dɪs / (BrE)
A tall fir tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, characterized by long, curved branches and deep green leaves; also known as the grand fir.
a tall fir tree of northwest North America

Examples

The towering Abies grandis trees dominate many forests in the Pacific Northwest.
Botanists studied the needle pattern of Abies grandis to distinguish it from other firs.
The fragrant boughs of Abies grandis are often used in holiday decorations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grand fir - common name for Abies grandis
  • Degree Higher: fir - tree of the genus Abies, evergreen conifer

Etymology

From Latin: 'Abies' meaning fir tree and 'grandis' meaning large or great.
