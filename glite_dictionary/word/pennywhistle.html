pennywhistle

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛniˌwɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛniˌwɪsəl / (BrE)
A small, inexpensive fipple flute, typically made of metal, commonly used in folk and traditional music.
small, inexpensive fipple flute

Examples

She learned to play the pennywhistle as a child in her local music group.
The melody was carried by a cheerful pennywhistle at the festival.
He bought a pennywhistle from the market for just a few dollars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tin whistle - A similar type of inexpensive fipple flute, especially in Irish music

Etymology

From 'penny', referring to its affordability, and 'whistle', describing its flute-like nature.
