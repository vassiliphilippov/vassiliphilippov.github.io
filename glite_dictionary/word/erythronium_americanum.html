Erythronium americanum

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌrɪθroʊniəm əˌmɛrɪˈkænəm / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪθrəʊniəm əˌmerɪˈkænəm / (BrE)
An eastern North American plant of the lily family, commonly known as the yellow trout lily, having solitary yellow flowers marked with brown or purple and spotted interiors.
yellow-flowered eastern North American plant

Examples

The forest floor in spring was dotted with blooming Erythronium americanum.
Botanists admire the delicate yellow petals of Erythronium americanum in its native habitat.
Many gardeners plant Erythronium americanum for its attractive mottled leaves and early blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogtooth violet - common name for several Erythronium species

Etymology

From New Latin: Erythronium from Greek 'erythros' (red), Americanum meaning 'of America'.
