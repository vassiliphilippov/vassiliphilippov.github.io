Wieland

1. PROPN

/ ˈwiːlænd / (AmE), / ˈwiːlənd / (BrE)
In European mythology, a legendary smith and king of the elves, often identified with the Norse figure Volund.
legendary smith in European mythology

Examples

According to legend, Wieland crafted magical swords for kings and warriors.
The story of Wieland has inspired countless myths and operas across Europe.
Historians debate whether the legend of Wieland has roots in real ancient smiths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wayland - variant spelling of Wieland, legendary smith in English folklore
  • Synonym: Volund - Norse counterpart of Wieland, mythical blacksmith

Etymology

From Old High German Wieland, related to Norse Völundr; a legendary smith in Germanic folklore.
