clamber

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈklæmbə / (BrE)
an awkward and laborious climb, especially one made using both hands and feet
an awkward or laborious climb

Examples

The hikers faced a steep clamber up the muddy hillside.
After a difficult clamber over the rocks, they reached the summit.
Getting into the attic required an awkward clamber up the narrow ladder.

Synset Examples

reaching the crest was a real clamber

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scramble - a hurried or disorderly climb, often over rough ground

Etymology

From Middle English *clamberen*, frequentative of *clamben* (to climb), influenced by 'climb.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00326328-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈklæmbər / (AmE), / ˈklæmbə / (BrE)
to climb or move in an awkward and laborious way, using hands and feet
climb awkwardly using hands and feet

Examples

The children clambered over the large rocks at the beach.
She clambered up the ladder as quickly as she could.
Several hikers clambered down the steep hillside to reach the trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scramble - to move hurriedly or awkwardly, often using hands

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *clamberen*, related to 'climb'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01925765-v
Synset POS: v
