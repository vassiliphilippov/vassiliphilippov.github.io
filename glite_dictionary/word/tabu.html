tabu

1. NOUN

/ tæˈbu / (AmE), / tæˈbuː / (BrE)
A social or religious custom that prohibits particular practices, people, or things because they are considered sacred or forbidden.
prohibited practice due to sacred or cultural beliefs

Examples

Breaking a tabu was considered a serious offense in traditional Polynesian societies.
Certain areas of the island were under tabu and only priests could enter them.
Eating the sacred animal was strictly tabu for all members of the tribe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taboo - A social or religious prohibition; alternate, common spelling

Etymology

From Tongan or Fijian 'tabu', meaning 'forbidden'; related to Polynesian roots.

2. ADJ

/ təˈbu / (AmE), / təˈbuː / (BrE)
forbidden to be used or touched, especially due to social or religious custom, particularly in South Pacific cultures
forbidden by custom or religion

Examples

It was considered tabu for outsiders to enter the sacred grove.
In traditional ceremonies, certain foods are tabu for women to eat.
The islanders explained that touching the artifact was strictly tabu.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taboo - forbidden by social custom or religious practice

Etymology

From Tongan or Fijian tabu (forbidden), adopted into English with variant spelling.

3. NOUN

/ təˈbu / (AmE), / təˈbuː / (BrE)
A social or cultural prohibition or restriction that arises from custom or emotional aversion.
prohibition by social custom or feeling

Examples

Breaking a cultural tabu can have serious social consequences in some communities.
The discussion of death remains a tabu topic in many societies.
Over time, what was once considered a tabu can become widely accepted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taboo - something forbidden by social custom
  • Antonym: acceptable - allowed or not prohibited by society or custom

Etymology

From Tongan or Fijian 'tabu' (prohibited, sacred), introduced into English in the 18th century.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
