Aythya ferina

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.θi.ə fəˈraɪ.nə / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.θɪ.ə fəˈriː.nə / (BrE)
A species of heavy-bodied Old World diving duck with a grey-and-black body and reddish head, commonly known as the common pochard.
species of diving duck (common pochard)

Examples

The Aythya ferina is often seen on European lakes during the winter months.
Birdwatchers spotted a pair of Aythya ferina nesting along the riverbank.
Unlike surface-feeding ducks, the Aythya ferina dives underwater to feed on aquatic plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pochard - common name for several species of diving ducks, including Aythya ferina

Etymology

From New Latin, combining genus Aythya (from Greek αυθυια, kind of sea-bird) and Latin ferina (wild).
