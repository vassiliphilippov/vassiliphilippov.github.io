Cygnus buccinator

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪɡ.nəs bjuːkɪˈneɪtər / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡ.nəs bjuːkɪˈneɪ.tə / (BrE)
a large pure white wild swan native to western North America, known for its loud, trumpet-like call; also called the trumpeter swan
trumpeter swan species

Examples

The Cygnus buccinator is North America's largest native waterfowl.
Birdwatchers traveled to the reserve to catch a glimpse of the rare Cygnus buccinator.
Conservation efforts have helped the population of Cygnus buccinator make a remarkable recovery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trumpeter swan - large North American swan with a trumpet-like call

Etymology

From Latin: 'Cygnus' meaning 'swan', 'buccinator' meaning 'trumpeter'.
