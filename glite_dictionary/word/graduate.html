graduate

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡrædʒuˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡrædʒuːət / (BrE)
of or relating to advanced studies done after earning a bachelor's degree, especially at a university or college
relating to advanced post-bachelor studies

Examples

She is enrolled in several graduate courses this semester.
Most graduate students work as teaching assistants while completing their studies.
The university offers a wide range of graduate programs in the sciences and humanities.

Synset Examples

graduate courses

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'graduatus' (graduated), from 'gradus' (step); sense development in higher education in English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01214337-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡrædʒuˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡrædʒuːət / (BrE)
a person who has successfully completed a course of study at a school, college, or university and received a degree or diploma
person who has received a degree or diploma

Examples

After four years of hard work, she is now a proud graduate of Harvard University.
Every graduate received a diploma during the commencement ceremony.
As a recent graduate, he is seeking his first full-time job in engineering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alumnus - a male former student or graduate of a particular school, college, or university
  • Antonym: student - a person who is studying at a school or college

Etymology

From Medieval Latin graduatus (graduated), from Latin gradus (step, degree).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09805779-n
Synset POS: n

6. NOUN

/ ˈɡrædʒuət / (AmE), / ˈɡrædʒuət / (BrE)
a glass vessel, such as a cup or cylinder, with marked lines used to measure the volume of liquids accurately
measuring container for liquid volumes

Examples

The laboratory technician poured the solution into the graduate to measure exactly 100 milliliters.
Always use a clean graduate when measuring chemicals to avoid contamination.
She reached for the graduate to accurately dispense the proper amount of liquid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: measuring cylinder - a cylindrical container marked with a scale for measuring liquids

Etymology

From Latin 'graduatus' (marked with degrees, arranged in steps), referring to graduated marks on the vessel.
