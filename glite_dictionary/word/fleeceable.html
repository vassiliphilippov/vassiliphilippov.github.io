fleeceable

1. ADJ

/ ˈfliːsəbəl / (AmE), / ˈfliːsəbl̩ / (BrE)
Easily deceived or tricked, especially into being swindled or defrauded.
easily deceived or swindled

Examples

Many fleeceable investors fell for the scam without asking enough questions.
Scammers look for fleeceable people to target with their schemes.
She soon realized she was not as fleeceable as they thought.

Synset Examples

at that early age she had been gullible and in love

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gullible - easily persuaded to believe something
  • Synonym: credulous - too ready to believe things

Etymology

From "fleece" (to swindle), with the suffix -able, meaning able to be fleeced.
