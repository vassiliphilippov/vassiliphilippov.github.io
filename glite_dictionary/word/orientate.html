to orientate

1. VERB

/ ˈɔːriənˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈɔːrɪənteɪt / (BrE)
to determine or adjust one's position or direction relative to one's surroundings or some reference point
determine or adjust position relative to surroundings
british

Examples

The hikers stopped to orientate themselves using the map and compass.
New students are given a tour to help them orientate to campus life.
He had to orientate himself in the unfamiliar city before finding his hotel.

Synset Examples

We had to orient ourselves in the forest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to orient - to align or position something relative to points of the compass or surroundings

Etymology

Variant of 'orient', from Latin 'oriens' (rising, east), with '-ate' suffix.
