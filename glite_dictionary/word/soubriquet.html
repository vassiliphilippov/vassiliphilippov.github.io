soubriquet

1. NOUN

/ suˈbriːkeɪ / (AmE), / ˈsuːbrɪkeɪ / (BrE)
a familiar or affectionate name for a person, often a shortened or altered form of their full name; a nickname
nickname or familiar name
archaic

Examples

In his school days, his soubriquet was 'Tiny', though he was anything but small.
The actor is better known by his soubriquet than by his real name.
Throughout history, many rulers have acquired a soubriquet that reflects their deeds or personality.

Synset Examples

Joe's mother would not use his nickname and always called him Joseph
Henry's nickname was Slim

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nickname - a familiar or humorous name given to a person instead of or as well as their real name

Etymology

From French 'soubriquet', meaning 'nickname, jest, quip'.
