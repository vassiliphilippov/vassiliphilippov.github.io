incoming

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪnˌkʌmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌkʌmɪŋ / (BrE)
arriving at or coming to a place, especially as a new arrival or entrant
arriving; newly coming in

Examples

The incoming flight is expected to land in fifteen minutes.
All incoming mail is sorted at the front desk each morning.
The university welcomed the incoming class with an orientation session.

Synset Examples

incoming class
incoming mail

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outgoing - departing from a place or position

Etymology

Formed from 'in-' (into) + 'coming' (from the verb 'come').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01297423-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪnˌkʌmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌkʌmɪŋ / (BrE)
about to assume a position or office, especially as a successor to another person
about to take office; successor

Examples

The incoming president addressed the nation after the election results were announced.
Preparations are underway for the transition to the incoming administration.
The board met to introduce the incoming director to the staff.

Synset Examples

the incoming president

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outgoing - leaving or relinquishing a position or office

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'to come in' plus the suffix '-ing'; used since the early 19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01297174-a
Synset POS: a
