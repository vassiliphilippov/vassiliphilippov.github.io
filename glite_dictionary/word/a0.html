A0

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ ˈzɪroʊ / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ˈzɪərəʊ / (BrE)
A standard paper size measuring 841 x 1189 millimeters, part of the ISO A series.
standard ISO paper size (841 x 1189 mm)

Examples

Blueprints for large buildings are often printed on A0 sheets.
The poster was too big for regular frames, so we used an A0 size.
She ordered several A0 canvases for her art exhibition.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: A1 - paper size, 594 x 841 mm, one size smaller than A0
  • Degree Lower: A2 - paper size, 420 x 594 mm, two sizes smaller than A0

Etymology

From German DIN (Deutsches Institut für Normung) 476 standard, adopted internationally as ISO 216.
