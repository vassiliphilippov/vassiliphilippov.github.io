feisty

1. ADJ

/ ˈfaɪsti / (AmE), / ˈfaɪsti / (BrE)
Full of lively determination, courage, or spirit; energetic and often willing to fight or argue.
lively and courageous

Examples

Despite her small size, the feisty puppy barked at the much larger dog.
He admired her feisty attitude in the face of adversity.
The debate became heated when the feisty candidate challenged the others' views.

Synset Examples

the champion is faced with a feisty challenger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spirited - full of energy, enthusiasm, or courage
  • Antonym: timid - lacking courage or confidence

Etymology

Probably from obsolete feist (a small dog), from Middle English fyst, from Old English fīst (breaking wind).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfaɪsti / (AmE), / ˈfaɪsti / (BrE)
Having a lively, determined, and sometimes aggressive or easily offended disposition.
spirited and quick to take offense

Examples

Despite her small size, the feisty kitten hissed and arched her back at the dog.
Grandpa describes her as a feisty woman who never backed down from an argument.
He grew more feisty when his ideas were challenged in the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spirited - full of energy and enthusiasm
  • Antonym: timid - lacking in courage or self-confidence

Etymology

From dialectal feist (dog, small mutt), related to Old English fīst (fart), later applied to people.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
