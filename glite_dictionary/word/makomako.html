makomako

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːkoʊˈmɑːkoʊ / (AmE), / ˌmɑːkəʊˈmɑːkəʊ / (BrE)
a graceful deciduous shrub or small tree of New Zealand (Aristotelia serrata), with attractive foliage and small red berries that turn black at maturity, used for making wine
New Zealand shrub or small tree (Aristotelia serrata)

Examples

The makomako produces clusters of berries that are highly valued by local birds.
In traditional Māori culture, juice from the makomako berries was fermented to make wine.
We planted a makomako in our garden to attract native birds and add beauty to the landscape.

Etymology

From Māori makomako, the indigenous New Zealand name for Aristotelia serrata.
