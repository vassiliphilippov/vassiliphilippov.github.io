Victor Hugo

1. PROPN

/ ˈvɪktər ˈhjuːgoʊ / (AmE), / ˈvɪktə ˈhjuːgəʊ / (BrE)
(1802–1885) French poet, novelist, and dramatist, leader of the Romantic movement in France, best known for works such as 'Les Misérables' and 'The Hunchback of Notre-Dame'.
French Romantic poet, novelist, and dramatist

Examples

Many critics consider Victor Hugo one of the greatest writers of the nineteenth century.
'Les Misérables' was written by the celebrated French author Victor Hugo.
The poetry of Victor Hugo often reflects his political and social ideals.
