haemoglobinopathy

1. NOUN

/ hiːməˌɡloʊbɪˈnɑːpəθi / (AmE), / hiːməˌɡləʊbɪˈnɒpəθi / (BrE)
a disease of the blood characterized by the presence of abnormal hemoglobin molecules
blood disease with abnormal hemoglobin
medical british

Examples

Sickle cell anemia is a type of haemoglobinopathy that affects millions worldwide.
Genetic counseling is recommended for families with a history of haemoglobinopathy.
The patient was diagnosed with a rare haemoglobinopathy after specialized blood tests.

Etymology

From haemoglobin + -pathy, meaning disease or disorder.
