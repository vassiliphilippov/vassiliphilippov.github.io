emasculation

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌmæskjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌmæskjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the surgical removal of the testicles of a male animal; neutering by castration
removal of testicles in male animals
medical

Examples

The emasculation of the bull was performed by a veterinarian for health reasons.
Farmers often practice emasculation to manage livestock populations.
Proper aftercare is essential following emasculation to prevent infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: castration - removal of testes in a male animal or human

Etymology

From Latin 'emasculare' (to castrate), from 'e-' (out) and 'masculus' (male).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌmæskjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌmæskjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of depriving a man or something considered masculine of its strength, power, or male identity
loss of power or masculinity

Examples

The character's repeated failures led to a deep sense of emasculation.
Many critics argue that workplace discrimination can contribute to the emasculation of men.
Some viewed the loss of authority as a form of social emasculation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: castration - removal of the testes, causing loss of masculinity
  • Antonym: empowerment - giving power or confidence, especially to groups or individuals

Etymology

From Latin 'emasculare' (to emasculate), from 'e-' (out) + 'masculus' (male).
