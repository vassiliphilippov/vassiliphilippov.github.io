Lord Nelson

1. PROPN

/ lɔrd ˈnɛlsən / (AmE), / lɔːd ˈnɛlsən / (BrE)
(1758–1805) English admiral noted for his leadership and victories against the French during the Napoleonic Wars, especially at the Battle of Trafalgar, where he was mortally wounded.
famous English admiral (1758–1805)

Examples

The statue of Lord Nelson stands atop Nelson's Column in Trafalgar Square, London.
Many consider Lord Nelson one of Britain's greatest naval heroes for his victory at Trafalgar.
In history class, we discussed the strategic brilliance of Lord Nelson during the Napoleonic Wars.
