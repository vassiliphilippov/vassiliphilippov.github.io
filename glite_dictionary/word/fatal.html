fatal

1. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈfeɪtəl / (BrE)
causing death; resulting in someone’s death.
causing death

Examples

The victim suffered a fatal wound during the accident.
Failure to wear a seat belt can have fatal consequences.
The disease is fatal if left untreated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deadly - causing death or capable of causing death
  • Antonym: nonfatal - not causing death

Etymology

From Latin 'fatalis', from 'fatum' (fate, death).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00996879-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈfeɪtəl / (BrE)
(of events or actions) causing disaster or ruin; having extremely harmful or dire consequences.
causing ruin or disaster

Examples

The company made a fatal error that led to its bankruptcy.
A fatal decision during the negotiations ruined the project.
That kind of carelessness can have fatal consequences for everyone involved.

Synset Examples

the stock market crashed on Black Friday
a calamitous defeat
the battle was a disastrous end to a disastrous campaign
such doctrines, if true, would be absolutely fatal to my theory
it is fatal to enter any war without the will to win it
a fateful error

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disastrous - causing great harm, damage, or suffering
  • Synonym: calamitous - causing great damage or suffering
  • Antonym: fortunate - bringing good fortune; resulting in good outcomes

Etymology

From Latin fatalis (decreed by fate, deadly), from fatum (fate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01053787-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈfeɪtəl / (BrE)
Controlled or decreed by fate; predetermined and unchangeable.
decreed by fate; predetermined

Examples

Her life seemed to follow a fatal path, no matter what choices she made.
The novel deals with the idea of a fatal prophecy that cannot be avoided.
He considered their meeting to be a fatal coincidence, guided by destiny.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fated - determined by fate; certain to happen
  • Synonym: predestined - destined to happen in a particular way; predetermined

Etymology

From Latin fatalis (ordained by fate), from fatum (fate).

of decisive or momentous importance

Examples
