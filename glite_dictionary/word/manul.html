manul

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.nʊl / (AmE), / ˈmæn.ʊl / (BrE)
A small wildcat native to the mountains of Siberia, Tibet, and Mongolia, known for its dense fur and stocky body.
small wildcat of Siberia, Tibet, and Mongolia

Examples

The manul is well adapted to cold climates, thanks to its thick fur and squat body.
Photographers travel to remote locations hoping to catch a glimpse of the elusive manul.
The manul differs from other wildcats in both appearance and behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pallas's cat - another common name for the manul, a small wildcat of central Asia

Etymology

From Russian манул (manul), likely from Mongolian мануул (manuul).
