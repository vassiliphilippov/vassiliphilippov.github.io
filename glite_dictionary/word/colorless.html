colorless

1. ADJ

/ ˈkʌlɚləs / (AmE), / ˈkʌlələs / (BrE)
having little or no color; lacking brightness or vividness
lacking color or vividness

Examples

The solution became completely colorless after the chemical reaction.
His paintings were criticized as dull and colorless by the art critic.
A diamond is valued higher when it is as colorless as possible.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: colorful - having bright or distinct colors; full of color

Etymology

From 'color' + -less (without).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkʌlɚləs / (AmE), / ˈkʌlələs / (BrE)
Lacking in variety, interest, or distinctive qualities; dull or uninteresting.
dull or uninteresting; lacking variety

Examples

The speaker had a colorless voice that failed to engage the audience.
Critics called the film colorless, saying it lacked originality and excitement.
His memoir was surprisingly colorless, despite his adventurous life.

Synset Examples

a colorless and unimaginative person
a colorless description of the parade

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement
  • Synonym: drab - lacking brightness or interest; drearily dull
  • Antonym: vivid - producing powerful feelings or strong, clear images; lively
