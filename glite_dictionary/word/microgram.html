microgram

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪkroʊˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈmʌɪkrəʊˌɡram / (BrE)
a unit of mass equal to one millionth (1/1,000,000) of a gram
unit of mass; 1/1,000,000 gram

Examples

The dosage of vitamin B12 was measured in micrograms per tablet.
The laboratory detected only a few micrograms of the substance in the sample.
Patients should not exceed 500 micrograms of this medication daily.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: milligram - a unit of mass equal to one thousandth of a gram
  • Degree Lower: nanogram - a unit of mass equal to one billionth of a gram

Etymology

From micro- (meaning 'small', from Greek 'mikros') + gram (from Greek 'gramma', meaning 'small weight').
