purificatorium

1. NOUN

/ ˌpjʊrɪfəˈkɑːriəm / (AmE), / ˌpjʊərɪfəˈkɔːrɪəm / (BrE)
A white linen cloth used to wipe the chalice during the Eucharist, after each communicant partakes.
cloth for wiping the chalice in church

Examples

During the Mass, the priest carefully wiped the chalice with the purificatorium.
The altar server placed a clean purificatorium beside the sacred vessels before the service began.
After each communicant received wine, the deacon used the purificatorium to clean the chalice.

Etymology

From Late Latin purificatorium (from purificare 'to purify').
