microdot

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪkroʊˌdɑt / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊdɒt / (BrE)
A very small photograph, especially one reduced to the size of a dot, used to transmit or conceal information covertly.
tiny photograph for covert information

Examples

The spy hid the message on a microdot to avoid detection.
Scientists developed a new process to print images as small as a microdot.
The secret plans were reduced to a single microdot and smuggled out of the country.

Etymology

From micro- (very small) + dot (a small spot or mark).
