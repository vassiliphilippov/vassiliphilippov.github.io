antrorse

1. ADJ

/ ˈæntrɔrs / (AmE), / ˈæntrɔːs / (BrE)
(of plant parts or hairs) bent or curved forward or upward, especially towards the apex of the organ.
bent or curved forward or upward (botany)

Examples

Many leaves have antrorse hairs that help them shed water quickly.
Under the microscope, the antrorse spines on the stem became clearly visible.
The botanist pointed out the antrorse orientation of the seed's tiny hooks.

Synset Examples

a plant having antrorse hairs on the stem

Related Terms

  • Antonym: retrorse - (of plant parts or hairs) directed or curved backward or downward

Etymology

From Latin antrorsum (forward, toward the front).
