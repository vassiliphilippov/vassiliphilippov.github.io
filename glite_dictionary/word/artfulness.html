artfulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrt.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɑːt.fəl.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being clever and skilful, especially in achieving one's aims by indirect or deceitful means
clever or deceitful cunning

Examples

Her artfulness was evident in the way she manipulated the negotiations to her benefit.
The artfulness of the scam made it difficult for the authorities to catch the culprit.
He navigated office politics with such artfulness that few realized his true intentions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cunning - cleverness or skill, especially at deceiving people
  • Synonym: guile - sly or cunning intelligence
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and fair

Etymology

From 'artful' + '-ness', ultimately from Latin 'ars' (art, skill).
