Alnus rugosa

1. NOUN

/ ˈælnəs ruˈɡoʊsə / (AmE), / ˈal.nəs ruˈɡəʊ.sə / (BrE)
A common North American shrub, also known as speckled alder, found in Canada and the northeastern United States, recognizable by its rust-colored down on shoots.
a North American shrub, speckled alder

Examples

The wetland was dominated by Alnus rugosa growing along the stream banks.
Botanists noted the presence of Alnus rugosa among other native shrubs in the study area.
The rust-colored down on the shoots helped her identify the plant as Alnus rugosa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speckled alder - Common name for Alnus rugosa, a North American shrub

Etymology

From Latin: *Alnus* (alder) and *rugosa* (wrinkled).
