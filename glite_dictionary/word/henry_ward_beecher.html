Henry Ward Beecher

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛnri wɔrd ˈbiːtʃər / (AmE), / ˈhɛnri wɔːd ˈbiːtʃə / (BrE)
(1813–1887) American clergyman and prominent leader in the movement to abolish slavery.
American clergyman and abolitionist

Examples

Henry Ward Beecher was renowned for his passionate sermons against slavery in the 19th century.
Many Americans admired Henry Ward Beecher for his commitment to social reform and equality.
The church led by Henry Ward Beecher became a center for abolitionist activity in Brooklyn.
