focussed

1. ADJ

/ ˈfoʊ.kəst / (AmE), / ˈfəʊ.kəst / (BrE)
(alternative spelling of focused) being in focus; clearly directed or concentrated.
alternative spelling of focused; clearly directed

Examples

The speaker remained focussed throughout her presentation, answering every question with confidence.
With his attention focussed on the task, he quickly found a solution.
She kept her mind focussed despite the many distractions in the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: focused - being in focus or brought into focus
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00786415-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈfoʊ.kəst / (AmE), / ˈfəʊ.kəst / (BrE)
(of light rays) directed so as to converge at a single point.
light rays converging to a point

Examples

The scientist adjusted the lens so the focussed beam illuminated the exact spot.
Only focussed sunlight, as through a magnifying glass, can ignite dry leaves.
A focussed ray will create a sharp, intense spot of light on the wall.

Synset Examples

focused light rays can set something afire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: focused - light rays converging on a point (alternative spelling)

Etymology

From the past participle of 'focus', which is from Latin 'focus' (hearth, fireplace).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00615127-s
Synset POS: s
