scatology

1. NOUN

/ skəˈtɑlədʒi / (AmE), / skəˈtɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The scientific or medical study and analysis of excrement, especially for the purposes of medical diagnosis or paleontological research.
study or analysis of excrement
medical

Examples

The lab performed scatology to help diagnose the patient's digestive disorder.
Paleontologists use scatology to analyze fossilized remains and learn about ancient diets.
Modern scatology can reveal infections or diseases through chemical analysis of stool samples.

Etymology

From Greek 'skōr' (dung) + 'logia' (study).

preoccupation with obscenity, excrement

Examples
