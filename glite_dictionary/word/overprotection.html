overprotection

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvɚprəˈtɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəprəˈtɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act of protecting someone, especially a child, too much or more than is necessary, often resulting in negative consequences
excessive protection of someone

Examples

His parents' constant overprotection made it difficult for him to develop independence.
Some experts believe overprotection can hinder a child's emotional growth.
Schools should balance safety with the risk of overprotection.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: protection - the act of keeping someone or something safe

Etymology

From over- (excessive) + protection.
