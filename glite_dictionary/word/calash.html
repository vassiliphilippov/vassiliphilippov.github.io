calash

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlæʃ / (AmE), / kəˈlæʃ / (BrE)
A large, collapsible hood worn by women in the 18th century to protect elaborate hairstyles.
large collapsible women's hood (18th century)
archaic

Examples

The museum displayed a delicate calash alongside gowns from the 1700s.
She wore a calash to protect her towering wig from the wind.
Historical dramas often feature characters donning a calash for outdoor scenes.

Etymology

From French 'calèche', originally referring to a light carriage with a folding top.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈlæʃ / (AmE), / kəˈlæʃ / (BrE)
a folding hood covering for a horse-drawn carriage, designed to protect passengers from the weather
folding carriage hood

Examples

The driver raised the calash to protect the passengers from the sudden rainstorm.
The antique carriage still has its original calash attached, though it's rarely used now.
When the sun appeared, they folded back the calash to enjoy the fresh air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carriage - a vehicle with wheels drawn by horses

Etymology

From French calèche (carriage with a folding hood), from German Kalesche, from Czech kolesa (wheels).
