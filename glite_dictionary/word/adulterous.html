adulterous

1. ADJ

/ əˈdʌltərəs / (AmE), / əˈdʌltərəs / (BrE)
involving, characterized by, or engaging in adultery; related to a sexual relationship with someone other than one's married partner
relating to adultery

Examples

The scandal was sparked by reports of an adulterous affair between the two politicians.
She struggled with the pain of her husband's adulterous behavior.
Rumors of his adulterous liaisons soon spread throughout the community.

Synset Examples

an adulterous relationship
extramarital affairs
the extracurricular activities of a philandering husband

Related Terms

  • Antonym: faithful - remaining loyal to a sexual partner; not committing adultery
  • Synonym: extramarital - (of a sexual relationship) occurring outside marriage

Etymology

From Latin 'adulterāri' (to commit adultery) + -ous.

2. ADJ

/ əˈdʌltəɹəs / (AmE), / əˈdʌltərəs / (BrE)
(of a person or relationship) having a sexual relationship with someone who is not one's spouse or committed partner; not faithful.
not faithful to a spouse or partner

Examples

Rumors spread quickly about the politician's adulterous affairs.
She ended her adulterous relationship after realizing the hurt it caused.
The novel tells the story of an adulterous marriage and its consequences.

Synset Examples

adulterous husbands and wives
a two-timing boyfriend

Related Terms

  • Antonym: faithful - remaining loyal to a spouse or partner; not committing adultery

Etymology

From Latin 'adulterāri' (to commit adultery) via Old French and Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00964384-s
Synset POS: s
