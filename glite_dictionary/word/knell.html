knell

1. NOUN

/ nɛl / (AmE), / nɛl / (BrE)
the sound of a bell rung slowly, especially to announce a death, a funeral, or the end of something
slow bell sound for death or funeral

Examples

The solemn knell echoed through the silent village as the funeral procession began.
Many saw the announcement as the knell for the old company, marking the end of an era.
The church bell's knell rang out at midnight, spreading a sense of foreboding over the town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toll - the sound of a large bell struck slowly to mark a death or event

Etymology

From Old English 'cnyll' (bell, toll) and Old High German 'knella' (clang).

2. VERB

/ nɛl / (AmE), / nɛl / (BrE)
(of a bell) to ring solemnly, especially to announce a death or funeral.
to ring a bell solemnly, esp. for death

Examples

The church bells knelled mournfully as the funeral procession passed by.
As dusk fell, a single bell knelled in the distance, signaling the end of an era.
Every year, the bell knells to commemorate those lost in the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toll - to ring a bell slowly, especially as a sign of death

Etymology

From Old English 'cnyll' (bell, knell), of imitative origin.
