polygonal

1. ADJ

/ pəˈlɪɡənl̩ / (AmE), / pɒˈlɪɡənl̩ / (BrE)
having many sides, or relating to shapes or surfaces marked by polygons
having many sides; relating to polygons

Examples

The ancient temple features several polygonal columns at its entrance.
The architect designed a polygonal skylight to give the room a modern look.
Scientists studied the polygonal patterns formed in dried-out lake beds.

Synset Examples

polygonal structure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polygon - a flat shape with many straight sides

Etymology

From Greek 'polygonos' (many-angled), from 'poly-' (many) and 'gonia' (angle).
