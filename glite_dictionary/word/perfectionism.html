perfectionism

1. NOUN

/ pərˈfɛkʃəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / pəˈfɛkʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
The tendency to set excessively high standards and to regard anything less than perfect as unacceptable.
belief in or pursuit of flawlessness

Examples

Her perfectionism drove her to check her work multiple times before submitting it.
While perfectionism can be motivating, it often leads to unnecessary stress and disappointment.
His perfectionism made collaborative projects challenging for his colleagues.

Synset Examples

his perfectionism seemed excessive to his students

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perfectionist - a person who refuses to accept any standard short of perfection

Etymology

From 'perfection' + '-ism'; 'perfection' from Latin perfectio (completion, finishing).
