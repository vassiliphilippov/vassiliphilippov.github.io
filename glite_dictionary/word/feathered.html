feathered

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɛðərd / (AmE), / ˈfɛðəd / (BrE)
adorned with or covered in feathers or plumes.
decorated with feathers

Examples

The actress wore an elaborate feathered gown to the gala.
A rare feathered dinosaur fossil was found in China.
He chose a feathered mask for the masquerade ball.

Synset Examples

a feathered hat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plumed - decorated with feathers or plumes
  • Antonym: bare - lacking adornment or feathers

Etymology

From Middle English 'federyd,' past participle of 'fetheren' (to feather), from 'fether' (feather).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɛðɚd / (AmE), / ˈfɛðəd / (BrE)
covered with or having feathers; having feathers as a distinguishing feature.
covered with feathers

Examples

Many feathered birds migrate south for the winter.
The artist painted a feathered headdress for the festival scene.
Owls are known for their soft, feathered wings that allow silent flight.

Synset Examples

our feathered friends

Related Terms

  • Antonym: featherless - not having feathers
  • Synonym: plumed - having or adorned with plumes or feathers
