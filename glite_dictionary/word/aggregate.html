aggregate

1. NOUN

/ ˈæɡ.rɪ.ɡət / (AmE), / ˈaɡ.rɪ.ɡət / (BrE)
material such as sand, gravel, or crushed stone used with cement and water to make concrete, mortar, or plaster
material used to make concrete, mortar, or plaster

Examples

The builders mixed cement, water, and aggregate to create a strong foundation.
Quality aggregate helps improve the durability of concrete structures.
They ordered several tons of aggregate for the new road construction project.

Etymology

From Latin aggregatus (collected), past participle of aggregare (to bring together).
