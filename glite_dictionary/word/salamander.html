salamander

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæləˌmændɚ / (AmE), / ˈsæləˌmændə / (BrE)
A long metal rod with a handle, used to stir or tend a fire.
metal tool for stirring fires

Examples

He used a salamander to stir the glowing embers in the fireplace.
The old house still had a set of salamanders next to the hearth.
After adding a new log, she reached for the salamander to adjust the fire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire iron - a metal tool for tending a fire

Etymology

From Latin *salamandra*, originally referring to the mythical fire-dwelling creature, later applied to fire-related tools.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsæləˌmændɚ / (AmE), / ˈsæləˌmændə / (BrE)
A mythical reptilian creature reputed to live in and withstand fire.
legendary fire-dwelling reptile

Examples

According to medieval legends, a salamander could walk through flames unscathed.
The alchemist claimed he had seen a salamander emerge from the hearth.
Stories of the salamander fascinated scholars who believed in creatures impervious to fire.

Etymology

From Latin 'salamandra', from Greek 'salamandra', denoting both the real animal and the mythical creature.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsæləˌmændər / (AmE), / ˈsæləˌmændə / (BrE)
a small amphibian with a slender body and tail, typically resembling a lizard, which lives on land but returns to water to breed
a lizard-like amphibian

Examples

A brightly colored salamander darted under a log in the forest.
Many species of salamander are threatened by habitat loss and pollution.
The salamander spends most of its life on land and returns to ponds only to breed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: newt - a small, semi-aquatic amphibian related to the salamander

Etymology

From Greek 'salamandra', meaning 'salamander'.
