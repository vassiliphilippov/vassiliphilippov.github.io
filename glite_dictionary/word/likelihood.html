likelihood

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪklihʊd / (AmE), / ˈlaɪklihʊd / (BrE)
the chance or probability that something will happen
probability or chance of something occurring

Examples

There is a strong likelihood that the event will be canceled due to bad weather.
Doctors assessed the likelihood of recovery as very high.
With no rain in the forecast, the likelihood of flooding remains low.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: probability - the likelihood or chance of something happening
  • Antonym: certainty - being completely sure that something will happen
  • Degree Lower: possibility - something that may happen, though not certain

Etymology

From Middle English liklihede, from Old English līclīcnes (likeness, similarity); formed from 'likely' + suffix '-hood'.
