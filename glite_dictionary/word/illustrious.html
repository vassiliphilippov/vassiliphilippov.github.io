illustrious

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈlʌstriəs / (AmE), / ɪˈlʌstriəs / (BrE)
well known, respected, and admired for past achievements or qualities
well known and respected

Examples

She received an award for her illustrious career in medicine.
The university invited an illustrious scientist to give the keynote lecture.
His illustrious ancestors were respected leaders in their time.

Synset Examples

a famous actor
a celebrated musician
a famed scientist
an illustrious judge
a notable historian
a renowned painter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: famous - known about by many people
  • Synonym: celebrated - greatly admired or acclaimed
  • Antonym: obscure - not well known

Etymology

From Latin illustris (bright, distinguished).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈlʌstriəs / (AmE), / ɪˈlʌstriəs / (BrE)
well known, respected, and admired for past achievements; having glory or distinction
famous and respected; distinguished

Examples

She was an illustrious scientist whose discoveries changed the world.
The medal was awarded for his illustrious service to the country.
Many illustrious guests attended the ceremony.

Synset Examples

an illustrious achievement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distinguished - well known or highly respected; recognized for excellence
  • Synonym: notable - worthy of attention or notice; remarkable
  • Antonym: obscure - not well known; not famous or distinguished

Etymology

From Latin illustrious (bright, distinguished), from illustrare (to illuminate, make clear).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
