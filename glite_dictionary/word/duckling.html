duckling

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌklɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʌklɪŋ / (BrE)
A young duck, especially one that is newly hatched.
young duck

Examples

The fuzzy little duckling followed its mother across the pond.
Children giggled as they watched a group of ducklings swim in the park fountain.
He rescued an abandoned duckling from the side of the road.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: duck - an aquatic bird with webbed feet and a broad bill

Etymology

From duck + -ling (a diminutive suffix from Old English).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʌklɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʌklɪŋ / (BrE)
the meat of a young domestic duck, used as food
meat of a young duck

Examples

The chef prepared a roasted duckling with a cherry glaze for the special dinner menu.
She ordered the duckling because she preferred its tender, delicate flavor to that of mature duck.
In French cuisine, duckling is often featured in classic dishes such as caneton à l'orange.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: duck - flesh of a mature domestic duck used as food

Etymology

From Old English *duce* (duck) + diminutive suffix *-ling*.
