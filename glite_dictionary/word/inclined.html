inclined

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈklaɪnd / (AmE), / ɪnˈklaɪnd / (BrE)
sloping or forming an angle with a horizontal or vertical surface
sloping; at an angle

Examples

The roof is inclined at a steep angle to allow snow to slide off.
We walked up the inclined path that led to the summit.
An inclined plane helps to move heavy objects upward with less effort.

Synset Examples

an inclined plane

Related Terms

  • Antonym: horizontal - parallel to the plane of the horizon; not sloping
  • Antonym: vertical - at a right angle to the horizon; upright

Etymology

From Latin 'inclīnāre' (to bend, lean towards) via Old French 'encliner'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈklaɪnd / (AmE), / ɪnˈklaɪnd / (BrE)
having a tendency or preference towards something; likely to have a particular attitude or act in a certain way, often used with 'to' or 'towards'
having a tendency or preference

Examples

She felt inclined to accept the offer despite her reservations.
He is inclined to forget people's names.
I'm not inclined to agree with you on this matter.

Synset Examples

wasn't inclined to believe the excuse
inclined to be moody

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disposed - having a tendency or willingness
  • Antonym: averse - having a strong dislike or opposition to something

Etymology

Past participle of Middle English 'inclinen', from Latin 'inclinare' (to lean towards).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01295251-a
