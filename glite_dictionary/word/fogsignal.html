fogsignal

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔɡˌsɪɡ.nəl / (AmE), / ˈfɒɡˌsɪɡ.nəl / (BrE)
a loud, low warning sound used to alert ships in foggy conditions
nautical warning sound in fog

Examples

The ship's captain listened for the fogsignal to alert him to nearby hazards.
When the fogsignal sounded, the crew knew they were approaching the channel entrance.
During the thick fog, the lighthouse used its fogsignal every five minutes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fog horn - a horn used as a warning signal in foggy conditions

Etymology

From 'fog' + 'signal', indicating a warning given in foggy weather.
