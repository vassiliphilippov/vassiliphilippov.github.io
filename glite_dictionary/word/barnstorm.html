to barnstorm

1. VERB

/ ˈbɑrnˌstɔrm / (AmE), / ˈbɑːnˌstɔːm / (BrE)
to travel around rural areas and perform shows, especially as a stunt flier or parachute jumper at fairs and carnivals
perform stunts at fairs or carnivals

Examples

After World War I, many pilots would barnstorm across the Midwest, thrilling crowds with aerial stunts.
She spent her summer barnstorming small towns, jumping from planes at county fairs.
The daredevil made a living by barnstorming and performing dangerous parachute jumps at carnivals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tour - to travel from place to place giving performances or demonstrations

Etymology

Originally referred to actors performing in barns in rural America in the late 19th century; later applied to stunt pilots.

2. VERB

/ ˈbɑrnˌstɔrm / (AmE), / ˈbɑːnˌstɔːm / (BrE)
to travel around various places, especially rural areas, making political speeches, giving lectures, or putting on performances
travel to perform or campaign

Examples

The senator will barnstorm the Midwest to rally support for her bill.
During the 1920s, pilots would barnstorm across America, performing daring aerial stunts.
The theatre group barnstormed through small towns, reaching audiences who rarely saw live drama.

Synset Examples

The presidential candidates are busy barnstorming this month

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campaign - to work in an organized and active way toward a goal, especially a political one
  • Synonym: tour - to travel from place to place for a specific purpose, such as performing or lecturing

Etymology

Originally referred to theatrical troupes performing in barns in rural America in the late 19th century.
