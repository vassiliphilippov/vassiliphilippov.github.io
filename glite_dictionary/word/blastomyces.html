Blastomyces

1. NOUN

/ blæs.toʊˈmaɪsiːz / (AmE), / ˌblæstəʊˈmaɪsiːz / (BrE)
A genus of pathogenic yeast-like fungi, some species of which can cause disease in humans and animals.
genus of pathogenic fungi
medical

Examples

Blastomyces species are known to cause the serious infection blastomycosis in humans.
The laboratory confirmed that the infection was due to Blastomyces.
Researchers are studying the environmental distribution of Blastomyces in the region.

Etymology

From Greek 'blastós' (sprout, bud) and 'mykēs' (fungus).

2. NOUN

/ blæs.toʊˈmaɪ.siːz / (AmE), / ˌblæs.təˈmaɪ.siːz / (BrE)
A genus of pathogenic, yeastlike fungi known to cause infections in humans and animals.
genus of pathogenic fungi
medical

Examples

Blastomyces is responsible for several serious fungal infections in humans.
Researchers identified the pathogen as a species in the genus Blastomyces.
The laboratory confirmed the presence of Blastomyces in the soil sample.

Etymology

From Greek 'blastos' (germ, bud) + 'mykes' (fungus). Name coined in scientific Latin.
