Utah

1. PROPN

/ ˈjuː.tɑː / (AmE), / ˈjuː.tɑː / (BrE)
A state in the western United States, known for its varied landscapes and as the historic settlement of the Mormons in 1847.
a state in the western U.S.

Examples

The hiking trails in Utah are famous for their breathtaking views of red rock formations.
In 1847, Brigham Young led the first group of Mormon settlers to Utah.
Salt Lake City, the capital of Utah, hosted the Winter Olympics in 2002.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nevada - a state bordering Utah to the west
  • Synonym: Colorado - a state bordering Utah to the east

Etymology

Named after the Ute people, a Native American tribe of the region.
