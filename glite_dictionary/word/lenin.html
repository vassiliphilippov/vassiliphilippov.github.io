Lenin

1. PROPN

/ ˈlɛnɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɛnɪn / (BrE)
(1870–1924) Russian revolutionary leader; founder of the Bolsheviks, architect of the Russian Revolution, and first head of the Soviet Union.
Russian revolutionary leader (1870–1924)

Examples

Lenin played a key role in the success of the October Revolution.
Many statues of Lenin still stand across former Soviet states.
Lenin's ideas greatly influenced the course of 20th-century history.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Stalin - Successor to Lenin as leader of the Soviet Union
  • Synonym: Trotsky - Russian revolutionary and Marxist theorist; key figure with Lenin in the Bolshevik Revolution

Etymology

Adopted nom de guerre of Vladimir Ilyich Ulyanov; Russian surname from Lena River.
