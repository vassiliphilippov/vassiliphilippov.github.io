fluid

1. ADJ

/ ˈfluː.ɪd / (AmE), / ˈfluː.ɪd / (BrE)
(of a situation or condition) likely to change; not fixed or stable.
likely to change; not stable

Examples

The company's strategy remains fluid as the market conditions shift rapidly.
Negotiations are still at a fluid stage, with no final agreement in sight.
After the merger, roles within the organization became increasingly fluid.

Synset Examples

a fluid situation fraught with uncertainty
everything was unstable following the coup

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stable - not likely to change; firmly established
  • Synonym: changeable - able or likely to change; not constant

Etymology

From Latin 'fluidus' (flowing, liquid), from 'fluere' (to flow).

4. NOUN

/ ˈfluː.ɪd / (AmE), / ˈfluː.ɪd / (BrE)
A substance, such as a liquid or gas, that can flow and takes the shape of its container.
substance that flows (liquid or gas)

Examples

The doctor advised her to drink plenty of fluid to stay hydrated.
Water and air are both examples of a fluid.
Engineers study how a fluid moves through pipes and pumps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liquid - a substance that flows freely but is of constant volume
  • Synonym: gas - a state of matter with no fixed shape or volume

Etymology

From Latin fluidus (flowing, liquid), from fluere (to flow).
