Garrulinae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡærəˈlɪniː / (AmE), / ˌɡarəˈlɪniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of the crow family (Corvidae), comprising the jays.
subfamily of jays in the crow family

Examples

The Garrulinae include several species of colorful and intelligent jays found worldwide.
Ornithologists debated the classification of new species within the Garrulinae subfamily.
Members of the Garrulinae are known for their complex vocalizations and social behaviors.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Corvidae - the crow family of birds
  • Synonym: jay - any of various often brightly colored birds of the subfamily Garrulinae

Etymology

From Latin 'garrulus' (chattering, talkative) with zoological suffix '-inae' denoting subfamily.

2. NOUN

/ gəˈruːlɪniː / (AmE), / gəˈruːlɪniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of birds in the crow family (Corvidae), commonly known as jays.
bird subfamily including jays

Examples

Jays belong to the Garrulinae subfamily within the larger Corvidae family.
Scientists have studied the evolutionary history of the Garrulinae to understand their diversification.
The Garrulinae includes various species of jays found across Eurasia and the Americas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jay - a bird in the crow family, often colorful and noisy
  • Degree Higher: Corvidae - the crow family of birds

Etymology

From New Latin 'Garrulinae', from Latin 'garrulus' (talkative, a jay).
