cult

1. NOUN

/ kʌlt / (AmE), / kʌlt / (BrE)
An interest, hobby, or activity that is followed with intense or exaggerated enthusiasm by a particular group of people.
intensely followed interest or fad

Examples

That obscure band has developed a cult following among indie music fans.
Yoga quickly became a cult in the city, drawing dedicated practitioners from all walks of life.
The television show achieved cult status thanks to its passionate and loyal viewers.

Synset Examples

he always follows the latest fads
it was all the rage that season

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fad - a fashion or interest taken up with great enthusiasm for a short time
  • Synonym: craze - an activity or object that is very popular for a short time
  • Antonym: tradition - a custom or belief passed down over generations

Etymology

From Latin 'cultus' (care, worship).

2. NOUN

/ kʌlt / (AmE), / kʌlt / (BrE)
A religion or religious sect regarded as unorthodox or spurious, often with a devoted following and practices considered outside mainstream society.
an unorthodox or extremist religion or sect

Examples

The police investigated the remote farmhouse believed to be a base for the cult.
Many families tried to rescue loved ones who had joined the cult.
The documentary explored the rise and fall of a notorious cult in the 1970s.

Synset Examples

it was a satanic cult

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sect - a subgroup of a religious, political, or philosophical belief system, often with distinct practices

Etymology

From Latin cultus (worship, cult, care), from colere (to cultivate).
