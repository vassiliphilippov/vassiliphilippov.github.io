Eruca sativa

1. NOUN

/ əˈruːkə səˈtiːvə / (AmE), / ɪˈruːkə səˈtiːvə / (BrE)
An annual Mediterranean plant whose young leaves are harvested for use in salads, commonly known as arugula or rocket.
Mediterranean plant; salad green (arugula, rocket)

Examples

The salad featured fresh Eruca sativa leaves mixed with tomatoes and olive oil.
Many chefs prefer Eruca sativa for its distinctive, peppery flavor in spring salads.
Farmers harvested fields of Eruca sativa early in the morning to ensure the leaves stayed tender.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arugula - Edible Mediterranean plant used in salads
  • Synonym: rocket - British English term for the same salad plant

Etymology

From Latin: 'eruca' (a kind of cabbage or colewort), 'sativa' (cultivated).
