prepupal

1. ADJ

/ ˌpriːˈpjuːpəl / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈpjuːpəl / (BrE)
relating to the stage in the development of some insects that occurs between the larval and pupal stages, typically characterized by inactivity
of the stage between larva and pupa in insects
medical

Examples

During the prepupal stage, the caterpillar stops feeding and prepares to form a chrysalis.
Chemical changes occur in the insect's body during the prepupal phase.
Researchers observed the insect's behavior entering its prepupal period before pupation.

Synset Examples

the prepupal stage

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: pupal - relating to the pupa stage in insect development
  • Degree Lower: larval - relating to the larva stage in insect development

Etymology

From Latin prefix pre- (before) + pupal (relating to the pupa).
