hopeless

1. ADJ

/ ˈhoʊpləs / (AmE), / ˈhəʊpləs / (BrE)
(of a person) unable to do something well; lacking skill or ability at a particular activity.
unable or incompetent at something

Examples

I'm absolutely hopeless at remembering names.
Despite taking lessons, she remained hopeless at driving.
He always felt hopeless at sports compared to his classmates.

Synset Examples

I'm hopeless at mathematics

Related Terms

  • Antonym: competent - having the necessary ability or skills to do something successfully
  • Antonym: skilful - having or showing skill at a particular activity

Etymology

From hope + -less, meaning 'without hope'; extended to mean lacking skill by analogy.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02237557-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈhoʊpləs / (AmE), / ˈhəʊpləs / (BrE)
having no chance of success; certain to fail or be unsuccessful.
certain to fail; without hope

Examples

After days of searching, their efforts seemed hopeless.
The team faced a hopeless situation when they were down by five goals.
Trying to fix the car without the right tools was hopeless.

Synset Examples

the situation is hopeless

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desperate - feeling or showing a loss of hope; having little chance of success
  • Antonym: promising - showing signs of future success; likely to succeed

Etymology

From Middle English hopeles, from hope + -less.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01828961-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈhoʊpləs / (AmE), / ˈhəʊpləs / (BrE)
Impossible to improve, manage, or deal with; not likely to succeed or be helped.
impossible to improve or manage
informal

Examples

After hours of searching, the team declared it a hopeless case and called off the rescue.
My desk became a hopeless mess by the end of the week.
He gave me a hopeless look that said he had already given up.

Synset Examples

she handed me a hopeless jumble of papers
he is a hopeless romantic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impossible - not able to be done or achieved
  • Antonym: manageable - able to be controlled or dealt with easily

Etymology

From hope + -less, dating from Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01131271-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈhoʊpləs / (AmE), / ˈhəʊpləs / (BrE)
feeling or showing no hope; having no possibility of improvement, success, or comfort
without hope or chance of success

Examples

After several failed attempts, she felt completely hopeless about the situation.
The economy was in such a hopeless state that recovery seemed impossible.
He gave her a hopeless look as he explained the bad news.

Synset Examples

in an agony of hopeless grief
with a hopeless sigh he sat down

Related Terms

  • Synonym: despairing - showing a total lack of hope
  • Antonym: optimistic - expecting the best possible outcome

Etymology

From hope + -less, the suffix forming adjectives meaning 'without'.

Source: WordNet
