outsole

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌsoʊl / (AmE), / ˈaʊtsəʊl / (BrE)
the outer bottom part of a shoe or boot that comes into contact with the ground
bottom part of a shoe or boot

Examples

The outsole of these running shoes is designed for extra grip on wet surfaces.
After months of use, the outsole started to wear thin in several places.
A durable rubber outsole can help prolong the life of your boots.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: insole - the inner sole of a shoe or boot

Etymology

Formed from 'out-' (outer) + 'sole' (bottom of a shoe).
