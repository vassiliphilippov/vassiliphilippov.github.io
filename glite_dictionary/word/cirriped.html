cirriped

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪrɪˌpɛd / (AmE), / ˈsɪrɪˌpɛd / (BrE)
A marine crustacean of the subclass Cirripedia, such as a barnacle, having feathery appendages for feeding and living attached to submerged surfaces as an adult.
type of marine crustacean (barnacle group)

Examples

Biologists studied the distribution of cirripeds along the rocky coastline.
The hard shells of cirripeds encrusted the hull of the old ship.
As larvae, cirripeds swim freely before settling onto submerged surfaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barnacle - a type of marine crustacean that attaches to surfaces

Etymology

From Latin 'cirripes', from 'cirrus' (curl, tuft) + 'pes' (foot), referring to their curled feeding appendages.
