undefiled

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌndɪˈfaɪld / (AmE), / ˌʌndɪˈfaɪld / (BrE)
Not spoiled, stained, or made impure; free from blemish or corruption.
free from stain or impurity

Examples

The mountain stream remained undefiled by human activity.
Their love for each other was undefiled by doubt or jealousy.
She prized her reputation as undefiled by scandal or gossip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pure - free from contamination, not mixed with anything else
  • Antonym: defiled - corrupted or made dirty or impure

Etymology

From un- (not) + defiled (from Old French defouler, from Latin defile).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌndɪˈfaɪld / (AmE), / ˌʌndɪˈfaɪld / (BrE)
(of language, character, or other things) not corrupted, polluted, or made impure; remaining in a pure or unspoiled state.
not made impure or corrupted

Examples

The ancient manuscript was written in undefiled Latin, untouched by later influences.
She valued an undefiled reputation above all else in her professional life.
His poetry is celebrated for its undefiled simplicity and emotional clarity.

Synset Examples

uncorrupted English
learn to speak pure English undefiled

Related Terms

  • Antonym: defiled - made dirty or impure; corrupted
  • Synonym: pure - not mixed or adulterated with any other substance or material

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'defiled', from Old French 'defouler' (to trample down) and Latin 'defilare' (to violate).
