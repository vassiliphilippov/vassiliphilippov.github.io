preliterate

1. ADJ

/ priˈlɪtərət / (AmE), / priˈlɪtərət / (BrE)
(of a society or culture) not having developed a system of writing.
without a developed system of writing

Examples

Many preliterate societies relied on oral tradition to pass down their history.
Anthropologists study the rituals and myths of preliterate cultures.
The transition from preliterate to literate society often leads to significant cultural changes.

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'pre-' (before) + 'literate' (able to read and write).

2. ADJ

/ priˈlɪt̬ərət / (AmE), / priˈlɪtərət / (BrE)
not having learned to read and write; occurring or existing before the development of written language
not yet able to read and write

Examples

Many anthropologists study preliterate societies to understand human cultural development.
Children in the preliterate stage often enjoy listening to stories before they can read them themselves.
The myths were passed down orally in a preliterate community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illiterate - unable to read or write
  • Antonym: literate - able to read and write

Etymology

From prefix 'pre-' (before) + 'literate' (able to read and write).
