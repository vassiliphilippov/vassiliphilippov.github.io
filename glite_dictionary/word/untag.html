to untag

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈtæɡ / (AmE), / ʌnˈtaɡ / (BrE)
to remove a tag, particularly a metadata label, from an online post or digital item.
remove a digital tag from content

Examples

After realizing she was untagged from the photo, Maria felt relieved.
You can untag yourself from any post you don't want on your profile.
He quickly untagged his name from several embarrassing pictures online.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tag - to add a metadata label to a digital post or image

Etymology

Formed by prefixing 'un-' (meaning 'not' or 'reversal of') to 'tag' (in the digital sense of a label or mark).
