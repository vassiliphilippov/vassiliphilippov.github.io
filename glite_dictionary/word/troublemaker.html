troublemaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrʌblˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈtrʌblˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
a person who deliberately causes problems or incites trouble, especially by stirring up conflict or unrest
person who causes trouble

Examples

The teacher quickly identified the troublemaker in the group and tried to separate him from the others.
Every office seems to have at least one troublemaker who enjoys spreading rumors.
Despite his reputation as a troublemaker, he was actually quite considerate when it mattered most.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agitator - a person who stirs up others for a cause, often causing trouble
  • Antonym: peacemaker - a person who tries to make peace and resolve conflict

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrʌbəlˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈtrʌb(ə)lˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
A person who deliberately causes problems or difficulties for others.
person who causes trouble

Examples

The teacher kept a close eye on the troublemaker at the back of the classroom.
Every group has a troublemaker who is always starting arguments.
His reputation as a troublemaker made it hard for him to fit in.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agitator - person who stirs up others in order to upset the status quo and further a political, social, or other cause
  • Antonym: peacemaker - person who brings about peace, especially by settling disputes
