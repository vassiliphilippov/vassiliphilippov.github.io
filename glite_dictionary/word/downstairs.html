downstairs

1. ADV

/ ˌdaʊnˈstɛrz / (AmE), / ˌdaʊnˈsteəz / (BrE)
on or to a lower floor of a building, especially the one directly below the main or upper floor
on or to a lower floor

Examples

The children are playing downstairs while the adults talk in the living room.
She hurried downstairs when she heard the doorbell.
If you're looking for the kitchen, it's downstairs next to the dining room.

Synset Examples

the tenants live downstairs

Related Terms

  • Antonym: upstairs - on or to an upper floor of a building
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00094946-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADJ

/ ˌdaʊnˈstɛrz / (AmE), / ˌdaʊnˈstɛəz / (BrE)
Situated on or relating to a lower floor of a building, especially the floor immediately below the one being considered.
on or of a lower floor

Examples

The downstairs bathroom was recently renovated.
We put the guests in the downstairs bedroom so they wouldn't have to use the stairs.
They have a downstairs office that opens onto the garden.

Synset Examples

the downstairs (or downstair) phone

Related Terms

  • Antonym: upstairs - located on or relating to a higher floor of a building

WordNet ID: oewn-02503770-a
Synset POS: a
