fluosilicate

1. NOUN

/ ˌfluː.oʊˈsɪl.ɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˌfluː.əʊˈsɪl.ɪ.keɪt / (BrE)
a salt derived from fluosilicic acid, typically containing the hexafluorosilicate ion.
a salt of fluosilicic acid
medical

Examples

The water sample contained a small amount of fluosilicate used for fluoridation purposes.
Chemists analyzed the industrial waste for traces of fluosilicate compounds.
Potassium fluosilicate is sometimes found in the process of glass manufacturing.

Etymology

From 'fluorine' and 'silicate', reflecting its chemical composition.
