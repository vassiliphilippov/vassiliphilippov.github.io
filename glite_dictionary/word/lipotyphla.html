Lipotyphla

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈpoʊtəflə / (AmE), / lɪˈpɒtɪflə / (BrE)
a former order of small, insect-eating mammals, including moles, hedgehogs, and true shrews, now largely obsolete in modern classification
former mammal order: moles, shrews, hedgehogs

Examples

Moles, hedgehogs, and shrews were traditionally classified under Lipotyphla in zoological taxonomy.
The order Lipotyphla is now largely replaced by other groupings in modern mammal classification schemes.
Early studies often grouped a variety of insectivorous mammals into Lipotyphla despite their evolutionary differences.

Etymology

From Greek 'lipos' (fat) + 'typhlos' (blind), referring to the fat and sometimes blind appearance of some members.

2. NOUN

/ ˌlɪpoʊˈtɪflə / (AmE), / ˌlɪpəˈtɪflə / (BrE)
a former zoological suborder that included moles, hedgehogs, and true shrews, now largely obsolete as a formal classification
former animal suborder (moles, hedgehogs, shrews)

Examples

The suborder Lipotyphla once included a variety of small insectivorous mammals such as moles and hedgehogs.
Recent genetic studies have led taxonomists to split Lipotyphla into several different groups.
Although Lipotyphla is now considered obsolete, it is still mentioned in older zoological literature.

Etymology

From Greek 'leipos' (left, lacking) + 'typhlos' (blind).
