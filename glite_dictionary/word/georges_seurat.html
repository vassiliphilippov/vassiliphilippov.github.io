Georges Seurat

1. PROPN

/ ʒɔʒ səˈrɑ / (AmE), / ʒɔːʒ səˈrɑː / (BrE)
(1859–1891) A French painter best known for developing the painting technique of pointillism.
French painter, creator of pointillism

Examples

Georges Seurat is famous for pioneering the pointillist method of painting.
Many art students study the work of Georges Seurat to learn about color theory.
The museum is exhibiting several masterpieces by Georges Seurat this summer.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: pointillism - A painting technique using small dots of color to form an image

2. PROPN

/ ʒɔːrʒ sɪˈrɑː / (AmE), / ʒɔːʒ sœˈʁa / (BrE)
(1859–1891) A French painter who developed the technique of pointillism and was a leading figure in the post-impressionist movement.
French painter, developer of pointillism

Examples

Many art historians credit Georges Seurat with revolutionizing modern painting through his scientific approach to color.
Visitors to the museum admired the intricate dot patterns in Georges Seurat's most famous works.
The technique introduced by Georges Seurat influenced generations of painters after him.

Related Terms
