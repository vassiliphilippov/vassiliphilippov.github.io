accusation

1. NOUN

/ ˌæk.jəˈseɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌæk.juːˈseɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a statement saying that someone has done something wrong or illegal, even if it has not been proved
statement blaming someone for wrongdoing

Examples

The manager strongly denied the accusation of misconduct made by his colleagues.
Her reputation was damaged by the false accusation published in the press.
The court must determine whether the accusation has any merit before proceeding.

Synset Examples

the newspaper published charges that Jones was guilty of drunken driving

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allegation - a claim that someone has done something wrong, typically without proof
  • Antonym: denial - a statement that something is not true or has not happened

Etymology

From Latin accusatio (a charge), from accusare (to accuse).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæk.juˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌæk.juˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a statement that someone has done something wrong or illegal, especially officially
formal statement of blame or wrongdoing

Examples

The politician denied the accusation of corruption during the press conference.
A false accusation can ruin someone's reputation.
After hearing the accusation, the judge asked the witness to testify.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allegation - a claim or assertion that someone has done something wrong, typically without proof
  • Synonym: charge - an official statement accusing someone of a crime
  • Antonym: defense - an argument or evidence offered to rebut an accusation

Etymology

From Latin accusatio (an accusation, charge), from accusare (to accuse).
