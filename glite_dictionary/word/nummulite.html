nummulite

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʌmjəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈnʌmjʊlʌɪt / (BrE)
A large, flat, coin-shaped fossilized protozoan, especially abundant in Tertiary period rocks.
large fossilized protozoan

Examples

The limestone was composed almost entirely of nummulite shells.
Geologists found a fossil nummulite dating back to the Tertiary period.
A single nummulite can be several centimeters in diameter.

Etymology

From Latin nummulus (little coin), from nummus (coin), referring to its shape.
