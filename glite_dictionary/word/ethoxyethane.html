ethoxyethane

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌθɑksiˈiːθeɪn / (AmE), / ɪˌθɒksiˈiːθeɪn / (BrE)
a colorless, volatile, highly flammable liquid formerly used as an inhalation anesthetic and solvent, also known as diethyl ether
highly flammable anesthetic liquid (diethyl ether)
medical

Examples

Traditional anesthesia sometimes involved the use of ethoxyethane as a sedative agent.
Because ethoxyethane is highly flammable, extra caution must be taken during its storage and handling.
Laboratory solvents such as ethoxyethane play a crucial role in many organic syntheses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ether - a colorless, volatile, flammable liquid used as an anesthetic (diethyl ether)

Etymology

From ethoxy (ethyl group bonded via oxygen) + ethane; named for its chemical structure.
