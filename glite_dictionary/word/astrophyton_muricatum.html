Astrophyton muricatum

1. NOUN

/ ˌæstrəˈfaɪtɑn ˌmjʊrɪˈkeɪtəm / (AmE), / ˌæstrəˈfaɪtən ˌmjʊərɪˈkeɪtəm / (BrE)
A species of large basket star found in tropical western Atlantic waters, known for its intricate, branched arms.
a species of basket star

Examples

The Astrophyton muricatum unfurled its long, branched arms across the coral reef as night fell.
Divers often encounter Astrophyton muricatum clinging to sponges in the Caribbean Sea.
Researchers study the feeding habits of Astrophyton muricatum to better understand reef ecosystems.

Etymology

From Greek 'astron' (star) + 'phyton' (plant) and Latin 'muricatus' (pointed or spiny).
