Dermacentor variabilis

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɜːrməˈsɛntɔːr vəˌriəˈbɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˌdɜːməˈsɛntɔː vəˌrɪəˈbɪlɪs / (BrE)
a species of tick native to North America that can transmit diseases such as Rocky Mountain spotted fever and tularemia
species of tick that transmits diseases
medical

Examples

Dermacentor variabilis is commonly known as the American dog tick.
Researchers study Dermacentor variabilis because it can transmit serious diseases to humans and animals.
You should check your pets for Dermacentor variabilis after walking them in grassy areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tick - a small parasitic arachnid

Etymology

From Latin: 'Dermacentor' meaning skin-biter, and 'variabilis' meaning variable.
