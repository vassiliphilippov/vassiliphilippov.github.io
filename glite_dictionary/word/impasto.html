impasto

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɑstoʊ / (AmE), / ɪmˈpæstəʊ / (BrE)
A technique in painting where paint is laid on the surface very thickly so that brush or palette knife marks are clearly visible.
thickly applied paint technique

Examples

The artist used impasto to give the painting a rich, textured surface.
You can see the deliberate impasto in the way the clouds were painted.
Her landscapes feature heavy impasto that almost leaps off the canvas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scumbling - a painting technique involving the application of a thin, translucent layer of paint

Etymology

From Italian 'impasto' (dough; mixture; paste), from 'impastare' (to knead).
