suave

1. ADJ

/ swɑv / (AmE), / swɑːv / (BrE)
(of a person or their manner) charming, confident, and elegant, often in a smooth and sophisticated way.
charming, confident, and elegant

Examples

James Bond is often portrayed as a suave and sophisticated secret agent.
The suave host welcomed each guest with a charming smile.
He tried to sound suave, but his nervousness showed through.

Synset Examples

he was too politic to quarrel with so important a personage
the manager pacified the customer with a smooth apology for the error

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charming - pleasant and attractive in manner
  • Synonym: sophisticated - having a refined knowledge of the world or culture
  • Antonym: awkward - lacking grace or social polish

Etymology

From French 'suave', from Latin 'suavis' meaning 'sweet' or 'agreeable'.

2. ADJ

/ swɑv / (AmE), / swɑːv / (BrE)
(especially of a man) confident, charming, and elegant in a smooth and sophisticated way.
smoothly charming and sophisticated

Examples

He gave a suave smile as he confidently entered the room.
Her boss is known for his suave manner and impeccable dress sense.
The actor played a suave spy who charmed everyone he met.

Synset Examples

a debonair gentleman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debonair - charming, confident, and elegant (usually of a man)
  • Synonym: urbane - courteous and refined in manner, typically of a man
  • Antonym: awkward - lacking social grace or confidence

Etymology

From Latin 'suavis' (sweet, agreeable) via French 'suave'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01954912-s
Synset POS: s
