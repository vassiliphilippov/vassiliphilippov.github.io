lapidary

1. ADJ

/ ˈlæpɪˌdɛri / (AmE), / ˈlæpɪdəri / (BrE)
relating to precious stones or the art of engraving, cutting, or polishing them.
relating to precious stones or their working

Examples

She took a course in lapidary techniques to learn how to cut and polish gemstones.
The museum features a rare collection of lapidary art from ancient civilizations.
This necklace has no lapidary value because the stones are not genuine.

Synset Examples

the ring is of no lapidary value
lapidary art

Etymology

From Latin lapidarius, from lapis (stone).

WordNet ID: oewn-02840123-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈlæpɪˌdɛri / (AmE), / ˈlæpɪdəri / (BrE)
a person who cuts, polishes, and engraves precious stones
person who cuts and engraves gems

Examples

The lapidary carefully shaped the sapphire into a brilliant oval.
As a renowned lapidary, she has crafted jewelry for royalty and celebrities alike.
A skilled lapidary can transform a rough stone into a sparkling gem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gemcutter - a person who cuts and polishes gems

Etymology

From Latin lapidarius (of stone), from lapis (stone).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10266208-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈlæpɪˌdɛri / (AmE), / ˈlæpɪdəri / (BrE)
an expert in precious stones and in the cutting, polishing, or engraving of them
expert in gemstones and cutting

Examples

The lapidary carefully examined the uncut diamond before deciding how to shape it.
As a respected lapidary, she is often consulted about rare gemstones.
The museum employed a skilled lapidary to restore its collection of engraved jewels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gemologist - a person who studies or deals in gems

Etymology

From Latin lapidarius (of or relating to stone), from lapis (stone).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10266400-n
Synset POS: n
