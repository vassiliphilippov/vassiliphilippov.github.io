backsheesh

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækʃiʃ / (AmE), / ˈbækʃiːʃ / (BrE)
a small amount of money given as a tip or gratuity, especially in some Asian and Middle Eastern countries
small tip or gratuity

Examples

It is common to give a small backsheesh to the porter who carries your luggage.
The guide expected a backsheesh at the end of the tour.
She slipped a backsheesh to the waiter for excellent service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tip - a small sum of money given for a service
  • Antonym: bribe - money given to persuade someone to act dishonestly

Etymology

From Persian 'bakhshīsh', meaning gift or gratuity, via Urdu and Turkish.
