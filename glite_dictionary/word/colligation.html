colligation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑː.lɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒ.lɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The process of linking individual facts or observations together by means of a general hypothesis or explanatory theory.
linking facts by a hypothesis

Examples

The scientist achieved colligation by proposing a theory that explained all the experimental results.
Historical research often requires the colligation of scattered data into a coherent narrative.
Her colligation of unrelated observations led to an entirely new understanding of the phenomenon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synthesis - the combination of components or elements to form a connected whole

Etymology

From Latin 'colligatio' (a binding together), from 'colligare' (to bind together).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːlɪˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒlɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act or state of being linked or connected together; a joining or binding together, especially of ideas, things, or grammatical elements.
linking or joining together

Examples

The scientist emphasized the colligation of ideas in forming a comprehensive theory.
In the grammar of English, colligation refers to the systematic co-occurrence of certain forms.
Their successful colligation of different approaches led to a breakthrough in research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connection - the act of connecting or the state of being connected
  • Antonym: separation - the action or state of moving or being moved apart

Etymology

From Latin colligare (to bind together), from com- (together) + ligare (to bind).
