insipid

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsɪpɪd / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪpɪd / (BrE)
lacking flavor, interest, or liveliness; dull or unexciting.
dull or uninteresting

Examples

The presentation was so insipid that several audience members began to nod off.
She found the movie insipid and could not wait for it to end.
His insipid remarks failed to capture anyone's attention.

Synset Examples

an insipid personality
jejune novel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bland - lacking strong features or characteristics; uninteresting
  • Synonym: boring - not interesting; tedious
  • Antonym: stimulating - encouraging interest or enthusiasm

Etymology

From Latin insipidus (tasteless), from in- (not) + sapidus (tasty).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsɪpɪd / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪpɪd / (BrE)
Lacking flavor, taste, or interest; dull.
lacking flavor or interest

Examples

The soup was disappointingly insipid and needed more salt and spices.
She found the conversation as insipid as the lukewarm tea they were drinking.
Many critics described the novel as insipid and lacking in originality.

Synset Examples

a bland diet
insipid hospital food
flavorless supermarket tomatoes
vapid beer
vapid tea

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bland - lacking strong features or characteristics; insipid in taste
  • Synonym: flavorless - without flavor; lacking taste
  • Antonym: tasty - having a pleasant, distinct flavor

Etymology

From Latin 'insipidus' (tasteless), from 'in-' (not) + 'sapidus' (tasty).
