Centrarchidae

1. NOUN

/ sɛnˈtrɑːrkəˌdiː / (AmE), / sɛnˈtrɑːkɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of freshwater fish known as the sunfish family, native to North America and including species such as sunfish, bass, and crappie
family of North American freshwater fish (sunfish family)

Examples

The Centrarchidae family includes popular sport fish like largemouth bass and bluegill.
Biologists often study Centrarchidae to understand freshwater ecosystems in North America.
Some aquariums display several species from the Centrarchidae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunfish - common name for several species in Centrarchidae

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'kentron' (spur) and 'archēs' (anus), referring to the spiny fins.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛntrɑːrˈkɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌsɛntrɑːˈkɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of freshwater fish native to North America, commonly known as sunfishes, including species such as bluegills and largemouth bass.
family of freshwater sunfish

Examples

The Centrarchidae family includes popular sport fish like bluegills and crappies.
Biologists conducted a survey of the Centrarchidae species in the lake ecosystem.
Some members of the Centrarchidae are prized for their aggressive feeding habits and ease of capture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunfish - common name for several species in the family Centrarchidae

Etymology

From Greek 'kentron' (sting) + 'archos' (anus), referring to the type of fish in this family.
