clammyweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæmiˌwid / (AmE), / ˈklæmiˌwiːd / (BrE)
A strong-scented herb native to the southern United States, characterized by its sticky texture and glandular, hairy surface.
a sticky, strong-scented herb found in the southern US

Examples

While hiking, they found a patch of clammyweed growing near the riverbank.
The clammyweed releases a pungent odor when touched.
Gardeners sometimes remove clammyweed because of its sticky leaves.
