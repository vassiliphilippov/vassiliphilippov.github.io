midterm

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdˌtɝm / (AmE), / ˈmɪdˌtɜːm / (BrE)
the middle stage of the gestation period in the development of an embryo or fetus
middle stage of pregnancy
medical

Examples

During midterm, important organs in the fetus continue their rapid development.
The doctor scheduled a detailed scan to check the baby's growth at midterm.
Pregnant women are often advised to have certain tests around midterm to ensure the pregnancy is progressing well.

Etymology

From 'mid-' meaning 'middle' + 'term', referring here to the duration of gestation.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdˌtɝm / (AmE), / ˈmɪdˌtɜːm / (BrE)
an examination given in the middle of an academic term to assess students' progress
examination in the middle of a term

Examples

I have to study hard for my midterm next week.
The midterm covered all the material from the first half of the course.
After the midterm, the professor gave us feedback on our performance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: final exam - an examination given at the end of an academic term

Etymology

Formed from 'mid-' (middle) and 'term' (a period of study); first used in academic contexts in the 20th century.

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdˌtɜrm / (AmE), / ˈmɪdˌtɜːm / (BrE)
the middle of an academic term or a period of office; also, an examination given in the middle of an academic term
middle of a term or period in office

Examples

Many students feel anxious about the upcoming midterm exams.
The president's popularity dropped significantly by the midterm of his first term.
She started studying early to prepare for her midterm this semester.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: final - end-of-term examination

Etymology

Formed from 'mid-' (middle) + 'term'.
