demonic

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈmɑnɪk / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒnɪk / (BrE)
Extremely evil or cruel; showing wickedness or cruelty as if inspired by the devil.
extremely evil or cruel

Examples

The villain's demonic laughter echoed through the empty hallways, sending chills down everyone's spine.
There was a demonic intensity in her eyes as she shouted her accusations.
After the demonic attack, the survivors described scenes of unspeakable cruelty and horror.

Synset Examples

unholy grimaces

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diabolical - extremely evil or wicked
  • Synonym: fiendish - extremely cruel or wicked
  • Antonym: angelic - exceptionally kind or innocent

Etymology

From Late Latin daemonicus, from Greek daimōnikos (of a spirit or demon).
