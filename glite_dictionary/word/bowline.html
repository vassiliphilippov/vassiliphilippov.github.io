bowline

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊ.lɪn / (AmE), / ˈbəʊ.lɪn / (BrE)
a type of knot that forms a fixed loop at the end of a rope and does not slip or jam
a knot that forms a fixed loop

Examples

He quickly tied a bowline to secure the boat to the dock.
Learning how to make a bowline is essential for any sailor.
The bowline is known for being easy to untie even after bearing a heavy load.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knot - an interlacement of rope or cord

Etymology

From Middle Low German *boeglijn* (a rope tied to the bow of a ship).
