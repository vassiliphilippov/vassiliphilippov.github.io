Ruta

1. PROPN

/ ˈruːtə / (AmE), / ˈruːtə / (BrE)
A genus of strong-scented Eurasian herbs in the family Rutaceae, including the common rue.
genus of Eurasian herbs (rue family)

Examples

Ruta graveolens is commonly known as common rue and has medicinal uses.
Several species within the genus Ruta are cultivated for their aromatic leaves.
Botanists studied the chemical compounds produced by plants in the Ruta genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rue - a strong-smelling Eurasian herb belonging to the genus Ruta

Etymology

From Latin 'ruta', meaning rue (the plant).

2. NOUN

/ ˈruːtə / (AmE), / ˈruːtə / (BrE)
A genus of strong-scented Eurasian herbs in the rue family (Rutaceae), including rue.
genus of strong-scented Eurasian herbs

Examples

The Ruta genus includes several species known for their medicinal properties.
Gardeners often plant Ruta for its aromatic leaves and decorative value.
Some traditional remedies use extracts from the Ruta plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rue - common name for plants in the genus Ruta

Etymology

From Latin 'ruta' (rue, herb), also used in botanical Latin for the genus.
