to broaden

1. VERB

/ ˈbrɔdən / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːd(ə)n / (BrE)
to become wider or more extensive; to increase in range or scope
become wider or more extensive

Examples

The river began to broaden as it approached the sea.
Her interests started to broaden after she moved to the city.
As the investigation continued, its scope broadened beyond the initial allegations.

Synset Examples

The road broadened

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to widen - to make or become wider
  • Antonym: narrow - to become less wide or more limited

Etymology

From broad + -en (English verb-forming suffix meaning 'to make or become').

2. VERB

/ ˈbrɔdə(n) / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːdn̩ / (BrE)
to make something include more categories, areas, or varieties; to diversify or expand in scope
expand or diversify

Examples

The company decided to broaden its product range to reach new markets.
She hopes to broaden her experience by working in different industries.
It is wise to broaden your investments to minimize risk.

Synset Examples

The company diversified

Related Terms

  • Antonym: narrow - to make less broad or less varied; to limit in scope
  • Synonym: to diversify - to make or become more varied or diverse; to expand into new areas

Etymology

From broad (adj.) + -en (verb-forming suffix).
