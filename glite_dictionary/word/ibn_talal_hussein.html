Ibn Talal Hussein

1. PROPN

/ ɪbˈn tæˈlɑːl huːˈseɪn / (AmE), / ɪbˈn tæˈlɑːl hʊˈseɪn / (BrE)
(1935–1999) King of Jordan from 1952 to 1999, credited with maintaining stability in Jordan and pursuing peace with Israel.
King of Jordan (1935–1999)

Examples

Ibn Talal Hussein played a significant role in shaping modern Jordan's political landscape.
Many remember Ibn Talal Hussein for his efforts toward peace in the Middle East.
During the reign of Ibn Talal Hussein, Jordan experienced a period of relative stability.

Etymology

From Arabic ibn (son of) and the names Talal and Hussein.
