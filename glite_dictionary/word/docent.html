docent

1. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊ.sənt / (AmE), / ˈdəʊ.sənt / (BrE)
a member of a university staff who teaches, especially at certain European universities, often ranking below a full professor
university teacher, often below professor

Examples

After completing his doctorate, he became a docent at the university.
At some European institutions, a docent holds a position between lecturer and professor.
She was promoted from docent to full professor after years of research and teaching.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: professor - a university teacher of the highest rank
  • Synonym: lecturer - a person who gives lectures, especially at a university or college

Etymology

From Latin 'docēns' (teaching), present participle of 'docēre' (to teach); adopted via German 'Dozent'.
