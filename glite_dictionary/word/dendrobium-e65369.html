Dendrobium

1. NOUN

/ dɛnˈdroʊbiəm / (AmE), / dɛnˈdrəʊbiəm / (BrE)
A large and diverse genus of chiefly epiphytic or lithophytic orchids native to tropical and subtropical Asia and Australasia.
a genus of tropical orchids

Examples

Several rare Dendrobium species were discovered in the rainforests of Borneo.
Her collection includes dozens of Dendrobium hybrids with brightly colored blooms.
The Dendrobium thrives in warm, humid environments with plenty of indirect light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - a plant of the family Orchidaceae

Etymology

From Greek dendron (tree) + bios (life), referring to the tree-dwelling habit of many species.
