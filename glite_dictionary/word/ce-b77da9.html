CE

1. ADV

/ si ˈi / (AmE), / siː ˈiː / (BrE)
used to refer to dates in the Common Era, the period beginning with year 1 according to the Gregorian calendar; often preferred by those who wish to avoid the explicitly Christian reference of "AD."
abbreviation for Common Era in dates

Examples

The city was founded in 150 CE and grew rapidly over the next century.
Many ancient Roman texts date back to the first century CE.
The temple was constructed around 350 CE during the reign of the emperor.

Synset Examples

in 200 CE

Related Terms

  • Synonym: AD - used to refer to years after the birth of Christ (Anno Domini)
  • Antonym: BCE - used to refer to years before the Common Era

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'Common Era', modeled after Latin 'Christi Era' used for 'AD'.
