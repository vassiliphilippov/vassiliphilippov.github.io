oblongness

1. NOUN

/ əˈblɔŋ.nəs / (AmE), / əˈblɒŋ.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being oblong in shape, especially resembling a rectangle with unequal adjacent sides
the state of being oblong in shape

Examples

The oblongness of the room made it ideal for hosting banquets.
Artists often use the oblongness of canvases to create particular visual effects.
Some fruits have a distinctive oblongness compared to the more common round varieties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rectangularity - the quality of being rectangular in shape

Etymology

From 'oblong' + suffix '-ness', denoting the state or quality of being oblong.
