cryptorchidy

1. NOUN

/ krɪpˈtɔːr.kɪ.di / (AmE), / krɪpˈtɔː.kɪ.di / (BrE)
A medical condition in which one or both testes fail to descend into the scrotum during fetal development.
failure of testes to descend
medical

Examples

The pediatrician diagnosed the infant with cryptorchidy after discovering only one testis in the scrotum.
Surgical intervention may be necessary to correct cryptorchidy if the testes do not descend naturally.
Untreated cryptorchidy can increase the risk of infertility and testicular cancer later in life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cryptorchidism - the condition of having undescended testes

Etymology

From Greek kryptos (hidden) + orchis (testicle).
