baryon

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæriˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈbarɪɒn / (BrE)
a subatomic particle, such as a proton or neutron, that is made up of three quarks and participates in strong interactions
type of subatomic particle (proton or neutron)

Examples

A proton is a common example of a baryon found in atomic nuclei.
Physicists study baryon interactions to better understand the forces inside an atom.
The discovery of a new baryon provided insight into the behavior of quarks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meson - a subatomic particle made of one quark and one antiquark
  • Antonym: antibaryon - a subatomic particle made of three antiquarks; the antiparticle of a baryon

Etymology

From Greek 'barus' (heavy) + '-on' (particle).
