2. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈdʒoʊzəf / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈdʒəʊzɪf / (BrE)
A Christian feast day commemorating St. Joseph, the husband of the Virgin Mary, celebrated on March 19 by the Roman Catholic Church and on other dates by some Orthodox churches.
Christian feast day for St. Joseph

Examples

Many Catholics attend Mass on Saint Joseph to honor the earthly father of Jesus.
In Italy, Saint Joseph is celebrated with special pastries and traditions on March 19.
Parishes dedicated to Saint Joseph often hold processions and feasts for the holiday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Feast of St. Joseph - Alternative name for Saint Joseph feast day
  • Synonym: Saint Patrick's Day - Christian feast day for Saint Patrick

Etymology

From Latin 'Sanctus Josephus', referring to St. Joseph, husband of Mary.
