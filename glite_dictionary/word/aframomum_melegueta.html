Aframomum melegueta

1. NOUN

/ ˌæfɹəˈmoʊməm məlɪˈɡeɪtə / (AmE), / ˌafɹəˈməʊməm mɛlɪˈɡiːtə / (BrE)
A West African plant of the ginger family, whose pungent, peppery seeds are used as a spice, also known as grains of paradise.
West African spice plant; grains of paradise

Examples

The seeds of Aframomum melegueta are often used to add a spicy flavor to West African dishes.
Traditional medicine utilizes Aframomum melegueta for its purported therapeutic properties.
In gourmet cuisine, chefs sometimes substitute Aframomum melegueta for black pepper to achieve a unique taste.

Etymology

From the genus name Aframomum (from Africa + Greek momum, a kind of spice) and the species name melegueta (from a West African language via Portuguese).
