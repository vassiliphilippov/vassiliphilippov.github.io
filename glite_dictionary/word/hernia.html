hernia

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɝː.ni.ə / (AmE), / ˈhɜː.ni.ə / (BrE)
A condition in which an organ or tissue pushes through an opening in the muscle or tissue that normally contains it, often resulting in a lump or bulge.
protrusion of an organ through muscle or tissue
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with an inguinal hernia after finding a noticeable bulge in his abdomen.
If left untreated, a hernia can cause serious complications and may require surgery.
Heavy lifting without proper technique can increase the risk of developing a hernia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rupture - a break or tear in tissue or an organ

Etymology

From Latin hernia (rupture), of uncertain origin.
