assignee

1. NOUN

/ əˌsaɪˈni / (AmE), / əˌsaɪˈniː / (BrE)
a person to whom a right, property, or responsibility is legally transferred, especially in a formal or contractual context
person receiving a legal transfer
legal

Examples

The contract ensures that any assignee has the same rights and obligations as the original party.
The assignee must notify the debtor within 30 days of the transfer of rights.
All intellectual property was transferred to the assignee after the merger.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: assignor - the person who transfers a right or property to another

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'assignatus', past participle of 'assignare' (to assign).
