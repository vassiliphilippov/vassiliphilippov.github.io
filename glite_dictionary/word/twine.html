twine

1. VERB

/ twaɪn / (AmE), / twaɪn / (BrE)
to make by twisting together, especially strands, threads, or fibers
form by twisting together strands

Examples

She twined several strands of thread to create a strong cord.
The vines quickly twine around the metal fence as they grow.
He twined together the strips of leather to make a simple rope.

Synset Examples

twine a rope

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twist - to form into a bent, curling, or spiral shape

Etymology

From Old English *twīnan* (to wind, twist), related to 'two' (referring to two things intertwined).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01520680-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ twaɪn / (AmE), / twaɪn / (BrE)
strong thread or light rope made of two or more strands twisted together, used for tying, binding, or packing.
lightweight cord made by twisting strands

Examples

She bought a roll of twine to tie up the packages.
The gardener used twine to support the tomato plants.
Pieces of twine were scattered across the workshop floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: string - thin cord used for tying or fastening
  • Degree Higher: rope - thick, strong cord made by twisting strands together
  • Degree Lower: thread - very thin strand of fiber

Etymology

From Old English *twīn* (thread, linen), related to *twī-*, meaning 'double, two' (cf. 'twist').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04345112-n
Synset POS: n
