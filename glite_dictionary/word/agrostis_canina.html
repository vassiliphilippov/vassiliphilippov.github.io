Agrostis canina

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡrɑstɪs kəˈnaɪnə / (AmE), / əˈɡrɒstɪs kəˈniːnə / (BrE)
A species of grass, commonly known as velvet bent, with slender stems and narrow leaves, often found in moist habitats.
species of grass; velvet bent

Examples

The golf course was seeded with Agrostis canina to provide a smooth putting surface.
Botanists often study Agrostis canina in wet meadows and marshes.
Many lawns in northern climates feature Agrostis canina because of its fine texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: velvet bent - common name for Agrostis canina, a type of grass

Etymology

From Latin: 'Agrostis' (type of grass), 'canina' (of a dog).
