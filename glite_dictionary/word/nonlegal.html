nonlegal

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˈliɡəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈliːɡəl / (BrE)
Not regulated, recognized, or sanctioned by law.
not recognized by law

Examples

The committee took nonlegal action to resolve the employee dispute.
His concerns were addressed through nonlegal channels, such as community mediation.
They received nonlegal advice from a support group instead of a lawyer.

Synset Examples

there were only extralegal recourses for their grievances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extralegal - beyond the authority of law
  • Antonym: legal - permitted by law; relating to the law

Etymology

From prefix 'non-' (not) + 'legal' (from Latin legalis, from lex 'law').
