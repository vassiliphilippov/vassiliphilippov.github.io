self-propelling

1. ADJ

/ sɛlf prəˈpɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / sɛlf prəˈpɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a vehicle or device) able to move or propel itself using its own power, without being pushed or pulled.
able to move by its own power

Examples

The scientists designed a self-propelling robot that can navigate rough terrain.
This self-propelling lawn mower saves both time and effort in the garden.
New advances have led to self-propelling wheelchairs, giving users greater independence.

Synset Examples

a self-propelled vehicle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-propelled - able to move by its own power

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' (referring to itself) and 'propelling' (moving forward); modeled after 'self-propelled'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlf prəˈpɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf prəˈpɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
Able to move forward by its own force or momentum, without external aid.
moving by own power or momentum

Examples

Certain robots are self-propelling and can navigate complex environments without human intervention.
He admired the ingenuity of the self-propelling wheelchair design.
Recent advances have made drones more self-propelling than ever before.

Synset Examples

a self-propelled egotist
the arms program is now self-propelled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-propelled - driven or operated by its own power

Etymology

From 'self' meaning 'oneself' and 'propel' from Latin 'propellere' (to drive forward).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00813733-s
Synset POS: s
