spatiotemporal

1. ADJ

/ ˌspeɪ.t̬ioʊˈtɛm.pɚ.əl / (AmE), / ˌspeɪ.ti.əʊˈtɛm.pər.əl / (BrE)
Relating to both space and time, or having both spatial and temporal aspects.
relating to space and time

Examples

The spatiotemporal distribution of rainfall can influence agricultural planning.
Our memories are organized in a spatiotemporal framework within the brain.
Researchers studied the spatiotemporal patterns of animal migration across the continent.

Etymology

From Latin 'spatium' (space) + 'temporalis' (of time).

2. ADJ

/ ˌspeɪ.ti.oʊˈtɛm.pɚ.əl / (AmE), / ˌspeɪ.ti.əʊˈtɛm.pər.əl / (BrE)
Relating to or existing in both space and time; having both spatial extent and temporal duration.
related to both space and time

Examples

The research investigates spatiotemporal patterns in urban development over the past century.
The animation showed the spatiotemporal spread of the disease across the country.
Scientists analyzed the spatiotemporal coherence of neuronal activity in the brain.

Synset Examples

spatiotemporal coherence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temporal - relating to time

Etymology

From Latin spatium (space) + temporalis (temporal, relating to time).
