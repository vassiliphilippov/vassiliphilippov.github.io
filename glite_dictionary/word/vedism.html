Vedism

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪdɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈviːdɪzəm / (BrE)
the ancient religion of the Vedic period in India, centered on the worship of deities and the performance of rituals as described in the Vedas
ancient Vedic religion of India

Examples

Vedism is considered the precursor to classical forms of Hinduism.
The rituals and hymns of Vedism are preserved in the four Vedas.
Scholars often study Vedism to understand the origins of Indian religious traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vedic - relating to the Vedas or the Vedic period

Etymology

From 'Veda', Sanskrit for 'knowledge', plus the English suffix '-ism'.
