Cyclostomata

1. NOUN

/ saɪ.kloʊˈstɑː.mətə / (AmE), / sɪ.klɒˈstɒ.mətə / (BrE)
An order or group of primitive jawless aquatic vertebrates, including lampreys and hagfishes, characterized by their round, sucker-like mouths.
jawless aquatic vertebrates group

Examples

Lampreys and hagfishes are members of the Cyclostomata, a group known for their jawless mouths.
The evolutionary significance of Cyclostomata lies in their primitive characteristics among vertebrates.
Students studied the anatomy of Cyclostomata during their zoology lab session.

Etymology

From Greek *kyklos* (circle) + *stoma* (mouth), referring to the round mouths of these animals.
