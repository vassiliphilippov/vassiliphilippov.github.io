marching

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrtʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of walking with regular, measured steps, especially in a procession or as part of a group
walking with regular steps in a group

Examples

The soldiers' marching echoed through the quiet city streets.
Marching in formation requires discipline and practice.
A band led the parade with lively marching music.

Synset Examples

it was a long march
we heard the sound of marching

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parade - a public procession, especially one celebrating a special day or event

Etymology

From the verb 'march,' from Old French 'marcher' (to stride, walk)
