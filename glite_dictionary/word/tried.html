tried

1. ADJ

/ traɪd / (AmE), / traɪd / (BrE)
(of a method, thing, or person) shown by experience to be reliable or effective.
proven to be reliable by experience

Examples

For this important project, we relied on tried methods to ensure success.
Many people prefer tried recipes that have stood the test of time.
She values the advice of tried friends who have always been there for her.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: untried - not yet tested or proved reliable

2. ADJ

/ traɪd / (AmE), / traɪd / (BrE)
tested and proven to be reliable or effective; shown by experience to be trustworthy or successful.
tested and proven reliable

Examples

She followed a tried recipe that always gave delicious results.
The organization relies on tried strategies for fundraising success.
Only methods that are tried and effective should be used in critical situations.

Synset Examples

a tested method

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tested - having been subjected to a test; tried out
  • Antonym: untried - not yet tested or proven

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'try', from Old French *trier* (to choose, sift, cull).
