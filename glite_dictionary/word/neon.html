neon

1. NOUN

/ ˈniːˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈniːɒn / (BrE)
a colorless, odorless chemical element (symbol Ne) that is an inert gas, occurring in small amounts in the air and giving a distinctive red glow when used in electric discharge tubes.
inert gaseous chemical element

Examples

The neon in the sign glowed brightly throughout the night.
Scientists isolate neon from the air by fractional distillation of liquid air.
A neon lamp produces a characteristic red-orange light in advertisements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: argon - another inert gaseous element
  • Synonym: helium - another inert gaseous element

Etymology

From Greek 'neos' meaning 'new'; named by Ramsay and Travers (1898) when discovered as a new element.

2. NOUN

/ ˈniːˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈniːɒn / (BrE)
a chemical element (symbol Ne), a colorless and odorless inert gas that emits a distinct red glow when electrified, used especially in signs and lighting
an inert gaseous chemical element; used in lights

Examples

The bright neon in the sign made the words glow vividly at night.
Scientists use neon to fill tubes for colorful lighting effects.
Natural neon is rare on Earth and is usually obtained from the air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: argon - another inert (noble) gaseous element; often used in lighting
  • Synonym: helium - a lighter inert gas in the noble gas group

Etymology

From Greek neos (new), as it was a newly discovered element in 1898.
