beehive

/ ˈbiˌhaɪv / (AmE), / ˈbiːˌhʌɪv / (BrE)
a women's hairstyle in which the hair is piled high in a conical shape, resembling a beehive
conical piled-up hairstyle

Examples

She was famous for her towering beehive that reached almost a foot above her head.
The retro beehive made a comeback at the fashion show last night.
Amy Winehouse often wore her signature beehive hairstyle on stage.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbiːˌhaɪv / (AmE), / ˈbiːˌhaɪv / (BrE)
a place, especially a workplace, where people are very busy and active
busy workplace

Examples

When I visited the newsroom, it was a beehive of activity as deadlines approached.
The kitchen became a beehive during the holidays, with everyone preparing food.
The hospital is always a beehive, with staff hurrying from room to room.

  • Synonym: hive - a place full of activity or busy people

3. NOUN

/ ˈbiːˌhaɪv / (AmE), / ˈbiːˌhaɪv / (BrE)
A man-made structure in which a colony of bees is kept for producing honey or pollinating crops.
man-made container for bees

Examples

The beehive in our garden produces several jars of honey every year.
After donning her suit, she carefully inspected each beehive for signs of disease.
Modern beehives make honey collection much easier for beekeepers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apiary - a place where beehives are kept for beekeeping

Etymology

From 'bee' + 'hive', dating to Middle English.

4. NOUN

/ ˈbiˌhaɪv / (AmE), / ˈbiːhaɪv / (BrE)
A structure, such as a hollow tree or an artificial box, that serves as a home for a colony of bees.
structure serving as a home for bees

Examples

A wild beehive was hidden inside the hollow trunk of the old oak tree.
Farmers often install a beehive in their gardens to encourage pollination.
When disturbed, bees will fiercely defend their beehive from intruders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apiary - a place where beehives are kept

Etymology

From 'bee' + 'hive', meaning a shelter where bees live and store honey.
