unaffected

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈfɛk.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈfɛk.tɪd / (BrE)
not changed, influenced, or altered by something; remaining the same despite an action or condition.
not changed; unchanged by influence or action

Examples

Despite the harsh weather, the structure remained unaffected by the storms.
The outcome of the experiment was unaffected by minor changes in temperature.
She appears unaffected by criticism and continues her work with confidence.

Synset Examples

entirely unaffected by each other's writings
fibers remained apparently unaffected by the treatment

Related Terms

  • Antonym: changed - having become different; altered
  • Antonym: altered - changed or made different

Etymology

From un- (prefix meaning 'not') + affected (past participle of 'affect').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00072600-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnəˈfɛktɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnəˈfɛktɪd / (BrE)
not influenced, moved, or concerned by something; unaware of or indifferent to it.
indifferent or not influenced by something

Examples

She seemed unaffected by the criticism and continued with her work.
Despite the chaos around him, he remained totally unaffected by the noise.
The children were largely unaffected by the changes at school.

Synset Examples

insensible to the suffering around him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indifferent - having no particular interest or sympathy; unconcerned
  • Synonym: unconcerned - not worried or not interested
  • Antonym: concerned - worried or involved; the opposite of unaffected

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + affected; 'affected' from Latin affectus (influenced).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02115107-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌʌnəˈfɛktɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnəˈfɛktɪd / (BrE)
(of a person or their manner) natural, sincere, and free from pretence or artificiality.
natural and sincere; not artificial

Examples

Despite her fame, her manner remains remarkably unaffected.
He spoke with an unaffected honesty that won everyone's trust.
The child's laughter was pure and unaffected.

Synset Examples

an unaffected grace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be; sincere
  • Synonym: natural - having a quality that is not artificial or affected
  • Antonym: affected - artificial, pretentious, or designed to impress

Etymology

Formed from the prefix un- (not) and affected (artificial or pretentious).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00074954-a
Synset POS: a

4. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈfɛk.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈfɛk.tɪd / (BrE)
not influenced by strong emotion; showing no visible emotional reaction.
not emotionally moved

Examples

Even when criticized harshly, she remained unaffected by their words.
He listened to the tragic story with an unaffected expression.
Despite the chaos around him, Mark appeared completely unaffected.

Synset Examples

always appeared completely unmoved and imperturbable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unmoved - not affected by emotion; not displaying feelings
  • Synonym: imperturbable - unable to be upset or excited; calm
  • Antonym: moved - emotionally affected; touched

Etymology

From un- (not) + affected (from Latin affectus, meaning 'emotion' or 'feeling').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01564713-a
Synset POS: a
