Eucalyptus fraxinoides

1. NOUN

/ juːˈkælɪptəs ˌfræksɪˈnɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / juːˈkælɪptəs ˌfraksɪˈnɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A large tree native to southeastern Australia, with dark compact bark on the lower trunk and smooth white bark above, yielding timber similar to that of European or American ashes.
Australian tree with bark like ash, used for timber

Examples

The timber from Eucalyptus fraxinoides is often compared to that of European ash for its quality.
Forests of Eucalyptus fraxinoides cover parts of southeastern Australia, forming distinctive landscapes.
The smooth white bark of Eucalyptus fraxinoides makes it easily recognizable among other tree species.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'eucalyptus' (well-covered); species name from Latin 'fraxinus' (ash tree) and '-oides' (resembling), meaning 'ash-like'.
