linguist

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪŋɡwɪst / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋɡwɪst / (BrE)
A person who studies or is an expert in linguistics; a specialist in the scientific study of language.
expert in linguistics

Examples

The conference attracted leading linguists from around the world.
As a professional linguist, she can speak five different languages fluently.
The young linguist published a groundbreaking paper on language acquisition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: philologist - a person who studies language in written historical sources

Etymology

From Latin 'lingua' (tongue, language) via French 'linguiste'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪŋɡwɪst / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋɡwɪst / (BrE)
a person who is able to speak several languages fluently
person who speaks several languages

Examples

She is a talented linguist who can converse in five languages.
As a linguist, he found it easy to travel and adapt to different cultures.
Employers value a linguist for their ability to communicate with international clients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polyglot - a person who knows and is able to use several languages

Etymology

From Latin 'lingua' (language, tongue) + '-ist' (one who practices).
