verbena

1. NOUN

/ vərˈbiːnə / (AmE), / vəˈbiːnə / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Verbena, often grown for its colorful flower spikes
flowering plant genus Verbena

Examples

The garden was brightened by a cluster of blooming verbena near the fence.
She chose purple verbena to add a splash of color to her balcony.
Many butterflies are attracted to verbena because of its vibrant, nectar-rich flowers.

Etymology

From Latin 'verbēna', meaning 'sacred bough' or 'leafy branch'.
