Verbena

1. NOUN

/ vərˈbiːnə / (AmE), / vɜːˈbiːnə / (BrE)
A genus of herbaceous perennial plants and subshrubs, commonly known for their attractive, often colorful flowers; the type genus of the family Verbenaceae.
a genus of flowering plants in Verbenaceae

Examples

Many gardeners select Verbena for their vibrant and long-lasting blooms.
Verbena species can thrive in a variety of climates, making them popular in ornamental gardens.
The purple flowers of some Verbena cultivars attract butterflies and bees.

Etymology

From Latin *verbena*, meaning 'sacred bough or leafy branch'.
