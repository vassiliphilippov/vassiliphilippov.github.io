Fulmarus

1. NOUN

/ fʊlˈmɛərəs / (AmE), / fʊlˈmɑːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of seabirds known as fulmars, found in cold northern and southern oceans, related to petrels.
a genus of seabirds (fulmars)

Examples

The genus Fulmarus includes both the northern and southern fulmars.
Researchers observed mating behaviors in Fulmarus colonies on remote cliffs.
Several conservation efforts focus on protecting Fulmarus habitats from climate change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fulmar - a northern seabird of the tubenose family, related to the petrels

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Fulmarus, formed irregularly from English 'fulmar'.

2. NOUN

/ fʊlˈmɛərəs / (AmE), / fʊlˈmɑːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of seabirds in the family Procellariidae, known as fulmars, found mainly in the North Atlantic and North Pacific oceans.
genus of seabirds called fulmars

Examples

The Fulmarus is widely distributed across subarctic and temperate oceans.
Members of the genus Fulmarus are robust seabirds with tube-shaped nostrils.
Ornithologists studied the nesting habits of Fulmarus on remote islands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fulmar - a seabird of the genus Fulmarus

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on 'fulmar', itself from Old Norse 'fúll' (foul) + 'már' (gull), referring to the birds' strong smell.
