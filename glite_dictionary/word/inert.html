inert

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈnɝt / (AmE), / ɪˈnɜːt / (BrE)
(of a substance) having little or no ability to react chemically; not active in chemical processes.
chemically inactive; unreactive

Examples

Helium is an inert gas, so it does not readily form compounds.
Gold remains inert under most conditions, making it ideal for jewelry.
The tank was filled with an inert substance to prevent unwanted chemical reactions.

Synset Examples

inert matter
an indifferent chemical in a reaction

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reactive - tending to react chemically; opposite of inert
  • Synonym: inactive - not active; without action, also used of substances

Etymology

From Latin inert-, iners meaning 'unskilled, inactive'.

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈnɝt / (AmE), / ɪˈnɜːt / (BrE)
lacking the ability or strength to move or act; slow and apathetic.
slow, inactive, or lacking energy

Examples

After weeks of illness, he felt too inert to even get out of bed.
The conversation grew inert, and nobody seemed interested in continuing it.
Her inert attitude made it difficult for her to succeed in the fast-paced job.

Synset Examples

she was fat and inert
a sluggish worker
a mind grown torpid in old age

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sluggish - lacking energy or alertness
  • Synonym: torpid - mentally or physically inactive
  • Antonym: energetic - showing or involving great activity or vitality

Etymology

From Latin iners, inertis (unskilled, idle, lazy), from in- (not) + ars, artis (skill).

3. ADJ

/ ɪˈnɝt / (AmE), / ɪˈnɜːt / (BrE)
lacking the ability or strength to move or act; motionless or inactive.
lacking ability to move or act

Examples

The patient remained inert on the hospital bed, unable to speak or move.
After the accident, the car was left inert in the middle of the road.
The chemicals are stored in inert containers to prevent any reactions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: active - doing things; moving energetically
  • Synonym: motionless - not moving; still

Etymology

From Latin 'iners' (unskilled, idle), from 'in-' (not) + 'ars' (skill).
