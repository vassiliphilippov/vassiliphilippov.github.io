fashionable

1. ADJ

/ ˈfæʃ.ən.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈfæʃ.ən.ə.bəl / (BrE)
having elegance, taste, or refinement in manners or dress; typical of or in accordance with the current style or trend
having style or elegance; in current fashion

Examples

Her fashionable outfit attracted many compliments at the gala.
The restaurant is popular among fashionable young professionals.
He moved to a fashionable neighborhood known for its upscale boutiques.

Synset Examples

a little less posh but every bit as stylish as Lord Peter Wimsey
the stylish resort of Gstadd

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stylish - having elegance or style
  • Antonym: unfashionable - not in accordance with current fashion; lacking style

Etymology

From fashion + -able, meaning suitable to fashion, attested since the late 15th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfæʃ.ən.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈfæʃ.ən.ə.bəl / (BrE)
popular among people in a particular time or group; following the current style or trend
following the current fashion or trend

Examples

She always wears fashionable clothes that turn heads wherever she goes.
It's no longer fashionable to smoke in public places.
That neighborhood has become one of the most fashionable parts of the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stylish - having elegance or style
  • Synonym: trendy - very fashionable or up to date
  • Antonym: unfashionable - not following current fashions or trends

Etymology

From French 'à la mode' (in fashion), based on 'fashion' + '-able'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01821590-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈfæʃənəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈfæʃənəbl̩ / (BrE)
following or in keeping with current styles or popular trends, especially in clothing, behavior, or lifestyle.
in accordance with current styles or trends

Examples

She wore a fashionable dress to the party that everyone admired.
The restaurant quickly became the most fashionable spot in town.
Living in a fashionable neighborhood is often associated with high status.

Synset Examples

fashionable clothing
the fashionable side of town
a fashionable cafe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stylish - having style, especially in dress or design
  • Synonym: trendy - very fashionable or up to date
  • Antonym: outdated - no longer in fashion or use
  • Antonym: unfashionable - not in keeping with current fashion

Etymology

From fashion + -able; earliest use in late Middle English, influenced by French 'à la mode'.
