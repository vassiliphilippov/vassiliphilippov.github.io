jolly

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑli / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒli / (BrE)
a small boat (yawl) carried by a ship, used by sailors for general tasks and transport
small ship's boat for general work

Examples

The crew lowered the jolly to ferry supplies to the shore.
Old sailing ships often carried a jolly for errands and transport.
Three sailors set out in the jolly to check the anchor line.

Etymology

Probably from 'jolly boat', of uncertain origin, possibly influenced by Dutch 'jol' (small boat).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɑli / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒli / (BrE)
cheerful and lively; full of high-spirited happiness and good humor
cheerful and lively

Examples

The jolly crowd erupted into laughter as the entertainer took the stage.
Everyone enjoyed the jolly atmosphere at the holiday party.
He had a jolly smile that made everyone around him feel welcome.

Etymology

3. VERB

/ ˈdʒɑli / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒli / (BrE)
to act in a playful or teasing way, to amuse or cheer someone by behaving in a fun or lighthearted manner
to tease or act in a playful way

Examples

The children jollied together in the park, laughing and telling jokes.
If you jolly him along, he’ll soon forget he was upset.
They spent the afternoon jollying each other with harmless pranks.

Synset Examples

After we relaxed, we just kidded around

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to kid around - to behave in a silly or playful way
  • Synonym: tease - to make fun of someone in a playful or mocking way

Etymology

From Middle English, probably from Old French joli, meaning 'festive, merry'.
