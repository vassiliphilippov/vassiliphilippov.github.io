2. NOUN

/ kʌb / (AmE), / kʌb / (BrE)
the young of certain carnivorous mammals, such as a bear, wolf, or lion
young of a carnivorous mammal

Examples

The lioness carefully carried her cub to a safer place.
A bear cub can be very playful and energetic.
We watched as the wolf cub explored its surroundings, closely watched by its mother.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pup - the young of certain other animals such as dogs or seals
  • Antonym: adult - a fully grown animal

Etymology

Probably from Middle English, from Old French *cub*, of uncertain origin.
