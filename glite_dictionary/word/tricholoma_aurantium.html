Tricholoma aurantium

1. NOUN

/ trɪkəˌloʊmə ɔːˈræntiəm / (AmE), / trɪkəˌləʊmə ɔːˈrantɪəm / (BrE)
A species of orange-tan mushroom whose gills turn brown with maturity, notable for its strong odor and taste.
orange-tan mushroom species

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with Tricholoma aurantium growing among the fallen leaves.
Experts advise against eating Tricholoma aurantium due to its strong taste and potential toxicity.
She photographed a cluster of Tricholoma aurantium while hiking in the woods.

Etymology

From New Latin, genus 'Tricholoma' (from Greek 'thrix', hair, and 'loma', fringe) and Latin 'aurantium' (orange).
