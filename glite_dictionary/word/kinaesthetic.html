kinaesthetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɪnəsˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌkɪnɪsˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to the perception of body movements, position, or muscular tension; connected with the sense of physical movement
relating to the sense of movement

Examples

Children with a kinaesthetic learning style learn best through hands-on activities.
The dance instructor emphasized kinaesthetic awareness to help students control their movements.
Many physical therapists use kinaesthetic feedback to guide patients during rehabilitation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kinesthetic - relating to the perception of movement (American spelling)

Etymology

From Greek kinein (to move) + aisthēsis (perception).
