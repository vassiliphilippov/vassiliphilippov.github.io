ganof

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑːnəf / (AmE), / ˈɡænəf / (BrE)
(Yiddish) a thief or dishonest or unscrupulous person, often used as a term of abuse.
dishonest person; thief (Yiddish)

Examples

I can't believe that ganof tried to steal my bicycle in broad daylight.
The old shopkeeper called the pickpocket a ganof and chased him down the street.
Don’t trust him—he’s a real ganof who cheats everyone he deals with.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thief - a person who steals, especially secretly or without open force
  • Synonym: scoundrel - a dishonest or unscrupulous person; a rogue

Etymology

From Yiddish גנב (ganév), from Hebrew גָּנָב (ganáv, ‘thief’).
