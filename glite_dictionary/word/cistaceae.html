Cistaceae

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈsteɪsiː / (AmE), / sɪˈstæsɪiː / (BrE)
a family of shrubs or woody herbs native to temperate regions, especially the Mediterranean, known commonly as the rockrose family
family of shrubs/herbs (rockrose)

Examples

The Cistaceae family includes many species adapted to dry Mediterranean climates.
Botanists have studied the ecological roles of Cistaceae in preventing soil erosion.
Some members of the Cistaceae are cultivated for their ornamental flowers.

Etymology

From New Latin, from 'Cistus' (a genus in the family) + '-aceae' (a standard suffix for plant family names).
