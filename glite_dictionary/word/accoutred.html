accoutred

1. ADJ

/ əˈkuːtɚd / (AmE), / əˈkuːtəd / (BrE)
equipped with the necessary articles or equipment for a particular purpose, especially for military or ceremonial use
equipped with necessary articles or gear
british

Examples

The soldiers were accoutred with helmets and body armor before heading into battle.
She arrived at the campsite fully accoutred for a week in the wilderness.
All participants must be accoutred with the necessary safety gear to enter the laboratory.

Synset Examples

troops accoutered for battle
properly accoutered for the trip

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equipped - provided with the necessary equipment or supplies
  • Antonym: unprepared - not equipped or ready for a particular purpose

Etymology

From French 'accoutrer' (to equip), influenced by Middle English.
