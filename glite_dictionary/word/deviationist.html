deviationist

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːviˈeɪʃənɪst / (AmE), / ˌdiːvɪˈeɪʃənɪst / (BrE)
A person who departs from the official party line or accepted ideology, especially in a communist context.
ideological dissenter, especially in communism

Examples

The party accused him of being a deviationist after he challenged the leadership's policies.
During the purges, many intellectuals were labeled as deviationists for their unorthodox views.
She was expelled from the movement as a political deviationist who refused to follow the party line.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heretic - a person holding beliefs contrary to established dogma
  • Antonym: orthodox - following the accepted or traditional beliefs; opposite of deviationist

Etymology

Formed from 'deviation' and the suffix '-ist', denoting a person who deviates from a doctrine.
