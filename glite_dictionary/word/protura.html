Protura

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈtjʊrə / (AmE), / prəʊˈtjʊərə / (BrE)
A small order of minute, wingless, soil-dwelling arthropods, also known as telsontails, belonging to the class Entognatha.
order of tiny wingless arthropods (telsontails)

Examples

Soil scientists often study Protura to understand biodiversity within decaying leaf litter.
The Protura are easily overlooked due to their minute size and lack of wings.
Researchers discovered several new species of Protura in the alpine forest soil.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, from Greek prōtos (first) + oura (tail).
