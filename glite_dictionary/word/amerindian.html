Amerindian

1. NOUN

/ ˌæm.əˈrɪn.di.ən / (AmE), / ˌam.əˈrɪn.dɪ.ən / (BrE)
a member of the indigenous peoples of North or South America, especially those existing before the arrival of Europeans
indigenous person of the Americas

Examples

Many Amerindian cultures developed advanced agricultural systems and complex societies long before European contact.
The study focused on the traditional medicines used by Amerindian healers in the Amazon region.
Her ancestry is a blend of European and Amerindian heritage, reflecting the region’s history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Native American - a member of the indigenous peoples of the United States (excluding Alaska and Hawaii)
  • Synonym: indigenous - originating or occurring naturally in a particular place; native
  • Antonym: European - a person from Europe, often contrasted with Amerindian in this context

Etymology

Blend of 'American' and 'Indian', coined in the 20th century.
