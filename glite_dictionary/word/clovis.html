Clovis

1. PROPN

/ ˈkloʊvɪs / (AmE), / ˈkləʊvɪs / (BrE)
(c. 466–511) King of the Franks who unified Gaul, established Paris as his capital, and founded the Frankish monarchy.
king of the Franks (466–511)

Examples

Clovis played a crucial role in unifying the various Frankish tribes under a single rule.
The reign of Clovis marked the beginning of the Merovingian dynasty in medieval Europe.
Many historians consider Clovis to be one of the founding fathers of modern France.

Etymology

From Latin 'Chlodovechus', later rendered in French as 'Louis'.
