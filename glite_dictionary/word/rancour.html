rancour

1. NOUN

/ ˈræŋkər / (AmE), / ˈræŋkə / (BrE)
A feeling of deep and persistent resentment or ill-will.
deep resentment or ill-will
british

Examples

Her voice was full of rancour as she recounted the events of that day.
Years after the dispute, a sense of rancour lingered between the two families.
He spoke with barely concealed rancour about his former business partner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rancor - American spelling of 'rancour'
  • Synonym: resentment - a feeling of persistent bitterness or indignation
  • Antonym: forgiveness - letting go of resentment or anger

Etymology

From Old French 'rancor', from Late Latin 'rancor' (a rancid smell; grudge, bitterness), from 'rancere' (to stink).
