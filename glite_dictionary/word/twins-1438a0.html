twins

1. NOUN

/ twɪnz / (AmE), / twɪnz / (BrE)
(Mineralogy) Two intergrown crystals that are mirror images of each other, formed such that one is a reflection of the other across a plane or axis.
two intergrown, mirrored crystals (mineralogy)
medical

Examples

The mineral specimen contained twins that were clearly visible under the microscope.
Formation of twins in crystals can affect their optical and physical properties.
Geologists identified the presence of twins by examining the crystal structure of the sample.
