scolion

1. NOUN

/ ˈskoʊliən / (AmE), / ˈskəʊlɪɒn / (BrE)
A song, often improvised, sung by guests at an ancient Greek banquet.
song sung at Greek banquet

Examples

During the symposium, each guest was invited to perform a scolion as part of the evening’s entertainment.
Scholars have studied the role of the scolion in fostering social bonds in ancient Greece.
He composed a witty scolion that delighted everyone at the banquet.

Etymology

From Greek *skolion* (banquet song), from *skolios* (crooked, as in the passing of the lyre in a winding manner).
