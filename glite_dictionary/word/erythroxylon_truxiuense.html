Erythroxylon truxiuense

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌrɪθrɑkˈsaɪlən truˌkʃuˈɛnsɪ / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪθrɒkˈsaɪlən truˌksjuˈɛnsɪ / (BrE)
A South American shrub whose leaves are used as a source of cocaine.
South American shrub; source of cocaine
medical

Examples

The leaves of Erythroxylon truxiuense are processed to extract cocaine.
Researchers are studying Erythroxylon truxiuense for its chemical properties.
Erythroxylon truxiuense is native to regions in South America where it has been cultivated for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Erythroxylon coca - Another South American shrub; most common source of cocaine

Etymology

From Greek erythros (red), xylon (wood), and truxiuense (referring to Truxillo, a locality in South America).
