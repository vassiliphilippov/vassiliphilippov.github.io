Dendromecon

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛndroʊˈmɛkən / (AmE), / ˌdɛndrəˈmiːkɒn / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs in the poppy family (Papaveraceae), known as bush poppies, native to California and Baja California.
genus of bush poppies

Examples

The Dendromecon is admired for its bright yellow flowers and drought tolerance.
Several species within the genus Dendromecon are cultivated in native plant gardens.
We found a rare Dendromecon growing along the hillside during our hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bush poppy - A shrub in the genus Dendromecon with yellow flowers

Etymology

From Greek dendron (tree) + mekon (poppy), referring to its tree-like poppy appearance.

2. NOUN

/ dɛnˈdroʊmiˌkɒn / (AmE), / dɛnˈdrəʊmɪˌkɒn / (BrE)
A small genus of flowering plants in the poppy family (Papaveraceae), known as bush poppy, consisting of a single species native to California.
a genus of bush poppy

Examples

The Dendromecon shrub produces bright yellow flowers in spring.
Many gardeners in California cultivate Dendromecon for its drought tolerance.
Botanists recently reclassified a subspecies within the Dendromecon genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bush poppy - a common name for Dendromecon species

Etymology

From Greek dendron (tree) + mekon (poppy), referring to its shrubby poppy-like appearance.
