chordamesoderm

1. NOUN

/ kɔːr.dəˈmɛs.əˌdɝm / (AmE), / kɔː.dəˈmɛs.əˌdɜːm / (BrE)
the region of the embryonic mesoderm that develops into the notochord during vertebrate development
embryonic mesoderm forming the notochord
medical

Examples

During embryonic development, the chordamesoderm gives rise to the notochord in vertebrates.
Researchers studied how the chordamesoderm signals to other tissues during early formation.
Defects in the chordamesoderm can lead to problems in the formation of the spinal column.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: mesoderm - the middle layer of an embryo in early development
  • Synonym: notochord - a flexible rod-shaped structure that supports the body in all embryonic and some adult chordate animals

Etymology

From Greek 'chordē' (string, cord) + 'mesoderm,' indicating its mesodermal origin and role in forming the notochord.
