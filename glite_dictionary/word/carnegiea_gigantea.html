Carnegiea gigantea

1. NOUN

/ kɑrˈneɪɡiə dʒaɪˈɡæntiə / (AmE), / kɑːˈniːɡiə dʒaɪˈɡantiə / (BrE)
A very large, tree-like cactus native to the deserts of the southwestern United States, known for its thick columnar trunk, sparse branches, white flowers, and edible red fruit; commonly known as the saguaro cactus.
species of giant cactus (saguaro)

Examples

The Sonoran Desert is famous for its towering Carnegiea gigantea that can live for over 150 years.
Researchers studied the blooming cycles of Carnegiea gigantea in response to climate change.
The fruit of Carnegiea gigantea is harvested by Native American communities for its sweet, edible pulp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saguaro - common name for Carnegiea gigantea, a giant cactus species of the Sonoran Desert

Etymology

Named after Andrew Carnegie; from the genus name Carnegiea and species epithet gigantea, meaning 'giant' in Latin.
