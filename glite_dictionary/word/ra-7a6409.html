Ra

1. NOUN

/ ɑːr eɪ / (AmE), / ɑː rɑː / (BrE)
the chemical symbol for radium, an intensely radioactive metallic element found in trace amounts in uranium ores
chemical symbol for radium

Examples

The element with the symbol Ra was discovered by Marie Curie in 1898.
Because Ra is highly radioactive, it must be handled with extreme care in laboratories.
Traces of Ra are found in some uranium ores mined for scientific research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radium - a radioactive metallic chemical element (symbol Ra)

Etymology

Shortened from 'radium'. 'Radium' from Latin 'radius' (ray).

2. PROPN

/ rɑː / (AmE), / rɑː / (BrE)
the ancient Egyptian sun god, often depicted with the head of a hawk, regarded as a universal creator and later merged with Amen to become Amen-Ra, king of the gods.
ancient Egyptian sun god

Examples

In ancient mythology, Ra was worshipped as the supreme creator and giver of life.
The priests of Ra held great influence in the city of Heliopolis.
Many pharaohs claimed to be the earthly sons of Ra, solidifying their divine right to rule.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Amen-Ra - the merged form of Amen and Ra, king of the Egyptian gods

Etymology

From Egyptian *Rꜥ* (sun).
