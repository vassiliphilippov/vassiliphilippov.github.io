to louden

1. VERB

/ ˈlaʊdən / (AmE), / ˈlaʊdən / (BrE)
to become louder; to increase in volume or intensity of sound
become louder

Examples

As the storm approached, the wind began to louden outside the window.
The music will louden during the climax of the performance.
Voices in the hallway loudened as more students arrived.

Synset Examples

The room loudened considerably

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to soften - to become softer, especially in sound
  • Synonym: to amplify - to increase the volume of sound

Etymology

Formed from 'loud' + '-en', a common English verb-forming suffix.

2. VERB

/ ˈlaʊdən / (AmE), / ˈlaʊdən / (BrE)
to make or become louder; to cause something to become loud
to make louder

Examples

Gradually, the music began to louden as the band reached the chorus.
She asked him to louden his voice so everyone in the room could hear.
As the storm approached, the wind seemed to louden with every passing minute.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: to deafen - to make someone unable to hear, especially with loud noise
  • Antonym: to soften - to make or become softer; to reduce volume or intensity of a sound
  • Synonym: to amplify - to increase the volume of sound
