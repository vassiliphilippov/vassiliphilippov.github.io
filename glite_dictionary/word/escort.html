escort

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛsˌkɔrt / (AmE), / ˈɛskɔːt / (BrE)
the act of going with someone or something to provide protection or as a courtesy
act of accompanying for protection

Examples

The president traveled with a military escort for added security.
A police escort guided the ambulance through the heavy traffic.
The convoy moved under the escort of two fighter jets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convoy - a group of vehicles or ships traveling together for protection

Etymology

From French escorte, from Italian scorta, from scorgere ‘to guide’.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00829443-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛskɔrt / (AmE), / ˈɛskɔːt / (BrE)
a person whose job is to accompany and protect or assist someone, especially on a journey or at public events
person hired to accompany and assist

Examples

The celebrity arrived at the premiere with a hired escort for security.
A police escort accompanied the ambassador to the conference.
She worked as an escort, assisting business travelers during their visits to the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaperone - a person who accompanies and looks after another, especially a younger woman
  • Synonym: bodyguard - a person employed to protect someone from harm or danger

Etymology

From French 'escorter', from Italian 'scorta' (guard, protection).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10083268-n
Synset POS: n

/ ˈɛskɔrt / (AmE), / ˈɛskɔːt / (BrE)
