Jabat al-Tahrir al-Filistiniyyah

1. PROPN

/ dʒæˈbɑːt æl tɑːˈriːr æl fɪlɪstiːˈnijə / (AmE), / dʒəˈbɑːt æl tɑːˈriːə æl fɪlɪstiːˈnijə / (BrE)
A Palestinian terrorist group formed in 1977 after splitting from the Popular Front for the Liberation of Palestine, later becoming a satellite of al-Fatah and known for attacks on Israel across the Lebanese border.
Palestinian terrorist group (founded 1977)

Examples

The Jabat al-Tahrir al-Filistiniyyah emerged as a separate entity after breaking away from a larger Palestinian organization.
Intelligence reports linked the Jabat al-Tahrir al-Filistiniyyah to several attacks across the Lebanese border in the 1980s.
Some historians discuss the role that Jabat al-Tahrir al-Filistiniyyah played in shifting alliances within Palestinian politics.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Popular Front for the Liberation of Palestine - Leftist Palestinian nationalist organization from which Jabat al-Tahrir al-Filistiniyyah split
  • Degree Higher: al-Fatah - Major Palestinian political and military organization to which Jabat al-Tahrir al-Filistiniyyah became affiliated

Etymology

From Arabic جبهة التحرير الفلسطينية (jabhat at-taḥrīr al-filasṭīniyya), meaning 'Palestinian Liberation Front'.
