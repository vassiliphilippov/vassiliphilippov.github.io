infamy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.fə.mi / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fə.mi / (BrE)
the state of being well known for something bad or evil; evil fame or notoriety
evil fame or notoriety

Examples

The dictator's brutal actions assured his infamy in history books.
Her betrayal would be remembered with infamy for generations to come.
The scandal brought such infamy that he could never return to public life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notoriety - the state of being famous for something bad
  • Antonym: fame - the state of being widely known or talked about, usually positively

Etymology

From Latin infamia (disgrace, dishonor), from infamis (of ill repute).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.fə.mi / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fə.mi / (BrE)
the state of being well known for having done something shameful, shocking, or wicked; extreme dishonor or disgrace
state of extreme dishonor or notoriety

Examples

He will always be remembered for the infamy of his actions during the scandal.
The dictator's name lives in infamy throughout the nation.
The attack brought a new level of infamy upon the organization.

Synset Examples

a date which will live in infamy
the name was a by-word of scorn and opprobrium throughout the city

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notoriety - the state of being famous or well known for a bad quality or deed
  • Antonym: renown - the state of being widely acclaimed or highly honored, typically for good reasons

Etymology

From Latin infamia (disgrace, ill fame), from in- (not) + fama (reputation, fame).
