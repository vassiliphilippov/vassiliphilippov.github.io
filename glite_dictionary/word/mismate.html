to mismate

1. VERB

/ ˌmɪsˈmeɪt / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈmeɪt / (BrE)
to pair or mate with an unsuitable or incompatible partner
to pair with an unsuitable mate

Examples

It's important not to mismate animals in breeding programs to ensure healthy offspring.
If you mismate these plants, they may not produce viable seeds.
The experiment failed because the scientist unintentionally mismated the test subjects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mate - to pair for reproduction or companionship
  • Antonym: match - to pair suitably or appropriately

Etymology

From prefix mis- (wrongly) + mate (to pair).
