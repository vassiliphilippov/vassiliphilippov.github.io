Chrysothamnus

1. NOUN

/ kraɪsəˈθæmnəs / (AmE), / krɪsəˈθamnəs / (BrE)
A genus of low, branching shrubs native to western North America, commonly known as rabbitbrush.
genus of shrubs in western North America

Examples

The dry plains were dotted with Chrysothamnus in full yellow bloom.
Botanists studied the impact of grazing on local Chrysothamnus populations.
Many birds nest among the branches of Chrysothamnus shrubs.

Etymology

From Greek 'chrysos' (gold) and 'thamnus' (shrub), referring to the golden flowers.
