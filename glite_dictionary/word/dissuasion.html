dissuasion

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsweɪʒən / (AmE), / dɪˈsweɪʒ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act of persuading someone not to do something
the act of discouraging or advising against

Examples

Her repeated dissuasion finally convinced him not to invest in the risky venture.
The company launched a campaign of dissuasion against buying counterfeit products.
His words of dissuasion made me reconsider my decision to quit my job.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: persuasion - the act of convincing someone to do something
  • Synonym: discouragement - the act of making someone less confident or enthusiastic

Etymology

From Latin dissuasio, from dissuadere (to dissuade).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈsweɪʒən / (AmE), / dɪˈsweɪʒ(ə)n / (BrE)
The act of persuading someone not to do something or not to believe something.
persuading not to do something

Examples

The dissuasion of reckless driving is a key goal of public safety campaigns.
Despite his friend's dissuasion, he went ahead with his risky investment.
Her parents' dissuasion made her reconsider studying abroad.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: persuasion - the act of persuading someone to do or believe something

Etymology

From Latin dissuasio, dissuasionis, from dissuadere (to advise against).
