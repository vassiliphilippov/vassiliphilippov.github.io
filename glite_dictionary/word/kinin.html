kinin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaɪnɪn / (AmE), / ˈkaɪnɪn / (BrE)
any of a class of plant hormones that promote cell division and delay the aging of leaves
plant hormone promoting cell division

Examples

Kinin levels rise in plant tissues during periods of rapid cell division and growth.
Gardeners may apply synthetic kinin to encourage greener, longer-lasting leaves.
Research shows that kinin can help delay leaf senescence in many crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cytokinin - a specific type of kinin plant hormone
