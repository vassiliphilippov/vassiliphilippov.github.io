vinegar

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪnəɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈvɪnɪɡə / (BrE)
a sour-tasting liquid made by fermenting alcoholic liquids like wine or cider, used as a condiment or for preserving food
sour liquid condiment

Examples

The recipe calls for two tablespoons of vinegar to give the dressing a tangy flavor.
She cleaned the windows with a mixture of vinegar and water.
A splash of vinegar helps preserve the pickled cucumbers for months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acetic acid - a chemical compound that is the main constituent of vinegar apart from water

Etymology

From Old French 'vinaigre', from 'vin' (wine) + 'aigre' (sour), from Latin 'vinum' (wine) and 'acer' (sour).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɪnəɡər / (AmE), / ˈvɪnɪɡə / (BrE)
a sharp-tasting liquid made from the fermentation of alcoholic liquids, used to flavor or preserve food
a sour liquid for flavoring or preserving food

Examples

She added a splash of vinegar to the salad dressing for a tangy flavor.
Cleaning with vinegar is an effective natural alternative to chemical products.
The recipe calls for white vinegar to pickle the cucumbers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acetic acid - the main component of vinegar apart from water

Etymology

From Old French 'vinaigre', from 'vin' (wine) + 'aigre' (sour), from Latin 'vinum' (wine) + 'acer' (sour).
