St. Louis

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈluːɪs / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈluːɪs / (BrE)
a major city in eastern Missouri situated on the Mississippi River, known as a historic river port and staging area for westward expansion in the 19th century
major city on the Mississippi River in Missouri

Examples

The Gateway Arch in St. Louis is a famous symbol of the city’s historical connection to the American West.
Many immigrants passed through St. Louis before traveling further west in the 1800s.
Major league baseball fans know St. Louis as the home of the Cardinals.

Etymology

Named after Louis IX of France, 'Saint Louis' in French.
