fabled

1. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪbəld / (AmE), / ˈfeɪbəld / (BrE)
celebrated in fable or legend; famous in stories and legends, often with exaggerated or mythical qualities
celebrated in fable or legend

Examples

The fabled city of Atlantis has fascinated explorers for centuries.
He told us a story about a fabled treasure hidden deep in the jungle.
Many tourists visit Greece to see sites mentioned in fabled myths and legends.

Synset Examples

the fabled Paul Bunyan and his blue ox
legendary exploits of Jesse James

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legendary - described or celebrated in legend
  • Synonym: mythical - existing only in myths or legends

Etymology

From Middle English, past participle of 'fable' from Old French 'fable', from Latin 'fabula' (story).
