Set

1. PROPN

/ sɛt / (AmE), / sɛt / (BrE)
an evil god in ancient Egyptian mythology, often depicted with the head of a beast with tall square ears and a long snout; brother and murderer of Osiris
evil Egyptian god, murderer of Osiris

Examples

Many ancient tales describe how Set killed his brother Osiris out of jealousy and ambition.
The priests depicted Set with a mysterious animal head, symbolizing chaos and destruction.
In Egyptian mythology, Set was eventually defeated by Horus, restoring balance to the gods.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Osiris - Egyptian god, brother and rival of Set
  • Synonym: Anubis - Egyptian god of mummification, related mythological context

Etymology

From ancient Egyptian 'Seth' or 'Setekh', name of the god.
