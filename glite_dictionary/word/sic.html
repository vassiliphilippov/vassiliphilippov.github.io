sic

1. ADV

/ sɪk / (AmE), / sɪk / (BrE)
used in brackets after a quoted word or phrase to show that it is written exactly as in the original, even if it appears to be a mistake.
used to show a quoted error is original

Examples

The letter read, "We was sic delighted to see you."
The newspaper printed the sentence as it appeared: "He done it all by hisself sic."
The transcript includes the phrase, "They is sic preparing for the event."

Etymology

From Latin sic (thus, so).

2. VERB

/ sɪk / (AmE), / sɪk / (BrE)
to urge (an animal, especially a dog, or a person) to attack someone or something
set an animal or person to attack

Examples

The guard sicked his dog on the trespasser.
She threatened to sic her lawyer on anyone who defamed her.
He sics his fierce cats on mice that invade the cellar.

Synset Examples

The owner sicked his dogs on the intruders
the shaman sics sorcerers on the evil spirits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attack - to try to hurt or damage someone or something

Etymology

Possibly a variant of the command "seek" used in dog training.
