macrobiotics

1. NOUN

/ ˌmækroʊbaɪˈɑːtɪks / (AmE), / ˌmakrəʊbaɪˈɒtɪks / (BrE)
a theory or method of promoting health and longevity, chiefly through a diet emphasizing whole grains, beans, and fresh natural foods
diet-focused health theory

Examples

She follows the principles of macrobiotics to improve her overall well-being.
Interest in macrobiotics has grown among those seeking natural approaches to health.
The restaurant offers a menu based on macrobiotics and organic ingredients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nutrition - the process by which living things take in and use food

Etymology

From Greek makros (long) + bios (life); coined in the 20th century.
