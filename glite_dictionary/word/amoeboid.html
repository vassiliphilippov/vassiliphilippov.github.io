amoeboid

1. ADJ

/ əˈmiːbɔɪd / (AmE), / əˈmiːbɔɪd / (BrE)
Resembling an amoeba, especially in having an irregular or constantly changing shape.
like an amoeba; irregular in shape

Examples

Under the microscope, the white blood cell exhibited an amoeboid movement.
The colony grew in an amoeboid mass, expanding and changing shape constantly.
Certain protozoa display an amoeboid form as they traverse their environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amoeba - a single-celled organism with an irregular shape

Etymology

From amoeba + -oid (Greek 'eidos', form or likeness).
