cursed

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɜːrst / (AmE), / ˈkɜːsɪd / (BrE)
Deserving or under a curse; sometimes used for emphasis to express annoyance or strong feeling.
deserving a curse; used for emphasis

Examples

The villagers believe the house is cursed and refuse to go near it.
He felt cursed with bad luck his entire life.
Not a cursed penny was left when they finished shopping.

Synset Examples

villagers shun the area believing it to be cursed
cursed with four daughters
not a cursed drop
his cursed stupidity
I'll be cursed if I can see your reasoning

Related Terms

  • Antonym: blessed - consecrated or favored; antonym of cursed

Etymology

From Middle English 'cursed', past participle of 'curse'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɜrst / (AmE), / ˈkɜːst / (BrE)
Condemned to suffer eternal punishment in Hell; damned.
condemned to eternal punishment

Examples

The legend said that those who betrayed the king would be forever cursed.
He feared that his sinful life had left him cursed and beyond redemption.
The ghost was said to be the soul of a cursed man wandering the earth.

Synset Examples

poor damned souls

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damned - condemned to eternal punishment

Etymology

Middle English, past participle of 'curse'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01454004-s
Synset POS: s
