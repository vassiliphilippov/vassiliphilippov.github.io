amadavat

1. NOUN

/ ˌæməˈdɑːvət / (AmE), / ˌæməˈdɑːvət / (BrE)
a small red Asian weaverbird, often kept as a cage bird
small red Asian cage bird

Examples

The brightly plumaged amadavat makes a popular pet among bird enthusiasts.
He observed a flock of amadavats feeding on the grass seeds near the village.
The bird dealer displayed several amadavat pairs in ornate cages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avaadavat - an alternative name for the amadavat bird
  • Synonym: red munia - another common name for the amadavat

Etymology

From Portuguese 'amadavade', from the city of Ahmedabad in western India, where the birds were exported from.
