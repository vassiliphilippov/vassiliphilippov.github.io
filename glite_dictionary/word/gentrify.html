to gentrify

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒɛn.trəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn.trɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to renovate or improve (an area, especially a neighborhood), so that it meets middle-class standards, often causing displacement of lower-income residents
renovate area to suit middle-class tastes

Examples

Several art galleries opened as developers began to gentrify the aging neighborhood.
Plans to gentrify the historic district have sparked protests from longtime residents.
The old industrial area was gentrified into trendy lofts and coffee shops.

Synset Examples

gentrify a row of old houses
gentrify the old center of town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regenerate - to bring new life or vigor to an area, especially urban
  • Antonym: to dilapidate - to bring a building or area to a state of decay

Etymology

From 'gentry' (people of high social status) + '-fy' (to make or become).
