multiplier

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌltɪˌplaɪər / (AmE), / ˈmʌltɪˌplaɪə / (BrE)
a number by which another number (the multiplicand) is multiplied
number by which another is multiplied

Examples

The multiplier in the equation 4 × 5 = 20 is 4.
In this math problem, the student accidentally switched the multiplier and the multiplicand.
When calculating the total cost, you must use the correct multiplier for the number of units.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: multiplicand - the number that is to be multiplied by another number (the multiplier)

Etymology

From Latin 'multiplicare' (to multiply) via Middle English, influenced by 'multiply'.
