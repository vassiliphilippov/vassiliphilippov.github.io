barrenness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbærənnəs / (AmE), / ˈbærənnɪs / (BrE)
the state of being unable to have children, especially referring to a woman
inability to have children

Examples

She struggled with feelings of sorrow stemming from her barrenness.
In some cultures, barrenness was unfairly considered a personal failing.
Medical advances now offer hope to many who face barrenness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infertility - the inability to conceive children or young

Etymology

From barren + -ness, ultimately from Old French *barain* (sterile, unproductive).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbær.ə.nəs / (AmE), / ˈbær.ən.nəs / (BrE)
The state of being unproductive or incapable of producing anything of value.
state of being unproductive

Examples

The barrenness of the desert made it almost impossible for any crops to grow.
She struggled with the emotional impact of barrenness after years of trying to conceive.
The novel highlights the barrenness of ambition without purpose or compassion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fertility - the quality of being able to produce abundantly
  • Synonym: sterility - the condition of being unable to produce offspring or vegetation

Etymology

From the adjective 'barren' + the suffix '-ness'. The root 'barren' comes from Old French 'baraigne'.
