Symphytum officinale

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪmˈfaɪtəm əˌfɪsɪˈnɑːli / (AmE), / ˌsɪmfɪtəm ˌɒfɪˈsiːnəleɪ / (BrE)
A perennial European herb with small white, pink, or purple flowers, commonly known as comfrey, often found as a weed in North America.
European herb (comfrey) with small flowers

Examples

Symphytum officinale is often grown in herbal gardens for its medicinal properties.
The rapid spread of Symphytum officinale has caused it to be regarded as a weed in some parts of North America.
Researchers are studying the potential uses of Symphytum officinale root extract in natural medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comfrey - common name for Symphytum officinale

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Symphytum', species epithet 'officinale' (used in medicine).
