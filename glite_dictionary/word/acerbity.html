acerbity

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɝːbɪti / (AmE), / əˈsɜːbɪti / (BrE)
harshness or bitterness of manner, tone, or temper
harshness or bitterness of manner

Examples

Her acerbity during the meeting discouraged others from sharing their opinions.
The critic was known for the acerbity of his reviews, rarely sparing anyone's feelings.
He spoke with such acerbity that his words seemed to sting his listeners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: severity - the quality of being severe, strict, or harsh
  • Antonym: gentleness - the quality of being gentle or mild

Etymology

From Latin 'acerbitas', from 'acerbus' (harsh, bitter).

2. NOUN

/ əˈsɝːbɪti / (AmE), / əˈsɜːbɪti / (BrE)
The quality of having a sharp, sour, or bitter taste.
sharp, sour taste

Examples

The acerbity of the lemon juice made my lips pucker.
Some green apples have a distinct acerbity that many people enjoy.
The chef balanced the acerbity of the sauce with a touch of honey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acidity - the quality of being acid or sour
  • Antonym: sweetness - the quality of having a sweet taste

Etymology

From Latin acerbitas, from acerbus (sour, bitter).

3. NOUN

/ əˈsɝːbəti / (AmE), / əˈsɜːbɪti / (BrE)
Sharpness or bitterness, especially in tone, manner, or speech.
sharpness or bitterness (in tone, etc.)

Examples

Her acerbity in meetings often discouraged open discussion among her colleagues.
He couldn't help noticing the acerbity in her reply to the criticism.
The reviewer's acerbity made the author reconsider his approach to writing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acrimony - bitterness or ill feeling, especially in speech or behavior
  • Antonym: sweetness - the quality of being pleasant or gentle, especially in manner

Etymology

From Latin 'acerbitas', from 'acerbus' meaning 'bitter, harsh'.
