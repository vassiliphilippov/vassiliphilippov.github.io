Cortland

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrtlənd / (AmE), / ˈkɔːtlənd / (BrE)
a large variety of apple with a red skin, known for its sweet flavor and crisp texture
a type of red-skinned apple

Examples

The pie was made with fresh Cortland apples from the local orchard.
I prefer to eat a Cortland because of its crisp texture and tartness.
They sell several varieties of apples, including Cortland and McIntosh.

Etymology

Named after Cortland County, New York, where it was first developed.
