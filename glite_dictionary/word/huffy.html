huffy

1. ADJ

/ ˈhʌfi / (AmE), / ˈhʌfi / (BrE)
Easily offended or annoyed; quick to become upset or take offense.
easily offended or annoyed

Examples

She became huffy when they questioned her decision.
There's no need to get huffy over a simple mistake.
Whenever he loses a game, he gets quite huffy and refuses to talk.

Synset Examples

stayed huffy a good while
she gets mad when you wake her up so early
mad at his friend
sore over a remark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritable - easily annoyed or angered
  • Antonym: calm - not easily upset or angered

Etymology

Formed from 'huff' (a fit of anger) plus the adjective suffix '-y'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈhʌfi / (AmE), / ˈhʌfi / (BrE)
Easily offended or annoyed; quick to take offense.
quick to take offense

Examples

She grew huffy when no one praised her idea at the meeting.
Don't get huffy—I was only making a suggestion, not criticizing you.
He walked away in a huffy manner after the argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: touchy - easily upset or offended
  • Synonym: irritable - easily annoyed or angered
  • Antonym: good-natured - friendly and not easily angered

Etymology

From English 'huff' (to take offense, become angry), with suffix -y.
