Thomas Becket

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɑməs ˈbɛkɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɒməs ˈbɛkɪt / (BrE)
(c. 1118–1170) The Archbishop of Canterbury from 1162 until his murder in 1170, known for his conflict with King Henry II over the rights and privileges of the Church.
Archbishop of Canterbury, murdered in 1170

Examples

Thomas Becket was canonized as a saint just three years after his death.
The conflict between King Henry II and Thomas Becket changed English history.
Visitors to Canterbury Cathedral often learn about the martyrdom of Thomas Becket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saint Thomas à Becket - Alternative name for the same historical figure
