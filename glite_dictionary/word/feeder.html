feeder

1. NOUN

/ ˈfidər / (AmE), / ˈfiːdə / (BrE)
an animal that eats or obtains its nourishment from a particular food source or type of material
animal that feeds on a specific food source

Examples

Many birds are insect feeders and help control pest populations.
The bear is an omnivorous feeder, consuming both plants and animals.
As a specialist mud feeder, this fish extracts nutrients from riverbed sediment.

Synset Examples

a bark feeder
a mud feeder

2. NOUN

/ ˈfidər / (AmE), / ˈfiːdə / (BrE)
a machine or device that automatically supplies a supply of material, such as food, fuel, or raw ingredients.
automatic supply machine or device

Examples

The farmer installed a new feeder to ensure the cattle were always supplied with fresh hay.
This automatic feeder can be programmed to provide meals to your pet throughout the day.
As soon as the hopper was empty, the feeder stopped the assembly line until it was refilled.

Synset Examples

the feeder discharged feed into a trough for the livestock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dispenser - a machine or container that releases a substance in controlled amounts

Etymology

From Old English *fēder* (one who feeds), from *fēdan* (to feed).

5. NOUN

/ ˈfidər / (AmE), / ˈfiːdə / (BrE)
a person or animal that eats food, especially as part of a classification by eating habits
person or animal that eats

Examples

Some birds are selective feeders, only eating particular types of seeds.
The veterinarian studied the dietary habits of different feeders in the ecosystem.
Among the fish, certain species are surface feeders while others feed at the bottom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eater - a person or animal that eats

Etymology

6. NOUN

/ ˈfidər / (AmE), / ˈfiːdə / (BrE)
a container or device placed outdoors to provide food for wild birds or other animals
outdoor device for feeding birds

Examples

She set up a new feeder in the garden to attract finches and sparrows.
Squirrels kept stealing seeds from the feeder outside our window.
Every winter we refill the feeder so the local birds have enough to eat.

Related Terms
