clothed

1. ADJ

/ ˈkloʊðd / (AmE), / ˈkləʊðd / (BrE)
wearing clothes or having clothes on; sometimes used in combination to describe the state or style of being dressed
wearing clothes; dressed

Examples

She was fully clothed when the unexpected guests arrived.
The children were warmly clothed for the winter weather.
He appeared calm and clothed in a simple robe.

Synset Examples

clothed and in his right mind
proud of her well-clothed family
nurses clad in white
white-clad nurses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clad - dressed or covered in, often used in combination
  • Antonym: naked - not wearing any clothes; bare
  • Synonym: dressed - wearing clothes, especially in a particular way

Etymology

Past participle of 'clothe', from Old English 'clāðian' (to clothe).

2. ADJ

/ kloʊðd / (AmE), / kləʊðd / (BrE)
covered with clothes, a wrap, or as if with a layer or garment.
covered with clothes or something like a covering

Examples

The valley was clothed in a thick layer of morning mist.
She admired the mountains, clothed in winter snow.
The trees were clothed with new green leaves in spring.

Synset Examples

leaf-clothed trees
fog-cloaked meadows
a beam draped with cobwebs
cloud-wrapped peaks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: covered - having something over or on all or part of a surface
  • Antonym: bare - uncovered or lacking a covering
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01699958-s
Synset POS: s
