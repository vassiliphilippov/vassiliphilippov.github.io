orchil

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːr.tʃɪl / (AmE), / ˈɔː.tʃɪl / (BrE)
a purple dye obtained from certain lichens, especially used in the past for textiles
purple dye from lichens

Examples

In medieval Europe, fabrics were often dyed using orchil extracted from lichens.
The distinctive color of the tapestry was achieved with orchil.
She studied the process of making natural orchil dyes in her chemistry class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archil - another name for orchil, a dye from lichens

Etymology

From Old French orcheil, related to Italian orcello, both referring to lichens used for dye.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔrtʃɪl / (AmE), / ˈɔːtʃɪl / (BrE)
Any of various lichens, especially of the genus Roccella, that yield the purple-red dye archil.
lichen that yields the dye archil

Examples

Certain species of lichen are valued for producing orchil, a natural purple dye.
The extraction of orchil was once an important industry in Mediterranean regions.
Textiles colored with orchil have a distinctive violet hue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archil - a purple-red dye obtained from certain lichens

Etymology

From Old French 'orceille', from Late Latin 'orseīlla', from 'orcinus' (pertaining to orca, a kind of lichen).
