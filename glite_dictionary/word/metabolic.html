metabolic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɛtəˈbɑlɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈbɒlɪk / (BrE)
(Biology) Undergoing or relating to the process of metamorphosis.
undergoing metamorphosis (biology)

Examples

Certain insects are metabolic during particular life stages, undergoing dramatic changes in form.
The metabolic state of a caterpillar ends when it becomes a butterfly.
Some amphibians exhibit a metabolic period as they transition from larvae to adults.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metamorphic - undergoing change of form or structure, especially in biology

Etymology

From Greek metabolē (change), via metabolism.

2. ADJ

/ ˌmɛtəˈbɑlɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈbɒlɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or resulting from the chemical processes that occur within a living organism in order to maintain life.
relating to the body's chemical processes
medical

Examples

The doctor monitored her metabolic rate during the study.
Regular exercise can help improve metabolic health.
Certain conditions, like thyroid disorders, can affect your metabolic processes.

Synset Examples

metabolic rate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metabolism - the chemical processes in living organisms required to maintain life

Etymology

From Greek 'metabolē' meaning change, transformation, via French 'métabolique'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02986641-a
Synset POS: a
