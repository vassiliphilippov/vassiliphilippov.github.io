louche

1. ADJ

/ luʃ / (AmE), / luːʃ / (BrE)
disreputable or sordid in an appealing or intriguing way; of dubious moral character but with a certain allure
disreputable in an attractive way

Examples

Her friends warned her against visiting the louche part of the city after dark.
The movie portrayed the louche glamour of 1920s nightclubs.
He was drawn to the louche charm of the old theater district.

Synset Examples

a louche nightclub
the louche world of the theater

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disreputable - not considered to be respectable in character or appearance
  • Antonym: respectable - regarded to be proper or decent in character or appearance

Etymology

From French louche (squinting, shady, suspicious), from Latin lusca (one-eyed).
