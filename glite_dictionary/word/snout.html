snout

1. NOUN

/ snaʊt / (AmE), / snaʊt / (BrE)
a beaklike projection at the front part of the head in some insects, especially weevils
beaklike projection on some insects' heads

Examples

The weevil's distinctive snout allows it to bore into seeds and fruits.
Some insects use their snout to feed by piercing plant tissues.
The scientist examined the shape of the beetle's snout under the microscope.

Etymology

From Old English 'snūt' (nose, snout), related to German 'Schnauze'.

2. NOUN

/ snaʊt / (AmE), / snaʊt / (BrE)
(informal) the human nose, especially when large or prominent.
informal word for the nose
informal

Examples

He broke his snout in a football game last year.
She wrinkled her snout in disgust at the smell.
He stuck his snout into everyone’s business, always prying.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nose - the part of the face that contains the nostrils and covers the nasal cavity

3. NOUN

/ snaʊt / (AmE), / snaʊt / (BrE)
the long, projecting part of an animal's head, especially the nose and jaws
projecting nose of an animal

Examples

The pig rooted through the mud with its snout, searching for food.
A crocodile's long snout helps it catch prey at the water's surface.
He watched as the fox poked its snout out of the burrow, sniffing the air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muzzle - the projecting part of the face, including the nose and mouth, of an animal

Etymology

From Middle English 'snoute', from Old English 'snūt', of Germanic origin.
