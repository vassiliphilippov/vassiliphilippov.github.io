believability

1. NOUN

/ bəˌliːvəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / bɪˌliːvəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being believable or credible; trustworthiness or plausibility
the quality of being believable

Examples

The believability of the story was questioned by the audience.
Good actors bring a sense of believability to their roles on stage.
The documentary gained praise for its accuracy and believability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: credibility - the quality of being trusted and believed in
  • Antonym: implausibility - the quality of being unlikely or not believable

Etymology

From 'believable' + suffix '-ity', indicating a state or quality.
