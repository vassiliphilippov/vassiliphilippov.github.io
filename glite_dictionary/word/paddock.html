paddock

1. NOUN

/ ˈpædək / (AmE), / ˈpædək / (BrE)
an enclosed area at a racetrack where racehorses are saddled and paraded before a race
enclosure for racehorses at racetracks

Examples

The trainer led his horse into the paddock before the big race.
Spectators gathered around the paddock to get a closer look at the thoroughbreds.
Jockeys consulted with their teams in the paddock before mounting up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pen - an enclosure for animals

Etymology

Probably from Medieval Latin *parlicium* (enclosure), altered by folk etymology.
