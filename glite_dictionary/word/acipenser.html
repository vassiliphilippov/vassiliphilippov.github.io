Acipenser

1. NOUN

/ ˌæsəˈpɛnsər / (AmE), / ˌæsɪˈpɛnsə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Acipenseridae, comprising sturgeons, large fish known for their roe (caviar).
genus of sturgeons (fish)

Examples

The Acipenser genus includes several species of sturgeon found in both Europe and North America.
Scientists have studied the spawning behavior of Acipenser in controlled environments.
Conservation efforts focus on protecting Acipenser species from overfishing and habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sturgeon - a large primitive fish whose roe is used as caviar

Etymology

From Latin acīpenser (sturgeon), of uncertain origin.
