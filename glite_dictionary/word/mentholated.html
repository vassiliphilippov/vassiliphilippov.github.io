mentholated

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɛnˌθoʊˌleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈmɛnθəleɪtɪd / (BrE)
Containing or treated with menthol, especially to provide a cooling or soothing effect.
containing or treated with menthol

Examples

Many cold remedies are mentholated to help soothe a sore throat.
He prefers mentholated lip balm to relieve chapped lips in winter.
The mentholated ointment provided a cooling sensation when applied to his chest.

Synset Examples

mentholated cough syrup

Related Terms

  • Antonym: non-mentholated - not containing menthol

Etymology

Formed from 'menthol' (from Latin 'mentha', mint) with the suffix '-ated' indicating something treated with or containing.
