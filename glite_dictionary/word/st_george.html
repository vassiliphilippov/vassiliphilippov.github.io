St. George

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈdʒɔrdʒ / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈdʒɔːdʒ / (BrE)
A Christian martyr and the patron saint of England, famous for the legend in which he slays a dragon and rescues a princess.
patron saint of England; legendary dragon-slayer

Examples

The story of St. George and the dragon is celebrated throughout England every April 23rd.
According to legend, St. George saved a princess from a fearsome dragon.
Many English towns host parades in honor of St. George on his feast day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saint - a person acknowledged as holy or virtuous and typically regarded as being in heaven

Etymology

From Latin 'Sanctus Georgius', from Greek 'Geōrgios', meaning 'farmer' or 'earth-worker'.
