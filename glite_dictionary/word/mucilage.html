mucilage

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjuːsɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmjuːsɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
a thick, sticky substance used as an adhesive, often made from plant extracts
sticky adhesive substance

Examples

He sealed the envelope with mucilage to ensure it stayed closed.
The bookbinder used mucilage to repair the torn pages.
A thin coating of mucilage was applied to the stamps for adhesion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glue - a general adhesive substance
  • Synonym: paste - a soft, moist adhesive mixture
  • Synonym: adhesive - a substance used to stick objects together

Etymology

From Latin mucilago (musty juice), from mucus (slime).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmjuːsɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmjuːsɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
A thick, gluey substance produced by plants, often found in seeds and used for its adhesive or soothing properties.
gelatinous plant substance

Examples

Some plant seeds are coated with a layer of mucilage that swells in water.
The mucilage extracted from aloe vera leaves is often used in cosmetics.
Scientists studied the composition of mucilage to understand its role in plant hydration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gum - a sticky substance obtained from plants
  • Synonym: resin - a sticky organic substance exuded by some trees

Etymology

From Latin 'mucilago' (musty juice), from 'mucus' (slime).
