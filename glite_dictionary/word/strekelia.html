Strekelia

1. NOUN

/ strɛˈkiːliə / (AmE), / strɛˈkiːliə / (BrE)
A small genus of flowering plants in the amaryllis family, native to Mexico and Central America, known for their showy red or orange flowers.
genus of amaryllis family plants

Examples

The vibrant flowers of Strekelia add a splash of color to spring gardens.
Botanists often compare Strekelia with other members of the amaryllis family for classification purposes.
Many collectors prize Strekelia for its unique flower shape and brilliant hues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hippeastrum - a related genus in the amaryllis family, also known for large showy flowers

Etymology

Named after Johann Baptist Strecker, German plant collector.
