balm

1. NOUN

/ bɑm / (AmE), / bɑːm / (BrE)
A fragrant, resinous substance obtained from certain plants, used for healing, soothing, or as a perfume.
aromatic healing resin

Examples

Ancient healers prized balm for its soothing effects on wounds and burns.
The forest air was filled with the sweet scent of pine balm.
She applied a homemade balm to her dry, cracked hands every evening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salve - an ointment used to promote healing of the skin
  • Synonym: balm of Gilead - an aromatic resin used for healing; biblical reference

Etymology

From Latin 'balsamum', from Greek 'balsamon', meaning aromatic resin.

2. NOUN

/ bɑm / (AmE), / bɑːm / (BrE)
a semisolid preparation, often containing medicine, applied to the skin to heal or soothe irritation
medicated ointment for soothing skin

Examples

The doctor recommended a herbal balm to help relieve my sunburn.
She gently applied the soothing balm to her inflamed skin.
This lip balm keeps my lips from getting chapped in winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ointment - smooth substance applied to skin for healing
  • Synonym: salve - healing ointment applied to skin

Etymology

From Old French *basme*, from Latin *balsamum*, from Greek *balsamon*.
