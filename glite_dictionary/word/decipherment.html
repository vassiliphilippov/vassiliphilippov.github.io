decipherment

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsaɪfərmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈsaɪfəmənt / (BrE)
the act or process of converting something written in code, cipher, or obscure language into plain, understandable text
conversion from code or obscure form into plain text

Examples

The decipherment of ancient Egyptian hieroglyphs opened a window into their civilization.
Successful decipherment of the intercepted messages changed the course of the war.
Researchers celebrated the decipherment of the coded manuscript after many years of effort.

Synset Examples

a secret key or password is required for decryption

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decoding - the process of converting coded information into a readable form
  • Antonym: encryption - the process of converting information into code to prevent unauthorized access

Etymology

Formed from 'decipher' + '-ment'; 'decipher' from Old French déchiffrer, based on 'cipher' (code).
