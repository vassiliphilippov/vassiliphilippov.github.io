to immobilize

1. VERB

/ ɪˈmoʊbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ɪˈməʊbɪlaɪz / (BrE)
to prevent someone or something from moving or operating; to make immobile
prevent from moving

Examples

The doctor used a splint to immobilize the patient's injured leg.
A strong adhesive was applied to immobilize the parts during assembly.
Police had to immobilize the suspect to prevent escape.

Synset Examples

The child was pinned under the fallen tree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to paralyze - to make unable to move or act
  • Antonym: to mobilize - to make able to move or act

Etymology

From French 'immobiliser', from Latin 'immobilis' (motionless, fixed).

3. VERB

/ ɪˈmoʊbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ɪˈməʊbəlaɪz / (BrE)
(Finance) To convert assets into fixed capital, making them less readily available for use or exchange.
(finance) convert assets to fixed capital

Examples

The company decided to immobilize its cash reserves by investing in new manufacturing plants.
Banks often immobilize certain assets to support long-term projects.
Financial regulations can influence how and when firms choose to immobilize their resources.

Etymology

From French 'immobiliser' (to immobilize), from Latin 'immobilis' (not movable).
