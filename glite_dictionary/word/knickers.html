knickers

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪkɚz / (AmE), / ˈnɪkəz / (BrE)
(plural) Women's underpants, especially as worn in British English.
women's underpants (British)
british

Examples

She realized too late that her knickers were visible beneath her skirt.
After the gym class, Sophie changed out of her sports knickers.
Those flowery knickers are my favorite pair.

Synset Examples

she was afraid that her bloomers might have been showing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panties - women's underpants (chiefly American English)

Etymology

From the diminutive of the surname Knickerbocker, used in the 19th century for knee-length pants, later applied in British English to women's underwear.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɪkərz / (AmE), / ˈnɪkəz / (BrE)
Trousers that end above the knee, typically worn as part of sports or children's clothing, especially in the past.
trousers ending above the knee

Examples

He wore a pair of knickers and long socks for the hike through the woods.
Old photographs show boys in knickers playing on the village green.
Golfers in traditional knickers and caps gathered for the vintage tournament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breeches - trousers fastened just below the knee

Etymology

From the nickname for short, loose-fitting trousers in 19th-century England, influenced by the comic character 'Knickerbocker'.
