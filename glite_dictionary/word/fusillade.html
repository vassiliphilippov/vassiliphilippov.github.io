fusillade

1. NOUN

/ ˌfjuːsəˈleɪd / (AmE), / ˌfjuːsɪˈlɑːd / (BrE)
a rapid and simultaneous discharge of many firearms or shots
simultaneous burst of gunfire

Examples

The soldiers responded with a fusillade that forced the enemy to retreat.
A sudden fusillade shattered the silence, scattering everyone in panic.
During the parade, a fusillade of blanks was fired into the air as a salute.

Synset Examples

our fusillade from the left flank caught them by surprise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrage - a concentrated artillery bombardment over a wide area
  • Synonym: volley - a number of bullets, rocks, or other objects thrown or fired at once

Etymology

From French 'fusillade' (volley of shots), from 'fusil' (firearm).

2. VERB

/ ˌfjuːsəˈleɪd / (AmE), / ˌfjuːsɪˈlɑːd / (BrE)
to attack or bombard with a rapid series of shots or missiles
attack with a rapid series of shots

Examples

The enemy position was fusilladed at dawn, forcing the soldiers to take cover.
Protesters were fusilladed with rubber bullets as they tried to cross the barricade.
As the troops advanced, they fusilladed the building with automatic gunfire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bombard - to attack continuously with bombs, shells, or other missiles

Etymology

From French 'fusillade', from 'fusil' (gun, rifle).

Synset POS: v
