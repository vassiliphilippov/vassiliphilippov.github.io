quartz

1. NOUN

/ kwɔrts / (AmE), / kwɔːts / (BrE)
A colorless glass made of almost pure silica, valued for its clarity and resistance to heat and chemicals.
colorless glass of pure silica

Examples

Laboratory equipment often uses quartz because of its high resistance to heat.
The optical properties of quartz make it ideal for precise scientific instruments.
Some watches have faces protected by quartz to prevent scratches and damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silica glass - glass made almost entirely of silicon dioxide (silica)

Etymology

From German 'Quarz', probably of Slavic origin.

2. NOUN

/ kwɔrts / (AmE), / kwɔːts / (BrE)
a hard, typically colorless or white mineral consisting of silicon dioxide, found widely in many rocks and used as a gemstone and in industry
a hard mineral (silicon dioxide) found in rocks

Examples

The countertop was made entirely of quartz, giving the kitchen a modern look.
Geologists discovered a large vein of quartz inside the mountain.
Fine yellow sand is usually composed mostly of quartz grains.

Etymology

From German Quarz, of uncertain origin, possibly through Slavic languages.
