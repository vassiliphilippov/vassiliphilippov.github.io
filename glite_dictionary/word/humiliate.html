to humiliate

1. VERB

/ hjuˈmɪliˌeɪt / (AmE), / hjʊˈmɪlieɪt / (BrE)
to make someone feel ashamed or foolish by injuring their dignity and self-respect, especially publicly
make someone feel ashamed or foolish

Examples

He felt utterly crushed after his boss humiliated him in front of his coworkers.
She would never humiliate a friend just to get a laugh from others.
They publicly humiliated the athlete for making a simple mistake.

Synset Examples

He humiliated his colleague by criticising him in front of the boss

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embarrass - to cause someone to feel awkward or self-conscious
  • Antonym: to dignify - to make someone feel worthy or honoured

Etymology

From Latin humiliāre, from humilis (low, humble).
