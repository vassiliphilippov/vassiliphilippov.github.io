premonition

1. NOUN

/ ˌpriːməˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːməˈnɪʃən / (BrE)
a strong feeling or sense that something, especially something bad, is about to happen
sense that something bad will happen

Examples

She had a sudden premonition of danger, which made her pause at the door.
Moments before the accident, he felt a strange premonition that something was wrong.
Despite her cheerful expression, a premonition of trouble lingered in her mind.

Synset Examples

a steadily escalating sense of foreboding
the lawyer had a presentiment that the judge would dismiss the case

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foreboding - a strong feeling that something bad will happen
  • Synonym: presentiment - an intuitive feeling about the future, especially a foreboding one

Etymology

From late Latin 'praemonitio(n-)' (warning beforehand), from Latin 'praemonere' (forewarn).

2. NOUN

/ ˌprɛməˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːməˈnɪʃən / (BrE)
a strong feeling or sense that something, especially something unpleasant, is going to happen; an early warning or foreboding.
a feeling that something (bad) will happen

Examples

She had a premonition that the trip would not go well.
Just before entering the building, I felt a strange premonition of danger.
He tried to ignore the premonition that something was about to go wrong.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foreboding - a feeling that something bad will happen
  • Synonym: omen - an event regarded as a sign of future good or evil
  • Synonym: presentiment - an intuitive feeling about the future

Etymology

From Latin praemonitio (a forewarning), from prae- (before) + monere (to warn).
