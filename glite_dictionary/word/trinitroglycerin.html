trinitroglycerin

1. NOUN

/ ˌtraɪˌnaɪtroʊˈɡlɪsərɪn / (AmE), / ˌtraɪˌnʌɪtrəʊˈɡlɪsərɪn / (BrE)
A heavy yellow, poisonous, oily, and explosive liquid obtained by nitrating glycerol, used in explosives and as a vasodilator in medicine.
explosive and medicinal chemical compound

Examples

The chemical plant has strict safety protocols for handling trinitroglycerin.
Doctors sometimes prescribe trinitroglycerin in small doses to treat chest pain.
The explosive power of trinitroglycerin revolutionized the mining industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nitroglycerin - explosive and medicinal compound, alternate name for trinitroglycerin

Etymology

From tri- (three) + nitro- (relating to nitro groups) + glycerin, referring to its chemical structure.
