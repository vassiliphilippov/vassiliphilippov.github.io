endocranium

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.doʊˈkreɪni.əm / (AmE), / ˌɛn.dəˈkreɪnɪ.əm / (BrE)
the membrane lining the inside of the skull
membrane lining skull interior
medical

Examples

The endocranium provides an important protective layer between the brain and the bones of the skull.
During the surgery, the doctor carefully examined the endocranium for any signs of infection.
Differences in the structure of the endocranium can be important in studying the evolution of vertebrates.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek endon (within) + cranium (skull).
