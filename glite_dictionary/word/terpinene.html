terpinene

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrpɪˌniːn / (AmE), / ˈtɜːpɪniːn / (BrE)
any of a group of isomeric hydrocarbons classified as terpenes, found in essential oils and used in organic chemistry.
a group of terpene hydrocarbons
medical

Examples

α-Terpinene is the most common type of terpinene found in tea tree oil.
Laboratory analysis revealed the presence of several terpinene isomers in the extract.
Some essential oils owe their distinctive scent to terpinene compounds.

Synset Examples

α-Terpinene is an example of a terpinene.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: terpene - a large class of organic compounds produced by plants, especially conifers

Etymology

From terpene + -ene, denoting an unsaturated hydrocarbon.
