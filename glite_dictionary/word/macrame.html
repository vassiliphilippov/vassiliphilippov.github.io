macrame

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæk.rəˈmeɪ / (AmE), / ˌmæk.rəˈmeɪ / (BrE)
A form of textile produced by knotting cords or strings in patterns to make decorative articles such as lace, wall hangings, or jewelry.
textile craft using knotted cords

Examples

She decorated her living room with a handmade macrame wall hanging.
Learning macrame can be a relaxing hobby for people who enjoy working with their hands.
The market stall offered beautiful macrame plant holders in a range of colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lace - decorative openwork fabric made by looping, twisting, or knitting threads

Etymology

From French 'macramé', from Italian 'macramè', from Arabic 'miqramah' (embroidered veil).

WordNet ID: oewn-03709269-n

2. VERB

/ ˌmæk.rəˈmeɪ / (AmE), / ˌmæk.rəˈmeɪ / (BrE)
to make decorative patterns or objects by knotting threads, cords, or strings together
to make items by knotting threads or cords

Examples

She likes to macrame colorful wall hangings in her spare time.
They decided to macrame plant holders for all the windows in the house.
He macramed a bracelet as a birthday gift for his friend.

Synset Examples

macrame a plant holder

Etymology

From French 'macramé', from Turkish 'makrama' (napkin, towel), from Arabic 'miqrama' (embroidered veil or cloth).

WordNet ID: oewn-01674796-v
Synset POS: v
