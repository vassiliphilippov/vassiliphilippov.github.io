Caudata

1. NOUN

/ kɔːˈdeɪtə / (AmE), / kɔːˈdeɪtə / (BrE)
An order of amphibians that includes salamanders, newts, and related species, characterized by elongated bodies and tails.
an order of amphibians (salamanders, newts)
medical

Examples

The order Caudata encompasses a wide variety of salamanders found around the world.
Most members of Caudata have the ability to regenerate lost limbs.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Caudata during their expedition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salamander - a tailed amphibian of the order Caudata
  • Synonym: newt - a small, slender salamander

Etymology

From Latin 'caudatus' meaning 'having a tail'.
