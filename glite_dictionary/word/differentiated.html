differentiated

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃieɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃieɪtɪd / (BrE)
made or become different or distinct from others, especially as a result of development or specialisation
made different or distinct

Examples

The cells in the sample were highly differentiated under the microscope.
Their company offers differentiated products that stand out in the market.
The curriculum was differentiated to meet the needs of all students.

Synset Examples

the differentiated markings of butterflies
the regionally differentiated results

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distinct - clearly different or separate
  • Antonym: uniform - not changing or varying; having the same form

Etymology

From the past participle of 'differentiate', which derives from Latin 'differentia' (difference).

2. ADJ

/ ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃieɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃieɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a cell or tissue) having become specialized for a particular function during development.
biologically specialized; adapted for a function
medical

Examples

The tissue sample contained both differentiated nerve cells and undifferentiated stem cells.
Highly differentiated cells perform specific roles within an organ system.
Cancer can develop when formerly differentiated cells lose their specialized functions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undifferentiated - (of a cell or tissue) not specialized; retaining the ability to develop into various cell types

Etymology

From Latin differentiare (to make different) plus the suffix -ed.
