expensiveness

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspɛnsɪvnəs / (AmE), / ɪkˈspensɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being expensive; high cost
the quality of being expensive

Examples

The expensiveness of the hotel rooms discouraged many potential guests.
She complained about the expensiveness of organic produce at local markets.
Rising tuition fees have increased the expensiveness of higher education in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cheapness - the quality of being inexpensive
  • Synonym: costliness - the quality of being costly

Etymology

From 'expensive' + '-ness', with 'expensive' from Latin 'expensivus' (costly).
