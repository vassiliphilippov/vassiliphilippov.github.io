durometer

1. NOUN

/ duˈrɑːmətər / (AmE), / djʊəˈrɒmɪtə / (BrE)
An instrument used for measuring the hardness of materials, especially metals and polymers.
instrument for measuring hardness

Examples

The technician used a durometer to test the hardness of the rubber sample.
Many engineers rely on a durometer when selecting materials for industrial applications.
The durometer reading indicated that the material was too soft for the intended use.

Synset Examples

The term durometer is often used to refer to the measurement as well as the instrument itself.

Etymology

From Latin 'durus' (hard) and Greek 'metron' (measure).
