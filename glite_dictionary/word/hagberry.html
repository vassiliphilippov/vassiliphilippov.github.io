hagberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæɡˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈhæɡbəri / (BrE)
a small wild cherry of northern and western Europe, especially the bird cherry (Prunus padus), whose fruit is popular with birds
a small wild cherry; bird cherry

Examples

The blackbirds flocked to the hagberry tree in search of ripe fruit.
She picked handfuls of hagberries for making a tart jam.
In spring, the hagberry blossoms filled the air with a sweet fragrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bird cherry - a species of cherry (Prunus padus) native to northern Europe and Asia

Etymology

Possibly from Old English *hægberie*, combining 'hæg' (enclosure, hedge) and 'berie' (berry).
