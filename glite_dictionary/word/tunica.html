tunica

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuː.nɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˈtjuː.nɪ.kə / (BrE)
an enveloping or covering membrane or layer of body tissue, especially as part of an organ
layer or membrane of body tissue
medical

Examples

The outer tunica of the blood vessel provides structural support and protection.
Damage to the inner tunica may impair normal organ function.
Each layer, or tunica, has a distinct cellular composition.

Etymology

From Latin tunica (a covering, tunic).
