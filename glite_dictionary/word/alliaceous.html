alliaceous

1. ADJ

/ ˌæliˈeɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˌælɪˈeɪʃəs / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of plants of the genus Allium, such as onions, garlic, and leeks, especially in reference to their scent or flavor
relating to onions or garlic (Allium genus)

Examples

The kitchen was filled with an alliaceous aroma after she chopped several garlic cloves.
Certain wines do not pair well with strongly alliaceous foods like onions and leeks.
Many gardeners enjoy cultivating alliaceous plants because they are easy to grow and deter pests.

Synset Examples

onions, leeks, garlic, chives are alliaceous plants

Etymology

From Latin 'allium' (garlic) + '-aceous' (of or pertaining to).

2. ADJ

/ ˌæliˈeɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˌælɪˈeɪʃəs / (BrE)
having a smell or taste similar to that of onions or garlic; relating to plants of the onion family.
smelling or tasting like onions or garlic

Examples

The alliaceous aroma of the stew made the kitchen smell appetizing.
Her garden was filled with alliaceous plants like onions and garlic.
He avoided alliaceous dishes to prevent lingering odors on his breath.

Etymology

From Latin 'allium' (garlic) + '-aceous' (of, relating to).
