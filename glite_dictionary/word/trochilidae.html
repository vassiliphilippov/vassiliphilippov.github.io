Trochilidae

1. NOUN

/ troʊˈkɪlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / trəʊˈkɪlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
the family of small, brightly colored, fast-flying birds known as hummingbirds, found in the Americas
the hummingbird family (birds)

Examples

Many species within the Trochilidae family exhibit iridescent plumage and remarkable hovering skills.
The Trochilidae are unique among birds for their ability to fly backward and hover in place.
Researchers study the migration patterns of Trochilidae to understand their adaptation to different climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hummingbird - a small, brightly colored bird with a fast-beating wing movement

Etymology

From New Latin Trochilidae, from Trochilus, the type genus, from Greek trokheilos (hummingbird).
