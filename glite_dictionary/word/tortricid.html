tortricid

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔr.trɪ.sɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɔː.trɪ.sɪd / (BrE)
Any of various small moths of the family Tortricidae, whose larvae commonly roll leaves or infest fruits and galls.
a small moth of the family Tortricidae

Examples

The apple orchard suffered heavy damage from a tortricid infestation this year.
A tortricid moth was discovered feeding on the leaves of the grapevine.
Scientists studied the life cycle of the tortricid to develop better pest control methods.

Etymology

From Modern Latin Tortricidae, the family name, derived from Latin torquere (to twist), alluding to the leaf-rolling habit of the larvae.
