bitmap

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪtˌmæp / (AmE), / ˈbɪt.mæp / (BrE)
A computer image composed of pixels arranged in a grid, with each pixel assigned a specific brightness or color value.
digital image made of pixels

Examples

The photo was saved as a bitmap to preserve the precise pixel details.
Enlarging a bitmap image can make it look blocky or pixelated.
Graphic designers often convert a bitmap into a vector image for scalability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raster image - an image made of a grid of pixels
  • Antonym: vector image - an image defined by mathematical shapes, not pixel grids

Etymology

From 'bit' (binary digit) + 'map' (diagram or representation); first used in computer graphics in the 1970s.
