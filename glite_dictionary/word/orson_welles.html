Orson Welles

1. PROPN

/ ˈɔːrsən ˈwɛlz / (AmE), / ˈɔːsən ˈwɛlz / (BrE)
(1915–1985) a celebrated American actor, director, writer, and producer known for films such as 'Citizen Kane' and his innovative contributions to theatre, radio, and cinema.
American actor, director, and filmmaker (1915–1985)

Examples

Orson Welles is best known for directing and starring in 'Citizen Kane,' which is often cited as one of the greatest films ever made.
The radio broadcast of 'The War of the Worlds' by Orson Welles caused widespread panic in 1938.
Many critics regard Orson Welles as a genius who revolutionized modern filmmaking.
