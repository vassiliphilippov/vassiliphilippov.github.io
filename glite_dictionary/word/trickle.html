trickle

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈtrɪkəl / (BrE)
a small, slow flow of liquid in drops, or a thin stream; the act or process of flowing or falling in drops
a slow, thin flow of drops

Examples

A trickle of water ran down the windowpane after the storm.
There was only a trickle coming from the old faucet.
The tap wouldn't shut off completely, leaving a constant trickle overnight.

Synset Examples

there's a drip through the roof

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drip - a small drop of liquid falling continuously
  • Degree Higher: stream - a steady flow of liquid or gas

Etymology

From Middle English trikelen, probably of imitative origin, related to trickle (v.).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07447159-n

2. VERB

/ ˈtrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈtrɪkl / (BrE)
(of a liquid or substance) to flow slowly and irregularly, often in drops or a thin stream.
flow slowly in drops or a thin stream

Examples

A small stream of water began to trickle down the windowpane.
Donations continued to trickle in after the charity event ended.
Tears trickled slowly down her cheeks as she listened to the story.

Synset Examples

water trickled onto the lawn from the broken hose
reports began to dribble in

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dribble - flow in drops or a thin, unsteady stream
  • Degree Higher: stream - flow continuously in a steady flow

Etymology

From Middle English trikelen, probably of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02074802-v
Synset POS: v
