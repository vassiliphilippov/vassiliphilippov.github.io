dragger

1. NOUN

/ ˈdræɡər / (AmE), / ˈdræɡə / (BrE)
a fishing boat that uses a trawl net or dragnet to catch fish
fishing boat using a trawl net

Examples

The old dragger returned to port with a full catch after three days at sea.
Many coastal communities rely on the dragger industry for employment.
Each dragger is equipped with large nets designed to sweep the ocean floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trawler - a fishing boat that uses a trawl net to catch fish

Etymology

From 'drag' + '-er', referring to a boat that drags a net.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdræɡər / (AmE), / ˈdræɡə / (BrE)
a person who pulls, tugs, or drags something, especially with effort
person who pulls or drags something

Examples

The dragger pulled the heavy boat ashore with great effort.
During the parade, each float required at least one dragger to keep it moving.
He became the unofficial dragger of suitcases for the entire family on their trips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puller - a person or thing that pulls something
  • Antonym: pusher - a person or thing that pushes something
