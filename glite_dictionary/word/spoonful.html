spoonful

1. NOUN

/ ˈspunˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈspuːnfʊl / (BrE)
An amount of something that fills a spoon.
amount that fills a spoon

Examples

She stirred a spoonful of honey into her tea every morning.
Add one spoonful of sugar to the batter and mix thoroughly.
He tasted the soup and decided it needed another spoonful of salt.

Synset Examples

he added two spoons of sugar

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: teaspoonful - amount that fills a teaspoon
  • Degree Higher: tablespoonful - amount that fills a tablespoon
