Prunus capuli

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs kəˈpuːli / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs kaˈpjuːli / (BrE)
A species of Mexican black cherry tree with edible fruit, native to Central and South America.
Mexican black cherry tree

Examples

The fruits of the Prunus capuli are enjoyed in traditional Latin American desserts.
Farmers in Mexico often cultivate Prunus capuli for its sweet, dark berries.
The wood from Prunus capuli trees is also valued for making furniture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Prunus serotina - Black cherry tree native to North America

Etymology

From Latin 'Prunus' (plum or cherry genus) and the Nahuatl word 'capuli' (cherry).
