patina

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtinə / (AmE), / ˈpætɪnə / (BrE)
a thin, usually greenish layer that forms on the surface of copper, bronze, and similar metals due to long-term oxidation; often valued for its aesthetic or historical significance
greenish layer on metal from oxidation

Examples

The ancient statue's patina revealed its long history.
Collectors highly prize bronze artifacts with a natural patina.
The copper roof developed a beautiful green patina over decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tarnish - dullness or discoloration of a metal surface caused by exposure to air

Etymology

From Italian 'patina' (shallow dish), from Latin 'patina' (pan, dish).

2. NOUN

/ pəˈtinə / (AmE), / ˈpætɪnə / (BrE)
a shiny or lustrous surface layer on wooden furniture, developed over time through age, polishing, or handling
sheen on wood from age or use

Examples

The antique table had developed a rich patina over decades of careful use.
Years of polishing had given the dresser a beautiful patina that collectors adore.
You should avoid sanding the wood so as not to remove its natural patina.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheen - a soft glow on a surface
  • Synonym: lustre - gentle sheen or soft glow, often on a polished surface

Etymology

From Italian 'patina' (shallow dish, film) and Latin 'patina' (shallow pan, dish).

3. NOUN

/ pəˈtinə / (AmE), / ˈpætɪnə / (BrE)
a surface or appearance that develops on something with age or experience, especially as a result of exposure or handling; often used figuratively to describe an acquired characteristic or aura
acquired surface or quality; aura

Examples

The old book possessed a patina of wisdom and charm that made it more appealing.
Years in the city had given him a patina of worldliness.
A patina of dust covered the furniture after the long winter absence.

Synset Examples

a patina of frost
a patina of good breeding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheen - a soft glow on a surface, often from age or polish

Etymology

From Latin patina (shallow dish, pan), later referring to the film on copper dishes.

4. NOUN

/ pəˈtinə / (AmE), / pəˈtiːnə / (BrE)
A thin layer or film that forms on the surface of something, especially due to age, use, or exposure, often giving it an attractive appearance.
surface layer formed by age or use

Examples

The antique table had developed a beautiful patina over the years.
Collectors value old coins that show a natural patina from handling and time.
The bronze statue's green patina made it look even more majestic.

Synset Examples

He admired the patina on the brass fittings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheen - a soft glow or shine, especially on a surface
  • Synonym: tarnish - a dullness that forms on metals due to oxidation

Etymology

From Italian 'patina' (shallow dish, film), from Latin 'patina' (dish, pan).
