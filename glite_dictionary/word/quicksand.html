quicksand

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪkˌsænd / (AmE), / ˈkwɪk.sænd / (BrE)
A dangerous or difficult situation from which it is hard to escape.
dangerous, inescapable situation

Examples

He found himself in political quicksand after making that controversial statement.
The company is trapped in financial quicksand and can't find a way out.
Getting involved in their dispute was like stepping into quicksand; the more I tried to help, the worse it became.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trap - something that catches or ensnares
  • Synonym: predicament - a difficult, unpleasant, or embarrassing situation
  • Antonym: escape - the act of getting away from a dangerous place or situation

Etymology

From Middle English 'quyksand', from 'quyk' (alive, moving) + 'sand'. Originally referred to loose, shifting sand.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪkˌsænd / (AmE), / ˈkwɪk.sænd / (BrE)
a deep mass of loose, wet sand into which heavy objects can sink and become trapped
a mass of loose, wet sand that sucks objects down

Examples

Many explorers feared getting trapped in quicksand during their jungle adventures.
After the heavy rains, portions of the trail turned into dangerous quicksand.
In old movies, characters often sink slowly into quicksand when they stray off the path.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bog - an area of wet, spongy ground

Etymology

From Middle English 'quyk sand', from Old English 'cwicesand' meaning 'living sand'.
