Picoides

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈkoʊ.ɪˌdiːz / (AmE), / pɪˈkəʊ.ɪˌdiːz / (BrE)
A genus of woodpeckers in the family Picidae, primarily found in North America.
genus of North American woodpeckers

Examples

The genus Picoides includes some of the most common woodpeckers in North America.
Researchers observed a Picoides species nesting in the old pine tree.
Several Picoides woodpeckers are easily identified by the distinctive markings on their heads.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'pikos' (woodpecker) and '-oidēs' (resembling).
