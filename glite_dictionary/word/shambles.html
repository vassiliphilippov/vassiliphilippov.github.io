shambles

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃæmbəlz / (AmE), / ˈʃæmbəlz / (BrE)
a state of complete disorder or mess
state of disorder or mess

Examples

After the party, the house was in a complete shambles.
The project quickly descended into shambles after the manager resigned.
Their finances were left in shambles following the unexpected expenses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaos - complete confusion and disorder
  • Antonym: order - a state in which everything is arranged properly

Etymology

Originally from Middle English 'shameles' meaning a slaughterhouse, from Old English 'sceamol' (stool, stall).

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃæmbəlz / (AmE), / ˈʃæmbəlz / (BrE)
(archaic) A place, especially a building, where animals are butchered for meat.
place where animals are butchered (archaic)
archaic

Examples

The old market was once a shambles where local farmers brought their livestock for slaughter.
In medieval towns, the shambles was often located away from residential areas due to the unpleasant smells.
Historical records mention that a notorious shambles operated at the city’s edge for several centuries.

Etymology

From Old English *sceamel* (stool, table), later used for butcher's table, then place of slaughter.
