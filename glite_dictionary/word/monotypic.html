monotypic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɑːnəˈtɪpɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒnɪˈtɪpɪk / (BrE)
(of a group or category) consisting of only one type, species, or member.
having only one type or species

Examples

The monotypic genus contains only a single species unique to this region.
This family of insects is monotypic, making it easy for researchers to classify.
Because the plant is monotypic, any extinction event would wipe out the entire genus.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polytypic - consisting of more than one type or species

Etymology

From Greek 'mono-' (single) and 'typos' (type).
