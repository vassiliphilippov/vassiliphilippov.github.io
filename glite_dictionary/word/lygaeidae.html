Lygaeidae

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈdʒiːɪdiː / (AmE), / laɪˈdʒiːɪdiː / (BrE)
A large family of mostly ground-dwelling true bugs, commonly known as seed bugs, many of which feed on seeds or plant juices.
family of seed bugs (true bugs)

Examples

The Lygaeidae are often found on the ground where they feed on seeds.
Many species in the Lygaeidae family display striking coloration as a warning to predators.
Recent studies have highlighted the ecological role of Lygaeidae in grassland habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seed bug - a common name for insects in the family Lygaeidae

Etymology

From New Latin Lygaeidae, based on the type genus Lygaeus.
