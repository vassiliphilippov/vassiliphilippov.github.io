objective

1. NOUN

/ əbˈdʒɛktɪv / (AmE), / əbˈdʒɛktɪv / (BrE)
the lens or group of lenses in a microscope or telescope that is closest to the object under observation
lens nearest the object in microscope/telescope

Examples

The objective of the microscope must be carefully cleaned before use.
High-powered objectives can reveal details invisible to the naked eye.
She replaced the low-power objective with a higher magnification to study the cells.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'objectivus', from Latin 'objectus' (something thrown before).

2. ADJ

/ əbˈdʒɛktɪv / (AmE), / əbˈdʒɛktɪv / (BrE)
(of information or evidence) based on observable facts rather than feelings or opinions; not influenced by personal feelings.
not influenced by personal feelings; factual

Examples

The judge considered only the objective facts of the case before making her ruling.
Scientific research tries to remain as objective as possible, relying on measurable data.
There is no objective evidence to support his extraordinary claims.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: subjective - influenced by personal feelings or opinions
  • Synonym: impartial - treating all sides equally; unbiased

Etymology

From Medieval Latin objectivus ('presented to the mind'), from Latin objectum ('thing presented'), from obicere ('to present').
