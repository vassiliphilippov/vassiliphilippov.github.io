Melampsora lini

1. NOUN

/ məˌlæmpˌsɔrə ˈlaɪnaɪ / (AmE), / mɛˌlæmpˌsɔːrə ˈlɪnaɪ / (BrE)
a species of fungus that causes flax rust, a disease affecting flax plants
fungus causing flax rust

Examples

Melampsora lini is responsible for the rust disease that devastates flax crops worldwide.
Researchers studied how Melampsora lini infects and spreads among flax plants.
Farmers must monitor for signs of Melampsora lini to prevent significant losses in flax production.

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Melampsora' (a genus name), and Latin 'lini' (of flax).
