epenthesis

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈpɛnθəsɪs / (AmE), / ɪˈpɛnθəsɪs / (BrE)
the addition of a vowel or consonant within a word, typically to make pronunciation easier
insertion of a sound in a word

Examples

The word 'athlete' sometimes undergoes epenthesis when pronounced as 'ath-e-lete.'
In some dialects, an extra vowel is added by epenthesis to make words easier to pronounce.
Linguists study epenthesis to understand how speech patterns change across languages.

Synset Examples

the insertion of a vowel in the plural of the word ‘bush’ is epenthesis

Related Terms

  • Antonym: elision - the omission of a sound or syllable
  • Synonym: intrusion - the addition of a sound, typically between vowels, in speech

Etymology

From Greek 'epenthesis', from 'epi-' (upon) + 'enthemi' (to put in).
