ceremonial

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛrəˈmoʊniəl / (AmE), / ˌsɛrəˈməʊniəl / (BrE)
(of an event, behaviour, or object) involving or relating to traditional ceremonies or formality; marked by pomp or ritual.
relating to formal ceremonies or rituals

Examples

The ambassador arrived in ceremonial robes for the state dinner.
They performed a ceremonial dance to celebrate the festival.
The contract signing was a ceremonial occasion attended by officials.

Synset Examples

a ceremonial occasion
ceremonial garb

Related Terms

  • Synonym: formal - done in accordance with rules of convention or etiquette
  • Antonym: informal - having a relaxed, unofficial, or friendly style, manner, or nature

Etymology

From Latin caerimonialis (pertaining to ceremony), from caerimonia (reverence, ceremony).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛrəˈmoʊniəl / (AmE), / ˌsɛrəˈməʊniəl / (BrE)
a formal event or act performed on a special or important occasion, often following traditional or prescribed procedures
formal event or ritual

Examples

The ambassador attended the ceremonial marking the country's independence.
A traditional Japanese ceremonial is held at the start of each year.
Many people wore special attire for the graduation ceremonial.

Synset Examples

a ceremony commemorating Pearl Harbor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ceremony - a formal religious or public occasion, typically one celebrating a particular event or anniversary

Etymology

From Late Latin ceremonialis (pertaining to ceremony), from Latin caerimonia (religious rite).
