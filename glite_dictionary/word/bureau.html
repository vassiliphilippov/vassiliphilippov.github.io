bureau

1. NOUN

/ ˈbjʊr.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈbjʊə.rəʊ / (BrE)
a government department or agency responsible for a particular area of administration
government department or agency
american

Examples

The Environmental Protection Bureau issued new regulations last week.
She works as an analyst for the Federal Investigation Bureau.
Each bureau reports its findings to the central authority on a monthly basis.

Synset Examples

the Central Intelligence Agency
the Census Bureau
Office of Management and Budget
Tennessee Valley Authority

Related Terms

  • Synonym: department - a distinct part of a larger organization, especially in government or business
  • Synonym: agency - an organization, often governmental, responsible for a specific function

Etymology

From French 'bureau' (office, desk), ultimately from Old French 'burel' (woolen cloth) which was used to cover desks.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbjʊroʊ / (AmE), / ˈbjʊərəʊ / (BrE)
A piece of furniture with drawers, used for storing clothes or other items.
chest of drawers for clothes

Examples

She placed her favorite jewelry box on top of the bureau in the bedroom.
The bureau has deep drawers that can hold all of your sweaters and shirts.
He carefully folded his clothes and put them away in the bureau before leaving for work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dresser - a piece of furniture with drawers for storing clothes
  • Synonym: chest of drawers - a piece of furniture with drawers for storing clothes

Etymology

From French 'bureau' (desk, office), ultimately from Old French 'burel' (cloth covering desks).
