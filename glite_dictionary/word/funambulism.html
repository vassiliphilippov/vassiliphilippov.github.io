funambulism

1. NOUN

/ fjʊˈnæmbjəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / fjʊˈnæmbjʊˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
the art or practice of walking on a tightrope or slack rope, especially as a performance
walking on a tightrope

Examples

She trained for years to master funambulism and perform in the circus.
The history of funambulism dates back to ancient Rome.
Modern exhibitions of funambulism often include breathtaking stunts at great heights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tightrope walking - the act of walking along a tightrope

Etymology

From Latin funambulus (rope-walker), from funis (rope) and ambulare (to walk).
