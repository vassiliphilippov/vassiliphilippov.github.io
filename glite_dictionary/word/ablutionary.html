ablutionary

1. ADJ

/ əˈbluːʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / əˈbluːʃənəri / (BrE)
relating to or intended for washing or cleansing the body, especially as part of a religious or ritual act
relating to ritual washing or cleansing

Examples

The priest performed the ablutionary rites before entering the temple.
Muslim tradition includes several ablutionary practices before prayer.
In the ceremony, an ablutionary bowl was used to purify the participants.

Synset Examples

ablutionary rituals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ablution - the act of washing oneself, often for ritual purposes

Etymology

From Latin ablutio (a washing) + -ary (relating to).
