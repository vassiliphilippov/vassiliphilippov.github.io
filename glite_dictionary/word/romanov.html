Romanov

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊməˌnɔv / (AmE), / ˈrəʊmənɒf / (BrE)
a member of the imperial family that ruled Russia from 1613 until the Russian Revolution of 1917
member of Russian imperial family

Examples

The last Romanov czar, Nicholas II, was overthrown during the Russian Revolution.
Several members of the Romanov family lived in exile after 1917.
Historians still debate the fate of the missing Romanov children.

Etymology

Russian Рома́нов (Románov), the family name of the Russian imperial dynasty.
