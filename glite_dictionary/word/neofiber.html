Neofiber

1. NOUN

/ ˈnioʊˌfaɪbər / (AmE), / ˈniːəʊˌfaɪbə / (BrE)
A genus of North American rodents commonly known as the round-tailed muskrat.
genus of round-tailed muskrats

Examples

The Neofiber is native to wetland areas in the southeastern United States.
Some researchers study the ecology of Neofiber in relation to habitat loss.
Unlike other muskrats, Neofiber tends to have a rounder tail and smaller body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ondatra - genus of the common muskrat

Etymology

From Greek neo- (new) + Latin fibra (fiber), referring to the animal's fine fur.

2. PROPN

/ ˌniːoʊˈfaɪbər / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
A genus of rodents in the family Cricetidae, containing the round-tailed muskrat native to southeastern North America.
rodent genus including the round-tailed muskrat

Examples

The Neofiber genus is represented by the round-tailed muskrat found in southeastern wetlands.
Researchers studied Neofiber populations to monitor wetland ecosystem health.
The round-tailed muskrat, classified as Neofiber, is unique to North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muskrat - a large semiaquatic rodent native to North America

Etymology

From Greek 'neos' meaning 'new' and Latin 'fiber', a genus name.
