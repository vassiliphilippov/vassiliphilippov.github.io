expert

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛk.spɚt / (AmE), / ˈɛk.spɜːt / (BrE)
Having or showing a high level of knowledge or skill in a particular area.
having special knowledge or skill

Examples

She handled the situation in an expert manner, impressing everyone in the room.
The chef prepared an expert presentation of the dish, with every element perfectly balanced.
His expert opinion was sought during the crucial stages of the investigation.

Synset Examples

adept in handicrafts
an adept juggler
an expert job
a good mechanic
a practiced marksman
a proficient engineer
a lesser-known but no less skillful composer
the effect was achieved by skillful retouching

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skilled - having or showing skill or competence
  • Synonym: proficient - competent or skilled in doing or using something
  • Antonym: unskilled - not having or showing skill
  • Antonym: novice - a person new to or inexperienced in a field

Etymology

From Latin 'expertus' (tried, proven), past participle of 'experiri' (to try, test).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛk.spɚt / (AmE), / ˈek.spɜːt / (BrE)
a person who has a high level of knowledge or skill in a particular subject or area
person with special skill or knowledge

Examples

She consulted an expert to help her with the legal documents.
As an expert in computer programming, he quickly fixed the problem.
The museum invited an art expert to authenticate the rare painting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: specialist - a person who is an expert in a specific area or field
  • Antonym: amateur - a person who engages in an activity for pleasure rather than as a profession or with expert skill

Etymology

From Latin 'expertus' (tried, experienced), past participle of 'experiri' (to try).

Synset POS: n
