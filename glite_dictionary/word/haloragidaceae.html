Haloragidaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌheɪ.ləˌrædʒ.ɪˈdeɪ.siː / (AmE), / ˌhæ.ləˌræ.dʒɪˈdiː.siː / (BrE)
a family of dicotyledonous flowering plants in the order Myrtales, commonly known as the water milfoil family
family of flowering plants (water milfoils)

Examples

The Haloragidaceae family includes many aquatic and semi-aquatic plants found in various climates.
Botanists studied the distinctive floral structures of Haloragidaceae to better understand plant evolution.
Members of the Haloragidaceae are often found in wetlands and shallow freshwater environments.

Etymology

From New Latin Haloragidaceae, based on genus Haloragis, from Greek hals (sea, salt) + rhagos (berry).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhæləˌrædʒɪˈdeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌhæləˌrædʒɪˈdeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of dicotyledonous plants in the order Myrtales, including aquatic and marsh species such as water milfoils
a family of flowering plants in Myrtales

Examples

Several aquatic plants in the Haloragidaceae family thrive in marshy wetlands worldwide.
Many species within Haloragidaceae play important roles in freshwater ecosystems.
The botanist specialized in the taxonomy of Haloragidaceae and related families.

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from Haloragis (type genus) + -aceae (suffix denoting plant family).
