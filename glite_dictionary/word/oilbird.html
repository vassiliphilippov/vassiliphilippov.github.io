oilbird

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔɪlˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈɔɪlˌbɜːd / (BrE)
a nocturnal fruit-eating bird of South America whose young are notably fatty and were traditionally harvested for their oil, used as a substitute for butter
South American nocturnal fruit-eating bird

Examples

The oilbird navigates caves using echolocation, much like a bat.
Researchers studied the nesting habits of the oilbird in remote regions of Venezuela.
Local communities once collected oilbird chicks for their valuable fat.
