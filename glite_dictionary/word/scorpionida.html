Scorpionida

1. NOUN

/ skɔːrˈpiːnɪdə / (AmE), / skɔːˈpɪnɪdə / (BrE)
An order of arachnids that comprises the true scorpions, characterized by segmented tails ending in a venomous stinger.
order of true scorpions

Examples

The Scorpionida order includes about 2,000 species worldwide.
Researchers studied the venom of several Scorpionida species for potential medical applications.
Unlike spiders, members of Scorpionida possess prominent pincers and segmented tails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scorpion - an arachnid with a venomous stinger

Etymology

From Latin 'scorpio' (scorpion) + New Latin taxonomic suffix '-ida'.
