torturesome

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɔrtʃərˌsʌm / (AmE), / ˈtɔːtʃəsəm / (BrE)
causing or experiencing great physical or mental pain; extremely painful
causing extreme pain or suffering

Examples

The recovery process was torturesome and seemed endless to him.
She found waiting for the final results to be torturesome.
The heat in the desert felt absolutely torturesome during the hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: painful - causing physical or mental pain
  • Synonym: excruciating - intensely painful; causing extreme agony
  • Antonym: pleasant - giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment

Etymology

From 'torture' + '-some', forming an adjective meaning 'full of torture'.
