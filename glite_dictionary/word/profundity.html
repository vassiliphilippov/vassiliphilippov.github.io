profundity

1. NOUN

/ prəˈfʌndəti / (AmE), / prəˈfʌndɪti / (BrE)
the quality or intellectual ability of having deep insight or understanding
deep insight or intellectual depth

Examples

Her profundity of thought impressed even the most experienced scholars in the room.
The novel is admired for its profundity as well as its detailed depiction of everyday life.
Despite his youth, his comments revealed a surprising profundity and seriousness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depth - the quality of being deep, often used figuratively for insight or understanding
  • Antonym: superficiality - lack of depth of knowledge or thought; superficial quality

Etymology

From Latin profunditas (depth), from profundus (deep).

3. NOUN

/ prəˈfʌndəti / (AmE), / prəˈfʌndɪti / (BrE)
great intellectual depth, deep insight, or complexity of thought or feeling
intellectual depth or deep insight

Examples

The profundity of his analysis impressed the entire panel of experts.
Sometimes, simple words can carry unexpected profundity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depth - the quality of being deep in thought or feeling
  • Antonym: superficiality - lack of depth or thoroughness

Etymology
