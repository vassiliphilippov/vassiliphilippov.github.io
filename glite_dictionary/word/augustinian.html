Augustinian

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔɡəˈstɪniən / (AmE), / ˌɔːɡəˈstɪniən / (BrE)
a Roman Catholic friar or monk who belongs to one of the monastic orders following the Rule of St Augustine.
a member of an Augustinian monastic order

Examples

The monastery was founded by Augustinians in the late Middle Ages.
He became an Augustinian and devoted his life to service and scholarship.
Many Augustinian friars established schools throughout Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Benedictine - a member of the Benedictine order of monks
  • Synonym: Franciscan - a member of the Franciscan order of monks

Etymology

From Latin Augustinianus, relating to St Augustine.
