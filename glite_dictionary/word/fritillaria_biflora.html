Fritillaria biflora

1. NOUN

/ frɪtɪˈlɛriə baɪˈflɔːrə / (AmE), / frɪtɪˈlæriə baɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
A herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States, known for its dark purple, bell-shaped flowers mottled with green.
herbaceous plant with dark purple mottled flowers

Examples

The rare Fritillaria biflora grew along the edge of the grassy hillside.
Wildflower enthusiasts visited the nature reserve to photograph blooming Fritillaria biflora.
Conservationists are working to protect habitats where Fritillaria biflora can thrive.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Fritillaria' (a genus name, from 'fritillus' meaning dice-box) and 'biflora' (bi- meaning 'two', flora meaning 'flowered').
