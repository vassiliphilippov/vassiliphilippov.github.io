clubby

1. ADJ

/ ˈklʌbi / (AmE), / ˈklʌbi / (BrE)
(of a place, group, or atmosphere) having an exclusive, sociable, or insular quality, especially one that excludes outsiders or those considered inferior.
exclusive or insular in a sociable way

Examples

The old university society had a clubby atmosphere that made newcomers feel unwelcome.
Her new job has a clubby culture where only long-time employees get invited to important meetings.
The restaurant's clubby decor made it feel exclusive and somewhat intimidating to first-time guests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exclusive - limiting or excluding others; not admitting outsiders
  • Antonym: inclusive - open to everyone; not excluding any group

2. ADJ

/ ˈklʌbi / (AmE), / ˈklʌbi / (BrE)
(of a group, organization, or atmosphere) characterized by effusive sociability or exclusivity, especially resembling that of a social club.
exclusive and sociable like a club

Examples

The boardroom had a clubby feel, with everyone on a first-name basis.
Journalists sometimes complain about the clubby relationship between politicians and the press.
He found the finance industry a bit too clubby for his taste.

Synset Examples

a clubbish set
we got rather clubby

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exclusive - limiting access to a select group; not open to everyone
  • Synonym: sociable - enjoying or characterized by friendly company

Etymology

Formed from 'club' (as in a social club) + '-y', meaning 'like' or 'having the quality of'.
