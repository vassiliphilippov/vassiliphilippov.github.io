antiredeposition

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.taɪˌriː.dɪˈpəʊ.zɪn / (AmE), / ˌan.tɪˌriː.dɪˈpəʊ.zɪn / (BrE)
the process of preventing previously removed particles or substances from settling again, especially onto surfaces or fabrics during cleaning
prevention of redepositing substances (esp. in cleaning)

Examples

The new detergent formula enhances antiredeposition to keep clothes looking bright after repeated washes.
Effective antiredeposition prevents dirt from settling back on cleaned surfaces.
Scientists study antiredeposition to improve cleaning agents in various industries.

Etymology

From anti- (against) + redeposition (the action of depositing again).
