postictal

1. ADJ

/ poʊsˈɪktl̩ / (AmE), / pəʊsˈɪktl̩ / (BrE)
relating to the period immediately following a seizure or convulsion, especially in epileptic patients
after a seizure or convulsion (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient experienced confusion and fatigue during the postictal state after her seizure.
Postictal drowsiness is common in people recovering from an epileptic event.
Doctors monitored his vital signs carefully throughout the postictal period.

Synset Examples

postictal drowsiness

Related Terms

  • Antonym: preictal - relating to the period before a seizure

Etymology

From Latin 'post' (after) + 'ictus' (blow, stroke, seizure).
