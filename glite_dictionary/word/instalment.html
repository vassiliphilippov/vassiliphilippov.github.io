instalment

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈstɑlmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈstɑːlmənt / (BrE)
each of a series of parts that make up a broadcast serial, such as a radio or television series.
part of a broadcast serial

Examples

The first instalment of the drama aired last night to excellent reviews.
Each week brought a new instalment in the thrilling detective series.
Fans eagerly awaited the final instalment to discover the fate of their favorite characters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: episode - a single program that is part of a broadcast series

Etymology

From late Latin *installmentum*, based on Latin *instaurare* (to restore, renew).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈstɑlmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈstɑːlmənt / (BrE)
one of several parts into which a story, book, or other work is divided, published or broadcast at intervals
part of a published series or serial

Examples

The magazine published the final instalment of the crime novel last month.
Fans eagerly awaited each new instalment of the popular TV series.
She read the first instalment and couldn't wait for the next chapter to be released.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: episode - a single part of a broadcast or serial
  • Synonym: installment - alternative spelling (US English) for 'instalment'

Etymology

From French 'estaler' (to fix payments), later generalized to parts of a series.
