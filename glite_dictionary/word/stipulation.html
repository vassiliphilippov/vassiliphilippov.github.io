stipulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌstɪpjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌstɪpjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a condition or requirement that is specified in an agreement or contract
specified condition in an agreement

Examples

There is a stipulation in the contract that requires employees to give two weeks’ notice.
The company agreed to the merger, subject to the stipulation that no jobs would be lost.
Every rental agreement includes a stipulation about the deposit refund procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condition - a requirement specified in a contract or agreement

Etymology

From Latin stipulatio (promise, formal agreement).

2. NOUN

/ ˌstɪpjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌstɪpjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a condition or requirement that is specified or demanded as part of an agreement or arrangement, on which the validity or effect of something else depends
a specified requirement or condition

Examples

The contract included a stipulation that work must be completed within six months.
Without the necessary stipulation, the agreement would not be legally binding.
He agreed to the deal, but only with the stipulation that his expenses would be covered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condition - a requirement that must be met for something to happen
  • Synonym: proviso - a condition attached to an agreement
  • Synonym: term - a condition or provision in an agreement

Etymology

From Latin stipulatio (an agreement), from stipulari (to demand or bargain).

3. NOUN

/ ˌstɪpjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌstɪpjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
(law) an agreement or arrangement between parties in a legal case, often relating to facts or procedures, that is formally accepted by the court.
formal legal agreement in court
legal

Examples

The lawyers reached a stipulation regarding the timeline for submitting evidence.
By signing the stipulation, both parties agreed to the facts presented in the case.
The judge accepted the stipulation and recorded it in the court documents.

Synset Examples

a stipulation of fact was made in order to avoid delay

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agreement - a negotiated arrangement between parties
  • Synonym: condition - something required as part of an agreement

Etymology

From Latin stipulatio (a formal agreement), from stipulari (to demand or bargain).
