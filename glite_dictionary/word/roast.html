roast

1. NOUN

/ roʊst / (AmE), / rəʊst / (BrE)
A large piece of meat that is cooked by roasting, and is big enough to be sliced into several portions.
large piece of roasted meat

Examples

We served a delicious roast with vegetables for Sunday dinner.
The roast needs to rest before you start carving it.
She bought a beef roast for the holiday meal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joint - a large piece of meat for roasting, commonly used in British English

Etymology

From Old French 'rost', related to 'rooster' and ultimately of Germanic origin.

2. ADJ

/ roʊst / (AmE), / rəʊst / (BrE)
(Of meat) cooked by dry heat in an oven or over an open fire.
meat cooked by dry heat

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious roast chicken for Sunday dinner.
She served slices of roast lamb with potatoes and vegetables.
He prefers roast beef to boiled meat at family gatherings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: boiled - cooked in water or other liquid at boiling temperature
  • Antonym: grilled - cooked on a grill over direct heat

Etymology

From Old French 'rostir', from Frankish 'raustjan' (to roast).

3. VERB

/ roʊst / (AmE), / rəʊst / (BrE)
to subject someone to good-natured ridicule, often in a humorous or public setting
subject to humorous ridicule
informal american

Examples

The comedian will roast several celebrities during the televised event.
They invited friends to roast the retiring manager at the party.
His classmates playfully roasted him for his unusual fashion sense.

Synset Examples

The satirists ridiculed the plans for a new opera house
The students poked fun at the inexperienced teacher
His former students roasted the professor at his 60th birthday

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mock - to make fun of or ridicule someone
  • Antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration of

Etymology

Originally meant 'to cook by exposure to heat'; the figurative sense of subjecting someone to 'heated' comedic criticism developed in American English in the 20th century.

to cook food, especially meat, using dry heat in an oven or over an open fire
cook with dry heat (in oven or over fire)

5. NOUN

/ roʊst / (AmE), / rəʊst / (BrE)
a speech or event in which a person is subjected to humorously insulting and critical comments, often as entertainment; negative or mocking criticism
humorous mocking criticism, often at an event
informal american

Examples

The comedian hosted a roast where celebrities took turns making scathing jokes about each other.
She wasn't offended by the roast; in fact, she laughed the hardest at the harshest remarks.
After enduring that brutal roast, he admitted he had never felt more embarrassed and amused at the same time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mock - to make fun of someone or something by imitating or ridiculing
  • Antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration

Etymology

Extended from the verb 'to roast' (to cook with dry heat), in the sense of subjecting someone to 'heat' or intense criticism, especially in American comedy events.
