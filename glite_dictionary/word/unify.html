to unify

1. VERB

/ ˈjuː.nəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈjuː.nɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to bring different people, groups, or things together to form a single unit or to act together for a shared purpose
bring together to form a single unit

Examples

The new leader's speech helped to unify the members of the team.
These reforms aim to unify various educational standards across the country.
Efforts to unify the company departments have led to better communication.

Synset Examples

the Democratic Party platform united several splinter groups

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unite - to join together as a whole or act collectively
  • Antonym: divide - to separate into parts or groups

Etymology

From Latin unificare, from unus (one) + facere (to make).
