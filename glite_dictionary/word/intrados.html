intrados

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtreɪdoʊs / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrɑːdɒs / (BrE)
the inner curve or surface of an arch, especially in architecture
inner curve of an arch

Examples

The architect carefully measured the intrados to ensure the arch would be structurally sound.
The decorative mosaics followed the curve of the intrados above the old church's doorway.
During restoration, cracks were discovered along the intrados of the ancient stone arch.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: extrados - the outer curve of an arch

Etymology

From French 'intrados', from Latin 'intra-' (inside) + 'dorsum' (back).
