discernable

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈsɜrnəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈsɜːnəbəl / (BrE)
able to be perceived or recognized by the senses or the mind; perceptible.
able to be perceived or recognized

Examples

There was no discernable change in his behavior after the announcement.
The painting's original colors are hardly discernable beneath layers of dust.
A faint but discernable aroma of flowers drifted through the open window.

Synset Examples

things happen in the earth and sky with no discernible cause
the newspaper reports no discernible progress in the negotiations
the skyline is easily discernible even at a distance of several miles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perceptible - able to be seen, noticed, or felt
  • Antonym: indiscernible - impossible to see or clearly distinguish

Etymology

From Latin discernere (to separate, distinguish).
