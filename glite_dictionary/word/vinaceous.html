vinaceous

1. ADJ

/ vɪˈneɪʃəs / (AmE), / vɪˈneɪsɪəs / (BrE)
of, relating to, or having the color of wine, especially red wine
relating to or the color of wine

Examples

The butterfly has vinaceous wings that shimmer in the sunlight.
She wore a vinaceous dress to the party, reminiscent of a deep red wine.
A vinaceous hue dominated the vineyard landscape at sunset.

Etymology

From Latin 'vinaceus' (of wine), from 'vinum' (wine).

2. ADJ

/ vɪˈneɪʃəs / (AmE), / vɪˈneɪsɪəs / (BrE)
Of a color resembling that of red wine; wine-colored.
wine-colored; like the color of wine

Examples

The bird's vinaceous plumage set it apart from the others in the flock.
She wore a stunning dress in a rich vinaceous shade to the gala.
The painter mixed pigments to achieve the perfect vinaceous tone for the landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burgundy - a deep red color like that of Burgundy wine
  • Degree Lower: purplish - having a tinge of purple

Etymology

From Latin vinaceus (of wine), from vinum (wine).
