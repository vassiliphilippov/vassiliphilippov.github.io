winding-sheet

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪndɪŋ ʃiːt / (AmE), / ˈwaɪndɪŋ ʃiːt / (BrE)
A cloth in which a dead body is wrapped for burial; a burial shroud.
cloth to wrap a corpse for burial

Examples

The old churchyard was filled with graves wrapped in a simple winding-sheet.
In many cultures, a white winding-sheet is used for burial rituals.
The poem described a soul releasing its earthly form, lying still beneath a winding-sheet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shroud - a cloth used to wrap a body for burial

Etymology

From the verb 'wind' (to wrap, coil) and 'sheet' (a piece of cloth).
