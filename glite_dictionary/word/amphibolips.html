Amphibolips

1. NOUN

/ ˌæm.fəˈboʊ.lɪps / (AmE), / ˌam.fɪˈbəʊ.lɪps / (BrE)
A genus of gall wasps in the family Cynipidae, especially known for causing oak-apple galls.
genus of gall wasps causing oak galls

Examples

The oak tree was covered in galls formed by Amphibolips wasps.
Scientists studied several species of Amphibolips to understand gall formation on oaks.
Galls produced by Amphibolips can vary greatly in size and appearance.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Amphibolips, genus name formed from Greek 'amphíbolos' (ambiguous) + '-ips' (related to wasps).

2. PROPN

/ ˌæmfəˈboʊlɪps / (AmE), / ˌamfɪˈbəʊlɪps / (BrE)
A genus of gall wasps (family Cynipidae) known for causing oak-apple galls, particularly on oak trees.
genus of gall wasps causing oak-apple galls

Examples

Several species of Amphibolips are known to induce large, round galls on oak trees.
The life cycle of Amphibolips includes a larval stage that develops within the gall.
Researchers studied the impact of Amphibolips infestations on local oak populations.
