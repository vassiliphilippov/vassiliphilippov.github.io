quandary

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑːn.də.ri / (AmE), / ˈkwɒn.d(ə).ri / (BrE)
a state of uncertainty or perplexity, especially when requiring a choice between equally undesirable options
state of uncertainty or perplexity

Examples

After losing his job, Mark found himself in a real quandary about how to pay his bills.
The committee was in a quandary over whether to approve the risky project.
She faced a moral quandary when asked to lie for her friend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dilemma - a situation in which a difficult choice has to be made between two or more alternatives
  • Antonym: certainty - the state of being completely confident or sure about something

Etymology

Origin unknown; first recorded in late 16th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑːn.dɛɹ.i / (AmE), / ˈkwɒn.dəri / (BrE)
a state of uncertainty or perplexity, especially as to what to do in a difficult situation
a difficult or uncertain situation

Examples

After losing his passport abroad, he found himself in a real quandary.
She was in a quandary about whether to accept the new job or stay at her current one.
Their sudden silence left us in a quandary over how to proceed with the project.

Synset Examples

finds himself in a most awkward predicament
the woeful plight of homeless people

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dilemma - a situation in which a difficult choice has to be made
  • Synonym: predicament - a difficult, unpleasant, or embarrassing situation
  • Antonym: solution - the act of solving a problem or difficult situation

Etymology

Perhaps from Latin quandō (when) or from dialectal English quander (to waver, be uncertain).
