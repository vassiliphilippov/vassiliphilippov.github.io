counterproject

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntərˌprɑːdʒɛkt / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌprɒdʒɛkt / (BrE)
A project, scheme, or proposal put forward in opposition to another, especially in negotiations or decision-making processes.
project proposed in opposition to another

Examples

The committee submitted a counterproject to address the shortcomings of the original proposal.
After much debate, the rival faction introduced a counterproject in parliament.
A counterproject was devised to challenge the company's new development plan.

Synset Examples

Yet little did Tolstoi realize in the spring of 1888 that even more serious challenges lay ahead in the fall, when his opponents, capitalizing on Alexander IIl's detachment from the reform process, would rally together to present a counterproject.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterproposal - an offer or plan made in response to another

Etymology

Formed from 'counter-' (against) and 'project'.
