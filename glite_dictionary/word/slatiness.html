slatiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsleɪtinəs / (AmE), / ˈsleɪtɪnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being like slate, especially in appearance or texture.
quality of resembling slate

Examples

The slatiness of the rocks made them easy to split into thin sheets.
Geologists commented on the unusual slatiness found in this region's soil.
Her painting beautifully captured the cold slatiness of the northern cliffs.

Synset Examples

Ascending the hill on the west I find the slatiness of the formation not much apparent except in weathering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slaty - resembling or characteristic of slate

Etymology

Formed from 'slaty' (resembling slate) + '-ness'. 'Slate' from Old French 'esclate', a splinter, fragment.
