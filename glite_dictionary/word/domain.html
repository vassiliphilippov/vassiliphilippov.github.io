domain

1. NOUN

/ doʊˈmeɪn / (AmE), / dəʊˈmeɪn / (BrE)
an area of activity, knowledge, or influence; a particular environment or sphere of life
area of activity or influence

Examples

She is an expert in the scientific domain of genetics.
Education was once considered solely the domain of the elite.
In the political domain, compromise is often necessary.

Synset Examples

his social sphere is limited
it was a closed area of employment
he's out of my orbit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sphere - an area of activity, interest, or expertise
  • Synonym: realm - a field or domain of activity or interest

Etymology

From Latin 'dominium' (ownership, property), from 'dominus' (lord, master).

2. NOUN

/ doʊˈmeɪn / (AmE), / dəʊˈmeɪn / (BrE)
(Mathematics) The set of all possible values of the independent variable for which a function is defined.
set of input values of a function (math)
medical

Examples

In this equation, the domain is all real numbers greater than zero.
You must specify the domain before graphing the function.
For the function f(x) = 1/x, zero is not included in its domain.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: range - (Mathematics) the set of all output values of a function

Etymology

From Latin 'dominium' (ownership, property), via Old French 'domaine'.
