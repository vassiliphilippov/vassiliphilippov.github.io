unsoluble

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsɑːljʊbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɒljʊbəl / (BrE)
not capable of being solved or overcome; impossible or very difficult to resolve.
not easily solved or resolved

Examples

After years of negotiation, the two countries faced an unsoluble border dispute.
The mathematics professor assigned an unsoluble equation as a challenge for his students.
Her frustration grew with each attempt to fix the unsoluble computer error.

Synset Examples

an apparently insolvable problem
public finance … had long presented problems unsolvable or at least unsolved

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unsolvable - impossible to solve
  • Antonym: soluble - able to be solved or resolved

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'un-' (not) + 'soluble' (from Latin 'solubilis', able to be loosened or solved).
