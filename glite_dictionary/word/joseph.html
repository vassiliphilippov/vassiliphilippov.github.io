Joseph

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒoʊzəf / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊzɪf / (BrE)
(in the New Testament) the husband of Mary and, in Christian belief, the foster father of Jesus.
husband of Mary; foster father of Jesus

Examples

In the nativity story, Joseph travels with Mary to Bethlehem for the census.
Many works of art depict Joseph as a caring and protective father figure.
Christians honor Joseph for his faith and devotion to his family.

Etymology

From Hebrew Yosef, meaning 'he will add'; via Latin and Greek forms.

2. PROPN

/ ˈdʒoʊzəf / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊzɪf / (BrE)
(In the Old Testament) the 11th son of Jacob, known for his multicolored coat and his brothers selling him into slavery in Egypt; regarded as one of the twelve patriarchs of Israel.
Old Testament patriarch, 11th son of Jacob

Examples

Joseph was renowned for his ability to interpret dreams while in Egypt.
The story of Joseph and his brothers is a famous biblical narrative.
Many paintings depict Joseph wearing his coat of many colors.

Etymology

From Hebrew Yosef, meaning 'he will add' or 'God increases'.
