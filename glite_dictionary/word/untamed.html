untamed

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈteɪmd / (AmE), / ʌnˈteɪmd / (BrE)
(of animals, plants, or places) in a natural state; not tamed, domesticated, or cultivated.
not tamed or domesticated

Examples

The horses running freely across the plain were truly untamed.
He was inspired by the beauty of the untamed wilderness surrounding the village.
Many explorers are drawn to the challenge of surviving in untamed environments.

Synset Examples

wild geese
edible wild plants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild - in a natural state; not domesticated or cultivated

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'un-' (not) + past participle 'tamed'.
