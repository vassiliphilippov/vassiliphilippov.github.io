narcissism

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːrsɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈnɑːsɪsɪzəm / (BrE)
excessive interest in or admiration of oneself and one's physical appearance or abilities
excessive self-admiration

Examples

The politician's narcissism made it difficult for him to listen to criticism from others.
Therapists often encounter cases where extreme narcissism damages personal relationships.
His constant need for attention was seen as a sign of narcissism by his friends.

Synset Examples

self-love that shut out everyone else

Related Terms

  • Synonym: egotism - the practice of talking and thinking about oneself excessively
  • Antonym: selflessness - concern more with the needs and wishes of others than one's own

Etymology

From Greek Narkissos (Narcissus), the youth in mythology who fell in love with his own reflection.
