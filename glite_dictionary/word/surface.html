surface

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.fɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.fɪs / (BrE)
the outermost layer or top part of an area, especially of the land, sea, or any material
outermost layer or top part

Examples

He collected samples from the ocean's surface to study pollution levels.
The ball bounced off the hard surface of the road.
Most plants grow on or just below the surface of the soil.

Synset Examples

earthquakes originate far below the surface
three quarters of the Earth's surface is covered by water

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interior - the inside part of something; opposite of surface

Etymology

From French 'surface' (surface), from Latin 'superficies' (upper side, surface).
