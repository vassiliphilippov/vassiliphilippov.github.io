Procavia capensis

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈkeɪ.vi.ə kəˈpɛn.sɪs / (AmE), / prəˈkeɪ.vɪə kəˈpen.sɪs / (BrE)
A species of hyrax that inhabits rocky areas in Africa and the Middle East, commonly known as the rock hyrax.
species of hyrax (rock hyrax)

Examples

The Procavia capensis is well adapted to life among rocky outcrops and cliffs.
Researchers observed a group of Procavia capensis basking in the morning sun.
Unlike other mammals, Procavia capensis has unique, rubbery soles to help it grip rocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rock hyrax - common name for Procavia capensis, a small mammal living in rocky habitats

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Procavia', species epithet 'capensis' meaning 'of the Cape' (referring to the Cape of Good Hope).
