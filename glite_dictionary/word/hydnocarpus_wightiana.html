Hydnocarpus wightiana

1. NOUN

/ hɪdˌnoʊˈkɑrpəs waɪˈtɪænə / (AmE), / hɪdˌnəʊˈkɑːpəs waɪˈtɪanə / (BrE)
a leathery-leaved tree native to western India, bearing round fruits with a hairy brown rind and oily pulp used to produce hydnocarpus oil
tree of western India used to produce hydnocarpus oil

Examples

Hydnocarpus wightiana is an important source of oil used in traditional Indian medicine.
The seeds of Hydnocarpus wightiana are processed to extract hydnocarpus oil.
Several conservation efforts are underway to protect Hydnocarpus wightiana in its natural habitat.
