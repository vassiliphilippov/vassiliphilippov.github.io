family Ptilonorhynchidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪlənɔːˈrɪnkaɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌpɪlənɔːˈrɪŋkɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of passerine birds known as bowerbirds, native to Australia and New Guinea, notable for the males' elaborate courtship bowers.
the bowerbird family of birds

Examples

The family Ptilonorhynchidae includes all species of bowerbirds found in Australia and New Guinea.
Researchers have long been fascinated by the elaborate courtship displays within the family Ptilonorhynchidae.
Members of the family Ptilonorhynchidae are renowned for constructing intricate structures to attract mates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bowerbird - a bird of the family Ptilonorhynchidae, known for building elaborate bowers

Etymology

From Modern Latin Ptilonorhynchus (type genus), from Greek ptilon (feather) + rhynchos (beak).
