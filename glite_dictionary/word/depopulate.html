to depopulate

1. VERB

/ diˈpɑpjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / diˈpɒpjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to reduce the number of people living in a place, especially so that few or none remain
reduce population of a place

Examples

The war threatened to depopulate entire regions.
Industrial decline can depopulate small towns over time.
A severe drought may depopulate agricultural villages.

Synset Examples

The epidemic depopulated the countryside

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to populate - to supply with inhabitants; to fill with people

Etymology

From Medieval Latin depopulatus, past participle of depopulare (to lay waste, ravage, depopulate), from Latin de- (removal) + populus (people).
