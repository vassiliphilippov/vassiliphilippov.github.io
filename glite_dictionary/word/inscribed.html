inscribed

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈskraɪbd / (AmE), / ɪnˈskrʌɪbd / (BrE)
(of words, designs, or marks) cut or impressed into a surface, typically to record or decorate.
cut or impressed into a surface

Examples

The ancient stone had inscribed names that were barely visible after centuries.
The artist presented a vase with inscribed patterns around its rim.
Each medal was inscribed with the winner's name and the year.

Synset Examples

an incised design
engraved invitations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engraved - cut or carved into a surface
  • Synonym: carved - cut into the surface for decoration or inscription

Etymology

From Latin inscribere, meaning 'to write on'.

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈskraɪbd / (AmE), / ɪnˈskraɪbd / (BrE)
written, carved, or engraved on a surface
written or carved on a surface

Examples

Her name was inscribed on the trophy for everyone to see.
The ancient tablet had mysterious symbols inscribed on its surface.
The dedication is inscribed inside the book in elegant handwriting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engraved - cut or carved onto a surface
  • Synonym: written - marked by writing; put into written form

Etymology

From Latin inscribere (to write on).
