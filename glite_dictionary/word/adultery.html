adultery

1. NOUN

/ əˈdʌl.tɚ.i / (AmE), / əˈdʌl.təri / (BrE)
voluntary sexual intercourse between a married person and someone who is not their spouse
sex outside marriage by a married person

Examples

In some cultures, adultery can have serious social and legal consequences.
She accused her husband of adultery during their contentious divorce proceedings.
The judge found that adultery was proven and granted the petition for separation.

Synset Examples

adultery is often cited as grounds for divorce

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infidelity - unfaithfulness in a marriage or relationship

Etymology

From Latin adulterium (adultery), from adulter (adulterer).
