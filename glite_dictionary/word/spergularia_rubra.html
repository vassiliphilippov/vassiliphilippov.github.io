Spergularia rubra

1. NOUN

/ spɚˌɡjʊˈlɛɹi.ə ˈruːbrə / (AmE), / spəˌɡjʊˈlɛːri.ə ˈruːbrə / (BrE)
A low-growing weedy herb with small pink flowers, native to Europe and Asia, and naturalized in eastern North America.
a prostrate weedy herb with pink flowers

Examples

Spergularia rubra often carpets sandy soils in coastal regions with its tiny pink blooms.
Botanists recorded the spread of Spergularia rubra along the abandoned railway tracks.
Gardeners sometimes mistake Spergularia rubra for other common weeds due to its low growth and small flowers.

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Spergularia', species 'rubra' meaning 'red'.
