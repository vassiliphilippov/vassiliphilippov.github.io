foliated

1. ADJ

/ ˈfoʊliˌeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈfəʊliˌeɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of rock, especially metamorphic rock) having thin, leaflike layers or strata.
having thin layered structure (rock)

Examples

The geologist pointed out the foliated structure of the schist.
Many metamorphic rocks are foliated, displaying distinct parallel layers.
Slate, a foliated metamorphic rock, splits easily along its thin layers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonfoliated - (of rocks) not having thin, leaflike layers

Etymology

From Latin foliatus, from folium (leaf), referring to leaflike layers.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfoʊ.li.eɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈfəʊ.li.eɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
decorated or ornamented with representations of leaves or leaf-like forms, especially in architecture or art
decorated with leaf-like forms

Examples

The cathedral's windows featured foliated tracery typical of Gothic architecture.
Each column was topped with a foliated capital adorned with intricate leaf patterns.
The furniture was expertly carved, displaying foliated designs along the edges.

Synset Examples

foliate tracery
a foliated capital

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foliate - decorated with leaves or leaf shapes

Etymology

Formed from 'foliate' (from Latin foliatus, "leafy") and the suffix '-ed'.
