Hexanchus griseus

1. NOUN

/ hɛkˈsæŋkəs ˈɡraɪsiəs / (AmE), / hɛkˈsaŋkəs ˈɡrɪsɪəs / (BrE)
a large, primitive shark species widely distributed in warm seas, commonly known as the bluntnose sixgill shark
species of large primitive shark

Examples

The Hexanchus griseus is known to inhabit deep waters around the world.
Marine biologists were excited to observe a live Hexanchus griseus near the continental shelf.
Despite its size, the Hexanchus griseus is not considered a major threat to humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluntnose sixgill shark - common name for Hexanchus griseus

Etymology

From Latin; Hexanchus from Greek 'hex' (six) + 'agkhi' (bend), and griseus meaning 'gray'.
