waw

1. NOUN

/ wɑː / (AmE), / wɔː / (BrE)
the sixth letter of the Hebrew alphabet, corresponding to 'w' or 'v' sounds
sixth letter of Hebrew alphabet

Examples

The Hebrew letter waw represents a sound similar to the English 'v' or 'w'.
In Hebrew grammar, waw is sometimes used as a conjunction meaning 'and'.
Many Torah scrolls begin with the letter waw in certain sections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vav - alternative name for the same Hebrew letter

Etymology

From Hebrew ו (waw).
