mammalogy

1. NOUN

/ məˈmælədʒi / (AmE), / məˈmælədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of zoology concerned with the study of mammals
study of mammals

Examples

She decided to specialize in mammalogy and spent years researching bat species.
The university offers advanced courses in mammalogy for graduate students interested in animal science.
Her passion for wildlife led her to a career in mammalogy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: zoology - the scientific study of animals
  • Synonym: mammal - a warm-blooded vertebrate animal with hair or fur, females produce milk for young

Etymology

From New Latin mammalogia: Latin mamma (breast) + -logia (study of)
