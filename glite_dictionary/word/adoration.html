adoration

1. NOUN

/ ˌædəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌadəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a feeling or expression of deep love, admiration, or respect for someone or something
deep love or admiration

Examples

Her adoration for her grandmother was clear in every word she spoke.
The singer expressed deep adoration for his loyal fans during the concert.
He looked at his newborn daughter with pure adoration in his eyes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worship - the feeling or expression of reverence and adoration for a deity
  • Synonym: admiration - a feeling of respect and approval

Etymology

From Latin adoratio (worship), from adorare (to worship).

2. NOUN

/ ˌædəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌadəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
Worship or profound reverence given to God, especially in religious contexts.
worship or reverence given to God

Examples

The faithful gathered at the church for an hour of adoration each evening.
In many religions, adoration is reserved solely for the divine.
His prayers were filled with adoration for God.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worship - the feeling or expression of reverence and adoration for a deity
  • Synonym: reverence - deep respect for someone or something, especially the divine
  • Antonym: blasphemy - the act of insulting or showing lack of reverence for God

Etymology

From Latin 'adoratio' (worship), from 'adorare' (to worship).

3. NOUN

/ ˌædəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌadəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a feeling of deep love and respect for someone or something
deep love and respect

Examples

She looked at her newborn son with pure adoration in her eyes.
His adoration for the actress was obvious to everyone.
The puppy's adoration of its owner was heartwarming to see.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worship - the act of showing reverence and adoration
  • Synonym: devotion - strong love or loyalty for someone or something
  • Antonym: contempt - the feeling of disdain or lack of respect

Etymology

From Latin 'adoratio', from 'adorare' (to worship).
