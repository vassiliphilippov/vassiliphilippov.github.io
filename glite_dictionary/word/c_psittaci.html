C. psittaci

1. NOUN

/ si pəˈsɪtaɪ / (AmE), / si pəˈsɪtaɪ / (BrE)
a species of bacteria that can cause infection in birds and humans, including respiratory and sometimes sexually transmitted diseases
bacterial species causing infections
medical

Examples

The veterinarian diagnosed the parrot with an infection caused by C. psittaci.
Researchers are studying how C. psittaci can be transmitted between birds and humans.
Antibiotics are used to treat diseases caused by C. psittaci in humans.

Etymology

Abbreviation of Chlamydia psittaci, from Greek psittakos (parrot), referring to its discovery in parrots.
