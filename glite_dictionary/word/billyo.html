billyo

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪl.i.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈbɪl.i.əʊ / (BrE)
an unimaginably large amount or to a great extent; used chiefly in the phrase 'like billyo' to mean extremely or vigorously.
an unimaginably large amount; vigorously (informal, British)
informal british

Examples

It was raining billyo by the time we left the pub.
He ran like billyo to catch the last bus home.
The engine was smoking billyo after the long drive.

Synset Examples

British say ‘it rained like billyo’ where Americans say ‘it rained like all get out’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: like all get out - an American informal phrase meaning with great intensity or to an extreme degree
  • Synonym: a lot - a large quantity or extent
