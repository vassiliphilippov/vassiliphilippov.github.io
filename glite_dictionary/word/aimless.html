aimless

1. ADJ

/ ˈeɪmləs / (AmE), / ˈeɪmləs / (BrE)
without purpose or direction; lacking a goal or plan
without purpose or direction

Examples

After graduation, he spent a year living an aimless life, not sure what to do next.
The conversation soon became aimless and wandered from topic to topic.
She walked aimless through the park, lost in thought.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: purposeful - having a clear aim or goal
  • Synonym: directionless - lacking direction or purpose

Etymology

From English 'aim' + '-less', patterned after other adjectives meaning 'without' (e.g. 'hopeless').

2. ADJ

/ ˈeɪmləs / (AmE), / ˈeɪmləs / (BrE)
having no purpose, direction, or plan; continually changing or moving without a clear goal or aim
without purpose or direction

Examples

After college, he spent an aimless year traveling through Europe with no fixed plans.
The conversation drifted in an aimless way, never settling on any particular topic.
She felt her life was aimless after losing her job and was unsure which direction to take.

Synset Examples

a drifting double-dealer
the floating population
vagrant hippies of the sixties

Related Terms

  • Antonym: purposeful - having a clear purpose or intention
  • Synonym: wandering - moving from place to place with no fixed home

Etymology

From ‘aim’ (Middle English ‘ayme’, from Old French ‘esmer’ meaning 'to estimate, calculate') + ‘-less’.
