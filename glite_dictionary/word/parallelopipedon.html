parallelopipedon

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəˌlɛləˈpaɪpɪˌdɑn / (AmE), / ˌparəˌlɛləˈpɪpɪdɒn / (BrE)
A three-dimensional solid whose six faces are all parallelograms, especially a type of prism with parallelogram bases.
prism with parallelogram bases

Examples

The shape of the box can be described as a parallelopipedon with opposite faces that are equal parallelograms.
In geometry, a parallelopipedon is a prism whose faces are all parallelograms.
The crystal was cut into the form of a parallelopipedon for the experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parallelepiped - A solid figure with six faces, all parallelograms (alternate spelling)
  • Degree Lower: cube - A solid figure with six square faces (special type of parallelepiped)

Etymology

From Greek 'parallelo-' (parallel) + 'epipedon' (plane surface).
