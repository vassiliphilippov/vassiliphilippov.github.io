umber

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈʌmbə / (BrE)
a natural brown or reddish-brown earth pigment containing iron oxide and manganese oxide, used in painting
a natural brown earth pigment

Examples

The artist mixed umber with white to create a soft, muted tone.
Many Renaissance paintings feature backgrounds colored with umber.
She bought tubes of burnt sienna and raw umber for her landscape project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sienna - a reddish-brown earth pigment also used in painting
  • Synonym: ochre - an earth pigment varying from yellow to deep orange or brown

Etymology

From Italian 'ombra' (shadow), from Latin 'umbra' (shade, shadow).

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈʌmbə / (BrE)
a medium to dark brown color, often with a reddish or yellowish tone, especially used as a pigment in painting
medium to dark brown color

Examples

The artist used umber to create deep shadows in the landscape painting.
A touch of umber can warm up a neutral color palette.
She chose an umber rug to add earthiness to the living room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sepia - a reddish-brown color
  • Synonym: sienna - a yellow-brown or reddish-brown color, especially as a pigment
  • Degree Higher: brown - a dark color formed by mixing red, yellow, and blue

Etymology

From Italian 'ombra' (shadow), from Latin 'umbra'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈʌmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈʌmbə / (BrE)
of a dark yellowish-brown or reddish-brown color, like that of the natural earth pigment umber.
dark brownish earth color

Examples

The artist selected an umber hue to depict the earthen walls in his painting.
She wore a scarf in a rich umber shade that complemented her brown eyes.
The umber landscape stretched out under the fading afternoon sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brown - a color ranging between red and yellow; dark wood or rich soil
  • Synonym: ochre - an earthy pigment containing ferric oxide, typically with clay, varying from light yellow to brown or red
  • Degree Higher: sienna - a type of earth pigment with a more reddish tone than umber

Etymology

From Latin 'umbra' (shade, shadow), referring to the use of umber pigment for creating shadows in painting.
