trowel

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraʊ.əl / (AmE), / ˈtraʊ.əl / (BrE)
a small hand tool with a flat metal blade and a handle, used for scooping, spreading, or smoothing substances such as plaster or cement
tool for spreading or smoothing plaster, cement, etc.

Examples

The mason picked up his trowel and began spreading mortar between the bricks.
She used a trowel to apply plaster smoothly to the wall.
Gardeners often use a trowel to transplant seedlings into the soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spatula - a tool with a broad, flat, blunt blade, used for mixing, spreading, or lifting

Etymology

From Old French *truelle*, from Late Latin *truella* (small ladle).

2. VERB

/ ˈtraʊəl / (AmE), / ˈtraʊəl / (BrE)
to use a trowel for light work in gardening, plastering, or bricklaying
use a trowel in gardening or plastering

Examples

He troweled the soil carefully around the new seedlings.
She trowelled fresh plaster onto the wall during renovations.
They spent the afternoon troweling in the vegetable garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dig - to break up and move earth with a tool or hands
  • Synonym: plaster - to cover a surface with plaster
