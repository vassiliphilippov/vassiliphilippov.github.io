Circaea lutetiana

1. NOUN

/ sɝːˈsiː.ə luːˌtiːʃiˈɑːnə / (AmE), / sɜːˈsiː.ə luːˌtiːʃiˈɑːnə / (BrE)
A perennial herbaceous plant in the evening primrose family, with small, inconspicuous white flowers, commonly known as enchanter's nightshade.
a species of enchanter's nightshade

Examples

Circaea lutetiana is often found in damp, shady woodlands throughout Europe.
Gardeners sometimes plant Circaea lutetiana for its delicate appearance and ease of care.
The white flowers of Circaea lutetiana are small and easily overlooked in the undergrowth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enchanter's nightshade - another name for Circaea lutetiana

Etymology

From Latin 'Circaea', named after the enchantress Circe in Greek mythology, and 'lutetiana', from 'Lutetia' (ancient Paris).
