Prakritic

1. ADJ

/ prəˈkrɪtɪk / (AmE), / prəˈkrɪtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of Prakrit, an ancient group of Indic languages spoken in India
relating to Prakrit languages

Examples

The inscription was written in a Prakritic script rather than in Sanskrit.
Linguists study Prakritic influences on modern Indian languages.
Ancient texts composed in a Prakritic dialect are important for understanding Buddhist history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Prakrit - any of the ancient Middle Indic languages of India

Etymology

From Sanskrit *Prākṛta* (primitive, natural), referring to the group of ancient Indic languages.
