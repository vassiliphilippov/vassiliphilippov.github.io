religion

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈlɪdʒən / (AmE), / rɪˈlɪdʒən / (BrE)
A strong belief in a supernatural power or powers, especially one or more deities, that influences or determines human destiny.
belief in a supernatural power or deity

Examples

Many people find comfort and hope through religion during difficult times.
Anthropologists study how religion shapes cultures and societies across the world.
Debates about science and religion have been ongoing for centuries.

Synset Examples

he lost his faith but not his morality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faith - Strong belief or trust, especially in a religious context
  • Antonym: atheism - Disbelief or lack of belief in the existence of God or gods

Etymology

From Latin 'religio' (reverence, obligation, religion).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈlɪdʒən / (AmE), / rɪˈlɪdʒən / (BrE)
an organized system of beliefs, ceremonies, and rules used to worship a god or group of gods
institution for worship of divine power

Examples

He was raised in the Baptist religion and attended church every Sunday.
Each religion has its own rituals and practices for worship.
She decided to study world religions in college to learn about their philosophies.

Synset Examples

he was raised in the Baptist religion
a member of his own faith contradicted him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faith - strong belief or trust in a god or in the doctrines of a religion

Etymology

From Latin 'religio' (reverence, obligation, holiness), probably from 'religare' (to bind).
