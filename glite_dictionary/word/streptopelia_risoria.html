Streptopelia risoria

1. NOUN

/ stɹɛp.toʊˈpiː.li.ə rɪˈsɔr.i.ə / (AmE), / stɹɛp.təʊˈpiː.li.ə rɪˈsɔː.rɪ.ə / (BrE)
A greyish Old World turtledove with a black band around the neck, often kept as a cage bird; also known as the Barbary dove.
species of turtledove, Barbary dove

Examples

The Streptopelia risoria is popular among bird enthusiasts for its gentle nature.
A distinctive feature of the Streptopelia risoria is the black collar around its neck.
Many pet stores offer Streptopelia risoria as a common domesticated dove species.

Etymology

From Latinized Greek: *streptos* (twisted, necklace) + *peleia* (dove); *risoria* from Latin 'ridere' (to laugh), referencing the bird's call.
