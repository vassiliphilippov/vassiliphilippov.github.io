fatwah

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæt.wɑ / (AmE), / ˈfæt.wɑː / (BrE)
An official ruling or legal opinion issued by an Islamic scholar on a point of Islamic law.
Islamic legal ruling or opinion

Examples

The cleric issued a fatwah regarding acceptable business practices for Muslims.
A controversial fatwah sparked international debate and protests.
Journalists explained the meaning of a fatwah to readers unfamiliar with Islamic law.

Synset Examples

bin Laden issued three fatwahs calling upon Muslims to take up arms against the United States

Etymology

From Arabic 'fatwā' (legal opinion), adopted into English via Islamic law discourse.
