corroborant

1. ADJ

/ kəˈrɑbəˌrænt / (AmE), / kəˈrɒb(ə)rənt / (BrE)
(of a medicine) having a strengthening or invigorating effect.
medicine that strengthens; invigorating
medical

Examples

The physician prescribed a corroborant tonic to aid the patient's recovery.
Ancient texts often mention corroborant remedies for cases of weakness.
A corroborant medicine can help restore energy after an illness.

Etymology

From Latin corroborans, present participle of corroborare (to strengthen).
