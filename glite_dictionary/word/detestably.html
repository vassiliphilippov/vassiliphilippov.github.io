detestably

1. ADV

/ dɪˈtɛstəbli / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛstəbli / (BrE)
in a way that arouses intense dislike or hatred; in a hateful, offensive manner
in a hateful or offensive manner

Examples

The villain in the novel acted detestably, making readers despise him.
She treated her colleagues detestably and was soon reprimanded for her behavior.
He lied detestably to avoid responsibility, which only made things worse.

Synset Examples

I don't know anyone who could have behaved so abominably

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abominably - in a very unpleasant or disgusting manner
  • Synonym: horribly - in a very unpleasant or shocking way

Etymology

From detestable + -ly, ultimately from Latin 'detestabilis' (to be detested).
