venula

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛn.jə.lə / (AmE), / ˈvɛnjʊlə / (BrE)
A very small vein that continues from a capillary, forming part of the venous blood circulation.
small vein from a capillary
medical

Examples

Oxygen-poor blood moves from capillaries to the venula, beginning its return to the heart.
Microscopic examination revealed inflammation around the venula in the tissue sample.
Each venula collects blood from several capillaries before merging into larger veins.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: vein - a blood vessel that carries blood toward the heart
  • Degree Lower: capillary - a small blood vessel connecting arteries and veins

Etymology

From Latin 'venula', diminutive of 'vena' (vein).
