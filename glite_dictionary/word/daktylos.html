daktylos

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæk.tɪˌloʊs / (AmE), / ˈdæk.tɪ.lɒs / (BrE)
an ancient Greek unit of length, about equal to one centimeter or the breadth of a finger
ancient Greek length unit (≈1 cm)

Examples

The ancient architect used a daktylos as a standard measure in his design.
A daktylos was approximately the width of an adult's finger in Ancient Greece.
Measurements on the old tablet are given in daktyloi rather than modern units.

Synset Examples

In Ancient Greece daktylos was approximately the length of a finger.

Etymology

From Greek δάκτυλος (dáktylos), meaning 'finger'.
