simulcast

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪməlˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈsɪmjʊlˌkɑːst / (BrE)
a broadcast that is transmitted simultaneously on two or more media or channels, such as radio and television, or different radio bands
simultaneous broadcast on multiple channels

Examples

The championship game will be simulcast on both television and radio for wider accessibility.
Our favorite show had a special simulcast event across three channels last night.
Many concerts now offer a live simulcast online for fans who cannot attend in person.

Etymology

Blend of 'simultaneous' and 'broadcast', first used in the mid 20th century.
