Alyssum

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɪsəm / (AmE), / əˈlɪsəm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Brassicaceae, typically with small yellow or white flowers, grown as annuals or perennials.
a genus of flowering plants

Examples

The gardener planted Alyssum along the borders for its delicate fragrance and attractive blooms.
Alyssum thrives in sunny locations and requires little maintenance.
She chose white Alyssum to add contrast to her colorful flower bed.

Etymology

From New Latin Alyssum, from Greek alysson, meaning 'curing madness' (a- “not” + lyssa “madness”).

2. NOUN

/ əˈlɪsəm / (AmE), / əˈlɪsəm / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the cabbage family (Brassicaceae), including annual and perennial herbs or small shrubs with oblong leaves and clusters of yellow or white flowers.
a plant genus of the cabbage family with small yellow or white flowers

Examples

The garden is filled with fragrant Alyssum blooming in neat clusters.
Many gardeners choose Alyssum for its delicate flowers and easy care.
Alyssum can be planted along borders to attract pollinators throughout the summer.

Etymology

From Greek alysson, referring to a plant believed to cure rabies ('a-' without + 'lyssa' rage).
