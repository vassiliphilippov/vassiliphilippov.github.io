alyssum

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɪsəm / (AmE), / əˈlɪsəm / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Alyssum, grown for its clusters of small yellow or white flowers, often used as a garden ornamental.
garden plant with small yellow or white flowers

Examples

The borders of the garden were lined with fragrant alyssum.
She chose alyssum because its white flowers attract bees.
In spring, clusters of alyssum bloom brightly in the flower beds.

Etymology

From New Latin Alyssum, from Greek alysson, a plant reputed to cure madness ('a-' not + 'lyssa' rage, rabies).
