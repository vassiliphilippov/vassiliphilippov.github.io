Myrtales

1. NOUN

/ mɪrˈteɪliːz / (AmE), / mɜːˈteɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of flowering plants that includes families such as Myrtaceae, Combretaceae, and Onagraceae
order of flowering plants

Examples

The Myrtales contain several important tropical and subtropical plants.
Scientists have discovered a new species within the order Myrtales.
Many of the plants classified under Myrtales have distinctive aromatic leaves.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on 'Myrtus' (the myrtle genus) + '-ales' (signifying plant orders).

2. NOUN

/ mɜrˈteɪliːz / (AmE), / mɜːˈteɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of flowering plants that includes families such as Myrtaceae, Combretaceae, and Onagraceae, characterized by aromatic leaves and often woody stems
order of flowering plants (botany)

Examples

The Myrtales order includes a variety of important woody plants, such as eucalyptus and guava.
Evolutionary studies have provided insights into the diversification of the Myrtales.
Botanists often study Myrtales to understand tropical forest ecology.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on the family name Myrtaceae, itself from Greek myrtos (myrtle).
