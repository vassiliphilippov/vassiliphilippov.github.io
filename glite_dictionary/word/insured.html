insured

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈʃʊrd / (AmE), / ɪnˈʃʊəd / (BrE)
having insurance; covered by an insurance policy against loss, damage, or liability.
covered by insurance

Examples

All employees must be insured before starting work on the construction site.
If your car is insured, the company will cover repair costs after an accident.
The artwork was fully insured against theft and damage.

Synset Examples

an insured risk
all members of the film cast and crew are insured

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uninsured - not covered by insurance

Etymology

From the verb 'insure', from Middle French 'ensurer', from Old French 'enseürer'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02104039-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈʃʊrd / (AmE), / ɪnˈʃɔːd / (BrE)
A person or entity whose interests are protected by an insurance policy.
person protected by insurance

Examples

The insured must file a claim within 30 days of the incident.
All insured parties are entitled to compensation as stated in the policy.
The company offers benefits to the insured in case of accidental injury.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: insurer - the company or person who provides insurance

Etymology

From insure (to secure against loss) + -ed (past participle/adjectival suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10229489-n
Synset POS: n
