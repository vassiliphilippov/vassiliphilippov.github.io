clear-sighted

1. ADJ

/ ˈklɪrˌsaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈklɪəˌsaɪtɪd / (BrE)
having sharp, clear vision; able to see things clearly
having clear, sharp vision

Examples

Even in dim light, the clear-sighted hawk spotted its prey far below.
Despite his age, my grandfather remained remarkably clear-sighted and never needed glasses.
The clear-sighted pilot easily identified landmarks from miles away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharp-sighted - having acute vision

2. ADJ

/ ˈklɪrˌsaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈklɪəˌsaɪtɪd / (BrE)
having the ability to think clearly, understand things quickly, and make accurate judgments
mentally acute; able to judge clearly

Examples

Her clear-sighted analysis helped the company avoid serious mistakes.
We need a clear-sighted leader who can handle this crisis calmly.
Despite the pressure, she remained clear-sighted and made the right decision.

Synset Examples

too clear-eyed not to see what problems would follow
chaos could be prevented only by clear-sighted leadership
much too perspicacious to be taken in by so spurious an argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perceptive - having a good understanding of things; quick to notice or realize
  • Synonym: insightful - showing a deep understanding of a situation or problem
  • Synonym: farsighted - able to understand what will be needed in the future
  • Antonym: shortsighted - lacking foresight; not considering the future

Etymology

Formed from 'clear' + 'sighted', influenced by the metaphor of visual clarity representing mental discernment.
