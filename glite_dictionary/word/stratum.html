stratum

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrætəm / (AmE), / ˈstrɑːtəm / (BrE)
a level or class of society consisting of people with the same social, economic, or educational status
social or economic class or level

Examples

The city is divided into different strata based on income and profession.
Access to higher education varies greatly depending on one's social stratum.
She worked hard to rise above the stratum she was born into.

Synset Examples

the working class
an emerging professional class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: class - a group of people sharing the same social or economic status
  • Synonym: caste - a rigid social group, especially in traditional societies

Etymology

From Latin stratum (something spread or laid down, layer), from sternere (to spread).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstreɪt̬əm, ˈstræɾəm / (AmE), / ˈstrɑːtəm / (BrE)
a level or layer within an abstract arrangement, such as in thought, organization, or meaning
an abstract level or layer

Examples

The philosopher explored each stratum of human consciousness in his work.
Poetry often reveals multiple strata of interpretation and meaning.
Her analysis uncovered a hidden stratum of symbolism in the novel.

Synset Examples

a good actor communicates on several levels
a simile has at least two layers of meaning
the mind functions on many strata simultaneously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: layer - a level or sheet of something, often used metaphorically or literally

Etymology

From Latin stratum, meaning 'something spread or laid down'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈstreɪtəm / (AmE), / ˈstrɑːtəm / (BrE)
A single layer of material or substance, especially one of several layers lying one on top of another, such as in rock, soil, or biological tissue.
layer of material or tissue

Examples

The fossils were found in a stratum of limestone dating back millions of years.
Each stratum of the atmosphere has unique properties and functions.
In the heart, a stratum of muscle fibers contracts to pump blood efficiently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: layer - a sheet, quantity, or thickness of material, typically one of several, covering a surface or body
  • Synonym: strata - plural form meaning layers

Etymology

From Latin stratum (something spread or laid down), past participle of sternere (to spread).

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ ˈstreɪ.təm, ˈstrɑː.təm / (AmE), / ˈstrɑː.təm / (BrE)
In statistics, a subgroup of a population defined for the purposes of stratified sampling.
statistical subgroup in sampling

Examples

In stratified sampling, each stratum is sampled independently to ensure representation across key groups.
The researchers divided the population into several strata based on age and income levels.
Selecting a random sample from every stratum helps minimize bias in the results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: layer - a level or sheet of material or people; in other contexts a synonym for stratum

Etymology

From Latin stratum (something spread or laid down).
