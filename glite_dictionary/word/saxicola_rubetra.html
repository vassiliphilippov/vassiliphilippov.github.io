Saxicola rubetra

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæksɪˈkoʊlə ruːˈbiːtrə / (AmE), / ˌsæksɪˈkəʊlə ruːˈbiːtrə / (BrE)
A small brown-and-buff European songbird of grassy meadows, also known as the whinchat.
European songbird of grassy meadows

Examples

While hiking through the meadow, we spotted a Saxicola rubetra perched on a fence post.
The Saxicola rubetra is easily recognized by its distinctive buff and brown plumage.
Many birdwatchers travel to observe the Saxicola rubetra during its migration season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whinchat - another common name for Saxicola rubetra

Etymology

From Latin: 'saxicola' meaning 'rock-dweller' and 'rubetra' referring to the whinchat.
