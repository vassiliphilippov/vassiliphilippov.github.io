preexisting

1. ADJ

/ ˌpriːɪɡˈzɪstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌpriːɪɡˈzɪstɪŋ / (BrE)
existing previously or before something else occurs or is introduced
existing before something else

Examples

The company was unable to use the land due to preexisting environmental restrictions.
Patients with preexisting conditions may require special medical coverage.
We built the extension onto the preexisting structure without demolishing any walls.

Synset Examples

variations on pre-existent musical themes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preexistent - existing beforehand or previously; alternate spelling or form
  • Antonym: new - not existing before; recently created or introduced

Etymology

From 'pre-' (before) + 'existing', from Latin 'existere' (to exist).
