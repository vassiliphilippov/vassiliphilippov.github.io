border

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔrdɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɔːdə / (BrE)
a strip forming the outer edge of something, often as a decoration or boundary
outer edge strip; decorative edge

Examples

The rug has a beautiful red border along its edges.
She planted flowers along the border of the walkway.
The painting is framed by a golden border that makes the colors stand out.

Synset Examples

the rug had a wide blue border

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edge - the outermost part or boundary of an object
  • Synonym: margin - the blank border or edge of a page or surface

Etymology

From Old French 'bordure', from 'border' (to border), from Frankish 'bord' (edge, board).
