phosphatase

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑsfəˌteɪs / (AmE), / ˈfɒsfəteɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that catalyzes the hydrolysis of organic phosphates by removing a phosphate group
enzyme that removes phosphate from compounds
medical

Examples

Alkaline phosphatase levels in the blood can indicate liver or bone disorders.
The reaction required the presence of a specific phosphatase to proceed efficiently.
Scientists studied the role of protein phosphatase in cellular signaling pathways.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: kinase - an enzyme that adds phosphate groups to other molecules

Etymology

From phosphate + -ase (enzyme suffix), first used in late 19th century scientific English.
