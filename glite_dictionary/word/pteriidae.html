Pteriidae

1. NOUN

/ tɪˈraɪ.ɪˌdiː / (AmE), / tɪˈraɪ.ɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine bivalve mollusks, including pearl and feather oysters.
family of pearl and feather oysters

Examples

The Pteriidae family is well known for producing pearls of high quality.
Marine biologists have discovered new species within the Pteriidae in tropical waters.
Oysters in the Pteriidae group often attach themselves to rocks or coral reefs.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name Pteria, from Greek pteron (wing, feather).

2. NOUN

/ ˌtɪər.iˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌtɪə.rɪˈiː.daɪ / (BrE)
a family of marine bivalve mollusks commonly known as pearl oysters or feather oysters, including species valued for pearls
family of pearl oysters (feather oysters)

Examples

Several species within the Pteriidae family are cultivated for their ability to produce pearls.
The Pteriidae are sometimes referred to as feather oysters because of the shape of their shells.
Researchers are studying the genetics of Pteriidae to improve pearl farming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pearl oyster - a marine bivalve mollusk that produces pearls

Etymology

From the genus name *Pteria* + taxonomic suffix -idae (from Greek -ίδαι), used in zoological classifications.
