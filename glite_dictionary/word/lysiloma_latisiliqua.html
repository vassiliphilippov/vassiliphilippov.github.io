Lysiloma latisiliqua

1. NOUN

/ ˌlaɪ.sɪˈloʊ.mə ˌlæt.ɪˈsɪl.ɪ.kwə / (AmE), / ˌlaɪ.sɪˈləʊ.mə ˌlæt.ɪˈsɪl.ɪ.kwə / (BrE)
a tree native to the West Indies, Florida, and Mexico, resembling tamarind and bearing long, flat pods
a West Indian and Mexican tree with flat pods

Examples

The Lysiloma latisiliqua tree produces long, flat pods that hang from its branches.
You can find Lysiloma latisiliqua growing naturally along the coastlines of Florida and the West Indies.
Local carpenters sometimes use wood from Lysiloma latisiliqua for making furniture.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name Lysiloma, combined with Latin latisiliqua, meaning 'broad pod'.
