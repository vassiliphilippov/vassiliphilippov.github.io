cabin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæb.ɪn / (AmE), / ˈkab.ɪn / (BrE)
the enclosed section of an aircraft or spacecraft in which passengers are carried
passenger area in aircraft or spacecraft

Examples

Passengers were asked to return to their cabin and fasten their seat belts.
The crew checked the pressure levels throughout the spacecraft cabin.
She walked down the aisle of the airplane, looking for her seat in the cabin.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cockpit - the area of an aircraft where the pilot sits and controls the plane
  • Synonym: compartment - a separate section or part of a vehicle

Etymology

From Middle French *cabine*, from Old Italian *cabina* (small room).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæb.ɪn / (AmE), / ˈkæb.ɪn / (BrE)
A small, simple house, usually built of wood and located in a rural or forested area.
small wooden house in a rural area

Examples

They spent the weekend relaxing in a cozy cabin deep in the woods.
The old cabin had a stone fireplace and simple furniture.
We rented a cabin by the lake for our family vacation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hut - a small, simple shelter

Etymology

From Middle French *cabane*, from Old Provençal *cabana*, from Late Latin *capanna* (hut).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02935784-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈkæbɪn / (AmE), / ˈkabɪn / (BrE)
a small room on a ship or boat where people sleep
small sleeping room on a ship or boat

Examples

After a long day on deck, the sailors returned to their cabin to rest.
We booked a private cabin on the cruise ship for our vacation.
The stormy night made everyone grateful for the warmth of their cabin.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: stateroom - a private room on a ship, usually more spacious and luxurious than a cabin
  • Synonym: berth - a fixed bunk or bed on a ship, train, or other transport
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02935611-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈkæb.ɪn / (AmE), / ˈkæb.ɪn / (BrE)
To confine or restrict (someone) to a small space or area, especially one as limited as a cabin.
confine to a small space
archaic

Examples

The children were cabined in a tiny room during the storm.
She felt cabined and constrained by the circumstances of her life.
He had been cabined so long that he longed for open spaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confine - to restrict someone or something within limits
  • Antonym: free - to allow someone freedom of movement or action; not confined
