Acer saccharum

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪsər səˈkɛərəm / (AmE), / ˈeɪsə sæˈkɑːrəm / (BrE)
the sugar maple; a North American tree with lobed leaves, valued for its hardwood and as the main source of maple syrup and sugar
the sugar maple tree

Examples

The dense forests of Vermont are known for the abundance of Acer saccharum, or sugar maple, trees.
Each spring, farmers tap Acer saccharum to collect the sap used in making maple syrup.
The hard wood of Acer saccharum is prized by furniture makers for its durability and attractive grain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sugar maple - common name for Acer saccharum, the North American tree that produces maple syrup

Etymology

From Latin, with *acer* meaning 'maple' and *saccharum* meaning 'sugar'.
