Komodo dragon

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmoʊdoʊ ˈdræɡən / (AmE), / kəˈməʊdəʊ ˈdræɡən / (BrE)
a very large species of lizard native to several Indonesian islands, recognized as the largest living lizard in the world
large Indonesian lizard; world's largest lizard

Examples

The Komodo dragon is capable of taking down prey much larger than itself.
Many tourists visit Indonesia hoping to catch a glimpse of a wild Komodo dragon.
Despite its fearsome reputation, the Komodo dragon is a vulnerable species threatened by habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monitor lizard - large lizards of the genus Varanus, to which the Komodo dragon belongs

Etymology

From Komodo, the name of the main island where it is found, and 'dragon' due to its size and appearance.
