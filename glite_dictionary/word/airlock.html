airlock

1. NOUN

/ ˈer.lɑk / (AmE), / ˈeə.lɒk / (BrE)
a sealed chamber with controlled air pressure that allows entry to or from environments with different pressures, such as in spacecraft or pressurized buildings
chamber allowing safe entry to pressurized space

Examples

The astronauts entered the airlock before stepping outside the space station.
You must wait for the airlock to pressurize before opening the inner door.
The submarine crew used the airlock to transition between internal and external environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decompression chamber - a chamber used to relieve or adjust pressure, especially for divers or astronauts

Etymology

From 'air' + 'lock', referring to a sealed compartment for air regulation.
