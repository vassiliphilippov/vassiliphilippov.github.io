masterpiece

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæstərˌpis / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstəpiːs / (BrE)
A work of art, literature, or skill that is considered outstanding or the best of its kind.
outstanding work of art or achievement

Examples

The novel is widely regarded as the author's masterpiece and has influenced generations of readers.
The painting was considered a masterpiece of Renaissance art and drew crowds from around the world.
Her performance in the ballet was a masterpiece of grace and technical precision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classic - a work judged to have lasting worth or significance
  • Antonym: failure - the opposite of a great achievement or success

Etymology

From Middle French *maistrepiece*, from *maistre* (master) + *piece* (piece).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæstɚˌpis / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstəpiːs / (BrE)
An outstanding work of art, craft, or skill; the best or most important work produced by an artist, writer, or craftsman.
the best work by an artist or craftsman

Examples

Many critics consider Leonardo da Vinci's 'Mona Lisa' to be his greatest masterpiece.
The director's latest film is a true masterpiece of storytelling and cinematography.
Each sculpture in the gallery is unique, but this one stands out as the artist's undisputed masterpiece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tour de force - an impressive achievement or performance; a feat of great skill
  • Antonym: failure - lack of success in achieving or creating something

Etymology

From Dutch *meesterstuk* or German *Meisterstück* (master’s work), originally referring to a craftsman's best work to qualify for guild membership.
