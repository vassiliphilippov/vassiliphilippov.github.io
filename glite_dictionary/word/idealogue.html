idealogue

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪdiəˌlɔg / (AmE), / ˈaɪdɪəlɒɡ / (BrE)
a person who develops or advocates theories, especially in science or art
person who theorizes, esp. in science or art

Examples

As an idealogue, she often challenged others to rethink core scientific assumptions.
Many artists view themselves as idealogues, shaping movements through new ideas.
The book was written by a prominent idealogue in the field of modern aesthetics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theorist - a person who forms theories about something

Etymology

From 'idea' and the Greek-derived suffix '-logue' (speaker or student of).
