angularity

1. NOUN

/ ˌæŋɡjəˈlɛrəti / (AmE), / ˌæŋɡjʊˈlarɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of having angles or sharp corners; the degree to which something is angular
the state of having angles or sharp corners

Examples

The angularity of the sculpture made it stand out among the smoother pieces in the gallery.
Architects admired the bold angularity of the new building's design.
Her face had a striking angularity that was often highlighted in photographs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: roundness - the quality of being round or curved
  • Synonym: angular - having angles or sharp corners

Etymology

From 'angular' + '-ity', with roots in Latin 'angulus' (angle).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæŋɡjəˈlɛrəti / (AmE), / ˌæŋɡjʊˈlarɪti / (BrE)
The quality of having sharp angles or distinct corners, or a shape characterized by one or more sharp angles.
quality of having sharp angles

Examples

The angularity of the sculpture gave it a modern, striking appearance.
Her face had a certain angularity that made her look both elegant and severe.
The building's angularity contrasts with the smooth lines of the park below.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: roundness - the quality of being round or curved, without sharp angles

Etymology

From Latin 'angulāritās,' from 'angulus' (angle).
