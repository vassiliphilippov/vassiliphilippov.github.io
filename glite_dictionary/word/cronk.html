to cronk

1. VERB

/ krɑŋk / (AmE), / krɒŋk / (BrE)
(of a goose) to make a loud, harsh cry; to honk or call like a goose.
cry like a goose; honk

Examples

In the early morning, the geese cronk loudly as they fly overhead.
The farm was filled with the sound of birds cronking throughout the marsh.
We heard several geese cronk as they settled near the pond.

Synset Examples

The geese were honking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honk - to make a loud, harsh sound like a goose

Etymology

Imitative of the harsh cry of a goose.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01055211-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ krɑŋk / (AmE), / krɒŋk / (BrE)
(of a bird, especially a raven) to utter a hoarse, croaking sound.
utter a hoarse croaking sound

Examples

In the early morning, ravens cronk loudly from the treetops.
The old crow cronked as it settled on the fence post.
You could hear the herons cronking across the misty marshland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: croak - to make a deep, harsh sound like a frog or raven
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01066423-v
Synset POS: v
