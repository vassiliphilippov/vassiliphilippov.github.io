postponement

1. NOUN

/ poʊstˈpoʊnmənt / (AmE), / pəʊstˈpəʊnmənt / (BrE)
the action or period of delaying something; a time when something expected is made to happen later than planned
the action or time of delaying

Examples

The unexpected rain led to a postponement of the football match.
Due to technical problems, the meeting experienced yet another postponement.
The concert's postponement disappointed many eager fans.

Synset Examples

instant replay caused too long a delay
he ordered a hold in the action

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delay - the period of causing something to happen later than planned

Etymology

From postpone + -ment, based on Latin ponere (to place).

2. NOUN

/ poʊstˈpoʊnmənt / (AmE), / pəʊstˈpəʊnmənt / (BrE)
The act of delaying something or arranging for it to take place at a later time than originally planned.
the act of delaying something

Examples

The meeting was subject to postponement due to bad weather conditions.
Any postponement of the court case will inconvenience the witnesses.
Her request for a postponement was granted, so the hearing will be next week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delay - a period of time by which something is late or postponed
  • Antonym: advancement - the act of bringing something forward to an earlier time

Etymology

From postpone + -ment; 'postpone' from Latin postponere (put after).
