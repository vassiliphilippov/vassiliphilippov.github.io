hypothalamic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪpoʊθəˈlæmɪk / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəʊθəˈlamɪk / (BrE)
relating to or affecting the hypothalamus, a region at the base of the brain that controls various bodily functions
relating to the hypothalamus of the brain
medical

Examples

The hypothalamic region controls hunger, thirst, and body temperature.
Researchers studied the effects of stress on hypothalamic function in laboratory rats.
Damage to hypothalamic neurons can disrupt the body's hormonal balance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypothalamus - region of the brain controlling bodily functions

Etymology

From hypothalamus + -ic, forming an adjective meaning 'of the hypothalamus'.
