chandelle

1. NOUN

/ ʃænˈdɛl / (AmE), / ʃanˈdɛl / (BrE)
an aircraft maneuver in which a sharp climbing turn is made to gain altitude while changing direction
aerial climbing turn maneuver

Examples

The pilot performed a perfect chandelle during the air show, impressing the crowd.
Learning how to execute a chandelle is a requirement for advanced pilot training.
She used a sharp chandelle to evade the incoming aircraft.

Etymology

From French 'chandelle' meaning 'candle', referring to the steep upward path of the maneuver.

2. VERB

/ ʃænˈdɛl / (AmE), / ʃænˈdɛl / (BrE)
(of an aircraft) to climb suddenly and steeply by executing a special turning maneuver.
suddenly climb steeply (aviation)

Examples

The pilot pulled back on the stick to chandelle over the trees.
In an emergency, an aircraft may chandelle to gain altitude quickly.
The flight instructor demonstrated how to chandelle during the advanced maneuvers session.

Synset Examples

The airplane chandelled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: climb - to move upward, especially in the context of flight
  • Antonym: to descend - to move or go downward

Etymology

From French 'chandelle' meaning 'candle'; in aviation, likening the aircraft's rapid ascent to a candle's upright position.
