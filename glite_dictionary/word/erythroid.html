erythroid

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈrɪθ.rɔɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈrɪθ.rɔɪd / (BrE)
relating to erythrocytes (red blood cells) or their precursors
relating to red blood cells
medical

Examples

The erythroid lineage gives rise to mature red blood cells in the bone marrow.
A deficiency in erythroid cells can lead to anemia and decreased oxygen transport.
Scientists studied erythroid development to better understand blood disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erythrocytic - relating to erythrocytes (red blood cells)

Etymology

From Greek 'erythros' (red) + '-oid' (form, resemblance).
