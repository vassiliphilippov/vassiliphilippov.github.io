monotony

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɑːtəni / (AmE), / məˈnɒtəni / (BrE)
the quality or state of being dull and unvarying, especially as a result of repetition or lack of variety
lack of variety; tedious sameness

Examples

After a few weeks of traveling, the monotony of hotel rooms started to bother him.
He tries to break the monotony of his daily routine by taking a new route to work.
The teacher introduced games and activities to prevent monotony in the classroom.

Synset Examples

he had never grown accustomed to the monotony of his work
he was sick of the humdrum of his fellow prisoners
he hated the sameness of the food the college served

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boredom - the state of being bored or uninterested
  • Antonym: variety - the quality of being different or diverse

Etymology

From Greek monotonia, from monos (single) + tonos (tone).

2. NOUN

/ məˈnɑːtəni / (AmE), / məˈnɒtəni / (BrE)
lack of variation in tone, pitch, or inflection, resulting in a dull or unchanging quality
lack of variation in tone or pitch

Examples

The professor's monotony made it difficult for students to stay engaged during the lecture.
She tried to add excitement to her performance to avoid the monotony of singing in the same tone.
The monotony in his voice suggested he was not interested in the conversation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: variety - the quality or condition of being diverse or different

Etymology

From Greek monotonia, from monos (single) and tonos (tone).
