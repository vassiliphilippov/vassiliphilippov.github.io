achlamydeous

1. ADJ

/ əˌklæmɪˈdiːəs / (AmE), / əˌklamɪˈdiːəs / (BrE)
(Botany) Lacking a perianth or floral envelope.
lacking a perianth (botany)
medical

Examples

Some primitive flowering plants are classified as achlamydeous because they lack a true perianth.
Botanists often describe flowers without petals or sepals as achlamydeous.
The identification key noted whether the specimen was achlamydeous or possessed a distinct floral envelope.

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' meaning 'without' and 'chlamys' meaning 'cloak', referring to the absence of a floral covering.
