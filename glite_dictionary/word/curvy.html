curvy

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɝː.vi / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.vi / (BrE)
(of a woman's body) attractively having rounded shapes, especially with a full bust and hips.
having attractive curves (of a woman)

Examples

Many fashion brands now celebrate curvy models on their runways.
She felt more confident after seeing other curvy women in the magazine.
The movie star was known for her curvy figure and glamorous style.

Synset Examples

Hollywood seems full of curvaceous blondes
a curvy young woman in a tight dress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curvaceous - (of a woman) having an attractively curvy figure

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɜːr.vi / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.vi / (BrE)
having curves; smoothly rounded or shaped with several curves.
having curves; smoothly rounded

Examples

The new sofa has a very curvy design that adds elegance to the living room.
Many people admire artwork that features curvy shapes and flowing lines.
She wore a dress that accentuated her curvy figure.

Synset Examples

a settee with only one curvy end

Related Terms

  • Antonym: straight - having no curves or bends

3. ADJ

/ ˈkɝː.vi / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.vi / (BrE)
having many curves or rounded parts; forming or marked by curves
having many curves

Examples

The new sofa has a beautiful curvy design that stands out in the living room.
She prefers to wear dresses that emphasize her curvy figure.
The road became increasingly curvy as we climbed higher into the mountains.

Synset Examples

a settee with only one curvy end

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curved - having a smoothly bending shape
  • Antonym: straight - having no curves or bends

Etymology

From 'curve' + -y, meaning 'full of curves'.
