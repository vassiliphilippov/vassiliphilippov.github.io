to reshoot

1. VERB

/ riˈʃut / (AmE), / ˌriːˈʃuːt / (BrE)
to film, photograph, or shoot again, typically in order to correct or improve the original
to shoot again, often in film or photography

Examples

The director decided to reshoot several scenes to improve the movie's ending.
We had to reshoot the photo because the lighting was off the first time.
After reviewing the footage, the team agreed to reshoot the entire interview.

Synset Examples

We had to reshoot that scene 24 times

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retake - to film or record a scene again

Etymology

Formed with the prefix 're-' (again) + 'shoot'.
