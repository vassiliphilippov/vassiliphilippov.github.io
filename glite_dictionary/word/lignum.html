lignum

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪɡ.nəm / (AmE), / ˈlɪɡ.nəm / (BrE)
the woody tissue of a plant, particularly that which makes up the main structural support in stems and trunks
woody tissue in plants

Examples

The craftsman selected a piece of lignum for its durability and rich color.
Certain insects bore into the lignum of trees, causing structural damage over time.
The scientist examined the lignum under a microscope to study the arrangement of its fibers.

Etymology

From Latin 'lignum' meaning 'wood'.
