Elaeis oleifera

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈleɪ.ɪs oʊ.liˈɛf.ə.rə / (AmE), / ɪˈleɪ.ɪs ɒ.lɪˈɛf.ərə / (BrE)
a species of palm native to Central and South America, known for its oil-rich fruit
Central/South American oil palm species

Examples

The fruit of Elaeis oleifera is a source of edible oil in some regions of Central America.
Researchers are studying Elaeis oleifera to improve disease resistance in commercial oil palm hybrids.
Unlike the African oil palm, Elaeis oleifera is less widely cultivated but has unique properties valued by breeders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oil palm - tropical palm trees yielding oil from their fruit (especially Elaeis guineensis and Elaeis oleifera)

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Elaeis' from Greek 'elaia' (olive), species epithet 'oleifera' meaning 'oil-bearing'.
