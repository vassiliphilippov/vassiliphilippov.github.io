decalcomania

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛlkəˈmeɪniə / (AmE), / ˌdiːkælˌkəʊˈmeɪniə / (BrE)
A technique for transferring designs or pictures from specially prepared paper to another surface, or the design itself once transferred.
transfer picture or process

Examples

The artist used decalcomania to create unique, abstract textures on his canvases.
I bought a decalcomania of flowers to decorate my notebook cover.
Surrealists often experimented with decalcomania to generate spontaneous art forms.

Etymology

From French décalcomanie, from décalquer (to transfer tracing) + manie (mania, craze).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛˌkælkəˈmeɪniə / (AmE), / ˌdiːˌkælkəˈmeɪniə / (BrE)
the process of transferring a design from specially prepared paper onto another surface such as wood, glass, or metal
artistic design transfer method

Examples

She created a series of intricate pieces using decalcomania on glass panels.
The artist demonstrated the decalcomania technique to her students during the workshop.
Many surrealists experimented with decalcomania to generate spontaneous textures and patterns.

Etymology

From French décalcomanie, from décalquer 'to transfer (a tracing)' and manie 'mania or craze'.
