brainwashing

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪnwɑʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbreɪnwɒʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of forcibly changing a person's beliefs or attitudes, often through pressure, manipulation, or indoctrination
forcible indoctrination of beliefs or attitudes

Examples

Some cults use brainwashing techniques to control their members' thoughts and behaviors.
He claimed he was a victim of brainwashing during his captivity.
The documentary explores the role of brainwashing in totalitarian regimes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indoctrination - the process of teaching a person or group to accept a set of beliefs uncritically
  • Synonym: propaganda - information, especially of a biased or misleading nature, used to promote a political cause or point of view

Etymology

Formed in the early 1950s from 'brain' + 'washing', influenced by the Chinese term 'xǐ năo' (wash brain).
