benthal

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɛnθəl / (AmE), / ˈbɛnθəl / (BrE)
relating to or occurring on the bottom of a body of water, such as the ocean, a lake, or a river
relating to the floor of a body of water

Examples

The benthal fauna of the lake is remarkably diverse.
Scientists are studying the benthal sediments for evidence of pollution.
Changes in temperature can affect benthal ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benthic - relating to the lowest ecological zone of a body of water

Etymology

From Greek *benthos* (depth of the sea).
