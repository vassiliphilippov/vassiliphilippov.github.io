tibia

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪbiə / (AmE), / ˈtɪbiə / (BrE)
the inner and larger of the two bones in the lower leg, extending from the knee to the ankle
inner, larger bone of lower leg
medical

Examples

The footballer suffered a fracture in his tibia after the collision.
An X-ray confirmed that the tibia had not been displaced by the injury.
Pain in the tibia is often experienced by runners with shin splints.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fibula - the outer and thinner of the two bones of the lower leg

Etymology

From Latin *tibia* (shinbone, flute).
