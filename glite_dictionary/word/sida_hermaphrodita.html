Sida hermaphrodita

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪdə hɜːrmæfrəˈdaɪtə / (AmE), / ˌsiːdə hɜːˌmæfrəˈdaɪtə / (BrE)
a tall perennial herb native to the southeastern United States, with maple-like leaves and white flowers, commonly known as Virginia mallow
a North American perennial herb (Virginia mallow)

Examples

The garden's wet corner was perfect for growing Sida hermaphrodita among native perennials.
Researchers are studying Sida hermaphrodita for its potential use in phytoremediation of contaminated soils.
During late summer, the white flowers of Sida hermaphrodita attract many pollinators to the meadow.

Etymology

From Latin: Sida (a plant genus) and hermaphrodita (hermaphrodite, referring to both male and female reproductive organs).
