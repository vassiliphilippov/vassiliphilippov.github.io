Mandragora officinarum

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæn.drəˈɡɔːrə ˌɒ.fɪ.sɪˈnɛrəm / (AmE), / ˌmæn.drəˈɡɔː.rə ˌɒ.fɪ.sɪˈnɑː.rəm / (BrE)
a perennial plant native to southern Europe and North Africa, with purple flowers, yellow fruit, and a forked root, historically believed to possess magical and medicinal properties
a plant with forked root, known as mandrake

Examples

In ancient times, Mandragora officinarum was thought to have mystical powers and was used in various rituals.
The forked root of Mandragora officinarum often resembles a human figure, leading to many legends.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties attributed to Mandragora officinarum in traditional herbal remedies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mandrake - common name for Mandragora officinarum

Etymology

From Latin 'mandragora' (mandrake), ultimately from Greek 'mandragoras'.
