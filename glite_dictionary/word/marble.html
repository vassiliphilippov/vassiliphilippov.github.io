marble

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrbəl / (AmE), / ˈmɑːbəl / (BrE)
a small, round ball made of glass, used by children in various games
small glass ball used in games

Examples

His favorite marble was blue with white swirls inside it.
They played a game of marble in the schoolyard after lunch.
She accidentally stepped on a marble and nearly lost her balance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taw - a large playing marble used to shoot at other marbles

Etymology

From Middle English 'marbel', from Old French 'marbre', from Latin 'marmor' (marble) – originally referring to the material, later to the small ball made from it or glass.

2. VERB

/ ˈmɑrbəl / (AmE), / ˈmɑːbəl / (BrE)
to paint or stain something so that it resembles the veined appearance of marble
to make something look like marble

Examples

The artist decided to marble the paper for a unique background effect.
She learned how to marble fabric using traditional dyeing techniques.
Bookbinders often marble the endpapers to add a decorative touch.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to marbleize - to make something look like marble, especially by painting or staining

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrbəl / (AmE), / ˈmɑːbəl / (BrE)
A hard, crystalline metamorphic rock that can be polished to a high sheen, commonly used for sculpture and as a building material.
hard, polished rock used in sculpture/building

Examples

The statue was carved from a single block of marble and stands in the museum.
The elegant floors of the grand hotel were made of polished marble.
She admired the marble columns that lined the ancient temple.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: granite - a very hard, granular, crystalline igneous rock used as a building stone
  • Degree Lower: limestone - a sedimentary rock mainly composed of calcium carbonate

Etymology

From Old French 'marbre' and Latin 'marmor', from Greek 'mármaros' meaning shining stone.

4. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːrbəl / (AmE), / ˈmɑːbəl / (BrE)
A sculpture made by carving marble, typically representing a human figure or other artistic subject.
sculpture carved from marble

Examples

The museum features a famous marble of Athena at its entrance.
An exquisite marble by Michelangelo draws crowds from around the world.
They restored the ancient marble after decades of neglect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: statue - a three-dimensional work of art, usually of a person or animal

Etymology

From Old French 'marbre', from Latin 'marmor', from Greek 'mármaros' (shining stone).
