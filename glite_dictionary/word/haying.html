haying

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈheɪ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of cutting, drying, and collecting grass or other plants to make hay
the harvesting of hay

Examples

The farmers started haying early in the morning to avoid the afternoon heat.
During haying season, everyone in the village lends a hand in the fields.
Wet weather can delay haying and reduce the quality of the crop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harvesting - the act or process of gathering a crop

Etymology

From 'hay' (Old English 'hēg', dried grass) + '-ing' (forming nouns denoting actions or results).

2. NOUN

/ ˈheɪ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈheɪ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or season of cutting, drying, and storing grass to be used as fodder for animals
season/process of making hay

Examples

Haying usually takes place in early summer when the grass is at its peak.
The whole family helped out during haying each year on the farm.
Good weather is crucial for successful haying, as rain can ruin the drying grass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haymaking - the process of making hay

Etymology

From 'hay' (Old English 'hēg') + suffix '-ing', denoting an action or process.
