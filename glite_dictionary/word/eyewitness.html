eyewitness

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪˌwɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈaɪˌwɪtnəs / (BrE)
a person who has personally seen an event, especially a crime or accident, and can describe what happened
person who has seen an event firsthand

Examples

The police interviewed an eyewitness to confirm the details of the accident.
As an eyewitness, she gave a detailed account of the robbery in court.
The journalist spoke to an eyewitness who saw the fire break out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witness - a person who sees an event and may testify about it

Etymology

From Middle English 'eye' + 'witness', combining sense of observation with testifying.

2. VERB

/ ˈaɪˌwɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈaɪˌwɪtnəs / (BrE)
to witness an event by being physically present and seeing it with one's own eyes
to personally see an event

Examples

He claimed to have eyewitnessed the accident from start to finish.
Several tourists eyewitnessed the historic event and later shared their accounts.
She didn't just hear about the fire; she actually eyewitnessed it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witness - to see an event happen, especially a crime or accident

Etymology

Formed from the noun 'eyewitness,' itself from 'eye' + 'witness.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02132979-v
Synset POS: v
