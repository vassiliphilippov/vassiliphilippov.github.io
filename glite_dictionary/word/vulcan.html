Vulcan

1. PROPN

/ ˈvʌl.kən / (AmE), / ˈvʌl.kən / (BrE)
In Roman mythology, the god of fire, metalworking, and craftsmanship, equivalent to the Greek god Hephaestus.
Roman god of fire and metalworking

Examples

According to legend, Vulcan forged weapons for the gods in his fiery workshop.
The temple of Vulcan was located near Rome, close to a volcanic crater.
Many sculptures of Vulcan depict him holding a hammer and anvil, symbolizing his craft.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hephaestus - Greek god of fire and metalworking; counterpart of Vulcan

Etymology

From Latin 'Vulcanus', the Roman god of fire.
