commercialised

1. ADJ

/ kəˈmɝː.ʃə.laɪzd / (AmE), / kəˈmɜː.ʃə.laɪzd / (BrE)
Organized primarily to make money rather than for other purposes.
oriented for profit-making

Examples

Many people feel that the Olympics have become too commercialised in recent decades.
Some artists prefer to avoid commercialised galleries to preserve their creative freedom.
The festival is now so commercialised that it's lost much of its original charm.

Synset Examples

Christmas has become a commercialized spectacle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profit-oriented - focused mainly on making money
  • Antonym: noncommercial - not intended to make a profit

Etymology

From 'commercial' + '-ise' (verb-forming suffix) + '-ed' (past participle/adjective).
