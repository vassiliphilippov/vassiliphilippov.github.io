to bespeak

1. VERB

/ bɪˈspiːk / (AmE), / bɪˈspiːk / (BrE)
to be a sign of or indicate something; to show evidence of a quality or condition
to be a sign or indicate

Examples

The elegant handwriting bespeaks a careful and educated writer.
His calm demeanor bespoke great confidence in the situation.
The dilapidated condition of the house bespeaks years of neglect.

Synset Examples

These symptoms indicate a serious illness
Her behavior points to a severe neurosis
The economic indicators signal that the euro is undervalued

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to indicate - to show, point out, or be a sign of
  • Synonym: signal - to be an indication or sign of something
  • Antonym: to conceal - to hide or keep from being seen or known

Etymology

From Old English 'bespecan', meaning 'to speak, address'.
