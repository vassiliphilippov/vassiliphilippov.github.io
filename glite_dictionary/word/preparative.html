preparative

1. ADJ

/ prɪˈpɛr.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / prɪˈpær.ə.tɪv / (BrE)
serving to prepare for something; done in advance to make ready for a later event or process
serving as preparation for something

Examples

Careful preparative work was required before the experiment could begin.
They discussed their preparative measures to ensure the event would run smoothly.
The chef's preparative tasks included chopping vegetables and marinating meat.

Synset Examples

preparatory steps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preparatory - serving as or carrying out preparation for a task or event
  • Antonym: final - coming at the end; serving as a conclusion

Etymology

From Latin praeparativus, from praeparare (to prepare).
