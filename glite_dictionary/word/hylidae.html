Hylidae

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈlɪdiː / (AmE), / hɪˈlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of frogs commonly known as tree frogs, characterized by their arboreal habits and adhesive toe pads.
family of tree frogs

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species in the Hylidae family in South America.
Members of the Hylidae are known for their climbing abilities and distinctive toe pads.
Rainforests provide a crucial habitat for many species within the Hylidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree frog - any frog in the family Hylidae

Etymology

From New Latin Hylidae, based on Hyla (a type genus of the family).
