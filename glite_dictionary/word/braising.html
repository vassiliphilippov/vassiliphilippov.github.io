braising

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbreɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
A method of cooking food slowly in a small amount of liquid in a covered pot, often after first browning it in fat.
slow cooking in liquid and fat

Examples

The chef recommends braising the beef to make it tender and flavorful.
Proper braising requires patience and a heavy, tightly sealed pot.
By braising the vegetables, she brought out their natural sweetness.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: simmering - cooking in liquid just below boiling point
  • Synonym: stewing - cooking slowly in liquid for a long time
  • Antonym: roasting - cooking food by dry heat in an oven or over fire

Etymology

From French *braiser* (to stew), related to *braise* (hot coals).
