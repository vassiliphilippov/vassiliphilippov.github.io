tongueless

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʌŋ.ləs / (AmE), / ˈtʌŋ.lɪs / (BrE)
(literary) Expressed or existing without spoken words; silent or unspoken.
unspoken; silent; without speech

Examples

The artist's tongueless plea for peace resonated with many viewers.
She gave him a tongueless look of sympathy across the crowded room.
Some grief is too deep for words, remaining tongueless and hidden.

Synset Examples

a mute appeal
a silent curse
best grief is tongueless
the words stopped at her lips unsounded
unspoken grief
choking exasperation and wordless shame

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silent - not making or accompanied by any sound
  • Synonym: wordless - not expressed in words; unspoken
  • Antonym: spoken - expressed in speech; uttered aloud

Etymology

From tongue + -less, meaning 'without a tongue', used figuratively.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtʌŋləs / (AmE), / ˈtʌŋləs / (BrE)
lacking a tongue; having no tongue
lacking a tongue

Examples

The tongueless lizard adapted by using other parts of its mouth to catch prey.
He wore tongueless shoes to make them easier to slip on and off.
The legend spoke of a tongueless ghost haunting the old castle.

Synset Examples

tongueless moccasins

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tongued - having a tongue; possessing a tongue

Etymology

Formed from 'tongue' + suffix '-less' indicating absence.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02439471-a
Synset POS: a
