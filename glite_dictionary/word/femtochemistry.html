femtochemistry

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛmtoʊˈkɛmɪstri / (AmE), / ˌfɛm təʊˈkɛmɪstri / (BrE)
the branch of chemistry that studies extremely rapid chemical reactions, especially those occurring within a few femtoseconds, often using femtosecond laser pulses
the study of ultrafast chemical reactions
medical

Examples

Ahmed Zewail received the Nobel Prize for his pioneering work in femtochemistry.
Scientists use lasers to observe molecular changes during femtochemistry experiments.
Understanding reaction mechanisms at the atomic level is a main goal of femtochemistry.

Etymology

From 'femto-' (meaning one quadrillionth) and 'chemistry'.
