Larix siberica

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑrɪks saɪˈbɪrɪkə / (AmE), / ˈlɑːrɪks sʌɪˈbɪərɪkə / (BrE)
A species of larch tree native to northeastern Russia and Siberia, known for its narrowly conical crown and soft, narrow, bright-green leaves, commonly cultivated for ornamental and forestry purposes.
Siberian larch tree species

Examples

The Larix siberica is prized in landscaping for its distinctive foliage and resilience to cold climates.
Many forests in Siberia are dominated by the Larix siberica, adapting well to the harsh environment.
Botanists have studied the growth patterns of Larix siberica to understand its ecological importance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: larch - any tree of the genus Larix

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Larix' (larch) and Latin 'sīberica' meaning 'of Siberia'.
