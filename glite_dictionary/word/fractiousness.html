fractiousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfræk.ʃəs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfræk.ʃəs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being irritable, unruly, or difficult to control or manage
tendency to be irritable and difficult to control

Examples

The fractiousness of the class made it difficult for the teacher to maintain order.
Her fractiousness was apparent whenever she was asked to follow rules she disliked.
Political debates often descend into fractiousness as tempers flare.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: docility - the trait of being easily managed or taught
  • Synonym: unruliness - the trait of being difficult to control or govern

Etymology

From 'fractious' + '-ness'; 'fractious' from Latin 'frangere' (to break).
