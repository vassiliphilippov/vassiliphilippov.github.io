leech

1. NOUN

/ liːtʃ / (AmE), / liːtʃ / (BrE)
An aquatic or terrestrial annelid worm with suckers at both ends, many species of which are parasitic and feed on the blood of other animals.
parasitic bloodsucking worm

Examples

A leech attached itself to my leg while I was swimming in the lake.
Some doctors once used a leech to draw blood as part of medical treatment.
Fish and frogs often fall victim to the bite of a leech in shallow ponds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parasite - organism that lives on or in another and benefits by deriving nutrients at the other's expense

Etymology

Old English *lǣce*, related to Old High German *lēhse* (physician, bloodsucker).

2. VERB

/ liːtʃ / (AmE), / liːtʃ / (BrE)
to draw blood from a person or animal by applying leeches, typically for medical treatment, especially in the past
to draw blood using leeches (historical)

Examples

Before modern medicine, doctors would sometimes leech their patients to try to cure illness.
The healer decided to leech the wound, hoping it would reduce swelling and pain.
Historically, physicians believed that to leech someone could balance their bodily humors.

Synset Examples

In the old days, doctors routinely bled patients as part of the treatment

Etymology

From Old English *læce* (physician, also the bloodsucking worm), related to the practice of bloodletting.

3. NOUN

/ lit͡ʃ / (AmE), / liːtʃ / (BrE)
a person who persistently exploits or takes advantage of others for personal gain, offering nothing in return
person who exploits others for gain

Examples

He was tired of being treated like a leech by his so-called friends.
The group saw her as a leech who only showed up when it was time to party.
Instead of being independent, he preferred to live as a leech off his wealthy relatives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sponger - a person who lives off others by taking advantage of their generosity
  • Synonym: parasite - a person who habitually relies on or exploits others
  • Antonym: benefactor - a person who gives help or financial aid

Etymology

From Old English *lǣce* (physician, leech), referring originally to the bloodsucking worm and later metaphorically to a person who drains others.
