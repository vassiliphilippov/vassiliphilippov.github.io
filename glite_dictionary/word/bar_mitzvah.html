bar mitzvah

1. NOUN

/ bɑr ˈmɪtsvə / (AmE), / bɑː ˈmɪtsvə / (BrE)
A Jewish coming-of-age ceremony for a boy on his 13th birthday, marking the assumption of religious responsibilities.
Jewish boy's coming-of-age ceremony

Examples

Jacob celebrated his bar mitzvah surrounded by family and friends at the synagogue.
Preparations for Ethan's bar mitzvah took several months, including studying the Torah portion.
Attending a bar mitzvah is a significant tradition in many Jewish communities.

Synset Examples

a bar mitzvah is an important social event

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bat mitzvah - Jewish girl's coming-of-age ceremony at age 12 or 13

Etymology

From Hebrew, literally ‘son of the commandment’.

2. VERB

/ bɑr ˈmɪtsvə / (AmE), / bɑː ˈmɪtsvə / (BrE)
to confirm or initiate a Jewish boy at the age of religious maturity (usually 13) in the bar mitzvah ceremony.
initiate (a boy) in the bar mitzvah ceremony

Examples

He was bar mitzvahed at his family's synagogue last spring.
Many boys bar mitzvah at the age of thirteen, marking their coming of age in the Jewish community.
The family traveled to Israel so their son could bar mitzvah at the Western Wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bat mitzvah - confirming a Jewish girl in the bat mitzvah ceremony

Etymology

From Hebrew 'bar' (son) + 'mitzvah' (commandment).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02480381-v
Synset POS: v
