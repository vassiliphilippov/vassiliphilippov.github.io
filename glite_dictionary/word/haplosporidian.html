haplosporidian

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæp.loʊ.spəˈrɪd.i.ən / (AmE), / ˌhæp.ləʊ.spəˈrɪd.i.ən / (BrE)
a microscopic parasitic organism that infects invertebrates and lower vertebrates, belonging to the class Haplosporidia
parasitic organism in invertebrates

Examples

Researchers discovered a new haplosporidian infecting oysters along the coastline.
The life cycle of a haplosporidian involves several developmental stages inside its host.
Marine biologists are studying how haplosporidians affect crab populations in estuarine environments.

Etymology

From Greek haplo- (single, simple) + sporidion (small spore), referring to its spore structure.
