tuppence

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌpəns / (AmE), / ˈtʌpəns / (BrE)
a former British coin worth two pennies, used in both pre-decimal and decimal systems
former British coin worth two pennies
british

Examples

She found an old tuppence in her grandfather's coin collection.
It used to cost only a tuppence to ride the tram years ago.
He's not worried about losing a tuppence—it's just a small coin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: two pence - a coin worth two pennies in the UK

Etymology

Alteration of 'two pence' in British English.
