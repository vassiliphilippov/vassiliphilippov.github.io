electroplate

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktroʊˌpleɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrəʊˌpleɪt / (BrE)
an object that has been coated with a thin layer of metal by the process of electrolysis
object plated with metal by electrolysis

Examples

The silver electroplate on the candlestick had worn thin over the years.
She displayed an antique electroplate tea set inherited from her grandmother.
Many cutlery items were made from electroplate rather than solid silver to reduce costs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electroplating - the process of coating objects with metal using electrolysis

Etymology

From electro- (referring to electricity) + plate (to cover with a thin layer of metal).

2. VERB

/ ɪˈlɛktroʊˌpleɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrəʊˌpleɪt / (BrE)
to cover the surface of an object with a thin layer of metal using electrolysis
coat with metal by electrolysis

Examples

A jeweler can electroplate a ring with gold to improve its appearance.
Some companies electroplate silverware to prevent it from tarnishing.
The manufacturer electroplated the parts to add a protective layer against corrosion.

Synset Examples

electroplate the watch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plate - to cover or coat with a thin layer of metal

Etymology

From electro- (relating to electricity) + plate (cover with a thin layer).
