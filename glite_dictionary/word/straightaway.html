straightaway

1. NOUN

/ ˈstreɪtəˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈstreɪtəweɪ / (BrE)
A straight section of a road, racecourse, or track, especially where vehicles or athletes can reach high speed.
straight section of a track or road

Examples

The riders gained speed on the main straightaway before approaching the final curve.
Spectators lined the straightaway to watch the runners sprint toward the finish line.
After navigating several sharp turns, the car accelerated down the straightaway.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bend - a curve in a road or track
  • Antonym: curve - a curved part of a road or path
