Lindesnes

1. PROPN

/ lɪnˈdɛzˌnɛs / (AmE), / lɪnˈdɛznɛs / (BrE)
a cape marking the southernmost point of mainland Norway, known as a prominent navigational landmark
cape at Norway's southernmost point

Examples

Many sailors have braved the waters around Lindesnes for centuries.
We stopped to take photographs at Lindesnes, the southern tip of Norway.
The lighthouse at Lindesnes is a popular tourist attraction.

Etymology

From Norwegian, originally Lindesnes, probably from Old Norse 'Líðandi nes' (sloping headland).
