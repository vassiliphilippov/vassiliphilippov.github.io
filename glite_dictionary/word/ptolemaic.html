Ptolemaic

1. ADJ

/ ˌtɑlɪˈmeɪɪk / (AmE), / ˌtɒlɪˈmeɪɪk / (BrE)
Relating to the astronomer Ptolemy or his theories, especially the geocentric model of the universe.
relating to the astronomer Ptolemy or his theories

Examples

Medieval scholars accepted the Ptolemaic model, which placed the Earth at the center of the universe.
The Ptolemaic system was eventually replaced by the heliocentric model proposed by Copernicus.
Her research focused on ancient Ptolemaic astronomy and its influence on later science.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geocentric - having the earth as the center, especially in astronomical models

Etymology

From Latin Ptolemaicus, from Greek Ptolemaïkos, from Ptolemaios (Ptolemy).

2. ADJ

/ ˌtɑːləˈmeɪɪk / (AmE), / ˌtɒlɪˈmeɪɪk / (BrE)
Relating to the geocentric astronomical system developed by the ancient astronomer Ptolemy, in which the Earth is considered the center of the universe.
relating to Ptolemy's geocentric system

Examples

The Ptolemaic model was the dominant theory of the cosmos in Europe for over a thousand years.
Many medieval scholars wrote commentaries explaining Ptolemaic astronomy to new generations of students.
The shift from the Ptolemaic to the Copernican system marked a turning point in scientific history.

Synset Examples

in the Ptolemaic system of planetary motion the earth is fixed as the center of the universe with the sun and moon and planets revolving around it

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Copernican - relating to the heliocentric model developed by Copernicus

Etymology

From the name of Claudius Ptolemy, a 2nd-century Greek-Egyptian astronomer and mathematician.
