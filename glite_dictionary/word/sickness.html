sickness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪknəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪknəs / (BrE)
the state of being ill; an impairment of normal physical health or functioning
impairment of normal physical health

Examples

He missed a week of work due to sickness.
There was a widespread sickness in the village after the flood.
The doctor is researching ways to prevent sickness in young children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illness - a state of poor health or disease
  • Antonym: health - the state of being free from illness or injury

Etymology

From Old English 'seocness', meaning disease or illness.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪknəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪknɪs / (BrE)
a state of moral, mental, or structural defectiveness or unsoundness
state of moral or structural defectiveness

Examples

His hatred revealed a deep sickness in his heart.
There was a sickness in the political system that nobody wanted to address.
Some critics argue that modern society suffers from a sickness of materialism.

Synset Examples

drugs have become a sickness they cannot cure
a great sickness of his judgment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corruption - moral decay or depravity
  • Antonym: integrity - the state of being morally sound

Etymology

From Old English *seocnes* (illness), from *seoc* (sick).

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɪknəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪknəs / (BrE)
the unpleasant feeling in the stomach that often comes before vomiting; nausea
feeling before vomiting; nausea

Examples

He felt a wave of sickness after eating some spoiled food.
The roller coaster gave her a sudden sickness in her stomach.
A strong smell of gasoline filled him with sickness and he had to sit down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nausea - a feeling of sickness with an inclination to vomit

Etymology

From Old English *seocness*, from *seoc* (sick), related to German *Siechtum*.
