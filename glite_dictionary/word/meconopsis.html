Meconopsis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmekəˈnɑːpsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmiːkəˈnɒpsɪs / (BrE)
A genus of herbaceous plants, often monocarpic, native chiefly to the mountains of China and Tibet, known for their colorful, poppy-like flowers.
genus of Himalayan flowering plants

Examples

The brilliant blue flowers of the Meconopsis are a highlight in many alpine gardens.
Researchers observed that Meconopsis plants often flower only once before dying.
Botanists have discovered new species of Meconopsis in remote areas of the Himalayas.

Etymology

From Greek mēkōn (poppy) and ópsis (appearance or view).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛkəˈnɑpəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmiːkəˈnɒpəsɪs / (BrE)
a genus of perennial or monocarpic plants, known for their poppy-like flowers, found mainly in mountainous regions of China and Tibet
genus of poppy-like mountain plants

Examples

The Meconopsis genus is famous for its striking blue poppies found in Himalayan regions.
Botanists often study Meconopsis due to its remarkable adaptation to high altitudes.
Gardeners prize Meconopsis for its vibrant flowers and rarity in cultivation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Papaver - another genus of flowering plants commonly known as poppies

Etymology

From Greek mēkōn (poppy) + opsis (appearance), referring to their poppy-like look.
