elegantly

1. ADV

/ ˈɛlɪɡəntli / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪɡəntli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows elegance or refined taste.
in an elegant or tasteful way

Examples

The actress was elegantly dressed for the awards ceremony.
The building blends elegantly into its surroundings.
He elegantly solved the complex equation on the chalkboard.

Synset Examples

the room was elegantly decorated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gracefully - in a smooth and elegant manner
  • Antonym: awkwardly - in an ungainly or clumsy manner

Etymology

From 'elegant' + '-ly' (adverbial suffix). 'Elegant' from Latin 'elegans'.

2. ADV

/ ˈɛlɪɡəntli / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪɡəntli / (BrE)
in a graceful and stylish manner; with elegance and sophistication
in a graceful and stylish manner

Examples

The ballerina moved elegantly across the stage, captivating the audience.
She solved the complex problem elegantly, impressing her peers with her skill.
The dining room was elegantly decorated for the wedding reception.

Synset Examples

the members of these groups do not express themselves as accurately or as elegantly as their critics do

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gracefully - in a manner showing grace or elegance
  • Antonym: awkwardly - in an ungraceful or clumsy manner

Etymology

Formed from 'elegant' + suffix '-ly'. 'Elegant' from Latin 'elegans' (tasteful, refined).
