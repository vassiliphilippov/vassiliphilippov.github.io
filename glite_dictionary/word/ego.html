ego

1. NOUN

/ ˈiɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈiːɡəʊ / (BrE)
a person's sense of self; the conscious awareness of one's own identity.
sense of self or identity

Examples

His enormous ego made it difficult for him to admit he was wrong.
Meditation can help you become more aware of your ego and its influence on your life.
Psychologists often discuss how the ego mediates between our instincts and social expectations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self - a person's essential being that distinguishes them from others

Etymology

From Latin *ego* (I), first used in English in the late 18th century in philosophical and psychological contexts.

2. NOUN

/ ˈiːɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈiːɡəʊ / (BrE)
An inflated sense of self-importance or pride in one's own superiority over others.
inflated sense of self-importance

Examples

Her ego prevented her from admitting she was wrong.
Fame can inflate a person's ego to unhealthy levels.
With a bruised ego, he struggled to handle the criticism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: egotism - Excessive reference to oneself or self-centeredness
  • Antonym: humility - A modest view of one's own importance

Etymology

From Latin 'ego' meaning 'I'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈiːɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈiːɡəʊ / (BrE)
(in psychoanalysis) the part of the mind that is responsible for a person's sense of self and reality, mediating between desires and the external world.
psychoanalytic term for the conscious mind

Examples

In Freudian theory, the ego works to balance the demands of the id and the superego.
Her strong ego helped her cope with difficult situations in a rational way.
The psychologist explained that the ego mediates between primal desires and moral conscience.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: id - (psychoanalysis) the part of the mind containing instinctive impulses
  • Antonym: superego - (psychoanalysis) the part of the mind reflecting moral and social standards

Etymology

From Latin 'ego' meaning 'I' (first person singular pronoun).
