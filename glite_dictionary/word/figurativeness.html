figurativeness

1. NOUN

/ fɪˈɡjʊrətɪvnəs / (AmE), / fɪˈɡjʊərətɪvnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being metaphorical rather than literal; the use of figures of speech.
the quality of being metaphorical

Examples

The figurativeness of her poetry makes it rich in imagery and meaning.
Debates about the figurativeness of Biblical passages have lasted for centuries.
Critics have noted a high degree of figurativeness in his prose style.

Synset Examples

the narrators are caught between the literality and figurativeness of suspension.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: literalness - the quality of being literal or exact
  • Synonym: metaphoricity - the quality of being metaphorical

Etymology

From 'figurative' + '-ness', 'figurative' from Late Latin figurativus (of or pertaining to a figure of speech).

2. NOUN

/ ˌfɪɡjərətɪvnəs / (AmE), / ˌfɪɡjʊərətɪvnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of representing forms, figures, or images, especially in art or language, rather than depicting things literally.
quality of being figurative

Examples

The figurativeness of the painting invites viewers to interpret its meaning beyond the obvious.
She admired the figurativeness in his poetic descriptions of everyday life.
Modern advertising often relies on the figurativeness of images to convey complex messages.

Synset Examples

To balance between these two aspects of the ad, the figurativeness of the visual image can play a role.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: literalness - the quality of being literal or lacking figurativeness
  • Synonym: figurative - not literal; using figures of speech or symbolic representation

Etymology

From figurative + -ness, meaning the quality of being figurative.
