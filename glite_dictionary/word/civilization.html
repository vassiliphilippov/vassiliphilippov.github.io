civilization

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪvələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪvɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
A particular society, culture, or group of people existing at a specific time and place, especially one notable for its achievements or way of life.
a society at a specific time and place

Examples

The ancient Greek civilization was known for its art and philosophy.
Archaeologists study the ruins of the Indus Valley civilization to learn about early urban life.
The collapse of the Roman civilization changed the course of European history.

Synset Examples

early Mayan civilization

Related Terms

  • Synonym: culture - the customs and achievements of a particular group or time
  • Antonym: barbarism - absence of culture and civilization

Etymology

From Latin 'civilis' (relating to citizens) via French 'civilisation'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɪvələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪvəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
an advanced stage of human society marked by a well-organized government, culture, and way of life
an advanced human society

Examples

The ancient Egyptians built a remarkable civilization along the Nile River.
After the collapse of the Western Roman Empire, much of the old civilization was lost.
Many historians study how civilization arose from earlier, simpler societies.

Synset Examples

the people slowly progressed from barbarism to civilization

Related Terms

  • Antonym: barbarism - a state of society lacking culture or refinement

Etymology

From Latin 'civilis' (relating to citizens) + '-ation'.

3. NOUN

/ ˌsɪv.ə.ləˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪv.ɪ.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The process by which a society or place reaches an advanced stage of social and cultural development and organization.
process of social development

Examples

The rise of agriculture was a turning point in the civilization of humankind.
Education plays a crucial role in the civilization of a society.
Historians study the process of civilization to understand cultural and technological progress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: development - the act or process of growing or causing something to grow or become larger or more advanced

Etymology

From French *civilisation*, from Latin *civilis* (civil, relating to citizens).
