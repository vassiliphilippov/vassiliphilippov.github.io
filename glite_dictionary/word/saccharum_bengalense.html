Saccharum bengalense

1. NOUN

/ ˌsækəˌruːm bɛnˈɡælɛnsi / (AmE), / ˌsakəˌruːm bɛnˈɡalɛnsi / (BrE)
a tall, tough grass native to Asia, whose stems are used for making ropes and baskets
tough Asiatic grass used for ropes and baskets

Examples

The villagers use Saccharum bengalense to weave sturdy baskets for everyday use.
Ropes made from Saccharum bengalense are known for their strength and durability.
Researchers are studying Saccharum bengalense for its potential in sustainable crafts and biofiber production.

Etymology

From Latin genus name Saccharum (sugarcane) and Bengalense, referring to Bengal.
