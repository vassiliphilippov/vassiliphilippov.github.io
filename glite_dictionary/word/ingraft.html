to ingraft

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈɡræft / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡrɑːft / (BrE)
to join parts of different plants so that they grow together as one
to graft parts of plants together

Examples

Gardeners often ingraft rosebushes to create hardier plants.
You can ingraft an apple branch onto a pear tree with careful techniques.
The horticulturist explained how to successfully ingraft scions onto mature rootstock.

Synset Examples

graft the cherry tree branch onto the plum tree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graft - to join a branch or bud from one plant onto another

Etymology

From Latin 'ingraffare', based on 'in-' (into) and 'graft'.
