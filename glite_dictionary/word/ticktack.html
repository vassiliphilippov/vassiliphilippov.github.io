ticktack

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪkˌtæk / (AmE), / ˈtɪktæk / (BrE)
a system of hand signals used by bookmakers at racetracks to communicate betting information quickly and discreetly
hand signal system used by bookmakers

Examples

The bookmakers on the far side of the track relied on ticktack to keep up with the rapidly changing odds.
Before modern technology, ticktack was the primary method for relaying information between betting stands.
She found the elaborate gestures of ticktack fascinating as she watched the race from the stands.

Etymology

From the imitation of clicking or tapping sounds; influenced by the rapid movement of hands or signals.

2. VERB

/ ˈtɪkˌtæk / (AmE), / ˈtɪkˌtæk / (BrE)
to make a repeated ticking sound like that of a clock or timer
make a repeated ticking sound

Examples

At night, the old wall clock ticktacked steadily in the hallway.
As she waited, the metronome quietly ticktacked on her desk.
The only sound in the cabin was the watch that ticktacked on the shelf.

Synset Examples

the clocks were ticking
the grandfather clock beat midnight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tick - to make a regular, repeated clicking sound, as a clock does
  • Synonym: tick-tock - to make a repeated ticking sound, especially of a clock

Etymology

Imitative of the sound produced by a clock or timer; related to 'tick-tock.'

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
