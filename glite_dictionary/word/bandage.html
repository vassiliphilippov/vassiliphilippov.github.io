bandage

1. NOUN

/ ˈbændɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbændɪdʒ / (BrE)
A piece of soft material used to cover and protect a wound or injured part of the body.
material covering for an injury

Examples

He wrapped a clean bandage around his injured hand to stop the bleeding.
After the surgery, the nurse changed the bandage on her knee every day.
Make sure the bandage is not too tight, or it might cut off circulation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dressing - a piece of material applied to a wound for healing or protection

Etymology

From French 'bandage', from 'bande' (strip, band).

2. VERB

/ ˈbændɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbændɪdʒ / (BrE)
to wrap a strip of material around a part of the body, often to protect or support an injury
wrap material around (a body part)

Examples

She carefully bandaged his injured arm after cleaning the wound.
Paramedics arrived quickly and bandaged the cyclist’s bleeding knee.
He knows how to bandage a sprained ankle properly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dress - to treat and cover a wound or injury

Etymology

From French 'bande' (a strip, band), from Old French, from Late Latin 'banda' (band, strip).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01306207-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈbæn.dɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbændɪdʒ / (BrE)
to cover or bind a wound or injured part of the body with a bandage, usually to protect or support it
cover or bind a wound or injury

Examples

The nurse carefully bandaged the patient's arm after cleaning the wound.
You should bandage the cut to prevent infection until you can see a doctor.
She bandaged her son's scraped knee before taking him back outside to play.

Synset Examples

The nurse bandaged a sprained ankle
bandage an incision

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dress - to clean and cover a wound

Etymology

From French 'bandage', from 'bande' (a strip, band).
