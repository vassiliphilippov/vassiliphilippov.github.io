Conilurus

1. NOUN

/ kɒˈnɪljʊrəs / (AmE), / kɒˈnɪljʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of rodents known as jerboa rats or rabbit rats, native to Australia and nearby regions.
a genus of jerboa or rabbit rats

Examples

The endangered Conilurus is rarely seen in its natural habitat today.
Scientists studied the behavior of several Conilurus specimens in the wild.
Conservationists are working to protect the remaining populations of Conilurus in Australia.

Etymology

From Latin 'cuniculus' (rabbit) and Greek 'oura' (tail).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊnɪˈluːrəs / (AmE), / ˌkɒnɪˈljʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of rodents known as jerboa rats or rabbit rats, native to Australia and New Guinea.
genus of jerboa or rabbit rats

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Conilurus during their expedition in northern Australia.
The jerboa rat, classified under Conilurus, is known for its rabbit-like tail.
Habitat loss has severely affected populations of Conilurus in certain regions.

Etymology

From Latin coni- (rabbit) + -urus (tail), referring to the rabbitlike tail of these rodents.
