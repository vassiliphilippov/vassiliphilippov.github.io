abfarad

1. NOUN

/ ˌæbˈfæræd / (AmE), / ˌabˈfarad / (BrE)
a unit of capacitance in the centimeter-gram-second (CGS) electromagnetic system, equal to one billion farads
unit of capacitance; 1 billion farads (CGS system)

Examples

The abfarad is mainly used in theoretical discussions of CGS units in physics.
One abfarad equals one billion farads, making it an extremely large unit of capacitance.
Although obsolete, the abfarad occasionally appears in older engineering texts.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: farad - the SI unit of capacitance

Etymology

Formed from ab- (for 'absolute') + farad, following patterns used in the CGS system.
