Lycoperdales

1. NOUN

/ laɪkoʊˈpɜːrdeɪliːz / (AmE), / laɪkəʊˈpɜːdeɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of fungi characterized by fleshy, often globose fruiting bodies, including puffballs and earthstars
order of fungi including puffballs

Examples

Some of the most familiar members of the Lycoperdales are commonly known as puffballs.
The Lycoperdales order includes fungi with distinctive globose fruiting bodies found in many woodlands.
Scientists recently revised the classification of Lycoperdales based on genetic analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puffball - a fungus producing a globe-shaped fruiting body
  • Synonym: earthstar - a fungus with a star-shaped fruiting body

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus name Lycoperdon and Latin plural Order suffix -ales.
