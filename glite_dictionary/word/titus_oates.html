Titus Oates

1. PROPN

/ ˈtaɪtəs ˈoʊts / (AmE), / ˈtaɪtəs ˈəʊts / (BrE)
(1649–1705) English conspirator who falsely claimed that there was a Jesuit plot to assassinate King Charles II, leading to the 'Popish Plot.'
English conspirator in the Popish Plot (1649–1705)

Examples

The story of Titus Oates demonstrates how false accusations can cause national panic.
Historians widely regard Titus Oates as the chief architect of the notorious Popish Plot.
In the late seventeenth century, Titus Oates became infamous for his deceitful schemes against Catholics.
