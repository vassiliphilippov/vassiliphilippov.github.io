Annwn

1. PROPN

/ ˈæn.uːn / (AmE), / ˈan.uːn / (BrE)
(in Welsh mythology) the otherworld; the land of the fairies and the dead.
Welsh mythological otherworld

Examples

In ancient tales, King Arthur is said to have journeyed to Annwn in search of magical treasures.
The spirits of the dead were believed to dwell in Annwn, a place of plenty and joy.
Many Welsh legends describe Annwn as an island paradise beyond mortal reach.

Etymology

From Welsh Annwn, meaning 'the underworld' or 'other world'.
