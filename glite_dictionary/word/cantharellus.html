Cantharellus

1. NOUN

/ kænˈθæɹələs / (AmE), / kanˈθarɪləs / (BrE)
a genus of fungi whose members are known as chanterelles, featuring funnel-shaped fruiting bodies and valued as edible mushrooms
genus of edible fungi (chanterelles)

Examples

The Cantharellus genus is prized by mushroom foragers for its delicious taste.
Many gourmet chefs use Cantharellus species in their signature dishes.
We identified the specimen as a Cantharellus because of its funnel-shaped cap and distinctive color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chanterelle - an edible wild mushroom with a yellow, funnel-shaped cap, member of genus Cantharellus

Etymology

From Greek kantharos (drinking cup), referring to the shape of the mushroom.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkænθəˈrɛləs / (AmE), / ˌkanθəˈrɛləs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi with funnel-shaped fruiting bodies, including the edible chanterelles.
genus of fungi including chanterelles

Examples

The Cantharellus genus is highly regarded among mushroom foragers for its edible species.
Many chefs prize Cantharellus mushrooms for their delicate flavor and texture.
Botanists studied the habitat of Cantharellus in deciduous forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chanterelle - an edible yellow mushroom of the genus Cantharellus

Etymology

From Greek kantharos (a kind of cup or drinking vessel), referencing the cup-shaped fruiting body.
