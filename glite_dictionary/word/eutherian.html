eutherian

1. ADJ

/ juːˈθɪriən / (AmE), / juːˈθɪəriən / (BrE)
relating to or belonging to the group of mammals (Eutheria) that give birth to live young who have developed within the uterus
relating to placental mammals

Examples

Humans are classified as eutherian mammals because they give birth to well-developed young.
The eutherian lineage is distinguished from marsupials and monotremes by its complex placenta.
Many scientists study eutherian evolution to understand the diversity of placental mammals.

Synset Examples

eutherian mammals

Related Terms

  • Antonym: marsupial - mammal of the order Marsupialia, such as kangaroos and koalas
  • Antonym: monotreme - egg-laying mammal, such as the platypus or echidna

Etymology

From New Latin Eutheria, from Greek eu- (good, well) + thērion (beast).

2. NOUN

/ juːˈθɪriən / (AmE), / juːˈθɪərɪən / (BrE)
a mammal belonging to the group Eutheria, characterized by having a placenta and including all mammals except monotremes and marsupials
placental mammal

Examples

The elephant is a large eutherian that gestates for nearly two years.
Unlike marsupials, eutherian mammals nourish their young through a well-developed placenta.
Most familiar animals, such as dogs and whales, are classified as eutherians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: placental - a mammal of the group that has a placenta
  • Antonym: marsupial - a mammal of the group that carries young in a pouch
  • Antonym: monotreme - an egg-laying mammal, such as a platypus or echidna

Etymology

From New Latin Eutheria, from Greek eu- (good, true) + thērion (beast).
