fastidious

1. ADJ

/ fæˈstɪdiəs / (AmE), / fæˈstɪdiəs / (BrE)
very attentive to detail, hard to please, or excessively concerned with cleanliness or accuracy
very attentive to detail, hard to please

Examples

She is fastidious about keeping her desk organized and spotless.
His fastidious attention to detail impressed everyone on the team.
Some editors are so fastidious that they will spend hours correcting minor errors.

Synset Examples

a fastidious and incisive intellect
fastidious about personal cleanliness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meticulous - showing great attention to detail; very careful and precise
  • Antonym: sloppy - careless and lacking attention to detail

Etymology

From Latin 'fastidiosus' (disdainful, squeamish), from 'fastidium' (aversion, nausea).

Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ fæˈstɪdiəs / (AmE), / fæˈstɪdɪəs / (BrE)
(of a microorganism or plant) having complex and specific nutritional requirements, especially growing only under special conditions in artificial cultures.
requiring special nutritional conditions (biology)
medical

Examples

Some bacteria are fastidious and require enriched media to grow in the laboratory.
The fastidious nature of these plants makes them challenging to cultivate outside their native habitat.
Because the pathogen is fastidious, only specialized lab techniques can successfully culture it.

Synset Examples

fastidious microorganisms
certain highly specialized xerophytes are extremely exacting in their requirements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exacting - making great demands on skill, attention, or resources

Etymology

From Latin *fastidiosus* (disdainful, squeamish), from *fastidium* (loathing, disgust).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00988799-a
Synset POS: a
