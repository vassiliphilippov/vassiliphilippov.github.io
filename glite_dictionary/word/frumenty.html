frumenty

1. NOUN

/ ˈfruːmənˌti / (AmE), / ˈfrʌmɪnti / (BrE)
a sweet spiced porridge made from hulled wheat, traditional in medieval English cuisine
sweet porridge made from wheat
archaic

Examples

During the medieval feast, the cook served bowls of frumenty to the guests.
Many traditional English recipes mention frumenty as a staple during festive celebrations.
He read about frumenty in a historical novel set in the Middle Ages.

Etymology

From Middle English *frumentee*, from Old French *frumentee*, from Latin *frumentum* (grain, especially wheat).
