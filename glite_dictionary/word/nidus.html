nidus

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪ.dəs / (AmE), / ˈnaɪ.dəs / (BrE)
A central point or focus where infection or disease develops in an organism.
central point of infection or disease
medical

Examples

The surgeon located the nidus of the infection and removed the affected tissue.
Dental plaque can serve as a nidus for bacterial growth in the mouth.
A foreign object under the skin may form a nidus for recurrent abscesses.

Synset Examples

the focus of infection

Etymology

From Latin nidus (nest).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪ.dəs / (AmE), / ˈnaɪ.dəs / (BrE)
A nest or place where insects or spiders deposit their eggs.
nest for eggs (insects or spiders)

Examples

The spider spun her nidus carefully in the corner of the shed.
Scientists discovered a nidus containing dozens of insect eggs under the bark.
Some insects protect their young by hiding their nidus in secluded places.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nest - a structure built by birds, insects, or other animals to hold eggs or provide shelter
