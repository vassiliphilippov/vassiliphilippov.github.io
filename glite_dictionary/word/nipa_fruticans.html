Nipa fruticans

1. NOUN

/ ˈniːpə fruːˈtaɪkænz / (AmE), / ˈniːpə fruːˈtɪkənz / (BrE)
A creeping semiaquatic palm of mangrove swamps and tidal estuaries; its sap is used to make liquor, its leaves for thatch, and its fruit contains edible seeds.
species of palm found in mangrove swamps

Examples

The villagers collected sap from the Nipa fruticans to produce traditional palm wine.
Local roofs are often made from the strong leaves of Nipa fruticans.
In flood-prone estuaries, the Nipa fruticans palm provides both food and building materials.

Etymology

From the Malay word 'nipa', and Latin 'fruticans' (bushy, shrub-like).
