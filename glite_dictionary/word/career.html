career

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrɪr / (AmE), / kəˈrɪə / (BrE)
the job or profession that a person is trained for and pursues as a major activity in their life
a person's main occupation or profession

Examples

She hopes to build a successful career in medicine after graduating from university.
Switching career paths in your thirties can be both challenging and rewarding.
He dedicated his entire career to public service and education.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vocation - a strong feeling of suitability for a particular career or occupation
  • Synonym: profession - a type of job that requires special education or training
  • Antonym: hobby - an activity done regularly in one's leisure time for pleasure

Etymology

From French *carrière* (racecourse), from Italian *carriera*, from Latin *carraria* (carriage road).

2. VERB

/ kəˈrɪr / (AmE), / kəˈrɪə / (BrE)
to move swiftly and uncontrollably in a specified direction, especially in an out-of-control or headlong manner
move swiftly and out of control

Examples

The runaway truck careered down the steep hill, narrowly missing several cars.
Spectators gasped as the cyclist careered off the track during the final lap.
The children careered through the hallway, laughing and shouting as they went.

Synset Examples

The cars careered down the road
The mob careered through the streets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hurtling - moving rapidly, often uncontrollably
  • Synonym: plunge - to move or fall suddenly and quickly

Etymology

From early modern English 'career', originally meaning a racecourse or running path.

3. NOUN

/ kəˈrɪr / (AmE), / kəˈrɪə / (BrE)
the series of jobs or professions that someone has during their working life, especially in a particular area or field
working life progression

Examples

She hopes to have a successful career in medicine.
After a twenty-year career as a teacher, he decided to retire.
The award recognizes his outstanding career in scientific research.

Synset Examples

the general had had a distinguished career
he had a long career in the law

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profession - a paid occupation, especially one that involves prolonged training or a formal qualification
  • Synonym: job - a paid position of regular employment

Etymology

From French 'carrière' (road, racecourse), from Late Latin 'carriaria' (carriage road).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
