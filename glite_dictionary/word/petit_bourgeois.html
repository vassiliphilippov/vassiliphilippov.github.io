petit bourgeois

1. NOUN

/ pəˌti bʊrˈʒwɑ / (AmE), / pəˌti bʊəˈʒwɑː / (BrE)
a member of the lower middle class, especially one who is perceived as conventional or materialistic
member of the lower middle class

Examples

He was proud of his petit bourgeois background and careful with his money.
The novel depicts the lives of a petit bourgeois family struggling to maintain their status.
Critics described his tastes as petit bourgeois rather than truly refined.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bourgeois - a member of the middle class, especially in reference to conventional attitudes
  • Degree Lower: proletarian - a member of the working class

Etymology

From French, literally 'small bourgeois', where 'petit' means 'small' and 'bourgeois' refers to a middle-class person.

2. NOUN

/ pəˌtiː bʊrˈʒwɑː / (AmE), / ˌpeti bʊəˈʒwɑː / (BrE)
a member of the lower middle class, such as a shopkeeper, small-scale businessman, or clerical worker, especially regarded from a social or political perspective
lower middle-class person (shopkeeper, clerk, etc.)

Examples

The petit bourgeois of the town tended to vote conservatively in local elections.
He rose from a petit bourgeois background to become a leading intellectual.
Many revolutions viewed the petit bourgeois as wavering between the working class and the elite.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bourgeois - middle-class person, typically referring to the broader class above the working class

Etymology

From French, literally 'small bourgeois' (middle-class).
