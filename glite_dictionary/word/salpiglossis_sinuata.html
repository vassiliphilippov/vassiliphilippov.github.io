Salpiglossis sinuata

1. NOUN

/ sæl.pɪˈɡlɑː.sɪs sɪˈnjuː.ə.tə / (AmE), / sælpɪˈɡlɒsɪs sɪˈnjuːətə / (BrE)
A Chilean herb of the nightshade family with velvety, funnel-shaped yellowish or violet flowers and long tonguelike styles at the corolla throat.
Chilean herb with colorful funnel-shaped flowers

Examples

The garden was brightened by rows of Salpiglossis sinuata with their vividly colored blooms.
She chose Salpiglossis sinuata for her flowerbed because of its unique, velvety petals.
Botanists are fascinated by the intricate patterns found in the flowers of Salpiglossis sinuata.

Etymology

From Latin 'Salpiglossis', from Greek 'salpinx' (trumpet) and 'glossa' (tongue), referring to the flower's shape.
