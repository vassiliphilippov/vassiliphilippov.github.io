1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɝː.bɚ.ə / (AmE), / ˈɡɜː.bər.ə / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants native to South Africa and Asia, known for their large, brightly colored daisy-like blooms
a genus of brightly colored daisy-like flowers

Examples

She decorated the table with a vase of Gerbera to add a pop of color.
The Gerbera is popular among florists for its striking appearance and long vase life.

Etymology

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɜrbərə / (AmE), / ˈɡɜːbərə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants native to South Africa and Asia, known for their large, brightly colored daisy-like flowers often grown as ornamentals.
genus of ornamental daisy-like flowers

Examples

The Gerbera in her garden bloomed in a vivid array of colors.
Florists often use Gerbera daisies to add a cheerful touch to bouquets.
Botanists classify these popular flowers under the genus Gerbera.

Etymology

Named after German naturalist Traugott Gerber (1710–1743).
