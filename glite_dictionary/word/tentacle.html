tentacle

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛn.tə.kəl / (AmE), / ˈtɛntəkəl / (BrE)
A long, flexible organ extending from the head or near the mouth of certain animals, used for touching, grasping, or movement.
elongated flexible organ of animals

Examples

The octopus used a tentacle to investigate the small opening in the rock.
Jellyfish sting their prey with their tentacles.
Some plants, like the sundew, have sticky tentacles for catching insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appendage - an external body part protruding from an organism's body

Etymology

From Latin tentaculum (feeler), from tentare (to feel, try).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛntəkəl / (AmE), / ˈtɛntəkl̩ / (BrE)
a thing that extends and exerts influence or control, especially in a way that is widespread and sometimes inescapable, like the grasp of an octopus's arm
something that grasps, controls, or spreads influence

Examples

The tentacles of the company reached into every sector of the industry.
She tried to free herself from the tentacles of addiction.
Many people fear the tentacles of government bureaucracy slowly overtaking their daily lives.

Synset Examples

caught in the tentacles of organized crime

Related Terms

  • Synonym: influence - the capacity to have an effect on someone or something
  • Synonym: grip - a strong hold or grasp; power of controlling

Etymology

From Latin tentaculum (feeler, means of feeling or touching), from tentare (to feel, try).
