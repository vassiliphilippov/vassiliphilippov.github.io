superfecundation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuːpərˌfiːkʌnˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˌfiːkʌnˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
the fertilization of two or more ova released during the same menstrual cycle by sperm from separate acts of intercourse, especially from different males
fertilization of multiple eggs by sperm from separate acts
medical

Examples

Cases of superfecundation in twins are rare but have been documented in medical literature.
The phenomenon of superfecundation can result in twins having different fathers.
Researchers studied superfecundation to better understand its genetic implications.

Etymology

From Latin super- (above) + fecundatio (fertilization)
