to commemorate

1. VERB

/ kəˈmɛməˌreɪt / (AmE), / kəˈmɛm.ə.reɪt / (BrE)
to honor the memory of a person or event, especially with a ceremony or activity
honor or remember with a ceremony

Examples

Each year, the city holds a parade to commemorate its founding.
A plaque was installed to commemorate those who lost their lives in the disaster.
The school organized an event to commemorate the achievements of its former students.

Synset Examples

We remembered the 50th anniversary of the liberation of Auschwitz
Remember the dead of the First World War

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to celebrate - to mark a special occasion or event, usually with festivities
  • Antonym: to forget - to fail to remember or honor

Etymology

From Latin commemoratus, past participle of commemorare (to call to mind, mention), from com- (altogether) + memorare (to remember).

3. VERB

/ kəˈmɛm.əˌreɪt / (AmE), / kəˈmɛm.əˌreɪt / (BrE)
to honor or mark the memory of a person or event, especially with a ceremony or observance
honor or mark an event/person

Examples

Each year, the community gathers to commemorate the founding of the town with a special ceremony.
A statue was erected to commemorate those who lost their lives in the conflict.
We lit candles to commemorate the anniversary of the historic event.

Synset Examples

The citizens mark the anniversary of the revolution with a march and a parade

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to celebrate - to honor or mark an occasion with festivities or ceremony
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something or someone

Etymology

From Latin commemoratus, past participle of commemorare ‘call to mind, remember together’.
