to binge-watch

1. VERB

/ ˈbɪndʒ ˌwɑtʃ / (AmE), / ˈbɪndʒ ˌwɒtʃ / (BrE)
To watch multiple episodes of a television series or program in rapid succession, typically in one sitting.
watch multiple episodes in one sitting

Examples

She binge-watched the entire season of her favorite show over the weekend.
After a stressful week, he binge-watched comedies to relax.
We binge-watched old sci-fi series until we fell asleep.

Synset Examples

I binge-watched every episode of Zim last weekend.
They binge-watched shows together on feevee all night.
I binge-watched some trash TV to unwind after work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marathon - To watch a series or set of films or episodes back-to-back

Etymology

Formed from binge (from English slang, a period of excessive indulgence) + watch.

2. VERB

/ ˈbɪndʒˌwɑtʃ / (AmE), / ˈbɪndʒˌwɒtʃ / (BrE)
to watch multiple episodes of a television program or series in rapid succession, typically in one sitting
watch several episodes in one sitting
informal

Examples

On rainy days, I love to binge-watch my favorite TV series from morning to night.
They binge-watched the entire season over the weekend and couldn't stop talking about it.
It's easy to lose track of time when you start binge-watching a new show.

Synset Examples

The slobette in me just wanted to binge-watch TV.
She binge-watches TV shows for my sins.
Scramblevision ruined our plans to binge-watch the series.
I binge-watched the latest space opera series on Netflix.
When it rains, I often suck the mop and binge-watch shows.
As a winter rat, I binge-watch shows all season long.
She binge-watched Black Mirror over the weekend.
I binge-watched all episodes of NuTrek last weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marathon - a long session of watching multiple episodes or movies in a row

Etymology

Formed from 'binge' (a short period of excessive consumption) and 'watch'; popularized in the 2010s with the rise of streaming services.
