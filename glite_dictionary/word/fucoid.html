fucoid

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjuː.kɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈfjʊə.kɔɪd / (BrE)
any of various brown seaweeds of the family Fucaceae.
a brown seaweed of the family Fucaceae

Examples

Several species of fucoid cover the rocky shores at low tide.
Marine biologists collected samples of fucoid for laboratory analysis.
The slippery texture of fucoid can make coastal rocks hazardous to walk on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alga - a simple, typically aquatic plant belonging to a group that includes seaweeds
  • Synonym: seaweed - large marine algae, especially those found on seashores

Etymology

From Latin 'fucus' (seaweed) + -oid (resembling).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːkɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈfjuːkɔɪd / (BrE)
a fossilized cast or impression of seaweed, especially of the order Fucales
fossil impression of seaweed

Examples

Ancient rocks in the region often contain fucoid markings, evidence of prehistoric algae.
The paleontologist carefully studied the fucoid to learn about ancient ocean life.
Some geologists once mistook ripple marks for fucoids, confusing them with fossilized seaweed.

Etymology

From Latin 'fucus' (seaweed) + '-oid' (resembling).
