fin-de-siècle

1. ADJ

/ fæ̃.də.siˈɛkəl / (AmE), / fæ̃.də.siˈɛk(ə)l / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of the end of a century, especially the end of the 19th century, often implying decadence or cultural transition.
relating to the end of a century (esp. 19th century)

Examples

Many writers of the fin-de-siècle period expressed feelings of uncertainty and disillusionment.
The exhibition presents fin-de-siècle art that captures the mood of change at the dawn of the twentieth century.
Her novel explores fin-de-siècle anxieties about society and morality.

Synset Examples

fin de siecle art

Etymology

From French 'fin de siècle' meaning 'end of the century'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌfæ̃ də siˈɛklə / (AmE), / ˌfæ̃ də siˈɛkl / (BrE)
characteristic of the end of a century, especially marked by a mood of decadence, self-indulgence, or moral decline; typically used of the late nineteenth century.
marked by decadence at century's end

Examples

The fin-de-siècle atmosphere of the city was filled with both creativity and a sense of moral exhaustion.
His novels are often set in fin-de-siècle Paris, capturing both its glamour and its decay.
Art and literature from the fin-de-siècle period frequently explore themes of excess and decline.

Synset Examples

a decadent life of excessive money and no sense of responsibility

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decadent - characterized by or reflecting a state of moral or cultural decline

Etymology

From French, literally 'end of (the) century'.
