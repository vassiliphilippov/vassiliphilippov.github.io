sedge

1. NOUN

/ sɛdʒ / (AmE), / sɛdʒ / (BrE)
a grasslike plant that typically grows in wet ground, having solid stems, narrow leaves, and inconspicuous flowers.
grasslike wetland plant

Examples

The marsh was full of sedge, which provided shelter for many waterbirds.
Botanists identified several rare sedge species growing along the riverbank.
After the rain, the ground between the reeds and sedge became too muddy to cross.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush - a plant similar in appearance to sedge, also found in wetlands

Etymology

Old English 'secg', related to Old High German 'segge' (sedge), and possibly to 'saw', referring to the plant's saw-like edges.
