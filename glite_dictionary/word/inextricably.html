inextricably

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈɛk.strɪ.kəb.li / (AmE), / ɪnˈɛk.strɪ.kəb.li / (BrE)
In a way that is impossible to separate or escape from.
in a way that cannot be separated

Examples

The two issues are inextricably linked and cannot be considered separately.
Modern life is inextricably connected to technology.
Their histories are inextricably intertwined throughout the centuries.

Synset Examples

motives inspired by Mammon were often inextricably blended with things pertaining to Caesar and to God

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inseparably - in a way that cannot be separated

Etymology

From the adjective 'inextricable', which comes from Latin 'inextricabilis' (impossible to disentangle), from 'in-' (not) + 'extricare' (to disentangle).
