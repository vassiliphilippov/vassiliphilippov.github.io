to rekindle

1. VERB

/ riˈkɪndl̩ / (AmE), / riˈkɪndl̩ / (BrE)
to make a feeling, interest, or hope become active again; to revive something that had faded
revive or make active again

Examples

Their reunion helped rekindle old friendships that had faded over the years.
The heartfelt speech managed to rekindle hope in the team despite their previous defeat.
A shared memory can sometimes rekindle lost love between two people.

Synset Examples

rekindle hopes
rekindle her love

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revive - to bring back to life, consciousness, or activity
  • Antonym: to extinguish - to put an end to, to cause to cease

Etymology

From re- (again) + kindle (to start a fire), originally meaning to light again, later extended figuratively.

2. VERB

/ riˈkɪndəl / (AmE), / riˈkɪndl / (BrE)
to light or start (a fire) again; to renew or revive (something, such as a feeling or interest)
to start or revive something again

Examples

After years apart, they managed to rekindle their friendship during the reunion.
The camper tried to rekindle the dying fire as the night grew colder.
Her speech helped to rekindle hope in the hearts of everyone present.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to kindle - to start a fire; to inspire an emotion

Etymology

From re- (again) + kindle, from Old Norse *kynda* (to set fire to).
