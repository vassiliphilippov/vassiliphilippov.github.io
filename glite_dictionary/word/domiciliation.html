domiciliation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɑːmɪˌsɪliˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɒmɪˌsɪliˈeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act or process of establishing a place of residence or official address in a particular location, especially for legal or administrative purposes
establishing official residence or address

Examples

Many multinational companies arrange domiciliation in countries with favorable tax laws.
Legal domiciliation in a new jurisdiction can have important consequences for your assets.
Proof of domiciliation is often required when enrolling children in a local school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: domicile - a person's legal residence

Etymology

From French domiciliation, from Latin domus (house, home).
