abruptly

1. ADV

/ əˈbrʌptli / (AmE), / əˈbrʌptli / (BrE)
In a sudden and unexpected way, without warning.
suddenly; without warning

Examples

The train came to an abruptly stop just outside the station.
He ended the phone call abruptly, leaving her confused.
Our conversation was abruptly interrupted by a loud crash outside.

Synset Examples

he stopped suddenly
suddenly she felt a sharp pain in her side
he decided to go to Chicago on the spur of the moment
he made up his mind suddenly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suddenly - quickly and without warning
  • Antonym: gradually - in a slow, unhurried, or step-by-step manner

Etymology

From Latin abruptus (broken off, steep), via the adjective 'abrupt' + -ly.
