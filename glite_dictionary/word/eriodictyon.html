Eriodictyon

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛri.oʊˈdɪkti.ˌɒn / (AmE), / ˌɪərɪ.əˈdɪkti.ən / (BrE)
A small genus of evergreen shrubs native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, commonly known as yerba santa.
genus of evergreen shrubs (yerba santa)

Examples

Eriodictyon species, such as yerba santa, are valued for their medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
Botanists recently discovered a new variety in the Eriodictyon genus growing in California's chaparral.
Eriodictyon shrubs are known for their aromatic, resinous leaves and clusters of small flowers.

Etymology

From Greek erion (wool) + diktyon (net), referring to the net-like woolly hairs on some species' leaves.
