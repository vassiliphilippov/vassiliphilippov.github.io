sanious

1. ADJ

/ ˈseɪniəs / (AmE), / ˈseɪnɪəs / (BrE)
(of a discharge) containing or resembling sanies, a thin fetid fluid from a wound or ulcer; bloody and serous.
containing or resembling sanies (wound discharge)
medical

Examples

The wound produced a sanious fluid that required frequent cleaning.
The doctor noted a persistent sanious discharge around the edges of the ulcer.
A sanious exudate is often a sign of serious infection in surgical wounds.

Synset Examples

an ichorous discharge
the sanious discharge from an ulcer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ichorous - of, relating to, or resembling ichor (thin watery or bloody discharge)

Etymology

From Latin 'saniosus', from 'sanies' (filthy discharge from a wound).
