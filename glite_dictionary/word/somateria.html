Somateria

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈmætəriə / (AmE), / sɒˈmatəriə / (BrE)
A genus of sea ducks known as eiders, found in northern coastal regions.
a genus of eider ducks

Examples

The Somateria genus includes several species of eider ducks known for their valuable down.
Researchers studied the nesting habits of Somateria in Arctic coastal regions.
Birdwatchers often hope to spot members of the Somateria genus during migration season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eider - a large sea duck of northern coasts; common name of birds in Somateria genus

Etymology

From Greek sōma (body) and erion (wool), alluding to the downy feathers.

2. NOUN

/ soʊˈmætiəriə / (AmE), / səʊˈmætɪəriə / (BrE)
A genus of sea ducks known as eiders, found in the northern hemisphere and noted for their soft down feathers.
genus of eider ducks

Examples

The Somateria genus includes several species of eider ducks known for their warm down.
Researchers observed a flock of Somateria near the Arctic shoreline.
Members of the Somateria genus are important to both ecological studies and the down industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eider - a species of large sea duck (Somateria mollissima)

Etymology

From Greek sōma (body) + erion (wool), referring to the ducks' down feathers.
