nonfunctional

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˈfʌŋkʃənl̩ / (AmE), / nɒnˈfʌŋkʃənl / (BrE)
Not working properly or not having any useful function.
not working; lacking a function

Examples

The printer has been nonfunctional since last week and needs repair.
This gene is considered nonfunctional due to a critical mutation.
The museum displays a nonfunctional replica of an ancient clock.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: functional - working properly; having a function

Etymology

From prefix 'non-' meaning 'not' + 'functional.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01094686-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌnɑnˌfʌŋkʃəˈnəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌfʌŋkʃəˈnəl / (BrE)
not working or unable to perform its normal function; not functional.
not working or unable to function

Examples

The old television is completely nonfunctional and can't be repaired.
The hospital replaced the nonfunctional equipment to improve patient care.
A nonfunctional website can frustrate users and hurt business.

Synset Examples

a malfunctioning valve

Related Terms

  • Antonym: functional - able to perform its regular function; working properly
  • Synonym: malfunctioning - not working or operating properly

Etymology

From prefix 'non-' (not) + 'functional' (relating to function).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01095835-a
Synset POS: a
