catatonic

1. ADJ

/ ˌkætəˈtɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˌkætəˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
Characterized by or relating to catatonia, especially showing muscular rigidity or extreme lack of movement in the limbs.
showing catatonia (muscular rigidity or immobility)
medical

Examples

After the traumatic incident, he became catatonic and remained motionless for hours.
The patient exhibited catatonic symptoms, barely responding to external stimuli.
In the movie, a character enters a catatonic state after witnessing a shocking event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catatonia - a state of psycho-motor immobility and behavioral abnormality

Etymology

From Greek 'katatonia', from 'katatonos' (stretching tight), used in psychiatry since the late 19th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˌkætəˈtɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˌkætəˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
in a state of immobility and unresponsiveness, often as a result of illness or shock
unresponsive and immobile state
medical

Examples

After the traumatic event, she was catatonic and barely spoke for days.
The patient appeared catatonic, staring blankly at the wall without moving.
He sat in a catatonic state after hearing the unexpected news.

Synset Examples

The medication left him feeling catatonic for hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unresponsive - not reacting or responding to stimuli
  • Antonym: alert - quick to notice and respond to situations

Etymology

From Greek katatonos (stretching tight), from kata- (down) + tonos (tone).
