flat-footed

1. ADJ

/ flæt ˈfʊtɪd / (AmE), / flæt ˈfʊtɪd / (BrE)
not prepared for something, and therefore unable to react quickly or effectively
unprepared and slow to react

Examples

The surprise question in the interview left her flat-footed and unsure how to answer.
The storm caught the entire town flat-footed, causing significant unpreparedness.
He was flat-footed when asked to explain the missing documents.

Synset Examples

the new product caught their competitors flat-footed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unprepared - not ready for something; not equipped or forewarned
  • Antonym: ready - prepared and able to respond quickly
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ flæt ˈfʊt̬ ɪd / (AmE), / flæt ˈfʊt ɪd / (BrE)
Standing or moving with the soles of the feet flat on the ground, not on the toes.
with feet flat on the ground

Examples

The children were told to stand flat-footed during the performance, not on their toes.
She walked flat-footed across the room, making no attempt to sneak quietly.
In martial arts, remaining flat-footed can make you less agile.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01035060-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌflætˈfʊtɪd / (AmE), / ˌflætˈfʊtɪd / (BrE)
(of an action or statement) marked by firmness, directness, or bluntness; forthright and explicit, especially in refusing or stating an opinion.
firm and explicit; forthright

Examples

The manager gave a flat-footed reply, leaving no room for negotiation.
Her flat-footed stance on policy changes surprised everyone at the meeting.
He issued a flat-footed denial when asked about the allegations.

Synset Examples

a flat-footed refusal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forthright - direct and outspoken; straightforward and honest
  • Antonym: evasive - avoiding giving a clear answer or statement

Etymology

Originally from the literal 'having flat feet', extended metaphorically to mean 'direct, blunt, or unambiguous'.

Source: WordNet

4. ADJ

/ flæt ˈfʊtɪd / (AmE), / flæt ˈfʊtɪd / (BrE)
having broad, flat feet that typically turn outward
having broad, flat feet

Examples

The old boxer walked with a distinctive flat-footed gait after years in the ring.
Children who are flat-footed may need special shoes for support.
Her flat-footed stance made her easy to recognize at dance rehearsals.

Synset Examples

a slow flat-footed walk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flatfooted - having broad, flat feet (alternative spelling)
  • Antonym: arched - having a high arch in the foot (opposite physical trait)
