plankton

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæŋk.tən / (AmE), / ˈplæŋk.tən / (BrE)
the small plant and animal organisms that float or drift in large numbers in fresh or salt water, forming a crucial part of aquatic ecosystems
tiny aquatic organisms (plant or animal)

Examples

During spring, plankton populations explode, providing food for many fish species.
Whales feed by filtering plankton from vast volumes of ocean water.
Scientists study plankton to monitor the health of marine environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zooplankton - animal component of plankton
  • Synonym: phytoplankton - plant (photosynthetic) component of plankton

Etymology

From Greek 'planktos' meaning 'wandering' or 'drifting'.
