kernel

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɜrnəl / (AmE), / ˈkɜːnəl / (BrE)
a single whole grain of a cereal, such as wheat, corn, or rice
single grain of a cereal

Examples

He inspected each kernel of wheat for defects.
The popcorn exploded from a small, hard kernel to a fluffy snack.
Each kernel of corn is packed with nutrients.

Synset Examples

a kernel of corn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grain - a small, hard, dry seed produced by cereal plants

Etymology

From Old English 'cyrnel', diminutive of 'corn' (grain).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-12177573-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɝː.nəl / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.nəl / (BrE)
the inner and usually edible part of a seed, grain, nut, or fruit stone, often found after removing the outer shell or husk
edible inner part of a seed or nut

Examples

She cracked open the walnut to extract the kernel inside.
The sunflower kernels are roasted and salted before packaging.
Each peach pit contains a single kernel that is not usually eaten.

Synset Examples

black walnut kernels are difficult to get out of the shell

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seed - reproductive part of a plant
  • Synonym: nut - a hard-shelled fruit with an edible kernel

Etymology

From Old English 'cyrnel' (seed, pip), diminutive of 'corn' (grain).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13157787-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɝː.nəl / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.nəl / (BrE)
the central, most important, or essential part of something, especially of an idea, experience, or argument.
core or essential part

Examples

The kernel of his argument was that everyone deserves equal opportunity.
After hours of discussion, we finally reached the kernel of the issue.
She tried to get past the details and focus on the kernel of the matter.

Synset Examples

the gist of the prosecutor's argument
the heart and soul of the Republican Party
the nub of the story

Related Terms

  • Synonym: core - the central, most important part of something
  • Synonym: essence - the intrinsic nature or indispensable quality of something, especially something abstract
  • Antonym: periphery - the outer limits or edge of an area or object

Etymology

From Old English 'cyrnel' (seed, kernel), diminutive of 'corn' (grain).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
