Yasser Arafat

1. PROPN

/ ˈjɑːsər ˈærəˌfæt / (AmE), / ˈjæsə ˈærəˌfæt / (BrE)
(1929–2004) Palestinian statesman and leader, chairman of the Palestine Liberation Organization from 1969 to 2004, recognized as a key figure in the Palestinian national movement.
Palestinian statesman and PLO leader (1929–2004)

Examples

Yasser Arafat played a central role in the peace negotiations between Israel and Palestine in the 1990s.
Many international leaders met with Yasser Arafat to discuss the future of the Middle East.
The Nobel Peace Prize was awarded to Yasser Arafat in 1994 along with Yitzhak Rabin and Shimon Peres.
