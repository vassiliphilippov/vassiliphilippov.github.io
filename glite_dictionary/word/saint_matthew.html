Saint Matthew

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈmæθ.ju / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈmæθ.juː / (BrE)
An apostle of Jesus in the New Testament, traditionally regarded as the author of the first Gospel.
apostle and Gospel author

Examples

The Gospel according to Saint Matthew is the first book of the New Testament.
Many churches are named after Saint Matthew to honor his legacy.
Saint Matthew is often depicted holding a book or a pen in religious art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Matthew - the apostle and Gospel writer; Saint Matthew
