coxsackievirus

1. NOUN

/ kɑkˈsækiˌvaɪrəs / (AmE), / kɒkˈsækiˌvʌɪərəs / (BrE)
a group of enteroviruses that cause various diseases, sometimes resembling poliomyelitis but typically without paralysis
group of enteroviruses causing disease
medical

Examples

Recent outbreaks of coxsackievirus have caused hand, foot, and mouth disease among children.
Researchers are studying the effects of coxsackievirus infections on the heart.
The pediatrician ordered tests to confirm whether the illness was caused by a coxsackievirus.

Etymology

Named after Coxsackie, a town in New York, where the virus was first identified.
