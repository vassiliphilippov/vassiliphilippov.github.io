to whup

1. VERB

/ wʌp / (AmE), / wʌp / (BrE)
to defeat someone decisively or to outperform someone completely, especially in a competition or fight
defeat decisively, outperform completely
informal american

Examples

The home team managed to whup their rivals, winning by over twenty points.
If you think you can whup me at pool, you're on!
She always talks big, but rarely actually whups anyone in debate.

Synset Examples

Let’s see if you can whup me in chess today.
He managed to whup the competition easily.
I’ll whup you in that game, just wait!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beat - to defeat someone in a contest or competition
  • Antonym: to lose - to be defeated in a contest or competition

Etymology

Variant of 'whip', influenced by American dialect and pronunciation.
