to obnubilate

1. VERB

/ ɑbˈnuːbɪˌleɪt / (AmE), / ɒbˈnjuːbɪleɪt / (BrE)
to make something less clear or less visible; to obscure or cloud over
make unclear or obscure

Examples

Dense fog can obnubilate the coastline, making navigation dangerous for ships.
Emotional turmoil may obnubilate clear thinking and sound judgment.
Soot from the old factory would regularly obnubilate the afternoon sun.

Synset Examples

The stars are obscured by the clouds
the big elm tree obscures our view of the valley

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obscure - to make something difficult to see or understand
  • Antonym: to clarify - to make something clearer or easier to understand

Etymology

From Latin obnūbilāre (to cloud over), from ob- (over) + nūbilāre (to cloud), from nūbilus (cloudy).

2. VERB

/ əbˈnuːbəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ɒbˈnjuːbɪleɪt / (BrE)
to make something unclear, indistinct, or blurred; to cloud or obscure mentally or visually
make unclear or obscure

Examples

The dense fog obnubilated the outlines of the distant mountains.
Complex jargon can obnubilate even the simplest message.
Political rhetoric sometimes obnubilates real issues beneath a cloud of confusion.

Synset Examples

Her remarks confused the debate
Their words obnubilate their intentions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obscure - to make difficult to see or understand
  • Antonym: to clarify - to make clear or easier to understand

Etymology

From Latin obnubilare, from ob- (over) + nubilare (to cloud).
