rivulet

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪv.jə.lət / (AmE), / ˈrɪv.jʊ.lət / (BrE)
a very small stream of water
a small stream of water

Examples

A narrow rivulet of water trickled down the side of the mountain.
During the rainstorm, rivulets formed on the window panes and raced downward.
She watched a rivulet meander through the lush green meadow.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: stream - a continuous flow of water in a natural channel
  • Synonym: brook - a small natural stream, often smaller than a creek

Etymology

From Latin *rivulus* (small stream), diminutive of *rivus* (stream).
