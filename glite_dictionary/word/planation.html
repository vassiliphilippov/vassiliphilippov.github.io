1. NOUN

/ plæˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / plæˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of erosion by which a nearly level surface is formed, usually by the gradual wearing away of rock or soil.
process of forming a level surface by erosion

Examples

Over millions of years, the river contributed to the planation of the entire valley.
Geologists study planation surfaces to understand an area's erosional history.
Wind can also play a significant role in the planation of desert landscapes.

Etymology

From Latin 'planatio' (a making level), from 'planus' (flat).
