peyote

1. NOUN

/ peɪˈoʊti / (AmE), / peɪˈəʊti / (BrE)
A small, spineless, globe-shaped cactus native to Mexico and the southwestern United States, known for its psychoactive properties; source of mescal buttons.
small spineless psychoactive cactus

Examples

The traditional ceremony used peyote for spiritual insight.
Researchers study the effects of peyote on the human brain.
Some indigenous tribes consider peyote a sacred plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mescal button - the dried disc-shaped top of the peyote cactus used as a hallucinogen

Etymology

From Spanish 'peyote,' from Nahuatl 'peyotl.'

2. NOUN

/ peɪˈoʊti / (AmE), / pɪˈəʊti / (BrE)
A hallucinogenic alkaloid that is the active ingredient found in the mescal buttons of the peyote cactus.
hallucinogenic alkaloid in peyote cactus

Examples

The Native American church uses peyote as a sacrament in its ceremonies.
Scientists have studied the effects of peyote on brain chemistry.
She described vivid visions brought on by the ingestion of peyote.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mescaline - the main hallucinogenic component of peyote

Etymology

From Nahuatl *peyotl* (cactus).
