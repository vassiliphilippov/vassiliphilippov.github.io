contemplative

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtɛm.plə.tɪv / (AmE), / kənˈtɛm.plə.tɪv / (BrE)
a person who spends a lot of time in quiet thought, especially for religious or spiritual reasons
person devoted to contemplation

Examples

The contemplative retired to a quiet monastery to focus on prayer and reflection.
Many Buddhists admire the way of life chosen by a contemplative.
She lived as a contemplative, dedicating her days to silence and meditation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meditator - a person who meditates, especially as a regular practice

Etymology

From Latin contemplativus (given to contemplation), from contemplari (to contemplate).

2. ADJ

/ kənˈtɛm.pləˌtɪv, ˈkɑn.təmˌpleɪ.tɪv / (AmE), / kənˈtɛm.plə.tɪv, ˈkɒn.təm.plə.tɪv / (BrE)
involving or given to deep or serious thought, especially in a reflective or meditative way
deeply thoughtful; reflective

Examples

She became contemplative after reading the philosophical essay, sitting quietly for hours.
His contemplative nature led him to pursue meditation and long walks in the garden.
Looking at the old photographs, Maria grew contemplative, reflecting on her childhood memories.

Synset Examples

Byron lives on not only in his poetry, but also in his creation of the ‘Byronic hero’ - the persona of a brooding melancholy young man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thoughtful - absorbed in thought; considerate

Etymology

From Latin contemplativus, from contemplari (to observe, consider).
