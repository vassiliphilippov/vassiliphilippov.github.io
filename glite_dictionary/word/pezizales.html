Pezizales

1. NOUN

/ pɛˌzaɪˈzeɪliːz / (AmE), / pɛzɪˈzɑːliːz / (BrE)
an order of mostly saprophytic fungi characterized by cup-shaped fruiting bodies (ascocarps)
order of cup fungi

Examples

Many members of the Pezizales are found growing on decaying wood in forests.
Some Pezizales species are important in nutrient cycling due to their saprophytic lifestyle.
The characteristic cup-like shape of the Pezizales fruiting bodies makes them easy to identify.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cup fungus - fungus with a cup-shaped fruiting body

Etymology

From New Latin, from genus Peziza (a type of cup fungus) and -ales (denoting orders).
