stuntedness

1. NOUN

/ ˈstʌntɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈstʌntɪdnəs / (BrE)
the condition of being abnormally short or small in stature, typically as a result of lack of growth or development
condition of abnormally short stature

Examples

The doctor's report highlighted the stuntedness of the child's growth compared to other children of the same age.
Poor nutrition during childhood can lead to stuntedness that may persist into adulthood.
Researchers are studying the causes of stuntedness in communities affected by famine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shortness - the condition of being short in height

Etymology

From 'stunted', meaning prevented from growing or developing properly, with the suffix '-ness' forming a noun.
