hunch

1. NOUN

/ hʌntʃ / (AmE), / hʌntʃ / (BrE)
a feeling or guess based on intuition rather than known facts
an intuitive feeling or suspicion

Examples

I had a hunch that he wasn't telling the whole truth.
Her hunch about the weather proved to be correct.
Even without evidence, the detective followed his hunch and solved the case.

Synset Examples

he had an intuition that something had gone wrong

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intuition - the ability to understand something instinctively without conscious reasoning
  • Synonym: suspicion - a feeling or belief that something is likely or true
  • Antonym: certainty - something that is known to be true or sure
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ hʌntʃ / (AmE), / hʌntʃ / (BrE)
the act or posture of bending one's body into a humped or stooped position
the act of bending into a stooped position

Examples

She sat with a hunch in her back, staring at the floor.
After hours of gardening, his hunch became more pronounced.
The old man walked with a hunch as he crossed the street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stoop - the act of bending one's head or body forward and downward
  • Antonym: to straighten - to make or become straight, the opposite of hunching
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00406935-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ hʌntʃ / (AmE), / hʌntʃ / (BrE)
to bend forward and draw the shoulders forward, causing the back to round
bend forward with rounded shoulders

Examples

He hunched over his desk, concentrating on the paperwork in front of him.
The old man hunched his shoulders against the cold wind as he walked down the street.
She hunched forward to get a better look at the tiny writing on the page.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slouch - to stand, move, or sit in a lazy, drooping way

Etymology

Origin unknown; first recorded in the 18th century.
