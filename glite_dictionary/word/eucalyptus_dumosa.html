Eucalyptus dumosa

1. NOUN

/ juːˌkʌlɪptəs duːˈmoʊsə / (AmE), / juːˌkælɪptəs djuːˈməʊsə / (BrE)
a species of small, shrubby mallee eucalyptus native to Australia, known for its bushy growth and adaptability to dry environments
a small, shrubby mallee eucalyptus species

Examples

The dense foliage of Eucalyptus dumosa provides excellent shelter for native wildlife.
Many Australian farmers plant Eucalyptus dumosa to prevent soil erosion in arid regions.
Botanists studied the unique adaptations of Eucalyptus dumosa to survive in harsh climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mallee - a type of multi-stemmed eucalyptus shrub or small tree

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Eucalyptus' (from Greek 'eu' meaning 'well' and 'kalyptos' meaning 'covered'); species epithet 'dumosa' from Latin for 'bushy' or 'full of bushes'.
