trackable

1. ADJ

/ ˈtræk.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈtræk.ə.bəl / (BrE)
able to be found, followed, or monitored by tracking.
able to be tracked or traced

Examples

The package was trackable throughout its journey thanks to the online tracking system.
In many wildlife studies, animals are fitted with trackable collars to monitor their movements.
The app generates a trackable link so users can follow the delivery in real time.

Synset Examples

a traceable riverbed
the traceable course of an ancient wall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traceable - able to be followed or discovered by tracking evidence
  • Antonym: untraceable - unable to be traced or tracked

Etymology

From 'track' + '-able', patterned after similar adjectives like 'traceable'.
