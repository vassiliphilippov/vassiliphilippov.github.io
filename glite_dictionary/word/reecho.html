to reecho

1. VERB

/ riˈɛkoʊ / (AmE), / riːˈekəʊ / (BrE)
(of a sound) to be repeated or reflected back like an echo; to echo again.
to echo or be echoed again

Examples

The cheers in the stadium reechoed throughout the night.
Her laughter reechoed down the marble hallway, lingering long after she was gone.
Thunder reechoed between the mountains, making the whole valley vibrate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: echo - to reflect a sound back; to repeat a sound

Etymology

From re- (again) + echo, meaning 'to echo again.'

2. VERB

/ riˈɛkoʊ / (AmE), / riːˈɛkəʊ / (BrE)
(of a sound) to be echoed again, or to send back an echo repeatedly; to repeat or reflect a sound that has already echoed.
to echo again or repeatedly

Examples

Their laughter reechoed through the empty hallways long after they had left.
The mountains reecho every peal of thunder during the summer storms.
His words reechoed in her mind as she walked through the silent house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: echo - to reflect or repeat a sound

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 're-' (again) and 'echo'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02188711-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ riˈɛkoʊ / (AmE), / riːˈekəʊ / (BrE)
to echo repeatedly or echo again
echo again or repeatedly

Examples

The sound of the gunshot reechoed through the valley long after it was fired.
Her laughter reechoes in my mind whenever I enter that room.
The old halls reechoed with memories of celebrations past.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: echo - to reflect a sound off a surface; to repeat a sound

Etymology

Formed from the prefix re- (again) + echo.
