Solanum nigrum guineese

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈleɪnəm ˈnaɪɡrəm ɡiˈniːs / (AmE), / səʊˈleɪnəm ˈnɪɡrəm ɡiˈniːs / (BrE)
A cultivated variety of black nightshade with small edible orange or black berries, developed for garden use.
garden variety of black nightshade

Examples

The Solanum nigrum guineese in the vegetable garden produced abundant orange berries this summer.
Many communities use Solanum nigrum guineese for its edible fruit and nutritional leaves.
Researchers are studying Solanum nigrum guineese as a potential crop for improving food security in West Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black nightshade - common nightshade plant with black berries (Solanum nigrum)

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Solanum' is the genus name (from Latin), 'nigrum' means 'black' in Latin, and 'guineese' refers to Guinea or West African origin.
