Dacrydium laxifolium

1. NOUN

/ ˌdækriˈdiːəm lækˈsaɪˌfəʊliəm / (AmE), / ˌdakrɪˈdiːəm lækˈsɪfəʊliəm / (BrE)
A low-growing or prostrate shrub with slender, trailing branches, native to New Zealand.
New Zealand shrub species

Examples

The Dacrydium laxifolium thrives in the alpine regions of New Zealand.
Botanists study Dacrydium laxifolium to understand plant adaptation in harsh climates.
Gardeners value Dacrydium laxifolium for its attractive, trailing branches and resilience.

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Dacrydium' and species name 'laxifolium', meaning 'with loose leaves'.
