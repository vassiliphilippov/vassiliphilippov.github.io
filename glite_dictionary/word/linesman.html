linesman

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪnzmən / (AmE), / ˈlaɪnzmən / (BrE)
an official in sports such as tennis, soccer, or football, who assists the referee, especially by indicating when the ball or puck is out of bounds or when a player is offside
assistant referee in sports

Examples

The linesman signaled that the player was offside.
A heated argument erupted after the linesman ruled the ball out of play.
During the match, the linesman was praised for his quick and accurate decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: referee - the main official who enforces the rules in sports

Etymology

From 'line' + 'man', referring to the official's duty along the sideline.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪnz.mən / (AmE), / ˈlaɪnz.mən / (BrE)
a person, especially a man, whose job is to install or repair electrical or telephone power lines
person who fixes power or telephone lines

Examples

The linesman worked through the night to restore power after the storm.
She called the utility company, and a linesman arrived an hour later to check the wires.
Wearing a safety harness, the linesman climbed the pole to repair the damaged cable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lineworker - a person who installs and repairs electrical or telephone lines

Etymology

From 'line' (referring to wires) + 'man'.

Synset POS: n
