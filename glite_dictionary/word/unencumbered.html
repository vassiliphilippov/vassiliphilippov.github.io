unencumbered

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪnˈkʌmbɚd / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪnˈkʌmbəd / (BrE)
not burdened with cares, responsibilities, or obligations; free from hindrance or restraint.
free from burdens or responsibilities

Examples

She enjoyed a truly unencumbered vacation, with no work emails or obligations to worry about.
Their decision was made easier by living an unencumbered lifestyle, free from debts or dependents.
He spoke frankly, unencumbered by concerns about what others might think.

Synset Examples

living an unencumbered life

Related Terms

  • Antonym: encumbered - burdened with heavy responsibilities or obligations

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + encumbered, from French encombrer (to obstruct).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪnˈkʌmbɚd / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪnˈkʌmbəd / (BrE)
not having any burden or impediment; free from debts, obligations, or restrictions.
free of burdens or restrictions

Examples

The property is unencumbered and ready for sale.
She preferred an unencumbered lifestyle, free from unnecessary possessions or obligations.
He inherited an unencumbered estate from his grandparents.

Synset Examples

inherited an unencumbered estate

Related Terms

  • Antonym: encumbered - subject to burden or restriction

Etymology

From un- (not) + encumber (from Old French encombrer 'to block up').
