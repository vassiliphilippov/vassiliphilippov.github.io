gilt-edged

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡɪlt ˌɛdʒd / (AmE), / ˌɡɪlt ˈɛdʒd / (BrE)
having edges that are covered with a thin layer of gold, as with the pages of a book
having gold-covered edges (of books, paper, etc.)

Examples

The rare first edition featured gilt-edged pages that shimmered in the light.
She admired the gilt-edged binding of the antique family Bible.
Collectors often prize books with gilt-edged leaves for their elegance and craftsmanship.

Etymology

From 'gilt' (past participle of 'gild', meaning to cover with gold) + 'edge'.

2. ADJ

/ ɡɪlt ɛdʒd / (AmE), / ɡɪlt ɛdʒd / (BrE)
of the highest quality, value, or reliability; especially used to describe securities regarded as safe investments.
of the highest quality or reliability

Examples

The company only invests in gilt-edged securities to minimize risk.
She has gilt-edged credentials for the position, making her the top candidate.
The painting was sold at a gilt-edged auction, attracting serious collectors from around the world.

Synset Examples

gilt-edged securities
gilt-edged credentials

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue-chip - used to describe high-quality, reliable investments or companies

Etymology

From 'gilt', meaning covered with gold leaf, and 'edged', referring to something having an edge; originally describing books with gold-leafed edges, later extended to securities regarded as safe investments.
