brash

1. ADJ

/ bræʃ / (AmE), / bræʃ / (BrE)
confident in a way that is rude, noisy, or overbearing; offensively bold
confident to the point of rudeness

Examples

The brash young executive challenged his supervisors during the meeting.
Her brash behavior at the dinner party shocked the other guests.
Some people found the comedian's brash style entertaining, while others thought it was rude.

Synset Examples

a brash newcomer disputed the age-old rules for admission to the club
a nervy thing to say

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bold - confident and willing to take risks; showing courage
  • Antonym: timid - showing a lack of courage or confidence; easily frightened
