exaggeration

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˌzædʒəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪɡˌzadʒəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
A statement or way of expressing something that makes it seem better, worse, larger, more important, etc., than it really is.
overstated representation

Examples

His story was full of exaggeration and not entirely believable.
The article's claim that the hurricane was the worst ever was clearly an exaggeration.
Without any exaggeration, the restaurant was the best I've visited this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overstatement - the action of stating something more strongly than it actually is
  • Antonym: understatement - the presentation of something as being smaller or less important than it really is

Etymology

From Latin 'exaggeratio', from 'exaggerare' meaning 'to heap up, exaggerate'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪɡˌzædʒəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪɡˌzædʒəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
A statement that makes something seem better, larger, worse, or more important than it really is; an overstatement.
overstatement, making something seem greater

Examples

The story was full of exaggeration, making the adventure sound far more dangerous than it actually was.
His claim that he could run a mile in three minutes was clearly an exaggeration.
While she tried to downplay her accomplishments, he had a tendency toward exaggeration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overstatement - an exaggerated statement
  • Antonym: understatement - presenting something as being less important or smaller than it actually is

Etymology

From Latin 'exaggerationem', from 'exaggerare' (to heap up, magnify).

3. NOUN

/ ɪɡˌzædʒəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪɡˌzædʒəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something more noticeable or prominent than it usually is, often to create a stronger effect or impression
making something more noticeable or prominent

Examples

His gestures were full of exaggeration, making the performance unusually lively.
The artist used exaggeration in the painting to emphasize the character’s emotions.
There was deliberate exaggeration in her tone to make the story more entertaining.

Synset Examples

the dance involved a deliberate exaggeration of his awkwardness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overstatement - the act of stating something in stronger terms than the facts merit
  • Antonym: understatement - the act of presenting something as being smaller or less important than it actually is

Etymology

From Latin exaggeratio (a magnifying, exaggeration), from exaggerare (to heap up, magnify).
