Carpathians

1. PROPN

/ kɑːrˈpeɪθiənz / (AmE), / kɑːˈpɛːθiənz / (BrE)
a mountain range in central and eastern Europe, extending from Slovakia and southern Poland through Ukraine to northeastern Romania
mountain range in central and eastern Europe

Examples

Many rare wildlife species inhabit the dense forests of the Carpathians.
Skiing in the Carpathians is becoming increasingly popular among tourists.
The winding rivers of the Carpathians provide breathtaking views for hikers.

Etymology

From Latin Carpathia, after the Carpathian Mountains.
