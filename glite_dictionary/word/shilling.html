shilling

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the basic unit of money in Kenya, equal to 100 cents
Kenyan currency unit

Examples

The taxi fare was 300 shillings from the airport to the city center.
He exchanged his dollars for Kenyan shillings at the bank.
A cup of coffee costs around fifty shillings in Nairobi.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cent - the subunit of currency equal to one-hundredth of a shilling

Etymology

From Old English 'scilling', used for various coins in Britain; now adopted as the currency name in several East African countries.
