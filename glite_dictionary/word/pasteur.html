Pasteur

1. PROPN

/ pæsˈtɝ / (AmE), / pæsˈtɜː / (BrE)
Louis Pasteur (1822–1895), a French chemist and biologist renowned for discovering that fermentation is caused by microorganisms and for developing the process of pasteurization.
French chemist and biologist, 1822–1895

Examples

Pasteur revolutionized the field of microbiology with his experiments on fermentation.
The process of pasteurization is named after Pasteur, who first developed it in the 19th century.
Many universities have research institutes named for Pasteur to honor his scientific legacy.

Etymology

From the surname of Louis Pasteur, French scientist.
