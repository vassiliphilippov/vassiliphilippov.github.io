dendroid

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛn.drɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈdɛn.drɔɪd / (BrE)
having a form or branching structure resembling that of a tree.
tree-like in form or branching

Examples

The dendroid growth of some corals makes them look like tiny underwater trees.
The scientists studied the dendroid branching pattern of the fungus.
Mathematicians often use dendroid diagrams to represent certain hierarchical structures.

Synset Examples

arborescent coral found off the coast of Bermuda
dendriform sponges

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arborescent - resembling a tree in form or growth
  • Synonym: dendriform - shaped like or resembling a tree

Etymology

From Greek dendroeidēs, from dendron (tree) + -oid (form, likeness).
