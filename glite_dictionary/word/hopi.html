Hopi

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊpi / (AmE), / ˈhəʊpi / (BrE)
the Uto-Aztecan language spoken by the Hopi people of northeastern Arizona
Uto-Aztecan language of the Hopi people

Examples

The Hopi language is taught in some schools on the Hopi reservation.
Linguists study Hopi to understand its unique grammatical structures.
She learned to speak Hopi from her grandmother.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hopi - a member of the Native American people who live in northeastern Arizona

Etymology

From the Hopi word Hopitu, meaning 'peaceful people'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊpi / (AmE), / ˈhəʊpi / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people living chiefly in northeastern Arizona.
member of a Native American people of Arizona

Examples

Many Hopi still live on the reservation in Arizona today.
The Hopi have a rich tradition of pottery and weaving.
A Hopi elder spoke to the students about the tribe's customs and values.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Navajo - a member of another southwestern Native American people

Etymology

From the Hopi language Hopituh Shi-nu-mu (the peaceful people).
