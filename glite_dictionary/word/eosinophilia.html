eosinophilia

1. NOUN

/ iˌoʊsɪnəˈfɪliə / (AmE), / iˌəʊsɪnəˈfɪliə / (BrE)
an abnormally high number of eosinophils in the blood, often occurring as a symptom of allergic conditions or certain infections
high eosinophil count in blood
medical

Examples

The doctor suspected eosinophilia after reviewing the patient's unusually high white blood cell count.
Allergic reactions are a common cause of eosinophilia.
A blood test revealed persistent eosinophilia, prompting further investigation for possible infections.

Etymology

From Greek 'eos' (dawn, referring to the staining property of eosin), 'philos' (loving), and '-ia' (condition).
