laying

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of producing eggs, especially by birds or poultry
production of eggs (especially by birds)

Examples

The increased daylight hours stimulate laying in chickens during spring.
Researchers studied the laying habits of different bird species.
Egg laying typically slows down as hens get older.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oviposition - the act of laying eggs, especially by insects

Etymology

From the verb 'lay' (to produce eggs), with suffix '-ing' denoting the action or process.
