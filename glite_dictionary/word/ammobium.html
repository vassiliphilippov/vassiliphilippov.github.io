Ammobium

1. NOUN

/ ˌæməˈboʊbiəm / (AmE), / ˌæməˈbəʊbiəm / (BrE)
a small genus of Australian herbs in the daisy family, grown for their papery flowers
Australian genus of flowering herbs

Examples

The silver foliage and bright blooms make Ammobium a favorite in dried flower arrangements.
Gardeners appreciate Ammobium for its resilience and low maintenance needs.
Native to Australia, Ammobium thrives in well-drained sandy soils.

Etymology

From Greek 'ammos' (sand) and 'bios' (life), indicating a plant growing in sandy places.
