abdicator

1. NOUN

/ ˈæb.dɪˌkeɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈæb.dɪˌkeɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person who formally renounces an office, duty, or responsibility, especially a throne
one who renounces an office or throne

Examples

After the king became an abdicator, his brother inherited the throne.
The abdicator faced criticism from those who believed he should have honored his duties.
Historians still debate the motivations of the most famous abdicator in British history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to abdicate - to formally relinquish an office, duty, or throne
  • Antonym: successor - a person who succeeds to an office or position

Etymology

From Latin abdicare (to renounce) via the verb abdicate.
