sundial

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌdaɪəl / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌdaɪ.əl / (BrE)
a device that tells the time of day by the position of a shadow cast by a pointer (gnomon) on a marked surface
device indicating time by shadow

Examples

The ancient garden featured a beautiful stone sundial at its center.
Before mechanical clocks, people relied on a sundial to keep track of time during the day.
The sundial showed that it was almost noon, based on the length of the shadow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clock - a device for measuring and showing time

Etymology

From sun + dial, meaning a dial showing the time by the sun's shadow.
