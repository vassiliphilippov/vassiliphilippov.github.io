respirator

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛspəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / ˈrɛspɪreɪtə / (BrE)
a protective device worn over the nose and mouth or entire face to filter harmful substances or gases from inhaled air
protective mask for filtering harmful substances

Examples

The workers wore respirators to protect themselves from toxic fumes in the factory.
During the wildfire, she put on a respirator to avoid inhaling smoke.
Proper fit is essential for a respirator to provide effective protection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mask - a covering worn over the face for protection or disguise

Etymology

From Latin respirare ('to breathe out').

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛspəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / ˈrɛspɪreɪtə / (BrE)
a device used to provide artificial respiration, especially for patients unable to breathe unaided over an extended period
device providing long-term artificial breathing
medical

Examples

The patient was placed on a respirator after having difficulty breathing on her own.
Strict sterilization protocols must be followed when handling a hospital respirator.
New guidelines recommend regular maintenance of the respirator in intensive care units.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ventilator - a machine that moves air in and out of the lungs for patients who cannot breathe unaided

Etymology

From Latin 'respirare' (to breathe out), via Modern Latin 'respiratorius'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
