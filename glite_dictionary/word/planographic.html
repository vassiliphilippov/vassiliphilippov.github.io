planographic

1. ADJ

/ ˌplænoʊˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌplanəˈɡrafɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving planography; produced by a printing process in which the printing and non-printing areas are on the same plane.
relating to planographic printing

Examples

Lithography is the most common planographic printing technique used by artists.
The museum features several planographic prints from the early twentieth century.
Planographic processes rely on the principle that oil and water do not mix.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: planography - a printing process in which the printing and non-printing areas are on the same plane

Etymology

From planography, itself from 'plano-' (plane) + 'graphy' (writing/drawing).
