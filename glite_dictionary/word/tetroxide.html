tetroxide

1. NOUN

/ tɛˈtrɑksaɪd / (AmE), / tɛˈtrɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound in which four oxygen atoms are combined with another element or elements
oxide with four oxygen atoms

Examples

Osmium tetroxide is used as a staining agent in electron microscopy.
Many metal tetroxides are powerful oxidizing agents.
The reaction produces nitrogen tetroxide as one of its main products.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: trioxide - an oxide containing three atoms of oxygen
  • Degree Higher: pentoxide - an oxide containing five atoms of oxygen

Etymology

From Greek 'tetra-' (four) + 'oxide'.
