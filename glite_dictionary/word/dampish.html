dampish

1. ADJ

/ ˈdæmpɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdæmpɪʃ / (BrE)
somewhat damp; slightly wet or moist
slightly damp or moist

Examples

After the rain stopped, the ground remained dampish for hours.
He noticed a dampish smell coming from the basement.
My socks are still dampish even though I left them near the heater.

Synset Examples

clothes damp with perspiration
a moist breeze
eyes moist with tears

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: damp - slightly wet, often unpleasantly so
  • Synonym: moist - slightly wet; often used in positive sense

Etymology

From 'damp' + suffix '-ish', indicating slight degree.
