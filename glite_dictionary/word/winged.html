winged

1. ADJ

/ wɪŋd / (AmE), / wɪŋd / (BrE)
(literary) moving or capable of moving very fast; as if possessing wings.
moving very fast; as if with wings

Examples

He ran across the field on winged feet, reaching the finish line before anyone else.
The hero arrived like a winged messenger, swift and unexpected.
With winged speed, the news spread through the small town.

Synset Examples

on winged feet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fleet - moving swiftly; very fast
  • Antonym: slow - not moving quickly; moving at a low speed
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00983478-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ wɪŋd / (AmE), / wɪŋd / (BrE)
having wings or appearing to have wings of a particular kind
having wings or wing-like features

Examples

The statue depicted Mercury with winged sandals, ready to take flight.
Our garden is full of winged insects in the summer months.
The legend described winged horses soaring through the clouds.

Synset Examples

the winged feet of Mercury

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wingless - without wings

Etymology

From Middle English 'winged', from 'wing' + '-ed'.
