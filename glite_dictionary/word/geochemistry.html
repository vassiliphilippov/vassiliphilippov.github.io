geochemistry

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiː.oʊˈkɛm.ɪ.stri / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː.əʊˈkɛm.ɪ.stri / (BrE)
the branch of science concerned with the chemical composition of the Earth's crust and the processes that affect it
the chemistry of the Earth's crust

Examples

She decided to specialize in geochemistry after taking an introductory course at university.
Recent advancements in geochemistry have improved our understanding of volcanic eruptions.
The research project focused on the geochemistry of hydrothermal vent systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geology - the science that deals with the Earth's physical structure and substance

Etymology

From Greek 'geo-' (earth) and 'chemistry'.
