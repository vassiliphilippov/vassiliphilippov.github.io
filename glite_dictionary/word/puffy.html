puffy

1. ADJ

/ ˈpʌfi / (AmE), / ˈpʌfi / (BrE)
(of clothing, hair, or a surface) swollen or inflated to give a rounded, full appearance.
swollen or inflated in appearance

Examples

She wore a puffy jacket to stay warm in the winter chill.
His new haircut was unusually puffy at the top, giving him extra volume.
A puffy skirt can create a dramatic silhouette for special events.

Synset Examples

a bouffant skirt

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flat - having a smooth, even surface without raised areas; opposite of puffy

Etymology

From puff + -y, meaning resembling or full of puffs.

2. ADJ

/ ˈpʌfi / (AmE), / ˈpʌfi / (BrE)
(of a part of the body) swollen or distended, especially because of fluid or gas.
swollen or distended (body part)

Examples

His puffy eyes showed he hadn’t slept well the night before.
The doctor examined her puffy ankles, which were swollen due to fluid retention.
After her allergic reaction, her lips became red and puffy.

Synset Examples

hungry children with bloated stomachs
he had a grossly distended stomach
eyes with puffed (or puffy) lids
swollen hands
tumescent tissue
puffy tumid flesh

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swollen - enlarged by fluid or inflammation
  • Synonym: bloated - abnormally swollen, often with fluid or gas
  • Antonym: sunken - having a hollow or depressed surface, as of cheeks or eyes

Etymology

From puff + -y, meaning 'full of air or swollen'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01178109-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈpʌfi / (AmE), / ˈpʌfi / (BrE)
(of wind) blowing in short, intermittent bursts or puffs.
blowing in short bursts (of wind)

Examples

The sailors struggled to steer the boat against the puffy offshore winds.
We decided to postpone the flight due to puffy conditions on the runway.
The clouds drifted quickly across the sky, driven by the puffy spring breezes.

Synset Examples

puffy off-shore winds
gusty winds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gusty - (of wind) blowing in sudden, brief bursts
