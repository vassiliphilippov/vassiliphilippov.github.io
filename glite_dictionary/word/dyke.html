dyke

1. VERB

/ daɪk / (AmE), / daɪk / (BrE)
to enclose or protect an area, especially land, with a dike to prevent flooding or control water
enclose with a dike

Examples

Farmers dyke low-lying fields to protect crops from seasonal floods.
They decided to dyke the riverbank to prevent water from overflowing into the village.
Engineers dyked the marshes as part of a major reclamation project.

Synset Examples

dike the land to protect it from water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dike - to enclose with a dike (alternate spelling)

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *dijk* (dike, embankment).

3. NOUN

/ daɪk / (AmE), / daɪk / (BrE)
A long wall or embankment built to prevent flooding from the sea or to control the flow of water.
embankment or wall to control water

Examples

After the storm, the villagers checked the dyke for any cracks or weaknesses.
The dyke along the coast protected the town from flooding during high tide.
Engineers decided to reinforce the old dyke to ensure it could withstand future storms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: levee - an embankment built to prevent the overflow of a river
  • Synonym: dam - a barrier constructed to hold back water

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *dijc*, *dijk* (dam, dike), related to Old English *dīc* (ditch, dike).
