thereabouts

1. ADV

/ ðɛr.əˈbaʊts / (AmE), / ðeər.əˈbaʊts / (BrE)
At or near that time, date, quantity, or place; approximately that.
approximately that time, place, or number

Examples

He plans to arrive at noon or thereabouts.
The repairs will cost $500, give or take a hundred dollars thereabouts.
She was born in 1975 or sometime thereabouts.

Synset Examples

come at noon or thereabouts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approximately - close to, but not exactly

Etymology

From 'thereabout' + '-s' (adverbial genitive plural).

2. ADV

/ ˌðɛr.əˈbaʊts / (AmE), / ˌðeər.əˈbaʊts / (BrE)
In or near that place or area.
near that place or area

Examples

He stayed in London or thereabouts for several weeks.
You'll find the museum on Baker Street or thereabouts.
I'm not sure of her exact age, but she's thirty or thereabouts.

Synset Examples

he stayed in London or thereabouts for several weeks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nearby - close in distance; not far away

Etymology

From 'there' + 'about,' with the adverbial suffix '-s.'
