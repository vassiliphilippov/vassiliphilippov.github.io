loquacity

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈkwæsəti / (AmE), / ləˈkwæsəti / (BrE)
the quality of being very talkative or tending to use many words
talkativeness; wordiness

Examples

Her loquacity made her a popular guest at parties, as she could keep any conversation going.
Despite his loquacity, he rarely said anything of real importance.
The professor's loquacity sometimes made his lectures exhausting but never boring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talkativeness - the quality of talking a lot
  • Antonym: reticence - quality of being reserved or not speaking much

Etymology

From Latin 'loquacitas', from 'loquax' (talkative), from 'loqui' (to speak).
