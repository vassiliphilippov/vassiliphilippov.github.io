dog-eared

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɔɡ ɪrd / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡ ɪəd / (BrE)
(of a page or object) worn or shabby from frequent use, or having corners turned down, especially of book pages.
pages or objects worn or with folded corners

Examples

The library book was so dog-eared that several pages were almost torn.
She loved her dog-eared copy of the novel, which she had read many times.
His notes were dog-eared, stained, and covered with scribbles after a semester of use.

Synset Examples

a somewhat dog-eared duke … a bit run down
an old book with dog-eared pages

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worn - damaged or impaired from use or age
  • Antonym: pristine - in its original condition; unspoiled

Etymology

From the resemblance of folded-down page corners to a dog's floppy ears.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɔɡ ˌɪrd / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡ ˌɪəd / (BrE)
(of a book or paper) having the corners of the pages turned down, typically from frequent use.
with folded page corners (from use)

Examples

She carried around a dog-eared paperback filled with notes in the margins.
His favorite cookbook is so dog-eared that the title has faded from the cover.
The library requested that no books be returned in a dog-eared condition.

Synset Examples

He marked his favorite quotes in his dog-eared novel.
Her favorite book was so old, it was dog-eared.
I love dog-eared pages; they show a book’s history.

Etymology

Alludes to the shape of a dog's floppy ear resembling a folded page corner.
