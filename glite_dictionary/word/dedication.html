dedication

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛdəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛdɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the quality of being committed or devoted to a task, purpose, or cause
committed devotion to a cause or activity

Examples

Her dedication to the job inspired everyone around her to work harder.
Years of dedication and practice led him to success in the competition.
The teacher's dedication to her students was evident in everything she did.

Synset Examples

his long commitment to public service
they felt no loyalty to a losing team

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commitment - a promise or firm decision to do something
  • Synonym: devotion - profound dedication to a cause or person

Etymology

From Latin 'dedicatio', from 'dedicare' (to declare, devote).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛdəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛdɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the quality of being committed or devoted to a task, purpose, or person with complete and wholehearted fidelity
committed devotion or loyalty

Examples

Her dedication to her work was evident in every project she completed.
With great dedication, the volunteers rebuilt the community center after the storm.
The athlete's dedication to daily training led to her eventual success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commitment - the state or quality of being dedicated to a cause or activity
  • Antonym: indifference - lack of interest or concern; the opposite of dedication

Etymology

From Latin dedicatio (a consecration), from dedicare (to set apart, proclaim, dedicate).

3. NOUN

/ ˌdɛdəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛdɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a short message at the beginning of a book, piece of music, or photograph, stating that it is given or devoted to someone or something
inscription dedicating a work to someone

Examples

The author wrote a heartfelt dedication to her parents at the beginning of the novel.
He composed a piece of music as a dedication to his late friend.
The photograph included a handwritten dedication in the corner.

Etymology

From Latin dedicatio (a consecrating), from dedicare (to declare, devote).
