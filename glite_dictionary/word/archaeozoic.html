Archaeozoic

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːr.ki.oʊˈzoʊ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌɑː.ki.əʊˈzəʊ.ɪk / (BrE)
the geological eon from about 3,800 to 2,500 million years ago, during which Earth's crust formed and the earliest unicellular life appeared
ancient geological eon with earliest life

Examples

Fossils from the Archaeozoic reveal the earliest forms of life on Earth.
Geologists study rocks formed during the Archaeozoic to learn about the planet's early history.
The Archaeozoic era saw the creation of Earth's first stable crust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Precambrian - the time in Earth's history before the Cambrian period, including the Archaeozoic and Proterozoic eons
  • Degree Higher: Proterozoic - the geological eon after the Archaeozoic, from 2,500 to 541 million years ago

Etymology

From Greek 'arkhaios' (ancient) + 'zōē' (life).
