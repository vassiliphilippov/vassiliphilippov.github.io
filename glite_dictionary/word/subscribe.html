to subscribe

1. VERB

/ səbˈskraɪb / (AmE), / səbˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to agree with or support a particular belief, opinion, or philosophy
agree with a belief or opinion

Examples

Many people subscribe to the idea that hard work leads to success.
I do not subscribe to his political beliefs.
The professor subscribed to a theory that was considered controversial at the time.

Synset Examples

I subscribe to your view on abortion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to agree - to have the same opinion as someone else
  • Antonym: to disagree - to have a different opinion

Etymology

From Latin 'subscribere', meaning 'to write underneath, sign'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00807976-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ səbˈskraɪb / (AmE), / səbˈskraɪb / (BrE)
(formal) To sign one's name to (a document), especially to indicate approval or consent.
sign one's name to a document

Examples

After reading the agreement, he subscribed his name at the bottom of the document.
All members are required to subscribe the code of conduct.
The testimony must be subscribed in the presence of a witness.

Synset Examples

She signed the letter and sent it off
Please sign here

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sign - write one's name on a document to indicate agreement

Etymology

From Latin subscribere (to write underneath, sign at the bottom).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00998530-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ səbˈskraɪb / (AmE), / səbˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to apply to buy or agree to purchase shares or stocks, typically during a new issue
apply to buy shares or stocks

Examples

Many investors chose to subscribe for shares in the company's initial public offering.
The firm decided to subscribe 10,000 units of the new stock issue.
She advised her client to subscribe before the deadline to secure the shares.

Synset Examples

The broker subscribed 500 shares

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purchase - to buy something

Etymology

From Latin subscribere (to write underneath, sign).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02304071-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ səbˈskraɪb / (AmE), / səbˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to arrange to receive something, such as a publication or service, regularly by paying in advance
arrange to receive something regularly

Examples

I subscribe to several science journals to keep up with the latest research.
You can subscribe to the newsletter for weekly updates on events.
They decided to subscribe to a meal delivery service to save time.

Synset Examples

We take the Times every day

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unsubscribe - to cancel a subscription to a publication or service

Etymology

From Latin subcribere, from sub- (under) + scribere (to write).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02214527-v
Synset POS: v

5. VERB

/ səbˈskraɪb / (AmE), / səbˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to pay an amount of money regularly as a contribution to a service or organization, often on a continuing basis
pay regularly for service or donation

Examples

Every month, I subscribe to a local magazine to support community journalism.
She decided to subscribe $20 a month to a children's hospital charity.
They subscribe annually to several online educational platforms.

Synset Examples

I pledged $10 a month to my favorite radio station

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to donate - to give money or goods for a cause
  • Antonym: unsubscribe - to stop paying for or receiving a service

Etymology

From Latin 'subscribere' (to write underneath), originally referring to signing a document at the bottom.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02304230-v
Synset POS: v
