to brecciate

1. VERB

/ ˈbrɛʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈbrɛʃieɪt / (BrE)
to break rock into angular fragments or convert into breccia
break rock into fragments

Examples

Repeated earthquakes can brecciate the rock along fault zones.
Geological processes sometimes brecciate limestone, forming distinctive patterns.
Engineers noticed that the impact had brecciated the bedrock beneath the crater.

Synset Examples

brecciate rock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fracture - to break or crack something, especially a rock or bone

Etymology

From 'breccia', Italian for 'broken stones', itself from Latin 'breccia'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00339774-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈbrɛʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈbrɛsɪeɪt / (BrE)
(of rock or geological material) to break up or form into breccia, a rock composed of angular fragments.
form into breccia (geology)
medical

Examples

The intense pressure caused the surrounding limestone to brecciate rapidly.
Geologists observed that the fault had brecciated the underlying rock layers.
Over millions of years, tectonic activity can brecciate even the hardest granite.

Synset Examples

brecciated rock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breccia - rock consisting of angular fragments cemented together

Etymology

From Italian 'breccia' (a kind of stone composed of broken fragments).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01587942-v
Synset POS: v
