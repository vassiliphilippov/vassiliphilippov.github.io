Louis Aragon

1. PROPN

/ lwi aɹəˈɡɑn / (AmE), / lwi aɹəˈɡɒn / (BrE)
(1897–1982) A French writer and poet known for helping bring surrealism into literature.
French writer and poet; surrealist movement figure

Examples

Louis Aragon played a leading role in the development of French surrealist literature.
The works of Louis Aragon continue to inspire poets and writers around the world.
Many critics regard Louis Aragon as a central figure in twentieth-century French literature.
