sub

1. VERB

/ sʌb / (AmE), / sʌb / (BrE)
to act as a substitute for someone or something; to temporarily take the place of another
act as a substitute
informal

Examples

Can you sub for me at work next Friday?
He often subs as a goalkeeper when the regular player is absent.
The coach asked if she could sub in for the injured player during the match.

Synset Examples

The young teacher had to substitute for the sick colleague
The skim milk substitutes for cream — we are on a strict diet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substitute - to act as a substitute for someone or something

Etymology

Shortened form of 'substitute'.

2. NOUN

/ sʌb / (AmE), / sʌb / (BrE)
a submarine, especially a military vessel capable of operating underwater and typically armed with torpedoes
submarine warship (military)

Examples

The navy deployed a sub to patrol the eastern waters.
After months underwater, the sub finally resurfaced.
They detected the enemy sub using advanced sonar technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submarine - a vessel capable of underwater operation

Etymology

Shortened form of 'submarine', from Latin 'submarinus' (under the sea).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04354832-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ sʌb / (AmE), / sʌb / (BrE)
a sandwich made from a long split roll filled with meats, cheese, and other ingredients such as lettuce, tomato, and onion; commonly called a submarine sandwich
long sandwich with various fillings
american

Examples

I ordered a turkey sub with lettuce, tomato, and extra cheese for lunch.
She prefers a veggie sub over any other type of sandwich.
The deli around the corner makes the best Italian subs in town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoagie - another American English term for a similar sandwich
  • Synonym: grinder - Northeastern US term for a similar sandwich
  • Degree Lower: sandwich - two or more slices of bread with a filling

Etymology

Shortening of 'submarine sandwich', so named for its shape.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07713570-n
Synset POS: n
