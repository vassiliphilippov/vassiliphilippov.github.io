to masturbate

1. VERB

/ ˈmæstəˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈmæstəbeɪt / (BrE)
to stimulate one's own genitals for sexual pleasure, often to orgasm; to engage in self-stimulation sexually
stimulate own genitals for sexual pleasure
vulgar

Examples

She admitted that she sometimes masturbates to relieve stress.
Some people feel embarrassed to talk about their decision to masturbate.
He was caught masturbating in his bedroom by mistake.

Synset Examples

The old man wanted to be masturbated by the prostitute

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-pleasure - engage in sexual stimulation of oneself
  • Antonym: to abstain - deliberately avoid sexual activity, including masturbation

Etymology

From Latin masturbationem (the act of self-abuse).

2. VERB

/ ˈmæstərˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈmastəbeɪt / (BrE)
to stimulate one's own genitals for sexual pleasure or gratification, typically to the point of orgasm.
stimulate own genitals for pleasure
vulgar

Examples

Some people feel embarrassed to masturbate, but it is a normal part of human sexuality.
He admitted he would sometimes masturbate to relieve stress after a long day.
Doctors agree that choosing to masturbate is generally not harmful to one's health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-pleasure - to give oneself sexual pleasure; synonym for masturbate

Etymology

From Latin 'masturbari', of uncertain origin.
