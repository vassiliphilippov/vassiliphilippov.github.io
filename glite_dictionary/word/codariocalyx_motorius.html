Codariocalyx motorius

1. PROPN

/ koʊˌdæri.oʊˈkælɪks moʊˈtɔri.əs / (AmE), / kəʊˌdarɪəʊˈkælɪks məʊˈtɔːrɪəs / (BrE)
an erect tropical Asian shrub, also known as the telegraph plant, whose small lateral leaflets rotate and move up and down in response to sunlight
tropical Asian shrub with moving leaves

Examples

The movements of the Codariocalyx motorius have fascinated botanists for centuries.
Researchers studied how the Codariocalyx motorius reacts to different levels of light.
A Codariocalyx motorius can be seen in the botanical garden's tropical plant section.

Etymology

From Latinized botanical nomenclature: Codariocalyx (genus) + motorius (referring to movement).
