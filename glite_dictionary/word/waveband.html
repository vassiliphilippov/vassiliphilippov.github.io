waveband

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪvˌbænd / (AmE), / ˈweɪvbænd / (BrE)
a range of adjacent radio frequencies designated for a particular purpose, such as broadcasting radio or television signals
range of frequencies for radio/TV signals

Examples

The radio station switched to a different waveband to reach a wider audience.
Modern televisions can detect multiple wavebands for better signal clarity.
The emergency services use a dedicated waveband for their communications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frequency band - a range of frequencies within a given band, especially that used for transmitting a signal

Etymology

Formed from 'wave' (referring to radio waves) + 'band' (meaning a range or section).
