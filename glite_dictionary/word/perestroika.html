perestroika

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrəˈstrɔɪkə / (AmE), / ˌpɛrəˈstrɔɪkə / (BrE)
The policy of economic and governmental reform introduced in the former Soviet Union in the 1980s to increase efficiency and openness.
Soviet policy of economic reform (1980s)

Examples

The policy of perestroika dramatically changed the economic landscape of the Soviet Union in the 1980s.
Many historians argue that perestroika played a major role in the dissolution of the Soviet Union.
During the era of perestroika, Soviet citizens experienced both hope and uncertainty about the future.

Etymology

Russian перестройка (perestroika), meaning 'restructuring.'
