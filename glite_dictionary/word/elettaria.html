Elettaria

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɛtəriə / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛtəriə / (BrE)
a genus of plants in the ginger family, including the species that produces true cardamom used as a spice
genus of plants producing cardamom

Examples

Elettaria is widely cultivated in tropical regions for its aromatic seeds.
The most common source of cardamom is the plant Elettaria cardamomum.
Botanists classify true cardamom under the genus Elettaria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardamom - aromatic spice from the seeds of a plant in the genus Elettaria

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name, based on earlier forms in botanical Latin.

2. NOUN

/ əˌlɛˈtɛriə / (AmE), / iˌlɛˈtɛːriə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical Asian plants in the ginger family, including the species that produces cardamom.
genus of plants including cardamom

Examples

The seeds of Elettaria are widely used as a spice in Indian cooking.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the genus Elettaria.
The flavor of Elettaria cardamomum is essential in many traditional recipes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardamom - an aromatic spice made from the seeds of Elettaria cardamomum

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Malayalam 'elattari', referring to cardamom.
