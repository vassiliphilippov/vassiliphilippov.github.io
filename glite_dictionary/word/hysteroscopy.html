hysteroscopy

1. NOUN

/ hɪˌstɛrəˈskɑpi / (AmE), / hɪˌstɛrəˈskɒpi / (BrE)
a medical procedure in which the inside of the uterus is visually examined using an endoscope passed through the vagina
examination of the uterus with an endoscope
medical

Examples

The gynecologist performed a hysteroscopy to investigate the cause of the patient’s abnormal bleeding.
A hysteroscopy allows doctors to diagnose and treat problems inside the uterus with minimal invasiveness.
After the hysteroscopy, the surgeon discussed the results and next steps with the patient.

Etymology

From Greek 'hystera' (uterus) + '-scopy' (viewing, observation).
