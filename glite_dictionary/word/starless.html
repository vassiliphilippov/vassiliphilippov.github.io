starless

1. ADJ

/ ˈstɑrləs / (AmE), / ˈstɑːləs / (BrE)
(of a sky or night) having no visible stars; not starry.
without visible stars, not starry

Examples

We walked home under a starless sky, guided only by the moon.
The poem describes a starless night filled with uncertainty and longing.
After the storm, the sky remained cloudy and starless until dawn.

Synset Examples

dark starless nights

Related Terms

  • Antonym: starry - full of or lit by stars

Etymology

Formed by combining 'star' with the suffix '-less,' meaning 'without.'
