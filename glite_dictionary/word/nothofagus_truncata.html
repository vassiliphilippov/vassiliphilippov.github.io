Nothofagus truncata

1. NOUN

/ noʊˌθɑːfəɡəs ˈtrʌŋkeɪtə / (AmE), / nɒˌθɒfəɡəs ˈtrʌŋkətə / (BrE)
A tall evergreen tree native to New Zealand, valued for its very hard reddish wood.
New Zealand hardwood tree species

Examples

The timber from Nothofagus truncata is highly prized for flooring and cabinetry.
Forests of Nothofagus truncata dominate certain regions on New Zealand's North Island.
Researchers studied the growth rings of Nothofagus truncata to learn about historical climate patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: southern beech - general term for trees of the Nothofagus genus

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus name from Greek nothos (false) + phagos (beech); truncata meaning 'truncated' or 'cut off' in Latin.
