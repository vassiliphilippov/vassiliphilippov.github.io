complementarity

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːmpləmɛnˈtɛrəti / (AmE), / ˌkɒmplɪmɛnˈtarəti / (BrE)
the principle that two different or opposite things can both be necessary or complete each other, especially when together they cover all possibilities
the state of mutually completing or balancing opposites

Examples

The concept of complementarity is fundamental in quantum mechanics and explains certain experimental results.
Many experts argue for the complementarity of traditional and modern medicine.
Color theory often discusses the complementarity of red and green hues.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: opposition - a state of being opposite or contrary
  • Synonym: mutualism - a relationship between entities that benefits both

Etymology

From complement + -arity, modeled after words like similarity.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːmpləˌmɛntəˈrɛrɪti / (AmE), / ˌkɒmplɪˌmɛntəˈrærɪti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being complementary, where two or more things supplement or complete each other
mutual supplementation or dependence

Examples

Scientists often discuss the complementarity of theory and experiment in advancing knowledge.
The success of the project depended on the complementarity of their different skills.
Many traditions emphasize the complementarity between individual freedom and social responsibility.

Synset Examples

the complementarity of the sexes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complementary - serving to complete or enhance; mutual supplementation

Etymology

From complement + -arity, modeled after words like polarity.
