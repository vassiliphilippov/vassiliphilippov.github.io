foxiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑksi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfɒk.si.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being clever and sly, especially in deceiving others
clever slyness; cunning

Examples

She was admired for her foxiness in negotiations, always managing to secure the best deal.
His foxiness became apparent when he cleverly outwitted his opponents.
Politicians are often accused of foxiness when they twist facts to suit their arguments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cunning - skill in achieving one's ends by deceit
  • Synonym: guile - deceitful cunning or trickery
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and sincere

Etymology

From 'foxy' + '-ness', based on the sly reputation of the fox in folklore.
