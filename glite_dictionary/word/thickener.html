thickener

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɪkənɚ / (AmE), / ˈθɪkənə / (BrE)
a substance added to a liquid or mixture to make it thicker or more viscous
substance making liquids thicker

Examples

Cornstarch is a common thickener used in soups and sauces.
Many industrial processes require a thickener to achieve the desired texture.
The recipe calls for a natural thickener instead of artificial additives.

Synset Examples

starch is used in cooking as a thickening

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stabilizer - a substance preventing separation of mixtures
  • Antonym: thinning agent - a substance used to make a liquid thinner

Etymology

From 'thicken' + '-er', denoting something that makes thick.
