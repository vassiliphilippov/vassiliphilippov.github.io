alkaloidal

1. ADJ

/ ˌæl.kəˈlɔɪ.dl̩ / (AmE), / ˌæl.kəˈlɔɪ.dl̩ / (BrE)
relating to or containing alkaloids, a class of naturally occurring organic compounds, usually of plant origin, that have pronounced physiological actions.
relating to alkaloids
medical

Examples

The scientist studied the alkaloidal content of the new plant species.
Several alkaloidal compounds are used in the treatment of various diseases.
Her research focused on the alkaloidal properties of medicinal herbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alkaloid - naturally occurring organic compound containing nitrogen

Etymology

From 'alkaloid' + '-al' (adjective-forming suffix).
