suspensive

1. ADJ

/ səˈspɛnsɪv / (AmE), / səˈspɛnsɪv / (BrE)
Undecided or marked by indecisiveness; involving or causing suspense or uncertainty.
undecided or causing suspense

Examples

The committee's suspensive conclusion left the matter unresolved for another week.
Her suspensive attitude made it difficult for the team to proceed confidently.
Due to its suspensive nature, the case remained open for months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indecisive - unable to make decisions quickly or firmly
  • Antonym: decisive - settling an issue; producing a definite result

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'suspensivus', from Latin 'suspensus' (suspended, uncertain).

2. ADJ

/ səˈspɛnsɪv / (AmE), / səˈspɛnsɪv / (BrE)
(of a situation or event) creating or filled with suspense.
causing suspense or a feeling of uncertainty

Examples

The suspensive atmosphere in the courtroom kept everyone on edge throughout the trial.
She crafted a suspensive tale that held her readers in eager anticipation until the final page.
The music added a suspensive quality to the film's climactic scene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suspenseful - causing a feeling of suspense or uncertainty

Etymology

From Latin 'suspensus' (suspended, doubtful).
