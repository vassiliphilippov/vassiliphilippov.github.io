Bairava

1. NOUN

/ baɪˈrɑːvə / (AmE), / baɪˈrɑːvə / (BrE)
An epithet of the Hindu god Shiva, associated with his fierce and terrifying form, often depicted as a guardian deity.
epithet of Shiva in Hinduism

Examples

In many temples, Bairava is honored with special rituals and offerings.
The legend says that Bairava protects devotees from evil and danger.
During the festival, devotees chanted prayers to Bairava throughout the night.

Synset Examples

the terrible Bairava

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shiva - principal deity in Hinduism, often called The Destroyer

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Bhairava' meaning 'terrible' or 'frightful', an epithet of Shiva.
