androsterone

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.drəˈstɛr.oʊn / (AmE), / ˌandrəˈstɛərəʊn / (BrE)
a weak androgenic hormone produced in the liver and testes, less potent than testosterone
a weak male hormone
medical

Examples

The presence of androsterone can be detected in urine tests.
Scientists discovered that androsterone is less active than testosterone in promoting male traits.
Some supplements claim to influence androsterone levels in the body.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: testosterone - primary male sex hormone
  • Synonym: androgen - any hormone that stimulates the development of male characteristics

Etymology

From Greek 'anēr, andros' (man) and 'stereos' (solid) + '-one' (hormone suffix).
