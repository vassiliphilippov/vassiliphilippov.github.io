devise

1. VERB

/ dɪˈvaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈvaɪz / (BrE)
to plan or invent a complex procedure, system, or plan by careful thought and effort
to plan or invent by careful thought

Examples

The engineers devised a new system to improve efficiency.
She devised a clever way to organize her busy schedule.
The committee is currently devising strategies to attract more members.

Synset Examples

machinate a plot
organize a strike
devise a plan to take over the director's office

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plan - to decide on and arrange in advance
  • Synonym: to invent - to create or design something that has not existed before
  • Antonym: to improvise - to create without preparation

Etymology

From Old French *deviser* (to arrange, to plan), from Latin *divisare*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01654986-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈvaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈvaɪz / (BrE)
(Law) A clause in a will that leaves real estate to someone.
bequest of real estate in a will
legal

Examples

The lawyer explained the devise of the family home to the eldest son.
Under the terms of the devise, Julia received her grandmother’s country estate.
Each devise in the will was discussed and approved by the executor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bequest - a gift left in a will, especially personal property

Etymology

From Old French 'deviser', from Latin 'divisare', meaning to distribute or arrange.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13437414-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ dɪˈvaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈvaɪz / (BrE)
to invent or plan something, especially after careful thought
to invent or plan mentally

Examples

The engineers worked late into the night to devise a new safety mechanism.
She managed to devise a clever solution to the persistent problem.
We need to devise a more efficient way to handle customer complaints.

Synset Examples

excogitate a way to measure the speed of light

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to invent - to create something for the first time
  • Synonym: to conceive - to form an idea or plan in the mind
  • Antonym: to discover - to find something for the first time

Etymology

From Old French 'deviser' (to divide, distribute, plan), from Latin 'divisare' (to divide, distribute).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01635953-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ dɪˈvaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈvʌɪz / (BrE)
(law) To leave or give (real property) to someone by a will.
to give property by will (law)
legal

Examples

She decided to devise the family estate to her grandson in her will.
The property was devised equally among the children after their father's death.
A lawyer can help you devise assets properly according to your wishes.

Etymology

From Old French *deviser* (to divide, distribute by will), from Latin *divisare* (to divide, distribute).

(Law) A gift of real property made in a will.
legal gift of real estate in a will
legal

Examples

Under the terms of the will, the house was left as a devise to her niece.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Old French *deviser* (to distribute by will), from Latin *divisare*, frequentative of *dividere* (to divide).
