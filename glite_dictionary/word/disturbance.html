disturbance

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈstɝːbəns / (AmE), / dɪˈstɜːbəns / (BrE)
electrical or acoustic activity that interferes with normal communication, often causing noise or signal problems.
unwanted electrical or sound interference

Examples

The engineers detected a disturbance in the satellite's communication signal.
Radio disturbance caused the broadcast to be unclear.
Acoustic disturbance from nearby construction made it difficult to hear the conversation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interference - unwanted signal that impedes communication
  • Synonym: noise - random or unwanted signals affecting transmission

Etymology

From Latin 'disturbare', meaning 'to throw into disorder'.
