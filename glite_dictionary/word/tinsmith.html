tinsmith

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪnˌsmɪθ / (AmE), / ˈtɪnˌsmɪθ / (BrE)
a person whose job is to make or repair tinware, such as household utensils made from tin or light metals
someone who makes or repairs tinware

Examples

The tinsmith carefully shaped the metal sheet into a new kettle for the customer.
My grandfather worked as a tinsmith in the village, repairing pots and making lanterns.
In the nineteenth century, almost every town had a skilled tinsmith to supply everyday household items.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coppersmith - someone who makes or repairs things made of copper
  • Synonym: blacksmith - a person who makes and repairs things in iron

Etymology

Formed from 'tin' and 'smith', after the pattern of 'blacksmith'.
