thyroid

1. ADJ

/ ˈθaɪˌrɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈθaɪ.rɔɪd / (BrE)
relating to the thyroid gland in the neck, which produces hormones regulating metabolism and growth
relating to the thyroid gland
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered a blood test to check her thyroid levels.
He was diagnosed with a thyroid disorder that affected his metabolism.
Patients with thyroid problems often experience changes in weight or energy.

Synset Examples

thyroid deficiency
thyroidal uptake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thyroidal - relating to the thyroid gland

Etymology

From Greek 'thureoeidēs' (shield-shaped), after the shape of the thyroid cartilage.

2. NOUN

/ ˈθaɪ.rɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈθaɪ.rɔɪd / (BrE)
A large ductless gland in the neck that secretes hormones regulating growth and metabolism.
gland in the neck secreting hormones
medical

Examples

The doctor examined her thyroid to check for any swelling.
An underactive thyroid can cause fatigue and weight gain.
He was diagnosed with a thyroid disorder after his blood tests.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: parathyroid - a gland next to the thyroid that regulates calcium levels

Etymology

From Greek thyreoeides (shield-shaped), because of the shape of the gland.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05337792-n
Synset POS: n
