Venice

1. PROPN

/ ˈvɛnɪs / (AmE), / ˈvɛnɪs / (BrE)
A city in northeastern Italy, famous for its canals, built on islands in a lagoon and serving as the capital of the Veneto region.
city in northeastern Italy, known for its canals

Examples

Many tourists dream of visiting Venice to see its beautiful canals and historic architecture.
The film festival in Venice attracts celebrities from around the world every year.
As the sun set, the gondolas glided quietly through the narrow waterways of Venice.

Etymology

From Italian 'Venezia', from Latin 'Venetia', named after the ancient Veneti people.
