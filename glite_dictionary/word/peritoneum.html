peritoneum

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛr.ɪ.təˈniː.əm / (AmE), / ˌpɛr.ɪ.təˈniː.əm / (BrE)
the thin, transparent membrane that lines the abdominal cavity and covers most of the abdominal organs in mammals
membrane lining the abdominal cavity
medical

Examples

The surgeon examined the peritoneum for any signs of infection during the procedure.
Inflammation of the peritoneum is known as peritonitis, which can be a life-threatening condition.
The peritoneum plays a crucial role in supporting organs within the abdominal cavity.

Etymology

From Greek *peritonaion* (stretched around).
