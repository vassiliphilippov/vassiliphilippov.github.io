Felicia bergeriana

1. NOUN

/ fəˈliːʃə ˌbɜrdʒəˈriːɑːnə / (AmE), / fəˈliːsɪə ˌbɜːdʒəˈriːɑːnə / (BrE)
A softly hairy herb native to South Africa, bearing flowers with bright blue rays; a species in the daisy family.
South African blue-flowered herb

Examples

The garden's border was lined with Felicia bergeriana, their blue flowers bright against the green foliage.
Botanists admire Felicia bergeriana for its unique, softly hairy leaves and vibrant color.
Growing Felicia bergeriana in well-drained soil can help it thrive and showcase its lovely blooms.

Etymology

Felicia from Latin, used as a botanical genus; bergeriana from the name of botanist or collector Berger.
