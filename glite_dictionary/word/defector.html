defector

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfɛktɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛktə / (BrE)
a person who deserts their allegiance, duty, or organization, especially to join an opposing group or country
person who deserts duty or allegiance

Examples

The soldier was branded a defector after he fled to the enemy's side during wartime.
Several high-ranking officials became defectors and shared sensitive information with rival nations.
She lived in exile for years after being labeled a defector by her homeland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deserter - person who abandons a post or duty without permission, especially in the military

Etymology

From Latin defectus (failure, desertion), past participle of deficere (to fail).
