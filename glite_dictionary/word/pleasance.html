pleasance

1. NOUN

/ ˈpliːzəns / (AmE), / ˈpliːzəns / (BrE)
A feeling or state of pleasure or pleasantness; a sense of satisfaction or delight.
the feeling of pleasure or pleasantness

Examples

After months of hard work, she basked in the pleasance of her well-earned accomplishment.
The tranquil garden filled him with a deep pleasance that words could not describe.
There is a quiet pleasance to sitting beside the fireplace on a cold night.

Synset Examples

he was tingling with pleasure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pleasure - a feeling of happy satisfaction and enjoyment
  • Antonym: pain - physical or emotional suffering, the opposite of pleasure

Etymology

From Old French plaisance (pleasure), from plaisant (pleasing), ultimately from Latin placere (to please).

2. NOUN

/ ˈplɛzəns / (AmE), / ˈplɛzəns / (BrE)
A pleasant, secluded area of a garden, often attached to a large house or mansion, intended for relaxation and enjoyment.
secluded part of a garden near a mansion

Examples

The old manor house was famous for its hidden pleasance filled with rare flowers and winding paths.
After lunch, the guests wandered through the shaded pleasance behind the estate.
She discovered a forgotten pleasance while exploring the grounds, enclosed by tall hedges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garden - a piece of ground for growing flowers, vegetables, or fruits

Etymology

From Old French *plesaunce* (pleasure), from *plaisance*, based on Latin *placere* (to please).
