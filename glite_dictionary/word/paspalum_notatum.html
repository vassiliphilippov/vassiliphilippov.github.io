Paspalum notatum

1. NOUN

/ pæsˈpæləm noʊˈteɪtəm / (AmE), / pæsˈpæləm nəʊˈtɑːtəm / (BrE)
A perennial tropical American grass, also known as bahiagrass, commonly used as pasture grass in arid areas such as the Gulf States.
species of perennial pasture grass

Examples

Paspalum notatum is widely planted as a drought-resistant pasture grass in the southeastern United States.
Farmers often choose Paspalum notatum for grazing livestock in sandy, nutrient-poor soils.
The deep roots of Paspalum notatum help prevent soil erosion in pasture lands.

Etymology

Paspalum from Greek *paspalos* (millet); notatum from Latin *notatus* (marked or noted).
