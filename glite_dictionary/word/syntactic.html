syntactic

1. ADJ

/ sɪnˈtæktɪk / (AmE), / sɪnˈtaktɪk / (BrE)
relating to the arrangement of words and phrases to create well-formed sentences in a language; of or according to syntax.
relating to syntax or sentence structure

Examples

The linguist studied the syntactic structure of complex sentences.
There are syntactic differences between English and Japanese.
Her paper focused on syntactic ambiguity in legal texts.

Synset Examples

the syntactic rules of a language

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syntactical - alternative form of syntactic
  • Antonym: semantic - relating to the meaning of words and language

Etymology

From Greek 'syntaktikos' (arranging together), from 'syntaxis' (arrangement).
