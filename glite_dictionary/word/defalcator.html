defalcator

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfælkeɪtər / (AmE), / dɪˈfælkeɪtə / (BrE)
a person who commits fraud by misappropriating funds entrusted to them
person who embezzles funds

Examples

The accountant was exposed as a defalcator after several irregularities were discovered in the firm's records.
As a notorious defalcator, she was prosecuted for siphoning off thousands from the charity.
Internal controls are designed to detect any defalcator before significant losses occur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embezzler - a person who steals money or property placed in their trust
  • Antonym: trustee - a person appointed to manage funds or property for another

Etymology

From Latin defalcare (to deduct), from de- (down from) + falx, falcis (scythe).
