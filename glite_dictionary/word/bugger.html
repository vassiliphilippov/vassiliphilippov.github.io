bugger

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌɡər / (AmE), / ˈbʌɡə / (BrE)
(offensive, vulgar) A person who engages in anal intercourse, especially a man who does so with another man.
person engaging in anal intercourse (offensive, vulgar)
offensive vulgar

Examples

The term bugger has long been used as a derogatory insult in English-speaking countries.
He was unfairly called a bugger by his classmates, who did not understand the word's meaning.
Some older literature uses bugger in a highly offensive and pejorative sense.

Etymology

From Middle French 'bougre', from Latin 'Bulgarus' (Bulgarian), used pejoratively in reference to heretics accused of sodomy.

2. VERB

/ ˈbʌɡər / (AmE), / ˈbʌɡə / (BrE)
(Vulgar, offensive, originally British) To engage in anal sex with (someone); to sodomize.
to engage in anal sex with (vulgar)
vulgar offensive british

Examples

The vulgar insult originated from an accusation that someone would bugger another man, reflecting old prejudices.
Some archaic laws criminalized those who buggered others, a reflection of historical attitudes.
He was accused, unfairly and cruelly, of having buggered his rival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sodomize - to have anal intercourse with (someone)

Etymology

From Middle English *bugger* (heretic), from Old French *bougre*, from Medieval Latin *Bulgarus* (Bulgarian), due to historical association with heresy.
