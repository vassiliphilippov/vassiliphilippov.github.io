investment

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvɛstmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛstmənt / (BrE)
the ceremonial act of putting on robes or vestments, especially in religious or academic contexts
act of putting on ceremonial robes

Examples

The bishop's investment was held before the cathedral congregation.
During the medieval ceremony, the investment of the new abbot was an elaborate affair.
Her investment with academic regalia marked the formal start of her term as chancellor.

Etymology

From Latin 'investīre' (to clothe), from 'in-' (in) + 'vestīre' (to clothe).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvɛstmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛstmənt / (BrE)
an outer layer or covering of an organ, body part, or organism
covering layer of an organ or body part
medical

Examples

The brain is protected by an investment of membranes known as the meninges.
Certain bacteria possess an investment that resists staining and provides additional protection.
The doctor explained that the tumor had formed beneath the investment of connective tissue.

Etymology

From Latin investīre (to clothe, cover).

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvɛstmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛstmənt / (BrE)
The act of putting money, time, or effort into something, especially a business, with the expectation of achieving a profit or benefit.
the act of investing money for profit

Examples

Making an investment in technology can help a company stay competitive.
They considered real estate a safe investment for their retirement savings.
Her biggest investment was buying shares in a promising startup.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: speculation - buying assets with high risk in hope of significant profit
  • Degree Lower: saving - the act of setting aside money rather than spending it

Etymology

From invest + -ment; ultimately from Latin 'investire' (to clothe, surround).

Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvɛstmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛstmənt / (BrE)
the ceremonial act of officially clothing someone with the insignia of an office, or formally conferring a rank or office upon them
formal ceremony for conferring office or rank

Examples

The bishop's investment was held in the cathedral with great ceremony.
The queen attended the investment of the new knights at Windsor Castle.
During the investment, the official robe and chain of office were bestowed upon her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: installation - the act of placing someone in an official position
  • Antonym: deposition - the act of removing someone from office

Etymology

From Latin investire (to clothe), via Old French investir (to install in office).

5. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvɛstmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛstmənt / (BrE)
the act of devoting time, effort, or other resources to a project or activity with the expectation of achieving a worthwhile result
commitment of time, effort, or emotion for expected benefit

Examples

Her investment in learning a new language opened up many career opportunities.
This relationship required a significant emotional investment from both partners.
Parents often make an enormous investment of time and energy in raising their children.

Synset Examples

this job calls for the investment of some hard thinking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commitment - state or quality of being dedicated to a cause or activity
  • Synonym: expenditure - the act of spending resources, usually time or energy, on something

Etymology

From invest + -ment, originally from Latin investire (to clothe, surround).

6. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvɛstmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛstmənt / (BrE)
money that is put into a business, project, property, or other venture with the expectation of making a profit or obtaining a financial return.
money put in for profit

Examples

She made a wise investment in real estate that paid off after a few years.
Many people see education as an important investment in their future.
The company attracted new investment to expand its operations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speculation - investment with higher risk and potential for quick profit
  • Antonym: expenditure - spending money without expectation of profit

Etymology

From invest + -ment; 'invest' from Latin 'investire' (to clothe, to invest with).
