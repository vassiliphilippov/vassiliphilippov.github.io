unspeakable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈspiːkəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈspiːkəbl / (BrE)
(of something, especially a name or word) too sacred or holy to be spoken aloud.
too sacred to be spoken

Examples

The priests regarded the deity's unspeakable name as too holy to utter.
He shuddered at the thought of mentioning the unspeakable word in the sanctuary.
In her faith, speaking the unspeakable name was forbidden to all but the high priest.

Synset Examples

the ineffable name of the Deity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ineffable - too great or sacred to be expressed in words

Etymology

From un- (not) + speak + -able.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈspiːkəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈspiːkəbl / (BrE)
Exceptionally bad or disgusting; so unpleasant or shocking that it is difficult to express in words.
exceptionally bad; indescribably unpleasant

Examples

The kitchen was filled with an unspeakable smell after the food spoiled.
She endured an unspeakable experience at the hands of her opponents.
His handwriting was so unspeakable that nobody could read it.

Synset Examples

atrocious taste
abominable workmanship
an awful voice
dreadful manners
a painful performance
terrible handwriting
an unspeakable odor came sweeping into the room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atrocious - exceptionally bad or unpleasant
  • Synonym: terrible - very unpleasant or serious
  • Antonym: wonderful - extremely good or pleasant

Etymology

From un- (not) + speak + -able; meaning 'too bad to be expressed in words'.

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈspiːkəbl̩ / (AmE), / ʌnˈspiːkəbl̩ / (BrE)
too extreme, intense, or awful to be described or expressed in words; impossible to talk about or explain.
too extreme to be described; beyond words

Examples

She felt an unspeakable joy when she saw her family reunited.
The survivors endured unspeakable hardships during the conflict.
The view from the mountain was of unspeakable beauty.

Synset Examples

indefinable yearnings
indescribable beauty
ineffable ecstasy
unspeakable happiness
unutterable contempt
a thing of untellable splendor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indescribable - impossible to describe accurately
  • Synonym: ineffable - too great or extreme to be expressed in words
  • Synonym: unutterable - too extreme or awful to be expressed

Etymology

from un- (not) + speak + -able, meaning 'not able to be spoken'

Source: WordNet
