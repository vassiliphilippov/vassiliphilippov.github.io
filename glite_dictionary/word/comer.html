comer

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌmər / (AmE), / ˈkʌmə / (BrE)
a person who arrives, especially one who has just arrived at a place or event
person who arrives

Examples

As a first-time comer to the city, she was mesmerized by its energy.
The awards are open to any comer willing to compete.
Each new comer brought fresh ideas to the group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: newcomer - a person who has recently arrived somewhere

Etymology

From Old English 'cuma' (a guest), related to 'come'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌmər / (AmE), / ˈkʌmə / (BrE)
a person who is likely to achieve success or recognition; someone with a promising future
a person likely to succeed

Examples

Everyone in the industry agrees that she's a real comer with a bright future.
That young artist is a comer in the contemporary art world.
Investors are always on the lookout for the next big comer in technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rising star - a person who is quickly becoming famous or successful

Etymology

From the verb 'come,' referring to someone 'coming into' prominence.

Synset POS: n
