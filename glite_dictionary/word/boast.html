boast

1. VERB

/ boʊst / (AmE), / bəʊst / (BrE)
to talk with excessive pride about one's achievements, possessions, or abilities; to show off
talk with excessive pride; show off

Examples

He loves to boast about his collection of rare stamps.
She couldn't help but boast after winning the award.
They often boast of their accomplishments to impress others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brag - to talk boastfully about oneself or achievements
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance

Etymology

From Old French *boster* (to praise, speak proudly), possibly of Germanic origin.

2. VERB

/ boʊst / (AmE), / bəʊst / (BrE)
to wear or display (something) in a way that shows excessive pride or draws attention
display with pride or show off

Examples

She arrived at the party to boast her designer handbag.
He likes to boast the latest gadgets at every meeting.
They boasted matching team jackets during the tournament.

Synset Examples

she was sporting a new hat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to show off - to display something to attract attention or admiration

Etymology

From Old French *boster* (to praise, vaunt), of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02637740-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ boʊst / (AmE), / bəʊst / (BrE)
an act of talking with excessive pride about one's achievements, possessions, or abilities
an act of bragging or self-praise

Examples

Her greatest boast was winning the national championship at such a young age.
Despite his boast of being the fastest runner, he came in third place.
The company's boast of superior quality attracted many curious customers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brag - an act of boasting or boastful talk
  • Antonym: humility - modesty or lack of pride about one's achievements

Etymology

From Middle English *bosten*, probably from Old Norse *bauta* (to strike, beat).

Source: WordNet
