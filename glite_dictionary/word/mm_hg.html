mm Hg

1. NOUN

/ ɛm ɛm eɪtʃ dʒi / (AmE), / ɛm ɛm eɪtʃ dʒi / (BrE)
a unit of pressure equal to one millimeter of mercury, commonly used in measuring blood pressure and atmospheric pressure
unit of pressure (millimeter of mercury)
medical

Examples

The patient's blood pressure was recorded as 120 over 80 mm Hg.
Normal atmospheric pressure at sea level is about 760 mm Hg.
The new device can measure even slight changes in mm Hg.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torr - a unit of pressure equal to one mm Hg

Etymology

Abbreviation for millimeter of mercury, after the Italian physicist Evangelista Torricelli.
