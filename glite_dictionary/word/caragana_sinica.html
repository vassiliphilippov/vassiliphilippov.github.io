Caragana sinica

1. NOUN

/ kəˌræɡənə ˈsɪnɪkə / (AmE), / kəˌræɡənə ˈsɪnɪkə / (BrE)
A shrub native to northern China, with dark-green glossy leaves and solitary pale yellow flowers.
Chinese shrub with yellow flowers

Examples

The Caragana sinica is often cultivated for its attractive yellow blossoms in traditional Chinese gardens.
In spring, the Caragana sinica blooms, brightening parks with its solitary flowers.
Botanists studied the hardiness of Caragana sinica compared to other ornamental shrubs.

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining genus name Caragana and Latin sinica meaning ‘Chinese’.
