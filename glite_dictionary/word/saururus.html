Saururus

1. NOUN

/ sɔːˈruːrəs / (AmE), / sɔːˈrʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic or semi-aquatic plants in the family Saururaceae, commonly known as lizard's-tails.
genus of aquatic plants (lizard's-tails)

Examples

The aquatic plant Saururus thrives in wetland habitats across North America.
Botanists studied the unique inflorescence of Saururus to understand its pollination mechanisms.
Commonly called lizard's-tail, Saururus is an important part of riparian ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lizard's-tail - A plant of the Saururus genus, especially Saururus cernuus

Etymology

From Greek 'sauros' (lizard) + 'oura' (tail), referring to the shape of the flower spike.

2. NOUN

/ sɔːˈrʊərəs / (AmE), / sɔːˈrʊərəs / (BrE)
A small genus of flowering plants in the family Saururaceae, commonly known as lizard's-tails, native to North America and East Asia.
plant genus of lizard's-tails

Examples

The genus Saururus includes aquatic plants known for their distinctive tail-like flower spikes.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of several Saururus species in wetlands.
The Saururus genus is notable for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.

Etymology

From Greek 'sauros' (lizard) and 'oura' (tail), referring to the appearance of the flower spikes.
