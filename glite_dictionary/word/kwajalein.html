Kwajalein

1. PROPN

/ kwɑː.dʒəˌleɪ.ɪn / (AmE), / kwɑː.dʒəˌleɪ.ɪn / (BrE)
an atoll in the western Marshall Islands, used as a Japanese air and naval base during World War II
atoll in the Marshall Islands

Examples

During World War II, American forces captured Kwajalein from the Japanese in a fierce battle.
The missile testing range on Kwajalein remains a key U.S. military facility in the Pacific.
Many Marshallese people live on islands surrounding Kwajalein Atoll.

2. PROPN

/ kwɑːdʒəˌleɪ.ɪn / (AmE), / kwɑːdʒəˌliː.ɪn / (BrE)
An atoll in the Marshall Islands, site of a major amphibious assault by American forces against Japanese troops during World War II in January 1944.
WWII battle site in the Marshall Islands

Examples

During World War II, American troops launched a major offensive at Kwajalein in January 1944.
My grandfather fought in the battle for Kwajalein and shared incredible stories of the conflict.
The capture of Kwajalein provided a strategic advantage to Allied forces in the Pacific.

Etymology

From the Marshallese name for the atoll; gained international prominence due to the WWII battle.
