dermatitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɜːrməˈtaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌdɜːməˈtaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition in which the skin becomes inflamed, irritated, and often itchy, sometimes resulting in blisters or rashes.
inflammation of the skin
medical

Examples

His doctor diagnosed the rash on his arms as dermatitis.
People with sensitive skin are more prone to developing dermatitis when exposed to certain chemicals.
The baby's persistent itching was eventually found to be caused by dermatitis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eczema - a medical condition causing inflamed, itchy, and red skin; a common type of dermatitis

Etymology

From Greek 'derma' (skin) + '-itis' (inflammation).
