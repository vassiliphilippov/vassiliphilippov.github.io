Cecropia peltata

1. NOUN

/ sɛˈkroʊpiə pɛlˈteɪtə / (AmE), / sɛˈkrəʊpiə pɛlˈtɑːtə / (BrE)
a tropical American tree with large, shield-shaped leaves and hollow stems, often found in disturbed areas or secondary forests
tropical American tree with large leaves

Examples

The Cecropia peltata quickly colonizes open spaces in tropical forests.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Cecropia peltata in disturbed habitats.
Birds and insects often shelter in the hollow stems of the Cecropia peltata tree.

Etymology

From the genus name Cecropia, named after Cecrops, a mythical king of Athens; peltata from Latin 'peltatus' (shield-shaped, referring to the leaves).
