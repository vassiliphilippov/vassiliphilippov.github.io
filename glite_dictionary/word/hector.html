Hector

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛktər / (AmE), / ˈhɛktə / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the leading Trojan prince and warrior, killed by Achilles in the Trojan War.
Trojan prince in Greek mythology

Examples

The epic poem describes how Hector bravely defended Troy against the Greeks.
Many scholars consider Hector an exemplar of heroic honor and loyalty.
In the Iliad, Hector's duel with Achilles is a pivotal moment in the Trojan War.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Achilles - Greek hero who killed Hector in the Trojan War

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἕκτωρ (Héktōr), a personal name in Greek mythology.
