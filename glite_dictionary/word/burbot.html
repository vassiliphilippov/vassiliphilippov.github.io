burbot

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːbət / (AmE), / ˈbɜːbət / (BrE)
A species of elongated freshwater fish in northern Europe, Asia, and North America, related to the cod, with barbel-like feelers around its mouth.
freshwater fish with barbels

Examples

The burbot is most active at night, searching for prey along the bottom of cold lakes.
Fishermen in Alaska prize the burbot for its delicate, white flesh.
A large burbot was caught near the river's mouth during the winter fishing season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cod - a large marine fish with firm white flesh
  • Degree Lower: eel - elongated fish with a snake-like body

Etymology

From Middle French 'barbot', diminutive of 'barbe' meaning 'barbel'.
