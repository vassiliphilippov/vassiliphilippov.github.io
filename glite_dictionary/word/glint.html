glint

1. VERB

/ ɡlɪnt / (AmE), / ɡlɪnt / (BrE)
to shine with a brief or small flash of reflected light, sometimes as if wet or glossy
shine with a brief flash of light

Examples

The sun made the dewdrops on the grass glint in the early morning light.
A hint of gold glinted in her hair when she stood by the window.
His watch glinted as he raised his hand to check the time.

Synset Examples

His eyes were glistening

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gleam - shine with a steady light
  • Synonym: glimmer - shine faintly with a wavering light

Etymology

Probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian glinte (to shine faintly).

2. NOUN

/ ɡlɪnt / (AmE), / ɡlɪnt / (BrE)
a small, quick flash or gleam of light
a brief flash of light

Examples

A sudden glint of sunlight reflected off the windshield.
She noticed a glint in his eyes as he smiled mischievously.
The ring caught a glint of light, making it sparkle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gleam - a brief or faint light, especially one reflected from something
  • Synonym: flash - a sudden, brief burst of bright light

Etymology

Probably from Middle English glenten (to shine), of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse glitra (to glitter).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07426910-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ɡlɪnt / (AmE), / ɡlɪnt / (BrE)
a small, quick flash of light reflected from a surface
a brief flash or spark of light

Examples

A glint of sunlight reflected off her watch and caught his eye.
There was a mischievous glint in his eyes as he smiled.
The diamond gave off a brilliant glint in the afternoon light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gleam - a soft or brief light, especially reflected
  • Synonym: shine - bright light or to emit light
  • Antonym: dullness - the lack of brightness or liveliness

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound or effect of a sudden light; possibly related to Middle English 'glenten' (to shine).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04960473-n
Synset POS: n
