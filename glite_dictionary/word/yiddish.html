Yiddish

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɪdɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈjɪdɪʃ / (BrE)
a language that developed from a dialect of High German, with influences from Hebrew and other languages, and traditionally spoken by Jews in central and eastern Europe; written in the Hebrew alphabet
Jewish language of Germanic origin

Examples

She learned to speak Yiddish from her grandparents, who grew up in Poland.
Several classic jokes and expressions have their origins in Yiddish.
The play was performed entirely in Yiddish with English subtitles.

Etymology

From Yiddish *Yidish*, literally ‘Jewish’ (language), from Middle High German *jüdisch*.
