kenogenesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkiːnoʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌkiːnəʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (BrE)
The introduction, during embryonic development, of characters or structures that were not present in the earlier evolutionary history of a species or lineage.
biological evolutionary innovation in embryos
medical

Examples

The evolution of the placenta in mammals is considered a classic case of kenogenesis.
Some scientists study kenogenesis to understand how new traits arise during development.
Unlike palingenesis, kenogenesis involves the appearance of novel structures not seen in evolutionary ancestors.

Etymology

From Greek 'kainos' (new) and 'genesis' (origin, creation).
