contented

1. ADJ

/ kənˈtɛntɪd / (AmE), / kənˈtɛntɪd / (BrE)
feeling or showing satisfaction with one's possessions, status, or situation; not desiring more or anything different
feeling or showing satisfaction; satisfied

Examples

She gave a contented sigh as she relaxed by the fireplace.
The cat looked contented after finishing its meal and curling up on the rug.
He wore a contented expression that made everyone around him smile.

Synset Examples

a contented smile

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satisfied - pleased and content with what has been achieved or received
  • Antonym: discontented - dissatisfied; not pleased with one's situation

Etymology

From past participle of 'content', from Latin 'contentus', past participle of 'continere' (to hold together).
