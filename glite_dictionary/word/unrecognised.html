unrecognised

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˈrɛkəɡnaɪzd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈrɛkəɡnaɪzd / (BrE)
not acknowledged, identified, or accepted as valid or familiar; not recognized.
not acknowledged or identified
british

Examples

Her talents went completely unrecognised during her lifetime.
The artist's work remained unrecognised until many years after his death.
She walked through the crowd unrecognised thanks to her clever disguise.

Synset Examples

he was unrecognized in his disguise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unrecognized - not acknowledged, identified, or accepted as valid or familiar; American spelling
  • Antonym: recognised - identified or acknowledged as valid or familiar

Etymology

From un- (not) + recognised (past participle of recognise)

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈrɛkəɡˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / ʌnˈrɛkəɡnaɪzd / (BrE)
not having received public acknowledgment or established reputation.
not publicly acknowledged or renowned

Examples

Many talented but unrecognised artists struggle to reach a wider audience.
Despite years of hard work, her efforts remained largely unrecognised by her peers.
He wrote several unrecognised novels before finally achieving literary success.

Synset Examples

short stories by unrecognized writers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: recognized - acknowledged or having a secure reputation
  • Synonym: unknown - not known or familiar

Etymology

From un- (not) + recognised, from Latin recognoscere (to recognize).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01641029-s
