tout ensemble

1. NOUN

/ tuː ɒnˈsɒmbəl / (AmE), / tuː ɒ̃ˈsɒ̃bl / (BrE)
the overall effect or total impression produced by a group of parts considered as a whole
overall effect; total impression

Examples

The tout ensemble of the décor and furniture created a warm and inviting atmosphere.
Her dress was lovely, but the tout ensemble was what captured everyone’s attention.
Critics praised the tout ensemble of the new ballet for its harmony and balance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ensemble - a group considered as a whole

Etymology

From French, literally 'all together'.

2. NOUN

/ tuː ɑnˈsɑːmbəl / (AmE), / tuː ɒnˈsɒ̃bl / (BrE)
The general effect or overall impression of something, especially considered as a whole rather than in separate parts; the totality or entirety of an assemblage.
overall effect; the whole considered as a unity

Examples

The tout ensemble of the exhibition was striking, even if some individual pieces were less impressive.
In fashion, designers strive for harmony, making sure the tout ensemble is aesthetically pleasing.
Critics admired the tout ensemble of the orchestra's performance, noting how well all sections blended together.

Etymology

From French 'tout ensemble' (literally 'all together').
