ominously

1. ADV

/ ˈɑː.mə.nəs.li / (AmE), / ˈɒ.mɪ.nəs.li / (BrE)
In a way that suggests something bad or unpleasant is going to happen; in a manner that is threatening or foreboding.
in a threatening or foreboding manner

Examples

The sky darkened ominously before the storm began.
He paused ominously before delivering the bad news to the team.
The air grew ominously silent as everyone waited for the verdict.

Synset Examples

the sun darkened ominously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ominous - suggesting that something bad is going to happen

Etymology

From 'ominous' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'omen' (sign, portent).
