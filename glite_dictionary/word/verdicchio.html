Verdicchio

1. NOUN

/ vɛrˈdɪkioʊ / (AmE), / vɛˈdɪkioʊ / (BrE)
A dry white Italian wine made from Verdicchio grapes, known for its crisp and fresh character.
dry Italian white wine

Examples

We ordered a bottle of Verdicchio to pair with our seafood dinner.
The crisp acidity of Verdicchio makes it an excellent choice for summer afternoons.
She prefers Verdicchio over other Italian white wines for its refreshing taste.

Etymology

Italian, from the name of the Verdicchio grape variety.

2. NOUN

/ vɛrˈdikioʊ / (AmE), / vɛˈdɪkioʊ / (BrE)
a variety of white wine grape grown mainly in Italy, used to produce crisp, dry wines of the same name
Italian white wine grape

Examples

The crisp acidity of Verdicchio makes it an excellent pairing with seafood dishes.
We toured a vineyard in Marche where Verdicchio grapes are harvested.
Many sommeliers recommend serving Verdicchio chilled to highlight its refreshing character.

Etymology

Italian, from 'verde' (green) referring to the greenish hue of the grape.
