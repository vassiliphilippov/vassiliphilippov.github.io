paleontological

1. ADJ

/ ˌpeɪliˌɑːntəˈlɑːdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌpaliːɒnˌtɒlədʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or concerned with paleontology, the study of prehistoric life forms through fossils
relating to paleontology or fossils

Examples

The paleontological record provides crucial evidence about the evolution of life on Earth.
He published several paleontological studies focusing on dinosaur fossils.
The museum’s paleontological collection attracts both researchers and the general public.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paleontologic - alternative (variant) form of 'paleontological'

Etymology

From paleontology + -ical; 'paleontology' from French 'paléontologie', from Greek 'palaios' (ancient) + 'onto-' (being) + '-logy' (study).
