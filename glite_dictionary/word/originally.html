originally

1. ADV

/ əˈrɪdʒənəli / (AmE), / əˈrɪdʒɪnəli / (BrE)
at first; in the beginning; before now or prior to later changes
at first; in the beginning

Examples

She originally planned to travel alone, but invited a friend at the last minute.
The building was originally a warehouse before it became a museum.
I had originally thought the meeting was tomorrow, not today.

Synset Examples

why didn't you tell me in the first place?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: initially - at the beginning
  • Antonym: eventually - in the end, after a period of time

Etymology

From Latin originalis (of origin), from originem (origin).

2. ADV

/ əˈrɪdʒ.ə.nəl.i / (AmE), / əˈrɪdʒ.ɪ.nəl.i / (BrE)
With reference to the origin, beginning, or earliest state of something.
from the origin; in the beginning

Examples

He was originally from Canada before moving to New York.
The building was originally a factory but is now a museum.
Originally, the plan was to meet at noon, but we changed it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: initially - at the beginning; at first
  • Antonym: finally - at the end; after everything else

Etymology

From 'original' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'originem' (origin).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
