malignance

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɪɡnəns / (AmE), / məˈlɪɡnəns / (BrE)
(medicine) The condition or quality of being malignant, especially a disease that is invasive, progressive, and resistant to treatment, often causing death.
malignant state (medical)
medical

Examples

The malignance of the tumor made it difficult for doctors to treat successfully.
Doctors feared the malignance would spread to other tissues rapidly.
Research is ongoing to find new therapies that can address the malignance of certain cancers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malignancy - the state of being malignant (in medicine, a cancerous growth or its tendency)

Etymology

From Latin malignans, malignantis (present participle of malignare, to act maliciously).

2. NOUN

/ məˈlaɪnəns / (AmE), / məˈlɪɡnəns / (BrE)
The quality or state of being evil in nature or having intense ill will; malevolence.
evil nature; intense ill will

Examples

Her words were full of malignance, intended only to hurt and wound.
The villain's malignance became obvious as he manipulated those around him.
Even in her silence, there seemed to be a hint of malignance in her stare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malevolence - the state or condition of wishing harm to others
  • Antonym: benevolence - the quality of being well meaning; kindness

Etymology

From Latin malignus (bad-natured) via Old French malignance.
