lemongrass

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛmənˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈlɛmənˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A tropical grass with a strong lemon scent, whose stalks and oil are used in cooking, perfumes, and medicines.
aromatic plant used in cooking and perfumery

Examples

The chef added chopped lemongrass to the soup for extra flavor.
Many herbal teas contain lemongrass because of its refreshing aroma.
Essential oils made from lemongrass are popular in aromatherapy.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛmənˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈlɛmənˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a tropical grass native to South and Southeast Asia, with a lemony aroma and flavor, commonly used in cooking and herbal remedies
aromatic tropical grass used in cooking

Examples

She added finely chopped lemongrass to the soup for a fresh, citrusy flavor.
Lemongrass tea is known for its calming properties and unique aroma.
The recipe calls for two stalks of lemongrass, sliced thinly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: citronella - a similar tropical grass used for its fragrant oil
