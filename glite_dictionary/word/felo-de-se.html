felo-de-se

1. NOUN

/ ˌfiːloʊ dɪ ˈsiː / (AmE), / ˌfiːləʊ dɪ ˈsiː / (BrE)
A person who commits suicide; or, in historical legal contexts, an act of deliberate self-destruction (suicide).
person who commits suicide (legal, historical)
legal archaic

Examples

The law once dictated that the property of a felo-de-se was forfeited to the crown.
In historical records, a felo-de-se was denied a Christian burial.
The coroner ruled the death as that of a felo-de-se, following the inquest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suicide - the act of intentionally causing one's own death

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *felo de se* meaning 'felon of himself'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌfɛloʊ dɪ ˈsiː / (AmE), / ˌfiːləʊ deɪ ˈsiː / (BrE)
a person who commits suicide intentionally; formerly used especially in legal or historical contexts.
person who intentionally commits suicide (archaic/legal)
archaic legal

Examples

Historically, a felo-de-se could not be buried in consecrated ground.
The law once treated the property of a felo-de-se differently from other deaths.
He read about the case of a notorious felo-de-se in the old legal records.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suicide - the act of intentionally killing oneself
  • Antonym: homicide - the act of killing another person

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'felo de se' meaning 'felon of himself'.
