avocado

1. ADJ

/ ˌævəˈkɑdoʊ / (AmE), / ˌavəˈkɑːdəʊ / (BrE)
a dull yellowish-green color resembling the flesh of an avocado
dull yellowish-green color

Examples

The walls were painted a soft avocado that felt both retro and calming.
She wore an avocado dress to match the garden party's summery theme.
Many kitchens in the 1970s featured avocado appliances and cabinetry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: olive - a shade of yellowish-green color

Etymology

From Spanish 'aguacate', originally describing the fruit; color name derived from the appearance of its flesh.

2. NOUN

/ ˌævəˈkɑdoʊ / (AmE), / ˌavəˈkɑːdəʊ / (BrE)
A pear-shaped fruit with a rough green or black skin, creamy yellowish-green flesh, and a large seed in the center, often eaten raw or used in salads and dips.
a creamy-fleshed tropical fruit

Examples

I sliced an avocado for my sandwich this morning.
The salad was topped with fresh avocado and tomato.
She mashed the avocado to make guacamole for the party.

Etymology

From Spanish 'aguacate', from Nahuatl 'ahuacatl'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07780823-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌævəˈkɑdoʊ / (AmE), / ˌævəˈkɑːdəʊ / (BrE)
A tropical American tree (Persea americana) cultivated for its large, pulpy green fruits with a single seed.
tropical tree with green fruits

Examples

The avocado grows best in warm subtropical climates.
Farmers are planting more avocado trees due to rising demand.
An avocado can take several years to begin bearing fruit.

Etymology

From Spanish 'aguacate', from Nahuatl 'āhuacatl' (testicle; avocado).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-11727230-n
Synset POS: n
