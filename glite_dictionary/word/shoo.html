to shoo

1. VERB

/ ʃu / (AmE), / ʃuː / (BrE)
to drive away or urge someone or something to go away, especially by calling out 'shoo!' or making similar noises
drive away by saying 'shoo!'

Examples

The farmer tried to shoo the crows away from his crops.
She waved her hand to shoo the stray cat off the porch.
Security had to shoo the reporters away from the building entrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chase - to drive someone or something away by running after them

Etymology

Imitative of the sound people make when trying to drive animals or people away.
