ox

1. NOUN

/ ɑks / (AmE), / ɒks / (BrE)
an adult castrated male of the cattle species, especially Bos taurus, used as a draft animal
castrated adult male cattle used for work

Examples

The farmer used an ox to plow the field each spring.
In many traditional societies, an ox is essential for heavy agricultural work.
The pair of oxen pulled the wagon through the muddy road.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bull - an uncastrated adult male bovine

Etymology

Old English 'oxa', of Germanic origin; related to Dutch 'os' and German 'Ochse'.

2. NOUN

/ ɑks / (AmE), / ɒks / (BrE)
A large domesticated or wild bovine animal, especially a castrated male used for draught work or a member of the genera Bos or Bibos.
a large bovine animal, often domesticated

Examples

The ancient farmers used an ox to plow their fields.
A wild ox is rarely seen in this region today.
The museum displayed the skull of a prehistoric ox.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bull - an uncastrated adult male bovine
  • Synonym: cow - the female of domestic cattle species

Etymology

Old English *oxa*, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch *os* and German *Ochse*.
