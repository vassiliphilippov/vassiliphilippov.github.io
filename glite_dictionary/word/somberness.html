somberness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːmbərnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɒmbənəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being serious, solemn, or gloomy in mood or appearance
state of being serious or solemn

Examples

His voice carried a note of somberness during the memorial service.
There was a distinct somberness in the air after the announcement.
The painting's muted colors reflect the somberness of the artist's mood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solemnity - the state of being serious and dignified
  • Antonym: cheerfulness - the quality of being noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From French 'sombre' (dark, gloomy), ultimately from Latin 'subumbrare' (to shade).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːmbɚnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɒmbənəs / (BrE)
A state of partial or total darkness; the quality of being shadowy or dimly lit.
state of darkness or dim light

Examples

The somberness of the room made it difficult to see anything clearly.
A thick fog settled over the village, cloaking it in complete somberness until dawn.
The artist captured the somberness of the old cellar in his painting.

Synset Examples

he struck a match to dispel the gloom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gloom - partial or total darkness
  • Antonym: brightness - the quality of being full of light

Etymology

From French sombre (dark, gloomy), from Latin subumbrāre (to shade).

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːmbərnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɒmbənɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being serious, sad, or dark in mood or appearance
state of being somber or serious

Examples

The somberness of the funeral was felt by everyone in attendance.
Her voice carried a touch of somberness when she spoke about her old friend.
A sense of somberness lingered in the room after the announcement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gloom - a state of partial or total darkness; a feeling of sadness
  • Antonym: cheerfulness - the state of being noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From French 'sombre' (dark, gloomy) plus the suffix '-ness'.
