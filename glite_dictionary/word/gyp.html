gyp

1. VERB

/ dʒɪp / (AmE), / dʒɪp / (BrE)
(sometimes offensive) To cheat or swindle someone out of money or goods.
cheat or swindle (offensive)
offensive

Examples

The mechanic tried to gyp me on the repair cost, but I noticed right away.
Tourists are often warned not to let vendors gyp them at the market.
She felt angry when she realized the seller had gypped her with a fake watch.

Synset Examples

the cashier gypped me when he gave me too little change

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheat - to act dishonestly to gain an advantage
  • Synonym: swindle - to use deception to deprive someone of money or possessions

Etymology

Origin likely from 'Gypsy', an ethnic slur referring to the Roma people, thus regarded as offensive.

2. NOUN

/ dʒɪp / (AmE), / dʒɪp / (BrE)
an act of cheating or swindling; a fraud or deception, sometimes considered offensive
an act of cheating or trickery
offensive

Examples

He felt it was a total gyp being charged so much for poor service.
The salesman pulled a clever gyp on unsuspecting tourists.
After discovering the hidden fees, customers realized they'd fallen victim to a gyp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swindle - an act of cheating; a fraud
  • Synonym: scam - a dishonest scheme; a fraud
  • Antonym: fairness - freedom from bias, dishonesty, or injustice

Etymology

Possibly from 'Gypsy', considered offensive due to association with ethnic stereotyping.
