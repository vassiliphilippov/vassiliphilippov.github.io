uptick

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌpˌtɪk / (AmE), / ˈʌp.tɪk / (BrE)
a small increase or rise, especially in the level or value of something
a small increase or rise

Examples

We noticed an uptick in website traffic after the campaign launched.
There has been a recent uptick in housing prices in the area.
Analysts reported an uptick in consumer confidence this quarter.

Synset Examples

the up-tick in terrorist activity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: increase - an instance of becoming larger or greater
  • Antonym: decrease - an instance of becoming smaller or less

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌpˌtɪk / (AmE), / ˈʌp.tɪk / (BrE)
a transaction in the stock market at a higher price than the previous trade
stock market rise in transaction price

Examples

After a period of stagnation, the market saw an uptick in trading activity.
The analyst noted a significant uptick in the stock’s closing price.
Restrictions on short selling are sometimes triggered by an uptick rule.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: downtick - a transaction in the stock market at a price below the previous transaction

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'up-' + 'tick,' referring to the upward movement in price.
