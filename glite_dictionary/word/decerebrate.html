to decerebrate

1. VERB

/ dɪˈsɛrəˌbreɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛrəˌbreɪt / (BrE)
to remove the cerebrum from (a human or animal body), especially for medical or experimental purposes
remove the cerebrum surgically
medical

Examples

The laboratory team decided to decerebrate several mice to study neural reflexes.
During the procedure, the surgeon must carefully decerebrate the subject to avoid damaging adjacent tissues.
The effects observed after they decerebrated the animal provided new insights into brain function.

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'de-' (down, away) + 'cerebrum' (brain).
