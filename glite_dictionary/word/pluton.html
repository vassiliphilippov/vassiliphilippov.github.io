pluton

1. NOUN

/ ˈpluː.tɑn / (AmE), / ˈpluː.tɒn / (BrE)
a large mass of intrusive igneous rock that has solidified deep below the Earth's surface
large mass of intrusive igneous rock
medical

Examples

The granite pluton was exposed after millions of years of erosion.
Geologists mapped the boundaries of the ancient pluton beneath the mountain range.
A pluton forms when magma cools and solidifies slowly deep within the earth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: batholith - a very large pluton exposed over an area of at least 100 square kilometers
  • Degree Lower: laccolith - a type of smaller igneous intrusion with a domed top

Etymology

From French 'pluton', based on Pluto, the Roman god of the underworld, referencing formation deep underground.
