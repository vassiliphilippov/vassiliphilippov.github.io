alkaloid

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.kəˌlɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈæl.kəlɔɪd / (BrE)
any of a class of naturally occurring organic compounds, mostly containing basic nitrogen atoms, found mainly in plants and often having marked physiological actions on humans or animals
nitrogen-containing natural compound in plants
medical

Examples

Caffeine is a well-known alkaloid found in coffee and tea.
Scientists study the effects of each plant alkaloid on the human nervous system.
Morphine is a powerful painkiller derived from an alkaloid present in opium poppies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glycoside - a compound formed from a simple sugar and another compound by replacement of a hydroxyl group

Etymology

From German 'Alkaloid', from 'Alkali' + '-oid' (resembling).
