Ostrogoth

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːstrəˌɡɑθ / (AmE), / ˈɒstrəˌɡɒθ / (BrE)
a member of the eastern branch of the Goths, a Germanic people who established a kingdom in northern Italy around 500 AD
member of the eastern Goths; historical group

Examples

The Ostrogoth kingdom played a significant role in the decline of the Western Roman Empire.
Historians have found evidence of Ostrogoth settlements in various parts of Italy.
As an Ostrogoth, Theodoric the Great became king of Italy in the early sixth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Goth - a member of a Germanic people who invaded the Roman Empire

Etymology

From Late Latin Ostrogothi, from Gothic 'austr' (east) and 'Gothi' (Goths).
