gingivitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɪndʒɪˈvaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌdʒɪndʒɪˈvaɪtɪs / (BrE)
Inflammation of the gums, often caused by bacterial infection and characterized by redness, swelling, and bleeding.
inflammation of the gums
medical

Examples

Poor dental hygiene can lead to gingivitis and more serious gum diseases.
Regular flossing helps prevent gingivitis by removing plaque from the gumline.
The dentist diagnosed her with early-stage gingivitis during her checkup.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: periodontitis - severe gum disease extending to the tooth-supporting bone
  • Degree Lower: healthy gums - gums with no signs of inflammation or disease

Etymology

From Latin 'gingiva' (gum) + '-itis' (indicating inflammation).
