disburser

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈbɜːrsər / (AmE), / dɪsˈbɜːsə / (BrE)
a person or organization that pays out money, especially from a fund or for specific purposes
person who pays out money

Examples

The disburser of the grant funds must ensure all regulations are followed.
As the designated disburser, she handled all outgoing payments for the project.
Each department nominates a disburser to manage its operating budget.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spender - someone who spends money, especially habitually
  • Synonym: payer - a person who pays money for something

Etymology

From 'disburse', from Old French 'desbourser' (to pay out).
