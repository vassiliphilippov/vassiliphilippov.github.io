planeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪn.nəs / (AmE), / ˈpleɪn.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being planar; the property of having two dimensions or being flat like a plane
the state of being two-dimensional or flat

Examples

The engineer checked the planeness of the metal sheet before using it in construction.
For accurate measurements, the planeness of the reference surface must be verified.
Mathematics often deals with objects whose planeness defines their properties and behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flatness - the condition of being flat or level
  • Antonym: curvature - the state of being curved

Etymology

From 'plane' (flat surface) + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting a state or quality).
