syllabled

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪləbəld / (AmE), / ˈsɪləbəld / (BrE)
(of a word or utterance) having one or more syllables or pronounced with a specified number of syllables.
having or pronounced with syllables

Examples

The poem uses carefully syllabled verses to create a unique rhythm.
Her name is beautifully syllabled and easy to pronounce.
Native speakers often produce less clearly syllabled words in rapid speech.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: monosyllabic - having only one syllable
  • Degree Higher: polysyllabic - having many syllables

Etymology

From 'syllable' + '-ed', denoting possession or the quality of being syllabic.
