to derogate

1. VERB

/ ˈdɛrəˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛrəɡeɪt / (BrE)
To cause something to appear less important, serious, or valuable; to play down or diminish.
lessen importance or value

Examples

The report did not derogate from the seriousness of the incident.
He tried to derogate his colleague's achievements during the meeting.
The article seemed designed to derogate the importance of scientific research.

Synset Examples

Don't belittle his influence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to belittle - regard or portray as less impressive or important
  • Synonym: to diminish - make or become less

Etymology

From Latin derogare (to detract, take away).
