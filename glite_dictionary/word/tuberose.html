tuberose

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːbəˌroʊz / (AmE), / ˈtjuːbəˌrəʊz / (BrE)
a tropical Mexican plant with spikes of highly fragrant, waxy white flowers, cultivated for use in perfumery and as an ornamental
fragrant tropical flower; Mexican herb

Examples

The scent of tuberose filled the room during the evening ceremony.
She planted tuberoses in her garden for their beautiful fragrance.
Many perfumes are made with extracts from the tuberose flower.

Etymology

From Latin tuberosa, from tuber (lump, swelling); referring to its tuberous roots.
