Urticaceae

1. NOUN

/ ɜr.təˈkeɪ.siˌi / (AmE), / ɜː.tɪˈkeɪ.siː.iː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, commonly known as the nettle family, which includes many species with stinging hairs
the nettle family of plants

Examples

The Urticaceae family includes both annual and perennial plants with stinging hairs.
Botanists have classified the nettle under the Urticaceae family due to its unique characteristics.
Members of the Urticaceae family are found in tropical and temperate regions worldwide.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Urtica (nettle).
