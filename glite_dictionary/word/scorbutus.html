scorbutus

1. NOUN

/ skɔrˈbjutəs / (AmE), / skɔːˈbjuːtəs / (BrE)
an old term for scurvy, a condition caused by deficiency of vitamin C, leading to symptoms such as bleeding gums and weakness
archaic term for scurvy
archaic medical

Examples

During the long voyage, several sailors developed scorbutus due to lack of fresh fruits and vegetables.
The physician recognized the symptoms of scorbutus among the malnourished patients.
Treating scorbutus effectively requires the administration of vitamin C-rich foods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scurvy - disease caused by vitamin C deficiency

Etymology

From Latin scorbutus, itself from Dutch 'scheurbuik' or related Germanic words referring to scurvy.
