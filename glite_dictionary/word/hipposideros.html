Hipposideros

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪpoʊˈsɪdəˌrɒs / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəʊˈsɪdərɒs / (BrE)
A genus of bats known as horseshoe bats, characterized by horseshoe-shaped nose-leaves, found chiefly in tropical and subtropical regions.
a genus of horseshoe bats

Examples

The genus Hipposideros includes numerous species of horseshoe bats found across Asia and Africa.
Researchers captured several Hipposideros in the cave to study their echolocation calls.
Unlike Hipposideros, other bat genera may lack the distinctive horseshoe-shaped nose-leaf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rhinolophus - another genus of horseshoe bats

Etymology

From Greek 'hippos' (horse) + 'sideros' (iron), probably referring to the shape of the nose-leaf resembling a horseshoe.

2. NOUN

/ hɪˌpoʊsɪˈdɛrɒs / (AmE), / hɪˌpɒsɪˈdɪərɒs / (BrE)
A genus of bats commonly known as horseshoe bats, characterized by their complex nose-leaf structure used for echolocation.
a genus of horseshoe bats

Examples

The genus Hipposideros includes many species of bats found throughout Asia, Africa, and Australia.
Researchers studied the echolocation patterns of Hipposideros in the rainforest.
Some caves are home to large colonies of Hipposideros bats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horseshoe bat - a bat of the genus Hipposideros or Rhinolophus, named after the shape of its nose-leaf

Etymology

From Greek 'hippos' (horse) + 'sideros' (iron), referring to the horseshoe shape of the nose-leaf.
