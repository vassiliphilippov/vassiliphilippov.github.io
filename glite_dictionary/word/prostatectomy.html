prostatectomy

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊstəˈtɛktəmi / (AmE), / ˌprɒstəˈtɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical operation to remove part or all of the prostate gland
surgical removal of the prostate gland
medical

Examples

The patient underwent a prostatectomy to treat his prostate cancer.
After his prostatectomy, he needed several weeks to recover.
A minimally invasive prostatectomy can reduce the risk of complications.

Etymology

From prostate + -ectomy (from Greek 'ektomē' meaning excision).
