populariser

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːpjələˌraɪzər / (AmE), / ˈpɒpjʊləˌraɪzə / (BrE)
a person who makes something attractive or accessible to the general public
someone who makes things appealing to the public

Examples

Carl Sagan was a brilliant populariser of science in the twentieth century.
The museum hired a populariser to make their collections more engaging for children.
As a populariser of art history, she wrote books that appealed to a wide audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: popularizer - US spelling of populariser

Etymology

From 'popularize' + agent suffix '-er', meaning one who popularizes.
