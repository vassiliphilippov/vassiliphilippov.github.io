to interact

1. VERB

/ ˌɪntɚˈækt / (AmE), / ˌɪntərˈækt / (BrE)
to communicate or act in such a way as to have an effect on each other, or to act together with someone or something
communicate or act together

Examples

Children learn important social skills when they interact with others at school.
The new software allows users to interact with the system using voice commands.
It's important for managers to interact regularly with their team members.

Synset Examples

He should interact more with his colleagues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to communicate - to share or exchange information, news, or ideas

Etymology

From Latin inter- (between) + agere (to do, act) via French interaction.
