plainsong

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪnˌsɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈpleɪnsɒŋ / (BrE)
A type of church music consisting of a single, unaccompanied melodic line, especially as used in Roman Catholic liturgical chant.
unaccompanied liturgical chant

Examples

Gregorian plainsong filled the cathedral during the early morning service.
Many choirs specialize in the performance of medieval plainsong.
The beauty of plainsong lies in its simplicity and purity of sound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chant - A repeated rhythmic phrase, typically spoken or sung

Etymology

From Middle English 'pleynsong', a calque of Latin 'cantus planus' (plain chant).
