carvedilol

1. NOUN

/ kɑrˈvɛdɪˌlɑl / (AmE), / kɑːˈvɛdɪlɒl / (BrE)
a beta blocker drug used to treat high blood pressure and heart failure
beta blocker medication for heart conditions
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed carvedilol to help manage his high blood pressure.
Patients with heart failure may benefit from taking carvedilol regularly.
She had to learn about the possible side effects before starting carvedilol.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beta blocker - type of drug that reduces blood pressure by blocking the effects of adrenaline

Etymology

Blend of carbazole and alcohol, referencing chemical structure.
