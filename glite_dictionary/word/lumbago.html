lumbago

1. NOUN

/ lʌmˈbeɪɡoʊ / (AmE), / lʌmˈbeɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
pain in the lower back, especially in the lumbar region, often caused by muscle strain, arthritis, or other conditions
pain in the lower back
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with lumbago after he complained of persistent lower back pain.
After lifting heavy boxes, she was laid up for days with severe lumbago.
Regular exercise and good posture may help prevent lumbago in later life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backache - general pain in the back

Etymology

From Medieval Latin lumbago, from Latin lumbus (loin).
