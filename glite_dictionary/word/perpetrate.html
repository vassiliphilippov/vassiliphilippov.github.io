to perpetrate

1. VERB

/ ˈpɝː.pəˌtreɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.pə.treɪt / (BrE)
to commit or carry out (an act, especially one that is harmful, illegal, or immoral)
commit a harmful or illegal act

Examples

He was arrested after attempting to perpetrate a sophisticated internet scam.
Only a cruel person could perpetrate such an act of violence.
The investigation revealed who had perpetrated the fraud.

Synset Examples

perpetrate a crime
pull a bank robbery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to commit - to carry out or perpetrate, especially a crime or immoral act
  • Antonym: to prevent - to stop something from happening or arising

Etymology

From Latin 'perpetrare' (to accomplish), from 'per-' (through) + 'patrare' (to perform).
