Balaena

1. NOUN

/ bəˈliːnə / (AmE), / bəˈliːnə / (BrE)
a genus of large baleen whales in the family Balaenidae, including the Greenland right whale.
genus of large baleen whales

Examples

The Balaena genus includes the bowhead whale as a living representative.
Scientists study fossils to understand the evolution of Balaena whales.
The Balaena species have distinctive baleen plates for filter feeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: right whale - a large baleen whale of the genus Eubalaena or Balaena

Etymology

From Latin 'balaena' (whale), from Greek 'phallaina', meaning whale.

2. NOUN

/ bəˈliːnə / (AmE), / bəˈliːnə / (BrE)
a genus of large baleen whales in the family Balaenidae, including the bowhead whale
genus of large baleen whales

Examples

The Balaena genus is known for its thick layers of blubber.
Scientists classify the bowhead whale under the genus Balaena.
Research on Balaena has helped us understand whale migration patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bowhead whale - a large baleen whale of the Arctic, species in genus Balaena

Etymology

From Latin 'balaena', meaning 'whale', from Greek 'phalaina'.
