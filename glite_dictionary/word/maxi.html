maxi

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæksi / (AmE), / ˈmæksi / (BrE)
a long skirt or dress that reaches below the calf, often to the ankles
long skirt or dress, below calf

Examples

She wore a bright red maxi with a floral pattern to the wedding.
During the 1970s, the maxi became a popular fashion statement among young women.
The new boutique is featuring a line of elegant maxis for the summer season.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mini - a very short skirt, typically ending well above the knee

Etymology

Shortened from 'maxi-skirt', influenced by 'maxi-' meaning 'long' or 'maximum'.

Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈmæksi / (AmE), / ˈmæksi / (BrE)
(of a woman's garment) having a hemline at or reaching the ankle.
ankle-length (of clothing)

Examples

She chose a beautiful maxi dress for the summer wedding.
The designer's new collection features several maxi skirts in bold prints.
Wearing a maxi coat kept her warm during the chilly evening.

Synset Examples

wanted a maxi-length coat
a maxidress

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mini - (of clothing) having a very short hemline, above the knee
  • Degree Lower: midi - (of clothing) having a hemline at mid-calf length

Etymology

Shortened form of 'maxi-length', from 'maximum'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01435956-a
Synset POS: a
