glial

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡlaɪ.əl / (AmE), / ˈɡlʌɪ.əl / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of neuroglia, the supportive cells of the nervous system.
relating to neuroglia (nerve support cells)
medical

Examples

The researchers observed an increase in glial cell activity following the brain injury.
Certain diseases may result from dysfunctions in glial rather than neuronal cells.
Recent studies highlight the crucial role of glial cells in supporting neural communication.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: neuronal - relating to neurons (nerve cells)

Etymology

From neuroglia, with '-al' forming the adjective; neuroglia from Greek 'neuron' (nerve) + 'glia' (glue).
