Mandrillus

1. NOUN

/ mænˈdrɪləs / (AmE), / mænˈdrɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of large primates in the family Cercopithecidae, comprising the mandrill and drill.
genus of primates including mandrill and drill

Examples

The genus Mandrillus includes some of the most strikingly colored primates in Africa.
Researchers often compare the social behavior of Mandrillus species with those of baboons.
Conservation programs are focused on protecting Mandrillus populations from habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mandrill - a large West African monkey with a colorful face, species within Mandrillus
  • Synonym: drill - a large monkey of West Africa, Mandrillus leucophaeus

Etymology

From New Latin Mandrillus, combining 'mandrill' and typical zoological Latin suffix -us.

2. NOUN

/ mænˈdrɪləs / (AmE), / mænˈdrɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of large Old World monkeys that includes the mandrill and the drill.
genus of monkeys (mandrill, drill)

Examples

The genus Mandrillus is native to the forests of west-central Africa.
Both the drill and the mandrill belong to the genus Mandrillus.
Researchers studied the diet diversity within the genus Mandrillus to understand their ecological adaptation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mandrill - a large, colorful Old World monkey belonging to Mandrillus
  • Synonym: drill - a large, mainly black primate closely related to the mandrill

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from ‘mandrill’ with Latinized suffix -us.
