phantasmal

1. ADJ

/ fænˈtæzməl / (AmE), / fanˈtazməl / (BrE)
resembling a phantom; ghostly or unreal in appearance or effect.
resembling a phantom; ghostly

Examples

The old mansion was said to be haunted by a phantasmal figure that vanished in daylight.
In the moonlight, the fog gave the entire street a phantasmal quality.
She felt a phantasmal hand on her shoulder, sending chills down her spine.

Synset Examples

a ghostly face at the window
a phantasmal presence in the room
spectral emanations
spiritual tappings at a seance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phantom - a ghost; an apparition; something seen or imagined but not real
  • Synonym: ghostly - resembling a ghost; eerie or spooky
  • Antonym: real - actually existing or occurring in fact; not imagined or supposed

Etymology

From Greek 'phantasma' (apparition, phantom), via Late Latin 'phantasmalis'.
