eel

1. NOUN

/ il / (AmE), / iːl / (BrE)
A long, slender fish with a snake-like body, commonly found in freshwater and marine environments, whose fatty flesh is eaten smoked, pickled, or cooked.
a long, slender fish; its edible flesh

Examples

The chef prepared a dish of grilled eel served with a sweet soy glaze.
Smoked eel is considered a delicacy in many European countries.
He caught a large eel in the river during their fishing trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fish - a cold-blooded aquatic animal with gills and fins

Etymology

From Old English *ǣl*, related to Dutch *aal* and German *Aal* (eel).

2. NOUN

/ il / (AmE), / iːl / (BrE)
A long, slender fish with smooth, slimy, usually scaleless skin, and a continuous fin along its body, living in marine or freshwater environments.
a long, slender fish

Examples

The eel slipped through the fisherman's hands and vanished into the water.
Grilled eel is a delicacy in Japanese cuisine.
The children were fascinated by the way the eel curved its body as it swam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lamprey - a jawless fish with a long, eel-like body

Etymology

From Old English *ǣl*, related to Dutch *aal*, German *Aal*, Old Norse *álr* (all meaning 'eel').
