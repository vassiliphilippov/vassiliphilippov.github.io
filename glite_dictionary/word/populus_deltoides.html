Populus deltoides

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑpjələs dɛlˈtɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˈpɒpjʊləs dɛlˈtɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A species of poplar tree native to eastern and central United States, valued for its rapid growth and broad leaves, and cultivated for timber.
species of North American poplar tree

Examples

The rapid growth of Populus deltoides makes it popular for use in reforestation projects.
Many riverbanks in the Midwest are lined with majestic Populus deltoides trees.
European foresters often plant Populus deltoides for timber production due to its fast maturation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cottonwood - common name for Populus deltoides and related species

Etymology

From Latin *Populus* (poplar) and *deltoides* (triangular, referring to the leaf shape).
