idiomatical

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪdioʊˈmætɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌɪdiəˈmætɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of idiom, or conforming to the natural expressions of a language.
relating to idiom or idiomatic style

Examples

Her speech was remarkably idiomatical for a non-native speaker.
The translator struggled to find an idiomatical equivalent in the target language.
His style is more idiomatical than literal, capturing the true spirit of the original text.

Synset Examples

idiomatic English

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idiomatic - relating to the natural expressions or idioms of a language

Etymology

From Late Latin 'idiomaticus', from Greek 'idiōmatikos' (peculiar, characteristic).
