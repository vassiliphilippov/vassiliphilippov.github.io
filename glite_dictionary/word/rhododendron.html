rhododendron

1. NOUN

/ ˌroʊdəˈdɛndrən / (AmE), / ˌrəʊdəˈdɛndrən / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub or small tree of the genus Rhododendron, with leathery leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers
type of flowering evergreen shrub

Examples

The garden was filled with rhododendron bushes in full bloom.
She planted a pink rhododendron near the front entrance for a splash of color.
Many parks feature rhododendron displays in the spring.

Etymology

From Greek rhodon (rose) + dendron (tree).
