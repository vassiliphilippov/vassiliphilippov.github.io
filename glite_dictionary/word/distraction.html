distraction

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈstrækʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈstrækʃən / (BrE)
the act of drawing someone's attention away from something else
the act of diverting attention

Examples

It was hard to concentrate with so much distraction in the office.
She used music as a distraction from her worries.
The loud noise outside proved to be an effective distraction during the exam.

Synset Examples

conjurers are experts at misdirection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diversion - something that turns attention away from what is happening
  • Antonym: focus - the act of concentrating attention on something

Etymology

From Latin distractio (a pulling apart), from distrahere (to draw apart).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈstrækʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈstrækʃən / (BrE)
a thing that prevents someone from giving full attention to something else; an obstacle to concentration or focus
something that diverts attention

Examples

She turned off her phone to eliminate any possible distraction while studying for the test.
The loud music in the café was a constant distraction from her work.
He found it hard to concentrate with so many distractions in the busy office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diversion - something that takes attention away from what is happening
  • Antonym: focus - concentration of attention or effort

Etymology

From Latin distractio (a pulling apart), from distrahere (pull in different directions).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈstrækʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈstrækʃən / (BrE)
an activity or entertainment that amuses or diverts attention, especially from worries or unpleasant thoughts
amusement or diversion from worries

Examples

Reading a novel is a welcome distraction from the stress of daily life.
The children enjoyed the magician's show as a lively distraction during the long afternoon.
She used music as a distraction to help her relax after a difficult day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amusement - something that entertains or amuses
  • Synonym: entertainment - an event, performance, or activity designed to entertain
  • Antonym: worry - a feeling of anxiety or concern

Etymology

From Latin distractio (a pulling apart), from distrahere (to draw apart).

4. NOUN

/ dɪˈstrækʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈstrækʃən / (BrE)
A state of mental turmoil or extreme agitation, often due to worry, anxiety, or overwhelming emotions.
mental turmoil; extreme agitation

Examples

The constant noise in the house nearly drove her to distraction.
Worrying about the test results sent him into a state of distraction.
She was so overcome by distraction that she couldn't focus on her work.

Synset Examples

he drives me to distraction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distress - extreme anxiety, sorrow, or pain
  • Antonym: calm - the state of being peaceful and free from agitation

Etymology

From Latin distractio(n-), from distrahere ‘pull apart’.
