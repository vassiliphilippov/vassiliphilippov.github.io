pottage

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑː.tɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɒt.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
a thick soup or stew made from vegetables and sometimes meat, especially one typical in historical or traditional settings
a thick vegetable (and sometimes meat) stew

Examples

The villagers gathered around the fire and shared a bowl of pottage for supper.
She prepared a simple pottage with root vegetables and herbs from the garden.
In medieval times, pottage was a staple dish for common people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stew - a dish of meat and vegetables cooked slowly in liquid

Etymology

From Old French potage (food cooked in a pot), from pot (pot).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑː.tɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɒ.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
a thick soup or stew, often of vegetables, sometimes with meat or other ingredients.
thick soup or stew

Examples

The monks dined on a simple pottage made from lentils and root vegetables.
He traded his inheritance for a mere bowl of pottage.
On cold nights, a hot pottage is both hearty and satisfying.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stew - dish of meat and vegetables cooked slowly in liquid

Etymology

From Old French 'potage' (something cooked in a pot), from 'pot' (pot).
