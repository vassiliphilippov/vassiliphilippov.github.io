JHVH

1. NOUN

/ ˈjeɪ tʃ viː ˈeɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪ tʃ viː ˈeɪtʃ / (BrE)
a representation of the name of God in the Old Testament, transliterated from the Hebrew Tetragrammaton (YHVH)
the Tetragrammaton; name for God in the Old Testament

Examples

Many early English translations of the Bible used JHVH to refer to the divine name.
Scholars often debate the pronunciation of JHVH, as the Hebrew text contains only consonants.
The name JHVH appears in various religious and academic writings about the Hebrew scriptures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yahweh - another transliteration of the Tetragrammaton, the name of God in Hebrew
  • Synonym: Jehovah - a more common English rendering of the Tetragrammaton

Etymology

From Latinized transliteration of Hebrew יהוה (YHWH), with 'J' historically used for 'Y'.
