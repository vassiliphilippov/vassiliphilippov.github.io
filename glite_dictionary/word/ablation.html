ablation

1. NOUN

/ æˈbleɪʃən / (AmE), / əˈbleɪʃən / (BrE)
The process by which the surface of a glacier is reduced in size, especially by melting and evaporation.
erosion or reduction of glacier by melting

Examples

During the summer months, ablation outpaces accumulation, causing the glacier to shrink.
Scientists measured the rate of ablation to assess changes in glacial mass over time.
Climate change is accelerating ablation in polar regions, leading to rising sea levels.

Etymology

From Latin ablatio (removal), from ab- (away) + latio (carrying).
