leftovers

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛftˌoʊvɚz / (AmE), / ˈlɛftˌəʊvəz / (BrE)
food remaining uneaten after a meal, often saved to be eaten later
uneaten food from a previous meal

Examples

We ate the leftovers from last night's party for lunch today.
After Thanksgiving, the fridge was packed with turkey leftovers.
She packed the leftovers in containers to take to work the next day.

Synset Examples

he had leftovers for dinner last night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remnants - small parts of something left over after the main part is gone (general sense)

Etymology

From 'left over' (remaining), formed as noun; originally recorded in the early 20th century.
