jewel

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuː.əl / (AmE), / ˈdʒuː.əl / (BrE)
a precious or semiprecious stone that has been cut and polished for use in jewelry
precious or semiprecious stone in jewelry

Examples

She wore a sparkling jewel set in a gold ring on her finger.
The crown is decorated with rare jewels from around the world.
Each jewel was carefully chosen for its color and clarity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gem - a precious or semiprecious stone, especially when cut and polished or engraved

Etymology

From Old French 'jouel', from Medieval Latin 'jocale' (plaything, jewel), from Latin 'jocus' (play, pastime).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːəl / (BrE)
a person who is highly valued or admired, especially for outstanding qualities; someone considered as precious as a gemstone
person of outstanding worth or value

Examples

She is truly a jewel in our team, always going above and beyond.
The teacher called her student a jewel for his kindness and hard work.
Everyone agrees that their grandmother is the real jewel of the family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gem - a person or thing that is especially excellent or admirable

Etymology

From Old French 'jouel' (jewel, ornament), from Medieval Latin 'jocale' (plaything).

3. VERB

/ ˈdʒuːəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːəl / (BrE)
to adorn or decorate something with precious stones
adorn with precious stones

Examples

The designer chose to jewel the neckline of the gown with sparkling sapphires.
Skilled craftsmen jeweled the crown with emeralds and rubies for the royal celebration.
Artisans are carefully jeweling the antique box to restore its original splendor.

Synset Examples

jeweled dresses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embellish - to make something more attractive by adding decorative details or features
  • Synonym: bejeweled - decorated with jewels or gems

Etymology

From Old French *jouel* (jewel), from Medieval Latin *jocale* (plaything, jewel).
