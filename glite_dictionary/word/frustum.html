frustum

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrʌstəm / (AmE), / ˈfrʌstəm / (BrE)
A solid shape formed by cutting a cone or pyramid with a plane parallel to its base and removing the top portion; the remaining part is called a frustum.
truncated cone or pyramid

Examples

The engineer calculated the volume of the frustum to determine how much material was needed for the base.
A lampshade often takes the shape of a frustum of a cone.
To make the model, first cut a cylinder, then shape it into a frustum by removing the top section.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cone - a solid or hollow object with a circular base that tapers to a point
  • Degree Lower: pyramid - a solid object with a polygonal base and triangular faces that meet at a point

Etymology

From Latin 'frustum' (piece, morsel).
