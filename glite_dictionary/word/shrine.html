shrine

1. VERB

/ ʃraɪn / (AmE), / ʃraɪn / (BrE)
to enclose or preserve something in or as in a shrine, often to honor or protect it
to enclose in a shrine

Examples

The ancient relics were shrined by the monks for protection.
Many cultures shrine important documents to preserve their legacy.
Over time, the city decided to shrine the statue in the new temple.

Synset Examples

the saint's bones were enshrined in the cathedral

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enshrine - to preserve something as sacred or cherished

Etymology

From Old English 'scrīn' (chest, reliquary), from Latin 'scrīnium' (case, chest for books or papers).

2. NOUN

/ ʃraɪn / (AmE), / ʃraɪn / (BrE)
A place regarded as holy because of its association with a sacred person or relic, and used as a site of worship.
holy place associated with veneration

Examples

Pilgrims traveled for miles to visit the ancient shrine on the hillside.
The cathedral's shrine houses the relics of a medieval saint.
She lit a candle at the family shrine to honor her ancestors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temple - building devoted to religious worship, especially in ancient religions
  • Synonym: altar - a table or flat-topped block used for religious rituals
  • Synonym: sanctuary - a sacred or holy place

Etymology

From Old English 'scrin' (a chest, shrine), from Latin 'scrinium' (case, chest for books or papers).
