to embody

1. VERB

/ ɛmˈbɑdi / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɒdi / (BrE)
to be a tangible or visible representation of an idea, quality, or feeling; to give bodily or concrete form to something abstract
to represent or give form to something

Examples

She truly embodies the spirit of generosity in everything she does.
The statue embodies the ideals of freedom and democracy.
His actions embody everything our company stands for.

Synset Examples

He embodies all that is evil wrong with the system
The painting substantiates the feelings of the artist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incarnate - to embody in human form
  • Synonym: to exemplify - to serve as a typical example of

Etymology

From en- (make) + body, modelled after Late Latin incorporare (to embody).

2. VERB

/ ɛmˈbɑːdi / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɒdi / (BrE)
to represent or express an idea, quality, or character in a tangible or visible way, especially by portraying a character on stage or in film
portray or represent (a character or idea)

Examples

The actor did his best to embody the spirit of the historical figure.
She embodies the role of Juliet with both passion and grace.
In the play, he embodied the conflicted nature of his character.

Synset Examples

Derek Jacobi was Hamlet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to personify - to represent an abstract quality in human form
  • Synonym: incarnate - to make concrete and real, embodied in human form
  • Antonym: abstract - to consider something theoretically rather than actually representing it

Etymology

From em- (variant of en-, 'put into') + body; first used in the late 16th century.

3. VERB

/ ɛmˈbɑdi / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɒdi / (BrE)
to represent or express an idea, quality, or feeling in a tangible or visible form
represent an idea in tangible form

Examples

The hero’s actions embody the values of courage and selflessness.
Her speech perfectly embodied the spirit of hope felt by the crowd.
The new law embodies a commitment to equal rights for all citizens.

Synset Examples

This painting embodies the feelings of the Romantic period

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incarnate - to embody or personify in a tangible or human form

Etymology

From French 'embodier' (to form into a body), from 'em-' (in) + 'body'.

Synset POS: v
