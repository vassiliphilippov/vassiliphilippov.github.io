tombac

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑmˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈtɒmbæk / (BrE)
an alloy of copper and zinc (and sometimes arsenic) that has a yellowish color and is used to imitate gold in cheap jewelry and for gilding
copper-zinc alloy imitating gold

Examples

The counterfeit coin was made of tombac and coated to look like real gold.
Many inexpensive ornaments are produced using tombac for its golden sheen.
He identified the necklace as tombac rather than true gold after careful examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brass - an alloy of copper and zinc
  • Antonym: gold - a precious yellow metallic element

Etymology

From French 'tombac', itself from Malay 'tumbaga', meaning copper.
