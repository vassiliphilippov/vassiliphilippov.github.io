Lycaena

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈsiːnə / (AmE), / lɪˈsiːnə / (BrE)
A genus of small, slender butterflies, typically with metallic blue, green, or copper coloring on the upper wing surface, belonging to the family Lycaenidae.
genus of small colorful butterflies

Examples

The Lycaena butterflies are admired for their vibrant metallic colors.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Lycaena genus in Central America.
Many gardens attract Lycaena with flowering plants and gentle landscapes.

Etymology

From New Latin, genus name used in scientific classification.
