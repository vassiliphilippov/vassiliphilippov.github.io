Limenitis archippus

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈmɛnɪtɪs ɑːrˈkɪpəs / (AmE), / lɪˈmɛnɪtɪs ɑːˈkɪpəs / (BrE)
a North American butterfly, commonly called the viceroy, that resembles the monarch butterfly but is smaller and has a distinct black line across its hind wings
a North American butterfly resembling the monarch

Examples

The striking coloration of the Limenitis archippus helps it avoid predators by mimicking the monarch butterfly.
Many entomologists study Limenitis archippus to understand mimicry in butterflies.
We spotted a beautiful Limenitis archippus resting on a milkweed plant during our hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viceroy - common name for Limenitis archippus, a butterfly similar to the monarch
  • Degree Higher: monarch butterfly - a larger orange and black butterfly, similar in appearance to Limenitis archippus

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Limenitis' and species name 'archippus'.
