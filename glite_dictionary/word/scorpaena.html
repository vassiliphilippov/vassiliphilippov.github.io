Scorpaena

1. NOUN

/ skɔrˈpiːnə / (AmE), / skɔːˈpiːnə / (BrE)
A genus of marine fishes in the family Scorpaenidae, commonly known as scorpionfishes, found in tropical and subtropical seas.
a genus of scorpionfishes

Examples

The venomous spines of the Scorpaena make them dangerous to handle among marine biologists.
Several species within the Scorpaena genus are prized for their vibrant coloration.
Divers in tropical waters should be cautious, as the Scorpaena can sting if threatened.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scorpaenidae - the family of scorpionfishes
  • Synonym: scorpionfish - common name for fishes of the Scorpaenidae family

Etymology

From Latin 'Scorpaena', from Greek 'skorpaina', a kind of fish.
