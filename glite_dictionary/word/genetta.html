Genetta

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈnɛtə / (AmE), / dʒɪˈnɛtə / (BrE)
A genus of small to medium-sized carnivorous mammals known as genets, native to Africa and parts of Europe.
genus of small carnivorous mammals (genets)

Examples

Many species in the genus Genetta are found throughout Africa's forests and savannahs.
The common genet, or Genetta genetta, is known for its spotted coat and agile movements.
Researchers are studying the evolutionary history of the Genetta genus to understand its diversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genet - a small, slender carnivorous mammal of the species Genetta

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Genetta', based on 'genet', the common name for these animals.

2. NOUN

/ dʒəˈnɛtə / (AmE), / dʒɪˈnɛtə / (BrE)
A genus of small, slender carnivorous mammals known as genets, native to Africa and parts of Europe and Asia.
genus of genets (carnivorous mammals)

Examples

The Genetta genus includes several species of genets found throughout Africa and southern Europe.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of different Genetta species in the wild.
Unlike some other carnivores, members of Genetta are noted for their agility and solitary behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genet - a small, agile, cat-like carnivorous mammal

Etymology

From Neo-Latin 'Genetta', based on 'genet', ultimately from Old French 'genete'.
