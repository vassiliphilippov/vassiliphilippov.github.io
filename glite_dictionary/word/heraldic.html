heraldic

1. ADJ

/ hɛrˈældɪk / (AmE), / hɛˈral.dɪk / (BrE)
relating to the study or practice of heraldry, or to coats of arms and armorial bearings
relating to heraldry or coats of arms

Examples

The castle was decorated with heraldic banners representing noble families.
His interest in heraldic symbols led him to study genealogy.
The shield features a heraldic lion in its center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heraldry - the study and design of armorial bearings or coats of arms

Etymology

From French *héraldique*, from *héraut* (herald), ultimately from Germanic origin.

2. ADJ

/ hɛrˈældɪk / (AmE), / hɛˈrældɪk / (BrE)
figurative: indicative of or announcing something to come; serving as a sign or forerunner of future events
indicative of things to come

Examples

The artist's early work was heraldic of the creative explosion that would define her later career.
Their innovation was heraldic of major changes in the industry over the next decade.
The sudden chill in the air was heraldic of the harsh winter soon to arrive.

Synset Examples

the Beatles were heraldic of a new style of music

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prophetic - predictive of future events
  • Synonym: ominous - giving the impression that something bad will happen

Etymology

From 'herald' (a messenger or one who proclaims) + -ic. 'Herald' from Old French 'heraut', of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00500331-s
Synset POS: s
