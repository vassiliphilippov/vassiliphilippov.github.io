groundwork

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊndˌwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊndˌwɜːk / (BrE)
the basic foundation or underlying basis for something, especially a plan, theory, or structure
basic foundation or basis for something

Examples

The researchers laid the groundwork for future studies with this important discovery.
Negotiations between the two countries provided the groundwork for the peace treaty.
Years of training formed the groundwork for her successful career as a dancer.

Synset Examples

the whole argument rested on a basis of conjecture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foundation - the lowest load-bearing part of a building; underlying basis or principle
  • Synonym: basis - underlying support or foundation for an idea or process

Etymology

From ground (basis) + work, indicating the work done to prepare the ground or foundation.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊndˌwɝk / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊndˌwɜːk / (BrE)
preparatory work or planning that provides the basis for something to develop or succeed
preparatory work or foundation

Examples

The team's success was possible because of the groundwork laid by previous managers.
Before starting the new project, extensive groundwork was necessary to ensure its feasibility.
Her research provided important groundwork for future developments in the field.

Synset Examples

we are prepared today because of groundwork that was done ten years ago

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foundation - the lowest load-bearing part of a building, often used figuratively for the basis of something
  • Synonym: preparation - the act of making something ready for use or consideration

Etymology

Compound of 'ground' (base or foundation) and 'work' (activity or task), first used in the 17th century.
