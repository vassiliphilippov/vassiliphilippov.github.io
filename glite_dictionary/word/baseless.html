baseless

1. ADJ

/ ˈbeɪsləs / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsləs / (BrE)
(of an idea, claim, or statement) having no foundation in fact; unfounded or unsupported.
without factual basis; unfounded

Examples

The investigation found that the accusations were completely baseless.
Her fears about losing her job turned out to be baseless.
He dismissed the rumors as baseless speculation spread by his rivals.

Synset Examples

baseless gossip
the allegations proved groundless
idle fears
unfounded suspicions
unwarranted jealousy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unfounded - having no basis in fact
  • Synonym: groundless - not based on any good reason or facts
  • Antonym: justified - having a good reason or basis

Etymology

Formed from 'base' + '-less', meaning 'without a base'.
