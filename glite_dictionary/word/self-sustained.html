self-sustained

1. ADJ

/ sɛlf səˈsteɪnd / (AmE), / sɛlf səˈsteɪnd / (BrE)
Able to continue operating or existing without need for external support or assistance.
able to function without outside support

Examples

The organization became self-sustained after several years of development and planning.
A self-sustained ecosystem can thrive without human intervention.
She strived to create a self-sustained lifestyle by growing her own food and generating her own energy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-sufficient - able to provide for oneself without the help of others
  • Antonym: dependent - relying on someone or something else for support

Etymology

From 'self' (oneself, independent) + 'sustained' (from Latin sustinere, to hold up).
