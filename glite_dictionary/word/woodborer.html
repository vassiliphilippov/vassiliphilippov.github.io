woodborer

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdˌbɔrər / (AmE), / ˈwʊdˌbɔːrə / (BrE)
an insect, larva, or mollusk that bores into and damages wood
insect or mollusk boring into wood

Examples

The old house was infested with woodborers that weakened the beams.
Some species of woodborer can cause significant economic damage to trees and timber.
The museum preserved a section of bark showing tunnels from a woodborer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: termite - an insect that feeds on and destroys wood

Etymology

From 'wood' + 'borer', indicating an organism that bores into wood.
