caliche-topped

1. ADJ

/ kəˈliːʧ ˌtɑpt / (AmE), / kəˈliːʧ ˌtɒpt / (BrE)
(of ground or surface) covered with or topped by caliche, a hard layer of calcium carbonate-encrusted soil common in arid regions.
covered with caliche (hard calcium-rich soil)

Examples

The trail winds over a caliche-topped ridge that gleams in the sunlight.
Farmers struggle to grow crops on caliche-topped fields due to poor drainage.
A caliche-topped road can be especially rough after a rainstorm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caliche - a hard deposit of calcium carbonate found in arid or semiarid regions

Etymology

From 'caliche', from Spanish, meaning a crusty deposit of calcium carbonate, and 'topped', meaning covered at the top.
