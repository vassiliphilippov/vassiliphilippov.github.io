quadratic

1. NOUN

/ kwɑˈdrætɪk / (AmE), / kwɒˈdrætɪk / (BrE)
a mathematical expression in which the highest power of the variable is two; a polynomial of degree two
a second-degree polynomial

Examples

The equation is a quadratic in the variable x.
You can solve a quadratic using the quadratic formula.
Her homework included several quadratic expressions to factor.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: cubic - a polynomial of the third degree
  • Degree Lower: linear - a polynomial of the first degree

Etymology

From Latin quadratus (square, squared), referring to the power of two.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05871085-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ kwəˈdrætɪk / (AmE), / kwɒˈdrætɪk / (BrE)
an equation in which the highest power of the unknown variable is a square, typically written in the form ax² + bx + c = 0
an equation with a squared term

Examples

She struggled to solve the quadratic on her math homework.
Every quadratic can be solved using the quadratic formula.
The graph of a quadratic is always a parabola.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quadratic equation - an equation of the form ax² + bx + c = 0

Etymology

From Latin quadratus (made square), referring to the square of the variable.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06682985-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ kwɑˈdrætɪk / (AmE), / kwɒˈdratɪk / (BrE)
involving or relating to the second and no higher power of a variable or degree, especially in mathematics
involving the second power (math)

Examples

The teacher explained how to solve a quadratic equation using the quadratic formula.
The graph of a quadratic function is always a parabola.
Engineers often encounter quadratic relationships when analyzing forces and motion.

Synset Examples

quadratic equation

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: linear - involving only the first power of a variable
  • Degree Higher: cubic - involving the third power of a variable

Etymology

From Latin 'quadratus' (squared), from 'quadrare' (to make square).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03119449-a
Synset POS: a
