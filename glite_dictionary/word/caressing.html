caressing

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrɛsɪŋ / (AmE), / kəˈrɛsɪŋ / (BrE)
gentle, affectionate touching or stroking, often as an expression of love or tenderness
gentle affectionate touching

Examples

The gentle caressing of his hand reassured her as they sat together.
She found comfort in the caressing that preceded their embrace.
His caressing made her feel truly cherished and loved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stroking - gentle and repeated movement of the hand over something or someone
  • Synonym: fondling - touching or handling someone or something affectionately or in a loving manner

Etymology

Present participle of 'caress', from French 'caresser', from Italian 'carezza' (caress), ultimately from Latin 'caritia' (dearness, affection).
