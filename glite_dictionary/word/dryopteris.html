Dryopteris

1. NOUN

/ draɪˈɑptərɪs / (AmE), / drɪˈɒptərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of medium-sized terrestrial ferns, widely distributed and found in many regions; commonly known as wood ferns.
a genus of medium-sized terrestrial ferns

Examples

Botanists often study Dryopteris to better understand the evolution of ferns.
The shady forest floor was covered in lush Dryopteris specimens.
Many gardeners plant Dryopteris for its attractive fronds and easy care.

Etymology

From Greek 'dryas' (oak) + 'pteris' (fern), referring to ferns often found in woodlands.

2. NOUN

/ draɪˈɑptərɪs / (AmE), / draɪˈɒptərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of medium-sized terrestrial ferns widely distributed in temperate regions, commonly known as wood ferns.
a genus of terrestrial ferns

Examples

The Dryopteris genus includes some of the most common woodland ferns found in North America.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the Dryopteris group.
Many gardeners plant Dryopteris ferns in shaded areas for their attractive fronds.

Etymology

From Greek 'drys' (oak) and 'pteris' (fern).
