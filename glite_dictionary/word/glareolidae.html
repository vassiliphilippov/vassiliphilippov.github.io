Glareolidae

1. NOUN

/ ɡlɛəriˈɑːlɪdi / (AmE), / ɡleəˌrɪəʊˈlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of birds that includes pratincoles and coursers, known for their slender bodies and long wings.
family of birds: pratincoles and coursers

Examples

The Glareolidae family includes several species, such as pratincoles, that are found in both Europe and Africa.
Researchers observed the migration patterns of Glareolidae across the Mediterranean region.
Some members of the Glareolidae family prefer open grasslands while others are more commonly found near wetlands.

Etymology

From modern Latin *Glareolidae*, from the type genus *Glareola*.
