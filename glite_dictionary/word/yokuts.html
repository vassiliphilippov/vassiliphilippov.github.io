Yokuts

1. NOUN

/ ˈjoʊkʌts / (AmE), / ˈjəʊkʌts / (BrE)
a member of a North American Indigenous people traditionally living in the San Joaquin Valley of California
member of a Native American people of California

Examples

Several Yokuts communities continue to preserve their traditions in California.
The anthropologist interviewed a Yokuts elder about tribal customs.
Many artifacts from the Yokuts are displayed at the local museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yokutsan - the language family spoken by the Yokuts people

Etymology

From the Yokuts word *yokoch*, meaning ‘person’.

2. NOUN

/ joʊˈkʊts / (AmE), / jəʊˈkʊts / (BrE)
a Penutian language traditionally spoken by the Yokuts people of the San Joaquin Valley in California
Penutian Native American language of California

Examples

Linguists have studied the Yokuts language to learn more about Native Californian cultures.
Only a few fluent speakers of Yokuts remain today, making preservation efforts critical.
The grammar of Yokuts includes features that are rare in other Penutian languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yokuts - a member of the Yokuts people

Etymology

From the name of the Yokuts people, a Native American group indigenous to California.
