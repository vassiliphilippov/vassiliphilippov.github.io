chic

1. ADJ

/ ʃik / (AmE), / ʃiːk / (BrE)
elegantly and stylishly fashionable.
elegantly stylish; fashionable

Examples

The hotel's chic interior impressed all of its guests.
She wore a chic black dress to the party.
They opened a chic little café in the city center.

Synset Examples

chic elegance
a smart new dress
a suit of voguish cut

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stylish - fashionable and elegant in appearance or style
  • Synonym: elegant - pleasingly graceful and stylish in appearance or manner
  • Antonym: dowdy - unfashionable and without style

Etymology

From French 'chic' (stylishness, elegance).

2. NOUN

/ ʃik / (AmE), / ʃiːk / (BrE)
Stylish elegance and sophistication, especially as shown in fashionable dress or lifestyle.
stylish elegance; fashionable style

Examples

The restaurant exuded an air of chic that attracted a trendy crowd.
Her effortless chic made her a fashion icon in the city.
The interior's modern chic set it apart from other cafes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elegance - the quality of being graceful and stylish in appearance or manner
  • Synonym: style - a manner of doing or presenting things, especially in fashion
  • Antonym: unfashionable - not in keeping with current fashion; lacking style or chic

Etymology

From French *chic* (stylishness, elegance).
