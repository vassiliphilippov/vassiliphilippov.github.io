arteria cerebelli

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈtɪriə ˌsɛrəˈbɛlaɪ / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə ˌsɛrəˈbɛlaɪ / (BrE)
an artery that supplies blood to the cerebellum in the brain
artery supplying the cerebellum
medical

Examples

During the surgery, the neurosurgeon carefully avoided damaging the arteria cerebelli.
A blockage in the arteria cerebelli can lead to a stroke affecting coordination.
The arteria cerebelli branches off from the basilar artery to supply the cerebellum.

Etymology

From Latin: 'arteria' (artery) + 'cerebelli' (of the cerebellum).
