to rive

1. VERB

/ raɪv / (AmE), / raɪv / (BrE)
to split or tear apart forcibly, especially with a sharp instrument
split or tear apart forcibly

Examples

The powerful axe rived the log into two pieces.
A bolt of lightning rived the old oak tree in the storm.
Centuries of conflict have riven the nation.

Synset Examples

cleave the bone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cleave - to split or sever (something), especially along a natural line
  • Synonym: tear - to pull or rip (something) apart or to pieces

Etymology

From Old Norse rīfa (to tear), related to Old English rīfan.

2. VERB

/ raɪv / (AmE), / raɪv / (BrE)
to tear or split something violently or forcefully; to be torn apart in this way
tear or split violently

Examples

The ancient oak was riven by a bolt of lightning during the storm.
Her heart was riven with grief after hearing the tragic news.
With sheer force, he rived the log into two pieces.

Synset Examples

The curtain ripped from top to bottom
pull the cooked chicken into strips

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tear - to pull apart or damage by force; to rip
  • Synonym: rip - to tear or split apart forcefully or quickly

Etymology

From Old English rīfan (to tear, split).
