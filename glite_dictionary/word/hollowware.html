hollowware

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑloʊˌwɛr / (AmE), / ˈhɒləʊˌweə / (BrE)
Tableware, such as bowls, pots, teapots, and serving dishes, that are typically hollow and used for serving or containing food and drink, especially when made of silver or other metal.
serving dishes such as bowls and teapots, often metal

Examples

The antique silver hollowware was displayed prominently on the sideboard.
She prefers collecting hollowware like teapots and sugar bowls rather than flatware.
The set includes both hollowware and matching utensils crafted by hand.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flatware - cutlery or eating utensils that are flat, such as knives, forks, and spoons

Etymology

From 'hollow' + 'ware', influenced by the object's shape.
