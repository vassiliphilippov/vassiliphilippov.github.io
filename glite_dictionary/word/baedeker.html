Baedeker

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪdəˌkər / (AmE), / ˈbeɪdɪkə / (BrE)
a guidebook, especially one from the famous series originally published by the German firm Baedeker
a travel guidebook, especially from Baedeker

Examples

He pulled out his trusted Baedeker before planning the route through Italy.
Many nineteenth-century travelers would not leave home without a Baedeker in hand.
The old Baedeker guides are now prized by collectors for their historical insights.

Etymology

From the name of Karl Baedeker (1801–1859), German publisher of travel guides.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪdɪkər / (AmE), / ˈbeɪdɪkə / (BrE)
any of a series of travel guidebooks published by the German firm established by Karl Baedeker, often used to refer to any authoritative travel guidebook
travel guidebook by Karl Baedeker's firm

Examples

She tucked a Baedeker into her bag before setting off for Europe.
The museum still displays a well-worn Baedeker from the nineteenth century.
Modern travel guides owe much to the influence of the original Baedeker series.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guidebook - book giving information for travellers

Etymology

From the name of Karl Baedeker (1801–1859), German publisher who founded the travel guidebook series.
