Ilex paraguariensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪlɛks ˌpærəˌɡwɛriˈɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌaɪlɛks ˌpærəˌɡwɛəriˈɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A species of holly native to South America, whose leaves are used to make the beverage mate.
South American holly used for mate

Examples

The leaves of Ilex paraguariensis are traditionally dried and steeped to make mate.
Cultivation of Ilex paraguariensis is an important industry in several South American countries.
Researchers are studying the health benefits of compounds found in Ilex paraguariensis.

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Ilex' means 'holm oak' and 'paraguariensis' denotes origin from Paraguay.
