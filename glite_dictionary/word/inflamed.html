inflamed

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈfleɪmd / (AmE), / ɪnˈfleɪmd / (BrE)
lit up or colored with a reddish or fiery appearance, as if set alight by flames
having a fiery red or glowing appearance

Examples

The horizon was painted with inflamed clouds as the sun went down.
Her cheeks were inflamed by the heat of the fire.
At dusk, the entire sky appeared inflamed with deep crimson and gold.

Synset Examples

streets ablaze with lighted Christmas trees
the inflamed clouds at sunset
reddened faces around the campfire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ablaze - brightly lit or glowing with light or color
  • Synonym: reddened - having become red or more red

Etymology

Past participle of 'inflame', from Latin inflammare (to set on fire).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈfleɪmd / (AmE), / ɪnˈfleɪmd / (BrE)
(of a part of the body) hot, swollen, red, and often painful, especially as a result of infection or injury.
hot, swollen, and red due to inflammation

Examples

Her inflamed throat made it difficult for her to swallow.
After scratching the mosquito bite, the skin became red and inflamed.
The doctor examined his inflamed eyes and recommended rest.

Synset Examples

eyes inflamed with crying

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swollen - enlarged by fluid or inflammation
  • Synonym: irritated - showing signs of irritation or mild inflammation
  • Antonym: healthy - not inflamed, free from infection or injury

Etymology

From Latin inflammare (to set on fire, inflame).

Synset POS: s
