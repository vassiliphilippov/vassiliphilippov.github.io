mischance

1. NOUN

/ mɪsˈtʃæns / (AmE), / mɪsˈtʃɑːns / (BrE)
an unpredictable event that results in bad luck or misfortune
unfortunate, unpredictable event

Examples

The storm struck with sudden mischance, ruining their plans for the festival.
He blamed his failure on mischance rather than personal mistakes.
Through mischance, her letter was lost in the mail and never received.

Synset Examples

if I didn't have bad luck, I wouldn't have any luck at all

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misfortune - bad luck; an unfortunate event
  • Antonym: luck - success or failure apparently brought by chance rather than one's own actions

Etymology

From Old French 'mescheance' (misfortune), from 'mescheoir' (to fall badly).

2. NOUN

/ mɪsˈtʃæns / (AmE), / mɪsˈtʃɑːns / (BrE)
an unlucky event; an instance of misfortune or bad luck
an instance of bad luck or misfortune

Examples

Due to a sudden mischance, the picnic was ruined by unexpected rain.
She faced each mischance in her life with unwavering optimism.
A single moment of mischance changed the course of his journey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misfortune - bad luck; an unfortunate event
  • Antonym: fortune - luck, especially good luck

Etymology

From Old French 'mescheance', from 'mescheoir' (to happen badly), from Latin 'male' (badly) + 'cadere' (to fall).
