metropolis

1. NOUN

/ məˈtrɑp.ə.lɪs / (AmE), / mɪˈtrɒp.əlɪs / (BrE)
A very large and densely populated city, often including surrounding areas or districts and acting as a central hub for a region.
very large, densely populated city

Examples

New York City is considered a metropolis because of its size and diverse population.
The conference was held in the bustling metropolis of Tokyo.
As the capital grew into a metropolis, thousands of people moved there for new opportunities.

Synset Examples

Ancient Troy was a great city

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: city - a large town; an urban area with a local government
  • Degree Higher: megalopolis - a very large, heavily populated urban complex

Etymology

From Greek metropolis, from mētēr (mother) + polis (city).

2. NOUN

/ məˈtrɑːpəlɪs / (AmE), / mɪˈtrɒpəlɪs / (BrE)
A very large, busy city, often the chief city of a region or country.
major large city

Examples

New York is considered a metropolis known for its vibrant culture and diversity.
The rapid growth of the metropolis has led to various urban planning challenges.
Ancient Athens was once a metropolis in the classical world.

Synset Examples

the city voted for Republicans in 1994

Related Terms

  • Synonym: city - a large human settlement

Etymology

From Greek mētropolis, from mētēr (mother) + polis (city).
