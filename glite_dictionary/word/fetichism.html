fetichism

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːtɪˌʃɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈfiːtɪˌʃɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the belief in the magical power of objects, or the practice of worshipping such objects as fetishes
belief in magical power of fetishes

Examples

Some indigenous cultures have practiced fetichism as part of their spiritual traditions.
Anthropologists have studied fetichism in various societies around the world.
The shrine was built as an expression of fetichism, venerating a sacred stone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fetishism - belief in or worship of magical objects or fetishes

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'fetishism', from French 'fétichisme', ultimately from Portuguese 'feitiço' (charm, sorcery), from Latin 'facticius' (artificial).
