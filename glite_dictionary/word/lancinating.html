lancinating

1. ADJ

/ ˈlænsɪˌneɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlansɪˌneɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of pain) sharp and stabbing, as if caused by a piercing instrument.
sharp, stabbing pain
medical

Examples

The patient described a lancinating pain shooting down her leg.
Migraine sufferers often experience intense, lancinating headaches.
A lancinating sensation in his abdomen made him double over in discomfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stabbing - (of pain) sharp and sudden, like a stab
  • Synonym: piercing - (of pain or sensation) extremely sharp or intense

Etymology

From Latin 'lancinare' (to tear, mangle), via French 'lanciner'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈlæn.sɪˌneɪ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlan.sɪ.neɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
(of pain or sensation) sharp and stabbing, like a cut from a knife; intensely piercing.
sharp, stabbing (pain or sensation)
medical

Examples

The patient described a lancinating pain shooting down his leg whenever he moved.
She was awakened by a lancinating headache that felt as if her skull was being pierced.
Doctors noted the lancinating nature of the nerve pain during the examination.
