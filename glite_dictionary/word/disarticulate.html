to disarticulate

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪsɑːrˈtɪkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɑːˈtɪkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to separate at the joints, especially in anatomical or culinary contexts
separate at the joints
medical

Examples

The surgeon had to disarticulate the joint to proceed with the operation.
She learned how to disarticulate a chicken for a cooking class.
Paleontologists often disarticulate fossils to study individual bones.

Synset Examples

disjoint the chicken before cooking it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disjoint - to separate at the joints
  • Synonym: separate - to cause to move or be apart

Etymology

From Latin dis- (apart) + articulatus (jointed).
