to plasticise

1. VERB

/ ˈplæstəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈplɑːstɪsaɪz / (BrE)
to make a material, especially a polymer, flexible or moldable by adding a plasticizer or similar substance
make a material flexible with a plasticizer

Examples

Manufacturers often plasticise PVC to create flexible tubing for medical use.
You need to plasticise the material before it can be molded into shape.
The addition of certain chemicals can plasticise otherwise rigid polymers.

Synset Examples

plasticized PVC

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to plasticize - to make plastic, as by the addition of a plasticizer (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'plastic' + '-ise', influenced by Latin 'plasticus' and Greek 'plastikos' (able to be molded).

2. VERB

/ ˈplæstɪˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈplɑːstɪsʌɪz / (BrE)
(of a material) to become soft, flexible, or moldable, especially due to the addition of a plasticizer.
become soft or flexible (esp. with plasticizer)

Examples

The material will plasticise when heated, making it easy to shape.
Certain additives cause PVC to plasticise and become more flexible.
If the mixture does not plasticise properly, the final product may crack.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to harden - to become hard or less flexible

Etymology

From 'plastic' + '-ise', meaning to make or become plastic.

Synset POS: v
