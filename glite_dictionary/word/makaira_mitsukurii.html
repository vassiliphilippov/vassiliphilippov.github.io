Makaira mitsukurii

1. NOUN

/ məˈkaɪrə mɪtsuˈkuːriː / (AmE), / məˈkaɪərə mɪtsʊˈkʊəriː / (BrE)
A large species of Pacific food and game fish, also known as the Pacific blue marlin, recognized by its dark blue vertical stripes.
species of Pacific blue marlin

Examples

The Makaira mitsukurii is highly prized by sport fishers in the Pacific Ocean.
Marine biologists tracked the migration routes of Makaira mitsukurii to learn more about its behavior.
Several restaurants in Hawaii serve dishes featuring the firm, flavorful meat of Makaira mitsukurii.

Etymology

From modern Latin; genus Makaira from Greek makaira (sword), after the fish's elongated snout, and Japanese surname Mitsukuri, honoring zoologist Kakichi Mitsukuri.
