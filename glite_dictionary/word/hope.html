hope

1. NOUN

/ hoʊp / (AmE), / həʊp / (BrE)
A feeling of expectation and desire for a certain thing to happen.
feeling of expectation or desire

Examples

Even in difficult times, she never lost her hope for a better future.
There was a feeling of hope in the air after the announcement.
His hope that things would improve kept him going.

Synset Examples

in spite of his troubles he never gave up hope

Related Terms

  • Antonym: despair - the complete loss or absence of hope
  • Synonym: optimism - a tendency to expect the best possible outcome

Etymology

From Old English hopa (confidence in the future).

6. NOUN

/ hoʊp / (AmE), / həʊp / (BrE)
In Christian theology, a belief in and desire for eternal life and trust in God's promises, regarded as one of the three theological virtues alongside faith and charity.
Christian virtue: trust in salvation

Examples

Christianity regards hope as a vital force that strengthens believers in times of difficulty.
The three theological virtues are faith, hope, and charity, according to St. Paul.
She relied on her hope in eternal life to guide her through hard times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faith - belief and trust in God, another Christian virtue
  • Synonym: charity - Christian love; another theological virtue
