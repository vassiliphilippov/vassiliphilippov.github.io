literate

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɪt̬.ə.rət / (AmE), / ˈlɪt.ər.ət / (BrE)
able to read and write, especially in the context of having basic education.
able to read and write

Examples

Only 70% of the population is literate.
The teacher worked hard to help all her students become literate.
Much of his work focuses on improving literate skills among adults.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: illiterate - unable to read or write

Etymology

From Latin litteratus (learned, lettered), from littera (letter).

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɪt̬.ɚ.ət / (AmE), / ˈlɪt.ər.ət / (BrE)
having knowledge or competence in a particular subject or field, especially beyond basic reading and writing abilities
knowledgeable or competent in a field

Examples

Employers increasingly expect workers to be computer literate in today's digital world.
She is literate in both environmental science and economics.
Many elderly people are not yet internet literate, which can limit their access to information.

Related Terms
