Geranium viscosissimum

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈreɪniəm vɪˌskɒsəˈsɪməm / (AmE), / dʒəˈreɪnɪəm vɪˌskɒsɪˈsɪməm / (BrE)
A species of wild geranium native to western North America, noted for its sticky leaves and pinkish-purple flowers in open clusters.
a wild geranium of western North America

Examples

The Geranium viscosissimum thrives in sunny meadows and open woodlands across the Rocky Mountains.
Gardeners appreciate the unique stickiness of Geranium viscosissimum when handling its foliage.
Wildflower guides often feature the pink-purple blossoms of Geranium viscosissimum as a native species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geranium - any plant of the genus Geranium

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Geranium and species epithet 'viscosissimum' meaning 'very sticky.'
