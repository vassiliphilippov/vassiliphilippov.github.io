skinful

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɪnfʊl / (AmE), / ˈskɪnfʊl / (BrE)
an amount of alcoholic drink that is enough to make someone drunk
enough alcohol to get drunk
informal british

Examples

After a skinful at the pub, he couldn't walk home straight.
She swore never to have a skinful again after that embarrassing night.
It's obvious from his behavior that he's had more than a skinful.

Synset Examples

someone had to drive me home last night because I had a skinful

Etymology

From 'skin' (referring to a skin used as a wine container) + 'ful' (full); originally referring to a quantity of drink held in a skin.
