Galatia

1. PROPN

/ ɡəˈleɪʃə / (AmE), / ɡəˈleɪʃiə / (BrE)
an ancient region in central Asia Minor, inhabited by Celtic tribes and later a province of the Roman Empire.
ancient region in central Asia Minor

Examples

The apostle Paul wrote a letter to the churches in Galatia.
Archaeologists have discovered ancient artifacts from Galatia in modern-day Turkey.
The people of Galatia were originally of Celtic origin.

Etymology

From Latin 'Galatia', from Ancient Greek 'Γαλατία' (Galatía), referring to the Gauls (Celts) who settled there.
