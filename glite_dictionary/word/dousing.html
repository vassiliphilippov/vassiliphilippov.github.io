dousing

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdaʊsɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of wetting something thoroughly by submerging it in liquid
the act of soaking by submerging

Examples

The sudden dousing of the campfire surprised everyone.
After a dousing in the lake, the clothes were completely soaked.
The flowers looked refreshed after a heavy dousing from the hose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soaking - the act of making something very wet by immersing it in liquid
  • Antonym: drying - the process of removing moisture; opposite of wetting

Etymology

From the verb 'douse', origin uncertain but attested from the late 17th century.
