Myosotis sylvatica

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪəˈsoʊtɪs sɪlˈvætɪkə / (AmE), / ˌmaɪəˈsəʊtɪs sɪlˈvætɪkə / (BrE)
A small biennial or perennial herb native to Europe, northern Africa, and western Asia, with blue, purple, or white flowers; commonly known as the wood forget-me-not.
a species of wood forget-me-not

Examples

The shady garden bed was filled with bright blue Myosotis sylvatica in early spring.
Many wildflower enthusiasts plant Myosotis sylvatica to attract bees and butterflies.
She identified the delicate blossoms as Myosotis sylvatica while hiking in the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forget-me-not - Common name for plants of the genus Myosotis, including Myosotis sylvatica

Etymology

From Greek 'myosotis' meaning 'mouse's ear' (referring to the shape of the leaves) and Latin 'sylvaticus' meaning 'of the woods'.
