torturing

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔrtʃərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɔːtʃərɪŋ / (BrE)
the deliberate infliction of physical or mental suffering in order to force someone to provide information, confess, or for other reasons
infliction of severe suffering

Examples

The torturing of prisoners was condemned by international organizations as a violation of human rights.
Historical accounts documented the torturing of suspects to obtain confessions during the inquisition.
Psychological torturing can be just as damaging as physical abuse.

Synset Examples

it required unnatural torturing to extract a confession

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torture - the act of inflicting severe pain as a punishment or to force information

Etymology

From the verb 'torture', ultimately from Latin 'torquere' (to twist).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtɔrtʃərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɔːtʃərɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing extreme pain or suffering; extremely painful or distressing.
extremely painful or distressing

Examples

The torturing pain in his leg made it impossible to walk.
She endured a torturing wait for the test results to arrive.
His silence was more torturing than any harsh words could have been.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agonizing - causing great physical or mental pain
  • Synonym: excruciating - extremely painful
  • Antonym: mild - not severe or strong

Etymology

From 'torture' + '-ing'; 'torture' from Latin 'torquēre' (to twist).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
