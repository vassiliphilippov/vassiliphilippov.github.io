prefiguration

1. NOUN

/ ˌpriːˌfɪɡjəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːˌfɪɡjʊˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
an event or example that foreshadows or anticipates what is to come
foreshadowing event or example

Examples

The hero's childhood trauma serves as a prefiguration of the challenges he faces as an adult.
Medieval artists often used Old Testament scenes as a prefiguration of events in the New Testament.
Her early interest in science was a clear prefiguration of her later career as a researcher.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foreshadowing - a warning or indication of a future event

Etymology

From Latin praefiguratio (a foreshadowing), from prae- (before) + figurare (to form, shape).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpriːˌfɪɡjəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːfɪɡjʊˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of representing, indicating, or suggesting something in advance, especially in a way that foreshadows or anticipates a later event or development
act of foreshadowing or anticipating

Examples

The mural was seen as a prefiguration of the city's future prosperity.
Some critics interpret the poem as a prefiguration of the social upheaval to come.
Her early novels contain a clear prefiguration of themes she would explore in later works.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foreshadowing - the act of indicating or suggesting something beforehand

Etymology

From Latin praefiguratio (foreshadowing), from prae- (before) + figuratio (representation).
