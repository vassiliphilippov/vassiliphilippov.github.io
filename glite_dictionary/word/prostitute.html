prostitute

1. VERB

/ ˈprɑː.stəˌtut / (AmE), / ˈprɒs.tɪ.tjuːt / (BrE)
(of a person) to engage in sexual activity in exchange for payment; to sell one's body for money.
engage in sex for money

Examples

She was forced to prostitute herself to make ends meet.
He admitted that he had prostituted himself several times last year.
They accused her of prostituting herself on the streets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to solicit - seek clients as a prostitute
  • Synonym: whore - to have sex with someone for payment

Etymology

From Latin 'prostituere' (to expose publicly, offer for sale).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.stəˌtut / (AmE), / ˈprɒs.tɪ.tjuːt / (BrE)
a woman who has sex with people in exchange for money
a woman who has sex for money

Examples

The police arrested a prostitute during their raid on the hotel.
She became a prostitute to support her family during hard times.
Some believe that legalizing prostitute work could protect vulnerable individuals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sex worker - person who provides sexual services in exchange for money
  • Antonym: john - a man who pays for sex with a prostitute

Etymology

From Latin prostituta, past participle of prostituere (to expose publicly, offer for sale).
