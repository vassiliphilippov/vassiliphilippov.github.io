3. ADJ

/ pɪŋk / (AmE), / pɪŋk / (BrE)
of a light red color, often associated with flowers of the same name, especially carnations.
light red color

Examples

She wore a beautiful pink dress to the party.
The sky turned a soft pink at sunrise.
He painted the walls a light pink to brighten the nursery.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: red - the color at the end of the spectrum next to orange and opposite violet
  • Synonym: rose - a pinkish-red color, named after the rose flower

Etymology

Possibly from Dutch *pink* (small) or *pink* (a small sea fish), relating to the flower's delicate color; popularized in English from the 17th century for the color.

4. VERB

/ pɪŋk / (AmE), / pɪŋk / (BrE)
(in sewing) to cut fabric with pinking shears, producing a zigzag edge that resists fraying.
cut fabric in a zigzag with pinking shears

Examples

She used sharp scissors to pink the edges of the fabric, giving the dress a decorative touch.
It’s important to pink the seams so the cloth won’t unravel so easily.
After cutting out the pattern, he decided to pink the hems for a clean finish.

Etymology

From 'pinking shears', sewing shears designed to make a zigzag pattern; 'pink' in this sense dates to the 16th century meaning 'to pierce' or 'decorate'.
