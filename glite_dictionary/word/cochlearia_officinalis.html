Cochlearia officinalis

1. NOUN

/ koʊˌklɪəriə ɒfɪˈsɪnəlɪs / (AmE), / kɒˌklɪəriə ɒfɪˈsɪnəlɪs / (BrE)
a species of Arctic cress, commonly known as scurvy-grass, historically used to prevent or treat scurvy due to its high vitamin C content
species of plant known as scurvy-grass

Examples

Researchers studied Cochlearia officinalis for its vitamin C concentration and its historical role in sailors' diets.
In Arctic regions, Cochlearia officinalis grows abundantly along rocky shores.
Herbalists once recommended Cochlearia officinalis to help prevent scurvy during long sea voyages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scurvy-grass - common name for Cochlearia officinalis

Etymology

From Latin: 'Cochlearia' meaning 'spoonwort' and 'officinalis' indicating medicinal use.
