Ozothamnus secundiflorus

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊzoʊˈθæmnəs sɪˌkʌndɪˈflɔrəs / (AmE), / ˌɒzəˈθamnəs sɪˌkʌndɪˈflɔːrəs / (BrE)
A shrub with white woolly branches and woolly leaves, bearing fragrant flowers in long sprays, suitable for drying; sometimes classified in the genus Helichrysum.
woolly shrub with fragrant flowers

Examples

Gardeners often plant Ozothamnus secundiflorus for its attractive sprays of fragrant white flowers.
The woolly leaves of Ozothamnus secundiflorus make it easy to identify in alpine regions.
Dried flowers from Ozothamnus secundiflorus are popular for use in floral arrangements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Helichrysum - a related genus of flowering plants sometimes grouping Ozothamnus secundiflorus

Etymology

From genus name Ozothamnus (Greek ‘ozo’ meaning ‘to smell’ and ‘thamnos’ meaning ‘shrub’); species name secundiflorus from Latin for 'with flowers on one side'.
