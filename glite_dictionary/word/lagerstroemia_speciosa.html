Lagerstroemia speciosa

1. NOUN

/ lɑːɡərˈstriːmiə spɪˈsoʊsə / (AmE), / ˌlæɡəˈstriːmiə spɪˈsəʊsə / (BrE)
a tropical tree native to Asia, Australia, and the East Indies, grown for its large, showy flowers and valued for its timber (pyinma)
ornamental tree with large flowers; source of pyinma timber

Examples

The Lagerstroemia speciosa blooms every summer, filling the park with vibrant pink flowers.
Many gardens in the tropics feature Lagerstroemia speciosa for its ornamental value.
Timber from Lagerstroemia speciosa, known as pyinma, is prized for its durability and beauty.

Etymology

From the genus name Lagerstroemia (after Swedish naturalist Magnus von Lagerström) and species Latin 'speciosa' meaning 'showy'.
