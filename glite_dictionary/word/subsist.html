to subsist

1. VERB

/ səbˈsɪst / (AmE), / səbˈsɪst / (BrE)
to manage to stay alive, especially with limited resources; to maintain or support oneself at a minimal level
maintain or support oneself

Examples

Many rural families subsist on what they can grow in their own fields.
During the war, prisoners had to subsist on very little food.
She managed to subsist on a small pension after retirement.

Synset Examples

he could barely exist on such a low wage
Can you live on $2000 a month in New York City?
Many people in the world have to subsist on $1 a day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to survive - to continue to live or exist, especially in spite of danger or hardship

Etymology

From Latin 'subsistere' (to stand firm, endure).
