old-hat

1. ADJ

/ ˈoʊld ˌhæt / (AmE), / ˈəʊld ˌhæt / (BrE)
no longer fashionable or interesting; outdated
unfashionable; outdated

Examples

That style of music is considered old-hat by most young people today.
His ideas about education seem rather old-hat in the modern world.
Wearing a tie to every meeting is a bit old-hat these days.

Synset Examples

a suit of rather antique appearance
demode (or outmoded) attire
outmoded ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outdated - no longer current or in fashion
  • Antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times
  • Degree Higher: trendy - very fashionable or up to date

2. ADJ

/ ˈoʊld ˈhæt / (AmE), / ˌəʊld ˈhæt / (BrE)
unoriginal or lacking freshness due to overuse; too familiar to be interesting or effective
unoriginal; overused

Examples

Many critics found the movie's plot to be a bit old-hat and uninspired.
His speech was full of old-hat phrases we've all heard before.
Wearing mismatched socks may be quirky, but it's really old-hat these days.

Synset Examples

his remarks were trite and commonplace
hackneyed phrases
a stock answer
repeating threadbare jokes
parroting some timeworn axiom
the trite metaphor ‘hard as nails’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trite - lacking originality or freshness; overused
  • Synonym: hackneyed - overused to the point of losing meaning or impact
  • Antonym: fresh - new or original in character

Etymology

Figurative use of 'old hat,' referring to something familiar and worn, attested from the 19th century.
