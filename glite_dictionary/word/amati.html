Amati

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɑːti / (AmE), / əˈmɑːti / (BrE)
a violin made by Nicolo Amati or a member of the Amati family, renowned Italian violin makers of the 16th to 18th centuries
violin by the Amati family

Examples

The museum displayed a rare Amati in its collection of historic instruments.
She played the Amati with remarkable delicacy during the concert.
Many musicians consider an Amati to be among the finest violins ever made.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Stradivarius - violin made by Antonio Stradivari
  • Synonym: Guarnerius - violin made by a member of the Guarneri family

Etymology

From the Italian surname Amati, famed violin makers of Cremona.

2. PROPN

/ ɑˈmɑːti / (AmE), / əˈmɑːti / (BrE)
A member of a famous Italian family of violin makers from Cremona, notably active in the 16th and 17th centuries, influential in the development of the modern violin.
famous family of Italian violin makers

Examples

The museum displayed a rare Amati violin crafted in the seventeenth century.
Musicians often dream of performing on an Amati because of its renowned sound quality.
During the concert, she played a beautiful Amati that had been restored by a master luthier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Stradivari - Italian violin maker, famous for high-quality instruments
  • Synonym: Guarneri - a family of Italian violin makers from Cremona

Etymology

Italian surname, from the family of violin makers in Cremona, Italy.
