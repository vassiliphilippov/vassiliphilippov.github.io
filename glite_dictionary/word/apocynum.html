Apocynum

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɑːsɪnəm / (AmE), / əˈpɒsɪnəm / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbs, commonly known as dogbane, with small pink or white flowers, native to North America and Eurasia.
a genus of perennial herbs (dogbane)

Examples

Apocynum is often found growing in wet meadows and along stream banks.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Apocynum for its attractive foliage and flowers.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of various Apocynum species.

Etymology

From Greek 'apokynon', meaning 'dogbane'.

2. NOUN

/ əˈpɑːsɪˌnʌm / (AmE), / əˈpɒsɪnəm / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbs, commonly known as dogbanes, with small pink or white flowers, found in North America and Eurasia.
a genus of perennial plants (dogbane)

Examples

The species Apocynum cannabinum is native to North America and commonly called Indian hemp.
Many members of the genus Apocynum are known for their milky sap, which is toxic to animals.
Botanists study Apocynum because of its traditional medicinal uses and distinctive flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogbane - common name for plants of the genus Apocynum

Etymology

From Latin *Apocynum*, from Greek *apokynon* ('dogbane').
