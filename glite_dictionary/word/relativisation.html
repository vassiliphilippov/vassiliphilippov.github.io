relativisation

1. NOUN

/ rɛl.ə.t̬ə.vaɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌlæt.ɪ.vaɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making something relative rather than absolute
the act of making something relative

Examples

The relativisation of moral values can lead to debates about universal ethics.
Modern physics necessitated the relativisation of concepts such as time and space.
Philosophers often discuss the relativisation of truth in different cultural contexts.

Synset Examples

The relativization of the concepts of length and intervals of time appears difficult to many, but probably only because it is strange.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relativization - alternate spelling, chiefly American

Etymology

From the verb 'relativise' plus the suffix '-ation'.
