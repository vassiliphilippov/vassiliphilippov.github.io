carambola

1. NOUN

/ ˌkærəmˈboʊlə / (AmE), / ˌkærəmˈbəʊlə / (BrE)
A deeply ridged, yellow-brown tropical fruit, used raw in salads or as a dessert when ripe, also known as starfruit.
tropical fruit also called starfruit

Examples

The carambola is often sliced to reveal its star-shaped cross-section in fruit salads.
You can eat a ripe carambola on its own or use it as a garnish for drinks.
Farmers in tropical regions cultivate carambolas for export to other countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: starfruit - another name for carambola

Etymology

From Portuguese 'carambola', from Marathi 'karambal'.

2. NOUN

/ kɛrəŋˈboʊlə / (AmE), / kærəmˈbəʊlə / (BrE)
An East Indian tree (Averrhoa carambola) bearing deeply ridged, yellow-brown fruit, also known as starfruit.
East Indian tree with star-shaped fruit

Examples

The carambola tree produces fruit that is crisp and juicy when ripe.
Slices of carambola add a decorative touch to fruit salads because of their star shape.
Farmers in tropical regions often cultivate carambola for its unique appearance and tart flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: starfruit - another name for the carambola fruit

Etymology

From Spanish and Portuguese 'carambola', from Marathi 'karambal'.
