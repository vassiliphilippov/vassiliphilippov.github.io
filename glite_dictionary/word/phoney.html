phoney

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊni / (AmE), / ˈfəʊni / (BrE)
a person who pretends to have certain beliefs or feelings in order to conceal their true motives; an insincere or fake person
insincere or fake person

Examples

Everyone could tell that Mark was a phoney who was only pretending to care about the cause.
She stopped trusting him after realizing he was a complete phoney.
The charity exposed the phoney who was pretending to collect donations for personal profit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fraud - a person who deceives others by pretending to be something they are not
  • Synonym: imposter - a person who pretends to be someone else to deceive others
  • Antonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be; authentic
  • Antonym: authentic - of undisputed origin; genuine

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly an alteration of 'fawney' (a gilt brass ring used by swindlers), from Irish fainne (ring).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfoʊni / (AmE), / ˈfəʊni / (BrE)
fraudulent or having a misleading appearance; not genuine or real
fraudulent; not genuine

Examples

His apology sounded a little too phoney to be believable.
The jewels turned out to be phoney after they were examined by an expert.
She gave him a phoney smile and quickly walked away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fake - not genuine; a forgery or imitation
  • Antonym: genuine - real; actually what something is said to be

Etymology

Originally American English slang; possibly an alteration of 'fawney' (a gilt brass ring used by swindlers), of Irish origin.
