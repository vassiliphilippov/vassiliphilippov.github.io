gambling

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæm.blɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡæm.blɪŋ / (BrE)
The activity of playing games of chance for money or other valuable stakes, hoping to win a prize.
playing games of chance for stakes

Examples

He lost a lot of money through gambling at the casino.
The government is trying to regulate online gambling.
She decided to quit gambling after realizing its negative impact on her life.

Synset Examples

his gambling cost him a fortune
there was heavy play at the blackjack table

Related Terms

  • Synonym: betting - the act of wagering money on an outcome
  • Synonym: gaming - the activity of playing games of chance for money

Etymology

From the verb 'gamble,' likely of uncertain Low German or Scandinavian origin.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡæm.blɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡæm.blɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person) habitually taking part in games of chance or engaged in risky betting activities.
habitually betting or playing games of chance

Examples

He was known as a gambling man, always looking for the next wager to place.
The novel features a gambling young woman who risks everything at the casino.
Her father warned her about the dangers of a gambling lifestyle.

Synset Examples

led a dissipated life
a betting man
a card-playing son of a bitch
a gambling fool
sporting gents and their ladies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: betting - engaging in or addicted to wagering money on outcomes
  • Synonym: dissipated - overindulging in sensual pleasures, especially gambling or drinking

Etymology

From the verb 'gamble' + '-ing'. 'Gamble' probably of English origin, linked to 'game'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01300894-s
Synset POS: s
