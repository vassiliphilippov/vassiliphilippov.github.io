Alcidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈælsəˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈalsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of web-footed diving seabirds of northern seas, including auks, puffins, guillemots, and murres.
family of diving seabirds (auks, puffins, etc.)

Examples

The Alcidae family includes well-known species such as puffins and guillemots.
Researchers studied the migratory patterns of several Alcidae species in the North Atlantic.
Unlike penguins, members of the Alcidae spend much of their lives at sea.

Etymology

From New Latin *Alcidae*, based on genus *Alca* (auk).
