stringy

1. ADJ

/ ˈstrɪŋi / (AmE), / ˈstrɪŋi / (BrE)
forming viscous or glutinous threads, often appearing in long, thin strands; resembling string
forming sticky, thread-like strands

Examples

The melted cheese on the pizza was so stringy that it stretched with every bite.
Some soups become stringy if the egg is not stirred properly as it cooks.
She didn't like the stringy texture of overcooked okra.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fibrous - containing, consisting of, or resembling fibers

Etymology

From 'string' + -y, denoting the quality of resembling string.

2. ADJ

/ ˈstrɪŋi / (AmE), / ˈstrɪŋi / (BrE)
consisting of or containing long, thin strands, or having a texture like string, often tough or fibrous
having long, thin, string-like strands

Examples

The meat was overcooked and became stringy, making it difficult to chew.
He picked up a piece of stringy cheese that stretched from his pizza to his mouth.
Her hair looked stringy after swimming in the pool all day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fibrous - containing, consisting of, or resembling fibers

Etymology

From 'string' + '-y', meaning resembling or having the qualities of string.

3. ADJ

/ ˈstrɪŋi / (AmE), / ˈstrɪŋi / (BrE)
(of meat or other food) containing tough, fibrous strands; difficult to chew or bite through.
containing tough, fibrous strands (esp. in food)

Examples

The roast was so stringy that it was hard to eat.
She pushed aside the stringy pieces of chicken in her soup.
Overcooking can make beef become tough and stringy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tough - (of food) difficult to cut or chew
  • Antonym: tender - (of meat) easy to cut or chew

Etymology

From 'string' + '-y', meaning having the quality of strings.

4. ADJ

/ ˈstrɪŋi / (AmE), / ˈstrɪŋi / (BrE)
(of a person's body or meat) lean and sinewy, with prominent muscles or little fat.
lean and sinewy (body or meat)

Examples

The old man's stringy arms showed the hard life he had lived.
This chicken is too stringy to eat—it feels tough and chewy.
She was tall and stringy, with wiry muscles from years of working outdoors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sinewy - lean and muscular, with strong muscles
  • Degree Lower: scrawny - unattractively thin and bony
  • Degree Higher: muscular - having well-developed muscles

Etymology

Formed from 'string' plus the suffix '-y', indicating resemblance.
