fawner

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔnər / (AmE), / ˈfɔːnə / (BrE)
a person who behaves in an excessively flattering or servile manner in order to gain favor; someone who lacks self-respect to overly please others
person who flatters or is servile to gain favor

Examples

Everyone could see that the new assistant was a fawner, always complimenting the boss excessively.
Tired of being surrounded by fawners, the manager appreciated honest feedback from her team.
He resented being labeled a fawner just because he treated his superiors with respect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sycophant - a person who acts obsequiously toward someone important
  • Synonym: flatterer - someone who praises others insincerely to gain favor
  • Synonym: toady - a person who behaves obsequiously to someone important

Etymology

From 'fawn' (to exhibit affection or servility), from Old French 'faon' (young animal).
