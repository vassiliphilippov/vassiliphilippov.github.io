scope

1. NOUN

/ skoʊp / (AmE), / skəʊp / (BrE)
an electronic instrument that displays visual representations of electrical signals, typically for measurement or analysis
electronic instrument for visualizing electrical signals

Examples

The technician used a scope to check the signal output from the circuit.
Modern laboratories often have at least one digital scope for troubleshooting electronic equipment.
She adjusted the scope to get a clearer view of the waveform on the screen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oscilloscope - an instrument for displaying, measuring, and analyzing electronic signals

Etymology

Short for 'oscilloscope,' from Greek 'skopein' (to look, examine), via French '-scope.'

2. NOUN

/ skoʊp / (AmE), / skəʊp / (BrE)
an optical device used to magnify images of distant objects, such as a telescope or rifle sight
optical device for magnifying distant objects

Examples

The hunter peered through the scope to spot deer on the far hill.
She adjusted the scope on her rifle to improve her aim.
Astronomers use a powerful scope to observe distant planets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telescope - an instrument for viewing distant objects
  • Synonym: microscope - an instrument for viewing small objects in detail

Etymology

Shortened form of 'telescope' and similar words, ultimately from Greek 'skopein' (to look at).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ skoʊp / (AmE), / skəʊp / (BrE)
the extent or range covered by an activity, subject, or authority; the area within which something has effect or influence
extent or range of something's influence

Examples

The scope of her research includes both ancient and modern history.
These rules are only valid within the scope of this agreement.
The committee debated whether the project fell outside their scope.

Synset Examples

a piano has a greater range than the human voice

within the compass of this article

outside the reach of the law
in the political orbit of a world power

Related Terms

  • Synonym: range - the extent covered between limits; the set of limits of action or influence
  • Synonym: ambit - the scope, extent, or bounds of something
  • Synonym: extent - the range to which something spreads or extends
  • Antonym: limit - a point or level beyond which something does not or may not extend or pass

Etymology

From Italian *scopo* (aim, purpose), from Latin *scopos*, from Greek *skopos* (target, aim).
