portulaca

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɔrt͡ʃuˈlækə / (AmE), / ˌpɔːtjuːˈlækə / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Portulaca, known for its small, ephemeral flowers in colors such as pink, red, purple, or white.
a flowering plant of the genus Portulaca

Examples

The portulaca is admired for its vibrant blossoms that open only in sunlight.
She planted several portulacas along the garden border to add more color.
Because portulaca thrives in dry soil, it is perfect for rock gardens.

Etymology

From Latin *portulaca* (purslane).
