armoured

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑːrmɚd / (AmE), / ˈɑːməd / (BrE)
protected by armor, especially in a military context
protected by armor
british

Examples

The convoy moved cautiously, escorted by several armoured vehicles.
He wore an armoured vest for extra protection during the mission.
The museum displays an impressive collection of armoured knights from the medieval period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armored - protected by armor, especially in a military context (American spelling)

Etymology

From Middle English 'armured', from Old French 'armuré', from 'armure' (armor).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɑːr.mɚd / (AmE), / ˈɑː.məd / (BrE)
(of animals) having a protective covering, such as a shell, scales, or thick skin, that offers defense against predators or environmental hazards.
having a protective covering (zoological)

Examples

The armoured armadillo rolls into a ball to defend itself from predators.
Many dinosaurs, such as the ankylosaurus, were armoured for protection.
The armoured scales of the pangolin make it difficult for most animals to harm it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protected - shielded from harm or danger
  • Antonym: vulnerable - susceptible to harm or attack

Etymology

From Old French 'armure', influenced by 'armor' meaning defensive covering.

Source: WordNet
