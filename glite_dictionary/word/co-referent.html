co-referent

1. ADJ

/ ˌkoʊ.rɪˈfɛr.ənt / (AmE), / ˌkəʊ.rɪˈfɛr.ənt / (BrE)
(of words or phrases) referring to the same person or thing in a given context.
referring to the same entity (linguistics)

Examples

In the sentence 'The president said he would resign,' 'the president' and 'he' are co-referent expressions.
Linguists study the conditions under which noun phrases become co-referent.
It is important to determine if two pronouns are co-referent in discourse analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coreferential - relating to or being words or phrases that refer to the same entity

Etymology

Formed from 'co-' (together) and 'referent' (thing referred to).
