breathalyzer

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɛθəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈbrɛθəˌlaɪzə / (BrE)
a device used to measure the amount of alcohol in a person's breath, typically to determine intoxication
device measuring alcohol in breath

Examples

The police officer asked him to take a breathalyzer test after noticing the smell of alcohol.
She failed the breathalyzer and was charged with driving under the influence.
Modern breathalyzers are portable and provide results within seconds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alcohol tester - a device for testing the presence of alcohol

Etymology

From 'breath' + 'analyzer'; originally a trademark in the US.
