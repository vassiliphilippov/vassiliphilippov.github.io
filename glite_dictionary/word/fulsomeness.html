fulsomeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊl.səm.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfʊl.səm.nəs / (BrE)
excessive or insincere praise, especially when it seems superficial or affected
excessive or insincere praise

Examples

His fulsomeness made everyone in the meeting uncomfortable, as the compliments seemed forced and insincere.
The actor was wary of fulsomeness from fans, preferring genuine feedback over exaggerated praise.
She responded to his fulsomeness with a polite smile, recognizing the compliment as empty and overdone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flattery - excessive or insincere praise
  • Antonym: sincerity - the quality of being honest or genuine

Etymology

From Middle English 'fulsom' (excessive, offensive), from 'ful' (full) + '-som' (some).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfʊl.səm.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfʊl.səm.nəs / (BrE)
excessive or insincere earnestness, especially when self-serving or intended to flatter
excessive insincere earnestness or flattery

Examples

His fulsomeness during the meeting made his compliments seem insincere.
Many politicians are criticized for the fulsomeness of their public statements.
The fulsomeness of her praise left everyone feeling slightly uncomfortable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insincerity - lack of sincerity or genuineness
  • Degree Lower: earnestness - seriousness or sincerity
  • Synonym: obsequiousness - excessive willingness to serve or please others

Etymology

From Middle English *fulsum* (copious, offensive); influenced by Old English *ful* (full, foul) and the suffix *-some*.
