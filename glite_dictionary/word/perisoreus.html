Perisoreus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrɪˈsɔriəs / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˈsɔːriəs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the crow family Corvidae, including the Canada jay and related species.
genus of birds (Canada jay group)

Examples

The genus Perisoreus includes the well-known Canada jay, also called the gray jay.
Researchers observed behavioral differences among the three species classified under Perisoreus.
The diet of a Perisoreus primarily consists of insects, berries, and small animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Canada jay - a bird species of the genus Perisoreus

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Greek 'peri-' (around) and 'soreus' (heap, possibly alluding to the bird’s habit of storing food).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɛr.ɪˈsɔːr.i.əs / (AmE), / ˌpɛr.ɪˈsɔːr.i.əs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the crow and jay family (Corvidae), including the Canada jay and related species.
genus of jays (birds)

Examples

The Perisoreus genus includes several species of jays found in northern forests.
Scientists observed food caching behavior in multiple members of Perisoreus.
The Canada jay, also known as the gray jay, belongs to the genus Perisoreus.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek roots: peri- (around) + soros (heap, pile), possibly referring to the birds’ food-hoarding behavior.
