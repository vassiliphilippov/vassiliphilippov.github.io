declination

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛklɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛklɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a downward slope or bend; the state or position of sloping downward
downward slope or bend

Examples

The mountain path became more difficult after the sharp declination near the summit.
Erosion gradually increased the declination of the hillside over the years.
They set up camp at the bottom of a gentle declination overlooking the valley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incline - an upward or downward slope
  • Antonym: ascent - an upward slope or climb

Etymology

From Latin 'declinatio' (a bending down), from 'declinare' (to bend down).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛklɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛklɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
(astronomy) The angular distance of a celestial object north or south of the celestial equator, measured in degrees and used with right ascension to specify its position in the sky.
angular distance from celestial equator (astronomy)

Examples

The star's declination determines how high it appears above the horizon.
Astronomers use right ascension and declination to locate objects in the night sky.
The planet's declination changed as it moved along its orbit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: right ascension - the angular distance of a point measured eastward along the celestial equator from the vernal equinox

Etymology

From Latin declinatio (a bending or turning away, a declining).
