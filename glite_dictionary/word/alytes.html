Alytes

1. NOUN

/ əˈlaɪtiːz / (AmE), / əˈlaɪtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of small European toads known as midwife toads, noted for the males' unique habit of carrying fertilized eggs on their hind legs until they hatch.
genus of midwife toads

Examples

The Alytes are often found in southern Europe, inhabiting forested or rocky areas.
Unlike many other amphibians, male Alytes take care of the eggs externally.
Biologists have studied Alytes to understand the evolution of parental care in amphibians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: midwife toad - a species of toad in the genus Alytes known for males carrying eggs

Etymology

From Greek alytos, meaning 'not released' or 'incessant'.

2. NOUN

/ əˈlaɪtiːz / (AmE), / əˈlaɪtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of toads known as midwife toads, characterized by the males carrying fertilized eggs wrapped around their hind legs until they hatch.
genus of midwife toads

Examples

The genus Alytes includes the well-known midwife toads of Europe.
Male members of Alytes are unique for carrying eggs on their hind legs.
Scientists discovered a new species within the genus Alytes in the mountains of Portugal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: midwife toad - a toad in the genus Alytes, notable for male egg-carrying behavior

Etymology

From Greek alytos (unceasing, never released).
