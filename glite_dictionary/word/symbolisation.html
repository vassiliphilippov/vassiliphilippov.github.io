symbolisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪmbələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪmbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of using symbols to represent ideas, qualities, or meanings
use of symbols to convey meaning

Examples

The symbolisation of abstract concepts is a vital part of human communication.
Modern art often relies heavily on the symbolisation of emotions and ideas.
Language acquisition in children includes the symbolisation of objects and actions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symbolization - alternative spelling, especially in American English

Etymology

From symbol + -isation, after French 'symbolisation' or Latin 'symbolizare'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɪmbələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪmbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of representing something by symbols or attributing symbolic meaning to objects, actions, or ideas
the act of giving symbolic meaning

Examples

In literature, symbolisation can turn an ordinary object into a powerful motif.
The symbolisation of colors varies greatly between cultures and contexts.
Psychoanalysis often explores the symbolisation of dreams and unconscious thoughts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symbolization - the American English spelling of 'symbolisation'
  • Antonym: literalization - the act of interpreting or representing something in a literal sense

Etymology

From French *symbolisation*, from *symboliser* (to symbolise), ultimately from Greek *symbolon* (token, sign).

3. NOUN

/ ˌsɪmbələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪmbəlaɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the use of a visible object or image to represent something abstract, especially by association or convention
representation of something abstract by a symbol

Examples

The dove is often used as a symbolisation of peace in religious art.
The flag's design includes many elements of symbolisation representing the nation's history.
Throughout literature, animals are frequently chosen for their symbolisation of human traits.

Synset Examples

the eagle is a symbol of the United States

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symbolism - the practice of using symbols to represent ideas or qualities
  • Synonym: representation - the action of speaking or acting on behalf of someone or the depiction of something

Etymology

From symbol + -isation, modeled after French 'symbolisation'.
