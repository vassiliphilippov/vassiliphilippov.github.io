eversion

1. NOUN

/ iˈvɜrʒən / (AmE), / ɪˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
the act or state of being turned outward, especially in reference to a part of the body such as the foot or eyelid
the state of being turned outward
medical

Examples

Physical therapists often check for eversion of the ankle during rehabilitation.
Abnormal eversion of the eyelid can require surgical correction.
The doctor demonstrated eversion of the foot to test for muscle weakness.

Synset Examples

the eversion of the foot

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inversion - the act or state of turning inward, especially of a body part

Etymology

From Latin evertere (to turn out), from e- (out) + vertere (to turn).

2. NOUN

/ iˈvɜr.ʒən / (AmE), / ɪˈvɜː.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of turning something inside out or outward
act of turning inside out

Examples

In medicine, eversion of the eyelid is sometimes performed to examine its inner surface.
The injury resulted in the eversion of the foot, causing damage to the ligaments.
Some sea creatures protect themselves by eversion of their internal organs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inversion - the act of turning something upside down or inward

Etymology

From Latin 'eversio' (an overturning), from 'evertere' (to overturn).
