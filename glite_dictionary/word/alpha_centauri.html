Alpha Centauri

1. PROPN

/ ˈæl.fə sɛnˈtɔːr.i / (AmE), / ˈælfə sɛnˈtɔːri / (BrE)
the brightest star in the constellation Centaurus and the second closest star system to the Sun
the brightest star in Centaurus; nearby star system

Examples

Scientists have identified a planet orbiting Alpha Centauri.
On a clear night, Alpha Centauri is visible to the naked eye from the Southern Hemisphere.
Ancient navigators often used Alpha Centauri as a guide for their journeys.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Proxima Centauri - the closest star to the Sun, part of the Alpha Centauri system

Etymology

From Greek 'alpha' (first letter of the Greek alphabet) and Latin 'Centauri' (of Centaurus, the constellation).
