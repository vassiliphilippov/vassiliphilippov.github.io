unlighted

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈlaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈlaɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a candle, lamp, cigarette, etc.) not set on fire or burning; not lit.
not set on fire or burning

Examples

The table was bare, with the unlighted candles casting no glow.
She put the unlighted cigarette back into the pack.
The street remained dark, as the lamps were still unlighted.

Synset Examples

the table was bare, the candles unlighted
held an unlit cigarette

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unlit - (of something that can be lit) not burning or shining

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'lighted' (past participle of 'light').

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈlaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈlaɪtɪd / (BrE)
not provided with light; not illuminated.
not illuminated; without light

Examples

We walked down the unlighted path, feeling our way in the darkness.
The parking lot was unlighted after the power outage last night.
He was uneasy about leaving his car on an unlighted street.

Synset Examples

came up the lightless stairs
the unilluminated side of Mars
through dark unlighted (or unlit) streets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unlit - not lit; not illuminated
  • Antonym: illuminated - lit up; provided with light

Etymology

Formed by prefix 'un-' (not) + 'lighted' (supplied with light).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00276851-s
Synset POS: s
