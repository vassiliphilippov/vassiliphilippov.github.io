miterwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪtərˌwɔrt / (AmE), / ˈmaɪtəˌwɜːt / (BrE)
Any of several perennial herbs of the genus Mitella, with capsules that resemble a bishop's miter.
herb of the genus Mitella

Examples

The shady woodland was dotted with clusters of delicate miterwort.
Botanists discovered a rare variety of miterwort in the mountain valley.
The heart-shaped leaves of the miterwort make it easy to identify in the wild.

Etymology

From 'miter', referring to the bishop's headdress, and 'wort', an old term for plant.
