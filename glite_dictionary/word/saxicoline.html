saxicoline

1. ADJ

/ ˌsæksɪˈkoʊliːn / (AmE), / ˌsæksɪˈkəʊliːn / (BrE)
(of a plant or animal) growing on or living among rocks.
living or growing among rocks

Examples

Some species of lizard are highly saxicoline, thriving exclusively among rocky outcrops.
The saxicoline plants on the mountain slopes are specially adapted to harsh, stony environments.
Researchers discovered a new saxicoline moss clinging to the cliffs near the coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saxicolous - (of an organism) growing on or inhabiting rocks

Etymology

From Latin 'saxum' (rock) + '-coline', from Latin 'colere' (to inhabit).
