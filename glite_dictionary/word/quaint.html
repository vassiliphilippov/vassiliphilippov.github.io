quaint

1. ADJ

/ kweɪnt / (AmE), / kweɪnt / (BrE)
attractively unusual or old-fashioned; charming in an appealingly old-fashioned way, though not necessarily authentic
attractively old-fashioned or charming

Examples

We stayed in a quaint cottage by the lake that looked like it came from another era.
The town is famous for its quaint shops and cobblestone streets, attracting many tourists each year.
Her grandmother's kitchen was full of quaint decorations and vintage utensils.

Synset Examples

houses with quaint thatched roofs
a vaulted roof supporting old-time chimney pots

Related Terms

  • Synonym: picturesque - visually attractive, especially in a quaint or charming way
  • Antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times as opposed to the remote past

Etymology

From Middle English 'queynt', from Old French 'cointe' (clever, elegant), from Latin 'cognitus' (known, acquainted).

2. ADJ

/ kweɪnt / (AmE), / kweɪnt / (BrE)
attractively unusual or old-fashioned; or, in some contexts, strange or incongruous in character or appearance
attractively unusual or old-fashioned; odd

Examples

The cottage had a quaint charm with its crooked walls and thatched roof.
Her quaint sense of humor always made everyone smile at the office.
He wore a quaint hat that looked like something from a different century.

Synset Examples

the head terminating in the quaint duck bill which gives the animal its vernacular name
came forth a quaint and fearful sight
a quaint sense of humor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: odd - strange or unusual
  • Antonym: conventional - normal and lacking in unusual or strange qualities

Etymology

From Middle English 'queynte', from Old French 'cointe' (clever, proud, elegant), from Latin 'cognitus' (known, recognized).

3. ADJ

/ kweɪnt / (AmE), / kweɪnt / (BrE)
Attractively unusual or old-fashioned in a way that is interesting or pleasing.
attractively unusual or old-fashioned

Examples

The village is full of quaint cottages and cobblestone streets.
She collects quaint porcelain teapots from antique shops.
We enjoyed dining at a quaint little café tucked away in an alley.

Synset Examples

quaint dialect words
quaint streets of New Orleans, that most foreign of American cities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eccentric - unconventional and slightly strange
  • Antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times, not old-fashioned

Etymology

From Middle English 'queinte', from Old French 'cointe' (clever, cunning), from Latin 'cognitus' (known).
