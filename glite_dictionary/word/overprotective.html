overprotective

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvərprəˈtɛktɪv / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəprəˈtɛktɪv / (BrE)
too concerned about someone's safety or well-being, often to the point of limiting their freedom or independence
too protective or controlling

Examples

Some people think their mother is overprotective because she worries constantly about their safety.
The overprotective teacher wouldn't let the children play outside unless the weather was perfect.
His overprotective attitude made it hard for his daughter to make her own decisions.

Synset Examples

overprotective parents

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: protective - giving protection; shielding from harm

Etymology

From 'over-' (excessively) + 'protective' (giving protection).
