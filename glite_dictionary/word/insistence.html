insistence

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɪstəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪstəns / (BrE)
the act of demanding something forcefully or persistently; the fact of stating or maintaining something firmly
the act of insisting or demanding firmly

Examples

Her insistence on attending the meeting surprised everyone, given her usual reluctance.
Despite his friend's advice, he pursued the plan with great insistence.
It was only due to her mother's insistence that she visited the doctor so soon.

Synset Examples

insistence on grammatical correctness is a conservative position

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demand - a strong request or requirement
  • Antonym: refusal - the act of declining something

Etymology

From Latin 'insistere' (to persist, insist), via Middle French and Late Latin.

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɪstəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪstəns / (BrE)
the act of demanding something forcefully, or the fact of refusing to be satisfied with anything less
persistent demands or firm assertion

Examples

His insistence on honesty sometimes made conversations uncomfortable.
Despite her parents' insistence, she decided to follow her own path.
The insistence of the protesters eventually led to changes in government policy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demand - an insistent and peremptory request
  • Degree Lower: request - an act of asking politely or formally for something
  • Synonym: insistence - the act of insisting or asserting

Etymology

From Latin *insistere* (to stand upon, persist) via French *insistance*.

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɪstəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪstəns / (BrE)
the act or state of demanding attention or action in a firm way; persistent urging or emphasis
the act of demanding attention or action

Examples

His insistence on discussing the issue surprised everyone at the meeting.
At her insistence, we checked every detail twice before starting the project.
The insistence of the alarm caught everyone's attention immediately.

Synset Examples

the insistence of their hunger
the press of business matters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demand - an insistent and peremptory request, made as of right
  • Degree Lower: request - an act of asking for something politely or formally
  • Synonym: insistence - the fact of insisting on something strongly

Etymology

From Latin 'insistentia', from 'insistere' (to persist).
