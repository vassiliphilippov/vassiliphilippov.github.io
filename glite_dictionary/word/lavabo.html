lavabo

1. NOUN

/ ləˈveɪboʊ / (AmE), / ləˈvɑːbəʊ / (BrE)
a basin, often found in a church, used for washing the hands
church handwashing basin

Examples

The old monastery has a beautifully carved lavabo in the entrance hall.
Before the service, the priest performed the ritual at the lavabo.
In some traditional homes, a lavabo was installed in the hallway for guests to wash their hands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: washbasin - a bowl or basin for washing hands and face

Etymology

From Latin 'lavabo' (I shall wash), from 'lavare' (to wash); originally from the opening word of a prayer recited by the priest when washing his hands.
