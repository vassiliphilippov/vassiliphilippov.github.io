fraternal

1. ADJ

/ frəˈtɝːnəl / (AmE), / frəˈtɜːnəl / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of brothers; showing brotherly feelings or friendship.
relating to brothers or brotherly friendship

Examples

The club promoted a fraternal spirit among its members.
They shared a deep, almost fraternal bond despite not being related by blood.
She appreciated the fraternal support during her difficult time.

Synset Examples

brotherly feelings
close fraternal ties

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brotherly - showing affection or qualities of a brother
  • Antonym: sisterly - showing affection or qualities of a sister

Etymology

From Latin fraternalis, from frater (brother).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00292337-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ frəˈtɝːnəl / (AmE), / frəˈtɜːnəl / (BrE)
of or relating to a fraternity or society, especially one composed of men or brothers
relating to a fraternity or brotherhood

Examples

The annual banquet was organized by the fraternal society in honor of its founding members.
He became a member of a fraternal organization dedicated to community service.
Several fraternal orders still play a significant role in charitable activities in the city.

Synset Examples

a fraternal order

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fraternity - a society or club of men, especially students

Etymology

From Latin fraternalis, from frater (brother).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02742435-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ frəˈtɝːnəl / (AmE), / frəˈtɜːnəl / (BrE)
(of twins) derived from two separate fertilized eggs, and therefore not genetically identical; dizygotic.
of twins, from two separate eggs

Examples

The doctor explained that they were fraternal twins, not identical ones.
Although fraternal twins can look very different, they are just like any other siblings.
The likelihood of having fraternal twins increases with certain genetic factors.

Synset Examples

fraternal twins are biovular

Related Terms

  • Antonym: identical - (of twins) derived from a single fertilized egg

Etymology

From Latin fraternalis, from frater (brother), reflecting the notion of siblings born at the same time but not identical.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00293164-a
Synset POS: a
