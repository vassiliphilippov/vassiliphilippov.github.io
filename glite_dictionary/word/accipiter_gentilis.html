Accipiter gentilis

1. NOUN

/ ækˈsɪpɪtər ˈdʒɛntɪlɪs / (AmE), / ækˈsɪpɪtə ˈdʒɛntɪlɪs / (BrE)
a large hawk native to Eurasia and North America, commonly known as the northern goshawk, and often used in falconry
a species of large hawk (northern goshawk)

Examples

The Accipiter gentilis is prized in falconry for its agility and hunting skills.
Researchers tracked the migration patterns of Accipiter gentilis across North America.
Unlike smaller hawks, the Accipiter gentilis prefers dense, wooded habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: northern goshawk - a large bird of prey of the family Accipitridae, native to Eurasia and North America

Etymology

From Latin: 'accipiter' meaning 'hawk', 'gentilis' meaning 'of nobility' or 'gentle'.
