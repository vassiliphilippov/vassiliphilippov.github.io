Zurvan

1. PROPN

/ ˈzɜːrvɑːn / (AmE), / ˈzɜːvən / (BrE)
In Zoroastrianism, the god who represents time and fate, regarded as the creator of the universe in the Zurvanite tradition.
Zoroastrian god of time and fate

Examples

Some ancient texts describe Zurvan as the primordial creator of the universe.
In Zurvanism, Zurvan embodies both time and destiny.
Zoroastrian scholars have debated the role of Zurvan in their religious tradition.

Etymology

From Avestan *Zurvan* (time), a concept in ancient Iranian religion.
