Thatcherite

1. NOUN

/ ˈθætʃəˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈθætʃəraɪt / (BrE)
A supporter or advocate of the political policies and ideology associated with Margaret Thatcher, especially those characterized by free-market policies and limited government intervention.
supporter of Margaret Thatcher's policies
british

Examples

He described himself as a proud Thatcherite who believes in individual responsibility and free markets.
During the 1980s, many young Conservatives became known as Thatcherites for their support of privatization policies.
The Thatcherite approach to economic reform influenced center-right parties across Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Thatcherism - the political and economic policies advocated by Margaret Thatcher

Etymology

From the name Margaret Thatcher (British prime minister 1979–1990) + -ite (follower or adherent).
