to gazump

1. VERB

/ ɡəˈzʌmp / (AmE), / ɡəˈzʌmp / (BrE)
(British) To refuse to complete a sale with a previously accepted offer, especially for a property, in order to accept a higher offer from someone else.
refuse a sale to take a higher offer
british slang

Examples

The seller decided to gazump us at the last minute, leaving us without a home.
We thought the house was ours, but we were gazumped by someone offering more money.
Buyers are often frustrated when they are suddenly gazumped during the final stages of purchasing property.

Etymology

British slang, probably a fanciful formation dating from the 1920s; popularized in real estate in the 1980s.

2. VERB

/ ɡəˈzʌmp / (AmE), / ɡəˈzʌmp / (BrE)
to raise the price of a property after previously agreeing to a lower price, often to accept a higher offer from someone else
raise a price after previously agreeing
british informal

Examples

The seller decided to gazump us at the last minute, demanding an extra £10,000 for the house.
We thought the deal was done, but the estate agent informed us we'd been gazumped.
It's frustrating when someone tries to gazump you after months of negotiation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to gazunder - to lower a previously agreed price before contract exchange

Etymology

Originally British slang, possibly from Yiddish 'gezumphn' (to overcharge).
