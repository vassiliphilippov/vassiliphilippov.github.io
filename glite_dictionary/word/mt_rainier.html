Mt. Rainier

1. PROPN

/ ˌmaʊnt rəˈnɪr / (AmE), / ˌmaʊnt reɪˈnɪə / (BrE)
the highest mountain in the Cascade Range, located in central Washington, USA, with an elevation of 14,410 feet (4,392 meters)
highest peak in Washington State

Examples

We could see Mt. Rainier in the distance, its snow-capped peak shining in the morning sun.
Many climbers attempt to summit Mt. Rainier during the summer months when conditions are safer.
The national park surrounding Mt. Rainier attracts thousands of nature lovers every year.

Etymology

Named after Rear Admiral Peter Rainier, a British Royal Navy officer.
