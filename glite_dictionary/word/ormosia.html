Ormosia

1. NOUN

/ ɔrˈmoʊziə / (AmE), / ɔːˈməʊziə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical shrubs and trees, usually with odd-pinnate leaves and large leaflets, noted for their pink to reddish wood.
genus of tropical shrubs and trees

Examples

The seeds of Ormosia are often brightly colored and used for jewelry.
Many species in the Ormosia genus grow in South American rainforests.
Researchers are studying Ormosia for its potential medicinal properties.

Etymology

From Greek 'ormos' (chain), referencing the chain-like arrangement of the pods.

2. NOUN

/ ɔrˈmoʊsiə / (AmE), / ɔːˈməʊsiə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical shrubs and trees with usually odd-pinnate leaves, large leaflets, and pink to reddish wood
a genus of tropical shrubs and trees

Examples

The seeds of some Ormosia species are used to make jewelry in South America.
Researchers discovered a new Ormosia tree during their expedition in the Amazon rainforest.
The pink wood of the Ormosia is valued in carpentry for its distinctive color.

Etymology

From Latinized form of Greek ὁρμός (hormos, meaning 'chain' or 'necklace'), possibly referring to the arrangement of seeds or pods.
