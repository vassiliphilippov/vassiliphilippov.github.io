vermiculite

1. NOUN

/ vərˈmɪkjəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / vəːˈmɪkjʊˌlaɪt / (BrE)
a group of yellow or brown hydrous silicate minerals with a layered, micaceous structure, often used for insulation or as a soil conditioner
a mineral used for insulation and gardening

Examples

The garden soil was improved by adding vermiculite to retain moisture.
When exposed to high heat, vermiculite expands into accordion-shaped pieces.
Insulation made from vermiculite was common in homes built before 1990.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mica - a group of silicate minerals with a layered structure, similar to vermiculite

Etymology

From Latin 'vermiculus' (little worm), referring to the worm-like exfoliation pattern when heated.
