vertigo

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɜrtɪˌgoʊ / (AmE), / ˈvɜːtɪgəʊ / (BrE)
a sensation of spinning or dizziness, often causing a feeling that you or your surroundings are moving or about to fall
sensation of spinning or dizziness
medical

Examples

After standing up too quickly, a wave of vertigo washed over her and she had to sit down.
People with inner ear problems may experience severe vertigo and nausea.
The doctor diagnosed his recurring dizziness as chronic vertigo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dizziness - a feeling of unsteadiness or lightheadedness

Etymology

From Latin vertigo (a turning or whirling), from vertere (to turn).
