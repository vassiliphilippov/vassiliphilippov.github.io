Cyclopterus lumpus

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈklɑptərəs ˈlʌmpəs / (AmE), / sɪˈklɒptərəs ˈlʌmpəs / (BrE)
A clumsy, thick-bodied fish of the northern Atlantic with pelvic fins fused into a sucker; its edible roe is often used as a caviar substitute.
northern Atlantic fish; source of caviar substitute

Examples

The roe of Cyclopterus lumpus is often dyed and sold as a less expensive alternative to sturgeon caviar.
Marine biologists studied Cyclopterus lumpus to better understand the adaptations of its suction disc.
A large Cyclopterus lumpus was caught by fishermen off the Icelandic coast last spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lumpfish - common name for Cyclopterus lumpus, a northern Atlantic fish

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'kyklos' (circle) + 'pteron' (wing), referring to the fish’s round shape and fins.
