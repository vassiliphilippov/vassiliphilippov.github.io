reddened

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛdnd / (AmE), / ˈrɛd(ə)nd / (BrE)
(of skin or eyes) having become red, especially as a result of emotion, exertion, or irritation.
having become red, especially skin or eyes

Examples

Her reddened cheeks showed that she had been crying for some time.
After a long day in the sun, his skin was noticeably reddened.
He avoided eye contact, embarrassed by his reddened face.

Synset Examples

crimson with fury
turned red from exertion
with puffy reddened eyes
red-faced and violent
flushed (or crimson) with embarrassment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flushed - (of skin) red or pink from emotion, heat, or exertion

Etymology

Past participle of 'redden', from the adjective 'red' + -en (to make).

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɛdənd / (AmE), / ˈrɛdənd / (BrE)
Lit up or colored with red light or a reddish hue, as if illuminated by flames.
lit with red light or color

Examples

The mountaintops were reddened by the light of the setting sun.
She sat close to the reddened coals of the fire, warming her hands.
The sky was reddened with the glow of distant city lights.

Synset Examples

streets ablaze with lighted Christmas trees
the inflamed clouds at sunset
reddened faces around the campfire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inflamed - red or glowing, often as if lit by fire
  • Synonym: ablaze - brightly lit, especially with a fiery or red light
