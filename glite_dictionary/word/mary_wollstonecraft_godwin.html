Mary Wollstonecraft Godwin

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɛri ˈwʊl.stən.kræft ˈɡɒd.wɪn / (AmE), / ˈmeəri ˈwʊl.stən.krɑːft ˈɡɒd.wɪn / (BrE)
(1759–1797) English writer and early advocate for women's rights, known for her works challenging male supremacy and promoting equal education for women.
English writer and early feminist (1759–1797)

Examples

Mary Wollstonecraft Godwin was a pioneer in advocating education reform for women in the eighteenth century.
Many scholars cite Mary Wollstonecraft Godwin as a foundational figure in the feminist movement.
The legacy of Mary Wollstonecraft Godwin continues to influence literature and gender equality discussions today.
