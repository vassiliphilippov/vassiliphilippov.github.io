Coccothraustes

1. NOUN

/ kɑkɵˈθrɔːstiːz / (AmE), / kɒkəˈθrɔːstiːz / (BrE)
A genus of large finches found in Eurasia, known for their robust beaks adapted for cracking hard seeds.
genus of large finches

Examples

The Coccothraustes genus includes the well-known hawfinch.
Birdwatchers spotted a rare member of the Coccothraustes in the forest reserve.
The powerful beak of Coccothraustes species allows them to crack open tough cherry stones.

Etymology

From Greek *kokkos* (kernel, seed) and *thraustes* (breaker).

2. NOUN

/ kɑː.kəˈθrɔː.stiːz / (AmE), / kɒ.kəˈθræu.stiːz / (BrE)
A genus of large finches, including species such as the hawfinch, found in Eurasia and North Africa.
genus of large finches

Examples

The Coccothraustes genus is well known for its members' powerful beaks capable of cracking hard seeds.
Researchers observed several species from the Coccothraustes genus in the mixed woodlands of Europe.
Ornithologists classify the hawfinch within the Coccothraustes genus due to its distinctive features.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawfinch - a large finch of the genus Coccothraustes

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek kokkos (kernel) + thraustes (breaker), referring to their strong beaks.
