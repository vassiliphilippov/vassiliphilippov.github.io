faineance

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪn.i.əns / (AmE), / ˈfeɪn.i.ɑːns / (BrE)
the quality of being idle or unwilling to work; habitual laziness or indolence.
idleness; reluctance to work

Examples

His total faineance made progress on the project nearly impossible.
She was criticized for her faineance during a time that required decisive action.
The culture of faineance threatened to undermine the company's formerly strong work ethic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indolence - avoidance of activity or exertion; laziness
  • Antonym: diligence - careful and persistent work or effort (opposite meaning)

Etymology

From French *fainéant* (do-nothing, idle), ultimately from Latin *facinus* (deed, crime) + *ne* (not).
