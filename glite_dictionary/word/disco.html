disco

1. VERB

/ ˈdɪsˌkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdɪskəʊ / (BrE)
to dance to disco music, especially in a lively or rhythmic manner typical of the disco era
dance to disco music
informal

Examples

We discoed all night at the club, barely stopping for a break.
He loves to disco whenever his favorite hits from the seventies play.
They were discoing under the glittering lights until the early hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dance - move rhythmically to music, typically following a set sequence of steps
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01901399-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪsˌkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdɪskəʊ / (BrE)
A style of popular dance music, especially from the late 1970s, characterized by a regular bass beat and melodic tunes, intended mainly for dancing at discotheques.
1970s dance music genre

Examples

He loves listening to disco while getting ready to go out.
Classic disco tracks from the 1970s are still played at parties today.
The club played a mix of electronic and disco music all night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dance music - music intended especially for dancing
  • Antonym: rock - a popular music genre distinct from disco

Etymology

Shortened from 'discotheque', from French 'discothèque' (record library, nightclub for dancing).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07073666-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɪskoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdɪskəʊ / (BrE)
A public dance club where people dance to recorded popular music, especially electronic or dance music.
dance club for recorded music

Examples

Let's meet at the disco tonight and dance until midnight.
In the 1970s, the disco was the hottest place in town.
Many cities still have a popular disco where young people gather on weekends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nightclub - A venue open at night for entertainment, dancing, or drinks
  • Antonym: ballroom - A large room used for dancing, typically to live music

Etymology

Shortened from 'discotheque', from French 'discothèque' (record library or nightclub for dancing).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03211003-n
Synset POS: n
