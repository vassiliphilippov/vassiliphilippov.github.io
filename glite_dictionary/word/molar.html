molar

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊlər / (AmE), / ˈməʊlə / (BrE)
A large, flat-topped tooth at the back of the mouth, used for grinding food; located behind the premolars.
a tooth for grinding, at the back of the mouth

Examples

She had to have a molar extracted because of severe decay.
The dentist explained how a molar differs from an incisor and a canine.
Food is ground into smaller pieces by your molars before you swallow.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: premolar - a tooth situated in front of a molar and behind the canine teeth

Etymology

From Latin 'molaris' (grinding), from 'mola' (millstone).
