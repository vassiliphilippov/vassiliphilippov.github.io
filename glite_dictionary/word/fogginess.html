fogginess

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːɡinəs / (AmE), / ˈfɒɡinəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being unclear, indistinct, or lacking sharp outlines
unclear or indistinct quality

Examples

The fogginess of the photograph made it hard to recognize anyone.
A sense of mental fogginess made concentrating on the lecture difficult.
She peered through the fogginess that had settled over the city streets.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easily understood or seen
  • Synonym: blurriness - the quality of being blurred or indistinct

Etymology

From 'foggy' + '-ness'; 'foggy' from 'fog' (mist, cloud) + '-y'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɑɡi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfɒɡ.i.nəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being filled with fog, resulting in reduced visibility
state of being filled with fog

Examples

The fogginess made it difficult for drivers to see more than a few meters ahead.
There was a distinct fogginess over the lake early in the morning.
After the rain, the fogginess in the valley slowly began to lift.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fog - thick cloud of tiny water droplets suspended in the atmosphere
  • Antonym: clarity - clearness or lucidity, especially in air or atmosphere

Etymology

From 'fog' + '-iness', formed after the adjective 'foggy'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14545250-n
Synset POS: n
