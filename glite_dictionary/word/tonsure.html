tonsure

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑnʃər / (AmE), / ˈtɒnʃə / (BrE)
the practice or act of shaving part or all of the crown of the head, especially by members of a religious order or clergy as a sign of humility or devotion
religious shaving of the head

Examples

The monk received his tonsure as a symbol of his commitment to the monastery.
Before being ordained, he underwent the traditional tonsure ceremony.
In medieval Europe, the tonsure distinguished clerics from laypersons.

Etymology

From Latin 'tonsura' (a shaving, shearing), from 'tondere' (to shear, shave).

2. VERB

/ ˈtɑːnʃər / (AmE), / ˈtɒnʃə / (BrE)
to shave all or part of the hair on the scalp, especially as a religious rite for monks or priests at induction
shave head as religious rite

Examples

At the ceremony, the abbot will tonsure the new monks as a sign of their devotion.
The young novice was tonsured before taking his vows at the monastery.
It is customary to tonsure candidates during their induction into the religious order.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shave - remove hair from the body using a razor or similar tool

Etymology

From Latin 'tonsura' (a shaving, clipping), from 'tondere' (to shear, shave).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɑn.ʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɒn.ʃə / (BrE)
The act or result of shaving part or all of the scalp, especially the crown, as a sign of religious devotion, particularly among monks or priests.
shaved crown of a monk's or priest's head

Examples

The novice prepared for the ceremony by receiving his tonsure at dawn.
Medieval art often depicts monks with a distinctive tonsure symbolizing humility and piety.
After being ordained, he wore the tonsure proudly as a sign of his commitment to his faith.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: baldness - the condition of having no hair on the scalp
  • Degree Lower: hair - the collection of strands growing from the scalp

Etymology

From Latin 'tonsura' (a shaving, clipping), from 'tondere' (to shear).
