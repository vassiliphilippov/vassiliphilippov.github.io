severable

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛvərəbəl / (AmE), / ˈsɛvərəbəl / (BrE)
capable of being separated, divided, or dissociated from something else.
able to be separated or divided

Examples

The agreement contains several severable clauses that can survive termination.
Their interests in the property were considered severable by the court.
In contract law, certain obligations may be severable from the main agreement.

Synset Examples

often drugs and crime are not dissociable
the siamese twins were not considered separable
a song … never conceived of as severable from the melody

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inseverable - not able to be separated or divided
  • Synonym: separable - able to be separated or divided

Etymology

From Latin 'severare' (to sever) + English '-able'.
