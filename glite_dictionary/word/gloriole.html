gloriole

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɔrioʊl / (AmE), / ˈɡlɔːrɪəʊl / (BrE)
a ring or circle of radiant light appearing around the head of a holy person in art, especially a saint; a halo
radiant circle around a saint's head

Examples

The painting depicts Saint Peter with a golden gloriole above his head.
In Renaissance art, the gloriole is often intricately decorated to signify holiness.
The artist used light and color to create a luminous gloriole around the figure of the Madonna.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halo - a circle of light shown around or above the head of a saint or holy person

Etymology

From French 'gloriole', from Latin 'gloria' (glory).
