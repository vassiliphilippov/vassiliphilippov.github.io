Tocqueville

1. PROPN

/ tɑkˈvɪl / (AmE), / tɒkˈviːl / (BrE)
Alexis de Tocqueville (1805–1859), French political thinker known for his influential analysis of American society and institutions.
French analyst of American society

Examples

Many scholars still cite Tocqueville's observations on American democracy.
In his famous work, Tocqueville examined the balance of liberty and equality in the United States.
The ideas of Tocqueville continue to influence political theory and discussion today.

Etymology

French surname, from the village of Tocqueville in Normandy.
