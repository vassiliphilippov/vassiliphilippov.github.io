to inmarry

1. VERB

/ ˈɪnˌmæri / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌmæri / (BrE)
to marry within one's own social, ethnic, religious, or cultural group
marry within a specific group

Examples

In some communities, people are encouraged to inmarry to maintain cultural traditions.
The villagers have inmarried for generations, resulting in strong family ties.
Researchers found that individuals who inmarry tend to share similar values and beliefs.

Synset Examples

The inhabitants of this isolated village tend to inmarry

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outmarry - to marry outside one's own group

Etymology

From in- (within) + marry, modeled after 'intermarry'.
