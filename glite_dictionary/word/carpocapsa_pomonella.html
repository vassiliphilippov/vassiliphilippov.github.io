Carpocapsa pomonella

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːrpəˈkæpsə ˌpoʊməˈnɛlə / (AmE), / ˌkɑːpəˈkæpsə ˌpɒməˈnɛlə / (BrE)
A small grey moth whose larvae are common pests in apples and walnuts, also known as the codling moth.
species of moth, apple pest

Examples

Farmers often monitor for Carpocapsa pomonella to protect their apple orchards from infestation.
The larvae of Carpocapsa pomonella burrow into apples, making the fruit unmarketable.
Pheromone traps are used to reduce Carpocapsa pomonella populations in commercial walnut groves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: codling moth - common name for Carpocapsa pomonella

Etymology

From Latin: 'carpo-' (fruit) + 'capsa' (box), 'pomonella' from 'pomum' (apple); scientific name for the species.
