Equisetum hyemale

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkwɪˈsitəm haɪˈimiːli / (AmE), / ˌiːkwɪˈsiːtəm haɪˈiːmiːli / (BrE)
A species of horsetail plant with evergreen, erect, rough-edged stems, formerly used for scouring utensils.
a species of horsetail plant

Examples

The stems of Equisetum hyemale are rough and were once used for cleaning pots.
Gardeners often plant Equisetum hyemale along ponds for its striking upright appearance.
A patch of Equisetum hyemale thrives even in wet, poorly drained soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horsetail - a vascular plant of the genus Equisetum

Etymology

From Latin: 'equisetum' meaning horsetail, 'hyemale' meaning 'of winter'.
