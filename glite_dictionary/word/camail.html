camail

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæˌmeɪl / (AmE), / ˈkæmeɪl / (BrE)
A piece of medieval armor consisting of a curtain of chain mail attached to a helmet (basinet) to protect the neck and shoulders.
medieval chain mail neck protector

Examples

The knight donned his camail before heading into battle.
The museum's collection includes a rare camail from the 14th century.
A camail was often used to protect the neck from sword blows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coif - a close-fitting cap of mail worn under a helmet

Etymology

From Old French *camail* (hood of mail), from Late Latin *capitālem* (covering for the head).
