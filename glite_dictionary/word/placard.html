placard

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæk.ɑrd / (AmE), / ˈplæk.ɑːd / (BrE)
a large notice or sign displayed in a public place, especially for advertising or information
publicly displayed sign or notice

Examples

Protesters held up a placard demanding climate action during the rally.
A brightly colored placard in the window advertised the store's grand opening.
He made a homemade placard to support his team's championship game.

Synset Examples

a poster advertised the coming attractions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poster - large printed notice or picture for display in a public place
  • Synonym: billboard - large outdoor board for displaying advertisements

Etymology

From French 'placard', meaning a notice or poster.

WordNet ID: oewn-06806283-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈplæk.ɑrd / (AmE), / ˈplæk.ɑːd / (BrE)
To display a sign, notice, or poster in a public place for information or advertisement.
to display a poster or notice publicly

Examples

Protesters placarded the city hall with signs demanding change.
They placarded warnings about the water shortage throughout the neighborhood.
The shop owner placarded the window with a notice about the upcoming sale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poster - a large printed notice used for advertising or display

Etymology

From French 'placarder', from 'placard' (a notice or poster).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01594756-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈplækɑrd / (AmE), / ˈplakɑːd / (BrE)
to publicize or announce something by displaying a notice or poster in a public place
to advertise or announce with a public notice

Examples

Activists placarded the city center to raise awareness about climate change.
The company placarded the conference hall with its new product advertisements.
During the protest, they placarded the walls with demands for reform.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poster - a large printed notice or picture for public display

Etymology

From French 'placarder', from 'placard' (a poster or notice).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00993932-v
Synset POS: v
