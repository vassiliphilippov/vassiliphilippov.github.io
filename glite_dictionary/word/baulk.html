baulk

1. NOUN

/ bɔk / (AmE), / bɔːk / (BrE)
the area on a billiard table behind the balkline, from which play is started or certain shots must be taken
cue sports: area behind the balkline

Examples

The referee placed the cue ball in the baulk after the foul shot.
He lined up his opening shot from within the baulk.
Players may not remove balls from the baulk except under certain conditions.

Synset Examples

a player with ball in hand must play from the balk

Etymology

From Old French *bauc*, of unknown origin; technical cue sports usage dates from the 18th and 19th centuries.

2. NOUN

/ bɔk / (AmE), / bɔːk / (BrE)
one of several parallel sloping beams that support a roof; a principal rafter in a roof structure
sloping roof beam; principal rafter

Examples

The old barn's roof was supported by massive baulks of oak timber.
Each baulk was carefully placed to ensure the roof's stability.
The carpenters repaired the damaged baulk to prevent the roof from sagging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rafter - a sloping beam supporting a roof, similar function to baulk

Etymology

From Old Norse *balkr* (ridge, partition), related to Old English *balc* (bank, ridge).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04052724-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ bɔːk / (AmE), / bɔːk / (BrE)
to hesitate or refuse to accept or comply with something; to resist or be unwilling to proceed
hesitate or refuse to comply
british

Examples

He baulked at the idea of working overtime without extra pay.
When asked to apologize, she baulked and refused to say another word.
Many investors baulk at putting money into high-risk ventures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balk - to hesitate or refuse to comply; to stop short

Etymology

Alternative spelling of 'balk', from Old English *balca* (ridge, bank, partition).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ bɔːk / (AmE), / bɔːk / (BrE)
An obstacle or hindrance that prevents or delays progress or action.
obstacle; hindrance to progress

Examples

The new regulations put a significant baulk in the way of the project's completion.
Despite many baulks and setbacks, she persisted with her plans.
Fear of failure can often act as a baulk to innovation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstacle - something that blocks one's way or prevents progress
  • Synonym: barrier - a fence or other obstacle that prevents movement or access
  • Antonym: aid - something that helps progress or achievement

Etymology

From Old Norse *balkr* (ridge, partition, obstacle); related to Old English *balca* (ridge, bank).
