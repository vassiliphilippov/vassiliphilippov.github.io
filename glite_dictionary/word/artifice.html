artifice

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrtɪfɪs / (AmE), / ˈɑːtɪfɪs / (BrE)
a clever or deceptive trick or strategy, especially used to avoid detection or achieve an end
a clever or deceptive trick

Examples

The spy's clever artifice allowed her to evade capture by the enemy.
His story was merely an artifice to distract us from his real motives.
The thief relied on artifice and disguise to escape from the police.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trick - a cunning act or scheme intended to deceive or cheat
  • Synonym: ruse - an action intended to deceive someone; a trick
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful or sincere

Etymology

From Latin artificium (craft, skill), from ars (art) + facere (to make).
