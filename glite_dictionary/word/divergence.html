divergence

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈvɝːdʒəns / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜːdʒəns / (BrE)
the act or process of moving or spreading apart from a common point or standard
the act of moving apart from a common point

Examples

The divergence of the two roads led travelers in opposite directions.
Over time, there was increasing divergence between the two scientific theories.
A river's divergence can create smaller branches that flow away from the main channel.

Synset Examples

an angle is formed by the divergence of two straight lines

Related Terms

  • Antonym: convergence - the act of moving toward a common point

Etymology

From Latin 'divergere' (to go in different directions).

2. NOUN

/ daɪˈvɜrdʒəns / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜːdʒəns / (BrE)
a difference or deviation from a standard, norm, or expected value
a deviation from the standard or norm

Examples

The divergence between theory and practice became clear during the experiment.
Small divergences in results can indicate errors in the measurement process.
There has been a noticeable divergence from traditional teaching methods in recent years.

Synset Examples

the deviation from the mean

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deviation - a departure from a standard or norm
  • Antonym: convergence - the process of coming together or becoming similar

3. NOUN

/ daɪˈvɜr.dʒəns / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜː.dʒəns / (BrE)
(Mathematics) The property of an infinite series or sequence that does not approach a finite limit.
failure to approach a limit (math)

Examples

The series 1 + 2 + 3 + ... is an example of divergence in mathematics.
When testing a series for divergence, we look for whether the partial sums fail to settle on a finite value.
Physicists sometimes encounter divergence when handling infinite sums in quantum theory.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: convergence - the property of a series to approach a finite limit

Etymology

From Latin divergere (to go in different directions), via French divergence.

4. NOUN

/ daɪˈvɜr.dʒəns / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜː.dʒəns / (BrE)
a difference or separation between two or more things, especially opinions, interests, or facts, that were previously similar
difference or separation between things

Examples

There was a significant divergence of opinion among the committee members.
Recent years have seen an increasing divergence between the two political parties' policies.
The divergence in their accounts raised questions about what really happened.

Synset Examples

a growing divergence of opinion

Related Terms

  • Antonym: convergence - coming together or becoming similar
  • Synonym: difference - the state of being unlike or dissimilar

Etymology

From Latin 'divergere' (to go in different directions), from 'di-' (apart) + 'vergere' (to turn).
