to severalise

1. VERB

/ ˈsɛvərəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɛvərəlaɪz / (BrE)
to distinguish as separate; to set apart or separate from others
separate or distinguish
archaic

Examples

The manager decided to severalise the departments to improve efficiency.
Early tax laws would severalise communal land among individual owners.
Efforts to severalise the items in the collection revealed subtle differences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: separate - to set or keep apart
  • Antonym: to unite - to bring together or combine

Etymology

From 'several' (separate, different) + '-ise' (forming verbs).
