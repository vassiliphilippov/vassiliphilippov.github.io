truculently

1. ADV

/ ˈtrʌkjələntli / (AmE), / ˈtrʌkjʊləntli / (BrE)
in a manner that is aggressively defiant or combative
in a fiercely defiant manner

Examples

He truculently refused to apologize for his actions.
The teenager responded truculently to his parent's questioning.
She glared truculently at anyone who challenged her opinion.

Synset Examples

the boy looked up truculently at his teacher

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aggressively - in a forceful or hostile manner
  • Antonym: meekly - in a quiet, gentle, and submissive manner

Etymology

From 'truculent' + '-ly'; 'truculent' from Latin 'truculentus' (fierce, savage).

2. ADV

/ ˈtrʌkjələntli / (AmE), / ˈtrʌkjʊləntli / (BrE)
in a manner that shows fierce or aggressive defiance or hostility
in an aggressively defiant manner

Examples

She replied truculently to the criticism, unwilling to admit any fault.
The manager truculently challenged every suggestion made during the meeting.
Protesters marched truculently through the streets, demanding change.

Synset Examples

they strive for security by truculently asserting their own interests

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aggressively - in a forceful or hostile manner

Etymology

From 'truculent' + -ly, ultimately from Latin 'truculentus' (fierce, savage).
