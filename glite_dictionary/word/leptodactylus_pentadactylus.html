Leptodactylus pentadactylus

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛp.toʊˈdæk.tɪl.əs ˌpɛn.təˈdæk.tɪl.əs / (AmE), / ˌlɛp.təʊˈdæk.tɪl.əs ˌpɛn.təˈdæk.tɪl.əs / (BrE)
a large, toothed frog native to South and Central America, resembling the bullfrog
large South American frog species

Examples

The Leptodactylus pentadactylus can grow larger than most other frogs in its habitat.
Scientists observed the Leptodactylus pentadactylus displaying unique vocal behaviors during the rainy season.
Herpetologists often compare the Leptodactylus pentadactylus to the North American bullfrog due to its size and appearance.

Etymology

From Greek 'leptos' (slender) + 'dactylos' (finger); scientific naming convention in taxonomy.
