adnate

1. ADJ

/ ˈæd.neɪt / (AmE), / ˈad.neɪt / (BrE)
(of different parts or organs, especially in plants) closely attached to each other along their length.
botany: closely attached organs
medical

Examples

The sepals were adnate to the petals, forming a tubular structure.
In some flowers, the stamens may become adnate to the style.
Botanists observed that the leaf blade was adnate to the stem along much of its length.

Synset Examples

a calyx adnate to the ovary

Etymology

From Latin 'adnatus', past participle of 'adnasci' (to grow on).
