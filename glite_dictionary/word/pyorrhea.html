pyorrhea

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪəˈriə / (AmE), / ˌpaɪəˈriːə / (BrE)
A discharge of pus, especially as a symptom of infection, commonly used to refer to a gum disease causing pus to be discharged from the gums.
discharge of pus (esp. gums)
medical

Examples

The dentist diagnosed the patient with pyorrhea after noticing pus oozing from his gums.
Untreated pyorrhea can lead to tooth loss and serious complications.
Symptoms such as bleeding and pyorrhea often indicate advanced gum disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppuration - the process of pus formation

Etymology

From Greek 'pyo-' (pus) and 'rhoia' (flow, discharge).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪəˈriə / (AmE), / ˌpʌɪəˈrɪə / (BrE)
A chronic inflammatory disease of the gums and tooth sockets, producing pus and often leading to the loosening of teeth.
chronic gum infection with pus
medical

Examples

Untreated pyorrhea can eventually result in the loss of multiple teeth.
The dentist diagnosed her gum problems as pyorrhea and started a treatment plan.
Chronic pyorrhea is more common in people with poor oral hygiene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: periodontitis - serious gum infection damaging soft tissue and bone

Etymology

From Greek 'pyo-' (pus) + 'rhein' (to flow).
