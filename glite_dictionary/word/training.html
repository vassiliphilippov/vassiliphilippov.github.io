training

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtreɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of learning the skills needed to do a particular job or activity
process of learning skills

Examples

All new employees must complete intensive training before starting their jobs.
She is attending a training course to improve her computer skills.
Physical training is required for all firefighters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instruction - the act of teaching or providing knowledge
  • Synonym: education - the process of receiving or giving systematic instruction

Etymology

From the verb 'train', which comes from Old French 'trainer' (to drag, draw), from Vulgar Latin *traginare.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪ.nɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtreɪ.nɪŋ / (BrE)
the development of knowledge and manners through good upbringing, especially relating to social behavior and refinement.
development of manners and social refinement

Examples

She was known for her impeccable training in social situations.
A person’s training often shows in their polite behavior and speech.
Despite his wealth, he lacked the training expected in such company.

Synset Examples

a woman of breeding and refinement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breeding - good upbringing or manners
  • Synonym: refinement - cultivation of manners and taste

Etymology

From Old French *trainer* (to drag, draw), ultimately from Latin *trahere* (to pull).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04929077-n
Synset POS: n
