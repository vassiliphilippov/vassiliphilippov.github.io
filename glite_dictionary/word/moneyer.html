moneyer

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌniər / (AmE), / ˈmʌnɪə / (BrE)
a person, especially in former times, who was authorized to mint or coin money
person who mints or coins money

Examples

In medieval England, the moneyer played a vital role in producing coins for trade.
The moneyer stamped the king's emblem onto each silver piece.
Archaeologists found tools once used by a Roman moneyer near the ancient mint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minter - a person or machine that mints coins

Etymology

From Old English 'myntiere', from Latin 'monetarius' (minter, moneyer), from 'moneta' (money).
