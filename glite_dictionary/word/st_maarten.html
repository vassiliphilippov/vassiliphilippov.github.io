St. Maarten

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈmɑːrtən / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈmɑːtən / (BrE)
An island in the northeast Caribbean, part of the Leeward Islands, administered jointly by France and the Netherlands.
island in the Caribbean jointly administered by France and the Netherlands

Examples

We spent our vacation in St. Maarten, enjoying the beaches and vibrant culture.
The island of St. Maarten is famous for its duty-free shopping and nightlife.
Flights to St. Maarten are popular among tourists seeking warm weather year-round.

Etymology

From Dutch and French, named after Saint Martin of Tours.
