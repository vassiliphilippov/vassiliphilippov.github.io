glister

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɪstər / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪstə / (BrE)
A bright, sparkling shine or gleam.
bright, sparkling shine
archaic

Examples

There was a magical glister to the freshly fallen snow under the moonlight.
The glister of jewels caught everyone's attention as she entered the room.
Even through the dust, the ancient vase retained a faint glister.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glitter - a bright, sparkling light or shine
  • Synonym: to glisten - to shine with a sparkling reflected light (as a noun or verb)
  • Antonym: dullness - lack of brightness or luster

Etymology

From Middle English *glistren* (to glitter, shine), related to 'glitter.'
