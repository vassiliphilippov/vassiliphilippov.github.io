facula

1. NOUN

/ ˈfækjələ / (AmE), / ˈfækjʊlə / (BrE)
A bright spot or area on the surface of a planet, especially seen in astronomical observations.
bright spot on a planet

Examples

Through the telescope, we could see a facula shining brightly near the planet's equator.
Scientists believe that the newly discovered faculae are linked to volcanic activity on Venus.
Each facula observed on the icy moon reveals valuable information about its surface composition.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sunspot - a dark spot on the surface of the sun

Etymology

From Latin *facula* (little torch), diminutive of *fax* (torch).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfækjələ / (AmE), / ˈfækjʊlə / (BrE)
A bright region on the Sun's photosphere, often appearing near sunspots.
bright spot on the Sun's surface

Examples

Solar astronomers observed a new facula forming beside the large sunspot group.
A bright facula can affect the Sun's total irradiance reaching Earth.
The faculae are often more visible near the edge of the Sun's disk.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sunspot - a dark area on the surface of the Sun

Etymology

From Latin 'facula' meaning 'little torch', diminutive of 'fax' (torch).
