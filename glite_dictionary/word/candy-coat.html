to candy-coat

1. VERB

/ ˈkændi ˌkoʊt / (AmE), / ˈkændi ˌkəʊt / (BrE)
to make something unpleasant seem more appealing or less harsh, especially by softening criticism or truth
make something seem less harsh

Examples

He tends to candy-coat his criticisms to avoid conflict.
Stop trying to candy-coat your mistakes and take responsibility.
There's no need to candy-coat the truth; just tell me what happened.

Synset Examples

He tends to candy-coat his criticisms to avoid conflict.
Stop trying to candy-coat your mistakes.
Don’t candy-coat the truth; just be honest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sugarcoat - to make something seem more pleasant or acceptable than it really is
  • Antonym: blunt - to make something less sharp or direct, often used for criticism

Etymology

Formed by analogy with the literal process of coating with candy, meaning to cover with something sweet.
