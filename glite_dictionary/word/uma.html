Uma

1. NOUN

/ ˈuːmə / (AmE), / ˈjuːmə / (BrE)
A genus of North American lizards known as fringe-toed lizards, typically found in sandy desert regions.
genus of fringe-toed lizards

Examples

Desert researchers often encounter the shy Uma while studying sandy habitats in the American Southwest.
The Uma uses its fringed toes to move quickly across loose sand dunes.
Biologists are concerned about the declining habitat of the threatened Uma population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fringe-toed lizard - a lizard adapted to sandy deserts, with fringes on its toes

Etymology

From Latin 'Uma', the scientific name assigned to the genus.

2. PROPN

/ ˈuːmə / (AmE), / ˈuːmə / (BrE)
A name for the Hindu goddess Parvati, regarded as a gentle and benevolent aspect of Devi, symbolizing beauty and splendor.
Hindu goddess, aspect of Devi

Examples

Many devotees worship Uma as the embodiment of compassion and motherly love.
The temple festival celebrates Uma’s marriage to Lord Shiva.
In Hindu mythology, Uma is often depicted with a serene expression and wearing beautiful ornaments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Parvati - another name and aspect of the Hindu goddess Uma
  • Synonym: Devi - the supreme goddess in Hinduism, of whom Uma is an aspect

Etymology

From Sanskrit उमा (umā), a name of Parvati in Hinduism.

3. NOUN

/ ˈuːmə / (AmE), / ˈuːmə / (BrE)
A genus of small desert-dwelling lizards known as fringe-toed lizards, native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.
a genus of fringe-toed lizards

Examples

Several species within the Uma genus are specially adapted to live in sandy desert environments.
The Uma lizard can be distinguished by its fringed toes, which help it move quickly on loose sand.
Biologists conducted field studies to observe the behavior of Uma in its natural habitat.

Etymology

From Latin Uma, used as a genus name in taxonomy.
