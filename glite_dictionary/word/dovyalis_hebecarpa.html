Dovyalis hebecarpa

1. NOUN

/ doʊˈvaɪ.əlɪs hɛˈbɛkɑrpə / (AmE), / dəʊˈvaɪ.əlɪs hɛˈbɛkɑːpə / (BrE)
a small shrubby spiny tree native to Sri Lanka and India, cultivated for its maroon-purple, gooseberry-flavored fruit
tropical fruit tree from Sri Lanka and India

Examples

Farmers in India often grow Dovyalis hebecarpa for its unique tart fruit.
The maroon fruit of Dovyalis hebecarpa is popular in homemade jams.
Botanists identified the shrub as Dovyalis hebecarpa due to its spiny branches and purple pulp berries.

Etymology

From Latinized botanical naming; genus Dovyalis, species hebecarpa, from Greek 'hebe' (youth) and 'karpos' (fruit).
