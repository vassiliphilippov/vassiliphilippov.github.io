self-control

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf.kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf.kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
The ability to control your emotions, desires, or actions by your own willpower.
ability to control oneself

Examples

Maintaining self-control in stressful situations is a valuable skill.
She struggled to keep her self-control when she heard the shocking news.
Children often test their self-control when tempted by sweets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discipline - the ability to control one's own behavior or actions
  • Antonym: impulsiveness - the tendency to act on sudden urges or desires without thought

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'control', meaning control over one’s own actions or feelings.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf.kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / ˌself.kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
The ability to control your emotions and actions, especially in difficult situations.
ability to control one's actions or feelings

Examples

Despite being angry, he showed remarkable self-control during the meeting.
Regular meditation can help improve your self-control over time.
Children often struggle with self-control when faced with temptation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willpower - the ability to control oneself and determine one's actions
  • Synonym: discipline - the practice of training oneself to obey rules or a code of behavior
  • Antonym: impulsiveness - the tendency to act without forethought

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'control'; 'control' from Old French 'contrerole' (a register, check).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
