Cortinarius subfoetidus

1. NOUN

/ kɔrˈtɪniː.ər ʌb.foʊˈiː.tɪ.dəs / (AmE), / kɔːˈtɪn.i.ə sʌb.fəʊˈɛt.ɪ.dəs / (BrE)
A species of fungus with a sticky lavender cap and stalk, whitish above and covered with a silky lavender sheath.
a species of fungus with a lavender cap and stalk

Examples

The mushroom Cortinarius subfoetidus can be identified by its sticky lavender cap.
Mycology enthusiasts often search for Cortinarius subfoetidus in damp forests during the fall.
You should avoid eating wild fungi like Cortinarius subfoetidus unless you are certain of their identity.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Cortinarius' is a genus of fungi, 'subfoetidus' means 'somewhat foul-smelling'.
