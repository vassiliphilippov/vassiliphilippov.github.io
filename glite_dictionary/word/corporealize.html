to corporealize

1. VERB

/ kɔrˈpɔriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / kɔːˈpɔːrɪəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something abstract, such as a feeling or idea, tangible or physical; to give physical form to something nonphysical
make abstract things physical

Examples

The artist hoped to corporealize her emotions through sculpture.
Philosophers debate whether it is possible to corporealize abstract concepts like love or justice.
The dance performance aimed to corporealize the theme of transformation on stage.

Synset Examples

The artist’s work focuses on the corporealization of emotions.
He wanted to corporealize his thoughts through painting.
The project aims to corporealize the concept of freedom.
Artists often seek to corporealize their visions in tangible forms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embody - to give a tangible or visible form to an idea or feeling
  • Synonym: to materialize - to cause something to appear in physical form

Etymology

From "corporeal" (relating to the body) + "-ize", modelled after Latin "corporeus" (bodily) and the verb-forming suffix.
