largesse

1. NOUN

/ lɑrˈʒɛs / (AmE), / lɑːˈʒɛs / (BrE)
generous gifts of money or the act of giving them, especially in a way that shows off the giver's generosity
generous gift or act of giving

Examples

The charity depended on the largesse of wealthy benefactors to fund its programs.
Local artists thrived thanks to the largesse of a generous patron.
Politicians often distribute gifts as largesse during election campaigns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generosity - the quality of being kind and willing to give money, help, or time freely
  • Antonym: stinginess - unwillingness to give or spend; meanness

Etymology

From Old French 'largesse' (generosity), from Latin 'largus' (abundant).

2. NOUN

/ lɑrˈʒɛs / (AmE), / lɑːˈʒɛs / (BrE)
generous giving of gifts or money, or the quality of being very generous
generous giving; generosity

Examples

The billionaire was known for his largesse to various charities.
Thanks to their employer’s largesse, all employees received generous holiday bonuses.
Many scholarships are funded through the largesse of private donors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generosity - the quality of being generous
  • Antonym: stinginess - unwillingness to give or spend; meanness

Etymology

From Old French 'largece', from 'large' (generous), from Latin 'largus' (abundant).
