Erysiphales

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌrɪsɪˈfeɪliːz / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪsɪˈfeɪliːz / (BrE)
An order of fungi that are primarily saprophytic or parasitic on plants, including many species that cause powdery mildew.
order of fungi parasitic on plants

Examples

Several species within the Erysiphales are responsible for the familiar white powdery mildew seen on garden plants.
Scientists study the Erysiphales to better understand how plant diseases spread in crops.
A microscope is needed to accurately identify members of the Erysiphales on infected leaves.

Etymology

From scientific Latin 'Erysiphales', based on the type genus 'Erysiphe'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛrɪˈsɪfəˌliːz / (AmE), / ˌɛrɪˈsɪfəˌliːz / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of saprophytic and parasitic fungi that typically live on plants, many species of which cause powdery mildew diseases
order of fungi causing powdery mildews
medical

Examples

Many species in the Erysiphales order are responsible for the white powdery coating on leaves known as powdery mildew.
The Erysiphales include fungi that are both saprophytic and parasitic on various plants.
Gardeners often struggle to control Erysiphales infections in their vegetable patches.

Etymology

From New Latin Erysiphales, based on the genus Erysiphe (from Greek 'erysiphē' meaning 'mildew').
