Seychellois

1. NOUN

/ seɪˌʃɛlˈwɑ / (AmE), / seɪˌʃɛlˈwɑ / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Seychelles
person from Seychelles

Examples

The Seychellois are known for their vibrant Creole culture.
A proud Seychellois, he shared stories from his island home.
Many Seychellois speak both French and English as official languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Seychelloise - female native or inhabitant of Seychelles (French feminine form)

Etymology

From French, Seychellois (masculine), Seychelloise (feminine), referring to Seychelles.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09750293-n

2. ADJ

/ seɪˌʃɛlˈwɑ / (AmE), / seɪˌʃɛlˈwɑː / (BrE)
relating to the Seychelles or its people
of or from the Seychelles

Examples

Many Seychellois musicians blend African, European, and Asian influences in their music.
A growing number of Seychellois students are studying abroad each year.
The restaurant serves traditional Seychellois cuisine featuring fresh seafood and tropical fruits.

Synset Examples

Seychellois fishermen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Seychelles - country in the Indian Ocean

Etymology

From French, literally 'from Seychelles'.
