rose

1. NOUN

/ roʊz / (AmE), / rəʊz / (BrE)
A flowering shrub of the genus Rosa, typically having fragrant, showy flowers with thorny stems, widely cultivated for ornament or perfume.
a flowering shrub with fragrant blooms

Examples

She planted a rose bush in her garden to add a touch of color.
The scent of the rose filled the entire room.
A single red rose can be a symbol of love.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flower - the reproductive structure of plants, often colorful or fragrant

Etymology

From Old French rose, from Latin rosa.

2. NOUN

/ roʊz / (AmE), / rəʊz / (BrE)
A dusty pink or light pink color, resembling that of a rose flower.
a dusty pink color

Examples

She chose a rose dress for the party, which complemented her complexion beautifully.
The artist mixed white and red paint to create the perfect rose for the background.
The living room walls were painted in a soft rose to give a warm, inviting feel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pink - a pale red color

Etymology

From the color of the rose flower, ultimately from Latin rosa (rose).
