Hyacinthus candicans

1. NOUN

/ haɪəˈsɪnθəs kænˈdaɪkənz / (AmE), / haɪəˈsɪnθəs kanˈdɪkənz / (BrE)
A southern African plant species with white, bell-shaped flowers often cultivated as an ornamental.
southern African plant with white bell flowers

Examples

The Hyacinthus candicans thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
Gardeners prize Hyacinthus candicans for its spectacular spikes of white flowers in summer.
We planted Hyacinthus candicans along the pathway to add elegance to the landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hyacinth - a genus of plants with fragrant, bell-shaped flowers

Etymology

From Latin genus name Hyacinthus, with species name candicans meaning 'becoming white' in Latin.
