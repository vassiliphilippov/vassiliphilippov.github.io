binge

1. VERB

/ bɪndʒ / (AmE), / bɪndʒ / (BrE)
to watch or consume a large amount of media, such as television programs or online videos, in one continuous session.
watch large amounts of media at once
informal

Examples

We decided to binge the entire season of the show in one night.
She loves to binge on documentaries over the weekend.
They spent Saturday binging all the Star Wars movies back to back.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to binge-watch - to watch multiple episodes of a TV show in succession
  • Antonym: graze - to consume content slowly and sporadically

Etymology

Originally from dialectal English 'binge' (to soak), of uncertain origin; senses extended in modern usage to media consumption.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-91000671-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ bɪndʒ / (AmE), / bɪndʒ / (BrE)
to eat or drink excessively, usually within a short period; to indulge in excessive consumption
eat, drink, or indulge excessively

Examples

After a stressful week at work, he would sometimes binge on junk food.
During the holidays, people often binge more than they realize.
She promised herself not to binge on sweets this weekend.

Synset Examples

She stuffed herself at the dinner
The kids binged on ice cream

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overeat - to eat more food than necessary or healthy
  • Antonym: fast - to abstain from all or some kinds of food or drink

Etymology

Originally American slang, likely from English dialect 'binge' meaning to soak (late 19th century).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01195632-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ bɪndʒ / (AmE), / bɪndʒ / (BrE)
A short period of excessive eating, drinking, or other forms of consumption, especially as a lapse from restraint.
period of excessive eating or drinking

Examples

After weeks of dieting, she went on a binge and ate three slices of cake.
He admitted to a drinking binge that lasted the entire weekend.
The documentary covers the dangers of a junk food binge among teenagers.

Synset Examples

they went on a bust that lasted three days

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spree - a lively or wild outburst of activity (especially drinking)

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; possibly 19th-century English dialect meaning 'to soak.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00511734-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ bɪndʒ / (AmE), / bɪndʒ / (BrE)
A short period devoted to indulging in an activity to excess, especially eating, drinking, or spending.
brief period of excessive indulgence

Examples

After a stressful week at work, she went on a binge of chocolate and ice cream.
His last shopping binge maxed out his credit cards.
The documentary explored the reasons behind weekend drinking binges among college students.

Synset Examples

an orgy of shopping
an emotional binge
a splurge of spending

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spree - a lively or wild outburst of activity, especially in spending or partying
  • Synonym: orgy - a period of unrestrained indulgence in an activity
  • Antonym: restraint - moderation or self-control in one's actions or feelings

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from dialectal English 'binge' (to soak, to drink heavily), early 19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01075748-n
Synset POS: n
