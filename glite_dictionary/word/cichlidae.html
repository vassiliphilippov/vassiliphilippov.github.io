Cichlidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪklɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈsɪklɪdiː / (BrE)
A large family of freshwater fish (the cichlids), including many colorful species popular in aquariums and important in ecology and evolutionary biology.
a family of freshwater fish (cichlids)

Examples

Many aquarium enthusiasts choose species from the Cichlidae family for their vibrant colors and interesting behavior.
The lakes of East Africa are famous for their incredible diversity of Cichlidae.
Researchers study Cichlidae to understand rapid evolutionary processes and ecological adaption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cichlid - a member of the Cichlidae family of fish

Etymology

From modern Latin Cichlidae, based on Greek 'kikhlē' (a kind of fish).
