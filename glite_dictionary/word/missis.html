missis

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪsɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɪsɪz / (BrE)
(informal) a term used to refer to one's wife or a woman in authority in the home.
informal term for someone's wife
informal

Examples

He said he'd have to check with the missis before making any plans.
The missis wasn't happy when I got home late last night.
Every Sunday, the missis insists on a family dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: missus - another informal term for someone's wife

Etymology

Variant of 'missus', from 'Mrs.', a contraction of 'mistress.'
