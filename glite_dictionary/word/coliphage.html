coliphage

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊ.lɪˌfeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.lɪˌfeɪdʒ / (BrE)
a bacteriophage that specifically infects the bacterium Escherichia coli.
a virus infecting E. coli bacteria
medical

Examples

Scientists used a coliphage to study how viruses infect bacterial cells.
The water sample was tested for the presence of coliphage as an indicator of contamination.
Research on coliphage genetics has helped advance molecular biology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacteriophage - a virus that infects and replicates within bacteria

Etymology

From 'coli', referring to Escherichia coli, and '-phage', from Greek 'phagein' (to eat).
