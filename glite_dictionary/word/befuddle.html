to befuddle

1. VERB

/ bɪˈfʌd.əl / (AmE), / bɪˈfʌd.əl / (BrE)
to confuse someone greatly so that they cannot think clearly or make decisions easily
to confuse or perplex

Examples

The unexpected question seemed to befuddle the entire panel for a moment.
Lack of sleep can easily befuddle your mind and impair your judgment.
She was befuddled by the complex instructions and asked for help.

Synset Examples

These questions confuse even the experts
This question completely threw me
This question befuddled even the teacher

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confuse - to make someone unable to think clearly or understand something
  • Antonym: to enlighten - to give someone greater knowledge or understanding about a subject or situation

Etymology

Probably from be- (intensive prefix) + fuddle (to confuse or get drunk), late 19th century.

2. VERB

/ bɪˈfʌd.əl / (AmE), / bɪˈfʌd.əl / (BrE)
to make someone confused or stupefied, especially as a result of alcohol or intoxication
confuse or stupefy, often with alcohol

Examples

A few drinks were enough to befuddle his senses.
Alcohol can easily befuddle your judgment if you are not careful.
She was so befuddled by the wine that she couldn't find her keys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confuse - to make someone unable to think clearly
  • Antonym: to clarify - to make something clear or easier to understand

Etymology

From prefix 'be-' (intensive) + 'fuddle' (to confuse with drink; possibly from dialect 'fuddle', to get drunk).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01193274-v
Synset POS: v
