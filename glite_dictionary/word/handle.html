handle

1. VERB

/ ˈhændl̩ / (AmE), / ˈhændl / (BrE)
to touch, lift, or hold something with the hands
touch or hold with the hands

Examples

Please do not handle the artifacts on display.
He carefully handled the fragile glass vase.
The store clerk asked me not to handle the merchandise.

Synset Examples

Don't handle the merchandise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: touch - to make physical contact with something
  • Synonym: grasp - to seize and hold securely
  • Antonym: release - to let something go after holding it

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈhændl̩ / (AmE), / ˈhændl / (BrE)
to be responsible for or deal with something; to take charge of and control or manage a situation, task, or object
deal with or manage (something)

Examples

She was promoted because she could handle difficult clients with professionalism.
The new software can't handle large amounts of data efficiently.
Let me handle the arrangements for the meeting tomorrow.

Synset Examples

I can deal with this crew of workers
This blender can't handle nuts
She managed her parents' affairs after they got too old

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to manage - to be in charge of or direct
  • Synonym: deal with - to take action in order to solve a problem or task
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to care for or attend to something

Etymology

From Old English 'handlen' (to touch, manage), related to 'hand'.

Source: WordNet

5. NOUN

/ ˈhændl̩ / (AmE), / ˈhændl / (BrE)
the part of an object designed to be held by the hand so that the object can be used or moved easily

Examples
