prostration

1. NOUN

/ prɑːˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / prɒˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
a sudden collapse or complete physical or mental exhaustion, often resulting in inability to function
complete physical or mental exhaustion

Examples

After hours of intense labor in the heat, he collapsed from sheer prostration.
The news of his failure left him in a state of emotional prostration.
The runner’s prostration at the finish line showed how grueling the marathon was.

Synset Examples

the commander's prostration demoralized his men

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhaustion - state of extreme physical or mental fatigue

Etymology

From Latin prostratio (a throwing down), from prosternere (to throw down).

2. NOUN

/ prɑːˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / prɒˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of lying stretched out on the ground, especially as a sign of submission or worship
act of lying face down, often in reverence

Examples

During the ceremony, the monks performed a full prostration before the altar.
Acts of prostration are common in many religious traditions as a mark of respect.
The student's prostration before the teacher showed great humility.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: kneeling - the act of supporting oneself on one's knees, often as a sign of respect or submission

Etymology

From Latin prostratio (a stretching out), from prosternere (to throw down).

3. NOUN

/ prɑːˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / prɒˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
the state of complete submission or surrender to someone or something, often accompanied by feelings of helplessness or abasement
complete submission or surrender

Examples

After months of stress, she finally collapsed into emotional prostration before her superiors.
The conqueror demanded the prostration of his enemies as proof of their loyalty.
His prostration in the face of authority made him lose the respect of his colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submission - the act of accepting the authority or control of someone else
  • Antonym: defiance - open resistance or bold disobedience

Etymology

From Latin 'prostratus', the past participle of 'prosternere' (to throw down).
