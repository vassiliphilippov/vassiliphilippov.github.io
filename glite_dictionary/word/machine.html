machine

1. NOUN

/ məˈʃin / (AmE), / məˈʃiːn / (BrE)
an organized group or system that works efficiently to achieve its objectives, often with coordinated and powerful means
efficient, organized group or system

Examples

The political machine controlled every aspect of city government.
The company's marketing machine rolled out the new product across the country.
The military machine mobilized quickly in response to the crisis.

Synset Examples

the war machine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organization - a group structured to achieve specific goals
  • Synonym: system - a coordinated set of things working together

Etymology

From Latin machina (device, engine), via French machine.

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ məˈʃin / (AmE), / məˈʃiːn / (BrE)
A piece of equipment with moving parts that uses power to perform a particular task or assist with human work.
mechanical or electrical device

Examples

The washing machine in the laundry room broke down last night.
Automated machines have replaced many manual tasks in factories.
She designed a simple machine to help lift heavy objects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: device - A thing made or adapted for a particular purpose, especially a piece of mechanical or electronic equipment.
  • Synonym: appliance - A device or piece of equipment designed to perform a specific task, typically a domestic one.
  • Synonym: tool - A device or implement, especially one held in the hand, used to carry out a particular function.

Etymology

From Latin 'machina', from Greek 'mēkhanē' (device, contrivance).
