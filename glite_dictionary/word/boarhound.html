boarhound

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔrˌhaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈbɔːˌhaʊnd / (BrE)
a large breed of hound used for hunting wild boars
large hound for hunting wild boars

Examples

The hunter relied on his trusty boarhound during the boar hunt in the dense forest.
Many centuries ago, nobles frequently kept a boarhound for the dangerous sport of wild boar hunting.
The strength and courage of the boarhound made it invaluable in pursuing large game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hound - a dog bred or trained for hunting
  • Synonym: boar - a wild male pig, especially as hunted

Etymology

From 'boar' (wild pig) + 'hound' (dog for hunting).
