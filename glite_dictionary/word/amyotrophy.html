amyotrophy

1. NOUN

/ ˌæmiˈɑːtrəfi / (AmE), / ˌæmɪˈɒtrəfi / (BrE)
progressive wasting or degeneration of muscle tissue, usually due to disease or nerve damage
progressive muscle tissue wasting
medical

Examples

Patients with spinal cord injuries often develop amyotrophy in their legs over time.
The doctor diagnosed the gradual thinning of his arms as a sign of amyotrophy.
Research in neurology has led to new treatments for muscular amyotrophy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muscle atrophy - degeneration or wasting of muscle tissue

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) + 'myo-' (muscle) + 'trophē' (nourishment).
