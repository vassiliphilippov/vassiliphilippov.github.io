Platanus wrightii

1. NOUN

/ pləˈtænəs ˈraɪti.aɪ / (AmE), / pləˈtænəs ˈraɪti.aɪ / (BrE)
A medium-sized tree native to Arizona and nearby areas, distinguished by its deeply lobed leaves and fruit clusters of 3 to 5.
species of tree native to Arizona region

Examples

The Platanus wrightii provides welcome shade along many Arizona streams.
Botanists documented a healthy population of Platanus wrightii near the canyon.
The deeply lobed leaves help identify Platanus wrightii during field studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arizona sycamore - another common name for Platanus wrightii

Etymology

Named after American botanist Charles Wright (1811–1885).
