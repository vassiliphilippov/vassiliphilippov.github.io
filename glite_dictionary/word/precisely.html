precisely

1. ADV

/ prɪˈsaɪsli / (AmE), / prɪˈsaɪsli / (BrE)
exactly; used to emphasize that something is correct or accurate, or as an affirmative response meaning 'exactly so.'
exactly; in a correct or accurate way

Examples

He answered precisely as instructed, without adding any extra details.
That is precisely what I meant when I made the comment.
"Precisely," she agreed, nodding at the clear explanation.

Synset Examples

‘Precisely, my lord,’ he said

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exactly - in a precise or correct way
  • Antonym: roughly - approximately, not exactly

Etymology

From precise + -ly; 'precise' from Latin 'precīsus' (cut off, strict).

2. ADV

/ prɪˈsaɪsli / (AmE), / prɪˈsaɪsli / (BrE)
in an exact and accurate way; used to emphasize accuracy or strictness
exactly; with accuracy

Examples

He followed her instructions precisely, leaving nothing to chance.
The medicine should be taken precisely at noon each day.
That is precisely what I meant when I mentioned the project.

Synset Examples

he was doing precisely (or exactly) what she had told him to do
it was just as he said — the jewel was gone
it has just enough salt
Properly speaking, all true work is religion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exactly - in a way that is completely correct or accurate
  • Antonym: approximately - not completely accurate or exact; close to the actual

Etymology

From precise + -ly, precise from Latin praecisus (cut off, strict).

3. ADV

/ prɪˈsaɪsli / (AmE), / prɪˈsaɪsli / (BrE)
in an exact and accurate way; with precision
exactly, with precision

Examples

The instructions must be followed precisely for optimal results.
She answered each question precisely and confidently during the interview.
The clock struck noon precisely as we entered the hall.

Synset Examples

she always expressed herself precisely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exactly - in a way that is completely correct or accurate
  • Antonym: imprecisely - in an inexact or inaccurate manner

Etymology

From precise + -ly, with precise from Latin praecisus (cut off, strict, exact).
