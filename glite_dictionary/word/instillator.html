instillator

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈstɪlətər / (AmE), / ɪnˈstɪlətə / (BrE)
a medical device used to introduce a liquid into a cavity of the body drop by drop
medical device for drop-by-drop liquid delivery
medical

Examples

The nurse used an instillator to administer the eye drops precisely.
Modern instillators are designed to minimize contamination during medication delivery.
An instillator allows liquid medication to be applied gradually for maximum absorption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dropper - an instrument for measuring or transferring small quantities of liquid

Etymology

From Latin 'instillare' (to drop in, to instill), with the agent suffix '-tor'.
