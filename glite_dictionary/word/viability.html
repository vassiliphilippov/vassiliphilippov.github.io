viability

1. NOUN

/ ˌvaɪ.əˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌvaɪ.əˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the ability to work successfully or to be done in a practical and useful way; feasibility
ability to work successfully; feasibility

Examples

The viability of the project depends on securing adequate funding.
They conducted a study to determine the viability of the proposed method.
Due to technical limitations, the viability of the plan was questioned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feasibility - the state or degree of being easily or conveniently done
  • Antonym: impracticality - the quality of not being workable or possible

Etymology

From Latin 'vita' (life) via French 'viabilité'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌvaɪ.əˈbɪl.ə.t̬i / (AmE), / ˌvaɪ.əˈbɪl.ə.ti / (BrE)
the ability of a living thing, such as an embryo or organism, to develop or grow normally and survive
ability to grow and develop (biology)
medical

Examples

The viability of the fetus was monitored carefully throughout the pregnancy.
Scientists tested the viability of the seeds stored in the laboratory.
Premature birth can sometimes threaten the viability of newborn animals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonviable - not capable of normal growth or development

Etymology

From Latin 'vita' (life) + '-bility' (ability, capacity).
