hypnoid

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɪp.nɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɪp.nɔɪd / (BrE)
Relating to or resembling a state of sleep or hypnosis.
pertaining to sleep or hypnosis
medical

Examples

The patient exhibited a hypnoid state during the therapy session.
After several minutes of deep relaxation, she entered a hypnoid trance.
Researchers observed hypnoid behaviors in subjects exposed to monotonic sounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypnotic - Inducing sleep or relating to hypnosis
  • Antonym: wakeful - Characterized by being awake or alert

Etymology

From Greek 'hypnos' (sleep) + '-oid' (like, resembling).
