Solidago sempervirens

1. NOUN

/ səˌlaɪdəɡoʊ ˌsɛmpərˈvaɪrɛnz / (AmE), / sɒlɪˌdeɪɡəʊ ˌsɛmpəˈvaɪrɛnz / (BrE)
a vigorous, showy species of goldenrod native to the eastern and Gulf coasts of North America, known for its bright yellow flowers
coastal goldenrod species

Examples

The dunes were covered with Solidago sempervirens, its golden blossoms bright against the sandy soil.
Gardeners often plant Solidago sempervirens to attract pollinators near the coastline.
Coastal restoration projects use Solidago sempervirens for its resilience to salt spray and wind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goldenrod - any species of the genus Solidago, known for yellow flower clusters

Etymology

From Latin: 'Solidago' (the plant's genus name, from solidare 'to make whole'), 'sempervirens' meaning 'evergreen' or 'always green'.
