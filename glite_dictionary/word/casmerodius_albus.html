Casmerodius albus

1. NOUN

/ kæzməˈroʊdiəs ˈælbəs / (AmE), / kazməˈrəʊdiəs ˈalbəs / (BrE)
A widely distributed large white egret found in the Old World, also known as the great egret.
large white Old World egret

Examples

The Casmerodius albus can often be seen wading in shallow wetlands across Europe and Asia.
Researchers observed a flock of Casmerodius albus feeding along the riverbank during the migration season.
Unlike some other egrets, the Casmerodius albus stands out for its striking all-white plumage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: great egret - a large, white heron with a yellow bill and black legs

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'kasmeros' (unknown origin) and Latin 'albus' (white).
