lifeless

1. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪfləs / (AmE), / ˈlaɪfləs / (BrE)
lacking animation, excitement, or activity; dull and unenergetic.
without excitement or activity

Examples

The room felt lifeless after everyone had left for the evening.
His speech was so lifeless that the audience quickly lost interest.
Without music, the art gallery seemed lifeless and uninviting.

Synset Examples

it was a lifeless party until she arrived

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement
  • Antonym: lively - full of life, energy, or activity

Etymology

From life + -less, meaning 'without life.'

2. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪfləs / (AmE), / ˈlaɪfləs / (BrE)
having no life; not living or not showing any signs of life
not living; showing no signs of life

Examples

The lifeless body lay on the floor, untouched and unmoving.
After the storm, the once vibrant garden appeared completely lifeless.
Her voice sounded strangely lifeless as she delivered the sad news.

Synset Examples

a lifeless body

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dead - no longer alive
  • Antonym: alive - living; not dead

Etymology

From life + -less (suffix, meaning 'without').

3. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪfləs / (AmE), / ˈlaɪfləs / (BrE)
(of a place or thing) not having the ability to support or sustain life.
unable to support life

Examples

Scientists discovered that the surface of Mars is lifeless due to extreme cold and lack of atmosphere.
Unlike Earth, the distant exoplanet appeared completely lifeless in the telescope images.
The robotic probe sent back data confirming the moon remains a lifeless wasteland.

Synset Examples

a lifeless planet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inhospitable - (of an environment) harsh and difficult to live in
  • Antonym: habitable - (of a place) suitable or good enough to live in

Etymology

From 'life' + '-less', meaning 'without life'.

Examples
