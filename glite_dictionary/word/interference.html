interference

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈfɪrəns / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈfɪərəns / (BrE)
the action of getting involved in a situation so as to hinder, obstruct, or impede progress or activity
the act of hindering or obstructing

Examples

The referee called a penalty for interference during the hockey game.
He resented his boss’s constant interference in his project.
Too much government interference can slow down economic growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstruction - the act of blocking or hindering progress
  • Antonym: assistance - the act of helping or supporting

Etymology

From interfere + -ence, ultimately from Latin inter- (between) and ferīre (to strike).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈfɪrəns / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈfɪərəns / (BrE)
the act or policy of intervening in the affairs of other countries, often without invitation or approval
intervention in another country's affairs

Examples

The government denied any interference in the neighboring country's political crisis.
International law prohibits interference in the domestic affairs of sovereign states.
He accused the foreign nation of undue interference in their trade negotiations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intervention - the action of becoming intentionally involved in a difficult situation, in order to improve it or prevent it from getting worse

Etymology

From Latin *interferre* (to strike against), via French *interférence*.

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈfɪrəns / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈfɪərəns / (BrE)
the act of obstructing or impeding progress or activity
obstruction or impediment

Examples

The constant interference from the audience made it difficult for the speaker to continue.
Heavy rain caused an interference with our travel plans yesterday.
She considered his suggestions as unwanted interference in her personal affairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstruction - something that blocks or impedes progress
  • Synonym: hindrance - something that prevents or delays progress
  • Antonym: assistance - help or support that aids progress, opposite of interference

Etymology

From Latin 'interferre', meaning to strike against.

(in American football) the act of blocking or impeding an opponent to protect or open the way for a teammate who has the ball.
blocking an opponent (American football)
american

Examples

Good interference allowed the quarterback more time to throw the ball downfield.
The coach emphasized the importance of effective interference in the upcoming game.

5. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈfɪrəns / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈfɪərəns / (BrE)
undesired electrical or acoustic activity that disrupts or impairs the transmission or reception of signals in communication systems
disturbance to signals in communication

Examples

The radio signal was lost due to heavy interference from nearby electrical equipment.
Engineers work hard to minimize interference in wireless networks.
Mobile phone conversations often suffer from interference in densely populated areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noise - random or unwanted disturbances that interfere with signal transmission

Etymology

From interfere (verb) + -ence, based on Latin inter- (between) and ferīre (to strike).
