daydream

1. VERB

/ ˈdeɪˌdriːm / (AmE), / ˈdeɪˌdriːm / (BrE)
to indulge in pleasant, often wishful thoughts or fantasies while awake, typically instead of paying attention to the present
to indulge in pleasant fantasies while awake

Examples

He would often daydream about traveling the world instead of listening in class.
She caught herself daydreaming during the boring meeting.
Whenever he is bored, he just daydreams about being a famous musician.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fantasize - to imagine things that are impossible or improbable

Etymology

Formed from 'day' + 'dream', first used in English in the 18th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪˌdriːm / (AmE), / ˈdeɪdriːm / (BrE)
a series of pleasant thoughts that distract one's attention from the present, typically occurring while awake
pleasant thoughts while awake

Examples

She often slips into a daydream during long meetings at work.
The teacher noticed him staring out the window, lost in a daydream.
A sudden noise snapped her out of her pleasant daydream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reverie - a state of being pleasantly lost in one's thoughts
  • Antonym: nightmare - a frightening or unpleasant dream while asleep

Etymology

Formed from 'day' + 'dream', first known use in early 18th century.
