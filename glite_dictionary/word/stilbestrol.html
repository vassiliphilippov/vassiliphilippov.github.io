stilbestrol

1. NOUN

/ stɪlˈbɛstrɔl / (AmE), / stɪlˈbɛstrɒl / (BrE)
a potent synthetic estrogen formerly used in medicine and as a growth promoter in livestock and poultry feed
potent synthetic estrogen compound
medical

Examples

The use of stilbestrol in animal feed was banned due to health concerns.
Researchers studied the effects of stilbestrol exposure on hormone levels.
Doctors once prescribed stilbestrol to prevent pregnancy complications, but it is no longer used for this purpose.

Etymology

From 'stilbene' (an organic compound) and 'estr-' (relating to estrogen).
