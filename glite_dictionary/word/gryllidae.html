Gryllidae

1. NOUN

/ ɡrɪˈlɪdiː / (AmE), / ɡrɪˈlɪdɪi / (BrE)
A family of insects commonly known as crickets, characterized by long antennae and the ability of males to produce a chirping sound by rubbing their wings together.
family of insects known as crickets

Examples

The Gryllidae family includes many species that are commonly known for their musical chirping.
Scientists study Gryllidae to better understand insect communication and behavior.
With over 900 species, Gryllidae are widespread in temperate and tropical regions around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cricket - an insect related to grasshoppers, known for its chirping sound

Etymology

From Greek *gryllos* (cricket) and scientific Latin -idae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ɡrɪˈlɪdiː / (AmE), / ɡrɪˈlɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of insects in the order Orthoptera, commonly known as crickets, characterized by their long antennae and chirping calls
family of insects, crickets

Examples

The Gryllidae family includes hundreds of different species of crickets found around the world.
Scientists study the Gryllidae to better understand acoustic communication in insects.
Many members of the Gryllidae are known for their distinctive chirping as a mating call.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cricket - insect known for its chirping sound, belonging to Gryllidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'gryllos' (cricket) and the taxonomic suffix '-idae'.
