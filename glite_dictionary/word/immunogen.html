immunogen

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmjunəˌdʒɛn / (AmE), / ɪˈmjuːnədʒɛn / (BrE)
a substance or organism that stimulates an immune response, producing immunity, when introduced into the body
substance causing immune response
medical

Examples

A vaccine contains an immunogen that helps the body recognize and fight specific pathogens.
Researchers tested the immunogen in mice to measure the immune system's response.
The effectiveness of an immunogen depends on its ability to elicit a strong and lasting immunity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antigen - a substance that causes the production of antibodies

Etymology

From Latin 'immunis' (exempt, immune) + '-gen' from Greek 'genein' (to produce).
