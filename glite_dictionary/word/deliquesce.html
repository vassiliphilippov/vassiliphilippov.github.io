to deliquesce

1. VERB

/ ˌdɛlɪˈkwɛs / (AmE), / ˌdɛlɪˈkwɛs / (BrE)
(of a solid) to melt or become liquid by absorbing moisture from the air, especially used of certain salts.
become liquid by absorbing moisture

Examples

Certain salts will deliquesce if stored in a humid environment for too long.
The fruit began to deliquesce after being left out in the open air.
Some chemicals deliquesce so rapidly that they must be kept in airtight containers.

Synset Examples

this type of salt deliquesces easily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melt - to change from solid to liquid by heat
  • Antonym: to solidify - to make or become solid

Etymology

From Latin deliquescere (to become liquid), from de- + liquescere (to become liquid).

2. VERB

/ ˌdɛlɪˈkwɛs / (AmE), / ˌdɛlɪˈkwɛs / (BrE)
(of a substance or organism) to become liquid, especially as a result of absorbing moisture from the air or through the process of decay.
to become liquid by absorbing moisture or decaying

Examples

After several days in the damp cellar, the old mushrooms began to deliquesce.
Certain salts will deliquesce if left exposed to humid air.
The corpse of the small animal seemed to deliquesce rapidly in the hot weather.

Synset Examples

The fungi eventually deliquesced

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dissolve - to become or cause to become incorporated into a liquid

Etymology

From Latin deliquescere (to melt, dissolve), from de- (down) + liquescere (to become liquid).
