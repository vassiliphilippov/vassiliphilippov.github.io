Choeronycteris mexicana

1. NOUN

/ koʊˌɛroʊˈnɪktərɪs ˌmɛksɪˈkænə / (AmE), / kəʊˌɛərəʊˈnɪktərɪs ˌmɛksɪˈkænə / (BrE)
A species of bat found in Mexico and the southwestern United States, known for its small ears and long slender nose.
species of Mexican long-nosed bat

Examples

The Choeronycteris mexicana plays an important role in pollinating desert plants in Mexico.
Researchers caught a glimpse of the elusive Choeronycteris mexicana during their night survey.
Unlike other bats, Choeronycteris mexicana prefers nectar and pollen over insects.

Etymology

From Greek 'choiros' (pig) + 'nycteris' (bat) and Latin 'mexicana' (Mexican).
