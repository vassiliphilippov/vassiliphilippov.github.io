Dies Irae

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːeɪs ˈɪreɪ / (AmE), / ˌdiːɛs ˈɪəriː / (BrE)
the opening words of a medieval Latin hymn about the Last Judgment, used also to refer to the hymn itself or its musical settings
medieval Latin hymn about the Last Judgment

Examples

The mournful sound of the Dies Irae echoed throughout the cathedral during the funeral service.
Many famous composers have incorporated the theme of the Dies Irae into their orchestral works.
The medieval hymn Dies Irae is often performed in requiem masses.

Etymology

Latin phrase meaning 'day of wrath'; from the hymn's first line, 'Dies irae, dies illa'.
