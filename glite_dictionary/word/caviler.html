caviler

1. NOUN

/ ˈkævələr / (AmE), / ˈkævɪlə / (BrE)
a person who makes petty or unnecessary objections; someone who quibbles over trivial matters
person who raises trivial objections

Examples

The meeting dragged on because one caviler constantly questioned every detail of the proposal.
He was known as a caviler who enjoyed finding faults in others' ideas.
She grew impatient with the caviler who kept interrupting the discussion with minor objections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quibbler - a person who argues or raises objections about trivial matters

Etymology

From French *caviller* (to quibble), from Latin *cavillari* (to jest, satirize).
