zoril

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɔː.rɪl / (AmE), / ˈzɒ.rɪl / (BrE)
A small carnivorous mammal of northern Africa, similar to a skunk and known for emitting a foul odor as a defense.
African carnivorous mammal, like a skunk

Examples

The zoril defends itself from predators by releasing a pungent smell.
Few people have seen a zoril in the wild due to its secretive habits.
Researchers are studying the habitat requirements of the zoril in northern Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muishond - A similar type of skunk-like mammal found in Africa

Etymology

From French 'zorille', from Spanish 'zorrillo' (little fox), ultimately from 'zorro' (fox).
