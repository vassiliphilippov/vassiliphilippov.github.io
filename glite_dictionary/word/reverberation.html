reverberation

1. NOUN

/ rɪˌvɜrbəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌvɜːbəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
an effect or result that is indirectly produced by an earlier action, often one that continues for some time
indirect consequence or aftereffect

Examples

The reverberations of the policy change affected the company for years after its introduction.
Global financial markets still felt the reverberation of the economic crisis decades later.
Her resignation sent reverberations through the entire organization, prompting further departures.

Synset Examples

his declaration had unforeseen repercussions
reverberations of the market crash were felt years later

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repercussion - an indirect effect or result, often negative
  • Synonym: consequence - a result or effect of an action or condition

Etymology

From Latin 'reverberatio', from 'reverberare' (to strike back).

2. NOUN

/ rəˌvɝː.bəˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌvɜː.bərˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the persistence or repetition of a sound after its source has stopped, caused by reflection of sound waves from surfaces such as walls or ceilings
prolonged reflected sound; echoing

Examples

After the thunderclap, the reverberation echoed through the valley for several seconds.
The reverberation of footsteps in the empty hall made the space seem even larger.
Engineers measured the reverberation time of the concert hall to improve its acoustics.

Synset Examples

she could hear echoes of her own footsteps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: echo - a sound or sounds caused by the reflection of sound waves from a surface

Etymology

From Latin reverberatio (beating back), from reverberare (to strike back).
