gouge

1. NOUN

/ ɡaʊdʒ / (AmE), / ɡaʊdʒ / (BrE)
A chisel with a curved blade used for cutting grooves or channels in wood or other materials.
chisel with a curved blade

Examples

She used a gouge to carve an intricate pattern into the wooden panel.
The craftsman carefully sharpened his gouge before starting the sculpture.
A gouge leaves a rounded groove, unlike a flat chisel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chisel - a tool with a straight blade for cutting or shaping material

Etymology

From Old French 'gouge', from Late Latin 'gubia' (chisel).

6. NOUN

/ ɡaʊdʒ / (AmE), / ɡaʊdʒ / (BrE)
A deep scratch or groove made in a surface, usually by scraping or striking with something sharp or forceful.
deep scratch or groove in a surface

Examples

There was a long gouge in the wooden table after the accident.
The moving company tried to repair the gouge left in the floor.
He ran his hand over the gouge and sighed, remembering how it happened.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: scratch - a thin mark or shallow cut on a surface
  • Degree Lower: dent - a slight hollow made in a surface by a blow or pressure

Etymology

From Old French gouge (chisel), from Late Latin gubia, gougia (chisel).
