Cypripedium arietinum

1. NOUN

/ saɪ.prɪˈpiː.di.əm ˌæriˈɛt.ɪ.nəm / (AmE), / sɪ.prɪˈpiː.di.əm ˌæriˈɛt.ɪ.nəm / (BrE)
A species of orchid native to northern North America, with a brownish-green flower and a distinctive red-and-white lip resembling a ram's head.
species of North American orchid

Examples

The rare Cypripedium arietinum can be found in wet forests across parts of Canada.
Botanists were excited to discover a cluster of Cypripedium arietinum in the nature reserve.
Gardeners value Cypripedium arietinum for its unique, ram's head-shaped flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ram's head lady's slipper - another common name for Cypripedium arietinum

Etymology

From Latin: Cypripedium (genus name, Greek *Kypris* Aphrodite + *pedilon* sandal) and arietinum (from Latin *arietinus* 'of a ram').
