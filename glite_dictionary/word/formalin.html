formalin

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔr.mə.lɪn / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.mə.lɪn / (BrE)
A 10% solution of formaldehyde in water, used as a disinfectant and for preserving biological specimens.
formaldehyde solution for preservation/disinfectant
medical

Examples

The laboratory technician stored the organ sample in formalin for preservation.
Schools often use formalin to keep specimens in biology classrooms.
Exposure to formalin can be hazardous without adequate ventilation.

Etymology

From French 'formaline', based on 'formaldehyde' and '-in' (a chemical suffix).
