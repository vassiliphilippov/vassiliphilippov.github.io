maternity

1. NOUN

/ məˈtɝː.nə.ti / (AmE), / məˈtɜː.nə.ti / (BrE)
the state of being pregnant; the period during which a woman carries a developing fetus in her uterus
the state of being pregnant

Examples

The company offers generous maternity leave for all female employees.
During her maternity, she attended regular check-ups at the clinic.
She wore special maternity clothes designed for comfort during pregnancy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pregnancy - the state of being pregnant

Etymology

From Latin 'maternitas', from 'mater' (mother).

2. NOUN

/ məˈtɜrnəti / (AmE), / məˈtɜːnəti / (BrE)
the state of being a mother; the relationship between a mother and her child
the state of being a mother

Examples

Her maternity was officially established through a DNA test.
The court had to determine maternity in a complicated family case.
Modern science can confirm maternity as well as paternity with high accuracy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: paternity - the state of being a father; fatherhood

Etymology

From Latin 'maternitas', from 'mater' meaning 'mother'.

3. NOUN

/ məˈtɜrnəti / (AmE), / məˈtɜːnəti / (BrE)
The quality of having or showing the tenderness, warmth, and affection characteristic of a mother.
motherly warmth and tenderness

Examples

The teacher's maternity was evident in the way she comforted her students.
Her maternity shone through as she lovingly cared for her younger siblings.
Even without children of her own, she displayed a natural maternity that everyone admired.

Synset Examples

the girl's motherliness made her invaluable in caring for the children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motherliness - the quality of being motherly; maternal affection and tenderness
  • Antonym: paternity - the quality or state of being a father; the paternal relationship

Etymology

From Latin 'maternitas', from 'mater' (mother).
