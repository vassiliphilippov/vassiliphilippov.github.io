lacking

1. ADJ

/ ˈlækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlækɪŋ / (BrE)
not present or not available; missing or absent
not present or missing

Examples

His enthusiasm was lacking during the important meeting.
The dish was tasty but lacking in spices.
Many essential details were lacking from the report.

Synset Examples

the thumb is absent
her appetite was lacking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absent - not present in a place, at an occasion, or as part of something
  • Synonym: missing - not able to be found because it is not in its expected place
  • Antonym: abundant - present in large quantities; more than enough

Etymology

Present participle of 'lack', Middle English 'lakken', from Old Norse 'laka' (to lack).

2. ADJ

/ ˈlækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlakɪŋ / (BrE)
not having enough of something; inadequate in amount or degree
not having enough; inadequate

Examples

The report was lacking in detail, making it hard to understand the results.
Her answer was lacking in conviction and failed to persuade the audience.
He felt that his cooking skills were still lacking compared to the chef's.

Synset Examples

a deficient education
deficient in common sense
lacking in stamina
tested and found wanting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deficient - not having enough of something essential
  • Synonym: wanting - lacking or missing a desired quality
  • Antonym: adequate - sufficient or satisfactory in quantity or quality

Etymology

Present participle of 'lack,' from Middle English 'lakken' (to lack, be wanting).
