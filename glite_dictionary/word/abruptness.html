abruptness

1. NOUN

/ əˈbrʌpt.nəs / (AmE), / əˈbrʌpt.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being very steep or sudden, especially in reference to a slope or incline
steepness or suddenness of a slope

Examples

The abruptness of the mountain made the climb especially challenging for the hikers.
Many roads in the region were closed due to the abruptness of the terrain after the landslide.
The abruptness of the hill took cyclists by surprise during the race.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steepness - the quality of being steep

Etymology

From Latin abruptus (broken off, steep) via French abrupt.

2. NOUN

/ əˈbrʌpt.nəs / (AmE), / əˈbrʌpt.nəs / (BrE)
a manner that is sudden and often rude or lacking in courtesy
sudden, discourteous manner

Examples

The abruptness of his reply caught everyone off guard.
She was criticized for the abruptness with which she ended the meeting.
His abruptness sometimes offended clients who expected a gentler approach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curtness - rudeness or brevity in speech or manner

Etymology

From Latin 'abruptus' (broken off, steep).

3. NOUN

/ əˈbrʌptnəs / (AmE), / əˈbrʌptnəs / (BrE)
the quality of occurring suddenly and unexpectedly, often with little or no warning
quality of being sudden and unexpected

Examples

The abruptness of his departure left everyone in shock.
I was startled by the abruptness with which the meeting ended.
Her abruptness caught her colleagues off guard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suddenness - the quality of happening quickly or without warning

Etymology

From Latin 'abruptus' (broken off) via 'abrupt' + '-ness'.
