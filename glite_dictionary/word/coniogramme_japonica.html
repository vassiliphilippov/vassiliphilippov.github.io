Coniogramme japonica

1. NOUN

/ koʊniˈɑːɡræmi dʒəˈpoʊnɪkə / (AmE), / kəʊniˈɒɡræmi dʒəˈpɒnɪkə / (BrE)
A fast-growing and sturdy fern native to Japan, cultivated for its attractive broad dark-green pinnate fronds.
species of fern native to Japan

Examples

Coniogramme japonica thrives in shaded gardens with moist, well-drained soil.
The broad, pinnate fronds of Coniogramme japonica add a lush, tropical feel to landscapes.
Gardeners often choose Coniogramme japonica for its hardiness and attractive appearance.

Etymology

From genus name Coniogramme (from Greek kōnion 'fern' + gramma 'line' for spore patterns) and specific epithet japonica (Latin for 'of Japan').
