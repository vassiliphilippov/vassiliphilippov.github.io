nonrandom

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˈrændəm / (AmE), / nɒnˈrændəm / (BrE)
Not occurring by chance; having a pattern, system, or method rather than being random.
not occurring by chance; with a pattern

Examples

The study used a nonrandom selection process to choose participants.
A nonrandom pattern was clearly visible in the test results.
The experiment failed because the samples were nonrandom and biased.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: random - occurring by chance, without a pattern

Etymology

Formed by combining the prefix 'non-' (not) with 'random'.
