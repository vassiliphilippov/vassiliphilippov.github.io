foot-ton

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊt ˌtʌn / (AmE), / ˈfʊt ˌtʌn / (BrE)
A unit of work or energy equal to 2,240 foot-pounds.
unit of work equal to 2,240 foot-pounds

Examples

The engine was rated at over 100,000 foot-tons of energy.
The calculations required converting the figure into foot-tons for consistency.
He explained that a single foot-ton represents a considerable amount of work in engineering terms.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: foot-pound - unit of work or energy equal to that done by a force of one pound acting through a distance of one foot

Etymology

Formed from 'foot-pound' (a unit of work) and 'ton' (referring to 2,240 pounds in British weight measurement).
