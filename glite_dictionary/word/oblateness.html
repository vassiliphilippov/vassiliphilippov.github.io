oblateness

1. NOUN

/ əˈbleɪtnəs / (AmE), / əˈbleɪtnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being flattened at the poles, so that the equatorial diameter is greater than the polar diameter; the shape of an object that is not perfectly spherical but somewhat squashed at the poles
the state of being flattened at the poles

Examples

The Earth's oblateness causes variations in gravity at different latitudes.
Astronomers study the oblateness of planets to learn about their internal structure.
The gas giant's rapid rotation contributes to its pronounced oblateness.

Synset Examples

the oblateness of the planet

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sphericity - the quality of being perfectly round or spherical

Etymology

From Latin 'oblātus' (flattened), ultimately from 'ob-' (towards) and 'lātus' (carried, borne), with the English suffix '-ness'.
