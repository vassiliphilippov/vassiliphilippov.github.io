Arctotis stoechadifolia

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrkˈtoʊtɪs stoʊˌkædəˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ɑːkˈtɒtɪs stəʊˌkædəˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
A bushy perennial plant native to South Africa, with white or violet flowers, often covering valley sides with color in its native habitat.
South African perennial with white or violet flowers

Examples

The hills were blanketed with blooming Arctotis stoechadifolia during spring.
Botanists study Arctotis stoechadifolia for its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils.
Gardeners prize Arctotis stoechadifolia for its vibrant white and violet flowers.

Etymology

From the genus name Arctotis (Greek arktos, 'bear', and ous, 'ear') and the species stoechadifolia, meaning 'with leaves like Stoechas' (a plant genus).
