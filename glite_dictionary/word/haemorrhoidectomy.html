haemorrhoidectomy

1. NOUN

/ hiˌmɔɹ.ɔɪˈdɛk.tə.mi / (AmE), / hɪˌmɒr.ɔɪˈdɛk.tə.mi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which haemorrhoids are tied off and removed
surgical removal of haemorrhoids
medical british

Examples

After years of discomfort, she finally decided to have a haemorrhoidectomy.
The doctor explained the recovery process following a haemorrhoidectomy.
Many patients report significant relief after undergoing haemorrhoidectomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemorrhoidectomy - surgical removal of hemorrhoids (US spelling)

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + -rrhoids (flowing) + -ectomy (excision, removal).
