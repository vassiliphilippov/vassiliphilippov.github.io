fornicatress

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɔrnɪˈkeɪtrɪs / (AmE), / ˌfɔːnɪˈkeɪtrɪs / (BrE)
a woman who commits fornication or adultery, especially as condemned by religious or moral standards
a woman guilty of adultery or fornication
archaic

Examples

In the old town records, she was harshly labeled a fornicatress and shunned by the community.
The preacher denounced the sins of the fornicatress during Sunday’s sermon.
Historical laws often imposed severe penalties on any woman branded a fornicatress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fornicator - a person who commits fornication, typically male or gender-neutral
  • Synonym: adulteress - a woman who commits adultery

Etymology

From Latin 'fornicari' (to fornicate) + '-ess', feminine suffix.
