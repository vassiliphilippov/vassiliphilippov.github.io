hydroponic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪdrəˈpɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪdrə(ʊ)ˈpɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving the cultivation of plants in water containing dissolved nutrients rather than in soil
growing plants in water with nutrients

Examples

The greenhouse specializes in hydroponic tomatoes, which are grown without soil.
Many urban farms are adopting hydroponic techniques to maximize their crop yields.
She prefers buying hydroponic lettuce because it is less likely to carry soil-borne diseases.

Synset Examples

aquacultural methods
hydroponic lettuce

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydroponics - the process of growing plants in water with nutrients instead of soil

Etymology

From hydro- (water) + Greek ponos (labor), referring to cultivation by means of water.
