Arctiidae

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrkˈtiːɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ɑːkˈtɪɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of brightly colored moths known as tiger moths, many of which have hairy caterpillars and often striking patterns
family of tiger moths

Examples

The brightly colored moth she found belonged to the Arctiidae family.
Many species in Arctiidae are known for their striking wing patterns and hairy larvae.
Biologists studied the chemical defenses used by Arctiidae against predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tiger moth - a moth belonging to the family Arctiidae, often with colorful patterns

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Arctia, the type genus, from Greek arktos meaning 'bear', referring to the hairy caterpillars.
