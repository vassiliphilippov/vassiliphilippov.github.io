to disbud

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈbʌd / (AmE), / dɪsˈbʌd / (BrE)
to remove or destroy the undeveloped horn buds of a young animal, especially cattle, to prevent horn growth
remove undeveloped horn buds of cattle, etc.
medical

Examples

Farmers often disbud calves at an early age to prevent injuries and simplify handling.
It is recommended that you disbud young goats when they are just a few days old.
After the vet disbudded the calves, they were returned to the pasture.

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'dis-' (expressing removal) + 'bud'.

WordNet ID: oewn-00197693-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ dɪsˈbʌd / (AmE), / dɪsˈbʌd / (BrE)
to remove some buds from a plant so that the remaining flowers grow larger or of better quality
remove some buds for flower quality

Examples

To encourage larger roses, gardeners often disbud the weaker side shoots.
It is important to disbud chrysanthemum plants early in the growing season.
She spent the afternoon carefully disbudding her dahlias to ensure a beautiful display.

Etymology

From prefix 'dis-' (apart, away) + 'bud' (young growth on a plant).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
