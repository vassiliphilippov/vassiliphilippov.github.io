Phalacrocorax

1. NOUN

/ fəˌlækroʊˈkɔːræks / (AmE), / fəˌlækrəʊˈkɔːræks / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Phalacrocoracidae, comprising cormorant birds
genus of cormorant birds

Examples

The genus Phalacrocorax includes many species of cormorant found worldwide.
Scientists recently described a new species within the genus Phalacrocorax.
Phalacrocorax populations can be important indicators of marine ecosystem health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cormorant - a large diving seabird in the family Phalacrocoracidae

Etymology

From Greek phalakros (bald) + korax (raven or crow), referring to the bird's appearance.

2. NOUN

/ fəˌlækroʊˈkɔːræks / (AmE), / fəˌlakrəʊˈkɒræks / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic birds known as cormorants, comprising the typical species in the family Phalacrocoracidae.
genus of aquatic birds (cormorants)

Examples

The Phalacrocorax genus includes several well-known species of cormorants.
Biologists recently identified a new species within the Phalacrocorax genus.
Members of Phalacrocorax are excellent divers, often seen fishing in coastal waters.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek phalakros (bald) and korax (raven), referring to the bird's appearance.
