Aphriza

1. NOUN

/ əˈfraɪzə / (AmE), / əˈfriːzə / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Scolopacidae, comprising shorebirds such as the surfbird.
genus of shorebirds (Scolopacidae)

Examples

The Aphriza genus is best known for the surfbird, commonly found along rocky shorelines.
Scientists observed several Aphriza species during the coastal migration survey.
Ornithologists have studied the unique feeding behavior of Aphriza in tidal regions.

Etymology

From Greek aphrodes (sea-foam), referring to the shore habitat of the birds.

2. NOUN

/ əˈfraɪzə / (AmE), / əˈfraɪzə / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Scolopacidae, containing the species known as the surfbird.
a bird genus in Scolopacidae

Examples

The Aphriza genus consists of only one living species, the surfbird.
Ornithologists have studied Aphriza to better understand shorebird evolution.
The surfbird, classified under Aphriza, inhabits rocky Pacific coasts.

Etymology

From New Latin, used in zoological nomenclature.
