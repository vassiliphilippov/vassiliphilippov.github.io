hypertonus

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈpɜr.toʊ.nəs / (AmE), / haɪˈpɜː.tə.nəs / (BrE)
An abnormal increase in muscle tone; the state of being hypertonic, especially in muscular tissue.
increased muscle tone (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient exhibited pronounced hypertonus in the lower limbs after the stroke.
Spasticity is a form of hypertonus that can interfere with movement and daily activities.
Neurologists assess hypertonus by evaluating resistance to passive movement in muscles.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypotonia - reduced muscle tone
  • Synonym: hypertonia - state of increased muscle tone or tension

Etymology

From hyper- (excess) + Greek tonos (tone).
