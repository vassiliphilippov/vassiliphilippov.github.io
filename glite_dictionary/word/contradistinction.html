contradistinction

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑntrədɪˈstɪŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒntrədɪˈstɪŋkʃən / (BrE)
a distinction made by contrasting one thing with another
a distinction by contrast

Examples

The law draws a contradistinction between civil and criminal cases.
Her teaching style, in contradistinction to her colleague's, encourages more student participation.
We must note the contradistinction between natural and synthetic materials.

Synset Examples

sculpture in contradistinction to painting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrast - the state of being strikingly different from something else

Etymology

From Latin 'contra-' (against) + 'distinguere' (to separate).
