limiting

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɪmɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɪmɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
restricting the extent, freedom, or possibilities of someone or something; placing a constraint or boundary
restrictive; placing a constraint

Examples

The company found the new regulations to be very limiting for its business operations.
Some people find uniforms limiting because they restrict personal expression.
A limiting belief can prevent you from achieving your goals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restrictive - imposing restrictions or limitations
  • Antonym: permissive - allowing a great deal of freedom

Etymology

From the verb 'limit', which comes from Latin 'limitare' (to bound, limit).

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɪmɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɪmɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
(Grammar) restricting or specifying the range or reference of a modified word or phrase, especially in a clause or adjective.
(Grammar) specifying or restricting meaning

Examples

In the phrase 'the books that had influenced him', 'that had influenced him' is a limiting clause.
A limiting adjective narrows down the group being referred to, such as 'these' in 'these books'.
The teacher explained how limiting clauses provide specific information, while non-limiting clauses do not.

Synset Examples

the restrictive clause in ‘Each made a list of the books that had influenced him’ limits the books on the list to only those particular ones defined by the clause

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restrictive - (Grammar) limiting the reference of a word or phrase; specifically referring to restrictive clauses as in 'the books that had influenced him'

Etymology

Present participle of 'limit', from Latin 'limitare' (to limit), from 'limes' (boundary, limit).
