anorthopia

1. NOUN

/ ænˌɔrˈθoʊpiə / (AmE), / ænˌɔːˈθəʊpiə / (BrE)
Distorted vision in which straight lines appear curved.
visual distortion; straight lines appear curved
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with anorthopia after reporting that straight lines looked wavy or bent.
People with anorthopia may find it difficult to read or drive due to the distortion in their vision.
Correcting anorthopia often requires addressing underlying eye conditions such as astigmatism.

Etymology

From Greek 'an-' (not) + 'orthos' (straight) + 'opsis' (sight).
