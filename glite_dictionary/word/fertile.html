fertile

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɝː.t̬əl / (AmE), / ˈfɜː.taɪl / (BrE)
(of land or soil) able to produce abundant vegetation or crops; highly productive or fruitful.
producing abundant crops or vegetation

Examples

The fertile fields of the valley supply vegetables to the entire region.
This river delta is renowned for its fertile soil and thriving farms.
After years of careful irrigation, the once barren land became fertile and lush.

Synset Examples

fertile farmland
a fat land
a productive vineyard
rich soil

Related Terms

  • Antonym: barren - (of land or soil) too poor to produce much or any vegetation
  • Synonym: productive - yielding or producing abundantly

Etymology

From Latin 'fertilis' (fruitful), from 'ferre' (to bear, carry).

3. ADJ

/ ˈfɝːtəl / (AmE), / ˈfɜːtaɪl / (BrE)
(of an animal, plant, or human) able to produce many offspring or fruits; highly productive or prolific.
able to produce many offspring or fruits

Examples

Some regions are famed for their fertile soil and abundant harvests.
That fertile rabbit quickly populated the entire enclosure with her offspring.

4. ADJ

/ ˈfɜr.t̬əl / (AmE), / ˈfɜː.taɪl / (BrE)
(of people, animals, or plants) able to produce young, seeds, or fruit; capable of reproduction.
able to reproduce (living things)

Examples

The soil in this area is so fertile that crops thrive year after year.
After the treatment, she became fertile and was able to have children.
Rabbits are known for being highly fertile and reproducing quickly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infertile - not able to reproduce or produce offspring
  • Antonym: sterile - unable to produce offspring or seeds

Etymology

From Latin fertilis (fruitful, productive), from ferre (to bear, carry).
