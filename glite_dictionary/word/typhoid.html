typhoid

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪ.fɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈtaɪ.fɔɪd / (BrE)
A serious infectious disease causing fever, abdominal pain, and ulcers in the intestines, spread mainly through contaminated food or water.
serious infectious disease, fever and intestinal ulcers
medical

Examples

An outbreak of typhoid in the region led to several hospitalizations last summer.
She contracted typhoid after drinking contaminated water while traveling abroad.
Vaccination can protect you from diseases like typhoid and hepatitis A.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: typhoid fever - another term for typhoid, highlighting fever as a symptom

Etymology

From French 'typhoïde', from Greek 'typhos' (smoke, stupor) and the suffix '-oid' (resembling).
