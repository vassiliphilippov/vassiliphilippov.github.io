Penstemon

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnstɪmən / (AmE), / ˈpɛnstiːmən / (BrE)
a genus of North American herbaceous plants or subshrubs with showy, tubular flowers in various colors, also known as beardtongues
genus of flowering plants (beardtongues)

Examples

Several varieties of Penstemon thrive in dry, rocky soils and are popular in native plant gardens.
We planted a row of Penstemon along the driveway to attract hummingbirds.
The Penstemon blooms added vibrant color to the wildflower meadow in early summer.

Etymology

From Modern Latin, formed from Greek 'pente' (five) and 'stēmōn' (thread), referring to the plant's five stamens.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnstəˌmɑn / (AmE), / ˈpɛnstɪˌmɒn / (BrE)
a genus of North American plants with showy flowers in various colors, commonly called beardtongues
genus of flowering plants (beardtongues)

Examples

Many gardeners choose Penstemon for its colorful, trumpet-shaped flowers that attract hummingbirds.
There are over 250 species within the Penstemon genus found largely in western North America.
The blue blossoms of Penstemon add striking color to native plant gardens.

Etymology

From Greek 'penta-' (five) + 'stēmōn' (thread), referring to the five stamens.
