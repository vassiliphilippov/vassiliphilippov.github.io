Platanthera bifolia

1. NOUN

/ pləˈtænθərə baɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / pləˈtanθərə bɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A south European species of orchid with fragrant, greenish-white flowers; also known as the lesser butterfly-orchid.
a south European orchid species

Examples

The rare Platanthera bifolia was spotted in the alpine meadow during our nature walk.
Gardeners are advised that Platanthera bifolia thrives best in moist, lightly shaded soil.
Many botanists travel to observe Platanthera bifolia in its natural habitat during its blooming season.

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Platanthera' from Greek 'platys' (broad) and 'anthera' (anther, part of a stamen), 'bifolia' meaning 'two-leaved'.
