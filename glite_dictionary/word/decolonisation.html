decolonisation

1. NOUN

/ diˌkɑːlənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌkɒlənʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a country or territory gains independence from colonial rule
transition from colonial rule to independence

Examples

The wave of decolonisation after World War II reshaped the political landscape of Africa and Asia.
Many former colonies faced economic challenges following decolonisation.
The United Nations played a significant role in supporting the process of decolonisation around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: independence - the condition of being independent, especially of a country from colonial rule
  • Antonym: colonisation - the action of establishing control over indigenous people or area

Etymology

From de- (expressing reversal) + colonisation, ultimately from Latin colonia (settlement, colony).
