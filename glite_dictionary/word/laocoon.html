Laocoon

1. PROPN

/ leɪˈɑːkoʊˌɑn / (AmE), / leɪˈɒkəʊˌɒn / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a Trojan priest of Apollo who warned against bringing the Greek wooden horse into Troy, and who, along with his sons, was killed by serpents sent by the gods.
Trojan priest killed by serpents in Greek myth

Examples

The famous statue of Laocoon and his sons depicts their tragic death by serpents.
Legend says that Laocoon warned the Trojans about the danger of accepting the wooden horse.
Many scholars reference Laocoon's fate in discussions of Greek mythology.

Etymology

From Latin Laocoon, from Ancient Greek Λαοκόων (Laokóōn).
