washstand

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑʃˌstænd / (AmE), / ˈwɒʃˌstænd / (BrE)
A piece of furniture with a table or stand designed to hold a basin and pitcher of water for washing.
table or stand for washing basin and pitcher

Examples

The antique washstand in the bedroom held an elegant porcelain basin and pitcher.
Before modern plumbing, every guest room had a washstand for freshening up in the morning.
She found the family photo tucked behind the old washstand in the attic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wash-hand stand - British term for a washstand

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːʃˌstænd / (AmE), / ˈwɒʃˌstænd / (BrE)
a piece of furniture or a fixture with a basin and water supply, used for washing hands and face, typically found in a bedroom or bathroom
furniture/fixture with a basin for washing

Examples

She placed her toiletries neatly on the washstand by the window.
The old-fashioned washstand added charm to the guest room.
He rinsed his hands quickly at the washstand before dinner.

Synset Examples

he ran some water in the basin and splashed it on his face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basin - a bowl for washing, often part of a washstand

Etymology

From 'wash' + 'stand', originally describing a stand that held a pitcher and basin for washing.
