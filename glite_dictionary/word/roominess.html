roominess

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːmi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈruːmi.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being spacious and having plenty of space, especially inside a building or vehicle
spacious quality; plenty of space

Examples

The roominess of the new apartment made it perfect for hosting parties.
Buyers often comment on the roominess of the van's interior.
She appreciated the roominess of her office compared to her previous workspace.

Synset Examples

the capaciousness of Santa's bag astounded the child
roominess in this size car is always a compromise
his new office lacked the spaciousness that he had become accustomed to

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spaciousness - the quality of being spacious
  • Antonym: crampedness - the quality of feeling cramped or lacking space

Etymology

From the word 'roomy' (spacious) + noun suffix '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈruːmɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈruːmɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of having a broad or open intellectual outlook; open-mindedness or breadth of mind
intellectual breadth or open-mindedness

Examples

Her roominess allowed her to consider everyone's point of view without judgment.
In philosophical debate, roominess is a valuable trait, fostering creative solutions.
His scholarly roominess made the department truly innovative.

Synset Examples

the very capaciousness of the idea meant that agreement on fundamentals was unnecessary
his unselfishness gave him great intellectual roominess

Related Terms

  • Synonym: open-mindedness - the quality of being receptive to new ideas
  • Antonym: narrow-mindedness - the quality of being unwilling to consider new ideas

Etymology

From 'roomy' + '-ness', modeled on the literal sense of spaciousness.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05626390-n
Synset POS: n
