Pneumovax

1. NOUN

/ ˈnjuːmoʊˌvæks / (AmE), / ˈnjuːməʊˌvæks / (BrE)
a trademarked brand of vaccine that protects against 23 of the most common strains of pneumococcal bacteria
brand of pneumococcal vaccine
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended a Pneumovax vaccination for elderly patients at risk of pneumonia.
Many travelers receive Pneumovax before visiting countries with high rates of pneumococcal infections.
She had already received her Pneumovax shot as part of her routine immunizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pneumococcal vaccine - a vaccine that protects against Streptococcus pneumoniae infections
