Macrotyloma

1. NOUN

/ ˌmækrəʊˈtaɪloʊmə / (AmE), / ˌmakrəʊˈtɪləʊmə / (BrE)
a genus of annual or perennial climbing plants native to Africa, India, and Australia, often placed in the family Fabaceae; includes species sometimes used as food crops.
genus of climbing plants (Fabaceae)

Examples

Macrotyloma species such as horse gram are cultivated for food in India and parts of Africa.
The roots of Macrotyloma plants can help improve soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.
Researchers are studying Macrotyloma for its drought-resistant properties in arid regions.

Etymology

From Greek 'makros' (large) and 'tylos' (knot, swelling), referring to features of the plant.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmækroʊˈtaɪlomə / (AmE), / ˌmakrəʊˈtɪləmə / (BrE)
A genus of annual or perennial climbing plants found in Africa, India, and Australia, sometimes classified within the genus Dolichos.
genus of vines in Africa, India, and Australia

Examples

Many farmers in Southeast Asia cultivate Macrotyloma as a source of protein-rich legumes.
The horse gram belongs to the Macrotyloma genus and is valued for its drought resistance.
Some species in the Macrotyloma genus are now being researched for their nutritional benefits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dolichos - A related genus of climbing plants often including species from Macrotyloma

Etymology

From Greek 'makros' (large) + 'tyloma' (swelling, nodule), referring to the plant's physical characteristics.
