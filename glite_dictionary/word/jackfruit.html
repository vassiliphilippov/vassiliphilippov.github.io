jackfruit

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒækˌfruːt / (AmE), / ˈdʒæk.fruːt / (BrE)
a large tropical fruit with rough green skin, edible sweet yellow flesh, and seeds that can be roasted, native to South and Southeast Asia
large tropical fruit with edible flesh and seeds

Examples

The ripe jackfruit is often used in desserts and sweet dishes across Southeast Asia.
She tried roasted jackfruit seeds for the first time and loved their nutty flavor.
Large pieces of jackfruit were stacked in the market, their scent filling the air.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'jaca', from Malayalam 'chakka'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒækˌfrut / (AmE), / ˈdʒæk.fruːt / (BrE)
A large tropical fruit with a rough yellow-green rind and sweet, fibrous edible flesh, or the tree that produces this fruit, native to South and Southeast Asia.
large tropical fruit or its tree

Examples

The ripe jackfruit is prized for its sweet, aromatic flesh in many Asian cuisines.
We saw a massive jackfruit hanging from a tree during our trip to Kerala.
Dried jackfruit seeds are often roasted and eaten as a snack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breadfruit - a tropical fruit similar in appearance and texture to jackfruit
  • Synonym: durian - a distinct tropical fruit with spiky rind and strong smell, also large and tropical

Etymology

Probably from Portuguese jaca, from Malayalam chakka.
