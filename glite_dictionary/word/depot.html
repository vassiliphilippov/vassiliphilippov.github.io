depot

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiː.poʊ, dɪˈpoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdep.əʊ / (BrE)
a place where large quantities of goods are stored before they are distributed or sold
place for storing goods

Examples

The company operates a large depot near the airport for its imported products.
The new buses will be delivered to the city’s main depot tomorrow morning.
After the harvest, the crops were sent directly to the central depot for storage.

Synset Examples

storehouses were built close to the docks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warehouse - a large building where goods are stored

Etymology

From French dépôt, from Latin depositum (something deposited).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdiːpoʊ, dəˈpoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdɛpəʊ, ˈdiːpəʊ / (BrE)
A place where buses, trains, or other vehicles regularly stop to load or unload passengers or goods.
station for loading or unloading passengers or goods

Examples

The bus arrived at the depot just before midnight, and everyone hurried inside.
All the packages are sorted at the central depot before being sent out for delivery.
When we reached the train depot, we waited for our luggage to be unloaded.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: station - a building where passengers can board or alight from trains or buses

Etymology

From French dépôt, from Latin dēpositum (something deposited).
