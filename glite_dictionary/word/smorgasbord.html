smorgasbord

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmɔrɡəsˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈsmɔːɡəsbɔːd / (BrE)
a wide variety or assortment of things; a diverse collection
a wide variety or assortment

Examples

The conference offered a smorgasbord of seminars and workshops for attendees to choose from.
Our local market features a smorgasbord of international foods and fresh produce.
The playlist is a smorgasbord of musical genres, from jazz to hip-hop to classical.

Synset Examples

a great assortment of cars was on display
he had a variety of disorders
a veritable smorgasbord of religions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assortment - a collection of different things or people
  • Synonym: variety - the quality or state of being different or diverse; a range of different things

Etymology

From Swedish *smörgåsbord* (sandwich table), originally referring to a buffet meal with many dishes.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsmɔrɡəsˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈsmɔːɡəsˌbɔːd / (BrE)
a meal served buffet-style with a variety of hot and cold dishes, originally of Scandinavian origin
varied buffet meal, esp. Scandinavian

Examples

The wedding reception featured an elaborate smorgasbord with delicacies from many countries.
Guests helped themselves to the smorgasbord set up along the side of the hall.
Every Sunday, the hotel offers a traditional Scandinavian smorgasbord for brunch.

Etymology

From Swedish 'smörgåsbord', from 'smörgås' (open sandwich) + 'bord' (table).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
