/ pɛk / (AmE), / pɛk / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) to eat by taking small, quick bites, like a bird.

Examples

3. NOUN

/ pɛk / (AmE), / pɛk / (BrE)
A United States unit of dry measure equal to 8 quarts, or about 537.6 cubic inches (8.81 liters).
US dry measure of 8 quarts

Examples

A peck of apples is more than enough for making several pies.
Farmers often sold produce in a peck or bushel at the local market.
The recipe calls for a peck of green tomatoes to make relish.

Etymology

From Middle English *pek*, from Old French *pek* (measure of capacity).
