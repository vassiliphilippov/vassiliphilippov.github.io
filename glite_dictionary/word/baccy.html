baccy

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæki / (AmE), / ˈbæki / (BrE)
Leaves of the tobacco plant that have been dried and prepared for smoking or ingestion; tobacco.
tobacco, especially for smoking
informal slang british

Examples

He reached into his pocket for some baccy to roll a cigarette.
I'm running low on baccy; could you lend me a bit?
Old Jim always had a pouch of baccy with him at the pub.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tobacco - leaves of the tobacco plant dried and prepared for smoking or ingestion

Etymology

Informal shortening of 'tobacco'.
