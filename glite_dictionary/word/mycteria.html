Mycteria

1. NOUN

/ mɪkˈtɪəriə / (AmE), / mɪkˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
a genus of large wading birds in the stork family, now mainly comprising the American wood ibis
genus of stork birds

Examples

The genus Mycteria is most notably represented by the American wood stork.
Scientists observed Mycteria nesting habits along the riverbanks.
Several species once included in Mycteria have since been reclassified.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stork - large wading bird with long legs and neck

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek *myktēr* (nose), referring to the bird's long beak.

2. NOUN

/ maɪkˈtɪəriə / (AmE), / mɪkˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
A genus of large tropical storks in the family Ciconiidae, now including only the American wood stork.
genus of storks in the family Ciconiidae

Examples

The wood stork, classified under the genus Mycteria, inhabits wetlands in the Americas.
Researchers discovered a new fact about feeding habits in the genus Mycteria.
Members of Mycteria are distinguished by their long legs and down-curved bills.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Ciconiidae - the biological family of storks

Etymology

From New Latin, named based on standard Linnaean taxonomic practice.
