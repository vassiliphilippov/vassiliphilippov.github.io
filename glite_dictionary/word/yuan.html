Yuan

1. PROPN

/ juˈɑːn / (AmE), / jʊˈɑːn / (BrE)
the imperial dynasty of China that ruled from 1279 to 1368, established by the Mongols under Kublai Khan
Chinese dynasty, 1279–1368

Examples

The Yuan dynasty introduced many administrative reforms in China.
Marco Polo traveled to China during the Yuan period and documented his experiences.
Under the rule of the Yuan, paper money became widely used in China.

Etymology

From Mandarin Chinese 元 (Yuán), meaning 'origin' or 'beginning'.
