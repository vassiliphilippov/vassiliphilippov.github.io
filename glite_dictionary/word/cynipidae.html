Cynipidae

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈnɪp.ɪˌdi / (AmE), / sɪˈnɪp.ɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small wasps known as gall wasps, belonging to the order Hymenoptera, many of which cause galls on plants.
family of gall wasps (insects)
medical

Examples

Members of the Cynipidae family are best known for inducing galls on oak trees.
The Cynipidae are of interest to entomologists due to their complex life cycles.
Researchers study Cynipidae to better understand plant-insect interactions.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'kynipos' (a kind of wasp).

2. NOUN

/ sɪˈnɪpəˌdiː / (AmE), / sɪˈnɪpɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small wasps, known as gall wasps, whose larvae induce galls on plants
family of gall wasps

Examples

The Cynipidae are best known for their role in the formation of plant galls.
Many members of the Cynipidae family lay their eggs in oak trees.
Scientists study Cynipidae to understand plant-insect interactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gall wasp - a wasp of the family Cynipidae, whose larvae induce plant galls

Etymology

From New Latin Cynipidae, based on the type genus Cynips.
