deliverable

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈlɪv.ɚ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪv.ər.ə.bəl / (BrE)
suitable to be delivered or ready for delivery, especially in the context of a project or contract.
ready or suitable for delivery

Examples

The software is now deliverable and ready for deployment to the client.
All materials must be deliverable by the agreed deadline.
After several revisions, the final report was considered deliverable to the stakeholders.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undeliverable - not able to be delivered

Etymology

From 'deliver' + '-able', indicating capability or suitability for delivery.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02721174-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈlɪvərəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪvərəbəl / (BrE)
a thing, such as a product or document, that must be supplied or completed as part of a project or agreement
item to be supplied in a project

Examples

The team worked overtime to complete every deliverable before the project deadline.
The contract specifies that all deliverables must meet quality control standards.
Each phase of the project produced its own unique deliverable for client review.

Synset Examples

under this contract the deliverables include both software and hardware

Related Terms

  • Synonym: output - something produced by a process, especially as a result of work or manufacturing

Etymology

From 'deliver' + '-able'; modelled after legal or business jargon.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03178402-n
Synset POS: n
