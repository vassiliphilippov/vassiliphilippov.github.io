hypothalamus

1. NOUN

/ haɪpəˈθæləˌməs / (AmE), / hʌɪpəˈθaləməs / (BrE)
a region at the base of the brain that regulates the autonomic nervous system and various vital functions such as hunger, temperature, and hormone release
part of brain regulating basic bodily functions
medical

Examples

Damage to the hypothalamus can disrupt the body's ability to regulate temperature.
The hypothalamus is responsible for controlling hunger and thirst.
Researchers are studying how the hypothalamus affects sleep patterns.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'hypo-' (under) + 'thalamus' (chamber).
