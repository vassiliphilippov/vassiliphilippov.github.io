Louis IX of France

1. PROPN

/ luːˈiː nʌɪnθ əv fræns / (AmE), / ˌluː.i ˈnʌɪnθ əv frɑ̃ːs / (BrE)
(1214–1270) King of France, known for his piety and justice, who led two Crusades and became a canonized saint.
King of France, led Crusades, saint

Examples

Louis IX of France is remembered for his fairness and deep religious devotion during his reign.
The Seventh and Eighth Crusades were both led by Louis IX of France.
Many churches across Europe are dedicated to Louis IX of France, who was canonized as a saint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saint Louis - another name for Louis IX of France, used in religious and historical contexts
