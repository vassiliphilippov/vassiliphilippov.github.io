fumbling

1. ADJ

/ ˈfʌm.blɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfʌm.blɪŋ / (BrE)
showing a lack of skill or clumsiness in performing a task or action
showing lack of skill or clumsiness

Examples

His fumbling attempt to fix the computer only made things worse.
During the interview, her fumbling responses betrayed her nervousness.
The actor played a fumbling detective who could never seem to solve the case.

Synset Examples

a bungling workman
did a clumsy job
his fumbling attempt to put up a shelf

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clumsy - showing awkwardness or lack of skill
  • Antonym: skillful - showing skill or dexterity

Etymology

Present participle of 'fumble,' which may originate from Norwegian 'fumla' (to grope),
