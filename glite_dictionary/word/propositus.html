propositus

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈpɑzɪtəs / (AmE), / prəʊˈpɒzɪtəs / (BrE)
the person immediately affected by or central to a particular action, event, or case, especially in law or genetics
person chiefly concerned in a case or event
legal medical

Examples

The study began by identifying the propositus in each family lineage.
In the legal dispute, the propositus was the individual whose actions triggered the investigation.
For genetic counseling, information was first collected from the propositus before examining other family members.

Etymology

From Latin propositus (proposed, set forth).
