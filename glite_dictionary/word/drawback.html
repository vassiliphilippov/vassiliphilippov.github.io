drawback

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɔˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈdrɔːˌbæk / (BrE)
a feature or aspect of something that makes it less acceptable or effective; a disadvantage or hindrance
a disadvantage; an undesirable feature

Examples

The main drawback of this solution is its high cost.
Every job has its drawbacks as well as its rewards.
One drawback to living in the city is the constant noise.

Synset Examples

he pointed out all the drawbacks to my plan

Related Terms

  • Antonym: advantage - a condition or circumstance that puts one in a favorable position
  • Antonym: benefit - an advantage or profit gained from something
  • Synonym: disadvantage - a condition or situation that causes problems or makes success harder

Etymology

From Middle English 'drawen' (to draw) + 'back', meaning something that draws one back or hinders forward progress.
