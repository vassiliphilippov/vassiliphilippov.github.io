Metroxylon

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɛtroʊˈzaɪlɒn / (AmE), / ˌmɛtrɒkˈsaɪlɒn / (BrE)
A genus of Malayan pinnate-leaved palm trees that flower and fruit once before dying, including the sago palm.
genus of Malayan palm trees (sago palms)

Examples

The Metroxylon genus is best known for the sago palm, a staple food source in some Asian regions.
After flowering, a Metroxylon palm completes its life cycle and dies.
Many villages rely on Metroxylon plants to harvest edible sago starch.

Etymology

From Greek 'metra' (womb or pith) and 'xylon' (wood), referring to the structure of the trunk.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtroʊˈzaɪlɒn / (AmE), / ˌmɛtrɒkˈsaɪlən / (BrE)
a genus of Malayan palm trees with pinnate leaves that flower and fructify once before dying, including the sago palm.
genus of Malayan palm trees

Examples

The Metroxylon genus includes several economically important palms such as the sago palm.
Researchers found a new species within the Metroxylon genus during their expedition in Borneo.
After Metroxylon palms flower and bear fruit, the entire plant dies, making them monocarpic.

Etymology

From Greek 'metra' (womb, pith) + 'xylon' (wood), referring to the fibrous pith used for sago.
