diminutive

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈmɪn.jə.tɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈmɪn.jʊ.tɪv / (BrE)
A word formed with a suffix or alteration to indicate smallness, endearment, or triviality, such as '-let' in 'booklet'.
word form indicating smallness or affection

Examples

In Russian, the name 'Sasha' is a diminutive of 'Alexander'.
Adding '-ette' at the end of a word often forms a diminutive in English.
The word 'duckling' is a classic example of a diminutive created by the suffix '-ling'.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: augmentative - a word or suffix expressing largeness or intensity

Etymology

From Latin diminutivus (tending to diminish), from de- (down) + minuere (to lessen).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈmɪn.jə.tɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈmɪn.jʊ.tɪv / (BrE)
extremely or unusually small; very tiny.
very small; tiny

Examples

Despite his diminutive size, the dog was incredibly brave.
She admired the diminutive sculpture on the windowsill.
Their diminutive cottage was cozy but beautifully decorated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tiny - very small in size
  • Synonym: petite - small and attractively dainty
  • Antonym: gigantic - extremely large in size

Etymology

From Latin 'dīminūtīvus' (indicating diminution), from 'dīminuere' (to lessen).
