shaken

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃeɪkən / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪkən / (BrE)
feeling shocked, upset, or disturbed, especially as a result of a sudden or unexpected event
emotionally disturbed or shocked

Examples

After witnessing the accident, she was visibly shaken and needed time to recover.
Although he gave a brave speech, he was inwardly shaken by the news.
The sudden noise left everyone in the room feeling shaken and uneasy.

Synset Examples

retrieved his named from her jolted memory
the accident left her badly shaken

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - not affected by disturbance or agitation
  • Synonym: disturbed - emotionally troubled or unsettled

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'shake', ultimately from Old English 'sceacan' (to move quickly, shake).
