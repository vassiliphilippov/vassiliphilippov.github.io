polymastigote

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɒliˈmæstɪˌɡoʊt / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈmæstɪɡəʊt / (BrE)
A type of flagellated protozoan that possesses several flagella.
flagellate with several flagella
medical

Examples

The polymastigote moves rapidly thanks to its many flagella.
Researchers identified the organism as a polymastigote under the microscope.
Some polymastigotes are important in studying protozoan diversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flagellate - a protozoan with one or more whip-like appendages (flagella)

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'mastix' (whip, referring to flagella) + '-ote' (a being or organism).
