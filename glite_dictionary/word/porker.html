porker

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrkər / (AmE), / ˈpɔːkə / (BrE)
A pig that has been fattened for the purpose of providing meat.
a pig fattened for meat

Examples

The farmer sold three porkers to the butcher before the festival.
That prize-winning porker weighed nearly 300 pounds at the county fair.
Modern breeds are selected to produce a high-quality porker for the market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hog - a domesticated pig, especially one over 120 pounds (54 kg) and fattened for market
  • Synonym: sow - an adult female pig, especially one that has farrowed

Etymology

From pork + -er, referring to an animal raised for pork.
