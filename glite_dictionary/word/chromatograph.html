chromatograph

1. NOUN

/ kɹoʊˈmæt̬əˌɡɹæf / (AmE), / krəˈmat.ə.ɡrɑːf / (BrE)
an instrument used for separating and analyzing mixtures in chemical analysis, typically by passing a mixture through a medium in which components move at different rates
instrument for separating chemical mixtures

Examples

The laboratory used a chromatograph to analyze the composition of the unknown sample.
Recent advances in chromatograph technology have improved the accuracy of many chemical tests.
After calibration, the chromatograph produced clearer data for the research team.

Synset Examples

A chromatogram is the visual output of the chromatograph.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chromatography - the process of separating mixtures using a chromatograph

Etymology

From Greek 'chroma' (color) and '-graph' (something written or drawn), related to early color-based separation methods.
