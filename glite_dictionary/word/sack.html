sack

1. NOUN

/ sæk / (AmE), / sæk / (BrE)
the act of looting or destroying a place by an army or mob, often with violence and widespread destruction
looting or destruction of a place by force

Examples

The sack of Rome in 410 AD marked a turning point in European history.
After the sack of the city, few buildings were left standing.
The chronicles describe the sack as especially brutal and destructive.

Synset Examples

Etymology

From Old French *sac* (bag, plunder), from Latin *saccus* (bag), referring to the taking away of goods in bags.
