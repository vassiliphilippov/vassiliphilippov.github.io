fresher

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɛʃər / (AmE), / ˈfrɛʃə / (BrE)
A student in their first year at a university or college, especially in the UK.
first-year university student (UK)
british

Examples

The university hosts a week of activities for all the new freshers.
As a fresher, Sarah was nervous about her first lecture.
The hall was filled with freshers eager to make new friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freshman - a first-year student at a university or college, chiefly in the US

Etymology

Originally formed from 'fresh' + '-er'; associated with 'freshman' in the US, but chiefly British in use.
