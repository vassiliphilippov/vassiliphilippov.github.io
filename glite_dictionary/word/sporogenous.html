sporogenous

1. ADJ

/ spɔˈroʊdʒənəs / (AmE), / spɔːˈrɒdʒənəs / (BrE)
(of a cell or tissue) producing spores or reproducing by the formation of spores.
producing spores (biology)
medical

Examples

Certain sporogenous cells develop into spores in the reproductive cycle of fungi.
The sporogenous layer is essential for spore formation in flowering plants.
Researchers studied the sporogenous tissue to understand how spores are produced.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spore - a reproductive cell capable of developing into a new individual

Etymology

From Greek *spora* (seed, sowing) + *-genous* (producing).
