tradesman

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪdz.mən / (AmE), / ˈtreɪdz.mən / (BrE)
a man who owns or manages a shop, especially one selling goods or services to the public
male shop owner or merchant

Examples

The local tradesman greeted each customer with a warm smile as they entered his shop.
He was a respected tradesman in the town, known for his fairness and honest dealings.
Many generations of tradesmen have run the bakery on the corner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merchant - a person involved in wholesale trade, especially with foreign countries or supplying merchandise to a particular trade
  • Synonym: shopkeeper - a person who owns or manages a small shop
  • Synonym: salesman - a man whose job involves selling products
  • Antonym: customer - a person who buys goods or services

Etymology

From trade + man, first recorded in English in the 17th century.
