receptor

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsɛptər / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛptə / (BrE)
a protein or cellular structure in the body that receives and responds to specific chemical signals, such as hormones or neurotransmitters
cellular structure detecting chemical signals
medical

Examples

The drug binds to a specific receptor on the surface of the cell.
Pain signals are transmitted through nerve cells containing specialized receptors.
Scientists have identified a new receptor involved in regulating blood pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensor - device or structure that detects or measures a physical property

Etymology

From Latin 'receptor', from 'recipere' meaning 'to receive'.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈsɛptər / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛptə / (BrE)
an organ or structure containing nerve endings that respond to specific stimuli and transmit signals to the nervous system
organ or structure responding to stimuli
medical

Examples

Pain is detected when a skin receptor is stimulated by a sharp object.
The human ear acts as a receptor for sound waves, allowing us to hear.
Certain diseases can damage the nerve receptors in the eyes, leading to vision problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sense organ - an organ specialized for receiving certain kinds of stimuli

Etymology

From Latin 'receptor', from 'receptus', past participle of 'recipere' (to receive).
