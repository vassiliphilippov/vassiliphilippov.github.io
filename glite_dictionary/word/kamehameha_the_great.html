Kamehameha the Great

1. PROPN

/ kəˌmeɪhəˈmeɪhə ðə ɡreɪt / (AmE), / kəˌmeɪhəˈmeɪhə ðə ɡreɪt / (BrE)
(c. 1758–1819) The Hawaiian king who united the Hawaiian Islands under a single rule, founding the Kamehameha dynasty.
first king of the unified Hawaiian Islands

Examples

The reign of Kamehameha the Great marked a turning point in Hawaiian history.
Many statues of Kamehameha the Great stand throughout Hawaii in his honor.
Under Kamehameha the Great, the Hawaiian Islands were first unified into a single kingdom.

Etymology

From Hawaiian Kāmehameha (meaning 'the Lonely One'), an honorific name, plus the English epithet 'the Great'.
