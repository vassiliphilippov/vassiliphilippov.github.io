ornateness

1. NOUN

/ ɔːrˈneɪtnəs / (AmE), / ɔːˈneɪtnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being elaborately or excessively decorated
quality of being ornate or highly decorated

Examples

The ornateness of the palace stunned the visitors as they entered the grand hall.
She preferred minimalism over the ornateness that filled her parents' living room.
Some critics argue that the ornateness of the design detracts from its practicality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decorativeness - the quality of being decorative
  • Antonym: plainness - the quality of being simple or lacking decoration

Etymology

From 'ornate' + '-ness', with 'ornate' from Latin 'ornatus' (adorned, decorated).

2. NOUN

/ ɔrˈneɪtnəs / (AmE), / ɔːˈneɪtnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being elaborate and highly decorated, especially in language or style; excessive use of ornamental language
elaborate and decorative quality

Examples

The ornateness of the author's language made the novel difficult to read for some.
Critics often point out the ornateness in his speeches, suggesting he prefers style over substance.
The ornateness of the architecture mirrored the elaborate prose found in period literature.

Synset Examples

the grandiosity of his prose
an excessive ornateness of language

Related Terms

  • Antonym: simplicity - the quality of being simple or plain in style
  • Synonym: grandiosity - impressive or pretentious style or appearance

Etymology

From ornate + -ness, where 'ornate' derives from Latin ornatus (adorned).
