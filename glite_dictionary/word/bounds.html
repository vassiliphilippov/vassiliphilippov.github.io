bounds

1. NOUN

/ baʊndz / (AmE), / baʊndz / (BrE)
the limits or boundaries marking the extent of an area or object
limits or boundaries

Examples

The garden is within the bounds of the old estate.
Scientists are exploring the bounds of the known universe.
You must not go beyond the bounds set by school rules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boundary - a real or imagined line that marks the edge of something
  • Synonym: limit - a point or level beyond which something does not or may not extend or pass
  • Antonym: infinity - the state or quality of being infinite; no limits or boundaries

Etymology

Plural of 'bound', from Middle English 'bounde' (limit), from Old French 'bonde', from Medieval Latin 'bodina'.
