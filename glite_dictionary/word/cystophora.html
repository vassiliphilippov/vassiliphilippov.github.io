Cystophora

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈstɑfərə / (AmE), / sɪˈstɒfəɹə / (BrE)
A genus of seals known as the hooded seals, characterized by a distinctive inflatable nasal sac in adult males, found in the North Atlantic.
genus of hooded seals

Examples

The Cystophora is notable for the males' inflatable nasal sac, which they use during mating displays.
Scientists are studying the migration habits of Cystophora in the North Atlantic Ocean.
A young Cystophora was rescued after becoming stranded on the shore.

Etymology

From Greek 'kystis' (bladder) + 'phoros' (bearing), referring to the male seal's nasal sac.

2. NOUN

/ saɪˈstɒfərə / (AmE), / sɪsˈtɒfərə / (BrE)
A genus of earless seals, including the hooded seal, native to the North Atlantic Ocean.
genus of earless seals

Examples

The Cystophora is best known for including the hooded seal with its distinctive nasal sac.
Scientists studied the Cystophora to understand adaptations to Arctic environments.
The genus Cystophora belongs to the phocid, or earless, seals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hooded seal - an earless seal with a characteristic inflatable nasal sac, belonging to the genus Cystophora
  • Degree Higher: Phocidae - the family of earless seals, which includes the genus Cystophora

Etymology

From Greek 'kystis' (bladder, sac) and 'phoros' (bearing), referring to the hood-like sac of the male hooded seal.
