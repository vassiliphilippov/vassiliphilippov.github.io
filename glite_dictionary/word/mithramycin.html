mithramycin

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪθrəˈmaɪsɪn / (AmE), / ˌmɪθrəˈmaɪsɪn / (BrE)
an antineoplastic drug used to treat certain types of cancer, especially of the testes; also known by the trade name Mithracin
antineoplastic cancer drug
medical

Examples

Mithramycin is prescribed in cases where other cancer treatments have failed.
The oncologist explained that mithramycin might be used for testicular cancer.
Because of potential side effects, mithramycin is administered under close medical supervision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antineoplastic - a drug that prevents, inhibits, or halts the development of a tumor

Etymology

From 'mycin', a common suffix for antibiotics, initially isolated from Streptomyces plicatus.
