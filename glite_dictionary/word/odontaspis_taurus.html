Odontaspis taurus

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊˌdɑnˈtæspɪs ˈtɔrəs / (AmE), / ˌəʊˌdɒnˈtaspɪs ˈtɔːrəs / (BrE)
a species of shallow-water shark with sharp jagged teeth, found in the Atlantic Ocean and sometimes known to be dangerous to swimmers
a type of shallow-water shark

Examples

The Odontaspis taurus can often be seen swimming slowly near coastal shipwrecks.
Divers sometimes spot Odontaspis taurus gliding through underwater caves in the Atlantic.
While usually not aggressive, the Odontaspis taurus has on occasion been involved in incidents with swimmers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sand tiger shark - common name for Odontaspis taurus
  • Degree Higher: shark - large fish with cartilaginous skeleton

Etymology

From Greek *odous* (tooth) + *aspis* (shield); *taurus* is Latin for 'bull'.
