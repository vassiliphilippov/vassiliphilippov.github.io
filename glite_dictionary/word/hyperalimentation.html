hyperalimentation

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpərˌælɪmənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpərˌælɪmɛnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the medical practice of providing all necessary nutrients intravenously, usually through a catheter into a large vein, for patients unable to eat or absorb food normally
intravenous feeding for nutritional support
medical

Examples

The patient was started on hyperalimentation due to severe gastrointestinal failure.
In cases of long-term coma, hyperalimentation can provide essential nutrients.
Doctors decided to use hyperalimentation after burns prevented normal feeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parenteral nutrition - feeding a person intravenously, bypassing the usual process of eating and digestion

Etymology

From Greek 'hyper-' (over, excessive) + Latin 'alimentatio' (nourishment).
