Vanessa

1. NOUN

/ vəˈnɛsə / (AmE), / vəˈnɛsə / (BrE)
A genus of brightly colored butterflies, including species such as the painted lady and red admiral.
a genus of butterflies

Examples

The Vanessa genus includes some of the most widespread and colorful butterflies in the world.
Many gardeners are thrilled to spot a Vanessa butterfly in their yard during spring.
The red admiral, a member of the Vanessa genus, is known for its striking wing patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red admiral - a butterfly species of the Vanessa genus, known for its dark wings with red bands
  • Synonym: painted lady - a butterfly species of the Vanessa genus, noted for its orange and black markings

Etymology

Introduced by Linnaeus; possibly inspired by the female name Vanessa created by author Jonathan Swift.
