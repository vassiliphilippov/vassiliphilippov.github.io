commonage

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.mən.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ən.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
land or property owned collectively by a community or group, often giving members shared rights to use it
property held in common

Examples

Many villages retained the right to graze animals on the commonage after enclosure.
Disputes sometimes arose over the boundaries of the commonage in rural communities.
The tradition of commonage still survives in parts of Ireland and Scotland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commons - land or resources shared by a community

Etymology

From 'common' (shared property) + '-age', a suffix forming nouns.
