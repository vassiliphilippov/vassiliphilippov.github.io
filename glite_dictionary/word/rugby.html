rugby

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌɡ.bi / (AmE), / ˈrʌɡ.bi / (BrE)
A team sport played with an oval ball that may be carried and passed, originating in England and played between two teams of fifteen or thirteen players.
a team sport played with an oval ball

Examples

He played rugby at university and captained the local team.
The rugby match drew thousands of enthusiastic fans to the stadium.
Many schools in England include rugby as part of their sports curriculum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rugby football - another term for rugby
  • Antonym: American football - a different form of football played mainly in the United States
  • Antonym: soccer - a form of football played chiefly with the feet

Etymology

Named after Rugby School in England, where the sport originated in the early 19th century.
