xenogeneic

1. ADJ

/ ˌzɛnəˈdʒiːniɪk / (AmE), / ˌzɛnəʊˈdʒiːniɪk / (BrE)
denoting or relating to cells or tissues originating from individuals of different species; used especially in the context of transplantation or grafts.
from individuals of different species (biological)
medical

Examples

Transplanting a xenogeneic heart valve carries a higher risk of immune rejection than human donor tissue.
Researchers studied the effects of xenogeneic stem cells in treating neurological disorders.
The surgeon explained the challenges of using xenogeneic grafts for organ repair.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: allogeneic - from genetically different individuals of the same species
  • Antonym: syngeneic - from genetically identical individuals of the same species

Etymology

From Greek 'xenos' (foreign) + 'genos' (race, kind) + English suffix '-ic'.
