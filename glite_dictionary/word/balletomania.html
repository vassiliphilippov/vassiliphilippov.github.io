balletomania

1. NOUN

/ ˌbæl.ə.toʊˈmeɪ.ni.ə / (AmE), / ˌbæl.ə.təʊˈmeɪ.ni.ə / (BrE)
Extreme or passionate enthusiasm for ballet as an art form.
passionate enthusiasm for ballet

Examples

During the 1920s, balletomania swept through Paris, drawing crowds to every performance.
Her balletomania led her to attend every major production in the country.
Some critics attribute the growth of dance festivals to a resurgence of balletomania.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balletomane - a person who is an ardent admirer of ballet

Etymology

From French 'balletomane' (ballet enthusiast), itself from Italian 'balletto' (ballet) + '-mania' (madness, obsession).
