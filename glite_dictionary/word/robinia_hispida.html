Robinia hispida

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈbɪniə hɪˈspɪdə / (AmE), / rəʊˈbɪnɪə hɪˈspɪdə / (BrE)
A large shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States, known for its bristly stems and large clusters of pink flowers.
a pink-flowered shrub or small tree of eastern US

Examples

The garden features a Robinia hispida with stunning clusters of pink blossoms each spring.
They planted Robinia hispida along the driveway for its ornamental appeal.
The bristly stems of Robinia hispida make it easily distinguishable from other shrubs.

Etymology

From the genus name Robinia (named after French botanist Jean Robin) and Latin 'hispida' meaning rough or bristly.
