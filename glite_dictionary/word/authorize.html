to authorize

1. VERB

/ ˈɔːθəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈɔːθəraɪz / (BrE)
to give official permission for something or to grant someone the power to do something
give official permission or power

Examples

The manager authorized the purchase of new office equipment.
You must be authorized before you can access those documents.
The board is authorizing an expansion of the project next year.

Synset Examples

She authorized her assistant to sign the papers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permit - to give permission for something to happen
  • Antonym: to forbid - to order someone not to do something; to refuse to allow

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'auctorizare', from Latin 'auctor' (author, creator).

WordNet ID: oewn-02479404-v

2. VERB

/ ˈɔːθəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈɔːθəraɪz / (BrE)
to give official permission for something to happen or for someone to do something
give official permission or approval

Examples

The manager must authorize all payments before they are processed.
Congress failed to authorize new spending for the program this year.
She refused to authorize the release of confidential information.

Synset Examples

Clear the manuscript for publication
The rock star never authorized this slanderous biography

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permit - to allow someone to do something, especially officially
  • Antonym: to forbid - to order somebody not to do something; to refuse to allow something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'auctorizare', from Latin 'auctor' (author, originator).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00804987-v
Synset POS: v
