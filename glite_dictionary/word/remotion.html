remotion

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈməʊʃən / (BrE)
the act of removing or taking something away; removal
the act of removing
archaic

Examples

The remotion of outdated policies improved the company's efficiency.
In ancient philosophy, knowledge was sometimes defined by the remotion of ignorance.
Legal documents occasionally use the term remotion to refer to the removal of an official from office.

Synset Examples

he had surgery for the removal of a malignancy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: removal - the act of taking something away or putting an end to something
  • Antonym: addition - the act of adding something

Etymology

From Latin 'remotio' (a removal), from 'removere' (to remove).
