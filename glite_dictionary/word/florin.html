florin

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɔrɪn / (AmE), / ˈflɒrɪn / (BrE)
the former basic monetary unit of the Netherlands, equal to 100 cents, replaced by the euro in 2002
former Dutch currency unit

Examples

The Netherlands replaced the florin with the euro in 2002.
Travelers needed to exchange their dollars for florins when visiting Amsterdam in the 1990s.
My grandfather still keeps a collection of old Dutch florin coins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guilder - the basic unit of money in the Netherlands before the euro

Etymology

From Old French 'florin', from Italian 'fiorino', from 'fiore' (flower), named after the lily on the original Florentine coin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈflɔrɪn / (AmE), / ˈflɒrɪn / (BrE)
the basic monetary unit of Suriname, equal to 100 cents
basic unit of currency in Suriname

Examples

Before independence, Suriname used the florin as its official currency.
He exchanged his dollars for Surinamese florins at the airport.
Each florin is divided into 100 cents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guilder - another name for Suriname's currency, also used elsewhere
  • Degree Lower: cent - a subunit of currency, one hundredth of a florin

Etymology

From Italian 'fiorino', named after the city of Florence.
