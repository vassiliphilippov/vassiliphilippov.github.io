termer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːmər / (AmE), / ˈtɜːmə / (BrE)
a person who serves for a specified term in a position, especially in government or office
person serving a specified term

Examples

She is the only third termer on the committee, bringing valuable experience.
Not many fourth termers have served in the legislature for so long.
The senator became a fifth termer after winning the recent election.

Synset Examples

there are not many fourth termers in the Senate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incumbent - the current holder of an office or position
