allomorphic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæləˈmɔːrfɪk / (AmE), / ˌal.əˈmɔː.fɪk / (BrE)
relating to or denoting allomorphs; characterized by variation in form within a morpheme or chemical compound
relating to allomorphs or variation in form

Examples

The plural endings in English are a classic example of allomorphic variation.
Researchers studied the allomorphic changes that occur in compound words.
The allomorphic patterns help explain irregular verb forms in English.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allomorph - any of the variant forms of a morpheme or chemical form

Etymology

From 'allomorph' (variant form) and the suffix '-ic', ultimately from Greek 'allos' (other) and 'morphē' (form).
