learning

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɝːnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɜːnɪŋ / (BrE)
deep and extensive knowledge acquired through study or scholarship
profound scholarly knowledge

Examples

The professor was admired for his remarkable learning in ancient history.
Her learning was evident in the depth of her lectures.
Many sought his advice, trusting in his vast learning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erudition - extensive knowledge acquired through study
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or information

Etymology

From Old English 'leornung' (the action of learning); related to 'learn'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɝː.nɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɜː.nɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of acquiring knowledge or skill through study, experience, or teaching
process of acquiring knowledge or skill

Examples

Active learning is encouraged in modern classrooms.
She believes that life is a continuous process of learning.
His rapid learning of new languages impressed everyone.

Synset Examples

the child's acquisition of language

Related Terms

  • Synonym: education - the process of receiving or giving systematic instruction
  • Synonym: teaching - the act of imparting knowledge or skill
  • Antonym: forgetting - the process of losing knowledge or information

Etymology

From Old English 'leornian' (to learn).
