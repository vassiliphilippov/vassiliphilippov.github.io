fieriness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪəri.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəri.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being like fire, especially having heat or a brilliant color
quality of being like fire

Examples

The fieriness of the sunset turned the sky brilliant orange and red.
You could sense the fieriness of the bonfire from across the field.
Her cheeks had the fieriness of embers after dancing in the cold night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiery - having the qualities of fire; intensely hot or bright

Etymology

From 'fiery' + '-ness', with 'fiery' derived from Middle English 'firy', from 'fire'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪəri.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə.ri.nəs / (BrE)
A quality of being passionate, intense, or quick-tempered.
passionate or quick-tempered nature

Examples

The fieriness in his speech inspired everyone in the audience.
Despite her fieriness, she remained logical under pressure.
His fieriness sometimes led to heated arguments with his colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passion - strong and barely controllable emotion
  • Antonym: calmness - the quality of being peaceful and free from agitation

Etymology

From 'fiery' + '-ness', ultimately from Old English 'fyr' (fire).
