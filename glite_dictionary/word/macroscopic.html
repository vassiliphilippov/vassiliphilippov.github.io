macroscopic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmæk.rəˈskɑː.pɪk / (AmE), / ˌmæk.rəˈskɒp.ɪk / (BrE)
large enough to be seen without a microscope; visible to the naked eye.
visible without a microscope

Examples

At this distance, the details are too fine to be macroscopic.
The geologist collected several macroscopic samples for analysis.
A macroscopic organism is one that can be seen without a microscope.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: microscopic - so small as to be visible only with a microscope

Etymology

From Greek makros (large) + skopos (seeing).

2. ADJ

/ ˌmæk.rəˈskɑː.pɪk / (AmE), / ˌmak.rəˈskɒp.ɪk / (BrE)
large enough to be visible to the naked eye; not requiring a microscope to be seen.
visible without a microscope

Examples

Bacteria are usually studied under a microscope because they are not macroscopic.
The macroscopic features of the rock can be observed without any special equipment.
Unlike macroscopic organisms, viruses cannot be seen with the naked eye.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: microscopic - too small to be seen by the naked eye; needing a microscope

Etymology

From French 'macroscopique', based on Greek 'makros' (large) + 'skopein' (to look).
