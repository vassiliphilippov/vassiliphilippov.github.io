backward

1. ADV

/ ˈbæk.wɚd / (AmE), / ˈbæk.wəd / (BrE)
towards a time in the past; with reference to earlier times or events
toward a past time

Examples

He glanced backward to remember how far he had come.
The historian looked backward to previous centuries for inspiration.
Sometimes it's good to reflect backward on your experiences before making a decision.

Synset Examples

set the clocks back an hour
never look back
lovers of the past looking fondly backward

Related Terms

  • Antonym: forward - towards a future time

Etymology

From Middle English 'bacward', from 'back' + 'ward', denoting direction toward the rear or the past.

3. ADJ

/ ˈbækwərd / (AmE), / ˈbækwəd / (BrE)
having made less progress or development than is normal or expected, especially in economic, social, or educational terms
less developed or advanced

Examples

Many rural areas remain backward due to a lack of investment.
The reforms aim to modernize backward sectors of the economy.
Educational opportunities are limited in backward regions.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: advanced - highly developed or further along in progress

Etymology

From Old English 'bæcweard' (with the back turned, behind).

6. ADV

/ ˈbæk.wɚd / (AmE), / ˈbæk.wəd / (BrE)
In the reverse direction, order, or manner from what is usual or expected.
in the reverse direction or order

Examples

You are wearing your shirt backward again.
She wrote the letters of his name backward by mistake.
He counted from ten backward to one before launching the rocket.

Synset Examples

the child put her jersey on backward

Related Terms

  • Antonym: forwards - in the direction that one is facing or moving; toward the front
  • Synonym: reverse - moving, happening, or done in the opposite direction

Etymology

From Middle English 'bakward', from Old English 'bæcweard', meaning 'with the back foremost'.
