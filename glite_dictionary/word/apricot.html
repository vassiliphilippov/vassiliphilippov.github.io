apricot

1. NOUN

/ ˈæp.rɪ.kɑt / (AmE), / ˈæp.rɪ.kɒt / (BrE)
A light shade of pink with a yellow tint, resembling the color of ripe apricots.
a pinkish-yellow color

Examples

The bedroom walls were painted a soft apricot to create a warm atmosphere.
She chose an apricot dress for the spring wedding.
The sunset cast an apricot glow over the city skyline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peach - a pale pinkish-orange color

Etymology

From the fruit name 'apricot', which comes from Arabic 'al-barqūq' and Latin 'praecoquum' (early-ripening).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæp.rɪ.kɑt, ˈeɪ.prɪ.kɑt / (AmE), / ˈæp.rɪ.kɒt, ˈeɪ.prɪ.kɒt / (BrE)
A small Asian tree of the rose family that bears clusters of usually white blossoms and produces edible fruit resembling the peach; also, the fruit of this tree.
tree bearing peach-like edible fruit

Examples

The apricot tree in our backyard is covered in delicate white blossoms every spring.
Farmers harvested baskets of ripe apricots to sell at the market.
She planted an apricot sapling, hoping it would one day provide shade and fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peach - a similar edible fruit from a related tree

Etymology

From Arabic al-birquq via Spanish albaricoque and French abricot.

3. NOUN

/ ˈæp.rɪˌkɑt, ˈeɪ.prɪˌkɑt / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.prɪ.kɒt / (BrE)
A small, round fruit with soft, downy skin and yellow to reddish color, resembling a small peach, with a sweet, slightly tart flavor.
a small, peach-like yellowish fruit

Examples

She bought a basket of fresh apricots from the market.
The tart was topped with juicy slices of apricot and a dusting of sugar.
I love the sweet flavor of dried apricot in my morning cereal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peach - a round, soft, juicy fruit with fuzzy skin and a large seed

Etymology

From Arabic al-birquq, via Spanish albaricoque and French abricot.
