Myrica pensylvanica

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈɹɪkə ˌpɛnsəlˈveɪnɪkə / (AmE), / mɪˈrɪkə ˌpɛnsɪlˈveɪnɪkə / (BrE)
A deciduous, aromatic shrub native to eastern North America, noted for its gray-green, wax-coated berries.
deciduous aromatic shrub of eastern North America

Examples

Myrica pensylvanica is often used in landscaping for its attractive foliage and berries.
Birds are known to feed on the wax-coated berries of Myrica pensylvanica during winter.
Gardeners appreciate Myrica pensylvanica for its hardiness and low maintenance needs.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Myrica' is the genus name, and 'pensylvanica' refers to Pennsylvania, where the plant is found.
