catastrophe

1. NOUN

/ kəˈtæstrəfi / (AmE), / kəˈtæstrəfi / (BrE)
A sudden and violent event causing significant change to the earth's surface, such as an earthquake, landslide, or volcanic eruption.
sudden violent change in the earth's surface

Examples

A powerful earthquake can create a catastrophe, reshaping entire landscapes in moments.
The volcanic eruption was a geological catastrophe that covered the area in ash and lava.
After the catastrophe, mountain villages were left isolated by landslides and broken roads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disaster - a sudden event causing great damage or loss of life

Etymology

From Greek katastrofē (overturning, sudden end), from kata- (down) + strephein (to turn).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈtæstrəfi / (AmE), / kəˈtæstrəfi / (BrE)
An event causing sudden and great damage, suffering, or destruction.
a sudden disaster or calamity

Examples

The oil spill was an environmental catastrophe that affected miles of coastline.
Millions lost their homes in the catastrophe caused by the hurricane.
If the dam fails, the resulting flood would be a true catastrophe for the region.

Synset Examples

the whole city was affected by the irremediable calamity
the earthquake was a disaster

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disaster - a sudden event causing serious damage or loss
  • Synonym: calamity - an event causing distress or misfortune
  • Synonym: tragedy - a very sad event that causes great suffering

Etymology

From Greek katastrophē (an overturning, a disaster), from kata- (down) + strophē (turning).

3. NOUN

/ kəˈtæstrəfi / (AmE), / kəˈtɑːstrəfi / (BrE)
an event causing great and usually sudden damage or suffering; a disaster or a state of extreme ruin and misfortune
a disastrous event or state of ruin

Examples

The oil spill was a major catastrophe for the coastal ecosystem.
Losing her job right before the holidays felt like a personal catastrophe.
The earthquake turned the city into a scene of utter catastrophe.

Synset Examples

lack of funds has resulted in a catastrophe for our school system
his policies were a disaster

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disaster - a sudden event causing great damage or loss
  • Synonym: calamity - an event causing great and often sudden damage or distress

Etymology

From Greek katastrophē (an overturning, a sudden end), from kata- (down) + strephein (to turn).
