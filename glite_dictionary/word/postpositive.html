postpositive

1. ADJ

/ poʊstˈpɑːzətɪv / (AmE), / pəʊstˈpɒzətɪv / (BrE)
(of a modifier) placed after the word it modifies, especially following the noun.
following the word it modifies

Examples

In the phrase 'attorney general', the adjective is postpositive because it follows the noun.
French frequently uses postpositive adjectives, placing them after the nouns they modify.
Some English titles, such as 'heir apparent', contain postpositive elements.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prepositive - (of a modifier) placed before the word it modifies

Etymology

From Latin postpositivus, from post- (after) + ponere (to place).
