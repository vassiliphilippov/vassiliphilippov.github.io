to secede

1. VERB

/ sɪˈsid / (AmE), / sɪˈsiːd / (BrE)
to formally withdraw from membership in an organization, political union, or alliance
formally withdraw from an organization or union

Examples

Several southern states voted to secede from the Union in the nineteenth century.
If the province wishes to secede, it must follow a complex legal process.
The organization faced a crisis when a key member threatened to secede over policy disagreements.

Synset Examples

After the break up of the Soviet Union, many republics broke away

Related Terms

  • Antonym: join - to become a member of a group or organization

Etymology

From Latin 'secedere', meaning 'to go apart' or 'to withdraw'.
