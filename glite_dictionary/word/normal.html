normal

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɔrməl / (AmE), / ˈnɔːməl / (BrE)
(of a process or phenomenon) conforming to scientific laws; not supernatural or anomalous.
conforming to scientific laws

Examples

The scientist confirmed that the reaction was normal and not the result of an unknown force.
According to physics, the object’s movement is considered normal under these conditions.
They were relieved to discover that the readings were completely normal, following established scientific principles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: natural - existing in or caused by nature; not made or caused by humans
  • Antonym: supernatural - attributed to some force beyond scientific understanding or the laws of nature

Etymology

From Latin normalis (made according to a carpenter's square), from norma (carpenter's square, rule).

3. ADJ

/ ˈnɔr.məl / (AmE), / ˈnɔː.məl / (BrE)
(of a line, plane, or surface) forming a right angle to a given line, plane, or surface; perpendicular.
at right angles; perpendicular

Examples

In geometry, a line drawn normal to a surface is perpendicular to that surface at the point of contact.
The technician aligned the laser beam normal to the glass plate for maximum transmission.
The force acts normal to the wall, minimizing any tangential effect.
