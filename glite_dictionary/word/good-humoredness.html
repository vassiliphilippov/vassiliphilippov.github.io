good-humoredness

1. NOUN

/ ɡʊd ˈhjuːmərdnəs / (AmE), / ɡʊd ˈhjuːmədnəs / (BrE)
a cheerful and pleasant disposition, especially marked by a ready willingness to be agreeable or accommodating
cheerful, obliging disposition

Examples

Her good-humoredness made her a favorite among her colleagues.
Despite the delays, his good-humoredness never seemed to waver.
I admire your good-humoredness when dealing with difficult customers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amiability - the quality of being friendly and pleasant
  • Antonym: ill-humor - a state of being in a bad temper

Etymology

Formed from 'good-humored' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality.
