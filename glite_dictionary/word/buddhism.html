Buddhism

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːdɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈbʊdɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A religion and philosophy based on the teachings of the Buddha, emphasizing the path to enlightenment through ethical conduct, meditation, and wisdom.
a religion based on the teachings of the Buddha

Examples

Many people around the world practice Buddhism as both a spiritual and philosophical path.
He began to study Buddhism after traveling through Southeast Asia.
Buddhism teaches that suffering can end through the cessation of desire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Buddhist - a follower of Buddhism or relating to Buddhism

Etymology

From Sanskrit *buddha* (enlightened one) + -ism.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbuːdɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈbʊdɪzəm / (BrE)
A major world religion, originating in India and based on the teachings of Buddha, with a focus on spiritual development and the attainment of enlightenment.
religion based on the teachings of Buddha

Examples

Many people around the world practice Buddhism as a way to find inner peace and understanding.
She became interested in Buddhism after reading about meditation and mindfulness.
Buddhism teaches compassion for all living things and the pursuit of enlightenment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Buddhist - a follower of Buddhism
  • Antonym: Christianity - the religion based on the teachings of Jesus Christ

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Buddha' (the Enlightened One) + English '-ism'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08115674-n
Synset POS: n
