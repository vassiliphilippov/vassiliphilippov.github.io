Stomatopoda

1. NOUN

/ stoʊˌmætəˈpoʊdə / (AmE), / stəʊˌmætəˈpəʊdə / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of marine crustaceans commonly known as mantis shrimps, noted for their specialized predatory appendages.
order of marine crustaceans (mantis shrimps)

Examples

The Stomatopoda are renowned for their powerful raptorial limbs, which they use to capture prey.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Stomatopoda order along the coral reef.
Unlike most crustaceans, Stomatopoda possess highly complex eyes with exceptional color vision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mantis shrimp - common name for members of Stomatopoda

Etymology

From Greek *stoma* (mouth) + *pous, podos* (foot).

2. NOUN

/ stoʊˌmætəˈpoʊdə / (AmE), / stəʊˌmætəˈpəʊdə / (BrE)
an order of marine crustaceans known as mantis shrimps, characterized by their raptorial appendages and complex eyes
order of marine mantis shrimps

Examples

Scientists have long studied the unique vision of Stomatopoda to learn about color perception.
Members of the Stomatopoda are sometimes called mantis shrimps because of their predatory claws.
A new species was recently discovered within the Stomatopoda in the Indian Ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mantis shrimp - marine crustacean known for powerful claws

Etymology

From Greek stomato- (mouth) + poda (foot), referring to their mouthparts and limb structure.
