traceable

1. ADJ

/ ˈtreɪsəbəl / (AmE), / ˈtreɪsəbl / (BrE)
able to be discovered or identified as originating from a particular source or cause, especially when followed by 'to'
able to be traced to a source or cause

Examples

The error was traceable to a simple miscalculation in the data.
Many health issues are traceable to environmental factors and lifestyle choices.
His accent was traceable to his years spent living in Australia.

Synset Examples

a failure traceable to lack of energy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attributable - able to be ascribed or credited to a particular source

Etymology

From trace + -able, meaning 'able to be traced.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00173569-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈtreɪsəbəl / (AmE), / ˈtreɪsəbl / (BrE)
capable of being found, discovered, or followed by examining evidence or marks left by something
able to be found or followed

Examples

All phone calls made from the hotel were easily traceable by police.
The origins of this rumor are barely traceable after so many years have passed.
They chose to donate anonymously to avoid their gift being traceable to them.

Synset Examples

a traceable riverbed
the traceable course of an ancient wall

Related Terms

  • Antonym: untraceable - unable to be traced or tracked

Etymology

From 'trace' + '-able', from Old French 'tracier' (to track) and Latin '-abilis' (able to be).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02462377-a
Synset POS: a
