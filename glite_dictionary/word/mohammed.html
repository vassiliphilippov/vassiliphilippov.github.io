Mohammed

1. PROPN

/ moʊˈhæməd / (AmE), / məˈhæmɪd / (BrE)
The Arab prophet and founder of Islam, regarded by Muslims as the last messenger of Allah (570–632 CE).
founder of Islam; Arab prophet (570–632)
american british

Examples

Muslims around the world honor Mohammed as the final prophet of Islam.
The teachings of Mohammed have had a profound influence on global history.
Many historical texts detail the life of Mohammed and the early spread of Islam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Muhammad - alternative spelling of the prophet's name

Etymology

From Arabic مُحَمَّد‎ (Muḥammad), meaning 'praised' or 'commendable'.
