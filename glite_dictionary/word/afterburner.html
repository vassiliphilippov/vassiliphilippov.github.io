afterburner

1. NOUN

/ ˈæftɚˌbɝːnɚ / (AmE), / ˈɑːftəˌbɜːnə / (BrE)
a device fitted to a jet engine, injecting fuel into the hot exhaust to provide additional thrust
jet engine device for extra thrust

Examples

The fighter jet engaged its afterburner to accelerate rapidly during takeoff.
Maintenance on the afterburner is crucial for supersonic aircraft performance.
When the afterburner ignited, flames shot from the rear of the engine.

Etymology

From 'after' + 'burner', indicating its function as a burner used after the main combustion.
