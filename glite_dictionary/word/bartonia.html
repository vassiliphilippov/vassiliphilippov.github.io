bartonia

1. NOUN

/ bɑːrˈtoʊniə / (AmE), / bɑːˈtəʊniə / (BrE)
An annual plant, especially grown for its fragrant golden flowers that bloom at night.
fragrant nocturnal-flowering annual plant

Examples

The bartonia releases its sweet fragrance as dusk falls.
Gardeners value bartonia for its showy golden flowers that open at night.
I planted several bartonias around the patio to enjoy their evening scent.

Etymology

Named after American botanist Benjamin Smith Barton (1766–1815).
