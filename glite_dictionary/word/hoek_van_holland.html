Hoek van Holland

1. PROPN

/ huk vɑn ˈhɑl.ənd / (AmE), / huk vɑn ˈhɒl.ənd / (BrE)
a cape on the southwestern coast of the Netherlands, near Rotterdam, known as the Hook of Holland
cape near Rotterdam, Netherlands

Examples

Many ferries depart from Hoek van Holland to the United Kingdom every day.
We spent the afternoon walking along the beach at Hoek van Holland.
During World War II, Hoek van Holland was an important strategic location.

Etymology

Dutch for 'Corner of Holland'.
