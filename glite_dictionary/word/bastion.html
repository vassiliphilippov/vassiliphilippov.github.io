bastion

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæstʃən / (AmE), / ˈbæstɪən / (BrE)
A projecting part of a fortification or rampart, typically with two faces forming an outward angle, allowing defensive fire in several directions.
projecting part of a fortification

Examples

The soldiers defended the bastion until reinforcements arrived.
A crumbling stone bastion overlooked the ancient riverbed.
From the top of the bastion, the guards surveyed the surrounding countryside.

Etymology

From French 'bastion', from Italian 'bastione', augmentative of 'bastia' (fortification).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæstʃən / (AmE), / ˈbæstɪən / (BrE)
A strongly fortified place or projection in a fortification, often used as a place of shelter during a battle.
strongly fortified defensive position

Examples

The soldiers retreated to the bastion to withstand the enemy's final assault.
Ancient cities often featured at least one bastion to protect their inhabitants.
During the siege, the refugees took shelter in the main bastion of the castle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stronghold - a place that has been fortified to protect against attack
  • Synonym: citadel - a fortress, typically on high ground, protecting or dominating a city

Etymology

From French 'bastion', from Italian 'bastione', related to 'bastire' (to build).

3. NOUN

/ ˈbæstʃən / (AmE), / ˈbæstɪən / (BrE)
an organization or group strongly defending or upholding particular principles, attitudes, or activities
defender of a principle or cause

Examples

The newspaper became a bastion of free speech during the political crisis.
This organization remains the last bastion of traditional values in the region.
For decades, the school was regarded as a bastion of academic excellence.

Synset Examples

a bastion against corruption
the last bastion of communism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stronghold - a place or group that is strongly defended or upholds something
  • Synonym: bulwark - a strong support or protection for something

Etymology

From Old French *bastille* (fortress), ultimately from Provençal *bastir* (to build).
