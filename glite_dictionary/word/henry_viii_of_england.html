Henry VIII of England

1. PROPN

/ ˌhɛnri ðə ˈeɪtθ əv ˈɪŋɡlənd / (AmE), / ˌhɛnrɪ ði ˈeɪtθ əv ˈɪŋɡlənd / (BrE)
(1491–1547) King of England from 1509 to 1547, known for his six marriages and for breaking with the Catholic Church, a move that led to the English Reformation.
King of England, 1509–1547; broke with Catholic Church

Examples

Many historians consider Henry VIII of England one of the most influential monarchs in British history.
The break with Rome began when Henry VIII of England sought to annul his marriage to Catherine of Aragon.
Several famous portraits depict Henry VIII of England in his later years as a powerful and imposing figure.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Elizabeth I - Queen of England, daughter of Henry VIII
  • Synonym: Tudor - Royal dynasty to which Henry VIII belonged
