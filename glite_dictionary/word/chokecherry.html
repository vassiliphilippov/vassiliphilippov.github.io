chokecherry

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃoʊkˌtʃɛri / (AmE), / ˈtʃəʊkˌtʃɛri / (BrE)
A North American wild cherry tree (Prunus virginiana) bearing clusters of small, bitter, dark red to black berries.
a wild North American cherry tree

Examples

The chokecherry bushes in the woods were heavy with dark, tart fruit this summer.
Birds flock to feed on chokecherry berries as soon as they ripen.
Early settlers used chokecherry fruit to make jams and jellies despite its bitterness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild cherry - any of several species of cherry that grow wild, not cultivated

Etymology

From 'choke', referring to the astringent taste, and 'cherry'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈt͡ʃoʊkˌt͡ʃɛri / (AmE), / ˈtʃəʊkˌtʃɛri / (BrE)
The small, sour, dark red or black fruit of the North American chokecherry tree, often used in jams, jellies, and preserves.
small, sour berry from the chokecherry tree

Examples

The jam was made from wild chokecherry collected in the northern woods.
Birds love to feast on ripe chokecherry in late summer.
He found a bush heavy with chokecherry, ready to be picked for jelly.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cherry - small, round, red or black fruit with a pit
  • Synonym: black cherry - the fruit of the black cherry tree, similar but less astringent

Etymology

Formed from 'choke' + 'cherry' due to its astringent taste.
