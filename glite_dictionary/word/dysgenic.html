dysgenic

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / dɪsˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
relating to or causing a decrease in the genetic quality of a population, especially by encouraging the reproduction of people with traits considered undesirable.
causing genetic degeneration

Examples

Some scientists warned that certain social policies might have a dysgenic effect on future generations.
The term dysgenic was used in early 20th-century debates about genetics and population health.
Selective breeding could be either dysgenic or eugenic, depending on which traits are encouraged.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: eugenic - relating to or causing the improvement of a population's genetic qualities

Etymology

From Greek dys- (bad) + -genic (producing or caused by).
