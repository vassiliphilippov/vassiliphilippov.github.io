aorist

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪərɪst / (AmE), / ˈeɪərɪst / (BrE)
a verb tense in some languages, such as classical Greek and Sanskrit, which expresses an action, particularly in the past, without indicating whether it was completed or ongoing
verb tense in some languages, esp. classical Greek

Examples

In ancient Greek, the aorist tense is often used to narrate simple past events.
Most modern European languages do not have an aorist tense.
Understanding the aorist is crucial for translating classical texts accurately.

Etymology

From Greek aoristos, meaning 'indefinite' or 'undefined'.
