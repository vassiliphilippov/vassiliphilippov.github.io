bullheadedness

1. NOUN

/ ˌbʊlˈhɛdɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˌbʊlˈhɛdɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of refusing to change your ideas or plans even when it is unreasonable
quality of being stubborn and obstinate

Examples

His bullheadedness often gets him into unnecessary arguments at work.
She admired his determination but worried that his bullheadedness would cause problems.
Despite their advice, his bullheadedness made him stick to his original plan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stubbornness - the quality of being determined not to change one's mind or attitude
  • Synonym: obstinacy - the quality of being stubborn or difficult to persuade

Etymology

Formed from 'bullheaded' (obstinately stubborn) + -ness (noun-forming suffix).
