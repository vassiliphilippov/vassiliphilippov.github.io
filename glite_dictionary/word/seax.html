seax

1. NOUN

/ seɪ.æks / (AmE), / ˈsiː.aks / (BrE)
a single-edged sword or large dagger used by Germanic peoples during the Migration period and Early Middle Ages, especially by the Saxons
Germanic single-edged sword or dagger

Examples

The warrior carried a seax at his side as part of his everyday arms.
Archaeologists discovered a decorated seax in the burial mound, indicating the status of its owner.
The distinctive shape of the seax blade made it both a weapon and a useful tool.

Synset Examples

Initially, seaxes were found in combination with double-edged swords and were probably intended as side arm.

Etymology

From Old English 'seax', related to Old Saxon and Old High German 'sahs', meaning 'knife' or 'sword'.
