Claviceps purpurea

1. NOUN

/ kləˈvaɪsəps pɜːrˈpjʊriə / (AmE), / kləˈvaɪsɛps pɜːˈpjʊəriə / (BrE)
a species of fungus that infects cereal plants such as rye, replacing grains with dark masses (ergots) and producing compounds used in medicine and chemistry, including lysergic acid
fungus infecting cereals, source of ergots and lysergic acid

Examples

The harvest was ruined by an outbreak of Claviceps purpurea in the rye fields.
Scientists extract lysergic acid from Claviceps purpurea for research purposes.
The presence of Claviceps purpurea in cereals can be dangerous to both humans and animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ergot - the dark, compact mass formed by Claviceps purpurea on cereal plants

Etymology

From Latin *clava* (club) + *ceps* (head), referring to the club-shaped sclerotia; *purpurea* meaning purple, from Latin.
