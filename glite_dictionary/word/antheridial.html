antheridial

1. ADJ

/ ˌænθəˈrɪdiəl / (AmE), / ˌanθəˈrɪdɪəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of an antheridium, a male reproductive structure in certain plants, algae, or fungi.
relating to an antheridium
medical

Examples

The fern's antheridial cells are responsible for producing sperm.
Certain mosses exhibit prominent antheridial clusters during reproduction.
Researchers noted differences in antheridial development among species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antheridium - the male reproductive organ in certain plants, algae, and fungi

Etymology

From New Latin 'antheridium' (male organ), plus '-al' (adjectival suffix).
