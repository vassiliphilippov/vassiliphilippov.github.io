pun

1. NOUN

/ pʌn / (AmE), / pʌn / (BrE)
a joke exploiting the different possible meanings of a word or the fact that there are words that sound alike but have different meanings
humorous play on words

Examples

He ended his speech with a clever pun that made everyone laugh.
The headline relied on a pun for its humor and impact.
She enjoys telling jokes that have an unexpected pun in the punchline.

Synset Examples

I do it for the pun of it
his constant punning irritated her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wordplay - the witty exploitation of the meanings and ambiguities of words

Etymology

Probably from Italian 'puntiglio' (quibble, equivocation).

2. VERB

/ pʌn / (AmE), / pʌn / (BrE)
to make a joke by exploiting different possible meanings of a word or words that sound alike
make a play on words

Examples

He loved to pun during English class, much to his teacher's delight.
It's not easy to pun in every language, as wordplay often relies on sound and meaning.
She punned cleverly about bread, calling herself the 'loaf' of the party.

Synset Examples

Japanese like to pun — their language is well suited to punning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pun - a joke exploiting different possible meanings of a word or words that sound alike (noun)

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; possibly from earlier 'pun' (noun).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
