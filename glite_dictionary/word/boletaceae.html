Boletaceae

1. NOUN

/ boʊlɪˈteɪsiˌi / (AmE), / bəʊlɪˈteɪsiːiː / (BrE)
a family of fleshy fungi distinguished by pores on the underside of the cap, which often separate easily from the tissue
family of fleshy fungi

Examples

The Boletaceae family includes many mushrooms that are prized by foragers for their flavor.
Scientists studied the genetic diversity within the Boletaceae to better understand fungal evolution.
Members of the Boletaceae typically have spongy pores instead of gills under their caps.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Boletus and the standard botanical suffix -aceae (denoting a family).

2. NOUN

/ boʊˌlɛtəˈsiː / (AmE), / bəʊˌlɛtəˈsiː / (BrE)
A family of fungi with fleshy fruiting bodies, where the pores on the underside of the cap are easily detachable.
a family of fleshy fungi

Examples

The Boletaceae family includes many edible mushrooms found in forests worldwide.
Identifying members of the Boletaceae often involves examining their spongy pore surfaces.
Some species within the Boletaceae can be mistaken for poisonous fungi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fungus - a group of spore-producing organisms feeding on organic matter

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Boletus and the family suffix -aceae.
