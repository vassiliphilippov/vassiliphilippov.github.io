Agaricus campestris

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡærɪkəs kæmˈpɛstrɪs / (AmE), / əˈɡarɪkəs kamˈpɛstrɪs / (BrE)
a common edible mushroom, also known as the field mushroom, found in grasslands and often cultivated for food
common edible field mushroom

Examples

The Agaricus campestris is prized by mushroom foragers for its mild flavor and delicate texture.
Farmers often grow Agaricus campestris commercially for supermarkets and restaurants.
After the rain, several Agaricus campestris appeared in the meadow near our house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: field mushroom - common edible mushroom found in fields and grasslands
  • Synonym: button mushroom - common cultivated variety of edible mushroom

Etymology

From Latin: 'Agaricus' (type of fungus) and 'campestris' (of the field).
