tauromachy

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɔːrəˈmɑːki / (AmE), / ˌtɔːrəˈmæki / (BrE)
the art or practice of bullfighting, especially as a traditional spectacle
the art or practice of bullfighting

Examples

The history and cultural significance of tauromachy have been widely debated in Spain.
Some consider tauromachy a controversial tradition, while others see it as an art form.
He wrote an entire book on the techniques and rituals of tauromachy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bullfighting - the sport or spectacle of fighting bulls

Etymology

From Spanish 'tauromaquia', from Greek 'tauros' (bull) + 'machē' (battle).
