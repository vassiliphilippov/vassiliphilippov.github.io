unquestionable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkwɛstʃənəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈkwɛstʃənəbl / (BrE)
genuine and authentic; not counterfeit or copied
genuine; not counterfeit

Examples

The experts confirmed the painting was an unquestionable masterpiece from the seventeenth century.
She provided unquestionable proof of her identity at the border.
The diamonds' unquestionable origin increased their value at auction.

Synset Examples

an authentic signature
a bona fide manuscript
an unquestionable antique
photographs taken in a veritable bull ring

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authentic - genuine; not false or copied
  • Antonym: fake - not genuine; a forgery or counterfeit

Etymology

From un- (not) + questionable (from question + -able), meaning 'not open to question'.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkwɛstʃənəbl / (AmE), / ʌnˈkwɛstʃənəbl / (BrE)
impossible to doubt or dispute; not open to question or challenge
impossible to doubt or dispute

Examples

Her loyalty to her family was unquestionable throughout the ordeal.
The evidence provided was unquestionable and convinced the jury immediately.
He responded with unquestionable confidence, leaving no room for doubt.

Synset Examples

an unquestionable (or unequivocal) loss of prestige

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indisputable - impossible to challenge or deny
  • Antonym: questionable - open to doubt or suspicion

Etymology

From un- (not) + questionable, modeled after Latin-derived adjectives.
