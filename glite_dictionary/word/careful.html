careful

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɛr.fəl / (AmE), / ˈkeə.fəl / (BrE)
giving attention and thought to what you are doing so that you avoid mistakes, accidents, or damage
showing caution and attention

Examples

She was very careful with the fragile vase when cleaning the shelf.
Always be careful when handling sharp objects in the kitchen.
The scientist conducted a careful review of the data before making conclusions.

Synset Examples

they were careful when crossing the busy street
be careful to keep her shoes clean
did very careful research
careful art restorers
careful of the rights of others
careful about one's behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cautious - showing care to avoid danger or mistakes
  • Antonym: careless - not giving enough attention to what you are doing

Etymology

From care + -ful, first recorded in Middle English.

2. ADJ

/ ˈker.fəl / (AmE), / ˈkeə.fəl / (BrE)
making sure to avoid harm or mistakes; giving close attention to what one does or says
giving close attention; cautious

Examples

She is always careful with her words when discussing sensitive topics.
Be careful of the icy roads when driving this morning.
He gave the package a careful inspection before signing for it.

Synset Examples

careful of her feelings
heedful of his father's advice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cautious - acting with care to avoid risks or mistakes
  • Antonym: heedless - not paying careful attention; careless or inattentive
  • Antonym: careless - not giving enough attention or thought to avoiding harm or errors

Etymology

From Middle English 'careful', from 'care' + '-ful'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01984552-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈkɛr.fəl / (AmE), / ˈkeə.fəl / (BrE)
Taking care to avoid waste or unnecessary expense; mindful of how money is spent.
prudent in spending money

Examples

She is very careful with money and always looks for the best deals.
If you want to save up, you need to be careful with every purchase.
My grandfather was extremely careful about how he spent his money.

Synset Examples

careful with money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrifty - using money and other resources carefully and not wastefully
  • Antonym: extravagant - spending much more than is necessary or wise

Etymology

From care + -ful, meaning 'full of care.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01901166-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈkɛr.fəl / (AmE), / ˈkɛə.fəl / (BrE)
(archaic) full of cares; troubled or anxious
(archaic) anxious; full of cares
archaic

Examples

In times past, many people were careful about the uncertainties of their daily lives.
She lived a careful existence, weighed down by constant worry.
The old texts often describe rulers as careful over the fate of their kingdoms.

Synset Examples

Thou art careful and troubled about many things

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxious - experiencing worry or unease; modern synonym for this sense
  • Antonym: carefree - free from anxiety or worry

Etymology

From care + -ful, originally meaning 'full of care or anxiety'.
