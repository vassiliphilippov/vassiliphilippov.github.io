Canella winterana

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnɛl.ə wɪnˈtɛr.ə.nə / (AmE), / kəˈnɛl.ə wɪnˈtɪə.rə.nə / (BrE)
A large evergreen shrub or small tree of the West Indies, with white aromatic bark, leathery leaves, and small purple to red flowers, known as wild cinnamon.
a West Indian evergreen shrub or tree (wild cinnamon)

Examples

The bark of Canella winterana is often used as a spice and tonic in traditional medicine.
Canella winterana grows mainly in the tropical regions of the West Indies and southern Florida.
The fragrant leaves and colorful flowers make Canella winterana a popular ornamental tree in gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild cinnamon - common name for Canella winterana

Etymology

From Latin, Canella meaning 'little cane' and winterana from the botanist John Wynter.
