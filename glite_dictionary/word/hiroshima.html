Hiroshima

1. PROPN

/ hɪˈroʊʃɪmə / (AmE), / hɪˈrɒʃɪmə / (BrE)
A port city in southwestern Honshu, Japan, known for being the first city devastated by an atomic bomb on August 6, 1945.
Japanese city bombed in 1945

Examples

Many tourists visit Hiroshima to learn about its World War II history and peace memorials.
The city of Hiroshima has been rebuilt and is now a symbol of peace and resilience.
After the atomic bombing, Hiroshima became an advocate for nuclear disarmament.
