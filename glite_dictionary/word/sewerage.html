sewerage

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuː.ɚ.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsjʊə.rɪdʒ / (BrE)
waste matter that is carried away in sewers or drains; the system by which waste water and other refuse is carried away from buildings and disposed of.
waste matter carried in sewers or drains

Examples

The city invested millions to improve its sewerage infrastructure after frequent floods.
Untreated sewerage poses a major risk to both public health and the environment.
Many rural areas still lack modern sewerage systems, relying instead on septic tanks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sewage - waste matter transported by sewers

Etymology

From 'sewer' (an artificial watercourse for carrying off drainage water and refuse) + '-age' (denoting an action or result).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsuː.ɚ.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsjʊə.rɪdʒ / (BrE)
A system of pipes and drains for carrying away sewage or surface water from buildings and other areas.
system for carrying away waste water

Examples

The city expanded its sewerage to accommodate the growing population.
Poorly maintained sewerage can lead to flooding after heavy rain.
Modern sewerage systems are essential for public health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sewer - an underground conduit for carrying off drainage water and waste matter
  • Synonym: drainage - the process or means of removing surplus water or liquid waste

Etymology

From 'sewer' (Middle English 'sewere') + '-age', denoting an action or resulting system.
