Lucius Tarquinius Superbus

1. PROPN

/ ˈluːʃəs tɑːrˈkwɪni.əs suːˈpɜːrbəs / (AmE), / ˈluːsi.əs tɑːˈkwɪni.əs suːˈpɜːbəs / (BrE)
the seventh and last legendary king of Rome, reputed for his tyranny, who was expelled from the throne and reigned from 534 to 510 BC.
legendary last king of Rome

Examples

According to Roman tradition, Lucius Tarquinius Superbus was overthrown because of his oppressive rule.
The reign of Lucius Tarquinius Superbus marked the end of the Roman monarchy.
Historians debate the accuracy of the stories told about Lucius Tarquinius Superbus and his downfall.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Superbus' means 'proud' or 'arrogant' in Latin.
