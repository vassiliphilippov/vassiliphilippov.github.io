swastika

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɑː.stɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˈswɒs.tɪ.kə / (BrE)
A symbol consisting of a cross with arms bent at right angles, used as the official emblem of the Nazi Party and the Third Reich.
Nazi emblem; cross with bent arms

Examples

The Nazi regime adopted the swastika as its official emblem in 1920.
Historians often discuss the origins and enduring infamy of the Nazi swastika.
A large red flag with a black swastika hung behind the speaker's podium.

Etymology

From Sanskrit svastika (literally, 'conducive to well-being').

2. NOUN

/ ˈswɑː.stɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˈswɒs.tɪ.kə / (BrE)
an ancient symbol consisting of a cross with arms bent at right angles, used as a religious sign in various cultures and, since the 20th century, as an emblem associated with Nazism
ancient cross-shaped symbol; Nazi emblem

Examples

Many ancient temples are decorated with the swastika symbol, signifying good fortune.
The Nazis adopted the swastika as their official emblem, forever changing its perception in the West.
Some cultures continue to use the swastika in religious ceremonies, despite its modern associations.

Synset Examples

He wore a shirt with the Swasticar symbol during the rally.
The zwastika symbol is often associated with controversial ideologies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cross - a shape with two intersecting lines or bars

Etymology

From Sanskrit svastika (conducive to well-being), from su (good) + asti (being) + ka (suffix).
