unconsciousness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈkɑnʃəsnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈkɒnʃəsnəs / (BrE)
a state in which a person is not aware of themselves or their surroundings, typically due to illness, injury, or fainting
state of lacking awareness; insensibility

Examples

The blow to his head resulted in immediate unconsciousness.
Doctors monitored her closely while she was in unconsciousness following the accident.
After several minutes of unconsciousness, he gradually began to wake up.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: coma - a prolonged state of deep unconsciousness
  • Degree Lower: fainting - a brief loss of consciousness or faintness
  • Antonym: awareness - the state of being conscious

Etymology

From un- (not) + consciousness, formed from conscious (from Latin conscius) + -ness.
