allotment

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɑtmənt / (AmE), / əˈlɒtmənt / (BrE)
an amount or share of something that is officially given to someone or reserved for a particular purpose
a portion set aside for a purpose

Examples

Each department received an allotment of funds to be used for research projects.
The allotment of supplies was not enough to meet the demand during the emergency.
She carefully managed her monthly allotment to ensure she had enough for essentials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allocation - the action or process of giving out something for a particular purpose

Etymology

From the verb 'allot', from Old French 'aloter', from 'a-' (to) + 'lot' (share, portion).

2. NOUN

/ əˈlɑtmənt / (AmE), / əˈlɒtmənt / (BrE)
the process of distributing or assigning something, especially according to a plan or set rules
the act of allocating or distributing

Examples

The allotment of resources was carefully planned to ensure fairness for all departments.
The committee discussed the allotment of funds for next year's projects.
The allotment of seats in the council is based on population.

Synset Examples

the apportionment of seats in the House of Representatives is based on the relative population of each state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allocation - the action or process of allocating or distributing something
  • Synonym: distribution - the action of sharing something out among a number of recipients

Etymology

From the verb 'allot,' from Old French 'aloter,' from 'a-' (to) + 'lot' (portion, share).
