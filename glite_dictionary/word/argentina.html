Argentina

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈdʒɛntɪnə / (AmE), / ɑːˈdʒɛntɪnə / (BrE)
A genus of small marine fishes in the family Argentinidae, commonly known as argentines.
genus of small marine fishes (argentines)

Examples

The genus Argentina includes several species of silvery fish found in deep waters.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Argentina genus off the Patagonian coast.
Members of Argentina are notable for their elongated bodies and shiny scales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Argentinidae - family of small silvery marine fishes, argentines

Etymology

From Latin 'argentum' meaning 'silver', referring to the silvery appearance of these fishes.

3. NOUN

/ ɑːr.dʒənˈtiː.nə / (AmE), / ɑː.dʒənˈtiː.nə / (BrE)
A genus of small, silvery marine fishes, the type genus of the family Argentinidae (argentines).
a genus of small silvery marine fish

Examples

The genus Argentina includes several species commonly known as argentines.
Marine biologists discovered a new species within the Argentina genus.
The silvery scales of fishes in the Argentina genus help them hide from predators.

Etymology

From Latin 'argentina', derived from 'argentum' meaning 'silver', referring to the fishes’ silvery appearance.
