Conodontophorida

1. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊ.nɔːn.təˈfɔr.ɪ.də / (AmE), / ˌkɒ.nɒn.təˈfɔː.rɪ.də / (BrE)
an extinct order of primitive jawless marine vertebrates, known mainly from tooth-like fossil remains; their precise taxonomic classification is debated, sometimes treated as a separate phylum
extinct order of primitive marine vertebrates

Examples

Fossils attributed to Conodontophorida have provided key insights into early vertebrate evolution.
The Conodontophorida are best known from their tiny, tooth-like elements found in Paleozoic rocks.
Paleontologists debate whether Conodontophorida should be classified as vertebrates or as a separate phylum.

Etymology

From Greek 'kōnos' (cone) + 'odous' (tooth), referring to the conical teeth-like microfossils.
