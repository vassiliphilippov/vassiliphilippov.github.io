Iguania

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈɡweɪniə / (AmE), / ɪˈɡweɪniə / (BrE)
a large group of lizards, including iguanas, anoles, chameleons, and related species, primarily found in the Americas and Madagascar
a group of lizards including iguanas and chameleons

Examples

Scientists have classified several species of chameleons within the Iguania suborder.
A majority of lizards seen in Central and South America belong to the group known as Iguania.
The Iguania clade is characterized by its members' distinctive tongue structure and arboreal habits.

Etymology

From Modern Latin, based on 'Iguana', from the Taino word 'iwana'.
