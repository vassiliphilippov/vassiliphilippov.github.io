Asteraceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌæstɚˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌæstəˈreɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a large family of flowering plants, including daisies, sunflowers, and asters, characterized by composite flower heads made up of many small florets
plant family including daisies and sunflowers

Examples

The Asteraceae family includes many familiar garden flowers like daisies and zinnias.
Sunflowers, marigolds, and dandelions are all part of the Asteraceae family.
Botanists study the diversity of Asteraceae around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Compositae - older alternative name for the family Asteraceae

Etymology

From Modern Latin Asteraceae, from *Aster* (type genus) + -aceae (a standard suffix for plant family names).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæstəˈreɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌæstəˈreɪsiːiː / (BrE)
a large family of flowering plants, including asters, daisies, sunflowers, and dandelions, characterized by composite flower heads made up of many small florets
family of flowering plants including daisies and sunflowers

Examples

The Asteraceae family includes popular garden plants like sunflowers and marigolds.
Botanists often study Asteraceae because of its remarkable diversity.
Many common weeds, such as dandelions, belong to the Asteraceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daisy - common flowering plant with a yellow center and white petals
  • Synonym: sunflower - tall plant with a large yellow flower

Etymology

From the botanical Latin family name, based on the genus Aster, from Greek astēr (star), referring to the star-like flowers.
