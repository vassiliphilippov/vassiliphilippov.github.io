holistic

1. ADJ

/ hoʊˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / həʊˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
Considering a system or situation as a whole, emphasizing the connections and relationships among its parts rather than viewing them separately.
emphasizing the whole and its parts' relationship

Examples

Modern medicine increasingly adopts a holistic approach, treating the patient as a whole rather than just focusing on symptoms.
The company promotes holistic solutions to address environmental challenges in a comprehensive way.
Her teaching style is holistic, integrating social, emotional, and academic learning.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reductionist - focusing on reducing complex systems to their individual parts

Etymology

From Greek holos (whole) + -istic (forming adjectives).
