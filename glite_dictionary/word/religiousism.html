religiousism

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈlɪdʒəˌzɪzəm / (AmE), / rɪˈlɪdʒɪəˌzɪzəm / (BrE)
exaggerated or affected piety and religious fervor; excessive religious zeal.
excessive religious zeal

Examples

The preacher's religiousism eventually alienated even his most faithful followers.
Public debates were often disrupted by those whose religiousism outweighed their willingness to listen.
Her actions bordered on religiousism, going far beyond genuine devotion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zealotry - fanatical and uncompromising pursuit of religious or political ideals
  • Degree Lower: piety - the quality of being religious or reverent
  • Synonym: fanaticism - extreme and uncritical enthusiasm or zeal

Etymology

Formed from 'religious' + '-ism', denoting a practice or ideology.
