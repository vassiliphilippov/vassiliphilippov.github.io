baker

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈbeɪkə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to bake and sell bread, cakes, and other baked goods, especially as a profession or business
person who bakes and sells baked goods

Examples

The baker wakes up early every morning to prepare fresh bread for the shop.
She became a skilled baker after years of working in her family's bakery.
Many people visited the local baker to buy cakes for special occasions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pastry chef - a professional cook who specializes in making pastries and desserts
  • Antonym: customer - someone who buys goods or services, often from a baker

Etymology

From Middle English *bakere*, from Old English *bæcere*, from *bacan* (to bake).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈbeɪkə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to bake bread, cakes, and other baked goods, especially for sale
person who bakes bread or cakes

Examples

The baker arrives before sunrise to prepare fresh loaves for the day.
She works as a baker in a small family-owned bakery.
Our local baker won an award for his delicious croissants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pastry chef - a chef who specializes in making pastries and desserts

Etymology

From Middle English bakere, from Old English bæcere (baker), from bacan (to bake).
