presidium

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈsɪdiəm / (AmE), / prɪˈsɪdiəm / (BrE)
a permanent executive committee in some socialist countries that holds the powers of a larger legislative body and acts on its behalf between sessions
executive committee in socialist countries

Examples

The presidium has the authority to make urgent decisions when the parliament is not in session.
After the revolution, the new government formed a presidium to oversee legislative affairs.
He addressed the presidium during its special session on constitutional reforms.

Etymology

From Russian 'prezidium', ultimately from Latin 'praesidium' (garrison, protection).
