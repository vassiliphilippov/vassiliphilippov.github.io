morphologic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɔr.fəˈlɑ.d͡ʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɔː.fəˈlɒ.d͡ʒɪk / (BrE)
relating to the formation and structure of words in a language; concerning morphology.
relating to word formation in linguistics

Examples

The researcher analyzed the morphologic features of the new dialect.
Certain morphologic rules determine how words change to express tense or number.
Their study focused on the morphologic differences between related languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morphological - alternative and more common form; pertaining to morphology

Etymology

From Greek morphē (form) and -logic (from logos, meaning study or science).

2. ADJ

/ mɔr.fəˈlɑ.dʒɪk / (AmE), / mɔː.fəˈlɒ.dʒɪk / (BrE)
relating to the form or structure of an object, especially in geology
relating to form or structure, esp. in geology

Examples

Geologists analyzed the morphologic characteristics of the rock formations to understand their history.
The morphologic development of the valley was influenced by ancient glacial activity.
Satellite imagery revealed several morphologic anomalies in the mountain range.

relating to form and structure (biology)

Examples
