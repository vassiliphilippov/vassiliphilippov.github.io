Zigadenus nuttalli

1. NOUN

/ zɪˈɡædənəs nʌˈtæl.aɪ / (AmE), / zɪˈɡædənəs nʌˈtal.i / (BrE)
A species of perennial, highly poisonous plant known as death camas, native to parts of the central United States from Tennessee to Texas.
a species of poisonous death camas plant

Examples

Livestock occasionally die after grazing on Zigadenus nuttalli in pasturelands.
Botanists conducted a survey of Zigadenus nuttalli populations in central Texas.
The bulbs of Zigadenus nuttalli are highly toxic if ingested by humans or animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: death camas - any of several highly poisonous plants of the genus Zigadenus

Etymology

From the genus name Zigadenus, derived from Greek 'zigadenos' (yoke-shaped); the species epithet commemorates the American botanist Thomas Nuttall.
