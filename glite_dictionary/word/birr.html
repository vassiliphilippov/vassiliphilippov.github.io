birr

1. NOUN

/ bɪr / (AmE), / bɪə / (BrE)
the basic monetary unit of Ethiopia, equal to 100 cents
unit of currency in Ethiopia

Examples

The price of coffee in the market is 50 birr per kilogram.
She exchanged her dollars for birr at the airport upon arrival in Addis Ababa.
The total cost of the tour was several thousand birr.

Etymology

From Amharic ብር (birr), meaning 'silver' or 'money'.

2. NOUN

/ bɝː / (AmE), / bɜː / (BrE)
A whirring or humming sound, especially that made by something moving rapidly.
whirring or humming sound

Examples

The birr of the large ceiling fan filled the quiet room.
He woke early, disturbed by the birr of a distant lawn mower.
She listened to the birr of the tiny drone as it disappeared into the sky.

Synset Examples

whir of a bird's wings
the whir of the propellers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whir - a continuous, low, rushing sound
  • Synonym: buzz - a low, continuous humming or murmuring sound

Etymology

From Scots birr (force, energy, a whirring sound), of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07414517-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ bɜr / (AmE), / bɜː / (BrE)
(of a machine or object) to make a soft, whirring or swishing sound.
make a soft whirring sound

Examples

The old sewing machine birred softly as she pressed the pedal.
A tiny drone birred overhead, barely audible above the traffic.
Through the quiet night, the fan in the corner birred steadily.

Synset Examples

the motor whirred
the car engine purred

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whir - make a low, continuous, regular sound
  • Synonym: purr - make a soft vibrating sound, like a cat
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
