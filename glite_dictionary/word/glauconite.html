glauconite

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlaʊ.kəˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡlɔː.kəˌnaɪt / (BrE)
a green mineral consisting of a hydrated silicate of potassium, iron, magnesium, or aluminum, commonly found in greensand and used in geology.
a green mineral found in greensand

Examples

The presence of glauconite gives the sandstone its characteristic green color.
Geologists often study deposits of glauconite to understand ancient marine environments.
The soil sample contained traces of glauconite, indicating it was once part of a shallow sea.

Etymology

From French 'glauconite', from Greek 'glaukos' (bluish-green).
