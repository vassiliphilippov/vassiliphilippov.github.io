excellence

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛksələns / (AmE), / ˈɛksələns / (BrE)
the quality of being outstanding or extremely good
the quality of being outstanding

Examples

The company strives for excellence in customer service.
Her painting displays a level of excellence rarely seen in modern art.
Academic excellence is encouraged and rewarded at this institution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superiority - the state of being superior to others
  • Antonym: mediocrity - the quality of being average or ordinary

Etymology

From Latin excellentia, from excellere (to surpass).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛksələns / (AmE), / ˈɛksələns / (BrE)
An outstanding quality or feature that makes someone or something very good or superior.
outstanding quality or feature

Examples

The university was recognized for its excellence in research and innovation.
Her artwork displays a level of excellence rarely seen in young artists.
Our company's excellence in customer service sets us apart from our competitors.

Synset Examples

a center of manufacturing excellence
the use of herbs is one of the excellencies of French cuisine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merit - the quality of being particularly good or worthy
  • Synonym: superiority - the state of being higher in quality or skill
  • Antonym: fault - a defect or imperfection

Etymology

From Latin excellentia (surpassing, superiority).
