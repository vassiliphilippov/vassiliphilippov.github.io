Tayassuidae

1. NOUN

/ taɪˈæsjuˌɪdi / (AmE), / taɪˈasjuːɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of medium-sized, pig-like hoofed mammals native to the Americas, commonly known as peccaries.
family of pig-like mammals (peccaries)

Examples

Scientists have been studying populations of Tayassuidae in South America to understand their ecological roles.
The Chacoan peccary is a recently discovered member of the Tayassuidae family.
Unlike pigs, members of the Tayassuidae have a unique arrangement of their stomachs for digesting tough vegetation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peccary - any member of the family Tayassuidae
  • Antonym: Suidae - family of true pigs, related to but distinct from Tayassuidae

Etymology

From modern Latin Tayassuidae, based on Tayassu, a genus within the family.

2. NOUN

/ taɪˌæsjuˈɪdi / (AmE), / taɪˌæsjuːˈɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of pig-like mammals known as peccaries, native to the Americas.
family of pig-like mammals (peccaries)

Examples

The Tayassuidae are found across much of Central and South America.
Scientists are studying the habitat loss threatening members of the Tayassuidae family.
Unlike pigs, Tayassuidae have smaller tails and different dental structures.

Etymology

From New Latin *Tayassuidae*, based on the genus Tayassu, derived from an indigenous term for peccary in South America.
