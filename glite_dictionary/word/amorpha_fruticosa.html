Amorpha fruticosa

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɔrfə fruːˈtɪkoʊsə / (AmE), / əˈmɔːfə fruːˈtɪkəʊsə / (BrE)
A dense shrub native to moist riverbanks and flood plains of the eastern United States, known for its fragrant foliage and dense clusters of dark purple flowers.
a North American shrub with dark purple flowers

Examples

The riverbank was lined with Amorpha fruticosa, its purple flowers attracting dozens of bees.
Gardeners appreciate Amorpha fruticosa for its ability to thrive in wet soils and its ornamental foliage.
Botanists studied the distribution of Amorpha fruticosa in Southern states to track changes in native plant populations.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Amorpha' from Greek 'amorphos' (shapeless), and species 'fruticosa' from Latin (shrubby).
