mini

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪni / (AmE), / ˈmɪni / (BrE)
A very short skirt, typically one ending well above the knees.
a very short skirt

Examples

She wore a bright red mini to the summer party.
The fashion magazine predicted that the mini would make a big comeback this year.
He complimented her on her stylish mini and matching shoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miniskirt - an extremely short skirt

Etymology

Shortened from 'miniskirt', first popularized in the 1960s.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03775972-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɪni / (AmE), / ˈmɪni / (BrE)
(of women's clothing) very short in length, with the hemline above the knee.
very short (of clothing)

Examples

She wore a bright red mini dress to the party, turning heads as she entered the room.
The latest summer collection features several stylish mini skirts and dresses.
Mini skirts became a major fashion trend in the 1960s, and the mini look still appears on runways today.

Synset Examples

a mini dress
miniskirts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miniskirt - a very short skirt with a hemline above the knee

Etymology

Shortened from 'miniature'; popularized in the 1960s with the introduction of the miniskirt.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01435587-a
Synset POS: a
