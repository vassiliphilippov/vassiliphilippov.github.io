Galax urceolata

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪlæks ˌɜrsiˈoʊlətə / (AmE), / ˈɡalaks ˌɜːsiˈəʊlətə / (BrE)
A tufted evergreen perennial herb native to the southeastern United States, with spikes of tiny white flowers and round to heart-shaped glossy leaves that turn coppery, maroon, or purplish in fall.
evergreen plant with white flowers and glossy leaves

Examples

In the shade garden, Galax urceolata provides year-round interest with its ever-changing leaf colors.
Botanists admire the distinctive white flower spikes of Galax urceolata in the spring.
The glossy foliage of Galax urceolata turns a striking maroon in the autumn months.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'galaxias' (milky), species epithet from Latin 'urceolus' (a small pitcher, urn-shaped, referring to the flowers).
