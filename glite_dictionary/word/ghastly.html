ghastly

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡæs.tli / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːst.li / (BrE)
causing horror or fear; shockingly repellent or gruesome
horrifying; shockingly repellent

Examples

The crime scene was a ghastly sight that left the detectives shaken.
She let out a ghastly scream when she saw the shadowy figure.
The hospital treated many victims with ghastly injuries after the accident.

Synset Examples

ghastly wounds
the grim aftermath of the bombing
the grim task of burying the victims
a grisly murder
gruesome evidence of human sacrifice
macabre tales of war and plague in the Middle ages
macabre tortures conceived by madmen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gruesome - causing repulsion or horror; grisly
  • Synonym: macabre - disturbing and horrifying because of involvement with or depiction of death and injury
  • Antonym: pleasant - giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment

Etymology

From Middle English gastly (frightful), from Old English gæstan (to terrify).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡæsli / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːstli / (BrE)
Frighteningly or shockingly unpleasant, especially suggestive of death or the supernatural.
frightening; suggestive of death

Examples

The abandoned house had a ghastly silence that made everyone uneasy.
The scene of the accident was so ghastly that witnesses turned away in horror.
She awoke from a ghastly nightmare, drenched in sweat and shaking.

Synset Examples

a charnel smell came from the chest filled with dead men's bones
ghastly shrieks
the sepulchral darkness of the catacombs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gruesome - causing repulsion or horror; grisly
  • Synonym: horrific - extremely disturbing or terrifying
  • Antonym: pleasant - giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment (opposite of ghastly)

Etymology

From Middle English 'gastly', from Old English 'gæstan' (to terrify, from 'gæst' meaning spirit or ghost).

Synset POS: s
