surd

1. NOUN

/ sɝd / (AmE), / sɜːd / (BrE)
A voiceless consonant, produced without vibration of the vocal cords.
voiceless consonant

Examples

In English, the sound of s in 'sip' is classified as a surd.
Linguists categorize 'f', 'k', and 't' as examples of surd consonants.
Unlike 'z', which is voiced, 's' is a surd since it lacks vocal cord vibration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voiceless consonant - a consonant produced without vocal cord vibration
  • Antonym: sonant - a voiced consonant produced with vibration of the vocal cords

Etymology

From Latin surdus (deaf, mute).

2. ADJ

/ sɜrd / (AmE), / sɜːd / (BrE)
(of a speech sound) produced without vibration of the vocal cords; voiceless.
voiceless speech sound (phonetics)

Examples

The letter 'p' is a surd consonant in English.
In phonetics, 'k' is classified as a surd sound, unlike 'g'.
Linguists study both surd and sonant consonants to understand speech patterns.

Synset Examples

unvoiced consonants such as ‘p’ and ‘k’ and ‘s’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voiceless - produced without vibration of the vocal cords
  • Antonym: sonant - produced with vibration of the vocal cords (voiced)

Etymology

From Latin 'surdus' meaning deaf or mute.
