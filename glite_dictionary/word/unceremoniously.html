unceremoniously

1. ADV

/ ˌʌnsəˌrɛməˈnoʊsli / (AmE), / ˌʌnsəˌrɛmɪˈnəʊsli / (BrE)
In a blunt or abrupt manner, without courtesy, formalities, or ceremony.
in a blunt/abrupt manner, without ceremony

Examples

The manager unceremoniously fired three employees without warning.
She was unceremoniously removed from the guest list at the last minute.
Their picnic was unceremoniously interrupted by a sudden downpour.

Synset Examples

he was dismissed unceremoniously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abruptly - in a sudden or unexpected way
  • Antonym: ceremoniously - in a formal or ceremonious manner

Etymology

un- (not) + ceremonious + -ly. From 'ceremony' (from Latin 'caerimonia', religious rite).
