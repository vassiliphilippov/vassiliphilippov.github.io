airwave

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrˌweɪv / (AmE), / ˈɛəˌweɪv / (BrE)
a radio frequency used for the transmission of radio and television broadcasts
radio or TV transmission frequency

Examples

Only a few channels were available on the airwaves in the early 1950s.
The new law aims to regulate what can be said on the airwaves.
Community radio stations compete for space on the crowded airwaves.

Synset Examples

the program was on the air from 9 til midnight
the president used the airwaves to take his message to the people

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broadcast - to transmit a program or signal over radio or television
  • Degree Lower: frequency - the rate at which a radio wave is transmitted, measured in hertz

Etymology

Formed from 'air' + 'wave', referring to electromagnetic waves traveling through the air.
