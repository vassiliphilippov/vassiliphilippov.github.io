dewy-eyed

1. ADJ

/ ˈduː.i aɪd / (AmE), / ˈdjuː.i aɪd / (BrE)
innocent and naive; showing childlike simplicity or credulity
innocent or naive; childlike

Examples

She gave him a dewy-eyed look as he told his remarkable story.
The dewy-eyed newcomer believed everyone was honest and trustworthy.
It's unrealistic to take such a dewy-eyed view of the world.

Synset Examples

childlike trust
dewy-eyed innocence
listened in round-eyed wonder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naive - showing lack of experience, wisdom, or judgement
  • Antonym: cynical - distrusting or disparaging the motives of others

2. ADJ

/ ˈduːi aɪd / (AmE), / ˈdjuːi aɪd / (BrE)
having an innocent, gentle, or emotional expression, often suggesting naivety or idealism
innocent or emotional in expression

Examples

She looked at him with a dewy-eyed innocence that was impossible to ignore.
The film's dewy-eyed portrayal of childhood dreams captured audiences' hearts.
As a newcomer, he was still a bit dewy-eyed about the realities of the business.

Synset Examples

His dewy-eyed expression revealed his innocence.
She gave a dewy-eyed look when he proposed.
The children watched the movie with dewy-eyed wonder.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clear-eyed - having a realistic or practical outlook
  • Synonym: wide-eyed - having a look of surprise or innocence
