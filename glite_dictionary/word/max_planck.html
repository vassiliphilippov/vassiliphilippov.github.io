Max Planck

1. PROPN

/ mæks ˈplæŋk / (AmE), / mæks ˈplaŋk / (BrE)
(1858–1947) German physicist who developed quantum theory by explaining blackbody radiation through quantized energy emissions.
German physicist, quantum theory pioneer

Examples

Max Planck revolutionized physics by introducing the concept of energy quanta.
The Max Planck Society in Germany was named in honor of Max Planck.
In 1918, Max Planck was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for his work.

2. PROPN

/ mæks ˈplɑŋk / (AmE), / mæks ˈplaŋk / (BrE)
(1858–1947) A German physicist who originated quantum theory, greatly influencing the development of modern physics.
German physicist, founder of quantum theory

Examples

Max Planck received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1918 for his discovery of energy quanta.
Many consider Max Planck the father of modern quantum theory.
Max Planck's work revolutionized our understanding of atomic and subatomic processes.
