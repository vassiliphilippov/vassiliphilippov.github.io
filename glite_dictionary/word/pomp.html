pomp

1. NOUN

/ pɑmp / (AmE), / pɒmp / (BrE)
ceremonial or ostentatious display and splendor, especially on public occasions
ceremonial grandeur; splendor

Examples

The royal wedding was marked by great pomp and ceremony.
She loved the pomp of graduation day, with its robes and music.
Despite all the pomp, the event felt surprisingly warm and personal.

Synset Examples

entered with much eclat in a coach drawn by eight white horses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pageantry - elaborate display or ceremony
  • Antonym: simplicity - lack of adornment or elaboration

Etymology

From Latin 'pompa' (procession, display) via Old French and Latin.

2. NOUN

/ pɑmp / (AmE), / pɒmp / (BrE)
a display of excessive grandeur, splendour, or pretentious show, often intended to impress others
pretentious or vain display

Examples

The royal wedding was filled with pomp and ceremony.
Despite his modest background, he loved to surround himself with pomp and luxury.
Critics accused the event of being all pomp and little substance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splendor - magnificent and splendid appearance
  • Antonym: humility - the quality of being humble

Etymology

From Latin 'pompa' (procession, display), via Old French 'pompe'.
