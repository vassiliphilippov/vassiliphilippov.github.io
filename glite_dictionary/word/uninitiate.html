uninitiate

1. NOUN

/ ʌn.ɪˈnɪʃ.i.ət / (AmE), / ʌn.ɪˈnɪʃ.i.ət / (BrE)
a person who has not been introduced to or is inexperienced in a particular field or activity.
person lacking initiation or experience

Examples

The technical jargon can easily confuse the uninitiate.
To the uninitiate, the ritual seemed strange and complicated.
Guides are available to help the uninitiate understand the basics of the game.

Synset Examples

it diverts the attention of the uninitiate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: novice - a person new to a field or activity
  • Antonym: initiate - a person who has been introduced to the mysteries of a field or activity

Etymology

From un- (not) + initiate, meaning not initiated.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪˈnɪʃiət / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪˈnɪʃiət / (BrE)
lacking experience or knowledge in a particular subject or activity; not having been introduced to something.
lacking experience or initiation

Examples

The ancient ritual baffled the uninitiate in the audience.
To the uninitiate, quantum physics can seem impossibly complex.
This guide is intended to help the uninitiate explore the basics of wine tasting.

Synset Examples

it seemed a bizarre ceremony to uninitiated western eyes
he took part in the experiment as a naive subject

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inexperienced - lacking experience or knowledge
  • Synonym: naive - lacking sophistication or experience
  • Antonym: initiated - having been introduced to a subject or activity

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + initiate (from Latin initiatus, past participle of initiare, to begin).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00940610-s
Synset POS: s
