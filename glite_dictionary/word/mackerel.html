mackerel

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæk.ɚ.əl / (AmE), / ˈmæk.ər.əl / (BrE)
A marine fish of the family Scombridae, known for its streamlined body and commonly used as food.
a type of marine fish

Examples

Grilled mackerel with lemon is a popular dish in many coastal regions.
The fisherman proudly displayed a large mackerel he had just caught.
Mackerel are known for swimming in large schools in the open ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuna - a large fish of the Scombridae family, similar to mackerel

Etymology

From Old French 'maquerel', of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmækɚəl / (AmE), / ˈmak(ə)rəl / (BrE)
the flesh of a small, oily Atlantic fish, used as food
flesh of a small fatty Atlantic fish (food)

Examples

Fresh mackerel is considered a delicacy in many coastal regions.
He ordered grilled mackerel served with lemon and herbs for lunch.
Canned mackerel is a convenient source of protein and omega-3 fatty acids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mackerel - a small, fast-swimming Atlantic fish, Scomber scombrus

Etymology

From Old French 'maquerel', of uncertain origin.
