torpidness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔːrpɪdnɪs / (AmE), / ˈtɔːpɪdnɪs / (BrE)
A state of physical or mental inactivity resulting from lethargy or lack of energy.
state of inactivity or sluggishness

Examples

After weeks of torpidness, he finally felt motivated to start exercising again.
The torpidness induced by the humid weather made it hard to focus on work.
Her illness resulted in a period of torpidness and inactivity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lethargy - a lack of energy and enthusiasm
  • Antonym: vigor - active bodily or mental strength or energy

Etymology

From Latin 'torpidus' (numb, sluggish) + '-ness'.
