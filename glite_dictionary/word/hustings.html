hustings

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌstɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈhʌstɪŋz / (BrE)
the meetings, events, or activities associated with political campaigning, especially those involving public speeches by candidates
platform or activities for political campaigning

Examples

The candidate addressed a large crowd from the hustings during his campaign.
She grew tired of life on the hustings but remained committed to her cause.
Debates on the hustings can often determine the outcome of an election.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campaign trail - the course of events and activities undertaken by a candidate during an election campaign

Etymology

From Old English 'hūsting' (deliberative assembly), via Old Norse 'hústhing' (house assembly).
