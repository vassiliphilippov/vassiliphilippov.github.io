Columba fasciata

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈlʌmbə fəˈsiːeɪtə / (AmE), / kəˈlʌmbə fæsiˈɑːtə / (BrE)
A wild pigeon native to western North America, commonly known as the band-tailed pigeon and sometimes mistaken for the extinct passenger pigeon.
a wild pigeon of western North America

Examples

The Columba fasciata is often spotted in oak forests throughout California.
Researchers are studying the migration patterns of Columba fasciata to understand their changing habitats.
Bird watchers sometimes confuse the Columba fasciata with the extinct passenger pigeon due to their similar appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: band-tailed pigeon - common name of Columba fasciata

Etymology

From Latin: 'Columba' meaning 'pigeon' and 'fasciata' meaning 'banded'.
