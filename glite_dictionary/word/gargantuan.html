gargantuan

1. ADJ

/ ɡɑrˈɡæn.tʃu.ən / (AmE), / ɡɑːˈɡæn.tjuː.ən / (BrE)
Extremely large; enormous or massive in size, extent, or amount.
extremely large; enormous

Examples

The restaurant is known for its gargantuan portions that could feed an entire family.
She was faced with the gargantuan task of cleaning out the garage after years of neglect.
A gargantuan statue dominated the city square, impressing all who passed by.

Synset Examples

a jumbo jet
jumbo shrimp

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enormous - very large in size, quantity, or extent
  • Synonym: huge - extremely large in size or amount
  • Antonym: tiny - very small in size or amount

Etymology

From French Gargantua, the name of a giant in Rabelais' 16th-century novels.
