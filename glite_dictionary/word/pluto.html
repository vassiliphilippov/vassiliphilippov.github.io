Pluto

1. PROPN

/ ˈpluːtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpluːtəʊ / (BrE)
A cartoon dog character created by Walt Disney, depicted as a medium-sized, short-haired, red-colored dog with black ears.
Disney cartoon dog character

Examples

My favorite Disney character is Pluto because he always gets into funny situations.
Children laughed as Pluto chased his tail across the television screen.
The plush toy of Pluto was a best-seller in the gift shop.

Etymology

Named after the Roman god Pluto; used for the Disney character since the 1930s.

2. PROPN

/ ˈpluːtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpluːtəʊ / (BrE)
a dwarf planet in the outer solar system that was formerly classified as the ninth planet; it follows an elliptical orbit around the sun
dwarf planet in the outer solar system

Examples

The New Horizons spacecraft sent back detailed images of Pluto in 2015.
Scientists reclassified Pluto as a dwarf planet in 2006.
When I was a child, Pluto was still considered the ninth planet in our solar system.

Synset Examples

Pluto was discovered by Clyde Tombaugh in 1930

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: planet - a celestial body orbiting a star, formerly including Pluto as the ninth planet
  • Degree Lower: asteroid - a small rocky body orbiting the sun, sometimes compared with Pluto

Etymology

Named after Pluto, the Roman god of the underworld.

3. PROPN

/ ˈpluːtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpluːtəʊ / (BrE)
In Roman mythology, the god of the underworld and ruler of the dead, equivalent to the Greek god Hades.
Roman god of the underworld

Examples

In ancient Roman stories, Pluto presided over the realm of the dead.
Many myths describe Pluto as a stern but just ruler of the underworld.
Unlike some gods, Pluto was seldom depicted on Roman temples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hades - Greek god of the underworld, equivalent to Pluto

Etymology

From Latin Pluto, from Greek Ploutōn (πλούτων), meaning 'wealth-giver' or 'rich one', due to the riches (minerals, fertility) under the earth.
