Mycobacterium tuberculosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkoʊbəˌktɪəriəm tuˌbɜrkjʊˈloʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmʌɪkəʊbækˌtɪəriəm tjuːˌbɜːkjʊˈləʊsɪs / (BrE)
a species of bacterium that causes tuberculosis in humans and other animals
the bacterium that causes tuberculosis
medical

Examples

Mycobacterium tuberculosis is responsible for millions of tuberculosis infections worldwide each year.
The discovery of Mycobacterium tuberculosis revolutionized the understanding of contagious diseases.
Doctors use special tests to identify Mycobacterium tuberculosis in patient samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuberculosis - infectious disease caused by Mycobacterium tuberculosis

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, blending Greek 'mykes' (fungus) and 'bakterion' (little rod), and Latin 'tuberculosis'.
