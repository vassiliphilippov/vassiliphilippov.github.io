Norsewoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔrsˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈnɔːswʊmən / (BrE)
a woman who is a native or inhabitant of Norway, especially during the period of Norse history.
female native of Norway

Examples

The legendary Norsewoman was known for her courage and leadership during the Viking Age.
As a Norsewoman, she played a vital role in her community's daily life and rituals.
Historians have uncovered evidence of powerful Norsewomen who influenced trade and exploration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Norseman - a man who is a native or inhabitant of Norway

Etymology

Formed from 'Norse' (relating to ancient Scandinavia or its peoples) + 'woman'.
