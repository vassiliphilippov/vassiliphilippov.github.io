to adjudge

1. VERB

/ əˈdʒʌdʒ / (AmE), / əˈdʒʌdʒ / (BrE)
to officially decide or declare something, especially in a court of law
officially declare or decide

Examples

The court adjudged him guilty of the crime after a lengthy trial.
The prize was adjudged to the most innovative entry.
She was adjudged incompetent by the panel of experts.

Synset Examples

She was declared incompetent
judge held that the defendant was innocent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to declare - to state something formally or officially
  • Synonym: to deem - to judge or consider in a particular way

Etymology

From Old French *ajuger*, from Latin *adjudicare* (to award, grant officially).
