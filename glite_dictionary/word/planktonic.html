planktonic

1. ADJ

/ plæŋˈtɑː.nɪk / (AmE), / plæŋˈtɒn.ɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of plankton, the tiny organisms that drift in water.
relating to plankton

Examples

Many marine animals feed on planktonic organisms in the open ocean.
The planktonic stage is crucial for the lifecycle of several fish species.
Scientists collect water samples to study the planktonic community present in different lakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plankton - the small and microscopic organisms drifting in water

Etymology

From 'plankton,' which derives from Greek *planktos* (drifting), plus the suffix '-ic' forming adjectives.
