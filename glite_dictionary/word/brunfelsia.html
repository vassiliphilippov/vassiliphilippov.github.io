Brunfelsia

1. NOUN

/ brʌnˈfɛlziə / (AmE), / brʌnˈfɛlziə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American shrubs cultivated for their attractive flowers and fleshy, berrylike fruits.
genus of tropical shrubs with showy flowers

Examples

The garden featured a beautiful Brunfelsia shrub in full bloom.
Horticulturists often recommend Brunfelsia for its vibrant purple flowers.
After flowering, the Brunfelsia produces small, fleshy berries.

Etymology

Named after Otto Brunfels, a German botanist (c. 1488–1534).

2. NOUN

/ brʌnˈfɛl.si.ə / (AmE), / brʌnˈfɛl.si.ə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American shrubs in the nightshade family, grown for their attractive flowers and fleshy berry-like fruits.
tropical plant genus with showy flowers

Examples

The garden's collection includes several varieties of Brunfelsia, known for their fragrant purple and white blossoms.
Horticulturists value Brunfelsia for the bright colors its flowers add to tropical landscapes.
After flowering, the Brunfelsia shrub produces distinctive berry-like fruits.

Etymology

Named after Otto Brunfels, a 16th-century German botanist.
