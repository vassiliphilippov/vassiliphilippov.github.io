racon

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪ.kɒn / (AmE), / ˈreɪ.kɒn / (BrE)
a radar beacon that sends back coded signals when triggered by a radar, used to help navigators determine their position
radar beacon for navigation

Examples

The coastguard vessel used its racon to signal its position to nearby ships.
A racon is often mounted on buoys to assist with marine navigation in poor visibility.
Pilots relied on the racon to avoid hazards during foggy conditions.

Etymology

Blend of 'radar' and 'beacon.'
