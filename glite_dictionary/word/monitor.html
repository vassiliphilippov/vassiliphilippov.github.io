Monitor

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɑːnɪtər / (AmE), / ˈmɒnɪtə / (BrE)
A famous ironclad warship built by the United States during the American Civil War, notable for its innovative design and battle with the Merrimac.
Civil War ironclad warship

Examples

The Monitor played a pivotal role in the Battle of Hampton Roads.
Historians often discuss the design innovations of the Monitor and its revolving turret.
The clash between the Monitor and the Merrimac changed naval warfare forever.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ironclad - a warship covered with protective iron plates
  • Antonym: Merrimac - the Confederate ironclad warship also known as the CSS Virginia

Etymology

Named after the Latin 'monitor' (warner, admonisher); USS Monitor was intended to warn or admonish the Confederacy.
