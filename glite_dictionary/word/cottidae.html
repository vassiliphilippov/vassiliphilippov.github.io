Cottidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːtəˌdaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɒt.ɪ.diː / (BrE)
A family of mostly small, bottom-dwelling marine and freshwater fish, commonly known as sculpins.
family of fish, sculpins

Examples

Many members of the Cottidae family inhabit rocky coastal waters.
Freshwater species in the Cottidae are important in North American streams.
Scientists are studying the adaptation of Cottidae to cold environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sculpin - a type of small fish found on the sea bottom

Etymology

From modern Latin Cottidae, based on the type genus Cottus.
