layabout

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪəˌbaʊt / (AmE), / ˈleɪəˌbaʊt / (BrE)
a person who habitually avoids work or activity; an idle or lazy person
idle or lazy person

Examples

After losing his job, he became a layabout and spent his days doing nothing.
Her parents accused her new boyfriend of being a layabout who had no ambition.
Many people unfairly stereotype artists as layabouts who refuse to get real jobs.

Synset Examples

a lazy bum

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idler - a person who avoids work; similar meaning to 'layabout'
  • Antonym: workaholic - a person who works excessively; the opposite of 'layabout'

Etymology

From 'lay' (lie, recline) and 'about', indicating a person who lies about idly.
