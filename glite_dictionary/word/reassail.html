to reassail

1. VERB

/ ˌriːəˈseɪl / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈseɪl / (BrE)
to attack or assail again, especially after an initial attempt
attack or assail again

Examples

Even after therapy, her doubts would reassail her in moments of silence.
Determined to win, the team reassailed their opponents with a renewed offensive.
Despite his efforts to move on, old memories reassailed him unexpectedly.

Synset Examples

Her old fears reassailed her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assail - to attack violently or aggressively

Etymology

From re- (again) + assail, ultimately from Latin ad- + salire (to leap).
