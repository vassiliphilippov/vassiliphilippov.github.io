Genista hispanica

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈnɪstə hɪˈspænɪkə / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛnɪstə hɪˈspænɪkə / (BrE)
An erect shrub of southwestern Europe with racemes of golden yellow flowers, belonging to the broom family.
shrub with golden yellow flowers (broom family)

Examples

The garden's rockery featured several mature Genista hispanica plants in full bloom.
Botanists documented the spread of Genista hispanica beyond its native range in southwestern Europe.
Genista hispanica is prized for its vibrant yellow flowers and drought resistance.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Genista' (broom), species 'hispanica' (Spanish; from Hispania, Spain).
