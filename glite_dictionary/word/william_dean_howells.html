William Dean Howells

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪljəm din ˈhaʊəlz / (AmE), / ˈwɪljəm diːn ˈhaʊəlz / (BrE)
(1837–1920) An American writer, literary critic, and editor, known for his influential role in 19th-century American literature.
American writer and editor; 1837–1920

Examples

William Dean Howells championed the realist movement in American literature through both his own novels and his editorial work.
The influence of William Dean Howells can be seen in the development of modern American fiction.
His correspondence with Mark Twain reveals much about William Dean Howells's thoughtful approach to writing.
