bakshis

1. NOUN

/ bɑkˈʃiʃ / (AmE), / bækˈʃiːʃ / (BrE)
a small amount of money given as a tip or gratuity for services, especially in some Middle Eastern or South Asian countries
small tip or gratuity

Examples

At the end of the meal, he slipped the waiter a generous bakshis for his excellent service.
In some countries, it's customary to offer a bakshis to guides or porters.
The porter expected a small bakshis for carrying our luggage to the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tip - a small sum of money given for a service
  • Synonym: gratuity - money given in return for service

Etymology

From Persian 'bakhshīsh' meaning 'gift', via Urdu and Turkish, adopted into English through colonial contact.
