Lemna

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛm.nə / (AmE), / ˈlɛm.nə / (BrE)
A genus of very small, free-floating aquatic plants, commonly called duckweed, found on the surface of still or slow-moving fresh water and consisting of a leaflike frond with a single root.
genus of tiny aquatic floating plants (duckweed)

Examples

Tiny green Lemna plants quickly covered the surface of the pond by midsummer.
The biologist collected samples of Lemna to study the ecosystem's health.
Because Lemna grows so rapidly, it can reduce oxygen levels in stagnant water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duckweed - common name for several genera of small, floating aquatic plants, including Lemna

Etymology

From Greek *lemna* (duckweed).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛmnə / (AmE), / ˈlɛm.nə / (BrE)
a genus of minute aquatic plants, commonly known as duckweed, that float on or just beneath the surface of still freshwater, consisting of small leaflike fronds and a single root
a genus of tiny aquatic floating plants (duckweed)

Examples

Lemna species are often found covering the surface of garden ponds and still lakes.
Scientists study Lemna to monitor water pollution due to its sensitivity to environmental changes.
Removing excess Lemna from a pond is sometimes necessary to maintain ecological balance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duckweed - common name for plants of the genus Lemna

Etymology

From Greek *lēmna* (marsh, pond); used in botanical Latin.
