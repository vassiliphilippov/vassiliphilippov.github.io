psychopomp

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪkoʊˌpɒmp / (AmE), / ˈsaɪkəʊˌpɒmp / (BrE)
a being or deity who guides souls to the afterlife, especially in mythology or religion
guide of souls to the afterlife

Examples

In ancient mythology, Hermes was often regarded as a psychopomp who led souls to the underworld.
The shaman acted as a psychopomp during the tribe's burial rituals.
Many cultures have myths about a psychopomp who escorts the dead on their final journey.

Synset Examples

Hermes was their psychopomp

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guide - a person who shows others the way

Etymology

From Greek psychopompos (guide of souls), from psykhē (soul) + pompos (conductor).
