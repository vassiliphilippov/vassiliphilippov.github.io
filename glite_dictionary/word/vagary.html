vagary

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪɡəri / (AmE), / ˈveɪɡəri / (BrE)
an unexpected and inexplicable change or fluctuation, especially in a situation or a person's behavior
unexpected or inexplicable change

Examples

Travelers should always be prepared for the vagaries of weather in the mountains.
Her mood is subject to the vagaries of stress and exhaustion.
The company had to adjust budget plans according to the vagaries of the global market.

Synset Examples

the vagaries of the weather
his wealth fluctuates with the vagaries of the stock market
he has dealt with human vagaries for many years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whim - a sudden and impulsive idea or desire

Etymology

From Latin 'vagari' (to wander).
