Catholicism

1. NOUN

/ kəˈθɑː.lɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / kəˈθæl.ɪ.sɪ.zəm / (BrE)
The faith, doctrine, system, and practice of the Catholic Church, especially the Roman Catholic Church.
the beliefs and practices of the Catholic Church

Examples

Many people around the world practice Catholicism as their religion.
Her studies focus on the history and traditions of Catholicism in Europe.
He converted to Catholicism after attending several church services.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Protestantism - the beliefs and practices of Protestant Christian churches

Etymology

From French 'catholicisme', from Late Latin 'catholicismus', from Greek 'katholikos' (universal).
