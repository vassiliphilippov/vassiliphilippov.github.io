smirk

1. NOUN

/ smɝk / (AmE), / smɜːk / (BrE)
A smile that shows smugness, self-satisfaction, or scorn rather than genuine pleasure.
a smug or scornful smile

Examples

She gave a sly smirk when she realized she was right.
His smirk betrayed his true feelings about the situation.
The teacher noticed a smirk on his face after the prank was revealed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grin - A broad smile, often showing the teeth
  • Antonym: frown - A facial expression showing displeasure or concern

Etymology

Probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian smirka (to smile smugly).

2. VERB

/ smɝk / (AmE), / smɜːk / (BrE)
to smile in a self-satisfied, smug, or irritatingly knowing way, often expressing smugness or scorn
smile smugly or derisively

Examples

She couldn't help but smirk when her rival tripped onstage.
He smirked at the question, clearly pleased with himself.
Stop smirking; it's not as clever as you think.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grin - to smile broadly, showing the teeth
  • Antonym: scowl - to frown in an angry or bad-tempered way

Etymology

Probably from Middle Low German smirken (to laugh or smile) or related Scandinavian words.
