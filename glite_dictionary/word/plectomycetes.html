Plectomycetes

1. NOUN

/ plɛk.toʊ.maɪˈsiːtiːz / (AmE), / plɛk.təʊ.maɪˈsiːtiːz / (BrE)
A class of fungi characterized by producing a closed fruiting body known as a cleistothecium, which releases spores only upon decay or disintegration.
class of fungi with closed fruiting bodies

Examples

The class Plectomycetes includes fungi with distinctive closed fruiting bodies called cleistothecia.
Researchers studying Plectomycetes have discovered new species in decaying plant matter.
Many members of the Plectomycetes are important for their role in the decomposition of organic material.

Etymology

From Greek 'plektos' (twisted, woven) + 'mycetes' (fungi).
