to improve

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈpruv / (AmE), / ɪmˈpruːv / (BrE)
to become better, or to make something become better
to become or make better

Examples

The company's performance began to improve after the new CEO took over.
Her health continued to improve throughout the week, much to everyone's relief.
Conditions in the city have improved significantly since the new policies were introduced.

Synset Examples

The weather improved toward evening

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to worsen - to become or make worse
  • Synonym: to enhance - to increase or further improve in quality, value, or extent

Etymology

From Latin 'improbare' via Old French 'emprouer', meaning to make better or more profitable.

2. VERB

/ ɪmˈpruv / (AmE), / ɪmˈpruːv / (BrE)
to make something better or to become better
to make or become better

Examples

She worked hard to improve her English skills before moving abroad.
Regular exercise can improve your overall health and well-being.
The company made several changes to improve customer service last year.

Synset Examples

The editor improved the manuscript with his changes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enhance - to increase or improve in value, quality, or attractiveness
  • Antonym: to worsen - to make or become worse

Etymology

From Latin 'improvare', from 'in-' (in, on) + 'probus' (good).
