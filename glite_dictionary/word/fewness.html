fewness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːnəs / (AmE), / ˈfjuːnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being small in number; scarcity or insufficiency in quantity.
small number; scarcity

Examples

The fewness of participants made it easy to organize the event.
Historians note the fewness of surviving documents from that period.
Despite the fewness of options, we managed to find a suitable solution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarcity - the state of being scarce or in short supply
  • Antonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something

Etymology

From 'few' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality.
