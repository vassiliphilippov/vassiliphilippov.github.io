perverse

1. ADJ

/ pərˈvɜrs / (AmE), / pəˈvɜːs / (BrE)
Deliberately behaving in a way that is unreasonable or resistant to guidance or discipline.
resistant to guidance or discipline

Examples

Despite knowing the consequences, he adopted a perverse attitude and refused to follow the rules.
Her perverse refusal to accept help frustrated everyone in the group.
In a perverse mood, the child ignored every instruction given to him.

Synset Examples

Mary, Mary, quite contrary
an obstinate child with a violent temper
a perverse mood
wayward behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrary - deliberately doing the opposite of what is expected or desired
  • Synonym: wayward - difficult to control because of willful or perverse behavior
  • Antonym: obedient - willing to comply with orders or requests

Etymology

From Latin perversus (turned away, contrary), past participle of pervertere (to turn around, corrupt).

2. ADJ

/ pərˈvɜːrs / (AmE), / pəˈvɜːs / (BrE)
Showing a deliberate and obstinate desire to behave in a way that is unreasonable or morally wrong; departing from what is considered right or good.
deliberately unreasonable or morally wrong

Examples

The judge was frustrated by the perverse behavior of the defendant in the courtroom.
It was a perverse decision to reward dishonesty rather than hard work.
His perverse enjoyment of other people's discomfort disturbed everyone at the meeting.

Synset Examples

depraved criminals
a perverted sense of loyalty
the reprobate conduct of a gambling aristocrat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depraved - morally corrupt or wicked
  • Antonym: virtuous - having or showing high moral standards

Etymology

From Latin perversus (turned away, contrary), past participle of pervertere (to turn around, corrupt).

3. ADJ

/ pərˈvɜrs / (AmE), / pəˈvɜːs / (BrE)
Showing a deliberate and unreasonable desire to behave in a way that is contrary to what is expected or accepted, often by opposing or contradicting others.
deliberately contrary or unreasonable

Examples

She took a perverse pleasure in rejecting every suggestion he made.
His perverse attitude often led to unnecessary arguments with his colleagues.
Despite knowing the right answer, he gave a perverse response just to be difficult.

Synset Examples

took perverse satisfaction in foiling her plans

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrary - tending to oppose or disagree
  • Synonym: obstinate - stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or course of action
  • Antonym: cooperative - willing to work together with others

Etymology

From Latin perversus (turned the wrong way, contrary), from pervertere (to turn around, corrupt).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01824324-s
Synset POS: s
