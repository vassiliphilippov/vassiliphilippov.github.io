ritz

1. NOUN

/ rɪts / (AmE), / rɪts / (BrE)
an ostentatious display of luxury, elegance, or glamour, often considered showy or pretentious
ostentatious luxury or elegance

Examples

She likes to add a little ritz to her dinner parties with fine china and elegant decor.
The hotel is full of ritz, attracting celebrities and wealthy tourists from all over the world.
Despite the ritz of the event, he felt out of place in the grand ballroom.

Synset Examples

they put on the ritz

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glamour - attractive or exciting quality that makes something seem special or desirable
  • Synonym: luxury - a state of great comfort or elegance, especially when involving great expense
  • Antonym: plainness - the quality of being simple or ordinary

Etymology

From the Ritz Hotel in Paris (opened 1898 by César Ritz), famed for its luxury and elegance.
