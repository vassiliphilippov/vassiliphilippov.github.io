unsavoriness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈseɪvəri.nəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈseɪvəri.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being extremely unpleasant or unpalatable to taste or smell
extreme unpleasantness in taste or smell

Examples

The unsavoriness of the spoiled soup made it impossible to swallow.
Guests complained about the unsavoriness of the dish served at the banquet.
Due to its unsavoriness, the mixture was rejected by the taste testers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: palatability - quality of being pleasant to the taste
  • Antonym: savoriness - the quality of being pleasant in taste or smell

Etymology

From un- (not) + savor + -iness.

2. NOUN

/ ʌnˈseɪvərinəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈseɪvərɪnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being morally or mentally unpleasant or unacceptable
unpleasant or unacceptable quality

Examples

The unsavoriness of the proposal shocked everyone in the room.
Rumors of unsavoriness surrounded the politician's business dealings.
Her refusal to compromise reflected her discomfort with any hint of unsavoriness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unsavory - unpleasant in character or taste
  • Synonym: disreputableness - the quality of being not respected or trusted
  • Antonym: acceptability - the quality of being acceptable

Etymology

From 'unsavory' (unpleasant, distasteful) + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting a state or quality).
