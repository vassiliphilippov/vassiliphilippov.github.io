equipotent

1. ADJ

/ ˌiːkwɪˈpoʊtənt / (AmE), / ˌiːkwɪˈpəʊtənt / (BrE)
having equal strength, power, or effect; having the same potency or efficacy as something else
having equal strength or power

Examples

In the experiment, the two chemicals proved to be equipotent in their effects on bacterial growth.
Mathematicians consider two sets equipotent if there is a one-to-one correspondence between their elements.
The drug's generic and branded versions are equipotent, offering the same level of efficacy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equal - having the same value, amount, or effect

Etymology

From Latin 'aequipotens', from 'aequus' (equal) + 'potens' (powerful).
