fascist

1. ADJ

/ ˈfæʃɪst / (AmE), / ˈfæʃɪst / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of fascism or fascists, especially in its authoritarian, nationalistic, or dictatorial aspects.
of or relating to fascism

Examples

The new law was criticized as fascist by many human rights groups.
Historians study the fascist regimes that rose to power in the early twentieth century.
The film depicts a society under fascist control.

Synset Examples

fascist propaganda

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authoritarian - favouring or enforcing strict obedience to authority

Etymology

From Italian *fascista* (supporter of Fascism), from *fascio* (group, bundle).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02932082-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈfæʃɪst / (AmE), / ˈfæʃɪst / (BrE)
A person who supports or advocates fascism or other authoritarian and dictatorial ideologies.
supporter of fascism or authoritarianism

Examples

He was accused of being a fascist because of his authoritarian policies.
Several countries suffered under fascist rule during the 20th century.
Calling him a fascist sparked a heated debate among the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dictator - A ruler with total power over a country, typically one who has obtained control by force.
  • Antonym: democrat - A person who supports democracy or democratic government.

Etymology

From Italian *fascista*, from *fascio* (bundle, group), referring to the symbol of authority in ancient Rome.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10099673-n
Synset POS: n
