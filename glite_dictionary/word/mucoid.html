mucoid

1. ADJ

/ ˈmjuː.kɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈmjuː.kɔɪd / (BrE)
resembling or relating to mucus, especially in appearance or consistency.
resembling or relating to mucus
medical

Examples

The lab technician observed a mucoid layer on the bacterial colony.
Some respiratory infections produce a thick, mucoid sputum.
Cystic fibrosis patients often have mucoid secretions in their lungs.

Synset Examples

a mucoid substance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mucous - relating to, producing, or covered with mucus

Etymology

From Greek 'myxa' (mucus) + '-oid' (resembling).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈmjuːkɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈmjuːkɔɪd / (BrE)
any of several glycoproteins similar to mucin, often found in bodily secretions and connective tissues
glycoprotein similar to mucin
medical

Examples

The scientist analyzed the chemical properties of the extracted mucoid.
Certain bacteria are able to produce a mucoid that helps them evade the immune system.
In histology, the presence of mucoid in tissues can be a significant diagnostic marker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mucin - a glycoprotein that is the principal constituent of mucus

Etymology

From Greek 'myxa' (mucus) + English '-oid' (resembling or like).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14980166-n
Synset POS: n
