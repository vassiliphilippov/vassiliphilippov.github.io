breechblock

1. NOUN

/ ˈbriːtʃˌblɑk / (AmE), / ˈbriːtʃˌblɒk / (BrE)
a metal block in a breech-loading firearm that is moved to allow loading and then closes the breech for firing
metal block in breech-loading firearms

Examples

The breechblock must be locked in place before the gun can be fired.
After cleaning, he carefully slid the breechblock back into the rifle.
A damaged breechblock can make a firearm unsafe to use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bolt - sliding metal bar used in some firearms to seal the breech

Etymology

From 'breech' (the rear part of a firearm) + 'block'.
