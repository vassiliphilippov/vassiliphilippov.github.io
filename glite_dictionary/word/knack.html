knack

1. NOUN

/ næk / (AmE), / næk / (BrE)
an acquired or natural skill at performing a particular task, often involving a special way of doing it
special skill or talent

Examples

She has a knack for making people feel comfortable right away.
He quickly developed a knack for solving complex problems under pressure.
Despite his efforts, he never quite got the knack of cooking pasta al dente.

Synset Examples

he had a bent for it
he had a special knack for getting into trouble
he couldn't get the hang of it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talent - natural aptitude or skill
  • Synonym: skill - the ability to do something well
  • Antonym: ineptitude - lack of skill or ability

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English *knak* (a sharp blow, a trick), possibly of imitative origin.
