whooper

1. NOUN

/ ˈwuːpər / (AmE), / ˈwuːpə / (BrE)
A rare North American crane with black-and-white plumage and a distinctive trumpeting call.
rare North American crane

Examples

Researchers spotted a whooper nesting near the lake this spring.
The loud, trumpeting call of the whooper can be heard for miles across the marshes.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the endangered whooper.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhuːpər / (AmE), / ˈhuːpə / (BrE)
a large Old World swan (Cygnus cygnus) noted for its loud, whooping call, native to Eurasia.
a swan with a loud, whooping call

Examples

The lake was home to a family of whooper swans during the winter months.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a whooper among the migrating flocks.
The distinctive call of the whooper echoed across the marsh at dawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whooper swan - another term for the whooper, the common Old World swan with a whooping call
