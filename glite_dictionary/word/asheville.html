Asheville

1. PROPN

/ ˈæʃˌvɪl / (AmE), / ˈæʃˌvɪl / (BrE)
A city in western North Carolina, situated in the Blue Ridge Mountains, known for its vibrant arts scene and historic architecture.
city in western North Carolina, USA

Examples

Many tourists visit Asheville each year to explore the Blue Ridge Mountains and local breweries.
After moving to Asheville, she quickly fell in love with its vibrant arts scene.
The Biltmore Estate is one of the most famous attractions in Asheville.

Etymology

Named after Samuel Ashe, a former governor of North Carolina.
