aviation

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ.viˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌeɪ.viˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the operation of aircraft, especially for the purpose of transporting people or goods
operation of aircraft for transportation

Examples

Advances in aviation have made it possible to travel across continents in hours.
The aviation industry has seen significant challenges in recent years due to rising fuel costs.
She has worked in aviation for over a decade, mainly focusing on flight operations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aeronautics - the science or practice of building and operating aircraft
  • Antonym: shipping - the transport of goods or people by sea

Etymology

From French 'aviation', from Latin 'avis' (bird).
