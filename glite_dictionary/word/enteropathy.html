enteropathy

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.təˈrɑː.pə.θi / (AmE), / ˌɛn.təˈrɒp.ə.θi / (BrE)
A disease or disorder affecting the intestinal tract.
disease of the intestinal tract
medical

Examples

Celiac disease is a type of enteropathy triggered by gluten intake.
The doctor suspected an enteropathy after the patient reported chronic gastrointestinal symptoms.
Certain medications can cause a rare form of enteropathy as a side effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gastroenteritis - inflammation of the stomach and intestines
  • Synonym: colitis - inflammation of the colon

Etymology

From Greek 'enteron' (intestine) + 'pathy' (disease, suffering).
