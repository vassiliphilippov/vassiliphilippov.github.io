spreader

1. NOUN

/ ˈsprɛdər / (AmE), / ˈsprɛdə / (BrE)
a machine or device used to scatter seeds, fertilizer, sand, or similar materials evenly over an area.
device for scattering materials

Examples

After the snowfall, the city used a spreader to evenly distribute sand on the roads.
The lawn spreader helped her plant grass seed quickly and efficiently.
Make sure the spreader is set correctly before using it to apply fertilizer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broadcast seeder - a device for spreading seeds broadly
  • Synonym: distributor - a device that distributes something

Etymology

From the verb 'spread' + agentive suffix '-er'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02906529-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈsprɛdər / (AmE), / ˈsprɛdə / (BrE)
a small tool used for spreading substances, such as butter or fertilizer, over a surface
tool used for spreading substances

Examples

He used a spreader to evenly apply the butter on his toast.
Make sure you clean the spreader after using it for fertilizer.
The picnic set includes a handy cheese spreader.

Synset Examples

he used his knife as a spreader

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knife - a utensil with a sharp blade used for cutting or as a spreader

Etymology

From the verb 'spread' + agent noun suffix '-er'.

WordNet ID: oewn-04295124-n
Synset POS: n
