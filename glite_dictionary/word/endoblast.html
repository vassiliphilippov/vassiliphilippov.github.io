endoblast

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.doʊˌblæst / (AmE), / ˈɛndəʊˌblɑːst / (BrE)
the innermost germ layer of an embryo, which develops into the lining of the digestive and respiratory systems
innermost germ layer in embryos
medical

Examples

During early embryonic development, the endoblast differentiates to form structures such as the gut lining.
Scientists study the endoblast to understand congenital disorders of the digestive tract.
The respiratory system is also derived from the endoblast in vertebrate embryos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endoderm - the innermost germ layer of an embryo

Etymology

From Greek 'endon' (within) + 'blastós' (germ, sprout).
