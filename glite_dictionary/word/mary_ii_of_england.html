Mary II of England

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɛri səˈkənd əv ˈɪŋɡlənd / (AmE), / ˈmɛːri səˈkənd ɒv ˈɪŋɡlənd / (BrE)
Queen of England, Scotland, and Ireland (1689–1694), who reigned jointly with her husband William III and was the eldest daughter of James II.
Queen of England (1689–1694), ruled with William III

Examples

Mary II of England reigned jointly with her husband William III during the Glorious Revolution.
The Bill of Rights was enacted during the rule of Mary II of England and William III.
Many historians study the unique co-monarchy of Mary II of England and her husband.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: William III of England - King of England who reigned jointly with Mary II
