sucrase

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːˌkreɪs / (AmE), / ˈsuːˌkreɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that catalyzes the breakdown of sucrose into glucose and fructose
enzyme that breaks down sucrose
medical

Examples

The small intestine produces sucrase to help digest table sugar.
A deficiency of sucrase can cause problems in sugar absorption.
Scientists isolated sucrase to study its role in carbohydrate metabolism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invertase - an enzyme that also catalyzes the hydrolysis of sucrose

Etymology

From French 'sucre' (sugar) + '-ase' (enzyme suffix).
