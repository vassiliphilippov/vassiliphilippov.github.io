Archaeornithes

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑrki.oʊˈnɪθiz / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪəʊˈnɪθiːz / (BrE)
a group of primitive, reptile-like fossil birds from the Jurassic or early Cretaceous periods, considered an early stage in avian evolution
primitive fossil birds of early avian evolution

Examples

The discovery of Archaeornithes fossils has shed light on the evolution of modern birds.
Paleontologists debate how Archaeornithes are related to both dinosaurs and contemporary birds.
Some characteristics of Archaeornithes closely resemble those of small theropod dinosaurs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Archaeopteryx - the best-known genus within Archaeornithes, a primitive fossil bird

Etymology

From Greek 'archaios' (ancient) + 'ornis' (bird), coined in scientific Latin.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrki.oʊˈnɪθiːz / (AmE), / ˌɑːki.əʊˈnɪθiːz / (BrE)
an extinct taxonomic subclass of primitive, reptile-like birds known from Jurassic or early Cretaceous fossils
extinct group of primitive fossil birds

Examples

Many paleontologists study the Archaeornithes to learn about the transition from dinosaurs to modern birds.
The Archaeornithes possessed both teeth and clawed wings, features not seen in today's birds.
Fossil evidence of Archaeornithes has greatly informed our understanding of avian evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Archaeopteryx - a genus of early prehistoric bird-like dinosaur, often classed within Archaeornithes

Etymology

From Greek arkhaíos (ancient) + ornithes (birds).
