psalmist

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːlmɪst / (AmE), / ˈsɑːlmɪst / (BrE)
a person who composes or sings psalms, especially one credited with writing biblical psalms
composer or singer of psalms

Examples

King David is often referred to as the psalmist in religious texts.
The psalmist wrote verses that are still sung in churches today.
Many believe the psalmist found inspiration through prayer and meditation.

Synset Examples

David is called The Psalmist because he is believed to be the author of the Book of Psalms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poet - a person who writes poetry

Etymology

From Old French psalmiste, from Late Latin psalmista, from Greek psalmistēs (player or singer of psalms).
