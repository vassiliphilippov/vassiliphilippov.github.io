enteron

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛntəˌrɑn / (AmE), / ˈɛntərɒn / (BrE)
the alimentary canal, especially in an embryo or in simple animals such as coelenterates
alimentary canal, esp. in embryos or simple animals
medical

Examples

The enteron of the embryo develops into the adult digestive tract.
Scientists observed the structure of the jellyfish's enteron under a microscope.
Damage to the enteron at this stage can affect the animal's ability to digest food later.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alimentary canal - the whole passage along which food passes through the body

Etymology

From Greek 'énteron' meaning 'intestine, gut'.
