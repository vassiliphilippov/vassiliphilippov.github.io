selflessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛlfləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɛlfləsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of caring more about the needs and wishes of others than about oneself
the quality of being selfless

Examples

Her selflessness during the crisis inspired everyone around her to offer help.
Acts of selflessness are highly valued in communities where cooperation is essential.
He was admired for his selflessness and willingness to put others first.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: altruism - the belief in or practice of disinterested and selfless concern for the well-being of others
  • Antonym: selfishness - the quality of caring only about yourself and not other people

Etymology

From 'selfless' + '-ness', where 'selfless' is from 'self' + '-less' (without).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛlfləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɛlfləsnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of caring more about other people's needs and wishes than your own; unselfishness.
quality of putting others' needs first

Examples

Her selflessness inspired everyone in the community to volunteer their time.
Acts of selflessness can make a real difference in times of crisis.
He was admired for his selflessness and willingness to help those in need.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: altruism - the principle or practice of selfless concern for the well-being of others
  • Antonym: selfishness - the quality or state of being concerned only with one's own advantage

Etymology

From 'selfless' + '-ness', modeled after 'selfless' (from 'self' + '-less').
