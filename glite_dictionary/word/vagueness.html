vagueness

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪɡ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈveɪɡ.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being unclear or lacking in precision, especially in expression or meaning
unclearness or lack of precision

Examples

The instructions were filled with vagueness, leaving everyone confused about what to do next.
She answered the question with deliberate vagueness to avoid committing herself.
Critics complained about the vagueness of the official statement on policy changes.

Synset Examples

the Conservative manifesto is a model of vagueness
these terms were used with a vagueness that suggested little or no thought about what each might convey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambiguity - uncertainty or inexactness of meaning
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear

Etymology

From vague + -ness; 'vague' from Latin 'vagus' (wandering, uncertain).

2. NOUN

/ ˈveɪɡ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈveɪɡ.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being indistinct, unclear, or lacking definite form or character
lack of clarity or distinctness

Examples

The vagueness of the outline made the figure difficult to recognize.
His instructions were marked by such vagueness that no one knew what to do.
Fog added a beautiful vagueness to the landscape, softening all the edges.

Synset Examples

the scene had the swirling vagueness of a painting by Turner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indistinctness - the quality of being unclear or not sharply defined
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to see or understand

Etymology

From Latin 'vagus' (wandering, uncertain) via English 'vague' + '-ness'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04712187-n
Synset POS: n
