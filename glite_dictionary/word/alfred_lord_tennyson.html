Alfred Lord Tennyson

1. PROPN

/ ˈælfrəd lɔrd ˈtɛnɪsən / (AmE), / ˈælfrɪd lɔːd ˈtɛnɪsən / (BrE)
(1809–1892) English poet who served as Poet Laureate of Great Britain and Ireland during much of Queen Victoria's reign, noted for works such as 'In Memoriam' and 'The Charge of the Light Brigade'.
English Victorian poet laureate (1809–1892)

Examples

Many students study Alfred Lord Tennyson when learning about Victorian literature.
The poem 'The Lady of Shalott' was written by Alfred Lord Tennyson.
Queen Victoria appointed Alfred Lord Tennyson as Poet Laureate in 1850.

Etymology

The surname 'Tennyson' is a patronymic meaning 'son of Tenney'.
