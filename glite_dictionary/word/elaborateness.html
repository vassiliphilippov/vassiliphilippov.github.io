elaborateness

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlæbərət.nəs / (AmE), / ɪˈlæb(ə)rət.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being intricate, detailed, or carefully worked out.
the quality of being detailed or intricate

Examples

The elaborateness of the wedding decorations amazed all the guests.
She admired the elaborateness of the clock's design, with its intricate gears and carvings.
The elaborateness of his explanation made the topic seem more complicated than it was.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: simplicity - the quality of being simple or uncomplicated
  • Synonym: complexity - the state of being complex or complicated

Etymology

From 'elaborate' + '-ness', with 'elaborate' from Latin 'elaboratus' (worked out carefully).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌlæbərətnəs / (AmE), / ɪˌlæbərətnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being elaborately or excessively decorated or detailed
the state of being very ornate or detailed

Examples

The elaborateness of the palace's interior was breathtaking, with gold accents and intricate patterns everywhere.
Some critics felt that the elaborateness of the design overwhelmed its original simplicity.
He admired the elaborateness of her wedding gown, covered in detailed lace and tiny pearls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ornateness - the quality of being highly decorated or ornate
  • Antonym: simplicity - the quality of being simple or plain; lack of ornamentation

Etymology

From 'elaborate' + '-ness'; 'elaborate' from Latin 'elaboratus', past participle of 'elaborare' (to work out).
