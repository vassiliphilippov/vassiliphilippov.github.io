zoophyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈzoʊ.əˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈzəʊ.əˌfaɪt / (BrE)
an invertebrate animal, such as a sea anemone, coral, or sponge, that resembles a plant in appearance
plant-like invertebrate animal

Examples

The delicate branching structure of the zoophyte fascinated the marine biologists.
Early naturalists debated whether the zoophyte was truly an animal or a plant.
Many corals are classified as zoophytes due to their plant-like appearance.

Etymology

From Greek zōion (animal) + phyton (plant).
