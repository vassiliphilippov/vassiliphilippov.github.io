greenbelt

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrinˌbɛlt / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnbɛlt / (BrE)
An area of parks or rural land surrounding a town or city, intended to limit urban expansion.
belt of rural land around a city

Examples

Many cities have established a greenbelt to control urban growth and preserve natural habitats.
The hiking trail runs through the greenbelt that encircles the town.
Property values tend to be higher in neighborhoods adjacent to the greenbelt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parkland - an area of open land with grass and trees, often for public recreation
  • Antonym: urban sprawl - the uncontrolled expansion of urban areas

Etymology

From 'green' + 'belt', referring to a ring or area of undeveloped land.
