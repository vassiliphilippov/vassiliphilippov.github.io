copal

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊ.pəl / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.pəl / (BrE)
a hard, brittle, and aromatic resin from various tropical trees, used chiefly in making varnishes and lacquers
brittle resin used in varnishes

Examples

The artisan applied a layer of copal varnish to give the sculpture a gleaming finish.
Traders imported copal from Central America to meet the high demand of furniture makers.
Some antique paintings have a distinctive shine due to the use of copal in their protective coatings.

Etymology

From Spanish *copal*, from Nahuatl *copalli* (resin).
