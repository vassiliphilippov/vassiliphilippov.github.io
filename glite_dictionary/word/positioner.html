positioner

1. NOUN

/ pəˈzɪʃənər / (AmE), / pəˈzɪʃənə / (BrE)
(computing) a device or actuator that moves a read/write head to the correct data track in a storage device.
device moving read/write head to data track

Examples

The positioner ensures the read/write head aligns precisely with the data track.
If the positioner fails, the hard drive cannot access the correct information.
Modern drives use a highly responsive positioner to increase data access speed.

Etymology

From 'position' + '-er', indicating 'one who or that which positions'.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈzɪʃ.ən.ɚ / (AmE), / pəˈzɪʃ.ən.ə / (BrE)
a device that regulates the position of an actuator by controlling its opening or closing in response to electric or pneumatic signals
device controlling an actuator's position

Examples

The positioner ensures that the valve operates at the correct angle for optimal flow.
Routine maintenance was performed on the actuator and its positioner to prevent system failures.
In automated systems, a pneumatic positioner receives signals to adjust machinery precisely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: controller - a device that manages or regulates the behavior of another device

Etymology

From 'position' + '-er', denoting a device that positions.
