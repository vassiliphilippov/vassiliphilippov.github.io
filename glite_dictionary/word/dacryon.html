dacryon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdækriˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈdækrɪɒn / (BrE)
the point on the human skull where the anterior border of the lacrimal bone meets the frontal bone
craniometric point in skull anatomy
medical

Examples

The surgeon identified the dacryon to guide the placement of the incision.
Precise measurement from the dacryon is important in facial reconstructive surgery.
Anatomists often use the dacryon as a landmark in skull studies.

Etymology

From Greek dakryon (tear), due to its proximity to the tear duct.
