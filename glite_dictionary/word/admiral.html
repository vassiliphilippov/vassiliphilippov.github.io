admiral

1. NOUN

/ ˈæd.mɚ.əl / (AmE), / ˈæd.mɪ.rəl / (BrE)
the highest-ranking officer in a navy after a fleet admiral; supreme commander of a fleet, ranking above a vice admiral
senior naval officer; commander of a fleet

Examples

The admiral inspected the ships before they set sail for their mission.
Her grandfather once served as an admiral in the navy.
During the ceremony, the admiral received a prestigious award for his years of leadership.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: vice admiral - naval officer ranking just below admiral
  • Degree Higher: fleet admiral - the highest naval rank, above admiral

Etymology

From Arabic 'amīr al-' (commander of), via Medieval Latin 'admiralis' and Old French 'amiral'.
