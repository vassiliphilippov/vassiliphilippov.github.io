Charles I of England

1. PROPN

/ tʃɑrlz ðə fɜrst əv ˈɪŋɡlənd / (AmE), / tʃɑːlz ði ˈfɜːst əv ˈɪŋɡlənd / (BrE)
(1600–1649) King of England, Scotland and Ireland from 1625 until his execution after being deposed by Oliver Cromwell.
King of England (1625–1649), executed after Civil War

Examples

Charles I of England ascended the throne in 1625 after the death of his father, James I.
The execution of Charles I of England marked a turning point in British history.
Many historians debate the legacy of Charles I of England and his role in the English Civil War.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Charles II of England - King of England, Scotland and Ireland who succeeded Charles I (his son)
