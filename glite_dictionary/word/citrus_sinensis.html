Citrus sinensis

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtrəs sɪˈnɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɪtrəs sɪˈnɛnsɪs / (BrE)
the sweet orange tree, widely cultivated for its fruit used as table and juice oranges
the sweet orange tree

Examples

The majority of commercial oranges are produced by Citrus sinensis plantations in subtropical regions.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Citrus sinensis under varying irrigation methods.
Juice made from Citrus sinensis is enjoyed worldwide for its sweet flavor and high vitamin C content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orange tree - a tree that bears oranges

Etymology

From Latin, 'citrus' (citron) and 'sinensis' (from China).
