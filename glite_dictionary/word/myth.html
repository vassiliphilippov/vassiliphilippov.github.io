myth

1. NOUN

/ mɪθ / (AmE), / mɪθ / (BrE)
a traditional story, especially one concerning the early history of a people or explaining a natural or social phenomenon, and typically involving supernatural beings or events
traditional story explaining beliefs or phenomena

Examples

Many ancient cultures have a creation myth to explain the origins of the world.
The story of King Arthur has become a national myth in Britain.
The thunder god was an important figure in Norse myth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legend - a traditional story sometimes believed to be historical but not authenticated
  • Synonym: folktale - a traditional narrative passed orally among common people
  • Antonym: truth - something that is true or in accordance with fact or reality

Etymology

From Greek mythos (speech, story, tale, legend).

2. NOUN

/ mɪθ / (AmE), / mɪθ / (BrE)
A widely held but false belief or idea; a traditional story or widely accepted misconception.
widely held false belief; traditional story

Examples

The idea that you need to drink eight glasses of water a day is a myth.
Many people still believe the myth that lightning never strikes the same place twice.
The documentary aims to debunk several financial myths about investing.

Synset Examples

They laughed about the myths surrounding the G-spot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legend - a traditional story sometimes popularly regarded as historical but unauthenticated
  • Antonym: fact - a thing that is known or proved to be true; the opposite of a myth as a misconception

Etymology

From Greek mŷthos (speech, story, legend, myth).
