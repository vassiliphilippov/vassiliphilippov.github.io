fissiparity

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɪsɪˈpærɪti / (AmE), / ˌfɪsɪˈparɪti / (BrE)
A form of asexual reproduction in which an organism divides into two or more parts, each of which develops into a separate individual.
reproduction by division in organisms
medical

Examples

Some starfish are capable of fissiparity, regenerating missing limbs after splitting in two.
In certain environments, fissiparity can be a highly effective mode of reproduction for survival.
Scientists observed fissiparity in the laboratory population of planarian worms.

Etymology

From Latin 'fissus' (split) and '-parity' (from 'parere', to produce or give birth).

2. NOUN

/ ˌfɪsəˈpærəti / (AmE), / ˌfɪsɪˈparɪti / (BrE)
The tendency or process of splitting or breaking into parts, or of dividing into separate entities.
tendency to split into parts

Examples

Some historians argue that political fissiparity led to the collapse of the empire.
Biologists observe fissiparity in certain simple organisms that reproduce by splitting.
The fissiparity of religious movements can result in many distinct sects over time.

Synset Examples

the fissiparity of religious sects

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragmentation - the process or state of breaking or being broken into small or separate parts
  • Antonym: cohesion - the act or state of sticking together; unity

Etymology

From Latin 'fissiparus', from 'fissus' (split) + '-parus' (bearing).
