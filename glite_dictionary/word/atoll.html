atoll

1. NOUN

/ ˈæt̬.ɑl / (AmE), / ˈæt.ɒl / (BrE)
a ring-shaped island or group of islets formed of coral enclosing a lagoon, often in tropical oceans
ring-shaped coral island with lagoon

Examples

The Maldives are famous for their beautiful atolls and turquoise lagoons.
Marine biologists studied the unique ecosystem within the coral atoll.
Aerial views reveal the stunning circular shape of the atoll surrounding the lagoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: islet - a small island
  • Degree Lower: reef - a ridge of jagged rock, coral, or sand just above or below the surface of the sea
  • Degree Higher: island - a piece of land surrounded by water

Etymology

From Maldivian (Divehi) 'atholhu', meaning 'island'.
