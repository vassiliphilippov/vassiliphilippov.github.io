Guinea

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡɪni / (AmE), / ˈɡɪni / (BrE)
A country in western Africa on the Atlantic coast; formerly a French colony, it became independent in 1958.
country in western Africa

Examples

She traveled to Guinea to work on a humanitarian project.
The capital of Guinea is Conakry, a vibrant and bustling city.
Since gaining independence in 1958, Guinea has experienced various political changes.

Etymology

From the Portuguese 'Guiné', denoting the West African region south of the Senegal River.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪni / (AmE), / ˈɡɪni / (BrE)
An offensive term for a person of Italian descent.
offensive slur for Italian person
offensive

Examples

He was angry when someone called him a Guinea on the street.
The movie was criticized for its use of the word Guinea to describe Italian immigrants.
She warned her friends never to use the term Guinea as it is highly offensive.

Etymology

Probably from 'Guinea,' formerly used for African-born slaves and later applied derogatorily to people from southern Europe, including Italians.
