skittish

1. ADJ

/ ˈskɪtɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈskɪtɪʃ / (BrE)
(especially of animals or people) easily excited or frightened; inclined to be unpredictable or nervous.
easily excitable or nervous (esp. animals)

Examples

The horse became skittish when it heard the loud noise nearby.
Young puppies can be quite skittish around strangers at first.
After the recent changes, investors have grown skittish about the stock market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nervous - easily agitated or alarmed
  • Synonym: timid - showing a lack of courage or confidence; easily frightened
  • Antonym: bold - showing courage; not easily frightened

Etymology

Probably from the North Germanic root of Norwegian dialect *skyt* (to jump up), suggesting rapid movement.
