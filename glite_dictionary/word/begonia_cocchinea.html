Begonia cocchinea

1. NOUN

/ bəˈɡoʊniə kɒkˈsɪniə / (AmE), / bɪˈɡəʊnɪə kɒkˈsɪniə / (BrE)
A species of South American begonia with fibrous roots, prominent basal leaf lobes resembling angel wings, and racemes of coral-red flowers.
a South American begonia species with coral-red flowers

Examples

The botanical garden recently added a beautiful Begonia cocchinea to its tropical plant collection.
Gardeners admire the striking red blooms of the Begonia cocchinea during the late spring.
The large, angel wing-shaped leaves distinguish the Begonia cocchinea from other begonias.

Etymology

From genus name 'Begonia' (after French botanist Michel Bégon) and Latin 'cocchinea', meaning 'scarlet-colored'.
