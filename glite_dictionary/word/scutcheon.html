scutcheon

1. NOUN

/ ˈskʌtʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈskʌtʃ.ən / (BrE)
a shield, especially one bearing a coat of arms, used in heraldry
heraldic shield displaying a coat of arms

Examples

The family's ancient scutcheon was proudly displayed above the fireplace.
In medieval times, knights often painted their scutcheon with intricate symbols representing their lineage.
The museum's exhibit featured a collection of scutcheons from various noble houses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escutcheon - heraldic shield or emblem bearing a coat of arms

Etymology

From Old French escuchon, from Latin scūtum (shield).

2. NOUN

/ ˈskʌtʃən / (AmE), / ˈskʌtʃ.ən / (BrE)
A flat plate or protective covering, especially around a keyhole or door handle, designed to prevent soiling or damage to the surrounding surface.
protective covering around a keyhole or handle

Examples

He replaced the old scutcheon on the front door to prevent fingerprints from marking the wood.
The brass scutcheon around the keyhole gleamed in the sunlight.
Please ensure the scutcheon matches the new doorknob in both color and style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escutcheon - a flat protective or ornamental plate on a door, often around a keyhole or door handle

Etymology

From Old French 'escuçon', from Latin 'scutio' (small shield).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
