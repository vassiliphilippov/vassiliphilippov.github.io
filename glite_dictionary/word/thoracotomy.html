thoracotomy

1. NOUN

/ ˌθɔːrəˈkɑːtəmi / (AmE), / ˌθɔːrəˈkɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical incision into the chest wall to gain access to the organs in the thoracic cavity, especially the lungs or heart
surgical incision into the chest wall
medical

Examples

The surgeon performed a thoracotomy to remove the damaged portion of the patient’s lung.
A thoracotomy may be required in cases of severe chest trauma to control internal bleeding.
Recovery from a thoracotomy can take several weeks and often involves significant pain management.

Etymology

From Greek 'thōraks' (chest) and '-tomy' (cutting).
