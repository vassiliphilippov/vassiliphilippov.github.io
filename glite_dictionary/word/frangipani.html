frangipani

1. NOUN

/ ˌfrændʒəˈpæni / (AmE), / ˌfræn.dʒɪˈpɑː.ni / (BrE)
a tropical American shrub or small tree of the genus Plumeria, with fragrant, colorful, funnel-shaped flowers and milky sap
a tropical shrub or tree with fragrant flowers

Examples

In the warm evening air, the scent of frangipani drifted through the garden.
A single frangipani blossom lay on the stone path, its colors bright against the grey.
They planted a frangipani tree by the front porch for its beautiful flowers and sweet fragrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plumeria - the genus to which frangipani belongs

Etymology

From Italian 'frangipani', said to be named after a 16th-century Italian nobleman, the Marquis Frangipani.
