unbalanced

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈbælənst / (AmE), / ʌnˈbælənst / (BrE)
(of an account or statement) having debits and credits that are not equal.
account with unequal debits and credits

Examples

The accountant noticed that the trial balance remained unbalanced after several checks.
An unbalanced ledger indicates an error in the financial records.
If a company’s books are unbalanced, it may signal a problem that requires immediate attention.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: balanced - having debits and credits that are equal (in accounting)
Source: WordNet

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈbælənst / (AmE), / ʌnˈbælənst / (BrE)
not in a state of equilibrium; lacking balance physically or figuratively
not in equilibrium; lacking balance

Examples

The washing machine became unbalanced during the spin cycle and started making a loud noise.
His unbalanced diet led to health problems over time.
After hearing the shocking news, her emotions were completely unbalanced.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unstable - liable to change; not stable
  • Antonym: balanced - being in a state of equilibrium
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00898050-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈbælənst / (AmE), / ʌnˈbælənst / (BrE)
(of a person) affected with madness or insanity; not mentally stable.
affected with madness or mental instability

Examples

Some people thought the artist was unbalanced, but others admired his creativity.
After the traumatic event, his behavior became increasingly unbalanced.
The character in the novel is portrayed as deeply unbalanced and unpredictable.

Synset Examples

a man who had gone mad

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insane - mentally ill; seriously disturbed
  • Antonym: sane - having a healthy mind; not insane

Etymology

From the prefix 'un-' meaning 'not' + 'balanced', in reference to mental equilibrium.
