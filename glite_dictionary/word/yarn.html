yarn

1. NOUN

/ jɑrn / (AmE), / jɑːn / (BrE)
A continuous strand of twisted fibers, such as cotton, silk, wool, or synthetic material, used for sewing, weaving, or knitting.
thread or strand used in textile crafts

Examples

She chose a bright red yarn to knit her new scarf.
The yarn is spun from natural wool and dyed in vibrant colors.
After untangling the yarn, she started weaving a blanket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thread - a fine cord of twisted fibers used especially for sewing

Etymology

From Old English 'garn', related to Old Norse 'garn' (yarn, gut, sinew).

2. VERB

/ jɑrn / (AmE), / jɑːn / (BrE)
to tell a long or imaginative story, often one that is exaggerated or implausible
tell a tall or fanciful story
informal

Examples

After dinner, Grandpa loved to yarn about his adventures at sea.
The campers sat around the fire and began to yarn late into the night.
She could yarn so well that everyone believed her wildest stories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: story - an account of imaginary or real people and events told for entertainment
  • Synonym: tale - a narrative that relates the details of some real or imaginary event, incident, or case; story

Etymology

Originally meaning 'thread or spun fiber', the sense 'story' evolved in the mid-19th century, likely from the notion of spinning tales as one spins yarn.

3. NOUN

/ jɑrn / (AmE), / jɑːn / (BrE)
a long or imaginative story, often one that is exaggerated or difficult to believe
a long or imaginative story

Examples

He told a fascinating yarn about his adventures as a sailor.
Grandpa is famous for spinning a good yarn at every family gathering.
The journalist was skeptical of the politician's wild yarn about his past.

Synset Examples

his narration was hesitant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tale - a story, especially one that is imaginatively recounted
  • Synonym: story - an account of imaginary or real people and events told for entertainment

Etymology

Originally meaning 'spun fiber,' later extended in the 19th century (Australia/New Zealand English) to mean 'story,' partly by association with spinning tales.
