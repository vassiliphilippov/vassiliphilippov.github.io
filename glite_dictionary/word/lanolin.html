lanolin

1. NOUN

/ ˈlænəlɪn / (AmE), / ˈlanəlɪn / (BrE)
a yellow, waxy substance obtained from sheep's wool, used in ointments, cosmetics, and skin creams
waxy substance from wool used in cosmetics

Examples

The new moisturizer contains lanolin to help soothe dry skin.
Many lip balms use lanolin as a natural softening agent.
She checked the label for lanolin because of her wool allergy.

Etymology

From Latin 'lana' (wool) + 'oleum' (oil).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlænəlɪn / (AmE), / ˈlanəlɪn / (BrE)
a yellowish, fatty substance obtained from sheep's wool, used as an ingredient in lotions and ointments to soften and protect the skin
fatty substance from sheep's wool, used in skin products

Examples

The hand cream contains lanolin to help heal dry, cracked skin.
Some people are allergic to lanolin, a common ingredient in lotions and ointments.
Pure lanolin can be applied directly to the skin for intense moisturizing.

Etymology

From Latin 'lāna' (wool) + 'oleum' (oil).
