Sitta canadensis

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtə kænəˈdɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɪtə kanəˈdɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A small bluish-grey nuthatch with a reddish breast, found in northern coniferous forests of North America; also known as the red-breasted nuthatch.
species of nuthatch (bird) in North America

Examples

The Sitta canadensis is commonly found in pine and spruce forests across Canada and the northern United States.
During migration, Sitta canadensis can occasionally be seen in suburban gardens seeking food.
Birdwatchers often identify Sitta canadensis by its distinctive nasal call and acrobatic feeding habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red-breasted nuthatch - A small North American nuthatch with a reddish breast, common name for Sitta canadensis

Etymology

From Latin: genus Sitta (nuthatches) and species name canadensis (of Canada).
