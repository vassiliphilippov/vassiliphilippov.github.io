brunette

1. NOUN

/ bruːˈnɛt / (AmE), / bruːˈnɛt / (BrE)
a person, usually a woman or girl, with dark brown or black hair
person with dark brown or black hair

Examples

She was a striking brunette with bright green eyes and a confident smile.
Three brunettes and two blondes auditioned for the lead role.
As a natural brunette, he rarely dyes his hair.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: blonde - a person with pale yellow or golden hair

Etymology

From French 'brunette', feminine of 'brunet', diminutive of 'brun' (brown).

WordNet ID: oewn-09897322-n

2. ADJ

/ bruˈnɛt / (AmE), / bruːˈnɛt / (BrE)
(of a person) having dark or relatively dark hair, skin, or eyes.
having dark hair, skin, or eyes

Examples

She admired the actress’s classic brunette features on the red carpet.
The brunette child stood out among her fair-haired classmates.
With her brunette hair and olive skin, she turned heads everywhere she went.

Synset Examples

a brunette beauty

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fair - having light hair or complexion

Etymology

From French 'brunet', diminutive of 'brun' (brown).
