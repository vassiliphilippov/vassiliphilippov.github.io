chickpea

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪkˌpi / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪk.piː / (BrE)
a round, pale-yellow seed of an Asiatic plant, used as food, especially when dried; also known as garbanzo bean.
edible legume seed; garbanzo bean

Examples

She added cooked chickpeas to the salad for extra protein.
Hummus is traditionally made from mashed chickpeas, tahini, and lemon juice.
A bag of dried chickpeas can last for months in the pantry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garbanzo - another name for the chickpea

Etymology

From early 18th century, from 'chick', alteration of 'chiche' (French, from Latin 'cicer'), and 'pea'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪkˌpi / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪk.piː / (BrE)
a round, pale-yellow seed of a plant of the legume family, commonly used as food, especially in Mediterranean and Middle Eastern dishes
an edible seed of a legume plant

Examples

She added chickpeas to the salad for extra protein and flavor.
Hummus is traditionally made with blended chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, and garlic.
After soaking the chickpeas overnight, he cooked them for a delicious stew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garbanzo - another name for chickpea, especially in American English

Etymology

From Spanish *garbanzo* or earlier French *pois chiche*, from Latin *cicer* (chickpea).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪkˌpi / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪk.piː / (BrE)
A plant of the pea family, cultivated for its edible round yellowish seed, commonly used as food.
edible seed and plant (Cicer arietinum)

Examples

She added chickpeas to the salad for extra protein and texture.
The recipe calls for soaking the chickpeas overnight before cooking.
Farmers in India cultivate chickpeas as a major source of food and income.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garbanzo - another name for chickpea, especially in American English

Etymology

From Spanish 'garbanzo' or earlier from French 'chiche' and 'pois', ultimately from Latin 'cicer' (pea).
