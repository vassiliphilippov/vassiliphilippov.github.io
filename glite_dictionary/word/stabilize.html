to stabilize

1. VERB

/ ˈsteɪbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsteɪbəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something steady or unlikely to change, or to stop it from becoming less steady
make stable; prevent changes

Examples

Central banks often intervene to stabilize currency exchange rates during periods of volatility.
After adding extra supports, the engineers managed to stabilize the bridge structure.
Doctors worked quickly to stabilize the patient after her condition suddenly deteriorated.

Synset Examples

The drug stabilized her blood pressure
stabilize prices

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to destabilize - make unstable or likely to change
  • Synonym: steady - to make or become steady

Etymology

From French *stabiliser*, from Latin *stabilis* (stable, firm).

2. VERB

/ ˈsteɪ.bəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsteɪ.bɪ.laɪz / (BrE)
to become stable or more stable; to stop changing, moving, or falling and maintain a steady condition
become stable or steady

Examples

After a period of rapid inflation, the currency finally began to stabilize.
The patient's condition will stabilize once the medication takes effect.
If the weather continues to stabilize, we can reopen the airport.

Synset Examples

The economy stabilized

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to destabilize - to make unstable or less stable
  • Synonym: steady - to make or become steady

Etymology

From French *stabiliser*, from Latin *stabilis* (firm, stable).

3. VERB

/ ˈsteɪ.bəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsteɪ.bɪ.laɪz / (BrE)
to make something steady or unlikely to change by supporting or holding it firmly in place
to make steady or stable

Examples

The crew worked quickly to stabilize the building after the earthquake.
Please hold my ladder to stabilize it while I climb.
Doctors managed to stabilize the patient's condition after the accident.

Synset Examples

brace your elbows while working on the potter's wheel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steady - to make or become firmly fixed, supported, or balanced
  • Antonym: to destabilize - to make unstable or cause to become unsteady

Etymology

From French *stabiliser*, from Latin *stabilis* (firm, stable).
