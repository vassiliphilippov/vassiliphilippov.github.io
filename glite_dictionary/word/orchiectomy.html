orchiectomy

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔr.kiˈɛk.tə.mi / (AmE), / ˌɔː.kiˈek.tə.mi / (BrE)
a surgical operation to remove one or both testicles
removal of one or both testicles (surgery)
medical

Examples

The urologist recommended an orchiectomy to treat the patient's testicular cancer.
After undergoing an orchiectomy, he needed follow-up hormone therapy.
An orchiectomy can be performed for both medical and gender affirmation reasons.

Etymology

From Greek 'orchis' (testicle) + '-ectomy' (removal).
