roving

1. ADJ

/ ˈroʊvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊvɪŋ / (BrE)
moving from place to place without a fixed home; wandering or migratory.
wandering; moving from place to place

Examples

Many of the early settlers led a roving life, moving with the seasons.
The roving bands of shepherds traveled across the plains in search of pasture.
She has a roving spirit, always eager to explore new countries and cultures.

Synset Examples

a restless mobile society
the nomadic habits of the Bedouins
believed the profession of a peregrine typist would have a happy future
wandering tribes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wandering - moving about without a fixed course or destination
  • Antonym: stationary - not moving; fixed in one place

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'rove', meaning to wander; origin uncertain, possibly from Middle English roven.

2. NOUN

/ ˈroʊvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊvɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of traveling from place to place without a fixed destination; wandering.
act of wandering or traveling about

Examples

His years of roving finally ended when he settled in the countryside.
The novel recounts her roving across continents in search of purpose.
After a decade of roving, he found peace in one small village.

Synset Examples

she followed him in his wanderings and looked after him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wandering - moving about without a fixed course, aim, or goal

Etymology

From the verb 'rove', which comes from Middle English 'roven', meaning 'to wander'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00297888-n
Synset POS: n
