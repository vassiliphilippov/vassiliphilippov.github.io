avenue

1. NOUN

/ ˈævəˌnu / (AmE), / ˈævɪnjuː / (BrE)
A wide street, especially one lined with trees or buildings, often used for major traffic through a city.
wide street or thoroughfare

Examples

They lived on a quiet avenue lined with maple trees.
The parade marched down the city's main avenue to the square.
Shops and cafés can be found on every avenue in the downtown district.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boulevard - a wide street in a city, typically lined with trees
  • Synonym: street - a public road in a city, town, or village
  • Antonym: alley - a narrow passage between or behind buildings

Etymology

From French 'avenue', from Old French 'avenir' (to come), meaning 'approach' or 'way of entry'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈævəˌnu / (AmE), / ˈævəˌnjuː / (BrE)
a possible way of achieving something or dealing with a problem; a method or approach
way of achieving something

Examples

After the funding fell through, they considered every possible avenue to keep the project alive.
Studying abroad can open new avenues for your career and personal growth.
The lawyer advised that pursuing legal action was not the only avenue available to resolve the dispute.

Synset Examples

they explored every avenue they could think of
it promises to open new avenues to understanding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: route - a way or course taken in getting from a starting point to a destination; can be used figuratively as a method
  • Synonym: means - a method or way of doing something; means to an end
  • Synonym: solution - a means of solving a problem or dealing with a difficult situation

Etymology

From French 'avenue' (approach, access), from 'avenir' (to come).
