misopedia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪs.oʊˈpiː.di.ə / (AmE), / ˌmɪs.əˈpiː.di.ə / (BrE)
A hatred or intense dislike of children.
hatred of children
medical

Examples

The psychologist's research focused on the origins of misopedia in certain personality disorders.
Literature sometimes explores the consequences of unchecked misopedia within dysfunctional families.
Despite his reputation for misopedia, he quietly supported several children's charities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misoneism - hatred or fear of change or innovation
  • Degree Higher: misanthropy - a dislike of humankind
  • Antonym: philanthropy - love of humanity

Etymology

From Greek 'misos' (hatred) + 'pais, paidos' (child).
