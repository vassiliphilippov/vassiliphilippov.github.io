Elaeocarpaceae

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌliːoʊkɑːrˈpeɪsiˌiː / (AmE), / ɪˌliːəʊkɑːˈpæsiːˌiː / (BrE)
A family of flowering trees and shrubs native to warm regions, some of which provide useful timber; sometimes classified in the family Santalaceae.
family of flowering trees and shrubs

Examples

Many species within the Elaeocarpaceae family are valued for their ornamental flowers and foliage.
Researchers are studying the timber properties of Elaeocarpaceae plants for sustainable forestry.
The Elaeocarpaceae family includes both evergreen and deciduous trees found in tropical areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Santalaceae - a family of flowering plants including sandalwood

Etymology

From New Latin Elaeocarpaceae, based on genus name Elaeocarpus, from Greek elaia (olive) + karpos (fruit).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌliː.oʊ.kɑːrˈpeɪ.siː.iː / (AmE), / ɪˌliː.əʊ.kɑːˈpæksiː / (BrE)
A family of trees and shrubs widely distributed in warm regions, some of which yield useful timber.
a family of tropical trees and shrubs

Examples

The Elaeocarpaceae family includes over 400 species across tropical and subtropical regions.
Some members of the Elaeocarpaceae produce timber valued for construction and furniture.
Botanists have debated whether to classify Elaeocarpaceae within the Santalaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Santalaceae - a family of flowering plants sometimes including Elaeocarpaceae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Elaeocarpus, from Greek elaia (olive) and karpos (fruit).
