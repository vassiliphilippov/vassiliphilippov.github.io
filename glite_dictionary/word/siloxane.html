siloxane

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈlɑk.seɪn / (AmE), / sɪˈlɒk.seɪn / (BrE)
Any of a large class of compounds in which silicon and oxygen atoms alternate in the structure, often used in the manufacture of silicones.
compound with alternating silicon and oxygen atoms
medical

Examples

Polydimethylsiloxane is widely used as a lubricant and an ingredient in cosmetics.
Scientists analyzed the structure of the siloxane chain using advanced spectroscopy techniques.
The flexibility of siloxane compounds makes them valuable in medical devices and implants.

Etymology

From silicon and oxane, indicating the presence of silicon and oxygen atoms in the structure.
