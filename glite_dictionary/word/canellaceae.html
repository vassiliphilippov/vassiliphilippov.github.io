Canellaceae

1. NOUN

/ kəˌnɛləˈsi.aɪ / (AmE), / kəˌnɛləˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of aromatic tropical trees native to eastern Africa, Florida, and the West Indies, belonging to the order Canellales
family of aromatic tropical trees

Examples

Members of the Canellaceae are known for their aromatic bark and medicinal uses.
The botanist specialized in the study of tropical families such as Canellaceae.
The Canellaceae family includes trees found in both Africa and the Caribbean.

Etymology

From the genus name *Canella*, from Latin *canna* (reed or cane), with the standard botanical family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ kəˌnɛləˈsiː.i / (AmE), / kəˌnɛləˈsiː.i / (BrE)
A family of aromatic tropical trees found in eastern Africa, Florida, and the West Indies, belonging to a single genus.
a family of aromatic tropical trees
medical

Examples

The Canellaceae family comprises aromatic trees native to Africa and tropical America.
Scientists studied the medicinal properties of plants within the Canellaceae family.
Specimens of Canellaceae can be found in botanic gardens specializing in tropical flora.

Etymology

From the genus name Canella, from Latin 'canna' (reed), due to the cane-like appearance of some species.
