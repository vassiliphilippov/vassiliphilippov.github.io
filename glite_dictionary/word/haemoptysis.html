haemoptysis

1. NOUN

/ hiːˈɑːmp.tɪ.sɪs / (AmE), / hɪˈmɒp.tɪ.sɪs / (BrE)
The coughing up of blood or blood-stained mucus from the lungs or airways, often due to infection or disease.
coughing up blood from respiratory tract
medical british

Examples

The presence of haemoptysis can indicate a serious infection that requires urgent evaluation.
He went to the hospital after noticing episodes of haemoptysis over several days.
Patients with chronic bronchitis are at increased risk of developing haemoptysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemoptysis - coughing up blood from the respiratory tract (American spelling)

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + ptysis (a spitting).
