programmability

1. NOUN

/ proʊˌɡræməˈbɪləti / (AmE), / prəʊˌɡræməˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality or ability of a device, system, or object to be programmed to perform different tasks or functions
ability to be programmed

Examples

The programmability of modern microcontrollers allows engineers to design flexible systems.
Before investing in new equipment, consider its programmability and how easily it can be updated.
Software-defined radios are valued for their high programmability, enabling quick adaptation to new standards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: programmable - able to be programmed

Etymology

Formed from 'programmable' + '-ity', indicating the state or quality of being programmable.
