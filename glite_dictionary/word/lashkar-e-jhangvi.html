Lashkar-e-Jhangvi

1. PROPN

/ læʃˌkɑɹ eɪ dʒɑŋˈvi / (AmE), / læʃˌkɑː eɪ dʒʌŋˈviː / (BrE)
a Sunni Muslim extremist group in Pakistan known for its sectarian attacks and links to al-Qaeda, serving as the armed wing of Sipah-e-Sahaba.
Pakistani Sunni extremist militant group

Examples

The Pakistani government has taken action to ban Lashkar-e-Jhangvi for its involvement in sectarian violence.
Authorities suspect that Lashkar-e-Jhangvi was behind several deadly bombings targeting minority groups.
International organizations have designated Lashkar-e-Jhangvi as a terrorist group due to its ties with al-Qaeda.

Etymology

From Urdu لشکرِ جھنگوی (Lashkar-e-Jhangvi), meaning 'Army of Jhangvi', named after Haq Nawaz Jhangvi, a founder of Sipah-e-Sahaba.
