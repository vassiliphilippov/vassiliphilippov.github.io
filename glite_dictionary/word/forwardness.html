forwardness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrwərdnəs / (AmE), / ˈfɔːwədnəs / (BrE)
promptness or willingness to take initiative or act without hesitation
prompt willingness or initiative

Examples

Her forwardness in volunteering for new projects made her a valued team member.
He was admired by his mentors for the forwardness he showed during group discussions.
Despite her shyness, her forwardness surprised everyone when she presented her ideas with confidence.

Synset Examples

readiness to continue discussions
they showed no eagerness to spread the gospel
they disliked his zeal in demonstrating his superiority
he tried to explain his forwardness in battle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: readiness - the state of being prepared or willing
  • Synonym: eagerness - enthusiastic willingness or desire
  • Antonym: reluctance - unwillingness or disinclination

Etymology

From 'forward' + '-ness', indicating the quality of being forward or ready.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrwərdnəs / (AmE), / ˈfɔːwədnəs / (BrE)
an advanced stage or degree of progress in a process or development
advanced stage or progress

Examples

The forwardness of the wheat surprised even the most optimistic farmers.
Due to the forwardness of construction, the building opened months ahead of schedule.
Her garden shows remarkable forwardness this spring, with flowers blooming earlier than usual.

Synset Examples

the forwardness of the harvest this year
the arts are in no kind of forwardness in this matter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advancement - the process of promoting or moving forward
  • Antonym: delay - a state of being late or behind schedule

Etymology

From 'forward' + '-ness', formed in Middle English to denote the state or quality of being forward or advanced.

offensive boldness or assertiveness

Examples

Some people admire forwardness, but others may find it inappropriate.

Related Terms
