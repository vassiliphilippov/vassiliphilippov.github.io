sine qua non

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪni kweɪ ˈnɑn / (AmE), / ˌsiːneɪ kwɑː ˈnɒn / (BrE)
An essential condition or element; something that is indispensable or absolutely necessary.
indispensable condition, essential prerequisite

Examples

Trust is the sine qua non of any successful partnership.
Access to clean water is a sine qua non for public health.
For democracy to function properly, an informed electorate is the sine qua non.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: necessity - the fact of being required or indispensable
  • Synonym: essential - absolutely necessary; extremely important
  • Antonym: superfluous - not necessary; more than enough

Etymology

From Latin, literally 'without which not'.
