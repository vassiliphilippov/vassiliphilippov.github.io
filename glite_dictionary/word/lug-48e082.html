lug

1. NOUN

/ lʌɡ / (AmE), / lʌɡ / (BrE)
a marine worm with a row of tufted gills along each side of its back, often used as fishing bait
marine worm used as fishing bait

Examples

Fishermen often dig for lug on the shore to use as bait.
The beach was teeming with lug at low tide.
She baited the hook with a juicy lug and cast her line into the sea.

/ lʌɡ / (AmE), / lʌɡ / (BrE)
A sail with four corners that is hoisted from a yard set obliquely to the mast, commonly used in small boats.

Examples

The fisherman raised the lug as the wind began to pick up.
Traditional Scottish boats often used a lug for sailing in coastal waters.
