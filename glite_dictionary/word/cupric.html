cupric

1. ADJ

/ ˈkuːprɪk / (AmE), / ˈkjuːprɪk / (BrE)
(of a compound) containing copper with a valence of two; relating to copper(II) ions.
containing copper(II) ions
medical

Examples

The blue color of the solution indicates the presence of cupric ions.
Scientists analyzed the cupric oxide sample for purity.
Cupric sulfate is commonly used in laboratory experiments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cuprous - of or containing monovalent copper (copper(I))

Etymology

From Latin 'cuprum' (copper) + '-ic', indicating a specific valence.
