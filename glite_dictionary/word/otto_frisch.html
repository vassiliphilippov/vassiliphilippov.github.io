Otto Frisch

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑtoʊ ˈfrɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈɒtəʊ ˈfrɪʃ / (BrE)
(1904–1979) Austrian-born British physicist who, with Lise Meitner, recognized and described the process of nuclear fission, and explained the potential of chain nuclear reactions.
Austrian-born British physicist on nuclear fission

Examples

Otto Frisch played a key role in explaining the physics behind nuclear fission.
The scientific achievements of Otto Frisch changed the course of history.
After emigrating to Britain, Otto Frisch continued his groundbreaking research in physics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lise Meitner - Austrian-Swedish physicist who collaborated with Otto Frisch on nuclear fission
  • Synonym: nuclear fission - Physical process first described and named by Frisch and Meitner
