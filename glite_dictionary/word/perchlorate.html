perchlorate

1. NOUN

/ pərˈklɔːˌreɪt / (AmE), / pəˈklɔːrət / (BrE)
A salt or ester of perchloric acid, containing the perchlorate ion (ClO₄⁻).
a salt of perchloric acid
medical

Examples

Some groundwater sources have been found to contain elevated levels of perchlorate.
The chemist synthesized perchlorate compounds in the laboratory.
Exposure to perchlorate can disrupt thyroid function in humans.

Etymology

From 'perchloric' (referring to perchloric acid) + '-ate' (salt or ester suffix).
