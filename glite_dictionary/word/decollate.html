to decollate

1. VERB

/ dɪˈkɑː.leɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈkɒl.eɪt / (BrE)
to cut the head off (a person or animal); to behead.
cut off the head; behead

Examples

The king was decollated at dawn for his crimes against the state.
Several statues had been decollated during the attack on the temple.
She read a tale where a villain threatened to decollate his enemies.

Synset Examples

the French King was beheaded during the Revolution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to behead - to cut off someone's head

Etymology

From Latin decollāre, from de- (down, off) + collum (neck).
