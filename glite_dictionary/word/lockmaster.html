lockmaster

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑkˌmæstər / (AmE), / ˈlɒkˌmɑːstə / (BrE)
a worker responsible for operating and overseeing a lock on a canal or waterway
person in charge of a canal lock

Examples

The lockmaster carefully monitored the rising water levels to ensure safe passage for the boat.
During the festival, the lockmaster gave tours explaining how the lock gates worked.
Every vessel must signal the lockmaster before entering the canal lock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lockkeeper - person who operates and maintains a canal lock

Etymology

From 'lock' (canal lock) + 'master' (person in charge).
