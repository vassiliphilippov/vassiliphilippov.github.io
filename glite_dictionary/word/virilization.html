virilization

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɪr.ə.lɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌvɪr.ɪ.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the development of male physical characteristics in a female, typically due to hormone imbalance or therapy
development of male traits in a female
medical

Examples

The patient showed signs of virilization after receiving certain steroid treatments.
Doctors were concerned about possible virilization due to prolonged hormone therapy.
Excess androgen production can lead to virilization in women.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: masculinization - the process of becoming more masculine, often used in medical or biological contexts

Etymology

From Latin virilis (manly) + -ization.
