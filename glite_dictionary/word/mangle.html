mangle

1. VERB

/ ˈmæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈmaŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
to smooth or press (laundry or cloth) using a mangle, a machine with rollers
press laundry with a mangle

Examples

In the old days, people would mangle their clothes to achieve a crisp finish.
My grandmother showed me how to mangle the sheets after washing them.
The workers used to mangle the linens at the hotel laundry room.

Synset Examples

mangle the sheets

Etymology

From Dutch 'mangel' (a laundry roller), from German 'Mangel'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈmæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈmæŋɡəl / (BrE)
a machine for drying and ironing laundry by passing it between two heavy heated rollers
machine for ironing laundry

Examples

The old-fashioned mangle stood in the corner of the laundry room.
She used a mangle to get the sheets perfectly smooth.
Vintage collectors sometimes seek out a mangle as a decorative piece.

Etymology

From Dutch 'mangel', from Middle Dutch, related to 'mangelen' (to mangle, press with rollers).
