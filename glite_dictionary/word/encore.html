encore

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːn.kɔr / (AmE), / ˈɒŋ.kɔː / (BrE)
an additional or repeated performance given at the end of a concert or show, typically in response to sustained applause from the audience
additional performance given after applause

Examples

The crowd demanded an encore after the band finished their set.
After delivering a stunning speech, she was called back for an encore.
The pianist played an unexpected encore that delighted the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repeat performance - a performance that is given again
  • Synonym: curtain call - an appearance by performers at the end of a show to acknowledge applause

Etymology

From French encore (again, still), from Latin hanc horam (at this hour).

2. VERB

/ ˈɑnˌkɔr / (AmE), / ˈɒŋ.kɔː / (BrE)
to call out as a request for a performer to repeat or give another performance, especially at the end of a show.
to request an additional performance

Examples

The audience began to encore loudly after the final song.
At the theater, fans encored the violinist until she played another piece.
Cheering crowds often encore their favorite performers at the end of concerts.

Etymology

From French 'encore' meaning 'again' or 'still'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00756358-v
Synset POS: v
