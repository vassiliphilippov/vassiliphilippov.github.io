Piranga ludoviciana

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈræŋgə ˌluːdəvɪˈsiːənə / (AmE), / pɪˈræŋgə ˌluːdəvɪˈsiːənə / (BrE)
A species of songbird found in western North America, the male of which is noted for its black, yellow, and orange-red plumage; commonly known as the western tanager.
species of western North American songbird (western tanager)

Examples

Every spring, birdwatchers hope to spot the brightly colored Piranga ludoviciana in the western forests.
The male Piranga ludoviciana is celebrated for his striking orange-red head and yellow body.
Researchers study the migration patterns of Piranga ludoviciana to learn more about changing climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: western tanager - A North American songbird with colorful male plumage, scientific name Piranga ludoviciana

Etymology

From Latin; Piranga from Tupi 'piranga' (a small bird), ludoviciana from Louisiana, USA.
