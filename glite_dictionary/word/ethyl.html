ethyl

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːθɪl / (AmE), / ˈiːθɪl / (BrE)
a chemical group or radical with the formula C2H5, derived from ethane by removing one hydrogen atom
chemical radical C2H5
medical

Examples

The ethyl group is commonly found in organic molecules such as ethanol.
During the reaction, an ethyl radical is formed as an intermediate.
Adding an ethyl substituent can significantly change a compound's physical properties.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: methyl - a chemical radical with the formula CH3, derived from methane

Etymology

From French 'éthyle', coined in the 19th century from 'ether' and the suffix '-yl', used for radicals.
