biff

1. VERB

/ bɪf / (AmE), / bɪf / (BrE)
to strike someone or something, usually with the fist, typically in a sudden or forceful way
strike with the fist
informal

Examples

He tried to biff his opponent during the heated argument.
She biffed the ball across the field with surprising force.
The children were biffing each other playfully in the backyard.

Synset Examples

The pedestrians pummeled the demonstrators

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punch - strike with a closed fist
  • Degree Lower: slap - strike with the palm of the hand

2. NOUN

/ bɪf / (AmE), / bɪf / (BrE)
A hard blow with the fist, especially in boxing or a fight.
a hard blow with the fist

Examples

The boxer delivered a quick biff to his opponent's jaw.
He got a biff on the nose during the scuffle.
She threatened to give him a biff if he kept teasing her.

Synset Examples

I gave him a clout on his nose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punch - a blow made with the fist
  • Synonym: clout - a heavy blow with the hand or a hard object
  • Degree Lower: slap - a sharp blow with the open hand
