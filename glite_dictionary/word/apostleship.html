apostleship

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɑːsəlˌʃɪp / (AmE), / əˈpɒsəlˌʃɪp / (BrE)
the status or office of being an apostle; the mission or authority of an apostle, especially in a religious context.
the office or role of an apostle

Examples

His apostleship was widely recognized by the early Christian church.
Paul often wrote about the nature and responsibilities of his apostleship.
She debated the qualities that defined true apostleship in their community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apostle - each of the chief disciples of Jesus Christ, or any pioneer of a movement

Etymology

Formed from 'apostle' + '-ship', denoting office or state.
