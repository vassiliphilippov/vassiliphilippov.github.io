incidence

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.sɪ.dəns / (AmE), / ˈɪn.sɪ.dəns / (BrE)
the rate or frequency at which something happens, especially something undesirable
rate or frequency of occurrence

Examples

The incidence of childhood obesity has increased dramatically over the past decade.
The city is working to reduce the incidence of violent crime in its neighborhoods.
Smoking raises the incidence of heart disease among adults.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prevalence - the total number of cases in a population

Etymology

From Medieval Latin incidentia, from Latin incidere (to fall upon, happen).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪnsɪdəns / (AmE), / ˈɪnsɪdəns / (BrE)
the striking of a ray or beam, especially of light, on a surface
the striking of a beam on a surface

Examples

The incidence of the laser beam on the prism determined how it would be refracted.
At the boundary, the incidence of the sunlight was almost perpendicular to the glass pane.
Understanding the angle of incidence is crucial in designing solar panels for maximum efficiency.

Synset Examples

he measured the angle of incidence of the reflected light

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angle of incidence - the angle at which a ray or wave strikes a surface

Etymology

From Latin incidentia (a falling upon), from incidere (to fall upon).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-11488396-n
Synset POS: n
