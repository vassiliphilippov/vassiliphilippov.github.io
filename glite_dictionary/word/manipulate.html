to manipulate

1. VERB

/ məˈnɪpjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / məˈnɪpjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to handle or move something with the hands, especially with skill
handle or move by hand

Examples

The surgeon carefully manipulated the delicate tissue during the procedure.
She manipulated the clay until it formed the shape she wanted.
Technicians manipulate tiny components under a microscope in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handle - hold or manipulate with the hands

Etymology

From Latin manipulatus, past participle of manipulare (to handle), from manus (hand) + plere (to fill).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01214263-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ məˈnɪpjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / məˈnɪpjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to change or control something in a skillful but often dishonest or unfair way, especially to deceive others
change or control dishonestly

Examples

The accountant tried to manipulate the financial statements to hide the losses.
She was accused of attempting to manipulate the data to support her theory.
Investigators found evidence that the company had manipulated sales records to deceive shareholders.

Synset Examples

Fudge the figures
cook the books
falsify the data

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to falsify - to alter information or evidence so as to mislead
  • Synonym: to fabricate - to invent or concoct something, often with the intent to deceive

Etymology

From Latin manipulus (a handful), from manus (hand).

6. VERB

/ məˈnɪpjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / məˈnɪpjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to treat or handle a part of the body manually, especially by massage or adjustment, for therapeutic purposes
move or massage body parts for therapy
medical

Examples

The chiropractor manipulated her spinal column as part of the treatment.
It is important to manipulate soft tissue carefully to avoid causing pain.
