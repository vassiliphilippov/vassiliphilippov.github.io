cambial

1. ADJ

/ ˈkæmbiəl / (AmE), / ˈkæmbiəl / (BrE)
relating to or functioning as a cambium, the layer of tissue in plants responsible for secondary growth.
relating to the cambium in plants
medical

Examples

The cambial layer is crucial for the tree's secondary growth and thickening of stems.
Researchers observed increased cambial activity during the spring season in most deciduous trees.
A disruption in normal cambial function can lead to abnormalities in plant development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cambium - the layer of tissue in plants responsible for secondary growth

Etymology

From Latin 'cambium' (exchange, change), referring to the plant tissue.
