Dindymene

1. PROPN

/ ˌdɪn.dəˈmiː.ni / (AmE), / ˌdɪn.dɪˈmiː.niː / (BrE)
the great nature goddess of ancient Phrygia, often identified with the Greek goddess Rhea and the Roman goddess Ops
Phrygian mother goddess

Examples

The ancient Phrygians worshipped Dindymene as the mother of all life and fertility.
Texts from antiquity often refer to Dindymene alongside Rhea and Cybele.
Sanctuaries dedicated to Dindymene have been discovered in several sites across Asia Minor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rhea - Greek mother goddess, counterpart of Dindymene
  • Synonym: Ops - Roman mother goddess, counterpart of Dindymene

Etymology

From Greek Dindymēnē, title of the Mother Goddess after Mount Dindymus in Phrygia.
