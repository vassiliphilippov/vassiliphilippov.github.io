insincerity

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnsɪnˈsɛrəti / (AmE), / ˌɪnsɪnˈsɛrəti / (BrE)
the quality of not expressing genuine feelings or intentions; the state of being deceitful or hypocritical
lack of sincerity; hypocrisy or deceit

Examples

She could sense the insincerity in his compliment, making her question his true intentions.
Politicians are often accused of insincerity when their promises sound too good to be true.
Their apology was marked by a clear insincerity that disappointed everyone in the room.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sincerity - the quality of being genuine, honest, and straightforward
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful
  • Synonym: hypocrisy - the practice of claiming to have moral standards to which one's own behavior does not conform

Etymology

From Latin 'insincerus' (not pure, untrue), with the suffix '-ity' forming nouns of quality.
