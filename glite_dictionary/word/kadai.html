Kadai

1. NOUN

/ kɑːˈdaɪ / (AmE), / kɑːˈdaɪ / (BrE)
A family of Sino-Tibetan languages spoken in southeastern Asia, including Thai and Lao.
language family of Southeast Asia

Examples

The Kadai languages are distributed across Thailand, Laos, and neighboring countries.
Linguists have studied the roots of the Kadai language family to understand migration patterns.
The Kadai group includes both tonal and non-tonal languages spoken by millions in Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tai–Kadai - another name for the same language family

Etymology

From the names of two key branches: Kra and Dai.
