Fräulein

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɔɪˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈfrɔɪ.laɪn / (BrE)
a German title of respect or form of address for an unmarried woman; formerly used for young women, now considered old-fashioned
German title for unmarried woman
archaic

Examples

The waiter addressed her as Fräulein, unaware that the term was no longer commonly used in Germany.
In old German novels, you will often find young women referred to as Fräulein.
She introduced herself as Fräulein Becker during the formal meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Miss - English title for an unmarried woman

Etymology

German, diminutive of Frau (woman, Mrs).
