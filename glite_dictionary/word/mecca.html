Mecca

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɛkə / (AmE), / ˈmɛkə / (BrE)
The holiest city of Islam, located in western Saudi Arabia; the birthplace of Muhammad and a major pilgrimage site for Muslims.
holiest city of Islam; birthplace of Muhammad

Examples

Thousands of Muslims travel to Mecca each year to perform the Hajj pilgrimage.
Non-Muslims are not permitted to enter the holy city of Mecca.
As the birthplace of Muhammad, Mecca holds deep significance in Islamic tradition.

Etymology

From Arabic Makkah.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛkə / (AmE), / ˈmɛkə / (BrE)
A place that attracts people who share a particular interest, regarded as the most significant or central location for that field or activity.
central place for enthusiasts

Examples

During the 1990s, Silicon Valley became the Mecca for technology startups.
Comic-Con is considered the Mecca for fans of comics and pop culture.
The Louvre is a Mecca for art lovers around the world.

Synset Examples

Paris is often seen as the Mecca for fashion enthusiasts.
For movie lovers, Hollywood is the Mecca of the film industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hub - the effective center of an activity, region, or network

Etymology

From the city of Mecca in Saudi Arabia, the holiest city in Islam, known as a pilgrimage destination.
