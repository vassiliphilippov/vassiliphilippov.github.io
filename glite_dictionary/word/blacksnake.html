blacksnake

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌsneɪk / (AmE), / ˈblækˌsneɪk / (BrE)
A large, blackish North American snake, especially the eastern racer (Coluber constrictor), commonly found in the eastern United States.
large blackish North American snake

Examples

A blacksnake slid quickly across the garden path and disappeared into the bushes.
Many farmers appreciate the presence of a blacksnake because it helps control rodents.
The blacksnakes in that area can grow as long as six feet.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌsneɪk / (AmE), / ˈblækˌsneɪk / (BrE)
A large, harmless, shiny black snake found in North America, especially the rat snake or racer.
large harmless North American black snake

Examples

We spotted a blacksnake slithering across the hiking trail.
The blacksnake is often seen on farms, where it helps control rodent populations.
Despite its size, the blacksnake poses no danger to humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rat snake - a common type of nonvenomous snake found in North America, often called blacksnake
  • Synonym: racer - a fast-moving North American snake sometimes called blacksnake
