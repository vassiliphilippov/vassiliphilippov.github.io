quadraphonic

1. ADJ

/ ˌkwɑːdrəˈfɑːnɪk / (AmE), / ˌkwædrəˈfɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to or using a system of four separate channels for sound reproduction, usually to create a more immersive listening experience.
using four audio channels for sound

Examples

The band released their new album in a quadraphonic format to showcase the surround sound.
He set up a quadraphonic system in his living room for a better audio experience.
In the 1970s, quadraphonic recordings were considered cutting-edge technology for music enthusiasts.

Synset Examples

his quadraphonic sound system

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: stereophonic - using two audio channels for sound reproduction

Etymology

From Latin 'quadri-' (four) and 'phonic' (relating to sound), patterned after 'stereophonic'.
