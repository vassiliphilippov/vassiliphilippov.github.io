biometry

1. NOUN

/ baɪˈɒmɪtri / (AmE), / baɪˈɒmɪtri / (BrE)
the application of statistical analysis to biological data or phenomena
statistical analysis of biological data

Examples

Modern advances in biometry have helped scientists better understand the spread of diseases.
Many graduate students in biology are now required to take courses in biometry.
The team used biometry to analyze growth patterns in the population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biostatistics - the branch of statistics dealing with data relating to living organisms

Etymology

From Greek bios (life) + metron (measure).
