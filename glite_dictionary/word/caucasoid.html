Caucasoid

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɔkəˌsɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɔːkəsɔɪd / (BrE)
relating to or denoting a group of people formerly classified as belonging to the Caucasian race, especially in anthropological contexts.
relating to the Caucasian race
archaic

Examples

Early anthropological texts often categorized people into Caucasoid, Mongoloid, and Negroid groups.
The term Caucasoid is now largely obsolete in the scientific community due to changes in understanding of human variation.
Some forensic reports may still use the label Caucasoid when describing skeletal features.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Caucasian - relating to the Caucasus region or traditional racial grouping; person classified as White

Etymology

From Caucasus (the region between Europe and Asia) + -oid (resembling), referring to people originally thought to originate from the Caucasus.
