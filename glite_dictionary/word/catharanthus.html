Catharanthus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæθəˈrænθəs / (AmE), / ˌkæθəˈrænθəs / (BrE)
a small genus of erect annual or perennial herbs native to Madagascar, widely naturalized in the tropics, formerly included in the genus Vinca
genus of flowering herbs native to Madagascar

Examples

The seeds of Catharanthus are sometimes sown in tropical gardens for their ornamental value.
Scientists discovered several important alkaloids in the Catharanthus genus.
Catharanthus is known for its showy, colorful flowers, especially in warm climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vinca - a genus of flowering plants commonly called periwinkle

Etymology

From Greek 'katharos' (pure) and 'anthos' (flower).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkæθəˈræntəs / (AmE), / ˌkæθəˈræntəs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the dogbane family, native to Madagascar, including several species grown as ornamentals and medicinal plants.
genus of Madagascar flowering plants

Examples

Many gardeners cultivate Catharanthus for its attractive flowers and ability to thrive in tropical climates.
Research has shown that Catharanthus produces several valuable alkaloids used in medicine.
The Catharanthus genus is native to Madagascar but has been widely introduced elsewhere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vinca - a closely related genus of flowering plants formerly including Catharanthus

Etymology

From Greek 'katharos' (pure) and 'anthos' (flower).
