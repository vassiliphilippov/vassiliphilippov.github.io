Anemopsis californica

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈmɑːpsɪs ˌkælɪˈfɔrnɪkə / (AmE), / ˌænɪˈmɒpsɪs ˌkælɪˈfɔːnɪkə / (BrE)
a stoloniferous herb native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, with pungent rootstock and small spicate flowers with white bracts resembling an anemone
stoloniferous herb of southwestern US and Mexico

Examples

Anemopsis californica is often found growing in marshy areas throughout the southwestern United States.
Traditional herbalists sometimes use the root of Anemopsis californica for its aromatic properties.
The white bracts of Anemopsis californica can make it appear similar to members of the anemone family.

Etymology

From Greek anemos (wind) + -opsis (appearance), and the Latin name for California.
