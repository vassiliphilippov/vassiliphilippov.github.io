Pierrot

1. PROPN

/ piˈɛroʊ / (AmE), / ˈpjɛərəʊ / (BrE)
a stock male character in French pantomime or commedia dell’arte, typically dressed in loose white clothing with a whitened face and expressive, melancholy features
stock male character in French pantomime

Examples

The actor played the Pierrot with such sadness that the audience was moved to tears.
Her costume for the masquerade ball was inspired by the traditional Pierrot character.
Paintings of Pierrot often capture his forlorn, dreamy expression.

Etymology

French, diminutive of Pierre (Peter); developed as a stock character in French pantomime and commedia dell’arte.
