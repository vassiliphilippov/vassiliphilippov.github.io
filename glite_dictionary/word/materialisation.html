materialisation

1. NOUN

/ məˌtɪriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / məˌtɪəriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of becoming real or actual; the act of something coming into existence
process of becoming real or actual

Examples

The materialisation of the new policy took several years of planning and debate.
Her dedication finally led to the materialisation of her lifelong goal.
We await the materialisation of the promised improvements in the city's transport system.

Synset Examples

the materialization of her dream

Related Terms

  • Synonym: realization - the act of making real or achieving something desired

Etymology

From 'materialise' (to become actual), formed from 'material' + '-ise' + '-ation'.

2. NOUN

/ məˌtɪriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / məˌtɪəriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of becoming real or actual; something that has come into existence as a result of an idea or plan
becoming real; realized result

Examples

The new community center is the materialisation of years of planning and fundraising.
His dream found materialisation in the form of a successful business.
Their collaboration resulted in the materialisation of an innovative technological solution.

Synset Examples

industrialism prepared the way for acceptance of the French Revolution's various socialistic offspring
this skyscraper is the solid materialization of his efforts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: realization - the act of making something real or achieving something desired

Etymology

From material + -isation, modeled after French matérialisation.

3. NOUN

/ məˌtɪriələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / məˌtɪəriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the appearance of something, especially a spirit, in bodily or physical form
appearance in bodily or physical form

Examples

The unexpected materialisation of the ghost startled everyone in the old mansion.
Some sceptics doubted the materialisation of spirits during the séance.
His recent success felt almost like the materialisation of a long-forgotten dream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manifestation - an appearance of something, especially a ghost or spirit

Etymology

From 'material' + '-isation'; 'material' from Latin 'materialis', from 'materia' (matter).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07337624-n
Synset POS: n
