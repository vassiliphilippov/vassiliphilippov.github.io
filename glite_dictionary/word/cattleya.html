cattleya

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætlɪə / (AmE), / ˈkatlɪə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American orchids known for their large, showy, and colorful flowers, often used in corsages.
type of tropical orchid

Examples

My grandmother grows a beautiful cattleya in her greenhouse.
The florist recommended a pink cattleya for the wedding bouquet.
Botanists value the cattleya for its vibrant colors and striking appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - a plant of the family Orchidaceae with complex flowers

Etymology

Named after William Cattley (1788–1835), English horticulturist who first cultivated the plant.
