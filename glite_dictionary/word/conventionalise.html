to conventionalise

1. VERB

/ kənˈvɛnʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / kənˈvɛnʃənaɪz / (BrE)
to make something conventional or adapt it to established conventions or standards
adapt to conventions or make conventional
british

Examples

The ritual was gradually conventionalised to fit religious customs.
Artists often conventionalise forms to achieve a certain style.
Over time, the company's unique approach was conventionalised into standard practice.

Synset Examples

conventionalized behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to conventionalize - make conventional or adapt to conventions (American spelling)
  • Antonym: to innovate - introduce new ideas or methods, opposite of making conventional

Etymology

From 'conventional' + '-ise', modeled on 'conventionalize'.
