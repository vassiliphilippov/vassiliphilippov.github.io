Dipnoi

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪpnɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈdɪpnɔɪ / (BrE)
A group of bony fishes (lungfishes) native to the Southern Hemisphere that have both gills and a modified air bladder for breathing; sometimes regarded as an order within Crossopterygii.
order of lungfishes (Southern Hemisphere)

Examples

The Dipnoi represent a unique group of fishes able to breathe air when water is scarce.
Some scientists study Dipnoi to understand the evolution of vertebrate lungs.
The fossils of Dipnoi provide valuable evidence for the transition from aquatic to terrestrial life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lungfish - air-breathing fish in the Dipnoi order
  • Antonym: teleost - a more advanced group of bony fishes for comparison

Etymology

From Greek 'dipnoos' (having two ways of breathing), from 'di-' (two) and 'pnoē' (breathing).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪpnɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈdɪpnɔɪ / (BrE)
a taxonomic subclass of bony fishes, known as lungfishes, found in the Southern Hemisphere, which breathe using both gills and a modified air bladder
a subclass of bony fishes (lungfishes)

Examples

Lungfishes belonging to the Dipnoi are known for their remarkable ability to survive out of water.
Scientists study the Dipnoi to better understand the evolution of vertebrate respiration.
The unique characteristics of the Dipnoi set them apart from other groups of bony fishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lungfish - a fish capable of breathing air using lungs as well as gills

Etymology

From Greek 'dipnoos' meaning 'having two modes of breathing' (di- 'two' + pnoe 'breath').
