touching

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌtʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʌtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of making contact by putting two things together so that there is no space between them
act of making contact

Examples

The touching of their hands was brief but meaningful.
Strict laboratory protocols prohibit the unnecessary touching of any samples.
He felt a sudden shock upon the touching of metal surfaces during the storm.

Synset Examples

at his touch the room filled with lights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contact - the state or act of physical touching

Etymology

From Middle English 'touchen', from Old French 'touchier', from Vulgar Latin '*toccāre' (to touch).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00047263-n

2. ADJ

/ ˈtʌtʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʌtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
causing strong feelings of sympathy, tenderness, or sadness; emotionally moving.
emotionally moving or affecting

Examples

Her speech was so touching that many people in the audience were moved to tears.
I found the reunion scene between the family members especially touching.
It was a touching tribute to those who had made sacrifices.

Synset Examples

the homecoming of the released hostages was an affecting scene
poignant grief cannot endure forever
his gratitude was simple and touching

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moving - causing strong feelings of emotion, especially sadness or sympathy
  • Synonym: poignant - evoking a keen sense of sadness or regret
  • Antonym: unfeeling - not showing emotion or sympathy

Etymology

Present participle of 'touch', from Middle English 'touchen', from Old French 'touchier'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
