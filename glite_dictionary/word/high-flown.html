high-flown

1. ADJ

/ haɪ floʊn / (AmE), / haɪ fləʊn / (BrE)
(of language or ideas) using elaborate or grandiose language, especially in a way that is pretentious or lacking substance.
using pretentious or grand language

Examples

The professor's high-flown rhetoric failed to convince the skeptical audience.
Critics dismissed the politician's high-flown promises as unrealistic and empty.
She preferred simple explanations instead of high-flown philosophical theories.

Synset Examples

high-flown talk of preserving the moral tone of the school
a high-sounding dissertation on the means to attain social revolution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grandiose - impressively grand or ambitious, often to excess
  • Antonym: modest - not pretentious or boastful; humble

Etymology

From the literal sense of 'flown' (past participle of 'fly'), suggesting being lofty or elevated, later applied figuratively to language or ideas.

2. ADJ

/ ˈhaɪ floʊn / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ fləʊn / (BrE)
(of language or ideas) very grand or elevated in style or moral tone, often to the point of being exaggerated or unrealistic.
grand or elevated in style or tone

Examples

The professor's high-flown speeches often left students feeling disconnected from practical realities.
She tends to express her ideas in a rather high-flown manner, making them sound more important than they are.
Critics described the film's message as high-flown, arguing it was idealistic but lacked practical relevance.

Synset Examples

an exalted ideal
argue in terms of high-flown ideals
a noble and lofty concept
a grand purpose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lofty - of imposing height or elevated character or tone
  • Synonym: grandiloquent - using lofty, pompous language
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of importance

Etymology

Formed from 'high' (lofty) + past participle of 'fly.'
