tip-off

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪp ˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈtɪp ɒf / (BrE)
the act of starting a basketball game by tossing the ball upward between two opposing players for a jump ball
start of a basketball game, jump ball

Examples

The referee blew the whistle and prepared for the tip-off.
After the tip-off, the home team quickly took possession of the ball.
Fans cheered loudly as the players leaped for the tip-off at center court.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɪp ˌɑf / (AmE), / ˈtɪp ˌɒf / (BrE)
A secret warning or piece of information about something, especially something illegal or surprising, that is going to happen.
secret advance warning or information

Examples

The police received a tip-off about the planned robbery and managed to stop it in time.
Thanks to an anonymous tip-off, the authorities raided the illegal warehouse.
Journalists claimed they acted on a tip-off from someone inside the company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warning - advance notice about impending danger or problem
  • Synonym: alert - an announcement that something requires attention

Etymology

From 'tip' (to give information) + 'off' (away), first attested early 20th century in British English.
