distention

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈstɛn(t)ʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈstɛn.ʃən / (BrE)
the state of being stretched or swollen beyond normal size
state of being stretched or swollen
medical

Examples

The doctor noted a distention of the patient's abdomen during the examination.
Fluid accumulation can cause significant distention in affected tissues.
Symptoms such as pain and distention should be reported to your physician promptly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swelling - an abnormal enlargement of a body part, typically as a result of injury or inflammation
  • Antonym: contraction - the process of becoming smaller or tighter

Etymology

From Latin 'distendere' meaning to stretch apart.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈstɛn(t)ʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈstɛnʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act of swelling or expanding by internal pressure; the state of being stretched or enlarged from within
swelling or expansion by internal pressure
medical

Examples

Abdominal distention can be a sign of gastrointestinal problems.
A rapid distention of the balloon occurred when air was pumped inside.
The patient complained of discomfort caused by distention of the stomach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dilation - expansion or widening, especially of a tubular structure
  • Antonym: constriction - the act of becoming narrower or tighter

Etymology

From Latin 'distensio', from 'distendere' meaning 'to stretch apart'.
