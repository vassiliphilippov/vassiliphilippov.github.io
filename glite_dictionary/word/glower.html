glower

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡlaʊ.ə / (BrE)
an angry or sullen look; a scowl
angry stare; scowl

Examples

Her glower was enough to silence the entire room.
The teacher gave the noisy students a fierce glower before continuing the lesson.
He responded to her criticism with a cold glower and said nothing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scowl - an angry or bad-tempered expression

Etymology

From Scots 'glour', probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian 'glora' (to glow, stare).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00880475-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈɡlaʊ.ər / (AmE), / ˈɡlaʊ.ə / (BrE)
to look at someone or something with an angry or sullen expression
look angrily or sullenly

Examples

She sat at the table and continued to glower at her brother after their argument.
The coach would often glower at players who made careless mistakes during practice.
He glowered so fiercely that everyone in the room fell silent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scowl - to frown in an angry or bad-tempered way
  • Antonym: smile - to make a happy or friendly facial expression

Etymology

From Middle English 'gloren' (to glare or look angrily), of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00032974-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈɡlaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡlaʊ.ə / (BrE)
to look at someone or something with a sullen or angry expression; to stare fixedly and angrily
stare with sullen or angry expression

Examples

He continued to glower at me across the table, barely saying a word.
She glowered at the noisy children in the cinema until they quieted down.
The coach was glowering from the sidelines after the poor performance.

Synset Examples

The girl glared at the man who tried to make a pass at her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glare - to stare in an angry or fierce way

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *gloren* or *glowren*, of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00033196-v
Synset POS: v
