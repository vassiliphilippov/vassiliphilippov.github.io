corrective

1. ADJ

/ kəˈrɛktɪv / (AmE), / kəˈrɛktɪv / (BrE)
intended to correct or enforce discipline; disciplinary.
intended to enforce discipline

Examples

The principal implemented corrective measures to address repeated misconduct.
She spoke to her son in a corrective tone, hoping he would learn from his mistake.
The school established a corrective program for students who continually broke the rules.

Synset Examples

the teacher's action was corrective rather than instructional
disciplinal measures
the mother was stern and disciplinary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disciplinary - intended to correct or punish for the purpose of discipline

Etymology

From Latin 'correctivus', from 'correctus' (corrected).

2. ADJ

/ kəˈrɛktɪv / (AmE), / kəˈrɛktɪv / (BrE)
Intended to correct or counteract something undesirable, or to restore to a normal condition.
intended to correct or counteract

Examples

The company implemented several corrective actions after discovering the error.
He wears corrective lenses to improve his eyesight.
The teacher provided corrective feedback to help students improve their essays.

Synset Examples

corrective measures
corrective lenses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remedial - intended as a remedy or solution

Etymology

From Medieval Latin correctivus, from Latin corrigere (to correct).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00234346-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ kəˈrɛktɪv / (AmE), / kəˈrɛktɪv / (BrE)
a device or appliance used to treat an injury or disease by correcting a physical problem
device used to correct injury or disease

Examples

She was fitted with a corrective to help straighten her spine.
The dentist recommended a dental corrective for her misaligned teeth.
After the injury, he wore a special corrective on his knee for six months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orthosis - an externally applied device used to modify the structural or functional characteristics of the neuromuscular and skeletal system

Etymology

From Latin 'correctivus', from 'corrigere' meaning to correct.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03116550-n
Synset POS: n
