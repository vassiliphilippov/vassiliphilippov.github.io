failing

1. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfeɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
(of work, grades, or performance) not meeting the minimum required standard; not passing.
not meeting minimum standard; not passing

Examples

She was worried after receiving several failing marks on her report card.
If your work is deemed failing, you may have to retake the course.
The school's new policy supports students at risk of failing grades.

Synset Examples

received failing grades

Related Terms

  • Antonym: passing - meeting the required standard or grade

Etymology

From the verb 'fail', from Old French 'faillir', from Latin 'fallere' (to deceive).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02089958-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfeɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
an instance of not achieving the minimum required standard or performance, especially in an examination or test
instance of failing to meet a standard

Examples

His failing in mathematics meant he had to repeat the course.
The school's policy is strict when it comes to repeated failings by students.
Despite a single failing, she managed to maintain her scholarship.

Synset Examples

his failing the course led to his disqualification
he got two flunks on his report

Related Terms

  • Synonym: failure - lack of success in achieving something

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'fail', from Old French 'faillir' (to fail), from Latin 'fallere' (to deceive, disappoint).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00067721-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfeɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
A fault or weakness in someone's character or in a system; a shortcoming.
a fault or weakness

Examples

Despite his many failings, he was loved by his friends.
One of the major failings of the plan was the lack of communication.
She acknowledged her failing and promised to work on it.

Synset Examples

he was quick to point out his wife's failings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weakness - a weak point or disadvantage
  • Antonym: strength - a good or strong quality
  • Synonym: flaw - an imperfection or fault

Etymology

From Old French 'faillir' (to fail), from Latin 'fallere' (to deceive).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14486530-n
Synset POS: n
