no

1. ADV

/ noʊ / (AmE), / nəʊ / (BrE)
Used as an adverb to indicate absence to any degree; not at all.
not at all; not in any degree (adverb)

Examples

He is no better today despite the medication.
The instructions were no clearer after his explanation.
There is no reason to doubt her sincerity.

Synset Examples

he is no better today

Related Terms

  • Synonym: not - used to make a word or group of words negative

Etymology

From Old English nā, contraction of ne ā (not ever).

2. ADV

/ noʊ / (AmE), / nəʊ / (BrE)
used to express refusal, denial, or disagreement, or to emphasize a negative statement
used to refuse or deny

Examples

No, I don't think that's a good idea.
He asked if I wanted more coffee, and I replied, 'no.'
No, you can't go outside until you finish your homework.

Synset Examples

no, you are wrong

Related Terms

  • Antonym: yes - word used to express affirmation or agreement

Etymology

From Old English 'nā', meaning 'never' or 'not ever'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00024715-r
Synset POS: r

3. NOUN

/ noʊ / (AmE), / nəʊ / (BrE)
A negative answer or response, especially to a question or request.
a negative response or rejection

Examples

When asked if he would attend, his no was immediate and firm.
The proposal was met with a resounding no from the committee.
Sometimes saying no is the healthiest choice you can make.

Synset Examples

his no was loud and clear

Related Terms

  • Antonym: yes - a positive answer or response
  • Synonym: rejection - the act of refusing or dismissing something

Etymology

From Old English *nā*, meaning 'not ever'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07219764-n
Synset POS: n

4. DET

/ noʊ / (AmE), / nəʊ / (BrE)
Used with mass nouns or plural count nouns to indicate a complete or almost complete lack or zero quantity of something.
zero quantity; not any

Examples

There were no tickets left for the concert.
He has no interest in watching football matches.
With no warning, the storm arrived suddenly.

Synset Examples

we have no bananas
no eggs left and no money to buy any
have you no decency?
did it with no help
I'll get you there in no time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: none - not any; no person or thing
  • Antonym: some - an unspecified amount or number of
  • Antonym: any - one or more, some, no matter which, used in questions/negatives

Etymology

From Old English nā (not any), from ne (not) + ā (ever).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02276242-a
Synset POS: a
