blemish

1. VERB

/ ˈblɛmɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈblɛmɪʃ / (BrE)
to spoil the appearance or quality of something, making it imperfect or less attractive
to spoil or make imperfect

Examples

A single scratch can blemish the otherwise perfect surface of the car.
The scandal blemished the governor's reputation beyond repair.
Careless handling may blemish the finish on the polished table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mar - to damage or spoil the quality or appearance of something
  • Antonym: flawless - without any imperfections or defects

Etymology

From Old French blemir (to make pale, disfigure), likely of Germanic origin.

4. NOUN

/ ˈblɛmɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈblɛmɪʃ / (BrE)
a small mark, flaw, or imperfection that spoils the appearance of something, especially on the skin
a mark or flaw spoiling appearance

Examples

She was self-conscious about the blemish on her cheek before the big event.
The antique vase had only one minor blemish, but it affected its value.
Using concealer can help cover a blemish for a flawless look.

Synset Examples

a facial blemish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flaw - an imperfection that detracts from the whole
  • Synonym: imperfection - a fault, blemish, or undesired feature
  • Synonym: scar - a mark left on the skin after a wound or injury

Etymology

From Old French *blemir* (to make pale, to stain) and Latin *blemma* (a stain).
