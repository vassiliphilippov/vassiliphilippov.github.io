nonresident

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑnˈrɛzɪdənt / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈrɛzɪdənt / (BrE)
a person who does not live in a particular place, especially one who is not a permanent resident of a country, state, or city
person not living in a specified place

Examples

As a nonresident, she had to pay higher tuition fees for the university.
Many nonresidents visit the city during the festival season.
Taxes for nonresident workers are calculated differently from those of residents.

Synset Examples

described by an admiring nonresident as a green and pleasant land

Related Terms

  • Antonym: resident - person who lives in a particular place

Etymology

From Latin prefix non- (not) + resident (from Latin residēns, present participle of residēre 'to remain, to stay').

2. ADJ

/ ˌnɑnˈrɛzɪdənt / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈrɛzɪdənt / (BrE)
not living in or permanently based in a particular place; relating to people or things that do not reside there
not living in a particular place

Examples

The university offers special programs for nonresident students.
Only nonresident vehicles are allowed to park in this lot during holidays.
Property taxes are higher for nonresident owners than for local residents.

Synset Examples

nonresident students who commute to classes
nonresident real estate

Related Terms

  • Antonym: resident - living in a particular place

Etymology

Prefix 'non-' meaning 'not' + 'resident' from Latin *residentem* (remaining, staying).

Synset POS: a
