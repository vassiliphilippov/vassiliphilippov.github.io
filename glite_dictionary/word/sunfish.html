sunfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A very large ocean-dwelling bony fish with a round, laterally compressed body, high dorsal and anal fins, and a rudder-like tail, found worldwide in warm waters.
large rounded ocean fish found in warm waters

Examples

The sunfish floated near the boat, its huge body slowly rising to the surface.
Marine biologists study the unique shape and behavior of the sunfish.
A fully grown sunfish can weigh more than a thousand kilograms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mola - an alternative common name for the sunfish, especially referring to the genus Mola
  • Synonym: moonfish - another common name for the sunfish

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A small carnivorous freshwater fish of North America, often with a laterally compressed body and a metallic sheen, including species such as crappies, black bass, bluegills, and pumpkinseed.
North American freshwater fish

Examples

The lake is known for its abundance of sunfish that attract anglers from all over.
We caught several colorful sunfish near the pier yesterday afternoon.
Sunfish typically feed on insects, small fish, and crustaceans in freshwater habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluegill - a species of sunfish commonly found in North America
  • Synonym: pumpkinseed - a brightly colored species of North American sunfish
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02564946-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈsʌn.fɪʃ / (BrE)
The flesh of any of various American perch-like fish of the family Centrarchidae, used as food.
flesh of American perch-like fish

Examples

The restaurant served grilled sunfish with a side of vegetables.
Local anglers often enjoy cooking sunfish after a day on the lake.
The delicate flavor of sunfish makes it a favorite among freshwater fish lovers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunfish - a small brightly colored freshwater fish of the family Centrarchidae

Etymology

From 'sun' + 'fish', probably referring to the sun-like shape or coloring of some species.
