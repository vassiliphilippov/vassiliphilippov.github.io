errand

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛr.ənd / (AmE), / ˈɛr.ənd / (BrE)
a short journey taken to carry out a specific task or duty, especially for someone else
short trip to do a task

Examples

She had to run a quick errand before returning home.
He asked his assistant to do an errand in town for him.
Parents often send their children on small errands around the neighborhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mission - a particular task or duty that someone is sent to do
  • Synonym: task - a piece of work to be done

Etymology

From Old English *ærende* (message, mission, errand).
