antineutrino

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ti.njuˈtri.noʊ / (AmE), / ˌanti.njuːˈtriː.nəʊ / (BrE)
A subatomic particle that is the antiparticle of a neutrino, having the same mass but opposite charge properties.
antiparticle of a neutrino

Examples

The antineutrino is produced during certain types of radioactive decay in nuclear reactors.
Physicists detected evidence of the elusive antineutrino using highly sensitive instruments.
Every neutrino has a corresponding antineutrino with opposite quantum numbers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: neutrino - a neutral subatomic particle with very little mass, related to the antineutrino

Etymology

From 'anti-' (opposite) and 'neutrino' (Italian, diminutive of 'neutro', neutral).
