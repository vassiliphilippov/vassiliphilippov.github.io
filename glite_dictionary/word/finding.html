finding

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪndɪŋ / (BrE)
a decision made by a court or jury on an issue of fact or law in a legal case
legal decision of a court or jury
legal

Examples

The jury's finding was based on the evidence presented during the trial.
The court's finding favored the defendant in the civil case.
After reviewing the facts, the judge issued a finding of not guilty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verdict - the formal decision or finding made by a jury in a trial

WordNet ID: oewn-01194465-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪndɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of establishing or determining the properties or facts of something, typically by research or calculation
the act of determining properties by research or calculation

Examples

The scientist's finding of the material's melting point was crucial to the experiment.
Accurate finding of blood pressure is essential in medical research.
Her finding of the species' genetic structure required years of careful analysis.

Synset Examples

the determination of molecular structures

Etymology

From Old English *findan* (to find) + -ing (forming a noun of action).

3. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪndɪŋ / (BrE)
a thing that is discovered, especially as the result of scientific research, investigation, or exploration
something discovered, especially in research

Examples

The archaeologist was excited about her latest finding at the ancient site.
Recent scientific findings support the theory of climate change.
Each finding in his report was carefully documented and reviewed.

Synset Examples

the findings in the gastrointestinal tract indicate that he died several hours after dinner
an area rich in archaeological findings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discovery - the process or act of finding something

Etymology

From Old English 'findan' (to find) + -ing (noun-forming suffix).
