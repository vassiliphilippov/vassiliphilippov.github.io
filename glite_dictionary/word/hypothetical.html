hypothetical

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpəˈθɛtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˈθɛtɪkəl / (BrE)
a supposed situation, circumstance, or proposition that is assumed for the sake of argument or investigation
a supposed situation or possibility

Examples

In philosophical debates, one often examines a hypothetical to see if the theory holds.
The researcher asked us to respond to a hypothetical where two laws conflicted.
Let’s use a hypothetical to clarify how the policy might work in extreme cases.

Synset Examples

consider the following, just as a hypothetical possibility

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypothesis - a proposed explanation for a phenomenon

Etymology

From Greek hupothetikos (supposed), via Late Latin hypotheticus.

2. ADJ

/ haɪ.pəˈθɛ.tɪ.kəl / (AmE), / haɪ.pəˈθɛ.tɪ.kəl / (BrE)
Based on or involving a suggested idea or theory without sufficient proof; supposed for the sake of argument or investigation.
based on a suggested idea; not proven

Examples

The scientist described a hypothetical scenario to explain the unusual results.
We discussed several hypothetical problems that might occur during the experiment.
Her argument relied on a hypothetical case that had never actually happened.

Synset Examples

theories about the extinction of dinosaurs are still highly conjectural
the supposed reason for his absence
suppositious reconstructions of dead languages
hypothetical situation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conjectural - based on guessing or supposition rather than knowing for certain
  • Synonym: supposed - assumed to be true or real, without evidence
  • Antonym: actual - existing in fact; real

Etymology

From Greek 'hypothetikos', from 'hypothesis' (base, supposition).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00864403-s
Synset POS: s
