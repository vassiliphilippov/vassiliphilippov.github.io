checkmate

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛkˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkˌmeɪt / (BrE)
a situation in chess where a player's king is in a position to be captured and there is no legal move to escape the threat, ending the game
final chess move trapping king

Examples

She realized it was checkmate and resigned from the game.
After ten moves, his opponent had him in checkmate.
The grandmaster delivered a stunning checkmate in only twenty moves.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stalemate - a chess position where no legal move is possible but the king is not in check

Etymology

From Persian 'shāh māt' meaning 'the king is helpless', via Arabic and Old French.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛkˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkˌmeɪt / (BrE)
A complete defeat or decisive victory, especially one that leaves an opponent with no possible response.
complete defeat; decisive victory

Examples

After months of negotiations, the company's acquisition of its rival was a business checkmate.
With this final piece of evidence, the prosecution delivered a legal checkmate in the courtroom.
His brilliant strategy in the debate led to a rhetorical checkmate over his opponent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: victory - the act of defeating an opponent or enemy; success in a contest
  • Antonym: defeat - the act of overcoming or being overcome in a contest or struggle

Etymology

From Old French 'esches mat', from Arabic 'shāh māt' (the king is helpless).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07490175-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈtʃɛkˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkˌmeɪt / (BrE)
to place an opponent's king under attack in such a way that it cannot escape, thus winning the game of chess
win the game of chess by putting the king in inescapable threat

Examples

Kasparov skillfully checkmated his opponent in less than twenty moves.
If you don't defend your king properly, you may be checkmated quickly.
She plans her strategy carefully to checkmate her rivals in the tournament.

Synset Examples

Kasparov checkmated his opponent after only a few moves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mate - in chess, to checkmate

Etymology

From Old French 'eschec mat', from Arabic 'shāh māt' (the king is helpless).

Synset POS: v
