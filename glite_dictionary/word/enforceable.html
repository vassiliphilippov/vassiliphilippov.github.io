enforceable

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈfɔrsəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɔːsəbəl / (BrE)
(of a law, rule, or agreement) able to be imposed or carried out by force or by legal means.
capable of being imposed or carried out

Examples

The new policy is only effective if it is enforceable by local authorities.
A contract must be enforceable in court to be legally binding.
They debated whether the terms of the agreement were enforceable under international law.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unenforceable - not able to be enforced

Etymology

From enforce + -able, rooted in Old French 'enforcier' and Latin 'in-' + 'fortis' (strong).
