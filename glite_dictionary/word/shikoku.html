Shikoku

1. PROPN

/ ʃɪˈkoʊku / (AmE), / ʃɪˈkəʊku / (BrE)
the smallest of the four main islands of Japan, located to the south of Honshu and east of Kyushu, noted for its forests and mountains
smallest main island of Japan

Examples

The pilgrimage in Shikoku is famous among Buddhist devotees.
Many tourists are drawn to Shikoku for its scenic mountains and ancient temples.
After leaving Honshu, they traveled south to explore Shikoku.

Etymology

From Japanese 四国 (Shikoku), meaning 'four provinces'.
