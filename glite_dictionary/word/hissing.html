hissing

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
A prolonged sharp sound like a sustained 's', especially made as an expression of disapproval or as a natural noise.
sharp, prolonged 's' sound; expression of disapproval

Examples

The politician's speech was drowned out by the hissing from the angry crowd.
As the snake slithered away, a low hissing could be heard in the grass.
The hissing from the audience made the performer nervous.

Synset Examples

the performers could not be heard over the hissing of the audience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: booing - the act of shouting 'boo' to express disapproval
