chelation

1. NOUN

/ kiˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / kiːˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
(medicine) the process of removing a heavy metal from the body by using agents that bind to the metal and allow it to be excreted, as in the treatment of lead or mercury poisoning.
removal of heavy metals using chelating agents
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended chelation to treat the patient’s lead poisoning.
Chelation therapy is a common method for removing mercury from the body.
Some alternative medicine practitioners promote chelation as a detoxification method.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chelate - a substance that binds to metal ions to form a stable complex

Etymology

From 'chelate', which is from Greek 'chēlē' (claw), referring to the way molecules grasp metal ions.

2. NOUN

/ kiˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / kiˈleɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the process in which a molecule forms several bonds to a single metal ion, resulting in a ring-shaped structure
formation of ring structures by bonding to a metal ion
medical

Examples

The chelation of the metal ion occurs when the ligand wraps around and binds at several points.
Some therapies use chelation to remove heavy metals from the bloodstream.
In coordination chemistry, chelation can significantly increase the stability of a complex.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ligand - an atom, ion, or molecule that binds to a central metal atom to form a coordination complex

Etymology

From Greek 'chēlē' (claw) + '-ation'.
