drekkar

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɛkɑr / (AmE), / ˈdrɛkaː / (BrE)
A Viking longship, often ornately decorated and described in historical sources as elegant, used by Norse raiders and explorers.
Viking longship used for raiding and exploration

Examples

The drekkar was revered for its speed and ability to navigate both open sea and shallow rivers.
Historians believe that the drekkar enabled Vikings to travel as far as North America.
Each drekkar was not only a warship but also a symbol of the owner's status and wealth.

Synset Examples

The drekkar's prows carried carvings of menacing beasts, such as dragons and snakes, allegedly to protect the ship and crew, and to ward off the terrible sea monsters of Norse mythology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: longship - a type of long, narrow ship used by Vikings

Etymology

From Old Norse 'dreki' (dragon), referring to the dragon-headed prows of such ships.
