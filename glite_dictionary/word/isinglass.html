isinglass

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪzɪnˌɡlæs / (AmE), / ˈaɪzɪnˌɡlɑːs / (BrE)
a transparent, flaky mineral (mica), especially used as an electrical insulator because of its resistance to electricity
transparent mineral used as an electrical insulator

Examples

Old stoves sometimes had panels made from isinglass instead of glass.
Because of its excellent insulating properties, isinglass was used in early radio equipment.
The electrical technician replaced the damaged isinglass sheeting in the capacitor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mica - a group of minerals that split into thin, elastic sheets

Etymology

From obsolete Dutch *huysenblas*, literally ‘sturgeon’s bladder’ (originally referring to a type of gelatin), later applied by analogy to mica due to its translucency.
