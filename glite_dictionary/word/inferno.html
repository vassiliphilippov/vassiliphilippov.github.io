inferno

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɜrnoʊ / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɜːnəʊ / (BrE)
(in Christianity) The place of eternal punishment for the wicked, considered as the abode of Satan and evil forces; hell.
the abode of Satan; hell

Examples

Medieval art often depicts sinners suffering in the inferno for eternity.
The novel's villain was convinced he was destined for the inferno.
Many sermons warned parishioners of the torments in the inferno awaiting the unrighteous.

Synset Examples

Hurl'd headlong … To bottomless perdition, there to dwell
a demon from the depths of the pit
Hell is paved with good intentions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hell - In many religions, the place of punishment for the wicked after death

Etymology

From Italian 'inferno', from Latin 'infernus' (of the lower regions, hell).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɜrnoʊ / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɜːnəʊ / (BrE)
a place or situation marked by intense heat, pain, turmoil, or destruction
place or condition of intense suffering or chaos

Examples

The city became an inferno as flames consumed entire blocks.
After the scandal broke, the office turned into an inferno of accusations and tension.
The refugees fled the inferno of war in search of safety.

Synset Examples

the hell of battle
the inferno of the engine room
when you're alone Christmas is the pits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hell - a place regarded in some religions as the abode of the damned; also used figuratively for great suffering
  • Antonym: paradise - a place or state of complete happiness and peace

Etymology

From Italian 'inferno' meaning 'hell', from Latin 'infernus' (lower, underground).

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɜrnoʊ / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɜːnəʊ / (BrE)
a large and uncontrollable fire that burns fiercely
an intense, uncontrollable blaze

Examples

Firefighters struggled for hours to contain the inferno that engulfed the warehouse.
The wildfire turned the forest into a raging inferno visible for miles around.
As the oil rig exploded, workers scrambled to escape the growing inferno.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blaze - a very large or fiercely burning fire
  • Synonym: conflagration - a large destructive fire

Etymology

From Italian 'inferno' (hell), from Latin 'infernus' (lower, of the underworld).
