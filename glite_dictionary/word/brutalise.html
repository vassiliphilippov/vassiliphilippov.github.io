to brutalise

1. VERB

/ ˈbruːtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈbruːtəlaɪz / (BrE)
to treat someone or something in a cruel, harsh, or violent way
treat cruelly or violently

Examples

Prison guards cannot be allowed to brutalise the inmates without consequence.
War can brutalise even the most compassionate individuals.
The documentary exposed how children were brutalised in orphanages during the civil war.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abuse - to treat someone with cruelty or violence
  • Synonym: to maltreat - to treat someone badly or cruelly

Etymology

From 'brutal' + '-ise', ultimately from Latin brutalis (beast-like).

WordNet ID: oewn-02521600-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈbruːtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈbruːtəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make someone or something cruel, violent, or insensitive; to deprive of compassion or humanity.
make cruel or insensitive

Examples

Years of violence had brutalised the community, eroding trust and compassion.
Prison life can brutalise even the most gentle individuals, stripping them of empathy.
The constant exposure to war brutalised the soldiers, hardening their hearts against suffering.

Synset Examples

Life in the camps had brutalized him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dehumanize - to deprive someone of human qualities; to make less human
  • Antonym: to civilize - to make more cultured, refined, or humane

Etymology

From French 'brutaliser', from 'brutal' (cruel, savage).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00113860-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈbruːtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈbruːtəlaɪz / (BrE)
(of a person or group) to become cruel, violent, or lacking in feeling, typically as a result of exposure to cruelty or violence.
to become cruel or insensitive

Examples

Years of war can brutalise even the most compassionate people.
Exposure to so much violence gradually brutalised the soldiers.
A society plagued by constant conflict may begin to brutalise over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to harden - to become emotionally tough or less sensitive
  • Antonym: to sensitise - to become more sensitive to feelings or stimuli

Etymology

From 'brutal' + '-ise', modeled after French 'brutaliser'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00113661-v
Synset POS: v
