speciously

1. ADV

/ spɪˈʃuːsli / (AmE), / spɪˈsjuːsli / (BrE)
In a way that appears true, correct, or plausible, but is actually false or deceptive.
in a deceptive or misleading way

Examples

The politician speciously argued that his policy would benefit everyone, though the facts showed otherwise.
The proposal was speciously attractive, but a closer look revealed many flaws.
She speciously justified her actions, hoping no one would notice the inconsistency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceptively - in a way that gives a false impression
  • Antonym: honestly - in a truthful or genuine way

Etymology

From Latin 'speciosus' (beautiful, plausible) + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.
