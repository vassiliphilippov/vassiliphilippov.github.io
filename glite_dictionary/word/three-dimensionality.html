three-dimensionality

1. NOUN

/ ˌθriː daɪˌmɛnʃəˈnæləti / (AmE), / ˌθriː daɪˌmɛnʃəˈnæləti / (BrE)
The quality or state of having or appearing to have three dimensions (height, width, and depth).
the quality of having three dimensions

Examples

The three-dimensionality of the sculpture makes it stand out from the wall.
Artists often use shading techniques to give their drawings a sense of three-dimensionality.
Advances in graphics technology have greatly improved the three-dimensionality of video game environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: three-dimensional - having or appearing to have three dimensions

Etymology

From 'three-dimensional' + '-ity', indicating a state or quality.
