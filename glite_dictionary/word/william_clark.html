William Clark

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪljəm klɑrk / (AmE), / ˈwɪljəm klɑːk / (BrE)
(1770–1838) A United States explorer who, with Meriwether Lewis, led the Lewis and Clark Expedition from St. Louis to the Pacific Coast and was responsible for mapping the region.
US explorer; leader of the Lewis and Clark Expedition

Examples

William Clark kept detailed journals that have provided historians with invaluable information about the early American West.
The Lewis and Clark Expedition, co-led by William Clark, greatly expanded knowledge of North America's geography and peoples.
Many schools and landmarks across the United States are named after William Clark in recognition of his contributions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Meriwether Lewis - U.S. explorer and co-leader of the Lewis and Clark Expedition
