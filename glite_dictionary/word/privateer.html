privateer

1. NOUN

/ ˌpraɪvəˈtɪr / (AmE), / ˌpraɪvəˈtɪə / (BrE)
A privately owned armed ship authorized by a government to attack enemy ships during wartime.
privately owned warship with government authorization

Examples

The government issued letters of marque to turn his ship into a privateer.
Many fortunes were made by privateers during the 18th century.
A captured privateer was often repurposed for use in the navy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pirate - a person who attacks and robs ships at sea without legal authority

Etymology

From French 'privatier', from 'privé' (private); related to 'private' + '-eer' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpraɪvəˈtɪr / (AmE), / ˌpraɪvəˈtɪə / (BrE)
a person, especially a sailor or officer, authorized by a government to attack foreign shipping during wartime; an officer or crew member of a privateer ship
person authorized to attack enemy ships

Examples

The notorious privateer sailed under the sanction of the crown.
Many sailors found fortune and fame as successful privateers during the war.
She read tales of the daring privateer who once raided Spanish galleons.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pirate - a person who attacks and robs ships at sea

Etymology

From French *privatier*, from *privé* (private), influenced by *private* in English.
