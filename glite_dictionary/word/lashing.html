lashing

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlæʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of beating someone with a whip, strap, or rope as a form of punishment
beating with a whip, strap, or rope as punishment

Examples

The prisoner received a severe lashing for attempting to escape.
Public lashings were once a common form of punishment in many societies.
He dreaded the lashing that awaited him after breaking the rules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flogging - the act of beating someone severely with a whip or stick, especially as punishment
  • Synonym: caning - punishment by beating with a cane

Etymology

From lash (to strike with a whip) + -ing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlæʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlæʃɪŋ / (BrE)
a rope or cord used to fasten one object to another, especially in sailing or camping.
rope or cord for fastening

Examples

The tent was secured with a thick lashing around the pole.
Sailors checked each lashing to make sure the cargo would not move.
A broken lashing caused the logs to shift during transport.

Synset Examples

the boats were held together by lashings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tie - a string, cord, or rope used for fastening something

Etymology

From the verb 'lash' (to tie or bind) + -ing; 'lash' probably from Middle French 'lacher'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03649387-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈlæʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlæʃɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person or action) urging on violently, especially by whipping or flogging.
urging on violently by whipping or flogging

Examples

The lashing riders urged their horses faster across the plain.
He could hear the lashing commands of the overseer from afar.
The prisoners dreaded the lashing punishment awaiting them for disobedience.

Synset Examples

looked at the lashing riders

Etymology

From the verb 'lash' (to strike with a whip), with the -ing suffix forming an adjectival or participial usage.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
