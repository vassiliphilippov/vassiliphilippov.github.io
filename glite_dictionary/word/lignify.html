to lignify

1. VERB

/ ˈlɪɡnɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈlɪɡnɪfʌɪ / (BrE)
(of tissue or cells) to become woody or to convert into wood, especially by the deposition of lignin.
become or make woody; turn into wood

Examples

During drought conditions, some plants rapidly lignify their stems to conserve water.
The root tissue will gradually lignify as the tree matures.
Botanists study how certain environmental factors cause plant cells to lignify.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: to petrify - to turn organic material into stone

Etymology

From Latin 'lignum' (wood) + '-ify' (to make).
