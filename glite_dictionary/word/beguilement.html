beguilement

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈɡaɪlmənt / (AmE), / bɪˈɡaɪlmənt / (BrE)
an activity or entertainment that charms or pleases, serving to distract someone from their troubles or concerns
pleasing entertainment or distraction

Examples

The magician’s show provided delightful beguilement for the bored children.
During the long journey, music became welcome beguilement from the monotony of the road.
She found great beguilement in solving crossword puzzles every evening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diversion - an activity that diverts the mind from serious concerns; amusement
  • Synonym: distraction - something that turns attention away from worry or distress
  • Antonym: tedium - the state of being tedious or boring; lack of excitement

Etymology

From Middle English 'begilen' (to deceive, to amuse), from be- + guile (trickery, charm).

2. NOUN

/ bɪˈɡaɪlmənt / (AmE), / bɪˈɡaɪlmənt / (BrE)
a captivating charm or attractiveness that enchants or fascinates, sometimes in a misleading way
captivating charm or enchantment

Examples

Her beguilement was impossible to resist, leaving everyone captivated by her presence.
The actor's beguilement drew audiences from around the world.
He was wary of her beguilement, suspecting there was more beneath her enchanting smile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charm - the power or quality of delighting, attracting, or fascinating others
  • Synonym: allure - the quality of being powerfully and mysteriously attractive or fascinating

Etymology

From Middle English 'beguile', from be- + guile (deceit, trickery).
