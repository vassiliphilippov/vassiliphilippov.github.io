biweekly

1. ADV

/ baɪˈwiːkli / (AmE), / baɪˈwiːkli / (BrE)
Happening or done every two weeks.
every two weeks (adverb)

Examples

The staff meeting takes place biweekly to keep everyone updated.
He receives a paycheck biweekly, instead of once a month.
We schedule our maintenance checks biweekly for maximum efficiency.

Synset Examples

he visited his cousins fortnightly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fortnightly - every two weeks

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'weekly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00256191-r
Synset POS: r

2. NOUN

/ baɪˈwiːkli / (AmE), / baɪˈwiːkli / (BrE)
A periodical that is published either twice a week or every two weeks.
periodical published twice a week or every two weeks

Examples

The company launched a new biweekly to keep readers informed about industry trends.
She writes a column for a biweekly that focuses on science and technology.
Their biweekly is distributed to subscribers on alternate Thursdays.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: monthly - A periodical published every month
  • Degree Lower: weekly - A periodical published every week
  • Degree Higher: daily - A periodical published every day

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'weekly' (relating to a week).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06607108-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADV

/ baɪˈwiːkli / (AmE), / baɪˈwiːkli / (BrE)
Twice a week; occurring or done every two times per week.
twice a week

Examples

The newsletter is sent out biweekly, so you'll get two issues every week.
Our team holds biweekly meetings on Mondays and Thursdays.
He attends physical therapy biweekly to accelerate his recovery.

Synset Examples

he called home semiweekly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: semiweekly - occurring twice a week
  • Degree Lower: biweekly - once every two weeks

Etymology

From bi- (meaning 'two') + weekly ('once a week'), indicating either 'every two weeks' or 'twice a week'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00256331-r
Synset POS: r
