deformational

1. ADJ

/ dɪˌfɔrˈmeɪʃənəl / (AmE), / dɪˌfɔːˈmeɪʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or causing the change in shape or size of a material body or geometric figure
causing or relating to deformation

Examples

The engineers studied the deformational behavior of metals under extreme heat.
Seismic waves can produce significant deformational effects on the earth’s crust.
This theory explains the deformational processes involved in the folding of rock layers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deformation - the process of changing in shape or size

Etymology

From 'deformation' (from Latin 'deformatio', alteration of shape) + '-al' (forming adjectives).
