morphia

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔːrfiə / (AmE), / ˈmɔːfɪə / (BrE)
A powerful narcotic drug derived from opium, used medically to relieve pain; morphine.
narcotic drug from opium, morphine
medical

Examples

The doctor administered morphia to ease the patient's severe pain.
He became addicted to morphia after undergoing surgery for a broken leg.
In the nineteenth century, morphia was widely used in hospitals despite its addictive properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morphine - a powerful narcotic drug derived from opium, used to relieve pain

Etymology

From Modern Latin 'morphium', from German 'Morphium', named after Morpheus, the Greek god of dreams.
