anaerobe

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.əˌroʊb / (AmE), / ˈæn.ə.rəʊb / (BrE)
an organism, especially a bacterium, that does not require air or free oxygen to live
organism not needing oxygen
medical

Examples

Some bacteria are anaerobes and can survive in environments without oxygen.
Clostridium is a well-known genus of obligate anaerobes.
During the experiment, scientists isolated several anaerobe species from the deep soil samples.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aerobe - organism that requires air or oxygen

Etymology

From Greek an- (not) + aer (air) + -obe (organism).
