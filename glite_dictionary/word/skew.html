skew

1. ADJ

/ skju / (AmE), / skju / (BrE)
Having an oblique, slanting, or not straight direction or position; set at an angle.
set at a slant or angle

Examples

The old painting looked skew on the living room wall until we adjusted it.
His tie was slightly skew, giving him a rather disheveled appearance.
Because the shelves were skew, the books kept sliding off.

Synset Examples

the picture was skew

Related Terms

  • Antonym: straight - following a direct or uncurved line or direction

Etymology

From Old North French *escuier* ('to turn aside, escape'); influenced by Old Norse *skjūgr* ('oblique, askew').

2. VERB

/ skju / (AmE), / skjuː / (BrE)
to turn or place at an angle; to make something not straight or not parallel
to turn or place at an angle

Examples

She tried to skew the picture frame to match the angle of the window.
The data may be skewed if the measurements are not taken properly.
He skewed his hat to the side for a more casual look.

Synset Examples

the lines on the sheet of paper are skewed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tilt - to move or cause to move into a sloping position
  • Antonym: to straighten - to make or become straight

Etymology

From Old North French 'escuier' (to turn aside), related to Old French 'esquier' (to oblique).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00467397-v
Synset POS: v
