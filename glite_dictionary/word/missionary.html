missionary

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɪʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˈmɪʃənəri / (BrE)
Relating to or connected with a religious mission or the work of spreading a religion.
relating to a religious mission

Examples

The organization focuses on missionary work in rural communities.
She lived in a missionary settlement for several years.
Their missionary activities include building schools and providing healthcare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evangelical - Relating to spreading the Christian gospel, often with missionary activity

Etymology

From Latin 'missionarius', from 'missio' (sending, mission).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˈmɪʃənəri / (BrE)
a person who is sent to promote and spread a particular religion or set of beliefs, especially by converting others
person sent to convert others to a belief

Examples

The missionary traveled to remote villages to spread his faith.
As a missionary, she worked to build schools and provide medical care alongside religious teaching.
The missionary faced many challenges while trying to introduce new ideas to the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evangelist - someone who seeks to convert others to the Christian faith; preacher of the gospel

Etymology

From modern Latin 'missionarius', from 'missio' (sending, mission).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10342615-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɪʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˈmɪʃənəri / (BrE)
a person who is sent to a foreign country to teach about a religion, do charitable work, or both, often as part of a religious mission
person on a religious or charitable mission

Examples

The missionary spent several years working in remote villages.
Many missionaries built schools and hospitals in the region.
She traveled to Africa as a missionary to provide educational support.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evangelist - a person who tries to convert others to a particular religion, especially Christianity

Etymology

From Latin missio (sending, mission), via French missionnaire.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10342840-n
Synset POS: n
