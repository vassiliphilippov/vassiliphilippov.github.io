banquet

1. VERB

/ ˈbæŋ.kwət / (AmE), / ˈbaŋ.kwɪt / (BrE)
to attend or participate in a formal meal or feast, often involving elaborate food and celebration
to attend a formal feast

Examples

The guests banqueted on delicacies from around the world.
During the holiday, the entire family banqueted in honor of their ancestors.
Nobles often banquet in the grand hall after a successful hunt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feast - to eat and drink sumptuously

Etymology

From Old French 'banquet' (feast), from Italian 'banchetto', diminutive of 'banco' (bench, table).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæŋkwət / (AmE), / ˈbaŋkwɪt / (BrE)
a formal meal or feast for a large number of people, often held to celebrate a particular event or occasion
formal ceremonial dinner or feast

Examples

The company hosted a lavish banquet to honor its retiring CEO.
Hundreds of guests attended the wedding banquet at the grand hotel.
The annual awards banquet will be held in the university dining hall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feast - a large meal, typically a celebratory one
  • Synonym: reception - a formal social occasion, often with food

Etymology

From Old French 'banquet' (feast), from Italian 'banchetto' (little bench), diminutive of 'banco' (bench).

3. NOUN

/ ˈbæŋkwət / (AmE), / ˈbaŋkwɪt / (BrE)
a large and elaborate meal, often for many people and usually to celebrate a special occasion
elaborate celebratory meal

Examples

After the award ceremony, everyone enjoyed a delicious banquet prepared by top chefs.
The annual charity banquet raised thousands of dollars for local children.

4. VERB

/ ˈbæŋkwət / (AmE), / ˈbaŋkwɪt / (BrE)
to entertain someone with a formal meal or feast; to provide a banquet for.
entertain with a formal feast

Examples

The king decided to banquet his guests in the grand hall.
They banqueted foreign dignitaries with an elaborate spread.
Each year, the company banquets its employees to celebrate their achievements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feast - to entertain sumptuously with food and drink

Etymology

From French 'banquet', from Italian 'banchetto' (little bench, feast).
