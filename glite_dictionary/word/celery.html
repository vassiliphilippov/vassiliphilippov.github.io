celery

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛləri / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.əri / (BrE)
A widely cultivated plant with crisp, pale green leaf stalks that are eaten raw or cooked as a vegetable.
edible plant with crisp green stalks

Examples

She added chopped celery to the salad for extra crunch.
A stick of celery makes a healthy snack with some peanut butter.
The soup recipe calls for onions, carrots, and celery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parsley - an aromatic herb often used in cooking, related to celery

Etymology

From French céleri, from Italian sedano, influenced by Latin selinon and Greek selinon (parsley).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛl.ə.ri / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.əri / (BrE)
A crunchy green vegetable with long stalks and feathery leaves, eaten raw, cooked, or used as seasoning.
crunchy green vegetable; edible stalks

Examples

She packed some celery sticks in her lunch for a healthy snack.
The soup recipe calls for chopped celery and onions.
He dipped a piece of celery into the creamy cheese spread.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parsley - an herb with curly or flat leaves, related to celery

Etymology

From French céleri, from Italian sedano, influenced by Latin selinon.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɛl.əri / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.əri / (BrE)
A cultivated plant with long, crisp, pale green stalks and feathery leaves, eaten raw or cooked as a vegetable.
crisp vegetable with edible stalks

Examples

She added chopped celery to the salad for extra crunch.
A fresh stick of celery makes a healthy snack.
The soup recipe calls for diced celery, carrots, and onions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parsley - a closely related aromatic herb
  • Synonym: fennel - an aromatic plant with edible stalks, related to celery

Etymology

From French *céleri*, from Italian *sedano* (variant *seleri*), ultimately from Greek *selinon* (parsley).
