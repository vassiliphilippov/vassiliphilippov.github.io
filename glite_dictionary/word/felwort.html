felwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛlˌwɝt / (AmE), / ˈfel.wɜːt / (BrE)
A European and Asian gentian plant having creamy white, fringed flowers, especially of the species Swertia perennis.
a gentian plant with creamy white fringed flowers

Examples

The meadow was dotted with felwort, its white fringed flowers catching the morning sun.
Botanists recorded several felwort plants along the riverbank in early summer.
Felwort thrives in damp meadows across parts of Europe and Asia.

Etymology

From Old English 'felwyrt', combining 'fel' (gall, bitterness) and 'wyrt' (plant, herb).
