basilary

1. ADJ

/ ˈbæsəˌleri / (AmE), / ˈbæsɪləri / (BrE)
of or relating to the base, especially situated at or forming the base of something, often used in anatomical or scientific contexts.
relating to a base, especially in anatomy

Examples

The doctor explained how the basilary artery supplies blood to the brainstem.
Microscopic analysis revealed damage to the basilary membrane in the patient's cochlea.
Researchers studied the basilary plate's role in cellular regeneration.

Synset Examples

the basilar membrane of the cochlea

Etymology

From Medieval Latin basilaris, from Latin basis (base).
