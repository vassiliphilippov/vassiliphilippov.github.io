gesso

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛsoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛsəʊ / (BrE)
A white coating composed chiefly of gypsum or plaster of Paris, used to prepare surfaces for painting or gilding, or the prepared surface itself.
plaster coating used for painting surfaces

Examples

Before applying oil paint, the artist covered the canvas with gesso to create a smooth surface.
The gesso was sanded between coats for a flawlessly prepared wooden panel.
Fresco painters often used a base layer of gesso to enhance color vibrancy.

Etymology

From Italian 'gesso', meaning 'gypsum,' from Latin 'gypsum' (plaster).
