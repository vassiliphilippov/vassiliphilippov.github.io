Anglo-Norman

1. NOUN

/ ˌæŋɡloʊ ˈnɔrmən / (AmE), / ˌæŋɡləʊ ˈnɔːmən / (BrE)
the variety of Norman French spoken and written in England, especially in the period from the 11th to the 15th centuries
Norman French language used in medieval England

Examples

Many legal documents in medieval England were written in Anglo-Norman.
Scholars must learn Anglo-Norman to study original texts from that era.
The influence of Anglo-Norman is still seen in modern English law vocabulary.

Etymology

From ‘Anglo-’ (England) and ‘Norman’ (from Latin Normannus, Norseman), indicating the Norman dialect in England.
