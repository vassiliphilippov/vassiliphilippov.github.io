cryptanalysis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪptəˈnæləsɪs / (AmE), / ˌkrɪptəˈnæləsɪs / (BrE)
the study or practice of analyzing and breaking codes, ciphers, or cryptographic systems
analysis of codes and ciphers

Examples

She specialized in cryptanalysis, working to decipher enemy communications during the war.
Advancements in cryptanalysis have led to stronger encryption methods.
The college offers an advanced course in cryptanalysis and information security.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cryptography - the practice and study of techniques for secure communication
  • Synonym: codebreaking - the act of deciphering coded messages

Etymology

From Greek 'kryptos' (hidden) + 'analysis'.
