second-rater

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛk.əndˈɹeɪ.tər / (AmE), / ˌsɛk.əndˈɹeɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person of mediocre or second-rate ability or quality; someone who is not outstanding or competent in a particular field
person of mediocre ability

Examples

Despite his efforts, he was dismissed as a second-rater compared to the rest of the team.
The novel is filled with second-raters who never reach their full potential.
No one wanted to be seen as a mere second-rater in the competitive industry.

Synset Examples

a team of aging second-raters
shone among the mediocrities who surrounded him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mediocrity - a person of mediocre ability; lack of distinction
  • Antonym: first-rater - a person of first-rate ability or quality; an outstanding individual

Etymology

From 'second-rate' (mediocre in quality) + '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkəndˌreɪtər / (AmE), / ˈsɛkəndˌreɪtə / (BrE)
a person who is regarded as being of inferior quality or ability compared to others
a person of inferior quality or status

Examples

Being called a second-rater motivated him to work harder and prove his worth.
She refused to let anyone treat her as a second-rater in the office.
Despite her talents, she always saw herself as a second-rater among her ambitious peers.

Synset Examples

Being called a second-rater motivated him to improve.
He felt like a second-rater compared to his peers.
She never thought of herself as a second-rater.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mediocrity - the quality of being average or not very good
  • Synonym: underdog - a person who is expected to lose or be at a disadvantage
  • Antonym: high-flier - a person who is very successful or talented

Etymology

From 'second-rate', meaning of mediocre or inferior quality, combined with the suffix '-er' to denote a person.
