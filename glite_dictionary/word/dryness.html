dryness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ.nəs / (BrE)
a manner of being unemotional, detached, or lacking warmth, especially in speech or behavior
detachment or lack of emotion

Examples

Her dryness during the meeting made her seem aloof to the others.
The dryness in his tone suggested he was not emotionally involved.
Some critics dislike the dryness of academic writing, preferring a warmer style.

Synset Examples

her manner assumed a dispassion and dryness very unlike her usual tone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detachment - the state of being objective or reserved in manner
  • Antonym: warmth - emotional enthusiasm or affection; opposite of detachment in manner

Etymology

Formed from 'dry' (Old English 'drȳge') + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ.nəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being without moisture or liquid, especially water
state of being dry; lack of moisture

Examples

Many skin problems are caused by dryness during the winter months.
The dryness of the soil made it difficult for crops to grow.
He noticed a persistent dryness in his throat after the long flight.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: moisture - presence of slight wetness; opposite of dryness
  • Synonym: aridity - extreme dryness; severe lack of moisture

Etymology

From Middle English 'dryness', from Old English 'drīġness', formed from 'dry' + '-ness'.
