agreement

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡriːmənt / (AmE), / əˈɡriːmənt / (BrE)
a situation in which people have the same opinion or make a joint decision; harmony of views or actions
harmony of opinions or actions

Examples

Despite their differences, the team reached an agreement on the new policy.
Both parties are finally in agreement about the terms of the contract.
There was general agreement that the event was a success.

Synset Examples

the two parties were in agreement

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disagreement - lack of harmony or consensus among people
  • Synonym: consensus - general agreement among a group of people

Etymology

From Old French 'agreement', from 'agreer' (to agree), from Latin 'ad-' + 'gratus' (pleasing).
