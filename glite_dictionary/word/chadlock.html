chadlock

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæd.lɑk / (AmE), / ˈtʃæd.lɒk / (BrE)
A weedy Eurasian plant, often regarded as a pest in grain fields.
a weedy pest plant in grain fields

Examples

Farmers often struggle with chadlock invading their wheat fields each spring.
The presence of chadlock can significantly reduce grain crop yields if not managed.
She studied the effects of chadlock on agricultural soil quality for her thesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corn cockle - a similar weedy plant often found in grain fields
