alcoholism

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.kəˌhɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˈæl.kəʊˌhɪ.zəm / (BrE)
Addiction to alcohol, characterized by a chronic and compulsive need to consume alcoholic drinks, leading to health and social problems.
addiction to alcohol
medical

Examples

His battle with alcoholism nearly destroyed his family life.
Medical professionals now recognize alcoholism as a serious disease that requires treatment.
Public awareness campaigns aim to reduce the stigma associated with alcoholism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: addiction - the state of being addicted to a substance or activity

Etymology

From alcohol + -ism (denoting a pathological condition).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæl.kəˌhɒl.ɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈæl.kəˌhɒl.ɪz.əm / (BrE)
A chronic disorder marked by an uncontrollable and persistent urge to consume excessive amounts of alcoholic drinks, often resulting in physical and mental health problems.
chronic excessive drinking disorder
medical

Examples

His family was deeply affected by his struggle with alcoholism.
Many treatment programs are available to help individuals recover from alcoholism.
The risks of developing health problems increase with long-term alcoholism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alcohol dependence - a condition in which a person feels compelled to consume alcohol
  • Antonym: sobriety - the state of not being intoxicated by alcohol

Etymology

From 'alcohol' + '-ism,' denoting a state or condition.
