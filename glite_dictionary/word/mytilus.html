Mytilus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪ.tɪ.ləs / (AmE), / ˈmɪ.tɪ.ləs / (BrE)
A genus of smooth-shelled marine mussels, forming the type genus of the family Mytilidae.
genus of smooth-shelled marine mussels

Examples

Scientists collected specimens of Mytilus from the rocky shore for their study.
The blue mussel, known scientifically as Mytilus edulis, is commonly found in European waters.
Several species within the genus Mytilus are important in aquaculture around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mussel - a type of edible bivalve mollusk

Etymology

From Latin 'mytilus', meaning 'mussel', from Greek 'mytilos'.
