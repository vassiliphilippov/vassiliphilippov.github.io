university

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːnɪˈvɜrsəti / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪˈvɜːsəti / (BrE)
The collective body of faculty and students at a university.
body of faculty and students at a university

Examples

The university gathered to celebrate its 100th anniversary.
The university voted to adopt a new honor code this year.
The university will be holding a special lecture series open to all students and staff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: college - the body of students and teachers of a college

Etymology

From Latin 'universitas' (community of teachers and scholars).

2. NOUN

/ juːnɪˈvɜrsəti / (AmE), / juːnɪˈvɜːsəti / (BrE)
an institution of higher education that offers courses and degrees in various fields and carries out research
institution of higher education

Examples

She hopes to attend a university in the city next year.
The university offers a wide range of courses in science and the arts.
After graduating from university, he started working in finance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: college - an institution of higher education smaller than a university, often focused on undergraduate studies

Etymology

From Latin 'universitas' (the whole, a society or corporation), especially used in the phrase 'universitas magistrorum et scholarium' (community of teachers and scholars).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ juːnɪˈvɝːsəti / (AmE), / juːnɪˈvɜːsəti / (BrE)
An institution of higher education that offers undergraduate and postgraduate degrees and research opportunities.
institution of higher education

Examples

She plans to attend a leading university to study engineering.
The university provides state-of-the-art research facilities for students and faculty.
After four years at university, he earned his degree in biology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: college - an institution of higher education, often smaller than a university

Etymology

From Latin universitas (a whole, a corporation, society, or company).
