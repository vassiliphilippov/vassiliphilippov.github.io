videlicet

1. ADV

/ vɪˈdɛlɪsɛt / (AmE), / vɪˈdiːlɪsɛt / (BrE)
used to introduce a detailed list, explanation, or summary, meaning 'namely' or 'as follows'
namely; as follows
archaic

Examples

The contract lists several parties, videlicet: the buyer, the seller, and the broker.
The board appointed three new members, videlicet Dr. Smith, Ms. Lee, and Mr. O'Brien.
His research focused on three topics—videlicet, climate change, biodiversity, and energy policy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: namely - used to introduce specific details or examples

Etymology

From Latin vide licet, meaning 'it is permitted to see,' commonly abbreviated as 'viz.'
