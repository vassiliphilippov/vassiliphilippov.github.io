NSA

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.ɛsˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛn.ɛsˈeɪ / (BrE)
the United States government agency responsible for cryptologic and intelligence activities, including the protection of U.S. information systems and the production of foreign intelligence.
U.S. cryptologic intelligence agency
american

Examples

The NSA monitors electronic communications worldwide as part of its intelligence-gathering mission.
Revelations about the NSA's surveillance practices sparked international debate.
My cousin works as a cryptanalyst for the NSA in Maryland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: CIA - Central Intelligence Agency, U.S. foreign intelligence service

Etymology

Acronym for National Security Agency.
