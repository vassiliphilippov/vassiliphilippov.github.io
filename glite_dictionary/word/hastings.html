Hastings

1. PROPN

/ ˈheɪstɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈheɪstɪŋz / (BrE)
A town in East Sussex, England, near the site of the Battle of Hastings in 1066.
town in East Sussex, England

Examples

We spent our summer holidays in Hastings exploring the historic old town.
Many tourists visit Hastings for its beautiful coastline and medieval sites.
The train from London to Hastings takes about one and a half hours.

2. PROPN

/ ˈheɪstɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈheɪstɪŋz / (BrE)
the Battle of Hastings, a decisive conflict in 1066 in which William the Conqueror defeated the Saxons, leading to the Norman Conquest of England.
battle in 1066 leading to Norman Conquest

Examples

The Battle of Hastings marked the beginning of Norman rule in England.
Many historians consider Hastings to be one of the most significant battles in English history.
William the Conqueror emerged victorious at Hastings in 1066.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Norman Conquest - the invasion and occupation of England by the Normans after 1066

Etymology

Named after the town of Hastings in East Sussex, England, near where the battle was fought.
