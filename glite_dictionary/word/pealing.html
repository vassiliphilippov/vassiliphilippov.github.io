pealing

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
a deep, prolonged sound, especially one made by thunder or large bells
deep, prolonged sound (as of bells or thunder)

Examples

The pealing of the church bells echoed through the valley on Christmas morning.
A distant pealing of thunder signaled the approach of the storm.
She paused to listen to the pealing that filled the air from the ancient cathedral.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peal - a loud ringing of a bell or bells

Etymology

From 'peal' (to sound loudly), ultimately from Old French 'appel' (call).
