impeller

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɛlər / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛlə / (BrE)
A rotating part with blades, used to increase the pressure and flow of a fluid, especially in a pump or compressor.
rotating blade in a pump or engine

Examples

The broken impeller was replaced to restore full function to the water pump.
A jet engine relies on the impeller to compress air efficiently before combustion.
Engineers analyzed the design of each impeller blade to improve fuel efficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rotor - the rotating part of a mechanical device

Etymology

From Latin 'impellere' (to drive on, urge), via English 'impel' + '-er'.
