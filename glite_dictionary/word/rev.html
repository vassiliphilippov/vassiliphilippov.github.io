rev

1. VERB

/ rɛv / (AmE), / rɛv / (BrE)
(of an engine or vehicle) to increase the running speed of the engine, especially by pressing the accelerator.
increase engine speed

Examples

She likes to rev the engine before taking off down the road.
The mechanic told me not to rev the car when it's cold.
He could hear motorcycles rev loudly outside the window.

Synset Examples

rev up an engine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accelerate - to increase speed, especially in a vehicle

Etymology

Shortened form of 'revolution' (as in a revolution of an engine).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00440653-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ rɛv / (AmE), / rɛv / (BrE)
a revolution of an engine per minute or a unit for measuring the rotational speed of a motor
engine revolution; unit of motor speed
informal

Examples

The engine was running at 3000 revs per minute on the highway.
Racing cars often reach higher rev limits than standard automobiles.
Let the engine idle at low revs before you start driving.

Synset Examples

the engine was doing 6000 revs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revolution - one complete turn around a fixed point

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'revolution'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-15307088-n
Synset POS: n
