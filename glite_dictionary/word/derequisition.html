to derequisition

1. VERB

/ ˌdiːˌrɛkwəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːˌrɛkwɪˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
to release (something) from government or official control, especially property or resources previously requisitioned.
release from government control

Examples

After the war, the government decided to derequisition several buildings that had been used as military barracks.
The council voted to derequisition the farmland, returning it to its original owners.
Once the emergency ended, authorities began to derequisition supplies back to private individuals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: requisition - to officially demand or take for government use

Etymology

Formed by prefix de- (reverse) + requisition (to demand or take for official use).
