entellus

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈtɛləs / (AmE), / ɛnˈtɛləs / (BrE)
A langur monkey (Semnopithecus entellus) native to southern Asia, especially India, where it is often regarded as sacred.
a langur monkey of India, sacred in Hinduism

Examples

The entellus is commonly seen in temples, where it is considered a sacred animal.
Local villagers protect the entellus because of its religious significance.
An entellus perched on the temple roof, scanning the crowds below.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: langur - a type of long-tailed monkey of Asia

Etymology

From Modern Latin, used in taxonomy for the species name Semnopithecus entellus.
