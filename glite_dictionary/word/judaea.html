Judaea

1. PROPN

/ dʒuˈdiːə / (AmE), / dʒuˈdiːə / (BrE)
the southern region of ancient Palestine, constituting a Roman province at the time of Christ, formerly the Kingdom of Judah
ancient region and Roman province in southern Palestine

Examples

In the first century, Judaea was under direct Roman rule.
Many important events in the New Testament took place in Judaea.
Roman governors, known as procurators, administered Judaea during the early imperial period.

Etymology

From Latin 'Judaea', from Greek 'Ioudaia', from Hebrew 'Yəhuda' (Judah).
