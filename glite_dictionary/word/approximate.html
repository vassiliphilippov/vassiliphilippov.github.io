approximate

1. ADJ

/ əˈprɑksɪmət / (AmE), / əˈprɒksɪmət / (BrE)
(of things) situated or growing close together but not united.
located close together (not united)

Examples

The approximate leaves are clearly separate, though they nearly touch each other on the stem.
In this species, the branches are approximate, forming dense clusters without actually merging.
The botanist noted how the approximate buds grew in pairs, close but unattached.

Synset Examples

with heads close together
approximate leaves grow together but are not united

Etymology

From Latin 'approximatus', past participle of 'approximare' (to come near).

/ əˈprɑːksəmət / (AmE), / əˈprɒksɪmət / (BrE)

Examples

It is an approximate description, lacking some of the finer details.

5. VERB

to judge or estimate something with reasonable accuracy but not exactly; to make a rough calculation or assessment
make an estimate or rough judgment

Examples

It is difficult to approximate the number of people who attended the festival.
The scientists can approximate the age of the fossils based on carbon dating.
He tried to approximate how long the trip would take by car.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estimate - form an approximate judgment or calculation
  • Synonym: guess - form an opinion or estimate without sufficient information
  • Degree Higher: to calculate - determine mathematically or by reasoning

Etymology

From Latin approximare (to come near), from ad- (to) + proximus (nearest).
