Castile

1. PROPN

/ kæˈstil / (AmE), / kæˈstiːl / (BrE)
a region in central Spain, formerly a powerful kingdom that united with Aragon to form modern Spain in 1479
historic region and former kingdom in Spain

Examples

The kingdom of Castile played a crucial role in the unification of Spain.
Many medieval castles still stand in Castile today, attracting tourists from all over the world.
The plains of Castile are known for their wheat fields and wide-open landscapes.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Castilla', related to 'castillo' (castle), reflecting the area's many castles.
