unclouded

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈklaʊdɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈklaʊdɪd / (BrE)
(of the sky, weather, etc.) free from clouds; clear.
free from clouds; clear

Examples

We enjoyed a picnic beneath an unclouded sky.
Her future looked bright and unclouded after graduation.
The mountain was visible only on rare, unclouded mornings.

Synset Examples

under a cloudless sky

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloudless - (of the sky) without any clouds; clear

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'clouded'.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈklaʊdɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈklaʊdɪd / (BrE)
(of a liquid or view) clear; not made opaque or cloudy by sediment or impurities.
clear; not made cloudy or opaque

Examples

The wine was perfectly unclouded, showing its fine quality.
Through the unclouded glass, she could see the sun setting.
His memory of that day remained unclouded by sadness.

Synset Examples

the wine was unclouded

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clear - free of cloudiness, impurities, or obstructions

Etymology

From un- (not) + clouded (obscured or made cloudy).

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈklaʊdɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈklaʊdɪd / (BrE)
(of a person's mind or judgment) clear and untroubled; not disturbed by confusion or disorder.
clear and untroubled (mind or judgment)

Examples

After a good night's sleep, her unclouded mind was ready to face the day's challenges.
He approached the problem with an unclouded sense of judgment and made the right decision.
Meditation helped him maintain an unclouded perspective even during stressful times.

Synset Examples

an unclouded mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clear-headed - able to think clearly; not confused
  • Antonym: confused - unable to think clearly; mentally disordered

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' (not) to 'clouded' (obscured, troubled).

4. ADJ

/ ʌnˈklaʊdɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈklaʊdɪd / (BrE)
(of a sound or color) free from anything that dulls or dims; pure and clear.
free from anything that dulls; pure or clear

Examples

Her voice was unclouded and rang out across the hall without any distortion.
The artist preferred to use unclouded tones to make his paintings more vivid.
The recording captured an unclouded purity in the orchestra's strings.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dim - not bright or clear; lacking sharpness or clarity

Etymology

From un- (not) + clouded, from Old English clūd (mass of rock; later 'cloud')
