matricentric

1. ADJ

/ ˌmætrɪˈsɛntrɪk / (AmE), / ˌmætrɪˈsɛntrɪk / (BrE)
(of a family, society, or system) having the mother as the central figure or authority.
centered around the mother

Examples

The household followed a matricentric structure, with all major decisions made by the mother.
Many indigenous societies are described as matricentric rather than patriarchal.
In matricentric cultures, family lines and inheritance often pass through the mother.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: patricentric - centered upon the father
  • Synonym: matriarchal - ruled or led by women, especially mothers

Etymology

From Latin 'mater' (mother) + 'centric' (centered).
