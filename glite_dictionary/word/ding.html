ding

1. NOUN

/ dɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪŋ / (BrE)
A small dent or impression in a surface caused by a blow or impact.
small dent or impression

Examples

There was a ding in the car door after the hailstorm.
She noticed a small ding on her phone after dropping it.
The suitcase had several dings from rough handling during the trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dent - a slight hollow in a hard even surface made by a blow or pressure
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04700732-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ dɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪŋ / (BrE)
to make a short, high-pitched ringing sound, like that of a small bell
make a ringing sound like a bell

Examples

The timer will ding when your food is ready.
Her phone began to ding with new text messages.
The bell at the front desk dings when someone needs assistance.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: dong - to make a deep ringing sound, like a big bell
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02185620-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ dɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪŋ / (BrE)
a short, high-pitched ringing sound, like that of a bell being struck
a ringing sound, as of a bell

Examples

The elevator made a soft ding when it arrived on our floor.
You could hear the ding of the microwave from the other room.
A cheerful ding signaled the start of the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clang - a loud, resonant metallic sound
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07397857-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ dɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪŋ / (BrE)
a short, high-pitched sound, especially the notification tone made by a mobile device
notification sound from a mobile device

Examples

A sudden ding from her phone made her check her messages.
He turned off all notifications so he wouldn’t hear a ding every few minutes.
The ding signaled a new email had arrived during the meeting.

Synset Examples

A ding from my phone alerted me of the message.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beep - a short, high-pitched electronic sound

Etymology

Imitative origin, mimicking the sound produced by a bell; expanded in digital age to refer to electronic alerts.
