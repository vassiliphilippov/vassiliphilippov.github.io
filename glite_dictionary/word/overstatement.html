overstatement

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərˈsteɪtmənt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈsteɪtmənt / (BrE)
the act of describing something as more important or serious than it actually is; exaggeration
exaggeration; making something seem more important

Examples

Calling the project a complete failure is an overstatement; there were actually some notable successes.
It's an overstatement to say he revolutionized the industry, though he did make significant contributions.
Her claim that everyone was upset by the news was clearly an overstatement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exaggeration - the act of making something seem more important or larger than it is
  • Antonym: understatement - a statement that makes something seem less important or serious than it actually is

Etymology

Formed from 'overstate' + '-ment', dating to mid-18th century English.
