to liquify

1. VERB

/ ˈlɪkwəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪfʌɪ / (BrE)
to become liquid or fluid, especially as a result of heating
become liquid (esp. when heated)

Examples

When the ice cream was left on the table, it began to liquify quickly.
The heat caused the metal to liquify and fill the mold.
As the candle burned, the wax would slowly liquify and drip down the sides.

Synset Examples

the frozen fat liquefied

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to solidify - to make or become solid
  • Synonym: to liquefy - alternate spelling meaning to become liquid or fluid

Etymology

From Latin 'liquefacere' (to make liquid), from 'liquere' (to be liquid) and 'facere' (to make).

2. VERB

/ ˈlɪkwɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make or become liquid, especially by heating or melting a solid substance
turn into liquid, especially by heating

Examples

Heat will liquify the wax in the candle.
The metal begins to liquify at a temperature of 1,000 degrees Celsius.
They used a blowtorch to liquify the frozen pipes.

Synset Examples

liquefy the silver

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to liquefy - to make or become liquid, especially by heating (variant spelling, identical meaning)

Etymology

From Latin 'liquefacere' (to make liquid), from 'liquere' (to be liquid) + 'facere' (to make).
