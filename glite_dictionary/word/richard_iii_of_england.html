Richard III of England

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɪʧərd ðə θɜrd ʌv ˈɪŋɡlənd / (AmE), / ˈrɪʧəd ðə ˈθɜːd əv ˈɪŋɡlənd / (BrE)
(1452–1485) King of England from 1483 to 1485; he seized the throne from his nephew Edward V and was defeated by Henry Tudor at the Battle of Bosworth Field, marking the end of the Plantagenet dynasty.
King of England (1483–1485), last Plantagenet ruler

Examples

Shakespeare’s play about Richard III of England depicts him as a villainous monarch.
Historians continue to debate the role of Richard III of England in the disappearance of the Princes in the Tower.
The defeat of Richard III of England at Bosworth Field marked the start of the Tudor dynasty.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Henry VII - King of England who defeated Richard III at Bosworth Field
  • Antonym: Edward V - Nephew whose throne was seized by Richard III
