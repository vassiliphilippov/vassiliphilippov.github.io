meniscus

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɪskəs / (AmE), / mɪˈnɪskəs / (BrE)
The curved upper surface of a liquid in a tube, caused by surface tension.
curved surface of liquid in a tube

Examples

The meniscus of the water in the graduated cylinder was concave.
Always read the measurement at the bottom of the meniscus for accurate results.
The meniscus forms because of the interaction between the liquid and the container's surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surface tension - the elastic tendency of liquids which makes them acquire the least surface area possible

Etymology

From Greek *mēniskos* (crescent), diminutive of *mēn* (moon).

2. NOUN

/ məˈnɪskəs / (AmE), / mɪˈnɪskəs / (BrE)
a lens that is concave on one side and convex on the other, used to focus or disperse light in optical instruments
lens curved concave on one side, convex on the other

Examples

The camera uses a meniscus to correct for image distortion.
A simple telescope may contain only a single meniscus lens.
He replaced the broken lens with a new meniscus to restore clarity.

Etymology

From Greek *meniskos* (crescent, diminutive of *mēnē* ‘moon’), referring to the curved shape.

3. NOUN

/ məˈnɪskəs / (AmE), / mɪˈnɪskəs / (BrE)
Anatomy: a crescent-shaped cartilage disk that acts as a cushion between the bones in a joint, especially in the knee.
cartilage disk in a joint
medical

Examples

The doctor said I had torn my meniscus during the basketball game.
Arthroscopic surgery was required to repair the damaged meniscus in his knee.
Physical therapy can help strengthen the joint after a meniscus injury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cartilage - firm, flexible connective tissue found in various forms in the body

Etymology

From Greek mēniskos (crescent), diminutive of mēnē (moon).
