banister

1. NOUN

/ ˈbænɪstər / (AmE), / ˈbænɪstə / (BrE)
a railing at the side of a staircase or balcony, designed to prevent people from falling
railing along stairs or balconies

Examples

She held onto the banister as she climbed the steep staircase.
The old wooden banister was beautifully carved and polished.
Children often like to slide down the banister for fun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: railing - a fence or barrier made of rails

Etymology

Alteration of the earlier word 'baluster', from French 'balustre', from Italian 'balaustro'.
