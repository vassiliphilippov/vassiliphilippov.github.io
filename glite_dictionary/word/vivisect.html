to vivisect

1. VERB

/ ˈvɪvəˌsɛkt / (AmE), / ˈvɪvɪsɛkt / (BrE)
to perform surgery or cut into a living body, especially for scientific or experimental purposes
cut into a living body for experimentation

Examples

Many medical students used to vivisect frogs for their laboratory classes.
Animal rights activists strongly oppose any attempt to vivisect animals for research.
The scientist refused to vivisect living creatures, believing it to be unethical.

Synset Examples

people no longer vivisect animals — it's considered unethical

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dissect - to cut apart a dead body or plant to study its internal parts

Etymology

From Latin 'vivus' (alive) + 'sectus', past participle of 'secare' (to cut).
