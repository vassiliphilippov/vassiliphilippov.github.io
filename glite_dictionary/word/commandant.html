commandant

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑː.mənˈdænt / (AmE), / ˌkɒm.ænˈdɑːnt / (BrE)
an officer who is in charge of a particular military unit or institution
officer in charge of a military unit

Examples

The commandant inspected the troops before the ceremony began.
After years of service, she became the commandant of the military academy.
The new commandant implemented several important changes during his tenure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commander - an officer in authority, often in charge of a military unit

Etymology

From French commandant (commanding), from commander (to command).
