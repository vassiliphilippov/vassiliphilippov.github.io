to parallelize

1. VERB

/ ˈpærəˌlɛlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpærəˌlɛlaɪz / (BrE)
to arrange or place (things) so that they are parallel to each other
arrange so as to be parallel

Examples

The engineer had to parallelize the wires inside the circuit to reduce interference.
The students were instructed to parallelize the desks before the exam began.
Carefully parallelizing the beams ensured that the structure would be stable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to align - to arrange in a straight line or in proper position

Etymology

From 'parallel' + '-ize', modeled after other verbs meaning 'to make (something) parallel'.
