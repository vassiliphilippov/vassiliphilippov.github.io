to decrepitate

1. VERB

/ dɪˈkrɛpɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈkrɛpɪteɪt / (BrE)
to crackle or burst noisily when heated, especially applied to certain salts or minerals in chemistry
crackle or burst noisily when heated

Examples

When strongly heated, the crystals decrepitate and split apart noisily.
As the salt began to decrepitate, a series of tiny pops filled the laboratory.
During the experiment, he watched the mineral decrepitate in the flame.

Synset Examples

The salt decrepitated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crackle - to make a series of short sharp noises

Etymology

From Latin 'decrepitare' (to rattle) from 'crepitare' (to crackle, rattle).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈkrɛpɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈkrɛpɪteɪt / (BrE)
(of a substance, especially a salt or mineral) to roast or heat so as to produce crackling noises or until crackling ceases.
roast to cause crackling (chemistry)

Examples

Heating the mineral until it decrepitates is a standard step in the analysis.
When salts are decrepitated, they emit a characteristic crackling noise.
The chemist carefully decrepitated the sample to eliminate all moisture.

Synset Examples

decrepitate salts

Etymology

From Latin 'decrepitare' (to crackle), from 'de-' (intensive) + 'crepitare' (to creak, crackle).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
