Cnemidophorus velox

1. NOUN

/ nɛmɪˌdɑfərəs ˈviːlɑks / (AmE), / nɛmɪˌdɪfəˈrɒr əs ˈviːlɒks / (BrE)
A species of whiptail lizard with distinct longitudinal stripes, native to the Colorado Plateau region from Arizona to western Colorado.
a striped whiptail lizard species

Examples

The Cnemidophorus velox is commonly found in arid regions of the southwestern United States.
Researchers observed the rapid movements of Cnemidophorus velox while conducting a field study in Colorado.
Distinctive stripes make the Cnemidophorus velox easy to identify among other lizard species.

Etymology

From Greek 'knēmis' (greave or shin-armor) and 'phoros' (bearing), with species name from Latin 'velox' (swift).
