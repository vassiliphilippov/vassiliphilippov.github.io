Maidu

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪduː / (AmE), / ˈmaɪduː / (BrE)
the Penutian language traditionally spoken by the Maidu people of northern California
Penutian language of the Maidu people

Examples

The Maidu language has several distinct dialects spoken across northern California.
She is working on a project to document and revitalize Maidu for future generations.
Few people today are fluent speakers of Maidu, making preservation efforts especially important.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Penutian - a proposed group of Native American languages of western North America

Etymology

From the self-designation of the Maidu people; ultimately from a Native American language.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪduː / (AmE), / ˈmaɪduː / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people traditionally living east of the Sacramento River in northern California
Native American people of northern California

Examples

The Maidu have a rich tradition of basket weaving and storytelling.
Anthropologists have studied the language and culture of the Maidu for many years.
A descendant of the Maidu tribe shared her family's oral history at the event.
