sanctification

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæŋktɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsaŋktɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a religious ceremony or act by which something is made holy or set apart as sacred
religious making of something holy

Examples

The sanctification of the new church was attended by the entire community.
According to tradition, the sanctification of the altar is performed by the bishop.
Special prayers were recited during the sanctification of the holy water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consecration - the act of declaring something sacred
  • Antonym: desecration - the act of treating something sacred with disrespect

Etymology

From Late Latin sanctificatio (making holy), from sanctificare (to make holy).
