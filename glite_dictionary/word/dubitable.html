dubitable

1. ADJ

/ ˈdjuːbɪtəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈdjuːbɪtəbl̩ / (BrE)
Open to doubt or question; uncertain or questionable.
open to doubt; questionable

Examples

The result of the experiment was dubitable given the lack of proper controls.
It remains dubitable whether the documents are authentic or not.
His explanation sounded plausible, but some facts were undeniably dubitable.

Synset Examples

the candidate's doubtful past
he has a dubious record indeed
what one found uncertain the other found dubious or downright false
it was more than dubitable whether the friend was as influential as she thought

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dubious - hesitating or doubting; not to be relied upon
  • Antonym: indubitable - impossible to doubt; unquestionable

Etymology

From Latin 'dubitabilis', from 'dubitare' (to doubt).
