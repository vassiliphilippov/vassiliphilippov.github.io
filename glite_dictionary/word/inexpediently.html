inexpediently

1. ADV

/ ˌɪnɪkˈspiːdiəntli / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪkˈspiːdiəntli / (BrE)
In a manner that is not suitable or advisable under the circumstances; imprudently or unwisely.
in an unsuitable or inadvisable way

Examples

The changes were inexpediently introduced without properly consulting the experts.
She inexpediently ignored the warnings, leading to further complications.
The plan was inexpediently executed, causing unnecessary delays and confusion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: expediently - in a suitable or advantageous manner

Etymology

From in- (not) + expedient + -ly (adverbial suffix).
