paterfamilias

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪtərfəˈmɪliəs / (AmE), / ˌpatəfəˈmɪliəs / (BrE)
The male head of a family or household, especially in ancient Rome.
male head of a family

Examples

In Roman society, the paterfamilias had absolute authority over household affairs.
As the paterfamilias, he made all the important decisions for the extended family.
Even today, some families still look to a paterfamilias figure for guidance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: matriarch - female head of a family or tribe

Etymology

From Latin, from pater (father) + familias (of the family).
