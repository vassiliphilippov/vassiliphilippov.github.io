Melicoccus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛlɪˈkɑkəs / (AmE), / ˌmiːlɪˈkɒkəs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American trees and shrubs that bear berries, part of the soapberry family.
genus of tropical trees or shrubs bearing berries

Examples

The Melicoccus tree is prized for its sweet, edible fruit in many Caribbean countries.
Botanists studied several species within the Melicoccus genus for their unique berry production.
Farmers are learning new methods to cultivate Melicoccus in tropical regions.

Etymology

From New Latin, derived from Greek 'mēlon' (apple) and 'kokkos' (berry, seed).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛlɪˈkɑkəs / (AmE), / ˌmɛlɪˈkɒkəs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American trees and shrubs in the soapberry family, bearing small edible berries.
genus of tropical American fruit trees

Examples

The fruit of the Melicoccus genus is enjoyed in many Caribbean countries.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Melicoccus genus in Central America.
Farmers cultivate Melicoccus trees for their sweet, edible berries.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'meli' (honey) and 'kokkos' (berry, seed), referring to the sweet fruit.
