Diplotaxis erucoides

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪpləˈtæksɪs ˌɛruˈkɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˌdɪpləˈtæksɪs ˌɛrʊˈkɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant in the mustard family, native to the Mediterranean region and widely naturalized in southern Europe, commonly known as white rocket.
Mediterranean flowering plant; white rocket

Examples

Diplotaxis erucoides often grows abundantly in Mediterranean fields and roadsides.
Botanists have studied the spread of Diplotaxis erucoides across southern Europe.
The delicate white flowers of Diplotaxis erucoides add a touch of brightness to wild landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white rocket - Common name for Diplotaxis erucoides

Etymology

From Greek 'diplos' (double) and 'taxis' (arrangement), referring to the plant's seed pod structure; 'erucoides' means 'resembling Eruca' (a related genus).
