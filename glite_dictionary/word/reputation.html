reputation

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛpjəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛpjʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
The beliefs or opinions that are generally held about someone or something, especially relating to a particular characteristic or quality.
beliefs held about someone or something

Examples

He quickly gained a reputation for being honest and reliable.
The restaurant has a reputation for excellent service and delicious food.
After the scandal, her reputation was damaged in the community.

Synset Examples

his reputation for promiscuity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notoriety - the state of being famous or well known for some negative quality or deed

Etymology

From Latin reputatio (consideration), from reputare (to reckon, consider).

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛpjəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛpjʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the opinion that people have about how good or honorable someone or something is, based on what has happened in the past
the opinion others have about someone or something

Examples

After the scandal, the company's reputation was severely damaged.
She worked hard to build a reputation for honesty and professionalism.
Their restaurant has a reputation for serving the best seafood in town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prestige - respect and admiration felt for someone based on their achievements or quality
  • Antonym: disrepute - the state of being held in low esteem by the public

Etymology

From Latin 'reputatio' (consideration, reckoning), from 'reputare' (to consider, count over).

3. NOUN

/ ˌrɛp.jəˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛp.jʊˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the general opinion or regard that the public has for someone or something, based on their past actions or character
general public opinion about a person or thing

Examples

Her excellent reputation in the company helped her earn a well-deserved promotion.
The actor’s reputation steadily grew after he starred in several successful films.
Despite the rumors, he worked hard to restore his reputation within the community.

Synset Examples

he acquired a reputation as an actor before he started writing
he was a person of bad report

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fame - the state of being known by many people for notable achievements
  • Antonym: notoriety - the state of being famous or well known for some bad quality or deed

Etymology

From Latin 'reputatio' (consideration), from 'reputare' (to think over, consider).
