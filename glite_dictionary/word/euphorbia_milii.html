Euphorbia milii

1. NOUN

/ juːˈfɔr.bi.ə ˈmɪl.i.aɪ / (AmE), / juːˈfɔː.bɪ.ə ˈmɪl.ii / (BrE)
A species of spiny, bushy shrub native to Madagascar, also known as the crown of thorns, with long, woody stems, sparse leaves, and small flowers surrounded by red or scarlet bracts.
spiny shrub known as crown of thorns

Examples

The gardener carefully transplanted the Euphorbia milii into a larger pot to give it more space.
Many people admire the bright red bracts of Euphorbia milii in tropical gardens.
Because of its hardy nature, Euphorbia milii is popular as a decorative indoor plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crown of thorns - common name for Euphorbia milii, a spiny flowering plant

Etymology

From the genus name Euphorbia, after Euphorbus (ancient Greek physician), and species name milli, honoring Baron Milius, a 19th-century governor of Réunion.
