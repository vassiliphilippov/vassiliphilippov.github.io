companionship

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpænjənˌʃɪp / (AmE), / kəmˈpænjənʃɪp / (BrE)
the pleasant feeling of being with someone and having their company
the state of being with someone

Examples

For many elderly people, companionship is just as important as medical care.
After moving to a new city, she found companionship in a local book club.
Dogs are known for providing loyal companionship to their owners.

Synset Examples

he missed their company
he enjoyed the society of his friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: company - the fact or condition of being with others, especially socially

Etymology

From companion + -ship, with companion from Old French compagnon (companion, associate).
