milo

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈmiːləʊ / (BrE)
a type of drought-resistant sorghum with large yellow or whitish grains, often grown for grain or as livestock feed
a drought-resistant sorghum grain

Examples

The farmer planted milo because it thrives during dry seasons.
Many livestock feeds contain ground milo as a main ingredient.
The warm climate and absence of rain favored the growth of milo over other grains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorghum - a genus of grass cultivated for its edible grain

Etymology

From Afrikaans, from Sotho 'maili' or similar Bantu origin.
