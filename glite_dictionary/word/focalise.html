to focalise

1. VERB

/ ˈfoʊkəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkəlaɪz / (BrE)
to concentrate or localize in a particular place or area
concentrate or localize in one place

Examples

The doctor explained how the infection would focalise in one organ before spreading.
Scientists observed that the disease tended to focalise in specific regions of the brain.
Efforts were made to focalise the campaign on areas most affected by the drought.

Synset Examples

The infection has localized in the left eye

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to localize - to restrict something to a particular place
  • Antonym: to disperse - to spread widely or move apart

Etymology

From French 'focaliser,' from 'focal' meaning 'focus.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02698177-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈfoʊkəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkəlaɪz / (BrE)
to bring into focus or alignment; to cause to converge, especially of ideas or emotions
bring into focus; cause to converge
british

Examples

The meeting helped to focalise the team's efforts on the most pressing issues.
Her speech focalised the emotions of the crowd into a single purpose.
These disparate ideas began to focalise into a coherent strategy over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: focus - bring into focus or adjust
  • Synonym: to focalize - American spelling for 'focalise'

Etymology

From French *focaliser*, derived from "focal" (of a focus) plus "-iser" (-ize).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00733774-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈfoʊkəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkəlaɪz / (BrE)
to bring an image into clear focus, especially by adjusting a lens or optical device
bring an image into focus

Examples

He adjusted the camera to focalise the distant buildings more sharply.
The technician asked me to focalise the microscope before starting the experiment.
It took a moment to focalise the image on the projector screen.

Synset Examples

Please focus the image: we cannot enjoy the movie

Related Terms

  • Synonym: focus - to adjust an optical instrument to make an image clear

Etymology

From French 'focaliser', from 'focal' (focus) + '-iser' (-ize).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00315073-v
Synset POS: v

/ ˈfoʊ.kə.laɪz / (AmE), / ˈfəʊ.kə.laɪz / (BrE)
to become focused or come into focus, especially in an optical or figurative sense
to become focused or come into focus
british

Examples

The image in the microscope began to focalise as she adjusted the lens.

The blurry scene started to focalise, revealing the mountain landscape beyond.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
