Vigna radiata

1. NOUN

/ ˌvaɪɡnə ɹædˈiːətə / (AmE), / ˌvɪɡnə rædˈɪətə / (BrE)
a bushy annual plant widely cultivated in Asia and other warm regions for its edible seeds, commonly known as mung beans; the main source of bean sprouts in Chinese cooking.
plant species, also called mung bean

Examples

The seeds of Vigna radiata are commonly used to produce bean sprouts in Asian cuisine.
Farmers in India plant Vigna radiata because it is well-suited to their warm climate.
Scientists study Vigna radiata for its nutritional benefits and agricultural value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mung bean - common name for Vigna radiata, cultivated for edible seeds

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Vigna' (after Italian botanist Domenico Vigna); species epithet 'radiata' meaning 'rayed' or 'radiating'.
