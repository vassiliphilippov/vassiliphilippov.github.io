Vangueria madagascariensis

1. NOUN

/ væŋˈɡɪəriə ˌmædəˌɡæskəˈriːɛnsɪs / (AmE), / væŋˈɡɪəriə ˌmædəˌɡæskəˈriːɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A shrubby tree native to Madagascar, sometimes cultivated for its edible, apple-shaped fruit.
shrubby tree with edible fruit, native to Madagascar

Examples

The Vangueria madagascariensis produces sweet, apple-shaped fruit enjoyed by local communities.
Farmers in Madagascar sometimes cultivate Vangueria madagascariensis as a source of food and income.
Botanists studied the growth habits of Vangueria madagascariensis in different regions of the island.

Etymology

From Latin: Vangueria (genus name) + madagascariensis (of Madagascar, indicating place of origin).
