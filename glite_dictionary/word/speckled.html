speckled

1. ADJ

/ ˈspɛkəld / (AmE), / ˈspɛkld / (BrE)
having a pattern of small spots or marks of a different color scattered over a surface
having small dots or spots

Examples

The eggs had a speckled appearance, making them hard to spot among the leaves.
She wore a speckled scarf that added color to her outfit.
The speckled trout is common in this river during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotted - having spots
  • Antonym: plain - without any spots or marks

Etymology

From Middle English 'speckel' (a small spot), related to 'speck'.
