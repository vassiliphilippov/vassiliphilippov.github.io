stickleback

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪkəlˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈstɪkəlbak / (BrE)
a small, spiny-backed fish of northern fresh and coastal waters, commonly studied for its behavior and biology
small spiny-backed fish

Examples

Researchers often use the stickleback to study animal behavior and evolutionary adaptation.
I spotted a tiny stickleback darting beneath the surface of the stream.
During mating season, the male stickleback builds an elaborate nest to attract a mate.

Etymology

From Middle English 'stikelbak', from 'stickel' (spine or thorn) + 'bak' (back).
