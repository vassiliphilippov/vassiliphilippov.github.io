fist

1. NOUN

/ fɪst / (AmE), / fɪst / (BrE)
a hand with the fingers curled tightly into the palm, often for hitting or as a gesture
a hand with fingers clenched

Examples

He clenched his fist in anger during the argument.
She raised her fist in celebration after winning the race.
The boy pounded on the door with his small fist.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hand - the end part of a person's arm beyond the wrist, including the palm, fingers, and thumb

Etymology

From Old English 'fyst' (fist, hand), of Germanic origin.
