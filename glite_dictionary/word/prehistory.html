prehistory

1. NOUN

/ priˈhɪstəri / (AmE), / priˈhɪstəri / (BrE)
The period in human history before the existence of written records.
period before written records

Examples

Many ancient human achievements occurred in prehistory, leaving no written documentation.
Archaeologists study tools and bones to understand life in prehistory.
The cave paintings of Lascaux date from deep in prehistory.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: history - the study of past events, especially in human affairs

Etymology

From French préhistoire, from pré- (before) + histoire (history).
