Thessaloniki

1. PROPN

/ ˌθɛsəloʊˈniki / (AmE), / ˌθɛsələˈniːki / (BrE)
the second largest city of Greece, a major port on the Aegean Sea in the country's northeast
major Greek port city; Greece's second largest city

Examples

Every summer, tourists flock to Thessaloniki to enjoy its vibrant culture and seaside attractions.
During the Roman era, Thessaloniki served as a significant economic and administrative center.
She moved to Thessaloniki to pursue her university studies.

Etymology

From Greek Θεσσαλονίκη (Thessaloníki), named after Thessalonike of Macedon.
