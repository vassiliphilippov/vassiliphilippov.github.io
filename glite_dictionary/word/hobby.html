hobby

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːbi / (AmE), / ˈhɒbi / (BrE)
a child's toy horse mounted on rockers, which a child straddles and pretends to ride
a rocking horse toy for children

Examples

The old wooden hobby was passed down from generation to generation in their family.
As a child, she loved pretending to race on her hobby every afternoon.
He spent hours riding his hobby in the nursery, dreaming of being a cowboy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rocking horse - a toy horse mounted on rockers for a child to sit and rock on

Etymology

Originally a diminutive form of the name 'Hobin', used for horses in Middle English; 'hobby-horse' was the earlier term for such toys.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːbi / (AmE), / ˈhɒbi / (BrE)
a small Old World falcon (Falco subbuteo) once trained and flown in falconry to hunt small birds
a small species of falcon

Examples

The hobby is renowned for its agile flight and swift pursuit of prey.
During medieval times, falconers would often train a hobby for hunting.
A hobby can easily outmaneuver larger birds due to its speed and agility.

Etymology

From Middle English hobi, from Old French hobe, used for small hawks.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːbi / (AmE), / ˈhɒbi / (BrE)
An activity done regularly in one's leisure time for pleasure, rather than as a main occupation.
leisure activity for pleasure

Examples

Gardening has always been my favorite hobby because it helps me relax.
Photography started as a hobby but grew into a passionate pursuit for her.
He spends his weekends building model airplanes, which is his main hobby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pastime - an activity that someone does regularly for enjoyment
  • Synonym: avocation - a hobby or minor occupation
  • Antonym: occupation - a person's usual or principal work or business

Etymology

From Middle English 'hobyn', a small horse or pony, later associated with children's toy horses and then with pastimes.

Source: WordNet
