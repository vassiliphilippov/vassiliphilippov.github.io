slip-up

1. NOUN

/ ˈslɪp ˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈslɪp ʌp / (BrE)
a small or careless mistake, typically one made inadvertently in speech, writing, or action
minor mistake or error

Examples

The accountant made a slip-up and entered the wrong figure.
Everyone has a slip-up now and then, so don't be too hard on yourself.
His apology was sincere, admitting the slip-up in yesterday's report.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: error - a mistake
  • Synonym: blunder - a careless mistake
  • Antonym: correction - the action of correcting an error

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'slip up' meaning 'make a mistake', recorded from the mid-19th century.
