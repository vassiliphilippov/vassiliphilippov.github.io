Hygrocybe acutoconica

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈɡroʊ.saɪˌbi ˌæ.kju.toʊˈkɒ.nɪ.kə / (AmE), / hɪˌɡrɒ.sɪˈbiː ˌa.kjuː.təˈkɒ.nɪ.kə / (BrE)
A species of mushroom with an acutely conical cap and dry stems, commonly found in grassy areas.
a species of waxcap mushroom

Examples

The grassland was dotted with bright yellow Hygrocybe acutoconica mushrooms after the rain.
Mycology students studied Hygrocybe acutoconica for its distinctive sharply conical cap.
Photographers often seek out Hygrocybe acutoconica due to its vivid coloration and unique shape.

Etymology

From Greek 'hygros' (moist) + 'kybe' (head) and Latin 'acutus' (sharp) + 'conicus' (conical), referring to its sharply conical cap.
