somnolent

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɑːm.nələnt / (AmE), / ˈsɒm.nəl.ənt / (BrE)
sleepy; inclined to cause or experience drowsiness.
sleepy or causing drowsiness

Examples

The professor's somnolent voice made it hard for the students to stay awake.
A somnolent afternoon breeze drifted through the open window.
The medication left him feeling unusually somnolent during the day.

Synset Examples

slumberous (or slumbrous) eyes
‘slumbery’ is archaic
the sound had a somnolent effect

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drowsy - feeling sleepy or lethargic
  • Antonym: alert - fully awake and attentive

Etymology

From Latin somnolentus, from somnus (sleep).
