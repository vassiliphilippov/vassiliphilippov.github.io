alastrim

1. NOUN

/ əˈlæstrɪm / (AmE), / əˈlæstrɪm / (BrE)
A mild form of smallpox caused by a less virulent strain of the virus.
mild form of smallpox
medical

Examples

In the early 20th century, cases of alastrim were mistakenly believed to be chickenpox in some regions.
Public health campaigns targeted both alastrim and the more severe forms of smallpox for eradication.
Although alastrim was less deadly, it still posed significant public health risks before the development of vaccines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smallpox - a serious, contagious viral disease with fever and skin eruptions
