to bumble

1. VERB

/ ˈbʌm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈbʌm.bəl / (BrE)
to move or walk in an awkward, unsteady, or confused manner, often tripping or staggering
move awkwardly and unsteadily

Examples

The children bumble across the uneven field, laughing as they trip over tufts of grass.
After spraining his ankle, he could only bumble down the hallway, clinging to the wall for support.
In the darkness, she bumbled around the room searching for the light switch.

Synset Examples

The drunk man stumbled about

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stumble - to trip or lose one's balance while walking or running
  • Synonym: to totter - to move in a feeble or unsteady way

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English bumblen, frequentative of bum (to hum, buzz), imitating a clumsy movement or low humming.

2. VERB

/ ˈbʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˈbʌmbəl / (BrE)
to speak or move in a confused, awkward, or hesitant way; to speak haltingly
speak or act hesitantly/awkwardly

Examples

He began to bumble through his speech when he realized the audience was much larger than expected.
She tends to bumble whenever she speaks in public, which makes her seem nervous.
The witness bumbled his response, unable to find the right words under pressure.

Synset Examples

The speaker faltered when he saw his opponent enter the room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to falter - to lose strength or speak hesitantly
  • Synonym: stammer - to speak with involuntary pauses or repetitions

Etymology

Perhaps imitative of the sound of murmuring or bumbling.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00983576-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈbʌm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈbʌm.bəl / (BrE)
to perform a task or activity clumsily or ineptly, often resulting in mistakes or a poor outcome
do something clumsily or ineptly

Examples

He tried to fix the faucet but ended up bumbling the whole repair job.
She was so nervous during her first speech that she completely bum bled her lines.
They didn't prepare for the presentation and bumbled their way through the meeting.

Synset Examples

I botched the dinner and we had to eat out
the pianist screwed up the difficult passage in the second movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botch - to carry out a task badly or carelessly
  • Synonym: bungle - to do something badly or clumsily
