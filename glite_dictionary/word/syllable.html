syllable

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪləbəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪləbəl / (BrE)
A single unit of sound in a word, typically containing a vowel sound and forming part or all of a word.
unit of sound in a word

Examples

The word 'banana' has three syllables.
Try clapping your hands once for each syllable in the word.
She divided the word into syllables to help her spell it correctly.

Synset Examples

the word ‘pocket’ has two syllables

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: phoneme - the smallest unit of sound in speech
  • Degree Higher: morpheme - the smallest grammatical unit in a language

Etymology

From Latin 'syllaba', from Greek 'syllabē' (that which is held together, a syllable).
