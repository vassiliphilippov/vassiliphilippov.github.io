swipe

1. NOUN

/ swaɪp / (AmE), / swaɪp / (BrE)
a sweeping hit, blow, or movement, especially one made with force
sweeping stroke or blow

Examples

He took a wild swipe at the ball but missed completely.
With one swipe of her arm, she knocked the vase off the table.
The cat gave a sudden swipe at the toy mouse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blow - a strong hit or strike
  • Degree Lower: tap - a light touch or hit

Etymology

Probably from a dialectal or earlier form meaning 'to strike with a sweeping motion', of uncertain origin.

2. VERB

/ swaɪp / (AmE), / swaɪp / (BrE)
to steal something, especially in a quick or casual way
steal something
informal

Examples

He tried to swipe a wallet from her purse during the crowded festival.
Someone swiped my sandwich from the office fridge.
The thief managed to swipe several phones before anyone noticed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steal - to take something without permission or legal right
  • Synonym: to rob - to take property illegally from a person or place, often using force or threat
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02281711-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ swaɪp / (AmE), / swaɪp / (BrE)
to hit or strike something with a sweeping or swinging motion
strike with a sweeping motion

Examples

He took a wild swipe at the ball but missed completely.
The cat tried to swipe the toy off the table.
She swiped at the mosquito buzzing near her ear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hit - to strike someone or something with force
  • Degree Lower: tap - to hit lightly, especially with a slight movement
  • Degree Higher: slam - to hit or strike with great force
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01396854-v
Synset POS: v
