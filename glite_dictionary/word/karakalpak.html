Karakalpak

1. NOUN

/ kɑːrəˈkɑːlpɑːk / (AmE), / karəˈkalpak / (BrE)
a member of a Turkic ethnic group living near Lake Aral in central Asia, primarily in Uzbekistan
member of a Turkic ethnic group in central Asia

Examples

Many Karakalpaks continue to practice traditional crafts and agriculture.
The majority of the Karakalpak population lives in Karakalpakstan, an autonomous republic in Uzbekistan.
Historians have studied the migration patterns of the Karakalpak people for centuries.

Etymology

From Karakalpak, a Turkic language, literally 'black hat'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkærəkælˈpɑk / (AmE), / ˌkarəkælˈpak / (BrE)
the Turkic language spoken mainly in Karakalpakstan, an autonomous republic in Uzbekistan
Turkic language of Karakalpakstan, Uzbekistan

Examples

The Karakalpak language is recognized as one of the official languages in Karakalpakstan.
She is fluent in both Karakalpak and Russian, which helps her work with local communities.
Many ancient poems and stories have been preserved in the Karakalpak language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kazakh - a Turkic language spoken in Kazakhstan and surrounding regions
  • Synonym: Uzbek - a Turkic language spoken in Uzbekistan

Etymology

From Karakalpak, referring both to the people and their language; from Turkic roots 'kara' (black) + 'kalpak' (cap).
