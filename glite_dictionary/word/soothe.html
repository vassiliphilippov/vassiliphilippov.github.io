to soothe

1. VERB

/ suð / (AmE), / suːð / (BrE)
to make someone feel calmer or less upset; to reduce pain or discomfort
make calmer; reduce pain or distress

Examples

Gently rocking the baby helped to soothe her cries.
A warm cup of tea can really soothe a sore throat.
Listening to soft music in the evening soothes his nerves after a long day.

Synset Examples

the medicine soothes the pain of the inflammation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calm - to make someone less agitated or upset

Etymology

From Old English sōthian (to verify, prove to be true), from sōth (truth).

2. VERB

/ suːð / (AmE), / suːð / (BrE)
to comfort someone by providing moral or emotional support; to make someone feel less anxious or upset
to comfort; to ease emotional distress

Examples

She tried to soothe her friend after he received bad news.
A gentle voice can often soothe children when they are upset.
He soothed his own nerves by taking deep breaths before the interview.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comfort - to make someone feel better; console
  • Antonym: to agitate - to make someone feel anxious or disturbed

Etymology

Perhaps from Old English *sōthian* (to verify, show to be true), influenced by 'sooth' (truth).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
