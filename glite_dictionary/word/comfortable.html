comfortable

1. ADJ

/ ˈkʌmfərtəbəl / (AmE), / ˈkʌmfətəbl / (BrE)
giving physical ease and relaxation; free from pain, discomfort, or stress
physically relaxed and at ease

Examples

I bought a pair of comfortable shoes for my trip.
She settled into a comfortable chair by the window and started reading.
We stayed at a comfortable hotel near the beach.

Synset Examples

comfortable clothes
comfortable suburban houses
made himself comfortable in an armchair
the antihistamine made her feel more comfortable
are you comfortable?
feeling comfy now?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uncomfortable - feeling physical discomfort or unease
  • Synonym: comfy - informal, shortened form of comfortable

Etymology

From Old French 'confortable', from 'conforter' (to comfort), ultimately from Latin 'confortare' (to strengthen).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkʌmfərtəbəl / (AmE), / ˈkʌmfətəb(ə)l / (BrE)
having a significant margin or advantage that is unlikely to be threatened, especially in a competition or contest.
having a large or safe margin or lead

Examples

The candidate held a comfortable majority throughout the night, easily securing the election.
By halftime, the basketball team had built a comfortable lead over their rivals.
With a comfortable margin, she finished the exam long before time ran out.

Synset Examples

the home team had a comfortable lead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commanding - having a position of clear superiority or control
  • Antonym: narrow - having a small margin or lead, barely sufficient
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02573924-s

/ ˈkʌmfərtəbl / (AmE), / ˈkʌmfətəbl / (BrE)
feeling mentally at ease, free from stress or worry, or creating a state of emotional relaxation and security.
mentally at ease; free from stress

Examples

I always feel comfortable talking to her about personal matters.
The manager created a comfortable atmosphere that made employees more productive.
He is not comfortable with public speaking, but he tries his best.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relaxed - free from tension and anxiety
  • Antonym: uneasy - feeling anxious or uncomfortable

Etymology

From Late Latin *confortabilis* (comforting), from Latin *confortare* (to strengthen).

4. ADJ

/ ˈkʌmfərtəbəl / (AmE), / ˈkʌmfətəbl / (BrE)
Having sufficient financial resources to live well and free from financial worry.
financially well off; affluent

Examples

She grew up in a comfortable household, never lacking for anything she needed.
Although not rich, he felt comfortable enough to retire early and travel.
Thanks to years of careful saving, they lived a comfortable life free from financial stress.

Synset Examples

they were comfortable or even wealthy by some standards
easy living
a prosperous family
his family is well-situated financially
well-to-do members of the community

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: wealthy - having a great deal of money, resources, or assets; rich
  • Synonym: well-off - having a good amount of money; financially comfortable
  • Antonym: poor - lacking sufficient money to live at a standard considered comfortable

Etymology

From late Latin *confortabilis* (comforting), via Old French *confortable*.

Source: WordNet

5. ADJ

/ ˈkʌmfərtəbəl / (AmE), / ˈkʌmfətəbl / (BrE)
sufficient to provide comfort, especially in terms of income, conditions, or provision; affording ease and security without luxury
affording sufficient comfort or security

Examples

She earns a comfortable living as a graphic designer.
The couple enjoys a comfortable lifestyle in the suburbs.
With his comfortable salary, he never worried about paying the bills.

Synset Examples

a comfortable salary

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: luxurious - providing great comfort and elegance, much more than necessary
  • Degree Lower: bare - providing only the minimum; lacking comfort

Etymology

From Latin 'confortare' (to strengthen greatly), via Old French 'conforter'.
