war-ridden

1. ADJ

/ wɔrˌrɪdən / (AmE), / wɔːˌrɪd.ən / (BrE)
(of a place or society) characterized by or suffering from ongoing war or conflict.
suffering from ongoing war or conflict

Examples

After years of violence, the war-ridden country struggled to rebuild its infrastructure.
Refugees fled the war-ridden region in search of safety and stability.
The documentary depicts life in a war-ridden city where daily survival is a challenge.

Synset Examples

belligerent (or warring) nations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: war-torn - damaged or devastated by war

Etymology

Formed from 'war' + 'ridden', where 'ridden' comes from the verb 'ride', meaning 'burdened with' or 'plagued by'.
