Kentish

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛn.tɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɛn.tɪʃ / (BrE)
a dialect of Middle English that was originally spoken in the southeast of England, including London and Essex
Middle English dialect of southeast England

Examples

Scholars have studied the Kentish dialect to better understand early English literature.
Several legal documents from the 13th century were written in Kentish.
The Kentish dialect shares some features with other southern varieties of Middle English.

Etymology

From Kent, the name of the county in Southeast England, plus the suffix -ish.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɛn.tɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɛn.tɪʃ / (BrE)
the southern dialect of Old English spoken in the Anglo-Saxon kingdom of Kent
Old English dialect of Kent

Examples

The Kentish dialect preserved older forms not found in other Old English varieties.
Studies of Kentish provide insights into early English regional speech patterns.
Linguists compare Kentish with other Old English dialects to track language change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Old English - the early form of the English language spoken in England before about 1150

Etymology

From Kent (the region) + -ish (English suffix indicating 'of or relating to').
