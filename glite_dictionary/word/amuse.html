to amuse

1. VERB

/ əˈmjuz / (AmE), / əˈmjuːz / (BrE)
to make someone laugh or feel entertained
make someone laugh or entertain

Examples

The clown managed to amuse the children with his silly tricks.
I always try to amuse my friends with funny stories.
Her clever jokes amuse everyone at the dinner table.

Synset Examples

The clown amused the children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to entertain - to provide amusement or enjoyment
  • Antonym: bore - to make someone feel uninterested or weary

Etymology

From Old French *amuser* (to divert, make someone stare in wonder), of uncertain origin.

2. VERB

/ əˈmjuːz / (AmE), / əˈmjuːz / (BrE)
to entertain or occupy someone in an enjoyable or pleasing way
entertain or occupy pleasantly

Examples

The comedian's quick wit amused the audience throughout the evening.
She brought a puzzle to amuse her children during the long car ride.
He found ways to amuse himself while waiting for the train.

Synset Examples

The play amused the ladies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to entertain - to provide amusement or enjoyment

Etymology

From Old French *amuser* (to divert, entertain), from Vulgar Latin *admūsāre* (to stare at with open mouth; to amaze).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02498011-v
Synset POS: v
