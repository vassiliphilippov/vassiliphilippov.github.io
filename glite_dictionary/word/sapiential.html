sapiential

1. ADJ

/ ˌsæpiˈɛnʃl̩ / (AmE), / ˌsapɪˈɛnʃəl / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by wisdom, especially divine or profound wisdom.
relating to wisdom, especially divine

Examples

The philosopher praised the sapiential writings that offered guidance for a good life.
The design of the temple reflects sapiential principles in its symbolism and structure.
Medieval scholars often searched for sapiential truths in religious texts.

Synset Examples

a sapiential government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wise - having or showing experience, knowledge, and good judgment

Etymology

From Latin 'sapientia' (wisdom), from 'sapiens' (wise).
