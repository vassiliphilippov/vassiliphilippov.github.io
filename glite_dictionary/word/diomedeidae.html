Diomedeidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪəˈmiːdiːˌaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌdʌɪəˈmiːdɪˌiːdiː / (BrE)
the family of large seabirds known as albatrosses, native to the Southern Ocean and the North Pacific
the family of albatrosses (seabirds)
medical

Examples

The Diomedeidae are notable for their extensive wingspans, allowing them to glide long distances over the ocean.
Researchers observed several species within the Diomedeidae family during the migration season.
All albatrosses belong to the Diomedeidae, a group distinguished by remarkable flight endurance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: albatross - large seabird with long wings; member of Diomedeidae

Etymology

From modern Latin *Diomedeidae*, based on the genus name *Diomedea*, after the mythological figure Diomedes.
