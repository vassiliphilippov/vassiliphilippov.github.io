imbroglio

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈbroʊl.joʊ / (AmE), / ɪmˈbrɒl.jəʊ / (BrE)
an extremely confused, complicated, or embarrassing situation
an extremely confusing or embarrassing situation

Examples

The meeting turned into an imbroglio when old rivalries resurfaced.
She found herself in an embarrassing imbroglio over the misaddressed love letter.
The company's financial imbroglio took months to resolve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confusion - a state of being bewildered or unclear
  • Synonym: entanglement - a complicated or compromising relationship or situation

Etymology

From Italian 'imbroglio', from 'imbrogliare' (to tangle, confuse).

2. NOUN

/ ɪmˈbroʊlioʊ / (AmE), / ɪmˈbrəʊliəʊ / (BrE)
A complicated and confusing situation, especially one involving a misunderstanding, conflict, or entanglement, often in a political or interpersonal context.
complicated, confusing situation

Examples

The diplomatic talks ended in an unexpected imbroglio that left both countries at odds.
Her financial imbroglio made front-page news after the scandal broke.
The novel depicts a romantic imbroglio full of betrayal and misunderstandings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confusion - lack of understanding; uncertainty
  • Synonym: entanglement - a complicated or compromising relationship or situation
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear; opposite of confusion

Etymology

From Italian 'imbroglio' (entanglement, confusion).
