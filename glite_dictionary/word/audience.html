audience

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑdiəns / (AmE), / ˈɔːdiəns / (BrE)
a group of people gathered to watch or listen to a performance, speech, or event
group attending a performance or event

Examples

The audience cheered loudly at the end of the concert.
During the speech, several members of the audience asked thoughtful questions.
Tickets quickly sold out as people rushed to be part of the audience.

Synset Examples

the audience applauded
someone in the audience began to cough

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spectators - people who watch an event, especially a sports event
  • Antonym: performers - people presenting a performance for an audience

Etymology

From Latin 'audientia' (a hearing), from 'audīre' (to hear).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔdiəns / (AmE), / ˈɔːdiəns / (BrE)
a formal meeting or opportunity to present one's case and be heard, especially before a person in authority
formal meeting to present one's case

Examples

The defendant was granted an audience with the magistrate to explain his situation.
She requested an audience before the committee to state her case.
Despite many attempts, he was denied an audience with the board of directors.

Synset Examples

they condemned him without a hearing
he saw that he had lost his audience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hearing - an opportunity to present a case and be heard, especially in a legal context

Etymology

From Latin *audientia* (a hearing, listening), from *audīre* (to hear).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14508362-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈɑdiəns / (AmE), / ˈɔːdiəns / (BrE)
a formal meeting with an important person, such as a monarch or other high official
formal meeting with an important person

Examples

The ambassador was granted an audience with the Pope to discuss international relations.
She requested a private audience with the President to present her proposal.
During the medieval period, commoners rarely obtained an audience with the king.

Synset Examples

he had a consultation with the judge
he requested an audience with the king

Etymology

From Latin *audientia* (a hearing, listening) via Old French *audience*.

4. NOUN

/ ˈɔdiəns / (AmE), / ˈɔːdiəns / (BrE)
a group of people who are interested in or consume information, entertainment, or products, such as from a performance, broadcast, or advertisement
group interested in or receiving information/entertainment

Examples

The new television series attracted a huge audience on its opening night.
The book was written to appeal to a young adult audience.
Advertisers are always looking for ways to reach their target audience.

Synset Examples

every artist needs an audience
the broadcast reached an audience of millions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spectator - a person who watches an event, especially a sports event
  • Degree Higher: crowd - a large number of people gathered together, typically in a disorganized or unruly way
  • Synonym: viewer - a person watching television or a broadcast

Etymology

From Latin 'audientia' (a hearing, listening), from 'audīre' (to hear).
