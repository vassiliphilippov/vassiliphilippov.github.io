free-lance

1. NOUN

/ ˈfriˌlæns / (AmE), / ˈfriːˌlɑːns / (BrE)
a writer, artist, or worker who sells their services to different employers without a long-term contract
self-employed worker for hire

Examples

After ten years at the magazine, she decided to become a free-lance and choose her own clients.
The company hires free-lance writers for special projects and campaigns.
As a free-lance, he enjoyed the flexibility but sometimes missed a regular paycheck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freelancer - a person who works as a free-lance

Etymology

From free (not bound) + lance (referring to a medieval mercenary); originally applied to mercenary soldiers without a fixed employer.
