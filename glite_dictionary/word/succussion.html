succussion

1. NOUN

/ səkˈʌʃən / (AmE), / səkˈʌʃən / (BrE)
the act of shaking a person to detect the presence of fluid in a body cavity, especially for medical diagnosis
shaking to detect body cavity fluid (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor performed succussion to check for fluid in the patient's chest.
During the examination, succussion revealed evidence of a pleural effusion.
Medical textbooks describe succussion as a technique for detecting abnormal fluid accumulation.

Etymology

From Latin 'succussio' (a shaking), from 'succutere' (to shake up).
