dominating

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɑːməˌneɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɒmɪˌneɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a height or viewpoint) overlooking and commanding the area around it; rising high above its surroundings.
rising high and overlooking surroundings

Examples

The castle was built on a dominating hill that allowed defenders to see for miles.
From the dominating cliffs, you can watch the waves crash far below.
The city's skyline is defined by a dominating skyscraper that towers above all other buildings.

Synset Examples

a commanding view of the ocean
looked up at the castle dominating the countryside
the balcony overlooking the ballroom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commanding - having a position of dominance or control, especially a view
  • Synonym: overlooking - having a view that looks down on an area

Etymology

Present participle of 'dominate,' from Latin *dominari* (to rule).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɑːməˌneɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɒmɪneɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
having the most power, importance, or influence in a particular situation or group.
most powerful or influential

Examples

The company has maintained a dominating position in the smartphone market for years.
Her dominating presence in the debate made her arguments hard to ignore.
That match was the dominating event of the entire tournament.

Synset Examples

the economically ascendant class
D-day is considered the dominating event of the war in Europe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dominant - most important, powerful, or influential; having authority or control

Etymology

From Latin 'dominari' (to rule, to dominate), via the verb 'dominate'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00795186-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈdɑmɪˌneɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɒmɪneɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
asserting power or control over others in an aggressive or overbearing way, often without justification.
asserting unwarranted power or control

Examples

Her dominating presence in meetings left little room for anyone else to speak.
The coach's dominating attitude made the players feel intimidated rather than inspired.
He has a reputation for being dominating and rarely listens to alternative viewpoints.

Synset Examples

an autocratic person
autocratic behavior
a bossy way of ordering others around
a rather aggressive and dominating character
managed the employees in an aloof magisterial way
a swaggering peremptory manner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bossy - fond of giving people orders; domineering
  • Synonym: overbearing - unpleasantly or arrogantly domineering
  • Antonym: submissive - ready to conform to the authority or will of others

Etymology

From Latin 'dominari' (to rule, to dominate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00791150-s
Synset POS: s
