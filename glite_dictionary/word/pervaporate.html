to pervaporate

1. VERB

/ pɝˈvæpəˌreɪt / (AmE), / pəˈvæpəˌreɪt / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to evaporate through a semipermeable membrane, especially as part of a separation process in chemical engineering.
evaporate through a membrane
medical

Examples

During the experiment, the solvent began to pervaporate rapidly, leaving behind a concentrated solution.
To purify the water, scientists allowed it to pervaporate through a specialized membrane.
If the compound fails to pervaporate, the separation process will not be effective.

Etymology

From Latin 'per' (through) + 'evaporate'.

2. VERB

/ ˌpɝːˈvæpəreɪt / (AmE), / ˌpɜːˈvæpəreɪt / (BrE)
to cause (a liquid) to evaporate through a semipermeable membrane, typically as part of a separation process
evaporate through a membrane

Examples

Modern separation techniques pervaporate solvents through advanced membranes to increase efficiency.
Engineers often pervaporate water from mixtures to purify industrial chemicals.
The laboratory team pervaporated the organic compound to isolate pure ethanol.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pervaporation - the process of separating mixtures of liquids by partial vaporization through a membrane

Etymology

From Latin 'per' (through) + 'vapor' (steam) + '-ate' (verb suffix).
