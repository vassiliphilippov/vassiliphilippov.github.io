squadron

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwɑːdrən / (AmE), / ˈskwɒdrən / (BrE)
an air force unit consisting of several aircraft and their crews, larger than a flight and smaller than a group
air force unit between flight and group

Examples

The commander inspected the entire squadron before the training exercise began.
Each squadron is made up of several flights, with their associated aircraft and personnel.
The air force sent a squadron of fighter jets to participate in the military drill.

Etymology

From Italian 'squadrone', augmentative of 'squadra' (a square, a squad).

2. NOUN

/ ˈskwɑːdrən / (AmE), / ˈskwɒdrən / (BrE)
a military unit of cavalry consisting of two or more troops with a headquarters and supporting arms
cavalry military unit

Examples

The cavalry squadron assembled at sunrise for inspection.
Each squadron was comprised of several troops and a headquarters unit.
During the battle, the squadron coordinated its attack with supporting artillery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: company - a military unit, smaller than a battalion, usually consisting of 80–150 soldiers
  • Degree Lower: platoon - a subdivision of a company, typically composed of two or more squads
  • Degree Higher: regiment - a large military unit, typically made up of several squadrons or battalions

Etymology

From Italian 'squadrone', from 'squadra' meaning 'square'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈskwɑːdrən / (AmE), / ˈskwɒdrən / (BrE)
a naval unit consisting of a group of ships detached from the fleet for a particular mission or task
naval group of ships

Examples

The admiral ordered the squadron to patrol the eastern coastline for enemy activity.
A squadron of destroyers was detached to support the amphibious assault.
After completing its mission, the squadron rejoined the main fleet at the harbor.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: fleet - the largest naval formation, comprising several squadrons and ships

Etymology

From Italian *squadrone*, augmentative of *squadra* (square, squad).
