aboard

1. ADV

/ əˈbɔrd / (AmE), / əˈbɔːd / (BrE)
(informal) As a member of a group, organization, or team.
as part of a group or team
informal

Examples

We are excited to have you aboard as our new sales manager.
It took her a month to really feel aboard with the team.
We'd love for you to come aboard and help us with this project.

Synset Examples

Bill's been aboard for three years now

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on board - part of a team or group

2. ADV

/ əˈbɔrd / (AmE), / əˈbɔːd / (BrE)
(in baseball) occupying one of the bases as a runner.
on base as a runner (baseball)
american

Examples

With two players aboard, the batter hit a home run.
He doubled with nobody aboard in the third inning.
The team loaded the bases, putting three men aboard before the next out.

Synset Examples

Their second homer with Bob Allison aboard

Etymology

From Middle English 'abord', from Old French 'à bord', literally 'on board'. Extended figuratively in American English baseball.

3. ADV

/ əˈbɔrd / (AmE), / əˈbɔːd / (BrE)
on or onto a ship, train, plane, or other vehicle.
on a ship, train, or plane

Examples

All passengers must be aboard the train before it departs.
Once aboard the plane, please fasten your seatbelt.
There were only a few crew members aboard at the time of the incident.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ashore - on or to the shore from a ship or boat

Etymology

From Old French 'à bord' meaning 'on board'.
