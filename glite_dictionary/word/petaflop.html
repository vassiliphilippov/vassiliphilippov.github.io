petaflop

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛtəˌflɑp / (AmE), / ˈpiːtəflɒp / (BrE)
a unit of computing speed equal to one quadrillion (10^15) floating-point operations per second
unit of computing speed (10^15 operations/sec)

Examples

The new supercomputer is capable of over one petaflop of performance.
Many scientific simulations require computing power measured in petaflops to be feasible.
Achieving a petaflop was once considered a major milestone in computer science.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: teraflop - unit of computing speed equal to one trillion floating-point operations per second
  • Degree Higher: exaflop - unit of computing speed equal to one quintillion floating-point operations per second

Etymology

From 'peta-' (a metric prefix for one quadrillion) and 'flop' (floating-point operation per second).
