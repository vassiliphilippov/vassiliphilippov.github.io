Zanthoxylum flavum

1. NOUN

/ zænˈθɒksɪləm ˈfleɪvəm / (AmE), / zanˈθɒksɪləm ˈfleɪvəm / (BrE)
A species of West Indian tree known for its smooth, lustrous, and slightly oily wood, often used in fine woodworking.
West Indian tree with lustrous wood

Examples

The dense wood of the Zanthoxylum flavum is prized for furniture making in the Caribbean.
Botanists studied the unique characteristics of Zanthoxylum flavum during their field trip to Jamaica.
The plantation's restoration project focuses on reintroducing native species like Zanthoxylum flavum.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'xanthos' (yellow) and 'xylon' (wood); 'flavum' from Latin meaning 'yellow', referring to the wood's color.
