elated

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈleɪtɪd / (BrE)
extremely happy and excited; filled with high-spirited delight
extremely happy and excited

Examples

She felt elated when she heard that she had won the award.
The team was elated after their impressive victory.
He walked out of the interview elated by the positive feedback.

Synset Examples

a joyful heart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecstatic - overwhelmed with intense joy
  • Antonym: depressed - in a state of unhappiness or low spirits

Etymology

From Latin elatus, past participle of efferre, meaning 'raised or carried away'.

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈleɪtɪd / (BrE)
extremely happy and excited, often because of a particular success or good news.
very happy and excited

Examples

She was elated when she heard that she had passed the exam with top marks.
The team felt elated after winning the championship match last night.
He walked out of the interview room looking elated and confident.

Synset Examples

the elated winner
felt elated and excited

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecstatic - feeling or expressing overwhelming happiness or joyful excitement
  • Antonym: depressed - very unhappy or low in spirits
  • Degree Lower: happy - feeling or showing pleasure or contentment

Etymology

From Latin 'elatus', past participle of 'efferre' (to raise, uplift).
