deprecatory

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛprəˌkeɪtɔri / (AmE), / ˈdɛprɪkətəri / (BrE)
expressing disapproval or criticism, or tending to make something seem unimportant
expressing disapproval or criticism

Examples

His deprecatory comments discouraged the team from pursuing the project further.
She gave a deprecatory smile, making it clear she doubted the compliment was sincere.
Critics made several deprecatory remarks about the new film's script.

Synset Examples

belittling comments
managed a deprecating smile at the compliment
deprecatory remarks about the book
a slighting remark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belittling - making someone or something seem unimportant
  • Synonym: disparaging - expressing the opinion that something is of little worth
  • Antonym: laudatory - expressing praise

Etymology

From Latin deprecatorius (pertaining to averting by prayer), from deprecari (to ward off by prayer).
