alcapton

1. NOUN

/ ælˈkæptɒn / (AmE), / ælˈkæptən / (BrE)
an acid that appears as an intermediate product in the metabolism of tyrosine and phenylalanine
metabolic acid; intermediate in amino acid metabolism
medical

Examples

Elevated levels of alcapton in the urine can indicate a rare metabolic disorder.
The doctor explained that the buildup of alcapton is responsible for the darkening of the patient's urine.
Researchers study alcapton to better understand disorders affecting amino acid metabolism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homogentisic acid - another name for alcapton, or the main acid found in alkaptonuria
