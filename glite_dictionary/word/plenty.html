plenty

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɛn.ti / (AmE), / ˈplɛn.ti / (BrE)
A large or sufficient amount or quantity; more than enough.
large or sufficient amount; abundance

Examples

There was plenty to eat at the party, so everyone left satisfied.
She brought plenty of ideas to the meeting, impressing her colleagues.
Don't worry, we have plenty of time to finish this project.

Synset Examples

a batch of letters
a deal of trouble
a lot of money
he made a mint on the stock market
see the rest of the winners in our huge passel of photos
it must have cost plenty
a slew of journalists
a wad of money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something
  • Antonym: scarcity - the state of being scarce or in short supply

Etymology

From Old French 'plente' (abundance), from Latin 'plentus' (full).

2. NOUN

/ ˈplɛnti / (AmE), / ˈplɛnti / (BrE)
A large or sufficient amount or quantity; more than enough.
a full or sufficient amount

Examples

There was plenty to eat at the party, so no one went hungry.
Even with plenty of time to prepare, she still felt nervous before the presentation.
Farmers in the region enjoyed plenty after a season of good rains.

Synset Examples

there was plenty of food for everyone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something
  • Antonym: scarcity - a shortage or insufficient amount of something

Etymology

From Old French 'plenté', from Latin 'plenitas' (fullness), from 'plenus' (full).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05122868-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADV

/ ˈplɛnti / (AmE), / ˈplɛnti / (BrE)
(informal) Used as an adverb to mean 'enough' or 'as much as necessary'.
enough; as much as necessary (adverb)
informal

Examples

I've eaten plenty, so I really can't manage dessert.
Don't worry, there's plenty to go around for everyone.
She said she's had plenty and doesn't want any more.

Synset Examples

have I eaten enough?
I've had plenty, thanks (‘plenty’ is nonstandard)

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enough - sufficient; as much as needed
