lied

1. NOUN

/ liːd / (AmE), / liːd / (BrE)
A German art song of the 19th century, typically composed for solo voice and piano.
German art song for voice and piano

Examples

The pianist accompanied the soprano in a beautiful lied by Schubert.
His favorite genre to perform is the nineteenth-century German lied.
Many composers wrote lied settings of German poetry during the Romantic era.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lieder - Plural of 'lied', German art songs for voice and piano

Etymology

German, literally ‘song’.
