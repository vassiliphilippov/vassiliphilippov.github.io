exalted

1. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈzɔltɪd / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɔːltɪd / (BrE)
(of ideas, feelings, or qualities) having a high moral or intellectual value; elevated in nature or style.
of high moral or intellectual value; elevated

Examples

The speaker delivered an exalted vision of justice that inspired the audience.
She was drawn to exalted ideals that guided her life choices.
The poet's style is marked by exalted language and profound themes.

Synset Examples

an exalted ideal
argue in terms of high-flown ideals
a noble and lofty concept
a grand purpose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lofty - of imposing height or elevated style or ideals
  • Synonym: noble - having or showing fine personal qualities or high moral principles
  • Antonym: base - lacking moral principles; ignoble

Etymology

From Latin 'exaltare' (to raise high).
