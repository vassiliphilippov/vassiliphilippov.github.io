troika

1. NOUN

/ ˈtroʊɪkə / (AmE), / ˈtrɔɪkə / (BrE)
a traditional Russian carriage or sleigh drawn by three horses harnessed side by side
Russian carriage drawn by three horses

Examples

The royal family rode through the snow in a troika during the winter festival.
Tourists admired the elegance of the old troika displayed at the museum.
The film featured a dramatic chase scene with a troika racing across the frozen plain.

Etymology

From Russian тройка (trójka, 'group of three'), from три (tri, 'three').

2. NOUN

/ ˈtroʊɪkə / (AmE), / ˈtrɔɪkə / (BrE)
A group of three people working together, especially in an administrative or leadership role; specifically, a governing trio in modern Russia.
governing group of three (esp. in Russia)

Examples

The country was ruled by a troika that shared all major decisions.
After the revolution, a new troika emerged to manage the transitional government.
The company’s future will be decided by the executive troika appointed last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triumvirate - a group of three people holding power

Etymology

From Russian тройка (trojka, 'group of three'), from трой- ('tri-', 'three').
