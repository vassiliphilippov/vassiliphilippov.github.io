to refute

1. VERB

/ rɪˈfjuːt / (AmE), / rɪˈfjuːt / (BrE)
to prove a statement or theory to be wrong or false by argument or evidence
prove false by argument or evidence

Examples

The scientist was able to refute the hypothesis with new experimental data.
She worked hard to refute the false accusations made against her.
During the debate, he skillfully refuted his opponent’s main arguments.

Synset Examples

The speaker refuted his opponent's arguments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rebut - to claim or prove that evidence or an accusation is false
  • Synonym: to disprove - to show that something is not true
  • Antonym: to prove - to demonstrate the truth or existence of something

Etymology

From Latin refutare (to drive back, repel, refute).

2. VERB

/ rɪˈfjut / (AmE), / rɪˈfjuːt / (BrE)
to prove that a statement, argument, or theory is false or incorrect
prove something false or incorrect

Examples

The scientist was able to refute the claim with new experimental evidence.
She confidently refuted every allegation made against her during the interview.
His theory was refuted by the results of the latest research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rebut - to argue against or contradict
  • Synonym: to disprove - to show something is not true or valid
  • Antonym: to confirm - to establish the truth or correctness of something

Etymology

From Latin refutare (to repel, rebut, refute).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00669665-v
Synset POS: v
