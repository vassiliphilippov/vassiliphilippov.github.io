Condylura

1. NOUN

/ kɑnˈdɪljʊrə / (AmE), / kɒnˈdɪljʊərə / (BrE)
A genus of North American moles known as star-nosed moles, recognizable by the star-shaped structure on their snouts.
genus of star-nosed moles

Examples

The genus Condylura is best known for its distinctive star-nosed mole species.
Researchers studied the underground burrows created by Condylura in wetlands.
Unlike other moles, Condylura uses its star-shaped nose to detect prey with remarkable speed.

Etymology

From Greek 'kondylos' (knob, joint) and 'oura' (tail).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɒn.dɪˈljʊr.ə / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.dɪˈljʊə.rə / (BrE)
A genus of moles known as star-nosed moles, native to North America, distinguished by a unique star-shaped structure on the nose.
genus of star-nosed moles

Examples

The Condylura genus is easily identified by the characteristic star-shaped appendages on their noses.
Scientists studied the Condylura in their natural wetland habitat.
Condylura are remarkable for their ability to detect prey using their sensitive snouts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: star-nosed mole - A species of mole with a star-shaped nose, found in North America

Etymology

From Greek 'kondylos' (knuckle) + 'oura' (tail), referring to anatomical features.
