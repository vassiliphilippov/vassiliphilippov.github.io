Manihot dulcis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæn.iˈhoʊt ˈdʌl.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌmanɪˈhɒt ˈdʌl.sɪs / (BrE)
A South American plant whose roots are used as a vegetable and whose foliage is used as livestock feed.
South American root vegetable plant

Examples

The roots of Manihot dulcis are harvested for use in traditional South American dishes.
Farmers grow Manihot dulcis as a valuable source of livestock fodder and staple food.
Research focuses on improving the yield and nutritional value of Manihot dulcis for both people and animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cassava - Tropical root plant also known as yuca or manioc

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Manihot' and species epithet 'dulcis' meaning 'sweet'.
