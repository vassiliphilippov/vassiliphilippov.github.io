Rakaposhi

1. PROPN

/ ˌrɑːkəˈpoʊʃi / (AmE), / ˌrakəˈpəʊʃi / (BrE)
a mountain peak in the Karakoram Range in northern Pakistan, standing at 7,788 meters (25,551 feet) high
mountain in the Karakoram Range, Pakistan

Examples

Many climbers dream of ascending Rakaposhi because of its breathtaking vertical rise above the Hunza Valley.
The view of Rakaposhi from the Karakoram Highway is considered one of the most spectacular in the world.
Snowfall on Rakaposhi creates dangerous avalanche conditions during the winter season.

Etymology

From Burushaski, meaning 'shining wall.'
