2. NOUN

/ ˈtʌŋɡə / (AmE), / ˈtʌŋɡə / (BrE)
A genus of fleas in the family Siphonaptera, which includes species such as the chigoe flea (Tunga penetrans) known for burrowing into the skin of mammals.
genus of fleas (includes chigoe flea)
medical

Examples

The chigoe flea, classified as Tunga penetrans, is known for burrowing into human skin.
Researchers have discovered several species within the genus Tunga in South America.
Infestations by members of Tunga can lead to severe discomfort and health complications in their hosts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flea - small wingless parasitic insect in the order Siphonaptera

Etymology

From Latin 'tunga', used in scientific taxonomy.
