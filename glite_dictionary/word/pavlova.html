pavlova

1. NOUN

/ pævˈloʊvə / (AmE), / pævˈləʊvə / (BrE)
A dessert made of a crisp meringue base topped with whipped cream and fresh fruit.
meringue-based dessert with cream and fruit

Examples

She made a delicious pavlova topped with fresh strawberries and kiwi.
The bakery sells individual pavlovas that are perfect for parties.
A classic summer dessert in Australia, pavlova is both light and sweet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meringue - dessert made of whipped egg whites and sugar

Etymology

Named after the Russian ballerina Anna Pavlova; first made in Australia or New Zealand in the 1920s.
