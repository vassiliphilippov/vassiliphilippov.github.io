supplementary

1. ADJ

/ ˌsʌpləˈmɛn.tə.ri / (AmE), / ˌsʌplɪˈmɛn.təri / (BrE)
added to complete or enhance something, or to make up for a deficiency
added to complete or make up a deficiency

Examples

The teacher provided supplementary materials to help the students understand the topic.
We needed a supplementary budget to cover unexpected expenses this year.
Two supplementary chapters were added in the second edition of the book.

Synset Examples

produced supplementary volumes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: additional - added, extra, or supplementary
  • Synonym: complementary - serving to complete or enhance; forming a complement

Etymology

From Latin 'supplementarius', from 'supplementum' (something added).

2. ADJ

/ ˌsʌpləˈmɛnˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˌsʌplɪˈmɛntri / (BrE)
serving to add to or support something; functioning as an addition to the main part
serving as an addition or support

Examples

The company provides supplementary training sessions for new employees.
This booklet contains supplementary information not included in the main text.
Students receive supplementary materials to help them prepare for the exam.

Synset Examples

the main library and its auxiliary branches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auxiliary - providing supplementary or additional help and support
  • Antonym: main - chief; primary; most important

Etymology

From Latin supplementarius, from supplementum (that which fills up or completes).

WordNet ID: oewn-01860057-s
Synset POS: s
