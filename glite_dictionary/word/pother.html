pother

1. VERB

/ ˈpʌðər / (AmE), / ˈpɒðə / (BrE)
to make a fuss or become agitated, especially about something trivial
make a fuss; be agitated
archaic

Examples

There's no need to pother over such a small mistake.
She pothered all afternoon about the seating arrangements for the dinner.
Stop pothering and just get started on the work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuss - show unnecessary or excessive concern about something
  • Synonym: to agitate - to make someone troubled or nervous

Etymology

Probably from older noun 'pother', origin uncertain; possibly related to 'pother' meaning commotion or fuss.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01823849-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑðər / (AmE), / ˈpɒðə / (BrE)
a state of fuss, commotion, or excited agitation about something, typically more noise or concern than the situation warrants
a fuss or commotion
archaic

Examples

The small mistake caused quite a pother in the office.
Why make such a pother over a simple misunderstanding?
She left quietly, not wishing to create a pother among her friends.

Synset Examples

he was in a dither
there was a terrible flap about the theft

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commotion - a state of confused and noisy disturbance
  • Antonym: calm - the state of being relaxed and free from agitation

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; possibly related to dialectal English "pother" (disturbance, fuss).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14427176-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈpʌðər / (AmE), / ˈpɒðə / (BrE)
to make someone upset, agitated, or troubled
to make someone upset or troubled
archaic

Examples

Please do not pother yourself over such a small disagreement.
The children pothered their babysitter with endless questions.
He tends to pother everyone around him with unnecessary worries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bother - to annoy or cause minor trouble to someone
  • Antonym: calm - to make someone peaceful or less upset

Etymology

Originated in the 17th century; likely of unknown or imitative origin related to the noun 'pother' meaning fuss or commotion.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
