bilabial

1. ADJ

/ baɪˈleɪbiəl / (AmE), / baɪˈleɪbɪəl / (BrE)
(of a speech sound) produced by using both lips together.
speech sound made with both lips

Examples

The English sound bilabial /p/ appears in words like 'pat' and 'spin'.
In the word 'map', both 'm' and 'p' are bilabial consonants.
Phonetics students often practice bilabial sounds by closing their lips together.

Synset Examples

bilabial fricatives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: labial - a speech sound produced with the lips

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'labium' (lip), meaning 'two lips'.

WordNet ID: oewn-03059771-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ baɪˈleɪbiəl / (AmE), / baɪˈleɪbiəl / (BrE)
a consonant sound produced with both lips coming together, such as /p/, /b/, or /w/
a consonant made with both lips

Examples

The English sound /p/ is an example of a bilabial in the language.
Phonetics students learned how to pronounce each bilabial correctly.
/b/, /m/, and /p/ are all classified as bilabial consonants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: labial - a consonant articulated with the lips

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'labium' (lip).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07132041-n
Synset POS: n
