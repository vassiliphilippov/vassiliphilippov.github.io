Asplenium adiantum-nigrum

1. NOUN

/ æsˈplɛniəm ˌædiˌæntəm ˈnɪɡrəm / (AmE), / æsˈpliːniəm ˌædiˈæntəm ˈnɪɡrəm / (BrE)
A species of spleenwort found in Europe, Africa, and Asia, with pinnate fronds and known for its astringent properties.
a species of spleenwort fern

Examples

The Asplenium adiantum-nigrum is commonly found growing on rocky slopes across southern Europe.
Botanists studied the medicinal properties of Asplenium adiantum-nigrum in traditional remedies.
A patch of Asplenium adiantum-nigrum fringed the forest path with delicate, dark green fronds.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Asplenium' is the genus name, and 'adiantum-nigrum' means 'black adiantum' (a reference to the fern's resemblance and coloration).
