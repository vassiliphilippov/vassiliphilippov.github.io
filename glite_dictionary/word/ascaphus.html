Ascaphus

1. NOUN

/ əˈskæfəs / (AmE), / əˈskæfəs / (BrE)
A genus of frogs comprising the tailed frogs, native to North America, and the type genus of the family Ascaphidae.
genus of tailed frogs

Examples

The tailed frogs of the genus Ascaphus are among the most primitive living frogs.
Researchers found a new habitat for Ascaphus in the streams of British Columbia.
The unique reproductive adaptations of Ascaphus have fascinated biologists for decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ascaphidae - family of tailed frogs, including Ascaphus

Etymology

From Greek askaphos (without shovel/spade), referring to tail-like structure.

2. NOUN

/ əˈskæfəs / (AmE), / əˈskæfəs / (BrE)
A genus of primitive frogs, commonly known as tailed frogs, native to North America and noted for their unique tail-like organ in males.
genus of primitive tailed frogs

Examples

The Ascaphus genus contains two living species found in the Pacific Northwest.
Researchers have studied the reproductive adaptations of Ascaphus in fast-flowing streams.
Unlike most frogs, males of Ascaphus possess a tail-like extension.

Etymology

From Greek askaphos, possibly meaning 'uncovered'.
