Lepidochelys olivacea

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛpɪˈdɑkəlɪs ˌɑlɪˈveɪʃə / (AmE), / ˌlɛpɪˈdɒkɪlɪs ˌɒlɪˈveɪsiː / (BrE)
A species of olive-colored sea turtle found in the tropical Pacific, Indian, and southern Atlantic oceans; also known as the olive ridley sea turtle.
olive-colored sea turtle species

Examples

The Lepidochelys olivacea is known for its synchronized nesting behavior called arribada.
Researchers observed hundreds of Lepidochelys olivacea turtles coming ashore to lay eggs.
Conservation efforts are helping protect Lepidochelys olivacea nesting sites from poachers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: olive ridley - common name for Lepidochelys olivacea
  • Degree Higher: loggerhead - a different species of sea turtle (Caretta caretta)

Etymology

From Latin: 'lepidochelys' meaning 'scale turtle', and 'olivacea' meaning 'olive-colored'.
