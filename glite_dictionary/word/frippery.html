frippery

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɪpəri / (AmE), / ˈfrɪpəri / (BrE)
showy or unnecessary ornamentation; something considered frivolous or of little value or significance.
showy, trivial ornamentation

Examples

The shelves were crowded with frippery and knickknacks collected from her travels.
He dismissed the decorations as needless frippery rather than important details.
Shoppers were drawn to the display of colorful frippery in the market stalls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bauble - a small, showy trinket or decoration
  • Synonym: trifle - something of little importance or value
  • Antonym: necessity - something essential or indispensable

Etymology

From French *friperie* (old clothes, secondhand finery), from *friper* (to fray).
