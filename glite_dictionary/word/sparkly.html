sparkly

1. ADJ

/ ˈspɑrkli / (AmE), / ˈspɑːkli / (BrE)
shining with many small points of light; glittering or twinkling.
shining with many glittering points of light

Examples

She wore a sparkly dress to the party that caught everyone's attention.
The sparkly snowflakes looked magical under the streetlights.
My daughter loves anything sparkly, from shoes to notebooks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glittery - shining with many tiny points of reflected light
  • Synonym: glittering - shining with a bright, shimmering, reflected light
  • Antonym: dull - lacking brightness, shine, or light

2. ADJ

/ ˈspɑrkli / (AmE), / ˈspɑːkli / (BrE)
full of lively energy, excitement, or high spirits; effervescent or scintillating in personality or style
lively, full of excitement or high spirits

Examples

Her sparkly personality lit up every room she entered.
The team gave a sparkly performance that thrilled the audience.
He's always in such a sparkly mood during the holidays.

Synset Examples

his fertile effervescent mind
scintillating personality
a row of sparkly cheerleaders

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scintillating - brilliantly lively, stimulating, or witty
  • Synonym: effervescent - vivacious and enthusiastic; marked by high spirits

Etymology

From 'sparkle' + '-y', meaning having the quality of sparkles or liveliness.
