octosyllabic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɑk.toʊ.sɪˈlæb.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌɒk.tə.sɪˈlæb.ɪk / (BrE)
(of a word, line, or verse) consisting of or characterized by eight syllables.
having eight syllables

Examples

The poet favored the octosyllabic line in his verse composition.
In French medieval poetry, octosyllabic structure was particularly common.
She challenged her students to write an octosyllabic poem for homework.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: decasyllabic - consisting of ten syllables
  • Degree Lower: hexasyllabic - consisting of six syllables

Etymology

From French 'octosyllabique', from Latin 'octo-' (eight) + Greek 'syllabē' (syllable).
