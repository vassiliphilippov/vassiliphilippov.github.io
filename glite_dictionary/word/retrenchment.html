retrenchment

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈtrɛntʃmənt / (AmE), / rɪˈtrɛntʃmənt / (BrE)
an additional interior fortification built within a larger defensive work, used to prolong resistance if the main defenses are breached
additional interior fortification (military)

Examples

The defenders withdrew behind the retrenchment when the outer walls began to fail.
The fortress's retrenchment provided a final stronghold during the prolonged siege.
Engineers constructed a retrenchment to strengthen the citadel against further attacks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entrenchment - a fortification made by digging a trench and throwing up earth

Etymology

From French *retranchement*, from *retrancher* (to cut off, retrench), ultimately from Latin *truncare* (to cut off).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈtrɛntʃmənt / (AmE), / rɪˈtrɛntʃmənt / (BrE)
the reduction of spending, especially by a business or government, in order to improve financial stability
reducing spending for financial stability

Examples

The company announced a period of retrenchment to address declining profits.
Government retrenchment led to cutbacks in public services across the country.
After years of growth, they implemented strict retrenchment measures to stabilize the budget.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reduction - the act of making something smaller or less in amount
  • Antonym: expansion - the act of increasing in size, amount, or importance

Etymology

From French 'retranchement', from retrancher (to cut away).
