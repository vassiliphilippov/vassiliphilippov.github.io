timorousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪmərəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈtɪmərəsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being timid, fearful, or lacking confidence, especially about the unknown or making decisions
state of being timid or fearful

Examples

His timorousness prevented him from taking the bold steps needed to advance his career.
The timorousness of the explorer vanished as he grew accustomed to the unfamiliar jungle.
She overcame her timorousness and finally spoke up during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timidity - the quality of being easily frightened or lacking confidence
  • Antonym: boldness - the quality of being confident and courageous

Etymology

Formed from 'timorous' + '-ness'; 'timorous' from Latin 'timorosus' (full of fear).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɪmərəsnɪs / (AmE), / ˈtɪmərəsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being timid or lacking confidence, especially in facing new or unfamiliar situations
the state of being timid or fearful

Examples

Her timorousness kept her from speaking up at the meeting, despite having good ideas.
The child's timorousness faded as he grew more comfortable in the new school environment.
He attributed his timorousness to a lack of experience with travel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timidity - the quality of being easily frightened or lack of courage
  • Antonym: boldness - the quality of taking risks and acting confidently

Etymology

From Latin 'timor' meaning 'fear'.
