to swelter

1. VERB

/ ˈswɛltɚ / (AmE), / ˈswɛltə / (BrE)
to be extremely hot and uncomfortable, or to suffer from intense heat.
suffer intensely from heat

Examples

We began to swelter as the afternoon sun beat down on the city pavement.
Hundreds of fans sweltered in the packed stadium while waiting for the concert to start.
Tourists sweltering in the midday heat sought shelter under the shady trees.

Synset Examples

we were sweltering at the beach

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broil - to suffer from or be subjected to intense heat
  • Antonym: freeze - to be affected by or suffer from intense cold

Etymology

From Middle English 'swelten' (to suffer from heat), likely related to Old English 'sweltan' (to die, perish).

2. VERB

/ ˈswɛltər / (AmE), / ˈswɛltə / (BrE)
to suffer or feel uncomfortably hot; to be oppressively warm or humid
be uncomfortably hot

Examples

The workers sweltered under the midday sun as they repaired the road.
We sweltered in the crowded train, wishing for a breeze.
Everyone sweltering at the summer festival sought out the shade of the trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broil - to be extremely hot; to suffer from great heat

Etymology

From Middle English swelten ('to die, perish'), later influenced in meaning by 'sweltering heat'.
