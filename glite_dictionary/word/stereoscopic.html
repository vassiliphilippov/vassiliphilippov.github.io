stereoscopic

1. ADJ

/ ˌstɛri.əˈskɑːpɪk / (AmE), / ˌstɪəri.əˈskɒpɪk / (BrE)
relating to or using a stereoscope, or creating the impression of depth and three-dimensionality, especially in images or vision.
relating to three-dimensional depth perception

Examples

The film was projected in stereoscopic 3D, giving the audience a sense of depth.
Early photographers used stereoscopic cameras to produce images that looked three-dimensional.
With stereoscopic vision, viewers can perceive the relative position of objects in space.

Synset Examples

stereoscopic lenses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: 3D - three-dimensional, giving the illusion of depth
  • Antonym: monoscopic - relating to a single viewpoint or lacking three-dimensional effect

Etymology

From Greek 'stereos' (solid) + 'skopein' (to look, to see).

2. ADJ

/ ˌstɛriəˈskɑpɪk / (AmE), / ˌstɪəriəˈskɒpɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving stereoscopy; giving or capable of giving a three-dimensional effect or image by means of stereoscopy.
relating to three-dimensional imaging

Examples

The 3D movie used stereoscopic technology to create the illusion of depth.
Using stereoscopic images, the scientists studied how animals perceive space.
Early viewers were amazed by the stereoscopic effect in photographs.

Synset Examples

stereoscopic vision

Related Terms

  • Synonym: three-dimensional - having or appearing to have height, width, and depth

Etymology

From Greek 'stereos' (solid) + 'skopein' (to look, to see), via French 'stéréoscopique'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03131844-a
Synset POS: a
