encephalograph

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈsɛfələˌɡræf / (AmE), / ɛnˈsɛfələˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
A medical instrument used to record the electrical activity of the brain, producing an encephalogram.
instrument for recording brain activity
medical

Examples

The hospital recently purchased a new encephalograph for the neurology department.
Technicians use an encephalograph to monitor brainwave patterns during sleep studies.
An encephalograph can help diagnose certain neurological disorders by capturing brain activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electroencephalograph - instrument for recording electrical activity of the brain

Etymology

From Greek 'enkephalos' (brain) + '-graph' (instrument for recording).
