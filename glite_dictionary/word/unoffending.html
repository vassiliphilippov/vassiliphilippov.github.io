unoffending

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈfɛndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈfɛndɪŋ / (BrE)
not causing anger, annoyance, or offense; inoffensive or harmless.
not causing offense or annoyance

Examples

Despite his unusual attire, he was entirely unoffending in both manner and speech.
The stray dog was unoffending, quietly watching people from a distance.
She preferred an unoffending color scheme for the living room to avoid strong reactions from her guests.

Synset Examples

inoffensive behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inoffensive - not likely to offend or irritate anyone; harmless

Etymology

un- (not) + offend + -ing; modeled after 'offending' as an adjective.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈfɛndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈfɛndɪŋ / (BrE)
Not causing offense or not at fault; innocent or blameless.
not causing offense; blameless

Examples

The police apologized after realizing the unoffending man was not involved in the incident.
She defended her unoffending remarks by explaining she meant no harm.
It was unfair to punish the unoffending students for someone else’s mistake.

Synset Examples

an unoffending motorist should not have been stopped

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innocent - not guilty of a crime or offense
  • Synonym: blameless - free from blame; innocent
  • Antonym: offending - causing offense or responsible for a fault

Etymology

Formed by prefixing 'un-' (not) to 'offending' (present participle of offend).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01635764-a
Synset POS: a
