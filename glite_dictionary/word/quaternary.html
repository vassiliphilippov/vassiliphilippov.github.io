quaternary

1. ADJ

/ kwəˈtɝː.nɛr.i / (AmE), / kwəˈtɜː.nər.i / (BrE)
coming fourth in a series; next after the third and just before the fifth in position, time, degree, or magnitude
fourth in a series

Examples

The quaternary phase in the process followed the completion of the third stage.
Geologists study fossils from the quaternary period to learn about recent Earth history.
He organized his notes into primary, secondary, tertiary, and finally quaternary categories.

Synset Examples

the quaternary period of geologic time extends from the end of the tertiary period to the present

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tertiary - third in a series
  • Degree Higher: quinary - fifth in a series

Etymology

From Latin quaternarius (consisting of four, four each), from quaterni (four each), from quattuor (four).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkwɑːtərˌneri / (AmE), / kwəˈtɜːnəri / (BrE)
consisting of or arranged in groups or sets of four
consisting of four parts or sets

Examples

The chemical reaction produces a quaternary compound with four distinct elements.
Certain plants have quaternary leaves that grow in whorls of four.
The data were organized into quaternary groups for statistical analysis.

Synset Examples

quaternate leaves
a quaternary compound

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quaternate - arranged in whorls of four

Etymology

From Latin quaternarius (consisting of four each), from quaterni (four each)

3. NOUN

/ kwəˈtɜrnɛri / (AmE), / kwɔːˈtɜːnəri / (BrE)
the number four; cardinal number representing the sum of three and one
the number four (4)

Examples

The DNA code can be represented using a quaternary system based on four nucleotides.
Each quaternary in the sequence was marked for analysis.
Mathematicians sometimes refer to the number four as a quaternary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: four - the number equal to three plus one

Etymology

From Latin 'quaternarius' (consisting of four), from 'quaterni' (four each).
