Endamoebidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.dəˈmiː.bɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌɛn.dəˈmiː.bɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of parasitic amoebas that inhabit and infect the digestive tracts of animals, including humans.
family of parasitic amoebas
medical

Examples

Members of the Endamoebidae family are known to cause intestinal infections in humans.
Researchers studied the life cycle of Endamoebidae in laboratory animals.
The spread of diseases caused by Endamoebidae can be reduced with proper sanitation.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Endamoeba' (a genus name) and '-idae' (a zoological family suffix).
