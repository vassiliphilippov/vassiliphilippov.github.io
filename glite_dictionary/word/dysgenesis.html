dysgenesis

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / dɪsˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (BrE)
A condition in which abnormal development occurs in an organ or tissue, or, in genetics, infertility resulting from hybridization between different strains or species.
abnormal development; hybrid infertility (biology)
medical

Examples

Hybrid sterility is a type of dysgenesis observed in certain crosses of fruit flies.
Researchers studied the dysgenesis seen when two different plant species were bred together.
A detailed analysis of gonadal dysgenesis revealed the underlying genetic incompatibilities.

Etymology

From Greek dys- (bad, abnormal) + genesis (origin, formation).
