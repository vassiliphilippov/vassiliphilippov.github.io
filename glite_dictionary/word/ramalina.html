Ramalina

1. NOUN

/ ræməˈlaɪnə / (AmE), / ræməˈliːnə / (BrE)
A genus of shrubby lichens in the family Usneaceae, characterized by a flattened, branching thallus.
genus of shrubby lichens

Examples

The forest was dotted with Ramalina hanging from the trees.
Researchers studied the chemical compounds found in several Ramalina species.
Ramalina grows best in clean, unpolluted air, making it a good indicator of environmental quality.

Etymology

From Latin, based on Greek *ramalus* (branch) due to the branched appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ˌræməˈlaɪnə / (AmE), / ˌraməˈlʌɪnə / (BrE)
A genus of shrubby lichens in the family Usneaceae, characterized by a flattened thallus.
a genus of shrubby lichens

Examples

The species of Ramalina often grow on tree branches in coastal areas.
Scientists studied the unique structure of Ramalina lichens to understand their adaptation to arid environments.
Many Ramalina lichens produce secondary metabolites used in traditional medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Usnea - a genus of lichens in the family Usneaceae closely related to Ramalina

Etymology

From Latin, used in botanical Latin to denote the genus name.
