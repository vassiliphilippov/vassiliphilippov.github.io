touchdown

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌtʃˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈtʌtʃ.daʊn / (BrE)
the moment when an aircraft or spacecraft lands and its wheels or landing gear make contact with the ground or runway
moment an aircraft lands

Examples

The pilot executed a perfect touchdown despite the heavy rain.
After a long flight, the passengers applauded upon touchdown.
The flight crew prepared the cabin for touchdown at the destination airport.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: landing - an instance of coming or bringing something to land, especially an aircraft

Etymology

From the verb 'touch' + 'down', indicating the act of coming into contact with the ground.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʌtʃˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈtʌtʃ.daʊn / (BrE)
(in American football) an act of scoring points by carrying or catching the ball in the opponent's end zone.
score in American football
american

Examples

The team celebrated after scoring a touchdown in the final seconds of the game.
He ran fifty yards to secure a touchdown for his team.
That was his third touchdown of the season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goal - a score in various sports achieved by sending the ball or puck into the opponent's net or goal area
  • Degree Lower: field goal - a score in American football by kicking the ball through the uprights

Etymology

From 'touch' + 'down', originally used in rugby to refer to grounding the ball behind the goal line.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00189615-n
Synset POS: n
