Cimicifuga racemosa

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪmɪˈsɪfjʊɡə ræˈsiːməsə / (AmE), / ˌsɪmɪˈsɪfjʊɡə raˈsiːməsə / (BrE)
A North American perennial plant, also known as black cohosh, native to regions from Maine and Ontario to Georgia, often used in herbal medicine.
species of North American plant (black cohosh)

Examples

Many herbal supplements contain extracts from Cimicifuga racemosa to help with menopausal symptoms.
The roots of Cimicifuga racemosa have been used in traditional Native American remedies.
Researchers are studying Cimicifuga racemosa for its potential effects on women's health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black cohosh - common name for Cimicifuga racemosa

Etymology

From Latin: 'Cimicifuga' meaning 'bug repeller' and 'racemosa' referring to its clustered flowers.
