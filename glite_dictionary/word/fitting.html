fitting

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
an item of furniture or equipment, especially as part of the fixed furnishings in a room, ship, or hotel; usually used in the plural.
furnishings or equipment (often plural)

Examples

The luxurious hotel is known for its elegant fittings and decor.
The ship's wooden fittings have been restored to their original state.
All the kitchen fittings will be replaced during the renovation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fixture - an item of equipment that is fixed in position in a building

/ ˈfɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
appropriate or suitable for a particular person, purpose, or occasion.
appropriate; suitable

Examples

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
a small part or accessory, often standardized, used to connect or assemble components in a larger system, such as a pipe, electrical, or mechanical system
standardized accessory for systems

Examples

The plumber replaced the old pipe fitting to stop the leak.
All electrical fittings must be installed according to safety regulations.
She ordered several brass fittings to complete the restoration of the antique lamp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fixture - an object fixed in position, often permanently, in a building or system

Etymology

From fit + -ing, indicating something that fits or is used for fitting.
