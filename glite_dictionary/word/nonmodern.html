nonmodern

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑnˈmɑdɚn / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈmɒdn̩ / (BrE)
not modern; relating to or characteristic of an earlier period or style
not modern; from an earlier time

Examples

The building's nonmodern architecture makes it stand out among the new skyscrapers.
Some people prefer nonmodern furniture because of its craftsmanship and style.
Her essays focused on the values found in nonmodern societies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' meaning 'not', plus 'modern'.
