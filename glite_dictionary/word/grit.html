grit

1. NOUN

/ ɡrɪt / (AmE), / ɡrɪt / (BrE)
a hard, coarse-grained sandstone consisting mainly of quartz or feldspar, used in construction and geology
coarse-grained sandstone

Examples

The building was constructed with local grit quarried from nearby hills.
Over time, the sharp edges of the grit became smoother due to weathering.
Geologists examined the layer of grit to learn more about the region's ancient environment.

Etymology

From Old English 'greot' (sand, earth, gravel), related to Old High German 'grioz'.

2. VERB

/ ɡrɪt / (AmE), / ɡrɪt / (BrE)
to clench or press (the teeth) tightly together, especially because of anger, determination, or pain
clench teeth together tightly

Examples

She tried to grit her teeth and stay calm during the stressful meeting.
He gritted his teeth in pain as the nurse cleaned his wound.
You have to grit your teeth and get through the difficult times.

3. VERB

/ ɡrɪt / (AmE), / ɡrɪt / (BrE)
to cover a surface, especially a road, with grit (small stones or sand) to provide traction or prevent slipping, particularly in icy conditions
cover with grit or small stones

Examples

During the winter, the city workers grit the roads to prevent accidents.
The maintenance crew gritted the icy walkway early this morning.
Drivers noticed that the streets had already been gritted before the snow started falling.

Synset Examples

grit roads

Etymology

From Old English 'grytt' (bran, chaff; coarse meal or grain).

courage and determination
