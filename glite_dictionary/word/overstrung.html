overstrung

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvərˈstrʌŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈstrʌŋ / (BrE)
(of a person) extremely nervous or tense; highly strung.
very tense or nervous

Examples

After days without sleep, she became increasingly overstrung and irritable.
His overstrung nerves made it impossible for him to concentrate on his work.
The children grew overstrung as the excitement built before the performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: high-strung - easily stressed or tense; highly nervous
  • Antonym: relaxed - not tense or anxious; free from stress

Etymology

Past participle of 'overstring', meaning to stretch or strain excessively.

2. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvərˈstrʌŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈstrʌŋ / (BrE)
strung too tightly or with excessive tension, especially referring to a stringed instrument or object under strain
strung or stretched too tightly

Examples

The violin sounded odd because it was overstrung and under too much tension.
An overstrung racquet may break more easily during intense matches.
Be careful not to leave your bow overstrung, as it can cause damage to the wood.

Synset Examples

an overstrung archery bow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overstretched - stretched too much or beyond limits
  • Degree Lower: taut - pulled tight; not slack

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (too much) + past participle of 'string'.
