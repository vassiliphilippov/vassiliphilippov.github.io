conspiracy

1. NOUN

/ kənˈspɪrəsi / (AmE), / kənˈspɪrəsi / (BrE)
A secret plan by a group of people to do something unlawful or harmful.
secret plan for unlawful act

Examples

The police uncovered a conspiracy to smuggle weapons into the country.
She was charged with conspiracy to commit fraud after the investigation.
Rumors of a political conspiracy spread quickly through the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plot - a secret plan made to achieve something, often illegal or harmful
  • Synonym: collusion - secret or illegal cooperation, especially to cheat or deceive others
  • Synonym: scheme - a large-scale systematic plan or arrangement for a particular purpose, sometimes underhanded

Etymology

From Latin conspirare (to plot, to agree together), via Old French conspiration.

2. NOUN

/ kənˈspɪɹəsi / (AmE), / kənˈspɪɹəsi / (BrE)
A secret plan by a group to do something unlawful or harmful, especially for political purposes.
secret plan for illegal or harmful act

Examples

The police uncovered a conspiracy to steal valuable artwork from the museum.
There were several arrests connected to a conspiracy against the government.
Rumors spread quickly about a supposed conspiracy between the two companies to fix prices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plot - a secret plan to accomplish something, especially an unlawful or harmful act
  • Synonym: scheme - a secret or underhand plan
  • Antonym: collaboration - the act of working together, especially with the enemy in wartime

Etymology

From Latin 'conspiratio' (agreement, union) from 'conspirare' (to plot, literally 'to breathe together').

3. NOUN

/ kənˈspɪrəsi / (AmE), / kənˈspɪrəsi / (BrE)
A secret plan by a group of people to do something harmful or illegal.
a secret plot by a group

Examples

The police uncovered a conspiracy to rig the election.
Several officials were arrested for their role in the conspiracy.
Rumors of a conspiracy spread quickly through the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plot - a secret plan to accomplish something, especially something illegal or harmful
  • Synonym: scheme - a secret plan, often for something illegal or dishonest
  • Synonym: collusion - secret cooperation for an illegal or dishonest purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'conspirare' (to plot, to breathe together).
