Jew-baiter

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuˌbeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːˌbeɪtə / (BrE)
A person who harbors hatred for Jews and seeks to harass, provoke, or persecute them.
person who persecutes Jews (offensive)
offensive

Examples

He was well known as a Jew-baiter in his community, often making hateful speeches.
Many condemned the politician's rhetoric as that of a Jew-baiter.
The newspaper article exposed several notorious Jew-baiters from the early 20th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anti-Semite - a person who is hostile toward or prejudiced against Jews
  • Antonym: tolerant person - someone who is accepting of people from all backgrounds and faiths

Etymology

Formed from 'Jew' and 'baiter', where 'to bait' means to deliberately taunt or provoke.
