Equisetum palustre

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkwɪˈsiːtəm pəˈlʌstri / (AmE), / ˌiːkwɪˈsiːtəm pəˈlɑːstri / (BrE)
A species of horsetail found widely in wet or boggy areas of the Northern Hemisphere, known as the scouring-rush horsetail.
a species of horsetail plant

Examples

Equisetum palustre commonly grows in the marshy meadows surrounding the lake.
Researchers identified Equisetum palustre as a predominant plant in the bog's ecosystem.
Some farmers avoid grazing livestock where Equisetum palustre is abundant, as it can be toxic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horsetail - a plant of the genus Equisetum with jointed stems

Etymology

From Latin: 'Equisetum' meaning 'horsetail', 'palustre' meaning 'of the marsh'.
