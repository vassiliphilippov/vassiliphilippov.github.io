disinterest

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsˈɪntrəst / (AmE), / ˌdɪsˈɪntrɪst / (BrE)
the state of not being influenced by personal involvement or interest; impartiality or lack of concern
lack of interest or involvement

Examples

His disinterest in the outcome made him a fair judge.
The teacher tried to overcome the students' disinterest in the lesson.
Her disinterest allowed her to make an impartial decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indifference - lack of interest or concern
  • Antonym: interest - the feeling of wanting to know or be involved

Etymology

From dis- (expressing reversal) + interest.

2. NOUN

/ dɪsˈɪntrəst / (AmE), / dɪsˈɪntrɪst / (BrE)
the state of not being interested or lacking concern in something
lack of interest

Examples

The manager's disinterest in the team's concerns led to low morale.
A sense of disinterest could be felt during the lengthy presentation.
His disinterest in politics is well known among his friends.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interest - the feeling of wanting to know or learn about something
  • Synonym: indifference - lack of interest, concern, or sympathy

Etymology

From prefix dis- (expressing reversal) + interest, modeled after similar French forms.
