lactation

1. NOUN

/ lækˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / lækˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of producing and secreting milk from the mammary glands, typically following childbirth
milk production by mammary glands
medical

Examples

During lactation, the mother's body supplies essential nutrients to the infant through breast milk.
Certain hormones regulate the onset and maintenance of lactation in mammals.
Proper hydration and nutrition are important factors for successful lactation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breastfeeding - the act of feeding a baby with milk from the breast

Etymology

From Latin lactatio (suckling), from lactare (to suckle), from lac (milk).

2. NOUN

/ lækˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / lækˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the production and secretion of milk by the mammary glands for feeding an infant by suckling at the breast
production and secretion of milk

Examples

Lactation is essential for providing nutrition and antibodies to newborns.
Many mammals experience a period of lactation after giving birth.
Problems with lactation can sometimes require medical advice or support.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breastfeeding - feeding a baby with milk from the breast

Etymology

From Latin 'lactatio', from 'lac, lactis' meaning 'milk'.

3. NOUN

/ lækˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / lækˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the period following birth during which a female mammal produces and secretes milk to nourish her young
period after birth when milk is produced

Examples

During lactation, mothers provide essential nutrients to their infants through breast milk.
Cows require a balanced diet to remain healthy throughout lactation.
The duration of lactation varies among different species of mammals.

Synset Examples

lactation normally continues until weaning

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: weaning - the process of gradually introducing an infant or young mammal to what will be its adult diet and withdrawing the supply of its mother's milk

Etymology

From Latin 'lactatio', from 'lac', 'lactis' meaning 'milk'.
