ransom

1. NOUN

/ ˈræn.səm / (AmE), / ˈræn.səm / (BrE)
the act of freeing someone from captivity or punishment, typically by paying a demanded price
the act of freeing from captivity or punishment

Examples

The family paid a large sum for his ransom after his kidnapping.
His ransom was negotiated by a team of international diplomats.
They awaited news of the prisoner's ransom with growing anxiety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: release - the act of setting someone free

Etymology

From Old French 'rançon', from Latin 'redemptio' (a buying back, redemption).

WordNet ID: oewn-00097671-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈræn.səm / (AmE), / ˈræn.səm / (BrE)
a sum of money demanded or paid for the release of a kidnapped or captured person
money demanded for a person's release

Examples

The kidnappers demanded a ransom of $1 million for the safe return of the hostages.
After weeks of negotiations, the family finally paid the ransom to secure his release.
The government refused to pay the ransom, insisting they would not negotiate with criminals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackmail - the act of demanding money by threatening to reveal compromising information
  • Antonym: release - the action of freeing someone from captivity

Etymology

From Old French 'rançon', from Latin 'redemptio' (a buying back).

3. NOUN

/ ˈræn.səm / (AmE), / ˈræn.səm / (BrE)
a sum of money demanded or paid for the release of a person who has been kidnapped or captured
payment for release of a captive

Examples

The kidnappers demanded a ransom of one million dollars for the safe return of the hostages.
He refused to pay the ransom, believing it would only encourage more crimes.
Police intercepted the ransom money before it could reach the criminals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extortion - the practice of obtaining something through force or threats

Etymology

From Old French ranson, from Latin redemptio (a buying back, redemption).

4. VERB

/ ˈrænsəm / (AmE), / ˈræn.səm / (BrE)
to obtain the release of someone or something by paying a demanded price or fulfilling certain conditions, typically under threat
obtain release by paying a demanded price

Examples

The organization managed to ransom the hostages after lengthy negotiations.
Kidnappers threatened harm unless the company agreed to ransom the employees.
He was forced to ransom his priceless painting from the thieves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to redeem - to buy back, recover, or pay off

Etymology

From Old French 'raçon', from Latin 'redemptio' (a buying back, redemption).
