Gwydion

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡwɪdiən / (AmE), / ˈɡwɪdiən / (BrE)
In Welsh mythology, a magician, sky god, and giver of arts and civilization.
Celtic/Welsh mythological magician and god

Examples

In ancient tales, Gwydion is known for his clever magical schemes.
The legend of Gwydion explores the origins of many artistic traditions in Wales.
Many poets have drawn inspiration from the mythic figure Gwydion.

Etymology

From Welsh mythology; cognate with earlier Celtic traditions.
