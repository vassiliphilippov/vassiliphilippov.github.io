woodcut

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈwʊdkʌt / (BrE)
a print or impression made from an engraved block of wood, or the block itself, used in art and illustration
print made from an engraved wood block

Examples

The museum displayed a rare woodcut dating back to the 16th century.
She learned how to make a woodcut in her printmaking class.
This old book is illustrated with beautiful woodcuts of plants and animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engraving - a print made from a design incised on a hard surface

Etymology

From the combination of 'wood' and 'cut', indicating the process of cutting a design into wood.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈwʊd.kʌt / (BrE)
a print made by pressing paper onto an inked surface of a carved block of wood.
print made from carved wood block

Examples

The museum displayed an original woodcut from the nineteenth century.
She admired the detail and craftsmanship of the Japanese woodcut on the wall.
He collects rare woodcuts depicting rural life in medieval Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engraving - a print made from an engraved surface, typically metal
  • Synonym: etching - a print made from a metal plate etched with acid

Etymology

Compound of 'wood' and 'cut', referring to the technique of cutting an image into wood for printing.
