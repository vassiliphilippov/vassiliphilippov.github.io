Artaxerxes I

1. PROPN

/ ˌɑrtəˈzɜrksiːz ðə ˈfɜrst / (AmE), / ˌɑːtəˈzɜːksiːz ði ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
(circa ?–424 BC) King of Persia who ruled from 465 BC to 424 BC, noted for sanctioning the practice of Judaism in Jerusalem.
King of Persia, reigned 465–424 BC

Examples

The decree of Artaxerxes I allowed the Jewish community to rebuild Jerusalem.
Historians credit Artaxerxes I with stabilising the Persian Empire after the assassination of Xerxes I.
Several ancient texts mention Artaxerxes I for his tolerance towards various religions.

Etymology

From Old Persian Artaxšaçā, Latinized as Artaxerxes.
