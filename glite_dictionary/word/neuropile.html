neuropile

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʊr.əˌpaɪl / (AmE), / ˈnjʊə.rəˌpaɪl / (BrE)
A dense network of interwoven nerve fibers, dendrites, and glial cell branches in the grey matter of the central nervous system, in which nerve cell bodies are embedded.
network of nerve fibers in CNS grey matter
medical

Examples

The cerebral cortex contains a rich neuropile where synaptic connections are formed.
Damage to the neuropile can disrupt normal brain function and information processing.
Researchers examined the structure of the neuropile in various regions of the spinal cord.

Etymology

From French 'neuropile', from Greek 'neuron' (nerve) + 'pilos' (felt, relating to its texture).
