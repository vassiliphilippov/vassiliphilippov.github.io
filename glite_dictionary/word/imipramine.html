imipramine

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɪprəˌmiːn / (AmE), / ɪˈmɪprəmiːn / (BrE)
a tricyclic antidepressant drug used to treat clinical depression and sometimes other conditions such as anxiety or bedwetting in children
a tricyclic antidepressant drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed imipramine to help manage her long-term depression.
Some patients may experience drowsiness as a side effect of imipramine.
It is important not to suddenly stop taking imipramine without consulting a physician.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antidepressant - a medicine used to alleviate depression

Etymology

From German Imipramin, coined from chemical components.
