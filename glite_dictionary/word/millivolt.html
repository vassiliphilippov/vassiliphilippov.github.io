millivolt

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪl.iˌvoʊlt / (AmE), / ˈmɪl.ɪ.vəʊlt / (BrE)
a unit of electric potential equal to one thousandth of a volt
unit of electric potential

Examples

The sensor detected a change of just a few millivolts in the circuit.
A reading of 500 millivolts indicated a weak signal.
Batteries often produce voltages in the range of several millivolts under certain conditions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: volt - unit of electric potential

Etymology

From milli- (thousandth) and volt, after Italian physicist Alessandro Volta.
