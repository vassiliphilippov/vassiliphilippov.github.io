arbiter

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrbɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɑːbɪtə / (BrE)
a person who is appointed or accepted to settle a dispute or make a judgment
person appointed to settle disputes

Examples

As an arbiter of taste, her opinion was highly respected by the art community.
The companies agreed to accept the decision of an independent arbiter in their contract dispute.
He was called in as an arbiter when negotiations between the two sides stalled.

Synset Examples

the critic was considered to be an arbiter of modern literature
the arbitrator's authority derived from the consent of the disputants
an umpire was appointed to settle the tax case

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arbitrator - a person officially appointed to settle a dispute
  • Synonym: umpire - a person who settles disputes, especially in sports
  • Antonym: participant - a person involved but not judging or deciding

Etymology

From Latin 'arbiter' (judge, umpire, eyewitness).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrbɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɑːbɪtə / (BrE)
a person who has the authority to judge or decide a dispute, or whose opinion or decision is considered final
person with power to decide disputes

Examples

The CEO acted as the arbiter in the dispute between the two departments.
In ancient times, kings often served as the final arbiter of justice.
Her reputation as an arbiter of taste made her advice highly sought after.

Synset Examples

she was the final arbiter on all matters of fashion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: judge - person who decides legal cases or disputes

Etymology

From Latin arbiter (judge, eyewitness)
