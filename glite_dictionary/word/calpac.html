calpac

1. NOUN

/ kælˈpæk / (AmE), / kælˈpæk / (BrE)
a high-crowned black cap, usually made of felt or sheepskin, traditionally worn by men in Turkey, Iran, and the Caucasus.
traditional high-crowned cap from Turkey and Iran

Examples

He donned his calpac before heading out into the chilly morning air.
The museum displayed a collection of traditional calpacs from different regions of the Caucasus.
Wearing a black calpac, the elder recited stories of his ancestors.

Etymology

From Turkish kalpak (high-crowned cap).
