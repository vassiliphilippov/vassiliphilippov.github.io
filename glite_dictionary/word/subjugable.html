subjugable

1. ADJ

/ sʌbˈdʒuːɡəbəl / (AmE), / sʌbˈdʒuːɡəbəl / (BrE)
capable of being brought under control or domination; able to be subjugated.
able to be subjugated

Examples

The island was considered subjugable due to its lack of military defenses.
Many cultures in history have fallen because their populations were seen as subjugable.
The emperor targeted only the most subjugable regions during his expansion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subjugated - brought under control; dominated
  • Antonym: unconquerable - impossible to subjugate or defeat

Etymology

From Latin 'subjugare' (to subjugate) plus the adjective-forming suffix '-able'.
