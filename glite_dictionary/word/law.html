law

1. NOUN

/ lɔ / (AmE), / lɔː / (BrE)
(informal) the police considered as a force enforcing the law.
the police; law enforcement
informal

Examples

After robbing the store, he was on the run from the law.
You can't hide forever; sooner or later the law will catch up to you.
He grew up admiring the law and wanted to become a police officer.

Synset Examples

the law came looking for him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: police - the civil force of a state responsible for maintaining public order and enforcing the law

2. NOUN

/ lɑ / (AmE), / lɔː / (BrE)
the profession concerned with the study and practice of legal principles, requiring postgraduate education and responsibility for the judicial system
the legal profession

Examples

After graduating from Harvard, she decided to practice law in New York.
He studied law because he wanted to become a judge one day.
A career in law often requires years of intensive education and training.

Synset Examples

he studied law at Yale

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legal profession - the collective body of people who practice law

Etymology

From Old English *lagu* (law, ordinance), influenced by Old Norse *lag* (something laid down, law).

/ lɔ / (AmE), / lɔː / (BrE)
the branch of philosophy concerned with legal systems and the principles that guide judicial decision-making; also called jurisprudence
legal philosophy; jurisprudence

Examples

He is interested in the law as a philosophical discipline, not just the legal system itself.
The study of law often includes exploring the principles that influence judicial reasoning.
She specializes in the law that guides courts to make their decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jurisprudence - the theory or philosophy of law

Etymology

From Old English lagu, from Old Norse *lagu* (something laid down or fixed).

4. NOUN

/ lɑː / (AmE), / lɔː / (BrE)
a rule or set of rules regarded as inherent in human nature and essential or binding on human society
fundamental rule or body of rules governing society

Examples

Many philosophers have debated whether natural law exists independently of human societies.
The concept of moral law has guided the ethics of many cultures for centuries.
Some believe there are universal laws that all human societies must respect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: statute - a written law enacted by a legislative body
  • Degree Lower: custom - a traditional and widely accepted way of behaving or doing something, which may have the force of law
  • Degree Lower: regulation - a rule made and maintained by an authority

Etymology

From Old English lagu (something laid down or fixed), influenced by Old Norse *lagu* (law), from Proto-Germanic *lagą*.

5. NOUN

/ lɔ / (AmE), / lɔː / (BrE)
a scientific principle or generalization based on repeated observations, describing natural phenomena or events that occur consistently under certain conditions
scientific principle describing regularity in nature

Examples

Newton’s law of motion explains how objects move under the influence of forces.
Charles’s law states that the volume of a gas increases with temperature at constant pressure.

6. NOUN

/ lɔ / (AmE), / lɔː / (BrE)
a rule or set of rules, usually established by a government or authority, that regulates or prohibits certain actions and is enforceable by penalties
legal rule or statute

Examples

The government passed a new law to protect endangered species.
Breaking a law can result in a fine or imprisonment.
He studied the law concerning intellectual property before filing his patent.

Synset Examples

there is a law against kidnapping

Related Terms

  • Synonym: statute - a written law passed by a legislative body
  • Synonym: regulation - a rule or directive made and maintained by an authority
  • Synonym: bylaw - a rule made by a company or society to control its members

Etymology

From Old English *lagu* (law, ordinance) and Old Norse *lagu* (something laid down or fixed).

7. NOUN

/ lɔ / (AmE), / lɔː / (BrE)
the system of rules recognized and enforced by a community, government, or authority to regulate the actions of its members
system of rules enforced by authority

Examples

Everyone is expected to respect the law if society is to function smoothly.
The law prohibits smoking in all public buildings.
She decided to study law at university.

Synset Examples

civilization presupposes respect for the law
the great problem for jurisprudence to allow freedom while enforcing order

Related Terms

  • Synonym: statute - a written law passed by a legislative body
  • Degree Lower: custom - a traditional and widely accepted way of behaving or doing something, which may form a basis for law
  • Degree Higher: constitution - a system of fundamental principles or established precedents according to which a state is governed

Etymology

From Old English lagu (law, ordinance); related to Old Norse *lagu* (law), from Proto-Germanic, meaning 'something laid down'.
