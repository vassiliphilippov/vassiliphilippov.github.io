foxhole

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑksˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈfɒksˌhəʊl / (BrE)
a small hole dug in the ground, used by a soldier as a shelter from enemy fire
military dugout shelter for one person

Examples

The soldiers huddled in a foxhole throughout the night to avoid enemy fire.
He quickly dug a foxhole when the battle erupted nearby.
After hours in his foxhole, he was finally able to move to a safer location.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dugout - a shelter dug in the ground, especially for soldiers

Etymology

From 'fox' (the animal known for digging burrows) + 'hole', referencing its function as a protective shelter.
