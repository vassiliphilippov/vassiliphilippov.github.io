Aglaonema modestum

1. NOUN

/ ˌæɡliəˈniːmə ˈmɑdəstəm / (AmE), / ˌaɡləʊˈniːmə ˈmɒdɛstəm / (BrE)
a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia, known for its erect or partially climbing habit and large green or variegated leaves; often grown as a houseplant
a tropical houseplant with large leaves

Examples

The Aglaonema modestum thrives in low-light conditions, making it ideal for offices and apartments.
Botanists admire Aglaonema modestum for its attractive, variegated foliage and air-purifying qualities.
She decorated her living room with a beautiful Aglaonema modestum placed by the window.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chinese evergreen - another common name for Aglaonema genus, indoor tropical plant

Etymology

From the genus name Aglaonema, derived from Greek 'aglaos' (bright) and 'nēma' (thread), combined with the Latin 'modestum' (modest).
