stipe

1. NOUN

/ staɪp / (AmE), / staɪp / (BrE)
a stalk or stem-like structure supporting the cap of a mushroom, the frond of a fern, or the pistil of a flower
supporting stalk of a mushroom or plant structure

Examples

The stipe of the mushroom was long and slender, supporting the broad cap above the forest floor.
In most ferns, the stipe connects the leafy blade to the plant's rhizome.
Botanists examined the stipe of the flower to determine its structural adaptations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stem - main structural axis of a plant
  • Synonym: petiole - the stalk attaching a leaf blade to a stem

Etymology

From Latin stipes (stake, log, post).
