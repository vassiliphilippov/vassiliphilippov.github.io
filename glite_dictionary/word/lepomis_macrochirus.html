Lepomis macrochirus

1. NOUN

/ ləˈpoʊmɪs ˌmækroʊˈkaɪrəs / (AmE), / lɛˈpəʊmɪs ˌmakrəʊˈkʌɪrəs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the bluegill, a common freshwater sunfish native to eastern and central United States, valued as food and for sport fishing
scientific name of the bluegill sunfish

Examples

Anglers prize Lepomis macrochirus for its spirited fight and delicious flavor.
The lake is well stocked with Lepomis macrochirus and other popular game fish.
Scientists studied the growth patterns of Lepomis macrochirus in different habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluegill - common name for Lepomis macrochirus, a freshwater sunfish

Etymology

From Greek *lepis* (scale) + *pōma* (cover) and Greek *makros* (large) + *cheir* (hand), referring to features of the fish.
