riffle

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈrɪfəl / (BrE)
A small wave or ripple on the surface of water or other liquid, often caused by a slight disturbance such as wind or a gentle current.
a small wave or ripple on liquid

Examples

The riffle of the stream caught the sunlight and sparkled throughout the afternoon.
A gentle riffle disturbed the surface of the pond as the breeze picked up.
Raindrops created a riffle across the otherwise still water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ripple - a small wave or series of waves on the surface of water
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07359263-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈrɪfəl / (BrE)
A method of shuffling playing cards by splitting the deck into two halves and interleaving their corners together.
card-shuffling method

Examples

He demonstrated a perfect riffle with the deck before dealing the cards.
Learning how to do a riffle takes a steady hand and some practice.
The sound of the riffle echoed across the poker table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shuffle - To mix a set of cards to randomize their order

Etymology

Probably related to Dutch *riffel* (groove) or German *riffeln* (to groove, scratch), originally referring to a grooved surface.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

to flip through pages quickly

5. VERB

/ ˈrɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈrɪfəl / (BrE)
To shuffle (playing cards) by dividing the deck into two parts and interleaving them together, usually using the thumbs.
shuffle cards by interleaving them

Examples

He expertly riffled the deck before dealing the cards.
She asked her friend to riffle the cards for a fair game.
After several rounds, Mike could riffle the cards almost without looking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shuffle - To mix up the order of playing cards

Etymology

Perhaps from 'ruffle', influenced by movement and sound of interleaving cards.

6. VERB

/ ˈrɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈrɪfəl / (BrE)
(of paper or other thin material) to move with a light, rapid fluttering or twitching motion.
move in a rapid, fluttering motion

Examples

She watched as the wind riffled the pages of her notebook.
His fingers riffled rapidly through the stack of cards.
A gentle breeze riffling the curtains caught her attention.

Synset Examples

the paper flicked

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flutter - to move with quick, light movements, especially repeatedly up and down or from side to side
  • Synonym: flick - to move or make something move with a sudden quick movement

Etymology

Perhaps from Old Norse *rifa* (‘to tear, scratch’); also related to the noun 'riffle' meaning a rapid or disturbance.
