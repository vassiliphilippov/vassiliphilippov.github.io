atheneum

1. NOUN

/ ˌæθəˈniːəm / (AmE), / ˌæθəˈniːəm / (BrE)
A building or institution where literary, scientific, or artistic materials are available for reading, study, or discussion; often used for libraries, reading rooms, or learned societies.
a place for reading or study, often a library

Examples

The city opened a new atheneum to encourage learning and community events.
She spent many afternoons reading in the peaceful atheneum near her home.
The historic atheneum houses rare manuscripts and hosts lectures on literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: library - a building where books and other reading materials are kept for public use
  • Synonym: reading room - a room where people can read or study quietly

Etymology

From Latin Athenaeum, originally a school in Rome named after the Greek goddess Athena.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæθəˈniːəm / (AmE), / ˌæθɪˈniːəm / (BrE)
A literary or scientific association or institution established for the promotion of learning.
literary or scientific association

Examples

The city's atheneum hosts lectures by visiting scholars throughout the year.
She became a member of the literary atheneum in her town to pursue her interest in poetry.
The atheneum was founded in the 19th century to encourage scientific debate and discovery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: academy - a place of study or training in a special field
  • Synonym: institute - an organization for advanced education or research

Etymology

From Latin Athēnaeum (a school or meeting place for scholars), from Greek Athēnaîon (temple of Athena).
