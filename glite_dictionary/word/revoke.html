revoke

1. VERB

/ rɪˈvoʊk / (AmE), / rɪˈvəʊk / (BrE)
(in card games) to fail to follow suit when able and required to do so.
fail to follow suit in cards

Examples

If a player revokes during the game, there may be a penalty.
He accidentally revoked in the second round, causing confusion at the table.
You should call attention if you see someone revoking in a tournament.

Etymology

From Latin revocare (to call back).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈvoʊk / (AmE), / rɪˈvəʊk / (BrE)
(in card games) the violation of the rules caused by a player failing to follow suit when able to do so.
card game infraction: failing to follow suit

Examples

If you make a revoke during the game, you could be penalized by losing points.
His careless revoke cost their team the chance to win the round.
Debate broke out at the table after a controversial revoke late in the match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to renege - to fail to follow suit in a card game

Etymology

From Latin revocare (to call back), applied in card games since the 18th century.

3. VERB

/ rɪˈvoʊk / (AmE), / rɪˈvəʊk / (BrE)
to officially cancel the validity or effect of something, such as a law, agreement, or privilege
officially cancel; withdraw

Examples

The company decided to revoke his access to sensitive files after the incident.
Her license was revoked due to repeated violations of the law.
If you violate the agreement, the organization may revoke your membership.

Synset Examples

He revoked the ban on smoking
lift an embargo
vacate a death sentence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cancel - to decide that something will not take place or is no longer valid
  • Synonym: to annul - to declare invalid, especially legally
  • Antonym: to confirm - to establish the truth or validity of something

Etymology

From Latin revocare (to call back), from re- (back) + vocare (to call).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00801460-v
Synset POS: v
