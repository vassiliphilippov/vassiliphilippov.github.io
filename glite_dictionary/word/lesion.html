lesion

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːʒən / (AmE), / ˈliːʒən / (BrE)
an area of damaged tissue in the body, especially one caused by injury or disease
area of damaged body tissue
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the skin lesion carefully to determine if it was cancerous.
After the accident, she developed a painful lesion on her arm.
Brain scans revealed a small lesion that might explain his symptoms.

Etymology

From Latin 'laesio' (injury, hurt), from 'laedere' (to hurt).

2. NOUN

/ ˈliːʒən / (AmE), / ˈliːʒ(ə)n / (BrE)
an area of abnormal tissue change or damage in a part of the body, often due to injury or disease
abnormal area of damaged body tissue
medical

Examples

The MRI revealed a small lesion on his spinal cord.
Doctors monitored the skin lesion for signs of infection.
After the injury, he developed a lesion on his arm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ulcer - an open sore on an external or internal surface of the body
  • Synonym: tumor - an abnormal mass of tissue

Etymology

From Latin 'laesio' (injury), from 'laedere' (to harm).
