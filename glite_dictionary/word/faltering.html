faltering

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɔltərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːltərɪŋ / (BrE)
Lacking strength or steadiness, especially in speech or movement.
unsteady or hesitant

Examples

Her faltering voice betrayed her anxiety about the interview.
The company's faltering progress worried investors.
He took a few faltering steps before regaining his balance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hesitant - uncertain or reluctant in speech or action
  • Antonym: confident - showing certainty or assurance

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'falter', likely from Middle English 'falteren'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɔltɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːltərɪŋ / (BrE)
A pause or moment of uncertainty, especially in speech or action.
pause or uncertainty, esp. in speech

Examples

There was a noticeable faltering in her voice as she shared the news.
His faltering during the presentation revealed his nervousness.
A brief faltering interrupted the otherwise smooth speech.

Synset Examples

there was a hesitation in his speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hesitation - the act of pausing or delaying due to uncertainty or indecision
  • Antonym: confidence - the quality of being certain or assured

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'falter' (late Middle English, likely of imitative origin) + '-ing'.
