Trilliaceae

1. NOUN

/ trɪˌliːˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / trɪˌlɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A small family of herbaceous plants, typically having flowers with three petals and three sepals; sometimes considered a subfamily of Liliaceae.
family of herbaceous plants with three-petaled flowers

Examples

Many woodland wildflowers in North America belong to the Trilliaceae family.
Some botanists include Trilliaceae as a subfamily within Liliaceae.
The distinctive three-petaled flowers make members of the Trilliaceae easy to identify.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Liliaceae - a large family of flowering plants including lilies and related groups

Etymology

From the genus name Trillium, the type genus of the family, combined with the standard botanical suffix -aceae (meaning family).
