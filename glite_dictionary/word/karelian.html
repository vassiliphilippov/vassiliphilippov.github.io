Karelian

1. NOUN

/ kəˈriːliən / (AmE), / kəˈriːliən / (BrE)
a Finnic language spoken chiefly in the region of Karelia, close to the border between Finland and Russia
Finnic language of Karelia

Examples

She spent several years studying the structure of the Karelian language.
Many folk songs are still sung in Karelian by the local communities.
The dictionary includes words borrowed from Karelian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Finnish - the Finnic language spoken in Finland

Etymology

From Karelia, the name of the region.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈrɛliən / (AmE), / kəˈrɛːlɪən / (BrE)
A member of a Finnic ethnic group native to Karelia, a region in northwestern Russia and parts of Finland.
a member of the Finnic people from Karelia

Examples

Her grandmother was a proud Karelian who spoke both Finnish and Russian fluently.
Many Karelians emigrated to Finland during the 20th century due to political changes in the region.
The traditions of the Karelian people are reflected in their unique folk music and crafts.

Etymology

From Karelia, the region inhabited by this group, plus English suffix -an.
