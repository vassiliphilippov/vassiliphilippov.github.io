unguis

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌŋɡwɪs / (AmE), / ˈʌŋɡwɪs / (BrE)
a hard, keratinous structure such as a nail, claw, or hoof found in humans and animals
a hard body structure (nail, claw, hoof)
medical

Examples

The veterinarian explained that the cat’s unguis had grown too long and needed to be trimmed.
Damage to the unguis can lead to infections if not properly cared for.
Certain reptiles have a sharply curved unguis adapted for climbing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nail - the hard, flat covering on the upper surface of the tip of the fingers and toes
  • Synonym: claw - a curved, pointed horny structure at the end of a toe or finger in some animals

Etymology

From Latin 'unguis' meaning nail or claw.
