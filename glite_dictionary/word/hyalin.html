hyalin

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪəlɪn / (AmE), / ˈhʌɪəlɪn / (BrE)
a glassy, translucent substance occurring in hyaline cartilage and certain tissues, especially under pathological conditions
glassy substance in cartilage and tissues
medical

Examples

The pathologist identified deposits of hyalin in the damaged tissue.
In hyaline cartilage, hyalin provides structural support and flexibility.
Skin lesions can sometimes contain a buildup of hyalin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyaline - having a glassy, translucent appearance

Etymology

From Greek hualos (glass) + -in (substance).
