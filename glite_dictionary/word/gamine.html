gamine

1. NOUN

/ ɡæˈmiːn, ɡəˈmiːn / (AmE), / ɡaˈmiːn, ɡəˈmiːn / (BrE)
A homeless girl who roams the streets, often with a mischievous or spirited appearance.
homeless girl on the streets

Examples

The film follows a young gamine as she struggles to survive in the city.
With her tattered clothes and quick wit, she was the very image of a gamine.
The authorities tried to help the gamine they found sleeping in the alleyway.

Etymology

French, feminine of 'gamin' (street urchin, mischievous child).

2. NOUN

/ gæˈmiːn / (AmE), / ɡaˈmiːn / (BrE)
a girl or young woman with an impish, mischievous charm, often with a boyish or waiflike quality
a girl with mischievous, boyish charm

Examples

The actress brought a gamine charm to the role that captivated audiences worldwide.
With her short haircut and mischievous smile, she had a distinct gamine look.
Audrey Hepburn is often remembered for her iconic gamine style and playful elegance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waif - a homeless, neglected, or abandoned person, especially a child

Etymology

From French 'gamine' (girl, waif).
