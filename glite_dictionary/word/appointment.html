appointment

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɔɪntmənt / (AmE), / əˈpɔɪntmənt / (BrE)
(usually appointments) the furnishings and equipment of a room, building, or vehicle, especially those of a ship or hotel.
furnishings and equipment (esp. of ship or hotel)

Examples

The hotel offered luxurious appointments in each room, including fine linens and custom furniture.
The yacht's elegant appointments impressed all the guests on board.
The conference hall was praised for its modern appointments and comfortable seating.

2. NOUN

/ əˈpɔɪntmənt / (AmE), / əˈpɔɪntmənt / (BrE)
(Law) The act of distributing property or assets by exercising a legal power granted, often through a will, trust, or other instrument.
legal distribution of property or assets
legal

Examples

She designated her nephew as a beneficiary of the estate by appointment.
The trustee made the appointment according to the terms laid out in the trust.
Through the power of appointment, he allocated assets to various family members.

Synset Examples

she allocated part of the trust to her church by appointment

3. NOUN

/ əˈpɔɪntmənt / (AmE), / əˈpɔɪntmənt / (BrE)
a person who has been officially chosen for a job or position
a person officially chosen for a post

Examples

The new appointment will assume responsibility for the organization’s finances next month.
After several interviews, she became the latest appointment to the board of directors.
Each appointment must undergo a thorough background check before starting their role.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appointee - a person who is appointed to a position

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *appointamentum*, from 'appoint'.

4. NOUN

/ əˈpɔɪntmənt / (AmE), / əˈpɔɪntmənt / (BrE)
a job or position to which a person is officially assigned or appointed, especially in a formal or professional context
job or position to which one is appointed

Examples

She received an appointment as a professor at the university.
Several candidates applied for the appointment in the finance department.
His appointment as ambassador was announced last week.

Synset Examples

he applied for an appointment in the treasury

Related Terms

  • Synonym: position - a job or role in an organization
  • Synonym: post - an official job or position
  • Antonym: dismissal - removal from a job or position

Etymology

From Middle French 'appointement' ('agreement, appointment'), from 'appointer' ('to appoint').
