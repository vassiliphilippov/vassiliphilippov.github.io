to vanquish

1. VERB

/ ˈvæŋ.kwɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈvæŋ.kwɪʃ / (BrE)
to defeat someone completely in a competition, conflict, or argument
defeat completely

Examples

The home team managed to vanquish their longtime rivals in a thrilling match.
The hero vowed to vanquish evil and restore peace to the kingdom.
She finally vanquished her fear of public speaking after years of effort.

Synset Examples

Agassi beat Becker in the tennis championship
We beat the competition
Harvard defeated Yale in the last football game

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defeat - to win a victory over someone in a battle or contest
  • Synonym: to conquer - to take control of a place or people by force
  • Antonym: surrender - to stop resisting and submit to an opponent

Etymology

From Old French 'vencus', from Latin 'vincere' (to conquer).
