infinitival

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˌfɪnɪˈtaɪvəl / (AmE), / ɪnˌfɪnɪˈtaɪvəl / (BrE)
relating to or using the infinitive form of a verb.
relating to the infinitive verb form

Examples

The sentence contains an infinitival clause acting as the subject.
In English, infinitival phrases often begin with 'to'.
The distinction between finite and infinitival verbs is important in grammar studies.

Synset Examples

infinitival clause

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infinitive - basic form of a verb, typically preceded by 'to' in English

Etymology

Formed from 'infinitive' with the suffix '-al'.
