Knightia

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtiə / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtiə / (BrE)
a small genus of trees or shrubs native to New Zealand and New Caledonia, belonging to the family Proteaceae.
genus of trees and shrubs from New Zealand and New Caledonia

Examples

Knightia is commonly found in the forests of New Zealand's North Island.
The leaves of Knightia species are often used in botanical research.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the genus Knightia.

Etymology

Named in honor of Thomas Knight, an English botanist.

genus of trees and shrubs (Proteaceae)

Examples

The genus Knightia includes several species unique to New Zealand's forests.
Botanists studied the leaves of Knightia to understand its adaptation to local climates.
A specimen of Knightia was collected during the expedition to New Caledonia.

Etymology

Named after Thomas Andrew Knight, English botanist (1759–1838).
