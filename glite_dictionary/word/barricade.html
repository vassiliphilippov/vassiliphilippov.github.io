barricade

1. VERB

/ ˈbærɪˌkeɪd / (AmE), / ˈbærɪkeɪd / (BrE)
to block or prevent access to a place by setting up a barrier
block or prevent access with a barrier

Examples

The protesters barricaded the entrance to prevent anyone from coming in.
After the hurricane warning, residents barricaded their windows with wooden boards.
The road was barricaded by the police following the accident.

Synset Examples

The street where the President lives is always barricaded

Related Terms

  • Synonym: block - to obstruct a passage or entrance
  • Antonym: to unblock - to remove an obstacle or barrier

Etymology

From French *barricader*, from *barrique* ('barrel').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01129641-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈbærɪˌkeɪd / (AmE), / ˈbærɪkeɪd / (BrE)
a barrier put up by police or authorities to block passage, especially as part of traffic control or security measures
barrier set up by police to block passage

Examples

The police set up a barricade at the end of the street to control traffic.
Protesters built a makeshift barricade to slow down the advancing vehicles.
We were stopped by a barricade and asked to show our identification.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roadblock - a barrier put up across a road by police, often to check traffic or stop suspects

Etymology

From French 'barricade', from 'barrique' (barrel), originally referring to barriers made of barrels.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04103942-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈbærɪˌkeɪd / (AmE), / ˌbærɪˈkeɪd / (BrE)
A barrier, usually quickly constructed, used to block or defend against movement, especially to impede an enemy's advance.
a quickly constructed barrier

Examples

Police erected a barricade at the end of the street to keep protesters back.
The rebels used overturned cars to make a makeshift barricade in the city center.
After the flood, a barricade was set up to prevent access to dangerous areas.

Synset Examples

they stormed the barricade

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstacle - something that blocks one's way or prevents progress
  • Synonym: barrier - a structure that prevents or controls movement

Etymology

From French 'barricade', from 'barrique' (barrel), originally referring to barriers made from barrels.

Source: WordNet

5. VERB

/ ˈbærɪˌkeɪd, ˌbærɪˈkeɪd / (AmE), / ˈbærɪkeɪd / (BrE)
to block or close off a place by putting up barriers, especially to prevent access or passage
block off by barriers

Examples

We had to barricade the door with furniture during the storm.
Workers quickly barricaded the entrance after the spill was discovered.

Etymology

From French 'barricader', from 'barrique' (barrel), originally referring to barrels used as makeshift barriers.
