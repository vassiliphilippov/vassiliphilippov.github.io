Psittacus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtəkəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪtəkəs / (BrE)
the genus of parrots that includes the African grey parrot and related species, serving as the type genus of the family Psittacidae
genus of parrots including the African grey

Examples

The African grey parrot belongs to the genus Psittacus.
Early zoologists classified several species under the genus Psittacus.
The characteristics of Psittacus distinguish it from other parrot genera.

Etymology

From Latin *Psittacus*, meaning 'parrot', from Ancient Greek *psittakos*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtəkəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪtəkəs / (BrE)
A genus of parrots, usually referring to the African grey parrot, in the family Psittacidae.
genus of parrots (African grey)

Examples

The African grey parrot belongs to the genus Psittacus.
Taxonomists recently reviewed the classification of Psittacus based on new genetic evidence.
Among the best-known members of Psittacus is the highly intelligent African grey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Psittacidae - the parrot family to which Psittacus belongs

Etymology

From Latin 'psittacus', meaning 'parrot', itself from Ancient Greek 'psittakos'.
