damnatory

1. ADJ

/ ˈdæm.nəˌtɔː.ri / (AmE), / ˈdæm.nə.təri / (BrE)
(of language, statements, etc.) threatening or condemning to damnation; expressing strong disapproval or condemnation.
threatening with damnation; condemning
archaic

Examples

The priest delivered a damnatory sermon warning the congregation of eternal consequences.
Her damnatory remarks left no doubt about her disapproval of their actions.
The document ends with a damnatory judgment on the accused.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condemnatory - expressing strong disapproval or condemnation

Etymology

From Latin damnatorius (condemning), from damnare (to condemn, to sentence).
