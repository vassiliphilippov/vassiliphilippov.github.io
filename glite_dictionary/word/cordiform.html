cordiform

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɔrdɪˌfɔrm / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdɪfɔːm / (BrE)
(of a leaf or object) shaped like a heart; heart-shaped.
heart-shaped (esp. of a leaf)

Examples

The plant’s cordiform leaves make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
Some art pieces from the Renaissance feature cordiform motifs to symbolize love.
The cordiform pattern on the shell makes this mollusk easy to identify.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heart-shaped - having the shape of a heart

Etymology

From Latin 'cor' (heart) + '-form' (having a shape).
