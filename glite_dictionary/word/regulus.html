Regulus

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɛɡjələs / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊləs / (BrE)
the brightest star in the constellation Leo, one of the brightest stars in the night sky
brightest star in Leo constellation

Examples

Regulus is easily visible to the naked eye on a clear night in spring.
Ancient astronomers often used Regulus to help navigate the seas.
During the Leo meteor shower, Regulus stands out near the radiant point.

Etymology

From Latin 'regulus' meaning 'prince' or 'little king', referring to its prominence in the sky.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛɡjələs / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊləs / (BrE)
a genus of small passerine birds known as kinglets, found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere
genus of kinglets (birds)

Examples

The Regulus genus includes charming birds like the goldcrest and firecrest.
Scientists observed several species within the Regulus genus during their field study.
Among European birds, members of the Regulus genus are some of the smallest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kinglet - a small songbird belonging to the genus Regulus

Etymology

From Latin *regulus*, meaning ‘prince’ or ‘little king’
