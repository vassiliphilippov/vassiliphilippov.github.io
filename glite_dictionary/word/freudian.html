Freudian

1. ADJ

/ ˈfrɔɪdiən / (AmE), / ˈfrɔɪdiən / (BrE)
relating to Sigmund Freud or his psychoanalytic theories, especially regarding the unconscious mind and motivations.
relating to Freud or his psychoanalytic ideas

Examples

Many modern therapists question Freudian interpretations of dreams.
His remark was a classic example of a Freudian slip.
The novel is influenced by Freudian concepts of the unconscious.

Synset Examples

Freudian theories

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychoanalytic - relating to psychoanalysis or its methods

Etymology

From the name Sigmund Freud, Austrian neurologist and founder of psychoanalysis, plus the suffix -ian.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03043247-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ frɔɪˈdiːən / (AmE), / frɔɪˈdiːən / (BrE)
a person who supports or practices the theories of Sigmund Freud, especially in psychology or psychoanalysis
follower of Freud's theories

Examples

As a committed Freudian, she interpreted the patient's dreams for hidden meanings.
The debate between the Freudian and behaviorist approaches continues in psychology.
Many modern therapists are influenced by Freudian thought, even if they don't follow it strictly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychoanalyst - a therapist trained in Freudian psychoanalysis
  • Antonym: Jungian - a follower of Carl Jung's theories

Etymology

From the name Sigmund Freud + -ian, denoting association with Freud's ideas.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10131548-n
Synset POS: n
