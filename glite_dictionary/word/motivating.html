motivating

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊ.təˌveɪ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈməʊ.tɪˌveɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of encouraging someone to take action by providing reasons or incentives.
the act of encouraging action or effort

Examples

Her motivating of the team led to improved performance throughout the season.
The manager's motivating was crucial during tough times at the company.
Effective motivating often involves understanding each person's unique goals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motivation - the reason or reasons one has for acting or behaving in a particular way

Etymology

From the verb 'motivate' (from Latin 'motivus', meaning 'moving'), with the suffix '-ing' forming a noun.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmoʊ.t̬ɪˌveɪ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈməʊ.tɪˌveɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
providing a reason or incentive to take action; encouraging or inspiring people to act
encouraging or inspiring action

Examples

The coach gave a motivating speech that inspired the team to victory.
She finds working with passionate people extremely motivating.
A motivating factor for many employees is the opportunity for promotion.

Synset Examples

it may well be that ethical language has primarily a motivative function
motive pleas
motivating arguments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inspiring - having the effect of inspiring someone
  • Antonym: discouraging - making someone less confident or enthusiastic

Etymology

From the verb 'motivate', itself from 'motive' (reason for action) and the suffix '-ing'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00325093-s
Synset POS: s
