hail-fellow

1. ADJ

/ ˈheɪl ˌfɛloʊ / (AmE), / ˈheɪl ˌfɛləʊ / (BrE)
(of a person) exhibiting hearty, friendly, and easy-going sociability; warmly congenial.
hearty and sociable (personality)
archaic

Examples

His hail-fellow manner made everyone feel instantly at ease at the party.
She disliked the hail-fellow attitude he adopted with strangers.
Though once reserved, he became almost hail-fellow in retirement, chatting with everyone he met.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: friendly - kind and pleasant in manner
  • Synonym: cordial - warm and friendly
  • Antonym: aloof - not friendly or forthcoming

Etymology

From the phrase 'hail, fellow, well met', an old greeting; 'hail' (to greet) + 'fellow' (companion).
