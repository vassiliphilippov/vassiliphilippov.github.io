cottonweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːtənˌwid / (AmE), / ˈkɒtənˌwiːd / (BrE)
any of several North American plants of the genus Froelichia, typically found in sandy or rocky soils and characterized by spikes of woolly white flowers
woolly-flowered plant of genus Froelichia

Examples

The sandy field was covered in cottonweed gently swaying in the summer breeze.
Gardeners enjoy growing cottonweed for its decorative, woolly white flower spikes.
Several species of cottonweed thrive in the rocky slopes of the American Southwest.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑtnˌwid / (AmE), / ˈkɒtnˌwiːd / (BrE)
a North American plant with soft, woolly leaves and clusters of small white, papery flowers; also called 'pearly everlasting'.
a woolly-leaved American plant

Examples

The meadows were sprinkled with cottonweed, its white blooms bright against the green grass.
Hikers admired the soft texture of the cottonweed leaves along the trail.
Several species of cottonweed are valued for their ornamental and medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pearly everlasting - North American plant with papery white flower heads, synonym for cottonweed
