heiress

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛr.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈheə.rɪs / (BrE)
a woman who inherits or is entitled to inherit the property, rank, or title of another
female who inherits property or title

Examples

After the death of her father, she became the sole heiress to the family fortune.
The wealthy heiress donated millions to charity each year.
Rumors swirled around the young heiress and her lavish lifestyle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heir - a person entitled to inherit property or title
  • Antonym: testator - a person who makes a will

Etymology

From Old French 'heritiere', feminine of 'heritier', from Latin 'heres' (heir).
