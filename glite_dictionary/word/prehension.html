1. NOUN

/ priˈhɛnʃən / (AmE), / prɪˈhɛnʃən / (BrE)
The act of grasping or seizing something, especially with the hands or tentacles.
the act of grasping or seizing

Examples

The study examined the prehension abilities of infants as they developed fine motor skills.
Octopuses use their tentacles for prehension to capture prey.
Impaired prehension can significantly affect an individual’s ability to perform daily tasks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grasp - to seize and hold firmly
  • Antonym: release - to let go; cease to hold

Etymology

From Latin prehensio (a taking, seizing), from prehendere (to seize, grasp).
