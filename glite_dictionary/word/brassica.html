Brassica

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɹæsɪkə / (AmE), / ˈbrasɪkə / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the mustard family, including cabbages, cauliflowers, and turnips.
genus of plants including cabbages and mustards

Examples

Many common vegetables, such as cabbage and broccoli, belong to the Brassica genus.
Farmers rotate Brassica crops to help control soil pests and diseases.
The nutritional value of Brassica vegetables is widely recognized by health experts.

Etymology

From Latin *brassica*, meaning cabbage.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbræsɪkə / (AmE), / ˈbrasɪkə / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the mustard family, including cabbages, cauliflowers, broccoli, turnips, and related vegetables.
genus of cabbage family plants

Examples

Many vegetables, including broccoli and kale, belong to the Brassica genus.
Scientists study Brassica species to improve crop yields and disease resistance.
Farmers rotate Brassica crops to maintain soil health and control pests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mustard - plants of the genus Brassica and related genera, often used as condiments

Etymology

From Latin *brassica* (cabbage).
