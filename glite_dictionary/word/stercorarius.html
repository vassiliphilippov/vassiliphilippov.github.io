Stercorarius

1. NOUN

/ stəˌrkɔːˈrɛəriəs / (AmE), / stɜːˌkɔːˈreəriəs / (BrE)
A genus of seabirds in the family Stercorariidae, commonly known as jaegers.
genus of seabirds (jaegers)

Examples

The Stercorarius genus includes several species of jaegers found in Arctic and subarctic regions.
Ornithologists observed a group of Stercorarius feeding near the breeding grounds.
Field guides often list Stercorarius as the type genus of the skua family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jaeger - a seabird in the genus Stercorarius

Etymology

From Latin 'stercorarius' meaning 'of dung', referring to the bird's habit of chasing other birds for food.

2. NOUN

/ stɛrˌkɔːrˈeəriəs / (AmE), / stɜːˌkɒrˈeəriəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Stercorariidae, comprising the jaegers or skuas—seabirds known for their predatory and scavenging behavior.
genus of seabirds (jaegers/skuas)

Examples

Many species in the genus Stercorarius are known for stealing food from other seabirds.
Unlike gulls, members of Stercorarius are aggressive predators and scavengers.
The South Polar Skua belongs to the genus Stercorarius.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jaeger - a type of seabird in the genus Stercorarius
  • Synonym: skua - another common name for birds in the genus Stercorarius

Etymology

From Latin stercorarius, meaning 'of dung', referring to the bird’s scavenging habits.
