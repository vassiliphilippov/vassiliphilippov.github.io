Ascaris lumbricoides

1. NOUN

/ ˈæskəˌrɪs lʌm.brɪˈkɔɪ.diːz / (AmE), / ˈæskərɪs ˌlʌm.brɪˈkɔɪ.diːz / (BrE)
a species of parasitic roundworm that infects the intestines of humans and pigs, causing ascariasis
intestinal parasitic roundworm infecting humans
medical

Examples

In regions with poor sanitation, Ascaris lumbricoides is a common cause of intestinal infections.
Doctors diagnosed the patient with ascariasis after finding Ascaris lumbricoides eggs in the stool sample.
Pigs can also serve as hosts for Ascaris lumbricoides, making farm hygiene important.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roundworm - a worm of the phylum Nematoda, typically parasitic, including Ascaris
  • Synonym: ascariasis - infestation with or disease caused by Ascaris lumbricoides

Etymology

From New Latin: genus Ascaris (from Greek askaris, intestinal worm) + species lumbricoides (resembling an earthworm, from Latin lumbrix).
