Heritiera macrophylla

1. NOUN

/ hɛˌrɪtiˈɪrə ˌmæk.rəˈfaɪ.lə / (AmE), / hɛˌrɪtiˈɪərə ˌmak.rəˈfʌɪ.lə / (BrE)
a large evergreen tree native to India and Burma, noted for its leaves that are silvery on the underside
large evergreen tree with silvery-backed leaves

Examples

The leaves of Heritiera macrophylla are easily recognized by their silvery undersides.
Local communities in India sometimes use the wood of Heritiera macrophylla for construction.
Heritiera macrophylla thrives in the moist forests of Burma, where it grows to impressive heights.

Etymology

From genus Heritiera (named after French botanist Charles Louis L’Héritier de Brutelle) and Latin macrophylla, from 'macro-' (large) + 'phylla' (leaved).
