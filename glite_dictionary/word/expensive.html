expensive

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈspɛnsɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛnsɪv / (BrE)
Costing a lot of money; having a high price.
high in price

Examples

The watch was too expensive for me to buy at full price.
Many tourists avoid the city's expensive restaurants and choose small local cafés instead.
Her taste in jewelry has always been rather expensive.

Synset Examples

expensive clothes
an expensive shop

Related Terms

  • Synonym: costly - having a high price or costing a lot
  • Antonym: cheap - low in price or costing little
  • Degree Higher: overpriced - priced higher than its value or worth
  • Degree Lower: affordable - reasonably priced; not too expensive

Etymology

From Latin 'expensivus', from 'expensa' (expenditure), via Old French 'expensif'.
