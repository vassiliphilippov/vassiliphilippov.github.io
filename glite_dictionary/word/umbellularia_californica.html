Umbellularia californica

1. NOUN

/ ʌmˌbɛljuːˈlɛəriə ˌkælɪˈfɔrnɪkə / (AmE), / ʌmˌbɛljʊˈleəriə ˌkælɪˈfɔːnɪkə / (BrE)
A tree native to the Pacific coast of North America, noted for its aromatic leaves, small clusters of flowers, and hard, tough wood; also called California bay or Oregon myrtle.
Pacific coast tree with aromatic leaves

Examples

The aromatic leaves of the Umbellularia californica are often used in cooking as a seasoning.
Hikers in California sometimes recognize the Umbellularia californica by the distinctive fragrance of its foliage.
Furniture makers prize the tough wood of the Umbellularia californica for its durability and beautiful finish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: California bay - another common name for Umbellularia californica
  • Synonym: Oregon myrtle - alternative name for the same species

Etymology

From Latin 'Umbellularia', the genus name, and 'californica', referring to California, its native region.
