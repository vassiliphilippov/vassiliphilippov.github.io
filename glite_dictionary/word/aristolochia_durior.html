Aristolochia durior

1. NOUN

/ ˌærɪstəˈloʊkiə dɜrˈaɪ.ər / (AmE), / ˌærɪstəˈləʊkiə djʊəˈraɪ.ə / (BrE)
a hardy deciduous climbing vine with large heart-shaped leaves and pipe-shaped flowers, often cultivated as an ornamental plant
species of ornamental climbing vine

Examples

The Aristolochia durior vine is often planted to provide dense summer shade on arbors.
Gardeners admire the large, heart-shaped leaves of Aristolochia durior.
The curious pipe-shaped flowers make Aristolochia durior easy to identify in the garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dutchman's pipe - another common name for Aristolochia durior

Etymology

From genus name Aristolochia (from Greek aristos ‘best’ + locheia ‘childbirth’) and species Latin durior, comparative of durus ‘hard’
