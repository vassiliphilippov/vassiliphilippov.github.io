crystalline

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrɪstəˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstəlaɪn / (BrE)
extremely clear or transparent, like crystal; transmitting light and able to be seen through with clarity
extremely clear or transparent

Examples

The mountain lake was so crystalline that the fish were visible beneath the surface.
She admired the crystalline structure of the snowflakes glittering in the sunlight.
Through the crystalline glass, they could see the garden in perfect detail.

Synset Examples

the cold crystalline water of melted snow
crystal clear skies
could see the sand on the bottom of the limpid pool
lucid air
a pellucid brook
transparent crystal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limpid - (of liquid) completely clear and transparent
  • Synonym: pellucid - transparently clear; easily understandable
  • Antonym: cloudy - not transparent; difficult to see through
  • Antonym: opaque - not able to be seen through; not transparent

Etymology

From Latin crystallinus, from Greek krustallos (crystal).

/ ˈkrɪstəˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstəlɪn / (BrE)
consisting of or containing crystals; having the structure or appearance of crystals
having the structure of crystals

Examples

Synset Examples
