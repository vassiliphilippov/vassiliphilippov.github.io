vena paraumbilicalis

1. NOUN

/ ˌveɪnə ˌpærəˌʌmbɪˈlaɪkəlɪs / (AmE), / ˌviːnə ˌpærəʌmbɪˈleɪkəlɪs / (BrE)
A small vein or group of veins arising in the skin around the navel that terminate as accessory portal veins in the liver.
small veins around the navel leading to the liver
medical

Examples

In certain liver diseases, the vena paraumbilicalis may become more prominent.
The surgeon identified the vena paraumbilicalis while performing the abdominal procedure.
Anatomical diagrams often include the vena paraumbilicalis near the umbilicus.

Etymology

From Latin: 'vena' (vein) + 'para-' (beside) + 'umbilicalis' (of the navel).
