henbit

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛn.bɪt / (AmE), / ˈhɛn.bɪt / (BrE)
A low Eurasian plant of the mint family, having toothed leaves and small two-lipped white or purplish-red flowers, often considered a weed.
a low Eurasian plant of the mint family

Examples

The garden was filled with henbit blooming early in the spring.
Farmers often have to remove henbit from their fields to prevent it from spreading.
Wild henbit attracts bees and other pollinators with its small purple flowers.

Etymology

From Middle English 'henbete', so called because hens feed on it.
