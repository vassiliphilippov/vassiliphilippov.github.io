architectonics

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrkɪˈtɑːnɪks / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪˈtɒnɪks / (BrE)
the scientific study of the principles and structure of architecture or of the organization of a system
science of architectural principles

Examples

The book offers a detailed analysis of the architectonics of ancient temples.
In philosophy, architectonics refers to the systematic organization of knowledge.
Modern architectonics incorporates both functionality and aesthetic appeal in building designs.

Etymology

From Greek arkhitektonikos (of or pertaining to architecture).
