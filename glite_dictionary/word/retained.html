retained

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈteɪnd / (AmE), / rɪˈteɪnd / (BrE)
kept in one's possession, use, or memory; continued to be held or remembered.
kept in possession, use, or memory

Examples

The company has a list of retained clients who receive special offers every year.
Much of the retained knowledge from our training helped us solve later problems.
He was grateful for the retained memories of his childhood, even as other details faded.

Synset Examples

in … the retained pattern of dancers and guests remembered

Related Terms

  • Antonym: forgotten - no longer remembered or kept in mind
  • Synonym: kept - kept in one's possession or care

Etymology

Past participle of 'retain', from Latin 'retinere' (to hold back).
