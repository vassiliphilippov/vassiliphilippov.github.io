Caryocar

1. NOUN

/ ˈkærioʊˌkɑr / (AmE), / ˈkærɪəʊˌkɑː / (BrE)
A genus of South American trees in the family Caryocaraceae, known for producing strong fine-grained wood and edible nuts.
genus of South American trees

Examples

The Caryocar trees are highly valued for their durable wood.
Scientists studied the growth patterns of several Caryocar species in the Amazon.
Locals harvest edible nuts from Caryocar trees for food and trade.

Etymology

From Greek karyon (nut) + -karpos (fruit).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkær.i.oʊˈkɑːr / (AmE), / ˌkær.i.əʊˈkɑː / (BrE)
A genus of South American trees in the Caryocaraceae family, known for strong, fine-grained wood and edible nuts.
genus of South American trees

Examples

The Caryocar genus includes trees that produce edible nuts prized in South America.
Researchers studied the ecological impact of Caryocar species in the Amazon rainforest.
The hard wood of Caryocar trees is often used in furniture making.

Etymology

From Greek karyon (nut) and -car, referring to the fruit-bearing nature.
