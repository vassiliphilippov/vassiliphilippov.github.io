Schistosomatidae

1. NOUN

/ skɪsˌtəsəˈmætɪdiː / (AmE), / skɪsˌtɒsəˈmætɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of parasitic flatworms (trematodes) that includes several species which cause schistosomiasis in humans and other animals.
family of parasitic flatworms (trematodes)
medical

Examples

The Schistosomatidae family includes the parasites responsible for schistosomiasis in humans.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Schistosomatidae during their fieldwork in Africa.
Proper identification of Schistosomatidae is crucial for effective disease control in endemic regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trematode - parasitic flatworm of the class Trematoda

Etymology

From the genus name Schistosoma, from Greek 'skhistos' (split) + 'soma' (body), with the taxonomic family suffix '-idae'.
