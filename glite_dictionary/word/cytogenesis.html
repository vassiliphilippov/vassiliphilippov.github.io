cytogenesis

1. NOUN

/ saɪtoʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / saɪtəʊˈdʒɛnɪsɪs / (BrE)
the origin, development, and variation of cells within an organism or tissue
origin and development of cells
medical

Examples

The process of cytogenesis is critical during embryonic development.
Researchers studied abnormal cytogenesis in cancerous tissues.
Advances in stem cell biology have led to new insights into cytogenesis.

Etymology

From Greek 'kytos' (cell) + 'genesis' (origin, creation).
