audio

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔdiˌoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɔːdiəʊ / (BrE)
sound, especially the range of frequencies that can be heard by the human ear, or the reproduction or transmission of such sound.
sound in the audible range

Examples

The audio on this recording is surprisingly clear for its age.
Engineers adjusted the audio levels to reduce background noise.
She specializes in audio production for film and television.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sound - vibrations that travel through air or another medium and can be heard

Etymology

From Latin 'audīre' meaning 'to hear'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05063537-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔːdioʊ / (AmE), / ˈɔːdiəʊ / (BrE)
the part of a television broadcast relating to sound, as distinct from the visual component.
sound portion of television broadcast

Examples

We lost the audio during the live broadcast due to a technical problem.
After adjusting the cables, the audio on the television became much clearer.
The movie’s audio was out of sync with the video, making it hard to follow.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: video - the visual part of a television broadcast

Etymology

From Latin 'audīre' (to hear), through French and English use in electronics.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02759756-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈɔdiˌoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɔːdiəʊ / (BrE)
A recording or transmission of sound, especially music, speech, or other acoustic signals.
recording or transmission of sound

Examples

The journalist played the audio from the interview at the press conference.
She sent me an audio file so I could listen to her song.
Due to technical difficulties, we could only access the audio and not the video.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: video - a recording or transmission of moving visual images

Etymology

From Latin 'audīre' (to hear), via English 'audio-' (sound).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04269788-n
Synset POS: n

the sound portion of a broadcast, recording, or transmitted signal, as opposed to the visual component

Examples

During the storm, the audio kept cutting out on the television broadcast.
She adjusted the audio settings to reduce background noise on her conference call.
