to aurify

1. VERB

/ ˈɔːrɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɔːrɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
to make or turn (something) golden in color or appearance
make or turn golden

Examples

The setting sun seemed to aurify the clouds with a brilliant golden light.
Artists often attempt to aurify everyday objects in their work, giving them a luminous, precious quality.
The poet described how autumn leaves aurify the landscape each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to gild - to cover thinly with gold or make golden in appearance

Etymology

From Latin aurum (gold) + -fy (make or become).

2. VERB

/ ˈɔrɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɔːrɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
to transform or convert into gold
transform into gold
archaic

Examples

The ancient legend tells of a king who could aurify anything he touched.
The scientist dreamed of discovering a chemical that would aurify lead into pure gold.
Greedy alchemists worked night and day, hoping to aurify base metals.

Etymology

From Latin 'aurum' (gold) + English '-fy' (make or become).
