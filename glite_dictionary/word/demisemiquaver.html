demisemiquaver

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛm.iˈsɛm.iˌkweɪ.vɚ / (AmE), / ˌdɛm.iˈsɛm.iˌkweɪ.və / (BrE)
a musical note with the time value of one thirty-second of a whole note; also called a thirty-second note
thirty-second musical note
british

Examples

The fast passage was filled with demisemiquaver runs that challenged even the most skilled musicians.
She found it difficult to keep time when the piece included several demisemiquavers in a row.
In British notation, a demisemiquaver lasts for one-thirty-second the duration of a whole note.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thirty-second note - a musical note with the time value of a thirty-second of a whole note; American term for demisemiquaver
  • Degree Lower: semiquaver - a musical note with the value of a sixteenth of a whole note

Etymology

From demi- (half) + semiquaver (a sixteenth note in British usage).
