bullterrier

1. NOUN

/ ˌbʊlˈtɛriər / (AmE), / ˌbʊlˈtɛriə / (BrE)
A powerful, short-haired breed of dog developed in England by crossing bulldogs with terriers.
a breed of muscular dog from England

Examples

The bullterrier is known for its distinctive egg-shaped head and muscular build.
My neighbor has two energetic bullterriers that love to play in the yard.
After researching several breeds, they chose a bullterrier for its loyal and affectionate nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bull terrier - muscular dog breed with egg-shaped head
  • Degree Lower: terrier - a type of small to medium dog originally used for hunting vermin
  • Degree Higher: bulldog - a muscular, medium-sized breed originally used for bull-baiting

Etymology

From 'bull' (as in bulldog) + 'terrier,' indicating its origins from both breeds.
