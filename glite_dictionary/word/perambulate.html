to perambulate

1. VERB

/ pəˈræmbjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / pəˈræmbjʊleɪt / (BrE)
(formal) to make an official inspection on foot, especially of the boundaries of a property.
make an official boundary inspection on foot
legal archaic

Examples

The council officially perambulates the village boundaries each spring to check for encroachments.
According to tradition, landowners must perambulate their property lines every few years.
After they perambulated the estate, they recorded the exact positions of the stone markers.

Synset Examples

Selectmen are required by law to perambulate the bounds every five years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inspect - to look at something carefully in order to assess its condition

Etymology

From Latin perambulare, meaning 'to walk through.'

2. VERB

/ pəˈræmbjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / pəˈræmbjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to walk or travel around, especially without a specific destination or for pleasure
walk or travel around aimlessly

Examples

Every evening, the old professor would perambulate through the quiet campus grounds, lost in thought.
Tourists like to perambulate around the historic district, taking in the sights at a leisurely pace.
After the rain stopped, she decided to perambulate along the riverside trail to clear her mind.

Synset Examples

we were walking around in the garden
after breakfast, she walked about in the park

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wander - to walk or move in a leisurely, casual, or aimless way
  • Synonym: stroll - to walk in a slow, relaxed manner, especially for pleasure

Etymology

From Latin perambulare (to walk through), from per- (through) + ambulare (to walk).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01914958-v
Synset POS: v
