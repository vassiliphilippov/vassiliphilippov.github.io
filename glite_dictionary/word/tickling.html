tickling

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɪklɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɪklɪŋ / (BrE)
causing excitement or laughter by touching lightly, often resulting in involuntary movements
causing laughter by light touch

Examples

The tickling sensation from the feather made him giggle uncontrollably.
A tickling breeze brushed against her face as she walked along the shore.
He tried to ignore the tickling feeling in his throat during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ticklish - sensitive to being tickled; easily made to laugh by touch
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00925680-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɪklɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɪklɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of touching someone lightly in a way that causes laughter or twitching due to sensitivity
the act of touching someone to make them laugh

Examples

Tickling always made her laugh uncontrollably, no matter how hard she tried not to.
They burst into giggles during the tickling game on the living room floor.
The children protested, but they secretly enjoyed the friendly tickling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tickle - to touch or stroke lightly to cause laughter

Etymology

From the verb 'tickle,' of uncertain origin, possibly imitative.
