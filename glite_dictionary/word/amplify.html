to amplify

1. VERB

/ ˈæm.pləˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈæm.plɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to make sound or an electrical signal louder, especially by using electronic equipment
make sound louder; increase volume

Examples

The speaker will amplify the music so everyone can hear it clearly.
You need to amplify the microphone to pick up all the voices.
They used special equipment to amplify the sound of the guitar during the concert.

Synset Examples

amplify sound

Related Terms

  • Synonym: increase - to become or make greater in size, amount, or degree
  • Antonym: to reduce - to make something smaller or less in amount or degree

Etymology

From Latin amplificare (to enlarge), from amplus (large, ample) + facere (to make).

2. VERB

/ ˈæm.pləˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈæm.plɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to increase the size, volume, or significance of something; to make stronger or greater
increase size, volume, or significance

Examples

The loudspeaker was used to amplify her voice so everyone could hear.
Recent events only served to amplify public concern about the issue.
He tried to amplify the impact of his message through repeated social media posts.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to magnify - to make something appear larger or more important
  • Antonym: to diminish - to make or become less

Etymology

From Latin 'amplificare', from 'amplus' (large) + 'facere' (to make).

3. VERB

/ ˈæm.pləˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈam.plɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to make something seem larger, greater, or more important than it really is; to exaggerate.
exaggerate or make something bigger

Examples

Her report tends to amplify the problems the city faces.
Politicians sometimes amplify small issues to attract more attention.
He amplified his achievements when talking to the interviewer.

  • Antonym: to minimize - to make something seem less important or smaller than it is

Etymology
