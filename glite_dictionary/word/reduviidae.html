Reduviidae

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈduːviˌɪdiː / (AmE), / rɛˈdjuːvɪˌɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of predatory insects known as assassin bugs, characterized by their elongated bodies and a prominent, curved proboscis used for piercing prey.
family of predatory assassin bugs
medical

Examples

Reduviidae are widely distributed in tropical and subtropical regions around the world.
Entomologists studied the feeding habits of Reduviidae to understand their predatory role in agriculture.
Some species within the family Reduviidae can transmit Chagas disease to humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assassin bug - common name for insects in the Reduviidae family

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Reduvius, from Latin 'reduvia' (a remnant or fragment, possibly referring to the insects' discarded exoskeletons).
