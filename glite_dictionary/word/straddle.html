straddle

1. NOUN

/ ˈstræd.əl / (AmE), / ˈstræd.əl / (BrE)
a gymnastic movement in which the legs are extended on either side of the parallel bars, with the body supported by the arms
gymnastic movement with legs apart

Examples

She performed a perfect straddle on the parallel bars during her routine.
The coach demonstrated how to do a straddle safely without losing balance.
His routine included a challenging straddle followed by a smooth dismount.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: split - a position or movement in gymnastics or dance in which the legs are extended in opposite directions

2. NOUN

/ ˈstrædəl / (AmE), / ˈstræd.əl / (BrE)
the act of sitting or standing with one leg on each side of something
the act of sitting or standing astride

Examples

She took a quick straddle of the fence to get to the other side.
The gymnast's straddle was perfect during her routine.
He performed a straddle before landing neatly on the mat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astride - with a leg on each side

Etymology

Possibly from dialectal straddle, frequentative of standard stride, from Old English strīdan (to stride).

6. NOUN

/ ˈstrædəl / (AmE), / ˈstræd.əl / (BrE)
an investment strategy involving the purchase of both a put and a call option on the same asset, with the same strike price and expiry date, allowing the holder to profit from significant price movements in either direction
options strategy with put and call on same asset

Examples

She decided to use a straddle to profit from the company's expected volatility.
Buying a straddle can help mitigate risk when the market direction is uncertain.
His portfolio includes a straddle on several technology stocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: option - a contract giving the right to buy or sell an asset at a set price
  • Degree Lower: call option - an option to buy an asset at an agreed price
  • Degree Lower: put option - an option to sell an asset at an agreed price

7. NOUN

/ ˈstræd.əl / (AmE), / ˈstræd.əl / (BrE)
a position or policy of refusing to commit fully to a single side or opinion, especially to avoid taking risks or offending others
noncommittal or equivocal position

Examples

The politician's straddle on the key issue disappointed both sides of the debate.
By maintaining a straddle, the company avoided alienating any part of its customer base.
His constant straddle between opposing opinions frustrated his colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neutrality - the state of not supporting or helping either side in a conflict
  • Antonym: commitment - the state or quality of being dedicated to a cause or activity

Etymology

From the verb 'straddle', meaning to sit or stand with legs wide apart; figurative sense developed by extension to indecision or lack of commitment.
