Governorate-General

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡʌvərnərət ˈdʒɛnərəl / (AmE), / ˌɡʌvənərət ˈdʒɛnərəl / (BrE)
an administrative-territorial division of the Russian Empire, established between 1775 and 1917, typically comprising several governorates and governed by a governor-general.
historical Russian administrative division

Examples

The Moscow Governorate-General played a significant role in the administration of the Russian Empire.
Several provinces were merged to form a new Governorate-General in the late 19th century.
Historians have studied the structure and influence of each Governorate-General in imperial Russia.

Synset Examples

Moscow and Saint-Petersburg governorates were designated into a separate governorate-generals.

Etymology

From 'governorate' (a governed territory) and 'general', reflecting the authority of a governor-general over several regions.
