Triga

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪɡə / (AmE), / ˈtrɪɡə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Triglidae, comprising certain gurnard fishes.
genus of gurnard fishes (Triglidae)

Examples

The genus Triga is distinguished by its armored head and large pectoral fins.
Marine biologists discovered a new species within the Triga genus during their expedition.
The distribution of Triga extends across several temperate and tropical seas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Triglidae - the family of fishes known as sea robins or gurnards

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'trigla', a kind of fish.
