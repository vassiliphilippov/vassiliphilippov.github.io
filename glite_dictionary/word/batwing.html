batwing

1. NOUN

/ ˈbætˌwɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbætˌwɪŋ / (BrE)
one of a pair of swinging doors, typically found at the entrance to a western saloon
one of a pair of saloon swinging doors

Examples

The sheriff pushed open the batwing and strode into the dimly lit saloon.
She could hear the squeak of the batwing as customers came and went from the bar.
The old movie set still had a pair of batwing doors at the entrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saloon door - a pair of swinging doors used in bars or saloons, particularly in the American West

2. ADJ

/ ˈbætˌwɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbatˌwɪŋ / (BrE)
(of clothing) having wide sleeves that are attached with deep armholes and taper towards the wrist, resembling the wing of a bat.
shaped like a bat's wing; wide, tapering sleeve style

Examples

She wore a stunning evening dress with batwing sleeves.
The designer's new collection features several batwing tops in bold colors.
Vintage fashion often includes sweaters with exaggerated batwing arms.
