Ptilonorhynchidae

1. NOUN

/ taɪlənɔːˈrɪŋkɪdi / (AmE), / taɪlənɒˈrɪŋkɪdi / (BrE)
A family of passerine birds known as bowerbirds, native to Australia and New Guinea, noted for the males' elaborate courtship structures called bowers.
family of bowerbirds

Examples

The family Ptilonorhynchidae includes species famous for their complex courtship behavior.
Researchers studied the mating rituals of the Ptilonorhynchidae in the forests of New Guinea.
Male birds of the Ptilonorhynchidae construct intricate bowers to attract females.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bowerbird - a bird known for building elaborate bowers

Etymology

From Greek 'ptilon' (feather) + 'rhynchos' (beak), with New Latin suffix '-idae' denoting a family.
