Nitrobacter

1. NOUN

/ ˌnaɪtroʊˈbæktər / (AmE), / ˌnɪtrəʊˈbæktə / (BrE)
a genus of rod-shaped soil bacteria that oxidize nitrites to nitrates, playing a crucial role in the nitrogen cycle
genus of soil bacteria

Examples

Nitrobacter helps convert nitrite to nitrate in the soil, supporting plant growth.
Scientists have studied Nitrobacter to understand its role in the nitrogen cycle.
The presence of Nitrobacter is important for maintaining soil fertility in agricultural fields.

Etymology

From Latin 'nitrum' (niter) + Greek 'baktērion' (small rod, bacterium).

2. NOUN

/ naɪtroʊˈbæktər / (AmE), / naɪtrəʊˈbæktə / (BrE)
A genus of rod-shaped soil bacteria that play a key role in the nitrogen cycle by converting nitrites into nitrates.
genus of soil bacteria

Examples

Nitrobacter is essential for the process of nitrification in soil.
Scientists isolated Nitrobacter from various agricultural fields to study its environmental impact.
The presence of Nitrobacter can improve the fertility of farmland by increasing nitrate levels.

Etymology

From Latin 'nitrum' (niter, saltpeter) and 'bacter' (bacterium).
