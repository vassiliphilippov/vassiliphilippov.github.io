metamorphosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtəˈmɔrfoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈmɔːfəsɪs / (BrE)
a marked and rapid physical transformation that occurs in some animals as they change from larva to adult form
biological transformation from larva to adult

Examples

The caterpillar’s metamorphosis into a butterfly fascinated the children.
During metamorphosis, the tadpole loses its tail and develops legs.
Scientists study metamorphosis to understand the dramatic changes in insect anatomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transformation - the act or process of changing completely

Etymology

From Greek metamorphōsis (transformation), from meta- (change) + morphē (form).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtəˈmɔrfəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈmɔːfəsɪs / (BrE)
a complete or remarkable change in form, appearance, character, or circumstances
a dramatic change; transformation

Examples

The city has undergone a remarkable metamorphosis over the past decade.
Her shy personality experienced a metamorphosis when she started college.
The caterpillar's metamorphosis into a butterfly fascinated the children.

Related Terms

3. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtəˈmɔrfəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈmɔːfəsɪs / (BrE)
a complete change of form, structure, or substance, especially one resulting from an apparent magical or supernatural cause
complete change of form or substance

Examples

The caterpillar's metamorphosis into a butterfly is one of nature's most astonishing changes.
After months abroad, her metamorphosis was apparent to everyone who knew her before.
The fairy tale describes a prince's metamorphosis into a frog by an evil witch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transformation - a thorough or dramatic change in form or appearance
  • Synonym: transmutation - the action of changing or the state of being changed into another form
  • Antonym: stasis - a state in which there is little or no apparent change

Etymology

From Greek metamorphōsis, from meta- (change) + morphē (form).
