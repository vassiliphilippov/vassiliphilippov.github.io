winsomeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪnsəmnəs / (AmE), / ˈwɪnsəmnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being attractive or charming in a fresh, innocent, or endearing way
charming or appealing quality

Examples

Her winsomeness captivated everyone at the party immediately.
Many people are drawn to the winsomeness of young children.
Despite her age, she retained a winsomeness that made her presence delightful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charm - the power or quality of delighting or attracting others
  • Synonym: sweetness - a delightful or pleasing quality, especially in temperament
  • Antonym: repulsiveness - the state of being repellent or causing aversion

Etymology

From Old English 'wynn' (joy, pleasure) + '-some' (characterized by), forming 'winsome'; '-ness' added for the noun.
