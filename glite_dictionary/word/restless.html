restless

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛstləs / (AmE), / ˈrɛstləs / (BrE)
unable to relax or stay still because of anxiety or boredom
unable to relax or be still

Examples

After a long flight, he felt restless and couldn't sit still any longer.
The children grew restless during the long, slow car ride.
Her mind was restless with worries as she waited for the news.

Synset Examples

a constant fretful stamping of hooves
a restless child

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions

Etymology

From Middle English restles, from rest + -less.

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɛstləs / (AmE), / ˈrɛstləs / (BrE)
never still; constantly in motion or activity
constantly moving or active

Examples

The restless wind kept howling outside the house all night long.
Ships rocked on the restless sea as the storm intensified.
A restless energy seemed to fill the streets during the festival.

Synset Examples

the restless sea
the restless wind

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - not moving or showing activity; still

Etymology

From Old English restleas (restless), from 'rest' + '-less'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈrɛstləs / (AmE), / ˈrɛstləs / (BrE)
unable to relax or stay still because of worry or uneasiness.
unable to relax due to worry or uneasiness

Examples

She felt restless and couldn't concentrate on her work.
The audience grew restless as the speaker continued past his allotted time.
After hearing the bad news, he spent a restless night tossing and turning in bed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxious - worried, nervous, or uneasy about something
  • Antonym: calm - free from worry or agitation

Etymology

From Old English restlēas (without rest), from 'rest' + 'less.'

Source: WordNet

4. ADJ

/ ˈrɛstləs / (AmE), / ˈrɛstləs / (BrE)
unable to relax or remain still; lacking or not affording physical or mental rest.
unable to rest or relax

Examples

After a restless night, she felt tired and irritable in the morning.
The children grew restless during the long bus ride and started fidgeting in their seats.
His restless mind kept racing with thoughts, making it hard to concentrate.

Synset Examples

a restless night
she fell into an uneasy sleep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uneasy - feeling anxious or uncomfortable
  • Antonym: calm - free from disturbance or agitation
