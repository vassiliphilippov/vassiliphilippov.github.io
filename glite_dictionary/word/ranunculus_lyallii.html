Ranunculus lyallii

1. NOUN

/ rəˈnʌŋkjələs laɪˈæliː / (AmE), / rəˈnʌŋkjələs laɪˈaliː / (BrE)
A showy perennial plant native to New Zealand, known for its large white flowers and round leaves, also called the Mount Cook buttercup.
New Zealand perennial with white flowers

Examples

The Ranunculus lyallii is admired for its stunning white blooms amid alpine landscapes.
Many hikers in New Zealand enjoy spotting Ranunculus lyallii during the spring months.
Botanists study Ranunculus lyallii to understand its adaptation to cold mountainous environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buttercup - any plant of the genus Ranunculus, usually with yellow flowers

Etymology

From Latin genus name Ranunculus (meaning 'little frog', referring to the plant’s wet habitat) and named after David Lyall, a Scottish explorer and botanist.
