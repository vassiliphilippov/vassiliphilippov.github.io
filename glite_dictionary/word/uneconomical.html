uneconomical

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˌiːkəˈnɑːmɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnˌiːkəˈnɒmɪkəl / (BrE)
not using resources such as money, fuel, or time effectively; wasteful or not cost-effective.
not cost-effective; wasteful

Examples

The old machine is too uneconomical to keep running given today's energy prices.
Replacing all the windows at once was considered uneconomical by the building inspector.
Although the idea was promising, the process turned out to be uneconomical in practice.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: economical - using resources carefully and not wastefully

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'economical'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˌɛkəˈnɑmɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnˌiːkəˈnɒmɪkəl / (BrE)
not using resources, time, or effort efficiently; wasteful or not cost-effective.
not efficient or cost-effective

Examples

Running an old car can be uneconomical due to its high fuel consumption.
The plan was rejected for being too uneconomical considering the project's limited budget.
Buying cheap products that break easily is often uneconomical in the long run.

Synset Examples

a clumsy and wasteful process
wasteful duplication of effort
uneconomical ebb and flow of power

Related Terms

  • Antonym: economical - using resources efficiently; not wasteful

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'economical'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00844698-s
Synset POS: s
