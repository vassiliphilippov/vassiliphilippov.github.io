Ezra Cornell

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛzrə kɔːrˈnɛl / (AmE), / ˈɛz.rə kɔːˈnɛl / (BrE)
(1807–1874) U.S. businessman and philanthropist who helped unify the telegraph system in the United States and, together with Andrew D. White, founded Cornell University in 1865.
U.S. businessman, founder of Cornell University

Examples

Ezra Cornell played a key role in revolutionizing communication by developing the country's telegraph system.
The university was named after Ezra Cornell, one of its founders.
Ezra Cornell's commitment to education has had a lasting impact on American academia.
