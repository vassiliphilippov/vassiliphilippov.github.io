corps

1. NOUN

/ kɔːr / (AmE), / kɔː / (BrE)
a large military unit, usually consisting of two or more divisions and their support
large military unit

Examples

The general commanded the entire corps during the campaign.
A new corps was formed to provide rapid response in emergencies.
Several divisions make up a corps in most armies.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: division - a military unit typically smaller than a corps

Etymology

From French 'corps' (body); from Latin 'corpus' (body).

2. NOUN

/ kɔːr / (AmE), / kɔː / (BrE)
a group of people engaged in a particular activity or working together as an organized body, especially within a profession or organization
organized group of people with a shared activity

Examples

The corps of engineers worked tirelessly on the new highway project.
She was honored to join the diplomatic corps after years of training.
A strong press corps attended the conference to cover the latest developments.

Synset Examples

diplomatic corps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unit - a single group within a larger organization or structure, especially in the military

Etymology

From French corps (body), from Latin corpus (body).
