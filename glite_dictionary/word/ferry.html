ferry

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛri / (AmE), / ˈfɛri / (BrE)
a service or means of transporting people or goods across water or, less commonly, by air, usually at regular intervals.
transport service by boat or aircraft

Examples

The last ferry leaves the island at 7 p.m., so don't miss it.
During the storm, the ferry service was suspended for safety reasons.
They took a ferry across the river to reach the mainland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ferryboat - a vessel used for ferrying
  • Antonym: bridge - a structure for crossing water or a gap, contrasting with ferry service

Etymology

From Old French 'ferier' (to carry over) and Latin 'feriare' (to carry).

2. VERB

/ ˈfɛri / (AmE), / ˈfɛri / (BrE)
to carry people or goods from one place to another, typically in a vehicle such as a boat, ship, or vehicle, usually on a regular basis.
carry people or goods between places

Examples

Each morning, the buses ferry hundreds of workers into the city.
Helicopters were used to ferry supplies to the stranded villagers.
The school district ferries students from rural areas to the central campus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transport - to carry something from one place to another

Etymology

From Middle English *ferien* (carry, transport), from Old English *ferian* (to convey, carry), related to *farian* (to travel).

3. VERB

/ ˈfɛri / (AmE), / ˈfɛri / (BrE)

transport by ferry boat

Examples

The company ferries over a thousand passengers each day between the islands.
They ferried supplies to the mainland during the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transport - to carry people or goods from one place to another

Etymology

From Old French *ferier* (to carry), from Latin *feria* (holiday, free passage), influenced by ferry as a noun.

4. NOUN

/ ˈfɛri / (AmE), / ˈfɛri / (BrE)
a boat or ship for carrying people, vehicles, or goods across a body of water on a regular schedule
boat carrying people/vehicles across water

Examples

We took the ferry across the bay to reach the island.
The last ferry leaves the port at midnight every day.
Passengers waited in line to board the ferry with their cars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ferryboat - a boat used as a ferry
  • Antonym: bridge - a structure for crossing over water, antonym as it replaces a ferry's function

Etymology

From Old French *ferier* (to carry over), from Latin *feriare*, related to *ferre* (to carry).

5. VERB

/ ˈfɛri / (AmE), / ˈfɛri / (BrE)
to transport people or goods by boat or other vehicle, especially across a body of water at regular intervals.
transport by boat or vehicle, especially across water

Examples

The boatman will ferry tourists across the river every hour.
They ferried supplies to the island during the storm.
Helicopters were used to ferry the injured workers to safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shuttle - to transport repeatedly between two points

Etymology

From Old French *ferier* (to carry across), from Late Latin *feriāre*, from Latin *ferre* (to carry).
