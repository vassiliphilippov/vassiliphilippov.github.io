nebular

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɛbjələr / (AmE), / ˈnɛbjʊlə / (BrE)
relating to, or resembling a nebula, especially in form or appearance
relating to a nebula

Examples

The nebular structure was clearly visible through the powerful telescope.
Scientists are studying nebular processes to understand star formation.
The nebular hypothesis explains how the solar system may have formed from a cloud of gas and dust.

Synset Examples

the nebular hypothesis of the origin of the solar system

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nebula - a cloud of gas and dust in outer space

Etymology

From Latin 'nebula' meaning 'mist, cloud'.

WordNet ID: oewn-03100835-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈnɛbjələr / (AmE), / ˈnɛbjʊlə / (BrE)
resembling a cloud; having a cloudy or indistinct appearance
cloud-like or cloudy in appearance

Examples

A faint, nebular glow surrounded the distant star in the telescope's view.
The artist chose soft, nebular colors to create an ethereal sky in the painting.
Mist rose from the lake in nebular formations at dawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloudy - having the appearance or quality of a cloud

Etymology

From Latin 'nebula' (mist, cloud), via New Latin 'nebula' used in astronomy.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00464162-s
Synset POS: s
