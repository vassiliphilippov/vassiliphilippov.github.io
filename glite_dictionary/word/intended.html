intended

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtɛndɪd / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛndɪd / (BrE)
planned or meant; resulting from a person's intentions rather than by accident or chance.
planned or meant to happen

Examples

The change in schedule was not an intended consequence of the plan.
She apologized for the intended insult after realizing it was hurtful.
Their intended route was blocked due to construction.

Synset Examples

your intended trip abroad
an intended insult

Related Terms

  • Antonym: accidental - happening by chance, not planned
  • Synonym: deliberate - done consciously and intentionally

Etymology

Past participle of 'intend', from Latin intendere ('to stretch out, aim at').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01340439-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtɛndɪd / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛndɪd / (BrE)
(of a woman) engaged to be married; betrothed.
engaged to be married
archaic

Examples

Everyone was eager to meet his intended at the engagement party.
The bride's intended gave a heartfelt speech during the dinner.
Victorian novels often refer to a person's betrothed as their intended.

Synset Examples

his intended bride

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiancée - a woman engaged to be married
  • Synonym: betrothed - the person to whom one is engaged

Etymology

Past participle of 'intend', from Latin 'intendere' (to stretch toward, mean).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00159503-s
Synset POS: s
