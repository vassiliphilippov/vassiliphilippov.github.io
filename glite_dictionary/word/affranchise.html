to affranchise

1. VERB

/ əˈfræn.tʃaɪz / (AmE), / əˈfræn.tʃaɪz / (BrE)
to grant freedom to someone, especially from slavery or servitude
grant freedom from slavery or servitude

Examples

After years of negotiation, the government decided to affranchise all bonded workers.
Some reformers petitioned the king to affranchise the nation's serfs.
The movement aimed to affranchise those held in involuntary servitude.

Synset Examples

Slaves were enfranchised in the mid-19th century

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emancipate - to set free, especially from legal, social, or political restrictions
  • Synonym: to enfranchise - to grant freedom or rights, particularly the right to vote
  • Antonym: to enslave - to make someone a slave; deprive of freedom

Etymology

From Old French affranchir, from Medieval Latin affrancare, from Latin ad- + francus (free).
