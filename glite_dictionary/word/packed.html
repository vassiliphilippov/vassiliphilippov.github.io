packed

1. ADJ

/ pækt / (AmE), / pækt / (BrE)
extremely crowded or filled to capacity; having no room for more people or things
extremely crowded or full

Examples

The concert hall was packed with enthusiastic fans last night.
We tried to get a seat at the restaurant, but it was absolutely packed.
The subway trains are always packed during rush hour.

Synset Examples

a suitcase jammed with dirty clothes
stands jam-packed with fans
a packed theater

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jam-packed - extremely full or crowded
  • Antonym: empty - containing nothing; not filled or occupied

2. ADJ

/ pækt / (AmE), / pækt / (BrE)
pressed together or compressed, often into a tight mass or space
pressed or compressed; tightly packed

Examples

The path was covered in packed snow, making it slippery to walk on.
She opened the freezer and found it packed with frozen dinners.
The concert hall was so packed that there was barely room to stand.

Synset Examples

packed snow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compressed - pressed into a smaller space; made compact
  • Antonym: loose - not firmly or tightly packed; not compacted

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'pack', from Middle English 'pakken', probably of Low German origin.
