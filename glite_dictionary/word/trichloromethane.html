trichloromethane

1. NOUN

/ traɪˌklɔːrəˈmɛθeɪn / (AmE), / traɪˌklɒrəˈmiːθeɪn / (BrE)
a chemical compound (CHCl3), also known as chloroform, formerly used as an anesthetic and solvent
a chemical compound, also called chloroform

Examples

The laboratory technician carefully measured the trichloromethane before starting the experiment.
Due to its toxic effects, trichloromethane is no longer used as an anesthetic.
The chemical spill involved several substances, including trichloromethane.

Synset Examples

chloroform was the first inhalation anesthetic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chloroform - a volatile liquid used as a solvent and formerly as an anesthetic

Etymology

From 'tri-' (three), 'chloro-' (chlorine), and 'methane', indicating three chlorine atoms replacing hydrogen atoms in methane.
