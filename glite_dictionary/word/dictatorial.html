dictatorial

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɪk.təˈtɔːr.i.əl / (AmE), / ˌdɪk.təˈtɔː.ri.əl / (BrE)
having or showing complete power and expecting others to obey without question; characteristic of an absolute ruler or absolute rule
having absolute, authoritarian power

Examples

The company was run in a dictatorial manner, with no input from employees.
Her dictatorial attitude made it difficult for anyone to disagree with her.
During the war, the government adopted dictatorial powers to control the country.

Synset Examples

an authoritarian regime
autocratic government
despotic rulers
a dictatorial rule that lasted for the duration of the war
a tyrannical government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authoritarian - favoring or enforcing strict obedience to authority, especially that of the government
  • Synonym: autocratic - relating to a ruler with absolute power
  • Antonym: democratic - relating to or supporting democracy or its principles

Etymology

From Medieval Latin dictātōrius, from Latin dictātor (dictator).

2. ADJ

/ ˌdɪk.təˈtɔri.əl / (AmE), / ˌdɪk.təˈtɔː.ri.əl / (BrE)
expecting or requiring unquestioning obedience; behaving in an autocratic or domineering manner
expecting obedience; domineering

Examples

The manager's dictatorial style left employees afraid to voice their opinions.
Her dictatorial commands made group work difficult for everyone involved.
He was criticized for his dictatorial approach to leadership.

Synset Examples

the timid child of authoritarian parents
insufferably overbearing behavior toward the waiter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authoritarian - favoring or enforcing strict obedience to authority
  • Synonym: overbearing - unpleasantly or arrogantly domineering
  • Antonym: democratic - relating to a system encouraging participation and equality

Etymology

From Latin 'dictator' (a magistrate with absolute power), with English suffix '-ial'.

WordNet ID: oewn-00790912-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌdɪk.təˈtɔːr.i.əl / (AmE), / ˌdɪk.təˈtɔː.ri.əl / (BrE)
typical of or relating to a dictator; tending to tell people what to do in an autocratic way
like a dictator; autocratic

Examples

The manager's dictatorial approach made employees reluctant to voice their opinions.
He ruled the organization in a dictatorial manner, allowing no dissent.
Her dictatorial style alienated many of her former supporters.

Synset Examples

dictatorial powers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autocratic - ruling with unlimited power; like an absolute ruler
  • Antonym: democratic - governed by the people or their elected representatives

Etymology

From Latin dictātōrius (belonging to a dictator), from dictātor (dictator).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02723414-a
Synset POS: a
