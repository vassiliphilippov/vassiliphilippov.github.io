camphor

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæm.fɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæm.fə / (BrE)
a white, crystalline substance with a strong odor, obtained from the camphor tree and used in medicine and the production of plastics and explosives
strong-smelling compound from camphor tree

Examples

The ointment contains camphor, which helps relieve muscle pain.
A faint scent of camphor filled the medicine cabinet.
They used camphor crystals to repel insects in the old wardrobe.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin camphora, from Arabic kāfūr, from Malay kapur barus (Barus lime).
