safranine

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæfrəˌnin / (AmE), / ˈsæfrəniːn / (BrE)
any of a class of red dyes used especially in biological and chemical staining
a red organic dye used in staining

Examples

The biologist stained the slide with safranine to highlight the cell nuclei.
Safranine is commonly used in laboratories as a counterstain in microscopy.
The solution turned a vivid red after adding a few drops of safranine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: safranin - alternative spelling of safranine, a red dye

Etymology

From French 'safranine', from 'safran' meaning saffron (for its color resemblance).
