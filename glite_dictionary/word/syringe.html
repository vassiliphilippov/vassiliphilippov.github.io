syringe

1. VERB

/ sɪˈrɪndʒ / (AmE), / sɪˈrɪndʒ / (BrE)
to spray or irrigate a part of the body with a syringe
spray or irrigate with a syringe

Examples

The nurse syringed the patient's ear to remove the blockage.
It is important to syringe wounds carefully to prevent infection.
The veterinarian syringed the dog's wound with saline solution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to irrigate - to wash out a body part with a flow of liquid

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *syringa*, from Greek *syrinx* (pipe, tube).

2. NOUN

/ səˈrɪndʒ / (AmE), / sɪˈrɪndʒ / (BrE)
a medical instrument with a hollow cylinder and a plunger, used for injecting or withdrawing fluids from the body
medical instrument for injecting or withdrawing fluids
medical

Examples

The nurse carefully filled the syringe before giving the vaccination.
He used a syringe to draw a blood sample from the patient.
Discard used syringes in the designated sharps container.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypodermic - a syringe with a hollow needle for injecting substances under the skin

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'syringa', from Greek 'syrinx' meaning 'tube'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04383963-n
Synset POS: n
