illustriousness

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlʌstriəsnəs / (AmE), / ɪˈlʌstriəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being highly distinguished or eminent; great fame or glory
the quality of being illustrious

Examples

Her illustriousness as a scientist earned her numerous accolades and international recognition.
The illustriousness of the ancient dynasty drew hundreds of visitors to the national museum.
He aspired to achieve the same illustriousness in his field as the legends who inspired him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distinction - the quality of being very good or special in some way
  • Antonym: obscurity - the state of being unknown or not famous

Etymology

From illustrious + -ness, ultimately from Latin illustris (bright, distinguished).
