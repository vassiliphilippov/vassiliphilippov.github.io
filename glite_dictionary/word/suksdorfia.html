suksdorfia

1. NOUN

/ sʌksˈdɔːrfiə / (AmE), / sʌksˈdɔːfɪə / (BrE)
Any of several American plants of the genus Suksdorfia, characterized by round or kidney-shaped succulent leaves and terminal clusters of white, rose, or violet flowers.
genus of North American flowering plants

Examples

Botanists discovered a rare suksdorfia growing near the mountain stream.
The leaves of suksdorfia are typically round and slightly succulent.
In the spring, suksdorfia produces delicate violet flowers in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Etymology

Named after Wilhelm N. Suksdorf, German-American botanist (1850–1932).
