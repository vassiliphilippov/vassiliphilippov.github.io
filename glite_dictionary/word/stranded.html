stranded

1. ADJ

/ ˈstrændɪd / (AmE), / ˈstrændɪd / (BrE)
left without the means to move from somewhere; cut off or left behind, especially in a helpless situation.
left helpless or unable to move

Examples

He was stranded at the airport overnight when his flight was canceled.
The stranded whales were found on the beach after the storm.
Without her wallet, she felt completely stranded in the city.

Synset Examples

an isolated pawn
several stranded fish in a tide pool
travelers marooned by the blizzard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marooned - left in a place from which you cannot escape
  • Synonym: isolated - far away from other things or people

Etymology

Past participle of 'strand' (to drive or leave aground), originally from Old Norse 'strönd' (shore, beach).
