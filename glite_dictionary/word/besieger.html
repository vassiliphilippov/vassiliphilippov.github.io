besieger

1. NOUN

/ bəˈsiːdʒər / (AmE), / bɪˈsiːdʒə / (BrE)
a person or group that surrounds and attacks a place in order to capture it, typically by cutting off essential supplies; someone who lays siege
person who lays siege to a place

Examples

The city held out bravely against the besieger for several months.
The besieger constructed trenches and towers to gain an advantage.
After days of negotiation, the besieger finally agreed to a truce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attacker - someone who attacks
  • Antonym: defender - someone who protects or resists attack

Etymology

From Middle English 'besiegen', from Old French 'siege' (a seat, a siege).
