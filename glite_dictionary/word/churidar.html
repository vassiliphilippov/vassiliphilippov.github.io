churidar

1. NOUN

/ tʃʊrɪˈdɑr / (AmE), / tʃʊəriˈdɑː / (BrE)
a type of tight-fitting trousers from the Indian subcontinent, usually worn with a kameez or kurta
tight-fitting trousers from South Asia

Examples

She wore a beautiful blue churidar to the wedding ceremony.
His wardrobe includes several churidars in different colors and fabrics.
The churidar fits snugly around the legs and gathers at the ankles.

Etymology

From Hindi 'churidar', from 'churi' (bangle) and 'dar' (like, resembling), referring to how the folds resemble bangles.
