blind

1. NOUN

/ blaɪnd / (AmE), / blaɪnd / (BrE)
people who are unable to see, considered as a group
people with severe visual impairments, as a group

Examples

The school has special programs designed for the blind.
Advocates speak out for the rights of the blind.
He volunteered as a reader for the blind at the community center.

Synset Examples

he spent hours reading to the blind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the visually impaired - people who have reduced or no vision

2. ADJ

/ blaɪnd / (AmE), / blaɪnd / (BrE)
(of feelings or actions) not based on reason or evidence; lacking rational justification.
not based on reason or evidence

Examples

Despite all the evidence, he had blind faith in his leader.
Her blind hatred prevented her from seeing the truth.
The crowd's blind panic led to chaos and confusion.

Synset Examples

blind hatred
blind faith
unreasoning panic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irrational - not logical or reasonable

Etymology

Old English 'blind' (unable to see), extended figuratively to describe lack of discernment or reason.
