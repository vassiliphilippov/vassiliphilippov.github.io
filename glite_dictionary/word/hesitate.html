to hesitate

1. VERB

/ ˈhezɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈhezɪteɪt / (BrE)
to pause briefly before saying or doing something, often because of uncertainty or indecision
pause briefly before acting or speaking

Examples

She began to hesitate before answering the difficult question.
He didn’t hesitate for long before continuing his speech.
If you hesitate too much, you might miss your chance.

Synset Examples

The speaker paused

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pause - to stop briefly before continuing
  • Antonym: to proceed - to continue without pausing or hesitation

Etymology

From Latin 'haesitare', frequentative of 'haerēre' (to stick, to hesitate).

2. VERB

/ ˈhɛzɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈhɛzɪteɪt / (BrE)
to pause before saying or doing something, often because you feel uncertain or unwilling
pause due to uncertainty

Examples

She didn't hesitate before answering the question confidently.
If you hesitate too long, you might miss your opportunity.
He often hesitates at the door, unsure whether to enter.

Synset Examples

Authorities hesitate to quote exact figures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waver - to be undecided between two opinions or courses of action
  • Antonym: to decide - to make a choice or come to a conclusion

Etymology

From Latin *haesitare* (to stick fast, hesitate), frequentative of *haerēre* (to stick).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02646524-v
Synset POS: v
