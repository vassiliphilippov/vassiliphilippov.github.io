leach

1. NOUN

/ liːtʃ / (AmE), / liːtʃ / (BrE)
the process of washing or draining out soluble substances from a material by the action of percolating liquid, especially water
the process of leaching out substances

Examples

During heavy rainfall, nutrients can leach from the soil into nearby waterways.
The leach of harmful chemicals from old batteries poses environmental risks.
Scientists study the leach of minerals to understand soil composition changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leaching - the action or process of leaching

Etymology

From Middle English lechen (to water, irrigate), from Old English leccan (to moisten).

2. VERB

/ litʃ / (AmE), / liːtʃ / (BrE)
to cause a liquid to filter or percolate through a substance, often resulting in the removal of soluble components.
cause a liquid to filter through something

Examples

Heavy rains can leach chemicals from the soil into nearby rivers.
The process is designed to leach nutrients from plant material using water.
Engineers developed a new method to leach waste from the mining site safely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to percolate - (of a liquid) to pass or filter through a porous substance

Etymology

Probably from Middle English leche, lecche (to let water drip), of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ litʃ / (AmE), / liːtʃ / (BrE)
(of a substance) to be gradually removed or carried away from something, especially soil, by the action of water passing through it.
to be gradually removed by water

Examples

Over time, rainwater can leach nutrients from the soil, making it less fertile.
Chemicals from the landfill slowly leach into the groundwater, posing an environmental risk.
The color will eventually leach out of the fabric if it is washed too often.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to percolate - (of a liquid) to pass through a substance slowly

Etymology

From Middle English lechen, probably from Old English leccan (to wet, moisten) or related Scandinavian words.

4. VERB

/ liːtʃ / (AmE), / liːtʃ / (BrE)
to remove soluble substances from a material by passing a liquid through it
remove substances by passing liquid through

Examples

Farmers often worry that irrigation will leach important minerals out of their fields.
The process is designed to leach contaminants from the groundwater before it enters the river.
