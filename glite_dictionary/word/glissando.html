glissando

1. NOUN

/ ɡlɪˈsɑndoʊ / (AmE), / ɡlɪˈsændəʊ / (BrE)
a rapid sliding movement or series of quickly played notes that creates a continuous ascending or descending effect in music
rapid sliding movement in music

Examples

The pianist executed a dramatic glissando up the keyboard during the finale.
The piece begins with a cello glissando that sets a mysterious tone.
He was impressed by the smooth glissando the violinist performed between notes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slide - a similar musical technique involving sliding between pitches

Etymology

From Italian 'glissando,' the gerund of 'glissare' (to glide, slide), modeled after French 'glisser'.

2. ADV

/ ɡlɪˈsɑndoʊ / (AmE), / ɡlɪˈsændəʊ / (BrE)
(as a musical direction) to be played in the style of a glissando, with a swiftly executed series of notes.
musical direction: in the manner of a glissando

Examples

The pianist is instructed to play the passage glissando for a dramatic effect.
He performed the scale glissando, sliding his fingers rapidly across the keys.
The conductor signaled the strings to enter glissando during the crescendo.

Synset Examples

this should be played glissando, please

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legato - in a smooth, connected manner (musical direction)
  • Antonym: staccato - in a short, detached manner (musical direction)

Etymology

From Italian 'glissando', present participle of 'glissare' (to glide), from French 'glisser' (to slip).
