grapheme

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræˌfim / (AmE), / ˈɡræfiːm / (BrE)
A written symbol that represents a single sound or unit of speech in a language.
written symbol representing speech sound

Examples

The English alphabet uses twenty-six different graphemes to represent its sounds.
In written Korean, each grapheme corresponds to a specific syllable block.
Children learn to associate each grapheme with its matching sound when learning to read.

Synset Examples

the Greek alphabet has 24 characters

Related Terms

  • Antonym: phoneme - the smallest unit of sound in speech

Etymology

From Greek 'graphein' (to write) and '-eme' (unit).
