handwriting

1. NOUN

/ ˈhændˌraɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhændˌraɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
writing done by hand; the style or manner of a person's written characters.
writing done by hand

Examples

She recognized his handwriting immediately on the envelope.
The teacher praised his neat handwriting in the exercise book.
Doctors are often joked about for having messy handwriting.

Synset Examples

she recognized his handwriting
his hand was illegible

Related Terms

  • Synonym: script - a person's handwriting or style of writing by hand
  • Antonym: typewriting - writing produced by a typewriter or keyboard, not by hand

Etymology

From hand + writing, indicating writing done with the hand.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhændˌraɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhændˌraɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of writing something by hand, using a pen, pencil, or similar instrument rather than a typewriter or computer.
the act of writing by hand

Examples

Learning handwriting skills is an important part of early education.
For people with arthritis, handwriting can sometimes be painful.
He prefers to take notes using handwriting rather than typing on a laptop.

Synset Examples

handwriting can be slow and painful for one with arthritis

Related Terms

  • Antonym: typing - the activity of writing using a typewriter or computer keyboard

Etymology

Formed from 'hand' + 'writing', first attested in English in the late Middle Ages.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00616017-n
Synset POS: n
