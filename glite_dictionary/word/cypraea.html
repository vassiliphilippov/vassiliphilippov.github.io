Cypraea

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈpriːə / (AmE), / sɪˈpriːə / (BrE)
a genus of marine gastropod molluscs in the family Cypraeidae, commonly known as the typical cowries, recognized for their glossy, brightly patterned shells.
genus of cowry sea snails
medical

Examples

The collector displayed several beautiful shells from the genus Cypraea.
Species in the Cypraea group have been prized for their unique patterns and shine.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Cypraea along the tropical coasts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowry - a marine mollusc with a glossy, domed shell, often brightly colored or patterned

Etymology

From Latin *Cypraea*, from Greek *kypraios* (of Cyprus), referencing the presence of these shells near Cyprus.
