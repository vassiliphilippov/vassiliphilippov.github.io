enchondroma

1. NOUN

/ ɛn.kɒnˈdroʊ.mə / (AmE), / ɛn.kɒnˈdrəʊ.mə / (BrE)
a benign, slow-growing tumor consisting of cartilage cells, usually found at the ends of the long bones of the hands or feet
benign cartilage tumor in bone
medical

Examples

The surgeon explained that the enchondroma in her finger was benign and could be removed safely.
Radiographs showed an enchondroma at the end of the patient's metacarpal bone.
Although an enchondroma is noncancerous, it requires monitoring to ensure it does not become problematic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: osteochondroma - a benign bone tumor containing both bone and cartilage
  • Antonym: chondrosarcoma - a malignant tumor of cartilage cells

Etymology

From Greek en- (in) + chondros (cartilage) + -oma (tumor, swelling).
