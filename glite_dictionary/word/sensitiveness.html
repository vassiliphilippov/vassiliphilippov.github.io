sensitiveness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnsətɪvnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsɪtɪvnɪs / (BrE)
(Physiology) the capacity to detect or respond to external stimuli; the faculty of sensation.
responsiveness to external stimuli (physiology)
medical

Examples

The patient's sensitiveness to touch indicated nerve damage.
In some individuals, excessive sensitiveness can cause pain from even light pressure.
Experiments measured the sensitiveness of skin to minute temperature changes.

Synset Examples

sensitivity to pain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensitivity - the quality of being sensitive; responsiveness to stimuli

Etymology

From 'sensitive' + '-ness', modeled after similar nouns.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.sɪ.tɪv.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsɪtɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being easily affected by emotional feelings, especially those of oneself or others
sensitivity to emotional feelings

Examples

Her sensitiveness to criticism made her cautious about sharing her opinions.
Teachers must be aware of students' sensitiveness to harsh words and feedback.
He appreciated her sensitiveness to the emotions of those around her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensitivity - the quality of being sensitive; responsiveness to others' feelings or to external stimuli
  • Antonym: insensitivity - lack of concern for others' feelings; inability to be easily affected emotionally

Etymology

From 'sensitive' + '-ness', formed by analogy with 'sensitivity'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnsətɪvnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsɪtɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the ability to detect or respond to very slight physical stimuli or small differences in amount
ability to respond to slight physical stimuli

Examples

The scientist measured the sensitiveness of the device to minimal electrical currents.
Due to its sensitiveness, the seismograph could detect even the faintest tremors.
The sensitiveness of her fingertips allowed her to distinguish the fabric's subtle textures.

Synset Examples

a galvanometer of extreme sensitivity
the sensitiveness of Mimosa leaves does not depend on a change of growth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensitivity - capacity to detect or respond to slight changes or signals

Etymology

Formed from 'sensitive' + '-ness', following the pattern of abstract nouns in English.

4. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.sə.tɪv.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsɪtɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being easily affected by or responsive to emotional changes in interpersonal situations; sensitivity to others' feelings or moods
emotional responsiveness in relationships

Examples

Her sensitiveness made her very aware of her friends' unspoken feelings.
He sometimes wishes he could suppress his sensitiveness at work to deal with criticism.
The therapist praised the child's remarkable sensitiveness to other people's emotions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensitivity - the quality of being sensitive; responsiveness to others or the environment
