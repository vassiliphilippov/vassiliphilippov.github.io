karyokinesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɛrioʊkɪˈniːsɪs / (AmE), / ˌkærɪəʊkɪˈniːsɪs / (BrE)
the process in which the nucleus of a cell divides during mitosis or meiosis
division of a cell's nucleus
medical

Examples

Karyokinesis is a critical step in both mitosis and meiosis.
Errors in karyokinesis can result in abnormal numbers of chromosomes in daughter cells.
Scientists observed the phases of karyokinesis under a powerful microscope.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cytokinesis - division of a cell's cytoplasm

Etymology

From Greek 'karyon' (nut, nucleus) + 'kinesis' (movement).
