bandbox

1. NOUN

/ ˈbænˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈbænd.bɒks / (BrE)
a light, cylindrical box, typically made of cardboard, used for storing or carrying lightweight clothing items, especially hats
light cylindrical box for attire, especially hats

Examples

She kept her favorite bonnet in a decorated bandbox on top of the wardrobe.
The old shop had shelves filled with bandboxes containing vintage hats.
He opened the bandbox carefully to reveal a delicate lace cap inside.

Etymology

From 'band' (a strip or ribbon) + 'box', originally used for storing hatbands.
