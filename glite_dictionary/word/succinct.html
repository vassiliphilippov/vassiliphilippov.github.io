succinct

1. ADJ

/ səkˈsɪŋkt / (AmE), / səkˈsɪŋkt / (BrE)
expressed in a clear and brief manner without unnecessary words
brief and clearly expressed

Examples

Her succinct explanation made the complex topic easy to understand.
I appreciate it when meeting summaries are succinct and direct.
Please keep your comments succinct to save time during the discussion.

Synset Examples

a short and compendious book
a compact style is brief and pithy
succinct comparisons
a summary formulation of a wide-ranging subject

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concise - expressed in few words; brief and to the point
  • Antonym: verbose - using more words than needed; wordy

Etymology

From Latin 'succinctus', past participle of 'succingere' (to gird up, tuck up).
