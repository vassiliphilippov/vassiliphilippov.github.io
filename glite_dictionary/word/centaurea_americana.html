Centaurea americana

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛntɔˈriə əˌmɛrɪˈkænə / (AmE), / ˌsɛntɔːˈriːə əˌmerɪˈkænə / (BrE)
An annual plant native to the southwestern United States, cultivated for its purple flower heads and basket-like bracts.
annual plant with purple flowers, native to US

Examples

The Centaurea americana adds a burst of color to summer gardens with its purple blooms.
Gardeners in Texas often choose Centaurea americana for its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
I planted Centaurea americana along the walkway to attract butterflies and bees.

Etymology

From Latin: Centaurea (the genus), and americana (of America), denoting its origin.
