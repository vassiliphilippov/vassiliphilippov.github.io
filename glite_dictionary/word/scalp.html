scalp

1. NOUN

/ skælp / (AmE), / skælp / (BrE)
the skin covering the top of the human head, usually bearing hair
skin covering the top of the head

Examples

The doctor examined her scalp for signs of irritation.
After a long hike in the sun, his scalp became slightly sunburned.
Regular shampooing helps keep the scalp clean and healthy.

Synset Examples

they wanted to take his scalp as a trophy

Etymology

From late Latin scalpus (the skull or scalp).

2. VERB

/ skælp / (AmE), / skælp / (BrE)
to sell something, especially tickets, illegally at a price higher than the official one
sell tickets or goods illegally at high price

Examples

He tried to scalp concert tickets outside the stadium.
Two people were arrested for attempting to scalp seats to the sold-out show.
It is illegal to scalp tickets on the street without a license.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to resell - to sell something again after buying it
  • Antonym: buy - to purchase an item, the opposite activity

Etymology

Originally from Middle English *scalpen* (to remove the scalp), developed figuratively in the late 19th century in American English for reselling tickets at a profit.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02246693-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ skælp / (AmE), / skælp / (BrE)
to remove the skin and hair covering the top of a person's head, especially as a trophy or in acts of violence
remove the scalp of someone

Examples

In ancient warfare, conquering tribes sometimes scalped their enemies as a sign of victory.
The story described how the hunters scalped their captives after the battle.
He read a historical account about soldiers who were scalped during the conflict.

Synset Examples

The enemies were scalped

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decapitate - to cut off the head

Etymology

From Middle English scalpe, from Old Norse skalpr (sheath, scabbard); the verb use dates from the 17th century, referring to the removal of the scalp.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00198852-v
Synset POS: v
