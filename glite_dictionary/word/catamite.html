catamite

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætəˌmaɪt / (AmE), / ˈkatəmʌɪt / (BrE)
a boy or young man who is in a sexual relationship with an older man, especially in historical or classical contexts
boy in a sexual relationship with a man (historical)
archaic

Examples

In ancient Rome, a wealthy man might keep a catamite as part of his household.
The poet referenced a catamite in his controversial verses about Greek society.
Literature often reflects on the power imbalance present in the relationship between a pederast and his catamite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pederast - an adult man who has sexual relations with boys

Etymology

From Latin catamītus, from Greek Katamītēs (a proper name), via Etruscan.
