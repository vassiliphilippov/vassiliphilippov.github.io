to optimise

1. VERB

/ ˈɑːptəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪmaɪz / (BrE)
to make something as effective, perfect, or useful as possible; to use in the best possible way
make as effective or useful as possible
british

Examples

We need to optimise our workflow to save time and resources.
The engine was carefully optimised for maximum efficiency.
She is always looking for ways to optimise her daily routine.

Synset Examples

optimize your resources

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to maximize - to make as large or great as possible
  • Antonym: to minimize - to make as small or unimportant as possible

Etymology

From Latin 'optimus' meaning 'best'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00172518-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈɑːptɪˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪmaɪz / (BrE)
to modify or adjust something so that it works as efficiently or effectively as possible
make as efficient as possible
british

Examples

The company hired an expert to optimise their supply chain processes.
We need to optimise this website to improve its loading speed.
She spent months trying to optimise the design for better performance.

Synset Examples

optimize a computer program

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to optimize - American spelling of 'optimise', meaning to make as efficient as possible
  • Synonym: to improve - to make or become better
  • Antonym: to degrade - to make worse; to lower in quality

Etymology

From Latin 'optimus' (best), via French 'optimiser'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00124034-v
Synset POS: v
