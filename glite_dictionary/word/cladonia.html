Cladonia

1. NOUN

/ kləˈdoʊni.ə / (AmE), / kləˈdəʊni.ə / (BrE)
A genus of lichens of the Cladoniaceae family, characterized by a crust-like base and upright stalks often topped with cap-like structures.
genus of lichens with stalked fruiting bodies

Examples

The forest floor was covered in patches of Cladonia and moss.
Researchers studied the unique reproductive structures of Cladonia lichens found in northern habitats.
Several species of Cladonia are important indicators of air quality.

Etymology

From Greek 'klados' meaning branch, referring to its branched structures.

2. NOUN

/ kləˈdoʊniə / (AmE), / kləˈdəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of lichens in the family Cladoniaceae, characterized by a crusty thallus and capitate fruiting bodies on simple or branched stalks (podetia).
genus of crustose lichens

Examples

Several species within the Cladonia genus are commonly known as reindeer lichen.
The Cladonia genus displays unique, branched podetia found in many tundra regions.
Lichenologists study Cladonia for its ecological role in soil stabilization.

Etymology

From Greek klados (branch), referring to the lichen's branched structures.
