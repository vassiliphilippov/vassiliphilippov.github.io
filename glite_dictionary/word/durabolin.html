Durabolin

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʊr.əˌboʊ.lɪn / (AmE), / ˈdjʊə.rəˌbəʊ.lɪn / (BrE)
A trade name for nandrolone phenylpropionate, an androgen used to treat testosterone deficiency, certain types of breast cancer, and osteoporosis.
androgen drug (nandrolone) for medical use
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Durabolin to help treat the patient's muscle wasting condition.
Athletes sometimes misuse Durabolin for its performance-enhancing effects, despite health risks.
Durabolin is administered by injection under medical supervision in clinical treatments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nandrolone - the active ingredient in Durabolin, a synthetic androgen
