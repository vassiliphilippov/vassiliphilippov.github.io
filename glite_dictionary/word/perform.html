to perform

1. VERB

/ pərˈfɔrm / (AmE), / pəˈfɔːm / (BrE)
to carry out or complete an action, task, or piece of work
carry out a task or action

Examples

The surgeon will perform the operation tomorrow morning.
She was asked to perform several tasks during her first week.
You must perform your duties with care and attention.

Synset Examples

I did my job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to execute - to carry out or put into effect a plan, order, or course of action
  • Synonym: to accomplish - to achieve or complete successfully
  • Antonym: to fail - to be unsuccessful in achieving a goal or completing a task

Etymology

From Old French 'parfornir', from Latin 'per-' (through) + 'formare' (to form).

2. VERB

/ pərˈfɔrm / (AmE), / pəˈfɔːm / (BrE)
to present a form of entertainment, such as music, drama, or dance, to an audience
present an entertainment for an audience

Examples

The band will perform live on stage tomorrow night.
She has performed in several plays throughout her career.
They performed a beautiful ballet at the theater last weekend.

Synset Examples

Horowitz is performing at Carnegie Hall tonight
We performed a popular Gilbert and Sullivan opera

Related Terms

  • Synonym: present - to give or show something publicly, especially as a performance
  • Synonym: to appear - to take part in a performance in front of an audience
  • Degree Lower: to rehearse - to practice a performance before presenting it to an audience

Etymology

From Old French *parfornir* (to accomplish, carry out), from Latin *per-* (completely) + *formare* (to form).

3. VERB

/ pərˈfɔrm / (AmE), / pəˈfɔːm / (BrE)
to carry out an action, task, or duty; to do something, especially as required or expected
carry out an action or task

Examples

She was asked to perform several tasks during the interview.
The technicians performed repairs on the damaged machinery last night.
Doctors must perform their duties with great care and responsibility.

Synset Examples

John did the painting, the weeding, and he cleaned out the gutters
the skater executed a triple pirouette
she did a little dance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to execute - to carry out, especially with precision or according to plans
  • Synonym: do - to carry out or perform an action or task
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to carry out an action or duty

Etymology

From Old French 'parfornir' (to accomplish, achieve), from 'par-' (through, completely) + 'fornir' (to furnish, provide), of Germanic origin.

carry out an action or function

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms
