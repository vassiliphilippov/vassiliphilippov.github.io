Erasmian

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈræzmiən / (AmE), / ɪˈrazmɪən / (BrE)
characteristic of, relating to, or in the style of Erasmus, especially his ideas, works, or reforms
of or relating to Erasmus

Examples

The Erasmian method of pronunciation is often taught in classical language courses.
Many scholars admire the Erasmian ideals of learning and reform.
The lecture focused on the influence of Erasmian humanism during the Renaissance.

Etymology

From Erasmus, the Latinized name of the Dutch humanist Desiderius Erasmus (1466–1536), with the suffix -ian indicating relation.
