penuche

1. NOUN

/ pəˈnuːtʃi / (AmE), / pəˈnjuːtʃɪ / (BrE)
A type of fudge made from brown sugar, butter, milk, and usually nuts, often with a characteristic caramel flavor.
brown sugar fudge with nuts

Examples

She brought a plate of homemade penuche to the holiday party.
The penuche had a rich caramel flavor and crunchy pecans.
Have you ever tried making penuche instead of chocolate fudge?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fudge - a soft candy made from sugar, butter, and milk, often with flavorings

Etymology

From Italian *panucci*, meaning 'fudge', derived from *pane* (bread), possibly via New England regional usage.
