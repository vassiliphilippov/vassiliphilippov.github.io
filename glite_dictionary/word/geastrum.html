Geastrum

1. NOUN

/ dʒiˈæstrəm / (AmE), / dʒiˈɑːstrəm / (BrE)
A genus of fungi belonging to the family Geastraceae, characterized by an outer layer that splits into star-shaped segments when dry.
star-shaped genus of fungi

Examples

The rare Geastrum species fascinated the mycologists with their star-shaped appearance.
While hiking, we discovered a Geastrum growing among the fallen leaves.
The exhibit included a dried specimen of Geastrum to demonstrate its unusual structure.

Etymology

From Greek 'gea' (earth) + 'astron' (star), referring to the star-like shape of mature fungi.
