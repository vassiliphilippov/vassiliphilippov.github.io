forearm

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔˌrɑrm / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌrɑːm / (BrE)
the part of the arm between the elbow and the wrist
part of the arm between elbow and wrist

Examples

She wore a bracelet on her forearm that sparkled in the sunlight.
The x-ray showed a fracture in his left forearm.
After lifting weights, his forearms felt sore and tense.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: upper arm - the part of the arm between the shoulder and the elbow

Etymology

From Old English 'fore-' (before) + 'arm'.

2. VERB

/ fɔːrˈɑːrm / (AmE), / ˌfɔːˈrɑːm / (BrE)
To arm or equip oneself with weapons or defenses in preparation for a possible confrontation or danger.
to arm oneself in advance

Examples

She decided to forearm herself with additional knowledge before entering the debate.
It is wise to forearm against possible threats when traveling abroad.
The team forearmed themselves with the latest security technology to prevent cyber attacks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prepare - to make ready beforehand for a specific purpose
  • Antonym: to disarm - to take away weapons or means of attack

Etymology

From prefix 'fore-' (meaning before) + 'arm' (to provide with weapons).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
