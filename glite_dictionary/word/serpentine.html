serpentine

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɜrpənˌtin, -ˌtaɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɜːpənˌtiːn / (BrE)
having a winding, twisting, or snake-like shape or movement
having a snake-like shape or movement

Examples

The serpentine road wound through the mountains, making the drive both beautiful and challenging.
She admired the serpentine patterns engraved into the ancient vase.
A serpentine river cut across the landscape, twisting and turning through the valley.

Synset Examples

a serpentine wall
snaky ridges in the sand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snaky - resembling a snake in form or movement

Etymology

From Latin serpentinus (of a serpent), from serpens (serpent).
