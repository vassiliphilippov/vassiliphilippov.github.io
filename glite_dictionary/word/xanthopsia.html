xanthopsia

1. NOUN

/ zænˈθɑːp.si.ə / (AmE), / zænˈθɒp.si.ə / (BrE)
a medical condition in which all objects appear to have a yellowish tint, sometimes occurring in jaundice and certain drug toxicities
visual defect causing yellow-tinted vision
medical

Examples

Some medications can induce xanthopsia as a side effect, making colors appear abnormally yellow.
Jaundice can be accompanied by xanthopsia, causing the patient to see a persistent yellow hue.
The ophthalmologist diagnosed the visual disturbance as xanthopsia after examining the symptoms.

Etymology

From Greek 'xanthos' (yellow) + 'opsis' (sight, vision).
