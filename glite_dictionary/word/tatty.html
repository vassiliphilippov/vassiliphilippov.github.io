tatty

1. ADJ

/ ˈtæti / (AmE), / ˈtæti / (BrE)
(of clothes, furniture, or other objects) in poor condition because of age or much use; shabby or worn.
shabby or in poor condition

Examples

The old sofa looked tatty after years of heavy use by the family.
She wore a tatty sweater with several holes in the sleeves.
The tatty pages of the book were yellowed and torn.

Synset Examples

a ratty old overcoat
shabby furniture
an old house with dirty windows and tatty curtains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shabby - in poor condition through long or hard use or lack of care
  • Antonym: pristine - in its original condition; unspoiled

2. ADJ

/ ˈtæti / (AmE), / ˈtæti / (BrE)
Tastelessly showy, flashy, or gaudy, often implying poor quality or cheapness.
tastelessly showy or gaudy

Examples

The living room was filled with tatty decorations that clashed loudly with the walls.
She wore a tatty costume covered in sequins and gaudy colors to the party.
The shop window was crowded with tatty jewelry and flashy trinkets.

Synset Examples

a flash car
a flashy ring
garish colors
a gaudy costume
loud sport shirts
a meretricious yet stylish book
tawdry ornaments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaudy - extravagantly bright or showy, typically so as to be tasteless
  • Synonym: flashy - attracting attention by being bright, ostentatious, or showy
  • Antonym: understated - presented or expressed in a subtle or restrained way
