Llano Estacado

1. PROPN

/ ˈjɑːnoʊ ˌestəˈkɑːdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈjɑːnəʊ ˌɛstəkˈɑːdəʊ / (BrE)
a large, semiarid plateau in the southern part of the Great Plains, spanning parts of Texas and New Mexico
plateau region in Texas and New Mexico

Examples

Many pioneers found the Llano Estacado challenging to cross due to its lack of water sources.
The high winds and flat terrain of the Llano Estacado shape its unique landscape.
Several cities, such as Lubbock, are located on the Llano Estacado.

Etymology

From Spanish, literally 'staked plain.'
