Anzu

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑːn.zu / (AmE), / ˈan.zu / (BrE)
A storm god in Mesopotamian mythology, often depicted as a massive black bird, associated with evil or chaos.
Mesopotamian storm god, black bird

Examples

The legend of Anzu describes him stealing the Tablet of Destinies from the gods.
Ancient texts depict Anzu as a monstrous bird with the ability to control storms.
Scholars continue to debate the symbolism of Anzu in Sumerian and Akkadian mythology.

Etymology

From Akkadian 'Anzu', a mythological creature in ancient Mesopotamian legends.
