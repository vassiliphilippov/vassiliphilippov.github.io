Gastrophryne

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡæstroʊˈfraɪni / (AmE), / ˌɡastrəʊˈfrʌɪni / (BrE)
A genus of primarily tropical narrow-mouthed toads found in the Americas.
genus of narrow-mouthed toads

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Gastrophryne in the rainforest.
The Gastrophryne toads are known for their unique call and pointed snouts.
Many Gastrophryne species inhabit leaf litter on the forest floor.

Etymology

From Greek 'gastro-' (stomach) + 'phryne' (toad).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɡæstroʊˈfraɪni / (AmE), / ˌɡæstrəˈfrʌɪni / (BrE)
A genus of primarily tropical narrow-mouthed toads native to the Americas.
genus of narrow-mouthed toads

Examples

Several species within the genus Gastrophryne are found in Central and South America.
Herpetologists recently discovered a new member of Gastrophryne in the rainforest.
The toads of Gastrophryne are known for their distinctive narrow mouths.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Microhylidae - the family of narrow-mouthed frogs and toads

Etymology

From Greek 'gastro-' meaning 'stomach' and 'phryne' meaning 'toad'.
