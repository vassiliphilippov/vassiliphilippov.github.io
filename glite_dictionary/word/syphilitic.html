syphilitic

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪfəˈlɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɪfɪˈlɪtɪk / (BrE)
A person who is suffering from syphilis.
person with syphilis
medical

Examples

The hospital provided treatment for every syphilitic who came through its doors.
In the late 19th century, a syphilitic often faced severe social stigma.
Doctors monitored the progress of each syphilitic in the infectious diseases ward.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syphilis - a sexually transmitted infection caused by the bacterium Treponema pallidum

Etymology

From syphilis (the disease) + -itic (indicating connection or relating to).
