pity

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪti / (AmE), / ˈpɪti / (BrE)
an unfortunate or regrettable situation or development; something that causes disappointment or sorrow
unfortunate or regrettable situation

Examples

Pity he missed the show; it was the best performance of the season.
It's a pity that the weather turned so bad during our trip.
What a pity that she couldn't come to the party last night.

Synset Examples

it's a pity he couldn't do it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shame - a regrettable situation or circumstance

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪti / (AmE), / ˈpɪti / (BrE)
the feeling of sorrow and compassion caused by the suffering and misfortunes of others, often accompanied by a desire to help
compassion for others' suffering

Examples

She looked at the stray dog with such pity that she decided to take it home.
He felt a deep sense of pity for the victims of the disaster.
It is not always helpful to show pity; sometimes people just want understanding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compassion - sympathetic pity and concern for others' sufferings
  • Antonym: indifference - lack of interest or concern

Etymology

From Old French pité (compassion), from Latin pietas (dutiful conduct, kindness).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɪti / (AmE), / ˈpɪti / (BrE)
A feeling of sympathy and sorrow caused by the suffering or misfortune of others.
sympathy and sorrow for others' misfortune

Examples

She felt deep pity for the child left alone in the rain.
He looked at the damaged painting with a sense of pity and regret.
It is not pity that the victims need, but support and respect.

Synset Examples

the blind are too often objects of pity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compassion - sympathetic concern for others' suffering
  • Synonym: empathy - the ability to understand and share the feelings of another
  • Antonym: indifference - lack of interest, concern, or sympathy

Etymology

From Old French *pite*, from Latin *pietas* (dutifulness, compassion).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07569430-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈpɪti / (AmE), / ˈpɪti / (BrE)
to feel or express sympathy for someone else’s suffering or misfortune
feel sympathy for someone’s suffering

Examples

She couldn't help but pity the injured bird as it struggled to fly.
I pitied him when I heard about his loss, but there was little I could do to help.
They pitied the homeless man, offering him food and warm clothing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sympathize - to feel or express compassion; to share in someone's feelings

Etymology

From Old French *pité*, from Latin *pietas* (piety, compassion).
