hard-shelled

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɑrd ˌʃɛld / (AmE), / ˈhɑːd ˌʃɛld / (BrE)
Having a hard outer shell, especially used to describe animals or plants.
having a hard outer shell

Examples

Most turtles are hard-shelled and can protect themselves from predators.
He cracked open the hard-shelled nuts with a hammer.
The coastal waters are home to many hard-shelled crabs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: soft-shelled - having a soft or less rigid shell
