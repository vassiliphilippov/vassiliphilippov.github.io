to explore

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈsplɔr / (AmE), / ɪkˈsplɔː / (BrE)
to examine or inquire into a subject, idea, or possibility in detail; to investigate thoroughly
investigate or examine in detail

Examples

The book explores the reasons behind the rise in crime rates.
We need to explore all possible solutions before making a decision.
She spent the afternoon exploring the different theories presented in the lecture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to investigate - to examine systematically or search into in detail
  • Synonym: research - to study in detail to discover new facts or information

Etymology

From Latin explorare (to investigate, search out).

2. VERB

/ ɪkˈsplɔr / (AmE), / ɪkˈsplɔː / (BrE)
to travel through or into an unfamiliar area in order to learn about it; to penetrate into or investigate something new or unknown
travel to or investigate an area

Examples

Scientists continue to explore the depths of the Amazon rainforest for new species.
The astronauts were eager to explore the surface of the moon during their mission.
She loves to explore cities on foot whenever she travels to a new country.

Synset Examples

explore unknown territory in biology

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to investigate - to examine or study closely

Etymology

From Latin explorare (to search out, investigate).

3. VERB

/ ɪkˈsplɔr / (AmE), / ɪkˈsplɔː / (BrE)
to examine an organ or part of the body for diagnostic purposes, especially during a surgical procedure
examine organs for diagnosis (medical)
medical

Examples

The surgeon needed to explore the patient's abdomen to locate the source of bleeding.
Doctors may explore the affected area surgically if non-invasive tests are inconclusive.
During the operation, the medical team carefully explored the tissues to check for signs of disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to examine - to inspect or investigate carefully
  • Synonym: to operate - to perform surgery

From Latin explorare (search out, investigate).
