bladder

1. NOUN

/ ˈblædər / (AmE), / ˈbladə / (BrE)
a hollow, flexible sac in the body or a device that can expand to hold liquid or gas, especially the organ in humans and animals that collects and stores urine.
flexible sac for holding liquid or gas

Examples

The doctor explained that the bladder stores urine before it is passed from the body.
Inflating the small bladder inside the soccer ball gives it its bounce.
A fish often uses its swim bladder to control buoyancy in the water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urinary bladder - the specific bladder organ for storing urine in humans and animals

Etymology

From Old English 'blǣdre' (bladder, blister, bubble), from Proto-Germanic *blaþrō.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblædɚ / (AmE), / ˈblædə / (BrE)
a thin, flexible sac, typically made of animal skin, rubber, or synthetic material, that can be filled with air or gas
a sac filled with air or gas

Examples

The rubber bladder inside the football keeps it inflated.
Many fish have a swim bladder that helps them control buoyancy.
The old-fashioned whoopee cushion contains a simple air bladder that makes a funny noise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balloon - a flexible bag that can be inflated with air or gas

Etymology

From Old English blǣdre (blister, bubble, bladder), related to blāwan (to blow).
