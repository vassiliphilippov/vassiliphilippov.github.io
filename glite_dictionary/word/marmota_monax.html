Marmota monax

1. NOUN

/ mɑrˈmoʊtə ˈmɑnæks / (AmE), / mɑːˈməʊtə ˈmɒnæks / (BrE)
the scientific name for the groundhog, a reddish-brown North American marmot
North American groundhog species

Examples

Scientists observed a Marmota monax emerging from its burrow at dawn.
The Marmota monax is also commonly known as the groundhog or woodchuck.
During winter, the Marmota monax hibernates underground to survive the cold temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: groundhog - North American rodent known for Groundhog Day
  • Synonym: woodchuck - Another common name for the groundhog

Etymology

From Latin; 'Marmota' refers to marmots, and 'monax' is of obscure origin, possibly from a local Native American language.
