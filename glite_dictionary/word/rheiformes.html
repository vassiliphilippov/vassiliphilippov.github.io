Rheiformes

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːəˈfɔːrmiːz / (AmE), / ˌriːɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
a small order of large, flightless birds native to South America, including the rheas, characterized by features intermediate between ostriches and emus
an order of large, flightless South American birds

Examples

The Rheiformes comprise the extant rheas and several extinct species once native to South America.
Unlike ostriches, members of the Rheiformes have three toes on each foot.
Many paleontologists study fossil records to learn about the evolution of Rheiformes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ratite - large, flightless bird with a flat breastbone

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Rhea' (the type genus) and '-formes' (Latin for 'form, shape'), used in zoological classifications.

2. NOUN

/ ˌriːɪˈfɔːrmiːz / (AmE), / ˌriːɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of large, flightless birds (ratites) native to South America, including both living and extinct species of rheas, and characterized by features intermediate between ostriches and emus.
order of large South American ratite birds (rheas)

Examples

There are two living species in the bird order Rheiformes, known as the greater and lesser rhea.
Unlike ostriches, members of Rheiformes are native to South American grasslands.
Extinct species of Rheiformes have been identified through fossil discoveries in Patagonia.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ostrich - large flightless African bird, family Struthionidae
  • Degree Lower: emu - large flightless Australian bird, family Dromaiidae
  • Synonym: rhea - large flightless bird of South America, order Rheiformes

Etymology

From Modern Latin *Rhea* (genus name) + *-formes* (having the form of).
