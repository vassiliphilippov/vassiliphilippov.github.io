demesne

1. NOUN

/ dəˈmeɪn / (AmE), / dɪˈmeɪn / (BrE)
Land attached to a manor and retained for the owner's own use, or a domain over which control is exercised.
land owned and controlled by someone

Examples

The lord kept the best fields as his demesne and let the rest to tenants.
Much of the woodland formed part of the king’s personal demesne.
After the war, the castle and its demesne passed into the hands of a new noble family.

Synset Examples

his domain extended into Europe
he made it the law of the land

Related Terms

  • Synonym: domain - an area of territory owned or controlled by a ruler or government
  • Synonym: realm - a kingdom or field or domain of activity or interest

Etymology

From Old French 'demeine', from Latin 'dominium' (ownership, property).

2. NOUN

/ dəˈmeɪn / (AmE), / dɪˈmeɪn / (BrE)
Land attached to a manor and retained for the owner's own use, rather than being leased to tenants.
land attached to a manor and kept by owner

Examples

The lord worked part of the demesne himself and leased the rest to tenants.
In medieval England, the demesne was managed directly by the landowner's agents.
The castle sat at the heart of a vast demesne stretching for miles across the countryside.

Synset Examples

the family owned a large estate on Long Island

Related Terms

  • Synonym: domain - an area of land owned or controlled by a single person or organization

Etymology

From Old French demeine, from Latin dominicum (lord's property). Related to domain.
