particular

1. NOUN

/ pərˈtɪkjələr / (AmE), / pɑːˈtɪkjʊlə / (BrE)
(Logic) A proposition that claims something about some, but not all, members of a class.
proposition about some members of a class (logic)

Examples

In syllogistic logic, a particular affirms something about only some members of the group.
The statement 'Some dogs are friendly' is a classic example of a particular.
A particular differs from a universal proposition, which refers to all members of a class.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: universal - a proposition asserting something about all members of a class in logic

Etymology

From Latin particula (a small part), diminutive of pars (part).
