Drosophyllum

1. NOUN

/ droʊˈsɒfɪləm / (AmE), / drɒˈsɒfɪləm / (BrE)
A genus of carnivorous plants native to the western Mediterranean region, known for its sticky leaves that trap insects.
genus of Mediterranean carnivorous plants

Examples

Drosophyllum is unique among carnivorous plants because it grows in dry, sandy soils.
Botanists were excited to discover a thriving population of Drosophyllum in southern Spain.
The sticky leaves of Drosophyllum help it capture and digest insects for nutrients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sundew - another genus of carnivorous plants (Drosera)

Etymology

From Greek *drosos* (dew) + *phyllon* (leaf), referring to the dew-like droplets on the leaves.

2. NOUN

/ droʊˈsɒfɪləm / (AmE), / drɒˈsɒfɪləm / (BrE)
A genus of carnivorous plants native to the western Mediterranean, containing the species Drosophyllum lusitanicum, known as the Portuguese sundew or dewy pine.
genus of Mediterranean carnivorous plants

Examples

Drosophyllum is unique among carnivorous plants for its dry habitat preference.
Botanists often study Drosophyllum to understand adaptations in Mediterranean ecosystems.
The sticky leaves of Drosophyllum help trap small insects for nourishment.

Etymology

From Greek 'drosos' (dew) + 'phyllon' (leaf), referring to the dew-like appearance of glandular secretions on its leaves.
