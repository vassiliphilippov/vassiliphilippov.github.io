phosphorus

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑs.fəˌrəs / (AmE), / ˈfɒs.fər.əs / (BrE)
a chemical element (symbol P), a highly reactive, nonmetallic element found in inorganic phosphate rocks and organic compounds in living cells
a highly reactive nonmetallic element (P)

Examples

The discovery of phosphorus revolutionized the manufacturing of matches.
Foods rich in phosphorus are important for strong bones and teeth.
White phosphorus must be handled carefully because it is highly flammable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phosphate - a salt or ester of phosphoric acid containing phosphorus
  • Degree Lower: sulfur - another reactive nonmetallic element in the same group

Etymology

From Greek phōsphoros, meaning 'light-bringer', referring to its luminous properties.
