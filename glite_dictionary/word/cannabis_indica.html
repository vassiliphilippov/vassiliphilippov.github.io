Cannabis indica

1. NOUN

/ ˌkænəbɪs ˈɪndɪkə / (AmE), / ˌkanəbɪs ˈɪndɪkə / (BrE)
A species of the cannabis plant, native to Central Asia, that is a source of bhang and hashish and is sometimes grown for its fiber.
species of cannabis plant, source of hashish

Examples

Many strains of Cannabis indica are known for their relaxing effects.
The cultivation of Cannabis indica is regulated in many countries.
Bhang is traditionally made from the leaves and flowers of Cannabis indica.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cannabis sativa - another species of the cannabis plant, source of hemp and marijuana

Etymology

From Latin; 'cannabis' meaning hemp and 'indica' meaning of India.
