analysis

1. NOUN

/ əˈnæləsɪs / (AmE), / əˈnæləsɪs / (BrE)
The process of separating something into its different parts to examine or study how they relate to each other.
examining parts of something to understand it

Examples

The scientist's analysis of the data revealed several important patterns.
Detailed analysis of the painting showed its true age and origins.
Her analysis helped the team understand the project's strengths and weaknesses.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: synthesis - the combination of parts to form a whole

Etymology

From Ancient Greek *analusis* (a breaking up), from *analuein* (to unloose).

5. NOUN

/ əˈnæləsɪs / (AmE), / əˈnæləsɪs / (BrE)
A method of literary criticism that examines and interprets the structure and elements of a piece of writing.
literary criticism by structural examination

Examples

The professor's analysis revealed hidden themes and motifs in the novel.
Her analysis of the poem focused on its use of metaphor and symbolism.
Students are asked to write an analysis of Shakespeare’s sonnets for their final assignment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interpretation - the act of explaining the meaning of something, especially in literature or art
  • Synonym: critique - a detailed analysis and assessment of something, especially a literary, philosophical, or political theory

Etymology

From Greek analysis (a breaking up, an unloosing), from ana- (up, throughout) + lysis (a loosening).
