eucalyptus

1. NOUN

/ juːˌkəˈlɪptəs / (AmE), / juːˌkəˈlɪptəs / (BrE)
A fast-growing evergreen tree native to Australia, belonging to the genus Eucalyptus, with aromatic leaves often used medicinally and as food for koalas.
an aromatic Australian evergreen tree

Examples

Several eucalyptus trees were planted in the park to provide shade and improve the air quality.
The distinctive scent of eucalyptus leaves filled the air during our walk through the forest.
Koalas feed almost exclusively on eucalyptus leaves, which are toxic to most other animals.

Etymology

From modern Latin Eucalyptus, coined from Greek 'eu-' meaning 'well' and 'kalyptos' meaning 'covered', referring to the cap covering the flower buds.

2. NOUN

/ juːˈkælɪptəs / (AmE), / juːˈkælɪptəs / (BrE)
the wood of any of various eucalyptus trees, valued as timber for construction or furniture-making
wood from a eucalyptus tree

Examples

The table was made from solid eucalyptus and had a beautiful grain.
Many outdoor decks use eucalyptus because of its durability and resistance to decay.
They chose eucalyptus flooring for its hardness and unique appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eucalyptus (tree) - any tree of the genus Eucalyptus, native to Australia

Etymology

From Greek 'eucalyptos', meaning 'well covered', referring to the bud cap that covers the flower.
