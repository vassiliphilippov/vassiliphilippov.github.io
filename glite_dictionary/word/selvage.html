selvage

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛl.vɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.vɪdʒ / (BrE)
a border made of ornamental fringe or finishing at the edge of fabric, especially at either end of an oriental carpet
border or edge of fabric, especially carpets

Examples

The selvage of the Persian rug featured intricate designs in gold and red.
She noticed that the selvage was slightly frayed after years of use.
To preserve the selvage, the restorer carefully repaired the fringe on the antique carpet.

Etymology

From Middle English, alteration of selvedge, itself from self + edge.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛl.vɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.vɪdʒ / (BrE)
the edge of a piece of fabric that is woven to prevent it from fraying or unraveling
woven edge of fabric

Examples

The fabric was cut close to the selvage to prevent unraveling.
Make sure to align the pattern with the selvage for a straighter grain.
She trimmed away the selvage before beginning her sewing project.

Etymology

From Middle English 'selvege', alteration of 'self-edge', meaning the edge of the fabric finished by the weaver.
