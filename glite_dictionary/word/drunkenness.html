drunkenness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrʌŋkənnəs / (AmE), / ˈdrʌŋkənnəs / (BrE)
The state of being regularly or habitually intoxicated with alcohol, often leading to poor health and addiction.
habitual intoxication from alcohol

Examples

His constant drunkenness began to affect his work and family life.
The film portrayed the dangers of drunkenness and its impact on health.
She lost her job due to repeated drunkenness at the workplace.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sobriety - the state of not being intoxicated; abstinence from alcohol
  • Synonym: intoxication - the state of being intoxicated, especially by alcohol

Etymology

From Old English 'druncenness', from 'druncen' (drunken).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdrʌŋkənnəs / (AmE), / ˈdrʌŋkənnɪs / (BrE)
A temporary state of being intoxicated due to excessive consumption of alcohol.
state of being intoxicated by alcohol

Examples

His drunkenness at the party was obvious to everyone.
Repeated drunkenness can have serious health consequences.
The officer arrested him for public drunkenness after the disturbance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sobriety - state of being sober, especially not affected by alcohol
  • Synonym: intoxication - the state of being intoxicated, especially by alcohol

Etymology

From Middle English dronkennesse, from Old English druncennes (drunk state).

3. NOUN

/ ˈdrʌŋkənnəs / (AmE), / ˈdrʌŋkənnɪs / (BrE)
the state or practice of consuming too much alcohol; intoxication resulting from excessive drinking
state of being drunk; intoxication

Examples

His drunkenness at the party caused quite a scene.
Repeated drunkenness can lead to serious health problems.
She regretted her drunkenness the next morning after the wedding.

Synset Examples

drink was his downfall

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sobriety - the state of being sober and not affected by alcohol
  • Synonym: intoxication - the state of being influenced by alcohol or drugs

Etymology

From Middle English 'drunkennesse', from Old English 'druncenness' (intoxication).
