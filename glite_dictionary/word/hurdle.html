hurdle

1. VERB

/ ˈhɝː.dl̩ / (AmE), / ˈhɜː.dl̩ / (BrE)
to jump over a hurdle or barrier, especially in a race
to jump over a barrier

Examples

The athlete skillfully hurdled the last barrier to win the race.
She tried to hurdle the fence but tripped and fell.
During training, he hurdles over many obstacles to improve his speed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leap - to jump forcefully or a long distance
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01971212-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɜrdl̩ / (AmE), / ˈhɜːdl̩ / (BrE)
A difficulty or problem that must be dealt with before achieving something.
obstacle to overcome

Examples

Passing the final exam was the toughest hurdle of the semester.
She overcame every hurdle on her way to starting her own business.
Securing funding proved to be the main hurdle for the new project.

Synset Examples

the last hurdle before graduation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstacle - something that blocks progress
  • Synonym: barrier - a physical or metaphorical blockade

Etymology

From Old English *hyrdel* (framework, hurdle), related to German *Hürde* (hurdle).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05699206-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɜrdl̩ / (AmE), / ˈhɜːdl̩ / (BrE)
a light, movable barrier that runners must jump over during a race
barrier jumped over in a race

Examples

The athletes lined up at the starting blocks, ready to leap over each hurdle on the track.
In the 400-meter race, clearing every hurdle smoothly is crucial for success.
He knocked down one hurdle but still managed to finish in first place.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstacle - something that blocks one's way or progress

Etymology

From Old English *hyrdel* (frame of twigs or rods).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03554769-n
Synset POS: n

/ ˈhɝː.dl̩ / (AmE), / ˈhɜː.dl̩ / (BrE)
the act of jumping over an obstacle, especially in a race or athletic event
act of jumping over an obstacle

Examples

The runner cleared each hurdle with remarkable agility during the competition.
Making it to the finals required overcoming every hurdle on the track.
Training for the 110-meter hurdle event demands both speed and precision.

Related Terms
