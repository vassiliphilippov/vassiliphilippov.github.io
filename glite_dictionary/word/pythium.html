Pythium

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪθiəm / (AmE), / ˈpɪθiəm / (BrE)
A genus of destructive root-parasitic fungi that attack the roots of plants, causing diseases such as damping-off.
genus of root-parasitic fungi

Examples

Pythium species can cause severe crop losses when environmental conditions are favorable for infection.
The nursery implemented strict hygiene to prevent the spread of Pythium among its seedlings.
Gardeners learned how to identify symptoms of Pythium root rot in their vegetable beds.

Etymology

From New Latin, created from Greek pythō meaning 'to cause to rot'.
