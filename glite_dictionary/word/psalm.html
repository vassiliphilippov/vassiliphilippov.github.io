psalm

1. NOUN

/ sɑm / (AmE), / sɑːm / (BrE)
a sacred song or hymn, especially any of those in the biblical Book of Psalms, traditionally attributed to King David
biblical song or hymn

Examples

Every morning, she reads a psalm from the Old Testament.
The choir sang a beautiful psalm during the church service.
Many people find comfort in reciting a psalm when they are anxious.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hymn - a song of praise, especially a religious one
  • Synonym: prayer - words spoken to God or a deity, especially in worship

Etymology

From Greek psalmos (song sung to a harp), via Latin psalmus.

2. VERB

/ sɑm / (AmE), / sɑːm / (BrE)
to sing or celebrate using psalms, especially in a religious context
sing or celebrate in psalms
archaic

Examples

The congregation began to psalm together as the service started.
He psalmed his gratitude with a joyful heart.
Long ago, the priests would psalm in the temple during ceremonies.

Synset Examples

He psalms the works of God

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chant - to sing or intone repetitively, often of sacred text

Etymology

From Greek psalmos, via Latin psalmus, meaning 'song sung to a harp', originally from the verb psallein 'to pluck (the harp)'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01733940-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ sɑm / (AmE), / sɑːm / (BrE)
a sacred song or hymn, especially one of those in the biblical Book of Psalms, sung or recited in praise of God or a deity
sacred hymn or song of praise

Examples

The choir sang a beautiful psalm during the Sunday service.
Many psalms express gratitude and praise towards God.
He found comfort in reciting his favorite psalm each morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hymn - a religious song of praise

Etymology

From Greek psalmos (song sung to a harp), via Latin psalmus.
