Cursorius cursor

1. NOUN

/ kɜːrˈsɔːri.əs ˈkɜːr.sər / (AmE), / kɜːˈsɔː.ri.əs ˈkɜː.sə / (BrE)
A species of bird, commonly known as the cream-colored courser, found in desert and semi-desert regions of the Old World.
desert-dwelling bird species

Examples

The Cursorius cursor is well adapted to run swiftly across sandy terrain in search of insects.
Birdwatchers in North Africa often hope to spot a Cursorius cursor during migration season.
The pale plumage of the Cursorius cursor helps it blend into its arid environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cream-colored courser - Common name for Cursorius cursor, a desert bird

Etymology

From Latin 'cursorius' (of running) and 'cursor' (runner), referring to its ground-running habits.
