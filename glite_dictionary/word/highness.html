Highness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ.nəs / (BrE)
a title used to address or refer to a royal person such as a prince or princess (used with 'Your', 'His', or 'Her')
royal title of address

Examples

Please follow Your Highness to the royal chamber.
We are honored by the presence of Her Highness at tonight's gala.
The ambassador addressed His Highness with great respect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Majesty - title used for kings, queens, and emperors
  • Synonym: Excellency - formal title for high officials or ambassadors

Etymology

From Middle English 'heynesse', from Old French 'hainesse', from 'hain' (high). Related to 'high'.
