scat

1. VERB

/ skæt / (AmE), / skæt / (BrE)
(informal) To go away quickly or suddenly; to flee.
to leave quickly; to flee
informal

Examples

As soon as the teacher entered the room, the students scatted back to their seats.
He told the kids to scat before he called their parents.
The cat scatted when it saw the dog coming.

Synset Examples

If you see this man, run!
The burglars escaped before the police showed up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to scram - to leave or go away quickly
  • Antonym: to linger - to stay in a place longer than necessary

2. NOUN

/ skæt / (AmE), / skæt / (BrE)
a style of jazz singing in which the singer improvises melodies and rhythms using nonsense syllables instead of words, often imitating instrumental sounds
jazz singing with improvised nonsense syllables

Examples

Her remarkable ability to scat added an exciting dimension to the band's performance.
In jazz, scat allows singers to improvise as if their voice were an instrument.
He practiced scat to master the vocal rhythms of classic jazz tunes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scat singing - jazz singing with improvised, wordless vocal sounds

/ skæt / (AmE), / skæt / (BrE)
(informal) To defecate outdoors, especially used of animals; or to discard or dispose of something quickly.
defecate outdoors or discard quickly (informal)
informal

Examples
