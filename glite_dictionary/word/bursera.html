Bursera

1. NOUN

/ bɜrˈsɪərə / (AmE), / bɜːˈsɪərə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical and subtropical American shrubs and trees, some of which produce timber and gum elemi, belonging to the family Burseraceae.
genus of tropical American shrubs and trees

Examples

Many species within the Bursera genus are known for their fragrant resin, commonly called elemi.
Botanists often study Bursera trees for their unique ecological roles in dry forests.
The timber of some Bursera species is used locally for carving and construction.

Etymology

From Latinized form of the name of Danish botanist Joachim Burser (1583–1639).

2. NOUN

/ bərˈsɪr.ə / (AmE), / bɜːˈsɪə.rə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical and subtropical American shrubs and trees in the family Burseraceae, some of which yield timber and gum elemi.
genus of American shrubs and trees

Examples

The wood of Bursera species is often used in local carpentry.
Several Bursera trees grow in dry tropical forests across Central America.
Gum elemi is obtained from certain Bursera species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Burseraceae - the botanical family to which Bursera belongs

Etymology

Named after Joachim Burser (1583–1649), a German botanist.
