apostrophe

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɑːstrəfi / (AmE), / əˈpɒstrəfi / (BrE)
the punctuation mark (’ or ') used to indicate the omission of one or more letters in a word, or to show possession
punctuation mark indicating omission or possession

Examples

You need to use an apostrophe in words like 'can't' to show the missing letters.
Many students forget to add an apostrophe when writing possessive nouns.
The teacher explained how an apostrophe is also used to show ownership.

Etymology

From Late Latin apostrophus, from Greek apostrophos (mark of elision), from apo- (away) + strephein (to turn).

2. NOUN

/ əˈpɑː.strə.fi / (AmE), / əˈpɒs.trə.fi / (BrE)
a rhetorical device in which a speaker addresses an absent or imaginary person, or a personified abstraction
address to an absent or imaginary person

Examples

In the play, the hero's apostrophe to fate reveals his deep despair.
The poet uses apostrophe when she cries out to the absent muse.
A common literary device, apostrophe gives voice to inanimate things or absent figures.

Etymology

From Greek apostrophē (turning away or aside).
