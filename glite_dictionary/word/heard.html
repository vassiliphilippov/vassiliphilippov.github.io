heard

1. ADJ

/ hɝd / (AmE), / hɜːd / (BrE)
perceived by means of the sense of hearing; detected by hearing.
perceived by hearing

Examples

The announcement was heard clearly by everyone in the room.
She described a distant melody heard only at night in the countryside.
A loud argument could be heard coming from the next apartment.

Synset Examples

a conversation heard through the wall

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unheard - not heard; not perceived by the ear

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'hear', from Old English *hieran*.
