psycholinguist

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkoʊˈlɪŋɡwɪst / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊˈlɪŋɡwɪst / (BrE)
a person who studies the psychological and neurobiological factors that enable humans to acquire, use, and understand language
person who studies language and psychology

Examples

After earning her doctorate, she became a leading psycholinguist at the research institute.
The psycholinguist presented findings on language development in children at the conference.
As a psycholinguist, he studies how the brain processes spoken and written words.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psycholinguistics - the study of the psychological and neurobiological factors in language
  • Degree Lower: linguist - a person skilled in or studying languages

Etymology

From psycho- (relating to the mind) and linguist (language specialist)
