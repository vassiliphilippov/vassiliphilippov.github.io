Camelina sativa

1. PROPN

/ kæməˈliːnə səˈtiːvə / (AmE), / kæməˈliːnə səˈtiːvə / (BrE)
an annual herbaceous plant of the Brassicaceae family, cultivated for its oil-rich seeds and fiber, and known as false flax
an oilseed plant, also called false flax

Examples

Camelina sativa is gaining attention as a sustainable source of plant-based oil.
Researchers are exploring the benefits of Camelina sativa for biofuel production.
Farmers in some regions have started growing Camelina sativa due to its hardy nature and economic potential.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: false flax - common name for Camelina sativa, an oilseed crop

Etymology

From Latin, genus name 'Camelina' and species 'sativa' meaning 'cultivated'.
