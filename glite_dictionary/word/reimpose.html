to reimpose

1. VERB

/ ˌriː.ɪmˈpoʊz / (AmE), / ˌriː.ɪmˈpəʊz / (BrE)
to impose something again, especially after it has previously been lifted or ended
to put in place again

Examples

After lifting the restrictions briefly, the government decided to reimpose them due to rising cases.
The company may reimpose the dress code policy after recent complaints.
If the rules are broken again, authorities will likely reimpose the ban.

Synset Examples

The fine was reimposed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to impose - to force something to be accepted or put in place

Etymology

From re- (again) + impose, from Latin imponere (to place upon).
