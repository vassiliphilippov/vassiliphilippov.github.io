aculeated

1. ADJ

/ əˈkjuːliˌeɪtɪd / (AmE), / əˈkjuːlɪeɪtɪd / (BrE)
having or resembling a stinger or barb; pointed or spiny like a needle or spine
having a stinger or barb; spiny

Examples

Some caterpillars are aculeated and use their spines for defense.
The bee is an aculeated insect, capable of stinging predators.
The plant's aculeated leaves make it difficult for animals to eat.

Synset Examples

aculeate insects such as bees and wasps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aculeate - having a sting; relating to insects with stingers

Etymology

From Latin aculeatus (provided with a sting or point), from aculeus (a sting).
