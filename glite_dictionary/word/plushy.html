plushy

1. ADJ

/ ˈplʌʃi / (AmE), / ˈplʌʃi / (BrE)
(of materials or textures) having a soft, thick, luxurious texture similar to plush fabric.
having a soft, luxurious texture

Examples

The hotel suite featured plushy carpets and velvet drapes throughout.
He bought a plushy teddy bear for his niece, knowing she loved soft toys.
The plushy seats in the new cinema made the whole experience much more comfortable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plush - a rich fabric with a thick, soft pile; soft and luxurious

Etymology

From 'plush', ultimately from French 'peluche' (a textile with a soft, rich pile).

2. ADJ

/ ˈplʌʃi / (AmE), / ˈplʌʃi / (BrE)
extravagant and luxurious, often in a way that shows great expenditure or abundance
extravagant and luxurious

Examples

The wedding was held at a plushy hotel with gold-trimmed chandeliers and marble floors.
She moved into a plushy apartment in the city center, decorated with expensive artwork.
They enjoyed a plushy dinner party with gourmet cuisine and fine wines.

Synset Examples

a lavish buffet
a lucullan feast

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lavish - sumptuously rich, elaborate, or luxurious
  • Synonym: opulent - rich and luxurious or lavish
  • Antonym: austere - severe or strict in manner, attitude, or appearance; lacking luxury

Etymology

From 'plush' (a rich fabric), with -y suffix to form an adjective.
