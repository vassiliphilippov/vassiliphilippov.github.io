kaon

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.ɑn / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.ɒn / (BrE)
An unstable subatomic particle of the meson family, produced during high-energy particle collisions.
unstable meson; subatomic particle

Examples

The physicist studied how a kaon decays into lighter particles.
Researchers observed a rare interaction involving a kaon in the particle accelerator experiment.
The properties of the kaon have provided important insights into the fundamental forces of nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meson - a subatomic particle consisting of a quark and an antiquark

Etymology

From the letter K, denoting its strangeness quantum number, plus -on (suffix for subatomic particles).
