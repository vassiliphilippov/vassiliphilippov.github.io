agrapha

1. NOUN

/ ˈæɡrəfə / (AmE), / ˈaɡrəfə / (BrE)
Sayings attributed to Jesus that are not recorded in the canonical Gospels.
sayings of Jesus outside the canonical Gospels

Examples

Scholars often debate the authenticity of the agrapha found in early Christian writings.
Some of the agrapha provide insights into teachings not included in the four Gospels.
The study of the agrapha can shed light on early Christian beliefs and traditions.

Etymology

From Greek ἄγραφα (a-grapha), plural of ἄγραφον (unwritten), from ἀ- (not) + γράφειν (to write).
