to randomize

1. VERB

/ ˈræn.dəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈræn.də.maɪz / (BrE)
to arrange or order something in a random or unpredictable way
arrange in random order

Examples

The software can randomize the list of participants for the experiment.
Teachers sometimes randomize seating arrangements to encourage interaction.
Researchers randomized the sample groups to prevent bias in the study.

Synset Examples

Randomize the order of the numbers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shuffle - to mix or rearrange in a random order
  • Antonym: order - to arrange in a particular sequence or pattern

Etymology

From 'random' + '-ize', where 'random' derives from Old French 'randir' (to gallop).
