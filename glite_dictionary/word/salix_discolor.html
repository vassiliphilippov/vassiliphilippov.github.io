Salix discolor

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlɪks ˈdɪskələr / (AmE), / ˈseɪlɪks ˈdɪskʌlə / (BrE)
A species of small willow native to eastern North America, known for its greyish leaves and silky catkins that appear before the leaves.
North American willow with silky catkins

Examples

The Salix discolor is often called the American pussy willow because of its furry catkins.
In early spring, Salix discolor produces silvery catkins before its leaves emerge.
Gardeners sometimes plant Salix discolor for its ornamental value in wetland gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pussy willow - common name for several North American willows with soft, furry catkins

Etymology

From Latin: 'Salix' meaning 'willow' and 'discolor' meaning 'of different color'.
