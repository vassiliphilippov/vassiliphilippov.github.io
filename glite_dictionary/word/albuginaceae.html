Albuginaceae

1. NOUN

/ ælˌbjudʒɪˈneɪsiː / (AmE), / ælˌbjuːdʒɪˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi that produce white, blister-like spore structures (sori) on certain flowering plants
a family of blister-producing fungi

Examples

Members of the Albuginaceae family can cause visible white blisters on the leaves of host plants.
The plant pathologist identified the fungus as belonging to Albuginaceae.
Some agricultural guides include a section on diseases caused by Albuginaceae fungi.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Albugo, from Latin albus (white).

2. NOUN

/ ælˌbjuːdʒɪˈneɪsiː / (AmE), / ælˌbjuːdʒɪˈniːsiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi that produce white, blister-like sori on certain flowering plants
a family of fungi with blister-like spores
medical

Examples

White blisters caused by fungi of the Albuginaceae family can harm many crops.
Scientists studied how Albuginaceae infects flowering plants in agricultural fields.
The presence of Albuginaceae can be identified by the characteristic white sori on leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Albugo - a genus of fungi in the family Albuginaceae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Albugo + suffix -aceae, denoting a family in taxonomy.
