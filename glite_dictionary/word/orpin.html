orpin

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrpɪn / (AmE), / ˈɔːpɪn / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to northern temperate regions, with toothed leaves and clusters of small purplish-white flowers.
perennial northern plant with purplish-white flowers

Examples

The gardener planted orpin among the succulents to add variety to the flower bed.
Clusters of orpin bloomed in the rocky patch behind the cottage in late summer.
Botanists often study orpin for its resilience in harsh northern climates.

Etymology

From Old French orpin, related to Latin auripigmentum (gold pigment), possibly referring to the yellowish color of some related species.
