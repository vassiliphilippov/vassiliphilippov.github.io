inciter

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsaɪtər / (AmE), / ɪnˈsaɪtə / (BrE)
a person who deliberately encourages others to cause trouble or commit wrongful acts
person who encourages trouble or unrest

Examples

The police arrested the inciter who convinced the crowd to block the road.
As the main inciter of the protest, she faced serious consequences.
Online forums sometimes attract inciters who spread false information and provoke arguments.

Synset Examples

she was the instigator of their quarrel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instigator - person who initiates trouble or urges others to action
  • Synonym: agitator - person who stirs up public feeling or unrest

Etymology

From the verb 'incite', from Latin incitāre (to set in motion, urge).
