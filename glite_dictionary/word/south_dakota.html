South Dakota

1. PROPN

/ saʊθ dəˈkoʊtə / (AmE), / saʊθ dəˈkəʊtə / (BrE)
a state in the north-central region of the United States, known for landmarks such as Mount Rushmore and the Badlands
a state in north-central United States

Examples

They traveled to South Dakota to visit Mount Rushmore and the Badlands National Park.
Winter in South Dakota can be very cold with heavy snowfall.
South Dakota is known for its scenic drives and Native American history.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: North Dakota - a state directly north of South Dakota, also named for the Dakota Sioux

Etymology

Named after the Dakota Sioux people; 'south' distinguishes it from North Dakota.
