Menotyphla

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛn.əˈtɪf.lə / (AmE), / ˌmɛn.əˈtɪf.lə / (BrE)
a former order of mammals that included elephant shrews and tree shrews, now obsolete in taxonomy
former mammal order (obsolete)

Examples

The order Menotyphla once grouped elephant shrews and tree shrews together.
Recent studies have shown that Menotyphla is not a natural grouping of species.
Few biologists today use the term Menotyphla in mammalian classification.

Etymology

From Greek 'meno-' (remaining) and 'typhlos' (blind), used in scientific classification.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛnoʊˈtaɪflə / (AmE), / ˌmɛnəʊˈtɪflə / (BrE)
a former suborder of mammals including the elephant shrews and tree shrews, now split into different groups in modern classifications
former suborder of mammals (elephant shrews, tree shrews)
medical

Examples

The classification of Menotyphla has undergone significant changes due to advances in molecular biology.
Scientists once placed both tree shrews and elephant shrews in the suborder Menotyphla.
Recent research suggests the animals previously grouped under Menotyphla are not as closely related as once thought.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: 'meno-' (possibly from Greek 'menos', spirit, force) + 'typhla', from Greek 'typhlos' (blind), used in scientific taxonomy.
