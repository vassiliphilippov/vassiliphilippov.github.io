to devaluate

1. VERB

/ diˈvæl.juˌeɪt / (AmE), / diˈvæl.ju.eɪt / (BrE)
to reduce or remove the value of something, especially the official value of a currency in relation to other currencies
reduce value, esp. currency

Examples

Following the crisis, the government decided to devaluate the national currency to boost exports.
She warned that the constant criticism could devaluate the hard work of the team.
An influx of counterfeit products might devaluate the brand's reputation over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to devalue - reduce the value of a currency or asset

Etymology

From Latin 'de-' (down) + 'valere' (to be worth).

2. VERB

/ ˌdiːˈvæljueɪt / (AmE), / ˌdiːˈvæljueɪt / (BrE)
(of money or currency) to decrease in value, especially in relation to foreign currencies, often by governmental action or due to economic factors.
to decrease in value (esp. currency)

Examples

The new policy caused the franc to devaluate sharply against the euro.
If the company’s shares devaluate further, investors may lose confidence.
Many countries fear that their currency might devaluate due to global market instability.

Synset Examples

The dollar depreciated again

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depreciate - to decrease in value, especially over time or due to economic factors
  • Antonym: to appreciate - to increase in value over time or due to economic factors
  • Synonym: to devalue - to lower the value of (esp. currency or assets)

Etymology

From Italian *devalutare*, from Latin *de-* (down) + *valere* (to be worth). Influenced by 'evaluate' and 'devalue.'
