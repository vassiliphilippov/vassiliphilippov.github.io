Aquitania

1. PROPN

/ ˌækwɪˈteɪniə / (AmE), / ˌakwɪˈteɪnɪə / (BrE)
a historical region in southwestern France, located between Bordeaux and the Pyrenees mountains
historical region in southwestern France

Examples

The Romans conquered Aquitania in the first century BC.
Many ancient artifacts from Aquitania are displayed in French museums.
Caesar described the culture and people of Aquitania in his Commentaries.

Etymology

From Latin Aquitania, referring to the territory of the ancient Aquitani people.
