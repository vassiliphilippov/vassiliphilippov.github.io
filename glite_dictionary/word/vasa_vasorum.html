vasa vasorum

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɑːsə vəˈsɔːrəm / (AmE), / ˌveɪsə vəˈsɔːrəm / (BrE)
small blood vessels that supply the walls of larger arteries or veins
small vessels supplying large arteries or veins
medical

Examples

The vasa vasorum play a crucial role in nourishing the outer layer of large arteries.
Inflammation of the vasa vasorum can contribute to vascular diseases such as atherosclerosis.
Researchers studied the distribution of the vasa vasorum in the aorta using advanced imaging techniques.

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'vessels of the vessels'.
