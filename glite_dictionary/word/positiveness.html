positiveness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːzətɪvnəs / (AmE), / ˈpɒzətɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being definite, unquestionable, or not open to dispute
quality of being definite or unquestionable

Examples

The positiveness of the evidence left no room for doubt at the trial.
Her statement was marked by a positiveness that silenced all objections.
The positiveness of scientific laws makes them widely accepted in the academic community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certainty - the quality of being reliably true or sure
  • Antonym: doubtfulness - the quality of being uncertain or questionable

Etymology

From 'positive' + '-ness'; ultimately from Latin positivus (settled by agreement).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑzətɪvnəs / (AmE), / ˈpɒzətɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being encouraging, optimistic, or likely to lead to success
encouraging or optimistic quality

Examples

Her positiveness inspired the whole team to believe they could win the championship.
Even in challenging times, his positiveness kept everyone motivated and hopeful.
The positiveness of the feedback encouraged us to continue with the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: positivity - the practice of being positive or optimistic in attitude
  • Antonym: negativeness - the quality of being pessimistic or discouraging

Etymology

From positive + -ness, dating from the late 16th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɑz.ɪ.tɪv.nəs / (AmE), / ˈpɒz.ɪ.tɪv.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being certain, accepting, affirming, or assertively confident
certainty or assertive confidence

Examples

Her positiveness in discussions often persuaded others to follow her ideas.
He spoke with such positiveness that no one questioned his decision.
The positiveness of her response reassured everyone in the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certainty - the quality of being reliably true or confident
  • Synonym: confidence - the feeling or belief that one can rely on something or someone
  • Antonym: uncertainty - the state of being unsure or having doubt

Etymology

From 'positive' + '-ness'; ultimately from Latin 'positivus' (settled by agreement, positive).
