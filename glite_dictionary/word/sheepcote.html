sheepcote

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃiːpˌkoʊt / (AmE), / ˈʃiːpkəʊt / (BrE)
a small enclosure or shelter for sheep
enclosure or shelter for sheep
archaic

Examples

The shepherd built a sturdy sheepcote to protect his flock from predators.
At night, the sheep were rounded up and brought safely into the sheepcote.
The old stone sheepcote stood at the edge of the meadow, unused for years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheepfold - an enclosure for sheep
  • Synonym: pen - an enclosure for confining animals

Etymology

From Old English 'scēapcote', formed from 'scēap' (sheep) and 'cote' (cottage or shelter).
