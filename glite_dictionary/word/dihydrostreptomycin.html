dihydrostreptomycin

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌhaɪdroʊˌstrɛptoʊˈmaɪsɪn / (AmE), / daɪˌhaɪdrəʊˌstrɛptəʊˈmaɪsɪn / (BrE)
an antibiotic that is a hydrogenated derivative of streptomycin, used to treat tuberculosis, tularemia, and some Gram-negative bacterial infections
hydrogenated antibiotic related to streptomycin
medical

Examples

Dihydrostreptomycin was once widely used in the treatment of tuberculosis before drug resistance became common.
Doctors sometimes prescribed dihydrostreptomycin for infections caused by Gram-negative bacteria.
The use of dihydrostreptomycin has declined due to its side effects and the emergence of more effective antibiotics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: streptomycin - an antibiotic derived from Streptomyces griseus

Etymology

From dihydro- (containing two additional hydrogen atoms) + streptomycin (antibiotic substance).
