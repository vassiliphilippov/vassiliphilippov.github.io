selachian

1. NOUN

/ səˈleɪkiən / (AmE), / sɪˈlækɪən / (BrE)
Any of various fishes of the class Chondrichthyes, including sharks, rays, and skates, characterized by a cartilaginous skeleton and placoid scales.
cartilaginous fish: sharks, rays, skates

Examples

Many selachian species, such as sharks and rays, play a crucial role in ocean ecosystems.
The fossil record shows that selachians have existed for over 400 million years.
Unlike bony fishes, selachians have skeletons made almost entirely of cartilage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cartilaginous fish - fish with a skeleton made primarily of cartilage
  • Antonym: bony fish - fish with a skeleton made mainly of bone (Osteichthyes)

Etymology

From Greek selachos (cartilaginous fish, shark) + -ian.
