Eddington

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛdɪŋtən / (AmE), / ˈɛdɪŋtən / (BrE)
(Sir Arthur Stanley) Eddington (1882–1944), English astronomer and physicist noted for his explanation of the theory of relativity and studies in astrophysics.
English astronomer and physicist (1882–1944)

Examples

Eddington played a major role in promoting Einstein's theory of relativity to the English-speaking world.
Many physicists regard Eddington's work as foundational for modern astrophysics.
As a scientific writer, Eddington was known for making complex ideas accessible to the general public.
