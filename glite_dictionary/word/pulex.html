Pulex

1. NOUN

/ ˈpjuːlɛks / (AmE), / ˈpjuːlɛks / (BrE)
A genus of fleas, including several species that are parasites of mammals, such as the human flea.
genus of fleas

Examples

The species Pulex irritans is commonly known as the human flea.
Researchers collected specimens from the genus Pulex during the field study.
Pulex fleas can act as vectors for certain diseases affecting mammals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flea - small wingless jumping insect that feeds on the blood of mammals and birds

Etymology

From Latin *pulex* (flea).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpjuːlɛks / (AmE), / ˈpjuːlɛks / (BrE)
A genus of fleas in the family Pulicidae, which includes the human flea and other related species.
genus of fleas (Pulicidae)

Examples

The species Pulex irritans is commonly known as the human flea.
Zoologists identified several Pulex specimens during their survey of local wildlife.
Outbreaks of flea-borne diseases can sometimes be traced to fleas of the genus Pulex.

Etymology

From Latin *pulex* (flea). Used as the genus name in scientific classification.
