part-time

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɑrt ˌtaɪm / (AmE), / ˈpɑːt ˌtaɪm / (BrE)
working or involving fewer hours than is usual or customary; not full time.
involving less than standard hours

Examples

She only works part-time while her children are in school.
Many students take part-time jobs to support themselves during university.
I'm looking for a part-time position with flexible hours.

Synset Examples

part-time employees
a part-time job

Related Terms

  • Antonym: full-time - working or involving the full number of hours considered normal or standard

3. ADV

/ ˈpɑrtˌtaɪm / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtˌtaɪm / (BrE)
for less than the usual or standard number of hours; not full-time, especially referring to work or study
for fewer than standard hours; not full-time

Examples

She decided to work part-time while finishing her degree.
Many students prefer part-time jobs to have more free time.
He was employed part-time at the supermarket last summer.

Synset Examples

he works part-time

Related Terms

  • Antonym: full-time - working for the whole of the usual or standard working hours

Etymology

from 'part' + 'time', first used in the early 20th century to describe employment for fewer hours than full time
