prolapse

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈlæps / (AmE), / prəʊˈlæps / (BrE)
a condition in which an organ slips or falls out of its normal position, especially within the body
slipping or displacement of an organ
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with a uterine prolapse after experiencing pelvic discomfort.
A rectal prolapse can cause pain and require surgical intervention.
Doctors discussed the risk factors that could lead to an organ prolapse in older adults.

Etymology

From Latin prolabere (to fall forward), from pro- (forward) + labi (to slip).

2. VERB

/ proʊˈlæps / (AmE), / prəˈlæps / (BrE)
(of a body part or organ) to slip or fall out of its normal position, especially through the opening it normally occupies.
(of a body part) slip out of place
medical

Examples

If the vaginal walls prolapse, they may protrude outside the body.
The surgeon explained how a disc in the spine can prolapse and cause pain.
After childbirth, some women develop a prolapsed uterus, requiring medical attention.

Synset Examples

prolapsed rectum

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dislocate - to disturb the normal position of a body part, often a joint

Etymology

From Latin prolapsus, past participle of prolabi 'to glide forward, to fall out.'
