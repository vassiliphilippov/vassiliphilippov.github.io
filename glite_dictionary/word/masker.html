masker

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæskər / (AmE), / ˈmɑːskə / (BrE)
a person who takes part in a masquerade, typically by wearing a mask or costume
person at a masquerade

Examples

Many maskers at the ball wore elaborate costumes that concealed their identities.
Each masker danced with a different partner throughout the evening.
The grand entrance of the masker drew the attention of everyone at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: masquerader - a person who takes part in a masquerade by wearing a disguise

Etymology

From Middle French *masqueur* (one who wears a mask), derived from *masque* (mask).
