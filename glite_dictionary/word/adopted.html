adopted

1. ADJ

/ əˈdɑptɪd / (AmE), / əˈdɒptɪd / (BrE)
(of a place, country, or culture) acquired and treated as one's own, often by choice rather than by birth.
acquired and treated as one's own (of place or culture)

Examples

She calls Paris her adopted city despite being born in Rome.
After moving to Canada, he finally felt at home in his adopted country.
The author often wrote about her experiences in her adopted state.

Synset Examples

my adopted state
an adoptive country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adoptive - relating to adoption, especially as a parent

Etymology

Past participle of 'adopt', from Latin 'adoptare' (to choose for oneself).
