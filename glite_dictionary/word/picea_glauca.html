Picea glauca

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪ.si.ə ˈɡloʊ.kə / (AmE), / ˌpiː.si.ə ˈɡlaʊ.kə / (BrE)
A species of medium-sized spruce tree native to northeastern North America, having short blue-green needles and slender cones; commonly known as the white spruce.
species of North American spruce tree

Examples

The Picea glauca in their backyard provides shelter for many birds year-round.
Foresters often plant Picea glauca to reforest areas in northern climates.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Picea glauca in varying soil conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white spruce - common name for Picea glauca, a North American spruce species

Etymology

From Latin: 'Picea' meaning 'spruce', and 'glauca' meaning 'bluish-grey'.
