jar

1. VERB

/ dʒɑr / (AmE), / dʒɑː / (BrE)
to be incompatible or to come into conflict; to have a harsh or discordant effect
be incompatible or in conflict

Examples

The modern design elements jar with the old architecture of the house.
Her loud voice can sometimes jar on people who prefer quiet.
The brightly colored curtains jar with the rest of the room's decor.

Synset Examples

These colors clash

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clash - to be in conflict or not go well together

3. NOUN

/ dʒɑɹ / (AmE), / dʒɑː / (BrE)
a sudden harsh or jarring impact or shock
sudden jarring impact

Examples

The train came to a halt with a jar that made everyone stumble.
She felt a sudden jar as the elevator stopped abruptly.
Every jar of the rough road rattled our teeth.

Synset Examples

the door closed with a jolt
all the jars and jolts were smoothed out by the shock absorbers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jolt - a sudden strong movement or shock
  • Synonym: shock - a sudden upsetting or jarring event or impact

Etymology

Perhaps imitative of the sound of a violent impact; related to earlier French 'jarrer' (to grumble, jar).

6. VERB

/ dʒɑr / (AmE), / dʒɑː / (BrE)
to have an unpleasant or disturbing effect; to shock, unsettle, or irritate, especially in a sensory or emotional way
to disturb or shock unpleasantly

Examples

The loud crash jarred everyone in the quiet library.
His dismissive comments jarred the listeners, making them feel uneasy.
The abrupt change in music style jarred the mood of the entire evening.

Synset Examples

This play jarred the audience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disturb - to interrupt the normal state or activity; to unsettle
  • Antonym: to soothe - to make calm or relieve discomfort

Etymology

Perhaps from Old French *jarrer* (to grumble or quarrel) or from Middle English *jarre* (dispute, quarrel), related to the sound of striking or clashing.

Source: WordNet
