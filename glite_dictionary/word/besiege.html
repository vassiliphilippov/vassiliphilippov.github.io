to besiege

1. VERB

/ bɪˈsidʒ / (AmE), / bɪˈsiːdʒ / (BrE)
to harass someone with a large number of requests, questions, or demands; to overwhelm with persistent attention
overwhelm with demands or attention

Examples

Reporters besieged the politician as he left the courthouse, firing questions from every direction.
Since announcing her new book, fans have besieged her with letters and emails every day.
After the match, autograph seekers besieged the football players outside the stadium.

Synset Examples

The press photographers besieged the movie star

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bombard - attack someone with persistent requests or questions
  • Synonym: to pester - repeatedly annoy someone with requests or demands

Etymology

From Old French *seoir* (to sit) and *besieger* (to sit down before; to lay siege to), from Latin *sedere* (to sit).

2. VERB

/ bɪˈsidʒ / (AmE), / bɪˈsiːdʒ / (BrE)
to surround a place with armed forces in order to capture it or force those inside to surrender
surround with armed forces to force surrender

Examples

The army decided to besiege the fortress until the defenders surrendered.
During the war, several cities were besieged for months at a time.
The rebels are besieging the capital from all sides, cutting off supplies.

Synset Examples

The Turks besieged Vienna

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to surround - to encircle or enclose completely
  • Synonym: storm - to attack suddenly with great force, especially to capture a fortified place
  • Antonym: to relieve - to free a place or person from siege, distress, or difficulty

Etymology

From Old French 'seoir' (to seat) via 'sege', meaning a siege, from Latin 'sedere' (to sit).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ bɪˈsidʒ / (AmE), / bɪˈsiːdʒ / (BrE)
to overwhelm (someone) with persistent requests, worries, or problems; to cause someone to feel stressed by constant attention or pressure
to overwhelm with requests, worries, or attention

Examples

After her promotion, she was besieged with requests for advice from her colleagues.
He felt besieged by doubts as the deadline approached.
Journalists besieged the actor with questions about his new film.

Synset Examples

She was besieged by so many problems that she got discouraged

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overwhelm - to give someone too much of a thing to deal with
  • Synonym: to harass - to subject someone to aggressive pressure or intimidation
  • Antonym: to reassure - to say or do something to remove someone's doubts or fears

Etymology

From Old French 'seoir' (to sit) via Middle English, influenced by the military sense of surrounding; figurative use developed later.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
