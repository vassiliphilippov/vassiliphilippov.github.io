Erodium texanum

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈroʊdiəm tɛkˈsænəm / (AmE), / ɪˈrəʊdiəm tɛkˈsænəm / (BrE)
A plant species of prairies and desert regions in the southwestern United States and Mexico, commonly known as Texas stork's bill.
plant native to southwestern US and Mexico

Examples

Erodium texanum thrives in the dry, sandy soils of the Chihuahuan desert.
Researchers observed a blooming Erodium texanum near the border of Texas and New Mexico.
Many pollinators are attracted to the purple flowers of Erodium texanum during springtime.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'erodios' (heron), referring to fruit shape; species named for Texas.
