temperamentally

1. ADV

/ ˌtɛmpərəˈmɛntəli / (AmE), / ˌtɛmpərəˈmɛntəli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to a person's temperament or natural disposition.
in relation to temperament

Examples

Despite being temperamentally different, the siblings managed to get along well.
She is temperamentally suited to a fast-paced working environment.
He found himself temperamentally incompatible with jobs that required little social interaction.

Synset Examples

temperamentally suited to each other

Etymology

From temperament + -ally, forming the adverb from the adjective temperamental.
