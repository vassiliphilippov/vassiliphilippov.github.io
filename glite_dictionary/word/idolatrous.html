idolatrous

1. ADJ

/ aɪˈdɑːlətrəs / (AmE), / aɪˈdɒlətrəs / (BrE)
Relating to or practicing idolatry; showing excessive devotion to idols or images.
relating to idolatry

Examples

The ancient tribe was known for its idolatrous ceremonies and sacred images.
Many reformers denounced the practice as idolatrous and sought to abolish it.
Critics described the luxurious rituals of the palace as dangerously idolatrous.

Synset Examples

idolatrous worship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idolatrize - to worship idols; to practice idolatry
  • Antonym: iconoclastic - opposing or attacking traditional religious beliefs, especially image worship

Etymology

From Late Latin idolatrous, from Greek eidōlolatrēs (idol worshipper).

2. ADJ

/ aɪˈdɑːlətrəs / (AmE), / aɪˈdɒlətrəs / (BrE)
showing blind or excessive devotion or adoration, often in a religious or figurative sense
showing blind or excessive devotion

Examples

The crowd gave an idolatrous reception to the pop singer, screaming and waving in adoration.
Some critics accuse fans of having an idolatrous attachment to celebrity culture.
He warned against idolatrous worship of material possessions, urging a simpler way of life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devoted - very loving or loyal
  • Antonym: iconoclastic - attacking or rejecting cherished beliefs, especially religious ones

Etymology

From Late Latin idolatrosus (full of idolatry), related to Latin idolum (idol).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
