Himantoglossum

1. NOUN

/ hɪˌmæntəˈɡlɒsəm / (AmE), / hɪˌmantəˈɡlɒsəm / (BrE)
A small genus of terrestrial orchids native to Europe and the Mediterranean region.
genus of terrestrial orchids

Examples

Several species within the Himantoglossum genus display striking, long-lipped flowers.
The lizard orchid, Himantoglossum hircinum, is rare in the United Kingdom.
Botanists studied the pollination mechanisms unique to Himantoglossum orchids.

Etymology

From Greek 'himantos' (strap, thong) + 'glossa' (tongue), referring to the strap-like shape of the orchid’s lip.

2. NOUN

/ hɪˌmæntəˈɡlɑsəm / (AmE), / hɪˌmantəˈɡlɒsəm / (BrE)
A genus of terrestrial orchids native to Europe and the Mediterranean region, known for their distinctive, often fringed flowers.
genus of European/Mediterranean orchids

Examples

The rare Himantoglossum orchids can be found in limestone meadows throughout southern Europe.
Botanists were excited to discover a new species within the genus Himantoglossum.
Many gardeners admire the unique blooms of Himantoglossum for their intricate shapes and vibrant colors.

Etymology

From Greek 'himant-' (strap) and 'glossa' (tongue), referring to the shape of the orchid's lip.
