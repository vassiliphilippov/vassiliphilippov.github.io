astrodynamics

1. NOUN

/ ˌæstrəʊdaɪˈnæmɪks / (AmE), / ˌæstrəʊdaɪˈnæmɪks / (BrE)
the branch of astronomy and engineering that studies the motion of natural and artificial bodies in space, such as planets, satellites, and spacecraft.
study of space object motion

Examples

Graduate students specializing in astrodynamics often calculate satellite orbits for space missions.
As a field, astrodynamics has advanced rapidly thanks to improved computational methods.
Many breakthroughs in orbital prediction have come from research in astrodynamics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celestial mechanics - the branch of astronomy dealing with the motions of celestial objects

Etymology

From astro- (relating to stars or space) + dynamics (study of forces and motion), modeled after similar physical sciences.
