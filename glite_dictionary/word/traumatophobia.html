traumatophobia

1. NOUN

/ trɔmətoʊˈfoʊbiə / (AmE), / trɔːmətəˈfəʊbɪə / (BrE)
an abnormal or morbid fear of battle or physical injury
irrational fear of injury or battle
medical

Examples

Her traumatophobia made it impossible for her to watch action movies with violent scenes.
People with traumatophobia may avoid situations where injuries are likely to occur.
His traumatophobia intensified after he witnessed a serious accident as a child.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phobia - an extreme or irrational fear of or aversion to something
  • Degree Lower: hemophobia - abnormal fear of blood

Etymology

From Greek 'trauma' (wound, injury) + '-phobia' (fear).
