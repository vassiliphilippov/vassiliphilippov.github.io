cybernetics

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪbərˈnɛtɪks / (AmE), / ˌsaɪbəˈnɛtɪks / (BrE)
the scientific study of communication and control processes in biological and artificial systems, especially comparing how information flows in both.
study of control & communication in systems

Examples

Many researchers in cybernetics focus on how feedback mechanisms operate within complex systems.
The principles of cybernetics have influenced both neuroscience and artificial intelligence.
Her interest in cybernetics led her to pursue a career in robotic engineering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: systems theory - interdisciplinary study of systems in science, nature, and society
  • Synonym: robotics - technology dealing with the design and application of robots

Etymology

From Greek 'kybernētēs' (steersman, governor), via French 'cybernétique'.
