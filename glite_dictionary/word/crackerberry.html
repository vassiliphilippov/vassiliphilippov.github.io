crackerberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrækɚˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈkrækəˌbɛri / (BrE)
A creeping perennial herb of northern regions, distinguished by its red berries and whorled leaves at shoot tips; also known as bunchberry (Cornus canadensis).
creeping northern perennial plant with red berries

Examples

Wild crackerberry grows in the shady woods of northern Canada.
Children picked the bright red crackerberry fruit along the forest path.
The leaves of the crackerberry form whorls at the tips of its creeping stems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bunchberry - another common name for Cornus canadensis, the same plant
