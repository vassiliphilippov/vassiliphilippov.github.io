pedunculate

1. ADJ

/ pɪˈdʌŋkjʊlət / (AmE), / pɪˈdʌŋkjʊlət / (BrE)
(of a plant or organism) having or growing on a peduncle or stalk.
having or growing on a stalk

Examples

The pedunculate flower stood out from the stem, unlike the sessile ones next to it.
Certain barnacles are pedunculate, meaning they attach to surfaces by a stalk.
Many oak trees in Europe are called pedunculate oaks due to their long stalks on the acorns.

Synset Examples

a pedunculate flower
a pedunculate barnacle is attached to the substrate by a fleshy foot or stalk

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sessile - attached directly by the base without a stalk

Etymology

From Latin pedunculatus, from 'pedunculus' (small stalk).
