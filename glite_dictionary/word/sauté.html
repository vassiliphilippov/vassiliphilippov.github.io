sauté

1. ADJ

/ soʊˈteɪ / (AmE), / sɔːˈteɪ / (BrE)
Fried quickly in a little fat, typically in a pan over high heat.
fried quickly in a little fat

Examples

The vegetables were sauté to bring out their natural flavors.
She served a sauté dish of mushrooms and onions alongside the steak.
The chef recommended a quick sauté to preserve the texture of the shrimp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fry - to cook food in hot fat or oil

Etymology

From French 'sauté', past participle of 'sauter' (to jump), referring to the tossing motion used in cooking.
