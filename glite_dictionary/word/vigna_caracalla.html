Vigna caracalla

1. NOUN

/ ˈviːɡnə kəˈrækələ / (AmE), / ˈvɪɡnə kəˈrækələ / (BrE)
a perennial tropical American vine cultivated for its clusters of showy yellow and purple flowers, with coiled corolla keels resembling snail shells; also known as the corkscrew vine
a tropical vine with spiral coiled flowers

Examples

The unique spirals of the Vigna caracalla flowers make it a favorite in ornamental gardens.
Gardeners appreciate the fragrance and unusual blooms of Vigna caracalla.
A trellis covered in Vigna caracalla is stunning during its flowering season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corkscrew vine - another name for Vigna caracalla, a vine with spiral flowers
  • Synonym: Phaseolus - a genus sometimes including Vigna caracalla, known for beans

Etymology

From Latin; Vigna after Domenico Vigna, Italian botanist, and caracalla meaning 'snail-shaped'.
