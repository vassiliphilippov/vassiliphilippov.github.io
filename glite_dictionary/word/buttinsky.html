buttinsky

1. NOUN

/ bʌˈtɪnski / (AmE), / bʌˈtɪnski / (BrE)
an informal term for a person who intrudes or meddles in other people's affairs, especially uninvited
someone who interferes; a meddler
informal american

Examples

Everyone wishes that buttinsky would mind her own business for once.
There's always a buttinsky at work who has to comment on everything.
He acted like a real buttinsky during the meeting, interrupting everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meddler - a person who interferes in others' affairs
  • Synonym: interloper - someone who becomes involved where they are not wanted

Etymology

Probably from 'butt in' (to intrude) + surname ending '-sky', originally US slang.
