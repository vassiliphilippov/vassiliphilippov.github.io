microscopy

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈkrɑːskəpi / (AmE), / mɪˈkrɒskəpi / (BrE)
the scientific study or technique of using microscopes to observe objects too small to be seen by the naked eye
use of microscopes for scientific study

Examples

The advancement of microscopy has allowed scientists to observe cells in unprecedented detail.
Confocal microscopy is widely used in biological research for imaging living tissues.
Her interest in microscopy began when she first looked at plant cells under a microscope.

Etymology

From New Latin *microscopium* (microscope) + -y (forming nouns denoting actions or processes).
