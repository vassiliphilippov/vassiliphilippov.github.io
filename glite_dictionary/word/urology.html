urology

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈrɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / jʊəˈrɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of medicine concerned with the diagnosis and treatment of diseases and disorders of the urinary tract and the male reproductive system
medical study of urinary tract and male reproductive system
medical

Examples

She decided to specialize in urology after completing her medical degree.
Advances in urology have greatly improved the treatment of kidney stones.
The hospital opened a new center devoted to urology and men's health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nephrology - the branch of medicine dealing with the kidneys

Etymology

From French urologie, from Greek ouron (urine) + -logy (study).
