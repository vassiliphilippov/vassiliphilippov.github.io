to despoil

1. VERB

/ dɪˈspɔɪl / (AmE), / dɪˈspɔɪl / (BrE)
to steal or violently remove valuable possessions from a place or person; to plunder or strip of value
to plunder or rob of valuables

Examples

The invading army despoiled the ancient temples, leaving them empty and ruined.
Over the years, illegal loggers have despoiled the forest of its rare trees.
Colonial powers often despoiled conquered lands of their natural resources.

Synset Examples

The soldiers raped the beautiful country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plunder - to steal goods using force, especially in war
  • Synonym: to rob - to take property unlawfully from a person or place by force or threat
  • Antonym: to restore - to return something to its original condition or owner

Etymology

From Old French *despoiller*, from Latin *despoliare* (to strip, plunder).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈspɔɪl / (AmE), / dɪˈspɔɪl / (BrE)
to steal goods or valuables from a place or person, typically using force and leaving destruction
steal property by force, plunder

Examples

During the war, invaders despoiled the villages, taking everything of value.
Ancient tombs were often despoiled by treasure hunters seeking gold and jewels.
The conquering army continued to despoil the land as they moved south.

Synset Examples

During the earthquake people looted the stores that were deserted by their owners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plunder - steal goods, typically with force, during war or civil disorder
  • Synonym: loot - steal goods from a place, typically during a war or riot
  • Antonym: to restore - to bring back or return something to its original condition

Etymology

From Old French *despoillier*, from Latin *despoliare* (to strip, plunder).
