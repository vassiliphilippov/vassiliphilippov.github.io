tiyin

1. NOUN

/ ˈtiːjɪn / (AmE), / ˈtiːjɪn / (BrE)
a monetary unit of Kazakhstan, equal to one hundredth of a tenge
Kazakh monetary unit; 1/100 of a tenge

Examples

The price of bread increased by several tiyins last month.
They found a few old tiyins in the market among the coins.
One tiyin is the smallest currency unit in Kazakhstan.

Etymology

From Uzbek *tiyin* (a small coin), also used in similar forms in other Turkic languages.

2. NOUN

/ tiːˈjin / (AmE), / tiːˈjiːn / (BrE)
a monetary subunit of the som, equal to one hundredth of a som, formerly used in Uzbekistan
monetary subunit of the Uzbek som

Examples

The shopkeeper refused to accept payment in tiyin because the coins are rarely used now.
One tiyin is worth one hundredth of a som, the currency of Uzbekistan.
She still keeps a collection of old Uzbek tiyins from her travels.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: som - the currency of Uzbekistan

Etymology

From Uzbek 'tiyin', meaning 'penny' or 'cent'.
