Boehmeria nivea

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈhɛməriə nɪˈviə / (AmE), / bəʊˈhɛməriə nɪˈviːə / (BrE)
a tall perennial plant of tropical Asia with dark green leaves, cultivated for the strong, silky fiber obtained from its woody stems
Asian plant yielding a flax-like fiber

Examples

The fiber extracted from Boehmeria nivea is valued for its strength and durability in textiles.
Farmers in Southeast Asia cultivate Boehmeria nivea for its high-quality bast fiber.
Textiles made from Boehmeria nivea are prized for their silky luster and resistance to mildew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ramie - strong natural fiber from the Boehmeria nivea plant

Etymology

From the genus name 'Boehmeria', after botanist Georg Rudolf Boehmer, and Latin 'nivea' meaning 'snowy'.
