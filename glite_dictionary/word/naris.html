naris

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛrɪs / (AmE), / ˈnɑːrɪs / (BrE)
Either of the external openings of the nose that allow air to enter the nasal cavities.
nostril; opening of the nose
medical

Examples

Each naris is responsible for drawing air into the nasal cavity during breathing.
The doctor examined the patient's left naris for any signs of infection.
In birds, the naris can take various shapes depending on the species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nostril - either of the two external openings of the nose

Etymology

From Latin 'naris', meaning 'nostril'.
