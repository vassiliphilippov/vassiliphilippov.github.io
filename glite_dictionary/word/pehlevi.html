Pehlevi

1. NOUN

/ pɛˈlɛvi / (AmE), / pɛˈlɛvi / (BrE)
the Middle Iranian language in which Zoroastrian literature was composed during the 3rd to 10th centuries CE
Middle Iranian language of Zoroastrian texts

Examples

Many ancient manuscripts of Zoroastrian faith were written in Pehlevi.
Researchers often rely on knowledge of Pehlevi to interpret historical Iranian texts.
Pehlevi was spoken and used in literature during the Sasanian Empire.

Etymology

From Persian 'Pahlavī', referring to Parthian or Middle Iranian origin.
