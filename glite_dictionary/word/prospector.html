prospector

1. NOUN

/ prəˈspɛktər / (AmE), / prəˈspɛktə / (BrE)
a person who explores an area in search of mineral deposits such as gold, oil, or other valuable resources
person searching for mineral deposits

Examples

The prospector spent months in the mountains searching for gold.
During the gold rush, many prospectors arrived hoping to make a fortune.
A lone prospector set up camp near the river, convinced he would strike it rich.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miner - a person who works underground mining minerals

Etymology

From Latin 'prospectare' meaning 'to look out for'.
