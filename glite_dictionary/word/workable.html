workable

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɜrkəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be done or put into practice successfully with the means or conditions available
capable of being done or put into practice

Examples

The committee is searching for a workable solution to the housing crisis.
This plan isn't perfect, but it's workable given our current budget constraints.
We need to develop a workable schedule that fits everyone's needs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: practicable - able to be done or put into practice successfully
  • Synonym: feasible - possible to do easily or conveniently
  • Antonym: impracticable - not able to be carried out or put into practice

Etymology

From 'work' + '-able', meaning capable of being worked.
