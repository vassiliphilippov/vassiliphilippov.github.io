enthronization

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˌθroʊnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌθrəʊnʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the formal ceremony of installing a new monarch on the throne
ceremony of installing a new monarch

Examples

The enthronization of the new king was attended by leaders from around the world.
Preparations for the enthronization ceremony began months in advance to ensure everything was perfect.
The cathedral was beautifully decorated for the enthronization of the monarch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coronation - the act or ceremony of crowning a monarch

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'enthronizatio', from 'enthronizare' (to enthrone), based on Greek 'en-' (in) + 'thronos' (throne).
