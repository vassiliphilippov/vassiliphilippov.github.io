to absquatulate

1. VERB

/ æbˈskwɑːtʃəˌleɪt / (AmE), / æbˈskwɒtjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to leave hurriedly or secretly, often taking something or someone along; to run away or decamp
leave hurriedly; run away

Examples

The suspect managed to absquatulate with a suitcase full of jewelry before the police arrived.
During the chaos, the mischievous child tried to absquatulate from the classroom unnoticed.
Legend has it that the pirate crew would absquatulate with their loot when danger was near.

Synset Examples

The thief made off with our silver
the accountant absconded with the cash from the safe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to abscond - to leave hurriedly and secretly, typically to avoid detection or arrest
  • Synonym: to flee - to run away from a place or situation of danger

Etymology

American English coinage, early 19th century, as a humorous pseudo-Latin formation.
