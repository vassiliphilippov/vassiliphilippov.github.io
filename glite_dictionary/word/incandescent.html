incandescent

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.kænˈdɛs.ənt / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kænˈdɛs.ənt / (BrE)
full of strong emotion, passion, or brilliance; intensely bright or impressive
full of strong passion or brilliance

Examples

Her incandescent speech moved everyone in the audience to tears.
The actor gave an incandescent performance that captivated critics and fans alike.
Their love for each other was incandescent, burning brightly for all to see.

Synset Examples

an incandescent performance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passionate - showing or caused by strong feelings or a strong belief
  • Antonym: dull - lacking brightness, vividness, or intensity

Etymology

From Latin 'incandescere' (to become white with heat), from 'in-' (in, upon) + 'candescere' (to glow).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.kænˈdɛs.ənt / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kænˈdɛs.ənt / (BrE)
emitting light as a result of being heated; glowing with intense heat.
emitting light by being heated

Examples

The incandescent filament glowed brightly inside the old lamp.
An incandescent metal rod can easily light up a dark room.
She replaced the incandescent bulb with an LED light to save energy.

Synset Examples

an incandescent bulb

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luminous - emitting or reflecting light
  • Antonym: fluorescent - emitting light through fluorescence, not heat

Etymology

From Latin incandescere (to become white with heat), from in- (in, into) + candescere (to become white).
