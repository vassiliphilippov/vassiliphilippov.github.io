Vipera aspis

1. NOUN

/ vəˈpɛrə ˈæspɪs / (AmE), / vɪˈpɪərə ˈæspɪs / (BrE)
A venomous viper species native to southern Europe, smaller than the common adder.
a venomous viper of southern Europe

Examples

The Vipera aspis is often found basking on rocky slopes in southern France.
Herpetologists studied the venom composition of Vipera aspis in their latest research.
Unlike the common adder, Vipera aspis tends to avoid densely wooded areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adder - a common venomous snake of Europe (Vipera berus)

Etymology

From Latin, 'vipera' meaning viper and 'aspis' meaning asp or shield.
