Lepidobotrys

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛpɪdoʊˈbɒtrɪs / (AmE), / ˌlɛpɪdəˈbɒtrɪs / (BrE)
A genus of dicotyledonous trees in the family Lepidobotryaceae, native to parts of Africa.
genus of African dicot trees

Examples

The rare Lepidobotrys species were studied for their unique floral structures.
Botanists discovered a new member of the Lepidobotrys genus in a remote African forest.
The family Lepidobotryaceae is represented chiefly by the genus Lepidobotrys.

Etymology

From Greek 'lepid-' (scale) and 'botrys' (bunch of grapes), referring to the plant's features.
