lepidobotrys

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛpɪdəˈbɑːtrɪs / (AmE), / ˌlɛpɪdəˈbɒtrɪs / (BrE)
A genus of African trees, sometimes classified in different plant families, notable for their similarity in structure and fruit to the Costa Rican caracolito.
African genus of trees

Examples

The rare lepidobotrys is native to tropical regions of Africa and is studied by botanists for its unique features.
Researchers compared the flowers of lepidobotrys to those of related species from Central America.
The wood of lepidobotrys has a structure similar to that of caracolito, despite their distant geographic origins.

Etymology

From Greek lepis (scale) + botrys (bunch of grapes), referring to the appearance of the fruit clusters.
