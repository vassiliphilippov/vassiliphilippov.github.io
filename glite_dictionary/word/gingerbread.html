gingerbread

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪndʒərˌbrɛd / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪndʒəbɹɛd / (BrE)
A sweet, spiced cake or biscuit typically flavored with ginger and other spices, often sweetened with honey, treacle, or molasses.
spiced cake or biscuit flavored with ginger

Examples

My grandmother bakes delicious gingerbread every Christmas.
The smell of freshly baked gingerbread filled the kitchen.
We decorated the gingerbread with icing and candy for the party.

Etymology

From Middle English 'gingebreed' (preserved ginger), from 'ginger' and 'bread' (meaning food, not just loaf).
