Hirundinidae

1. NOUN

/ hɪˌrʌndɪˈniːdiː / (AmE), / hɪˌrʌndɪˈniːdiː / (BrE)
a family of passerine birds comprising swallows and martins, known for their slender bodies, long pointed wings, and aerial feeding habits
family of birds including swallows and martins

Examples

The Hirundinidae are easily recognized by their streamlined bodies and agile flight.
Scientists discovered several new species within the Hirundinidae family.
Members of the Hirundinidae are found on every continent except Antarctica.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swallow - a small passerine bird in the family Hirundinidae, known for graceful flight
  • Synonym: martin - a type of swallow or closely related bird in the family Hirundinidae

Etymology

From Latin *hirundo* (swallow) + scientific suffix *-idae* (family).

2. NOUN

/ hɪˌrʌndəˈnaɪdiː / (AmE), / hɪˌrʌndɪˈniːdiː / (BrE)
a family of small passerine birds, including swallows and martins, characterized by slender bodies, long pointed wings, and aerial feeding habits
family of birds: swallows and martins

Examples

The Hirundinidae are known for their remarkable aerial agility and insect-catching skills.
Many migratory species within the Hirundinidae family travel thousands of miles each year.
Biologists often study Hirundinidae to understand adaptations for flight in birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swallow - a small, agile songbird known for graceful flight
  • Synonym: martin - a type of swallow, especially in the genus Progne

Etymology

From New Latin Hirundinidae, from Latin hirundo (swallow), used in zoological taxonomy.
