comma

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmə / (AmE), / ˈkɒmə / (BrE)
a North American anglewing butterfly (Polygonia c-album) with a comma-shaped mark on the underside of each hind wing
type of butterfly with a comma-shaped mark

Examples

The comma is easily recognized by the small white mark on its wing.
She spotted a comma resting on a nettle in the garden.
Many butterfly enthusiasts delight in identifying the elusive comma in woodlands.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.mə / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ə / (BrE)
a punctuation mark (,) used to separate parts of a sentence or elements in a list
punctuation mark: ,

Examples

Remember to use a comma after each item in the series.
The sentence was confusing without a comma between the clauses.
She added a comma to separate the adjectives.

Etymology

From Latin 'comma' (clause in a sentence), from Greek 'kómma' (something cut off).
