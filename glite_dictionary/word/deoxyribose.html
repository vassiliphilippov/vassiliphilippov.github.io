deoxyribose

1. NOUN

/ diˌɑksiˈraɪboʊs / (AmE), / diˌɒksɪˈraɪbəʊs / (BrE)
A sugar that forms part of the backbone of DNA, replacing ribose by lacking one oxygen atom.
sugar in DNA nucleic acids
medical

Examples

DNA is composed of a backbone made of deoxyribose and phosphate groups.
The difference between RNA and DNA lies largely in the presence of ribose versus deoxyribose.
Each nucleotide in DNA contains a deoxyribose sugar, a phosphate group, and a nitrogenous base.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ribose - a five-carbon sugar found in RNA

Etymology

From deoxy- (indicating the removal of an oxygen atom) + ribose (a type of sugar).
