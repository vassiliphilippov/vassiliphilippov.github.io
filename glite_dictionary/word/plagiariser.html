plagiariser

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪdʒəˌraɪzər / (AmE), / ˈpleɪdʒərʌɪzə / (BrE)
A person who uses another person's words or ideas and presents them as their own.
person who copies others' work as their own
british

Examples

The professor warned that any plagiariser would face serious consequences.
He was labeled a plagiariser after the investigation revealed he had copied large parts of his essay.
Publishing work without giving credit to the original author makes you a plagiariser.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plagiarist - person who commits plagiarism
  • Antonym: original thinker - person who creates and uses their own ideas

Etymology

From Latin 'plagiarius' (kidnapper, literary thief), via French and English 'plagiarist'; -iser suffix is British English spelling variant.
