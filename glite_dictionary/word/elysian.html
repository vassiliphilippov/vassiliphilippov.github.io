elysian

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈlɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪzɪən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the Elysian Fields; blissful or heavenly.
heavenly; relating to the Elysian Fields

Examples

The poet described the garden as an elysian retreat, far removed from worldly worries.
Under the clear night sky, the valley had an almost elysian beauty that left us speechless.
She sought an elysian existence, filled with peace, joy, and endless sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paradisiacal - resembling paradise; heavenly
  • Antonym: infernal - relating to hell; diabolical

Etymology

From Latin Elysium (the abode of the blessed after death in Greek mythology), from Greek Ēlysion.

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈlɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪziən / (BrE)
of or relating to great happiness, beauty, or heavenly perfection; blissful or sublime, as if inspired by the gods
heavenly or blissful; sublime

Examples

The gardens had an elysian beauty that left visitors in awe.
She remembered the elysian days of her youth, filled with laughter and sunlight.
Their honeymoon in the mountains was an elysian experience they would never forget.

Synset Examples

her pies were simply divine
the divine Shakespeare
an elysian meal
an inspired performance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divine - of such excellence, beauty, or goodness as to resemble or suggest a deity
  • Synonym: sublime - of such excellence, grandeur, or beauty as to inspire great admiration or awe

Etymology

From Latin Elysium (the abode of the blessed after death in Greek mythology) + -an.
