gadgetry

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡædʒɪtri / (AmE), / ˈɡædʒɪtri / (BrE)
Mechanical or electronic devices collectively, especially those considered novel or innovative.
devices or gadgets collectively

Examples

The kitchen drawer was full of gadgetry that promised to make cooking easier.
Modern offices are filled with digital gadgetry to boost productivity.
Much of the new gadgetry at the trade fair impressed the technology enthusiasts.

Synset Examples

laborsaving gadgetry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gadget - a small mechanical or electronic device, especially a clever or novel one

Etymology

From 'gadget' + '-ry', forming a collective noun.
