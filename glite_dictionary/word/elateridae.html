Elateridae

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlætəˈraɪdiː / (AmE), / ɪˌlatəˈriːdɪiː / (BrE)
a family of beetles known as click beetles, including certain fireflies, distinguished by their clicking mechanism used to escape predators.
family of click beetles

Examples

The Elateridae are known for their unique ability to snap themselves into the air when threatened.
Scientists have identified hundreds of Elateridae species across different continents.
Members of the Elateridae family are sometimes called click beetles due to the sound they make.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: click beetle - a common name for beetles in the family Elateridae

Etymology

From New Latin Elateridae, derived from the genus Elater, from Greek elatēr (driver, agitator).
