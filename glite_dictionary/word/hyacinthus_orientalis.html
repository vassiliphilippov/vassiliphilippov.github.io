Hyacinthus orientalis

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈæsɪnθəs ɔːriˈɛntəlɪs / (AmE), / haɪˈæsɪnθəs ɔːriˈɛntælɪs / (BrE)
a species of hyacinth widely cultivated for its fragrant, white, pink, blue, or purplish flowers
species of ornamental hyacinth

Examples

Many gardeners choose to plant Hyacinthus orientalis in the spring for its vibrant blooms.
The fragrance of Hyacinthus orientalis filled the conservatory during the flower show.
She carefully arranged several stems of Hyacinthus orientalis in a glass vase.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyacinth - a bulbous flowering plant of the genus Hyacinthus

Etymology

From Latin, based on Greek 'Hyakinthos' (a mythological figure and a flower) and Latin 'orientalis' (eastern).
