ringhals

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪŋ.hɑlz / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋ.hɑːlz / (BrE)
A highly venomous snake of southern Africa, capable of spitting its venom up to seven feet as a defense mechanism.
venomous spitting snake of southern Africa

Examples

The ringhals can project its venom accurately to defend itself from threats.
Farmers in southern Africa are cautious of the ringhals when working in the fields.
Unlike most cobras, the ringhals prefers to feign death when confronted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spitting cobra - venomous snake able to eject venom as a defense

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'ringhals', meaning 'ring neck', referring to the snake's characteristic neck markings.
