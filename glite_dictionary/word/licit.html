licit

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɪsɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪsɪt / (BrE)
Permitted by law; lawful; not forbidden by rules or law.
permitted by law; lawful

Examples

The company ensured that all aspects of its operations were strictly licit.
While some medications are illicit without a prescription, others are entirely licit and available over the counter.
Engaging in licit business practices helps build trust with partners and clients.

Synset Examples

a legitimate government

Related Terms

  • Antonym: illicit - not permitted by law or rules
  • Synonym: legal - permitted by law

Etymology

From Latin 'licitus' (allowed, lawful), from 'licēre' (to be permitted).

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɪsɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪsɪt / (BrE)
permitted by law, custom, or morality; lawful.
permitted; lawful

Examples

Although gambling is licit in some countries, it remains illegal in others.
The company only conducts licit business activities, with strict adherence to the law.
In many cultures, certain relationships are considered licit while others are forbidden.

Synset Examples

a wife's licit love

Related Terms

  • Antonym: illicit - not permitted by law, custom, or morality; unlawful

Etymology

From Latin 'licitus' (allowed, lawful), from 'licere' (to be permitted).
