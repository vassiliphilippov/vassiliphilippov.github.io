Jewess

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuː.ɛs / (AmE), / ˈdʒuː.ɛs / (BrE)
a woman who is Jewish; a female Jew (now often considered old-fashioned or offensive)
Jewish woman (dated, often considered offensive)
archaic offensive

Examples

The novel's protagonist is a proud Jewess living in 19th-century Vienna.
In older literature, the term Jewess was commonly used to describe female Jewish characters.
She disliked being called a Jewess, preferring to be referred to simply as Jewish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jew - a person who belongs to the Jewish people
  • Antonym: gentile - a person who is not Jewish

Etymology

Formed by adding the suffix -ess (denoting female) to 'Jew'.
