Charlotte Corday

1. PROPN

/ ʃɑrˈloʊt kɔrˈdeɪ / (AmE), / ʃɑːˈlɒt kɔːˈdeɪ / (BrE)
(1768–1793) A French revolutionary and Girondist known for assassinating Jean-Paul Marat during the French Revolution.
French revolutionary; assassin of Marat

Examples

Charlotte Corday is remembered for her dramatic role in the French Revolution.
The assassination of Marat by Charlotte Corday changed the course of revolutionary politics.
Historians often debate the motives of Charlotte Corday in killing Marat.
