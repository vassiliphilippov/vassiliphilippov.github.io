thunderstorm

1. NOUN

/ ˈθʌndɚˌstɔrm / (AmE), / ˈθʌndəˌstɔːm / (BrE)
A storm characterized by thunder and lightning, often accompanied by heavy rain or hail, caused by strong rising air currents.
storm with thunder and lightning

Examples

A loud thunderstorm woke me up in the middle of the night.
The forecast predicts a severe thunderstorm this afternoon with possible hail.
We had to cancel our picnic because a thunderstorm rolled in suddenly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storm - a violent disturbance of the atmosphere
  • Degree Lower: rainstorm - a storm with heavy rain
  • Synonym: hailstorm - a storm with hail

Etymology

Formed from 'thunder' and 'storm' in English, first recorded in the 18th century.
