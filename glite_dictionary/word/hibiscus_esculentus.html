Hibiscus esculentus

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈbɪskəs ɛˌskjʊˈlɛntəs / (AmE), / hɪˈbɪskəs ɛˌskjʊˈlɛntəs / (BrE)
The scientific name for okra, a tall, coarse annual plant widely cultivated for its edible green pods used in cooking.
scientific name for okra plant

Examples

Hibiscus esculentus is widely grown in warm climates for its edible seed pods.
The mucilage from Hibiscus esculentus gives gumbo its characteristic texture.
Botanists may refer to okra as Hibiscus esculentus in scientific literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: okra - common name for Hibiscus esculentus, an edible green pod vegetable

Etymology

From Latin: 'Hibiscus' (a genus name from Greek 'hibiskos', mallow), 'esculentus' meaning 'edible'.
