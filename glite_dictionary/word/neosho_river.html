Neosho River

1. PROPN

/ niˈoʊʃoʊ ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / niˈəʊʃəʊ ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
A river that rises in eastern Kansas and flows eastward into Oklahoma, where it becomes a tributary of the Arkansas River.
river in Kansas and Oklahoma, USA

Examples

The Neosho River is known for its diverse wildlife and scenic beauty.
Residents of the small town enjoy fishing along the Neosho River during the summer.
Heavy rainfall caused the Neosho River to overflow its banks last spring.

Etymology

From Osage (Siouan language), possibly meaning 'clear water' or 'water with muddy banks'.
