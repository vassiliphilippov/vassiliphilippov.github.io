Synercus caffer

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈnɜrkəs ˈkæfər / (AmE), / sɪˈnɜːkəs ˈkæfə / (BrE)
a large, often aggressive buffalo native to southern Africa, known for its upward-curving horns, commonly found in game reserves
African buffalo species of southern Africa

Examples

The Synercus caffer is a prominent species among Africa's big game animals.
Researchers observed a herd of Synercus caffer grazing near the river in the reserve.
Because of its unpredictable nature, approaching the Synercus caffer can be extremely dangerous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: African buffalo - the large buffalo species native to Africa

Etymology

From New Latin Synercus (genus name) and caffer (from Latin, meaning African), used in taxonomy.
