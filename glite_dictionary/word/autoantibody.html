autoantibody

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔtoʊˈæntɪˌbɑdi / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəʊˈantɪˌbɒdi / (BrE)
an antibody produced by an organism that reacts against its own tissues
antibody attacking own tissues
medical

Examples

The presence of autoantibodies can indicate an autoimmune disease such as lupus.
Researchers detected high levels of autoantibodies in the patient's blood sample.
Certain therapies aim to suppress the production of harmful autoantibodies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antibody - a protein produced by the immune system to neutralize pathogens

Etymology

From Greek 'auto-' (self) + 'antibody'.
