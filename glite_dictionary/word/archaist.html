archaist

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːr.keɪ.ɪst / (AmE), / ˈɑː.keɪ.ɪst / (BrE)
a person who deliberately uses or imitates archaic language, style, or customs
person who uses archaic forms

Examples

The poet was known as an archaist, often reviving words lost to modern English.
Some critics dismissed him as an archaist for his deliberate imitation of old literary forms.
As an amateur archaist, she enjoyed reconstructing medieval speech for historical reenactments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archaism - the use or imitation of archaic words or styles

Etymology

From archaic + -ist, meaning a person who practices or is concerned with something.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːr.keɪ.ɪst / (AmE), / ˈɑː.keɪ.ɪst / (BrE)
a person who is an expert in or collector of antiquities, especially those relating to ancient times
expert or collector of antiquities

Examples

The museum hired an archaist to catalog its growing collection of ancient artifacts.
As an archaist, her passion was discovering forgotten relics from lost civilizations.
He developed a reputation as an archaist with a remarkable eye for genuine antiquities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiquarian - a person who studies or collects antiques or antiquities

Etymology

From Greek arkhaios (ancient, old) via Latin archaista.
