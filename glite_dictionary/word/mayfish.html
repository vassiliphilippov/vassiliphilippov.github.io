mayfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a black-barred fish found in bays and coastal marshes along the Atlantic and Gulf Coast of the United States
coastal fish of Atlantic and Gulf U.S.

Examples

Fishermen often catch mayfish in the shallow waters of coastal marshes.
The distinctive stripes of the mayfish make it easy to spot among other bay species.
During late spring, mayfish are commonly seen near the Atlantic shoreline.
