awakened

1. ADJ

/ əˈweɪkənd / (AmE), / əˈweɪkənd / (BrE)
(of feelings, interests, etc.) aroused or brought into a state of awareness or activity.
aroused or brought to awareness

Examples

The book led to an awakened sense of social responsibility in many readers.
After her travels, she felt an awakened curiosity about the world.
The workshop created an awakened passion for painting in him.

Synset Examples

an awakened interest in ballet

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dormant - not active or having no activity
  • Synonym: activated - made active or operative

2. ADJ

/ əˈweɪkənd / (AmE), / əˈweɪkənd / (BrE)
Having been woken from sleep; no longer asleep.
having been woken up

Examples

The awakened baby began to cry loudly in the nursery.
He felt confused and awakened by the sudden noise outside his window.
After the alarm rang, everyone in the dormitory was awakened and started getting ready.

Synset Examples

the awakened baby began to cry

Related Terms

  • Antonym: asleep - in a state of sleep
  • Synonym: awake - not sleeping; conscious
  • Antonym: sleeping - in the state of sleep

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'awaken', from Middle English, from Old English 'awacian'.
