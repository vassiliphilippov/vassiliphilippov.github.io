cwt

1. NOUN

/ siː dʌbljuː tiː / (AmE), / siː dʌbljuː tiː / (BrE)
A United States unit of weight equal to 100 pounds, used especially in trade and shipment.
U.S. unit of weight (100 pounds)

Examples

The price of wheat rose to $7 per cwt last season.
They ordered 200 cwt of steel for the construction project.
The shipment contained 50 cwt of potatoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hundredweight - A unit of weight; in the U.S. equals 100 pounds, in the UK equals 112 pounds

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'hundredweight', from Old English 'hund' (hundred) + 'weight'.

2. NOUN

/ siː dʌbəl.juː tiː / (AmE), / siː dʌbəl.juː tiː / (BrE)
A British unit of weight equal to 112 pounds (approximately 50.8 kilograms).
British unit of weight (112 pounds)
british

Examples

The shipment weighed five cwt and needed special equipment for unloading.
Prices for potatoes are often quoted per cwt in Britain.
The farm produced nearly thirty cwt of grain last season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hundredweight - A unit of weight equal to 112 pounds in the UK

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'hundredweight', from Old English 'hundred' + 'weight'.
