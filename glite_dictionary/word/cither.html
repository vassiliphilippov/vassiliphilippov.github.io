cither

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪðər / (AmE), / ˈsɪθə / (BrE)
a 16th-century musical instrument resembling a guitar, with a pear-shaped soundbox and wire strings
historical stringed musical instrument

Examples

The museum displayed a rare cither from the Renaissance era.
He learned to play the cither as part of his study of early music.
The delicate sound of the cither filled the old concert hall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zither - musical instrument with strings stretched over a flat body, played by plucking

Etymology

From Greek kithara (a stringed instrument), via Latin cithara and Old French cithare.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪðər / (AmE), / ˈsɪtə, ˈkɪtə / (BrE)
a stringed musical instrument with strings stretched over a flat sounding board, played while laid flat using a plectrum and fingers
flat stringed musical instrument

Examples

The musician carefully tuned the cither before performing a traditional folk melody.
She plucked the strings of her antique cither with remarkable delicacy and skill.
A rare cither was displayed in the museum's collection of historical musical instruments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zither - a similar musical instrument with strings over a flat body, played flat

Etymology

From Latin *cithara*, from Ancient Greek *kithara*, denoting a type of lyre.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
