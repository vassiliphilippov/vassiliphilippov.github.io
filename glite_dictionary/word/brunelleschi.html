Brunelleschi

1. PROPN

/ bruːnɛˈlɛski / (AmE), / bruːnɛˈlɛski / (BrE)
Filippo Brunelleschi (1377–1446), a Florentine architect celebrated as the first great architect of the Italian Renaissance, known for his innovative work on the dome of Florence Cathedral.
Florentine Renaissance architect (1377–1446)

Examples

The dome of Florence Cathedral was engineered by Brunelleschi, demonstrating his mastery of architectural innovation.
Many consider Brunelleschi the father of Renaissance architecture due to his groundbreaking designs.
Art students study Brunelleschi's use of perspective to understand the development of modern architecture.

Etymology

From the Italian surname Brunelleschi.
