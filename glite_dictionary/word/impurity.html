impurity

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpjʊr.ə.ti / (AmE), / ɪmˈpjʊə.rɪ.ti / (BrE)
an unwanted or harmful substance that is present in something and should be removed, especially in a chemical or physical context
unwanted or harmful substance

Examples

The laboratory tested the sample for impurity to ensure it was safe for consumption.
Iron must be refined to remove any impurity before it can be used in construction.
Even a tiny impurity in the solution can affect the outcome of the experiment.

Synset Examples

there were impurities in the water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contaminant - a substance that makes something impure
  • Antonym: pure - not mixed with anything else; without impurity

Etymology

From Latin 'impuritas', from 'impurus' (impure).

2. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpjʊrəti / (AmE), / ɪmˈpjʊərɪti / (BrE)
The state or quality of being impure; the condition of containing foreign or undesirable substances or elements.
the state of being impure

Examples

The water requires further treatment to remove any impurity before it is safe to drink.
Even a slight impurity in the ingredients can affect the quality of the final product.
Gold is often mixed with some impurity, making it less valuable.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: purity - the state of being pure

Etymology

From Latin 'impuritas', from 'impurus' (impure).
