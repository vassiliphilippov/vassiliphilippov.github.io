lee

1. ADJ

/ liː / (AmE), / liː / (BrE)
Situated on or facing the side sheltered from the wind; away from the wind.
sheltered from the wind

Examples

The sailors took refuge on the lee side of the island during the storm.
We anchored the boat on the lee shore where the wind couldn't reach us.
They sought shelter in the lee valley for protection from the harsh weather.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: windward - facing or situated toward the wind

Etymology

From Middle English *le*, from Old English *hlēo* (shelter, protection).

2. NOUN

/ liː / (AmE), / liː / (BrE)
the side or area of something that is sheltered from the wind
sheltered side from the wind

Examples

We anchored the boat on the lee of the island to avoid the strong winds.
The hikers set up their camp in the lee of a large rock.
Cows gathered in the lee of the barn during the storm.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: windward - the side from which the wind is blowing

Etymology

From Old English hlēo (shelter, protection).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08609908-n
Synset POS: n
