Massachusetts

1. PROPN

/ ˌmæsəˈtʃuːsɪts / (AmE), / ˌmæsəˈtʃuːsɪts / (BrE)
a state in the northeastern United States, one of the original thirteen colonies, with Boston as its capital
northeastern US state; original 13 colony

Examples

She moved to Massachusetts after graduating from college.
The Pilgrims landed in what is now Massachusetts in 1620.
Harvard University is located in Massachusetts.

Etymology

From Algonquian, probably meaning 'near the great hill'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmæsəˈtʃuːsɪts / (AmE), / ˌmæsəˈtʃuːsɪts / (BrE)
a member of the Algonquian people who formerly lived around Massachusetts Bay in the northeastern United States
a Native American group from Massachusetts Bay

Examples

The Massachusetts traded with early English settlers in the region.
Members of the Massachusetts tribe played an important role in local history.
Archaeologists study artifacts left by the Massachusetts along the bay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Massachusett - the Algonquian language spoken by the Massachusetts people

Etymology

From Massachusett, their own name for themselves, probably meaning 'at the great hill'.

3. PROPN

/ ˌmæsəˈtʃuːsɪts / (AmE), / ˌmæsəˈtʃuːsɪts / (BrE)
A state in the northeastern United States, originally established as one of the British colonies; its capital is Boston.
a state in the northeastern United States

Examples

The city of Boston is the capital of Massachusetts and a major center of education.
In 1788, Massachusetts became the sixth state to join the United States.
Many historical sites in Massachusetts date back to the colonial period.

Etymology

From a Native American Massachusett tribe name meaning 'at the great hill'.

4. NOUN

/ ˌmæsəˈtʃuːsɪts / (AmE), / ˌmæsəˈtʃuːsɪts / (BrE)
the Algonquian language spoken by the Massachuset people, now extinct
extinct Algonquian language of the Massachuset

Examples

Linguists have studied Massachusetts to better understand Algonquian language structures.
The last fluent speaker of Massachusetts died in the 19th century.
Some modern tribes are working to revive the Massachusetts language through education.

Etymology

From the Massachusett people's self-identification; related to Massachusetts (the state), from Algonquian place name.
