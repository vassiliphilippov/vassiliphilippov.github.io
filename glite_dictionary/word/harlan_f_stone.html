Harlan F. Stone

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɑːrlən ɛf stoʊn / (AmE), / ˈhɑːlən ɛf stəʊn / (BrE)
(1872–1946) United States jurist who served as Chief Justice of the Supreme Court from 1941 to 1946.
Chief Justice of U.S. Supreme Court (1941–1946)

Examples

President Roosevelt appointed Harlan F. Stone as Chief Justice in 1941.
Harlan F. Stone made several important contributions to constitutional law during his tenure.
Many legal scholars have studied the decisions made by Harlan F. Stone on the Supreme Court.
