to detusk

1. VERB

/ diˈtʌsk / (AmE), / diˈtʌsk / (BrE)
to remove the tusks of an animal, especially an elephant
remove the tusks of an animal

Examples

Wildlife officials had to detusk the elephant to prevent further aggression.
The veterinarian carefully detusked the injured boar as part of the treatment.
Some conservation programs aim to detusk rhinos to protect them from poachers.

Synset Examples

tusk an elephant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tusk - the long pointed tooth of certain animals, such as elephants or walruses

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'de-' (removal) + 'tusk'.
