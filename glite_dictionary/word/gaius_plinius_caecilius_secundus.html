Gaius Plinius Caecilius Secundus

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡaɪ.əs plɪˈniː.əs siːˈsɪl.i.əs sɪˈkʌn.dəs / (AmE), / ˈɡaɪ.əs plɪˈniː.əs siːˈkɪli.əs sɪˈkʌn.dəs / (BrE)
(62–c.113 AD) A Roman writer and official, known as Pliny the Younger, famous for his published letters that provide insight into Roman life and history.
Roman writer and official (Pliny the Younger)

Examples

Gaius Plinius Caecilius Secundus is better known in English as Pliny the Younger.
The letters of Gaius Plinius Caecilius Secundus provide valuable insights into daily life in ancient Rome.
Scholars often consult the works of Gaius Plinius Caecilius Secundus when studying the eruption of Vesuvius.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pliny the Younger - Roman writer and official; common English name for Gaius Plinius Caecilius Secundus

Etymology

Latin name; known in English as Pliny the Younger, nephew of Pliny the Elder.
