acedia

1. NOUN

/ əˈsiːdiə / (AmE), / əˈsiːdɪə / (BrE)
spiritual apathy and inactivity, especially as regarded as a form of deadly sin in early Christian theology.
spiritual apathy; one of the deadly sins

Examples

Medieval monks considered acedia to be a dangerous spiritual affliction.
His struggle with acedia left him listless and unmotivated in his pursuits of virtue.
The ancient texts warn against falling into acedia during times of hardship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sloth - laziness; reluctance to work or act, also a deadly sin
  • Antonym: diligence - persistent work or effort, contrasted with acedia as a virtue

Etymology

From Late Latin *acēdia* (carelessness, apathy), from Ancient Greek *akēdía* (carelessness, indifference).
