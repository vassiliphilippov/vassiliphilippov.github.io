Czech

1. NOUN

/ tʃɛk / (AmE), / tʃɛk / (BrE)
the West Slavic language spoken primarily in the Czech Republic
West Slavic language of the Czech Republic

Examples

He is fluent in both English and Czech.
The novel was translated from Czech into several other languages.
She studied Czech at university for four years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Slovak - the West Slavic language closely related to Czech
  • Degree Lower: Czech Republic - a country in Central Europe where Czech is the official language

Etymology

From the Czech word 'čeština', derived from 'Čech' (a Czech person).

2. NOUN

/ tʃɛk / (AmE), / tʃɛk / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of the Czech Republic or the former Czechoslovakia
a person from the Czech Republic

Examples

My grandfather was a proud Czech who emigrated to the United States in the 1940s.
At the international festival, many Czechs wore traditional costumes and shared their culture.
She speaks fluent Czech because her mother is a Czech from Prague.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Slovak - a native or inhabitant of Slovakia or former Czechoslovakia

Etymology

From Czech *Čech*, denoting a person of Czech nationality.
