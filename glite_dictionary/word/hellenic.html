Hellenic

1. ADJ

/ hɛˈlɛnɪk / (AmE), / hɛˈlɛnɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of ancient Greek civilization, especially in its classical period.
of or relating to classical Greek civilization

Examples

The architecture of the building displays distinct Hellenic influences.
She studied the great philosophers of the Hellenic period at university.
Many museums feature exhibitions on Hellenic art and culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Greek - of or relating to Greece, its people, or their language

Etymology

From Greek Hellēnikos (Greek), from Hellēn (a Greek).

2. NOUN

/ həˈlɛnɪk / (AmE), / hɛˈlɛnɪk / (BrE)
the branch of the Indo-European language family that includes Greek
Greek branch of Indo-European languages

Examples

Classical Hellenic is the language found in most ancient Greek literature.
Modern Hellenic has undergone significant changes from its ancient forms.
The study of Hellenic languages is central to understanding Indo-European linguistics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Greek - the language belonging to the Hellenic branch, spoken in Greece

Etymology

From Greek Hellēnikós, meaning 'of the Greeks'.
