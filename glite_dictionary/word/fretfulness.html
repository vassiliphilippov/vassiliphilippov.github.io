fretfulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɛtfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈfrɛtfʊlnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being irritable, uneasy, or easily upset
state of being irritable or uneasy

Examples

The baby's fretfulness kept her parents awake for most of the night.
His fretfulness increased as the deadline approached and nothing seemed to go right.
A sense of fretfulness hung over the office after the sudden announcement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritability - the quality of being easily annoyed
  • Antonym: calmness - the quality of being calm or peaceful

Etymology

From 'fretful' (irritable), ultimately from Old English 'fretan' (to devour, eat away).
