hemostasis

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːməˈsteɪsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˈsteɪsɪs / (BrE)
The stopping of bleeding or the flow of blood, especially as a result of a surgical procedure.
stopping of bleeding (surgical/medical)
medical

Examples

The surgeon achieved hemostasis quickly to prevent excessive blood loss.
Proper hemostasis is essential during major operations.
Medication can sometimes be used to aid hemostasis in wounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coagulation - process by which blood changes from a liquid to a gel, forming a blood clot
  • Antonym: hemorrhage - an escape of blood from a ruptured blood vessel; excessive bleeding

Etymology

From Greek hēmastasis, from haima (blood) + stasis (standing, stopping).
