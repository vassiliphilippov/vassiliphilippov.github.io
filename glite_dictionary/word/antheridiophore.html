antheridiophore

1. NOUN

/ ænˌθɛrɪˈdiːəˌfɔr / (AmE), / ænˌθɛrɪˈdiːəˌfɔː / (BrE)
A stalk or structure that bears antheridia (the male reproductive organs), found in certain mosses and liverworts.
stalk bearing male organs in mosses/liverworts
medical

Examples

The antheridiophore supports the antheridia at its tip in the reproductive cycle of certain liverworts.
Mosses with distinct antheridiophores often show clear separation between male and female structures.
Scientists examined the antheridiophore under a microscope to study the arrangement of the antheridia.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: archegoniophore - stalk bearing female organs (archegonia) in mosses and liverworts

Etymology

From Greek antheridion (small anther) + -phore (bearer).
