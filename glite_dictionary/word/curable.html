curable

1. ADJ

/ ˈkjʊrəb(ə)l / (AmE), / ˈkjʊərəb(ə)l / (BrE)
(of a substance) capable of being hardened or set by some additive or agent, especially in reference to resins or polymers.
able to be hardened or set (of substances)

Examples

The adhesive is curable at room temperature with the addition of a catalyst.
Certain plastics are curable by exposure to ultraviolet light.
The manufacturer provides both curable and non-curable versions of the sealant.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uncurable - not capable of being hardened or set
  • Synonym: cure - to cause (a substance) to harden or set by chemical reaction

Etymology

From Latin 'curare' (to care for), but this technical sense derives from the verb 'to cure' meaning 'to harden or set a material'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkjʊrəbəl / (AmE), / ˈkjʊərəbəl / (BrE)
(of a disease or condition) able to be cured; capable of being healed or remedied.
able to be cured or healed

Examples

Many forms of cancer are now considered curable with early detection and proper treatment.
The doctor assured her that the infection was curable with a course of antibiotics.
While the condition can be serious, it is usually curable if treated promptly.

Synset Examples

curable diseases

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incurable - unable to be cured or healed
  • Synonym: treatable - able to be treated, especially medically

Etymology

From Latin *curabilis*, from *curare* (to care, to cure).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00997760-a
Synset POS: a
