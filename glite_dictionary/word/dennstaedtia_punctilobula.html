Dennstaedtia punctilobula

1. NOUN

/ dɛnˈstɛdiə ˌpʌŋktɪˈloʊbjələ / (AmE), / dɛnˈstɛdɪə ˌpʌŋktɪˈləʊbjʊlə / (BrE)
a fern of eastern North America with pale green, finely divided fronds and an aroma similar to hay
fern native to eastern North America

Examples

The edge of the woodland was covered in Dennstaedtia punctilobula, giving the area a soft green appearance.
Botanists noted the hay-like scent when they brushed against the Dennstaedtia punctilobula ferns.
Some gardeners plant Dennstaedtia punctilobula for its delicate foliage and resilience in shaded areas.

Etymology

From genus Dennstaedtia (named after German botanist August W. Dennstaedt) and Latin punctilobula (meaning 'finely lobed').
