thirsty

1. ADJ

/ ˈθɜrsti / (AmE), / ˈθɜːsti / (BrE)
(of land, plants, etc.) needing moisture; very dry or parched.
needing moisture (of land, plants, etc.)

Examples

The thirsty soil cracked in the summer heat from lack of rain.
After weeks without water, the thirsty lawn turned brown and brittle.
The farmer prayed for rain to revive his thirsty crops.

Synset Examples

thirsty fields under a rainless sky

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parched - extremely dry, especially due to heat; synonym for dry land needing moisture

Etymology

From Old English thurstig, related to thirst.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02564910-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈθɜrsti / (AmE), / ˈθɜːsti / (BrE)
(of a material or object) able to absorb or take in large quantities of moisture.
able to absorb a lot of moisture

Examples

These thirsty towels are perfect for drying up large spills quickly.
He bought a thirsty sponge to mop up the water on the floor.
Only thirsty paper can handle that much ink when printing posters.

Synset Examples

thirsty towels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absorbent - able to soak up liquid easily

Etymology

From Old English *thyrstig* (feeling a need to drink), extended in modern usage to mean 'absorbent'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00007716-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈθɜrsti / (AmE), / ˈθɜːsti / (BrE)
having a strong and intense desire for something, especially for knowledge, attention, or success
feeling a strong desire (figurative)

Examples

After his first taste of success, he became thirsty for more recognition in his field.
She was thirsty for knowledge and spent her evenings reading about science.
The ambitious young politician was thirsty for power and influence.

Synset Examples

athirst for knowledge
hungry for recognition
thirsty for information

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hungry - having a strong desire or need for something, especially food or recognition

Etymology

Old English *thurstig*, related to *thirst* (from Proto-Germanic roots).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00891492-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈθɜrsti / (AmE), / ˈθɜːsti / (BrE)
feeling a need or desire to drink
feeling a need to drink

Examples

After running in the heat, I felt extremely thirsty.
The hikers were thirsty and searched for a stream to refill their bottles.
If you're thirsty, there's water in the fridge.

Synset Examples

after playing hard the children were thirsty

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: parched - very dry or extremely thirsty
  • Degree Lower: dry - having little or no moisture; needing water
  • Antonym: hungry - feeling a need or desire to eat

Etymology

Old English þyrstig, from þurst (thirst).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01273114-a
