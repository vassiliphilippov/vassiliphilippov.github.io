vista

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪstə / (AmE), / ˈvɪstə / (BrE)
a pleasing view, especially one seen through a long, narrow opening or from a high position
a pleasing or scenic view

Examples

From the summit, the vista stretched for miles across lush green valleys.
A row of tall windows offered a spectacular vista of the city skyline.
As we rounded the bend, a breathtaking vista suddenly came into view.

Synset Examples

the most desirable feature of the park are the beautiful views

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panorama - an unbroken view of the whole region surrounding an observer
  • Synonym: prospect - a view, especially a distant one
  • Antonym: obstruction - something that blocks or closes an opening or view

Etymology

From Italian vista (view), from vedere (to see), from Latin videre.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɪstə / (AmE), / ˈvɪstə / (BrE)
a pleasing view, especially one seen through a long, narrow opening or across a wide area
pleasing or beautiful view

Examples

From the summit, we enjoyed a breathtaking vista stretching for miles.
The picture window frames a spectacular vista of the mountains.
Travelers stopped to admire the stunning vista along the coastal highway.

Synset Examples

She opened the window to reveal a beautiful vista of the countryside.
The city offers a stunning vista of the skyline at sunset.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panorama - an unbroken view of the whole region surrounding an observer
  • Synonym: prospect - a view, especially of scenery

Etymology

From Italian 'vista' (sight, view), from Latin 'videre' (to see).
