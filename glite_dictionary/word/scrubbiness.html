scrubbiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈskrʌbinəs / (AmE), / ˈskrʌbɪnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being small, stunted, or inferior in size or condition
inferior or stunted quality

Examples

The scrubbiness of the surrounding vegetation made the landscape look bleak.
She was disappointed by the scrubbiness of the garden after the dry summer.
They tried to restore the park, but the scrubbiness of the soil limited their success.

Synset Examples

the scrawniness of sickly trees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrawniness - the condition of being thin and bony
  • Antonym: lushness - the quality of being rich and abundant in growth

Etymology

From 'scrubby' (stunted, inferior) + '-ness' (forming nouns indicating a state or quality).
