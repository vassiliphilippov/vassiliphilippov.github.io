Tricholoma populinum

1. NOUN

/ trɪkəˌloʊmə pɒˈpjuːlɪnəm / (AmE), / trɪkəˌləʊmə pɒˈpjuːlɪnəm / (BrE)
a species of edible mushroom that grows in large clusters, especially under cottonwood trees in sandy soil
an edible mushroom species found under cottonwoods

Examples

The Tricholoma populinum is often foraged by mushroom enthusiasts in the fall.
Clusters of Tricholoma populinum can be found near stands of cottonwood after heavy rains.
Before eating Tricholoma populinum, it is important to properly identify it to avoid confusion with toxic species.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Tricholoma', from Greek 'trikhos' (hair) + 'loma' (fringe), and specific epithet 'populinum' from 'populus' (poplar).
