evangelistic

1. ADJ

/ ɪˌvæn.dʒəˈlɪs.tɪk / (AmE), / ɪˌvan.dʒəˈlɪs.tɪk / (BrE)
Marked by strong or enthusiastic support for a cause, idea, or movement, often in a way that seeks to persuade others.
zealously enthusiastic for a cause

Examples

She spoke with such evangelistic passion that everyone in the room felt inspired.
The manager's evangelistic support of the new policy won over many skeptics.
His evangelistic enthusiasm for environmental issues often motivates others to get involved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zealous - showing great energy or enthusiasm in pursuit of a cause

Etymology

From 'evangelist', ultimately from Greek 'euangelion' (good news), by analogy to religious fervor.

2. ADJ

/ ɪˌvæn.dʒəˈlɪs.tɪk / (AmE), / ɪˌvan.dʒəˈlɪs.tɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or promoting the preaching and spread of the Christian gospel.
promoting the Christian gospel

Examples

Her evangelistic zeal inspired many others to join the church.
The group organized an evangelistic campaign throughout the community.
He demonstrated evangelistic passion when speaking about his faith.

Synset Examples

evangelistic fervor
the evangelistic concerns of the early church

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evangelical - relating to the Christian gospel or to evangelism

Etymology

From evangelical + -istic, based on Greek evangelion (good news, gospel).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02934594-a
Synset POS: a
