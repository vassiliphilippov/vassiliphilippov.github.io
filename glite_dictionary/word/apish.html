apish

1. ADJ

/ ˈeɪpɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈeɪpɪʃ / (BrE)
imitative in a way that is servile or foolish, resembling the behavior of an ape
servilely imitative; like an ape

Examples

His apish behavior at the meeting made everyone uncomfortable.
Trying to impress the boss, she adopted an apish imitation of his speaking style.
The comedian's apish mimicry of celebrities drew mixed reactions from the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imitative - copying others, especially in manner or behavior
  • Antonym: original - not imitative, independent in thought or style

Etymology

From 'ape' + '-ish', meaning 'like an ape'.
