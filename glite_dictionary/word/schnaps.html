schnaps

1. NOUN

/ ʃnɑps / (AmE), / ʃnaps / (BrE)
A strong alcoholic spirit, especially one distilled from potatoes or grain, often associated with Germany or the Netherlands.
strong distilled spirit, especially from potatoes or grain

Examples

After dinner, Hans offered his guests a glass of schnaps to warm them up.
She brought back a bottle of schnaps from her trip to Austria as a souvenir.
The traditional festival featured homemade sausages and locally distilled schnaps for everyone to taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brandy - a strong alcoholic spirit distilled from wine or fermented fruit juice

Etymology

From German 'Schnaps', meaning 'a sip' or 'liquor'.
