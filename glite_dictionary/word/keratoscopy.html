keratoscopy

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɛrəˈtɑskəpi / (AmE), / ˌkɛrəˈtɒskəpi / (BrE)
The examination of the cornea using a keratoscope to identify irregularities or defects in its anterior surface.
examination of the cornea with a keratoscope
medical

Examples

The ophthalmologist performed a keratoscopy to assess the patient's corneal surface for any distortions.
Modern keratoscopy techniques allow for precise detection of slight corneal irregularities.
After her eye injury, the doctor recommended keratoscopy to check for hidden defects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keratometer - an instrument used to measure the curvature of the anterior surface of the cornea

Etymology

From Greek 'keras' (horn, relating to the cornea) and 'skopein' (to look, to examine).
