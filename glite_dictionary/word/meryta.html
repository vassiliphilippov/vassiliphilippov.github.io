Meryta

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛrɪtə / (AmE), / ˈmɛrɪtə / (BrE)
A genus of small to medium evergreen trees, native to oceanic climates, often called puka.
genus of evergreen oceanic trees

Examples

The Meryta species are commonly found in the South Pacific islands.
Scientists studied the leaves of the Meryta to understand their unique adaptations to oceanic climates.
A mature Meryta can serve as an important windbreak in coastal gardens.

Etymology

From Latinized genus name coined by botanists, used in scientific classification.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛrɪtə / (AmE), / ˈmɛrɪtə / (BrE)
a genus of small to medium evergreen dioecious trees native to oceanic regions, including the puka tree
genus of oceanic evergreen trees

Examples

The Meryta genus includes the distinctive puka tree native to the South Pacific islands.
Botanists have recently discovered a new species within the Meryta genus in French Polynesia.
Many members of the Meryta genus are valued for their decorative foliage in tropical gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puka - a common name for species in the genus Meryta, especially Meryta sinclairii

Etymology

Named after French botanist Mery; genus name assigned in modern botanical Latin.
