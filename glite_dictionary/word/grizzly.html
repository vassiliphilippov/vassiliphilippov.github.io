grizzly

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɪz.li / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪz.li / (BrE)
a large, powerful brownish-gray bear found in the uplands of western North America, known for its size and distinct shoulder hump
a large North American bear

Examples

We were lucky enough to spot a grizzly near the river during our hike.
The grizzly is renowned for its strength and impressive size.
Park rangers warn campers about proper food storage in grizzly country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brown bear - a species of bear found across Eurasia and North America; grizzly is a subspecies

Etymology

From grizzle, meaning 'gray' or 'grizzled', referring to its fur coloration.
