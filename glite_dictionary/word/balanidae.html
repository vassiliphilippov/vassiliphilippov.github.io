Balanidae

1. NOUN

/ bəˈlænɪˌdiː / (AmE), / bəˈlanɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of stalkless barnacles found in marine environments, characterized by their conical shells and attachment to hard surfaces.
family of stalkless barnacles

Examples

The Balanidae are commonly found attached to rocks and ship hulls.
Scientists study various species within the Balanidae family to understand marine colonization.
Many members of the Balanidae play an important ecological role in coastal ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barnacle - a type of marine crustacean with a hard shell

Etymology

From New Latin 'Balanidae', based on 'Balanus', type genus from Greek 'balanos' meaning 'acorn'.
