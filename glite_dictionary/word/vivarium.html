vivarium

1. NOUN

/ vɪˈværiəm / (AmE), / vɪˈvɛːriəm / (BrE)
an enclosure, typically indoors, designed for keeping and observing living animals or plants under natural conditions
enclosure for observing live animals or plants

Examples

The biology class maintained a vivarium filled with frogs and salamanders for observation.
She carefully arranged plants, insects, and rocks in her homemade vivarium.
Museum visitors were fascinated by the butterfly vivarium in the children's discovery center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrarium - an enclosure for keeping land animals or plants, usually terrestrial species
  • Synonym: aquarium - a water-filled enclosure for keeping aquatic animals or plants

Etymology

From Latin 'vivarium' (place of life), from 'vivus' (alive).
