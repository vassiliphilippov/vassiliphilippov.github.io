sensing

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of becoming aware of something through the senses
perception through the senses

Examples

The cat’s sensing of danger made it run away before the dog arrived.
Modern technology often relies on sensing changes in the environment.
Her sharp sensing of other people’s moods helped her navigate social situations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perception - the ability to see, hear, or become aware of something through the senses

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of perceiving or detecting that something has happened or a particular state exists
perceiving or detecting occurrence or state

Examples

Accurate sensing is crucial for modern robotic systems to interact safely with their environment.
Her sensing of danger proved to be justified when the storm arrived unexpectedly.
The device is capable of remote temperature sensing in hazardous environments.

Synset Examples

early detection can often lead to a cure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detection - the action or process of identifying the presence of something
  • Degree Lower: intuition - the ability to understand something instinctively

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'sense' plus the suffix '-ing', denoting the action or process.
