A-bomb

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ bɑm / (AmE), / ˈeɪ bɒm / (BrE)
a nuclear weapon that releases energy by splitting the nuclei of heavy elements such as uranium or plutonium; atomic bomb
atomic bomb; fission nuclear weapon

Examples

The threat of the A-bomb changed the course of international relations in the twentieth century.
Scientists raced to develop the A-bomb during World War II.
The devastation caused by the A-bomb in Hiroshima is remembered to this day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atomic bomb - a bomb whose destructive power comes from fission of atomic nuclei
  • Degree Higher: hydrogen bomb - a nuclear weapon using fusion of isotopes of hydrogen

Etymology

Shortened form of 'atomic bomb'.
