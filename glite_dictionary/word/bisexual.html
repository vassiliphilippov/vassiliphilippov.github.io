bisexual

1. ADJ

/ baɪˈsɛkʃuəl / (AmE), / baɪˈsɛksjʊəl / (BrE)
sexually attracted to both men and women; relating to or characteristic of bisexuality
sexually attracted to both men and women

Examples

She openly identified as bisexual during college, talking about her relationships with both men and women.
In recent years, more bisexual characters have appeared in popular films and television series.
He found acceptance among a community that included gay, lesbian, and bisexual members.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: heterosexual - sexually attracted to people of the opposite sex
  • Antonym: homosexual - sexually attracted to people of the same sex

Etymology

From Latin 'bis' (twice) + 'sexualis' (sexual).

2. NOUN

/ baɪˈsɛkʃuəl / (AmE), / baɪˈsɛksjʊəl / (BrE)
a person who is sexually attracted to both men and women
person attracted to both sexes

Examples

Sarah came out as bisexual during her first year at university.
He identifies as bisexual and is active in the LGBTQ+ community.
More people are openly describing themselves as bisexual today than in previous generations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: heterosexual - a person sexually attracted to people of the opposite sex
  • Antonym: homosexual - a person sexually attracted to people of the same sex
  • Synonym: pansexual - a person who is attracted to people regardless of gender

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'sexual' (relating to sex).
