homogenized

1. ADJ

/ həˈmɑdʒəˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / həˈmɒdʒɪnaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a substance) made uniform by blending unlike elements, especially by breaking down one element into particles and dispersing them evenly throughout another.
made uniform by blending elements

Examples

Many dairy products are homogenized to keep the fat from separating.
The researchers used a homogenized solution to ensure accuracy in their experiments.
Some customers prefer homogenized milk because of its smooth texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uniform - always the same; unvarying in form or character

Etymology

From 'homogenize' + '-ed'; 'homogenize' from Greek 'homogenēs' (of the same kind) via French.

2. ADJ

/ hoʊˈmɑdʒəˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / həˈmɒdʒənaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a substance) made uniform or consistent throughout, especially by thoroughly mixing its components.
made uniform or consistent throughout

Examples

Most milk sold in stores is homogenized to prevent the cream from separating.
The company developed a homogenized solution for use in pharmaceuticals.
These days, many cultures are being homogenized by global media.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homogeneous - consisting of parts all of the same kind
  • Antonym: heterogeneous - consisting of parts that are different or mixed

Etymology

Formed from 'homogenize' + -ed, from Greek 'homogenēs' (of the same kind) + English suffix.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01204160-s
Synset POS: s
