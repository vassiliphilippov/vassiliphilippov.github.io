Avahi

1. NOUN

/ əˈvɑːhi / (AmE), / əˈvɑːhi / (BrE)
A genus of woolly lemurs, native to Madagascar, belonging to the family Indriidae.
a genus of woolly lemurs

Examples

The Avahi is known for its nocturnal lifestyle and slow movements.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the genus Avahi in Madagascar.
All members of Avahi have dense, woolly fur to help them withstand the cool nights.

Etymology

From Malagasy 'avahy', a local name for these lemurs, adopted in scientific Latin.

A genus of woolly lemurs in the family Indriidae, native to Madagascar.
a genus of woolly lemurs

Examples

The Avahi are nocturnal primates found only in Madagascar's forests.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Avahi genus in northern Madagascar.
All members of the Avahi feed primarily on leaves and flowers.

Etymology

From Malagasy Avahi, the local name for the animal.
