chervil

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɜr.vəl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɜː.vɪl / (BrE)
an aromatic annual herb of the parsley family, with finely divided leaves used especially in cooking for flavoring soups and salads
a culinary herb with finely divided leaves

Examples

Fresh chervil adds a delicate flavor to this spring salad.
She garnished the soup with finely chopped chervil leaves.
In French cuisine, chervil is often included in the classic fines herbes mixture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parsley - common culinary herb with flat or curly green leaves

Etymology

From Old French *cerfeuil*, from Latin *chaerephyllum*, from Greek *khairephyllon*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɝːvəl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɜːvɪl / (BrE)
a delicate herb with finely divided leaves, used especially to flavor salads, soups, and egg dishes.
a mild-flavored herb for seasoning

Examples

She sprinkled freshly chopped chervil over the omelet before serving.
The recipe calls for a handful of chervil to be added to the salad.
He prefers chervil to parsley for its milder, anise-like flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parsley - a common garden herb with crinkly or flat leaves, often used as a garnish

Etymology

From Old French *cerfeuil*, from Latin *chaerophyllum*, from Greek *khairephyllon* (meaning rejoicing leaf).
