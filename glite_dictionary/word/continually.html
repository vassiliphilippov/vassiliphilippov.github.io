continually

1. ADV

/ kənˈtɪn.ju.ə.li / (AmE), / kənˈtɪn.ju.ə.li / (BrE)
repeatedly and frequently over a period of time, often with short breaks or interruptions; seemingly without interruption.
frequently repeated; again and again

Examples

He continually checked his phone for new messages throughout the meeting.
The city continually updates its public transportation schedule to improve service.
She continually reminded her team about the project deadline.

Synset Examples

complained continually that there wasn't enough money

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: continuously - without any interruption; without stopping

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French 'continuel', from Latin 'continuus' (uninterrupted, continuous).
