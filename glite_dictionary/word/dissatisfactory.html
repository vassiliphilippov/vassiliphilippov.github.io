dissatisfactory

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɪsˌsætɪsˈfæktɔri / (AmE), / ˌdɪsˌsætɪsˈfæktəri / (BrE)
not as good as expected or required; unsatisfactory.
not meeting expectations; unsatisfactory

Examples

The manager considered the employee's performance to be dissatisfactory and recommended further training.
We received dissatisfactory service at the restaurant, so we decided not to return.
If the results are dissatisfactory, the experiment will need to be repeated.

Synset Examples

a disappointing performance from one who had seemed so promising

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unsatisfactory - not good enough; failing to meet expectations or requirements
  • Antonym: satisfactory - fulfilling expectations or requirements

Etymology

From dis- (expressing reversal) + satisfactory.
