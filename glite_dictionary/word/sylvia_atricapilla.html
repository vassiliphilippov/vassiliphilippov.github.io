Sylvia atricapilla

1. NOUN

/ sɪl.vi.ə ˌæ.trɪˈkæp.ɪ.lə / (AmE), / ˈsɪl.vi.ə ˌæ.trɪˈkæp.ɪ.lə / (BrE)
A small brownish-grey warbler, commonly known as the blackcap, characterized by its distinctive black crown.
a small warbler bird with a black crown

Examples

The distinctive song of the Sylvia atricapilla can be heard throughout European woodlands in spring.
Unlike other warblers, the male Sylvia atricapilla sports a striking black crown on its head.
Many birdwatchers are excited to spot a Sylvia atricapilla during their early morning walks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackcap - common name for Sylvia atricapilla, a small warbler with a black crown

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Sylvia' meaning woodland (from Latin silva 'forest'), and 'atricapilla' from Latin 'ater' (black) + 'capillus' (hair).
