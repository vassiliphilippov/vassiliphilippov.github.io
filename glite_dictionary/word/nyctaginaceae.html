Nyctaginaceae

1. NOUN

/ nɪkˌtædʒɪˈneɪsiː / (AmE), / nɪkˌtadʒɪˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants in the order Caryophyllales, commonly known as the four o'clock family, including many tropical and subtropical species.
family of flowering plants (four o'clock family)

Examples

The family Nyctaginaceae includes the well-known four o'clock flowers.
Some species in the Nyctaginaceae family are cultivated as ornamental plants in gardens.
Botanists study the characteristic floral structures found in Nyctaginaceae.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Nyctago, from Greek nyktí (night), referring to the night-flowering habits of some species.

2. NOUN

/ ˌnɪktəˌdʒaɪnəˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / ˌnɪktəˌdʒɪɡɪˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants in the order Caryophyllales, commonly known as the four o'clock family, including various herbs, shrubs, and small trees
family of flowering plants (four o'clock family)

Examples

The Nyctaginaceae family includes the popular garden plant known as the four o'clock.
Botanists study Nyctaginaceae to better understand flower morphology in the Caryophyllales order.
Many members of Nyctaginaceae thrive in tropical and subtropical regions.

Etymology

From the type genus Nyctaginia, from Greek nyktos (night) and Latin agere (to drive).
