blushful

1. ADJ

/ ˈblʌʃfəl / (AmE), / ˈblʌʃfʊl / (BrE)
Having the color of a blush; tinged with a rosy or pinkish hue.
having a rosy or pinkish color

Examples

The morning sky was veiled in blushful clouds at sunrise.
Her cheeks took on a blushful tint as she spoke.
The artist painted the petals in blushful shades, giving them a lifelike delicacy.

Synset Examples

blushful mists

Etymology

Formed from 'blush' (to turn red) + '-ful' (full of).

2. ADJ

/ ˈblʌʃ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈblʌʃ.fəl / (BrE)
(of a person) having a red face due to embarrassment, shame, agitation, or emotional upset.
showing a red face from emotion

Examples

The blushful boy couldn't meet her gaze after the compliment.
Her blushful expression gave away her secret crush.
He was so blushful when called on in class that everyone noticed.

Synset Examples

the blushing boy was brought before the Principal
her blushful beau
was red-faced with anger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blushing - showing a red face due to embarrassment or shyness
  • Synonym: red-faced - having a red or flushed face, especially from embarrassment or anger

Etymology

Formed from 'blush' (from Old English 'blyscan', to redden in the face) + '-ful' (full of).
