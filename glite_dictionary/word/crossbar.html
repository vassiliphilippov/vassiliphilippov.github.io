crossbar

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔsˌbɑr / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsbɑː / (BrE)
a long, thin, horizontal bar fixed between two vertical posts, especially as part of a structure or frame
horizontal bar between two vertical posts

Examples

The soccer ball struck the crossbar and bounced back into play.
Workers installed a steel crossbar for extra support between the posts.
The child swung from the playground's crossbar and laughed happily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upright - a vertical post that supports a crossbar

Etymology

From 'cross' + 'bar', meaning a bar that crosses between two points.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔsˌbɑr / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌbɑː / (BrE)
a horizontal bar used in sports that athletes must jump or vault over, especially in high jump or pole vault events
horizontal bar to be jumped or vaulted over in sports

Examples

The athlete cleared the crossbar with ease during the pole vault competition.
Officials raised the crossbar to a new height after each successful jump.
If you touch the crossbar during your jump, the attempt does not count.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bar - a long, rigid piece of metal or wood

Etymology

From 'cross' (across) + 'bar', indicating a bar set horizontally.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03140439-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔsˌbɑr / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsbɑː / (BrE)
a horizontal bar that goes across something, especially as part of a structure or frame
horizontal bar across an object

Examples

The goalkeeper leaped and tipped the ball onto the crossbar to prevent a goal.
She steadied herself by gripping the crossbar of the bicycle.
The window had a sturdy crossbar for extra security.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beam - a long, sturdy piece of wood or metal used in construction

Etymology

From cross + bar, indicating a bar that crosses another element.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03140568-n
Synset POS: n
