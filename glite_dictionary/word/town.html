town

1. NOUN

/ taʊn / (AmE), / taʊn / (BrE)
all the people living in a municipality, especially one smaller than a city.
the residents of a municipality

Examples

The whole town attended the annual parade in the main square.
After the championship win, the town celebrated late into the night.
News spread quickly through the town as everyone discussed the mayor's announcement.

Synset Examples

the whole town cheered the team

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: city - a large town, typically with its own government
  • Degree Lower: village - a small group of houses in a rural area

Etymology

From Old English 'tūn', meaning enclosure or settlement.

2. NOUN

/ taʊn / (AmE), / taʊn / (BrE)
An urban area with a fixed boundary that is smaller than a city.
urban area smaller than a city

Examples

She has lived in this town all her life and knows everyone.
After graduating, he moved from a small town to the big city.
The town holds a festival every summer that attracts many visitors.

Synset Examples

they drive through town on their way to work

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: city - a large urban area, typically larger than a town
  • Degree Lower: village - a small cluster of houses in the countryside, smaller than a town

Etymology

From Old English 'tūn' (enclosure, settlement), related to Old Norse 'tūn'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08683242-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ taʊn / (AmE), / taʊn / (BrE)
An administrative division of a county, especially one with its own local government and responsibilities.
administrative division of a county

Examples

The town has the authority to enact its own zoning laws.
Our town provides public services such as libraries and parks.
Residents attended the town meeting to discuss proposed budget changes.

Synset Examples

the town is responsible for snow removal

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: city - large and generally more autonomous municipal area
  • Degree Lower: village - smaller rural settlement, often less autonomous

Etymology

From Old English tūn (enclosure, settlement).

Source: WordNet
