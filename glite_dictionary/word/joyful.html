joyful

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɔɪ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔɪ.fəl / (BrE)
feeling, expressing, or causing great happiness or delight
full of happiness or delight

Examples

She had a joyful smile after hearing the good news.
The children were joyful as they played in the park.
His speech ended on a joyful note, inspiring everyone in the room.

Synset Examples

a joyful heart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: happy - feeling or showing pleasure or contentment
  • Antonym: miserable - very unhappy or uncomfortable

Etymology

From Middle English 'joiful', from Old French 'joie' (joy) + '-ful'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɔɪ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔɪ.fəl / (BrE)
feeling, expressing, or causing great happiness or delight.
feeling or showing great happiness

Examples

She was joyful when she heard the good news.
Children ran around the park, their joyful laughter filling the air.
It was a joyful reunion after years of being apart.

Synset Examples

make a joyful noise
a joyful occasion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: happy - feeling or showing pleasure or contentment
  • Antonym: sad - feeling or showing sorrow; unhappy
  • Degree Higher: ecstatic - feeling or expressing overwhelming happiness
  • Degree Lower: content - in a state of peaceful happiness

Etymology

From joy + -ful, based on Old French joie (joy) and English suffix -ful (full of).
