disgustingness

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈɡʌstɪŋnəs / (AmE), / dɪsˈɡʌstɪŋnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being extremely unpleasant, revolting, or offensive to the senses or feelings
quality of being disgusting

Examples

The disgustingness of the spoiled food made it impossible to eat even a single bite.
She recoiled at the disgustingness of the dirty bathroom.
He described the movie's gore scenes with a sense of fascinated disgustingness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repulsiveness - quality of causing strong dislike or disgust
  • Antonym: deliciousness - quality of being highly pleasant to the taste

2. NOUN

/ dɪsˈɡʌstɪŋnəs / (AmE), / dɪsˈɡʌstɪŋnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being extremely unpleasant, offensive, or revolting to the mind or senses
the quality of being disgusting

Examples

The disgustingness of the spoiled food was enough to make everyone leave the room.
Many people agreed that the disgustingness of the situation was beyond belief.
She couldn't hide her reaction to the disgustingness she witnessed in the neglected apartment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgust - a strong feeling of dislike or revulsion
  • Antonym: attractiveness - the quality of being pleasing or appealing

Etymology

From 'disgusting' + '-ness', forming nouns denoting a state or quality.
