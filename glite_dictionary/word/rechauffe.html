rechauffe

1. NOUN

/ reɪˈʃoʊfeɪ / (AmE), / reɪˈʃɒfeɪ / (BrE)
A dish made from warmed-up leftovers, typically using food prepared earlier.
warmed leftover dish

Examples

Last night's roast beef became a delicious rechauffe for lunch today.
The chef cleverly disguised the rechauffe so guests wouldn't know it was leftovers.
In French cuisine, a rechauffe can be just as satisfying as the original meal.

Etymology

From French réchauffé, past participle of réchauffer (to reheat).
