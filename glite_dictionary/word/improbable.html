improbable

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈprɑːbəbl̩ / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɒbəbl̩ / (BrE)
Not likely to be true or to happen.
unlikely to happen or be true

Examples

His excuse for being late was so improbable that no one believed him.
Winning the lottery twice in one week is highly improbable.
The theory sounded interesting but seemed improbable given the available data.

Synset Examples

legislation on the question is highly unlikely
an improbable event

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unlikely - not likely to happen or be true
  • Antonym: probable - likely to happen or be true

Etymology

From Latin 'improbabilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'probabilis' (likely, credible).

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈprɑːbəbəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɒbəbəl / (BrE)
not likely to be true or to happen; having a very low chance of occurring.
unlikely to happen or be true

Examples

It seemed improbable that he would arrive on time given the traffic.
The team made an improbable comeback in the last few minutes of the game.
She dismissed the rumor as highly improbable and not worth worrying about.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unlikely - not likely to happen or be true
  • Antonym: probable - likely to happen or be true
  • Degree Higher: impossible - not able to occur or be done

Etymology

From Latin 'improbabilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'probabilis' (provable, likely).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01805119-s
Synset POS: s
