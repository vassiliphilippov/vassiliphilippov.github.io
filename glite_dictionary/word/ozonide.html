ozonide

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊzəˌnaɪd / (AmE), / ˈəʊzənaɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound formed by the reaction of ozone with an unsaturated compound, typically unstable and containing the O3 group
unstable compound formed by addition of ozone
medical

Examples

Researchers studied the decomposition of the ozonide under controlled laboratory conditions.
The reaction between cyclohexene and ozone produces an ozonide intermediate.
Most ozonide compounds are too unstable to be stored for long periods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ozone - a form of oxygen containing three atoms per molecule (O3)

Etymology

From ozone + -ide, denoting a derived chemical compound.
