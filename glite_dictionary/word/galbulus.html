galbulus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæl.bjʊ.ləs / (AmE), / ˈɡal.bjʊ.ləs / (BrE)
the fleshy, seed-producing cone of a cypress tree or a similar plant
seed cone of a cypress tree

Examples

The blue galbulus of the cypress is often mistaken for a berry.
Each galbulus contains numerous seeds that mature in the second year.
Botanists collected several galbuli for further study under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cone - the reproductive structure of certain plants, especially conifers

Etymology

From Latin *galbulus* (small round fruit or berry).
