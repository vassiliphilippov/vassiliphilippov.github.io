Prunus incisa

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs ɪnˈsaɪsə / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs ɪnˈsiːsə / (BrE)
A shrubby Japanese cherry tree with pale pink blossoms, often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
a Japanese cherry tree with pale pink blossoms

Examples

The Prunus incisa adds a delicate touch to many Japanese gardens in early spring.
Gardeners admire the pale pink blossoms of Prunus incisa each year.
We planted a Prunus incisa near the pond to enjoy its springtime flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cherry blossom - the flower of any cherry tree, especially ornamental Japanese varieties
  • Synonym: Prunus serrulata - another species of Japanese cherry tree

Etymology

From Latin, 'prunus' meaning 'plum/tree' and 'incisa' meaning 'cut into, incised', referring to the nature of its leaves.
