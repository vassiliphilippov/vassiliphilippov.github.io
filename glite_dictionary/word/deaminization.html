deaminization

1. NOUN

/ diˌæmɪnəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌamɪnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the removal of an amino group from an amino acid or another amino compound
removal of amino group from a compound
medical

Examples

The liver is primarily responsible for deaminization of amino acids during metabolism.
Enzymes facilitate the deaminization process in cells, ensuring excess amino groups are safely removed.
A defect in deaminization could lead to the accumulation of toxic substances in tissues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deamination - removal of an amino group, especially from an amino acid

Etymology

From de- (removal) + amine + -ization (process).
