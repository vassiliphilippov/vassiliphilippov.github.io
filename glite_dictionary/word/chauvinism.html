chauvinism

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃoʊvɪnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊvɪnɪzəm / (BrE)
Fanatical or aggressive patriotism, especially when it is prejudiced and unreasonable.
fanatical patriotism

Examples

The politician's chauvinism alienated those who favored peaceful international relations.
Many critics argued that the film promoted a dangerous form of chauvinism.
Her chauvinism became apparent whenever she spoke about her homeland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nationalism - strong identification with one's own nation
  • Synonym: patriotism - love and devotion to one's country
  • Antonym: internationalism - principle of cooperation among nations

Etymology

From French 'chauvinisme', after Nicolas Chauvin, a legendary excessively patriotic soldier in the Napoleonic army.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃoʊ.vəˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊ.vɪˌnɪz.əm / (BrE)
the belief that one gender, typically men, is naturally superior to the other, especially expressed in attitudes or behavior towards women
belief in the superiority of one gender, especially men

Examples

Her manager's blatant chauvinism made it difficult for women to advance in the company.
Many people today are working to challenge and eradicate chauvinism in the workplace.
The protestors called out the government's chauvinism towards female citizens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sexism - prejudice or discrimination based on sex or gender
  • Antonym: egalitarianism - belief in the equality of all people, especially in political, social, or economic life

Etymology

From French 'chauvinisme', named after Nicolas Chauvin, a legendary excessively patriotic soldier.
