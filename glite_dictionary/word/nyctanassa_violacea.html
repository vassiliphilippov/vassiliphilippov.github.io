Nyctanassa violacea

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɪktəˈnæsə vaɪ.oʊˈleɪsiə / (AmE), / ˌnɪktəˈnæsə vaɪ.əˈleɪsɪə / (BrE)
A species of night heron native to the Americas, known as the yellow-crowned night heron, characterized by its distinctive yellowish head markings and nocturnal habits.
a North American night heron species

Examples

The Nyctanassa violacea is easily identified by its yellow-crowned head and striking appearance.
Researchers have observed that Nyctanassa violacea prefers coastal wetlands and marshes for nesting.
Because Nyctanassa violacea is mainly nocturnal, it often goes unnoticed by casual birdwatchers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellow-crowned night heron - common name for Nyctanassa violacea

Etymology

From Greek 'nykt-', meaning 'night', and 'anassa', meaning 'queen', combined with the species name from Latin 'violacea' (violet-colored).
