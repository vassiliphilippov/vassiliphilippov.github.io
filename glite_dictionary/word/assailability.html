assailability

1. NOUN

/ əˌseɪləˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / əˌseɪləˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being vulnerable to attack or capable of being assaulted.
vulnerability to attack

Examples

Their plan failed due to the assailability of their defense systems.
She analyzed the company’s assailability and suggested ways to strengthen its security.
The general was concerned about the assailability of the fortress walls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vulnerability - the quality or state of being exposed to attack or harm
  • Antonym: invulnerability - the quality of being immune to attack or damage

Etymology

From assailable + -ity; ultimately from Old French *assaillir* (to attack).
