Nu Zillund

1. PROPN

/ nuː ˈzɪlənd / (AmE), / njuː ˈzɪlənd / (BrE)
A humorous or colloquial imitation of the New Zealand accent when saying 'New Zealand.'
colloquial imitation of 'New Zealand'
informal

Examples

He moved to Nu Zillund for a fresh start after university.
Some comedians love to joke about how people from Nu Zillund pronounce certain words.
Travelers often say Nu Zillund has breathtaking landscapes and unique wildlife.

Synset Examples

They say Nu Zillund has some of the friendliest people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: New Zealand - A country in the southwestern Pacific Ocean

Etymology

Humorous respelling from the local English pronunciation of 'New Zealand.'
