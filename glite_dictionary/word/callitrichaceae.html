Callitrichaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌkælɪtrɪˈkeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌkælɪtrɪˈkeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of dicotyledonous aquatic herbs, commonly known as the water-starwort family, found in freshwater habitats worldwide
family of aquatic dicotyledonous herbs

Examples

The Callitrichaceae are often found in clean, slow-moving streams across Europe and North America.
Botanists recently reclassified some genera previously included in the Callitrichaceae.
Many species within the Callitrichaceae serve as important indicators of freshwater ecosystem health.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Callitriche, from Greek kallos (beauty) and thrix (hair).
