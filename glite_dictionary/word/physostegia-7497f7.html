physostegia

1. NOUN

/ ˌfaɪsəˈstiːdʒiə / (AmE), / ˌfɪsɒˈstiːdʒɪə / (BrE)
A genus of North American plants in the mint family with narrow leaves and showy white, pink, or lavender flowers, commonly known as false dragonhead.
a genus of flowering plants in the mint family

Examples

The physostegia in our garden bloomed with vibrant lavender flowers all summer.
Gardeners often choose physostegia for its long-lasting, showy spikes of blossoms.
Native bees are attracted to the abundant nectar of physostegia flowers.

Etymology

From New Latin Physostegia, from Greek physa (bladder) + stegos (cover), referring to the inflated calyx.
