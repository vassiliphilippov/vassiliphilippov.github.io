modern

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɑːdərn / (AmE), / ˈmɒdən / (BrE)
(of a language) being the current or most recent stage in its development, as opposed to earlier forms.
of the current stage of a language

Examples

The novel is written in Modern English, making it accessible to contemporary readers.
She specializes in the study of Modern Greek, as opposed to ancient Greek.
Hebrew underwent significant changes before becoming Modern Hebrew.

Synset Examples

Modern English
New Hebrew is Israeli Hebrew

Related Terms

  • Antonym: archaic - relating to an earlier stage of a language
  • Antonym: classical - referring to the historically established stage of an ancient language

Etymology

From Latin modernus, from modo (just now, recently).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːdərn / (AmE), / ˈmɒd.ən / (BrE)
A style of typeface characterized by regular shapes, thin hairline serifs, and strong vertical strokes, based on 18th-century designs such as that of Gianbattista Bodoni.
a typeface with thin serifs and strong verticals

Examples

The headline stood out in bold modern type across the front page.
He prefers to use a traditional modern font for all his print materials.
Many designers admire the elegance of a modern typeface in their layouts.

3. ADJ

/ ˈmɑːdɚn / (AmE), / ˈmɒdən / (BrE)
relating to contemporary styles, ideas, or movements in art, music, literature, or architecture, especially those that depart from traditional forms

Examples

The exhibition features modern paintings that break away from historical traditions.

His taste in music is very modern, favoring experimental and electronic sounds.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: traditional - following or relating to older styles or conventions

Etymology

From Latin 'modernus' (of today), from 'modo' (just now, recently).
