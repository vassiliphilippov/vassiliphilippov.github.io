synaesthesia

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪniˈsθiʒə / (AmE), / ˌsɪniˈzθiːziə / (BrE)
A condition in which the stimulation of one sense leads to automatic, involuntary experiences in a second sense, such as seeing colors when hearing music.
condition where senses blend

Examples

The artist claimed that her synaesthesia allowed her to paint music as colors on the canvas.
Some people with synaesthesia might taste flavors when they hear certain words.
His experience of synaesthesia made reading letters appear as vibrant colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synesthesia - Alternate (American) spelling of 'synaesthesia'

Etymology

From Greek 'syn-' (together) + 'aisthēsis' (sensation).
