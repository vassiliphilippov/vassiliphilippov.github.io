indeed

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈdid / (AmE), / ɪnˈdiːd / (BrE)
used to emphasize a statement or response, or to confirm and stress the truth of a preceding statement
used to emphasize or confirm a statement

Examples

The new policy is, indeed, a significant improvement over the old one.
She is indeed one of the most talented artists of her generation.
It was cold indeed when we arrived at the mountain cabin.

Synset Examples

they said the car would break down and indeed it did
it is very cold indeed
was indeed grateful
indeed, the rain may still come
he did so do it!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certainly - used to show that something is definitely true or will definitely happen
  • Synonym: really - used to emphasize the truth or reality of something

Etymology

From Middle English 'indede' (in fact), from 'in' + 'dede' (deed, action).

2. ADV

/ ɪnˈdid / (AmE), / ɪnˈdiːd / (BrE)
used as an interjection to express surprise, skepticism, or irony
expression of surprise or irony

Examples

He said he would pay for dinner? Indeed! I’ll believe it when I see it.
‘You’ve finished all the work already?’ ‘Indeed!’ she replied with a grin.
You think you can beat me at chess, indeed!

Synset Examples

Wants to marry the butler? Indeed!

Etymology

From Middle English 'indeed', equivalent to 'in' + 'deed' (in fact, in truth).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00037864-r
Synset POS: r
