synoicous

1. ADJ

/ sɪˈnɔɪəkəs / (AmE), / sɪˈnɔɪəkəs / (BrE)
(of some bryophytes) having male and female reproductive organs together in the same gametoecium.
male and female organs in the same gametoecium
medical

Examples

Many mosses are synoicous, producing both sperm and eggs within the same structure.
A synoicous gametoecium maximizes the chances of successful reproduction in certain bryophytes.
In contrast to dioicous species, synoicous bryophytes house both sexual organs together.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dioicous - having male and female organs on separate individuals (bryology)
  • Antonym: monoicous - having separate male and female organs on the same individual (bryology)

Etymology

From Greek syn- (together) and oikos (house).
