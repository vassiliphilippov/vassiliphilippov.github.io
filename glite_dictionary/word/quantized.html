quantized

1. ADJ

/ ˈkwɑn.taɪzd / (AmE), / ˈkwɒn.taɪzd / (BrE)
(of a property, value, or process) existing or occurring only in discrete amounts or states, not in a continuous range, especially as related to quantum theory.
existing in discrete or fixed states

Examples

The energy levels of electrons in an atom are quantized, meaning only specific values are allowed.
Digital audio signals are quantized during the process of analog-to-digital conversion.
A quantized magnetic field allows the phenomenon of flux quantization in superconductors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: continuous - existing without breaks; not discrete

Etymology

From 'quantum', after Latin 'quantus' (how much), with '-ize' and past participle '-ed'.
