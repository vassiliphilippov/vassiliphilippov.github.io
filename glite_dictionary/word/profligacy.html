profligacy

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.flə.ɡə.si / (AmE), / ˈprɒf.lɪ.ɡə.si / (BrE)
reckless or immoral extravagance, especially in pursuit of sensual pleasure or luxury
immoral extravagance or sensual indulgence

Examples

The millionaire's profligacy was legendary, with lavish parties held every night at his mansion.
Years of profligacy eventually led to his financial ruin and social disgrace.
Critics blamed the nation's economic crisis on government profligacy and unchecked spending.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dissipation - overindulgence in sensual pleasures; debauchery
  • Antonym: self-restraint - control over one's own actions; moderation

Etymology

From Latin profligatus (overthrown, corrupt), from profligare (to overthrow, ruin).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.flɪ.ɡə.si / (AmE), / ˈprɒf.lɪ.ɡə.si / (BrE)
reckless extravagance or wastefulness in the use of resources
reckless extravagance or wastefulness

Examples

Years of government profligacy left the country with a massive debt.
His profligacy at the casino shocked even his wealthiest friends.
Corporate profligacy can lead to financial collapse if left unchecked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extravagance - excessive spending or lack of restraint
  • Antonym: thrift - careful use of money and resources

Etymology

From Latin profligatus (overthrown, ruined), from profligare (to overthrow).
