offhand

1. ADV

/ ˌɔfˈhænd / (AmE), / ˌɒfˈhænd / (BrE)
without previous thought or preparation; immediately and without careful consideration
without preparation; impromptu

Examples

I can't answer that question offhand, but I'll check and get back to you.
She made a suggestion offhand during the meeting that everyone liked.
He didn't know the exact number offhand, but he thought it was around fifty.

Synset Examples

couldn't give the figures offhand
we decided offhand to go to Canada
she had made these remarks offhandedly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impromptu - done without being planned or rehearsed
  • Synonym: spontaneously - in a way that is not planned, but done freely

Etymology

From the phrase 'off hand', originally referring to something done without using one's hand, hence done without preparation.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɔfˌhænd / (AmE), / ˌɒfˈhænd / (BrE)
(of remarks, actions, etc.) made or done without previous thought or preparation; casual or spontaneous.
done without preparation; spontaneous

Examples

She gave an offhand response to the question, without thinking it through.
His offhand remarks during the meeting surprised everyone with their honesty.
I can't recall the details offhand, but I can look them up for you.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impromptu - done without being planned, organized, or rehearsed
  • Synonym: extemporaneous - spoken or done without preparation
  • Antonym: prepared - done with prior thought or planning

Etymology

From 'off' + 'hand', originally referring to something done off the hand, i.e., immediately, without delay or preparation.

3. ADJ

/ ˌɔfˈhænd / (AmE), / ˌɒfˈhænd / (BrE)
not showing much thought, interest, or concern; done or said without care or consideration; casually thoughtless or inconsiderate.
casually thoughtless or inconsiderate

Examples

He gave an offhand response that made her feel ignored.
Janet's offhand attitude offended her colleagues during the meeting.
The waiter seemed offhand when taking our order, barely making eye contact.

Synset Examples

an offhand manner
she treated most men with offhand contempt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: careless - failing to give sufficient attention or thought
  • Antonym: thoughtful - showing careful consideration for others

Etymology

From the phrase 'off hand', meaning 'improvised' or 'without preparation', first attested in the 18th century.

Source: WordNet

4. ADV

/ ˌɔfˈhænd / (AmE), / ˌɒfˈhænd / (BrE)
in a casually inconsiderate or unprepared manner; without previous thought or consideration
in a casual or unprepared manner

Examples

He dismissed the question offhand, not giving it a second thought.
She made an offhand remark that offended several people in the room.
When asked about the budget, he answered offhand without checking any facts.

Synset Examples

replied offhand, his mind a million miles away
she threw him over offhandedly without even a Dear-John letter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offhandedly - in an offhand manner
  • Antonym: deliberately - in a careful and intentional manner
  • Synonym: spontaneously - happening without planning

Etymology

From the phrase 'off hand', originally meaning 'at once' or 'without delay', later evolving to 'without preparation'.
