isomorphism

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsəˈmɔrˌfɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈmɔː.fɪ.zəm / (BrE)
(Biology) similarity or identity of form, shape, or structure between organisms or their parts.
similarity of form or structure (biology)

Examples

The two species display a remarkable isomorphism in their root structures despite being distantly related.
Researchers observed isomorphism between the leaves of certain plants and those of unrelated species.
In biology, isomorphism can help in understanding convergent evolution patterns.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: homomorphism - resemblance in structure but not necessarily in function
  • Antonym: heteromorphism - dissimilarity of form or structure among organisms

Etymology

From Greek isos (equal) + morphē (form) + -ism.
