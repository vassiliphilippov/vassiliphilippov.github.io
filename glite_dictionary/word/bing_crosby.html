Bing Crosby

1. PROPN

/ bɪŋ ˈkrɑzbi / (AmE), / bɪŋ ˈkrɒzbi / (BrE)
(1903–1977) an American singer and film actor, known for his smooth baritone voice and influential recordings, especially in the mid-20th century.
American singer and actor (1903–1977)

Examples

Bing Crosby was famous for his recording of 'White Christmas,' which became one of the best-selling singles of all time.
My grandfather listened to Bing Crosby on the radio every weekend as a child.
Classic holiday movies often feature performances by Bing Crosby.

Etymology

'Bing' was a childhood nickname; 'Crosby' is his family name of Irish origin.
