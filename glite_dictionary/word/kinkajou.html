kinkajou

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪŋkəˌdʒu / (AmE), / ˈkɪŋkədʒuː / (BrE)
a small, nocturnal, arboreal mammal of Central and South America, with a long prehensile tail and a diet mainly of fruit
arboreal mammal of Central/South America

Examples

The kinkajou uses its long tail to balance as it climbs through the rainforest canopy.
Unlike many mammals, the kinkajou is most active at night when it searches for fruit.
A kinkajou was spotted feeding on bananas in the wildlife reserve.

Etymology

From French 'kinkajou', from a native word in Guiana.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɪŋkəˌdʒu / (AmE), / ˈkɪŋkədʒuː / (BrE)
A small nocturnal mammal found in Central and South America, related to raccoons and known for its long prehensile tail and golden-brown fur.
nocturnal mammal of Central/South America

Examples

The kinkajou used its long tail to hang from a tree branch while eating fruit.
She spotted a kinkajou curled up in the canopy during her rainforest trek.
Many children are fascinated by the playful nature of the kinkajou at the zoo.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: potto - A slow-moving nocturnal primate from Africa, Perodicticus potto

Etymology

From French 'kinkajou', ultimately from a Central American indigenous language, possibly Guaraní.
