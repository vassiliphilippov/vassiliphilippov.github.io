urtication

1. NOUN

/ ɜːr.tɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ɜː.tɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a stinging or prickling sensation on the skin, similar to the feeling caused by nettle stings
a stinging or prickling skin sensation

Examples

Exposure to the plant resulted in an intense urtication that lasted for several hours.
Some people experience urtication after contact with certain chemicals or plants.
The doctor described her symptoms as classic urtication following a nettle sting.

Etymology

From Latin urtica (nettle) + -ation.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɜrtɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɜːtɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
an itchy skin rash with raised weals and red margins, often caused by an allergic reaction
itchy skin rash with weals
medical

Examples

After eating shrimp, she developed urtication on her arms and legs.
The doctor diagnosed the red, itchy patches as urtication caused by an allergic reaction.
Insect bites often result in urtication that can be relieved with antihistamines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hives - skin condition marked by itchy, raised weals
  • Degree Higher: rash - eruption of the skin with reddening or swelling

Etymology

From Latin urtica (nettle), referring to the stinging sensation similar to a nettle sting.
