Galician

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈlɪʃən / (AmE), / ɡəˈlɪʃən / (BrE)
the Romance language spoken in the region of Galicia in northwestern Spain
the language of Galicia, Spain

Examples

She learned to speak Galician during her year abroad in Santiago de Compostela.
The poem was originally written in Galician before being translated into English.
Many residents of northwestern Spain are bilingual in both Spanish and Galician.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Portuguese - the Romance language spoken in Portugal and Brazil

Etymology

From Galicia, the region in Spain, plus -an.
