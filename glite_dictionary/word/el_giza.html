El Giza

1. PROPN

/ ɛl ˈɡiːzə / (AmE), / ɛl ˈɡiːzə / (BrE)
an ancient city in Egypt on the west bank of the Nile, opposite Cairo, famous for the three Great Pyramids and the Sphinx.
ancient Egyptian city with pyramids and Sphinx

Examples

The Great Pyramid of El Giza is one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.
Tourists flock to El Giza every year to marvel at the Sphinx and the pyramids.
Archaeologists have made significant discoveries at the site of El Giza.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cairo - the capital city of Egypt, located opposite El Giza across the Nile

Etymology

From Arabic الجيزة (al-Jīza), meaning 'the plateau' or 'the crossing'.
