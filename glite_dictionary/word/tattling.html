tattling

1. ADJ

/ ˈtætlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtatlɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person) habitually divulging secrets or confidential information, especially in a petty or gossipy manner.
habitually revealing secrets

Examples

The tattling child was always quick to report on her classmates' mistakes.
No one liked to share secrets with the tattling neighbor down the hall.
A tattling employee can quickly undermine trust within a team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gossipy - habitually spreading rumors or private matters
  • Antonym: discreet - careful not to reveal private information

Etymology

From the verb 'tattle', which likely comes from imitative origins reflecting the sound of chatter.
