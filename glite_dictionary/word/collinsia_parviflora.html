Collinsia parviflora

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪnziə pɑrˈvɪflərə / (AmE), / kəˈlɪnziə pɑːˈvɪflərə / (BrE)
A small branching plant native to western North America, with tiny blue-and-white flowers; also known as maiden blue-eyed Mary.
a small western North American plant with blue-and-white flowers

Examples

The meadow was dotted with Collinsia parviflora, adding a delicate blue-and-white hue to the landscape.
Botanists collected samples of Collinsia parviflora during their survey of the Colorado foothills.
In early spring, Collinsia parviflora blooms can be found along shaded woodland trails.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Collinsia', honoring American botanist Zaccheus Collins, and Latin 'parviflora' meaning 'small-flowered'.
