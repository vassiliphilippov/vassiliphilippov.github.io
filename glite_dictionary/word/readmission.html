readmission

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːədˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːədˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of being admitted again, especially to a place or organization after having left or been excluded
the act of being admitted again

Examples

The doctor was concerned about the patient's readmission only a week after discharge.
Schools have strict policies regarding the readmission of students after extended absences.
The hospital tracks its readmission rates to measure quality of care.

Synset Examples

the surgery was performed on his readmission to the clinic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admission - the act of allowing someone to enter a place or join an organization

Etymology

From re- (again) + admission.
