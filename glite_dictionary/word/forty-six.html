forty-six

1. NUM

/ ˌfɔrtiˈsɪks / (AmE), / ˌfɔːtiˈsɪks / (BrE)
being six more than forty; the number 46.
the number 46

Examples

She will turn forty-six next month and plans a big celebration.
The company was founded forty-six years ago today.
There were forty-six students who participated in the competition.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: forty-five - being five more than forty; the number 45
  • Degree Higher: forty-seven - being seven more than forty; the number 47

Etymology

From Old English 'fēowertig' (forty) plus 'six'.

2. NUM

/ ˈfɔr.ti sɪks / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.ti sɪks / (BrE)
the number 46; being six more than forty
the number 46

Examples

He turned forty-six years old last week and celebrated with his family.
The team scored forty-six points in the first half of the game.
There were exactly forty-six students who signed up for the workshop.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: forty-five - the number 45; being one less than forty-six
  • Degree Higher: forty-seven - the number 47; being one more than forty-six

Etymology

From Old English 'feowertiʒ' (forty) and 'six' for 'six', forming 'forty-six'.
