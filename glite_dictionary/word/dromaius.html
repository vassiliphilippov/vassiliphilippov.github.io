Dromaius

1. NOUN

/ droʊˈmeɪ.i.əs / (AmE), / drəʊˈmeɪ.i.əs / (BrE)
A genus of large, flightless birds in the order Casuariiformes, including the emu.
genus of birds (includes the emu)

Examples

The genus Dromaius is best known for its sole living species, the emu.
Scientists studied the skeletal structure of fossils in the Dromaius genus.
Within the order Casuariiformes, Dromaius is closely related to the cassowary genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emu - a large, flightless Australian bird in the genus Dromaius

Etymology

From Greek *dromeus* (runner).

2. NOUN

/ droʊˈmaɪəs / (AmE), / drəʊˈmaɪəs / (BrE)
a genus of large, flightless birds in the order Casuariiformes, native to Australia, of which the emu is the only living species
genus of large flightless Australian birds (emus)

Examples

The Dromaius genus is best known for its only living member, the emu.
Scientists classify the emu under the genus Dromaius within the order Casuariiformes.
Fossil records indicate that extinct species of Dromaius once inhabited Tasmania and King Island.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emu - large, flightless bird native to Australia; sole extant species of Dromaius

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek dromaios ('swift, running').
