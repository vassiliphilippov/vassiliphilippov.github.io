voluminous

1. ADJ

/ vəˈluːmɪnəs / (AmE), / vəˈluːmɪnəs / (BrE)
large in volume or bulk; having great size, fullness, or extent
large in volume or size

Examples

The artist wore a voluminous coat to protect himself from the cold studio.
Her voluminous hair attracted a lot of attention at the party.
The library contained voluminous records dating back centuries.

Synset Examples

a voluminous skirt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bulky - taking up much space; large and unwieldy
  • Antonym: compact - closely and neatly packed together; small in size

Etymology

From Latin 'voluminosus', from 'volumen' (roll, scroll).

2. ADJ

/ vəˈluːmənəs / (AmE), / vəˈluːmɪnəs / (BrE)
consisting of great volume, size, or extent; large in number, quantity, or bulk, especially of writing or discourse
large in amount or extent

Examples

The library contained a voluminous collection of ancient manuscripts.
Her voluminous correspondence with friends filled several boxes over the years.
The senator was known for making voluminous speeches during debates.

Synset Examples

she took copious notes
a subject of voluminous legislation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copious - large in quantity; abundant
  • Antonym: scant - barely sufficient or adequate in amount

Etymology

From Latin voluminosus (full of volumes), from volumen (roll, book).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00014877-s
Synset POS: s
