to decide

1. VERB

/ dɪˈsaɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈsaɪd / (BrE)
to cause (someone) to make a decision; to influence or determine someone's choice
cause to make a decision

Examples

Her friend's advice finally decided her to change careers.
The poor weather decided us against going hiking this weekend.
It was the opportunity to travel that decided him to accept the job offer.

Synset Examples

This new development finally decided me!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to determine - to influence or decide the outcome of something

Etymology

From Latin decidere (to cut off, determine), from de- (off) + caedere (to cut).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00701251-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ dɪˈsaɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈsaɪd / (BrE)
to influence or determine the result of something
influence or determine a result

Examples

The committee's choice will decide who wins the grant.
Sometimes a single point can decide an entire match.
The judge's ruling may decide the fate of the new law.

Synset Examples

The vote in New Hampshire often decides the outcome of the Presidential election

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to determine - to decide or establish exactly, especially as a result of research or calculation
  • Synonym: affect - to have an effect on; to make a difference to

Etymology

From Latin 'decidere' (to determine, literally 'to cut off').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00703643-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ dɪˈsaɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈsaɪd / (BrE)
to bring a disagreement or contest to an end by making a final judgment or choice; to settle conclusively
settle or resolve conclusively

Examples

The committee will decide the outcome of the appeal next week.
After hours of debate, the jury finally decided the case.
Sometimes it's difficult for a judge to decide disputes that involve family members.

Synset Examples

The case was decided
The judge decided the case in favor of the plaintiff
The father adjudicated when the sons were quarreling over their inheritance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: settle - to resolve or bring to an end a dispute or uncertainty
  • Synonym: to adjudicate - to make a formal judgment or decision about a problem or disputed matter
  • Antonym: to reopen - to begin again after being closed or resolved

Etymology

From Latin 'decidere', from 'de-' (off) + 'caedere' (to cut).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00700621-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ dɪˈsaɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈsaɪd / (BrE)
to make a choice or judgment about something after thinking about the possibilities
make a choice or judgment

Examples

After much thought, she finally decided to accept the job offer.
The committee will decide which proposal to approve by next week.
He couldn't decide between the red shirt and the blue one.

Synset Examples

We finally decided after lengthy deliberations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to determine - to officially decide something, especially after consideration
  • Synonym: to choose - to select from a number of possibilities
  • Antonym: to hesitate - to be slow to act or decide

Etymology

From Latin 'decidere' (to determine, to cut off), from 'de-' (off) + 'caedere' (to cut).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00699355-v
Synset POS: v
