asperity

1. NOUN

/ əˈspɛr.ə.ti / (AmE), / əˈspɛr.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
harshness or sharpness of tone, temper, or manner; severity in speech or behavior.
harshness of manner or tone

Examples

She spoke with unusual asperity, surprising everyone in the meeting.
The teacher's asperity discouraged the students from asking more questions.
Despite his asperity, his criticism was constructive and fair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: severity - the quality of being severe; harshness
  • Antonym: gentleness - the quality of being gentle; mildness of manner

Etymology

From Latin asperitas (roughness, harshness), from asper (rough).

2. NOUN

/ əˈspɛrəti / (AmE), / əˈspɛrɪti / (BrE)
harshness or severity, especially of conditions or climate; something difficult or unpleasant to endure.
harshness or severity; hardship

Examples

The asperity of life in the Arctic tests even the strongest explorers.
They struggled to survive the asperity of the harsh winter.
Despite the asperity of her circumstances, she remained determined to succeed.

Synset Examples

the asperity of northern winters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hardship - severe suffering or deprivation
  • Antonym: mildness - gentleness or moderation, not severe

Etymology

From Latin asperitas (roughness, harshness), from asper (rough).
