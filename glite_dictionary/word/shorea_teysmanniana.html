Shorea teysmanniana

1. NOUN

/ ʃɔːˈriːə teɪsˌmɑːniˈɑːnə / (AmE), / ʃɔːˈrɪə teɪzˌmænɪˈɑːnə / (BrE)
A valuable timber tree native to the Philippines, belonging to the family Dipterocarpaceae.
Philippine timber tree species

Examples

The wood of the Shorea teysmanniana is highly prized for construction due to its durability.
Efforts are being made to conserve Shorea teysmanniana in its native habitats in the Philippines.
Researchers are studying the growth patterns of Shorea teysmanniana to support sustainable forestry initiatives.

Etymology

Genus name Shorea from Sir John Shore (former Governor-General of India); species name honors botanist Johannes Elias Teijsmann.
