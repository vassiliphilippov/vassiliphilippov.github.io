Spilogale

1. PROPN

/ spaɪˈloʊɡəli / (AmE), / spaɪˈləʊɡəli / (BrE)
A genus of mustelid mammals comprising the spotted skunks, native to North and Central America.
genus of spotted skunks

Examples

The genus Spilogale includes several species of spotted skunks native to the Americas.
Researchers are studying the habitats of Spilogale to understand their distribution.
Unlike other skunks, members of Spilogale have distinctive spotted patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skunk - a small, black-and-white North American mammal known for its strong odor

Etymology

From Greek 'spilos' (spot) and 'gale' (weasel).
