dado

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdeɪdəʊ / (BrE)
A rectangular groove cut into a piece of wood, into which another piece is fitted, especially in joinery or carpentry.
rectangular woodworking groove

Examples

The carpenter cut a dado into the shelf to fit the back panel securely.
To make a strong joint, you should create a dado for the board to slot into.
A router is often used to make a dado in woodworking projects.

Etymology

From Italian dado (die, cube), likely referring to the square section; adopted into English in the 17th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdeɪdəʊ / (BrE)
the part of a pedestal between the base and the surbase, typically forming the main body of the pedestal
section of a pedestal between base and surbase

Examples

The intricate carvings on the dado made the pedestal a striking work of art.
In classical architecture, the dado supports the upper members of the structure.
The restoration included repairing the damaged dado to match the original design.

Etymology

From Italian *dado* (cube, die), of Latin origin *datum* (something given).

3. VERB

/ ˈdeɪdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdeɪdəʊ / (BrE)
to cut a slot or groove (a dado) in a piece of wood, or to fit something into such a slot
cut a groove in wood; fit into a groove

Examples

The carpenter expertly dadoed the edges of the shelf to make it fit securely.
Each panel was carefully dadoed to create space for the backing board.
He spent the afternoon dadoing grooves in the cabinet sides for the shelves.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Italian 'dado' (die, cube, plinth), referring to the rectangular shape of the groove.

4. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪˌdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdeɪdəʊ / (BrE)
The lower part of an interior wall, finished in a different material or color from the rest of the wall, often paneled or decorated.
lower part of an interior wall

Examples

The living room walls were painted ivory, with a wooden dado running along the bottom.
Restoring the Victorian house, they carefully matched the original dado in the hallway.
A richly carved dado separates the patterned wallpaper from the plain upper wall.

Etymology

From Italian *dado* (die, cube), referring to the shape or projection of the lower wall part.
