diaphragm

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪəˌfræm / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəfræm / (BrE)
a thin flexible membrane in an electro-acoustic device, such as a microphone or loudspeaker, that vibrates to receive or produce sound waves
membrane in audio device for sound transmission

Examples

The microphone's diaphragm converts sound vibrations into electrical signals.
A damaged diaphragm in a loudspeaker can distort the audio output.
Thin plastic is often used for the diaphragm in high-quality headphones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: membrane - a thin layer of material acting as a barrier or filter

Etymology

From Greek diaphragma (partition, barrier), from dia- (across) and phragma (fence).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪəˌfræm / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəfræm / (BrE)
a mechanical device in a camera that controls the size of the lens aperture, regulating the amount of light that enters
device controlling a camera's aperture

Examples

Many modern cameras allow the diaphragm to be adjusted automatically for different lighting conditions.
The photographer selected a small diaphragm opening to achieve a greater depth of field.
If the diaphragm is set too wide, the resulting image may be overexposed.

Synset Examples

the new cameras adjust the diaphragm automatically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aperture - the opening in a camera through which light passes

Etymology

From Greek diafragma (partition, barrier), via Latin diaphragma.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪəˌfræm / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.ə.fræm / (BrE)
a muscular sheet that separates the chest and abdominal cavities and plays a major role in breathing
muscular partition aiding respiration
medical

Examples

The diaphragm contracts to help draw air into the lungs during inhalation.
A tear in the diaphragm can lead to serious medical complications.
The singer practiced breathing techniques to strengthen her diaphragm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thorax - the part of the body between the neck and the abdomen
  • Synonym: abdomen - the part of the body containing the digestive organs; the belly

Etymology

From Greek diaphragma (partition, barrier), from diaphrassein (to barricade).

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪ.əˌfræm / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.ə.fræm / (BrE)
a flexible, dome-shaped rubber or plastic cup used as a contraceptive device, fitted over the cervix to prevent pregnancy
contraceptive device fitted over cervix

Examples

The doctor explained how to use a diaphragm as a method of birth control.
She chose a diaphragm because it was a non-hormonal contraceptive option.
Before intercourse, she inserted the diaphragm and applied spermicide as instructed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cervical cap - a contraceptive device similar to a diaphragm but smaller, fits tightly over the cervix
