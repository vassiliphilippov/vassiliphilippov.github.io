hardheartedness

1. NOUN

/ hɑrdˈhɑrtɪdnəs / (AmE), / hɑːdˈhɑːtɪdnɪs / (BrE)
a lack of compassion or concern for the feelings or welfare of others
lack of compassion

Examples

His hardheartedness made it impossible for him to comfort those in need.
Many were shocked by the hardheartedness of her decision to turn away the beggar.
The novel explores the dangers of hardheartedness in a world that urgently needs kindness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: callousness - lack of feeling or empathy for others
  • Antonym: compassion - sympathetic concern for others' suffering
  • Synonym: unfeeling - not displaying compassion or emotion

Etymology

From 'hardhearted' + '-ness', where 'hardhearted' is from 'hard' + 'heart'.
