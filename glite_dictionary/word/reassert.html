to reassert

1. VERB

/ ˌriːəˈsɝːt / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈsɜːt / (BrE)
to strengthen or make something more firm or certain; to state or show again with increased force or confidence
to strengthen or reinforce (a statement, position, etc.)

Examples

After the interruption, she paused to reassert her main argument with greater clarity.
The president promised to reassert the nation's role in international affairs.
Every so often, it is necessary to reassert authority to maintain discipline.

Synset Examples

The witnesses confirmed the victim's account

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to affirm - to state something as true in a confident way
  • Antonym: to deny - to state that something is not true

Etymology

From re- (again) + assert, from Latin assertus (claimed, affirmed).
