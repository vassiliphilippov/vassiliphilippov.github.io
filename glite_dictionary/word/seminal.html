seminal

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛmɪnəl / (AmE), / ˈsɛmɪnəl / (BrE)
relating to, containing, or consisting of semen.
relating to semen
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that seminal fluid plays a role in transporting sperm.
Researchers studied the chemical composition of seminal plasma in mammals.
He was diagnosed with an infection in his seminal vesicles.

Synset Examples

seminal fluid

Etymology

From Latin 'semen' (seed) + -al, meaning 'pertaining to seed'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɛm.ə.nəl / (AmE), / ˈsiː.mɪ.nəl / (BrE)
(of a work, idea, or discovery) strongly influencing later developments; highly original or formative.
influential; formative of developments

Examples

Her seminal research changed the direction of neuroscience for decades to come.
The book is regarded as a seminal text in the field of environmental studies.
Many scientists drew inspiration from his seminal theories.

Synset Examples

seminal ideas of one discipline can influence the growth of another

Related Terms

  • Synonym: influential - having great influence on someone or something
  • Synonym: formative - serving to form something, especially having a profound and lasting influence
  • Antonym: derivative - (of a work or idea) imitative of another, and therefore less original

Etymology

From Latin 'seminālis' (of seed), from 'semen' (seed).
