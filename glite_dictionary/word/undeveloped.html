undeveloped

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌndɪˈvɛləpt / (AmE), / ˌʌndɪˈvɛləpt / (BrE)
not having grown, advanced, or been brought to completion or maturity; not fully formed, elaborated, or refined.
not fully developed or matured

Examples

The company is investing in undeveloped regions to boost economic growth.
She felt her artistic skills were still undeveloped but wanted to improve.
Many ideas in his proposal remained undeveloped and required further research.

Synset Examples

courses in interior design were rare and undeveloped
undeveloped social awareness

Related Terms

  • Antonym: developed - having been brought to a more advanced or mature state
  • Synonym: immature - not fully developed or mature in form or function

Etymology

Prefix un- (not) + developed.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌndɪˈvɛləpt / (AmE), / ˌʌndɪˈvɛləpt / (BrE)
(of land, resources, or something else) not advanced, improved, or used for a practical purpose; lacking development.
not developed or improved

Examples

Many countries have vast areas of undeveloped farmland waiting to be cultivated.
Property values are often lower for undeveloped land compared to areas with buildings and infrastructure.
Some regions remain largely undeveloped due to environmental protection laws.

Synset Examples

vast unexploited (or undeveloped) natural resources
taxes on undeveloped lots are low

Related Terms

  • Antonym: developed - having been advanced, improved, or used
  • Synonym: exploited - used or developed for a purpose

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'developed'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00946057-a
Synset POS: a
