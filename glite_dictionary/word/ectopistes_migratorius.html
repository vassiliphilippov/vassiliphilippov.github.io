Ectopistes migratorius

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.toʊˈpɪs.tiːz ˌmaɪ.ɡrəˈtɔːr.i.əs / (AmE), / ˌɛk.təˈpɪs.tiːz ˌmɪ.ɡrəˈtɔː.ri.əs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the passenger pigeon, a once-abundant but now extinct migratory bird native to North America
scientific name for the extinct passenger pigeon

Examples

The last known Ectopistes migratorius, named Martha, died in Cincinnati Zoo in 1914.
Research into Ectopistes migratorius highlights the devastating impact of human hunting on wildlife.
The vast flocks of Ectopistes migratorius once darkened the skies of eastern North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passenger pigeon - common name for Ectopistes migratorius, now extinct North American bird

Etymology

From Greek *ektos* (outside), *pistēs* (faithful), and Latin *migratorius* (migratory).
