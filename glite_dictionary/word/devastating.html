devastating

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛvəˌsteɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəsteɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
causing or capable of causing severe damage or destruction; extremely harmful or ruinous
causing severe destruction or harm

Examples

The earthquake had a devastating impact on the small town, leaving thousands homeless.
The tornado caused devastating destruction throughout the region, flattening buildings and uprooting trees.
Her resignation was a devastating blow to the project team's morale.

Synset Examples

possessing annihilative power
a devastating hurricane
the guns opened a withering fire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destructive - causing great and irreparable damage
  • Synonym: catastrophic - involving or causing sudden great damage
  • Antonym: harmless - not able or likely to cause harm

Etymology

From English 'devastate' (to lay waste), from Latin 'devastare' (to lay waste, ravage).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɛvəˌsteɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəsteɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
causing severe shock, distress, or damage, especially through intense criticism or forceful impact
causing great damage or distress

Examples

Her devastating critique of the novel left the author speechless.
The comedian delivered a devastating reply to his heckler.
The report contained a devastating analysis of the company's failures.

Synset Examples

afire with annihilating invective
a devastating portrait of human folly
to compliments inflated I've a withering reply

Related Terms

  • Synonym: withering - intended to make someone feel humiliated or small through scornful criticism
  • Synonym: annihilating - extremely destructive or damaging, especially in criticism

Etymology

From 'devastate' + -ing; 'devastate' from Latin devastare (to lay waste).

3. ADJ

/ ˈdɛvəˌsteɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdevəsteɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
causing severe shock, damage, or distress; extremely destructive or overwhelming
extremely damaging or overwhelming

Examples

The devastating news left the entire community in shock and disbelief.
She suffered a devastating injury that ended her athletic career.
The hurricane caused devastating damage to large parts of the city.

Synset Examples

a crushing blow
a crushing rejection
bone-crushing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crushing - causing overwhelming distress or destruction
  • Synonym: ruinous - causing severe damage or destruction

Etymology

From English 'devastate' + '-ing', based on Latin 'devastare' (to lay waste).
