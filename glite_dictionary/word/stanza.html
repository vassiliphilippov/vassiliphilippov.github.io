stanza

1. NOUN

/ ˈstænzə / (AmE), / ˈstænzə / (BrE)
A group of lines forming a unit within a poem, typically separated from others by a space; a verse.
group of lines in a poem

Examples

The poet uses each stanza to express a different stage of emotion.
After reading the first stanza, she paused to reflect on its imagery.
Every stanza in this poem follows the same rhyme scheme.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verse - a single line of poetry or a group forming a unit

Etymology

From Italian 'stanza' (room, standing place), from Latin 'stantia' (a standing), from 'stare' (to stand).
