Zygnema

1. NOUN

/ zaɪgˈniːmə / (AmE), / zaɪgˈniːmə / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater filamentous green algae, characterized by unbranched threads with star-shaped chloroplasts, and type genus of the family Zygnemataceae.
genus of freshwater green algae

Examples

The freshwater pond was covered with long threads of Zygnema.
Researchers studied the photosynthetic processes in Zygnema collected from alpine streams.
Under the microscope, Zygnema can be identified by its distinct star-shaped chloroplasts.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, from Greek 'zygon' (yoke) + '-nema' (thread), referring to its filaments.

2. NOUN

/ zaɪˈnɛmə / (AmE), / zɪɡˈniːmə / (BrE)
A genus of filamentous green algae in the family Zygnemataceae, commonly found in freshwater environments.
a genus of green algae

Examples

Zygnema is often found in slow-moving streams and ponds, forming tangled masses of green filaments.
Researchers studied the photosynthetic efficiency of Zygnema under different environmental conditions.
Many species in the genus Zygnema reproduce by conjugation, a fascinating process among algae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alga - a simple, typically aquatic plant-like organism
  • Synonym: Spirogyra - a related genus of filamentous green algae

Etymology

From Greek 'zygon' (yoke) and 'nēma' (thread), referring to the shape of the cells.
