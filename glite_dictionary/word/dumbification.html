dumbification

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʌm.ə.fəˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʌm.ɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something, especially a story or content, less intelligent, more simplistic, or less sophisticated
making something less intelligent or simplistic

Examples

The dumbification of the story frustrated readers who loved the original novel's depth.
Critics accused the director of dumbification when adapting the book for a younger audience.
Many fans noticed the dumbification of complex themes in the film version.

Synset Examples

The movie's dumbification of the story disappointed fans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simplification - the process of making something simpler or easier to understand

Etymology

Formed from 'dumb' + '-ification' (noun-forming suffix).
