to sentimentalize

1. VERB

/ ˌsɛntəˈmɛntəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌsɛntɪˈmɛntəlaɪz / (BrE)
to treat or portray something in an overly emotional or romanticized way, often making it seem more sentimental than it really is.
treat with exaggerated sentimentality

Examples

The movie tends to sentimentalize the lives of its characters, glossing over the difficulties they faced.
She tried not to sentimentalize her childhood memories when writing her memoir.
Critics argue that the novel sentimentalizes poverty instead of addressing its harsh realities.

Synset Examples

Too much poetry sentimentalizes the mind
These experiences have sentimentalized her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to romanticize - to make something seem more appealing or romantic than it really is

Etymology

From 'sentimental' + '-ize'; 'sentimental' from French 'sentimental', ultimately from Latin 'sentīre' (to feel).

2. VERB

/ ˌsɛn.təˈmɛn.təˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌsɛn.tɪˈmɛn.təl.aɪz / (BrE)
to describe or treat something in a way that expresses or encourages excessive or unwarranted sentiment or emotion
express or treat with excessive sentiment

Examples

Critics argue that movies often sentimentalize historical events, glossing over harsh realities.
She tends to sentimentalize the past, remembering only the good times and forgetting the struggles.
It is easy to sentimentalize childhood, but every era has its challenges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to romanticize - to make something seem more appealing or romantic than it really is
  • Antonym: to downplay - to make something appear less important or significant

Etymology

From sentiment + -alize, meaning to make or become sentimental.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00012937-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌsɛn.təˈmɛn.t̬ə.laɪz / (AmE), / ˌsɛn.tɪˈmɛn.təl.aɪz / (BrE)
to view or treat something with excessive or unwarranted sentiment or emotion, often making it seem more appealing or tender than it really is
regard or portray with excessive sentiment

Examples

Many films tend to sentimentalize childhood, glossing over its difficulties.
He refused to sentimentalize his experience growing up in poverty.
It is easy to sentimentalize history when you ignore its harsh realities.

Synset Examples

Don't sentimentalize the past events

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to romanticize - to make something seem more attractive or interesting than it really is, often by idealizing it

Etymology

From sentiment + -alize, patterned after sentimental.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
