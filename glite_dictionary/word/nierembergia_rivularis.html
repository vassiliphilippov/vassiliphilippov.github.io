Nierembergia rivularis

1. NOUN

/ niˌɛrəmˈbɜrdʒiə rɪˌvjuˈlɛərɪs / (AmE), / niˌɛrɛmˈbɜːdʒiə rɪˌvjuːˈlɛːrɪs / (BrE)
A prostrate woody herb native to South America, known for its white, tubular flowers that are often tinged with blue or rose.
South American herb with white tubular flowers

Examples

Nierembergia rivularis thrives in moist, shaded areas near streams in its native habitat.
The delicate white flowers of Nierembergia rivularis add a touch of elegance to botanical gardens.
Botanists often study Nierembergia rivularis for its unique floral coloration and growth pattern.

Etymology

Named after Spanish Jesuit Juan Eusebio Nieremberg; species epithet from Latin 'rivularis' meaning 'of a brook' or 'growing by streams'.
