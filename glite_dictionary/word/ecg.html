ECG

1. NOUN

/ i si ˈdʒi / (AmE), / iː siː ˈdʒiː / (BrE)
a test that records the electrical activity of the heart as a graph, produced by an electrocardiograph
recording of the heart's electrical activity
medical

Examples

The doctor reviewed the patient's ECG to check for any irregularities in heart rhythm.
A routine ECG can help detect early signs of heart disease.
He was hooked up to an ECG monitor throughout the procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electrocardiogram - graphical record of the heart's electrical activity
  • Synonym: EKG - alternative term for electrocardiogram

Etymology

Acronym of electrocardiogram, from Greek 'kardia' (heart) + 'gramma' (something written).
