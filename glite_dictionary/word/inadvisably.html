inadvisably

1. ADV

/ ˌɪn.ədˈvaɪ.zəb.li / (AmE), / ˌɪn.ədˈvaɪ.zə.bli / (BrE)
in a way that is not wise or recommended; not advisable
in an unwise or not recommended manner

Examples

They inadvisably went hiking during the storm despite the weather warnings.
She inadvisably invested all her savings in one risky venture.
The company inadvisably ignored early signs of trouble, leading to bigger problems.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: advisably - in a wise or advisable manner

Etymology

From the prefix 'in-' (not) + 'advisable' (advice-worthy).
