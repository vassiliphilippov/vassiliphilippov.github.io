pelecypod

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlɛsɪˌpɑd / (AmE), / pɛˈliːsɪˌpɒd / (BrE)
a mollusk of the class Pelecypoda, having a shell with two hinged parts; a bivalve
mollusk with a two-part shell (bivalve)

Examples

Fossil pelecypods are commonly found in marine sedimentary rocks.
The pelecypod shells displayed intricate patterns formed over millions of years.
Marine biologists study the pelecypod to learn about ancient aquatic environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bivalve - an aquatic mollusk with a body enclosed within a hinged shell

Etymology

From Greek pelekys (axe) + pous (foot), alluding to the shape of the animal's foot.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈlɛsɪˌpɑd / (AmE), / pɪˈliːsɪˌpɒd / (BrE)
A marine or freshwater mollusk with a soft body and platelike gills, enclosed within a hinged, two-part shell; a bivalve.
bivalve mollusk

Examples

The museum displayed a large collection of fossilized pelecypods from the Cretaceous period.
Many pelecypods play important roles in aquatic ecosystems as filter feeders.
The biology class studied the anatomy of a freshwater pelecypod under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bivalve - a mollusk with a shell consisting of two hinged parts

Etymology

From Greek 'pelekys' (axe) and 'pous' (foot), referring to the hatchet-shaped foot.
