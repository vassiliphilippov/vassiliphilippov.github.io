Pandanales

1. NOUN

/ ˌpændəˈneɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌpandəˈneɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of monocotyledonous flowering plants that includes the families Typhaceae, Sparganiaceae, and Pandanaceae.
botanical order of flowering plants

Examples

The Pandanales include several families of aquatic and tropical plants.
Researchers studied the evolutionary relationships within the Pandanales.
The fibrous leaves of many Pandanales species are used for weaving and mat-making.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Pandanus (type genus) + -ales (order suffix).
