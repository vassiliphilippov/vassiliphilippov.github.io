lemming

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛm.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɛm.ɪŋ / (BrE)
a small, short-tailed rodent found in Arctic regions, known for its periodic mass migrations
small Arctic rodent

Examples

The Arctic lemming is well adapted to survive harsh winters in the tundra.
Scientists studied the migration patterns of the lemming to understand population cycles.
A group of lemmings moved together across the snowy landscape in search of food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rodent - a small mammal with continually growing incisors

Etymology

From Norwegian 'lemming', related to Old Norse 'lómundr', meaning the animal.
