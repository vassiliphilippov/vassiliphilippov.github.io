Yellowstone

1. PROPN

/ ˈjɛloʊˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊˌstəʊn / (BrE)
a major tributary of the Missouri River in the United States, flowing through Yellowstone National Park and known for its scenic beauty and significance.
river flowing through Yellowstone National Park

Examples

The Yellowstone is renowned for its clear waters and dramatic canyons.
Many visitors to the park hike along the banks of the Yellowstone.
The Yellowstone eventually merges with the Missouri River in North Dakota.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Missouri River - major North American river into which the Yellowstone flows

Etymology

Named after the yellow rocks found in the Grand Canyon of the Yellowstone River.
