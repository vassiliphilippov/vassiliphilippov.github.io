seasoner

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːzənər / (AmE), / ˈsiːzənə / (BrE)
an ingredient or substance added to food to enhance its flavor
ingredient added for flavor

Examples

Salt is a common seasoner in many recipes around the world.
The chef used a special blend of seasoners to enhance the dish's taste.
Pepper served as the only seasoner for the simple meal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seasoning - a substance used to add flavor to food

Etymology

Formed from 'season' + '-er', indicating an agent or thing that seasons.
