to distinguish

1. VERB

/ dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃ / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃ / (BrE)
to recognize or point out the difference between two or more things; to identify something as different from others, especially in scientific or technical contexts.
recognize or point out differences

Examples

It can be hard to distinguish one species of butterfly from another without close observation.
Trained botanists can distinguish edible mushrooms from poisonous ones.
With experience, you learn to distinguish subtle differences between similar plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to differentiate - to recognize or show the difference between things
  • Synonym: to identify - to recognize and name someone or something

Etymology

From Latin distinguere (to separate, discern), from dis- (apart) + stinguere (to prick, extinguish).

5. VERB

/ dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃ / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃ / (BrE)
to make someone or something conspicuous or noteworthy; to set apart from others by a special quality

Examples

The bright red door distinguishes their house from every other one on the street.
He became distinguished in his field by working harder than anyone else.

Related Terms
