O.K.

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈkeɪ / (AmE), / əʊˈkeɪ / (BrE)
Official permission or authorization to proceed with something.
approval; official permission

Examples

We finally got the O.K. from the manager to start the project.
The launch was delayed until the engineers gave their O.K..
You can't publish the article without the editor's O.K..

Synset Examples

they gave us the O.K. to go ahead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approval - the action of approving; official agreement or permission
  • Synonym: authorization - official permission for something to happen or be done
  • Antonym: denial - refusal to give permission or approval

Etymology

Origin uncertain; popularized in the U.S. in the 19th century as an abbreviation of 'oll korrect' (a humorous misspelling of 'all correct').

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊˈkeɪ / (AmE), / ˌəʊˈkeɪ / (BrE)
to give official approval to something; to sanction or authorize
give official approval

Examples

The manager O.K.ed the new marketing proposal yesterday.
Has the city council O.K.ed the construction plans yet?
Please let me know when the director O.K.s our budget request.

Synset Examples

I approve of his educational policies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to approve - to agree with or accept officially
  • Antonym: to disapprove - to have or express an unfavorable opinion

Etymology

Originated as an abbreviation for 'oll korrect,' a jocular misspelling of 'all correct' in 19th-century American English.

Synset POS: v
