woodiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdinəs / (AmE), / ˈwʊdɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being abundant in trees or woody plants
quality of being rich in trees

Examples

The woodiness of the landscape made it a perfect place for nature walks.
Researchers studied the woodiness of different habitats to understand biodiversity.
Some regions have lost much of their original woodiness due to deforestation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woody - characterized by or full of wood or woods

Etymology

From 'woody' + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈwʊd.i.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being woody, especially regarding the coarse texture characteristic of wood or woody plant parts
quality of being woody

Examples

The asparagus had a noticeable woodiness near the ends.
Gardeners often select plants with less woodiness for softer foliage.
Overcooked green beans can develop an unpleasant woodiness in texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woody - having wood or characteristics of wood

Etymology

From 'woody' + '-ness', forming a noun indicating state or quality.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04958129-n
Synset POS: n
