oddment

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑdmənt / (AmE), / ˈɒdmənt / (BrE)
a piece or remnant of material, especially a leftover part of cloth after the main portion has been used or sold
a leftover piece of cloth or material

Examples

She used a colorful oddment to patch the torn sleeve of her jacket.
The tailor sold several oddments of cloth at a discount.
Craft enthusiasts often collect oddments for small sewing projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remnant - a small remaining quantity of something, especially fabric

Etymology

From the word 'odd' (meaning remaining or left over) with the suffix '-ment' denoting result or means.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑd.mənt / (AmE), / ˈɒd.mənt / (BrE)
an unusual or leftover item, especially one kept or collected as a curiosity
unusual or leftover item

Examples

She keeps a box full of oddments she finds at antiques fairs.
The museum's display included various oddments from Victorian daily life.
Over the years, several oddments have accumulated on his desk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curio - an unusual or interesting object, typically small and collectible

Etymology

From 'odd' + '-ment', with 'odd' originating from Middle English 'odde', meaning left over.
