Sarcoptes

1. NOUN

/ sɑrˈkɒp.tiz / (AmE), / sɑːˈkɒp.tiːz / (BrE)
A genus of mites that includes species causing scabies and itchy skin conditions in humans and animals.
genus of itch-causing mites
medical

Examples

Sarcoptes is responsible for causing scabies in humans and mange in animals.
Veterinarians often treat animals infested with Sarcoptes mites.
Under a microscope, Sarcoptes can be identified by its round body and short legs.

Etymology

From Greek sarkōptēs, meaning 'flesh-cutter', from sarx 'flesh' and koptein 'to cut'.

2. NOUN

/ sɑrˈkɑp.tiːz / (AmE), / sɑːˈkɒp.tiːz / (BrE)
A genus of itch mites, including species that cause scabies in humans and animals.
genus of itch mites causing scabies
medical

Examples

Scabies is caused by the Sarcoptes mite burrowing into the skin.
Veterinarians often test animals for Sarcoptes infestation when itching is severe.
Sarcoptes is a medically important genus due to its role in skin diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mite - A small arachnid, related to ticks, some of which are parasitic.

Etymology

From Greek sarkōptēs, from sarx (flesh) + koptein (to cut).
