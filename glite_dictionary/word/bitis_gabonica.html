Bitis gabonica

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪ.tɪs ɡəˈboʊ.nɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˈbɪ.tɪs ɡəˈbəʊ.nɪ.kə / (BrE)
A large, heavy-bodied, brilliantly marked, and highly venomous viper native to West Africa.
large, venomous West African viper

Examples

The Bitis gabonica is known for its striking pattern and potent venom.
During the safari, we spotted a Bitis gabonica camouflaged among the fallen leaves.
Researchers study the Bitis gabonica to better understand its role in the ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaboon viper - large venomous viper of West Africa, alternative common name

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name 'Bitis' and the species epithet 'gabonica,' referring to Gabon.
