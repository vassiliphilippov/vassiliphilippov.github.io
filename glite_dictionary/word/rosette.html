rosette

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈzɛt / (AmE), / rəʊˈzɛt / (BrE)
A disease of potatoes caused by the fungus Rhizoctonia, leading to stunted growth and yellowing of leaves.
potato disease caused by Rhizoctonia

Examples

Farmers lost a significant part of their crop when rosette spread through the fields.
Proper crop rotation can help minimize the risk of rosette in potatoes.
The agronomist identified the yellow patches on the plants as symptoms of rosette.

2. NOUN

/ roʊˈzɛt / (AmE), / rəʊˈzɛt / (BrE)
a circular window with decorative stonework or tracery, typically found in Gothic architecture
circular window with decorative tracery

Examples

The cathedral's magnificent rosette illuminated the nave with colored light at sunset.
Architects restored the original rosette painstakingly, preserving its intricate Gothic design.
Through the stained glass rosette, morning sunlight cast vibrant patterns on the floor.

Etymology

From French 'rosette', diminutive of 'rose', referring to its rose-like shape.
