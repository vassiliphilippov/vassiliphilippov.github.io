Lathyrus nissolia

1. NOUN

/ ləˈθaɪrəs nɪˈsoʊliə / (AmE), / ləˈθaɪrəs nɪˈsɒliə / (BrE)
An annual European vetch with red flowers, commonly known as grass vetchling.
annual European vetch with red flowers

Examples

The meadows were dotted with the bright red flowers of Lathyrus nissolia.
Botanists studied the habitat preferences of Lathyrus nissolia during the spring survey.
Lathyrus nissolia is often found growing alongside grasses in European fields.

Etymology

From Latin: Lathyrus (a genus of flowering plants) and nissolia (the species epithet).
