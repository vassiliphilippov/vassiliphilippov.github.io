Napaea dioica

1. NOUN

/ nəˈpiː.ə daɪˈoʊ.ɪ.kə / (AmE), / nəˈpiː.ə daɪˈəʊ.ɪ.kə / (BrE)
A tall, coarse herbaceous plant of eastern and central United States, with palmate leaves and small white dioecious flowers, typically found in alluvial soils.
tall American herb with palmate leaves and white flowers

Examples

Napaea dioica thrives in moist, rich soils along riverbanks in the Midwest.
Botanists are studying the population dynamics of Napaea dioica in its native habitat.
The large palmate leaves of Napaea dioica make it easy to identify among other wildflowers.

Etymology

From scientific Latin: 'Napaea,' a genus name from Greek 'napaia' (dwelling in valleys); 'dioica,' meaning 'of two houses' (separate male and female plants) from Greek.
