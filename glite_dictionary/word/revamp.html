to revamp

1. VERB

/ riˈvæmp / (AmE), / riˈvæmp / (BrE)
to improve or renovate something, especially by making changes or repairs
improve or renovate; make new again

Examples

The company decided to revamp its outdated logo to appeal to younger customers.
They plan to revamp the entire curriculum to keep up with modern teaching methods.
After the flood, the family had to revamp their basement before moving back in.

Synset Examples

They revamped their old house before selling it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to renovate - to restore to a good state; to repair
  • Synonym: to restore - to bring back to original condition

Etymology

From re- (again) + vamp, where 'vamp' refers to the upper part of a shoe that can be replaced or repaired.
