parazoan

1. NOUN

/ ˌpær.əˈzoʊ.ən / (AmE), / ˌpar.əˈzəʊ.ən / (BrE)
a primitive multicellular marine animal, such as a sponge, whose body is porous and supported by a fibrous skeletal framework, usually forming sessile colonies
primitive multicellular marine animal (e.g., sponge)

Examples

Sponges are considered parazoans because of their simple organization.
The evolutionary split between parazoans and eumetazoans marks a major division in animal life.
Researchers studied the genetic makeup of several parazoan species to understand early multicellularity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sponge - a simple multicellular marine animal with a porous body

Etymology

From Greek para- (beside) + zōion (animal), indicating animals beside or distinct from typical animals.
