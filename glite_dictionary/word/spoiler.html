spoiler

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɔɪlər / (AmE), / ˈspɔɪlə / (BrE)
A device attached to the rear of a car, typically shaped as an airfoil, designed to reduce lift and improve stability at high speeds.
device reducing car lift at speed

Examples

The race car's spoiler helps it stay firmly on the track during sharp turns.
He installed a new spoiler on his sports car to improve its performance.
A damaged spoiler can negatively affect a vehicle's aerodynamics at high speed.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɔɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈspɔɪlə / (BrE)
a hinged device on the upper surface of an aircraft wing that can be raised to reduce lift and increase drag, usually to assist in landing or controlling descent
device on aircraft wing to reduce lift and increase drag

Examples

The pilot deployed the spoiler to help slow the plane during landing.
Modern jetliners are equipped with multiple spoilers for improved control.
When the spoiler was raised, the aircraft's descent rate increased noticeably.

Etymology

From 'spoil' + '-er'; technical sense developed in the 20th century in aviation.

3. NOUN

/ ˈspɔɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈspɔɪlə / (BrE)
a candidate in an election who is unlikely to win but may draw enough votes to affect the chances of the leading candidates
candidate who affects election outcome

Examples

Some analysts believe that the independent candidate played the role of a spoiler in the tight gubernatorial race.
The presence of a spoiler on the ballot can change the outcome between the top contenders.
Strategists feared the third-party hopeful would act as a spoiler, splitting the vote.

4. NOUN

/ ˈspɔɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈspɔɪlə / (BrE)
a person who overindulges someone, often by giving them too much of what they want, thereby potentially spoiling their character or behavior
person who overindulges someone

Examples

My grandmother was a notorious spoiler, always buying me treats and gifts.
As a spoiler, he found it hard to say no to his children, often giving them anything they wanted.
Some believe that being a spoiler harms children's ability to handle disappointment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indulger - a person who gives in to someone's wishes or desires

Etymology

From spoil + -er, meaning one who spoils.

5. NOUN

/ ˈspɔɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈspɔɪlə / (BrE)
a person who seizes spoils or plunder, especially during war; one who pillages
person who seizes spoils or plunder

Examples

The victorious soldiers were seen as spoilers in the eyes of the defeated villagers.
Medieval chronicles lament the devastation wrought by spoilers across the countryside.
Laws were later enacted to punish spoiler bands preying on wartime refugees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plunderer - a person who steals goods, especially during war or civil disorder
  • Synonym: marauder - a person who raids and plunders
  • Antonym: defender - a person who protects or guards against attack or loss

Etymology

From Middle English spoil (plunder), from Old French espoille, from Latin spolium (booty).
