segment

1. VERB

/ ˈsɛɡ.mənt / (AmE), / ˈsɛɡ.mənt / (BrE)
to divide something into separate parts or sections
divide into separate parts

Examples

The video editor will segment the footage into three main parts.
After fertilization, the embryo's cells rapidly segment.
The market can be segmented by age, income, or behavior.

Synset Examples

The cells segmented

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divide - to separate into parts or groups
  • Antonym: to unite - to bring together or combine into one

Etymology

From Latin segmentum (a piece cut off), from secare (to cut).
