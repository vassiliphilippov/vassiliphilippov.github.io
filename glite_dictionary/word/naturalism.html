naturalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈnætʃ(ə)rəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈnatʃ(ə)rəlɪzəm / (BrE)
A style and movement in art and literature in 19th-century France that aimed for highly detailed, realistic, and factual representation of life.
19th-century French realist art/literary movement

Examples

The arrival of naturalism transformed the way French painters depicted everyday life.
Émile Zola's novels are closely associated with naturalism in literature.
Many artists rejected romanticism in favor of naturalism during that era.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: realism - The practice of representing things as they actually are in art or literature

Etymology

From French 'naturalisme', from Latin 'natura' (nature).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnætʃ(ə)rəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈnatʃ(ə)rəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
(philosophy) The doctrine that the world and everything in it can be explained through scientific observation and laws, without relying on spiritual or supernatural explanations.
philosophical view; world explained scientifically

Examples

Many scientists adhere to naturalism when interpreting the origins of the universe.
The debate between naturalism and supernaturalism has shaped much of modern philosophy.
Because of his commitment to naturalism, he rejects the idea of miracles.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: supernaturalism - the doctrine that supernatural forces or entities are needed to explain the world
  • Synonym: materialism - the doctrine that only physical matter exists, with no spiritual or supernatural elements

Etymology

From Latin natura (nature) + -ism (doctrine or belief).
