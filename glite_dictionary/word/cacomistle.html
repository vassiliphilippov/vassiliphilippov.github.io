cacomistle

1. NOUN

/ ˌkækəˈmɪsəl / (AmE), / ˌkækəˈmɪsəl / (BrE)
A raccoon-like mammal found in Mexico and the southwestern United States, with a long bushy tail marked by black and white rings; an omnivorous member of the ringtail family.
raccoon-like mammal of Mexico and southwestern US

Examples

The cacomistle stealthily searched for insects among the rocks at dusk.
Scientists observed a cacomistle using its bushy tail for balance while climbing a tree.
Local legends often feature the cacomistle as a clever survivor in the harsh desert.

Etymology

From Nahuatl *tlahcomiztli* (half cat), via Spanish *cacomixtle*.
