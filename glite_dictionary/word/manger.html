manger

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪndʒər / (AmE), / ˈmeɪndʒə / (BrE)
A trough or open container in a stable or barn from which cattle or horses eat.
feeding trough for animals

Examples

The horses gathered around the manger early every morning for their oats.
During the nativity scene, baby Jesus is often shown lying in a manger.
The old barn still had a wooden manger attached to one of the walls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trough - a long, narrow container from which animals eat or drink

Etymology

From Old French 'mangier' (to eat), from Latin 'manducare'.
