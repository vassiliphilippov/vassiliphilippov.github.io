aleph-nought

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪlɛf nɔt / (AmE), / ˈeɪlɪf nɔːt / (BrE)
the smallest infinite cardinal number; the cardinality of the set of natural numbers
the smallest infinite number in math

Examples

Cantor proved that the set of natural numbers has a cardinality of aleph-nought.
The concept of aleph-nought is central in set theory and the study of infinite sets.
No finite number can equal aleph-nought, as it represents the smallest infinity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aleph-null - alternative form for the smallest infinite cardinal number
  • Synonym: cardinality - the mathematical concept of the size of a set

Etymology

From Hebrew 'aleph' (the first letter of the Hebrew alphabet) and English 'nought' (zero).
