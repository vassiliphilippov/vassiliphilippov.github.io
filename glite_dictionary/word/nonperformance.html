nonperformance

1. NOUN

/ nɑn.pɚˈfɔr.məns / (AmE), / nɒn.pəˈfɔː.məns / (BrE)
failure to do something that is required or expected, especially a duty or obligation; lack of performance.
failure to perform a required action

Examples

The contract was cancelled due to the nonperformance of the agreed services.
Her repeated nonperformance eventually led to her dismissal from the company.
Legal action may result from nonperformance of your obligations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breach - the act of breaking or failing to observe a law or agreement

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'non-' (not) and 'performance.'
