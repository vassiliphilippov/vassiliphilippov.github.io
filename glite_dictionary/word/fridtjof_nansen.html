Fridtjof Nansen

1. PROPN

/ ˈfrɪt.jɔf ˈnæn.sən / (AmE), / ˈfrɪt.jɒf ˈnæn.sən / (BrE)
(1861–1930) A Norwegian explorer, scientist, diplomat, and humanitarian known for Arctic expeditions and work with refugees after World War I.
Norwegian Arctic explorer and humanitarian

Examples

Fridtjof Nansen led a groundbreaking expedition across Greenland's ice cap in 1888.
The League of Nations appointed Fridtjof Nansen to oversee aid for refugees after World War I.
Many institutions honor Fridtjof Nansen for his work in science and humanitarianism.
