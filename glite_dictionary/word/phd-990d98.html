Ph.D.

1. NOUN

/ pi eɪtʃ ˈdi / (AmE), / piː eɪtʃ ˈdiː / (BrE)
a doctoral degree awarded by universities, typically for advanced research in a specific field, usually requiring several years of study and a dissertation
highest academic doctoral degree

Examples

After five years of research, she finally earned her Ph.D. in chemistry.
Dr. Patel holds a Ph.D. from Oxford University specializing in artificial intelligence.
Many academic jobs require applicants to have a Ph.D. or equivalent degree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doctorate - the highest academic degree (doctor's degree) awarded by a university

Etymology

Abbreviation of Latin 'Philosophiae Doctor' (Doctor of Philosophy).
