Entelea

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈtɛliə / (AmE), / ɛnˈtiːlɪə / (BrE)
a genus of evergreen shrubs native to New Zealand, known for their softwood and large leaves
genus of evergreen shrubs from New Zealand

Examples

The Entelea genus is recognized for its rapid growth and large, heart-shaped leaves.
Scientists studied the ecological role of Entelea in native New Zealand forests.
Some indigenous traditions use the bark of Entelea for making cordage.

Etymology

From Greek enteleia meaning 'completeness' or 'perfection', used as a botanical genus name.
