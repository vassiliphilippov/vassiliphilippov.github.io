Anabrus

1. NOUN

/ ˈænəˌbrʌs / (AmE), / ˈanəbrəs / (BrE)
A genus of insects in the family Tettigoniidae, commonly known as long-horned grasshoppers or katydids, native to North America.
genus of long-horned grasshoppers (katydids)

Examples

The Mormon cricket is a well-known species of the genus Anabrus.
Scientists gathered several Anabrus specimens during the field study in Utah.
The Anabrus genus is notable for its large, flightless orthopterans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: katydid - any of various long-horned grasshoppers of the family Tettigoniidae

Etymology

From Greek 'anabros', meaning 'eaten up'. Used in taxonomy since the 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈænəˌbrʌs / (AmE), / ˈanəbrəs / (BrE)
A genus of large, flightless katydids in the family Tettigoniidae, native to North America and including the Mormon cricket.
a genus of North American katydids

Examples

The Anabrus genus includes the insect known as the Mormon cricket.
Many researchers study Anabrus to understand the behavior of migratory insects.
Specimens of Anabrus are often collected in the western United States.

Etymology

Modern Latin, used in scientific classification.
