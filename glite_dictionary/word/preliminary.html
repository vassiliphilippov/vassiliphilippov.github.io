preliminary

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈlɪməˌnɛri / (AmE), / prɪˈlɪmɪnəri / (BrE)
a minor contest or event held before the main competition, especially in sports; a preliminary match
minor event before main contest

Examples

The boxing crowd watched the preliminary before the championship match began.
Several exciting preliminaries set the tone for the main sporting event.
Fans arrived early to catch the preliminary matches before the main fight.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: main event - the principal or most important competition in a sequence
  • Synonym: prelim - an informal shortened synonym for preliminary as a minor match

Etymology

From Latin *prae-* (before) + *limen* (threshold).

2. ADJ

/ prɪˈlɪməˌnɛri / (AmE), / prɪˈlɪmɪnəri / (BrE)
Occurring before or in preparation for something more important; introductory.
preceding or preparatory; introductory

Examples

The committee held a preliminary meeting to outline the main topics.
We conducted a preliminary survey before launching the full study.
These are just preliminary results; the final report will be available next month.

Synset Examples

a preliminary investigation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: introductory - serving as an introduction; preliminary
  • Antonym: concluding - serving to end or finalize

Etymology

From Latin praeliminaris, from prae- (before) + limen (threshold).

3. NOUN

/ prɪˈlɪməˌnɛri / (AmE), / prɪˈlɪmɪnəri / (BrE)
an action or event that precedes and prepares for something more important; an introductory step or event
an action or event that comes before something important

Examples

Before the negotiations could begin, certain preliminaries had to be addressed.
The ceremony began with a few short preliminaries before the main event.
They completed the preliminaries quickly so the competition could get underway.

Synset Examples

training is a necessary preliminary to employment
drinks were the overture to dinner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prelude - an event serving as an introduction to something more important
  • Antonym: conclusion - the ending or final part of something

Etymology

From Latin prae 'before' + limen 'threshold', meaning something that comes before the main part.
