Silybum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪləbəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪlɪbʌm / (BrE)
A small genus of thistle-like herbs native to east Africa and Europe, best known for species such as the milk thistle (Silybum marianum).
genus of thistle-like herbs

Examples

The Silybum genus includes the well-known milk thistle, valued for its medicinal properties.
Many herbalists recommend extracts from Silybum plants for supporting liver health.
Botanists have classified several spiny plants under the genus Silybum.

Etymology

From Greek *silybos* (a kind of thistle or edible plant).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlɪbəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪlɪbəm / (BrE)
A small genus of thistle-like plants, native to parts of Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East, including the milk thistle.
genus of thistle-like plants

Examples

The plant Silybum marianum is widely known for its medicinal properties.
Botanists have classified several species within the genus Silybum.
Milk thistle, a popular herbal remedy, belongs to the Silybum genus.

Etymology

From Greek silybon (a thistle-like plant).
