reductant

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈdʌktənt / (AmE), / rɪˈdʌktənt / (BrE)
a substance that causes reduction in another substance by donating electrons and is itself oxidized, commonly used in chemical reactions and photography
substance that causes reduction by being oxidized

Examples

The chemist added a reductant to the solution to complete the reaction.
Sodium thiosulfate is commonly used as a reductant in photographic processing.
The effectiveness of a reductant depends on its ability to donate electrons to other substances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reducing agent - substance that causes reduction in a chemical reaction
  • Antonym: oxidant - a substance that oxidizes another; accepts electrons in a reaction

Etymology

From Latin 'reductus' (led back), from 'reducere' (to lead back). Modelled after 'reducing agent'.
