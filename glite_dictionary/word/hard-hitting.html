hard-hitting

1. ADJ

/ hɑrdˈhɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / hɑːdˈhɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of speech, writing, or action) characterized by forceful and direct impact; uncompromising and powerful.
forceful and uncompromising

Examples

The journalist published a hard-hitting story that exposed government corruption.
Her hard-hitting questions put the politician on the spot during the interview.
The play received praise for its hard-hitting portrayal of social issues.

Synset Examples

a hard-hitting expose
a trenchant argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trenchant - vigorous or incisive in expression or style

2. ADJ

/ ˈhɑrdˌhɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌhɑːdˈhɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a speech, article, criticism, etc.) direct, forceful, and often aggressively effective or persuasive.
direct and forceful; aggressively persuasive

Examples

The journalist wrote a hard-hitting exposé that revealed major corruption in the government.
Her hard-hitting questions put the politician on the spot during the interview.
The company's hard-hitting marketing strategy quickly attracted public attention.

Synset Examples

a hard-hitting advertising campaign
a high-pressure salesman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forceful - strong and assertive in expressing ideas
  • Antonym: mild - gentle or moderate in force or effect
