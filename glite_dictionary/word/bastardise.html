to bastardise

1. VERB

/ ˈbæstɚˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈbɑː.stə.daɪz / (BrE)
to declare (a child) to be illegitimate, especially formally or legally
declare a child illegitimate

Examples

The court moved to bastardise the child after the legitimacy was challenged.
Decades ago, certain laws could bastardise a child born outside of marriage.
He was unjustly bastardised because of his parents' circumstances.

Etymology

From bastard + -ise, after French 'bastardiser'.

2. VERB

/ ˈbæstɚˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈbɑːstədaɪz / (BrE)
to spoil or corrupt something, especially by reducing its quality, value, or purity
to spoil or corrupt by reducing quality

Examples

Some critics argue that mass production has bastardised traditional craftsmanship.
The translation was so loose that it bastardised the original meaning of the poem.
Adding too many modern elements can bastardise a classic recipe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to debase - to reduce something in quality or value
  • Synonym: corrupt - to make something less pure or honest

Etymology

Alteration of 'bastard', originally from Old French 'bastard', meaning illegitimate child.
