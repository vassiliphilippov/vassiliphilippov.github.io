Calliope

1. PROPN

/ kəˈlaɪ.ə.pi / (AmE), / kəˈlaɪ.ə.pi / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the Muse of epic poetry and eloquence.
Muse of epic poetry in Greek mythology

Examples

Many poets in antiquity prayed to Calliope for inspiration when beginning an epic work.
Homer was believed to have been guided by Calliope as he composed the Iliad and the Odyssey.
The painter depicted Calliope with a writing tablet, symbolizing her role as Muse of epic poetry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Clio - the Muse of history in Greek mythology
  • Synonym: epic poetry - long narrative poetry on heroic themes

Etymology

From Greek Kalliope, meaning 'beautiful-voiced' (kallos 'beauty' + ops 'voice').
