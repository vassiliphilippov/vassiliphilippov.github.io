oppressively

1. ADV

/ əˈprɛsɪvli / (AmE), / əˈprɛsɪvli / (BrE)
In a manner that is burdensome, overwhelming, or heavy, making people feel uncomfortable or distressed.
in a burdensome or overwhelming way

Examples

The humidity made the room oppressively stuffy during the summer afternoon.
She felt oppressively watched by the strict headmaster at the school assembly.
Clouds hung oppressively low, threatening a torrential downpour at any moment.

Synset Examples

it was oppressively hot in the office

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oppressive - causing discomfort by being harsh, severe, or burdensome

Etymology

From 'oppressive' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'opprimere' (to press down).
