stockpiling

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑkˌpaɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstɒkˌpaɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of accumulating and storing a large supply of something for future use
accumulating and storing reserves

Examples

Due to fears of shortages, stockpiling of canned food increased rapidly.
The government began stockpiling vaccines ahead of flu season.
International treaties aim to prevent the stockpiling of nuclear weapons.

Synset Examples

the stockpiling of war materials

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoarding - the act of collecting and keeping a large amount of something, often secretly

Etymology

From 'stockpile' (verb) + '-ing'; 'stockpile' from 'stock' + 'pile'.
