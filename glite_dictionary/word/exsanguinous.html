exsanguinous

1. ADJ

/ ɛkˈsæŋɡwɪnəs / (AmE), / ɛkˈsaŋɡwɪnəs / (BrE)
(of an organism, tissue, etc.) lacking blood or apparently so; bloodless.
lacking blood; bloodless
medical

Examples

The patient appeared exsanguinous after the severe injury.
Pathologists examined the exsanguinous tissue sample under the microscope.
The bird's exsanguinous appearance suggested it had been attacked by a predator.

Synset Examples

the bloodless carcass of my Hector sold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bloodless - without blood or pale from lack of blood

Etymology

From Latin exsanguis (bloodless), from ex- (out of) + sanguis (blood).
