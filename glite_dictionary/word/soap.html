soap

/ soʊp / (AmE), / səʊp / (BrE)
(slang) Gamma hydroxybutyrate (GHB), especially when used as a recreational drug.

2. VERB

/ soʊp / (AmE), / səʊp / (BrE)
to rub or cover with soap, typically in order to clean
rub or cover with soap

Examples

She soaped her hands thoroughly before rinsing them under warm water.
The nurse soaped the patient's arm to prepare it for the injection.
He soaped himself up quickly and stepped out of the shower.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wash - to clean with water or other liquid

3. NOUN

/ soʊp / (AmE), / səʊp / (BrE)
A substance made from the salts of vegetable or animal fats, used for washing and cleaning.
substance for washing and cleaning

Examples

She used soap and water to wash her hands before dinner.
The shelf in the bathroom was lined with colorful bars of soap.
Liquid soap is often preferred for washing dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detergent - a cleaning agent that removes dirt and stains, especially in laundry and dishwashing

Etymology

From Old English sāp (soap), likely from a Germanic source related to Latin 'sapo' (soap).
