Yb

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɑː.tə.bɪt / (AmE), / ˈjɒ.tə.bɪt / (BrE)
A unit of information equal to 1,000 zettabits or 10^24 bits, known as a yottabit.
unit of 10^24 bits (yottabit)

Examples

Data storage capacities are now measured in Yb for the largest scientific projects.
The file transfer required more than one Yb of bandwidth.
Advances in computing may make Yb-scale data storage commonplace in the future.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: zb - a unit of information equal to 10^21 bits (zettabit)

Etymology

From 'yotta-' (SI prefix for 10^24) and 'bit'.

2. NOUN

/ waɪ biː / (AmE), / waɪ biː / (BrE)
the chemical symbol for ytterbium, a soft silvery rare-earth metallic element of the lanthanide series
chemical symbol for ytterbium (element)

Examples

The element Yb has applications in certain types of lasers and electronics.
Chemists measured the purity of the Yb sample before the experiment.
Monazite deposits are one of the main commercial sources of Yb.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ytterbium - a soft silvery rare-earth metal of the lanthanide series

Etymology

From Latinized form of Ytterby, a village in Sweden where the ore was first discovered.
