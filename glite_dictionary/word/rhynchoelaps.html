Rhynchoelaps

1. NOUN

/ rɪŋkoʊˈiːlæps / (AmE), / rɪŋkəʊˈiːlæps / (BrE)
A genus of small, venomous snakes known as Australian coral snakes, found in Australia.
genus of Australian coral snakes

Examples

The genus Rhynchoelaps includes several species of small, venomous snakes native to Australia.
Researchers discovered a new Rhynchoelaps species in the arid regions of Western Australia.
Unlike many other snakes, Rhynchoelaps are known for their burrowing habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Brachyurophis - genus of similar Australian coral snakes

Etymology

From Greek 'rhynchos' (snout) and possibly 'elaps' (a kind of snake).

2. NOUN

/ rɪŋkoʊˈelæps / (AmE), / rɪŋkəʊˈelaps / (BrE)
A genus of Australian coral snakes in the family Elapidae.
genus of Australian coral snakes

Examples

The Rhynchoelaps are a genus of small, venomous snakes native to Australia.
Herpetologists have recently studied the habitats of the Rhynchoelaps in Queensland.
The distinctive coloration of Rhynchoelaps serves as a warning to potential predators.

Etymology

From Greek 'rhynchos' (snout) and 'Elaps' (a former genus of snakes).
