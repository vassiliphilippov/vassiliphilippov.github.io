precipitateness

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈsɪpɪtət.nəs / (AmE), / prɪˈsɪpɪtətnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of acting or happening with sudden haste or without prior warning
sudden or hasty manner

Examples

The precipitateness of their decision led to several unforeseen problems.
She regretted her precipitateness in leaving the job without another offer.
His precipitateness during the negotiations surprised his colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impulsiveness - the tendency to act on impulse rather than thought
  • Antonym: deliberateness - the quality of being careful and unhurried

Etymology

From 'precipitate' + '-ness'; ultimately from Latin praecipitare (to throw headlong).
