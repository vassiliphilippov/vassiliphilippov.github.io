fastidiousness

1. NOUN

/ fəˈstɪdiəsnəs / (AmE), / fəˈstɪdiəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being very attentive to and concerned about accuracy, detail, or matters of taste or style
careful attention to detail or style

Examples

Her fastidiousness in choosing furniture ensures that her home always looks elegant.
The chef's fastidiousness about ingredients made his dishes stand out from the rest.
His fastidiousness when it comes to grammar often irritates his friends.

Synset Examples

neatness and fastidiousness of dress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meticulousness - the quality of being very careful and precise
  • Antonym: carelessness - lack of attention and caution

Etymology

From Latin 'fastidiosus' (disdainful, squeamish) via French 'fastidieux'.
