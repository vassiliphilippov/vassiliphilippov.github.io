chlamydeous

1. ADJ

/ kləˈmɪdiəs / (AmE), / kləˈmɪdiːəs / (BrE)
(botany) Having a perianth or floral envelope consisting of a calyx and/or corolla.
having a calyx and/or corolla (botany)

Examples

The tulip is a chlamydeous flower because it has both a calyx and a corolla.
Many garden flowers exhibit chlamydeous structures in their perianths.
In botanical taxonomy, chlamydeous characteristics help distinguish various plant species.

Etymology

From Greek *chlamys, chlamydos* (a cloak), referring to an enveloping structure.
