lysinemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌlaɪsɪˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˌlaɪsɪˈniːmiə / (BrE)
A rare inherited disorder caused by the body's inability to metabolize the amino acid lysine, resulting in symptoms such as muscular weakness and intellectual disability.
inherited metabolic disorder affecting lysine processing
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the child with lysinemia after observing symptoms of muscle weakness.
Early detection of lysinemia is crucial for managing the developmental challenges it presents.
Research into treatments for lysinemia has provided insight into amino acid metabolism disorders.

Etymology

From 'lysine,' an amino acid, and suffix '-emia' (condition of the blood), from Greek ‘haima’ (blood).
