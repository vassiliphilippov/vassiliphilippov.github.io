eaglet

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːɡlət / (AmE), / ˈiːɡlɪt / (BrE)
A young eagle, especially one that has not yet left the nest.
a young eagle

Examples

The eaglet waited impatiently in the nest for its mother to return with food.
Scientists attached a tiny tracking device to the wing of the rescued eaglet.
After several weeks, the young eaglet began to practice flapping its wings.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: eagle - a large bird of prey with a massive hooked bill and long broad wings

Etymology

From Old French 'aiglet', diminutive of 'aigle' (eagle), from Latin 'aquila'.
