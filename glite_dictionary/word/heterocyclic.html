heterocyclic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhɛt̬.ə.roʊˈsaɪ.klɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɛt.ər.əˈsɪk.lɪk / (BrE)
(of a compound or ring) containing a closed ring of atoms in which at least one atom is not carbon.
containing a non-carbon atom in a ring (chemistry)
medical

Examples

Many pharmaceuticals are based on heterocyclic compounds for their biological activity.
Pyridine is a classic example of a heterocyclic aromatic compound.
Chemists synthesized a new heterocyclic molecule to study its potential as an antibiotic.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: homocyclic - containing a ring made only of carbon atoms (chemistry)

Etymology

From Greek heteros (different) + kyklos (circle, ring).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɛt̬ɚˈsaɪklɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəˈsɪklɪk / (BrE)
A chemical compound that contains a ring structure composed of atoms of at least two different elements (typically carbon and another element, such as nitrogen, oxygen, or sulfur).
compound with a heterocyclic ring
medical

Examples

Many pharmaceuticals are based on heterocyclic structures to improve efficacy and specificity.
The researcher synthesized a new heterocyclic to test its properties as an antibiotic.
Some dyes contain a heterocyclic component that affects their color and reactivity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heterocycle - a ring structure composed of more than one kind of atom

Etymology

From hetero- (different) and cyclic (circle or ring), referring to rings of different elements.
