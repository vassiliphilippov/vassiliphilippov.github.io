equalization

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːkwələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌiːkwəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making things equal or uniform, especially by adjusting differences between them
the act of making equal or uniform

Examples

The government introduced tax equalization to reduce disparities between regions.
Audio engineers use sound equalization to balance frequencies for a clearer mix.
The equalization of educational opportunities remains a key goal in many societies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: standardization - the process of making things conform to a standard
  • Antonym: differentiation - the process of becoming or making something different

Etymology

From Latin 'aequalis' (equal) + English suffix '-ization'.
