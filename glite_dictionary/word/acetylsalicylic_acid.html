acetylsalicylic acid

1. NOUN

/ əˌsiːtəlˌsælɪˈsɪlɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / əˌsiːtəlˌsælɪˈsɪlɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound used as an analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and antipyretic drug, commonly known as aspirin
the chemical name for aspirin
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended acetylsalicylic acid to help reduce inflammation and relieve the pain in her joints.
Laboratory analysis confirmed that the tablets contained pure acetylsalicylic acid as the active ingredient.
Some people are allergic to acetylsalicylic acid and should avoid medications that contain it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aspirin - common name for acetylsalicylic acid, used as an analgesic and antipyretic

Etymology

From acetyl (referring to the acetyl group) + salicylic (from salicylic acid, from Latin salix 'willow').
