Rupicola peruviana

1. NOUN

/ ruːˈpɪkələ pəˈruːviənə / (AmE), / ruːˈpɪkələ pəˈruːviːənə / (BrE)
A brightly colored bird of the Andes, known as the Andean cock-of-the-rock, recognized for its vivid orange plumage and large fan-shaped crest.
Andean cock-of-the-rock bird

Examples

We spotted a Rupicola peruviana displaying its vibrant plumage in the misty Andean forest.
The male Rupicola peruviana attracts mates with an elaborate courtship dance.
Researchers travel to Peru to study the unique habits of Rupicola peruviana.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rupicola rupicola - Guianan cock-of-the-rock, a closely related species

Etymology

From Latin; Rupicola meaning 'rock-dweller' and peruviana referring to Peru.
