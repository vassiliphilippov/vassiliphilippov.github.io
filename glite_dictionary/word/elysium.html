Elysium

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈliːziəm / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪzɪəm / (BrE)
a place or state of perfect happiness; paradise
a place/state of perfect happiness

Examples

After years of hardship, she felt as if she had finally entered Elysium.
Poets often describe their vision of Elysium as a land of eternal spring.
He spoke of his childhood home as his personal Elysium, a place of pure joy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paradise - a place or condition of extreme happiness
  • Synonym: utopia - an imagined place where everything is perfect

Etymology

From Greek *Elysion* (field of the blessed after death in Greek mythology).

2. PROPN

/ ɪˈlɪziəm / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪziəm / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the place where the blessed after death dwell in perfect happiness; a conception of paradise.
paradise for the blessed in Greek mythology

Examples

In many poems, heroes who died honorably are said to rest in Elysium for eternity.
The garden was so peaceful and beautiful that it reminded her of Elysium itself.
Ancient Greeks believed the bravest souls would dwell in Elysium after death.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paradise - a place or state of perfect happiness
  • Antonym: underworld - the realm of the dead in various mythologies

Etymology

From Latin 'Elysium', from Greek 'Ēlýsion (pedíon)' meaning 'Elysian (plain)'.
