ballcock

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔl.kɑk / (AmE), / ˈbɔːl.kɒk / (BrE)
a device consisting of a floating ball connected to a valve, used to control the water level in a tank or cistern
floating-ball valve in tank/cistern

Examples

The plumber replaced the faulty ballcock to stop the tank from overflowing.
A broken ballcock can result in continuous water running into the toilet cistern.
Modern toilet systems often use a ballcock to automatically refill the bowl after flushing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: float valve - valve that uses a float to control fluid level

Etymology

From 'ball', referring to the float, and 'cock', an old term for a valve or tap.
