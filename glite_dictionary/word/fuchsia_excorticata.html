Fuchsia excorticata

1. NOUN

/ ˈfjuːʃə ɛkˌskɔːr.tɪˈkɑː.tə / (AmE), / ˈfjuːʃə ɛkˌskɔː.tɪˈkɑː.tə / (BrE)
A species of deciduous shrub or small tree native to New Zealand, with maroon flowers and rough bark.
New Zealand shrub or small tree with maroon flowers

Examples

The Fuchsia excorticata is commonly known as the tree fuchsia in New Zealand.
Botanists admire Fuchsia excorticata for its striking maroon flowers and rough, peeling bark.
Many native birds feed on the berries of Fuchsia excorticata during the summer months.

Etymology

Genus name from German botanist Leonard Fuchs; species name from Latin 'excorticatus' (stripped of bark).
