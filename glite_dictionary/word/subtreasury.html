subtreasury

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˈtrɛʒəri / (AmE), / sʌbˈtrɛʒəri / (BrE)
a subordinate treasury or a place designated for the deposit and custody of government funds, often under a central or state treasury
a subordinate treasury or deposit place

Examples

The government established a subtreasury in each major city to handle local transactions.
Funds were safely stored in the regional subtreasury until they could be transferred to the capital.
The audit revealed discrepancies in the records of the provincial subtreasury.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: treasury - a place where money or valuables are kept; government department managing public finances

Etymology

From sub- (meaning 'under' or 'secondary') and treasury; first used in the context of the U.S. Treasury system in the 19th century.
