Burhinidae

1. NOUN

/ bɜːrˈhɪnɪdiː / (AmE), / bɜːˈhɪnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large wading birds, known as stone-curlews, resembling plovers and found in warm regions.
family of large wading birds (stone-curlews)

Examples

The Burhinidae are most commonly found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and deserts.
As members of the Burhinidae, stone-curlews are characterized by their large eyes and cryptic plumage.
Scientists are studying the global distribution of Burhinidae to understand changes in migratory bird patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stone-curlew - a bird species within the family Burhinidae

Etymology

From New Latin Burhinidae, based on the type genus Burhinus.
