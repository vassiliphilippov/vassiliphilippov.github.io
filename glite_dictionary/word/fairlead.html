fairlead

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛrˌlid / (AmE), / ˈfeəˌliːd / (BrE)
A device, such as a pulley or ring, used on a ship to guide a rope or cable and prevent chafing.
rope-guiding device on a ship

Examples

The sailor carefully threaded the line through the fairlead to avoid damaging the rope.
Modern yachts often use stainless steel fairleads for durability and smooth operation.
When docking, always check that the fairlead is properly aligned with the mooring line.

Etymology

From 'fair' meaning 'smooth' or 'clear' + 'lead', meaning 'to guide'.
