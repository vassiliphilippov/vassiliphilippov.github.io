fesse

1. NOUN

/ fɛs / (AmE), / fɛs / (BrE)
(Heraldry) A broad horizontal band across the middle of a shield as an ordinary.
broad horizontal band on a heraldic shield

Examples

The family crest features a silver fesse on a blue background.
A fesse is often used in coats of arms to represent the military belt or girdle of honour.
On the ancient shield, the fesse divides the upper and lower fields with a bold stripe.

Etymology

From Old French 'fesse', from Latin 'fascia' (band, sash).
