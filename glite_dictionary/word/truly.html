truly

1. ADV

/ ˈtruːli / (AmE), / ˈtruːli / (BrE)
In a sincere and genuine way; without pretense.
in a sincere way; genuinely

Examples

They apologized truly for the mistake they had made.
I can truly say that I have never seen anything like it.
He truly believes that honesty is the best policy.

Synset Examples

she praised him sincerely for his victory
was unfeignedly glad to see his old teacher
we are truly sorry for the inconvenience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sincerely - in a genuine and heartfelt way
  • Antonym: falsely - in a deceptive or insincere way

Etymology

From Middle English 'truli', from 'true' + '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ ˈtruːli / (AmE), / ˈtruːli / (BrE)
really; used to emphasize the truth or accuracy of a statement or to intensify an adjective.
really; in truth; as an intensifier

Examples

She was truly grateful for all the help she received.
That was a truly remarkable performance by the young violinist.
Do you truly believe we can finish the project by Friday?

Synset Examples

in truth, moral decay hastened the decline of the Roman Empire
really, you shouldn't have done it
a truly awful book

Related Terms

  • Synonym: really - used to emphasize a statement or opinion
  • Synonym: certainly - used to emphasize the truth of something

Etymology

From Middle English 'trewely', from Old English 'trēowlīce' (faithfully, honestly), from 'trēowe' (faith, loyalty).

3. ADV

/ ˈtruːli / (AmE), / ˈtruːli / (BrE)
in a way that reflects the truth, facts, or reality; genuinely or really.
in a true or genuine way

Examples

She truly understands how difficult this situation is for everyone involved.
Do you truly believe he will change his mind?
The novel is truly inspiring and left a lasting impression on me.

Synset Examples

she was now truly American
a genuinely open society
they don't really listen to us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: really - in actual fact, as opposed to what is said or imagined
  • Antonym: falsely - in a manner not in accordance with the truth

Etymology

From Middle English trewely, from Old English trēowlīċe (faithfully, honestly), from trēowe (true).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00037620-r
Synset POS: r
