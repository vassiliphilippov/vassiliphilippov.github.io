restricted

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈstrɪktɪd / (AmE), / rɪˈstrɪktɪd / (BrE)
(of documents) officially classified at the lowest level, indicating limited circulation and requiring some protection from disclosure.
lowest level of document classification

Examples

Access to restricted documents is allowed only to authorized personnel.
The file was marked as restricted and could not be viewed by the public.
Even though it's restricted, the information is less sensitive than higher classifications.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: confidential - a higher level of document classification indicating greater secrecy

Etymology

From Latin 'restringere' meaning 'to confine, bind back'.

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈstrɪktɪd / (AmE), / rɪˈstrɪktɪd / (BrE)
Limited in extent, amount, or access; subject to specific controls or limitations.
limited or subject to restriction

Examples

Only authorized personnel are allowed in the restricted area.
The company granted us restricted access to the financial data.
Because of the confidential information, the document has a restricted classification.

Synset Examples

of restricted importance

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unrestricted - not controlled or limited; free
  • Synonym: limited - not great in amount, number, or extent

Etymology

Past participle of 'restrict', from Latin 'restrictus' (drawn tight).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02009323-a
Synset POS: a
