Salvia farinacea

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæl.vi.ə ˌfær.ɪˈneɪ.si.ə / (AmE), / ˈsal.vɪ.ə ˌfaː.rɪˈneɪ.si.ə / (BrE)
A species of sage native to Texas and adjacent Mexico, commonly known as Texas sage, with intensely blue flowers.
a species of sage with blue flowers

Examples

The garden center recommended planting Salvia farinacea for a splash of vibrant blue color in summer.
Bees and butterflies are often attracted to the blossoms of Salvia farinacea.
Unlike many sages, Salvia farinacea can thrive in hot, dry Texas climates.

Etymology

From Latin: 'salvia' (sage) and 'farinacea' (mealy, referring to the powdery texture of parts of the plant).
