incivility

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnˌsɪˈvɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɪn.sɪˈvɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
rude or impolite behavior; lack of courtesy or politeness, especially when deliberate
rude or impolite behavior

Examples

The manager would not tolerate incivility from customers or employees.
Even small acts of incivility can damage workplace morale over time.
Her incivility at the dinner table made everyone uncomfortable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rudeness - the quality of being impolite or disrespectful
  • Antonym: civility - formal politeness and courtesy in behavior or speech

Etymology

From Latin incivilitas (discourtesy), from in- (not) + civilitas (courtesy, civility).
