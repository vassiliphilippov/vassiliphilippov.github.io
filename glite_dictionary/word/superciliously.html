superciliously

1. ADV

/ ˌsuː.pɚˈsɪl.i.əs.li / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈsɪl.i.əs.li / (BrE)
in a way that shows haughty disdain or the attitude of being superior to others
in a haughty or disdainful manner

Examples

She glanced superciliously at the waiter who forgot her order.
He spoke superciliously about their efforts, making everyone feel small.
The art critic superciliously dismissed the young painter's latest exhibition.

Synset Examples

‘I don't believe in these customs,’ he said sneeringly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrogantly - in a manner that shows an exaggerated sense of one's own importance
  • Synonym: haughtily - in a disdainful or proud manner
  • Antonym: modestly - in a way that is humble or not boastful

Etymology

From Latin supercilium (eyebrow) via supercilious + -ly.
