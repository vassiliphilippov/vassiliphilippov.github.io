cave

1. NOUN

/ keɪv / (AmE), / keɪv / (BrE)
a large natural underground chamber, typically with an opening to the surface
natural underground chamber

Examples

The explorers discovered a hidden cave behind the waterfall.
Many bats make their home in the dark cave near the beach.
The children were excited to venture into the mysterious cave during their field trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grotto - a small picturesque cave, especially an artificial one
  • Synonym: cavern - a large cave or chamber in a cave

Etymology

From Latin 'cavus' (hollow).

/ keɪv / (AmE), / keɪv / (BrE)
(of a force such as water or wind) to hollow out something as if forming a cave or opening.
to hollow out as if making a cave

Examples

The flowing river gradually began caving the banks, widening its course each year.
Strong winds cave the rock, shaping the dramatic formations found in the desert.
