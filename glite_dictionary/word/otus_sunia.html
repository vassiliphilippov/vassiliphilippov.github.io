Otus sunia

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊtəs ˈsuːniə / (AmE), / ˈəʊtəs ˈsuːnɪə / (BrE)
A small species of owl known as the Asian scops owl, found across southern Asia.
species of Asian scops owl

Examples

Birdwatchers were thrilled to spot an Otus sunia perched quietly in the bamboo thicket.
The call of the Otus sunia is often heard at dusk in the forests of India.
Researchers have noted that Otus sunia feeds primarily on insects and small vertebrates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: owl - a nocturnal bird of prey with large forward-facing eyes

Etymology

From Latin 'Otus' (a genus of owls) and 'sunia' (species epithet).
