craniology

1. NOUN

/ ˌkreɪniˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌkreɪniˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of the size, shape, and structure of human skulls, especially in relation to different races
study of human skulls

Examples

The museum displays several old texts on craniology and early human anthropology.
In the nineteenth century, craniology was widely practiced as a scientific discipline.
Many researchers have criticized the biases inherent in historical studies of craniology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phrenology - pseudo-scientific study of skull shape to determine mental traits

Etymology

From Latin 'cranium' (skull) + Greek '-logia' (study).
