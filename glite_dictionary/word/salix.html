Salix

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlɪks / (AmE), / ˈseɪlɪks / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous trees and shrubs known as willows, found widely in temperate regions.
genus of plants; willows

Examples

The garden features several varieties of Salix that thrive in moist soil.
Many species in the Salix genus are prized for their flexible branches used in basket weaving.
Researchers are studying Salix for its potential in environmental restoration projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - A tree or shrub of the genus Salix, with slender branches and narrow leaves.

Etymology

From Latin 'salix' meaning 'willow'.
