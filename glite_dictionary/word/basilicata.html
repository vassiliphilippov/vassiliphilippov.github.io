Basilicata

1. PROPN

/ bəˌsɪlɪˈkɑːtə / (AmE), / baˌsiːlɪˈkɑːtə / (BrE)
a region of southern Italy, forming the instep of the Italian peninsula
region of southern Italy

Examples

Many tourists visit Basilicata to explore its ancient towns and beautiful landscapes.
Matera, a city in Basilicata, is famous for its cave dwellings.
The cuisine of Basilicata is renowned for its traditional flavors and local ingredients.

Etymology

From Italian 'Basilicata', origin uncertain; possibly from Byzantine Greek 'basilikos' (royal or relating to a governor).
