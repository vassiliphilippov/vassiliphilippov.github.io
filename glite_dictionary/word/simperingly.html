simperingly

1. ADV

/ ˈsɪmpərɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpərɪŋli / (BrE)
In a manner that is affectedly coy, self-conscious, or lacking in confidence and determination, often with a silly or ingratiating smile.
in a coy or self-conscious manner

Examples

She apologized simperingly, clearly hoping to avoid serious consequences.
He stood simperingly at the entrance, unable to assert himself.
The student answered the question simperingly, lacking the confidence to speak boldly.

Synset Examples

simperingly, the accused begged for mercy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coyly - in a shy or modest manner, often to be alluring

Etymology

From 'simper' (to smile in a silly, self-conscious way) + '-ingly' (adverbial suffix).
