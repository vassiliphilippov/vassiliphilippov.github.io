Zen

1. NOUN

/ zɛn / (AmE), / zɛn / (BrE)
A Japanese school of Mahayana Buddhism that emphasizes meditation, intuition, and direct experience to attain enlightenment.
Japanese school of Buddhism focused on meditation

Examples

She has practiced Zen for many years, finding peace through meditation.
The teachings of Zen encourage mindfulness and living in the present moment.
Many artists draw inspiration from the principles of Zen in their creative processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Buddhism - A widespread Asian religion or philosophy

Etymology

From Japanese 'Zen', from Chinese 'Chán', from Sanskrit 'dhyāna' (meditation).

3. NOUN

/ zɛn / (AmE), / zɛn / (BrE)
A form of Buddhism that emphasizes meditation and direct, intuitive insight as the path to enlightenment.
Buddhist school emphasizing meditation and intuition

Examples

He found that practicing Zen meditation helped him cope with stress.
The teachings of Zen focus on achieving enlightenment through direct experience.
Many people are drawn to Zen for its simplicity and emphasis on mindfulness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Buddhism - A major world religion and philosophy founded by Siddhartha Gautama

Etymology

From Japanese 禅 (Zen), from Chinese 禪 (Chán), ultimately from Sanskrit ध्यान (dhyāna, 'meditation').
