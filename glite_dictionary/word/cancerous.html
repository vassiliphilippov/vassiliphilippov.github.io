cancerous

1. ADJ

/ ˈkæn.sɚ.əs / (AmE), / ˈkæn.sər.əs / (BrE)
containing, consisting of, or caused by cancer; affected with cancer.
relating to or affected by cancer

Examples

The doctor was concerned that the cancerous tissue might spread quickly.
A cancerous tumor can pose a serious risk to a patient's health.
The laboratory tests confirmed that the cells were indeed cancerous.

Synset Examples

a cancerous growth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malignant - (of a disease) very virulent or infectious, especially cancer
  • Antonym: benign - (of a disease) not harmful in effect, especially when referring to tumors

Etymology

From Latin 'cancerosus' (full of cancer), from 'cancer' (crab, tumor).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkænsɚəs / (AmE), / ˈkænsərəs / (BrE)
(of something negative or harmful) spreading or growing uncontrollably, like a cancer; having a dangerous, destructive influence that increases over time.
spreading destructively, like a cancer

Examples

Remorse was cancerous within him, eating away at his conscience.
Misinformation can have a cancerous effect on public trust.
Corruption became so cancerous in the organization that it destroyed morale.

Synset Examples

remorse was cancerous within him
pornography is cancerous to the moral development of our children

Etymology

From Latin cancerosus (full of tumors), from cancer (crab, cancer).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00228893-s
Synset POS: s
