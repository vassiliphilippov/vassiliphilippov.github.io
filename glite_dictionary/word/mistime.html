to mistime

1. VERB

/ mɪsˈtaɪm / (AmE), / mɪsˈtaɪm / (BrE)
to do something at the wrong time or with incorrect timing
to time incorrectly

Examples

He mistimed his jump and landed awkwardly on the mat.
If you mistime your swing, you won't hit the ball properly.
She mistimed her entry and missed the opening scene.

Synset Examples

She mistimed the marathon runner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: time - to schedule or measure when something occurs

Etymology

From the prefix 'mis-' (wrongly, badly) + 'time' (verb).
