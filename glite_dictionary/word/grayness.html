grayness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪnəs / (BrE)
a neutral color that is midway between white and black; the quality or state of being gray in color
the state or quality of being gray

Examples

The grayness of the sky made the afternoon feel dreary and cold.
She painted the walls a soft grayness to create a calm and neutral space.
He could see the grayness of dawn seeping through the curtains as he woke up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gray - a color between black and white
  • Degree Higher: whiteness - the quality or state of being white
  • Degree Lower: blackness - the quality or state of being black

Etymology

From 'gray' + '-ness', forming an abstract noun indicating the quality or state of being gray.
