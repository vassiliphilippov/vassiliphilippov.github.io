self-coloured

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛlf ˌkʌlɚd / (AmE), / ˈsɛlf ˌkʌləd / (BrE)
having the same colour throughout; not variegated or patterned.
of uniform colour throughout

Examples

The curtains were self-coloured, providing a simple and elegant look to the room.
She preferred self-coloured fabrics for her sewing projects, avoiding prints and patterns.
The self-coloured roses stood out among the more variegated varieties in the garden.

Synset Examples

solid color

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solid-colour - having a single, uniform colour with no pattern or variation

Etymology

Formed from 'self' (meaning 'same') + 'coloured', denoting something coloured all over in the same hue.
