bullate

1. ADJ

/ ˈbʊ.leɪt / (AmE), / ˈbʊ.leɪt / (BrE)
(of leaves or surfaces) having a blistered or puckered appearance, as if swollen in places.
blistered or puckered (esp. leaves)

Examples

The bullate texture of the leaf helps it retain water in arid environments.
Upon examination, the botanist described the specimen as having distinctly bullate margins.
Some ornamental plants are chosen for their unusual bullate foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blistered - having the appearance of blisters; swollen in spots
  • Antonym: smooth - even and without any roughness or irregularities

Etymology

From Latin bullatus (covered with bubbles), from bulla (bubble).
