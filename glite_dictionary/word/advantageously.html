advantageously

1. ADV

/ ˌæd.vənˈteɪ.dʒəs.li / (AmE), / ˌæd.vənˈteɪ.dʒəs.li / (BrE)
in a way that provides an advantage or benefit; in a manner likely to result in success or gain
in a beneficial or advantageous way

Examples

The company invested its resources advantageously to gain a stronger market position.
He arranged his schedule advantageously to maximize his study time for exams.
If you negotiate advantageously, you may receive extra benefits from the deal.

Synset Examples

she married well
The children were settled advantageously in Seattle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beneficially - in a manner resulting in benefit
  • Antonym: unfavorably - in a manner that is not advantageous

Etymology

From 'advantageous' + '-ly', the adverbial suffix.
