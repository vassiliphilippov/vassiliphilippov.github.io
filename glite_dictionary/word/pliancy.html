pliancy

1. NOUN

/ ˈplaɪənsi / (AmE), / ˈplaɪənsi / (BrE)
the quality of being easily adaptable in mind or character; flexibility or willingness to yield to influence or change
adaptability or flexibility of character

Examples

Her pliancy made her an exceptional diplomat, able to mediate in difficult negotiations.
The company's success owes much to the pliancy of its management style.
Some critics argue that excessive pliancy can be mistaken for a lack of principles.

Synset Examples

he was valued for his reliability and pliability
he increased the leanness and suppleness of the organization

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flexibility - the quality of bending easily without breaking; willingness to change or compromise
  • Antonym: rigidity - inflexibility; unwillingness to change or adapt

Etymology

Formed from 'pliant' + '-cy', modeled after 'compliancy'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈplaɪ.ən.si / (AmE), / ˈplaɪ.ən.si / (BrE)
The quality or state of being easily bent, flexible, or adaptable.
flexibility; adaptability

Examples

The pliancy of the material makes it ideal for intricate designs.
Her pliancy in negotiations allowed both sides to reach a compromise.
The tree's pliancy enables it to bend rather than break in strong winds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flexibility - the quality of bending easily without breaking
  • Antonym: rigidity - the quality of being stiff, unyielding, or inflexible

Etymology

From Middle French *pliance* (flexibility), from the verb *plier* (to bend), from Latin *plicare* (to fold).
