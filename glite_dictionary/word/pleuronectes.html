Pleuronectes

1. NOUN

/ plʊˈrɒn.ɛk.tiːz / (AmE), / plʊəˈrɒn.ɛk.tiːz / (BrE)
A genus of flatfish in the family Pleuronectidae, including species such as the European plaice.
genus of flatfish

Examples

The genus Pleuronectes includes several species of commercially important flatfish.
One well-known member of Pleuronectes is the European plaice found in northern waters.
In marine biology, Pleuronectes plays a key role in the study of benthic fish populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pleuronectidae - the family of flatfish including Pleuronectes

Etymology

From Greek 'pleura' (side) and 'nectes' (swimmer), indicating a fish that swims on its side.
