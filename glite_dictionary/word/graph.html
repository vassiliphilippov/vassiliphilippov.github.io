graph

1. VERB

/ ɡræf / (AmE), / ɡrɑːf / (BrE)
to plot or represent data in the form of a graph
to plot data on a graph

Examples

Please graph these results to illustrate the trend over time.
The students graphed the data using a computer program for their science project.
She spent the afternoon graphing temperature changes throughout the week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chart - to make a chart of; to represent data with a chart
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01759135-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ɡræf / (AmE), / ɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a diagram that shows the relationship between two or more sets of numbers or other data, usually as lines, bars, or points on an axis
visual diagram displaying data

Examples

The teacher drew a graph on the board to explain the results.
This graph shows the rise in temperature over the last decade.
I included a bar graph in my report to make the data easier to understand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chart - a sheet of information, often presented as a table or in a visual form such as a graph
  • Synonym: diagram - a simplified drawing showing the appearance, structure, or workings of something

Etymology

From Greek 'graphō' (to write or draw) via French 'graphe'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07012462-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ɡræf / (AmE), / ɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a diagram showing the relationship between two or more quantities, typically plotted as points, lines, curves, or bars on a set of axes
diagram showing relationships between variables

Examples

The teacher used a graph to show the increase in temperature over time.
Before the presentation, she plotted the survey data on a graph for better visualization.
Our math homework involved drawing a graph of the quadratic equation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diagram - a simplified drawing showing the appearance, structure, or workings of something
  • Antonym: table - a set of facts or figures systematically displayed

From Greek 'graphō' (to write, draw).
