concourse

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnˌkɔrs / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋˌkɔːs / (BrE)
a large open area where people gather, especially in a public building such as a station or airport; also, a coming together of people
large public gathering area

Examples

The commuters waited in the bustling concourse of the train station.
A large concourse formed outside the stadium before the concert began.
We met by chance in the airport's main concourse before our flights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gathering - an assembly or meeting of people

Etymology

From Latin 'concursus' (a running together, assembly), from 'concurrere' (to run together).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːnˌkɔrs / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋ.kɔːs / (BrE)
A large open hall or wide passage in a public building, especially in airports or stations, where crowds can assemble or walk.
wide hallway or open area in a building

Examples

The train station's main concourse was filled with travelers rushing to catch their trains.
We met under the large digital clock in the airport concourse.
Vendors set up their booths along the concourse during the annual festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corridor - a long passage in a building from which doors lead into rooms
  • Synonym: hallway - a passage in a building that leads to rooms

Etymology

From Latin 'concursus' (a running together, assembly).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːnˌkɔrs / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋˌkɔːs / (BrE)
a large group or gathering of people assembled together
a large gathering of people

Examples

A concourse of fans filled the stadium after the team's victory.
The mayor addressed the concourse gathered in the main square.
A sudden concourse formed when the celebrity arrived.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crowd - a large number of people gathered together

Etymology

From Latin 'concursus' (a running together, assembly), from 'concurrere' (to run together).
