immanent

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪmənənt / (AmE), / ˈɪmənənt / (BrE)
(of a quality, especially a divine presence) existing or operating within; inherent in and throughout something.
inherent; existing within something

Examples

The belief that the sacred is immanent in all things is central to many spiritual traditions.
She argued that creativity is immanent in human beings, not learned from outside.
The philosopher described moral law as immanent in the fabric of society.

Synset Examples

ambition is immanent in human nature
we think of God as immanent in nature

Related Terms

  • Antonym: transcendent - existing outside or beyond the normal limits; not inherent
  • Synonym: inherent - existing as a natural or essential part of something

Etymology

From Latin immanens, present participle of imminere (to remain within).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪm.ə.nənt / (AmE), / ˈɪm.ə.nənt / (BrE)
Existing or taking place entirely within the mind rather than being expressed outwardly; inherent and internal.
existing within the mind; internal

Examples

The philosopher argued that thought is an immanent process, not dependent on external expression.
Her immanent reasoning led her to a decision without any spoken words.
Some theories of perception emphasize the immanent nature of mental acts.

Synset Examples

a cognition is an immanent act of mind

Related Terms

  • Antonym: transcendent - existing or operating outside or beyond the limits of the mind or material world

Etymology

From Latin 'immanens', present participle of 'immanēre' (to remain in, dwell in).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01277020-a
Synset POS: a
