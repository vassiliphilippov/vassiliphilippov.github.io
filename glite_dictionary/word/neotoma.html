Neotoma

1. NOUN

/ niˈoʊtoʊmə / (AmE), / niːˈəʊtəmə / (BrE)
A genus of North American rodents, commonly called packrats, known for collecting various objects and building complex nests.
a genus of rodents called packrats

Examples

The desert Neotoma builds elaborate nests from twigs, leaves, and anything it can find.
Biologists observed a colony of Neotoma near the edge of the forest.
Many species within the genus Neotoma are found throughout western North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: packrat - a rodent of the genus Neotoma, known for hoarding objects

Etymology

From Neo- (new) and Greek tomē (cutting), referring to the structure of their teeth.

2. NOUN

/ niˈoʊtəmə / (AmE), / niˈəʊtəmə / (BrE)
A genus of rodents native to North America, commonly known as packrats or woodrats.
a genus of rodents (packrats)

Examples

The Neotoma are sometimes called woodrats due to their nesting behavior.
Scientists studied several species within the Neotoma genus to learn more about rodent adaptation.
Nests built by Neotoma often contain a variety of shiny objects collected from the surrounding area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: packrat - informal term for members of genus Neotoma, rodents known for collecting items

Etymology

From Greek 'neos' (new) + 'tome' (cut, section), used in taxonomy.
