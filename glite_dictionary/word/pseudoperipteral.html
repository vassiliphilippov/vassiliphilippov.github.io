pseudoperipteral

1. ADJ

/ suːˌdoʊpəˈrɪp.tər.əl / (AmE), / sjuːˌdəʊpəˈrɪp.tər.əl / (BrE)
(of a building, especially in classical architecture) having columns arranged so as to appear to surround the structure, but with some columns engaged or embedded in the walls rather than standing free.
having columns apparently circling a structure

Examples

The Roman temple is considered pseudoperipteral because its side columns are embedded in the cella walls.
Architects admired the pseudoperipteral design for its harmonious façade and illusion of a colonnade.
A pseudoperipteral plan blends aesthetic appeal with practical structural support in classical buildings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: peripteral - having a single row of free-standing columns all around a structure

Etymology

From pseudo- (false) + peripteral (having a single row of columns all around), from Greek peripteron (building surrounded by a row of columns).
