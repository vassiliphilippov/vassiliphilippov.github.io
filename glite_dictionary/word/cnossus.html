Cnossus

1. PROPN

/ ˈnɑːsəs / (AmE), / ˈnɒsəs / (BrE)
an ancient city on the island of Crete, famous for its Bronze Age Minoan civilization and archaeological ruins
ancient city on Crete, site of Minoan civilization

Examples

Archaeologists have uncovered remarkable Minoan artifacts at Cnossus.
The ruins of Cnossus attract thousands of visitors each year.
Ancient legends say that the labyrinth of the Minotaur was located at Cnossus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Knossos - alternative spelling of Cnossus, ancient city on Crete

Etymology

From Greek Knossos (Κνωσός), the ancient name of the city.
