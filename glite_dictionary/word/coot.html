coot

1. NOUN

/ kut / (AmE), / kuÀêt / (BrE)
a medium-sized water bird, usually slate-black, with a white bill and forehead, that resembles a duck and belongs to the rail family (Rallidae)
slate-black water bird resembling a duck

Examples

A flock of coots paddled quietly along the edge of the lake.
The distinctive white forehead of the coot makes it easy to identify.
During migration, hundreds of coots can gather on large wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moorhen - another water bird in the rail family

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'cote', related to Dutch 'koet' (coot).
