crustaceous

1. ADJ

/ krʌˈsteɪʃəs / (AmE), / krʌˈsteɪʃəs / (BrE)
having or resembling a hard outer shell or crust, especially like that of a crustacean.
having a hard outer shell or crust

Examples

The snail’s crustaceous covering protects it from predators.
Some soils in arid regions develop a crustaceous layer after heavy rainfall.
The artist depicted the crab’s crustaceous armor in vivid detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crustacean - an animal of the group including crabs, lobsters, and shrimp, with hard shells
  • Synonym: shell-like - resembling or having the qualities of a shell

Etymology

From Latin crusta (crust, rind) + -aceous (having the nature of).

2. ADJ

/ krəˈsteɪʃəs / (AmE), / krʌˈsteɪʃəs / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of crustaceans, a class of mostly aquatic arthropods such as crabs and lobsters
relating to crustaceans (crabs, lobsters, etc.)

Examples

Several crustaceous species thrive in the coral reefs of the Pacific Ocean.
The fossil displayed a crustaceous exoskeleton, indicating its classification as a prehistoric shrimp.
Experts believe that crustaceous animals are a vital part of the aquatic food chain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crustacean - an animal of the class Crustacea, such as a crab or lobster

Etymology

From Latin 'crustaceus' meaning 'having a shell'.
