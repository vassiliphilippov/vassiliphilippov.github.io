Scomber

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɑmbər / (AmE), / ˈskɒmbə / (BrE)
a genus of mackerels in the family Scombridae, including several important food fish species.
genus of mackerel fish

Examples

The Atlantic mackerel belongs to the genus Scomber and is widely distributed in temperate waters.
Several species within Scomber are commercially important for fisheries around the world.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of Scomber to understand their population dynamics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mackerel - an edible fish of the Scombridae family, known for its oily flesh

Etymology

From Latin 'scomber' meaning 'mackerel', from Ancient Greek 'skombros'.
