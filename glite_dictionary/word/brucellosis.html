brucellosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌbruːsəˈloʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌbruːsɪˈləʊsɪs / (BrE)
an infectious bacterial disease transmitted to humans from animals, especially through contact with infected animals, meat, or milk, causing fever and headache
infectious disease from animals, causes fever
medical

Examples

The veterinarian suspected brucellosis after the farmer's repeated fevers and muscle pain.
Public health officials warned that unpasteurized milk could transmit brucellosis to consumers.
Travelers to rural areas are at risk of brucellosis if they come into contact with livestock.

Etymology

From the genus name Brucella, named after Scottish microbiologist David Bruce, combined with -osis (disease suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌbruːsəˈloʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌbruːsəˈləʊsɪs / (BrE)
an infectious disease of domestic animals, such as cattle and sheep, often causing spontaneous abortion and transmittable to humans, where it causes fever and other symptoms
infectious disease affecting animals and humans
medical

Examples

The veterinarian diagnosed the cow with brucellosis after observing several symptoms.
Farmers are advised to vaccinate their cattle to prevent the spread of brucellosis.
Humans can contract brucellosis through unpasteurized dairy products from infected animals.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Brucella' (the genus of bacteria causing the disease), named after Sir David Bruce + '-osis'.
