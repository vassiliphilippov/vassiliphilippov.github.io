Usnea

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌzniə / (AmE), / ˈʌzniə / (BrE)
A genus of widely distributed lichens with a greyish or yellowish, freely branched, pendulous thallus, often called beard lichen.
a genus of greyish, branched lichens

Examples

The forest was draped with Usnea hanging from the branches like long grey beards.
Scientists collected samples of Usnea to study its medicinal properties.
You can often spot Usnea in humid, unpolluted woodlands.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Usnea', after Arabic 'ūsniyā', meaning lichen.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌzniə / (AmE), / ˈʌzniːə / (BrE)
A genus of widely distributed lichens, typically greyish or yellow, with a pendulous, freely branched thallus, often called beard lichens.
a genus of beard lichens

Examples

The old tree was draped in Usnea, giving it a ghostly appearance.
Scientists collected samples of Usnea to study its air pollution sensitivity.
Beard lichens of the genus Usnea are often used in traditional medicine.

Etymology

From New Latin Usnea, adopted from Arabic 'usnia' (a kind of moss or lichen).
