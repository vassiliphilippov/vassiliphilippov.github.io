Boniface VIII

1. PROPN

/ ˌbɑːnəˈfeɪs ðə ˈeɪtθ / (AmE), / ˌbɒnɪˈfeɪs ði ˈeɪtθ / (BrE)
(1235–1303) Pope of the Roman Catholic Church who asserted the supremacy of the papacy over secular rulers.
Pope (1235–1303) declaring papal supremacy

Examples

Boniface VIII issued the papal bull Unam Sanctam, claiming ultimate spiritual and temporal authority.
The conflict between Boniface VIII and King Philip IV of France marked a turning point in church-state relations.
Historians debate the legacy of Boniface VIII within the context of medieval papal power.
