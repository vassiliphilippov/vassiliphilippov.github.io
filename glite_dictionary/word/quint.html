quint

1. NOUN

/ kwɪnt / (AmE), / kwɪnt / (BrE)
one of five children born at the same time to the same mother
one of five children born together

Examples

The Smith family made headlines when they welcomed a set of quints into the world.
Managing five newborns at once is a massive challenge for parents of quints.
Medical advancements have increased the survival rate of quints born prematurely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quintuplet - one of five children born at the same time
  • Degree Lower: quad - one of four children born at the same time
  • Degree Higher: sextuplet - one of six children born at the same time

Etymology

Shortened form of 'quintuplet', from Latin 'quintus' (fifth).
