St. Lucia

1. PROPN

/ ˌseɪnt ˈluːʃə / (AmE), / ˌseɪnt ˈluːʃə / (BrE)
an island country in the eastern Caribbean, located in the Windward Islands, south of Martinique
Caribbean island country

Examples

Many tourists visit St. Lucia for its beautiful beaches and volcanic mountains.
The capital of St. Lucia is Castries, located on the northwest coast.
Hurricane season poses a risk to islands like St. Lucia in the Caribbean.

Etymology

Named after Saint Lucy of Syracuse by early European explorers.

2. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈluːʃə / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈluːsiə / (BrE)
an island country in the eastern Caribbean, known for its mountainous terrain and beaches; gained independence from Great Britain in 1979
island country in the Caribbean

Examples

We spent our vacation in St. Lucia enjoying the beaches and rainforests.
St. Lucia is famous for the twin volcanic peaks known as the Pitons.
In 1979, St. Lucia achieved full independence from the United Kingdom.

Etymology

Named after Saint Lucy of Syracuse.
