yack

1. VERB

/ jæk / (AmE), / jæk / (BrE)
to talk incessantly and in a tiresome or trivial way, especially about unimportant matters
talk incessantly or tiresomely
informal slang

Examples

My coworkers yack all day about reality TV shows when I'm trying to focus.
They yacked for hours about everything and nothing.
Please stop yacking on the phone and help me with this task.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chatter - to talk quickly and continuously about trivial matters
  • Synonym: prattle - to talk at length in a foolish or inconsequential way

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound of incessant talking, related to 'yak' (slang for talk).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01039370-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ jæk / (AmE), / jæk / (BrE)
loud, incessant, or trivial talk; chatter, often considered annoying or unnecessary
noisy or trivial chatter
informal

Examples

They spent hours in the café surrounded by constant yack from the other tables.
Sometimes the endless yack during meetings makes it hard to focus.
His yack about office gossip quickly became tiresome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chatter - rapid, trivial, and continuous talk
  • Synonym: babble - foolish or meaningless talk

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'yak,' originally imitative.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07151648-n
Synset POS: n
