Trotskyism

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɑtskiˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈtrɒtskiˌɪzəm / (BrE)
A Marxist ideology based on the theories of Leon Trotsky, advocating for international proletarian revolution and opposing the policies of Stalinism.
Marxist ideology of Leon Trotsky

Examples

He wrote a book exploring the influence of Trotskyism on modern socialist movements.
During the meeting, members debated the merits of Trotskyism versus Stalinism.
Some political parties in the twentieth century were strongly inspired by Trotskyism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Stalinism - the communist policies and practices of Joseph Stalin, contrasted with Trotskyism

Etymology

From the name of Leon Trotsky (Russian revolutionary) + -ism.
