Diatomophyceae

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌæt̬əˌmoʊˈfaɪsii / (AmE), / daɪˌætəməʊˈfʌɪsɪiː / (BrE)
A class of eukaryotic algae known as diatoms, found in both marine and freshwater environments.
class of algae: diatoms
medical

Examples

The Diatomophyceae play a crucial role in the global carbon cycle as primary producers.
Researchers discovered a new species belonging to the Diatomophyceae in Antarctic waters.
Both marine and freshwater ecosystems often depend on Diatomophyceae for oxygen production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diatom - a single-celled alga with a silica cell wall

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'diatom' (from Greek 'diatomos', cut through) and '-phyceae' (from Greek 'phykos', seaweed, algae).

Scientists observed Diatomophyceae in water samples collected from diverse lakes around the world.

Related Terms

From Neo-Latin, combining 'diatoma' (a genus of diatoms) and Greek 'phyke' (seaweed, algae).
