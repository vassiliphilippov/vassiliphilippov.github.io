Hippoglossoides platessoides

1. NOUN

/ hɪpoʊˌɡlɒsɔɪˈdiːz plætɛsˈɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / hɪpəˌɡlɒsɔɪˈdiːz plætɛsˈɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A large species of flatfish, commonly known as the American plaice, found in the North Atlantic and valued as a food fish.
a large flatfish; American plaice

Examples

The fisheries along the North Atlantic coast harvest Hippoglossoides platessoides for commercial purposes.
Scientists studied the migration patterns of Hippoglossoides platessoides to better understand its habitat.
The restaurant featured a special dish prepared with freshly caught Hippoglossoides platessoides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American plaice - common name of Hippoglossoides platessoides, a flatfish species

Etymology

From Modern Latin: genus name 'Hippoglossoides' combining 'hippoglossus' (halibut genus) and Greek '-oeidēs' (like, resembling); species name from Latin 'platessa' (plaice) + '-oides' (like).
