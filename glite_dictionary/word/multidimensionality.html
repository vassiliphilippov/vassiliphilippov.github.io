multidimensionality

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌlti.daɪˌmɛnʃəˈnæləti / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪ.daɪˌmɛnʃəˈnælɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of having many different aspects, dimensions, or facets.
the state of having many aspects

Examples

The multidimensionality of the project made it both fascinating and challenging to all involved.
Researchers explored the multidimensionality of intelligence rather than viewing it as a single trait.
She admired the multidimensionality in his art, which revealed new meanings upon each viewing.

Synset Examples

This paper argues for a broader understanding of complexity; an understanding that speaks to the multidimensionality of environmental problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complexity - the state of being complex; many interconnected parts or aspects
  • Antonym: simplicity - the quality of being simple or plain; having few aspects

Etymology

From 'multidimensional' + '-ity'; 'multidimensional' from Latin 'multi-' (many) + 'dimension' (measured out).
