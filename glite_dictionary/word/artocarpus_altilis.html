Artocarpus altilis

1. NOUN

/ ɑɹ.təˌkɑɹ.pəs ælˈtɪl.ɪs / (AmE), / ˌɑː.təˈkɑː.pəs alˈtɪl.ɪs / (BrE)
A tropical tree native to the Pacific islands, cultivated for its large, starchy edible fruit commonly known as breadfruit.
tropical tree yielding breadfruit

Examples

The Artocarpus altilis is a vital food source in many Pacific island communities.
Scientists are studying Artocarpus altilis for its potential use in sustainable agriculture.
The fruit of Artocarpus altilis can be baked, boiled, or fried and used as a staple food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breadfruit - edible starchy fruit of the Artocarpus altilis tree

Etymology

From Latin; genus Artocarpus from Greek 'artos' (bread) + 'karpos' (fruit); 'altilis' meaning 'fat' or 'rich'.
