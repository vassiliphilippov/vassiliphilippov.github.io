Citrus decumana

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtrəs dɛˈkjuːmənə / (AmE), / ˈsɪtrəs dɪˈkjuːmənə / (BrE)
A southeastern Asian tree that produces large fruits resembling grapefruits, commonly known as the pomelo.
southeastern Asian tree producing pomelo fruits

Examples

The Citrus decumana tree is widely cultivated throughout Southeast Asia for its sweet, juicy fruit.
Researchers studied the growth habits of Citrus decumana in different soil conditions.
She enjoyed eating fresh Citrus decumana segments on hot summer days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pomelo - the large citrus fruit produced by Citrus decumana

Etymology

From Latin genus Citrus (citrus tree) and decumana, referring to a type of large fruit.
