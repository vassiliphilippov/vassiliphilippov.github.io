Muscardinus

1. NOUN

/ mʌsˈkɑrdɪnəs / (AmE), / mʌsˈkɑːdɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of small rodents in the family Gliridae, commonly known as hazel dormice.
genus of small rodents (hazel dormice)

Examples

The European hazel dormouse, classified as Muscardinus avellanarius, inhabits woodlands across Europe.
Researchers found several nests belonging to rodents of the genus Muscardinus in the restored forest area.
Conservation efforts focus on protecting the habitat of Muscardinus species, which are threatened in many regions.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Latin *muscardinus* (related to dormouse).

2. NOUN

/ məsˈkɑːrdɪnəs / (AmE), / mʌsˈkɑːdɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of small rodents in the family Gliridae, which includes the hazel dormouse.
rodent genus (includes hazel dormouse)

Examples

The Muscardinus genus is best known for its only living species, the hazel dormouse.
Researchers studied the habitat range of Muscardinus across Europe.
Some conservation programs focus on protecting Muscardinus populations due to their declining numbers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gliridae - the family of rodents known as dormice

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name Muscardinus, derived from Italian muscardino (hazel dormouse).
