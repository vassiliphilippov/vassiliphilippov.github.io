Hydnocarpus laurifolia

1. NOUN

/ haɪdˈnoʊ.kɑːr.pəs ˌlɔːr.ɪˈfoʊ.li.ə / (AmE), / hɪdˈnɒ.kə.pəs ˌlɔː.rɪˈfəʊ.lɪ.ə / (BrE)
A leathery-leaved tree native to western India, producing round fruits with a brown, hairy rind and an oily pulp used for hydnocarpus oil.
tree of western India; source of hydnocarpus oil

Examples

The seeds of Hydnocarpus laurifolia are pressed to extract a medicinal oil.
Botanists studied Hydnocarpus laurifolia for its unique fruit and traditional uses.
Farmers in western India have grown Hydnocarpus laurifolia for generations.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Hydnocarpus and species name laurifolia ('laurel-leaved').
