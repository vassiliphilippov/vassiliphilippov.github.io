Agrippina the Younger

1. PROPN

/ əˌɡrɪˈpinə ði ˈjʌŋɚ / (AmE), / əˌɡrɪˈpiːnə ðə ˈjʌŋə / (BrE)
(AD 15–59) A Roman empress, the wife of Claudius and mother of Nero, known for her political influence and alleged involvement in poisoning Claudius.
Roman empress, mother of Nero

Examples

Agrippina the Younger was a central figure in Roman imperial politics during the reigns of Claudius and Nero.
Many historians debate whether Agrippina the Younger truly orchestrated the death of her husband, Emperor Claudius.
The story of Agrippina the Younger illustrates the power struggles within the Roman imperial family.

Etymology

From Latin Agrippina, diminutive of Agrippa, a Roman family name.
