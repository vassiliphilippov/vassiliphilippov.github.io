nonconformity

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑːnkənˈfɔːrməti / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkənˈfɔːməti / (BrE)
the refusal or failure to conform to accepted standards, conventions, or rules of behavior
failure to conform to accepted standards

Examples

Her nonconformity made her stand out in a company that valued tradition.
The artist's nonconformity inspired others to challenge social norms.
Choosing nonconformity over acceptance, he often questioned the expectations placed upon him.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conformity - compliance with standards or rules
  • Synonym: dissent - holding or expressing opinions different from those commonly or officially held

Etymology

From non- (not) + conformity, from Late Latin conformitas (conformity).

2. NOUN

/ ˌnɑnkənˈfɔrməti / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkənˈfɔːməti / (BrE)
The state of not being in agreement or harmony with something, especially a lack of correspondence between things.
lack of harmony or correspondence

Examples

There was a clear nonconformity between the design and the client’s instructions.
His proposal was rejected due to its nonconformity with established procedures.
The inspector noted several nonconformities in the building's structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incongruity - the state of being out of place or lacking harmony
  • Antonym: agreement - state of being in accord or harmony

Etymology

From non- (not) + conformity, ultimately from Latin 'conformare' (to form).

3. NOUN

/ ˌnɑn.kənˈfɔr.mə.ti / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.kənˈfɔː.mə.ti / (BrE)
the refusal or failure to conform to established customs, attitudes, or beliefs, especially in religion or ideology
refusal to conform to established beliefs

Examples

His nonconformity made him an outsider within the strictly conservative community.
The history of scientific progress is often a story of nonconformity and challenging accepted wisdom.
Her nonconformity to traditional values inspired others to speak their minds.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orthodoxy - authorized or generally accepted theory, doctrine, or practice
  • Synonym: dissent - holding or expressing opinions that are at variance with those previously, commonly, or officially held

Etymology

From non- (not) + conformity, based on Latin conformāre (to shape, form).

4. NOUN

/ ˌnɑːnkənˈfɔrməti / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkənˈfɔːməti / (BrE)
the refusal or failure to conform to established customs, attitudes, or standards, especially in society or religion
refusal to conform to established standards

Examples

Her nonconformity made her stand out in a school known for its strict dress code.
The artist's nonconformity challenged conventional ideas about beauty and creativity.
He was often criticized for his nonconformity but remained true to his beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conformity - compliance with standards, rules, or laws

Etymology

From Latin roots: 'non-' (not) + 'conformity' from 'conformis' (of the same form).
