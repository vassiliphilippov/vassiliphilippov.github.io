worth

1. ADJ

/ wɝːθ / (AmE), / wɜːθ / (BrE)
having a specified value or amount, or considered to deserve a particular thing
having a specified value or deserving something

Examples

The painting is worth over a million dollars.
Is it worth spending so much time on this project?
The necklace wasn't worth much, but it had sentimental value.

Synset Examples

not worth his salt
worth her weight in gold
an appraisal determined the antique carved chair was worth $900

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valuable - having considerable monetary or personal value
  • Degree Higher: priceless - so valuable that its worth cannot be determined

Etymology

From Old English 'weorþ' (worthy, valuable), from Proto-Germanic '*werthaz'.

WordNet ID: oewn-02511965-s

2. NOUN

/ wɜrθ / (AmE), / wɜːθ / (BrE)
an amount or quantity of something equivalent in value to a specified sum or measurement
a quantity equivalent to a stated value

Examples

He bought five dollars' worth of stamps.
The store had a year's worth of supplies in stock.
She poured a cup's worth of milk into the batter.

Synset Examples

10 dollars worth of gasoline

Etymology

From Old English 'weorth', meaning 'valuable' or 'worthy'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13786482-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ wɜrθ / (AmE), / wɜːθ / (BrE)
(of an action, effort, or quality) deserving to be treated in a particular way; sufficiently good, important, or interesting to justify a specified action.
deserving a particular treatment or action

Examples

Your suggestion is certainly worth considering before we make a final decision.
The museum is worth a visit if you're interested in local history.
Is this old laptop worth repairing, or should I replace it?

Synset Examples

an idea worth considering
the deserving poor
a deserving cause

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deserving - having qualities worthy of reward or punishment
  • Antonym: unworthy - not deserving respect or consideration
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02596626-s
Synset POS: s

4. NOUN

/ wɝːθ / (AmE), / wɜːθ / (BrE)
The quality that makes something desirable, valuable, or useful.
desirability or value of something

Examples

The worth of the painting was recognized only years after the artist died.
She always knew the true worth of loyal friends.
Please consider the worth of the information before making your decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: value - the importance, worth, or usefulness of something
  • Antonym: worthless - having no real value or use

Etymology

From Old English 'weorþ' (value, price, worth).

Source: WordNet
