virtual

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɝː.tʃu.əl / (AmE), / ˈvɜː.tʃu.əl / (BrE)
almost or nearly as described, but not completely or according to strict definition; existing or resulting in effect though not in actual fact.
almost; in effect but not in fact

Examples

The organization has a virtual monopoly on supplying water to the town.
Despite being separated by thousands of miles, they maintained a virtual friendship through online communication.
The new policy resulted in a virtual ban on smoking in public places.

Synset Examples

a virtual dependence on charity
a virtual revolution
virtual reality

Related Terms

  • Antonym: actual - existing in fact; real; opposite of virtual
  • Synonym: nominal - existing in name only; not real or actual

Etymology

From Medieval Latin virtualis, from Latin virtus (virtue, power, efficacy).

2. ADJ

/ ˈvɝː.tʃu.əl / (AmE), / ˈvɜː.tʃu.əl / (BrE)
almost or nearly as described, but not completely or according to strict definition; being so in effect though not in fact or name
almost as described; effectively but not formally

Examples

After such a long drought, the crops were in virtual ruin.
With their lead in the polls, the candidate was a virtual certainty to win the election.
Owing to the severe budget cuts, the project was in virtual collapse.

Synset Examples

a practical failure
the once elegant temple lay in virtual ruin

Related Terms

  • Antonym: actual - existing in fact; real and not merely possible or apparent

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'virtualis', from Latin 'virtus' (virtue, efficacy, strength).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01948431-s
Synset POS: s
