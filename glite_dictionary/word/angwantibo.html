angwantibo

1. NOUN

/ æŋˈwɑntiboʊ / (AmE), / æŋˈwɒntɪbəʊ / (BrE)
A small, arboreal primate of the species Arctocebus calabarensis, native to West Africa, known for its yellow or golden fur.
West African primate with golden fur

Examples

The angwantibo is rarely seen in the wild due to its secretive habits.
Researchers discovered a new group of angwantibos in a protected rainforest area.
Unlike other primates, the angwantibo has a slow, stealthy way of moving among the branches.

Etymology

From a West African local name for the animal.
