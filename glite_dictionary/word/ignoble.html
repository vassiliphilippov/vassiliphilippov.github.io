ignoble

1. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈnoʊbəl / (AmE), / ɪɡˈnəʊbəl / (BrE)
Not belonging to the nobility; of humble or common birth.
not of the nobility; of humble birth

Examples

She was dismissed as ignoble simply because her family held no titles.
In feudal societies, an ignoble birth often limited one's opportunities in life.
The law once discriminated between ignoble and noble citizens.

Synset Examples

of ignoble (or ungentle) birth
untitled civilians

Related Terms

  • Antonym: noble - belonging to the nobility; of high social class

Etymology

From Latin ignobilis (unknown, obscure, not noble).

2. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈnoʊbəl / (AmE), / ɪɡˈnəʊbəl / (BrE)
Having or showing a lack of nobility; dishonorable or of low character, purpose, or quality.
dishonorable; lacking nobility

Examples

Many considered his actions ignoble and undeserving of praise.
She felt ashamed of her ignoble motives for helping.
The hero refused to stoop to such ignoble behavior in the face of defeat.

Synset Examples

something cowardly and ignoble in his attitude
I think it a less evil that some criminals should escape than that the government should play an ignoble part

Related Terms

  • Antonym: noble - having or showing high moral qualities; honorable
  • Synonym: dishonorable - not honorable; lacking integrity or respect

Etymology

From Latin ignobilis, from in- (not) + gnobilis (noble).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01593624-a
Synset POS: a
