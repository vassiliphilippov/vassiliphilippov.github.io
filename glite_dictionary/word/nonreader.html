nonreader

1. NOUN

/ nɑnˈriːdər / (AmE), / nɒnˈriːdə / (BrE)
a person, especially a student, who is unable or very slow to learn to read
person unable or very slow to read

Examples

Despite extra help, one student remained a nonreader throughout the year.
Identifying a nonreader early can help provide the necessary support.
Her work focuses on improving literacy for nonreaders in elementary schools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illiterate - unable to read or write

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'non-' (not) + 'reader'.

2. NOUN

/ nɑnˈriːdər / (AmE), / nɒnˈriːdə / (BrE)
a person who is unable to read, especially because they have not learned how
person unable to read

Examples

The new literacy program aims to help every nonreader in the community become confident with books.
As a child, he was a nonreader until age nine due to a lack of schooling.
The survey found that many nonreaders felt excluded from important information.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illiterate - person unable to read or write

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'non-' (not) and 'reader' (one who reads).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10218722-n
Synset POS: n
