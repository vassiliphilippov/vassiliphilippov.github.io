brougham

1. NOUN

/ ˈbruːəm / (AmE), / ˈbruːəm / (BrE)
a type of closed automobile with an open driver's seat and no roof over the driver's compartment
car with open driver's seat, closed passenger area

Examples

The vintage brougham was the highlight of the classic car show.
They restored an old brougham with intricate woodwork and leather seats.
The chauffeur drove the brougham while the passengers enjoyed the enclosed interior.

Etymology

Named after Lord Brougham (1778–1868), who originally commissioned the design for a horse-drawn carriage of this type.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbroʊəm / (AmE), / ˈbruːəm / (BrE)
A horse-drawn carriage with a roof, four wheels, and an open driver's seat in front, typically pulled by a single horse.
horse-drawn carriage with roof and open driver's seat
archaic

Examples

The guests arrived at the mansion in a splendid brougham drawn by a fine bay horse.
He admired the elegant lines of the old brougham displayed at the museum.
In Victorian London, a brougham was a popular mode of private transportation among the wealthy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carriage - a vehicle with wheels pulled by horses

Etymology

Named after Lord Brougham, who designed this style of carriage in the 19th century.
