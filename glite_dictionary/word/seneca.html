Seneca

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnɪkə / (AmE), / ˈsɛnɪkə / (BrE)
a member of the Iroquoian people traditionally living in western New York State, south of Lake Ontario
member of Native American Seneca people

Examples

The Seneca played a crucial role in the Iroquois Confederacy's political decisions.
A Seneca elder shared traditional stories with the younger generation.
Many members of the Seneca community continue to preserve their language and customs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iroquois - a member of the confederacy of Indigenous peoples originally inhabiting northeast North America

Etymology

From the name of the Seneca people, likely from their own term Onišonˀhkaʼ (place of the stone).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnɪkə / (AmE), / ˈsɛnɪkə / (BrE)
the Iroquoian language traditionally spoken by the Seneca people, part of the Iroquois Confederacy in North America.
the Iroquoian language of the Seneca people

Examples

Linguists have worked to document the Seneca language before it disappears.
He is fluent in both English and Seneca, thanks to his grandparents.
Several schools are now offering courses in Seneca to revitalize the language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cayuga - an Iroquoian language spoken by the Cayuga people

Etymology

From the name of the Seneca people, a Native American group.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06929185-n
Synset POS: n
