homomorphy

1. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊməˈmɔrfi / (AmE), / ˌhəʊməˈmɔːfi / (BrE)
(biology, mathematics) the similarity of form between organisms, objects, or structures, especially when they are not closely related evolutionarily or functionally.
similarity of form

Examples

Some unrelated animals display remarkable homomorphy due to convergent evolution.
The principle of homomorphy can be observed in various crystalline structures that share similar shapes.
Biologists study homomorphy to understand how different species can independently evolve similar forms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isomorphy - similarity of form or structure
  • Synonym: homomorphism - a mapping that preserves structure; also refers to similarity in form
  • Antonym: heteromorphy - difference in form

Etymology

From Greek homo- (same) + morphē (form).
