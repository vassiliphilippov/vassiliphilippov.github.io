Juniperus procera

1. NOUN

/ dʒuˈnɪpərəs proʊˈsɛrə / (AmE), / dʒuːˈnɪpərəs prəʊˈsɪərə / (BrE)
A tall tropical African timber tree with fragrant wood, native to eastern Africa and Arabia, also known as African pencil cedar.
tropical African timber tree

Examples

The wood from Juniperus procera is valued for its fragrance and durability.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect remaining populations of Juniperus procera.
Carpenters often use Juniperus procera wood for making high-quality furniture.

Etymology

From Latin 'Juniperus' (juniper) and 'procera' (tall, lofty).
