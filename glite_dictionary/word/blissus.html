Blissus

1. PROPN

/ ˈblɪsəs / (AmE), / ˈblɪsəs / (BrE)
A genus of insects in the family Blissidae, commonly known as chinch bugs, some species of which are agricultural pests.
a genus of chinch bugs (insects)

Examples

Several crops in the Midwest have been damaged by infestations of Blissus.
The most common type of chinch bug found in North America belongs to the genus Blissus.
Researchers are studying the life cycle of Blissus to develop better pest control methods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chinch bug - a small insect pest of grasses and crops

Etymology

From Latin, used in taxonomic naming conventions for insect genera.
