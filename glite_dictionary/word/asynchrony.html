asynchrony

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɪŋkrəni / (AmE), / əˈsɪŋkrəni / (BrE)
the state or relation of two or more things not occurring at the same time; lack of synchronization.
lack of synchronization in timing

Examples

Researchers observed asynchrony between heart rate and breathing patterns in the initial phase of the experiment.
Network delays can lead to asynchrony between data transmission and reception.
The team's movements showed noticeable asynchrony, making their performance appear uncoordinated.

Synset Examples

the stimulus produced a desynchronizing of the brain waves

Related Terms

  • Antonym: synchrony - the state of things occurring at the same time

Etymology

From Greek a- (not) + synchronos (contemporary, at the same time).
