to adverbise

1. VERB

/ ædˈvɜːrˌbaɪz / (AmE), / ədˈvɜːbaɪz / (BrE)
to enhance a sentence or phrase by adding adverbs or by making its expression more adverbial
add adverbs or make adverbial

Examples

The editor told me not to adverbise my writing too much.
He tended to adverbise every description, making the narrative longer than necessary.
Students are encouraged not to adverbise excessively in academic essays.

Synset Examples

We need to adverbise our product descriptions effectively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adjectivise - to make something more like an adjective or add adjectives
  • Degree Higher: adverb - a word that modifies a verb, adjective, or other adverb

Etymology

From 'adverb' + '-ise', patterned on similar verb formations.
