skinny-dip

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɪni dɪp / (AmE), / ˈskɪni dɪp / (BrE)
an act of swimming while naked, especially outdoors
naked swim

Examples

After midnight, they went for a spontaneous skinny-dip in the lake.
A skinny-dip under the stars was the highlight of their camping trip.
The annual skinny-dip attracted dozens of adventurous swimmers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skinny dipping - the act of swimming naked, typically outdoors

Etymology

From the colloquial phrase 'skinny dipping', meaning to swim without clothes, first recorded in the early 20th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈskɪni dɪp / (AmE), / ˈskɪni dɪp / (BrE)
to swim or bathe naked, especially for fun
swim or bathe naked
informal

Examples

After midnight, they decided to skinny-dip in the lake under the stars.
It's illegal to skinny-dip at this public beach, even late at night.
They have fond memories of skinny-dipping as teenagers during their summer holidays.

Synset Examples

The young people were skinny-dipping in the pond

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bathe - to wash or be immersed in water

Etymology

Formed in early 20th-century American English from 'skinny' (meaning 'naked') and 'dip' (to plunge into water).
