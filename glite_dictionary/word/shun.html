to shun

1. VERB

/ ʃʌn / (AmE), / ʃʌn / (BrE)
to deliberately avoid or reject someone or something, especially by excluding them from a group or community
to deliberately avoid or exclude

Examples

After the scandal, the community began to shun him completely.
She felt hurt when her colleagues started to shun her at work.
Members who break the rules may be shunned by the entire group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to avoid - to keep away from someone or something
  • Synonym: to ostracize - to exclude someone from a group or society

Etymology

From Old English scunian (to shun, abhor); cognate with German scheuen.

2. VERB

/ ʃʌn / (AmE), / ʃʌn / (BrE)
to deliberately avoid or stay away from someone or something
deliberately avoid someone or something

Examples

After the scandal, many former friends began to shun him in public.
Vegetarians often shun dishes that contain any form of meat or animal by-products.
She tried to shun publicity and preferred to live a quiet life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to avoid - to keep away from or stop oneself from doing something
  • Antonym: embrace - to accept or support willingly or enthusiastically

Etymology

From Old English *scunian* (to shun, avoid, abhor).
