incorruption

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.kəˈrʌp.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kəˈrʌp.ʃən / (BrE)
The quality of being free from corruption; integrity or honesty.
integrity; freedom from corruption

Examples

Her reputation for incorruption was well known throughout the organization.
The judge was admired for his incorruption and sense of justice.
Public officials must strive for incorruption in all aspects of their work.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: corruption - dishonest or fraudulent conduct; moral perversion
  • Synonym: integrity - the quality of being honest and having strong moral principles

Etymology

From Latin incorruptio (freedom from corruption), from in- (not) + corruptio (corruption).
