acclimatisation

1. NOUN

/ əˌklaɪmə.təˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / əˌklaɪ.mɪ.taɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of becoming accustomed to a new climate, environment, or conditions, especially through physiological or behavioral adjustment
adaptation to a new climate or environment

Examples

The team's acclimatisation to the high altitude was essential for their success on the mountain.
Rapid acclimatisation helps travelers avoid the symptoms of altitude sickness.
Successful acclimatisation to a tropical climate often takes several weeks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adaptation - the process of adjusting to new conditions
  • Antonym: maladaptation - inadequate or faulty adaptation to an environment or situation

Etymology

From French *acclimatisation*, from 'acclimater' (to acclimatize), ultimately derived from 'climat' (climate).
