to befoul

1. VERB

/ bɪˈfaʊl / (AmE), / bɪˈfaʊl / (BrE)
to make dirty, impure, or polluted; to spoil by contamination
make dirty or pollute

Examples

The oil spill will befoul the pristine coastline.
He accused them of trying to befoul his reputation with false rumors.
If you befoul this place again, you will not be welcome back.

Synset Examples

The townspeople defiled the river by emptying raw sewage into it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to contaminate - make something impure by exposure to or addition of a poisonous or polluting substance
  • Synonym: to pollute - make water, air, or soil dirty or harmful to people, animals, and plants
  • Antonym: to cleanse - make something clean or pure

Etymology

From be- (intensive prefix) + foul (dirty, unclean), Middle English.
