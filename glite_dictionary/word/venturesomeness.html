venturesomeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛn.tʃər.səm.nəs / (AmE), / ˈvɛn.tʃə.səm.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being willing to take risks or try new and exciting things; adventurousness
willingness to take risks

Examples

Her venturesomeness inspired others to embrace change and try new things.
The venturesomeness of early explorers led to incredible discoveries across the globe.
You need a bit of venturesomeness if you want to start your own business.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adventurousness - the quality of being adventurous or inclined to take risks
  • Antonym: caution - the trait of being careful and avoiding risks

Etymology

From 'venturesome' (risk-taking) + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting a state or quality).
