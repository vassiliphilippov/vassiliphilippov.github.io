endozoic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛndoʊˈzoʊɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛndəʊˈzəʊɪk / (BrE)
living within a living animal, often as a parasite
living inside an animal (esp. as parasite)

Examples

Certain endozoic bacteria can live harmlessly within the intestines of their animal hosts.
Scientists studied endozoic fungi found in marine sponges for their potential medical benefits.
The endozoic larvae eventually emerge from inside the fish, completing their parasitic cycle.

Synset Examples

entozoic worms

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ectozoic - living on the outside of an animal

Etymology

From Greek 'endon' (within) and 'zōion' (animal).
