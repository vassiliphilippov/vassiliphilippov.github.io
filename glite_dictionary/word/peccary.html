peccary

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛk.ə.ri / (AmE), / ˈpɛk.əri / (BrE)
A nocturnal, pig-like wild mammal found in North and South America, noted for its social behavior and bristly coat.
pig-like wild mammal of the Americas

Examples

A herd of peccary moved noisily through the underbrush, searching for food at dusk.
The peccary is smaller than most domestic pigs but just as social.
We spotted a group of peccaries while hiking in the South American forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: javelina - another name for certain peccary species, especially in the southwest United States
  • Degree Higher: pig - domesticated or wild animal related to the peccary, found mainly in Eurasia and Africa

Etymology

From Spanish 'pecarí', from Carib 'pakira'.
