Phlox bifida

1. NOUN

/ floʊks ˈbaɪfɪdə / (AmE), / flɒks ˈbɪfɪdə / (BrE)
A low, mat-forming herb native to rocky areas in the United States, known for its pale blue or lavender flowers.
a North American mat-forming herb

Examples

The rocky garden was covered in Phlox bifida, adding a splash of blue in spring.
We spotted several patches of Phlox bifida growing naturally along the hiking trail.
Gardeners value Phlox bifida because it thrives in dry, rocky soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phlox - a genus of flowering plants with colorful clusters of flowers

Etymology

From the genus name 'Phlox' (Greek phlox, meaning 'flame') and the species epithet 'bifida' (Latin for 'split in two').
