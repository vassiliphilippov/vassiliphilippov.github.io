Erie

1. PROPN

/ ˈɪɹi / (AmE), / ˈɪəri / (BrE)
A port city on Lake Erie in northwestern Pennsylvania, United States.
port city in Pennsylvania, USA

Examples

The city of Erie is known for its beautiful lakefront and maritime history.
Shipping and commerce have always played an important role in Erie's development.
Every summer, tourists flock to Erie to enjoy the beaches of Presque Isle State Park.

Etymology

Named after the Erie people, a Native American tribe that lived in the region.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪəri / (AmE), / ˈɪəri / (BrE)
a member of an Iroquoian people formerly inhabiting the south shore of Lake Erie in northern Ohio, northwest Pennsylvania, and western New York
a member of a historic Iroquoian people

Examples

An Erie chief negotiated a treaty with the French explorers.
The ancient Erie were known for their pottery and fortified villages.
Archaeologists discovered artifacts linked to the Erie in the region near Lake Erie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iroquois - a group of indigenous peoples of North America

Etymology

From the name of Lake Erie, itself from an Iroquoian word (Eriele or Erielhonan meaning 'long tail', possibly referring to the panther, their totem).

3. PROPN

/ ˈɪəri / (AmE), / ˈɪəri / (BrE)
the fourth largest of the Great Lakes of North America, located between the United States and Canada, and connected to the Hudson River by the New York State Barge Canal.
the fourth largest of the Great Lakes

Examples

Lake Erie is known for its shallow depth compared to the other Great Lakes.
The fishing industry thrives along the shores of Erie.
A storm over Erie caused shipping delays throughout the region.

Etymology

Named after the Erie people, an indigenous group who lived along its southern shore.
