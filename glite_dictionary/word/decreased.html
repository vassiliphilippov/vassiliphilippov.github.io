decreased

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈkrist / (AmE), / dɪˈkriːst / (BrE)
Having become less in size, amount, or degree.
made less in size, amount, or degree

Examples

The patient showed decreased appetite after starting the new medication.
After the policy changes, there was a decreased interest in the program.
The company's decreased profits worried investors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: increased - made greater in size or amount or degree
  • Synonym: reduced - made smaller or less in amount, degree, or size

Etymology

From the verb 'decrease', originating from Old French 'decreistre', from Latin 'decrescere' (to grow less).
