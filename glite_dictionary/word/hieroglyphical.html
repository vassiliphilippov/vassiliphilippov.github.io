hieroglyphical

1. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪrəˈɡlɪfɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌhaɪərəˈɡlɪfɪkəl / (BrE)
Resembling or characteristic of hieroglyphic writing; written in or like hieroglyphs.
resembling hieroglyphic writing

Examples

The ancient manuscript was covered in hieroglyphical symbols, making it nearly impossible to decipher.
Her dreams were filled with hieroglyphical patterns that seemed to convey a hidden message.
The note he left for me was so hieroglyphical I couldn't understand what he meant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hieroglyphic - written in, or relating to, hieroglyphs

Etymology

From Greek 'hieroglyphikos' via Latin, referring to sacred carvings.

2. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪərəˈɡlɪfɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪərəˈɡlɪfɪkəl / (BrE)
written in or relating to a writing system that uses pictorial symbols, especially hieroglyphs.
relating to pictorial symbol writing systems

Examples

The ancient tomb was covered in hieroglyphical inscriptions.
Researchers spent years deciphering the hieroglyphical writings on the temple walls.
The artifact featured a hieroglyphical script that revealed much about the lost civilization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hieroglyphic - written in or relating to hieroglyphs or hieroglyphic writing

Etymology

From Greek hieroglyphikos (sacred carving) via Late Latin hieroglyphicus, with the English adjectival suffix -al.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02752960-a
Synset POS: a
