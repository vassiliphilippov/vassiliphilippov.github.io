FDA

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛf.diːˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛf.diːˈeɪ / (BrE)
an abbreviation for the Food and Drug Administration, a federal agency of the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services responsible for regulating food, drug, and health-related products.
U.S. health and food safety regulatory agency
american

Examples

FDA approval is required before any new drug can be marketed in the United States.
Concerns about food safety prompted the FDA to increase its inspections of imported products.
The FDA issued a warning about the risks associated with unapproved dietary supplements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Food and Drug Administration - the full name of FDA, U.S. federal health regulatory agency

Etymology

Abbreviation of Food and Drug Administration.
