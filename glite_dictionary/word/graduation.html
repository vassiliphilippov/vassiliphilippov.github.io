graduation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡrædʒuˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡrædʒuˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
a mark or line on a measuring instrument, such as a ruler or container, indicating a specific measurement
mark on a measuring instrument

Examples

The laboratory flask has each graduation carefully labeled for precise measurement.
The scale’s smallest graduation measures one millimeter.
Check the graduations on the ruler to ensure you’re measuring accurately.

Synset Examples

the ruler had 16 graduations per inch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: division - a mark that divides a scale or instrument into parts

Etymology

From Latin 'graduatio', from 'gradus' (step, degree).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɡrædʒuˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡrædʒuˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of arranging or dividing into grades or steps
arranging into grades or steps

Examples

The graduation of the solution's concentration was carefully marked on the laboratory flask.
Engineers ensured the proper graduation of the scale for precise measurement.
The graduation of weights allows the machine to be calibrated accurately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gradation - a series of successive changes, stages, or degrees

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *graduatio* (a step or grade), from Latin *gradus* (step, degree).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɡrædʒuˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡrædʒuˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the successful completion of a course of study at a school, college, or university, often marked by a ceremony
completion of a course of study

Examples

She felt a great sense of achievement at her graduation from medical school.
The university will hold its graduation ceremony next Saturday.
After his graduation, he started working at a major accounting firm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commencement - a ceremony in which degrees or diplomas are conferred upon graduating students

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'graduatio', from Latin 'gradus' meaning 'step'.
