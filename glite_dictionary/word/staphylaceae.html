Staphylaceae

1. NOUN

/ stæfəˈlɪəsiː / (AmE), / stæfəˈlɪəsiː / (BrE)
A family of dicotyledonous plants in the order Sapindales, found mainly in the northern temperate zone.
plant family in order Sapindales

Examples

The Staphylaceae are predominantly distributed in the north temperate region.
Botanists have revised the classification of several genera within Staphylaceae.
Detailed studies of Staphylaceae reveal unique adaptations to temperate climates.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name Staphylus, with -aceae standard for plant family names.

2. NOUN

/ stæˌfɪliˈeɪsi.i / (AmE), / stafɪliˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of dicotyledonous plants in the order Sapindales, found mainly in the north temperate zone
family of dicotyledonous plants in Sapindales

Examples

The Staphylaceae are primarily distributed in the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
Researchers studied the floral characteristics of the Staphylaceae to understand their evolutionary relationships.
Many species in the Staphylaceae family are shrubs or small trees found in woodlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sapindales - order of flowering plants including Staphylaceae and others

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus *Staphylea* + family suffix *-aceae*.
