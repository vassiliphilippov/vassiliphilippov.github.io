ultraconservative

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌltrəkənˈsɝːvətɪv / (AmE), / ˌʌltrəkənˈsɜːvətɪv / (BrE)
extremely conservative in politics, opinions, or behavior; favoring or adhering to very traditional or established beliefs and values, especially to an extreme degree.
extremely conservative

Examples

Many voters were alarmed by the ultraconservative policies proposed by the new candidate.
The organization's ultraconservative views often put it at odds with more moderate groups.
She comes from an ultraconservative family that values strict tradition above all else.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: conservative - favoring traditional views and values; conventional

Etymology

Formed from ultra- (Latin, 'beyond') + conservative.

2. NOUN

/ ˌʌltrəkənˈsɜːrvətɪv / (AmE), / ˌʌltrəkənˈsɜːvətɪv / (BrE)
a person who holds extremely conservative views, especially one strongly opposed to progress or liberalism
person with extremely conservative views

Examples

The senator was labeled an ultraconservative by his opponents due to his rigid views.
Many ultraconservatives resisted the new policies, fearing they threatened traditional values.
As an ultraconservative, he opposed nearly every form of social and political reform.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: conservative - a person who is averse to change and holds traditional values
  • Antonym: progressive - a person favoring social reform or new ideas; opposite of conservative

Etymology

From ultra- (meaning 'extremely') + conservative.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10528068-n
Synset POS: n
