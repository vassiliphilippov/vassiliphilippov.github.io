hemidrachm

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛmɪˈdrækm / (AmE), / ˌhɛmɪˈdrækm / (BrE)
an ancient Greek silver coin valued at half a drachm, or three obols
ancient Greek silver coin, half drachm

Examples

The museum displayed an ancient hemidrachm among its impressive collection of Greek coins.
He discovered a silver hemidrachm while excavating the ancient city.
A hemidrachm was worth three obols in classical Athens.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: drachm - an ancient Greek silver coin and monetary unit
  • Degree Lower: obol - an ancient Greek coin worth one sixth of a drachm

Etymology

From Greek hemi- (half) + drachmē (drachma, a unit of currency).
