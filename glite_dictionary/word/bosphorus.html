Bosphorus

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɑːs.fər.əs / (AmE), / ˈbɒs.fər.əs / (BrE)
the narrow strait in northwestern Turkey that connects the Mediterranean Sea to the Black Sea, separating the European and Asian parts of Istanbul
strait between Europe and Asia in Turkey

Examples

Many ships pass through the Bosphorus every day, carrying goods between continents.
The city of Istanbul stretches across both sides of the Bosphorus.
Tourists often take boat rides on the Bosphorus to enjoy stunning views of the city.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Bosporos, meaning 'ox-ford'.
