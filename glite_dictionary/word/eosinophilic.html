eosinophilic

1. ADJ

/ ˌioʊsɪnəˈfɪlɪk / (AmE), / ˌiːəsɪnəˈfɪlɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characterized by eosinophils, a type of white blood cell, or to substances that are readily stained by eosin dye.
relating to eosinophils (white blood cells)
medical

Examples

The doctor noted an eosinophilic response in the blood sample.
Certain eosinophilic disorders can affect multiple organs in the body.
The tissue appeared eosinophilic when stained and examined under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eosinophil - a type of white blood cell involved in allergic reactions and immune response

Etymology

From 'eosinophil' + '-ic'; 'eosin' from Greek 'eos' (dawn) due to the dye's pink color.
