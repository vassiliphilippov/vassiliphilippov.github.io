hadji

1. NOUN

/ ˈhædʒi / (AmE), / ˈhædʒi / (BrE)
A title of respect used for a Muslim who has made the pilgrimage to Mecca (the Hajj).
Muslim pilgrim to Mecca (title)

Examples

After completing the pilgrimage, he was honored with the title hadji by his community.
The respected hadji shared his experiences from Mecca with the congregation.
Many families celebrate when a relative returns as a hadji after their journey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haji - Alternative spelling of hadji
  • Synonym: Hajj - The pilgrimage to Mecca required by Islam

Etymology

From Arabic ḥājj, meaning 'one who has completed the Hajj'.
