phenomenality

1. NOUN

/ fəˌnɑːməˈnæləti / (AmE), / fəˌnɒməˈnælɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being highly remarkable, exceptional, or extraordinary.
the state of being exceptional or extraordinary

Examples

The phenomenality of her achievements inspired everyone in the room.
Critics marveled at the phenomenality of the artist's most recent performance.
The story explores the phenomenality of human resilience in the face of adversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phenomenon - a remarkable or exceptional person or thing
  • Antonym: ordinariness - the quality of being ordinary or unremarkable

Etymology

Formed from 'phenomenal' + '-ity', after Latin roots.
