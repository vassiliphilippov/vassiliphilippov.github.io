autosexing

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔtoʊˈsɛksɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəʊˈsɛksɪŋ / (BrE)
(of domestic animals, especially poultry) breeding designed to produce offspring with visible sex differences at hatching, allowing easy sex identification.
breeding method for easy sex identification in offspring

Examples

Autosexing breeds of chickens allow farmers to determine the sex of chicks immediately after hatching.
The poultry farm prefers autosexing methods to save time during the sorting process.
Developing autosexing strains has improved efficiency in commercial breeding programs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sex-linked - relating to traits or characteristics determined by sex chromosomes

Etymology

Formed from 'auto-' (self) + 'sexing', indicating self-revealing of sex at birth/hatching.
