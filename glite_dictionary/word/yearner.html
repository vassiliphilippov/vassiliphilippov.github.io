yearner

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɪrnər / (AmE), / ˈjɪːnə / (BrE)
a person who strongly desires or longs for something
person who longs or desires

Examples

As a yearner for adventure, she was always dreaming of faraway places.
He was a yearner for recognition, striving to make his mark in the world.
Every yearner for freedom hopes for a better future.

Synset Examples

a longer for money
a thirster after blood
a yearner for knowledge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: longer - a person who longs for something

Etymology

From the verb 'yearn', which originates from Old English *giernan* (to desire).
