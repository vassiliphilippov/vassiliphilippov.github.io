Silesianness

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈliːʒən.nəs / (AmE), / saɪˈliːzi.ən.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being Silesian in character, identity, or origin.
quality of being Silesian

Examples

The exhibition celebrated the Silesianness of the region's art and culture.
His pride in his Silesianness was evident in every conversation.
The novel explores themes of Silesianness and identity after the war.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Englishness - the quality of being English
  • Synonym: Polishness - the quality of being Polish

Etymology

From Silesian (of Silesia, a historical region in Central Europe) + -ness (forming nouns denoting a state or condition).
