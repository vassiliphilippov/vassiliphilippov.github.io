chlamydial

1. ADJ

/ kləˈmɪdiəl / (AmE), / kləˈmɪdiəl / (BrE)
relating to chlamydia, especially the sexually transmitted infection or the bacteria that cause it
relating to chlamydia or its infection
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed antibiotics to treat the chlamydial infection.
Researchers are studying chlamydial strains that are resistant to common treatments.
A chlamydial test is often recommended during routine sexual health screenings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chlamydia - a genus of bacteria, or the infection it causes

Etymology

From 'chlamydia' with the adjectival suffix '-al'. 'Chlamydia' is from Greek *khlamýs* (cloak), referencing the organism's membrane structure.
