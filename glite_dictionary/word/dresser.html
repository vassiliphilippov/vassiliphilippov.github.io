dresser

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɛs.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈdrɛs.ə / (BrE)
a piece of furniture with drawers, used for storing clothes
a chest of drawers for clothes

Examples

She keeps all her t-shirts folded neatly in the top drawer of her dresser.
The antique dresser in the bedroom was handed down from her grandmother.
He bought a new dresser for his apartment to organize his clothes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chest of drawers - a piece of furniture with drawers stacked one above another
  • Antonym: wardrobe - a tall piece of furniture for hanging clothes

She worked as a dresser in the theater for over a decade.

4. NOUN

/ ˈdrɛsər / (AmE), / ˈdrɛsə / (BrE)
a piece of furniture with shelves, often with drawers or cupboards below, used for storing and displaying items such as dishes or kitchenware, especially in a kitchen or dining room
cabinet with shelves for storage/display
british

Examples

She displayed her collection of china plates on the oak dresser in the dining room.
The antique dresser had beautifully carved shelves and drawers for storing utensils.
In many traditional homes, a dresser serves as both storage and a place to show off family heirlooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sideboard - a piece of dining room furniture for storing dishes and serving food
  • Synonym: cabinet - a cupboard with shelves or drawers for storage or display

Etymology

From Middle English 'dressen' (to prepare), related to the function of preparing and storing items.

5. NOUN

/ ˈdrɛsər / (AmE), / ˈdrɛsə / (BrE)
a piece of furniture with drawers and a mirror, used for storing clothes and as a place to dress or apply makeup
low table with drawers and mirror for dressing

Examples

She kept her jewelry neatly arranged on the dresser in her bedroom.
Every morning, he sat at the dresser to fix his hair before work.
The old oak dresser had a large mirror and several drawers for cosmetics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vanity - a piece of furniture with a mirror, used for dressing or applying makeup

Etymology

From Old French dresseur ('one who arranges'), from dresser ('to arrange').
