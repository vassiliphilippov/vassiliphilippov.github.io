searcher

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːtʃər / (AmE), / ˈsɜːtʃə / (BrE)
a large metallic blue-green beetle of North America that preys on caterpillars
a predatory North American beetle

Examples

The searcher is known for its striking blue-green coloration and voracious appetite for caterpillars.
Gardeners welcome the searcher as it helps control pest populations in their crops.
During the summer, I spotted a searcher hunting for prey on a tree trunk.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːtʃər / (AmE), / ˈsɜːtʃə / (BrE)
a customs official whose job is to search baggage, goods, or vehicles for contraband or dutiable items
customs officer who inspects for contraband

Examples

The searcher meticulously inspected each suitcase for prohibited goods.
At the border, a searcher discovered undeclared jewelry hidden in a car.
In the 18th century, a searcher played a vital role at customs houses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inspector - an official who examines objects, vehicles, or premises for compliance or safety

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːtʃər / (AmE), / ˈsɜːtʃə / (BrE)
a person who searches for something or conducts inquiries
person who searches or inquires

Examples

The searcher combed the beach for lost jewelry.
She hired a professional searcher to help locate her birth parents.
As a tireless searcher for knowledge, he spent hours reading in the library.

Synset Examples

they are seekers after truth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seeker - a person who seeks or looks for something
  • Synonym: investigator - a person who investigates or asks questions

Etymology

From Middle English sercher, from Old French cercheor, from cerchier (to search).
