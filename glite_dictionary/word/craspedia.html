Craspedia

1. NOUN

/ kræsˈpiːdiə / (AmE), / kræsˈpiːdiə / (BrE)
A genus of herbaceous plants native to Australia and New Zealand, known for their bright, spherical yellow flower heads.
genus of Australian and New Zealand herbs

Examples

Craspedia is often used in floral arrangements for its striking yellow, spherical blooms.
Many gardens in Australia feature Craspedia for its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
The florist recommended adding Craspedia to the bouquet for a touch of bright color.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek kraspedon (edge, border).
