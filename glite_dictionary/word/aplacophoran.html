aplacophoran

1. NOUN

/ ˌæpləˈkɑfərən / (AmE), / ˌæpləˈkɒfərən / (BrE)
A deep-sea, worm-like mollusk that lacks calcareous plates on its body but has a covering mantle with fine, slimy spicules.
worm-like deep-sea mollusk

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species of aplacophoran during the deep-sea expedition.
Unlike most mollusks, the aplacophoran does not have a hard external shell.
The body of an aplacophoran is covered with tiny, spiky spicules instead of calcareous plates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mollusk - a soft-bodied invertebrate of the phylum Mollusca

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) + 'plakos' (plate) + '-phoros' (bearing), meaning 'without plate-bearing'.
