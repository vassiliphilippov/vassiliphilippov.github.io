Urdu

1. PROPN

/ ˈɚrdu / (AmE), / ˈɜːduː / (BrE)
the official literary language of Pakistan, closely related to Hindi and written in Arabic script; widely used in India, especially by Muslims
the official language of Pakistan; related to Hindi

Examples

She speaks Urdu fluently thanks to her upbringing in Karachi.
Many Bollywood songs are written in both Hindi and Urdu.
The Urdu script is derived from the Persian and Arabic alphabets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hindi - the official language of India, closely related to Urdu

Etymology

From Turkish ordu (army), reflecting the language's origins as a lingua franca among military camps.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɝːdu / (AmE), / ˈɜːduː / (BrE)
the official literary language of Pakistan, closely related to Hindi and widely used in India, especially by Muslims; written in the Arabic script
South Asian literary language, official in Pakistan

Examples

She grew up speaking both Hindi and Urdu at home.
Many famous poets have written extensively in Urdu.
The national anthem of Pakistan is composed in Urdu.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hindi - the most widely spoken language of northern India

Etymology

From Hindi-Urdu urdū (short for zabān-e-urdū, 'language of the camp'), from Turkish ordu ('camp, army').
