to proliferate

1. VERB

/ prəˈlɪfəˌreɪt / (AmE), / prəˈlɪfəreɪt / (BrE)
to cause (something) to grow or increase rapidly in number or amount
cause to grow rapidly

Examples

International organizations aim not to proliferate weapons of mass destruction around the world.
The risks increase if you proliferate unnecessary rules within a company.
Some governments actively proliferate advanced technologies to support industrial growth.

Synset Examples

We must not proliferate nuclear arms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multiply - increase greatly in number or quantity
  • Antonym: to diminish - to become or make smaller or less

Etymology

From Latin 'proles' (offspring) and '-fer' (bearing, carrying).

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ prəˈlɪfəˌreɪt / (AmE), / prəˈlɪf.ər.eɪt / (BrE)
to increase rapidly in number or amount; to grow or spread quickly
increase or spread rapidly

Examples

Rumors tend to proliferate rapidly in a close-knit community.
Fast-food restaurants continue to proliferate along the highway.
Wildflowers proliferate in the meadows each spring.

Synset Examples

Pizza parlors proliferate in this area

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multiply - increase greatly in number; to become numerous
  • Antonym: decrease - to become less in number or amount; diminish

Etymology

From Latin 'proles' (offspring) and 'ferre' (to bear), via French 'proliférer'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00311417-v
Synset POS: v
