Stephanotis floribunda

1. NOUN

/ ˌstɛfəˈnoʊtɪs flɔːˈrɪbəndə / (AmE), / ˌstɛfəˈnəʊtɪs flɒˈrɪbʊndə / (BrE)
A twining woody vine native to Madagascar, prized for its thick evergreen leaves and clusters of large, fragrant, waxy white flowers; commonly cultivated in warm climates.
fragrant Madagascan flowering vine

Examples

The bride carried a bouquet of Stephanotis floribunda for its delicate fragrance and elegant appearance.
Gardeners often choose Stephanotis floribunda for their conservatories due to its showy, sweet-smelling flowers.
In warmer regions, Stephanotis floribunda can be grown outdoors as a decorative climbing plant.

Etymology

From Greek 'stephanos' (crown) and 'otís' (ear), referring to the flower shape; 'floribunda' is Latin for 'abundantly flowering'.
