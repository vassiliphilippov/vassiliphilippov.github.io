oxalis

1. NOUN

/ ɑkˈsælɪs / (AmE), / ɒkˈsælɪs / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Oxalis, typically with clover-like leaves and usually yellow or pink flowers.
a plant of the genus Oxalis

Examples

We found a patch of oxalis growing wild at the edge of the garden.
The leaves of oxalis resemble those of clover, but the flowers are more vibrant.
Some species of oxalis are cultivated as ornamental plants for their attractive foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorrel - a common name for certain species of Oxalis and similar sour-tasting plants

Etymology

From Latin 'oxalis', meaning 'sorrel', from Greek 'oxalis' (sour plant), from 'oxys' (sharp, sour).
