keratectasia

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɛrəˌtɛkˈteɪziə / (AmE), / ˌkɛrəˌtɛkˈteɪzɪə / (BrE)
A condition in which the cornea of the eye abnormally bulges outward, often leading to vision problems.
abnormal bulging of the cornea
medical

Examples

After LASIK surgery, he developed keratectasia, requiring further treatment.
Early detection of keratectasia is crucial for preventing severe vision loss.
Research is ongoing to improve therapies for corneal disorders like keratectasia.

Etymology

From Greek 'keras' (horn, cornea) + 'ektasis' (extension, dilation).
