Gazania rigens

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈzeɪniə ˈraɪɡənz / (AmE), / ɡəˈzeɪniə ˈrɪdʒɛnz / (BrE)
A South African perennial plant with low, spreading stems, densely leafy, and orange flowers with black eyespots at the base of the petals.
ornamental South African plant with orange flowers

Examples

Many gardeners plant Gazania rigens for its vibrant orange blooms that last throughout the summer.
The low-growing Gazania rigens is ideal for edging flower beds or as ground cover.
Unlike some perennials, Gazania rigens thrives in poor soil and full sun conditions.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Gazania', named after Theodore Gaza, a Greek-Italian scholar, and species name 'rigens' meaning 'stiff' or 'rigid' in Latin.
