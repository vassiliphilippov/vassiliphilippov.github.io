menstruum

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛnstruəm / (AmE), / ˈmɛnstruːəm / (BrE)
(archaic) A substance, typically a liquid, that dissolves another substance; a solvent.
archaic term for a solvent
archaic

Examples

In alchemical texts, mercury often served as the menstruum for extracting the essence of gold.
The herbalist described alcohol as a powerful menstruum for tincture preparation.
Early chemists experimented with wine as a menstruum to dissolve medicinal compounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solvent - a substance that dissolves another to form a solution

Etymology

From Latin 'menstruum', originally meaning 'monthly discharge', later used in alchemy for a solvent thought to possess transformative powers.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛnstruːəm / (AmE), / ˈmɛnstruːəm / (BrE)
the monthly discharge of blood from the uterus of nonpregnant women, occurring from puberty to menopause; menstruation
menstrual discharge; menstruation
archaic medical

Examples

In ancient medical texts, the term menstruum frequently appears instead of the modern word menstruation.
The regular flow of the menstruum was once considered essential for a woman's health.
References to a woman's menstruum can often be found in historical literature and documents.

Synset Examples

the women were sickly and subject to excessive menstruation
a woman does not take the gout unless her menses be stopped
the semen begins to appear in males and to be emitted at the same time of life that the catamenia begin to flow in females

Related Terms

  • Synonym: menstruation - the monthly process of discharging blood from the uterus
  • Synonym: menses - the monthly flow of blood and cellular debris from the uterus

Etymology

From Latin 'menstruum' (month), from 'mensis' (month), originally referencing monthly period.
