Sequoiadendron

1. NOUN

/ səˌkwɔɪəˈdɛndrən / (AmE), / sɪˌkwɔɪəˈdɛndrən / (BrE)
A genus of giant sequoia trees native to the western United States, known for their enormous size and longevity.
genus of giant sequoia trees

Examples

The Sequoiadendron in California's Sierra Nevada are among the world's largest living things.
Botanists debated whether Sequoiadendron should be classified in its own genus.
The distinctive cones of Sequoiadendron help differentiate it from other redwoods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sequoia - a related genus of redwood trees

Etymology

From Latin 'Sequoia' (a genus name) + Greek 'déndron' (tree).

2. NOUN

/ sɪˌkwɔɪ.əˈdɛn.drən / (AmE), / sɪˌkwɔɪ.əˈdɛn.drən / (BrE)
A genus of giant sequoia trees native to California, sometimes included in the genus Sequoia.
genus of giant sequoia trees

Examples

The giant trees in the Sierra Nevada belong to the genus Sequoiadendron.
Botanists debated whether Sequoiadendron should be classified separately from Sequoia.
The genus Sequoiadendron contains only one living species: the giant sequoia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sequoia - genus of redwood trees closely related to Sequoiadendron

Etymology

From Latin 'Sequoia' (after the Cherokee leader Sequoyah) and Greek 'dendron' (tree).
