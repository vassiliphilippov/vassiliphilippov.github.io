Iris filifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪrɪs ˌfɪlɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈaɪrɪs ˌfɪlɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A species of bulbous iris native to Spain, known for its red-violet flowers.
Spanish iris species with red-violet flowers

Examples

The gardener planted several Iris filifolia bulbs to add color to the spring borders.
A rare variety of Iris filifolia bloomed earlier than expected this year.
Botanists studied the natural habitat of Iris filifolia during their trip to Spain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iris - a genus of flowering plants with showy flowers

Etymology

Genus name from Greek iris (rainbow); species from Latin filum (thread) and folium (leaf), referring to its thread-like leaves.
