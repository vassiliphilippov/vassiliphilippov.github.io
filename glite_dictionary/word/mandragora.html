Mandragora

1. NOUN

/ ˌmændrəˈɡɔrə / (AmE), / ˌmændrəˈɡɔːrə / (BrE)
A genus of stemless herbs in the nightshade family (Solanaceae), including the mandrake, noted for their thick roots and use in traditional medicine and folklore.
genus of nightshade herbs, including mandrake

Examples

The roots of Mandragora have long been associated with mystical properties in folklore.
Several species within the genus Mandragora are native to regions around the Mediterranean.
Botanists studied the chemical compounds found in Mandragora to explore their potential medical uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mandrake - A plant of the genus Mandragora, noted for its medicinal and mythical properties

Etymology

From Latin *mandragora*, from Ancient Greek *mandragóras* (mandrake).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmæn.drəˈɡɔːrə / (AmE), / ˌmæn.drəˈɡɔː.rə / (BrE)
a genus of stemless herbs in the nightshade family, Solanaceae, known for the mandrake plants
genus of stemless herbs (mandrake)

Examples

The roots of Mandragora have been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
Botanists classify the mandrake plant within the genus Mandragora.
Several species in the Mandragora genus are native to the Mediterranean region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mandrake - a plant of the genus Mandragora, known for its forked root

Etymology

From Latin Mandragora, from Greek mandragoras, referring to the mandrake plant.
