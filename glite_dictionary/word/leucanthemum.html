Leucanthemum

1. NOUN

/ luˈkænθəməm / (AmE), / ljuːˈkænθɪməm / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, native mainly to southern and central Europe, often known as ox-eye daisies.
genus of European daisies

Examples

The field was full of Leucanthemum blooming in the summer sun.
Gardeners often plant Leucanthemum for their beautiful white petals and yellow centers.
Several species within the Leucanthemum genus are popular as ornamental flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chrysanthemum - a genus of flowering plants, often confused or formerly grouped with Leucanthemum

Etymology

From Greek leukos (white) + anthemon (flower).

2. NOUN

/ luːˈkænθɪməm / (AmE), / ljuːˈkænθɪməm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae, native mainly to southern and central Europe, often formerly included in the genus Chrysanthemum.
genus of European flowering plants

Examples

The common daisy belongs to the genus Leucanthemum, which includes several popular garden species.
Many Leucanthemum species are characterized by their white petals and yellow centers.
She specializes in the cultivation of plants from the genus Leucanthemum for ornamental gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chrysanthemum - a related genus of flowering plants in the Asteraceae family

Etymology

From Latin *leucanthemum*, from Greek *leukos* (white) + *anthemon* (flower), referring to the typically white flowers.
