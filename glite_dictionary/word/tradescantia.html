Tradescantia

1. NOUN

/ trædˌskænˈtiːə / (AmE), / trædˌskænˈtɪə / (BrE)
a genus of herbaceous plants commonly known as spiderworts, native to the Americas and grown as ornamentals for their attractive flowers and foliage
genus of plants known as spiderworts

Examples

Many gardens feature Tradescantia because of its colorful, easy-to-grow flowers.
The leaves of the Tradescantia are often used in houseplant arrangements.
She identified the plant by its characteristic purple stems as a species of Tradescantia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiderwort - common name for plants in the genus Tradescantia

Etymology

Named after John Tradescant (c.1570–1638), English gardener and botanist.
