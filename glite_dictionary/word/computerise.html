to computerise

1. VERB

/ kəmˈpjuːtəˌraɪz / (AmE), / kəmˈpjuːtəraɪz / (BrE)
to equip or convert (something) with or to the use of computers.
to equip or convert with computers
british

Examples

The company decided to computerise its entire accounting system to improve efficiency.
Many libraries were slow to computerise their catalogues in the early 2000s.
After we computerised the processes, paperwork was reduced significantly.

Synset Examples

Our office is fully computerized now

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to computerize - to equip or convert with computers; American spelling

Etymology

From 'computer' + '-ise', patterned after similar verbs formed in the 20th century.

to convert (information, systems, or processes) so that they are operated or stored by a computer
convert to computer operation or storage
british

Examples

The library decided to computerise its entire catalogue to improve accessibility.
Many businesses are seeking to computerise their accounting systems for greater efficiency.
After they computerised patient records, accessing information became much faster.

Synset Examples

3. VERB

/ kəmˈpjuːtəˌraɪz / (AmE), / kəmˈpjuːtəraɪz / (BrE)
to control, automate, or operate a function, process, or system using computers; to convert something to operate by computer
operate or automate with computers
british

Examples

The company plans to computerise its accounting system within the next year.
Many factories have computerised their production lines to improve efficiency.
Hospitals are quickly computerising patient records for better data management.

Synset Examples
