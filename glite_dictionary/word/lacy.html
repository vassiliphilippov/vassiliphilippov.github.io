lacy

1. ADJ

/ ˈleɪsi / (AmE), / ˈleɪsi / (BrE)
Made of or resembling lace, especially in having a delicate and intricate openwork pattern.
made of or resembling lace

Examples

She wore a beautiful lacy dress to the party.
The sunlight filtered through the lacy curtains in the living room.
The edges of the leaves had a delicate, lacy appearance.

Synset Examples

a lacy gown
a lacy leaf

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lace - a delicate fabric made of twisted threads

Etymology

From 'lace' with the adjective-forming suffix '-y'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈleɪsi / (AmE), / ˈleɪsi / (BrE)
having a delicate, open structure like that of lace; resembling or made of lace
having an open, lace-like pattern

Examples

She wore a lacy dress to the party, which shimmered under the lights.
The frost made lacy patterns on the windowpane overnight.
Tiny white flowers formed a lacy border around the garden path.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lace - a fine open fabric, typically of cotton or silk, made by looping, twisting, or knitting threads in patterns

Etymology

From 'lace' + -y, indicating 'like lace'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02014163-s
Synset POS: s
