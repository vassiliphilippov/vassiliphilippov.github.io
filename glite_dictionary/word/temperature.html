temperature

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpərəˌtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɛmp(ə)rətʃə / (BrE)
The degree of heat present in a substance or an environment, usually measured on a standard scale.
degree of heat or coldness

Examples

The temperature dropped drastically after the sun went down.
Please check the temperature before you set the oven.
Her body temperature was slightly above normal, indicating a mild fever.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heat - the quality of being hot; high temperature
  • Antonym: cold - the quality of being cold; low temperature

Etymology

From Latin 'temperatura' (a mixing, tempering), from 'temperare' (to mix in proportion).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmpɚˌtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərətʃə / (BrE)
the degree of hotness or coldness as felt by the body or measured by a thermometer
degree of heat or cold

Examples

When you have a fever, your temperature is higher than normal.
The temperature outside dropped below freezing last night.
She could feel the room's temperature rising as the sun came out.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: heat - the quality of being hot; high temperature
  • Degree Lower: cold - the quality of being cold; low temperature

Etymology

From Latin 'temperatura' (correct mixture), from 'temperare' (to mix in proper proportions).
