Salvia lyrata

1. NOUN

/ ˈsælviə laɪˈreɪtə / (AmE), / ˈsalvɪə lɪˈreɪtə / (BrE)
A plant species of the sage family native to the eastern United States, known for its lyre-shaped leaves and pale blue flowers.
a sage plant native to eastern U.S.

Examples

The Salvia lyrata thrives in moist woodlands across the southeastern United States.
Gardeners often plant Salvia lyrata for its attractive blue flowers and unusual foliage.
Researchers are studying the potential medicinal uses of Salvia lyrata in traditional remedies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lyre-leaf sage - another common name for Salvia lyrata
  • Degree Higher: sage - aromatic plant of the genus Salvia

Etymology

From Latin: 'Salvia' (the genus name for sage plants) and 'lyrata' (lyre-shaped), referring to the shape of the leaves.
