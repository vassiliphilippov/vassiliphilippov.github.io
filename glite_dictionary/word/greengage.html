greengage

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡriːnˌɡeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnˌɡeɪdʒ / (BrE)
a sweet green or greenish-yellow variety of plum, known for its delicate flavor
a type of sweet green plum

Examples

The orchard was full of ripe greengages ready for picking in late summer.
She made a delicious jam from fresh greengages and sugar.
A slice of tart greengage pie was served with cream for dessert.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: plum - a soft, round, sweet fruit with a smooth red or purple skin and a single stone inside

Etymology

Named after Sir William Gage, who introduced this plum to England in the 18th century.
