kafkaesque

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːfkiˈɛsk / (AmE), / ˌkæfkiˈɛsk / (BrE)
Marked by a nightmarish, surreal distortion of reality, often involving oppressive bureaucracy or a sense of helplessness.
surreal, nightmarish, oppressive (like Kafka's works)

Examples

The new company policy created a kafkaesque maze of paperwork that no one could navigate.
He found himself trapped in a kafkaesque nightmare of endless meetings and pointless procedures.
The film’s kafkaesque atmosphere left the audience feeling unsettled and confused.

Synset Examples

the kafkaesque terror of the endless interrogations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surreal - having the qualities of surrealism; bizarre

Etymology

From the name of Czech writer Franz Kafka (1883–1924), whose works often feature surreal and oppressive situations, plus the English suffix -esque.
