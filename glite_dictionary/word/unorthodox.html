unorthodox

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɔr.θəˌdɑks / (AmE), / ʌnˈɔː.θə.dɒks / (BrE)
not conforming to established customs, beliefs, or practices; independent in behavior or thought
not conforming to accepted customs or beliefs

Examples

His unorthodox approach to problem-solving impressed his colleagues.
The artist became famous for her unorthodox style and use of color.
Some critics disapproved of his unorthodox interpretations of classical music.

Synset Examples

she led a somewhat irregular private life
maverick politicians

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonconformist - a person or attitude not conforming to prevailing ideas or practices
  • Antonym: orthodox - conforming to established or traditional beliefs or practices

Etymology

From un- (not) + orthodox, from Greek orthodoxos (having the right opinion).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɔrˌθɑks / (AmE), / ʌnˈɔːθədɒks / (BrE)
Not conforming to established or accepted rules, traditions, or beliefs; unconventional or unusual.
not traditional or conventional

Examples

Her unorthodox approach to teaching inspired her students to think creatively.
He followed an unorthodox diet that surprised his doctors.
The artist gained fame for her unorthodox use of materials in her sculptures.

Synset Examples

an unorthodox lifestyle

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orthodox - following traditional or established beliefs or practices
  • Synonym: unconventional - not based on or conforming to what is generally done or believed

Etymology

From un- (not) + orthodox, from Greek orthodoxos (having the right opinion).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01695526-a
Synset POS: a
