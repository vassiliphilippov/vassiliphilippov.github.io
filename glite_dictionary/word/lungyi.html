lungyi

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʊn.dʒi / (AmE), / ˈlʌŋ.gi / (BrE)
a long piece of brightly colored cloth, typically cotton or silk, worn as a skirt, loincloth, or sash in India, Pakistan, and Burma (Myanmar)
traditional cloth garment from South and Southeast Asia

Examples

He wrapped a lungyi around his waist before heading out into the evening.
In Myanmar, both men and women commonly wear the lungyi for comfort and tradition.
The market stall displayed lungyis of every color and pattern imaginable.

Etymology

From Burmese လုံချည် (*lon hkyi*), meaning 'something tied around.'
