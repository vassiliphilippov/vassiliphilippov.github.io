Athyrium pycnocarpon

1. NOUN

/ əˈθɪriəm pɪknoʊˈkɑrpən / (AmE), / əˈθɪəriəm pɪknəˈkɑːpən / (BrE)
A North American fern with narrow fronds on yellowish leafstalks, commonly found in moist woodlands.
a North American woodland fern species

Examples

The shady ravine was carpeted with Athyrium pycnocarpon in early summer.
Botanists are studying the habitat preferences of Athyrium pycnocarpon in northeastern forests.
I recognized Athyrium pycnocarpon by its delicate, narrow fronds and yellowish stems.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Athyrium' from Greek 'athurion' (little door), species epithet from Greek 'pyknos' (dense) and 'karpos' (fruit).
