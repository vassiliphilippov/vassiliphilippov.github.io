wodge

1. NOUN

/ wɑdʒ / (AmE), / wɒdʒ / (BrE)
a large, thick piece or amount of something, especially a mass of paper or money
large, thick mass or lump
informal british

Examples

He stuffed a wodge of cash into his pocket before leaving the house.
I handed the receptionist a wodge of paperwork to process.
She found a wodge of old receipts tucked into a drawer.

Synset Examples

I found a wodge of old letters in the attic.
He handed me a wodge of cash for the concert tickets.
She grabbed a wodge of paper to take notes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wad - a thick pile or bundle, especially of paper or money

Etymology

British informal; origin uncertain, possibly a variant of "wedge".
