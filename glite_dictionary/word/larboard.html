larboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːr.bɔrd / (AmE), / ˈlɑː.bəd / (BrE)
the left side of a ship or aircraft to someone facing the bow or nose; now chiefly replaced by 'port'
the left side of a ship (now archaic)
archaic

Examples

The captain issued orders from the larboard side of the deck.
Heavy waves battered the larboard during the storm.
The sailors hauled in the ropes on the larboard as they prepared to dock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: port - the left side of a ship or aircraft when facing forward
  • Antonym: starboard - the right side of a ship or aircraft when facing forward

Etymology

From Middle English 'ladebord', probably referring to the side of the ship used for loading cargo; altered by association with 'starboard'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɑːrbɔːrd / (AmE), / ˈlɑːbəd / (BrE)
(archaic) Relating to or located on the left side of a ship or aircraft when facing forward; port side.
left side of a ship (archaic)
archaic

Examples

The captain ordered the crew to turn the ship to larboard to avoid the reef.
Old nautical charts label the larboard side for clarity in navigation instructions.
During the storm, waves crashed against the larboard windows of the galley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: port - the left side of a ship or aircraft when facing forward
  • Antonym: starboard - the right side of a ship or aircraft when facing forward

Etymology

From Middle English laderbord, likely from 'lade' (to load) and 'bord' (board), with 'port' replacing it to avoid confusion with 'starboard'.
