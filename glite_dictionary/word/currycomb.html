currycomb

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɜriˌkoʊm / (AmE), / ˈkʌriˌkəʊm / (BrE)
A square or rectangular comb with rows of small teeth used for grooming horses or other animals.
comb for grooming horses or animals

Examples

She used a currycomb to remove dirt from the horse's coat.
Every stable keeps several currycombs for daily grooming sessions.
After riding, he cleaned his horse with a stiff currycomb before returning it to the stall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brush - a tool with bristles for cleaning or grooming

Etymology

From 'curry' (to groom a horse) + 'comb'.

2. VERB

/ ˈkɜːriˌkoʊm / (AmE), / ˈkʌrɪˌkəʊm / (BrE)
to clean a horse using a currycomb, a brush with short teeth used for grooming.
to groom a horse with a currycomb

Examples

After a muddy ride, she patiently currycombed the horse until its coat gleamed.
He currycombs his horse every morning to keep the animal's skin healthy.
Stable hands were busy currycombing the horses in preparation for the show.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brush - to clean or groom using a brush

Etymology

From 'currycomb', the noun, referring to the grooming tool, itself from Middle English 'currey' (to rub clean) and 'comb'.

Synset POS: v
