tow

1. NOUN

/ toʊ / (AmE), / təʊ / (BrE)
an act of pulling a vehicle or object behind another, typically with a rope, chain, or tow bar
act of pulling something by a rope or hitch

Examples

After her car broke down, she needed a tow to the nearest repair shop.
The broken-down truck received a tow from a helpful passerby.
If you park here, your vehicle may get a tow by the city.

Synset Examples

the truck gave him a tow to the garage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haul - the act of pulling or dragging something with effort or force
Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ toʊ / (AmE), / təʊ / (BrE)
to pull or drag something, typically a vehicle, behind another vehicle or animal using a rope, chain, or similar device.
pull or drag behind (often a vehicle)

Examples

The mechanic had to tow the broken car to the nearest garage.
A small boat was towing a barge along the river early this morning.
If you park here, your vehicle may be towed away by the city authorities.

Synset Examples

Horses used to tow barges along the canal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drag - to pull something along the ground with effort or difficulty
  • Antonym: push - to move something away from oneself by exerting force

Etymology

From Old English 'tōwan' (to pull, drag).
