Piper

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪpər / (AmE), / ˈpaɪpə / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly climbing tropical shrubs, including pepper plants, that belong to the family Piperaceae.
genus of tropical pepper plants

Examples

The black pepper commonly used in cooking is derived from the Piper genus.
Many species within the Piper genus are valued for their aromatic qualities.
Botanists have discovered a new variety in the Piper genus growing in the Amazon.

Etymology

From Latin 'piper' meaning pepper.
