Kattegatt

1. PROPN

/ ˈkætɪˌɡæt / (AmE), / ˈkatɪˌɡat / (BrE)
a strait of the North Sea between the Jutland Peninsula and Sweden, connecting to the North Sea via the Skagerrak.
strait between Jutland and Sweden

Examples

Many ships pass through the Kattegatt on their way to the Baltic Sea.
The coastlines along the Kattegatt are popular for sailing and fishing.
Strong currents can make navigation in the Kattegatt challenging during winter.

Etymology

From Dutch 'Kattegat', literally 'cat's hole' (narrow passage), referring to its navigational difficulty.
