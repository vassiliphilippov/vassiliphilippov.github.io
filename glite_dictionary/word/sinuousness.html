sinuousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪnjuəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪnjʊəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being winding or curving, especially in reference to roads, rivers, or paths
the state of being winding or curvy

Examples

The sinuousness of the countryside road made the drive both beautiful and challenging.
She admired the sinuousness of the river as it wound through the valley.
Too much sinuousness in the trail can slow down the hiking group.

Synset Examples

he hated the sinuosity of mountain roads

Related Terms

  • Antonym: straightness - the quality of being straight, without bends

Etymology

From Latin 'sinuosus' (full of bends) + '-ness'.
