imperfectness

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɝːfɪktnəs / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜːfɪktnəs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being imperfect; the condition of having faults or defects.
state of being imperfect

Examples

She embraced the imperfectness of her painting, seeing beauty in its flaws.
Despite its imperfectness, the antique vase was highly valued for its age.
The coach focused on improving the team's imperfectness rather than expecting perfection from the start.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imperfection - the state or quality of being imperfect
  • Antonym: perfection - the state or quality of being perfect

Etymology

Formed from 'imperfect' + '-ness', modeled after Latin and Old French patterns.
