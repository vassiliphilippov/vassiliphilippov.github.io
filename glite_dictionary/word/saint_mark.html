Saint Mark

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt mɑrk / (AmE), / seɪnt mɑːk / (BrE)
an apostle and companion of Saint Peter, traditionally regarded as the author of the second Gospel in the New Testament
apostle; author of the second Gospel

Examples

Many churches around the world are named after Saint Mark in honor of his contributions to Christianity.
The Gospel according to Saint Mark is the second book of the New Testament.
As a companion of Peter, Saint Mark played an important role in spreading the teachings of Jesus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saint Peter - apostle and close companion of Jesus
  • Synonym: Gospel of Mark - the second book of the New Testament, attributed to Saint Mark
