sandarac

1. NOUN

/ ˈsændəræk / (AmE), / ˈsandərak / (BrE)
a durable, fragrant wood obtained from coniferous trees, especially used in building and woodworking
a fragrant, durable wood used in building

Examples

The roof was constructed using precious sandarac to enhance both strength and aroma.
Artisans prized sandarac for its durability in fine woodwork.
In historic buildings, sandarac beams can still be found supporting ancient structures.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin sandaraca, from Arabic ṣandara, referring to certain aromatic resins and woods.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsændəræk / (AmE), / ˈsændərək / (BrE)
a large evergreen coniferous tree (Tetraclinis articulata) native to North Africa and Spain, known for its fragrant wood and resin used in varnishes.
a coniferous tree of North Africa and Spain

Examples

The fragrant wood of the sandarac is highly valued in woodworking.
Varnishes made from sandarac resin are prized for their clear, hard finish.
The sandarac tree grows abundantly in certain regions of Morocco and Algeria.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin sandaraca, from Arabic ṣandaraq, referring to both the tree and the resin.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsændəræk / (AmE), / ˈsændəræk / (BrE)
a brittle, faintly aromatic, translucent resin obtained from certain North African trees, used in making varnishes and incense
brittle resin used in varnish and incense

Examples

High-quality violins are sometimes coated with a varnish made from sandarac.
The artisan added sandarac to the paint mixture to improve its gloss.
Historically, sandarac was valued for its use in making incense and sealing wax.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resin - a sticky substance exuded by some trees and plants

Etymology

From Medieval Latin sandaraca, from Arabic ṣandaraq, originally from Greek sandarake (red lead, resin).
