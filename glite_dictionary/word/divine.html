divine

1. NOUN

/ dəˈvaɪn / (AmE), / dɪˈvaɪn / (BrE)
a clergyman, theologian, or person devoted to religious studies, especially within Christianity
clergyman or theologian
archaic

Examples

The young scholar aspired to become a respected divine at the university.
Many influential divines contributed to the theological debates of the seventeenth century.
As a prominent divine, he was often consulted on matters of church doctrine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theologian - a person who studies theology
  • Synonym: priest - an ordained minister of religion

Etymology

From Latin divinus (of a god, divine), later used for religious scholars in English.
