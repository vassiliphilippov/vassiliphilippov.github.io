Uvularia

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːvjəˈlɛriə / (AmE), / ˌjuːvjʊˈlɛːrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbaceous plants with rhizomes, native to the southern and southeastern United States, belonging to the family Colchicaceae.
a genus of North American perennial herbs

Examples

The woods were dotted with clusters of Uvularia in early spring.
Botanists studied the unique drooping flowers of Uvularia in their natural habitat.
Gardeners appreciate Uvularia for its delicate yellow blossoms and woodland appeal.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Latin 'uvula' (little grape, uvula), referring to the hanging shape of the flower.
