Pachysandra terminalis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpækiˈsændrə tərˈmɪnəlɪs / (AmE), / ˌpakɪˈsandrə təˈmɪnəlɪs / (BrE)
a slow-growing evergreen subshrub native to Japan, with terminal spikes of white flowers, often grown as a ground cover
Japanese evergreen ground cover plant

Examples

Pachysandra terminalis forms a dense mat that keeps weeds at bay in shady garden areas.
For shaded borders, gardeners often choose Pachysandra terminalis because of its attractive evergreen leaves.
The white flower spikes of Pachysandra terminalis bloom in early spring.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek *pachys* (thick) and *andros* (man/male), referring to the thick stamens; species epithet from Latin *terminalis* (at the end).
