progressivity

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊɡrɛˈsɪvɪti / (AmE), / ˌprəʊɡrɛˈsɪvɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of advancing toward better conditions, especially in terms of policies, reforms, or methods
advancement toward improvement, especially in policies or methods

Examples

The progressivity of the tax system ensures that higher earners pay a greater share of their income.
Many support increased progressivity in social policies to improve equality.
The changes reflected a new progressivity in the company's environmental strategy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: progressivism - advocacy or implementation of social reform or new, liberal ideas
  • Antonym: regressivity - tendency to become less advanced or to revert to a former or less developed state

Etymology

From 'progressive' + '-ity', patterned after similar formations from Latin origins.
