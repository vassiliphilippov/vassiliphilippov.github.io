Jean-Honoré Fragonard

1. PROPN

/ ʒɑn ɒnɔːˈreɪ fræɡəˌnɑːrd / (AmE), / ʒɑ̃ ɔnɔːˈʁe fʁaɡɔnaʁ / (BrE)
(1732–1806) A French painter known for his exuberant and sensuous Rococo style, particularly depicting scenes of aristocratic life and love.
French Rococo painter (1732–1806)

Examples

Many art historians consider Jean-Honoré Fragonard a master of the Rococo movement.
The playful and romantic scenes in Jean-Honoré Fragonard's paintings reflect the spirit of 18th-century France.
At the museum, we admired several works by Jean-Honoré Fragonard in the European art gallery.

Etymology

French personal name; Fragonard is derived from the artist's family name.
