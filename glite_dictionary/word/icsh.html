ICSH

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ ˌsi ˈɛɪtʃ ˌɛs ˈeɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˌaɪ ˌsiː ˈɛtʃ ˌɛs ˈɛtʃ / (BrE)
a hormone secreted by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates ovulation in females and the release of androgens in males
hormone stimulating sex cell activity
medical

Examples

The doctor checked the patient's ICSH levels to evaluate hormone function.
Abnormal ICSH secretion can affect both male and female reproductive systems.
Some textbooks refer to luteinizing hormone and ICSH as essentially the same hormone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luteinizing hormone - a hormone with a similar function, often considered synonymous with ICSH

Etymology

Acronym from Interstitial Cell Stimulating Hormone.
