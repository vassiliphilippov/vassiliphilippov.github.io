ignorance

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪɡnərəns / (AmE), / ˈɪɡnərəns / (BrE)
the state of lacking knowledge, understanding, or awareness about something
lack of knowledge or understanding

Examples

Her ignorance of the rules led to several mistakes during the game.
We should not blame him for his ignorance; he was never taught about this topic.
They discussed how ignorance can be a barrier to understanding between cultures.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: knowledge - information, understanding, or skill gained through learning or experience
  • Antonym: awareness - having knowledge or perception of a situation or fact

Etymology

From Old French *ignorance*, from Latin *ignorantia* (want of knowledge).
