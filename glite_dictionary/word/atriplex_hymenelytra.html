Atriplex hymenelytra

1. NOUN

/ ˈætrɪplɛks ˌhaɪməniˈlaɪtrə / (AmE), / ˈætrɪplɛks ˌhɪmɛnɪˈliːtrə / (BrE)
A low-growing saltbush native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and Mexico, with blue-green, prickly-edged leaves often used for Christmas decorations.
species of saltbush native to the southwestern US and Mexico

Examples

The Atriplex hymenelytra is well adapted to survive in the harsh, dry climates of the desert.
Botanists studied how Atriplex hymenelytra retains moisture with its blue-green, prickly leaves.
Many people use the leaves of Atriplex hymenelytra to add a festive touch to their holiday decorations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saltbush - any plant of the genus Atriplex, typically found in saline soils

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Atriplex (the genus name, from classical Latin for 'orach') and hymenelytra (from Greek hymen 'membrane' + elytron 'covering, sheath').
