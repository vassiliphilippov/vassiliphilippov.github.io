uncovering

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈkʌvərɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈkʌvərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of discovering or revealing something that was hidden or unknown
the act of discovering something

Examples

The scientist's uncovering of the ancient fossils led to new theories about evolution.
Their uncovering of corruption in the company made headlines.
The uncovering of the lost painting thrilled art historians around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discovery - the act of finding or learning something for the first time
  • Antonym: concealing - the act of keeping something hidden

2. NOUN

/ ʌnˈkʌvɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈkʌvərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of removing a covering or revealing something that was hidden or concealed
removal of a covering

Examples

The uncovering of the ancient ruins attracted archaeologists from around the world.
During the ceremony, the uncovering of the statue was met with applause.
Spring cleaning often involves the uncovering of items that have been stored away all winter.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: covering - something that covers or conceals
  • Synonym: revelation - the act of revealing or disclosing something hidden
