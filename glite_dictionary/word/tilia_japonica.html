Tilia japonica

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪliə dʒəˈpoʊnɪkə / (AmE), / ˈtɪlɪə dʒæˈpəʊnɪkə / (BrE)
A medium-sized deciduous tree native to Japan, commonly grown as an ornamental and known as the Japanese lime or Japanese linden.
Japanese lime or linden tree

Examples

The gardens feature several Tilia japonica, prized for their elegant foliage and flowers.
Botanists recommend Tilia japonica for urban landscapes due to its hardiness.
During spring, the Tilia japonica trees attract many bees with their fragrant blossoms.

Etymology

From scientific Neo-Latin: genus name 'Tilia' (linden) + specific epithet 'japonica' (of Japan).
