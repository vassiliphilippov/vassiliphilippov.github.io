Castanopsis chrysophylla

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæstəˈnɑpsɪs ˌkrɪsoʊˈfɪlə / (AmE), / ˌkɑːstəˈnɒpsɪs ˌkrɪsəʊˈfɪlə / (BrE)
A small ornamental evergreen tree native to the Pacific Coast of North America, with glossy yellow-green leaves that are yellow underneath and produces edible nuts.
evergreen tree of the Pacific Coast with edible nuts

Examples

The garden features several Castanopsis chrysophylla that provide shade and beautiful foliage.
Botanists often study Castanopsis chrysophylla because of its unique golden leaves and edible nuts.
Local wildlife depends on the nuts produced by Castanopsis chrysophylla as a seasonal food source.

Etymology

From genus name 'Castanopsis' (Greek 'kastanon', chestnut + 'opsis', appearance) and species epithet 'chrysophylla' (Greek 'chrysos', gold + 'phyllon', leaf), referring to the golden underside of the leaves.
