eponym

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛpəˌnɪm / (AmE), / ˈɛpənɪm / (BrE)
A person after whom a place, people, invention, or other item is named.
person after whom something is named

Examples

Constantine I is the eponym for Constantinople.
The Alzheimer's disease gets its name from its eponym, Dr. Alois Alzheimer.
The city of Alexandria takes its eponym from Alexander the Great.

Synset Examples

Constantine I is the eponym for Constantinople

Related Terms

  • Synonym: namesake - a person or thing that has the same name as another

Etymology

From Greek 'epōnymos' (giving one's name to something), from 'epi-' (upon) + 'onyma' (name).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛpəˌnɪm / (AmE), / ˈɛpənɪm / (BrE)
A person after whom something, such as a discovery, invention, place, institution, or disease, is named or thought to be named; also, the name derived from such a person.
person or thing after whom something is named

Examples

The disease known as Parkinson's is an eponym named after Dr. James Parkinson.
The word "sandwich" is an eponym derived from the Earl of Sandwich.
Down's syndrome serves as an eponym for the English physician John Down.

Synset Examples

Down's syndrome is an eponym for the English physician John Down

Related Terms

  • Synonym: namesake - one who or that which is named after another

Etymology

From Greek epōnymos, from epi- (upon) + onyma (name).
