docket

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːkɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɒkɪt / (BrE)
(Law) A list of cases to be tried in a court or a summary of the court's scheduled activities.
list of court cases or activities
legal

Examples

The judge checked the docket to see which cases would be heard first.
There are several important hearings on the docket this week.
The lawyer reviewed the court's docket before preparing his arguments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agenda - a list of items to be discussed at a meeting

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman 'doguet', diminutive of 'dogue', meaning 'document, register'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06497282-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈdɑːkɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɒkɪt / (BrE)
to make a summary or abstract of a legal document and record it in a formal list or register
to summarize and list a legal document
legal

Examples

The court clerk will docket each case as soon as the paperwork arrives.
Attorneys docketed the motion for summary judgment in the official record.
The legal team dockets every new document to ensure nothing is overlooked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: register - to record or enroll officially

Etymology

From Middle English *doget*, possibly from Anglo-Norman *doquet* (summary, note) or Middle Dutch *docke* (document).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01010573-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɑk.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɒk.ɪt / (BrE)
a list or schedule of matters to be dealt with, especially cases to be heard in a court
list of scheduled matters or cases

Examples

The court's docket was full, so the case was postponed until next month.
The manager reviewed the day's docket before assigning tasks to the team.
New items were added to the meeting docket at the last minute.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agenda - a list of items to be discussed or acted upon
  • Synonym: calendar - a schedule of events or proceedings
  • Synonym: case list - a list of cases scheduled in court

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *docquet*, a summary, note.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05919534-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈdɑːkɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɒkɪt / (BrE)
to officially enter (a case) onto a list or schedule of cases to be heard in court
enter a case onto a legal schedule
legal

Examples

The court clerk docketed several new cases this morning.
After the appeal was filed, it was docketed for a hearing next month.
The judge asked if the matter had been properly docketed before proceeding.

Synset Examples

Only 5 of the 120 cases docketed were tried

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schedule - arrange or plan (an event) to take place at a particular time
  • Antonym: remove - to take off a schedule or list, usually with reference to cases or tasks

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman French 'doquet', a small note or summary.
