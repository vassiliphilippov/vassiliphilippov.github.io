ahimsa

1. NOUN

/ əˈhɪmsə / (AmE), / əˈhɪmsə / (BrE)
The principle of nonviolence toward all living things, especially in Hinduism, Buddhism, and Jainism.
nonviolence principle in Indian religions

Examples

Many followers of Jainism strictly observe ahimsa in all aspects of their lives.
Gandhi made ahimsa a central tenet of his political philosophy.
Practicing ahimsa involves showing compassion to every living creature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonviolence - the practice of avoiding harm to others

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'ahiṃsā' (non-injury, nonviolence).
