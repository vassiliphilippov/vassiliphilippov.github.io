Centrospermae

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛntroʊˈspɜːrmiː / (AmE), / ˌsɛntrəʊˈspɜːmiː / (BrE)
a group of flowering plants used in former classification systems, approximately corresponding to the modern order Caryophyllales.
obsolete group of flowering plants

Examples

The family Amaranthaceae was once classified under Centrospermae in older botanical texts.
Botanists now consider Centrospermae an outdated taxonomic grouping, replacing it with Caryophyllales.
Historical references to Centrospermae can still be found in early 20th-century plant taxonomy literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Caryophyllales - the modern botanical order that includes families formerly classed as Centrospermae

Etymology

From Greek 'kentron' (center) and 'sperma' (seed), referring to the central position of the ovule.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛn.trəʊˈspɜr.mi / (AmE), / ˌsɛn.trəʊˈspɜː.miː / (BrE)
a former botanical group in classification systems, roughly equivalent to the modern order Caryophyllales
former botanical group, ≈ Caryophyllales

Examples

Many plants formerly classified under Centrospermae are now placed in the order Caryophyllales.
The family Amaranthaceae was traditionally included within Centrospermae in older taxonomic systems.
Centrospermae is no longer widely used, but appears in many historical botany texts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Caryophyllales - modern botanical order including species previously in Centrospermae

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, from Greek 'kentron' (center) + 'sperma' (seed), referring to the position of ovules/seeds.
