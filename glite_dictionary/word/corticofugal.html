corticofugal

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɔːrtɪkoʊˈfjuːɡəl / (AmE), / ˌkɔːtɪkəʊˈfjuːɡəl / (BrE)
(of a nerve fiber) passing outward from the cerebral cortex to a subcortical structure or region.
nerve fiber leaving the cerebral cortex
medical

Examples

The scientist studied the effects of corticofugal pathways on sensory processing.
Damage to corticofugal fibers can disrupt motor control.
Corticopetal and corticofugal connections were analyzed in the experiment.

Synset Examples

corticofugal discharges

Related Terms

  • Antonym: corticopetal - (of a nerve fiber) passing toward the cerebral cortex

Etymology

From Latin 'cortex' (bark, outer layer) + 'fugere' (to flee).
