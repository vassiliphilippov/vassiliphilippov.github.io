genome

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːˌnoʊm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəʊm / (BrE)
the complete set of genetic material, including all the genes, present in a cell or organism
the complete set of genes in an organism

Examples

Scientists have successfully mapped the entire genome of several plant species.
Understanding the genome can help us develop targeted medical treatments.
The project aimed to decode the genome of the endangered animal.

Synset Examples

the human genome contains approximately three billion chemical base pairs

Etymology

From German 'Genom', coined from 'Gen' (gene) + '-om' (as in 'chromosome') in the 1920s.
