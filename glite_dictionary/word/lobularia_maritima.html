Lobularia maritima

1. NOUN

/ loʊˌbjuːˈlɛriə məˈrɪt̬ɪmə / (AmE), / ləˌbjuːˈlɛəriə məˈrɪtɪmə / (BrE)
A perennial European plant with clusters of small fragrant, usually white flowers, widely cultivated in gardens; commonly known as sweet alyssum.
fragrant flowering garden plant (sweet alyssum)

Examples

Lobularia maritima adds a delicate touch and pleasant scent to many flower borders.
Gardeners often plant Lobularia maritima for ground cover because of its abundant white blooms.
The cluster of tiny white flowers seen here is characteristic of Lobularia maritima.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet alyssum - common English name for Lobularia maritima

Etymology

From modern Latin, with 'Lobularia' from Latin 'lobulus' (small lobe), and 'maritima' meaning 'of the sea', referring to its natural seaside habitat.
