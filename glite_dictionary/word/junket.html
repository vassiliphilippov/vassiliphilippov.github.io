junket

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋkɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋkɪt / (BrE)
a sweet dessert made from milk that is curdled with rennet and often flavored or sweetened
sweet dessert of curdled milk with rennet
british

Examples

We had junket for dessert after our Sunday lunch.
The traditional recipe calls for using fresh milk to make junket.
Junket is not as common today, but it was once a favorite at English country fairs.

Etymology

From Old French 'jonquette', from 'jonc' (rush), originally referring to a dish served on a rush mat.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋkət / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋkɪt / (BrE)
a trip or excursion taken by an official, especially one funded by public expense, often with an implication of pleasure or lack of necessity
official trip at public expense

Examples

The senator went on a junket to Europe at taxpayer expense.
Critics accused the officials of using public money for lavish junkets.
Many believe the annual conference overseas is just another junket for politicians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boondoggle - an unnecessary, wasteful, or fraudulent project or activity, especially in public policy

Etymology

From Old French 'jonquette' (rush basket), later evolving to mean a feast and then, in the U.S., a pleasure trip paid for by public funds.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00309507-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋkət / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋkɪt / (BrE)
a trip or excursion, especially one taken for pleasure at another's expense, often with the implication of being unnecessary or lavish
pleasure trip, often at another's expense

Examples

The senators went on a week-long junket to a tropical resort funded by lobbyists.
The documentary follows a group of journalists on a press junket to promote the film overseas.
Critics accused the board members of using company money for a lavish European junket.

Synset Examples

many summer excursions to the shore
it was merely a pleasure trip
after cautious sashays into the field

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excursion - a short journey or trip, especially one engaged in as a leisure activity
  • Synonym: trip - a journey or excursion, especially for pleasure
  • Antonym: commute - regular travel between one's home and place of work

Etymology

From Old French 'jonquette', from Medieval Latin 'juncata', originally meaning a cream cheese served on a rush mat, later extended to pleasure outings where such food was served.

4. VERB

/ ˈdʒʌŋ.kət / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋ.kɪt / (BrE)
to go on a trip or excursion for pleasure, often at another's expense or as part of one's work, especially when such trips are seen as unnecessary or luxurious
go on a pleasure trip, often at others' expense

Examples

The group will junket to Paris next week for the international conference.
Government officials were criticized for junketing around the world at taxpayers' expense.
She junketed to several exotic destinations last summer as part of her work with the travel magazine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excursion - a short journey or trip, especially one taken as a leisure activity
  • Synonym: tour - a journey for pleasure during which several different places are visited

Etymology

Originally from Middle English, meaning 'a feast or banquet', possibly via Old French 'jonquette', meaning 'reed mat' or 'rush basket', on which such food was served.
