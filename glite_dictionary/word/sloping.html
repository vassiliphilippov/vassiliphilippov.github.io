sloping

1. ADJ

/ ˈsloʊpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsləʊpɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a surface or object) inclining at an angle; not level or straight.
inclining at an angle; not level

Examples

The cottage had a sloping roof that collected snow in winter.
We walked down a sloping path toward the river.
The garden is beautifully terraced on a sloping hillside.

Synset Examples

an area of gently sloping hills
a room with a sloping ceiling

Related Terms

  • Antonym: level - having a flat or even surface

Etymology

From the verb 'slope' + -ing, Middle English 'aslope', from Old Norse 'slúpra' (to bend down).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsloʊpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsləʊpɪŋ / (BrE)
Inclined at an angle; rising or falling in a slanted direction rather than being flat or level.
inclined or slanted

Examples

The path was sloping gently down toward the river.
They built their house on a sloping hillside with a beautiful view.
Be careful walking on the sloping roof—it can be quite slippery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inclined - having a slanted or sloping position
  • Antonym: level - having a flat, horizontal surface; not sloping

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'slope', from Middle English 'slopen' (to slip, lean).

Source: WordNet
