Melophagus ovinus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛləˈfoʊdʒəs oʊˈvaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌmɛləˈfəʊɡəs əʊˈvaɪnəs / (BrE)
A species of wingless fly that is an external parasite of sheep and sometimes cattle, also known as the sheep ked.
wingless parasitic fly on sheep/cattle
medical

Examples

The veterinarian found several Melophagus ovinus attached to the sheep's fleece.
Sheep infested with Melophagus ovinus may suffer from irritation and skin damage.
Regular inspections help farmers detect and control Melophagus ovinus infestations.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Greek 'melos' (member/limb) + 'phagos' (eating), and Latin 'ovinus' (of sheep).
