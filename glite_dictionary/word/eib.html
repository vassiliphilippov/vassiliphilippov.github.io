EiB

1. NOUN

/ i ˈbi / (AmE), / i ˈbi / (BrE)
a unit of information equal to 1,024 pebibytes or 2^60 bytes, used to measure digital data
unit of digital information (2^60 bytes)

Examples

The new server can store up to 2 EiB of data, making it ideal for large-scale data centers.
Scientists analyzed several EiB of climate data to model future weather patterns.
As technology advances, more companies are dealing with storage measured in EiB rather than petabytes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exbibyte - a unit of information equal to 2^60 bytes
  • Synonym: EB - a unit of information equal to 10^18 bytes (exabyte)

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'exbibyte', combining 'exa-' (from exa-, denoting 10^18) and '-bi' (binary) with 'byte'.
