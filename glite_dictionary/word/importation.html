importation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪm.pɔɹˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪm.pɔːˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of bringing goods or services into a country from abroad for the purpose of trade; goods or services that have been brought in this way
act of bringing in foreign goods or services

Examples

Government regulations can make importation of certain products more expensive.
The country's economy relies heavily on the importation of oil and machinery.
Strict controls are placed on the importation of endangered animal species.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exportation - the act of sending goods or services to another country for sale
  • Synonym: import - a product or service brought into a country from abroad

Etymology

From Latin 'importatio', from 'importare' meaning 'to bring in'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪm.pɔrˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪm.pɔːˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of bringing goods or products into a country from abroad for sale or trade
bringing goods into a country for sale

Examples

The government has imposed new regulations on the importation of food products.
Illegal importation of endangered animals is a serious issue worldwide.
After the war, there was a significant increase in the importation of machinery.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exportation - the act of sending goods to another country for sale
  • Synonym: import - to bring goods or services into a country from abroad

Etymology

From Latin 'importatio', from 'importare' (to bring in).
