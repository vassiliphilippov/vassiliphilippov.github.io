vernacular

1. ADJ

/ vərˈnækjələr / (AmE), / vəˈnækjʊlə / (BrE)
(of language) expressed in or characteristic of the everyday speech used by ordinary people, rather than formal or literary language.
in everyday spoken language

Examples

Some dictionaries draw distinctions between formal English and vernacular speech.
She prefers to write in a vernacular style to make her stories accessible to all readers.
The documentary included interviews in the region's vernacular dialects.

Synset Examples

common parlance
a vernacular term
vernacular speakers
the vulgar tongue of the masses
the technical and vulgar names for an animal species

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colloquial - (of language) used in ordinary or familiar conversation; not formal or literary

Etymology

From Latin vernaculus (native, domestic).

2. NOUN

/ vərˈnækjələr / (AmE), / vəˈnakjʊlə / (BrE)
the language or dialect spoken by the ordinary people of a particular country or region, or by a specific group
language of a specific group or region

Examples

The novel was written in the vernacular to make it accessible to everyday people.
In New Orleans, local musicians often blend their vernacular with jazz traditions.
The group's secret vernacular made it difficult for outsiders to understand their conversations.

Synset Examples

they don't speak our lingo

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dialect - a form of a language specific to a region or group
  • Synonym: jargon - specialized language used by a particular group

Etymology

From Latin 'vernaculus' (native, indigenous).

3. NOUN

/ vərˈnækjələr / (AmE), / vəˈnækjʊlə / (BrE)
the language or dialect spoken by ordinary people in a particular country or region, especially as distinct from the literary or official language
everyday spoken language of a region

Examples

Many writers sought to capture the vernacular of their home regions in their novels.
The government issued pamphlets in the local vernacular so everyone could understand them.
Medieval scholars often wrote in Latin rather than in the vernacular of the people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colloquial - language used in ordinary or familiar conversation; not formal or literary
  • Antonym: literary - relating to the more formal, written language

Etymology

From Latin 'vernaculus' (native, domestic), from 'verna' (home-born slave).
