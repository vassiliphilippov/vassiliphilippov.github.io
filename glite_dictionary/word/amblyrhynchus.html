Amblyrhynchus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæmbliˈrɪŋkəs / (AmE), / ˌambliˈrɪŋkəs / (BrE)
A genus of iguanas comprising the marine iguana, native to the Galápagos Islands, known for its ability to swim and forage in the sea.
genus of marine iguanas

Examples

Researchers studied the unique adaptations of Amblyrhynchus to a marine environment.
Only Amblyrhynchus regularly feed in the sea among modern lizards.
The conservation status of Amblyrhynchus has attracted attention from environmentalists worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marine iguana - a lizard species of the Galápagos Islands that forages in the sea

Etymology

From Greek amblus (blunt) + rhynchos (snout), referring to the animal's blunt snout.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæm.blɪˈrɪŋ.kəs / (AmE), / ˌam.blɪˈrɪŋ.kəs / (BrE)
A genus of iguanas found primarily in the Galápagos Islands, known as marine iguanas, unique for their ability to forage in the sea.
genus of marine iguanas in the Galápagos

Examples

The Amblyrhynchus genus includes the only marine lizard in the world.
Members of Amblyrhynchus are endemic to the Galápagos Islands.
Researchers studied how Amblyrhynchus adapts to its coastal environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marine iguana - a species of lizard adapted to marine life, found in the Galápagos

Etymology

From Greek amblys (blunt) + rhynchos (snout), referring to the animal's rounded snout.
