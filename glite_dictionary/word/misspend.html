to misspend

1. VERB

/ mɪsˈspɛnd / (AmE), / mɪsˈspɛnd / (BrE)
to spend time, money, or other resources in a foolish or wasteful way
spend time or money unwisely

Examples

He misspent his youth on reckless adventures instead of focusing on his studies.
If you misspend your time at university, you may regret it later.
They realized they had misspent most of their budget on unnecessary expenses.

Synset Examples

He misspent his youth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to squander - to waste something, especially money or time, in a reckless manner

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + spend; modeled on patterns from Old and Middle English.

2. VERB

/ mɪˈspɛnd / (AmE), / mɪˈspɛnd / (BrE)
to spend money or other resources in a foolish or wasteful way
spend resources unwisely

Examples

If you misspend your savings on unnecessary luxuries, you may regret it later.
Many governments have been criticized for how they misspend public funds.
She realized she had misspent most of her youth on trivial pursuits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to squander - to waste something, especially money or time, in a reckless manner
  • Synonym: waste - to use carelessly, extravagantly, or to no purpose
  • Antonym: save - to keep and store up something, especially money, for future use

Etymology

From Latin 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'spend', first attested in the 16th century.
