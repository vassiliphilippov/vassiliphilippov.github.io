stirk

1. NOUN

/ stɝk / (AmE), / stɜːk / (BrE)
A young cow or bull, especially one between one and two years old.
young cow or bull (1-2 years old)

Examples

The farmer separated the stirk from the rest of the herd for inspection.
In the spring, the stirks were moved to a new pasture to graze.
A healthy stirk can grow quickly with proper care and feeding.

Etymology

From Old English *stierc* (young bullock).
