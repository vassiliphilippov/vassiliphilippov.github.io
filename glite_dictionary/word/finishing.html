finishing

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪnɪʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɪnɪʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of bringing something to completion or an end
the act of completing something

Examples

The audience applauded at the finishing of the performance.
Her strong finishing in the marathon secured her a place on the podium.
After months of work, the project's finishing was celebrated by the entire team.

Synset Examples

his best finish in a major tournament was third
the speaker's finishing was greeted with applause

Related Terms

  • Synonym: completion - the action or process of completing or finishing something
  • Antonym: start - the act of beginning something

Etymology

From the verb 'finish', which comes from Old French 'finir', from Latin 'finire' (to limit, end).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪnɪʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɪnɪʃɪŋ / (BrE)
a decorative texture or layer applied to a surface to give it a particular appearance or protection
decorative or protective surface coating

Examples

The wooden table features a glossy finishing that highlights the natural grain.
He added a layer of protective finishing to the metal sculpture.
The finishing on the kitchen cabinets resists both stains and scratches.

Synset Examples

the boat had a metallic finish
he applied a coat of a clear finish
when the finish is too thin it is difficult to apply evenly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finish - a final decorative or protective surface applied to something
