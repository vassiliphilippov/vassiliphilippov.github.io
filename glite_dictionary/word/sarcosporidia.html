Sarcosporidia

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɑr.kə.spɔːrˈɪd.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌsɑː.kə.spɔːˈrɪd.i.ə / (BrE)
A group of microscopic parasites that infect the muscles of vertebrates, sometimes causing disease.
muscle-infecting parasites of vertebrates
medical

Examples

Sarcosporidia are often detected in the skeletal muscles of livestock during post-mortem examinations.
Veterinarians monitor the presence of Sarcosporidia to prevent outbreaks in farm animals.
The lifecycle of Sarcosporidia involves both a definitive and an intermediate host.

Etymology

From Greek 'sarx' (flesh) and 'sporos' (seed).
