tennis

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛnɪs / (AmE), / ˈtɛnɪs / (BrE)
A game in which two or four players use rackets to hit a ball over a net dividing a rectangular court.
game played with rackets and a net

Examples

Serena Williams is one of the greatest tennis players of all time.
We played a game of tennis on the new court at the park.
He watches tennis matches every weekend during the major tournaments.

Etymology

From French 'tenez' (take, receive), called out as a warning during play.
