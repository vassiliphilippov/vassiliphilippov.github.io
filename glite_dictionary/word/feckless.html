feckless

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɛklɪs / (AmE), / ˈfɛklɪs / (BrE)
Lacking determination, effectiveness, or responsibility; incompetent or ineffectual.
lacking effectiveness or responsibility

Examples

The project failed due to his feckless leadership and lack of direction.
Her feckless attitude toward her studies led to poor grades.
The company's feckless response to the crisis caused customers to lose trust.

Synset Examples

feckless attempts to repair the plumbing
inept handling of the account

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inept - having or showing no skill; clumsy
  • Antonym: competent - having the necessary ability or skills; able

Etymology

From Scots 'feckless', from 'feck' (effect, value, vigor) + '-less'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɛkləs / (AmE), / ˈfɛkləs / (BrE)
lacking initiative or strength of character; not fit to assume responsibility
lacking responsibility or initiative

Examples

The manager was frustrated with his feckless assistant, who missed every important deadline.
Critics often describe the administration as feckless in the face of crisis.
His feckless attitude made him an unreliable partner in group projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irresponsible - not showing a proper sense of responsibility
  • Antonym: responsible - having an obligation to do something, or having control over or care for someone

Etymology

From Scots 'feckless', from 'feck' (effect, value, vigor) + '-less'.

Synset POS: s
