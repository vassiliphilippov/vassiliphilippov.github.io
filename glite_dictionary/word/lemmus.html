Lemmus

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛməs / (AmE), / ˈlɛməs / (BrE)
A genus of rodents in the family Cricetidae, commonly known as lemmings, native to Arctic and subarctic regions.
genus of lemmings

Examples

The genus Lemmus includes several species of lemmings found in northern Europe and Asia.
Researchers observed behavioral differences among Lemmus populations in various habitats.
Climate change threatens the survival of some Lemmus species in the Arctic tundra.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemming - small Arctic rodent known for population cycles

Etymology

From New Latin, genus name 'Lemmus', of Scandinavian origin, related to Norwegian 'lemman' (lemming).
