panoply

1. NOUN

/ ˈpænəpli / (AmE), / ˈpanəpli / (BrE)
an impressive and complete collection or display of things
impressive or complete display

Examples

The museum offers a panoply of ancient artifacts from around the world.
The ceremony included a panoply of colorful costumes and lively music.
Modern technology provides us with a panoply of ways to communicate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: array - an orderly or impressive arrangement of items

Etymology

From Greek 'panoplia' (full armor), from 'pan-' (all) + 'hopla' (arms).
