thuggery

1. NOUN

/ ˈθʌɡɚi / (AmE), / ˈθʌɡəri / (BrE)
violent or brutal behavior, especially that characteristic of thugs
violent or brutal acts

Examples

His rise to power was marked by intimidation and thuggery throughout the city.
The organization was accused of using thuggery to silence its critics.
There is no place for thuggery in civil society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violence - behavior involving physical force intended to hurt or damage
  • Synonym: brutality - savage physical violence; great cruelty

Etymology

From 'thug' + '-ery'; 'thug' from Hindi 'ṭhag' meaning 'swindler' or 'deceiver'.
