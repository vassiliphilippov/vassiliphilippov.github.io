blacktail

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈblækteɪl / (BrE)
A mule deer, especially one found in the western Rocky Mountains, known for its black-tipped tail.
a mule deer with a black-tipped tail

Examples

The hunter spotted a blacktail moving through the dense underbrush.
We saw several blacktails grazing near the edge of the forest at dusk.
The blacktail is easily recognized by the distinctive color of its tail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mule deer - a species of deer native to western North America
