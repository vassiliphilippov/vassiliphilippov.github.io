ramrod

1. NOUN

/ ˈræmˌrɑd / (AmE), / ˈræmrɒd / (BrE)
a rod for pushing the charge and wadding down the barrel of a muzzle-loading firearm
rod for loading old firearms

Examples

The soldier slid the ramrod into the barrel to pack the powder tightly.
He couldn't fire the musket until he had used the ramrod.
The antique rifle displayed in the museum still had its original ramrod beneath the barrel.

2. NOUN

/ ˈræmˌrɑd / (AmE), / ˈramˌrɒd / (BrE)
a rod used for cleaning or loading the barrel of a firearm
rod for cleaning or loading a firearm

Examples

The soldier used a ramrod to push the powder down the musket barrel.
After firing, he cleaned the rifle carefully with a ramrod.
Old flintlock guns always came with a metal ramrod attached under the barrel.

Etymology

From 'ram' + 'rod', referring to its use to ram the charge into the firearm barrel.

3. NOUN

/ ˈræmˌrɑd / (AmE), / ˈræm.rɒd / (BrE)
a person who is stern, strict, and in charge, often demanding obedience from others; a harshly demanding overseer
strict, demanding overseer

Examples

The new manager was a real ramrod, insisting that everyone arrive early and stay late.
Few employees appreciated working under such a ramrod, but productivity did increase.
Despite his reputation as a ramrod, he was respected for his fairness and attention to detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taskmaster - a person who imposes hard work or discipline
  • Synonym: tyrant - a cruel and oppressive ruler or boss
  • Synonym: slave driver - someone who makes people work extremely hard

Etymology

Originally from 'ramrod,' a rod used to force the charge into the barrel of a firearm; metaphorical sense refers to someone enforcing discipline.
