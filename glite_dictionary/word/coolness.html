coolness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkuːlnəs / (AmE), / ˈkuːlnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being moderately cold
moderate coldness; cool temperature

Examples

I enjoy the coolness of the shade on a summer day.
The coolness in the air hinted at the coming of autumn.
She wrapped herself in a sweater to guard against the morning coolness.

Synset Examples

the chilliness of early morning

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: chilliness - the quality of being unpleasantly cold
  • Antonym: warmth - the quality or state of being warm

Etymology

From 'cool' + '-ness', with 'cool' from Old English 'cōl' (cold, not warm).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkuːlnəs / (AmE), / ˈkuːlnəs / (BrE)
a calm and unruffled quality, especially when facing stress or danger
calm self-assurance under stress

Examples

Despite the pressure, she maintained her coolness throughout the interview.
His coolness under fire earned him the respect of his peers.
The coach admired her coolness when the team was losing.

Synset Examples

he performed with all the coolness of a veteran

Related Terms

  • Synonym: composure - calmness and self-control, especially under pressure
  • Antonym: agitation - a state of nervousness or unrest

Etymology

From the adjective 'cool' plus the suffix '-ness', forming a noun.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkuːlnəs / (AmE), / ˈkuːlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being emotionally detached or showing little warmth or enthusiasm
lack of warmth or enthusiasm

Examples

There was a certain coolness in her reply that made me uneasy.
After the argument, a noticeable coolness developed between the two colleagues.
He spoke with a coolness that suggested he didn't really care.

Synset Examples

a distressing coldness of tone and manner

Related Terms

  • Antonym: warmth - the quality of being friendly or enthusiastic
  • Synonym: aloofness - the state of being distant or uninvolved

Etymology

From 'cool' + '-ness', meaning the state or quality of being cool.

4. NOUN

/ ˈkuːlnəs / (AmE), / ˈkuːlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of remaining calm and composed, especially in difficult or dangerous situations; fearless self-possession
calmness and composure under pressure

Examples

She showed remarkable coolness during the crisis and reassured everyone around her.
Maintaining coolness under pressure is essential for emergency responders.
Even when faced with accusation, his coolness never faltered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: composure - calmness and self-control, especially under pressure
  • Antonym: panic - sudden uncontrollable fear or anxiety
  • Synonym: calmness - the quality of being free from agitation

Etymology

From 'cool' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality of being cool (calm).
