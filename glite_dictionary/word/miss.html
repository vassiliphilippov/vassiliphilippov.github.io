Miss

1. NOUN

/ mɪs / (AmE), / mɪs / (BrE)
a title used before the surname or full name of an unmarried woman or girl
title for unmarried woman or girl

Examples

Please hand your homework to Miss Carter at the front of the class.
The invitation was addressed to Miss Julia Smith.
When I was a child, we always called our teacher Miss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ms - Title for a woman without specifying marital status
  • Antonym: Mrs - Title for a married woman

Etymology

From Old French 'mes', from Latin 'missa', used as a title (earlier 'mistress').
