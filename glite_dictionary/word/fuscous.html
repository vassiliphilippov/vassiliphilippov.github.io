fuscous

1. ADJ

/ ˈfʌskoʊs / (AmE), / ˈfʌskəs / (BrE)
(of color) dusky or dark brownish-gray.
dusky brownish-gray color

Examples

The moth had fuscous wings with subtle white markings.
A fuscous cloud hung over the city, threatening rain.
He noted the fuscous tinge in the mineral sample under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dark - having little or no light; closer to black than white
  • Degree Higher: black - the darkest color, resulting from the absence of light

Etymology

From Latin 'fuscus' (dark, dusky).
