Amygdalus communis

1. NOUN

/ əˌmɪɡdələs kəˈmjunɪs / (AmE), / əˌmɪɡdɑːləs kəˈmjuːnɪs / (BrE)
A small deciduous tree native to Asia and North Africa, cultivated for its edible nuts (almonds) and pink blossoms.
almond tree; small deciduous tree with edible nuts

Examples

The Amygdalus communis is widely grown for its nutritious almonds in Mediterranean climates.
Botanists classify the almond tree as Amygdalus communis in scientific literature.
Pink blossoms appear on Amygdalus communis in early spring, before the leaves emerge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: almond tree - the common name for Amygdalus communis, cultivated for edible almonds

Etymology

From Latin 'amygdalus' (almond tree) and 'communis' (common).
