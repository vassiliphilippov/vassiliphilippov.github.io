bakeapple

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪkˌæpəl / (AmE), / ˈbeɪkˌæpəl / (BrE)
a creeping plant found in northern regions, bearing yellow or orange edible berries; another name for the cloudberry
northern berry-producing plant; cloudberry

Examples

The marsh was filled with ripe bakeapple, ready for picking in early August.
She made a delicious jam using fresh bakeapples from the bog.
Tourists are often surprised by the unique taste of the bakeapple berry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloudberry - northern plant with edible orange berries

Etymology

Perhaps from a mishearing of French 'baie qu'appelle' ('what is this berry called?'), referring to the fruit.
