Shahaptian

1. NOUN

/ ʃəˈhæptiən / (AmE), / ʃəˈhæptiən / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people formerly living in Oregon along the Columbia River and its tributaries in Washington and northern Idaho
member of a Native American people of Oregon and nearby regions

Examples

Her grandmother was a proud Shahaptian who passed down traditions to her family.
The Shahaptian tribes have a rich history along the Columbia River.
Researchers studied the cultural practices of the Shahaptian people in the region.

Etymology

From native self-designation via ethnological use in American English.

2. NOUN

/ ʃəˈhæptiən / (AmE), / ʃəˈhæptiən / (BrE)
A group of closely related Penutian languages spoken by members of the Sahaptin and Nez Percé peoples in the Pacific Northwest of the United States.
a Penutian language of the Pacific Northwest

Examples

The linguist specialized in documenting the Shahaptian languages of the Columbia Plateau.
Several texts in Shahaptian have survived from the early contact period.
Efforts are underway to revitalize the Shahaptian language among tribal communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sahaptin - a specific language of the Shahaptian group

Etymology

From the name of the Sahaptin people, whose language forms part of this group, with '-ian' denoting language affiliation.
