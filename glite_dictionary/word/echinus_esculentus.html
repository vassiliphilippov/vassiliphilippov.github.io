Echinus esculentus

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈkaɪnəs ˌɛskjəˈlɛntəs / (AmE), / ɪˈkaɪnəs ˌɛskjʊˈlɛntəs / (BrE)
A species of edible sea urchin found in the northeast Atlantic Ocean and adjacent seas.
edible sea urchin species

Examples

The spiny shell of the Echinus esculentus can often be found washed up on rocky beaches.
In some coastal regions, Echinus esculentus is considered a delicacy and harvested for food.
Marine biologists are studying the population decline of Echinus esculentus in recent years.

Etymology

From Latin: 'echinus' (hedgehog/sea urchin) and 'esculentus' (edible).
