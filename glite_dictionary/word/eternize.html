to eternize

1. VERB

/ ɪˈtɜrnaɪz / (AmE), / ɪˈtɜːnʌɪz / (BrE)
To make lasting or famous forever; to immortalize.
make eternal or famous forever

Examples

The legendary writer hoped that his novels would eternize his name in literary history.
This sculpture will eternize the hero's bravery for generations to come.
Many artists seek ways to eternize their work through timeless beauty.

Synset Examples

This melody immortalized its composer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to immortalize - to make someone or something remembered forever

Etymology

From French éterniser, from Latin aeternus (eternal).

2. VERB

/ ɪˈtɝː.naɪz / (AmE), / ɪˈtɜː.nʌɪz / (BrE)
to make something last forever or immortalize, especially in memory or art
make eternal; immortalize

Examples

The monument was built to eternize the hero's memory for future generations.
Poetry can eternize fleeting moments, preserving them long after we are gone.
By writing his biography, she hoped to eternize his achievements for history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to immortalize - to make someone or something remembered forever
  • Antonym: to forget - to fail to remember

Etymology

From French 'éterniser', from Latin 'aeternus' (eternal).
