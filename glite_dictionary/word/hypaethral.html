hypaethral

1. ADJ

/ haɪˈiːθrəl / (AmE), / hɪˈpiːθrəl / (BrE)
(of a building, especially a temple) having no roof; open to the sky, either wholly or partly.
open to the sky (architecture)

Examples

The ancient Greek temple was hypaethral, allowing sunlight to illuminate its central altar.
Many early sanctuaries were designed as hypaethral structures with open courtyards.
The museum's latest exhibition includes a model of a hypaethral amphitheater.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roofless - having no roof

Etymology

From Greek 'hypaithros' (exposed to the air), from 'hypo-' (under) + 'aithēr' (air).
