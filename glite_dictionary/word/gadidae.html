Gadidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡædəˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈɡadɪdiː / (BrE)
A large family of mostly marine fishes, including cod, haddock, and related species, many of which are important as food fish.
family of cod and related fishes

Examples

The Gadidae family includes many species widely consumed around the world.
Scientists are studying the evolutionary history of Gadidae to understand their adaptation to cold waters.
Several members of the Gadidae are commercially important, especially in North Atlantic fisheries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cod - a common fish in the Gadidae family, important as food
  • Synonym: haddock - another key species in the Gadidae family, used for food

Etymology

From Modern Latin 'Gadidae', based on 'Gadus' (the cod genus).
