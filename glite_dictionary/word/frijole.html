frijole

1. NOUN

/ friˈhoʊleɪ / (AmE), / friˈhəʊleɪ / (BrE)
A Mexican bean, especially one that is usually dried and used in cooking.
Mexican dried bean used in cooking

Examples

We made a delicious stew using black frijoles and fresh vegetables.
Many traditional Mexican dishes feature frijoles as a main ingredient.
I bought a bag of dried frijoles at the market to cook this weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bean - an edible seed, typically kidney-shaped, of various plants of the legume family

Etymology

From Mexican Spanish 'frijol', ultimately from Latin 'phaseolus' (bean).

2. NOUN

/ friˈhoʊleɪ / (AmE), / friˈhəʊleɪ / (BrE)
A type of bean, especially one with large red kidney-shaped seeds, commonly used in Mexican and Southwestern US cuisine.
bean used in Mexican and Southwestern cooking
american

Examples

The restaurant's burrito is filled with rice, cheese, and frijoles.
She simmered the frijoles for hours to make them perfectly tender.
Frijoles negros are a staple ingredient in many traditional Cuban dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bean - the edible seed of various plants of the legume family

Etymology

From Mexican Spanish 'frijol', from Latin 'phaseolus' (bean).
