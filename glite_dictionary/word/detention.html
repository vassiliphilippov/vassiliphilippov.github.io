detention

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
A punishment given to a student in which they are required to stay at school after normal hours.
school punishment requiring extra time

Examples

He received detention for talking during class.
If you are late again, you'll be given detention.
Many students dread staying after school for detention.

Synset Examples

the detention of tardy pupils

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suspension - temporary removal of privilege to attend classes as punishment

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
the state of being kept confined, especially as a punishment or while awaiting a decision
state of being confined, usually temporarily

Examples

The suspect was held in detention for several hours before being released.
School detention was given to students who arrived late.
His family was concerned about his prolonged detention without formal charges.

Synset Examples

his detention was politically motivated
the prisoner is on hold
he is in the custody of police

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imprisonment - the state of being confined, especially for a long time or as a punishment
  • Antonym: release - the act of setting someone free from confinement

Etymology

From Latin 'detentio', from 'detinere' meaning 'to hold back'.
