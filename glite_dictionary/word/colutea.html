Colutea

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈluːtiə / (AmE), / kɒˈluːtɪə / (BrE)
a small genus of Eurasian shrubs, known for their yellow flowers and inflated, bladder-like seed pods.
genus of Eurasian shrubs with yellow flowers and bladder pods

Examples

Botanists were excited to find a rare species of Colutea during their fieldwork.
The Colutea shrubs in the garden produced abundant yellow blossoms this spring.
Some gardeners appreciate Colutea for its unusual bladder-like seed pods.

Etymology

From Latin Colutea, the classical name for the plant.
