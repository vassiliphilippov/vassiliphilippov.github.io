Pluvialis

1. NOUN

/ pluːˈvaɪəlɪs / (AmE), / pluːˈvaɪəlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the plover family, commonly known as golden plovers.
a genus of golden plovers

Examples

The genus Pluvialis includes several species of golden plovers found across the world.
Ornithologists recently discovered a new species within the Pluvialis genus.
Many birdwatchers travel great distances to observe members of Pluvialis during migration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden plover - a migratory wading bird of the genus Pluvialis

Etymology

From Latin pluvialis, meaning 'of rain' or 'rainy', referring to the birds' association with rainy weather.

2. NOUN

/ pluˈvaɪəlɪs / (AmE), / pluˈvaɪəlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the plover family, including the golden plovers.
genus of golden plovers

Examples

The Pluvialis genus includes several species of golden plovers found across the world.
Ornithologists often study Pluvialis to understand migratory patterns in shorebirds.
Members of the Pluvialis genus are known for their striking plumage during breeding season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plover - a type of wading bird with a short bill, including members of Pluvialis

Etymology

From Latin *pluvialis* (pertaining to rain), as these birds were thought to predict rain.
