swart

1. ADJ

/ swɔrt / (AmE), / swɑːt / (BrE)
(archaic) having naturally dark skin or a dark complexion.
archaic: dark-skinned, dark-complexioned
archaic

Examples

In the dim candlelight, his swart features appeared almost sculpted from shadow.
The old poem described a swart knight whose presence was both mysterious and imposing.
Among the crowd stood a swart stranger, his face glowing with quiet confidence.

Synset Examples

a dark-skinned beauty
gold earrings gleamed against her dusky cheeks
a smile on his swarthy face
‘swart’ is archaic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swarthy - having a dark complexion
  • Antonym: fair - having light complexion

Etymology

From Old English *sweart* (“black, dark”), related to Old High German *swarz* and Old Norse *svartr*.
