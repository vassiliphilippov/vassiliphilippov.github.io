Coregonus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːrəˈɡoʊnəs / (AmE), / ˌkɒrɪˈɡəʊnəs / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater whitefishes found mainly in cold waters of the Northern Hemisphere, including several species important for fisheries.
genus of freshwater whitefishes

Examples

Some species within the genus Coregonus are valued for their delicate flavor and commercial importance.
Researchers discovered a new population of Coregonus in the northern lakes last year.
The ecological impact of overfishing on Coregonus species is a growing concern among conservationists.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, formed from Greek 'kore' (pupil of the eye) + 'gōnía' (angle), in reference to a characteristic of the fish.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːrəˈɡoʊnəs / (AmE), / ˌkɒrɪˈɡəʊnəs / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater whitefishes in the family Coregonidae, found mainly in cold lakes and rivers of the Northern Hemisphere.
genus of whitefish

Examples

The Coregonus genus includes several species of commercially important whitefish.
Scientists have studied the migration patterns of Coregonus populations in northern lakes.
Members of the Coregonus genus are known for their silvery bodies and economic value.

Etymology

From Greek 'kore' (pupil of the eye) + 'gonia' (angle), referring to a morphological feature.
