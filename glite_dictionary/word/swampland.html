swampland

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɑmˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈswɒmˌlænd / (BrE)
Low-lying land that is seasonally flooded, typically supporting more woody plants than a marsh and having better drainage than a bog.
seasonally flooded low land with woody plants

Examples

Large areas of swampland are home to many rare birds and reptiles.
The developers plan to drain the swampland and build new homes on the site.
Her research focuses on the unique plants that thrive in southern swampland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swamp - area of low-lying, waterlogged ground
  • Degree Lower: marsh - wetland often dominated by herbaceous plants
  • Degree Higher: bog - wetland with poor drainage and mainly peat moss

Etymology

Formed from 'swamp' + 'land'.
