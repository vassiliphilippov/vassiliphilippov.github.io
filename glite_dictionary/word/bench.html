bench

1. NOUN

/ bɛntʃ / (AmE), / bɛntʃ / (BrE)
The magistrate, judge, or judges collectively occupying the seat of a court of law in judicial capacity.
the judges collectively in a court
legal

Examples

The bench delivered its verdict after three days of deliberation.
Her argument was directed at the bench, urging the judges to reconsider.
It is unusual for the entire bench to sit for minor cases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: court - a tribunal presided over by a judge, judges, or a magistrate in civil and criminal cases
  • Antonym: bar - the legal profession collectively or lawyers as a group

Etymology

From Old English 'benc', from Proto-Germanic *bankiz, originally meaning a seat; extended to mean the seat of a judge and, by extension, the judicial authority.
