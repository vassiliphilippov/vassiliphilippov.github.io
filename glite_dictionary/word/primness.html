primness

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪmnəs / (AmE), / ˈprɪmnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being very formal, proper, or showing affected modesty or respectability.
excessive formality or modesty

Examples

Her primness made her seem distant at social gatherings.
He spoke with such primness that others felt uncomfortable.
Despite her outward primness, she enjoyed a good joke now and then.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modesty - the quality of being modest; lack of vanity or boastfulness
  • Antonym: immodesty - lack of modesty; shamelessness

Etymology

From 'prim' (precise, formal) + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting a state or quality).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɪmnəs / (AmE), / ˈprɪmnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being excessively proper, formal, or demure, often in a way that seems exaggerated or affected
exaggerated formality or propriety

Examples

Her primness made others feel uncomfortable at the relaxed family gathering.
There was an air of primness in her speech, as if she disapproved of everything informal.
The teacher's primness contrasted sharply with the students' playful attitudes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propriety - conformity to accepted standards of behavior
  • Antonym: impropriety - failure to observe accepted standards; improper behavior
  • Synonym: prudishness - being easily shocked by matters relating to sex or nudity

Etymology

From 'prim' (neat, demure) + '-ness' (forming nouns of quality).
