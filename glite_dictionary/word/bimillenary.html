bimillenary

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.məˈlɛn.ə.ri / (AmE), / ˌbɪ.mɪˈlɛn.əri / (BrE)
the 2000th anniversary of an event or the celebration marking it
2000th anniversary or celebration

Examples

The city organized a grand festival to celebrate its bimillenary in 2025.
Historians published a book for the bimillenary of the ancient temple.
Rome marked its bimillenary with a series of public events and exhibitions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bicentennial - the 200th anniversary or celebration
  • Degree Lower: millenary - the 1000th anniversary or celebration

Etymology

From Latin 'bis' (twice) + 'millenarius' (containing a thousand), from 'mille' (thousand).

2. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪməˈlɛnəri / (AmE), / ˌbɪməˈlɛnəri / (BrE)
A period or anniversary of 2,000 years.
a period of 2,000 years

Examples

Scientists studied climate trends over a bimillenary to understand long-term changes.
The city is preparing celebrations for its bimillenary next year, marking 2,000 years since its founding.
Historical records from the bimillenary offer insights into ancient customs and beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: millennium - a period of 1,000 years
  • Degree Higher: triennium - a period of 3,000 years

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) and 'millenarius' (of a thousand).
