altar

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔltɚ / (AmE), / ˈɔːltə / (BrE)
A raised structure or table used in religious ceremonies for making offerings, sacrifices, or worshipping a deity.
raised structure for religious offerings

Examples

The bride and groom stood before the altar to exchange their vows.
Offerings of fruit and flowers were placed on the altar during the festival.
The ancient temple featured a stone altar where sacrifices were made.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrine - a place regarded as holy where offerings are made

Etymology

From Latin 'altare', related to 'altus' (high, elevated).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔltɚ / (AmE), / ˈɔːltə / (BrE)
a table or flat-topped block in a Christian church at which the bread and wine are consecrated in communion services
table in a Christian church for communion

Examples

The priest placed the chalice on the altar before the service began.
Many wedding ceremonies take place in front of the church altar.
Fresh flowers are arranged on the altar every Sunday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: communion table - table used for the celebration of communion

Etymology

From Latin altare (high place for sacrifice), related to altus (high).
