reprehensibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛprihɛn.səˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌrɛprihɛnsɪˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The quality of being deserving of blame, criticism, or disapproval.
deserving of blame or criticism

Examples

The reprehensibility of his actions shocked everyone in the community.
Many criticized the reprehensibility of corporations that pollute the environment for profit.
Debates about the reprehensibility of lying often arise during ethical discussions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reprehensible - deserving censure or condemnation

Etymology

From Latin 'reprehensibilis' (blameworthy), from 'reprehendere' (to blame, seize).
