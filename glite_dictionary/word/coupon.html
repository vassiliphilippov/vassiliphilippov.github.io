coupon

1. NOUN

/ ˈkuːˌpɑn / (AmE), / ˈkuːpɒn / (BrE)
a detachable certificate, voucher, or ticket that can be exchanged for a financial benefit or discount, often as part of a bond, offer, or promotion
detachable voucher or certificate for redemption

Examples

She handed the cashier a coupon for five dollars off her groceries.
Each bond includes a coupon that can be redeemed for interest at regular intervals.
You can use this coupon to get a free drink with any meal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voucher - a small printed piece of paper that entitles the holder to a discount or exchange for goods or services

Etymology

From French 'coupon', from 'couper' (to cut).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkuːpɑn / (AmE), / ˈkuːpɒn / (BrE)
a small sample of a material or substance used for testing purposes, especially in scientific or engineering contexts
test sample of a material or substance

Examples

The lab technician prepared a coupon of the alloy for strength testing.
Each coupon was labeled and catalogued before undergoing analysis.
A stainless steel coupon was used to measure the corrosion resistance of the new coating.

Etymology

From French 'coupon' (a piece cut off), from couper (to cut).
