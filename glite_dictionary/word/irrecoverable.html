irrecoverable

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪrəˈkʌvərəbl / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪˈkʌvərəbl / (BrE)
(of something lost, damaged, or gone) not able to be recovered, regained, or restored.
not able to be recovered or regained

Examples

The files were deleted in an irrecoverable way, making them impossible to restore.
The museum mourned the irrecoverable loss of several priceless artifacts during the fire.
After the scandal, her reputation suffered irrecoverable damage.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: recoverable - able to be recovered or regained

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'in-' (not) + 'recoverable', from Latin 'recuperare' (to recover).
