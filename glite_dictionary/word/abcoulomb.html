abcoulomb

1. NOUN

/ ˌæbˈkuːlɑːm / (AmE), / ˌæbˈkuːlɒm / (BrE)
a unit of electrical charge equal to 10 coulombs, used in the CGS electromagnetic system of units
unit of electrical charge (10 coulombs)

Examples

The abcoulomb was used in early scientific literature to measure electrical charge.
One abcoulomb is equivalent to ten coulombs in the SI system.
Electrical calculations in the CGS system often involve the abcoulomb as the unit of charge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coulomb - the SI unit of electric charge

Etymology

From 'ab-' (absolute, from the CGS system) + 'coulomb' (named after French physicist Charles-Augustin de Coulomb).
