Martynia annua

1. NOUN

/ mɑːrˈtɪniə ˈænjuə / (AmE), / mɑːˈtɪniə ˈanjʊə / (BrE)
a sprawling annual or perennial herb native to Central America and the West Indies, known for its bell-shaped flowers and distinctive horned fruit
herbaceous plant with horned fruit

Examples

The Martynia annua is often grown for its unusual horned fruit.
Botanists found several specimens of Martynia annua along the forest edge.
The bell-shaped flowers of Martynia annua range from creamy-white to deep purple.

Etymology

From modern Latin, named after the Swedish botanist John Martyn.
