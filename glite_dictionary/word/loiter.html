to loiter

1. VERB

/ ˈlɔɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɔɪtə / (BrE)
to stand or wait around a place without apparent purpose; to linger aimlessly in or about a place
stand or wait without purpose

Examples

The security guard asked the teenagers not to loiter near the entrance.
Shops often display signs warning people not to loiter around the premises.
He would sometimes loiter in the park after school, enjoying the quiet.

Synset Examples

The high school students like to loiter in the Central Square
Who is this man that is hanging around the department?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to linger - to stay in a place longer than necessary because of a reluctance to leave
  • Antonym: hurry - to move or act quickly

Etymology

From Middle English 'loiteren', probably from Middle Dutch 'loteren' (to stagger, totter).
