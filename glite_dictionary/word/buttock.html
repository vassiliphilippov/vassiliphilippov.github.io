buttock

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌtək / (AmE), / ˈbʌtək / (BrE)
either of the two rounded fleshy parts at the back of the hips, forming the lower part of the human trunk
either of the rounded parts of the human rump

Examples

She slipped and landed on her buttock during the icy winter morning.
The physical therapist instructed him to tense his right buttock to help with his injury.
A bruise appeared on his left buttock after he bumped into the table.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: buttocks - both rounded parts at the back of the hips
  • Synonym: rump - the upper rounded part of the buttocks

Etymology

From Middle English 'buttok', likely of Germanic origin.
