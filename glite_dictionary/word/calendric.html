calendric

1. ADJ

/ kəˈlɛn.drɪk / (AmE), / kəˈlɛn.drɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a calendar or the measurement of time
relating to calendars or time measurement

Examples

The ancient Mayan civilization developed complex calendric systems.
Astronomers study calendric cycles to track celestial events.
The solstice has significant calendric importance in many cultures.

Synset Examples

calendric systems
solstice is a time of calendric importance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calendar - a chart or series of pages showing the days, weeks, and months of a particular year

Etymology

From 'calendar' by analogy with adjectives such as 'numeric', with '-ic' suffix.
