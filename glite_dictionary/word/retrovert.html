to retrovert

1. VERB

/ ˈɹɛtɹoʊˌvɝːt / (AmE), / ˈɹɛtɹəʊˌvɜːt / (BrE)
to return to a previous condition or state
return to an earlier state

Examples

After years of modernization, the town decided to retrovert to its original municipal structure.
The company chose to retrovert its policy in response to employee feedback.
Without clear direction, organizations can easily retrovert to outdated practices.

Synset Examples

We reverted to the old rules

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revert - to return to a former state or condition
  • Antonym: progress - to move forward or advance to a new state

Etymology

From Latin retrovertere (to turn back), from retro- (back) + vertere (to turn).
