langoustine

1. NOUN

/ læŋˈɡuːstiːn / (AmE), / læŋˈɡuːstiːn / (BrE)
A large edible prawn or shellfish resembling a small lobster, found in European waters and valued as seafood.
edible shellfish similar to a small lobster

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious pasta dish with fresh langoustine.
We ordered grilled langoustines at the seaside restaurant in France.
Langoustine is considered a delicacy in many Mediterranean countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobster - a large marine crustacean with a muscular tail and pincers

Etymology

From French 'langoustine', diminutive of 'langouste' (spiny lobster), ultimately from Latin 'locusta' (lobster, locust).
