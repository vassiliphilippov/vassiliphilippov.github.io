Mastigomycota

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæs.tɪɡ.oʊ.maɪˈkoʊ.tə / (AmE), / ˌmas.tɪ.ɡəʊ.maɪˈkəʊ.tə / (BrE)
a group of fungi characterized by motile spores and gametes; in some classification systems, formerly included in Phycomycetes with Zygomycota
group of fungi with motile spores and gametes
medical

Examples

Members of the Mastigomycota are distinguished by their motile spores.
Some older classification systems include Mastigomycota as a subdivision of Phycomycetes.
Researchers study Mastigomycota to understand fungal evolution and diversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zygomycota - a group of fungi forming sexual spores called zygospores

Etymology

From Greek *mastix* (whip) + *mycota* (fungi), referring to the whip-like flagella.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmæs.tɪ.ɡoʊˈmaɪ.kə.tə / (AmE), / ˌmæs.tɪ.ɡəʊˈmʌɪ.kə.tə / (BrE)
a former subdivision of fungi characterized by spores and gametes that are motile, sometimes classified with Zygomycota in the group Phycomycetes
group of fungi with motile spores and gametes

Examples

Members of the Mastigomycota typically have motile spores that help in their dispersal.
Some mycologists now include Mastigomycota species within other groups based on molecular evidence.
The Mastigomycota were once grouped with Zygomycota as Phycomycetes in older classification systems.

Etymology

From Greek mastix (whip, referring to flagella) + mycota (fungi).
