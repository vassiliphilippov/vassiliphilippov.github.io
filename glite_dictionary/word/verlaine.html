Verlaine

1. PROPN

/ vɛərˈleɪn / (AmE), / vɛəˈlɛn / (BrE)
Paul Verlaine (1844–1896), a French symbolist poet known for his evocative lyricism and influence on modern poetry.
French symbolist poet (1844–1896)

Examples

Many scholars consider Verlaine a master of musicality in French poetry.
The influence of Verlaine can be seen in subsequent French and European poets.
His collection 'Romances sans paroles' is among Verlaine's most acclaimed works.

Etymology

French surname, notably Paul Verlaine, 19th-century poet.
