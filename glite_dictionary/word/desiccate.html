desiccate

1. VERB

/ ˈdɛsɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛsɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to preserve something by removing all water and moisture from it, especially to prevent decay
remove moisture to preserve

Examples

When camping, many people desiccate fruits to make lightweight snacks.
Some microorganisms can desiccate their environment to survive in harsh conditions.
The company uses special machines to desiccate herbs before packaging them for sale.

Synset Examples

carry dehydrated food on your camping trip

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dehydrate - to remove water from, especially to preserve food

Etymology

From Latin desiccare, from de- (remove) + siccare (to dry).

2. VERB

/ ˈdɛsɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛsɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to lose moisture or become thoroughly dried out
lose moisture or become dried out

Examples

The hot sun will quickly desiccate the muddy paths after the rain.
Stored herbs may desiccate over time if not properly sealed.
If left uncovered, the soil can desiccate and become unsuitable for planting.

Synset Examples

In the desert, you get dehydrated very quickly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dehydrate - to remove water from; to lose water

Etymology

From Latin 'desiccare' (to dry up), from 'de-' (away) + 'siccare' (to dry).
