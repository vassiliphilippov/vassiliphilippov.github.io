patronne

1. NOUN

/ pæˈtroʊn, pəˈtrɒn / (AmE), / paˈtʁɔn, ˈpætrɒn / (BrE)
A woman who is a patron or manager, especially of a restaurant or business; the female equivalent of 'patron'.
female patron or manager

Examples

The patronne greeted each guest personally as they entered the bistro.
As the patronne of the hotel, she ensured the highest standards for her customers.
Everyone respected Madame Dupont, the patronne of the celebrated café.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patron - a person who gives financial or other support to a person, organization, cause, or activity

Etymology

From French 'patronne', feminine of 'patron'.
