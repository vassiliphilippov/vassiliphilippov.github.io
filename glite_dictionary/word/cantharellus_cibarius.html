Cantharellus cibarius

1. NOUN

/ ˌkænθəˈrɛləs sɪˈbɛəriəs / (AmE), / ˌkanθəˈrɛləs sɪˈbɑːriəs / (BrE)
A widely distributed, edible yellow mushroom with a smooth cap and a pleasant apricot-like aroma, also known as the chanterelle.
species of edible yellow mushroom (chanterelle)

Examples

Fresh Cantharellus cibarius mushrooms are highly prized by chefs for their unique flavor and aroma.
She found several Cantharellus cibarius growing in the forest after the rain.
Dishes featuring Cantharellus cibarius are popular in many European cuisines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chanterelle - common English name for Cantharellus cibarius, an edible yellow mushroom

Etymology

From Latin: Cantharellus (genus name), from Greek kantharos (drinking cup); cibarius meaning 'of food, edible'.
