to afflict

1. VERB

/ əˈflɪkt / (AmE), / əˈflɪkt / (BrE)
to cause pain, suffering, or distress to someone or something
to cause suffering or pain

Examples

Many communities have been afflicted by drought in recent years.
The disease afflicts thousands of people each year in the region.
She was afflicted with severe migraines that prevented her from working.

Synset Examples

afflict with the plague

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torment - to cause severe physical or mental suffering
  • Antonym: to relieve - to reduce or remove pain or distress

Etymology

From Latin affligere (to strike down, afflict).

2. VERB

/ əˈflɪkt / (AmE), / əˈflɪkt / (BrE)
to cause pain, suffering, or distress to someone or something
cause pain or distress

Examples

Chronic illness can afflict millions of people worldwide every year.
Many rural communities are often afflicted by poverty and lack of access to clean water.
A severe drought afflicted the region, leading to widespread crop failures.

Synset Examples

she was afflicted by the death of her parents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distress - to cause anxiety, sorrow, or pain
  • Antonym: comfort - to ease the pain or distress of

Etymology

From Latin afflictus, past participle of affligere (to strike down, damage).
