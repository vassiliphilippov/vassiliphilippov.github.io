footer

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊdɚ / (AmE), / ˈfʊtə / (BrE)
A printed note, line, or section placed at the bottom of each page, typically containing information such as page numbers, document title, or copyright details.
note or line at bottom of a page

Examples

Each page of the report includes a footer displaying the page number and document title.
Please make sure your company's address is on the footer of every letterhead.
He added the date to the footer of the presentation slides for reference.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: header - a printed note placed at the top of a printed page

Etymology

From 'foot' + '-er', referring to its position at the foot of the page.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtər / (AmE), / ˈfʊtə / (BrE)
(used only in combination) a measure indicating the height or length of something in feet; used especially in phrases such as 'six-footer' or '40-footer'.
length or height in feet (in combination)

Examples

He played basketball with a teammate who was a towering six-footer.
The museum just acquired a rare 50-footer for its collection of historic yachts.
Everyone cheered when she made the difficult 25-footer putt on the final hole.

Synset Examples

he is a six-footer
the golfer sank a 40-footer
his yacht is a 60-footer

Etymology

From 'foot' + the suffix '-er', commonly used in English to form terms indicating something measured in feet.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13671843-n
Synset POS: n
