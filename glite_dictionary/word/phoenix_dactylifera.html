Phoenix dactylifera

1. NOUN

/ fəˈniːks dækˈtɪlɪfərə / (AmE), / fəˈniːks dækˈtɪlɪfərə / (BrE)
a tall tropical palm tree native to the Middle East and North Africa, cultivated for its sweet edible fruit, the date
date palm tree species

Examples

The ancient Egyptians cultivated the Phoenix dactylifera for its nutritious fruit and shade.
Rows of Phoenix dactylifera flourish in the oasis, providing dates for the local market.
Botanists studied the resilience of Phoenix dactylifera in arid desert conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: date palm - common name for Phoenix dactylifera, the tree bearing edible dates

Etymology

From Latin, combining the genus 'Phoenix' and species 'dactylifera', meaning 'date-bearing' (Greek 'daktylos' = 'date').
