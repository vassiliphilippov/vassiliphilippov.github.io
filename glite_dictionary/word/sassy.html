sassy

1. ADJ

/ ˈsæsi / (AmE), / ˈsæsi / (BrE)
Bold, lively, and cheeky, often in a way that is considered rude or disrespectful.
cheeky or boldly disrespectful
informal

Examples

The child gave a sassy reply to her teacher, surprising the whole class.
She's known for her sassy sense of humor and quick comebacks.
Don't get sassy with me just because you think you know better.

Synset Examples

don't be fresh with me
impertinent of a child to lecture a grownup
an impudent boy given to insulting strangers
Don't get wise with me!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impudent - not showing due respect for another person
  • Synonym: impertinent - not showing proper respect; rude
  • Antonym: respectful - showing respect and politeness

Etymology

Probably from "sass", an alteration of "sauce" (impudence), plus "-y".
