Rhinoptera bonasus

1. NOUN

/ raɪˈnɒptərə bəˈneɪsəs / (AmE), / raɪˈnɒptərə bəˈneɪsəs / (BrE)
A species of large ray, known as the cownose ray, found along the eastern coast of North America.
species of large ray (cownose ray)

Examples

The Rhinoptera bonasus migrates in large groups along the Atlantic coast every spring.
Marine biologists have studied Rhinoptera bonasus to better understand their impact on oyster populations.
A diver spotted a school of Rhinoptera bonasus gliding gracefully through the shallow waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cownose ray - Common name for Rhinoptera bonasus, a large ray found in the western Atlantic Ocean

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus Rhinoptera from Greek rhis, rhin- ‘nose’ + pteron ‘wing’; species epithet bonasus of unknown origin.
