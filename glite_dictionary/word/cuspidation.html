cuspidation

1. NOUN

/ kʌs.pɪˈdeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / kʌs.pɪˈdeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a decorative form or ornamentation featuring cusps, especially in architecture or design
ornamentation with cusps

Examples

The Gothic cathedral's windows are notable for their elaborate cuspidation.
Medieval artisans often incorporated cuspidation into church arches and moldings.
The architect explained that the repeated cuspidation provided both form and symbolic meaning to the design.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cusp - a pointed end where two curves meet, used as an architectural term

Etymology

From Latin 'cuspis' (point) + English '-ation' (action or process).
