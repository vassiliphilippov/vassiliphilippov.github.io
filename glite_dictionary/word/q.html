Q

1. NOUN

/ kju / (AmE), / kjuː / (BrE)
the seventeenth letter of the English alphabet
the 17th letter of the alphabet

Examples

The word 'queen' begins with the letter Q.
Children practiced writing both uppercase and lowercase Q in their notebooks.
There is only one Q tile in a standard Scrabble set.

Etymology

From the Latin letter 'Q', derived from the Greek letter koppa and ultimately from Phoenician qoph.

2. NOUN

/ kju / (AmE), / kjuː / (BrE)
the 17th letter of the English alphabet
the 17th English letter

Examples

The word 'queen' begins with the letter Q.
On the eye chart, I had trouble reading the small q at the bottom.
She signed her name by first writing a large Q.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: letter - a character representing a speech sound in writing; part of an alphabet

Etymology

From the Latin letter Q, derived from the Greek letter koppa (Ϙ), itself from Phoenician qoph.
