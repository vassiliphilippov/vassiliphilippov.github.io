factotum

1. NOUN

/ fækˈtoʊtəm / (AmE), / fækˈtəʊtəm / (BrE)
a person employed to do all kinds of work, especially one who handles many different tasks for someone else
person employed to do varied tasks

Examples

As the office factotum, she was relied upon to solve any problem that arose.
He worked as a general factotum in the wealthy man's household, fixing and fetching as needed.
Hired as a factotum, his days were filled with everything from paperwork to running errands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handyman - a person skilled at a wide range of repairs, typically around the home

Etymology

From Latin 'fac totum', meaning 'do everything'.
