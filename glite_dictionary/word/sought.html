sought

1. ADJ

/ sɔt / (AmE), / sɔːt / (BrE)
that is searched for or desired; sought after
looked for; desired

Examples

The treasure was a much sought prize among adventurers.
Her expertise in the field made her a sought consultant.
Affordable housing in the area is highly sought by young families.

Synset Examples

the long sought relatives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desired - wanted or wished for
  • Degree Higher: elusive - difficult to find or catch

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'seek,' from Old English 'sēcan'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03166897-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ sɔt / (AmE), / sɔːt / (BrE)
greatly desired or searched for by many people; much in demand.
much desired or in demand

Examples

This is one of the most sought paintings in the entire collection.
Admission to the program is highly sought by students worldwide.
The chef’s unique dishes are sought after by food critics.

Synset Examples

the most sought-after item was the silver candelabrum

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sought-after - highly desired or in demand

Etymology

Past participle of 'seek,' from Old English 'sēcan'.
