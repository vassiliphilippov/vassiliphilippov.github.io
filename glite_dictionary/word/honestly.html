honestly

1. ADV

/ ˈɑːnɪstli / (AmE), / ˈɒnɪstli / (BrE)
In an honest manner; in a way that is truthful and sincere.
in an honest manner

Examples

He answered all the questions honestly during the interview.
If you do your job honestly, people will respect you.
She admitted honestly that she had made a mistake.

Synset Examples

if he can't get it honestly, he is willing to steal it
was known for dealing aboveboard in everything

Related Terms

  • Synonym: truthfully - in a truthful manner
  • Antonym: dishonestly - in a dishonest manner

Etymology

Formed from 'honest' + '-ly', from Old French 'honeste' and Latin 'honestus' (honourable).

2. ADV

/ ˈɑː.nɪst.li / (AmE), / ˈɒn.ɪst.li / (BrE)
Used to emphasize that what you are saying is true, sincere, or reflects your real opinion.
sincerely; truly (intensifier)

Examples

Honestly, I have no idea how they managed to finish so quickly.
Honestly, you should have told me about this sooner.
Honestly, I don't think I've ever been more surprised in my life.

Synset Examples

honestly, I don't believe it
candidly, I think she doesn't have a conscience
frankly, my dear, I don't give a damn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frankly - used to emphasize truthfulness or directness in speech
  • Synonym: candidly - in an honest and straightforward way

Etymology

From honest + -ly, from Old French 'honeste' from Latin 'honestus' (honorable).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00316228-r
Synset POS: r
