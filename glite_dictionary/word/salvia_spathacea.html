Salvia spathacea

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæl.vi.ə spæˈθeɪ.si.ə / (AmE), / ˈsæl.vɪ.ə spæˈθeɪ.si.ə / (BrE)
A species of sage native to California, characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and fragrant, magenta flowers.
a sage species native to California

Examples

Salvia spathacea thrives in coastal California and is valued for its fragrant leaves and vibrant flowers.
Gardeners plant Salvia spathacea to attract hummingbirds and pollinators to their yards.
The drought-tolerant Salvia spathacea is a popular choice for native California gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sage - any plant of the genus Salvia, esp. S. officinalis, whose aromatic leaves are used in cooking and medicine

Etymology

From Latin: 'salvia' (sage) and 'spathacea' (with a spathe, referencing its bract).
