hither

1. ADV

/ ˈhɪðɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɪðə / (BrE)
to this place; toward the speaker (now chiefly archaic or literary)
to this place; toward the speaker (archaic)
archaic

Examples

The knight rode hither at the king's command.
Many travelers journeyed hither seeking fortune.
She called her dog hither with a whistle.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: thither - to that place; in that direction (archaic)
  • Synonym: here - in, at, or to this place or position

Etymology

Old English hider, of Germanic origin, related to Dutch her and German hierher.
