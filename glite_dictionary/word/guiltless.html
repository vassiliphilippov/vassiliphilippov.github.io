guiltless

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡɪltləs / (AmE), / ˈɡɪltləs / (BrE)
Not responsible for doing something wrong; free from blame or wrongdoing.
without guilt or blame

Examples

She was found guiltless of all charges after the trial.
The guiltless child looked up at the adults with wide, trusting eyes.
Despite the rumors, he maintained a guiltless conscience throughout the ordeal.

Synset Examples

an innocent child
the principle that one is innocent until proved guilty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innocent - not guilty of a crime or offense
  • Antonym: culpable - deserving blame

Etymology

Formed from "guilt" + "-less" (meaning without).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡɪltləs / (AmE), / ˈɡɪltlɪs / (BrE)
not responsible for any wrongdoing; free from guilt or blame.
not responsible for any wrongdoing

Examples

Despite the accusations, he insisted he was completely guiltless.
After the investigation, she was found to be guiltless of any involvement.
He looked at her with a guiltless expression, certain of his innocence.

Synset Examples

They maintained a guiltless demeanor throughout the trial.
She approached the situation with a guiltless heart.
He claimed to be guiltless in the matter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innocent - not guilty of a crime or offense
  • Synonym: blameless - not deserving blame; free from fault or guilt
  • Antonym: guilty - responsible for a specified wrongdoing

Etymology

From 'guilt' + '-less', an Old English suffix meaning 'without'.
