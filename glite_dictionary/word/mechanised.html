mechanised

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɛkəˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈmɛk.ə.naɪzd / (BrE)
(of an activity, process, or force) operated by or equipped with machines or vehicles, especially in contrast to being done manually or with animal power.
operated by machines or vehicles
british

Examples

The mechanised infantry advanced quickly across the open terrain.
With the introduction of mechanised farming, agricultural productivity increased dramatically.
During the war, mechanised divisions replaced horse-drawn artillery.

Synset Examples

motorized warfare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motorized - operated by a motor or engine, especially vehicles or troops

Etymology

From mechanise + -ed; mechanise from French mécaniser, itself from mécanique (mechanical).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɛkəˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈmɛkənaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a process, system, or place) equipped with or operated by machinery, especially to replace human or animal labor.
operated with machinery

Examples

After the farm became mechanised, productivity increased dramatically.
The company invested in a mechanised production line to speed up manufacturing.
Traditional weaving was gradually replaced by mechanised looms during the Industrial Revolution.

Synset Examples

a mechanized factory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: automated - operated by largely automatic equipment
  • Antonym: manual - operated by hand, not by machinery

Etymology

From 'mechanise' + -ed; 'mechanise' from Greek mēkhanē (machine) via French and Latin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01504234-s
Synset POS: s
