oestradiol

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛstrəˈdaɪɒl / (AmE), / ˌiːstrəˈdaɪɒl / (BrE)
the most potent naturally occurring estrogen hormone, used medically to treat estrogen deficiency and breast cancer
powerful naturally occurring estrogen hormone
medical british

Examples

The doctor prescribed oestradiol patches to help manage her menopausal symptoms.
Blood tests showed low levels of oestradiol in the patient.
Treatment with oestradiol is sometimes recommended after surgical removal of the ovaries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estradiol - the US spelling for the same hormone
  • Degree Higher: oestrogen - general term for female sex hormones including oestradiol

Etymology

From Greek oistros (frenzy, inspiration), combined with -adi- (referring to the estrane steroid nucleus) and -ol (alcohol suffix).
