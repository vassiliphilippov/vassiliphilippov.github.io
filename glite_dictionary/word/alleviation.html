alleviation

1. NOUN

/ əˌliːviˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌliːvɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something, especially pain or suffering, less severe or more bearable
the act of making pain or suffering less severe

Examples

The new medication provided significant alleviation of his chronic back pain.
Efforts were made to ensure the alleviation of suffering after the disaster.
She donated to several charities committed to the alleviation of poverty.

Synset Examples

he asked the nurse for relief from the constant pain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relief - the removal or reduction of something painful or unpleasant
  • Antonym: exacerbation - the act of making something worse, especially a disease or problem

Etymology

From Latin 'alleviatio', from 'alleviare' meaning 'to lighten, relieve'.

2. NOUN

/ əˌliːviˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌliːviˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
a feeling of relief experienced when something burdensome is removed or reduced
relief after something burdensome is eased

Examples

After the diagnosis, Emily felt immense alleviation from her months of uncertainty.
The new medication brought a sense of alleviation he desperately needed.
There was noticeable alleviation of tension in the room once the issue was resolved.

Synset Examples

as he heard the news he was suddenly flooded with relief

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relief - a feeling of reassurance and comfort after distress has been alleviated

Etymology

From Latin 'alleviare' (to lighten), via Medieval Latin 'alleviatio'.

Source: WordNet
