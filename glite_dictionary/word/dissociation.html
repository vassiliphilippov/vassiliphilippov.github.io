dissociation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪˌsoʊsiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪˌsəʊsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of separating or removing something from association or connection with something else.
act of removing from association

Examples

The dissociation of the company from its former partner was announced publicly last week.
There was a clear dissociation between his opinions and those of the group.
The new evidence led to a dissociation of the artist's name from the controversial movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: separation - the action or state of moving or being moved apart
  • Antonym: association - the act of joining or being joined together

Etymology

From Latin dissociatio (‘separation’), from dissociare (‘to disunite’).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsoʊsiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəʊsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Chemistry) the process in which a molecule or ion separates into smaller molecules, atoms, or ions, usually reversibly.
chemical separation into smaller parts
medical

Examples

The dissociation of sodium chloride in water results in separate sodium and chloride ions.
At higher temperatures, the dissociation of ammonia increases significantly.
Acid strength depends partly on the degree of dissociation in solution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ionization - the process in which an atom or molecule acquires a negative or positive charge by gaining or losing electrons
  • Antonym: association - the process in which molecules or ions combine to form larger units

Etymology

From Latin dissociatio (separation), from dissociare (to separate).

3. NOUN

/ ˌdɪˌsoʊsiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌsəʊsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
a psychological condition in which part of a person's mind becomes separated from the rest, often functioning independently, as seen in disorders such as dissociative identity disorder
psychological separation of mental processes
medical

Examples

In cases of severe trauma, dissociation may help a person cope by separating painful memories from conscious awareness.
The therapist explained that dissociation can interrupt a person's sense of identity and memory.
She experienced episodes of dissociation when under extreme stress, feeling detached from her body and surroundings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: integration - combining parts into a whole, as in a unified personality

Etymology

From Latin dissociatio (a separation), from dis- (apart) + sociare (to join).
