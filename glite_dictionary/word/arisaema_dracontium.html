Arisaema dracontium

1. NOUN

/ ˌærɪˈsiːmə drəˈkɒntiəm / (AmE), / ˌærɪˈsiːmə drəˈkɒntiəm / (BrE)
A plant species native to eastern North America, resembling jack-in-the-pulpit, with digitate leaves, a slender greenish yellow spathe, and an elongated spadix; also known as the green dragon.
North American plant, also called green dragon

Examples

The Arisaema dracontium blooms in early spring along wooded streams in eastern North America.
Gardeners prize Arisaema dracontium for its unique spathe and distinctive foliage.
Unlike its cousin jack-in-the-pulpit, Arisaema dracontium has a slender, elongated spadix.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jack-in-the-pulpit - A related North American plant, Arisaema triphyllum, with similar appearance

Etymology

From the genus name Arisaema (from Greek 'arum' and 'haima' = blood) and species name dracontium (from Greek 'drakōntion', meaning 'dragonwort').
