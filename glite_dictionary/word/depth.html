depth

1. NOUN

/ dɛpθ / (AmE), / dɛpθ / (BrE)
the quality of having serious thought or profound understanding; the ability to think in a complex and insightful way
seriousness or profundity of thought

Examples

Her depth of understanding impressed the entire panel of judges.
The novel is praised for the depth of its philosophical insights.
Despite his youth, he demonstrated remarkable depth in his analysis of complex issues.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: superficiality - lack of thoroughness or depth in thought

Etymology

From Old English 'dēop' (deep) with suffix '-th' forming abstract nouns.

2. NOUN

/ dɛpθ / (AmE), / dɛpθ / (BrE)
the quality of having intellectual or emotional profundity or complexity
intellectual or emotional profundity

Examples

Her writing shows remarkable depth and insight into human nature.
The philosopher was admired for the depth of his understanding.
There is little depth to his arguments, making them easy to challenge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profundity - the quality of being profound; intellectual depth
  • Antonym: shallowness - lack of intellectual or emotional depth

Etymology

From Middle English 'deppe', from Old English 'dēop' (deep), with the suffix '-th' forming abstract nouns.

3. NOUN

/ dɛpθ / (AmE), / dɛpθ / (BrE)
(usually plural) a very low or extreme state of morality, emotion, or condition; used to describe the worst level of something, especially bad behavior or misery.
lowest state, morally or emotionally

Examples

The politician's corruption had reached the depths of moral depravity.
She found herself in the depths of despair after the loss of her job.
Many believed he could never recover from the depths to which he had fallen.

Synset Examples

he had sunk to the depths of addiction

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pinnacle - the highest or most successful point

Etymology

From Middle English 'depe', from Old English 'dēop' (deep) + '-th' (noun suffix).

4. NOUN

/ dɛpθ / (AmE), / dɛpθ / (BrE)
The distance from the top or surface to the bottom of something, or the extent inward or backward from an edge.
distance from surface to bottom

Examples

Divers measured the depth of the lake before entering the water.
Please check the depth of the shelf to make sure the boxes will fit.
The closet's depth allows you to store bulky items easily.

Synset Examples

the depth of the water
depth of a shelf
depth of a closet

Related Terms

  • Antonym: height - the measurement from base to top; the opposite dimension to depth
  • Synonym: width - the measurement or extent of something from side to side

Etymology

From Old English *dēop* ('deep') + -th (noun-forming suffix).
