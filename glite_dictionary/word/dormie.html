dormie

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɔr.mi / (AmE), / ˈdɔː.mi / (BrE)
(in golf) having a lead in match play equal to the number of holes remaining, so that at worst a tie is guaranteed.
in golf, guaranteed at least a tie in match play

Examples

She finished the sixteenth hole dormie two, needing only to avoid losing the last two holes to win.
After his birdie on the fifteenth, he was dormie three in the championship match.
If a player is dormie, their worst possible outcome is a tie if they lose all remaining holes.

Synset Examples

he was dormie three and still lost the match

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, possibly from Scots 'dormy', related to French 'dormir' (to sleep), suggesting the match is 'put to sleep'.
