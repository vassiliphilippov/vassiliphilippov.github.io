potent

1. ADJ

/ ˈpoʊtənt / (AmE), / ˈpəʊtənt / (BrE)
having a strong or powerful effect, especially on the body or mind; highly effective or influential, especially in producing a chemical or physiological response
having a strong effect

Examples

This medication is extremely potent and should be used with caution.
The scientist warned that the substance was a potent chemical hazard.
She brewed a potent herbal tea to help him recover.

Synset Examples

a potent toxin
potent liquor
a potent cup of tea
a stiff drink

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powerful - having great power or strength
  • Antonym: weak - lacking strength or intensity

Etymology

From Latin 'potens', present participle of 'posse' (to be able, to have power).
