marine

1. ADJ

/ məˈrin / (AmE), / məˈriːn / (BrE)
of or relating to military forces trained to serve on both land and sea, especially the United States Marine Corps.
relating to military/naval forces (esp. U.S. Marines)

Examples

The soldiers stationed at the marine barracks trained daily for amphibious operations.
A marine officer led the expedition from the ship to shore.
The unit was known for its strict adherence to marine protocol.

Synset Examples

marine barracks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naval - relating to a navy or warships

Etymology

From Latin marinus (of the sea), later applied to naval military forces.

/ məˈrin / (AmE), / məˈriːn / (BrE)

native to or living in the sea

Examples

Coral reefs are important marine ecosystems that support diverse species.
Researchers monitor marine pollution to protect aquatic life.

3. NOUN

/ məˈrin / (AmE), / məˈriːn / (BrE)
a member of a military force trained to serve both on land and at sea, especially as part of a navy
naval infantry soldier

Examples

The marine was trained to handle both amphibious and land operations.
Every marine takes pride in completing the rigorous boot camp.
Dozens of marines boarded the ship for their next mission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sailor - a person who works on a ship as part of the crew
  • Synonym: soldier - a person who serves in an army

Etymology

From Latin 'marinus' (of the sea), via French 'marin'.

Source: WordNet
