Calvinistical

1. ADJ

/ ˌkælvɨˈnɪstɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌkælvɪˈnɪstɪkəl / (BrE)
Relating to Calvinism or its followers; characteristic of the doctrines or beliefs of Calvinism.
relating to Calvinism or its adherents

Examples

The debate took a distinctly Calvinistical tone, emphasizing predestination and divine sovereignty.
His Calvinistical upbringing influenced his views on morality and personal responsibility.
Many early settlers in New England carried strong Calvinistical convictions into their communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Calvinist - an adherent of Calvinism

Etymology

From Calvinist + -ical, ultimately from the name of the theologian John Calvin.
