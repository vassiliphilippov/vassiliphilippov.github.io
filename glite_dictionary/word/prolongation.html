prolongation

1. NOUN

/ prəˌlɔŋˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / prəˌlɒŋˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something last longer or extending its duration
the act of extending something's duration

Examples

The prolongation of the meeting made everyone late for their next appointment.
Doctors were concerned about the unnecessary prolongation of the patient's suffering.
Negotiators agreed to a prolongation of the ceasefire to allow more time for discussions.

Synset Examples

there was an indefinite prolongation of the peace talks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extension - the act of extending or lengthening something
  • Antonym: shortening - the act of making something shorter in time or length

Etymology

From Latin prolongatio, from prolongare 'to prolong'.

2. NOUN

/ proʊˌlɔŋˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / prəˌlɒŋˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or result of making something last longer in time; an extension of duration
extension of duration

Examples

The prolongation of the meeting caused many attendees to leave early.
Doctors are researching the prolongation of human life through advanced medicine.
The film's unnecessary prolongation made it less enjoyable for the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extension - an increase in length or time
  • Antonym: shortening - the act of making something shorter

Etymology

From Latin 'prolongatio', from 'prolongare' (to prolong).
