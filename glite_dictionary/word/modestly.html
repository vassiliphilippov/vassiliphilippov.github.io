modestly

1. ADV

/ ˈmɑːdɪstli / (AmE), / ˈmɒdɪstli / (BrE)
in a way that shows modesty; in a modest manner, without boasting or excessive pride
in a modest manner

Examples

She spoke modestly about her achievements during the interview.
He lives modestly, despite having a significant fortune.
The restaurant is modestly decorated but serves excellent food.

Synset Examples

the dissertation was entitled, modestly, ‘Remarks about a play by Shakespeare’

Related Terms

  • Antonym: proudly - in a proud manner

Etymology

From 'modest' + '-ly', where 'modest' is from Latin 'modestus' (keeping due measure).
