stew

1. NOUN

/ stuː / (AmE), / stjuː / (BrE)
a state of agitation, excitement, or worry, especially when someone is anxious or upset about something
state of agitation or worry

Examples

Try not to get yourself into a stew over things you can't control.
She was in a complete stew after hearing the unexpected news.
I could tell by his fidgeting that he was in quite a stew about the interview.

Synset Examples

don't get in a stew
he's in a sweat about exams

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panic - sudden uncontrollable fear or anxiety
  • Antonym: calm - state of being free from agitation or worry

2. NOUN

/ stuː / (AmE), / stjuː / (BrE)
A dish consisting of meat or fish and vegetables cooked slowly in liquid.
dish of meat or fish cooked with vegetables

Examples

She served a delicious beef stew for dinner.
The aroma of the bubbling stew filled the kitchen.
On cold days, nothing is better than a hot stew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: casserole - a dish in which food is baked and served, often referring to a similar type of stewed or baked meal
  • Degree Lower: soup - a liquid food made by boiling meat, fish, or vegetables

Etymology

From Old French *estuver* (to bathe, stew), from Vulgar Latin *extufare* (to evaporate, raise steam).

3. VERB

/ stuː / (AmE), / stjuː / (BrE)
to remain silently angry or upset about something, often by thinking about it for a long time
remain upset or resentful

Examples

She continued to stew over his rude comment long after the conversation had ended.
If you keep stewing about the mistake, you’ll never move forward.
He stewed in silence, refusing to talk to anyone for hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brood - to think deeply about something that makes you unhappy, angry, or worried

4. VERB

/ stuː / (AmE), / stjuː / (BrE)
to cook food slowly in liquid over low heat for a long time
cook food slowly in liquid

Examples

You can stew the beef with onions and carrots for extra flavor.
She let the apples stew gently until they became soft and sweet.
The chef prefers to stew the vegetables slowly rather than boiling them.

Synset Examples

Stew the vegetables in wine

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: boil - to cook food in water or liquid at 100°C
  • Degree Lower: simmer - to cook food gently just below boiling point

Etymology

From Old French *estuver* (to bathe in heated air), later used for cooking in liquid.

/ stuː / (AmE), / stjuː / (BrE)
To remain silently angry or upset, often letting negative feelings build up.
to be silently angry or upset

Examples

After the argument, he would just stew in his room for hours, refusing to speak to anyone.
She tends to stew over minor mistakes instead of moving on quickly.
Don’t stew all day—talk to me about what's bothering you.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brood - to think deeply about something that makes one unhappy
  • Synonym: fume - to be visibly angry or irritated
