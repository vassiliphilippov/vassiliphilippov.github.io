sept

1. NOUN

/ sɛpt / (AmE), / sɛpt / (BrE)
a division of a family, especially in Ireland or Scotland, consisting of people descended from a common ancestor
division of a family clan

Examples

Her family belongs to the O'Brien sept of County Clare.
The ancient septs of Ireland played significant roles in history.
Many Scottish clans are divided into distinct septs with different surnames.

Synset Examples

his family has lived in Massachusetts since the Mayflower

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clan - a group of families with a common ancestor, especially in Scotland
  • Synonym: tribe - a social group made up of families or clans

Etymology

Probably from Latin 'saeptum' (enclosure), influenced by Old Irish 'sliocht' (seed, offspring).
