to descale

1. VERB

/ diˈskeɪl / (AmE), / diːˈskeɪl / (BrE)
to remove the scales from something, especially a fish or a surface
remove scales from (fish or surface)

Examples

Before cooking, you need to descale the fish thoroughly to remove all the outer skin.
It's important to descale your kettle regularly to maintain its efficiency.
He used a sharp knife to descale the freshly caught trout.

Synset Examples

scale fish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scale - to remove the scales from (a fish)

Etymology

From de- (removal) + scale (the covering on fish or surfaces).
