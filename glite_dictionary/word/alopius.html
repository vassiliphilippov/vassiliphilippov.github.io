Alopius

1. NOUN

/ əˈloʊpiəs / (AmE), / əˈləʊpɪəs / (BrE)
A genus of sharks in the family Alopiidae, commonly known as thresher sharks.
genus of thresher sharks

Examples

The Alopius genus is best known for its members' unique long tails.
Many researchers study Alopius for insights into shark evolution.
The Alopius species are distributed across tropical and temperate oceans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Alopiidae - the shark family that includes thresher sharks

Etymology

From Greek alōpēx (fox), due to the fox-like appearance of the shark's tail.

2. NOUN

/ əˈloʊpiəs / (AmE), / əˈləʊpiəs / (BrE)
A genus of sharks that is the type genus of the family Alopiidae; in some classifications, considered a genus of the family Lamnidae.
genus of sharks in Alopiidae family

Examples

The Alopius genus is commonly known as the thresher shark.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Alopius genus.
The long tail of the Alopius species is used to stun prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thresher shark - a large shark of the genus Alopius, known for its long tail

Etymology

From Greek alōpēx (fox), referencing the common name 'thresher shark' or 'fox shark'.
