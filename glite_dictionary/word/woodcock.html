woodcock

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdˌkɑk / (AmE), / ˈwʊdkɒk / (BrE)
A game bird of the sandpiper family, with a long bill, short legs, and brownish mottled plumage, often found in woodland areas.
a species of game bird

Examples

The hunter spotted a woodcock moving quietly through the undergrowth at dawn.
Many birdwatchers visit the forest in spring to catch a glimpse of the elusive woodcock.
Roasted woodcock is considered a delicacy in some European cuisines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snipe - a wading bird with a long straight bill, related to the woodcock

Etymology

From Old English wudu-coc, meaning 'wood cock' (cock: bird), referring to its woodland habitat.
