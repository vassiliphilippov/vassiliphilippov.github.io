Dacrydium cupressinum

1. NOUN

/ ˌdækrɪˈdiːəm ˌkjuːprəˈsɪnəm / (AmE), / ˌdækrɪˈdiːəm ˌkjuːprəˈsɪnəm / (BrE)
A tall evergreen conifer native to New Zealand, known as rimu, valued for its timber.
a tall New Zealand conifer tree (rimu)

Examples

The wood of Dacrydium cupressinum is prized for its durability and attractive reddish hue.
Many old forests in New Zealand are dominated by Dacrydium cupressinum.
Conservationists are working to protect Dacrydium cupressinum from logging and habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rimu - common name for Dacrydium cupressinum, New Zealand timber tree

Etymology

From Greek 'dakryon' (tear, referencing resin) and Latin 'cupressus' (cypress), denoting its resemblance to cypress trees.
