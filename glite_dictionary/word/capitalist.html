capitalist

1. ADJ

/ ˈkæpɪtəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈkapɪtəlɪst / (BrE)
favoring or supporting capitalism, or relating to the people or systems that practice capitalism
favoring or related to capitalism

Examples

The government introduced more capitalist policies to encourage private investment.
She argued that the capitalist approach to healthcare leads to inequality.
Some critics believe that a capitalist society prioritizes profit over people.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: socialist - favoring or practicing socialism

Etymology

From capital + -ist; 'capital' from Latin 'capitalis' (of the head), 'caput' (head).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00298269-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæpɪtəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈkapɪtəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who invests money in a business, especially a large business, in order to make a profit
person who invests money in a business

Examples

The capitalist invested millions to expand the technology company's operations.
Many early American railroads were funded by wealthy capitalists seeking large returns.
As a venture capitalist, she looks for startups with high growth potential.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: investor - someone who puts money into financial plans, property, etc. for profit

Etymology

From capital + -ist, after French capitaliste (early 19th century).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09632262-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈkæp.ɪ.t̬ə.lɪst / (AmE), / ˈkæp.ɪ.təl.ɪst / (BrE)
relating to or supporting capitalism or capitalists; characteristic of a capitalist system or its advocates.
relating to capitalism or capitalists

Examples

The country adopted a capitalist approach to economic reform in the 1990s.
Many believe that capitalist policies encourage entrepreneurship and innovation.
A capitalist society values individual profit and private ownership.

Synset Examples

a capitalist nation
capitalistic methods and incentives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capitalistic - relating to or characteristic of capitalism
  • Antonym: socialist - relating to or supporting socialism or socialists

Etymology

From Latin 'capitale' (principal, capital sum), via French 'capitaliste'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03011618-a
Synset POS: a

4. NOUN

/ ˈkæpɪtəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈkapɪtəlɪst / (BrE)
A person who supports or advocates capitalism, especially a conservative who believes in private ownership and free markets.
person who advocates capitalism

Examples

The new policy was championed by a prominent capitalist who opposed government intervention.
As a self-described capitalist, she invests heavily in private enterprises.
Many critics argue that a capitalist system leads to greater income inequality.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: socialist - a person who advocates socialism

Etymology

From capital + -ist, patterned after French capitaliste.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09911714-n
Synset POS: n
