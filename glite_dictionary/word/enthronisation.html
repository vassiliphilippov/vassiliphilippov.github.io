enthronisation

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˌθroʊnɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌθrəʊnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the formal ceremony of installing a new monarch on the throne
ceremony of installing a monarch

Examples

The enthronisation of the new king was attended by leaders from across the region.
A lavish enthronisation is traditional in many European monarchies.
Many citizens gathered to witness the enthronisation in the ancient cathedral.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coronation - the act or ceremony of crowning a monarch

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'enthronizatio'; formed from 'en-' (in) + 'throne'.
