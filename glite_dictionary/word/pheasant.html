pheasant

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛzənt / (AmE), / ˈfɛzənt / (BrE)
The flesh of a pheasant, used as food, typically braised or roasted.
meat of a pheasant (used as food)

Examples

We had roasted pheasant with wild mushrooms for dinner last night.
The chef recommended braising the pheasant to keep the meat tender and flavorful.
In autumn, many restaurants offer dishes featuring local pheasant as a seasonal delicacy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partridge - meat of a partridge (used as food)
  • Synonym: chicken - meat of a chicken (used as food)
  • Synonym: pheasant - large long-tailed game bird

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɛzənt / (AmE), / ˈfɛznt / (BrE)
a large bird with a long tail, native to Asia and other parts of the Old World, often introduced elsewhere and hunted for sport or food
large long-tailed game bird

Examples

A pheasant flew out of the bushes as we walked through the field.
He roasted a wild pheasant for the family dinner.
We spotted several brightly colored pheasants near the edge of the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partridge - a short-tailed game bird related to the pheasant
  • Degree Lower: quail - a small, short-tailed game bird

Etymology

From Old French 'faisan', via Latin 'phasiana', from Greek 'phasianos (ornis)' (bird of the Phasis River, in Colchis, now in Georgia).
