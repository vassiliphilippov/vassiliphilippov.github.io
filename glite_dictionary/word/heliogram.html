heliogram

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːliəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈhiːliəˌɡram / (BrE)
a message sent by reflecting sunlight, usually with a mirror, as a means of communication
message sent using reflected sunlight

Examples

The explorers used a heliogram to signal their location to the base camp.
In the 19th century, sending a heliogram was an effective way to transmit messages over long distances.
A single flash of a heliogram could be seen from miles away on a clear day.

Etymology

From Greek hēlios (sun) + -gram (something written or recorded).
