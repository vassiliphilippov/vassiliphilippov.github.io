no-account

1. ADJ

/ ˈnoʊ əˌkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈnəʊ əˌkaʊnt / (BrE)
Having little or no value, importance, or merit; worthless.
of little worth or value

Examples

She dismissed him as a no-account drifter who would never amount to anything.
The old shed was just a no-account structure ready to fall apart.
He regretted wasting time on such a no-account idea.

Synset Examples

a sorry horse
a sorry excuse
a lazy no-count, good-for-nothing goldbrick
the car was a no-good piece of junk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worthless - having no value or usefulness
  • Antonym: valuable - having great value or usefulness

Etymology

From 'no' + 'account,' meaning without worth or reckoning (19th century American English, originally colloquial).

2. ADJ

/ ˈnoʊ əˌkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈnəʊ əˌkaʊnt / (BrE)
(of a person) regarded as worthless or insignificant.
worthless or insignificant person
informal american

Examples

Despite his promises, her brother was always a no-account who never kept a job for long.
She decided she deserved better than a no-account boyfriend who brought her only trouble.
In the movie, the villain is portrayed as a no-account drifter who causes chaos wherever he goes.

Synset Examples

She broke up with him because he was no-account.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: good-for-nothing - a person regarded as lazy or useless
  • Antonym: somebody - a person of importance or value
