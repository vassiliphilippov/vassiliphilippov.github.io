mayhaw

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪˌhɔ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪˌhɔː / (BrE)
A small tree of the southern United States (a type of hawthorn), producing a juicy, acidic scarlet fruit that is often used to make jellies or preserves.
southern US hawthorn tree with edible fruit

Examples

The mayhaw trees are in full bloom this spring, promising a good harvest for jelly-making.
Locals gather fresh mayhaws from the riverbanks to make traditional preserves.
She spread mayhaw jelly on her toast, savoring its tart, fruity flavor.

Etymology

From 'may' (the month when it ripens) + 'haw' (from Old English *hage*, meaning hawthorn berry).
