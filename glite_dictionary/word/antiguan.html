Antiguan

1. ADJ

/ ænˈtiːɡwən / (AmE), / ænˈtɪɡjuːən / (BrE)
of or relating to Antigua or its people.
relating to Antigua or its people

Examples

She loved the Antiguan landscape, with its beautiful beaches and palm trees.
The Antiguan community in New York celebrates its national holidays with great enthusiasm.
He prepared a traditional Antiguan dish for the festival.

Synset Examples

Antiguan islands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Antigua - a Caribbean island nation

Etymology

From Antigua (the Caribbean island) + -an (forming adjectives and demonyms).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03055181-a

2. NOUN

/ ænˈtiɡwən / (AmE), / ænˈtiːɡjʊən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Antigua, an island in the Caribbean
native or inhabitant of Antigua

Examples

The famous cricketer is an Antiguan who has represented his country internationally.
Many Antiguans celebrate Carnival with colorful parades and dancing every year.
As an Antiguan, she takes pride in her island's rich cultural heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Barbudan - native or inhabitant of Barbuda, Antigua's neighboring island

Etymology

From Antigua, the name of the island, plus the English suffix -an indicating origin or nationality.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09710420-n
Synset POS: n
