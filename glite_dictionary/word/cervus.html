Cervus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜrvəs / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvəs / (BrE)
A genus of deer in the family Cervidae, including species such as the red deer and sika deer.
genus of deer in Cervidae

Examples

The red deer, belonging to the genus Cervus, is widespread across Europe and Asia.
Biologists recently discovered a new species within the Cervus genus.
Many species classified under Cervus display large, branching antlers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deer - hoofed grazing animal of the family Cervidae

Etymology

From Latin 'cervus' meaning 'stag, deer'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɜr.vəs / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.vəs / (BrE)
A genus of large deer that includes species such as the red deer and sika deer, forming the type genus of the family Cervidae.
genus of large deer

Examples

Scientists classified the red deer under the genus Cervus due to its distinctive antler structure.
Several important deer species, such as the sika deer, belong to the genus Cervus.
The distribution of the genus Cervus spans across Europe and Asia.

Etymology

From Latin *cervus* meaning 'stag' or 'deer'.
