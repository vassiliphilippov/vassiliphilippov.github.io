twenty-four

1. NUM

/ ˌtwɛntiˈfɔr / (AmE), / ˌtwentiˈfɔː / (BrE)
The number 24; the cardinal number that is the sum of 23 and 1.
the number 24

Examples

We need twenty-four chairs for the conference room.
She finished the puzzle in twenty-four minutes flat.
There are twenty-four hours in a day.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: twenty-three - the number 23; one less than twenty-four
  • Degree Higher: twenty-five - the number 25; one more than twenty-four

2. ADJ

/ ˌtwɛntiˈfɔr / (AmE), / ˌtwɛntiˈfɔː / (BrE)
equivalent to the number twenty-four; being four more than twenty
the number 24; four more than twenty

Examples

He bought twenty-four roses for her birthday.
There are twenty-four students in the class this year.
The clock shows twenty-four hours in a full day.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: twenty-three - the number 23; one less than twenty-four
  • Degree Higher: twenty-five - the number 25; one more than twenty-four

Etymology

Formed from 'twenty' plus 'four'; 'twenty' from Old English 'twēntig', 'four' from Old English 'feower'.
