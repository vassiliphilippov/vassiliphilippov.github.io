grumble

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌmbəl / (BrE)
a continuous low, dull sound, often caused by thunder or distant movement
a low, dull, continuous sound

Examples

We sat in silence, listening to the distant grumble of a passing train.
The grumble of thunder warned us that a storm was approaching.
A low grumble echoed through the old, empty building.

Synset Examples

they heard the rumbling of thunder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rumble - a deep, heavy, continuous sound

Etymology

From Middle English grumbelen, an imitative formation suggesting a low rumbling sound.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07407083-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡrʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌmbəl / (BrE)
a low, indistinct complaint or expression of discontent
a muted complaint

Examples

His only grumble was about the early start to the meeting.
She let out a soft grumble as she picked up the heavy box.
Despite a few grumbles, everyone agreed the trip was a success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complaint - the expression of dissatisfaction or annoyance
  • Antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration

Etymology

Probably from Middle English grumbelen, an imitative formation related to 'grumble' as a verb.
