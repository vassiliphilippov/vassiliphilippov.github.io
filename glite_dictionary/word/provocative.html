provocative

1. ADJ

/ prəˈvɑːkətɪv / (AmE), / prəˈvɒkətɪv / (BrE)
intended or likely to arouse sexual desire or interest, especially by being sexually suggestive or alluring.
intended to arouse sexual desire

Examples

The advertisement featured a provocative image that sparked controversy for being too suggestive.
She wore a provocative dress to the party, catching everyone's attention.
His comments about her provocative behavior made her uncomfortable.

Synset Examples

her gestures and postures became more wanton and provocative

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alluring - powerfully attractive or tempting; seductive
  • Antonym: modest - not intended to attract attention, especially of a sexual nature

Etymology

From Latin provocativus, from provocare (to call forth, challenge).

2. ADJ

/ prəˈvɑkətɪv / (AmE), / prəˈvɒkətɪv / (BrE)
Causing annoyance, anger, or another strong reaction; stimulating thought, discussion, or excitement.
causing strong reaction or stimulating discussion

Examples

Her provocative comments sparked a heated debate among the panelists.
The artist's provocative work challenged traditional ideas about beauty.
He gave her a provocative smile that made her blush.

Synset Examples

a provocative remark
a provocative smile
provocative Irish tunes which … compel the hearers to dance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stimulating - encouraging interest or enthusiasm
  • Synonym: incendiary - tending to stir up conflict or controversy
  • Antonym: reassuring - making someone feel less anxious

Etymology

From Latin provocativus (challenging), from provocare (to call forth, provoke).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01902545-a
Synset POS: a
