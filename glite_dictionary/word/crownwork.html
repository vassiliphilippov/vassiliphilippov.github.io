crownwork

1. NOUN

/ ˈkraʊnwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈkraʊnwɜːk / (BrE)
a type of fortification consisting of a full bastion with walls on either side ending in half-bastions, from which longer flank walls run back toward the main fortress, used to extend and strengthen defensive lines.
type of fortification with bastions and flanking walls

Examples

The engineers designed a crownwork to protect the vulnerable northern approach to the fortress.
During the siege, soldiers took up positions along the crownwork to repel the advancing enemy forces.
The remains of the old crownwork can still be seen near the entrance of the fortified town.

Synset Examples

The crownwork was used to extend the fortified area in a particular direction often in order to defend a bridge, prevent the enemy occupying an area of high ground, or simply strengthen the overall fortifications in the expected direction of attack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hornwork - a fortification similar to a crownwork but with demi-bastions instead of a full central bastion

Etymology

From crown + work, describing its crown-like structure in military fortification (17th century).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkraʊnˌwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈkraʊnwɜːk / (BrE)
A dental appliance consisting of an artificial crown fitted to a broken or decayed tooth.
artificial dental crown
medical

Examples

The dentist recommended a crownwork to restore the damaged molar.
Modern crownwork can be made of porcelain or metal, depending on the case.
He was relieved when the crownwork procedure was painless and quick.

Synset Examples

tomorrow my dentist will fit me for a crown

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dental crown - an artificial cap placed over a tooth

Etymology

From 'crown' (a covering for a tooth) + 'work' (something made or done).
