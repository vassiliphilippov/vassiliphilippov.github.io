deadhead

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛdˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈdɛdˌhɛd / (BrE)
a train, bus, or taxi that is traveling empty, especially to or from a location to pick up passengers or cargo
a vehicle traveling empty (transport)

Examples

The bus made the return journey as a deadhead after dropping off the last passengers.
Railway companies try to minimize deadhead trips for cost efficiency.
The taxi driver complained about too many deadhead miles at the end of his shift.

Etymology

From 'dead' + 'head', used in transport jargon since early 20th century to mean traveling without revenue passengers or cargo.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛdˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈdɛdˌhɛd / (BrE)
a person, especially an employee, who avoids work or does not contribute productively; someone who benefits without paying or contributing
an unproductive or nonpaying person
informal

Examples

The company decided to lay off several deadheads who were drawing salaries but not contributing to projects.
She complained that too many deadheads were slowing down the team’s progress.
It's important for organizations to identify and deal with deadheads to remain efficient.

Synset Examples

the deadheads on the payroll should be eased out as fast as possible

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loafer - someone who avoids work; idle or lazy person
  • Synonym: shirker - a person who avoids work or responsibility
  • Antonym: worker - a person engaged in productive work
