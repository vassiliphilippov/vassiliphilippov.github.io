glottis

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɑːtɪs / (AmE), / ˈɡlɒtɪs / (BrE)
the part of the larynx consisting of the vocal cords and the opening between them, where sounds are produced
part of the larynx with the vocal cords
medical

Examples

The doctor used a mirror to examine her glottis for any abnormalities.
During speech, the glottis opens and closes to control airflow.
Swelling of the glottis can cause difficulty in breathing.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: larynx - the hollow muscular organ forming an air passage to the lungs and holding the vocal cords
  • Synonym: vocal cord - either of the two folds of mucous membrane stretched across the larynx

Etymology

From Greek *glōttis* (the tongue, mouth of the windpipe), related to *glōssa* (tongue).
