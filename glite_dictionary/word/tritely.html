tritely

1. ADV

/ ˈtraɪtli / (AmE), / ˈtraɪtli / (BrE)
In a way that is dull, unoriginal, or overused due to lack of freshness or novelty.
in a dull or unoriginal manner

Examples

She spoke tritely about the importance of teamwork, offering nothing new to the discussion.
The movie ended tritely, with a predictable sunset scene and a cliché farewell.
His advice was tritely familiar, echoing what many had already said.

Synset Examples

tritely expressed emotions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trite - lacking originality or freshness; dull on account of overuse
  • Antonym: freshly - in a new or original manner

Etymology

From 'trite' + '-ly', with 'trite' from Latin 'tritus', past participle of 'terere' (to rub).
