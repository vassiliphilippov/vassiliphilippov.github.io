nautch

1. NOUN

/ nɑtʃ / (AmE), / nɔːtʃ / (BrE)
A traditional Indian dance performed by professional female dancers, often featuring intricate movements; especially popular during the Mughal period and colonial era.
traditional Indian dance by professional women

Examples

The royal court was mesmerized by the elegance of the nautch performance.
Several British officials attended a nautch at the governor's residence.
The documentary explored the history and evolution of the nautch in Indian culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dance - a series of rhythmic movements to music; general term

Etymology

From Hindi 'nāc' (dance), from Sanskrit 'nātya' (dancing, drama).
