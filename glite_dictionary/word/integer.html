integer

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.t̬ɚ.dʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tɪ.dʒə / (BrE)
a whole number; a number without fractions, including zero, positive numbers, and negative numbers
whole number (incl. negatives and zero)

Examples

Every even integer is divisible by 2.
Negative numbers like -3 are also considered an integer.
Zero is the only integer that is neither positive nor negative.

Synset Examples

an integer is a number that is not a fraction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whole number - a number without fractions; an integer
  • Antonym: fraction - a number that expresses part of a whole; not a whole number

Etymology

From Latin 'integer' (whole, untouched, entire).
