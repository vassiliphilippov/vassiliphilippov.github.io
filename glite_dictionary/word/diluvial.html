diluvial

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈluːviəl / (AmE), / dɪˈljuːvɪəl / (BrE)
relating to or caused by a flood, especially a great or ancient flood.
relating to a flood or deluge

Examples

Recent excavations revealed diluvial deposits left by an ancient flood.
The scientist studied the diluvial formations along the old riverbed.
Some myths describe a diluvial event that reshaped the entire region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alluvial - relating to deposits made by flowing water

Etymology

From Latin 'diluvialis', from 'diluvium' meaning 'flood'.
