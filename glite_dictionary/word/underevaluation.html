underevaluation

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌndɚˌiːvæl.juˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌʌndərˌiːvæl.juˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an assessment or appraisal that underestimates the true value of something
appraisal underestimating value

Examples

The company's assets suffered from an underevaluation that affected its market reputation.
The auditors discovered an underevaluation of inventory in last year's financial report.
Because of underevaluation, the property was sold well below its actual worth.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overvaluation - an appraisal that overestimates the value of something
  • Synonym: underestimate - to estimate something as being smaller or less important than it actually is

Etymology

Formed from 'under-' (meaning 'below' or 'insufficient') and 'evaluation'.
