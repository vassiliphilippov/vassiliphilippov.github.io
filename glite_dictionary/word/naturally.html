naturally

1. ADV

/ ˈnætʃɚəli / (AmE), / ˈnatʃ(ə)rəli / (BrE)
As might be expected; in a way that is unsurprising or only logical.
as expected; unsurprisingly

Examples

Naturally, the restaurant was crowded on a Friday night.
She naturally assumed that he was coming to the party as well.
Naturally, everyone was upset when the event was cancelled.

Synset Examples

naturally, the lawyer sent us a huge bill

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obviously - in a way that is easily perceived or understood
  • Antonym: surprisingly - in a way that causes surprise; contrary to what might be expected
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00039019-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ˈnætʃ.ɚ.li / (AmE), / ˈnætʃ.ə.li / (BrE)
In a way that is part of a person's or thing's inherent character or tendency, rather than the result of external influences.
inherently; by nature

Examples

He was naturally lazy, preferring to relax rather than work hard.
She is naturally curious and always asks many questions about the world.
Some people are naturally talented at music, while others need to practice more.

Synset Examples

he was naturally lazy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innately - in a way that exists from birth, not learned
  • Antonym: artificially - not occurring by nature; through human intervention

Etymology

Formed from 'natural' + '-ly'; 'natural' from Latin 'naturalis', from 'natura' (birth, nature).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00507808-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ˈnætʃ.ɚ.li / (AmE), / ˈnætʃ.ə.li / (BrE)
In a natural or normal way, without artificiality or effort.
in a natural or normal manner

Examples

She moved so naturally on stage that the audience forgot she was acting.
The children naturally picked up the language after a few months of living abroad.
He responded naturally to the surprising news, showing genuine excitement.

Synset Examples

speak naturally and easily

Related Terms

  • Antonym: artificially - in a way that is not natural; with effort or contrivance

Etymology

From natural + -ly, modeled on Latin forms.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00490971-r
Synset POS: r

4. ADV

/ ˈnætʃ.ɚ.li / (AmE), / ˈnætʃ.ər.əli / (BrE)
In a way that is in accordance with nature; without artificial aid or interference.
in accordance with nature

Examples

The vegetables were grown naturally without any chemical fertilizers.
She prefers products that are made naturally rather than synthetically.
This mineral occurs naturally in the Earth's crust.

Synset Examples

naturally grown flowers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: artificially - by artificial means or human intervention

Etymology

From natural + -ly, modeled after Latin naturaliter.

Source: WordNet
