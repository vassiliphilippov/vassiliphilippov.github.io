liner

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪnɚ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪnə / (BrE)
(Baseball) A ball that is hit hard and travels in a straight, low trajectory through the air, typically parallel to the ground.
baseball hit that flies straight out from batter
american

Examples

He swung at the pitch and sent a liner into right field for a single.
The shortstop caught the liner just before it hit the ground.
Fans cheered as the liner sped past the pitcher's mound.

Synset Examples

the batter hit a liner to the shortstop

Related Terms

  • Synonym: line drive - a baseball hit that travels straight and fast in the air

Etymology

From 'line drive' in baseball slang, referring to the straight 'line' path of the ball.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪnɚ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪnə / (BrE)
a large commercial ship, especially one that carries passengers on a regular route or schedule
a large commercial passenger ship

Examples

The luxury liner set sail from Southampton to New York last night.
Many immigrants crossed the Atlantic on a passenger liner in the early 20th century.
The shipping company launched a new ocean liner designed for transatlantic travel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruise ship - a large passenger ship used for pleasure voyages
  • Antonym: freighter - a ship that carries cargo but not passengers

Etymology

From 'line' (meaning a regular route) + -er (denoting an agent or thing related to).

Related Terms

/ ˈlaɪnɚ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪnə / (BrE)

fabric sewn inside a garment

Examples

Many luxury handbags have a leather exterior and a fabric liner for extra durability.

Related Terms
