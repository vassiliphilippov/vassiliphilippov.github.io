Vernonia

1. NOUN

/ vərˈnoʊniə / (AmE), / vɜːˈnəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical herbs or shrubs, mainly from the Americas, characterized by cymose clusters of tubular flowers.
genus of tropical herbs or shrubs

Examples

The Vernonia species are commonly found in tropical regions of the New World.
Botanists are studying the medicinal properties of several Vernonia plants.
A field in Brazil was lush with blooming Vernonia shrubs.

Etymology

Named after English botanist William Vernon (17th–18th century).
