childbearing

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃaɪldˌbɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪldˌbɛːrɪŋ / (BrE)
relating to the process of giving birth to children or suitable for childbirth
relating to childbirth

Examples

Women of childbearing age need special attention to their health and nutrition.
Certain medications are not recommended for childbearing women.
Access to healthcare is crucial during the childbearing years.

Synset Examples

of childbearing age

Related Terms

  • Synonym: childbirth - the act or process of giving birth to a child

Etymology

From 'child' + 'bearing', meaning to carry or give birth to a child.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02704846-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪldˌbɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪldˌbeərɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of giving birth to children; the act or period of having babies
the process of giving birth

Examples

Many women choose to delay childbearing until their careers are established.
Advances in medicine have made childbearing safer for both mothers and infants.
Some cultures place great importance on childbearing within marriage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: childbirth - the act or process of giving birth to a child
  • Antonym: infertility - the inability to conceive children or become pregnant

Etymology

From 'child' + 'bearing', meaning carrying or bringing forth children.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13469507-n
Synset POS: n
