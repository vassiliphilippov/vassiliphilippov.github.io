to exuviate

1. VERB

/ ɪgˈzuːvieɪt / (AmE), / ɛgˈzjuːvieɪt / (BrE)
(of an animal) to cast off or shed hair, skin, feathers, or an outer layer as part of a natural process.
shed or cast off outer layer (biol.)

Examples

Some reptiles exuviate their old skin regularly as they grow.
During its metamorphosis, the insect will exuviate and emerge in a new form.
Many birds exuviate their feathers before the migration season.

Synset Examples

our dog sheds every Spring

Related Terms

  • Synonym: molt - to shed the outer skin, feathers, or shell
  • Synonym: shed - to lose or cast off naturally, especially outer layers

Etymology

From Latin exuviae (things stripped off, cast-off clothes or armor).
