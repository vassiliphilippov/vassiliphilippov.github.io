phonogramic

1. ADJ

/ ˌfoʊ.nəˈɡræm.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌfəʊ.nəˈɡræm.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a phonogram
relating to a phonogram

Examples

The ancient script uses phonogramic signs to represent individual consonant sounds.
Her research focuses on the phonogramic aspects of early writing systems.
A phonogramic symbol conveys a specific sound rather than a whole word or idea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phonogram - a written character representing a sound

Etymology

From 'phonogram' (from Greek 'phōnē' meaning 'sound' + 'gramma' meaning 'something written') and the suffix '-ic'.
