barber

1. VERB

/ ˈbɑrbər / (AmE), / ˈbɑːbə / (BrE)
to cut the hair and/or beard of someone as a barber does
cut hair or beard professionally

Examples

He used to barber for extra income when he was a student.
She learned how to barber at a young age from her father.
Some soldiers will barber their comrades between duties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haircut - the act of cutting hair

Etymology

From Old French *barbier*, from Latin *barba* (beard).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00042523-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːrbər / (AmE), / ˈbɑːbə / (BrE)
A person whose job is to cut men's hair and shave or trim beards.
person who cuts men's hair and shaves beards

Examples

He goes to the barber every two weeks to keep his hair short.
The barber offered him a shave along with a haircut.
After years as a barber, she opened her own shop downtown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hairdresser - a person whose job is to cut, style, and dress hair, especially women's

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman and Old French 'barbier', from Latin 'barba' meaning 'beard'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09858283-n
Synset POS: n
