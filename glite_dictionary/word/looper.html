looper

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːpər / (AmE), / ˈluːpə / (BrE)
a small hairless caterpillar with legs only on its front and rear segments, especially the larva of a moth in the family Geometridae, that moves in a looping manner
caterpillar moving in loops; moth larva

Examples

The looper moves in a characteristic arching motion across leaves.
Several plants in the garden had been eaten by a green looper.
Scientists studied the feeding habits of the oak leaf looper for their research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inchworm - a small caterpillar, especially of the family Geometridae, that moves in a looping fashion

Etymology

From the word 'loop', referring to its looping movement as it crawls.
