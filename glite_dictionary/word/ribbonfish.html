ribbonfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪb.ən.fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈrɪb.ən.fɪʃ / (BrE)
a marine fish with a long, thin, compressed body resembling a ribbon, found in deep waters of temperate and tropical seas
long, slender marine fish

Examples

The ribbonfish is known for its distinctive, elongated silver body that glimmers underwater.
Fishermen sometimes catch a ribbonfish by accident while trawling in deep ocean waters.
A ribbonfish can grow remarkably long, making it an impressive sight when brought to the surface.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪb.ənˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈrɪb.ənˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A long, thin deep-water fish found in tropical seas, typically measuring 20 to 30 feet and noted for its red dorsal fin.
long, thin tropical deep-water fish

Examples

The ribbonfish is easily recognized by its long, eel-like body and bright red dorsal fin.
Divers spotted a massive ribbonfish gliding through the deep blue waters off the coast.
Scientists have studied the migration patterns of the elusive ribbonfish for several years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oarfish - a very large, elongated deep-sea fish formerly considered mythical
