Azadirachta

1. PROPN

/ əzəˈdɪrɑktə / (AmE), / ˌazədɪˈraktə / (BrE)
A genus of large tropical trees native to South and Southeast Asia, including the neem tree, known for its medicinal and agricultural uses.
genus of tropical trees; includes neem

Examples

The Azadirachta genus includes species widely used in traditional Indian medicine.
Researchers are studying Azadirachta plants for their natural pesticide properties.
The neem tree, or Azadirachta indica, is common in tropical regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neem - a tree in the Azadirachta genus, valued for medicinal uses

Etymology

From Persian *āzād dirakht* (free or noble tree).

2. NOUN

/ əˌzædəˈræktə / (AmE), / əˌzædɪˈræktə / (BrE)
A genus of large trees native to East India, including the neem tree, valued for their medicinal and agricultural uses.
genus of trees including neem

Examples

The Azadirachta genus includes trees well known for their medicinal properties.
Farmers often plant Azadirachta to act as natural pesticides.
Researchers are studying compounds extracted from Azadirachta species for pharmaceutical use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neem - a tree of the species Azadirachta indica, known for its medicinal uses

Etymology

From Persian 'āzād-darakht' meaning 'free tree' or 'noble tree', Latinized in botanical nomenclature.
