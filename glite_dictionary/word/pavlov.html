Pavlov

1. PROPN

/ pɑvˈlɔv / (AmE), / ˈpavlɒv / (BrE)
Ivan Pavlov (1849–1936), a Russian physiologist famous for his research on conditioned reflexes, especially through experiments with dogs.
Russian physiologist, studied conditioned reflexes

Examples

Many psychology students learn about Pavlov and his famous experiments involving dogs and bells.
The theory of classical conditioning is directly linked to the work of Pavlov.
Researchers continue to build upon the discoveries first made by Pavlov in the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classical conditioning - a learning process first described by Ivan Pavlov
  • Synonym: conditioned reflex - a reflex acquired through conditioning, as described by Pavlov

Etymology

From the Russian surname Pavlov (Па́влов), associated with Ivan Pavlov.
