Pinus echinata

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪnəs ɛˈkaɪnətə / (AmE), / ˌpaɪnəs ɛˈkɪnətə / (BrE)
A large pine tree native to the southern United States, characterized by short needles in clusters of two to three and red-brown bark when mature; also known as shortleaf pine.
a large pine tree of the southern U.S.

Examples

The Pinus echinata is valued for its strong timber in the construction industry.
Forests of Pinus echinata are common throughout the southeastern United States.
Many birds and mammals rely on the seeds of Pinus echinata for food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shortleaf pine - Common name for Pinus echinata, a large pine tree native to the southern U.S.

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Pinus' meaning 'pine' and 'echinata' meaning 'spiny' or 'prickly.'
