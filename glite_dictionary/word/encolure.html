encolure

1. NOUN

/ ɑ̃.kɔˈlʊr / (AmE), / ɑ̃.kɔˈljʊə / (BrE)
the neck or mane of a horse, especially as considered in terms of form and carriage
the neck or mane of a horse

Examples

The judge praised the stallion’s impressive encolure during the dressage competition.
Attention to grooming the encolure is essential for show horses.
The elegant arch of the horse's encolure made it stand out in the parade.

Etymology

From French encolure (neck, nape, mane of a horse).
