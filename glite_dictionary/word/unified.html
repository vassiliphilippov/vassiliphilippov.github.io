unified

1. ADJ

/ ˈjuː.nəˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈjuː.nɪ.faɪd / (BrE)
formed or brought together to make a single entity or unit; made whole or combined.
formed or united into a whole

Examples

The two companies merged into a unified organization focused on innovation.
The country became increasingly unified after years of conflict.
Their efforts created a unified voice advocating for change.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: divided - separated into parts or groups; not unified

Etymology

From Latin 'unus' (one) via the verb 'unify' plus the suffix '-ed'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈjuːnɪˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪfaɪd / (BrE)
formed or made into a single unit or entity; operating as a whole.
made into a single unit; integrated

Examples

The company presented a unified marketing strategy across all platforms.
Efforts were made to create a unified response to the crisis.
A unified team often achieves better results than one with divided goals.

Synset Examples

a unified utility system
a coordinated program

Related Terms

  • Synonym: integrated - combined into a whole or functioning as one
  • Antonym: fragmented - broken into separate or distinct parts

Etymology

From unify + -ed; 'unify' from Latin 'unificare' (to make one).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01331181-s
Synset POS: s
