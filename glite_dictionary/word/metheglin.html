metheglin

1. NOUN

/ məˈθɛɡlɪn / (AmE), / mɛˈθɛɡlɪn / (BrE)
a type of mead flavored with spices or medicinal herbs
spiced or medicated mead

Examples

Some traditional Welsh recipes for metheglin include spices like nutmeg and cinnamon.
She brewed a batch of metheglin using honey, rosemary, and cloves for the village festival.
Historians believe that metheglin was often served at medieval banquets for its supposed health benefits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mead - an alcoholic drink made from fermenting honey with water

Etymology

From Welsh *meddyglyn*: 'meddyg' (healing) + 'llyn' (liquor).
