dementia

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈmɛn(t)ʃə / (AmE), / dɪˈmɛn(t)ʃə / (BrE)
A chronic or persistent disorder of the mental processes caused by organic or functional disease, marked by memory disorders, personality changes, and impaired reasoning.
serious mental deterioration (medical condition)
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that her forgetfulness was a symptom of dementia.
Many families struggle to care for loved ones living with dementia.
Early diagnosis of dementia can help manage the progression of the disease.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: delirium - acute disturbance of mental abilities that results in confused thinking and reduced awareness
  • Degree Higher: insanity - a severe mental illness causing inability to think or behave rationally

Etymology

From Latin 'dementia' (madness), from 'demens' (out of one's mind).
