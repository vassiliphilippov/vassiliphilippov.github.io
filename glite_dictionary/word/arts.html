arts

1. NOUN

/ ɑrts / (AmE), / ɑːts / (BrE)
Subjects of study in higher education intended to provide general knowledge and intellectual skills, often distinguished from technical or vocational subjects.
academic subjects in humanities and social sciences

Examples

She decided to study the arts at university, focusing on literature and philosophy.
Many colleges offer both arts and sciences degrees to give students a broad education.
The faculty of arts includes departments such as history, languages, and sociology.

Synset Examples

the college of arts and sciences

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sciences - academic disciplines based on systematic study of the structure and behavior of the physical and natural world

Etymology

Plural of 'art', from Latin 'ars' (skill, craft, art).
