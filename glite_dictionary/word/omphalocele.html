omphalocele

1. NOUN

/ ɑmˈfæloʊˌsil / (AmE), / ɒmˈfæləsiːl / (BrE)
a congenital defect where the intestine and omentum protrude through a hernia near the navel, often present at birth
protrusion of intestine near the navel (medical)
medical

Examples

The newborn was diagnosed with an omphalocele requiring surgical intervention.
Doctors explained that an omphalocele is a rare abdominal wall defect.
Treatment for an omphalocele depends on the size and the involvement of other organs.

Etymology

From Greek 'omphalos' (navel) + 'kēlē' (hernia, swelling).
