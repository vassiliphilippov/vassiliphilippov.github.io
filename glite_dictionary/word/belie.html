to belie

1. VERB

/ bɪˈlaɪ / (AmE), / bɪˈlaɪ / (BrE)
to give a false impression of something; to show to be false or contradict
give a false impression; contradict

Examples

Her calm expression belied the anxiety she felt inside.
The simple exterior of the house belies the luxury within.
His cheerful manner belied his sadness after the loss.

Synset Examples

This statement misrepresents my intentions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to misrepresent - to give an untrue or misleading idea; to falsely describe
  • Antonym: to reveal - to make something known or visible that was previously hidden or unknown

Etymology

From Old English 'belīgan' (to deceive by lying), from 'be-' (about, around) + 'lēogan' (to lie).

2. VERB

/ bɪˈlaɪ / (AmE), / bɪˈlaɪ / (BrE)
to give a false impression of something; to show to be false or in contradiction with
to show as false or contradict

Examples

Her calm expression belied the anxiety she felt inside.
The poor sales belie the company's reputation for quality products.
His gentle manner belied his fierce determination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to contradict - to assert the opposite of a statement
  • Synonym: to conceal - to keep something from being seen or known; hide

Etymology

From Old English belēogan (to deceive by lying).
