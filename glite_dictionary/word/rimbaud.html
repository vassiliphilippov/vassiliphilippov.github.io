Rimbaud

1. PROPN

/ ræmˈboʊ / (AmE), / ræmˈboʊ / (BrE)
(1854–1891) A French poet known for his influential and innovative poetry, considered a precursor to the surrealists.
French poet; precursor to surrealism

Examples

The poetry of Rimbaud continues to inspire writers and artists around the world.
Many critics consider Rimbaud to be a leading figure of modern literature.
She wrote her thesis on the revolutionary style of Rimbaud's work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Verlaine - French poet closely associated with Rimbaud

Etymology

French surname. Arthur Rimbaud (full name Jean Nicolas Arthur Rimbaud).
