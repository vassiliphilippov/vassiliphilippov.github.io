Chiloe

1. PROPN

/ tʃɪˈloʊ.eɪ / (AmE), / tʃɪˈləʊ.eɪ / (BrE)
the largest island of Chile, located off the south-central coast and known for its distinct culture and history
largest Chilean island off south-central Chile

Examples

Many tourists visit Chiloe for its wooden churches and unique folklore.
The coastline of Chiloe is famous for its stunning views and traditional palafito houses.
Scientists study wildlife on Chiloe, including rare species found only on the island.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Chiloé', derived from the Mapuche word 'chilhué' meaning 'place of seagulls'.
