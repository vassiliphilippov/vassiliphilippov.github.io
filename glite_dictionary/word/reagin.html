reagin

1. NOUN

/ riˈeɪɡɪn / (AmE), / riːˈeɪdʒɪn / (BrE)
An antibody, specifically immunoglobulin E, that is produced in response to allergens and causes allergic reactions by releasing histamine and other substances.
antibody causing allergic reactions
medical

Examples

Recent studies have shown that high levels of reagin are present in individuals with severe hay fever.
The allergist explained that reagin plays a central role in triggering allergic symptoms.
A blood test can help determine the amount of reagin in a patient's system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antibody - a protein produced by the immune system to neutralize or destroy foreign substances

Etymology

From Latin 'reagere' (to react) + English '-in' (chemical suffix).
