bullying

1. ADJ

/ ˈbʊliɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʊliɪŋ / (BrE)
noisily domineering and inclined to intimidate or browbeat others
noisily domineering; intimidating

Examples

The manager's bullying manner created a toxic atmosphere in the office.
Her bullying attitude left little room for open discussion in the team.
Some parents worry about bullying behaviors among children at school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overbearing - unpleasantly or arrogantly domineering
  • Antonym: meek - having a quiet, gentle, and submissive nature

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʊliɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʊl.i.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of intimidating or mistreating someone, especially someone weaker, to force them to do something or to assert power over them
intimidation or mistreatment of a weaker person

Examples

The school implemented new policies to prevent bullying among students.
She spoke publicly about her experiences with bullying in the workplace.
Cyber bullying has become a major concern for parents and educators alike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intimidation - the act of making someone afraid, especially to force them to do something
  • Synonym: harassment - aggressive pressure or intimidation, especially repeated or persistent

Etymology

From the verb 'bully' + -ing.
