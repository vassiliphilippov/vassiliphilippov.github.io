wanting

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɑntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɒntɪŋ / (BrE)
lacking or insufficient in amount or degree; not meeting a required standard.
lacking; insufficient

Examples

His explanation was found wanting by the committee and rejected.
The plan is wanting in several key areas that need more detail.
If her qualifications are wanting, she will need additional training.

Synset Examples

a deficient education
deficient in common sense
lacking in stamina
tested and found wanting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deficient - not having enough of something; insufficient
  • Synonym: lacking - not having; deficient in
  • Antonym: sufficient - enough; adequate

Etymology

Present participle of 'want', from Old Norse *vanta* (to lack).

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɑntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɒntɪŋ / (BrE)
lacking or missing; not present or not sufficient.
lacking; not present

Examples

Her explanation was wanting in clarity and detail.
The evidence required to convict him was clearly wanting.
Many basic supplies are still wanting after the storm.

Synset Examples

the thumb is absent
her appetite was lacking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lacking - not present or not available
  • Synonym: absent - not present in a place
  • Antonym: present - existing or occurring in a place or thing

Etymology

From the verb 'want,' from Middle English 'wanten' (to lack), from Old Norse 'vanta.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00931073-s
Synset POS: s
