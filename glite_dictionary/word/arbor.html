arbor

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrbər / (AmE), / ˈɑːbə / (BrE)
a tree, especially one distinguished from a shrub; used chiefly in technical or literary contexts
a tree (not a shrub)
archaic

Examples

The ancient park was dotted with a variety of impressive arbors, each centuries old.
In botanical texts, an arbor is often contrasted with a shrub.
The garden designer recommended planting a flowering arbor to provide shade and beauty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree - a perennial plant with an elongated stem, or trunk, supporting branches and leaves

Etymology

From Latin arbor (tree).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑrbɚ / (AmE), / ˈɑːbə / (BrE)
a rotating shaft or spindle that serves as an axis for larger rotating machine parts
rotating shaft or spindle in machinery

Examples

The engineer replaced the worn arbor in the milling machine to restore its precision.
When assembling the gearbox, ensure the arbor is properly aligned to prevent vibration.
This tool is mounted on an arbor that allows it to spin at high speed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spindle - a rod or shaft serving as an axis in machinery

Etymology

From Latin 'arbor' (tree), possibly in reference to the central supporting role, like a trunk.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɑrbər / (AmE), / ˈɑːbə / (BrE)
a framework in a garden or park that supports climbing plants, often creating a shaded area beneath
garden structure for climbing plants

Examples

A wooden arbor in the backyard was covered with blooming roses.
They sat beneath the arbor to escape the midday sun.
The vines climbed high over the arbor, forming a perfect green canopy.

Synset Examples

the arbor provided a shady resting place in the park

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pergola - a structure usually consisting of parallel colonnades supporting an open roof of girders and cross rafters
  • Synonym: trellis - a framework of light wooden or metal bars, chiefly used as a support for fruit trees or climbing plants

Etymology

From Latin 'arbor' meaning 'tree'.
