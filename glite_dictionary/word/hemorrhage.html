hemorrhage

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛmɚɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈhɛm(ə)rɪdʒ / (BrE)
a heavy or uncontrolled flow of blood from a ruptured blood vessel, often caused by injury or disease
uncontrolled bleeding from a blood vessel
medical

Examples

The patient suffered a severe hemorrhage after the accident and required immediate surgery.
A brain hemorrhage can cause lasting damage if not treated quickly.
Excessive blood loss due to internal hemorrhage is a medical emergency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bleeding - the general process of losing blood, usually through a wound
  • Antonym: clot - a thick mass of coagulated blood that stops bleeding

Etymology

From Greek 'haimorrhagia' (a violent bleeding), from 'haima' (blood) + 'rhēgnynai' (to break).

2. VERB

/ ˈhɛmɚˌɹɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈhɛmərɪdʒ / (BrE)
to lose blood heavily from a ruptured blood vessel, often in a serious or uncontrolled way
to bleed heavily or uncontrollably
medical

Examples

After the accident, he began to hemorrhage from a deep wound in his leg.
Without immediate surgery, the patient might hemorrhage to death internally.
Doctors rushed to control the artery before she could hemorrhage further.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bleed - to lose blood from the body

Etymology

From Greek 'haimorrhagia' (flowing of blood), from 'haima' (blood) + 'rhēgnynai' (to break).
