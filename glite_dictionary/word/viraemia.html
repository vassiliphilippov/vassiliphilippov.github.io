viraemia

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈriːmiə / (AmE), / vɪəˈriːmiə / (BrE)
The presence of a virus in the bloodstream.
presence of a virus in the blood
medical british

Examples

The doctor suspected viraemia after observing the patient's high fever and chills.
Complications can occur rapidly once viraemia develops in immunocompromised patients.
Early detection of viraemia is crucial for managing certain viral infections effectively.

Synset Examples

viremia spread the smallpox virus to the internal organs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viremia - presence of a virus in the blood (American spelling)

Etymology

From New Latin, from 'virus' (poison, slime) + '-aemia' (condition of the blood, from Greek 'haima' meaning 'blood').
