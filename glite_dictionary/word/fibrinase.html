fibrinase

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪbrɪˌneɪs / (AmE), / ˈfɪbrɪˌneɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme (active form of factor XIII) that stabilizes fibrin in blood clotting
enzyme stabilizing fibrin clots
medical

Examples

The activation of fibrinase is essential for forming stable blood clots.
Without sufficient fibrinase, clots may remain weak and easily dissolve.
Researchers studied the function of fibrinase in patients with bleeding disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrombin - enzyme that converts fibrinogen to fibrin during blood clotting

Etymology

From 'fibrin' (a protein involved in clotting) + '-ase' (enzyme suffix).
