pyroscope

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪrəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈpaɪərəˌskəʊp / (BrE)
A device for measuring high temperatures by observing the color of light emitted by a hot object.
temperature-measuring device by emitted color

Examples

The scientist used a pyroscope to determine the temperature of the glowing metal.
By comparing the colors observed through the pyroscope, she estimated the furnace's heat.
A pyroscope can be particularly useful when measuring the temperature of molten glass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyrometer - an instrument for measuring high temperatures

Etymology

From Greek 'pyr-' meaning 'fire' and '-scope' meaning 'instrument for viewing'.
