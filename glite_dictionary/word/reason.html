reason

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːzən / (AmE), / ˈriːzn̩ / (BrE)
the ability to think, understand, and form judgments logically and sensibly
good sense or sound judgment

Examples

In a crisis, it is important to keep your reason and stay calm.
When emotions run high, reason can sometimes be ignored.
After a heated discussion, she appealed to his reason to resolve their disagreement.

Synset Examples

his rationality may have been impaired
he had to rely less on reason than on rousing their emotions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irrationality - lack of good sense or sound judgment
  • Synonym: rationality - the quality of being based on reason or logic

Etymology

From Old French *raison*, from Latin *ratio* (reckoning, understanding).
