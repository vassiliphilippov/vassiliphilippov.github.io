gook

1. NOUN

/ ɡʊk / (AmE), / ɡʊk / (BrE)
any thick, viscous substance; a sticky or gooey material.
a thick, sticky substance

Examples

The engine was covered in a layer of dark, oily gook.
He wiped the mysterious gook off his shoes before entering the house.
There was sticky gook clogging the drain after the repairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goo - a sticky or viscous substance

2. NOUN

/ ɡʊk / (AmE), / ɡʊk / (BrE)
An extremely offensive and derogatory term for a person of Asian descent, especially used during wars involving the United States in Asia.
offensive slur for Asians
offensive

Examples

The film was criticized for its use of the word gook in several scenes.
He was shocked when someone shouted gook at him on the street.
The slur gook is considered extremely offensive and should never be used.

Synset Examples

He felt offended by the use of the term gook in the film.

Etymology

Origin unclear; possibly from American military slang during the Korean War or earlier.
