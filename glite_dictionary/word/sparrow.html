sparrow

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛroʊ / (AmE), / ˈspærəʊ / (BrE)
a small, brownish or grayish songbird, especially of the family Passeridae, common in Europe and many other parts of the world
small brownish or gray songbird

Examples

A sparrow perched on the windowsill, chirping loudly in the morning sun.
We watched a flock of sparrows pecking at crumbs in the park.
She pointed out a sparrow darting quickly through the garden shrubs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finch - small passerine bird with a conical beak
  • Synonym: starling - small to medium-sized passerine birds, often mistaken for sparrows

Etymology

From Old English *spearwa*, of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈspærəʊ / (BrE)
A small, dull-colored songbird, typically feeding on seeds or insects, found in many parts of the world.
a small seed-eating songbird

Examples

A sparrow perched on the windowsill, chirping cheerfully as the sun rose.
Many people enjoy watching sparrows hop around city parks in search of crumbs.
The garden was filled with the lively songs of sparrows each morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finch - a small songbird with a short, stout bill, often similar in appearance to sparrows

Etymology

Old English *spearwa*, related to Old High German *sparo*, Latin *passer* (sparrow).
