Aramaism

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛrəˈmeɪɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌærəˈmeɪɪzəm / (BrE)
a word, expression, or other feature characteristic of the Aramaic language found in another language
feature of Aramaic found in another language

Examples

The text contains several Aramaisms that suggest the writer was influenced by Aramaic culture.
Scholars often debate whether a certain phrase is an Aramaism or simply a regional expression.
The presence of Aramaisms in the manuscript indicates a possible translation from Aramaic sources.

Synset Examples

Even Joachim Jeremias, one of the prime champions of this criterion, recognized that the mere presence of an Aramaism could not prove the authenticity of an independent unit of translation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hebraism - a feature characteristic of Hebrew occurring in another language
  • Synonym: Latinism - a feature characteristic of Latin found in another language

Etymology

From Aramaic (a Semitic language) + -ism (forming nouns indicating a characteristic).
