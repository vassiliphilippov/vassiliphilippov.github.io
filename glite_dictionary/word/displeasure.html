displeasure

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈplɛʒɚ / (AmE), / dɪsˈplɛʒə / (BrE)
A feeling of annoyance, dissatisfaction, or lack of approval.
annoyance or dissatisfaction

Examples

She couldn't hide her displeasure when she saw the messy room.
His actions caused great displeasure among the staff.
The teacher expressed her displeasure at the students' behavior.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: satisfaction - a pleasant feeling that you get when you receive something you wanted
  • Synonym: annoyance - the feeling of being annoyed or irritated

Etymology

From Old French 'desplaisir' (displease), from Latin prefix 'dis-' (not) + French 'plaisir' (to please).
