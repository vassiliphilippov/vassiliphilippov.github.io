to convulse

1. VERB

/ kənˈvʌls / (AmE), / kənˈvʌls / (BrE)
(of a person) to shake violently, especially with uncontrollable laughter or emotion.
shake violently, esp. with laughter or emotion

Examples

The crowd convulsed with laughter at the comedian's jokes.
She convulsed so hard with giggles that she could barely breathe.
His witty remark convulsed everyone at the dinner table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collapse - to fall down or cave in; also used for being overcome with laughter

Etymology

From Latin convulsus (past participle of convellere, meaning 'to tear loose, shake violently').

2. VERB

/ kənˈvʌls / (AmE), / kənˈvʌls / (BrE)
(of the body or a part of it) to shake or move violently, especially in an uncontrolled way due to illness, emotion, or shock.
move or shake violently, often uncontrollably

Examples

The patient began to convulse as the seizure struck.
Crowds watched as the building seemed to convulse during the earthquake.
The child's body would convulse whenever the severe coughing fit occurred.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Latin convulsus, past participle of convellere (to pull violently, shatter).
