manikin

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænɪkɪn / (AmE), / ˈmanɪkɪn / (BrE)
a person who is very small in stature but is not otherwise deformed or abnormal
very small person (not deformed)

Examples

The character was described as a manikin, hardly reaching the height of the table.
Despite being a manikin, she commanded respect with her presence.
In the crowd, the manikin struggled to see over the heads of others.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: dwarf - a person of unusually small stature, often due to a medical condition

Etymology

From Dutch mannekijn, diminutive of man (man).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmænɪkɪn / (AmE), / ˈmænɪkɪn / (BrE)
a woman who models clothes to display fashions, especially for designers or photographers
female fashion model

Examples

The designer hired a new manikin to showcase her latest collection on the runway.
Many aspiring models dream of becoming a celebrated manikin in the fashion industry.
The manikin posed gracefully for the cameras during the photo shoot.

Synset Examples

she was too fat to be a mannequin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: model - person who poses or displays clothes for fashion or advertising
  • Synonym: mannequin - person who models clothing or a dummy used for display

Etymology

From Dutch 'manneken', diminutive of 'man' (man).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmænɪkɪn / (AmE), / ˈmænɪkɪn / (BrE)
a life-size dummy used by tailors, dressmakers, or in shops to display or fit clothes
life-size dummy for displaying clothes

Examples

The designer arranged the dress carefully on the manikin in the shop window.
Each manikin was dressed in the latest fashion for the display.
The art class used a manikin to practice sketching human poses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mannequin - life-size dummy for displaying clothes

Etymology

From Dutch 'manneken' (little man), diminutive of 'man' (man).
