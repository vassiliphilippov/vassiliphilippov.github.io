Biscutalla laevigata

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɪs.kjuːˈtæl.ə ˌliː.vɪˈɡeɪ.tə / (AmE), / ˌbɪs.kjuːˈtæl.ə ˌliː.vɪˈɡɑː.tə / (BrE)
A plant native to southeastern Europe, with yellow, mustard-like flowers and distinctive flat seed pods; commonly called buckler-mustard.
species of plant with yellow flowers and flat pods

Examples

The rocky slopes were dotted with clusters of Biscutalla laevigata in full bloom.
Botanists study Biscutalla laevigata because of its unusual buckler-shaped seed pods.
In southeastern Europe, Biscutalla laevigata often grows alongside other members of the mustard family.

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Biscutella' meaning 'little two-shielded', referring to the shape of the seed pods.
