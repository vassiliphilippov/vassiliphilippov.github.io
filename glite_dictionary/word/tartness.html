tartness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑrtnəs / (AmE), / ˈtɑːtnəs / (BrE)
a sharp, slightly sour taste or flavor, often found in fruits such as lemons or green apples
sharp, sour taste

Examples

The tartness of the lemonade made my mouth pucker.
She enjoyed the tartness of the green apples in her salad.
The recipe balances the tartness of yogurt with a touch of honey.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sweetness - a sweet taste or flavor
  • Synonym: acidity - the quality of being acid or sour

Etymology

From Middle English 'tart', of uncertain origin, + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɑrtnəs / (AmE), / ˈtɑːtnəs / (BrE)
the sharp, acidic quality of taste, like that of vinegar or lemon juice
sharp, acidic taste quality

Examples

The tartness of the lemonade made it especially refreshing on a hot day.
She added a splash of vinegar to increase the tartness of the salad dressing.
Apples with a touch of tartness are perfect for making pies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sourness - having a taste like lemon or vinegar
  • Antonym: sweetness - the quality of being sweet in taste

Etymology

From 'tart' (sharp in taste) + '-ness', Middle English origin.

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɑrtnəs / (AmE), / ˈtɑːtnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being sharp, bitter, or cutting in manner or speech
sharpness or bitterness of manner

Examples

Her tartness in response to his question made everyone in the room pause.
The critic's tartness was evident throughout the harsh review.
Despite his friend's tartness, he appreciated the honesty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acerbity - sharpness or bitterness of tone, temper, or manner
  • Antonym: gentleness - the quality of being kind and mild in manner

Etymology

From Old French 'tart', of Germanic origin, related to 'tart' (sharp in taste or manner).
