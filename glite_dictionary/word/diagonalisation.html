diagonalisation

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌæɡənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / daɪˌaɡənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of transforming a square matrix into a diagonal matrix by a similarity transformation, so that all non-zero elements are on the principal diagonal
process of making a matrix diagonal

Examples

The diagonalisation of a symmetric matrix simplifies many calculations in physics.
An important step in quantum mechanics involves the diagonalisation of certain operators.
Numerical methods exist for efficient diagonalisation of large matrices.

Synset Examples

the diagonalization of a normal matrix by a unitary transformation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diagonalization - alternative spelling; diagonal form in mathematics

Etymology

From 'diagonal' + '-isation'; technical term in mathematics.
