spraying

1. NOUN

/ ˈspreɪ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspreɪ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of dispersing substances such as fertilizers, fungicides, or insecticides onto crops, often from an aircraft
dispersion of substances on crops

Examples

The spraying of pesticides must be carefully monitored to protect nearby wildlife.
Early morning spraying helps ensure the chemicals adhere to the crops.
Aerial spraying is commonly used to cover large fields efficiently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spray - a liquid distributed in small drops from a nozzle or atomizer

2. NOUN

/ ˈspreɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspreɪɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of applying a liquid in the form of fine droplets, usually by means of a sprayer or similar device.
the act of applying liquid as fine droplets

Examples

The spraying of pesticides must be controlled to protect the environment.
After spraying the crops, the farmers wore protective clothing for safety.
Regular spraying of disinfectant helped keep the area clean and germ-free.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spray - liquid sent out in fine droplets; the act of spraying
