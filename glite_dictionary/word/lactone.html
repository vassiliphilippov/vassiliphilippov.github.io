lactone

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæk.toʊn / (AmE), / ˈlæk.təʊn / (BrE)
a cyclic organic compound formed by the intramolecular condensation of a hydroxy acid, creating an internal ester bond
cyclic compound formed from hydroxy acid
medical

Examples

Many antibiotics, such as erythromycin, contain a lactone structure in their chemical composition.
A gamma-lactone has a five-membered ring formed from a hydroxy acid.
Researchers synthesized a new lactone to study its bioactive properties.

Synset Examples

Lactones are formed by intramolecular esterification of the corresponding hydroxycarboxylic acids, which takes place spontaneously when the ring that is formed is five- or six-membered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ester - an organic compound formed by reaction between an acid and an alcohol

Etymology

From French lactone, based on 'lact-' (from Latin lac, 'milk') and '-one', indicating its origin from lactic acid.
