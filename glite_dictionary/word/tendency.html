tendency

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛn.dən.si / (AmE), / ˈtɛn.dən.si / (BrE)
an inclination or natural disposition to behave or develop in a particular way
a natural inclination or tendency

Examples

She has a strong tendency to arrive late for meetings.
The material has a tendency to wrinkle if not ironed properly.
There is a growing tendency among young people to use online learning platforms.

Synset Examples

the alkaline inclination of the local waters
fabric with a tendency to shrink

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inclination - a feeling or tendency to be a certain way or to do something
  • Synonym: propensity - an inclination or natural tendency to behave in a particular way
  • Antonym: aversion - a strong dislike or disinclination

Etymology

From Latin 'tendentia', from 'tendere' meaning 'to stretch',' to tend'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛn.dən.si / (AmE), / ˈtɛn.dən.si / (BrE)
an inclination toward a particular characteristic, type of behavior, or preference; an attitude that favors one alternative over others
an inclination or disposition toward something

Examples

He has a tendency to overthink even the simplest decisions.
Her tendency to arrive late annoyed her colleagues.
There is a tendency among students to procrastinate before exams.

Synset Examples

he had an inclination to give up too easily
a tendency to be too strict

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inclination - a person's natural tendency or urge to act or feel in a particular way
  • Synonym: propensity - an inclination or natural tendency to behave in a particular way

Etymology

From Medieval Latin tendentia, from Latin tendere (to stretch, aim).

4. NOUN

/ ˈtɛndənsi / (AmE), / ˈtɛndənsi / (BrE)
an inclination or likelihood for something to move or develop in a particular direction or way
inclination or direction of movement or development

Examples

The river has a natural tendency to overflow its banks during heavy rain.
She has a tendency to arrive late for meetings.
Current economic data indicate a tendency toward higher inflation rates.

Synset Examples

the shoreward tendency of the current
the trend of the stock market

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trend - a general direction in which something is developing or changing
  • Synonym: propensity - an inclination or natural tendency to behave in a particular way
  • Antonym: aversion - a strong dislike or unwillingness

Etymology

From Latin 'tendere' (to stretch, aim at) via Medieval Latin 'tendentia'.
