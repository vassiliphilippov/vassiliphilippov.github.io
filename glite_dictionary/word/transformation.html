transformation

1. NOUN

/ ˌtrænsfəˈrmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtrænzfəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Genetics) The modification of a cell or bacterium by the uptake and incorporation of external DNA.
genetic modification by DNA uptake
medical

Examples

The scientist induced transformation in the bacterial culture by adding a plasmid containing an antibiotic resistance gene.
Natural transformation allows bacteria to rapidly acquire new traits from their environment.
Gene transformation is commonly used in biotechnology to produce genetically modified organisms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transduction - genetic transfer of DNA by a virus
  • Synonym: conjugation - direct transfer of DNA between bacteria

Etymology

From Latin transformatio (change of shape or form), via transformare (to transform).

5. NOUN

/ ˌtræns.fərˈmeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌtrænz.fəˈmeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
(mathematics) A function that changes the position or direction of the axes of a coordinate system.
mathematical function altering axes

Examples

The transformation rotated the entire graph by 90 degrees.
A reflection is a type of geometric transformation that flips a figure over a line.
Linear algebra studies how a transformation can change a vector's position in space.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mapping - A function that assigns elements of one set to elements of another set, often used as a synonym for transformation in mathematics

Etymology

From Latin transformare (to change in shape or form), via French transformation.
