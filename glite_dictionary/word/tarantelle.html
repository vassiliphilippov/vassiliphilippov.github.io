tarantelle

1. NOUN

/ təˌrænˈtɛl / (AmE), / təˌranˈtɛl / (BrE)
a lively, fast-paced Italian folk dance for two people, characterized by rapid whirling movements
lively Italian folk dance for two

Examples

The troupe performed a spirited tarantelle that delighted the audience.
During the festival, villagers celebrated by dancing the traditional tarantelle in the town square.
She learned the intricate steps of the tarantelle to honor her Italian heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tarantella - a lively Italian folk dance with a fast upbeat tempo

Etymology

From Italian tarantella, named after the Italian city of Taranto.
