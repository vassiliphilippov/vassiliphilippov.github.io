malaprop

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæləˌprɑp / (AmE), / ˈmaləprɒp / (BrE)
An incorrect use of a word in place of one that sounds similar, often with unintentionally amusing effect.
accidental misuse of a similar-sounding word

Examples

The politician's speech was full of funny malaprops that made the audience chuckle.
Her classic malaprop was saying 'pineapple of politeness' instead of 'pinnacle of politeness.'
Teachers sometimes collect students' malaprops as a source of amusement and learning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malapropism - the unintentional misuse of a word by confusion with a similar-sounding one

Etymology

From the character Mrs. Malaprop in Richard Brinsley Sheridan's play 'The Rivals' (1775), whose name is based on the French 'mal à propos' (inappropriate).
