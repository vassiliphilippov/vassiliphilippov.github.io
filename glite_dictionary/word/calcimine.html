calcimine

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælsɪˌmaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkalsɪˌmiːn / (BrE)
A water-based paint containing zinc oxide, glue, and coloring, used especially as a wash for walls and ceilings.
type of water-based wall paint

Examples

The old house had layers of calcimine peeling from the ceilings.
Before repainting, you must wash off any remaining calcimine from the walls.
He purchased a bucket of calcimine to brighten the kitchen ceiling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whitewash - A solution of lime and water used to whiten walls

Etymology

From Latin *calx* (lime) and likely influenced by French *calciné* (calcined).

2. VERB

/ ˈkæl.səˌmaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkalsɪˌmiːn / (BrE)
to cover or coat (a wall or ceiling) with calcimine, a type of whitewash or water-based paint
to coat with calcimine, a whitewash

Examples

We decided to calcimine the kitchen walls to give them a fresh look.
The workers calcimined the ceiling with a new coat of whitewash.
She spent the afternoon calcimining the old, dingy room.

Synset Examples

calcimine the walls

Etymology

From Latin *calx* (lime) and obsolete French *ciment* (cement).
