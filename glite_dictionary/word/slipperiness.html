slipperiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈslɪpəri.nəs / (AmE), / ˈslɪp.ər.i.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being smooth and likely to cause slipping
quality of being slippery

Examples

The slipperiness of the ice made walking extremely dangerous.
He underestimated the slipperiness of the floor after it was cleaned.
Tires are designed to reduce slipperiness on wet roads.

Synset Examples

he could feel the slickness of the tiller

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slickness - the quality of being smooth and slippery
  • Antonym: roughness - the quality of being uneven or not smooth

Etymology

From 'slippery' + '-ness'; 'slippery' from Middle English 'slipery', related to 'slip'.
