passbook

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæsˌbʊk / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsˌbʊk / (BrE)
a booklet given by a bank to an account holder for recording deposits, withdrawals, and interest earned on a savings account
bank booklet for recording transactions

Examples

She took her passbook to the bank to update the balance after depositing her paycheck.
The teller stamped the transaction details into my passbook during each visit.
Many banks now use digital statements instead of a traditional passbook.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bankbook - a booklet for recording bank transactions, especially savings accounts

Etymology

Formed from 'pass' (to hand from one person to another) and 'book'.
