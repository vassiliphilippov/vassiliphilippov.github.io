crying

1. NOUN

/ ˈkraɪ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkraɪ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of shedding tears, typically accompanied by sounds expressing distress or pain
the act of shedding tears

Examples

The sound of crying could be heard from the next room.
She tried to ignore the baby's crying while she finished her work.
His crying stopped once he saw his mother enter the room.

Synset Examples

I hate to hear the crying of a child
she was in tears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weeping - the act of expressing grief or emotion by shedding tears

Etymology

From the verb 'cry' + -ing, dating to Middle English.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkraɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkraɪɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a state or action) shockingly bad, outrageous, or deserving strong condemnation; used to emphasize the seriousness of a situation, often in fixed phrases like 'a crying shame'.
shockingly bad; deserving strong condemnation

Examples

It's a crying shame that the historic building will be demolished.
The decision to ignore the evidence was a crying injustice.
Many believed it was a crying disgrace to treat the workers so unfairly.

Synset Examples

a crying shame
an egregious lie
flagrant violation of human rights
a glaring error
gross ineptitude
gross injustice
rank treachery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: egregious - outstandingly bad; shocking
  • Synonym: flagrant - conspicuously offensive or bad
  • Degree Lower: minor - not serious; small in importance

3. ADJ

/ ˈkraɪ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkraɪ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a problem, need, etc.) demanding urgent attention or action.
demanding urgent attention

Examples

There is a crying need for more affordable housing in the city.
Some schools are in crying need of repairs and better equipment.
It is a crying shame that so many children go hungry in a wealthy country.

Synset Examples

clamant needs
a crying need
regarded literary questions as exigent and momentous
insistent hunger
an instant need

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urgent - requiring immediate action or attention
  • Synonym: pressing - requiring quick or immediate action; urgent

Etymology

From the verb 'cry', ultimately from Old French 'crier', from Latin 'quiritare' (to cry out).
