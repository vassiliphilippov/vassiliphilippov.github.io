bibliopolist

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɪblioʊˈpɑlɪst / (AmE), / ˌbɪblɪˈɒpəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who sells secondhand or rare books, especially as a professional dealer
seller of secondhand or rare books

Examples

The bibliopolist had a shop filled with leather-bound first editions and other literary treasures.
A skilled bibliopolist can track down even the rarest manuscripts for passionate collectors.
She went to the old district known for its bibliopolists, hoping to find an out-of-print novel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bookseller - a person who sells books

Etymology

From Greek *biblion* (book) + *pōlḗs* (seller), via Late Latin *bibliopolista*.
