Amphineura

1. NOUN

/ ˌæm.fəˈnjuː.rə / (AmE), / ˌam.fɪˈnjʊə.rə / (BrE)
a former taxonomic class of molluscs in the phylum Mollusca, comprising chitons and related forms
class of molluscs (chitons, etc.)

Examples

The Amphineura were once classified as a distinct class within the phylum Mollusca.
Recent studies have questioned the traditional grouping of Amphineura.
Chitons, which belong to the Amphineura, display a unique shell structure.

Etymology

From Greek 'amphi-' (on both sides) and 'neuron' (nerve), referring to the arrangement of nerve cords.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæmfəˈnuːrə / (AmE), / ˌæmfɪˈnjʊərə / (BrE)
a former subclass of mollusks, comprising chitons and related organisms, within the phylum Mollusca, now often divided into Polyplacophora and Aplacophora.
historical subclass of mollusks

Examples

The subclass Amphineura is no longer commonly used in modern taxonomy.
Researchers studied the nervous systems of Amphineura to understand mollusk evolution.
Members of Amphineura include chitons, which have unique shell structures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Polyplacophora - the class of chitons, now classified separately from Amphineura

Etymology

From Greek 'amphi-' (both) + 'neuron' (nerve), referring to their bilaterally arranged nerve cords.
