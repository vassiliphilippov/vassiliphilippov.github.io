Humulus

1. PROPN

/ ˈhjuːmjələs / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmjʊləs / (BrE)
a genus of perennial climbing plants, including hops, native to Europe, Asia, and North America, valued especially for their use in brewing beer
genus of climbing plants including hops

Examples

The genus Humulus includes the well-known hops used in brewing.
Researchers studied wild Humulus species found in central Asia.
Cultivation of Humulus plants has become increasingly important for the craft beer industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hop - the flowers of the Humulus plant used in brewing

Etymology

From Latin *humulus* (hop plant), of Germanic origin.
