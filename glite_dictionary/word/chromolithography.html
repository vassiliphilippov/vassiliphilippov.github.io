chromolithography

1. NOUN

/ ˌkroʊmoʊlɪˈθɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌkrəʊməʊlɪˈθɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
A method of printing in which each color is applied using a separate lithographic stone or plate, allowing the production of multi-colored images.
multi-color lithographic printing process

Examples

The museum's collection includes several stunning examples of chromolithography from the 19th century.
Artists used chromolithography to create vibrant posters and advertisements before the advent of digital printing.
Learning about chromolithography helped her appreciate the complexity behind old illustrated books.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lithography - printing from a flat surface using a greasy substance and water

Etymology

From Greek 'chrōma' (color) and 'lithographia' (stone printing).
