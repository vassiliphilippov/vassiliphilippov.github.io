mediate

1. VERB

/ ˈmiːdiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈmiːdiˌeɪt / (BrE)
to occupy an intermediate or middle position or to serve as a connecting link or stage between two things
be intermediate or a link between two things

Examples

This cultural movement mediates between traditional customs and modern practices.
The book mediates the gap between scientific research and public understanding.
A layer of air mediates between the ocean and the atmosphere.

Synset Examples

mediate between the old and the new

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intervene - to come between so as to prevent or alter a result or course of events
  • Antonym: separate - to set or keep apart

Etymology

From Latin 'mediatus', past participle of 'mediare' (to be in the middle), from 'medius' (middle).

Synset POS: v

2. ADJ

/ ˈmiːdiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈmiːdiˌeɪt / (BrE)
acting or occurring through an intervening agent or means rather than directly
acting through an intervening agency

Examples

The virus can be transmitted by mediate contact, such as through contaminated objects.
Some chemicals exert their effects in a mediate way, involving several biological steps.
Unlike direct communication, mediate communication often relies on third-party technologies.

Synset Examples

the disease spread by mediate as well as direct contact

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immediate - acting or occurring directly, without any intervening agency

Etymology

From Latin 'mediatus', past participle of 'mediare' (to be in the middle).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00774315-a
Synset POS: a

3. VERB

/ ˈmiːdiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈmiːdieɪt / (BrE)
to act as an intermediary in order to resolve a disagreement or bring about an agreement between conflicting parties
act as intermediary to resolve disputes

Examples

The manager was asked to mediate in the dispute between employees.
She skillfully mediated a compromise that satisfied both parties.
He is often called upon to mediate disagreements in the community.

Synset Examples

He interceded in the family dispute
He mediated a settlement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to arbitrate - to officially settle a dispute between two parties
  • Synonym: to intervene - to become involved in a situation, especially to improve or prevent it from getting worse
  • Antonym: to exacerbate - to make a situation or a conflict worse

Etymology

From Latin mediāri (to be in the middle), from medius (middle).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00763001-v
Synset POS: v
