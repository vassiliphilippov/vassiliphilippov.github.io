emendation

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːˌmɛnˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌiːˌmɛnˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
a correction made to a text, especially as a result of scholarly editing
a correction to a text

Examples

The scholar's emendation clarified the meaning of the ancient manuscript.
After reviewing the translation, several emendations were incorporated to improve accuracy.
Critical emendation often requires a deep understanding of the original language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: correction - the act of making something right

Etymology

From Latin emendatio (correction, improvement), from emendare (to correct).
