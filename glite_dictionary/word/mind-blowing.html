mind-blowing

1. ADJ

/ ˈmaɪndˌbloʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪndˌbləʊɪŋ / (BrE)
Extremely impressive, surprising, or shocking; intensely affecting the mind or emotions.
extremely impressive or astonishing
informal

Examples

The documentary offered a mind-blowing look into the universe.
Her performance was so mind-blowing that the audience gave a standing ovation.
It was a mind-blowing discovery that changed the course of science.

Synset Examples

spending a week in the jungle was a mind-blowing experience
a mind-blowing horror story

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astonishing - Very surprising; difficult to believe
  • Antonym: unimpressive - Not especially striking or remarkable

Etymology

Formed from "mind" + present participle of "blow"; originated in 1960s American slang.
