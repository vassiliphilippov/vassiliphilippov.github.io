Sphyrapicus varius

1. NOUN

/ sfaɪrəˈpaɪkəs ˈvɛəriəs / (AmE), / sfiːrəˈpaɪkəs ˈvɛəriəs / (BrE)
A species of woodpecker native to eastern North America, commonly known as the yellow-bellied sapsucker, recognizable by its pale yellow abdomen.
a North American woodpecker species

Examples

The Sphyrapicus varius drills neat rows of holes in tree bark to feed on sap and insects.
During migration, birdwatchers often spot the Sphyrapicus varius in mixed woodlands.
Researchers have studied nesting habits of the Sphyrapicus varius in Canadian forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellow-bellied sapsucker - a common name for Sphyrapicus varius, a North American woodpecker

Etymology

From Greek sphura (hammer) and pikos (woodpecker); Latin varius (variegated or mottled).
