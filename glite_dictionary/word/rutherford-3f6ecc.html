rutherford

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌðərˌfɔrd / (AmE), / ˈrʌðəfəd / (BrE)
a former unit of radioactivity equal to one million disintegrations per second
unit of radioactivity (1 million disintegrations/sec)

Examples

The sample was measured to emit radioactivity of 2 rutherfords.
This isotope decays at a rate of one rutherford.
Although the rutherford is an obsolete unit, it is still found in older scientific literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: becquerel - SI unit of radioactivity (one disintegration per second)

Etymology

Named after Ernest Rutherford, the New Zealand-born physicist.
