solubleness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːljəbəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɒljʊblnəs / (BrE)
The quality or property of being able to be dissolved, especially in a liquid.
ability to be dissolved

Examples

The solubleness of salt in water makes it easy to prepare saline solutions.
Different chemicals have different levels of solubleness depending on temperature and pressure.
The chemist was interested in the solubleness of the new compound in organic solvents.

Synset Examples

he measure the dissolubility of sugar in water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solubility - the ability of a substance to dissolve in a solvent
  • Antonym: insolubility - the inability of a substance to dissolve

Etymology

Formed from 'soluble' (from Latin 'solubilis', able to be dissolved) with the suffix '-ness'.
