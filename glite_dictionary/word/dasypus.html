Dasypus

1. PROPN

/ ˈdæ.sɪ.pəs / (AmE), / ˈdæsɪpəs / (BrE)
A genus of armadillos, commonly known as the long-nosed armadillos, and the type genus of the family Dasypodidae.
genus of long-nosed armadillos

Examples

Among the various species of Dasypus, the nine-banded armadillo is the most widespread in the Americas.
Researchers discovered a new member of the genus Dasypus in South America.
The Dasypus genus is notable for its long snout and protective armor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armadillo - nocturnal armored mammal native to the Americas

Etymology

From Greek dasypous, meaning 'hairy-footed'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdæsɪpəs / (AmE), / ˈdasɪpəs / (BrE)
A genus of armadillos in the family Dasypodidae, known for species such as the nine-banded armadillo.
genus of armadillos

Examples

The nine-banded armadillo, a member of the genus Dasypus, is found throughout the Americas.
Scientists discovered a new species within Dasypus during their expedition in South America.
The distinctive characteristics of Dasypus set it apart from other armadillo genera.

Etymology

From Greek dasypous, meaning 'hairy-footed'.
