childlike

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃaɪldˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪldˌlaɪk / (BrE)
(of an adult or their qualities) having the good qualities, such as innocence or trust, that are typical of a child.
having qualities of a child (innocence/trust)

Examples

Her childlike curiosity made her a joy to work with.
He viewed the world with a childlike sense of wonder.
The artist captured a childlike innocence in her paintings.

Synset Examples

childlike charm

Related Terms

  • Antonym: childish - (of an adult or their behavior) silly or immature; typical of a child in a negative way

2. ADJ

/ ˈtʃaɪldˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪldˌlaɪk / (BrE)
(of an adult) having qualities associated with a child, such as innocence, trust, or simplicity.
innocent or simple like a child

Examples

Her childlike enthusiasm was infectious and made the experience more enjoyable for everyone.
He looked at the painting with a childlike sense of wonder.
Despite her age, she maintained a childlike trust in the goodness of people.

Synset Examples

childlike trust
dewy-eyed innocence
listened in round-eyed wonder

Related Terms

  • Antonym: childish - typical of a child, often in a negative sense (silly or immature)

Etymology

Formed from 'child' + 'like', meaning resembling a child.
