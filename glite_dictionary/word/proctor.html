proctor

1. VERB

/ ˈprɑk.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈprɒk.tə / (BrE)
to supervise or monitor students during an examination to prevent cheating
supervise an examination

Examples

It is the responsibility of the teacher to proctor the final exams this semester.
Graduate students often proctor undergraduate tests as part of their duties.
She proctored the standardized test to ensure all rules were followed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to invigilate - to supervise students during an examination (chiefly British)

Etymology

From Latin 'procurator' (manager, agent), via Middle English and Anglo-Norman.

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːktɚ / (AmE), / ˈprɒktə / (BrE)
a person who supervises an examination to prevent cheating and ensure rules are followed
exam supervisor

Examples

The proctor walked quietly around the room, observing the students as they took their exams.
If you have any questions during the test, raise your hand and wait for a proctor.
Each classroom needs a proctor to monitor the final examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invigilator - a person who supervises candidates during an examination (mainly British)

Etymology

From Middle English prokitour, shortened from 'procurator' (Latin procurator, agent, manager).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10498545-n
Synset POS: n
