sausage

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔsɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsɒsɪdʒ / (BrE)
A food made from finely minced and seasoned meat stuffed into a casing, typically cylindrical in shape.
seasoned meat stuffed in casing

Examples

He cooked a delicious breakfast of eggs and sausage.
German sausage varieties include bratwurst and frankfurter.
The sausage was sliced and added to the pasta sauce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hot dog - a cooked sausage served in a sliced bun, often with condiments

Etymology

From Old French *saussiche*, from Latin *salsicia*, from *salsus* (salted).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɔsɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsɒsɪdʒ / (BrE)
a small, nonrigid airship, often used for observation or as a barrage balloon, typically shaped like a sausage
nonrigid airship or barrage balloon

Examples

During World War I, a sausage was often deployed to spot enemy movements from above.
The distinctive shape of the sausage airship made it easy to recognize from the ground.
Observers rode in the gondola attached beneath the sausage to relay information back to command.
