Lecanoraceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛkəˌnɔːˈreɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌlɛkənɒˈreɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of lichen-forming fungi in the division Lichenes, characterized by disc-shaped fruiting bodies
family of lichen-forming fungi

Examples

The Lecanoraceae family includes many of the common crustose lichens found on rocks and trees.
Researchers discovered a new species belonging to the Lecanoraceae in the Arctic tundra.
Members of the Lecanoraceae are known for their ecological importance in nutrient cycling.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Lecanora, itself from Greek lekanē (dish, basin).
