FLNC

1. NOUN

/ ɛf ɛl ɛn si / (AmE), / ɛf ɛl ɛn si / (BrE)
an armed separatist group advocating for Corsican independence, founded in 1976 and known for acts of sabotage and violence on Corsica
Corsican separatist organization (founded 1976)

Examples

The FLNC claimed responsibility for several bomb attacks in Corsica last year.
French authorities have struggled to negotiate with the FLNC over the future of Corsican autonomy.
Many locals are wary of the FLNC's tactics but support the idea of more self-government.

Etymology

Acronym of French 'Fronte di Liberazione Naziunale Corsu' (National Liberation Front of Corsica).
