lovely

1. ADJ

/ ˈlʌv.li / (AmE), / ˈlʌv.li / (BrE)
Endearing or delightful in a way that is reminiscent of childlike innocence or naivety.
endearing in a childlike or naive way

Examples

The puppy's lovely face charmed everyone who met her.
She gave a lovely smile, full of innocence and joy.
His lovely way of speaking made even strangers feel at ease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adorable - extremely charming or lovable, especially in a childlike way

Etymology

From Middle English 'lovelich', from Old English 'luflic' (worthy of love).

2. ADJ

/ ˈlʌvli / (AmE), / ˈlʌvli / (BrE)
Very attractive or beautiful in a way that appeals to the emotions as well as the eye.
very attractive or beautiful

Examples

She wore a lovely dress to the party that made everyone notice her.
The garden looks especially lovely in the early morning light.
It was a lovely gesture when he brought flowers for her birthday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beautiful - pleasing the senses or mind aesthetically
  • Synonym: charming - pleasant or attractive in a delightful way
  • Antonym: ugly - unpleasant to look at; not attractive

Etymology

Old English *luflic*, from *lufu* (love) + *-lic* (-ly, having the qualities of).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
