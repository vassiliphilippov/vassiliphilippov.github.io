furcula

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɝːkjələ / (AmE), / ˈfɜːkjʊlə / (BrE)
A forked bone formed by the fusion of the clavicles, found in most birds and some other animals, commonly known as the wishbone.
forked bone in birds (wishbone)

Examples

The archaeologist discovered the furcula of an ancient bird species at the dig site.
Birds use their furcula to strengthen their skeleton during flight.
Chicken furcula are often used as wishbones during holiday meals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wishbone - the forked bone in birds, especially the chicken, traditionally used for making wishes

Etymology

From Latin 'furcula', diminutive of 'furca' (fork).
