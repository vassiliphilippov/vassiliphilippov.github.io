Andy Warhol

1. PROPN

/ ˈændi ˈwɔrˌhɑl / (AmE), / ˈandi ˈwɔːˌhɒl / (BrE)
(1930–1987) An American artist, film-maker, and leading figure of the Pop Art movement, known for his works depicting mass-produced consumer goods and celebrities.
famous American Pop Art artist (1930–1987)

Examples

Many museums showcase Andy Warhol's iconic Campbell's Soup Cans paintings.
The influence of Andy Warhol can be seen in contemporary pop culture and advertising.
When studying Pop Art, Andy Warhol is often mentioned alongside Roy Lichtenstein.
