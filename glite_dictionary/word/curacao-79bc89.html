curacao

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɜrəˈsoʊ / (AmE), / ˌkyʊərəˈsaʊ / (BrE)
a liqueur flavored with the peel of bitter or sour oranges, often used in cocktails
orange-flavored liqueur

Examples

He ordered a cocktail made with curacao and lemon juice.
The bright blue curacao gave the drink its striking color.
You can substitute orange liqueur for curacao in this recipe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triple sec - a type of orange-flavored liqueur

Etymology

From Curaçao, the Caribbean island where the drink originated; influenced by Spanish and Dutch usage.
