Equetus

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈkwiːtəs / (AmE), / ɪˈkwiːtəs / (BrE)
a genus of tropical marine drumfish, known for their elongated dorsal fins and striped patterns, found in the western Atlantic Ocean
genus of tropical drumfish

Examples

The aquarium has a beautiful specimen of the Equetus in its marine exhibit.
Marine biologists study the unique patterns on Equetus species to understand their evolutionary history.
Juvenile Equetus are often found hiding among coral reefs for protection.

Etymology

From Latin 'equus' (horse), likely referring to the elongated dorsal fin resembling a horse's mane.
