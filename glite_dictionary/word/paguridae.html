Paguridae

1. NOUN

/ pəˈɡjʊrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / pəˈɡjʊərɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of decapod crustaceans commonly known as hermit crabs, characterized by their habit of inhabiting empty shells for protection.
family of hermit crabs

Examples

The Paguridae family includes many species of hermit crabs found in coastal waters worldwide.
Researchers studied the behavior of Paguridae in selecting suitable shells for protection.
Shell morphology often varies among different genera within the Paguridae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hermit crab - a decapod crustacean that inhabits empty shells

Etymology

From New Latin, based on 'Pagurus', the type genus, from Greek 'pagouros' (hermit crab).
