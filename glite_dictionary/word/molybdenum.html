molybdenum

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɪbdəˌnum / (AmE), / məˈlɪbdɪnəm / (BrE)
a chemical element (symbol Mo), a hard, silvery metal used to strengthen steel and other alloys
a chemical element; metallic element used in alloys

Examples

Many industrial machines use steel alloys containing molybdenum for added strength and durability.
The chemical properties of molybdenum make it valuable for use in electrical contacts and filaments.
Researchers are studying how molybdenum can improve corrosion resistance in marine environments.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin molybdenum, from Greek molybdos (lead), due to its similarity to lead ores.
