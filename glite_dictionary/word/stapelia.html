Stapelia

1. NOUN

/ stəˈpiːliə / (AmE), / stəˈpiːlɪə / (BrE)
a genus of succulent plants with star-shaped, often foul-smelling flowers, native to southern Africa and parts of India, resembling cacti in appearance.
a genus of foul-smelling succulent plants

Examples

The star-shaped flowers of Stapelia attract flies with their foul odor.
Botanists often study Stapelia for its unique pollination mechanisms.
A single Stapelia bloom can fill a greenhouse with an unpleasant smell.

Etymology

Named after Dutch botanist Johannes van Stapel (1602–1636).
