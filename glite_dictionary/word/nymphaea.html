Nymphaea

1. NOUN

/ nɪmˈfiːə / (AmE), / nɪmˈfiːə / (BrE)
a genus of aquatic plants known as water lilies, belonging to the family Nymphaeaceae
genus of aquatic water lilies

Examples

The pond was filled with blooming Nymphaea of various colors.
Botanists often study Nymphaea as representative water plants.
A single white Nymphaea flower floated serenely in the middle of the lake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water lily - an aquatic plant with large floating leaves and showy flowers

Etymology

From Latin *nymphaea*, from Greek *nymphaia* (water lily), relating to the nymphs of classical mythology.

2. NOUN

/ nɪmˈfiːə / (AmE), / nɪmˈfiːə / (BrE)
a genus of aquatic flowering plants known as water lilies, serving as the type genus of the family Nymphaeaceae
genus of water lilies

Examples

The Nymphaea is admired for its striking, fragrant blossoms on ponds and lakes.
Botanists discovered a new species within the Nymphaea genus in the Amazon basin.
Many varieties of Nymphaea are cultivated in water gardens around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water lily - an aquatic plant with broad floating leaves and showy flowers

Etymology

From Latin 'nymphaea', from Greek 'nymphaia', relating to nymphs (mythological water spirits), referencing the aquatic habitat.
