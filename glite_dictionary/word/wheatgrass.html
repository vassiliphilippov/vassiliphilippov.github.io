wheatgrass

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːtˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈwiːtˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a perennial grass of the genus Agropyron, sometimes used as a nutritional supplement in juice or powder form
a type of grass, often used as a health supplement

Examples

Many people add wheatgrass to their smoothies for its supposed health benefits.
The bar offered fresh wheatgrass shots to customers seeking a nutritional boost.
She grows wheatgrass in her kitchen to juice every morning.

Etymology

From 'wheat' + 'grass', due to its wheat-like appearance.
