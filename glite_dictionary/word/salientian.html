salientian

1. ADJ

/ sæliˈɛnʃən / (AmE), / sæliˈɛnʃən / (BrE)
relating to frogs and toads; pertaining to members of the order Anura (frogs and toads).
relating to frogs and toads

Examples

The researcher focused on the salientian features distinguishing frogs from other amphibians.
Several salientian species have adapted to both aquatic and terrestrial environments.
Her thesis explores the evolution of salientian locomotion.

Etymology

From Latin 'salire' (to leap), via modern taxonomic use in Salientia, former name for the order Anura.

2. NOUN

/ seɪˈliːn.ti.ən / (AmE), / seɪˈliːn.ti.ən / (BrE)
Any member of a group of tailless, stout-bodied amphibians with long hind limbs for leaping, including frogs and toads.
tailless leaping amphibian (frog or toad)
medical

Examples

The museum's collection includes several rare salientian specimens from South America.
Many salientian species have adapted to both aquatic and terrestrial habitats.
Biologists studied the evolution of salientians to understand how frogs and toads diversified.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frog - a tailless amphibian with smooth skin and long legs for leaping
  • Synonym: toad - a tailless amphibian with dry, warty skin

Etymology

From Latin *salīre* (to leap), referencing their jumping ability.
