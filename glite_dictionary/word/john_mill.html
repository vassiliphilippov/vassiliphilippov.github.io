John Mill

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn mɪl / (AmE), / dʒɒn mɪl / (BrE)
(1806–1873) English philosopher and economist celebrated for his interpretations of empiricism and utilitarianism.
English philosopher and economist, 1806–1873

Examples

Many scholars continue to study John Mill's contributions to liberal political theory.
The works of John Mill significantly shaped modern views on utilitarianism.
Several university courses feature John Mill in their philosophy curriculum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empiricism - the theory that all knowledge is derived from sense-experience
  • Synonym: utilitarianism - the doctrine that actions are right if they are useful or for the benefit of a majority
