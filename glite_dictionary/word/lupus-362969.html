lupus

1. NOUN

/ ˈluː.pəs / (AmE), / ˈluː.pəs / (BrE)
A chronic autoimmune disease, primarily marked by inflammation and ulcerative lesions of the skin, but which can also affect other organs.
autoimmune disease with skin lesions
medical

Examples

She was diagnosed with lupus after several months of unexplained skin rashes and joint pain.
Doctors are still unsure what causes lupus, but it is believed to involve the immune system attacking healthy tissue.
Some people with lupus experience only mild skin problems, while others may suffer more serious symptoms.

Etymology

From Latin lupus (wolf), referring to the appearance of skin lesions.
