self-induced

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlf ɪnˈdust / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf ɪnˈdjuːst / (BrE)
(of a phenomenon, especially in electricity) produced by self-induction within a system or device.
produced by self-induction (electricity)

Examples

The circuit was damaged due to a self-induced voltage surge.
Researchers studied the effects of self-induced currents within the transformer coil.
A self-induced electromagnetic field can lead to oscillations in the system.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: induced - produced by an external cause, especially in electricity
