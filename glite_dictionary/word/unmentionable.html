unmentionable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈmɛnʃənəbl̩ / (AmE), / ʌnˈmɛnʃənəbl̩ / (BrE)
(of something) unsuitable or forbidden as a topic of conversation; taboo or not to be discussed.
not suitable to discuss; taboo

Examples

The politician avoided any unmentionable topics during the debate.
Certain unmentionable words were censored from the broadcast.
They whispered about past scandals, careful not to mention anything truly unmentionable.

Synset Examples

unmentionable words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taboo - prohibited or restricted by social custom

Etymology

From un- (not) + mention + -able.

2. NOUN

/ ʌnˈmɛn(t)ʃ(ə)nəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈmɛnʃ(ə)nəbəl / (BrE)
(humorous, dated) An item of underwear, especially one considered embarrassing or inappropriate to mention directly.
(dated) item of underwear

Examples

She was embarrassed when her unmentionables were visible on the laundry line.
The comedian made a joke about losing his unmentionables at summer camp.
Victorian novels often referred to ladies' underwear as unmentionables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: underwear - clothing worn under outer clothes, typically next to the skin

Etymology

From un- (not) + mentionable, originally referring to something not to be spoken of.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04515458-n
Synset POS: n
