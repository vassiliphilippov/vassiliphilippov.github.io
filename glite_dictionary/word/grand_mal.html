grand mal

1. NOUN

/ ɡrænd ˈmɑl / (AmE), / ɡrɑːn ˈmɑːl / (BrE)
A type of epilepsy in which seizures involve sudden loss of consciousness followed by generalized muscle spasms and jerking movements.
severe type of epileptic seizure
medical

Examples

After his diagnosis, the doctor explained what a grand mal seizure might look like.
Witnessing a grand mal can be frightening due to the intense muscle spasms.
Patients with uncontrolled epilepsy are at higher risk for a grand mal episode.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: petit mal - a mild form of epilepsy with brief, sudden lapses in attention

Etymology

French, literally 'great illness'.

2. NOUN

/ ɡræn ˈmɑl / (AmE), / ɡrɑːn ˈmɑːl / (BrE)
A type of epileptic seizure characterized by loss of consciousness and violent muscle contractions affecting the entire body.
type of epileptic seizure with convulsions
medical

Examples

The patient experienced a grand mal seizure in the emergency room.
After his first grand mal episode, doctors prescribed anti-epileptic medication.
Witnesses described the grand mal convulsions lasting for several minutes before help arrived.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: petit mal - a mild form of epileptic seizure without convulsions

Etymology

From French 'grand mal' meaning 'great illness.'
