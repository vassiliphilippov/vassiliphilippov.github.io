Filoviridae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfaɪloʊˈvɪrɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌfɪləʊˈvɪrɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of threadlike RNA viruses that cause severe diseases in humans and other primates, including Ebola and Marburg viruses
family of threadlike RNA viruses
medical

Examples

The Ebola virus is a member of the Filoviridae family, known for its filamentous structure.
Outbreaks caused by Filoviridae viruses have resulted in high mortality rates in Africa.
Researchers are developing vaccines targeting Filoviridae to prevent future pandemics.

Etymology

From New Latin: 'filum' (thread) + 'viridae' (virus family name suffix).
