saprolite

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæp.rəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈsæp.rə.laɪt / (BrE)
A soft, clay-rich, weathered rock that remains in its original place of formation, formed by the chemical breakdown of bedrock.
weathered clay-rich rock in original place

Examples

The mining company encountered a thick layer of saprolite during drilling operations.
Gold can sometimes be recovered from saprolite deposits because the rock is easily broken up.
In tropical regions, saprolite forms as a result of intense weathering over millions of years.

Etymology

From Greek sapros (rotten) + lithos (stone), referring to decayed stone.
