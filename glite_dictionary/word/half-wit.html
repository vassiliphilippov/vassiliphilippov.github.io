half-wit

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæfˌwɪt / (AmE), / ˈhɑːfˌwɪt / (BrE)
an offensive term for a person who is considered to have subnormal intelligence or to be foolish
a person of subnormal intelligence (offensive)
offensive

Examples

He dismissed his opponent as a half-wit unworthy of serious debate.
Calling someone a half-wit is considered highly rude and offensive today.
The play featured a comic character portrayed as a lovable half-wit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool - a person lacking good sense or judgment
  • Synonym: idiot - an offensive term for a person with very low intelligence
  • Antonym: genius - an exceptionally intelligent person

Etymology

Formed from 'half' and 'wit', where 'wit' means intelligence or mental capacity.
