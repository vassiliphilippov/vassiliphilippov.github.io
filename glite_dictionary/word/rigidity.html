rigidity

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈdʒɪdəti / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɪdəti / (BrE)
The quality of being strict, uncompromising, or inflexible in rules or behavior.
strictness; inflexibility

Examples

The manager's rigidity in enforcing the new policies left employees no room for flexibility.
His rigidity about deadlines frustrated the more creative members of the team.
Some critics point out the rigidity of the school's disciplinary approach.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flexibility - the quality of being adaptable or variable
  • Synonym: severity - the quality of being very harsh or strict

Etymology

From Latin rigiditas, from rigidus (stiff, unyielding).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈdʒɪdəti / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɪdəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being stiff, inflexible, and resistant to bending or deformation
stiffness; resistance to bending

Examples

The rigidity of the steel beams makes the structure very stable.
High rigidity is important in materials used for building bridges.
The scientist explained that glass has greater rigidity than rubber.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flexibility - the quality of bending easily without breaking
  • Synonym: stiffness - the state of being rigid or not easily bent

Etymology

From Latin rigiditas, from rigidus (stiff, hard).
