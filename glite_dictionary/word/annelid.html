annelid

1. ADJ

/ ˈæn.ə.lɪd / (AmE), / ˈan.ɪ.lɪd / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of worms of the phylum Annelida.
relating to segmented worms (Annelida)
medical

Examples

The annelid nervous system is more complex than that of flatworms.
Researchers studied the regeneration ability of annelid species in controlled environments.
The earthworm is a commonly known annelid organism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annelid - a worm belonging to the phylum Annelida

Etymology

From Latin 'anellus' (a little ring).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæn.ə.lɪd / (AmE), / ˈæn.əl.ɪd / (BrE)
a worm with a long, cylindrical body that is divided into segments, belonging to the phylum Annelida
segmented worm of the phylum Annelida

Examples

The earthworm is a well-known example of an annelid found in gardens.
Marine biologists study various species of annelids to understand their ecological roles.
Leeches are also classified as annelids because of their segmented bodies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worm - an elongated, soft-bodied invertebrate animal

Etymology

From Latin 'anellus' (little ring), referring to the worm’s segmented body.

Synset POS: n
