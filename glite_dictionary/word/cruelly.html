cruelly

1. ADV

/ ˈkruːəli / (AmE), / ˈkruːəli / (BrE)
In a way that deliberately causes pain or suffering to others; with cruelty.
in a cruel manner

Examples

The dictator governed his people cruelly for decades, showing no mercy.
She was cruelly teased at school because of her accent.
The dog was cruelly abandoned by its previous owners.

Synset Examples

he treated his students cruelly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mercifully - in a merciful manner, with compassion or leniency

Etymology

From 'cruel' + adverbial suffix '-ly', from Old French 'cruel', from Latin 'crudelis'.

2. ADV

/ ˈkruːəli / (AmE), / ˈkruːəli / (BrE)
to an excessive or extreme degree; very intensely
extremely; very intensely

Examples

The team was cruelly unlucky to lose the match in the final seconds.
This year, the summer heat was cruelly oppressive.
The novel's ending is cruelly sad, leaving readers deeply moved.

Synset Examples

a cruelly bitter winter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extremely - to a very great degree
  • Antonym: mildly - to a small degree

Etymology

From Middle English cruely, from Old French cruele, from Latin crūdēlitās (cruelty).
