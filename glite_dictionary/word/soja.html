soja

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊ.dʒə / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ.dʒə / (BrE)
an annual bushy plant native to Asia, cultivated for its oil-rich seeds and used in food, forage, and soil improvement; also known as the soybean
annual plant; soybean

Examples

The farmer decided to plant soja in his fields to improve the soil quality.
Researchers are studying the oil content of soja seeds for new food applications.
In some regions, soja is a primary crop important for both livestock and human consumption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soybean - the plant Glycine max, cultivated for its edible seeds

Etymology

From Japanese 'shōyu' (soy sauce) and Latinized as 'soja' in botanical names.
