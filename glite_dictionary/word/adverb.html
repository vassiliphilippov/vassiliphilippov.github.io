adverb

1. NOUN

/ ˈæd.vɝːb / (AmE), / ˈæd.vɜːb / (BrE)
A word that modifies a verb, adjective, or another adverb to express manner, place, time, or degree.
word that modifies verbs, adjectives, adverbs

Examples

In the sentence 'He quickly ran home,' the word quickly is an adverb.
The teacher explained that an adverb can answer questions like how, when, and where.
Not every word ending in '-ly' is an adverb, but many are.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adjective - a word that modifies a noun or pronoun

Etymology

From Latin adverbium, from ad- (to) + verbum (word).

2. NOUN

/ ˈædˌvɝb / (AmE), / ˈædvɜːb / (BrE)
A word that modifies a verb, an adjective, or another adverb, often indicating manner, time, place, or degree.
word class modifying verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs

Examples

Quickly is an example of an adverb modifying the verb 'run'.
In the sentence 'She sings beautifully', 'beautifully' functions as an adverb.
The adverb often comes after the verb it modifies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adjective - word class that modifies nouns
  • Antonym: noun - word class identifying people, places, or things
  • Antonym: verb - word class describing actions or states

Etymology

From Latin 'adverbium', from 'ad' (to) + 'verbum' (word; verb).

Synset POS: n
