indisputability

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndɪˌspjuːtəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˌspjuːtəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being impossible to dispute or doubt; unquestionableness.
quality of being undisputable

Examples

The indisputability of the evidence convinced everyone of his innocence.
She prided herself on the indisputability of her research findings.
Historians debate many facts, but the indisputability of the king's death remains.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disputability - the quality of being open to dispute or doubt

Etymology

From indisputable + -ity; indisputable from Latin in- (not) + disputabilis (able to be disputed).
