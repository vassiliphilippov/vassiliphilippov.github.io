muton

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjuˌtɑn / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtɒn / (BrE)
the smallest unit of a gene that can undergo mutation
smallest unit in DNA where mutation occurs
medical

Examples

A single muton can sometimes be changed by exposure to radiation.
Geneticists study how mutations in a muton impact protein function.
The concept of the muton was introduced during the early days of molecular genetics.

Etymology

From Latin 'mutare' (to change) with scientific '-on' suffix.
