Larrea tridentata

1. NOUN

/ ləˈriː.ə traɪˈdɛn.tə.tə / (AmE), / ləˈriːə traɪˈdɛn.tətə / (BrE)
A desert shrub of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, with resinous aromatic foliage and small yellow flowers, commonly known as the creosote bush.
desert shrub known as creosote bush

Examples

Many animals in the desert rely on the Larrea tridentata for shade and food.
The distinctive smell of Larrea tridentata fills the air after a desert rain.
Botanists study Larrea tridentata to understand how plants survive in arid climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creosote bush - common name for Larrea tridentata

Etymology

From Latin; Larrea after Spanish botanist J.A. Larrea, tridentata meaning 'three-toothed.'
