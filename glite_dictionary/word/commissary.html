commissary

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.məˌseri / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ɪ.səri / (BrE)
a snack bar or cafeteria, especially one in a film studio or other workplace, where meals and snacks are served to staff.
food service area in a workplace, especially a film studio

Examples

Lunch is usually served at the commissary on the studio lot.
The actors relaxed over coffee in the commissary between takes.
She met the director for a quick meeting at the commissary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cafeteria - self-service restaurant in a school or workplace
  • Synonym: canteen - a place in a school or workplace where food and drinks are served

Etymology

From French commissaire (commissioner), later used in American English for supply offices and canteens.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.məˌseri / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ɪ.sə.ri / (BrE)
a store on a military base that sells food and other supplies to military personnel
military base store for personnel

Examples

The new commissary on base offers fresh produce and household items to military families.
He stopped by the commissary after work to pick up groceries for the week.
Military personnel often find better prices at the commissary than at stores off base.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: PX - post exchange; a store on a military base
  • Degree Higher: supermarket - a large self-service grocery store

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'commissarius' (person entrusted with a special duty), from Latin 'committere' (to entrust).
