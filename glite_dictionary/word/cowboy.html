cowboy

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊˌbɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊ.bɔɪ / (BrE)
a person who is reckless or irresponsible, especially in driving vehicles
reckless or irresponsible person (esp. driver)
slang

Examples

That new delivery driver is a real cowboy—he nearly hit three cars this morning.
The roads would be safer if there were fewer cowboys behind the wheel.
I don't trust that mechanic; he has a reputation for being a cowboy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maniac - an extremely reckless or dangerous driver
  • Antonym: professional - someone who is careful and follows proper procedures

2. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊ.bɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊ.bɔɪ / (BrE)
a man whose job is to look after cattle and perform related tasks, especially on horseback, typically in the western US or Canada
man who looks after cattle on horseback

Examples

The life of a cowboy on the open range was full of hard work and adventure.
He dreams of becoming a cowboy and riding horses every day.
Several cowboys gathered around the campfire after a long day herding cattle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rancher - a person who owns or manages a ranch
  • Antonym: cowgirl - a woman who works with cattle, performing similar duties to a cowboy

Etymology

Formed in English from 'cow' + 'boy', first used in the 18th century in North America.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊˌbɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊˌbɔɪ / (BrE)
a man who performs in exhibitions of riding, roping, and similar skills, especially in rodeos or wild west shows
male performer skilled in rodeo riding and roping

Examples

As a professional cowboy, he traveled the country competing in rodeos.
The audience cheered as the cowboy expertly roped the charging steer.
He dreamed of becoming a famous cowboy after watching his idol perform at the arena.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rodeo rider - a person who participates in rodeo events as a performer

Etymology

Originally from cow + boy, meaning a boy or man who tends cattle; later used for performers in rodeos and shows.

Source: WordNet
