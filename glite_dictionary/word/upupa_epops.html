Upupa epops

1. NOUN

/ juːˈpuːpə ˈɛpɒps / (AmE), / juːˈpjuːpə ˈiːpɒps / (BrE)
a species of hoopoe, a pinkish-brown bird with black-and-white wings, native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa
a species of pinkish-brown hoopoe bird

Examples

The striking crest of the Upupa epops makes it easy to recognize in the wild.
Scientists observed the migratory patterns of Upupa epops across several continents.
The black-and-white wings of the Upupa epops contrast beautifully with its pinkish-brown plumage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoopoe - a colorful bird known for its distinctive crown of feathers

Etymology

From Latin *Upupa* (hoopoe) and species name *epops* from Ancient Greek ἔποψ (epops), meaning 'hoopoe'.
