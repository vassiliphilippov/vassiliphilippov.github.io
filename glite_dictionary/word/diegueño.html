Diegueño

1. NOUN

/ diˈeɪɡwəˌnjoʊ / (AmE), / diˈeɪɡwɛn.jəʊ / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people from southern California and northern Baja California.
Native American people of southern California

Examples

Many Diegueño traditions are passed down through oral storytelling.
The Diegueño people have lived in the region for centuries.
Efforts to preserve Diegueño language and culture continue today.

Etymology

From Spanish, after San Diego, California, where the group was first encountered by Europeans.

2. NOUN

/ diːˈɡeɪnjoʊ / (AmE), / diːˈɡeɪnjəʊ / (BrE)
the Yuman language spoken by the Diegueño people of southern California and northern Baja California
Yuman language of the Diegueño people

Examples

Linguists have documented the Diegueño language to help preserve its unique vocabulary.
Only a few elders in the community are fluent speakers of Diegueño.
Researchers compare Diegueño with other Yuman languages to trace historical relationships.

Etymology

From Spanish, named after Mission San Diego (Diego) + Spanish suffix -eño (denoting belonging or origin).
