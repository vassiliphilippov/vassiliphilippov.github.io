theophylline

1. NOUN

/ θiˈɑːfɪˌlin / (AmE), / θiˈɒfɪliːn / (BrE)
a colorless, crystalline substance found in tea leaves or made synthetically, used in medicine to treat respiratory diseases by relaxing the airways
a compound used as a bronchial dilator
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed theophylline to help manage her asthma symptoms.
Researchers are studying the side effects of theophylline in elderly patients.
Tea naturally contains small amounts of theophylline, along with other related compounds.

Etymology

From Greek 'thea' (tea) + 'phyllon' (leaf) + chemical suffix '-ine'.
