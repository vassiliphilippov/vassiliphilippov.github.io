Morelia spilotes variegatus

1. NOUN

/ mɔːˈriːliə spaɪˈloʊtiːz vəˌriːˈɡeɪtəs / (AmE), / mɒˈriːliə spaɪˈləʊtiːz vəˌriːˈɡeɪtəs / (BrE)
a subspecies of Australian python known for its distinctive variegated pattern along its back, commonly referred to as the carpet python
Australian python subspecies with variegated pattern

Examples

The Morelia spilotes variegatus is often called the carpet python due to its intricate patterns.
Researchers in Queensland observed the Morelia spilotes variegatus basking on tree branches.
Many reptile enthusiasts admire the coloration of the Morelia spilotes variegatus in captivity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carpet python - common name for Morelia spilotes variegatus, an Australian python

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining the generic name Morelia, the specific epithet from Greek 'spilos' (spot), and 'variegatus' (variegated) from Latin.
