verapamil

1. NOUN

/ vəˈræpəmɪl / (AmE), / vəˈrapəmɪl / (BrE)
a drug used as a calcium channel blocker to treat high blood pressure, certain heart conditions, and migraines
a medicine for blood pressure and heart conditions
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed verapamil to help control her high blood pressure.
Some patients take verapamil to prevent recurring migraines.
You should not stop taking verapamil suddenly without consulting your physician.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calcium channel blocker - a class of drugs that block calcium channels in cells

Etymology

From 'vera-' (possibly suggesting 'true' or 'effective') and '-pamil' of uncertain origin; coined as a drug name in the 1960s.
