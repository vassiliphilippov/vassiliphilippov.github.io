phlegmatical

1. ADJ

/ flɛɡˈmætɪkəl / (AmE), / flɛɡˈmatɪkəl / (BrE)
having or showing little emotion; calm and unexcitable.
unemotional; calm and unexcitable
archaic

Examples

Despite the chaos around him, the phlegmatical doctor remained unfazed and continued his work.
Her phlegmatical response to the shocking news surprised everyone in the room.
The phlegmatical expression on his face made it hard to tell what he was really feeling.

Synset Examples

a phlegmatic … and certainly undemonstrative man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phlegmatic - showing little or no emotion; calm and unexcitable

Etymology

From Medieval Latin phlegmaticus, from Greek phlegmatikos, relating to phlegm and temperament.
