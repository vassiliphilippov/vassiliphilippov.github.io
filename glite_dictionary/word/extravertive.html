extravertive

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛk.strəˈvɜr.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌɛk.strəˈvɜː.tɪv / (BrE)
(of a person or behavior) concerned with the social and physical environment rather than with oneself; oriented toward external events and people.
concerned with the external environment

Examples

Her extravertive personality made her a natural at networking events.
People with an extravertive disposition often thrive in busy, social settings.
The workshop favored extravertive approaches to problem-solving over individual reflection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extraverted - outgoing and socially confident
  • Antonym: introvertive - concerned with one's own thoughts and feelings; inward-looking

Etymology

Formed from 'extravert' (from Latin 'extra-' meaning 'outside' + 'vertere,' to turn) and the suffix '-ive.'
