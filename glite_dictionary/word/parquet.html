parquet

1. NOUN

/ pɑrˈkeɪ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːkeɪ / (BrE)
a floor made of small pieces of wood arranged in a decorative pattern
decorative wooden flooring

Examples

The grand ballroom featured a polished parquet that gleamed in the sunlight.
They decided to install parquet in the living room for a more elegant appearance.
The museum restored the original parquet after years of wear and tear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parquetry - inlaid work of flooring using geometric patterns of wooden pieces
  • Degree Lower: floorboard - a long flat piece of wood used to make a floor
  • Degree Lower: tile - a thin piece of hard material used for covering floors

Etymology

From French 'parquet' (a small enclosed space, wooden flooring).

2. NOUN

/ pɑrˈkeɪ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːkeɪ / (BrE)
the seating area on the main floor of a theater, located between the orchestra section and the parquet circle
main floor seating area in a theater

Examples

Our seats in the parquet provided an excellent view of the stage.
She preferred the parquet to the balcony for its proximity to the actors.
The parquet was quickly filled as theatergoers arrived for the show.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: orchestra - the area of seats in a theater nearest the stage
  • Degree Higher: parquet circle - a tier of seats above the main floor in a theater

Etymology

From French 'parquet' (a small enclosed space).
