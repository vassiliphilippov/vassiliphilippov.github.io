Notostraca

1. NOUN

/ ˌnoʊtəˈstreɪkə / (AmE), / ˌnɒtəˈstrɑːkə / (BrE)
an order of small freshwater crustaceans with a shield-shaped carapace, found in temporary pools and ponds
order of small freshwater crustaceans

Examples

The Notostraca are often called tadpole shrimp because of their appearance.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Notostraca in a remote Australian pond.
During the rainy season, the pools teemed with Notostraca swimming just beneath the surface.

Etymology

From Greek notos (back) + ostrakon (shell).

2. NOUN

/ ˌnoʊtəˈstrækə / (AmE), / ˌnɒtəˈstrækə / (BrE)
an order of small freshwater crustaceans characterized by a broad, shield-shaped carapace, commonly known as tadpole shrimp
order of freshwater crustaceans (tadpole shrimp)

Examples

Scientists have discovered a new species belonging to the order Notostraca in a remote freshwater pond.
The carapace shape is a distinctive feature of all members of Notostraca.
Many Notostraca can survive the drying of their habitats by laying drought-resistant eggs.

Etymology

From Greek notos (back) and ostrakon (shell), referring to the shield-like carapace.
