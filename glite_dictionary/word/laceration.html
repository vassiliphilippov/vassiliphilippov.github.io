laceration

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæs.əˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌlæs.əˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The act of tearing or cutting something, especially flesh, roughly or deeply.
the act of tearing or cutting

Examples

The laceration of the document was done deliberately as a protest.
Prolonged laceration of the tissues can complicate the healing process.
The animal's laceration of the prey was swift and brutal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tear - the act of pulling something apart forcefully

Etymology

From Latin laceratio, from lacerare (to tear).

2. NOUN

/ ˌlæsəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌlæsəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a deep cut or tear in the skin or flesh, often with ragged edges
deep cut or tear in skin or flesh

Examples

The doctor cleaned the laceration before stitching it up.
He suffered a deep laceration on his arm after falling onto broken glass.
The cyclist's laceration required immediate medical attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut - an opening or wound caused by a sharp object
  • Antonym: abrasion - a wound caused by scraping of the skin

Etymology

From Latin laceratio (a tearing), from lacerare (to tear).
