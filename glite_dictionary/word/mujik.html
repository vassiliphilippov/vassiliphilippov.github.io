mujik

1. NOUN

/ muˈʒik / (AmE), / mjuːˈʒiːk / (BrE)
A Russian peasant, especially before the 1917 Revolution.
Russian peasant before 1917

Examples

In pre-revolutionary Russia, the life of a mujik was marked by poverty and hard labor.
The mujiks worked long hours in the fields for their landlords.
As a mujik, he had little power or privilege in Tsarist Russia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serf - an agricultural laborer bound under feudalism

Etymology

From Russian мужик (muzhik), meaning 'man' or 'peasant'.
