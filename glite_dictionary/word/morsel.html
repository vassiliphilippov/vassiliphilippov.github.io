morsel

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔr.səl / (AmE), / ˈmɔː.səl / (BrE)
a small piece or amount of food, especially a mouthful
small piece or mouthful of food

Examples

She took a delicate morsel of cake and savored the taste.
Not a single morsel of food was wasted at the picnic.
He offered the stray dog a morsel from his sandwich.

Synset Examples

all they had left was a bit of bread

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mouthful - the amount that can be put in the mouth at one time
  • Degree Higher: chunk - a thick, solid piece of something

Etymology

From Old French *morsel*, from Vulgar Latin *morsellum*, diminutive of Latin *morsus* (a bite).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɔr.səl / (AmE), / ˈmɔː.səl / (BrE)
a small piece or amount of something, especially food
small piece or amount

Examples

She offered her dog a morsel of cheese as a treat.
There was not a single morsel of evidence to support the claim.
He savored every morsel of the delicious chocolate cake.

Synset Examples

a morsel of paper was all he needed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragment - a small part broken off or detached
  • Synonym: scrap - a small piece or portion that remains after the greater part has been used or removed

Etymology

From Old French 'morsel', from Vulgar Latin *morsellum, diminutive of Latin morsus (a bite), from mordere (to bite).
