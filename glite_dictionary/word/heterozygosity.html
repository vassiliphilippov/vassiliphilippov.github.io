heterozygosity

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛt̬əˌzaɪɡəˈsɪɾi / (AmE), / ˌhɛt.əʊˌzaɪ.ɡəˈsɪt.i / (BrE)
the state of having two different alleles at a particular gene locus
state of having different alleles at a gene locus
medical

Examples

In population genetics, a high level of heterozygosity indicates substantial genetic variation.
Loss of heterozygosity is often associated with certain types of cancer.
Scientists calculated the heterozygosity at multiple loci to evaluate the genetic health of the species.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: homozygosity - the state of having two identical alleles at a gene locus

Etymology

From heterozygous + -ity, patterned after similar scientific terms.
