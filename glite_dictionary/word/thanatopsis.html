thanatopsis

1. NOUN

/ ˌθænəˈtɑp.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌθan.əˈtɒp.sɪs / (BrE)
an essay or written meditation expressing a contemplation on death, especially as a literary form
essay on or meditation about death

Examples

The poet wrote a moving thanatopsis reflecting on the cycle of life and death.
Students analyzed Bryant's famous thanatopsis in their American literature class.
Writing a personal thanatopsis helped her come to terms with her grief.

Etymology

From Greek *thanatos* (death) + *opsis* (view, sight).
