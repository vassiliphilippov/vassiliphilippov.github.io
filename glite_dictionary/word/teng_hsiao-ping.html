Teng Hsiao-ping

1. PROPN

/ tʌŋ ˌʃaʊˈpɪŋ / (AmE), / tɛŋ ˌʃaʊˈpɪŋ / (BrE)
(1904–1997) Chinese communist leader who was the paramount figure in China from the late 1970s to the early 1990s, introducing major economic reforms.
Chinese communist leader, 1970s–1990s

Examples

Teng Hsiao-ping ushered in a new era of economic reform in China after the Cultural Revolution.
The leadership style of Teng Hsiao-ping has been the subject of extensive scholarly analysis.
Many credit Teng Hsiao-ping with opening China to the global economy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Deng Xiaoping - Alternative romanization (pinyin) of Teng Hsiao-ping, Chinese leader
