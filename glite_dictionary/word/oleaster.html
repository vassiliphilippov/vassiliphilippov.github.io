oleaster

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊliˈæstər / (AmE), / ˌəʊliˈæstə / (BrE)
a shrub of the genus Elaeagnus, typically with silvery twigs, yellow flowers, and small fruit resembling olives
shrub of genus Elaeagnus

Examples

The garden was bordered by a row of oleaster shrubs, their silvery leaves shimmering in the sun.
Some birds are attracted to the olivelike fruits produced by the oleaster.
We planted an oleaster near the driveway because of its hardiness and attractive foliage.

Etymology

From Latin 'oleaster' (wild olive), from 'olea' (olive).
