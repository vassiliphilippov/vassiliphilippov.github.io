Chelonia

1. NOUN

/ kəˈloʊniə / (AmE), / kɪˈləʊniə / (BrE)
A scientific order of reptiles that includes all tortoises and turtles.
order of reptiles: tortoises and turtles

Examples

Scientists have studied the evolution of Chelonia for decades to understand their adaptation to aquatic life.
The Chelonia order encompasses both terrestrial tortoises and marine turtles.
Conservation efforts focus on protecting endangered species within Chelonia worldwide.

Etymology

From Greek *khelōnē* (tortoise or turtle).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈloʊniə / (AmE), / kɪˈləʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of sea turtles, especially the green turtle, found in tropical and subtropical seas around the world.
genus of sea turtles, esp. green turtle

Examples

Chelonia is commonly known for including the green turtle, one of the largest sea turtles.
Marine biologists study the habitats of Chelonia in tropical oceans around the world.
The conservation status of Chelonia is a concern due to habitat loss and poaching.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: green turtle - a large sea turtle with a smooth greenish shell

Etymology

From Greek 'khelōnia', plural of 'khelōnē' (tortoise, turtle).

3. NOUN

/ kiˈloʊniə / (AmE), / kɪˈləʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of sea turtles, especially referring to the green turtle (Chelonia mydas).
genus of sea turtles (esp. green turtle)

Examples

The Chelonia genus is best known for its member, the green turtle.
Researchers study the nesting habits of Chelonia in coastal regions.
Conservation efforts have helped increase the population of Chelonia in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: green turtle - a large sea turtle of tropical oceans (Chelonia mydas)

Etymology

From Greek *khelōnē* meaning 'tortoise'.
