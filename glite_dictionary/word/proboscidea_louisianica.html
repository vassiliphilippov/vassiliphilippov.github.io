Proboscidea louisianica

1. NOUN

/ proʊˌbɑːsɪˈdiːə luˌiːziˈænɪkə / (AmE), / prəˌbɒsɪˈdiːə luˌiːziˈænɪkə / (BrE)
A species of annual plant native to the southern United States and Mexico, known for its large whitish or yellowish flowers mottled with purple and a long, curving beak-like fruit.
annual plant with curved seed pod, southern U.S./Mexico

Examples

Proboscidea louisianica is sometimes called devil's claw because of the shape of its fruit.
In botanical gardens, Proboscidea louisianica can be found blooming in late summer.
The distinctive seed pods of Proboscidea louisianica are often collected for decorative purposes.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Proboscidea' refers to 'elephant trunk', from Greek 'proboskis', referencing the beak-like fruit; 'louisianica' denotes origin from Louisiana.
