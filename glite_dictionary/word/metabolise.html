to metabolise

1. VERB

/ məˈtæbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / məˈtab.ə.laɪz / (BrE)
(of a living organism or cell) to produce substances by means of metabolic processes.
produce by metabolism

Examples

Certain bacteria can metabolise toxic chemicals into harmless substances.
After ingestion, the liver metabolises the drug into active compounds.
Some plants are able to metabolise nitrogen efficiently from the soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to metabolize - American English spelling; to produce by metabolism

Etymology

From Greek 'metabole' (change), via French and modern Latin.
