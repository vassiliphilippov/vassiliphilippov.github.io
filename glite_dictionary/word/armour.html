armour

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrmɚ / (AmE), / ˈɑːmə / (BrE)
A protective covering, usually made of metal, worn in combat to defend against weapons.
protective metal covering for combat
british

Examples

The knight wore heavy armour to protect himself in battle.
Medieval armour is on display at the museum.
She admired the intricate design of the samurai armour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armor - Alternative (American) spelling: protective metal covering for combat

Etymology

From Old French *armure*, from Latin *armatura* (equipment, armor).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrmər / (AmE), / ˈɑːmə / (BrE)
A military unit consisting of armored fighting vehicles such as tanks.
military unit of armored vehicles

Examples

The division's armour advanced swiftly across the open terrain.
Modern armour often relies on advanced technology and integrated support units.
The general inspected the armour before the launch of the new offensive.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infantry - soldiers fighting on foot

Etymology

From Old French 'armeure', from Latin 'armatura' (arms, equipment).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrmər / (AmE), / ˈɑːmə / (BrE)
a tough, rigid protective covering on an animal or plant, such as a shell or thickened skin, that defends against predators or injury
protective animal or plant covering

Examples

The armadillo's armour helps protect it from predators in the wild.
The beetle's armour is made up of hardened exoskeletal plates.
The turtle retracts into its armour when threatened by danger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shell - hard outer covering of certain animals such as mollusks and turtles

Etymology

From Old French *armure*, from Latin *armatura* (equipment, armour).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01905391-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈɑːrmər / (AmE), / ˈɑːmə / (BrE)
to equip with armor or protective covering, especially for military purposes
to equip with armor
british

Examples

The engineers armoured the vehicles for maximum protection against explosives.
During the renovation, the castle walls were armoured with steel plates.
They plan to armour the ship before deploying it into hostile waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armor - equip with armor (US spelling of 'armour', verb)

Etymology

From Old French *armeure* (armor), from Latin *armatura* (equipment, armament).
