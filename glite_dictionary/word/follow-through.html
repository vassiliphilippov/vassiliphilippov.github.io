follow-through

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːloʊ θruː / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊ θruː / (BrE)
the forward movement that continues after the main part of a stroke or swing, especially in sports, to complete the action smoothly and effectively
completion of a stroke or swing in sports

Examples

His follow-through made his golf swing more powerful and accurate.
Good tennis players practice their follow-through to maintain control over the ball.
She injured her shoulder due to an improper follow-through during the match.

Synset Examples

his follow-through was straight down the line toward the target
squash can be dangerous if your opponent has a long follow-through

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stroke - the action of striking the ball in sports
  • Antonym: backswing - the backward movement before a stroke in sports

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɑloʊ ˌθru / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊ ˌθruː / (BrE)
the action of completing a project, task, or intention as promised or planned
completion of a plan or promise

Examples

Her strong follow-through ensured the project was completed on time.
The team's lack of follow-through caused the initiative to stall.
I appreciate your follow-through on what you promised to do.

Synset Examples

I appreciated his follow-through on his promise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: completion - the act of finishing something
  • Antonym: abandonment - the act of giving up before completion

Etymology

Formed from the phrasal verb 'follow through' (to carry out to completion), used as a noun.
