Anemia

1. NOUN

/ əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / əˈniːmɪə / (BrE)
A genus of terrestrial or rock-growing ferns with pinnatifid fronds, chiefly found in tropical America.
genus of tropical American ferns

Examples

Several species of Anemia are commonly found in the rainforests of South America.
The delicate fronds of Anemia make it a popular plant among fern enthusiasts.
Researchers discovered a new Anemia species growing on rocky outcrops in Brazil.

Etymology

From Greek 'anemos' meaning 'wind', possibly referencing the delicate fronds.

2. NOUN

/ əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / əˈniːmiə / (BrE)
A genus of terrestrial or lithophytic ferns with pinnatifid fronds, chiefly found in tropical America.
genus of tropical American ferns

Examples

The Anemia ferns are easily recognized by their slender, divided fronds.
Several species in the Anemia genus thrive in humid, tropical forests.
Botanists discovered a new variant within the Anemia genus last year.

Etymology

From Greek 'anemos' meaning 'wind', probably alluding to the delicate fronds.
