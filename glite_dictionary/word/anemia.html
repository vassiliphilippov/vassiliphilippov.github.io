anemia

1. NOUN

/ əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / əˈniːmiə / (BrE)
A condition in which the blood lacks enough healthy red blood cells, resulting in fatigue and weakness.
deficiency of red blood cells
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with anemia after reviewing her blood test results.
Fatigue and pale skin are common symptoms of anemia.
Iron supplements are often prescribed to treat anemia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anemia - reduced number of red blood cells or hemoglobin
  • Antonym: polycythemia - an excess of red blood cells

Etymology

From Greek 'anaimia', from 'an-' (without) + 'haima' (blood).

2. NOUN

/ əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / əˈniːmɪə / (BrE)
A lack of vitality, spirit, or energy; weakness or lifelessness, especially in a figurative sense.
lack of vitality or spirit (figurative)

Examples

The economy is suffering from a period of anemia that has slowed growth.
Her speech was critiqued for its surprising anemia and lack of passion.
There is a certain anemia in the party's campaign that worries longtime supporters.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vitality - energy and exuberance; the state of being strong and active
  • Antonym: liveliness - the quality of being full of life and energy

Etymology

From New Latin anaemia, from Greek anaimia (want of blood).
