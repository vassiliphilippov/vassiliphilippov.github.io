snake doctor

1. NOUN

/ ˈsneɪk ˌdɑktɚ / (AmE), / ˈsneɪk ˌdɒktə / (BrE)
A regional term for a dragonfly, a slender-bodied insect with iridescent wings that are held outspread at rest; both adults and nymphs feed on mosquitoes and similar insects.
regional term for a dragonfly
informal american

Examples

Local children call the snake doctor a magical bug that follows snakes along the riverbank.
A snake doctor landed on my fishing rod, its bright wings shimmering in the sunlight.
Some believe that a snake doctor hovers nearby to heal injured snakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dragonfly - a large, slender-bodied insect with two pairs of iridescent wings; feeds on mosquitoes

Etymology

Refers to folk belief in Southern U.S. regions that dragonflies could heal or accompany snakes.
