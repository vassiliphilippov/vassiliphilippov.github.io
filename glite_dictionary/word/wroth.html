wroth

1. ADJ

/ rɔθ / (AmE), / rɒθ / (BrE)
full of wrath; intensely angry or incensed, especially in a condemnatory way (archaic).
intensely angry (archaic)
archaic

Examples

The king was wroth when he heard of the betrayal by his trusted advisor.
She sent him a wroth letter, expressing all of her indignation and grief.
Though wroth at the injustice, he kept his composure in front of the court.

Synset Examples

they trembled before the wrathful queen
but wroth as he was, a short struggle ended in reconciliation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angry - feeling or showing anger
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling anger

Etymology

From Old English *wrāth*, related to wrath.
