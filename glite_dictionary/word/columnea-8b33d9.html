Columnea

1. NOUN

/ koʊlˈumniə / (AmE), / kɒlˈjuːmnɪə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American subshrubs and climbing plants, often grown for their ornamental flowers.
genus of tropical American subshrubs and lianas

Examples

Many species of Columnea are popular with indoor gardeners for their bright, tubular flowers.
The unique leaves and trailing habit make Columnea a favorite choice for hanging baskets.
Some Columnea varieties require high humidity to thrive indoors.

Etymology

Named after Italian botanist Fabio Colonna (Latin: Columna).
