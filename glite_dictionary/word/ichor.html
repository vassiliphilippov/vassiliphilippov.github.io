ichor

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪkər / (AmE), / ˈɪkɔː / (BrE)
a thin, watery, often foul-smelling discharge from a wound or ulcer, especially as a result of inflammation
watery discharge from a wound
medical

Examples

The surgeon noted a yellowish ichor oozing from the infected tissue.
A persistent ichor seeped from the patient's ulcer despite treatment.
The wound gave off a foul-smelling ichor that concerned the attending nurse.

Etymology

From Greek *ichōr* (the fluid that flows in the veins of the gods).

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪkər / (AmE), / ˈaɪkɔː / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) an ethereal fluid that flows in the veins of the gods, instead of blood.
mythical fluid in gods' veins

Examples

According to legend, ichor spilled from the wounded god and gave life to the earth.
The poet described the hero's blood as turning to ichor in the presence of the divine.
In ancient tales, mere mortals could not bear the sight or smell of ichor.

Etymology

From Greek ikhōr, the fluid that flows in the veins of the gods.
