centrifugation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛntrəˌfjuˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛntrɪfjʊˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of separating substances of different densities by spinning them in a centrifuge
separation process using a centrifuge
medical

Examples

The blood samples underwent centrifugation to separate plasma from red cells.
Effective centrifugation is essential in biochemistry labs to isolate cellular components.
After centrifugation, the heavier particles settled at the bottom of the tube.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: centrifuge - a machine that separates substances of different densities by spinning them rapidly

Etymology

From French 'centrifugation', from 'centrifuger' (to centrifuge), based on Latin 'centrum' (center) and 'fugare' (to drive away).
