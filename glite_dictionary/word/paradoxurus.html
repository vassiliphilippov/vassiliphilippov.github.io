Paradoxurus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəˈdɑksjʊrəs / (AmE), / ˌparəˈdɒksjʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of small to medium-sized, nocturnal mammals known as palm civets, native to South and Southeast Asia.
genus of palm civets

Examples

The Asian palm civet, classified as Paradoxurus hermaphroditus, is widespread in tropical forests.
Researchers studied several species within the Paradoxurus genus to understand their behaviors.
Paradoxurus can be identified by their long bodies and bushy tails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palm civet - any small, tree-dwelling mammal of the family Viverridae

Etymology

From Greek paradoxos (unexpected, strange) and oura (tail).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpærəˈdɑksərəs / (AmE), / ˌparəˈdɒksjʊrəs / (BrE)
A genus of small to medium-sized nocturnal mammals known as palm civets, native to South and Southeast Asia.
genus of palm civets

Examples

The Asian palm civet belongs to the genus Paradoxurus.
Several species in the genus Paradoxurus are found in tropical rainforests.
Researchers discovered a new species of Paradoxurus in Borneo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palm civet - nocturnal mammal of Asia and Africa, member of Viverridae family

Etymology

From Greek paradoxos (unexpected, strange) + oura (tail), referring to the animal's unusual tail.
