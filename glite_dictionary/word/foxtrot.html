foxtrot

1. VERB

/ ˈfɑks.trɑt / (AmE), / ˈfɒks.trɒt / (BrE)
to perform the foxtrot, a ballroom dance with a rhythm of slow steps and quick steps
to dance the foxtrot

Examples

They foxtrotted gracefully across the polished dance floor.
She learned how to foxtrot in her first ballroom class.
At the party, couples foxtrotted to the music of a live band.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waltz - to dance the waltz

Etymology

Named after Harry Fox, an early performer of the dance.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01900206-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːkstrɑːt / (AmE), / ˈfɒkstrɒt / (BrE)
a ballroom dance in quadruple time, characterized by smooth, progressive movements and a combination of slow and quick steps.
a type of ballroom dance

Examples

They swept gracefully across the dance floor, performing the foxtrot to the sound of a jazz band.
She learned the steps of the foxtrot before trying more complex dances like the tango.
At the ballroom competition, their foxtrot was praised for its elegance and precision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waltz - a ballroom dance in triple time with a strong accent on the first beat

Etymology

Named after Harry Fox, an American vaudeville performer who popularized the dance in the early 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00536993-n
Synset POS: n
