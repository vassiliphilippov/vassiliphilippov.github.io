Saragossa

1. PROPN

/ ˌsærəˈɡoʊsə / (AmE), / ˌsærəˈɡəʊsə / (BrE)
an ancient city on the Ebro River in northeastern Spain, formerly the capital of Aragon; also known in Spanish as Zaragoza.
historic city in northeastern Spain

Examples

The historic city of Saragossa attracts thousands of tourists every year.
During the Peninsular War, Saragossa became famous for its resistance against French forces.
Many cultural traditions in Saragossa date back several centuries.

Etymology

From Spanish Zaragoza, from Latin Caesaraugusta (named after the Roman Emperor Augustus).
