to relegate

1. VERB

/ ˈrɛlɪˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛlɪɡeɪt / (BrE)
to send or assign (a matter or decision) to another person or authority for consideration or judgment
refer a matter to another person or authority

Examples

The manager chose to relegate the dispute to the human resources department for review.
Rather than making a ruling himself, the judge decided to relegate the matter to an independent arbitrator.
She often tries to relegate controversial topics to her assistant instead of addressing them directly.

Synset Examples

She likes to relegate difficult questions to her colleagues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delegate - to entrust a task or responsibility to another

Etymology

From Latin 'relegare' (to send away, dispatch)

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02595169-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈrɛlɪˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛlɪɡeɪt / (BrE)
to assign someone or something to a particular class, group, or category
assign to a class or group

Examples

In some taxonomies, experts relegate certain species to a different genus.
Some scholars relegate this style of painting to the category of naive art.
The committee decided to relegate the proposal to a sub-group for further discussion.

Synset Examples

How should algae be classified?
People argue about how to relegate certain mushrooms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to classify - to arrange or assign to categories based on shared qualities
  • Synonym: to assign - to allocate or designate something to a particular category or role

Etymology

From Latin 'relegare' (to send away, remove), from 're-' (back) + 'legare' (to send).

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈrɛləˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛlɪˌɡeɪt / (BrE)
to officially send someone away, especially as a punishment, or to assign to a lower or less important position
to officially send away or downgrade

Examples

After repeated violations, he was relegated to a remote outpost by the authorities.
The unpopular player was relegated from the main team to the reserves.
During the scandal, several officials were relegated to lesser roles as a form of punishment.

Synset Examples

he was banished from his own country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to banish - to send someone away from a place as a punishment
  • Antonym: to promote - to raise someone to a higher or more important position

Etymology

From Latin 'relegare', meaning 'to send away, remove'.

4. VERB

/ ˈrɛlɪˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛlɪɡeɪt / (BrE)
to assign someone or something to a lower position or rank; to reduce in status or importance
assign to a lower rank or position

Examples

After the team's poor performance, the coach decided to relegate them to a lower division.
The manager was relegated to an advisory role after the merger.
If sales don't improve, we may have to relegate this product to our clearance section.

Synset Examples

She was demoted because she always speaks up
He was broken down to Sergeant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to demote - to reduce someone to a lower rank or less senior position
  • Antonym: to promote - to raise someone to a higher or more important position

Etymology

From Latin relegare (to send away, remove) via past participle relegatus.
