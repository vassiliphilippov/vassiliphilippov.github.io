Pontederiaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːn.təˌdɛr.iˈeɪ.siˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˌpɒn.tɪˌdɪə.rɪˈiː.siˌaɪ / (BrE)
A family of aquatic or bog plants, including water hyacinths, characterized by their floating or emergent leaves and often showy flowers.
family of aquatic or bog plants

Examples

The Pontederiaceae family includes well-known aquatic plants such as the water hyacinth.
Scientists are studying the rapid growth of species in the Pontederiaceae in various wetland habitats.
Many members of the Pontederiaceae exhibit specialized adaptations to life in waterlogged environments.

Etymology

From New Latin, named after Giulio Pontedera (1688–1757), Italian botanist, with standard botanical Latin suffix -aceae for plant families.

2. NOUN

a family of aquatic or bog plants, including the water hyacinth and related genera, commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions.
family of aquatic or bog plants

Examples

Plants belonging to the Pontederiaceae family are commonly found in tropical wetlands.
The invasive water hyacinth is a notable member of the Pontederiaceae.
Botanists have studied the unique flower structures of Pontederiaceae species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water hyacinth - an aquatic plant of the Pontederiaceae family

Etymology

From the genus name Pontederia, named after Italian botanist Giulio Pontedera (1688–1757), with standard botanical family suffix -aceae.
