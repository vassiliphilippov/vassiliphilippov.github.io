despotical

1. ADJ

/ dɛsˈpɑtɪkəl / (AmE), / dɛsˈpɒtɪkəl / (BrE)
Belonging to or having the characteristics of a despot; tyrannical or autocratic.
characteristic of a despot; tyrannical

Examples

The emperor's despotical rule left little room for personal liberties.
Many feared a despotical system replacing their hard-won democracy.
His despotical management style alienated employees throughout the company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: despotic - characteristic of a despot; tyrannical

Etymology

From French despotique, modelled after 'despotic', from Greek despotēs (master, absolute ruler).
