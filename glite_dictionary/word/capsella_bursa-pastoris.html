Capsella bursa-pastoris

1. NOUN

/ kæpˈsɛlə ˌbɜrsə ˈpæstərɪs / (AmE), / kapˈsɛlə ˌbɜːsə ˈpastərɪs / (BrE)
A white-flowered annual plant of the mustard family, native to Europe and widely naturalized as a weed, bearing triangular notched seed pods.
white-flowered weedy plant with triangular pods

Examples

Many gardeners consider Capsella bursa-pastoris an invasive weed in their flowerbeds.
Capsella bursa-pastoris produces small, heart-shaped seed pods that are easy to recognize.
You can often find Capsella bursa-pastoris growing in disturbed soil along roadsides and fields.

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'shepherd's purse,' referring to the shape of its seedpods.
