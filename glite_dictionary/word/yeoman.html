yeoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈjoʊmən / (AmE), / ˈjəʊmən / (BrE)
(historical) A man who owned and cultivated his own land, ranking below the gentry in former English society.
historical small landowner
archaic

Examples

A yeoman in medieval England would often work his own fields alongside hired help.
The social class of the yeoman played a vital role in rural communities.
Shakespeare sometimes described the loyalty and honesty of a yeoman.

Etymology

Middle English *yeman*, of uncertain origin, perhaps from Old English *geaman* (villager).

2. NOUN

/ ˈjoʊmən / (AmE), / ˈjəʊmən / (BrE)
an officer in the ceremonial bodyguard of the British monarch, traditionally responsible for the security and ceremonial duties at royal events
officer in British royal bodyguard
british

Examples

A yeoman stood guard at the entrance to the royal residence during the ceremony.
Tourists enjoyed taking photos with the yeomen dressed in their historic uniforms.
Each yeoman is selected for their distinguished service in the military.

Etymology

From Old English *geōman* (attendant, servant), later specialized in meaning.
