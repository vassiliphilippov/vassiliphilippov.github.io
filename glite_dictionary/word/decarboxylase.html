decarboxylase

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːkɑːrˈbɒk.sɪˌleɪs / (AmE), / ˌdiːkɑːˈbɒk.sɪ.leɪs / (BrE)
an enzyme that catalyzes the removal of a carboxyl group from a molecule
enzyme that removes carboxyl group
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the lack of a certain decarboxylase enzyme can lead to serious metabolic disorders.
Researchers measured the activity of decarboxylase in various tissue samples to study the biochemical pathway.
Some bacteria possess a special decarboxylase that allows them to break down amino acids efficiently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enzyme - a substance produced by a living organism that acts as a catalyst

Etymology

From 'de-' (removal) and 'carboxyl' (a functional group), combined with '-ase' (enzyme suffix).
