osteochondroma

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːstioʊkɑnˈdroʊmə / (AmE), / ˌɒstiəʊkɒnˈdrəʊmə / (BrE)
a benign tumor composed of both bone and cartilage, typically developing near the end of a long bone
benign tumor of bone and cartilage
medical

Examples

The surgeon removed the osteochondroma from the patient's femur.
X-rays revealed an osteochondroma near the end of her upper arm bone.
Children with an osteochondroma often experience no symptoms unless the tumor presses on nearby tissue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exostosis - a benign outgrowth of cartilaginous tissue on a bone

Etymology

From Greek osteon (bone) + chondros (cartilage) + -oma (tumor).
