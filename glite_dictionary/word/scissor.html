to scissor

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪsər / (AmE), / ˈsɪsə / (BrE)
to cut or move like scissors, or to cut using scissors.
to cut with or like scissors

Examples

She scissored the paper into tiny pieces for her art project.
The gymnast's legs scissored in the air as she leapt across the beam.
Carefully, he scissored the threads to remove the old patch from his jeans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cut - to divide with a sharp-edged instrument

Etymology

From the noun 'scissors', from Latin 'cisoria' (cutting instrument), based on 'cis-' (cut).
