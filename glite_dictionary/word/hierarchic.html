hierarchic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪəˈrɑrkɪk / (AmE), / ˌhaɪəˈrɑːkɪk / (BrE)
arranged or classified according to a hierarchy, with items or people ranked in levels or layers of authority, importance, or status
arranged in ranked levels or layers

Examples

The company maintained a strictly hierarchic management structure despite calls for more collaboration.
Many traditions are passed down through hierarchic institutions within society.
Her research explores hierarchic systems in animal behavior.

Synset Examples

it has been said that only a hierarchical society with a leisure class at the top can produce works of art
in her hierarchical set of values honesty comes first

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hierarchical - organized in order of rank or authority

Etymology

From Greek 'hierarchēs' (leader of sacred rites), via Medieval Latin 'hierarchia'.
