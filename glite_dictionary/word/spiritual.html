spiritual

1. ADJ

/ ˈspɪrɪtʃuəl / (AmE), / ˈspɪrɪtjʊəl / (BrE)
Resembling or characteristic of a ghost, spirit, or phantom; spectral or supernatural in appearance or nature.
resembling a ghost or phantom

Examples

There was a spiritual presence in the abandoned house that made everyone uneasy.
The fog gave the cemetery a spiritual atmosphere, as if ghosts might appear at any moment.
She described seeing a spiritual figure standing by the old oak tree late at night.

Synset Examples

a ghostly face at the window
a phantasmal presence in the room
spectral emanations
spiritual tappings at a seance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ghostly - resembling or characteristic of a ghost
  • Synonym: spectral - relating to a ghost or specter

Etymology

From Latin 'spiritus' (breath, spirit) via Old French 'espirituel'.

5. NOUN

/ ˈspɪrɪtʃuəl / (AmE), / ˈspɪrɪtʃuəl / (BrE)
a religious song of a kind originating among Black people in the southern United States, expressing deep emotion and often featuring call-and-response singing
religious song from Black American tradition

Examples

The choir performed an old spiritual during the concert, moving the audience to tears.
Many spirituals were created as expressions of hope and faith under slavery.
He learned to sing spirituals from his grandmother, who had grown up in the South.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gospel - a style of religious music closely associated with spirituals but generally more modern and with different musical structure
  • Synonym: hymn - a religious song or poem of praise to God, often sung in Christian worship, but generally different in style from spirituals

Etymology

Derived from the word 'spirit'; the musical sense arose in the mid-19th century, associated with the African American religious tradition.
