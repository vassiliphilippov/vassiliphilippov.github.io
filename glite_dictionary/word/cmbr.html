CMBR

1. NOUN

/ siː ɛm biː ɑːr / (AmE), / siː ɛm biː ɑː / (BrE)
the faint cosmic microwave background radiation left over from the Big Bang, now filling the universe at a temperature of about 2.7 kelvin
cosmic microwave background radiation

Examples

The CMBR provides strong evidence supporting the theory of the Big Bang.
Sensitive telescopes are required to measure the faint signal of the CMBR.
Scientists analyze fluctuations in the CMBR to study the structure of the early universe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cosmic microwave background - the weak background radiation from the early universe, also called CMBR

Etymology

Acronym for Cosmic Microwave Background Radiation.
