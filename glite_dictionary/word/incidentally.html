incidentally

1. ADV

/ ˌɪn.sɪˈdɛn.t̬əl.i / (AmE), / ˌɪn.sɪˈdɛn.təli / (BrE)
in a way that is minor, secondary, or not of main importance; in a subordinate manner
in a minor or subordinate way

Examples

The new regulation only incidentally affects our business operations.
Her research is incidentally related to the main topic of the conference.
The painting's colors were incidentally inspired by local traditions.

Synset Examples

these magnificent achievements were only incidentally influenced by Oriental models

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marginally - to a slight or small degree; in a way that is not central or important
  • Antonym: mainly - for the most part; chiefly

Etymology

From Latin 'incidentalis' (occurring as a consequence), from 'incidere' (to fall into).

2. ADV

/ ˌɪnsɪˈdɛntəli / (AmE), / ˌɪnsɪˈdɛntəli / (BrE)
used to introduce a new topic or to add further information that is not directly related to what was being said before
used to change the subject or add unrelated information

Examples

Incidentally, have you seen the new restaurant on Main Street?
Incidentally, I met your brother at the conference yesterday.
The main meeting will start at 10 a.m.; incidentally, refreshments will be served afterward.

Synset Examples

incidentally, I won't go to the party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: by the way - used to introduce a new topic or add a comment

Etymology

From Latin 'incidentalis' (occurring), from 'incidere' (to fall upon).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00157363-r
Synset POS: r
