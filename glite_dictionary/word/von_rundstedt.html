von Rundstedt

1. PROPN

/ fɒn ˈrʌndʃtɛt / (AmE), / fɒn ˈrʊntʃtɛt / (BrE)
(1875–1953) German field marshal in World War II who directed the conquest of Poland and led the Ardennes counteroffensive.
German WWII field marshal (1875–1953)

Examples

von Rundstedt played a pivotal role during the German invasion of Poland in 1939.
Allied commanders closely studied the strategies employed by von Rundstedt in Europe.
Historians debate whether von Rundstedt's leadership influenced the outcome of the Battle of the Bulge.
