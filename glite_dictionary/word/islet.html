islet

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.lət / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.lɪt / (BrE)
a very small island, especially one in a river or a lake
a very small island

Examples

We took a boat out to a remote islet in the middle of the lake.
Birds nested on the rocky islet just off the coast.
The tiny islet disappeared at high tide.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: island - a piece of land surrounded by water
  • Synonym: cay - a small, low-elevation, sandy island on the surface of a coral reef

Etymology

From French 'îslette', diminutive of 'île' (island), from Latin 'insula'.
