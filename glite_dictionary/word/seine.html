seine

1. VERB

/ seɪn / (AmE), / seɪn / (BrE)
to fish or catch fish using a seine net
to fish with a seine net

Examples

The fishermen seined the river at dawn to catch the migrating salmon.
They spent the afternoon seining for baitfish near the rocky shore.
Local regulations restrict when you can seine in this part of the bay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: net - to catch fish using nets
  • Synonym: trawl - to fish by dragging a net along the bottom of a body of water

Etymology

From Old French *seine*, from Latin *sagena* (a large fishing net).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ seɪn / (AmE), / seɪn / (BrE)
a large fishing net that hangs vertically in the water, held up by floats at the top and weighted at the bottom, used to encircle fish
a large vertical fishing net

Examples

The fishermen dragged the seine through the bay to catch sardines.
They learned how to set a seine properly to maximize their catch.
Large schools of fish were trapped in the seine near the shore.

Etymology

From Old French *seine*, from Latin *sagena* (a fishing net), from Greek *sagēnē*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04175602-n
Synset POS: n
