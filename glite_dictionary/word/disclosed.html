disclosed

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈkloʊzd / (AmE), / dɪsˈkləʊzd / (BrE)
made known or revealed, especially something previously secret or concealed
made known; revealed

Examples

The financial statements were disclosed to the public last week.
Details of the agreement remain disclosed only to the company board.
The disclosed information led to an official investigation.

Synset Examples

the disclosed purpose of their wicked plan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revealed - made known; no longer secret or hidden
  • Antonym: concealed - kept secret; hidden from view or knowledge

Etymology

From the verb 'disclose', from Old French 'desclos', past participle of 'desclosir', from Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'claudere' (to close).
