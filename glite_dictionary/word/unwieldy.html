unwieldy

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈwildi / (AmE), / ʌnˈwiːldi / (BrE)
difficult to move, handle, or use because of size, shape, or weight.
hard to manage due to size or shape

Examples

The piano was too unwieldy for two people to carry safely down the stairs.
He struggled to steer the unwieldy cart through the narrow doorway.
As technology advanced, the company replaced its unwieldy equipment with more compact machines.

Synset Examples

we set about towing the unwieldy structure into the shelter
almost dropped the unwieldy parcel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cumbersome - large or heavy and therefore difficult to carry or use
  • Antonym: manageable - able to be handled or controlled easily

Etymology

From un- (not) + wieldy (able to be handled), from Middle English, from Old English wieldan (to control).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈwildi / (AmE), / ʌnˈwiːldi / (BrE)
Difficult to handle, use, or manage because of size, shape, or complexity.
hard to manage or handle

Examples

The new software update made the once intuitive interface unwieldy and confusing to navigate.
The committee found the current tax system too unwieldy to reform quickly.
He struggled to carry the unwieldy package up the narrow staircase.

Synset Examples

unwieldy rules and regulations

Related Terms

  • Antonym: manageable - easy to control or handle
  • Synonym: cumbersome - large or heavy and therefore difficult to carry or use

Etymology

From un- (not) + wieldy, from wield, Old English *wieldan* (to control, handle).

Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈwildi / (AmE), / ʌnˈwiːldi / (BrE)
Lacking grace, ease, or agility in movement or posture; awkward or clumsy.
awkward or clumsy in movement or posture

Examples

The large suitcase was so unwieldy that she struggled to carry it up the stairs.
His unwieldy movements on the dance floor drew amused glances from the crowd.
Because of its unwieldy shape, the old armchair was difficult to move through the narrow doorway.

Synset Examples

a gawky lad with long ungainly legs
clumsy fingers
what an ungainly creature a giraffe is
heaved his unwieldy figure out of his chair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clumsy - lacking skill or grace in movement or action
  • Antonym: graceful - showing grace or elegance in movement or form

Etymology

From un- (not) + wieldy, related to Old English *wieldan* (to control, govern).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01144412-s
Synset POS: s
