to signalize

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪɡ.nə.laɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡ.nə.laɪz / (BrE)
to point out or make notable carefully and clearly; to draw attention to in a marked way.
to point out clearly; make notable

Examples

The historian signalized the importance of this event in his speech.
Her achievements were signalized by the award of a prestigious medal.
The director signalized the new policy changes at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: highlight - to emphasize or make something stand out
  • Antonym: obscure - to make something less visible or clear

Etymology

From French 'signaliser', from 'signal' (mark, sign).

WordNet ID: oewn-00926583-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈsɪɡnəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡnəlaɪz / (BrE)
to equip (a road, intersection, etc.) with traffic signals
equip with traffic signals

Examples

The city plans to signalize the intersection to improve safety for pedestrians.
After several accidents, engineers decided to signalize the busy junction.
Many rural crossroads remain unsignalized, but urban planners hope to signalize them soon.

Synset Examples

signalize a busy intersection

Etymology

From 'signal' + '-ize', modeled after French 'signaliser'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02301945-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈsɪɡ.nə.laɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡ.nə.laɪz / (BrE)
to communicate or convey information using signals or signs, especially non-verbally
communicate by signals or signs

Examples

During the noisy meeting, she signalized her agreement with a quick nod.
He signalized the driver by waving a red flag at the intersection.
In the field, scouts often signalize important discoveries using hand gestures.

Synset Examples

He signed his disapproval with a dismissive hand gesture
The diner signaled the waiters to bring the menu

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signal - to communicate with a gesture, action, or device

Etymology

From French signaliser, from signal (signal).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈsɪɡ.nəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡ.nə.laɪz / (BrE)
to make something conspicuous or noteworthy; to draw attention to or highlight
make something conspicuous or noteworthy

Examples

The ceremony was intended to signalize the nation's independence.
The architect used a striking entrance to signalize the importance of the building.
They signalized their success with a grand celebration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: highlight - to make something stand out or attract attention
  • Antonym: obscure - to make something less visible or less noticeable

Etymology

From French signaliser, based on 'signal', ultimately from Latin signum (mark, sign).
