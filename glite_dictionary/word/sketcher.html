sketcher

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɛtʃər / (AmE), / ˈskɛtʃə / (BrE)
an implement or tool used for making sketches or drawings
tool for making sketches

Examples

He always carries a sketcher and notebook when he travels.
The set includes every sketcher an artist might need, from pencils to charcoal sticks.
Your sketcher leaves very soft, light marks, perfect for rough outlines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pencil - a tool for writing or drawing, typically with graphite
  • Synonym: pen - an instrument for writing or drawing with ink

Etymology

From 'sketch' + '-er', denoting something that performs the action of sketching.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskɛtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈskɛtʃə / (BrE)
a person who draws sketches, especially as an artist or hobbyist
a person who draws sketches

Examples

The sketcher captured the busy scene at the market with quick, confident lines.
As a lifelong sketcher, she carried a notebook everywhere to draw whatever inspired her.
Many museums organize tours led by a sketcher who demonstrates technique to visitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artist - a person who creates art, especially visual art
  • Synonym: painter - an artist who paints pictures

Etymology

From 'sketch' + '-er', denoting a person who sketches.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10624288-n
Synset POS: n
