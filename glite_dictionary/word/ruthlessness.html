ruthlessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːθ.ləs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈruːθ.ləs.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of having no pity or mercy; a willingness to be cruel or harsh in order to achieve one's aims.
lack of pity or mercy

Examples

Her ruthlessness in business earned her both admiration and fear from her competitors.
The villain's ruthlessness left a trail of destruction wherever he went.
Some leaders believe that a certain degree of ruthlessness is necessary to succeed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mercy - compassion or forgiveness shown towards someone
  • Antonym: compassion - sympathetic pity and concern for others
  • Synonym: cruelty - callous indifference to or pleasure in causing pain

Etymology

From 'ruthless' (without compassion), itself from 'ruth' (pity, compassion) + '-less'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈruːθ.ləs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈruːθ.ləs.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of having or showing no pity or compassion for others; extreme lack of mercy or empathy.
lack of pity or compassion

Examples

Her ruthlessness in business made her both feared and respected by her competitors.
He was known for his ruthlessness toward anyone who stood in his way.
The leader’s ruthlessness saw him betray even his closest allies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mercilessness - lack of mercy
  • Antonym: compassion - sympathetic concern for others

Etymology

From 'ruthless' + '-ness'; 'ruthless' derives from 'ruth' (pity, compassion) + '-less'.
