Cephalotaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛfələˈteɪsi.i / (AmE), / ˌsɛfələˈteɪsɪ.i / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants in the order Rosales, consisting solely of the genus Cephalotus.
plant family containing Cephalotus

Examples

The Cephalotaceae are notable for containing only one genus, Cephalotus.
Botanists classified the Australian pitcher plant within the family Cephalotaceae.
Recent studies have revised the taxonomy of Cephalotaceae based on molecular evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cephalotus - genus of carnivorous plant found in southwestern Australia

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus name Cephalotus and standard family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛfəloʊˈteɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌsɛfəˌlɒtəˈsiːʌiː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants in the order Rosales, comprising only the genus Cephalotus, known for the Australian pitcher plant.
plant family of the Australian pitcher plant

Examples

The Cephalotaceae are best known for their unique pitcher-shaped leaves that trap insects.
Botanists have long debated the evolutionary relationships within the Cephalotaceae.
The family Cephalotaceae consists of a single genus endemic to southwestern Australia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cephalotus - genus containing the Australian pitcher plant

Etymology

From genus name Cephalotus, from Greek 'kephalē' (head), plus standard botanical family suffix '-aceae'.
