experiment

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspɛrəmənt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛrɪmənt / (BrE)
a scientific procedure undertaken to make a discovery, test a hypothesis, or demonstrate a known fact
controlled scientific test or procedure

Examples

The results of the experiment supported the scientist's original hypothesis.
Students conducted a simple experiment to observe the reaction between vinegar and baking soda.
Before publishing his findings, he repeated the experiment several times to ensure accuracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: test - a procedure intended to establish the quality, performance, or reliability of something
  • Synonym: trial - a test of performance, qualities, or suitability

Etymology

From Latin 'experimentum' (test, trial), from 'experiri' (to try).

5. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspɛrəmənt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛrɪmənt / (BrE)
an act or process of trying out a new idea, method, or activity to see what happens or to learn from the results
trying something new; test or trial

Examples

As an experiment, she took a different route home to see if it was faster.
He viewed his new diet as an experiment to improve his health.
The company launched a marketing experiment to reach younger customers.

Synset Examples

as an experiment he decided to grow a beard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trial - a test of performance or suitability
  • Synonym: test - an action to discover something or establish validity
  • Antonym: routine - a regular and unvarying procedure; not new

Etymology

From Latin experimentum (a trial, test), from experīrī (to try).
