deoxyribonucleic acid

1. NOUN

/ diˌɑksiˌraɪboʊnuˈkleɪɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / diˌɒksɪˌraɪbəʊnjuːˈkleɪɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a molecule consisting of long chains of nucleotides found in the cell nucleus, carrying genetic information in almost all living organisms
molecule carrying genetic information (DNA)
medical

Examples

Scientists study deoxyribonucleic acid to understand genetic disorders.
Every cell in the human body contains deoxyribonucleic acid that determines our hereditary traits.
The discovery of the double-helix structure of deoxyribonucleic acid revolutionized biology.

Synset Examples

DNA is the king of molecules

Related Terms

  • Synonym: DNA - abbreviation for deoxyribonucleic acid

Etymology

From deoxy- (removal of oxygen), ribose (a sugar), nucleic (relating to the cell nucleus), and acid.
