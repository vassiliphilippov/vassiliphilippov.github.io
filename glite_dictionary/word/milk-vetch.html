milk-vetch

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlkˌvɛtʃ / (AmE), / ˈmɪlkˌvɛtʃ / (BrE)
any of various herbaceous plants of the genus Astragalus, often found in temperate regions and known for their clusters of small flowers
plant of the genus Astragalus

Examples

The milk-vetch thrives in sandy soils across the western United States.
Botanists collected samples of milk-vetch for their research on native flora.
Some species of milk-vetch are important sources of food for insects and livestock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Astragalus - genus of plants known as milk-vetches

Etymology

From 'milk' + 'vetch' (the latter from Old French vei(l)che, from Latin vicia, a type of leguminous plant).
