Thrinax parviflora

1. NOUN

/ ˈθraɪnæks ˌpɑrvɪˈflɔrə / (AmE), / ˈθrɪnaks ˌpɑːvɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
A small palm tree native to southern Florida and the West Indies, closely resembling the silvertop palmetto.
species of small palm tree

Examples

The garden features several Thrinax parviflora trees along its tropical walkway.
Botanists are studying Thrinax parviflora for its resemblance to the silvertop palmetto.
Thrinax parviflora thrives in the warm climates of southern Florida and the West Indies.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Thrinax' + species epithet 'parviflora' (small-flowered).
