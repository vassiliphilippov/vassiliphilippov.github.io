rage

1. NOUN

/ reɪdʒ / (AmE), / reɪdʒ / (BrE)
A feeling of intense, uncontrollable anger.
intense anger

Examples

He stormed out of the room in a fit of rage after hearing the news.
Her eyes were filled with rage as she confronted the liar.
The driver honked his horn in rage at the traffic jam.

Synset Examples

hell hath no fury like a woman scorned
his face turned red with rage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anger - strong feeling of displeasure or hostility
  • Synonym: fury - violent or intense anger
  • Antonym: calm - the absence of anger; tranquility

Etymology

From Old French *rage*, from Latin *rabies* (madness, fury).
