sleigh

1. NOUN

/ sleɪ / (AmE), / sleɪ / (BrE)
a vehicle mounted on runners and drawn by horses or dogs, used for traveling over snow or ice
vehicle for traveling over snow

Examples

The children rode in a sleigh pulled by a team of horses across the snowy fields.
Santa Claus is famously said to travel in a sleigh on Christmas Eve.
They took a romantic sleigh ride through the wintry countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sled - a vehicle for traveling over snow, often smaller and not horse-drawn

Etymology

From Dutch *slee* (sledge) or German *Schlitten* (sled), entering English in the 18th century.

2. VERB

/ sleɪ / (AmE), / sleɪ / (BrE)
to ride or travel on a sleigh, especially over snow
ride or travel on a sleigh

Examples

Every winter, children love to sleigh down the big hill near the park.
We sleigh across the frozen fields when the snow is deep enough.
They had sleighed through the forest before sunrise, enjoying the quiet morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sled - to travel on a sled

Etymology

From the noun 'sleigh', which comes from Dutch 'slee' meaning 'sled'.
