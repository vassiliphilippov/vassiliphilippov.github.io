unaccountable

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnəˈkaʊntəbəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnəˈkaʊntəbl̩ / (BrE)
Not subject to control, supervision, or responsibility; not required to justify actions or decisions.
not subject to control or responsibility

Examples

The new agency was largely unaccountable to the public, raising concerns about oversight.
He preferred to operate in an unaccountable manner, avoiding rules and scrutiny.
Without checks and balances, officials can become unaccountable and abuse their power.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: accountable - required to justify actions or decisions; responsible

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'un-' (not) + 'accountable' (answerable, responsible), latter from 'account' (to explain) + '-able'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnəˈkaʊntəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌʌnəˈkaʊntəbl̩ / (BrE)
Impossible to explain or understand; not able to be accounted for.
impossible to explain

Examples

There was an unaccountable delay in the delivery of our package.
She felt an unaccountable sense of sadness that she couldn't describe.
For some unaccountable reason, the radio stopped working overnight.

Synset Examples

perceptible only as unaccountable influences that hinder progress
an unexplainable fear

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inexplicable - impossible to explain or understand
  • Antonym: accountable - required or expected to justify actions or decisions; responsible

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'accountable' (answerable, explainable).
