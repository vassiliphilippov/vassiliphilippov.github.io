tradespeople

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪdzˌpipəl / (AmE), / ˈtreɪdzˌpiːpəl / (BrE)
People who work in a skilled trade, especially those involved in manual or practical work such as electricians, plumbers, or carpenters.
people in skilled manual trades

Examples

The renovation project required several tradespeople, including plumbers and electricians.
Many self-employed tradespeople advertise their services online these days.
Skilled tradespeople are in high demand due to a shortage of apprentices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: craftsmen - people skilled in a particular craft
  • Antonym: professionals - people engaged in professional occupations (not manual trades)

Etymology

From 'trade' + 'people', used since the 18th century to refer collectively to those in skilled trades.
