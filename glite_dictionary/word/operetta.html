operetta

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑː.pəˈrɛ.tə / (AmE), / ˌɒp.əˈrɛt.ə / (BrE)
a short, light, and often amusing opera, typically with spoken dialogue and songs
light comic opera

Examples

The local theater group staged an operetta that left the audience laughing throughout the night.
Many famous composers, including Gilbert and Sullivan, wrote popular operettas.
Her favorite operetta features witty dialogue and catchy melodies.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: opera - a dramatic work in which the actors sing most or all of the lines

Etymology

From Italian 'operetta', diminutive of 'opera'.
