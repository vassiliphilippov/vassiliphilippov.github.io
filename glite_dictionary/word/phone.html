phone

1. NOUN

/ foʊn / (AmE), / fəʊn / (BrE)
(Phonetics) An individual speech sound considered without regard to its status as a phoneme in any language.
speech sound (phonetics)

Examples

In phonetic analysis, each phone is represented by a unique symbol in the IPA.
The difference between a phone and a phoneme is important in linguistics.
The [p] in 'spin' and 'pin' are different phones because they are pronounced differently.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: phoneme - distinct unit of sound that distinguishes meaning in a language

Etymology

From Greek phōnē (voice, sound).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07125755-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ foʊn / (AmE), / fəʊn / (BrE)
A device that converts sound into electrical signals for transmission over distances, then reconverts received signals back into sounds.
device for transmitting sound over distances

Examples

She left her phone on the kitchen counter and missed three important calls.
You can reach me on my phone if anything urgent comes up.
His phone rang just as he was about to leave for work.

Synset Examples

I talked to him on the telephone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telephone - a device for transmitting voices over a distance using wires or radio

Etymology

Short for 'telephone', from Greek phōnē (voice, sound).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04408223-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ foʊn / (AmE), / fəʊn / (BrE)
A device that converts electrical signals into sound, typically worn over or inserted into the ear for listening.
electro-acoustic transducer for sound (ear device)

Examples

She replaced her old phones with a new set that offered better sound quality.
Please use your phones to monitor the audio levels during the recording.
The technician handed me a pair of phones to check the clarity of the mix.

Synset Examples

it was not the typing but the earphones that she disliked

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earphone - small audio device for the ear
  • Synonym: headphone - pair of small speakers for ears, worn over the head

Etymology

Shortened from 'telephone', reflecting the technical use in audio equipment.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03266479-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ foʊn / (AmE), / fəʊn / (BrE)
to contact or try to contact someone by telephone
contact by telephone

Examples

I'll phone you when I arrive at the airport.
She forgot to phone her parents last night.
Could you phone the doctor and make an appointment?

Synset Examples

I tried to call you all night
Take two aspirin and call me in the morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: call - to communicate or attempt to communicate by telephone
  • Antonym: text - to send a written message electronically, especially by phone

Etymology

Shortened from 'telephone', from Greek tēle (far) + phōnē (voice, sound).
