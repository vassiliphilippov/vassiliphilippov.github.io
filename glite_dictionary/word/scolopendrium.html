scolopendrium

1. NOUN

/ skəˌloʊˈpɛn.dri.əm / (AmE), / skɒl.əˈpɛn.drɪ.əm / (BrE)
A species of fern with narrow, strap-shaped fronds, often thought to resemble a millipede.
a fern with narrow strap-shaped fronds

Examples

The scolopendrium thrives in shaded, moist woodland areas.
She planted a scolopendrium in the corner of her fern garden for its ornamental leaves.
Botanists often refer to scolopendrium by its common name, hart's-tongue fern.

Etymology

From Latin *scolopendra* (millipede), referring to the frond's resemblance.
