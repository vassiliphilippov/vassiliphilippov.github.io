boned

1. ADJ

/ boʊnd / (AmE), / bəʊnd / (BrE)
(in combination) having bones of a specified kind or quality.
having specified kind of bones (in compounds)

Examples

She has a small-boned frame that makes her look delicate.
The veterinarian commented on the dog's heavy-boned structure.
His long-boned arms made him an excellent swimmer.

Synset Examples

his lanky long-boned body

Related Terms

  • Antonym: boneless - having no bones

Etymology

Formed from 'bone' + '-ed' as an adjectival suffix to indicate possessing bones.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00296927-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ boʊnd / (AmE), / bəʊnd / (BrE)
(of meat or fish) having had the bones removed.
with bones removed (meat or fish)

Examples

For tonight's dinner, I bought a boned chicken to make preparation easier.
The restaurant offers boned trout as one of its specialties.
When making stock, use boned meat to achieve a clearer broth.

Synset Examples

a boneless rib roast
a boned (or deboned) fish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deboned - having had the bones removed
  • Antonym: bone-in - containing bones

Etymology

Formed from the verb "bone" (to remove bones) with the past participle suffix "-ed".

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00297348-s
Synset POS: s
