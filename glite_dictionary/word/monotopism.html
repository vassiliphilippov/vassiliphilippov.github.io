monotopism

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːnoʊˈtoʊpɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəʊˈtəʊpɪzəm / (BrE)
The origin of a systematic group in biology occurring only once, or at a single geographic location, often due to mutation.
single-point origin in biology

Examples

The researcher proposed that the family originated through monotopism in the Tertiary period.
Debates continue regarding whether the species' distribution resulted from monotopism or polytopism.
Understanding monotopism helps clarify patterns in the global spread of certain plants.

Synset Examples

Discusses the significance of monotopism and polytopism in quantitative analyses of Tertiary floras.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polytopism - origin of a systematic group at multiple locations

Etymology

From Greek 'mono-' (single) + 'topos' (place) + '-ism'.
