homogeny

1. NOUN

/ həˈmɑːdʒəni / (AmE), / hɒˈmɒdʒəni / (BrE)
(biology) similarity in structure or origin between organisms or parts as a result of shared evolutionary ancestry.
biological similarity from common ancestry
medical

Examples

The presence of similar bone structures in birds and mammals demonstrates homogeny resulting from a distant common ancestor.
Scientists study homogeny to understand the evolutionary relationships between different species.
The concept of homogeny is central to modern evolutionary biology and taxonomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homology - biological similarity due to shared ancestry
  • Antonym: analogy - biological similarity due to convergent evolution, not common ancestry

Etymology

From Greek 'homogeneia' meaning 'of the same kind', from 'homo-' (same) + 'genos' (kind, race).
