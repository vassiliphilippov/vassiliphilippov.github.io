swallowtail

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɑloʊˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈswɒləʊˌteɪl / (BrE)
a man's formal jacket with long, tapering tails at the back, typically worn as evening dress
man's formal jacket with tails

Examples

He looked elegant in his black swallowtail at the gala.
The orchestra conductor wore a classic swallowtail for the performance.
Swapping his usual suit for a swallowtail, he prepared for the formal dinner.

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to the forked tail of a swallow (the bird).
