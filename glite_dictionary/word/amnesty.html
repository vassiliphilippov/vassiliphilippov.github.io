amnesty

1. NOUN

/ ˈæm.nə.sti / (AmE), / ˈæm.nə.sti / (BrE)
an official period during which people who have committed offenses are pardoned and exempt from punishment
official pardon and exemption from punishment

Examples

The government declared an amnesty for political prisoners, allowing many to return home.
During the amnesty, thousands of weapons were handed in to the police without penalty.
A tax amnesty was granted to encourage citizens to disclose previously undeclared income.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pardon - the action of forgiving or being forgiven for an error or offense
  • Synonym: reprieve - a cancellation or postponement of punishment

Etymology

From Greek amnēstia (forgetfulness), via Latin amnestia.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæm.nə.sti / (AmE), / ˈæm.nə.sti / (BrE)
an official pardon for people who have been convicted of political offenses or granted release from punishment for an offense
official pardon for offenses
legal

Examples

The government declared an amnesty for all political prisoners.
He was released from jail after receiving an amnesty.
The rebels surrendered under the promise of amnesty for past crimes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pardon - an official decision to forgive someone for a crime
  • Synonym: clemency - mercy or lenience shown in punishing someone

Etymology

From Greek amnēstia (forgetfulness, pardon), from amnēstos (forgotten).

3. NOUN

/ ˈæm.nə.sti / (AmE), / ˈæm.nə.sti / (BrE)
an official pardon granted by a government to a group or class of people, usually for political offenses or crimes, often resulting in their liberation
official pardon, usually for political offenses
legal

Examples

The new government declared an amnesty for all political prisoners.
After years in exile, the activists were allowed to return following the presidential amnesty.
A general amnesty was announced for those who had evaded military service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pardon - the official forgiveness of a crime and cancellation of punishment
  • Synonym: clemency - mercy or leniency shown by someone in authority

Etymology

From Greek amnēstia (forgetfulness), from amnēstos (forgotten).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01251862-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈæm.nə.sti / (AmE), / ˈæm.nə.sti / (BrE)
to grant an official pardon to a group of people, especially for political offenses
grant an official pardon

Examples

The government decided to amnesty all political prisoners after the revolution.
The new leader promised to amnesty those who had opposed the regime.
It is rare for a country to amnesty people convicted of serious crimes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pardon - to officially forgive someone for an offense

Etymology

From Greek amnēstia (forgetfulness), from amnēstos (forgotten).
