conservative

1. ADJ

/ kənˈsɜːrvətɪv / (AmE), / kənˈsɜːvətɪv / (BrE)
unwilling to accept or support changes or new ideas, especially in politics or religion, and preferring traditional practices or values
opposed to change; favoring tradition

Examples

Her parents have very conservative views about marriage and family life.
As a conservative politician, she opposed most policies aimed at reform.
The school maintains a conservative dress code that has not changed in decades.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: liberal - open to new behavior or opinions and willing to discard traditional values

Etymology

From Latin 'conservare' (to preserve).

Many people in rural areas tend to be more conservative than those in cities.

4. NOUN

/ kənˈsɝː.və.tɪv / (AmE), / kənˈsɜː.və.tɪv / (BrE)
a person who is resistant to change and new ideas, typically favoring traditional views and values
person who resists change; favors tradition

Examples

As a conservative, she opposed most of the reforms proposed by the new administration.
His views were seen as old-fashioned, making him the only conservative in the group.
Despite being a conservative, he was willing to listen to opposing arguments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traditionalist - a person who supports traditional views and values
