Eragrostis tef

1. NOUN

/ ɛrəˈɡrɑstɪs tɛf / (AmE), / ɛrəˈɡrɒstɪs tɛf / (BrE)
a species of grass native to Ethiopia, cultivated for its small edible seeds (teff), which are used as a cereal grain and for forage
an African cereal grass, source of teff grain

Examples

Ethiopian farmers have cultivated Eragrostis tef for thousands of years as a staple crop.
The gluten-free flour produced from Eragrostis tef is gaining popularity in Western baking.
Research is ongoing to improve the yield and drought resistance of Eragrostis tef.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teff - the edible seed produced by Eragrostis tef, used as a cereal grain

Etymology

From scientific Latin, genus name *Eragrostis* and species epithet *tef*, from Amharic *ṭēff* (the grain).
