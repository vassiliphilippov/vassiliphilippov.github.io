Cathaya

1. NOUN

/ kæˈθaɪ.ə / (AmE), / kæˈθaɪ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen coniferous trees native to China, first discovered in 1955, not widely cultivated outside its native habitat.
genus of Chinese evergreen conifers

Examples

The rare Cathaya was once thought extinct until its discovery in the Chinese mountains.
Botanists are studying Cathaya to understand its evolutionary relationship to other conifers.
Unlike pine or fir, Cathaya trees remain largely unknown outside China.

Etymology

Named after 'Cathay,' an old name for China, indicating its native region.

2. NOUN

/ kæˈθaɪ.ə / (AmE), / kæˈθaɪ.ə / (BrE)
a genus of coniferous trees in the family Pinaceae, related to Pseudotsuga and Larix, with only one known species native to China
a genus of coniferous trees (Pinaceae)

Examples

The discovery of Cathaya in China was significant for botanists studying conifer evolution.
Researchers found that Cathaya is closely related to Pseudotsuga and Larix.
Only one living species belongs to the genus Cathaya, making it extremely rare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pseudotsuga - a genus of coniferous trees related to Cathaya, commonly known as Douglas-fir
  • Synonym: Larix - a genus of coniferous trees known as larches, related to Cathaya

Etymology

Named from Chinese place names and botanical Latin practice.
