mealy-mouthed

1. ADJ

/ ˈmiːli ˌmaʊθt / (AmE), / ˈmiːli ˌmaʊθt / (BrE)
hesitant or unwilling to state facts or opinions simply and directly, often out of timidity, insincerity, or hypocrisy
insincerely indirect in speech

Examples

The manager's mealy-mouthed explanation failed to reassure the staff.
She grew impatient with his mealy-mouthed responses to her direct questions.
Voters are tired of mealy-mouthed promises from politicians.

Synset Examples

a mealymouthed politician

Related Terms

  • Antonym: direct - honest and straightforward in speech or manner
  • Antonym: forthright - direct and outspoken; straightforward and honest

Etymology

From 'mealy' + 'mouthed', originally with reference to having meal (ground grain) in the mouth and thus being unable to speak clearly.
