to exenterate

1. VERB

/ ɪɡˈzɛntəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɛntəreɪt / (BrE)
to surgically remove the contents of an organ, especially in a medical context
to remove the contents of an organ
medical

Examples

The surgeons decided to exenterate the affected sinus due to the severity of the infection.
In some cases of advanced cancer, doctors may need to exenterate the pelvic organs.
The procedure to exenterate the cavity was lengthy but ultimately successful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eviscerate - to remove the internal organs, especially the entrails

Etymology

From Latin exenteratus, past participle of exenterare, from ex- (out) + Greek enteron (intestine).
