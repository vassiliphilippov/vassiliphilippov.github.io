airflow

1. NOUN

/ ˈerˌfloʊ / (AmE), / ˈɛəfləʊ / (BrE)
the movement of air, especially as it relates to the way it moves through and around things
the movement of air

Examples

The window was opened to increase the airflow in the stuffy room.
Proper airflow is essential for cooling electronic equipment.
Pilots monitor airflow over the wings to ensure safe takeoff conditions.

Synset Examples

she adjusted the fan so that the airflow was directed right at her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: air current - a mass of air moving in a certain direction

Etymology

Formed from 'air' + 'flow' in English, first attested in the 20th century.
