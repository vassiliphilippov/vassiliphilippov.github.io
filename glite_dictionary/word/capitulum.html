capitulum

1. NOUN

/ kəˈpɪtʃələm / (AmE), / kəˈpɪtjʊləm / (BrE)
A dense cluster of flowers or foliage, often forming a compact head, as seen in some plants such as daisies or sunflowers.
dense cluster of flowers or foliage

Examples

The sunflower's bright yellow capitulum attracts bees and other pollinators.
A dense capitulum sits at the top of each stem, composed of many small florets.
Botanists observed the capitulum to study patterns of flower arrangement.

Synset Examples

a head of cauliflower
a head of lettuce

Related Terms

  • Synonym: head - the compact inflorescence or cluster of a plant

Etymology

From Latin 'capitulum' (little head), diminutive of 'caput' (head).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈpɪtʃələm / (AmE), / kəˈpɪtʃjʊləm / (BrE)
a cluster of leafy branches forming the top or head of a tree or plant; in botany, an inflorescence with numerous small flowers collected on a flat or rounded structure
cluster of leafy branches or flower heads

Examples

The oak tree's capitulum provided shelter for many birds during the summer.
A botanist examined the capitulum to identify the species of the rare tree.
Some plants develop a dense capitulum as part of their flowering structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inflorescence - a group or cluster of flowers on a stem

Etymology

From Latin *capitulum* (little head), diminutive of *caput* (head).

3. NOUN

/ kəˈpɪtjələm / (AmE), / kəˈpɪtjʊləm / (BrE)
A short scripture reading, especially one following a psalmody during religious services.
short liturgical scripture reading

Examples

During the evening service, the deacon read the capitulum before the prayers began.
The choir paused so a capitulum could be recited by the presiding priest.
The congregation listened attentively to the brief capitulum following the psalm.

Synset Examples

We can distinguish between a short and long reading, called capitula or lectio respectively.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: lectio - a longer liturgical scripture reading

Etymology

From Latin capitulum (a little head, chapter).

4. NOUN

/ kəˈpɪtʃələm / (AmE), / kəˈpɪtjʊləm / (BrE)
a compact head or cluster of stalkless flowers or fruiting bodies, especially in plants such as cereals and daisies
compact flower or fruit cluster

Examples

The sunflower produces a large capitulum that is actually a cluster of many small florets.
Each ear of corn develops from a single capitulum at the top of the stalk.
Botanists examined the capitulum to study seed development in barley.

Etymology

From Latin capitulum (small head), diminutive of caput (head).
