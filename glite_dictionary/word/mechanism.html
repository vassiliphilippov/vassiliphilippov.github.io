mechanism

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛkəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmɛkənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
a natural structure or process that functions like a machine, especially in anatomy or biology
natural structure working like a machine

Examples

Scientists have studied the mechanism of the immune response in detail.
The mechanism of hearing involves a series of complex biological processes.
Researchers are still investigating the mechanism by which cells repair DNA damage.

Synset Examples

the mechanism of the ear
the mechanism of infection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: structure - an arrangement of parts in a system or organism

Etymology

From Greek mēkhanē (machine) via Latin mechanismus.

5. NOUN

/ ˈmɛkəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmɛkənɪzəm / (BrE)
(Philosophy) The theory that all natural phenomena can be explained entirely by physical or biological processes, without recourse to metaphysics or the supernatural.
philosophical theory of physical explanation

Examples

Some philosophers argue that mechanism cannot account for consciousness.
The rise of mechanism in the 17th century marked a shift in scientific thinking.
Critics of mechanism claim it overlooks the role of subjective experience.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vitalism - the theory that living things are fundamentally different from non-living things because of a 'vital force'

Etymology

From French 'mécanisme', from Greek 'mēkhanē' (machine).

WordNet ID: oewn-05981648-n
Synset POS: n
