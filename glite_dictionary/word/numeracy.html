numeracy

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːmərəsi / (AmE), / ˈnjuːmərəsi / (BrE)
the ability to understand and work with numbers, especially basic mathematics
mathematical ability

Examples

Good numeracy skills are essential for managing personal finances effectively.
The school focuses on improving children's numeracy as well as their literacy.
Businesses often require a basic level of numeracy from their employees.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: literacy - the ability to read and write
  • Antonym: innumeracy - lack of ability to understand and work with numbers

Etymology

Formed in English in the 1950s from 'numeral' + '-acy', modeled on 'literacy'.
