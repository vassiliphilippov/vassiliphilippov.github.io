Myrmecophaga jubata

1. NOUN

/ mɜːrmɪˌkɒfəɡə dʒuːˈbeɪtə / (AmE), / mɜːmɪˌkɒfəɡə dʒuːˈbɑːtə / (BrE)
the scientific name for the giant anteater, a large, shaggy-haired, toothless mammal native to South America known for its long tongue and powerful claws
scientific name for giant anteater

Examples

The Myrmecophaga jubata is easily recognized by its elongated snout and bushy tail.
Researchers are studying the eating habits of Myrmecophaga jubata in its natural habitat.
A documentary featured the powerful claws of Myrmecophaga jubata as it searched for ants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant anteater - a large South American mammal known for eating ants and termites

Etymology

From Greek 'myrmex' (ant) + 'phagein' (to eat); 'jubata' from Latin meaning 'maned' or 'crested'.
