conditioning

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdɪʃ.ə.nɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃ.ən.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which an organism learns to associate a particular stimulus with a specific response, so that its behavior becomes dependent on that stimulus
learning process involving stimulus-response associations
medical

Examples

Classical conditioning helps explain how certain phobias develop in people.
Through operant conditioning, the rats learned to press the lever for food.
The dog's salivation at the sound of a bell was a result of conditioning.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: habituation - the diminishing of a physiological or emotional response to a frequently repeated stimulus
  • Degree Higher: sensitization - an increased reaction to a stimulus following repeated exposure
  • Synonym: learning - the process of gaining knowledge or skill through study, experience, or teaching

Etymology

From the verb 'condition', in the sense of 'train, teach a response'. Use in psychology dates from early 20th century.
