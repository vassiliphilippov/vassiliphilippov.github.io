Pericles

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɛrɪˌkliz / (AmE), / ˈpɛrɪkliːz / (BrE)
(c. 495–429 BC) An influential Athenian statesman and general during Athens' Golden Age, known for advancing democracy and commissioning the Parthenon.
Athenian statesman and leader in the Golden Age

Examples

Pericles played a key role in developing the democratic institutions of ancient Athens.
The Parthenon was built under the leadership of Pericles.
Many historians consider Pericles as one of the greatest statesmen in Western history.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Περικλῆς (Periklēs), meaning 'surrounded by glory'.
