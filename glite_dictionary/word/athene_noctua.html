Athene noctua

1. NOUN

/ əˈθini nɑkˈtuə / (AmE), / əˈθiːni nɒkˈtjuːə / (BrE)
A species of small European owl commonly known as the little owl, recognized by its rounded head and bright yellow eyes.
species of small European owl

Examples

The Athene noctua is widely found throughout southern Europe and parts of Asia.
The Athene noctua prefers open countryside and farms where it hunts insects and small mammals.
In mythology, Athene noctua is often associated with wisdom and the goddess Athena.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: little owl - common English name for Athene noctua

Etymology

From Greek goddess Athene and Latin noctua (owl), referring to the owl's association with wisdom and night.
