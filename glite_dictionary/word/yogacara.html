Yogacara

1. NOUN

/ joʊgəˈtʃɑrə / (AmE), / jəʊgəˈtʃɑːrə / (BrE)
a major tradition of Mahayana Buddhism that teaches only consciousness is real and external objects exist only as ideas or mental states
Buddhist school teaching reality is only consciousness

Examples

She studied Yogacara philosophy to better understand the Buddhist perspective on the nature of reality.
Many scholars believe Yogacara had a profound influence on East Asian Buddhism.
The Yogacara doctrine emphasizes that all experiences arise from consciousness rather than from independent external objects.

Etymology

From Sanskrit *yogācāra* (yoga practice, practitioner of yoga), referring to its emphasis on meditative practice.
