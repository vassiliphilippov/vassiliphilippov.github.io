intravenous

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.trəˈvi.nəs / (AmE), / ˌɪn.trəˈviː.nəs / (BrE)
Administered or occurring within or by way of a vein.
within or via a vein
medical

Examples

The doctor administered the medication intravenously to ensure quick absorption.
An intravenous drip was set up to keep the patient hydrated during surgery.
Nutrients are sometimes given by intravenous infusion when patients cannot eat.

Synset Examples

an intravenous inflammation
intravenous feeding

Related Terms

  • Antonym: subcutaneous - under the skin; not into a vein
  • Antonym: intramuscular - within a muscle; a different injection route

Etymology

From Latin 'intra-' (within) + 'vena' (vein).
