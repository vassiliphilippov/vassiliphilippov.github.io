myxobacterium

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪk.soʊ.bækˈtɪr.i.əm / (AmE), / ˌmɪks.əʊ.bækˈtɪə.ri.əm / (BrE)
a type of bacterium that forms colonies within self-produced slime, typically found in moist soil or decaying organic matter.
type of bacterium forming slimy colonies
medical

Examples

Scientists studied a colony of myxobacterium thriving in the decaying leaf litter.
The myxobacterium moved across the moist soil by producing a slime trail.
Researchers discovered a new species of myxobacterium in animal waste.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bacterium - a single-celled microorganism

Etymology

From Greek myxa (slime, mucus) + bacterium.
