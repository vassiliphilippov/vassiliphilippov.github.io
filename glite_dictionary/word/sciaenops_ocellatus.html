Sciaenops ocellatus

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈæn.ɒps oʊˌsɛˈleɪ.təs / (AmE), / saɪˈæn.ɒps əʊˌsɛˈlɑː.təs / (BrE)
a large edible marine fish, also known as the red drum, found along the Atlantic coast of the United States from Massachusetts to Mexico
large edible fish; the red drum

Examples

Many anglers travel to the Gulf Coast hoping to catch a Sciaenops ocellatus for sport and food.
Conservation efforts have helped restore populations of Sciaenops ocellatus along the Atlantic seaboard.
The Sciaenops ocellatus is appreciated both by seafood lovers and recreational fishermen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red drum - a common name for Sciaenops ocellatus, a large edible fish of Atlantic coastal waters

Etymology

From Latin: 'Sciaenops' (fish genus) and 'ocellatus' (spotted), referring to the fish's characteristic spot(s).
