Eumenides

1. NOUN

/ juːˈmɛnɪˌdiːz / (AmE), / juːˈmɛnɪˌdiːz / (BrE)
(in classical mythology) the three hideous, snake-haired goddesses who pursued and punished unpunished criminals; also known as the Furies.
mythological goddesses who punish criminals

Examples

In Aeschylus' play, the Eumenides appear to pursue Orestes after he avenges his father's murder.
The ancient Greeks believed the Eumenides punished those who escaped earthly justice.
Statues of the Eumenides often depict them with serpents in their hair and a fearsome appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Furies - the same group of avenging goddesses in Roman mythology
  • Synonym: Erinyes - another name for the Eumenides in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Greek *Eumenides* (Εὐμενίδες), meaning 'the gracious ones', a euphemistic name for the Furies.
