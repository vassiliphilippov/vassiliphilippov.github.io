physiatrics

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɪziˈætrɪks / (AmE), / ˌfɪziˈætrɪks / (BrE)
the branch of medicine that uses physical agents such as exercise, massage, and other modalities to treat disease or injury
branch of medicine using physical therapy
medical

Examples

After his stroke, he began a program of physiatrics to regain motor function.
The clinic specializes in physiatrics for patients with musculoskeletal disorders.
Many hospitals now offer physiatrics as part of their rehabilitation services.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: physiatry - branch of medicine focused on physical rehabilitation and therapy
  • Synonym: physical therapy - treatment using physical methods such as exercise and massage

Etymology

From Greek physis (nature, physical) + iatreia (healing).
