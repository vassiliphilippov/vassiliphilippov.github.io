informality

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnfɔrˈmæləti / (AmE), / ˌɪnfɔːˈmæləti / (BrE)
the quality of being relaxed and free from constraint or embarrassment
state of being relaxed and unconstrained

Examples

There was a sense of informality at the gathering, which put everyone at ease.
She appreciated the informality of their conversations, feeling she could speak freely.
The informality of the event meant guests could wear whatever they liked.

Synset Examples

I am never at ease with strangers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: formality - the rigid observance of conventions or etiquette

Etymology

From 'informal' + '-ity', modeled after Latin '-itas' meaning state or condition.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnfɔːrˈmæləti / (AmE), / ˌɪnfɔːˈmælɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being relaxed and unconcerned with strict rules, forms, or ceremonies
relaxed, unofficial manner

Examples

There was a pleasant informality in the way she welcomed her guests.
The informality of the ceremony made everyone feel comfortable.
His informality was refreshing after so many rigid meetings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: formality - strict adherence to established rules or customs
  • Synonym: casualness - the quality of being relaxed or unconcerned

Etymology

From French informalité, from Medieval Latin informalitas, from in- (not) + formalis (formal).
