to enrobe

1. VERB

/ ɛnˈroʊb / (AmE), / ɪnˈrəʊb / (BrE)
to dress or cover with a robe, or to cover as if with a robe
to dress or cover with a robe

Examples

The priest was enrobed in ceremonial garments before the procession.
A layer of chocolate enrobes each delicate truffle.
She enrobed herself in a velvet cloak for the masquerade ball.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robe - a long, loose outer garment

Etymology

From French 'enrober', from 'en-' (in) + 'robe' (robe).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00044795-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈroʊb / (AmE), / ɪnˈrəʊb / (BrE)
to cover or coat (something) with a layer, especially in cooking or manufacturing
to cover or coat with a layer

Examples

The chocolatier will enrobe the strawberries in dark chocolate for dessert.
Manufacturers often enrobe cereal pieces with a sugary coating to improve taste.
After roasting, the chef decided to enrobe the nuts in caramel for added flavor.

Synset Examples

enrobe the nuts with chocolate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coat - to cover something with a layer or substance
  • Synonym: cover - to put something on or over, so as to protect or enclose

Etymology

From French 'enrober', from 'en-' (in) and 'robe' (robe, covering).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01337884-v
Synset POS: v
