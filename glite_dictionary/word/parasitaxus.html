Parasitaxus

1. PROPN

/ pɛrəsaɪˈtæksəs / (AmE), / pærəsɪˈtæksəs / (BrE)
A monotypic genus of conifer from New Caledonia, the only known parasitic conifer species, commonly called the parasite yew.
parasitic conifer genus (parasite yew)

Examples

Parasitaxus is unique among conifers for its completely parasitic lifestyle.
Scientists traveled to New Caledonia to study the rare Parasitaxus plant.
The survival of Parasitaxus depends entirely on its host trees for nutrients.

Etymology

From Greek 'parasitos' (parasitic) and 'Taxus' (yew genus).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpær.ə.sɪˈtæks.əs / (AmE), / ˌpær.ə.sɪˈtæks.əs / (BrE)
A genus of coniferous plants containing a single known species, Parasitaxus usta, a parasitic yew found in New Caledonia.
genus of parasitic yews

Examples

Parasitaxus is notable as the only known parasitic gymnosperm.
Botanists traveled to New Caledonia to study Parasitaxus in its natural habitat.
Unlike other conifers, Parasitaxus derives nutrients from the roots of Falcatifolium trees.

Etymology

From Greek 'pará' (beside) + 'Taxus' (the yew genus), indicating its relationship to yew trees.
