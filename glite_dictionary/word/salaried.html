salaried

1. ADJ

/ ˈsælərid / (AmE), / ˈsælərid / (BrE)
receiving a regular salary for employment, especially as opposed to being paid hourly or per job
receiving a regular salary

Examples

All salaried employees will receive health insurance benefits.
The company offered both salaried and hourly positions.
Many salaried workers have set office hours but sometimes work overtime.

Synset Examples

salaried members of the staff

Related Terms

  • Antonym: waged - receiving wages, usually paid hourly or per day/week

Etymology

From 'salary,' which comes from Latin 'salarium' (money given to soldiers for salt), plus English '-ed' suffix.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00866904-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈsælərid / (AmE), / ˈsælərid / (BrE)
(of a person or position) receiving a fixed regular payment or salary for work, especially in contrast to being paid hourly or by commission.
receiving a fixed regular salary

Examples

Many salaried employees do not track the exact hours they work each week.
The company offers benefits only to its salaried staff, not to part-time workers.
He moved from an hourly wage job to a salaried position last year.

Synset Examples

salaried workers
a stipendiary magistrate

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hourly - paid according to the number of hours worked
  • Synonym: stipendiary - receiving a stipend or fixed regular payment

Etymology

From salary + -ed, denoting having a salary.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01713351-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈsælərid / (AmE), / ˈsælərid / (BrE)
(of a job or position) receiving a regular salary as payment, rather than being paid by the hour or for each task.
receiving a regular salary as payment

Examples

She accepted a salaried position as a project manager at the company.
Many workers prefer salaried employment for the stability it offers.
Teachers in this district are salaried employees with excellent benefits.

Synset Examples

a paying job
remunerative work
salaried employment
stipendiary services

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paid - receiving payment for work
  • Antonym: waged - paid by the hour or day rather than a salary

Etymology

From 'salary', from Old French 'salaire', from Latin 'salarium' (money given to soldiers for salt).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01713116-s
Synset POS: s
