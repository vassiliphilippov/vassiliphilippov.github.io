maxillaria

1. NOUN

/ mækˈsɪləriə / (AmE), / makˈsɪləriə / (BrE)
A large genus of orchids, often cultivated for their large, brightly colored solitary flowers.
genus of orchids with showy flowers

Examples

The greenhouse boasts a rare maxillaria among its orchid collection.
Botanists discovered several new maxillaria species in the South American rainforest.
The maxillaria is prized for its dramatic, solitary blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - a plant of the family Orchidaceae, often with showy flowers

Etymology

From Latin 'maxilla' (jaw), referring to the jaw-like shape of flowers in this genus.
