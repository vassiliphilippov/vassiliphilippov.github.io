Maxillaria

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæksɪˈlɛriə / (AmE), / ˌmæksɪˈlɪəriə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical American epiphytic orchids with leathery leaves and usually bearing single-flowered stalks
a genus of tropical American orchids

Examples

The Maxillaria orchid is popular among collectors for its unique flowers.
Many species in the Maxillaria genus thrive in warm, humid environments.
Botanists recently discovered a new Maxillaria species deep in the rainforest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - a plant of the family Orchidaceae, known for its complex flowers

Etymology

From Latin 'maxilla' meaning 'jaw', referring to the jaw-like structure of the flower.
