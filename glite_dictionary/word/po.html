Po

1. PROPN

/ poʊ / (AmE), / pəʊ / (BrE)
a river in northern Italy that flows eastward into the Adriatic Sea
a major river in northern Italy

Examples

The Po is the longest river in Italy, passing through many important cities.
Flooding of the Po has historically caused problems for the surrounding regions.
Agricultural land along the Po valley is some of the most fertile in Europe.

2. NOUN

/ poʊ / (AmE), / pəʊ / (BrE)
the symbol for the chemical element polonium, a rare, highly radioactive metal found in uranium ores
symbol for polonium (element)

Examples

The chemical symbol Po stands for polonium on the periodic table.
Scientists studied Po to learn more about radioactive elements.
Marie Curie was the first to isolate Po from uranium ores.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polonium - a rare, highly radioactive metallic element (symbol: Po)

Etymology

From the name 'Poland', birthplace of Marie Curie, who discovered the element.
