Clematis crispa

1. NOUN

/ kləˈmætɪs ˈkrɪspə / (AmE), / klɪˈmætɪs ˈkrɪspə / (BrE)
A climbing plant native to the southern United States, distinguished by its bluish-purple flowers.
climbing plant with bluish-purple flowers

Examples

The garden trellis was covered in Clematis crispa, adding a splash of blue and purple to the yard.
Many gardeners in the southern states admire the delicate flowers of Clematis crispa.
We planted a Clematis crispa near the fence to attract butterflies and hummingbirds.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: clematis - a genus of climbing plants often with showy flowers

Etymology

From Latin 'Clematis' (the genus name) and Latin 'crispa', meaning 'curled' or 'curly'.
