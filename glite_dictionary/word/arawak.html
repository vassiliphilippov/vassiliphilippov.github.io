Arawak

1. NOUN

/ ˈærəˌwɑk / (AmE), / ˈarəwak / (BrE)
a family of indigenous languages spoken in northeastern South America, especially by the Arawak peoples
South American indigenous language family

Examples

The Arawak languages are still spoken in parts of Guyana and Suriname.
Linguists study the Arawak family to understand pre-Columbian cultures.
Several dialects within the Arawak group have survived despite colonial influences.

Etymology

From the name of the Arawak people, originally from the northern coast of South America and the Caribbean.

2. NOUN

/ ˈærəˌwæk / (AmE), / ˈærəwæk / (BrE)
a member of a group of Indigenous peoples native to northeastern South America and the Caribbean
Indigenous people of South America and the Caribbean

Examples

Many modern descendants of the Arawak still live in Guyana and Suriname.
Historians believe the Arawak were among the first peoples encountered by Columbus.
Artifacts from the Arawak have been found across the Caribbean islands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Carib - another Indigenous group native to the Caribbean, often associated with the Arawak

Etymology

From Spanish 'arahuaco', from the self-designation of the Arawak people.
