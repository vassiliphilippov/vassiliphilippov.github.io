Ficus

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪkəs / (AmE), / ˈfaɪkəs / (BrE)
A large genus of tropical trees, shrubs, or climbing plants, including the fig tree, with leathery leaves and often producing syconium fruit.
genus of tropical trees and shrubs, including figs

Examples

The Ficus in our living room has grown rapidly over the past year.
Some species within the Ficus genus produce edible fruit known as figs.
Botanists study different Ficus varieties to understand their ecological roles in tropical forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fig - tree or fruit especially of Ficus carica

Etymology

From Latin *ficus* (fig tree, fig), used in scientific naming.
