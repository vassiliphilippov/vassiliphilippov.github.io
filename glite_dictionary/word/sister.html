sister

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪstɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪstə / (BrE)
a female person who shares one or both parents with another person; a female sibling
female sibling

Examples

My sister is three years younger than me.
She called her sister to share the good news.
Both sisters have always been very close.

Synset Examples

my sister married a musician

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brother - a male person who has the same parents as another person; male sibling

Etymology

From Old English sweostor, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch zuster, German Schwester, and Latin soror (sister).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪstɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪstə / (BrE)
A female member of the same organization, such as a sorority, labor union, or religious community.
female fellow member of a group

Examples

The union sisters gathered to support one another during the negotiations.
She considers every woman in her sorority a sister for life.
The sisters in the religious order meet daily for prayer and discussion.

Synset Examples

none of her sisters would betray her

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brother - a male member of a group or organization
