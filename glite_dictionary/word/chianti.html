Chianti

1. NOUN

/ kiˈɑːn.ti / (AmE), / kiˈanti / (BrE)
A dry red Italian wine produced in the Chianti region of Tuscany.
a dry red wine from Tuscany, Italy

Examples

She brought a bottle of Chianti to the dinner party.
The rich flavor of Chianti pairs well with pasta dishes.
He sipped his glass of Chianti while enjoying the sunset on the terrace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wine - an alcoholic drink made from fermented grapes

Etymology

From Italian Chianti, the name of a region in Tuscany, Italy, known for its wine.
