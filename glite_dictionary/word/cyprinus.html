Cyprinus

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈpraɪnəs / (AmE), / sɪˈprɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater fish in the family Cyprinidae, which includes the common carp.
genus of freshwater fish (carp)

Examples

The common carp belongs to the genus Cyprinus.
Several species in Cyprinus are widely farmed for food and ornamental purposes.
Researchers studied the population dynamics of Cyprinus in the local river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carp - a large freshwater fish of the family Cyprinidae

Etymology

From Latin *cyprinus* (carp), from Greek *kyprinos*.
