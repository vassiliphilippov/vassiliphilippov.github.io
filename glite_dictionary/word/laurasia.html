Laurasia

1. PROPN

/ lɔˈreɪʒə / (AmE), / lɔːˈreɪzɪə / (BrE)
a large ancient supercontinent in the Northern Hemisphere that split to form North America, Europe, and Asia according to plate tectonic theory
ancient northern supercontinent

Examples

Many scientists believe Laurasia split into several continents during the Mesozoic era.
Fossil evidence supports the past existence of Laurasia as a single landmass.
The breakup of Laurasia had significant effects on Earth's climate and biodiversity.

Etymology

Formed from 'Laurentia' (a name for the North American craton) and 'Eurasia'.
