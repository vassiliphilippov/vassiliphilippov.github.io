Kapsiki

1. NOUN

/ kæpˈsiːki / (AmE), / kapˈsiːki / (BrE)
a Chadic language spoken mainly south of Lake Chad, particularly in Adamawa State, Nigeria, and the Mandara mountains region.
a Chadic language of Nigeria and Cameroon

Examples

The Kapsiki language is spoken by thousands of people in northern Nigeria.
Several linguistic studies have focused on the grammar of Kapsiki.
He traveled to the Mandara mountains to learn about Kapsiki culture and traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chadic - relating to a branch of the Afro-Asiatic language family
