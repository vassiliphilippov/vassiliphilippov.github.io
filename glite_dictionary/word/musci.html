Musci

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌskaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmʌskɪ / (BrE)
A class of bryophytes comprising the true mosses, characterized by leafy rather than thalloid gametophytes.
class of true mosses (bryophytes)

Examples

The Musci play a key role in many forest ecosystems by retaining moisture and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
Early botanists studied the Musci to better understand plant evolution among bryophytes.
Due to their leafy gametophytes, Musci are easily distinguished from liverworts in the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moss - a small, green, nonvascular plant
  • Antonym: hepatics - liverworts: bryophytes with thalloid or leafy gametophytes, not true mosses

Etymology

From Latin muscus (moss).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌs.aɪ / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.aɪ / (BrE)
A class of non-vascular plants known as true mosses, characterized by leafy gametophytes and including several orders such as Bryales and Sphagnales.
class of true mosses (plants)

Examples

Scientists classify Sphagnum peat moss within the Musci.
The Musci have leafy stems unlike the liverworts, which are more thalloid.
Many forest floors are carpeted with members of the Musci.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moss - small, non-vascular green plant of the division Bryophyta

Etymology

From Latin 'musci', plural of 'muscus' (moss).
