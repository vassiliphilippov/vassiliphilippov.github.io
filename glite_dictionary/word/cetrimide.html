cetrimide

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːtrəˌmaɪd / (AmE), / ˈsiːtrɪmʌɪd / (BrE)
a cationic detergent and antiseptic used as a powerful disinfectant, especially in medical settings
cationic disinfectant and antiseptic
medical

Examples

The wound was cleaned with a solution containing cetrimide to minimize the risk of infection.
Medical instruments are often sterilized using cetrimide in hospitals and clinics.
The label indicates that the disinfectant contains cetrimide as its active ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiseptic - a substance that prevents infection by inhibiting the growth of microorganisms

Etymology

From the chemical name cetyltrimethylammonium bromide.
