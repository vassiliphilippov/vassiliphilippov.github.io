Habenaria dilatata

1. PROPN

/ hæbəˈnɛəriə daɪˈleɪtə / (AmE), / hæbəˈnɛːrɪə dɪˈleɪtə / (BrE)
A species of orchid native to wet or boggy areas in western and northern North America, known for its erect stems and spikes of fragrant white flowers.
a North American orchid species

Examples

The Habenaria dilatata blooms in midsummer, filling the damp meadows with its sweet scent.
Botanists often visit boggy areas to study the Habenaria dilatata in its natural habitat.
The delicate white flowers of Habenaria dilatata are a beautiful sight in northern forests.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Habenaria', from Latin 'habena' (rein, strap), and the species epithet 'dilatata', from Latin 'dilatare' (to widen).
