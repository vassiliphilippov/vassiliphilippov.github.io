Pompadour

1. PROPN

/ pɑːmpəˌdʊr / (AmE), / ˈpɒmpədɔː / (BrE)
Madame de Pompadour (1721–1764), a French noblewoman who was the official chief mistress and influential advisor to King Louis XV.
Mistress and advisor to Louis XV

Examples

The château was once visited by Pompadour, who left a mark on its style.
Historians often discuss the political influence wielded by Pompadour at the French court.
The letters written by Pompadour reveal her close relationship with Louis XV.

Etymology

From the title of Jeanne-Antoinette Poisson, Marquise de Pompadour.
