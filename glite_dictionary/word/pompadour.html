pompadour

1. VERB

/ ˈpɑːmpəˌdɔr / (AmE), / ˈpɒmpədɔː / (BrE)
to style hair in a pompadour, arranging it high over the forehead and often swept upward or back
to arrange hair in a pompadour style

Examples

She asked the stylist to pompadour her hair for the party.
In the 1950s, many women would pompadour their hair to emulate movie stars.
He carefully pompadoured her hair, creating a stylish and dramatic look.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bouffant - a similarly voluminous hairstyle

Etymology

From French 'pompadour', named after Madame de Pompadour (1721–1764), mistress of Louis XV, who made the hairstyle popular.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːmpəˌdɔr / (AmE), / ˈpɒmpədɔː / (BrE)
a hairstyle in which the front hair is brushed or swept upward from the forehead and worn high over the forehead, sometimes over a roll
hairstyle with hair brushed up from forehead

Examples

He styled his hair in a classic pompadour for the party.
The rockabilly singer was famous for his slick pompadour and flashy suits.
After her haircut, she tried a modern twist on the traditional pompadour.

Etymology

From Madame de Pompadour (Jeanne-Antoinette Poisson), mistress of Louis XV of France, who popularized the style.

Synset POS: n
