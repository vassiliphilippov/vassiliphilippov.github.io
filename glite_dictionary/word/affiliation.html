affiliation

1. NOUN

/ əˌfɪliˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌfɪliˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of becoming formally connected or associated with an organization or group
formal connection or association

Examples

His affiliation with the professional society boosted his career opportunities.
The company recently announced its affiliation with several nonprofit organizations.
She ended her political affiliation after becoming disillusioned with the party.

Synset Examples

welcomed the affiliation of the research center with the university

Related Terms

  • Synonym: association - a connection or cooperative link between people or organizations
  • Antonym: dissociation - the act of ending a connection or association

Etymology

From Medieval Latin affiliatio, from Latin affiliatus, past participle of affiliare (to adopt as a son).

2. NOUN

/ əˌfɪliˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌfɪlɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
a connection or association with a group, organization, or person, often for social or business purposes
connection or association with a group or organization

Examples

Her affiliation with the university provided many research opportunities.
The company gained credibility through its affiliation with an international network.
Membership requires an official affiliation with a recognized professional body.

Synset Examples

a valuable financial affiliation
he was sorry he had to sever his ties with other members of the team
many close associations with England

Related Terms

  • Synonym: association - a connection or relationship between people or organizations
  • Synonym: connection - a link or bond between people or things
  • Antonym: separation - the act of ending a relationship or connection

Etymology

From Medieval Latin affiliatio, from Latin affiliare meaning 'to adopt as a son'.
