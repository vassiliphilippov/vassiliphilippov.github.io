belch

1. NOUN

/ bɛltʃ / (AmE), / bɛltʃ / (BrE)
an act of noisily expelling air from the stomach through the mouth; a burp
noisy expulsion of air from stomach (burp)

Examples

A loud belch echoed through the room after he finished his soda.
She covered her mouth to stifle a tiny belch during dinner.
Excusing himself, he let out an accidental belch at the formal event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burp - a reflex that expels gas noisily from the stomach through the mouth

Etymology

From Middle English belchen, from Old English bealcian (to belch).

2. VERB

/ bɛltʃ / (AmE), / bɛltʃ / (BrE)
(of a volcano or chimney) to send out large amounts of something, especially smoke, fire, lava, or rocks, suddenly and forcefully.
send out lava or rocks forcefully (volcano)

Examples

The old volcano began to belch lava and smoke into the night sky.
During the eruption, the mountain belched rocks and ash for several hours.
Clouds of steam belched from the crater after the seismic activity increased.

Synset Examples

Vesuvius erupts once in a while

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erupt - to become active and eject lava, ash, or gas (volcano)

Etymology

From Middle English belchen, probably of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00308350-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ bɛltʃ / (AmE), / bɛltʃ / (BrE)
to expel gas noisily from the stomach through the mouth
expel gas noisily from the mouth

Examples

He couldn't help but belch loudly after drinking the soda.
It's considered rude to belch at the dinner table in many cultures.
She covered her mouth quickly when she felt the urge to belch.

Synset Examples

Please don't burp at the table

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burp - to expel gas from the stomach through the mouth, typically with a sound

Etymology

From Middle English belchen, from Old English bealcian (to belch, to belch forth).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
