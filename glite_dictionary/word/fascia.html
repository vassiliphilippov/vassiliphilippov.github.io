fascia

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæʃ.i.ə / (AmE), / ˈfæʃ.ɪ.ə / (BrE)
A sheet or band of fibrous connective tissue that separates or binds together muscles, organs, and other structures in the body.
sheet of fibrous tissue in the body
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully separated the muscle from the fascia during the operation.
Stretching can help maintain the flexibility of your fascia and reduce stiffness.
Damage to the plantar fascia can cause pain in the heel and sole of the foot.

Etymology

From Latin 'fascia' meaning 'band' or 'bandage'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfæʃiə / (AmE), / ˈfæsiə / (BrE)
the dashboard or instrument panel of a car or airplane, containing dials and controls
dashboard or instrument panel in vehicles
british

Examples

The new model features a digital display in the center of the fascia.
He reached across the fascia to adjust the air conditioning controls.
Aircraft designers paid special attention to the layout of the fascia to improve usability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dashboard - the panel facing the driver of a vehicle, containing controls and instruments

Etymology

From Italian *fascia* (band, strip), ultimately from Latin *fascia* (band, bandage).
