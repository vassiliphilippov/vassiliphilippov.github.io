caregiver

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛrˌɡɪvər / (AmE), / ˈkeəˌɡɪvə / (BrE)
a person who provides help or support to someone who is ill, disabled, or unable to care for themselves
person who supports the ill or disabled

Examples

After her surgery, Maria hired a caregiver to assist with daily tasks at home.
Many elderly people rely on a caregiver for companionship and medical support.
Being a caregiver for a family member requires a lot of patience and dedication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caretaker - a person employed to look after people or animals

Etymology

Formed from 'care' + 'giver', meaning one who gives care.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɛrˌɡɪvər / (AmE), / ˈkeəˌɡɪvə / (BrE)
a person who looks after a child, elderly person, or someone who is ill or disabled and needs help with daily activities
person caring for a dependent

Examples

After her father's illness, she became his primary caregiver.
Many caregivers experience emotional stress from their responsibilities.
The hospital provides support for family caregivers of patients with dementia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caretaker - person employed to look after people or property

Etymology

Formed from 'care' + 'giver', referring to someone who gives care.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09915013-n
Synset POS: n
