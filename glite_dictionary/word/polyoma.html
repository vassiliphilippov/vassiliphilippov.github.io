polyoma

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑliˈoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈəʊmə / (BrE)
a virus that can initiate various tumors, especially in mice, and is studied for its role in cancer research
tumor-causing virus in mice
medical

Examples

Scientists studied the polyoma virus to understand its effects on tumor development in mice.
In the laboratory, infection with polyoma led to rapid formation of abnormal cell growth.
The discovery of polyoma has advanced cancer research significantly over the past decades.

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'oma' (tumor), reflecting the virus's ability to cause multiple tumors.
