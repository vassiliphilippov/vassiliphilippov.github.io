Lobipes

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ.bɪˌpiːz / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.bɪ.piːz / (BrE)
A genus of small wading birds in the family Phalaropidae, comprising the red-necked phalarope and related species.
genus of wading birds (Phalaropidae)

Examples

Scientists observed a flock of Lobipes feeding in the shallow marsh waters.
The Lobipes genus is known for its members' unusual swimming behaviors.
Red-necked phalaropes, belonging to the genus Lobipes, can often be seen in Arctic breeding grounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Phalaropus - another genus of phalaropes in the family Phalaropidae

Etymology

From Greek lobos (lobe) + pous (foot), referring to the lobed toes of these birds.

2. NOUN

/ ˈloʊbɪˌpiːz / (AmE), / ˈlɒbɪˌpiːz / (BrE)
a genus of birds in the family Phalaropidae, consisting of the single species the Red-necked Phalarope.
genus of birds (Phalaropidae)

Examples

The Lobipes genus is best known for the Red-necked Phalarope, a migratory shorebird.
Ornithologists have studied Lobipes populations to understand their unique feeding behaviors.
Lobipes is distinguished by its members' specialized lobed toes, adapted for swimming.

Etymology

From Greek 'lobos' (lobe) + '-pous' (foot), referring to the lobed toes of the birds.
