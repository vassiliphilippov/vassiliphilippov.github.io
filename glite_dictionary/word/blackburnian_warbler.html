Blackburnian warbler

1. NOUN

/ ˌblækˈbɝːniən ˈwɔːrblər / (AmE), / ˌblækˈbɜːniən ˈwɔːblə / (BrE)
A North American wood warbler with striking black-and-white plumage and an orange-and-black head and throat.
a North American warbler with orange-and-black head

Examples

Birdwatchers often travel just to catch a glimpse of the Blackburnian warbler during migration season.
The Blackburnian warbler's vivid coloring makes it easy to spot among the treetops in spring.
I photographed a male Blackburnian warbler feeding on insects high in the forest canopy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warbler - a small songbird known for its singing ability

Etymology

Named after Anna Blackburne, English naturalist.
