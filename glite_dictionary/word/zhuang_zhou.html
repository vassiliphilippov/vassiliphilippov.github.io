Zhuang Zhou

1. PROPN

/ dʒwɑŋ dʒoʊ / (AmE), / dʒwɑːŋ dzəʊ / (BrE)
(c. 4th century BCE) A Chinese philosopher whose works and ideas form a major foundation of Taoist philosophy.
4th-century Chinese philosopher, founder of Taoist ideas

Examples

The writings of Zhuang Zhou have influenced generations of philosophers and poets.
Many stories attributed to Zhuang Zhou explore the nature of reality and illusion.
Zhuang Zhou is often regarded as one of the most important figures in classical Chinese philosophy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Laozi - ancient Chinese philosopher, traditionally said to be the author of the Tao Te Ching

Etymology

From Mandarin Chinese 庄周 (Zhuāng Zhōu), his given name.
