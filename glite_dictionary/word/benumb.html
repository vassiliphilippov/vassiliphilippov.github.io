to benumb

1. VERB

/ bɪˈnʌm / (AmE), / bɪˈnʌm / (BrE)
to make numb or deprive of feeling or sensitivity; to render insensitive physically or emotionally
make numb or insensitive

Examples

The icy wind began to benumb his fingers as he waited for the bus.
Grief seemed to benumb her mind, leaving her unable to respond.
The anesthetic will benumb the area before the minor surgery.

Synset Examples

The shock numbed her senses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: numb - deprived of feeling or responsiveness

Etymology

From Middle English 'benumen' (to deprive), from Old English 'beniman' (to take away).
