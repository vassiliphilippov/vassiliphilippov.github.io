manfully

1. ADV

/ ˈmænfəli / (AmE), / ˈmanfəli / (BrE)
In a brave, determined, or resolute manner, showing qualities traditionally associated with men.
in a brave or resolute manner

Examples

Despite his fear, he faced the challenge manfully and did not back down.
She manfully accepted her responsibilities, even though the task was daunting.
He manfully held back his emotions during the difficult farewell.

Synset Examples

having said her say, Peggy manfully shouldered her burden and prepared to break up yet another home

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bravely - in a way that shows courage
  • Antonym: cowardly - in a way that lacks courage

Etymology

From 'manful' + '-ly', with 'manful' (having qualities traditionally attributed to men) deriving from Old English 'mann'.
