Dermacentor

1. NOUN

/ dɜːrˈmæs.ənˌtɔːr / (AmE), / dɜːˈmæs.əntɔː / (BrE)
A genus of ticks that are vectors of diseases affecting humans and animals.
genus of disease-carrying ticks
medical

Examples

Dermacentor ticks are responsible for transmitting Rocky Mountain spotted fever.
Veterinarians warn pet owners about the dangers of Dermacentor infestations.
Researchers study Dermacentor species to better understand tick-borne diseases.

Etymology

From Greek 'derma' (skin) and 'kentron' (pricker, spur).

2. NOUN

/ dɜːrˈmæsɛntɔr / (AmE), / dɜːˈmæsəntɔː / (BrE)
A genus of hard ticks that are vectors of significant diseases in humans and animals.
genus of disease-carrying hard ticks
medical

Examples

Several species in the genus Dermacentor are known to transmit Rocky Mountain spotted fever.
The Dermacentor ticks are commonly found on livestock and wild animals.
Veterinarians often monitor Dermacentor populations to prevent outbreaks of tick-borne diseases.

Etymology

From Greek derma (skin) + kentor (pricker, stinger).
