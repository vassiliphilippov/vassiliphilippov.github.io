Citroncirus

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪtrɒnˈsaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˌsɪtrɒnˈsɪərəs / (BrE)
A hybrid genus of plants resulting from a cross between the sweet orange (Citrus sinensis) and the trifoliate orange (Poncirus trifoliata).
hybrid plant genus (Citrus × Poncirus)

Examples

Researchers are developing new fruit varieties by hybridizing members of the Citroncirus genus.
The cold resistance of Citroncirus makes it valuable for rootstock breeding programs.
Botanists classified the plant as Citroncirus due to its mixed parental heritage.

Etymology

Formed from the genus names Citrus and Poncirus.
