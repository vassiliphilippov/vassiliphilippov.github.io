Certhia

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜrθiə / (AmE), / ˈsɜːθɪə / (BrE)
A genus of small passerine birds known as treecreepers, comprising the typical treecreepers of the family Certhiidae.
genus of treecreeper birds

Examples

The Eurasian treecreeper belongs to the genus Certhia and can be found across Europe and Asia.
Ornithologists have observed several new behaviors in Certhia species during their winter migration.
The defining features of Certhia include their slender, curved bills and stiff tail feathers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Certhiidae - family of birds including Certhia and related genera

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the Ancient Greek *kerthios*, a small tree-dwelling bird mentioned by Aristotle.
