ampicillin

1. NOUN

/ ˌæmpɪˈsɪlɪn / (AmE), / ˌæmpɪˈsɪlɪn / (BrE)
a semisynthetic broad-spectrum penicillin antibiotic, used to treat a variety of bacterial infections
a penicillin-type antibiotic
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed ampicillin to treat the bacterial infection.
Many strains of bacteria have developed resistance to ampicillin over time.
Ampicillin is often administered orally, but it can also be given by injection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penicillin - a group of antibiotics produced naturally by certain blue molds

Etymology

Formed from 'amino' + 'penicillin', due to its chemical structure.
