urate

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʊr.eɪt / (AmE), / ˈjʊə.reɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester of uric acid, often found in the body as a component of urine or in medical conditions such as gout
a salt or ester of uric acid
medical

Examples

Elevated urate levels in the blood can lead to gout.
The doctor measured the patient's urate concentration in the urine sample.
Sodium urate crystals may accumulate in the joints, causing inflammation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uric acid - a compound produced by the breakdown of purines in the body

Etymology

From French 'urate', from New Latin 'uratum', from 'uricus' (uric) + '-ate'.
