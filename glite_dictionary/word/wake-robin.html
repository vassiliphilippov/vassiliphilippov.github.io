wake-robin

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪk ˌrɑːbɪn / (AmE), / ˈweɪk ˌrɒbɪn / (BrE)
A woodland plant of the genus Trillium, characterized by a whorl of three leaves at the top of the stem and a single three-petaled flower.
woodland plant of the genus Trillium

Examples

We spotted a cluster of wake-robins blooming beside the forest trail.
The wake-robin is often one of the first flowers to appear in spring.
Gardeners admire the striking three-petaled flower of the wake-robin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trillium - a woodland plant of the genus Trillium, with three-petaled flowers

Etymology

From 'wake' (to awaken) and 'robin', referring to the plant's early spring flowering, coinciding with the return of robins.

2. NOUN

/ ˈweɪk ˌrɑːbɪn / (AmE), / ˈweɪk ˌrɒbɪn / (BrE)
A common North American woodland plant, especially Trillium species, with sheathing leaves, a club-shaped spadix, and a spathe that produces scarlet berries.
North American woodland plant, especially Trillium

Examples

The forest floor was sprinkled with blooming wake-robin in early April.
She gently stepped around a cluster of wake-robins to avoid damaging the wildflowers.
Botanists studied the habitat requirements of the wake-robin to inform conservation efforts.

Etymology

Alludes to the plant's early spring blooming, often coinciding with the return of robins.
