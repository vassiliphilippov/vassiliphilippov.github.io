Bairdiella chrysoura

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɛərdiˈɛlə kraɪˈsɔrə / (AmE), / ˌbɛːdɪˈɛlə kraɪˈsɔːrə / (BrE)
A small silvery drumfish often mistaken for white perch, found along the coasts of the United States from New York to Mexico.
species of small silvery drumfish

Examples

The Bairdiella chrysoura is commonly found in estuarine waters along the southeastern United States coastline.
Fishermen sometimes confuse Bairdiella chrysoura with the much larger white perch because of its appearance.
Environmental studies have shown that Bairdiella chrysoura populations are indicators of estuary health.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus 'Bairdiella' and the species name 'chrysoura' (from Greek 'chrysos', gold, and 'oura', tail).
