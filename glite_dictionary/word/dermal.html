dermal

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɜrməl / (AmE), / ˈdɜːməl / (BrE)
relating to or affecting the skin; of or pertaining to the skin.
relating to the skin
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the dermal layers for signs of infection.
Sunscreen is important for protecting dermal tissue from UV damage.
A dermal reaction can occur when the skin comes into contact with an allergen.

Synset Examples

cutaneous nerves
a cutaneous infection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutaneous - relating to or affecting the skin

Etymology

From Greek derma (skin) via Latin dermalis.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɜrməl / (AmE), / ˈdɜːməl / (BrE)
relating to or located in the dermis, the thick layer of living tissue below the epidermis of the skin
relating to the dermis (skin)
medical

Examples

The patient developed a dermal rash after using the new medication.
Researchers are studying dermal absorption of chemicals through the skin.
A dermal filler was injected to smooth out the wrinkles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutaneous - relating to or affecting the skin

Etymology

From Greek derma (skin), through New Latin dermalis.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02887234-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈdɝːməl / (AmE), / ˈdɜːməl / (BrE)
relating to the skin or a cuticle; pertaining to the outer or protective layers of an organism
relating to skin or a cuticle
medical

Examples

Many protective creams are designed to improve dermal health.
The snake’s scales serve as a dermal barrier against dehydration.
He specializes in treating dermal and epidermal conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epidermal - relating to the outermost layer of skin (epidermis)

Etymology

From Greek derma (skin) via Latin dermalis.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02887392-a
Synset POS: a
