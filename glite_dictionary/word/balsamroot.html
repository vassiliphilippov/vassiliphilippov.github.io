balsamroot

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑlsəmˌruːt / (AmE), / ˈbɔːlsəmˌruːt / (BrE)
a perennial plant of the genus Balsamorhiza, native to western North America, with downy leaves in a rosette and yellow daisy-like flowers
a western North American plant with yellow flowers

Examples

The balsamroot bloomed across the hillside, displaying its bright yellow flowers in the spring sunlight.
Native Americans traditionally used the balsamroot for food and medicinal purposes.
Hikers admired the clusters of balsamroot mixed with wild lupines along the trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrowleaf balsamroot - a species of balsamroot (Balsamorhiza sagittata) with arrow-shaped leaves

Etymology

From balsam + root, referring to the plant's balsam-scented roots.
