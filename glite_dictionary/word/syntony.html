syntony

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪn.tə.ni / (AmE), / ˈsɪn.təni / (BrE)
a state of being in harmony or responsive to one's environment, especially in psychology or physiology
state of harmony with environment

Examples

Her emotional syntony with nature helped her recover from stress more quickly.
The therapist commented on his apparent syntony with different social settings.
Achieving syntony between body and surroundings is essential in certain forms of meditation.

Synset Examples

Syntony means resonance, to tune in or harmonize with each other, our surroundings, and the way in which we flow with them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resonance - the quality of being resonant or in harmony
  • Antonym: dissonance - a lack of harmony between elements

Etymology

From Greek suntonia (concord, harmony), from sun- (together) + tonos (stretch, tone).
