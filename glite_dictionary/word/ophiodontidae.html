Ophiodontidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊfi.oʊˈdɒntɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌəʊfi.əʊˈdɒntɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of fishes closely related to greenlings, commonly found in the North Pacific Ocean.
fish family related to greenlings

Examples

The Ophiodontidae family includes several species that inhabit rocky coastal areas.
Researchers studied the evolutionary history of Ophiodontidae to understand their relation to greenlings.
Several members of Ophiodontidae are valued in commercial and recreational fisheries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greenling - a fish of the family Hexagrammidae, related to Ophiodontidae

Etymology

From Greek 'ophis' (snake) + 'odous, odont-' (tooth) + scientific family suffix '-idae'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌoʊfi.oʊˌdɒnˈtɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌɒfi.əʊˌdɒnˈtɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of marine fishes closely related to greenlings, found in the North Pacific Ocean
a family of fishes related to greenlings

Examples

The fish in the Ophiodontidae family are primarily found along the Pacific coasts of North America and Asia.
Members of the Ophiodontidae are characterized by elongated bodies and strong, sharp teeth.
Marine biologists study Ophiodontidae to better understand their ecological relationship with greenlings.

Etymology

From Greek 'ophis' (snake) and 'odous' (tooth), referring to tooth structure in the type genus.
