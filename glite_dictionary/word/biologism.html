biologism

1. NOUN

/ baɪˈɑlədʒɪzəm / (AmE), / bʌɪˈɒlədʒɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the interpretation or explanation of human, especially social, behavior primarily in terms of biological factors or principles
explanation of behavior using biological principles

Examples

Some critics argue that biologism overlooks the influence of culture on human behavior.
The debate between sociologists often centers around the merits of biologism versus cultural explanations.
She rejected biologism in favor of theories that emphasize social and environmental factors.

Etymology

Formed from 'biology' and the suffix '-ism', indicating a doctrine or system.
