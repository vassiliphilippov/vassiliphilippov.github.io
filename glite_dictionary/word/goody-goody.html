goody-goody

1. ADJ

/ ˌɡʊdiˈɡʊdi / (AmE), / ˌɡʊdiˈɡʊdi / (BrE)
(of a person) affectedly or smugly virtuous or self-righteous.
self-righteously virtuous person
informal

Examples

She always acts like a goody-goody in front of the teachers, but she's not so perfect at home.
He got tired of being called a goody-goody just because he followed the rules.
Nobody likes a goody-goody who tattles on everyone else for the slightest mistake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prig - a self-righteously moralistic person
  • Synonym: self-righteous - having or showing a strong belief that one's own actions or opinions are right and others are wrong

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡʊdi ˌɡʊdi / (AmE), / ˈɡʊdi ˌɡʊdi / (BrE)
a person who behaves in an overly virtuous or well-behaved manner, especially to gain favor with authority figures
overly virtuous or well-behaved person

Examples

Nobody liked the new student because she acted like a goody-goody in every class.
He was teased for being a goody-goody who never broke the rules.
Don't be such a goody-goody; it's okay to have a little fun sometimes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teacher's pet - a student who seeks favor by being obedient or helpful to the teacher
  • Synonym: brown-noser - someone who flatters authority to gain favor

Etymology

Reduplication of 'goody', a term for a virtuous person.
