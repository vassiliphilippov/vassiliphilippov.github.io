puccoon

1. NOUN

/ pəˈkun / (AmE), / pəˈkuːn / (BrE)
A North American perennial plant with a red root, often used as a source of dye and sometimes in traditional medicine.
North American plant used for red dye

Examples

Early settlers learned how to use puccoon roots to produce a vibrant red dye.
The puccoon blooms briefly each spring, displaying its solitary white flower in woodland areas.
Traditional herbalists valued puccoon for its supposed medicinal properties, especially for respiratory complaints.

Etymology

From Virginia Algonquian *pakán*, referring to dye plants.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈkun / (AmE), / pəˈkuːn / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to eastern North America, known for its hairy foliage and roots that yield a red or yellow dye.
plant yielding red/yellow dye

Examples

Early settlers used the roots of puccoon to make natural dyes for fabrics.
The puccoon blooms in spring with bright yellow flowers along woodland edges.
Artists valued puccoon for the vibrant pigment it provided for their paints.

Etymology

From Powhatan (an Algonquian language) 'poughkone', meaning 'red dye'.
