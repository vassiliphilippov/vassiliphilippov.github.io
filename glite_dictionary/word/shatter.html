to shatter

1. VERB

/ ˈʃæt̬ər / (AmE), / ˈʃætə / (BrE)
to completely destroy or severely damage something, especially someone's feelings, hopes, or life; to cause to break apart emotionally or mentally
completely destroy or severely damage

Examples

The sudden loss of her job completely shattered her confidence.
News of the accident shattered the whole community.
His dreams of becoming an artist were shattered when he lost his sight.

Synset Examples

The news of her husband's death shattered her life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to devastate - to cause great damage or suffering
  • Synonym: ruin - to destroy or severely damage
  • Antonym: to heal - to make well again after damage or suffering
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00334860-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈʃæt̬.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈʃæt.ə / (BrE)
to break suddenly into many small pieces; to cause something to break in this way
break into many pieces

Examples

The children accidentally shattered the vase while playing in the living room.
A sudden explosion shattered all the windows in the building.
Her dreams were shattered when she heard the disappointing news.

Synset Examples

The wine glass shattered

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break - to separate into pieces as a result of a blow, shock, or strain
  • Synonym: smash - to break into pieces violently or noisily

Etymology

From Middle English 'schateren', probably of Low German or Dutch origin (compare Middle Dutch 'schateren')

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00334568-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈʃæt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈʃæt.ə / (BrE)
to break or make something break suddenly into many small pieces
break into many small pieces

Examples

Careless handling can easily shatter a fragile glass vase.
The explosion caused the windows to shatter instantly.
If you drop that plate, it will shatter on the floor.

Synset Examples

shatter the plate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break - separate into pieces as a result of a blow or strain

Etymology

Probably from Middle English shateren, imitation of sound or from Middle Dutch schateren.
