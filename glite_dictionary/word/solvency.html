solvency

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːlvənsi / (AmE), / ˈsɒlvənsi / (BrE)
The ability of a person or organization to pay all debts as they become due; the state of being financially sound.
financial ability to pay debts

Examples

The company was concerned about its solvency after suffering several quarters of losses.
Maintaining solvency is crucial for banks to inspire trust in their clients.
Her careful financial planning ensured the long-term solvency of her business.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: insolvency - the inability to pay debts as they become due

Etymology

From Latin solvere (to loosen, pay).
