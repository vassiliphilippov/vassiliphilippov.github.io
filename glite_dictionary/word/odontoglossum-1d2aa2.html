odontoglossum

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊdəntəˈɡlɑsəm / (AmE), / ˌəʊdəntəˈɡlɒsəm / (BrE)
A plant of a genus of orchids native to tropical America, known for bearing showy and often large flowers in various colors.
a genus of showy orchids
medical

Examples

The odontoglossum is prized among collectors for its striking and vibrant blossoms.
She carefully watered her odontoglossum orchids to encourage a spectacular blooming season.
Botanists have discovered a new species within the odontoglossum genus in the Andes.

Etymology

From Greek 'odous' (tooth) and 'glōssa' (tongue), referring to shapes in the flower's structure.
