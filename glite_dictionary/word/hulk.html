hulk

1. NOUN

/ hʌlk / (AmE), / hʌlk / (BrE)
a ship that has been wrecked and abandoned, often left to decay or serve another use
wrecked and abandoned ship

Examples

The old hulk had been rusting in the harbor for decades.
Divers explored the hulk of the shipwreck lying on the seabed.
Salvagers stripped valuable metal from the hulk abandoned after the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wreck - a ship that has been destroyed, especially by sinking or running aground

Etymology

From Middle English 'hulke', from Old French 'holque', probably of Low German origin.

2. NOUN

/ hʌlk / (AmE), / hʌlk / (BrE)
a very large or heavy person, animal, or thing; someone or something impressive in size or strength
very large or heavy person or thing

Examples

The football player was a real hulk compared to his teammates.
Moving that old piano required a hulk of a man to lift it.
Despite his size, the gentle hulk had a soft heart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant - a person or thing of great size
  • Antonym: dwarf - a person or thing much smaller than usual

Etymology

From Middle English *hulke* (large ship), of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10148670-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ hʌlk / (AmE), / hʌlk / (BrE)
to appear large, heavy, and imposing, often looming over or dominating the surroundings
to loom large or dominate in appearance

Examples

The old factory hulked over the deserted streets, casting a long shadow.
A mountain hulks above the valley, dominating the entire landscape.
Dark clouds hulked on the horizon as the storm approached.

Synset Examples

The huge sculpture predominates over the fountain
Large shadows loomed on the canyon wall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loom - to appear as a large, indistinct, and often threatening shape

Etymology

From the noun 'hulk,' meaning a large, heavy, or unwieldy object.
