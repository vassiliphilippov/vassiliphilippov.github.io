Masdevallia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæs.dəˈvæl.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌmaz.dɪˈval.ɪ.ə / (BrE)
a large genus of tropical American orchids, mostly epiphytic, whose flowers have sepals fused at the base to form a tube; includes species sometimes classified in related genera such as Dracula and Dryadella
genus of tropical American epiphytic orchids

Examples

The Masdevallia orchids are prized for their unique and vibrant flowers among collectors.
Many Masdevallia species thrive in the cool, misty climates of the Andes mountains.
Botanists recently discovered a new Masdevallia variety in Ecuador's cloud forests.

Etymology

Named after José Masdevall (1740–1801), a Spanish physician and naturalist.
