Mugil curema

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjuːdʒɪl kjuːˈriːmə / (AmE), / ˈmjuːdʒɪl kjʊˈriːmə / (BrE)
A species of silvery mullet found along the Atlantic and Pacific coasts of the Americas, commonly called the white mullet.
a species of silvery mullet fish

Examples

Local fishermen often catch Mugil curema in the shallow coastal waters during the summer.
The diet of Mugil curema mainly consists of detritus and small invertebrates.
Biologists recorded a large population of Mugil curema along the Caribbean coastline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white mullet - common name for Mugil curema, a silvery mullet of American coasts

Etymology

From Latin 'mugil' (mullet) and specific epithet 'curema', used in taxonomy.
