Shaktism

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɑk.tɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈʃæk.tɪz.əm / (BrE)
a major tradition within Hinduism in which the goddess Shakti is worshipped as the supreme deity.
Hindu tradition worshipping the goddess Shakti

Examples

Shaktism emphasizes the worship of the Divine Mother as the source of all power and creation.
The main temples of Shaktism are dedicated to various forms of the goddess Shakti.
Her family follows Shaktism and celebrates traditional festivals honoring the goddess.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vaishnavism - Hindu tradition worshipping Vishnu as the supreme deity
  • Synonym: Shaivism - Hindu tradition worshipping Shiva as the supreme deity

Etymology

From Sanskrit śākta, relating to Shakti (the divine feminine power) + -ism.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɑːktɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈʃʌktɪzəm / (BrE)
A major Hindu tradition focusing on the worship of Shakti, the divine feminine energy, especially as the consort of Shiva.
Hindu tradition worshipping Shakti

Examples

Shaktism emphasizes the reverence of the goddess as the supreme being.
Many temples in India are dedicated to the worship of the divine feminine in Shaktism.
Scholars often compare Shaktism with other major Hindu denominations such as Shaivism and Vaishnavism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shaivism - Hindu tradition primarily worshipping Shiva
  • Antonym: Vaishnavism - Hindu tradition primarily worshipping Vishnu

Etymology

From Sanskrit śakti (power, energy, goddess) + English '-ism'.
