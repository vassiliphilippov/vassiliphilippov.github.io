adulator

1. NOUN

/ ˈædʒəˌleɪtər / (AmE), / ˈæd.jʊ.leɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person who praises someone excessively or insincerely, often for personal gain
someone who flatters excessively

Examples

The king surrounded himself with adulators who told him only what he wanted to hear.
As an adulator, he often exaggerated his praise to gain favor.
She could easily spot a genuine friend from an adulator.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flatterer - a person who flatters others
  • Antonym: critic - a person who expresses disapproval or finds faults

Etymology

From Latin adulātor, meaning 'flatterer'.
