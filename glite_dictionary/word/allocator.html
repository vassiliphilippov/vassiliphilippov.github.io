allocator

1. NOUN

/ ˈæləˌkeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈaləˌkeɪtə / (BrE)
a person or thing that allocates or distributes resources or tasks
one who allocates resources or tasks

Examples

The project manager acted as the allocator of all available resources within the department.
An independent allocator was appointed to distribute relief funds fairly among the applicants.
In many computer programs, an allocator assigns memory to different processes as needed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distributor - a person or thing that distributes something

Etymology

From 'allocate' (from Latin 'allocatus', past participle of 'allocare', meaning to assign) + '-or', agent noun suffix.
