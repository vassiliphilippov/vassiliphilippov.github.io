ipsilateral

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪpsəˈlæt̬ɚl / (AmE), / ˌɪpsɪˈlatərəl / (BrE)
on or relating to the same side of the body or of an organ
on or relating to the same side (of the body)
medical

Examples

The ipsilateral limb showed signs of swelling after the injury.
Damage to the ipsilateral nerves can result in weakness on the same side of the body.
Doctors noted an ipsilateral response to the stimulus in the patient's left leg.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: contralateral - on or relating to the opposite side (of the body)

Etymology

From Latin 'ipse' (self, same) + 'lateralis' (side).
