derogative

1. ADJ

/ dəˈrɑɡ.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈrɒɡ.ətɪv / (BrE)
expressing a low opinion or intended to belittle; disparaging
expressing a low opinion

Examples

The article contained several derogative statements about the politician's past.
Her derogative tone made everyone in the meeting uncomfortable.
He made a derogative remark that upset his colleague.

Synset Examples

derogatory comments
disparaging remarks about the new house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: derogatory - expressing criticism or a low opinion; disparaging
  • Antonym: laudatory - expressing praise or commendation

Etymology

From Late Latin derogativus, from Latin derogare (to take away, detract).
