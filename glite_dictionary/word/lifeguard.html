lifeguard

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪfˌɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˈlaɪfˌɡɑːd / (BrE)
a person employed to supervise swimmers at a beach or pool and to rescue them if they are in danger
person who rescues swimmers

Examples

The lifeguard quickly noticed the child struggling in the water and jumped in to help.
During the summer, there is always a lifeguard on duty at the public swimming pool.
She trained for months to qualify as a certified lifeguard at the seaside resort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swimming instructor - person who teaches swimming
  • Antonym: victim - person being rescued (contrasts with lifeguard)

Etymology

From 'life' + 'guard'—refers to one who guards lives, especially in aquatic settings.
