hysterocatalepsy

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪstɚoʊˌkætəˈlɛpsi / (AmE), / ˌhɪstərəʊˌkætəˈlɛpsi / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by cataleptic symptoms occurring in the context of hysteria.
hysteria with cataleptic symptoms
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with hysterocatalepsy after displaying both emotional disturbances and periods of muscular rigidity.
A rare case of hysterocatalepsy was discussed at the psychiatric conference last week.
Treatment for hysterocatalepsy often requires a combination of psychotherapy and medication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catalepsy - a medical condition characterized by muscular rigidity and fixity of posture

Etymology

From Greek *hystera* (womb, later: hysteria) + *katalepsis* (seizure, catalepsy).
