cenogenetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsiːnəˌdʒəˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌsiːnəʊdʒəˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or resulting from cenogenesis, especially the appearance of new or modified features in embryo development that do not occur in the ancestral form.
relating to cenogenesis in biology

Examples

The cenogenetic features in the embryo are adaptations not seen in ancestral species.
Many fish species develop cenogenetic structures during their larval stage.
Some evolutionary changes are best understood by studying the cenogenetic aspects of embryology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cenogenetic - relating to cenogenesis; alternative spelling 'caenogenetic'

Etymology

From German 'zänogenetisch', from Greek 'kainos' (new) + 'genesis' (origin, creation).
