to channel-surf

1. VERB

/ ˈtʃæn.əl ˌsɝf / (AmE), / ˈtʃæn.əl ˌsɜːf / (BrE)
To rapidly switch from one television channel to another, often without watching any program for long.
to quickly switch TV channels
informal

Examples

He likes to channel-surf during commercials to see what else is on.
I often channel-surf late at night when I can't sleep.
She spent an hour channel-surfing before she settled on a movie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zapping - quickly switching TV channels

Etymology

Formed from 'channel' (television station) and 'surf' (move quickly from one site to another, as in 'surf the web').
