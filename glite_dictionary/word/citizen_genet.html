Citizen Genet

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɪtəzən ʒəˈneɪ / (AmE), / ˈsɪtɪzən ʒəˈneɪ / (BrE)
(1763–1834) French diplomat who attempted to involve the United States in the war between France and Great Britain during the 1790s.
French diplomat in early US history

Examples

Citizen Genet arrived in the United States in 1793, hoping to rally American support for France.
The actions of Citizen Genet tested the neutrality of President Washington’s administration.
Historians often cite Citizen Genet as a pivotal figure in early American foreign policy.

Etymology

From French "citoyen" (citizen) and Genêt, his surname; "Citizen" was adopted as a revolutionary title.
