sanguine

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæŋɡwɪn / (AmE), / ˈsaŋɡwɪn / (BrE)
A blood-red color, often used in the context of art, textiles, or heraldry.
a blood-red color

Examples

The artist preferred to sketch with sanguine for its warm, rich tones.
The tapestry was woven with deep sanguine threads that stood out against the gold.
The coat of arms featured a band of sanguine as a symbol of courage.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: red - a primary color at the end of the visible spectrum

Etymology

From Latin *sanguineus* (of blood), from *sanguis* (blood).
