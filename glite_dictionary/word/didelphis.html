Didelphis

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈdɛlfɪs / (AmE), / daɪˈdɛlfɪs / (BrE)
a genus of marsupials in the family Didelphidae, commonly known as large American opossums
genus of large American opossums

Examples

The best-known species in the genus Didelphis is the Virginia opossum.
Members of Didelphis are notable for their ability to play dead as a defense mechanism.
Researchers studied several Didelphis specimens collected from the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opossum - a marsupial mammal native to the Americas

Etymology

From Greek didelphys, meaning 'double womb'.

2. NOUN

/ daɪˈdɛlfɪs / (AmE), / daɪˈdɛlfɪs / (BrE)
A genus of marsupials in the family Didelphidae, which includes several species of opossums found in the Americas.
genus of opossums (marsupials)

Examples

The Virginia opossum belongs to the genus Didelphis.
Several species within Didelphis are widely distributed throughout Central and South America.
Researchers observed distinct behavioral patterns among different Didelphis species in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opossum - common name for animals of the Didelphis genus

Etymology

From Greek didelphus, meaning 'double womb', referring to marsupial reproductive anatomy.
