Singapore

1. PROPN

/ ˌsɪŋəˈpɔr / (AmE), / ˌsɪŋəˈpɔː / (BrE)
the capital city of Singapore, known as a major international port and financial center in Southeast Asia
capital city and major port of Singapore

Examples

The port of Singapore is one of the busiest in the world.
Many multinational companies have their Asian headquarters in Singapore.
Tourists flock to Singapore for its blend of modern architecture and diverse culture.

Etymology

From Malay 'Singapura', meaning 'Lion City', from Sanskrit 'siṃha' (lion) + 'pura' (city).

2. PROPN

/ ˈsɪŋəˌpɔr / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋəpɔː / (BrE)
A country in Southeast Asia consisting of the island of Singapore and some smaller islands, known for being a global financial center and city-state; it became an independent republic in 1965.
country and city-state in Southeast Asia

Examples

After graduating from university, she moved to Singapore to work in the banking sector.
Singapore is famous for its cleanliness, efficiency, and multicultural society.
The annual Grand Prix in Singapore attracts thousands of tourists from around the world.

Etymology

From Malay 'Singapura', from Sanskrit 'siṃha' (lion) and 'pura' (city), meaning 'Lion City'.
