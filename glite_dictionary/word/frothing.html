frothing

1. ADJ

/ ˈfrɔðɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfrɒθɪŋ / (BrE)
producing or covered with foam or lathery sweat or saliva, especially from exhaustion, disease, or agitation
covered with or producing foam or froth

Examples

The vet approached the frothing dog with caution, suspecting it might be rabid.
After hours of running, the horse was frothing at the mouth from exhaustion.
The patient's frothing lips were a disturbing sign for the medical staff.

Synset Examples

the rabid animal's frothing mouth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foaming - producing or covered with a mass of small bubbles

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'froth', from Middle English 'frothe', from Old Norse 'frotha'.
