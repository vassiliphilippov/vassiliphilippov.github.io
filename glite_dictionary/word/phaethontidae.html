Phaethontidae

1. NOUN

/ feɪˌɪθɒnˈtɪdiː / (AmE), / feɪˌɪθɒnˈtɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, tropical seabirds known as tropicbirds, characterized by long tail feathers and mostly white plumage.
family of seabirds known as tropicbirds

Examples

The Phaethontidae are commonly seen flying gracefully over tropical oceans.
Members of the Phaethontidae family are noted for their long, streamer-like tail feathers.
Biologists study the migration patterns of Phaethontidae to better understand seabird ecology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tropicbird - a large, mostly white seabird with long tail feathers

Etymology

From Modern Latin Phaethontidae, based on the type genus Phaethon.
