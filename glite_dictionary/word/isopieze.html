isopieze

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsəˈpiːz / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈpiːz / (BrE)
A line on a map or diagram that connects points of equal groundwater pressure.
line connecting equal groundwater pressure points
medical

Examples

The hydrogeologist drew an isopieze to illustrate areas of equal water pressure in the aquifer.
After heavy rain, the pattern of isopieze lines on the map changed noticeably.
Engineers used several isopiezes to evaluate potential well sites.

Etymology

From Greek 'iso-' (equal) and 'piezō' (to press).
