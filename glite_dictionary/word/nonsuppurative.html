nonsuppurative

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑn səˈpjʊrətɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒn səˈpjʊərətɪv / (BrE)
(of a disease or condition) not producing or associated with the formation of pus.
not producing pus
medical

Examples

Rheumatic fever is often classified as a nonsuppurative complication of streptococcal infection.
The doctor noted that the patient had a nonsuppurative form of arthritis, with no sign of pus present.
Unlike abscesses, nonsuppurative infections do not result in the accumulation of pus.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: suppurative - producing or associated with the formation of pus

Etymology

From Latin prefix non- (not) + suppurative (from Latin suppurare, to form pus).
