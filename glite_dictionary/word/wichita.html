Wichita

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪtʃɪtɔ / (AmE), / ˈwɪtʃɪtɑː / (BrE)
a city in southern Kansas, United States, located on the Arkansas River; the largest city in the state.
largest city in Kansas, US

Examples

I grew up in Wichita before moving to the East Coast.
The Arkansas River flows through the heart of Wichita.
Every summer, Wichita hosts several large festivals and events.

Etymology

From the name of the Wichita people, a Native American tribe indigenous to the region.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪtʃɪtɑː / (AmE), / ˈwɪtʃɪtɑː / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people formerly living between Kansas and central Texas
member of a Native American people

Examples

The Wichita were known for their thatched grass lodges and agricultural skills.
She traced her ancestry to the Wichita who lived in present-day Oklahoma.
A group of Wichita gathered to celebrate their traditional festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Caddo - a member of another Native American people historically related to the Wichita

Etymology

From the self-designation of the people, possibly via French from the Wichita language (Kitikiti'sh).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɪtʃɪtɑː / (AmE), / ˈwɪtʃɪtɑː / (BrE)
the Caddoan language formerly spoken by the Wichita people of the southern plains of the United States.
Caddoan language of the Wichita people

Examples

Linguists have documented the grammar and vocabulary of Wichita in order to preserve it.
Few fluent speakers of Wichita remain today, making language revitalization efforts crucial.
University courses sometimes include lessons on Wichita as part of Native American studies.

Etymology

Named after the Wichita people, an Indigenous group from the southern plains.
