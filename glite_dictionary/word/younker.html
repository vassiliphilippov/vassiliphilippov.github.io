younker

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʌŋkər / (AmE), / ˈjʌŋkə / (BrE)
An archaic term for a young person, especially a young man or boy.
archaic term for a young man or boy
archaic

Examples

In his youth, the old captain had been a daring younker eager for adventure.
The painting depicts a rosy-cheeked younker with a mischievous grin.
Though now grey, he recalled his days as a younker running wild through the fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: youth - a young person, typically a young man
  • Synonym: lad - a boy or young man (especially in British English)

Etymology

From Middle Dutch joncker (young lord, young nobleman), related to 'young'.
