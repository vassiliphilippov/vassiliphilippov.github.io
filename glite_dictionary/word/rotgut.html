rotgut

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑtˌɡʌt / (AmE), / ˈrɒtˌɡʌt / (BrE)
Alcoholic drink of very poor quality, often cheap and harsh in taste.
inferior, cheap alcoholic drink

Examples

The cheap bar around the corner serves only rotgut to its late-night patrons.
He woke up with a headache after drinking too much rotgut whiskey.
During the Prohibition era, people often resorted to making their own rotgut at home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moonshine - illegally made or poor-quality alcohol
  • Synonym: firewater - strong alcoholic liquor, especially inferior or harsh varieties

Etymology

From 'rot' and 'gut', referring to the belief that such liquor is harmful to the stomach.
