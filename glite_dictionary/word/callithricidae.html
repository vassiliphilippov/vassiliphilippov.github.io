Callithricidae

1. NOUN

/ kəˌlɪθrɪˈsaɪdiː / (AmE), / kəˌlɪθrɪˈsaɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small South and Central American monkeys that includes the marmosets and tamarins
the marmoset and tamarin monkey family

Examples

The Callithricidae family includes both marmosets and tamarins found in South America.
Researchers are studying the social behavior of Callithricidae in rainforest environments.
Several endangered species belong to the Callithricidae, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marmoset - a small, agile South American monkey with soft fur and claw-like nails

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Callithrix (a genus of marmosets) and -idae, the zoological family suffix.
