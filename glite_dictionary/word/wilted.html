wilted

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɪltɪd / (AmE), / ˈwɪltɪd / (BrE)
(of plants) limp and drooping, usually as a result of heat, loss of water, or disease.
limp or drooping (of plants)

Examples

After a week without rain, the wilted flowers drooped sadly in the garden.
Please remove any wilted spinach leaves before serving the salad.
The lettuce had become wilted from sitting out in the heat too long.

Synset Examples

wilted lettuce

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fresh - recently picked, firm, and not wilted (of plants)

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'wilt', meaning to lose turgor or droop.

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɪltɪd / (AmE), / ˈwɪltɪd / (BrE)
lacking energy, strength, or determination; weak or exhausted
lacking energy or strength

Examples

By the end of the marathon, he looked completely wilted and could barely stand.
The intense pressure at work left her feeling wilted by the afternoon.
After a long day in the sun, the players emerged wilted from the field.

Synset Examples

gave a limp handshake
a limp gesture as if waving away all desire to know
the afternoon heat left her feeling wilted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weak - lacking physical strength or energy
  • Synonym: exhausted - extremely tired or drained of energy
  • Antonym: energetic - showing or involving great activity or vitality

Etymology

Past participle of 'wilt', originally meaning to lose freshness or vigor, especially of plants.
