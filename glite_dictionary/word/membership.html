membership

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛm.bɚ.ʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈmɛm.bə.ʃɪp / (BrE)
all the members of a group, organization, or society considered collectively
the members of a group collectively

Examples

The union's membership voted overwhelmingly in favor of the new contract.
After the scandal, the club's membership declined sharply.
Debate broke out among the membership on how to allocate the funds.

Synset Examples

they polled their membership
they found dissension in their own ranks
he joined the ranks of the unemployed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: membership - state of being a member of a group
  • Antonym: leadership - the leaders of a group or organization collectively

Etymology

From member + -ship (Old English -scipe, condition or quality).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛm.bɚˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈmɛm.bə.ʃɪp / (BrE)
the state of belonging to a group, organization, or club
state of being a member

Examples

She applied for membership in the tennis club last month.
The cost of membership includes access to all facilities and events.
Our gym offers special discounts for family membership.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affiliation - the state of being affiliated with a group
  • Antonym: membershipless - lacking membership

Etymology

From member + -ship (suffix forming nouns denoting condition or status).

WordNet ID: oewn-13954660-n
Synset POS: n
