millimeter

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪləˌmitɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪˌmiːtə / (BrE)
a metric unit of length equal to one thousandth of a meter
metric unit of length

Examples

The rainfall yesterday measured just six millimeters.
Most screws used in electronics are only a few millimeters long.
A sheet of paper is about one-tenth of a millimeter thick.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: centimeter - a metric unit of length equal to one hundredth of a meter
  • Degree Lower: micrometer - a metric unit of length equal to one millionth of a meter
  • Degree Higher: meter - the base unit of length in the metric system

Etymology

From French millimètre, from milli- (thousandth) + mètre (meter).
