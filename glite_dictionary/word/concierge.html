concierge

1. NOUN

/ kɑn.siˈɛrdʒ / (AmE), / kɒn.siˈɛəʒ / (BrE)
an employee in a hotel or apartment building whose job is to assist guests by handling mail, luggage, reservations, and overseeing access to the premises
hotel or apartment receptionist and caretaker

Examples

The concierge greeted us warmly and gave us directions to our room.
If you need tickets to a concert, the concierge can arrange them for you.
The concierge took care of my luggage while I checked in.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porter - a person employed to carry luggage or assist guests in a hotel or station

Etymology

From French 'concierge' (caretaker, porter).
