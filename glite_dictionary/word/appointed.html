appointed

1. ADJ

/ əˈpɔɪntɪd / (AmE), / əˈpɔɪntɪd / (BrE)
chosen or officially selected for a position or responsibility
chosen for a position or duty

Examples

She was the appointed manager after a long search process.
The appointed spokesperson addressed the media on behalf of the company.
Only the appointed members have the authority to vote in the meeting.

Synset Examples

the one appointed for guard duty

Related Terms

  • Antonym: elected - chosen by vote for a position

Etymology

Past participle of 'appoint', from Old French 'apointer', from Late Latin 'appunctare' (to bring to a point).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00850735-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ əˈpɔɪntɪd / (AmE), / əˈpɔɪntɪd / (BrE)
(of a person) having been officially assigned to a position or role, typically by selection rather than election.
officially assigned to a position

Examples

She became the newly appointed director of the organization.
The board consists of seven appointed members and three elected ones.
Only appointed officials can access certain confidential documents.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: elected - chosen for a position by vote or election

Etymology

From the verb 'appoint', from Old French 'apointier' (arrange, settle) and Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'punctum' (point).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00850001-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ əˈpɔɪntɪd / (AmE), / əˈpɔɪntɪd / (BrE)
(of a room or building) provided with furniture and accessories, especially in a tasteful or stylish way.
furnished (tastefully)

Examples

The hotel rooms were elegantly appointed with luxurious linens and modern furnishings.
They moved into a newly appointed office that impressed their clients.
The apartment was fully appointed with everything they needed for a comfortable stay.

Synset Examples

a house that is beautifully appointed
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01101008-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ əˈpɔɪntɪd / (AmE), / əˈpɔɪntɪd / (BrE)
(of a time, place, etc.) fixed or set officially, especially by an authority or by prior agreement.
fixed or set officially

Examples

Everyone should arrive at the appointed location by noon.
We met at the appointed hour to discuss the project.
The ceremony will begin at the appointed time regardless of the weather.

Synset Examples

at the time appointed (or the appointed time)

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scheduled - arranged or planned according to a timetable
  • Synonym: designated - officially assigned or set for a purpose
  • Antonym: random - made, done, or happening without method or conscious decision

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'appoint', from Old French 'apointer', from Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'punctum' (point).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02137098-s
Synset POS: s
