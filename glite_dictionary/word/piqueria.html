Piqueria

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈkɪər.i.ə / (AmE), / pɪˈkɪə.ri.ə / (BrE)
A small genus of tropical American perennial herbs or subshrubs in the family Asteraceae, with white to pale yellow flowers, sometimes included in the genus Stevia.
genus of tropical American flowering plants

Examples

The botanist discovered a new species within the genus Piqueria during her expedition to Central America.
Piqueria plants are sometimes confused with Stevia due to their similar floral structures.
The white and pale yellow flowers of Piqueria attract a variety of pollinators in their native habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Stevia - genus of plants in Asteraceae, sometimes including Piqueria

Etymology

Named after François Piquer (1746–1823), a Spanish physician and botanist.
