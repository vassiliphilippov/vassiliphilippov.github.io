WAC

1. NOUN

/ wæk / (AmE), / wæk / (BrE)
(in the US) Women's Army Corps; a unit of the United States Army organized during World War II for women, now defunct as a separate branch.
Women's Army Corps (US WWII military unit)
american

Examples

During World War II, my grandmother joined the WAC and served overseas.
The contributions of the WAC were crucial in supporting the U.S. military during the war.
After the war, many former WAC members continued their careers in the armed forces.

Etymology

Acronym from 'Women's Army Corps'.
