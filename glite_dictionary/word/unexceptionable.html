unexceptionable

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪkˈsɛpʃənəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪkˈsɛpʃənəbl̩ / (BrE)
(formal) Not open to objection or criticism; entirely acceptable.
entirely acceptable; beyond reproach

Examples

Her credentials for the position were unexceptionable, impressing everyone on the panel.
He spoke in an unexceptionable manner, never giving offense to any party.
The committee's decision was unexceptionable and accepted by all members.

Synset Examples

two unexceptionable witnesses
a judge's ethics should be unexceptionable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unimpeachable - impossible to discredit or challenge; reliable
  • Antonym: exceptionable - open to objection or criticism

Etymology

From un- (not) + exceptionable (liable to objection), patterned after 'unexceptionable' in 17th-century English.
