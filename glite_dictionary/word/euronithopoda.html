Euronithopoda

1. NOUN

/ jʊəroʊˌnɪθəˈpoʊdə / (AmE), / jʊərəʊˌnɪθəˈpəʊdə / (BrE)
a major group of herbivorous dinosaurs that includes duck-billed dinosaurs and their early relatives, such as hadrosaurs, trachodons, and iguanodon
group of herbivorous dinosaurs including hadrosaurs

Examples

The fossils discovered in the valley belong to the Euronithopoda, showcasing their diverse evolutionary traits.
Scientists debate the migratory patterns of Euronithopoda during the Late Cretaceous period.
Museum exhibits often feature skeletal reconstructions of Euronithopoda to illustrate dinosaur diversity.

Etymology

From Greek 'eu' (good), 'ornithos' (bird), and 'pous/pod' (foot), referring to their bird-like feet.

2. NOUN

/ jʊroʊˌnɪθəˈpoʊdə / (AmE), / jʊərəʊˌnɪθəˈpəʊdə / (BrE)
A suborder of herbivorous dinosaurs that includes duck-billed dinosaurs (hadrosaurs) and their early relatives, such as iguanodon.
dinosaur suborder including hadrosaurs and iguanodon

Examples

The Euronithopoda thrived during the Cretaceous period, developing a variety of adaptations for grazing.
Scientists debate whether certain fossils truly belong to the Euronithopoda suborder or represent a separate lineage.
Hadrosaurs, prominent members of the Euronithopoda, are well-known for their duck-billed snouts.

Etymology

From Greek 'eu-' (good), 'ornithos' (bird), and 'pous/pod-' (foot), referring to bird-like feet.
