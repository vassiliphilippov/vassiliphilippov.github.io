Zygomycotina

1. NOUN

/ zaɪˌɡoʊmaɪkoʊˈtaɪnə / (AmE), / zaɪˌɡəʊmaɪkəʊˈtɪnə / (BrE)
a former division of fungi characterized by the production of sexually produced zygospores
division of fungi producing zygospores
medical

Examples

Many members of the Zygomycotina are saprophytes found in soil or decaying plant material.
Research on Zygomycotina has advanced our understanding of fungal reproduction.
Rhizopus, a common bread mold, was once classified within the Zygomycotina.

Etymology

From Greek zygōn (yoke) + mykēs (fungus) + -ina (suffix indicating group).

2. NOUN

/ zaɪɡoʊmaɪkoʊˈtaɪnə / (AmE), / zaɪɡəʊmaɪkəʊˈtiːnə / (BrE)
a former division of fungi characterized by the production of zygospores during sexual reproduction; now mostly classified within the phylum Mucoromycota
former fungal division producing zygospores
medical

Examples

Many bread molds belong to the Zygomycotina, which are known for their rapid growth.
Fungi within Zygomycotina reproduce sexually through the formation of zygospores.
Scientists have reclassified most organisms formerly in Zygomycotina due to advances in DNA analysis.

Etymology

From Greek zygon (yoke) + mykes (fungus), referring to the yoke-shaped spores.
