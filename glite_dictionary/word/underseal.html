underseal

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndɚˌsil / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌsiːl / (BrE)
a coating of tar or rubberlike material applied to the underside of a motor vehicle to protect it from corrosion
protective coating under vehicles

Examples

The mechanic applied a fresh layer of underseal to the car to prevent rust.
Regular underseal can extend your vehicle's life in harsh weather conditions.
It's important to check the underseal for damage every few years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rustproofing - application of protective material to prevent rusting

Etymology

Formed from 'under-' (below) and 'seal' (to close tightly), referring to sealing the underside of vehicles.
