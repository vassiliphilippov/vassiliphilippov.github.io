to bless

1. VERB

/ blɛs / (AmE), / blɛs / (BrE)
to make something holy by performing a religious ceremony or prayer
make holy by religious rite

Examples

The priest will bless the new church during the ceremony next week.
They gathered to bless the bread and wine before the meal.
The monk blessed the holy water before sprinkling it on the congregation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consecrate - to make or declare something sacred
  • Antonym: curse - to invoke harm or misfortune on something

Etymology

From Old English *blētsian* (to consecrate, make holy), from *blōd* (blood), originally referring to consecration with blood.

2. VERB

/ blɛs / (AmE), / blɛs / (BrE)
to speak words of blessing or benediction over someone or something, especially as part of a religious or formal act
to give a benediction; to bless

Examples

The priest blessed the congregation at the end of the service.
Before the meal, she blessed the food with a short prayer.
He blessed his newborn daughter as part of the ceremony.

Synset Examples

The dying man blessed his son

Related Terms

  • Antonym: curse - to invoke or wish evil upon; the opposite of blessing

Etymology

From Old English *blētsian* (to consecrate, make holy), related to *blōd* (blood), originally meaning 'to mark with blood in a religious rite'.

3. VERB

/ blɛs / (AmE), / blɛs / (BrE)
to confer prosperity, happiness, or good fortune on someone or something
confer prosperity or happiness

Examples

May this new year bless you with joy and success.
Her generosity has blessed many people in the community.
We hope the rains will bless our crops this season.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: curse - to invoke harm or misfortune upon someone

Etymology
