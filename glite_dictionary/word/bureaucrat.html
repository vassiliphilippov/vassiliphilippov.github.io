bureaucrat

1. NOUN

/ bjʊˈɹɑː.kræt / (AmE), / bjʊəˈɹɒ.kræt / (BrE)
an official in a government or other organization who is part of the administrative system, often regarded as concerned with procedural correctness at the expense of people's needs
official in an administrative system

Examples

The bureaucrat processed the paperwork slowly, insisting every form be filled out exactly.
As a senior bureaucrat, she oversees several departments within the ministry of health.
Some people criticize bureaucrats for sticking too closely to rules and procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: functionary - an official who performs specific duties in a bureaucracy
  • Synonym: administrator - a person responsible for running an organization or part of an organization

Etymology

From French 'bureaucrate', from 'bureaucratie' (bureaucracy), ultimately from 'bureau' (office) and Greek 'kratos' (power, rule).
