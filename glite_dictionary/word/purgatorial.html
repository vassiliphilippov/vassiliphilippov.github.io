purgatorial

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɝːɡəˈtɔːriəl / (AmE), / ˌpɜːɡəˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
Relating to or resembling purgatory; suffering through a process of purification or temporary hardship.
relating to purgatory; involving temporary suffering

Examples

The novel describes her purgatorial journey through loss and healing.
He felt trapped in a purgatorial state between hope and despair.
The exam period was a purgatorial experience for many students.

Synset Examples

purgatorial fires

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purgatory - a place or state of suffering inhabited by the souls of sinners being purified

Etymology

From Latin 'purgatorius' (of cleansing or purifying), from 'purgare' (to purify).

2. ADJ

/ pɝːgəˈtɔːriəl / (AmE), / pɜːgəˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
Relating to the cleansing or purging of sin, especially in the context of purgatory or purification rites.
relating to cleansing or purging of sin

Examples

The monks performed purgatorial rites to atone for the sins of the deceased.
He viewed his hardships as a purgatorial experience leading to spiritual growth.
The novelist described the war as a purgatorial ordeal that tested the soul.

Synset Examples

purgatorial rites

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purifying - making pure by removing impurities

Etymology

From Medieval Latin purgatorius (of cleansing), from Latin purgare (to purge).
