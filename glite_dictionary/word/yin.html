yin

1. NOUN

/ jɪn / (AmE), / jɪn / (BrE)
(in Chinese philosophy) the dark, passive, and feminine principle of the universe, which interacts with yang to maintain harmony in the cosmos.
dark, feminine force in Chinese philosophy

Examples

The principles of yin and yang are central to traditional Chinese medicine.
In the symbol, the dark half represents yin and the light half represents yang.
She explained how yin energy is associated with calmness and introspection.

Synset Examples

the interaction of yin and yang maintains the harmony of the universe

Related Terms

  • Antonym: yang - the bright, active, masculine principle in Chinese cosmology

Etymology

From Chinese (Mandarin) yīn (阴), meaning 'shady side' or 'feminine principle.'
