pretermission

1. NOUN

/ ˌpriːtərˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːtəˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of passing over or omitting something, especially deliberately or by neglect
the act of omitting something

Examples

The pretermission of key evidence affected the outcome of the trial.
His pretermission of such an important detail was surprising to everyone involved.
Literary critics debated whether the pretermission of certain themes was intentional.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: omission - the act of leaving out or neglecting something
  • Antonym: inclusion - the act of including something

Etymology

From Latin praetermissio (a passing by, omission), from praeter (beyond) + mittere (to send).
