bramble

1. NOUN

/ ˈbræmbəl / (AmE), / ˈbræm.bəl / (BrE)
a rough, thorny shrub or vine, especially one of the genus Rubus such as a blackberry or raspberry bush
thorny shrub or vine, e.g., blackberry

Examples

The path was overgrown with bramble, making it difficult to walk through.
They picked fresh blackberries from the bramble in the hedgerow.
A bird was caught in the tangled bramble near the old fence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackberry - a fruit-bearing bramble; the plant producing blackberries

Etymology

Old English brǣmel, related to 'broom'.
