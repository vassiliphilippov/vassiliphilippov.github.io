Galbulidae

1. NOUN

/ gælˈbjuːlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / gælˈbjuːlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical American birds known as jacamars, characterized by their slender bodies and long, sharp bills.
family of birds (jacamars)

Examples

The Galbulidae are well known for their iridescent plumage and insect-eating habits.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Galbulidae family in the Amazon rainforest.
Unlike many other birds, members of the Galbulidae often perch quietly while waiting for prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jacamar - any bird of the family Galbulidae, with iridescent plumage and long bills

Etymology

From New Latin Galbulidae, based on the genus Galbula.
