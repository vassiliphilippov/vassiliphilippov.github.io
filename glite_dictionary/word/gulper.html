gulper

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌlpər / (AmE), / ˈɡʌlpə / (BrE)
A person or animal who swallows large amounts of food or drink greedily or quickly.
one who swallows greedily or quickly

Examples

He was such a gulper that he finished his drink in seconds.
The little dog proved to be a real gulper at mealtime, barely chewing its food.
Among the guests, one enthusiastic gulper kept asking for refills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gulp - to swallow food or drink quickly or in large amounts

Etymology

From 'gulp' (to swallow quickly or greedily) + '-er' (agent suffix).
