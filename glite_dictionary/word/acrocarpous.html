acrocarpous

1. ADJ

/ ˌæk.roʊˈkɑːr.pəs / (AmE), / ˌæ.krəˈkɑː.pəs / (BrE)
(of mosses) having the reproductive organs (archegonia) at the top of the stem.
mosses with archegonia at stem tip
medical

Examples

Many upright mosses are described as acrocarpous because their capsules form at the top of the main stem.
The acrocarpous growth habit is an essential feature used to identify certain moss species.
Only acrocarpous mosses were found on the rocky outcrops during the survey.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pleurocarpous - (of mosses) having reproductive organs on short side branches

Etymology

From Greek akros (topmost) + karpos (fruit).
