partiality

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːrʃiˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌpɑːʃiˈælɪti / (BrE)
the act of showing favoritism toward one person, group, or opinion over others; bias
favoritism; bias toward someone or something

Examples

The judge was accused of partiality in favor of the prosecution.
It is important to avoid partiality when making decisions that affect many people.
Her obvious partiality for her younger child was clear to everyone.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: impartiality - equal and fair treatment without bias
  • Synonym: bias - prejudice in favor of or against something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin partialitas, from Late Latin partialis (part, portion).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɑrʃiˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌpɑːʃiˈælɪti / (BrE)
an unfair preference or fondness for one thing, person, or group over others
unfair preference or fondness

Examples

The teacher’s partiality toward certain students was obvious to the whole class.
He showed a marked partiality for spicy food, always choosing the hottest dishes.
Her partiality for classical music influenced the concert program.

Synset Examples

he had a fondness for whiskey
she had dismissed him quite brutally, relegating him to the status of a passing fancy, or less

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preference - a greater liking for one alternative over another or others
  • Antonym: impartiality - the state of not favoring one side over another

Etymology

From Late Latin partialitas, from Latin pars (part).
