delinquent

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈlɪŋkwənt / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪŋkwənt / (BrE)
(of a debt or account) past due and not paid at the scheduled time.
overdue; unpaid debt or account

Examples

The company called to remind her about the delinquent payment on her loan.
Several accounts have become delinquent after months without any payments.
A delinquent bill can hurt your credit rating if left unpaid.

Synset Examples

an overdue installment
a delinquent account

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overdue - not paid or done by the expected time

Etymology

From Latin delinquent-, stem of delinquere (to fail, to be lacking).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00138209-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ dəˈlɪŋkwənt / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪŋkwənt / (BrE)
(of a person, especially a young one) showing or characterized by a tendency to commit crime or engage in antisocial behaviour.
guilty of a misdeed or offense

Examples

The judge decided to give the delinquent teenager a second chance.
Special programs aim to rehabilitate delinquent youths in the community.
School counselors work with students who are considered at risk of becoming delinquent.

Synset Examples

delinquent minors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criminal - a person who has committed a crime
  • Antonym: law-abiding - obeying the law and not committing crimes

Etymology

From Latin 'delinquens', present participle of 'delinquere' (to fail, to be wanting, to offend).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01325440-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ dəˈlɪŋkwənt / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪŋkwənt / (BrE)
failing to perform a duty or obligation, especially in paying money owed or in fulfilling responsibilities
failing in a required duty or obligation

Examples

He was delinquent in submitting his tax returns for several years.
Some borrowers become delinquent on their mortgage payments during financial hardship.
It was delinquent of her not to respond to important work emails.

Synset Examples

derelict (or delinquent) in his duty
neglectful of his duties
remiss of you not to pay your bills

Related Terms

  • Synonym: negligent - failing to take proper care over something
  • Antonym: dutiful - conscientiously or obediently fulfilling one's duty

Etymology

From Latin delinquent-, delinquens, present participle of delinquere (to fail, be lacking).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00758741-s
Synset POS: s

4. NOUN

/ dɪˈlɪŋkwənt / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪŋkwənt / (BrE)
a young person who commits crimes or engages in antisocial or illegal behavior
young offender

Examples

The youth center works with local delinquents to help them get back on track.
Police arrested several delinquents for vandalizing the park last night.
Programs for delinquent teens often focus on education and job training.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offender - person who commits a crime
  • Synonym: criminal - person who breaks the law

Etymology

From Latin 'delinquens', present participle of 'delinquere' (to fail, be lacking, do wrong).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10020538-n
Synset POS: n
