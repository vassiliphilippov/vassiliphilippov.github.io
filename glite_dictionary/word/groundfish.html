groundfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊndˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊnd.fɪʃ / (BrE)
a fish that lives on or near the sea bottom, especially those of commercial importance such as cod, haddock, or flounder
a fish living near the sea bottom

Examples

The fishing industry relies heavily on groundfish such as cod and haddock.
Scientists are concerned about declining groundfish populations in the North Atlantic.
Regulations limit the yearly catch of various groundfish species to protect marine ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pelagic - fish that live in the open water, away from the sea bottom

Etymology

From 'ground' (sea bottom) + 'fish'.
