wish-wash

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪʃˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈwɪʃˌwɒʃ / (BrE)
silly or nonsensical talk or writing; meaningless chatter.
nonsensical talk or writing

Examples

I don't have time to listen to your wish-wash right now.
His speech was full of wish-wash and empty promises.
Stop filling the report with wish-wash; just stick to the facts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - words or ideas that make no sense
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06624351-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪʃˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈwɪʃˌwɒʃ / (BrE)
A thin, watery drink lacking in flavor or substance.
thin, watery drink

Examples

He complained that the soup tasted like nothing but wish-wash.
The drink she served was so diluted it was mostly wish-wash.
After the ice melted, the soda turned into a strange wish-wash with hardly any flavor left.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swill - a thin, weak, or inferior drink
Source: WordNet
