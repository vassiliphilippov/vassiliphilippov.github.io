uncanny

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkæni / (AmE), / ʌnˈkæni / (BrE)
strange or mysterious, especially in a way that is unsettling or difficult to explain; surpassing what is normal or ordinary.
strange or mysterious; beyond normal

Examples

He has an uncanny ability to predict what will happen next.
There was an uncanny resemblance between the two strangers.
The forest was so quiet that it felt uncanny to walk through it alone.

Synset Examples

Beyond his preternatural affability there is some acid and some steel
his uncanny sense of direction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eerie - strange and frightening
  • Antonym: normal - conforming to the usual, typical, or expected

Etymology

From Scottish dialect, variant of 'canny' (shrewd) with prefix 'un-' (not).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkæni / (AmE), / ʌnˈkani / (BrE)
strange or mysterious in a way that suggests supernatural influences; difficult to explain or unsettlingly unusual
strangely mysterious; suggesting the supernatural

Examples

There was an uncanny silence in the abandoned house that made everyone uneasy.
Her uncanny resemblance to the old family portrait shocked us all.
The magician performed an uncanny trick that seemed to defy explanation.

Synset Examples

an eldritch screech
the three weird sisters
stumps … had uncanny shapes as of monstrous creatures
an unearthly light
he could hear the unearthly scream of some curlew piercing the din

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eerie - strange and frightening; mysterious
  • Synonym: spooky - causing a sensation of fear; eerie
  • Antonym: normal - conforming to a standard; usual or expected

Etymology

From Scottish un- (not) + canny (careful, prudent, or knowing), later 'mysterious or beyond normal',' originally 18th century.
