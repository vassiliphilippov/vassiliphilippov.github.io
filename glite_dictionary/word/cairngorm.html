cairngorm

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛrnˌɡɔrm / (AmE), / ˈkeənˌɡɔːm / (BrE)
a smoky yellow or brown variety of quartz, often used as a gemstone
smoky yellow or brown quartz gemstone

Examples

The brooch was adorned with a large cairngorm at its center.
She wore a pendant featuring a polished cairngorm set in silver.
A cairngorm is often prized for its rich, smoky hue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smoky quartz - a grey or brown variety of quartz

Etymology

Named after the Cairngorm Mountains in Scotland, where this mineral was first found.
