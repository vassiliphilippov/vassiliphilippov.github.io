Peziza domicilina

1. NOUN

/ pɛˈzaɪzə ˌdoʊmɪˈsɪlɪnə / (AmE), / pɛˈziːzə ˌdɒmɪˈsɪlɪnə / (BrE)
a species of cup fungus with fragile, ghostly white fruiting bodies that stain yellow when broken, commonly found in strongly alkaline habitats
a species of cup fungus

Examples

The fragile fruiting body of Peziza domicilina is notable for its ghostly white color.
Researchers found Peziza domicilina thriving in the strongly alkaline soil near the old building.
When broken, Peziza domicilina stains yellow, making it easy to distinguish from other fungi.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Peziza' (Latin, a cup-shaped fungus) and specific epithet 'domicilina,' referring to its occasional growth in domestic settings.
