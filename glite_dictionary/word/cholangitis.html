cholangitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊ.lænˈdʒaɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ˌkəʊ.lænˈdʒaɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
Inflammation of the bile ducts, often caused by infection or blockage.
inflammation of the bile ducts
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with cholangitis after presenting symptoms of fever and jaundice.
A blockage in the bile ducts can lead to severe cholangitis if not treated promptly.
Doctors treated her cholangitis with antibiotics and supportive care.

Etymology

From Greek *chole* (bile) + *angeion* (vessel) + *-itis* (inflammation).
