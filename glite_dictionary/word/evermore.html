evermore

1. ADV

/ ˌɛvərˈmɔr / (AmE), / ˌɛvəˈmɔː / (BrE)
for all future time; always from now on; forever
forever; always in the future

Examples

She vowed to cherish his memory evermore.
The legend will live on evermore in our hearts.
He left his homeland, never to return evermore.

Synset Examples

lead a blameless life evermore

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forever - for all time; always
  • Antonym: nevermore - at no future time; never again

Etymology

From Middle English, combining 'ever' and 'more'.
