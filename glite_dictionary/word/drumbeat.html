drumbeat

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrʌmˌbit / (AmE), / ˈdrʌmˌbiːt / (BrE)
(military) A beating of a drum used as a signal for lowering the flag at sundown.
military drum signal at sundown

Examples

At sunset, the soldiers stood silent as the drumbeat signaled the lowering of the flag.
The ceremonial drumbeat marked the end of the day's duties at the fort.
A single steady drumbeat echoed across the field, calling everyone to attention.

Etymology

From 'drum' + 'beat', referring to the act of beating a drum.

/ ˈdrʌmˌbiːt / (AmE), / ˈdrʌmˌbiːt / (BrE)
the sound produced by striking a drum repeatedly
sound made by beating a drum

Examples

He listened to the distant drumbeat coming from the festival grounds.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdrʌmˌbit / (AmE), / ˈdrʌmˌbiːt / (BrE)
a persistent and forceful public demand or advocacy, especially for a cause or action
persistent advocacy or demand

Examples

There was a constant drumbeat of criticism against the proposed law.
Politicians responded to the public drumbeat demanding environmental reform.
The newspaper maintained a drumbeat for governmental transparency throughout the year.

Synset Examples

the warmongers kept up their drumbeat on Iraq

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campaign - an organized course of action to achieve a goal

Etymology

From 'drum' (percussion instrument) and 'beat', referring originally to the rhythmic sound produced by drumming, later used metaphorically for persistent advocacy.
