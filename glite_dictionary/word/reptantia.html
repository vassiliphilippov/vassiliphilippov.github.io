Reptantia

1. NOUN

/ rɛpˈtæntiə / (AmE), / rɛpˈtæntiə / (BrE)
A subgroup of decapod crustaceans including lobsters, crabs, and similar crawling species.
group of crawling decapod crustaceans

Examples

The fossil record of Reptantia reveals the evolutionary history of crabs and lobsters.
Researchers studied the locomotion patterns in various species within Reptantia.
The order Decapoda is divided into groups such as Reptantia and Natantia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Brachyura - infraorder of crabs, closely related group
  • Degree Higher: Macrura - subgroup including long-tailed decapods like lobsters

Etymology

From Latin 'reptans' (crawling), present participle of 'repere' (to crawl).
