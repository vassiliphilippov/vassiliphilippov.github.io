Oncorhynchus nerka

1. NOUN

/ ɑnkəˈrɪŋkəs ˈnɜrkə / (AmE), / ɒŋkɒˈrɪŋkəs ˈnɜːkə / (BrE)
A species of small salmon with red flesh, native to rivers and tributaries of the northern Pacific, valued as food; adults die after spawning.
a species of red-fleshed Pacific salmon

Examples

The population of Oncorhynchus nerka has declined in several rivers due to habitat loss.
Scientists are studying the migration patterns of Oncorhynchus nerka along the Pacific coast.
Smoked Oncorhynchus nerka is a delicacy in many countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sockeye salmon - the common name for Oncorhynchus nerka

Etymology

From Greek onkos (hook) and rhynchos (snout); species epithet from Russian 'nerka' (red salmon).
