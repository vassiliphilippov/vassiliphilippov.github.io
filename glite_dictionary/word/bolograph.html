bolograph

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊləˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈbəʊləˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a record or recording produced by a bolometer, used to measure radiant energy
record made by a bolometer
medical

Examples

The scientist analyzed the bolograph to determine the intensity of the infrared radiation.
After running the experiment, she carefully plotted the results on a bolograph.
A clear bolograph can reveal subtle variations in thermal energy across a surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bolometer - an instrument for measuring radiant energy

Etymology

From 'bolometer' and '-graph', indicating an instrument for recording.
