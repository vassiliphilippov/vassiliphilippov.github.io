werewolf

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛrˌwʊlf / (AmE), / ˈwɪəwʊlf / (BrE)
(in folklore) a person who, according to legend, can transform into a wolf, typically during a full moon.
mythical human-wolf shapeshifter

Examples

Legends told of a werewolf terrorizing the village every full moon.
In the movie, the character is bitten and becomes a werewolf.
Some believe that the myth of the werewolf began as an explanation for unusual behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lycanthrope - a person who believes they can transform into a wolf

Etymology

From Old English 'wer' (man) + 'wulf' (wolf).
