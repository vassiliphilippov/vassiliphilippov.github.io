William IV

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪljəm ðə ˈfɔrθ / (AmE), / ˈwɪljəm ðə ˈfɔːθ / (BrE)
King of the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland from 1830 to 1837, noted for his long naval career and as the son of George III.
British king, reigned 1830–1837

Examples

William IV was known as the 'Sailor King' due to his service in the Royal Navy.
The reign of William IV saw significant reforms, including the abolition of slavery in the British Empire.
After the death of his brother George IV, William IV ascended the throne in 1830.
