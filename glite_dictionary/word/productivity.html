productivity

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊ.dəkˈtɪv.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌprɒd.ʌkˈtɪv.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The rate at which goods or services are produced, especially in relation to the amount of input such as labor, capital, or equipment.
rate of producing goods or services

Examples

The new software has greatly improved our team's productivity.
High productivity is crucial for the success of any manufacturing business.
After a good night's sleep, her productivity increased noticeably.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: efficiency - the ability to do something well without wasting time or resources

Etymology

From Latin 'productivitas', from 'producere' (to produce).

2. NOUN

/ ˌproʊ.dəkˈtɪv.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌprɒd.ʌkˈtɪv.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
(Economics) The rate at which goods or services are produced, especially as measured relative to the amount of labor, time, or resources used.
output per unit of labor or resource

Examples

Higher productivity means that more goods are produced with the same amount of labor.
The company implemented new software to boost productivity among its employees.
A drop in productivity can have significant impacts on the economy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: efficiency - achieving maximum productivity with minimum wasted effort or expense

Etymology

From French 'productivité', from Latin 'productivus' (productive).
