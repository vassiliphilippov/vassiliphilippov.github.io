sheepwoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃipˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈʃiːpwʊmən / (BrE)
A woman whose occupation is to tend or herd sheep, especially on open land.
female sheep herder

Examples

The sheepwoman guided her flock across the grassy hillside at dawn.
As a skilled sheepwoman, she knew every sheep by name and could spot illness quickly.
The novel features a lone sheepwoman struggling to maintain her family's traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shepherdess - a woman who tends sheep
  • Antonym: sheepman - a man who herds or owns sheep

Etymology

From 'sheep' + 'woman', formed in analogy to 'sheepman' and related compounds.
