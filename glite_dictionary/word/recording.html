recording

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈkɔrdɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪˈkɔːdɪŋ / (BrE)
A storage medium on which sounds or images have been captured and can be played back.
device with stored sounds or images

Examples

The recording of their wedding ceremony was stored on a DVD.
She listened to an old jazz recording from the 1950s.
Police reviewed the video recording for evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tape - magnetic recording medium for audio or video
  • Synonym: album - collection of recorded music tracks released together

Etymology

From record + -ing; 'record' from Latin 'recordari' (to remember).

2. NOUN

/ rəˈkɔrdɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪˈkɔːdɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of capturing sounds, images, or data for later reproduction or reference, especially on audio or video media
capturing sounds, images, or data for playback

Examples

We listened to the old recording of her singing from the school concert.
The interview was saved as a recording for future reference.
She made a recording of the meeting, just in case anyone missed it.

Synset Examples

she watched the recording from a sound-proof booth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: record - to capture and store sounds, images, or information

Etymology

From the verb 'record' + -ing, originally from Old French 'recorder' (to recall, remember).

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈkɔrdɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪˈkɔːdɪŋ / (BrE)
a signal, especially of sound or picture, that has been captured and stored for later playback or reproduction
stored signal of sound or picture

Examples

The police listened carefully to the recording of the suspect’s phone call.
I have a recording of the concert we attended last year.
She deleted the recording from her phone to free up space.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: record - to capture and store (sounds, images, data, etc.)

Etymology

From the verb 'record', from Old French *recorder* (to record, remember), from Latin *recordari* (call to mind).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
