manzanita

1. NOUN

/ ˌmænzəˈniːtə / (AmE), / ˌmænzəˈniːtə / (BrE)
An evergreen shrub or small tree native to western North America, with glossy leaves and clusters of small, edible red berries.
evergreen shrub or tree with red berries

Examples

The trail was bordered by dense stands of manzanita with bright red berries.
Hikers used wood from the manzanita tree to make walking sticks.
The glossy leaves of manzanita make it a popular choice for native landscaping.

Etymology

From Spanish manzanita (diminutive of 'manzana', meaning 'apple'), referring to the small apple-like fruit.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmænzəˈniːtə / (AmE), / ˌmænzəˈniːtə / (BrE)
a type of evergreen shrub native to warm, dry regions of western North America, especially known for its smooth reddish bark and small round fruit
evergreen shrub native to western North America

Examples

The hikers rested in the shade of a manzanita bush during their trek through the hills.
In California, the distinctive red stems of the manzanita make it easy to identify.
Some species of manzanita produce edible berries that wildlife enjoy.

Etymology

From Spanish 'manzanita', meaning 'little apple', referring to the plant's small, apple-like fruit.
