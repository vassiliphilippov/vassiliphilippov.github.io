W. B. Yeats

1. PROPN

/ ˌdʌbəl ˈju ˌbi ˈjeɪts / (AmE), / ˌdʌbl juː ˌbiː ˈjeɪts / (BrE)
(1865–1939) Irish poet and dramatist, a leading figure of 20th-century literature and a founder of the Irish Literary Revival.
Irish poet and dramatist

Examples

W. B. Yeats won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1923.
Many of W. B. Yeats's poems explore Irish mythology and mysticism.
The plays of W. B. Yeats are still performed in theaters today.
