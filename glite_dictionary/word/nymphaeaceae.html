Nymphaeaceae

1. NOUN

/ nɪmˌfiːəˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / nɪmˌfiːɪˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of aquatic flowering plants commonly known as the water-lily family, characterized by floating leaves and showy flowers
the water-lily plant family

Examples

The Nymphaeaceae are renowned for their strikingly beautiful water lilies found in ponds worldwide.
Botanists classify water lilies under the family Nymphaeaceae.
Many members of the Nymphaeaceae have large, floating leaves and delicate, colorful flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water lily - an aquatic plant with large floating leaves and beautiful flowers

Etymology

From Latin 'nymphaea', meaning water lily, and the botanical suffix '-aceae' for plant families.

2. NOUN

/ nɪmˌfiːəˈsiːˌiː / (AmE), / nɪmˌfiːɪˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of aquatic dicotyledonous plants comprising the water lilies and their relatives.
family of aquatic plants (water lilies)

Examples

The Nymphaeaceae family contains many beautiful water lilies found in ponds worldwide.
Researchers studied the reproductive biology of several members of Nymphaeaceae.
The leaves of plants in the Nymphaeaceae family are often large and float on water surfaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water lily - any aquatic plant of the genus Nymphaea

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Nymphaeaceae', based on Latin 'nymphaea' (water lily).
