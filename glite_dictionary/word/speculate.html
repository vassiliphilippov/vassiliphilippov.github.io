to speculate

1. VERB

/ ˈspɛkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈspɛkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to think deeply or carefully about a subject, often considering different possibilities or theories
reflect deeply or theorize

Examples

Philosophers continue to speculate about the existence of life beyond Earth.
Scientists often speculate on possible explanations before conducting experiments.
She likes to speculate about the meaning of dreams in her journal.

Synset Examples

I mulled over the events of the afternoon
philosophers have speculated on the question of God for thousands of years
The scientist must stop to observe and start to excogitate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ponder - to think carefully about something for a long time
  • Synonym: to contemplate - to think deeply about something
  • Synonym: to consider - to think about something carefully

Etymology

From Latin speculatus, past participle of speculari (to observe), from specula (watchtower), related to specere (to look).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00632042-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈspɛkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈspɛkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to invest money in ways that involve risk, hoping to gain a profit from short-term price fluctuations
invest at a risk for profit

Examples

Many people speculate on the stock market hoping to make quick profits.
He decided to speculate in real estate, buying properties to sell them at higher prices.
Some investors prefer to speculate rather than follow a long-term investment strategy.

Synset Examples

I bought this house not because I want to live in it but to sell it later at a good price, so I am speculating

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to invest - to put money into financial schemes, shares, or property with the expectation of achieving a profit

Etymology

From Latin speculatus, past participle of speculari (to observe, watch).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02276935-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈspɛkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈspɛkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
To form opinions or make guesses about something without having all the facts or sufficient evidence.
form a theory or guess without firm evidence

Examples

The professor encouraged us to speculate about the future of artificial intelligence in society.
It is too early to speculate on the reasons for the company's sudden collapse.
Some historians speculate that the lost city lies beneath the ocean.

Synset Examples

Scientists supposed that large dinosaurs lived in swamps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guess - to form an opinion or supposition without sufficient evidence
  • Synonym: conjecture - to form an opinion or conclusion based on incomplete information
  • Antonym: to know - to be aware of through information or experience

Etymology

From Latin 'speculatus', past participle of 'speculari' (to observe), from 'specula' (watchtower).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00635105-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈspɛkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈspɛkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to form theories or express guesses about something without having firm evidence
to guess or theorize without firm evidence

Examples

The scientists speculated about the origins of the mysterious object found in the forest.
We often speculate on what life will be like in the future.
She speculated that her colleague might be planning to retire soon.

Synset Examples

We were speculating whether the President had to resign after the scandal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guess - to form an opinion or answer without sufficient information
  • Synonym: conjecture - to form an opinion based on incomplete information
  • Antonym: to know - to be certain or have definite information

Etymology

From Latin speculatus, past participle of speculari (to observe), from specula (watchtower), from specere (to look at).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00929020-v
Synset POS: v
