ricketiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪkətinəs / (AmE), / ˈrɪkɪtɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being unstable or not securely fixed in place
instability; lack of secure fixation

Examples

The ricketiness of the old wooden bridge made everyone cross quickly and nervously.
After years of neglect, the ricketiness of the garden shed became dangerously apparent.
He was concerned about the ricketiness of the chair, which wobbled every time he sat down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instability - the state of being unstable
  • Antonym: solidity - the quality of being solid or stable

Etymology

From 'rickety', which is of uncertain origin, perhaps from 'rickets', a disease causing bone weakness.
