bayonet

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪ.ə.nɪt / (AmE), / ˈbeɪ.ə.nɪt / (BrE)
a blade that can be attached to the muzzle of a rifle or similar firearm and used as a weapon
blade fixed to a rifle as a weapon

Examples

The soldier fixed his bayonet to his rifle before the charge.
During the inspection, the sergeant checked that every weapon had a bayonet attached.
He displayed an antique bayonet from the 19th century in his collection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dagger - a short knife with a pointed and edged blade, used as a weapon

Etymology

From French 'baïonnette', probably after Bayonne, a city in France where such weapons were made.

2. VERB

/ ˈbeɪ.əˌnɛt / (AmE), / ˈbeɪ.ə.nɪt / (BrE)
to stab or kill someone with a bayonet.
stab with a bayonet

Examples

The soldier threatened to bayonet anyone who got too close to the prisoner.
During the battle, he bayoneted an enemy combatant while advancing through the trenches.
The terrified villagers watched as the soldiers bayonetted sacks of grain, searching for hidden weapons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stab - to wound or kill by piercing with a pointed weapon

Etymology

From French *baïonnette*, diminutive of Bayonne, a city in France where the weapon was first made.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01234544-v
Synset POS: v
