reveler

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛvələr / (AmE), / ˈrɛvələ / (BrE)
a person who enjoys themselves noisily, especially at parties or festivities
person who parties or celebrates noisily

Examples

The streets were filled with revelers celebrating the New Year.
At midnight, the revelers cheered and released balloons into the sky.
Security had to manage the crowd as more revelers entered the festival grounds.

Synset Examples

the clubs attract revelers as young as thirteen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partygoer - a person who attends parties
  • Synonym: celebrant - a person who celebrates or participates in a celebration
  • Antonym: killjoy - a person who spoils the fun of others

Etymology

From revel + -er, where 'revel' comes from Old French 'reveler' (to make merry).
