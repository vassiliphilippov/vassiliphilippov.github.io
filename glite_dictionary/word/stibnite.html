stibnite

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪbˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈstɪbnaɪt / (BrE)
a soft, gray mineral consisting of antimony sulfide, the principal ore of antimony
a mineral ore of antimony

Examples

Geologists discovered a rich vein of stibnite deep underground.
The shiny, metallic luster of stibnite makes it easy to identify in the field.
Much of the world's antimony is produced by smelting stibnite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antimony - a metallic element extracted from stibnite

Etymology

From Latin *stibium* (antimony), from Greek *stimmi*, via French *stibine* and German *Stibnit*.
