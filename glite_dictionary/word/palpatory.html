palpatory

1. ADJ

/ ˈpæl.pəˌtɔr.i / (AmE), / ˈpælpəˌtəri / (BrE)
relating to or involving palpation, especially as a method of medical examination by touch
relating to medical examination by touch
medical

Examples

The doctor performed a palpatory assessment of the abdomen to detect any tenderness.
Palpatory findings indicated a possible swelling in the patient's lymph nodes.
Students practiced their palpatory skills during the clinical examination class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palpation - the act of examining by touch, especially in medicine

Etymology

From Latin 'palpare' (to touch, to stroke), modeled on palpation.
