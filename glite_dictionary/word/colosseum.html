Colosseum

1. PROPN

/ kəˈlɒsiəm / (AmE), / kɒl.əˈsiː.əm / (BrE)
the large ancient amphitheater in Rome built in the first century AD, known for gladiatorial combats and public spectacles
ancient Roman amphitheater in Rome

Examples

Thousands of tourists visit the Colosseum every year to admire its ancient architecture.
The Colosseum once hosted gladiator battles and other public spectacles in ancient Rome.
Restoration work is ongoing to preserve the iconic Colosseum for future generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amphitheater - an open, circular or oval building for public events

Etymology

From Latin 'Colosseum', referring to its colossal size.
