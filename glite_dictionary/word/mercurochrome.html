Mercurochrome

1. NOUN

/ mərˈkjʊrəˌkroʊm / (AmE), / məˈkjʊərəˌkrəʊm / (BrE)
A red, mercury-containing compound used as a topical antiseptic for minor cuts and scrapes; originally a trademark name.
antiseptic compound for minor wounds

Examples

My grandmother always kept a bottle of Mercurochrome in her medicine cabinet for treating scrapes.
The nurse dabbed Mercurochrome on my knee after I fell on the playground.
Because of concerns about mercury, Mercurochrome is rarely used today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiseptic - substance that prevents infection

Etymology

From 'mercury', referring to its mercury content, and 'chrome,' from its red color; originally a trademark.
