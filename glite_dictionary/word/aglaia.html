Aglaia

1. PROPN

/ əˈɡleɪ.i.ə / (AmE), / aˈɡlaɪ.ə / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, one of the Three Graces, known as the goddess who presides over beauty, splendor, and adornment.
one of the Three Graces in Greek mythology

Examples

According to legend, Aglaia was celebrated for her radiant beauty and kindness.
In ancient art, Aglaia is often depicted alongside her two sisters.
The story of Aglaia has inspired many poets throughout history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Euphrosyne - another of the Three Graces in Greek mythology
  • Synonym: Thalia - another of the Three Graces in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἀγλαΐα (Aglaía), meaning 'splendor, beauty'.
