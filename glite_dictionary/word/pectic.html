pectic

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɛktɪk / (AmE), / ˈpɛktɪk / (BrE)
relating to or derived from pectin, a group of plant polysaccharides important in cell walls and food gelling agents
relating to pectin or its derivatives

Examples

The ripening fruit underwent pectic changes that affected its texture.
Researchers isolated several pectic substances from the apple samples.
The plant's cell wall contains pectic polysaccharides important for structure.

Synset Examples

pectic acid

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pectin - a soluble gelatinous polysaccharide in plant cell walls

Etymology

From pectin + -ic (Greek pektos, 'congealed' or 'curdled')
