viceroyship

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪsˌrɔɪˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈvaɪs.rɔɪ.ʃɪp / (BrE)
the position, rank, or term of office of a viceroy—an official ruling a country or province as the representative of a sovereign
the position of a viceroy

Examples

His appointment to the viceroyship marked a turning point in colonial administration.
During her viceroyship, she implemented significant reforms in the region.
Many historians debate the legacy of Britain's last viceroyship in India.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viceroy - a ruler acting in place of a king or sovereign

Etymology

From 'viceroy' + '-ship', denoting office or position.
