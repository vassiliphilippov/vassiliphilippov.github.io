Equisetales

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːkwɪˈsiːteɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌiːkwɪˈsiːteɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of primitive vascular plants, commonly known as horsetails, that have existed since the Devonian period
order of plants including horsetails

Examples

Fossils show that Equisetales have persisted since the Devonian period.
Many members of the Equisetales order, like horsetails, still grow today.
Paleobotanists study Equisetales to learn about ancient plant evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horsetail - a type of primitive, spore-producing plant in the genus Equisetum

Etymology

From Latin 'equisetum' (horsetail) + '-ales' (order, suffix in plant taxonomy).
