Solresol

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑl.rɛ.soʊl / (AmE), / ˈsɒl.rɛ.sɒl / (BrE)
an artificial language developed in the 19th century, based on the seven notes of the musical scale, with vocabulary formed from combinations of these syllables and able to be communicated via sound, color, gesture, and other modes
a constructed language based on musical notes

Examples

He spent months studying Solresol and learning to communicate through musical notes.
The Solresol language can be spoken, sung, or even signed with hand gestures.
In her presentation, she demonstrated how Solresol uses color to represent words.

Etymology

From the solfège syllables sol-re-sol, reflecting its musical basis; invented by François Sudre in the early 19th century.
