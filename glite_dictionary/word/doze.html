doze

1. NOUN

/ doʊz / (AmE), / dəʊz / (BrE)
a short period of light or fitful sleep, often taken unintentionally
a brief light sleep

Examples

After lunch, she enjoyed a quiet doze in her armchair.
He awoke from his doze to the sound of birds outside the window.
A brief doze is sometimes all you need to feel refreshed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nap - a short sleep, especially during the day

2. VERB

/ doʊz / (AmE), / dəʊz / (BrE)
to sleep lightly or for a short period of time, often unintentionally
sleep lightly; nap briefly

Examples

She began to doze on the couch while watching television.
He would often doze during long car rides.
After lunch, workers sometimes doze at their desks for a few minutes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nap - to sleep for a short time, especially during the day
  • Synonym: slumber - to sleep, especially lightly or peacefully
  • Antonym: awake - not sleeping; to stop sleeping

Etymology

Probably from Dutch 'doezen', meaning to be drowsy.
