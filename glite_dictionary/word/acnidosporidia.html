Acnidosporidia

1. NOUN

/ ækˌnaɪdoʊspəˈrɪdiə / (AmE), / ækˌnɪdəʊspɒˈrɪdɪə / (BrE)
a subclass of sporozoan protozoans distinguished by the absence of polar capsules, primarily parasitic in invertebrates
a subclass of sporozoan protozoans

Examples

Research on Acnidosporidia has advanced our understanding of invertebrate parasites.
The diversity within Acnidosporidia is remarkable, especially among aquatic species.
Scientists debated the evolutionary relationships of Acnidosporidia for many years.

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) + 'knid-' (nettle) + 'spora' (seed, spore) + '-idia' (a suffix for class/subclass).

a subclass of Sporozoa (protozoan parasites)
medical

Examples

Members of the Acnidosporidia subclass are studied in medical parasitology.
