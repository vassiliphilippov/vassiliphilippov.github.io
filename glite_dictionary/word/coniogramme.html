Coniogramme

1. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊni.oʊˈɡræmi / (AmE), / ˌkəʊni.əˈɡræmi / (BrE)
A genus of terrestrial ferns found in Pacific islands and Asia.
genus of ferns in Asia and Pacific islands

Examples

The Coniogramme ferns thrive in shady, moist forest environments across East Asia.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the Coniogramme genus on a remote Pacific island.
Several members of Coniogramme are cultivated in botanical gardens for their distinctive fronds.

Etymology

From Greek 'konis' (dust) and 'gramme' (line), referring to the dust-like spores and lined sori of the fern.
