synclinal

1. ADJ

/ sɪnˈklaɪnəl / (AmE), / sɪnˈklaɪnəl / (BrE)
(of strata or rock layers) sloping downward from opposite directions so as to form a trough or fold.
sloping downward to form a trough (geology)

Examples

The rock layers in this region are synclinal, creating a natural trough between two ridges.
Geologists mapped the synclinal structure to better understand the area's history.
A synclinal fold can trap water, making it significant in hydrogeology.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anticlinal - sloping upward to form an arch or crest (geology)

Etymology

From Greek 'syn-' (together) + 'klinein' (to lean).
