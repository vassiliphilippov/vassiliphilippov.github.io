Dryopteris oreades

1. NOUN

/ draɪˈɒptərɪs ɔːˈriːədiːz / (AmE), / draɪˈɒptərɪs ɔːˈriːədiːz / (BrE)
A species of fern in the genus Dryopteris, commonly known as the mountain male-fern, found in forests and mountain areas.
a species of mountain male-fern

Examples

The dense woodlands were abundant with Dryopteris oreades growing among the rocks.
Botanists identified the specimen as Dryopteris oreades during their mountain survey.
Several conservation programs aim to protect Dryopteris oreades and its unique woodland habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - a nonflowering vascular plant with feathery or leafy fronds

Etymology

From Greek *dryas* (oak nymph) and *pteris* (fern); species epithet from Greek *Oreades* (mountain nymphs), reflecting its mountainous habitat.
