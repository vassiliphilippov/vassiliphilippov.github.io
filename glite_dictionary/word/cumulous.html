cumulous

1. ADJ

/ ˈkjuːmjələs / (AmE), / ˈkjuːmjʊləs / (BrE)
Relating to or resembling cumulus clouds; characterized by heaped, fluffy masses.
relating to cumulus clouds

Examples

The sky was dotted with cumulous clouds, promising fair weather for the afternoon.
A cumulous formation hovered over the mountains, casting soft shadows on the slopes below.
Pilots often watch for cumulous buildups as indicators of changing weather conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cumulus - a type of cloud forming rounded masses

Etymology

From Latin 'cumulus' (heap, mass), used in meteorology for describing certain types of clouds.
