Darmera

1. NOUN

/ dɑːrˈmɛrə / (AmE), / dɑːˈmɪərə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the saxifrage family, known for the species Indian rhubarb (Darmera peltata), native to western North America.
a plant genus including Indian rhubarb

Examples

The large leaves of Darmera provide striking foliage in the garden.
Botanists studied the native habitat of Darmera in California.
Darmera peltata is prized for its pink flower clusters in spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Peltiphyllum - another genus sometimes classified with Darmera

Etymology

Named after Karl Darmer, a 19th-century German horticulturist.

genus of flowering plants

Examples

Darmera thrives in moist, shaded environments near streams and ponds.
