ovenbird

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌv.ənˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈʌv.ənˌbɜːd / (BrE)
A small brownish South American bird of the family Furnariidae, known for building oven-shaped clay nests.
South American bird that builds clay nests

Examples

The ovenbird constructs a remarkable nest that looks like a miniature clay oven.
Several species of ovenbird can be found in Argentina's grasslands.
Researchers were fascinated by the unique architecture of the ovenbird's nest.

Etymology

Named for the bird's habit of building oven-shaped nests; from oven + bird.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌvənˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈʌvənˌbɜːd / (BrE)
a small North American warbler (Seiurus aurocapilla) known for building a dome-shaped nest on the ground
North American warbler with dome-shaped nest

Examples

We spotted an ovenbird in the forest, just as it began singing its distinctive song.
The ovenbird is famous for its cleverly hidden, domed nest built right on the forest floor.
During our hike, a pair of ovenbirds darted through the underbrush near their nest.

Etymology

So named because its nest resembles an old-fashioned oven.
