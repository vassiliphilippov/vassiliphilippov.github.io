moribund

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɔːrɪˌbʌnd / (AmE), / ˈmɒr.ɪ.bʌnd / (BrE)
(of a thing) in a state of stagnation or decline, showing no signs of growth, activity, or vitality.
in decline or lacking vitality

Examples

Many experts agree that the company’s moribund product line urgently needs innovation to survive.
The once bustling town center is now moribund, with empty shops and little foot traffic.
Interest in that traditional art form has become moribund over the past decade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stagnant - not flowing or changing, lacking movement or development
  • Antonym: flourishing - growing or developing in a healthy or vigorous way

Etymology

From Latin moribundus meaning 'dying', from mori (to die).

at the point of death; dying
