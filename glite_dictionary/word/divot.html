divot

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪvət / (AmE), / ˈdɪvət / (BrE)
a small piece of turf that is cut out of the ground, especially by a golf club or an animal's hooves
piece of turf removed from ground

Examples

After his swing, a large divot flew into the air and landed a few feet away.
Golfers are expected to replace their divots after hitting the ball on the fairway.
The horse's hooves left several divots in the soft, wet field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turf - upper layer of soil with grass

Etymology

Probably from Scottish Gaelic "dibh" (piece, portion).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪvət / (AmE), / ˈdɪvət / (BrE)
A small patch of turf that is cut out of the ground, especially by a golf club during a stroke, leaving a cavity.
piece of turf removed by a golf stroke

Examples

After his shot, he carefully replaced the divot he had created on the fairway.
The ball landed awkwardly in a divot, making the next shot more difficult.
Golfers are encouraged to repair any divot marks to keep the course in good condition.

Synset Examples

it was a good drive but the ball ended up in a divot

Etymology

Probably from Scots, of uncertain origin, originally meaning a piece of turf cut out of the ground.
