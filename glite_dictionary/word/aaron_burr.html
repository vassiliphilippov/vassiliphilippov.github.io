Aaron Burr

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛrən bɜr / (AmE), / ˈærən bɜː / (BrE)
(1756–1836) United States politician who served as vice president under Thomas Jefferson and fatally wounded Alexander Hamilton in a duel.
U.S. vice president, dueled Hamilton

Examples

Many historians debate whether Aaron Burr intended to kill Alexander Hamilton during their infamous duel.
Aaron Burr served as the third vice president of the United States, under President Thomas Jefferson.
The duel between Aaron Burr and Hamilton became a symbol of early American political conflicts.
