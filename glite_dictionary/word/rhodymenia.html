Rhodymenia

1. NOUN

/ roʊˌdɪˈmiːniə / (AmE), / rəʊˌdɪˈmiːniə / (BrE)
A genus of red algae that is the type genus of the family Rhodymeniaceae.
genus of red algae

Examples

Rhodymenia is commonly found along temperate and tropical coastlines around the world.
Marine biologists study the life cycles of Rhodymenia to understand seaweed ecosystems.
The fronds of Rhodymenia are often harvested for use in food and industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alga - a simple, typically aquatic plant, especially one of red, green, or brown types
  • Synonym: Rhodymeniaceae - the botanical family to which Rhodymenia belongs

Etymology

From Greek 'rhódon' (rose) and 'mênis' (moon), referring to the appearance of its fronds.
