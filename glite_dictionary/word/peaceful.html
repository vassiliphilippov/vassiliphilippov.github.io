peaceful

1. ADJ

/ ˈpisfəl / (AmE), / ˈpiːsf(ə)l / (BrE)
Free from disturbance, strife, or war; calm and tranquil.
free from war or disturbance

Examples

The citizens were grateful for the peaceful period after years of conflict.
She enjoyed a peaceful walk in the quiet countryside.
The leaders signed an agreement to ensure a peaceful future for both nations.

Synset Examples

a peaceful nation
peaceful times
a far from peaceful Christmas
peaceful sleep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calm - free from noise or agitation
  • Synonym: tranquil - free from disturbance or turmoil
  • Antonym: violent - involving physical force to harm or damage

Etymology

From peace + -ful, first recorded in the 16th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈpisfəl / (AmE), / ˈpiːsfəl / (BrE)
Not involving violence or aggression, especially when responding to injustice; advocating or practicing nonviolent resistance.
nonviolent, not using force

Examples

The students staged a peaceful protest in the city square, carrying banners but refusing to shout or confront police.
His response to unfair treatment was always peaceful, believing that violence would only worsen the situation.
Nations around the world praised the leader for his peaceful approach to conflict resolution.

Synset Examples

passive resistance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonviolent - not using physical force
  • Antonym: aggressive - inclined to attack or confront

Etymology

From peace + -ful, dating to Middle English; 'peace' from Old French 'pais', from Latin 'pax'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02523159-s
Synset POS: s
