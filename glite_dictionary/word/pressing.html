pressing

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprɛsɪŋ / (BrE)
a metal or plastic part formed by being pressed into shape in a mechanical press
a part made by pressing in manufacturing

Examples

The factory produces thousands of pressings for the automotive industry every day.
Each pressing is inspected for quality before being assembled into the final product.
They ordered a custom pressing to fit the unique design of their machinery.

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɛsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprɛsɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of applying pressure to something, especially by pushing or squeezing
the act of applying pressure

Examples

At the pressing of a button, the machine started to operate.
The pressing of grapes is an important step in winemaking.
A gentle pressing helped stop the bleeding from the wound.

Synset Examples

he gave the button a press
he used pressure to stop the bleeding
at the pressing of a button

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pressure - the continuous physical force exerted on or against an object
  • Antonym: release - the act of letting go or removing pressure

Etymology

From the verb 'press,' which derives from Old French 'presser,' from Latin 'pressare,' frequentative of 'premere' (to press).

3. ADJ

/ ˈprɛsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprɛsɪŋ / (BrE)
requiring immediate attention or action; urgent.
requiring urgent attention

Examples

The most pressing issue facing the committee is the budget shortfall.
Firefighters responded quickly to the pressing need for assistance after the storm.
We must address these pressing concerns before they escalate further.

Synset Examples

too pressing to permit of longer delay
the urgent words ‘Hurry! Hurry!’
bridges in urgent need of repair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urgent - requiring swift action or attention
  • Synonym: immediate - occurring or done at once; instant
  • Antonym: trivial - of little value or importance, not urgent

Etymology

From the verb 'press' (Middle English, from Old French *presser*, from Latin *pressare* 'to press').
