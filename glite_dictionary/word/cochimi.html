Cochimi

1. NOUN

/ koʊ.tʃiːˈmiː / (AmE), / kəʊ.tʃɪˈmiː / (BrE)
A member of an Indigenous people formerly living in central Baja California, Mexico.
member of an Indigenous group from Baja California

Examples

The Cochimi once inhabited large areas of central Baja California.
Anthropologists have studied the traditions and language of the Cochimi.
Today, descendants of the Cochimi people work to preserve their ancestral heritage.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Cochimí', of unknown origin.

2. NOUN

/ koʊ.tʃiːˈmiː / (AmE), / kəʊ.tʃɪˈmiː / (BrE)
an extinct Yuman language formerly spoken by the Cochimi people of Baja California, Mexico
extinct Yuman language of Baja California

Examples

Scholars have studied Cochimi texts to learn more about early Yuman languages.
Very few speakers of Cochimi survived into the twentieth century.
Linguists classify Cochimi as a distinct branch within the Yuman language family.

Etymology

From the name of the Cochimi people of Baja California, Mexico.
