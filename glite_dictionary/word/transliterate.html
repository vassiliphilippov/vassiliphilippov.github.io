to transliterate

1. VERB

/ trænsˈlɪtəˌreɪt / (AmE), / trænzˈlɪtəreɪt / (BrE)
to write or represent words or letters in the characters of another alphabet or script
write in a different script

Examples

Many scholars transliterate ancient Greek texts into the Latin alphabet for easier study.
The Japanese word was transliterated using English letters for the brochure.
It can be challenging to transliterate Arabic sounds accurately into the Roman script.

Synset Examples

The Sanskrit text had to be transliterated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to transcribe - to write out or copy (spoken or written text), often in the same script

Etymology

From Latin transliterare (to transfer letters), based on trans- (across) + littera (letter).
