Andalusia

1. PROPN

/ ˌændəˈluːziə / (AmE), / ˌandəˈluːziə / (BrE)
a region in southern Spain, known for its rich history, especially as a center of Moorish culture, and its location on both the Atlantic Ocean and the Mediterranean Sea
region in southern Spain

Examples

She spent her summer vacation exploring the cultural heritage of Andalusia.
The architecture of Andalusia reflects its diverse historical influences.
Flamenco music is deeply rooted in the traditions of Andalusia.

Etymology

From Spanish Andalucía, from Arabic al-Andalus (Muslim-ruled territory in Spain).
