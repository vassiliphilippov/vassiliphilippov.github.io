variola vaccina

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɛəriˈoʊlə vəˈsaɪnə / (AmE), / ˌvarɪˈəʊlə vakˈsiːnə / (BrE)
an infection produced in humans by inoculation with the cowpox virus, formerly used to confer immunity to smallpox; typically lasts about three weeks and often leaves a pitted scar
infection from cowpox virus used for smallpox immunity
medical

Examples

The practice of inoculating with variola vaccina helped eradicate smallpox worldwide.
After receiving variola vaccina, patients often developed a characteristic scar at the injection site.
In the 19th century, variola vaccina was a breakthrough in public health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vaccination - the act of introducing vaccine to provide immunity against disease
  • Synonym: cowpox - a mild viral disease in cows, used for smallpox vaccination
  • Antonym: smallpox - a serious, contagious disease eradicated by vaccination

Etymology

From Latin 'variola' (smallpox) and 'vaccina' (from cows), referencing the use of cowpox in vaccination.
