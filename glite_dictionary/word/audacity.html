audacity

1. NOUN

/ ɔˈdæsəti / (AmE), / ɔːˈdæsəti / (BrE)
the willingness to take bold risks; fearless daring or courage
fearless daring; boldness

Examples

She had the audacity to speak out against the board's decision.
His audacity impressed the recruiters during the high-risk operation.
It took a lot of audacity to start a business in such uncertain times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boldness - willingness to take risks; courage
  • Antonym: timidity - lack of courage or confidence; shyness

Etymology

From Latin audacitas, from audax (bold, daring), from audere (to dare).

2. NOUN

/ ɔˈdæsəti / (AmE), / ɔˈdæsəti / (BrE)
a willingness to take bold risks, often showing a disregard for conventional rules or respect; daring or impudence
bold or daring behavior; impudence

Examples

She had the audacity to ask for a raise after only one week on the job.
His audacity shocked everyone at the meeting when he spoke out against the director.
I admired her audacity in challenging the established rules so openly.

Synset Examples

he had the audacity to question my decision

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boldness - willingness to take risks; confidence
  • Antonym: timidity - lack of courage or confidence; shyness

Etymology

From Latin 'audacitas' (boldness), from 'audax' (bold, daring).
