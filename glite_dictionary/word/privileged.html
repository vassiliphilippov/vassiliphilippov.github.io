privileged

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɪvəlɪdʒd / (AmE), / ˈprɪvɪlɪdʒd / (BrE)
(of information, communication, or actions) not subject to usual rules or penalties, especially protected from disclosure or legal repercussions.
not subject to usual rules or penalties

Examples

Lawyers are often careful to keep privileged communications with their clients confidential.
The judge ruled that the document was privileged and could not be admitted as evidence.
As a journalist, she could not be forced to reveal her privileged sources in court.

Synset Examples

a privileged statement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protected - kept safe from harm or unauthorized access
  • Synonym: confidential - meant to be kept private or secret
  • Antonym: unprotected - not shielded from harm or disclosure
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02374646-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈprɪvəlɪdʒd / (AmE), / ˈprɪvəlɪdʒd / (BrE)
Having special rights, advantages, or immunities not enjoyed by others.
having special rights or advantages

Examples

She grew up in a privileged family and had access to the best education.
Only a privileged few were allowed to enter the exclusive club.
His privileged background gave him opportunities that others did not have.

Synset Examples

the privileged few

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disadvantaged - lacking privileges or basic advantages
  • Synonym: entitled - having a right to something
  • Degree Lower: fortunate - receiving good things, often undeserved or by chance

Etymology

From privilege, via Old French priviligié, from Latin privilegium (law affecting one person).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01870073-a

3. ADJ

/ ˈprɪv.ə.lɪdʒd / (AmE), / ˈprɪv.ɪ.lɪdʒd / (BrE)
Restricted to a particular group; available or accessible only to a select or exclusive group of people.
restricted to an exclusive group

Examples

Only a privileged few had access to the classified government documents.
He was not privileged to attend the secret meeting.
The report contained privileged information not meant for public release.

Synset Examples

privy to inner knowledge
inside information
privileged information

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confidential - meant to be kept secret or private
  • Antonym: public - open or accessible to all people

Etymology

From Latin 'privilegium' (law applying to one person), via Old French and Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01869016-s
Synset POS: s
