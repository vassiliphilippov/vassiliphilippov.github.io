filtrate

1. VERB

/ ˈfɪltreɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɪltreɪt / (BrE)
to remove or separate substances by passing them through a filter
remove or separate by filtering

Examples

Modern water treatment plants filtrate contaminants to ensure safe drinking water.
The laboratory technician needs to filtrate the solution before further analysis.
After the reaction, scientists filtrated the mixture to separate the solid from the liquid.

Synset Examples

filter out the impurities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filter - to remove unwanted substances from a liquid, gas, etc., by using a filter

Etymology

From Medieval Latin filtratus, past participle of filtrare (to filter).

WordNet ID: oewn-01461293-v

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪl.treɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɪl.treɪt / (BrE)
a liquid or gas that has passed through a filter, especially as the product of a filtration process
liquid or gas passed through a filter

Examples

After the solution was poured through the paper, the clear filtrate was collected in a beaker.
The filtrate must be examined to ensure all impurities are removed.
In wastewater treatment, the filtrate is often further purified before release.

  • Synonym: filter - a device or material that removes impurities from a substance by passing it through

From Medieval Latin filtratus, past participle of filtrare (to filter).
