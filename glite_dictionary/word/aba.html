aba

1. NOUN

/ əˈbɑ / (AmE), / ˈɑːbə / (BrE)
A loose, sleeveless outer garment made from aba cloth, traditionally worn by Arabs.
loose outer garment worn by Arabs

Examples

The merchant wore a flowing aba as he crossed the bustling market square.
Her father draped his aba over his shoulders to shield himself from the harsh desert sun.
You can often see elders in the village dressed in a traditional aba during special ceremonies.

Etymology

From Arabic 'ʿabā', meaning cloak or mantle.

2. NOUN

/ əˈbɑ / (AmE), / ˈɑːbə / (BrE)
A coarse fabric woven from goat hair or camel hair, traditionally used in Middle Eastern garments.
fabric of goat or camel hair

Examples

The traditional Bedouin cloak is made from thick aba to protect against harsh desert conditions.
Textiles woven from aba are valued for their durability and unique texture.
He purchased a beautifully dyed aba during his travels through the Middle East.

Etymology

From Arabic ʿabāʾ (cloak, garment).
