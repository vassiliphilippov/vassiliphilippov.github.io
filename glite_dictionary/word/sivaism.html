Sivaism

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃiːvɑˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈʃiːvəɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A branch of Hinduism devoted to the worship of Shiva as the supreme deity.
branch of Hinduism focused on Shiva

Examples

Many temples in southern India are dedicated to Sivaism and its rituals.
The principles of Sivaism emphasize devotion to Shiva as the highest god.
He studied the influence of Sivaism on Indian art and culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shaivism - worship of Shiva, alternate spelling

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Śaiva' (relating to Shiva) + English suffix '-ism'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsiːvaɪˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsiːvəɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The Hindu sect whose members worship Shiva as the supreme god.
Hindu sect devoted to Shiva

Examples

Sivaism emphasizes the worship of Shiva as the supreme being in Hindu tradition.
Many temples in South India are centers of Sivaism and attract thousands of devotees.
The philosophy and rituals of Sivaism differ from other major Hindu sects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shaivism - The worship of Shiva as a principal deity, variant spelling

Etymology

From Shiva + -ism, denoting belief in or worship of Shiva.

Synset POS: n
