infamous

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪn.fə.məs / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fə.məs / (BrE)
well known for being bad or for having done something shameful or wicked.
well known for a bad reason

Examples

The infamous criminal was finally brought to justice after years on the run.
The city is infamous for its poor air quality and heavy traffic jams.
His infamous betrayal left him shunned by friends and family alike.

Synset Examples

a notorious gangster
the tenderloin district was notorious for vice
the infamous Benedict Arnold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notorious - well known for some bad quality or deed
  • Antonym: famous - well known by many people, usually in a positive way

Etymology

From Latin *infamis* (of ill fame), from *in-* (not) + *fama* (fame).
