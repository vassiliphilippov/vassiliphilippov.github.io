Geum canadense

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒi əm kəˈnædənˌs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː əm kəˈnædənsiː / (BrE)
A species of North American avens with white flowers, commonly found in woodlands and meadows.
North American white-flowered avens

Examples

The woodland floor was dotted with clusters of Geum canadense in full bloom.
She studied the pollination habits of Geum canadense for her thesis.
The delicate white petals of Geum canadense stood out amongst the ferns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avens - any plant of the genus Geum

Etymology

From Latin: Genus name 'Geum' and species 'canadense', meaning 'of Canada'.
