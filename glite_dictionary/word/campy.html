campy

1. ADJ

/ ˈkæmpi / (AmE), / ˈkampi / (BrE)
(of art, entertainment, or style) deliberately exaggerated and theatrical, often so as to provide sophisticated amusement through its artificiality, vulgarity, or sentimentality.
deliberately exaggerated and theatrical; amusingly artificial

Examples

The movie was intentionally campy, full of over-the-top costumes and melodramatic dialogue.
Her campy performance in the play had the audience roaring with laughter.
They decorated the house in a campy retro style for the party.

Synset Examples

they played up the silliness of their roles for camp effect
campy Hollywood musicals of the 1940's

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camp - an aesthetic style that perceives bad taste as ironic or sophisticated amusement

Etymology

From 'camp' in the sense of exaggerated style or behavior, popularized in the early 20th century.
