bigamy

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪɡəmi / (AmE), / ˈbɪɡəmi / (BrE)
the crime of marrying one person while still legally married to another
marriage to two people at once (crime)
legal

Examples

He was charged with bigamy after investigators discovered his second marriage.
Bigamy is considered a criminal offense in many countries.
The court annulled both marriages on the grounds of bigamy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polygamy - the practice of having more than one spouse at the same time
  • Antonym: monogamy - the practice of being married to only one person at a time

Etymology

From Latin 'bigamia', from Greek 'bigamos', meaning 'twice married'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɪɡəmi / (AmE), / ˈbɪɡəmi / (BrE)
the criminal offense of marrying someone while already legally married to another person
offense of marrying while already married
legal

Examples

In some countries, bigamy is punishable by imprisonment.
He was charged with bigamy after marrying for a second time without legally divorcing his first wife.
The court heard evidence relating to her alleged bigamy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polygamy - the practice of having more than one spouse at the same time
  • Antonym: monogamy - the state of being married to only one person at a time

Etymology

From Middle French 'bigamie', from Late Latin 'bigamia', from Greek 'digamos' (twice married).
