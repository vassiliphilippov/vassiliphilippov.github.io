arteriosclerotic

1. ADJ

/ ɑːrˌtɪri.oʊ.skəˈrɑː.tɪk / (AmE), / ɑːˌtɪəri.əʊ.skləˈrɒt.ɪk / (BrE)
Affected by or relating to arteriosclerosis, a condition in which the walls of the arteries become thickened and lose elasticity.
affected by arteriosclerosis
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with an arteriosclerotic condition following a medical examination.
Advanced age increases the risk of arteriosclerotic changes in the circulatory system.
His arteriosclerotic arteries required careful management by his healthcare provider.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arteriosclerosis - thickening and hardening of arterial walls

Etymology

From arteriosclerosis + -otic, indicating a condition or relation.
