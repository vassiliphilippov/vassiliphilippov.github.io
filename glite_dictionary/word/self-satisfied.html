self-satisfied

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlfˈsætɪsfaɪd / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈsætɪsfaɪd / (BrE)
Feeling or showing excessive satisfaction with oneself or one's achievements, often in a way that others find annoying.
excessively pleased with oneself

Examples

She gave a self-satisfied smile after winning the award.
His self-satisfied attitude irritated his colleagues at the meeting.
Don't be so self-satisfied; there's always room for improvement.

Synset Examples

a smug glow of self-congratulation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smug - having or showing an excessive pride in oneself or one's achievements
  • Antonym: modest - having a humble or moderate view of one's own abilities or achievements

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'satisfied,' meaning satisfied with oneself.

2. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlfˈsætɪsˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈsætɪsfaɪd / (BrE)
Feeling or showing excessive satisfaction with oneself or one's achievements, often with a lack of awareness of possible faults or shortcomings.
excessively pleased with oneself

Examples

After receiving so much praise, he wore a self-satisfied smile all day.
Her self-satisfied attitude made it hard for others to offer constructive criticism.
The manager's self-satisfied remarks annoyed his hardworking team.

Synset Examples

he had become complacent after years of success
his self-satisfied dignity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complacent - uncritically satisfied with oneself or one's situation
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest opinion of oneself

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'satisfied', first recorded in the 18th century.

Source: WordNet
