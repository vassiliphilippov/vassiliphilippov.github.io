milliroentgen

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪliˈroʊntdʒən / (AmE), / ˌmɪlɪˈrəʊntdʒən / (BrE)
a unit of exposure to ionizing radiation equal to one thousandth of a roentgen
unit of radiation exposure
medical

Examples

The patient's exposure during the procedure was kept below 50 milliroentgens to minimize risk.
Annual background radiation can vary from 100 to 300 milliroentgens depending on location.
A reading of just a few milliroentgens triggered the safety alarm in the laboratory.

Synset Examples

The typical exposure to normal background radiation for a human being is about 200 milliroentgens per year, or about 23 microroentgens per hour.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: roentgen - unit of ionizing radiation exposure

Etymology

From 'milli-' meaning 'one thousandth' and 'roentgen', after German physicist Wilhelm Röntgen.
