Peltandra virginica

1. NOUN

/ pɛlˈtændrə vɜrˈdʒɪnɪkə / (AmE), / pɛlˈtændrə vɜːˈdʒɪnɪkə / (BrE)
A perennial herb native to eastern North America, with arrowhead-shaped leaves, a long pointed spathe, and green berries.
a perennial herb of eastern North America

Examples

Peltandra virginica is commonly found in wetlands and along the banks of slow-moving streams.
Botanists identified the rare specimen as Peltandra virginica during their survey of the marsh.
The berries of Peltandra virginica turn green as they mature in late summer.

Etymology

Peltandra: from Greek 'peltē' (small shield) and '-andra' (male), 'virginica': referring to Virginia, USA.
