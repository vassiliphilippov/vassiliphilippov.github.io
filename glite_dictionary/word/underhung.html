underhung

1. ADJ

/ ˈʌndərˌhʌŋ / (AmE), / ˈʌndəhʌŋ / (BrE)
(of a structure or object) supported from below, especially resting on a track rather than being suspended from above.
supported from below; resting on a track

Examples

The engineers installed underhung cranes in the new warehouse for improved safety.
The cabinet doors use an underhung mechanism that lets them slide smoothly along the bottom track.
Many modern closets feature underhung sliding doors to save space.

Synset Examples

underhung sliding doors

2. ADJ

/ ˈʌndɚˌhʌŋ / (AmE), / ˈʌndəhʌŋ / (BrE)
having the lower part projecting beyond the upper part, especially used to describe jaws or similar structures
with a lower part jutting out

Examples

The bulldog's underhung jaw is a distinctive feature of the breed.
She had an underhung lower lip that gave her face a unique profile.
Some machinery has an underhung design for stability and support.

Synset Examples

an underhung jaw
undershot bulldog

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overshot - having the upper part projecting beyond the lower part (e.g., an overshot jaw)
