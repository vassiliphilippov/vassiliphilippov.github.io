Primula

1. PROPN

/ ˈprɪmjələ / (AmE), / ˈprɪmjʊlə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in temperate Europe and Asia, known for their bright and showy flowers, including primroses and related species.
genus of flowering plants (primroses, etc.)

Examples

The Primula genus includes many varieties that bloom in early spring.
Gardeners often plant Primula for their vibrant colors and hardiness.
Botanists study Primula species to understand plant adaptation to temperate climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primrose - a common flower species within Primula

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'primula', diminutive of Latin 'prima' (first), referring to the early blooming of some species.
