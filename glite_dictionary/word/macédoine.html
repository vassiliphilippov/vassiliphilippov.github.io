macédoine

1. NOUN

/ mæsəˈdwæn, ˌmæsəˈdwɑn / (AmE), / ˌmas.eɪˈdwɑːn, ˌmas.eɪˈdwæn / (BrE)
A dish consisting of a mixture of diced fruits or vegetables, served hot or cold.
mixture of diced fruits or vegetables

Examples

For dessert, she served a refreshing macédoine of fresh summer fruits.
The chef prepared a vegetable macédoine as a starter for the dinner party.
You can use canned or fresh ingredients when making a fruit macédoine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salad - a mixed dish of raw or cooked vegetables

Etymology

From French 'macédoine', originally referring to the ethnic mixture in the Macedonian region.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmæ.səˈdwæn / (AmE), / ˌmas.əˈdwän / (BrE)
A mixture of small, evenly cut cubes of fruits or vegetables, often served as a salad.
mixture of cubed fruits or vegetables

Examples

The chef prepared a colorful macédoine to accompany the main course.
A macédoine of vegetables was served chilled as an appetizer.
She garnished the dish with a fresh macédoine of seasonal fruits.

Synset Examples

The salad featured vibrant macédoines of fruits and vegetables.
Her chef's specialty is a refreshing macédoines of tropical fruits.

Etymology

French, from Macédoine, referring to the ethnic diversity of Macedonia; used in cookery since the 18th century.
