to lessen

1. VERB

/ ˈlɛs.ən / (AmE), / ˈlɛs.ən / (BrE)
to make or become smaller in amount, degree, or intensity
make or become smaller

Examples

New regulations are intended to lessen the environmental impact of factories.
The pain began to lessen after she took the medication.
Nothing seemed to lessen his anxiety before the exam.

Synset Examples

He decreased his staff

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to diminish - to make or become less
  • Antonym: increase - to become or make greater in size, amount, or degree

Etymology

From Old French lessenir, from less (less) + -en (verb-forming suffix).

2. VERB

/ ˈlɛs.ən / (AmE), / ˈlɛs.ən / (BrE)
to make or become less in size, amount, degree, or intensity
make or become less

Examples

Taking deep breaths helped to lessen her anxiety before the exam.
The government introduced new measures to lessen air pollution in the city.
After the rain stopped, the floodwaters gradually lessened and the streets became passable.

Synset Examples

The amount of homework decreased towards the end of the semester
The cabin pressure fell dramatically
her weight fell to under a hundred pounds
his voice fell to a whisper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to diminish - to make or become less
  • Synonym: to reduce - to make something smaller or less in amount
  • Antonym: increase - to become or make greater in size, amount, or degree

Etymology

From Old French 'lesse(er)' (to make less), from Latin 'minuare' (to make smaller).

3. VERB

/ ˈlɛs.ən / (AmE), / ˈlɛs.ən / (BrE)
to become less strong, intense, or severe; to decrease in degree or amount
become less intense or strong

Examples

Over time, the swelling will lessen as your body heals.
Her fear did not lessen after hearing the news.
The pain finally began to lessen after I took some medicine.

Synset Examples

The pain subsided

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to diminish - to become or make less
  • Synonym: decrease - to become smaller or less in size, amount, or degree
  • Antonym: increase - to become greater in size, amount, or intensity

Etymology

From Old French 'lessener', from 'moins' (less), from Latin 'minus'.
