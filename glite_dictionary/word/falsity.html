falsity

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔl.sɪ.ti / (AmE), / ˈfɔːl.sɪ.ti / (BrE)
A statement or assertion that is untrue; the quality of being false.
a false statement; untruth

Examples

The witness was accused of falsity in his testimony.
No amount of repetition can turn a falsity into the truth.
They exposed the falsity of the company's claims during the investigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: falsehood - an untrue statement or the state of being untrue
  • Antonym: truth - the quality or state of being true

Etymology

From Latin falsitas (falseness, deceit), from falsus (false).

the quality or state of being false or untrue; lack of truth or accuracy
the state of being false; untruth

Examples

The witness's statement was proved to be a falsity during cross-examination.
She recognized the falsity of the rumors circulating about her.
Philosophers debate the falsity or truth of certain beliefs.

Synset Examples

argument could not determine its truth or falsity

Related Terms

  • Antonym: truth - the quality or state of being true
