metropolitan

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɛtrəˈpɑlətən / (AmE), / ˌmɛtrəˈpɒlɪtən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of a large city or metropolis, especially in terms of culture, population, or influence.
of or relating to a large city

Examples

The metropolitan lifestyle is fast-paced and diverse.
Many young professionals are drawn to metropolitan areas for better job opportunities.
The museum is a highlight of the metropolitan culture in the city.

Synset Examples

metropolitan area

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urban - relating to a city or town

Etymology

From Latin 'metropolitanus', from Greek 'metropolis' (mother city).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtrəˈpɑlətən / (AmE), / ˌmɛtrəˈpɒlɪtən / (BrE)
a bishop of higher rank in some Eastern Christian churches, ranking below a patriarch and often corresponding to an archbishop in Western Christianity
high-ranking bishop in Eastern Christianity

Examples

The newly elected metropolitan presided over the ceremony in the cathedral.
As the metropolitan of the region, he held authority over several bishops.
The Orthodox synod gathered to appoint a new metropolitan for the province.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archbishop - a chief bishop in the Western Christian tradition, overseeing an archdiocese

Etymology

From Late Latin 'metropolitanus', from Greek 'metropolitēs' (citizen of a mother city), referring to a chief bishop in a metropolis.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10332897-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌmɛtrəˈpɑlɪtən / (AmE), / ˌmɛtrəˈpɒlɪtən / (BrE)
a person who lives in, or comes from, a large city or metropolis, especially one familiar with urban life
a city dweller; city resident

Examples

As a metropolitan, she thrived on the fast pace and endless activities of New York.
The metropolitan enjoyed exploring trendy restaurants and galleries in the heart of the city.
Unlike his rural relatives, the metropolitan was used to noisy streets and crowded subways.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urbanite - a person who lives in a city
  • Antonym: provincial - a person from a province, often implying someone from outside the metropolis

Etymology

From Latin 'metropolitanus', from Greek 'mētropolitēs' (inhabitant of a metropolis).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
