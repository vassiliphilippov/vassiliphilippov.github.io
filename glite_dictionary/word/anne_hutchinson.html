Anne Hutchinson

1. PROPN

/ æn ˈhʌtʃɪnsən / (AmE), / æn ˈhʌtʃɪnsən / (BrE)
(1591–1643) American colonist and religious leader born in England, known for her role in the Antinomian Controversy and banishment from the Massachusetts Bay Colony for her religious beliefs.
American religious dissenter and colonist

Examples

Anne Hutchinson was a prominent figure in early American history, challenging the religious authorities of her time.
The trial of Anne Hutchinson highlighted tensions over religious freedom in colonial Massachusetts.
Historians credit Anne Hutchinson with contributing to the development of religious tolerance in America.
