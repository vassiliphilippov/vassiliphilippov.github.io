endoderm

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛndoʊˌdɜrm / (AmE), / ˈɛndəʊˌdɜːm / (BrE)
the innermost of the three primary germ layers in an embryo, which develops into the lining of the digestive and respiratory systems
innermost embryonic germ layer
medical

Examples

During embryonic development, the endoderm forms the lining of the gut and lungs.
The liver and pancreas originate from the endoderm layer of the embryo.
Scientists studied how the endoderm differentiates into various organs.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: mesoderm - the middle germ layer in an embryo
  • Degree Higher: ectoderm - the outer germ layer in an embryo

Etymology

From Greek 'endon' (within) + 'derma' (skin).
