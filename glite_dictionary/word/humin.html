humin

1. NOUN

/ ˈhjuːmɪn / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmɪn / (BrE)
a black organic substance found in soil, formed from decomposed plant material and not soluble in water
insoluble black organic soil substance

Examples

The scientist extracted humin from the soil sample for further analysis.
Because humin is not soluble in water, it remains as residue after other substances are washed away.
Researchers study humin to better understand soil organic matter composition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humic acid - a group of organic acids found in humus, soluble in alkali

Etymology

From Latin 'humus' meaning 'earth' or 'ground'.
