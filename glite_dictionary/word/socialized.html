socialized

1. ADJ

/ ˈsoʊ.ʃə.laɪzd / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ.ʃə.laɪzd / (BrE)
(of a service, program, or institution) placed under group or government ownership or control for the collective benefit of society.
under group or government control

Examples

The country introduced socialized medicine to ensure healthcare for all citizens.
Many economists debate the efficiency of socialized industries versus private ones.
Some Americans worry that a socialized system would limit patient choice.

Synset Examples

socialized ownership
socialized medicine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nationalized - placed under the control or ownership of the national government

Etymology

From 'socialize' + '-ed', with 'socialize' from French 'socialiser', ultimately from Latin 'socialis' (of companionship).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsoʊʃəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəlaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a service, industry, etc.) brought under the control or ownership of a group or the government, especially for public benefit.
under government or group control

Examples

The country introduced socialized medicine to provide health care for all citizens.
Debate continues over whether energy should be socialized or remain privately owned.
Some view socialized industries as essential for promoting equality and public welfare.

Synset Examples

socialized ownership
socialized medicine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nationalized - (of an industry or asset) transferred from private to state ownership or control
  • Antonym: privatized - (of a service or industry) transferred from public to private ownership or control

Etymology

From the verb 'socialize', based on 'social' + -ize; sense developed in sociopolitical discourse.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00578774-s
Synset POS: s
