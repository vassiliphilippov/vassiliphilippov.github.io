to discolor

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈkʌlər / (AmE), / dɪsˈkʌlə / (BrE)
to change the color of something, often making it less attractive
to change color, often undesirably

Examples

If you wash bright fabrics in hot water, they may discolor.
The sun can discolor artwork left in direct light for too long.
Some cleaning chemicals tend to discolor bathroom tiles over time.

Synset Examples

The shirts discolored

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stain - to mark with discoloration
  • Antonym: bleach - to make white or lighter in color

Etymology

From Latin 'discolor', meaning of different color, from 'dis-' (apart) + 'color' (color).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00281861-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ dɪsˈkʌlɚ / (AmE), / dɪsˈkʌlə / (BrE)
to cause something to lose its original color or to change to an unwanted or different color
cause to lose or change color

Examples

The detergent discolored my shirts after just one wash.
Exposure to sunlight can discolor photographs over time.
Improper cleaning products may discolor the surface of your kitchen counters.

Synset Examples

The detergent discolored my shirts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stain - to mark or change the color of something, often leaving it dirty or less attractive
  • Degree Lower: bleach - to make something white or lighter in color, often by chemical means

Etymology

From Old French descolorer, from Latin discolorare ('to make of another color') from dis- ('apart') + colorare ('to color').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00282836-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ dɪsˈkʌlər / (AmE), / dɪsˈkʌlə / (BrE)
to lose color, or to make something lose its original color and become faded or stained
to lose or change color

Examples

Exposure to sunlight will eventually discolor the fabric of the sofa.
If you leave the apple out, it will begin to discolor and turn brown.
The chemicals can easily discolor your clothes if you're not careful.

Synset Examples

The painting discolored

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fade - to lose brightness or color gradually
  • Synonym: bleach - to make white or colorless by removing color
  • Antonym: stain - to mark or change the color of something, especially in a way that cannot be easily removed

Etymology

From Latin 'discolor' (of another color), from 'dis-' (apart, away) + 'color' (color).
