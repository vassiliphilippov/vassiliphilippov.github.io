Podocarpus latifolius

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊdəˈkɑrpəs ˌlætɪˈfoʊliəs / (AmE), / ˌpɒdəʊˈkɑːpəs ˌlætɪˈfəʊliəs / (BrE)
an erect or shrubby evergreen tree of Africa with ridged dark grey bark and rigid, glossy, medium to long leaves; also known as the real yellowwood
a southern African evergreen tree (real yellowwood)

Examples

The towering Podocarpus latifolius can live for centuries in the mountain forests of South Africa.
Botanists study the leaf structure of Podocarpus latifolius to better understand its adaptation to local climates.
The furniture was made from the durable wood of Podocarpus latifolius, valued for its quality and beauty.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus name Podocarpus, from Greek 'pous, podos' (foot) + 'karpos' (fruit); species epithet 'latifolius' from Latin 'latus' (broad) + 'folium' (leaf).
