Babel

1. PROPN

/ ˈbeɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈbeɪbl / (BrE)
the tower mentioned in the Book of Genesis which Noah's descendants attempted to build to reach heaven, but whose construction was halted when God confused their language
biblical tower, site of language confusion

Examples

The story of Babel explains why there are so many different languages in the world.
Many artists have depicted the Tower of Babel in their works throughout history.
The legend of Babel serves as a cautionary tale about human pride.

Etymology

From Hebrew *Bāḇel*, via Greek *Babylōn*, referring to Babylon.
