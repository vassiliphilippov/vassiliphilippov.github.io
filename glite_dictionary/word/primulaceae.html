Primulaceae

1. NOUN

/ praɪˌmjuːləˈsiːiː / (AmE), / prɪˌmjuːləˈsiːɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, commonly known as the primrose family, with regular flowers and wide distribution in the Northern Hemisphere
primrose plant family

Examples

Many common garden plants belong to the Primulaceae family.
The Primulaceae are particularly diverse in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
Botanists reclassified several species into and out of Primulaceae based on genetic evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Primula - the type genus of the Primulaceae family, including primroses

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Primula and the family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpraɪmjəˈleɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌprɪmjʊˈleɪsɪiː / (BrE)
a family of dicotyledonous flowering plants with regular, usually five-parted flowers, widely distributed in the Northern Hemisphere; commonly known as the primrose family
family of flowering plants (primrose family)

Examples

Many spring wildflowers in Europe belong to the Primulaceae family.
Botanists are studying the genetic diversity within the Primulaceae.
The Primulaceae include well-known plants such as primroses and cyclamens.

Etymology

From the genus name Primula, the type genus of the family, from Latin 'primus' (first), referring to early flowering.
