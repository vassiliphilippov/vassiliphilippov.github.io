moderationist

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːdəˈreɪʃənɪst / (AmE), / ˌmɒdəˈreɪʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who drinks alcoholic beverages in moderation, as opposed to someone who abstains completely
person who drinks alcohol in moderation

Examples

As a moderationist, he enjoyed an occasional glass of wine with dinner but never overindulged.
The club welcomes both moderationists and teetotalers to its social events.
She considered herself a moderationist, believing that responsible enjoyment of alcohol was entirely possible.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abstainer - a person who refrains from drinking alcohol

Etymology

From 'moderation' + '-ist' (suffix denoting a person who practices or is concerned with something).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːdəˈreɪʃənɪst / (AmE), / ˌmɒdəˈreɪʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who holds moderate or centrist political views, avoiding extremes
person with centrist political views

Examples

As a moderationist, she supported policies that balanced progressive and conservative values.
The party attracted many moderationists who were dissatisfied with the extremes on both sides.
His moderationist stance made him a popular compromise candidate among undecided voters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: centrist - a person whose political opinions are not extreme, falling in the center

Etymology

From 'moderation' + '-ist', denoting one who advocates or practices moderation.
