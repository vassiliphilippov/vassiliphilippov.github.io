2. NUM

/ aɪ ˈai ˈai / (AmE), / aɪ ˈai ˈai / (BrE)
The Roman numeral for the number three.
the Roman numeral for three

Examples

The clock displayed the time as III on its face.
The king was known as Henry III of England.
Chapters are numbered I, II, and III in the book.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: three - The number equivalent to three; the Arabic numeral 3
  • Degree Lower: II - The Roman numeral for two
  • Degree Higher: IV - The Roman numeral for four

Etymology

From Latin Roman numeral system, in which 'I' represents one and 'III' represents three.
