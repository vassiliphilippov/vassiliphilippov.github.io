encyclopaedia

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌsaɪkləˈpiːdiə / (AmE), / ɪnˌsaɪklɪˈpiːdiə / (BrE)
a book or set of books containing articles on various topics, usually arranged in alphabetical order, covering all areas of knowledge or a particular subject
reference work with articles on many topics

Examples

He found the answer in a well-known encyclopaedia at the library.
Many families used to own a set of encyclopaedias for reference at home.
The online encyclopaedia covers everything from history to modern science.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dictionary - a reference work listing words and their meanings

Etymology

From Greek 'enkuklopaideia' (general education), from 'enkuklios' (circular, general) + 'paideia' (education).
