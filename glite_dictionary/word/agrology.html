agrology

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡrɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / əˈɡrɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of soils, especially in relation to crops and agriculture
science of soils and crops
medical

Examples

She studied agrology to better understand which crops would thrive on her farm.
Advancements in agrology have led to more sustainable agricultural practices.
The conference will focus on recent discoveries in agrology and soil science.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pedology - the study of soils in their natural environment

Etymology

From French *agrologie* (science of cultivated land), from Greek *agros* (field) + *-logia* (study).
