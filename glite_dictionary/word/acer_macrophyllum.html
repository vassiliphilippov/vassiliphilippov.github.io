Acer macrophyllum

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪsər ˌmækroʊˈfɪləm / (AmE), / ˈeɪsə ˌmækrəʊˈfɪləm / (BrE)
A species of maple native to western North America, noted for its large five-lobed leaves that turn orange in autumn; commonly known as the bigleaf maple.
species of maple with large leaves in western North America

Examples

The Acer macrophyllum is known for its exceptionally large leaves, which can reach up to twelve inches across.
In autumn, the leaves of Acer macrophyllum turn vibrant shades of orange and gold.
Many parks in the Pacific Northwest feature mature Acer macrophyllum specimens providing ample shade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bigleaf maple - common name for Acer macrophyllum

Etymology

From Latin: 'Acer' meaning 'maple', 'macrophyllum' from Greek 'makros' (large) and 'phyllon' (leaf).
