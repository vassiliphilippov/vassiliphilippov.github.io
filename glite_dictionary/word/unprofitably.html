unprofitably

1. ADV

/ ʌnˈprɑf.ɪ.t̬əb.li / (AmE), / ʌnˈprɒf.ɪ.tə.bli / (BrE)
In a way that does not produce profit or benefit; in an unproductive or disadvantageous manner.
in a manner that yields no profit

Examples

The company operated unprofitably for several years before finally closing its doors.
He invested his money unprofitably, missing out on better opportunities.
The store was run unprofitably and eventually went out of business.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: profitably - in a way that produces profit or benefit

Etymology

From 'unprofitable' (not yielding profit) + '-ly' (adverb suffix).

2. ADV

/ ʌnˈprɑfɪtəbli / (AmE), / ʌnˈprɒfɪtəbli / (BrE)
In a way that does not produce profit or gain.
in a way that lacks profit

Examples

The business operated unprofitably for several years before turning a profit.
He invested his savings unprofitably in a series of failing startups.
The company has been running unprofitably since the market crash.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: profitably - in a manner that produces profit or gain

Etymology

From 'unprofitable' (not profitable) + '-ly' (adverbial suffix).
