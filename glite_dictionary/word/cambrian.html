Cambrian

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæmbriən / (AmE), / ˈkæmbriən / (BrE)
a native or resident of Wales, especially in a historical or literary context
native or resident of Wales (historical)

Examples

As a proud Cambrian, she cherished the traditions of her homeland.
Several famous poets were Cambrians who wrote passionately about Wales.
The historian described the struggles faced by the Cambrian population in the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Welshman - a man from Wales
  • Antonym: Englishman - a man from England

Etymology

From Latin 'Cambria', the medieval Latin name for Wales, ultimately from the Welsh 'Cymru' (Wales).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæmbriən / (AmE), / ˈkæmbriən / (BrE)
the first period of the Paleozoic era, lasting from about 541 to 485 million years ago, marked by the appearance of diverse marine invertebrates
Paleozoic geological period

Examples

The Cambrian period saw the rapid development of many complex marine animals.
Fossil evidence from the Cambrian helps scientists understand early life on Earth.
Trilobites were especially abundant during the Cambrian.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Ordovician - the geological period following the Cambrian
  • Degree Lower: Precambrian - the geological time before the Cambrian period

Etymology

From Cambria, the Latin name for Wales, where rocks from this period were first studied.
