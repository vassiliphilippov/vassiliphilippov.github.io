unsecured

1. ADJ

/ ʌn.sɪˈkjɝd / (AmE), / ʌn.sɪˈkjʊəd / (BrE)
not protected by collateral or financial security, especially relating to loans or debt instruments.
not secured by collateral (finance)

Examples

The company issued an unsecured bond to raise capital for expansion.
Because the loan was unsecured, the interest rate was significantly higher than for secured loans.
He invested in several unsecured promissory notes, despite the risks involved.

Synset Examples

an unsecured note

Related Terms

  • Antonym: secured - protected by collateral or financial security

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'secured' (past participle of 'secure').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02102473-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ʌn.sɪˈkjʊrd / (AmE), / ʌn.sɪˈkjʊəd / (BrE)
not firmly fastened, fixed, or made safe; not protected or locked
not firmly fastened or locked

Examples

The thief entered through an unsecured window at the back of the house.
Make sure that no unsecured items are left on the shelves during an earthquake.
The documents were left on an unsecured computer, causing a data breach.

Synset Examples

an unbarred door
went through the unlatched gate into the street
an unlocked room

Related Terms

  • Antonym: secured - firmly fastened or protected
  • Synonym: unlocked - not fastened with a lock
  • Synonym: unlatched - not fastened with a latch

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + secured (made safe or fixed).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02103662-s
Synset POS: s
