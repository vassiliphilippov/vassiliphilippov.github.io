Anomala

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɑːmələ / (AmE), / əˈnɒmələ / (BrE)
a genus of beetles whose larvae feed mainly on plant roots, including some species that are pests of cultivated grasses
genus of beetles, plant root feeders

Examples

Many species in the genus Anomala are considered significant pests in agriculture due to their root-feeding grubs.
The Japanese beetle, once classified as Anomala orientalis, has caused extensive damage to lawns and crops.
Field studies have shown that Anomala larvae infestations can severely reduce turfgrass quality.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Latin 'anomalus' (irregular, anomalous) due to varied appearance.

2. NOUN

/ əˈnɑːmələ / (AmE), / əˈnɒmələ / (BrE)
A genus of beetles whose larvae feed mainly on plant roots, including several species that are pests of cultivated grasses.
genus of beetles; plant-root pests

Examples

The Anomala genus includes several species that cause damage to lawn grasses.
Scientists studied the feeding habits of Anomala beetles to better understand their impact on crops.
Some members of Anomala are considered major agricultural pests due to their root-feeding grubs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beetle - An insect of the order Coleoptera

Etymology

From Latin 'anomala', meaning 'anomalous' or 'irregular'.
