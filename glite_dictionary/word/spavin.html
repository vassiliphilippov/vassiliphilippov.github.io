spavin

1. NOUN

/ ˈspævɪn / (AmE), / ˈspævɪn / (BrE)
a swelling or bony growth on the hock joint of a horse, often causing lameness
swelling of a horse's hock joint
medical

Examples

The veterinarian diagnosed the horse with spavin after noticing persistent swelling near the hock.
Horses suffering from spavin often show signs of lameness and discomfort while walking.
Proper care and early detection of spavin can help prevent severe joint damage in horses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bone spavin - a type of spavin caused by bony growth on the hock joint
  • Synonym: bog spavin - a type of spavin characterized by fluid swelling without bony growth

Etymology

From Old French espavain.
