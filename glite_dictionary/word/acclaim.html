acclaim

1. VERB

/ əˈkleɪm / (AmE), / əˈkleɪm / (BrE)
to praise enthusiastically and publicly, especially by applause or cheering
praise or applaud enthusiastically

Examples

The critics acclaimed the actor’s performance with a standing ovation.
As she left the stage, the audience acclaimed her for several minutes.
He was acclaimed as the best director at the festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to applaud - to show approval by clapping hands
  • Antonym: boo - to show disapproval by shouting or making noise

Etymology

From Latin 'acclamare', meaning 'to shout at'.

2. VERB

/ əˈkleɪm / (AmE), / əˈkleɪm / (BrE)
to praise or welcome someone or something enthusiastically and publicly
to praise enthusiastically in public

Examples

The audience acclaimed the performance with a standing ovation.
She was acclaimed as a pioneer in her field at the awards ceremony.
The film was acclaimed by critics for its originality and depth.

Synset Examples

The critics hailed the young pianist as a new Rubinstein

Related Terms

  • Synonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration of someone or something
  • Antonym: to criticize - to express disapproval of someone or something

Etymology

From Latin *acclamare* (to shout at, to applaud), from *ad-* (to) + *clamare* (to shout).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00863389-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ əˈkleɪm / (AmE), / əˈkleɪm / (BrE)
enthusiastic and public praise or approval, especially for an achievement
enthusiastic public praise

Examples

Her first novel received widespread acclaim from critics and readers alike.
The scientist's groundbreaking research brought her international acclaim.
Despite initial skepticism, his work eventually achieved great acclaim.

Synset Examples

the book met with modest acclaim
he acknowledged the plaudits of the crowd
they gave him more eclat than he really deserved

Related Terms

  • Synonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration
  • Synonym: applause - approval or praise expressed by clapping
  • Antonym: criticism - the expression of disapproval or pointing out faults

Etymology

From Latin acclamare, meaning 'to shout at' or 'to acclaim'.

Synset POS: n
