ecliptic

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈklɪptɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈklɪptɪk / (BrE)
the great circle on the celestial sphere marking the apparent annual path of the sun, or the plane of Earth's orbit around the sun
the sun's apparent annual path; plane of Earth's orbit

Examples

The planets all move close to the ecliptic, making solar eclipses possible.
The zodiac constellations lie along the ecliptic in the night sky.
Astronomers use the ecliptic as a reference when mapping the positions of stars.

Synset Examples

all of the planets rotate the sun in approximately the same ecliptic

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: celestial equator - the imaginary circle on the celestial sphere in the same plane as Earth's equator

Etymology

From Latin ecliptica (line of eclipses), from Greek ekleiptikos.
