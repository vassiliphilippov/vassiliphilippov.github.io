nonagenarian

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑnədʒəˈnɛriən / (AmE), / ˌnɒnədʒəˈneəriən / (BrE)
a person who is between 90 and 99 years old
person aged 90–99

Examples

The nonagenarian delighted everyone with stories from her childhood in the town.
Despite being a nonagenarian, he still completed the crossword every morning.
Many of the nonagenarians in the village meet regularly for tea on Sundays.

Synset Examples

the nonagenarian inhabitants of the nursing home

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: octogenarian - a person who is between 80 and 89 years old
  • Degree Higher: centenarian - a person who is 100 years old or older

Etymology

From Latin 'nonageni' (ninety each) + '-arian'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌnɑːnədʒəˈnɛriən / (AmE), / ˌnɒnədʒəˈneəriən / (BrE)
a person who is between 90 and 99 years old
person aged between 90 and 99

Examples

The local newspaper featured a profile of the remarkable nonagenarian who still volunteers every week.
The birthday party was a joyous celebration for the town’s oldest nonagenarian.
As a nonagenarian, she amazed everyone with her sharp memory and wit.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: octogenarian - a person who is between 80 and 89 years old
  • Degree Higher: centenarian - a person who is 100 years old or older

Etymology

From Latin 'nonagenarius' (containing ninety), from 'nonageni' (ninety each).
