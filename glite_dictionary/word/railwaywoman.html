railwaywoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪlˌweɪˌwʊm.ən / (AmE), / ˈreɪl.weɪˌwʊm.ən / (BrE)
a woman employed by a railroad, especially in an operational or technical role
female railroad employee

Examples

The company hired its first railwaywoman as an engineer in 1942.
Several railwaywomen played key roles during the war effort.
As a railwaywoman, Jane was proud to operate the train through challenging conditions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: railwayman - a man who works for a railway

Etymology

Formed from 'railway' and 'woman' after the pattern of 'railwayman'.
