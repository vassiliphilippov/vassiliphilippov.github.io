Duchamp

1. PROPN

/ duːˈʃɑːmp / (AmE), / djuːˈʃɑ̃ː / (BrE)
Marcel Duchamp (1887–1968), French-born artist known for his influential role in the Dada movement and for introducing ready-made objects as art.
French Dada artist (1887–1968)

Examples

Many art historians consider Duchamp the father of conceptual art.
The influence of Duchamp can be seen in much of twentieth-century modern art.
Her thesis focused on the impact of Duchamp's ready-made sculptures.
