Appalachians

1. NOUN

/ ˌæpəˈleɪtʃiənz, ˌæpəˈlætʃiənz / (AmE), / ˌæpəˈleɪtʃiənz, ˌæpəˈlætʃiənz / (BrE)
a major mountain range in eastern North America, stretching from southeastern Canada to central Alabama in the United States
mountain range in eastern North America

Examples

The Appalachians are known for their stunning fall foliage and diverse wildlife.
Early settlers found the Appalachians to be a formidable barrier to westward expansion.
We spent a week hiking through the scenic trails of the Appalachians in North Carolina.

Etymology

From the name of the Apalachee people, a Native American tribe in Florida, via Spanish explorers.
