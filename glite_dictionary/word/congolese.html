Congolese

1. ADJ

/ kɑŋɡəˈliz / (AmE), / kɒŋɡəˈliːz / (BrE)
relating to the Congo region or its people, language, or culture
of or relating to the Congo or its people

Examples

The Congolese government announced new regulations for international business in the region.
She wore traditional Congolese clothing during the festival.
Many Congolese artists are gaining recognition in the international music scene.

Synset Examples

Congolese rulers
the Congolese republic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Congolese - a native or inhabitant of the Congo region

Etymology

From Congo + -ese, following the pattern of demonyms in English (as in Chinese, Sudanese).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03067520-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːŋɡəˈliːz / (AmE), / ˌkɒŋɡəˈliːz / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of the Republic of the Congo
person from the Republic of the Congo

Examples

Many Congolese have moved to the capital in search of better opportunities.
The restaurant is run by a family of Congolese who specialize in traditional dishes.
A group of Congolese artists performed at the international festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Congolese - of or relating to the Congo or its people (adjective form)

Etymology

From Congo (the country or river) + -ese (demonymic suffix from Italian -ese).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09718040-n
Synset POS: n
