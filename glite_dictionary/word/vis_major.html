vis major

1. NOUN

/ vɪs ˈmeɪ.dʒər / (AmE), / vɪs ˈmeɪ.dʒə / (BrE)
An irresistible natural force or event, such as an earthquake or flood, that cannot be foreseen or prevented and interrupts normal activities; often used in legal contexts.
unavoidable natural catastrophe (legal term)
legal

Examples

The company could not be held liable because the shipping delay was due to vis major.
Certain insurance policies exclude claims arising from vis major, such as earthquakes or floods.
Legal contracts often include a clause addressing obligations in cases of vis major.

Synset Examples

he discovered that his house was not insured against acts of God

Related Terms

  • Synonym: act of God - an event caused solely by natural forces, without human intervention
  • Synonym: force majeure - unforeseeable circumstances preventing fulfillment of a contract

Etymology

From Latin 'vis' (force) and 'major' (greater).
