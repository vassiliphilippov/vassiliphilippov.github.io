spruce

1. NOUN

/ sprus / (AmE), / spruːs / (BrE)
A type of evergreen coniferous tree of the genus Picea, with needle-like leaves and cones, commonly found in northern temperate regions.
evergreen tree of genus Picea

Examples

The forest was filled with tall spruce trees covered in snow.
We decorated the living room with branches from a spruce during the holidays.
A rare species of spruce can only be found in this region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fir - an evergreen coniferous tree of the genus Abies
  • Synonym: pine - an evergreen coniferous tree of the genus Pinus

Etymology

Probably from Middle Low German *spruce* or *sprucebaum*, originally referring to Prussia (a historical region), from where the trees were imported.

2. NOUN

/ spruːs / (AmE), / spruːs / (BrE)
the light, soft, and moderately strong wood of spruce trees, especially used for timber and millwork
soft wood from spruce trees, used in timber

Examples

The beams in the cabin were made from spruce because of its light weight and durability.
Most guitars use spruce for the soundboard due to its resonant qualities.
They decided to use spruce for the window frames as it is easy to work with.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pine - the wood from pine trees, also used for timber
  • Antonym: oak - wood from oak trees, known for hardness and strength
