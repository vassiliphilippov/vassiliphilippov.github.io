on

1. ADJ

/ ɑn / (AmE), / ɒn / (BrE)
(of events) arranged, planned, or scheduled to take place; not cancelled.
scheduled to happen (events)

Examples

The concert is still on despite the weather warnings.
Do you know if the meeting is on for tomorrow?
Our plans for dinner are on as scheduled.

Synset Examples

the picnic is on, rain or shine
we have nothing on for Friday night

Related Terms

  • Antonym: off - (of events) cancelled, postponed, or not happening
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01656183-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADV

/ ɑn / (AmE), / ɒn / (BrE)
(of a device or system) in a state of operation or ready to function; activated.
activated; in operation (device/system)

Examples

Please leave the lights on while you are in the room.
The radio was already on when I walked in.
Is your computer on or should I turn it on for you?

Synset Examples

turn the lights on
get a load on

Related Terms

  • Antonym: off - not operating; deactivated (device/system)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00069872-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADJ

/ ɑn / (AmE), / ɒn / (BrE)
(of a device, machine, or power supply) in operation or switched to an active state.
switched on; operating (of a device)

Examples

Please leave the kitchen light on until I get home.
Make sure the television is on before you try to change the channel.
The alarm system stays on all night for security reasons.

Synset Examples

left the oven on
the switch is in the on position

Related Terms

  • Antonym: off - (of a device or machine) switched to a non-operating state
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01655638-a
Synset POS: a

4. ADV

/ ɑn / (AmE), / ɒn / (BrE)
(adverb) continuing in time, especially without stopping; still happening or being done.
continuing without stopping; still happening

Examples

The meeting went on for several hours after lunch.
Even after many challenges, the project carried on successfully.
She read on despite the noise around her.

Synset Examples

his spirit lives on
shall I read on?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: still - continuing to happen at the present time; not stopped
  • Antonym: off - no longer happening; not continuing
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00069746-r
Synset POS: r
