iridium

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈrɪdiəm / (AmE), / ɪˈrɪdiəm / (BrE)
a heavy, brittle, silvery-white metallic element of the platinum group, used in high-strength alloys and found in natural alloys with platinum or osmium
a heavy metallic element (Ir), platinum group

Examples

Scientists use iridium in making durable crucibles and other equipment for high-temperature conditions.
Traces of iridium in geological layers often indicate ancient asteroid impacts.
The pen's tip contains iridium to ensure smooth and long-lasting writing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: platinum - a precious silvery-white metal, element of the platinum group
  • Synonym: osmium - a hard, brittle, blue-gray or blue-black transition metal, element of the platinum group

Etymology

From New Latin iridium, named after Iris, the Greek goddess of the rainbow, due to the varied colors of its salts.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈrɪdiəm / (AmE), / ɪˈrɪdiəm / (BrE)
a heavy, hard, and brittle metallic chemical element (symbol Ir), belonging to the platinum group, used chiefly in alloys and for making electrical contacts and other specialized equipment.
a metallic chemical element (Ir), platinum group

Examples

The pen's nib is tipped with iridium for increased durability.
Scientists discovered traces of iridium in the geological layer, suggesting a meteorite impact.
Iridium alloys are often used in equipment that must withstand high temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: platinum - a precious silvery-white metal, element with symbol Pt
  • Synonym: osmium - a hard, brittle, bluish-white transition metal, chemical element (Os)

Etymology

From Latin *iris*, meaning 'rainbow', due to the colors of its salts.
