isomerisation

1. NOUN

/ aɪˌsoʊmɚaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəməraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the chemical process of converting a compound into one of its isomers, having the same atoms but arranged differently
conversion of a compound into its isomer
medical

Examples

The isomerisation of alkanes is an important step in producing high-octane fuels.
Catalysts are used to speed up the isomerisation process in industrial chemistry.
During the reaction, isomerisation produces a compound with the same formula but a different structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isomerization - alternative spelling, especially in American English

Etymology

From 'isomer' (same parts) + '-isation', ultimately from Greek 'isos' (equal) and 'meros' (part).
