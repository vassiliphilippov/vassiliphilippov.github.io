Alar

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.lɑːr / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.lɑː / (BrE)
a chemical used as a plant growth regulator, especially sprayed on fruit trees to synchronize ripening for harvest
a plant growth-regulating chemical

Examples

The use of Alar on apple orchards was once common until health concerns arose.
Many farmers relied on Alar to coordinate the harvest of their fruit crops.
Public debate over the safety of Alar led to its reduced use in agriculture.
