volcanism

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɑːl.kəˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈvɒl.kəˌnɪz.əm / (BrE)
the processes and phenomena related to the eruption of magma, gases, and ash from volcanoes or fissures in the Earth's crust
processes related to volcanic activity

Examples

The region's unique landscape is shaped by millions of years of volcanism.
Scientists study volcanism to better understand the formation of new landmasses.
Recent volcanism has led to the creation of several new volcanic islands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: volcanic activity - eruptions and related processes involving volcanoes

Etymology

From Latin 'volcanus' (Vulcan, Roman god of fire) + '-ism'.
