Phoradendron serotinum

1. NOUN

/ foʊˌrædəndrɒn səˈroʊtɪnəm / (AmE), / fəˌrædənˈdrɒn sərɒˈtɪnəm / (BrE)
A species of mistletoe native to North America, traditionally used as Christmas mistletoe in the United States, that grows parasitically on deciduous trees and can considerably weaken its host.
American mistletoe species, Christmas tradition

Examples

Many families in the United States hang Phoradendron serotinum in their homes during Christmas for good luck.
The deciduous trees on the property were weakened by a heavy infestation of Phoradendron serotinum.
Unlike European mistletoe, Phoradendron serotinum is native to North America and is commonly used in holiday decorations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistletoe - A parasitic plant traditionally used in Christmas decorations

Etymology

From Greek 'phorádzō' (to carry) and 'déndron' (tree); 'serotinum' from Latin meaning 'late' or 'late-flowering'.
