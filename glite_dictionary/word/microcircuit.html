microcircuit

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkroʊˈsɜːrkɪt / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊˈsɜːkɪt / (BrE)
A very small electronic circuit, typically built into a semiconductor chip, which forms a complete system rather than a single component.
tiny electronic circuit on a chip

Examples

The new smartphone uses a highly advanced microcircuit to manage power consumption efficiently.
Engineers tested each microcircuit for faults before assembling the final device.
Recent advances in microcircuit design have significantly reduced the size of electronic gadgets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: integrated circuit - an electronic circuit formed on a small piece of semiconductor material

Etymology

From micro- (small) + circuit, indicating a miniaturized electrical circuit.
