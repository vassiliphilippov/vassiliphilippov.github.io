pottery

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑː.t̬ɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈpɒt.ər.i / (BrE)
A workshop or factory where clay objects are made and fired.
workshop where clayware is made

Examples

She owns a small pottery just outside the village.
The artist displayed her new vases at the local pottery.
Tours of the pottery are offered every Saturday for visitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ceramics - the art of making objects from clay
  • Synonym: studio - a place where an artist works

Etymology

From Old French poterie, from potier (potter) + -ie (noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːtəri / (AmE), / ˈpɒtəri / (BrE)
objects made of clay and hardened by heat, such as pots, dishes, and vases.
objects made from baked clay

Examples

The ancient village is famous for its beautiful pottery.
She collects handmade pottery from local artists.
We visited the museum's exhibit on Greek pottery techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ceramics - objects made from clay and hardened by heat

Etymology

From Old French 'poterie', from 'potier' meaning 'potter'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɑː.t̬ɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈpɒt.ər.i / (BrE)
the craft or activity of making objects such as pots, bowls, and vases from clay and hardening them by heat
the craft of making earthenware

Examples

She has studied pottery for years and now creates beautiful ceramic vases.
A local artist teaches pottery classes every weekend at the community center.
Many ancient cultures expressed their creativity through pottery and painted designs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ceramics - the art of making objects from clay and similar materials and firing them

Etymology

From Old French 'poterie', from 'poter' (to make pots), based on Latin 'pottus' (pot).
