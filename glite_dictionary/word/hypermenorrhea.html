hypermenorrhea

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪ.pər.mɛn.əˈriː.ə / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.pə.mɛn.əˈriː.ə / (BrE)
abnormally heavy or prolonged menstrual bleeding, often a symptom of underlying conditions such as uterine tumors and potentially leading to anemia if untreated
abnormally heavy or prolonged menstruation
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with hypermenorrhea after experiencing abnormally heavy menstrual bleeding for several months.
Women suffering from hypermenorrhea may develop iron-deficiency anemia if the condition persists.
Treatment options for hypermenorrhea depend on the underlying cause and the severity of symptoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: menorrhagia - excessively heavy or prolonged menstrual bleeding

Etymology

From Greek hyper- (over, excessive) + menorrhea (excessive menstrual flow) from men (month) and rhoia (flow).
