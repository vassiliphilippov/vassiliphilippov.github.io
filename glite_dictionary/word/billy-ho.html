billy-ho

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪliˌhoʊ / (AmE), / ˈbɪliˌhəʊ / (BrE)
an extremely large amount; to a great extent or degree (chiefly used in comparisons such as "like billy-ho")
an unimaginably large amount (slang, chiefly British)
slang british

Examples

It rained like billy-ho last night, flooding the whole street.
When she started laughing, she laughed like billy-ho and couldn't stop for minutes.
He worked like billy-ho to finish the project on time.

Synset Examples

British say ‘it rained like billyo’ where Americans say ‘it rained like all get out’

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly a playful alteration of 'Billy Hell' or a fanciful formation. Usage popularized in late 19th-century British English.
