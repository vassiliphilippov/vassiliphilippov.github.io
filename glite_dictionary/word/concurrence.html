concurrence

1. NOUN

/ kənˈkɝːəns / (AmE), / kənˈkʌrəns / (BrE)
the state of agreeing or coming to the same opinion or result as another person or group
agreement in opinion or result

Examples

There was a strong concurrence among the experts regarding the cause of the problem.
The study's findings were in concurrence with previous research results.
The committee reached concurrence after a lengthy discussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agreement - the state of having the same opinion or judgment
  • Antonym: disagreement - lack of consensus or having different opinions

Etymology

From Latin 'concurrentia' (a coming together), from 'concurrere' (to run together).
