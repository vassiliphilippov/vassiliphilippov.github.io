fasciolopsiasis

1. NOUN

/ fæʃiˌoʊlɑpˈsaɪəsɪs / (AmE), / fæʃɪˌɒlɒpˈsaɪəsɪs / (BrE)
A parasitic infection caused by infestation with the large intestinal fluke Fasciolopsis buski, common in parts of eastern Asia.
infection by Fasciolopsis buski fluke
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with fasciolopsiasis after reviewing the lab results.
Cases of fasciolopsiasis are more prevalent in rural areas with inadequate sanitation.
Treatment for fasciolopsiasis usually involves antiparasitic medications prescribed by a physician.

Etymology

From genus name Fasciolopsis (the parasite) + -iasis (disease condition).
