Icterus galbula galbula

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪktərəs ɡælˈbjuːlə ɡælˈbjuːlə / (AmE), / ˈɪktərəs ɡælˈbjuːlə ɡælˈbjuːlə / (BrE)
the eastern subspecies of the northern oriole, a songbird native to eastern North America
eastern subspecies of northern oriole (bird)

Examples

The bright orange and black plumage of the Icterus galbula galbula makes it easy to spot among spring foliage.
Birdwatchers in the eastern United States often hope to catch a glimpse of the Icterus galbula galbula during migration.
Unlike its western relatives, the Icterus galbula galbula prefers nesting in tall deciduous trees.

Etymology

From Latin: 'icterus' (jaundice, yellow bird), 'galbula' (yellowish).
