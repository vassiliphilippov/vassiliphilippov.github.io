Carya myristicaeformis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛəriə mɪˌrɪstɪkiˈfɔrmɪs / (AmE), / ˈkeəriə mɪˌrɪstɪkɪˈfɔːmɪs / (BrE)
A species of hickory native to the southern United States and Mexico, known for its hard, nutmeg-shaped nuts.
species of hickory tree

Examples

The Carya myristicaeformis is prized for its tough wood and distinctively shaped nuts.
Botanists found a rare grove of Carya myristicaeformis in southern Texas.
The nuts of Carya myristicaeformis are difficult to crack but edible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hickory - a type of North American tree in the walnut family

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Carya' referring to the hickory genus and 'myristicaeformis' meaning 'shaped like nutmeg'.
