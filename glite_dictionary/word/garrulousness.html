garrulousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡær.ə.ləs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɡær.ʊ.ləs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being excessively talkative, especially about trivial matters
excessive talkativeness

Examples

We were amused by his garrulousness during the family dinner.
Her garrulousness often makes meetings longer than necessary.
The novel's narrator is known for his wit and garrulousness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garrulity - talkative or wordy behavior
  • Antonym: taciturnity - quality of being reserved or silent

Etymology

From Latin 'garrulus' (talkative) + English suffix '-ness'.
