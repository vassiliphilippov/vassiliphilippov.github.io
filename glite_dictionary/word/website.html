website

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛb.saɪt / (AmE), / ˈweb.saɪt / (BrE)
A set of related web pages located under a single domain name, typically produced by a single person or organization and accessible via the Internet.
set of web pages on the Internet

Examples

Many people visit the company's website to learn about new products.
The school's website was recently updated with the latest announcements.
You can purchase tickets through the museum's official website.

Synset Examples

the Israeli web site was damaged by hostile hackers

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: webpage - a single document on the World Wide Web

Etymology

Blend of 'web' (from World Wide Web) and 'site' (location).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06370600-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɛbˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈwɛb.saɪt / (BrE)
A collection of related web pages located under a single domain name, typically produced by a single person or organization, and accessible via the Internet.
internet collection of web pages

Examples

She created a beautiful website to showcase her photography portfolio.
The company's website was updated with new product information last week.
If you have any questions, visit our website for more details.

Synset Examples

the Israeli web site was damaged by hostile hackers

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: web page - a single document within a website, accessible via a unique URL

Etymology

From 'web' (referring to the World Wide Web) + 'site' (location).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06370600-n
Synset POS: n
