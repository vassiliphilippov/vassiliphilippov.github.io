lentic

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɛn.tɪk / (AmE), / ˈlɛn.tɪk / (BrE)
relating to, living in, or characteristic of still or slow-moving water, such as lakes or ponds.
relating to still or stagnant waters

Examples

Many aquatic plants are adapted to lentic environments such as ponds and lakes.
The lentic ecosystem was home to a variety of insects and amphibians.
Researchers studied the effects of pollution in lentic and lotic habitats across the region.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lotic - relating to or living in flowing water, such as rivers or streams

Etymology

From Latin 'lentus' (slow, calm).
