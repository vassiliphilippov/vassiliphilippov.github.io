takeoff

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪkˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈteɪkɒf / (BrE)
the moment when an aircraft leaves the ground and begins to ascend
initial ascent of an aircraft

Examples

The takeoff was delayed due to poor weather conditions.
She watched the airplane's takeoff from the observation deck.
A smooth takeoff is essential for a comfortable flight.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: landing - the act of an aircraft coming to the ground

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'take off', meaning to leave the ground, first used in aviation in the early 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈteɪkˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈteɪkɒf / (BrE)
the act of an aircraft becoming airborne and leaving the ground
departure of an aircraft from the ground

Examples

The plane's takeoff was delayed due to fog.
Please remain seated during takeoff and landing.
She watched the jet's powerful takeoff from the terminal window.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: landing - the act of an aircraft coming down onto the ground

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'take off'.

humorous imitation or parody

Examples

She does a brilliant takeoff of famous politicians on stage.
