phototelegraph

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊtoʊˈtɛləˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊˈtɛlɪɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a device used for transmitting photographs or images over a distance by telegraphy.
device for transmitting images by telegraph

Examples

The newspaper office used a phototelegraph to send press photos across the country.
Early phototelegraphs revolutionized the transmission of images long before the rise of fax machines.
Engineers demonstrated the phototelegraph at the world exhibition, impressing visitors with its capabilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: facsimile - a system or device for transmitting copies of documents electronically

Etymology

From photo- (relating to light or photography) and telegraph (from Greek tele 'far' + graphein 'to write').
