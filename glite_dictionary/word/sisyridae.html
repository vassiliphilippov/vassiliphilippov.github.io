Sisyridae

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈsɪrɪˌdi / (AmE), / sɪˈsɪrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects in the order Neuroptera, commonly known as spongeflies.
family of insects (spongeflies)

Examples

The Sisyridae are often found near aquatic habitats, where their larvae feed on freshwater sponges.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Sisyridae during their expedition to South America.
Members of the Sisyridae are characterized by their delicately veined wings and aquatic larvae.

Etymology

From New Latin Sisyridae, based on the type genus Sisyra.

2. NOUN

/ sɪˈsɪrɪdiː / (AmE), / sɪˈsɪrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects in the order Neuroptera, commonly known as spongeflies, whose larvae feed on freshwater sponges.
family of insects known as spongeflies

Examples

The larvae of Sisyridae are known to feed on freshwater sponges and bryozoans.
Researchers discovered several new species belonging to the Sisyridae family in the Amazon basin.
Field guides to Neuroptera often include detailed descriptions of the Sisyridae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Neuroptera - an order of insects including lacewings, antlions, and their relatives

Etymology

From the type genus Sisyris, + -idae, a standard suffix for animal family names, from New Latin.
