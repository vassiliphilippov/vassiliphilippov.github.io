joined

1. ADJ

/ dʒɔɪnd / (AmE), / dʒɔɪnd / (BrE)
(of objects) connected by a link or fastener so as to form a continuous unit.
connected by a link or fastener

Examples

The two joined cars made the train longer than usual.
Each joined trailer must be checked for secure coupling before departure.
We walked through several joined compartments to reach the dining car.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connected - joined together so as to provide access or communication
  • Antonym: detached - not joined or connected; separate

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'join', from Old French 'joindre', from Latin 'iungere' (to join).
