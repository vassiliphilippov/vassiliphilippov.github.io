leatherleaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛðərˌlif / (AmE), / ˈlɛðəˌliːf / (BrE)
A low-growing evergreen shrub of boggy areas in northern temperate regions, having leathery leaves and small white cylindrical flowers, especially Chamaedaphne calyculata.
bog shrub with leathery evergreen leaves

Examples

The leatherleaf thrives in wetlands and provides ground cover for many species.
Botanists often study leatherleaf to understand adaptations to bog environments.
In early spring, the leatherleaf produces small clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛðɚˌlif / (AmE), / ˈlɛðəˌliːf / (BrE)
a stiff, leathery-leaved fern native to western North America, with ovate fronds divided to the midrib
fern with stiff leathery leaves, western North America

Examples

The leatherleaf thrives in moist, shaded forest areas across western North America.
Botanists studied the thick fronds of the leatherleaf to understand its adaptation to dry climates.
Gardeners often choose leatherleaf for landscaping because of its hardiness and unique texture.

Etymology

Named for its tough, leather-like leaves; compound of 'leather' and 'leaf.'
