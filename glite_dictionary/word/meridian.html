meridian

1. ADJ

/ məˈrɪdiən / (AmE), / məˈrɪdiən / (BrE)
(archaic) Relating to or occurring at noon; pertaining to midday.
of or occurring at noon (archaic)
archaic

Examples

The travelers rested during the meridian heat.

At the meridian hour, the church bells rang out across the town.

Etymology

From Latin 'meridianus' (of midday, southern), from 'meridies' (midday).

4. NOUN

/ məˈrɪdiən / (AmE), / məˈrɪdiən / (BrE)
An imaginary line on the Earth's surface, extending from the North Pole to the South Pole and passing through points of the same longitude.
imaginary geographical line from pole to pole

Examples

All points along the same meridian share the identical longitude.
The Prime Meridian runs through Greenwich, England, serving as the reference for global time zones.
Navigators use a meridian to determine longitude when mapping positions on Earth.

Synset Examples

all points on the same meridian have the same longitude

Related Terms

  • Synonym: longitude - distance east or west of the prime meridian

Etymology

From Latin meridianus (of midday), from meridies (midday), originally referring to the sun at its highest point.
