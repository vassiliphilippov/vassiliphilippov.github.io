settler

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛtlɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛtlə / (BrE)
a clerk in a betting shop who calculates and pays out the winnings to customers
clerk who pays out betting winnings
british

Examples

The settler checked the betting slips and then handed out the winnings to the punters.
If you have any questions about your payout, ask the settler at the counter.
As a settler in the shop, John was responsible for ensuring all the bets were correctly settled.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛtlɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛtlə / (BrE)
a person who moves to a new country, colony, or area and establishes residence there, often as part of a group colonizing the region
person who moves to a new territory to live

Examples

The first settlers arrived in the valley in the early 1800s.
As a settler, she faced many hardships adapting to the new land.
Many settler communities still celebrate the founding of their towns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colonist - a person who settles in a new colony

Etymology

From Middle English 'seteler', from 'settle' (to place, establish).
