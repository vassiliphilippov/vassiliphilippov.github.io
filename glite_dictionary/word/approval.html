approval

1. NOUN

/ əˈpruːvəl / (AmE), / əˈpruːvəl / (BrE)
a statement or message expressing a favorable opinion or agreement
favorable statement or message

Examples

She smiled at his comment, taking it as a quiet sign of approval.
A word of approval from her mentor meant a great deal to her.
His boss rarely offers approval, making positive feedback especially valuable.

Synset Examples

words of approval seldom passed his lips

Related Terms

  • Synonym: praise - expressing admiration or approval
  • Antonym: disapproval - a message expressing an unfavorable opinion

Etymology

From approve + -al, ultimately from Latin approbare (to approve).

WordNet ID: oewn-06699481-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ əˈpruvəl / (AmE), / əˈpruːvəl / (BrE)
a feeling or showing that you think someone or something is good or acceptable
feeling of liking or endorsing

Examples

She always looked for signs of her father's approval before making decisions.
The project received universal approval from the committee members.
He smiled in approval when she finished her presentation.

Synset Examples

although she fussed at them, she secretly viewed all her children with approval

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disapproval - a feeling that something or someone is bad or not acceptable
  • Synonym: endorsement - an expression of approval or support

Etymology

From approve + -al, from Old French 'aprover' (to approve), from Latin 'approbare'.

3. NOUN

/ əˈpruvəl / (AmE), / əˈpruːvəl / (BrE)
the belief that something or someone is good or acceptable; official agreement or permission
acceptance or agreement

Examples

She waited anxiously for her parents' approval of her decision.
The plan received approval from the city council last night.
The product was sent to customers on approval so they could decide if they wanted to keep it.

Synset Examples

he bought it on approval

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disapproval - the feeling that something or someone is bad or unacceptable
  • Synonym: permission - the act of officially allowing something

Etymology

From approve + -al, from Old French 'aprover', from Latin 'approbare' (to approve).

4. NOUN

/ əˈpruvəl / (AmE), / əˈpruːvəl / (BrE)
the formal act of officially agreeing to or accepting something
formal act of agreeing or accepting

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disapproval - the act of not approving or having a negative opinion of something
  • Synonym: endorsement - an act of declaring approval or support

Etymology
