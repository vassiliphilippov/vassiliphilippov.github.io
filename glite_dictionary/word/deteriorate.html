to deteriorate

1. VERB

/ dəˈtɪriəˌreɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈtɪəriəreɪt / (BrE)
to become progressively worse in quality, condition, or value
to become worse over time

Examples

If we do not repair the roof soon, it will deteriorate further.
His health began to deteriorate rapidly after the accident.
Relations between the two countries continue to deteriorate amid ongoing disputes.

Synset Examples

Her condition deteriorated
Conditions in the slums degenerated
The discussion devolved into a shouting match

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to improve - to become better
  • Synonym: decline - to gradually worsen or decrease
  • Synonym: degenerate - to become worse, especially morally or physically

Etymology

From Late Latin deterioratus, past participle of deteriorare (to make worse).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈtɪriəˌreɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈtɪəriəreɪt / (BrE)
to become progressively worse in quality, value, or condition
become worse in quality or condition

Examples

Over time, the abandoned building began to deteriorate due to weather and neglect.
If you don't repair the leak soon, the damage will quickly deteriorate.
Her health continued to deteriorate despite the treatments.

Synset Examples

His mind deteriorated

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to improve - to become better; the opposite of deteriorate
  • Synonym: decline - to gradually become less, fewer, or worse

Etymology

From Late Latin deterioratus, past participle of deteriorare (to make worse), from Latin deterior (worse).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00208905-v
Synset POS: v
