Fritillaria pluriflora

1. NOUN

/ frɪtɪˈlɛəriə pluːrɪˈflɔːrə / (AmE), / frɪtɪˈlɪərɪə pluːrɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant native to California, with pinkish purple, bell-shaped flowers; also known as the adobe lily.
California plant with pinkish purple flowers

Examples

The hills were dotted with blooming Fritillaria pluriflora during the spring.
Botanists are working to protect the rare Fritillaria pluriflora in its native habitat.
Many wildflower enthusiasts hope to catch a glimpse of Fritillaria pluriflora on their hikes.

Etymology

From genus name Fritillaria (Latin 'fritillus', dice-box, referencing the checkered pattern of some species), and Latin 'pluriflora' meaning 'many-flowered'.
