Amphicarpaea

1. NOUN

/ ˌæmfɪˈkɑrpiə / (AmE), / ˌamfɪˈkɑːpiə / (BrE)
a genus of small, twining vines native to North America and Asia, including the hog peanut
genus of twining vines (hog peanut)

Examples

Amphicarpaea thrives in shaded woodlands and is known for its edible seeds.
The hog peanut, belonging to the genus Amphicarpaea, is a native vine in North America.
Botanists classify several twining vines under Amphicarpaea found across Asia and North America.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'amphi-' (around, both) and 'karpos' (fruit).
