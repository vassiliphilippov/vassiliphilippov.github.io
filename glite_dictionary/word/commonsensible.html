commonsensible

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːmənˈsɛnsəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌkɒmənˈsɛnsəbl̩ / (BrE)
having or showing sound, practical judgment based on simple perception of the situation or facts; exhibiting native good judgment.
showing sound, practical judgment

Examples

She gave a commonsensible answer to the complex question.
The most commonsensible approach is often the best one.
His commonsensible nature made him a trusted adviser among his friends.

Synset Examples

arrive home at a reasonable hour
commonsense scholarship on the foibles of a genius
unlearned and commonsensical countryfolk were capable of solving problems that beset the more sophisticated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commonsensical - having or showing common sense; practical and prudent
  • Synonym: reasonable - fair, sensible, and showing good judgment
  • Antonym: foolish - lacking good sense or judgment

Etymology

Formed from 'common sense' with the suffix '-ible', modelled on 'commonsensical'.
