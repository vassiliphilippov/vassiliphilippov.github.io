Allium paradoxum

1. NOUN

/ ˈæliəm pəˈrædɑksəm / (AmE), / ˈælɪəm pəˈrædɒksəm / (BrE)
A species of wild garlic, also known as few-flowered leek, native to Russia and Iran, which produces bulbils instead of flowers.
species of wild garlic native to Eurasia

Examples

The woodland floor was carpeted with Allium paradoxum in early spring.
Gardeners sometimes find Allium paradoxum spreading quickly in moist, shady areas.
Allium paradoxum is prized for its edible leaves, but can be invasive outside its native range.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Allium ursinum - another species of wild garlic, known as ramsons or bear's garlic

Etymology

From Latin; 'Allium' is Latin for garlic, and 'paradoxum' means 'unexpected' or 'strange.'
