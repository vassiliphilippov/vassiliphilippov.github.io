prophecy

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.fə.si / (AmE), / ˈprɒf.ə.si / (BrE)
knowledge of the future, especially as revealed by a divine source
knowledge of the future from a divine source

Examples

Many ancient cultures believed that the prophecy foretold the rise of a great leader.
He claimed that his dreams were a form of prophecy from the gods.
The villagers lived in fear that the old prophecy about disaster would come true.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prediction - a statement about what will happen in the future

Etymology

From Old French 'prophecie', from Latin 'prophetia', from Greek 'prophēteia' (gift of interpreting the will of the gods).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːfəsi / (AmE), / ˈprɒfəsi / (BrE)
A statement that predicts what will happen in the future, especially one made under divine inspiration.
divinely inspired prediction

Examples

The ancient seer delivered a prophecy about the fall of the kingdom.
According to the prophecy, a chosen one will unite the divided lands.
Her prophecy was recorded and studied for generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prediction - statement about what will happen, not necessarily divinely inspired
  • Synonym: prophet - a person regarded as an inspired teacher or proclaimer of the will of God

Etymology

From Old French 'profecie', from Latin 'prophetia', from Greek 'prophēteia' (gift of interpreting the will of the gods).
