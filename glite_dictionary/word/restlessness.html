restlessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛstləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɛstləsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being continually active, moving, or unable to remain still
state of being unable to remain still

Examples

She felt a sense of restlessness during the long, quiet afternoon.
The restlessness of the children made it difficult for the teacher to keep order.
Wind rattled the windows all night, its restlessness keeping everyone awake.

Synset Examples

the restlessness of the wind

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calmness - the quality of being still or peaceful
  • Synonym: agitation - the quality of being disturbed or excited

Etymology

From 'restless' + '-ness', where 'restless' derives from Middle English 'restles', from 'rest' + '-less'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛstləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɛstləsnəs / (BrE)
the state of being unable to rest, relax, or remain still, often due to anxiety or boredom
inability to rest or relax

Examples

Her restlessness kept her awake all night, tossing and turning in bed.
During the long meeting, his restlessness became apparent as he shifted in his seat.
The dog paced the room with obvious restlessness, unable to settle down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agitation - a state of nervousness or excitement
  • Antonym: calmness - the state of being free from agitation or excitement

Etymology

From English 'restless' + '-ness', forming a noun indicating the state or quality.

3. NOUN

/ ˈrɛstləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɛstləsnɪs / (BrE)
the state of being unable to rest or relax due to anxiety or boredom, often expressed by continual motion or agitation
inability to rest or relax; agitation

Examples

His restlessness made it hard for him to sit through the long meeting.
Children sometimes show restlessness when they are bored or tired.
Her restlessness grew as she waited for the important phone call.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fidgetiness - the quality of being fidgety, especially due to nervousness
  • Antonym: calmness - the state of being free from agitation or excitement

Etymology

From 'restless' + '-ness'; 'restless' from Middle English, from Old English 'restleas' (rest + -less).

4. NOUN

/ ˈrɛstləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɛstləsnəs / (BrE)
the state of being unable to rest or relax, especially due to boredom, impatience, or irritation with delay
inability to relax or wait patiently

Examples

Her restlessness made it impossible for her to sit through the long meeting.
There was a general restlessness among the crowd as they waited for the concert to begin.
His restlessness grew with every minute that the train was delayed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impatience - lack of patience; unwillingness to wait
  • Antonym: calm - the state of being peaceful and relaxed

Etymology

From 'restless' + '-ness', where 'restless' comes from Middle English, from 'rest' + '-less'.
