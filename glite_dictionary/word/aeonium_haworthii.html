Aeonium haworthii

1. NOUN

/ iˈoʊniəm həˈwɔrθaɪ / (AmE), / iˈəʊniəm həˈwɔːθiː / (BrE)
A perennial subshrub native to Tenerife, with leaves arranged in rosettes that resemble pinwheels.
species of perennial subshrub from Tenerife

Examples

Aeonium haworthii is a popular succulent grown for its attractive rosettes and easy care.
Gardeners often choose Aeonium haworthii for rock gardens due to its drought tolerance.
You can propagate Aeonium haworthii easily from leaf cuttings in spring.

Etymology

From genus name Aeonium (Greek aiōnios, 'everlasting') and haworthii, honoring British botanist Adrian Hardy Haworth.
