dogmatic

1. ADJ

/ dɔɡˈmætɪk / (AmE), / dɒɡˈmætɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by dogma; expressing opinions or beliefs as if they are certainly correct.
relating to dogma; opinionated

Examples

The philosopher is known for his dogmatic writings about morality.
Her dogmatic approach to teaching left little room for discussion or debate.
Some critics argue that the organization's statements are overly dogmatic and dismissive of alternative views.

Synset Examples

dogmatic writings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doctrinaire - imposing strict adherence to doctrine or theory
  • Antonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas; not dogmatic

Etymology

From Late Latin dogmaticus, from Greek dogmatikos (relating to doctrines, opinions).

2. ADJ

/ dɔgˈmætɪk / (AmE), / dɒgˈmætɪk / (BrE)
inclined to lay down principles as undeniably true, often without consideration of evidence or the opinions of others; relating to strong, authoritative beliefs or doctrines.
strongly asserting beliefs as true

Examples

Her dogmatic approach to teaching left little room for student questions or alternative viewpoints.
Although he was knowledgeable, his dogmatic opinions often stifled debate during meetings.
Critics accused the leader of being too dogmatic in enforcing party doctrine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authoritarian - favoring strict obedience to authority
  • Antonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas

Etymology

From Greek dogmatikos (of doctrines or opinions), from dogma (opinion, tenet).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03073387-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ dɔgˈmætɪk / (AmE), / dɒgˈmætɪk / (BrE)
Tending to lay down opinions as undeniably true, without consideration of evidence or other opinions.
assertively opinionated, inflexible

Examples

She has a dogmatic approach to teaching and refuses to consider alternative methods.
His dogmatic statements left little room for discussion or debate.
Many people find dogmatic attitudes frustrating in group projects.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas
  • Synonym: rigid - unwilling to change or adapt

Etymology

From Greek 'dogmatikos', from 'dogma' meaning 'opinion, tenet'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00288936-s
Synset POS: s
