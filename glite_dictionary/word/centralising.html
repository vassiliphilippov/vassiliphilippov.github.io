centralising

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛntrəˌlaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrəlaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
Tending to concentrate power, resources, or activity toward a central point or authority.
tending to concentrate toward the centre

Examples

The government adopted a centralising approach to education, reducing local control.
A centralising tendency can sometimes lead to inefficiency in large organizations.
Many critics argued that the reforms were overly centralising in nature.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decentralising - tending to distribute away from a central point

Etymology

From 'centralize' + '-ing', based on centre.
