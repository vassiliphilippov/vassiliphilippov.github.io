Ursus maritimus

1. PROPN

/ ˈɝː.səs məˈrɪ.tɪ.məs / (AmE), / ˈɜː.səs məˈrɪ.tɪ.məs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the polar bear, a large white bear native to Arctic regions
scientific name for the polar bear

Examples

The Ursus maritimus is specially adapted to life in the harsh Arctic climate.
Researchers observed a group of Ursus maritimus hunting on the sea ice.
Climate change poses a major threat to the survival of Ursus maritimus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polar bear - large white bear native to Arctic regions

Etymology

From Latin: 'ursus' meaning bear and 'maritimus' meaning of the sea.
