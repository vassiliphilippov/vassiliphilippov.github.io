Crocodilia

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɑːkəˈdɪliə / (AmE), / ˌkrɒkəˈdɪliə / (BrE)
an order of large, predatory, semi-aquatic reptiles that includes crocodiles, alligators, caimans, and gavials
order of large reptiles: crocodiles, alligators, etc.

Examples

The order Crocodilia includes some of the largest living reptiles on Earth.
Students in the zoology class studied the evolutionary history of Crocodilia.
Unlike lizards, members of Crocodilia share several unique anatomical features.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crocodile - large predatory reptile of the family Crocodylidae

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek krokódilos (crocodile).
