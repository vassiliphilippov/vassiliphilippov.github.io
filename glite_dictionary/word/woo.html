to woo

1. VERB

/ wuː / (AmE), / wuː / (BrE)
to seek the affection or love of someone, especially with the intention of marriage; to try to win someone's romantic interest
try to win someone's love or affection

Examples

He tried to woo her with flowers and romantic poems.
The prince spent months wooing the princess before she agreed to marry him.
She is clearly being wooed by several suitors at once.

Synset Examples

John is courting Mary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: court - to try to win the love or favor of someone

Etymology

From Old English 'wōgian', meaning 'to court, to woo'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02540158-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ wuː / (AmE), / wuː / (BrE)
to try to gain the support, favor, or approval of someone or a group, often by showing attention or making efforts to please them
try to gain someone's favor or support

Examples

Several companies are trying to woo investors with promises of rapid growth.
The politician traveled across the country to woo undecided voters.
Tech firms are actively wooing top graduates with attractive job offers.

Synset Examples

China is wooing Russia

Related Terms

  • Synonym: court - to try to win the favor or support of someone, especially in a political context

Etymology

From Old English wōgian (to court, woo).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02540602-v
Synset POS: v
