lateen

1. NOUN

/ ləˈtin / (AmE), / ləˈtiːn / (BrE)
a triangular sail set on a long yard mounted at an angle on the mast, used especially on boats in the Mediterranean region
triangular sail, Mediterranean nautical term

Examples

The fishing boat was rigged with a single lateen sail that caught the afternoon breeze.
He admired the traditional design of the lateen on the old dhow in the harbor.
Navigating the narrow channels was easier with the maneuverable lateen sail.

Etymology

From French 'latine', possibly from 'latin' (Latin), referring to its use by Mediterranean sailors.

2. ADJ

/ ləˈtin / (AmE), / ləˈtiːn / (BrE)
(of a vessel or sail) rigged with or having a triangular sail set on a long yard mounted at an angle on the mast.
rigged with a triangular sail

Examples

The fisherman skillfully sailed his lateen boat across the bay.
Most traditional dhows are lateen-rigged, allowing them to navigate narrow waters.
A lateen sail is especially effective for tacking into the wind.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: square-rigged - rigged with square sails

Etymology

From French 'latine', from Italian 'latina (vela)' meaning 'Latin (sail)'.
