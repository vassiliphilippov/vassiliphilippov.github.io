misoneism

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪsəˈniːɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌmɪsəˈniːɪzəm / (BrE)
A strong dislike or fear of change or innovation.
strong dislike of change or innovation

Examples

The organization's misoneism made it resistant to adopting new technologies.
Academic institutions sometimes struggle with misoneism when faced with curriculum reforms.
His misoneism was apparent in his refusal to accept any modern theories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neophobia - fear or dislike of anything new

Etymology

From Italian 'misoneismo', from Greek 'misos' (hatred) + 'neos' (new).
