suffragette

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌf.rəˈdʒɛt / (AmE), / ˌsʌf.rəˈdʒɛt / (BrE)
A woman who campaigned for women's right to vote, especially one involved in the early 20th-century British movement.
woman campaigning for women's suffrage

Examples

The suffragette movement played a crucial role in securing voting rights for women.
She dressed as a suffragette for the historical reenactment at the museum.
Many suffragettes were imprisoned for their protests during the early 1900s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suffragist - person advocating for the extension of voting rights

Etymology

Formed from 'suffrage' (right to vote) plus the diminutive suffix '-ette'; popularized in early 20th-century Britain.
