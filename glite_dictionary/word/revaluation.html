revaluation

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːvæl.juˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌvæl.juˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of making a new assessment or appraisal of something, especially its value or importance
new assessment or appraisal

Examples

The company ordered a complete revaluation of its assets after the merger.
After recent market changes, a revaluation of property prices was necessary.
The museum's collection underwent a revaluation to determine its current worth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appraisal - the act of assessing something or someone
  • Synonym: evaluation - the making of a judgment about the value of something
  • Antonym: devaluation - reduction in the official value of something

Etymology

From re- (again) + valuation (from Latin 'valere', to be worth).
