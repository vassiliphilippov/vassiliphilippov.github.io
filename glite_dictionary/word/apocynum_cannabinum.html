Apocynum cannabinum

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɑːsɪnəm ˌkænəˈbɪnəm / (AmE), / əˈpɒsɪnəm ˌkanəˈbɪnəm / (BrE)
A North American plant, also known as Canadian dogbane or Indian hemp, that yields tough fiber used for cordage and has been used in traditional medicine for joint pain and inflammation.
a plant known as Canadian dogbane used for fiber and folk medicine

Examples

Native Americans once used Apocynum cannabinum fibers to make nets and ropes.
Traditional medicine sometimes employs extracts from Apocynum cannabinum for joint pain relief.
Botanists often study Apocynum cannabinum for its ecological role in North American habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Canadian dogbane - Common name for Apocynum cannabinum, a North American plant producing tough fiber
  • Synonym: Indian hemp - Another common name for Apocynum cannabinum, used for fiber and in folk medicine

Etymology

From Greek apokynon (dogbane) and Latin cannabis (hemp), referring to similarities to hemp plants.
