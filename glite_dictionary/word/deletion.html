deletion

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈliʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈliːʃən / (BrE)
the process by which sounds or words are omitted from spoken words or phrases
omission of sounds or words in speech

Examples

The deletion of the vowel sound made the word hard to recognize.
In rapid speech, the deletion of syllables is common in many languages.
Linguists study deletion patterns to understand language evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elision - the omission of a sound or syllable when speaking

Etymology

From Latin 'deletio' (an erasing), from 'deletus', past participle of 'delere' (to destroy, delete).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈliʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈliːʃən / (BrE)
the act of removing or erasing something that has been written or printed
removal of written or printed content

Examples

The editor made a deletion from the final draft of the manuscript.
Please indicate any deletion of information in the revised document.
After the deletion of the offensive word, the letter was approved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: removal - the act of taking something away or off
  • Antonym: addition - the act of adding something

Etymology

From Latin deletio (erasure), from delēre (to destroy, erase).

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈliʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈliːʃən / (BrE)
(Genetics) The loss or absence of one or more nucleotides from a chromosome, resulting in a genetic change.
loss of nucleotides in a chromosome
medical

Examples

A deletion in this gene can cause a serious genetic disorder.
Scientists discovered a deletion on chromosome 5 in the sample.
The disease was traced to a single base pair deletion in the DNA sequence.

Etymology

From Latin deletio (the act of erasing or destroying), from deletus, past participle of delere (to destroy).

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ dɪˈliʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈliːʃən / (BrE)
the act of omitting or removing something, especially words or sections, from a text during editing
removal of text in editing

Examples

The editor marked several deletions in the manuscript before it went to print.
Some deletions were necessary to shorten the introduction for publication.
His document contained so many deletions that the original meaning was lost.

Synset Examples

an editor's deletions frequently upset young authors
both parties agreed on the excision of the proposed clause

Related Terms

  • Synonym: omission - the act of leaving out or excluding something
  • Antonym: insertion - the act of adding something, especially into text

Etymology

From Latin deletio(n-) (removal, erasure), from deletus, past participle of delere (to destroy, erase).
