to misguide

1. VERB

/ mɪsˈɡaɪd / (AmE), / mɪsˈɡaɪd / (BrE)
to lead someone or advise someone wrongly; to give bad advice or cause someone to go in the wrong direction or make a wrong decision
to give bad advice; to lead astray

Examples

He did not intend to misguide you, but his advice was based on incorrect information.
Politicians who misguide the public can cause serious harm to society.
As a teacher, she would never intentionally misguide her students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mislead - to cause someone to have a wrong idea or impression
  • Antonym: guide - to show someone the way; to lead or direct

Etymology

From 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'guide' (to lead).

2. VERB

/ mɪsˈɡaɪd / (AmE), / mɪsˈɡaɪd / (BrE)
to lead or direct someone wrongly, either physically or in terms of advice
lead someone wrongly

Examples

The teacher did not intend to misguide her students, but her explanation was unclear.
False information on the internet can easily misguide people who are seeking answers.
His friends tried to warn him, but he was misguided by his own assumptions.

Synset Examples

The pedestrian misdirected the out-of-town driver

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mislead - cause someone to have a wrong idea or impression
  • Antonym: guide - show or direct someone to a destination

Etymology

From the prefix 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'guide', from Old French 'guider'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02004320-v
Synset POS: v
