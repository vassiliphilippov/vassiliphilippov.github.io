seid

1. NOUN

/ seɪd / (AmE), / seɪð / (BrE)
an ancient Norse form of sorcery or shamanistic magic practiced in Scandinavian society during the Late Iron Age and Viking Age
Norse shamanistic magic or sorcery

Examples

The practice of seid played an important role in Viking rituals and mythology.
Some modern neopagan groups attempt to reconstruct and perform seid ceremonies today.
Historical texts describe seid as involving trance states and prophecy.

Synset Examples

Of all the reconstructed systems of archaic magickal practice, Seiðr seems to be one of the most misunderstood.

Etymology

From Old Norse seiðr (sorcery, magical practice).
