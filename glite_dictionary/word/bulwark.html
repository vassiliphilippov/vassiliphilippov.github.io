bulwark

1. VERB

/ ˈbʊlˌwɑrk / (AmE), / ˈbʊl.wək / (BrE)
to defend or protect (someone or something) with or as if with a strong support or safeguard
to defend or protect strongly

Examples

The city attempted to bulwark itself against flooding with higher levees.
Laws and regulations bulwark the rights of employees in the workplace.
She bulwarked her argument with extensive research and evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to defend - to protect from harm or danger
  • Antonym: attack - to act aggressively against; to harm or try to defeat

Etymology

From Middle Dutch 'bolwerk' (logwork, rampart).

Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʊl.wɚk / (AmE), / ˈbʊl.wək / (BrE)
a defensive wall or embankment built to protect a place from attack
defensive wall or embankment

Examples

The ancient city was surrounded by a massive bulwark to keep invaders out.
Soldiers took cover behind the stone bulwark during the siege.
Engineers reinforced the crumbling bulwark to prevent it from collapsing.

Synset Examples

they stormed the ramparts of the city
they blew the trumpet and the walls came tumbling down

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rampart - a defensive wall of a castle or walled city
  • Synonym: barricade - a barrier erected for defense or obstruction
  • Synonym: parapet - a low protective wall along the edge of a roof, bridge, or balcony

Etymology

From Middle Dutch 'bolwerc' (defensive wall).

3. NOUN

/ ˈbʊl.wɚk / (AmE), / ˈbʊl.wək / (BrE)
a solid structure built along a shore or into the water to protect a beach or coastline from erosion by waves
structure protecting a shore from erosion

Examples

A stone bulwark was built to shield the village from the relentless tides.
The city reinforced the old bulwark to prevent further beach erosion during storms.
Construction of a concrete bulwark has helped preserve the coastline for decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breakwater - a barrier built out into a body of water to protect a coast or harbor from the force of waves

Etymology

From Middle English bulwerk, from Middle Dutch *bolwerk* (meaning 'plank work, defensive wall').

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ ˈbʊl.wɚk / (AmE), / ˈbʊl.wək / (BrE)
A strong wall or barrier built on the sides of a ship's deck to protect those on board from the sea or falling overboard.
protective ship railing or wall

Examples

The ship's bulwark offered protection from the crashing waves during the storm.
Crew members leaned against the bulwark as they looked out at the horizon.
Salt from the sea collected on the wooden bulwark over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parapet - a low protective wall along the edge of a roof, bridge, or balcony
  • Synonym: rampart - a broad embankment or defensive wall

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *bolwerc*, meaning 'a defensive wall.'
