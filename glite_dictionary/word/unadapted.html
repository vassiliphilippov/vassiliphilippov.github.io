unadapted

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈdæp.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈdæp.tɪd / (BrE)
not changed or modified in form or character for a specific purpose
not changed or modified

Examples

Many classic novels remain unadapted for film or television.
These animals are unadapted to the harsh conditions of the desert.
The script was left unadapted, preserving the original playwright's vision.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adapted - changed to be suitable for a new purpose or situation

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈdæp.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈdæp.tɪd / (BrE)
not having adjusted or become suited to new conditions or environments.
not having adapted; not adjusted to new conditions

Examples

Many species are now unadapted to the rapidly changing climate in their habitats.
The refugees remained unadapted to city life, struggling with new customs and language.
Because the film was left unadapted, much of the original story was lost in translation.

Synset Examples

several unadjusted refugees

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adapted - having adjusted to new conditions

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'adapted', from Latin 'adaptare' (to fit to).
