to stylise

1. VERB

/ ˈstaɪlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈstaɪlaɪz / (BrE)
to represent or design something according to a particular style, often in a way that is not realistic
represent in a conventional or artistic style
british

Examples

The artist chose to stylise the portrait, giving it exaggerated features for dramatic effect.
Many video games stylise their graphics to achieve a unique look rather than realism.
The director decided to stylise the setting to evoke the mood of 1950s noir films.

Synset Examples

a stylized female head

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stylize - to represent or design something according to a particular style (US spelling)

Etymology

From French *styliser*, derived from *style* (manner of expression).
