driver

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪvər / (AmE), / ˈdraɪvə / (BrE)
a person who operates a motor vehicle, such as a car, truck, or bus
person who operates a vehicle

Examples

The driver stopped the car at the red light.
After the accident, the police questioned the driver of the truck.
She became a licensed driver when she turned eighteen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motorist - person who drives a car, especially as a regular activity

2. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪvər / (AmE), / ˈdraɪvə / (BrE)
A golfer who hits the ball with a driver (the club used for long-distance shots at the start of a hole).
golfer using a driver club

Examples

As a driver, she consistently outdistances her competitors on the first tee.
The club champion is known as an excellent driver who sets up easy second shots.
Every aspiring driver wants to perfect their swing for maximum distance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: driver - a type of golf club used for long-distance shots

The new titanium driver helps increase both distance and accuracy.

4. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪvər / (AmE), / ˈdraɪvə / (BrE)
(Computing) A program that controls how a computer interacts with a peripheral device, such as a printer or graphics card.
program for controlling computer hardware

Examples

The printer would not work until I installed the correct driver on my computer.
Updating your graphics driver can often fix visual glitches in games.
Each device connected to the system requires its own driver for communication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: device driver - software that controls a particular type of device attached to a computer

5. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪvər / (AmE), / ˈdraɪvə / (BrE)
a person who drives animals that pull a vehicle, especially a carriage or wagon
person who drives animals pulling a vehicle

Examples

The driver urged the horses forward along the muddy road.
In the 19th century, a skilled driver was essential for controlling a stagecoach team.
The farm hired an experienced driver to handle the oxen that pulled the wagon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teamster - a person who drives a team of animals, especially horses or oxen

Etymology

From drive + -er, meaning one who drives.
