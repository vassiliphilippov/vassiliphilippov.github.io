lymphopenia

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪm.foʊˈpiː.ni.ə / (AmE), / ˌlɪm.fəʊˈpiː.ni.ə / (BrE)
a condition in which there is an abnormally low number of lymphocytes in the circulating blood
abnormally low lymphocyte count
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with lymphopenia after reviewing his blood test results.
Severe infections can sometimes lead to lymphopenia and weaken the immune response.
A low white blood cell count, such as lymphopenia, may indicate an underlying health issue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leukopenia - a reduction in the number of white cells in the blood

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'lympho-' (lymphocyte) and Greek 'penia' (poverty, deficiency).
