Lathyrus sativus

1. NOUN

/ ləˈθaɪrəs səˈtaɪvəs / (AmE), / ləˈθaɪrəs səˈtiːvəs / (BrE)
A species of European annual plant grown as forage, whose seeds are used for food in India and as animal feed elsewhere.
annual plant; seeds used for food and forage

Examples

Lathyrus sativus is commonly grown for its drought-resistant properties in marginal agricultural areas.
In India, the seeds of Lathyrus sativus are an important source of protein for rural communities.
Farmers often rely on Lathyrus sativus as a forage crop for their livestock during dry seasons.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Lathyrus' from Greek 'lathuros' (a kind of pulse), 'sativus' meaning 'cultivated'.
