dictated

1. ADJ

/ dɪkˈteɪtɪd / (AmE), / dɪkˈteɪtɪd / (BrE)
determined or imposed by someone in authority, rather than by choice or chance
imposed by authority

Examples

The company had to accept the dictated terms of the contract.
All meeting times were dictated by the manager, leaving no room for negotiation.
He resented the dictated schedule, preferring more flexibility in his work.

Synset Examples

date and place are already determined
the dictated terms of surrender
the time set for the launching

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imposed - set or established by authority without consent
  • Antonym: optional - not required; left to choice

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'dictate', from Latin 'dictare' (to say repeatedly, order).
