exclaiming

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈskleɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪkˈskleɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
an abrupt, excited, or emphatic utterance or instance of calling out
abrupt excited utterance

Examples

Her loud exclaiming drew everyone's attention in the busy room.
There was much exclaiming when the surprise was revealed.
He startled the crowd with his sudden exclaiming during the ceremony.

Synset Examples

she gave an exclamation of delight
there was much exclaiming over it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exclamation - a sudden cry or remark expressing surprise, strong emotion, or pain

Etymology

From Latin exclamare (to cry out), via exclaim + -ing.
