Boltonia

1. NOUN

/ boʊlˈtoʊniə / (AmE), / bɒlˈtəʊnɪə / (BrE)
A genus of tall, leafy perennial herbs native to eastern America and eastern Asia, bearing flowers that resemble asters.
a genus of tall aster-like herbs

Examples

Several species of Boltonia are cultivated for their attractive, daisy-like flowers.
The Boltonia blooms profusely in late summer, adding color to garden borders.
Botanists recently discovered a new species of Boltonia in eastern Asia.

Etymology

Named after James Bolton (1735–1799), English botanist and naturalist.
