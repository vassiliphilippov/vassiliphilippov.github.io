cockleburr

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːklˌbɜr / (AmE), / ˈkɒklˌbɜː / (BrE)
A coarse, weedy plant of the genus Xanthium, known for its spiny burrs that attach to animal fur and clothing.
weedy plant with spiny burrs (Xanthium genus)

Examples

The farmer spent hours pulling cockleburr plants from the cornfield.
After hiking, my socks were covered in sharp cockleburrs.
Livestock can suffer minor injuries when cockleburr gets tangled in their coats.

Etymology

From 'cockle' (a weed or shell) and 'burr' (a rough, prickly seed).
