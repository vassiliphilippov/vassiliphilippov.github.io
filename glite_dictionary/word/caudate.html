caudate

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɔːˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.deɪt / (BrE)
having a tail or a tail-like appendage; tailed.
having a tail or tail-like appendage

Examples

Many amphibians are classified as caudate because they possess well-developed tails.
The new species of fish was noted for its distinctly caudate appearance.
A caudate leaf has a tail-like extension at its tip.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tailless - lacking a tail or tail-like appendage

Etymology

From Latin caudatus, from cauda (tail).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.deɪt / (BrE)
a tail-shaped mass of gray matter forming part of the basal ganglia, located in a lateral ventricle of the brain
tail-shaped brain structure
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that damage to the caudate can affect a person's movement and behavior.
MRI scans revealed a lesion in the patient's left caudate nucleus.
Researchers are studying the role of the caudate in learning and memory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: putamen - another nucleus in the basal ganglia of the brain
  • Synonym: globus pallidus - a component of the basal ganglia involved in regulation of voluntary movement

Etymology

From Latin 'caudatus' (having a tail), from 'cauda' (tail).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05505392-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈkɔː.deɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.deɪt / (BrE)
(of a leaf or other structure) tapering gradually to a long, slender tail-like tip.
tapering to a tail-like tip (esp. leaves)

Examples

The orchid’s caudate leaves distinguish it from similar species.
Some tropical plants have caudate petals that extend into thin, pointed tails.
The botanist noted the caudate shape at the tip of each leaf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acuminate - tapering to a long point (used for leaf shapes)
  • Antonym: obtuse - having a blunt or rounded tip (biology/botany)

Etymology

From Latin 'caudatus' (tailed), from 'cauda' (tail).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02175070-s
Synset POS: s

4. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.deɪt / (BrE)
an amphibian of the order Caudata, typically resembling a lizard and comprising salamanders and newts
lizard-like amphibian (salamander or newt)

Examples

Many caudates inhabit moist woodlands where they are rarely seen during the day.
The research focused on the regeneration abilities in several species of caudate.
Both newts and salamanders are considered caudates due to their tails and body shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salamander - an amphibian with a lizard-like appearance
  • Antonym: anuran - an amphibian of the order Anura (frogs and toads), lacking a tail

Etymology

From Latin 'caudatus' (having a tail), from 'cauda' (tail).
