Primula veris

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɪmjələ ˈvɛrɪs / (AmE), / ˌprɪmjʊlə ˈvɛːrɪs / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant in the primrose family, commonly called cowslip, with fragrant yellow or occasionally purple flowers, native to Europe and often found in meadows and grassy places.
flowering meadow plant (cowslip)

Examples

The meadow was dotted with Primula veris blooming brightly in the early spring.
Many gardeners cultivate Primula veris for its delicate fragrance and cheerful yellow flowers.
In Britain, Primula veris is often seen growing wild along hedgerows and grassy banks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowslip - Common name for Primula veris, a yellow-flowered meadow plant
  • Degree Lower: primrose - A related plant species with pale yellow flowers, Primula vulgaris

Etymology

From Latin: 'primula' meaning 'little first one' (early blooming), 'veris' meaning 'of the spring'.
