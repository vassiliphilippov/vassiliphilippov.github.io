populist

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑː.pjə.lɪst / (AmE), / ˈpɒp.jʊ.lɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or seeks to represent the interests and rights of ordinary people, often in opposition to the established elite
person advocating for common people over elite

Examples

The populist promised to give a stronger voice to the working class during the election campaign.
Many analysts debated whether the new president was a genuine populist or simply exploiting popular frustration.
In recent years, several populists have gained prominence by challenging traditional political parties.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: elitist - a person who believes that society should be led by an elite
  • Synonym: demagogue - a political leader who seeks support by appealing to desires and prejudices rather than rational argument

Etymology

From French *populiste*, from Latin *populus* (people).
