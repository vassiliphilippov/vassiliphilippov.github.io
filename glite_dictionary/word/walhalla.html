Walhalla

1. NOUN

/ wɑlˈhɑlə / (AmE), / wælˈhɑːlə / (BrE)
(in Norse mythology) the great hall where the souls of slain heroes are received by Odin.
hall for slain heroes in Norse mythology

Examples

In ancient sagas, brave warriors aspired to spend eternity in Walhalla after falling in battle.
The myth of Walhalla symbolizes honor and valor in Norse culture.
Tourists visited the museum designed to resemble Walhalla, intrigued by its mythological roots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Valhalla - the great hall in Norse mythology where slain warriors are received

Etymology

From Old Norse 'Valhöll', meaning 'hall of the slain'.
