sapphic

1. ADJ

/ ˈsæfɪk / (AmE), / ˈsæfɪk / (BrE)
Relating to romantic or sexual attraction between women; of or pertaining to female homosexuality.
relating to female homosexuality

Examples

She feels a strong connection to sapphic literature and often seeks out novels with lesbian protagonists.
Many ancient poems that explore sapphic themes are attributed to Sappho of Lesbos.
The film was praised for its honest depiction of sapphic relationships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lesbian - A woman who is romantically or sexually attracted to other women

Etymology

From Late Latin Sapphicus, from Greek Sapphikos, after Sappho, the ancient Greek poet from Lesbos famously associated with expressions of female love.
