sublunar

1. ADJ

/ sʌbˈluːnɚ / (AmE), / sʌbˈluːnə / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of the earth or the world as opposed to the heavens; earthly or worldly.
earthly; relating to the world rather than the heavens

Examples

Philosophers have long debated the nature of sublunar affairs and their relationship to the divine.
The poet described love as a fleeting sublunar pleasure, easily lost with the passing of time.
She refused to let sublunar concerns overshadow her spiritual aspirations.

Synset Examples

transcendental motives for sublunary actions
fleeting sublunary pleasures
the nearest to an angelic being that treads this terrestrial ball

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrestrial - Relating to the earth; earthly; worldly
  • Antonym: celestial - Relating to the sky or heavens

Etymology

From Latin sublūnāris, from sub- (beneath) + lūna (moon).

2. ADJ

/ sʌbˈluː.nɚ / (AmE), / sʌbˈluː.nə / (BrE)
belonging to or situated in the region beneath the moon; pertaining to the earthly realm as opposed to the celestial
relating to the region beneath the moon

Examples

Philosophers once believed that everything in the sublunar world was subject to change and decay.
The ancient model divided the cosmos into the perfect heavens and the imperfect sublunar sphere.
He referred to the sublunar realm when discussing earthly affairs as distinct from the celestial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrestrial - relating to the earth or its inhabitants

Etymology

From Latin sublunaris, from sub- (below) + luna (moon).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02768218-a
Synset POS: a
