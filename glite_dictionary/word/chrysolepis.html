Chrysolepis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪsəˈlɛpɪs / (AmE), / ˌkrɪsəˈlɛpɪs / (BrE)
A small genus of evergreen trees and shrubs native to western North America, commonly known as golden chinkapins.
genus of evergreen trees and shrubs

Examples

The Chrysolepis genus includes the golden chinkapin, a tree valued for its hard wood.
Scientists studied the distribution of Chrysolepis in the mountainous regions of California.
Both species in the Chrysolepis genus display evergreen leaves and golden-hued nuts.

Etymology

From Greek 'chrysos' (gold) and 'lepis' (scale), referring to the golden scales on the fruit.

2. NOUN

/ kraɪsoʊˈlepɪs / (AmE), / krɪsəʊˈliːpɪs / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen trees or shrubs in the beech family, native to western North America, including the golden chinkapins.
a genus of evergreen trees (golden chinkapins)

Examples

Several species in the genus Chrysolepis are valued for their ornamental appeal.
The nuts produced by Chrysolepis trees are edible and were used by Native Americans.
Researchers studied the habitat of Chrysolepis to understand its ecological role in western forests.

Etymology

From Greek chrysos (gold) + lepis (scale), referring to the golden scales on the fruit.
