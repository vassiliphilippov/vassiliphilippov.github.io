dowel

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊəl / (AmE), / ˈdaʊəl / (BrE)
A cylindrical rod, usually made of wood, metal, or plastic, used for holding two pieces together by fitting into holes in both.
cylindrical fastener for joining parts

Examples

The shelves were joined together using wooden dowels for extra stability.
He inserted a metal dowel into the drilled holes to align the parts precisely.
Plastic dowels are often used in assembling flat-pack furniture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peg - a small cylindrical or tapered object used to fasten or secure something
  • Synonym: nail - a slender, pointed metal pin used for fastening pieces of wood together

Etymology

From Middle English 'doule' (part of a barrel), perhaps of Germanic origin.
