Lobelia cardinalis

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈbiliə kɑrˈdɪnəlɪs / (AmE), / ləˈbiːliə kɑːˈdɪnəlɪs / (BrE)
A North American plant species in the bellflower family, noted for its striking red flowers; commonly called cardinal flower.
North American plant with red flowers

Examples

The vibrant Lobelia cardinalis added a splash of red to the garden's border.
Many hummingbirds are attracted to the brilliant flowers of Lobelia cardinalis.
Botanists often study Lobelia cardinalis for its unique wetland habitat preferences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardinal flower - common name for Lobelia cardinalis

Etymology

Genus name from New Latin 'Lobelia' after the Flemish botanist Matthias de Lobel; species 'cardinalis' from Latin, referring to its cardinal-red flowers.
