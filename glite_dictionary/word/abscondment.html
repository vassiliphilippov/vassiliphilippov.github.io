abscondment

1. NOUN

/ əbˈskɑnd.mənt / (AmE), / əbˈskɒnd.mənt / (BrE)
the action of leaving secretly, especially to avoid arrest or prosecution
secret act of fleeing, often to avoid arrest

Examples

The sudden abscondment of the accountant shocked the entire company.
After his abscondment with the funds, investigators launched a nationwide search.
The court charged her in connection with the abscondment of sensitive documents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to abscond - to leave secretly and hide, often to escape the law

Etymology

From 'abscond' (to leave secretly), ultimately from Latin 'abscondere' (to hide away), with the English noun suffix '-ment'.
