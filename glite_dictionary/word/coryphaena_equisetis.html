Coryphaena equisetis

1. PROPN

/ kɔːˌɹɪfiˈænə ˌɛkwɪˈsiːtɪs / (AmE), / kɒˌrɪfiˈænə ˌɛkwɪˈsiːtɪs / (BrE)
a species of dolphinfish, commonly known as the pompano dolphinfish, found in warm ocean waters
species of dolphinfish (pompano dolphinfish)

Examples

The Coryphaena equisetis can be identified by its elongated body and vibrant coloration.
Scientists recently studied the migration patterns of Coryphaena equisetis in the Atlantic Ocean.
Commercial fishermen sometimes catch Coryphaena equisetis when targeting larger pelagic species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dolphinfish - common name for species in the genus Coryphaena

Etymology

From Greek koryphaina (a kind of fish) and Latin equisetis ('like a horse's tail', referring to the fin).
