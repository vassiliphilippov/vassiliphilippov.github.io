wahoo

1. NOUN

/ wɑːˈhuː / (AmE), / ˈwɑːhuː / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub (Euonymus atropurpureus) native to eastern North America, with purple seed capsules that enclose bright scarlet seeds.
North American shrub with purple capsules

Examples

The wahoo is often found growing in moist woodlands and along riverbanks.
In autumn, the wahoo produces striking purple seed capsules with red seeds.
Gardeners sometimes plant wahoo as an ornamental shrub for its colorful fruit.

Etymology

From Ojibwe *waagoo* (arrow-wood).

2. NOUN

/ wɑːˈhuː / (AmE), / wɑːˈhuː / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub or small tree (Euonymus atropurpureus) native to eastern North America, noted for its crimson pods and seeds.
a North American shrub with crimson pods

Examples

In autumn, the wahoo is easily recognized by its bright red fruit and colorful leaves.
The wahoo often grows along riverbanks and woodland edges in the eastern United States.
Gardeners value the wahoo for its ornamental crimson pods and vibrant autumn foliage.

Etymology

From an American Indian (Dakota) word for the plant.

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːhuː / (AmE), / ˈwɑːhuː / (BrE)
a large, fast-moving predatory fish (Acanthocybium solandri) found in tropical and subtropical seas worldwide, valued as a food and game fish.
large predatory marine fish

Examples

The chef grilled fresh wahoo caught that morning for the special of the day.
Anglers consider the wahoo one of the most thrilling fish to catch due to its speed.
We spotted a school of wahoo swimming near the coral reef during our dive.

Etymology

Probably from an American Spanish adaptation of the Carib name for the fish.
