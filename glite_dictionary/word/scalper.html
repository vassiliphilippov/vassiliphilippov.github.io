scalper

1. NOUN

/ ˈskælpɚ / (AmE), / ˈskælpə / (BrE)
a person who buys tickets or goods in order to resell them at a much higher price, especially when demand is high
someone reselling tickets at much higher prices

Examples

He bought concert tickets from a scalper outside the arena.
Scalpers were selling playoff seats for five times their original price.
Because the show was sold out, she turned to a scalper to get her tickets.

Synset Examples

he got theater tickets through a scalper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tout - someone who buys tickets to resell at a profit (chiefly British)
