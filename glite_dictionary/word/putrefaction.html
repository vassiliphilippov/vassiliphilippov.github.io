putrefaction

1. NOUN

/ ˌpjuːtrəˈfækʃən / (AmE), / ˌpjuːtrɪˈfækʃən / (BrE)
The process or state of decay or rotting in organic matter, typically producing a foul smell.
decay of organic matter with foul odor

Examples

The smell of putrefaction coming from the abandoned building was overwhelming.
Rapid putrefaction set in after the animal died in the summer heat.
Scientists studied the stages of putrefaction in decomposing organisms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decomposition - breakdown of organic substances, especially after death
  • Synonym: decay - the process of rotting or decomposing
  • Antonym: preservation - the act of keeping something from decaying

Etymology

From Latin 'putrefactio', from 'putrefacere' meaning to make rotten.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpjuːtrəˈfækʃən / (AmE), / ˌpjuːtrɪˈfækʃən / (BrE)
the process of decay or rotting in a body or other organic matter, typically caused by bacterial or fungal activity
decay caused by microorganisms

Examples

The smell of putrefaction made the abandoned house unbearable to enter.
Rapid putrefaction occurs if a dead animal is left in warm, humid conditions.
Scientists studied the bacteria responsible for putrefaction in organic material.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decay - the process of rotting or decomposition
  • Degree Lower: fermentation - chemical breakdown, especially by microorganisms, but not necessarily involving foul odors
  • Synonym: decomposition - the process of breaking down organic matter

Etymology

From Latin 'putrefactio', from 'putrefacere' meaning 'to make rotten'.

3. NOUN

/ ˌpjuːtrəˈfækʃən / (AmE), / ˌpjuːtrɪˈfækʃən / (BrE)
(formal) The state of moral corruption or perversion; moral decay and loss of virtue.
moral decay or corruption

Examples

Many critics believe that the novel is a reflection on the putrefaction of society's values.
The dictator's regime brought about the political and moral putrefaction of the nation.
Some historians argue that the putrefaction of ethical standards led to the downfall of the empire.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depravity - moral corruption; wickedness
  • Synonym: corruption - dishonest or immoral behavior
  • Antonym: virtue - moral excellence

Etymology

From Latin putrefactio (process of rotting), from putrefacere (to make rotten).
