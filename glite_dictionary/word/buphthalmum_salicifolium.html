Buphthalmum salicifolium

1. NOUN

/ bʌpˈθælməm səˌlɪsɪˈfoʊliəm / (AmE), / bʌpˈθælməm səˌlɪsɪˈfəʊliəm / (BrE)
A hairy perennial plant native to Eurasia, bearing deep yellow daisy-like flowers on slender, willowy stems, typically found in open woodland and rocky slopes.
Eurasian perennial plant with yellow daisies

Examples

Buphthalmum salicifolium can often be seen blooming along the rocky hillsides in late spring.
Gardeners appreciate Buphthalmum salicifolium for its vibrant yellow flowers and delicate stems.
The open woodland was dotted with patches of Buphthalmum salicifolium thriving in the sunlight.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Buphthalmum' from Greek 'bouphthalmos' (ox-eyed), and species name 'salicifolium' from Latin 'salix' (willow) and 'folium' (leaf).
