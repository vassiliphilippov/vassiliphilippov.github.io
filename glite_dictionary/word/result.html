result

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈzʌlt / (AmE), / rɪˈzʌlt / (BrE)
a consequence or outcome that happens as a result of an action, process, or event
outcome; consequence

Examples

The scientific result confirmed their hypothesis after months of testing.
We are still waiting for the official results of the election.
Her hard work produced excellent results in her final exams.

Synset Examples

he listened for the results on the radio

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consequence - something that follows as a result of an action or condition
  • Antonym: cause - the reason or motive for an action or event; what makes something happen
  • Synonym: effect - a change that is the result of an action or other cause

Etymology

From Latin 'resultare' (to spring back).

5. NOUN

A thing that happens or exists because of something else; the outcome or consequence of an action or event.
outcome or consequence of an action

Examples

The experiment was repeated to confirm the result.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consequence - a result or effect of an action or condition
  • Antonym: cause - that which makes something happen
