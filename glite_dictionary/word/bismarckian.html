Bismarckian

1. ADJ

/ bɪzˈmɑːrkiən / (AmE), / bɪzˈmɑːkiən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of Prince Otto von Bismarck or his policies, especially his style of pragmatic and forceful statecraft.
relating to Otto von Bismarck or his policies

Examples

The new government adopted a Bismarckian approach to foreign policy, emphasizing realpolitik over ideology.
Some historians praise the Bismarckian welfare reforms as foundational to the modern social state.
Her methods were described as Bismarckian, combining pragmatism with a willingness to use power assertively.

Etymology

From Bismarck (surname of Otto von Bismarck, 1815–1898), with the suffix -ian.
