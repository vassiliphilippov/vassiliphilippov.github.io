rickettsial

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈkɛtsiəl / (AmE), / rɪˈkɛtsiəl / (BrE)
relating to or caused by rickettsias, a group of bacteria that cause various diseases in humans and animals
caused by or relating to rickettsias
medical

Examples

Several rickettsial diseases are transmitted to humans through the bite of infected ticks or lice.
Prompt diagnosis and treatment of rickettsial infections can greatly improve patient outcomes.
The region reported a sudden increase in rickettsial fever cases during the summer.

Etymology

From New Latin Rickettsia (the bacterial genus), named after American pathologist H. T. Ricketts (1871–1910), plus the suffix -al.
