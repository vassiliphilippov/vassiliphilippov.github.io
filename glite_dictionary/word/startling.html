startling

1. ADJ

/ ˈstɑrtlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstɑːtlɪŋ / (BrE)
very surprising, shocking, or unexpected, often causing momentary shock or alarm
very surprising or shocking

Examples

The painting's startling colors immediately drew everyone's attention.
He made a startling announcement that left the audience in silence.
It was startling to see how quickly the storm approached the city.

Synset Examples

Sydney's startling new Opera House
startling news
startling earthquake shocks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surprising - causing surprise; unexpected
  • Synonym: shocking - causing intense surprise, disgust, or horror
  • Antonym: reassuring - making someone feel less worried or afraid

Etymology

From the verb 'startle', from Middle English 'startlen', frequentative of 'sterte' (to jump, leap).
