immunodeficiency

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌmjunəˌdɪˈfɪʃənsi / (AmE), / ɪˌmjuːnəʊdɪˈfɪʃənsi / (BrE)
A disorder in which the body's immune system is inadequate, resulting in reduced resistance to infections.
inadequate immune system disorder
medical

Examples

Primary immunodeficiency diseases are often inherited and affect various components of the immune system.
People with immunodeficiency are at higher risk for frequent and severe infections.
HIV is a virus that leads to immunodeficiency by attacking certain white blood cells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immunosuppression - deliberate reduction of immune response, often via drugs
  • Antonym: immunity - the body's ability to resist infections

Etymology

From Latin 'immunis' (exempt, immune) + 'deficientia' (lack, failure).
