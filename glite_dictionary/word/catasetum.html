Catasetum

1. NOUN

/ ˌkætəˈsiːtəm / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈsiːtəm / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American orchids with distinctive, usually showy male and female flowers borne on separate inflorescences.
genus of tropical American orchids

Examples

The Catasetum orchid is prized by collectors for its unique flowering patterns.
Botanists are fascinated by the sexual dimorphism found in Catasetum species.
A rare Catasetum bloomed in the botanical garden this summer, attracting many visitors.

Etymology

From Greek 'kata', meaning down, and 'seta', meaning bristle, referring to parts of the flower.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkætəˈsiːtəm / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈsiːtəm / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American orchids with showy male and female flowers typically borne on separate inflorescences.
genus of tropical American orchids

Examples

The Catasetum orchid is prized for its unusual and dramatic flowers.
Many collectors specialize in growing rare species of Catasetum.
Male and female flowers of Catasetum differ greatly in appearance and location.

Etymology

From Greek 'kata' (down) + 'seta' (bristle), referring to the shape of certain plant parts.
