quetzal

1. NOUN

/ kɛtˈsɑl / (AmE), / kɛtˈsɑːl / (BrE)
the basic unit of currency in Guatemala, equal to 100 centavos.
Guatemalan unit of currency

Examples

The souvenir cost fifty quetzals at the market in Guatemala City.
She exchanged her dollars for quetzals upon arriving in Guatemala.
The price is listed as 120 quetzals on the receipt.

Etymology

From Spanish 'quetzal', named after the national bird of Guatemala.

2. NOUN

/ kɛtˈsɑl / (AmE), / kɛtˈsɑːl / (BrE)
a large tropical bird of Central and South America, noted for its brilliant golden-green and scarlet plumage, especially the male with long tail feathers
brightly colored Central/South American bird

Examples

The mythical beauty of the quetzal has made it a symbol of freedom in many Central American cultures.
Birdwatchers traveled deep into the rainforest hoping to catch a glimpse of a quetzal among the branches.
The male quetzal displays especially long, iridescent tail feathers during the breeding season.

Etymology

From Nahuatl *quetzalli* meaning 'tail feather' or 'precious thing.'

3. NOUN

/ kɛtˈsɑl / (AmE), / kɛtˈsɑːl / (BrE)
a large, brightly colored bird of Central and South America, with golden-green and scarlet plumage, belonging to the trogon family
colorful Central and South American bird

Examples

The quetzal is considered a symbol of freedom in Guatemala, where it is also the national bird.
We spotted a vibrant quetzal perched on a mossy tree branch during our rainforest trek.
Because of its stunning feathers, the quetzal has been revered in local mythology for centuries.

Etymology

From Nahuatl *quetzalli* (tail feather of the quetzal, precious thing).
