Quercus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs / (BrE)
A genus of trees and shrubs commonly known as oaks, native to the northern hemisphere, including many species of economic and ecological importance.
genus of trees known as oaks

Examples

The white oak, Quercus alba, is native to eastern North America.
Several species within the Quercus genus are valued for their timber.
Botanists discovered a new Quercus species in the remote forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - a tree or shrub of the genus Quercus

Etymology

From Latin *Quercus* (oak).
