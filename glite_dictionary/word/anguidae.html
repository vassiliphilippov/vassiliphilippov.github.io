Anguidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈænɡjuːˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡjʊɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of lizards that includes slowworms, glass lizards, and alligator lizards
family of lizards including slowworms and glass lizards

Examples

Biologists have identified more than eighty species within the Anguidae family.
The glass lizard, a member of the Anguidae, has a long, legless body resembling a snake.
The distribution of Anguidae species spans North America, Europe, and Asia.

Etymology

From modern Latin Anguidae, from genus name Anguis (meaning 'snake' in Latin) + -idae (zoological family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæŋɡjuˌdaɪ / (AmE), / ˈaŋɡjuːˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of lizards that includes slowworms, glass lizards, and alligator lizards
a family of lizards (slowworms, glass lizards, etc.)

Examples

The Anguidae family includes both legless slowworms and species with fully developed limbs.
Scientists have observed unique scale patterns among members of the Anguidae.
Alligator lizards, which belong to the Anguidae, are common in parts of North America.

Etymology

From New Latin Anguidae, from Anguis (a genus of slowworm) + -idae (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).
