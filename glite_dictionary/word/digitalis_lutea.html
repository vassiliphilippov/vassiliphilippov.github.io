Digitalis lutea

1. NOUN

/ dɪdʒɪˈteɪlɪs ˈluːtiə / (AmE), / dɪdʒɪˈteɪlɪs ˈluːtɪə / (BrE)
A European wildflower species in the foxglove genus, with yellow tubular flowers.
European yellow-flowered foxglove

Examples

The garden features a rare specimen of Digitalis lutea among many other wildflowers.
Many herbalists study Digitalis lutea for its medicinal properties and distinctive appearance.
Digitalis lutea blooms attract bees and butterflies throughout the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foxglove - A plant of the genus Digitalis, known for its tall spikes of tubular flowers

Etymology

From Latin, 'Digitalis' (the foxglove genus), and 'lutea' meaning 'yellow'.
