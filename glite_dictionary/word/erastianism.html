Erastianism

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈræstiənˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˈræstɪənɪzəm / (BrE)
the doctrine that the state should have authority over the church in ecclesiastical affairs
state supremacy over church matters

Examples

Many Enlightenment thinkers debated the principles of Erastianism and its impact on church autonomy.
Some argued that Erastianism threatened religious freedom by placing all decisions in the hands of the state.
Modern church-state relations in Europe can still reflect elements of Erastianism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Caesaropapism - the doctrine combining the power of secular government with religious authority

Etymology

From the name of Swiss theologian Thomas Erastus (1524–1583), who advocated these views.
