gynecocracy

1. NOUN

/ ˌgaɪnəˈkɑkrəsi / (AmE), / ˌgʌɪnɪˈkɒkrəsi / (BrE)
A government or political system ruled by women.
government ruled by women

Examples

In ancient legends, the island nation was famous for its gynecocracy and female warriors.
Many anthropologists debate whether true gynecocracies have ever existed in history.
The novel imagines a future world transformed by a global gynecocracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: matriarchy - a social system in which females hold primary power
  • Antonym: patriarchy - a social system in which males hold primary power

Etymology

From Greek 'gynē' (woman) and 'kratos' (power, rule).
