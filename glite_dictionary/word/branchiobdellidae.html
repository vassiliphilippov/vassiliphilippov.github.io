Branchiobdellidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌbræŋkiˌɑbˌdɛlˈɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌbraŋkɪˌɒbdɛˈlɪdɪiː / (BrE)
A family of small annelid worms characterized by the posterior end forming an adhesive sucker, often found as ectosymbionts on crayfish and formerly considered modified leeches.
family of small annelid worms with adhesive sucker
medical

Examples

Researchers found several Branchiobdellidae attached to the crayfish in the stream.
The unique adhesive sucker is a defining feature of Branchiobdellidae.
Members of the Branchiobdellidae family play an important role in crayfish health.

Etymology

From Greek *branchia* (gill) + *bdella* (leech) + Latin -idae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌbræn.ki.əbˌdɛl.ɪˈdiː / (AmE), / ˌbran.kɪ.ɒbˌdɛl.ɪˈdiː / (BrE)
A family of small annelid worms having the posterior end modified into an adhesive sucker, formerly considered to be modified leeches.
family of small annelid worms

Examples

The Branchiobdellidae are mostly found living on the gills and shells of freshwater crayfish.
Researchers observed a mutualistic relationship between Branchiobdellidae worms and their crayfish hosts.
Unlike true leeches, members of the Branchiobdellidae possess a distinct adhesive sucker at the rear end.

Etymology

From New Latin Branchiobdellidae, from Branchiobdella (type genus) + -idae (family suffix).
