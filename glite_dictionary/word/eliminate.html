to eliminate

1. VERB

/ ɪˈlɪməˌneɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to kill or remove a large number of people or things, often deliberately
kill or remove in large numbers

Examples

The dictator ordered the army to eliminate all opposition in the region.
The disease threatened to eliminate entire communities if left unchecked.
New policies were introduced to eliminate vermin that had infested the farmlands.

Synset Examples

the plague wiped out an entire population

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exterminate - to destroy completely, especially a population or group

Etymology

From Latin eliminare (to turn out of doors), from e- (out) + limen (threshold).
