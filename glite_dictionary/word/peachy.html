peachy

1. ADJ

/ ˈpiːtʃi / (AmE), / ˈpiːtʃi / (BrE)
Very good; excellent.
excellent; very good
informal american

Examples

Everything has been peachy since she started her new job.
The weather was peachy for our picnic yesterday.
He gave a peachy performance in the school play.

Synset Examples

he did a bully job
a neat sports car
had a great time at the party

we had a grand old time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excellent - of the highest quality; extremely good
  • Antonym: awful - very bad or unpleasant

Etymology

From 'peach' (a desirable fruit), with '-y' forming an adjective; American slang (early 20th century).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpiːtʃi / (AmE), / ˈpiːtʃi / (BrE)
Having a color similar to that of a ripe peach; soft pinkish-orange.
resembling a peach in color

Examples

The walls were painted a lovely peachy shade that brightened the room.
She chose a dress in a soft peachy hue for the summer wedding.
The morning sky glowed with peachy tones as the sun began to rise.

Etymology

From 'peach'+-y, meaning 'like a peach' in appearance or color.
