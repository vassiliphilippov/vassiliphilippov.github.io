substantially

1. ADV

/ səbˈstænʃəli / (AmE), / səbˈstanʃəli / (BrE)
In a sturdy, solid, or substantial manner; strongly or robustly.
in a solid or sturdy manner

Examples

The bridge was substantially reinforced to withstand strong winds.
Their new home is substantially constructed, with thick stone walls and a solid roof.
The company headquarters was substantially renovated to meet modern safety standards.

Synset Examples

the house was substantially built

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strongly - in a strong or forceful way
  • Antonym: fragilely - in a fragile or weak manner

Etymology

From 'substantial' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'substantia' (substance).

2. ADV

/ səbˈstæn.ʃəli / (AmE), / səbˈstæn.ʃəli / (BrE)
to a great extent or degree; considerably
to a great extent or degree

Examples

The company's profits increased substantially in the last quarter.
Her performance has improved substantially since she started training regularly.
The rules have changed substantially over the past decade.

Synset Examples

I'm afraid the film was well over budget
painting the room white made it seem considerably (or substantially) larger
the house has fallen considerably in value
the price went up substantially

Related Terms

  • Synonym: considerably - to a noteworthy or large degree
  • Degree Lower: marginally - to a very small extent or degree

Etymology

From Latin substantialis (substantial) + -ly.
