Ruhr

1. PROPN

/ ɹʊɹ / (AmE), / ɹʊə / (BrE)
a river in western Germany that is a tributary of the Rhine, famous for the industrial region along its valley.
river in western Germany; tributary of the Rhine

Examples

The Ruhr played a central role in Germany's industrial development.
Many coal mines were once located along the Ruhr.
The Allies occupied the Ruhr after World War I to enforce reparations.

Etymology

From German Ruhr, the name of the river.

2. PROPN

/ ɹʊɚ / (AmE), / ɹʊə / (BrE)
a major industrial and coal mining region in the valley of the Ruhr river in northwestern Germany
industrial region of Germany

Examples

The Ruhr has long been considered the industrial heartland of Germany.
During the early 20th century, the Ruhr region became a focal point for coal and steel production.
After World War I, the Allies occupied the Ruhr to force Germany to pay reparations.

Etymology

From the name of the Ruhr river in Germany.
