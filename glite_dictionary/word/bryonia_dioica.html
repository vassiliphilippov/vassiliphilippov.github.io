Bryonia dioica

1. NOUN

/ braɪˌoʊni.ə daɪˈoʊɪkə / (AmE), / brɪˌəʊniːə daɪˈəʊɪkə / (BrE)
A climbing perennial plant of the gourd family, with fleshy roots, pale green flowers, and small red berries, native to Europe, North Africa, and western Asia.
a climbing plant with red berries, native to Europe and Asia

Examples

Bryonia dioica is often found growing wild in hedgerows across southern England.
The small red berries of Bryonia dioica are considered toxic if ingested.
Gardeners sometimes struggle to control the rapid spread of Bryonia dioica in their plots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white bryony - a common name for Bryonia dioica

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Bryonia (the genus) and dioica, meaning ‘dioecious’ (having male and female flowers on separate plants).
