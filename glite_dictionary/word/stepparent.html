stepparent

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛpˌpɛrənt / (AmE), / ˈstɛpˌpeərənt / (BrE)
a person who is married to your parent but is not your biological father or mother
spouse of a parent who is not a biological parent

Examples

My stepparent helps me with my homework every evening.
After the wedding, Emily struggled at first to get along with her new stepparent.
Many children develop strong bonds with a stepparent over time.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: biological parent - a person's natural father or mother
  • Synonym: stepchild - a child of one's spouse from a previous relationship

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'step-' (from Old English 'steop-', meaning 'orphaned' or 'bereaved') + 'parent'.
