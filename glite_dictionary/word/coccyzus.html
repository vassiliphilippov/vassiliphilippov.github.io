Coccyzus

1. NOUN

/ kɑkˈsaɪzəs / (AmE), / kɒkˈsɪzəs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the cuckoo family (Cuculidae), which includes several species of cuckoos native to the Americas.
genus of American cuckoos

Examples

The Coccyzus genus includes well-known North American birds such as the yellow-billed cuckoo.
Researchers observed several members of Coccyzus during their field study in Central America.
Many species within the genus Coccyzus migrate long distances between their breeding and wintering grounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuckoo - a bird known for laying its eggs in the nests of other birds

Etymology

From Greek kokkyzō (to cry cuckoo), reflecting the typical call of the cuckoo.
