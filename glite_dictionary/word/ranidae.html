Ranidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈrænɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈrænɪdiː / (BrE)
A large family of frogs commonly referred to as true frogs, found almost worldwide.
family of true frogs

Examples

The biology textbook describes Ranidae as one of the most diverse families of frogs.
Scientists have identified hundreds of Ranidae species on different continents.
Members of the Ranidae family are often distinguished by their long legs and smooth skin.

Etymology

From New Latin Ranidae, from Latin rana (frog) + -idae (zoological family suffix).
