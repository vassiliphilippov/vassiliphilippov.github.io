Asclepiadaceae

1. NOUN

/ əˌskliːpiəˈdeɪsi.iː / (AmE), / əˌskliːpɪəˈdeɪsɪ.iː / (BrE)
a large family of mainly tropical and subtropical herbs and shrubs, most of which have milky sap, formerly classified under the order Gentianales
family of herbs and shrubs with milky sap
medical

Examples

The family Asclepiadaceae includes many species with medicinal uses.
Many plants belonging to the Asclepiadaceae family exude a characteristic milky sap when cut.
Recent classifications have moved most Asclepiadaceae species into the Apocynaceae family.

Etymology

From modern Latin Asclepiadaceae, based on Asclepias (type genus), from Greek Asklēpiós (Asclepius, god of medicine).

2. NOUN

/ əˌskliːpiəˈdeɪsiːˌiː / (AmE), / əˌskliːpɪəˈdeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of widely distributed herbs and shrubs, mostly with milky sap, formerly classified in its own family but now usually included in Apocynaceae
plant family of mostly milky-sapped herbs and shrubs

Examples

Many members of the Asclepiadaceae family produce a characteristic milky sap.
The Asclepiadaceae include plants such as milkweed and other climbing vines.
Botanists now often classify Asclepiadaceae as a subfamily within Apocynaceae.

Etymology

From New Latin Asclepiadaceae, based on the genus Asclepias, named after Asclepius, Greek god of healing.
