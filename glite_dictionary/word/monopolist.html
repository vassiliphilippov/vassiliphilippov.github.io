monopolist

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɑː.pə.lɪst / (AmE), / məˈnɒp.əlɪst / (BrE)
a person or organization that has exclusive control over the supply or trade of a commodity or service
person or group with exclusive control in a market

Examples

The oil monopolist dictated prices to the entire region for decades.
A true monopolist faces little competition in the market.
Regulators sought to break up the monopolist company to encourage fair trade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monopoly - exclusive control by one entity over a market or industry
  • Antonym: competitor - a person or organization that competes against others, especially in business

Etymology

From French *monopoliste*, from Latin *monopolium*, from Greek *monopōlion* (‘single sale’).
