Ixodes ricinus

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈzoʊdiːz rɪˈsaɪnəs / (AmE), / ɪkˈsəʊdiːz rɪˈsɪnəs / (BrE)
a species of tick found in Europe, known for parasitizing mammals including sheep, cattle, and humans, and acting as a vector for Lyme disease and other illnesses
a European tick species and disease vector
medical

Examples

Ixodes ricinus is commonly found in wooded and grassy areas throughout Europe.
Sheep farmers monitor their flocks for ticks like Ixodes ricinus, which can transmit louping ill.
Researchers study Ixodes ricinus to better understand the spread of Lyme disease.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining genus name Ixodes (Greek: 'tick') and Latin ricinus ('castor oil plant'), referring to its shape.
