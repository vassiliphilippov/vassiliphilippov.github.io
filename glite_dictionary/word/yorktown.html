Yorktown

1. PROPN

/ ˈjɔrk.taʊn / (AmE), / ˈjɔːk.taʊn / (BrE)
A town in Virginia, USA, site of the decisive 1781 battle at which British forces, led by Cornwallis, surrendered to American and French troops, effectively ending the American Revolution.
site of decisive battle ending American Revolution

Examples

The Battle of Yorktown is considered one of the most significant events in American history.
Cornwallis surrendered his troops at Yorktown, marking the end of the Revolutionary War.
Every year, historians gather at Yorktown to commemorate the surrender and its lasting impact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saratoga - site of a major battle in the American Revolution

2. PROPN

/ ˈjɔrkˌtaʊn / (AmE), / ˈjɔːkˌtaʊn / (BrE)
A historic village in southeastern Virginia, United States, known as the site of the final major battle of the American Revolution in 1781.
historic village; Revolutionary War battle site

Examples

The surrender at Yorktown marked the end of major fighting in the American Revolutionary War.
Many tourists visit Yorktown to learn about its pivotal role in American history.
During our trip to Virginia, we toured the historic sites of Yorktown.

Etymology

Named after York, a city in northern England.
