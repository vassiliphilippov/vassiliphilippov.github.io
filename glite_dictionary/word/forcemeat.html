forcemeat

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrsˌmit / (AmE), / ˈfɔːsˌmiːt / (BrE)
A mixture of finely ground or chopped meat, often combined with other ingredients such as vegetables, fat, seasonings, and sometimes bread or eggs, used as stuffing or as a base for sausages, pâtés, and similar dishes.
mixture of finely ground meat and other ingredients

Examples

The chef prepared a delicate forcemeat to stuff the chicken breast for the main course.
Traditional sausages are often made with a seasoned forcemeat consisting of pork and herbs.
He added pistachios and truffles to the forcemeat, giving the terrine a rich flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pâté - a mixture of finely chopped or pureed meat, often served as a spread

Etymology

From French 'farce' (stuffing) + meat.
