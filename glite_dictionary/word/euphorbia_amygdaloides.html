Euphorbia amygdaloides

1. NOUN

/ juːˈfɔrbiə æˌmɪɡdəˈlɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / juːˈfɔːbɪə æˌmɪɡdəˈlɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
a European perennial herb with greenish-yellow terminal flower clusters, commonly known as wood spurge
species of European perennial herb (wood spurge)

Examples

Euphorbia amygdaloides is often found in shady woodland areas throughout Europe.
Gardeners appreciate Euphorbia amygdaloides for its attractive evergreen foliage and yellow flower clusters.
Botanists recorded several new populations of Euphorbia amygdaloides in the national park.

Etymology

From genus name Euphorbia and specific epithet from Latin 'amygdaloides' (almond-shaped).
