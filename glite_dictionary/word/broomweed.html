broomweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈbruːmˌwiːd / (AmE), / ˈbruːmˌwiːd / (BrE)
An annual plant of the southwestern United States, with rigid woody branches, sticky foliage, and yellow flowers.
a plant with woody branches and yellow flowers

Examples

The fields were dotted with clusters of broomweed swaying in the breeze.
Ranchers often consider broomweed a nuisance because it competes with native grasses.
After the summer rains, broomweed bursts into bright yellow bloom across the prairie.
