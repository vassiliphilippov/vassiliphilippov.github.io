bolo

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊloʊ / (AmE), / ˈbəʊləʊ / (BrE)
a long, heavy knife with a single edge; originally from the Philippines, used as a tool and weapon
a heavy Philippine knife

Examples

He carried a bolo to help clear a path through the thick jungle.
Farmers in the region still use the bolo for cutting crops and wood.
The museum displayed a traditional bolo among other Philippine tools.

Etymology

From Philippine Spanish 'bolo', from Tagalog 'bolo' (knife, machete).

2. NOUN

/ ˈboʊloʊ / (AmE), / ˈbəʊləʊ / (BrE)
a cord fastened around the neck with an ornamental clasp, worn as a necktie, especially in Western fashion
cord necktie with ornamental clasp

Examples

He wore a silver bolo with a turquoise clasp to the wedding.
The cowboy adjusted his bolo before stepping onto the stage.
Western wear often features a bolo instead of a traditional necktie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: necktie - a piece of cloth worn around the neck, tied in a knot or bow

Etymology

Shortened from 'bolo tie', first popularized in the American Southwest in the mid-20th century.
