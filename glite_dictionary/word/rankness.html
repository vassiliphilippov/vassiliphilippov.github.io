rankness

1. NOUN

/ ˈræŋknəs / (AmE), / ˈræŋknəs / (BrE)
The quality of producing abundant and vigorous growth, especially of vegetation.
luxuriant or excessive growth

Examples

The rankness of the garden after weeks of rain made it hard to find the path.
After years of neglect, rankness had overtaken the once carefully trimmed lawn.
Farmers must sometimes fight the rankness of weeds that thrive in fertile soil.

Synset Examples

he praised the richness of the soil
weeds lovely in their rankness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luxuriance - the property of being lush and rich in growth
  • Antonym: barrenness - the state of being unproductive or infertile

Etymology

From 'rank' (luxuriant, overgrown) + -ness.

2. NOUN

/ ˈræŋknəs / (AmE), / ˈræŋknəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having a strong, offensive, or unpleasant smell
strong, offensive smell (quality)

Examples

The rankness of rotting garbage filled the alley with an unbearable odor.
After weeks of neglect, the rankness of the locker room became obvious to everyone.
The rankness of the swamp air made breathing deeply almost impossible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stench - a very unpleasant smell
  • Antonym: fragrance - a pleasant, sweet smell

Etymology

From Middle English 'rank' (strong-smelling, offensive) + '-ness' (forming nouns of quality).
