halfpenny

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪpni, ˈhæfˌpɛni / (AmE), / ˈheɪpni, ˈhæfˌpɛni / (BrE)
a former British coin worth half of a penny, used in the United Kingdom until 1984
former British coin worth half a penny
british archaic

Examples

He paid with a halfpenny he found on the street.
Back then, a loaf of bread cost only a halfpenny.
She collected old halfpennies as a hobby.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: penny - a small British coin worth one cent or one penny

Etymology

From Middle English halfpeni, from Old English healfpening, meaning 'half penny.'
