embrasure

1. NOUN

/ ɛmˈbreɪʒər / (AmE), / ɪmˈbreɪʒə / (BrE)
an opening in a wall, especially of a fortification or armored vehicle, designed for firing weapons through
a firing opening in a wall or vehicle

Examples

The soldiers took cover behind the thick walls, firing through the embrasure at the approaching enemy.
A cannon protruded from the embrasure in the fortress, ready to repel any attackers.
The tank's embrasure offered the crew protection while allowing them to observe the battlefield.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loophole - a narrow opening in a wall for firing through

Etymology

From French 'embrasure' (opening, slit), from 'embraser' (to widen an opening).

2. NOUN

/ ɛmˈbreɪʒər / (AmE), / ɪmˈbreɪʒə / (BrE)
a small opening or slit in a wall, especially in fortifications, designed for observation or to allow weapons to be fired through
small opening in a wall for observation or defense

Examples

The guard watched the courtyard through the narrow embrasure in the wall.
Light filtered softly into the room through the stone embrasure above the staircase.
During the siege, soldiers took aim at the enemy from every embrasure in the fort.

Synset Examples

We looked through the embrasure to spot the enemy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loophole - a narrow opening in a wall, especially for firing through

Etymology

From French embrasure, from embraser (to widen an opening).
