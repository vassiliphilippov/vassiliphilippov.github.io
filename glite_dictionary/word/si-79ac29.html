Si

1. NOUN

/ siː aɪ / (AmE), / siː aɪ / (BrE)
The chemical symbol for silicon, a tetravalent nonmetallic element that is abundant in the earth's crust and used especially as a semiconductor.
chemical symbol for silicon

Examples

The microchip contains large amounts of Si for its semiconducting properties.
Pure Si wafers are essential for the electronics industry.
Sand is primarily made up of Si, in the form of silicon dioxide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silicon - a chemical element with symbol Si and atomic number 14

Etymology

From the International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry (IUPAC) symbol for silicon, from Latin 'silex' (flint).
