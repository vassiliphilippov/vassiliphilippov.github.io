input

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌpʊt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.pʊt / (BrE)
something, such as information or an event, that stimulates action or response; a contribution that influences a process or outcome.
stimulating information or event

Examples

The teacher welcomed student input on classroom rules to encourage participation.
External input from experts helped the committee reach a better decision.
His research provided valuable input for the development of the new policy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stimulus - something that causes a reaction or response
  • Antonym: output - the result or product of a process, especially after 'input' is provided

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (in) + 'put' (Old English 'putian', to thrust).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05836008-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌpʊt / (AmE), / ˈɪnpʊt / (BrE)
a statement or piece of information, advice, or opinion that is given to help or influence a process or decision
advice or information given

Examples

We encourage everyone to share their input during the meeting.
Her input was essential in making the final decision.
Teachers value input from parents about their children's progress.

Synset Examples

from time to time she contributed a personal comment on his account
we would appreciate input from our users on how we can improve our software

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feedback - information or opinions about something, used for improvement

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (into) + 'put' (from 'putare', to think, consider).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06777755-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌpʊt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.pʊt / (BrE)
a signal or piece of data that is sent into an electronic system for processing
signal or data sent into a system

Examples

The audio input on the computer is not working.
The engineer checked the sensor input to diagnose the problem.
You need to connect the microphone to the correct input port.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: output - the signal or data produced by a system
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07279488-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌpʊt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.pʊt / (BrE)
a resource, such as labor, material, or information, used in the process of producing goods or services
resource used in production

Examples

Labor is a crucial input in the manufacturing process.
Farmers monitor every input, including water and fertilizer, to maximize their crop yields.
Reducing the cost of each input can greatly increase the company's profits.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: output - the amount of something produced by a person, machine, or industry

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (into) + English 'put'; first used in the context of data and process in the mid-20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03578305-n
Synset POS: n

5. VERB

/ ˈɪnˌpʊt / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌpʊt / (BrE)
to enter (data or a program) into a computer for processing or storage
enter data or program into a computer

Examples

Please input your password to continue.
The data was manually input into the spreadsheet.
She spent the whole afternoon inputting survey results into the database.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enter - to put data into a computer or system

Etymology

Derived from 'input' (noun), originally meaning 'putting in'; computer sense from mid-20th century.
