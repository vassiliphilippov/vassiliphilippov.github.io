sightedness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪtɪdnɪs / (AmE), / ˈsaɪtɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the state or condition of having normal vision; the ability to see clearly without impairment
the state of having normal vision

Examples

Routine eye exams help ensure continued sightedness as people age.
Early treatment of infections is important for preserving sightedness.
Advocates promote awareness about the challenges faced by those lacking sightedness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: blindness - the condition of lacking sight

Etymology

Formed from 'sighted' (having sight) + '-ness' (state or condition).
