topognosia

1. NOUN

/ ˌtoʊ.pəɡˈnoʊ.zi.ə / (AmE), / ˌtɒp.ɒɡˈnəʊ.zi.ə / (BrE)
the ability to recognize the location of a touch or stimulus on the skin
recognition of a touch location on the skin
medical

Examples

Neurologists assessed the patient's topognosia by lightly touching different areas of his forearm.
Loss of topognosia can indicate certain types of nerve or brain damage.
Children usually develop topognosia as part of normal sensory and neurological growth.

Etymology

From Greek 'topos' (place) and 'gnōsis' (knowledge).
