ateliosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌætəˈlioʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌætɪlɪˈəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition marked by physical underdevelopment with normal intelligence, often resulting in dwarfism with childlike features.
physical underdevelopment with normal intelligence
medical

Examples

The child was diagnosed with ateliosis but showed normal cognitive development.
Despite having ateliosis, her learning abilities were the same as her peers.
Medical researchers have studied the genetic causes of ateliosis for decades.

Etymology

From Greek atelēs (incomplete) + -osis (condition).
