to inscribe

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈskraɪb / (AmE), / ɪnˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to address or write a brief, informal note in a book or work of literature, often less formal than a dedication
write a brief note in a book

Examples

The author agreed to inscribe a personal message on the title page of my book.
He would often inscribe short verses inside the covers of novels he gifted to friends.
She asked him to inscribe the poetry collection to her daughter, adding a few kind words.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: to dedicate - to address or inscribe a work formally to someone

Etymology

From Latin inscribere, from in- ‘in, on’ + scribere ‘to write’.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00888846-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈskraɪb / (AmE), / ɪnˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to write or sign one's name on something, especially as a formal or permanent record or dedication
write or sign (especially as a dedication)

Examples

The author agreed to inscribe my copy of his novel.
She asked the artist to inscribe her name on the painting.
He will inscribe a personal message inside each book he signs.

Synset Examples

The author autographed his book

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sign - to write one's signature on something
  • Synonym: autograph - to write one's signature, especially for a fan or in a book

Etymology

From Latin inscribere (to write on).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00999178-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈskraɪb / (AmE), / ɪnˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to write, engrave, or print words or symbols on something, especially as a lasting record
write or engrave as a permanent record

Examples

Visitors often inscribe their names in the guestbook at the museum.
The artist inscribed a personal message on the painting's frame.
Before burial, they inscribed the tombstone with a loving epitaph.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to engrave - to cut or carve text or designs into a surface
  • Synonym: to etch - to cut or carve a design or words into a surface
  • Antonym: to erase - to remove or rub out written or engraved words

Etymology

From Latin inscribere, from in- (in) + scribere (to write).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01003053-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ɪnˈskraɪb / (AmE), / ɪnˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to write, carve, or engrave words or symbols onto a surface, especially in a permanent way
write or carve words onto a surface

Examples

The artist carefully inscribed his signature onto the stone sculpture.
They inscribed their names on the inside of the wedding rings.
A dedication is inscribed at the base of the monument.

Synset Examples

engrave a pen
engraved the trophy cup with the winner's name
the lovers scratched their names into the bark of the tree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to engrave - to carve or cut a design or writing into a hard surface
  • Synonym: to etch - to cut or carve a design or writing into a surface, often using acid

Etymology

From Latin 'inscribere' (to write on), from 'in-' (in, on) + 'scribere' (to write).

5. VERB

/ ɪnˈskraɪb / (AmE), / ɪnˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to draw or construct a figure inside another so that it touches the boundary at as many points as possible, especially in geometry
draw a figure inside another, touching at many points

Examples

You can inscribe a circle in a triangle so that it touches all three sides.
In geometry, a regular polygon may be inscribed within a circle.
The task was to inscribe the largest possible square within the rectangle.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to circumscribe - draw around a figure so as to contain it within the boundary

Etymology

From Latin inscribere, from in- (in) + scribere (to write).

6. VERB

/ ɪnˈskraɪb / (AmE), / ɪnˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to register formally as a participant or member of a group or institution
formally register as a member

Examples

She was inscribed as a member of the association at the annual meeting.
New students must inscribe in the university register before attending classes.
He inscribed himself on the list of volunteers for the charity event.

Synset Examples

The party recruited many new members

Related Terms

  • Synonym: register - to enter or enroll formally, especially as a participant
  • Synonym: to enroll - to officially join or sign up for membership
  • Antonym: to expel - to remove from membership or participation

Etymology

From Latin 'inscribere' meaning 'to write upon'.
