matured

1. ADJ

/ məˈtʃʊrd / (AmE), / məˈtʃʊəd / (BrE)
(of a plan, opinion, or work) fully considered and perfected; brought to a state of completion after thoughtful development.
fully developed or perfected

Examples

The committee presented a matured proposal after several months of research and discussion.
Only after several drafts did the author consider his novel matured and ready for publication.
A matured plan should anticipate potential risks and outline clear steps for action.

Synset Examples

mature plans

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mature - fully developed, grown-up, or having reached an advanced stage
  • Antonym: immature - not fully developed or lacking maturity

Etymology

From the past participle of 'mature', from Latin 'maturare' (to ripen).

2. ADJ

/ məˈtʃʊrd, məˈtʃɜrd / (AmE), / məˈtjʊəd, məˈtjʊrd / (BrE)
Having reached full natural growth or development; fully ripe or at the height of bloom.
fully developed; at the height of bloom

Examples

The matured apples fell from the tree when the wind blew.
Each matured rose in the garden was at its most fragrant.
Only matured cheese develops a deep, complex flavor.

Synset Examples

a full-blown rose

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immature - not fully developed or ripe
  • Synonym: ripe - fully developed or matured, suitable for harvesting or eating

Etymology

Past participle of 'mature', from Latin 'maturus' (ripe, timely).
