Glis

1. NOUN

/ ɡlɪs / (AmE), / ɡlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of rodents in the family Gliridae, including the edible dormouse.
genus of dormice (rodents)

Examples

The edible dormouse belongs to the genus Glis and is found throughout Europe.
Scientists studied the hibernation patterns of several Glis species in the wild.
Members of Glis are noted for their long periods of torpor during winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gliridae - the dormouse family of rodents to which Glis belongs

Etymology

From Latin glīs (dormouse). Used as a genus name in zoological taxonomy.
