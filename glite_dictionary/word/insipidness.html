insipidness

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɪpɪdnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪpɪdnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being dull, lacking in spirit, flavor, or interest.
dullness or lack of interest

Examples

The insipidness of the movie's plot disappointed most of the audience.
Critics complained about the insipidness of her writing style in the new novel.
He was frustrated by the insipidness of the conversation at the dinner party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insipid - lacking flavor or interest
  • Antonym: vividness - the quality of being lively or stimulating

Etymology

From Latin insipidus (tasteless, without flavor) via insipid + -ness.

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɪp.ɪd.nəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪp.ɪd.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality of lacking flavor, zest, or interest; dullness or blandness.
the quality of being dull or flavorless

Examples

The insipidness of the soup left everyone longing for something more flavorful.
Critics blamed the film’s failure on its insipidness and lack of originality.
She was disappointed by the insipidness of the conversation at the dinner party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blandness - the quality of being uninteresting or lacking strong flavor
  • Antonym: vividness - the quality of being striking and full of life or color

Etymology

From 'insipid' + '-ness'. 'Insipid' is from Latin 'insipidus', meaning tasteless.
