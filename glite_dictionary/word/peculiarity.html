peculiarity

1. NOUN

/ pɪˌkjulˈɛrəti / (AmE), / pɪˌkjuːlɪˈærɪti / (BrE)
an unusual or distinctive characteristic or quality, especially one that is odd or unusual
odd or distinctive characteristic

Examples

His habit of wearing mismatched socks is just one peculiarity that makes him memorable.
Each region of the country has its own peculiarity when it comes to traditional foods.
The scientist was fascinated by the peculiarity she observed in the data.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quirk - a peculiar behavioral habit
  • Antonym: normality - the state of being normal or usual

Etymology

From Latin 'peculiaris' (of private property, special), via Old French and Middle English.

2. NOUN

/ pɪˌkjulˈɚəti / (AmE), / pɪˌkjuːliˈærəti / (BrE)
a feature or quality that makes someone or something different or unusual from others
a distinguishing or unusual feature

Examples

His habit of collecting teaspoons is a peculiarity that amuses his friends.
One peculiarity of the painting is the use of bright, unexpected colors.
The dialect has many peculiarities not found in standard English.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: characteristic - a typical feature or quality of someone or something
  • Antonym: normality - the state of being normal or typical

Etymology

From Latin 'peculiaris' (of private property, special), via Late Latin 'peculiaritas'.

3. NOUN

/ pɪˌkjuljˈærəti / (AmE), / pɪˌkjuːliˈærɪti / (BrE)
an unusual or distinctive feature or detail; the quality of being peculiar or distinctive
an unusual or distinctive feature

Examples

The old house had many peculiarities, including secret doors and oddly-shaped rooms.
Her greatest peculiarity is her habit of talking to her plants every morning.
Each language has its own peculiarities that make it unique.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oddity - a strange or unusual thing
  • Antonym: normality - the state of being usual, typical, or expected

Etymology

From Latin 'peculiaritas', from 'peculiaris' (of private property, special).
