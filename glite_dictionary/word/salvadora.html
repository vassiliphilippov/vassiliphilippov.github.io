Salvadora

1. NOUN

/ sæl.vəˈdɔɹ.ə / (AmE), / sæl.vəˈdɔː.rə / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen trees or shrubs with drupaceous fruit, found from Africa through Arabia to India and China.
a genus of trees or shrubs native to Africa and Asia

Examples

The Salvadora genus includes the well-known toothbrush tree, Salvadora persica.
Botanists recently discovered a new species in the Salvadora genus growing in arid regions.
Local populations use the fruit of Salvadora shrubs as a traditional food source.

Etymology

From Latin Salvadora, the genus name, in honor of Juan Salvador, Spanish botanist.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsæl.vəˈdɔ.rə / (AmE), / ˌsal.vəˈdɔː.rə / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen trees or shrubs native to Africa, Arabia, India, and China, whose fruit is a drupe.
genus of evergreen trees or shrubs

Examples

The Salvadora genus includes several species used in traditional medicine across Africa and India.
Researchers conducted a field study on Salvadora shrubs growing in arid regions of Arabia.
The fruit produced by Salvadora plants is a drupe, often consumed by local wildlife.

Etymology

From Latin Salvadora, named after Spanish botanist Juan Salvador (1598–1681).
