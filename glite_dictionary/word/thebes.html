Thebes

1. PROPN

/ θibz / (AmE), / θiːbz / (BrE)
An ancient Greek city in Boeotia, famous for its role in Greek history and mythology, destroyed by Alexander the Great in 336 BC.
ancient Greek city in Boeotia

Examples

Many legends of Greek mythology are set in Thebes and its surrounding region.
Archaeologists have unearthed remarkable artifacts in the ruins of ancient Thebes.
Alexander the Great's destruction of Thebes was a pivotal event in Greek history.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Θῆβαι (Thēbai).

2. PROPN

/ θibz / (AmE), / θiːbz / (BrE)
an ancient Egyptian city located on the Nile, known for its splendid temples and tombs; flourished between the 22nd and 18th centuries BC
ancient city in Egypt on the Nile River

Examples

Many tourists travel to Thebes to see its famous temples and the Valley of the Kings.
Archaeologists have uncovered new artifacts in the ruins of Thebes.
In ancient times, Thebes was a major center of political and religious power in Egypt.

Etymology

From Greek Thēbai, referring to the ancient Egyptian city.
