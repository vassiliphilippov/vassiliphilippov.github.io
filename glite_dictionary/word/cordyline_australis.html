Cordyline australis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːr.dɪˈlaɪn ˌɔːˈstreɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / ˌkɔː.dɪˈlaɪn ˌɒːˈstreɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
A tall, elegant tree native to New Zealand, having either a single or a branching trunk, with clusters of long, narrow leaves and large panicles of fragrant white, yellow, or red flowers.
tree native to New Zealand with long narrow leaves

Examples

The Cordyline australis is often planted in gardens for its striking foliage and fragrant flowers.
In New Zealand, the Cordyline australis is commonly known as the cabbage tree.
The shelter provided by a mature Cordyline australis helps many native birds to nest safely.

Etymology

From the genus name Cordyline, from Greek 'kordyle' (club), and Latin 'australis' (southern).
