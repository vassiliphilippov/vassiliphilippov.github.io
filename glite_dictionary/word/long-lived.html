long-lived

1. ADJ

/ lɔŋˈlɪvd / (AmE), / lɒŋˈlɪvd / (BrE)
Existing or lasting for a long time; having a long duration or lifespan.
lasting or existing for a long time

Examples

The long-lived tree in their garden has survived many storms over the centuries.
Her research led to a long-lived solution that benefited generations to come.
Certain species of tortoises are known for their long-lived nature.

Synset Examples

hopes for a durable peace
a long-lasting friendship

Related Terms

  • Antonym: short-lived - existing or lasting only for a short time
  • Synonym: durable - able to withstand wear, pressure, or damage; long-lasting

Etymology

Formed from 'long' + 'lived', meaning to have lived long.
