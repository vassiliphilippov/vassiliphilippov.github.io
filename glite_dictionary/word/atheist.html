atheist

1. ADJ

/ ˈeɪθiɪst / (AmE), / ˈeɪθɪɪst / (BrE)
Relating to or characterized by atheism; pertaining to disbelief in the existence of deities.
relating to atheism

Examples

His atheist beliefs often influenced his approach to philosophical discussions.
During the debate, her atheist perspective was evident.
The organization has a number of atheist members who advocate for secular policies.

Synset Examples

atheist leanings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atheistic - Relating to or characteristic of atheism or atheists
  • Antonym: theistic - Relating to belief in the existence of a god or gods

Etymology

From Greek 'atheos' (without god), from 'a-' (without) + 'theos' (god).

2. NOUN

/ ˈeɪθiɪst / (AmE), / ˈeɪθiɪst / (BrE)
A person who does not believe in the existence of a god or gods.
person who does not believe in a god

Examples

As an atheist, she often participates in discussions about religion and philosophy.
Despite being raised in a religious family, he became an atheist in his twenties.
Many atheists advocate for the separation of church and state.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agnostic - person who believes the existence of God is unknown or unknowable
  • Antonym: believer - person who believes in the existence of a god or gods

Etymology

From Greek 'atheos' (without god), from 'a-' (without) + 'theos' (god).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09839449-n
Synset POS: n
