icteric

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈtɛrɪk / (AmE), / ɪkˈtɛrɪk / (BrE)
affected by jaundice, which causes yellowing of the skin, eyes, or mucous membranes
suffering from jaundice
medical

Examples

The patient appeared icteric, with yellowish discoloration of his eyes and skin.
An icteric tinge was noted in the newborn shortly after birth.
Doctors immediately suspected liver dysfunction when she developed an icteric complexion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jaundiced - having yellowish skin due to jaundice

Etymology

From Greek *ikterikos* (relating to jaundice), from *ikteros* (jaundice).
