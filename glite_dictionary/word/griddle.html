griddle

1. VERB

/ ˈɡrɪd.əl / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪd.əl / (BrE)
to cook (food) on a griddle, a flat heated surface or pan.
cook food on a flat pan or griddle

Examples

She loves to griddle pancakes for breakfast every Sunday.
The chef griddled some vegetables as a healthy side dish.
After marinating the fish, he griddled it quickly to give it a crisp texture.

Synset Examples

griddle pancakes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fry - to cook in hot fat or oil, typically in a shallow pan

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɪd.əl / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪd.əl / (BrE)
a flat, heated metal surface or pan used for cooking food, especially pancakes or sandwiches, on a stovetop or over a fire
flat pan or surface for cooking

Examples

She cooked the pancakes on a large griddle over the campfire.
The old griddle gave the sandwiches a perfect golden crust.
Before you pour the batter, make sure the griddle is hot enough.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grill - a device for cooking over direct heat

Etymology

From Middle English *gredil*, from Old French *gredil* (grill), from Latin *craticula* (small grate).
