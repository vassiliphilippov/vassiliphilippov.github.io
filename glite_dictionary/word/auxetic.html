auxetic

1. ADJ

/ ɔːgˈzɛtɪk / (AmE), / ɔːkˈsiːtɪk / (BrE)
of or relating to growth by auxesis, especially involving an increase in volume without a corresponding increase in cell number; also, exhibiting the property of becoming wider when stretched.
relating to growth by auxesis or materials that expand when stretched
medical

Examples

The researchers studied several auxetic materials that become wider rather than thinner when stretched.
Certain biological tissues exhibit auxetic properties, expanding laterally during growth.
The auxetic nature of the foam made it ideal for impact absorption applications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auxesis - increase in size without increase in cell number

Etymology

From Greek auxetos (that may be increased), from auxein (to increase).
