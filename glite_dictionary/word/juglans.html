Juglans

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌɡ.lænz / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌɡ.lænz / (BrE)
the genus of deciduous trees including the walnuts, forming the type genus of the family Juglandaceae
genus of walnut trees

Examples

Species in the genus Juglans are widely cultivated for their edible nuts.
The black walnut, Juglans nigra, is native to eastern North America.
Botanists classify all true walnuts under the genus Juglans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walnut - the nut or tree belonging to the Juglans genus

Etymology

From Latin 'juglans', meaning 'walnut'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌɡˌlænz / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌɡ.lænz / (BrE)
The type genus of the walnut family (Juglandaceae), including several species of walnuts and butternuts.
type genus of the walnut family
medical

Examples

The genus Juglans includes both the English walnut and the black walnut.
Botanists classify several species of walnuts under the genus Juglans.
Several members of the genus Juglans are cultivated for their valuable nuts and timber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walnut - a tree or nut of the genus Juglans

Etymology

From Latin 'juglans' meaning 'walnut'.
