deviation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːviˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːviˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the error in a compass reading caused by local magnetic disturbances, such as those from nearby metal objects or electrical equipment
compass error from local magnetic effects

Examples

The ship's navigator corrected for the deviation caused by the metal equipment near the compass.
Before setting sail, it's important to account for any possible deviation in the compass readings.
Local magnetic fields can cause a significant deviation that must be considered to ensure accurate navigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: variation - the angle between true north and magnetic north

Etymology

From Latin deviatio (a turning aside), from deviāre (to turn aside).

3. NOUN

/ ˌdiːviˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːvɪˈeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the amount by which a single measurement differs from a fixed value such as the mean or expected value, especially in statistics.
a difference from an expected value (math/statistics)

Examples

The scientist calculated the deviation of each measurement from the average.
Statistical analysis showed a significant deviation from the expected results.
Reducing experimental error lowers the standard deviation in data.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: variance - the statistical measure of how much values differ from the mean

Etymology

From Late Latin 'deviatio', from Latin 'deviare' (to turn aside).
