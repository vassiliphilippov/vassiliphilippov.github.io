Kelt

1. NOUN

/ kɛlt / (AmE), / kɛlt / (BrE)
a member of an ancient European people who lived in areas of Britain, Spain, and Gaul before Roman times
ancient European person (pre-Roman)

Examples

Archaeologists discovered a burial site believed to belong to a Kelt from the Iron Age.
The Kelt tribes played a significant role in the early history of Western Europe.
Legends about the Kelt warriors have influenced many works of literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Celt - a member of the ancient Celtic people

Etymology

From Greek Keltoi, Latin Celtae; alternate spelling of 'Celt'.
