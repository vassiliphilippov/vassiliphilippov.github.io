Hydrocharis

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdrɑːkərɪs / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɒkərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small, floating aquatic plants belonging to the family Hydrocharitaceae, commonly called frogbit.
genus of aquatic plants (frogbit)

Related Terms

2. NOUN

/ haɪˈdrɑːkərɪs / (AmE), / hɪˈdrɒkərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small, free-floating aquatic plants, commonly known as frogbit, found in still or slow-moving waters.
genus of aquatic plants (frogbit)

Examples

Hydrocharis thrives in calm ponds and lakes, spreading rapidly across the water surface.
Frogbit, belonging to the genus Hydrocharis, is valued in ornamental water gardens for its floating leaves.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Hydrocharis in regions with varying water quality.

Etymology

From Greek 'hydro' (water) and 'charis' (grace), referring to the plant's elegant appearance on water.
