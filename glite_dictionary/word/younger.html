younger

1. ADJ

/ ˈjʌŋər / (AmE), / ˈjʌŋə / (BrE)
(used after a name) used to distinguish the son from the father when both have the same name.
son with same name as father

Examples

After his father retired, people started calling him younger John Smith to avoid confusion.
The estate was inherited by William younger, not his father.
In the legal documents, the company listed Thomas younger as the new director.

Synset Examples

John Junior
John Smith, Jr.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: junior - a suffix used to distinguish a son from his father having the same name

Etymology

Comparative of 'young', from Old English 'geong'.
