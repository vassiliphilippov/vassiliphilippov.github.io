kieselguhr

1. NOUN

/ ˈkiːzəlˌɡuɚ / (AmE), / ˈkiːzəlˌɡʊə / (BrE)
A light, porous material made up of siliceous remains of diatoms, commonly used as a filter or absorbent.
porous material of diatom remains, used in filtering

Examples

The laboratory uses kieselguhr as a filtering aid for various chemical processes.
A layer of kieselguhr was packed into the column to purify the solution.
Manufacturers sometimes add kieselguhr to dynamite for its absorbent properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diatomaceous earth - another term for kieselguhr, siliceous earth from diatoms

Etymology

From German Kieselgur, from Kiesel (pebble) + Gur (earth)
