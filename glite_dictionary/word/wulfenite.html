wulfenite

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊlfəˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈwʊlfɪnaɪt / (BrE)
A yellow, orange, or brown mineral consisting of lead molybdate, used as an ore of molybdenum.
a yellow to brown mineral; ore of molybdenum

Examples

The discovery of wulfenite crystals attracted mineral collectors from around the world.
Miners extract wulfenite as a source of molybdenum for various industrial uses.
A specimen of wulfenite can display brilliant orange-red tabular crystals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: molybdenite - a mineral, chief ore of molybdenum

Etymology

Named after Franz Xavier von Wulfen (1728–1805), Austrian mineralogist, with suffix '-ite' for minerals.
