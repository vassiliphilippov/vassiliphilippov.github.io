arbalist

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrbəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈɑːbəlɪst / (BrE)
An archer who uses an arbalest, a type of crossbow.
archer using an arbalest

Examples

The skilled arbalist prepared his weapon before the tournament began.
During medieval battles, a arbalist could often change the course of a fight.
A museum exhibit displays the armor and crossbows once used by the arbalists of the 15th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crossbowman - person who uses a crossbow

Etymology

From Old French *arbaleste* (crossbow) + -ist (person who practices), from Latin *arcuballista* (crossbow).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrbəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈɑːbəlɪst / (BrE)
A type of crossbow, especially a heavy one with a steel bow, used in the late Middle Ages.
type of crossbow with a steel bow

Examples

The museum's medieval collection includes a rare arbalist from the fifteenth century.
He demonstrated how the arbalist could launch bolts with remarkable force.
Unlike a simple bow, the arbalist required a winch to draw the steel bow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crossbow - a medieval weapon with a bow fixed across a wooden stock

Etymology

From Old French 'arbaleste', from Latin 'arcuballista' (a bow for shooting projectiles).
