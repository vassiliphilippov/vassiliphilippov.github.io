hematocrit

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈmætəkrɪt / (AmE), / hɪˈmatəkrɪt / (BrE)
the proportion of the volume of red blood cells to the total volume of blood, usually expressed as a percentage
proportion of red blood cells in blood
medical

Examples

The patient's hematocrit was lower than normal, indicating possible anemia.
Doctors routinely check the hematocrit level during annual physicals.
After the transfusion, her hematocrit increased to a healthy range.

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' (blood) + 'kritēs' (judge).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhiːmətoʊˌkrɪt / (AmE), / hɪˈmatəkrɪt / (BrE)
An instrument used to measure the proportion of red blood cells to plasma in blood, usually by centrifugation.
instrument for measuring blood cell ratio
medical

Examples

The laboratory technician placed the blood sample into the hematocrit for analysis.
After spinning the tube in the hematocrit, the doctor assessed the patient's red blood cell level.
A portable hematocrit is often used in field hospitals to quickly determine blood cell concentrations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: centrifuge - a machine used to separate substances of different densities, often in medical tests

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' (blood) + 'kritēs' (judge).
