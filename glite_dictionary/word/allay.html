to allay

1. VERB

/ əˈleɪ / (AmE), / əˈleɪ / (BrE)
to make a feeling such as fear, anger, or worry less strong or to relieve it
reduce or soothe feelings

Examples

She tried to allay his fears by explaining the situation clearly.
The medicine helped allay the child's pain after the injury.
Regular communication can allay employees' concerns during organizational changes.

Synset Examples

The news eased my conscience
still the fears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to alleviate - make (a problem or feeling) less severe
  • Antonym: to aggravate - make worse or more severe

Etymology

From Old English 'alecgan' (to put down, lay aside), from 'a-' (down) + 'lecgan' (to lay).

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈleɪ / (AmE), / əˈleɪ / (BrE)
to relieve or satisfy (a feeling such as thirst); to make (thirst, desire, etc.) less strong or easier to bear
to relieve or satisfy thirst or desire

Examples

A glass of water helped to allay his terrible thirst after the long run.
The soup was so refreshing it allayed my thirst on the cold evening.
She drank slowly, hoping to allay her thirst before the meeting.

Synset Examples

The cold water quenched his thirst

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to quench - to satisfy (thirst, desire, etc.), especially by drinking

Etymology

From Old English *alecgan* (to lay down, put down, remit).

Source: WordNet
