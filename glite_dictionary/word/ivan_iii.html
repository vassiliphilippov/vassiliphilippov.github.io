Ivan III

1. PROPN

/ ˌiːvɑn ðə ˈθɜːrd / (AmE), / ˌiːvan ði ˈθɜːd / (BrE)
The Grand Duke of Muscovy (1440–1505), known for defeating the Tartars and unifying Russian territories, laying the foundation for the Russian state.
Grand Duke of Muscovy, Russian unifier (1440–1505)

Examples

Ivan III was instrumental in ending Mongol rule over Russian lands.
Historians often credit Ivan III with establishing the Russian autocracy.
The Kremlin was significantly expanded during the reign of Ivan III.
