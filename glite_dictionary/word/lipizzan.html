Lipizzan

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈpɪz.ɑn / (AmE), / lɪˈpɪz.æn / (BrE)
a breed of compact, sturdy saddle horse originally bred in Austria and known for its intelligence, docility, and excellence in dressage, especially at the Spanish Riding School in Vienna
Austrian breed of dressage horse

Examples

The Lipizzan is famed for its elegant performances at the Spanish Riding School in Vienna.
When born, a Lipizzan is usually dark, but its coat turns white as it matures.
She traveled to Austria to see the Lipizzan stallions perform their classical dressage.

Synset Examples

a Lippizan is black or brown when born but turns white by the time it is five years old

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: horse - a large domesticated mammal used for riding and work

Etymology

From Lipica, a village in Slovenia where the breed originated, and Italian Lipizzano.
