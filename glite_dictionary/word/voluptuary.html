voluptuary

1. NOUN

/ vəˈlʌp.tʃuˌɛr.i / (AmE), / vəˈlʌp.tjʊə.ri / (BrE)
a person whose life is devoted to the pursuit and enjoyment of luxury and sensual pleasures
person devoted to luxury and pleasure

Examples

The wealthy voluptuary indulged in extravagant feasts and lavish parties every weekend.
Throughout history, many rulers have been criticized as voluptuaries who cared little for the hardships of their people.
He lived more as a voluptuary than a philosopher, always seeking new delights for his senses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sybarite - a person devoted to luxury and pleasure
  • Synonym: hedonist - a person who pursues pleasure as a primary goal
  • Antonym: ascetic - a person who practices severe self-discipline and abstention from pleasure

Etymology

From Latin 'voluptuarius' (pertaining to pleasure), from 'voluptas' (pleasure).

2. ADJ

/ vəˈlʌp.tʃuˌɛr.i / (AmE), / vəˈlʌp.tjʊ.əri / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by luxury and the gratification of the senses; devoted to sensual pleasure.
characterized by luxury and sensual pleasure

Examples

The palace was decorated in a distinctly voluptuary style, filled with gold accents and silken draperies.
She led a voluptuary existence, surrounded by fine wine, exquisite food, and soft music.
The novel portrays the voluptuary lifestyle of the aristocracy in vivid detail.

Synset Examples

Lucullus spent the remainder of his days in voluptuous magnificence
a chinchilla robe of sybaritic lavishness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luxurious - marked by luxury, especially in living or furnishing
  • Antonym: austere - severe or strict in manner, attitude, or appearance; lacking luxuries

Etymology

From Latin voluptuarius (of pleasure), from voluptas (pleasure, delight).
