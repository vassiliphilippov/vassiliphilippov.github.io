decimal

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛsɪməl / (AmE), / ˈdɛsɪməl / (BrE)
a number expressed as a proper fraction with a denominator that is a power of ten, often written with a decimal point
a fraction with denominator a power of ten

Examples

She learned how to convert fractions to decimals in math class.
The number 0.75 is a decimal equivalent to three-quarters.
When adding decimals, it's important to align the decimal points.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decimal point - the dot used to separate whole numbers from fractions in decimal notation

Etymology

From Latin decimus (tenth), from decem (ten).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛs.ɪ.məl / (AmE), / ˈdɛs.ɪ.məl / (BrE)
A number expressed in the decimal system, especially a number written with a decimal point.
number in the decimal system

Examples

You can write one half as the decimal 0.5.
The answer must be given as a decimal rather than a fraction.
Children learn to convert fractions into decimals in school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fraction - a numerical quantity that is not a whole number

Etymology

From Medieval Latin decimalis, from Latin decimus (tenth), from decem (ten).

3. ADJ

/ ˈdɛs.ɪ.məl / (AmE), / ˈdɛs.ɪ.məl / (BrE)
(of a system) based on the number ten, especially in terms of counting, measurement, or notation.
based on the number ten

Examples

The decimal system makes calculations much easier than older methods.
Measurements are commonly made using the decimal metric system worldwide.
He explained the differences between decimal and binary number systems to the class.

Synset Examples

the decimal system

Related Terms

  • Antonym: binary - based on the number two (especially in computing)

Etymology

From Latin decimus (tenth), from decem (ten).
