Solenostemon blumei

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊlɪˈnɑstəˌmɒn ˈbluːmiː / (AmE), / ˌsɒlɪˈnɒstɪˌmɒn ˈbluːmiː / (BrE)
A perennial aromatic herb native to southeastern Asia, known for its large, brightly colored or blotched leaves and spikes of blue-violet flowers; also called coleus.
ornamental aromatic herb; also called coleus

Examples

Many gardeners grow Solenostemon blumei for its striking foliage and easy care requirements.
The leaves of Solenostemon blumei come in a wide variety of vivid patterns.
We planted several Solenostemon blumei in the shade garden to add vibrant color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coleus - common name for Solenostemon blumei, an ornamental plant with colorful leaves

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Solenostemon (genus name), from Greek *solen* (tube) + *stēmōn* (thread); species name blumei in honor of botanist Karl L. Blume.
