Taccaceae

1. NOUN

/ tæˈkeɪsiaˌi / (AmE), / takˈkeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
A small family of tropical herbs, often with broad leaves and unusual flowers, formerly recognized in botanical classification.
family of tropical herbs (botany)

Examples

The Taccaceae are known for their unique and striking inflorescences.
Botanists once considered the Taccaceae a distinct family before recent taxonomic revisions.
Several species within the Taccaceae family are used in traditional medicine in tropical regions.

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from 'Tacca' (the type genus) and the suffix '-aceae' denoting plant families.
