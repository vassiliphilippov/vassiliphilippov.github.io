isoniazid

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsəˈnaɪəzɪd / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈnaɪəzɪd / (BrE)
an antibacterial drug used primarily to treat and prevent tuberculosis
antibacterial drug for tuberculosis
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed isoniazid as part of her tuberculosis treatment regimen.
Patients taking isoniazid should be monitored for potential side effects, including liver toxicity.
Resistance to isoniazid has become a concern in the management of tuberculosis worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nydrazid - trade name for isoniazid

Etymology

From isonicotinic acid hydrazide, reflecting its chemical structure.
