rabble

1. NOUN

/ ˈræb.əl / (AmE), / ˈræb.əl / (BrE)
A large group of people who are noisy, disorderly, or difficult to control, especially considered as a mob.
disorderly crowd or mob

Examples

The politician nervously addressed the rabble gathered outside the city hall.
Security guards struggled to control the rabble during the chaotic sale event.
The quiet town was disturbed by a rabble marching through the streets, shouting slogans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mob - a large crowd of people, often disorderly or unruly
  • Synonym: throng - a large, densely packed crowd of people
  • Antonym: elite - the most powerful, rich, or skilled people in a group

Etymology

From Middle English *rabel* (throng), probably from Old French *rabele* (pack, crowd).

2. NOUN

/ ˈræb.əl / (AmE), / ˈræb.əl / (BrE)
a disorganized or disorderly crowd of people, especially regarded with contempt or as the common masses
disorderly crowd or the common people (derogatory)

Examples

The politician dismissed the protesters as nothing more than a rabble making trouble.
A rabble gathered outside the palace, shouting demands for change.
He viewed the crowds at the festival as a mindless rabble, lacking discipline or purpose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mob - a large, disorganized, and unruly crowd of people
  • Antonym: elite - the group considered to be the best or most powerful in society
  • Synonym: crowd - a large number of people gathered together, typically in a disorganized or unruly way

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French *rabel* (pack, crowd).

3. NOUN

/ ˈræb.əl / (AmE), / ˈræb.əl / (BrE)
A large group of people who are noisy, unruly, and often considered to be of low social status.
disorderly or noisy crowd

Examples

The mayor tried to address the rabble gathered in front of the city hall.
Security struggled to control the rabble that had formed outside the stadium.
He dismissed his critics as nothing more than a rabble with no real influence.

Synset Examples

The rabble outside the venue was getting restless.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mob - A large, disorderly, and often violent crowd of people

Etymology

From Middle English rabel (pack, multitude), possibly from Old French rabel (pack, gang, rabble).
