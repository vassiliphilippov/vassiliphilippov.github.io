brittlebush

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪtɫ̩ˌbʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈbrɪt.əl.bʊʃ / (BrE)
a fragrant shrub native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, with brittle stems, blue-green leaves, and yellow flowers, valued for its resin used in incense, varnish, and traditional medicine
fragrant southwestern US desert shrub

Examples

The brittlebush blooms brightly each spring in the desert.
Local artisans collect resin from the brittlebush to make incense.
Hikers are often drawn to the silver-green foliage of the brittlebush along dusty trails.

Etymology

Named for its brittle stems; 'brittle' + 'bush'.
