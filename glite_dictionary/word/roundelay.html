roundelay

1. NOUN

/ ˈraʊndəˌleɪ / (AmE), / ˈraʊndɪleɪ / (BrE)
a song or poem in which a line or phrase is regularly repeated as a refrain
song with a repeated refrain

Examples

The choir performed a cheerful roundelay at the festival, delighting the audience with its repeated chorus.
In medieval courts, it was common to hear a roundelay echoing through the halls.
The poet included a roundelay in his collection, using the refrain to underline the poem’s central theme.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rondeau - a poem or song with a recurring refrain, similar fixed form

Etymology

From Old French rondelet, diminutive of rondel (little round, song with a refrain).
