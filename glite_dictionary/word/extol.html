to extol

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈstoʊl / (AmE), / ɪkˈtəʊl / (BrE)
to praise someone or something highly; to glorify or acclaim with enthusiasm
to praise highly

Examples

The professor would often extol the achievements of her most dedicated students.
Many articles extol the benefits of regular exercise for physical and mental health.
He extolled his grandmother's wisdom during his speech at the family reunion.

Synset Examples

extol the virtues of one's children
glorify one's spouse's cooking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: praise - to express approval or admiration for someone or something
  • Antonym: to criticize - to express disapproval of someone or something

Etymology

From Latin 'extollere', from 'ex-' (out, up) + 'tollere' (to raise).
