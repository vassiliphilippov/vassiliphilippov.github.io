Aldous Huxley

1. PROPN

/ ˈældəs ˈhʌksli / (AmE), / ˈældəs ˈhʌksli / (BrE)
(1894–1963) English writer best known for his novel 'Brave New World' and for his exploration of philosophical and social issues.
English writer and author of 'Brave New World'

Examples

Many readers consider Aldous Huxley's 'Brave New World' to be a masterpiece of dystopian fiction.
Aldous Huxley wrote extensively about the dangers of scientific progress unchecked by ethics.
The philosophical essays of Aldous Huxley reveal his deep interest in human consciousness.
