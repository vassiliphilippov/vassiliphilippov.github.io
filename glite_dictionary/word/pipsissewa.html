pipsissewa

1. NOUN

/ pɪpˈsɪsɪˌwɑ / (AmE), / pɪpˈsɪsɪwə / (BrE)
a low evergreen plant of the genus Chimaphila, found in North America and Eurasia, with glossy leaves and small pink or white flowers; also known for its traditional medicinal use
a small evergreen plant of the Chimaphila genus

Examples

Traditional healers used pipsissewa to treat a variety of ailments in Native American medicine.
The forest understory was carpeted with blooming pipsissewa in early summer.
Botanists studied the medicinal properties of pipsissewa for potential pharmaceutical uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prince's pine - another common name for pipsissewa, an evergreen plant of the genus Chimaphila

Etymology

From Cree (Algonquian) *pipišihsuweu*, meaning 'it breaks into small pieces' (referring to uses in medicine).
