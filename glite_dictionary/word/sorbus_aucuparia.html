Sorbus aucuparia

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔrbəs ˌaʊ.kjəˈpɛr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˈsɔː.bəs ˌaʊ.kjuːˈpɛː.ri.ə / (BrE)
a species of Eurasian tree, also known as the rowan or mountain ash, with pinnate leaves and clusters of orange-red berrylike fruits
Eurasian tree with orange-red berrylike fruits

Examples

The Sorbus aucuparia adds a splash of bright color to gardens in autumn.
Birds are often attracted to the orange-red berries of the Sorbus aucuparia.
Many traditional landscapes across Europe feature the Sorbus aucuparia as an ornamental tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rowan - common name for Sorbus aucuparia, a tree with red berries
  • Synonym: mountain ash - another common name for Sorbus aucuparia, an ornamental Eurasian tree

Etymology

From Latin: 'Sorbus' meaning 'service tree' and 'aucuparia' meaning 'of bird-catching', as its fruit attracts birds.
