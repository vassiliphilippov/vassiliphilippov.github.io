Spraguea umbellatum

1. NOUN

/ spræɡiə ʌmˈbɛlətəm / (AmE), / spræɡiə ʌmˈbɛlətəm / (BrE)
A plant species with pink, densely packed flowers in clusters on low stems, found in coniferous forests of western North America.
a North American plant with pink clustered flowers

Examples

Spraguea umbellatum is commonly found carpeting the forest floor in parts of Oregon and California.
Wildflowers such as Spraguea umbellatum add vibrant color to the understory of coniferous woodlands.
Botanists admire the unique structure of Spraguea umbellatum and its resemblance to cats' paws.

Etymology

From the genus name Spraguea and the Latin umbellatum meaning 'bearing umbels.'
