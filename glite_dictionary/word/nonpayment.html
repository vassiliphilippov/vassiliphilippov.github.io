nonpayment

1. NOUN

/ nɑnˈpeɪmənt / (AmE), / nɒnˈpeɪmənt / (BrE)
the fact of not paying money that is owed, resulting in a loss or default
failure to pay money owed

Examples

The company suffered significant losses due to the nonpayment of customer invoices.
Your account may be suspended as a result of nonpayment of your monthly bill.
Nonpayment of rent can lead to eviction under the terms of the lease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: default - failure to fulfill a financial obligation

Etymology

From prefix non- (not) + payment.

2. NOUN

/ nɑnˈpeɪmənt / (AmE), / nɒnˈpeɪmənt / (BrE)
The deliberate act of not paying money that is owed.
failure to pay money owed

Examples

The company faced penalties due to nonpayment of taxes.
He was sued for nonpayment of rent after missing several months.
Repeated nonpayment can damage your credit score.

Synset Examples

his evasion of all his creditors
he was indicted for nonpayment

Related Terms

  • Antonym: payment - the act of paying money owed
  • Synonym: default - failure to fulfill an obligation, especially to repay a loan

Etymology

From prefix 'non-' (not) + 'payment' (act of paying).

3. NOUN

/ nɑnˈpeɪmənt / (AmE), / nɒnˈpeɪmənt / (BrE)
the failure to pay a debt or fulfill a financial obligation
failure to pay a debt

Examples

The tenant was evicted due to repeated nonpayment of rent.
The company faces penalties for nonpayment of taxes.
If you fail to pay your bill, a nonpayment notice will be sent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: default - failure to fulfill an obligation, especially to repay a loan
  • Antonym: payment - the action or process of paying someone or something

Etymology

From prefix 'non-' (not) + 'payment' (from pay), denoting a lack of payment.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13321434-n
Synset POS: n
