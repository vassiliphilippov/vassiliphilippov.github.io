recycling

1. NOUN

/ riˈsaɪklɪŋ / (AmE), / riˈsaɪklɪŋ / (BrE)
materials that have been set aside or collected so they can be processed and used again to make new products.
materials collected for reuse

Examples

Residents are encouraged to take their recycling to the local collection center.
The city collects recycling and trash on different days of the week.
Please separate your recycling from regular household waste.

Synset Examples

Every Monday, we put our recycling out on the curb for pick-up

Related Terms

  • Antonym: waste - unwanted or unusable materials, especially that which is discarded

Etymology

From 'recycle', based on Greek 'kyklos' (circle), meaning to use again in a cycle.

2. NOUN

/ riˈsaɪklɪŋ / (AmE), / riˈsaɪklɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of collecting and processing used or waste materials so that they can be made into new products
processing of waste into new materials

Examples

Our city encourages recycling to reduce landfill waste and protect the environment.
She started a recycling program at her school to collect paper and plastic.
The company uses recycling to turn old tires into playground surfaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reuse - the action of using something again
  • Antonym: disposal - the act of getting rid of something

Etymology

From recycle + -ing; 'recycle' from re- (again) + cycle (circle, process).

Source: WordNet
