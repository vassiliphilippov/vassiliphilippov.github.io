separability

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛpərəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌsɛpərəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being able to be separated.
ability to be separated

Examples

The separability of the components makes recycling this product much easier.
Legal scholars debated the separability of the two contracts in court.
Mathematicians often discuss the separability of variables in differential equations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inseparability - the quality of being unable to be separated
  • Synonym: separable - able to be separated

Etymology

From separable + -ity, ultimately from Latin separare (to separate).
