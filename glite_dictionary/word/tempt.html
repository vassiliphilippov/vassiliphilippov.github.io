to tempt

1. VERB

/ tɛmpt / (AmE), / tɛmpt / (BrE)
to make someone want to do or have something, especially something that is not good or wise, by being attractive or inviting
make someone want to do/have something bad

Examples

The appealing aroma of fresh bread tempted him into the bakery.
She tried not to tempt fate by leaving late, but the train was still delayed.
The sale signs in the shop window tempt customers to spend more than they intended.

Synset Examples

the window displays tempted the shoppers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to entice - to lure someone by offering hope or reward
  • Antonym: to deter - to discourage someone from doing something

Etymology

From Latin temptare (to test, try, attempt).

2. VERB

/ tɛmpt / (AmE), / tɛmpt / (BrE)
to try to persuade someone to do something, often something wrong or unwise, by promising or suggesting something attractive
try to persuade, usually to do something wrong

Examples

He tried to tempt her with the promise of a better job.
The aroma of freshly baked bread can easily tempt anyone on a diet.
She refused to let the money tempt her into making a bad decision.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to deter - to discourage someone from doing something

Etymology
