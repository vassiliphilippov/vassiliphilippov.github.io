Tai

1. NOUN

/ taɪ / (AmE), / taɪ / (BrE)
the main branch of the Kadai language family, spoken widely in Southeast Asia, including languages such as Thai and Lao.
principal branch of the Kadai language family

Examples

The Tai languages are spoken in several countries across Southeast Asia.
Linguists have traced the origins of the Tai language to southern China.
Thai and Lao are both members of the Tai language group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Thai - the official language of Thailand, a member of the Tai languages

Etymology

From the self-designated name of the Tai peoples and languages.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ taɪ / (AmE), / taɪ / (BrE)
relating to the group of languages spoken by the Tai peoples of Southeast Asia, or to the Tai peoples themselves.
of the Tai language group or people

Examples

Many Tai languages share common grammatical features.
He is studying the traditional music of various Tai groups.
The Tai scripts have influenced writing systems in several Southeast Asian countries.

Synset Examples

Thai tones

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Thai - relating to Thailand, its people, or their official language

Etymology

From Chinese *Tài*, the name for the ethnic group.
