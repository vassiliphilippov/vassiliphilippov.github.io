extrusion

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstruʒən / (AmE), / ɪkˈstruːʒən / (BrE)
the act or process of forcing something out by applying pressure
forcing something out by pressure

Examples

An extrusion of toothpaste from the tube can be messy if you squeeze too hard.
The extrusion of clay is an important step in pottery making.
During the experiment, we observed the extrusion of fluid from the cell membrane.

Synset Examples

an unexpected extrusion of toothpaste from the bottom of the tube
the expulsion of pus from the pimple

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expulsion - the process of forcing something out

Etymology

From Latin 'extrudere' (to thrust out).

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstruːʒən / (AmE), / ɪkˈstruːʒən / (BrE)
something that sticks out from a surface or projects outward from its surroundings; a protuberant part or feature.
projecting or bulging part

Examples

On the cave wall, a strange extrusion caught the light of his flashlight.
The geologist pointed out the hardened lava extrusion on the mountainside.
A bony extrusion above his eye gave his face a unique profile.

Synset Examples

the gun in his pocket made an obvious bulge
the hump of a camel
he stood on the rocky prominence
the occipital protuberance was well developed
the bony excrescence between its horns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protuberance - something that juts out from the surrounding surface
  • Synonym: bulge - a rounded swelling or projection
  • Antonym: depression - a sunken or lowered area compared with the surroundings

Etymology

From Latin 'extrudere' (to thrust out, force out), via 'extrusionem' (a pushing out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13917127-n
Synset POS: n
