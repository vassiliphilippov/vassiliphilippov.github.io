Benito Mussolini

1. PROPN

/ bəˈniːtoʊ ˌmʊsəˈliːni / (AmE), / bəˈniːtəʊ ˌmʊsəˈliːni / (BrE)
(1883–1945) Italian fascist dictator; Prime Minister of Italy from 1922 until 1943 and founder of Italian Fascism.
Italian fascist dictator (1883–1945)

Examples

Benito Mussolini came to power in Italy in 1922, establishing a fascist regime that lasted more than two decades.
Historians still debate the impact of Benito Mussolini's rule on twentieth-century European politics.
The alliance between Adolf Hitler and Benito Mussolini played a significant role in World War II.
