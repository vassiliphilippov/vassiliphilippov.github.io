Tetragoniaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛtrəˌɡɒniˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˌɡɒnɪˈiːsiː / (BrE)
a family of succulent herbs or small shrubs, mainly found in South Africa, New Zealand, and North America, commonly known as carpetweeds or fig marigolds
succulent herb or shrub plant family (carpetweeds; fig marigolds)

Examples

The Tetragoniaceae family includes species adapted to arid environments.
Several ornamental plants belong to the Tetragoniaceae and are prized for their unique foliage.
Researchers studied the distribution of Tetragoniaceae in coastal regions.

Etymology

From the genus name Tetragonia, derived from Greek 'tetra-' (four) and 'gonia' (angle), referring to the shape of the fruit.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtɛtrəˌɡoʊniˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˌɡəʊniˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of succulent herbs or small shrubs found mainly in South Africa, New Zealand, and North America, including carpetweeds and fig marigolds
family of succulent herbs or small shrubs

Examples

Members of the Tetragoniaceae family are notable for their succulent leaves and widespread distribution.
Several species in the Tetragoniaceae are cultivated as ornamental ground covers.
The sandy soils of coastal regions provide an ideal habitat for many Tetragoniaceae plants.

Etymology

From New Latin Tetragonia (the type genus) + -aceae (denoting a plant family).
