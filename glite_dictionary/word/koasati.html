Koasati

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈæsəti / (AmE), / kəʊˈæsəti / (BrE)
the Muskogean language spoken by the Koasati people of Louisiana and Texas
Muskogean language of the Koasati people

Examples

The Koasati language is currently spoken by a small community in Louisiana.
Researchers recorded traditional stories in Koasati for linguistic preservation.
She is learning Koasati to help keep her cultural heritage alive.

Etymology

From the name Koasati, the self-designation of the people who speak this language.

2. NOUN

/ koʊˈɑːˌsɑːti / (AmE), / kəʊˈɑːˌsɑːti / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people formerly living in northern Alabama and a part of the Creek Confederacy.
member of a Native American people of Alabama

Examples

The Koasati were once part of the powerful Creek Confederacy in the southeastern United States.
A proud heritage is celebrated every year by descendants of the Koasati.
Researchers have documented the traditions and language of the Koasati people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Creek - a member of a Native American people forming the Creek Confederacy
  • Synonym: Muskogee - a member of the Creek Confederacy; another Muskhogean people

Etymology

From the Koasati tribe's own name, recorded in various forms in French and English colonial sources.
