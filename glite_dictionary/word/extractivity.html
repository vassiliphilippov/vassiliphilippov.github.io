extractivity

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkˌstrækˈtɪv.ɪ.ti / (AmE), / ˌɛkˌstrækˈtɪv.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the amount of extractable material, especially sugars, that grains of malt yield, typically measured as a percentage
amount of extract obtainable from malt

Examples

The extractivity of this malt sample is exceptionally high, resulting in a more efficient brewing process.
Brewers aim for malts with an extractivity above 80% to maximize alcohol yield.
Quality malt is characterized by no less than 80% extractivity.

Synset Examples

Quality malt is characterized by no less than 80% extractivity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extract - substance that is extracted, especially in brewing, the soluble material from grain

Etymology

From 'extract' + '-ivity', modeled after words like 'activity', indicating a condition or capacity.
