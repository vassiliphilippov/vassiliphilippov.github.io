doodia

1. NOUN

/ ˈduːdiə / (AmE), / ˈduːdɪə / (BrE)
A fern of the genus Doodia, known for its pinnate fronds with sharply toothed pinnae.
a genus of ferns with toothed fronds

Examples

The doodia thrives in moist, shaded areas of the forest.
Botanists discovered a new species within the doodia genus last year.
Gardeners often choose doodia for its decorative, sharply serrated foliage.

Etymology

Named after Samuel Doody (1656–1706), an English botanist.
