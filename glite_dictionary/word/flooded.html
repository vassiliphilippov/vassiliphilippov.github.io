flooded

1. ADJ

/ ˈflʌdɪd / (AmE), / ˈflʌdɪd / (BrE)
(of a place or object) covered or filled with water, typically due to excessive rainfall or overflow.
covered with water

Examples

The basement was completely flooded after the heavy rain last night.
We had to evacuate the flooded streets by boat when the river burst its banks.
My shoes got soaked when I stepped into a flooded field.

Synset Examples

the main deck was afloat (or awash)
the monsoon left the whole place awash
a flooded bathroom
inundated farmlands
an overflowing tub

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inundated - covered with water, submerged
  • Antonym: dry - not covered with water; lacking moisture

Etymology

From the verb 'flood' (to cover with water), based on Old English 'flōd' (a flowing of water, flood).
