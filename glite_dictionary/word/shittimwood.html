shittimwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪtɪmˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈʃɪtɪmˌwʊd / (BrE)
A shrubby, thorny, deciduous tree of the southeastern United States, known for its white flowers and small black, berry-like fruit.
a thorny tree native to the southeastern US

Examples

The cabinet was crafted from rare shittimwood imported from the southern states.
Botanists noted the abundance of shittimwood in the coastal forests of Mississippi.
In spring, the shittimwood produces clusters of delicate white flowers.

Etymology

From Hebrew *shittīm*, meaning acacia wood, as used in the Bible.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪtɪmˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈʃɪtɪmˌwʊd / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States and Mexico, often identified with the black locust or acacia, and sometimes mentioned in biblical contexts.
type of deciduous tree; black locust or acacia

Examples

The craftsman used shittimwood to build the intricate panels for the museum exhibit.
Biblical texts often mention shittimwood as the material for sacred structures and artifacts.
In some regions, shittimwood is prized for its durability and resistance to insects.

Etymology

From Hebrew *shittim* (plural of *shittah*), referring to the acacia tree, especially as mentioned in the Bible.

3. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪtɪmˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈʃɪtɪmˌwʊd / (BrE)
The wood of the shittah tree, mentioned in the Bible as used for making the Ark of the Covenant and other sacred objects.
wood used in the Biblical Ark of the Covenant

Examples

The Ark of the Covenant was constructed from shittimwood, covered with pure gold.
Ancient craftsmen sought out shittimwood for its strength and durability in sacred objects.
Biblical references often mention shittimwood as the material for holy artifacts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acacia - wood from acacia trees, to which shittimwood is related

Etymology

From Hebrew 'shittim', plural of 'shittah', referring to a type of acacia tree.
