to overawe

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈɔ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərˈɔː / (BrE)
to subdue, restrain, or intimidate someone by inspiring awe or fear
intimidate by awe or fear

Examples

The sheer size of the cathedral seemed to overawe the tourists who entered.
He tried to overawe his opponents with an intimidating speech.
She was overawed by the presence of so many famous people at the event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intimidate - to frighten or threaten someone, usually to make them do what you want
  • Antonym: to encourage - to give hope, confidence, or support to someone

Etymology

From 'over-' + 'awe', first used in the 17th century.
