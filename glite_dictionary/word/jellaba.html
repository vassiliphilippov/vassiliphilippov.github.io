jellaba

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈlɑːbə / (AmE), / dʒɛˈlɑːbə / (BrE)
a long, loose-fitting outer robe with a hood, traditionally worn in the Middle East and North Africa
loose hooded robe from North Africa or Middle East

Examples

He wore a traditional jellaba during the festival in Marrakech.
The jellaba kept her cool as she walked through the busy markets.
Tourists admired the colorful jellabas displayed in the shop windows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: djellaba - alternative spelling for jellaba, a loose hooded cloak

Etymology

From Arabic *jallāba*, meaning a type of robe or cloak.
