Dacrymycetaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌdækrɪˌmaɪsɪˈteɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌdakrɪˌmɪsɪˈtiːsiː / (BrE)
A family of basidiomycetous fungi in the order Tremellales, characterized by bifurcate, non-septate basidia.
family of fungi (Tremellales order)

Examples

The Dacrymycetaceae family includes jelly fungi commonly found on decaying wood.
Several species in Dacrymycetaceae are studied for their unique basidial structure.
Mycology students learn to identify Dacrymycetaceae by their bifurcate basidia.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Tremellaceae - a related family of fungi within the same order

Etymology

From New Latin Dacrymycetaceae, based on the type genus Dacrymyces.
