shillelagh

1. NOUN

/ ʃɪˈleɪli / (AmE), / ʃɪˈleɪli / (BrE)
a thick stick of hardwood, especially oak or blackthorn, used as a cudgel, traditionally associated with Ireland
Irish hardwood cudgel

Examples

The old man proudly displayed his shillelagh, saying it had been in his family for generations.
During the parade, several men carried a shillelagh as part of their traditional Irish costume.
He used a sturdy shillelagh to clear a path through the dense undergrowth.

Etymology

From Irish *síol Éalaigh* (descendant of Éalach), referring to Shillelagh, a village in County Wicklow, Ireland, noted for its oak forests.

2. NOUN

/ ʃəˈleɪli / (AmE), / ʃɪˈleɪli / (BrE)
A thick stick of hardwood, especially oak or blackthorn, traditionally used in Ireland as a cudgel or walking stick.
Irish hardwood cudgel or walking stick

Examples

The old man walked with a sturdy shillelagh carved from blackthorn.
During the festival, he carried a polished shillelagh as part of his traditional costume.
A genuine shillelagh can be an impressive keepsake or walking stick.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cudgel - a short thick stick used as a weapon

Etymology

From Irish 'sail éille', meaning 'thonged willow' or from Shillelagh, a town in County Wicklow, Ireland, noted for its oaks.
