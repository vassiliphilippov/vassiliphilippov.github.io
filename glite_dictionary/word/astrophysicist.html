astrophysicist

1. NOUN

/ ˌæstrəˈfɪsɪsɪst / (AmE), / ˌastrə(ʊ)ˈfɪsɪsɪst / (BrE)
a scientist who studies the physical laws and properties of celestial bodies and phenomena in space
scientist specializing in astrophysics

Examples

The astrophysicist explained the mysteries of black holes to the audience.
As an astrophysicist, she spends most of her time analyzing data from telescopes.
A renowned astrophysicist recently discovered a new exoplanet orbiting a distant star.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astronomer - a scientist who studies celestial objects, space, and the universe as a whole

Etymology

From astro- (Greek astron, 'star') + physicist (from Greek physis, 'nature').
