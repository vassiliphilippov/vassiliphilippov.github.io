solebar

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊlˌbɑr / (AmE), / ˈsəʊlbɑː / (BrE)
a longitudinal beam along the side of a railway vehicle to which the bodywork is attached, forming part of the floor or side structure
beam at side of railway vehicle

Examples

The engineer inspected the solebar for damage after the minor derailment.
New regulations require that the solebar be reinforced to meet safety standards.
During restoration, the old wooden solebar was replaced with a steel one.

Etymology

From 'sole' (meaning base or bottom) + 'bar' (a rigid piece forming structure).
