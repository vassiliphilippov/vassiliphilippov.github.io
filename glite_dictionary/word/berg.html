berg

1. NOUN

/ bɜrg / (AmE), / bɜːg / (BrE)
a large mass of ice floating at sea, usually broken off from a polar glacier
large floating mass of sea ice

Examples

The ship navigated carefully to avoid hitting a berg in the fog.
A massive berg drifted slowly past the Arctic research station.
Melting of a large berg can affect local seawater temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iceberg - a very large floating mass of ice

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'berg' (mountain), ultimately from Dutch.
