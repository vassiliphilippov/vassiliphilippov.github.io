octuple

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑktəpəl / (AmE), / ˈɒktjʊpəl / (BrE)
Consisting of eight parts or elements; having eight units or components.
having eight parts or components

Examples

The chemical compound forms an octuple bond under certain conditions.
The researcher discovered an octuple system of stars orbiting together.
They received an octuple dividend due to the impressive results.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: quadruple - consisting of four parts or components
  • Degree Lower: sextuple - consisting of six parts or components

Etymology

From Latin 'octuplus', from 'octo' (eight) + '-plus' (fold).
