dating

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of estimating the age of geological specimens, artifacts, or fossils using chemical analysis
estimating age using chemical analysis

Examples

Radiocarbon dating revolutionized the field of archaeology by providing more accurate timelines for ancient artifacts.
Scientists used uranium-lead dating to determine the age of the oldest rocks on Earth.
Advances in dating techniques have made it possible to verify the timeline of human evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chronometry - the scientific measurement of time or time intervals
