Cestrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛstrəm / (AmE), / ˈsiːstrəm / (BrE)
A genus of fragrant tropical shrubs and small trees in the nightshade family, native to the Americas, some of which are grown as ornamental plants.
genus of tropical fragrant shrubs

Examples

Many gardeners choose to plant Cestrum for its showy, fragrant flowers.
The scent of Cestrum filled the air on warm summer evenings.
Bees are often attracted to Cestrum blossoms in the garden.

Etymology

From Latinized form of Greek *kestron*, name for an unknown plant.
