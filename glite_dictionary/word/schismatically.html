schismatically

1. ADV

/ skɪzˈmætɪkli, sɪzˈmætɪkli / (AmE), / ˈsɪzmətɪkli, ˈskɪzmətɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner that promotes, relates to, or is characteristic of schism or division, especially in a religious context.
in a divisive or schismatic manner

Examples

The reformers argued schismatically against the established doctrines.
He was accused of acting schismatically during the council, promoting division.
The church split occurred when several members spoke schismatically about leadership.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schism - a split or division, especially in a religious organization
  • Antonym: unifyingly - in a manner that unifies or brings together

Etymology

From schismatic + -ally, after schism, from Greek skhisma (division) via Latin and Old French.
