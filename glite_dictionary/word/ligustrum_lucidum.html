Ligustrum lucidum

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈɡʌstrəm ˈluːsɪdəm / (AmE), / lɪˈɡʌstrəm ˈluːsɪdəm / (BrE)
An evergreen tree or large shrub native to East Asia, with glossy leaves and clusters of white flowers, commonly known as glossy privet.
species of evergreen shrub or tree (glossy privet)

Examples

The garden was shaded by a large Ligustrum lucidum near the fence.
Horticulturists recommend Ligustrum lucidum for its resistance to pollution and hardy nature.
In some regions, Ligustrum lucidum is considered invasive due to its rapid spread.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: privet - shrub of the genus Ligustrum, often used as hedging
  • Degree Lower: Ligustrum japonicum - species closely related to Ligustrum lucidum, known as Japanese privet

Etymology

From Latin: 'Ligustrum' meaning 'privet', and 'lucidum' meaning 'shining', referring to the glossy leaves.
