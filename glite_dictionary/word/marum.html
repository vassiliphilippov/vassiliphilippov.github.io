marum

1. NOUN

/ ˈmærəm / (AmE), / ˈmarəm / (BrE)
A Mediterranean plant (Teucrium marum) in the mint family, with small hairy leaves and reddish-purple flowers, known to attract cats.
Mediterranean plant, Teucrium marum

Examples

The marum in her garden always attracted the neighbor's cats.
Botanists are studying the effects of marum extracts on feline behavior.
He planted marum for its aromatic leaves and lovely purple flowers.

Etymology

From Latin *marum*, a name for this plant, used by classical authors.
