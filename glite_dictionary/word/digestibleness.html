digestibleness

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈdʒɛstəbəlnɪs / (AmE), / dɪˈdʒɛstɪblnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being easy to digest.
ease with which something is digested

Examples

The digestibleness of the meal made it ideal for young children and the elderly.
Manufacturers often test products for digestibleness before releasing them to the market.
She was advised to choose foods with high digestibleness after her surgery.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indigestibility - the quality of being difficult or impossible to digest
  • Synonym: digestibility - the degree to which something can be digested; often used synonymously with digestibleness

Etymology

From digestible + -ness.
