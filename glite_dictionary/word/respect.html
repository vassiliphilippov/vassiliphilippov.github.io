respect

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈspɛkt / (AmE), / rɪˈspɛkt / (BrE)
The condition of being admired or esteemed by others for your abilities, qualities, or achievements.
being admired or esteemed

Examples

She has earned the respect of her colleagues through years of hard work.
The teacher commanded respect from everyone in the classroom.
Winning the championship brought him great respect in the community.

Synset Examples

it is held in esteem
a man who has earned high regard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: esteem - high regard or respect
  • Antonym: disrespect - lack of respect or courtesy

Etymology

From Latin 'respectus' (regard, consideration), from 'respiciō' (to look back at, regard).
