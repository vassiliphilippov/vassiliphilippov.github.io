vinegarroon

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɪnɪɡəˈruːn / (AmE), / ˌvɪnɪɡəˈruːn / (BrE)
a large whip-scorpion found in Mexico and the southern United States, noted for emitting a vinegary odor when threatened
large whip-scorpion emitting vinegary odor

Examples

The vinegarroon raised its tail defensively when approached by the curious hikers.
Many people are surprised to learn that a vinegarroon is harmless to humans despite its fierce appearance.
Researchers studied the chemical composition of the odor released by the vinegarroon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whip scorpion - an arachnid of the order Thelyphonida, known for a whip-like tail

Etymology

From American Spanish 'vinagrón', from 'vinagre' (vinegar) due to its odor.
