Paleozoic

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪ.li.oʊˈzoʊ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌpæli.əˈzəʊ.ɪk / (BrE)
the era of geological time lasting from about 541 to 252 million years ago, characterized by the development of early life forms
geological era, 541–252 million years ago

Examples

Many of the earliest marine animals appeared during the Paleozoic.
Fossils from the Paleozoic provide evidence of the evolution of early plants and animals.
Mass extinction events marked the end of the Paleozoic era.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Mesozoic - the era following the Paleozoic, known for dinosaurs
  • Degree Lower: Precambrian - the era preceding the Paleozoic, before complex life

Etymology

From Greek 'palaios' (ancient) + 'zōion' (animal), meaning 'ancient life'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌpeɪ.li.əˈzoʊ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌpæl.i.əʊˈzəʊ.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to or denoting the Paleozoic era, a major division of geological time characterized by the emergence of most major animal groups
of the Paleozoic era (geology)

Examples

The fossils found in this region are predominantly from the Paleozoic era.
Geologists studied Paleozoic rock formations to understand ancient marine environments.
Many important mineral deposits were formed during Paleozoic times.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Mesozoic - relating to or denoting the Mesozoic era, following the Paleozoic
  • Degree Lower: Precambrian - relating to or denoting the geological time before the Paleozoic era

Etymology

From Greek palaios (ancient) + zōē (life), meaning 'ancient life'.
