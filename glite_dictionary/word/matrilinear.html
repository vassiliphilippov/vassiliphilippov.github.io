matrilinear

1. ADJ

/ ˌmætrəˈlɪniɚ / (AmE), / ˌmætrɪˈlɪniə / (BrE)
(of descent or inheritance) based on or tracing lineage through the female line.
tracing descent through the female line

Examples

Some cultures organize family identity according to matrilinear principles, emphasizing the mother's lineage.
Inheritance in that community followed a matrilinear pattern, with property passed from mother to daughter.
The anthropologist studied tribes with matrilinear clan structures, where ancestry is traced through females.

Synset Examples

matrilineal inheritance

Related Terms

  • Antonym: patrilinear - tracing descent through the male line
  • Synonym: matrilineal - tracing descent through the female line; more common synonym

Etymology

From Latin 'mater' (mother) + '-linear', from 'linea' (line).
