totalitarianism

1. NOUN

/ toʊˌtæləˈtɛriənɪzəm / (AmE), / təʊˌtæləˈteəriənɪzəm / (BrE)
a system of government in which the ruling authority has absolute power and does not allow opposing parties, often controlling all aspects of public and private life
absolute dictatorial government system

Examples

Many historians argue that totalitarianism emerged in the twentieth century with regimes like Nazi Germany and the Soviet Union.
The novel describes a future society under ruthless totalitarianism, where citizens have no personal freedoms.
Debates about totalitarianism often focus on how governments suppress dissent and individual rights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authoritarianism - strict obedience to authority at the expense of personal freedom
  • Antonym: democracy - a form of government in which power is vested in the people

Etymology

From Italian totalitario (total, complete), plus English '-ism'; popularized in the 20th century to describe regimes with total control.

2. NOUN

/ toʊˌtæləˈtɛriəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / təʊˌtælɪˈteəriənɪzəm / (BrE)
The belief in or practice of a political system in which the government holds total and unrestricted authority over every aspect of public and private life.
belief in absolute government control

Examples

The rise of totalitarianism in the twentieth century led to major global conflicts.
He feared that new laws might pave the way for totalitarianism in his country.
Academics often debate the causes and consequences of totalitarianism in modern history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authoritarianism - the enforcement or advocacy of strict obedience to authority at the expense of personal freedom
  • Antonym: democracy - a system of government by the whole population, typically through elected representatives

Etymology

Formed from 'totalitarian' + '-ism'; 'totalitarian' from Italian totalitario, from totale 'total'.
