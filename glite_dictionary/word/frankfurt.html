Frankfurt

1. PROPN

/ ˈfræŋkfɚt / (AmE), / ˈfraŋkfət / (BrE)
A major city in central Germany, known as an industrial, commercial, and financial center.
German city; financial and commercial hub

Examples

The headquarters of the European Central Bank are located in Frankfurt.
Every autumn, Frankfurt hosts one of the world’s largest book fairs.
After landing at Frankfurt Airport, they took a train to the city center.

Etymology

From the German 'Frankfurt am Main', meaning 'Ford of the Franks on the Main (river)'.
