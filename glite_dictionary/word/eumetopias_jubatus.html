Eumetopias jubatus

1. NOUN

/ juːməˈtoʊpiəs dʒuːˈbeɪtəs / (AmE), / juːmɛˈtəʊpiəs dʒuːˈbɑːtəs / (BrE)
a species of large sea lion native to the northern Pacific, also known as the Steller sea lion
large sea lion of the northern Pacific

Examples

The researchers tracked a group of Eumetopias jubatus along the Alaskan coastline.
A mature Eumetopias jubatus can weigh over a thousand kilograms.
Climate change has impacted the population of Eumetopias jubatus in recent decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Steller sea lion - common name for Eumetopias jubatus

Etymology

From Greek 'eu' (good) + 'metopon' (forehead) and Latin 'jubatus' (maned).
