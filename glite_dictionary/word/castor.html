Castor

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæs.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.stə / (BrE)
The genus comprising beavers, large semiaquatic rodents known for building dams and lodges.
genus of beavers

Examples

The North American beaver belongs to the genus Castor.
Castor is distinguished from other rodent genera by its unique adaptations for aquatic life.
European species of Castor were nearly hunted to extinction for their pelts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beaver - large semiaquatic rodent that builds dams and lodges

Etymology

From Latin 'castor', from Greek 'kastōr', meaning 'beaver'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈkæs.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæs.tə / (BrE)
a multiple star system composed of six stars, the second brightest object in the constellation Gemini, located near Pollux
multiple star system in Gemini

Examples

Through my telescope, I could clearly see Castor, shining brightly in the Gemini constellation.
Castor is notable in astronomy because it is actually a system of six stars orbiting each other.
The guide pointed out both Castor and Pollux as prominent features of the winter night sky.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Pollux - the brightest star in Gemini, close to Castor

Etymology

From Latin 'Castor', one of the mythological twins Castor and Pollux.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkæs.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.stə / (BrE)
the genus of large, aquatic rodents known as beavers, native to North America and Eurasia
genus of beavers

Examples

The Castor genus includes the well-known North American and Eurasian beaver species.
Researchers observed unique dam-building behavior in animals belonging to the Castor genus.
The European beaver, scientifically named Castor fiber, is native to rivers and lakes across Eurasia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beaver - large, semiaquatic rodent known for building dams

Etymology

From Latin *Castor*, from Greek *kástōr* meaning 'beaver'.
