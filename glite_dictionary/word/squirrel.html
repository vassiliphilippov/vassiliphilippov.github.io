squirrel

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwɝːrəl / (AmE), / ˈskwɪrəl / (BrE)
the soft fur of a squirrel, often used for trimming or lining garments
fur of a squirrel, used for clothing

Examples

The coat was lined with squirrel for added warmth.
In the 19th century, squirrel was a popular trim for winter hats.
She admired the gloves made from fine squirrel.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskwɝːrəl / (AmE), / ˈskwɪr.əl / (BrE)
a small rodent with a long bushy tail, typically living in trees and feeding on nuts and seeds
small tree-dwelling rodent

Examples

A squirrel darted across the lawn and disappeared up an oak tree.
We left some nuts outside, and a curious squirrel came to investigate.
Red squirrels are less common than their gray counterparts in some areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chipmunk - a small striped rodent related to the squirrel

Etymology

From Old French 'esquirel', from Latin 'sciurus', from Greek 'skiouros' (shadow-tailed).
