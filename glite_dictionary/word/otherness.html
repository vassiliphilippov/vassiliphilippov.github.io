otherness

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌðərnəs / (AmE), / ˈʌðənəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being different or distinct from what is familiar, usual, or expected
quality of being different or distinct

Examples

Many artists explore the concept of otherness in their work.
Her sense of otherness made it hard for her to fit in at school.
The novel examines racial otherness and cultural identity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: difference - the way in which two or more things are not the same
  • Antonym: sameness - the quality of being the same; lack of variety

Etymology

From 'other' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality of being other.
