developed

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈvɛləpt / (AmE), / dɪˈvɛləpt / (BrE)
(of land or property) made more useful or profitable by constructing buildings, roads, or other infrastructure.
(of land) improved by building or infrastructure

Examples

The company transformed the empty lot into a developed residential community.
Prices for developed land are much higher than for raw acreage in the area.
Local businesses benefit when previously unused areas become developed.

Synset Examples

condominiums were built on the developed site

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undeveloped - (of land or property) not improved by construction
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01292018-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈvɛləpt / (AmE), / dɪˈvɛləpt / (BrE)
(of a country, society, etc.) having a high level of industrialization, economic growth, and standard of living.
having advanced economic and industrial development

Examples

Japan is considered a developed country with a high standard of living.
Many international organizations provide aid to less developed regions.
Global trade agreements often differentiate between developed and developing nations.

Synset Examples

developed countries

Related Terms

  • Antonym: developing - a country or region with a lower level of industrialization and standard of living
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01304775-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ dɪˈvɛləpt / (AmE), / dɪˈvɛləpt / (BrE)
having advanced or grown to a more complete, mature, or useful state through a process of change or improvement.
having advanced or matured through change

Examples

Many countries are striving to become more developed in terms of infrastructure and technology.
His musical skills are highly developed after years of practice.
Only birds with fully developed wings are able to migrate long distances.

Synset Examples

the developed qualities of the Hellenic outlook
they have very small limbs with only two fully developed toes on each

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undeveloped - not having grown, advanced, or matured; lacking development
  • Synonym: mature - having reached full development or growth
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00745450-a
Synset POS: a
