wrongheaded

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɔŋˌhɛdəd / (AmE), / ˈrɒŋˌhɛdɪd / (BrE)
Having or showing stubborn adherence to wrong or misguided ideas or opinions.
stubbornly holding wrong opinions

Examples

Many experts warned that the committee's wrongheaded decision would have negative consequences.
Despite evidence to the contrary, the government persisted with its wrongheaded approach to the crisis.
She has often been criticized for her wrongheaded views on environmental policy.

Synset Examples

a wrongheaded policy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstinate - stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or action
  • Synonym: stubborn - unreasonably or perversely unyielding
  • Antonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas

Etymology

From 'wrong' + 'headed', originally meaning having a 'wrong head' (i.e., wrong thinking). First recorded in the 18th century.
