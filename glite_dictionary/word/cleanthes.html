Cleanthes

1. PROPN

/ kliˈænθiːz / (AmE), / klɪˈænθiːz / (BrE)
(c. 330–230 BC) An ancient Greek philosopher who succeeded Zeno of Citium as the head of the Stoic school.
ancient Greek Stoic philosopher

Examples

Cleanthes is best known for his hymn to Zeus, illustrating his dedication to Stoic philosophy.
After Zeno's death, Cleanthes became the leader of the Stoic school in Athens.
Philosophers continue to study Cleanthes for his contributions to ancient ethics and metaphysics.
