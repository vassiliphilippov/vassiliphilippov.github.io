Quercus muehlenbergii

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜːrkəs mjuːˌhɛlənˈbɜːrɡiːˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs mjuːˌhɛlənˈbɜːɡiːˌaɪ / (BrE)
A medium-sized deciduous tree native to the eastern United States, valued for its strong, durable wood; also known as chinquapin oak.
a North American deciduous tree species

Examples

The Quercus muehlenbergii thrives in limestone-rich soils throughout the Midwest.
Carpenters prize Quercus muehlenbergii for its exceptionally strong and durable lumber.
Botanists conducted a survey to determine the distribution of Quercus muehlenbergii in the region.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Quercus' (oak) + named after botanist Gotthilf Heinrich Ernst Muehlenberg.
