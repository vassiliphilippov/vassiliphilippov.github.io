Polypodiales

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːlɪpoʊˈdaɪəliˌz / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪpəˈdaɪəliːz / (BrE)
An order of true ferns, known as leptosporangiate ferns, that includes the majority of modern fern species.
an order of true ferns

Examples

Many common ferns in temperate forests belong to the Polypodiales order.
Botanists classify the largest diversity of fern species under Polypodiales.
The Polypodiales exhibit a wide range of leaf shapes and reproductive adaptations.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'polypous' (many-footed) + '-iales' (order suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːlipoʊˈdaɪəliːz / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪpəˈdaɪəliːz / (BrE)
an order of true ferns distinguished by having leptosporangiate sporangia, encompassing a large number of fern species worldwide
an order of true ferns

Examples

The majority of familiar garden ferns belong to the Polypodiales.
Scientists estimate that the Polypodiales contain more than 80% of living fern species.
The fronds of Polypodiales species are often delicate and intricately divided.

Etymology

From Modern Latin, based on genus Polypodium and the taxonomic ending -ales.
