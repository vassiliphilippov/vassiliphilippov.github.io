subluxation

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˌlʌkˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / sʌbˌlʌkˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
a partial dislocation or displacement of a joint or organ, less severe than a complete dislocation
partial dislocation of a joint or organ
medical

Examples

The X-ray revealed a subluxation of her shoulder after the fall.
Prompt treatment is necessary to prevent further damage from the subluxation.
Chiropractors often diagnose and treat spinal subluxations.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: dislocation - complete loss of contact between joint surfaces

Etymology

From Latin sub- (under) + luxare (to dislocate).
