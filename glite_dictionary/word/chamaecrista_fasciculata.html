Chamaecrista fasciculata

1. NOUN

/ kəˌmiː.krɪs.tə ˌfæs.ɪ.kjəˈleɪ.tə / (AmE), / kæˌmiː.krɪs.tə ˌfæs.ɪ.kjʊˈlɑː.tə / (BrE)
A tropical American plant species with leaflets that are somewhat sensitive to touch, sometimes classified under the genus Cassia.
tropical American touch-sensitive plant

Examples

Chamaecrista fasciculata is often grown in gardens for its attractive yellow flowers.
The leaves of Chamaecrista fasciculata fold when touched, making it a favorite among children.
Researchers study Chamaecrista fasciculata to understand leaf movement in plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cassia - a genus of tropical and subtropical leguminous plants

Etymology

From Greek 'chamai' (on the ground) + 'khristos' (useful), and Latin 'fasciculata' (bundled).
