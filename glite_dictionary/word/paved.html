paved

1. ADJ

/ peɪvd / (AmE), / peɪvd / (BrE)
(of a surface) covered with a firm material such as concrete, asphalt, or stones, making it suitable for walking or driving.
covered with a hard surface

Examples

The paved road made travel much easier for the villagers.
We walked hand-in-hand down the paved path in the city park.
Many of the streets in the old town are not paved yet.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unpaved - not covered with a hard surface

Etymology

From the verb 'pave', from Old French 'paver', from Latin 'pavire' (to beat, ram down, pave).
