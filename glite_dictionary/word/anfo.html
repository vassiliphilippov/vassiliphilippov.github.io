ANFO

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.foʊ / (AmE), / ˈæn.fəʊ / (BrE)
an industrial explosive made by mixing ammonium nitrate with fuel oil, commonly used in mining and construction
industrial explosive (ammonium nitrate/fuel oil)

Examples

The demolition crew used ANFO to bring down the unstable building safely.
Due to its cost-effectiveness and ease of handling, ANFO is popular in mining operations.
A precise mixture is crucial to ensure ANFO functions effectively as an explosive.

Etymology

Acronym from 'Ammonium Nitrate Fuel Oil'.
