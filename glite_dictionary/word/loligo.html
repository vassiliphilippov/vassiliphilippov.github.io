Loligo

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈliːɡoʊ / (AmE), / ləʊˈliːɡəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of squids commonly found in temperate and tropical seas, known for their elongated bodies and fins running along the sides.
a genus of squids

Examples

The Loligo species are harvested worldwide for culinary use.
Scientists observed the unique swimming patterns of Loligo in their marine habitat.
A member of the Loligo genus can change its color to blend with the surroundings.

Etymology

From Latin 'lolīgō' (calamary, squid).
