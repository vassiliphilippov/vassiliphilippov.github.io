absorptive

1. ADJ

/ əbˈsɔrptɪv, æbˈsɔrptɪv / (AmE), / əbˈzɔːptɪv, æbˈsɔːptɪv / (BrE)
having the ability or tendency to absorb liquids, energy, or other substances
having the ability to absorb

Examples

Cotton is highly absorptive, making it ideal for towels and bandages.
The scientist measured how absorptive the new material was to solar radiation.
Her skin was exceptionally absorptive of the moisturizer, leaving it soft and hydrated.

Synset Examples

as absorbent as a sponge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absorbent - having the ability to absorb; capable of soaking up

Etymology

From Latin 'absorptio' (absorption) and '-ive', forming adjectives meaning 'having the quality of'.
