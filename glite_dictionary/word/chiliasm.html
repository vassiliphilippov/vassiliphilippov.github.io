chiliasm

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪliˌæzəm / (AmE), / ˈkɪlɪˌazəm / (BrE)
the belief in a thousand-year reign of Christ on earth, as prophesied in the Book of Revelation
belief in Christ's thousand-year reign

Examples

The early church saw chiliasm as a legitimate interpretation of the Book of Revelation.
Modern theologians continue to debate the relevance of chiliasm in contemporary Christianity.
His research focused on the historical development of chiliasm in medieval Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: millenarianism - belief in a coming major transformation by a thousand-year reign of Christ
  • Antonym: amillennialism - the belief that there will not be a literal thousand-year reign of Christ

Etymology

From Greek 'khiliasmos' (belief in a thousand years), from 'khilias' (of a thousand).
