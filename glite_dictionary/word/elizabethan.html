Elizabethan

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɪzəˈbiθən / (AmE), / ɪˌlɪzəˈbiːθən / (BrE)
a person who lived during the reign of Queen Elizabeth I of England (1558–1603)
person from the time of Elizabeth I

Examples

Many Elizabethans contributed to the flourishing of English drama and poetry.
As an Elizabethan, she witnessed the transformation of London into a global city.
Historians often study the daily life of the Elizabethan to understand the era's culture.

Synset Examples

William Shakespeare was an Elizabethan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tudor - person belonging to the Tudor period or dynasty

Etymology

From Elizabeth, referring to Queen Elizabeth I of England + -an (denoting association).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10070520-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ɪˌlɪzəˈbiθən / (AmE), / ɪˌlɪzəˈbiːθən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of the period when Elizabeth I was queen of England (1558–1603), or to the people, culture, or works of that time.
relating to the reign of Elizabeth I of England

Examples

The Elizabethan theatre produced some of the greatest plays in English literature.
She specializes in Elizabethan poetry and its historical context.
Many buildings from the Elizabethan era still stand in England today.

Synset Examples

Elizabethan music

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Victorian - relating to the reign of Queen Victoria, or the period she ruled

Etymology

Formed from the name Elizabeth (I) and the suffix -an, denoting relation or belonging.

WordNet ID: oewn-03042914-a
Synset POS: a
