miff

1. NOUN

/ mɪf / (AmE), / mɪf / (BrE)
a slight feeling of irritation or annoyance
slight feeling of irritation

Examples

She left the meeting in a bit of a miff after the proposal was rejected.
His mild miff quickly faded when he received an apology.
There's no need to get into a miff over such a small mistake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annoyance - a feeling of being annoyed
  • Degree Higher: anger - strong feeling of displeasure or hostility
  • Antonym: contentment - a state of happiness and satisfaction

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, possibly from dialectal English.

2. VERB

/ mɪf / (AmE), / mɪf / (BrE)
to annoy or offend someone slightly; to cause to be annoyed
to annoy or offend slightly

Examples

Her sarcastic remarks miffed him more than she expected.
It really miffed me when they arrived late without calling.
He tried not to show that he was miffed by the criticism.

Synset Examples

His behavior really miffed me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to annoy - to make someone slightly angry
  • Antonym: please - to make someone happy or satisfied

Etymology

Probably from earlier noun use 'miff' (a fit of ill humor), origin uncertain but possibly imitative.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01790439-v
Synset POS: v
