entrapment

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtræpmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtræpmənt / (BrE)
a defense in law in which the defendant claims to have been induced by law enforcement officials to commit a crime they otherwise would not have committed
legal defense of being induced to commit a crime
legal

Examples

The defendant argued that police entrapment led him to commit the offense.
Her lawyer claimed entrapment after she was encouraged by undercover officers to break the law.
Many legal experts debate whether entrapment should result in an automatic acquittal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incitement - the act of urging someone to commit a crime
  • Synonym: provocation - action or speech that makes someone angry or prompts them to act illegally

Etymology

From 'entrap' + '-ment', modeled after related legal terms in English.
