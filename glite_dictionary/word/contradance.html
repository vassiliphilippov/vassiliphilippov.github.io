contradance

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.trəˌdæns / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.trəˌdɑːns / (BrE)
a type of folk dance in which couples are arranged in sets or lines and perform a sequence of figures, often facing one another
folk dance with couples in lines or sets

Examples

The festival featured a lively contradance with musicians playing traditional tunes.
She learned the steps of the contradance at a local folk dance workshop.
In a classic contradance, couples switch partners as they move up and down the set.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: country dance - an English folk dance similar to contradance

Etymology

From French 'contredanse', itself a corruption of English 'country dance'.

2. VERB

/ ˈkɑːntrəˌdæns / (AmE), / ˈkɒntrəˌdɑːns / (BrE)
to take part in or perform a contradance, a type of folk dance with couples in two facing lines
to perform a contradance

Examples

Every Saturday night, the community gathers to contradance in the town hall.
She learned how to contradance while traveling in New England.
They contradanced to lively music all evening at the festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contra dance - a type of folk dance in two facing lines

Etymology

From French 'contredanse', meaning 'counter-dance', itself a reinterpretation of English 'country dance'.
