tatterdemalion

1. ADJ

/ ˌtætərdɪˈmeɪliən / (AmE), / ˌtatədɪˈmeɪlɪən / (BrE)
Worn or torn to shreds; wearing or consisting of ragged or tattered clothing.
ragged or tattered (often clothing or person)
archaic

Examples

The tatterdemalion coat hung loosely on the beggar's thin frame.
He wandered through the streets in a tatterdemalion outfit that attracted curious stares.
The tatterdemalion banners fluttered weakly as the parade passed by.

Synset Examples

a man in a tattered shirt
the tattered flag
tied up in tattered brown paper
a tattered barefoot boy
a tatterdemalion prince

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tattered - old and torn; in poor condition

Etymology

Origin uncertain, possibly from 'tatter' and archaic 'demalion', the latter of unknown origin.

2. ADJ

/ ˌtætərdɪˈmeɪliən / (AmE), / ˌtætədɪˈmeɪliən / (BrE)
In a state of disrepair or ruin; dilapidated and ragged in appearance or condition.
dilapidated or ragged; in poor condition

Examples

The abandoned warehouse had a tatterdemalion look, with broken windows and peeling paint.
They lived on a tatterdemalion street where most houses were crumbling and neglected.
Her tatterdemalion garden was overgrown, with weeds sprouting through the cracked path.

Synset Examples

a street of bedraggled tenements
a broken-down fence
a ramshackle old pier
a tumble-down shack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dilapidated - In a state of disrepair or ruin
  • Synonym: ramshackle - Badly built or in disrepair
  • Synonym: derelict - In very poor condition as a result of disuse and neglect
  • Antonym: pristine - In its original condition; unspoiled

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; possibly from 'tatter', meaning torn cloth, combined with fanciful ending.

3. NOUN

/ ˌtætərdəˈmeɪliən / (AmE), / ˌtatədɪˈmeɪlɪən / (BrE)
a person, especially a child, who is dressed in old, ragged, and dirty clothes
a person in ragged clothes

Examples

A tatterdemalion wandered through the streets, his clothes torn and face smudged with dirt.
The children resembled tatterdemalions after playing all afternoon in the muddy fields.
She couldn't help but offer a warm meal to the tatterdemalion at her doorstep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ragamuffin - a person, typically a child, who is raggedly dressed

Etymology

Probably from 'tatter' (ragged cloth) and 'medallion' modeled playfully as a mock-Italianate formation.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10524257-n
Synset POS: n
