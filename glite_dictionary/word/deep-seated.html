deep-seated

1. ADJ

/ ˈdiːpˌsiːtɪd / (AmE), / ˈdiːpˌsiːtɪd / (BrE)
(of feelings, beliefs, or ideas) firmly established and difficult to change; deeply rooted.
firmly established; deeply rooted

Examples

She has a deep-seated fear of failure that affects her decisions.
Their deep-seated mistrust of outsiders made cooperation difficult.
This policy change challenged some deep-seated traditions within the community.

Synset Examples

deep-rooted prejudice
deep-seated differences of opinion
implanted convictions
ingrained habits of a lifetime
a deeply planted need

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ingrained - (of feelings, habits, or beliefs) firmly fixed; difficult to change
  • Antonym: superficial - existing or occurring at or on the surface; not deep or thorough

Etymology

From 'deep' + 'seated', originally meaning 'situated deep within'; current figurative use dates from the 17th century.
