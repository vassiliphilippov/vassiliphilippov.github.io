haemolysis

1. NOUN

/ hiːˈmɑːləsɪs / (AmE), / hiːˈmɒləsɪs / (BrE)
The destruction of red blood cells leading to the release of hemoglobin into the surrounding fluid.
destruction of red blood cells
medical british

Examples

Severe infections can cause haemolysis and lead to anemia.
The laboratory test showed signs of haemolysis in the sample.
Certain medications increase the risk of haemolysis in sensitive individuals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemolysis - destruction of red blood cells (American spelling)

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' (blood) + 'lysis' (loosening, dissolution).
