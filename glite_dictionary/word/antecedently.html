antecedently

1. ADV

/ ˌæn.tɪˈsiː.dənt.li / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈsiː.dənt.li / (BrE)
At an earlier time; previously or before something else.
at an earlier time; previously

Examples

The law applies only to rights antecedently acquired, not to those arising afterward.
He assumed that the arrangement was antecedently agreed upon by all parties.
Their actions were antecedently justified by earlier decisions of the court.

Synset Examples

she had previously lived in Chicago
he was previously president of a bank
better than anything previously proposed
a previously unquestioned attitude
antecedently arranged

Related Terms

  • Synonym: previously - at an earlier time; before now
  • Antonym: afterward - at a later time; subsequently

Etymology

From antecedent + -ly; antecedent from Latin antecedere (to go before).
