complimentary

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːm.pləˈmen.tə.ri / (AmE), / ˌkɒm.plɪˈmen.tər.i / (BrE)
expressing praise or admiration; given as a compliment
expressing praise or admiration

Examples

She made a complimentary comment about my work.
His speech was full of complimentary remarks that lifted everyone's spirits.
After the performance, we received several complimentary emails from the audience.

Synset Examples

a complimentary remark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: praiseful - expressing praise
  • Antonym: critical - expressing disapproval or criticism

Etymology

From compliment +‎ -ary, ultimately from Latin 'complēmentum' (completion).

2. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːmpləˈmentəri / (AmE), / ˌkɒmplɪˈmentəri / (BrE)
given without charge; costing nothing
free of charge; not costing anything

Examples

The hotel provides complimentary breakfast for all its guests.
All conference attendees received complimentary tote bags at the registration desk.
We were thrilled to receive complimentary tickets to the concert from our friend.

Synset Examples

complimentary tickets
free admission

Related Terms

  • Synonym: free - not costing or charging anything

Etymology

From compliment + -ary, originally meaning 'given as a courtesy' in English.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
