avidly

1. ADV

/ ˈævɪdli / (AmE), / ˈavɪdli / (BrE)
with great enthusiasm or eagerness; in a way that shows keen interest or desire
with great enthusiasm or eagerness

Examples

She read the new novel avidly, unable to put it down until she finished.
The crowd avidly cheered for their team during the championship match.
Children avidly collected stickers from every country they learned about in school.

Synset Examples

whatever the flavor or color of your local paper, do remember that these are read avidly for local information

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eagerly - with keen or enthusiastic desire
  • Antonym: indifferently - without interest or concern

Etymology

From 'avid' (from Latin avidus, 'eager, greedy'), plus the adverbial suffix '-ly'.
