to acerbate

1. VERB

/ ˈæsɚˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈasəˌbeɪt / (BrE)
to make someone feel bitter or resentful
make bitter or resentful
archaic

Examples

The constant criticism only acerbated his feelings toward his classmates.
Years of neglect acerbated the community's resentment against the authorities.
Her bitter disappointment was acerbated by their lack of understanding.

Synset Examples

These injustices embittered her even more

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embitter - to make someone bitter or resentful

Etymology

From Latin *acerbatus*, past participle of *acerbare* (to make harsh or bitter), from *acerbus* (bitter).

2. VERB

/ ˈæsərˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈæsəˌbeɪt / (BrE)
to make something sour or bitter; to embitter
make sour or bitter
archaic

Examples

The leader's harsh remarks only acerbated the already tense atmosphere.
Chemical additives can acerbate the taste of wine if not used carefully.
Years of neglect had acerbated the bitterness between the two families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embitter - to make (someone) feel bitter or resentful

Etymology

From Latin 'acerbus' (sour, bitter), via Latin 'acerbare' (to make bitter).
