evocative

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈvɑːkətɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈvɒkətɪv / (BrE)
bringing strong images, memories, or feelings to mind
bringing strong memories or feelings to mind

Examples

The artist's latest painting is particularly evocative of the sea at dawn.
Her memoir is filled with evocative descriptions of her childhood home.
The music was so evocative that it brought tears to his eyes.

Synset Examples

cannot forbear to close on this redolent literary note
a campaign redolent of machine politics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suggestive - making people think of something; indirectly indicating or hinting at something
  • Synonym: reminiscent - tending to remind one of something

Etymology

From Latin evocare (to call forth), via Late Latin evocativus.
