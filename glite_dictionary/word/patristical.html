patristical

1. ADJ

/ pəˈtrɪstɪkəl / (AmE), / pəˈtrɪstɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the writings or teachings of the early Christian church fathers.
of early church fathers' writings

Examples

The scholar devoted years to studying patristical texts from the second century.
Her dissertation focused on patristical influences in early Christian doctrine.
Many theological debates refer back to patristical sources as foundational authorities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patristic - relating to the early Christian church fathers; variant form

Etymology

From Latin 'patristicus', from 'pater' (father), referring to early Christian fathers.
