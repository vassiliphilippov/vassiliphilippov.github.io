seta

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːtə / (AmE), / ˈsiːtə / (BrE)
A slender stalk supporting the capsule in mosses and certain other lower plants.
stalk of a moss capsule

Examples

The seta of the moss is delicate and easily damaged.
When the seta matures, it raises the capsule above the moss leaves.
Scientists measured the length of each seta to study its growth patterns.

Etymology

From Latin seta (bristle, hair).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsiːtə / (AmE), / ˈsiːtə / (BrE)
a stiff hair or bristle, especially one found on the bodies of animals or plants
a stiff hair or bristle

Examples

The caterpillar is covered in tiny setae that can cause skin irritation if touched.
Under the microscope, each segment of the worm has pairs of setae for movement.
The underside of the gecko's toes is lined with microscopic setae that allow it to climb smooth surfaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bristle - a short, stiff hair or filament

Etymology

From Latin seta (bristle, stiff hair).
