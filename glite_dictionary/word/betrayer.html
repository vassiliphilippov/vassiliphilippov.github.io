betrayer

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈtreɪər / (AmE), / bɪˈtreɪə / (BrE)
a person who is disloyal or betrays the trust of others
one who is disloyal or unfaithful

Examples

Being called a betrayer by his friends deeply hurt him.
History remembered Brutus as the ultimate betrayer of Julius Caesar.
She regarded anyone who leaked information as a betrayer of trust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traitor - a person who betrays their country, friends, or principles

Etymology

From betray + -er, from Old French *trair* (to betray).

2. NOUN

/ bɪˈtreɪər / (AmE), / bɪˈtreɪə / (BrE)
a person who reveals confidential information or betrays someone's trust, especially to an authority
one who reveals confidential information

Examples

He was branded a betrayer after he gave information to the police.
No one trusts a betrayer in the group anymore.
Her actions proved she was a betrayer of their secrets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traitor - a person who betrays a friend, country, or principle
  • Synonym: informant - a person who gives information to another, especially to the police
  • Antonym: supporter - a person who supports another or is loyal

Etymology

From betray +‎ -er; 'betray' from Old French 'betrayer'.
