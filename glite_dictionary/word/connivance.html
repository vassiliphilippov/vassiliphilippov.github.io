connivance

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnaɪ.vəns / (AmE), / kəˈnaɪ.vəns / (BrE)
secret or tacit cooperation in a wrongful or illegal act; the act of conniving.
secret cooperation in wrongdoing

Examples

The company's success was achieved through the connivance of several key officials.
There was evidence of connivance between the two organizations to fix prices.
She was accused of connivance in the fraudulent scheme.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collusion - secret agreement for a wrongful purpose
  • Synonym: complicity - involvement as an accomplice in a wrongful act

Etymology

From Latin *conniventia*, from *connivere* (to close the eyes, to wink).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈnaɪvəns / (AmE), / kəˈnʌɪvəns / (BrE)
The act of secretly allowing or tacitly approving of wrongful or illegal acts, especially for personal advantage.
secret approval of wrongdoing
legal

Examples

The company's connivance in the illegal dumping of waste was eventually exposed.
Her silence was interpreted as connivance in her partner's fraudulent activities.
He was accused of connivance after ignoring the obvious signs of corruption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collusion - secret or illegal cooperation to deceive others
  • Synonym: complicity - the state of being involved with others in wrongdoing
  • Antonym: condemnation - the act of expressing strong disapproval; opposite of connivance

Etymology

From French 'connivence', from Latin 'conniventia' (a shutting of the eyes, connivance), from 'connivere' (to close the eyes, to overlook).
