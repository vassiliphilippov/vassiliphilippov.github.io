Machaeranthera tortifolia

1. NOUN

/ məˌkɪəɹənˈθɪrə ˌtɔːɹtəˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / məˌkɪərænˈθɪərə ˌtɔːtɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A wild aster species with greyish leafy stems and flower heads featuring narrow pale lavender or violet rays, native to rocky desert slopes in California, Arizona, and Utah.
species of wild aster from southwestern US

Examples

During the spring bloom, Machaeranthera tortifolia adds a splash of violet to the desert landscape.
Many hikers recognize Machaeranthera tortifolia by its distinctive narrow lavender rays.
Botanists study Machaeranthera tortifolia to understand plant adaptation in arid environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aster - plant genus with star-shaped flowers

Etymology

From Greek 'machaira' (sword) and 'anthera' (anther), referring to the plant's anther shape; species name from Latin 'tortifolia' (twisted leaves).
