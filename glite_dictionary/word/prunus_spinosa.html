Prunus spinosa

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs spaɪˈnoʊsə / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs spaɪˈnəʊsə / (BrE)
A thorny Eurasian shrub with small, plumlike blue-black fruits, commonly known as blackthorn or sloe.
thorny Eurasian shrub; blackthorn

Examples

The hedgerows were thick with Prunus spinosa, their branches heavy with ripe sloes.
In early spring, Prunus spinosa dazzles with a cloud of small white blossoms.
Farmers often plant Prunus spinosa to create dense, protective boundaries around their fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackthorn - Common name for Prunus spinosa, a thorny shrub with blue-black fruits
  • Synonym: sloe - The small, dark fruit of Prunus spinosa

Etymology

From Latin: 'prunus' meaning 'plum tree' and 'spinosa' meaning 'thorny.'
