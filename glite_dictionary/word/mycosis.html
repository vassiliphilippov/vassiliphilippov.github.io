mycosis

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈkoʊsɪs / (AmE), / maɪˈkəʊsɪs / (BrE)
An infection or disease in humans or animals caused by a fungus.
fungal infection or disease
medical

Examples

The patient developed a rare mycosis after receiving a transplant.
Doctors treated the mycosis with a combination of antifungal medications.
Some animals are also vulnerable to mycosis under certain environmental conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fungal infection - an infection caused by fungi

Etymology

From Greek mykēs (fungus) + -osis (condition).
