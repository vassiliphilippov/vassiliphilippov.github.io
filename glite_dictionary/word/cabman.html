cabman

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæbˌmən / (AmE), / ˈkab.mən / (BrE)
a man who drives a taxi or cab, especially a horse-drawn one in earlier times
taxi or cab driver (esp. historical)

Examples

The cabman waited patiently outside the hotel for his next fare.
In Victorian London, calling out to a cabman on a busy street was common.
The old photograph showed a cabman beside his horse-drawn carriage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taxi driver - a person who drives a taxi for a living

Etymology

From 'cab' (short for ‘cabriolet’, a type of carriage) + ‘man’, first used in the 19th century.
