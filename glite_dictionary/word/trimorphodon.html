Trimorphodon

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈmɔrf.oʊ.dɒn / (AmE), / traɪˈmɔː.fə.dɒn / (BrE)
A genus of nonvenomous colubrid snakes commonly known as lyre snakes, native to North and Central America.
a genus of lyre snakes

Examples

The Trimorphodon is easily recognized by the lyre-shaped marking on its head.
Researchers observed several Trimorphodon hunting for lizards at dusk.
A Trimorphodon can climb rocky slopes with remarkable agility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lyre snake - common name for snakes in the genus Trimorphodon

Etymology

From Greek tri- (three) + morphē (form) + odōn (tooth), referring to the three shapes of teeth.

2. NOUN

/ traɪˈmɔrfəˌdɑn / (AmE), / trɪˈmɔːfəˌdɒn / (BrE)
A genus of slender, nocturnal North American snakes known as lyre snakes, recognized for their lyre-shaped head markings.
a genus of North American lyre snakes

Examples

The Trimorphodon genus includes snakes commonly called lyre snakes due to the shape on their heads.
Researchers observed a Trimorphodon in the rocky canyons of Arizona.
Many herpetologists study Trimorphodon for its unique rear-fanged venom delivery system.

Etymology

From Greek 'tri-' (three) + 'morphē' (form) + 'odous' (tooth), referring to the arrangement of teeth.
