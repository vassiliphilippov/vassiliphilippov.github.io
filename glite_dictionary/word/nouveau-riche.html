nouveau-riche

1. NOUN

/ nuːˌvoʊˈriːʃ / (AmE), / ˌnuːvəʊˈriːʃ / (BrE)
A person who has recently acquired wealth, typically one perceived as lacking the culture or refinement associated with long-established upper classes.
person who is newly rich

Examples

After winning the lottery, he became a nouveau-riche and spent extravagantly on luxury cars.
The old money families looked down on the nouveau-riche who recently moved into the neighborhood.
She was determined not to behave like a stereotypical nouveau-riche despite her sudden fortune.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parvenu - a person of obscure origin who has gained wealth and position suddenly

Etymology

From French 'nouveau riche', meaning 'new rich'.

2. ADJ

/ nuˌvoʊ ˈriʃ / (AmE), / nuːˌvəʊ ˈriːʃ / (BrE)
Characteristic of a person who has recently acquired wealth but lacks the social refinement or taste traditionally associated with it.
displaying new wealth without refinement

Examples

The nouveau-riche guests flaunted their designer labels at every opportunity.
Her nouveau-riche taste in decor was obvious from the gold-plated furniture.
Many saw his nouveau-riche manners as tacky compared to the old aristocracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parvenu - a person who has suddenly risen to a higher social or economic class but is not accepted by others in that class

Etymology

From French 'nouveau riche' meaning 'new rich'.
