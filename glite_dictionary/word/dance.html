dance

1. VERB

/ dæns / (AmE), / dɑːns / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) to move lightly and quickly up and down or from side to side, often in a lively or irregular way.
move lightly up, down, or sideways

Examples

The flames danced in the fireplace, casting flickering shadows on the walls.
Sunlight danced across the surface of the rippling lake.
The children danced with excitement when they heard the good news.

Synset Examples

Dancing flames
The children danced with joy

/ dæns / (AmE), / dɑːns / (BrE)
a social event where people gather to dance

Examples

/ dæns / (AmE), / dɑːns / (BrE)
