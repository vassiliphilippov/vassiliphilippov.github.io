Anas clypeata

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəs klaɪˈpiːətə / (AmE), / ˌænəs klaɪˈpiːətə / (BrE)
A species of freshwater duck of the Northern Hemisphere, commonly known as the northern shoveler, recognized by its broad, flat bill.
species of duck with a broad, flat bill

Examples

I spotted an Anas clypeata swimming in the marsh this morning.
The distinctive bill of the Anas clypeata makes it easy to identify among other ducks.
During migration, flocks of Anas clypeata can be seen across northern lakes and wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: northern shoveler - common name for Anas clypeata, a duck with a broad, flat bill

Etymology

From Latin: 'Anas' meaning 'duck', 'clypeata' meaning 'shield-bearing', referring to its distinctive bill.
