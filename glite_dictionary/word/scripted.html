scripted

1. ADJ

/ ˈskrɪptɪd / (AmE), / ˈskrɪptɪd / (BrE)
(of a broadcast, film, play, etc.) written in advance rather than improvised; following a prepared script.
written in advance, not improvised

Examples

The interview was clearly scripted and lacked spontaneity.
Many reality TV shows are actually partly scripted.
Her speech sounded scripted, leaving little room for genuine emotion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unscripted - not written or planned in advance; improvised

Etymology

From 'script', meaning the written text of a play, film, or broadcast, plus the -ed suffix indicating the past participle or adjective form.
