Scaphosepalum

1. NOUN

/ skæfoʊˈsiːpələm / (AmE), / skafəʊˈsiːpələm / (BrE)
A genus of small, tropical American orchids with diminutive flowers borne on a single stalk.
genus of tropical American orchids

Examples

The Scaphosepalum orchids are known for their unusually shaped flowers.
Collectors value Scaphosepalum species for their compact size and unique appearance.
Several Scaphosepalum varieties are native to cloud forests in Central and South America.

Etymology

From Greek 'skaphos' (boat) and 'sepalum' (sepal), referring to the boat-shaped sepals of these orchids.

2. NOUN

/ skæf.oʊˈsɛp.əl.əm / (AmE), / skaf.əʊˈsɛp.əl.əm / (BrE)
A genus of small, tropical American orchids with tiny flowers borne singly on a scape, formerly sometimes classified under Masdevallia.
a genus of small tropical American orchids

Examples

Several rare Scaphosepalum species are found in the cloud forests of Ecuador.
Botanists recently discovered a new Scaphosepalum in the Andean region.
The tiny flowers of Scaphosepalum orchids make them popular with collectors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Masdevallia - a related genus of orchids to which Scaphosepalum species were sometimes assigned

Etymology

From Greek 'skaphe' (boat) + 'sepalum' (sepal), referring to the boat-shaped sepals of the flower.
