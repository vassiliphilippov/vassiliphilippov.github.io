ARNG

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ ɑr ɛn ˈdʒi / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ɑː ɛn ˈdʒiː / (BrE)
A civilian reserve component of the United States Army, composed of guardsmen who serve during overseas peacekeeping missions and local emergencies; short for Army National Guard.
U.S. Army National Guard reserve component
american

Examples

The ARNG was deployed during the recent hurricane to assist with relief efforts.
After completing basic training, he joined the ARNG to serve his community and country.
Many members of the ARNG balance civilian careers with their military duties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Army National Guard - The full name of ARNG, a component of the United States Army composed of part-time soldiers.

Etymology

Acronym from 'Army National Guard'.
