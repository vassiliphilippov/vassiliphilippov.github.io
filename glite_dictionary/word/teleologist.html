teleologist

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛliˈɑːlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ˌtɛlɪˈɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes in or advocates the doctrine that natural phenomena have purpose or design (teleology)
proponent of purpose in nature

Examples

As a teleologist, she argued that evolutionary processes are directed toward specific ends.
Many philosophers debate with teleologists about whether nature truly exhibits purposeful design.
The teleologist presented his theory during the philosophy seminar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teleology - the philosophical doctrine that natural phenomena have purpose or design

Etymology

From teleology + -ist; 'teleology' from Greek telos (end, purpose) and -logia (study).
