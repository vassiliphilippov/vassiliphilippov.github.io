to clarify

1. VERB

/ ˈklɛrəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈklærɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make something clear or easier to understand; to remove confusion or ambiguity
make clear or easier to understand

Examples

Could you please clarify your last point for me?
The instructions were rewritten to clarify the process for all employees.
Her response helped clarify what was expected from the team.

Synset Examples

clarify the mystery surrounding her death

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to explain - make an idea or situation clear to someone
  • Antonym: to confuse - make unclear or difficult to understand

Etymology

From Latin clarificare, from clarus (clear) + facere (to make).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00941888-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈklær.əˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈklær.ɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to make (a liquid) clear by removing impurities or solids, often by heating
make a liquid clear by removing impurities

Examples

You need to clarify the butter before adding it to the sauce.
The brewer used a special process to clarify the beer.
After straining, you should clarify the broth for a clearer soup.

Synset Examples

clarify the butter
clarify beer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to purify - to remove contaminants or unwanted elements; to make pure
  • Synonym: filter - to remove impurities or particles from a liquid using a filter

Etymology

From Late Latin clarificare, from clarus (clear) + -ficare (to make).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00506232-v
Synset POS: v
