flustered

1. ADJ

/ ˈflʌstərd / (AmE), / ˈflʌstəd / (BrE)
Nervous, confused, or agitated, especially because of unexpected circumstances or pressure.
nervous or confused; agitated

Examples

She became flustered when the interviewer asked her an unexpected question.
He looked flustered as he searched his pockets for the missing keys.
After running late, Jenna was flustered and spilled coffee on her shirt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rattled - informal; nervous or unable to think clearly
  • Antonym: composed - calm and in control of oneself

Etymology

From English verb "fluster" (to confuse or agitate), probably from a Scandinavian source such as Danish "flustre" (to bustle about).
