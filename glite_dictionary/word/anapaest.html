anapaest

1. NOUN

/ ˈænəˌpiːst / (AmE), / ˈanəpiːst / (BrE)
A metrical foot in poetry consisting of two unstressed syllables followed by a stressed syllable.
metrical foot: unstressed-unstressed-stressed

Examples

The line was written in anapaest to create a lively, galloping rhythm.
Many comic verses use anapaests to maintain a light and bouncing flow.
You can hear the distinctive beat of an anapaest in this poem's meter.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dactyl - metrical foot: stressed-unstressed-unstressed
  • Synonym: iamb - metrical foot: unstressed-stressed

Etymology

From Greek anapaistos (struck back, reversed), from ana- (back) + paiein (to strike).
