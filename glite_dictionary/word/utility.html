utility

1. ADJ

/ juːˈtɪləti / (AmE), / juːˈtɪlɪti / (BrE)
(of a player) able to substitute in multiple positions on a team, especially in sports such as baseball or football.
of a player, able to fill multiple positions

Examples

She was known as a utility player, often stepping in wherever the team needed help.
The coach values his utility infielder for his flexibility and reliability.
Having a utility player on the roster gives the manager more strategic options.

Synset Examples

a utility infielder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: versatile - able to adapt or be used for many different functions or activities

2. NOUN

/ juˈtɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / juːˈtɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being useful, especially for a particular purpose; practical usefulness
practical usefulness

Examples

The utility of this new software has transformed the way we work.
Before buying a new car, consider the utility and reliability it offers.
Researchers debated the utility of the proposed method in real-world situations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: usefulness - the quality of being useful
  • Antonym: uselessness - the quality of having no practical use
