dyslogia

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈloʊdʒiə / (AmE), / dɪsˈlɒdʒɪə / (BrE)
An impaired ability to express ideas verbally, usually due to difficulties in reasoning, as seen in certain mental disorders.
impaired verbal expression due to disordered reasoning
medical

Examples

The psychiatrist diagnosed the patient with dyslogia after noticing his difficulty in forming coherent explanations.
Individuals suffering from certain types of psychosis may display symptoms of dyslogia.
Dyslogia can interfere with communication and daily functioning if left untreated.

Etymology

From Greek dys- (bad, difficult) + logos (speech, word).
