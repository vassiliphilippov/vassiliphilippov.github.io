chivalric

1. ADJ

/ ʃɪˈvæl.rɪk / (AmE), / ʃɪˈval.rɪk / (BrE)
Relating to chivalry or knighthood, especially in the Middle Ages; characteristic of the manners, values, or practices associated with medieval knights.
relating to chivalry or knighthood

Examples

The ceremony was held in a grand hall decorated in a chivalric style befitting the occasion.
He devoted his life to the study of chivalric values of honor and courage.
The novel is set against the backdrop of chivalric adventures and legendary quests.

Synset Examples

chivalric rites
the knightly years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knightly - Relating to knights or chivalry

Etymology

From Old French 'chevalerique', from 'chevalier' (knight), from Late Latin 'caballarius' (horseman).
