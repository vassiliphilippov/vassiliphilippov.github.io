vaccination

1. NOUN

/ ˌvæksɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvæksɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of administering a vaccine to a person or animal to protect against a specific disease
the act of receiving a vaccine
medical

Examples

Many schools require proof of vaccination against measles and mumps.
After receiving her flu vaccination, she felt reassured during flu season.
Public health campaigns promote vaccination as a way to prevent outbreaks of serious diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immunization - the process by which a person becomes protected against a disease
  • Antonym: infection - the process of being invaded by pathogenic organisms

Etymology

From Latin 'vaccinus' (from cows), after the use of cowpox matter in early vaccines.
