Arenaria serpyllifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛrəˈnɛəriə ˌsɜr.pɪˈloʊli.ə / (AmE), / ˌær.əˈnɛə.ri.ə ˌsɜː.pɪˈlɪə / (BrE)
A low, sprawling annual plant of the pink family, native to Eurasia and widespread in North America, with small white flowers.
sprawling Eurasian annual plant; naturalized in North America

Examples

Arenaria serpyllifolia often grows in dry, sandy soils along roadsides and fields.
Botanists have identified Arenaria serpyllifolia as an invasive species in certain North American areas.
With its delicate white flowers, Arenaria serpyllifolia can be easily overlooked among other wild plants.

Etymology

From Latin: 'arenaria' meaning 'sandwort', and 'serpyllifolia' meaning 'with leaves like wild thyme' (serpyllum).
