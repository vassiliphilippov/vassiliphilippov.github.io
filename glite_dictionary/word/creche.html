creche

1. NOUN

/ kreɪʃ / (AmE), / kreɪʃ / (BrE)
A model or tableau representing the scene of Christ's birth in the stable at Bethlehem, typically displayed at Christmas.
nativity scene display

Examples

Every Christmas, the church sets up a beautiful creche near the altar.
The children enjoyed placing the figures into the creche as part of the holiday tradition.
A handmade wooden creche stood on the mantel throughout December.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nativity scene - a display or model depicting the birth of Jesus

Etymology

From French 'crèche' (crib, manger), from Old Provençal 'crescha', from Latin 'cripta' (a crib).

2. NOUN

/ kreɪʃ / (AmE), / kreɪʃ / (BrE)
a hospital or institution where foundlings (infant children of unknown parents) are taken in and cared for
institution for foundlings

Examples

The abandoned newborn was brought to the local creche where specialists could care for her.
Historically, a creche was established to provide shelter for foundlings in large cities.
The nuns worked tirelessly at the creche to ensure every child received food and comfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orphanage - an institution for children whose parents are dead or unable to care for them

Etymology

From French 'crèche' (crib, manger), originally referring to a crib for the nativity scene, later extended to institutions caring for children.
