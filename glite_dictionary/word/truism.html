truism

1. NOUN

/ ˈtruːɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈtruːɪzəm / (BrE)
a statement that is so obviously true as to be hardly worth mentioning
an obvious or self-evident truth

Examples

His speech was filled with truisms that everyone already agreed upon.
It is a truism that time waits for no one.
The manager's comments amounted to little more than a truism about hard work leading to success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: platitude - a remark or statement, especially one with a moral content, that has been used too often to be interesting or thoughtful
  • Synonym: cliché - an overused phrase or opinion that lacks originality

Etymology

From French *truism*, from true + -ism.
