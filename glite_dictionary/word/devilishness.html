devilishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛvəlɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəlɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of resembling or displaying the characteristics of a devil, such as mischief, wickedness, or playfully evil behavior
quality of being devil-like; mischief or wickedness

Examples

There was a certain devilishness in his grin that made everyone wary of his next move.
Her devilishness came out during the prank, leaving her friends both amazed and startled.
The actor captured the villain's devilishness perfectly in his performance.

Synset Examples

The devilishness of his smile then faded to one more of shyness as he spoke further.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mischievousness - the quality of causing trouble in a playful way
  • Antonym: angelicness - the quality of being like an angel; innocence or goodness

Etymology

From 'devilish' (devil + -ish) + -ness (forming nouns expressing state or quality).
