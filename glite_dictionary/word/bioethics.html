bioethics

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊˈɛθ.ɪks / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.əʊˈɛθ.ɪks / (BrE)
The study of ethical issues and moral principles arising in the fields of medicine, biology, and biomedical research.
ethical issues in medicine and biology

Examples

The rapid advancement of technology in medicine has raised many questions in bioethics.
She decided to pursue a degree in bioethics after witnessing ethical dilemmas in clinical practice.
Debates in bioethics often focus on topics like euthanasia, genetic engineering, and patient consent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medical ethics - the study of moral values and judgments in medicine

Etymology

From Greek bios (life) + ethics (study of morals).
