unsegregated

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnˌsɛɡ.rɪˈɡeɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.sɛɡ.rɪˈɡeɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
no longer subject to segregation; integration has been achieved
not segregated; integrated

Examples

After years of activism, the school district was finally declared unsegregated.
The dining hall became unsegregated, allowing everyone to sit wherever they wished.
Many believe that an unsegregated workplace fosters a more inclusive environment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: segregated - kept separate or apart, especially by law or custom

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' meaning 'not' and 'segregated'.
