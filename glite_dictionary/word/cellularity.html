cellularity

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛl.jəˈlær.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌsɛl.juˈlar.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being composed of cells or having a cellular structure
state of having or composed of cells
medical

Examples

The pathologist assessed the cellularity of the tissue sample under the microscope.
Tumors can be classified by their degree of cellularity and structure.
Increased cellularity was observed in the inflamed organ tissue.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acellularity - absence of cells

Etymology

From 'cellular' + '-ity', formed after cellular (from Latin 'cellula', a small room) and '-ity' denoting state or condition.
