Polemoniaceae

1. NOUN

/ poʊˌlɛməˌniˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / pɒˌlɛməniːˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of chiefly herbaceous plants, often with showy flowers, including phlox and related genera
a plant family with showy flowers (phlox family)

Examples

Many species in the Polemoniaceae family are cultivated for their bright and attractive flowers.
Phlox, a well-known member of the Polemoniaceae, can be found in gardens worldwide.
Researchers have discovered new species within the Polemoniaceae family in North America.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on the genus Polemonium, named after the Greek philosopher Polemon.

2. NOUN

/ poʊˌliːmoʊniˈæsiːˌiː / (AmE), / pɒlɪˌməʊniˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of widely distributed, chiefly herbaceous plants in the order Polemoniales, often with showy flowers, including phlox and Jacob's ladder.
a family of flowering plants (phlox family)

Examples

The Polemoniaceae are known for their bright and attractive blossoms.
Jacob’s ladder, a member of the Polemoniaceae, thrives in cool temperate regions.
Several garden favorites, such as phlox, belong to the Polemoniaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phlox - a genus of flowering plants in the Polemoniaceae family

Etymology

From the genus name Polemonium, from Greek polemonion (a type of plant).
