equinox

1. NOUN

/ ˈikwəˌnɑks / (AmE), / ˈiːkwɪnɒks / (BrE)
Either of the two times in the year when the sun crosses the celestial equator, resulting in days and nights of equal length.
time of equal day and night

Examples

The spring equinox marks the start of longer days in the Northern Hemisphere.
Ancient cultures often held ceremonies to celebrate the autumn equinox.
During the equinox, day and night are nearly the same length all over the world.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: solstice - either of the two times in the year when the sun is at its greatest distance from the celestial equator

Etymology

From Latin aequinoctium, from aequus (equal) + nox, noctis (night).

2. NOUN

/ ˈiː.kwɪ.nɑks / (AmE), / ˈiː.kwɪ.nɒks / (BrE)
Either of the two times each year when the sun crosses the equator and day and night are approximately equal in length.
time when day and night are equal

Examples

The spring equinox marks the beginning of longer days in the northern hemisphere.
Many cultures celebrate festivals around the autumn equinox.
On the equinox, day and night are nearly the same length everywhere on Earth.

Etymology

From Latin 'aequinoctium', from 'aequus' (equal) + 'nox, noctis' (night).
