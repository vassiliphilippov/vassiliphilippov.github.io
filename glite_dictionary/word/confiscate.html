confiscate

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑnfɪskeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒnfɪskeɪt / (BrE)
(of property) taken or surrendered as a penalty, especially by authority.
taken by authority as a penalty

Examples

The police stored the confiscate goods in a secure facility until the investigation was completed.
After the trial, the government auctioned off several confiscate properties linked to criminal activity.
The customs officer listed the confiscate items in an official report for further examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seized - taken possession of forcibly or by authority
  • Synonym: forfeited - lost or surrendered as a penalty for wrongdoing

Etymology

From Latin confiscatus, past participle of confiscare, from com- (together) + fiscus (public treasury).

2. VERB

/ ˈkɑːn.fə.skeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.fɪ.skeɪt / (BrE)
to officially take something away from someone, especially as a penalty or for legal reasons
officially take away; seize by authority

Examples

The airport officials decided to confiscate any forbidden items found in passengers' luggage.
School authorities have the right to confiscate mobile phones if they are used during class.
Police were forced to confiscate the counterfeit goods during the raid.

Synset Examples

The FBI seized the drugs
The customs agents impounded the illegal shipment
The police confiscated the stolen artwork

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to seize - to take hold of suddenly or forcibly, especially by legal authority
  • Synonym: to impound - to take legal possession of something until a debt or claim is settled
  • Antonym: return - to give back something taken or borrowed

Etymology

From Latin confiscare, from com- (together) + fiscus (public treasury).
