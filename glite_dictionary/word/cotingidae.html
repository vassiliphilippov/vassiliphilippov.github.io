Cotingidae

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈtɪn.ɪˌdi / (AmE), / kəʊˈtɪn.ɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical American birds that includes cotingas and umbrella birds, known for their varied and often colorful plumage and diverse vocalizations.
family of tropical American birds (cotingas, umbrella birds)

Examples

The Cotingidae family includes some of the most vibrantly colored birds in South America.
Researchers observed rare mating dances among members of the Cotingidae during the expedition.
Many Cotingidae species inhabit the dense rainforests of the Amazon basin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cotinga - a brightly colored South American bird of the Cotingidae family
  • Synonym: umbrella bird - a bird with a large umbrella-like crest, member of Cotingidae

Etymology

From New Latin Cotingidae, from Cotinga (type genus) + -idae (family suffix).
