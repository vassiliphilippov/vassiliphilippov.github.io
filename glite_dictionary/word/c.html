c

1. NOUN

/ siː / (AmE), / siː / (BrE)
the scientific symbol for the speed of light in a vacuum, exactly 299,792,458 meters per second
the speed of light in a vacuum (symbol)

Examples

In Einstein's famous equation, c represents the speed of light in a vacuum.
The value of c is fundamental to modern physics.
Scientists use c when calculating distances in space.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speed of light - the rate at which light travels in a vacuum

Etymology

Scientific symbol from Latin 'celeritas' meaning 'swiftness.'
