bimetallic

1. ADJ

/ ˌbaɪ.məˈtæl.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.məˈtæl.ɪk / (BrE)
Consisting of or formed from two different metals or alloys, especially sheets bonded together.
made of two different metals or alloys

Examples

Many thermostats use a bimetallic strip to control temperature changes.
A bimetallic coin is harder to counterfeit than coins made from a single metal.
The engineer explained how the bimetallic sheet responded to heat by bending.

Etymology

From prefix 'bi-' (two) and 'metal', meaning relating to two metals.

2. ADJ

/ ˌbaɪməˈtælɪk / (AmE), / ˌbaɪmɪˈtælɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving a monetary system based on the use of two metals, typically gold and silver, as legal tender at a fixed ratio.
relating to use of two metals in a monetary system

Examples

The country adopted a bimetallic standard, allowing both gold and silver coins to circulate as money.
Debates about the bimetallic monetary system were central to 19th-century economic policy.
Advocates of bimetallic standards believed it would stabilize currency values by using both metals.

Synset Examples

in a bimetallistic system both gold and silver can constitute legal tender

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monometallic - using a monetary standard based on a single metal

Etymology

From bi- (two) + metallic (relating to metal); used in the context of monetary systems since the 19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02675059-a
Synset POS: a
