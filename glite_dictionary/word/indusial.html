indusial

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈduːsiəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈdjuːsɪəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of an indusium (a botanical or zoological covering or membrane)
relating to an indusium (covering or membrane)

Examples

The indusial layer protects the developing sporangia on the underside of fern fronds.
Researchers examined the indusial structures formed by caddisfly larvae along the stream bed.
Botanists noted the presence of indusial coverings during their survey of the local fern species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indusium - a membranous covering or layer in plants or insects

Etymology

From Latin indusium (cloak, tunic, covering).
