prince

1. NOUN

/ prɪns / (AmE), / prɪns / (BrE)
a male member of a royal family, especially the son of a king or queen who is not the sovereign himself
male royal; son of a monarch

Examples

The young prince traveled abroad to strengthen diplomatic ties.
Everyone in the kingdom celebrated when the prince was born.
As a prince, he had many royal duties to fulfill.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: princess - a female member of a royal family, especially the daughter of a monarch or wife of a prince
  • Degree Higher: king - the male sovereign ruler of a kingdom
  • Degree Lower: duke - a nobleman of high rank, just below a prince

Etymology

From Old French 'prince', from Latin 'princeps' (first citizen, leader).
