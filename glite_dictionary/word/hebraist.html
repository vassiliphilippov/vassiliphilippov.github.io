Hebraist

1. NOUN

/ hiˈbreɪɪst / (AmE), / hɪˈbreɪɪst / (BrE)
a scholar or linguist specializing in the Hebrew language or literature
scholar of Hebrew language or literature

Examples

The university appointed a renowned Hebraist to the faculty of religious studies.
As a Hebraist, she translated several ancient manuscripts into English.
Many Hebraists contribute to our understanding of biblical texts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hebrew scholar - a person who studies or is an expert in the Hebrew language or literature

Etymology

From Hebrew + -ist (person concerned with or specializing in)
