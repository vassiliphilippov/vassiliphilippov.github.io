role

1. NOUN

/ roʊl / (AmE), / rəʊl / (BrE)
the part played by an actor portraying a character in a play, film, or other performance
part played by an actor

Examples

She landed the leading role in the school play.
He prepared for weeks to get into character for the demanding role of Hamlet.
Playing a comic role was a new challenge for the experienced actor.

Synset Examples

she played the part of Desdemona

Related Terms

  • Synonym: part - the character played by an actor
  • Synonym: character - a person portrayed in a play or film

Etymology

From French rôle (part in a play), from Old French roule (roll of parchment), referring to the actor's script.

2. NOUN

/ roʊl / (AmE), / rəʊl / (BrE)
the function or position that someone has in a particular situation, organization, or society
function or position of a person

Examples

She was given a leading role in the company's new project.
As a parent, his role is to guide and support his children.
Each member of the committee has a specific role to play in the decision-making process.

Synset Examples

what is your role on the team?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: function - the purpose or typical activity of something or someone
  • Synonym: position - a job or role in an organization
  • Synonym: duty - a moral or legal obligation; a responsibility

Etymology

From French 'rôle', originally referring to a part played by an actor.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00723312-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ roʊl / (AmE), / rəʊl / (BrE)
the set of actions, responsibilities, or behaviors assigned to or expected of a person or group in a particular situation
expected actions or duties of a person or group

Examples

Every member of the team has a specific role to play in the project.
As a parent, her role involves both nurturing and disciplining her children.
The committee discussed the role of technology in modern education.

Synset Examples

the function of a teacher
the government must do its part
play its role

Related Terms

  • Synonym: function - the job or purpose that something or someone has

Etymology

From French rôle (part played by an actor), from Old French rolle (roll, list), from Latin rotulus (roll, little wheel).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
