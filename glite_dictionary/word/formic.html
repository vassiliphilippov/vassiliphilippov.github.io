formic

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɔrmɪk / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmɪk / (BrE)
relating to or containing formic acid, or derived from it.
containing or derived from formic acid

Examples

Some insects secrete formic acid as a defense mechanism against predators.
The laboratory is studying the properties of formic compounds in organic chemistry.
Exposure to formic vapors can cause irritation to the eyes and respiratory tract.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: formic acid - a colorless pungent acid found in ant venom and some plants

Etymology

From Latin 'formica' (ant), because formic acid was first obtained from ants.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɔːr.mɪk / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.mɪk / (BrE)
relating to or derived from ants, especially used to describe acids or substances produced by ants
relating to ants or substances derived from ants

Examples

The scientists studied the formic acid secreted by the ants as a defense mechanism.
The distinct odor in the laboratory was attributed to a formic compound.
Some plants mimic formic scents to attract ant protectors.

Etymology

From Latin 'formica' (ant), as in 'formic acid', first obtained from ants.
