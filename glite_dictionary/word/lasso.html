lasso

1. NOUN

/ ləˈsoʊ / (AmE), / ˈlasəʊ / (BrE)
a long rope with a sliding noose at one end, used especially for catching cattle or horses.
noosed rope for catching animals

Examples

The cowboy quickly swung his lasso and caught the runaway calf.
She learned how to throw a lasso while working on the ranch.
The performer demonstrated his impressive lasso skills at the rodeo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noose - a loop formed in a rope, used for catching or holding something

Etymology

From Spanish 'lazo' (loop, snare), from Latin 'laqueus' (noose, snare).

2. VERB

/ læˈsoʊ / (AmE), / læˈsəʊ / (BrE)
to catch an animal by throwing a lasso around it
catch with a lasso

Examples

The cowboy managed to lasso the stray calf before it ran off into the hills.
She tried to lasso the wild pony, but it kept dodging the rope.
During the show, he lassoed three cattle in less than a minute.

Synset Examples

rope cows

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rope - to catch with a rope, especially in ranching; synonym for 'lasso' as a verb

Etymology

From Spanish 'lazo' (noose, snare), from Latin 'laqueus' (noose, snare).
