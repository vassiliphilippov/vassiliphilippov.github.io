anchorage

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋkɚɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈæŋkərɪdʒ / (BrE)
the act of dropping and securing an anchor; the process of anchoring a vessel
act of dropping and securing an anchor

Examples

The captain supervised the anchorage of the vessel as they approached the harbor.
Proper anchorage is essential to prevent a ship from drifting during strong winds.
Night fell quickly after the anchorage was completed, and the crew finally relaxed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mooring - the act of making a boat fast with cables or anchors

Etymology

From anchor + -age, influenced by Old French ancrage.

/ ˈæŋ.kɚ.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈæŋ.kər.ɪdʒ / (BrE)

Related Terms
