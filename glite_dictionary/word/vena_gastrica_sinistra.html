vena gastrica sinistra

1. NOUN

/ ˌveɪ.nə ɡæˈstrɪ.kə sɪnˈɪs.trə / (AmE), / ˌviː.nə ɡæˈstrɪ.kə sɪnˈɪs.trə / (BrE)
the left gastric vein; a vein arising from the region of the stomach's cardia, running in the lesser omentum, and emptying into the portal vein
the left gastric vein of the stomach
medical

Examples

The vena gastrica sinistra drains blood from the upper part of the stomach into the portal vein.
A blockage in the vena gastrica sinistra can lead to complications in the hepatic portal system.
Surgeons must take care not to damage the vena gastrica sinistra during gastric procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: portal vein - large vein carrying blood from stomach, intestines, and other organs to the liver

Etymology

From Latin: 'vena' (vein), 'gastrica' (of the stomach), 'sinistra' (left).
