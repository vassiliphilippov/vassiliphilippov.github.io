undoing

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈduɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈduːɪŋ / (BrE)
an act of reversing or cancelling the effect of a previous action
reversal or cancellation of an earlier action

Examples

The court's decision amounted to the undoing of the previous verdict.
Her apology was the undoing of all the progress they had made.
The new law was intended as the undoing of the reforms introduced last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reversal - an act of changing to the opposite direction or position

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (reversing) and 'doing' (an act).

2. NOUN

/ ʌnˈduːɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈduːɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of unfastening or loosening ties or bindings
act of unfastening or loosening

Examples

After the ceremony, the undoing of the many ribbons took almost as long as tying them.
She spent several minutes on the undoing of the intricate knots.
The magician's swift undoing of the ropes amazed the audience.

Synset Examples

the tying of bow ties is an art; the untying is easy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: untying - the act of loosening a knot or tie
  • Antonym: fastening - the act of securing or tying something

Etymology

From Old English 'undōn' (to unfasten, open), with the noun formed by adding the suffix '-ing'.
