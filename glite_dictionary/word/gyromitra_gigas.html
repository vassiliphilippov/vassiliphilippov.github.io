Gyromitra gigas

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒaɪrəˈmaɪtrə ˈɡaɪgəs / (AmE), / ˌdʒaɪərəˈmaɪtrə ˈɡɪɡəs / (BrE)
A species of large false morel mushroom with a yellow to brown, wrinkled cap and an irregular stalk, known for its early spring fruiting.
a species of false morel mushroom

Examples

We found a cluster of Gyromitra gigas in the damp forest during our spring hike.
The Gyromitra gigas is often mistaken for edible morels, though it can be toxic.
Botanists study the life cycle and habitat preferences of Gyromitra gigas across Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: false morel - a group of mushrooms that resemble true morels but are often toxic

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name Gyromitra (from Greek 'gyros' meaning circle, and 'mitra' meaning headband or turban) and species epithet gigas (from Greek 'gigas', meaning giant).
