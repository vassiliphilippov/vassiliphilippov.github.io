dazzling

1. ADJ

/ ˈdæz.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdæz.lɪŋ / (BrE)
extremely impressive, attractive, or brilliant, often in a way that captures attention or is suggestive of intense brightness.
strikingly impressive or brilliant

Examples

The actress wore a dazzling diamond necklace to the gala.
His dazzling performance earned him a standing ovation from the audience.
Sunlight flooding through the window created a dazzling display of colors on the floor.

Synset Examples

the skater's dazzling virtuosic leaps
these great best canvases still look as astonishing and as invitingly new as they did … when … his fulgurant popularity was in full growth
adventures related … in a style both vivid and fulgurous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brilliant - very bright or impressive
  • Synonym: blinding - so bright as to make seeing difficult
  • Antonym: dull - lacking brightness, vividness, or impressiveness

Etymology

From the verb 'dazzle,' which likely originated from Middle English 'dasel,' of uncertain origin.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdæzlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdæzlɪŋ / (BrE)
shining with a very bright or intense light, making it difficult to see
shining very brightly; intensely bright

Examples

The dazzling sunlight reflected off the snow, making it hard to see.
She was momentarily blinded by the dazzling headlights of the oncoming car.
The ice sparkled with a dazzling brilliance under the winter sun.

Synset Examples

the blazing sun
blinding headlights
dazzling snow
fulgent patterns of sunlight
the glaring sun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blinding - so bright it makes vision difficult
  • Synonym: glaring - giving out a strong, harsh light
  • Antonym: dim - not shining brightly or clearly

Etymology

From dazzle (to blind temporarily), likely Middle English, from Old Norse 'dasask' (to become weary).
