factual

1. ADJ

/ ˈfæktʃuəl / (AmE), / ˈfæktʃuəl / (BrE)
concerned with or containing facts rather than opinions or interpretations
relating to facts

Examples

The documentary provided a factual account of the events.
Her report was praised for being clear and factual, without personal bias.
Please separate factual information from your opinions in your essay.

Synset Examples

factual considerations

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fictional - relating to fiction; invented or imagined

Etymology

From fact + -ual, patterned after actual.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02910364-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈfæk.tʃu.əl / (AmE), / ˈfæk.tʃu.əl / (BrE)
existing in fact; real or actual, rather than imagined or theoretical.
existing in fact; real

Examples

We must base our conclusions on factual evidence, not on guesswork.
The documentary provided a factual account of the historical events.
She insisted that her report was strictly factual and free from personal opinions.

Synset Examples

rocks and trees … the actual world
actual heroism
the actual things that produced the emotion you experienced

Related Terms

  • Synonym: actual - existing in fact; real and not imagined

Etymology

From Latin factum (a thing done) + -al.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01940178-s
Synset POS: s
