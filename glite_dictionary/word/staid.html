staid

1. ADJ

/ steɪd / (AmE), / steɪd / (BrE)
Serious, respectable, and unadventurous; not showy or lively.
serious and dignified

Examples

He comes from a staid family that values tradition above all else.
The office environment was far too staid for her creative personality.
The city has a reputation for being staid, but it's lively beneath the surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sedate - calm, dignified, and unhurried
  • Antonym: lively - full of life and energy

Etymology

Past participle of 'stay', from Middle English 'stayed', meaning 'fixed, settled'.
