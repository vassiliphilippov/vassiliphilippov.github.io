Baphia nitida

1. NOUN

/ bæˈfiːə nɪˈtiːdə / (AmE), / ˈbæfɪə nɪˈtɪdə / (BrE)
A small shrubby West African tree with hard wood used for dyeing, producing a red dye.
African tree used for red dye

Examples

The wood of Baphia nitida is prized for its vivid red dye.
Artisans in West Africa have used Baphia nitida for centuries to color fabrics.
Botanists study Baphia nitida for its unique chemical properties.

Etymology

From Modern Latin, the scientific name for the species.
