typography

1. NOUN

/ taɪˈpɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / taɪˈpɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the art or process of arranging type, printing with it, or designing printed matter
arrangement and design of printed type

Examples

The typography of the magazine is both modern and elegant, making each page a pleasure to read.
He studied typography in art school to learn how to design books professionally.
Advances in digital printing have dramatically changed the field of typography.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: typesetting - arranging type for printing

Etymology

From Greek 'typos' (impression, mark) + 'graphia' (writing).

2. NOUN

/ taɪˈpɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / taɪˈpɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the art and technique of arranging type to make written language legible, readable, and visually appealing when displayed or printed
art of arranging printed type

Examples

Modern graphic designers study typography to improve the appearance of text in their projects.
The book's elegant typography made it a pleasure to read.
She developed an interest in typography after taking a course on printing history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: typesetting - the process of setting text for printing

Etymology

From Greek 'typos' (impression, mark) and 'graphia' (writing).

Synset POS: n
