cryogenic

1. ADJ

/ kraɪ.oʊˈdʒɛn.ɪk / (AmE), / kraɪ.əʊˈdʒɛn.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving very low temperatures, especially those used in scientific and industrial applications.
relating to very low temperatures

Examples

Scientists use cryogenic equipment to store and transport liquefied gases safely.
Some medical procedures require cryogenic temperatures to preserve biological samples.
The space industry relies on cryogenic fuels for rocket propulsion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cryogenic - involving the production or use of very low temperatures
  • Antonym: thermogenic - producing or pertaining to heat, especially in biological contexts

Etymology

From Greek 'kryos' (frost) + 'genic' (producing or caused by).
