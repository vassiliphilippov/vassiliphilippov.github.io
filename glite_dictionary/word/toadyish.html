toadyish

1. ADJ

/ ˈtoʊdiɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈtəʊdiɪʃ / (BrE)
Behaving in a way that is excessively flattering or servile in order to gain favor from someone in authority.
excessively flattering or servile

Examples

Despite his qualifications, his toadyish attitude annoyed his colleagues.
She gave a toadyish speech praising the boss at every opportunity.
His constant, toadyish compliments made everyone uncomfortable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sycophantic - behaving in a way that flatters powerful people too much
  • Synonym: obsequious - excessively eager to please or obey someone important
  • Antonym: independent - not influenced or controlled by others; not servile

Etymology

From 'toady' (a sycophantic person) + '-ish' (forming adjectives).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtoʊdiɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈtəʊdiɪʃ / (BrE)
behaving in an excessively flattering or servile way towards someone important in order to gain advantage
excessively flattering or servile

Examples

His toadyish compliments to the boss annoyed his colleagues.
The politician surrounded himself with toadyish advisers who rarely disagreed with him.
She adopted a toadyish attitude, praising everyone in power at every opportunity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sycophantic - excessively flattering to people in authority; behaving like a sycophant
  • Synonym: obsequious - obedient or attentive to an excessive or servile degree
  • Antonym: independent - free from outside control; not depending on another's authority

Etymology

From 'toady' (a person who flatters others for personal gain) + '-ish' (forming adjectives).
