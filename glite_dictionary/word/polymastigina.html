Polymastigina

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɒliˌmæˈstɪdʒɪnə / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˌmastɪˈdʒiːnə / (BrE)
A group of small, usually parasitic flagellates belonging to the protozoan suborder Polymastigina.
group of parasitic protozoan flagellates

Examples

The gut of some termites contains Polymastigina that assist in digesting wood.
Under the microscope, researchers observed several Polymastigina moving rapidly.
Most members of Polymastigina are known for their parasitic lifestyles in different host organisms.

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) and 'mastix' (whip), referring to multiple flagella.

2. NOUN

An order of small, usually parasitic flagellate protozoans.
order of parasitic flagellates

Examples

Researchers discovered new species within the Polymastigina in tropical soils.
Members of the Polymastigina are known for their parasitic relationship with invertebrates.
The biology class studied the life cycle of several Polymastigina species under the microscope.

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'mastix' (whip, referring to flagella).
