forwarding

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrwərdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːwədɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of sending something on to another destination, especially mail or communications
the act of sending on to another destination

Examples

The forwarding of important documents was handled by the office staff.
Automatic forwarding of emails can save you a lot of time.
The company charges a fee for the forwarding of parcels to overseas locations.

Synset Examples

the forwarding of mail to a new address is done automatically
the forwarding of resumes to the personnel department

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transmission - the act of sending or conveying from one person or place to another
  • Synonym: delivery - the act of bringing something to a recipient

Etymology

From the verb 'forward,' itself from Old English 'foreweard' (toward the front, in advance).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrwərdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːwədɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of advancing or helping the progress of something, especially a project or enterprise
advancement or promotion of something

Examples

His innovative ideas contributed significantly to the forwarding of the project.
The committee is dedicated to the forwarding of educational reform.
Her research played a crucial role in the forwarding of scientific knowledge.

Synset Examples

his experience in marketing resulted in the forwarding of his career

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advancement - the process of promoting or moving something forward
  • Synonym: promotion - the act of encouraging or supporting the progress of something

Etymology

From the verb 'forward' (to advance), formed by adding the noun suffix '-ing'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00250647-n
Synset POS: n
