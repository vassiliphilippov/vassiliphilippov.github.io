Muscadet

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌskəˈdeɪ / (AmE), / ˌmʌskəˈdeɪ / (BrE)
A dry white wine produced in the Loire Valley region of France, especially around Nantes.
dry white wine from France's Loire Valley

Examples

She ordered a chilled bottle of Muscadet to go with the fresh oysters.
The crisp acidity of Muscadet makes it perfect for pairing with seafood dishes.
During their trip to France, they toured several Muscadet vineyards near the Atlantic coast.

Etymology

From French 'Muscadet', named after the region near Nantes where it is produced.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmʌskəˈdeɪ / (AmE), / ˌmʌskəˈdeɪ / (BrE)
a dry white wine made from the Melon de Bourgogne grape, produced in the Loire Valley region of France
dry French white wine from the Loire

Examples

We enjoyed a chilled bottle of Muscadet with our seafood dinner.
The menu recommends pairing oysters with a crisp Muscadet.
He prefers Muscadet to Chardonnay because of its light, fresh flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sancerre - dry white wine from the Loire Valley, France
  • Synonym: Chardonnay - type of white wine and the grape used to make it

Etymology

French, from the word muscade (nutmeg), referring to the aroma of the wine.
