wonky

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɑŋki / (AmE), / ˈwɒŋki / (BrE)
not steady, stable, or firmly fixed; shaky or likely to collapse, especially because of structural weakness or defect
unstable or shaky
informal

Examples

The table in the corner was so wonky that it spilled my drink twice.
After years in the garden, the old bench has become rather wonky.
Be careful on that wonky step; it's not safe to stand on.

Synset Examples

a rickety table
a wobbly chair with shaky legs
the ladder felt a little wobbly
the bridge still stands though one of the arches is wonky

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rickety - liable to collapse or break; shaky from weakness
  • Synonym: wobbly - tending to move unsteadily from side to side

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɑŋ.ki / (AmE), / ˈwɒŋ.ki / (BrE)
Not straight; turned or twisted to one side; askew or crooked.
askew; not straight or level
informal

Examples

The picture frame on the wall looked a bit wonky after the earthquake.
She noticed the shelf was wonky and wobbling when she tried to place a book on it.
His glasses sat wonky on his nose, making him look slightly eccentric.

Synset Examples

a … youth with a gorgeous red necktie all awry
his wig was, as the British say, skew-whiff

Related Terms

  • Synonym: askew - not in a straight or level position
  • Synonym: crooked - bent, twisted, or not in a straight line
  • Antonym: straight - without a bend or curve
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02320130-s
Synset POS: s
