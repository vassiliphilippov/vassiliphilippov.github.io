coeducation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊˌɛdʒuˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkəʊˌɛdjuˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the system of educating male and female students together in the same institution or classes
mixed-gender education

Examples

Many universities have adopted coeducation to promote equality between genders.
The introduction of coeducation changed the social dynamics of the school significantly.
Some parents still prefer single-sex schools over coeducation for their children.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: single-sex education - education in which males and females are taught separately

Etymology

From co- (together) and education; first used in the 19th century.
