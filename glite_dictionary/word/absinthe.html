absinthe

1. NOUN

/ ˈæb.sɪnθ / (AmE), / ˈæb.sɪnθ / (BrE)
a strong green alcoholic liqueur flavored with wormwood and anise, traditionally associated with France and Switzerland
strong green liqueur with wormwood and anise

Examples

The bartender poured a glass of absinthe and set it aflame as part of the ritual.
Artists in 19th-century Paris were famously fond of drinking absinthe for inspiration.
Its unique flavor comes from the blend of wormwood, anise, and other herbs in absinthe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pastis - an anise-flavored spirit from France, similar to absinthe but without wormwood

Etymology

From French 'absinthe', from Latin 'absinthium' (wormwood), from Greek 'apsínthion'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæb.sɪnθ / (AmE), / ˈæb.sɪnθ / (BrE)
An aromatic herb (Artemisia absinthium) of temperate Eurasia and North Africa, noted for its bitter taste and used in making the liqueur absinthe.
aromatic herb for liqueur making

Examples

The absinthe plant grows wild in many parts of southern Europe.
Chefs sometimes add dried absinthe to bitters for its unique flavor.
Harvesting absinthe requires care due to its intensely bitter taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wormwood - another name for the herb Artemisia absinthium

Etymology

From French absinthe, from Latin absinthium, from Greek apsínthion (ἀψίνθιον), referring to wormwood.
