alternate

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔl.tɚ.neɪt / (AmE), / ˈɔːl.tə.neɪt / (BrE)
(of things in a series) every second one; happening or appearing every other time.
every second one; every other

Examples

The class meets on alternate Saturdays throughout the semester.
We take alternate routes to avoid the morning traffic.
Alternate pages of the book are printed in color.

Synset Examples

the cleaning lady comes on alternate Wednesdays

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consecutive - following continuously in an unbroken sequence

Etymology

From Latin 'alternatus', past participle of 'alternare' (to do by turns), from 'alternus' (one after the other).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
