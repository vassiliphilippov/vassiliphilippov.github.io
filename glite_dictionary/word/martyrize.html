to martyrize

1. VERB

/ ˈmɑrtəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtəraɪz / (BrE)
to torture or torment (someone) as if making them a martyr
torture or torment like a martyr

Examples

The unjust ruler sought to martyrize anyone who spoke out against his regime.
She felt as though fate had chosen to martyrize her with one hardship after another.
Writers have described how oppressive regimes martyrize dissidents to instill fear in others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torture - to inflict severe pain as a punishment or in order to force someone to do or say something
  • Synonym: to persecute - to subject someone to hostility and ill-treatment, especially because of race or political or religious beliefs

Etymology

From 'martyr' (meaning one who suffers for a cause) + '-ize' (verb-forming suffix).
