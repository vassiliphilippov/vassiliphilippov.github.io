Lavandula officinalis

1. NOUN

/ ləˌvændjʊlə ˌɑfɪˈsɪnəlɪs / (AmE), / ləˌvændjʊlə ˌɒfɪˈsɪnəlɪs / (BrE)
An aromatic Mediterranean shrub, commonly known as lavender, widely cultivated for its lilac flowers, which are often dried and used in sachets or perfumes.
aromatic Mediterranean lavender shrub

Examples

The essential oil of Lavandula officinalis is prized for its calming scent.
Many gardeners plant Lavandula officinalis due to its fragrant, lilac flowers.
Dried Lavandula officinalis blossoms are often used in scented sachets for drawers and closets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lavender - common name for Lavandula species; widely grown aromatic plant with purple flowers

Etymology

From Latin, 'lavandula' meaning lavender and 'officinalis' indicating traditional medicinal use.
