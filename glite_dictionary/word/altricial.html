altricial

1. ADJ

/ ælˈtrɪʃəl / (AmE), / ælˈtrɪsɪəl / (BrE)
(of young animals, especially birds) born or hatched in an undeveloped state and requiring care and feeding by the parents.
undeveloped at birth and dependent on parents

Examples

Unlike their precocial cousins, altricial chicks are helpless at birth and require constant parental care.
Most songbirds are altricial, hatching naked and blind from their eggs.
The scientist explained that mammals like mice are typically altricial and unable to fend for themselves after birth.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: precocial - (of young animals) born or hatched in an advanced state and able to feed itself almost immediately

Etymology

From Latin altrix (nurse), from alere (to nourish).
