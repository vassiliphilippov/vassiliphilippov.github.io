unorthodoxy

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈɔrθəˌdɑksi / (AmE), / ʌnˈɔːθədɒksi / (BrE)
The quality or state of not conforming to established or accepted beliefs, customs, or practices.
lack of conformity to accepted beliefs

Examples

Her unorthodoxy in approaching scientific problems led to several breakthrough discoveries.
The teacher's unorthodoxy was both admired and criticized by her colleagues.
Many great artists have achieved success through their creative unorthodoxy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonconformity - Refusal to conform to established customs or beliefs
  • Antonym: orthodoxy - Conventional or established beliefs and practices

Etymology

Formed from 'unorthodox' + '-y'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌʌnˈɔr.θəˌdɑk.si / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈɔː.θəˌdɒk.si / (BrE)
the quality or state of not conforming to established or traditional beliefs, doctrines, or practices
state of not conforming to accepted beliefs

Examples

His unorthodoxy often led him into conflicts with traditional leaders.
The scientist was celebrated for her unorthodoxy and willingness to challenge old assumptions.
The novel explores the risks and rewards of religious unorthodoxy in a conservative society.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orthodoxy - the quality or state of conforming to established or traditional beliefs
  • Synonym: heresy - a belief or opinion that goes against established doctrine

Etymology

From un- (not) + orthodoxy, which comes from Greek orthodoxia ('correct opinion').

3. NOUN

/ ʌnˈɔr.θəˌdɑk.si / (AmE), / ʌnˈɔː.θəˌdɒk.si / (BrE)
the state or quality of not conforming to established or accepted beliefs, especially in religion; deviation from orthodox doctrine or practice
deviation from accepted beliefs

Examples

The philosopher's unorthodoxy attracted both admiration and criticism from the academic community.
His unorthodoxy in matters of faith eventually led to his excommunication.
Literary unorthodoxy often paves the way for new artistic movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heterodoxy - having opinions or doctrines at variance with the accepted or orthodox position
  • Antonym: orthodoxy - conforming to established or traditional beliefs, especially in religion

Etymology

From un- (not) + orthodoxy (from Greek orthodoxia, 'correct opinion').
