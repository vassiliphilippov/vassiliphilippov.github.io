to dehumanize

1. VERB

/ diˈhjuː.məˌnaɪz / (AmE), / diˈhjuː.mə.naɪz / (BrE)
to deprive (a person or group) of positive human qualities, dignity, or individuality.
deprive of human qualities or dignity

Examples

The harsh conditions of the prison system can dehumanize inmates and staff alike.
Propaganda is often used to dehumanize enemy groups during wartime.
Some argue that excessive technology in the workplace may dehumanize employees.

Synset Examples

Life in poverty has dehumanized them

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to humanize - to make more human or civilize; the opposite process

Etymology

Formed from the prefix de- (removal) and human + -ize (to make or become).

2. VERB

/ diˈhjuːməˌnaɪz / (AmE), / diˈhjuːmənaɪz / (BrE)
To make (someone or something) appear or feel less personal, warm, or human, often by making actions mechanical or routine.
make impersonal or mechanical

Examples

Intensive factory work can dehumanize employees by turning their labor into a set of monotonous tasks.
Many critics argue that modern bureaucracy tends to dehumanize public services, making them rigid and impersonal.
Long hours and endless paperwork can quickly dehumanize even the most dedicated professionals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mechanize - make something operate like a machine; render mechanical

Etymology

From Latin 'humanus' (human) with the prefix 'de-' (removal, reversal).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
