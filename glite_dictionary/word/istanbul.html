Istanbul

1. PROPN

/ ˌɪstænˈbʊl / (AmE), / ˌɪstanˈbʊːl / (BrE)
The largest city in Turkey, formerly known as Byzantium and Constantinople, historically significant as the former capital of the Byzantine and Ottoman Empires.
the largest city in Turkey, formerly Constantinople

Examples

I spent a week exploring Istanbul and was fascinated by its rich history and vibrant markets.
The Bosphorus Strait divides Istanbul between the continents of Europe and Asia.
Hagia Sophia, located in Istanbul, was once a church, then a mosque, and now a museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Constantinople - the historical name of Istanbul prior to 1930

Etymology

From Turkish İstanbul, from a Medieval Greek phrase 'eis tin polin' meaning 'to the city'.
