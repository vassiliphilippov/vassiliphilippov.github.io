dubiousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈduːbiəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈdjuːbiəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being uncertain, doubtful, or suspicious about something
state of being doubtful or uncertain

Examples

There was a clear sense of dubiousness in her voice when she agreed to the plan.
His dubiousness about the project's chances slowed down the team's progress.
The company's financial statements created considerable dubiousness among the investors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doubt - a feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction
  • Antonym: certainty - the state of being certain or confident about something

Etymology

From Latin 'dubius' (doubtful) + English '-ness' (state, quality).

/ ˈduːbiəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈdjuːbiəsnəs / (BrE)

Examples

His proposal was met with dubiousness by the team, who wanted more details.
