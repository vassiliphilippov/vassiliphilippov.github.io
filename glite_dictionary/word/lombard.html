Lombard

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑmˌbɑrd / (AmE), / ˈlɒm.bɑːd / (BrE)
a member of a Germanic people who invaded and settled in northern Italy during the 6th century
member of a Germanic people in northern Italy

Examples

The Lombard kingdom played a significant role in shaping medieval Italy.
After the fall of Rome, the Lombards established their own territories in the north.
Historians often study the migration of the Lombards for insight into early European history.

Etymology

From Late Latin Langobardus/Lombardus, from Proto-Germanic elements meaning ‘long’ and ‘beard’.
