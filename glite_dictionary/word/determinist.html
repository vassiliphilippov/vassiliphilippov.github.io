determinist

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈtɜːrmɪnɪst / (AmE), / dɪˈtɜːmɪnɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that all events, including human actions, are determined by previously existing causes and that individuals are powerless to change their fate
believer in determinism

Examples

As a determinist, she argued that every decision was shaped by prior events and conditions.
Some philosophers identify as determinists, believing that free will is merely an illusion.
His determinist outlook led him to accept life's challenges with resignation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fatalist - a person who believes that all events are predetermined and inevitable
  • Antonym: free will - the power of acting without the constraint of necessity or fate

Etymology

From 'determinism', itself from Late Latin 'determinare' (to limit, determine) + English suffix '-ist'.
