positively

1. ADV

/ ˈpɑːz.ə.tɪv.li / (AmE), / ˈpɒz.ɪ.tɪv.li / (BrE)
In a way that shows confidence, approval, or affirmation; in a positive manner.
in a positive or affirmative way

Examples

She responded positively to the suggestion and agreed to participate.
The news was positively received by investors, causing the stock price to rise.
The teacher praised him for contributing positively to the classroom discussion.

Synset Examples

she intended her remarks to be interpreted positively

Related Terms

  • Antonym: negatively - in a negative or disapproving manner

Etymology

Formed from 'positive' + '-ly', from Latin 'positivus' (settled by agreement) + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ ˈpɑzətɪvli / (AmE), / ˈpɒzətɪvli / (BrE)
used to emphasize an adjective or adverb, meaning absolutely or completely; downright.
absolutely; downright (intensifier)

Examples

The building's size was positively overwhelming for a small town.
She was positively certain that she had made the right decision.
The new movie is positively hilarious; I laughed the entire time.

Synset Examples

it was positively monumental

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absolutely - completely; totally
  • Synonym: utterly - completely; to a total degree
  • Antonym: slightly - to a small degree or amount

Etymology

From Latin positivus (settled by agreement), via Old French positif and Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00183685-r
