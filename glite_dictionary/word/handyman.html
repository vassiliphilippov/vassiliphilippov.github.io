handyman

1. NOUN

/ ˈhændiˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈhændimæn / (BrE)
a man who is skilled at a wide range of repairs, maintenance work, and odd jobs, especially around the home or workplace
man skilled in small repairs and maintenance

Examples

When the faucet started leaking, we called a handyman to fix it.
The school's handyman takes care of all the minor repairs and maintenance.
After retiring, he worked as a handyman to keep busy and earn extra money.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jack-of-all-trades - a person who can do many different types of work

Etymology

Formed from 'handy' (useful, skilled with the hands) + 'man'.
