scandium

1. NOUN

/ ˈskændiəm / (AmE), / ˈskandɪəm / (BrE)
a soft, silvery-white metallic element, symbol Sc, that is used in some alloys and occurs naturally in minerals such as thortveitite.
a soft, silvery-white metallic element

Examples

Scientists discovered that scandium can improve the strength of certain aluminum alloys.
The mineral thortveitite is the main source of scandium.
Because of its properties, scandium is often used in aerospace components.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yttrium - a similar rare earth metal element
  • Synonym: aluminum - a lightweight metallic element with some similar uses

Etymology

From Latin 'Scandia' (Scandinavia), where it was first identified, plus the '-ium' suffix for metals.
