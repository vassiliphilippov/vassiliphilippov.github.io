Broussonetia

1. NOUN

/ bruːˌsɒˈniːʃiə / (AmE), / bruːˌsɒˈniːʃɪə / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the mulberry family, including the paper mulberry, native to Asia and widely cultivated for its fiber.
a genus of plants in the mulberry family

Examples

The bark of Broussonetia is widely used for making traditional paper in Asia.
Some species of Broussonetia are valued for their fibrous stems.
Researchers are studying the growth patterns of Broussonetia in new climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paper mulberry - a tree of the Broussonetia genus used for making paper

Etymology

Named after Pierre Marie Auguste Broussonet (1761–1807), a French naturalist.

The bark of Broussonetia is traditionally used to produce handmade paper in East Asia.

Several species within Broussonetia are valued for their fibrous stems and ornamental qualities.
