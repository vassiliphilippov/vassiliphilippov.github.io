bookseller

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊkˌsɛlɚ / (AmE), / ˈbʊkˌsɛlə / (BrE)
a person or company that sells books, especially as the owner or proprietor of a bookstore
person who sells books

Examples

The local bookseller recommended a fascinating new novel to me.
As a bookseller, she takes pride in curating a wide selection of literary classics.
Many independent booksellers struggle to compete with large online retailers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bookstore - a shop where books are sold
  • Degree Higher: publisher - a person or company that prepares and issues books for sale

Etymology

From book + seller; attested in English since the late 16th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʊkˌsɛlɚ / (AmE), / ˈbʊkˌsɛlə / (BrE)
A person or business that sells books, especially as a retailer.
a person or business selling books

Examples

She opened a small bookseller in her home town, attracting many avid readers.
The bookseller recommended a fascinating novel to the customer.
Many independent booksellers struggle to compete with large online retailers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bookshop - a store where books are sold
  • Antonym: librarian - a person who works in a library (contrasts with bookseller)

Etymology

From book + seller; modelled after seller (one who sells).
