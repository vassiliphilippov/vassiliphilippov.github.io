cretinism

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɛt.nˌɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˈkrɛt.ɪ.nɪ.zəm / (BrE)
A medical condition caused by severe hypothyroidism, resulting in physical and mental stunting, particularly if untreated in infancy or childhood.
severe hypothyroidism causing stunted growth
medical

Examples

Untreated infants with cretinism may develop irreversible mental and physical disabilities.
The symptoms of cretinism include growth retardation and delayed mental development.
Early diagnosis and treatment can help prevent cretinism in newborns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypothyroidism - underactivity of the thyroid gland

Etymology

From French 'crétinisme', from 'crétin' (person afflicted with cretinism), originally from Swiss French dialect.
