Simeon

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɪmiən / (AmE), / ˈsɪmiən / (BrE)
(In the Bible) the second son of Jacob and Leah, and one of the twelve patriarchs of Israel.
Biblical patriarch, son of Jacob

Examples

The tribe of Simeon was allotted land within the territory of Judah.
According to the book of Genesis, Simeon was one of Jacob's twelve sons.
Many scholars have studied the role of Simeon in the history of ancient Israel.

Etymology

From Hebrew Shim'on, meaning 'he has heard'.
