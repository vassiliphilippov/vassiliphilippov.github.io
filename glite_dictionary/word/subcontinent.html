subcontinent

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌbˈkɑːn.tɪ.nənt / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˈkɒn.tɪ.nənt / (BrE)
a large, distinguishable landmass that forms a distinct part of a continent, often separated by geographical or cultural features
large, distinct part of a continent

Examples

The Indian subcontinent is home to over a billion people and diverse cultures.
Greenland is often considered a subcontinent because of its large size and distinct geography.
Geographers classify the Arabian Peninsula as a subcontinent due to its unique landmass characteristics.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: continent - one of the main continuous expanses of land (Europe, Asia, etc.)

Etymology

From Latin sub- (under, below) + continent (from Latin continens, continentis)
