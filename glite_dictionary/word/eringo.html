eringo

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈrɪŋɡoʊ / (AmE), / ɪˈrɪŋɡəʊ / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Eryngium, known for its spiny leaves and often bluish or purplish flowers, sometimes called sea holly
plant of the genus Eryngium

Examples

The dried roots of eringo were once used in herbal medicine.
Gardeners admire the unique appearance of the eringo in summer borders.
She planted two varieties of eringo alongside ornamental grasses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea holly - common English name for plants of the genus Eryngium

Etymology

From Italian 'eringio', from Latin 'eryngium' (sea holly).
