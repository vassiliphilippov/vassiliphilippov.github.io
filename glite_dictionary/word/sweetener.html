sweetener

1. NOUN

/ ˈswiːt̬ənər / (AmE), / ˈswiːtənə / (BrE)
a substance added to food or drink to make it taste sweeter, especially in place of sugar
a substance that makes food sweeter

Examples

She prefers using a natural sweetener instead of sugar in her coffee.
Many diet drinks contain an artificial sweetener to reduce calories.
Honey can be used as a sweetener in baking recipes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sugar - sweet crystalline substance used as a sweetener
  • Synonym: artificial sweetener - substance used instead of sugar to sweeten foods or drinks

Etymology

From 'sweeten' + '-er', indicating something that sweetens.

2. NOUN

/ ˈswiːtənər / (AmE), / ˈswiːtənə / (BrE)
something added to make an offer or proposal more attractive, often as an incentive.
incentive; inducement

Examples

The bonus was offered as a sweetener to persuade her to accept the new job.
They included free shipping as a sweetener to encourage more customers to buy online.
The company added extra stock options as a sweetener to the merger deal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incentive - something that motivates a person to take action; encouragement
  • Synonym: inducement - a thing that persuades or leads someone to do something

Etymology

From 'sweeten' + '-er', originally referring to something that makes things sweet, later extended figuratively.
