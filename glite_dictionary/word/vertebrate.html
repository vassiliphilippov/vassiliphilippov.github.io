vertebrate

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɜrtəˌbreɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɜːtɪbrət / (BrE)
an animal with a backbone or spinal column, including mammals, birds, reptiles, amphibians, and fishes
animal with a backbone

Examples

A fish is a typical example of a vertebrate animal.
Unlike insects, all vertebrates have a well-developed nervous system.
The fossil revealed the earliest known vertebrate from that geological period.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: invertebrate - an animal lacking a backbone

Etymology

From Latin 'vertebratus' (jointed), from 'vertebra' (joint or spinal bone).

2. ADJ

/ ˈvɜrtəˌbreɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɜːtɪbrət / (BrE)
(of an animal) having a backbone or spinal column.
having a backbone or spinal column

Examples

Frogs are classified as vertebrate species because they possess a backbone.
Unlike insects, all mammals are vertebrate animals.
A bird's skeleton is typical of a vertebrate structure, with a well-defined spine.

Synset Examples

fishes and amphibians and reptiles and birds and mammals are vertebrate animals

Related Terms

  • Antonym: invertebrate - lacking a backbone or spinal column

Etymology

From Latin 'vertebratus', from 'vertebra' (joint, backbone).
