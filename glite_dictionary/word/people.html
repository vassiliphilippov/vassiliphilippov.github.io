people

1. NOUN

/ ˈpipəl / (AmE), / ˈpiːp(ə)l / (BrE)
(informal) a person's family, ancestors, or relatives, especially considered as a family line or background.
family/ancestors; family line
informal

Examples

His people came from Ireland in the 1800s.
I'll introduce you to my people at the family reunion.
Her people have lived in this town for three generations.

Synset Examples

his people have been farmers for generations
are your people still alive?

Etymology

From Old French 'pople', from Latin 'populus' (people, nation).
