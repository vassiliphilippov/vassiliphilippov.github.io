Mesoamerican

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɛzoʊəˈmɛrɪkən / (AmE), / ˌmɛsəʊəˈmɛrɪkən / (BrE)
Relating to the peoples, languages, or cultures of Mesoamerica, especially before the Spanish conquest.
of Mesoamerica; its people or culture

Examples

Archaeologists have discovered new evidence about Mesoamerican civilizations such as the Maya and the Aztecs.
The Mesoamerican languages are known for their rich diversity and complexity.
Many Mesoamerican traditions influenced modern Central American culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mesoamerica - a region and cultural area in the Americas, extending from central Mexico through Central America

Etymology

From 'Mesoamerica,' from Greek 'mesos' (middle) + 'America.'

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛzoʊəˈmɛrɪkən / (AmE), / ˌmɛsəʊəˈmɛrɪkən / (BrE)
a member of one of the indigenous peoples of Mesoamerica, an area extending from central Mexico to northern Central America before the Spanish conquest.
member of a Mesoamerican indigenous people

Examples

Many ancient Mesoamericans built complex cities with intricate architectural designs.
Some Mesoamerican groups developed advanced calendars and writing systems.
Archaeologists continue to study the daily life of Mesoamericans before European contact.

Etymology

From 'Meso-' meaning 'middle' and 'American', referring to the geographic region of central America.
