Psophia

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈfaɪ.i.ə / (AmE), / səʊˈfɪə / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Psophiidae, commonly known as trumpeters, native to South America.
genus of birds (trumpeters)

Examples

The genus Psophia includes several species of trumpeters found in the Amazon rainforest.
Researchers observed that Psophia species are highly social and live in flocks.
The distinctive cry of Psophia birds can often be heard echoing through the forest.

Etymology

From Greek 'psophos' meaning 'noise' or 'sound', referring to the loud calls of these birds.
