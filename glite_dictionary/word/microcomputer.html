microcomputer

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.kroʊ.kəmˈpjuː.t̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.krəʊ.kəmˈpjuː.tə / (BrE)
a small computer that uses a microprocessor as its central processor, designed for use by one person at a time
small computer using a microprocessor

Examples

Early microcomputers revolutionized the way individuals accessed and processed information.
Many schools in the 1980s began incorporating microcomputers into their classrooms to improve learning.
The hobbyist built a basic microcomputer from spare electronic parts and a simple microprocessor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: personal computer - a computer designed for use by one person at a time

Etymology

Formed from 'micro-' (small) and 'computer', arising with the advent of microprocessors in the 1970s.
