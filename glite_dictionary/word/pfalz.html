Pfalz

1. PROPN

/ pfaːlts / (AmE), / pfaːlts / (BrE)
A historical territory in southwestern Germany, formerly governed by the counts palatine.
historic territory in Germany

Examples

The region known as the Pfalz produced some of the finest wines in Europe.
Many castles in the Pfalz region date back to the Middle Ages.
Historians have studied the governance of the Pfalz under the counts palatine.

Etymology

German, from Middle High German 'Pfalz', related to 'palatine'.
