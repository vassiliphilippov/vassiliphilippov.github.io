unemployed

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪmˈplɔɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪmˈplɔɪd / (BrE)
without a paid job; not engaged in gainful employment
not having a paid job

Examples

After being laid off, she was unemployed for almost six months.
Many unemployed people rely on government assistance during their job search.
The city has launched a program to help unemployed youth gain new skills.

Synset Examples

unemployed workers marched on the capital

Related Terms

  • Antonym: employed - having a paid job
  • Synonym: jobless - not having employment; out of work

Etymology

Prefix un- (not) + employed (from Old French employer, from Latin implicare 'enfold, involve').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00867880-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˌʌnɪmˈplɔɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪmˈplɔɪd / (BrE)
people who do not have a job and are available for work, considered as a group
people without jobs (as a group)

Examples

The government introduced new training programs for the unemployed.
Support centers have been set up to help the long-term unemployed.
Statistics show the number of unemployed is decreasing this year.

Synset Examples

the long-term unemployed need assistance

Related Terms

  • Antonym: employed - having a paid job

Etymology

From un- (not) + employed, from French employer, from Latin implicare (to involve).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08494318-n
Synset POS: n
