to unclip

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈklɪp / (AmE), / ʌnˈklɪp / (BrE)
to remove a clip or the fastening of a clip from something
remove a clip from

Examples

She carefully unclipped the name tag from her shirt before washing it.
I need to unclip this paper from the folder to make a copy.
He unclipped the dog's leash and let it run freely in the park.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clip - to fasten or attach with a clip

Etymology

Formed by the prefix 'un-' (meaning 'reverse an action') + 'clip'.
