monolithic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɑː.nəˈlɪθ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒ.nəˈlɪθ.ɪk / (BrE)
Imposing in size, bulk, or solidity; massive and uniform in form or structure.
large and solid in size or form

Examples

The skyscraper's monolithic façade dominated the city skyline.
Soviet-era buildings are often criticized for their monolithic appearance and lack of subtlety.
A monolithic wall of stone separated the ancient fortress from the rest of the city.

Synset Examples

massive oak doors
Moore's massive sculptures
the monolithic proportions of Stalinist architecture
a monumental scale

Related Terms

  • Synonym: massive - Large and heavy or solid
  • Synonym: monumental - Impressively large or enduring
  • Antonym: delicate - Very fine in texture or structure; fragile

Etymology

From Greek monolithos (single stone): monos (single) + lithos (stone).

2. ADJ

/ ˌmɑːnəˈlɪθɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈlɪθɪk / (BrE)
large, powerful, and intractably indivisible and uniform, often referring to organizations, movements, or societies that are massive, rigid, and lack diversity or flexibility.
massive, uniform, and rigid

Examples

The company had a monolithic structure that made it difficult to implement change.
Many critics argue that the media is not as monolithic as it appears.
The government's monolithic approach left little room for regional differences.

Synset Examples

a monolithic society
a monolithic worldwide movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rigid - not flexible; unable to bend or be forced out of shape
  • Antonym: flexible - capable of bending easily without breaking; easily modified

Etymology

From Greek monolithos, from monos (single) + lithos (stone).
