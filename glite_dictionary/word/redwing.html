redwing

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdˌwɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌwɪŋ / (BrE)
A North American blackbird (Agelaius phoeniceus) with distinctive scarlet and yellow patches on its wings, often found in wetlands and open fields.
North American blackbird with wing patches

Examples

The distinctive call of the redwing echoed through the marsh at sunrise.
We spotted several redwings perched among the cattails during our walk.
A male redwing flashed its brilliant scarlet shoulder patches while defending its territory.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdˌwɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌwɪŋ / (BrE)
a small European thrush (Turdus iliacus) with reddish flanks and underwing, commonly found in woodland and hedgerows
small European thrush with reddish flanks

Examples

We spotted a redwing feeding among the autumn leaves.
During migration, large flocks of redwings can be seen across northern Europe.
The distinctive call of the redwing is often heard at dusk in rural areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrush - songbird of the family Turdidae

Etymology

From 'red' + 'wing', referring to the species’ reddish patches on its wings.
