debate

1. VERB

/ dɪˈbeɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈbeɪt / (BrE)
to discuss something formally or argue about different opinions, often in a structured way
to discuss or argue about something

Examples

The two candidates debated the issues on television last night.
They debated for hours without reaching an agreement.
We are still debating whether to accept the offer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to discuss - talk about something with others, especially in order to exchange ideas or reach a decision
  • Synonym: to argue - give reasons for or against something, sometimes in a heated way

Etymology

From Old French 'debatre', from Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'battuere' (to fight).

/ dɪˈbeɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈbeɪt / (BrE)

Examples

They debated for hours about whether to invest in the new project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to discuss - talk about something in detail
  • Synonym: to argue - exchange or express diverging views, typically in a heated way

Etymology

From Old French *debatre* (to fight, contend), from Latin *battuere* (to beat).

3. NOUN

/ dəˈbeɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈbeɪt / (BrE)
a formal discussion in which opposing arguments are presented about a particular issue or proposal
formal discussion with opposing arguments

Examples

There is ongoing debate about how to address climate change.
The candidates participated in a televised debate before the election.
The school organized a debate on the pros and cons of social media.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discussion - the action or process of talking about something to reach a decision

From Old French 'debatre' (to fight or contend), from Latin 'battuere' (to beat).
