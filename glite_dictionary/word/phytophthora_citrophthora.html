Phytophthora citrophthora

1. NOUN

/ faɪˈtɒfθərə ˌsɪtrəfˈθɔrə / (AmE), / faɪˈtɒfθərə ˌsɪtrəfˈθɔːrə / (BrE)
a species of oomycete (fungus-like organism) that causes brown rot gummosis in citrus fruits
fungus causing disease in citrus fruits
medical

Examples

The outbreak of Phytophthora citrophthora devastated the region's orange groves.
Brown rot in lemons is often caused by Phytophthora citrophthora infecting the fruit.
Growers implement strict irrigation controls to prevent the spread of Phytophthora citrophthora.

Etymology

From Greek 'phyton' (plant) + 'phthora' (destruction), and Latinized species name 'citrophthora' referring to citrus destruction.
