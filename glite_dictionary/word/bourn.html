bourn

1. NOUN

/ bɜrn / (AmE), / bɔːn / (BrE)
(Archaic) A boundary or limit, especially of land; a dividing line.
archaic word for boundary or limit
archaic

Examples

The river served as the bourn between the two ancient kingdoms.
He journeyed beyond the known bourns of his land.
In literature, death is sometimes called the undiscovered bourn from which no traveler returns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boundary - a line that marks the limits of an area; a dividing line

Etymology

From Old French *borne* (boundary), from Medieval Latin *bodina* (limit).

2. NOUN

/ bɔrn / (AmE), / bɔːn / (BrE)
(Archaic) A goal, destination, or boundary, especially as a metaphor for the end of a journey or life.
archaic word for goal or destination
archaic

Examples

In his soliloquy, Hamlet refers to the 'undiscovered country from whose bourn no traveller returns.'
The adventurers set out, each seeking a different bourn in the distant land.
Philosophers have long debated the nature of humanity’s ultimate bourn.

Etymology

From Old French *borne* (limit, boundary), from Medieval Latin *bodina*.
