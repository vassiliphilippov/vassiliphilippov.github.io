instructress

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈstrʌktrəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈstrʌktrɪs / (BrE)
a female instructor (now often regarded as old-fashioned or obsolete)
female instructor (old-fashioned)
archaic

Examples

The piano instructress was known for her strict teaching style.
In Victorian England, it was common to have a governess or an instructress for young ladies.
The ballet instructress guided her students through every difficult move.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instructor - a person who teaches or instructs

Etymology

From 'instructor' + '-ess', a suffix used to form female nouns from male equivalents.
