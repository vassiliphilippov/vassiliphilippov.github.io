to depreciate

1. VERB

/ dɪˈpriʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈpriːʃieɪt / (BrE)
to reduce or cause the value or price of something to decrease over time, especially in financial contexts
lower in value; decrease in worth

Examples

Rising inflation can depreciate the purchasing power of your savings.
If the central bank intervenes, it may depreciate the national currency.
The company’s assets began to depreciate rapidly after the market downturn.

Synset Examples

The Fed depreciated the dollar once again

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to appreciate - increase in value or worth over time

Etymology

From Late Latin 'depretiatus', past participle of 'depretiare' (to lower the price), from Latin 'de-' (down) + 'pretiare' (to value, price).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈpriʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈpriːʃieɪt / (BrE)
(of a currency, asset, or commodity) to decrease in value over time.
lose value; decrease in worth

Examples

Many cars depreciate quickly during their first year of use.
The local currency began to depreciate after the economic crisis.
Some assets like real estate can also depreciate under certain conditions.

Synset Examples

The dollar depreciated again

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to appreciate - (of currency/asset) to increase in value
  • Synonym: to devalue - to reduce or cause to reduce in value, especially of currency

Etymology

From Late Latin 'depretiatus', past participle of 'depretiare' (to lower in value), from Latin 'de-' (down) + 'pretium' (price).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00315821-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ dɪˈpriːʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈpriːʃieɪt / (BrE)
to make someone or something seem unimportant or of less value; to belittle
belittle; reduce in value or importance

Examples

It is wrong to depreciate the efforts of others when they are trying their best.
The article seemed to depreciate the achievements of the local community.
She felt hurt when her colleagues would depreciate her contributions to the project.

Synset Examples

The teacher should not deprecate his student's efforts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to belittle - to make someone or something seem unimportant
  • Antonym: to appreciate - to recognize the value or importance of something

Etymology

From Latin *depretiatus*, past participle of *depretiare* (to lower in price).
