glimmery

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡlɪməri / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪməri / (BrE)
shining with a soft, intermittent light
shining softly and intermittently

Examples

The glimmery surface of the lake caught the last rays of sunlight.
She decorated the room with glimmery tinsel for the party.
Candlelight cast a glimmery glow across the old photographs.

Synset Examples

glimmery candlelight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glimmering - shining faintly or unsteadily
  • Degree Higher: sparkling - shining with bright flashes of light
  • Antonym: dull - not bright or shiny

Etymology

Formed from 'glimmer' (to shine with a faint or wavering light) plus the adjectival suffix '-y'.
