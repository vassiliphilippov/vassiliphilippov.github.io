soundproof

1. ADJ

/ ˈsaʊndˌpruːf / (AmE), / ˈsaʊnd.pruːf / (BrE)
(of a room, building, or material) not allowing sound to pass through or out; impervious to sound.
impervious to sound

Examples

We recorded the podcast in a soundproof studio to eliminate background noise.
The hotel rooms are soundproof, so guests can sleep peacefully even in the city center.
They installed soundproof windows to reduce the noise from traffic outside.

Synset Examples

a soundproof room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noiseproof - made to prevent the passage of noise

Etymology

Formed from 'sound' + 'proof', following the pattern of 'waterproof'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01779965-s
Synset POS: s

2. VERB

/ ˈsaʊndˌpruf / (AmE), / ˈsaʊnd.pruːf / (BrE)
to make (a room, building, etc.) resistant to the passage of sound, especially unwanted noise, by insulating it
make resistant to sound

Examples

They hired a contractor to soundproof the conference room for confidential meetings.
After moving near the train tracks, she decided to soundproof her bedroom windows.
Studios often soundproof their walls to prevent external noise from interfering with recordings.

Synset Examples

Proust had his apartment soundproofed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to insulate - to protect or cover with a material that reduces the passage of heat, electricity, or sound

Etymology

From 'sound' (noise) + 'proof' (resistant), modeled after similar compounds like waterproof.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00496441-v
Synset POS: v
