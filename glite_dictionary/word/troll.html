troll

1. VERB

/ troʊl / (AmE), / trəʊl / (BrE)
to sing loudly and without inhibition, often in a cheerful or hearty manner
sing loudly and freely

Examples

The cheerful group began to troll old songs as they marched through the village.
She would troll a lively tune whenever she worked in the kitchen.
Children trolled carols outside as snow fell gently on the street.

Etymology

From Old French *troller* (wander about, sing loudly), possibly from a Germanic root.

3. NOUN

/ troʊl / (AmE), / trəʊl / (BrE)
(in Scandinavian folklore) a supernatural being, often depicted as a dwarf or a giant, that is said to live in caves or mountains.

Examples

Legends say a troll lurked beneath the old stone bridge.
In the fairy tale, the troll tried to stop the goats from crossing the river.
Trolls are often depicted as either huge and menacing or small and mischievous in Scandinavian myths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant - a being of great size and strength; mythological creature
  • Synonym: dwarf - a being from mythology of very small stature

Etymology

/ troʊl / (AmE), / trəʊl / (BrE)

Examples

He prefers to troll for bass in the early morning when the lake is calm.
During our fishing trip, we decided to troll along the river’s edge to attract more fish.

Possibly from Old French *troller* (to stroll or wander), applied to fishing method in late 17th century.
