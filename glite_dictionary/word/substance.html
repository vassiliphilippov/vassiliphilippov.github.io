substance

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbstəns / (AmE), / ˈsʌbstəns / (BrE)
a particular kind or species of matter that has uniform properties and composition
a kind of matter with uniform properties

Examples

Lead is a heavy substance used in batteries and construction.
Oxygen is a colorless, odorless substance essential for life.
The scientist analyzed the mysterious substance found in the test tube.

Synset Examples

shigella is one of the most toxic substances known to man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: material - matter from which a thing is made or can be made
  • Antonym: mixture - a combination of different substances

Etymology

From Latin 'substantia' meaning 'being, essence, material'.
