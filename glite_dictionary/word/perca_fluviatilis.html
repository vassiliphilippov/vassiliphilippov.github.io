Perca fluviatilis

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜːrkə fluːviˈætɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˈpɜːkə fluːvɪˈætɪlɪs / (BrE)
a species of freshwater perch native to Europe and northern Asia, recognized for its greenish body and distinctive red fins
European freshwater perch species

Examples

Anglers often seek out Perca fluviatilis in freshwater lakes across Europe.
The Perca fluviatilis is known for its distinctive red fins and striped body.
Scientists study the habitat preferences of Perca fluviatilis to improve conservation efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: European perch - common name for Perca fluviatilis

Etymology

From Latin: 'perca' (perch) and 'fluviatilis' (of rivers).
