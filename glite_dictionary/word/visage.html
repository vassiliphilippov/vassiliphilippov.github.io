visage

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪzɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈvɪzɪdʒ / (BrE)
a person's face, with reference to its appearance or expression
the appearance of a person's face

Examples

Her cheerful visage brightened the atmosphere in the room.
The man's stern visage made the children nervous.
A mask concealed his true visage from the crowd.

Synset Examples

a pleasant countenance
a stern visage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: countenance - the face or facial expression

Etymology

From Old French 'visage', from Latin 'visus' (appearance, sight).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɪzɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈvɪzɪdʒ / (BrE)
the face, especially in reference to the form or expression it presents
the human face or facial expression

Examples

Her solemn visage revealed nothing of her inner feelings.
The old man's wrinkled visage told a story of hardship and endurance.
He studied her visage for any hint of amusement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: face - the front part of a person's head

Etymology

From Old French 'visage' (face), from Latin 'visus' (a look, vision).
