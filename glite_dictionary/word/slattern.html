slattern

1. NOUN

/ ˈslætərn / (AmE), / ˈslætən / (BrE)
A woman who is habitually dirty and untidy.
untidy or dirty woman

Examples

Despite her wealth, the duchess was called a slattern for her dirty clothes and messy hair.
The kitchen was so neglected that she felt like a slattern living there.
He warned his sons not to marry a slattern who couldn't keep a home tidy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slob - a person who is lazy and dirty or messy
  • Antonym: neat freak - a person who is compulsively neat and orderly

Etymology

From Middle English slateren, probably from dialectal slatter (to slop, spill).

2. NOUN

/ ˈslætərn / (AmE), / ˈslætən / (BrE)
an untidy or promiscuous woman, especially one who solicits men for sex on the streets; a prostitute
promiscuous or untidy woman; street prostitute
archaic

Examples

In Victorian literature, a slattern might be depicted roaming dimly lit city streets.
The term slattern was once commonly used to describe women who solicited clients at night.
She was unfairly labeled a slattern by those who judged appearances without knowing the truth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prostitute - a person who engages in sexual activity for payment

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'slat', related to 'slatternly' (untidy, slovenly)
