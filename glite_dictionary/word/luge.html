luge

1. VERB

/ luːʒ / (AmE), / luːʒ / (BrE)
to travel or race while lying on a small sledge with the feet forward, typically at high speed down an ice track
to race on a luge sled

Examples

Athletes from around the world come to luge on this famous ice track.
She learned to luge during the winter Olympics training program.
The children dreamed of one day getting to luge down an Olympic course.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toboggan - to ride on a long, flat-bottomed sled, usually downhill

Etymology

From French 'luge' meaning 'small sled'.

2. NOUN

/ luːʒ / (AmE), / luːʒ / (BrE)
a small, light sled for one or two people, used for racing down an ice track while lying on one's back and feet first.
a sled used for ice racing

Examples

She raced down the icy track on her luge at breathtaking speed.
The luge competition is one of the fastest events at the Winter Olympics.
After repairing his luge, he returned to the top of the hill for another run.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bobsleigh - a larger, enclosed sled for team ice racing
  • Synonym: toboggan - a long, flat sled with no runners

Etymology

From French 'luge', meaning small coasting sled.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03701079-n
Synset POS: n
