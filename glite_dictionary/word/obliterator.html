obliterator

1. NOUN

/ əˈblɪtəˌreɪtɚ / (AmE), / əˈblɪtəreɪtə / (BrE)
a person or thing that completely destroys or removes all traces of something
something that destroys completely

Examples

The ancient city fell to the obliterator of time and was lost forever.
Some viruses act as an obliterator of healthy cells within the body.
The superhero was known as the Obliterator, feared by criminals everywhere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destroyer - a person or thing that destroys
  • Antonym: preserver - a person or thing that keeps or maintains something

Etymology

From 'obliterate' (to destroy all trace), ultimately from Latin 'oblitterare' (to erase, efface).
