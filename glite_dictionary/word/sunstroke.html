sunstroke

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌstroʊk / (AmE), / ˈsʌnstrəʊk / (BrE)
A serious condition caused by prolonged exposure to intense heat or direct sunlight, resulting in symptoms such as weakness, confusion, and sometimes collapse.
illness from excessive sun or heat

Examples

The hiker collapsed from sunstroke after walking all afternoon without a hat.
If you experience confusion or headache after being in the sun, it could be sunstroke.
Medical staff treated several festival-goers for sunstroke during the heatwave.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heatstroke - a more general term for illness caused by overheating of the body

Etymology

Formed from 'sun' + 'stroke', literally a stroke or blow from the sun.
