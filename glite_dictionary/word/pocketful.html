pocketful

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑkɪtfʊl / (AmE), / ˈpɒkɪtfʊl / (BrE)
An amount of something that fills a pocket.
as much as a pocket can hold

Examples

He took a pocketful of coins to the arcade.
She picked a pocketful of wildflowers during her walk.
After the party, the children left with a pocketful of candy each.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handful - the amount that can be held in one hand
  • Synonym: armful - the amount that can be carried in one's arms

Etymology

Formed from 'pocket' + 'ful', patterned after similar constructions (e.g., handful).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑkɪtˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈpɒkɪtˌfʊl / (BrE)
An amount or quantity of something that is enough to fill a pocket.
as much as fits in a pocket

Examples

She returned from the market with a pocketful of coins.
The child gathered a pocketful of stones during the walk.
He always seems to have a pocketful of interesting ideas.

Synset Examples

She returned home with a pocketful of fresh herbs.
He collected a pocketful of seashells at the beach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handful - an amount that can be held in one hand
  • Synonym: cupful - the amount a cup can hold

Etymology

Formed from 'pocket' and the suffix '-ful' (indicating 'as much as will fill').
