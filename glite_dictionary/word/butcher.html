butcher

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊtʃər / (AmE), / ˈbʊtʃə / (BrE)
a person whose job is cutting up and selling meat in a shop
person who sells meat

Examples

We bought fresh sausages from the local butcher this morning.
The butcher recommended a tender cut of beef for our stew.
Many people prefer visiting a butcher instead of the supermarket for quality meat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meat cutter - a person who cuts meat as a job
  • Antonym: fishmonger - a person who sells fish

Etymology

From Old French 'boucher', from 'bouc' (male goat).

3. NOUN

/ ˈbʊtʃər / (AmE), / ˈbʊtʃə / (BrE)
A brutal or indiscriminate murderer; someone who kills people in a cruel or ruthless way.
a person who murders brutally or indiscriminately

Examples

The dictator was widely feared, known as the butcher of his own people.
History remembers him as a butcher for the violence he unleashed on civilians.
In the prosecutor’s words, the defendant was a cold-blooded butcher of the innocent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murderer - a person who unlawfully kills another
  • Synonym: killer - a person who kills someone
  • Antonym: savior - a person who saves others, especially from danger

Etymology

From Old French *boucher*, from *bouc* (male goat), originally referring to someone who slaughtered animals.

4. VERB

/ ˈbʊtʃər / (AmE), / ˈbʊtʃə / (BrE)
to kill animals, usually for food consumption
kill animals for food

Examples

Farmers often butcher their own livestock during the autumn season.
The chef insisted on butchering the meat himself to ensure its quality.
They butchered a pig and used every part for food.

Related Terms
