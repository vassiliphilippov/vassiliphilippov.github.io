soup

1. NOUN

/ suːp / (AmE), / suːp / (BrE)
an unpleasant or difficult situation; trouble (informal, chiefly in the phrase 'in the soup')
an unfortunate or difficult situation (informal)
informal

Examples

If we don't finish this project on time, we'll be in soup.
After losing the documents, he found himself deep in soup.
They're really in the soup with the bank after missing another payment.

Synset Examples

we're in the soup now

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trouble - difficulty or problems
  • Synonym: predicament - a difficult or awkward situation

2. NOUN

/ suːp / (AmE), / suːp / (BrE)
A liquid dish, typically made by boiling meat, fish, or vegetables in stock or water, often containing pieces of solid food.
liquid food made by boiling meat, fish, or vegetables

Examples

She made a delicious chicken soup for dinner.
A hot bowl of soup is perfect on a cold day.
Vegetable soup is a healthy option for lunch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broth - clear liquid in which meat or vegetables have been cooked
  • Synonym: stew - dish of meat and vegetables cooked slowly in liquid

Etymology

From French 'soupe' (soup, broth), related to 'supper', from Late Latin 'suppa' (bread soaked in broth).

3. NOUN

/ suːp / (AmE), / suːp / (BrE)
Any mixture or substance that resembles soup in appearance or consistency.
thick or liquid mixture like soup

Examples

Scientists believe life began in a chemical soup on the primitive Earth.
The lake was covered with a thick soup of algae by midsummer.
Fog drifted in, turning the city streets into a gray soup with almost no visibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mash - soft, pulpy mixture often of food
  • Synonym: broth - liquid in which meat, fish, or vegetables have been cooked

Etymology

From French 'soupe' (broth, soup), from Latin 'suppa' (bread soaked in broth).
