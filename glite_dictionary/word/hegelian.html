Hegelian

1. NOUN

/ həˈɡeɪliən / (AmE), / hɛˈɡeɪliən / (BrE)
A person who supports or follows the philosophy of Georg Wilhelm Friedrich Hegel.
follower of Hegel's philosophy

Examples

She became a prominent Hegelian after studying German idealist philosophy at university.
The debate between the Hegelian and Marxist schools shaped much of nineteenth-century thought.
As a Hegelian, he believed in the dialectical process of history.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Marxist - a follower of the philosophy of Karl Marx
  • Synonym: Kantian - a follower of the philosophy of Immanuel Kant

Etymology

From the name of German philosopher Georg Wilhelm Friedrich Hegel + -ian (denoting adherence or relation).

2. ADJ

/ həˈɡeɪliən / (AmE), / hɛˈɡeɪlɪən / (BrE)
relating to the German philosopher Georg Wilhelm Friedrich Hegel or to his system of dialectical philosophy
relating to Hegel or his philosophy

Examples

Many modern philosophers have tried to incorporate Hegelian concepts into their theories.
Her dissertation examines the Hegelian dialectic and its influence on political thought.
The professor explained the complex Hegelian process of thesis, antithesis, and synthesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dialectical - relating to logical argumentation or the dialectic method

Etymology

From the name of the German philosopher Hegel (1770–1831) plus the suffix -ian.
