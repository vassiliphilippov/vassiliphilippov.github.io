merit

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛrɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɛrɪt / (BrE)
the quality of being particularly good, deserving praise, reward, or assistance
quality of deserving praise or reward

Examples

The judges considered the merit of each proposal before making a decision.
Scholarships are awarded solely on academic merit rather than financial need.
We should evaluate candidates by their abilities and merit, not by who they know.

Synset Examples

there were many children whose deservingness he recognized and rewarded

Related Terms

  • Antonym: demerit - a quality or feature deserving censure or blame
  • Synonym: worth - the quality of being valuable or useful

Etymology

From Latin meritum (due reward), from mereri (to earn, deserve).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛrɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɛrɪt / (BrE)
a quality that deserves praise or approval; an admirable feature or attribute
an admirable quality or feature

Examples

The museum's collection is of considerable merit, attracting visitors from around the world.
Each candidate was evaluated on the merit of their proposal.
That suggestion has real merit and deserves further discussion.

Synset Examples

work of great merit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: value - the worth or importance of something
  • Antonym: fault - a flaw or defect; something that detracts from merit

Etymology

From Latin meritum (due reward, merit), from mereri (to earn, deserve).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05146223-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈmɛrɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɛrɪt / (BrE)
to be worthy of or deserve something, especially praise or reward
to be worthy or deserve something

Examples

His actions merit careful consideration before any conclusion is drawn.
Such outstanding service merits recognition from the entire community.
Your proposal merits further discussion by the board.

Synset Examples

You deserve a promotion after all the hard work you have done

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deserve - to be worthy of something, especially a reward or punishment

Etymology

From Latin 'meritum' (a reward, merit), from 'mereri' (to earn, deserve).
