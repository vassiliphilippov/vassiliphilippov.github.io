structure

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrʌktʃər / (AmE), / ˈstrʌktʃə / (BrE)
the way in which the people within a society are organized, especially in terms of relationships and social roles
organization of people in a society

Examples

Recent changes have significantly altered the structure of society in many countries.
Sociologists analyze the structure of families to understand social change.
Class and class relations are important components of the social structure.

Synset Examples

the social organizations of England and America are very different
sociologists have studied the changing structure of the family

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organization - the arrangement of things or people in a systematic way

Etymology

From Latin structura (a fitting together, building), from struere (to build).
