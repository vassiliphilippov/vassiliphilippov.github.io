rhombohedron

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɑːmboʊˈhiːdrən / (AmE), / ˌrɒmbəʊˈhiːdrən / (BrE)
A three-dimensional geometric shape with six faces, each of which is a rhombus or parallelogram.
3D shape with six rhombus faces

Examples

The calcite crystals often form a classic rhombohedron shape.
A rhombohedron differs from a cube because its faces are slanted, not perpendicular.
In geometry, a rhombohedron is an important example of a parallelepiped.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parallelepiped - a six-faced figure where each face is a parallelogram

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'rhombos' (rhombus) + 'hedra' (seat, base).
