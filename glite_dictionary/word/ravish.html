to ravish

1. VERB

/ ˈræv.ɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈrav.ɪʃ / (BrE)
(archaic or literary) To force someone to have sex against their will; to rape.
force sex upon; rape (archaic)
archaic

Examples

In the ancient legend, the princess was ravished by an invading warlord.
The story claimed the villagers feared their daughters would be ravished by outlaws lurking in the woods.
She wrote in her diary about her terror at the thought she might be ravished during the siege.

Synset Examples

The woman was raped on her way home at night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rape - to force someone to have sex against their will

Etymology

From Old French *ravir*, from Latin *rapere* (to seize, carry off, abduct).

2. VERB

/ ˈrævɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈravɪʃ / (BrE)
to fill someone with intense delight or to enchant so greatly that they are held spellbound
enchant; fill with intense delight

Examples

The beauty of the sunset seemed to ravish everyone on the beach into silence.
He was ravished by the music, unable to think of anything else as it played.
Her performance on stage ravished the audience, leaving them breathless with admiration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enchant - to delight greatly or cast a spell over
  • Antonym: bore - to make uninterested or weary

Etymology

From Old French ravir (to seize, carry away), from Latin rapere (to seize, snatch).

Synset POS: v
