fable

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈfeɪbəl / (BrE)
A short story, typically featuring animals as characters, that conveys a moral lesson.
short story with a moral, often with animals

Examples

As children, we learned a fable about a clever fox and a crow.
Aesop's fables are famous for teaching important life lessons through animal characters.
Each fable in the book ends with a clearly stated moral.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parable - A simple story used to illustrate a moral or spiritual lesson
  • Synonym: myth - A traditional story, especially one involving supernatural beings or events

Etymology

From Latin 'fabula' (story, tale, narrative).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈfeɪbəl / (BrE)
A deliberately false or improbable account; a fabricated story or myth.
fabricated or improbable account

Examples

His entire story about winning the lottery was just a fable to impress his friends.
Many politicians' promises turn out to be nothing more than a fable once they are elected.
The rumor about the haunted house proved to be a fable after a thorough investigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myth - a traditional or fictitious story, often explaining a phenomenon
  • Antonym: fact - something that is true or real, as opposed to fiction or fabrication

Etymology

From Latin 'fabula' (story, tale, narrative).

3. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪ.bəl / (AmE), / ˈfeɪ.bəl / (BrE)
a short story, typically with animals as characters, conveying a moral lesson, often involving mythical or supernatural events
short story with a moral, often featuring animals

Examples

The teacher read a fable about a clever fox and a proud crow to the class.
Each fable in the collection teaches a different lesson about honesty and kindness.
Long ago, people believed every fable contained a grain of truth about the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parable - a simple story used to illustrate a moral or spiritual lesson
  • Synonym: myth - a traditional story explaining natural or social phenomena, often involving supernatural beings

Etymology

From Latin fabula (story, tale), from fari (to speak).
