cockpit

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːk.pɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒk.pɪt / (BrE)
the area in a racing car where the driver sits and controls the vehicle
driver's seat in a racing car

Examples

The racing team's engineers redesigned the cockpit for better safety and comfort.
He climbed into the cockpit and adjusted his seat before the race began.
Modern Formula 1 cars use advanced technology in the cockpit to help drivers monitor every aspect of performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cab - the compartment where the operator sits in a vehicle

Etymology

Originally referred to a pit for fighting cocks, later used for the control area of aircraft and vehicles.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑkˌpɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒkˌpɪt / (BrE)
(archaic) A pit or enclosed area where cockfights are held.
pit for cockfights (archaic)
archaic

Examples

In the 18th century, the old tavern was famous for its cockpit in the basement.
They gathered around the cockpit to watch the illegal cockfight.
The city outlawed all forms of gambling and closed the last public cockpit.

Etymology

From 'cock' (the bird) and 'pit', originally referring to an arena for cockfighting.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɑkˌpɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒk.pɪt / (BrE)
the area in an aircraft where the pilot sits and controls the plane
pilot's compartment in an aircraft

Examples

The co-pilot entered the cockpit to review the flight plan before departure.
Modern commercial jets often have advanced computer systems in the cockpit.
Visitors were invited to tour the cockpit after the plane landed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flight deck - the area in a large aircraft where the pilots operate the controls

Etymology

Originally a term for an arena for cockfighting, later transferred to the control area of aircraft in the early 20th century.
