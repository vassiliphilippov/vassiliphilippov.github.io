to misadvise

1. VERB

/ ˌmɪs.ædˈvaɪz / (AmE), / ˌmɪs.ədˈvaɪz / (BrE)
to give wrong or bad advice to someone
give wrong or bad advice

Examples

The lawyer was accused of misadvising his client during the contract negotiations.
If you misadvise your friend, it could lead to serious consequences for both of you.
They claimed the consultant had misadvised them about the investment risks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to advise - to give advice to someone
  • Synonym: to mislead - to cause someone to have a wrong idea or impression

Etymology

From prefix 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'advise' (to give advice).
