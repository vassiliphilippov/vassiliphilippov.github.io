to yaup

1. VERB

/ jɔp / (AmE), / jɔːp / (BrE)
to give long, loud, and harsh cries, especially repeatedly
emit long, loud, harsh cries
archaic

Examples

The injured fox began to yaup in pain as night fell.
Barn owls sometimes yaup at dusk, startling nearby animals.
During the storm, the wind seemed almost to yaup through the empty streets.

Synset Examples

wail in self-pity
howl with sorrow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wail - to make a prolonged, high-pitched cry of pain, grief, or anger
  • Synonym: howl - to make a long, loud, doleful cry or wailing sound

Etymology

From Middle English *yolpen* (to yelp, cry out), related to Old Norse *jalpa* (to yelp).
