putrescine

1. NOUN

/ pjuˈtrɛˌsin / (AmE), / pjuˈtrɛsiːn / (BrE)
a colorless, crystalline compound with a foul odor, produced during the decomposition of animal tissue
foul-smelling compound from decaying tissue

Examples

The foul odor emanating from the laboratory was caused by the release of putrescine during the chemical reaction.
During decomposition, putrescine is one of the first compounds to be produced from amino acids.
Scientists study putrescine to understand the chemical processes occurring in decaying animal matter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cadaverine - a similar foul-smelling compound formed during protein decomposition

Etymology

From Latin 'putrescere' (to rot), by analogy with chemical naming conventions.
