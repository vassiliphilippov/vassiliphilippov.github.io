Cliftonia monophylla

1. NOUN

/ ˌklɪfˈtoʊniə ˌmɒnəˈfɪlə / (AmE), / ˌklɪfˈtəʊniə ˌmɒnəˈfɪlə / (BrE)
A small tree native to the southeastern United States, with glossy leaves and racemes of fragrant white flowers, found in low-lying coastal areas.
tree of the southeastern United States

Examples

The wetlands were dotted with Cliftonia monophylla, their white flowers adding fragrance to the air.
Botanists studied the distribution of Cliftonia monophylla along the Gulf Coast.
Gardeners value Cliftonia monophylla for its glossy foliage and ornamental blooms.

Etymology

From the genus name Cliftonia, honoring William Clifton, and Latin monophylla meaning 'single-leaved'.
