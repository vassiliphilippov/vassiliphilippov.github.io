blond

1. NOUN

/ blɑnd / (AmE), / blɒnd / (BrE)
a man or boy with light-colored hair and fair skin
fair-haired man or boy

Examples

The young blond sat quietly in the sunlight, his hair shining even brighter.
As a blond, he often stood out among his darker-haired friends.
The casting director was searching for a tall blond for the leading role.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blonde - a woman or girl with light hair and fair skin

Etymology

From French 'blond' (fair-haired), ultimately from Latin 'blundus' (yellow, fair).

2. ADJ

/ blɑnd / (AmE), / blɒnd / (BrE)
(of a person) having pale or yellowish hair and often fair skin and light-colored eyes.
having pale or yellowish hair

Examples

She admired the blond child playing in the park.
His blond hair shone in the sunlight as he walked down the street.
Many people associate Scandinavia with tall, blond individuals.

Synset Examples

blond Scandinavians
a house full of light-haired children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blonde - female form; also fair-haired woman
  • Antonym: brunette - having dark brown or black hair

Etymology

From French blond (fair-haired), ultimately from Medieval Latin blundus (yellow, fair).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00244463-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ blɑnd / (AmE), / blɒnd / (BrE)
a light grayish yellow to near white color, often associated with hair, wood, or fabrics.
light grayish yellow color

Examples

The walls were painted a soft blond that made the room feel brighter.
She chose a blond wood for the dining table to keep the look light and airy.
The artist mixed a touch of white to achieve the perfect shade of blond.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blonde - a light yellow hair color (sometimes also the noun for the color)

Etymology

From French 'blond' (fair-haired, light-colored), from medieval Latin 'blundus' (yellow).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04973194-n
Synset POS: n
