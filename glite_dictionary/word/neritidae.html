Neritidae

1. NOUN

/ nɛˈrɪtəˌdi / (AmE), / nɛˈrɪtɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small to medium-sized aquatic snails, often found in fresh or brackish water, known as neritids.
family of aquatic snails (neritids)

Examples

The Neritidae family includes many species adapted to both freshwater and marine habitats.
Some aquarists prefer Neritidae snails because they are effective algae eaters.
Fossils of Neritidae have been found in sediments dating back millions of years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neritid - an individual member of the Neritidae family

Etymology

From New Latin Neritidae, from Nerita (a genus of snails) + -idae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ nɛˈrɪtɪˌdiː / (AmE), / nɛˈrɪtɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of small to medium-sized aquatic snails, known as neritids, belonging to the class Gastropoda, commonly found in marine, freshwater, and brackish habitats
family of aquatic snails (neritids)

Examples

Members of the Neritidae family are often kept in freshwater aquariums for their attractive shells.
Several species from Neritidae can survive in both saltwater and freshwater environments.
Scientists study Neritidae to better understand the evolution of gastropods.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Nerita.
