shakeable

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃeɪkəbəl / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪkəbl / (BrE)
capable of being weakened, disturbed, or made less certain
able to be weakened or disturbed

Examples

His belief in his friends was not shakeable despite the rumors.
During the trial, the witness's testimony appeared shakeable under cross-examination.
Their confidence in the team's victory proved less shakeable than expected.

Synset Examples

the crisis proved his confidence was shakable

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unshakeable - not able to be weakened or disturbed
  • Degree Higher: fragile - easily broken or damaged; vulnerable
  • Degree Lower: strong - having strength or stability; not easily disturbed

Etymology

From 'shake' + '-able', meaning capable of being shaken or affected.
