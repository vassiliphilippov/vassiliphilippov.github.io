Jude the Apostle

1. PROPN

/ dʒuːd ði əˈpɑːsəl / (AmE), / dʒuːd ði əˈpɒsəl / (BrE)
One of the twelve Apostles of Jesus, traditionally regarded as the brother of St. James and invoked as the patron saint of desperate cases.
one of the twelve Apostles of Jesus

Examples

Many people pray to Jude the Apostle for help in desperate situations.
Churches dedicated to Jude the Apostle can be found around the world.
Stories of miracles attributed to Jude the Apostle have inspired countless believers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saint Jude - another name for Jude the Apostle
