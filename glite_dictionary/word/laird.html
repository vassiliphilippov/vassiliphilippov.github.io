laird

1. NOUN

/ lɛrd / (AmE), / lɛəd / (BrE)
(in Scotland) a landowner, especially one owning a large estate.
Scottish landowner

Examples

The local laird invited everyone to the annual harvest celebration on his estate.
After inheriting the title, she became the new laird of Glenview.
Many Scottish towns were historically dominated by a wealthy laird and his family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: landowner - a person who owns land
  • Antonym: tenant - a person who rents land from a landowner

Etymology

Scots, from northern Middle English *laverd* (lord), akin to 'lord'.
