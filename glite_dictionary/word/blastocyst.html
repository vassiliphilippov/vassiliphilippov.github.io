blastocyst

1. NOUN

/ ˈblæstəˌsɪst / (AmE), / ˈblɑːstəˌsɪst / (BrE)
an early stage in the development of placental mammals, consisting of a hollow ball of cells with cell differentiation
early embryo stage in mammals
medical

Examples

The implantation of the blastocyst is a crucial step in early pregnancy.
Researchers studied how the outer layer of the blastocyst develops into the placenta.
The blastocyst contains cells that will form both the embryo and supporting tissues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blastula - an early stage embryo consisting of a hollow sphere of cells

Etymology

From Greek 'blastos' (germ, sprout) + '-cyst' (bladder, sac).
