Eustoma

1. NOUN

/ juˈstoʊmə / (AmE), / juːˈstəʊmə / (BrE)
a genus of herbaceous plants native to warm regions of southern North America to northern South America, known for their bell-shaped flowers
a genus of flowering plants

Examples

Eustoma is often cultivated for its attractive, rose-like flowers.
Gardeners prize Eustoma for its long-lasting blooms and vibrant colors.
The florist included several stems of Eustoma in the arrangement to add elegance.

Etymology

From Greek eu- (good) + stoma (mouth), referring to the shape of the flower.

2. NOUN

/ ˌjuːˈstoʊmə / (AmE), / juːˈstɒmə / (BrE)
A small genus of flowering herbs native to warmer regions of southern North America to northern South America, known for species such as the lisianthus.
genus of flowering herbs (e.g., lisianthus)

Examples

Eustoma species, such as lisianthus, are popular in the floral industry for their beautiful blooms.
The botanist collected several samples of Eustoma during her field research in Texas.
Florists value Eustoma for its delicate, rose-like flowers and variety of colors.

Etymology

From Greek 'eu' meaning good and 'stoma' meaning mouth, referring to the flowers' appearance.
