Fuji-san

1. PROPN

/ ˈfuːdʒi ˌsɑːn / (AmE), / ˈfuːdʒi ˌsæn / (BrE)
an extinct volcano in south-central Honshu, Japan; the country's highest peak, famous for its symmetrical snow-capped summit and significance in Japanese culture and religion
the highest mountain in Japan

Examples

Many tourists dream of climbing Fuji-san to watch the sunrise from its summit.
Photographers around the world are captivated by the perfect shape of Fuji-san.
For centuries, Fuji-san has been considered a sacred site in Japanese culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mount Fuji - the English name for Fuji-san, Japan's highest mountain

Etymology

From Japanese 富士山 (Fuji-san), meaning 'Mount Fuji'.
