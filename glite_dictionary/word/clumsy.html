clumsy

1. ADJ

/ ˈklʌm.zi / (AmE), / ˈklʌm.zi / (BrE)
lacking grace or skill in movement or posture; moving in an awkward or uncoordinated way
awkward in movement or posture

Examples

His clumsy attempt to climb the fence made everyone laugh.
The clumsy waiter spilled soup on the customer's lap.
As a child, she was often called clumsy because she tripped over her own feet.

Synset Examples

a gawky lad with long ungainly legs
clumsy fingers
what an ungainly creature a giraffe is
heaved his unwieldy figure out of his chair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awkward - lacking ease or grace in movement or manner
  • Antonym: graceful - having or showing grace or elegance in movement

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse klumsa (to be overcome with amazement).

2. ADJ

/ ˈklʌmzi / (AmE), / ˈklʌmzi / (BrE)
difficult to handle or manage, especially because of awkward shape or size.
awkward to handle due to shape

Examples

The large, clumsy box wouldn't fit through the doorway without turning it sideways.
Carrying the clumsy bundle up the stairs was more difficult than she had expected.
A clumsy mug with an uneven handle kept slipping out of his grasp.

Synset Examples

an awkward bundle to carry
a load of bunglesome paraphernalia
clumsy wooden shoes
the cello, a rather ungainly instrument for a girl

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awkward - difficult to use, deal with, or manage
  • Synonym: ungainly - lacking grace or difficult to handle due to shape

Etymology

From Middle English *clomsen* (to be numb or benumbed).

3. ADJ

/ ˈklʌmzi / (AmE), / ˈklʌmzi / (BrE)
(of expression) lacking elegance, polish, or grace; awkward or inept.
lacking grace or elegance in expression

Examples

His clumsy apology only made the situation worse.
The novel's dialogue felt clumsy and unnatural at key moments.
She tried to explain her reasons in a rather clumsy fashion.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awkward - lacking grace or skill; ungainly, especially in movement or expression
  • Synonym: inept - lacking skill or ability; clumsy
  • Antonym: elegant - pleasingly graceful and stylish in appearance or manner

Etymology

Probably from Scandinavian; compare Swedish klumpsig (clumsy).
