germanite

1. NOUN

/ dʒɚˈmænˌaɪt / (AmE), / dʒəˈmæn.aɪt / (BrE)
a rare reddish-grey mineral composed of copper iron germanium sulfide
rare reddish-grey mineral

Examples

The mine yielded several specimens of germanite for the collection.
Germanite was first discovered in Namibia in the early 20th century.
Geologists identified the reddish-grey crystal as germanite based on its composition.

Etymology

From German 'Germanium' (element name) + '-ite', a common mineral suffix.
