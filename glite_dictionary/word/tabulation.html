tabulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌtæbjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtabjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
information that is systematically arranged in a table or tabular form, especially for the purpose of analysis or comparison.
information arranged in table form

Examples

The sales data was presented in a clear tabulation for the board meeting.
Careful tabulation of the results revealed several interesting patterns.
Her report included a concise tabulation of survey responses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: table - an arrangement of data in rows and columns

Etymology

From Latin 'tabula' (table, list) + English suffix '-ation'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtæbjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtabjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of arranging data or information in tabular form
the act of arranging data in tables

Examples

The tabulation of survey results provided clear insight into public opinion.
Accurate tabulation is essential for compiling scientific data.
Election officials are responsible for the tabulation of all votes cast.

Synset Examples

the tabulation of the results

Related Terms

  • Synonym: table - a systematic arrangement of data in columns and rows

Etymology

From Latin 'tabula' (a board, table), via French 'tabulation'.
