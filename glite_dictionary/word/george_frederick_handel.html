George Frederick Handel

1. PROPN

/ ˈhændəl / (AmE), / ˈhændl̩ / (BrE)
(1685–1759) A British composer of German birth, renowned for his baroque music, especially the oratorio 'Messiah'.
famous British baroque composer

Examples

The works of George Frederick Handel are regularly performed in concert halls worldwide.
Many people attend annual performances of George Frederick Handel's Messiah during the holiday season.
George Frederick Handel composed music for royal occasions, including the famous Water Music suites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Johann Sebastian Bach - prominent German baroque composer, contemporary of Handel
