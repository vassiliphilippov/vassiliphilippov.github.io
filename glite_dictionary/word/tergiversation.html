tergiversation

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɜːrdʒɪvərˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtɜːdʒɪvəˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of avoiding a straightforward statement or action by using ambiguous or evasive language; equivocation.
evasion by ambiguous language

Examples

The senator's tergiversation during the interview left many confused about his true position.
Repeated tergiversation eroded the public's trust in the company's official statements.
Critics accused her speech of blatant tergiversation, as she never addressed the question directly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equivocation - the use of ambiguous language to conceal the truth
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and straightforward

Etymology

From Latin 'tergiversatio', from 'tergiversari' meaning 'to turn one's back, to evade'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtɜːrdʒɪvərˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtɜːdʒɪvəˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of abandoning one's party, cause, or principles, typically for self-serving reasons
abandoning a party or cause

Examples

The senator's tergiversation during the vote was condemned by his former allies.
Her reputation suffered after her sudden tergiversation in support of the opposition.
Political history is replete with examples of tergiversation in times of upheaval.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defection - the act of abandoning allegiance or duty, especially to a country or cause
  • Antonym: faithfulness - remaining loyal and true to a cause or party

Etymology

From Latin tergiversatio (evasion, shuffling), from tergiversari (to turn one's back, evade).
