anuran

1. ADJ

/ əˈnʊrən / (AmE), / əˈnjʊərən / (BrE)
relating to, or characteristic of, frogs and toads (animals of the order Anura).
relating to frogs and toads

Examples

The anuran population in the wetland declined following the drought.
Researchers observed anuran larvae developing in temporary ponds.
Several species of anuran are endangered due to habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frogs - tailless amphibians of the order Anura
  • Synonym: toads - amphibians similar to frogs, in the order Anura

Etymology

From New Latin Anura, the order name, from Greek an- (without) + oura (tail).

2. NOUN

/ əˈnjʊrən / (AmE), / əˈnjʊərən / (BrE)
A member of the order of amphibians that lack tails as adults, including frogs and toads.
a tailless amphibian (frog or toad)

Examples

The tropical rainforest is home to a vast diversity of anuran species.
Scientists are studying how climate change affects local anuran populations.
The presence of anurans often indicates a healthy wetland ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frog - a tailless amphibian, typically having a smooth skin and living in or near water
  • Synonym: toad - a tailless amphibian, typically with a warty skin and more terrestrial than frogs

Etymology

From Greek 'an-' (without) + 'oura' (tail).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
