SAS

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛs.eɪˈɛs / (AmE), / ˌɛs.eɪˈɛs / (BrE)
a specialist regiment of the British Army trained for commando operations and clandestine missions, especially against terrorist threats.
British Army special forces regiment
british

Examples

The SAS was deployed to assist in the high-profile hostage rescue.
Many aspire to join the SAS due to its intense reputation and challenge.
He read a book about the secret missions carried out by the SAS.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Special Forces - military units trained for unconventional missions

Etymology

Acronym for Special Air Service.
