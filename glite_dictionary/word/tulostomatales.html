Tulostomatales

1. NOUN

/ ˌtjuːloʊˌstoʊməˈteɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌtjuːlɒstəˈmɑːtəliːz / (BrE)
an order of fungi in the class Gasteromycetes, characterized by stalked puffballs and related species
order of stalked puffball fungi

Examples

Several species within the Tulostomatales can be found in arid and semi-arid regions around the world.
Mycologists recently discovered a new genus of fungi in the Tulostomatales order.
The unique spore dispersal mechanism is one feature that distinguishes Tulostomatales from other fungi.

Etymology

From the genus name Tulostoma, itself from Greek 'tulos' (knob, lump) and 'stoma' (mouth), with taxonomic suffix -ales.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtjuːloʊstoʊˈmeɪtiːlz / (AmE), / ˌtjuːlɒstəʊˈmeɪtiːliːz / (BrE)
an order of fungi in the class Gasteromycetes, comprising puffball-like species characterized by their spore-bearing structures
order of fungi in Gasteromycetes

Examples

The Tulostomatales include fungi with stalked spore cases, often found in arid regions.
Recent studies have revised the classification of Tulostomatales based on DNA evidence.
Members of Tulostomatales are commonly called stalked puffballs due to their appearance.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Gasteromycetes - class of fungi that bear spores inside their fruit bodies

Etymology

From genus Tulostoma + Latin -ales, indicating an order in taxonomy.
