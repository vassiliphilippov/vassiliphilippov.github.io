Actaea alba

1. NOUN

/ ækˈtiːə ˈælbə / (AmE), / ækˈtiːə ˈælbə / (BrE)
A North American plant species with white, poisonous berries, also known as white baneberry or doll's eyes.
North American herb with white poisonous berries

Examples

Hikers should be cautious not to touch the berries of Actaea alba as they are highly toxic.
The white, eye-like berries of Actaea alba make it easy to identify in the forest.
Botanists conducted a study on the distribution of Actaea alba in North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white baneberry - Common name for Actaea alba, North American herb with white poisonous berries
  • Synonym: Actaea pachypoda - Synonym for Actaea alba, also called white baneberry

Etymology

From Latin: 'Actaea' (the genus, from Greek 'aktaios', meaning 'coastal') and 'alba' (meaning 'white').
