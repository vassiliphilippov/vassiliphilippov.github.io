Trifolium pratense

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈfoʊliəm prəˈtɛns / (AmE), / trɪˈfəʊlɪəm prəˈtɛns / (BrE)
A species of clover with red-purple to pink flowers, commonly grown as a forage plant; also known as red clover.
species of clover, red clover

Examples

Trifolium pratense is often used as a cover crop to improve soil fertility.
Farmers sow Trifolium pratense in their fields because of its high value as livestock feed.
The meadow was filled with the pink blooms of Trifolium pratense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red clover - common name for Trifolium pratense

Etymology

From Latin: *Trifolium* (three-leaved) + *pratense* (found in meadows).
