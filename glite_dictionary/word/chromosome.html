chromosome

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊ.məˌsoʊm / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊ.mə.səʊm / (BrE)
a threadlike structure made of DNA and protein found in the nucleus of most living cells, carrying genetic information in the form of genes
structure carrying genetic information in cells

Examples

Down syndrome is caused by the presence of an extra chromosome in the cells.
Each parent contributes one chromosome to their child for every pair.
Scientists analyzed the chromosome to identify possible genetic mutations.

Synset Examples

humans have 22 chromosome pairs plus two sex chromosomes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gene - a unit of heredity carried on chromosomes

Etymology

From Greek 'khrōma' (color) + 'sōma' (body), from the staining properties of chromosomes.
