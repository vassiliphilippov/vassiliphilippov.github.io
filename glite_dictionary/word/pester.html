to pester

1. VERB

/ ˈpɛstər / (AmE), / ˈpɛstə / (BrE)
to annoy or bother someone repeatedly or continually, often with requests or interruptions
annoy or bother persistently

Examples

If you pester your parents long enough, they might let you stay up late.
He kept pestering me with questions about my private life.
The little boy pestered his sister until she gave him some of her candy.

Synset Examples

The children teased the boy because of his stammer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to annoy - to irritate someone slightly or repeatedly
  • Synonym: to harass - to bother or trouble someone repeatedly
  • Antonym: calm - to make someone quiet or less agitated

Etymology

From Middle English 'pesteren', probably from Anglo-Norman 'pestre' (to trouble) and ultimately from Latin 'pestis' (plague).
