chordomesoderm

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔr.doʊˈmɛz.oʊ.dɝm / (AmE), / ˌkɔː.dəʊˈmɛz.ə.dɜːm / (BrE)
the region of embryonic mesoderm that develops into the notochord
embryonic tissue forming the notochord
medical

Examples

The chordomesoderm plays a crucial role in the early development of vertebrate embryos.
Researchers examined the differentiation of chordomesoderm tissue under a microscope.
The notochord arises from the chordomesoderm during embryogenesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mesoderm - the middle layer of an embryo in early development
  • Degree Higher: notochord - an embryonic structure that develops into the backbone

Etymology

From Greek *chorda* (string, notochord) + *mesoderm* (middle layer of an embryo).
