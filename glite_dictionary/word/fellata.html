Fellata

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlɑːtə / (AmE), / fəˈlɑːtə / (BrE)
a member of a nomadic pastoral people of western Africa, traditionally Muslim and engaged in cattle herding
nomadic ethnic group of western Africa

Examples

The Fellata are known for their distinctive traditional dress and cattle herding practices.
Many Fellata communities maintain their nomadic lifestyle across several West African countries.
A group of Fellata set up camp near the river during their seasonal migration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fulani - the same ethnic group, commonly used alternative name

Etymology

From Hausa 'Fulata', related to 'Fulani' (another name for the group).
