Francis of Assisi

1. PROPN

/ ˈfræn.sɪs əv əˈsi.si / (AmE), / ˈfræn.sɪs əv əˈsiː.si / (BrE)
(c. 1181–1226) An Italian monk and preacher who founded the Franciscan order of friars and is venerated as a saint in the Roman Catholic Church.
Italian monk, founder of Franciscans, Catholic saint

Examples

Many people are inspired by Francis of Assisi and his dedication to a life of poverty and service.
The Feast of Francis of Assisi is celebrated by Catholics around the world each October 4th.
Francis of Assisi is often associated with a love for animals and nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saint Francis - Alternative name for Francis of Assisi, Catholic saint
