cotillion

1. NOUN

/ kəˈtɪljən / (AmE), / kəˈtɪljən / (BrE)
a formal ball, especially one at which young women are introduced to society
formal ball for young women's debut

Examples

Each spring, the city's elite gathered for the annual cotillion.
She wore a white gown to her debut cotillion last weekend.
Hosting a cotillion was once a major event for many Southern families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ball - a large formal social gathering for dancing
  • Synonym: debutante ball - a formal event introducing young women to society

Etymology

From French 'cotillon' (petticoat, kind of dance), ultimately from Old French 'cote' (coat)

2. NOUN

/ kəˈtɪliən / (AmE), / kəˈtɪljən / (BrE)
a formal social dance or ball, especially one at which young women are introduced to society for the first time
formal ball introducing young women to society

Examples

She wore a beautiful white gown to her first cotillion.
The annual cotillion was the highlight of the social season.
Many families prepared for months before their daughters' cotillion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debutante ball - a formal ball presenting young women to society

Etymology

From French 'cotillon' (petticoat, dance), diminutive of 'cotte' (petticoat).

3. NOUN

/ koʊˈtɪliən / (AmE), / kəˈtɪliən / (BrE)
a lively, formal dance that originated in 18th-century France, often performed in groups and later associated with social balls
lively 18th-century French dance

Examples

The historical society hosted a cotillion to celebrate the town's founding anniversary.
Young people practiced the steps of the cotillion before the grand ball.
The novel described an elegant cotillion held in a glittering French ballroom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quadrille - a square dance from the same era, similar to the cotillion

Etymology

From French 'cotillon', meaning 'petticoat', likely referring to the lifting of skirts during the dance.
