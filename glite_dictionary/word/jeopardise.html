to jeopardise

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒɛpərˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛpədaɪz / (BrE)
to put someone or something in danger of harm, loss, or failure; to endanger
put at risk; endanger

Examples

Careless driving can jeopardise your safety and the safety of others.
The new policy may jeopardise our chances of winning future contracts.
Bringing outside food into the laboratory could jeopardise important experiments.

Synset Examples

The pollution is endangering the crops

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to endanger - to put at risk; to expose to danger
  • Antonym: to protect - to keep safe from harm or injury

Etymology

From Old French 'jeu parti' (a divided game or contest), later influenced by the word 'jeopardy'.
