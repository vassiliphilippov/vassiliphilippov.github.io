elaphure

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlæfjʊr / (AmE), / ɪˈlæfjʊə / (BrE)
a large Chinese deer (Père David's deer) that survives only in domesticated herds; now extinct in the wild
large Chinese deer, extinct in the wild

Examples

The only remaining elaphure live in protected reserves and zoological parks.
Researchers are working to reintroduce the elaphure to its native habitats in China.
The elaphure is unique for its combination of antlers and long tail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Père David's deer - a species of deer native to China, also known as elaphure

Etymology

From Modern Latin 'Elaphurus', the genus name, from Greek 'elaphos' (deer).
