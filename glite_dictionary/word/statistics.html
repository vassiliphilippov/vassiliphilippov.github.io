statistics

1. NOUN

/ stəˈtɪstɪks / (AmE), / stəˈtɪstɪks / (BrE)
the branch of mathematics dealing with the collection, analysis, interpretation, and presentation of numerical data, and the use of probability theory to estimate parameters in a population
mathematical field for data analysis

Examples

Many students find statistics challenging because it combines mathematics and real-world data.
He is pursuing a degree in statistics at the university.
Understanding statistics is essential for analyzing scientific research results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: probability - the likelihood or chance that a particular event will occur

Etymology

From New Latin 'statisticus' (of state affairs), from Italian 'statistica' (statecraft, statistics), relating to 'status' (state).
