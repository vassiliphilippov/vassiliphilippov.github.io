enantiomorph

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnæn.tioʊˌmɔːrf / (AmE), / ɪˈnæn.ti.əˌmɔːf / (BrE)
either of a pair of objects, especially molecules or crystals, that are mirror images of each other but cannot be superimposed
mirror-image object, non-superimposable
medical

Examples

The two forms of the crystal are enantiomorphs of each other.
In biology, certain amino acids exist as left-handed and right-handed enantiomorphs.
Chemists study the properties of enantiomorphs to understand how they affect biological systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enantiomer - one of a pair of molecules that are mirror images

Etymology

From Greek enantios (opposite) + -morph (form).
