perversive

1. ADJ

/ pərˈvɜrsɪv / (AmE), / pəˈvɜːsɪv / (BrE)
Tending to corrupt or pervert; having a morally corrupting influence.
tending to corrupt or pervert

Examples

The novel explores the perversive effects of power on human nature.
Some critics argued that the film had a perversive influence on young viewers.
He was alarmed by the perversive atmosphere that had begun to dominate the office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perverse - showing a deliberate and obstinate desire to behave unacceptably

Etymology

From Latin perversus (turned away, corrupted) + -ive (forming adjectives).
