foghorn

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑɡ.hɔrn / (AmE), / ˈfɒɡ.hɔːn / (BrE)
a device that produces a loud, low warning sound to aid ships' navigation in foggy conditions
loud warning device for ships in fog

Examples

The foghorn echoed across the bay, warning nearby ships of the hidden shoreline.
With visibility near zero, the captain relied on the foghorn to guide vessels safely through the harbor.
A deep booming sound from the foghorn startled the seagulls resting on the pier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: siren - a device that makes a loud warning sound, especially for emergencies

Etymology

From 'fog' (mist) and 'horn' (wind instrument or device).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːɡˌhɔrn / (AmE), / ˈfɒɡˌhɔːn / (BrE)
a device that produces a loud, low sound as a warning signal in foggy conditions, typically used on ships or near coasts to prevent collisions.
loud warning horn for foggy conditions

Examples

A loud foghorn sounded across the harbor as the ship approached in thick mist.
During the storm, the coastal foghorn was essential for guiding vessels safely to shore.
He could barely sleep with the foghorn blaring throughout the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: siren - a device that makes a loud warning sound

Etymology

From 'fog' + 'horn'; first used in the 19th century to describe warning devices used in fog.
