to iodise

1. VERB

/ ˈaɪəˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈaɪədaɪz / (BrE)
to treat or impregnate with iodine, especially to prevent infection
treat with iodine to prevent infection

Examples

The nurse carefully iodised the wound to prevent infection.
Doctors often iodise cuts and abrasions before applying a bandage.
He iodised the surgical area as a precaution against bacteria.

Synset Examples

iodize a wound

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disinfect - clean something to destroy bacteria or viruses
  • Antonym: to infect - to contaminate with harmful microorganisms

Etymology

From iodine + -ise (or -ize), suffix forming verbs.

2. VERB

/ ˈaɪəˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈaɪədaɪz / (BrE)
to treat or impregnate (a substance) with iodine
treat with iodine
british

Examples

Many countries iodise their table salt to help prevent iodine deficiency in the population.
The laboratory technician will iodise the samples before further analysis.
Food producers in the UK commonly iodise salt as a public health measure.

Synset Examples

iodize salt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to iodize - treat with iodine (American spelling)

Etymology

Derived from iodine, with the British English verb-forming suffix -ise.
