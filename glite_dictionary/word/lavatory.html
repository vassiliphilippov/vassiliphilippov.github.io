lavatory

1. NOUN

/ ˈlævəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈlævətəri / (BrE)
A room or fixture equipped with a toilet that is cleaned by the flow of water through it.
toilet or bathroom with running water

Examples

The airplane lavatory is located at the rear of the cabin.
Please make sure the lavatory is clean before guests arrive.
She excused herself and went to the lavatory during the intermission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toilet - a fixture for defecation and urination, typically connected to a plumbing system
  • Synonym: restroom - a public room with toilets and sinks

Etymology

From Latin 'lavatorium' (place for washing), from 'lavare' (to wash).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlævəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈlævət(ə)ri / (BrE)
A room or building equipped with one or more toilets, often including a sink for washing hands.
room or building with toilet(s)

Examples

The train's lavatory was out of order during the entire journey.
Please make sure to wash your hands after using the lavatory.
Passengers are reminded that the lavatory is located at the rear of the plane.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toilet - a fixture for defecation or urination, or a room containing it
  • Synonym: restroom - a public room containing toilets and sinks (American English)
  • Synonym: bathroom - a room containing a toilet and sink, often also a bath or shower

Etymology

From Late Latin lavatorium, from lavare (to wash).

3. NOUN

/ ˈlævəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈlavətəri / (BrE)
A bathroom sink that is permanently installed and connected to a water supply and drain, used for washing hands and face.
permanently installed bathroom sink

Examples

She leaned over the lavatory to wash her face with cold water.
The new house has an elegant marble lavatory in the guest bathroom.
Please keep the lavatory clean after use, especially if you've spilled water.

Synset Examples

he ran some water in the basin and splashed it on his face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basin - a bowl-shaped bathroom sink
  • Synonym: washbasin - a basin used for washing, especially in bathrooms
  • Synonym: sink - a fixed basin with a water supply and drain

Etymology

From Latin 'lavatorium' (a place for washing), from 'lavare' (to wash).
