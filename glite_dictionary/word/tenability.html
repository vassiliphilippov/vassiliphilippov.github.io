tenability

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛnəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌtɛnəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being able to be defended, maintained, or accepted as reasonable or valid.
quality of being defendable or reasonable

Examples

The scientists debated the tenability of the proposed explanation for the phenomenon.
She doubted the tenability of his position in light of the new evidence.
Legal experts discussed the tenability of the company's defense in court.

Synset Examples

he questioned the tenability of my claims

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tenable - able to be maintained or defended against attack or objection
  • Antonym: untenable - not able to be maintained or defended

Etymology

From Latin tenere (to hold) via French tenable (able to be held) + English -ity.
