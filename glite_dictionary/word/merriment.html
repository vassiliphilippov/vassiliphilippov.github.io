merriment

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛrɪmənt / (AmE), / ˈmɛrɪm(ə)nt / (BrE)
a feeling of cheerful enjoyment or fun; lively amusement and laughter
cheerful enjoyment or fun

Examples

The merriment of the holiday party lasted well into the night.
Their laughter and merriment could be heard down the street.
The festival was filled with music, dancing, and general merriment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joy - a feeling of great pleasure and happiness
  • Synonym: gaiety - the state of being cheerful and lively
  • Antonym: sadness - the condition or quality of being sad

Etymology

From Middle English 'merymente', from 'mery' (merry) + '-ment'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛrɪmənt / (AmE), / ˈmɛrɪmənt / (BrE)
cheerful or lively enjoyment, often involving laughter or celebration
cheerful enjoyment or amusement

Examples

The sound of merriment filled the hall as the guests celebrated together.
Children's merriment echoed through the playground on the first day of summer.
Amidst all the merriment, she quietly slipped away to enjoy a moment alone.

Synset Examples

I do it for the fun of it
he is fun to have around

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fun - enjoyment, amusement, or lighthearted pleasure
  • Synonym: gaiety - the state of being cheerful and lively

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French *mirement*, from *mirer* (to be pleased).
