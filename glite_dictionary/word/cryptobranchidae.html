Cryptobranchidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪptoʊˌbræŋˈkɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌkrɪptəʊˌbræŋˈkɪdɪiː / (BrE)
a family of large aquatic salamanders that includes the hellbenders and giant salamanders of eastern Asia and eastern North America
family of large aquatic salamanders

Examples

The family Cryptobranchidae includes the largest salamanders in the world.
Scientists study Cryptobranchidae to understand the evolution of amphibians.
Conservationists are concerned about habitat loss affecting Cryptobranchidae populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salamander - a group of amphibians resembling lizards, with a tail throughout life

Etymology

From Greek kryptos (hidden) + branchia (gills) + -idae (family suffix in zoological nomenclature).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪptoʊˌbræŋkɪˈdiː / (AmE), / ˌkrɪptəʊˌbræŋkɪˈdiː / (BrE)
a family of large aquatic salamanders that includes the hellbenders and giant salamanders
family of large aquatic salamanders

Examples

The Cryptobranchidae are known for their impressive size and fully aquatic lifestyle.
Members of the Cryptobranchidae family, like the hellbender, are critically important to freshwater ecosystems.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect endangered species within the Cryptobranchidae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hellbender - a large aquatic salamander native to eastern North America
  • Synonym: giant salamander - large salamanders in the family Cryptobranchidae found in Asia

Etymology

From Greek 'kryptos' (hidden) and 'branchia' (gills), referring to their concealed gill structures.
