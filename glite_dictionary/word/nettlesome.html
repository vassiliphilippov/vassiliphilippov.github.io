nettlesome

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɛt.əl.səm / (AmE), / ˈnɛt.əl.səm / (BrE)
Easily irritated, annoyed, or difficult to deal with; causing annoyance or discomfort.
easily irritated or annoying

Examples

The manager found the employee's nettlesome questions disruptive during the meeting.
Dealing with nettlesome regulations often frustrates small business owners.
Her nettlesome attitude made it hard for others to work with her.

Synset Examples

an incorrigibly fractious young man
not the least nettlesome of his countrymen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritable - easily annoyed or angered
  • Antonym: placid - not easily upset or excited

Etymology

From 'nettle' (a stinging plant) + '-some' (having the quality of).
