Turdus migratorius

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝdəs ˌmaɪɡrəˈtɔriəs / (AmE), / ˈtɜːdəs ˌmaɪɡrəˈtɔːrɪəs / (BrE)
A large North American thrush with a distinctive rust-red breast and abdomen, commonly known as the American robin.
American robin; a North American thrush

Examples

The nest of the Turdus migratorius is often found in suburban gardens across the United States.
Birdwatchers eagerly anticipate the return of the Turdus migratorius each spring.
The song of the Turdus migratorius is a familiar sound on early mornings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American robin - Common name for Turdus migratorius, a North American thrush
  • Degree Higher: thrush - Songbird of the family Turdidae, to which Turdus migratorius belongs

Etymology

From Latin; 'Turdus' meaning 'thrush' and 'migratorius' meaning 'migratory'.
