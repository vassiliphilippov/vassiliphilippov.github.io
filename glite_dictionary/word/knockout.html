knockout

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɑːkˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈnɒkˌaʊt / (BrE)
extremely strong, impressive, or vigorous; having a powerful effect
extremely strong or impressive

Examples

That boxer delivered a knockout punch that ended the match in seconds.
The team won by a knockout performance in the final round.
Her knockout argument convinced everyone in the room.

Synset Examples

a hard left to the chin
a knockout punch
a severe blow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powerful - having great strength or force
  • Antonym: weak - lacking physical strength or intensity

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɑkˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈnɒkˌaʊt / (BrE)
An act of knocking someone unconscious with a blow, especially in boxing; a hit or punch that results in an opponent being unable to continue.
a blow that renders an opponent unconscious

Examples

The boxer won the match with a stunning knockout in the third round.
His right hook delivered a knockout that left his opponent unconscious.
Spectators erupted in cheers after the unexpected knockout.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: knockdown - a blow that knocks an opponent to the ground but not unconscious

Etymology

From the phrase 'knock out', meaning to render unconscious; first used in boxing in the late 19th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːkˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈnɒkaʊt / (BrE)
a very attractive or strikingly beautiful woman, often in a way that attracts admiration
very attractive woman
informal

Examples

Everyone at the party agreed that Lisa was a real knockout in her red dress.
He couldn't help but stare when the knockout walked into the room.
The film's leading actress is a knockout admired for both her talent and beauty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stunner - a woman who is extremely attractive
  • Synonym: bombshell - an extremely attractive woman

Etymology

From the boxing term 'knockout', meaning a decisive victory, later used figuratively for impressive attractiveness (mid-20th century).
