Gasterosteidae

1. NOUN

/ ɡæs.təˈrɑsti.aɪˌdi / (AmE), / ɡastəˈrɒstiːɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small spiny-finned fishes commonly known as sticklebacks, found in fresh and coastal marine waters of the northern hemisphere.
family of fishes known as sticklebacks

Examples

The family Gasterosteidae includes several well-known species of sticklebacks.
Biologists study Gasterosteidae to understand evolutionary adaptation in fish.
Members of the Gasterosteidae family are found in both marine and freshwater environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stickleback - any small fish of the family Gasterosteidae, known for their spiny backs

Etymology

From New Latin *Gasterosteus* (type genus), from Greek *gastēr* (stomach) + *osteon* (bone).
