factorisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæktəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfæktəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of breaking down an expression into factors that multiply together to give the original expression, especially in mathematics
mathematical splitting into factors

Examples

The factorisation of quadratic equations is an important algebraic skill.
Prime factorisation is used to express a number as a product of prime numbers.
She checked her answer by multiplying the factors from the factorisation back together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: factorization - alternative spelling, primarily used in American English

Etymology

From factor + -isation, modelled after the verb 'to factorise'.
