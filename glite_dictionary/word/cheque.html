cheque

1. NOUN

/ tʃek / (AmE), / tʃɛk / (BrE)
A written order instructing a bank to pay a specified sum of money from a person's account to the person named on the order.
written order to pay money from a bank account
british

Examples

He wrote a cheque to cover the cost of the repairs.
Please make all cheque payments payable to Smith & Co.
She deposited a cheque from her employer into her savings account.

Synset Examples

he paid all his bills by check

Related Terms

  • Synonym: check - American term for a written order to a bank to pay money
  • Synonym: bank draft - A written order for payment of funds by a bank

Etymology

From French *chèque*, itself from English *check* (banking sense), originally meaning ‘counterfoil, check’ in bookkeeping.
