Hexamita

1. NOUN

/ hɛkˈsæmɪtə / (AmE), / hɛkˈsamɪtə / (BrE)
A genus of flagellated protozoan parasites that are free-living or inhabit the intestines of birds and other animals.
a genus of flagellated protozoan parasites
medical

Examples

Hexamita infections can cause digestive disorders in certain bird species.
Veterinarians may test for Hexamita when diagnosing intestinal issues in poultry.
Treatment for Hexamita often involves specific antiparasitic medications.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining hexa- (six) and Greek mitos (thread), referring to the six flagella.

2. NOUN

/ hɛkˈsæmɪtə / (AmE), / hɛkˈsæmɪtə / (BrE)
a genus of flagellate protozoa, some species of which are free-living or parasitic in the intestines of birds and other animals
genus of flagellate protozoa

Examples

Hexamita species can be found in the intestines of many waterfowl.
Researchers studied the effects of Hexamita infection on the bird population.
The life cycle of Hexamita includes both free-living and parasitic stages.

Etymology

From Greek 'hexa' (six) and 'mitos' (thread), referring to the organism's flagella.
