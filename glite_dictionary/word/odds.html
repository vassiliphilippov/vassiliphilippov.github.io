odds

1. NOUN

/ ɑdz / (AmE), / ɒdz / (BrE)
the ratio representing the likelihood of an event, commonly expressing how much one person's wager is compared to another
ratio expressing likelihood or wager in betting

Examples

The odds for the horse race were five to one, attracting many hopeful gamblers.
He checked the odds before placing his bet on the football match.
Bookmakers set the odds based on their assessment of each team's chances.

Synset Examples

he offered odds of two to one

Related Terms

  • Synonym: probability - the likelihood or chance of an event occurring
  • Antonym: even money - a bet where the amount won is equal to the amount staked

Etymology

Originally from Middle English 'odds', plural of 'odd' (unequal), of Scandinavian origin.

2. NOUN

/ ɑdz / (AmE), / ɒdz / (BrE)
the probability that a particular event will happen, especially as expressed by numbers in betting or statistics
probability or likelihood (esp. in betting)

Examples

The odds of winning the lottery are extremely low.
Bookmakers gave high odds on the underdog team winning the match.
What are the odds that it will rain tomorrow?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: probability - the likelihood of something happening
  • Synonym: chance - likelihood that something will happen
  • Antonym: certainty - something sure or certain to happen

Etymology

From Middle English 'oddes', plural of 'odde' (meaning unequal or uneven), related to Old Norse 'oddi' (point of land, triangle, odd number).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
