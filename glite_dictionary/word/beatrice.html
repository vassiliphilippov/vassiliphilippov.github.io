Beatrice

1. PROPN

/ ˈbiːətrɪs / (AmE), / ˈbiːətrɪs / (BrE)
the woman who guided Dante through Paradise in the Divine Comedy, symbolizing divine love and wisdom.
Dante's guide in Paradise in the Divine Comedy

Examples

In Dante's Divine Comedy, Beatrice leads the poet through the celestial spheres of Paradise.
Many scholars see Beatrice as a symbol of divine grace in Italian literature.
The character of Beatrice has inspired countless works of art and poetry.

Etymology

From Italian Beatrice, from Latin Beatrix meaning 'she who makes happy'.
