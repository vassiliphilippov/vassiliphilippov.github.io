Notoryctus

1. NOUN

/ noʊ.təˈrɪk.təs / (AmE), / nəʊ.təˈrɪk.təs / (BrE)
A genus of marsupials known as marsupial moles, native to Australia, and the only genus in the family Notoryctidae.
genus of Australian marsupial moles

Examples

The Notoryctus is rarely seen due to its subterranean lifestyle.
Researchers captured a video of a Notoryctus burrowing through the sand in the Australian outback.
The family Notoryctidae consists of only one genus, Notoryctus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marsupial mole - a small, burrowing, mole-like marsupial of Australia

Etymology

From Greek notos (south) + oryktos (dug up, mined), referring to the burrowing habits and southern distribution.
