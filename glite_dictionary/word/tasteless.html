tasteless

1. ADJ

/ ˈteɪst.ləs / (AmE), / ˈteɪst.ləs / (BrE)
Having no perceptible flavor or taste.
lacking flavor or taste

Examples

The soup was completely tasteless and needed more seasoning.
After recovering from a cold, everything she ate seemed tasteless.
I found the omelet tasteless, so I added some hot sauce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flavorless - lacking flavor or taste
  • Antonym: tasty - having a pleasant, distinct flavor

Etymology

From 'taste' + '-less', indicating absence of taste.

2. ADJ

/ ˈteɪstləs / (AmE), / ˈteɪstləs / (BrE)
Showing a lack of aesthetic judgment or social appropriateness; in poor taste.
lacking aesthetic or social taste

Examples

The comedian's tasteless jokes offended many people at the event.
She thought the bright orange sofa was tasteless and clashed with the rest of the room.
Posting rumors about colleagues online was considered not only rude but profoundly tasteless.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vulgar - lacking sophistication or good taste
  • Antonym: refined - having good taste or manners

Etymology

Formed from 'taste' + '-less,' meaning 'without taste.'

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
