informatics

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnfərˈmætɪks / (AmE), / ˌɪnfəˈmatɪks / (BrE)
the science of collecting, processing, storing, retrieving, and classifying information, especially using computers
information science, especially with computers

Examples

She is pursuing a degree in informatics at the university.
The field of informatics plays a crucial role in modern healthcare systems.
Advances in informatics have transformed the way we analyze large amounts of data.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: information science - the study of the collection and management of information

Etymology

From French 'informatique', coined around 1960, combining 'information' and 'automatique' (automatic).
