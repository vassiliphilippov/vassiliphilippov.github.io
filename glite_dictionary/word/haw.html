haw

1. NOUN

/ hɔ / (AmE), / hɔː / (BrE)
a spring-flowering shrub or small tree of the genus Crataegus, typically with thorny branches, white or pink flowers, and red berries
thorny shrub or tree (Crataegus)

Examples

The hedgerows were filled with blossoming haw in the early spring.
Birds often feed on the red berries produced by the haw tree.
Gardeners sometimes plant haw for its attractive flowers and dense thorns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawthorn - another name for the Crataegus shrub or tree

Etymology

Old English *haga* meaning 'hedge', referring to the plant's use in hedges and boundaries.

2. NOUN

/ hɑ / (AmE), / hɔː / (BrE)
the nictitating membrane in the inner corner of the eye, especially in horses, which acts as a protective third eyelid
nictitating membrane in a horse's eye

Examples

The veterinarian examined the horse's haw for signs of infection.
If the haw becomes inflamed, it can cause discomfort and vision problems.
She gently pulled down the eyelid to check the haw after the accident.

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin; used in veterinary and equine contexts.

3. VERB

/ hɑ / (AmE), / hɔː / (BrE)
to utter the sound 'haw', especially as a hesitation or inarticulate response during speech
utter 'haw' as hesitation

Examples

When asked for his opinion, he began to haw awkwardly before answering the question.
She couldn't help but haw when put on the spot during the interview.
Instead of giving a straight answer, he started to haw and lose his train of thought.

Synset Examples

he hemmed and hawed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hem - to utter the sound 'hem' as a hesitation or pause

Etymology

Imitative of the inarticulate sound 'haw', used as a hesitation in speech.
