bittersome

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɪtərˌsʌm / (AmE), / ˈbɪtəsəm / (BrE)
Having a quality that is a mix of sadness and reflection, often evoking a sense of gentle melancholy.
sad and reflective quality

Examples

The film possessed a bittersome mood that lingered long after the credits rolled.
Her smile had a bittersome edge, suggesting recent memories weighed on her mind.
A bittersome silence settled over the room as they remembered old times.

Synset Examples

The news had a bittersome quality that left everyone reflective.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bittersweet - having a taste or feeling that is both bitter and sweet; mixed emotions of pleasure and sadness

Etymology

Formed from "bitter" + suffix "-some," following the pattern of adjectives like "tiresome" and "troublesome."
