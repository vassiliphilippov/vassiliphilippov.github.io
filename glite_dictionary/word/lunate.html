lunate

1. ADJ

/ ˈluː.neɪt / (AmE), / ˈluː.neɪt / (BrE)
shaped like a crescent or resembling the new moon in shape.
crescent-shaped; resembling a new moon

Examples

The fossil displayed a lunate mark on its surface.
The architect designed a window with a lunate curve at the top.
A lunate shape was visible in the night sky as the moon began to wax.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crescent - having the shape of a segment of a ring tapering to points at the ends, like a new moon

Etymology

From Latin 'lunatus', from 'luna' (moon).
