stinting

1. ADJ

/ ˈstɪn.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstɪn.tɪŋ / (BrE)
unwilling to spend or give freely; sparing or frugal in resource use
not generous; sparing

Examples

Her stinting approach to spending ensured the project stayed under budget.
The stinting portions at the restaurant left many customers unsatisfied.
Despite his wealth, his stinting nature prevented him from donating to charity.

Synset Examples

an economical meal

a frugal lunch

sparing in their use of heat and light
stinting in bestowing gifts
‘scotch’ is used only informally

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frugal - using money or resources carefully and sparingly
  • Antonym: generous - willing to give more of something than is strictly necessary or expected
  • Synonym: sparing - moderate or economical in use

Etymology

Present participle of 'stint', from Old English 'styntan' (to make blunt, lessen).
