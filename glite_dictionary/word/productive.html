productive

1. ADJ

/ prəˈdʌktɪv / (AmE), / prəˈdʌktɪv / (BrE)
producing good or useful results; achieving or capable of achieving a lot
yielding good results; efficient

Examples

Today was a very productive day at work, and I finished all my pending tasks.
She always tries to make meetings more productive by sticking to the agenda.
After hours of discussion, we finally had a productive conversation and reached an agreement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fruitful - producing good or helpful results
  • Antonym: unproductive - not yielding positive or useful results

Etymology

From Latin 'productivus' (producing), from 'producere' (bring forth).

2. ADJ

/ prəˈdʌktɪv / (AmE), / prəˈdʌktɪv / (BrE)
Having the ability to produce or originate; able to generate or create something.
having the ability to produce or create

Examples

The soil in this region is extremely productive, yielding large harvests every year.
Her research proved especially productive in generating new theories.
A productive environment encourages innovation and creativity.

Synset Examples

generative power
generative forces

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generative - having the power or function of generating or producing

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'productivus', from Latin 'producere' (to lead forth, produce).

3. ADJ

/ prəˈdʌktɪv / (AmE), / prəˈdʌktɪv / (BrE)
producing or capable of producing large amounts of something; fertile or fruitful.
producing large amounts; fertile

Examples

The region's productive farmland provides food for thousands of people.
After years of drought, the soil finally became productive again.
This valley is known for its productive orchards and vineyards.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fertile - able to produce abundantly; highly productive, especially of land or soil
  • Antonym: barren - not productive; unable to produce plants or crops

Etymology

From Latin 'productivus' (fit for production).

/ prəˈdʌktɪv / (AmE), / prəˈdʌktɪv / (BrE)
producing or capable of producing, especially in large amounts; yielding results or benefits.
producing a lot; yielding results

Examples

The company had a highly productive year, launching several new products.
She uses her mornings in a productive way, focusing on her most important tasks.
After a productive meeting, the team left with clear goals and a plan of action.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fruitful - producing good or abundant results
  • Antonym: unproductive - not producing or achieving much; not fruitful
