catabiosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkætəˈbaɪəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈbʌɪəsɪs / (BrE)
the normal process of cell aging and deterioration
normal aging of cells
medical

Examples

Scientists study catabiosis to understand how tissues deteriorate with age.
Certain genetic factors can accelerate the rate of catabiosis in living organisms.
Preventing or slowing catabiosis could lead to anti-aging therapies in the future.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: senescence - the process of aging
  • Antonym: rejuvenation - the process of making something young again

Etymology

From Greek 'kata-' (down) + 'bios' (life) + '-osis' (process or condition).
