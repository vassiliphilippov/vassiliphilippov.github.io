cortina

1. NOUN

/ kɔrˈtiːnə / (AmE), / kɔːˈtiːnə / (BrE)
A cobweb-like partial veil made of silky fibers that covers the gills of certain mushrooms, especially in their immature stages.
cobweb-like veil in some mushrooms

Examples

The young mushroom was protected by a delicate cortina stretching from the cap to the stem.
As the mushroom matured, the cortina broke, leaving faint fibers around the stem.
Mycologists use the presence of a cortina to help identify certain species of fungi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: veil - a membranous tissue covering the gills or spores of some fungi

Etymology

From Latin 'cortina' meaning 'curtain'.
