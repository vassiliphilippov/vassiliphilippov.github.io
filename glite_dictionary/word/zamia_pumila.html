Zamia pumila

1. NOUN

/ ˈzeɪmiə ˈpjuːmɪlə / (AmE), / ˈzeɪmɪə ˈpjuːmɪlə / (BrE)
a small, woody cycad native to Florida, the West Indies, and Cuba, known for its tough roots and stems used to produce arrowroot
a small cycad of Florida and the Caribbean

Examples

The Zamia pumila is commonly found in sandy soils across southern Florida.
Native peoples traditionally used the roots of Zamia pumila to make arrowroot.
Botanists study Zamia pumila for its resilience and unique reproductive cycle.

Etymology

Zamia from Greek 'zamia' (damage, loss), used for the genus; pumila from Latin 'pumilus' (dwarf).
