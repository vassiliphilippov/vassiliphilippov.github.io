uncompleted

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.kəmˈpliː.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kəmˈpliː.tɪd / (BrE)
not yet finished; not brought to completion.
not yet finished

Examples

The building remains uncompleted due to a lack of funds.
Several uncompleted projects filled his office with paperwork.
Her novel lay uncompleted on her desk for months.

Synset Examples

his thesis is still incomplete
an uncompleted play

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incomplete - not having all necessary parts or not finished
  • Antonym: finished - having been completed

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' meaning 'not' + 'completed'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.kəmˈpliː.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kəmˈpliː.tɪd / (BrE)
not finished or not successfully carried out or achieved; especially in sports, not caught or not achieved as intended.
not finished; not achieved

Examples

The quarterback threw an uncompleted pass in the final seconds of the game.
Construction was delayed, and the project remains uncompleted to this day.
Her set of novels is still uncompleted, with readers awaiting the final volume.

Synset Examples

an uncompleted pass

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incomplete - not finished; lacking some parts

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' to 'completed'. 'Complete' from Latin 'completus' (filled up, finished).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00527492-s
Synset POS: s
