lumbus

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌmbəs / (AmE), / ˈlʌmbəs / (BrE)
either side of the lower back between the hipbone and the ribs in humans and quadrupeds
lower back region beside the spine
medical

Examples

The physician examined the patient's lumbus for signs of tenderness.
Pain in the lumbus can indicate problems with the kidneys or musculoskeletal system.
In animals, injury to the lumbus may affect their mobility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loin - the part of the body on either side of the backbone between the ribs and the pelvis

Etymology

From Latin *lumbus* (loin, the loins or lower back).
