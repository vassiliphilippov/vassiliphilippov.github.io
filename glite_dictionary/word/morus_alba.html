Morus alba

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɔr.əs ˈæl.bə / (AmE), / ˌmɔː.rəs ˈæl.bə / (BrE)
A species of mulberry tree native to Asia, known for its white to pale red fruit and leaves used as food for silkworms.
Asian mulberry tree; silkworm food

Examples

The leaves of Morus alba are essential for raising healthy silkworms.
Orchards of Morus alba thrive in temperate regions of Asia.
Some herbal remedies use the dried fruit of Morus alba for medicinal purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white mulberry - common name for Morus alba

Etymology

From Latin Morus (mulberry) and alba (white).
