aliment

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.ə.mənt / (AmE), / ˈæl.ɪ.mənt / (BrE)
food; nourishment; something that provides sustenance to the body.
food; nourishment

Examples

Many ancient texts refer to honey as both an aliment and a medicine.
Proper aliment is essential for the growth and health of young children.
The botanist studied plants used as aliment by indigenous communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: food - any nutritious substance that people or animals eat or drink to maintain life and growth
  • Synonym: nourishment - the substances necessary for growth, health, and good condition

Etymology

From Latin alimentum (nourishment, food), from alere (to nourish).

2. VERB

/ ˈælɪˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈælɪm(ə)nt / (BrE)
to provide nourishment or sustenance to; to feed
to nourish or feed
archaic

Examples

The caretaker did her best to aliment the injured bird until it recovered.
In times of famine, it was difficult to aliment the entire population.
He aliments his plants with a homemade fertilizer mix.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to nourish - to provide with food necessary for life and growth
  • Antonym: to starve - to suffer or die from lack of food

Etymology

From Latin alimentum (nourishment, food), from alere (to nourish).
