maid

1. NOUN

/ meɪd / (AmE), / meɪd / (BrE)
(archaic) An unmarried girl or young woman, especially one who is a virgin.
unmarried girl or virgin (archaic)
archaic

Examples

In old ballads, a noble maid is often pursued by many suitors.
The poet wrote of a fair maid lost in the forest.
Legends speak of a maid whose virtue was never questioned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maiden - a young unmarried woman, especially a virgin

Etymology

From Old English *mægden* (maiden, virgin).

2. NOUN

/ meɪd / (AmE), / meɪd / (BrE)
a female servant whose job is to clean, cook, or otherwise take care of housework in someone else's home
female domestic servant

Examples

The wealthy family hired a new maid to keep the house clean.
She worked as a maid in a hotel during the summer holidays.
The old mansion has several maids who help with daily chores.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: servant - a person employed to perform duties for others, especially in a household

Etymology

From Old English mægd (maiden, young woman).
