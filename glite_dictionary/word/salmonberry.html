salmonberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæm.ənˌbɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈsæm.ənˌbɛr.i / (BrE)
a North American shrub of the rose family with yellow or orange edible berries, resembling raspberries
a shrub with yellow or orange berries

Examples

The trail was lined with salmonberry bushes heavy with ripe fruit.
We made jam from the salmonberry harvest this summer.
Birds often feed on the sweet salmonberry in early autumn.

Etymology

Named for the salmon-pink color of its berries.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsæmənˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈsæmənˌbɛri / (BrE)
A shrub native to western North America with white or pink flowers and thimble-shaped orange or red edible berries.
shrub with edible orange-red berries

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with salmonberry bushes heavy with ripe fruit.
Native people have long eaten salmonberry berries and shoots as traditional foods.
She planted a salmonberry in her backyard to attract birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raspberry - a soft red edible fruit; shrub of the rose family

3. NOUN

/ ˈsæm.ənˌbɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈsæm.ənˌbɛr.i / (BrE)
a shrub native to western North America, with large pinkish-orange edible berries and red or pink flowers
shrubs with edible orange-pink berries

Examples

The salmonberry bushes flourished all along the riverbank, attracting hungry birds and hikers alike.
She picked a handful of ripe salmonberries to snack on during her walk in the woods.
The jam made from fresh salmonberries has a unique, delicate flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raspberry - a soft red edible fruit of a plant in the rose family; the plant itself

Etymology

From the resemblance of the berry's color to the flesh of salmon fish.
