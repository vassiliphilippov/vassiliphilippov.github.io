Nauruan

1. NOUN

/ nɔːˈruːən / (AmE), / nɔːˈruːən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Nauru, an island country in the Pacific Ocean
person from Nauru

Examples

The Nauruan delegation presented their statement at the United Nations conference.
She learned a lot about Nauruan culture during her visit to the island.
Many Nauruans are engaged in phosphate mining, which has been vital to the country's economy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nauru - an island country in Micronesia, Pacific Ocean

Etymology

From Nauru (country name) + -an (denoting belonging or relating to).

2. ADJ

/ nɑːˈruː.ən / (AmE), / nɑːˈruː.ən / (BrE)
of or relating to the island republic of Nauru or its people.
relating to Nauru or its people

Examples

The Nauruan government introduced new policies to address climate change.
She enjoyed learning about Nauruan culture during her travels in the Pacific.
A group of Nauruan students visited Australia for an educational exchange.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nauru - a small island country in the Pacific Ocean

Etymology

From Nauru, the name of the Pacific island nation, with English adjectival suffix -an.

WordNet ID: oewn-03100426-a
Synset POS: a
