slithering

1. ADJ

/ ˈslɪðərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈslɪðərɪŋ / (BrE)
Moving smoothly and quietly, as if sliding along a slippery surface.
moving by sliding smoothly

Examples

The snake was slithering silently through the thick grass.
After the rain, the children went slithering down the muddy hillside.
He made a slithering escape across the icy pavement.

Synset Examples

his slipping and slithering progress over the ice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sliding - moving smoothly along a surface while maintaining continuous contact

Etymology

Present participle of 'slither', which is from Middle English 'slitheren', of uncertain origin, perhaps imitative.
