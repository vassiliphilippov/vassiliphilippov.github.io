vagabond

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæɡəˌbɑnd / (AmE), / ˈvaɡəbɒnd / (BrE)
A thing that behaves like a wanderer, having no fixed place and moving unpredictably.
thing that has no fixed place

Examples

Those abandoned cars were like vagabonds on the city streets, never staying in one spot for long.
The comet was considered a vagabond of the sky, roaming across the heavens unpredictably.
Empty cargo ships, vagabonds of the ocean, drifted wherever the current took them.

Synset Examples

pirate ships were vagabonds of the sea

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wanderer - someone or something that moves from place to place with no fixed home

Etymology

From Latin vagabundus (wandering), from vagari (to wander).

2. ADJ

/ ˈvæɡəˌbɑnd / (AmE), / ˈvaɡəbɒnd / (BrE)
wandering from place to place with no fixed home and no ties to a community; having a nomadic or unsettled lifestyle.
wandering with no fixed home

Examples

After years of living a vagabond existence, he finally settled down in a small town.
She wrote a memoir about her vagabond travels across three continents.
His vagabond lifestyle made it hard for him to maintain friendships.

Synset Examples

led a vagabond life
a rootless wanderer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nomadic - living a wandering life, moving from place to place
  • Antonym: settled - living in one place permanently, opposite of wandering

Etymology

From Latin vagabundus (wandering, strolling), from vagari (to wander).

3. NOUN

/ ˈvæɡəˌbɑnd / (AmE), / ˈvæɡəbɒnd / (BrE)
a person who wanders from place to place without a home or regular employment
person who wanders without a home or job

Examples

The old vagabond wandered the countryside, living off whatever he could find.
She was fascinated by tales of vagabonds who traveled the world without a care.
Many regarded him as a vagabond rather than a respectable citizen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drifter - a person who moves from place to place with no fixed home or job
  • Synonym: tramp - a person who travels from place to place on foot in search of work or as a vagrant
  • Antonym: settler - a person who settles in a new region or community

Etymology

From Latin vagabundus (wandering), from vagari (to wander).
