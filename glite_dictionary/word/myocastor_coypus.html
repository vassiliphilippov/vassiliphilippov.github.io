Myocastor coypus

1. NOUN

/ maɪ.oʊˈkæstər ˈkɔɪpəs / (AmE), / maɪ.əʊˈkɑːstə ˈkɔɪpəs / (BrE)
a large, semi-aquatic South American rodent, also known as the coypu, bred for its fur and now introduced to many regions
South American aquatic rodent (coypu)

Examples

Myocastor coypus was introduced into North America, where it has since become invasive in wetland areas.
Farmers have struggled to control the Myocastor coypus population, which damages crops and riverbanks.
The luxurious fur of the Myocastor coypus is highly valued in the fashion industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coypu - another name for Myocastor coypus
  • Synonym: beaver - large semiaquatic rodent, similar in appearance but unrelated

Etymology

From Greek 'mys' (mouse) and 'kástōr' (beaver); species name from indigenous South American terms.
