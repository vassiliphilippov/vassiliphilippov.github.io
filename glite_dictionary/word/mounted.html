mounted

1. ADJ

/ ˈmaʊntɪd / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪd / (BrE)
assembled or prepared for use, especially by being fixed or attached to a support or base
attached to a support or base

Examples

The mounted photographs were displayed in elegant frames on the gallery wall.
She admired the mounted deer head above the fireplace, a hunting trophy from her grandfather.
A mounted projector can save desk space in a small office.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unmounted - not attached to a support or base
Source: WordNet

2. ADJ

/ ˈmaʊntɪd / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪd / (BrE)
decorated or equipped with an added material or ornament, especially as part of the object's structure; often used in combination
decorated or equipped with an added material

Examples

He handed her the gold-mounted mirror, which sparkled in the sunlight.
The brass-mounted sword hung proudly on the wall of his study.
Many Victorian albums were leather-mounted and edged with silver.

Synset Examples

the trim brass-mounted carbine of the ranger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ornamented - decorated with a decorative detail or feature
  • Antonym: plain - without any extra decoration or added material

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'mount', from Old French 'monter', from Latin 'montare' (to go up).
