Limosa

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈmoʊsə / (AmE), / lɪˈməʊsə / (BrE)
A genus of large wading birds in the family Scolopacidae, commonly known as godwits.
genus of birds (godwits)

Examples

The genus Limosa includes several species of migratory godwits found around the world.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of Limosa in northern Europe.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a member of the Limosa genus in the wetland reserve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: godwit - a large wading bird with a long bill, belonging to the genus Limosa

Etymology

From Latin *limosa* ('muddy'), referring to the birds' preferred muddy habitats.
