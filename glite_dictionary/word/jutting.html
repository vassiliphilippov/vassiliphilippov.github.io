jutting

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒʌtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌtɪŋ / (BrE)
extending out above or beyond a surface or boundary; projecting outward
extending out; projecting outward

Examples

The jutting rocks made it difficult to walk along the shoreline.
A jutting balcony overlooked the busy street below.
His jutting chin gave his face a determined look.

Synset Examples

the jutting limb of a tree
massive projected buttresses
his protruding ribs
a pile of boards sticking over the end of his truck

Related Terms

  • Synonym: projecting - extending out from a surface
  • Antonym: receding - moving back from a surface; not extending outward

Etymology

From the verb 'jut', which comes from Middle English 'jowten' (to butt, thrust).
