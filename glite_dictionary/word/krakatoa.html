Krakatoa

1. PROPN

/ ˌkrækəˈtoʊə / (AmE), / ˌkrækəˈtəʊə / (BrE)
a small volcanic island in Indonesia between Java and Sumatra, known for its catastrophic eruption in 1883, one of the most powerful in recorded history
Indonesian volcanic island; site of 1883 eruption

Examples

The eruption of Krakatoa in 1883 caused global climatic effects and massive tsunamis.
Scientists continue to study the geological impact of Krakatoa well over a century after its destruction.
The explosion of Krakatoa was heard thousands of kilometers away.

Etymology

From Indonesian 'Krakatau', the native name of the island.

2. PROPN

/ ˌkrɑːkəˈtoʊə / (AmE), / ˌkrækəˈtəʊə / (BrE)
a volcanic island in Indonesia, situated between Java and Sumatra, famous for its catastrophic eruption in 1883.
Indonesian volcanic island, 1883 eruption

Examples

The eruption of Krakatoa in 1883 produced tsunamis that devastated coastal towns.
Scientists continue to study Krakatoa to understand volcanic eruptions and their global effects.
After Krakatoa exploded, the island was largely destroyed and new landforms appeared.

Etymology

From Indonesian 'Krakatau'.
