tallis

1. NOUN

/ tɑːˈlɪs / (AmE), / ˈtalɪs / (BrE)
A traditional Jewish prayer shawl with fringes (tzitzit) on each corner, worn during morning prayers.
Jewish prayer shawl with fringes

Examples

He wrapped his tallis around his shoulders before starting the morning prayers.
Customarily, a bar mitzvah boy receives his first tallis as part of the ceremony.
Some synagogue members proudly display ornate designs on their tallis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tzitzit - ritually knotted fringes attached to the corners of a tallis

Etymology

From Hebrew tallith (shawl, cloak).
