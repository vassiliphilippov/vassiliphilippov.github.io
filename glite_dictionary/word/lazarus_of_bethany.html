Lazarus of Bethany

1. PROPN

/ ˈlæzərəs əv ˈbɛθəni / (AmE), / ˈlæzərəs əv ˈbɛθəni / (BrE)
In the New Testament, the man whom Jesus resurrected four days after his death, as described in the Gospel of John.
man resurrected by Jesus in the Bible

Examples

The story of Lazarus of Bethany is considered one of the most remarkable miracles in the Gospel of John.
Many artists have depicted the raising of Lazarus of Bethany in their works.
The account of Lazarus of Bethany has inspired discussions about faith and resurrection throughout history.

Etymology

From Latin Lazarus, from Greek Lazaros, from Hebrew Elʿāzār (Eleazar), meaning 'God has helped'; Bethany is the town near Jerusalem.
