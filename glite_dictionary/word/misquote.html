misquote

1. VERB

/ ˌmɪsˈkwoʊt / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈkwəʊt / (BrE)
to repeat or cite someone's words incorrectly; to quote inaccurately
repeat someone's words incorrectly

Examples

The journalist was accused of misquoting the senator during the interview.
Please make sure you do not misquote the instructions in your explanation.
He claimed his words were misquoted by the newspaper.

Synset Examples

He had misquoted the politician

Related Terms

  • Antonym: quote - to repeat someone's exact words
  • Synonym: misreport - to report information inaccurately

Etymology

From prefix mis- (wrongly) + quote, meaning to quote incorrectly.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01025790-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ mɪsˈkwoʊt / (AmE), / mɪsˈkwəʊt / (BrE)
An incorrect or inaccurate quotation; a statement that does not precisely reproduce what was originally said or written.
an incorrect quotation

Examples

The article contains a misquote that changes the meaning of the original statement.
She was frustrated by the misquote attributed to her in the newspaper.
Historians often have to correct a misquote that has been repeated for decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quotation - a group of words taken from a text or speech and repeated by someone other than the original author or speaker

Etymology

From prefix 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'quote'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06625929-n
Synset POS: n
