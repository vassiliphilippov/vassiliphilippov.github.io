fraudulent

1. ADJ

/ ˈfrɔdʒələnt / (AmE), / ˈfrɔːdjʊlənt / (BrE)
intended to deceive or cheat; involving fraud or deception.
intended to deceive or cheat

Examples

The company was found guilty of fraudulent accounting practices that misled investors.
He was arrested for his role in a fraudulent insurance claim.
Customers lost thousands of dollars to a fraudulent online scheme.

Synset Examples

deceitful advertising
fallacious testimony
smooth, shining, and deceitful as thin ice
a fraudulent scheme to escape paying taxes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceitful - dishonest or misleading, intending to deceive
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit or untruthfulness, sincere

Etymology

From Latin 'fraudulentus', from 'fraus' (deceit, injury, fraud).
