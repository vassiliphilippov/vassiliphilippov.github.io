lypressin

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈprɛsɪn / (AmE), / lɪˈprɛsɪn / (BrE)
a synthetic antidiuretic hormone used as a medication, especially to treat diabetes insipidus
synthetic antidiuretic hormone (medication)
medical

Examples

Lypressin is administered as a nasal spray to manage symptoms of diabetes insipidus.
Doctors may prescribe lypressin when the body does not produce enough antidiuretic hormone.
Adverse effects of lypressin can include irritation in the nasal passages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vasopressin - another antidiuretic hormone used in medicine
