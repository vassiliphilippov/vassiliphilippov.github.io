linsey-woolsey

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪnzi ˈwʊlzi / (AmE), / ˈlɪnzi ˈwʊlzi / (BrE)
A coarse fabric with a linen warp and a wool or cotton weft, formerly used for clothing and furnishings.
coarse fabric of linen and wool or cotton

Examples

The pioneer family used linsey-woolsey to make warm clothing for the winter.
She admired the rough texture of the old linsey-woolsey quilt displayed at the museum.
Colonial homes often featured blankets woven from linsey-woolsey for their durability.

Etymology

From 'linsey' (from linen) and 'woolsey' (from wool), denoting its composition.
