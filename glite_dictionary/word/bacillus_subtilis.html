Bacillus subtilis

1. NOUN

/ bəˈsɪləs ˈsʌtɪlɪs / (AmE), / bəˈsɪləs ˈsʌtɪlɪs / (BrE)
A rod-shaped, gram-positive bacterium commonly found in soil and decaying organic matter; some strains produce antibiotics and are used in research and industry.
species of soil-dwelling bacterium
medical

Examples

Bacillus subtilis plays a critical role in decomposing organic material in the soil.
Researchers often use Bacillus subtilis as a model organism in laboratory studies.
Certain strains of Bacillus subtilis are used to produce antibiotics for medical applications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacillus - rod-shaped bacterium

Etymology

From Latin; 'bacillus' means 'little rod', and 'subtilis' means 'fine' or 'thin'.
