to orate

1. VERB

/ ˈɔːˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈɔːˌreɪt / (BrE)
to speak formally, often in a pompous or showy manner, especially in public
speak formally or pompously

Examples

The politician loves to orate at every public gathering, regardless of the occasion.
She began to orate passionately about the importance of civic responsibility.
Rather than participate in the discussion, he chose to orate for twenty minutes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lecture - to deliver an educational or instructive talk to an audience
  • Synonym: pontificate - to express one's opinions in a pompous or dogmatic way

Etymology

From Latin oratus, past participle of orare (to speak, plead).
