affinity

1. NOUN

/ əˈfɪnəti / (AmE), / əˈfɪnɪti / (BrE)
(immunology) The degree of attraction between an antigen and an antibody, determining the strength of their binding.
immunology: attraction between antigen and antibody
medical

Examples

The affinity of the antibody for its antigen is a key factor in immune response.
Researchers measured the affinity between the therapeutic antibody and various viral proteins.
Different antibodies can exhibit varying degrees of affinity for the same antigen.

Etymology

From Latin affinitas (relationship, connection).
