ping pong

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪŋ ˌpɑŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɪŋ ˌpɒŋ / (BrE)
a game for two or four players in which a lightweight ball is hit back and forth with small paddles across a table divided by a net
table game with paddles and a small ball

Examples

They set up the table in the garage and challenged each other to a game of ping pong.
After dinner, she invited her friends to play ping pong in the basement.
He bought a new paddle for ping pong and practiced his serve every afternoon.

Synset Examples

They played ping pong in the recreation room.
The kids enjoyed playing ping pong after school.
We had a friendly ping pong match last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: table tennis - a game similar to ping pong, also the official sport's name

Etymology

Trademark from the name 'Ping-Pong', registered in 1901, imitating the sound of the ball.
