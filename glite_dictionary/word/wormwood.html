wormwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɜrmˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈwɜːmwʊd / (BrE)
any of several aromatic, chiefly bitter plants of the genus Artemisia, especially Artemisia absinthium, often used in medicine or as a flavoring
a bitter aromatic plant of the genus Artemisia

Examples

Wormwood is commonly used as a flavoring in the production of absinthe and other liqueurs.
Some herbalists recommend wormwood tea to aid digestion, but its bitter taste is unmistakable.
In many gardens, wormwood is grown both for its silvery foliage and its ability to repel insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absinthe - a green, bitter, aromatic spirit flavored with wormwood and other herbs

Etymology

Old English *wermōd*, of Germanic origin; related to German *Wermut*.
