to nourish

1. VERB

/ ˈnɜːrɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈnʌrɪʃ / (BrE)
to provide with the food or substances necessary for growth, health, and good condition
supply with what is needed for growth or health

Examples

A balanced diet helps to nourish the body and maintain energy levels.
The mother eagle will nourish her chicks until they can fly.
A good book can nourish the mind as well as the soul.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feed - to give food to
  • Antonym: to starve - to deprive of food

Etymology

From Old French *nouriss-, stem of *nourir* (to nourish), from Latin *nutrire* (to feed, nurse).

2. VERB

/ ˈnɜrɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈnʌrɪʃ / (BrE)
to provide with the food or other substances necessary for growth, health, and good condition
provide with food or sustenance

Examples

A balanced diet helps to nourish the body and keep you healthy.
The mother panda will nourish her cub with milk during its first months.
Plants nourish themselves through sunlight, water, and nutrients from the soil.

Synset Examples

We sustained ourselves on bread and water
This kind of food is not nourishing for young children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feed - to give food to (a person, animal, or plant)

Etymology

From Old French *nourir* (to feed, nourish), from Latin *nutrire* (to feed, nurse).
