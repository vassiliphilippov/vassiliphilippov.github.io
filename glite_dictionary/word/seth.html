Seth

1. PROPN

/ sɛθ / (AmE), / sɛθ / (BrE)
(in the Bible) the third son of Adam and Eve, given by God in place of Abel.
third son of Adam and Eve in the Bible

Examples

According to Genesis, Seth was born after the death of Abel.
Genealogies in the Bible often trace human ancestry through Seth.
Many biblical scholars study the descendants of Seth to understand early human history.

Etymology

From Hebrew *Šēt* (Seth).

2. PROPN

/ sɛθ / (AmE), / sɛθ / (BrE)
an evil god in ancient Egyptian mythology, often depicted with the head of a beast, known as the brother and murderer of Osiris
evil Egyptian god, brother of Osiris

Examples

In Egyptian mythology, Seth was the god responsible for chaos and violence.
The temple was dedicated to both Osiris and his adversary, Seth.
Legends describe how Seth betrayed and murdered his brother Osiris.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Osiris - Egyptian god of the afterlife, brother of Seth
  • Synonym: Set - Alternate name for Seth, Egyptian god of chaos

Etymology

From Ancient Egyptian Sutekh or Setekh, the name of the deity.
