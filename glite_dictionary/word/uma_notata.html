Uma notata

1. PROPN

/ ˈumə noʊˈtɑːtə / (AmE), / ˈuːmə nəʊˈtɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of lizard found in desert regions of the United States and Mexico, characterized by long pointed scales around its toes.
lizard species of US and Mexico deserts

Examples

The Uma notata thrives in the sandy deserts of Southern California.
Researchers studied the adaptation mechanisms of Uma notata to survive in arid environments.
Unlike other lizards, Uma notata has long pointed scales that help it move across loose sand.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Uma' is the genus name, 'notata' means 'marked' or 'spotted'.
