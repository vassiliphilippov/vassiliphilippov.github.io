to hie

1. VERB

/ haɪ / (AmE), / haɪ / (BrE)
to go quickly or hasten, often used in literary or archaic contexts
go quickly; hasten (archaic/literary)
archaic

Examples

She bade him hie to the village and fetch help at once.
They must hie away before nightfall to avoid the storm.
We hied through the fields as the sun was setting.

Synset Examples

He rushed down the hall to receive his guests
The cars raced down the street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hasten - to move or act quickly
  • Antonym: to linger - to be slow in leaving; to delay

Etymology

From Old English hȳgan (to strive, hasten).
