witty

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɪti / (AmE), / ˈwɪti / (BrE)
showing quick and inventive verbal humor; combining clever ideas with amusing or facetious expression
clever and amusing in expression

Examples

Everyone laughed at the witty remarks she made during the meeting.
His witty responses made him a favorite guest on talk shows.
She admires writers who are both insightful and witty in their observations.

Synset Examples

his sermons were unpredictably witty and satirical as well as eloquent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clever - quick to understand and devise or apply ideas; intelligent
  • Synonym: humorous - funny or making you laugh
  • Antonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement; not clever or witty

Etymology

From wit (mental sharpness) + -y (adjective suffix); first attested in Middle English.
