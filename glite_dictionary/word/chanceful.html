chanceful

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃænsfəl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːnsfəl / (BrE)
involving uncertainty or risk; having an unpredictable outcome
having an uncertain or risky outcome

Examples

Starting a new business is always a chanceful endeavor, requiring courage and preparation.
They faced a chanceful journey across the mountains in winter.
His chanceful decision to invest in the startup paid off in the end.

Synset Examples

an extremely dicey future on a brave new world of liquid nitrogen, tar, and smog

Related Terms

  • Synonym: risky - involving risk or danger
  • Antonym: safe - not involving risk or danger

Etymology

Formed from 'chance' + '-ful', modeled like other adjectives such as 'hopeful'.
