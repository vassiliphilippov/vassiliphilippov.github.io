Myxinoidea

1. NOUN

/ mɪkˌsɪniˈɔɪdiə / (AmE), / mɪkˌsɪniˈɔɪdɪə / (BrE)
A taxonomic group comprising the hagfishes, distinguished from lampreys; a class of jawless fish.
class of jawless fish (hagfishes)

Examples

The class Myxinoidea includes all known species of hagfishes.
Researchers study Myxinoidea to understand the evolution of vertebrates.
Unlike lampreys, members of Myxinoidea produce large quantities of slime as a defense mechanism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hagfish - jawless, eel-like fish of the family Myxinidae
  • Antonym: lamprey - jawless, eel-like parasitic or non-parasitic fish

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek myxa (slime) and -eidos (form).
