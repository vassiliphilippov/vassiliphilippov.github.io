Nizhny Novgorod

1. PROPN

/ ˈniʒni nɒˈvɡɔrəd / (AmE), / ˈnɪʒni nɒvˈɡɒrəd / (BrE)
An industrial city in the European part of Russia, located at the confluence of the Volga and Oka rivers; noted for its historical and cultural significance.
major city in western Russia

Examples

Many tourists visit Nizhny Novgorod to see its historic Kremlin and vibrant arts scene.
After moving to Nizhny Novgorod, she found a job in the automotive industry.
The two rivers meet in Nizhny Novgorod, creating a picturesque view of the city.

Etymology

From Russian Нижний Новгород (literally 'Lower Newtown').
