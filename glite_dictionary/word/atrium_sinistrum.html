atrium sinistrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.tri.əm sɪˈnɪs.trəm / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.tri.əm sɪˈnɪs.trəm / (BrE)
the left upper chamber of the heart that receives oxygenated blood from the pulmonary veins
left upper heart chamber (left atrium)
medical

Examples

The surgeon inspected the atrium sinistrum for signs of abnormal tissue.
Oxygenated blood enters the atrium sinistrum via the pulmonary veins.
A defect in the atrium sinistrum can lead to inefficient blood circulation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: left atrium - the left upper chamber of the heart that receives blood from the lungs
  • Antonym: atrium dextrum - the right upper chamber of the heart

Etymology

From Latin 'atrium' (entrance hall) and 'sinistrum' (left).
