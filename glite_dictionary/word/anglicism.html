Anglicism

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋɡlɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡlɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
a custom, practice, or characteristic typical of England or its people
custom typical of England

Examples

Many American tourists struggled to understand certain Anglicisms during their visit to rural England.
The celebration of afternoon tea is an Anglicism that has spread to other parts of the world.
Some European countries have adopted Anglicisms in their sporting traditions.

Etymology

From French 'anglicisme', from Medieval Latin 'Anglicismus', meaning English way or characteristic.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæŋɡlɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈaŋɡlɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
A word, phrase, or idiom characteristic of English, especially British English, or borrowed from English into another language.
expression peculiar to British English

Examples

The word 'lorry' is an Anglicism not commonly used in American English.
Many French speakers use the Anglicism 'weekend' instead of their native term.
Some Anglicisms can be confusing for non-British English learners.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: americanism - an expression characteristic of American English
  • Synonym: Gallicism - an expression borrowed from French

Etymology

From French 'anglicisme', derived from 'anglais' (English) and '-isme' (-ism).
