Eriophorum angustifolium

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛriˌɑːˈfɔrəm ˌæŋɡjəstəˈfoʊliəm / (AmE), / ˌɛrɪˌɒfəˈrʌm ˌæŋɡjʊstəˈfəʊliəm / (BrE)
A species of cottongrass with densely tufted white cottony or downy flower heads, commonly found in boggy or wet acidic ground.
a species of cottongrass with cottony flower heads

Examples

The wetlands were dotted with Eriophorum angustifolium, their white tufts swaying in the breeze.
Botanists identified the plant as Eriophorum angustifolium during their survey of the bog.
The distinctive cottony heads of Eriophorum angustifolium make it easily recognizable in marshy landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cottongrass - a common name for plants of the genus Eriophorum

Etymology

From Greek 'erion' (wool) + 'phoros' (bearing) and Latin 'angustifolium' (narrow-leaved).
