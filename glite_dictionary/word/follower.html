follower

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːloʊər / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊə / (BrE)
a person who supports and accepts the guidance or leadership of someone else
person who accepts leadership or guidance

Examples

He was a loyal follower of the king, always ready to serve.
Many young people became devoted followers of the famous musician.
The political candidate has thousands of followers on social media.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: leader - a person who leads or commands a group
  • Synonym: supporter - a person who supports a cause, organization, or person

Etymology

From Old English 'folgere' (one who follows), from 'folgian' (to follow).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɑləwɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɒləwə / (BrE)
a person who moves behind or pursues someone else
person who moves behind or pursues

Examples

The detective noticed a follower lurking in the shadows behind her.
Hikers should never lose sight of the group leader and not stray as a follower.
The celebrity was often frustrated by the relentless follower who tracked her every move.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: leader - the person who leads or is at the front
  • Synonym: pursuer - someone who chases or follows another

Etymology

From Old French 'foloeur', from 'foler' (to follow).
