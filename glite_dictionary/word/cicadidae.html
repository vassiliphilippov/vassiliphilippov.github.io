Cicadidae

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈkædəˌdiː / (AmE), / sɪˈkædəˌdiː / (BrE)
The family of large insects known as cicadas, characterized by transparent wings and loud, distinctive mating calls, found in warm climates worldwide.
the family of cicadas (insects)

Examples

The family Cicadidae includes over 3,000 species of cicadas found in temperate and tropical regions.
Scientists are studying the life cycle of several Cicadidae species emerging after seventeen years underground.
Many members of the Cicadidae family are known for their periodic, synchronized appearances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cicada - a large tree-dwelling insect of the family Cicadidae

Etymology

From New Latin Cicadidae, from Cicada (the type genus) + -idae (a family suffix in zoological taxonomy).
