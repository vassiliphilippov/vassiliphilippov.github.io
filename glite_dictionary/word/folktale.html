folktale

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊkˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkˌteɪl / (BrE)
A traditional story passed down orally among the common people, often reflecting cultural beliefs or moral lessons.
traditional story passed down orally

Examples

My grandmother used to tell us a folktale every night before bed.
Many cultures have a folktale explaining how the world was created.
The class studied a Japanese folktale about a magical crane.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legend - a traditional story sometimes popularly regarded as historical but unauthenticated
  • Synonym: myth - a traditional story explaining natural or social phenomena, typically involving supernatural beings

Etymology

From 'folk' (people) + 'tale', indicating a story of the people.
