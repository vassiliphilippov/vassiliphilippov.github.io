audibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːdəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌɔːdəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being able to be heard or perceived by the ear
quality of being heard

Examples

The audibility of his voice was diminished by the background noise.
Engineers tested the audibility of the warning alarm under different conditions.
Improving microphone audibility is crucial during crowded conference calls.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inaudibility - the quality of being impossible to hear
  • Synonym: audible - able to be heard

Etymology

From Latin 'audibilis' (that can be heard) + '-ity' (forming nouns denoting quality or condition).
