to literalise

1. VERB

/ ˈlɪtəˌrɑːlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈlɪtərəlaɪz / (BrE)
To interpret or express something in a literal or strict sense; to make something literal rather than figurative.
make or interpret as literal
british

Examples

Some teachers tend to literalise metaphors in their explanations.
It is important not to literalise ancient myths when interpreting them today.
The director chose to literalise the stage directions for comedic effect.

Synset Examples

literalize metaphors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to literalize - To make literal; American English spelling

Etymology

From 'literal' + '-ise', after the pattern of similar English verbs. (Also spelled 'literalize' in American English.)
