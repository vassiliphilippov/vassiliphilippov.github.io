Tribonemaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌtraɪbəˌnɛməˈsiːiː / (AmE), / ˌtrɪbənɪˌmæsiːˈiː / (BrE)
A family of simple filamentous freshwater yellow-green algae belonging to the order Tribonematales.
family of yellow-green freshwater algae

Examples

The Tribonemaceae are commonly found in freshwater ponds and streams.
Scientists studied the cell structure of Tribonemaceae under the microscope.
Members of Tribonemaceae play a role in aquatic ecosystems by producing oxygen.

Etymology

From the genus name Tribonema, with the standard botanical family suffix -aceae.
