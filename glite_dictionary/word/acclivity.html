acclivity

1. NOUN

/ əˈklɪvəti / (AmE), / əˈklɪvɪti / (BrE)
An upward slope or incline, especially in a road or terrain.
upward slope or incline

Examples

The hikers paused at the base of the acclivity before continuing their ascent.
A steep acclivity made the driveway difficult to shovel in winter.
The road wound up a gentle acclivity into the rolling hills.

Synset Examples

the car couldn't make it up the rise

Related Terms

  • Antonym: declivity - a downward slope or decline
  • Synonym: slope - a surface that has one end or side higher than the other

Etymology

From Latin acclivitas, from acclivis (sloping upward).
