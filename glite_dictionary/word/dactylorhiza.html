Dactylorhiza

1. NOUN

/ ˌdæk.tɪ.lɔˈraɪ.zə / (AmE), / ˌdæk.tɪ.ləˈraɪ.zə / (BrE)
A genus of terrestrial orchids native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, known for their spurred flowers and tuberous roots.
genus of terrestrial orchids

Examples

Several species within the genus Dactylorhiza are prized by gardeners for their vibrant flowers.
The marsh orchid, a member of Dactylorhiza, thrives in damp meadows and marshes.
Researchers have documented the habitat preferences of Dactylorhiza across northern Europe.

Etymology

From Greek dáktylos (finger) + rhiza (root), referring to the finger-like roots.
