lawcourt

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔːˌkɔrt / (AmE), / ˈlɔːˌkɔːt / (BrE)
A formal place where legal cases are heard and decided by a judge or magistrate.
legal tribunal where cases are heard

Examples

The defendant appeared before the lawcourt to answer the charges.
Many historic buildings in the city center were once lawcourts or administrative offices.
A new lawcourt was constructed to manage the increasing number of cases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: court - A tribunal where legal cases are heard and judgments are made
  • Synonym: tribunal - A body established to settle certain types of disputes

Etymology

Formed from 'law' + 'court', indicating a court that administers the law.
