private

1. ADJ

/ ˈpraɪ.vət / (AmE), / ˈpraɪ.vət / (BrE)
concerning matters that are deeply personal and not intended to be shared with others.
personal, not to be shared

Examples

She was careful to keep her private life out of the news.
This diary contains many private thoughts and feelings.
Please respect that these are private family issues and not for public discussion.

Synset Examples

intimate correspondence
private family matters

Related Terms

  • Antonym: public - open or accessible to all people; not private
  • Synonym: personal - relating to one's private life, thoughts, or feelings

Etymology

From Latin 'privatus' (set apart, personal), from 'privare' (to separate, deprive).

WordNet ID: oewn-01773890-s

2. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪvət / (AmE), / ˈpraɪvət / (BrE)
a soldier of the lowest enlisted rank in the Army or Marines
lowest military rank (soldier)

Examples

The young private stood at attention during roll call.
After training, she was promoted from private to corporal.
As a private, he followed his sergeant's orders closely.

Synset Examples

our prisoner was just a private and knew nothing of value

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sergeant - a noncommissioned officer rank above private
  • Degree Lower: recruit - a newly enlisted soldier, sometimes used as a lower rank

Etymology

From Latin 'privatus' (set apart, personal; not in public office).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10496052-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈpraɪ.vət / (AmE), / ˈpraɪ.vət / (BrE)
Not shared or expressed with others; kept to oneself.
kept to oneself; not expressed

Examples

She kept her private thoughts to herself and never shared them with anyone.
He prefers to keep his feelings private, rarely talking about his emotions.
Despite being famous, she insisted on having a private life away from the media.

Synset Examples

secret (or private) thoughts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secret - kept hidden or unknown from others
  • Antonym: public - openly shared or expressed; not private

Etymology

From Latin privatus (withdrawn from public life, peculiar to oneself), from privare (to separate).

5. ADJ

/ ˈpraɪ.vət / (AmE), / ˈpraɪ.vət / (BrE)
Belonging to or for the use of one particular person or group only; not shared with others.
for or belonging to one person only

Examples

Each guest has a private bathroom attached to their room.
He kept his private documents locked in a secure drawer.
We enjoyed a private dinner away from the crowded restaurant.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: public - relating to or concerning all people collectively
  • Synonym: personal - belonging to or affecting a particular person

Etymology

From Latin 'privatus', meaning 'set apart, belonging to oneself'.
