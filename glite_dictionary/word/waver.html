waver

1. VERB

/ ˈweɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˈweɪvə / (BrE)
to pause or hold back in uncertainty or unwillingness; to be indecisive or hesitant
to pause in uncertainty or be indecisive

Examples

She began to waver when faced with such a difficult decision.
His confidence seemed to waver as he listened to the arguments.
The committee may waver on its final recommendation until more evidence is presented.

Synset Examples

Authorities hesitate to quote exact figures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hesitate - to pause before saying or doing something, often due to uncertainty
  • Antonym: to decide - make a choice or come to a conclusion

Etymology

From Middle English waveren, meaning 'to fluctuate or be moved to and fro.'
