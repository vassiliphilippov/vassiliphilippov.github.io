reharmonisation

1. NOUN

/ riˌhɑrməˌnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / riːˌhɑːmənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or result of altering the original harmony of a piece of music, often to create a new harmonic effect
altering a song's original harmony

Examples

The jazz pianist performed a brilliant reharmonisation of the classic tune, surprising the audience with unexpected chords.
Many contemporary covers of older songs use reharmonisation to give familiar melodies a fresh sound.
The reharmonisation of that piece maintained its original melody but completely changed its emotional impact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrangement - the adaptation of a musical composition

Etymology

From re- (again) + harmonisation, ultimately from Greek harmonia (harmony).
