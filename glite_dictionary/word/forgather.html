to forgather

1. VERB

/ fərˈɡæðər / (AmE), / fəˈɡæðə / (BrE)
to come together or assemble in one place; to gather
to assemble or gather together
archaic

Examples

Every year, scholars from around the world forgather to share their research in this ancient hall.
The villagers would forgather in the square to celebrate the harvest festival.
After the ceremony, friends and family forgathered in the garden to enjoy refreshments.

Synset Examples

We assembled in the church basement
Let's gather in the dining room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gather - to come together; to assemble
  • Antonym: to disperse - to scatter or move apart in different directions

Etymology

Variant of 'foregather', influenced by 'gather'.
