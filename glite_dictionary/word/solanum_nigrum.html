Solanum nigrum

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈleɪnəm ˈnaɪɡrəm / (AmE), / sɒˈleɪnəm ˈnɪɡrəm / (BrE)
A species of Eurasian herbaceous plant, commonly known as black nightshade, with white flowers and black berries that can be poisonous or edible.
a species of herbaceous plant, black nightshade

Examples

Solanum nigrum can be found growing in fields and gardens across Europe and Asia.
Certain edible varieties of Solanum nigrum are used in traditional dishes after careful preparation.
The berries of Solanum nigrum may be toxic if consumed without proper identification.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black nightshade - common name for Solanum nigrum

Etymology

From Latin: 'Solanum' (the plant's genus) and 'nigrum' (meaning 'black').
