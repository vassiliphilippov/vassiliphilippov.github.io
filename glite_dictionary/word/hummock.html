hummock

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌmək / (AmE), / ˈhʌmək / (BrE)
a small, rounded natural hill or mound, especially one rising above a low-lying area
small natural hill or mound

Examples

A grassy hummock rose above the surrounding marshland, providing a perfect spot to rest.
The sheep grazed on the hummocks scattered across the field.
Each hummock was dotted with wildflowers by late spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hillock - a small hill
  • Degree Higher: hill - a naturally raised area of land, not as high or craggy as a mountain

Etymology

Perhaps from Dutch *hommel* (a small mound) or related to Middle Low German *hummock* (hillock).
