white-livered

1. ADJ

/ ˈwaɪt ˌlɪvɚd / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt ˌlɪvəd / (BrE)
Very cowardly or easily frightened; lacking courage.
very cowardly; easily frightened
archaic

Examples

The general dismissed him as white-livered and unfit for command.
She called her opponent white-livered for refusing to debate the issue openly.
Literature often depicts villains as white-livered cowards who flee at the first hint of danger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowardly - lacking courage; easily frightened
  • Antonym: brave - possessing or displaying courage; able to face danger or pain

Etymology

From the belief that a pale liver indicated lack of courage, as the liver was once thought to be the seat of bravery.
