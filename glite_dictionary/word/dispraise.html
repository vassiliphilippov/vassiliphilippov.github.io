dispraise

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈpreɪz / (AmE), / dɪsˈpreɪz / (BrE)
the act of expressing disapproval or speaking contemptuously of someone or something
act of expressing disapproval

Examples

The harsh dispraise she received hurt more than she expected.
Instead of encouragement, the artist met only dispraise from the critics.
His constant dispraise of modern music annoyed his friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criticism - the expression of disapproval based on perceived faults or mistakes
  • Antonym: praise - the expression of approval or admiration

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French 'despreisier', from Latin 'de-' (down) + 'pretium' (price, value).
