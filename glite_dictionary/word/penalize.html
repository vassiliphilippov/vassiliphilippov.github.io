to penalize

1. VERB

/ ˈpiːnəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpiːnəlaɪz / (BrE)
to punish someone or treat them unfairly for breaking a rule or law, especially by making them pay a penalty or suffer a disadvantage
impose a penalty or disadvantage

Examples

The referee decided to penalize the player for an illegal tackle.
Employees may be penalized for consistently arriving late to work.
If you miss the application deadline, the university will penalize you by deducting points.

Synset Examples

The students were penalized for showing up late for class
we had to punish the dog for soiling the floor again

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to punish - to cause someone to suffer because they have done something wrong
  • Antonym: reward - to give something positive to someone for good behavior or achievement

Etymology

From French *pénaliser*, from *pénal* (penal).
