short

1. NOUN

/ ʃɔrt / (AmE), / ʃɔːt / (BrE)
A failure in an electrical circuit caused by accidental contact between two points at different voltages, resulting in a short circuit.
an electrical fault (short circuit)

Examples

The electrician discovered a short in the wiring that needed urgent repair.
A sudden short caused the lights to flicker and the fuse to blow.
They traced the malfunction to a short behind the wall socket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: short circuit - an electrical circuit that allows current to travel along an unintended path
