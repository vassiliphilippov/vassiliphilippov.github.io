Leontopodium

1. NOUN

/ liˌɑːntəˈpoʊdiəm / (AmE), / liˌɒntəˈpəʊdiəm / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the daisy family, which includes the edelweiss and related species, native to Asia and Europe.
genus of plants including edelweiss

Examples

The Leontopodium genus is famous for the iconic alpine flower known as edelweiss.
Several species in the Leontopodium genus are adapted to high-altitude environments.
Botanists collected specimens of Leontopodium during their expedition in the mountains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edelweiss - a well-known species within the Leontopodium genus, with white, woolly flowers

Etymology

From Greek 'leon' (lion) + 'pous' (foot), referring to the shape of the plant's inflorescence.
