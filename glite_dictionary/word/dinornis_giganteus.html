Dinornis giganteus

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈnɔrnɪs dʒaɪˌgænˈtiːəs / (AmE), / daɪˈnɔːnɪs dʒɪˌgænˈtiːəs / (BrE)
an extinct species of giant moa in New Zealand, believed to have been the largest, reaching up to 12 feet in height
largest extinct species of moa

Examples

The Dinornis giganteus likely stood taller than any other bird that ever lived.
Scientists study Dinornis giganteus fossils to understand prehistoric New Zealand fauna.
The bones of Dinornis giganteus have been discovered in several archaeological sites across New Zealand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moa - any of several extinct flightless birds from New Zealand

Etymology

From Greek 'deinos' (terrible) + 'ornis' (bird) and Latin 'giganteus' (gigantic).
