Dicentra cucullaria

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈsɛntrə ˌkjuːkʊˈlɛriə / (AmE), / daɪˈsɛntrə ˌkjuːkʊˈlɛːrɪə / (BrE)
a delicate spring-flowering plant native to eastern North America, recognized for its white, double-spurred flowers and finely divided leaves
spring-flowering North American plant with white, spurred flowers

Examples

In early spring, Dicentra cucullaria brightens forest floors with its delicate white flowers.
Gardeners seek out Dicentra cucullaria for its unique double-spurred blooms and attractive foliage.
Some hikers recognize Dicentra cucullaria by its lacy leaves and pants-shaped flowers growing in woodlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dutchman's breeches - common name for Dicentra cucullaria, referring to the flower's pant-like shape

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Dicentra' from Greek 'di-' (two) + 'kentron' (spur); 'cucullaria' from Latin 'cucullus' (hood or cowl), referring to the shape of the flower.
