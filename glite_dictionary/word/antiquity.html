antiquity

1. NOUN

/ ænˈtɪkwəti / (AmE), / ænˈtɪkwɪti / (BrE)
the period of history before the Middle Ages, especially in Europe, typically referring to ancient Greece and Rome
the period before the Middle Ages in Europe

Examples

Scholars have extensively studied the art and philosophy of antiquity.
During antiquity, civilizations like Rome and Greece laid the foundations for modern Europe.
Many of the monuments from antiquity still captivate visitors today.

Etymology

From Latin 'antiquitas' (ancient times, antiquity), from 'antiquus' (ancient, old).

2. NOUN

/ ænˈtɪkwəti / (AmE), / ænˈtɪkwəti / (BrE)
the quality of being very old; great age
extreme oldness; great age

Examples

The antiquity of the cathedral attracts historians from around the world.
Many artifacts of great antiquity have been preserved in the museum.
Scholars debate the antiquity of the manuscript found in the cave.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: modernity - the quality or condition of being modern or current
  • Synonym: age - length of existence; duration of life or existence

Etymology

From Latin antiquitas (ancient times, old age), from antiquus (ancient).

3. NOUN

/ ænˈtɪkwəti / (AmE), / ænˈtɪkwəti / (BrE)
an object or artifact from ancient times, especially one of historical or archaeological interest
object from ancient times

Examples

The museum has a remarkable collection of antiquity from the Roman Empire.
A rare coin found at the site is believed to be an antiquity of great value.
Many antiquity are displayed in galleries to educate the public about past civilizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relic - an object surviving from an earlier time, especially one of historical value
  • Antonym: modernity - the quality or condition of being modern

Etymology

From Latin 'antiquitas' (ancientness, old age), from 'antiquus' (ancient).
