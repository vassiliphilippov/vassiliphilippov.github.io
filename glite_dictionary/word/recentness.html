recentness

1. NOUN

/ ˈriː.sənt.nəs / (AmE), / ˈriː.sənt.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having happened or appeared not long ago
state of being recent

Examples

The recentness of the news made it especially shocking to the public.
She questioned the recentness of the information before making her decision.
Historians often debate the recentness of certain archaeological discoveries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recency - the state of being recent
  • Antonym: antiquity - the quality of being ancient

Etymology

From 'recent' (from Latin recens) + suffix '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrisəntnəs / (AmE), / ˈriːsntnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having occurred or existed only a short time ago
quality of being recent

Examples

The recentness of the discovery made it a hot topic among scientists.
We should consider the recentness of this data when analyzing the results.
Her excitement about the trip was influenced by the recentness of her return.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: antiquity - the ancient past, especially before the Middle Ages
  • Antonym: oldness - the quality of being old or aged

Etymology

From 'recent' + '-ness', with 'recent' from Latin 'recentem' (fresh, new).
