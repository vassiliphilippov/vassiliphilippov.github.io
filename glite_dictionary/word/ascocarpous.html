ascocarpous

1. ADJ

/ ˌæs.kəˈkɑr.pəs / (AmE), / ˌæs.kəˈkɑː.pəs / (BrE)
pertaining to or having ascocarps, the spore-producing structures of certain fungi
relating to ascocarps in fungi
medical

Examples

The fungus is ascocarpous, producing visible fruiting bodies during reproduction.
Scientists studied the ascocarpous species to better understand fungal development.
Because it is ascocarpous, this mold can be identified by its spore-producing structures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ascocarp - the fruiting body of ascomycete fungi, bearing spores

Etymology

From New Latin 'ascocarpus', from Greek 'askos' (bag) + 'karpos' (fruit), referring to the structure in fungi.
