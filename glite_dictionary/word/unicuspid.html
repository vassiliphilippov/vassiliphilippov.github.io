unicuspid

1. ADJ

/ ˌjuːnɪˈkʌspɪd / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪˈkʌspɪd / (BrE)
having a single cusp or point, especially referring to a tooth
having one cusp or point (biology, dentistry)
medical

Examples

The fossilized jawbone revealed a unicuspid tooth, helping scientists classify the species.
Some fish species have unicuspid teeth used to grasp slippery prey.
A dentist noted the presence of a rare unicuspid premolar in the patient's mouth.

Synset Examples

a unicuspid tooth

Related Terms

  • Antonym: multicuspid - having more than one cusp (especially a tooth)

Etymology

From Latin 'uni-' (one) + 'cuspis' (point, cusp).
