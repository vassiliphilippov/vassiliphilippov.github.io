teleostan

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛliˈɑːstən / (AmE), / ˌtɛliˈɒstən / (BrE)
a bony fish belonging to the subclass Teleostei, which includes most modern fish species
a bony fish of the subclass Teleostei

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species of teleostan in the river ecosystem.
The majority of freshwater fish are classified as teleostans.
A typical teleostan has a movable jaw and a homocercal tail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teleost - a bony fish of the subclass Teleostei

Etymology

From New Latin Teleostei, the subclass name, from Greek teleos (complete) and osteon (bone).
