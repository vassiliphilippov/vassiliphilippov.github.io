backflowing

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌfloʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbækˌfləʊɪŋ / (BrE)
the movement of a fluid or gas in the reverse direction, returning toward its source
reverse flow toward source

Examples

Improper installation can cause backflowing of contaminated water into the supply line.
The engineer observed backflowing in the pipe due to a sudden pressure drop.
To prevent backflowing, a valve was installed at the exit point of the system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backflow - the movement of liquid or gas backward, especially in plumbing or engineering
