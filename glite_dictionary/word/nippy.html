nippy

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɪpi / (AmE), / ˈnɪpi / (BrE)
(of food or drink) having a sharp or biting taste; tangy or pungent.
having a sharp or biting taste

Examples

This nippy cheese adds a bold flavor to the sandwich.
The sauce was too nippy for my taste, overpowering the other ingredients.
She prefers mild cheeses over nippy varieties that sting the palate.

Synset Examples

a nippy cheese

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tangy - having a strong, piquant flavor or smell
  • Antonym: bland - lacking strong taste or character

2. ADJ

/ ˈnɪpi / (AmE), / ˈnɪpi / (BrE)
(of weather) cold and brisk in a way that is stimulating and refreshing.
briskly cold or invigorating (weather)

Examples

It's a bit nippy outside, so you'll want to wear a jacket.
We went for a walk on a nippy autumn morning.
The air grew nippy as the sun set behind the trees.

Synset Examples

crisp clear nights and frosty mornings
a nipping wind
a nippy fall day
snappy weather

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crisp - (of weather) cold, dry, and invigorating
  • Degree Higher: cold - having a low temperature, especially when compared to the temperature of the human body
  • Degree Lower: mild - (of weather) not cold, moderate in temperature
