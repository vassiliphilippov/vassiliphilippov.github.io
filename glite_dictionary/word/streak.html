streak

1. NOUN

/ striːk / (AmE), / striːk / (BrE)
a narrow line or band of a different color or texture from the surrounding surface
narrow line or band of different color

Examples

There was a white streak running through her dark hair.
Lightning left a bright streak across the sky.
The stone had a reddish streak that made it unique.

Synset Examples

a green toad with small black stripes or bars
may the Stars and Stripes forever wave

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stripe - a long narrow band or strip, typically one of several, that differ in color or texture from the surrounding surface

Etymology

From Middle English streke, related to Old English strīcan (to go, move, stroke)
