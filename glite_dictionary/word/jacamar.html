jacamar

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒækəˌmɑr / (AmE), / ˈdʒakəˌmɑː / (BrE)
A tropical American bird with a long sharp bill and iridescent green or bronze plumage, noted for catching insects.
tropical bird with long bill

Examples

The vibrant feathers of the jacamar glistened in the sunlight as it perched on a tree branch.
A jacamar can often be seen darting through the air to catch flying insects.
Bird watchers in South America eagerly search for the elusive jacamar in tropical forests.

Etymology

From Portuguese *jacamar*, from Tupi *ya'kãma* (a bird).
