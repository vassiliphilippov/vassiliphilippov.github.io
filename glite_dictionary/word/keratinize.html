to keratinize

1. VERB

/ kəˈræt̬.ə.naɪz / (AmE), / kəˈræt.ɪ.naɪz / (BrE)
(of cells or tissue) to become impregnated with keratin and harden, forming a horny layer.
to become impregnated with keratin
medical

Examples

As skin cells move toward the surface, they gradually keratinize and die.
Certain conditions may cause tissues inside the mouth to keratinize abnormally.
Feathers, hair, and nails keratinize as they develop, forming tough outer coverings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornify - to become horny or form a horny layer

Etymology

From keratin + -ize, indicating 'to make or become like keratin'.

2. VERB

/ kəˈræt̬.ə.naɪz / (AmE), / kəˈræt.ɪ.naɪz / (BrE)
(of cells or tissue) to become or make to become filled with keratin protein, often resulting in hardening or toughening.
become filled with keratin protein
medical

Examples

Dead skin cells keratinize to form a protective outer layer.
Doctors observed that the tissue had begun to keratinize during healing.
As the wound heals, its surface may gradually keratinize and become tougher.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornify - to become horn-like or hardened (by keratin)

Etymology

From keratin + -ize, after French kératiniser.
