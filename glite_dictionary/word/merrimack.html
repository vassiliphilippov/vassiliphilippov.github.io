Merrimack

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɛrɪˌmæk / (AmE), / ˈmɛrɪˌmæk / (BrE)
a river in the northeastern United States, rising in southern New Hampshire and flowing through Massachusetts into the Atlantic Ocean
a river in New Hampshire and Massachusetts

Examples

The Merrimack River is known for its role in the early American textile industry.
We went kayaking on the Merrimack during our trip to New England.
Pollution in the Merrimack has decreased significantly over the last decade.

Etymology

From the Pennacook word *Monnomoke* or *Merramake*, meaning 'swift water' or 'place of strong current'.
