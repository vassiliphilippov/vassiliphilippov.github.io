t-scope

1. NOUN

/ ti skoʊp / (AmE), / ti skəʊp / (BrE)
a scientific instrument used in psychology to present visual stimuli for very brief periods
psychological instrument for brief visual stimuli

Examples

The researcher used a t-scope to display the image for only 50 milliseconds.
Many classic attention studies relied on the t-scope for accurate timing of stimuli.
A malfunctioning t-scope can affect the results of visual perception experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tachistoscope - an instrument for rapid visual presentation, especially in psychological research

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'tachistoscope', from Greek 'tachistos' (swiftest) + 'skopein' (to look).
