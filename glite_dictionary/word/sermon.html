sermon

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.mən / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.mən / (BrE)
a talk on a religious or moral subject, usually given by a member of the clergy during a church service
religious address or speech in church

Examples

The pastor delivered a thoughtful sermon about forgiveness and compassion.
During the Easter service, the bishop's sermon inspired many in the congregation.
She listened attentively as the sermon addressed the importance of helping others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homily - a short sermon or moralizing lecture

Etymology

From Latin 'sermo' (discourse, speech).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.mən / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.mən / (BrE)
a long or serious talk in which someone criticizes or tries to correct another person's behavior, often in a moralizing way
a moralistic speech or lecture

Examples

Dad gave me a long sermon about the dangers of staying out late.
Nobody wanted to hear another sermon from their boss about hard work.
After hearing her sermon on honesty, I promised to tell the truth.

Synset Examples

your preaching is wasted on him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lecture - a long, serious talk, especially one that criticizes someone's behavior
  • Synonym: scolding - an angry or critical talk given to someone speaking about their behavior

Etymology

From Old French *sermon*, from Latin *sermonem* (discourse, speech).
