Caesaropapism

1. NOUN

/ ˌsiːzəroʊˈpeɪpɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌsiːzərəʊˈpeɪpɪzəm / (BrE)
The doctrine that the state has supreme authority over the church in ecclesiastical matters.
state supremacy over the church

Examples

The Byzantine Empire is often cited as an example of Caesaropapism in history.
Some historians debate whether the Russian Tsars truly established Caesaropapism within their realms.
Critics argue that Caesaropapism undermines the church's independence and spiritual authority.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: theocracy - a system of government in which priests rule in the name of God or a god

Etymology

From Latin 'Caesar' (emperor) + 'papism' (the office of the pope).
