powwow

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊˌwaʊ / (AmE), / ˈpaʊˌwaʊ / (BrE)
a meeting or gathering, especially among Native Americans, often involving discussion, celebration, or ceremony
Native American council or gathering

Examples

The tribal elders called a powwow to discuss important matters affecting the community.
Every summer, people gather at the annual powwow to celebrate their heritage with music and dance.
He was invited to speak at the powwow as a gesture of respect from the tribe.

Etymology

From Narragansett or other Algonquian languages, originally meaning 'shaman' or 'spiritual leader'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊˌwaʊ / (AmE), / ˈpaʊˌwaʊ / (BrE)
an informal meeting or discussion, especially one held to make plans or decisions quickly and privately
informal private meeting or discussion
informal

Examples

Let's have a quick powwow before the presentation to make sure we're all on the same page.
The managers called a powwow to settle the dispute quietly.
After a brief powwow in the hallway, they returned with a decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meeting - an assembly of people for a particular purpose, especially for formal discussion
  • Synonym: conference - a formal meeting for discussion
  • Synonym: discussion - the action or process of talking about something

Etymology

From Narragansett (Algonquian) 'powwow' meaning 'spiritual leader'; adopted in American English for meetings or discussions.

3. VERB

/ ˈpaʊˌwaʊ / (AmE), / ˈpaʊˌwaʊ / (BrE)
to hold a conference or discussion, especially an informal meeting or consultation
have a meeting or discussion

Examples

Let’s powwow tomorrow to finalize the details.
The managers powwowed in the break room after the announcement.
We powwowed all afternoon but didn’t reach a decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conference - a formal meeting for discussion
  • Synonym: meeting - an assembly of people for a particular purpose
  • Synonym: to discuss - to talk about something with someone

Etymology

From Narragansett or Algonquian 'pau wau' (spiritual leader).
