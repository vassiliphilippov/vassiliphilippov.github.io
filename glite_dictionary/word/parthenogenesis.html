parthenogenesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːrθənoʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɑːθənəʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (BrE)
A form of reproduction in which an egg develops into a new individual without being fertilized by a male.
reproduction without male fertilization
medical

Examples

Some species of lizards reproduce through parthenogenesis, requiring no male involvement.
The possibility of parthenogenesis in mammals has attracted significant scientific interest.
Scientists have observed parthenogenesis occurring naturally in certain types of insects and plants.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sexual reproduction - reproduction involving the fusion of male and female gametes

Etymology

From Greek parthenos (virgin) + genesis (origin, creation).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːrθənoʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɑːθənəʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (BrE)
A form of reproduction in which an unfertilized egg develops into a new individual, occurring naturally in some animals and plants.
reproduction from an unfertilized egg
medical

Examples

Some species of lizards can reproduce through parthenogenesis, producing offspring without mating with a male.
Scientists have observed parthenogenesis in captive Komodo dragons at several zoos.
In bees, parthenogenesis often leads to the development of male drones from unfertilized eggs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asexual reproduction - reproduction without the fusion of gametes
  • Antonym: sexual reproduction - reproduction involving the fusion of male and female gametes

Etymology

From Greek parthenos (virgin) + genesis (origin, creation).
