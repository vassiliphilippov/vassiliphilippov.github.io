grandiloquent

1. ADJ

/ ɡrænˈdɪləkwənt / (AmE), / ɡranˈdɪləkwənt / (BrE)
using grand or elaborate language, especially in a way that is pompous or intended to impress
using pompous, extravagant language

Examples

The speaker's grandiloquent style was impressive at first, but soon felt artificial and insincere.
She dismissed his grandiloquent claims as nothing more than empty boasting.
Despite the grandiloquent tone of his letter, the message was surprisingly trivial.

Synset Examples

a grandiloquent and boastful manner
overblown oratory
a pompous speech
pseudo-scientific gobbledygook and pontifical hooey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pompous - affectedly grand, solemn, or self-important in speech or manner
  • Synonym: bombastic - high-sounding but with little meaning; inflated
  • Antonym: modest - not showing excessive pride; humble

Etymology

From Latin 'grandiloquus', from 'grandis' (grand) + 'loqui' (to speak).

2. ADJ

/ ɡrænˈdɪləkwənt / (AmE), / ɡrænˈdɪləkwənt / (BrE)
(of speech or writing) using very formal, impressive, or elaborate words or style, often in order to sound important or grand.
using lofty or pompous language

Examples

The politician's grandiloquent speech was filled with big words but little substance.
He tends to use grandiloquent language even when explaining the simplest concepts.
Critics found the poet's style too grandiloquent and preferred more direct expression.

Synset Examples

he engages in so much tall talk, one never really realizes what he is saying

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bombastic - using big or impressive-sounding words, often with little meaning
  • Synonym: pretentious - attempting to impress by affecting greater importance or merit than is actually possessed
  • Antonym: simple - plain or easy to understand, without elaborate words

Etymology

From Latin 'grandiloquus', from 'grandis' (great) + 'loqui' (to speak).

Synset POS: s
