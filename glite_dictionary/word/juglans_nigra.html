Juglans nigra

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌɡ.lænz ˈnaɪ.ɡrə / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌɡ.lænz ˈnɪɡ.rə / (BrE)
A North American walnut tree with hard, dark wood and edible nuts, commonly known as the black walnut.
North American black walnut tree

Examples

Many antique furniture pieces are crafted from Juglans nigra because of its beautiful, durable wood.
The nuts of Juglans nigra are highly prized by both wildlife and humans.
Farmers often plant Juglans nigra as a valuable timber resource and for its edible nuts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black walnut - Common name for Juglans nigra, a North American tree with dark wood and edible nuts

Etymology

From Latin 'Juglans' (walnut) and 'nigra' (black), referring to the tree's dark wood.
