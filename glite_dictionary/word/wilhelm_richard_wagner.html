Wilhelm Richard Wagner

1. PROPN

/ ˈvɑːɡnər / (AmE), / ˈvɑːɡnə / (BrE)
(1813–1883) A German composer known for his operas and for pioneering the concept of the musical drama, where drama, spectacle, and music are united.
German opera composer (1813–1883)

Examples

Wilhelm Richard Wagner revolutionized opera with his concept of the Gesamtkunstwerk, or ‘total work of art’.
The operas of Wilhelm Richard Wagner continue to influence composers and artists today.
Many music enthusiasts travel to Bayreuth to experience the works of Wilhelm Richard Wagner in the theatre he designed.
