subtropics

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˈtrɑpɪks / (AmE), / sʌbˈtrɒpɪks / (BrE)
the regions of the Earth that lie immediately north and south of the tropics, characterized by hot, humid summers and mild winters
regions just outside the tropics

Examples

Much of southern China is located in the subtropics and has a warm and humid climate.
The subtropics are home to many unique species of plants and animals.
She studied the weather patterns that occur in the subtropics throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tropics - regions surrounding the Equator, between the Tropic of Cancer and the Tropic of Capricorn
  • Degree Higher: temperate zone - regions located between the subtropical and polar zones, with moderate climates

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'sub-' (meaning 'under, near') and 'tropics'.
