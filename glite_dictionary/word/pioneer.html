pioneer

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪəˈnɪr / (AmE), / ˌpaɪəˈnɪə / (BrE)
a person who is among the first to develop or apply new methods, areas of knowledge, or activities
person who initiates new developments

Examples

Marie Curie was a true pioneer in the field of radioactivity.
Many pioneers faced ridicule for their unconventional ideas.
The company became a pioneer of renewable energy technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trailblazer - a person who is the first to do something or go somewhere
  • Synonym: innovator - a person who introduces new methods, ideas, or products

Etymology

From Middle French *pionnier* (foot soldier), from Old French *peonier*, based on *peon* (foot soldier).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪəˈnɪr / (AmE), / ˌpaɪəˈnɪə / (BrE)
one of the first people to settle in a new area or territory, preparing it for others to follow
early settler in a new area

Examples

Many pioneers traveled hundreds of miles to start new lives in the American West.
As pioneers, they faced harsh winters and scarce resources in the unfamiliar land.
The town's museum honors the early pioneers who built the first homes in the region.

Synset Examples

they went west as pioneers with only the possessions they could carry with them

Related Terms

  • Synonym: settler - a person who moves to a new area to live, especially as one of the first people there
  • Synonym: colonist - a person who settles in a new colony

Etymology

From Middle French pionnier (foot soldier, pioneer), ultimately from Old French peonier (foot soldier), based on Latin pedo (foot soldier), from pes (foot).
