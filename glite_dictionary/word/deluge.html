deluge

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛl.juːdʒ / (AmE), / ˈdɛl.juːdʒ / (BrE)
A severe flood or a heavy, drenching rain.
a heavy rain or flood

Examples

A sudden deluge forced everyone to seek shelter under the bridge.
After a week of sunshine, a deluge washed out the picnic plans.
The city was unprepared for the deluge that flooded the streets overnight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: downpour - a heavy and sudden fall of rain
  • Degree Lower: drizzle - a light rain in fine drops
  • Synonym: flood - an overflow of water beyond normal limits, especially over land

Etymology

From Latin 'diluvium' meaning 'flood'.

to flood or overwhelm with a large amount of water or something else, especially in a short period of time
to flood or overwhelm

Examples

During the sale, the store was deluged by shoppers looking for bargains.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɛludʒ / (AmE), / ˈdɛljuːdʒ / (BrE)
A large quantity of something that arrives at the same time, overwhelming in number or amount.
overwhelming quantity or flood

Examples

The customer service team was faced with a deluge of emails after the software crash.
A sudden deluge of criticism forced the politician to reconsider the proposal.
During the holiday sale, the website experienced a deluge of orders within hours.

Synset Examples

a torrent of abuse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flood - a large amount of something that comes at once
  • Synonym: torrent - a sudden, large amount of something (figurative)

4. NOUN

/ ˈdɛl.judʒ / (AmE), / ˈdɛl.juːdʒ / (BrE)
a severe flood in which a large amount of water covers an area that is usually dry land
severe flood; inundation

Examples

After days of heavy rain, the river burst its banks, causing a deluge that stranded several villages.
The ancient text describes a great deluge that swept across the entire region.
When the dam failed, a sudden deluge washed away the farmland near the riverbanks.

Synset Examples

plains fertilized by annual inundations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flood - an overflow of water that submerges land
  • Synonym: inundation - an overwhelming flood, especially of water
  • Antonym: drought - a long period with no rain

Etymology

From Latin 'diluvium' (flood, inundation).
