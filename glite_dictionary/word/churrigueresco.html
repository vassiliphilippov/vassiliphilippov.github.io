churrigueresco

1. ADJ

/ tʃuːˌriːɡəˈrɛskoʊ / (AmE), / tʃʊəˌrɪɡəˈrɛskəʊ / (BrE)
(of architecture) characterized by very elaborate and symmetrical ornamentation, in the style of the Spanish Churrigueresque movement.
highly ornate architectural style

Examples

The church's façade is a stunning example of churrigueresco design.
Many cathedrals in Spain feature the complex detailing typical of the churrigueresco style.
The ornate altar, almost overwhelming in its churrigueresco splendor, captivates every visitor.

Synset Examples

the building … frantically baroque

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baroque - highly ornate style in art and architecture

Etymology

From Spanish 'churrigueresco', after the Churriguera family, Spanish architects in the 17th–18th centuries.
