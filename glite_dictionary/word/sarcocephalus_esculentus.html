Sarcocephalus esculentus

1. NOUN

/ sɑːrkoʊˈsɛfələs ˌɛskjəˈlɛntəs / (AmE), / sɑːkəʊˈsɛfələs ˌɛskjʊˈlɛntəs / (BrE)
a stout, spreading or semi-climbing tropical shrub native to Africa, bearing round brownish-red warty fruit
African tropical shrub with warty fruit

Examples

The fruit of Sarcocephalus esculentus is locally harvested and sometimes eaten fresh.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Sarcocephalus esculentus found in several regions of West Africa.
Traditional healers often use different parts of Sarcocephalus esculentus in herbal remedies.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Sarcocephalus' and species epithet 'esculentus' meaning 'edible'.
