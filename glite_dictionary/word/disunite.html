to disunite

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪs.juˈnaɪt / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.juˈnaɪt / (BrE)
to cause people or things to become separated or not united; to break apart a union or connection
cause to become separated or not united

Examples

A major scandal threatened to disunite the party's leadership.
The issue of land reform could disunite the close-knit community if not handled carefully.
Attempts to disunite the two friends only made their bond stronger.

Synset Examples

He separated the fighting children
Moses parted the Red Sea

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to unite - to bring together or join into a single entity
  • Synonym: separate - to set or keep apart

Etymology

From Latin dis- (apart) + unite (from Latin unitus, united).

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌdɪs.juˈnaɪt / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.juːˈnaɪt / (BrE)
to separate or break the connection or association between people, groups, or things
to separate or break association

Examples

The committee voted to disunite from the umbrella organization over policy disagreements.
Over time, the two groups began to disunite due to conflicting interests.
She chose to disunite herself from the project after learning about its unethical practices.

Synset Examples

She disassociated herself from the organization when she found out the identity of the president

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to unite - to come or bring together to form a single entity

Etymology

From Latin dis- (apart) + unitus (united, joined).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02435427-v
Synset POS: v
