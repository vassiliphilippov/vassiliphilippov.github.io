to emblazon

1. VERB

/ ɛmˈbleɪzən / (AmE), / ɪmˈbleɪzən / (BrE)
to decorate or inscribe with heraldic symbols, designs, or conspicuous decoration
decorate with heraldic arms or designs

Examples

The knight's shield was emblazoned with a golden lion.
Banners emblazoned with the family crest hung along the hall.
The team’s logo is proudly emblazoned on their uniforms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adorn - to decorate or add beauty to
  • Synonym: to decorate - to make something look more attractive by adding items to it

Etymology

From Old French 'emblasoner' (to adorn with a coat of arms).

2. VERB

/ ɛmˈbleɪzən / (AmE), / ɪmˈbleɪz(ə)n / (BrE)
to decorate or adorn something vividly, especially with color, heraldic designs, or other striking features
decorate vividly with colors or symbols

Examples

The family crest was emblazoned on the front gate for all to see.
His shirt was emblazoned with bold, colorful patterns that drew everyone's attention.
The banners emblazoned with the team logo lined the stadium walls.

Synset Examples

color the walls with paint in warm tones

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decorate - to make something look more attractive by adding items or designs

Etymology

From Middle English 'enblasonen', from Old French 'emblasoner' (to depict a coat of arms), from 'blason' (shield).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01700543-v
Synset POS: v
