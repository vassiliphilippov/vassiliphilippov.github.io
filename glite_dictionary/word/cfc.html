CFC

1. NOUN

/ si ɛf si / (AmE), / siː ɛf siː / (BrE)
a chlorofluorocarbon; a compound containing carbon, chlorine, and fluorine, once used in refrigerants and aerosol propellants but harmful to the ozone layer
chlorofluorocarbon, ozone-depleting compound

Examples

Many countries have banned the use of CFC in aerosol sprays due to environmental concerns.
The release of CFC gases has been linked to ozone layer depletion above Antarctica.
Scientists monitor the levels of CFC in the atmosphere to track recovery of the ozone layer.

Synset Examples

the chlorine in CFCs causes depletion of atmospheric ozone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chlorofluorocarbon - a compound of carbon, chlorine, and fluorine used in refrigerants and propellants

Etymology

Abbreviation of chlorofluorocarbon, from Greek 'chloros' (green), Latin 'fluere' (to flow), and 'carbon'.
