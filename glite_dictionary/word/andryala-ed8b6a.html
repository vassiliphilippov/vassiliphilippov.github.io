Andryala

1. NOUN

/ ændriˈeɪlə / (AmE), / ændrɪˈɑːlə / (BrE)
A genus of hardy, hairy, latex-producing perennial plants native to the Mediterranean region.
genus of Mediterranean perennial plants

Examples

The Andryala genus includes several species adapted to dry Mediterranean climates.
Botanists studied the unique latex produced by Andryala plants.
You can spot Andryala by its distinctive hairy leaves and yellow flowers.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the ancient Greek personal name Andryalos.
