Nebraska

1. PROPN

/ nəˈbræskə / (AmE), / nɪˈbrɑːskə / (BrE)
A state located in the Midwestern region of the United States, situated on the Great Plains.
U.S. state in the Great Plains

Examples

My grandparents moved to Nebraska in the 1950s to start a farm.
Every summer, we drive across Nebraska on our way to Colorado.
The University of Nebraska has a well-known football team.

Etymology

From the Omaha language, meaning 'flat water', referring to the Platte River.
