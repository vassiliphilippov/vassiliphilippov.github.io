Aepyceros melampus

1. NOUN

/ eɪˌpɪsəˌroʊs məˈlæmpəs / (AmE), / iːˌpɪsərɒs mɛˈlæmpəs / (BrE)
A species of African antelope, commonly known as the impala, characterized by ridged curved horns and the ability to make large leaps.
African antelope (impala)

Examples

The Aepyceros melampus is often seen grazing in open woodlands across southern Africa.
Researchers observed how the Aepyceros melampus escapes predators by leaping in great bounds.
You can recognize a male Aepyceros melampus by its distinctive, ridged curved horns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impala - African antelope known for leaping ability

Etymology

From Greek 'aipus' (high, steep) + 'keras' (horn) and Greek 'melas' (black) + 'pous' (foot).
