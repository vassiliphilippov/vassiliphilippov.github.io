backwoods

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌwʊdz / (AmE), / ˈbækˌwʊdz / (BrE)
an area that is remote, sparsely inhabited, and undeveloped; often heavily forested and distant from civilization.
remote, undeveloped area

Examples

Few people have ventured deep into the backwoods of that region.
He grew up in the backwoods and learned how to survive off the land.
Stories about mysterious creatures living in the backwoods have always fascinated locals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wilderness - an uncultivated, uninhabited, and inhospitable region
  • Synonym: outback - remote, sparsely populated areas (esp. Australia)

Etymology

Compound of 'back' and 'woods', originally referring to forested land behind settlements in North America.
