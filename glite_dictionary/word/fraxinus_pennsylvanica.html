Fraxinus pennsylvanica

1. NOUN

/ ˌfræksɪnəs ˌpɛnslveɪˈvænɪkə / (AmE), / ˌfræksɪnəs ˌpɛnsɪlveɪˈvænɪkə / (BrE)
A species of ash tree native to North America, with smallish size, velvety young branches, and leaves with fuzzy undersides; commonly called green ash.
a North American species of ash tree

Examples

The Fraxinus pennsylvanica is widely planted along city streets for its shade and resilience.
Many birds nest in the branches of the Fraxinus pennsylvanica found across the Midwest.
Researchers studied the effects of disease on local populations of Fraxinus pennsylvanica trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: green ash - Common name for Fraxinus pennsylvanica, the North American ash tree species

Etymology

From Latin; 'Fraxinus' meaning 'ash tree' and 'pennsylvanica' referring to Pennsylvania (the region where the species was first identified).
