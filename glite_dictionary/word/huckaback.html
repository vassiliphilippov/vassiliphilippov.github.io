huckaback

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌk.əˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈhʌk.ə.bæk / (BrE)
A coarse, absorbent cotton or linen fabric, typically used for making towels and similar items.
absorbent towel fabric

Examples

The old-fashioned guest towels were made of huckaback for extra absorbency.
She bought several yards of huckaback fabric to sew new kitchen towels.
The textured surface of huckaback makes it ideal for drying dishes quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrycloth - woven cotton fabric with loops that can absorb large amounts of water

Etymology

Probably from the obsolete German dialect *Huckeback*, perhaps originally meaning a kind of linen fabric for peddlers (hucksters).
