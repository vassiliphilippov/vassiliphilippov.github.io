Rubus spectabilis

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːbəs spɛkˈteɪbɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˈruːbəs spɛkˈteɪbɪlɪs / (BrE)
A species of large, erect raspberry native to western North America, with red flowers and large pinkish-orange berries.
species of North American raspberry

Examples

The Rubus spectabilis blooms brightly along the forest edges in early spring.
Researchers studied the nutritional value of Rubus spectabilis in indigenous diets.
Hikers often spot Rubus spectabilis due to its distinctive pinkish-orange berries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salmonberry - common name for Rubus spectabilis, the plant with edible orange-pink berries

Etymology

From Latin; 'Rubus' meaning 'bramble' or 'raspberry', 'spectabilis' meaning 'remarkable, showy'.
