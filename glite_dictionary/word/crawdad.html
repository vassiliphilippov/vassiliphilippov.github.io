crawdad

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔˌdæd / (AmE), / ˈkrɔːˌdæd / (BrE)
A small freshwater crustacean that resembles a lobster, also known as a crayfish, especially in the southern and central United States.
freshwater crustacean resembling a lobster
american

Examples

We spent the afternoon catching crawdads in the creek behind the house.
The biology class studied the anatomy of a live crawdad during lab.
Some restaurants in Louisiana serve spicy boiled crawdads during the summer festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crayfish - small freshwater crustacean with a lobster-like appearance
  • Synonym: crawfish - American variant term for crayfish

Etymology

American regional variant of 'crayfish', influenced by folk etymology and dialectal forms such as 'crawfish'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔˌdæd / (AmE), / ˈkrɔːˌdæd / (BrE)
A small freshwater crustacean resembling a lobster, commonly found in streams and rivers, especially in North America.
small freshwater crustacean; crayfish
american informal

Examples

We spent the afternoon catching crawdads in the shallow creek behind the house.
The restaurant special tonight is boiled crawdad served with corn and potatoes.
As kids, we used to compete to see who could find the biggest crawdad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crayfish - small freshwater crustacean; alternative term for crawdad

Etymology

American English, probably a variant of 'crayfish,' influenced by regional dialects.
