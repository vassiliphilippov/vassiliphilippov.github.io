osmoreceptor

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑzmoʊrɪˈsɛptər / (AmE), / ˌɒzməʊrɪˈsɛptə / (BrE)
a sensory cell or nerve ending that detects changes in osmotic pressure, especially in bodily fluids
sensory cell detecting osmotic pressure
medical

Examples

Specialized osmoreceptors in the brain help regulate water balance in the body.
The activity of osmoreceptors increases when blood osmolarity rises.
Researchers study how osmoreceptors respond to dehydration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: receptor - a cell or group of cells that receives stimuli

Etymology

From osmo- (relating to osmosis) + receptor (from Latin recipere, to receive).
