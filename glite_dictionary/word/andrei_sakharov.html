Andrei Sakharov

1. PROPN

/ ˈændreɪ ˈsɑːkərɒf / (AmE), / ˈændreɪ ˈsækhərɒf / (BrE)
(1921–1989) A Soviet physicist, Nobel Peace Prize laureate, and dissident known for his role in developing the hydrogen bomb and for his advocacy of nuclear disarmament and human rights.
Soviet physicist and human rights activist

Examples

Andrei Sakharov was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1975 for his efforts to promote human rights.
Many people regard Andrei Sakharov as the conscience of the Soviet Union during the Cold War.
The legacy of Andrei Sakharov continues to inspire those who fight for freedom and justice around the world.
