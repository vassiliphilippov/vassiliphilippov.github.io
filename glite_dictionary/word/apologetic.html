apologetic

1. ADJ

/ əˌpɑl.əˈdʒɛt̬.ɪk / (AmE), / əˌpɒl.əˈdʒɛt.ɪk / (BrE)
showing or feeling regret or sorrow for having done something wrong; offering or expressing an apology.
expressing apology or regret

Examples

She gave him an apologetic smile after accidentally bumping into him.
His apologetic tone suggested he was truly sorry for his mistake.
The manager sent an apologetic email to the customers about the delayed shipment.

Synset Examples

an apologetic note
an apologetic manner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrite - feeling or expressing remorse or guilt
  • Synonym: remorseful - filled with regret or sorrow for wrongdoing
  • Antonym: defiant - showing resistance or bold disobedience

Etymology

From Late Latin apolōgēticus (defending by speech), from Greek apologētikos.
