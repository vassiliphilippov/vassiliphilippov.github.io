Allium ursinum

1. NOUN

/ ˈæliəm ɚˈsaɪnəm / (AmE), / ˈalɪəm ɜːˈsiːnəm / (BrE)
A Eurasian wild plant of the onion family, with broad leaves and a strong garlic smell, often called wild garlic or ramsons.
wild garlic; Eurasian plant with garlic scent

Examples

Every spring, the woods fill with the scent of Allium ursinum in bloom.
Chefs love to add Allium ursinum leaves to salads for their mild garlic flavor.
Foragers gathered baskets of Allium ursinum from the forest edge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild garlic - common name for Allium ursinum, a pungent-leaved woodland plant

Etymology

From Latin: allium (garlic) and ursinum (of the bear), referring to bears' fondness for the plant.
