plimsoll

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɪmˌsoʊl / (AmE), / ˈplɪmsəl / (BrE)
a light canvas shoe with a rubber sole, typically worn for sports or physical education, especially in Britain
light canvas sports shoe with rubber sole
british

Examples

At school, we were required to wear plimsolls for gym class.
He found his old plimsolls at the back of the wardrobe, covered in dust.
The children put on their plimsolls before heading to the playground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sneaker - a soft shoe worn for sports or casual occasions, especially in the US
  • Synonym: trainer - a sports shoe, especially as used in the UK

Etymology

Named after Samuel Plimsoll, whose name was also used for the Plimsoll line on ships, as the shoe’s rubber band resembled the line.
