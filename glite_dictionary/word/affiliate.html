affiliate

1. NOUN

/ əˈfɪliˌeɪt / (AmE), / əˈfɪlieɪt / (BrE)
a person or organization officially connected to a larger body, often as a subordinate or subsidiary part
a connected subordinate organization or member

Examples

The television network has an affiliate in nearly every major city.
She works for an affiliate of the national charity organization.
Our local branch is an affiliate of a much larger international association.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subsidiary - a company controlled by a holding or parent company

Etymology

From Medieval Latin affiliatus, past participle of affiliare, from ad- (to) + filius (son).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09795787-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ əˈfɪliˌeɪt / (AmE), / əˈfɪlɪeɪt / (BrE)
an organization that is officially connected to a larger company or group but remains independent
subordinate organization connected to a bigger one

Examples

Several local TV stations are affiliates of the national broadcasting network.
The company operates through a number of overseas affiliates to reach international markets.
As an affiliate, the organization follows the guidelines set by the parent group but manages its own affairs.

Synset Examples

network affiliates

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subsidiary - a company controlled by another company
  • Antonym: parent company - a company that controls other companies

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *affiliatus*, past participle of *affiliare* (to adopt as a son), from Latin *ad-* (to) + *filius* (son).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08026210-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ əˈfɪliˌeɪt / (AmE), / əˈfɪlieɪt / (BrE)
to associate closely with a group or person; to keep company with or spend time together
associate or keep company with

Examples

She tends to affiliate with creative people at work.
Some teenagers affiliate with certain groups for a sense of belonging.
He consciously chose not to affiliate with any club in high school.

Synset Examples

He associates with strange people
She affiliates with her colleagues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: associate - to mix socially or spend time with
  • Antonym: to avoid - to keep away from or not associate with

Etymology

From Medieval Latin affiliatus, past participle of affiliare (to adopt as a son), from Latin ad- (to) + filius (son).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ əˈfɪliˌeɪt / (AmE), / əˈfɪlieɪt / (BrE)
to officially join or become connected with an organization or group
to join or connect with an organization

Examples

The small club decided to affiliate with the national organization for greater support.
After several meetings, the local library affiliated with a large educational network.
She chose not to affiliate with any political party during the election.

Synset Examples

The two colleges affiliated
They affiliated with a national group

Related Terms

  • Synonym: associate - to connect or join with an organization
  • Antonym: separate - to stop being connected to an organization or group

Etymology

From Medieval Latin affiliatus, past participle of affiliare (to adopt as a son), from Latin ad- (to) + filius (son).
