relapse

1. VERB

/ rɪˈlæps / (AmE), / rɪˈlæps / (BrE)
(of a person) to become ill again after apparent recovery, or to return to a previous, often worse, state or condition.
to fall back into illness or a previous condition

Examples

After several weeks of improvement, his condition began to relapse once again.
Patients with this disease may relapse if they stop taking their medication.
She managed to stay clean for a year before she sadly relapsed.

Synset Examples

he relapsed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to recover - to return to a normal state of health, mind, or strength

Etymology

From Medieval Latin relapsare, from Latin relabi (to slip back).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈlæps / (AmE), / rɪˈlæps / (BrE)
a return to a previous worse state after a period of improvement, especially in health or behavior
return to a worse state

Examples

After months of sobriety, he suffered a relapse and began drinking again.
The patient remains at risk of relapse even after the symptoms disappear.
Her relapse into depression worried her family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recurrence - the act of something happening again
  • Antonym: remission - a period of improvement or decrease in symptoms

Etymology

From Latin 'relapsus', past participle of 'relabi' (to slip back).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00069153-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ riˈlæps / (AmE), / rɪˈlæps / (BrE)
to go back to a previous bad or undesirable behavior, habit, or condition after having improved
revert to bad behavior or condition

Examples

After several months of sobriety, he unfortunately relapsed into old drinking habits.
Without proper support, patients may relapse even after successful treatment.
She managed to quit smoking for a year before she relapsed under stress.

Synset Examples

Those who recidivate are often minor criminals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revert - to return to a previous state or condition
  • Synonym: to backslide - to lapse into bad habits after improvement

Etymology

From Latin 'relapsus', past participle of 'relabi' (to slip back).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00093079-v
Synset POS: v
