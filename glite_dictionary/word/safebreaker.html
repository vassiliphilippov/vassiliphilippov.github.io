safebreaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪfˌbreɪkər / (AmE), / ˈseɪfˌbreɪkə / (BrE)
A person who breaks open safes to steal valuables, especially as a criminal occupation.
person who breaks into safes to steal

Examples

The notorious safebreaker was finally caught after a string of high-profile heists.
Old crime films often feature a safebreaker working at midnight with a stethoscope and steady hands.
Police suspected that the bank's vault was opened by a professional safebreaker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burglar - a person who breaks into buildings to commit theft
  • Synonym: robber - someone who takes property unlawfully, often by force or threat

Etymology

From 'safe' (a strong container for valuables) + 'breaker' (one who breaks).
