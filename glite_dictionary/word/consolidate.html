to consolidate

1. VERB

/ kənˈsɑː.lɪˌdeɪt / (AmE), / kənˈsɒl.ɪ.deɪt / (BrE)
to make something stronger or more secure, especially to reinforce achievements, position, or control
make something stronger or more secure

Examples

The leader hoped to consolidate his control over the party after the elections.
The company managed to consolidate its position in the market despite new competitors.
She worked hard to consolidate her early gains and maintain her lead.

Synset Examples

consolidate one's gains
consolidate one's hold on first place

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to strengthen - make something stronger or more effective
  • Antonym: to weaken - make something less strong or secure

Etymology

From Latin consolidare (to make firm), from com- (together) + solidare (to make solid).

3. VERB

/ kənˈsɑː.lɪˌdeɪt / (AmE), / kənˈsɒ.lɪˌdeɪt / (BrE)
to combine several things, especially organizations or pieces, into a single, more effective or coherent whole
combine or bring together into one

Examples

The two companies decided to consolidate their operations to improve efficiency.
