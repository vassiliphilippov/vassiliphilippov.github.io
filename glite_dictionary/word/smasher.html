smasher

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmæʃər / (AmE), / ˈsmæʃə / (BrE)
a person or thing that is very successful or impressive; a conspicuous success
a notable or outstanding success
informal

Examples

Their new product launch was a real smasher and exceeded all expectations.
The comedian's latest show is a smasher and has sold out every night.
That movie turned out to be a smasher at the box office.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hit - a thing or person that is very successful, especially a song or show
  • Antonym: failure - lack of success; an unsuccessful person or thing

3. NOUN

/ ˈsmæʃər / (AmE), / ˈsmæʃə / (BrE)
a person who smashes or breaks things, especially deliberately or forcefully
person who smashes things

Examples

The police finally caught the infamous smasher who was breaking windows across town.
He's a real smasher on the cricket field, sending the ball flying every time.
After hearing the loud crash, they suspected a smasher had broken into the shop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destroyer - a person or thing that destroys something
  • Synonym: breaker - a person who breaks something

Etymology

From the verb 'smash' + the agent noun suffix '-er'.
