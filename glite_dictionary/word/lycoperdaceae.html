Lycoperdaceae

1. NOUN

/ laɪˌkɑːpərˈdeɪsiː / (AmE), / laɪˌkɒpəˈdeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi in the order Lycoperdales, including puffballs and similar species
fungus family including puffballs

Examples

The Lycoperdaceae are known for producing round, spore-filled fruiting bodies called puffballs.
Biologists classified the mysterious mushroom under the Lycoperdaceae family.
Many edible varieties of Lycoperdaceae can be found in temperate forests worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puffball - a type of fungus in the family Lycoperdaceae that releases spores in a puff when mature

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek lykos (wolf) + perdē (to break wind), referencing the puff of spores when squeezed.

2. NOUN

/ laɪˌkɑːpərˈdeɪsiː / (AmE), / laɪˌkɒpəˈdeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi in the order Lycoperdales, including the puffballs and related species
family of fungi including puffballs

Examples

The Lycoperdaceae are known for their distinctive puffball fruiting bodies.
Many edible mushrooms are found within the Lycoperdaceae family.
Scientists studied several species in the Lycoperdaceae to better understand their spore dispersal mechanisms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puffball - a type of fungus in the Lycoperdaceae family that releases spores when mature

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'lykos' (wolf) + 'perdo' (to break wind), referring to 'wolf's fart' (puffballs), with 'aceae' the standard family suffix.
