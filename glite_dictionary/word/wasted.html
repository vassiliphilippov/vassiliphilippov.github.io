wasted

1. ADJ

/ ˈweɪstɪd / (AmE), / ˈweɪstɪd / (BrE)
(of an organ or body part) weakened and reduced in size, typically as a result of disease, injury, or lack of use.
reduced in size or strength (body part)

Examples

After months in a cast, her leg appeared wasted and weak.
The patient's wasted muscles made movement difficult after the long illness.
Physical therapy can help restore strength to wasted limbs.

Synset Examples

partial paralysis resulted in an atrophied left arm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atrophied - (of body tissue) gradually declined in effectiveness or vigor due to underuse or neglect

2. ADJ

/ ˈweɪstɪd / (AmE), / ˈweɪstɪd / (BrE)
serving no useful purpose; pointless or ineffective
serving no useful purpose

Examples

All their efforts turned out to be wasted when the plan was cancelled.
He hated giving wasted advice that nobody ever followed.
They spent hours in wasted meetings that achieved nothing.

Related Terms

3. ADJ

/ ˈweɪstɪd / (AmE), / ˈweɪstɪd / (BrE)
Used or expended without bringing any useful result; not utilized effectively.
not used effectively or to good advantage

Examples

She felt her talents had been wasted in her previous job.
All the time and energy put into the project ended up being wasted.
It was a wasted opportunity that they later regretted.

Synset Examples

squandered money cannot be replaced
a wasted effort

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squandered - used wastefully or extravagantly without gain
  • Synonym: fruitless - failing to achieve the desired result
  • Antonym: productive - producing good or useful results

4. ADJ

/ ˈweɪstɪd / (AmE), / ˈweɪstɪd / (BrE)
(of a person or their body) extremely thin and weak, usually as a result of illness, hunger, or hardship.
extremely thin and weak, often from illness or hunger

Examples

Years of illness had left his body wasted and frail.
The refugees arrived wasted and pale after weeks without food.
Her wasted frame told the story of her long struggle against the disease.

Synset Examples

a nightmare population of gaunt men and skeletal boys
eyes were haggard and cavernous
small pinched faces
kept life in his wasted frame only by grim concentration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaunt - extremely thin, especially from suffering, hunger, or age
  • Synonym: emaciated - abnormally thin or weak, especially because of illness or lack of food
  • Antonym: robust - strong and healthy; vigorous

Etymology

From the verb 'waste' (to consume, diminish), from Old French *waster*, from Latin *vastare* (to lay waste).
