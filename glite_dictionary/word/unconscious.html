unconscious

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkɑːnʃəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈkɒnʃəs / (BrE)
(of an action or process) done without deliberate awareness or intention; not consciously planned or controlled.
done without awareness or intention

Examples

Much of our behavior is guided by unconscious motives we do not fully understand.
She gave an unconscious smile, not realizing she was being watched.
Many habits, like tapping your fingers, are unconscious and hard to break.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: automatic - performed without conscious thought or intention
  • Antonym: deliberate - done consciously and intentionally

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + conscius (knowing, aware).

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkɑnʃəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈkɒnʃəs / (BrE)
not awake or aware of and responding to one's environment; lacking consciousness, as if asleep or dead
not awake or aware; insensible

Examples

The boxer was knocked unconscious in the final round of the match.
She was found unconscious by the side of the road after the accident.
During surgery, patients are kept unconscious with anesthesia.

Synset Examples

lay unconscious on the floor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insensible - not able to feel or perceive; without sensation
  • Antonym: conscious - aware and able to respond to one's surroundings

Etymology

From Latin 'un-' (not) + 'conscious' from Latin 'conscius' (knowing with others or in oneself).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00573907-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkɑnʃəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈkɒnʃəs / (BrE)
not aware of or not noticing something; not knowing or perceiving (often used with 'of')
not aware of something

Examples

She walked past the painting, completely unconscious of its artistic value.
He seemed unconscious of the effect his words had on others.
The children were laughing, unconscious of the approaching storm.

Synset Examples

happily unconscious of the new calamity at home

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aware - knowing about something; conscious of
  • Synonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness in general; uneducated or unsophisticated

Etymology

From Latin 'un-' (not) + 'conscious' from Latin 'conscius' (knowing with, conscious).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00193933-s
Synset POS: s

4. NOUN

/ ʌnˈkɑn.ʃəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈkɒn.ʃəs / (BrE)
the part of the mind that contains thoughts, feelings, and memories of which a person is not aware
part of the mind outside awareness

Examples

Some psychologists believe that childhood experiences remain buried in the unconscious.
Dreams are often thought to reveal hidden desires from the unconscious mind.
Fears and anxieties can sometimes be rooted deep within the unconscious.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subconscious - the part of the mind below the level of conscious perception
  • Antonym: conscious - the part of the mind aware of itself and surroundings

Etymology

From Latin 'un-' (not) + 'conscīre' (to be conscious of).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05621057-n
Synset POS: n
