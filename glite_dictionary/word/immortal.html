immortal

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈmɔrtəl / (AmE), / ɪˈmɔːtəl / (BrE)
Living forever; not subject to death.
not subject to death

Examples

The legend tells of an immortal being who has watched centuries pass.
Some people dream of achieving immortal fame through their works.
Vampires are often portrayed as immortal creatures in literature.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mortal - subject to death
  • Synonym: eternal - lasting forever

Etymology

From Latin immortalis, from in- (not) + mortalis (mortal).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɔrtəl / (AmE), / ɪˈmɔːtəl / (BrE)
a person, especially a writer or artist, whose fame endures for generations
person of enduring fame

Examples

Throughout history, only a handful of writers have been called immortal for their lasting influence.
After his remarkable achievements, many consider him an immortal in the world of science.
The museum dedicated an entire exhibition to the immortals of nineteenth-century art.

Synset Examples

Shakespeare is one of the immortals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legend - a person who is very famous and admired, often from the past
  • Synonym: icon - a person or thing regarded as a representative symbol or as worthy of veneration

Etymology

From Latin immortalis, from in- (not) + mortalis (mortal).

3. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɔrtl / (AmE), / ɪˈmɔːtl / (BrE)
a supernatural being, such as a god or goddess, who is believed to live forever and often has power over part of the world or nature
a god or supernatural being

Examples

In ancient Greece, the immortals often meddled in the affairs of mortals.
Legends say the immortal watched over the mountain for centuries.
Each immortal in the myth had a unique power linked to nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deity - a god or goddess; a divine being
  • Antonym: mortal - a being who is subject to death

Etymology

From Latin immortalis, from in- (not) + mortalis (mortal).
