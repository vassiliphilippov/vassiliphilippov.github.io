Fusanus acuminatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌfjuːˈseɪnəs ˌækjʊˈmɪnətəs / (AmE), / ˌfjuːˈsænəs ˌækjʊˈmɪnətəs / (BrE)
An Australian tree whose edible flesh and nut-like seeds are consumed, also known as quandong.
Australian tree with edible fruit and seeds

Examples

The fruit of the Fusanus acuminatus is a popular bush food among Indigenous Australians.
Researchers are studying the nutritional value of seeds from the Fusanus acuminatus.
Several dishes featured the unique, tangy flavor of Fusanus acuminatus fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quandong - another name for Fusanus acuminatus, an Australian fruit tree
