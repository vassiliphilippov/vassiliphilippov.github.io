Mobula

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊ.bjʊ.lə / (AmE), / ˈməʊ.bjʊ.lə / (BrE)
A genus of large rays known as devil rays, belonging to the family Mobulidae.
genus of large rays (devil rays)

Examples

Scientists recently discovered a new species in the Mobula genus along the coast of Mexico.
The Mobula rays are often seen leaping out of the water in large groups.
Conservation efforts have increased to protect various Mobula species threatened by fishing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devil ray - a common name for rays in the Mobulidae family

Etymology

Modern Latin, from Latin 'mobula' (a type of large ray).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊ.bjələ / (AmE), / ˈməʊ.bjʊ.lə / (BrE)
A genus of large rays, commonly known as mobulas or devil rays, found in tropical and temperate oceans.
genus of large rays (devil rays)

Examples

The Mobula is known for its spectacular leaps out of the water.
Marine biologists recently discovered a new species within the Mobula genus.
Unlike manta rays, members of the Mobula genus tend to form large schools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mantis ray - another common name for rays in the genus Mobula

Etymology

From Latin 'mobula', a type of ray.
