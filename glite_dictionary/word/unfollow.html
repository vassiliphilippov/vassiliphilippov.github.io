to unfollow

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈfɑːloʊ / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɒləʊ / (BrE)
to stop following a particular user or account on a social media platform so that you no longer receive their updates
stop following a user on social media

Examples

After the argument, she decided to unfollow him on all social media platforms.
If someone's posts annoy you, you can always unfollow their account without blocking them.
I've noticed that fewer people unfollow brands when their content is entertaining.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to follow - subscribe to updates from a user on social media

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' to 'follow', modeled on verbs describing digital actions.
