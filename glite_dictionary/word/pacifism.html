pacifism

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæsɪˌfɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpasɪˌfɪzəm / (BrE)
The belief that all violence is unjustifiable and that disputes should be settled by peaceful means.
belief that all violence is unjustifiable

Examples

Her commitment to pacifism inspired her work with peace organizations around the world.
Many people adopted pacifism after witnessing the horrors of war.
Gandhi was a famous proponent of pacifism in his fight for Indian independence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonviolence - The use of peaceful means, not force, to bring about political or social change
  • Antonym: militarism - The belief or desire that a country should maintain a strong military capability

Etymology

From French *pacifisme*, from Latin *pacificus* (peace-making).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpæsɪˌfɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpæsɪfɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief that international disputes and conflicts should be settled by peaceful means, especially arbitration, rather than war or violence
belief in peaceful resolution of disputes

Examples

Her lifelong commitment to pacifism influenced her activism against war.
The organization's principles are rooted in pacifism and diplomacy.
He embraced pacifism after witnessing the devastation caused by armed conflict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonviolence - the practice of refusing to use violence, especially for moral, religious, or political reasons
  • Antonym: militarism - the belief or policy that a country should maintain a strong military capability and be prepared to use it aggressively

Etymology

From French 'pacifisme', from 'pacifique' (peaceful).
