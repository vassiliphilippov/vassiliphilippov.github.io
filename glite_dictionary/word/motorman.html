motorman

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊtərˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈməʊtəmæn / (BrE)
a person who operates a streetcar, tram, or similar electrically powered public transport vehicle
operator of a streetcar or tram

Examples

The motorman skillfully guided the streetcar through the busy downtown intersection.
In his uniform, the motorman greeted every passenger with a friendly nod.
Training to become a motorman required learning how to operate the tram controls safely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conductor - person responsible for taking tickets and assisting passengers on public transport

Etymology

Formed from 'motor' and 'man', dating from late 19th century with the advent of electric streetcars.
