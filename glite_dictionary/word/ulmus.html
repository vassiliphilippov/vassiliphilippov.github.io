Ulmus

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌlməs / (AmE), / ˈʌlməs / (BrE)
The genus of deciduous trees in the family Ulmaceae, commonly known as elms, found widely in temperate regions and noted for their simple, serrated leaves.
the elm tree genus

Examples

Many urban parks feature the Ulmus genus for its shade and graceful appearance.
The Dutch elm disease has devastated populations of Ulmus across Europe and North America.
Botanists studied the leaf structure unique to the Ulmus trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elm - a tree of the genus Ulmus

Etymology

From Latin Ulmus, meaning 'elm'.
