hark-back

1. NOUN

/ hɑrk bæk / (AmE), / hɑːk bæk / (BrE)
an instance of returning or referring to something from an earlier time, especially in styles, trends, or opinions
reference or return to the past

Examples

The director's latest film is a hark-back to the golden age of Hollywood.
Her retro dress is a clear hark-back to 1960s fashion.
The politician's speech was a hark-back to attitudes of a previous generation.

Synset Examples

The film's style is a hark-back to classic cinema.
His comments were a hark-back to our previous discussion.
The fashion hark-back is very popular this season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throwback - a reversion to an earlier type or style, especially in art or fashion

Etymology

From earlier verb phrase 'hark back', originally from hunting, meaning to turn back along a path; then figuratively in reference to recalling or returning to earlier things.
