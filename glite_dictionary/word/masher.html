masher

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæʃər / (AmE), / ˈmæʃə / (BrE)
A kitchen utensil with a flat or perforated surface, used for crushing or mashing food such as potatoes.
kitchen utensil for mashing food

Examples

She used a masher to prepare the potatoes for dinner.
The old masher was missing from the kitchen drawer.
After boiling the vegetables, he reached for the masher.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: potato masher - a masher specifically designed for potatoes

Etymology

From 'mash' (to crush or smash soft), + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæʃər / (AmE), / ˈmæʃə / (BrE)
a man who behaves in an overconfident or aggressive way towards women, especially in making romantic advances
man aggressively pursuing women

Examples

In old films, a masher would often harass women on the street corners.
She warned her friend to stay away from the masher at the bar.
The novel's antagonist was described as a notorious masher in the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: womanizer - a man who engages in numerous casual sexual affairs with women

Etymology

Originally 19th-century American slang, possibly from 'smash' in the sense 'to impress or overwhelm'.
