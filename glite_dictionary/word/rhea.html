rhea

1. NOUN

/ ˈriː.ə / (AmE), / ˈriː.ə / (BrE)
A large, fast-running, flightless bird native to South America, similar to the ostrich but smaller and having three toes on each foot.
large South American flightless bird

Examples

A single rhea can often be seen striding across the open plains of Argentina.
The rhea is distinguished from the ostrich by its three-toed feet and smaller size.
Farmers in South America sometimes raise rheas for their meat and eggs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostrich - a very large, two-toed, flightless bird native to Africa

Etymology

From Greek Rhea (Ῥέα), mother of the gods, later applied to the bird in modern Latin scientific usage.

2. NOUN

/ ˈriː.ə / (AmE), / ˈriː.ə / (BrE)
A large, flightless bird of South America, resembling an ostrich but smaller and with three toes, found in regions from Peru to the Strait of Magellan.
large flightless South American bird

Examples

The rhea is native to the grasslands of South America and is known for its speed.
Several rheas roamed freely on the farm, eating the tall grass.
Like the ostrich and emu, the rhea cannot fly but runs swiftly across open plains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostrich - a large flightless bird native to Africa
  • Synonym: emu - large Australian flightless bird similar to ostrich

Etymology

From Modern Latin *Rhea*, the genus name, after the Titaness Rhea in Greek mythology.
