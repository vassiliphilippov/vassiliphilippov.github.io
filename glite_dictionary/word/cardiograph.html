cardiograph

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑr.di.oʊˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.di.əˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
A medical instrument used to record the mechanical force of the heart's contractions and measure the amount of blood passing through the heart by detecting the body's recoil as blood is pumped from the ventricles.
instrument for recording heart contractions
medical

Examples

The doctor used a cardiograph to record the patient's heart activity during the stress test.
Modern hospitals are equipped with an advanced cardiograph for monitoring cardiac function.
Once the cardiograph was calibrated, the technician began the procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electrocardiograph - an instrument for recording the electrical activity of the heart

Etymology

From Greek *kardia* (heart) + *-graph* (instrument for recording).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrdiəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdiəˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a medical instrument that records the electrical currents produced by the contractions of the heart
instrument recording heart's electrical activity
medical

Examples

The doctor used a cardiograph to monitor the patient's heart activity during the examination.
After experiencing chest pain, she was connected to a cardiograph for further testing.
Technicians regularly calibrate the cardiograph to ensure accurate heart readings in the clinic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electrocardiogram - a recording of the heart's electrical activity
  • Synonym: electrocardiograph - a medical instrument for recording the heart's electrical activity

Etymology

From Greek 'kardia' (heart) + '-graph' (writing instrument).
