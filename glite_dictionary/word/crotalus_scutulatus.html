Crotalus scutulatus

1. NOUN

/ kroʊˈtæləs skjuːˈtjuːlətəs / (AmE), / krəʊˈtæləs skjuːˈtjuːlətəs / (BrE)
A highly venomous species of rattlesnake, commonly known as the Mojave rattlesnake, found in the Mojave Desert, western Texas, and northern Mexico.
a venomous Mojave rattlesnake species

Examples

Hikers in the desert should watch out for the Crotalus scutulatus, as its venom is particularly potent.
The Crotalus scutulatus is often identified by its distinctive greenish hue and diamond-shaped patterns.
Researchers tagged a Crotalus scutulatus near the Arizona border to study its movement patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mojave rattlesnake - common English name for Crotalus scutulatus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Crotalus' (rattle) and 'scutulatus' (shield-shaped), referring to scale patterns.
