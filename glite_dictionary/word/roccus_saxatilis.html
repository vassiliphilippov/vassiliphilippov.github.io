Roccus saxatilis

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɑːkəs sækˈsætɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˌrɒkəs sakˈsatɪlɪs / (BrE)
A species of marine food and game fish, also known as the striped bass, with dark longitudinal stripes; it migrates upriver to spawn and is sometimes placed in the genus Morone.
species of striped bass (fish)

Examples

Fishermen along the Atlantic coast often try to catch Roccus saxatilis during its spring migration.
The diet of Roccus saxatilis consists mainly of smaller fish and crustaceans.
Conservation measures are in place to protect Roccus saxatilis populations in several rivers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: striped bass - common name for Roccus saxatilis, a popular game fish

Etymology

From Latin: 'roccus' (name for a fish) and 'saxatilis' (dwelling among rocks).
