recreant

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛkriənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛkriənt / (BrE)
having deserted a cause, principle, or loyalty; cowardly or unfaithful to duty
having deserted a cause or principle
archaic

Examples

After the defeat, a recreant general was accused of betraying his comrades.
Some officials turned recreant when the reforms faced opposition.
He condemned the recreant members who abandoned their longstanding principles.

Synset Examples

some provinces had proved recreant
renegade supporters of the usurper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: renegade - a person who deserts and betrays an organization, country, or set of principles

Etymology

From Old French *recreant* (yielding), present participle of *recreire* (to yield), from Latin *recrescere* (to grow again, but via medieval legal senses).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛkriənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛkriənt / (BrE)
A person who is cowardly or faint-hearted, especially one who shows lack of courage in the face of danger or difficulty.
coward; faint-hearted person
archaic

Examples

The knight was branded a recreant after fleeing the battlefield.
No recreant would dare to show his face among the brave warriors.
Shakespeare often used the word recreant to insult a cowardly character.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coward - a person who lacks courage
  • Antonym: hero - a person admired for courage or noble qualities

Etymology

From Old French *recreant* ‘yielding, surrendering’, from the verb *recreant* ‘to yield’ (from Latin *recredere*, ‘to entrust’).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10471580-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈrɛkriənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛkriənt / (BrE)
Having no courage; abjectly cowardly or craven.
extremely cowardly or craven

Examples

The recreant knight dropped his sword and begged for mercy before the battle even began.
Her recreant response to the crisis disappointed everyone who expected her to take a stand.
The general called his troops recreant when they refused to advance under enemy fire.

Synset Examples

the craven fellow turned and ran
a craven proposal to raise the white flag
this recreant knight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: craven - extremely cowardly
  • Synonym: cowardly - lacking courage
  • Antonym: brave - showing courage

Etymology

From Old French *recreant* (surrendering), from the verb *recreire* (to yield), from Latin *recrescere* (to grow back, withdraw).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00266362-s
Synset POS: s

4. NOUN

/ ˈriːkriənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛkriənt / (BrE)
a person who betrays or deserts their cause, religion, political party, or friends; a traitor or coward
person who betrays or deserts a cause
archaic

Examples

The knight was declared a recreant and cast out of the order.
He was branded a recreant for abandoning his comrades in battle.
In the eyes of his peers, the recreant could never regain their trust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traitor - a person who betrays someone or something
  • Antonym: loyalist - a person who remains loyal to a cause or leader

Etymology

From Old French *recreant* 'surrendering', present participle of *recreant* 'to yield, surrender'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
