MPS

1. NOUN

/ ɛm pi ɛs / (AmE), / ɛm pi ɛs / (BrE)
a system of free and fixed macrophages, derived from bone marrow, that are distributed throughout the body and involved in immune defense and phagocytosis.
the mononuclear phagocyte system in immunology
medical

Examples

Doctors examined the MPS to assess the patient's immune response.
Research on the MPS has led to a better understanding of how infections are cleared from the body.
Damage to the MPS can compromise the body’s ability to fight certain diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reticuloendothelial system - an older term for the system of cells including macrophages and related cells

Etymology

Abbreviation of mononuclear phagocyte system, a medical term.
