assassination

1. NOUN

/ əˌsæs.ɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / əˌsæs.ɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The act of murdering a prominent or important person, often for political or ideological reasons, usually by a surprise attack.
the killing of a prominent person

Examples

The shocking assassination of the president led to nationwide protests.
He was arrested for plotting the assassination of a political leader.
The novel explores the aftermath of a failed assassination attempt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murder - the unlawful killing of another human being

Etymology

From Medieval Latin ‘assassinatio’, from ‘assassin’, referring to a sect of secret killers in the Middle Ages.

2. NOUN

/ əˌsæsɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌsæsɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of deliberately attempting to damage someone's reputation, especially through false or malicious statements
attack on someone's reputation

Examples

The newspaper was accused of engaging in assassination of the senator's character.
Her reputation never fully recovered after the vicious assassination carried out by her political rivals.
The documentary exposes the methods used in the assassination of public figures' reputations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defamation - the act of damaging someone's good reputation; slander or libel
  • Synonym: slander - the action or crime of making a false spoken statement damaging to a person's reputation

Etymology

From French 'assassinat', from 'assassiner' (to assassinate), ultimately from Arabic 'hashshāshīn'.
