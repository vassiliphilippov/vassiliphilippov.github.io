verisimilitude

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɛrɪsɪˈmɪlɪˌtjud / (AmE), / ˌvɛrɪsɪˈmɪlɪtjuːd / (BrE)
the appearance of being true or real; the quality of seeming to be true
the appearance of truth or realism

Examples

The film's verisimilitude made viewers believe the story was based on real events.
Critics praised the novel for its verisimilitude and believable characters.
The artist strove for verisimilitude in every detail of the historical painting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: realism - the quality or fact of representing a person or situation in a way that is true to life

Etymology

From Latin verisimilitudo, from verus (true) + similitudo (likeness).
