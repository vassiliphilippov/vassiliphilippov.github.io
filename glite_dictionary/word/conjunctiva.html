conjunctiva

1. NOUN

/ kənˌdʒʌŋkˈtaɪvə / (AmE), / kɒnˌdʒʌŋkˈtʌɪvə / (BrE)
the transparent mucous membrane that lines the inner surface of the eyelids and covers the white part of the eyeball
membrane covering the eye and eyelid
medical

Examples

The doctor examined her conjunctiva for signs of infection.
Exposure to dust can irritate the conjunctiva and cause redness.
Inflammation of the conjunctiva is known as conjunctivitis.

Etymology

From Latin 'conjunctiva (membrana)', meaning 'connecting membrane'.
