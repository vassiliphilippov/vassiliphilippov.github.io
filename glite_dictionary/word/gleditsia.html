Gleditsia

1. NOUN

/ ɡlɛˈdɪtsiə / (AmE), / ɡlɛˈdɪtsiə / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous trees in the pea family, commonly known as honey locusts, native to North and South America and Asia.
genus of honey locust trees

Examples

The genus Gleditsia includes several species commonly planted in city parks.
Botanists recently discovered a new Gleditsia species in eastern Asia.
The wood of Gleditsia trees is valued for its durability and attractive grain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honey locust - a common tree in the genus Gleditsia, known for its pods and thorns

Etymology

Named after German botanist Johann Gottlieb Gleditsch (1714–1786).
