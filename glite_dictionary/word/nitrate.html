nitrate

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪˌtreɪt / (AmE), / ˈnaɪ.treɪt / (BrE)
a chemical compound containing the nitrate group, especially a salt or ester of nitric acid
chemical compound with the nitrate group

Examples

Farmers often use nitrate fertilizers to increase crop yields.
High levels of nitrate in drinking water can be harmful to health.
Ammonium nitrate is commonly used in explosives and fertilizers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: nitrite - chemical compound containing the nitrite group

Etymology

From French nitrate, from nitric (from Latin nitrum, 'saltpetre') + -ate (forming names of salts).

2. VERB

/ ˈnaɪˌtreɪt / (AmE), / ˈnʌɪtreɪt / (BrE)
to treat a substance with nitric acid in order to convert it into a nitrate, especially in a chemical process
treat with nitric acid to form a nitrate

Examples

To produce explosives, manufacturers nitrate certain organic compounds in controlled laboratory conditions.
When you nitrate cellulose, you obtain materials used in smokeless powder.
Chemists often nitrate substances to modify their chemical properties.

Synset Examples

nitroglycerin is obtained by nitrating glycerol

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nitric acid - a strong acid used in nitration reactions

Etymology

From nitrate (noun), from French 'nitrate', from nitric, relating to nitre or saltpeter.
