gadfly

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡædˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡæd.flʌɪ / (BrE)
a person who persistently annoys or provokes others, often by criticizing or challenging them
persistently annoying or provoking person

Examples

The senator was known as a political gadfly, constantly questioning established policies.
She played the role of gadfly in the meeting, never letting any issue go unchallenged.
His gadfly behavior sometimes frustrated his colleagues, but it spurred important changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuisance - someone or something causing inconvenience or annoyance
  • Synonym: provocateur - someone who provokes others, especially to action

Etymology

From gad (to go about restlessly) + fly; originally referred to an insect that bites livestock, later figuratively to a person.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡædˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡæd.flai / (BrE)
a large fly that annoys or bites livestock, such as a horsefly or botfly
large fly that annoys livestock

Examples

The presence of gadflies made the cows restless in the pasture.
Farmers often take measures to protect their animals from gadflies during the summer months.
A single gadfly can cause cattle to stampede if it continues to bite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horsefly - a type of biting fly that torments horses and cattle
  • Synonym: botfly - a parasitic fly that lays eggs on mammals

Etymology

From Middle English gadde (goad, spike) + fly, referring to the insect's irritating nature.
