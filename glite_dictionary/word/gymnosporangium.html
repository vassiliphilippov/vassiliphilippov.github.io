Gymnosporangium

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪmˌnoʊspəˈrændʒiəm / (AmE), / dʒɪmˌnɒspəˈrandʒɪəm / (BrE)
a genus of fungi that produce galls on cedar and juniper trees and cause rust diseases on apples, pears, and other plants in the rose family
genus of fungi causing rust on plants
medical

Examples

Gymnosporangium is responsible for cedar-apple rust, a common disease affecting apple orchards.
Researchers studied the life cycle of Gymnosporangium to develop disease-resistant plant varieties.
The presence of orange galls on juniper trees often signals an infection by Gymnosporangium.

Etymology

From Greek gymnos (naked) + sporangium (spore case), referring to the exposed spore-bearing structures.

2. NOUN

/ dʒɪm.nə.spɔːˈrændʒ.i.əm / (AmE), / dʒɪm.nə.spɒˈrændʒ.ɪ.əm / (BrE)
A genus of fungi that produce galls on cedar and other conifers, and cause rust spots on apples, pears, and other members of the Rosaceae family.
genus of rust fungi affecting cedars and fruit trees
medical

Examples

The fungus Gymnosporangium is responsible for causing cedar-apple rust on fruit trees.
Orchard managers monitor for Gymnosporangium infections every spring to prevent crop damage.
You can identify a Gymnosporangium infection by the orange galls it forms on juniper trees.

Etymology

From Greek 'gymnos' (naked) and 'sporangium' (spore case).
