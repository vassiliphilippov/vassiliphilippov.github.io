surcease

1. NOUN

/ sərˈsis / (AmE), / səːˈsiːs / (BrE)
the act of stopping or ending something; a cessation
a stopping; cessation

Examples

She prayed for a surcease from her pain and suffering.
The treaty brought a welcome surcease to years of conflict.
After hours of rain, there was finally a surcease and the sun broke through the clouds.

Synset Examples

a cessation of the thunder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cessation - the process of ending or being brought to an end

Etymology

From Old French *surcis*, past participle of *surseoir* (to refrain, delay), from Latin *sursedere* (to give up, cease).
