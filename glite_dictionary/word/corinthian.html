Corinthian

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrɪnθiən / (AmE), / kəˈrɪnθiən / (BrE)
A native or inhabitant of Corinth, an ancient or modern city in Greece.
person from Corinth, Greece

Examples

The ancient Corinthian was known for his wisdom and diplomatic skills.
Many Corinthians played a significant role in the spread of Christianity.
A proud Corinthian explained the city's rich history to the visitors.

Etymology

From Latin Corinthius, from Corinthus (Corinth).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈrɪnθiən / (AmE), / kəˈrɪnθɪən / (BrE)
A man devoted to the pursuit of pleasure and luxurious living, often used of wealthy or fashionable men in the 18th and 19th centuries.
pleasure-seeking man (historical)
archaic

Examples

In Regency England, a true Corinthian was renowned for his lavish lifestyle and love of entertainment.
He fancied himself a Corinthian, frequenting the most fashionable clubs and races in the city.
The novel's protagonist, a notorious Corinthian, squandered his fortune on gambling and parties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hedonist - a person who pursues pleasure as a goal

Etymology

From Latin Corinthius, relating to Corinth, associated with luxury and licentiousness in classical times.

4. ADJ

/ kəˈrɪnθiən / (AmE), / kəˈrɪnθiən / (BrE)
relating to or denoting the Corinthian order, a classical style of architecture characterized by slender columns with elaborate capitals decorated with acanthus leaves.
of the Corinthian style of architecture

Examples

The museum's entrance features towering Corinthian columns that recall the grandeur of ancient Greece.
Many architects admire the delicate details of Corinthian capitals in classical buildings.
The courthouse was built in the Corinthian style, showcasing ornate columns and intricate carvings.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Doric - relating to the Doric order, a simple classical architectural style
  • Degree Lower: Ionic - relating to the Ionic order, a classical architectural style between Doric and Corinthian

Etymology

From Latin Corinthius, from Corinthus (Corinth), an ancient Greek city, in reference to the architectural order originating there.
