Synchytrium

1. NOUN

/ sɪnˈkaɪtrɪəm / (AmE), / sɪnˈkɪtrɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of simple parasitic fungi, some of which infect pond scum and other aquatic organisms.
genus of parasitic fungi

Examples

The fungus Synchytrium can cause disease in many aquatic plants.
Researchers discovered a new species of Synchytrium infecting algae in the pond.
Proper identification of Synchytrium is essential for managing fungal outbreaks in freshwater habitats.

Etymology

From Greek syn- (together) + chytra (pot, vessel), referring to the form of the fungal sporangium.

2. NOUN

/ sɪnˈkɪtriəm / (AmE), / sɪnˈkɪtrɪəm / (BrE)
a genus of simple parasitic fungi, some of which are known to parasitize pond scum and other plants
genus of parasitic fungi

Examples

The scientist identified the pond parasite as belonging to the genus Synchytrium.
Members of Synchytrium are known for their simple structure and parasitic lifestyle.
Some species in the Synchytrium genus cause diseases in algae and higher plants.

Etymology

From Greek 'syn-' (together) + 'chytra' (pot, vessel), referring to the grouping of sporangia.
