Eupatorium purpureum

1. NOUN

/ juˌpeɪtɔriəm pɝːˈpjuriəm / (AmE), / juːˌpatɔːriəm pjʊəˈpjʊəriəm / (BrE)
A tall North American herb with whorled leaves and terminal clusters of purple-spotted flowers, commonly known as Joe-Pye weed.
a North American herbaceous plant

Examples

The garden attracted butterflies thanks to the blooming Eupatorium purpureum beside the pond.
Many herbalists value Eupatorium purpureum for its purported medicinal properties.
Clusters of purple-spotted flowers help Eupatorium purpureum stand out in the wildflower meadow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Joe-Pye weed - common name for Eupatorium purpureum

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining the genus name 'Eupatorium,' from the Greek 'Eupatorion,' and the Latin 'purpureum' (purple).
