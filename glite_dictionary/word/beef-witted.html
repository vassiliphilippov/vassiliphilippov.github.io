beef-witted

1. ADJ

/ ˈbiːf ˌwɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈbiːf ˌwɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a person) slow-witted; lacking intelligence or quickness of mind, foolish.
slow-witted or foolish (archaic)
archaic

Examples

His beef-witted remarks revealed a lack of understanding of the issue.
In old comedies, the beef-witted character often became the butt of jokes.
She called him beef-witted after he made another obvious mistake.

Synset Examples

Don't be so beef-witted; think it through carefully.
Being beef-witted won't help you solve this problem.
He made a beef-witted decision that cost him the job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foolish - lacking good sense or judgment
  • Antonym: clever - quick to understand, learn, and devise or apply ideas

Etymology

Formed from 'beef' (suggesting heaviness or dullness, as of mind) and 'witted' (having wit or intelligence).
