Fayetteville

1. PROPN

/ ˈfeɪətˌvɪl / (AmE), / ˈfeɪətˌvɪl / (BrE)
A city in south central North Carolina, USA, known as the home of Fort Liberty (formerly Fort Bragg), a major U.S. Army installation.
city in North Carolina, USA

Examples

Many military families live in Fayetteville because of its proximity to Fort Liberty.
The annual Dogwood Festival attracts visitors from across the region to Fayetteville.
She moved from Raleigh to Fayetteville for a new job opportunity.

Etymology

Named after the Marquis de Lafayette, a French hero of the American Revolutionary War.

2. PROPN

/ ˈfeɪətˌvɪl / (AmE), / ˈfeɪətˌvɪl / (BrE)
A city in northwestern Arkansas, United States, known as a university town located in the Ozark Mountains.
city in Arkansas, US; university town

Examples

The University of Arkansas is located in Fayetteville, attracting students from across the country.
After moving to Fayetteville, she quickly settled into the vibrant community.
Every fall, visitors flock to Fayetteville for the annual craft fairs and scenic mountain views.

Etymology

Named after Marquis de Lafayette, a French military officer who fought in the American Revolutionary War.
