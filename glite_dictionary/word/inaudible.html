inaudible

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈɑdəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈɔːdəbl / (BrE)
impossible to hear; not detectable by the ear.
impossible to hear

Examples

The sound of the bats was inaudible to humans without special equipment.
She whispered something inaudible under her breath as she left the room.
The recording had a section that was completely inaudible due to background noise.

Synset Examples

an inaudible conversation

Related Terms

  • Antonym: audible - able to be heard

Etymology

From Latin 'inaudibilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'audibilis' (able to be heard).
