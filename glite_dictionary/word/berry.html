berry

/ ˈbɛri / (AmE), / ˈbɛri / (BrE)

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛri / (AmE), / ˈbɛri / (BrE)
a small, usually juicy and edible fruit, typically with many seeds and without a stone, such as a strawberry, blueberry, or raspberry.
small, juicy fruit, often edible

Examples

The children picked wild berries in the forest for their dessert.
Blueberry muffins are best when made with fresh berries.
Each berry is filled with vitamins and antioxidants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fruit - the sweet and fleshy product of a tree or other plant that contains seed

Etymology

Old English 'berie', of Germanic origin; related to Dutch 'bes' and German 'Beere'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɛri / (AmE), / ˈbɛri / (BrE)
a small, round, and typically edible fruit that is usually juicy and often used in desserts, jams, or preserves
small edible fruit, often juicy

Examples

The recipe calls for fresh berries and a sprinkle of sugar.
She picked a berry from the bush and tasted it cautiously.
Many jams are made from summer berries like strawberries and raspberries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fruit - the sweet and fleshy product of a tree or other plant that contains seed and can be eaten as food

Etymology

Old English 'berie', of Germanic origin; related to Dutch 'bes' and German 'Beere'.
