Diplodocus

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈploʊdəkəs / (AmE), / dɪˈplɒdəkəs / (BrE)
a large, long-necked herbivorous dinosaur of the genus Diplodocus, which lived during the Late Jurassic period
herbivorous Jurassic dinosaur genus

Examples

The skeleton of a Diplodocus dominates the museum's main hall.
Many scientists have studied the long neck of the Diplodocus to understand its eating habits.
Children are often fascinated by the enormous size of the Diplodocus in dinosaur exhibitions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sauropod - a large, long-necked herbivorous dinosaur

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, from Greek diploos (double) + dokos (beam), referring to the double-beamed bones in its tail.
