Rhodophyceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌroʊdəˈfaɪsiːi / (AmE), / ˌrəʊdəˈfaɪsiːiː / (BrE)
a large class of red algae, coextensive with the Rhodophyta, found mainly in marine environments
class of red algae

Examples

Many species of Rhodophyceae are important sources of agar and carrageenan.
Scientists study the Rhodophyceae for their role in coral reef ecosystems.
The brilliant red color of Rhodophyceae is due to unique pigments in their cells.

Etymology

From Greek 'rhodon' (rose) + 'phyton' (plant), referring to the red color of the algae.

2. NOUN

/ ˌroʊdəˈfaɪsiˌiː / (AmE), / ˌrəʊdəˈfʌɪsiːiː / (BrE)
a class of red algae, coextensive with the division Rhodophyta, found chiefly in marine environments
class of red algae

Examples

Researchers discovered several new species belonging to the Rhodophyceae in the coastal waters.
The pigments found in Rhodophyceae allow them to photosynthesize efficiently at greater depths.
Many edible seaweeds, such as nori, are actually members of the Rhodophyceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red algae - photosynthetic algae of the phylum Rhodophyta
  • Degree Lower: Chlorophyceae - class of green algae
  • Degree Lower: Phaeophyceae - class of brown algae

Etymology

From Greek 'rhodon' (rose, red) + 'phykos' (seaweed), with scientific Latin '-eae' indicating a class.
