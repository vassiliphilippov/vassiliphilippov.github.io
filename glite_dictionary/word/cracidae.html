Cracidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈkræsəˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈkræsɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of large, mainly arboreal birds native to Central and South America, including curassows, guans, and chachalacas
family of birds: curassows, guans, chachalacas

Examples

The rainforests of South America are home to many species in the Cracidae family.
Ornithologists study the behavior and habitat of Cracidae throughout the tropics.
Some members of the Cracidae are threatened by habitat loss and hunting.

Etymology

From modern Latin Cracidae, from Cracinae (a genus of birds) + '-idae' (zoological family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkræsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈkræsɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of large, mainly tropical American birds including curassows, guans, and chachalacas
family of tropical American birds (curassows, guans, chachalacas)

Examples

The Cracidae are known for their loud calls and striking plumage.
Several species within the Cracidae are threatened due to habitat destruction.
Scientists study the Cracidae to understand tropical forest ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curassow - large, pheasant-like tropical American bird
  • Synonym: guan - tree-dwelling bird of Central and South America
  • Synonym: chachalaca - noisy, tree-living bird of the Cracidae family

Etymology

From New Latin Cracidae, based on Cracinae (curassows) plus the family suffix -idae.
