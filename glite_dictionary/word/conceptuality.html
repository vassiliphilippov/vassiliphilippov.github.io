conceptuality

1. NOUN

/ kənˌsɛp.tʃuˈæ.lə.ti / (AmE), / kənˌsɛp.tjuˈæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being conceptual; the degree to which something is characterized by, or based on, concepts or abstract ideas
quality of being conceptual

Examples

The conceptuality of her thesis impressed the examination committee.
Some critics find his paintings lacking in conceptuality and too focused on technique.
Philosophy often values conceptuality over empirical observation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abstraction - the quality of dealing with ideas rather than events

Etymology

From conceptual + -ity, after French conceptualité.
