cleft

1. NOUN

/ klɛft / (AmE), / klɛft / (BrE)
a narrow opening or crack, especially in rock or the ground; a fissure or split
narrow opening or crack

Examples

A narrow cleft in the rock allowed sunlight to seep into the cave.
Water trickled down the cleft between the two mountain peaks.
He lost his footing and almost slipped into the cleft in the ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fissure - a long, narrow opening or crack
  • Synonym: gap - an opening or break in something

Etymology

From Old English *clift*, related to *cleofan* (to split).

divided or split, especially along a natural line or into two or more parts, often used to describe anatomical features or objects

3. NOUN

/ klɛft / (AmE), / klɛft / (BrE)
A natural split, crack, or indentation in a surface, especially one occurring in the palate or chin.
split or indentation, especially in palate or chin

Examples

He was born with a cleft palate that required surgery.
The actor's strong jawline was marked by a distinctive cleft in his chin.
A narrow cleft in the rock allowed plants to take root.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fissure - A long, narrow opening or line of breakage made by cracking or splitting, especially in rock or earth.
  • Synonym: groove - A long, narrow cut or depression in a surface.

Etymology

From Old English *cleofan* (to split), related to Old High German *klebhan*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13931215-n
Synset POS: n

4. ADJ

/ klɛft / (AmE), / klɛft / (BrE)
(of a leaf) having one or more deep indentations reaching nearly to the midrib.
having deep indentations to the midrib (botanical)

Examples

The maple tree's leaves are distinctly cleft, giving them a characteristic shape.
Some ferns display cleft fronds that appear almost divided into separate segments.
The botanist pointed out the cleft leaves as a key feature of the species.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: lobed - (of a leaf) having distinct protruding divisions but not reaching the midrib

Etymology

From Old English *cleofan* (to split), related to 'cleave.'
