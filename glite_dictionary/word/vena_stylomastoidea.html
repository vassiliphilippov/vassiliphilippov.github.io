vena stylomastoidea

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪ.nə ˌstaɪ.loʊ.mæˈstoɪ.di.ə / (AmE), / ˈviː.nə ˌstaɪ.ləʊ.mæsˈtɔɪ.diː.ə / (BrE)
A vein that drains blood from the tympanic cavity and empties into the retromandibular vein.
vein draining the tympanic cavity
medical

Examples

The vena stylomastoidea plays a crucial role in draining the middle ear cavity.
During surgery near the ear, the surgeon must take care not to damage the vena stylomastoidea.
Inflammation of the tympanic cavity may affect the flow through the vena stylomastoidea.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: retromandibular vein - a vein receiving blood from the face and scalp, into which the vena stylomastoidea empties

Etymology

From Latin: 'vena' meaning 'vein' and 'stylomastoidea' referring to the stylomastoid (region/process) of the temporal bone.
