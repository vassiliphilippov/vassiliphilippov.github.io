imperishableness

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɛr.ɪ.ʃə.bəl.nəs / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛr.ɪ.ʃə.bəl.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of not being able to decay or be destroyed
quality of being unable to decay

Examples

The imperishableness of gold makes it valuable for making jewelry and coins.
Many ancient myths speak of the imperishableness of the gods.
He praised the imperishableness of the synthetic materials used in the construction.

Synset Examples

he advertised the imperishability of the product

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imperishability - the quality of being imperishable; inability to perish
  • Antonym: perishableness - the quality of being likely to decay or perish

Etymology

From Latin 'im-' (not) + 'perire' (to perish) + English '-ness'.
