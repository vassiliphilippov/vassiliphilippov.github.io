venue

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛnju / (AmE), / ˈvɛnjuː / (BrE)
(Law) The jurisdiction or location where a trial is conducted or a legal case is heard.
legal location of a trial or hearing
legal

Examples

The defense attorney filed a motion to change the venue due to pretrial publicity.
Determining the proper venue is crucial for ensuring a fair trial.
Jurisdiction and venue are often addressed in the opening statements of a case.

Etymology

From Old French 'venue' (a coming), from feminine past participle of 'venir', from Latin 'venire' (to come).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɛnju / (AmE), / ˈvɛnjuː / (BrE)
A place where an event or meeting takes place, especially a public one such as a concert, sports game, or conference.
place where an event occurs

Examples

The band will perform at a larger venue next year to accommodate more fans.
The conference organizers were searching for the perfect venue downtown.
Due to the rain, the wedding venue was changed at the last minute.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: location - a particular place or position
  • Synonym: site - an area or place where something is happening or has happened

Etymology

From Old French 'venue' (a coming), from venir (to come), from Latin 'venīre'.
