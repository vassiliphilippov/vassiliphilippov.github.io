Prunus virginiana

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs vɜrˌdʒɪˈneɪn(i)ə / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs vɜːˌdʒɪˈneɪnɪə / (BrE)
a species of wild cherry native to eastern North America, known for its small, bitter black berries favored by birds
wild cherry species native to North America

Examples

The berries of Prunus virginiana are often used to make jams and jellies despite their bitterness.
Birds were feasting on the ripening fruit of the Prunus virginiana tree in early summer.
Wild stands of Prunus virginiana can sometimes form dense thickets along roadsides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chokecherry - common name for Prunus virginiana

Etymology

From Latin: 'Prunus' (plum or cherry genus) and 'virginiana' (of Virginia).
