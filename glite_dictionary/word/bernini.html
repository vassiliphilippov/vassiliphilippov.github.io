Bernini

1. PROPN

/ bɛrˈniːni / (AmE), / bɛˈniːni / (BrE)
(1598–1680) Italian sculptor and architect of the Baroque period, known for designing churches, chapels, tombs, and fountains in Italy.
Italian Baroque sculptor and architect

Examples

The art historian gave a lecture about Bernini and his influence on Baroque sculpture.
Many tourists visit Rome to admire the fountains designed by Bernini.
Bernini's masterpieces are celebrated for their dramatic movement and emotional expression.

Etymology

Italian surname; from the name of the Bernini family.
