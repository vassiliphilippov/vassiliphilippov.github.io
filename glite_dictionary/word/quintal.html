quintal

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪn.təl / (AmE), / ˈkwɪn.tl̩ / (BrE)
a unit of weight equal to 100 kilograms, used especially in certain countries for agricultural products
unit of weight, 100 kilograms

Examples

The farmer harvested 20 quintals of wheat this season.
Rice is often sold in quintal units in many parts of the world.
The annual production reached five quintals per hectare.

Etymology

From Latin 'quintale', from Arabic 'qintar', from Late Latin 'centenarium' (hundredweight).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪn.tl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkwɪn.təl / (BrE)
A unit of weight used in the United States, equal to 100 pounds.
U.S. unit of weight (100 pounds)

Examples

The price of wheat is often quoted per quintal in the United States.
Farmers stored several quintals of grain in their barns for the winter.
Each bag weighed exactly one quintal, making it easy to calculate the total shipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hundredweight - a unit of weight equal to 100 pounds (U.S.) or 112 pounds (British)

Etymology

From Latin 'quintale', from Arabic 'qintar', from Late Greek 'kentēnarion' (hundredweight), ultimately from Latin 'centenarium'.
