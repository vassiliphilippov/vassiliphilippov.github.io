smallholding

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmɔlˌhoʊldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsmɔːlˌhəʊldɪŋ / (BrE)
a piece of land, typically under 50 acres, sold or rented to an individual or family for cultivation
a small farm for cultivation

Examples

They bought a smallholding in the countryside to start organic farming.
Managing a smallholding requires a lot of dedication and hands-on work.
The government encouraged young families to move to smallholdings for self-sufficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farm - an area of land used for growing crops or keeping animals
  • Degree Higher: plantation - a large piece of land for cultivating crops, especially in the tropics

Etymology

Formed from 'small' + 'holding', where 'holding' refers to land held for farming.
