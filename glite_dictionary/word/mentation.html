mentation

1. NOUN

/ mɛnˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / mɛnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of thinking or using the mind, especially when considering something carefully
the process of thinking

Examples

During meditation, the flow of mentation becomes slower and more focused.
Some medications can alter normal mentation, leading to confusion or drowsiness.
Creative mentation often leads to innovative solutions for complex problems.

Synset Examples

thinking always made him frown
she paused for thought

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thought - the process of thinking; an idea or opinion produced by thinking
  • Synonym: cognition - the mental action or process of acquiring knowledge and understanding through thought

Etymology

From Latin 'mentatio', from 'mens' meaning 'mind'.
