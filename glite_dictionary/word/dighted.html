dighted

1. ADJ

/ ˈdaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈdaɪtɪd / (BrE)
dressed or adorned, especially in preparation for a specific event or occasion (archaic or literary use)
dressed or adorned (archaic/literary)
archaic

Examples

The young knight was dighted in shining armor before the battle.
In the poem, the maidens are dighted in garments of pure white.
He entered the hall dighted in all the riches his family could provide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adorned - decorated or made more beautiful
  • Antonym: disrobed - undressed or stripped of clothing

Etymology

From Middle English 'dighten', from Old English 'dihtan' (to arrange, treat, compose), influenced by Latin 'dictare'.
