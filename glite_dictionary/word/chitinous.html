chitinous

1. ADJ

/ ˈkaɪtənəs / (AmE), / ˈkɪtɪnəs / (BrE)
Consisting of or resembling chitin; having the nature of chitin.
consisting of chitin; chitin-like

Examples

The insect’s outer shell is chitinous and provides excellent protection.
Crabs and lobsters have chitinous exoskeletons that must be shed for growth.
The scientist examined the chitinous layer under a microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chitin - a tough, semitransparent substance that is the main component of the exoskeletons of arthropods

Etymology

From chitin + -ous, modeled after French chitiné.
