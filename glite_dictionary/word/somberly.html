somberly

1. ADV

/ ˈsɑːmbɚli / (AmE), / ˈsɒmbəli / (BrE)
In a serious, sad, or solemn manner.
in a serious or solemn manner

Examples

He spoke somberly about the challenges facing the community.
She somberly delivered the news to the team.
The crowd listened somberly as the names of the victims were read aloud.

Synset Examples

‘That's sure bad news,’ said Dowd, somberly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solemnly - in a formal and dignified manner
  • Antonym: cheerfully - in a happy and optimistic way

Etymology

From "somber" + "-ly"; "somber" from French "sombre," from Latin "subumbrare" (to shade).
