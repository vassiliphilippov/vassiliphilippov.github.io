Pelecaniformes

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛlɪˌkænəˈfɔrˌmiːz / (AmE), / ˌpɛlɪˌkænɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of large water birds including pelicans, cormorants, frigate birds, and gannets
order of large water birds

Examples

The Pelecaniformes are known for their distinctive beaks and aquatic habits.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of several bird families within the Pelecaniformes.
Pelicans and cormorants, both members of the Pelecaniformes, share similar nesting areas.

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining 'Pelecanus' (the pelican genus) and '-formes' (having the form of).
