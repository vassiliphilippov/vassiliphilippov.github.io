celebration

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛləˈbreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛlɪˈbreɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the public performance of a sacrament or solemn ceremony with all the required rituals
performance of a sacrament or ritual

Examples

The celebration of marriage was conducted with great solemnity in the ancient church.
The Easter vigil is an important celebration in the Christian calendar.
Their family attended the celebration of the baptism last Sunday.

Synset Examples

the celebration of marriage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ceremony - a formal religious or public occasion, typically one celebrating a particular event

Etymology

From Latin celebratio (a frequent assembly, thronging, celebrating), from celeber (frequented, much-visited, celebrated).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛl.əˈbreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛl.ɪˈbreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an enjoyable activity or event held to mark a special occasion or simply for happiness; a festivity or joyous diversion
joyful event; festivity

Examples

The city organized a celebration to mark its 100th anniversary.
Their wedding was a joyful celebration with friends and family.
After the victory, the streets were filled with music and celebration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: festivity - a joyful event or celebration
  • Synonym: party - a social gathering for enjoyment
  • Antonym: mourning - expression of grief or sorrow, often after loss

Etymology

From Latin celebratio (a frequent honoring) via Old French célébration.

3. NOUN

/ ˌsɛl.əˈbreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛl.ɪˈbreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a joyful event or gathering held to mark a special occasion or achievement
joyful event marking a special occasion

Examples

The whole family gathered for a celebration of her graduation.
They organized a surprise celebration for his 50th birthday.
Fireworks filled the sky during the New Year's Eve celebration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: festivity - a lively and enjoyable celebration
  • Synonym: commemoration - a ceremony or event to remember and celebrate an occasion
  • Antonym: mourning - the expression of deep sorrow; opposite in mood to celebration

Etymology

From Latin celebratio (frequent assembly, celebration), from celebrare (to celebrate).
