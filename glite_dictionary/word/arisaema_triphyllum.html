Arisaema triphyllum

1. NOUN

/ ˌærəˈsiːmə trɪˈfɪləm / (AmE), / ˌærɪˈsiːmə trɪˈfɪləm / (BrE)
A North American woodland plant, also known as Jack-in-the-pulpit, with sheathing leaves, an upright club-shaped spadix, and a green and purple spathe producing scarlet berries.
North American woodland plant (Jack-in-the-pulpit)

Examples

Native bees are attracted to the distinctive flowers of Arisaema triphyllum each spring.
Many hikers find Arisaema triphyllum growing in shaded, moist forests across eastern North America.
The bright red berries of Arisaema triphyllum appear in late summer after the flowering season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jack-in-the-pulpit - Common name for Arisaema triphyllum

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name 'Arisaema' from Greek 'aron' (araceous plant) + 'haima' (blood), and Latin 'triphyllum' (three-leaved).
