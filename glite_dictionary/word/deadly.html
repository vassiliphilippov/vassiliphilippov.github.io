deadly

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛdli / (AmE), / ˈdɛdli / (BrE)
(of a disease) progressing rapidly and causing severe or fatal effects.
rapidly fatal or severe disease

Examples

The spread of the deadly virus prompted urgent action from health officials.
Doctors struggled to contain the deadly outbreak in the rural region.
New treatments have reduced mortality from what was once a deadly disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lethal - causing death; capable of causing death

Etymology

From Old English deadlīc, from dead +‎ -ly.
