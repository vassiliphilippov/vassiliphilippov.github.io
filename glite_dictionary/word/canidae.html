Canidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈkænɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈkanɪdiː / (BrE)
the biological family of carnivorous mammals that includes dogs, wolves, foxes, jackals, and related animals
family of animals: dogs, wolves, foxes, etc.

Examples

Members of the Canidae family include wolves, foxes, and domestic dogs.
Researchers compared the skull shapes of various Canidae species to understand their evolution.
The Canidae are widespread and adapt to a variety of habitats across the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canine - relating to or resembling dogs

Etymology

From Latin 'canis' (dog), with scientific suffix '-idae' indicating a family in zoological taxonomy.
