Orycteropodidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːrɪktəˈrɑːpədəˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌɒrɪktəˈpɒdɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of mammals comprising the aardvarks, native to Africa and characterized by burrowing habits and a diet mainly of ants and termites.
family of mammals: aardvarks

Examples

The Orycteropodidae family contains only a single living species, the aardvark.
Researchers study the Orycteropodidae to understand evolutionary adaptations for digging.
Fossil records suggest that Orycteropodidae were once more widespread in ancient Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aardvark - A nocturnal burrowing mammal native to Africa, the single living species in Orycteropodidae

Etymology

From Greek oryktēr (digger) + pous, podos (foot), with family suffix -idae.
