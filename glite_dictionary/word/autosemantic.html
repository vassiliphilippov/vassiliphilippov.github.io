autosemantic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɔtoʊsɪˈmæntɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəʊsɪˈmæntɪk / (BrE)
(of a word or phrase) having meaning independently and can stand alone without needing context.
meaningful in isolation

Examples

A noun is typically an autosemantic word because it expresses meaning on its own.
Unlike conjunctions, which are synsemantic, verbs are usually autosemantic.
She explained that articles are not autosemantic, as they need other words to convey meaning.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: synsemantic - (of a word or phrase) meaningful only in context; dependent on other words

Etymology

From Greek auto- (self) and semantic (relating to meaning).
