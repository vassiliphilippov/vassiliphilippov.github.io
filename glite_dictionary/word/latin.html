Latin

1. NOUN

/ ˈlætən / (AmE), / ˈlatɪn / (BrE)
A person who belongs to the group of peoples whose languages are derived from Latin, such as Italians, French, Spanish, Portuguese, and Romanians.
member of a Latin-derived language group

Examples

Many Latins share a common cultural heritage rooted in Roman civilization.
She is proud to be a Latin, tracing her ancestry back to Spain.
Throughout history, Latins have contributed greatly to art, science, and literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Romance - relating to the group of languages derived from Latin

Etymology

From Latin 'Latinus' (of Latium), referring to the region around Rome.
