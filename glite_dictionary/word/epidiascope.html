epidiascope

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈdaɪ.əˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdʌɪ.ə.skəʊp / (BrE)
an optical projector designed to display images of both transparent and opaque objects onto a screen
projector for transparent and opaque images

Examples

The teacher used an epidiascope to display pages from the textbook for the entire class.
Old laboratories often featured an epidiascope for projecting both slides and physical objects.
Before digital projectors, an epidiascope was a valuable tool for scientific demonstrations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opaque projector - a projector for displaying images of opaque objects
  • Degree Lower: overhead projector - a projector for displaying transparent objects, especially sheets

Etymology

From Greek 'epi' (upon) + 'dia' (through) + 'skopein' (to look).
