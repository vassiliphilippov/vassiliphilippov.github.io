Chamaecyparis nootkatensis

1. NOUN

/ kəˌmiːsɪˈpɛrɪs nuːt.kəˈtɛn.sɪs / (AmE), / kaˌmɛsɪˈpærɪs nuːt.kəˈtɛn.sɪs / (BrE)
a tall evergreen tree native to the Pacific coast of North America, often cultivated for ornamental purposes; also known as Nootka cypress or Alaska cedar
evergreen tree of NW America (Nootka cypress)

Examples

The Chamaecyparis nootkatensis is prized for its durability and resistance to decay in coastal gardens.
Many parks in the region feature mature Chamaecyparis nootkatensis as ornamental trees.
Carpenters often use wood from the Chamaecyparis nootkatensis for specialty woodworking and outdoor furniture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nootka cypress - common name for Chamaecyparis nootkatensis
  • Synonym: Alaska cedar - another common name for Chamaecyparis nootkatensis

Etymology

From Greek khamai (on the ground) and kuparissos (cypress), combined with the Nootka region of British Columbia.
