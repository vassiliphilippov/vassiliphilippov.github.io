landgrave

1. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌɡreɪv / (AmE), / ˈlanɡreɪv / (BrE)
A nobleman of high rank in certain German states, who had jurisdiction over a large territory, chiefly during the medieval period.
nobleman with jurisdiction in medieval Germany

Examples

The landgrave exercised considerable authority over the surrounding region during the Middle Ages.
After the death of the last landgrave, the territory was divided among several other nobles.
She studied the history of the landgrave and the role he played in shaping medieval German politics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: count - a nobleman ranking below a marquis and above a viscount

Etymology

From German 'Landgraf', from 'Land' (land) + 'Graf' (count).
