disaccharide

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈsækəˌraɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈsæk.ə.raɪd / (BrE)
a carbohydrate composed of two monosaccharide units joined together, which yield two monosaccharide molecules upon complete hydrolysis
carbohydrate made of two monosaccharides

Examples

Lactose is a common disaccharide found in milk.
During digestion, a disaccharide is broken down into two simple sugars.
Sucrose, a familiar table sugar, is a type of disaccharide composed of glucose and fructose.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: monosaccharide - a simple sugar molecule; the basic unit of carbohydrates
  • Degree Higher: polysaccharide - a carbohydrate made of many monosaccharide units

Etymology

From di- (two) and saccharide (sugar), from Greek sakkharon (sugar).
