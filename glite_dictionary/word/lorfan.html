Lorfan

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔːrˌfæn / (AmE), / ˈlɔːfæn / (BrE)
A trade name for a drug related to morphine that counteracts the respiratory depression caused by morphine overdose without affecting its pain-relieving effects.
drug used to reverse morphine's respiratory effects
medical

Examples

The doctor administered Lorfan to reverse the effects of the morphine overdose.
Emergency protocols call for the use of Lorfan in cases of respiratory depression caused by opioids.
While Lorfan counteracts dangerous side effects, it does not reduce the pain relief provided by morphine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nalorphine - a drug used as an antidote to morphine poisoning
  • Degree Higher: morphine - an opioid painkiller
