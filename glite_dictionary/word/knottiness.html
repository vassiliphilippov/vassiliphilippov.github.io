knottiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːt̬i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈnɒt.i.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being difficult to understand or solve; extreme complexity or intricacy.
state of complexity or difficulty

Examples

The knottiness of the legal arguments made the case difficult for the jury to follow.
She admired the knottiness of classic mystery novels, enjoying their intricate puzzles.
The historian discussed the knottiness inherent in interpreting ancient manuscripts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complexity - the state of being intricate or complicated
  • Antonym: simplicity - the quality of being easy to understand or do

Etymology

Formed from 'knotty' + '-ness'. 'Knotty' ultimately derives from Old English 'cnotta' (knot).
