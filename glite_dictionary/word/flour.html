flour

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈflaʊə / (BrE)
A fine powder made by grinding grain, especially wheat, and used to make bread, cakes, and other foods.
powdered grain used in cooking

Examples

She sifted the flour before adding it to the cake mixture.
Many breads are made from wheat flour.
A dusting of flour covered the kitchen counter after baking.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: meal - coarse ground grain, less finely ground than flour
  • Degree Lower: wholemeal flour - flour made from the whole grain, less refined

Etymology

From Old French 'fleur' (flower, finest part of meal), from Latin 'flos' (flower).

2. VERB

/ ˈflaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈflaʊə / (BrE)
to convert grain into flour by grinding or milling
convert grain into flour

Examples

The millers flour the wheat using traditional stone grinders.
Centuries ago, farmers would flour their own grain at local mills.
Some small bakeries still flour grains themselves to create unique textures.

Etymology

From Old French *fleur* (flower, finest part), later meaning the finest ground meal, ultimately from Latin *flos* (flower).

3. VERB

/ ˈflaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈflaʊə / (BrE)
To cover or coat (food) with flour, typically before cooking.
coat with flour (cooking)

Examples

Remember to flour the chicken pieces before adding them to the hot oil.
You should always flour the counter before rolling out the dough.
The chef floured each fillet to ensure a crisp, golden crust.

Synset Examples

flour fish or meat before frying it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dust - to sprinkle a light powdery layer over something

Etymology

From Old French 'fleur' (flower, the best part), later referring to the finest part of meal.
