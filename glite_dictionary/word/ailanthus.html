Ailanthus

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈlænθəs / (AmE), / aɪˈlanθəs / (BrE)
A small genus of East Asian and Chinese trees known for their odd-pinnate leaves and long, twisted winged seeds (samaras), including the tree of heaven.
genus of East Asian/Chinese trees (tree of heaven etc.)

Examples

The Ailanthus is often called the tree of heaven in urban landscapes.
Several cities have planted Ailanthus trees because they tolerate poor soil and pollution.
The distinctive samaras of Ailanthus make it easy to identify during the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree of heaven - commonly known species in the genus Ailanthus, noted for rapid growth

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on the Moluccan (Indonesian) word 'ailanto' meaning 'tree of the gods' or 'tree that reaches the sky.'
