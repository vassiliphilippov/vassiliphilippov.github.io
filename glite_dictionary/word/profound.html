profound

1. ADJ

/ prəˈfaʊnd / (AmE), / prəˈfaʊnd / (BrE)
having a very great and far-reaching effect; thoroughgoing or significant in influence or change
of deep or far-reaching effect

Examples

The discovery had a profound impact on our understanding of the universe.
Her research led to profound changes in the field.
The reforms brought about profound improvements in education.

Synset Examples

the fundamental revolution in human values that has occurred
the book underwent fundamental changes
committed the fundamental error of confusing spending with extravagance
profound social changes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fundamental - forming a necessary base or core; of central importance

Etymology

From Latin profundus (deep), from pro- (forth) + fundus (bottom).

2. ADJ

/ prəˈfaʊnd / (AmE), / prəˈfaʊnd / (BrE)
(of a place or thing) situated or extending to a great depth; very deep or unfathomable.
very deep; extending far down

Examples

The submarine disappeared into the profound depths of the ocean.
Divers explored the profound caves hidden beneath the coastline.
He gazed into the profound darkness of the old well, unable to see the bottom.

Synset Examples

unplumbed depths of the sea
remote and unsounded caverns

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shallow - of little depth; not deep

Etymology

From Latin profundus (deep, bottomless), from pro- (forth) + fundus (bottom).
