coastguard

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊstˌɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˈkəʊstˌɡɑːd / (BrE)
an official organization responsible for the safety and security of people and ships at sea, especially near coasts
maritime safety and rescue service

Examples

The coastguard launched a rescue mission after receiving reports of a capsized boat near the harbor.
Local fishermen praised the coastguard for patrolling the waters during the storm.
In case of an emergency at sea, you should contact the coastguard immediately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lifeguard - person responsible for rescuing swimmers at a beach or pool
  • Synonym: navy - the branch of a nation's armed forces that conducts military operations at sea
  • Antonym: smuggler - a person who illegally transports goods, often at sea; an opposite role to coastguard

Etymology

Formed from 'coast' + 'guard,' first used in the early 19th century in reference to British and American services.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊstˌɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˈkəʊstˌɡɑːd / (BrE)
a person whose job is to protect and assist people at sea as part of an official organization along the coast
member of the coastguard

Examples

The coastguard rescued three sailors from a sinking boat last night.
As a coastguard, he patrols the shoreline every morning checking for signs of trouble.
A retired coastguard recalled dramatic rescues during stormy weather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lifeguard - a person responsible for the safety of swimmers at a beach or pool
  • Synonym: coastguard - the organization responsible for patrolling and protecting a country's coastal waters

Etymology

From 'coast' and 'guard', originally referring to those guarding coastlines.
