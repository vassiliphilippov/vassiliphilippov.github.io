to handcolour

1. VERB

/ ˈhændˌkʌlər / (AmE), / ˈhændˌkʌlə / (BrE)
to add color to a photograph, print, or illustration by hand, typically using paint or tint.
manually add color to an image or print
british

Examples

The artist decided to handcolour the map to make its details stand out.
They handcoloured black-and-white postcards to give them a vintage look.
Each print was carefully handcoloured to achieve a unique, vibrant effect.

Synset Examples

Some old photographs are handcolored

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hand-color - to add color to an image or print by hand
