Pinopsida

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈnɑpsɪdə / (AmE), / pɪˈnɒpsɪdə / (BrE)
A class of gymnospermous plants that includes most conifers; sometimes treated as a subdivision (Pinophytina) in plant classification.
class of conifers (plants)
medical

Examples

The majority of trees in the northern forests belong to the Pinopsida.
Members of the Pinopsida include pines, spruces, and firs.
Scientists study Pinopsida to understand the evolution of gymnosperms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conifer - A tree that bears cones and needle-like leaves, such as a pine.
  • Degree Higher: gymnosperm - A plant group with unenclosed seeds, including conifers.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Latin 'pinus' (pine) + '-opsida' (a suffix for plant classes).

2. NOUN

/ paɪˈnɑpsɪdə / (AmE), / pɪˈnɒpsɪdə / (BrE)
A class of mostly coniferous trees and shrubs within the gymnosperms, including pines, firs, and their relatives, recognized in some botanical classification systems.
class of coniferous trees and shrubs
medical

Examples

The Pinopsida include familiar trees such as pines, spruces, and firs.
Biologists have identified over six hundred species within the Pinopsida.
The structural diversity of Pinopsida has ecological importance in many forest ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conifer - a tree that bears cones and evergreen needlelike or scalelike leaves

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Pinus' (pine) and Greek '-opsis' (appearance or form).
