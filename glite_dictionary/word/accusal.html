accusal

1. NOUN

/ əˈkjuːzəl / (AmE), / əˈkjuːzəl / (BrE)
A formal accusation or charge of wrongdoing made against someone; the act of assigning blame or guilt.
formal charge of wrongdoing; accusation

Examples

The police made an accusal of theft against the suspect after reviewing the evidence.
Her public accusal led to a lengthy investigation by the authorities.
He vehemently denied every accusal that was brought before the court.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accusation - a formal charge or assertion that someone has done something wrong

Etymology

From 'accuse' + '-al', patterned after related nouns like 'refusal'.
