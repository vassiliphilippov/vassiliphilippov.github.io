Hemachatus haemachatus

1. NOUN

/ hɛməˈkeɪtəs hɛməˈkeɪtəs / (AmE), / hɛməˈkeɪtəs hɛməˈkeɪtəs / (BrE)
a highly venomous snake of southern Africa, also known as the rinkhals, able to spit its venom for defense
venomous spitting snake of southern Africa

Examples

The Hemachatus haemachatus can spit its venom at predators as a defense mechanism.
Researchers studied the behavior of the Hemachatus haemachatus in its natural habitat.
Farmers in southern Africa are often wary of the Hemachatus haemachatus because of its potent venom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rinkhals - another name for Hemachatus haemachatus, a spitting snake

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) and Latinized form used in zoological nomenclature.
