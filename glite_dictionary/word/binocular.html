binocular

1. ADJ

/ bɪˈnɑkjələr / (AmE), / bɪˈnɒkjʊlə / (BrE)
relating to both eyes or to vision using both eyes together
relating to both eyes

Examples

Owls have excellent binocular vision, allowing them to judge distance accurately when hunting.
Damage to either eye can affect binocular depth perception in humans.
Certain visual tests are designed to measure binocular coordination and acuity.

Synset Examples

binocular vision

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monocular - relating to only one eye or vision in one eye

Etymology

From Latin 'bini' (two each) + 'ocularis' (of the eye).
