Altair

1. PROPN

/ ælˈtɛr / (AmE), / ælˈteə / (BrE)
the brightest star in the constellation Aquila, located about 15.7 light years from Earth
brightest star in Aquila constellation

Examples

Many summer nights, the bright light of Altair is visible high in the eastern sky.
Altair is part of the famous Summer Triangle along with Vega and Deneb.
Astronomers use Altair as a reference point when studying the Aquila constellation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vega - brightest star in the constellation Lyra
  • Synonym: Deneb - brightest star in the constellation Cygnus

Etymology

From Arabic النسر الطائر (al-nasr al-ṭā'ir), meaning 'the flying eagle'.
