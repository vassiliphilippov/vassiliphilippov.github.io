to elucidate

1. VERB

/ ɪˈluːsɪˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈluːsɪdeɪt / (BrE)
to make something clear or easy to understand; to explain more fully
make clear; explain

Examples

The professor was able to elucidate some of the more difficult concepts in the lecture.
Can you elucidate your plan so everyone understands the steps involved?
Her research helped to elucidate the underlying causes of the disease.

Synset Examples

Could you clarify these remarks?
Clear up the question of who is at fault

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clarify - to make (a statement or situation) less confused and more comprehensible
  • Antonym: obscure - to make something unclear or difficult to understand

Etymology

From Latin elucidare (to make clear), from ex- (out) + lucidus (clear, bright).

2. VERB

/ ɪˈluːsɪˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈluːsɪdeɪt / (BrE)
to make something clear; to explain or clarify
to explain or make clear

Examples

The professor tried to elucidate the complex theory for his students.
Could you elucidate your point a bit more clearly for the audience?
Her explanation helped to elucidate the confusing instructions.

Synset Examples

clarify the mystery surrounding her death

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to clarify - to make something less confused and more clearly comprehensible
  • Antonym: obscure - to make something less clear or difficult to understand

Etymology

From Latin 'elucidare', from 'e-' (out) + 'lucidus' (lucid, clear).
