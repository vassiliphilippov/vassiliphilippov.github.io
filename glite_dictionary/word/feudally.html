feudally

1. ADV

/ ˈfjuːdəl.i / (AmE), / ˈfjuːdəli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to or resembles the social, economic, or political system of feudalism.
in a feudal manner

Examples

The land was governed feudally for centuries, with lords granting plots to vassals.
Taxes were collected feudally, giving much power to the local nobility.
Many rural areas in the region were still organized feudally in the 18th century.

Synset Examples

a feudally organized society

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feudal - relating to the social system of feudalism

Etymology

From "feudal," modeled after the noun "feudalism," with the adverbial suffix "-ly."
