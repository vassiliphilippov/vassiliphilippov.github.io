caryatid

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæriˈætɪd / (AmE), / ˌkærɪˈætɪd / (BrE)
a supporting column or pillar carved in the form of a draped female figure, used in classical architecture
pillar shaped like a woman

Examples

The entrance was flanked by a magnificent caryatid supporting the portico.
The museum's collection includes a marble caryatid from the ancient temple.
Architects admired the graceful proportions of the caryatid pillars.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: atlas - a supporting column in the form of a man, in architecture

Etymology

From Greek karyatides, meaning 'maidens of Karyai', an ancient town in Laconia.
