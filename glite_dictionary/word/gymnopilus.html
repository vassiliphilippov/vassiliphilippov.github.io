Gymnopilus

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪmˈnɑpɪləs / (AmE), / dʒɪmˈnɒpɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi characterized by an orange spore deposit, commonly found on wood and containing several notable species.
genus of orange-spored fungi

Examples

The mushroom she collected belonged to the Gymnopilus genus, distinguished by its rust-colored spores.
Identifying Gymnopilus species can be challenging due to their similarity to other orange-capped fungi.
Researchers discovered a new Gymnopilus species growing on decaying logs in the forest.

Etymology

From Greek 'gymnos' (naked) + 'pilos' (cap), referring to the bare cap of the mushroom.
