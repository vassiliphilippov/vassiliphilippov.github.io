parvo

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrˌvoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːvəʊ / (BrE)
Any of a group of viruses containing DNA in an icosahedral protein shell, which can cause serious disease in dogs and cattle but is not known to affect humans.
a virus causing disease in dogs and cattle
medical

Examples

The veterinarian diagnosed the puppy with parvo, explaining that early treatment is critical.
Shelters often vaccinate animals against parvo to prevent outbreaks.
Cases of parvo in cattle are rare compared to those in dogs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parvovirus - a virus from the Parvoviridae family, causing disease in animals

Etymology

Shortened form of 'parvovirus', from Latin 'parvus' (small) + 'virus'.
