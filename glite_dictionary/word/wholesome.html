wholesome

1. ADJ

/ ˈhoʊl.səm / (AmE), / ˈhəʊl.səm / (BrE)
Conducive to or suggestive of good health or moral well-being; exhibiting soundness in body or mind.
conducive to good health or well-being

Examples

Daily walks have a wholesome effect on both body and mind.
She prefers to cook wholesome meals using fresh ingredients.
His wholesome attitude makes him well-liked among his peers.

Synset Examples

exercise develops wholesome appetites
a grin on his ugly wholesome face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: healthful - promoting health
  • Antonym: unwholesome - not conducive to health or well-being

Etymology

From Old English 'hālsum', from 'hāl' (healthy, whole) + '-sum' (some).

2. ADJ

/ ˈhoʊl.səm / (AmE), / ˈhəʊl.səm / (BrE)
conducive to or suggestive of good health or moral well-being; promoting or characterized by healthiness and goodness.
promoting health or moral well-being

Examples

She always prepares wholesome meals for her family, using fresh ingredients from the market.
His wholesome attitude inspires those around him to act with kindness.
The retreat offered plenty of wholesome activities like hiking and yoga.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unwholesome - harmful to health or morals
