strap

1. NOUN

/ stræp / (AmE), / stræp / (BrE)
a long, narrow strip of leather or similar material used for tying, fastening, or holding things in position
long narrow strip for fastening

Examples

He secured the package with a strong strap to prevent it from opening.
She adjusted the strap on her backpack for a better fit.
The old suitcase had a leather strap to keep the lid closed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belt - a strip of material worn around the waist, often to hold up clothing
  • Antonym: strapless - (of clothing) without straps

Etymology

From Old English *stropp* (strap), from Latin *stroppus* (strap, band).
