to assail

1. VERB

/ əˈseɪl / (AmE), / əˈseɪl / (BrE)
to attack someone violently, either physically or emotionally
attack someone physically or emotionally

Examples

The journalist was assailed by critics after publishing her article.
Anxiety can assail people unexpectedly in stressful situations.
The fortress was assailed by enemy troops at dawn.

Synset Examples

The mugger assaulted the woman
Nightmares assailed him regularly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attack - to take aggressive action against
  • Antonym: to defend - to protect from attack

Etymology

From Latin 'assilire', from 'ad-' (toward) + 'salire' (to leap).

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈseɪl / (AmE), / əˈseɪl / (BrE)
to attack someone or something violently, especially in speech or writing
to attack verbally or physically

Examples

The article assailed the government's latest economic policy.
She assailed her critics in a passionate speech.
Several opinion pieces assail the company's environmental record.

Synset Examples

The editors of the left-leaning paper attacked the new House Speaker

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attack - to take aggressive action against; to criticize or oppose
  • Antonym: to defend - to protect from harm or attack; to speak or write in favor of

Etymology

From Latin 'assilire' (to leap upon), from 'ad-' (to) + 'salire' (to leap).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00864347-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ əˈseɪl / (AmE), / əˈseɪl / (BrE)
to attack someone or something violently or forcefully, either physically or verbally
attack violently or forcefully

Examples

The soldiers prepared to assail the heavily fortified enemy position at dawn.
She was assailed by critics after her controversial statement.
The castle has been assailed many times throughout its long history.

Synset Examples

Hitler attacked Poland on September 1, 1939 and started World War II
Serbian forces assailed Bosnian towns all week

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attack - to take aggressive action against someone or something
  • Antonym: to defend - to protect against attack or danger

Etymology

From Old French assaillir, from Latin assilire (to leap upon).

Source: WordNet
