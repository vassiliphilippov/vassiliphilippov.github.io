rottenness

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːtnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɒtnəs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being rotten or decayed.
state of being rotten or decayed

Examples

The rottenness of the old fruit was obvious from its foul smell.
Years of neglect led to the rottenness of the wooden beams in the barn.
He was appalled by the rottenness that had spread through the entire crop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decay - the process of rotting or decomposing
  • Antonym: freshness - the state of being fresh and not decayed

Etymology

Formed from 'rotten' + suffix '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːtənnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɒtənnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being rotten, especially as characterized by decay or putrefaction
state of being rotten or decayed

Examples

The rottenness of the fruit made it unfit for eating.
Years of neglect led to the rottenness of the building's wooden beams.
The inspector noted the rottenness in the foundation and recommended repairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decay - the process of rotting or decomposition
  • Antonym: freshness - the state of being fresh, not decayed or rotten

Etymology

Formed from 'rotten' + '-ness' (suffix forming nouns denoting a state or condition).
