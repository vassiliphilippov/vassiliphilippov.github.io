proverb

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.vɝːb / (AmE), / ˈprɒv.ɜːb / (BrE)
A short, well-known saying that expresses a general truth or piece of advice.
short, wise traditional saying

Examples

The proverb 'actions speak louder than words' encourages people to act rather than just talk.
He used an old proverb to illustrate his point during the meeting.
Many cultures have a proverb about the value of hard work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maxim - a short statement expressing a general truth or rule of conduct
  • Synonym: aphorism - a concise statement of a principle or truth

Etymology

From Latin 'proverbium' (proverb, saying).
