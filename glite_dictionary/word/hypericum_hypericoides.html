Hypericum hypericoides

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈpɪərɪkəm haɪˌpɪərɪˈkɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / hɪˈpɛrɪkəm hɪˌpɛrɪˈkɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A low shrub with yellow, four-petaled flowers, native to Bermuda, the southeastern United States, the West Indies, and eastern Mexico.
a shrubby plant with yellow flowers, native to the Americas

Examples

The garden featured several specimens of Hypericum hypericoides in full bloom.
Botanists observed that Hypericum hypericoides prefers sandy soils in the southeast US.
We identified the shrub as Hypericum hypericoides by its distinctive yellow cross-shaped flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: St. Andrew's cross - common name for Hypericum hypericoides

Etymology

From Greek 'hyperikon' (a type of herb), scientific Latin naming convention.
