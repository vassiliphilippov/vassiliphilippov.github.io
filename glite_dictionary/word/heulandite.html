heulandite

1. NOUN

/ ˈhjuːləndaɪt / (AmE), / ˈhjuːləndaɪt / (BrE)
a mineral of the zeolite group, consisting of a hydrous aluminum silicate of sodium and calcium, typically found in volcanic rocks
a mineral of the zeolite group

Examples

The volcanic rock contained large crystals of heulandite alongside other zeolites.
She identified the mineral specimen as heulandite using X-ray diffraction techniques.
Collectors prize rare orange forms of heulandite for their vibrant color and clarity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stilbite - a related zeolite mineral commonly found with heulandite

Etymology

Named after the English mineralogist Henry Heuland (1778–1856).
