mushroom

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌʃˌruːm / (AmE), / ˈmʌʃ.rʊm / (BrE)
a fleshy, spore-producing body of certain fungi, often edible and typically shaped like an umbrella
edible fleshy fungus body

Examples

He picked a wild mushroom and added it to his salad.
The recipe calls for sliced mushrooms and garlic.
They went hiking in the woods to search for edible mushrooms.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: toadstool - a poisonous or inedible fungus with a stalk and cap

Etymology

From Old French *mousseron*, from Late Latin *mussirio* (type of fungus).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌʃˌruːm / (AmE), / ˈmʌʃ.ruːm / (BrE)
A fleshy, spore-producing body of a fungus, typically with a domed cap on a stalk; includes various related fungi such as toadstools, puffballs, and morels.
fleshy fungus with a cap and stalk

Examples

We picked several wild mushrooms during our hike in the forest.
The chef added fresh mushrooms to the pasta sauce for extra flavor.
Some types of mushroom are poisonous and should not be eaten.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toadstool - fungus with a cap and stalk, often considered poisonous

Etymology

From Old French 'mousseron', from Late Latin 'mussirio', origin uncertain.
