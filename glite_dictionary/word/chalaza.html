chalaza

1. NOUN

/ kəˈleɪzə / (AmE), / kəˈleɪzə / (BrE)
The part of a plant ovule at the base, opposite the micropyle, where the integument and the nucellus are joined.
base of a plant ovule opposite the micropyle

Examples

In botanical diagrams, the chalaza is always marked at the base of the ovule.
Nutrients are transported from the chalaza to the developing embryo sac in flowering plants.
The fusion of integument and nucellus at the chalaza is vital for seed development.

Etymology

From Greek khalaza (small lump, hailstone), reflecting the structure's shape.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈleɪzə / (AmE), / kəˈleɪzə / (BrE)
either of two spiral bands of tissue that connect the yolk of an egg to its surrounding membrane at either end of the shell
spiral band connecting egg yolk to membrane
medical

Examples

The chalaza keeps the yolk suspended in the center of the egg.
When separating an egg, you might notice the chalaza attached to the yolk.
Some recipes advise removing the chalaza for a smoother custard texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yolk - the yellow part of an egg
  • Synonym: albumen - the white of an egg

Etymology

From Greek khalaza (small knot or lump).
