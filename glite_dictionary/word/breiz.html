Breiz

1. PROPN

/ breɪz / (AmE), / breɪz / (BrE)
The Breton name for Brittany, a former province in northwestern France located on a peninsula between the English Channel and the Bay of Biscay.
Breton name for Brittany (French region)

Examples

The traditional music of Breiz is an important part of local identity.
Scholars gathered in Breiz to study Celtic languages and culture.
Many festivals in Breiz celebrate its distinct heritage from the rest of France.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Brittany - a historical region and peninsula of northwestern France

Etymology

Breton form of 'Brittany', from Old Breton 'Britannia', ultimately from Latin.
