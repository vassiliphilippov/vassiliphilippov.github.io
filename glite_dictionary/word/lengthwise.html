lengthwise

1. ADV

/ ˈlɛŋθˌwaɪz / (AmE), / ˈlɛŋθˌwaɪz / (BrE)
in the direction of the longest side; along the length of something
in the direction of the length

Examples

Slice the cucumber lengthwise to make thin strips.
The log was split lengthwise for easier transport.
She folded the scarf lengthwise before tying it around her neck.

Synset Examples

He cut the paper lengthwise

Related Terms

  • Antonym: crosswise - in the direction of the width (across the shortest dimension)

Etymology

Formed from 'length' + the suffix '-wise' (meaning 'in the manner or direction of').

2. ADV

/ ˈlɛŋθˌwaɪz / (AmE), / ˈlɛŋθwʌɪz / (BrE)
in the direction of the length; along the longest side or axis of something.
in the direction of length

Examples

Cut the cucumber lengthwise to make long, thin strips.
The crack runs lengthwise along the wooden plank.
She folded the scarf lengthwise before tying it around her neck.

Synset Examples

the lengthwise dimension

Related Terms

  • Synonym: longitudinal - running lengthwise, rather than across
  • Antonym: crosswise - in the direction across the width, not along the length
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01447704-a
Synset POS: a
