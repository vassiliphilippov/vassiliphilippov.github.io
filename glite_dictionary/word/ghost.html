ghost

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊst / (AmE), / ɡəʊst / (BrE)
to move quietly, smoothly, or stealthily like a ghost
move stealthily or silently

Examples

The cat ghosted silently along the garden wall, barely making a sound.
Late at night, he ghosted through the empty corridors, unnoticed by anyone.
Shadows ghosted across the wet pavement as the fog rolled in.

Synset Examples

The masked men ghosted across the moonlit yard

Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ɡoʊst / (AmE), / ɡəʊst / (BrE)
a memory or image of a past experience, especially one that is disturbing or associated with regret or trauma
memory of a disturbing past experience

Examples

Try as he might, he could not shake the ghost of his childhood regrets.
Memories of the accident haunted her like a ghost every night.
The ghost of his past mistakes lingered and affected his decisions.

Synset Examples

he looked like he had seen a ghost
it aroused specters from his past

Related Terms

  • Synonym: specter - a haunting or disturbing image of the past
  • Synonym: phantom - something that exists only as a mental image

Etymology

From Old English 'gāst' (spirit, soul), related to German 'Geist'.

3. NOUN

/ ɡoʊst / (AmE), / ɡəʊst / (BrE)
the spirit or soul of a dead person that appears to the living, typically as a visible or tangible figure
spirit of a dead person

Examples

She claimed to have seen a ghost in the old house late at night.
Many cultures have stories about a ghost haunting a particular location.
As a child, he was afraid that a ghost might appear while he was alone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phantom - a ghostly appearing figure; something seen, heard, or sensed but having no physical reality
  • Synonym: spirit - the non-physical part of a person, often believed to live on after death
  • Antonym: corporeal being - a living being with a physical body, as opposed to a ghost

Etymology

From Old English 'gāst' (spirit, soul), related to German 'Geist' (spirit).

4. VERB

/ ɡoʊst / (AmE), / ɡəʊst / (BrE)
to write material, such as a book, speech, or article, for another person, who is credited as the author
write as an uncredited author for someone else

Examples

She was hired to ghost the celebrity's autobiography.
Many politicians have their speeches ghosted by professional writers.
He makes a living by ghosting novels for well-known authors.

Synset Examples

How many books have you ghostwritten so far?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ghostwrite - to write (a book, article, etc.) for another person who is named as the author

Etymology

From the noun 'ghostwriter': 'ghost' meaning 'unseen' plus 'writer'.
