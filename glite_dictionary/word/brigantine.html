brigantine

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪɡənˌtin / (AmE), / ˈbrɪɡəntiːn / (BrE)
a two-masted sailing ship with a square-rigged foremast and a fore-and-aft rigged mainmast
two-masted sailing vessel

Examples

The crew of the brigantine prepared to set sail at dawn.
In the museum, there is a model of a 19th-century brigantine.
Pirates often favored the brigantine for its speed and maneuverability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schooner - a sailing ship with two or more masts, typically with all masts rigged fore-and-aft
  • Synonym: barque - a sailing ship with three or more masts, having the fore- and mainmasts square-rigged and the mizzenmast fore-and-aft rigged

Etymology

From Italian 'brigantino', diminutive of 'brigante' (brigand), originally referring to a small vessel used by pirates.
