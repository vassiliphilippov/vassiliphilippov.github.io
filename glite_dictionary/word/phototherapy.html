phototherapy

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊtoʊˈθɛrəpi / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊˈθɛrəpi / (BrE)
the use of light, especially ultraviolet or other specific wavelengths, as a treatment for certain medical conditions such as skin disorders and neonatal jaundice
treatment using light
medical

Examples

The dermatologist recommended phototherapy to help manage her psoriasis symptoms.
Babies with severe jaundice may undergo phototherapy in the hospital.
Recent studies have shown the effectiveness of phototherapy for treating certain types of eczema.

Etymology

From Greek 'phōs' (light) + 'therapeia' (healing).
