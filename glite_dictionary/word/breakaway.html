breakaway

1. ADJ

/ ˈbreɪkəˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkəˌweɪ / (BrE)
having separated from a larger group, organization, or policy, often in order to act independently or form a new group.
having separated from a larger group

Examples

The party was divided by the emergence of a breakaway faction demanding autonomy.
A breakaway group of scientists issued their own independent statement.
Tensions increased after several members formed a breakaway organization.

Synset Examples

a breakaway faction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: separatist - supporting separation from a larger group or country
  • Antonym: mainstream - belonging to or characteristic of the dominant group or policy

2. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪkəˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkəˌweɪ / (BrE)
an act or instance of separating from a group, organization, or tradition, often to form something new or become independent
an act of separating or withdrawing

Examples

The political party experienced a significant breakaway by several of its senior members.
The band's latest album reflects their breakaway from traditional rock influences.
There was a dramatic breakaway in the final lap of the cycling race.

Synset Examples

there was a breakaway by the discontented members
a breaking away from family and neighborhood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secession - the action of formally withdrawing from an organization or alliance
  • Synonym: split - an instance of dividing or breaking away, especially in organizations or groups
  • Antonym: union - the action of joining together or being joined

Etymology

Formed from 'break' + 'away', originally attested in the early 20th century.
