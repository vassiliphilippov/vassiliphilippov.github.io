sucking

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsʌkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of drawing in liquid or air through the mouth, especially by using the lips and tongue
the act of drawing in liquid or air with the mouth

Examples

The infant's sucking was strong and rhythmic during feeding.
Vacuum cleaners use the principle of sucking to remove dust from carpets.
He could hear the faint sucking sound as the straw emptied the glass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suck - to draw in liquid or air through the mouth
