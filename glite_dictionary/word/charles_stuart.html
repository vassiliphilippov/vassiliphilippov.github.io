Charles Stuart

1. PROPN

/ ˈtʃɑrlz ˈstuːərt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːlz ˈstjuːət / (BrE)
(1600–1649) King of England, Scotland, and Ireland, deposed and executed during the English Civil War.
King of England, Scotland, and Ireland (1600–1649)

Examples

Charles Stuart was executed in 1649 after being tried for treason.
The reign of Charles Stuart was marked by conflict with Parliament and the outbreak of civil war.
Historians debate whether Charles Stuart could have avoided war with more political skill.
