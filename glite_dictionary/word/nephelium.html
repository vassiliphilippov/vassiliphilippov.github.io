Nephelium

1. NOUN

/ nəˈfiːliəm / (AmE), / nɛˈfiːliəm / (BrE)
A genus of tropical trees in the family Sapindaceae, native to Asia and Australia, including the rambutan.
genus of tropical trees (e.g. rambutan)

Examples

The Nephelium genus includes several commercially important fruit trees in Southeast Asia.
Botanists recently discovered a new species of Nephelium in the forests of Borneo.
The rambutan is perhaps the most famous member of the Nephelium genus.

Etymology

From Greek nephelē (cloud), referring to the appearance of the flowers.

2. NOUN

/ nɛˈfiliəm / (AmE), / nɪˈfiːlɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of tropical trees in the family Sapindaceae, native to Asia and Australia, including the rambutan tree.
a genus of tropical trees (Sapindaceae)

Examples

The rambutan tree is a member of the Nephelium genus.
Several species within Nephelium produce edible fruit popular in Southeast Asia.
Botanists have classified these tropical trees under the Nephelium genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rambutan - a tropical fruit from trees of the Nephelium genus

Etymology

From Greek *nephelē* (cloud), referring to the fluffy appearance of some species’ fruit.
