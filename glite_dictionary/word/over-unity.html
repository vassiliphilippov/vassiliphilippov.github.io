over-unity

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvɚˈjuː.nə.ti / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈjuː.nɪ.ti / (BrE)
(of a device or system) claiming to produce more energy than it consumes, contrary to the laws of physics.
claiming to generate more energy than used

Examples

Many inventors have tried to create over-unity machines, but none have been scientifically validated.
The concept of over-unity is often mentioned in discussions of free energy devices.
No device can truly achieve over-unity according to the current laws of physics.

Synset Examples

Achieving over-unity in energy systems is controversial.
Scientists are skeptical about over-unity claims.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perpetual motion - motion that continues indefinitely without an external energy source

Etymology

Formed from 'over' (exceeding) + 'unity' (ratio equal to one), referencing energy output/input ratio.
