hortensia

1. NOUN

/ hɔrˈtɛnsiə / (AmE), / hɔːˈtɛnsɪə / (BrE)
a type of flowering plant, especially any of various species of the genus Hydrangea, often cultivated for its large, showy blooms
ornamental flowering plant (Hydrangea)

Examples

The garden was filled with pink and blue hortensias in full bloom.
She planted a hortensia near the front porch to brighten the entrance.
Many floral arrangements included branches of hortensia because of their striking appearance.

Etymology

From Latinized form of the French feminine name Hortense.

2. NOUN

/ hɔrˈtɛnʃə / (AmE), / hɔːˈtɛnʃə / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub with rounded clusters of flowers that open green and turn pink or blue as they age, commonly known as a hydrangea.
deciduous shrub with round flower clusters

Examples

The hortensia in the garden bloomed with large pink blossoms after the first rain.
She planted a row of white hortensias along the fence for a splash of color.
Many landscapers prefer hortensia for its vibrant flower clusters and easy care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrangea - a shrub or climbing plant with round clusters of pink, blue, or white flowers

Etymology

From New Latin Hortensia, named after Frenchwoman hortense Lepaute; a genus name later applied to the hydrangea.
