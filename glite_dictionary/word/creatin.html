creatin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkriːətɪn / (AmE), / ˈkriːətɪn / (BrE)
an amino acid-like compound found in the muscle tissue of vertebrates, occurring in free form and as phosphocreatine, and involved in supplying energy for muscle contraction
compound in muscles, stores energy
medical

Examples

The body's muscle cells use creatin to help supply the energy needed for brief, intense physical activity.
Researchers studied the concentration of creatin in the thigh muscles of athletes.
In some medical conditions, creatin levels in the blood may be measured to assess muscle health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creatine - a compound similar to creatin, also found in muscle tissue and involved in energy supply

Etymology

From German 'Kreatin', from Greek 'kreas' (flesh).
