stewing

1. NOUN

/ ˈstuːɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstjuː.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of cooking food slowly in liquid over low heat after first bringing it to a boil
cooking food slowly in liquid

Examples

Stewing is an ideal method for preparing tougher cuts of meat.
The recipe calls for several hours of stewing to develop the flavors.
Vegetarians often use stewing to make hearty dishes with beans and vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: braising - cooking food by browning it first, then simmering it in liquid in a covered pot

Etymology

From the verb 'stew', ultimately from Old French *estuver* (to bathe, stew), from Vulgar Latin *extufare*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstuːɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstjuːɪŋ / (BrE)
a state of intense worry or agitation, especially when someone is anxiously thinking about something for a prolonged period
a state of worry or agitation

Examples

After the argument, her stewing kept her busy for hours, replaying every word in her mind.
His constant stewing over the missed opportunity made it hard to focus on anything else.
A night of stewing left him exhausted, but no closer to resolving his concerns.

Synset Examples

his stewing over the fight kept him awake most of the night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worrying - experiencing anxiety or concern
  • Synonym: brooding - deep, often silent or persistent worry

Etymology

From the verb 'stew', meaning to be in a state of agitation or worry (metaphorically from cooking in hot liquid).
