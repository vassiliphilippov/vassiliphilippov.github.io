Dryopithecus

1. NOUN

/ ˌdraɪoʊpɪˈθikəs / (AmE), / ˌdraɪəʊpɪˈθiːkəs / (BrE)
a genus of extinct Old World hominoid apes from the Miocene and Pliocene epochs
genus of extinct Miocene-Pliocene apes

Examples

The fossils of Dryopithecus provide important clues about the evolution of great apes.
Paleontologists discovered a Dryopithecus skull in Europe dating to the Miocene epoch.
Research suggests that Dryopithecus may have been partly arboreal, spending much of its time in trees.

Etymology

From Greek 'drys' (tree) + 'pithekos' (ape), referring to tree-dwelling apes.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdraɪ.oʊˈpɪθəkəs / (AmE), / ˌdraɪ.əʊˈpɪθɪkəs / (BrE)
A genus of extinct Old World hominoids that lived during the Miocene and Pliocene epochs.
genus of extinct Miocene apes

Examples

Fossils of Dryopithecus have been found in various parts of Europe and Asia.
Paleontologists believe that Dryopithecus is an important link in ape evolution.
The limb structure of Dryopithecus suggests it was adapted for life in the trees.

Etymology

From Greek drys (oak) + pithekos (ape), referring to 'oak ape'.
