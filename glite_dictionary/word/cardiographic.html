cardiographic

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːrdioʊˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌkɑːdiəˈɡræfɪk / (BrE)
relating to the use or results of a cardiograph, especially in recording heart activity
relating to a cardiograph
medical

Examples

A cardiographic examination was performed to assess the patient's heart function.
The doctor studied the cardiographic results before making a diagnosis.
Advancements in cardiographic technology have improved the detection of heart abnormalities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardiograph - an instrument for recording the heart's activity

Etymology

From 'cardio-' (relating to the heart) + '-graphic' (relating to writing or recording).
