grove

1. NOUN

/ ɡroʊv / (AmE), / ɡrəʊv / (BrE)
A small group of trees growing close together, usually without much underbrush.
small group or cluster of trees

Examples

We enjoyed a picnic in a peaceful grove near the lake.
The lemon grove behind the house produced fruit all summer.
A small grove of birches provided shelter from the hot sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodland - land covered with trees; a forested area
  • Degree Higher: forest - a large area covered chiefly with trees and undergrowth

Etymology

From Old English *grāf* (grove, copse).

2. NOUN

/ ɡroʊv / (AmE), / ɡrəʊv / (BrE)
a small group of trees, especially one without undergrowth, often cultivated as part of a garden or orchard
small group of trees, often cultivated

Examples

They walked through the peaceful grove behind the house, enjoying the cool shade.
An olive grove covered the hillside, its trees perfectly aligned in neat rows.
The wedding ceremony was held in a secluded grove not far from the main gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copse - a small group of trees or bushes growing together
  • Degree Higher: orchard - a piece of land planted with fruit trees

Etymology

Old English *graf* (grove, copse), of Germanic origin.
