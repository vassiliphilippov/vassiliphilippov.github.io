hick

1. ADJ

/ hɪk / (AmE), / hɪk / (BrE)
(of a person or place) awkwardly simple, unsophisticated, and characteristic of the countryside; provincial.
unsophisticated and provincial

Examples

She thought the little diner was too hick for her tastes.
They dismissed the town's traditions as old-fashioned and hick.
His clothes looked a bit hick compared to the city's fashionable crowd.

Synset Examples

bumpkinly country boys
rustic farmers
a hick town
the nightlife of Montmartre awed the unsophisticated tourists

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rustic - simple or unsophisticated, characteristic of the countryside
  • Antonym: urban - relating to or characteristic of a city or town

2. NOUN

/ hɪk / (AmE), / hɪk / (BrE)
an unsophisticated or socially backward person from the countryside, often considered unintelligent or uncultured (often used disparagingly)
unsophisticated rural person (often derogatory)
offensive informal

Examples

The city folks looked down on him, calling him a hick because of his accent and manners.
She always felt out of place at university, worried others would see her as just a hick from a small town.
He refused to let their hick jokes hurt his confidence in his rural upbringing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yokel - an uneducated and unsophisticated person from the countryside
  • Synonym: bumpkin - an awkward and unsophisticated country person
  • Antonym: city slicker - a sophisticated person from a city

Etymology

Probably imitative or possibly from 'Hick', a pet form of the name 'Richard', used disparagingly for rural folk.
