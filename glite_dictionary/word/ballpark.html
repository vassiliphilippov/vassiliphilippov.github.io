ballpark

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑlˌpɑrk / (AmE), / ˈbɔːlˌpɑːk / (BrE)
a stadium or field where baseball or other ball games are played, especially referring to baseball stadiums
baseball stadium

Examples

The children were excited to visit the ballpark for their first baseball game.
She bought season tickets at the new ballpark downtown.
Vendors walk through the ballpark selling snacks during the game.

Synset Examples

take me out to the ballpark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stadium - a large enclosed area for sports events and concerts
  • Synonym: arena - a level area surrounded by seats for sports or entertainment

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɔlˌpɑrk / (AmE), / ˈbɔːlˌpɑːk / (BrE)
an approximate range or estimate, especially regarding an amount or value
approximate range or estimate

Examples

That number isn't even in the ballpark of what we expected.
Can you give me a ballpark estimate for the cost of repairs?
Her proposal was at least in the right ballpark compared to the other bids.

Synset Examples

his answer wasn't even in the right ballpark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estimate - a rough calculation or assessment
  • Synonym: guess - an approximate or speculative answer

Etymology

Originally in reference to a baseball stadium, later figuratively meaning an area or range.
