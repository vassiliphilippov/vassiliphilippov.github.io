Paranthias furcifer

1. NOUN

/ pəˌrænθi.əs ˈfɝːsɪfɚ / (AmE), / pəˌrænθɪ.əs ˈfɜːsɪfə / (BrE)
A species of deep-sea fish found in the tropical Atlantic Ocean, known for its forked tail.
deep-sea fish of the tropical Atlantic

Examples

The biologist was excited to spot a Paranthias furcifer during the deep-sea expedition.
Rarely seen by divers, the Paranthias furcifer usually inhabits steep reef drop-offs.
A detailed study of the Paranthias furcifer contributed to our knowledge of Atlantic marine life.

Etymology

From New Latin, the genus name 'Paranthias' and species name 'furcifer' (fork-bearer in Latin, referring to the tail shape).
