compressor

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈprɛsɚ / (AmE), / kəmˈprɛsə / (BrE)
a machine or device that increases the pressure of a gas by reducing its volume
device that compresses gas

Examples

The air conditioning unit relies on a compressor to circulate refrigerant through the system.
The mechanic replaced the faulty compressor in the car's engine.
Industrial plants often use a large compressor to power pneumatic tools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pump - a device for moving fluids by mechanical action

Etymology

From Latin 'comprimere' (to press together), via 'compress' + '-or'.

2. NOUN

/ kəmˈprɛsɚ / (AmE), / kəmˈprɛsə / (BrE)
an electronic device used to reduce the dynamic range of an audio signal, making loud sounds quieter and quiet sounds louder
electronic device reducing audio signal range

Examples

The recording engineer adjusted the compressor to smooth out the lead vocalist's volume peaks.
A compressor is essential for achieving a polished, professional sound in modern music production.
When mixing drums, using a compressor can help each hit sound more consistent and controlled.

Synset Examples

The signal entering a compressor is split, one copy sent to a variable-gain amplifier and the other to a side-chain where a circuit controlled by the signal level applies the required gain to an amplifier stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limiter - an audio device that prevents signal levels from exceeding a certain threshold
  • Antonym: expander - an audio device that increases the dynamic range of a signal
