gulp

1. NOUN

/ ɡʌlp / (AmE), / ɡʌlp / (BrE)
an act of swallowing a large amount of liquid or food quickly and often noisily
large hurried swallow

Examples

He drained the glass in a single gulp and set it down heavily.
She took a gulp of water before starting her presentation.
With each nervous gulp, his anxiety grew more obvious.

Synset Examples

he finished it at a single gulp

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sip - a small mouthful of liquid, especially taken slowly
  • Synonym: swig - a large and often noisy or greedy swallow, especially of a drink

Etymology

Imitative of the sound of swallowing, first recorded in late Middle English.

2. VERB

/ ɡʌlp / (AmE), / ɡʌlp / (BrE)
to utter a choking or swallowing sound, especially when swallowing quickly or with difficulty
make a choking or swallowing sound

Examples

He tried to gulp out a warning, but his throat was too dry.
After swallowing the medicine too quickly, she began to gulp noisily.
The frightened child could only gulp for air as he tried to speak.

Synset Examples

He gulped for help after choking on a big piece of meat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swallow - to cause or allow something to pass down the throat

Etymology

Perhaps imitative of the sound of swallowing.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01069024-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ɡʌlp / (AmE), / ɡʌlp / (BrE)
An act of swallowing, especially one made quickly or with difficulty, often producing a visible or audible movement of the throat.
an act of swallowing, often sudden or audible

Examples

She took a nervous gulp before stepping onto the stage.
He swallowed the medicine in one large gulp.
With each gulp, his anxiety became more apparent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swallow - to cause or allow something to pass down the throat; act of ingesting
  • Degree Lower: sip - an act of drinking in small quantities

Etymology

Imitative origin, probably echoic of the sound of swallowing.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00842024-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ɡʌlp / (AmE), / ɡʌlp / (BrE)
To swallow food or drink quickly or in large amounts, often greedily or because of thirst or urgency.
swallow quickly or greedily

Examples

He tried to gulp his water after the long run.
She nervously gulped down the medicine without tasting it.
The children gulped their drinks before heading back outside to play.

Synset Examples

The men gulped down their beers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swallow - to cause or allow something, especially food or drink, to pass down the throat
  • Antonym: sip - to drink something in small amounts

Etymology

Imitative origin, echoing the sound of swallowing.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01204688-v
Synset POS: v
