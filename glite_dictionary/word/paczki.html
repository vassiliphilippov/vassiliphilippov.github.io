paczki

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑtʃki / (AmE), / ˈpʊtʃki / (BrE)
A traditional Polish doughnut, usually filled with sweet filling and fried, often eaten before Lent.
Polish filled doughnut

Examples

Many bakeries sell paczki on Fat Tuesday as part of the pre-Lenten tradition.
She brought a box of delicious paczki filled with raspberry jam to the office.
Have you ever tasted homemade paczki during Carnival season?

Etymology

From Polish 'pączki', plural of 'pączek' (doughnut).
