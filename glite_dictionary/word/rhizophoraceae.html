Rhizophoraceae

1. NOUN

/ raɪˌzɑfəˈreɪsiː / (AmE), / raɪˌzɒfəˈreɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical trees and shrubs, often forming dense jungles along seacoasts, including many mangrove species.
family of tropical coastal trees and shrubs
medical

Examples

The Rhizophoraceae dominate the mangrove forests along the tropical coastline.
Many species in the Rhizophoraceae family play a crucial role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
Researchers are studying how Rhizophoraceae adapt to saline environments.

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Rhizophora' (the type genus) + '-aceae' (a suffix used in botanical family names, from Latin).
