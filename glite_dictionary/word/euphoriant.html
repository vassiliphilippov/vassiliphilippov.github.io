euphoriant

1. NOUN

/ juˈfɔriənt / (AmE), / juˈfɔːriənt / (BrE)
A psychoactive drug that tends to produce feelings of elation and euphoria.
drug causing euphoria
medical

Examples

The patient became dependent on the euphoriant prescribed to manage his pain.
Some herbal supplements are marketed as natural euphoriants with mood-lifting effects.
Doctors warn that repeated use of a euphoriant can lead to addiction.

Etymology

From French 'euphorient', from 'euphorie' (euphoria) + '-ant'.

2. ADJ

/ juˈfɔriənt / (AmE), / juːˈfɔːrɪənt / (BrE)
Tending to produce a feeling of intense happiness or excitement (euphoria), especially used to describe certain drugs or substances.
causing intense happiness or euphoria
medical

Examples

The treatment involved a euphoriant effect that helped ease the patient's anxiety.
Some recreational drugs are classified as euphoriant due to their impact on mood.
Researchers studied the euphoriant properties of the new medication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: euphoric - feeling intense excitement and happiness

Etymology

From French 'euphorique' and English '-ant,' based on 'euphoria' from Greek 'euphoros' (healthy).
