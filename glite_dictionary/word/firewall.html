firewall

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪrˌwɔl / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəwɔːl / (BrE)
A fireproof or fire-resistant wall designed to prevent the spread of fire within a building or vehicle.
fire-resistant barrier in buildings or vehicles

Examples

The building code requires a firewall between the garage and the living space.
The engineers installed a steel firewall to protect the cabin from engine fires.
After the accident, inspectors checked the firewall to make sure it had not been damaged.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚˌwɑl / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌwɔːl / (BrE)
(colloquial, aviation) The act of applying maximum thrust by pushing the throttle fully forward.
aviation: application of maximum thrust
informal

Examples

The pilot pushed the throttle to the firewall and the jet surged forward.
In an emergency, it's common to move the throttle to the firewall for a quick escape.
With the runway running out, she slammed the lever all the way to the firewall.

Synset Examples

he moved the throttle to the firewall

Etymology

From 'firewall' (the wall protecting pilots from engine fire), referencing pushing the throttle to the wall.

3. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪrˌwɑl / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌwɔːl / (BrE)
(computing) a security system, often comprised of hardware and software, that restricts unauthorized access to or from a private computer network.
network security system in computing

Examples

A strong firewall is essential to protect company data from cyberattacks.
The IT department updated the firewall to block new types of malware.
Make sure your firewall is always active when connecting to public Wi-Fi networks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: router - a device that forwards data packets between computer networks
  • Synonym: gateway - a network node used in telecommunications that connects two networks using different protocols
  • Degree Lower: antivirus - software designed to detect and destroy computer viruses

Etymology

Originally referred to a barrier designed to prevent the spread of fire; adopted in computing to describe a barrier protecting computer systems.
