plangency

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæn.dʒən.si / (AmE), / ˈplæn.dʒən.si / (BrE)
the quality of having a loud, resonant, and often mournful sound
resonant, mournful sound quality

Examples

The plangency of the bell echoed through the silent valley, drawing everyone's attention.
She was moved by the plangency in his voice as he sang the old folk song.
The plangency of the cello gave the piece a deeply emotional tone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resonance - the quality of being deep, full, and reverberating

Etymology

From Latin plangens (lamenting, beating), from plangere (to strike, lament).
