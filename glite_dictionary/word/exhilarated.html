exhilarated

1. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈzɪləˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɪləreɪtɪd / (BrE)
very happy, excited, and full of energy.
very happy and excited

Examples

She felt exhilarated after finishing the challenging marathon.
The team was exhilarated by their unexpected victory.
He was exhilarated to ride in a hot air balloon for the first time.

Synset Examples

the sun and the wind on his back made him feel exhilarated — happy to be alive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elated - very happy or proud; in high spirits
  • Antonym: depressed - unhappy and lacking hope or enthusiasm

Etymology

From Latin exhilaratus, past participle of exhilarare (to gladden, cheer).
