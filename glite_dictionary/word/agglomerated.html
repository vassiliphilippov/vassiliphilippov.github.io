agglomerated

1. ADJ

/ əˈɡlɑːmɚˌeɪtɪd / (AmE), / əˈɡlɒməˌreɪtɪd / (BrE)
gathered or clustered into a mass or group, without forming a coherent or organized structure
clustered into a mass

Examples

The dust particles became agglomerated on the surface of the filter.
Researchers observed agglomerated cells forming uneven tissue masses in the sample.
The seeds were agglomerated together by a sticky residue.

Synset Examples

an agglomerated flower head

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clustered - grouped closely together
  • Antonym: dispersed - spread out or scattered

Etymology

From Latin 'agglomeratus', past participle of 'agglomerare' (to wind into a ball), from 'ad-' (to) + 'glomerare' (to form into a ball).
