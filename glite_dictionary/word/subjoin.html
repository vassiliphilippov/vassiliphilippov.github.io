to subjoin

1. VERB

/ səbˈdʒɔɪn / (AmE), / səbˈdʒɔɪn / (BrE)
to add (a statement, comment, etc.) at the end of something, especially in writing
add something at the end

Examples

He decided to subjoin a brief explanation to the end of his letter.
The author subjoins several footnotes after each chapter for further reading.
If you wish to clarify your point, you may subjoin additional details in the appendix.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to append - to add as a supplement or attachment at the end
  • Synonym: affix - attach or add on, usually at the end

Etymology

From Latin subjungere, from sub- (under, after) + jungere (to join).
