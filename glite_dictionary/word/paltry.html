paltry

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɔltri / (AmE), / ˈpɔːltri / (BrE)
(of an amount or thing) very small or insignificant; not worth considering.
insignificant; very small or worthless

Examples

The company offered a paltry bonus to its employees despite record profits.
He was disappointed by the paltry sum he received for his old car.
She dismissed his paltry excuse and demanded a proper explanation.

Synset Examples

he considered the prize too paltry for the lives it must cost
piffling efforts
a trifling matter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trifling - of little value or importance
  • Synonym: insignificant - too small or unimportant to be worth consideration
  • Antonym: substantial - of considerable importance, size, or worth

Etymology

From Middle English *paltrie* (worthless), of uncertain origin, possibly from dialectal *palt* (rag, trash).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɔl.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɔːl.tri / (BrE)
(of an amount) very small and unimportant; so small as to be almost worthless or contemptible.
very small or insignificant (amount)

Examples

The bonus he received was a paltry sum compared to his hard work.
Many workers are struggling to survive on a paltry wage.
She made a paltry contribution to the fundraising campaign, disappointing the organizers.

Synset Examples

a measly tip
the company donated a miserable $100 for flood relief
a paltry wage
almost depleted his miserable store of dried beans

Related Terms

  • Synonym: measly - very small or insignificant (amount)
  • Synonym: miserable - very small or inadequate (amount, quality)
  • Antonym: generous - showing a readiness to give more of something, especially money; opposite of paltry

Etymology

From early modern English, possibly from dialectal paltry (trashy), derived from palt (trash) + -ery.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
