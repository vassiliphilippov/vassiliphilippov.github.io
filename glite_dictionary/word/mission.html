mission

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
a group of people sent by a government, organization, or country to carry out a specific task, often abroad
group of representatives sent for a purpose

Examples

An economic mission will visit Japan next week to discuss trade.
The UN sent a mission to the region to investigate human rights abuses.
A diplomatic mission arrived in the capital for peace talks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delegation - a group of people chosen to represent others

Etymology

From Latin 'missio' (act of sending), from 'mittere' (to send).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08419399-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈmɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a particular task or duty assigned to a person or group, often one that is important or official
an official or important assignment

Examples

The government sent her on a top-secret mission overseas.
Completing this rescue mission will require teamwork and courage.
He considered it his personal mission to help the community.

Synset Examples

a confidential mission to London
his charge was to deliver a message

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assignment - a task given to someone as part of their job or studies
  • Synonym: duty - something that one is required to do as part of a job or position
  • Degree Lower: routine - a regular or usual task (less important than mission)

Etymology

From Latin 'missio' (sending), from 'mittere' (to send).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00732520-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
an organized activity or work carried out by a group, especially by religious missionaries, to spread their faith or provide charitable service in a region
religious outreach or charitable work

Examples

The church established a mission in the remote village to help improve education and healthcare.
She devoted her life to the mission in Africa, working tirelessly for the local community.
Many volunteers joined the mission to provide food and shelter to those in need.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ministry - the work or vocation of a minister in a Christian church

Etymology

From Latin missio (the act of sending), from mittere (to send).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00732282-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
a specific operation or task assigned to a person or group, especially by a higher authority, such as in the military or official contexts
assigned operation or task

Examples

The pilots prepared thoroughly for their dangerous mission over enemy territory.
She was assigned a top-secret mission that required absolute discretion.
Our unit completed its reconnaissance mission successfully and returned to base.

Synset Examples

the planes were on a bombing mission

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assignment - a task or piece of work assigned to someone
  • Synonym: operation - a planned activity involving many people, especially in the military
  • Degree Lower: routine - a regular or habitual task, less urgent than a mission

Etymology

From Latin 'missio' (sending), from 'mittere' (to send).

5. NOUN

/ ˈmɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈmɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
an organization of missionaries in a foreign country, sent to spread their religion and do charitable work
religious organization in a foreign land

Examples

The Catholic mission set up a school and a clinic in the remote village.
She volunteered at a Baptist mission during her travels in Africa.
The mission provides food and shelter as well as religious instruction.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: church - a building for Christian worship

Etymology

From Latin missio (sending, dispatching).
