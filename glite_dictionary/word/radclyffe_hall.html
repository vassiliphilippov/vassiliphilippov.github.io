Radclyffe Hall

1. PROPN

/ ˈrædˌklaɪf hɔl / (AmE), / ˈræd.klɪf hɔːl / (BrE)
(1880–1943) English author best known for her novel 'The Well of Loneliness', which was banned in Britain due to its depiction of a lesbian relationship.
English novelist; author of 'The Well of Loneliness'

Examples

Radclyffe Hall is celebrated for challenging social norms with her groundbreaking novel 'The Well of Loneliness'.
Many literary scholars have explored the impact of Radclyffe Hall on LGBTQ representation in British literature.
The works of Radclyffe Hall continue to be discussed in studies of 20th-century English authors.
