passer-by

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæsər ˌbaɪ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsə ˌbaɪ / (BrE)
A person who is going past a place, especially by chance.
person passing by

Examples

A passer-by noticed the smoke and called the fire department immediately.
Several passers-by stopped to help the injured cyclist.
The artist enjoyed sketching passers-by as they walked through the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pedestrian - a person walking along a road or in a developed area

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'pass' and the preposition 'by', modeled on the pattern of agent nouns with prepositions.
