racemose

1. ADJ

/ ˈreɪsɪˌmoʊs / (AmE), / ˈræsɪməʊs / (BrE)
(of flowers or inflorescences) having stalked flowers arranged along an elongated stem, with the lower flowers opening first as the stem continues growing.
having stalked flowers on a growing stem

Examples

Lily of the valley has a racemose inflorescence, with new flowers opening progressively from the bottom up.
The botanist described the wildflowers' racemose arrangement as a key identification feature.
Many shrubs display racemose clusters, providing a steady succession of blooms throughout the season.

Synset Examples

lilies of the valley are racemose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spicate - bearing flowers arranged on a spike; a similar type of inflorescence

Etymology

From Latin racemosus, from racemus (a cluster or bunch).
