John of Gaunt

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn əv ɡɑnt / (AmE), / dʒɒn əv ɡɑːnt / (BrE)
(1340–1399) The fourth son of Edward III of England; a powerful nobleman and statesman who served as the effective ruler of England during the later years of Edward III's reign and the minority of Richard II.
English nobleman, son of Edward III

Examples

John of Gaunt played a central role in the politics of late fourteenth-century England.
Many historians credit John of Gaunt with maintaining stability during Richard II's early reign.
John of Gaunt's lineage led to the rise of the House of Lancaster.
