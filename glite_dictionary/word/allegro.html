allegro

1. ADV

/ əˈlɛɡˌroʊ / (AmE), / əˈlɛɡ.rəʊ / (BrE)
at a quick and lively tempo (used especially as a musical direction)
in a quick, lively tempo (music)

Examples

The pianist played the scherzo allegro, as indicated in the score.
Please start the piece allegro to capture its energetic mood.
The conductor signaled the orchestra to resume allegro after the slow section.

Synset Examples

play this section allegro

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: andante - at a moderately slow tempo (music)
  • Degree Higher: presto - at a very fast tempo (music)

Etymology

From Italian 'allegro' (cheerful, lively), from Latin 'alacer' (brisk, lively).
