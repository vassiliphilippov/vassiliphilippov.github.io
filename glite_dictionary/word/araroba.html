araroba

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrəˈroʊbə / (AmE), / ˌarəˈrəʊbə / (BrE)
a bitter yellow powder obtained from the heartwood of certain South American trees, used in the treatment of skin diseases such as psoriasis and eczema.
bitter yellow medicinal powder for skin diseases
medical

Examples

The dermatologist recommended applying araroba to manage the persistent skin rash.
Traditional remedies in South America often include araroba as an ingredient for treating eczema.
Some over-the-counter creams for psoriasis contain extracts of araroba.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'araroba', from Tupi-Guarani 'ararooba' (literally 'tree that produces bitter sap').
