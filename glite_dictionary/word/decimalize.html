to decimalize

1. VERB

/ ˈdɛs.əm.əˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdɛs.ɪ.mə.laɪz / (BrE)
to change or convert something to a decimal system, especially in measurements or currency
to convert to a decimal system

Examples

The government decided to decimalize the currency to simplify transactions.
Several countries decimalized their measurements during the 20th century.
Plans are underway to decimalize the traditional system of weights and measures.

Synset Examples

The country decimalized the currency in 1975

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to metricate - to change to the metric system

Etymology

Formed from "decimal" (from Latin "decimus", tenth) + "-ize".

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈdɛsɪməˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdɛsɪməlaɪz / (BrE)
to change a system, especially of currency or measurement, from fractions to decimals
convert to a decimal system

Examples

Many countries decimalized their currency systems in the twentieth century.
The government decided to decimalize all weight measurements by the end of the decade.
Some industries have resisted efforts to decimalize standards, preferring the old fractional methods.

Synset Examples

Stock prices will be decimalized in the year 2000

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decimalisation - the process of converting to a decimal system; chiefly British spelling

Etymology

From decimal + -ize, modeled after French décimaliser.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
