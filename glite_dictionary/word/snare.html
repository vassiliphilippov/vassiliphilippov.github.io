snare

1. NOUN

/ snɛr / (AmE), / snɛə / (BrE)
a surgical instrument made of a wire loop that can be tightened around the base of polyps or small tumors to cut them off, typically used inside body cavities
surgical instrument: wire loop for polyp/tumor removal
medical

Examples

The surgeon used a snare to remove the polyp from the patient's colon.
Flexible snares are often preferred for polyp resection during endoscopic procedures.
A wire snare is an essential tool in gastrointestinal endoscopy.
