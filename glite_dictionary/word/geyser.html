geyser

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡaɪzɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡaɪzə / (BrE)
A natural spring that intermittently ejects hot water and steam into the air, typically as a result of geothermal activity.
a spring that ejects hot water and steam

Examples

Many tourists visit Yellowstone National Park to watch the geyser erupt.
The eruption of the geyser sent boiling water high into the air every hour.
Scientists study the patterns of geysers to understand geothermal activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hot spring - a spring of naturally hot water

Etymology

From Icelandic 'geysir', from 'geysa' (to gush).

2. VERB

/ ˈɡaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈɡiːzə / (BrE)
to burst out or overflow suddenly and forcefully like a geyser
to burst or overflow forcefully

Examples

Water suddenly geysered from the broken main, flooding the street in minutes.
The champagne geysered out of the bottle as soon as the cork was popped.
After the rainstorm, mud geysered up through the cracks in the playground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spout - to send out a liquid forcefully in a stream
