potence

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊtəns / (AmE), / ˈpəʊtəns / (BrE)
the state of being potent, especially a man's ability to achieve or sustain an erection and have sexual intercourse
male sexual potency; ability to have intercourse

Examples

Medical advances have helped improve potence in men with certain health conditions.
Concerns about lost potence sometimes affect a man's confidence.
The treatment aims to restore potence and sexual wellbeing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: impotence - inability of a man to achieve or sustain an erection
  • Synonym: virility - manly qualities, especially sexual potency

Etymology

From Latin potēntia (power, potency) via Old French potence (power, might).
