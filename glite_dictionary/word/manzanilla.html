Manzanilla

1. NOUN

/ ˌmænzəˈniːjə / (AmE), / ˌmansəˈniːjə / (BrE)
A very dry, pale Spanish sherry wine, especially from the Sanlúcar de Barrameda region.
very dry pale Spanish sherry wine

Examples

She ordered a glass of Manzanilla to pair with her seafood paella.
Manzanilla is prized for its crisp, salty freshness, especially when served chilled.
Many wine experts consider Manzanilla the best sherry to enjoy with tapas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fino - a type of dry, pale Spanish sherry similar to Manzanilla

Etymology

Spanish, meaning 'little apple,' said to refer to its apple-like flavor notes.
