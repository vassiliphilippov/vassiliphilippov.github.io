toboggan

1. VERB

/ təˈbɑːɡən / (AmE), / təˈbɒɡən / (BrE)
to travel or slide downhill over snow or ice using a toboggan or similar sled
slide downhill on a toboggan

Examples

Every winter, children gather at the hill to toboggan down the snowy slopes.
We tobogganed all afternoon until the sun began to set.
She loves tobogganing with her friends after a fresh snowfall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sled - travel on a sled over snow or ice

Etymology

From Canadian French 'tabaganne', likely from an Algonquian language (such as Mi'kmaq or Abenaki).

2. NOUN

/ təˈbɑɡən / (AmE), / təˈbɒɡən / (BrE)
a long, narrow sled made from a single flat board or several boards, curving up at the front and without runners, used for sliding downhill over snow or ice
a sled without runners, used on snow

Examples

The children rode a toboggan down the snowy hill, laughing all the way.
We brought our old wooden toboggan to the winter festival for the race.
After the first big snowfall, the park was filled with kids and their toboggans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sled - a vehicle of various forms mounted on runners for use on snow, ice, or grass

Etymology

From Canadian French 'tabaganne', from Algonquian (Micmac or Abenaki) word for 'sled'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04450567-n
Synset POS: n
