to vanish

1. VERB

/ ˈvænɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈvanɪʃ / (BrE)
to disappear suddenly and completely; to cease to exist or be visible
disappear suddenly and completely

Examples

The city seemed to vanish overnight, leaving no trace behind.
Old traditions sometimes vanish in the face of modernization.
As the fog lifted, the mysterious shapes began to vanish from view.

Synset Examples

An entire civilization vanished

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disappear - to cease to be visible or present
  • Degree Lower: fade - to gradually disappear or become less distinct
  • Antonym: to remain - to continue to exist or stay

Etymology

From Latin 'vanescere' meaning 'to vanish' (from 'vanus' empty).

2. VERB

/ ˈvænɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈvænɪʃ / (BrE)
to disappear suddenly and completely, especially from sight or notice
disappear suddenly or completely

Examples

All traces of the ancient civilization seemed to vanish overnight.
As the fog lifted, the mysterious figure slowly vanished from view.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disappear - to cease to be visible or present
  • Antonym: to appear - to become visible or come into sight

Etymology

From Latin evanescere (to disappear) via Old French esvanir.

3. VERB

/ ˈvænɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈvænɪʃ / (BrE)
to disappear suddenly and completely, especially in a way that is mysterious or unexpected
disappear suddenly and completely

Examples

As the mist thickened, the distant mountains seemed to vanish from view.
My keys seem to vanish whenever I'm in a hurry to leave the house.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disappear - cease to be visible or present
  • Antonym: to appear - become visible or come into view

Etymology

4. VERB

/ ˈvænɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈvanɪʃ / (BrE)
to disappear or pass away suddenly and completely, especially quickly
disappear rapidly; pass away quickly

Examples

As soon as the sun set, the warm daylight seemed to vanish from the landscape.
The excitement of the crowd would often vanish within moments of the final whistle.
Years can vanish before you realize how much has changed.

Synset Examples

Time flies like an arrow
Time fleeing beneath him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disappear - cease to be visible or present
  • Synonym: fade - gradually disappear or lose strength
  • Antonym: to linger - stay longer than expected; not disappear quickly

Etymology

From Latin 'evanescere' (to disappear), via Middle English and Old French.

5. VERB

/ ˈvænɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈvænɪʃ / (BrE)
to decrease rapidly and disappear completely, especially in a way that is surprising or difficult to follow
disappear completely and rapidly

Examples

All the evidence seemed to vanish overnight, leaving nothing behind.
Profits can vanish quickly in a volatile stock market.
His hopes for a promotion vanished as soon as he heard the news.

Synset Examples

the money vanished in las Vegas
all my stock assets have vaporized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disappear - to cease to be visible or present
  • Synonym: vanish into thin air - to disappear suddenly and completely, leaving no trace

Etymology

From Old French *esvanir* (to disappear), from Latin *evanescere* (to vanish, fade away).
