Harry S Truman

1. PROPN

/ ˈhæri ɛs ˈtruːmən / (AmE), / ˈhæri ɛs ˈtruːmən / (BrE)
(1884–1972) the 33rd President of the United States (1945–1953), who authorized the use of atomic bombs against Japan and led the country through the end of World War II and the start of the Cold War.
33rd U.S. President (1945–1953)

Examples

Harry S Truman became President after Franklin D. Roosevelt's death in 1945.
The decision to drop atomic bombs on Japan was made by Harry S Truman.
Many historians debate Harry S Truman's foreign policy in the early years of the Cold War.
