Harry S. Truman

1. PROPN

/ ˈhæri ˌɛs ˈtruːmən / (AmE), / ˈhæri ˌɛs ˈtruːmən / (BrE)
(1884–1972) the 33rd President of the United States, serving from 1945 to 1953, who authorized the use of atomic bombs against Japan and led the country through the final stages of World War II and the beginning of the Cold War.
33rd U.S. president (1945–1953)

Examples

Harry S. Truman became president following Franklin D. Roosevelt's death in 1945.
The decision to drop atomic bombs on Japan was made by Harry S. Truman.
Many historians credit Harry S. Truman with shaping postwar American foreign policy.
