Bryozoa

1. NOUN

/ braɪ.əˈzoʊ.ə / (AmE), / ˌbrʌɪ.əˈzəʊ.ə / (BrE)
A phylum of small aquatic animals, primarily marine or freshwater, that form colonies of interconnected individuals called zooids.
phylum of colonial aquatic animals

Examples

Researchers discovered a thriving colony of Bryozoa attached to the shipwreck.
Bryozoa play an important role in aquatic ecosystems as filter feeders.
The museum exhibit displayed fossils of ancient Bryozoa from the Paleozoic era.

Etymology

From Greek bryon (moss) + zoon (animal), referring to their moss-like appearance.
