to argufy

1. VERB

/ ˈɑrɡjəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡjʊfaɪ / (BrE)
to argue or dispute, usually over trivial matters
to argue or dispute
archaic

Examples

There's no need to argufy about such a minor detail.
They would argufy for hours over the simplest things.
I refuse to argufy with you when you're in that mood.

Synset Examples

We quarreled over the question as to who discovered America
These two fellows are always scrapping over something

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarrel - to argue or have a heated disagreement
  • Synonym: debate - to discuss opposing points, especially formally

Etymology

From English 'argue' with an extended, dialectal or humorous ending '-fy'.
