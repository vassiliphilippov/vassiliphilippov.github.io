Bishop Berkeley

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɪʃəp ˈbɜrkli / (AmE), / ˈbɪʃəp ˈbɑːkli / (BrE)
(1685–1753) An Irish philosopher and Anglican bishop known for his influential criticism of materialism and for developing the doctrine of idealism.
Irish philosopher and bishop (1685–1753)

Examples

Bishop Berkeley argued that reality consists only of minds and their ideas.
Many university courses include readings from Bishop Berkeley in discussions of idealism.
The writings of Bishop Berkeley continue to influence both philosophy and theology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: George Berkeley - Irish philosopher who advocated subjective idealism
