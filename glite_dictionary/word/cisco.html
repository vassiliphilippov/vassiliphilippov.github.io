cisco

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪskoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɪskəʊ / (BrE)
a small, silvery, cold-water fish of the whitefish family, found in the lakes of northern North America, especially Lake Superior.
a small, cold-water fish of North American lakes

Examples

Fishermen in the region often catch cisco during the colder months.
The population of cisco has declined in some of the Great Lakes due to overfishing.
Smoked cisco is considered a delicacy in parts of Canada.

Etymology

From Canadian French 'cisco', of Ojibwa origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪskoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɪskəʊ / (BrE)
A silvery freshwater fish found in cold deep lakes of North America, important as food and for sport fishing.
freshwater food fish of North America

Examples

Fishermen in the Great Lakes region often catch cisco in the deeper, colder waters.
Smoked cisco is considered a delicacy by many people in Ontario.
The population of cisco has been affected by changes in lake ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whitefish - a related freshwater fish found in northern lakes, valued for food

Etymology

From Canadian French 'cisco', likely derived from Ojibwa 'zhaashig(amega:)' (a kind of fish).
