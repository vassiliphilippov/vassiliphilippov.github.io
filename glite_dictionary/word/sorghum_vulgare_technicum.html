Sorghum vulgare technicum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔrɡəm vʌlˈɡɛrɛ tɛkˈnɪkəm / (AmE), / ˈsɔːɡəm vʌlˈɡɛːrə tɛkˈnɪkəm / (BrE)
A tall grass cultivated for its long, stiff-branched flower clusters, which are used in making brooms and brushes.
a grass used for making brooms

Examples

Farmers in the region plant Sorghum vulgare technicum to supply material for traditional handcrafted brooms.
In the workshop, she explained how dried Sorghum vulgare technicum is bound together to make sturdy brushes.
The museum features a display on the uses of Sorghum vulgare technicum in 19th-century household tools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broomcorn - another name for Sorghum vulgare technicum, a grass grown for making brooms

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Sorghum' and species name 'vulgare technicum', indicating its common technical (industrial) use.
