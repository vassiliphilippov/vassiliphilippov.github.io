destalinisation

1. NOUN

/ diˌstɑlɪnəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌstælɪnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of eliminating the influence of Joseph Stalin, especially by revising his policies, removing monuments honoring him, and renaming places bearing his name.
removal of Stalin's influence/policies

Examples

The country underwent destalinisation after a period of political reform in the 1950s.
New policies were introduced as part of destalinisation to move away from authoritarian rule.
Monuments dedicated to Stalin were removed during the wave of destalinisation across Eastern Europe.

Synset Examples

his statue was demolished as part of destalinization

Related Terms

  • Synonym: de-Stalinization - the American spelling of 'destalinisation'

Etymology

From de- (removal) + Stalin + -isation, modelled after similar political terms.
