Lacerta viridis

1. NOUN

/ ləˈsɜːrtə vəˈriːdɪs / (AmE), / ləˈsɜːtə vɪˈriːdɪs / (BrE)
a common Eurasian lizard species, about a foot long, known as the European green lizard
a Eurasian green lizard species

Examples

The bright green scales of the Lacerta viridis make it easy to spot in open grasslands.
Researchers observed a Lacerta viridis basking on a sunlit rock during their field study.
Many gardens in southern Europe are home to the Lacerta viridis due to its adaptability.

Etymology

From Latin: 'lacerta' (lizard) and 'viridis' (green).
