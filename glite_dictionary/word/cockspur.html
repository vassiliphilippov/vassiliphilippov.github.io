cockspur

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑk.spɜr / (AmE), / ˈkɒk.spɜː / (BrE)
a stout Old World grass (Dactyloctenium aegyptium), cultivated and growing wild in pastures and for hay
a type of coarse grass used for pasture or hay

Examples

The field was covered in cockspur, providing ample grazing for the cattle.
Farmers often harvest cockspur for use as hay during the dry season.
Some gardeners consider cockspur a weed because of its rapid growth in open ground.

Etymology

Named for the resemblance of its flower spikelets to a rooster's spur.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑkˌspɜr / (AmE), / ˈkɒkˌspɜː / (BrE)
A small, spiny tree native to the West Indies, noted for its thorny branches.
spiny tree native to the West Indies

Examples

The cockspur is commonly found in coastal forests across the West Indies.
Gardeners appreciate the cockspur for its ornamental thorns and distinctive silhouette.
Birds often build nests in the protective branches of the cockspur.

Etymology

Alludes to the shape of the tree's thorns, resembling a cock's spur.
