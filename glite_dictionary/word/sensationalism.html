sensationalism

1. NOUN

/ sɛnˌseɪʃəˈnælɪzəm / (AmE), / sɛnˌseɪʃəˈnælɪzəm / (BrE)
(Philosophy) The ethical doctrine that feeling or sensation is the only criterion for determining what is good.
philosophical doctrine: feeling as sole moral criterion

Examples

Some philosophers advocate sensationalism as a basis for morality, arguing that only feelings can determine good.
His theory of ethics is rooted in sensationalism, prioritizing sensation above reason or tradition.
Critics of sensationalism claim it fails to consider rational or objective standards of right and wrong.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hedonism - the doctrine that pleasure or happiness is the highest good
  • Antonym: utilitarianism - the doctrine that actions are right if they benefit the majority

Etymology

From sensation + -alism, modeled after similar philosophical terms.

2. NOUN

/ sɛnˈseɪʃənəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / sɛnˈseɪʃənəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the use of exciting, shocking, or exaggerated subject matter intended to attract attention or appeal to vulgar tastes
use of shocking or exaggerated content

Examples

The newspaper was accused of sensationalism after publishing shocking headlines about the celebrity scandal.
Many critics argue that media sensationalism distracts from more serious news reporting.
The film was criticized for its sensationalism and graphic content rather than its artistic merit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensationalist - a person who uses or promotes sensationalism

Etymology

From sensation + -alism, first recorded in English in the early 19th century.

4. NOUN

/ sɛnˈseɪʃənəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / sɛnˈseɪʃənəˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
(Philosophy) The doctrine that knowledge originates solely from sensory experience.
philosophical doctrine: knowledge from experience

Examples

In the lecture, she explained sensationalism as the view that all ideas arise from physical sensations.
Critics of sensationalism argue that reason also plays a vital role in gaining knowledge.
The philosophy department offered a course on sensationalism and other theories of perception.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empiricism - the philosophical belief that knowledge comes primarily from sensory experience
  • Antonym: rationalism - the philosophical doctrine that knowledge comes primarily from reason

Etymology

From 'sensation' + '-alism'; 'sensation' from Latin 'sensus' (feeling, perception).
