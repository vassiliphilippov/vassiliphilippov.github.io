scorpionfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɔːr.pi.ənˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈskɔː.pi.ənˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a marine fish with a tapering body, spiny armored head, and venomous spines, found especially in warm seas
marine fish with armored head and venomous spines

Examples

Divers are advised to avoid touching the scorpionfish because of its venomous spines.
The vibrant coloration of a scorpionfish helps it blend into coral reefs while waiting for prey.
A fisherman caught a rare scorpionfish during his trip in the tropical waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lionfish - a type of scorpionfish with distinctive stripes and elongated fins

Etymology

From 'scorpion' (due to the resemblance of its spines to a scorpion’s sting) + 'fish'.
