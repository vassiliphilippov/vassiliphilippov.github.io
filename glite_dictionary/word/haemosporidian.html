haemosporidian

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːmoʊspəˈrɪdiən / (AmE), / ˌhiːməʊspəˈrɪdiən / (BrE)
A minute protozoan parasite that, at some stage in its life cycle, infects the blood cells of vertebrates, including many important pathogens.
protozoan parasite infecting vertebrate blood cells
medical

Examples

The haemosporidian infected the bird's red blood cells, causing severe illness.
Malaria parasites are classified as haemosporidians due to their complex life cycles involving blood.
Researchers studied several haemosporidian species to understand their role in avian disease transmission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protozoan - microscopic single-celled organism, often pathogenic

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' (blood) + 'sporos' (seed), referring to its blood-based life cycle.
