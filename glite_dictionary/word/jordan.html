Jordan

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒɔrdn̩ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːdən / (BrE)
A country in southwestern Asia, officially known as the Hashemite Kingdom of Jordan, located on the east bank of the Jordan River.
Arab kingdom in southwestern Asia

Examples

I visited Jordan last summer to explore the ancient city of Petra.
The capital of Jordan is Amman, known for its history and culture.
Jordan shares borders with Saudi Arabia, Iraq, Syria, Israel, and Palestine.

Etymology

Named after the Jordan River, from Hebrew 'Yarden'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈdʒɔrdn̩ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːdn̩ / (BrE)
a river in the Middle East that flows through Israel and Jordan into the Dead Sea, significant in religious history as the site where John the Baptist baptized Jesus.
river in the Middle East, site of Jesus' baptism

Examples

Many Christian pilgrims visit the Jordan each year to see where Jesus was baptized.
The Jordan serves as a natural boundary between Jordan and Israel.
The water level of the Jordan has declined due to regional droughts and water diversion.

Etymology

From Hebrew *Yarden* (descender), referring to the river's descent from its source to the Dead Sea.
