hemoglobinopathy

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːməˌɡloʊbɪˈnɑpəθi / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˌɡləʊbɪˈnɒpəθi / (BrE)
any of a group of blood disorders characterized by the presence of abnormal hemoglobin in red blood cells
blood disorder with abnormal hemoglobin
medical

Examples

Sickle cell anemia is a well-known hemoglobinopathy that affects millions of people worldwide.
Doctors often order blood tests when they suspect a patient may have a hemoglobinopathy.
Thalassemias are classified as a type of hemoglobinopathy due to their abnormal hemoglobin structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sickle cell anemia - a type of hemoglobinopathy with sickle-shaped red blood cells
  • Synonym: thalassemia - a group of inherited hemoglobinopathies marked by abnormal hemoglobin production

Etymology

From hemo- (relating to blood) + globin (protein component of hemoglobin) + -pathy (disease), formed in medical English.
