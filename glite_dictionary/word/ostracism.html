ostracism

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑstrəˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɒstrəˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
the act of deliberately excluding someone from a group or society, often by general agreement
exclusion from a group or society

Examples

After speaking out against corruption, he faced social ostracism from his community.
Fear of ostracism often keeps people from expressing unpopular opinions.
The company was criticized for its policy of ostracism towards whistleblowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exclusion - the act of preventing someone from entering or being part of something
  • Antonym: inclusion - the act of including or being included within a group or structure

Etymology

From Greek *ostrakismos* (banishment by voting with potsherds).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑstrəˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɒstrəsɪzəm / (BrE)
The practice or state of deliberately excluding someone from a group, society, or community.
exclusion from a group or society

Examples

The fear of ostracism often discourages people from speaking out against injustice.
After the scandal, he experienced social ostracism and found it difficult to make friends.
Victims of ostracism can suffer from loneliness and low self-esteem.

Synset Examples

the association should get rid of its elderly members — not by euthanasia, of course, but by Coventry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exclusion - the act of preventing someone from entering or participating
  • Antonym: acceptance - the process of being received into a group or community

Etymology

From Greek *ostrakismos* (banishment by voting with potsherds).
