sleuth

1. NOUN

/ sluːθ / (AmE), / sluːθ / (BrE)
A detective, especially one who investigates and follows clues to solve crimes.
detective; investigator

Examples

The famous sleuth solved the case with his remarkable attention to detail.
As an amateur sleuth, she loved reading mystery novels and solving puzzles.
The police brought in their best sleuth to follow the trail left by the thief.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detective - a person, especially a police officer, whose job is to investigate crimes and catch criminals

Etymology

From Old Norse 'slóð' (track, trail), originally referring to a bloodhound used in tracking.

2. VERB

/ sluːθ / (AmE), / sluːθ / (BrE)
to investigate or search for information about someone or something in a secretive or discreet way
to investigate secretly

Examples

Reporters tried to sleuth out the source of the leaked documents.
She spent the afternoon sleuthing through old archives at the library.
The detective sleuthed quietly behind the suspect all night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to investigate - to carry out a systematic or formal inquiry to discover facts
  • Synonym: spy - to observe secretly

Etymology

Back-formation from 'sleuthhound', meaning a detective or tracker, from Old Norse 'slóð' (track).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
