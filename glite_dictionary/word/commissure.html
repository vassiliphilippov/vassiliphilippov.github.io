commissure

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.məˌʃʊr / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ɪ.ʃjʊə / (BrE)
a bundle of nerve fibers that connects corresponding parts on the two sides of the brain or spinal cord
nerve fiber bundle connecting brain or spinal cord sides
medical

Examples

The corpus callosum is the largest commissure in the human brain.
Damage to a commissure can affect communication between the two hemispheres of the brain.
The anterior commissure plays a role in transferring sensory information.

Etymology

From Latin commissura (a joining together), from committere (to join).
