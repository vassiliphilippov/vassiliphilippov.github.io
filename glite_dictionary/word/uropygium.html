uropygium

1. NOUN

/ ˌjʊrəˈpɪdʒiəm / (AmE), / ˌjʊərəˈpɪdʒɪəm / (BrE)
the posterior part of a bird's body from which the tail feathers grow
part of a bird's body at tail base

Examples

The wax produced by the uropygium helps birds keep their feathers waterproof and flexible.
A bright patch of color can sometimes be seen on the uropygium of certain bird species.
During preening, birds rub their beaks against the uropygium to collect oil for feather maintenance.

Etymology

From Greek oura (tail) + pugion (rump, buttock).
