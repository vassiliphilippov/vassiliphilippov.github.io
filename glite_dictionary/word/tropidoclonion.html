Tropidoclonion

1. NOUN

/ troʊˌpɪdəˈkloʊniːən / (AmE), / trɒˌpɪdəkˈləʊniən / (BrE)
A genus of small North American snakes, commonly known as lined snakes, belonging to the family Colubridae.
genus of small North American lined snakes

Examples

The genus Tropidoclonion is known for its distinctly lined snakes found across the central United States.
Researchers studied Tropidoclonion habitats to understand their environmental requirements.
Many herpetologists are fascinated by the subtle coloration of Tropidoclonion species.

Etymology

From Greek 'tropis' (keel) and 'klonion' (little branch or twig), referring to distinctive features of the snakes' scales.
