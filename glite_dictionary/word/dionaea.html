Dionaea

1. NOUN

/ daɪ.əˈni.i.ə / (AmE), / daɪ.əˈniː.ə / (BrE)
A genus of carnivorous plants in the family Droseraceae, best known for including the Venus flytrap.
genus of carnivorous plants (e.g., Venus flytrap)

Examples

The Dionaea is famous for its unique mechanism of trapping insects.
Botanists have long studied Dionaea for its rapid plant movements.
The Venus flytrap, or Dionaea muscipula, is native to subtropical wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Venus flytrap - a well-known species of Dionaea, a carnivorous plant

Etymology

From Greek 'Dionaea', meaning 'daughter of Dione' (referring to Aphrodite/Venus in mythology).

2. NOUN

/ daɪ.əˈni.ə / (AmE), / daɪ.əˈniː.ə / (BrE)
a genus of carnivorous plants in the family Droseraceae, best known for the Venus flytrap (Dionaea muscipula)
genus of Venus flytrap plants

Examples

The Dionaea genus includes the famous Venus flytrap, native to the Carolinas in the United States.
Botanists study Dionaea for its unique mechanism of capturing insects.
The cultivation of Dionaea species requires a specific soil and moisture regime.

Etymology

From Greek Dione (a goddess, mother of Aphrodite) + -aea, of or pertaining to.
