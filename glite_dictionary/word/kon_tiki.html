Kon Tiki

1. NOUN

/ kɑn ˈtiːki / (AmE), / kɒn ˈtiːki / (BrE)
a light raft made of balsa wood, especially one modeled after or referring to the raft used by Thor Heyerdahl in his 1947 Pacific expedition
light balsa raft; Heyerdahl's 1947 raft

Examples

The explorers journeyed across the Pacific on the Kon Tiki to test ancient sailing routes.
A replica of the Kon Tiki now stands in a museum in Oslo.
Inspired by Thor Heyerdahl, students built their own Kon Tiki out of balsa logs.

Etymology

Named after the original Kon-Tiki raft, from Quechua and Inca mythology (Kon-Tiki, a deity), used by Thor Heyerdahl for his 1947 Pacific crossing.
