to inhale

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈheɪl / (AmE), / ɪnˈheɪl / (BrE)
to breathe in air, smoke, or gas through the nose or mouth into the lungs
breathe in air or gas

Examples

She closed her eyes and inhaled the scent of blooming flowers.
Please inhale deeply, then exhale slowly for the test.
He accidentally inhaled some smoke while cooking.

Synset Examples

Inhale deeply
inhale the fresh mountain air
The patient has trouble inspiring
The lung cancer patient cannot inspire air very well

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to exhale - to breathe out air from the lungs
  • Synonym: to breathe in - to draw air into the lungs
  • Synonym: to inspire - to draw air into the lungs (formal/medical)

Etymology

From Latin 'inhalare', from 'in-' (in) + 'halare' (breathe).

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈheɪl / (AmE), / ɪnˈheɪl / (BrE)
to breathe air, smoke, or gas into the lungs
breathe in; draw into lungs

Examples

She took a moment to inhale deeply before speaking to the audience.
If you inhale smoke during a fire, it can be very dangerous.
He laughed so hard that he accidentally inhaled some water.

Synset Examples

Clinton smoked marijuana but never inhaled

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to exhale - to breathe out air from the lungs

Etymology

From Latin 'inhalare', from 'in-' (into) + 'halare' (breathe).
