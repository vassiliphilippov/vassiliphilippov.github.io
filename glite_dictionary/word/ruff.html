ruff

1. NOUN

/ rʌf / (AmE), / rʌf / (BrE)
a large, stiff, high collar of pleated fabric, often worn in the 16th and 17th centuries as a fashionable accessory
large stiff pleated collar

Examples

The portrait showed the nobleman in a velvet doublet and a ruff around his neck.
Queen Elizabeth I was famous for wearing an elaborate ruff as part of her royal attire.
Theater productions often use a ruff to recreate period costumes from the Renaissance.

Etymology

From Middle French *rouf*, from Old French *ruf* (“rough”), possibly referring to the frilled nature.

2. NOUN

/ rʌf / (AmE), / rʌf / (BrE)
A large wading bird (Calidris pugnax) found in Eurasia, with the male distinguished by a conspicuous erectile neck ruff during the breeding season.
Eurasian wading bird with male neck ruff

Examples

During mating season, the male ruff displays a spectacular collar of feathers to attract females.
We spotted a ruff feeding at the edge of the marsh with other wading birds.
The unique breeding plumage of the ruff makes it a favorite among birdwatchers.

Etymology

From Middle Dutch 'roef', meaning 'ruff' or 'frill', referencing the bird's distinctive plumage.

3. VERB

/ rʌf / (AmE), / rʌf / (BrE)
(in card games) to play a trump card in order to win a trick when a suit has been led that the player does not hold.
play a trump card in cards

Examples

If you have no hearts left, you may ruff with a spade.
She expertly ruffed her partner's lead to win the trick.
You should avoid ruffing too early in the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trump - to play a trump card in a card game

Etymology

From French 'roufle' (a term in card playing), probably of Germanic origin.

5. NOUN

/ rʌf / (AmE), / rʌf / (BrE)
In card games, the act of playing a trump card to win a trick when unable to follow the suit led.
trump card play in card games

Examples

Unable to follow suit, he decided to ruff with a heart and took the trick.
Her last two ruffs surprised the players and turned the game in her favor.
A clever ruff can sometimes change the outcome of a close match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trump - A card that ranks above the others in a particular suit

Etymology

Probably from the alteration of 'rough', from Middle English 'roughen' (to make rough), in specialized gaming context.
