vaporiser

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪpəˌraɪzər / (AmE), / ˈveɪpəraɪzə / (BrE)
A device that emits a substance in the form of vapor, especially for medicinal inhalation.
device that emits vapor, esp. for medicine
british

Examples

The doctor recommended using a vaporiser to ease her breathing during the night.
He filled the vaporiser with eucalyptus oil before turning it on.
Many people use a vaporiser to add moisture to dry indoor air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vaporizer - device that emits a substance in vapor form, especially for medicinal use (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'vapor' + '-iser', modelled on analogous formation in Latin-based words.
