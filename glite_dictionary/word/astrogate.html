to astrogate

1. VERB

/ ˈæs.trəˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈæs.trəˌɡeɪt / (BrE)
to navigate or guide a spacecraft in interplanetary or interstellar travel.
navigate in space travel

Examples

The pilot had to astrogate through a dangerous asteroid belt to reach Mars safely.
It takes years of training to learn how to astrogate using only the stars.
The crew depended on her skills to astrogate the vessel across the vast emptiness between planets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to navigate - to plan and direct the course of a ship, aircraft, etc.

Etymology

From astro- (relating to stars or space) + -gate (from 'navigate').

2. VERB

/ ˈæstəˌɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˈæstəˌɡreɪt / (BrE)
to navigate a spacecraft through space
navigate in space

Examples

The pilot had to astrogate the ship through a dense asteroid belt.
Learning how to astrogate is a crucial skill for any aspiring starship captain.
As she astrogated toward the distant nebula, the crew watched the navigation screens intently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to navigate - to plan and direct the course of a vessel or vehicle

Etymology

Formed from astro- (relating to stars or space) and -gate (from navigate).
