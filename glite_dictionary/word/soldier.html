soldier

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊldʒər / (AmE), / ˈsəʊldʒə / (BrE)
An enlisted man or woman who serves in an army, especially one who is not an officer.
enlisted member of an army

Examples

The soldier marched in perfect step with his unit.
As a soldier, she learned discipline and teamwork.
The wounded soldiers were brought back from the battlefield.

Synset Examples

the soldiers stood at attention

Related Terms

  • Antonym: officer - a member of the armed forces who holds a position of authority, usually with commissioned rank
  • Synonym: warrior - a person experienced or specialized in combat or warfare, especially outside a formal military

Etymology

From Old French soudeour (mercenary), from soudee (soldier's pay), from Latin solidus (a Roman coin).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊldʒər / (AmE), / ˈsəʊldʒə / (BrE)
A wingless sterile ant or termite with a large head and powerful jaws, adapted for defending the colony.
defensive caste of ant or termite

Examples

Within the colony, soldier ants protect against invading insects.
The soldier termites blocked the entrance with their large heads and powerful jaws.
Unlike workers, soldier ants are easily recognized by their massive mandibles.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: worker - a sterile member of an ant or termite colony that performs labor for the colony
  • Degree Higher: queen - the fertile female in an ant or termite colony

Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈsoʊldʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊldʒə / (BrE)
to serve as a soldier in the military
serve as a soldier

Examples

After high school, he decided to soldier for four years in the army.
Many young men were required to soldier during the war.
She soldiered bravely through many difficult missions overseas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serve - perform duties for or be employed by an organization or person, especially in the military
