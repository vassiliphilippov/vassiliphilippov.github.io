to deter

1. VERB

/ dɪˈtɜr / (AmE), / dɪˈtɜː / (BrE)
to discourage someone from taking an action by persuasion or warning of possible consequences
discourage by persuasion or warning

Examples

Strict laws can deter people from committing crimes.
The heavy rain did not deter us from going on our hike.
Negative reviews may deter customers from trying the new restaurant.

Synset Examples

Negative campaigning will only dissuade people

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dissuade - to persuade someone not to do something
  • Antonym: to encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to someone (opposite of deter)

Etymology

From Latin deterrere, from de- (away) + terrere (to frighten).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈtɝː / (AmE), / dɪˈtɜː / (BrE)
to make someone decide not to do something, especially by making them realize it will have bad consequences or by showing opposition
to discourage from an action

Examples

The government hopes the new law will deter people from texting while driving.
The presence of security cameras helped deter theft in the store.
High fines are intended to deter illegal dumping in the park.

Synset Examples

We should discourage this practice among our youth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to discourage - to try to prevent someone from doing something
  • Antonym: to encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to someone (opposite sense)

Etymology

From Latin deterrēre: de- (away) + terrēre (to frighten).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00910052-v
Synset POS: v
