infiltrator

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.fɪlˌtreɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪl.treɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person who gains access to a place or organization secretly, especially in order to obtain secret information or cause damage
person who enters secretly for espionage

Examples

The organization was compromised by an infiltrator who shared confidential data with the enemy.
Authorities arrested the infiltrator before he could access the restricted area.
During the investigation, they discovered an infiltrator posing as an employee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spy - a person employed to obtain secret information about an enemy or competitor
  • Synonym: agent - a person who acts on behalf of another, especially for secretive purposes
  • Antonym: defender - a person who protects from harm or danger; an antonym in the context of adversaries

Etymology

From Latin 'infiltrare' (to filter in), via infiltrate + -or.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.fɪlˌtreɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fɪlˌtreɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person who enters or gains access to a place or organization secretly, especially in order to acquire secret information or cause harm
a secret intruder, often hostile

Examples

The security forces managed to capture the infiltrator before he could access the confidential files.
She posed as an employee but was discovered as an infiltrator trying to steal sensitive data.
During the raid, the team identified a potential infiltrator among the group of suspects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spy - a person who secretly collects and reports information about enemies or competitors
  • Synonym: invader - someone who enters a place in order to take control by force or deception

Etymology

From Latin 'infiltrare' (to filter in), from 'in-' (into) + 'filtrum' (felt, filter).
