multivalent

1. ADJ

/ ˌmʌl.tɪˈveɪ.lənt / (AmE), / ˌmʌl.tɪˈveɪ.lənt / (BrE)
(of a chemical element, ion, or molecule) having more than one valence, or a valence of three or higher.
having more than one valence
medical

Examples

This compound is multivalent and can form several types of bonds.
A multivalent vaccine targets multiple strains of a virus in one shot.
Iron is multivalent, existing in both Fe2+ and Fe3+ ionic states.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monovalent - having a valence of one
  • Antonym: bivalent - having a valence of two

Etymology

From Latin multi- (many) + valent (from valens, present participle of valere, to be strong or powerful).

2. ADJ

/ ˌmʌltiˈveɪlənt / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪˈveɪlənt / (BrE)
having many values, meanings, or appeals; capable of being interpreted or applied in several ways
having multiple values or meanings

Examples

The novel offers a multivalent narrative open to diverse interpretations.
Her sculptures are intentionally multivalent, conveying different emotions to each viewer.
Critics praised the movie for its multivalent symbolism and rich layers of meaning.

Synset Examples

subtle, multivalent allegory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambiguous - having more than one possible meaning
  • Antonym: univalent - having only one value or meaning

Etymology

From Latin multi- (many) + valentia (strength, worth).

3. ADJ

/ ˌmʌl.tɪˈveɪ.lənt / (AmE), / ˌmʌl.tɪˈveɪ.lənt / (BrE)
(of chromosomes) involving the association of three or more homologous chromosomes during the first division of meiosis.
association of multiple chromosomes in meiosis
medical

Examples

In certain plant species, multivalent chromosomes are observed during meiosis.
The formation of multivalent associations can lead to complex genetic inheritance patterns.
Researchers noted the presence of multivalent structures in the hybrid cells under microscopic examination.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bivalent - pair of homologous chromosomes associated during meiosis

Etymology

From Latin 'multi-' (many) and 'valens' (having power), used in scientific terminology.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01551174-a
Synset POS: a
