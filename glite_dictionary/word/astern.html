astern

1. ADV

/ əˈstɝn / (AmE), / əˈstɜːn / (BrE)
behind or towards the rear of a ship or aircraft
behind a ship or aircraft

Examples

The lifeboat drifted astern as the ship moved forward.
Enemy aircraft approached from astern during the training exercise.
He noticed floating debris astern of the vessel.

Synset Examples

we dropped her astern on the end of a seven-inch manilla, and she laid comfortably on the ebb tide

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ahead - in front of a ship or aircraft

Etymology

From Middle English 'astern', from Old Norse 'á stjórn' (toward the rudder), with 'stjórn' meaning 'rudder'.

2. ADV

/ əˈstɝn / (AmE), / əˈstɜːn / (BrE)
toward the stern of a ship or aircraft; in a backward direction
toward the rear (nautical/aviation)

Examples

With the engine in reverse, the boat slowly moved astern away from the dock.
A loud noise made the captain order the crew to go astern immediately.
As soon as the obstacle appeared, the pilot shifted the propellers to drive the craft astern.

Synset Examples

the steamer went astern at half speed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ahead - toward the front (especially of a ship or vehicle)

Etymology

From a- (in the direction of) + stern (rear of a ship).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00277302-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ əˈstɝn / (AmE), / əˈstɜːn / (BrE)
towards or at the rear (stern) of a ship or the tail of an aircraft
at or towards the rear of a vessel or aircraft

Examples

The lookout saw another vessel astern and alerted the captain.
As the boat drifted astern, the crew prepared to drop anchor.
He glanced astern to check the ship's wake.

Synset Examples

stow the luggage aft
ships with square sails sail fairly efficiently with the wind abaft
the captain looked astern to see what the fuss was about

Related Terms

  • Antonym: forward - toward the front (bow) of a ship or nose of an aircraft
  • Synonym: aft - toward the stern (rear) of a ship or aircraft

Etymology

From 'a-' (meaning 'in, on') + 'stern' (the rear of a ship).

Source: WordNet
