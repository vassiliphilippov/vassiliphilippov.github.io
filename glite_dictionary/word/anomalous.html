anomalous

1. ADJ

/ əˈnɑːmələs / (AmE), / əˈnɒmələs / (BrE)
different from what is usual or expected; deviating from the normal or typical order, form, or rule.
deviating from the norm

Examples

The results of the experiment were so anomalous that scientists questioned the methodology.
His anomalous behavior at the meeting raised concerns among his colleagues.
The region's anomalous weather patterns have baffled meteorologists for decades.

Synset Examples

advanced forms of life may be anomalous in the universe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abnormal - not normal; unusual or unexpected
  • Antonym: normal - conforming to the standard or common type

Etymology

From Greek anōmalos (uneven, irregular), from an- (not) + homalos (even, level).
