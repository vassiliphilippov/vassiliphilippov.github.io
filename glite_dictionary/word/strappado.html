strappado

1. NOUN

/ strəˈpɑdoʊ / (AmE), / strəˈpɑːdəʊ / (BrE)
a form of torture in which a person's hands are tied behind their back and they are suspended by a rope attached to their wrists, then dropped so their fall is abruptly stopped by the rope
a historical method of torture

Examples

The inquisitors threatened the prisoner with strappado if he refused to confess.
Historical records document the use of strappado during medieval interrogations.
Survivors of strappado often suffered from dislocated shoulders and crushed nerves.

Etymology

From Italian 'strappare' (to pull, to tear), related to the method of inflicting pain by pulling.
