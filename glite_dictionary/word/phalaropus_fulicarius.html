Phalaropus fulicarius

1. NOUN

/ fəˌlɛɹ.ə.pəs ˌfjuː.lɪˈkɛɹ.i.əs / (AmE), / fəˌlær.ə.pəs ˌfjuː.lɪˈkeə.ri.əs / (BrE)
A species of phalarope found in northern oceans and lakes, known for its distinctive breeding plumage and migratory habits.
a species of phalarope bird

Examples

The Phalaropus fulicarius migrates thousands of miles between breeding and wintering grounds each year.
During the summer, the plumage of the Phalaropus fulicarius becomes a striking reddish color.
Researchers observed a flock of Phalaropus fulicarius feeding on invertebrates in the Arctic tundra.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red phalarope - common name for Phalaropus fulicarius
  • Degree Higher: phalarope - bird of the family Phalaropodidae, known for swimming ability

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; 'Phalaropus' from Greek 'phalaris' (coot) + 'pous' (foot); 'fulicarius' from Latin 'fulica' (coot).
