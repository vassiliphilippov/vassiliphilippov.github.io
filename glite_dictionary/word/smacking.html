smacking

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsmækɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of striking something or someone with an open hand, typically making a sharp sound
an open-handed blow

Examples

The child's loud smacking of his hands on the table startled everyone in the room.
He received a stinging smacking for talking back to his teacher.
A quick smacking on the back got him to sit up straight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slap - an act of hitting someone or something with the palm of the hand or a flat object

Etymology

From the verb 'smack', likely of imitative origin.
