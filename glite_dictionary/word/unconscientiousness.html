unconscientiousness

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnˌkɑnˈʃɛnʃəsnəs / (AmE), / ˌʌnˌkɒnˈʃɛnʃəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being careless or not attentive to duty; a lack of conscientiousness
lack of carefulness or dutifulness

Examples

His repeated mistakes at work showed a worrying unconscientiousness that alarmed his supervisors.
The project failed partly due to the unconscientiousness of several team members.
Teachers are concerned about the growing unconscientiousness among students when it comes to studying.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conscientiousness - the quality of being diligent and careful

Etymology

Formed from the prefix un- (not) + conscientious + -ness (noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌʌnˌkɑːnˈʃɛnʃəsnəs / (AmE), / ˌʌnˌkɒnˈʃɛnʃəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being unconscientious; a lack of concern for what is right or moral, or willingness to ignore one's conscience
lack of concern for what is right; carelessness of conscience

Examples

His unconscientiousness in handling confidential files put the entire project at risk.
The company's culture of unconscientiousness led to repeated breaches of ethical standards.
She was frustrated by the unconscientiousness of some team members, who ignored important safety protocols.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conscientiousness - the quality of being careful to do what is right

Etymology

From un- (not) + conscientious + -ness (forming nouns denoting state or quality).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
