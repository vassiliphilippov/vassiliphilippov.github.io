shipway

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪpˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈʃɪp.weɪ / (BrE)
a canal large enough to allow seagoing vessels to pass through
canal for seagoing vessels

Examples

The new shipway greatly improved transportation between the river and the sea.
Engineers designed the shipway to accommodate even the largest freighters.
Dredging the shipway ensured that deep-draft vessels could access the port.

Etymology

From 'ship' + 'way', indicating a route for ships.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪpˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈʃɪpweɪ / (BrE)
a sloping structure built to carry ships to and from the water for building, launching, or repair
sloping structure for launching ships

Examples

Workers carefully guided the vessel down the shipway during the launch ceremony.
The old shipway at the dockyard was used for repairing fishing boats.
After construction was complete, the ship was lowered into the river via the shipway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slipway - a ramp or inclined plane for moving boats to and from the water

Etymology

Formed from 'ship' + 'way', meaning a path or route for ships.
