Hyphantria

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈfæntɹiə / (AmE), / hɪˈfan.tri.ə / (BrE)
A genus of moths in the family Erebidae, whose larvae are commonly known as fall webworms.
genus of moths; fall webworms

Examples

The caterpillars of Hyphantria are known for the extensive webs they spin on trees in late summer.
Researchers studied the impact of Hyphantria infestations on local vegetation.
Farmers monitor their crops for signs of Hyphantria to prevent widespread damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fall webworm - the larval stage of Hyphantria moths

Etymology

From Greek 'hyphē' (web) and 'anēr, andros' (man), likely referring to the web-making behavior of the larvae.

2. NOUN

/ haɪˈfæntriə / (AmE), / hɪˈfantrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of moths in the family Erebidae, best known for the fall webworm, whose caterpillars form large silken webs in the branches of various trees.
genus of moths, fall webworms

Examples

The Hyphantria genus includes species whose larvae weave webs on trees during late summer.
Researchers studied the distribution of Hyphantria in North America.
The distinctive webs created by Hyphantria caterpillars can damage the appearance of trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fall webworm - caterpillar of Hyphantria species, forms webbed nests on trees

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek roots hyphē (web) + antron (cave), referring to the web-spinning habit.
