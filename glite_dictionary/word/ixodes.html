Ixodes

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈzoʊdiːz / (AmE), / ɪkˈsəʊdiːz / (BrE)
A genus of hard-bodied ticks, including several species that are important as disease vectors in humans and animals.
genus of hard-bodied ticks

Examples

Several species within the genus Ixodes are known to transmit Lyme disease.
Researchers collected Ixodes ticks from various locations to study their behavior.
The Ixodes genus includes ticks that parasitize mammals, birds, and reptiles.

Etymology

From Greek 'ixōdēs' (sticky), referring to the tick's method of attachment.

2. NOUN

/ aɪˈzoʊdiːz / (AmE), / ɪkˈsəʊdiːz / (BrE)
A genus of ticks in the family Ixodidae, including several species that are important as disease vectors.
a genus of ticks

Examples

The species Ixodes scapularis is known for transmitting Lyme disease.
Several members of the genus Ixodes are of medical significance due to their role as vectors.
Researchers discovered a new species belonging to the genus Ixodes in the remote region.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Ixodes', from Greek 'ixōdēs' meaning 'sticky'.
