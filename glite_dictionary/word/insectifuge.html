insectifuge

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɛktɪˌfjudʒ / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛktɪˌfjuːdʒ / (BrE)
A chemical substance used to repel insects.
chemical that repels insects

Examples

Applying an insectifuge to your skin can help protect you from mosquito bites.
This new insectifuge is effective against a wide range of flying pests.
Scientists are developing an eco-friendly insectifuge that doesn't harm beneficial insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insect repellent - a substance applied to skin, clothing, or surfaces to deter insects

Etymology

From Latin 'insectum' (insect) + 'fugare' (to drive away, to flee).
