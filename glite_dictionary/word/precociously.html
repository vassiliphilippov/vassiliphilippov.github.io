precociously

1. ADV

/ prɪˈkoʊʃəsli / (AmE), / prɪˈkəʊʃəsli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows unusually early development or maturity, especially in abilities or behavior.
in a precocious manner

Examples

The young pianist played precociously, impressing everyone with her talent.
He spoke precociously about topics far beyond his years.
The child answered the teacher's questions precociously, surprising the entire class.

Synset Examples

her child behaves precociously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precocious - having developed abilities at an earlier age than usual

Etymology

From "precocious" + "-ly"; "precocious" from Latin prae- (before) + coquere (to ripen, to cook).
