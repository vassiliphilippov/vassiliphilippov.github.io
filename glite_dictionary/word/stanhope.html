stanhope

1. NOUN

/ ˈstænˌhoʊp / (AmE), / ˈstæn.həʊp / (BrE)
a light, open horse-drawn carriage with two or four wheels and a single seat.
light, open horse-drawn carriage

Examples

The museum displayed a restored stanhope from the early 19th century.
She arrived at the estate in a stylish stanhope, turning heads as she rode past.
Owning a stanhope was once a symbol of high social standing.

Etymology

Named after Captain Hon. Henry FitzRoy Stanhope (1787–1828), who popularized this style of carriage.
