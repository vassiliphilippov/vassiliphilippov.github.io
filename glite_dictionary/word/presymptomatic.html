presymptomatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpriːsɪmp.təˈmæt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌpriː.sɪmp.təˈmæt.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to the stage of a disease before any symptoms have appeared, making diagnosis difficult at this stage.
before symptoms of a disease appear
medical

Examples

Many infectious diseases have a presymptomatic period during which individuals can still spread the virus.
Researchers are studying the presymptomatic phase to better understand early detection methods.
She was identified as positive while still presymptomatic, before any signs of illness developed.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: asymptomatic - showing no symptoms of disease
  • Degree Higher: symptomatic - showing symptoms of a disease

Etymology

From prefix pre- (before) + symptomatic (showing symptoms).
