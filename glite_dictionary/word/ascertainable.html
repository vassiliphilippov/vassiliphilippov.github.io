ascertainable

1. ADJ

/ ˌæsərˈteɪnəbəl / (AmE), / ˌæsəˈteɪnəbl / (BrE)
that can be discovered, determined, or made certain
able to be discovered or determined

Examples

The exact value of the lost property is not ascertainable at this time.
Only ascertainable facts were presented as evidence during the trial.
It is important that the data used in the study is clearly ascertainable.

Synset Examples

ascertainable facts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ascertain - find out or learn with certainty
  • Antonym: indeterminable - impossible to determine

Etymology

From the verb 'ascertain' + suffix '-able', meaning 'able to be ascertained'.
