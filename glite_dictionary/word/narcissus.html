Narcissus

1. PROPN

/ nɑrˈsɪsəs / (AmE), / nɑːˈsɪsəs / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a beautiful young man who fell in love with his own reflection, ultimately leading to his downfall.
a youth from Greek mythology who loved his own reflection

Examples

The myth of Narcissus warns of the dangers of excessive self-love and vanity.
Many artists have depicted Narcissus gazing at his own reflection beside a pool.
The psychologist referenced the story of Narcissus to explain the origins of narcissism.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: narcissism - excessive self-love or admiration

Etymology

From Greek Narkissos, a character in classical mythology.

2. NOUN

/ nɑːrˈsɪsəs / (AmE), / nɑːˈsɪsəs / (BrE)
A genus of Old World perennial bulbous plants in the amaryllis family, which includes daffodils and jonquils, valued for their bright, trumpet-shaped flowers.
genus of bulbous flowering plants (daffodils)

Examples

The Narcissus blooms each spring with vibrant yellow and white flowers in the garden.
Many varieties of Narcissus are cultivated as ornamental plants worldwide.
She planted several Narcissus bulbs along the pathway to add color in early spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daffodil - common name for plants of the genus Narcissus
  • Synonym: jonquil - a species of Narcissus with slender leaves and fragrant yellow flowers

Etymology

From Latin 'narcissus', from Ancient Greek 'nárkissos', possibly related to 'nárkē' (numbness), referring to the plant's narcotic properties.
