Tricholoma sejunctum

1. NOUN

/ ˌtrɪkəˈloʊmə səˈdʒʌŋktəm / (AmE), / ˌtrɪkəˈləʊmə sɪˈdʒʌŋktəm / (BrE)
A species of agaric mushroom with a cap coated in dark fibrils in the center and yellowish margins.
a species of agaric mushroom

Examples

The distinctive cap of Tricholoma sejunctum makes it easy to identify in the field.
Several mycologists have studied the habitat preferences of Tricholoma sejunctum.
The yellowish margins of Tricholoma sejunctum stand out among other forest mushrooms.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus Tricholoma (from Greek 'trichos', hair + 'loma', fringe) and species 'sejunctum', meaning 'separated' in Latin.
