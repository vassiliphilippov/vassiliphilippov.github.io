twosome

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːsəm / (AmE), / ˈtuːsəm / (BrE)
a pair of people who do things together or are closely associated with each other
a pair of people together

Examples

They made a charming twosome as they danced together at the wedding.
We often saw the twosome walking hand in hand along the beach.
In the tournament, the two golfers played as a twosome.

Synset Examples

the engaged couple
an inseparable twosome

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pair - two people or things that are together
  • Degree Higher: threesome - a group of three people doing something together

Etymology

Formed from 'two' + suffix '-some', meaning a group of two.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtuːsəm / (AmE), / ˈtuːsəm / (BrE)
A set or group of two people or things of the same kind.
a pair; set of two

Examples

The restaurant offered a special dessert for a twosome to share.
Each card in the deck comes in a twosome for matching games.
We spotted a twosome of swans gliding across the lake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pair - two things considered together of the same kind
  • Degree Higher: threesome - three people or things together

Etymology

From 'two' + '-some', a suffix meaning 'group of'.
