genre

1. NOUN

/ ˈʒɑːn.rə / (AmE), / ˈʒɒ̃.rə / (BrE)
A category of artistic composition, as in music, literature, or art, characterized by a particular style, form, or content.
category of art or literature

Examples

Science fiction is my favorite genre of literature.
Each film festival focuses on a different genre of cinema.
He decided to experiment with a new genre in his latest painting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: style - a particular procedure by which something is done; a manner or way

Etymology

From French 'genre' (kind, sort), from Latin 'genus'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʒɑn.rə / (AmE), / ˈʒɒ̃.rə / (BrE)
A category or type of literary, artistic, or musical work characterized by a particular style, form, or content.
category of artistic or literary work

Examples

She prefers the romance genre when choosing books to read.
Horror is a genre that often uses suspense and fear to engage the audience.
Jazz is an influential genre within the history of modern music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: category - a class or division of things regarded as having particular shared characteristics

Etymology

From French 'genre' (kind, sort), from Latin 'genus' (race, kind).

3. NOUN

/ ˈʒɑn.rə / (AmE), / ˈʒɒ̃.rə / (BrE)
a style or category of art, music, or literature, especially a particular form of writing distinguished by specific features, themes, or techniques
category or style of art, music, or literature

Examples

She prefers to write in the science fiction genre because it allows for more creativity.
The detective novel is a popular genre among many readers worldwide.
His work spans several genres, including poetry, drama, and prose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: category - a class or division of things regarded as having particular shared characteristics
  • Synonym: style - a manner of doing something, especially writing or art

Etymology

From French 'genre' (kind, sort), from Latin 'genus' (descent, family, type).
