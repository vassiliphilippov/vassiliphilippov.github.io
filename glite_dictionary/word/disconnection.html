disconnection

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪskəˈnɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪskəˈnɛkʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
The state of being disconnected or separated from something; the act or process of breaking a connection.
state of being disconnected

Examples

There was a sudden disconnection in the power supply during the storm.
Some people experience a sense of disconnection from their work or community.
The faulty cable caused the disconnection of the internet service.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: connection - state of being joined or linked

Etymology

From Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'connect' (link together, from Latin 'connectere').

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪskəˈnɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪskəˈnɛkʃən / (BrE)
a lack of understanding or communication between people, groups, or ideas, often resulting in a sense of separation
lack of understanding or connection

Examples

There was a profound disconnection between management and staff after the policy changes.
The film explores the disconnection people feel in modern urban life.
A disconnection between theory and practice can hinder real-world progress.

Synset Examples

he felt a gulf between himself and his former friends
there is a vast disconnect between public opinion and federal policy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disconnect - a lack of connection or agreement, especially between ideas or people
  • Antonym: connection - a relationship in which a person, thing, or idea is linked or associated with something else

Etymology

From 'disconnect' + '-ion', ultimately from Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'connectere' (to join together).

3. NOUN

/ ˌdɪskəˈnɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪskəˈnɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act of breaking or ending a connection between two or more things
act of breaking a connection

Examples

A sudden disconnection of the power supply left the entire city in darkness.
She felt an emotional disconnection from her coworkers after moving to a different department.
Internet disconnection can be frustrating during important online meetings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: connection - the state of being joined or linked together
  • Synonym: separation - the action of moving or being moved apart

Etymology

From Latin prefix dis- (apart) + English connection.
