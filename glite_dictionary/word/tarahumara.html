Tarahumara

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɑːrəˈhumərə / (AmE), / ˌtærəˈhʊmərə / (BrE)
a member of an indigenous people of north central Mexico, known for their long-distance running ability and traditional culture
member of an indigenous Mexican people

Examples

The Tarahumara are renowned for their endurance running in the highlands of Chihuahua.
Many Tarahumara still live in remote areas and maintain their traditional ways of life.
Researchers have studied the Tarahumara diet for clues to their remarkable health and stamina.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rarámuri - the self-designation of the Tarahumara people

Etymology

From Spanish 'Tarahumara', from the group's own name for themselves, 'Rarámuri.'
