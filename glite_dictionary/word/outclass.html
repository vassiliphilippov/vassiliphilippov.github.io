to outclass

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈklæs / (AmE), / aʊtˈklɑːs / (BrE)
to be much better than someone or something; to surpass or excel so clearly that others seem inferior in comparison
surpass greatly; be much better than

Examples

The new phone easily outclassed its competitors in speed and design.
She outclassed all her rivals to win the championship.
Their team outclassed every opponent they faced this season.

Synset Examples

The Yankees outclassed Cincinnati

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to surpass - to be greater or better than
  • Synonym: to excel - to do extremely well or be superior to others
  • Synonym: to outshine - to be much more impressive or outstanding than

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'out-' (surpass) + 'class' (group with similar rank or quality).
