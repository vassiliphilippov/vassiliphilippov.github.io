hemiparasite

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛmiˈpærəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˌhɛmiˈpærəsʌɪt / (BrE)
A parasitic plant that contains some chlorophyll and is capable of photosynthesis, but still obtains some nutrients from a host.
parasitic plant capable of photosynthesis
medical

Examples

The mistletoe is a well-known hemiparasite that extracts water and minerals from its host tree.
Unlike holoparasites, hemiparasites are able to perform some photosynthesis on their own.
Researchers are studying how hemiparasites affect the growth of surrounding vegetation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: holoparasite - a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and depends entirely on the host

Etymology

From hemi- (half) + parasite, indicating partial parasitism.
