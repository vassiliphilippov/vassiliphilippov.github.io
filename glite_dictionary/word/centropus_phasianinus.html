Centropus phasianinus

1. PROPN

/ sɛnˈtroʊpəs fejziˈænɪnəs / (AmE), / sɛnˈtrəʊpəs fɛziˈænɪnəs / (BrE)
A species of Australian bird in the cuckoo family, notable for its long, pheasant-like tail; commonly known as the pheasant coucal.
Australian bird with a long tail (pheasant coucal)

Examples

The Centropus phasianinus is often seen skulking through tall grasslands in northern Australia.
During the summer, the Centropus phasianinus can be heard calling at dawn and dusk.
Unlike most cuckoos, the Centropus phasianinus builds its own nest and raises its young.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pheasant coucal - Common name for Centropus phasianinus, Australian cuckoo with a long tail

Etymology

From genus Centropus (Greek kentron 'spur' + pous 'foot') and species phasianinus, from Latin phasianus 'pheasant-like'.
