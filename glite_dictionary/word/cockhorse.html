cockhorse

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑkˌhɔrs / (AmE), / ˈkɒkˌhɔːs / (BrE)
a toy horse, especially a rocking horse, or a person's knee used for a child to ride on during play
toy horse or adult's knee used in play
archaic

Examples

The little girl pretended to ride her cockhorse around the nursery.
Grandpa bounced Timmy on his knee like a cockhorse until the boy giggled with delight.
Many Victorian nurseries featured a wooden cockhorse for children to play with.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rocking horse - a child's toy horse mounted on rockers

Etymology

Probably from 'cock' (rooster; to strut) + 'horse,' originally meaning a high-spirited horse or a toy horse.
