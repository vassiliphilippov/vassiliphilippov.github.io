crozier

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊʒər / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊzɪə / (BrE)
a staff with a curved top carried by bishops and high-ranking church officials as a symbol of their office
bishop's ceremonial staff

Examples

The bishop entered the cathedral holding his crozier aloft.
During the ceremony, the ornate crozier was placed beside the altar.
The crozier is a symbol of pastoral authority in many Christian traditions.

Etymology

From Old French 'croisier', from 'crois' (cross), influenced by Latin 'crux' (cross).
