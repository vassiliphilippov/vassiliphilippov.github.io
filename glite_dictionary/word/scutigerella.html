Scutigerella

1. NOUN

/ skuː.tɪ.dʒəˈrɛl.ə / (AmE), / skjuː.tɪ.dʒəˈrel.ə / (BrE)
A genus of small, white, eyeless arthropods in the family Scutigerellidae, commonly known as garden centipedes, found in soil and decaying plant material.
genus of small soil-dwelling arthropods (garden centipedes)

Examples

Scutigerella species are often found in greenhouses and moist garden beds.
Gardeners may encounter Scutigerella while turning over compost or soil.
Although Scutigerella look like centipedes, they are not true centipedes but a distinct group called symphylans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garden centipede - common name for members of Scutigerella genus

Etymology

From Latin 'scutiger' (shield-bearer) with diminutive suffix '-ella'.

2. NOUN

/ skuːˌtɪdʒəˈrɛlə / (AmE), / skjuːˌtɪdʒəˈrelə / (BrE)
A genus of small, soil-dwelling arthropods commonly known as garden centipedes, found worldwide and known for living among decaying matter.
genus of garden centipedes

Examples

The soil in the greenhouse was crawling with Scutigerella that helped break down plant matter.
Several species within the genus Scutigerella are commonly found in moist garden soils.
Scientists identified the mysterious centipedes as members of Scutigerella.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Latin 'scutiger' (shield-bearing) and '-ella' (diminutive suffix).
