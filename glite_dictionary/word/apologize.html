to apologize

1. VERB

/ əˈpɑl.əˌdʒaɪz / (AmE), / əˈpɒl.ə.dʒaɪz / (BrE)
to express regret for something you have done wrong or for a fault or failing
express regret for a wrongdoing

Examples

I want to apologize for missing your call last night.
She apologized to her friend after their argument.
They are apologizing for any inconvenience caused by the delay.

Synset Examples

I apologized for being late
He apologized for the many typos

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excuse - to attempt to lessen the blame; to justify or explain
  • Antonym: blame - to assign responsibility for a fault or wrong

Etymology

From Greek 'apologizesthai' (to give an account, defend oneself), via Late Latin and Old French.
