anemometrical

1. ADJ

/ ˌænɪmoʊˈmɛtrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌanɪməˈmɛtrɪkəl / (BrE)
Relating to the measurement of wind speed and direction.
pertaining to wind measurement
medical

Examples

The meteorologist conducted an anemometrical analysis to assess wind behavior across the region.
There are specialized instruments for anemometrical studies in both aviation and environmental science.
Their research focuses on the anemometrical properties of coastal wind patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anemometric - relating to the measurement of wind speed and direction (alternative form)

Etymology

From 'anemometer' + '-ical'; 'anemometer' from Greek 'anemos' (wind) and 'metron' (measure).
