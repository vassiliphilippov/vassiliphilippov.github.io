shuffle

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃʌf.əl / (AmE), / ˈʃʌf.əl / (BrE)
the act of mixing playing cards so that their order is random
mixing playing cards

Examples

After each round, he performed a quick shuffle of the deck.
A good shuffle is essential to ensure a fair game.
She taught her younger brother how to do a proper shuffle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: riffle - a method of shuffling cards by interleaving them
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00341289-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃʌf.əl / (AmE), / ˈʃʌf.əl / (BrE)
A way of walking in which you drag your feet along the ground without lifting them fully.
slow, dragging walk

Examples

The old man walked down the hallway with a slow shuffle, barely lifting his feet.
After hours of standing, her tired legs could only manage a shuffle toward the door.
You could hear the distinct shuffle of slippers on the kitchen floor early in the morning.

Synset Examples

from his shambling I assumed he was very old

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shamble - To walk with an awkward, dragging gait
  • Antonym: stride - To walk with long, decisive steps
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00293556-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈʃʌfəl / (AmE), / ˈʃʌf(ə)l / (BrE)
to move things or people about from one place or position to another, often repeatedly or in a disorganized or secretive way
move or rearrange repeatedly

Examples

The company decided to shuffle its top executives to different departments for a fresh perspective.
He would often shuffle his books around his desk while studying, searching for better organization.
She keeps shuffling her schedule to make time for all her commitments.

Synset Examples

He shuffled his funds among different accounts in various countries so as to avoid the IRS

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rearrange - to change the position of something
  • Antonym: to organize - to arrange in order or structure

4. VERB

/ ˈʃʌfəl / (AmE), / ˈʃʌf(ə)l / (BrE)
to walk by dragging one's feet along the ground rather than lifting them fully
walk by dragging one's feet

Examples

The old man shuffled slowly across the street, his shoes scraping the pavement.
After a long day, she shuffled to the kitchen for a glass of water.
He shuffled out of his office, clearly too tired to lift his feet.

Synset Examples

he shuffled out of the room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trudge - walk slowly and with heavy steps
  • Synonym: stagger - walk or move unsteadily, as if about to fall
  • Antonym: stride - walk with long, decisive steps

Etymology

Probably frequentative of 'shove', influenced by Low German schuffeln (to drag the feet).

5. VERB

/ ˈʃʌf.əl / (AmE), / ˈʃʌf.əl / (BrE)
to mix a set of things, especially playing cards, so that their order is random
randomly mix, especially cards

Examples

Before each game, someone needs to shuffle the deck thoroughly.
She likes to shuffle her music playlist so that the songs play in a random order.
The contestants were asked to shuffle the flashcards before starting the quiz.

Synset Examples

shuffle the cards

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mix - to combine or put together various things

Etymology

Probably from early modern English 'shovel' (to move with the hands or a tool), influenced by Middle English 'schovelen'.
