gallus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæl.əs / (AmE), / ˈɡæl.əs / (BrE)
(Scottish) A pair of elastic straps worn over the shoulders to hold up trousers; braces or suspenders.
elastic straps for holding up trousers
british

Examples

He always wore his old-fashioned gallus to keep his trousers up.
The farmer adjusted his gallus before heading out to the fields.
My grandfather still prefers gallus instead of a belt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: braces - elastic straps worn over the shoulders to support trousers (UK)
  • Synonym: suspenders - elastic straps worn to hold up trousers (US)

Etymology

From Scottish English, possibly related to 'gallows', likening the straps to the shape of gallows.
