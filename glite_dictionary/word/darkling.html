darkling

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɑːrklɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɑːklɪŋ / (BrE)
(poetic) Occurring in the dark or at night; happening during darkness.
happening in the dark or night (poetic)
archaic

Examples

They set off on a darkling adventure, guided only by the moon.
The poet wrote of darkling woods where shadows danced.
A darkling silence fell over the quiet, sleeping city.

Synset Examples

a darkling journey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nocturnal - active at night or relating to night

Etymology

From Old English 'deorcling', used in poetry to mean 'in the dark'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɑrklɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɑːklɪŋ / (BrE)
uncannily or threateningly dark, shadowy, or obscure, often creating a sense of mystery or foreboding; used especially in literary language
uncannily dark, obscure, or shadowy (literary)
archaic

Examples

The moon shone over the darkling woods, casting eerie shadows across the path.
She gave him a darkling look that sent chills down his spine.
Legends speak of darkling valleys where few dare to wander at night.

Synset Examples

a darkling glance
secret operatives and darkling conspiracies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shadowy - full of shadows or not clearly seen or understood
  • Antonym: bright - full of light; shining strongly

Etymology

From Old English *deorc* (dark) with adverbial suffix *-ling*; originally used adverbially, later also as adjective in poetic use.
