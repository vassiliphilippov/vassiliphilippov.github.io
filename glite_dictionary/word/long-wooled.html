long-wooled

1. ADJ

/ lɔŋ ˈwʊld / (AmE), / lɒŋ ˈwʊld / (BrE)
(of sheep) having relatively long wool.
having relatively long wool (of sheep)

Examples

The farm breeds several long-wooled sheep for their high-quality fleeces.
A long-wooled variety tends to thrive in colder climates due to the extra insulation.
She purchased a long-wooled ram to improve the flock's wool yield.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: short-wooled - (of sheep) having relatively short wool
