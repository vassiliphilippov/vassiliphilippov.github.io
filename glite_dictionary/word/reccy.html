reccy

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛki / (AmE), / ˈrɛki / (BrE)
an informal term for a reconnaissance or exploratory trip, especially to investigate or survey a place before an event or task
informal term for reconnaissance
informal british

Examples

We're going on a reccy of the campsite before the rest of the group arrives.
The team did a quick reccy to find the best filming locations.
She suggested we do a reccy of the hiking trail before our main trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reconnaissance - the act of scouting or exploring an area, especially for military or strategic purposes

Etymology

Shortened form of 'reconnaissance'.
