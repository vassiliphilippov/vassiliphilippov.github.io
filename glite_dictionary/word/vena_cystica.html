vena cystica

1. NOUN

/ ˈviːnə sɪsˈtiːkə / (AmE), / ˈviːnə sɪsˈtiːkə / (BrE)
the vein that drains blood from the gallbladder into the portal vein system
vein that drains the gallbladder
medical

Examples

During the surgery, the vena cystica was carefully ligated to prevent bleeding.
Blockage of the vena cystica can result in gallbladder congestion.
The vena cystica is a key landmark for hepatobiliary surgeons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cystic artery - artery supplying blood to the gallbladder
  • Synonym: portal vein - major vein carrying blood from digestive organs to the liver

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein) + 'cystica' (of the bladder or sac).
