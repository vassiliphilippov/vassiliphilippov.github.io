to sough

1. VERB

/ saʊ / (AmE), / saʊ / (BrE)
(of wind, water, etc.) to make a soft, murmuring, or sighing sound.
make a murmuring or sighing sound

Examples

The wind would sough through the pines on quiet autumn nights.
We listened as the distant sea sighed and soughed along the shore.
All night long, the river soughed softly outside the cabin window.

Synset Examples

the water was purling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murmur - make a low, continuous sound
  • Synonym: rustle - make a soft, fluttering sound

Etymology

Old English *sūgan* (to suck, make a rushing noise), of Germanic origin.
