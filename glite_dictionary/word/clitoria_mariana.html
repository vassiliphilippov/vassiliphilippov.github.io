Clitoria mariana

1. NOUN

/ klɪˈtɔriə məˈriænə / (AmE), / klɪˈtɒriə məˈriːənə / (BrE)
A large-flowered wild twining vine of southeastern and central United States, bearing pale blue flowers.
wild vine with pale blue flowers (southeastern and central US)

Examples

The delicate blue flowers of the Clitoria mariana brighten many meadows in the southeastern United States.
Botanists studied the growth pattern of Clitoria mariana in a variety of soil types.
Gardeners often plant Clitoria mariana to attract pollinators and add color to natural landscapes.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Clitoria' named for resemblance to the clitoris, 'mariana' indicating the region or dedication.
