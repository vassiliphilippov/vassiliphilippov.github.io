gaucherie

1. NOUN

/ ɡoʊʃəˈri / (AmE), / ɡəʊʃəˈriː / (BrE)
a socially awkward or tactless act or manner; lack of social grace
a socially awkward act or manner

Examples

Despite his good intentions, his gaucherie at the dinner made everyone uncomfortable.
Her attempt at a compliment was marred by a slight gaucherie that embarrassed both of them.
The speech was full of charm, but a moment of gaucherie drew a few awkward laughs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awkwardness - lack of social grace or ease
  • Antonym: tact - sensitivity in dealing with others; skill in social situations

Etymology

From French gaucherie (awkwardness), from gauche (awkward, left).

awkward or tactless behavior

Examples

His gaucherie at the dinner party was obvious to everyone present.
She tried to hide her gaucherie, but her nervous fidgeting gave her away.
The play humorously exaggerated the gaucherie of its young protagonist.

Related Terms
