anklet

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋklət / (AmE), / ˈaŋklət / (BrE)
a type of shoe for women or children that has a strap around the ankle
woman's or child's shoe with ankle strap

Examples

The little girl wore shiny patent leather anklets to the party.
Her favorite pair of anklets matched her blue dress perfectly.
Many children's anklets are designed with support for growing feet.

Etymology

From 'ankle' + '-et' (diminutive suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæŋklət / (AmE), / ˈæŋklɪt / (BrE)
a piece of jewellery worn around the ankle
jewellery worn around the ankle

Examples

She wore a delicate silver anklet that jingled with every step.
The artisan designed a beaded anklet to match the necklace.
Many cultures have traditional meanings for the anklet as a piece of jewelry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bracelet - an ornamental band worn on the wrist

Etymology

From 'ankle' + diminutive suffix '-let'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈæŋklət / (AmE), / ˈæŋklɪt / (BrE)
A short sock that covers the foot and reaches just above the ankle.
short sock above the ankle

Examples

She wore a pair of colorful anklets with her sneakers.
My gym bag is full of mismatched anklets after laundry day.
Anklets are popular for sports because they're comfortable and breathable.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sock - a garment for the foot and lower part of the leg
  • Degree Higher: crew sock - a sock that covers the foot and the lower part of the calf

Etymology

From 'ankle' + diminutive suffix '-et', indicating a small item related to the ankle.
