deoxynucleotide

1. NOUN

/ diˌɑksiˈnuːkliəˌtaɪd / (AmE), / diˌɒksɪˈnjuːkliəˌtaɪd / (BrE)
A nucleotide in which the sugar component is deoxyribose instead of ribose, found especially in DNA.
nucleotide with deoxyribose sugar
medical

Examples

A deoxynucleotide is one of the building blocks of DNA.
Researchers added a fluorescent deoxynucleotide to track the replication process.
Each deoxynucleotide can pair with a complementary base during DNA synthesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nucleotide - A basic unit of nucleic acids, consisting of a sugar, phosphate, and base
  • Antonym: ribonucleotide - A nucleotide whose sugar is ribose, found especially in RNA

Etymology

From 'deoxy-' (indicating absence of an oxygen atom) and 'nucleotide'.
