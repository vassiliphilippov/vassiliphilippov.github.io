facsimile

1. VERB

/ fækˈsɪməli / (AmE), / fækˈsɪmɪli / (BrE)
to send (a document) by a facsimile (fax) machine; to transmit electronically as a fax
send by a facsimile (fax) machine

Examples

Please facsimile the signed agreement to our office before 5 p.m.
He facsimiled the blueprints to the construction team immediately.
They asked if we could facsimile all the necessary forms instead of mailing them.

Synset Examples

Can you fax me the report right away?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fax - to send a document by fax machine

Etymology

From Latin fac simile (make similar), originally used for exact copies; verb use developed from the noun (facsimile machine).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01009703-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ fækˈsɪməli / (AmE), / fækˈsɪmɪli / (BrE)
a machine or system for sending and receiving exact copies of printed or written documents, typically by wire or radio
machine for sending document copies electronically

Examples

The office still keeps an old facsimile machine in case of emergencies.
She sent the contract via facsimile to ensure a quick response.
Though rarely used now, facsimile transmission was once a standard business practice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fax - an exact copy or a machine for sending document copies electronically

Etymology

From Latin fac simile, meaning 'make similar.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03321050-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ fækˈsɪmɪli / (AmE), / fækˈsɪmɪli / (BrE)
an exact copy or reproduction, especially of a document, artwork, or printed material
exact copy or reproduction

Examples

The museum displayed a facsimile of the ancient manuscript instead of the original.
Please send a facsimile of the signed contract to our office.
Modern technology makes it easy to produce a facsimile of valuable documents for archival purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copy - something made to be identical to another
  • Antonym: original - the first or earliest form from which copies are made

Etymology

From Latin 'fac simile' meaning 'make similar'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03321219-n
Synset POS: n
