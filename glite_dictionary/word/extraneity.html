extraneity

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.strəˈniː.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɛk.strəˈniː.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The state or quality of being not essential or not forming a vital part; the condition of being irrelevant or external.
state of being nonessential or irrelevant

Examples

The committee debated the extraneity of certain proposed agenda items before removing them.
She was frustrated by the extraneity of irrelevant details in the report.
Many critics pointed out the extraneity of the subplot to the overall story.

Synset Examples

A listening to Ginsberg's own steady rendition of this poem on The Beat Generation audio recordings points out the extraneousness of the theatrical trappings, which go so far as to drown out the words of the poem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irrelevance - the quality of being not related or pertinent to something
  • Antonym: essentiality - the quality of being essential or vital

Etymology

From Latin extraneus (foreign, external) + -ity.
