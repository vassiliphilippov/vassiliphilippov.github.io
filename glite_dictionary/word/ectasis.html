ectasis

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛk.tə.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈɛk.tə.sɪs / (BrE)
the abnormal dilatation or distension of a hollow organ
abnormal dilatation of an organ
medical

Examples

The radiologist noted bronchial ectasis in the patient's lungs.
Renal ectasis was detected during the routine ultrasound examination.
Chronic infection can sometimes lead to ectasis of the affected organ.

Etymology

From Greek ektasis (extension, stretching out).
