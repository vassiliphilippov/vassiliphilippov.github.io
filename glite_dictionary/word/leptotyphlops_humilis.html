Leptotyphlops humilis

1. NOUN

/ lɛp.toʊ.tɪˈfloʊps ˈhjuː.mɪlɪs / (AmE), / lɛp.tə.tɪˈflɒps ˈhjuː.mɪlɪs / (BrE)
A small, burrowing species of threadsnake found in deserts, rocky hillsides, and beach sand in the western United States.
a small burrowing threadsnake species

Examples

The Leptotyphlops humilis spends most of its life underground, making it difficult to spot in the wild.
Because it is so small, the Leptotyphlops humilis can easily slip beneath rocks or into loose soil.
Naturalists in the southwestern United States sometimes search for Leptotyphlops humilis under desert shrubs after rain.

Etymology

Genus from Greek 'leptos' (slender) + 'typhlops' (blind); species from Latin 'humilis' (low, humble).
