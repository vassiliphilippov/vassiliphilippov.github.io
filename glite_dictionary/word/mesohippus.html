mesohippus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛs.oʊˈhɪp.əs / (AmE), / ˌmɛs.əʊˈhɪp.əs / (BrE)
an extinct genus of small, three-toed horses that lived in North America during the Oligocene epoch, not directly ancestral to modern horses
extinct three-toed Oligocene horse

Examples

Fossils of mesohippus reveal it had three toes on each foot rather than a single hoof.
The mesohippus roamed North America approximately 37 to 32 million years ago.
Researchers believe mesohippus was adapted to woodland habitats, unlike later grassland-dwelling horses.

Etymology

From Greek 'mesos' (middle) + 'hippos' (horse), meaning 'middle horse'.
