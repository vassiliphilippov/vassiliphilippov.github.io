Carmelite

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑrməˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɑːməˌlaɪt / (BrE)
of or relating to the Carmelite order of friars or to its members, practices, or institutions.
relating to the Carmelite friars or order

Examples

The ancient city is home to several Carmelite monasteries.
She admired the peaceful gardens of the Carmelite convent.
Historical records describe disputes between Carmelite friars and other religious orders.

Synset Examples

Carmelite monasteries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monastic - relating to monks or monasteries

Etymology

From Medieval Latin Carmelita (member of the order), from Carmel (Mount Carmel in Israel, where the order was founded).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrməˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɑːməlaɪt / (BrE)
a member of a Roman Catholic religious order founded on Mount Carmel, known especially for living a life of prayer and contemplation
member of the Carmelite religious order

Examples

The Carmelite devoted his life to prayer, simplicity, and service in the community.
She visited the old Carmelite monastery during her trip to Spain.
Many Carmelites work as teachers, preachers, and counselors around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: friar - a member of a religious order, especially in the Roman Catholic Church

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'Carmelita', from Mount Carmel in Israel, where the order originated.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10797816-n
Synset POS: n
