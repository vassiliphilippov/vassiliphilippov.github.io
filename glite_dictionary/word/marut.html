Marut

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːrut / (AmE), / ˈmɑːrʊt / (BrE)
In Hindu mythology, any of a group of storm gods who are the offspring of Rudra.
Hindu storm god; offspring of Rudra

Examples

The Rigveda describes the Maruts as powerful storm deities who brought rain and thunder.
Worship of the Maruts was prominent in ancient Indian rituals for rain and fertility.
In Hindu mythology, the Maruts are considered the sons of Rudra and attendants of Indra.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rudra - A Rigvedic deity associated with wind and storms, father of the Maruts in Hinduism

Etymology

From Sanskrit मَرुत (marut), meaning 'wind, storm god'.
