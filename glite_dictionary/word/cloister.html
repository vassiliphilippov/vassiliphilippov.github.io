cloister

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɔɪstɚ / (AmE), / ˈklɔɪstə / (BrE)
a place, often a monastery or convent, where members of a religious community live in seclusion from the outside world
religious residence in seclusion

Examples

After years in the cloister, he found city life overwhelming.
The ancient cloister provided a tranquil haven for the monks.
She sought guidance from the nuns living in the secluded cloister.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monastery - a building or complex where monks live and work
  • Synonym: convent - a residence for a community of nuns

Etymology

From Old French 'clostre', from Latin 'claustrum' (enclosure, barrier).

/ ˈklɔɪstɚ / (AmE), / ˈklɔɪstə / (BrE)
a covered walk, open gallery, or corridor surrounding a courtyard, especially in a religious institution such as a monastery or convent

Examples

Sunlight streamed through the arches of the ancient cloister, casting intricate shadows on the stone floor.
Many tourists visit the abbey to admire its peaceful cloister and beautiful gardens.
