streptococci

1. NOUN

/ ˌstrɛptəˈkɑkaɪ / (AmE), / ˌstrɛptəˈkɒkaɪ / (BrE)
Spherical Gram-positive bacteria that occur in pairs or chains and can cause illnesses such as scarlet fever and tonsillitis.
type of chain-forming bacteria
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the patient's sore throat was caused by streptococci.
Some species of streptococci can lead to serious complications if not treated promptly.
Tests confirmed the presence of streptococci in the infection site.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: staphylococci - cluster-forming spherical bacteria

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'streptos' (twisted) + 'kokkos' (berry, grain), referencing the bacteria's shape and chain formation.
