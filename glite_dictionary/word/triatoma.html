Triatoma

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈætəmə / (AmE), / trʌɪˈatəmə / (BrE)
A genus of blood-sucking insects commonly called kissing bugs or conenoses, some species of which are known to transmit Chagas disease.
genus of kissing bugs (insects)

Examples

Some species of Triatoma can transmit dangerous diseases to humans and animals.
Researchers studied the spread of Triatoma in rural South America to understand its role in Chagas disease.
The Triatoma bug is sometimes called the kissing bug because it often bites around the mouth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kissing bug - Blood-sucking insect that may transmit Chagas disease
  • Synonym: conenose - Common name for bugs of the genus Triatoma

Etymology

From Greek tri- (three) + atoma (uncuttable, indivisible), originally referring to segmented features.
