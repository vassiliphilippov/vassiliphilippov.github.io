lacrosse

1. NOUN

/ ləˈkrɑs / (AmE), / ləˈkrɒs / (BrE)
A team sport in which players use long-handled sticks with nets to catch, carry, and throw a small ball, aiming to score by shooting it into the opponents' goal.
team sport with sticks and netted ball

Examples

She started playing lacrosse in high school and quickly became a star attacker.
The university's lacrosse team won the national championship last year.
Many Native American tribes originally played a version of lacrosse for ceremonial purposes.

Etymology

From Canadian French 'la crosse' (the stick), referring to the stick used in the game.
