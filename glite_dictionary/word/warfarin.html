warfarin

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔr fə rɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɔː fə rɪn / (BrE)
a medication that acts as an anticoagulant, used to prevent and treat blood clots such as thrombosis or embolism
anticoagulant medication
medical

Examples

Patients taking warfarin require regular blood tests to monitor their clotting levels.
The doctor prescribed warfarin to reduce the risk of another stroke.
Certain foods can interfere with the effectiveness of warfarin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Coumadin - brand name for warfarin

Etymology

From the acronym WARF (Wisconsin Alumni Research Foundation) + -arin (as in coumarin, related compound).
