registration

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛdʒɪˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛdʒɪˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
an official document certifying that something or someone has been registered
document certifying registration

Examples

You must carry your registration with you when driving the car.
The school requires proof of registration before classes begin.
Her vehicle’s registration was renewed last week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certificate - official document attesting a fact

Etymology

From Latin 'registratio', from 'registrare' (to record).

The morning registration was quickly processed by the administration staff.

3. NOUN

/ ˌrɛdʒɪˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛdʒɪˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of adjusting something to match a standard or bring it into alignment, especially in technical contexts such as printing or engineering
adjustment to match a standard

Examples

Precise registration of the printing plates ensured perfect color alignment in the final print.
The technician worked on the registration of the telescope lenses for sharper images.
Correct registration of the instrument's sensors is essential for accurate measurements.

Etymology

From Latin 'registratio', from 'registrare' (to record), but this sense extends to technical adjustment contexts.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01001729-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˌrɛdʒəˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛdʒɪˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
(music) The combination of organ stops chosen and adjusted to produce a particular sound for a piece of music; also, the technique of selecting and arranging the stops.
musical combination of organ stops

Examples

The registration chosen by the organist brought a rich, full sound to the hymn.
Understanding registration is essential for performing Baroque organ music authentically.
She experimented with different registrations to achieve the desired tone color for each piece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stop - one of the controls on an organ that affects its sound

5. NOUN

/ ˌrɛdʒəˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛdʒɪˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of enrolling or signing up formally for something, such as a course, event, or organization
the act of enrolling

Examples

Registration for the summer program begins next week and spaces fill up quickly.
Students completed their registration online before the start of the semester.
The conference registration includes access to all workshops and keynote sessions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enrollment - the act of signing up or enrolling in a course or organization
  • Antonym: withdrawal - the act of removing oneself from enrollment or participation

Etymology

From Latin 'registratio', from 'registrare' (to record).
