diamante

1. NOUN

/ diˌɑːˈmɑːnteɪ / (AmE), / diːəˈmænteɪ / (BrE)
A small piece of shiny, often artificial material used as decoration for clothing, accessories, or other items.
shiny decorative piece for clothing

Examples

She wore a dress covered in sparkling diamante for the party.
The purse was decorated with tiny diamante accents that caught the light.
He added a diamante brooch to his jacket for a touch of glamour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhinestone - a small imitation diamond, used as jewelry or decoration

Etymology

From Italian 'diamante', meaning 'diamond'.

2. NOUN

/ diˌɑːˈmɑːnteɪ / (AmE), / diˈamɑːnteɪ / (BrE)
(of fabric or clothing) decorated with glittering ornaments such as sequins or rhinestones; also, a glittering ornament itself.
fabric or ornament with glittering decorations

Examples

Her evening gown was adorned with diamante along the neckline, catching the light beautifully.
The shoes sparkled thanks to the diamante embellishments on their straps.
He bought a purse covered in diamante for the special occasion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhinestone - an imitation diamond, used in cheap jewelry and to decorate clothes
  • Synonym: sequin - a small shiny disc sewn onto clothing for decoration

Etymology

From Italian 'diamante' (diamond), referring to sparkling decoration.

Synset POS: n
