Calvin Coolidge

1. PROPN

/ ˈkæl.vɪn ˈkuː.lɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkæl.vɪn ˈkuː.lɪdʒ / (BrE)
(1872–1933) the 30th President of the United States, serving from 1923 to 1929 after succeeding Warren G. Harding.
30th President of the United States (1923–1929)

Examples

Calvin Coolidge assumed the presidency after the sudden death of Warren G. Harding in 1923.
Many historians regard Calvin Coolidge as a symbol of the Roaring Twenties in America.
During his time in office, Calvin Coolidge promoted policies of limited government intervention.
