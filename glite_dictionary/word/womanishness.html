womanishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊmənɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈwʊmənɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being womanish; effeminacy, especially when regarded as inappropriate for a man
the state of being womanish or effeminate

Examples

He regarded any sign of womanishness as a weakness in men.
Throughout history, Spartans criticized Athenians for their supposed womanishness.
The novel explores how society reacts to womanishness in boys.

Synset Examples

the students associated science with masculinity and arts with effeminacy
Spartans accused Athenians of effeminateness
he was shocked by the softness of the atmosphere surrounding the young prince, arising from the superfluity of the femininity that guided him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effeminacy - the quality of being effeminate
  • Antonym: masculinity - possession of masculine qualities

Etymology

From 'womanish' (having qualities attributed to a woman) + '-ness' (state or quality).
