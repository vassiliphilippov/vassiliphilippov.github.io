houselights

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊsˌlaɪts / (AmE), / ˈhaʊs.laɪts / (BrE)
the lights that illuminate the audience area in a theater or auditorium, usually turned off during performances
lights illuminating theater audience area

Examples

The houselights dimmed, signaling the start of the play.
After the performance ended, the houselights gradually brightened.
Please remain seated until the houselights are fully on.

Etymology

From 'house' (auditorium or public area in theater) + 'lights'.
