to instruct

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈstrʌkt / (AmE), / ɪnˈstrʌkt / (BrE)
to teach someone a skill or subject, or give someone formal information on how to do something
to teach or give information

Examples

The coach will instruct the team on new strategies during practice.
She was hired to instruct beginners in digital photography.
Parents often instruct their children on how to behave in public.

Synset Examples

I taught them French
He instructed me in building a boat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to teach - to impart knowledge or skills to someone
  • Synonym: to educate - to give intellectual, moral, or social instruction to someone
  • Antonym: to learn - to gain or acquire knowledge or skill oneself

Etymology

From Latin 'instruere' meaning 'to build, teach, equip'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00830768-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈstrʌkt / (AmE), / ɪnˈstrʌkt / (BrE)
to inform someone about something; to make someone aware of specific information, often formally or officially
formally inform or make aware

Examples

All employees must be instructed of the new company policy before it takes effect.
The lawyer instructed his client about their rights ahead of the meeting.
Parents should always instruct their children of potential dangers when traveling.

Synset Examples

Have the students been apprised of the tuition hike?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inform - to give knowledge of a fact or situation
  • Synonym: to notify - to formally tell someone about something

Etymology

From Latin 'instruere' meaning 'to build, prepare, teach', from 'in-' (in) + 'struere' (to build).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00832735-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈstrʌkt / (AmE), / ɪnˈstrʌkt / (BrE)
to give someone directions or orders on how to do something
to give orders or directions

Examples

The coach instructed the team on how to improve their defense.
Parents often instruct their children to look both ways before crossing the street.
She instructed us to complete the assignment by Friday.

Synset Examples

She instructed the students to work on their pronunciation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: direct - to give an official order or instruction; to oversee an activity
  • Synonym: order - to command or tell someone to do something
  • Degree Lower: to advise - to give advice or suggestions

Etymology

From Latin 'instruere' meaning 'to build, prepare, teach'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00751969-v
Synset POS: v
