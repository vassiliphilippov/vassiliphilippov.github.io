advantage

1. NOUN

/ ədˈvæn.tɪdʒ / (AmE), / ədˈvɑːn.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
(in tennis) the first point scored after deuce, which gives the player or side a lead that, if followed by another point, wins the game.
first point after deuce in tennis

Examples

If the score is deuce and you win the next point, you have advantage.
He lost his advantage after double-faulting on the next serve.
At deuce, Maria gained the advantage with a strong forehand winner.

2. NOUN

/ ədˈvæn.tɪdʒ / (AmE), / ədˈvɑːn.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
a condition or circumstance that puts someone in a superior or more favorable position compared to others
superior or favorable position

Examples

Having language skills can give you an advantage in today's global job market.
Her quick thinking put her at an advantage during the negotiations.
The new technology offers a significant advantage over traditional methods.

Synset Examples

the experience gave him the advantage over me

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disadvantage - an unfavorable condition or circumstance
  • Synonym: benefit - an advantage or profit gained from something

Etymology

From Old French 'avantage' (profit, superiority), from 'avant' (before).

3. VERB

/ ədˈvæn.t̬ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ədˈvɑːn.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
to put in a more favorable position; to give an advantage to
put in a favorable position (verb)

Examples

Some policies may advantage certain groups over others.
The new schedule is designed to advantage early risers.
This tax system clearly advantages high-income earners.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: favor - to show preference or partiality towards
  • Antonym: disadvantage - to put in an unfavorable position

Etymology

From Old French avantage, from avant ('before', 'in advance'). The verb derives from the noun.

4. NOUN

/ ədˈvæn.tɪdʒ / (AmE), / ədˈvɑːn.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
a condition or circumstance that puts one in a favorable or superior position; a benefit resulting from something
a benefit or favorable condition

Examples

Winning the early rounds gave her a significant advantage in the tournament.
Many students saw online resources as an advantage for exam preparation.
Flexible working hours are a clear advantage of this job.

Synset Examples

it turned out to my advantage
reaping the rewards of generosity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benefit - an advantage or profit gained from something
  • Antonym: disadvantage - an unfavorable circumstance or condition; a limitation or drawback

Etymology

From Old French avantages (advantage), from avant (before, ahead), ultimately from Latin ab ante (from before).
