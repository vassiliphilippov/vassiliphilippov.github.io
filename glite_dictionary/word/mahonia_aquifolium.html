Mahonia aquifolium

1. NOUN

/ məˈhoʊniə ˌækwiˈfoʊliəm / (AmE), / məˈhəʊniə ˌakwɪˈfəʊlɪəm / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub native to the Pacific coast of North America, with dark green pinnate leaves, yellow flower clusters, and blue-black berries, often grown as an ornamental plant
evergreen shrub, also called Oregon grape

Examples

The garden boasts several Mahonia aquifolium shrubs, prized for their attractive foliage and vibrant yellow flowers.
In spring, the Mahonia aquifolium produces clusters of bright yellow blossoms that attract bees.
The blue-black berries of Mahonia aquifolium can be used to make jams and jellies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Oregon grape - common name of Mahonia aquifolium, an evergreen shrub with holly-like leaves

Etymology

From genus name Mahonia (from Bernard McMahon, American horticulturist), and Latin aquifolium (holly-leaved, from 'acus' needle + 'folium' leaf).
