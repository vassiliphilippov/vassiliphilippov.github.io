deportee

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːpɔrˈtiː / (AmE), / ˌdiːpɔːˈtiː / (BrE)
a person who has been expelled from a country by official order
person expelled from a country

Examples

The government sent the deportee back to his home country after his visa expired.
Each deportee was given a notice explaining the reasons for their removal.
The documentary told the stories of deportees separated from their families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deport - to expel someone from a country
  • Antonym: immigrant - a person who comes to live permanently in a foreign country
  • Antonym: emigrant - a person who leaves their own country to settle elsewhere

Etymology

From French 'déporté', past participle of 'déporter' (to deport).
