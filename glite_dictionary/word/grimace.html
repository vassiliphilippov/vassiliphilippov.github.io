grimace

1. VERB

/ ɡrɪˈmeɪs / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪməs / (BrE)
to twist your face in an ugly or contorted way, usually to express pain, disgust, or wry amusement
to twist the face to show pain or discomfort

Examples

She grimaced as she bit into the sour lemon.
He grimaced in pain when the doctor pressed on his ankle.
The child grimaced at the sight of the broccoli on her plate.

Synset Examples

He grimaced when he saw the amount of homework he had to do

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scowl - to frown in an angry or bad-tempered way

Etymology

From French *grimacer* (to make a face), from *grimace* (a facial expression).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00034281-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɪmɪs, ɡrɪˈmeɪs / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪməs, ɡrɪˈmeɪs / (BrE)
an expression on your face in which your features are twisted, usually showing pain, disgust, or wry amusement
twisted facial expression

Examples

She tried to hide her grimace when she tasted the sour lemon.
With a pained grimace, he lifted the heavy box.
The comedian pulled a funny grimace to make the children laugh.

Synset Examples

she made a grimace at the prospect

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scowl - an angry or bad-tempered expression
  • Antonym: smile - a pleased, kind, or amused facial expression

Etymology

From French 'grimace' (a distortion of the face).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06890628-n
Synset POS: n
