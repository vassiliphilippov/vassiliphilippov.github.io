inferior alveolar artery

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɪriər ælˌvioʊlər ˈɑrˌtɛri / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪəriə ælˌvɪələ ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
the artery that passes through the mandibular canal to supply blood to the lower teeth
artery supplying the lower teeth
medical

Examples

The inferior alveolar artery supplies blood to all the lower teeth.
Damage to the inferior alveolar artery can cause serious bleeding during dental surgery.
Your dentist avoids the inferior alveolar artery when administering a local anesthetic injection.

Etymology

From Latin *alveolaris* (pertaining to a hollow) and *arteria* (artery), 'inferior' meaning 'lower'.
