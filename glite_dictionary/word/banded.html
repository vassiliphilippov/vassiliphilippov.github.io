banded

1. ADJ

/ ˈbændɪd / (AmE), / ˈbændɪd / (BrE)
marked with bands or stripes of contrasting color or texture
having bands or stripes

Examples

The banded snake slithered across the forest floor, its colors standing out against the leaves.
Geologists examined the banded layers in the rock to understand its formation.
The banded pattern on the butterfly's wings helps it blend into its environment.

Synset Examples

a banded rock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: striped - marked with stripes of color or texture

Etymology

From the noun 'band' (a strip, stripe, or line), plus the suffix '-ed' meaning 'having'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbændɪd / (AmE), / ˈbændɪd / (BrE)
having a band or stripe, especially a white one, encircling the body or part of it.
having a colored or white band around the body

Examples

The banded cattle grazed peacefully in the field, their white belts distinct against dark coats.
We found a banded snake near the riverbank, easily recognizable by the ring of color around its body.
The new breed of banded chickens became popular for their unusual feather patterns.

Synset Examples

banded cattle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: striped - having stripes of a different color
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00224967-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈbændɪd / (AmE), / ˈbændɪd / (BrE)
marked with or having a band, especially a ring or strip placed around part of the body for identification
having a band for identification

Examples

The banded owls returned to the same nesting spot year after year.
Scientists released the banded falcon after collecting important data from its tag.
It is easier to track banded birds as each has a unique identification ring on its leg.

Synset Examples

kept watch for the return of their banded birds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ringed - having a ring or rings, especially for identification

Etymology

From the noun 'band' (a strip or ring) + -ed, forming the past participle and adjective.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00224561-a
Synset POS: a
