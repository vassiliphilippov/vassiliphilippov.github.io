sleight

1. NOUN

/ slaɪt / (AmE), / slaɪt / (BrE)
Skill or dexterity in using the hands, especially in a clever or deceptive manner.
skillful or dexterous use of the hands

Examples

The magician amazed the audience with his sleight during the card trick.
True sleight requires years of practice and perfect coordination.
His coin vanished thanks to the sleight of his nimble fingers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dexterity - skill in performing tasks, especially with the hands

Etymology

From Old Norse 'slœgð' (cunning, craftiness), related to Old Norse 'slœgr' (sly, crafty).
