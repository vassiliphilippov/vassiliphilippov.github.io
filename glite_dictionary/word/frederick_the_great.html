Frederick the Great

1. PROPN

/ ˈfrɛdərɪk ðə ɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˈfrɛdərɪk ðə ɡreɪt / (BrE)
the King of Prussia from 1740 to 1786, known for his military victories and for elevating Prussia to a major European power.
King of Prussia, 1740–1786

Examples

Frederick the Great was admired for his reforms in administration and religious tolerance.
Historians often credit Frederick the Great with making Prussia a leading European power.
The strategies used by Frederick the Great are still studied in military academies today.

Etymology

From German Friedrich der Große, his popular epithet distinguishing him as a major ruler.
