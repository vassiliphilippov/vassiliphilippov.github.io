demineralisation

1. NOUN

/ diˌmɪnəɹəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌmɪnərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of removing minerals and mineral salts from a liquid, especially from water
removal of minerals from liquid

Examples

Demineralisation of drinking water helps to prevent scale buildup in household appliances.
The plant uses a sophisticated demineralisation system to purify industrial water.
After demineralisation, the water is suitable for laboratory experiments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: remineralisation - the process of restoring minerals to a liquid or material

Etymology

From de- (removal) + mineralisation (process of adding minerals), after French déminéralisation.

2. NOUN

/ diːˌmɪnərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diːˌmɪnərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the abnormal loss of mineral salts, especially from bone or teeth
loss of mineral salts from tissues
medical british

Examples

Osteoporosis is often associated with demineralisation of the bones, increasing the risk of fractures.
The dentist explained that excessive sugar intake can accelerate demineralisation of tooth enamel.
After months of immobility, the patient showed significant demineralisation in his leg bones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demineralization - abnormal loss of mineral salts (especially from bone)

Etymology

From de- (removal) + mineral + -isation (from French -isation, based on Latin -izare)
