glibly

1. ADV

/ ˈɡlɪbli / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪbli / (BrE)
In a smooth, easy, and often insincere or superficial manner.
in a smooth but insincere way

Examples

He answered the reporter's tough question glibly, without really addressing the issue.
The politician glibly denied all accusations, charming some but convincing few.
She glibly explained away the problem, but her colleagues weren't fooled.

Synset Examples

he talked glibly

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: smoothly - in a confident and effortless manner
  • Antonym: sincerely - in an honest and genuine manner

Etymology

From 'glib', probably from Low German glibberig (slippery).
