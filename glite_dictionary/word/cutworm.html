cutworm

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌtˌwɝm / (AmE), / ˈkʌtˌwɜːm / (BrE)
A moth caterpillar that feeds on the stems of seedlings and young plants, often cutting them off at the ground level, especially common in North America.
moth larva that cuts off young plants

Examples

Farmers lost many seedlings this year because cutworms destroyed them overnight.
The gardener used barriers to protect her tomatoes from cutworms.
If you find your young plants cut off at the base, check for cutworms in the soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armyworm - larva of certain moths that moves in large groups destroying crops

Etymology

So named because the larva cuts down plants at ground level.
