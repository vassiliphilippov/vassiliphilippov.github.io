bladderwrack

1. NOUN

/ ˈblædərˌræk / (AmE), / ˈbladəˌrak / (BrE)
a common brown seaweed (Fucus vesiculosus) found on the coasts of the North Atlantic, used in preparing kelp and as manure
a type of brown seaweed

Examples

The shoreline was covered with bladderwrack washed up by the tide.
Herbalists sometimes use bladderwrack in traditional remedies for thyroid problems.
Farmers once valued bladderwrack as a natural fertilizer for their fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rockweed - another name for similar brown seaweeds found on coasts

Etymology

From 'bladder' (referring to the air-filled vesicles) and 'wrack' (seaweed).

2. NOUN

/ ˈblædərˌræk / (AmE), / ˈblædəˌræk / (BrE)
a brown seaweed (Fucus vesiculosus) found on coasts of the North Sea, western Baltic Sea, and Atlantic and Pacific Oceans, noted for its air-filled bladders and use in herbal remedies.
a type of brown seaweed

Examples

The herbalist recommended bladderwrack for its rich iodine content.
Children collected bladderwrack along the rocky shore during low tide.
Bladderwrack is commonly used in seaweed baths for its soothing properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rockweed - a type of brown seaweed commonly found on rocks

Etymology

From 'bladder' (referring to the air bladders) + 'wrack' (an old word for seaweed).
