seizing

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsiːzɪŋ / (BrE)
A small cord or line used in lashing together two or more ropes, especially in maritime contexts.
small cord for fastening ropes together (nautical)

Examples

The sailor used a seizing to secure the two ropes at the joint.
Before climbing, check that each seizing is tight and in good condition.
He replaced the worn seizing with fresh cord to ensure a strong hold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lash - to tie or bind together tightly
  • Synonym: splice - to join two ropes by interweaving their strands

Etymology

From the verb 'seize', meaning to fasten securely.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsiːzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsiːzɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of gripping or holding something firmly, especially with the hands or similar appendages
the act of gripping firmly

Examples

The sudden seizing of the steering wheel prevented the car from veering off the road.
Her seizing of his hand was so tight that it left marks.
Octopuses use the rapid seizing of prey with their tentacles to catch food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grasping - the act of taking and holding something firmly
  • Antonym: letting go - the act of releasing or ceasing to hold something

Etymology

From the verb 'seize', which comes from Old French 'seisir', from Medieval Latin 'sacīre'.
