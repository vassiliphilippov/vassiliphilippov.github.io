microbial

1. ADJ

/ maɪˈkroʊbiəl / (AmE), / mʌɪˈkrəʊbɪəl / (BrE)
relating to or caused by microorganisms, such as bacteria, viruses, or fungi
relating to microorganisms

Examples

Scientists study microbial activity in soil to understand nutrient cycles.
Proper hygiene can help prevent microbial infections in hospitals.
The food industry uses microbial cultures to produce cheese and yogurt.

Synset Examples

microbial warfare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: microbe - a microorganism, especially a bacterium causing disease

Etymology

From microbe + -ial, with microbe from French 'microbe', itself from Greek 'mikros' (small) + 'bios' (life).
