backstay

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌsteɪ / (AmE), / ˈbakˌsteɪ / (BrE)
a stay, cable, or rod used to support the back of a structure, especially in sailing to support a mast from behind
supporting cable or rod at the back of a structure

Examples

The crew adjusted the backstay to optimize the yacht's performance in strong winds.
A broken backstay can seriously endanger the stability of the mast.
Engineers reinforced the tower with a heavy backstay to prevent it from toppling in high winds.

Etymology

From 'back' + 'stay', referring to its position and function as a support.
