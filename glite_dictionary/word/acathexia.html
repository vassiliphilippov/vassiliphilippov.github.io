acathexia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæk.əˈθɛk.si.ə / (AmE), / ˌak.əˈθek.si.ə / (BrE)
An inability to retain bodily secretions, often referring to loss of control over urine or feces.
inability to retain bodily secretions
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with acathexia after he reported frequent incontinence.
Animals suffering from severe acathexia may require constant care.
Treatments for acathexia depend on the underlying cause of the loss of control.

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'kathexein' (to retain).
