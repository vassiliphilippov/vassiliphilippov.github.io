Perognathus flavescens

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrəɡˈneɪθəs fləˈvɛsəns / (AmE), / ˌpɛrəɡˈneɪθəs fləˈvɛsəns / (BrE)
A small species of pocket mouse native to open plains areas of the United States.
species of pocket mouse

Examples

The Perognathus flavescens is well adapted to the dry grasslands of the central United States.
Researchers set up live traps to monitor populations of Perognathus flavescens during the summer months.
The fur of Perognathus flavescens is pale yellow, helping it blend into its sandy habitat.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Perognathus' (from Greek 'peros' meaning 'maimed' and 'gnathos' meaning 'jaw') and species Latin 'flavescens' (yellowish).
