mastigophoran

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæs.tɪˈɡɑː.fəˌræn / (AmE), / ˌmæs.tɪˈɡɒ.fər.ən / (BrE)
a mostly nonphotosynthetic, free-living protozoan that moves using one or more whiplike flagella; some species are pathogens of humans and animals
flagellated protozoan
medical

Examples

The mastigophoran relies on its whiplike flagella for movement through water.
Some mastigophorans are known to cause diseases in humans and other animals.
Biologists studied a newly discovered mastigophoran under the microscope.

Etymology

From Greek 'mastix' (whip) + 'phoros' (bearing), referring to the whip-like flagella.
