panentheism

1. NOUN

/ pænˈɛnθiˌɪzəm / (AmE), / panˈɛnθɪˌɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief that God is both present in the universe and transcends it, being greater than and independent of the universe while also interacting with it
theological belief that God is both immanent and transcendent

Examples

Some theologians advocate panentheism as a way to reconcile God's transcendence and immanence.
Unlike pantheism, panentheism maintains that God is more than just the sum of the universe.
The concept of panentheism has influenced modern process theology and philosophical discussions about divinity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pantheism - the belief that God and the universe are identical

Etymology

From Greek pan (all) + en (in) + theos (god).
