electioneering

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛkʃəˈnɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkʃəˈnɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity of actively working to persuade people to vote for a particular candidate or party during an election campaign
campaigning to persuade voters

Examples

The law restricts electioneering within 100 meters of the polling station.
His electioneering tactics were criticized by the opposition for being too aggressive.
During electioneering, candidates often promise to address local concerns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campaigning - the organized effort to influence voters or promote a cause

Etymology

From 'election' + '-eering' (as in 'pioneering'), first used in the late 18th century.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛkʃəˈnɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkʃəˈnɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity of actively working to persuade people to vote for a particular candidate or party during an election
campaign activities aimed at winning an election

Examples

The party ramped up its electioneering efforts in the weeks leading up to the vote.
Laws restrict electioneering near polling stations to ensure a fair process.
Her experience in electioneering made her a valuable asset to the campaign team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campaigning - active participation in a planned set of activities to achieve a specific result, especially in politics

Etymology

From 'election' (from Latin 'electionem') + '-eering' (as in 'pioneering').
