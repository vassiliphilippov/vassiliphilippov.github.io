Ginkgoaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡɪŋkoʊˈeɪsiːˌiː / (AmE), / ˌɡɪŋkəʊˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of gymnosperm plants, including the living genus Ginkgo and several extinct relatives, in the order Ginkgoales
family of gymnosperm plants including Ginkgo

Examples

The Ginkgoaceae family is represented today by the single living species, Ginkgo biloba.
Fossils indicate that Ginkgoaceae were more diverse during the Jurassic period.
Botanists classify Ginkgo trees under the Ginkgoaceae family due to their unique reproductive structures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ginkgo - a genus of gymnosperm trees, including the only living species Ginkgo biloba

Etymology

From Modern Latin, based on Ginkgo (genus name) + -aceae (a standard suffix for plant family names).
