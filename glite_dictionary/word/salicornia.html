Salicornia

1. NOUN

/ sæ.ləˈkɔr.ni.ə / (AmE), / sæ.lɪˈkɔː.ni.ə / (BrE)
a genus of succulent, salt-tolerant plants commonly known as glassworts, typically found in coastal and salt marsh habitats
a genus of salt-tolerant plants (glassworts)

Examples

Salicornia can often be found growing along coastal wetlands where the soil is salty.
Chefs sometimes use young Salicornia shoots as a salty, crunchy ingredient in gourmet dishes.
Scientists study Salicornia for its potential to thrive in saline environments and as a source of biofuel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glasswort - common name for Salicornia and related plants

Etymology

From Latin 'salicornium', likely based on 'sal' (salt) and 'cornu' (horn), referring to the plant's shape and salty environment.
