lactogenic

1. ADJ

/ ˌlæk.təˈdʒɛn.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌlæk.təʊˈdʒɛn.ɪk / (BrE)
causing or promoting the production of milk in mammary glands, especially relating to substances or hormones.
inducing milk production
medical

Examples

Some medications are known to have lactogenic effects and can increase milk supply in nursing mothers.
Prolactin is a hormone with strong lactogenic properties, essential for breastfeeding.
The doctor prescribed a lactogenic supplement to support the patient's lactation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: galactagogue - a substance that promotes lactation

Etymology

From Latin 'lac', 'lactis' (milk) and Greek 'genes' (producing, born).
