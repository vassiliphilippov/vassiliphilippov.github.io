apoapsis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæp.oʊˈæp.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌæp.əʊˈap.sɪs / (BrE)
(astronomy) The point in an orbit that is farthest from the body being orbited.
point in orbit farthest from central body

Examples

The spacecraft reached its apoapsis before beginning its descent toward the planet.
At apoapsis, the satellite is at its greatest distance from Earth.
Engineers adjusted the orbit so the apoapsis occurred over the observation site.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: periapsis - the point in an orbit closest to the body being orbited

Etymology

From Greek 'apo-' (away from) + 'apsis' (arch, vault).
