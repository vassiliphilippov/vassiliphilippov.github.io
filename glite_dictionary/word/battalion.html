battalion

1. NOUN

/ bəˈtæliən / (AmE), / bəˈtæliən / (BrE)
a large military unit typically consisting of a headquarters and several companies, usually commanded by a lieutenant colonel
large military unit of several companies

Examples

The battalion was ordered to advance toward the enemy position at dawn.
He served as the commander of an infantry battalion during the war.
Several battalions participated in the military exercise last month.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: company - a military unit typically smaller than a battalion
  • Degree Higher: regiment - a military unit typically larger than a battalion

Etymology

From French 'bataillon' (battalion), from Italian 'battaglione' (battle squad), from 'battaglia' (battle).
