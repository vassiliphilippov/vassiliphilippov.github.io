to reanimate

1. VERB

/ riˈænəˌmeɪt / (AmE), / riˈænɪmeɪt / (BrE)
to give new life or energy to someone or something; to restore to life or vigor
to give new life or energy

Examples

The scientist aimed to reanimate the long-extinct species using advanced genetic technology.
After hours of discussion, her enthusiasm for the project began to reanimate.
The coach's speech managed to reanimate the team's spirit after their defeat.

Synset Examples

A hot soup will revive me
This will renovate my spirits
This treatment repaired my health

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revive - to bring back to life, consciousness, or activity
  • Synonym: to resuscitate - to revive from apparent death or unconsciousness

Etymology

From Latin 'reanimatus', past participle of 'reanimare' (to restore to life), from 're-' (again) + 'animare' (to give life).
