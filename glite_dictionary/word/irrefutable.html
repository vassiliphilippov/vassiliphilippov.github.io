irrefutable

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈrɛfjətəbəl / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛfjʊtəbəl / (BrE)
impossible to deny or disprove; indisputable.
impossible to deny or disprove

Examples

The scientist presented irrefutable evidence supporting her theory.
His innocence was established by irrefutable proof in court.
Their reasoning was so strong that it became irrefutable in the discussion.

Synset Examples

incontrovertible proof of the defendant's innocence
proof positive
an irrefutable argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indisputable - unable to be challenged or denied
  • Synonym: incontrovertible - not able to be denied or disputed
  • Antonym: refutable - able to be proved false or wrong

Etymology

From Latin 'irrefutabilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'refutare' (to refute).
