coalpit

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊlˌpɪt / (AmE), / ˈkəʊlˌpɪt / (BrE)
a mine or pit from which coal is extracted from the ground
mine for extracting coal

Examples

Many small villages grew up around the old coalpit that once drove the local economy.
In the 19th century, working in a coalpit was dangerous and often poorly paid.
The region is dotted with abandoned coalpits, now overgrown with grass and trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coal mine - an underground or surface excavation for extracting coal

Etymology

From 'coal' + 'pit', referring to early methods of surface or shallow mining.
