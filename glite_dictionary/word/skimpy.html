skimpy

1. ADJ

/ ˈskɪm.pi / (AmE), / ˈskɪm.pi / (BrE)
insufficient in amount or size; providing less than what is needed or expected
insufficient or meager in amount

Examples

The company's skimpy budget made expansion impossible this year.
She gave him only a skimpy portion of soup for dinner.
His explanation for being late was rather skimpy and unconvincing.

Synset Examples

a lean budget
a skimpy allowance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meager - lacking in quantity or quality; insufficient
  • Antonym: ample - enough or more than enough; plentiful

2. ADJ

/ ˈskɪmpi / (AmE), / ˈskɪmpi / (BrE)
(of clothing) very small and revealing, covering only a small part of the body.
revealing or barely covering clothing

Examples

She felt self-conscious in the skimpy swimsuit her friend had lent her.
Many dancers on stage wore skimpy costumes as part of the performance.
The summer collection includes several skimpy crop tops and short skirts.

Synset Examples

her dress was scanty and revealing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scanty - (of clothing) small and revealing

Etymology

From English 'skimp' (to supply scantily), plus the adjectival suffix '-y'.
