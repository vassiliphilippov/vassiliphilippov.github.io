Torygraph

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔːriˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈtɔːriɡrɑːf / (BrE)
an informal nickname for the British newspaper The Daily Telegraph, referring to its perceived conservative political stance
nickname for The Daily Telegraph (UK)
informal british

Examples

Some critics claim that the Torygraph rarely publishes articles critical of the Conservative Party.
He prefers reading the Torygraph in the mornings for its political commentary.
The columnist published her opinion pieces in the Torygraph for over a decade.

Synset Examples

The Torygraph often features conservative viewpoints.
Many politicians give interviews to the Torygraph regularly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: The Daily Telegraph - British daily national newspaper

Etymology

Blend of 'Tory' (a term for a member of the Conservative Party) and 'Telegraph'.
