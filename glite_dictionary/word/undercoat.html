undercoat

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndɚˌkoʊt / (AmE), / ˈʌndəkəʊt / (BrE)
the dense, soft layer of fur beneath the longer, coarser guard hairs of an animal's coat
fine inner layer of animal fur

Examples

Some breeds of dogs have a thick undercoat that protects them from cold weather.
Cats shed their undercoat in the spring as the weather becomes warmer.
The groomer carefully brushed out the loose undercoat from the husky.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: guard hair - the longer, coarser outer hairs in an animal's coat

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌndɚˌkoʊt / (AmE), / ˈʌndəkəʊt / (BrE)
a protective layer of tar or rubberlike material applied to the underside of a motor vehicle to prevent corrosion
protective coating under a vehicle

Examples

The mechanic applied a fresh undercoat to protect the car during the winter months.
It's important to check if your vehicle already has an undercoat before scheduling maintenance.
Regularly renewing the undercoat helps prevent rust and prolongs the vehicle's life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sealant - material used to block the passage of fluids
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04514984-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈʌndɚˌkoʊt / (AmE), / ˈʌndəkəʊt / (BrE)
the first or preliminary layer of paint or primer applied to a surface before the finishing coat
preliminary layer of paint or primer

Examples

The painter began by applying an undercoat to ensure the wall was properly sealed.
A good undercoat helps the final layer of paint adhere and look more vibrant.
If you skip the undercoat, the color might not cover evenly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primer - a preparatory coat applied before painting
  • Antonym: topcoat - the final layer of paint applied for finish and protection
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03365691-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈʌndərˌkoʊt / (AmE), / ˈʌndəkəʊt / (BrE)
to cover a surface with a primer as preparation for painting
apply a primer before painting

Examples

Before painting, you should always undercoat the walls to ensure a smooth finish.
The carpenter carefully undercoated the wooden panels before applying the final color.
She spent the morning undercoating the doors to prepare them for the top coat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prime - to apply a preparatory coating to a surface

Etymology

From 'under-' (beneath) + 'coat' (layer of covering).
