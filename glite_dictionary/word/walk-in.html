walk-in

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɑk ɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːk ɪn / (BrE)
(of a room or space) large enough for a person to enter and move around inside, especially used of closets, refrigerators, or similar enclosures.
large enough to enter (of a room or space)

Examples

She keeps all her clothes organized in a spacious walk-in closet.
The restaurant installed a new walk-in refrigerator to store fresh produce.
They converted a small room into a walk-in pantry for extra food storage.

Synset Examples

a deep walk-in refrigerator
walk-in closets
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00694774-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔk ɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːk ɪn / (BrE)
an easy or assured victory, especially in an election
an assured or easy victory

Examples

The candidate's popularity made the election a walk-in for her party.
Some expected the game to be a walk-in, but it turned out to be very close.
It was clear from the polls that the mayoral race would be a walk-in for the incumbent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: landslide - an overwhelming victory in an election
Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɔkˌɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːkˌɪn / (BrE)
a person, especially an intelligence operative, who voluntarily approaches another country or organization to offer information or seek political asylum without prior arrangement.
a voluntary defector or informant

Examples

The embassy staff were unsure how to handle the unexpected walk-in seeking asylum.
A walk-in provided crucial intelligence to the agency during the Cold War.
Not every walk-in turns out to be a genuine source of information.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defector - a person who abandons their country or cause for another

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'walk in', implying entering voluntarily without prior appointment.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈwɑk.ɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːk.ɪn / (BrE)
a small room or enclosed area that is large enough for a person to enter, especially for storage or changing purposes
small room large enough to enter

Examples

The apartment features a spacious walk-in for extra storage.
She hung her coats in the walk-in near the entryway.
Our new house has a walk-in that serves as both a closet and a dressing room.

Etymology

Formed from 'walk' + 'in', indicating the ability to walk into such a space.
