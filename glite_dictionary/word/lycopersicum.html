Lycopersicum

1. NOUN

/ laɪ.kəˈpɜːr.sɪ.kəm / (AmE), / lɪ.kə(ʊ)ˈpɜː.sɪ.kəm / (BrE)
A former genus name for the tomato and related plants, now classified as Solanum; used especially in botanical or historical contexts.
former botanical name for the tomato genus

Examples

The scientific name Lycopersicum was commonly used for tomatoes in older botanical texts.
Some heirloom seed catalogues still refer to tomatoes as Lycopersicum varieties.
The plant once classified as Lycopersicum esculentum is now known as Solanum lycopersicum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tomato - the edible red fruit commonly eaten as a vegetable

Etymology

From Latin *Lycopersicum*, from Greek *lykos* (wolf) + *persikon* (peach).
