visceral

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɪsəɹəl / (AmE), / ˈvɪsərəl / (BrE)
(of feelings or reactions) based on deep, instinctive emotion rather than on reason or logic.
based on instinctive or emotional response

Examples

She had a visceral reaction to the disturbing news, unable to explain her feelings logically.
His visceral dislike of the politician wasn't based on any particular policy.
The movie provoked a visceral fear in the audience, far beyond what words could express.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rational - based on logic or reasoning
  • Synonym: instinctive - based on instinct or innate feeling

Etymology

From Latin 'viscera' (internal organs), originally referring to emotions as arising from the body's inner organs.

2. ADJ

/ ˈvɪsərəl / (AmE), / ˈvɪsərəl / (BrE)
relating to the internal organs, especially those within the chest or abdomen, such as the heart, liver, and intestines.
relating to internal organs (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient exhibited signs of visceral pain after surgery.
Her condition required careful monitoring of visceral functions such as digestion and respiration.
Visceral fat accumulation is linked to higher risks of cardiovascular disease.

Synset Examples

visceral bleeding
a splanchnic nerve

Related Terms

  • Antonym: somatic - relating to the body, especially as distinct from the viscera

Etymology

From Latin visceralis, from viscera (internal organs).
