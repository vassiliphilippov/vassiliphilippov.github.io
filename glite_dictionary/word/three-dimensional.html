three-dimensional

1. ADJ

/ ˌθriː.dɪˈmɛn.ʃə.nəl / (AmE), / ˌθriː.dɪˈmɛn.ʃən.əl / (BrE)
having or appearing to have length, width, and depth; relating to or giving the impression of three dimensions
having length, width, and depth

Examples

The artist created three-dimensional sculptures that captivated everyone at the show.
This virtual reality system offers a three-dimensional experience that feels incredibly realistic.
Her explanation provided a three-dimensional view of the problem, considering multiple perspectives.

Synset Examples

lifelike three-dimensional characters
a three-dimensional account of conditions under the new government
they shot the movie in three-D

Related Terms

  • Antonym: 2-dimensional - having only two dimensions (length and width)

Etymology

From Latin 'dimensio' (a measuring), combined with 'three' (the number 3).

2. ADJ

/ ˌθriː.dɪˈmɛn.ʃən.əl / (AmE), / ˌθriː.dɪˈmɛn.ʃən.əl / (BrE)
having or appearing to have length, width, and depth; not flat
having length, width, and depth

Examples

The sculpture was truly three-dimensional, allowing viewers to admire it from every angle.
Children learn to recognize shapes by distinguishing between flat and three-dimensional objects such as spheres and cubes.
Movies in three-dimensional format can make you feel as if you're part of the action.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: two-dimensional - having or appearing to have only length and width; flat

Etymology

Formed from 'three' + 'dimensional'. 'Dimensional' is from Latin 'dimensio' (measured out).
