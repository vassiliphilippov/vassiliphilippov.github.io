sonant

1. ADJ

/ ˈsoʊnənt / (AmE), / ˈsɒnənt / (BrE)
(of a speech sound) produced with vibration of the vocal cords; voiced.
voiced speech sound

Examples

The letter 'z' is a sonant consonant because it is pronounced with vocal cord vibration.
Linguists use the term sonant to describe sounds like 'b', 'd', and 'g'.
Unlike the voiceless 's', the 'z' is a sonant because the vocal cords vibrate.

Synset Examples

voiced consonants such as ‘b’ and ‘g’ and ‘z’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voiced - (of a speech sound) produced with vibration of the vocal cords
  • Antonym: voiceless - (of a speech sound) produced without vibration of the vocal cords

Etymology

From Latin 'sonans', present participle of 'sonare' (to sound).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊ.nənt / (AmE), / ˈsɒn.ənt / (BrE)
a speech sound produced with vibration of the vocal cords; a voiced sound
a voiced speech sound

Examples

The letter 'm' is a sonant because it is pronounced with vocal cord vibration.
Linguists classify sounds such as 'l', 'm', and 'n' as sonants.
The distinction between a sonant and a voiceless sound can be heard in 'z' versus 's'.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voiced sound - a speech sound produced with vocal cord vibration
  • Antonym: voiceless sound - a speech sound produced without vocal cord vibration

Etymology

From Latin 'sonans', present participle of 'sonare' (to sound).
