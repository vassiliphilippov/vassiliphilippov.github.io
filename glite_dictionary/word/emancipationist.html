emancipationist

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌmæn.sɪˈpeɪ.ʃə.nɪst / (AmE), / ɪˌmæn.sɪˈpeɪ.ʃən.ɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or advocates the abolition of slavery.
advocate for abolition of slavery

Examples

The emancipationist movement gained momentum in the decades leading up to the Civil War.
As an outspoken emancipationist, she dedicated her life to ending slavery in all its forms.
Many emancipationists faced fierce opposition from those who benefited from the slave trade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abolitionist - a person who advocates the abolition of slavery

Etymology

From emancipation (Latin emancipatio, the act of freeing) + -ist (denoting an advocate or supporter).
