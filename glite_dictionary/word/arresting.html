arresting

1. ADJ

/ əˈrɛstɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈrɛstɪŋ / (BrE)
Attracting and holding attention; striking or impressive.
striking; commanding attention

Examples

The exhibition featured an arresting photograph that drew all eyes to the center of the room.
Her arresting voice captivated the entire audience from the very first note.
That novel begins with an arresting opening chapter that immediately hooks the reader.

Synset Examples

an arresting drawing of people turning into animals
a sensational concert — one never to be forgotten
a stunning performance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: striking - impressively noticeable or remarkable
  • Antonym: unremarkable - not attracting attention; ordinary or ordinary

Etymology

From the verb 'arrest', from Old French 'arester', ultimately from Latin 'arrestare' (to stop, check).
