Proxima

1. PROPN

/ ˈprɑksɪmə / (AmE), / ˈprɒksɪmə / (BrE)
the closest known star to the Sun, located about 4.3 light years away in the constellation Centaurus; also called Proxima Centauri
nearest star to the Sun

Examples

Scientists believe Proxima could have planets within its habitable zone.
Proxima is visible through powerful telescopes in the southern hemisphere.
Astronomers announced the discovery of an exoplanet orbiting Proxima in 2016.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Alpha Centauri - triple star system that includes Proxima Centauri

Etymology

From Latin 'proxima' meaning 'nearest (female),' referring to its proximity to the Sun.
