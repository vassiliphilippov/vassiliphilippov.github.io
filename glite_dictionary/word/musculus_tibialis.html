musculus tibialis

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌs.kjəl.əs tɪbɪˈælɪs / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kjʊ.ləs tɪbɪˈælɪs / (BrE)
either of two muscles in the lower leg arising from the tibia, responsible for movement of the foot
muscle in lower leg aiding foot movement
medical

Examples

The musculus tibialis plays a critical role in lifting the foot during walking.
Injuries to the musculus tibialis can cause pain and limit mobility.
The surgeon carefully examined the musculus tibialis before proceeding with the operation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tibialis anterior - the muscle in front of the tibia that flexes the foot upward
  • Synonym: tibialis posterior - the muscle behind the tibia that helps invert the foot

Etymology

From Latin 'musculus' (muscle) and 'tibialis' (of the tibia, shinbone).
