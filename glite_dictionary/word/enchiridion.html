enchiridion

1. NOUN

/ ɛnkaɪˈrɪdiən / (AmE), / ɛnkaɪˈrɪdɪən / (BrE)
A concise handbook or manual containing essential information about a subject or location.
concise handbook or manual

Examples

The philosopher's enchiridion offered practical guidance for everyday living.
She studied the enchiridion to prepare for her new role at the museum.
A useful enchiridion is essential for travelers who want quick information at their fingertips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manual - book giving instructions or information
  • Synonym: handbook - concise reference book

Etymology

From Greek encheiridion (handbook), from en- (in) + cheir (hand).
