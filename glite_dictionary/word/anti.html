anti

1. ADJ

/ ˈæn.taɪ / (AmE), / ˈæn.ti / (BrE)
not in favor of or opposed to a particular policy, action, or idea, often used as a prefix to form compound words.
opposed to; not in favor of

Examples

Many people are anti smoking because of its health risks.
She spoke out at the meeting, clearly anti the proposed changes.
The group organized an anti war rally in the city center.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pro - in favor of; supporting
  • Synonym: against - in opposition to

Etymology

From Greek anti (against, opposite).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæn.taɪ / (AmE), / ˈæn.ti / (BrE)
a person who is opposed to a particular action, policy, or practice
a person opposing something

Examples

The antis organized a peaceful protest against the new law.
She was known as a vocal anti in the environmental debate.
Many antis spoke out at the town hall meeting.

Synset Examples

the antis smelled victory after a long battle

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pro - a person who is in favor of something

Etymology

From prefix 'anti-' (against), used as a noun since the 20th century to refer to opponents of specific causes.
