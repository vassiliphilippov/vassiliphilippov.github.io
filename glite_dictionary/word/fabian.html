Fabian

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪ.bi.ən / (AmE), / ˈfeɪ.bi.ən / (BrE)
a member of the Fabian Society, a British socialist organization advocating gradual and reformist rather than revolutionary means to achieve socialism.
member of the Fabian Society
british

Examples

As a prominent Fabian, she argued for gradual social reforms in Parliament.
More than a century after its founding, the Fabian tradition remains influential in British politics.
The book details the life and works of several key Fabians in the early twentieth century.

Etymology

From the Fabian Society, named after the Roman general Quintus Fabius Maximus, famed for his cautious tactics.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪ.bi.ən / (AmE), / ˈfeɪ.bi.ən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of Fabianism or the Fabian Society, a British socialist movement advocating gradual and reformist rather than revolutionary means to social change.
relating to Fabianism or the Fabian Society

Examples

The Fabian approach to social reform favored gradual change through legislative measures.
The organization was founded by a group of Fabian intellectuals in London.
Her ideas on economic policy are distinctly Fabian in their emphasis on progress through incremental steps.

Synset Examples

the Fabian society

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reformist - favoring gradual reform over revolution
  • Antonym: revolutionary - favoring rapid or radical political or social change

Etymology

From the Fabian Society, named after the Roman general Quintus Fabius Maximus, known for his cautious strategy.

3. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪ.bi.ən / (AmE), / ˈfeɪ.bɪ.ən / (BrE)
using a cautious and gradual approach to achieve a goal, especially by avoiding direct confrontation and wearing down opposition over time
gradual, cautious strategy; avoiding direct conflict

Examples

The party adopted a Fabian strategy, gradually implementing reforms over several years.
He was criticized for his Fabian reluctance to engage in direct debate.
Their Fabian tactics frustrated opponents who expected swift confrontation.

Synset Examples

a fabian policy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delaying - causing a postponement or retardation of action
  • Antonym: aggressive - pursuing goals forcefully and directly

Etymology

From the name of Roman general Quintus Fabius Maximus, known for delaying tactics against Hannibal.
