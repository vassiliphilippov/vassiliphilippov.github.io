arboreous

1. ADJ

/ ɑːrˈbɔriəs / (AmE), / ɑːˈbɔːriəs / (BrE)
(of animals or plants) living in or frequenting trees; relating to or resembling trees.
living in or relating to trees

Examples

Many birds exhibit arboreous habits, nesting high among the branches.
The region is home to several arboreous mammals, such as certain species of monkeys.
Sloths are classic examples of arboreous animals, spending most of their lives in trees.

Synset Examples

arboreal apes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arboreal - inhabiting or frequenting trees; living in trees

Etymology

From Latin 'arboreus' (of a tree), from 'arbor' (tree).

2. ADJ

/ ɑrˈbɔriəs / (AmE), / ɑːˈbɔːrɪəs / (BrE)
(of a place) full of or covered with trees; abounding in trees.
full of or covered with trees

Examples

The parkland is beautifully arboreous, making it a pleasant area for walks in the summer.
An arboreous region supports a rich diversity of animal and plant life.
Travelers marveled at the arboreous hills stretching along the horizon.

Synset Examples

an arboreous landscape
violets in woodsy shady spots
a woody area near the highway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wooded - covered with trees
  • Antonym: treeless - lacking trees

Etymology

From Latin 'arboreus', from 'arbor' meaning 'tree'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02583630-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ɑrˈbɔriəs / (AmE), / ɑːˈbɔːrɪəs / (BrE)
having the form or branching structure of a tree; tree-like.
tree-like in form or structure

Examples

The arboreous shape of the coral made it a unique sight for divers.
Many ancient ferns adopted an arboreous growth pattern to reach more sunlight.
Some sculptures in the garden imitate arboreous forms, blending seamlessly with the surrounding trees.

Synset Examples

arborescent coral found off the coast of Bermuda
dendriform sponges

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arborescent - resembling a tree; tree-like in form or branching
  • Synonym: dendroid - having the shape or form of a tree

Etymology

From Latin arboreus, from arbor (tree).
