Finno-Ugrian

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɪnoʊˈuːɡriən / (AmE), / ˌfɪnəʊˈjuːɡrɪən / (BrE)
A branch of the Uralic language family, comprising languages spoken in Scandinavia, Hungary, Russia, and western Siberia before the Slavic expansion.
branch of the Uralic language family

Examples

Finnish is a well-known Finno-Ugrian language spoken by millions in Finland.
The study of Finno-Ugrian languages reveals fascinating connections between Estonian and Hungarian.
She majored in Finno-Ugrian linguistics at university, focusing on minority languages in Russia.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Uralic - the language family that includes Finno-Ugrian languages

Etymology

From 'Finno-' (referring to the Finns) + 'Ugrian' (referring to the Ugrians, peoples related to the Magyars and Mansi).
