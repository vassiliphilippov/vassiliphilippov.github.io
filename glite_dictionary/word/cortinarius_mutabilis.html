Cortinarius mutabilis

1. NOUN

/ kɔrˈtɪnəriəs mjuːˈteɪbɪlɪs / (AmE), / kɔːˈtɪnəriəs mjuːˈteɪbɪlɪs / (BrE)
A species of fungus with a reddish-purple, smooth, slimy cap, close violet gills, and all parts that stain dark purple when bruised.
a species of purple-staining fungus

Examples

Cortinarius mutabilis is notable for its cap, which turns a darker shade when handled.
Mycology students often study Cortinarius mutabilis to learn about staining reactions in fungi.
The woods near the lake were home to several clusters of Cortinarius mutabilis.

Etymology

From Latin: 'cortinarius' (bearing a veil) and 'mutabilis' (changeable).
