throttling

1. NOUN

/ ˈθrɑt̬.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθrɒt.lɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of strangling or suffocating someone by compressing their throat and restricting their airway
the act of strangling or suffocating

Examples

The forensic report indicated throttling as the cause of death.
There were visible marks on her neck consistent with throttling.
The detective explained that throttling requires significant force and intent.

Synset Examples

no evidence that the choking was done by the accused

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strangulation - the act of squeezing someone's throat to prevent breathing
  • Synonym: choking - blocking someone's airway, causing them to stop breathing

Etymology

From the verb 'throttle', likely from Middle English 'throttil' (throat).
