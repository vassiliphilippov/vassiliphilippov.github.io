Thelypteris

1. NOUN

/ θəˈlɪp.tər.ɪs / (AmE), / θəˈlɪp.tər.ɪs / (BrE)
A genus of mostly tropical and temperate ferns, commonly known as marsh ferns, sometimes classified within the genus Dryopteris.
a genus of marsh ferns

Examples

Many wetlands are home to the Thelypteris species, which thrive in moist environments.
Botanists recently identified a new Thelypteris growing along the riverbank.
The fronds of Thelypteris are distinctive and often overlooked by casual hikers.

Etymology

From Greek thelys (female) + pteris (fern).

2. NOUN

/ θɛlˈɪptərɪs / (AmE), / θɛlˈɪptərɪs / (BrE)
a genus of ferns commonly known as marsh ferns, sometimes classified under the genus Dryopteris in certain botanical systems
a genus of marsh ferns

Examples

The Thelypteris genus includes several species commonly found in wetland habitats.
Some taxonomists classify Thelypteris species within the genus Dryopteris based on certain morphological traits.
Botanists studied the reproductive structures of Thelypteris to understand its evolutionary relationships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dryopteris - a large genus of ferns known as wood ferns or shield ferns

Etymology

From Greek 'thelys' (female) and 'pteris' (fern), referring to certain characteristics of the fern.
