dimer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪmər / (AmE), / ˈdʌɪmə / (BrE)
a chemical compound whose molecules consist of two identical monomer units bonded together
compound of two identical monomers

Examples

The protein forms a dimer through the interaction of its two subunits.
In solution, acetic acid often exists as a dimer rather than as single molecules.
Researchers observed that the molecule spontaneously organizes into a dimer under certain conditions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monomer - a molecule that can join others to form a polymer
  • Degree Higher: polymer - a compound whose molecules are made of many repeated units

Etymology

From Greek 'dis' (twice) + 'meros' (part), meaning 'two parts'.
