oscitant

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑːsɪtənt / (AmE), / ˈɒsɪtənt / (BrE)
showing inattentiveness or boredom; yawning or drowsy.
showing inattentiveness or boredom
archaic

Examples

The professor was disheartened by the oscitant faces in the lecture hall.
During the lengthy ceremony, the audience appeared increasingly oscitant.
His oscitant manner suggested he found the discussion quite dull.

Synset Examples

the yawning congregation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inattentive - not paying attention; distracted
  • Antonym: alert - quick to notice and respond; attentive

Etymology

From Latin oscillāns, oscillant-, present participle of 'oscitare' (to yawn).
