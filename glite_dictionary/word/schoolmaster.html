schoolmaster

1. NOUN

/ ˈskuːlˌmæstər / (AmE), / ˈskuːlˌmɑːstə / (BrE)
a male teacher, especially the head teacher or principal of a school, often used historically
male head teacher of a school (historical)

Examples

The old schoolmaster guided students with a firm but gentle hand.
During the Victorian era, the schoolmaster was both respected and feared by his pupils.
The new schoolmaster introduced several innovative teaching methods at the village school.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: schoolmistress - a female teacher or head of a school
  • Synonym: principal - the head or leader of a school

Etymology

From Middle English 'scolemaister', from Latin 'scholasticus' (scholar, teacher) and Old English 'maegester' (master).

2. NOUN

/ ˈskuːlˌmæstər / (AmE), / ˈskuːlˌmɑːstə / (BrE)
a male teacher in a school, especially in a primary or secondary school; sometimes used for the head of a school
male schoolteacher; sometimes headmaster

Examples

The new schoolmaster introduced strict rules to the classroom.
As a young schoolmaster, he inspired many students with his passion for literature.
In nineteenth-century England, the schoolmaster often lived in a cottage beside the school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teacher - a person who teaches, especially in a school
  • Antonym: schoolmistress - female teacher in a school

Etymology

From school + master; 'master' from Old English 'mægister', via Latin 'magister' meaning 'teacher, chief'.
