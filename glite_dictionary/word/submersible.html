submersible

1. NOUN

/ səbˈmɜrsəbəl / (AmE), / sʌbˈmɜːsəbəl / (BrE)
A vessel, especially a warship, designed to operate underwater.
underwater vessel, often military

Examples

The navy deployed a new submersible capable of remaining underwater for weeks.
Advances in technology have made the modern submersible quieter and more difficult to detect.
During the mission, the submersible navigated dangerous waters with skilled precision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submarine - A warship capable of underwater operation

Etymology

From Latin 'sub-' (under) + 'mergere' (to plunge).

Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ səbˈmɝːsəbəl / (AmE), / səbˈmɜːsɪbəl / (BrE)
capable of being submerged in water, especially without being damaged or rendered inoperative
able to be submerged in water

Examples

Most submersible watches are designed to function at significant depths underwater.
The team installed a submersible pump to prevent the basement from flooding.
To clean the aquarium, use a submersible heater for consistent water temperature.

Synset Examples

a submersible pump
a submergible electric frying pan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waterproof - not allowing water to enter or pass through
  • Synonym: submergible - able to be submerged, variant form

Etymology

From Latin 'submergere' (to sink, submerge) with the English suffix '-ible'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02482168-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ səbˈmɝːsɪbəl / (AmE), / səbˈmɜːsɪbəl / (BrE)
a small vehicle or device designed to operate under water, especially for research or exploration
underwater vehicle or device

Examples

The research team used a submersible to explore the ocean depths.
A tiny submersible was deployed to examine the shipwreck on the sea floor.
Modern oil platforms often rely on remotely operated submersibles for underwater maintenance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submarine - a warship designed to operate underwater

Etymology

From Latin 'submersus' (submerged) + English '-ible', meaning 'able to be submerged'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04355437-n
Synset POS: n
