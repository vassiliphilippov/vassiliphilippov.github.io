mica

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.kə / (BrE)
a group of minerals, typically shiny and easily split into thin, flexible sheets, used especially as electrical insulators and in decorative applications
a group of shiny, sheet-forming minerals

Examples

The electrician used mica as an insulating material between the metal parts.
You can find mica flakes sparkling in many types of rocks and soils.
Sheets of mica were once common in old stove windows because they withstand high heat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biotite - a common type of mica, dark in color
  • Synonym: muscovite - a light-colored variety of mica

Etymology

From Latin *micare* (to glitter).
