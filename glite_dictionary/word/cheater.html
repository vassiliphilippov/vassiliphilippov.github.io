cheater

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃiːtər / (AmE), / ˈtʃiːtə / (BrE)
a person who behaves dishonestly, especially by breaking rules or lying to gain an advantage or mislead others
person who behaves dishonestly; deceiver

Examples

He was called a cheater after he lied about the results of the game.
Nobody likes to play cards with a cheater because it's impossible to trust them.
The company fired the cheater who manipulated the financial reports.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceiver - a person who causes someone to believe something that is not true
  • Antonym: honest person - someone who tells the truth or behaves honorably

Etymology

From 'cheat' plus agent suffix '-er'; 'cheat' from Old French *escheat*, to fall to a lord by forfeiture.
