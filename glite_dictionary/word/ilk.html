ilk

1. NOUN

/ ɪlk / (AmE), / ɪlk / (BrE)
a type or kind of person or thing, especially one regarded with disapproval
type or kind of person or thing

Examples

He refused to associate with politicians of that ilk.
Writers of his ilk rarely receive the recognition they deserve.
The new policy was criticized by environmentalists and others of their ilk.

Synset Examples

We'll not see his like again
I can't tolerate people of his ilk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kind - type or category of people or things with similar characteristics
  • Synonym: sort - a category of people or things sharing a common feature
  • Synonym: variety - the quality or state of being different in kind

Etymology

From Middle English *ilk* (same), from Old English *ilca* (same), used in 'of that ilk' before developing its modern sense.
