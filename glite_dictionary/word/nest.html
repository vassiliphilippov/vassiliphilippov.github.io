nest

1. NOUN

/ nɛst / (AmE), / nɛst / (BrE)
a set of furniture pieces, such as tables or boxes, made to fit one inside another for storage or space-saving purposes
set of furniture items fitting inside each other

Examples

She bought a nest of tables for her living room to save space.
The antique shop displayed a beautiful nest of drawers from the 19th century.
A nest of boxes sat neatly in the corner, each fitting perfectly inside the next.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stack - a set of similar objects placed one on top of another
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

/ nɛst / (AmE), / nɛst / (BrE)
(of a bird or other animal) to build and occupy a nest, usually as a place to lay eggs or raise young.
to build and occupy a nest

Examples

Several pairs of sparrows nest under the eaves each year.
Sea turtles return to the same beach to nest every season.
The swallows have started to nest in our garage again.

3. NOUN

/ nɛst / (AmE), / nɛst / (BrE)
a cosy or secluded place or retreat, often for rest or privacy
a cosy or secluded retreat

Examples

She created a nest of cushions by the fire and read her book in peace.
The old cabin served as a secret nest for the two friends to escape the city.
Every evening, he retreated to his little nest in the attic to write.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanctuary - a place of refuge or safety
  • Synonym: refuge - a place providing shelter or protection

Etymology

From Old English 'nest' (a place birds lay eggs), related to German 'Nest' and Latin 'nidus'.
