gestation

1. NOUN

/ dʒɛˈsteɪʃən / (AmE), / dʒɛˈsteɪʃən / (BrE)
the period of development inside the womb from conception until birth
period of development in the womb

Examples

The elephant's gestation period is nearly two years, one of the longest in the animal kingdom.
During gestation, proper nutrition is essential for both mother and baby.
The doctor explained that human gestation typically lasts about 40 weeks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pregnancy - the state of carrying a developing fetus within the body

Etymology

From Latin gestatio (carrying), from gestare (to carry).

2. NOUN

/ dʒɛˈsteɪʃən / (AmE), / dʒɛˈsteɪʃən / (BrE)
the period during which an embryo or fetus develops inside the womb, from conception to birth
the period of pregnancy; fetal development
medical

Examples

The doctor monitored the mother's health throughout the gestation of the baby.
Elephants have a much longer gestation period compared to most other mammals.
A healthy diet is essential during gestation to support the development of the fetus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pregnancy - the condition of carrying a developing fetus within the womb

Etymology

From Latin gestatio (a carrying), from gerere (to carry).

3. NOUN

/ dʒɛˈsteɪʃən / (AmE), / dʒɛˈsteɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of developing an idea or plan in the mind before it becomes fully formed
development of an idea or plan

Examples

The gestation of the project took several months before it was ready to launch.
His novel went through a long period of gestation before he finally started writing.
The gestation of that business idea began during her college years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incubation - the process of developing something, especially an idea, before it is ready

Etymology

From Latin gestatio (a carrying), from gestare (to carry).
