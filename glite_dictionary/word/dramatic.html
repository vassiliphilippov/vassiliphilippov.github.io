dramatic

1. ADJ

/ drəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / drəˈmatɪk / (BrE)
suitable for or characteristic of drama; intended to attract attention or create excitement, as in a play or performance.
related to drama or theatrical style

Examples

Her dramatic pause before revealing the secret kept everyone on the edge of their seats.
The actor made a dramatic gesture to emphasize his point during the scene.
The rescue operation was a dramatic event that drew national attention.

Synset Examples

a dramatic entrance in a swirling cape
a dramatic rescue at sea

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theatrical - relating to drama or the theatre; showy or exaggerated

Etymology

From Medieval Latin dramaticus, from Greek dramatikos, from drama (action, play).

4. ADJ

/ drəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / drəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
(of a singer or singing voice) marked by great power, expressiveness, and a highly theatrical or emotional style, especially in opera.
powerful and expressive operatic singing voice

Examples

The role calls for a dramatic soprano with the stamina to project over the orchestra.
She auditioned with her dramatic tenor, impressing the judges with both power and nuance.
Casting a singer with a dramatic voice can transform the emotional impact of an opera.

Synset Examples

a dramatic tenor
a dramatic soprano

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lyric - of a singing voice that is light, flexible, and expressive

Etymology

From Greek *dramatikos*, from *drama* (action, play).
