/ ˈrætˌtræp / (AmE), / ˈratˌtrap / (BrE)
A building or place that is in very poor, dirty, and unsafe condition; extremely dilapidated housing.
filthy, run-down housing

Examples

They were forced to live in a rattrap after losing their jobs.
The inspector condemned the apartment as a rattrap unfit for habitation.

Related Terms

3. NOUN

/ ˈrætˌtræp / (AmE), / ˈrætˌtræp / (BrE)
A difficult or tangled situation that is hard to escape from.
entangling or difficult situation

Examples

She soon found herself in a rattrap of bureaucracy and red tape.
Once you get into that kind of debt, it's a rattrap that's hard to escape.
The company's financial dealings turned into a legal rattrap for everyone involved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predicament - a difficult or unpleasant situation

Etymology

From 'rat' and 'trap', originally denoting a device for catching rats; figurative use for complicated situations dates from the late 19th century.
