catostomid

1. NOUN

/ kəˈtɑstəˌmɪd / (AmE), / kəˈtɒstəmɪd / (BrE)
a fish of the family Catostomidae, commonly known as suckers, found primarily in North America
a fish of the Catostomidae family

Examples

The river is home to several species of catostomid prized by local anglers.
A catostomid can be recognized by its distinctive mouth, adapted for vacuuming food from riverbeds.
Biologists conducted a study on the migration patterns of catostomids in the lake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sucker - a common name for a fish of the Catostomidae family

Etymology

From Catostomidae, the scientific name for the sucker family of fishes.
