Parseeism

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrsiˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsiˌɪzəm / (BrE)
the religious beliefs and practices of the Zoroastrian community known as Parsees, especially in India
Zoroastrian faith of the Parsees in India

Examples

Many Indian communities have been influenced by Parseeism over the centuries.
He studied the rituals unique to Parseeism as practiced in Mumbai.
The history of Parseeism is closely tied to the migration of Zoroastrians to India.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zoroastrianism - the ancient Persian religion founded by Zoroaster

Etymology

From 'Parsee', from Persian 'Pārsī' (Persian), with '-ism' denoting the system of belief.
