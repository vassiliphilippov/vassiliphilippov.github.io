coat

1. NOUN

/ koʊt / (AmE), / kəʊt / (BrE)
an outer piece of clothing with sleeves, worn outdoors, that covers the upper body and sometimes the legs, typically for warmth or protection
outer garment with sleeves, worn outdoors

Examples

He put on his coat before going outside into the cold.
She bought a new winter coat with a fur-lined hood.
Please hang your coat on the rack by the door.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jacket - a short coat; a garment for the upper body, shorter than a coat

Etymology

From Old French cote (coat, tunic), from Latin *cottus*.

6. NOUN

/ koʊt / (AmE), / kəʊt / (BrE)
the layer of hair, wool, or fur that covers the body of an animal
animal's layer of fur, hair, or wool

Examples

The dog's coat becomes thick and fluffy in winter to protect it from the cold.
She brushed the horse's coat until it was smooth and shiny.
A cat with a glossy coat is usually healthy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pelt - the skin of an animal with the fur, wool, or hair still attached
  • Synonym: hide - animal skin, especially with fur, wool, or hair attached

Etymology

From Old French cote (a coat), of Germanic origin; the sense of animal covering evolved later.
