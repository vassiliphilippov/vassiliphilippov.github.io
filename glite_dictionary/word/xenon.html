xenon

1. NOUN

/ ˈziːnɑn, ˈzɛnɑn / (AmE), / ˈziːnɒn / (BrE)
a chemical element (symbol Xe), a colorless, odorless, inert gas present in trace amounts in the earth's atmosphere
chemical element, inert gas

Examples

Neon lights are common, but specialty lighting can use gases like xenon for a bluish glow.
Scientists studied the physical properties of xenon under high pressure.
Trace amounts of xenon are found naturally in the earth’s atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: argon - a colorless, odorless inert gaseous element of the noble gas group
  • Synonym: krypton - a colorless, inert gaseous element in the noble gas group

Etymology

From Greek 'xenos' meaning 'strange' or 'foreign.' Named for its rare occurrence.
