Osmeridae

1. NOUN

/ ɑzˈmɛrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ɒzˈmɛrɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of small, slender, soft-finned fishes, commonly known as smelts, found in northern waters.
family of fishes including smelts

Examples

Species in the Osmeridae family are an important food source for larger fish.
Biologists studied the migration patterns of Osmeridae in Alaskan rivers.
Members of Osmeridae are known for their delicate flavor and are sought after by anglers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smelt - small fish of the family Osmeridae

Etymology

From New Latin Osmeridae, from Osmerus (type genus) + -idae (family suffix).
