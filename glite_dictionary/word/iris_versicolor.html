Iris versicolor

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪrɪs vər.sɪˈkʌl.ər / (AmE), / ˈaɪrɪs vəː.sɪˈkʌl.ə / (BrE)
a common iris of eastern North America with blue or blue-violet flowers, formerly used medicinally for its root
a blue-flowered iris of eastern North America

Examples

The beautiful Iris versicolor bloomed along the edge of the pond in early summer.
Many gardeners plant Iris versicolor for its striking blue-violet flowers.
The root of Iris versicolor was once used in traditional medicine for various ailments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue flag - another name for Iris versicolor, a blue-flowered iris

Etymology

From Latin, 'iris' meaning 'rainbow' and 'versicolor' meaning 'of various colors'.
