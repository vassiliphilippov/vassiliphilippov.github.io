splinter

1. VERB

/ ˈsplɪntɚ / (AmE), / ˈsplɪntə / (BrE)
(of a material) to break or cause to break into small, sharp fragments or slivers.
break into sharp fragments

Examples

The old chair finally splintered under his weight.
A strong gust of wind caused the fence to splinter.
During the accident, the glass windshield splintered into dozens of tiny pieces.

Synset Examples

The wood splintered

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shatter - to break suddenly into many pieces

Etymology

From Middle English 'splenter', from Middle Dutch 'splinter' meaning 'splinter, sliver'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00338713-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈsplɪntɚ / (AmE), / ˈsplɪntə / (BrE)
to break or divide into thin, sharp fragments; to split or cause to split into smaller parts, especially suddenly or violently
break or split into thin fragments

Examples

The glass table splintered into dozens of tiny fragments when it hit the floor.
Several factions splintered off from the main political party.
During the storm, the old wooden mast splintered under the force of the wind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shatter - to break suddenly into many pieces
  • Antonym: to unite - to come or bring together to form a single unit

Etymology

From Middle English 'splentre', from Old French 'esplinter', from Late Latin 'splintare', from 'splinta' (splint).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02474697-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈsplɪntɚ / (AmE), / ˈsplɪntə / (BrE)
to withdraw or break away from an organization, association, or group, especially as a result of disagreement
withdraw from a group or organization

Examples

Several members decided to splinter from the main party and form a new alliance.
The church splintered into several factions after the controversial decision.
Disagreements over policy led some activists to splinter off and form a rival group.

Synset Examples

After the break up of the Soviet Union, many republics broke away

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to secede - to formally withdraw from an organization or state
  • Synonym: to break away - to leave a group, especially because of disagreement
  • Antonym: join - to become a member of a group or organization
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02541123-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ ˈsplɪntɚ / (AmE), / ˈsplɪntə / (BrE)
a small, thin, sharp piece of material, such as wood, glass, or metal, broken off from a larger object
tiny sharp piece of material

Examples

He got a splinter in his finger while fixing the fence.
The wooden crate broke apart, scattering splinters across the floor.
Be careful with that glass; you might get a splinter if it shatters.

Synset Examples

he got a splinter in his finger
it broke into slivers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sliver - a small, thin, narrow piece of something cut or split off from a larger piece
  • Synonym: chip - a small fragment of something broken off from the whole

Etymology

From Middle English *splentre*, from Old French *esplentre*, from Latin *splintera* (splint).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09465742-n
Synset POS: n
