multi-colored

1. ADJ

/ ˈmʌltiˌkʌlɚd / (AmE), / ˈmʌltiˌkʌləd / (BrE)
Having sections or patches of different, usually bright, colors.
having different colors in sections or patches

Examples

The artist created a stunning multi-colored mural on the city wall.
She wore a multi-colored scarf that brightened up her outfit.
A flock of multi-colored balloons floated above the festival crowd.

Synset Examples

a jester dressed in motley
the painted desert
a particolored dress
a piebald horse
pied daisies

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monochrome - having only one color or shades of one color

Etymology

From Latin 'multi' (many) and English 'colored'.
