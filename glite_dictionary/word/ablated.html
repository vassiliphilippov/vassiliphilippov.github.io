ablated

1. ADJ

/ əˈbleɪtɪd / (AmE), / əˈbleɪtɪd / (BrE)
reduced in size, amount, or mass by melting, erosion, or vaporization, typically as a result of exposure to heat or other energy.
reduced by melting or erosion

Examples

The heat shield became ablated as the spacecraft re-entered the Earth's atmosphere.
The glacier showed sections of ablated ice where it met the warm ocean currents.
Certain tumors are ablated using focused laser treatment in medical procedures.

Synset Examples

the rocket's ablated head shield

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eroded - worn away by erosion
  • Antonym: intact - not damaged or diminished in any way

Etymology

From Latin ablatum (carried away), past participle of auferre (to carry away); related to 'ablation'.
