Dorylinae

1. NOUN

/ dɔːˈrɪlɪniː / (AmE), / dɒˈrɪlɪniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of predatory ants commonly known as army ants, characterized by their aggressive foraging behavior and nomadic lifestyle.
subfamily of army ants

Examples

Dorylinae are famous for their aggressive group foraging and nomadic colonies.
Recent studies have revealed new species within the Dorylinae subfamily in South America.
Researchers observed the hunting behavior of Dorylinae ants in the rainforest understory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: army ant - any member of several ant groups known for aggressive swarming behavior; related taxon

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Dorylus, with typical zoological subfamily ending -inae.

2. NOUN

/ dɔːrˈɪlaɪniː / (AmE), / dɒrɪˈliːniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of ants commonly known as army ants, characterized by their aggressive foraging behavior and complex social organization.
subfamily of army ants

Examples

The Dorylinae are known for forming massive foraging columns that overwhelm their prey.
Many naturalists have studied Dorylinae to understand the evolution of social insects.
In tropical forests, Dorylinae play a crucial role in controlling populations of other insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: army ant - any of several ant species in subfamily Dorylinae famous for aggressive behavior

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Dorylus.
