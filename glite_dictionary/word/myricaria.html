Myricaria

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪrɪˈkɛəriə / (AmE), / ˌmɪrɪˈkeəriə / (BrE)
A small genus of deciduous shrubs or subshrubs native to southern Europe, Siberia, and China, often tolerant of chalky soil.
genus of deciduous shrubs

Examples

Several species of Myricaria thrive along riverbanks in Central Asia.
Botanists studied the unique adaptations of Myricaria to alkaline and chalky soils.
It is common to find Myricaria growing alongside willows and poplars in its native range.

Etymology

From Greek myrike (tamarisk) + -aria (suffix forming taxonomic names).
