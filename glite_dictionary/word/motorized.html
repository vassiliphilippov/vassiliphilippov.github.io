motorized

1. ADJ

/ ˈmoʊ.təˌraɪzd / (AmE), / ˈməʊ.təˌraɪzd / (BrE)
equipped with a motor or motors to provide movement or power
equipped with a motor

Examples

The hospital recently purchased several motorized beds for patient comfort.
He prefers using a motorized scooter to get around the city quickly.
A motorized lawn mower makes yard work much easier than a manual one.

Synset Examples

a motorized wheelchair

Related Terms

  • Antonym: manual - operated by hand rather than by a motor

Etymology

From 'motor' + '-ized', indicating equipped with a motor.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01563877-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈmoʊ.təˌraɪzd / (AmE), / ˈməʊ.təˌraɪzd / (BrE)
(of vehicles, equipment, or activities) equipped with or powered by a motor, especially an internal combustion engine.
powered by a motor, especially a vehicle

Examples

The army deployed a motorized infantry unit to support the operation.
Many rural areas now have motorized transport, making travel much easier.
The museum displayed a motorized bicycle from the 1940s.

Synset Examples

motorized warfare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mechanized - operated by machinery or made with machinery

Etymology

From 'motor' + '-ize' + '-ed'; 'motor' from Latin 'motor' (mover).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01527569-s
Synset POS: s
