mezzanine

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛz.əˌnin / (AmE), / ˈmɛz.əniːn / (BrE)
an intermediate floor between main floors of a building, typically just above the ground floor
intermediate floor above ground level

Examples

Our new office occupies the mezzanine overlooking the lobby.
The restaurant added extra seating on a mezzanine level.
A spiral staircase connects the ground floor with the mezzanine above.

Etymology

From French 'mezzanine', from Italian 'mezzanino' (middle), from 'mezzo' (middle).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛz.əˌnin / (AmE), / ˈmɛz.əniːn / (BrE)
the first or lowest balcony in a theatre or auditorium, situated above the main floor and below higher balconies
first or lowest balcony in a theatre

Examples

We found our seats quickly on the mezzanine, which gave us a great view of the stage.
Tickets for the mezzanine sold out before any other section of the theatre.
From the mezzanine, you can see both the performers and the orchestra pit clearly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balcony - platform projecting from the wall of a building, typically above the ground floor
  • Synonym: gallery - upper floor or balcony in a theatre

Etymology

From French 'mezzanine', from Italian 'mezzanino', diminutive of 'mezzano' (middle), from Latin 'medianus'.
