John Llewellyn Lewis

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn ˈluːəlɪn ˈluːɪs / (AmE), / dʒɒn ˈluːəlɪn ˈluːɪs / (BrE)
(1880–1969) American labor leader who served as president of the United Mine Workers of America (1920–1960) and president of the Congress of Industrial Organizations (1935–1940).
prominent American labor leader (1880–1969)

Examples

John Llewellyn Lewis was a powerful figure in the American labor movement during the first half of the 20th century.
The reforms advocated by John Llewellyn Lewis improved working conditions for countless miners.
Historians often credit John Llewellyn Lewis with shaping the direction of organized labor in America.
