pathologic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpæθəˈlɑdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌpæθəˈlɒdʒɪk / (BrE)
relating to or caused by pathology or disease; pertaining to the practice or science of pathology.
relating to pathology or disease
medical

Examples

The biopsy was sent to a pathologic laboratory for analysis.
Her pathologic condition required specialized tests to confirm the diagnosis.
Medical students often study tissue samples in a pathologic context.

Synset Examples

pathological laboratory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pathological - relating to disease; affected by or showing symptoms of a disease

Etymology

From Greek pathologikos, from pathos (suffering or disease) and -logia (study).

2. ADJ

/ ˌpæθəˈlɑdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌpæθəˈlɒdʒɪk / (BrE)
relating to or caused by disease or pathology; abnormal due to disease.
relating to disease; abnormal
medical

Examples

The biopsy confirmed that the mass was pathologic in origin.
Doctors studied the pathologic changes in the tissue under the microscope.
Pathologic findings indicated the presence of an aggressive infection.

Synset Examples

diseased tonsils
a morbid growth
pathologic tissue
pathological bodily processes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pathological - relating to disease or pathology
  • Antonym: healthy - free from disease or abnormal condition

Etymology

From Greek pathologikos, from pathologia (study of disease).
