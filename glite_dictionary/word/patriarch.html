patriarch

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪtriˌɑrk / (AmE), / ˈpætrɪɑːk / (BrE)
A man who is the head of a family or tribe, or a senior figure in a group or organization, especially one with authority or wisdom.
male head of family or tribe

Examples

After the death of his father, John became the patriarch of the family.
In many traditional societies, the eldest male serves as the patriarch.
The patriarch shared stories of their ancestors with the younger generation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: matriarch - the female head of a family or tribe

Etymology

From Greek 'patriarkhēs' (chief or head of a family), from 'patria' (family) + 'arkhēs' (ruler).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪ.triˌɑrk / (AmE), / ˈpeɪ.tri.ɑːk / (BrE)
a title given to the heads of certain Eastern Orthodox Churches, such as those in Istanbul, Alexandria, Moscow, and Jerusalem.
head of Eastern Orthodox Church

Examples

The patriarch of Moscow played a crucial role in the church's response to political events.
Delegates travelled to Istanbul to meet with the Ecumenical patriarch.
The patriarch issued a statement to Orthodox Christians worldwide during Easter.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pope - the head of the Roman Catholic Church
  • Synonym: archbishop - a senior bishop in charge of an archdiocese

Etymology

From Greek patriarkhēs, from patria ‘family’ + arkhēs ‘ruler’.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪtriˌɑrk / (AmE), / ˈpeɪtrɪɑːk / (BrE)
An older man who is respected as the leader or head of a family, clan, or group, often holding the highest rank or authority.
male head or leader of a family or group

Examples

At family gatherings, everyone looked to the patriarch for guidance and wisdom.
The company's founder was seen as a patriarch who shaped its culture for decades.
The aging patriarch still presides over important decisions in the extended family.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: matriarch - a woman who is the head of a family or group

Etymology

From Greek patriarkhēs, from patria (family) + arkhēs (ruler).

4. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪtriˌɑrk / (AmE), / ˈpeɪ.tri.ɑːk / (BrE)
Any of the early male figures in the Bible, such as Abraham, Isaac, or Jacob, regarded as founding fathers of the human race or of the Hebrew people.
early biblical founding figure

Examples

Abraham is often considered the most prominent patriarch in the Old Testament.
The stories of the biblical patriarchs have shaped Western religious traditions.
Isaac and Jacob are among the patriarchs referenced throughout the Book of Genesis.

Etymology

From Greek patriarkhēs, from patria (family, clan) + arkhein (to rule).
