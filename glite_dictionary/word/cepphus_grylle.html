Cepphus grylle

1. PROPN

/ ˌsɛfəs ˈɡrɪl.i / (AmE), / ˌkɛfəs ˈɡrɪli / (BrE)
A species of seabird in the auk family found in the northern Atlantic, commonly known as the black guillemot.
species of seabird (black guillemot)

Examples

The Cepphus grylle is easily recognized by its black plumage and distinctive white wing patches.
Researchers observed a flock of Cepphus grylle nesting along the rocky coastline.
During winter, the Cepphus grylle migrates to more sheltered coastal waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black guillemot - Common name for Cepphus grylle, a northern Atlantic auk

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Cepphus' (genus name for certain auks), 'grylle' (name applied to this bird in Scandinavian languages).
