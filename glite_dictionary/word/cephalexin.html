cephalexin

1. NOUN

/ sɛfəˈlɛksɪn / (AmE), / sɛfəˈlɛksɪn / (BrE)
a broad-spectrum cephalosporin antibiotic used to treat bacterial infections of the skin, respiratory tract, ears, and urinary tract
a cephalosporin antibiotic medication
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed cephalexin to treat her skin infection.
Patients allergic to penicillin should consult their physician before taking cephalexin.
My veterinarian gave my dog cephalexin for his ear infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antibiotic - a medicine that inhibits the growth of or destroys microorganisms

Etymology

From cephalosporin (the antibiotic class) + -exin (a typical suffix in pharmaceutical nomenclature).
