prebend

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛbənd / (AmE), / ˈprɛbənd / (BrE)
A stipend allocated by a cathedral or church to a canon as part of their income.
stipend for a church canon

Examples

The cathedral provided a prebend to each of its senior clergy members.
He was appointed a canon and granted a generous prebend for his services.
During the Middle Ages, many scholars relied on a prebend as their main source of income.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benefice - a permanent church appointment for which property and income are provided

Etymology

From Latin 'praebenda' (things to be supplied), from 'praebere' (to supply).
