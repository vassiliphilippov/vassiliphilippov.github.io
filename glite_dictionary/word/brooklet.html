brooklet

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrʊk.lət / (AmE), / ˈbrʊk.lɪt / (BrE)
A very small stream; a small brook.
a very small stream

Examples

A narrow path wound alongside the brooklet, shaded by willows and wildflowers.
After the rain, a gentle brooklet trickled through the meadow, reflecting the sunlight.
Children dipped their toes into the cool water of the brooklet on a hot summer day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stream - a small, narrow river
  • Degree Higher: river - a large natural stream of water

Etymology

Diminutive of 'brook', from Middle English 'brok'.
