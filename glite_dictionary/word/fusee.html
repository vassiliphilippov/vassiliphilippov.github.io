fusee

1. NOUN

/ fjuːˈzi / (AmE), / fjʊˈziː / (BrE)
a spirally grooved tapered spindle in a mechanical clock or watch, used to equalize the force delivered by the mainspring as it unwinds
spirally grooved spindle in mechanical clocks

Examples

The antique pocket watch still had its original fusee to maintain accurate timekeeping.
A broken fusee can cause a clock to lose power unevenly as the spring unwinds.
Restoring the 18th-century clock required carefully cleaning the fusee and its chain.

Etymology

From French 'fusée', meaning 'spindle', from Latin 'fusus' (spindle).

2. NOUN

/ fjuːˈzi / (AmE), / fjuːˈziː / (BrE)
a friction match with a large head, designed to stay alight in the wind
wind-resistant match with large head

Examples

The railway worker struck a fusee and waved it to signal the oncoming train.
Due to the strong wind, only a fusee was reliable enough to light the campfire.
She kept a fusee in her emergency kit for lighting fires in harsh conditions.

Etymology

From French 'fusée' (spindle, rocket), originally referring to objects that spark or burn.

3. NOUN

/ fjuˈzi / (AmE), / fjʊˈziː / (BrE)
a colored flare used as a warning signal by vehicles such as trucks and trains
colored warning flare for vehicles

Examples

The train crew lit a fusee to alert workers about the approaching engine.
After the accident, a fusee was placed on the road to warn other drivers.
The emergency kit includes a fusee for signaling during breakdowns.

4. NOUN

/ fjuˈzi / (AmE), / fjuˈziː / (BrE)
an igniter used to start the burning of a propellant, especially in fireworks, explosives, or flares
type of igniter for propellants

Examples

The technician inserted a fusee to safely ignite the rocket’s engine.
A road flare acts as a fusee to warn approaching vehicles of an accident.
Pyrotechnicians always check that the fusee is properly attached before any fireworks display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuse - a device for igniting an explosive

Etymology

From French 'fusée' (spindle, fuse), from Latin 'fūsus' (spindle).
