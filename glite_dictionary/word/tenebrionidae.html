Tenebrionidae

1. NOUN

/ tɛ.nəˌbrɪˈɑː.nɪ.di / (AmE), / tɛn.əˌbrɪˈɒ.nɪ.diː / (BrE)
A large family of beetles, known as darkling beetles, including species such as mealworms; commonly found in dry habitats.
family of darkling beetles

Examples

The Tenebrionidae are commonly found in deserts and dry forests worldwide.
Scientists study Tenebrionidae to understand how insects adapt to arid environments.
Some members of the Tenebrionidae family, like mealworms, are raised commercially for animal feed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: darkling beetle - common name for beetles in the Tenebrionidae family

Etymology

From Latin 'tenebrio' (a darkening person, beetle); used in scientific classification.
