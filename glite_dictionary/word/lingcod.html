lingcod

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪŋ.kɑd / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋ.kɒd / (BrE)
a large North Pacific food fish (Ophiodon elongatus) valued for its lean white flesh
a North Pacific food fish

Examples

Grilled lingcod is a popular dish in many coastal restaurants.
They caught several lingcod while fishing off the coast of California.
The lingcod fillets were served with a lemon butter sauce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pollock - another white-fleshed North Pacific food fish

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪŋˌkɑd / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋˌkɒd / (BrE)
A large food fish of the northern Pacific Ocean, related to greenlings, with a long body and a large mouth.
large northern Pacific food fish

Examples

Fishermen in Alaska often catch lingcod for both sport and commercial purposes.
The restaurant special featured grilled lingcod served with a lemon butter sauce.
Biologists are concerned about the sustainability of lingcod populations in heavily fished areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greenling - a fish of the family Hexagrammidae, related to the lingcod
  • Synonym: cod - popular food fish of the genus Gadus

Etymology

From 'ling' (a type of fish) and 'cod' (a common fish name), due to its resemblance to both.
