Chloris truncata

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɔːrɪs trʌŋˈkeɪtə / (AmE), / ˈklɔːrɪs trʌŋˈkeɪtə / (BrE)
A perennial Australian grass with numerous long spikes arranged like the vanes of a windmill.
Australian perennial grass species

Examples

Chloris truncata is a common sight in the grasslands of southeastern Australia.
Farmers value Chloris truncata for its drought-resistant properties.
The distinctive windmill-like spikes make Chloris truncata easy to identify.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Chloris,' from Greek 'khlōris' (greenish-yellow), and Latin 'truncata' (cut off, shortened).
