dermatoglyphics

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɜːrmətəˈɡlɪfɪks / (AmE), / ˌdɜːmətəˈɡlɪfɪks / (BrE)
the scientific study of the patterns of ridges (whorls, loops, arches) on the fingertips, palms, and soles of the feet
study of fingerprint and skin ridge patterns
medical

Examples

She published her research on dermatoglyphics in a leading forensic science journal.
Advances in dermatoglyphics have improved criminal identification methods.
Certain genetic disorders can be detected through abnormalities in dermatoglyphics.

Synset Examples

some criminologists specialize in dermatoglyphics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fingerprint - an impression or mark made by the ridges on a finger

Etymology

From Greek derma (skin) + glyphē (carving, engraving).
