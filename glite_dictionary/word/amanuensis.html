amanuensis

1. NOUN

/ əˌmænjuˈɛnsɪs / (AmE), / əˌmanjuˈɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a person whose job is to write down what someone else dictates or to copy manuscripts; someone skilled in transcription, especially from dictation
person who writes or types for another

Examples

The professor dictated his latest article, while his amanuensis carefully typed every word.
During the trial, the official amanuensis recorded all the witness testimonies.
She served as an amanuensis for the author, transcribing his handwritten notes into a computer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secretary - a person employed to assist with correspondence and administrative tasks

Etymology

From Latin *amanuensis*, from *ab* (by) + *manus* (hand) + *-ensis* (belonging to).
