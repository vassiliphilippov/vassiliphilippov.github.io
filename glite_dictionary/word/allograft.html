allograft

1. NOUN

/ ˈæləˌɡræft / (AmE), / ˈaləʊˌɡrɑːft / (BrE)
a tissue or organ transplant from a donor of the same species but with a different genetic makeup than the recipient
transplant from same-species donor
medical

Examples

The surgeon explained that an allograft would be necessary to repair the damaged tissue.
Rejection of an allograft can occur unless the recipient’s immune system is suppressed.
Some burn victims receive an allograft as a temporary solution until their own skin heals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: autograft - a graft of tissue transplanted from one part of an individual's body to another part of the same body
  • Antonym: xenograft - a transplant of tissue from a donor of a different species
  • Synonym: transplant - an act of transferring tissue or an organ to another body

Etymology

From Greek 'allo-' (other) + 'graft' (implant or transplant).
