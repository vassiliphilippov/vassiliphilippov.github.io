Euphorbiaceae

1. NOUN

/ juˌfɔr.biˈeɪ.siˌaɪ / (AmE), / juːˌfɔː.bɪˈeɪ.sɪˌiː / (BrE)
a large family of flowering plants, commonly known as the spurge family, including many herbs, shrubs, and trees such as the poinsettia and rubber tree
plant family (spurge)

Examples

Many members of the Euphorbiaceae family produce a milky sap that can be toxic if ingested.
The rubber tree, an important source of natural rubber, belongs to the Euphorbiaceae.
Some ornamental plants, such as the poinsettia, are well-known representatives of the Euphorbiaceae.

Etymology

From the genus name Euphorbia, named after Euphorbus, the Greek physician of King Juba II of Mauretania, with the botanical family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ juːˌfɔːrbiˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / juːˌfɔːbɪˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a large family of flowering plants, commonly known as the spurge family, which includes herbs, shrubs, and trees such as poinsettia and cassava
family of flowering plants (spurges)

Examples

The Euphorbiaceae family contains many species with milky sap that can be toxic.
Cassava, a staple food in many countries, belongs to the Euphorbiaceae.
Botanists have identified more than 6,000 species within the Euphorbiaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spurge - any plant of the genus Euphorbia or the family Euphorbiaceae

Etymology

From the genus name Euphorbia, which honors Euphorbus, a Greek physician; with botanical Latin suffix -aceae (family).
