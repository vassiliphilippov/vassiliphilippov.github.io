pledger

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɛdʒər / (AmE), / ˈplɛdʒə / (BrE)
a person who makes a formal promise or undertakes a pledge, especially to donate money or support a cause
person who makes a pledge

Examples

Each pledger was recognized at the charity gala for their generous commitments.
The campaign hoped to reach a thousand pledgers before the end of the month.
As a first-time pledger, she felt proud to support the community project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pledge - a solemn promise or undertaking

Etymology

From English 'pledge' + '-er', indicating an agent or person who acts.
