quibbler

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪb.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈkwɪb.lə / (BrE)
a person who makes petty or trivial objections, often in an annoying or evasive manner
person who raises petty objections

Examples

The lawyer was known as a quibbler who stalled proceedings with endless trivial arguments.
Rather than addressing the main issue, he acted like a quibbler over minor details.
It's frustrating to work on the project when there's always a quibbler in the team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quibble - to argue or raise objections over trivial matters
  • Synonym: nitpicker - someone who finds faults with small or unimportant details

Etymology

From English 'quibble' + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
