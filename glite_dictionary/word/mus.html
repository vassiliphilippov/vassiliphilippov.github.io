Mus

1. PROPN

/ ˈmʌs / (AmE), / ˈmʌs / (BrE)
A genus of small rodents, including the common house mouse, forming the type genus of the family Muridae.
genus of mice (house mice)

Examples

The laboratory often uses Mus species for genetic experiments.
The genus Mus includes the widely studied house mouse.
Researchers discovered a new member of the Mus genus in Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mouse - small rodent; commonly refers to Mus musculus, the house mouse
  • Synonym: Muridae - the rodent family including mice and rats

Etymology

From Latin *mus* (mouse). Used in zoological taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ mʌs / (AmE), / mʌs / (BrE)
The genus of rodents that includes the common house mice, characterized by a square notch at the tips of their upper incisors.
genus of common house mice

Examples

The laboratory uses the Mus genus as a model for genetic research.
Species within the genus Mus are found in habitats across the world.
The common house mouse belongs to the Mus genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mouse - small rodent; member of the genus Mus

Etymology

From Latin *mus* (mouse).

Synset POS: n
