supervention

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuː.pərˈvɛn.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈvɛn.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of following upon or occurring in addition to something else, especially as an unexpected or supplementary event
a following or addition

Examples

The supervention of unforeseen problems delayed the completion of the project.
His recovery was complicated by the supervention of a secondary infection.
They hoped for a smooth process, but the supervention of new circumstances changed their plans.

Etymology

From Latin supervenīre (to come upon, to happen unexpectedly), from super- (over, above) + venīre (to come).
