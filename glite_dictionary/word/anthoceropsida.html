Anthoceropsida

1. NOUN

/ ˌænˌθoʊsəˈrɒpsɪdə / (AmE), / ˌænθəʊsəˈrɒpsɪdə / (BrE)
A class of non-vascular plants known as hornworts, sometimes included in the class Hepaticopsida in certain classification systems.
class of non-vascular plants (hornworts)

Examples

The Anthoceropsida are distinguished by their horn-shaped sporophytes.
Some botanists classify hornworts under the class Anthoceropsida rather than Hepaticopsida.
Recent research has revised the taxonomy of Anthoceropsida to better reflect genetic relationships.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hornwort - simple non-vascular plant of phylum Anthocerotophyta
  • Degree Lower: Hepaticopsida - class of plants including liverworts; sometimes includes hornworts

Etymology

From Greek 'anthos' (flower) and 'keras' (horn), referring to the horn-like sporophyte.

a class of non-vascular plants known as hornworts, sometimes included in the class Hepaticopsida in some classification systems
class of hornworts (plants)

Examples

The class Anthoceropsida comprises simple non-vascular plants commonly known as hornworts.
Recent studies have debated whether Anthoceropsida should be included within the class Hepaticopsida.
Students examined several species belonging to the class Anthoceropsida in their botany lab.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Hepaticopsida - a class of liverworts, sometimes including hornworts

Etymology

From Greek 'anthos' (flower) and 'keras, keratos' (horn), referring to the horn-shaped sporophyte.
