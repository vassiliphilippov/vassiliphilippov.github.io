actinopod

1. NOUN

/ ˈæk.tɪ.nəˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈæk.tɪ.nəˌpɒd / (BrE)
a protozoan organism with stiff, rod-like, radiating pseudopods used for movement and feeding
protozoan with radiating pseudopods
medical

Examples

Unlike amoebas, actinopods possess radiating pseudopods that assist them in capturing food particles.
Scientists observed the movement of the actinopod under the microscope.
The distinctive pseudopods of an actinopod are essential for its locomotion and feeding.

Etymology

From Greek aktis (ray) + pous, podos (foot).
