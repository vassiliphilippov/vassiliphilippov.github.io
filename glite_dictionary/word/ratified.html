ratified

1. ADJ

/ ˈrætɪˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈratɪfʌɪd / (BrE)
formally approved and given legal force or authority
formally approved; given legal force
legal

Examples

The treaty will not take effect until it is ratified by both governments.
All amendments must be ratified before becoming part of the constitution.
The board announced that the contract had been ratified after weeks of discussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approved - officially accepted or confirmed
  • Antonym: rejected - not accepted or not given approval

Etymology

From Latin 'ratificare' (to confirm, approve), via French 'ratifier'.
