intuitionist

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.tuˈɪ.ʃə.nɪst / (AmE), / ˌɪn.tjuˈɪ.ʃə.nɪst / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of intuitionism, especially in philosophy or mathematics
relating to intuitionism

Examples

The intuitionist approach challenges the validity of certain mathematical proofs.
Some critics argue that intuitionist ethics lacks objectivity.
His intuitionist perspective influenced his interpretation of logical systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intuitionism - the doctrine in philosophy and mathematics that proposes knowledge or truth is known by intuition rather than deduction

Etymology

From 'intuition' + '-ist', referring to proponents of intuitionism (early 20th century).
