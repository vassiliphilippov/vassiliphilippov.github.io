abstractly

1. ADV

/ əbˈstræk(t)li / (AmE), / əbˈstrɑːktli / (BrE)
in a way that is concerned with ideas or concepts rather than specific instances or concrete realities
in an abstract or theoretical way

Examples

She spoke abstractly about justice and morality, avoiding any specific examples.
The artist explained his work abstractly, focusing on concepts rather than techniques.
It's sometimes easier to think abstractly about a problem before looking for practical solutions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: concretely - in a way that relates to specific instances or realities

Etymology

From 'abstract' + '-ly', forming the adverb from the adjective.
