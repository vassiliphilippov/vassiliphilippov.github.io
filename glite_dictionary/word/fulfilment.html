fulfilment

1. NOUN

/ fʊlˈfɪlmənt / (AmE), / fʊlˈfɪlmənt / (BrE)
The achievement or realization of something desired, promised, or expected.
achievement of a desire or promise

Examples

For many people, career success brings a sense of fulfilment.
The fulfilment of her childhood dream made her very happy.
He worked hard to ensure the fulfilment of his promises.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: completion - the act of finishing something
  • Antonym: frustration - the feeling of being prevented from achieving something

Etymology

From Old French 'fulfillement', from fuller 'to fill' + 'ment'; related to 'fulfil'.

2. NOUN

/ fʊlˈfɪlmənt / (AmE), / fʊlˈfɪlmənt / (BrE)
a feeling of satisfaction or happiness because you have achieved your desires or done something important
feeling of satisfaction from achievement

Examples

After years of hard work, she finally found fulfilment in her career.
Many people seek fulfilment through helping others and making a difference.
Traveling the world gave him a deep sense of fulfilment he had never known before.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contentment - a state of being satisfied with what one has
  • Antonym: disappointment - a feeling of unhappiness because something is not as good as expected

Etymology

From Old English *fulfyllan* (to fill full), influenced by Middle English *fulfille* + *-ment*
