Windsor

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪnzər / (AmE), / ˈwɪnzə / (BrE)
the British royal family, established in 1917 when King George V adopted 'Windsor' as the family name
the British royal family since 1917
british american

Examples

Many people are fascinated by the history and traditions of the Windsor family.
The Windsor monarchy has seen significant changes over the past century.
Prince William is a prominent member of the Windsor family.

Etymology

Adopted in 1917 by King George V from Windsor Castle, the royal residence; originally from the town of Windsor, Berkshire.

2. PROPN

/ ˈwɪnzɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɪnzə / (BrE)
a city in southwestern Ontario, Canada, located on the Detroit River directly opposite Detroit, Michigan.
city in Ontario, Canada, opposite Detroit

Examples

Many tourists visit Windsor to explore its unique history and proximity to Detroit.
The Ambassador Bridge connects Windsor with Detroit, making it a vital trade route.
After moving to Windsor, she enjoyed exploring the waterfront parks and local festivals.

Etymology

Named after Windsor, Berkshire, England.
