flume

1. NOUN

/ fluːm / (AmE), / fluːm / (BrE)
a narrow, steep-sided natural gorge, typically with a stream flowing through it
narrow gorge with a stream

Examples

Tourists hiked along the wooden walkway to view the spectacular flume deep in the mountains.
The icy water rushed through the narrow flume, carving the rocks over centuries.
Mist rose from the flume as sunlight filtered through the trees above.

Etymology

From Latin 'flumen' (river) via Old French 'flum'.

2. NOUN

/ fluːm / (AmE), / fluːm / (BrE)
an artificial open channel or chute for directing water, often used to transport logs or provide water power
artificial channel for water or logs

Examples

The logs floated swiftly down the flume to the sawmill.
Early mining operations built a wooden flume to transport water from the river.
Tourists admired the old stone flume that once powered the village's mill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aqueduct - artificial channel for conveying water, often raised above ground

Etymology

From Latin 'flumen' meaning 'river'.
