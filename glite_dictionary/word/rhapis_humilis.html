Rhapis humilis

1. NOUN

/ ˈræpɪs hjuːˈmɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˈræpɪs hjuːˈmɪlɪs / (BrE)
A species of Chinese lady palm distinguished by its slender stems and finer sheath fibers compared to Rhapis excelsa.
species of slender-stemmed Chinese lady palm

Examples

The botanical garden features a rare Rhapis humilis near the conservatory entrance.
Unlike its relative, Rhapis humilis thrives in shaded indoor environments.
Collectors prize Rhapis humilis for its elegant, slender appearance in decorative landscaping.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Rhapis excelsa - Chinese lady palm species with thicker stems

Etymology

From Latin: 'Rhapis' from Greek 'rhapidos' (rod, staff), 'humilis' meaning 'low' or 'humble' in Latin.
