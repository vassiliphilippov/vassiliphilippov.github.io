2. NOUN

/ kɪŋ / (AmE), / kɪŋ / (BrE)
a male sovereign who rules a kingdom or nation, typically by hereditary right
male ruler of a kingdom

Examples

The king addressed his subjects from the palace balcony.
In chess, the main goal is to checkmate your opponent's king.
He was crowned king after the death of his father.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: queen - a female sovereign; ruler of a kingdom
  • Synonym: monarch - a sovereign head of state, especially a king or queen
  • Degree Lower: prince - the male heir to a throne, son of a king or queen

Etymology

From Old English cyning, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch koning and German König.

5. NOUN

/ kɪŋ / (AmE), / kɪŋ / (BrE)

Examples

A check occurs when the king is threatened by an opposing piece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: queen - the most powerful piece in chess
  • Degree Lower: pawn - the least valuable piece in chess
