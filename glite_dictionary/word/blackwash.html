blackwash

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈblækˌwɒʃ / (BrE)
a mixture of calomel and limewater formerly used to treat syphilitic sores
old medical treatment for syphilitic sores
medical archaic

Examples

In 19th-century medicine, blackwash was applied to skin lesions caused by syphilis.
The physician prescribed blackwash in an attempt to soothe the patient's sores.
Historical records mention the use of blackwash as a topical remedy for certain infections.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈblækˌwɒʃ / (BrE)
A coating or liquid wash applied to give a surface a black color.
black-colored surface wash

Examples

The old shed was given a fresh coat of blackwash to protect the wood.
To create an antique look, some artists use blackwash on metal sculptures.
A layer of blackwash darkened the walls, hiding imperfections.

3. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈblækˌwɒʃ / (BrE)
an attempt to deliberately damage or destroy someone's reputation, especially by making false or unfair allegations
attack on someone's reputation

Examples

The article was dismissed as a blackwash aimed at undermining the politician’s credibility.
After the rumor spread, many saw it as nothing more than a blackwash orchestrated by her rivals.
The company faced a blackwash in the media following the unfounded accusations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slander - the action or crime of making a false spoken statement damaging to a person's reputation
  • Synonym: libel - the written defamation of someone's character

Etymology

From 'black' + 'wash', likening the act to covering with a dark or discrediting layer.

4. VERB

/ ˈblækˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈblækˌwɒʃ / (BrE)
to apply a black stain or wash to a surface, especially as a coloring or finishing technique.
apply a black stain or wash

Examples

The artist decided to blackwash the wooden frame to give it an antique look.
After years of exposure, the stone walls were blackwashed to protect them from further weathering.
He carefully blackwashes each metal sculpture to achieve a consistent, matte finish.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: whitewash - to cover a surface with a white liquid mixture, especially for coloring or concealing

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'whitewash'; from 'black' + 'wash' (as in to cover with a mixture).
