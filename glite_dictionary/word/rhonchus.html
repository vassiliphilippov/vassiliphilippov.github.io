rhonchus

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːŋkəs / (AmE), / ˈrɒŋkəs / (BrE)
A whistling or snoring sound heard during breathing, especially with a stethoscope, usually caused by air passing through obstructed airways.
snoring sound in the lungs (med.)
medical

Examples

The doctor detected a rhonchus in the patient's right lung during the examination.
After listening to his chest, the nurse noted the presence of rhonchus on expiration.
Treatment was adjusted when the persistent rhonchus failed to resolve after initial therapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wheeze - a high-pitched whistling sound made while breathing
  • Synonym: rales - abnormal rattling sounds in the lungs, also known as crackles

Etymology

From Latin rhonchus (snoring, creaking), from Ancient Greek rhonchos (snoring, snorting).
