Fatherhood

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑðɚˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈfɑːðəhʊd / (BrE)
(in Christianity) God as the first person of the Trinity; the aspect of God regarded as a father.
God as the first person of the Trinity

Examples

Many Christians pray to Fatherhood as a way of addressing God in prayer.
The doctrine of the Trinity emphasizes the significance of God's Fatherhood.
The hymn praised the Fatherhood of God, invoking comfort and guidance.
