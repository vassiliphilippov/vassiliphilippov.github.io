undernourishment

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌndərˈnɜrɪʃmənt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈnʌrɪʃmənt / (BrE)
a condition resulting from not having enough food to develop or function normally
lack of adequate nutrition

Examples

The undernourishment of children remains a major concern in many developing countries.
Extreme undernourishment can have long-term effects on physical and mental development.
Efforts to reduce undernourishment focus on improving access to nutritious food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malnutrition - poor health caused by insufficient, excessive, or imbalanced diet

Etymology

From under- (not enough) + nourishment, related to Latin *nutrire* (to nourish).
