ill-advised

1. ADJ

/ ɪl ədˈvaɪzd / (AmE), / ɪl ədˈvaɪzd / (BrE)
(of an action or decision) not showing good judgment or careful consideration; likely to result in negative consequences.
not wise; lacking careful judgment

Examples

It would be ill-advised to make major investments without proper research.
The company took an ill-advised risk by expanding too quickly.
Ignoring expert advice proved to be ill-advised in the end.

Synset Examples

ill-advised efforts
it would be ill-advised to accept the offer
took the unadvised measure of going public with the accusations

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wise - showing good judgment; sensible
  • Antonym: sensible - done or chosen in accordance with wisdom or prudence
  • Synonym: rash - acting or done without careful consideration of the possible consequences

Etymology

Formed from 'ill' (badly) + 'advised' (given advice).
