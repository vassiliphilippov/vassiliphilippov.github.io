Caryota

1. NOUN

/ kæriˈoʊtə / (AmE), / kærɪˈəʊtə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical Asian palms known as fishtail palms, characterized by their bipinnate leaves resembling a fish's tail
a genus of fishtail palms

Examples

The Caryota is often planted as an ornamental tree in tropical and subtropical gardens.
Leaves from the Caryota are sometimes used for thatching roofs in rural areas.
The unique shape of the Caryota leaves makes this palm easy to identify.

Etymology

From Greek karyōtēs, meaning 'date palm,' from karyon (nut, kernel).

2. NOUN

/ kɛrˈjoʊtə / (AmE), / kəˈrɪətə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical Asian palms known as fishtail palms, recognized for their unique, jagged leaves that resemble a fish's tail.
genus of fishtail palms

Examples

The Caryota is easily recognized by its unique, fishtail-shaped leaves.
Several species within the genus Caryota are popular ornamental plants in tropical gardens.
Unlike other palms, Caryota produces clusters of small fruits after flowering.

Etymology

From Greek karyōtēs (having nuts like acorns), referring to the fruits; used in scientific Latin.
