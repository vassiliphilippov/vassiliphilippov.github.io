laxation

1. NOUN

/ lækˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / lækˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of eliminating fecal waste from the body through the anus
elimination of fecal waste
medical

Examples

Adequate fiber intake is important for regular laxation.
Medications that promote laxation are commonly known as laxatives.
The patient experienced improved laxation after dietary changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defecation - the discharge of feces from the body

Etymology

From Latin 'laxatio' (a loosening, relaxing), from 'laxare' (to loosen).

2. NOUN

/ lækˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / lækˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something less tight or loosened
the act of loosening

Examples

The laxation of the corset provided immediate relief.
Engineers allowed slight laxation in the cables to reduce tension.
Manual laxation of the band was necessary during the procedure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tightening - the act of making something tight or tighter

Etymology

From Latin 'laxatio' (a loosening), from 'laxare' (to loosen).
