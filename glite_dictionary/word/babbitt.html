babbitt

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæbɪt / (AmE), / ˈbæbɪt / (BrE)
an alloy of tin with copper and antimony, used as a lining for bearings to reduce friction
a soft alloy for lining bearings

Examples

The mechanic replaced the worn babbitt in the engine's main bearing.
Proper use of babbitt can extend the lifespan of industrial machinery.
The engineer specified a babbitt alloy for its excellent antifriction properties.

Etymology

Named after Isaac Babbitt (1799–1862), its American inventor.

2. VERB

/ ˈbæbɪt / (AmE), / ˈbæbɪt / (BrE)
to line a bearing or similar surface with Babbitt metal to reduce friction
line with Babbitt metal

Examples

The mechanic will babbitt the bearing to ensure smooth operation of the machine.
After years of use, the old shaft needed to be babbitted again.
They specialize in babbitting industrial equipment for various manufacturing plants.

Etymology

From the name of Isaac Babbitt, the inventor of Babbitt metal (19th century).
