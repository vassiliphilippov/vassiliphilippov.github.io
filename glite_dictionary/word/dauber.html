dauber

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔbɚ / (AmE), / ˈdɔːbə / (BrE)
a person who paints in an unskilled or amateurish way, especially as a painter of pictures.
an unskilled or amateurish painter

Examples

Despite his enthusiasm, the critics dismissed him as merely a dauber.
Her work was often compared to that of an untrained dauber.
Unlike his peers, John refused to be called a dauber, insisting on the value of his expressive style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: painter - a person who paints pictures or other surfaces
  • Degree Higher: artist - a person who creates art, especially paintings or drawings

Etymology

From 'daub' (to smear) + '-er' (agent suffix).
