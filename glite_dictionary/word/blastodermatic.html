blastodermatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌblæstəˌdərˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌblæstəʊdəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a blastoderm, the layer of cells formed at an early stage of embryonic development
of or relating to a blastoderm
medical

Examples

The scientist observed blastodermatic changes during the earliest phase of chick embryo development.
One important blastodermatic structure is the germinal disc in avian eggs.
Certain abnormalities can affect the blastodermatic layer, leading to failed embryogenesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blastoderm - the layer of cells forming the early embryo

Etymology

From blastoderm + -atic, patterned after other scientific adjectives.
