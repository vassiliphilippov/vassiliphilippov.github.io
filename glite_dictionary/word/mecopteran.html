mecopteran

1. NOUN

/ məˈkɑptərən / (AmE), / mɪˈkɒptərən / (BrE)
any of various carnivorous insects of the order Mecoptera, characterized by elongated heads and membranous wings
carnivorous insect of order Mecoptera

Examples

The mecopteran is easily recognized by its elongated face and slender wings.
Scientists recently discovered a new species of mecopteran in the rainforest.
Many mecopterans feed on dead insects, helping to recycle nutrients in their ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scorpionfly - an insect of the family Panorpidae within the Mecoptera, with a curved tail like that of a scorpion

Etymology

From New Latin Mecoptera, the name of the order, from Greek mēkos (length) + pteron (wing).
