to kibitz

1. VERB

/ ˈkɪbɪts / (AmE), / ˈkɪbɪts / (BrE)
to make unwanted and intrusive comments, especially while observing a game or activity
make unwanted, intrusive comments

Examples

Please try not to kibitz while we are playing chess; it distracts the players.
He couldn't resist kibitzing during the meeting, making jokes and comments from the back of the room.
They kibitzed loudly as the team worked, giving advice no one wanted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to interfere - to get involved in something without invitation or necessity
  • Synonym: to meddle - to intrude in other people's affairs without being asked

Etymology

From Yiddish *kibitsn*, possibly from German *Kiebitz* (a busybody, literally: lapwing).
