spiffing

1. ADJ

/ ˈspɪfɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspɪfɪŋ / (BrE)
Excellent or splendid; very good (chiefly British, dated or informal).
excellent; splendid (British, dated)
informal british archaic

Examples

That cake you made was absolutely spiffing!
We had a spiffing time at the seaside last weekend.
What a spiffing idea to have a picnic in the garden!

Synset Examples

that's a perfectly spiffing idea

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splendid - very impressive; excellent
  • Synonym: excellent - extremely good; outstanding
  • Antonym: dreadful - very bad or unpleasant

Etymology

Origin uncertain, possibly from 'spiff' (well-dressed, smart) + '-ing'. Popularized in British slang in the late 19th to early 20th century.
