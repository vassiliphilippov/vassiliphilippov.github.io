Asarum virginicum

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɛɹəm vɜrˈdʒɪnɪkəm / (AmE), / əˈsɑːɹəm vɜːˈdʒɪnɪkəm / (BrE)
A species of low-growing, evergreen perennial plant with mottled green and silvery-grey heart-shaped pungent leaves, native to areas from Virginia to South Carolina.
evergreen perennial plant of eastern US

Examples

The shady garden featured a patch of Asarum virginicum thriving beneath the trees.
Botanists often study Asarum virginicum for its uniquely patterned leaves and distinctive aroma.
In parts of the southeastern United States, Asarum virginicum can be found carpeting forest floors.

Etymology

From genus name Asarum, Latin, and specific epithet virginicum, referring to Virginia.
