cathodic

1. ADJ

/ kəˈθɑdɪk / (AmE), / kəˈθɒdɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving a cathode; occurring at or pertaining to a cathode, especially in electrochemical contexts.
relating to a cathode

Examples

The cathodic reaction is essential in corrosion protection systems.
During electroplating, cathodic processes result in metal deposition.
Engineers monitor cathodic areas to prevent pipeline corrosion.

Synset Examples

cathodic deposition of metals

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anodic - relating to or involving an anode

Etymology

From 'cathode' + '-ic' (forming adjectives); 'cathode' from Greek 'kathodos' (way down).
