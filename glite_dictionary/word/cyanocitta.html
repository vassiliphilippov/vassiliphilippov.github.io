Cyanocitta

1. NOUN

/ saɪ.ə.nəˈsɪtə / (AmE), / saɪ.ə.nəˈsɪtə / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the crow family, including the blue jay and Steller's jay, native to North and Central America.
genus of New World jays

Examples

The blue jay, a member of the genus Cyanocitta, is common in eastern North America.
Scientists have studied the migration patterns of several Cyanocitta species.
The Steller's jay, classified as Cyanocitta stelleri, inhabits western forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jay - any of various birds in the crow family with noisy calls and brightly colored plumage

Etymology

From Greek 'kyanos' (dark blue) + 'kitta' (jay).

/ ˌsaɪənoʊˈsɪtə / (AmE), / ˌsaɪənəʊˈsɪtə / (BrE)
A genus of American jays, including the blue jay and Steller's jay, found in North and Central America.
genus of American jays (birds)

Examples

The Cyanocitta genus includes the familiar blue jay found across North America.
Ornithologists have long studied Cyanocitta to understand jay behavior and evolution.
Many birdwatchers hope to spot both species of Cyanocitta during their trips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jay - a bird of the crow family with brightly colored plumage

Etymology

From Greek 'kyanos' (dark blue) and 'kitta' (jay).
