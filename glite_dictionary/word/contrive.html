to contrive

1. VERB

/ kənˈtraɪv / (AmE), / kənˈtraɪv / (BrE)
to invent or devise (an idea, plan, or solution) through careful thought or ingenuity; to bring about through mental effort
devise or invent after mental effort

Examples

After hours of discussion, the team managed to contrive a viable solution to the problem.
She contrived an excuse to avoid attending the meeting.
They are trying to contrive a system that will save both time and money.

Synset Examples

excogitate a way to measure the speed of light

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devise - to plan or invent by careful thought
  • Synonym: to concoct - to invent or devise, especially in a clever or deceitful way

Etymology

From Old French 'controver', from Latin 'contropare' (to compare), later influenced by 'trouver' (to find).

2. VERB

/ kənˈtraɪv / (AmE), / kənˈtraɪv / (BrE)
to plan or devise a way of achieving something, often through skill or ingenuity
plan or devise with ingenuity

Examples

She managed to contrive a meeting between the two leaders despite their tight schedules.
He contrived a clever solution to the complex problem.
They had to contrive a way to get past the locked gate without being noticed.

Synset Examples

They contrived to murder their boss
design a new sales strategy
plan an attack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devise - to plan or invent by careful thought
  • Synonym: plan - to decide on and arrange in advance

Etymology

From Old French *contrever* (to invent, imagine), from Late Latin *contropare* (to compare, invent).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
