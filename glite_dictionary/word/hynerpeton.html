Hynerpeton

1. PROPN

/ haɪnˈɜːrpɪˌtɒn / (AmE), / haɪˈnɜːpɪˌtɒn / (BrE)
an extinct genus of early amphibians from the Devonian period, known from fossils found in Pennsylvania
extinct Devonian amphibian genus

Examples

The discovery of Hynerpeton has shed new light on the evolution of early amphibians.
Paleontologists identified the fossil as belonging to the genus Hynerpeton.
The Devonian rocks in Pennsylvania are famous for producing Hynerpeton specimens.

Etymology

From Greek 'Hyner' (referring to Hyner, Pennsylvania, where the fossils were found) and Greek 'herpeton' (creeping thing, reptile).

2. NOUN

/ haɪ.nɚˈpiː.tɑn / (AmE), / haɪ.nəˈpɛ.tɒn / (BrE)
an extinct genus of early amphibians from the Devonian period, considered among the earliest fossil amphibians discovered, found in Pennsylvania.
extinct Devonian amphibian genus

Examples

The fossilized remains of Hynerpeton were a significant discovery for paleontologists studying early vertebrate evolution.
Researchers believe Hynerpeton inhabited swampy areas of what is now Pennsylvania during the Devonian period.
Hynerpeton is notable for its transitional features between fish and early land-dwelling vertebrates.

Etymology

From Hyner, Pennsylvania (location of discovery) + Greek 'peton' (creature).
