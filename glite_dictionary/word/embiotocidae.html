Embiotocidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛmbiəˈtɑsɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌɛmbɪəʊˈtɒsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of viviparous percoid fishes, commonly known as surf fishes, found mainly along the Pacific coasts of North America and Asia.
family of viviparous surf fishes

Examples

Members of the Embiotocidae are known for giving birth to live young rather than laying eggs.
The Embiotocidae family includes several species commonly seen along California's rocky shores.
Unlike many other fish families, Embiotocidae exhibit parental care for their offspring.

Etymology

From scientific Latin, based on Greek 'embios' (living in) and 'tokos' (birth), referring to their live-bearing reproduction.
