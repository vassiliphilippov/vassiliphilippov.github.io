musculus quadriceps femoris

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌs.kjə.ləs kwɑːˈdrɪs.ɛps ˈfɛm.ə.rɪs / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kjʊ.ləs kwɒˈdrɪs.ɛps ˈfɛm.ə.rɪs / (BrE)
a large muscle at the front of the thigh, responsible for extending the leg at the knee joint
muscle at the front of the thigh
medical

Examples

A torn musculus quadriceps femoris can make it difficult to walk or stand.
The surgeon examined the patient's musculus quadriceps femoris for signs of injury.
Strengthening the musculus quadriceps femoris can help prevent knee problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quadriceps - the large muscle at the front of the thigh

Etymology

From Latin: 'musculus' (muscle), 'quadriceps' (four-headed), 'femoris' (of the thigh).
