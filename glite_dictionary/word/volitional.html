volitional

1. ADJ

/ voʊˈlɪʃənl / (AmE), / vəˈlɪʃənl / (BrE)
done by conscious choice or deliberate intention; relating to the exercise of one's will
relating to conscious choice or will

Examples

The move was entirely a volitional decision on her part, not something she was forced into.
Participation in the study must be volitional, requiring informed consent from all subjects.
Psychologists are interested in both involuntary and volitional actions in human behavior.

Synset Examples

a volitional act

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intentional - done on purpose; deliberate
  • Antonym: involuntary - done without conscious control or choice

Etymology

From Medieval Latin volitionalis, from Latin voluntās (will, wish).
