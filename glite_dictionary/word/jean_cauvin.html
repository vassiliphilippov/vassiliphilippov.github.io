Jean Cauvin

1. PROPN

/ ʒɑ̃ koʊˈvæ̃ / (AmE), / ʒɑ̃ koʊˈvɛ̃ / (BrE)
(1509–1564) French-born Swiss theologian and reformer whose doctrines on predestination and justification by faith significantly influenced Presbyterianism and Reformed Christianity.
French-born Swiss reformer and theologian

Examples

Jean Cauvin was a leading figure in the Protestant Reformation and is better known by the English name John Calvin.
The doctrines developed by Jean Cauvin continue to influence Reformed churches around the world.
Many historians study the impact of Jean Cauvin on European religious history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: John Calvin - Anglicized name of Jean Cauvin, French-born Swiss reformer

Etymology

French name for John Calvin, derived from Latinized 'Calvinus' (from his French surname Cauvin).
