blackheart

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌhɑrt / (AmE), / ˈblækˌhɑːt / (BrE)
A variety of heart-shaped cherry with dark flesh and skin.
a dark-fleshed heart cherry

Examples

The orchard specializes in growing blackheart cherries, which are prized for their rich flavor.
She picked a ripe blackheart from the tree and admired its deep color.
Many prefer the taste of blackheart cherries over lighter varieties.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌhɑrt / (AmE), / ˈblækˌhɑːt / (BrE)
A disease affecting plants or crops in which the central tissues turn black, often caused by environmental factors or infection.
plant disease causing blackened center

Examples

Potatoes affected by blackheart often show a darkened core upon cutting.
Poor ventilation in storage facilities can lead to blackheart in apples.
Farmers inspect their crops regularly to prevent the spread of blackheart.
