copartner

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈpɑrtnɚ / (AmE), / kəʊˈpɑːtnə / (BrE)
a person who shares partnership with one or more others, especially in a business venture
a joint business partner

Examples

He became a copartner in the law firm after years of hard work.
The two copartners disagreed on how to expand the business.
As a copartner, she shared both profits and losses equally.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partner - a person who takes part in an undertaking, especially in a business

Etymology

From Latin 'co-' (together) + 'partner'.
