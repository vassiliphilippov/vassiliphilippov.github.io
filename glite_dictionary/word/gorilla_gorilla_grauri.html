Gorilla gorilla grauri

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈrɪl.ə ɡəˈrɪl.ə ˈɡraʊri / (AmE), / ɡəˈrɪl.ə ɡəˈrɪl.ə ˈɡraʊri / (BrE)
the largest subspecies of gorilla, native to certain central African forests, characterized by its size and robust build
largest subspecies of gorilla

Examples

The Gorilla gorilla grauri is known for its massive size compared to other gorilla subspecies.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the dwindling population of Gorilla gorilla grauri in the wild.
Researchers observed a group of Gorilla gorilla grauri feeding quietly in the dense rainforest.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: western gorilla - species of gorilla found in western Africa

Etymology

From modern Latin: binomial name following scientific naming conventions, with 'grauri' typically honoring a person.
