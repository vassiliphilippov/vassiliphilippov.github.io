Aix galericulata

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪks ˌɡæləˌrɪkjəˈleɪtə / (AmE), / ˌeɪks ˌɡalərɪkjʊˈleɪtə / (BrE)
a brightly colored East Asian duck with a distinctive crest, often kept as an ornamental bird; also known as the mandarin duck
a species of ornamental East Asian duck

Examples

The Aix galericulata is admired for its vivid plumage and graceful shape in ornamental ponds.
Many birdwatchers visit parks in search of the elusive Aix galericulata during migration season.
Zoos often display the Aix galericulata due to its spectacular coloration and gentle temperament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mandarin duck - a showy Asian duck, sometimes called 'Aix galericulata'

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Aix' (a kind of duck) and species 'galericulata' (wearing a small cap), referring to its crested appearance.
