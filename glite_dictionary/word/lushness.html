lushness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌʃ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈlʌʃ.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being rich, abundant, and pleasing to the senses, often in reference to vegetation, appearance, or texture
quality of being rich and pleasing

Examples

The lushness of the garden made it a favorite spot for afternoon walks.
Her paintings captured the lushness of the tropical rainforest in vivid detail.
We admired the lushness of the velvet curtains in the old theater.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luxuriance - the quality of being rich and profuse in growth or detail
  • Antonym: barrenness - the quality of lacking vegetation or life, empty

Etymology

From 'lush' (luxuriant) + '-ness' (state or quality).
