Encephalartos caffer

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˌsɛfəˈlɑrtəs ˈkæfər / (AmE), / ɛnˌsɛfəˈlɑːtɒs ˈkæfə / (BrE)
A species of cycad native to South Africa, whose starchy pith from the fruit is sometimes used as food.
a South African cycad species, edible pith

Examples

The ancient San people once utilized the starchy pith of Encephalartos caffer as a famine food.
Botanists are working to conserve Encephalartos caffer due to its endangered status.
The distinctive, palm-like appearance of Encephalartos caffer makes it popular among rare plant collectors.

Etymology

From Greek enkephalos (brain) and artos (bread), referring to the starchy pith, and the species name 'caffer' meaning 'of southern Africa.'
