graham

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪ.əm / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪ.əm / (BrE)
A type of flour made by grinding the whole wheat grain, including the bran and germ, producing a coarse brown flour.
coarse whole wheat flour

Examples

The recipe calls for one cup of graham to make the cookies healthier.
He prefers graham over white flour for its richer flavor and texture.
Whole wheat and graham are often recommended for high-fiber diets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wholemeal flour - British term for flour made from whole wheat grains
  • Antonym: refined flour - flour that has had the bran and germ removed

Etymology

Named after Sylvester Graham (1794–1851), an American dietary reformer.
