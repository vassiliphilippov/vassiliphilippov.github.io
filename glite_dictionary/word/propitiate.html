to propitiate

1. VERB

/ prəˈpɪʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / prəˈpɪʃieɪt / (BrE)
to win or regain the favor of a person, spirit, or god by doing something that pleases them; to appease or pacify.
to appease or win favor

Examples

The villagers made offerings to propitiate the angry spirits.
He tried to propitiate his boss after arriving late to the meeting.
Appeasing rituals were performed to propitiate the ancient gods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to appease - to calm or pacify by acceding to demands or soothing anger
  • Synonym: to pacify - to calm, soothe, or bring peace to someone
  • Antonym: to enrage - to cause to become very angry

Etymology

From Latin 'propitiare' (to render favorable), from 'propitius' (favorable, gracious).
