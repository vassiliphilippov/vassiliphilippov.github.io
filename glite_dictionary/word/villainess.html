villainess

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪlənɛs / (AmE), / ˈvɪlənɪs / (BrE)
A woman who is a villain, especially in stories, plays, or films.
female villain, especially in fiction

Examples

The villainess plotted against the hero at every turn in the novel.
Many classic fairy tales feature a wicked villainess as the antagonist.
She played a convincing villainess in the popular television series.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: villain - a character whose evil actions or motives are important to the plot

Etymology

Formed from 'villain' + '-ess', a suffix denoting female gender, from Latin '-issa' via Old French '-esse'.
