Fistulina

1. NOUN

/ fɪs.tʃʊˈliː.nə / (AmE), / fɪs.tjʊˈliː.nə / (BrE)
A genus of fungi characterized by having each pore separate but crowded, typically known for species like the beefsteak fungus.
genus of fungi with separate crowded pores

Examples

The Fistulina can be found growing on old oak trees in the forest.
Scientists identified the fungus as Fistulina because of its unique pore structure.
In some regions, Fistulina is commonly referred to as the beefsteak fungus due to its appearance.

Etymology

From Latin 'fistula' (a pipe or tube), alluding to the tube-like pores.
