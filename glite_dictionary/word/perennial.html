perennial

1. NOUN

/ pəˈrɛniəl / (AmE), / pəˈrɛn.i.əl / (BrE)
A plant that lives for several years, typically surviving through more than two growing seasons.
a plant that lives for several years

Examples

Many gardeners prefer to plant perennials because they bloom year after year.
Hostas are popular perennials known for their lush green foliage.
You should cut back your perennials in the fall to encourage healthy growth in the spring.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: annual - a plant that completes its life cycle in one year
  • Antonym: biennial - a plant that takes two years to complete its life cycle

Etymology

From Latin perennialis, from perennis ('lasting through the year').

2. ADJ

/ pəˈrɛniəl / (AmE), / pəˈrɛniəl / (BrE)
(of a plant) living for several years, typically more than two, and flowering each year.
lasting several years (of plants)

Examples

Lavender is a perennial herb that can thrive for many seasons in your garden.
Most gardeners prefer perennial flowers because they do not need to be replanted each year.
The perennial shrubs produce blossoms every spring without fail.

Synset Examples

the common buttercup is a popular perennial plant

Related Terms

  • Antonym: annual - plant that completes its life cycle in one year
  • Degree Lower: biennial - plant that takes two years to complete its life cycle

Etymology

From Latin perennis (lasting through the year), from per- (through) + annus (year).
