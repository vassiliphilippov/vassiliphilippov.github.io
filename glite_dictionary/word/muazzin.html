muazzin

1. NOUN

/ muˈɑː.zɪn / (AmE), / mjuˈæz.ɪn / (BrE)
the Muslim official at a mosque who calls the faithful to prayer from the minaret five times a day
Muslim official who calls to prayer

Examples

The muazzin began the call to prayer as the sun set over the city.
Visitors listened respectfully while the muazzin chanted from the top of the minaret.
Each morning, the muazzin’s voice echoed through the quiet streets.

Etymology

From Arabic *mu’adhdhin* (one who calls [to prayer]).
