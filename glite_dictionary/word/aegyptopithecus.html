Aegyptopithecus

1. NOUN

/ iˌɡɪptəˈpɪθəkəs / (AmE), / iˌɡɪptəˈpɪθɪkəs / (BrE)
an extinct genus of primates that lived approximately 38 million years ago, with fossils discovered in Egypt
extinct primate genus; fossils from Egypt

Examples

The fossils of Aegyptopithecus provide valuable information about early primate evolution.
Scientists discovered a nearly complete skull of Aegyptopithecus in Egyptian rock formations.
Many researchers believe Aegyptopithecus bridges evolutionary gaps between earlier primates and modern apes.

Etymology

From Greek 'Aigyptos' (Egypt) and 'pithekos' (ape/monkey).

2. NOUN

/ iːˌdʒɪptəˈpɪθɪkəs / (AmE), / iːˌdʒɪptəˈpɪθɪkəs / (BrE)
a genus of early fossil primates belonging to the family Propliopithecidae, known from the Oligocene period and considered important in the evolution of hominoids.
fossil genus of early primates

Examples

Fossils of Aegyptopithecus have provided valuable insight into the evolution of early primates.
Paleontologists discovered the remains of Aegyptopithecus in Egypt's Fayum Depression.
The dental structure of Aegyptopithecus suggests it had a varied diet including fruit and leaves.

Etymology

From Greek Aigyptos (Egypt) + pithekos (ape), referring to its discovery in Egypt.
