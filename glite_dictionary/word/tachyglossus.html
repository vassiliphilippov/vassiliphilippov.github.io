Tachyglossus

1. PROPN

/ ˌtækiˈɡlɑsəs / (AmE), / ˌtækɪˈɡlɒsəs / (BrE)
A genus of monotreme mammals, comprising the short-beaked echidna, native to Australia and New Guinea.
genus including the short-beaked echidna

Examples

The short-beaked echidna is the only surviving member of the genus Tachyglossus.
Scientists studied the unique tongue mechanics of Tachyglossus to understand its feeding habits.
Tachyglossus is well adapted to finding ants and termites in Australia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: echidna - spiny anteater belonging to the same family

Etymology

From Greek *tachys* (swift) + *glōssa* (tongue), referring to the animal's rapidly moving tongue.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtækiˈɡlɑsəs / (AmE), / ˌtækɪˈɡlɒsəs / (BrE)
A genus of monotreme mammals comprising the short-beaked echidna, the type genus of the family Tachyglossidae.
genus of echidnas; monotreme mammal
medical

Examples

The short-beaked echidna belongs to the genus Tachyglossus.
Researchers have studied the defensive behavior of Tachyglossus species in Australia.
Fossil records indicate that Tachyglossus has ancient roots in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: echidna - spiny, egg-laying mammal of Australasia

Etymology

From Greek 'takhus' (swift) + 'glōssa' (tongue), referring to the animal's feeding adaptation.
