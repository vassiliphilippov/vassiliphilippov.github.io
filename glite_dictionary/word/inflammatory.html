inflammatory

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈflæməˌtɔri / (AmE), / ɪnˈflaməˌtəri / (BrE)
characterized by or causing inflammation, especially in a part of the body
caused by or relating to inflammation
medical

Examples

The patient showed signs of an inflammatory response after the surgery.
Doctors prescribed medication to reduce the inflammatory process in her joints.
Some skin conditions are considered inflammatory diseases and require special treatment.

Synset Examples

an inflammatory process
an inflammatory response

Related Terms

  • Antonym: noninflammatory - not involving or caused by inflammation

Etymology

From Latin inflammare (to set on fire), from inflammatio (a setting on fire, inflammation).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈflæməˌtɔri / (AmE), / ɪnˈflæmət(ə)ri / (BrE)
(of speech or writing) intended to arouse anger or strong emotion, or to provoke action or rebellion.
intended to provoke anger or action

Examples

The politician's inflammatory remarks led to widespread protests across the city.
His article contained several inflammatory statements that incited strong public reactions.
Avoid making any inflammatory comments during the debate to keep things civil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incendiary - intended to stir up conflict or anger
  • Synonym: provocative - causing annoyance or anger, or another strong reaction

Etymology

From Latin inflammare (to set on fire), via Medieval Latin inflammatorius.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01903445-s
Synset POS: s
