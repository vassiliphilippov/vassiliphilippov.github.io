encampment

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkæmpmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkæmpmənt / (BrE)
A place where a group, especially of soldiers, sets up temporary living quarters, typically outdoors or in tents.
military temporary camp

Examples

The soldiers set up an encampment near the river for the night.
Archaeologists discovered the remains of an ancient encampment in the valley.
The general inspected the encampment to ensure that everyone had proper shelter.

Synset Examples

wherever he went in the camp the men were grumbling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camp - place with temporary shelters for troops or others

Etymology

From French 'encampement', from 'encamper' (to encamp); 'en-' (in, into) + 'camp' (camp).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkæmpmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkæmpmənt / (BrE)
the act of setting up and living in a camp, especially in tents or temporary shelters
setting up and living in a camp

Examples

The soldiers set up an encampment at the edge of the forest.
During their expedition, the team established a temporary encampment by the river.
The festival's encampment was filled with colorful tents and lively music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camp - a place where tents or temporary shelters are set up

Etymology

From French 'encampement', from 'encamper' (to encamp), based on 'camp'.

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkæmpmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkæmpmənt / (BrE)
a place, especially an area of land, where people on holiday can set up tents and stay temporarily
site for holiday tents

Examples

We arrived at the encampment just before sunset and quickly set up our tent.
During the festival, the riverside encampment was filled with families enjoying the outdoors.
The children explored the woodland area surrounding the encampment all afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campsite - a place used for camping

Etymology

From French 'campement', from 'camper' (to camp).
