thumbstall

1. NOUN

/ ˈθʌmstɔl / (AmE), / ˈθʌmstɔːl / (BrE)
a protective covering worn over the thumb, typically to protect it when injured or to prevent injury during activities
protective covering for the thumb

Examples

The nurse placed a thumbstall over his thumb to protect the wound.
He wore a leather thumbstall while handling the heavy machinery.
After injuring her thumb, she was advised to use a thumbstall when gardening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thimble - a protective cap worn on the finger, especially for sewing

Etymology

From 'thumb' + 'stall', where 'stall' means a protective covering.
