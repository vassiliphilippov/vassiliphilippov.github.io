haemothorax

1. NOUN

/ hiːməˈθɔːræks / (AmE), / hiːməˈθɔːræks / (BrE)
An accumulation of blood in the pleural cavity, the space between the lungs and the chest wall.
blood collection in the pleural cavity
medical british

Examples

The patient developed a haemothorax after a severe chest injury.
Emergency surgery was required to treat the haemothorax.
On the X-ray, a haemothorax was visible on the left side of his chest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemothorax - accumulation of blood in the pleural cavity (American spelling)

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' (blood) + 'thorax' (chest).
