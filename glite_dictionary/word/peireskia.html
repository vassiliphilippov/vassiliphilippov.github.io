Peireskia

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈrɛskiə / (AmE), / paɪˈrɛskiə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American shrubby trees and woody climbers, notable for their slender branches, broad flat leaves, and large clusters of flowers.
genus of tropical American shrubs and climbers

Examples

Peireskia is unique among cacti for its broad, flat leaves and woody stems.
Botanists studied a rare species of Peireskia found in the rainforest.
The gardener was delighted when his Peireskia produced its first large cluster of flowers.

Etymology

From Latinized form of the name of Nicolas-Claude Fabri de Peiresc, a French botanist.

genus of tropical American shrubby trees and climbers

The Peireskia genus is notable for its broad, flat leaves, which are unusual among cacti.
