Hypericales

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌpɛrɪˈkeɪliːz / (AmE), / hʌɪˌpɛrɪˈkeɪliːz / (BrE)
a large order of dicotyledonous plants in the subclass Dilleniidae, including families such as Hypericaceae (St. John's wort family) and Clusiaceae.
an order of dicotyledonous plants
medical

Examples

Many members of the Hypericales order are known for their medicinal properties.
Researchers have reclassified several plant families previously included in Hypericales.
The Hypericales include familiar plants such as St. John's wort.

Etymology

From the genus name Hypericum, with the standard botanical suffix -ales denoting plant orders.

2. NOUN

/ haɪˌpɛrɪˈkeɪliːz / (AmE), / haɪˌpɛrɪˈkeɪliːz / (BrE)
a large order of dicotyledonous plants in the subclass Dilleniidae, including the St. John's wort family and related groups
an order of flowering plants

Examples

The Hypericales include many species valued for their medicinal properties.
Researchers are studying the evolutionary history of the Hypericales.
Several ornamental plants belong to the Hypericales order.

Etymology

From the genus name Hypericum, type genus of the St. John's-wort family, plus the taxonomic suffix -ales indicating order.
