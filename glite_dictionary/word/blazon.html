blazon

1. NOUN

/ ˈbleɪzən / (AmE), / ˈbleɪz(ə)n / (BrE)
the official symbols or emblem of a family, state, or organization, typically depicted in heraldry
official heraldic symbols or emblems

Examples

The ancient castle's gates bore the family blazon for all to see.
Each knight's shield was painted with his personal blazon.
Their university adopted a new blazon to symbolize its history and values.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coat of arms - a unique heraldic design on a shield or escutcheon

Etymology

From Old French blason (coat of arms, shield), of uncertain origin.

2. VERB

/ ˈbleɪ.zən / (AmE), / ˈbleɪ.zən / (BrE)
to decorate or display (a surface) with heraldic arms, symbols, or designs; to depict or exhibit prominently
to adorn with heraldic arms or display prominently

Examples

The king ordered his shield to be blazoned with the royal crest.
Ancient banners were blazoned with family coats of arms and symbols.
The artist spent days carefully blazoning the emblems onto the knight’s armor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emblazon - to decorate with heraldic or other designs

Etymology

From Old French *blason* (a shield), related to heraldic display.
