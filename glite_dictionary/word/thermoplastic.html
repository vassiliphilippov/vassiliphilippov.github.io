thermoplastic

1. NOUN

/ ˌθɝːmoʊˈplæstɪk / (AmE), / ˌθɜːməʊˈplæstɪk / (BrE)
a plastic material that becomes soft when heated and hardens when cooled, a process that can be repeated without significant chemical change
type of plastic that softens with heat

Examples

Polyethylene is a common thermoplastic used in packaging and containers.
When heated, the thermoplastic can be molded into various shapes for manufacturing.
Unlike thermoset plastics, a thermoplastic can be remelted and reshaped multiple times.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: thermoset - a plastic material that hardens permanently after being heated and shaped

Etymology

From thermo- (relating to heat) + plastic (capable of being shaped or molded).

2. ADJ

/ ˌθɝːmoʊˈplæstɪk / (AmE), / ˌθɜːməʊˈplæstɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance or material) capable of becoming soft or moldable when heated and hard again when cooled, a process which can be repeated without major chemical change.
softens when heated, hardens when cooled (repeatedly)

Examples

Most toys made from thermoplastic materials can be recycled by melting and remolding them.
The engineer chose a thermoplastic polymer for the component due to its reusability.
Because PVC is thermoplastic, it can be easily shaped into pipes or fittings when heated.

Synset Examples

thermoplastic materials can be remelted and cooled time after time without undergoing any appreciable chemical change

Related Terms

  • Antonym: thermosetting - (of polymers) unable to be remelted after curing; opposite of thermoplastic

Etymology

From thermo- (relating to heat) + plastic (capable of being shaped or molded).
