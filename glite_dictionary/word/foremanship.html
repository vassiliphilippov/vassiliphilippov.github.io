foremanship

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔːrmənˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmənʃɪp / (BrE)
the position or status of being a foreman, especially in a workplace or industrial setting
the position of being a foreman

Examples

He was promoted to foremanship after years of hard work on the factory floor.
Maintaining effective communication is vital in foremanship.
Her strong leadership skills made her an ideal candidate for foremanship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foreman - a person in charge of a group of workers

Etymology

Formed from "foreman" + "-ship," a suffix meaning state, condition, or quality.
