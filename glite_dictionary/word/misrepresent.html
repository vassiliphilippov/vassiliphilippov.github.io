to misrepresent

1. VERB

/ ˌmɪsˌrɛprɪˈzɛnt / (AmE), / ˌmɪsrɛprɪˈzɛnt / (BrE)
to give a false or misleading account or representation of something or someone
to describe falsely or misleadingly

Examples

She accused the newspaper of trying to misrepresent her views on the issue.
The company was fined for misrepresenting the safety of its products.
It is unethical to deliberately misrepresent data in scientific research.

Synset Examples

This statement misrepresents my intentions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to falsify - to alter (information or evidence) so as to mislead
  • Synonym: to distort - to change the shape or meaning of something so that it is no longer accurate
  • Degree Lower: to overstate - to state something in too strong terms
  • Degree Higher: to fabricate - to invent or make up something, typically with deceitful intent

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + represent; first attested in late Middle English.

2. VERB

/ ˌmɪsˌrɛprɪˈzɛnt / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˌrɛprɪˈzɛnt / (BrE)
to deliberately give a false or misleading account of something, especially with the intention to deceive
give a false or misleading account (to deceive)

Examples

The company was accused of trying to misrepresent its financial results to investors.
He chose to misrepresent the facts in order to gain public support.
They deliberately misrepresented the data to make their case seem stronger.

Synset Examples

Fudge the figures
cook the books
falsify the data

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to falsify - to alter information or evidence in order to mislead
  • Synonym: to distort - to change the facts or meaning of something to mislead
  • Antonym: to clarify - to make something clearer or more accurate

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + represent, from Latin repraesentare (to present)
