pyjama

1. NOUN

/ pəˈdʒæmə / (AmE), / pɪˈdʒɑːmə / (BrE)
loose-fitting clothes consisting of a jacket top and trousers, worn for sleeping or lounging, usually in the plural form
loose-fitting sleepwear; jacket and trousers
british

Examples

He changed into his pyjamas before going to bed.
The children wore matching pyjamas at the sleepover.
She bought a new pair of silk pyjamas for the trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pajama - loose-fitting sleepwear; American spelling variant

Etymology

From Hindi *pājāma*, from Persian *pāy-jāmeh* ('leg garment').

2. NOUN

/ pəˈdʒɑmə / (AmE), / pəˈdʒɑːmə / (BrE)
A pair of loose-fitting trousers, usually tied around the waist with a drawstring, traditionally worn by men and women in South and East Asian countries.
loose trousers worn in some Asian countries
british

Examples

He wore a traditional pyjama during the summer festival in Delhi.
The hotel provided guests with a comfortable cotton pyjama for relaxing in the room.
In some regions, a pyjama is considered suitable casual wear at home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pajama - sleepwear consisting of loose trousers and a matching shirt; primarily US spelling

Etymology

From Hindi 'pajāmā', from Persian 'pāy' (leg) + 'jāma' (garment).
