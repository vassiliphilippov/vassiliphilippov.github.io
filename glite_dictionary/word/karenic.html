Karenic

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrɛnɪk / (AmE), / kəˈrɛnɪk / (BrE)
the group of Tibeto-Burman languages spoken by the Karen people, primarily along the Thailand-Myanmar border
a group of Tibeto-Burman languages

Examples

Most speakers of the Karenic languages live in the mountainous regions along the Myanmar-Thailand border.
He is conducting research on the phonological systems of several Karenic dialects.
The cultural traditions of Karenic communities are closely linked to their native languages.

Etymology

From 'Karen' (the ethnic group) + -ic (adjective or group-forming suffix).
