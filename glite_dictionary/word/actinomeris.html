Actinomeris

1. NOUN

/ ˌæktɪˈnɑːmərɪs / (AmE), / ˌæktɪˈnɒmərɪs / (BrE)
a former genus of plants in the family Asteraceae, now usually included in the genus Verbesina.
obsolete plant genus name (Asteraceae)

Examples

The species formerly classified as Actinomeris are now placed in the genus Verbesina.
Some older botanical guides still list Actinomeris as a valid genus.
Researchers compared herbarium specimens labeled as Actinomeris with modern classifications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Verbesina - genus of flowering plants now including Actinomeris species

Etymology

From Greek 'aktis' (ray) + 'meros' (part), referring to radiate flowers.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæktɪˈnɑmərɪs / (AmE), / ˌæktɪˈnɒmərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants, formerly recognized in some botanical classifications and now usually included in the genus Verbesina.
plant genus, now usually in Verbesina

Examples

Several species once classified as Actinomeris are now considered part of Verbesina.
Botanists rarely use the name Actinomeris in modern classifications.
Historical botanical texts sometimes refer to the genus Actinomeris when describing these sunflower relatives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Verbesina - A genus of flowering plants in the aster family, which now includes Actinomeris species

Etymology

From Greek 'aktis' (ray) + 'meros' (part), possibly referring to the radiate arrangement of flower parts.
