Andreaea

1. NOUN

/ ænˈdriːə / (AmE), / ænˈdrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of brown or blackish Alpine mosses with capsules that split open with four longitudinal slits.
genus of Alpine mosses

Examples

The biologist collected samples of Andreaea during his expedition to the Alpine tundra.
Andreaea is easily recognized by its dark color and unusual capsule structure.
Research on Andreaea has expanded our understanding of moss adaptation to harsh, mountainous climates.

Etymology

Named after J. G. Andreae, an 18th-century German botanist.
