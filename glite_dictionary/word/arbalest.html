arbalest

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrbəlɛst / (AmE), / ˈɑːbəlɪst / (BrE)
A type of large, powerful crossbow, especially one with a steel bow, used in medieval Europe.
medieval crossbow with a steel bow

Examples

The museum displayed a medieval arbalest among its collection of ancient weapons.
Knights often used the arbalest during siege warfare for its power and range.
He demonstrated how to load and fire the heavy arbalest at the historical reenactment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crossbow - a weapon for shooting arrows, consisting of a bow mounted on a stock

Etymology

From Old French arbaleste, based on Latin arcuballista (arcus 'bow' + ballista 'missile-throwing engine').
