most-favored-nation

1. ADJ

/ ˌmoʊst ˌfeɪvərd ˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌməʊst ˌfeɪvəd ˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
Designating a status or treatment given to a country by another under a commercial treaty, ensuring it receives the same trading advantages (such as low tariffs or high import quotas) as any other nation.
equal trading rights status in treaties
legal

Examples

The countries signed a most-favored-nation agreement to ensure equal trading opportunities.
Under the most-favored-nation principle, any tariff reduction for one country must apply to all others.
China was granted most-favored-nation status, making its exports more competitive.
