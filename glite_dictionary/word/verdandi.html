Verdandi

1. PROPN

/ vɛrˈdɑːndi / (AmE), / vɜːˈdandi / (BrE)
In Norse mythology, one of the three Norns; the goddess who personifies the present.
Norse goddess personifying the present

Examples

According to Norse legends, Verdandi is one of the Norns who weave the fate of men.
In ancient lore, Verdandi was believed to govern the present, while her sisters ruled past and future.
The statue in the museum depicts Verdandi standing between Urd and Skuld.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Urd - Norse goddess representing the past
  • Synonym: Skuld - Norse goddess representing the future

Etymology

From Old Norse 'Verðandi', meaning 'becoming' or 'present', participle of 'verða' (to become).
