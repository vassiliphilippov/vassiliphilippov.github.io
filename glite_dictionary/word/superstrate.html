superstrate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuː.pərˌstreɪt / (AmE), / ˈsuː.pəˌstreɪt / (BrE)
A language introduced by later invaders or dominant groups that is imposed on an indigenous population and contributes features to their language.
dominant invading language influencing another

Examples

The French language served as a superstrate in the formation of Middle English.
When examining the vocabulary of creole languages, linguists often identify the superstrate influence.
Portuguese functioned as the superstrate in many West African pidgin languages.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: substrate - a language spoken by an indigenous population that is influenced by a dominant invading language

Etymology

From Latin superstratum (something spread or laid over), from super- (over) + stratum (layer).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsuː.pɚˌstreɪt / (AmE), / ˈsuː.pə.streɪt / (BrE)
a stratum or layer that is superimposed on or above another; especially, a language or material layer that overlies another.
layer placed above another layer

Examples

The glass panel acts as a superstrate over the underlying metal sheet.
In linguistics, a superstrate language influences the vocabulary of a community's original language.
Researchers studied the effect of applying a new superstrate to improve durability.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: substrate - a layer or stratum beneath another

Etymology

From Latin superstratum, from super- (over) and stratum (layer).
