kieserite

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaɪ.zəˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈkiː.zəˌraɪt / (BrE)
a white or grayish mineral consisting of hydrous magnesium sulfate, commonly found in salt deposits and used in fertilizers
hydrated magnesium sulfate mineral

Examples

The geologist identified the sample as kieserite after analyzing its composition.
Large deposits of kieserite are often extracted from salt mines for industrial use.
The presence of kieserite on Mars suggests there was once liquid water on the planet.

Etymology

Named after Dietrich Georg Kieser, a German physician and scientist.
