csardas

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑr.dɑs / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑː.dɑːʃ / (BrE)
a traditional Hungarian dance, or a piece of music composed for this dance, typically characterized by a slow introduction followed by a fast section.
Hungarian dance or its music

Examples

The orchestra performed a lively csardas during the festival.
She learned to dance the csardas as part of her Hungarian heritage.
Liszt's famous csardas captivated the audience with its shifting tempos.

Synset Examples

The Csárdás is characterized by a variation in tempo: it starts out slowly (lassú) and ends in a very fast tempo (friss, literally ‘fresh’).

Etymology

From Hungarian 'csárdás', meaning 'folk dance', from 'csárda' (tavern).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑr.dɑs / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑː.dɑːʃ / (BrE)
a piece of music in the rhythm of the traditional Hungarian Csárdás dance, typically marked by a varied tempo and lively character
Hungarian dance music piece

Examples

The violinist performed a beautiful csardas that captivated the entire audience.
Many composers have written pieces inspired by the traditional csardas dance.
The lively rhythm of the csardas made it popular at music festivals.

Synset Examples

Classical composers who have used csárdás themes in their works include Emmerich Kálmán, Franz Liszt, Johannes Brahms, Léo Delibes, Johann Strauss, Pablo de Sarasate, Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky and others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: czardas - alternative spelling of csardas

Etymology

From Hungarian 'csárdás', meaning 'of the tavern', referring to a traditional dance.
