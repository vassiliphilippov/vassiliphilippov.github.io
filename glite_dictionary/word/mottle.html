mottle

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑtəl / (AmE), / ˈmɒtəl / (BrE)
an irregular arrangement of spots or patches of different colors, often forming a pattern on a surface
irregular pattern of colored patches

Examples

The marble floors were decorated with a delicate mottle of white and gray.
The bird's eggs were marked with a brown mottle that helped camouflage them.
Through the trees, sunlight created a mottle of light and shadow on the grass.

Synset Examples

it was not dull grey as distance had suggested, but a mottle of khaki and black and olive-green

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mottling - the process or result of forming mottles; a pattern of spots or blotches
  • Antonym: stain - a colored patch or mark that is difficult to remove

Etymology

From Middle English motil, from motley (variegated in color).

2. VERB

/ ˈmɑt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈmɒtl̩ / (BrE)
to mark with spots or blotches of different colors or shades as if stained
mark with spots or blotches of color

Examples

The artist chose to mottle the background of the painting with shades of blue and green.
Sunlight filtering through the leaves would mottle the forest floor with patches of light and shadow.
Over time, exposure to the weather began to mottle the building’s façade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dapple - to mark with spots or rounded patches of color
  • Synonym: speckle - to cover or mark with many small spots or patches of color

Etymology

Probably from Middle English mot(te) (speck, particle).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈmɑt.əl / (AmE), / ˈmɒt.əl / (BrE)
to mark or colour with streaks, spots, or blotches of different shades or colours
mark with spots or blotches of colour

Examples

The artist used a sponge to mottle the wall with shades of blue and green.
As the leaves aged, their surfaces began to mottle and fade.
The frog's skin is mottled with patches of brown and olive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marble - to give a streaked or patterned appearance, like marble
  • Synonym: speckle - to cover or mark with small spots or patches of color

Etymology

From Middle English motyl, probably a diminutive of mot (speck, spot).
