Phallus

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæləs / (AmE), / ˈfaləs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi characterized by a cap (pileus) that hangs freely around the stem, commonly known as stinkhorns.
genus of fungi with free-hanging cap

Examples

The species Phallus impudicus can often be found in woodlands during the summer.
Researchers are studying the various ecological roles of fungi in the Phallus genus.
The distinctive appearance of Phallus species makes them easy to identify in the field.

Etymology

From Greek phallos (phallus), referring to the characteristic shape of the mature fungi.
