Angiopteris evecta

1. NOUN

/ ˌændʒiɑpˈtɛrɪs ɪˈvɛktə / (AmE), / ˌandʒiɒpˈtɛrɪs ɪˈvɛktə / (BrE)
a species of very large primitive fern native to the tropical Pacific regions, especially in areas with high rainfall; notable for its stout rhizomes and enormous fronds
a large primitive fern species of Pacific tropics

Examples

The lush valleys of Fiji are home to the massive Angiopteris evecta, whose fronds can reach several meters in length.
Botanists were excited to discover a thriving colony of Angiopteris evecta along the rainforest stream.
In tropical gardens, Angiopteris evecta is often planted for its dramatic, architectural foliage.

Etymology

From modern Latin: genus Angiopteris from Greek angeion (vessel) + pteris (fern); species name evecta from Latin ēvectus (carried out, elevated).
