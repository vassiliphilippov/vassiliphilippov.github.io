polybutene

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑliˈbjutin / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈbjuːtiːn / (BrE)
a synthetic polymer made from butylene, used in lubricants, adhesives, and synthetic rubber products
a polymer used in lubricants and rubber

Examples

The manufacturer uses polybutene to improve the viscosity of the lubricant.
A new adhesive formula combines polybutene with other synthetic resins.
Because of its flexibility, polybutene is chosen for certain types of piping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polypropylene - a polymer made from propylene, used in a wide range of applications

Etymology

From 'poly-' (many) and 'butene' (the monomer butylene).
