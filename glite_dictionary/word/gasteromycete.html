gasteromycete

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡæstəroʊˈmaɪsiːt / (AmE), / ˌɡastərəʊˈmʌɪsiːt / (BrE)
Any fungus belonging to the class Gasteromycetes, characterized by spores that develop inside the fruiting body and are released only when mature.
a fungus of the class Gasteromycetes

Examples

Certain puffballs are classic examples of gasteromycete fungi found in temperate forests.
The gasteromycete releases its spores through an opening when the fruiting body matures.
Researchers studied the unique spore dispersal mechanisms of this gasteromycete species.

Etymology

From Greek gaster (stomach) + mycete (fungus), referring to the internal development of spores.
