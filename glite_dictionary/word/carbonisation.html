carbonisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑrbənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɑːbənʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of heating coal in the absence of air to produce coke and other byproducts; destructive distillation of coal.
destructive distillation of coal

Examples

The carbonisation of coal produces coke used in steel manufacturing.
Advances in carbonisation technology have improved energy efficiency in the industry.
During carbonisation, volatile substances are driven off, leaving carbon-rich coke behind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbonization - alternative spelling (American English) of carbonisation

Etymology

From 'carbon' + '-isation', influenced by French 'carbonisation'.
