praise

1. NOUN

/ preɪz / (AmE), / preɪz / (BrE)
words or acts expressing reverence or homage, especially as an act of worship towards a deity
worshipful words or acts

Examples

Every morning, the monks offer praise in the temple.
Her song was a beautiful praise to the divine.
The congregation raised their voices in praise during the ceremony.

Synset Examples

they sang a hymn of praise to God

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worship - show reverence and adoration for a deity

Etymology

From Old French *preise*, from Latin *pretium* (price, value).

WordNet ID: oewn-06706247-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ preɪz / (AmE), / preɪz / (BrE)
An expression of approval or admiration for someone or something.
expression of approval or admiration

Examples

The teacher gave praise to the students for their hard work.
Public praise can be a great motivator for employees.
She blushed at the unexpected praise from her colleagues.

Synset Examples

he always appreciated praise for his work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commendation - formal or official praise or approval
  • Antonym: criticism - the expression of disapproval of someone or something

Etymology

From Old French preisier (to value) and Latin pretium (price, value).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06705943-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ preɪz / (AmE), / preɪz / (BrE)
to express approval or admiration of someone or something
express approval or admiration

Examples

The manager openly praised her team for their dedication to the project.
He was praised for his honesty during the interview.
Teachers often praise students when they show improvement or effort.

Synset Examples

The parents praised their children for their academic performance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to commend - to praise formally or officially
  • Antonym: to criticize - to express disapproval or find fault

Etymology

From Old French *preisier* (to prize, value), from Latin *pretiare* (to value).
