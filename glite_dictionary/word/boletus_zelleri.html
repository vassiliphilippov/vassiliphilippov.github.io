Boletus zelleri

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈliːtəs ˈzɛlɚi / (AmE), / bəʊˈliːtəs ˈzɛləri / (BrE)
A species of edible mushroom with a brown cap, greenish-yellow under surface, and a stalk that turns dull red with age.
a species of edible mushroom

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with patches of Boletus zelleri, prized by local foragers.
Many chefs seek out Boletus zelleri for its unique flavor and firm texture in dishes.
With its distinctive coloring, Boletus zelleri is relatively easy for mushroom hunters to identify.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boletus - a genus of mushrooms with pores instead of gills

Etymology

Named after mycologist Sanford Myron Zeller; 'Boletus' from Latin, meaning 'mushroom'.
