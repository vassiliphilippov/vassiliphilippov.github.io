Acris crepitans

1. NOUN

/ ˈækrɪs krəˈpaɪtənz / (AmE), / ˈækrɪs krɛˈpɪtænz / (BrE)
A species of small North American frog, commonly known as the northern cricket frog, found in eastern and central United States.
a species of cricket frog native to North America

Examples

The distinctive clicking call of the Acris crepitans can be heard near ponds and streams in summer.
Biologists studied the population trends of Acris crepitans in several wetland habitats.
Because of its camouflage, Acris crepitans often goes unnoticed by predators and observers alike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cricket frog - small frogs of the genus Acris, known for their clicking calls

Etymology

From New Latin; Acris is a genus name, crepitans is Latin for 'crackling' or 'crepitating'.
