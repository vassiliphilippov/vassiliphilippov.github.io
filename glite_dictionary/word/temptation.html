temptation

1. NOUN

/ tɛmˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / tɛmpˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the desire to do something, especially something wrong or unwise; something that entices or allures someone to act, often against their better judgment
something that entices or allures

Examples

The chocolate cake on the table was a strong temptation for anyone trying to diet.
Despite the temptation to skip class, she decided to go anyway.
He found it hard to resist the temptation to check his phone during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allurement - something that attracts or tempts
  • Antonym: resistance - the act of opposing or withholding from temptation

Etymology

From Latin temptatio (an attempt, trial, temptation), from temptare (to handle, test, try).

2. NOUN

/ tɛmˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / tɛmpˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of influencing someone by exciting their hope or desire, especially to do something wrong or unwise
act of influencing by exciting desire

Examples

The temptation to skip work and go to the beach was almost too strong to resist.
She used every temptation she could think of to convince him to stay.
Despite the temptation of quick money, he refused to break the law.

Synset Examples

his enticements were shameless

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enticement - the act of attracting someone to do something, especially by offering pleasure or advantage
  • Antonym: discouragement - the act of persuading someone not to do something

Etymology

From Latin temptatio (a testing, trial; an enticement), from temptare (to test, try, attempt).

3. NOUN

/ tɛmˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / tɛmpˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
A strong desire to do or have something, especially something that is wrong, bad, or unwise.
desire to do something wrong or unwise

Examples

The temptation to eat another piece of cake was almost impossible to resist.
She tried to avoid temptation while shopping by not walking down the candy aisle.
He struggled with temptation every time he passed the bar on his way home.

Synset Examples

he felt the temptation and his will power weakened

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allure - the quality of being powerfully attractive or fascinating
  • Antonym: resistance - refusal to accept or comply with something

Etymology

From Latin temptatio (a testing, temptation), from temptare (to test, try).
