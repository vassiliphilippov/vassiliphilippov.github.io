discard

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪsˌkɑrd / (AmE), / dɪsˈkɑːd / (BrE)
an act of playing or removing a card from one's hand, especially in a card game when unable or unwilling to follow the suit led
the act of discarding a card in a card game

Examples

He made a clever discard that changed the course of the game.
Players debated whether his last discard was a mistake.
A strategic discard can sometimes give away information to opponents.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: draw - an act of taking a card from the deck in a card game

Etymology

From Latin 'discordare' (to throw away), later influenced by 'discard' (verb).

2. NOUN

/ dɪsˈkɑrd / (AmE), / dɪsˈkɑːd / (BrE)
an object or item that has been thrown away or rejected as no longer useful
something thrown away

Examples

The fisherman sorted through the discard to check if anything valuable had been thrown away.
She picked up a discard from the pile to use in her art project.
The recycling center collects every discard left on the curb.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reject - an object that has been refused or not accepted

Etymology

From the verb 'discard', which comes from Middle French 'discarder', from Italian 'scartare', meaning 'to reject'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɪs.kɑrd / (AmE), / ˈdɪs.kɑːd / (BrE)
An item or object that has been thrown away because it is no longer wanted or needed.
something thrown away or rejected

Examples

After sorting through her closet, she put all the discards into a donation box.
In poker, the player added his discard to the pile before drawing a new card.
The recycling bin was filled with old magazines and other discards from the office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reject - something or someone that has been rejected or not accepted
  • Antonym: keep - to retain something rather than get rid of it

Etymology

From the verb 'discard', from Old French 'des-' (expressing reversal) + 'carder' (to throw away cards).

4. VERB

/ dɪsˈkɑrd / (AmE), / dɪsˈkɑːd / (BrE)
to throw something away or get rid of it because it is no longer wanted or needed
throw away; get rid of

Examples

She discarded the old papers she no longer needed.
After the accident, he had to discard his damaged bicycle helmet.
You should discard all food past its expiration date.

Synset Examples

Put away your worries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dispose - to get rid of something, especially by throwing it away
  • Antonym: keep - to retain possession of

Etymology

From Middle French 'descarder', from Old Italian 'scartare' (to remove from a pack of cards), from 'scarto' (scrap).
