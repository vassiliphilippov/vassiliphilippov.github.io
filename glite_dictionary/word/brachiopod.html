brachiopod

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrækiəˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈbrækɪəˌpɒd / (BrE)
a marine animal with a bivalve shell and a pair of arms bearing tentacles used for capturing food, belonging to the phylum Brachiopoda
marine animal with bivalve shell

Examples

Fossils of brachiopods are common in Paleozoic rocks around the world.
Unlike bivalve mollusks, brachiopods have a different internal anatomy and symmetry.
Marine biologists discovered a new species of brachiopod off the South American coast.

Etymology

From Greek 'brakhion' (arm) + 'pous, podos' (foot).

2. ADJ

/ ˈbrækiəˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈbrækɪəˌpɒd / (BrE)
of or relating to the phylum Brachiopoda, a group of marine invertebrate animals with bivalve shells.
relating to the Brachiopoda phylum

Examples

The brachiopod fossil record provides valuable insights into ancient marine environments.
This researcher specializes in studying brachiopod anatomy.
Many brachiopod species became extinct at the end of the Permian period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brachiopod - a marine invertebrate animal with bivalve shell (noun)

Etymology

From brachio- (relating to arms) + -pod (foot), referring to the animal's lophophore (feeding organ).
