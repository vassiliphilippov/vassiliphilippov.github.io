subdivider

1. NOUN

/ sʌb.dɪˌvaɪ.dɚ / (AmE), / sʌb.dɪˌvaɪ.də / (BrE)
a person or company that divides something, especially land, into smaller parts or sections
person or company dividing land

Examples

The city required each subdivider to provide public parks in their plans.
After purchasing the farmland, the subdivider parceled it into dozens of lots.
Zoning regulations set strict limits on what a subdivider can do with the property.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: developer - a person or company that creates new buildings on land
  • Degree Lower: landowner - a person who owns land

Etymology

Formed from 'subdivide' + '-er', where 'subdivide' is from Latin 'sub-' (under, below) and 'dividere' (to divide).
