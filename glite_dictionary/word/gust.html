gust

1. NOUN

/ ɡʌst / (AmE), / ɡʌst / (BrE)
a sudden, strong burst of wind or air
sudden strong burst of wind

Examples

A sudden gust slammed the door shut.
She pulled her coat tighter as a chilly gust swept across the street.
The gust of wind scattered the papers everywhere.

Synset Examples

the tree was bent almost double by the gust

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: breeze - a gentle or light wind
  • Degree Higher: gale - a very strong wind

Etymology

From Old Norse 'gustr' meaning 'a cold blast of wind'.
