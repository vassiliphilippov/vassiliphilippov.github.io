ignominiousness

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪɡ.nəˈmɪn.i.əs.nəs / (AmE), / ˌɪɡ.nəˈmɪn.i.əs.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of deserving or causing public disgrace or shame.
public disgrace; shameful state

Examples

The ignominiousness of his actions led to his resignation from public office.
She felt the ignominiousness of being exposed as a fraud.
History books record the ignominiousness with which the traitors were treated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ignominy - public shame or disgrace
  • Synonym: shame - a painful feeling of humiliation or distress

Etymology

From Latin 'ignominiosus' (disgraceful), from 'ignominia' (disgrace, shame).
