Aleurites fordii

1. NOUN

/ əˈlʊr.əˌtiːz ˈfɔr.diː / (AmE), / alˈjʊə.raɪtiːz ˈfɔː.diː / (BrE)
A Chinese tree that produces seeds yielding tung oil, widely used in varnishes and paints.
Chinese tree producing tung oil seeds

Examples

The seeds of the Aleurites fordii are pressed to produce tung oil.
Farmers cultivated Aleurites fordii extensively for its valuable oil.
Aleurites fordii has become a significant crop in several subtropical regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tung tree - tree producing tung oil (vernacular name for Aleurites fordii)

Etymology

From the genus name Aleurites (Greek aleuron, 'flour', referring to the mealy coating on seeds) and the species named after C.H. Ford.
