miss

1. VERB

/ mɪs / (AmE), / mɪs / (BrE)
to feel the absence of someone or something and wish they were present
feel the absence of; long for

Examples

Since her best friend moved away, she really misses having someone to talk to every day.
It's been months since I visited home, and I miss my family a lot.
I didn't realize how much I'd miss city life until I moved to the countryside.

Synset Examples

He misses his mother

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long for - to want or yearn for something or someone very much

Etymology

From Old English 'missan', meaning 'to fail to hit, miss, or lack'.
