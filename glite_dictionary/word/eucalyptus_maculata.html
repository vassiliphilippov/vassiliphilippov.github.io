Eucalyptus maculata

1. NOUN

/ juːˌkliːptəs ˌmækjuˈleɪtə / (AmE), / juːˌklɪptəs ˌmækjʊˈleɪtə / (BrE)
A large Australian gum tree with distinctive mottled bark, commonly known as the spotted gum.
Australian gum tree with mottled bark

Examples

The durable timber of the Eucalyptus maculata is highly valued in construction.
We walked beneath the towering Eucalyptus maculata lining the Australian coastline.
The characteristic bark of Eucalyptus maculata gives it the name 'spotted gum'.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotted gum - Common name for Eucalyptus maculata, an Australian tree with spotted bark

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name Eucalyptus (from Greek 'eu' meaning well and 'kalyptos' meaning covered, referring to the cap on its flower buds) and species maculata meaning 'spotted'.
