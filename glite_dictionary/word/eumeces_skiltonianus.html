Eumeces skiltonianus

1. NOUN

/ juˈmiːsiːz skɪlˈtoʊniˌænəs / (AmE), / juːˈmiːsiːz skɪlˈtəʊniˌænəs / (BrE)
A species of small skink commonly known as the western skink, found in grasslands and open woodlands of western North America.
species of skink (western North America)

Examples

The Eumeces skiltonianus often basks on rocks to regulate its body temperature.
Researchers observed the behavior of Eumeces skiltonianus in several California woodlands.
The distinctive blue tail is a well-known feature of juvenile Eumeces skiltonianus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skink - a type of smooth-scaled lizard

Etymology

From genus Eumeces (Greek eu- 'good' + mēkēs 'length'), with species named after Joseph Skilton, 19th-century American biologist.
