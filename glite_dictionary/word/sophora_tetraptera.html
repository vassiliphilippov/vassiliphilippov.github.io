Sophora tetraptera

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈfɔːrə tɛˈtræptərə / (AmE), / səˈfɔːrə tɛˈtræptərə / (BrE)
A species of shrub or small tree native to New Zealand and Chile, known for its drooping clusters of tubular golden-yellow flowers and hard, strong wood.
a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand and Chile

Examples

The Sophora tetraptera in the botanical garden was in full bloom, attracting many visitors with its yellow flowers.
Carpenters in the region sometimes use Sophora tetraptera wood because of its hardness and durability.
Conservationists are studying the distribution of Sophora tetraptera to better protect its natural habitats.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; 'Sophora' is a genus name, and 'tetraptera' means 'four-winged' in Greek, referring to the seeds.
