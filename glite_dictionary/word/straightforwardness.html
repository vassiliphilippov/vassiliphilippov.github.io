straightforwardness

1. NOUN

/ streɪtˈfɔrwərdnəs / (AmE), / streɪtˈfɔːwədnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being direct, honest, and without unnecessary complexity or ambiguity
quality of being direct and honest

Examples

Her straightforwardness made her a trusted leader among her colleagues.
Some people mistake straightforwardness for bluntness or lack of tact.
He admired her straightforwardness when discussing difficult topics.

Synset Examples

what some people take for rudeness is really straightforwardness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candor - the quality of being open and honest
  • Antonym: indirectness - the quality of being not direct or straightforward

Etymology

From 'straightforward' (direct, honest) + '-ness' (noun-forming suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌstreɪtˈfɔrwərdnəs / (AmE), / ˌstreɪtˈfɔːwədnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being honest and direct; not hiding one's intentions or feelings
quality of being honest and direct

Examples

Her straightforwardness made people trust her immediately.
He appreciated the straightforwardness of her answer to the difficult question.
The manager's straightforwardness in dealing with problems impressed the whole team.

Synset Examples

the singleness of his motives could not be questioned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and sincere
  • Antonym: deceit - the act of hiding the truth, especially to get an advantage

Etymology

From 'straightforward' + '-ness' (forming nouns of quality or state).
