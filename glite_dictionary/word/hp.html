H.P.

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪtʃ ˈpi / (AmE), / ˌeɪtʃ ˈpiː / (BrE)
abbreviation for 'horsepower', a unit of power equal to 746 watts, commonly used to measure the strength of engines.
abbreviation for horsepower (746 watts)

Examples

The engine is rated at 200 H.P. for maximum performance.
This generator delivers 50 H.P. of continuous power.
Most modern cars use metrics, but some still list the output in H.P..

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horsepower - unit of power equal to 746 watts, used for engines

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'horsepower', a term coined by engineer James Watt in the late 18th century.
