HP

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪtʃ ˈpi / (AmE), / ˌeɪtʃ ˈpiː / (BrE)
A unit of power equal to 746 watts, commonly used for measuring the power output of engines and motors.
unit of power (horsepower); 746 watts

Examples

The car's engine produces 400 HP, making it very powerful.
This electric motor is rated at 2 HP.
Many lawn mowers operate with an engine of less than 5 HP.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horsepower - unit of power equal to 746 watts

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'horsepower', from horse + power.
