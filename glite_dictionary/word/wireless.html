wireless

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪərləs / (AmE), / ˈwaɪələs / (BrE)
A system or medium of communicating or transmitting signals without the use of wires, typically by means of radio waves or other electromagnetic methods.
system for transmitting signals without wires

Examples

They installed a new wireless in the office to improve communication.
Before television, families listened to the news on the wireless every evening.
The museum displays an early wireless used for ship-to-shore communication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radio - system for sending audio or signals using electromagnetic waves

Etymology

Formed from 'wire' + '-less'; first used in late 19th century for radio communication.

4. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪərləs / (AmE), / ˈwaɪəlɪs / (BrE)
a system of communicating by sending and receiving electromagnetic waves, especially radio waves, without the use of wires or cables
radio-based communication system
british

Examples

Many homes in the 1930s had a wireless in the living room for news and music.
She prefers listening to concerts on the wireless rather than watching TV.
The invention of the wireless revolutionized global communication in the twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radio - a system of sending and receiving signals by electromagnetic waves

Etymology

From 'wire' + '-less', meaning 'without wires'; used since the late 19th century to describe early radio technology.

/ ˈwaɪr.ləs / (AmE), / ˈwaɪə.ləs / (BrE)
a radio receiver for detecting and amplifying transmitted signals, especially used in reference to early radio sets
radio receiver (especially old-fashioned term)
british archaic

Examples
