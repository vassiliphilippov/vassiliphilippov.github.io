heterodoxy

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛtəroʊˈdɑksi / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəˈdɒksi / (BrE)
The quality or state of having opinions, beliefs, or practices that differ from accepted or orthodox standards or doctrines.
unorthodoxy; unorthodox belief or practice

Examples

The professor's heterodoxy made her lectures both controversial and fascinating.
Throughout history, heterodoxy has often been met with suspicion by the authorities.
He was proud of his heterodoxy in an age of conformity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orthodoxy - the quality of conforming to established or traditional beliefs

Etymology

From Greek heterodoxos (of another opinion), from heteros (other) + doxa (opinion).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɛt̬əroʊˌdɑksi / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəʊˌdɒksi / (BrE)
opinions or doctrines that differ from the official or orthodox position, especially in religion or philosophy
beliefs differing from orthodoxy

Examples

His heterodoxy made him a controversial figure among traditional scholars.
The church was known for rejecting heterodoxy and enforcing strict doctrine.
Intellectual heterodoxy is often encouraged in academic debates.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orthodoxy - accepted or traditional beliefs, especially in religion or ideology
  • Synonym: heresy - a belief or opinion that is contrary to orthodox religious doctrine

Etymology

From Greek 'heterodoksia', from 'heteros' (other) + 'doxa' (opinion).
