vers libre

1. NOUN

/ vɛr ˈliːbrə / (AmE), / vɛː ˈliːbrə / (BrE)
Poetry written in unrhymed lines without a regular meter or fixed rhythmic pattern.
unrhymed, non-metrical poetry

Examples

Her poems, written in vers libre, break away from the constraints of traditional forms.
Modernist poets often favored vers libre to express their ideas more freely.
The collection is notable for its deft use of vers libre and vivid imagery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: free verse - poetry without regular rhyme or meter

Etymology

From French 'vers libre' (free verse).
