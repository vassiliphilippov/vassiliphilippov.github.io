screener

1. NOUN

/ ˈskriːnər / (AmE), / ˈskriːnə / (BrE)
a person, especially at an airport, whose job is to check passengers or their luggage at a security checkpoint for prohibited items
person checking passengers or luggage at checkpoints

Examples

The screener at the airport asked me to remove my shoes before passing through security.
After the new regulations, every screener received additional training on identifying prohibited items.
A job as a screener requires attention to detail and a calm demeanor under pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inspector - an official who examines things for compliance or safety
  • Synonym: security guard - person who protects a place and checks for security risks

Etymology

From 'screen' (to examine or filter) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
