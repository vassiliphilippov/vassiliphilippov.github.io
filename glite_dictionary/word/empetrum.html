Empetrum

1. NOUN

/ ɛmˈpiːtrəm / (AmE), / ɛmˈpiːtrəm / (BrE)
A genus of low evergreen shrubs known as crowberries, bearing small black or red edible berries, native to temperate and arctic regions.
a genus of shrubs; crowberries

Examples

The arctic tundra is home to Empetrum, which produces berries eaten by local wildlife.
Botanists studied the distribution of Empetrum in northern Europe.
We found several patches of Empetrum along the windswept coastline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crowberry - a small black or red edible berry from Empetrum shrubs

Etymology

From Ancient Greek 'empetron' (a plant growing among rocks).

2. NOUN

/ ɛmˈpiːtrəm / (AmE), / ɛmˈpiːtrəm / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen, berry-producing shrubs commonly known as crowberries, native to subarctic and temperate regions.
genus of evergreen shrubs (crowberries)

Examples

The Empetrum genus thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic soils of cold northern climates.
Researchers studied the distribution of Empetrum species across the Arctic tundra.
Gardeners often use Empetrum as ground cover due to its hardy nature and attractive berries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crowberry - the common name for the fruit of Empetrum species

Etymology

From Ancient Greek *empetron*, meaning 'growing on rocks'.
