petechia

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtiːkiə / (AmE), / pɪˈtiːkɪə / (BrE)
A small red or purple spot that appears on the skin as a result of minor bleeding from broken capillary blood vessels.
small skin spot from minor bleeding
medical

Examples

The doctor noted several petechiae on the patient's lower legs during the examination.
Unexplained petechiae can be a sign of a serious underlying condition that requires medical attention.
After her high fever, small petechiae developed on her arms and torso.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: purpura - large area of purple discoloration caused by bleeding under the skin

Etymology

From New Latin, from Italian *petecchia* (freckle, eruption), from Latin *petigo* (scab, eruption).
