bight

1. NOUN

/ baɪt / (AmE), / baɪt / (BrE)
A loop or bend in a rope, especially one that does not cross itself.
loop or bend in a rope

Examples

To tie a bowline, first form a bight in the rope.
The sailor threw a bight over the post to secure the vessel.
Always check the bight for twists before you finish your knot.

Etymology

Old English 'byht', meaning a bend or angle.

2. NOUN

/ baɪt / (AmE), / baɪt / (BrE)
the middle part of a rope, especially when it is slack, not including the ends
middle section of a slack rope

Examples

To form a knot, take the bight of the rope and fold it over itself.
He pulled the anchor using the bight rather than the rope's end.
Be careful not to stand in the bight when winching, as it can tighten suddenly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loop - a curved shape made when a rope or line doubles back on itself

Etymology

From Old English 'byht' (a bend, angle, or corner).

3. NOUN

/ baɪt / (AmE), / baɪt / (BrE)
a bend or curve in a geographical feature, especially a coastline, forming a large open bay
a widespread curve in a coastline or river

Examples

The sailors navigated their way around the bight to avoid the rocky shoreline.
Several small villages are nestled along the bight of the coast, sheltered from the wind.
A broad bight in the river created a calm harbor for fishing boats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bay - a broad inlet of the sea where the land curves inward
  • Degree Lower: cove - a small indentation or recess in the shoreline

Etymology

From Old English 'byht' (a bend, angle), related to 'bēogan' (to bend).

5. VERB

/ baɪt / (AmE), / baɪt / (BrE)
To fasten or secure (something) by making a loop or bend in a rope or similar material.
fasten by making a loop (knotting term)

Examples

The sailor expertly bighted the rope to secure the sail to the deck.
Before pulling tight, make sure you bight the line properly through the ring.
She bighted the cord around the pole, preparing it for the next stage of the knot.

Etymology

From Old English *byht* (a bend, bow).
