Parkinson's

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrkɪnsənz / (AmE), / ˈpɑːkɪnsənz / (BrE)
A progressive neurological disorder of the central nervous system characterized by tremor, muscle rigidity, and impaired movement, usually referring to Parkinson's disease.
degenerative neurological disorder (Parkinson's disease)
medical

Examples

Her grandfather was diagnosed with Parkinson's five years ago.
Parkinson's affects millions of people worldwide, often causing shaking and difficulty with movement.
New treatments for Parkinson's are being developed to help manage its symptoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Parkinson's disease - A degenerative disorder of the central nervous system causing tremor and muscle rigidity

Etymology

Named after English physician James Parkinson (1755–1824), who first described the disease.
