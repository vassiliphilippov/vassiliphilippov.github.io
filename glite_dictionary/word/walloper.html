walloper

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑl.ə.pər / (AmE), / ˈwɒl.ə.pə / (BrE)
A person or team that wins by a large margin; a decisive winner.
decisive winner by a large margin

Examples

After the final round, he emerged as the clear walloper of the competition.
The team was a real walloper this season, crushing all their opponents.
No one expected her to be such a walloper in the marathon, but she finished minutes ahead of everyone else.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winner - someone who wins

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɑl.ə.pɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɒl.ə.pə / (BrE)
an obvious or outrageous lie; a brazen untruth
obvious or outrageous lie
informal

Examples

He told us a complete walloper about why he was late.
Everyone knew that story was a total walloper.
Don't believe him—he always comes up with a walloper when he gets in trouble.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whopper - a gross or blatant lie

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɑl.ə.pɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɒl.ə.pə / (BrE)
a person who hits with great force or strength, especially in sports or fighting
a very hard hitter
informal

Examples

The boxer was known as a walloper in the ring for his powerful punches.
You don't want to mess with him; he's a real walloper on the field.
The new player is a walloper and can change the game's momentum with one swing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slugger - a person who hits hard, especially in baseball or boxing
  • Antonym: lightweight - a person with less strength or power (opposite in context of hitting hard)

Etymology

From 'wallop' (to strike with force) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
