Johannes Kepler

1. PROPN

/ joʊˈhɑːnəs ˈkɛplər / (AmE), / jəʊˈhɑːnəs ˈkɛplə / (BrE)
(1571–1630) a German astronomer who formulated the laws of planetary motion, making significant contributions to the scientific revolution.
German astronomer, formulated laws of planetary motion

Examples

Johannes Kepler was instrumental in advancing our understanding of the solar system.
Astronomers often refer to Johannes Kepler's laws when describing planetary orbits.
Many consider Johannes Kepler one of the founders of modern astronomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Copernicus - Polish astronomer who proposed the heliocentric model
  • Synonym: Isaac Newton - English physicist and mathematician who built on Kepler's work
