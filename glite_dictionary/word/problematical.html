problematical

1. ADJ

/ ˌprɑːbləˈmætɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌprɒblɪˈmætɪkəl / (BrE)
Open to doubt or debate; not certain or settled.
open to doubt or debate

Examples

The effectiveness of the new policy remains problematical until more data are collected.
His explanation for the error was highly problematical and failed to convince the jury.
Whether the company can recover from its losses is problematical at this point.

Synset Examples

If you ever get married, which seems to be extremely problematic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: problematic - open to question or doubt; uncertain

Etymology

From late Latin problematicus, from Greek problematikos (pertaining to a problem).

2. ADJ

/ ˌprɑː.bləˈmæt.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌprɒb.ləˈmæt.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
difficult to understand, solve, or deal with; posing problems or complications.
difficult or complicated; posing problems

Examples

The new policy is problematical because it raises more questions than it answers.
Their plan seemed sound at first, but it soon became problematical in practice.
The evidence for his theory remains problematical despite extensive research.

Synset Examples

a baffling problem
I faced the knotty problem of what to have for breakfast
a problematic situation at home

Related Terms

  • Synonym: problematic - posing a problem or difficulty
  • Antonym: straightforward - easy to understand or do

Etymology

From late Latin problematicalis, from Greek problēmatikos, from problēma (problem).
