chalybite

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælɪˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈkælɪˌbaɪt / (BrE)
an iron ore consisting of ferrous carbonate, also known as siderite
iron ore; ferrous carbonate (mineral)

Examples

The sample contained several minerals, including chalybite and quartz.
Geologists identified chalybite as the primary iron ore in the region.
The museum's mineral collection features a rare specimen of chalybite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: siderite - an iron carbonate mineral; another name for chalybite

Etymology

From Latin chalybs (steel), from Greek khalups (steel), referencing the Chalybes, an ancient people known for their ironworking.
