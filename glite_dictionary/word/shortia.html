shortia

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɔrtiə / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːtiə / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen perennial herbs with smooth, leathery basal leaves and showy, solitary white flowers, found chiefly in eastern Asia and eastern North America.
genus of evergreen perennial herbs

Examples

The rare shortia bloomed along the forest trail, delighting botanists with its delicate white flower.
Many gardeners prize shortia for its attractive evergreen foliage and winter hardiness.
The shortia species are often found in shaded, mountainous woodlands.

Etymology

Named after Dr. Charles Wilkins Short, an American botanist (1794–1863).
