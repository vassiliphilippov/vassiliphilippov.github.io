Hydrobates

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdrɑːbeɪtiːz / (AmE), / hʌɪˈdrəʊbeɪtiːz / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Hydrobatidae, comprising storm petrels, small seabirds found mainly at sea.
genus of storm petrels (seabirds)

Examples

The genus Hydrobates includes several species of storm petrels.
Researchers observed a colony of Hydrobates nesting on a remote island.
Many birdwatchers hope to spot a Hydrobates during ocean voyages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hydrobatidae - the family of small seabirds including storm petrels

Etymology

From Ancient Greek 'hydro' (water) + 'bates' (walker), referring to the bird's habit of 'walking' on water.
