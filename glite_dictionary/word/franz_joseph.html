Franz Joseph

1. PROPN

/ fræns ˈjoʊzəf / (AmE), / frants ˈjəʊzɪf / (BrE)
(1830–1916) Emperor of Austria and king of Hungary, who reigned from 1848 to 1916 and was a key figure in the Austro-Hungarian Empire.
Austrian emperor and Hungarian king (1830–1916)

Examples

Franz Joseph reigned for nearly 68 years, making him one of Europe's longest-serving monarchs.
The Austro-Hungarian Empire reached its zenith under Franz Joseph's rule.
The assassination of Franz Joseph's nephew, Archduke Franz Ferdinand, sparked World War I.
