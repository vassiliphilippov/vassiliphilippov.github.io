tuition

1. NOUN

/ tuˈɪʃən / (AmE), / tjuˈɪʃən / (BrE)
the money that you pay for being taught, especially at a college or university
fee for instruction, esp. college or university

Examples

The tuition at private universities can be very expensive.
Many students rely on scholarships to help pay their tuition.
He worked part-time to cover his college tuition.

Synset Examples

tuition and room and board were more than $25,000

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fee - an amount of money paid for a service

Etymology

From Latin 'tuitionem' (a looking after, guardianship), from 'tueri' (to look after).

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ tuˈɪʃən / (AmE), / tjuˈɪʃən / (BrE)
Private instruction, especially individual teaching given by a tutor.
private or individual teaching

Examples

She received weekly tuition in mathematics to prepare for her exams.
After struggling in French, he sought private tuition to improve his skills.
The school offers personalized tuition outside regular class hours for students who need extra help.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tutoring - the act of giving private instruction to a single student or small group

Etymology

From Latin 'tuitionem' (a looking after, guardianship), from 'tueri' (to watch, protect).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
