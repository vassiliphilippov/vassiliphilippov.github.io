Madia elegans

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ.di.ə ɛˈlɛɡ.ənz / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.di.ə ɛˈlɛɡ.ənz / (BrE)
A species of annual plant in the daisy family, native to California, with yellow flowers marked with red-brown spots near the base of the rays.
California annual plant with yellow, spotted flowers

Examples

Madia elegans blooms in midsummer, brightening dry meadows with its distinct yellow flowers.
Botanists frequently study Madia elegans for its adaptation to California's climate.
The garden featured several native species, including Madia elegans among the wildflowers.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Madia' is the genus name, and 'elegans' means 'elegant' or 'graceful'.
