hemophiliac

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːməˈfɪliˌæk / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˈfɪliːæk / (BrE)
a person who has hemophilia and is prone to uncontrollable bleeding due to an inability of the blood to clot properly
person with hemophilia
medical

Examples

The foundation offers support to every hemophiliac and their families.
As a hemophiliac, he had to be very careful to avoid injuries.
Blood transfusions are sometimes necessary for a hemophiliac after minor surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemophilia - a medical condition in which the blood does not clot properly

Etymology

Formed from 'hemophilia' (from Greek haima 'blood' + philia 'affection') + suffix -ac (denoting a person affected by).
