Lotus americanus

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊtəs əˌmɛrɪˈkænəs / (AmE), / ˈləʊtəs əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnəs / (BrE)
A North American annual plant with red or rose-colored flowers, classified in the legume family.
North American annual plant with red or rose flowers

Examples

The botanical garden recently added Lotus americanus to its wildflower collection.
We spotted several Lotus americanus plants blooming by the riverbank in late spring.
Researchers are studying the ecological role of Lotus americanus in native prairie habitats.

Etymology

From Latin: 'lotus' (a type of plant) + 'americanus' (of America).
