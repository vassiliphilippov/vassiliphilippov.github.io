Genevan

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈnivən / (AmE), / dʒɪˈniːvən / (BrE)
a native or resident of Geneva, Switzerland.
native or resident of Geneva

Examples

As a proud Genevan, she often spoke about the city's history.
The conference included several Genevans among the international delegates.
He considers himself a true Genevan despite moving away years ago.

Etymology

From Geneva, the name of the Swiss city.

2. NOUN

/ dʒəˈnivən / (AmE), / dʒɪˈniːvən / (BrE)
An adherent of the theological doctrines of John Calvin, especially as practiced in Geneva during the Reformation.
follower of Calvinist doctrine

Examples

The Genevan approach to church governance was influential throughout Protestant Europe.
As a devoted Genevan, he embraced Calvin's teachings with great enthusiasm.
The council was dominated by Genevans seeking religious reform in the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Calvinist - an adherent of the religious doctrines of John Calvin

Etymology

From Geneva, the Swiss city where John Calvin led the Protestant Reformation.
