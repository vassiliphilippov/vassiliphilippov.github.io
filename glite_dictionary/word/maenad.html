maenad

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪnæd / (AmE), / ˈmiːnad / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a female follower of Dionysus, often depicted as frenzied or ecstatic and participating in orgiastic rites.
female follower of Dionysus in Greek myth

Examples

The maenads danced wildly in the forest to honor Dionysus.
Euripides' play 'The Bacchae' depicts the frenzied state of the maenads.
Paintings of maenads show women in ecstatic celebration, often carrying thyrsi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacchante - a female follower of Bacchus/Dionysus in Roman myth

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Maenás, Maenádos (μαινάς, μαινάδος), meaning 'mad woman'; from mainomai 'to rage, to be mad'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪnæd / (AmE), / ˈmiːnad / (BrE)
A woman who behaves in an unnaturally frenzied or ecstatic way, especially as depicted in ancient Greek rites.
frenzied or ecstatic woman (Greek myth)

Examples

She danced through the forest like a maenad, wild and untamed.
The artist painted her as a modern maenad, eyes blazing with passion.
Critics described her performance as that of a true maenad, full of ecstatic energy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacchante - A female follower of Bacchus; a frenzied or ecstatic woman

Etymology

From Greek Mainás, Mainádos, meaning 'raving one', follower of Dionysus.
