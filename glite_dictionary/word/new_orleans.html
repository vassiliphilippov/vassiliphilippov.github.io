New Orleans

1. PROPN

/ nuː ɔːrˈliːnz / (AmE), / njuː ɔːˈliːənz / (BrE)
a major port city in southeastern Louisiana, USA, known for its cultural heritage, including jazz music and the annual Mardi Gras festival
city in Louisiana, USA
american

Examples

Many tourists visit New Orleans to experience its famous Mardi Gras celebrations.
Jazz music has deep roots in New Orleans, where it first developed in the late 19th century.
After Hurricane Katrina, New Orleans underwent major reconstruction efforts.

Etymology

Named after Philippe II, Duke of Orléans, Regent of France.
