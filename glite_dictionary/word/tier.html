tier

1. NOUN

/ tɪr / (AmE), / tɪə / (BrE)
one of a series of rows, levels, or layers placed one above another; a stratum within a structure or arrangement
a level or layer in a stack

Examples

The stadium was built with three tiers of seating for spectators.
A wedding cake often has several tiers decorated with icing and flowers.
They sat in the upper tier of the theater to get a better view of the stage.

Synset Examples

tier upon tier of huge casks
a three-tier wedding cake

Etymology

From Old French 'tire' (rank, sequence, order), of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ tɪr / (AmE), / tɪə / (BrE)
a level, rank, or position in a graded group or system, indicating relative status or value
a rank or level in a system

Examples

She has reached the highest tier of management at her company.
Second-tier teams rarely get as much media attention as the top contenders.
Admission to first-tier universities is highly competitive.

Synset Examples

lumber of the highest grade

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rank - a position in a hierarchy or scale
  • Synonym: grade - a particular level of quality or position

Etymology

From Old French *tire* (rank, order, sequence).
