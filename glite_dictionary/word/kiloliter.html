kiloliter

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪləˌliːtər / (AmE), / ˈkɪləˌliːtə / (BrE)
a metric unit of volume equal to 1,000 liters or approximately 264.17 gallons
a metric unit of 1,000 liters

Examples

The tanker can carry up to 10 kiloliters of water.
They measured the pool's volume as nearly eight kiloliters.
One kiloliter is enough to supply several households with water for a day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liter - the basic metric unit of volume

Etymology

From French 'kilolitre', from 'kilo-' (thousand) + 'litre' (liter).
