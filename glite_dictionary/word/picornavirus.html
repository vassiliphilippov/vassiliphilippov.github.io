picornavirus

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈkɔrnəˌvaɪrəs / (AmE), / pɪˈkɔːnəˌvaɪərəs / (BrE)
Any of a group of small single-stranded RNA viruses with a protein coat, including those that cause diseases such as polio and the common cold.
group of small RNA viruses
medical

Examples

Researchers identified a picornavirus as the cause of the recent outbreak.
Some types of picornavirus are responsible for the common cold.
The laboratory specializes in studying the genetic structure of picornaviruses.

Etymology

From pico- (denoting 'small') + Latin 'rna' (ribonucleic acid) + virus; named for their small size and RNA genome.
