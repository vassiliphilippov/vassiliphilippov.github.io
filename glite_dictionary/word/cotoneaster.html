cotoneaster

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈtoʊniˌæstər / (AmE), / kəˈtəʊniːˌæstə / (BrE)
a shrub of the genus Cotoneaster, with colourful autumn foliage, small white or pinkish flowers, and small red or black berries
a shrub with colourful foliage and small berries

Examples

A row of cotoneaster bushes formed a natural hedge along the driveway.
The garden was brightened in autumn by the red berries of the cotoneaster.
Birds are attracted to the cotoneaster because of its abundance of fruit.

Etymology

From Latin *cotoneaster*, from *cotoneum* (quince) + *-aster* (resembling).
