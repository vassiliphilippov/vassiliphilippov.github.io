translucence

1. NOUN

/ trænˈluːsəns / (AmE), / trænˈluːsəns / (BrE)
the quality of allowing light to pass through, but not detailed shapes; semi-transparency
quality of being semi-transparent

Examples

The translucence of the frosted glass let in soft, diffused light.
Artists appreciate the translucence of watercolor paint for its subtle effects.
She admired the translucence of the thin rice paper when held up to the sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: opacity - the quality of being opaque or not letting light through
  • Degree Higher: transparency - the quality of allowing light to pass through so that objects behind can be seen clearly

Etymology

From Latin 'translucēns', present participle of 'translucēre' (to shine through).
