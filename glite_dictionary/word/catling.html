catling

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkatlɪŋ / (BrE)
a long double-edged knife formerly used for surgical amputations
double-edged surgical amputation knife

Examples

The surgeon carefully selected the catling for the amputation procedure.
Historical displays often include a catling among old surgical tools.
A catling was considered essential in nineteenth-century operating rooms.

Etymology

From the surname Catling, of uncertain origin, used for the instrument's inventor or maker.
