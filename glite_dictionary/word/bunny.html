bunny

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌni / (AmE), / ˈbʌni / (BrE)
An informal term for a rabbit, especially a young one.
young or small rabbit (informal)
informal

Examples

The little girl held her bunny gently in her hands.
They saw a bunny hopping across the garden early in the morning.
At Easter, children often receive chocolate bunny gifts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rabbit - a small, furry mammal with long ears

Etymology

Diminutive of 'bun', an old dialect word for rabbit.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌni / (AmE), / ˈbʌni / (BrE)
a young woman who works as a waitress in a nightclub or casino and wears a costume featuring a rabbit tail and ears, especially at a Playboy Club
waitress in rabbit costume, especially at Playboy Club

Examples

She once worked as a bunny at the famous nightclub downtown.
The bunny greeted guests with a bright smile and classic rabbit ears.
In the 1960s, becoming a bunny was a glamorous job for many young women.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: playmate - a model featured in Playboy magazine
