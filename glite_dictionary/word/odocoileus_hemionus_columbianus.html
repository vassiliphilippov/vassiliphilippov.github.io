Odocoileus hemionus columbianus

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊdəˈsɔɪliəs hiˈmaɪ.ən.əs kəˌlʌm.biˈɑː.nəs / (AmE), / ˌəʊdəˈsɔɪliəs hɪˈmaɪ.ən.əs kəˌlʌm.bɪˈɑː.nəs / (BrE)
a subspecies of mule deer found in the western Rocky Mountains, commonly called the Columbian black-tailed deer
a subspecies of mule deer (Columbian black-tailed deer)

Examples

The Odocoileus hemionus columbianus is often seen grazing near forest edges in the Pacific Northwest.
Wildlife researchers track the migration routes of Odocoileus hemionus columbianus throughout the year.
Habitat loss has threatened populations of Odocoileus hemionus columbianus in some areas of the Rocky Mountains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mule deer - a large deer native to western North America, with mule-like ears

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus Odocoileus, species hemionus, and subspecies columbianus referring to the Columbia region.
