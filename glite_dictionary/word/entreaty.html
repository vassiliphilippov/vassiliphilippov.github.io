entreaty

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtriːti / (AmE), / ɪnˈtriːti / (BrE)
an earnest or urgent request, often made with deep emotion or seriousness
earnest or urgent request

Examples

She made an entreaty for understanding, hoping her friends would forgive her mistake.
Despite his desperate entreaty, the council refused to reopen the case.
Their entreaties for help were met with silence from the authorities.

Synset Examples

an entreaty to stop the fighting
an appeal for help
an appeal to the public to keep calm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appeal - an urgent or heartfelt request
  • Synonym: plea - a serious and emotional request
  • Antonym: demand - an insistent and firm request

Etymology

From Old French *entraitie* (entreaty), from *entraiter* (to treat, entreat).
