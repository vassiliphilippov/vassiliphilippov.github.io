hopscotch

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑpˌskɑtʃ / (AmE), / ˈhɒpˌskɒtʃ / (BrE)
a children's game in which players toss a small object onto a numbered pattern marked on the ground and hop through the spaces to retrieve it
children's hopping game

Examples

The children were playing hopscotch on the sidewalk after school.
She drew the hopscotch pattern with chalk and invited her friends to join.
Memories of carefree summers often include games of hopscotch in the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skipping - a game involving jumping over a rope swung under the feet

Etymology

From 'hop' + 'scotch', referring to hopping and a scored line or scratch on the ground.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑpˌskɑtʃ / (AmE), / ˈhɒpˌskɒtʃ / (BrE)
A children's game in which players hop through numbered spaces drawn on the ground, usually in a set sequence, often using a marker such as a stone.
a jumping children's playground game

Examples

The children drew a hopscotch grid on the pavement with chalk.
During recess, the students eagerly played hopscotch together.
She tossed her stone onto the hopscotch board and began hopping.

Synset Examples

We used chalk to draw a hopscotch game in the park.

Etymology

From 'hop' (jump on one foot) + 'scotch' (line, from the Scottish word for score or scratch).
