chondriosome

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.dri.əˌsoʊm / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.drɪ.ə.səʊm / (BrE)
an organelle within the cell that contains enzymes for producing energy, also known as a mitochondrion
cell organelle for producing energy
medical

Examples

The chondriosome is often referred to as the powerhouse of the cell.
Researchers studied how the chondriosome converts glucose into usable energy in living organisms.
When the chondriosome malfunctions, a cell's ability to produce energy is significantly reduced.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mitochondrion - an organelle in cells that generates energy

Etymology

From Greek 'khondrion' (small grain) + 'soma' (body).
