bushbuck

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊʃˌbʌk / (AmE), / ˈbʊʃˌbʌk / (BrE)
A medium-sized African antelope with white markings and spirally twisted horns, typically found in forests and bushland.
African antelope with white markings

Examples

We saw a bushbuck darting through the undergrowth during our safari in Kenya.
The park is home to many species, including the elusive bushbuck.
A pair of bushbucks grazed quietly near the riverbank at dawn.

Etymology

From 'bush' (wilderness) + 'buck' (male antelope/deer), originally South African English.
