ineluctability

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌnɛləktəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪnɪˌlʌktəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being impossible to avoid or escape.
impossibility of being avoided or escaped

Examples

The ineluctability of change is something that every company must eventually face.
Some philosophers have written about the ineluctability of fate.
He felt trapped by the ineluctability of his circumstances, unable to find a way out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inevitability - the quality of being certain to happen
  • Antonym: avoidability - the possibility of being avoided

Etymology

From Latin 'ineluctabilis' (that cannot be struggled out of), from 'in-' (not) + 'eluctari' (to struggle out).
