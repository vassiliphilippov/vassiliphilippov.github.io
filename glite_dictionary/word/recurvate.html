recurvate

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈkɝː.veɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈkɜː.veɪt / (BrE)
curved backward or inward
curved backward or inward

Examples

The bird's beak is slightly recurvate, helping it to probe for insects.
Some plant leaves are recurvate at the tips, curling toward the stem.
The fossil was identified by its recurvate claws, which curved sharply inward.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curved - having a shape that is smoothly bending without angles

Etymology

From Latin 'recurvatus', past participle of 'recurvare' meaning 'to bend back'.
