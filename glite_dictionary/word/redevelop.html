to redevelop

1. VERB

/ ˌriː.dɪˈvɛ.ləp / (AmE), / ˌriː.dɪˈvɛl.əp / (BrE)
to formulate or develop something again, especially in an improved or revised form, such as a theory or hypothesis
develop again, often in an improved form

Examples

After reviewing new data, the researchers decided to redevelop their initial hypothesis.
The team worked to redevelop the software architecture, addressing several core issues and inefficiencies.
Scientists often have to redevelop their theories as new evidence comes to light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to develop - to create or improve something over time

Etymology

Prefix re- (again) + develop; 19th century.

2. VERB

/ ˌriːdɪˈvɛləp / (AmE), / ˌriːdɪˈvɛləp / (BrE)
to process a photographic negative or print again to enhance its contrast, colour, or other qualities
process a photo negative or print again

Examples

If the initial print is too dark, you may need to redevelop it to improve the contrast.
The photographer decided to redevelop the negative to enhance the image's color balance.
Sometimes, prints are redeveloped to correct exposure errors made during the first development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to develop - to treat a photographic film or print with chemicals to produce a visible image
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02143834-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌriːdəˈvɛləp / (AmE), / ˌriːdɪˈvɛləp / (BrE)
to change or improve the use of land, especially by replacing old buildings with new ones or by changing its function
make new use of land or buildings

Examples

The city plans to redevelop the old factory site into apartment complexes.
They hope to redevelop the waterfront area to attract more tourists and businesses.
After the shopping center closed, investors decided to redevelop the land for residential use.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: to develop - to build on land or for buildings; to make something more advanced

Etymology

From re- (again) + develop (to make or become more advanced).
