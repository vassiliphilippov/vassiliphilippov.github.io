Technicolor

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛknɪˌkʌlər / (AmE), / ˈtɛknɪˌkʌlə / (BrE)
a trademarked process used to produce color motion pictures, especially noted for its vivid colors, widely used in the early and mid-20th century
early color film process

Examples

The old musicals were famous for their vivid Technicolor scenes.
Many classic films of the 1940s were shot in Technicolor.
She loved the brilliant hues that only Technicolor could produce on screen.

Etymology

From 'techni-' (relating to technology) + 'color'; originally a trademark of Technicolor Motion Picture Corporation, USA (1915).
