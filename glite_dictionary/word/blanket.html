blanket

1. VERB

/ ˈblæŋ.kɪt / (AmE), / ˈblæŋ.kɪt / (BrE)
to cover something completely, especially in a way that suggests being wrapped or concealed, like a blanket
cover completely like a blanket

Examples

A thick fog blanketed the entire city at dawn.
She feels warmth when fresh snow blankets the ground outside her home.
After the explosion, darkness blanketed the area, making it hard to see.

Synset Examples

snow blanketed the fields

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover - place or spread something over

2. NOUN

/ ˈblæŋkət / (AmE), / ˈblaŋkɪt / (BrE)
A layer of material, such as lead, surrounding and protecting the core of a nuclear reactor.
protective layer in a nuclear reactor

Examples

The reactor's blanket absorbs neutrons to increase safety and efficiency.
Technicians inspected the lead blanket around the active core for any signs of damage.
A depleted uranium blanket can be used to breed plutonium-239 in certain reactor designs.

5. VERB

/ ˈblæŋkɪt / (AmE), / ˈblaŋkɪt / (BrE)
to cover something completely, especially in a way that resembles a blanket being spread over it; to envelop or obscure.
to cover completely, as with a blanket

Examples

The city was blanketed in thick fog that made driving hazardous.
Snow blanketed the entire valley overnight, leaving everything silent and white.
A wave of silence blanketed the room after the announcement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover - to place something over or upon, as protection or concealment

Etymology

From Old French 'blanchet', diminutive of 'blanc' (white), originally referring to a white cloth.

6. NOUN

/ ˈblæŋkət / (AmE), / ˈblaŋkɪt / (BrE)
A large piece of soft cloth, often woolen, used on a bed to keep a person warm.
bed covering for warmth

Examples

She pulled the blanket up to her chin to stay warm on the cold night.
The hotel provided an extra blanket for each guest during winter.
After the picnic, they sat together on a thick woolen blanket under the stars.

Synset Examples

he pulled the covers over his head and went to sleep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quilt - a warm bed covering with padding, stitched together
  • Synonym: coverlet - a decorative bedspread, usually lighter than a blanket
  • Degree Lower: sheet - a large rectangular piece of cloth for laying on or covering a bed
  • Degree Higher: duvet - a soft quilt filled with down or feathers used as a bed covering

Etymology

From Old French 'blanchet' (white cloth), diminutive of 'blanc' (white).
