tuberculoid

1. ADJ

/ tuːˈbɜːrkjəˌlɔɪd / (AmE), / tjuːˈbɜːkjʊlɔɪd / (BrE)
resembling or characteristic of tuberculosis, especially in appearance or pathology; specifically, referring to conditions or lesions that resemble those caused by tuberculosis.
resembling tuberculosis; characteristic of tuberculosis
medical

Examples

The patient exhibited several tuberculoid lesions on her forearm.
Doctors diagnosed the disease as tuberculoid leprosy based on the appearance of the skin patches.
Biopsy confirmed a tuberculoid pattern of inflammation in the tissue sample.

Synset Examples

tuberculoid lesions
tuberculoid leprosy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tubercular - affected with or resembling tuberculosis

Etymology

From 'tubercle' (a nodular lesion) + '-oid' (resembling), indicating likeness to tuberculosis.
