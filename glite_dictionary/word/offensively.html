offensively

1. ADV

/ əˈfɛnsɪvli / (AmE), / əˈfɛnsɪvli / (BrE)
In a manner that causes offense or is unpleasantly disagreeable.
in an unpleasantly offensive way

Examples

He smelled offensively unwashed after several days of hiking.
The comedian's jokes were offensively crude, upsetting many in the audience.
She spoke offensively about his work, not caring if she hurt his feelings.

Synset Examples

he smelled offensively unwashed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: defensively - in a defensive manner; so as to defend oneself
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00308726-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ əˈfɛnsɪvli / (AmE), / əˈfɛnsɪvli / (BrE)
In a manner intended to attack or be aggressive.
in an aggressive or attacking manner

Examples

The team decided to play offensively to gain an early advantage.
During the debate, she argued offensively rather than responding to criticism.
The general outlined a plan to advance offensively against enemy positions.

Synset Examples

‘In this crisis, we must act offensively,’ the President said
the admiral intends to act offensively in the Mediterranean

Related Terms

  • Antonym: defensively - in a manner intended to defend or protect
  • Synonym: aggressively - in a manner that is forceful or attacking

Etymology

From 'offensive' + '-ly'; 'offensive' from Latin 'offensivus' (causing offense).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00308075-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ əˈfɛnsɪvli / (AmE), / əˈfɛnsɪvli / (BrE)
in an obnoxious, insulting, or disagreeable manner; in a way that causes displeasure or offense
in an obnoxious or insulting manner

Examples

His remarks were offensively rude, making everyone in the room uncomfortable.
She glared offensively at anyone who disagreed with her ideas.
Some advertisements are offensively stereotypical and have sparked public backlash.

Synset Examples

he said so in one of his more offensively intellectually arrogant sentences

Related Terms

  • Antonym: defensively - in a manner intended to defend or protect; opposite of offensively

Etymology

From 'offensive' + '-ly', formed from Latin 'offensivus' (causing offense) + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00308302-r
Synset POS: r
