inhalation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnhəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnhəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a medication that is administered by breathing it in through the mouth or nose
medication taken by inhaling
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an inhalation to help manage her asthma symptoms.
You should use the inhalation every morning and evening for best results.
This type of inhalation delivers medication directly to the lungs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inhaler - a device used to administer medication by inhalation

Etymology

From Latin inhalatio (act of breathing in), from 'in-' (in) + 'halare' (to breathe).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnhəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnhəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of breathing in air or other gases into the lungs
the act of breathing in

Examples

Deep inhalation of fresh air can help calm your nerves.
The doctor warned that inhalation of toxic fumes could be dangerous.
She felt better after a long, slow inhalation followed by a steady exhale.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exhalation - the act of breathing out air from the lungs
  • Synonym: to inspire - to draw air into the lungs

Etymology

From Latin 'inhalatio', from 'inhalare' (to breathe in).
