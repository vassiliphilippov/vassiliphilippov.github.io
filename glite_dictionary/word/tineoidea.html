Tineoidea

1. NOUN

/ taɪniˈɔɪdiə / (AmE), / tɪniˈɔɪdiə / (BrE)
A superfamily of moths including clothes moths, carpet moths, and various leaf miners.
superfamily of moths (clothes, carpet, leaf miners)

Examples

Tineoidea contains a number of species known for damaging fabrics and stored products.
Moths in the superfamily Tineoidea are found in many parts of the world.
Researchers are studying Tineoidea to understand the evolution of feeding habits in moths.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Tinea + -oidea (indicating superfamily).
