rhymed

1. ADJ

/ raɪmd / (AmE), / raɪmd / (BrE)
(of verse or words) having corresponding sounds, especially at the ends of lines or words.
having sounds that correspond, especially at line ends

Examples

He wrote a rhymed couplet for the school poetry contest.
The play is written entirely in rhymed verse, giving it a musical rhythm.
Many children's books use rhymed text to keep young readers engaged.

Synset Examples

rhymed verse
rhyming words

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unrhymed - (of verse or poetry) not having terminal sounds that correspond
  • Synonym: rhyming - (of words or poetry) forming rhyme; having a similar ending sound

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'rhyme', from Middle English 'rime', from Old French 'rime'.
