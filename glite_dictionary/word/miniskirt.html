miniskirt

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪniˌskɝt / (AmE), / ˈmɪniˌskɜːt / (BrE)
A very short skirt, typically with a hemline well above the knees, often worn as a fashionable garment.
very short skirt

Examples

She wore a bright red miniskirt to the summer festival.
The miniskirt became a symbol of youth culture in the 1960s.
Retail stores display miniskirts in a variety of fabrics and colors this season.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: skirt - an outer garment worn from the waist down, typically by women

Etymology

Formed from 'mini-' (short) + 'skirt', first popularized in the 1960s.
