chocolate

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑklət / (AmE), / ˈtʃɒklət / (BrE)
A sweet, brown food made from roasted and ground cacao beans, typically eaten as a confection or used for flavoring.
sweet food from cacao beans

Examples

She bought a box of chocolate as a gift for her friend.
The rich aroma of chocolate filled the kitchen as the cake baked.
He prefers dark chocolate over milk chocolate for its intense flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cocoa - a powder made from roasted cacao beans and used in cooking or as a drink
  • Degree Lower: carob - a sweet, chocolate substitute made from carob tree pods

Etymology

From Nahuatl *chocolātl* via Spanish *chocolate*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑːklət / (AmE), / ˈtʃɒklət / (BrE)
A hot drink made by mixing cocoa powder, milk, and sugar, usually served hot.
hot cocoa-based drink

Examples

On cold winter nights, I love to drink a cup of chocolate by the fire.
She ordered a steaming mug of chocolate with whipped cream at the café.
After sledding, the children warmed up with delicious chocolate made on the stove.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hot chocolate - a beverage made from chocolate or cocoa and heated milk or water

Etymology

From Nahuatl *xocolātl*, via Spanish *chocolate*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07939004-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑːklət / (AmE), / ˈtʃɒklət / (BrE)
a medium brown to dark-brown color resembling that of chocolate
a medium to dark brown color

Examples

She painted her living room a rich shade of chocolate.
He wore a chocolate sweater that matched his eyes perfectly.
The chocolate curtains made the room feel warm and cozy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: brown - a color ranging between red and yellow, darker than beige
  • Degree Lower: beige - a pale sandy yellowish-brown color

Etymology

From Nahuatl *chocolātl* via Spanish *chocolate*; extended to describe the color resembling chocolate.
