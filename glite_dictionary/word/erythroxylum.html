Erythroxylum

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌrɪθrɑkˈsaɪləm / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪθrɒkˈsaɪləm / (BrE)
A genus of South American shrubs and small trees in the family Erythroxylaceae, including species such as the coca plant.
genus of South American shrubs and trees

Examples

The genus Erythroxylum includes the coca plant, from which cocaine is derived.
Several species within Erythroxylum have economic and medicinal importance in South America.
Botanists studied Erythroxylum to better understand its distribution across tropical regions.

Etymology

From Greek 'erythros' (red) + 'xylon' (wood).
