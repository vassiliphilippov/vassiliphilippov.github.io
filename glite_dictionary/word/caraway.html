caraway

1. NOUN

/ ˈkærəˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈkærəweɪ / (BrE)
A Eurasian plant of the parsley family with small white flowers that yields aromatic seeds used as a spice.
Eurasian plant yielding aromatic seeds

Examples

The recipe called for a teaspoon of caraway to enhance the flavor of the bread.
She sprinkled caraway seeds over the roasted vegetables for a unique aroma.
Caraway is often grown in herb gardens for its culinary use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cumin - A similar spice from another plant, used in cooking

Etymology

From Old Spanish 'carahuaya', from Arabic 'al-karawiyā'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæɹəˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈkærəweɪ / (BrE)
The leaves of the caraway plant, used sparingly as an herb in soups and stews.
leaves of the caraway plant used as herb

Examples

The soup was garnished with finely chopped caraway for an aromatic touch.
She added a few caraway leaves to the stew to enhance its flavor.
Using fresh caraway in salads gives a subtle hint of spice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caraway seed - the seeds of the caraway plant, used as spice

Etymology

From Old French 'caroi', from Latin 'carum', from Greek 'karon' (the caraway plant).
