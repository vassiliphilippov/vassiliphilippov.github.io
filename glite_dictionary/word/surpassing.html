surpassing

1. ADJ

/ sərˈpæsɪŋ / (AmE), / səˈpɑːsɪŋ / (BrE)
exceptional; far greater or better than what is usual or expected
exceptionally great or extraordinary

Examples

The pianist gave a surpassing performance that left the entire audience in awe.
This book is of surpassing importance for anyone studying medieval history.
Her kindness and generosity are truly surpassing in every way.

Synset Examples

a night of exceeding darkness
an exceptional memory
olympian efforts to save the city from bankruptcy
the young Mozart's prodigious talents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exceptional - unusually excellent or extraordinary
  • Antonym: ordinary - usual or normal; not extraordinary

Etymology

From Middle English 'surpassen', from Old French 'surpasser', from sur- 'over' + passer 'to pass'.

2. ADJ

/ sərˈpæsɪŋ / (AmE), / səˈpɑːsɪŋ / (BrE)
Exceptionally great or outstanding in quality or degree; exceeding what is usual or expected.
exceptionally great or outstanding

Examples

Her surpassing beauty captivated everyone at the gala.
The artist displayed surpassing skill in every painting he created.
His generosity is truly surpassing, going far beyond what is expected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extraordinary - very unusual, remarkable
  • Antonym: mediocre - of only average quality; not very good

Etymology

From Middle English 'surpassen', from Anglo-French 'surpasser'.
