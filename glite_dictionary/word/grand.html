grand

1. NOUN

/ ɡrænd / (AmE), / ɡrænd / (BrE)
a thousand dollars (or pounds), used especially in informal contexts
a thousand dollars or pounds (informal)
informal

Examples

He paid five grand for his new car.
She won ten grand in the lottery last weekend.
The prize money was a cool twenty grand.

Etymology

Possibly from Italian 'gran', short for 'mille grana' (a thousand grains; a type of coin).
