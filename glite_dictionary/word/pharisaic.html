pharisaic

1. ADJ

/ ˌfɛrəˈseɪɪk / (AmE), / ˌfærɪˈseɪɪk / (BrE)
Having or showing excessive or hypocritical piety.
excessively or hypocritically pious

Examples

The committee's pharisaic attitude made open discussion impossible.
He was well known for his pharisaic condemnation of others' behavior.
Many criticized her speech as pharisaic rather than genuinely caring.

Synset Examples

a sickening sanctimonious smile

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanctimonious - making a show of being morally superior to others
  • Synonym: hypocritical - behaving in a way that suggests one has higher standards than is the case
  • Antonym: sincere - genuine, without pretense or hypocrisy

Etymology

From Late Latin Pharisaicus, from Greek Pharisaïkos, from Pharisaios (Pharisee).
