roadstead

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊd.stɛd / (AmE), / ˈrəʊd.stɛd / (BrE)
an area of water near the shore that is partly sheltered and can be used by ships for anchoring, but is not as enclosed or protected as a harbor
partly sheltered anchorage near shore

Examples

Several ships waited in the roadstead for favorable winds before continuing their journey.
The coastal village offered a safe roadstead for fishing boats during storms.
A fleet anchored in the sheltered roadstead, avoiding the rougher open sea.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: harbor - a place on the coast where vessels may find shelter
  • Synonym: anchorage - a place suitable for a ship to anchor

Etymology

From Middle English 'rodstede', from 'road' (nautical: road, anchorage) + 'stead' (place).
