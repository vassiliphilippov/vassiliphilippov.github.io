William Faulkner

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪljəm ˈfɔːknɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɪljəm ˈfɔːknə / (BrE)
(1897–1962) An American novelist and short-story writer noted for depicting life in the Southern United States, especially through works such as 'The Sound and the Fury'.
renowned American novelist, 1897–1962

Examples

Many critics consider William Faulkner one of the most important American writers of the twentieth century.
The novels of William Faulkner often explore the complexities of Southern society.
She wrote her thesis on the works of William Faulkner and his influence on modern literature.
