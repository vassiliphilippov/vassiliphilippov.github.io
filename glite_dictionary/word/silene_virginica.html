Silene virginica

1. PROPN

/ saɪˈliːni vəˈdʒɪnɪkə / (AmE), / sɪˈliːni vɜːˈdʒɪnɪkə / (BrE)
A perennial herb native to eastern North America, noted for its bright red flowers with narrow, notched petals.
a North American perennial herb with red flowers

Examples

The brilliant red flowers of Silene virginica attract hummingbirds in summer gardens.
Botanists often refer to Silene virginica as fire pink because of its vivid coloration.
Silene virginica thrives in open woodlands and rocky slopes throughout its native range.

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name 'Silene', and species 'virginica' referencing Virginia, USA.
