ovary

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvəˌri / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəri / (BrE)
Either of a pair of female reproductive organs in which ova are produced and which secrete estrogen and progesterone.
female reproductive organ producing ova
medical

Examples

The surgeon explained that the ovary was healthy and functioning normally.
Hormones produced by the ovary play a crucial role in regulating the menstrual cycle.
Damage to the ovary can affect fertility in women.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: testis - male reproductive organ producing sperm
  • Synonym: egg - the female reproductive cell; ovum

Etymology

From Latin ovārium (egg receptacle), from ovum (egg).

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.və.ri / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.vəri / (BrE)
the part of a flower that contains the ovules and develops into the fruit after fertilization
flower organ containing ovules

Examples

The ovary of a flower swells and becomes the fruit after pollination.
In botany class, we dissected a lily to study its ovary and ovules.
The ovary is a crucial organ involved in the plant's reproductive process.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stamen - the pollen-producing reproductive organ of a flower
  • Synonym: pistil - the female reproductive part of a flower, including the ovary, style, and stigma

Etymology

From Latin *ovarium* (egg receptacle), from *ovum* (egg).
