grande dame

1. NOUN

/ ɡræn ˈdæm / (AmE), / ɡrɑːnd ˈdɑːm / (BrE)
a woman of great dignity, prestige, or influence, especially in a particular sphere
distinguished, respected woman

Examples

The city's art scene owes much of its vibrancy to its grande dame of culture.
After decades on stage, she became known as the grande dame of Broadway.
Every charity event in town was graced by the presence of its grande dame.

Synset Examples

As a grande dame, her influence is undeniable.
She is considered the grande dame of the theater.
The gala honored her as the grande dame of philanthropy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: matriarch - an older woman who is powerful within a family or organization

Etymology

From French, literally 'great lady'.
