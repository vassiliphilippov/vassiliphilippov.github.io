to deject

1. VERB

/ dɪˈdʒɛkt / (AmE), / dɪˈdʒɛkt / (BrE)
to make someone feel sad or dispirited; to lower someone's spirits
make someone feel sad or dispirited

Examples

The lack of progress did not deject her; she carried on with determination.
It would deject anyone to hear such disappointing news from a trusted friend.
Don't let harsh criticism deject you from pursuing your dreams.

Synset Examples

These news depressed her
The bad state of her child's health demoralizes her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depress - to make someone feel sad or without hope
  • Antonym: to elate - to make someone ecstatically happy

Etymology

From Latin deicere, deject-, meaning 'to throw down'.
