ball-hawking

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɔl ˌhɑkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɔːl ˌhɔːkɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a player) skilled at intercepting, stealing, or taking the ball from opponents, especially in sports such as baseball, football, or basketball.
skilled at taking or intercepting the ball (sports)

Examples

The team improved its defense with the addition of a ball-hawking cornerback.
His ball-hawking instincts helped him lead the league in interceptions.
A ball-hawking center fielder can change the outcome of a close game.

Synset Examples

a ball-hawking center fielder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defensive - focused on preventing the opponent from scoring or advancing

Etymology

Formed from 'ball' + 'hawk', from the idea of a hawk seizing prey.
