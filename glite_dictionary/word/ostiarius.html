ostiarius

1. NOUN

/ ɑsˈtiː.ɛəri.əs / (AmE), / ɒsˈtɪə.ri.əs / (BrE)
the holder of the lowest of the minor Holy Orders in the unreformed Western Church, a rank abolished by the Roman Catholic Church
the lowest minor Holy Order in the early Western Church

Examples

In the Middle Ages, an ostiarius was responsible for guarding the church doors during services.
He studied the four minor orders before being ordained as an ostiarius.
The office of ostiarius was discontinued after reforms by the Catholic Church.

Etymology

From Latin 'ostiarius' (doorkeeper), from 'ostium' (door).
