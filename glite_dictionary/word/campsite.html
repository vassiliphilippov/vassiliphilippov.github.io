campsite

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæmp.saɪt / (AmE), / ˈkæmp.saɪt / (BrE)
an area where people can stay in tents, usually while on holiday
area for pitching tents when camping

Examples

We found a beautiful campsite next to the river for our weekend getaway.
The campsite had showers, toilets, and a small shop for supplies.
Due to heavy rain, the campsite was closed for safety reasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campground - an area where people can camp, often with facilities

Etymology

From 'camp' (temporary place to stay) + 'site' (location), first used in early 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæmp.saɪt / (AmE), / ˈkæmp.saɪt / (BrE)
An area or place where people are allowed to camp, often with basic facilities provided.
place designated for camping

Examples

We found a beautiful campsite by the river for our holiday.
The national park has several campsites with toilets and showers.
It was so peaceful to wake up at the campsite surrounded by trees and birds.

Synset Examples

As a campist, she loves exploring different campsites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campground - an area equipped for camping with facilities

Etymology

Formed from 'camp' + 'site', referring to the site or place for a camp.
