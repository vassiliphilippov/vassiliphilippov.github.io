starets

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑr.ɛts / (AmE), / ˈstɑː.rɛts / (BrE)
a spiritual elder or adviser, especially in the Eastern Orthodox Church, often serving as a guide to monks and laypeople
Orthodox spiritual elder or adviser

Examples

Many pilgrims traveled great distances to seek the counsel of a respected starets.
The starets offered wisdom and spiritual comfort to those who visited the monastery.
In Russian literature, the figure of the starets often symbolizes moral authority and guidance.

Etymology

From Russian стáрец (stárets), meaning 'elder'.
