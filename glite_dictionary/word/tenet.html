tenet

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛnɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɛnɪt / (BrE)
a principle, belief, or doctrine generally held to be true, especially by a group or movement
a principle or doctrine held as true

Examples

One of the main tenets of their faith is nonviolence.
The organization bases its policies on the tenet that every individual has worth.
He disagreed with several tenets of the new philosophy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doctrine - a belief or set of beliefs taught by a church or other group

Etymology

From Latin 'tenet' (he/she holds), from 'tenere' (to hold).
