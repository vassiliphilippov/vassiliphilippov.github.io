moaner

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊnɚ / (AmE), / ˈməʊnə / (BrE)
a person who complains or whines excessively
person who complains a lot

Examples

My colleague is such a moaner—he's always finding fault with something.
Try to ignore the moaner in the back who never has anything positive to say.
She used to be a moaner, but now she tries to stay positive about challenges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complainer - a person who complains
  • Synonym: whiner - a person who whines or complains
  • Antonym: optimist - a person who tends to be hopeful and confident

Etymology

From 'moan' (to complain audibly) + '-er' (agent suffix).
