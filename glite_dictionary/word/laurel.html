laurel

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔr.əl / (AmE), / ˈlɒr.əl / (BrE)
any of various aromatic evergreen trees or shrubs of the laurel family, especially the bay tree, known for their glossy leaves and sometimes used in cooking and decoration
aromatic evergreen tree or shrub

Examples

The laurel in her garden filled the air with a sweet, spicy scent.
Cooks often use laurel leaves, also known as bay leaves, to flavor soups and stews.
The ancient Greeks crowned victors with a wreath of laurel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bay - an aromatic tree of the laurel family, often used in cooking

Etymology

From Latin 'laurus' (laurel tree, bay tree).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɔrəl / (AmE), / ˈlɒrəl / (BrE)
(in ancient times) a wreath made from the leaves of the laurel tree, worn on the head as a sign of victory or honor.
victor's wreath; sign of honor

Examples

The victorious athlete was presented with a laurel after the ancient Olympic Games.
In Rome, generals would wear a laurel to celebrate their military triumphs.
Poets in antiquity were sometimes honored with a laurel placed upon their heads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crown - a circular ornamental headdress, often symbolic of victory, rank, or honor

Etymology

From Latin *laurus* (laurel tree, laurel wreath).

3. NOUN

/ ˈlɔrəl / (AmE), / ˈlɒrəl / (BrE)
honor or recognition for an achievement, especially as a mark of distinction or success
honor or achievement recognized

Examples

After years of dedication, he finally earned the laurel of his profession.
She rested on her laurel and stopped pursuing further success.
The scientist accepted the laurel with a humble speech.

Synset Examples

Winning the championship brought her the laurel she deserved.
The artist received laurel for her groundbreaking work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honor - public recognition or esteem for achievement
  • Antonym: disgrace - loss of reputation or respect as a result of wrongdoing

Etymology

From Latin *laurea* (laurel tree or wreath), a symbol of victory in ancient Rome.
