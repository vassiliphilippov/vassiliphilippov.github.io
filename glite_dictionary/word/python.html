python

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪˌθɑn / (AmE), / ˈpʌɪθən / (BrE)
(Archaic) A soothsaying spirit or a person believed to be possessed by such a spirit, especially one who delivers oracles.
archaic: soothsaying spirit or oracle-giver
archaic

Examples

In ancient texts, the priestess was referred to as a python who could divine the future.
Some believed the python spoke with the voice of the gods during the sacred rituals.
The villagers sought advice from a python whenever uncertainty clouded their harvest plans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oracle - a priest or priestess acting as a medium through whom advice or prophecy was sought

Etymology

From Greek *Pythōn*, the name of the mythical serpent at Delphi slain by Apollo, later associated with oracles.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪ.θɑn / (AmE), / ˈpaɪ.θən / (BrE)
a very large non-venomous snake found in Africa, Asia, and Australia, belonging to the family Pythonidae, which kills its prey by constriction
large non-venomous constricting snake

Examples

The python silently coiled itself around the tree branch before striking at its prey.
A fully grown python can reach lengths of over twenty feet in the wild.
Visitors to the zoo were fascinated by the enormous python in the reptile house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boa - a large constrictor snake of the family Boidae

Etymology

From Latin 'python', from Greek 'Pythōn', the name of a mythical serpent killed by Apollo.
