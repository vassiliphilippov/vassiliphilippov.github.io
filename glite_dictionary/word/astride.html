astride

1. ADV

/ əˈstraɪd / (AmE), / əˈstraɪd / (BrE)
with one leg on each side of something; sitting or standing with legs on either side
with a leg on each side

Examples

She rode the horse astride, her posture confident and secure.
The boy sat astride the fence, watching the field.
He posed astride a fallen log for the photo.

Synset Examples

she sat astride the chair

Etymology

From Middle English, a- (on) + stride (step).

2. ADV

/ əˈstraɪd / (AmE), / əˈstraɪd / (BrE)
with one leg on each side of something; with the legs stretched far apart
with legs on either side; straddling

Examples

She sat astride the horse, ready to ride.
The boy walked astride the fallen tree, carefully balancing himself.
He stood astride the stream, looking down into the water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: straddle - sit or stand with a leg on each side of something

Etymology

From Old English 'on' (on) + 'stride' (stride, step with long steps).
