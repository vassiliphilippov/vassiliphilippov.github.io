to romanticize

1. VERB

/ roʊˈmæn.tɪ.saɪz / (AmE), / rəʊˈmæn.tɪ.saɪz / (BrE)
to give something a romantic or sentimental style or character, especially in appearance or presentation
give romantic style or character

Examples

The fashion house sought to romanticize traditional rural clothing in its new collection.
She loves to romanticize her apartment with vintage furnishings and soft lighting.
Artists often romanticize city life, giving it an air of mystery and charm.

Synset Examples

The designer romanticized the little black dress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to idealize - to regard or represent as perfect or better than reality

Etymology

From 'romantic' + '-ize', patterned after French 'romantiser'.

2. VERB

/ roʊˈmæntɪˌsaɪz / (AmE), / rəʊˈmæntɪˌsaɪz / (BrE)
to make something seem more attractive or appealing than it really is, often by ignoring its negative aspects
view as more attractive than reality

Examples

It's common for people to romanticize the past, forgetting its many difficulties.
The movie tends to romanticize rural life, making it seem much simpler than it actually is.
We shouldn't romanticize poverty or hardship; they are serious issues that need solutions.

Synset Examples

Don't romanticize this uninteresting and hard work!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to idealize - to regard or represent as perfect or better than in reality
  • Antonym: realistic - having or showing a sensible and practical idea of what can be achieved or expected

Etymology

From 'romantic' + '-ize'.

3. VERB

/ roʊˈmæntɪˌsaɪz / (AmE), / rəʊˈmæntɪsaɪz / (BrE)
to make something seem more attractive, ideal, or appealing than it really is, often by ignoring its negative aspects
make something seem more attractive or ideal than it is

Examples

It's easy to romanticize the past when you forget about its hardships.
Movies often romanticize life in small towns, showing only the joys and not the struggles.
She tends to romanticize relationships in her writing, portraying them as perfect and effortless.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to idealize - to regard or represent as perfect or better than in reality
  • Antonym: to downplay - to make something appear less important

Etymology

From 'romantic' + '-ize', modeled after French romancer (to relate as in a romance).
