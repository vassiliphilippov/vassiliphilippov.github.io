Oxylebius

1. NOUN

/ ɑksɪˈliːbiəs / (AmE), / ɒksɪˈliːbiəs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Hexagrammidae, native to the North Pacific Ocean.
genus of marine fish (family Hexagrammidae)

Examples

The Oxylebius is easily recognized by its distinct markings among the kelp beds.
Scientists observed that Oxylebius species prefer rocky coastal habitats.
Juvenile Oxylebius often serve as a food source for larger predatory fish.

Etymology

From Greek oxys (sharp) + lebion (small fish), used in scientific taxonomy.
