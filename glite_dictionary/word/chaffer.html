to chaffer

1. VERB

/ ˈtʃæfər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːfə / (BrE)
to bargain or haggle over the price or terms of something
bargain or haggle
archaic

Examples

The merchants would chaffer for hours before settling on a price.
She refused to chaffer over the few coins he demanded.
They chaffered long into the night, each determined to get a better deal.

Synset Examples

Let's not haggle over a few dollars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haggle - to argue persistently, especially about a price
  • Synonym: bargain - to negotiate the terms or price of a purchase

Etymology

From Middle English 'chaffare', from Old English 'ceapfaru' (business), related to 'cheap'.

2. VERB

/ ˈtʃæfər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːfə / (BrE)
to talk in a friendly way without sharing anything important; to engage in light, inconsequential conversation
to chat casually and inconsequentially
archaic

Examples

The two old friends sat on the porch and chaffered until dusk.
They chaffered by the market stall, exchanging stories along with their goods.
While waiting for the train, the travelers chaffered about the weather and their plans.

Synset Examples

the men were sitting in the cafe and shooting the breeze

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chat - to talk in a friendly and informal way
  • Synonym: chitchat - casual conversation about unimportant things

Etymology

From Middle English 'chaffaren' (to bargain), from Old French 'chafrer', related to barter and chat.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01040538-v
Synset POS: v
