tinned

1. ADJ

/ tɪnd / (AmE), / tɪnd / (BrE)
(of food) preserved and sealed in a can or tin.
preserved in a can or tin
british

Examples

She bought several cans of tinned tomatoes at the supermarket.
During the hike, we relied mostly on tinned beans for our meals.
Many recipes call for fresh or tinned fruit, depending on the season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canned - preserved and sealed in a can (American English usage)

Etymology

From 'tin' (the metal) + '-ed', originally referring to items packed in tins.
