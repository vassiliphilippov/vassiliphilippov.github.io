hereafter

1. ADV

/ ˌhɪrˈæftər / (AmE), / ˌhɪəˈɑːftə / (BrE)
in a future existence or life, especially after death
in a future life or existence

Examples

Many religions teach that actions in this life determine one's fate in the hereafter.
She prayed for her loved ones, hoping they would meet again in the hereafter.
He believed that good deeds would be rewarded in the hereafter.

Synset Examples

hope to win salvation hereafter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: afterlife - life after death

Etymology

From Middle English herafter, from here (‘in this place’) + after (‘later’).

3. NOUN

/ hɪrˈæftɚ / (AmE), / hɪəˈɑːftə / (BrE)
the life that comes after death, especially as believed in religious or spiritual traditions.
life after death

Examples

Many religions teach that there is a hereafter where souls are judged.
She devoted her life to charitable work, hoping it would benefit her in the hereafter.
The philosopher pondered the nature of the hereafter and whether consciousness might survive death.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: afterlife - life after physical death

Etymology

From Middle English 'here' (in this place) + 'after' (later), used since the 14th century.

Source: WordNet
