crawlspace

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔlˌspeɪs / (AmE), / ˈkrɔːlˌspeɪs / (BrE)
A narrow, unfinished space beneath the floor of a building, typically used to access wiring, plumbing, or ventilation.
low space beneath a building's floor

Examples

The electrician had to squeeze into the crawlspace to repair the faulty wiring.
They discovered a leak in the pipes running through the crawlspace under the house.
Before selling the house, she cleaned out the debris from the crawlspace.
