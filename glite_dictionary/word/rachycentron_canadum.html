Rachycentron canadum

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪkiˈsɛntrən kəˈneɪdəm / (AmE), / ˌrækɪˈsɛntrən kəˈneɪdəm / (BrE)
A large tropical marine fish with dark stripes, valued as food and game, found in warm coastal and open waters worldwide; also known as the cobia.
large tropical fish; the cobia

Examples

Fishermen prize the Rachycentron canadum for its fighting ability and tasty flesh.
The diet of the Rachycentron canadum consists mainly of smaller fish and crustaceans.
Scientists are studying the growth rates of Rachycentron canadum in aquaculture environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cobia - common name for Rachycentron canadum
  • Synonym: remora - family of fish related to Rachycentron canadum

Etymology

From Greek rhachis (spine) + kentron (sting) and Latin canadum (tail).
