Regnellidium

1. NOUN

/ rɛɡˌnɛlɪˈdiːəm / (AmE), / rɛɡˌnɛlɪˈdiːəm / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic or semiaquatic ferns, containing only one species, native to South America.
a genus of aquatic or semiaquatic ferns

Examples

The rare aquatic fern Regnellidium can be found in South American wetlands.
Scientists studied the single species within the genus Regnellidium for its adaptation to marsh habitats.
Regnellidium is notable for its distinctive leaf structure among aquatic ferns.

Etymology

Named after Anders Fredrik Regnell, Swedish botanist, with the standard botanical suffix -idium.

2. NOUN

/ rɛɡˌnɛˈlɪdiəm / (AmE), / rɛɡˌnɛˈlɪdiəm / (BrE)
A small, aquatic fern of the genus Regnellidium, native to southern Brazil and known for containing latex.
aquatic fern genus from southern Brazil

Examples

The Regnellidium thrives in the shallow waters of marshes and ponds across southern Brazil.
Scientists have studied Regnellidium to understand its unique latex-producing capabilities.
A rare specimen of Regnellidium was collected during the expedition to the Pantanal wetlands.

Etymology

Named after Anders Fredrik Regnell, Swedish botanist, with the modern Latin suffix -idium.
