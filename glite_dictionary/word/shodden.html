shodden

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃɑdən / (AmE), / ˈʃɒdən / (BrE)
wearing shoes or other footgear; shod.
wearing footwear
archaic

Examples

The travelers were shodden against the cold, muddy road.
Unlike the shodden soldiers, some villagers walked barefoot.
He arrived at the inn, weary but properly shodden for the journey.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unshod - not wearing shoes or footgear
  • Synonym: shod - wearing shoes; equipped with a shoe (often of a horse)

Etymology

Formed from 'shod' (past participle of 'shoe'), with the suffix -en.
