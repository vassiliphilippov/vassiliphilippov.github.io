Lepisma

1. NOUN

/ ləˈpɪzmə / (AmE), / lɛˈpɪzmə / (BrE)
A genus of primitive wingless insects in the family Lepismatidae; commonly known as silverfish.
genus of insects (silverfish)

Examples

The species Lepisma saccharinum is commonly found in homes worldwide.
Entomologists classify silverfish under the genus Lepisma.
Members of the genus Lepisma have flat, elongated bodies covered with silvery scales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silverfish - a small, wingless insect known for its silvery scales

Etymology

From Greek *lepisma* (a flake or scale), referring to the scaly appearance of the insect.
