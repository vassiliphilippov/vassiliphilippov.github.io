Sesbania

1. NOUN

/ sɛzˈbeɪniə / (AmE), / sɛzˈbɑːniə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical and subtropical leguminous herbs, shrubs, or trees, commonly found in wet or marshy areas
a genus of leguminous tropical plants

Examples

The Sesbania genus includes several species used in agroforestry and soil improvement.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Sesbania genus in tropical Africa.
Farmers often plant Sesbania along riverbanks to prevent erosion and enrich the soil.

Etymology

From New Latin Sesbania, derived from Arabic 'saisabān', a name for one of the plants in this genus.
