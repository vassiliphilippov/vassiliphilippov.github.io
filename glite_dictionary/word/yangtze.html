Yangtze

1. PROPN

/ ˈjɑŋtsi / (AmE), / ˈjaŋtsi / (BrE)
the longest river in Asia, flowing eastward from Tibet through China to the East China Sea near Shanghai
longest river in Asia, in China

Examples

The Yangtze River is home to many unique species of plants and animals.
Flooding along the Yangtze has historically caused major damage to surrounding regions.
They took a boat cruise down the Yangtze to see the Three Gorges.

Etymology

From Chinese 长江 (Cháng Jiāng, 'Long River'), known as 'Yangtze' after the Yangzi region.
