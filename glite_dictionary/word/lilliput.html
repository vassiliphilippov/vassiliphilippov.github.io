Lilliput

1. PROPN

/ ˈlɪlɪˌpʊt / (AmE), / ˈlɪlɪˌpʊt / (BrE)
an imaginary country in Jonathan Swift's 'Gulliver's Travels', inhabited by tiny people
imaginary land of tiny people in Swift's writing

Examples

In the story, Gulliver awakens on the shores of Lilliput, surrounded by its tiny inhabitants.
The term 'Lilliput' has come to symbolize anything very small or miniature.
Many literary references compare tiny nations or groups to Lilliput.

Etymology

Named by Jonathan Swift in 'Gulliver's Travels' (1726), coined as a fictional place name.
