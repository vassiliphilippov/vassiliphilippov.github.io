excitant

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈsaɪtənt / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɪtənt / (BrE)
A drug or agent that temporarily stimulates or hastens a vital physiological process.
a drug or agent that stimulates activity
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an excitant to help the patient recover from the depressive episode.
Certain beverages, such as coffee, contain natural excitants that boost alertness.
Caffeine is commonly used as an excitant in energy drinks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stimulant - a substance that raises levels of physiological or nervous activity

Etymology

From Latin excitant-, present participle of excitare (to rouse, excite).

2. ADJ

/ ɪkˈsaɪtənt / (AmE), / ɪkˈsaɪtənt / (BrE)
(of drugs or substances) having the ability to excite or stimulate physiological or psychological activity.
producing excitement or stimulation
medical

Examples

Some medications act as an excitant on the central nervous system, increasing alertness.
Caffeine is commonly regarded as an excitant due to its stimulating effects.
The doctor advised avoiding excitant substances before bedtime to prevent insomnia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stimulant - a substance that raises levels of physiological or nervous activity
  • Antonym: sedative - a substance that induces sedation by reducing irritability or excitement

Etymology

From French 'excitant', present participle of 'exciter', from Latin 'excitare' meaning 'to excite'.
