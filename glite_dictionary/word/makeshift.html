makeshift

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪkˌʃɪft / (AmE), / ˈmeɪkʃɪft / (BrE)
a temporary substitute or device used to meet an urgent need or emergency
temporary substitute; improvised solution

Examples

We built a makeshift shelter out of branches when the tent was destroyed.
During the blackout, candles served as a makeshift source of light.
With the bridge out, a fallen tree became a makeshift crossing for hikers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stopgap - a temporary way of dealing with a problem
  • Antonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last indefinitely

Etymology

Formed from 'make' + 'shift' (noun sense of 'means, expedient') in the 16th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmeɪkˌʃɪft / (AmE), / ˈmeɪkʃɪft / (BrE)
made or used as a temporary substitute with whatever is at hand, often because nothing better is available
temporary substitute using available materials

Examples

After the tent collapsed, we built a makeshift shelter out of branches and blankets.
The doctor used a makeshift splint to stabilize the man's injured leg until help arrived.
We had to eat dinner on a makeshift table made from cardboard boxes.

Synset Examples

crossed the river on improvised bridges
the survivors used jury-rigged fishing gear
the rock served as a makeshift hammer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improvised - created or done without preparation, using what is available

Etymology

From make + shift, originally meaning 'means or expedient for getting along' (17th century).
