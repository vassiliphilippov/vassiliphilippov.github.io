outfielder

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌfiːldɚ / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌfiːldə / (BrE)
(Baseball) a player positioned in the outfield, responsible for fielding balls hit beyond the infield.
baseball player in the outfield

Examples

The team's fastest outfielder made an amazing catch in center field.
She has played as an outfielder since joining the baseball team.
During practice, the coach focused on improving the outfielders' throwing accuracy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infielder - a player positioned in the infield in baseball

Etymology

From 'outfield' (the area of the field beyond the infield) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌfiːldəɹ / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌfiːldə / (BrE)
a player in cricket who fields in the outfield, away from the wicket and close to the boundary
cricket player in the outfield

Examples

As an outfielder, she often chased balls near the boundary line.
The team added another outfielder to strengthen their defense in the deep field.
After his sprint, the outfielder threw the ball back to the wicketkeeper.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: fielder - a player whose job is to field the ball
  • Antonym: infielder - a cricket player who fields closer to the wicket

Etymology

Formed from 'outfield' (the outer part of the field) + '-er' (one who performs an action).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10406573-n
Synset POS: n
