Gaius Octavianus

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡaɪ.əs ɒkˌteɪ.viˈæn.əs / (AmE), / ˈɡaɪ.əs ɒkˌteɪ.vɪˈæn.əs / (BrE)
The Roman statesman who established the Roman Empire and became its first emperor, later known as Augustus (63 BC–AD 14).
first Roman emperor, also called Augustus

Examples

Historians regard Gaius Octavianus as a pivotal figure in Roman history.
After the assassination of Julius Caesar, Gaius Octavianus rose rapidly to power.
The Battle of Actium was the turning point for Gaius Octavianus and the future of Rome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Augustus - Title adopted by Gaius Octavianus as first emperor of Rome

Etymology

Latin personal name; 'Gaius' a common praenomen, 'Octavianus' from the gens Octavia.
