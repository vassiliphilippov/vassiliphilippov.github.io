microvolt

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪkroʊˌvoʊlt / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌvəʊlt / (BrE)
a unit of electric potential equal to one millionth of a volt
unit of electric potential (μV)

Examples

The instrument can detect voltage changes as small as one microvolt.
Brain activity was measured in microvolts during the experiment.
A signal below ten microvolts is difficult for most sensors to register.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: volt - unit of electric potential, standard unit

Etymology

From 'micro-' (Greek mikros, 'small') + 'volt', named after Alessandro Volta.
