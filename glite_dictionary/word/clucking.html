clucking

1. NOUN

/ ˈklʌkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklʌkɪŋ / (BrE)
the sound made by a hen, especially when calling her chicks
sound a hen makes, calling chicks

Examples

We woke up early to the clucking of the hens outside the window.
The mother hen's gentle clucking called her chicks to gather for food.
In the quiet barnyard, only the clucking of chickens could be heard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cluck - the characteristic call of a hen

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'cluck' with the suffix -ing.
