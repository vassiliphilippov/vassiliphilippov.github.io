deplorable

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈplɔrəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈplɔːrəbəl / (BrE)
deserving strong condemnation or disapproval; shockingly bad or unacceptable
deserving strong disapproval

Examples

The living conditions in the camp were absolutely deplorable and shocked the inspectors.
His deplorable behavior at the meeting led to widespread criticism from his colleagues.
It is deplorable that so many children go to bed hungry every night.

Synset Examples

a criminal waste of talent
a deplorable act of violence
adultery is as reprehensible for a husband as for a wife

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reprehensible - deserving censure or condemnation
  • Antonym: commendable - deserving praise or approval

Etymology

From French 'déplorable', from Latin 'deplorare' (to bewail).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈplɔːrəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈplɔːrəbəl / (BrE)
Deserving strong condemnation; shockingly bad in quality or condition.
shockingly bad; deserving strong condemnation

Examples

The refugees were living in deplorable conditions with little food or water.
His behavior at the meeting was utterly deplorable and shocked everyone present.
The building was found to be in a deplorable state of disrepair.

Synset Examples

my finances were in a deplorable state
a lamentable decision
her clothes were in sad shape
a sorry state of affairs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lamentable - deserving to be lamented; deplorable
  • Antonym: praiseworthy - worthy of praise; commendable

Etymology

From French *déplorable*, from Latin *deplorabilis* (that may be deplored).

3. ADJ

/ dɪˈplɔːrəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈplɔːrəbəl / (BrE)
of shockingly bad quality or condition; deserving strong condemnation
shockingly bad or unacceptable condition

Examples

The refugees were forced to live in deplorable conditions for months.
Many felt the company's handling of the crisis was simply deplorable.
The school board apologized for the deplorable state of the gymnasium.

Synset Examples

deplorable housing conditions in the inner city
woeful treatment of the accused
woeful errors of judgment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appalling - very bad; shocking
  • Antonym: commendable - deserving praise; admirable

Etymology

From French 'déplorable', from Latin 'deplorabilis', from 'deplorare' (to bewail, lament).
