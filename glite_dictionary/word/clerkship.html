clerkship

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɜrkˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈklɑːkʃɪp / (BrE)
the position or period of working as a clerk, especially as a formal or academic role in law, business, or medicine
the job or period of being a clerk

Examples

She obtained a prestigious clerkship with a federal judge after graduating from law school.
During his clerkship at the hospital, he gained valuable medical experience.
Many graduates seek a clerkship to launch their careers in public service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clerk - a person employed in an office or bank to keep records or accounts, deal with customers, etc.

Etymology

From clerk + -ship, forming nouns denoting office or position.
