Fraxinus oregona

1. NOUN

/ ˌfræksɪnəs ɔːˈrɛɡənə / (AmE), / ˌfraksɪnəs ɒˈrɛɡənə / (BrE)
A timber tree native to western North America, closely related to the red ash, known for its hard, light wood.
species of North American ash tree

Examples

The wood of the Fraxinus oregona is valued for its strength and lightness in making furniture.
Botanists identified the tall tree as a mature Fraxinus oregona growing near the riverbank.
In western forests, the Fraxinus oregona provides habitat for many bird species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fraxinus pennsylvanica - red ash, a closely related species of ash tree

Etymology

From Latin 'Fraxinus' (ash tree); 'oregona' referring to the Oregon region.
