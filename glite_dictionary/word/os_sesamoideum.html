os sesamoideum

1. NOUN

/ oʊs sɛsəˌmɔɪdiəm / (AmE), / ɒs sɛsəˌmɔɪdiəm / (BrE)
A small round bone found within a tendon where it passes over a joint, especially in the hands and feet.
small bone in a tendon near a joint
medical

Examples

The surgeon pointed out the os sesamoideum embedded in the patient's thumb tendon.
Damage to the os sesamoideum can result in pain and restricted movement of the joint.
An X-ray of the big toe may reveal an os sesamoideum near the metatarsophalangeal joint.

Etymology

From Latin 'os' (bone) and 'sesamoideum' (sesame-shaped, from Greek 'sēsamon', sesame seed).
