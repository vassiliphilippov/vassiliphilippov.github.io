canulisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæn.ju.ləˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkan.ju.lɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the insertion of a cannula or tube into a hollow organ, especially for medical purposes
insertion of a cannula or tube (medical procedure)
medical

Examples

The surgeon performed canulisation of the vein to administer the medication directly.
Complications can sometimes arise during canulisation of the bile duct in an ERCP procedure.
Proper training is required for nurses to carry out canulisation safely and effectively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catheterization - insertion of a catheter into a body cavity

Etymology

From Latin *canna* (reed, tube) via French *canule* (tube) + English suffix -isation.
