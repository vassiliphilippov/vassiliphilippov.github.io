St. Martin

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈmɑːrtən / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈmɑːtɪn / (BrE)
A French bishop of the 4th century, recognized as a patron saint of France, known for his acts of kindness and charity.
French bishop and patron saint of France

Examples

Many churches in Europe are named after St. Martin in honor of his charitable deeds.
The feast of St. Martin is celebrated on November 11th each year.
St. Martin is often depicted sharing his cloak with a beggar in religious art.

Etymology

From Latin 'Sanctus Martinus'; Martin from Latin 'Martinus', derived from the name of the Roman god Mars.

2. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈmɑːrtɪn / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈmɑːtɪn / (BrE)
An island in the western Leeward Islands of the Caribbean, administered jointly by France and the Netherlands.
Caribbean island split between France and the Netherlands

Examples

Many tourists visit St. Martin each year for its beautiful beaches and vibrant culture.
The island of St. Martin is unique because it is divided between two countries.
Flights to St. Martin often arrive at Princess Juliana International Airport.
