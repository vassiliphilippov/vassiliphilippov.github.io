St Mihiel

1. PROPN

/ ˌsænt miˈɛl / (AmE), / ˌsæn miˈjɛl / (BrE)
a major World War I battle in 1918, where American troops launched their first offensive in France during the Meuse-Argonne campaign
World War I battle, 1918, American offensive in France

Examples

The Battle of St Mihiel marked a turning point for American forces in World War I.
Historians often study St Mihiel to understand the development of U.S. military strategy.
My great-grandfather fought at St Mihiel as part of the American Expeditionary Forces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Meuse-Argonne offensive - major Allied offensive campaign in World War I including St Mihiel

Etymology

Named after the town of Saint-Mihiel in northeastern France, where the battle took place.
