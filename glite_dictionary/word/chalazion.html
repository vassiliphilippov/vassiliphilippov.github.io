chalazion

1. NOUN

/ kəˈleɪziən / (AmE), / kəˈleɪziən / (BrE)
a small, usually painless lump or cyst that forms on the eyelid due to a blocked Meibomian gland
eyelid cyst from blocked gland
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the chalazion on her upper eyelid and recommended warm compresses.
After a few weeks, the chalazion subsided without the need for surgery.
A chalazion can sometimes be mistaken for a stye, but it is usually less painful.

Etymology

From Greek khalazion, diminutive of khalaza (hailstone).
