defamation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛfəˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛfəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of damaging the good reputation of someone by making false statements; slander or libel.
false attack on someone's reputation
legal

Examples

She filed a lawsuit alleging defamation after the magazine published several false stories about her.
Public figures often struggle to balance free speech with protection against defamation on social media.
The court found him guilty of defamation and ordered him to pay damages to the plaintiff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slander - spoken defamation of a person
  • Synonym: libel - written defamation of a person

Etymology

From Latin 'diffamatio', from 'diffamare' (to spread abroad by report), related to 'fama' (reputation).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛfəˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛfəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of making false or malicious statements about someone in order to damage their reputation.
false statement harming reputation
legal

Examples

The company filed a lawsuit claiming defamation after the article was published.
He lost his job because of a defamation that later proved to be unfounded.
Public figures are often targets of defamation on social media.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slander - spoken false statement damaging reputation
  • Synonym: libel - written false statement damaging reputation

Etymology

From Latin 'diffamatio', from 'diffamare' (to spread abroad, defame).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06732328-n
Synset POS: n
