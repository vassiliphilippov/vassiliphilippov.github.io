Leontodon

1. NOUN

/ liˈɑːn.təˌdɑn / (AmE), / liˈɒn.təˌdɒn / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, commonly known as hawkbits, native to Europe and Asia
genus of flowering plants (hawkbits)

Examples

The meadow was filled with bright yellow Leontodon among the grass.
Botanists classified the new species under the genus Leontodon.
Some gardeners cultivate Leontodon for its cheerful flowers and adaptability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawkbit - common name for species in the Leontodon genus

Etymology

From Greek leōn (lion) + odous, odont- (tooth), referring to the toothed leaves.

a genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, commonly known as hawkbits, native to Europe, North Africa, and Asia.
genus of plants; hawkbits

Examples

Several species of Leontodon thrive in grasslands throughout Europe.
The yellow flowers of Leontodon are often mistaken for dandelions by casual observers.
Botanists examined a rare specimen of Leontodon discovered in the alpine meadow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawkbit - common name for plants in the genus Leontodon

From Greek 'leon' (lion) and 'odous' (tooth), referring to the toothed leaves of some species.
