perceptible

1. ADJ

/ pərˈsɛptəbl̩ / (AmE), / pəˈsɛptəbl̩ / (BrE)
That can be seen, heard, or noticed; able to be perceived by the senses or the mind.
able to be seen, heard, or noticed

Examples

The temperature dropped by a perceptible amount overnight.
There was a perceptible pause before she replied to the question.
The artist achieved a perceptible difference in tone between the two paintings.

Synset Examples

a perceptible limp
easily perceptible sounds
perceptible changes in behavior
a perceptible sense of expectation in the court

Related Terms

  • Antonym: imperceptible - impossible to perceive; not noticeable
  • Synonym: detectable - able to be detected or discovered

Etymology

From Late Latin 'perceptibilis', from Latin 'percipere' (to perceive).

2. ADJ

/ pərˈsɛptəbəl / (AmE), / pəˈsɛptəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be seen, noticed, or detected, especially because the difference is obvious or clear enough to sense
able to be detected or noticed

Examples

There was a perceptible change in her mood after the news.
A perceptible tremor could be felt during the earthquake.
The improvement in air quality is hardly perceptible to most people.

Synset Examples

a detectable note of sarcasm
he continued after a perceptible pause

Related Terms

  • Antonym: imperceptible - too slight or subtle to be perceived
  • Synonym: detectable - able to be discovered or identified

Etymology

From Late Latin perceptibilis (able to be perceived), from Latin percipere (to perceive).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01290284-s
Synset POS: s
