Agrobacterium

1. NOUN

/ ˌæɡroʊbækˈtɪriəm / (AmE), / ˌaɡrəʊbækˈtɪəriəm / (BrE)
A genus of small, motile rod-shaped bacteria that reduce nitrates and can cause galls on the stems of plants.
genus of rod-shaped plant-pathogenic bacteria

Examples

Agrobacterium infects many plants, often resulting in tumor-like growths on the stems.
Scientists use Agrobacterium to introduce new genes into plant cells for genetic engineering.
The presence of Agrobacterium in the soil can have significant impacts on crop health.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Greek 'agros' (field) and 'bakterion' (small rod, bacterium).

genus of plant-pathogenic bacteria
medical

Examples

Agrobacterium is commonly used in laboratories to transfer genes into plants for research purposes.
Crown gall disease in roses is often caused by bacteria of the Agrobacterium genus.
Researchers are studying how Agrobacterium infects plant cells to prevent crop diseases.

Etymology

From Greek 'agros' (field) and 'bacterium' (rod-shaped microorganism).
