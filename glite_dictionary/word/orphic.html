Orphic

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔːrfɪk / (AmE), / ˈɔːfɪk / (BrE)
Relating to Orpheus or to literature, music, or beliefs attributed to him, especially those connected with mysticism and poetry.
relating to Orpheus or works attributed to him

Examples

The philosopher delivered an Orphic vision of the universe rooted in ancient myth.
Many poets have been drawn to Orphic themes of death and rebirth.
The ceremony included Orphic hymns dedicated to the mysteries of life and the afterlife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mystical - having a spiritual or supernatural significance

Etymology

From Greek Orphikos, relating to Orpheus, the legendary musician and poet.
