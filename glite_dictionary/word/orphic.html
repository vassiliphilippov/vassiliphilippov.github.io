orphic

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔr.fɪk / (AmE), / ˈɔː.fɪk / (BrE)
Mysterious or esoteric, having a meaning that is not clear or is beyond ordinary understanding.
mysterious; beyond ordinary understanding

Examples

The ancient rites were shrouded in orphic secrecy that mystified all outsiders.
The poem's orphic symbolism attracted scholars and spiritual seekers alike.
She felt an orphic aura in the quiet forest, as if reality concealed deeper truths.

Synset Examples

mysterious symbols
the mystical style of Blake
occult lore
the secret learning of the ancients

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mysterious - difficult or impossible to understand, explain, or identify
  • Synonym: mystical - having spiritual significance or meaning that transcends human understanding
  • Antonym: obvious - easily perceived or understood; clear, self-evident

Etymology

From Greek Orphikos, relating to Orpheus, a legendary poet and musician associated with mystical doctrines.
