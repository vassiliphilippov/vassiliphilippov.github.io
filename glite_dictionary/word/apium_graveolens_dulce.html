Apium graveolens dulce

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪpiəm ɡrəˈviːəˌlɛnz ˈdʌlsi / (AmE), / ˈæpiəm ɡræˈviːəˌlɛnz ˈdʊlseɪ / (BrE)
a variety of celery widely cultivated for its aromatic leaf stalks, which are eaten raw or cooked
variety of cultivated celery

Examples

Apium graveolens dulce is commonly found in supermarkets under the name celery.
Farmers select specific strains of Apium graveolens dulce for their tender stalks and mild flavor.
Soups and salads often use Apium graveolens dulce for its crunchy texture and aromatic taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celery - a vegetable with crisp, pale green stalks often eaten raw or used in cooking

Etymology

From Latin: Apium (celery) + graveolens (strong-smelling) + dulce (sweet).
