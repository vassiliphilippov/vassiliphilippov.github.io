decreasing

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈkrisɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈkriːsɪŋ / (BrE)
becoming less in quantity, size, intensity, or degree
becoming less or smaller

Examples

The company's profits have been decreasing for three consecutive years.
Scientists observed a decreasing trend in air pollution levels.
There is a decreasing number of students enrolling in the course.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: increasing - becoming greater in quantity, size, intensity, or degree

Etymology

From the verb 'decrease', from Old French 'decreistre', from Latin 'decrescere' (to grow less).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈkriːsɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈkriːsɪŋ / (BrE)
(of tempo or volume in music) becoming gradually slower or quieter.
becoming slower or quieter (music)

Examples

The conductor signaled for a decreasing tempo as the piece neared its end.
The music faded out with a decreasing volume that left the audience in silence.
The composition features a decreasing intensity, both in speed and loudness, in the final movement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: increasing - becoming greater in tempo or volume (music)

Etymology

From the verb 'decrease', which comes from Old French 'decreistre', from Latin 'decrescere' (to grow less).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02548631-a
Synset POS: a
