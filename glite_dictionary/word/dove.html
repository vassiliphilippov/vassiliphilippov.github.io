dove

1. NOUN

/ doʊv / (AmE), / dəʊv / (BrE)
A symbol of peace, often represented by a white dove.
emblem of peace

Examples

The white dove released at the ceremony represented hope for lasting peace.
Artists often depict a dove carrying an olive branch to convey messages of harmony.
She wore a necklace with a small silver dove, a token of her beliefs in peace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peace - freedom from disturbance; tranquility

2. NOUN

/ dʌv / (AmE), / dʌv / (BrE)
A small pigeon, typically with a slender body and long tail, often symbolizing peace.
small pigeon, often a peace symbol

Examples

A white dove flew above the crowd, symbolizing hope and peace.
The garden is often visited by various species of dove.
She watched a dove build its nest outside her window.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pigeon - a bird of the same family, generally larger and stockier

Etymology

Old English dūfe (feminine of dūfa, meaning pigeon or dove).

3. NOUN

/ doʊv / (AmE), / dəʊv / (BrE)
a person who advocates peaceful or diplomatic solutions instead of military action, especially in political or international affairs
advocate of peaceful solutions in politics

Examples

During the debate, Senator Graham positioned himself as a dove regarding military interventions.
The administration is divided between doves and hawks over how to handle the crisis.
Her reputation as a dove made her popular among voters seeking an end to the conflict.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hawk - someone who advocates for military action or aggressive policies

Etymology

Metaphorical use from the bird 'dove', a symbol of peace, first recorded in U.S. political slang during the Vietnam War era.
