Dove

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʌv / (AmE), / ˈdʌv / (BrE)
A constellation in the Southern Hemisphere near Puppis and Caelum, also known as Columba.
constellation near Puppis and Caelum

Examples

The Dove constellation can be seen from southern latitudes during the summer.
Ancient astronomers named Dove to represent a symbol of peace in the sky.
From my telescope, I was able to identify Dove near the more prominent stars of Puppis.

Etymology

From Latin 'columba', meaning 'dove'; adopted as the constellation's name by astronomer Nicolas Louis de Lacaille in the 18th century.
