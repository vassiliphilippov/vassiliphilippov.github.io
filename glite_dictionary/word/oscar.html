Oscar

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑːskər / (AmE), / ˈɒskə / (BrE)
An annual award presented by the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences for excellence in motion picture production and performance.
prestigious film industry award

Examples

Winning an Oscar is considered the highest honor in the film industry.
She received her first Oscar nomination for Best Actress.
The director proudly accepted the Oscar for Best Picture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Academy Award - alternative official name for the Oscar film award

Etymology

Named after the supposedly affectionate nickname given by Academy librarian Margaret Herrick, who said the statuette resembled her Uncle Oscar; officially adopted by the Academy in 1939.
