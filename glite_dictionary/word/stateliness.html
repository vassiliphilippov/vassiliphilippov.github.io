stateliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪtlinəs / (AmE), / ˈsteɪtlinɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being dignified, majestic, or elaborate in manner or appearance
dignified or majestic manner

Examples

The queen walked through the halls with great stateliness, impressing everyone present.
He conducted the ceremony with a stateliness that set it apart from ordinary gatherings.
The stateliness of the old mansion was evident in its high ceilings and ornate decorations.

Synset Examples

she served coffee with great stateliness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grandeur - impressive beauty or splendor
  • Synonym: elegance - refined grace or dignified propriety
  • Antonym: humbleness - quality of being humble or modest

Etymology

From 'stately' + '-ness'; 'stately' from Middle English, meaning 'imposing in appearance'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪtlinəs / (AmE), / ˈsteɪtlinɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being impressive and dignified, especially in appearance or proportion
impressiveness and dignity in appearance or proportion

Examples

The stateliness of the old cathedral left visitors in awe.
Her movements had a certain stateliness that drew everyone's attention.
The stateliness of the oak trees lined along the avenue was remarkable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grandeur - impressive beauty or splendor
  • Synonym: majesty - impressive dignity or greatness
  • Antonym: plainness - lack of decoration or impressiveness

Etymology

From 'stately' + '-ness'; 'stately' from Middle English, ultimately from Anglo-French 'estat' (state, condition).
