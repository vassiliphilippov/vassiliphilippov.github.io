fervidness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɝːvɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈfɜːvɪdnəs / (BrE)
intense and passionate feeling; great enthusiasm or zeal.
intense enthusiasm or passion

Examples

The fervidness of her speech inspired everyone in the audience to action.
He admired the fervidness with which the team pursued their goals.
Despite his fervidness for the cause, he remained respectful of other opinions.

Synset Examples

he spoke with great ardor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ardor - intense passion or enthusiasm
  • Synonym: zeal - great energy or enthusiasm in pursuit of a cause
  • Antonym: apathy - lack of interest or enthusiasm

Etymology

From Latin 'fervidus' (boiling, glowing, ardent), from 'fervere' (to boil).
