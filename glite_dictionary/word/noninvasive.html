noninvasive

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑnɪnˈveɪsɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒnɪnˈveɪsɪv / (BrE)
(of a medical procedure or technique) not involving the puncturing of the skin or entry into a body cavity.
not involving penetration of the body
medical

Examples

An MRI scan is a noninvasive imaging technique commonly used in hospitals.
Laser therapy offers a noninvasive alternative to traditional surgical procedures.
The doctor recommended a noninvasive test to assess the patient's heart function.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: invasive - (of medical procedures) involving entry into the body

Etymology

Formed from Latin prefix 'non-' (not) + 'invasive' (entering, attacking).
