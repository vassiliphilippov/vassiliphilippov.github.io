Ovis

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.vɪs / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.vɪs / (BrE)
The genus of mammals that includes sheep and related species.
genus including sheep

Examples

The domestic sheep is classified as Ovis aries in the scientific community.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of several species within the genus Ovis.
Mountain-dwelling Ovis species are known for their impressive climbing abilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheep - a domesticated ruminant animal with a thick woolly coat

Etymology

From Latin *ovis* (sheep).
