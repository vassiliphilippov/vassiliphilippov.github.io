confusion

1. NOUN

/ kənˈfjuːʒən / (AmE), / kənˈfjuːʒən / (BrE)
A state of disorder caused by unpredictable or chaotic behavior.
state of disorder or chaos

Examples

The army retreated in confusion after the sudden attack.
There was confusion in the classroom when the fire alarm unexpectedly rang.
The meeting ended in confusion, with everyone talking at once.

Synset Examples

the army retreated in confusion

Related Terms

  • Antonym: order - state in which everything is arranged methodically
  • Synonym: disarray - a lack of order or organization

Etymology

From Latin 'confusio', from 'confundere' meaning 'to mingle together'.

/ kənˈfjuʒən / (AmE), / kənˈfjuːʒən / (BrE)

Examples

The laboratory experienced a confusion of chemicals, resulting in a new, unidentified substance.

Related Terms

5. NOUN

/ kənˈfjuʒən / (AmE), / kənˈfjuːʒən / (BrE)
a mistake made when one thing is wrongly taken for another, leading to misunderstandings or misidentification
mistake from taking one thing for another

Examples

There was confusion at the airport when two passengers had the same name.
The company rebranded its product to avoid confusion with a competitor.
His handwriting caused confusion when the teacher mixed up two students' assignments.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misidentification - the act of identifying someone or something incorrectly
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to understand or distinguish

Etymology

From Latin 'confusio' (a mixing together), from 'confundere' (to mix together).
