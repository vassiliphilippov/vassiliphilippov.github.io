Ardennes

1. PROPN

/ ɑrˈdɛn(z) / (AmE), / ɑːˈdɛn(z) / (BrE)
a region of wooded hills and plateaus in southeastern Belgium, Luxembourg, and northeastern France, notable for being the site of major battles in both World Wars
region of wooded plateaus in Belgium, Luxembourg, and France

Examples

The Ardennes was the scene of bitter fighting during the Battle of the Bulge in World War II.
Every summer, hikers are drawn to the scenic trails of the Ardennes.
Historians study the strategies used in the Ardennes offensive in 1944.

Etymology

From French 'Ardennes,' from Latin 'Arduenna (silva)' meaning 'Ardennes forest.'
