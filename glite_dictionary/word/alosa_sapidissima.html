Alosa sapidissima

1. NOUN

/ əˈloʊsə sæpɪˈdɪsɪmə / (AmE), / əˈləʊsə sapɪˈdɪsɪmə / (BrE)
A species of shad native to the Atlantic coast of North America, now also found on the Pacific coast.
a species of North American shad

Examples

The Alosa sapidissima has become an important species for both commercial and recreational fisheries.
Biologists are studying how Alosa sapidissima adapts to different river environments.
The introduction of Alosa sapidissima to the Pacific coast transformed local ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American shad - common name for Alosa sapidissima

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus Alosa, from Latin alausa (a kind of fish), and species epithet sapidissima meaning 'most savory' in Latin.
