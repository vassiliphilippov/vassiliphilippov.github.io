infertility

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.fərˈtɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɪn.fəˈtɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The inability to produce offspring; in women, the inability to conceive, and in men, the inability to cause conception.
inability to have children
medical

Examples

After several unsuccessful attempts to have a baby, the couple sought medical help for infertility.
Advances in reproductive technology have provided hope for those struggling with infertility.
Doctors explained that age can significantly affect a woman's infertility.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fertility - the ability to produce offspring

Etymology

From Latin infertilitas (unfruitfulness), from in- (not) + fertilis (fertile).
