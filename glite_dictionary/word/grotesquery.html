grotesquery

1. NOUN

/ ɡroʊˈtɛskəri / (AmE), / ɡrəʊˈtɛskəri / (BrE)
the quality or state of being grotesque, characterized by absurd, unnatural, or distorted features; a grotesque thing or instance
quality of being grotesque; grotesque object

Examples

The film was a parade of grotesquery, shocking the audience with each new distortion.
Modern art sometimes delights in grotesquery to challenge viewers' perceptions.
He recoiled from the grotesquery of the carnival's bizarre attractions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grotesque - strangely or absurdly distorted appearance or character

Etymology

From 'grotesque' (from French 'grotesque', from Italian 'grottesca'), with the suffix '-ery' forming nouns denoting qualities or collections.
