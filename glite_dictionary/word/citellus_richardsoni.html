Citellus richardsoni

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈtɛləs rɪˌtʃɑːrdˈsəni / (AmE), / sɪˈtɛləs rɪˌtʃɑːdˈsəni / (BrE)
A species of ground squirrel native to sagebrush and grassland areas of the western United States and Canada, also known as Richardson's ground squirrel.
Richardson's ground squirrel species

Examples

The Citellus richardsoni can often be seen standing upright in open grasslands, keeping watch for predators.
Researchers have studied the hibernation patterns of Citellus richardsoni in the prairies of Canada.
Farmers sometimes consider Citellus richardsoni a pest because of its extensive burrowing activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Richardson's ground squirrel - common name for Citellus richardsoni

Etymology

Named after Scottish explorer Sir John Richardson; from Latin *Citellus* (ground squirrel genus).
