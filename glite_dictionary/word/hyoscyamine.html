hyoscyamine

1. NOUN

/ haɪəˈskaɪəmiːn / (AmE), / haɪəˈsɪəmiːn / (BrE)
a poisonous crystalline alkaloid found in certain plants, used medically to treat gastrointestinal disorders and as an antispasmodic
a poisonous alkaloid used in medicine
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed hyoscyamine to relieve his stomach cramps.
Hyoscyamine is extracted from plants like deadly nightshade and henbane.
Excessive doses of hyoscyamine can be toxic and cause serious side effects.

Etymology

From Modern Latin hyoscyamus (the plant henbane) + -ine (chemical suffix).
