Podocarpus elongatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊdəˈkɑːrpəs ɪˌlɒŋˈɡeɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌpɒdəˈkɑːpəs ɪˌlɒŋˈɡɑːtəs / (BrE)
A South African tree or shrub with a rounded crown, commonly known as the Breede River yellowwood.
South African tree or shrub species

Examples

Podocarpus elongatus is often planted as an ornamental tree in South African gardens.
The leaves of Podocarpus elongatus are narrow and leathery, distinguishing it from other yellowwoods.
Scientists are studying Podocarpus elongatus to better understand its ecological role along riverbanks.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Podocarpus' (footed fruit) and species 'elongatus' (elongated).
