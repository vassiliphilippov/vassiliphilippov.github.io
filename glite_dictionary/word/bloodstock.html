bloodstock

1. NOUN

/ ˈblʌd.stɑk / (AmE), / ˈblʌd.stɒk / (BrE)
thoroughbred horses collectively, especially those bred for racing or breeding purposes
thoroughbred horses bred for racing

Examples

The farm is renowned for its high-quality bloodstock and championship winners.
Investors are keen to purchase bloodstock at the annual auction.
The value of bloodstock has increased significantly with rising demand for top racehorses.

Etymology

From 'blood' (hereditary lineage) + 'stock' (breeding animals), originally used in horse breeding.
