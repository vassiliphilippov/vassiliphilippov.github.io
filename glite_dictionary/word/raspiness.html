raspiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈræs.pi.nɪs / (AmE), / ˈrɑːs.pi.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of having a rough, harsh, or grating sound, especially in a voice.
rough or harsh vocal sound quality

Examples

His voice had a natural raspiness that made every word sound intense.
Many singers try to add a bit of raspiness to their vocals for emotional effect.
The morning cold gave her speech an extra raspiness she couldn't hide.

Synset Examples

The thing about Janis Joplin was that for all her raspiness, she had power and expressiveness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoarseness - rough or weak voice, usually from illness
  • Antonym: smoothness - the quality of being smooth in texture or sound

Etymology

From 'raspy' + '-ness'; 'raspy' is from 'rasp' (to scrape or grate).
