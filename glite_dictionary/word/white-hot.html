white-hot

1. ADJ

/ waɪt hɑt / (AmE), / waɪt hɒt / (BrE)
Heated to such a high temperature that it glows with a white light.
glowing white with intense heat

Examples

After hours in the forge, the metal rod was white-hot and dangerous to touch.
The argument in the room reached a white-hot intensity.
Molten steel glowed white-hot as it poured from the furnace.

Synset Examples

white flames
a white-hot center of the fire

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: red-hot - heated to the point of glowing red; extremely hot

2. ADJ

/ ˈwaɪtˌhɑt / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtˌhɒt / (BrE)
Extremely intense or passionate; showing strong or fervent feelings.
extremely intense or passionate

Examples

The protestors displayed white-hot anger at the proposed law.
Her white-hot ambition drove her to achieve every goal she set.
The writer's white-hot creativity produced three novels in one year.

Synset Examples

fierce white-hot loyalty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fervent - having or displaying passionate intensity
  • Antonym: lukewarm - moderately warm, or showing little enthusiasm

Etymology

From the literal sense of being heated until it glows white, figuratively used from the mid-19th century.
