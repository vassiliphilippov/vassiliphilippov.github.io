poudrin

1. NOUN

/ puːˈdreɪn / (AmE), / puːˈdrɛ̃ / (BrE)
A small crystal of ice, typically forming part of fine snow or frost.
small crystal of ice

Examples

Delicate poudrin sparkled on the frozen windowpane in the early morning light.
Researchers collected samples of poudrin to study the formation of frost patterns.
After the light snow, the ground was dusted with a layer of fine poudrin.

Etymology

From French 'poudrin', diminutive of 'poudre' (powder).
