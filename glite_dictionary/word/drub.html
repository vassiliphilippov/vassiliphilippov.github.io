to drub

1. VERB

/ drʌb / (AmE), / drʌb / (BrE)
to defeat someone thoroughly and decisively, especially in a competition or fight
to defeat thoroughly

Examples

The home team managed to drub their rivals with a score of 5-0.
If we don't improve our defense, they'll drub us again next weekend.
She was determined not to let him drub her in the chess tournament this year.

Synset Examples

We licked the other team on Sunday!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrash - to defeat decisively in a contest or fight
  • Synonym: to trounce - to defeat heavily in a contest
  • Antonym: to lose - to fail to win a competition

Etymology

Probably from Arabic *ḍarab* (he beat, struck), via Turkish *dövmek* (to beat).
