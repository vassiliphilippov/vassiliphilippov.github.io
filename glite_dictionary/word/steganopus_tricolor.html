Steganopus tricolor

1. NOUN

/ ˌstɛɡəˈnoʊpəs ˈtraɪkələr / (AmE), / ˌstiːɡəˈnəʊpəs ˈtrɪkəlɔː / (BrE)
A species of small shorebird, commonly known as the Wilson's phalarope, which breeds on the northern great plains of Canada.
species of shorebird (Wilson's phalarope)

Examples

The Steganopus tricolor migrates thousands of miles each year between its breeding and wintering grounds.
Researchers observed the nesting behavior of Steganopus tricolor in the prairie wetlands.
Conservationists are monitoring Steganopus tricolor populations in response to habitat changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wilson's phalarope - common name for Steganopus tricolor

Etymology

From Greek 'steganos' (covered) and 'pous' (foot), and Latin 'tricolor' (three-colored).
