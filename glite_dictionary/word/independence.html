independence

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndəˈpɛndəns / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈpɛndəns / (BrE)
the state of being free from the control or influence of others
freedom from control or influence

Examples

After years of struggle, the country finally achieved independence from colonial rule.
Living on her own gave her a strong sense of independence.
Financial independence is often considered an important milestone in adulthood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autonomy - the right or condition of self-government
  • Antonym: dependence - the state of relying on or being controlled by someone or something else

Etymology

From Latin 'independens', from 'in-' (not) + 'dependere' (to hang from).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪndɪˈpɛndəns / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈpɛndəns / (BrE)
The achievement of political freedom by a country, especially the successful ending of the American Revolution that resulted in the United States becoming independent from Britain.
achievement of political freedom, especially in US history

Examples

The signing of the Treaty of Paris in 1783 formally recognized American independence.
Many Americans celebrate the Fourth of July as the anniversary of their country's independence.
Relations with Britain changed dramatically after the colonies achieved independence.

Synset Examples

they maintained close relations with England even after independence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freedom - the state of being free, especially from political oppression
  • Antonym: subjugation - the act of bringing someone or something under control; oppression

Etymology

From Latin 'independentia', from 'in-' (not) + 'dependere' (to hang from).
