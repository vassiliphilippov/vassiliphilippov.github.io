to dry-nurse

1. VERB

/ ˈdraɪ ˌnɜrs / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ ˌnɜːs / (BrE)
to take care of an infant without breastfeeding
care for an infant without breastfeeding

Examples

For several months, she dry-nursed the child while the mother was away.
The nanny was hired to dry-nurse twins whose mother couldn't breastfeed.
Some parents choose to dry-nurse infants for various health reasons.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wet-nurse - to breastfeed and care for another's baby

Etymology

Formed by combining 'dry' (not producing milk) and 'nurse' (someone who cares for a child), contrasting with 'wet-nurse'.
