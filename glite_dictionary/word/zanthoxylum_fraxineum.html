Zanthoxylum fraxineum

1. NOUN

/ zænˈθɑksɪləm frækˈsaɪniəm / (AmE), / zanˈθɒksɪləm frækˈsiːniəm / (BrE)
A small deciduous aromatic shrub or tree with spiny branches and yellowish flowers, native to eastern North America.
aromatic shrub or tree of eastern North America

Examples

The bark of Zanthoxylum fraxineum has traditionally been used for its medicinal properties.
In spring, Zanthoxylum fraxineum blooms with small yellowish flowers along its spiny branches.
Many native gardens feature Zanthoxylum fraxineum because of its aromatic leaves and ability to attract pollinators.

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Zanthoxylum' from Greek 'xanthos' (yellow) and 'xylon' (wood); 'fraxineum' indicating ash-like (Fraxinus) characteristics.
