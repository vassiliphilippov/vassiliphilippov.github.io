peckish

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɛkɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɛkɪʃ / (BrE)
feeling slightly hungry
slightly hungry
british

Examples

I'm feeling a bit peckish; maybe I'll have a snack.
She always gets peckish around mid-afternoon at work.
We were peckish after the long walk, so we stopped for sandwiches.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: hungry - feeling a need or desire for food
  • Degree Higher: starving - extremely hungry
  • Antonym: full - having eaten to satisfaction

Etymology

Originally British slang; perhaps from 'peck' (to eat in small amounts).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɛkɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɛkɪʃ / (BrE)
slightly hungry; inclined to eat shortly before a meal or after physical activity, used especially in British English.
slightly hungry
informal british

Examples

After a busy morning, I was feeling peckish for a snack.
If you're peckish for something sweet, there are biscuits in the cupboard.
She grew peckish for lunch while finishing her work.

Synset Examples

After the long walk, she was peckish for some fruit.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: hungry - feeling the need for food
  • Degree Higher: ravenous - very hungry
  • Antonym: full - having eaten enough or too much

Etymology

From English 'peck' (to eat small amounts) + -ish (having the quality of).
