amicableness

1. NOUN

/ ˈæmɪkəblnəs / (AmE), / ˈæmɪkəblnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being friendly and not wanting to quarrel; friendliness or goodwill
friendliness; good-naturedness

Examples

Everyone appreciated her amicableness during the heated discussion.
His amicableness makes him easy to work with, even under stress.
The success of the negotiation was due to the amicableness of both parties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amicability - the quality of being friendly or agreeable
  • Antonym: hostility - unfriendliness or opposition
  • Synonym: friendliness - the quality of being friendly

Etymology

From Latin 'amicabilis' (friendly), from 'amicus' (friend).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæmɪkəblnɪs / (AmE), / ˈæmɪkəblnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being friendly and pleasant towards others.
quality of being friendly

Examples

Her amicableness made her popular among her colleagues.
The amicableness of the negotiations helped to resolve the conflict quickly.
He was admired for his amicableness and ability to get along with everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: friendliness - the quality of being friendly
  • Antonym: hostility - the quality of being unfriendly or antagonistic

Etymology

Formed from 'amicable' with the suffix '-ness', from Latin 'amicabilis' (friendly).
