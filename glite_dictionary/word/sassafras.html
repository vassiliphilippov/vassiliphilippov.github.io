Sassafras

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæsəˌfræs / (AmE), / ˈsæsəfrəs / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous trees in the laurel family, known for their aromatic bark, leaves, and roots.
genus of aromatic deciduous trees

Examples

The Sassafras genus includes several species native to North America and Asia.
Early settlers used the roots of Sassafras trees to make medicinal teas.
The aromatic leaves of Sassafras have been traditionally used in cooking and folk remedies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lauraceae - the laurel family of flowering plants

Etymology

From Spanish 'sasafrás', likely from a Native American language.
