encrustation

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˌkrʌˈsteɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌkrʌˈsteɪʃən / (BrE)
a decorative coating or layer of contrasting material applied to a surface, often as an inlay or overlay
decorative overlay or inlay layer

Examples

The jewelry box had an elegant encrustation of mother-of-pearl on its lid.
Medieval churches often feature gold encrustation on their altars and walls.
Restorers carefully cleaned the stone to preserve the original encrustation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inlay - a decoration or design set into the surface of something

Etymology

From Latin 'incrustatio', from 'incrustare' (to cover with a crust or coating).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.krəˈsteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛn.krʌsˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a hard outer layer that forms on or covers something
hard outer layer covering something

Examples

The ship's hull was covered in a thick encrustation of barnacles and algae.
An encrustation of minerals formed on the inside of the old pipe.
The jewelry was damaged by a heavy encrustation of dirt and grime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coating - a layer of a substance covering a surface

Etymology

From Latin 'incrustare' (to cover with crust).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.krəˈsteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛn.krʌˈsteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of forming a crust or a hard outer layer on a surface
formation of a crust or hard layer

Examples

The ship’s hull showed heavy encrustation after years at sea.
Mineral encrustation can often be found on the inside of old pipes.
Years of neglect led to an encrustation of salt on the abandoned equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crust - a hard outer layer or covering

Etymology

From Latin ‘incrustare’ (to cover with a crust) via French ‘encrustation’.
