adrenalectomy

1. NOUN

/ əˌdriːnəlˈɛktəmi / (AmE), / əˌdriːnəˈlɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure to remove one or both adrenal glands
surgical removal of adrenal glands
medical

Examples

The patient recovered quickly after undergoing an adrenalectomy to remove his tumor.
A bilateral adrenalectomy is needed if both adrenal glands are affected.
Doctors may recommend an adrenalectomy in cases of severe adrenal disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nephrectomy - surgical removal of a kidney
  • Synonym: appendectomy - surgical removal of the appendix

Etymology

From adrenal (referring to the adrenal glands) + -ectomy (from Greek ektomē, meaning 'excision')
