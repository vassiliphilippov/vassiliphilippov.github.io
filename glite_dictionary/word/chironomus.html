Chironomus

1. NOUN

/ kɛrəˈnoʊməs / (AmE), / kɪrəˈnəʊməs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Chironomidae, consisting of non-biting midges often found near water.
genus of non-biting midges

Examples

Many Chironomus species are important in freshwater ecosystems as food for fish.
The larvae of Chironomus are commonly known as bloodworms due to their red color.
Researchers studied Chironomus populations to assess water quality in the lake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chironomidae - family of flies commonly known as non-biting midges

Etymology

From Greek 'kheironomos' (one who gesticulates with the hands), referring to the midge's motion.

2. NOUN

/ kɪˈrɑːnəməs / (AmE), / kɪˈrɒnəməs / (BrE)
A genus of non-biting midges, serving as the type genus of the family Chironomidae.
genus of non-biting midges

Examples

The larvae of Chironomus are often found in freshwater habitats worldwide.
Scientists studied the life cycle of Chironomus to understand aquatic ecosystems.
Some species within Chironomus are used as indicators of water quality.

Etymology

From Greek kheirōnos (a kind of gnat) + Latin genus.
