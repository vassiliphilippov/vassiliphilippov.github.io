broadband

1. ADJ

/ ˈbrɔdˌbænd / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːd.bænd / (BrE)
(of a system, signal, or device) responding to or operating at a wide range of frequencies, especially in telecommunications and electronics.
operating over a wide frequency range

Examples

The engineer installed a broadband antenna to improve the range of frequencies received.
Modern communication systems often depend on broadband technology for faster data transmission.
A broadband amplifier can process signals from many different channels simultaneously.

Synset Examples

a broadband antenna

Related Terms

  • Antonym: narrowband - operating over a narrow range of frequencies

Etymology

Formed from 'broad' + 'band', referring to a wide bandwidth.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02681743-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈbrɔdˌbænd / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːdˌbænd / (BrE)
relating to a communications system or network that allows multiple simultaneous signals or a wide range of frequencies, enabling high-speed data transmission
high-capacity communication network

Examples

Many households rely on broadband connections for fast internet access.
The government is investing in rural broadband infrastructure to improve connectivity.
High-definition video streaming is possible thanks to broadband technology.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: narrowband - a communications network with a small range of frequencies, allowing fewer simultaneous signals

Etymology

From 'broad' (wide) + 'band' (range of frequencies), referring to the wide bandwidth used in these networks.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02681907-a
Synset POS: a
