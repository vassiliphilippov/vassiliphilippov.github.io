cure

1. VERB

/ kjʊr / (AmE), / kjʊə / (BrE)
to make someone healthy again by eliminating a disease or medical condition
make healthy again; eliminate disease

Examples

Modern medicine can often cure many illnesses that were once fatal.
They hope to cure her of the infection before it spreads further.
The new drug proved effective and quickly cured most patients.

Synset Examples

The treatment cured the boy's acne
The quack pretended to heal patients but never managed to

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to heal - to make or become healthy again after an injury or illness

Etymology

From Latin 'curare' (to care for).

2. VERB

/ kjʊr / (AmE), / kjʊə / (BrE)
to cause a material, such as resin, cement, or soap, to harden and become stable, often by drying, heating, or chemical reaction
harden or stabilize a material

Examples

The adhesive needs to cure for 24 hours before you can use the product.
If you don't properly cure the concrete, it may crack over time.
Exposure to sunlight will cure the resin, resulting in a solid and durable finish.

Synset Examples

cure resin
cure cement
cure soap

Etymology

From Latin curare (to care for), but meaning evolved in technical contexts.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00529015-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kjʊr / (AmE), / kjʊə / (BrE)
(of food) to be or become preserved, especially by drying, salting, or smoking.
become preserved by drying, salting, etc.

Examples

The apricots cure in the sun for several days before packaging.
In warmer climates, fish are often left out to cure naturally.
It takes several weeks for the ham to properly cure in the smokehouse.

Synset Examples

the apricots cure in the sun

Related Terms

Etymology

From Latin 'curare' (to take care of).

5. NOUN

/ kjʊr / (AmE), / kjʊə / (BrE)
A medicine, treatment, or therapy that makes an illness go away or relieves pain.
medicine or treatment that cures

Examples

Scientists are searching for a cure for cancer.
The new cure was approved after rigorous testing.
Many believe that laughter is the best cure for stress.

Related Terms

Etymology
