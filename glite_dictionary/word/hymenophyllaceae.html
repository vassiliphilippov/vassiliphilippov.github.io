Hymenophyllaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪmɪnəˌfɪliˈeɪsi.i / (AmE), / ˌhɪmɪnəˌfɪliˈeɪsɪ.iː / (BrE)
A family of chiefly tropical ferns, commonly known as filmy ferns, characterized by thin, delicate fronds and often found in moist or epiphytic habitats.
family of tropical filmy ferns
medical

Examples

The Hymenophyllaceae are known for their delicately thin leaves that resemble sheets of cellophane.
Many species in the Hymenophyllaceae family thrive as epiphytes in cloud forests.
Botanists study Hymenophyllaceae to understand adaptations to moist, shaded environments.

Etymology

From New Latin, named after the type genus Hymenophyllum, from Greek hymen (membrane) + phyllon (leaf).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪmɪnoʊˌfɪliˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌhɪmɪnəˌfɪlɪˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of mostly tropical ferns known as filmy ferns, which are either terrestrial or epiphytic and have delicate, translucent fronds.
a family of filmy ferns

Examples

Many species in the Hymenophyllaceae are prized by enthusiasts for their delicate, translucent leaves.
Scientists discovered a rare member of the Hymenophyllaceae family growing on the rainforest canopy.
The Hymenophyllaceae thrive in humid environments, often clinging to tree trunks or rocks.

Etymology

From Greek hymen (membrane) + phyllon (leaf), referring to the thin, membranous fronds.
