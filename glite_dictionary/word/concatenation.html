concatenation

1. NOUN

/ kɑnˌkætəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌkætɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a series of events, ideas, or things that are connected or linked together in a chain-like sequence
series of connected things or events

Examples

The concatenation of unfortunate events led to the failure of the project.
The mystery novel relies on a clever concatenation of clues to build suspense.
A strange concatenation of coincidences brought them together that evening.

Synset Examples

the chain of command
a complicated concatenation of circumstances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sequence - a particular order in which things follow each other
  • Synonym: chain - a connected series of things or events

Etymology

From Latin 'concatenatio', from 'concatenare' (to link together), from 'con-' (together) + 'catena' (chain).
