cogitation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːdʒəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒdʒɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
careful thought, consideration, or meditation on a subject
thoughtful consideration or meditation

Examples

After much cogitation, she finally reached a decision about her career.
His face was drawn in deep cogitation as he pondered the complex problem.
The proposal deserves careful cogitation before any final judgment is made.

Synset Examples

after much cogitation he rejected the offer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reflection - serious thought or consideration
  • Synonym: meditation - the act of thinking deeply or focusing one's mind

Etymology

From Latin cogitatio (thinking, reflection), from cogitare (to think).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːdʒɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒdʒɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
A carefully considered thought or the act of deep thinking about something.
careful, deep thought

Examples

After much cogitation, she decided to accept the job offer.
His nightly cogitation helped him solve many complex problems.
The philosopher's cogitation on existence was both profound and inspiring.

Synset Examples

his cogitations were dutifully recorded in his daybook

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reflection - serious thought or consideration
  • Synonym: meditation - the process of thinking deeply or focusing the mind
  • Antonym: impulse - a sudden, unreflective urge or desire to act

Etymology

From Latin cogitatio, from cogitare (to think).
