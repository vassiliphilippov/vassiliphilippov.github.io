overstrain

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərˈstreɪn / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈstreɪn / (BrE)
excessive strain or stress, especially resulting in damage or fatigue.
excessive strain; overexertion

Examples

Too much overstrain can lead to injury in athletes.
The doctor warned him that mental overstrain was affecting his health.
Years of overstrain at work took a toll on her well-being.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: strain - physical or mental stress or force

Etymology

From over- (excessively) + strain (from Old French 'estreindre', from Latin 'stringere', to draw tight).

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈstreɪn / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈstreɪn / (BrE)
to stretch or exert (something or oneself) excessively, often to the point of damage or exhaustion.
strain or exert excessively

Examples

If you overstrain your back while lifting, you may injure yourself.
The old bridge collapsed after being overstrained by heavy traffic.
She tends to overstrain herself by taking on too many responsibilities at once.

Synset Examples

He overextended himself when he accepted the additional assignment

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: strain - to stretch or exert something to its limit

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (meaning excessively) + 'strain'.

Synset POS: v
