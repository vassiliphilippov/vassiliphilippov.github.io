cenotaph

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnəˌtæf / (AmE), / ˈsɛnətɑːf / (BrE)
A monument erected in honor of a person or group of people whose remains are elsewhere or cannot be recovered.
monument for those buried elsewhere

Examples

The public gathered at the cenotaph to commemorate the fallen soldiers.
A striking cenotaph stands in the center of the memorial park.
Every year, wreaths are laid at the cenotaph during the Remembrance Day ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tomb - a structure for burying the dead

Etymology

From Greek 'kenos' (empty) + 'taphos' (tomb).
