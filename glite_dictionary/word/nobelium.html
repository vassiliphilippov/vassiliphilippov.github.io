nobelium

1. NOUN

/ noʊˈbɛliəm / (AmE), / nəʊˈbiːliəm / (BrE)
a radioactive transuranic chemical element with the symbol No and atomic number 102, synthesized in laboratories
radioactive chemical element (No, atomic number 102)

Examples

Scientists first produced nobelium by bombarding curium with carbon ions in a laboratory.
The atomic number of nobelium is 102, making it one of the heavier synthetic elements.
Because nobelium is highly radioactive, it has limited practical applications outside of research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: element - a substance that cannot be chemically broken down into simpler substances
  • Synonym: curium - a radioactive transuranic element used to synthesize nobelium

Etymology

Named after Alfred Nobel, the Swedish chemist and inventor.
