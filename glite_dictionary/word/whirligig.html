whirligig

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːlɪˌɡɪɡ / (AmE), / ˈwɜːlɪɡɪɡ / (BrE)
a large, rotating machine with seats for children to ride on, typically found at fairs or amusement parks.
rotating amusement ride for children

Examples

The children laughed as the whirligig spun rapidly at the fairground.
As a child, her favorite ride was always the brightly painted whirligig.
A line formed quickly for the old-fashioned whirligig at the village festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carousel - a rotating amusement ride with seats, often horses, for children

Etymology

From Middle English whirlegigg, from whirl (to spin) + gig (a top or playful device).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːlɪˌɡɪɡ / (AmE), / ˈwɜːlɪɡɪɡ / (BrE)
a child's toy that spins rapidly on a point when set in motion, typically shaped like a cone and sometimes called a top
spinning child's toy; top

Examples

The children took turns spinning the brightly colored whirligig in the living room.
He won a wooden whirligig from the carnival's game booth.
With a quick twist of the string, the whirligig spun swiftly across the floor.

Synset Examples

he got a bright red top and string for his birthday

Related Terms

  • Synonym: top - a child's toy that spins on a point; synonym for 'whirligig' in this sense

Etymology

From Middle English 'whirligig', from 'whirlen' (to whirl) + 'gigge' (top, toy).
