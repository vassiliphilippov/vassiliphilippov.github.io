strum

1. VERB

/ strʌm / (AmE), / strʌm / (BrE)
to play a stringed instrument by sweeping the fingers or a pick across its strings, usually in a rhythmic way
play by sweeping fingers across strings

Examples

She began to strum her guitar quietly in the corner of the room.
He loves to strum chords while singing his favorite songs.
The musician strummed a lively tune that filled the café with energy.

Synset Examples

strum a guitar

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pluck - to play a stringed instrument by pulling and releasing the strings individually
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02184806-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ strʌm / (AmE), / strʌm / (BrE)
the sound produced by strumming a musical instrument, especially a guitar
sound of strumming (e.g., guitar)

Examples

The gentle strum of the guitar filled the quiet room.
She awoke to the strum of a street musician's banjo outside her window.
Every strum resonated with emotion during his heartfelt performance.

Synset Examples

the strum of a guitar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strumming - the act or sound of playing an instrument by sweeping the fingers across the strings
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07410720-n
Synset POS: n
