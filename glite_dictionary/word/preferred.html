preferred

1. ADJ

/ prɪˈfɝrd / (AmE), / prɪˈfɜːd / (BrE)
regarded with special favor or partiality above others
treated as more desirable than others

Examples

Out of all applicants, he was the preferred candidate for the job.
Chocolate is my preferred flavor of ice cream.
She was always the preferred child in the family, receiving more attention than her siblings.

Synset Examples

the favored child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: favored - regarded with special preference
  • Antonym: disfavored - regarded with less or no preference

2. ADJ

/ prɪˈfɜrd / (AmE), / prɪˈfɜːd / (BrE)
Regarded with favor or chosen as more desirable than others.
more desirable; favored

Examples

She always orders her preferred dish at the restaurant, never trying anything new.
The preferred method of communication in this office is by email.
He wrote his preferred first name, 'Alex', on the form.

Synset Examples

coffee is preferable to tea
Danny's preferred name is ‘Dan’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preferable - more suitable or desirable
  • Antonym: undesirable - not wanted or desirable

Etymology

Past participle of 'prefer', from Latin praeferrere (to carry before, to prefer).
