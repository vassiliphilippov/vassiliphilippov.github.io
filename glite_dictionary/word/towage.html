towage

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtəʊɪdʒ / (BrE)
the act or process of towing or hauling something, especially by means of a rope, chain, or hitch
the act of towing or hauling

Examples

The company charges a fee for towage of disabled vehicles from the highway.
The port authorities regulate all towage operations within the harbor.
Special vessels are designed solely for towage of large ships.

Synset Examples

the truck gave him a tow to the garage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: towing - the act of pulling a vehicle or object behind another

Etymology

From Old English 'tōwan' (to pull) + -age (forming nouns).
