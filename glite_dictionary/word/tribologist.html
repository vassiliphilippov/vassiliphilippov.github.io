tribologist

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈbɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / traɪˈbɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a scientist or engineer who specializes in tribology, the study of friction, wear, and lubrication between surfaces in relative motion
specialist in the study of friction and lubrication

Examples

After graduating with a degree in mechanical engineering, she aspired to become a leading tribologist in the automotive industry.
A tribologist can help evaluate which lubricant is best for minimizing machinery wear.
The conference brought together every prominent tribologist in the field to discuss the latest research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engineer - a person who designs, builds, or maintains engines, machines, or structures
  • Synonym: scientist - a person who is studying or has expert knowledge of one or more natural or physical sciences

Etymology

From tribology, from Greek 'tribos' (rubbing) + English '-logist' (specialist).
