coenobitic

1. ADJ

/ siːnəˈbɪtɪk / (AmE), / siːnəˈbɪtɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of cenobites or their practices of communal living, especially in a monastic context.
communal monastic living

Examples

Many early monastic communities followed a coenobitic lifestyle, living and praying together under a shared rule.
The coenobitic traditions emphasized discipline and cooperation among the monks.
Unlike hermits, coenobitic monks share meals and daily routines within the monastery.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: eremitic - Relating to the practice of living in solitude, as a hermit

Etymology

From Late Latin coenobiticus, from Greek koinobion (communal life).
