asterisk

1. VERB

/ ˈæstɚˌɪsk / (AmE), / ˈæstərɪsk / (BrE)
to mark with an asterisk (*)
to mark with an asterisk

Examples

The editor decided to asterisk all corrected sections of the manuscript.
Please asterisk any items that require further discussion at the meeting.
In the appendix, the authors asterisked every dubious source.

Synset Examples

Linguists star unacceptable sentences

Related Terms

  • Synonym: star - to mark with a star symbol (in linguistics, to indicate unacceptability)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00511950-v

2. NOUN

/ ˈæstərɪsk / (AmE), / ˈastərɪsk / (BrE)
a symbol (*) used in writing and printing, typically to indicate a footnote, omission, or special meaning
star-shaped symbol (*) in writing or printing

Examples

Please add an asterisk to indicate the required fields.
The author used an asterisk to refer the reader to a note at the bottom of the page.
If you see an asterisk next to a word, it means there's additional information.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: star - a symbol shaped like a star; sometimes used for similar typographical symbols

Etymology

From Greek 'asteriskos' (little star), from 'aster' (star).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06841439-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈæstərɪsk / (AmE), / ˈæstərɪsk / (BrE)
to mark (text or items) with an asterisk symbol to indicate importance or call attention to something
mark with an asterisk symbol

Examples

Please asterisk any items that require immediate action.
He asterisked all the key points in his presentation notes.
While reviewing the contract, she began asterisking sections she wanted to discuss further.

Synset Examples

Don't forget to asterisk the important points in your notes.

Etymology

From Greek 'asteriskos' (little star), from 'astēr' (star).
