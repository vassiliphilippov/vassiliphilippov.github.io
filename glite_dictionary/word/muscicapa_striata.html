Muscicapa striata

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌsɪˈkeɪpə straɪˈɑːtə / (AmE), / ˌmʌsɪˈkɑːpə strɪˈɑːtə / (BrE)
A common European woodland flycatcher with greyish-brown plumage.
European woodland flycatcher species

Examples

The Muscicapa striata is known for its agile flight while catching insects in midair.
Many birdwatchers travel to Europe in hopes of spotting the elusive Muscicapa striata.
The woodland habitat provides the perfect environment for the Muscicapa striata to thrive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotted flycatcher - common name for Muscicapa striata

Etymology

From Latin: 'muscicapa' (flycatcher) + 'striata' (striped).
