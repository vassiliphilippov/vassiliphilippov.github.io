to realize

1. VERB

/ ˈriːəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈrɪəlaɪz / (BrE)
(in baroque music) To expand or complete a part by supplying the harmonies indicated in the figured bass.
supply harmonies in figured bass (music)

Examples

The harpsichordist skillfully realized the complex figured bass in the Bach cantata.
Students are required to realize the basso continuo for their music theory assignment.
It was common practice for musicians to realize only the chords suggested by the composer.

Etymology

From French réaliser (to make real), ultimately from Late Latin realis.
