Tulipa armena

1. NOUN

/ tuˈliːpə ɑrˈmiːnə / (AmE), / tjuːˈliːpə ɑːˈmiːnə / (BrE)
A species of small, early-blooming tulip native to the Caucasus and surrounding regions, often cultivated for its ornamental flowers.
a species of early-blooming tulip

Examples

The botanical garden displayed a rare specimen of Tulipa armena in full bloom.
She specializes in cultivating Tulipa armena, which flourish in early spring.
Many collectors value Tulipa armena for its vibrant, early-season flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tulip - a bulbous spring-flowering plant of the genus Tulipa

Etymology

From the genus name Tulipa (Latin adaptation of Turkish 'tülbent', turban) and specific epithet armena (Latin, referring to Armenia).
