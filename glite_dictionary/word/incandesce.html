to incandesce

1. VERB

/ ˌɪnkænˈdɛs / (AmE), / ˌɪnkænˈdɛs / (BrE)
to glow or become incandescent with heat
to glow or become incandescent

Examples

The metal began to incandesce as the furnace temperature rose.
In certain light bulbs, a filament incandesces to produce visible light.
If heated enough, charcoal will incandesce in the darkness.

Synset Examples

the lamp was incandesced

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glow - emit a steady light without flame
  • Antonym: dim - to become or make less bright

Etymology

From Latin 'incandescere', meaning 'to become white hot'.

2. VERB

/ ˌɪn.kænˈdɛs / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kænˈdɛs / (BrE)
to become incandescent or glow with heat
to glow or emit light with heat

Examples

The metal began to incandesce as the heat increased in the furnace.
Certain minerals incandesce vividly when exposed to a blowtorch flame.
The coals incandesced softly in the dim light of the fire pit.

Synset Examples

an incandescing body

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glow - to emit a steady light; to shine brightly without flame
  • Degree Lower: sparkle - to shine with flashes of light; to emit sparks

Etymology

From Latin incandescere, from in- (in, upon) + candescere (to become white or glow).
