eardrop

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪrˌdrɑp / (AmE), / ˈɪəˌdrɒp / (BrE)
an earring that has a pendant or dangling ornament
dangling earring with a pendant

Examples

She wore a sparkling eardrop that caught the light beautifully at the party.
Antique eardrops are often passed down as family heirlooms.
His gift to her was a pair of gold eardrops set with small rubies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stud - an earring that fits closely to the earlobe without dangling

Etymology

From 'ear' + 'drop', describing the way the ornament 'drops' from the ear.
