Sculptor

1. PROPN

/ ˈskʌlptər / (AmE), / ˈskʌlptə / (BrE)
a faint constellation in the Southern Hemisphere located near Phoenix and Cetus
constellation in Southern Hemisphere

Examples

The Sculptor constellation is best viewed from the Southern Hemisphere during the spring months.
Astronomers have studied several galaxies located within the boundaries of Sculptor.
Due to its faintness, Sculptor is difficult to spot with the naked eye.

Etymology

Named by the French astronomer Nicolas Louis de Lacaille as 'Apparatus Sculptoris' (Latin for 'sculptor's studio'), later shortened to 'Sculptor'.
