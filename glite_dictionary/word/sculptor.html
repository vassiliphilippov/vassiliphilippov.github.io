sculptor

1. NOUN

/ ˈskʌlptər / (AmE), / ˈskʌlptə / (BrE)
An artist who makes sculptures, usually by carving or shaping materials such as stone, metal, or wood.
artist who makes sculptures

Examples

The sculptor spent years perfecting the marble statue in his studio.
Modern art museums often feature works by renowned sculptors from around the world.
As a child, she dreamed of becoming a sculptor and working with bronze.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sculptress - female sculptor (historical/archaic usage)
  • Antonym: painter - an artist who paints pictures

Etymology

From Latin 'sculptor' (carver, engraver).
