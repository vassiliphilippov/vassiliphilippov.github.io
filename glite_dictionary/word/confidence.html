confidence

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.fɪ.dəns / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.fɪ.dəns / (BrE)
a private and trusting relationship in which secrets or private matters are shared
trusting relationship for sharing secrets

Examples

She took me into her confidence and told me about her plans.
Over the years, a deep confidence developed between the two friends.
He always keeps my secrets whenever I share something in confidence with him.

Synset Examples

he took me into his confidence
he betrayed their trust

Etymology

From Latin *confidentia* (firmly trusting, assurance).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.fɪ.dəns / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.fɪ.dəns / (BrE)
A feeling or belief that things will turn out well; a hopeful expectation of favorable outcomes.
hopeful expectation of good outcomes

Examples

The unexpected recovery of the stock market restored confidence in the global economy.
Her confidence in her team's abilities inspired everyone to work harder.
There is growing confidence that the country's future will be prosperous.

Synset Examples

public confidence in the economy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trust - firm belief in the reliability or truth of someone or something
  • Antonym: doubt - a feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction

Etymology

From Latin 'confidentia' (firmly trusting), from 'confidere' (to have full trust).
