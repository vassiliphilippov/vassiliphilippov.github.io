Sir Roger Bannister

1. PROPN

/ sɜr ˈrɑdʒər ˈbænɪstər / (AmE), / sə ˈrɒdʒə ˈbænɪstə / (BrE)
(1929–2018) English athlete who, in 1954, became the first person to run a mile in under four minutes.
British runner; first to break four-minute mile

Examples

Sir Roger Bannister broke the four-minute mile barrier, inspiring generations of athletes.
Many regard Sir Roger Bannister's achievement as one of the greatest moments in sports history.
In 1954, Sir Roger Bannister became a household name thanks to his remarkable running feat.
