Hellespont

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛlɪˌspɒnt / (AmE), / ˈhɛlɪspɒnt / (BrE)
the narrow strait between the Aegean Sea and the Sea of Marmara, separating European Turkey from Asian Turkey
strait between Aegean and Sea of Marmara

Examples

The ancient Greeks believed the Hellespont was crossed by the Persian army under Xerxes.
Legend says that Leander swam across the Hellespont every night to reach his beloved Hero.
Strategically controlling the Hellespont has long been vital for empires in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dardanelles - modern name for the Hellespont strait

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Hellespontos, meaning 'Sea of Helle'.
