Coeloglossum viride

1. NOUN

/ ˌsiːoʊˈɡlɑːsəm ˈvɪrɪdeɪ / (AmE), / ˌsiːələˈɡlɒsəm ˈvɪrɪdiː / (BrE)
A species of orchid with hooded, long-bracted green to yellow-green flowers often tinged with purple.
a species of orchid

Examples

The alpine meadow was dotted with Coeloglossum viride in early summer.
Botanists were excited to discover a patch of Coeloglossum viride near the forest edge.
Rare in some regions, Coeloglossum viride thrives in moist, sheltered grasslands.

Etymology

From Greek 'koilos' (hollow) + 'glossa' (tongue), and Latin 'viride' (green).
