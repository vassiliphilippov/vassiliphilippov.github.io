mausoleum

1. NOUN

/ mɔːsoʊˈliːəm / (AmE), / mɔːsəˈliːəm / (BrE)
A large, stately building housing a tomb or tombs, typically above ground.
large above-ground tomb

Examples

The royal family was entombed in a magnificent mausoleum overlooking the river.
The ancient city is famous for its grand mausoleum built for the emperor.
Many tourists visit the mausoleum each year to pay their respects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tomb - a vault or chamber for burial
  • Synonym: crypt - an underground burial vault

Etymology

From Latin 'mausoleum', from Greek 'Mausōleion', the monumental tomb of Mausolus, a Persian satrap.
