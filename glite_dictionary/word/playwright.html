playwright

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈpleɪ.raɪt / (BrE)
a person who writes plays for the theatre
person who writes plays

Examples

The playwright spent years perfecting his new drama before it opened on Broadway.
Shakespeare is considered by many to be the greatest playwright in English literature.
Aspiring writers attended the workshop to learn from a famous playwright.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dramatist - a person who writes plays

Etymology

From play (theatrical piece) + -wright (Old English wryhta 'worker, maker').
