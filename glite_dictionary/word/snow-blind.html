snow-blind

1. ADJ

/ ˈsnoʊˌblaɪnd / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊˌblaɪnd / (BrE)
Temporarily unable to see or experiencing impaired vision due to excessive exposure to light reflected by snow or ice.
temporarily blinded by snow-reflected light
medical

Examples

After hiking all day without sunglasses, he became snow-blind and had to rest in the cabin.
The climbers were warned about the risk of becoming snow-blind at high altitudes.
She wore protective goggles to avoid getting snow-blind during the expedition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snow blindness - temporary vision loss from reflected snow glare

Etymology

Formed from 'snow' + 'blind', referring to blindness caused by snow glare.

2. VERB

/ ˈsnoʊˌblaɪnd / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊˌblaɪnd / (BrE)
(of the sun or bright snow) to cause someone to be temporarily blinded or to impair vision due to reflected sunlight from snow.
to cause temporary blindness from snow glare
medical

Examples

The intense sunlight at high altitudes can snow-blind inexperienced climbers.
Without proper eye protection, it is easy to get snow-blinded on a bright winter day.
She wore dark goggles so the sun wouldn't snow-blind her during the expedition.

Synset Examples

the glare of the sun snow-blinded her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dazzle - to blind temporarily by bright light
