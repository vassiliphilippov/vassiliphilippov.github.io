Hordeum pusillum

1. NOUN

/ hɔrˈdiːəm pjuˈsɪləm / (AmE), / hɔːˈdiːəm pjuːˈsɪləm / (BrE)
A species of annual barley native to western North America and widespread in the southern United States and tropical America.
a species of annual barley

Examples

Hordeum pusillum is often found in prairies and open fields throughout the southern United States.
Botanists have documented the spread of Hordeum pusillum in disturbed soils.
Farmers sometimes encounter Hordeum pusillum as a wild barley species among other grasses.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Hordeum' meaning 'barley' and 'pusillum' meaning 'very small' or 'insignificant'.
