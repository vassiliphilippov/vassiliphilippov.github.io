screech

1. NOUN

/ skritʃ / (AmE), / skriːtʃ / (BrE)
a loud, high-pitched, and piercing cry or sound
sharp, piercing cry or sound

Examples

The distant screech of an owl echoed through the forest at night.
There was a sudden screech as the car's brakes were slammed.
Her screech startled everyone in the quiet library.

Synset Examples

her screaming attracted the neighbors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shriek - a loud, sharp, high-pitched cry
  • Synonym: scream - a loud, high-pitched, and usually long cry
  • Antonym: whisper - a soft or low voice, the opposite of making a loud sound

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound, first recorded in Middle English.

2. NOUN

/ skritʃ / (AmE), / skriːtʃ / (BrE)
a loud, high-pitched, harsh sound resembling a human scream
high, harsh, piercing noise

Examples

A sudden screech from the brakes startled everyone on the bus.
The barn owl let out a chilling screech in the darkness.
Children ran away at the screech of the microphone feedback.

Synset Examples

he ducked at the screechings of shells
he heard the scream of the brakes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shriek - a loud, sharp, high-pitched cry or sound
  • Antonym: whisper - a soft or low sound produced by speaking very quietly

Etymology

Imitative origin, resembling the sound itself; related to 'screak' and 'shriek'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07407761-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ skritʃ / (AmE), / skriːtʃ / (BrE)
to make a loud, harsh, and usually high-pitched cry or sound
make a loud, harsh cry

Examples

The owl began to screech loudly in the middle of the night.
She screeched when she saw the spider on her pillow.
The car screeched to a halt as it approached the intersection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shriek - to utter a high-pitched piercing sound or words, especially as an expression of terror, pain, or excitement
  • Antonym: murmur - a soft, indistinct sound made by a person or group of people speaking quietly or at a distance

Etymology

Probably imitative in origin, reflecting the harsh sound.

Related Terms
