Accipiter

1. NOUN

/ ækˈsɪpɪtər / (AmE), / ækˈsɪpɪtə / (BrE)
A genus of birds of prey in the family Accipitridae, including hawks and goshawks.
genus of hawks/goshawks

Examples

The Accipiter genus includes several species of hawks and goshawks found worldwide.
Researchers studied the hunting patterns typical of Accipiter species in forested habitats.
The sharp-shinned hawk is a well-known member of the Accipiter genus.

Etymology

From Latin *accipiter* (hawk).

2. NOUN

/ ækˈsɪpɪtər / (AmE), / ækˈsɪpɪtə / (BrE)
A genus of birds of prey in the family Accipitridae, comprising the typical hawks or goshawks.
genus of hawks in Accipitridae

Examples

The Accipiter genus includes well-known species such as the goshawk and the sparrowhawk.
In North America, several Accipiter species are commonly observed during migration season.
Researchers studied the hunting techniques of various Accipiter members in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawk - bird of prey with sharp talons and beak

Etymology

From Latin *accipiter* (hawk, bird of prey).
