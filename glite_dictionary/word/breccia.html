breccia

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɛtʃiə / (AmE), / ˈbrɛtʃɪə / (BrE)
A rock composed of sharp, angular fragments of minerals or rocks cemented together by a finer material such as clay or sand.
rock of angular fragments cemented together

Examples

The geologist collected a sample of breccia from the canyon wall.
The road was built using crushed breccia because of its strength and durability.
Layers of volcanic breccia indicate past explosive eruptions in the region.

Etymology

From Italian 'breccia' (broken stone, rubble).
