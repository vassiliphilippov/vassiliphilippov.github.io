ascosporous

1. ADJ

/ ˌæskəˈspɔrəs / (AmE), / ˌæskəˈspɔːrəs / (BrE)
relating to or producing ascospores (spores formed inside an ascus, characteristic of certain fungi)
relating to ascospores
medical

Examples

Many ascosporous fungi play important roles in the decomposition of organic matter.
The ascosporous structures were visible under the microscope after staining.
Researchers observed the formation of ascosporous cells during spore development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ascospore - a spore contained in an ascus (in fungi)

Etymology

From ascospore + -ous, modelled on related biological terms.
