ulatophia

1. NOUN

/ juːˌlæˈtrəʊfiə / (AmE), / juːˌlæˈtrəʊfiə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which the gums recede or withdraw from the teeth, exposing the roots.
recession of the gums
medical

Examples

The dentist diagnosed him with ulatophia after noticing significant gum recession during the check-up.
If left untreated, ulatophia can lead to tooth sensitivity and potential tooth loss.
Regular dental hygiene can help prevent the progression of ulatophia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gingival recession - Retraction of the gum tissue exposing tooth roots
