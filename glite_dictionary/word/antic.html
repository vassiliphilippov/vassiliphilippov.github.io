antic

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.tɪk / (AmE), / ˈæn.tɪk / (BrE)
a playful, silly, or grotesque act done for entertainment or amusement
playful or silly act for entertainment

Examples

The children laughed at the clown's antics during the birthday party.
Her outrageous antics made everyone in the office smile despite the stressful day.
Tired of the puppy's constant antics, he finally put away his favorite shoes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prank - a trick played for amusement or mischief

Etymology

From Italian *antico* (antique, odd), referring to strange or grotesque gestures in theatrical performances.

2. ADJ

/ ˈæn.tɪk / (AmE), / ˈæn.tɪk / (BrE)
strangely funny or bizarre in a way that is grotesque or ludicrously odd
grotesquely odd; bizarre

Examples

His antic behavior at the party left everyone both amused and baffled.
The actor adopted an antic appearance to play the mad king.
Children wore antic masks for the school’s quirky costume contest.

Synset Examples

Hamlet's assumed antic disposition
fantastic Halloween costumes
a grotesque reflection in the mirror

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grotesque - comically or repulsively ugly or distorted
  • Synonym: fantastic - strange or fanciful; bizarre

Etymology

From Italian *antico* (antique), originally used of grotesque figures found in ancient ruins.

Source: WordNet
