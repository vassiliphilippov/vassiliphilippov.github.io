curtsy

1. VERB

/ ˈkɜrtsi / (AmE), / ˈkɜːtsi / (BrE)
to lower the body slightly by bending the knees, usually as a polite greeting or sign of respect, especially by women
to bend the knees as a respectful greeting

Examples

The queen smiled as the little girl curtsied politely before her.
She practiced how to curtsy gracefully for the ceremony.
When introduced to the ambassador, Anne curtsied as a sign of respect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bow - to bend forward as a gesture of respect or greeting

Etymology

From earlier curtsy (noun), a variant of courtesy, influenced by the gesture of respect.

2. VERB

/ ˈkɜːrtsi / (AmE), / ˈkɜːtsi / (BrE)
to lower your body briefly by bending your knees, with one foot forward, as a traditional gesture of respect by women and girls
make a respectful bow by bending knees (by women)

Examples

The little girl curtsied politely before leaving the stage.
When introduced to the ambassador, she curtsied gracefully.
She practiced curtsying in front of the mirror before the ceremony.

Synset Examples

She curtsied when she shook the Queen's hand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bow - to bend the body forward as a sign of respect

Etymology

From Middle English 'courteisie' (courtesy), influenced by gesture of respect.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00901115-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɝːtsi / (AmE), / ˈkɜːtsi / (BrE)
a respectful gesture made by bending the knees and lowering the body, performed especially by women as a greeting or sign of respect
gesture by bending knees (women)

Examples

The young girl performed a perfect curtsy before the queen.
At the end of her dance, she gave a deep curtsy to the audience.
In some traditional ceremonies, a curtsy is still expected as a mark of respect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bow - a gesture of bending the upper body forward, usually by men, to show respect

Etymology

From Middle English 'courtesy', later altered in spelling to distinguish the gesture from general politeness.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07289595-n
Synset POS: n
