teju

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪ.dʒu / (AmE), / ˈteɪ.dʒu / (BrE)
a large blackish, yellow-banded lizard from South America, known for raiding henhouses and sometimes used as food.
large South American yellow-banded lizard

Examples

The farmer was startled to find a teju in his henhouse early one morning.
Local markets often sell teju meat as a traditional delicacy in some regions of Brazil.
A fully grown teju can reach up to three feet in length.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tegu - large South American lizard of the genus Tupinambis

Etymology

From Portuguese 'teju', from Tupi (an indigenous language of Brazil).
