Monotremata

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːnəˈtrimətə / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈtriːmətə / (BrE)
An order of egg-laying mammals that includes the platypus and echidnas, coextensive with the subclass Prototheria.
order of egg-laying mammals

Examples

The Monotremata are unique among mammals for laying eggs rather than giving birth to live young.
Both the platypus and echidnas belong to the order Monotremata.
Scientists study the evolutionary history of Monotremata to learn about early mammals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monotreme - any member of the Monotremata order; egg-laying mammal such as the platypus or echidna

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) + 'trēma' (hole), referring to the single opening for excretion and reproduction.
