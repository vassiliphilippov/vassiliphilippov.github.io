tellurium

1. NOUN

/ təˈlʊriəm / (AmE), / tɛˈljʊəriəm / (BrE)
a brittle, silver-white metalloid element, related to selenium and sulfur, used in alloys and as a semiconductor
chemical element (metalloid)

Examples

The rare element tellurium is used to improve the machinability of steel alloys.
Solar panels often include semiconductors made from cadmium and tellurium.
Scientists discovered a new telluride mineral rich in tellurium inside copper ore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: selenium - a nonmetallic element chemically similar to tellurium
  • Synonym: sulfur - a yellow chemical element, also chemically related to tellurium

Etymology

From Latin tellus, telluris (earth), reflecting its occurrence in the earth.
