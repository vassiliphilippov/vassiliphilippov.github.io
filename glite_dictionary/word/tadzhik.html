Tadzhik

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˈtadʒɪk / (BrE)
a member of an ethnic group, mostly Muslim, living mainly in Tajikistan and neighboring regions of Uzbekistan, Afghanistan, and China
member of a Central Asian ethnic group

Examples

Many Tadzhik families have maintained their traditional customs and language for centuries.
The Tadzhik community celebrates its own unique festivals and religious practices.
A large number of Tadzhik people live in Afghanistan near the northern border.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tajik - member of a Central Asian ethnic group (standard spelling)

Etymology

From Russian 'Tadzhik', from Persian 'Tājīk'.

2. NOUN

/ tɑːdˈʒiːk / (AmE), / tædˈʒiːk / (BrE)
A person who is a native or inhabitant of Tajikistan and neighboring areas of Uzbekistan, Afghanistan, and China, especially one belonging to the Tajik ethnic group.
a native or ethnic person from Tajikistan

Examples

Many Tadzhik communities have maintained their traditional customs.
He visited the mountainous regions inhabited by Tadzhik herders.
The Tadzhik language is closely related to Persian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tajik - a native or inhabitant of Tajikistan or a member of the Tajik ethnic group

Etymology

From Russian таджик (tadzhik), from Persian تاجیک (tājīk).

3. NOUN

/ tɑːdˈʒiːk / (AmE), / tædˈʒiːk / (BrE)
the Iranian language spoken by the Tajik people, closely related to Farsi, used primarily in Tajikistan and parts of Iran
an Iranian language of Tajikistan and Iran

Examples

Several poems have been translated from Tadzhik into English for the first time.
The official language of Tajikistan is Tadzhik.
He is studying Tadzhik to better understand the region's culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tajik - the Iranian language of the Tajik people
  • Synonym: Farsi - the modern Persian language spoken in Iran

Etymology

From Russian 'tadzhik', from Persian 'tājīk' (Tajik).
