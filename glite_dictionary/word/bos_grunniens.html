Bos grunniens

1. NOUN

/ boʊs ˈɡrʌni.ˌɛnz / (AmE), / bəʊs ˈɡrʌni.ˌenz / (BrE)
the scientific name for the domesticated yak, a large, long-haired wild ox native to Tibet, often kept for its milk, meat, and as a pack animal
scientific name for the domesticated yak

Examples

The Bos grunniens is essential to the livelihood of many Tibetan nomads.
Domesticated Bos grunniens are often used as pack animals in high-altitude regions.
Milk from Bos grunniens is used to make butter and cheese in Tibetan cuisine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yak - a domesticated long-haired bovine animal found in Tibet and Central Asia

Etymology

From Latin: 'bos' meaning 'ox' and 'grunniens' meaning 'grunting'.
