Zhukov

1. PROPN

/ ˈʒuːkɔv / (AmE), / ˈʒuːkɒf / (BrE)
Georgy Zhukov (1896–1974), a Soviet general who led major military operations during World War II, including the counteroffensive at Stalingrad and the capture of Berlin.
Soviet general in WWII

Examples

As a military strategist, Zhukov played a crucial role in the Soviet victory at Stalingrad.
Many historians credit Zhukov with leading the Red Army to capture Berlin in 1945.
During WWII, Zhukov successfully lifted the siege of Leningrad, changing the course of the war.
