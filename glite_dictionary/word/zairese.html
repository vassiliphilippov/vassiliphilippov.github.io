Zairese

1. NOUN

/ zaɪˈiːriz / (AmE), / zaɪˈiːriːz / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Zaire (now the Democratic Republic of the Congo, 1971–1997)
native or inhabitant of Zaire

Examples

During the 1980s, many Zairese athletes competed on the international stage.
The Zairese contributed richly to African art and culture.
She learned French while living among the Zairese in Kinshasa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Congolese - a native or inhabitant of the Congo

Etymology

From Zaire (former name of the Democratic Republic of the Congo, 1971–1997) + -ese (a suffix denoting people of a place).

2. ADJ

/ zaɪˈɪris / (AmE), / zaɪˈɪəriːz / (BrE)
of or relating to Zaire (now Democratic Republic of the Congo) or its people.
relating to Zaire or its people

Examples

The museum displayed several Zairese artifacts from the 1970s.
He interviewed many Zairese citizens about their daily life.
The Zairese government implemented new policies before the country's name was changed.

Synset Examples

Zairean elections

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Congolese - relating to the Democratic Republic of the Congo or its people

Etymology

From Zaire (former name of the Democratic Republic of the Congo) + -ese (denoting origin or relation).
