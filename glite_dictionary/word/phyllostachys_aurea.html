Phyllostachys aurea

1. NOUN

/ fɪˌlɑːsˈtækɪs ɔːˈriːə / (AmE), / fɪˌlɒsˈtækɪs ɔːˈriːə / (BrE)
A species of small bamboo native to southeastern China, known for its slender, flexible young stems and often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
a species of ornamental bamboo

Examples

The garden's centerpiece was a cluster of Phyllostachys aurea swaying gently in the breeze.
Many landscapers select Phyllostachys aurea for its attractive, golden stems and rapid growth.
We planted several Phyllostachys aurea along the fence to create a living privacy screen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bamboo - a fast-growing woody grass of the subfamily Bambusoideae

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name Phyllostachys (from Greek phyllon 'leaf' + stachys 'spike') and species name aurea meaning 'golden' in Latin.
