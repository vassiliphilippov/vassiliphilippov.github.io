Ammotragus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæməˈtreɪɡəs / (AmE), / ˌaməˈtraːɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of wild sheep native to rocky mountains in northern Africa, known for their curved horns and long shaggy fur.
genus of wild sheep

Examples

The Ammotragus genus includes the Barbary sheep, found in North African mountains.
Researchers studied the behavioral patterns of Ammotragus in their natural habitat.
Members of Ammotragus have distinctive horns and are well adapted to arid environments.

Etymology

From Greek ammos (sand) + tragos (goat), referring to their habitat and appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæməˈtreɪɡəs / (AmE), / ˌaməˈtraːɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of wild sheep native to North Africa, best known for the Barbary sheep or aoudad.
genus of wild sheep (Barbary sheep)

Examples

The Ammotragus genus primarily inhabits the mountainous regions of North Africa.
Scientists have studied Ammotragus to understand how desert animals adapt to arid conditions.
The Barbary sheep, or Ammotragus, is noted for its impressive horns and agility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Barbary sheep - a species in the genus Ammotragus, also called aoudad

Etymology

From Greek ammos (sand) + tragos (goat), referring to its desert habitat.
