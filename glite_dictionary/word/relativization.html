relativization

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛlətɪvəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛlətɪvaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making something relative, or viewing it as relative rather than absolute.
the act of making relative

Examples

The relativization of cultural values is often discussed in sociology classes.
Einstein's theories contributed to the relativization of concepts like time and space.
Critics argue that the relativization of truth undermines objective standards.

Synset Examples

The relativization of the concepts of length and intervals of time appears difficult to many, but probably only because it is strange.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: absolutization - the act of making something absolute or viewing it as absolute

Etymology

From 'relative' with suffix '-ization,' patterned after similar English formations.
