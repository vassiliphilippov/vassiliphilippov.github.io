dastardly

1. ADJ

/ ˈdæstərdli / (AmE), / ˈdɑːstədli / (BrE)
wicked and cruel in a cowardly way; despicably cowardly or treacherous
wicked and cowardly

Examples

The villain was known for his dastardly plots against the city.
Such a dastardly act shocked the entire community.
The general condemned the dastardly attack as both cowardly and unjustifiable.

Synset Examples

the unprovoked and dastardly attack by Japan on … December 7th

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowardly - lacking courage; not brave
  • Antonym: brave - showing courage; not afraid

Etymology

From Middle English dastard (coward), possibly from Old Norse dǫstr (lazy) + -ard (pejorative suffix).
