syndetic

1. ADJ

/ sɪnˈdɛtɪk / (AmE), / sɪnˈdɛtɪk / (BrE)
(of words, phrases, or clauses) joined or connected by a conjunction, especially a coordinating conjunction such as 'and' or 'or'.
joined by a conjunction

Examples

The list was written in a syndetic style, with 'and' joining the final items.
A syndetic coordination uses a conjunction to link clauses or words.
Teachers sometimes highlight syndetic coordination to illustrate the function of conjunctions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: asyndetic - not connected by a conjunction

Etymology

From Greek 'syndetikos' meaning 'binding together', from 'syndein' (to bind together).
