decay

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈkeɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈkeɪ / (BrE)
the spontaneous disintegration of a radioactive substance, usually accompanied by the emission of ionizing radiation
radioactive disintegration process

Examples

The rate of decay of uranium is used to date rocks and fossils.
Scientists measured the decay of the isotope over several hours.
Nuclear decay releases energy in the form of radiation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radioactive decay - the same process, specifically named as such in scientific contexts

Etymology

From Old French decaïr (to decay), from Latin decadere (to fall down, decline).

/ dɪˈkeɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈkeɪ / (BrE)
(of organic matter) to decompose or rot through the action of bacteria and fungi.
decompose; rot

Examples
