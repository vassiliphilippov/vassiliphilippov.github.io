unengaged

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪnˈɡeɪdʒd / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪnˈɡeɪdʒd / (BrE)
not busy or occupied; free from activity or commitment
not busy; free from activity

Examples

After the meeting was postponed, she found herself unengaged for the rest of the day.
He remained unengaged during the summer, hoping for a job opportunity to arise.
The staff are unengaged at this hour and can assist with other tasks.

Synset Examples

the cancellation left her unengaged a good part of the afternoon

Related Terms

  • Antonym: engaged - busy; occupied; involved in activity
  • Synonym: free - not occupied or busy; available

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' meaning 'not' + 'engaged'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪnˈɡeɪdʒd / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪnˈɡeɪdʒd / (BrE)
not promised or committed to be married; not engaged to be married
not engaged to be married

Examples

She is currently unengaged and focusing on her career.
After their breakup, both remained unengaged for several years.
He asked if she was unengaged before inviting her to the party.

Synset Examples

continued seeing him but on an unengaged basis

Related Terms

  • Antonym: engaged - formally promised to be married

Etymology

Prefix 'un-' (not) + 'engaged' (committed to marry) from French 'engager'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00159811-s
Synset POS: s
