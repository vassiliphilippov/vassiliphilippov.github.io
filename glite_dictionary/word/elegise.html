to elegise

1. VERB

/ ˈɛlɪˌdʒaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪˌdʒaɪz / (BrE)
to compose or write an elegy, especially as a tribute to someone who has died
compose an elegy
british

Examples

The poet chose to elegise the fallen leader in a moving verse.
After her friend's passing, she began to elegise his memory in her journals.
He was commissioned to elegise the victims of the tragedy for the memorial service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to elegize - compose or write an elegy (American spelling)
  • Synonym: to deplore - to express strong disapproval or sorrow, especially publicly

Etymology

From 'elegy' + '-ise' (British spelling); compare American spelling 'elegize'.
