tonight

1. NOUN

/ təˈnaɪt / (AmE), / təˈnaɪt / (BrE)
The present or the immediately coming night, especially the period between sunset and going to bed on the current day.
the present or coming night

Examples

Are you going out tonight or staying in?
The concert is scheduled for tonight at 8 p.m.
I won’t be able to sleep well tonight because of the storm.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: today - the present or current day
  • Antonym: tomorrow - the day after today

Etymology

From Middle English to night (“this night”), merging 'to' (this) and 'night'.

2. ADV

/ təˈnaɪt / (AmE), / təˈnaɪt / (BrE)
During the night of the present day.
in the night of this day

Examples

Are you coming over tonight or tomorrow?
The stars look especially bright tonight.
Let’s watch a movie tonight after dinner.

Synset Examples

drop by tonight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: today - on this present day (adverb)
  • Antonym: tomorrow - on the day after the present day (adverb)

Etymology

From Middle English 'to night' (to+night), later contracted.

Synset POS: r
