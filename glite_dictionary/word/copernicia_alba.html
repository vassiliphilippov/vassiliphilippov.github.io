Copernicia alba

1. NOUN

/ koʊˌpɜrnɪˈʃiːə ˈælbə / (AmE), / kəʊˌpɜːnɪˈsiːə ˈalbə / (BrE)
A South American species of palm tree that produces a wax similar to carnauba wax.
South American wax-producing palm

Examples

The Copernicia alba thrives in the wetlands of the Gran Chaco region.
Farmers harvest wax from the Copernicia alba for use in various industries.
Unlike other palms, the Copernicia alba can tolerate occasional flooding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carnauba wax - A hard wax from the leaves of Copernicia prunifera, another palm species

Etymology

The genus name honors Nicolaus Copernicus; 'alba' is Latin for 'white'.
