fourfold

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɔrˌfoʊld / (AmE), / ˈfɔːfəʊld / (BrE)
Four times as great or as many; multiplied by four.
four times as much or many

Examples

Over the last decade, the company experienced a fourfold increase in profits.
The medication was administered at fourfold the usual dose during the trial.
Efforts to reduce pollution led to a fourfold decrease in emissions over five years.

Synset Examples

a fourfold increase in the dosage

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: threefold - three times as great or many
  • Degree Higher: fivefold - five times as great or many

Etymology

Formed from 'four' + 'fold', meaning 'times' (as in 'multiplied by').
