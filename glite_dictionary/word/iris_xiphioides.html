Iris xiphioides

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪrɪs zɪˌfaɪ.oiːˈdiːz / (AmE), / ˈaɪrɪs zɪˌfɪ.ɔɪˈdiːz / (BrE)
A bulbous iris native to the Pyrenees, widely cultivated for its large, delicate flowers in various colors except yellow.
species of iris native to the Pyrenees

Examples

Many gardeners prize the Iris xiphioides for its striking blue and violet blossoms.
The Iris xiphioides thrives in well-drained soil with plenty of sunlight.
Unlike some irises, the Iris xiphioides does not produce yellow flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iris - a plant of the genus Iris, known for its showy flowers

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name Iris and species epithet xiphioides (meaning 'sword-like', from Greek xiphos 'sword').
