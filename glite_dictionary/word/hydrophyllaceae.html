Hydrophyllaceae

1. NOUN

/ haɪdroʊˌfɪliˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / hʌɪdrəʊˌfɪlɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of perennial woodland herbs, commonly known as the waterleaf family, native mainly to North America and characterized by lobed or divided leaves and funnel-shaped flowers.
family of perennial woodland herbs (waterleafs)

Examples

Many woodland species belong to the Hydrophyllaceae family and are valued for their attractive foliage.
The classification of Hydrophyllaceae has changed with advances in botanical research.
Plants in the Hydrophyllaceae are common in moist habitats throughout North America.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'hydro-' (water) + 'phyllon' (leaf), referring to the moisture-loving leaves.
