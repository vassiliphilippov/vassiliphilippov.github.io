Plumiera

1. NOUN

/ pluˈmiːrə / (AmE), / pluˈmɪərə / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous shrubs and trees native to tropical America, with spreading branches and fragrant white or pink flowers.
genus of tropical flowering trees

Examples

The Plumiera in our garden is blooming with fragrant pink flowers.
Many tropical resorts feature Plumiera trees near their pools for their beauty and scent.
Botanists often study the unique branching pattern of the Plumiera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frangipani - common name for several species in the genus Plumiera

Etymology

Named after French botanist Charles Plumier (1646–1704).
