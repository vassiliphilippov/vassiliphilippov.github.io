coaming

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊmɪŋ / (BrE)
a raised border or framework around a hatchway, cockpit, or other opening on a ship or boat, designed to keep water out
raised border around a hatchway on a ship

Examples

The crew secured the cargo hatch and checked the coaming for any leaks.
Water splashed over the deck, but the raised coaming kept it from entering the hold.
She climbed through the narrow coaming into the ship's lower deck.

Etymology

From combe (to comb), perhaps influenced by 'comb' (the raised ridge) in Middle English.
