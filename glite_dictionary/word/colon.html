colon

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊ.lən / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.lən / (BrE)
the major part of the large intestine extending from the cecum to the rectum, responsible for absorbing water from food residues before excretion
part of the large intestine
medical

Examples

After the surgeon examined the colon, she determined surgery was necessary.
A healthy diet can help maintain the proper function of the colon.
Polyps in the colon can sometimes develop into cancer if not removed.

Etymology

From Latin *colon*, from Greek *kolon* (large intestine).

2. NOUN

/ koʊˈloʊn / (AmE), / kɒˈlɒn / (BrE)
the basic unit of currency in Costa Rica, equal to 100 centimos
Costa Rican currency unit

Examples

I exchanged my dollars for several thousand colones at the airport.
The price of a coffee in San José is around five hundred colones.
The colon has been the official currency of Costa Rica since 1896.

Etymology

From Spanish 'colón', named after Christopher Columbus (Cristóbal Colón in Spanish).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊlən / (AmE), / ˈkəʊlən / (BrE)
the punctuation mark (:) used to introduce a list, explanation, example, or to separate clauses, as well as after the salutation in formal correspondence.
punctuation mark ‘:’

Examples

Please pay attention to the following items: bread, cheese, and milk.
He ended the greeting with a: Dear Sir or Madam:
The recipe calls for these ingredients: flour, sugar, and eggs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: semicolon - punctuation mark (;) used to link closely related clauses

Etymology

From Latin *colon*, via Greek *kōlon* meaning 'limb, clause'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊlɑn / (AmE), / ˈkəʊlɒn / (BrE)
the basic monetary unit of El Salvador, formerly in use and equal to 100 centavos
former unit of currency in El Salvador

Examples

The price for the souvenir was listed as twenty-five colones.
Before switching to the U.S. dollar, El Salvador used the colon as its official currency.
My grandfather still keeps a few colones as a reminder of the old monetary system.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: centavo - a monetary unit equal to one hundredth of various currencies, including the colon

Etymology

Named after Christopher Columbus (Spanish: Cristóbal Colón).
