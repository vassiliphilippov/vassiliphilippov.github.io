Strad

1. NOUN

/ stræd / (AmE), / stræd / (BrE)
A violin made by Antonio Stradivari or a member of his family, noted for its exceptional quality and craftsmanship.
violin made by Stradivari family

Examples

He played his treasured Strad at the concert last night.
The value of a genuine Strad has soared in recent years.
Musicians around the world dream of owning a Strad for its legendary sound quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Stradivarius - a violin made by Antonio Stradivari or his family

Etymology

Shortened from 'Stradivarius', from the surname of Antonio Stradivari (c. 1644–1737), Italian violin maker.
