heterodactyl

1. ADJ

/ ˌhɛtərəˈdæktɪl / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəˈdæktɪl / (BrE)
(of bird feet) having the first and second toes pointing backward and the third and fourth toes pointing forward.
bird foot with two toes back, two forward

Examples

The trogon is known for its heterodactyl feet, which help it perch securely on branches.
Unlike zygodactyl birds, some species in the Trogonidae family have a heterodactyl foot arrangement.
Ornithologists use the term heterodactyl to describe a rare digital configuration in birds.

Etymology

From Greek hetero- (different) + daktylos (finger, toe).
