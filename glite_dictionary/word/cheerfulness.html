cheerfulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪrfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪəfʊlnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being noticeably happy and optimistic; a disposition or appearance that dispels gloom
quality of being cheerful

Examples

Her cheerfulness brightened the mood of everyone in the room.
Despite the bad weather, his cheerfulness never faded.
A sense of cheerfulness filled the air during the festive celebration.

Synset Examples

flowers added a note of cheerfulness to the drab room

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gloominess - the quality of being sad or lacking hope
  • Synonym: happiness - the state of being happy

Etymology

From 'cheerful' + '-ness'; 'cheerful' from Middle English 'cherful', from Old French 'chier' (face, mood).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪr.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪə.f(ə)l.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being noticeably happy and optimistic, especially in mood or attitude
state of being happy and optimistic

Examples

Her cheerfulness brightened the entire office on a dreary Monday morning.
Even in difficult times, his cheerfulness was contagious to those around him.
They admired her cheerfulness, which made social gatherings much more enjoyable.

Synset Examples

his cheerfulness made everyone feel better

Related Terms

  • Synonym: happiness - the state of being happy
  • Antonym: gloominess - a state of being sad or depressed

Etymology

From the adjective 'cheerful' + '-ness', where 'cheerful' derives from Middle English 'chere' (face, expression, mood) and 'full'.
