Globicephala

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡloʊbɪˈsɛfələ / (AmE), / ˌɡləʊbɪˈsɛfələ / (BrE)
A genus of large, oceanic dolphins commonly known as pilot whales.
genus of pilot whales

Examples

The Globicephala genus includes two main species, both known as pilot whales.
Scientists studied the social behavior of Globicephala in the North Atlantic.
Due to their intelligence, Globicephala are often the subject of marine mammal research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pilot whale - a large, dark-colored whale of the genus Globicephala

Etymology

From Latin 'globus' (globe) and Greek 'kephalē' (head), referring to the rounded head of the species.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɡloʊbɪˈsɛfələ / (AmE), / ˌɡlɒbɪˈsɛfələ / (BrE)
A genus of large oceanic dolphins known as pilot whales, comprising two species found in deep seas worldwide.
genus of marine mammals (pilot whales)

Examples

The Globicephala genus includes both the short-finned and long-finned pilot whales.
Marine biologists have long studied Globicephala for their complex social behaviors.
Members of Globicephala are among the largest species in the oceanic dolphin family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pilot whale - large, dark-colored dolphin of the genus Globicephala

Etymology

From New Latin, from Latin globus (globe) + Greek kephalē (head), referring to the rounded head shape.
