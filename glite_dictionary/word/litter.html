litter

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪtə / (BrE)
a group of young animals born to an animal at one time
offspring born at one time (animals)

Examples

The cat gave birth to a litter of five kittens last night.
Each litter usually contains between four and eight puppies.
The sow cared for her litter diligently, keeping her piglets close.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brood - a group of young birds hatched at one time

Etymology

From Old French *litiere*, meaning 'bed' or 'layer', referring to the bedding of animals.

3. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪtə / (BrE)
material, such as straw or wood shavings, spread in a stable or cage to provide bedding and absorb waste for animals
material used for animal bedding

Examples

Please replace the litter in the rabbit hutch every week to keep it clean.
The cat refused to use the new litter in her tray.
Sawdust is often used as litter for small pets like hamsters.

Etymology

From Old French *litiere* (bed, bedding), from Medieval Latin *lectaria*, ultimately from Latin *lectus* (bed).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

/ ˈlɪtər / (AmE), / ˈlɪtə / (BrE)

strew objects or trash over a surface

5. VERB

/ ˈlɪt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪtə / (BrE)
to make a place untidy by leaving rubbish or a large number of things scattered around
to make a place messy with rubbish

Examples

Please do not litter the park with empty bottles and wrappers.
After the festival, people began to litter the streets with flyers and plastic cups.
Tourists sometimes litter the beach despite the warning signs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pollute - to make air, water, or land dirty or harmful

Etymology

From Middle English litteren, from Old French litiere, from Medieval Latin lectāria (bed for lying on), later associated with rubbish scattered as on a bed.

6. NOUN

/ ˈlɪtər / (AmE), / ˈlɪtə / (BrE)
waste or rubbish that has been carelessly left lying in an open or public place.
rubbish left in public places

Examples

Please use the bin and do not drop litter on the street.
Volunteers helped to pick up litter in the park after the festival.
Fines are issued to people who are caught throwing litter out of their car windows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garbage - waste material; unwanted or discarded matter
  • Synonym: trash - items that are no longer useful or wanted and that have been thrown away
  • Antonym: cleanliness - the state of being clean or free from litter

Etymology

From Middle English 'litter', from Old French 'litiere' (bed, bedding, straw), later extended to mean refuse or rubbish strewn about.

7. NOUN

/ ˈlɪtər / (AmE), / ˈlɪtə / (BrE)
a vehicle consisting of a chair or bed carried on two poles by bearers, used for transporting people, especially in former times
chair or bed carried by bearers

Examples

The injured nobleman was carried through the streets on a litter borne by four servants.
In ancient Rome, wealthy citizens often traveled in a litter when they went to public events.
The explorers constructed a makeshift litter to transport their wounded companion out of the jungle.

Etymology

From Old French *litiere* (bed, litter), from Medieval Latin *lectaria*, from Latin *lectus* (bed).
