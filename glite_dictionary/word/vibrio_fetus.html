Vibrio fetus

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪbri.oʊ ˈfiːtəs / (AmE), / ˈvɪbri.əʊ ˈfiːtəs / (BrE)
a species of bacteria that causes abortion in sheep and can infect other animals or humans
bacterium causing abortion in sheep
medical

Examples

The veterinarian identified Vibrio fetus as the cause of the sheep's abortion outbreak.
Farmers are advised to vaccinate livestock to protect them from infections like Vibrio fetus.
Advanced testing confirmed the presence of Vibrio fetus in the contaminated samples.

Etymology

From Latin 'vibrio' (to vibrate, referring to the bacterium's movement) and Latin 'fetus' (offspring)
