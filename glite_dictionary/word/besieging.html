besieging

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈsiːdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / bɪˈsiːdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
the action of surrounding and attacking a fortified place in order to force it to surrender
surrounding and attacking a fortified place

Examples

The besieging of the city lasted for more than three months, causing widespread hardship among its inhabitants.
During the besieging, supplies and reinforcements were cut off from the defenders inside the fortress.
Medieval history books describe the besieging of castles as long and grueling processes for both attackers and defenders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: siege - a military operation in which armed forces surround a place to force its surrender

Etymology

From the verb 'besiege', which comes from Middle English besegen, from be- (intensive prefix) + segen (to besiege).
