Accipiter cooperii

1. NOUN

/ ˌæksɪˈpɪtər ˌkuːpəˈraɪ / (AmE), / ˌæksɪˈpɪtə ˌkuːpəˈriː / (BrE)
A bluish-grey North American hawk with rapid, darting flight, commonly called Cooper's hawk.
a North American hawk species

Examples

The Accipiter cooperii is often seen darting through forests in pursuit of its prey.
Birdwatchers gathered early to spot an Accipiter cooperii near the riverbank.
Unlike other hawks, the Accipiter cooperii prefers wooded habitats where it can hunt smaller birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cooper's hawk - commonly used name for Accipiter cooperii

Etymology

Named for the American naturalist William Cooper (1798–1864); genus from Latin accipiter 'hawk'.
