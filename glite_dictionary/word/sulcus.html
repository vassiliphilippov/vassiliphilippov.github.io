sulcus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌlkəs / (AmE), / ˈsʌlkəs / (BrE)
Anatomy: a narrow groove or furrow on the surface of an organ or tissue, especially those marking the convolutions of the brain.
narrow groove in an organ or tissue
medical

Examples

The central sulcus separates the frontal and parietal lobes of the brain.
A prominent sulcus can be seen in brain scans of healthy adults.
The surgeon identified the sulcus to avoid damaging important brain areas.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gyrus - a ridge between two sulci in the brain

Etymology

From Latin sulcus (furrow, groove).
