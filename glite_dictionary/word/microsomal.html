microsomal

1. ADJ

/ ˌmaɪkrəˈsoʊməl / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəˈsəʊməl / (BrE)
relating to or derived from microsomes, especially in cells or tissues.
relating to microsomes in cells
medical

Examples

The research focused on the microsomal enzymes involved in drug metabolism.
A deficiency in microsomal activity can lead to certain metabolic disorders.
The scientists compared microsomal fractions from different tissues to analyze protein levels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: microsome - a small vesicle derived from fragmented endoplasmic reticulum in cells

Etymology

From 'microsome' plus the adjective suffix '-al'.
