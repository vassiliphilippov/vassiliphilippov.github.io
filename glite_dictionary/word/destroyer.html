destroyer

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈstrɔɪər / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɔɪə / (BrE)
a person, thing, or force that destroys, ruins, or lays waste to something
person or thing that destroys

Examples

The hurricane was a powerful destroyer of homes and infrastructure.
As an environmental activist, she spoke out against the destroyer of forests.
He was seen by history as the destroyer of the ancient city.

Synset Examples

a destroyer of the environment
jealousy was his undoer
uprooters of gravestones

Related Terms

  • Antonym: creator - a person or thing that brings something into existence

Etymology

From Old French *destruire* and Latin *destruere* (to destroy).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈstrɔɪ.ɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɔɪ.ə / (BrE)
a small, fast, lightly armored but heavily armed warship, used especially for escorting larger vessels and defending against submarines and aircraft
a fast, armed warship

Examples

The navy deployed a destroyer to patrol the coastline during the conflict.
A fleet of destroyers escorted the aircraft carrier through the dangerous waters.
The destroyer launched torpedoes at the enemy submarine.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: frigate - a medium-sized warship, lighter than a destroyer
  • Degree Higher: cruiser - a large warship, bigger and more heavily armed than a destroyer
