dying

1. ADJ

/ ˈdaɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdaɪɪŋ / (BrE)
Extremely eager or longing to do or know something.
extremely eager or longing

Examples

I've been dying to try that new restaurant everyone is talking about.
She was dying to find out what was inside the mysterious package.
They are dying to know if they got the job after the interview.

Synset Examples

anxious to see the new show at the museum
dying to hear who won

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxious - eager or wanting very much to do something
  • Synonym: keen - wanting to do something very much

2. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdaɪɪŋ / (BrE)
The end or termination of something, especially hopes, ideas, or periods.
the end of something; termination

Examples

The dying of optimism in the community was felt by everyone after the announcement.
During the dying of the old year, people reflected on past mistakes.
She felt a dying of hope as more bad news arrived.

Synset Examples

it was the death of all his plans
a dying of old hopes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: death - the end of life or existence
  • Antonym: birth - the beginning or emergence of something

Etymology

Present participle of 'die', used as a noun since the 17th century.

3. ADJ

/ ˈdaɪ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
in the process of passing from life or about to cease to exist
in the process of dying or ending

Examples

The doctor sat by the dying patient, holding her hand gently.
Fewer and fewer people speak the dying language of the island.
His dying words were a message to his family.

Synset Examples

a dying man
his dying wish
a dying fire
a dying civilization

Related Terms

  • Antonym: living - alive; continuing to exist
  • Synonym: moribund - at the point of death or near extinction

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'die,' from Middle English 'dien' (to die) of Germanic origin.
