violoncello

1. NOUN

/ ˌvaɪ.ə.lɔnˈtʃɛl.oʊ / (AmE), / ˌvaɪ.əl.ɒnˈtʃɛl.əʊ / (BrE)
a large stringed musical instrument of the violin family, held upright between the knees when played and producing notes one octave lower than the viola; also called 'cello'
large stringed musical instrument ('cello')

Examples

She practiced the violoncello for hours every day to prepare for the recital.
The deep, rich sound of the violoncello filled the concert hall.
He purchased an antique violoncello from a renowned instrument maker in Italy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cello - common short form for 'violoncello', a large stringed instrument

Etymology

From Italian 'violoncello', diminutive of 'violone' (large viol), itself a derivative of 'viola'.
