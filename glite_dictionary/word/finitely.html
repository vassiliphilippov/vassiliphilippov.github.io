finitely

1. ADV

/ ˈfaɪ.nɪt.li / (AmE), / ˈfaɪ.nɪt.li / (BrE)
In a way that is limited or has an end; not infinitely.
in a limited or finite way

Examples

The equation can be solved finitely in this case, with a set number of answers.
Resources are always finitely available, making careful planning essential.
The process terminates finitely, requiring only a limited number of steps.

Synset Examples

there are finitely many solutions to this problem

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infinitely - without limit or end; endlessly

Etymology

Formed from "finite" (from Latin finitus, meaning 'limited') plus the adverbial suffix -ly.
