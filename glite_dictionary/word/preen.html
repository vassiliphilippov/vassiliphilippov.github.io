to preen

1. VERB

/ priːn / (AmE), / priːn / (BrE)
(usually reflexive) To show satisfaction and pride in oneself, especially after an achievement.
show pride or self-satisfaction

Examples

She couldn't help but preen after receiving the award at the ceremony.
He likes to preen himself on his clever business strategies.
After their team's victory, the coach was preening in the spotlight, basking in praise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: congratulate oneself - to praise or feel pleased with oneself
  • Antonym: humble oneself - to act modestly or lower one's pride

Etymology

Originally referred to birds cleaning and arranging their feathers; figurative sense developed in English.

2. VERB

/ priːn / (AmE), / priːn / (BrE)
(of a bird) to clean and arrange its feathers with its beak.
clean and arrange feathers with the beak (bird)

Examples

The parrot sat on its perch and preened for hours.
Water birds often preen their feathers to keep them waterproof.
The starling preened its wings after a bath.

Synset Examples

The birds preened

Related Terms

  • Synonym: groom - to clean or maintain the appearance of (oneself or another animal)

3. VERB

/ priːn / (AmE), / priːn / (BrE)
to dress or groom oneself with great care and attention to appearance
dress or groom oneself carefully

Examples

Before the party, she spent an hour to preen in front of the mirror.
He would always preen himself before important meetings, choosing his clothes carefully.
The actors preened backstage, making sure they looked perfect before stepping on stage.

Synset Examples

She likes to dress when going to the opera

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to primp - to groom or dress oneself with careful attention to appearance
  • Antonym: to dress down - to dress in less formal clothes

Etymology

From Middle English *preynen*, probably from Old French *porreiner* (to arrange), related to *proigner* (to prune, trim).
