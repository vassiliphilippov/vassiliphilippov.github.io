mitogen

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪt̬.ə.dʒən / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.tə.dʒən / (BrE)
a substance that stimulates cell division, specifically by triggering mitosis
substance stimulating cell division
medical

Examples

Phytohemagglutinin is a commonly used mitogen in immunology experiments.
The presence of a mitogen can induce lymphocytes to proliferate in culture.
Researchers measured the response of cells after exposure to the mitogen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mitosis - cell division resulting in two identical daughter cells

Etymology

From Greek 'mitos' (thread, referring to chromosomes) and '-gen' (producer or originator).
