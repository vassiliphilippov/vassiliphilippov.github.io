scincid

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪn.sɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɪn.sɪd / (BrE)
A lizard of the family Scincidae, typically agile with reduced limbs, an elongated body, and shiny scales, often found in moist tropical regions.
a lizard of the family Scincidae (skink)

Examples

The tropical forest is home to several species of scincid that thrive in moist environments.
Many scincids are distinguished by their shiny, smooth scales and elongated bodies.
Researchers discovered a new scincid with exceptionally reduced limbs in Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skink - a lizard of the family Scincidae

Etymology

From modern Latin Scincidae, derived from Greek skinkos (a kind of lizard).
