cabaret

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæbəˈreɪ / (AmE), / ˈkæbəreɪ / (BrE)
a restaurant or nightclub that offers entertainment, such as singing, dancing, or comedy, often with food and drinks served to the audience
entertainment venue with music and food

Examples

We spent the evening at a lively cabaret enjoying live music and cocktails.
The city is famous for its historic cabarets that feature singers and dancers every night.
After dinner, they went to a cabaret downtown for some late-night entertainment.

Synset Examples

don't expect a good meal at a cabaret
the gossip columnist got his information by visiting nightclubs every night
he played the drums at a jazz club

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nightclub - a club that is open late at night and provides entertainment, food, and drink

Etymology

From French 'cabaret' (tavern, nightclub), originally from Middle Dutch 'cabaret' (small inn).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæbəˌreɪ / (AmE), / ˈkæbəreɪ / (BrE)
entertainment consisting of music, dance, or comedy acts performed in a nightclub or restaurant while the audience eats or drinks
nightclub entertainment show

Examples

She performed her favorite song at the local cabaret last weekend.
The club hosts a lively cabaret every Friday night, featuring singers and dancers.
We enjoyed dinner and a cabaret with comedians and magicians throughout the evening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revue - a type of multi-act popular theatrical entertainment
  • Synonym: variety show - a form of entertainment with a variety of acts

Etymology

From French 'cabaret', meaning tavern or small inn.

Synset POS: n
