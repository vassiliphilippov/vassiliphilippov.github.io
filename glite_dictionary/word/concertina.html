concertina

1. VERB

/ ˌkɑnsərˈtinə / (AmE), / ˌkɒnsəˈtiːnə / (BrE)
to collapse, fold, or compress into alternating ridges and grooves like the bellows of a concertina.
collapse or fold up like a concertina

Examples

The car's front end concertinaed in the messy traffic accident.
The metal barriers were designed to concertina for easy storage.
During the earthquake, the bridge seemed to concertina in on itself.

Etymology

From the noun 'concertina', a type of musical instrument with expanding and collapsing bellows.

2. NOUN

/ kɑn.sɚˈti.nə / (AmE), / kɒn.səˈtiː.nə / (BrE)
coiled barbed wire used as an obstacle, especially in military or security operations.
coiled barbed wire barrier

Examples

The soldiers installed a line of concertina along the perimeter for added security.
Migrants were deterred by the sharp concertina topping the border fence.
Removing the tangled concertina required special protective gloves and cutters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barbed wire - strong wire with sharp barbs used for fencing or obstacles

Etymology

From the resemblance to the musical instrument 'concertina,' which unfolds in a similar coiling manner.

3. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnsərˈtiːnə / (AmE), / ˌkɒnsəˈtiːnə / (BrE)
a small musical instrument similar to an accordion, played by pressing its ends together to force air through reeds and producing musical notes
free-reed musical instrument

Examples

He played the concertina at the local folk festival.
The haunting sound of the concertina filled the quiet room.
She bought an antique concertina from the market last weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accordion - a free-reed musical instrument with a keyboard

Etymology

From Italian concertina (diminutive of concerto, meaning 'concert').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03090712-n
Synset POS: n
