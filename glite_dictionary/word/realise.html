to realise

1. VERB

/ ˈriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈrɪə.laɪz / (BrE)
to expand or complete (a part in a piece of baroque music) by supplying the harmonies indicated in the figured bass
musically expand a figured bass

Examples

The continuo player was asked to realise the figured bass for tonight's performance.
Scholars often debate how best to realise the harmonies in early baroque works.
He carefully realised the part, adding imaginative chords to the original bass line.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01711354-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈriːəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈrɪəlaɪz / (BrE)
to convert (an asset, goods, or property) into cash or money, especially by selling it
convert an asset into cash

Examples

The company decided to realise some of its assets to raise funds.
She hopes to realise a substantial sum from the sale of her late uncle's property.
The paintings were realised at auction for more than expected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to liquidate - to convert assets into cash, especially to pay debts
  • Antonym: to acquire - to buy or obtain an asset or property

Etymology

From Old French *realiser* or Medieval Latin *realisare*, from *realis* (relating to things, real).

Synset POS: v
