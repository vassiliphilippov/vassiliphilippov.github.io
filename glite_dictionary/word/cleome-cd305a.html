cleome

1. NOUN

/ kliˈoʊmi / (AmE), / kliˈəʊmi / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Cleome, typically strong-smelling and having showy, spider-shaped flowers.
genus of showy-flowered plants

Examples

The vibrant cleome in her garden attracted dozens of butterflies every morning.
Cleome is often planted for its unique, spider-like blossoms and tolerance of hot, dry conditions.
He noticed the strong scent of cleome as he walked past the flower beds.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Cleome', derived from Greek *kleōmē* (a plant name).
