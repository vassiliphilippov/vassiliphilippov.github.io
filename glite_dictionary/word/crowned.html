crowned

1. ADJ

/ kraʊnd / (AmE), / kraʊnd / (BrE)
having an artificial crown fitted to a tooth
having a dental crown
medical

Examples

After his root canal treatment, the dentist recommended the tooth be crowned.
She has several crowned molars due to past cavities.
Getting a crowned tooth can restore its strength and appearance.

Synset Examples

had many crowned teeth

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uncrowned - not provided with a crown; in this sense, a tooth not fitted with a dental crown
Source: WordNet

2. ADJ

/ kraʊnd / (AmE), / kraʊnd / (BrE)
Adorned or honored with a crown, especially as a symbol of victory or achievement.
adorned/recognized as victorious

Examples

After years of hard work, she was crowned champion at the national tournament.
The athlete stood crowned with laurel, basking in the glory of his achievement.
Their performance was crowned by thunderous applause from the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: victorious - having achieved victory
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01384703-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈkraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈkraʊnd / (BrE)
having a crown, or something resembling a crown, on top; often used in combination to describe something topped or adorned as with a crown.
having a crown or crown-like feature

Examples

The city was overlooked by a hill crowned with ancient ruins.
She wore a crowned tiara at the gala event.
The statue is crowned with a wreath symbolizing victory.

Synset Examples

a high-crowned hat
an orange-crowned bird
a crowned signet ring

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uncrowned - not having a crown; not formally invested with a crown

Etymology

From Middle English, past participle of 'crown' (to put a crown on).
