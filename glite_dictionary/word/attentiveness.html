attentiveness

1. NOUN

/ əˈtɛn.tɪv.nəs / (AmE), / əˈtɛn.tɪv.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being considerate and thoughtful towards others; careful attention to the needs or feelings of others
thoughtful, considerate attention to others

Examples

Her attentiveness to her friends' needs made her very popular.
A good teacher's attentiveness helps students feel valued and understood.
He showed great attentiveness by remembering everyone's birthdays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consideration - thoughtful concern for others
  • Antonym: inconsideration - lack of concern for others' feelings

Etymology

Formed from 'attentive' + '-ness', based on Latin 'attendere' (to give heed).

2. NOUN

/ əˈtɛn.tɪv.nəs / (AmE), / əˈtɛn.tɪv.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being observant and paying close attention to someone or something
the quality of paying close attention

Examples

Her attentiveness to every detail impressed the meeting organizers.
Teachers appreciate attentiveness from their students during important lessons.
He showed great attentiveness by noticing the small changes in her mood.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inattentiveness - lack of attention or concentration
  • Synonym: alertness - state of being quick to notice things

Etymology

From attentive + -ness; 'attentive' from Latin 'attentus' (attentive, heedful).

3. NOUN

/ əˈtɛn.tɪv.nəs / (AmE), / əˈtɛn.tɪv.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being attentive; the state of paying close and thoughtful attention, especially to the needs or wishes of others.
quality of being attentive

Examples

Her attentiveness to her grandmother's needs was appreciated by everyone in the family.
The teacher praised the students for their attentiveness during the lecture.
His attentiveness to detail made him an excellent proofreader.

Synset Examples

his attentiveness to her wishes
he spends without heed to the consequences

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inattentiveness - lack of attention or care
  • Synonym: attention - the act of concentrating or focusing the mind
  • Antonym: heedlessness - lack of care or attention

Etymology

From 'attentive' + '-ness', using the root from Latin 'attendere' (to attend to).
