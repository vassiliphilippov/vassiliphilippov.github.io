pastern

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæstərn / (AmE), / ˈpɑːstən / (BrE)
The part of a horse's leg between the fetlock and the hoof.
area between fetlock and hoof of a horse

Examples

The vet carefully examined the horse’s pastern for any signs of injury.
Mud caked onto the pastern can lead to skin irritation if not cleaned regularly.
A strong pastern helps support the horse’s weight and absorb shock while running.

Etymology

From Middle English pastern, related to Old French pasturon (halter), from past (shackle for a horse).
