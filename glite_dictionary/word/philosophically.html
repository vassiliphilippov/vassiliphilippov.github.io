philosophically

1. ADV

/ fɪˌlɑːsəˈfɪkli / (AmE), / fɪˌlɒsəˈfɪkəli / (BrE)
In a way that shows calmness, wisdom, or an attitude of acceptance toward difficult situations; in a manner relating to philosophy or philosophical reasoning.
in a philosophical or calm manner

Examples

Despite the setback, she handled the news philosophically and focused on her next steps.
He spoke philosophically about the meaning of happiness during the lecture.
When he lost the competition, he reacted philosophically rather than with frustration.

Synset Examples

she took it philosophically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calmly - in a calm manner
  • Antonym: emotionally - in an emotional manner

Etymology

From 'philosophical' + '-ly', ultimately from Greek 'philosophia' (love of wisdom).

2. ADV

/ ˌfɪləˈsɑfɪkli / (AmE), / ˌfɪləˈsɒfɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to the study or principles of philosophy.
in a way relating to philosophy

Examples

She approached the problem philosophically, considering various ethical theories.
He remained philosophically detached during the debate, refusing to get emotional.
The movement is philosophically indebted to Rousseau.

Synset Examples

the movement is philosophically indebted to Rousseau

Related Terms

  • Synonym: philosophical - relating to philosophy, having a calm attitude toward difficulties

Etymology

From 'philosophical' + '-ly'; 'philosophical' from Greek 'philosophikos'.

Synset POS: r
