Nemophila maculata

1. NOUN

/ ˌniːməˈfaɪlə məˈkjuːlətə / (AmE), / ˌniːməˈfɪlə məˈkjuːlətə / (BrE)
A species of annual plant native to California, with white flowers bearing a deep purple blotch on each petal.
Californian annual plant with purple-blotched white flowers

Examples

In the spring, the meadows were dotted with blooming Nemophila maculata flowers.
Gardeners often plant Nemophila maculata for its distinctive purple-blotched white petals.
Pollinators are attracted to Nemophila maculata because of its prominent markings.

Etymology

From Greek 'nemos' (grove) + 'philos' (loving); species epithet from Latin 'maculata', meaning 'spotted'.
