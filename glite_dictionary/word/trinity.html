trinity

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪnəti / (AmE), / ˈtrɪnɪti / (BrE)
A group of three people or things regarded as a single unit.
a group of three; a triad

Examples

The leaders formed a trinity that dominated the industry for decades.
Many religions are based upon a sacred trinity of deities or principles.
Their trinity of friendship was unbreakable, even in tough times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triad - a group or set of three related people or things
  • Degree Higher: quartet - a group of four people or things
  • Degree Lower: pair - a set of two people or things

Etymology

From Latin trinitas (triad, threefold), from trinus (three each, triple).
