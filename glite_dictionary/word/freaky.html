freaky

1. ADJ

/ ˈfriːki / (AmE), / ˈfriːki / (BrE)
very strange in a way that is unsettling or slightly frightening.
strange and somewhat frightening
informal

Examples

That abandoned house at the end of the street looks really freaky at night.
She had a freaky feeling that she was being watched.
The weather was so freaky yesterday that it changed from sunny to stormy in just minutes.

Synset Examples

the whole experience was really freaky

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weird - strange or strange in an unsettling way
  • Synonym: creepy - causing an unpleasant feeling of fear or unease

Etymology

From 'freak' (a strange or unusual person/thing) + '-y' suffix forming adjectives. Popularized in the 20th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfriːki / (AmE), / ˈfriːki / (BrE)
Very strange, unusual, or unconventional; bizarre.
very strange or bizarre
informal

Examples

The movie had a freaky plot twist that surprised everyone in the audience.
He wore a freaky costume to the party that turned a lot of heads.
The old house gave off a freaky vibe, especially at night.

Synset Examples

restaurants of bizarre design — one like a hat, another like a rabbit
famed for his eccentric spelling
a freakish combination of styles
his off-the-wall antics
the outlandish clothes of teenagers
outre and affected stage antics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bizarre - very strange or unusual
  • Synonym: outlandish - looking or sounding bizarre or unfamiliar
  • Antonym: conventional - based on or in accordance with what is generally done or believed

Etymology

From 'freak' (a person or thing that is unusual), with the adjectival suffix '-y'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00611168-s
