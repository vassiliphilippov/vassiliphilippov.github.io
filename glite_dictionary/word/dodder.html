dodder

1. VERB

/ ˈdɑːdɚ / (AmE), / ˈdɒdə / (BrE)
to walk unsteadily, often with slow or shaky short steps, typically due to age or weakness
walk unsteadily with short steps

Examples

The old man began to dodder across the street, clutching his cane tightly.
After her surgery, she could only dodder around her room for short periods.
Children like to imitate their grandparents and sometimes dodder for fun.

Synset Examples

small children toddle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to totter - to walk or move in an unsteady way, as if about to fall
  • Synonym: stagger - to walk or move unsteadily as if about to fall

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'doderen' (to totter), of imitative origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːdər / (AmE), / ˈdɒdə / (BrE)
a leafless parasitic plant of the genus Cuscuta with thin, twining, yellow or whitish stems that obtains nourishment from other plants
a parasitic leafless vine (Cuscuta)

Examples

The field was overrun with dodder, choking the growth of the crops.
Gardeners must remove dodder quickly to stop it from spreading to healthy plants.
The yellow threads of dodder are easy to spot as they twine around their host.

Etymology

From Middle English dodder, of uncertain origin, possibly related to Middle Dutch dodder (dodder, Cuscuta).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
