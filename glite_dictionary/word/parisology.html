parisology

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrəˈsɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌparɪˈsɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the use of words or expressions that are ambiguous in meaning
use of ambiguous words

Examples

Legal documents must avoid parisology to ensure their meaning is clear.
The politician's parisology left the audience confused about his true stance.
Writers sometimes use parisology to create deliberate ambiguity in their stories.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek *parisos* (equal, similar) + *-logy* (study or use of).
