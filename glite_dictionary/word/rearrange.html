to rearrange

1. VERB

/ ˌriːəˈreɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈreɪndʒ / (BrE)
to change the position or order of things; to put into a new arrangement
put into a new order or arrangement

Examples

Could you please rearrange the chairs so everyone can see the stage?
After moving to her new apartment, Maria decided to rearrange the furniture.
The teacher asked the students to rearrange the words to form a correct sentence.

Synset Examples

Please rearrange these files
rearrange the furniture in my room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to arrange - to put things in a particular order

Etymology

From re- (again) + arrange; 'arrange' from French 'arranger', from Old French, from Vulgar Latin *arrangiare.
