Belamcanda

1. NOUN

/ bɛlˌæmˈkændə / (AmE), / bɛlˌamˈkandə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the iris family (Iridaceae), known commonly as the blackberry lily or leopard lily.
genus of flowering plants (blackberry lily)

Examples

The vibrant orange flowers of the Belamcanda brightened the summer garden.
Botanists recently reclassified Belamcanda as Iris domestica, though the old name is still widely used.
Horticulturists value Belamcanda for its ornamental spotted petals and hardy nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iris - Genus of plants in the family Iridaceae

Etymology

From New Latin, possibly from Greek 'belos' (dart) and 'amkandos' (spotted), referring to the shape and pattern of the flowers.

2. NOUN

/ bɛləˈmˈkændə / (AmE), / bɛləˈmaːkəndə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Iridaceae, commonly known as the blackberry lily or leopard lily.
genus of flowering plants (blackberry lily)

Examples

The Belamcanda is prized for its vibrant orange flowers dotted with red spots.
In botany class, we learned that Belamcanda belongs to the iris family.
The seeds of Belamcanda resemble blackberries, which inspired its common name.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iris - genus of flowering plants in Iridaceae family, related group

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from Greek 'belos' (dart) and 'akantha' (thorn/spine).
