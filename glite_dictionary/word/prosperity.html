prosperity

1. NOUN

/ prɑːˈspɛrəti / (AmE), / prɒˈspɛrɪti / (BrE)
A state of economic growth, wealth, and success, especially marked by rising profits and full employment.
state of economic growth and success

Examples

The government's new policies led to greater prosperity for the entire nation.
Periods of economic prosperity often bring increased job opportunities and rising incomes.
After years of hardship, the town finally enjoyed a time of peace and prosperity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affluence - wealth and abundance; synonym for prosperity
  • Antonym: poverty - state of being extremely poor; antonym for prosperity

Etymology

From Latin prosperitas (favorable condition, success), from prosperus (favorable).

2. NOUN

/ prɑːˈspɛr.ə.ti / (AmE), / prɒˈspɛr.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the state of being successful, especially in financial or economic terms; flourishing or thriving condition
the state of being prosperous

Examples

The country's economic prosperity has led to a higher standard of living for its citizens.
They worked hard for many years before finally enjoying a period of prosperity.
A strong education system is often the foundation of national prosperity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wealth - an abundance of valuable possessions or money
  • Antonym: poverty - the state of being extremely poor

Etymology

From Latin prosperitas, from prosperus (favorable, fortunate).
