to chouse

1. VERB

/ tʃaʊs / (AmE), / tʃaʊs / (BrE)
to cheat or swindle someone, especially by deception or trickery
cheat or swindle by trickery
archaic

Examples

Legend says the merchant was choused out of his entire fortune by a clever imposter.
If you are not careful, they will chouse you with false promises and disappear.
The unscrupulous dealer choused several gullible customers before being caught.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheat - to act dishonestly or unfairly to gain an advantage
  • Synonym: to defraud - to illegally obtain money from someone by deception

Etymology

From Turkish 'çavuş' (a messenger or usher) via obsolete English slang, associated with trickery in 17th century England.
