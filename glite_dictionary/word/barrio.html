barrio

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːrioʊ / (AmE), / ˈbarɪəʊ / (BrE)
A district of a town or city in the United States where many residents are Spanish-speaking or of Latin American descent.
Spanish-speaking urban district

Examples

The city’s most vibrant cultural events often take place in the barrio during summer.
He grew up in a lively barrio known for its music and street art.
Plans to revitalize the barrio include new parks and community centers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neighborhood - a district or community within a town or city

Etymology

From Spanish 'barrio' (neighborhood, district), from Arabic 'barriy' (of the open country).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑrioʊ / (AmE), / ˈbarɪəʊ / (BrE)
an urban district or neighborhood in a Spanish-speaking country; often refers to a community of Spanish-speaking inhabitants within a city.
Spanish-speaking urban neighborhood

Examples

Many immigrants settled in the barrio when they first arrived in the city.
The barrio is known for its vibrant markets and colorful street art.
Local leaders in the barrio organized a festival to celebrate cultural traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neighborhood - a district or community within a town or city

Etymology

From Spanish 'barrio' (district, neighborhood), from Arabic 'barrīyah' (open country, land).
