series

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪr.iːz / (AmE), / ˈsɪə.riːz / (BrE)
(mathematics) The sum of a finite or infinite sequence of numbers or expressions, especially as calculated by adding each term of the sequence together.
sum of a sequence in mathematics

Examples

The geometric series converges if the absolute value of the ratio is less than one.
Mathematicians study the convergence of infinite series in calculus.
She calculated the value of the arithmetic series using a standard formula.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sequence - an ordered list of numbers or terms, especially in mathematics

Etymology

From Latin 'series' (row, chain, sequence).

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪriːz / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriːz / (BrE)
(electronics) a connection of electrical components arranged so that current flows through each one sequentially, one after the other.
electrical connection in sequence

Examples

The light bulbs were connected in series, so if one failed, they all went out.
In a series circuit, the total resistance increases with each additional resistor.
Safety precautions must be observed when joining capacitors in series.

Synset Examples

the voltage divider consisted of a series of fixed resistors

Related Terms

  • Antonym: parallel - (electronics) a connection where components share the same voltage and the current divides between them

Synset POS: n
