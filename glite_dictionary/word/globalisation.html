globalisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡloʊbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡləʊbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which businesses or other organizations develop international influence or start operating on a global scale
process of becoming worldwide in scope

Examples

The globalisation of markets has created new opportunities and challenges for businesses.
Many critics argue that globalisation can threaten local cultures and traditions.
Technological advances have accelerated the pace of globalisation in recent decades.

Synset Examples

the globalization of the communication industry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: internationalization - the process of making something international
  • Antonym: localisation - the process of making something fit local conditions

Etymology

From global + -isation, formed on the base of 'global' and influenced by the suffix '–isation' after French and Latin patterns.
