Plumeria alba

1. NOUN

/ pluˈmɪəriə ˈælbə / (AmE), / pluːˈmɪərɪə ˈalbə / (BrE)
a tall, sparsely branched tree with large, fragrant yellow flowers with white centers, commonly grown in tropical regions
a tropical tree with fragrant yellow and white flowers

Examples

The garden features a beautiful Plumeria alba in full bloom near the entrance.
She planted a Plumeria alba to enjoy its fragrant flowers every spring.
The white and yellow blossoms of Plumeria alba are popular for making leis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frangipani - another name for trees of the genus Plumeria, known for fragrant flowers

Etymology

From Latin 'plumeria', named after French botanist Charles Plumier, and 'alba' meaning 'white'.
