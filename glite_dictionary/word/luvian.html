Luvian

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːviən / (AmE), / ˈluːvɪən / (BrE)
an extinct Anatolian language closely related to Lycian, spoken in ancient Anatolia.
extinct Anatolian language

Examples

Scholars have found many Luvian inscriptions in western Anatolia.
The Luvian language offers valuable insight into ancient Indo-European tongues.
Few people today can read texts written in Luvian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lycian - an extinct Anatolian language closely related to Luvian

Etymology

From Latin Lūvius, from the native name of the people of Anatolia.
