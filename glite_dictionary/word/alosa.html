Alosa

1. NOUN

/ əˈloʊsə / (AmE), / əˈləʊsə / (BrE)
a genus of fish in the herring family, commonly known as shads, found in northern hemisphere rivers and seas
genus of shad fish

Examples

The American shad, a member of the genus Alosa, is an important migratory fish in Atlantic rivers.
Scientists are studying the spawning behavior of several Alosa species to aid conservation efforts.
The genus Alosa includes both freshwater and marine species distributed across Europe, Asia, and North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shad - any of several species of fish in the genus Alosa

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'alos', a kind of fish.
