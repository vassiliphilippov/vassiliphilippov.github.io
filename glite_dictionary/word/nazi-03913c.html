nazi

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːtsi / (AmE), / ˈnɑːtsi / (BrE)
A person who is fanatically dedicated to or seeks to control an activity, practice, etc.; used as a derogatory term.
overzealous, controlling person (derogatory)
offensive

Examples

The grammar nazi in our group always corrects everyone's spelling mistakes.
Some people call her a health nazi because she's strict about diets and exercise.
Don't be a parking nazi—let people relax a little about the rules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dictator - A person who behaves in an autocratic way
  • Synonym: martinet - A person who demands strict obedience to rules
  • Antonym: liberal - A person who is open-minded or not strict about rules (in contrast)

Etymology

From German 'Nazi', short for 'Nationalsozialist' (National Socialist); figurative use is by analogy to the authoritarianism and zealotry of the Nazi party.
