hydrokinetics

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdroʊkɪˈnɛtɪks / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪdrəʊkɪˈnɛtɪks / (BrE)
the branch of physics that deals with the motion of fluids, especially liquids.
study of fluids in motion

Examples

He specialized in hydrokinetics during his graduate studies, focusing on how water currents influence riverbanks.
Advances in hydrokinetics have improved our understanding of ocean circulation patterns.
The principles of hydrokinetics are crucial in designing efficient water turbines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrodynamics - study of fluids in motion, especially the forces acting on them
  • Antonym: hydrostatics - study of fluids at rest

Etymology

From hydro- (water) and kinetics (from Greek kīnētikos, relating to motion).
