tamarin

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæmərɪn / (AmE), / ˈtamərɪn / (BrE)
a small South American monkey of the marmoset family, with silky fur and a long nonprehensile tail
small South American monkey

Examples

The golden lion tamarin is famous for its bright orange fur and playful nature.
Scientists observed a group of tamarins moving quickly through the rainforest canopy.
The zoo recently welcomed a baby tamarin to its primate exhibit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marmoset - small New World monkeys with long tails

Etymology

From French 'tamarin', probably from Tupi 'tamarána' (a kind of monkey).
