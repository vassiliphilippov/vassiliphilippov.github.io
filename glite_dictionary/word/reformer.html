reformer

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈfɔrmər / (AmE), / rɪˈfɔːmə / (BrE)
an apparatus used to alter the molecular structure of hydrocarbons, typically to increase the octane rating and produce higher-quality fuel.
apparatus for processing hydrocarbons into richer fuel

Examples

The refinery installed a new reformer to improve the efficiency of gasoline production.
A catalytic reformer plays a crucial role in modern fuel processing plants.
Technicians routinely monitor the reformer for any changes in catalyst performance.

Synset Examples

a catalytic reformer

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈfɔrmər / (AmE), / rɪˈfɔːmə / (BrE)
a person who advocates or works for change and improvement in a system, society, or institution
person seeking reform or improvement

Examples

Florence Nightingale was a famous reformer who transformed nursing practices.
The new education reformer argued for smaller class sizes and modern curricula.
Throughout history, every major social movement had at least one determined reformer leading the way.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conservative - person resistant to change or supports tradition
  • Synonym: progressive - person favoring or advocating progress, change, or improvement

Etymology

From Latin reformare (to form again, change).
