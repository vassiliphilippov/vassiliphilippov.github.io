to prorate

1. VERB

/ proʊˈreɪt / (AmE), / prəʊˈreɪt / (BrE)
to divide, distribute, or assess something proportionally according to a specific factor, such as time or amount
divide or allocate proportionally

Examples

The company decided to prorate the annual bonus based on each employee’s start date.
If you cancel your subscription, the fee for the remaining days will be prorated.
They will prorate your membership dues if you join partway through the year.

Synset Examples

The rent was prorated for the rest of the month

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to allocate - to distribute resources for a particular purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'pro rata' (according to the rate), used in accounting and law.

2. VERB

/ proʊˈreɪt / (AmE), / prəʊˈreɪt / (BrE)
to divide, distribute, or calculate something proportionally according to a particular rate or share
to divide or distribute proportionally

Examples

The company will prorate your refund based on the number of months you used the service.
Utilities are prorated if a tenant moves in or out during the middle of a billing cycle.
We had to prorate the rent for the days you actually lived in the apartment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to apportion - to divide and allocate according to a plan or proportion
  • Synonym: to distribute - to give out or allocate among a group
  • Antonym: to accumulate - to gather or collect (opposite of dividing)

Etymology

From Latin prō rata parte (according to the calculated part).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00640993-v
Synset POS: v
