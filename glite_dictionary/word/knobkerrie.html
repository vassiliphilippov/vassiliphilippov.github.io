knobkerrie

1. NOUN

/ nɑbˈkɛri / (AmE), / nɒbˈkɛri / (BrE)
A short wooden club with a heavy rounded knob on one end, traditionally used as a weapon or walking stick in southern Africa.
African club with a heavy end

Examples

The chief carried an ornate knobkerrie as a symbol of authority.
He displayed a collection of weapons, including a Zulu knobkerrie, on his wall.
During the traditional dance, the men waved their knobkerries above their heads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: club - a heavy stick used as a weapon

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'knopkierie', from Dutch 'knop' (knob) + Khoikhoi 'kirri' (walking stick).
