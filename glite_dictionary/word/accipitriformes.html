Accipitriformes

1. NOUN

/ ækˌsɪpɪtrɪˈfɔːrmiːz / (AmE), / ækˌsɪpɪtrɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
An order of birds that includes most diurnal birds of prey, such as hawks, eagles, vultures, and kites; sometimes used as an alternative to Falconiformes in some classifications.
order of birds including hawks and eagles

Examples

The Accipitriformes include many of the world's hawks, eagles, and old world vultures.
Recent studies have led some ornithologists to classify falcons separately from the Accipitriformes.
Our guide pointed out several species belonging to the Accipitriformes during the birdwatching tour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Falconiformes - scientific order including birds of prey like falcons and sometimes hawks

Etymology

From Latin accipiter (hawk) + -formes (having the form of).

2. NOUN

/ ækˌsɪpɪtrəˈfɔrmiːz / (AmE), / ækˌsɪpɪtrɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of birds that includes most of the diurnal birds of prey, such as hawks, eagles, vultures, and kites; in some classifications considered an alternative name for Falconiformes.
order of birds of prey (hawks, eagles, etc.)

Examples

Scientists recently reclassified many raptors under the Accipitriformes order based on genetic studies.
Most members of the Accipitriformes have keen eyesight and strong talons for hunting.
Vultures are included within the Accipitriformes, alongside hawks and eagles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Falconiformes - order of birds of prey including falcons and caracaras; sometimes overlaps with Accipitriformes in classification

Etymology

From Latin *accipiter* (hawk) + *-formes* (having the form of).
