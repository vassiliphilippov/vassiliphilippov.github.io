carnality

1. NOUN

/ kɑrˈnæləti / (AmE), / kɑːˈnælɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being concerned with physical, especially sexual, desires; sensuality or lewdness
sensual or sexual desire

Examples

The novel explored the carnality of the human experience without resorting to explicit scenes.
Many philosophers have debated whether carnality should be embraced or transcended.
He struggled to balance his spiritual beliefs with the carnality of his desires.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensuality - the enjoyment or pursuit of physical pleasure, especially sexual
  • Antonym: spirituality - the quality of being concerned with the human spirit as opposed to material or physical things

Etymology

From Late Latin carnalis (fleshly) + -ity, from Latin caro (flesh).
