Spalax

1. NOUN

/ ˈspeɪ.læks / (AmE), / ˈspeɪ.laks / (BrE)
A genus of blind mole rats native to eastern Europe and western Asia, noted for their subterranean habits.
genus of blind mole rats

Examples

The genus Spalax is characterized by its adaptation to a subterranean lifestyle.
Researchers studied several species within the Spalax genus found in southeastern Europe.
Blind mole rats belonging to Spalax have evolved remarkable physiological mechanisms for underground life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mole rat - burrowing rodent adapted to life underground

Etymology

From New Latin, derived from Greek spalax (blind rat, mole).
