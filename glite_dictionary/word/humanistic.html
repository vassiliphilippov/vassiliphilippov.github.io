humanistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhjuːməˈnɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌhjuːməˈnɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to the humanities or to the study of human culture, such as literature, philosophy, and the arts
relating to the humanities

Examples

The university is known for its humanistic approach to education.
Her research focuses on the humanistic values expressed in Renaissance art.
The curriculum provides a humanistic foundation in literature, history, and philosophy.

Synset Examples

humanistic studies
a humane education

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humanities - academic disciplines studying human culture

Etymology

From humanist + -ic; ultimately from Latin humanitas (human nature, learning).

2. ADJ

/ ˌhjuːməˈnɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌhjuːmənˈɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to a philosophy that emphasizes human dignity, reason, and scientific inquiry, often rejecting religious beliefs.
relating to humanism or humanist philosophy

Examples

The organization promotes humanistic values such as rational thinking and respect for all individuals.
Her approach to education is fundamentally humanistic, focusing on the development of each student's potential.
Many scientists hold a humanistic view, believing that human progress depends on knowledge and reason.

Synset Examples

the humanist belief in continuous emergent evolution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humanist - a person who follows the principles of humanism
  • Antonym: religious - relating to or believing in a religion

Etymology

From humanist + -ic, patterned after the noun 'humanism.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02754704-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˌhjuːməˈnɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌhjuːməˈnɪstɪk / (BrE)
Relating to Renaissance humanism, particularly its revival of classical learning and emphasis on human values.
relating to Renaissance humanism

Examples

The humanistic movement sparked profound changes in European art and education.
Many scholars credit the humanistic ideals of the Renaissance for modern secular philosophy.
His interests in ancient texts reflect a humanistic approach to scholarship.

Synset Examples

the humanistic revival of learning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humanism - A Renaissance movement emphasizing classical learning and human values

Etymology

From humanist (from Latin humanista) + -ic.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02754522-a
Synset POS: a

4. ADJ

/ ˌhjuːməˈnɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌhjuːməˈnɪstɪk / (BrE)
showing a strong concern for human welfare, values, and dignity; related to or characteristic of humanism and its principles.
concerned with human values and welfare

Examples

The organization is guided by humanistic ideals and always puts people's needs first.
Her humanistic approach to education emphasizes respect and care for every student.
They developed policies based on humanistic values to improve social welfare.

Synset Examples

released the prisoner for humanitarian reasons
respect and humanistic regard for all members of our species

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humanitarian - concerned with promoting human welfare
  • Antonym: inhumane - lacking compassion for suffering or causing cruelty

Etymology

From humanist + -ic, relating to humanism, which is from Latin humanitas (human nature, kindness).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01265356-s
Synset POS: s
