centralizing

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛntrəˌlaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrəlaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
Tending to draw or bring things toward a central point, authority, or control.
tending to draw to a center

Examples

The government adopted a centralizing policy, increasing control over local administrations.
A centralizing approach may stifle local innovation and responsiveness.
Many critics argue that centralizing power limits diversity and regional autonomy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decentralizing - tending to distribute away from a central point or authority

Etymology

From 'centralize' (verb), itself from 'central' + '-ize'.
