indissoluble

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.dɪˈsɑl.jə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dɪˈsɒl.jə.bəl / (BrE)
(of a substance) not able to be dissolved in a liquid; incapable of being dissolved.
incapable of being dissolved

Examples

The compound is completely indissoluble even in strong acids.
Heavy metals often form indissoluble salts that persist in the environment.
This plastic is virtually indissoluble in water, making it difficult to recycle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insoluble - (of a substance) incapable of being dissolved

Etymology

From Latin indissolubilis, from in- (not) + dissolubilis (dissoluble).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.dɪˈsɑl.jə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dɪˈsɒl.jə.bəl / (BrE)
impossible to be dissolved, undone, or broken; lasting and permanent, especially of contracts or decisions
impossible to dissolve or break

Examples

The treaty was intended to create an indissoluble union between the two nations.
Their marriage was described as an indissoluble bond that nothing could break.
The contract is indissoluble except by mutual consent of both parties.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dissoluble - able to be dissolved or undone
  • Synonym: permanent - lasting without significant change

Etymology

From Latin indissolubilis (not able to be dissolved), from in- (not) + dissolubilis (able to be dissolved).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01759930-s
Synset POS: s
