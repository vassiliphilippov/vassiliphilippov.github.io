fabric

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæbrɪk / (AmE), / ˈfæbrɪk / (BrE)
A material produced by weaving, knitting, or felting fibers, used in making clothes, curtains, and other items.
material made from woven or knitted fibers

Examples

The designer chose a vibrant fabric for the new collection of dresses.
She bought several yards of blue fabric to make curtains for the living room.
This delicate fabric should be washed by hand to avoid damage.

Synset Examples

the fabric in the curtains was light and semitransparent
woven cloth originated in Mesopotamia around 5000 BC
she measured off enough material for a dress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloth - material made by weaving or knitting fibers
  • Synonym: textile - type of cloth or woven fabric

Etymology

From Latin 'fabrica' (workshop, trade, fabric), from 'faber' (worker, artisan).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfæbrɪk / (AmE), / ˈfæbrɪk / (BrE)
The basic structure or framework of something, especially in a society or system.
basic structure or framework

Examples

The fabric of the community was threatened by the rapid changes in technology.
Trust is an essential part of the fabric of any successful organization.
Political scandals have the potential to tear apart the very fabric of democracy.

Synset Examples

providing a factual framework for future research
it is part of the fabric of society

Related Terms

  • Synonym: framework - a basic structure underlying a system or concept
  • Synonym: foundation - the lowest load-bearing part or underlying basis of something
  • Synonym: structure - the arrangement or organization of parts to form an entity

Etymology

From Latin 'fabrica' (a workshop, trade, or craft), from 'faber' (artisan, smith).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
