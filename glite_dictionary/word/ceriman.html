ceriman

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛrɪˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈsɛrɪˌman / (BrE)
A tropical fruit with a cylindrical shape, resembling a pinecone, known for its pineapple-banana flavor; the edible fruit of the Monstera deliciosa plant.
tropical fruit resembling a pinecone

Examples

The flavor of ceriman is often described as a delicious mix of pineapple and banana.
After peeling the ceriman, she enjoyed its unique, tropical sweetness for dessert.
In tropical markets, ceriman is a sought-after fruit for fresh eating and smoothies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Monstera deliciosa - the plant that produces the ceriman fruit

Etymology

From Spanish 'ceriman', derived from Filipino languages, possibly from Tagalog 'sarimanok'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛrɪˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈsɛrɪˌman / (BrE)
A tropical American climbing plant (Monstera deliciosa) with aerial roots and cylindrical fruit that tastes like pineapple and banana.
tropical American climbing plant

Examples

The ceriman in the greenhouse began to climb up the wall, its aerial roots hanging down.
She tasted the fruit of the ceriman and noted its unique mix of pineapple and banana flavors.
Botanists studied the ceriman to understand how its roots absorb moisture from the air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Monstera deliciosa - scientific name for ceriman, a tropical climbing plant
