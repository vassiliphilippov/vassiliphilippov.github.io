roomer

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːmər / (AmE), / ˈruːmə / (BrE)
a person who rents a room in someone else's house, especially without meals included
tenant renting a room in someone's house
american

Examples

She decided to take in a roomer to help pay the bills.
The roomer shares the bathroom with the landlord.
Since losing his job, Tom has become a roomer in his friend's home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lodger - a person who rents accommodation in another's house
