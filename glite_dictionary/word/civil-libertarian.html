civil-libertarian

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪvəl lɪˌbɜrˈtɛriən / (AmE), / ˈsɪvəl lɪˌbɜːˈtɛəriən / (BrE)
having or showing active concern for the protection of civil liberties guaranteed by law
supportive of civil liberties

Examples

The senator took a civil-libertarian stance, strongly opposing the new surveillance laws.
Many civil-libertarian activists attended the rally to protest police overreach.
She admired his civil-libertarian values and his dedication to freedom of speech.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: libertarian - advocating minimal state intervention in the lives of citizens

Etymology

From 'civil liberties' + '-arian,' patterned after 'libertarian'.
