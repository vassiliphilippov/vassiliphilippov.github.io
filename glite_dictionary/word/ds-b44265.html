Ds

1. NOUN

/ di ɛs / (AmE), / diː ɛs / (BrE)
the chemical symbol for darmstadtium, a synthetic radioactive chemical element with atomic number 110
symbol for the element darmstadtium

Examples

The element Ds has no stable isotopes and is produced artificially in laboratories.
In the periodic table, Ds is classified as a transition metal.
Scientists synthesized Ds (darmstadtium) for the first time in the late twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: darmstadtium - a synthetic radioactive element with atomic number 110

Etymology

Named after Darmstadt, Germany, where it was first synthesized.
