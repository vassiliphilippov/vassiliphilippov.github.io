wineglass

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪnˌɡlæs / (AmE), / ˈwaɪnˌɡlɑːs / (BrE)
A glass with a stem, used for serving wine.
glass with stem for wine

Examples

She raised her wineglass in a toast to the happy couple.
The waiter brought a fresh wineglass for each guest at the table.
Red wine is usually served in a larger wineglass than white wine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goblet - a drinking glass with a foot and a stem
  • Antonym: tumbler - a flat-bottomed drinking glass without a stem

Etymology

From 'wine' + 'glass', indicating a glass specifically for wine.
