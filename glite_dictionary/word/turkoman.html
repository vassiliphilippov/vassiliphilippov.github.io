Turkoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrkəˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈtɜːkəmæn / (BrE)
a member of a Turkic ethnic group native to Turkmenistan and neighboring regions.
member of a Turkic people of Turkmenistan

Examples

Many Turkomans still follow traditional nomadic lifestyles in the deserts of Central Asia.
The history of the Turkoman people is closely linked to the rise of the Silk Road.
He researched the art and culture of the Turkoman tribes for his thesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turkmen - a member of the Turkic ethnic group primarily in Turkmenistan

Etymology

From Turkish 'Türkmen', influenced by Persian use of the term.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrkoʊˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈtɜːkəmæn / (BrE)
the Turkic language spoken by the Turkoman people, primarily in Turkmenistan and surrounding regions
a Turkic language of the Turkoman people

Examples

The Turkoman language is spoken by millions of people in Turkmenistan.
She is fluent in Turkoman as well as Russian and Persian.
Researchers published a dictionary to help preserve the Turkoman language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turkmen - the Turkic language spoken in Turkmenistan

Etymology

From Turkish 'Türkmen', referring to the Turkoman people.
