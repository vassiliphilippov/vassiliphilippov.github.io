conventionalism

1. NOUN

/ kənˈvɛnʃənəlɪzəm / (AmE), / kənˈvɛnʃənəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
Adherence to conventional standards, especially in belief, conduct, or artistic expression; orthodoxy resulting from following established conventions.
adherence to traditional standards

Examples

The art exhibit was criticized for its conventionalism and lack of originality.
His conventionalism made it difficult for him to accept new ideas.
Some writers reject conventionalism in favor of experimental styles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orthodoxy - authorized or generally accepted theory, doctrine, or practice
  • Antonym: nonconformity - refusal to conform to established customs or doctrines

Etymology

From French 'conventionalisme', from 'conventionnel' (conventional).
