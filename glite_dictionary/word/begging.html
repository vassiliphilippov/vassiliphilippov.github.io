begging

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɛɡɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of asking people for money or food, usually in public and often because of poverty
the act of asking for money or food in public

Examples

Street begging has become more visible in major cities over the past decade.
He tried to survive through begging after losing his job.
Local authorities are developing programs to help people who turn to begging for a living.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panhandling - the act of asking people for money in public places, especially in American English

Etymology

From Middle English beggen (to beg) + -ing (suffix forming nouns).
