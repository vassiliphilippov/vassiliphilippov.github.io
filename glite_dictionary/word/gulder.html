gulder

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌldər / (AmE), / ˈɡʌldə / (BrE)
a loud, resonant outburst or uproar, especially of laughter or noise from a crowd
a loud outburst or uproar

Examples

The gulder from the audience surprised even the performers.
A sudden gulder of laughter broke the silence during the meeting.
As soon as the joke landed, a gulder filled the entire room.

Synset Examples

The gulder from the crowd was deafening during the concert.
He let out a gulder of laughter that echoed through the hall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roar - a full, deep, prolonged loud sound
  • Synonym: shout - a loud call or cry

Etymology

Probably of northern English or Scots origin, related to 'guilder' (to shout or yell).
