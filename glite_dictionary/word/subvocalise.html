to subvocalise

1. VERB

/ sʌbˈvoʊkəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / sʌbˈvəʊkəlaɪz / (BrE)
to articulate words or sounds without making them audible, especially while reading silently
articulate silently; read without audible sound

Examples

Some students subvocalise while reading difficult texts to themselves.
If you subvocalise during an exam, the teacher might notice your lips moving.
She tried to subvocalise the speech before delivering it aloud.

Synset Examples

she was reading to herself and merely subvocalized

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: whisper - to speak very softly without vibrating the vocal cords
  • Degree Higher: to vocalize - to utter or produce with the voice

Etymology

From sub- (under, less than) + vocalize, modeled after 'subvocal' (under the level of audible voice).
