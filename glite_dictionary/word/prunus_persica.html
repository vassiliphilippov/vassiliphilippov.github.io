Prunus persica

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs ˈpɜːrsɪkə / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs ˈpɜːsɪkə / (BrE)
the peach tree, a species of tree cultivated in temperate regions for its edible fruit
the peach tree (scientific name)

Examples

The Prunus persica thrives in regions with cool winters and warm summers.
Many orchards in Georgia specialize in growing Prunus persica for commercial fruit production.
Researchers studied the genetic diversity of Prunus persica across different climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peach - a round, juicy fruit with sweet yellow flesh and pinkish-orange skin

Etymology

From Latin; 'Prunus' (plum genus) and 'persica', meaning 'Persian', referring to its supposed origin.
