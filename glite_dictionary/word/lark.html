lark

1. NOUN

/ lɑrk / (AmE), / lɑːk / (BrE)
A North American songbird with a yellow breast, known for its melodious singing.
North American songbird with yellow breast

Examples

The distinctive song of the lark echoed across the prairie.
We spotted a lark with a bright yellow breast during our hike.
Many birdwatchers come here in spring to see the colorful larks nesting in the fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meadowlark - A North American songbird with a yellow breast, closely related to larks

3. NOUN

/ lɑrk / (AmE), / lɑːk / (BrE)
a small songbird found in open country, known for its melodious singing and streaked brown feathers; especially of the family Alaudidae.
a small ground-dwelling songbird

Examples

A lark sang cheerfully as the sun rose over the fields.
We spotted a lark darting between the tall grasses.
The countryside is home to many bird species, including the lark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skylark - a species of lark known for singing while flying high in the sky

Etymology

From Old English *lāwerce*, related to Dutch *leewerik* and German *Lerche*.

4. NOUN

/ lɑrk / (AmE), / lɑːk / (BrE)
a small songbird, especially one of several species of the family Alaudidae, known for its melodious singing and found mostly in Europe, Asia, and Africa
a small singing bird

Examples

The lark filled the early morning with its cheerful song.
She listened as a lark soared above the fields, singing beautifully.
Several larks were spotted in the meadow at dawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skylark - a common Eurasian lark known for singing while soaring high in the air

Etymology

From Old English *lāwerce*, related to Old Norse *lǫvrikja* and Dutch *lewerik*.
