turnpike

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːnˌpaɪk / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnpaɪk / (BrE)
a main road, especially an expressway, on which a toll is collected for use
toll expressway
american

Examples

We paid five dollars to use the turnpike on our way to Boston.
The new turnpike has reduced travel time between the two cities considerably.
Traffic on the turnpike was heavy during the holiday weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expressway - a major highway designed for fast traffic
  • Antonym: freeway - a highway without tolls

Etymology

Originally referred to a spiked barrier used on roads. Later transferred to toll roads in the US.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːnˌpaɪk / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnˌpaɪk / (BrE)
(historical) A gate or barrier set across a road, especially from the 16th to 19th centuries, to prevent passage until a toll had been paid.
historical road gate for toll collection
archaic

Examples

The old coach was delayed at the turnpike while the driver paid the toll.
Several turnpikes once controlled entry to the city after dark.
A guard stood by the turnpike, collecting coins from weary travelers.

Etymology

From Middle English 'turnpyke', from 'turn' + 'pike' (a sharp stick or spike).
