dalliance

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæliəns / (AmE), / ˈdæliəns / (BrE)
a period or act of playful or flirtatious behavior, especially with sexual undertones
playful or flirtatious behavior

Examples

Their brief dalliance at the office party became the talk of the company.
Rumors spread quickly about the politician’s latest dalliance with a famous actress.
What started as harmless dalliance soon developed into something more serious.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flirtation - behavior that suggests a romantic or sexual interest without serious intent
  • Degree Higher: affair - a romantic or sexual relationship, typically one that is secret or illicit
  • Degree Lower: platonic - a relationship that is purely friendly, without sexual interest

Etymology

From Old French 'dallier' (to waste time), influenced by Middle English 'dalien' (to chat, play).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdæliəns / (AmE), / ˈdalɪəns / (BrE)
a period or act of spending time in a playful or idle way instead of working; frivolous delay
playful or idle delay

Examples

Her dalliance with social media cost her several hours each day when she should have been studying.
The manager warned the staff against dalliance during office hours.
Instead of getting to work, he indulged in a brief dalliance by the window, watching the rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idleness - the state of being inactive or avoiding work
  • Synonym: procrastination - the act of delaying or postponing something

Etymology

From Middle English 'dalyaunce', meaning 'conversation, flirtation', from 'dalian' (to dally).
