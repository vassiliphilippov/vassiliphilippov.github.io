fetology

1. NOUN

/ fiˈtɑlədʒi / (AmE), / fiˈtɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of medicine that studies the fetus, especially within the uterus, including its development and diseases
medical study of the fetus
medical

Examples

She decided to specialize in fetology after developing an interest in prenatal medicine.
Fetology has advanced significantly with the use of modern imaging techniques.
Lectures in fetology covered fetal development, prenatal diagnosis, and congenital disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embryology - the branch of biology that studies embryos and their development
  • Synonym: obstetrics - the branch of medicine concerned with childbirth and the care of women giving birth

Etymology

From Latin "fetus" (offspring, young one) + "-logy" (study of).
