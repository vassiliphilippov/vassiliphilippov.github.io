disapproval

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsəˈpruvəl / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈpruːvəl / (BrE)
A feeling of dislike or objection towards someone or something; the expression of an unfavorable opinion.
the feeling or expression of dislike or objection

Examples

Her parents showed obvious disapproval when she announced her decision to quit her job.
He could sense the disapproval in their faces as he explained his plan.
The policy was met with widespread disapproval from the public.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: approval - the feeling or expression of agreeing with or accepting something
  • Degree Higher: condemnation - strong disapproval or criticism

Etymology

From dis- (expressing reversal) + approve, ultimately from Latin approbare.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.əˈpruː.vəl / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.əˈpruː.vəl / (BrE)
the act of expressing or feeling that something is wrong or bad; a negative judgment or objection
expression of a negative judgment

Examples

She could sense her father's disapproval without him saying a word.
The new policy was met with widespread disapproval from the public.
His frown was a clear sign of disapproval.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: approval - favorable opinion or acceptance
  • Degree Higher: condemnation - expression of very strong disapproval; severe criticism
  • Synonym: criticism - the act of expressing disapproval and noting problems or faults

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'dis-' (expressing reversal) and 'approval', from Latin 'approbare' (to approve).

3. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.əˈpruː.vəl / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.əˈpruː.vəl / (BrE)
the feeling or expression of a negative opinion about someone or something; the act of not approving or condemning
expression or feeling of not approving

Examples

Her parents expressed their disapproval of her decision to drop out of college.
I could sense the disapproval in his voice when I mentioned my new job.
Public disapproval forced the company to withdraw the advertisement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: approval - the act of agreeing with or accepting something as satisfactory
  • Synonym: condemnation - the expression of very strong disapproval; censure

Etymology

From dis- (expressing reversal) + approval, after French désapprobation.

4. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.əˈpruː.vəl / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.əˈpruː.vəl / (BrE)
A feeling or expression of dislike or judgment that something or someone is wrong or unacceptable.
the feeling or expression of disliking something

Examples

Her parents made their disapproval clear when she announced her decision.
He was aware of the disapproval in his boss's voice after the mistake.
Public disapproval forced the company to change its advertising campaign.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: approval - the act of accepting or thinking well of something
  • Degree Higher: condemnation - strong disapproval or denunciation
  • Antonym: acceptance - the act of consenting to or receiving something

Etymology

From dis- (expressing reversal) + approval, after French désapprobation.
