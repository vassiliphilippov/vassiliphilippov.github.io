subtropic

1. ADJ

/ səbˈtrɑːpɪk / (AmE), / sʌbˈtrɒpɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the regions just north or south of the tropics, especially with warm or mild climates
relating to regions near the tropics

Examples

The subtropic climate supports a variety of citrus fruit orchards.
Some plants can only thrive in subtropic regions where winters are mild.
High humidity is common in subtropic zones along the southern coast.

Synset Examples

even near the equator vegetation at 5000 ft is subtropical rather than tropical

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tropical - relating to the tropics; very hot or humid
  • Degree Lower: temperate - relating to regions with moderate climate
  • Synonym: subtropical - relating to or characteristic of the subtropics

Etymology

From 'sub-' meaning 'under' or 'near' and 'tropic', referring to the tropics, from Greek 'tropikos'.
