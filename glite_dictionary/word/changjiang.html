Changjiang

1. PROPN

/ tʃɑŋˈdʒjɑŋ / (AmE), / tʃæŋˈdʒjæŋ / (BrE)
the longest river in Asia, flowing east from Tibet through China to the East China Sea near Shanghai; also known as the Yangtze River.
the Yangtze River in China

Examples

The Yangtze, also known as the Changjiang, plays a vital role in China's economy.
Pollution levels in the Changjiang have become a major environmental concern in recent years.
Many species of fish in the Changjiang are threatened due to changes in water quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yangtze - another name for the Changjiang; the longest river in Asia

Etymology

From Mandarin 长江 (Cháng Jiāng), meaning 'Long River'.
