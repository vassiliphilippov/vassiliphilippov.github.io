sultriness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌltrɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈsʌltrɪnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being oppressively hot and humid, especially in reference to weather.
oppressively hot, humid weather

Examples

The sultriness of the afternoon made everyone feel tired and uncomfortable.
We escaped the sultriness of the city by heading to the mountains for the weekend.
You could feel the sultriness in the air even before the clouds signaled an approaching storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humidity - the state of being humid; moisture in the air
  • Antonym: coolness - the state or quality of being cool, especially moderately cold temperature

Etymology

From sultry + -ness, sultry from alteration of earlier sweltry, related to swelter.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌltrinəs / (AmE), / ˈsʌltrɪnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being sexually attractive or arousing desire
quality of being sexually alluring

Examples

The sultriness of the actress's performance captivated the entire audience.
There was an unmistakable sultriness in the way she spoke to him.
The novel's atmosphere is thick with the sultriness of desire and longing.

Synset Examples

the sultriness of her look was unmistakable
the sultriness of lust was in the air

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seductiveness - the quality of being sexually alluring
  • Antonym: innocuousness - the quality of being harmless or not sexually suggestive

Etymology

From 'sultry' plus the noun suffix '-ness'.
