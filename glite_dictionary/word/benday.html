to benday

1. VERB

/ ˈbɛnˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈben.deɪ / (BrE)
to reproduce an image using the Benday process, a printing technique employing dots or patterns to create shading, texture, or color effects
print using the Benday process

Examples

The artist chose to benday the background for a realistic gradient effect.
Some comic book illustrators still benday their drawings by hand for authenticity.
She bendayed the image to match the style of classic prints.

Etymology

From the name of Benjamin Day (1838–1916), American illustrator and printer who invented the process.
