arcsec

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrkˌsɛk / (AmE), / ˈɑːkˌsɛk / (BrE)
the inverse function of the secant; the angle whose secant is a given number
inverse function of secant (trigonometry)

Examples

To solve for x, take the arcsec of both sides of the equation.
The function arcsec(y) returns the angle whose secant is y.
You can find the value of arcsec using most scientific calculators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arccos - inverse function of cosine
  • Synonym: arcsin - inverse function of sine
  • Synonym: arcsecant - full form of 'arcsec', the inverse function of secant

Etymology

From 'arc' (as in arcus, Latin for arc/angle) and 'sec', an abbreviation of 'secant'.
