craps

1. NOUN

/ kræps / (AmE), / kræps / (BrE)
A gambling game played with two dice, where specific outcomes on the first throw win or lose, and subsequent throws determine the result based on set rules.
dice gambling game

Examples

He won a lot of money at craps during his trip to Las Vegas.
The rules of craps can seem complicated to beginners, but it's a popular casino game.
Some players believe in lucky rituals when they play craps at the casino.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dice - cube-shaped objects used for generating random numbers in games

Etymology

Probably from English dialect 'crap' (meaning 'to lose in a game'), possibly influenced by French 'crapaud' (toad), referring to the players' stance.
