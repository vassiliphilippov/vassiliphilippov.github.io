Catharanthus roseus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæθəˈræntəs ˈroʊzi.əs / (AmE), / ˌkæθəˈræntəs ˈrəʊzi.əs / (BrE)
a woody herb native to Madagascar, widely cultivated for its large pink, white, or red flowers; known as Madagascar periwinkle and used in traditional and modern medicine
woody herb with large pink, white, or red flowers

Examples

Researchers are studying Catharanthus roseus for its anticancer properties.
Many gardens feature Catharanthus roseus because of its vibrant flowers and easy care.
The medicinal compounds extracted from Catharanthus roseus have saved countless lives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: periwinkle - a common name for Catharanthus roseus, particularly referring to its ornamental use

Etymology

From Greek 'katharos' (pure) and 'anthos' (flower), with the species name 'roseus' meaning 'rosy' or 'pink' in Latin.
