Camassia quamash

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmæsiə kwəˈmæʃ / (AmE), / kəˈmasiə kwəˈmaʃ / (BrE)
a plant native to western North America with a large edible bulb, linear basal leaves, and tall racemes of blue star-shaped flowers
North American plant with edible bulb and blue flowers

Examples

Native peoples traditionally harvested Camassia quamash bulbs as a staple food.
In spring, meadows are covered with the blue flowers of Camassia quamash.
Digging for Camassia quamash requires careful identification, as its bulbs are edible but similar to toxic plants.

Etymology

From the genus name Camassia, from Nez Perce *qém’es* (the plant name) and Latinized botanical naming convention.
