oncology

1. NOUN

/ ɑnˈkɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ɒŋˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of medicine that deals with the study and treatment of tumors, including cancer
study and treatment of tumors
medical

Examples

She decided to specialize in oncology after seeing the impact cancer had on her family.
Advances in oncology have greatly improved survival rates for many types of cancer.
The hospital's oncology department provides comprehensive care to cancer patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cancer - a disease caused by uncontrolled cell growth
  • Synonym: hematology - the branch of medicine concerned with blood

Etymology

From Greek onkos (mass, tumor) + -logy (study).
