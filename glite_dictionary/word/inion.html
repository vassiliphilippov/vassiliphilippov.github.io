inion

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪniən / (AmE), / ˈɪniən / (BrE)
the most prominent point at the back of the skull, located at the external occipital protuberance
prominent point on the back of the skull
medical

Examples

During the examination, the doctor gently touched the inion to locate the external occipital protuberance.
The measurement was taken from the glabella to the inion for anthropometric analysis.
Head injuries near the inion can sometimes affect neck muscle attachment points.

Etymology

From Greek *inion* (back of the head, occiput).
