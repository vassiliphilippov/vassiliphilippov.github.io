crammer

1. NOUN

/ ˈkræmər / (AmE), / ˈkræmə / (BrE)
a teacher who prepares students intensively for examinations, often in a short period
teacher who prepares students for exams

Examples

She hired a crammer to help her prepare for the entrance exams.
The crammer scheduled daily review sessions before the final test.
Many students rely on a crammer when they need to improve their grades quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tutor - a private teacher

Etymology

From 'cram', meaning to study intensively in a short time.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkræmər / (AmE), / ˈkræmə / (BrE)
a book used to help someone quickly learn a large amount of information, especially for an examination
textbook for rapid exam preparation

Examples

He relied on a crammer to prepare for his final exams in just a week.
Many students purchase a crammer before important standardized tests.
The crammer condensed the subject matter into easy-to-remember notes.

Etymology

From 'cram' (to study intensively) + '-er' (denoting agent or thing).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkræmər / (AmE), / ˈkræmə / (BrE)
a student who studies intensively over a short period, especially before an examination
student who studies intensively before an exam

Examples

The crammer spent all night reviewing his notes before the final exam.
Unlike a diligent learner, a crammer often relies on last-minute memorization.
Her reputation as a crammer worried her teachers, who encouraged steady study habits instead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cram - to study intensively in a short period

Etymology

From 'cram' (to study intensively) + '-er' (agent suffix).

4. NOUN

/ ˈkræmər / (AmE), / ˈkɹamə / (BrE)
a school or course that prepares students intensively for examinations, often in a short period
intensive exam-preparation school or course

Examples

He enrolled in a crammer to improve his exam results before the finals.
Many students attend a crammer to boost their test scores quickly.
After several weeks at the crammer, she felt more confident about her university entrance exam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tutor - a private teacher, typically one who teaches a single pupil or a very small group
  • Synonym: academy - a place of study or training in a special field

Etymology

From verb 'cram' (to study intensively) + agent suffix '-er'.
