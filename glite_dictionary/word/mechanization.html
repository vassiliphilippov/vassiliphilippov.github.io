mechanization

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛkənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmiːkənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of using machines or automatic devices to perform tasks previously done by people, often with the aid of advanced technology
use of machines to perform tasks

Examples

The mechanization of agriculture dramatically reduced the number of farm laborers needed.
Recent advances in mechanization have increased productivity in the factory.
Widespread mechanization changed the landscape of industrial production in the twentieth century.

Synset Examples

automation replaces human workers by machines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: automation - the use of automatic equipment in manufacturing or other processes

Etymology

From mechanism + -ization, modeled after French mécanisation.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛkənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɛkənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process or condition of using machines or technical systems to perform tasks or operations, especially in place of manual labor.
use of machines in place of manual labor

Examples

The mechanization of agriculture dramatically increased productivity and reduced the need for manual labor.
Factory mechanization transformed the textile industry in the nineteenth century.
Many traditional crafts declined with the widespread mechanization of manufacturing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: automation - the use of automatic equipment in manufacturing or other processes

Etymology

From French 'mécanisation', from 'mécaniser' (to mechanize).

Synset POS: n
