batiste

1. NOUN

/ bəˈtist / (AmE), / bəˈtiːst / (BrE)
A fine, thin, plain-weave fabric made from cotton or linen, often used for shirts or dresses.
fine, thin cotton or linen fabric

Examples

The dress was made of delicate batiste and felt cool against her skin.
Many vintage handkerchiefs are crafted from fine batiste fabric.
He chose a white batiste shirt for the summer wedding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cambric - a light, closely woven white linen or cotton fabric similar to batiste

Etymology

From French 'batiste', possibly after Baptiste, a 13th-century linen weaver from Cambrai, France.
