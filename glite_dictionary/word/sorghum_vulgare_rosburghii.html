Sorghum vulgare rosburghii

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔrɡəm vʊlˈɡɛəri rɒzˈbʊɡiaɪ / (AmE), / ˈsɔːɡəm vʊlˈɡɛəri rɒzˈbʊɡiaɪ / (BrE)
a variety of sorghum with slender, dry stalks and small, hard grains, originally introduced to the United States from India
a type of sorghum plant with small, hard grains

Examples

Sorghum vulgare rosburghii is valued in certain regions for its drought resistance and reliable yield.
Farmers in arid areas often cultivate Sorghum vulgare rosburghii due to its hardiness and low water needs.
The grains of Sorghum vulgare rosburghii are sometimes used to make traditional flatbreads in India.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorghum - a widely cultivated cereal grass, Sorghum bicolor

Etymology

From Latin: 'sorghum' (a grain plant), combined with species and variety epithets; 'rosburghii' named after the botanist W.H. Rosburgh.
