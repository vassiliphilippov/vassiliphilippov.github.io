Okinawa Island

1. PROPN

/ ˌoʊ.kɪˈnɑː.wə ˈaɪ.lənd / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.kɪˈnɑː.wə ˈaɪ.lənd / (BrE)
the largest of the central Ryukyu Islands in Japan, known for its historical significance and subtropical climate
largest island of the central Ryukyu Islands

Examples

The Battle of Okinawa Island was one of the largest military engagements in the Pacific during World War II.
Tourists are attracted to Okinawa Island for its beautiful beaches and unique culture.
Coral reefs around Okinawa Island are home to diverse marine life.

Etymology

From Japanese 沖縄 (Okinawa), meaning 'rope in the open sea'.
