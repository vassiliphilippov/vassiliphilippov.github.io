allopurinol

1. NOUN

/ ˌæləˈpjʊrəˌnɔl / (AmE), / ˌæləˈpjʊərɪnɒl / (BrE)
a drug used to reduce the production of uric acid, commonly prescribed to treat gout and certain types of kidney stones
drug to lower uric acid (for gout)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed allopurinol to help control his gout symptoms.
People taking allopurinol should have their kidney function monitored regularly.
A severe reaction to allopurinol is rare but possible in sensitive individuals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zyloprim - brand name for allopurinol
  • Degree Lower: gout - a form of arthritis caused by excess uric acid in the blood
