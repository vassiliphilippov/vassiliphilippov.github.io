Dipodomys ordi

1. NOUN

/ daɪpəˈdoʊmɪs ˈɔːrdaɪ / (AmE), / dɪpəˈdəʊmɪs ˈɔːdi / (BrE)
A species of kangaroo rat found widely across the plains and mountain regions of central and western United States.
a widely distributed species of kangaroo rat

Examples

The Dipodomys ordi thrives in arid plains and mountain regions of the American West.
Researchers studied Dipodomys ordi to understand its unique adaptations to dry environments.
Compared to other kangaroo rats, Dipodomys ordi occupies one of the broadest geographic ranges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kangaroo rat - small jumping rodent native to North America

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'dipoús' (two-footed) and 'mys' (mouse); species named after George Ord, American zoologist.
