Comstockery

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmˌstɑkəri / (AmE), / ˈkɒmˌstɒkəri / (BrE)
the practice of censoring books, art, or other materials because they are thought to be obscene or immoral
censorship of perceived obscenity or immorality

Examples

The city's ban on the controversial book was condemned as Comstockery by free speech advocates.
Historians argue that Comstockery stifled artistic expression during the early 20th century.
She criticized the board's actions as outdated Comstockery that had no place in modern society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: censorship - the suppression or prohibition of books, films, or other materials

Etymology

From the name of Anthony Comstock (1844–1915), a US crusader against vice, combined with -ery.
