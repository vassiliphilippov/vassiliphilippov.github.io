long-bodied

1. ADJ

/ lɔŋ ˈbɑdiːd / (AmE), / lɒŋ ˈbɒdid / (BrE)
having a body that is notably longer in proportion compared to typical examples of its kind.
having a relatively long body

Examples

The long-bodied dog could easily squeeze under the fence.
Some species of fish are known for their long-bodied shape.
A long-bodied insect rested on the branch, its frame spanning several leaves.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: short-bodied - having a relatively short body
