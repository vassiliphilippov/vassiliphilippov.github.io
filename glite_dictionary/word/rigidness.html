rigidness

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪdʒɪdnɪs / (AmE), / ˈrɪdʒɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being rigid, strict, or unable to bend or adapt
quality of being rigid or strict

Examples

The rigidness of the school's rules left no room for creativity.
Her rigidness in negotiations made it difficult to reach an agreement.
Some people mistake rigidness for strength, but flexibility is often more effective.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rigidity - the quality or state of being stiff and inflexible; strictness

Etymology

From 'rigid' + '-ness'; 'rigid' from Latin 'rigidus' (stiff).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪdʒɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɪdʒɪdnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being stiff and resistant to bending or deformation.
state of being stiff or inflexible

Examples

The rigidness of the metal beam allows it to support heavy loads.
Excessive rigidness in design can lead to brittleness and failure.
We need to test the rigidness of this plastic before using it in construction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rigidity - the quality of being stiff and unable to bend
  • Antonym: flexibility - the ability of a material to bend easily without breaking

Etymology

From 'rigid' (Latin rigidus, meaning stiff) + -ness.
