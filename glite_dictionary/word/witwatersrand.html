Witwatersrand

1. PROPN

/ ˌwɪtˌwɑːtərzˈrɑːnd / (AmE), / ˌwɪtwɔːtəzˈrænd / (BrE)
a rocky region in northeastern South Africa, renowned for its rich gold deposits as well as coal and manganese resources
gold-rich region in northeastern South Africa

Examples

The discovery of gold in the Witwatersrand transformed Johannesburg into a major city.
Many mining companies operate in the Witwatersrand to extract valuable minerals.
The economic development of South Africa was greatly influenced by the resources found in the Witwatersrand.

Etymology

From Afrikaans, literally 'ridge of white waters'.
