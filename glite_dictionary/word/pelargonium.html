Pelargonium

1. NOUN

/ pɛlɑːrˈɡoʊniəm / (AmE), / pɛlɑːˈɡəʊniəm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants native mainly to South Africa, widely cultivated for their brightly colored and fragrant flowers, commonly known as geraniums.
genus of cultivated flowering plants

Examples

The Pelargonium in her garden added a splash of pink to the flowerbeds.
Many gardeners choose Pelargonium varieties because of their attractive scent.
He bought several potted Pelargoniums for the windowsill display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geranium - a related genus of flowering plants sometimes confused with Pelargonium; also called cranesbills

Etymology

From Greek pelargos (stork), referring to the shape of the fruit.

2. NOUN

/ pɛlɑrˈɡoʊniəm / (AmE), / pɛlɑːˈɡəʊniəm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants, commonly called geraniums, native mainly to South Africa and widely grown as garden ornamentals.
a genus of flowering plants (geraniums)

Examples

The garden was filled with colorful Pelargoniums that bloomed throughout the summer.
Horticulturists often recommend Pelargonium species for their ease of care and vibrant flowers.
She bought a pot of Pelargonium to place on her sunny windowsill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geranium - a related genus of flowering plants often called true geraniums or cranesbills

Etymology

From Greek pelargos (stork), referring to the shape of the fruit, via Modern Latin.
