landscapist

1. NOUN

/ ˈlænd.skeɪ.pɪst / (AmE), / ˈlæn.skə.pɪst / (BrE)
an artist who paints landscapes
artist who paints landscapes

Examples

The famous landscapist captured the rolling hills in remarkable detail.
Many landscapists travel to remote places seeking inspiration for their paintings.
As a young landscapist, she was influenced by the French Impressionists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: landscape painter - an artist who paints landscapes

Etymology

From 'landscape' (17th century Dutch 'landschap', painting of natural scenery) + '-ist' (a person who practices an art).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlændskeɪpɪst / (AmE), / ˈlæn(d)skeɪpɪst / (BrE)
a person who arranges or designs features of a landscape or garden in an attractive way
person who designs landscapes or gardens

Examples

The landscapist transformed the barren yard into a beautiful garden with winding paths and vibrant flowers.
She hired a professional landscapist to design the public park's new layout.
As a skilled landscapist, he understands how to combine plants and stones to create harmonious outdoor spaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: landscape architect - a person professionally qualified to design landscapes
  • Synonym: gardener - a person who tends and cultivates a garden

Etymology

From 'landscape' + '-ist', indicating a person skilled in landscape design.
