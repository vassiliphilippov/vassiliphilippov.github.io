salvo

1. NOUN

/ ˈsælvoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsælvəʊ / (BrE)
a simultaneous or rapid series of shots or blows, especially from firearms or artillery
simultaneous discharge of weapons

Examples

The artillery fired a salvo that reverberated across the valley.
A salvo of gunfire erupted as the parade began.
They answered the enemy's attack with a fierce salvo of their own.

Synset Examples

our fusillade from the left flank caught them by surprise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: volley - a number of bullets, arrows, or other projectiles discharged at one time
  • Synonym: barrage - a concentrated artillery bombardment over a wide area

Etymology

From Italian 'salva' (salute, volley), from Latin 'salva' (salvation).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsælvoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsalvəʊ / (BrE)
a sudden outburst of cheers, applause, or similar expression; a simultaneous discharge or outpouring
sudden outburst of cheers or applause

Examples

The speaker's comment was met with a salvo of laughter from the audience.
A salvo of applause erupted as the curtain fell on the final act.
She received a salvo of support after announcing her ambitious plans.

Synset Examples

there was a salvo of approval

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrage - a rapid or overwhelming outpouring (e.g., words, missiles)
  • Synonym: burst - a sudden brief occurrence of something

Etymology

From Italian 'salva' (salutation, volley) and Latin 'salva' (salute), originally referring to a simultaneous discharge of guns as a salute.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsælvoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsalvəʊ / (BrE)
A sudden, vigorous or aggressive burst or series, especially of sound, applause, or words, resembling the discharge of firearms or the release of bombs.
sudden outburst (especially of noise or words)

Examples

A salvo of applause erupted as the speaker concluded his speech.
The politician's announcement was met with a salvo of criticism from the opposition.
She opened the meeting with a salvo of questions directed at the management team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrage - a concentrated outpouring, as of questions or blows
  • Synonym: volley - a number of bullets or other projectiles discharged at one time

Etymology

From Italian 'salva' (salute, discharge of guns), from Latin 'salvāre' (to save).

Synset POS: n
