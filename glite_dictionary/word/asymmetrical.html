asymmetrical

1. ADJ

/ ˌeɪsɪˈmɛtrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌeɪsɪˈmɛtrɪkəl / (BrE)
Having two sides or parts that are not the same in size, shape, or position; not symmetrical.
not symmetrical in form or arrangement

Examples

The designer chose an asymmetrical hemline to make the dress stand out.
The branches of the tree grew in an asymmetrical pattern after the storm.
His face appeared asymmetrical because of the swelling on one side.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: symmetrical - having similar or identical parts on each side of a dividing line or plane

Etymology

From Greek 'asymmetria', meaning lack of proportion.

2. ADJ

/ ˌeɪ.sɪˈmɛ.trɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌeɪ.sɪˈmɛ.trɪ.kəl / (BrE)
Not having symmetry or regularity in shape or arrangement; lacking equality between parts on opposite sides of a dividing line or around a center.
not symmetrical; irregular in shape

Examples

The artist deliberately designed the sculpture to be asymmetrical, giving it a unique and dynamic appearance.
She wore a striking asymmetrical dress with an uneven hemline to the gala.
The building's asymmetrical roof makes it stand out among the traditional structures nearby.

Synset Examples

asymmetrical features
a dress with a crooked hemline

Related Terms

  • Antonym: symmetrical - having symmetry; corresponding in shape or arrangement

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'symmetria' (symmetry).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01967858-s
Synset POS: s
