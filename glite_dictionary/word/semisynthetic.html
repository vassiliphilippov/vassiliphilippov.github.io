semisynthetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛmiˌsɪnˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˌsɪnˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance or material) made by chemical alteration or modification of a natural product, combining natural and synthetic elements.
partly natural, partly synthetic

Examples

Penicillin derivatives such as amoxicillin are considered semisynthetic antibiotics.
Some opioids, like oxycodone, are semisynthetic because they are derived from natural opiates and modified in the laboratory.
The new fabric is semisynthetic, blending natural fibers with engineered polymers.

Synset Examples

man-made fibers
synthetic leather

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synthetic - artificially made; not natural

Etymology

Formed from 'semi-' (half) and 'synthetic', meaning partially synthetic.
