efflorescence

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛfləˈrɛsəns / (AmE), / ˌɛfləˈrɛsəns / (BrE)
the process or period during which flowers bloom or blossom
blooming or blossoming period

Examples

The garden reached its peak of efflorescence in early spring.
During the period of efflorescence, the orchard was painted with vivid colors.
Poets often use efflorescence as a metaphor for youth and growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bloom - a period of flowering or flourishing

Etymology

From Latin 'efflorescentia' (blooming), from 'efflorescere' (to blossom).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛfloʊˈrɛsəns / (AmE), / ˌɛfləˈrɛsəns / (BrE)
A red eruption or rash on the skin, especially as a symptom of a disease.
red skin eruption (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the efflorescence on her arm and asked about other symptoms.
A measles infection is often accompanied by a characteristic efflorescence on the skin.
The sudden appearance of a efflorescence can indicate an allergic reaction.

Etymology

From Latin 'efflorescere' meaning 'to bloom or flourish.'

3. NOUN

/ ˌɛf.ləˈrɛs.əns / (AmE), / ˌɛf.ləˈrɛs.əns / (BrE)
a whitish, powdery deposit that forms on the surface of materials, especially bricks, concrete, or plaster, caused by the movement of salts to the surface where they are left after evaporation of water
a powdery deposit on a surface

Examples

The old brick wall was covered in efflorescence after months of heavy rain.
Builders had to remove the efflorescence from the concrete before painting could begin.
White efflorescence appeared on the basement walls, indicating moisture problems.

Etymology

From Latin efflorescere (to blossom), originally referring to the process of flowering or blooming.

4. NOUN

/ ˌɛf.ləˈrɛs.əns / (AmE), / ˌɛf.ləˈrɛs.əns / (BrE)
the period during which something flourishes or reaches its highest point of development
peak period of flourishing or productivity

Examples

The city experienced its cultural efflorescence during the Renaissance.
Many consider the 19th century to be the efflorescence of Russian literature.
Her career reached its efflorescence in the late 1990s, when she won several major awards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heyday - the period of greatest success, power, or popularity
  • Antonym: decline - the period when something becomes weaker or less successful

Etymology

From French 'efflorescence', from Latin 'efflorescere' (to blossom).
