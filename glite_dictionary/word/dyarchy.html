dyarchy

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈɑrki / (AmE), / daɪˈɑːki / (BrE)
A system of government in which two individuals jointly share the functions and powers of ruler or chief authority.
government with two joint rulers

Examples

The British Raj briefly experimented with dyarchy as a political system in colonial India.
Under a dyarchy, both leaders must agree before any major decisions are made.
Scholars often study dyarchy to understand shared governance in history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diarchy - alternative spelling and synonym for dyarchy

Etymology

From Greek 'dyo' (two) and 'archē' (rule, government).
