diplomatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk / (BrE)
having or showing skill in dealing with people or sensitive situations without causing offense; tactful.
tactful; skilled in handling sensitive matters

Examples

She handled the difficult conversation in a very diplomatic manner, avoiding any hurt feelings.
It's important to be diplomatic when offering criticism to your colleagues.
He gave a diplomatic response to avoid offending either side in the dispute.

Synset Examples

the hostess averted a confrontation with a diplomatic change of subject

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tactful - showing skill and sensitivity in dealing with others
  • Antonym: blunt - uncompromisingly forthright and direct

Etymology

From French diplomatique, from Modern Latin diplomaticus, originally relating to documents (diplomas), later applied to tact in negotiation.

2. ADJ

/ ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of diplomacy, or the management of international relations.
relating to diplomacy or international relations

Examples

The ambassador took a diplomatic approach to resolve the conflict between the countries.
She has always handled difficult situations with a diplomatic touch.
The president granted diplomatic immunity to the foreign envoy.

Synset Examples

diplomatic immunity

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undiplomatic - not having or showing skill in dealing with people or sensitive matters
  • Synonym: tactful - showing sensitivity and skill in dealing with others or with difficult issues

Etymology

From French diplomatique, from Greek diploma (folded paper, official document).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02724451-a
Synset POS: a
