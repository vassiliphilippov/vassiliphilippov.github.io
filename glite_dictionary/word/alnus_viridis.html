Alnus viridis

1. NOUN

/ ˈælnəs vəˈraɪdɪs / (AmE), / ˈælnəs vɪˈrɪdɪs / (BrE)
A species of shrub or small tree, commonly known as green alder, found in mountainous regions of Europe.
shrub (green alder) of Europe

Examples

The Alnus viridis thrives in the high-altitude slopes of the Alps.
Researchers are studying the ecological role of Alnus viridis in mountain soil regeneration.
Local guides pointed out the distinctive green foliage of Alnus viridis during our hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alder - a tree or shrub of the genus Alnus

Etymology

From Latin: 'alnus' (alder) and 'viridis' (green).
