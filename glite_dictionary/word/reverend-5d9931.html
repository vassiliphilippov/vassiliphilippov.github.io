reverend

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛv.ər.ənd / (AmE), / ˈrɛv.ər.ənd / (BrE)
a member of the clergy, especially in the Christian Church, often used as a title or form of address
Christian clergy member; title for a priest or minister

Examples

The reverend delivered an inspiring sermon to the congregation on Sunday morning.
Everyone in the town respected the reverend for his kindness and wisdom.
After years of service, she was ordained as a reverend in the local church.

Etymology

From Latin 'reverendus', meaning 'one who must be respected', from 'revereri' (to revere).

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɛvərənd / (AmE), / ˈrɛvərənd / (BrE)
Worthy of deep respect or reverence; deserving of honor or veneration.
deserving of deep respect

Examples

The ancient temple was considered reverend by all who visited.
She spoke in a reverend tone when discussing the founder of her faith.
The reverend silence of the cathedral added to its sense of awe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: venerable - deserving of respect because of age, character, or position
  • Synonym: admirable - deserving of admiration or approval
  • Antonym: disreputable - not considered to be respectable

Etymology

From Latin 'reverendus' (revered, to be respected), gerundive of 'revereri' (to stand in awe of).
