polychrome

1. VERB

/ ˈpɑliˌkroʊm / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪˌkrəʊm / (BrE)
to color or decorate with many different colors; to make something multicolored
to color with many colors

Examples

The artist decided to polychrome the sculpture, giving it a vibrant and lively appearance.
Restorers worked carefully to polychrome the ancient carvings with historically accurate pigments.
They polychromed the ceiling, transforming the once plain surface into a tapestry of color.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monochrome - to make (something) in a single color

Etymology

From Greek polychrōmos, from poly- (many) + khrōma (color).

/ ˈpɑliˌkroʊm / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪkrəʊm / (BrE)
having or exhibiting many colors; multicolored.
having many colors

Examples

Ancient temples often feature polychrome tiles arranged in intricate patterns.

Related Terms

From Greek polychrōmos, from poly- (many) + khrōma (color).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːliˌkroʊm / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪˌkrəʊm / (BrE)
A work of art composed of or decorated in many colors.
a multi-colored work of art

Examples

The museum displayed a rare polychrome from the medieval period.
The artist created a stunning polychrome using glass and ceramic tiles.
We admired the intricate details and vibrant hues of the ancient polychrome.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monochrome - a work of art using only one color or shades of one color

Etymology

From Greek 'polychrōmos', from 'poly-' (many) + 'chrōma' (color).
