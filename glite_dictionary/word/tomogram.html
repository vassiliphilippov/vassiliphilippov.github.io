tomogram

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊməˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈtɒməˌɡræm / (BrE)
An image produced by tomography, especially an X-ray image showing a cross-section of the body.
medical imaging cross-sectional X-ray image
medical

Examples

The doctor reviewed the patient's tomogram to identify any internal injuries.
A chest tomogram can help detect lung abnormalities not visible in standard X-rays.
Modern CT scanners produce a detailed tomogram in a matter of seconds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tomography - imaging method producing tomograms

Etymology

From Greek 'tomos' (slice, section) + '-gram' (something written or drawn).
