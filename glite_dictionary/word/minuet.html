minuet

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪn.juˈɛt / (AmE), / ˌmɪn.juˈɛt / (BrE)
a slow, stately dance in triple time, or a piece of music composed to accompany it; often found in suites or sonatas
stately dance/music in triple time

Examples

The orchestra played a beautiful minuet during the wedding reception.
This movement of the symphony is structured as a minuet and trio.
During the Baroque period, the minuet was a popular courtly dance.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: waltz - a ballroom dance in triple time, more lively than the minuet

Etymology

From French 'menuet', diminutive of 'menu' (small, delicate), from Latin 'minutus'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɪnjuˈɛt / (AmE), / ˌmɪnjuˈɛt / (BrE)
A slow, stately dance of French origin popular in the 17th and 18th centuries, typically performed in triple time.
stately 17th-18th century court dance

Examples

The orchestra played a minuet as the guests entered the ballroom.
He studied the elegant steps of the minuet for the historical reenactment.
The graceful minuet was once the highlight of courtly festivities.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: waltz - a ballroom dance in triple time performed by couples

Etymology

From French 'menuet', diminutive of 'menu' (small), referring to the small steps of the dance.
