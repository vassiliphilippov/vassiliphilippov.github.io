ruga

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːɡə / (AmE), / ˈruːɡə / (BrE)
(anatomy) a fold or ridge, especially one found in an organ or tissue, such as the internal surface of the stomach.
anatomical fold or ridge
medical

Examples

The inner surface of the stomach is lined with rugae that allow it to expand after a meal.
The doctor pointed out a prominent ruga on the roof of the patient's mouth.
In anatomy class, we studied the function of each ruga within the digestive tract.

Synset Examples

rugae of the stomach

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fold - a crease or ridge formed in something by doubling it over on itself
  • Synonym: crease - a line or ridge produced on paper or cloth by folding, pressing, or crushing

Etymology

From Latin ruga (a wrinkle, fold, or crease).
