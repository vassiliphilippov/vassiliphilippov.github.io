foggy

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɑɡi / (AmE), / ˈfɒɡi / (BrE)
unable to think clearly; confused or slow to react, especially due to tiredness, blows, or intoxication
mentally confused or dazed

Examples

After staying up all night, I felt foggy during the meeting the next day.
He was still foggy from the medication and struggled to focus.
The boxer's mind was foggy after taking several hard punches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dazed - unable to think clearly, often due to shock or fatigue
  • Antonym: clear-headed - able to think clearly and sensibly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00879271-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɑɡi / (AmE), / ˈfɒɡi / (BrE)
Not clear or distinct; appearing hazy or blurred as if covered by fog.
indistinct or hazy in outline

Examples

The photograph was so foggy that I could hardly make out any faces.
His recollection of the event was rather foggy after all these years.
A foggy outline of a mountain appeared on the distant horizon.

Synset Examples

a landscape of blurred outlines
the trees were just blurry shapes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blurry - not clear or sharply defined; indistinct
  • Antonym: clear - easy to perceive, see, or understand

Etymology

From 'fog' + -y, first recorded in English in the 16th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00785203-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈfɑːɡi / (AmE), / ˈfɒɡi / (BrE)
filled or abounding with fog or mist; obscured by fog.
full of fog or mist

Examples

Driving was difficult because the road was so foggy this morning.
It was a foggy night, and you could barely see the streetlights.
London is famous for its foggy weather in the winter.

Synset Examples

a brumous October morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misty - filled or covered with mist; similar meaning
  • Antonym: clear - free from fog, mist, or cloud; opposite of foggy

Etymology

From 'fog' + '-y', meaning covered with fog.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00463642-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈfɑgi / (AmE), / ˈfɒgi / (BrE)
full of or obscured by fog; not clear due to the presence of mist or fog.
full of or obscured by fog

Examples

It was so foggy that the cars could barely move along the road.
The foggy morning made the city skyline completely invisible.
His glasses became foggy as he entered the warm house from the cold outside.

Synset Examples

he could barely see through the fogged window

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misty - slightly obscured by mist; filled with mist
  • Antonym: clear - free from darkness, obscurity, or cloud; easily understood or seen

Etymology

From fog + -y, originally from Scandinavian sources.

Source: WordNet
