perdurable

1. ADJ

/ pərˈdʊrəbəl / (AmE), / pəˈdjʊərəbl / (BrE)
enduring or lasting for an extremely long time; highly durable
enduring a very long time

Examples

The ancient castle's perdurable stone walls have withstood centuries of storms and wars.
Historians marvel at the perdurable impact of some philosophical ideas throughout human history.
This alloy is known for its perdurable properties, making it ideal for use in harsh environments.

Synset Examples

less durable rocks were gradually worn away to form valleys
the perdurable granite of the ancient Appalachian spine of the continent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: durable - lasting for a long time without significant deterioration
  • Antonym: transient - lasting only for a short time

Etymology

From Late Latin 'perdurabilis', from 'perdurare' (to endure) + '-able'.
