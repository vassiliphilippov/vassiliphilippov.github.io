Amoebina

1. NOUN

/ əˈmiːbɪnə / (AmE), / əˈmiːbɪnə / (BrE)
an order of protozoan organisms that includes amoebas, characterized by their ability to move and feed by extending pseudopodia
order of protozoans containing amoebas

Examples

Many species in the order Amoebina are found in freshwater environments.
The classification of Amoebina has evolved as scientists learn more about protozoan relationships.
Researchers observed unique movement patterns in several Amoebina species under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amoeba - single-celled organism in the order Amoebina

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on 'Amoeba' with the taxonomic suffix '-ina'.

2. NOUN

/ əˈmiːbɪnə / (AmE), / əˈmiːbɪnə / (BrE)
a taxonomic order of protozoans that includes the amoebas, characterized by their shapeless bodies and movement via pseudopodia
order of protozoans including the amoebas

Examples

The Amoebina are known for their ability to change shape as they move.
Researchers have discovered new species within the Amoebina order.
Studies of Amoebina help scientists understand the evolution of simple life forms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Amoeba - a single-celled protozoan of the genus Amoeba, found in water and soil

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Amoeba' (genus name) + '-ina', a suffix used in taxonomic names.
