cosignatory

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈsɪɡ.nə.tɔri / (AmE), / kəʊˈsɪɡ.nə.təri / (BrE)
a person or party who signs a document jointly with one or more others, especially a treaty or agreement
joint signer of a document

Examples

Each cosignatory is responsible for complying with the terms of the agreement.
The peace treaty required that every cosignatory ratify the document before it took effect.
If one cosignatory defaults, the others may still be obligated under the contract.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signatory - a person or party that signs a document

Etymology

From co- (jointly) + signatory, from Latin signatorius (of sealing, signing).

2. NOUN

/ koʊˈsaɪnətɔri / (AmE), / kəʊˈsɪɡnətəri / (BrE)
A person or party that signs a document jointly with one or more others.
joint signer of a document

Examples

Each cosignatory of the contract is legally bound by its terms.
The bank required a cosignatory to approve the loan agreement.
As a cosignatory, she was responsible for ensuring all obligations were met.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signatory - a person who has signed a document

Etymology

From co- (together) + signatory (from Latin signātōrius, from signāre 'to sign').

Synset POS: s
