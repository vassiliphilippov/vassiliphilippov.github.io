underclass

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndɚˌklæs / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌklɑːs / (BrE)
the group of people in a society occupying the lowest position in the social hierarchy, often facing poverty and social disadvantage
the lowest social class in society

Examples

Many government policies aim to provide support for the underclass living in urban areas.
The children born into the underclass often face significant barriers to education and employment.
Sociologists debate the causes and consequences of the existence of an underclass in modern societies.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: working class - the social group consisting of people who are employed for wages, especially in manual or industrial work
  • Degree Higher: upper class - the social group with the highest status and wealth in society

Etymology

From under- (meaning 'below') + class (in the sense of a group in society).

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌndərˌklæs / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌklɑːs / (BrE)
the lowest social group in a society, often with the least wealth and opportunities
the lowest social class

Examples

Many social programs are aimed at supporting the underclass in urban areas.
The long-term unemployed often become part of the underclass facing persistent poverty.
Sociologists study the effects of discrimination on the underclass and their access to education.

Synset Examples

underclass mothers and children

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: working class - the social group consisting of people who do physical work

Etymology

From 'under-' meaning 'lower' and 'class' as in social class.
