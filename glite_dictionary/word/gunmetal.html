gunmetal

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌnˌmɛt̬əl / (AmE), / ˈɡʌnˌmɛtəl / (BrE)
a type of bronze, typically composed of copper, tin, and zinc, used especially for parts that must resist wear or corrosion, notably by seawater
a corrosion-resistant bronze alloy

Examples

The ship's propeller was made of gunmetal to withstand the corrosive effects of seawater.
Antique cannons were often cast from gunmetal for durability.
The engineer recommended using gunmetal fittings for the saltwater pipes.

Etymology

Named for its historical use in making cannons and gun parts; 'gun' + 'metal'.
