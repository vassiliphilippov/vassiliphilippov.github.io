Fort Worth

1. PROPN

/ fɔrt ˈwɜrθ / (AmE), / fɔːt ˈwɜːθ / (BrE)
a city in northeastern Texas, west of Dallas, known as a major industrial and cultural center
city in northeastern Texas, USA
american

Examples

Many businesses have established headquarters in Fort Worth due to its economic growth.
The museum district in Fort Worth attracts visitors from all over the country.
After flying into Dallas, she drove west to Fort Worth for a conference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dallas - a large city in northeastern Texas, adjacent to Fort Worth

Etymology

Named after Major General William Jenkins Worth, a U.S. Army officer.
