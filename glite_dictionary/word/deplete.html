to deplete

1. VERB

/ dɪˈplit / (AmE), / dɪˈpliːt / (BrE)
to use up the supply or resources of something so that there is little or none left
use up resources or supplies

Examples

Overfishing can deplete the ocean's fish stocks if regulations are not enforced.
Years of war have depleted the country's natural resources and infrastructure.
The long illness gradually depleted his savings, leaving him with little money.

Synset Examples

this car consumes a lot of gas
We exhausted our savings
They run through 20 bottles of wine a week

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhaust - to use up completely; to consume all of

Etymology

From Latin 'depletus', past participle of 'deplere' (to empty out), from 'de-' (down, away) + 'plere' (to fill).
