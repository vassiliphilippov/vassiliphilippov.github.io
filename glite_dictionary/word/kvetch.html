kvetch

1. NOUN

/ kvɛtʃ / (AmE), / kvɛtʃ / (BrE)
a nagging or habitual complaint, or someone who habitually complains
a nagging complaint or complainer
informal

Examples

I don’t want to hear another kvetch about your job tonight.
Her constant kvetch about the food made dinner less enjoyable.
He can turn even the smallest inconvenience into a major kvetch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complaint - an expression of dissatisfaction or annoyance

Etymology

From Yiddish *kvetshn* (to squeeze, press), figuratively 'to complain'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07226163-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ kvɛtʃ / (AmE), / kvɛtʃ / (BrE)
a person who complains habitually, especially about trivial matters
constant complainer

Examples

My uncle is such a kvetch that nothing ever seems to satisfy him.
The restaurant staff tried to be patient with the notorious kvetch at table five.
Every group has at least one kvetch who finds fault with everything.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complainer - a person who frequently complains
  • Synonym: grumbler - a person who grumbles or mutters discontentedly

Etymology

From Yiddish *kvetshn* (to squeeze, complain), itself from Middle High German *quetschen* (to squeeze, press).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10260618-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ kvɛtʃ / (AmE), / kvɛtʃ / (BrE)
to complain persistently, especially about trivial matters, often with irritation or dissatisfaction
complain persistently or constantly

Examples

If you don't like the meal, don't just kvetch—let the chef know.
He's always kvetching about the weather, no matter the season.
My coworkers kvetch whenever a meeting runs longer than expected.

Synset Examples

My mother complains all day
She has a lot to kick about

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance about something

Etymology

From Yiddish *kvetshn* (to squeeze, pinch, complain), derived from Middle High German *quetschen* (to squeeze).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00909100-v
Synset POS: v
