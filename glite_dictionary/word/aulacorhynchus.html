Aulacorhynchus

1. NOUN

/ ˌaʊ.lə.kəˈrɪŋ.kəs / (AmE), / ˌaʊ.lə.kəˈrɪŋ.kəs / (BrE)
a genus of toucans in the family Ramphastidae, native to Central and South America, commonly known as green toucanets.
a genus of green toucanets

Examples

The species in the genus Aulacorhynchus are commonly known as green toucanets.
Researchers observed several Aulacorhynchus individuals in the cloud forests of Colombia.
Members of the genus Aulacorhynchus are characterized by their predominantly green plumage.

Etymology

From Greek 'aulos' (flute, pipe) + 'rhynchos' (beak).
