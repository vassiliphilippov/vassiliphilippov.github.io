to braze

1. VERB

/ breɪz / (AmE), / breɪz / (BrE)
to join two metal pieces together by melting and flowing a hard solder with a high melting point between them
join metals using hard solder

Examples

To repair the crack, the technician decided to braze the metal components together.
It is important to use proper safety gear when you braze pipes in plumbing work.
They brazed the joints to ensure a strong, leak-proof connection.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: solder - join two metal parts using a soft solder

Etymology

From Old French 'braser' (to solder), from Latin 'brassare' (to make of brass).
