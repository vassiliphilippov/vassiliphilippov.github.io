telecasting

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛlɪˌkæstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪˌkɑːstɪŋ / (BrE)
the broadcasting of visual images, typically for television; the transmission of television programmes to the public
broadcasting of television images

Examples

The live telecasting of the football match reached millions of viewers worldwide.
New technology has transformed the quality of telecasting over the past decade.
She works in the telecasting industry as a producer for a major network.

Synset Examples

she is a star of screen and video
Television is a medium because it is neither rare nor well done

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broadcasting - transmitting signals or programs by radio or television

Etymology

Formed by combining 'television' and 'broadcasting'.
