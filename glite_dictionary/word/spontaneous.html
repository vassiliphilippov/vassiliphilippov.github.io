spontaneous

1. ADJ

/ spɑnˈteɪniəs / (AmE), / spɒnˈteɪniəs / (BrE)
happening or done without being planned or caused by something outside; occurring naturally and without apparent external influence.
happening without external cause or planning

Examples

The crowd erupted in spontaneous applause after the performance.
Her spontaneous decision to travel surprised everyone.
The scientist observed a spontaneous chemical reaction in the lab.

Synset Examples

spontaneous laughter
spontaneous combustion
a spontaneous abortion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: automatic - (of an action) done by itself, without conscious thought or external stimulus
  • Antonym: deliberate - done consciously and intentionally

Etymology

From Latin spontaneus (of one's free will).

2. ADJ

/ spɑnˈteɪniəs / (AmE), / spɒnˈteɪniəs / (BrE)
done or said naturally and without premeditation or planning; not planned or rehearsed in advance.
done without planning or preparation

Examples

He gave a spontaneous speech that moved the entire audience.
Their laughter was so spontaneous that everyone in the room joined in.
Sometimes the best adventures happen when you make a spontaneous decision.

Synset Examples

he made a few ad-lib remarks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impromptu - done without being planned or rehearsed
  • Antonym: planned - deliberately arranged in advance

Etymology

From Latin spontaneus (of one's free will, voluntary).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02221226-s
Synset POS: s
