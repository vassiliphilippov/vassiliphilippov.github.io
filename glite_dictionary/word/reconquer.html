to reconquer

1. VERB

/ riˈkɑŋkər / (AmE), / riˈkɒŋkə / (BrE)
to conquer again, especially territory that was previously lost or taken away
conquer again

Examples

The army planned to reconquer the lands lost during the conflict.
After years of occupation, the nation vowed to reconquer its former territories.
Historians debated whether the king truly managed to reconquer the rebellious province.

Synset Examples

The country reconquered the territory lost in the previous war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to conquer - to overcome and take control of a place or people by force

Etymology

From Latin 're-' (again) + 'conquērere' (to conquer).
