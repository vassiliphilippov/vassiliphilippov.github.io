classification

1. NOUN

/ ˌklæsəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌklɑːsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of arranging things into categories or classes according to shared qualities or characteristics
arranging into categories or classes

Examples

The classification of animals is an important part of biology.
They discussed the classification of books in the library according to subject.
Her job involves the classification of data for research purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: categorization - the process of placing things into categories

Etymology

From French 'classification', from Latin 'classis' (class) and 'facere' (to make).

2. NOUN

/ ˌklæsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌklæsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a group of people or things arranged by class or category
a group organized by class or category

Examples

All mammals belong to a particular classification based on their characteristics.
The library uses a specific classification to arrange its books by topic.
Each scientific classification contains species with similar traits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: category - a group of people or things sharing a common feature

Etymology

From French 'classification', from 'classer' (to class) + '-fication'.

3. NOUN

/ ˌklæsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌklasɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of arranging people or things into groups based on shared qualities or characteristics
the act of categorizing things

Examples

The classification of animals is based on shared characteristics.
All books are arranged according to a strict classification system in the library.
Recent advances in machine learning have improved the classification of medical images.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: categorization - the process of placing things in categories
  • Antonym: segregation - the act of separating people or things into different groups

Etymology

From French 'classification', from 'classer' (to class), from Latin 'classis' (class).

4. NOUN

/ ˌklæsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌklɑːsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the restriction placed by a government on documents, weapons, or information so that only authorized people can access them
government-imposed access restriction

Examples

The report had a high level of classification, so only a few officials were allowed to read it.
Any violation of the classification of military secrets may result in severe penalties.
She obtained special clearance to access information above her usual classification.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: declassification - the process of removing a classification restriction

Etymology

From Latin classis (class) + -fication (making).
