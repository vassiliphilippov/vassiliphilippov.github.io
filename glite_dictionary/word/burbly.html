burbly

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɝːbli / (AmE), / ˈbɜːbli / (BrE)
(of speech or tone) characterized by a bubbling, enthusiastic, and lively quality.
enthusiastic and lively in tone

Examples

The host welcomed everyone with a burbly charm that made the party come alive.
Her burbly voice made even mundane topics sound fascinating.
He recited the poem in a burbly manner, filling the room with laughter.

Synset Examples

a novel told in burbly panting tones

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effervescent - full of life and enthusiasm; vivacious
  • Antonym: flat - lacking in animation, enthusiasm, or liveliness

Etymology

Formed from 'burble' + '-y' to suggest a bubbling or effervescent quality.
