Friesian

1. NOUN

/ ˈfriʒən / (AmE), / ˈfriːʒən / (BrE)
A breed of large black-and-white dairy cattle originating from northern Holland, known for high milk production.
breed of dairy cattle from Holland

Examples

The dairy farm invested in several Friesian cows to boost milk production.
A prize-winning Friesian was showcased at the agricultural fair last weekend.
Many farmers prefer Friesian cattle because of their reliable yields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Holstein - A breed of dairy cattle closely related to the Friesian, especially in North America

Etymology

Named after Friesland, a province in the northern Netherlands.
