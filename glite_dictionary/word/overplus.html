overplus

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊ.vərˈplʌs / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.vəˈplʌs / (BrE)
an amount that is more than what is needed; an extreme excess
extreme excess, surplus

Examples

The company found itself burdened with an overplus of unsold goods after the holiday season.
There was such an overplus of volunteers that many were turned away.
His garden had an overplus of tomatoes, far more than the family could eat.

Synset Examples

an embarrassment of riches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surplus - an amount that is left over; excess
  • Antonym: deficit - the amount by which something is less than required

Etymology

From Old French overplus, from over ('in excess') + plus ('more').
