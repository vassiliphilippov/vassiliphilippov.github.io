Clethrionomys

1. NOUN

/ klɛθˌriːəˈnɒmɪs / (AmE), / klɛθˌrɪəˈnɒmɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small rodent mammals in the family Cricetidae, known as red-backed voles.
genus of red-backed voles

Examples

The Siberian Clethrionomys has adapted well to coniferous forest habitats.
Several species in the genus Clethrionomys play key roles in northern ecosystems.
Researchers studied the population dynamics of Clethrionomys in Scandinavia.

Etymology

From Greek 'klethra' (alder tree) and 'nomys' (dweller, from 'nemein' meaning to inhabit), signifying 'dweller among alders'.

Clethrionomys is known for its distinctive reddish fur on the back.
