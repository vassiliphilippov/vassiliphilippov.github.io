tacamahac

1. NOUN

/ ˌtækəməˈhæk / (AmE), / ˌtækəməˈhæk / (BrE)
A poplar tree of northeastern North America with broad heart-shaped leaves, especially the balsam poplar.
North American poplar tree

Examples

The old tacamahac in our yard stands tall among the maples and oaks.
Native Americans once used resin from the tacamahac tree for medicinal purposes.
The broad leaves of the tacamahac create welcome shade along the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balsam poplar - A species of poplar tree native to North America known for its fragrant resinous buds.

Etymology

From Nahuatl *tecomahaca*, referring to balsam-producing trees.
