essentiality

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌsɛnʃiˈæləti / (AmE), / ɪˌsɛnʃɪˈælɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being absolutely necessary or of basic importance; essential nature
the quality of being essential; basic importance

Examples

The essentiality of science education in today's world cannot be overstated.
Researchers debated the essentiality of the nutrient for human health.
He questioned the essentiality of that step in the process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: necessity - the state of being necessary or indispensable
  • Antonym: unimportance - the quality of not being important or essential

Etymology

From Medieval Latin essentialitas, from Latin essentia (essence).
