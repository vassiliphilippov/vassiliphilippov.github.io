nerve

/ nɜrv / (AmE), / nɜːv / (BrE)

It takes a lot of nerve to challenge tradition in such a public way.

2. NOUN

/ nɝv / (AmE), / nɜːv / (BrE)
A bundle of fibers in the body that transmits impulses of sensation to the brain or spinal cord, and impulses from these to the muscles and organs.
bundle of fibers transmitting impulses in the body
medical

Examples

The optic nerve carries visual information from the eye to the brain.
Injury to a nerve can lead to loss of sensation in the affected area.
Doctors were able to repair the damaged nerve during surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neuron - a nerve cell that transmits nerve impulses

Etymology

From Latin nervus (sinew, tendon, nerve), via Old French and Middle English.

3. NOUN

/ nɝːv / (AmE), / nɜːv / (BrE)
courage or boldness in difficulty or danger; the ability to continue despite fear or adversity.
courage or boldness

Examples

She found the nerve to speak up for herself in the meeting.
It took a lot of nerve to jump off the highest diving board.
I wish I had the nerve to ask him out.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courage - the ability to do something that frightens one
  • Synonym: bravery - courageous behavior or character
  • Antonym: timidity - lack of courage or confidence

Etymology

From Latin *nervus* (sinew, tendon, nerve), figuratively associated with strength and courage.
