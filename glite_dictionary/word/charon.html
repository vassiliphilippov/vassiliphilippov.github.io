Charon

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɛr.ən / (AmE), / ˈkær.ɒn / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the ferryman who carried the souls of the dead across the river Styx or Acheron to the underworld.
Greek mythological ferryman of the dead

Examples

According to legend, Charon required payment before ferrying souls across the river into Hades.
A gold coin was sometimes placed in the mouth of the dead as an offering to Charon.
In the play, the ghost asked Charon for passage across the river Styx.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Χάρων (Khárōn), the name of the mythological ferryman.
