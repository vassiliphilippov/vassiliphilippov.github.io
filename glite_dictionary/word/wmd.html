WMD

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʌbəl.juː ˌɛm ˈdiː / (AmE), / ˌdʌbəl.juː ˌɛm ˈdiː / (BrE)
an abbreviation for weapon of mass destruction; a weapon designed to kill or injure large numbers of people, including civilians, such as nuclear, chemical, or biological weapons
weapon of mass destruction (abbreviation)

Examples

The international community is concerned about the spread of WMDs in unstable regions.
There was no evidence that the country possessed any WMDs after the invasion.
Talks focused on efforts to reduce global WMD stockpiles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weapon of mass destruction - a weapon capable of causing widespread death and destruction

Etymology

Acronym from the initial letters of 'weapon of mass destruction.'
