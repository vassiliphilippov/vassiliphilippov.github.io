abandoned

1. ADJ

/ əˈbændənd / (AmE), / əˈbændənd / (BrE)
(of a place or thing) deserted or left empty, especially by the previous owner or inhabitants.
deserted; left empty by inhabitants

Examples

The abandoned house at the end of the street looked eerie in the moonlight.
After the factory closed, the area became filled with abandoned buildings and empty lots.
We explored the abandoned amusement park, carefully watching our steps.

Synset Examples

weed-grown yard of an abandoned farmhouse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deserted - (of a place) empty of people, abandoned

Etymology

From the past participle of 'abandon', which comes from Old French 'abandoner'.

2. ADJ

/ əˈbændənd / (AmE), / əˈbændənd / (BrE)
(of a feeling, action, or manner) unrestrained and uninhibited; showing a lack of self-control or reserve.
unrestrained; uninhibited

Examples

She danced with an abandoned joy that was infectious to everyone present.
The artist painted with abandoned enthusiasm, caring little for convention or criticism.
His speech was full of abandoned passion, captivating the audience completely.

Synset Examples

an abandoned sadness born of grief

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uninhibited - not restrained; free in behavior or feelings
  • Antonym: restrained - characterized by reserve or moderation
