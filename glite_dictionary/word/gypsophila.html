Gypsophila

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪpˈsɑfɪlə / (AmE), / dʒɪpˈsɒfɪlə / (BrE)
A genus of Mediterranean herbaceous plants in the carnation family, known for their small white or pink flowers; commonly called baby's-breath.
a genus of small-flowered herbs (baby's-breath)

Examples

The delicate Gypsophila added an airy touch to the wedding bouquets.
Many gardeners plant Gypsophila for its long-lasting, cloud-like blooms.
Dried Gypsophila is often used to complement floral arrangements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baby's-breath - common name for Gypsophila, especially Gypsophila paniculata

Etymology

From Greek *gypsos* (gypsum, chalk) + *philos* (loving), referring to its preference for chalky soils.

2. NOUN

/ dʒɪpˈsɑːfɪlə / (AmE), / dʒɪpˈsɒfɪlə / (BrE)
A genus of Mediterranean herbs in the carnation family, having small white or pink flowers.
a genus of herbs with small white or pink flowers

Examples

Many florists use Gypsophila as a filler in bouquets for its delicate, airy appearance.
The Gypsophila genus includes several species native to the Mediterranean region.
Gardeners appreciate Gypsophila for its small, attractive white or pink flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baby's breath - a common name for several species of Gypsophila, especially Gypsophila paniculata

Etymology

From Greek 'gypsos' (gypsum) and 'philos' (loving), referring to its preference for gypsum-rich soils.
