Idiom Neutral

1. PROPN

/ ˈɪdiəm ˈnuːtrəl / (AmE), / ˈɪdiəm ˈnjuːtrəl / (BrE)
an artificial language developed in the early 20th century as a proposed international auxiliary language, based on Volapük and drawing vocabulary from widely used international roots
constructed auxiliary language based on Volapük

Examples

Idiom Neutral was created to facilitate communication between people of different native languages.
Unlike Esperanto, Idiom Neutral did not achieve widespread adoption but remains of historical interest.
Linguists study Idiom Neutral to understand early attempts at constructing global auxiliary languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Volapük - an early constructed international auxiliary language
  • Synonym: Esperanto - the most widely spoken constructed international auxiliary language

Etymology

From the English words 'idiom' (language, dialect) and 'neutral', referring to its intended neutrality and international character.
