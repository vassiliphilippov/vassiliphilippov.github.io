abaca

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːbəˈkɑː / (AmE), / ˌæbəˈkɑː / (BrE)
A Philippine banana plant (Musa textilis) with leafstalks that produce Manila hemp, used for making rope and paper.
Philippine plant yielding Manila hemp

Examples

The fiber of the abaca plant is prized for its strength and durability.
Farmers in the Philippines grow abaca for use in making high-quality ropes.
Traditional paper making in some regions still uses fibers from abaca.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Manila hemp - strong fiber from the abaca plant

Etymology

From Spanish 'abacá', from Tagalog 'abaka'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːbəˈkɑː / (AmE), / ˌabəˈkɑː / (BrE)
A strong fiber obtained from the leaf stalks of a banana plant native to the Philippines, used for making rope, textiles, and paper.
fiber from a Philippine banana plant

Examples

The ropes were made from abaca, known for its exceptional strength and durability.
Harvesters stripped the abaca fibers from the banana-like plants in the early morning.
Demand for abaca has grown as manufacturers seek sustainable alternatives to synthetic materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manila hemp - another name for abaca fiber

Etymology

From Spanish 'abacá', from Tagalog 'abaka' (the plant).
