Cryptogamia

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪptoʊˈɡeɪmiə / (AmE), / ˌkrɪptəʊˈɡeɪmɪə / (BrE)
(in former classification systems) a major division of plants that do not produce seeds, including ferns, mosses, algae, and fungi.
former plant division including non-seed plants

Examples

In the nineteenth century, scientists classified ferns and mosses under Cryptogamia.
Algae and fungi were once grouped as Cryptogamia because they lack seeds or flowers.
The term Cryptogamia is rarely used in modern botany, but appears in historical texts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: phanerogam - plant with visible reproductive organs (seed plants)

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek kryptos (hidden) and gamos (marriage), referring to the concealed reproductive organs.
