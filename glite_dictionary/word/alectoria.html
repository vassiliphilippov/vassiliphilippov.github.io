Alectoria

1. NOUN

/ əˌlɛkˈtɔriə / (AmE), / əˌlɛkˈtɔːriə / (BrE)
A genus of lichens with dark brown, erect or hanging, much-branched, cylindrical thallus.
genus of branched lichens

Examples

The forest was covered with hanging strands of Alectoria, giving it a mystical look.
Scientists collected samples of Alectoria to study air quality in the region.
Many animals use Alectoria as a source of food and shelter in northern forests.

Etymology

From Greek 'alektōr' (rooster), alluding to the lichen’s hair-like appearance.

2. NOUN

/ əˌlɛkˈtɔriə / (AmE), / əˌlɛkˈtɔːriə / (BrE)
A genus of lichens characterized by dark brown, erect or hanging, much-branched cylindrical thalli.
genus of lichens with branched cylindrical thallus

Examples

The lichen Alectoria is often found hanging from trees in northern forests.
Researchers collected several samples of Alectoria for their ecological study.
Certain species of Alectoria are indicators of clean air in forest ecosystems.

Etymology

From Greek alektōr, meaning 'cock' or 'rooster', possibly referring to the appearance of certain species.
