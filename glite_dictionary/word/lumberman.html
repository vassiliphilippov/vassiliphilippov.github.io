lumberman

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌmbərˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈlʌmbəˌman / (BrE)
a man whose job is to cut down trees and prepare timber
man who fells trees for timber

Examples

The experienced lumberman spent most of his life working in the forests of Canada.
As a young lumberman, he learned how to use an axe safely and efficiently.
The documentary followed a day in the life of a modern lumberman.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: logger - a person whose job is to cut down trees for timber
  • Synonym: lumberjack - a person whose work is cutting down trees and transporting timber

Etymology

From 'lumber', meaning 'timber', and 'man', denoting a person involved in the work.
