acetyl

1. NOUN

/ əˈsiːtəl / (AmE), / əˈsiːtɪl / (BrE)
the organic group derived from acetic acid, with the structure CH₃CO-, found in various compounds
organic group (CH₃CO-) from acetic acid
medical

Examples

The acetyl group plays a crucial role in many biochemical reactions.
Aspirin is formed by adding an acetyl group to salicylic acid.
Many drugs contain an acetyl moiety due to its chemical properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: methyl - the organic group CH₃-, derived from methane

Etymology

From French acétyle, from acetum (Latin for vinegar) and the suffix -yl (indicating a chemical radical).
