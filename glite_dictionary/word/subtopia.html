subtopia

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˈtoʊpiə / (AmE), / sʌbˈtəʊpɪə / (BrE)
a monotonous urban sprawl characterized by standardized and featureless buildings, especially in the suburbs
monotonous suburban sprawl

Examples

Many critics argue that the city's outer districts are a classic case of subtopia, lacking any distinctive character.
After decades of poor planning, much of the region is now an endless subtopia of shopping centers and housing estates.
The film explores how cultural identity is lost in the relentless rise of subtopia across the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sprawl - spread of urban or suburban development over a large area
  • Antonym: utopia - an imagined perfect or ideal place, often contrasted with reality

Etymology

Coined by British writer Ian Nairn in 1955 from 'suburb' and '-topia' (as in 'utopia').
