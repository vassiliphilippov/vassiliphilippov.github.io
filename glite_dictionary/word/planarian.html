planarian

1. NOUN

/ pləˈner.i.ən / (AmE), / pləˈneə.ri.ən / (BrE)
a small, mostly freshwater flatworm, often studied for its remarkable ability to regenerate lost body parts
freshwater flatworm known for regeneration

Examples

The planarian can regrow its head if it is cut off, making it a fascinating model for research.
Scientists use planarians to study the process of tissue regeneration in the laboratory.
A freshwater planarian glided slowly along the bottom of the petri dish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flatworm - a type of soft-bodied invertebrate animal; member of phylum Platyhelminthes

Etymology

From New Latin Planaria (a genus name), from Latin planus (flat).
