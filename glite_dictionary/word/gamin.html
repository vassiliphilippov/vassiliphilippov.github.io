gamin

1. NOUN

/ ɡæˈmæn / (AmE), / ˈɡam.æn / (BrE)
(sometimes offensive) a homeless boy, especially one who has been abandoned and roams the streets.
homeless or abandoned boy
offensive

Examples

The novel features a gamin who survives by his wits on the city streets.
She took pity on the gamin begging near the train station.
Once a gamin, he grew up to become a successful artist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: street urchin - a child who spends a lot of time in the streets, often homeless or poor

Etymology

From French 'gamin' (street urchin, youngster).
