effluvium

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈfluviəm / (AmE), / ɪˈfluːviəm / (BrE)
a foul-smelling outflow or vapor, especially a gaseous waste
foul-smelling vapor or outflow

Examples

A strong effluvium seeped from the chemical plant, making it hard to breathe nearby.
After the pipe burst, a foul effluvium filled the air around the factory.
The stagnant pond gave off an effluvium that made the locals avoid it in summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miasma - an unpleasant or unhealthy vapor or atmosphere
  • Synonym: emanation - something that spreads out from a source, especially a gas or smell

Etymology

From Latin effluvium (discharge, outflow), from effluere (to flow out).
