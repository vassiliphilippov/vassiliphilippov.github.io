self-luminous

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlfˈluː.mə.nəs / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈluː.mɪ.nəs / (BrE)
Emitting light by its own inherent power, not as a result of reflected or transmitted light.
emitting light by itself

Examples

The firefly is a self-luminous insect, producing its own glow in the dark.
Jupiter is not self-luminous; it shines by reflecting the Sun's light.
The artist painted a self-luminous orb to represent an eternal flame.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luminous - emitting or reflecting light
  • Antonym: non-luminous - not emitting its own light

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' (by oneself) and 'luminous' (from Latin 'luminosus', full of light).

2. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlfˈluːmɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈluːmɪnəs / (BrE)
Emitting light by itself without requiring an external source of illumination.
emitting light on its own

Examples

The self-luminous dial on his wristwatch made it easy to check the time at night.
Certain deep-sea creatures are self-luminous and can produce their own light in the darkness.
Her new sculpture seemed almost self-luminous under the gallery lights, drawing all eyes to it.

Synset Examples

The self-luminous watch glowed brightly in the dark.
Her artwork had a self-luminous quality that captivated viewers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luminous - emitting or radiating light
  • Degree Lower: phosphorescent - emitting light without heat after exposure to radiation

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' (by oneself) and 'luminous' (full of light or shining), from Latin 'lumen' (light).
