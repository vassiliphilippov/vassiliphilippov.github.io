agrimony

1. NOUN

/ ˈæɡrəˌmoʊni / (AmE), / əˈɡrɪməni / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Agrimonia, typically with spikelike clusters of small yellow flowers, found in temperate regions.
a plant with small yellow flowers (genus Agrimonia)

Examples

The herbalist recommended agrimony for its traditional healing properties.
Agrimony grows wild along woodland edges and sunny meadows.
Clusters of yellow flowers identified the plant as agrimony in the field guide.

Etymology

From Latin 'agrimonia', from Greek 'argemōnē' (a plant used to treat cataracts).
