weapon

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛpən / (AmE), / ˈwɛpən / (BrE)
A thing used to gain an advantage in an argument or competition, especially in a figurative sense.
means of gaining advantage, especially figurative

Examples

Humor proved to be his most effective weapon in the debate.
She saw charm as a valuable weapon in business negotiations.
He brought out every conversational weapon he had to win the argument.

Synset Examples

he used all his conversational weapons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tool - something used to achieve a purpose, often figuratively

Etymology

From Old French *wepon* (weapon), from Proto-Germanic *wēpon.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɛp.ən / (AmE), / ˈwɛp.ən / (BrE)
An object or tool used to injure, defeat, or destroy someone or something, especially in fighting or hunting.
object used for fighting or hunting

Examples

He was arrested for carrying a concealed weapon without a permit.
The hunters equipped themselves with every type of weapon before heading into the forest.
A knife can be considered a weapon if it is used to threaten someone.

Synset Examples

he was licensed to carry a weapon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arm - a weapon, especially a firearm or armament
  • Degree Lower: tool - an instrument used for practical purposes, not specifically fighting
  • Degree Higher: arsenal - a collection or supply of weapons

Etymology

From Old English 'wæpen' (weapon, instrument of fighting), related to Old High German 'wafan'.

Synset POS: n
