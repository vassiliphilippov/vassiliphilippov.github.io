TNT

1. NOUN

/ ti ɛn ti / (AmE), / tiː en tiː / (BrE)
an explosive substance consisting of a yellow crystalline compound (trinitrotoluene), used especially in munitions
a powerful explosive chemical

Examples

The demolition team used TNT to bring down the old building safely.
Mining operations sometimes rely on TNT to blast through solid rock.
Because TNT is stable to handle, it's commonly used in military explosives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dynamite - a high explosive consisting of nitroglycerine mixed with an absorbent material

Etymology

Abbreviation of trinitrotoluene, from Latin roots: tri- (three), nitro (nitrogen), toluene (from tolu).
