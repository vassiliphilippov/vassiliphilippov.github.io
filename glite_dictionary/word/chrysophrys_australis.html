Chrysophrys australis

1. NOUN

/ kɹaɪˌsɒf.ɹɪs ˌɔːsˈtreɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / kɹɪˌsɒf.ɹɪs ˌɔːsˈtrɑː.lɪs / (BrE)
A dark-colored edible fish native to Australian waters, valued both as food and for sport fishing.
Australian edible and game fish species

Examples

The Chrysophrys australis is a highly prized catch among recreational fishers in Australia.
Chefs value Chrysophrys australis for its delicate flavor and firm white flesh.
Conservation efforts have helped stabilize Chrysophrys australis populations in some regions.

Etymology

From Greek 'chrysos' (gold) and 'ophrys' (eyebrow), and Latin 'australis' (southern), referring to its features and location.
