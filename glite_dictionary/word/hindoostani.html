Hindoostani

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪnduˈstɑni / (AmE), / ˌhɪnduˈstɑːni / (BrE)
an old term for a form of Hindi, especially as spoken around Delhi or used as a lingua franca in northern India
old term for Hindi dialect around Delhi

Examples

The British administration often used Hindoostani as a lingua franca in colonial India.
Poetry and literature in Hindoostani flourished during the Mughal era.
She learned to speak Hindoostani while living near Delhi with her family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hindi - principal language of northern India
  • Synonym: Urdu - a form of Hindustani with Persian-Arabic vocabulary

Etymology

From Persian Hindūstānī, meaning 'of Hindustan' (northern India).
