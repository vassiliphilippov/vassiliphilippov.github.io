finger

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪŋɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡə / (BrE)
a unit of linear measurement based on the width or length of a finger, used especially in older or literary contexts
a unit based on a finger's width or length
archaic

Examples

The recipe calls for a finger of ginger, roughly the width of your own.
He measured the depth of water by the simple method of using his finger as a guide.
The old carpenter estimated the gap to be about a finger wide.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡə / (BrE)
Each of the five long, thin parts at the end of the human hand, especially those apart from the thumb.
one of the digits of the hand

Examples

She wore a ring on her index finger every day.
He cut his finger while chopping vegetables.
The baby grabbed my finger and refused to let go.

Synset Examples

her fingers were long and thin

Related Terms

  • Antonym: thumb - the short, thick first digit of the human hand
  • Synonym: toe - one of the digits of the foot

Etymology

From Old English 'finger', of Germanic origin; related to Dutch 'vinger' and German 'Finger'.
