pointedly

1. ADV

/ ˈpɔɪntɪdli / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪntɪdli / (BrE)
in a way that is deliberate and intended to make something obvious or clearly evident
deliberately and obviously

Examples

She pointedly avoided making eye contact with him during the meeting.
He pointedly refused to answer the reporter's question.
The teacher pointedly mentioned the importance of punctuality after several students arrived late.

Synset Examples

he pointedly ignored the question

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deliberately - in a way that is done on purpose
  • Degree Higher: bluntly - in a very direct and sometimes rude manner
  • Degree Lower: subtly - in a way that is not obvious or makes something less evident

Etymology

From 'pointed' + '-ly', where 'pointed' comes from Middle English, from Anglo-Norman French pointé.
