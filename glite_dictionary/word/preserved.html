preserved

1. ADJ

/ prɪˈzɝvd / (AmE), / prɪˈzɜːvd / (BrE)
kept in its original state or in good condition, often by preventing decay, damage, or change.
kept in original or good condition

Examples

The ancient manuscripts were carefully preserved in the library's archives.
Her grandmother made preserved lemons using salt and spices.
This castle has been preserved in remarkable condition for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protected - kept safe from harm or damage
  • Antonym: spoiled - damaged, ruined, or no longer in a good condition

Etymology

Past participle of 'preserve,' from Latin 'praeservare' (to guard beforehand).

2. ADJ

/ prɪˈzɝvd / (AmE), / prɪˈzɜːvd / (BrE)
kept from decaying, spoiling, or being destroyed, often by being specially treated for future use
kept from decay or spoilage

Examples

The preserved fruit in the jar lasted all winter without spoiling.
Ancient organisms are sometimes found preserved in amber for millions of years.
She bought several cans of preserved vegetables for the trip.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fresh - not preserved, recently obtained or produced
  • Synonym: cured - preserved by methods such as salting, smoking, or drying
