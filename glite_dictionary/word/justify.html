to justify

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒʌstəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌstɪfaɪ / (BrE)
(in printing or computing) to adjust the spaces between words so that lines of text align evenly along left and right margins.
align text evenly by adjusting spaces

Examples

Most word processors have a feature to justify text automatically.
She prefers to justify both margins in her reports to give them a neat appearance.
You can justify paragraphs to make the left and right edges line up perfectly.

Synset Examples

justify the margins

2. VERB

/ ˈdʒʌstɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌstɪfaɪ / (BrE)
(Theology) (of God) to declare someone innocent or absolved, especially from the penalty of sin.
(theology) declare innocent or absolve from sin

Examples

Many Christians believe that God will justify those who have faith in Him.
Through grace, God is said to justify the repentant sinner.
The doctrine teaches that God alone has the power to justify a soul.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to absolve - to declare someone free from guilt or blame

Etymology

From Latin justificare, from justus (just) + facere (to make).

Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ ˈdʒʌstəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌstɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to show or prove that something is right, reasonable, or justified, often by providing reasons or evidence
prove something is right

Examples

The lawyer tried to justify his client's actions in court.
Nothing can justify the use of violence against innocent people.
She failed to justify her absence to her supervisor.

Synset Examples

vindicate a claim

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to vindicate - to clear someone of blame or suspicion
  • Synonym: excuse - to seek to lessen the blame for something
  • Antonym: to condemn - to express strong disapproval or to pronounce guilty

Etymology

From Latin justificare, from justus (just) + facere (to make).
