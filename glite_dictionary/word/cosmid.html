cosmid

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːz.mɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɒz.mɪd / (BrE)
(genetics) a type of cloning vector derived from a bacteriophage, used to transfer and clone genes or gene fragments in genetic engineering.
gene-cloning vector in genetics
medical

Examples

A cosmid can carry larger DNA fragments than a standard plasmid vector.
Researchers used a cosmid library to identify the gene responsible for the disorder.
The gene was successfully cloned using a cosmid vector.

Etymology

From ‘cos’ (cohesive end site, a sequence from lambda bacteriophage DNA) + ‘-mid’ (as in plasmid).
