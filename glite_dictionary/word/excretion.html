excretion

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈskriːʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˈskriːʃən / (BrE)
the process by which living organisms eliminate waste products from the body
elimination of bodily waste
medical

Examples

The kidneys play a vital role in the excretion of waste products from the blood.
Regular excretion is important for maintaining good health.
Some diseases can interfere with normal excretion and lead to toxin buildup.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: secretion - the process of producing and releasing substances by cells or glands

Etymology

From Latin 'excretio', from 'excretus' (separated, discharged).

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˈskriʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˈskriːʃən / (BrE)
waste matter, such as urine, feces, or sweat, that is discharged from the body
bodily waste eliminated from the body
medical

Examples

The body eliminates most chemical waste through excretion in urine and feces.
The doctor asked if the patient's excretion patterns had changed recently.
Sweat is a form of excretion that helps regulate body temperature.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: secretion - substances produced and released by cells or glands, not always waste

Etymology

From Latin excretio (a separating), from excretus, past participle of excernere (to separate).
