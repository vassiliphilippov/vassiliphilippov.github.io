retreat

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈtrit / (AmE), / rɪˈtriːt / (BrE)
the act of withdrawing or moving back, especially to escape danger or something unpleasant
act of withdrawing or moving back

Examples

The soldiers made a hasty retreat when the enemy launched a counterattack.
After her embarrassing mistake, she beat a quiet retreat from the stage.
The hikers planned their retreat once they noticed the storm approaching.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: advance - the act of moving forward toward a goal
  • Synonym: withdrawal - the action of withdrawing something

Etymology

From Old French 'retret' (withdrawal), from Latin 'retrahere' (to pull back).
