lukewarm

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɔkˌwɔrm / (AmE), / ˈluːkˌwɔːm / (BrE)
showing little enthusiasm, interest, or conviction; unenthusiastic or indifferent
unenthusiastic or indifferent

Examples

The proposal was met with a lukewarm response from the committee.
Her performance received only lukewarm applause from the audience.
He gave a lukewarm endorsement of the candidate, failing to show real support.

Synset Examples

a halfhearted effort
gave only lukewarm support to the candidate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halfhearted - showing little enthusiasm or effort
  • Antonym: enthusiastic - feeling or showing intense interest or excitement

Etymology

From Middle English 'lew', meaning tepid, and 'warm'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈluːkˌwɔrm / (AmE), / ˈluːkˌwɔːm / (BrE)
(of liquid or food) only moderately warm; neither hot nor cold.
moderately warm (not hot or cold)

Examples

The soup was only lukewarm, so I put it back in the microwave.
She gave his idea a lukewarm reception, showing little enthusiasm.
Please make sure the baby's bottle is lukewarm before feeding her.

Synset Examples

he hates lukewarm coffee
tepid bath water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tepid - slightly warm; lukewarm
  • Degree Higher: hot - having a high degree of heat or a high temperature
  • Degree Lower: cold - having a low temperature, especially when compared to the temperature of the human body

Etymology

From Middle English 'lewke', from Old English 'hlēow' (warm) + 'warm'.
