to reharmonise

1. VERB

/ riˈhɑːrmənaɪz / (AmE), / riˈhɑːməniːz / (BrE)
to provide a different harmony or harmonic accompaniment to an existing melody or musical passage
provide new harmony to music

Examples

The jazz pianist decided to reharmonise the standard tune for a fresh, modern sound.
Composers often reharmonise melodies to create different moods or emotions within a piece.
She chose to reharmonise the song for her arrangement, giving it a unique and unexpected twist.

Synset Examples

reharmonize the melody

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to harmonise - to provide harmony to music

Etymology

From re- (again) + harmonise, ultimately from Greek harmonia (agreement, harmony).
