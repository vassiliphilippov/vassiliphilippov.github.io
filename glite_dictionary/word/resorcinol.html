resorcinol

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsɔrsɪnˌɔl / (AmE), / rɪˈsɔːsɪnɒl / (BrE)
a crystalline organic compound used especially in the preparation of dyes and as an ingredient in some medical ointments and shampoos
crystalline compound used in dyes and medicated products
medical

Examples

The dermatologist prescribed a cream containing resorcinol to treat stubborn acne on her face.
Many dandruff shampoos list resorcinol as an active ingredient because of its antifungal properties.
Chemists often use resorcinol as a starting material in the synthesis of azo dyes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phenol - an aromatic organic compound used in chemical manufacture

Etymology

From resorcin (from resin and orcin) + -ol (denoting alcohols).
