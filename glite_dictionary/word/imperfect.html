imperfect

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɜr.fɪkt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜː.fɪkt / (BrE)
not perfect; having faults or flaws; not complete or fully adequate.
not perfect; having flaws

Examples

Our knowledge of the past is always imperfect, leaving room for speculation and debate.
The building's imperfect foundation caused cracks to appear in the walls over time.
Despite its imperfect design, the machine was still able to function reasonably well.

Synset Examples

had only an imperfect understanding of his responsibilities
imperfect mortals
drainage here is imperfect

Related Terms

  • Antonym: perfect - having all the required or desirable elements; free from flaws
  • Synonym: flawed - having imperfections or faults

Etymology

From Latin 'imperfectus', from 'im-' (not) + 'perfectus' (completed, perfect).

2. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɝːfɪkt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜːfɪkt / (BrE)
(in grammar) a verb tense used to describe an action or state that was ongoing or repeated in the past.
past tense showing ongoing/repeated action

Examples

In Spanish, the imperfect is used to talk about habitual actions in the past.
The imperfect tense often expresses an action without a definite endpoint.
She struggled to decide if the sentence should use the preterite or the imperfect.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: perfect - a verb tense indicating a completed action

Etymology

From Latin 'imperfectus' (unfinished, incomplete), used in grammar to contrast with the 'perfect' tense.

Synset POS: n
