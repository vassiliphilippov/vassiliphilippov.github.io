voicelessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɔɪsləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈvɔɪsləsnɪs / (BrE)
the state of having no influence or ability to participate in the management or control of affairs
lack of influence or say in affairs

Examples

The report highlights the voicelessness experienced by migrant workers in many countries.
Activists aim to speak for the voicelessness found within marginalized communities.
Her sense of voicelessness grew as decisions were made without her input.

Synset Examples

the voicelessness of those who live in situations of hopelessness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powerlessness - lack of power or ability to act
  • Antonym: empowerment - the act of giving power or authority

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɔɪslɪsnəs / (AmE), / ˈvɔɪslɪsnɪs / (BrE)
(Phonetics) The quality or state of being pronounced without vibration of the vocal cords, as in sounds like /t/, /s/, or /f/.
absence of vocal cord vibration in speech sounds
medical

Examples

The voicelessness of the sound /s/ distinguishes it from its voiced counterpart /z/.
In English, the voicelessness of certain consonants can change the meaning of a word.
Students learned to produce voicelessness by not vibrating their vocal cords during pronunciation exercises.

Synset Examples

Instead, he plans to maintain voicelessness of the consonant and an unintended consequence of that effort is a pitch perturbation following release.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: voicedness - state of being spoken with vocal cord vibration (as in /b/ or /z/)

3. NOUN

/ ˈvɔɪsləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈvɔɪslɪsnəs / (BrE)
the state or condition of being unable to produce voice, especially due to a disorder of the vocal organs
loss or inability to produce voice

Examples

After throat surgery, Mary experienced temporary voicelessness and had to communicate by writing.
The singer feared that chronic laryngitis might lead to permanent voicelessness.
Therapy and rest helped him recover from a period of voicelessness following the illness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphonia - loss of ability to speak due to vocal cord damage

Etymology

From 'voiceless' + '-ness'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈvɔɪsləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈvɔɪsləsnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being produced without vocal cord vibration, especially in speech sounds
absence of vocal cord vibration

Examples

The distinction between voicelessness and voicing is important when learning English pronunciation.
Many consonants, such as 'p' and 't', are characterized by their voicelessness.
Whispered speech is notable for its complete voicelessness throughout.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: voicedness - state of being produced with vocal cord vibration

Etymology

From 'voiceless' + '-ness'.
