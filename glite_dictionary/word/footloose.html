footloose

1. ADJ

/ ˈfʊtˌlus / (AmE), / ˈfʊtˌluːs / (BrE)
free to go or do as one pleases; not tied down by responsibilities or commitments
free, unrestricted; not tied down

Examples

After graduating from college, she felt footloose and ready for adventure.
Many people envy the footloose lifestyle of digital nomads who work from anywhere.
He remained footloose until his late thirties, traveling and exploring new countries.

Synset Examples

Americans have always been a footloose people always moving on
a footloose young man eager to see the big city

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unattached - not connected or committed to anyone or anything
  • Antonym: tied down - restricted by responsibilities or commitments

Etymology

From foot + loose, first recorded in the 17th century, originally meaning 'free to move about'.
