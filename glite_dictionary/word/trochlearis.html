trochlearis

1. NOUN

/ troʊˌkliːəˈrɪs / (AmE), / trɒkˌlɪəˈrɪs / (BrE)
either of the two fourth cranial nerves that control the superior oblique muscles of the eyes
the fourth cranial nerve of the eye
medical

Examples

Damage to the trochlearis can cause double vision when looking downwards.
The surgeon carefully avoided injuring the trochlearis during the procedure.
Anatomy students must learn the exact pathway of the trochlearis for exams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trochlear nerve - the fourth cranial nerve, controls movement of the superior oblique muscle

Etymology

From Latin 'trochlea' (pulley), referring to the nerve's passage through a pulley-like structure.
