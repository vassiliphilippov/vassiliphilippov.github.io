rugelach

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːɡəlɑx / (AmE), / ˈruːɡələx / (BrE)
A small, crescent-shaped pastry made with a cream cheese dough and filled with ingredients such as raisins, walnuts, cinnamon, chocolate, or fruit preserves.
crescent-shaped pastry with sweet fillings

Examples

My grandmother makes the best rugelach with chocolate and walnuts for every holiday.
At the bakery, I couldn't resist buying a dozen freshly baked rugelach.
The cookbook includes a traditional Jewish recipe for rugelach filled with apricot preserves.

Etymology

From Yiddish רוגעלעך (rugelekh), plural of רוגל (rogel), meaning 'little twist' or 'spiral'.
