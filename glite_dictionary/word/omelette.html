omelette

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːmlət / (AmE), / ˈɒmlət / (BrE)
A dish made by beating eggs and frying them until just set, often folded around a filling such as cheese, ham, or vegetables.
egg dish cooked and often folded with filling

Examples

She made a delicious omelette with mushrooms and spinach for breakfast.
His favorite lunch is a cheese and ham omelette served with toast.
Would you like your omelette plain or with vegetables?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frittata - an Italian egg dish similar to an omelette, but not folded

Etymology

From French 'omelette', variant of earlier 'amelette', from 'alemette', derived from 'alemelle' (blade; referring to its flat shape).
