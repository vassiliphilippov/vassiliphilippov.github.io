Dorking

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔːrkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɔːkɪŋ / (BrE)
an English breed of large domestic chicken known for having five toes instead of the usual four
English breed of chicken with five toes

Examples

The farmer raised several Dorking chickens renowned for their unusual five-toed feet.
A Dorking is often recognized by its stocky body and gentle temperament.
Many poultry enthusiasts appreciate the heritage value of the Dorking breed.

Etymology

Named after the town of Dorking in Surrey, England, where the breed was developed.
