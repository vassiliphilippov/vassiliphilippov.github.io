Galilaean

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡælɪˈliːən / (AmE), / ˌɡalɪˈliːən / (BrE)
A native or inhabitant of Galilee, especially used as an epithet for Jesus Christ.
inhabitant of Galilee; epithet for Jesus

Examples

The early Christians were often referred to as Galilaeans by their contemporaries.
Jesus is sometimes called the Galilaean in historical texts.
A proud Galilaean stood before the crowd, proclaiming his origins.

Etymology

From Latin Galilaeus, from Greek Galilaios, meaning 'of Galilee'.

2. ADJ

/ ɡəˌlɪˈliən / (AmE), / ɡəˌlɪˈliːən / (BrE)
of or relating to Galilee or its inhabitants.
of Galilee or its people

Examples

The ancient city had a thriving Galilaean community during the first century.
He spoke with a distinct Galilaean accent that set him apart from the others.
Many Galilaean traditions have been preserved through centuries of cultural exchange.

Etymology

From Latin Galilaeus, from Greek Galilaios, meaning 'of Galilee'.
