appendage

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɛndɪdʒ / (AmE), / əˈpɛndɪdʒ / (BrE)
a part that is attached to something larger or more important, especially a limb or similar structure; also, something secondary or subsidiary added to a main body or system
a part joined to something larger

Examples

The crab used its large appendage to protect itself from predators.
A leaf is an appendage of a plant's stem.
Some regarded the new committee as merely an appendage to the main organization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limb - an arm, leg, or similar appendage of an animal
  • Antonym: main body - the principal or central part to which an appendage is attached

Etymology

From Latin 'appendere' meaning 'to hang upon'.

2. NOUN

/ əˈpɛndɪdʒ / (AmE), / əˈpɛndɪdʒ / (BrE)
an external body part that protrudes from the main body, such as a limb or tail
external projecting body part

Examples

The octopus uses each appendage independently to explore its environment.
Insects have various appendages such as antennae, legs, and wings.
A lobster’s claw is an appendage specialized for grasping and defense.

Synset Examples

it is important to keep the extremities warm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limb - an arm, leg, wing, or similar projecting body part

Etymology

From Latin appendere (to hang upon), via appendere (append) + -age.

3. NOUN

/ əˈpɛndɪdʒ / (AmE), / əˈpɛndɪdʒ / (BrE)
a part that is attached to something larger or more important, especially a natural projection on an animal or plant, such as a limb, tail, or fin
body part attached to an organism

Examples

The starfish uses each appendage to move slowly across the ocean floor.
A crab’s pincers are specialized appendages used for feeding and defense.
Botanists often examine the small appendage found at the base of certain leaves.

Synset Examples

a bony process

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limb - an arm, leg, or similar body part of an animal

Etymology

From Latin appendere (to hang upon), from ad- (to) + pendere (to hang).

Source: WordNet
