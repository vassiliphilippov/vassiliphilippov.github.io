Parnassianism

1. NOUN

/ pɑrˈnæsiəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / pɑːˈnasɪəˌnɪzəm / (BrE)
A literary movement among 19th-century French poets characterized by an emphasis on formal precision, objective expression, and the principle of 'art for art's sake.'
19th-century French poetic movement

Examples

The rise of Parnassianism marked a shift away from the overt emotion of Romantic poetry.
Many poets influenced by Parnassianism sought technical perfection in their verse.
His criticism of Parnassianism centered on its denial of passionate feeling in poetry.

Synset Examples

In the early 1880s he embarked on a literary career in Paris, rejecting with equal vehemence the Naturalism of Émile Zola and the Parnassianism of Leconte de Lisle.

Etymology

From Mount Parnassus, sacred to Apollo and the Muses in Greek mythology, via French 'parnassien.'
