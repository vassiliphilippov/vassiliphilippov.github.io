antimagnetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.ti.mæɡˈnɛt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.mæɡˈnɛt.ɪk / (BrE)
Not affected by magnetic fields; resistant to becoming magnetized.
not affected by magnetic fields

Examples

This antimagnetic watch is ideal for engineers working near strong magnets.
Many modern devices have antimagnetic components to prevent interference.
The laboratory required antimagnetic tools to ensure accurate experiments.

Synset Examples

an antimagnetic watch

Related Terms

  • Antonym: magnetic - having the property of attracting iron and other metals

Etymology

From anti- (against) + magnetic, denoting resistance to magnetism.
