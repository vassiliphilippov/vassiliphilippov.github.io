bin

1. NOUN

/ bɪn / (AmE), / bɪn / (BrE)
a container, typically with a lid, used for storing or disposing of items such as trash, food, or commodities
container, usually with a lid

Examples

He threw the empty can into the bin in the corner of the kitchen.
The recycling bin is collected every Tuesday morning.
Please place your unwanted clothes in the donation bin outside the store.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: box - a container with flat sides and a lid, typically rectangular
  • Synonym: barrel - a cylindrical container typically made of wood, metal, or plastic

Etymology

From Old French *bin* (basket, bin), probably of Germanic origin.

2. VERB

/ bɪn / (AmE), / bɪn / (BrE)
to place or store (something) in a bin or container for storage
to store in a bin or container

Examples

After sorting the apples, workers binned them for shipment.
Please bin all recyclables as soon as you finish with them.
The warehouse staff quickly bins the items to keep the floor clear.
