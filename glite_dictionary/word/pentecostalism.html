Pentecostalism

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛn.tɪˈkɑː.stəˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌpɛn.tɪˈkɒs.təˌlɪz.əm / (BrE)
A form of Christianity emphasizing direct personal experience of God through baptism with the Holy Spirit, often marked by speaking in tongues, healing, and enthusiastic worship.
Christian movement focused on the Holy Spirit

Examples

Pentecostalism spread rapidly across the globe during the twentieth century.
Her family practices Pentecostalism, and church services are lively and filled with singing.
Many aspects of Pentecostalism focus on spiritual gifts and direct experiences of faith.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Evangelicalism - a Protestant movement emphasizing the authority of the Bible and conversion
  • Synonym: fundamentalism - strict adherence to basic religious principles

Etymology

From 'Pentecostal', referring to Pentecost, a Jewish festival mentioned in the New Testament, and '-ism'.
