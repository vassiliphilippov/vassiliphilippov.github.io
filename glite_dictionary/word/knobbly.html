knobbly

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɑːbli / (AmE), / ˈnɒbli / (BrE)
having small rounded lumps or knobs on the surface; uneven or bumpy
having small lumps or knobs

Examples

The old tree had a knobbly trunk covered with rough bark.
His knobbly knees stuck out from his shorts when he sat down.
She chose the knobbly potatoes for roasting.

Synset Examples

had knobbly knees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bumpy - having many raised areas or lumps on the surface
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface with no lumps or bumps

Etymology

From 'knob' + '-ly', following the pattern of forming adjectives from nouns.
