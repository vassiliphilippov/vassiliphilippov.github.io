Eriophyllum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛriˈɑfɪləm / (AmE), / ˌɛriˈɒfɪləm / (BrE)
A genus of hairy herbs and shrubs native to western North America, known for their yellow, daisy-like flowers.
genus of western North American herbs and shrubs

Examples

The wildflowers blooming along the trail included Eriophyllum with its distinctive yellow blossoms.
Many species of Eriophyllum prefer dry, sandy soils in open habitats.
Botanists are studying how Eriophyllum adapts to climate changes in its native regions.

Etymology

From Greek 'erion' (wool) + 'phyllon' (leaf), referring to the woolly leaves of these plants.

Examples
