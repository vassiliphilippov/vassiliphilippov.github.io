meagrely

1. ADV

/ ˈmiɡɚli / (AmE), / ˈmiːɡəli / (BrE)
In a way that is lacking in quantity, quality, or extent; to a meagre degree.
in a sparse or insufficient manner

Examples

The food was meagrely distributed among the survivors after the disaster.
Their savings were meagrely invested, yielding minimal returns over the years.
The local wildlife is meagrely represented in this remote region.

Synset Examples

these voices are meagerly represented at the conference
the area is slenderly endowed with natural resources

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sparsely - in a way that is thinly scattered or distributed
  • Antonym: abundantly - in large quantities; plentifully

Etymology

From 'meagre' (from Old French maigre, from Latin macer 'thin'), with adverbial -ly suffix.
