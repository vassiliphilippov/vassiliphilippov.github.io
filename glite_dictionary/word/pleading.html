pleading

1. NOUN

/ ˈpliːdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpliːdɪŋ / (BrE)
(Law) a formal written statement submitted to a court by a party in a legal case, outlining the party's claims, defenses, or other matters.
legal statement in a court case
legal

Examples

The attorney submitted a pleading to the court, outlining her client's defense against the charges.
All pleadings must be filed within 30 days of the initial lawsuit according to court rules.
The judge reviewed the pleading to determine if there was sufficient evidence to proceed with the trial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motion - a formal request made to a court during a case
  • Antonym: verdict - the decision made by a jury or judge in a trial

Etymology

From Old French *plaidier* (to plead), from Medieval Latin *placitare* (to plead a case).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpliːdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpliːdɪŋ / (BrE)
showing or expressing a strong, often emotional request or appeal; begging for something earnestly
expressing an urgent or begging request

Examples

She gave him a pleading look, hoping he would change his mind.
His pleading voice made it difficult to refuse his request.
With pleading eyes, the child asked for another piece of cake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: begging - asking for something urgently or desperately
  • Antonym: demanding - insisting firmly on something

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'plead', from Old French 'plaider' (to plead a case), from Medieval Latin 'placitare'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
