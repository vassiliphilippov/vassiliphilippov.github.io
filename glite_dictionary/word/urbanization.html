urbanization

1. NOUN

/ ˌɝː.bən.əˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɜː.bən.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process by which towns and cities grow as more people begin living and working in central areas
the growth of cities and urban life

Examples

Rapid urbanization has transformed many rural villages into bustling cities.
The government is preparing for the challenges brought by increased urbanization.
Experts debate the environmental impact of large-scale urbanization.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: suburbanization - the process of population movement from cities to surrounding suburbs

Etymology

From urban +‎ -ization; urban from Latin urbs (city).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɜrbənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɜːbənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which an area becomes urban, involving an increase in the proportion of people living in cities and towns
the process of becoming urban

Examples

Rapid urbanization has transformed the rural landscape into a bustling metropolis.
Many countries are facing challenges due to the effects of urbanization on housing and infrastructure.
The urbanization of coastal regions often leads to environmental concerns such as pollution.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: suburbanization - the process of population movement from cities to suburbs

Etymology

From urban + -ization, modeled on French urbanisation.
