justness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌst.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌs(t).nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being just, fair, or morally right.
fairness; quality of being just

Examples

The judge was admired for the justness of her decisions.
People seek justness in the laws that govern society.
The justness of their actions was later recognized by the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fairness - The quality of treating people equally or in a way that is right or reasonable
  • Antonym: injustice - The lack of fairness or justice

Etymology

From Latin 'justus' (just) with the English suffix '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌstnəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌstnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being in accordance with standards of correctness, fairness, or propriety
quality of being just, fair, or proper

Examples

The justness of the judge's decision was praised by all parties involved.
She argued for the justness of her actions, insisting she had acted fairly.
The justness and beauty of the performance impressed the entire audience.

Synset Examples

it was performed with justness and beauty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fairness - quality of being fair and impartial
  • Antonym: injustice - lack of fairness or justice

Etymology

From just + -ness, ultimately from Latin 'justus' (just, fair).
