Mammuthus primigenius

1. NOUN

/ məˈmjuːθəs praɪˌmɪdʒiˈeɪniəs / (AmE), / məˈmæmjuːθəs prɪˌmɪdʒɪˈeɪnɪəs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the woolly mammoth, a large, hairy extinct elephant that lived in cold regions of the Northern Hemisphere during the Ice Age
scientific name for the woolly mammoth

Examples

Samples of Mammuthus primigenius DNA have given scientists new insights into Ice Age life.
The remains of Mammuthus primigenius have been discovered in both Europe and North America.
Researchers believe that Mammuthus primigenius became extinct about 4,000 years ago.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woolly mammoth - an extinct, hairy elephant adapted to cold climates

Etymology

From Latin: genus Mammuthus (mammoth) + species primigenius (original, first-born), translating to 'original mammoth.'
