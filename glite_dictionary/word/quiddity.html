quiddity

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪdəti / (AmE), / ˈkwɪdɪti / (BrE)
A subtle or evasive distinction or objection, especially one that distracts from the main point of an argument.
subtle or evasive distinction in argument

Examples

The lawyer's quiddity during cross-examination frustrated the judge and wasted everyone's time.
Rather than address my concerns, he offered a quiddity about the definition of a single term.
Her argument dissolved into quiddity instead of focusing on the real issue at hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quibble - a trivial objection or criticism; especially one meant to evade the main point

Etymology

From Medieval Latin quiddītās, from Latin quidditas (essence), from quid (what).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪd.ɪ.ti / (AmE), / ˈkwɪd.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The essential nature or quality that makes something what it is; the distinctive essence of a thing.
essential quality; distinctive essence

Examples

The philosopher pondered the quiddity of human existence, searching for what makes us uniquely human.
Each species possesses its own quiddity, distinguishing it from all others.
She struggled to express the quiddity of the painting—the quality that set it apart from mundane works.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: essence - the intrinsic nature or indispensable quality of something
  • Antonym: haecceity - the property of being a unique individual thing

Etymology

From Medieval Latin quiddītās (essence), from Latin quid (what) with the suffix -ity.
