lonely

1. ADJ

/ ˈloʊnli / (AmE), / ˈləʊnli / (BrE)
Preferring or characterized by solitude; tending to be alone by choice or situation.
preferring or characterized by solitude

Examples

After his retirement, he led a lonely life in the countryside.
She enjoyed her lonely walks along the quiet beach at dawn.
The old house stood at the end of a lonely road, far from the nearest village.

Synset Examples

a lone wolf
a lonely existence
a man of a solitary disposition
a solitary walk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solitary - preferring to be alone or existing alone

Etymology

From lone + -ly, formed in English in the 16th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈloʊnli / (AmE), / ˈləʊnli / (BrE)
(of a place) having no people or animals present; deserted or unfrequented.
deserted or unfrequented (of a place)

Examples

We reached a lonely stretch of beach untouched by tourists.
The old farmhouse stood on a lonely road miles from the nearest town.
She enjoyed hiking in the lonely woods where no one else ventured.

Synset Examples

a lonely crossroads
a solitary retreat
a trail leading to an unfrequented lake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deserted - (of a place) empty of people
  • Antonym: populated - (of a place) inhabited or full of people

Etymology

From 'lone' (shortened from 'alone') + -ly.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01316603-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈloʊnli / (AmE), / ˈləʊnli / (BrE)
feeling sad because one has no friends or company; experiencing dejection from being alone
sad because one is alone

Examples

She felt lonely after moving to a new city where she knew no one.
It was the loneliest winter he had ever experienced, far from family and friends.
He sometimes grows lonely living by himself in the countryside.

Synset Examples

felt sad and lonely
the loneliest night of the week
lonesome when her husband is away
spent a lonesome hour in the bar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lonesome - feeling lonely; solitary and sad
  • Synonym: solitary - preferring to be alone or existing alone; not accompanied
  • Antonym: popular - liked or enjoyed by many people, often not alone

Etymology

From 'lone' + -ly; 'lone' is an alteration of 'alone'.

4. ADJ

/ ˈloʊnli / (AmE), / ˈləʊnli / (BrE)
feeling sad because one has no friends or company; lacking companionship.
feeling sad and alone; without company

Examples

After moving to a new city, she felt especially lonely during the holidays.
The lonely cottage stood at the edge of the silent forest.
Despite being surrounded by people, he couldn't shake his lonely feeling.

Synset Examples

he was alone when we met him
she is alone much of the time
the lone skier on the mountain
a lonely fisherman stood on a tuft of gravel
a lonely soul
a solitary traveler

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alone - having no one else present
  • Synonym: solitary - existing or living alone
  • Antonym: popular - liked or enjoyed by many people; not lonely

Etymology

Formed from 'lone' (shortened from 'alone') + -ly, first used in the 16th century.
