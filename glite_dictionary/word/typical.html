typical

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɪpɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈtɪpɪkəl / (BrE)
Having the characteristic qualities of a particular person, thing, or group; representative or distinctive of a particular kind.
characteristic; representative quality

Examples

It was typical of him to arrive late to important meetings.
This building is typical of Victorian architecture.
Her response was typical of someone who cares deeply about the issue.

Synset Examples

Jerusalem has a distinctive Middle East flavor
that is typical of you!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: atypical - not representative or characteristic; not typical
  • Synonym: characteristic - typical of a particular person, place, or thing

Etymology

From Late Latin typicus (of a type), from Greek typikos, from typos (impression, figure, type).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtɪpɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈtɪpɪkəl / (BrE)
having the usual qualities or characteristics of a particular type of person, thing, or group; representing what is normal for a particular category.
having the usual qualities of a type

Examples

She gave a typical answer, calm and thoughtful as always.
Rainy weather is typical for this region in spring.
That meal was a typical example of local cuisine.

Synset Examples

a typical American girl
a typical suburban community
the typical car owner drives 10,000 miles a year
a painting typical of the Impressionist school
a typical romantic poem
a typical case of arteritis

Related Terms

  • Antonym: atypical - not typical; not having the usual qualities or characteristics
  • Synonym: representative - typical of a particular group, class, or body of opinion

Etymology

From Greek typikos (of a type, symbolic), from typos (a type, figure).
