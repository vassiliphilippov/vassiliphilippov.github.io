Falstaff

1. PROPN

/ ˈfæl.stæf / (AmE), / ˈfæl.stɑːf / (BrE)
A dissolute and humorous character in several plays by Shakespeare, known for his wit, boastfulness, and love of drink and mischief.
comic, dissolute Shakespearean character

Examples

In many discussions of Shakespeare, Falstaff is cited as one of the greatest comic characters.
The actor delivered his portrayal of Falstaff with both humor and pathos.
Her laughter echoed through the theater during every scene featuring Falstaff.

Etymology

Named after Sir John Falstaff, a character created by William Shakespeare.
