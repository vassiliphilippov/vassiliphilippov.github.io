robin

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːbɪn / (AmE), / ˈrɒbɪn / (BrE)
A small Old World songbird with a red or orange breast, common in Europe and parts of Asia.
small songbird with a red breast

Examples

A robin perched on the garden fence and sang a cheerful tune.
Every spring, the sight of a robin signals the start of warmer weather.
The bright red breast of the robin makes it easy to identify among other birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: European robin - the specific European species (Erithacus rubecula) commonly referred to as robin

Etymology

From Middle English 'robyn', pet form of the given name Robert, traditionally applied to the bird.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɑbɪn / (AmE), / ˈrɒbɪn / (BrE)
a large North American thrush with a rust-red breast and abdomen, commonly seen in gardens and forests
North American songbird with red breast

Examples

A robin perched on the fence, its red breast clearly visible against the snow.
We watched a robin build its nest in our backyard tree.
In spring, the arrival of the first robin is often seen as a sign that winter is ending.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrush - a type of songbird related to the robin

Etymology

From Old French *robyn*, used as a pet form of the name Robert and later applied to the bird.
