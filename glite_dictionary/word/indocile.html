indocile

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdoʊsəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈdəʊsaɪl / (BrE)
Not easily controlled, disciplined, or managed; unruly or resistant to authority.
not easily controlled or managed

Examples

The young horse proved indocile, resisting every attempt at training.
A classroom full of indocile students can challenge even the most experienced teacher.
Her indocile nature made it hard for her to accept authority without question.

Synset Examples

the little boy's parents think he is spirited, but his teacher finds him unruly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unruly - difficult to control or discipline
  • Antonym: docile - easily taught or managed

Etymology

From Latin 'indocilis,' from 'in-' (not) + 'docilis' (teachable).
