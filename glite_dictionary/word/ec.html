EC

1. NOUN

/ i si / (AmE), / iː siː / (BrE)
abbreviation for European Community, an international organization of European countries formed after World War II to promote economic cooperation and reduce trade barriers; later incorporated into the European Union.
European Community (European organization)

Examples

The collapse of the Soviet Union paved the way for the expansion of the EC.
Britain joined the EC in 1973, significantly impacting its trade policies.
The creation of the single market was a milestone for the EC.

Synset Examples

he tried to take Britain into the European Union

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: European Union - political and economic union of European countries that succeeded the EC

Etymology

Abbreviation of European Community.
