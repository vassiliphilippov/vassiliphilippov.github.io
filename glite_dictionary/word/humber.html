Humber

1. PROPN

/ ˈhʌmbər / (AmE), / ˈhʌmbə / (BrE)
an estuary in northeastern England, formed by the confluence of the Ouse and Trent rivers, flowing into the North Sea
estuary in northeastern England

Examples

The Humber Bridge spans the estuary, providing a vital link between the two banks.
Shipping on the Humber has played a key role in the region’s economy.
Birdwatchers flock to reserves along the Humber for its rich wildlife.

Etymology

Possibly from Old English *Humbra*, an ancient river name of unknown origin.
