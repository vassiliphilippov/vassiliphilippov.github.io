squealer

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwiːlər / (AmE), / ˈskwiːlə / (BrE)
a domestic pig, especially a young one; a piglet
a young domestic pig; piglet

Examples

The children at the farm loved feeding the smallest squealer in the pen.
A newborn squealer can make a surprising amount of noise.
Every spring, dozens of squealers are born on the ranch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piglet - a young pig

Etymology

From 'squeal' (the sound pigs make) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈskwiːlər / (AmE), / ˈskwiːlə / (BrE)
a person who informs on others, especially to the police; an informer
an informer or police informant
slang

Examples

Nobody trusts him anymore since he became a squealer for the police.
The gang kicked out the squealer who revealed their plans to the authorities.
Calling someone a squealer can cause real trouble in some neighborhoods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: informer - a person who gives information about others, especially to the police
  • Synonym: snitch - a person who secretly gives information about someone to the authorities

Etymology

From 'squeal' (to betray by giving information) + -er (agent noun suffix).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
