weathered

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɛðərd / (AmE), / ˈwɛðəd / (BrE)
(of an object or surface) worn or altered in appearance by long exposure to the weather.
worn by exposure to weather

Examples

The old barn had a weathered red door that creaked in the wind.
Her hands were rough and weathered from years of outdoor work.
We admired the weathered stones that lined the ancient garden path.

Synset Examples

a house of weathered shingles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aged - (of objects or materials) having acquired a look or quality due to time
  • Antonym: pristine - in perfect condition; unspoiled, not weathered
