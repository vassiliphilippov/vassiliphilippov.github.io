Filago

1. NOUN

/ fɪˈleɪɡoʊ / (AmE), / fɪˈlɑːɡəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of small woolly herbs in the family Asteraceae, commonly known as cottonrose or cudweed.
a genus of small woolly herbs

Examples

Several species within the genus Filago are adapted to dry, sandy soils.
The small, woolly appearance of Filago makes it easy to identify in the wild.
Botanists studied the distribution of Filago across Mediterranean regions.

Etymology

From Latin *filago*, referring to a plant used for making wicks, related to *filum* (thread).
