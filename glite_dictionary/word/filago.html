filago

1. NOUN

/ fəˈleɪɡoʊ / (AmE), / fɪˈlɑːɡəʊ / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Filago, characterized by small woolly flower heads that form capitate clusters.
plant genus with woolly flower heads

Examples

The fields were dotted with patches of filago among the grasses.
Botanists studied the unique structure of the filago flower heads.
Some species of filago are used in traditional herbal remedies.

Etymology

From Latin 'filago', a plant name, possibly related to 'filum' meaning thread, referring to the woolly appearance.
