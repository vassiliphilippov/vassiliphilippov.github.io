Caprimulgidae

1. NOUN

/ kæprəˈmʌlɡɪˌdiː / (AmE), / kæprɪˈmʌldʒɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of nocturnal birds known as goatsuckers, including nightjars and nighthawks, characterized by their camouflaged plumage and silent flight.
family of nocturnal birds (goatsuckers)

Examples

The Caprimulgidae are renowned for their cryptic plumage that blends seamlessly into their surroundings.
Several species in the Caprimulgidae family are found in both North and South America.
Scientists have long studied the Caprimulgidae to understand their nocturnal hunting strategies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nightjar - a nocturnal bird in the Caprimulgidae family
  • Synonym: nighthawk - a night-flying bird in the Caprimulgidae family

Etymology

From Latin 'Caprimulgus' (goatsucker), from 'capra' (goat) and 'mulgere' (to milk), plus taxonomic suffix '-idae'.
