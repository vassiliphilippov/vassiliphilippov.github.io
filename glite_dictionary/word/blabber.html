blabber

1. NOUN

/ ˈblæbər / (AmE), / ˈblæbə / (BrE)
a person who reveals confidential information, especially to authorities.
person who reveals secrets, esp. to authorities
informal

Examples

The gang refused to trust anyone they thought might be a blabber.
No one wanted to be labeled a blabber after last year’s incident.
She was called a blabber for telling the principal about the secret meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: informant - a person who provides confidential information to authorities
  • Synonym: whistleblower - a person who exposes confidential or illicit activities within an organization
  • Antonym: confidant - a person with whom one shares confidential information

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'blabber', likely imitative of the sound of babbling or talking excessively.

2. VERB

/ ˈblæb.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈblæb.ə / (BrE)
to talk rapidly and continuously about trivial or unimportant things
talk rapidly about trivial things
informal

Examples

Please don't blabber all the details of our conversation to everyone at work.
She tends to blabber on the phone for hours about nothing in particular.
He was blabbering so much that I could barely concentrate on my work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chatter - to talk quickly about unimportant things
  • Synonym: babble - to talk quickly in a way that is difficult to understand, often about unimportant things
  • Antonym: to clam up - to suddenly stop talking, especially when nervous or unwilling

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English *blaberen*, of imitative origin.
