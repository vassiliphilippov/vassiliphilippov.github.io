fardel

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑrdəl / (AmE), / ˈfɑːdəl / (BrE)
A burden, especially one regarded as a heavy or oppressive load; often used figuratively or poetically.
a burden or oppressive load
archaic

Examples

Hamlet speaks of the heartache and the thousand natural fardels that flesh is heir to.
The weary traveler carried his fardel through the deserted streets, weighed down by years of hardship.
She laid her emotional fardel aside, determined to start anew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burden - a heavy load or duty

Etymology

From Old French 'fardel' (package, bundle), from Medieval Latin 'fardellus'.
