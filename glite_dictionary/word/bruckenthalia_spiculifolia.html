Bruckenthalia spiculifolia

1. NOUN

/ brʌkˈɛnθəliə spaɪˌkjuloʊˈfaɪliə / (AmE), / brʊkˈɛnθəliə spɪˌkjuːlɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
A small evergreen mat-forming shrub native to southern Europe and Asia Minor, with stiff stems and terminal clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers.
a small evergreen shrub native to Europe and Asia Minor

Examples

The Bruckenthalia spiculifolia thrives in rocky alpine gardens due to its hardiness.
Gardeners admire the delicate flowers of Bruckenthalia spiculifolia in early summer.
He planted Bruckenthalia spiculifolia along the border for year-round greenery.

Etymology

From the genus name Bruckenthalia, named after Samuel von Brukenthal, a Transylvanian governor and botanist, and the species epithet spiculifolia, from Latin 'spicula' (small spike) and 'folia' (leaves).
