prodigious

1. ADJ

/ prəˈdɪdʒəs / (AmE), / prəˈdɪdʒəs / (BrE)
remarkably great in size, force, or extent; extraordinary; impressively large, impressive, or exceptional
extraordinarily large or impressive

Examples

The scientist achieved prodigious results in her groundbreaking experiments.
His prodigious appetite astonished everyone at the dinner table.
She displayed a prodigious memory, recalling the tiniest details from years before.

Synset Examples

a night of exceeding darkness
an exceptional memory
olympian efforts to save the city from bankruptcy
the young Mozart's prodigious talents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exceptional - unusually good or outstanding
  • Synonym: extraordinary - very unusual or remarkable
  • Antonym: ordinary - with no special or distinctive features; normal

Etymology

From Latin prodigiosus (marvelous), from prodigium (omen, portent).

3. ADJ

/ prəˈdɪdʒəs / (AmE), / prəˈdɪdʒəs / (BrE)
remarkably great in extent, size, or significance; extraordinary or ominously impressive
remarkably great; ominously significant

Examples

The comet was considered a prodigious omen by the villagers.
He achieved prodigious success at an unusually young age.
The sudden silence was a prodigious warning that something was about to happen.

Synset Examples

such a portentous … monster raised all my curiosity
a prodigious vision

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extraordinary - very unusual or remarkable
  • Synonym: portentous - giving a sign or warning that something bad or unpleasant is going to happen
  • Antonym: ordinary - with no special or distinctive features; normal

Etymology

From Latin prodigiosus (marvelous, unnatural), from prodigium (portent, monster).
