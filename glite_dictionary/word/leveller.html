leveller

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛvələr / (AmE), / ˈlɛvələ / (BrE)
a person who advocates the elimination of social hierarchies and distinctions; historically, a member of a radical political movement during the English Civil War promoting equality
advocate of abolishing social hierarchies

Examples

During the English Civil War, the Levellers demanded equal rights and political reforms.
She described herself as a leveller, fighting against social inequality.
The activist's leveller philosophy was controversial in conservative circles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: egalitarian - a person who believes in the equality of all people
  • Antonym: aristocrat - a member of a privileged social class

Etymology

From the verb 'level', meaning to make equal, particularly in social or political context.
