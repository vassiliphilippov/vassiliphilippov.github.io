Rubus loganobaccus

1. NOUN

/ ˈruː.bəs loʊˌɡænoʊˈbæk.əs / (AmE), / ˈruː.bəs ləʊˌɡanəʊˈbæk.əs / (BrE)
A bramble species native to western North America, known for producing the loganberry, a red edible fruit.
bramble species producing loganberry

Examples

Rubus loganobaccus is prized by gardeners for its large, flavorful berries.
The hybrid nature of Rubus loganobaccus makes it resistant to several common plant diseases.
Researchers studied the distribution of Rubus loganobaccus from Oregon to Baja California.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loganberry - the fruit produced by Rubus loganobaccus, a hybrid berry

Etymology

From Latin 'Rubus' (bramble genus) and 'loganobaccus', referencing the loganberry.
