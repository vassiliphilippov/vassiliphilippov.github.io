convoluted

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑnvəˌlutɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɒnvəˌluːtɪd / (BrE)
(of a leaf, petal, or similar structure) rolled longitudinally upon itself, especially in the bud.
rolled or coiled upon itself (botanical)

Examples

The flower has a convoluted petal structure when in bud form.
Some grasses produce convoluted leaves that unroll as they mature.
The botanist examined the convoluted edges of the plant's sepals under a microscope.

Synset Examples

a convolute petal

Etymology

From Latin convolutus, past participle of convolvere (to roll together).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɑːn.vəˌluː.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.vəˌluː.tɪd / (BrE)
extremely complex and difficult to follow, especially in a way that seems unnecessary or confusing.
extremely complex; hard to follow

Examples

The scientist's explanation was so convoluted that nobody in the audience could understand it.
Her argument became increasingly convoluted as she tried to justify her decision.
The contract is written in such convoluted language that it is almost impossible to interpret.

  • Synonym: complicated - consisting of many interconnecting parts; difficult to understand
  • Antonym: simple - easily understood or done; presenting no difficulty
