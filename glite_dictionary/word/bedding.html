bedding

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɛdɪŋ / (BrE)
material such as straw or hay used to provide a bed for animals
material for animals' bedding

Examples

The farmer changed the bedding in the horse stalls every morning.
Fresh straw is commonly used as bedding for cattle.
The chickens seemed comfortable on the new wood shavings bedding.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɛdɪŋ / (BrE)
the sheets, blankets, and other coverings used on a bed
bed coverings such as sheets and blankets

Examples

She carefully washed all the bedding before the guests arrived.
The hotel provides fresh bedding for every new guest.
On cold nights, they add extra bedding to keep warm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bedclothes - the sheets and covers that are used on a bed
  • Synonym: linen - sheets and pillowcases for a bed

Etymology

From Middle English 'bedynge', from 'bed' + '-ing'.
