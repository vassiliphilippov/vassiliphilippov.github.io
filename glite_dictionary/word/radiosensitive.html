radiosensitive

1. ADJ

/ ˌreɪdioʊˈsɛnsətɪv / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊˈsɛnsətɪv / (BrE)
easily affected or damaged by exposure to radiation, especially used of cells, tissues, or organisms.
sensitive to radiation
medical

Examples

Some tumors are highly radiosensitive, making them good candidates for radiation therapy.
Scientists study which bacteria are radiosensitive to understand how radiation affects living cells.
Because lymphocytes are radiosensitive, their count often drops after radiation exposure.

Synset Examples

radiosensitive cancer cells can be treated with radiotherapy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: radioresistant - resistant to the harmful effects of radiation

Etymology

From radio- (relating to radiation) + sensitive.
