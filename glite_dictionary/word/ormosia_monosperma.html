Ormosia monosperma

1. NOUN

/ ɔrˈmoʊziə ˌmɒnəˈspɜrmə / (AmE), / ɔːˈməʊziə ˌmɒnəˈspɜːmə / (BrE)
A small tree native to the West Indies and northeastern Venezuela, having large oblong pointed leaflets, clusters of purple flowers, and black or scarlet seeds with black spots.
small West Indian tree with purple flowers and spotted seeds

Examples

The seeds of Ormosia monosperma are often used as beads because of their striking colors.
Botanists studied the unique leaf structure of Ormosia monosperma in its native habitat.
The garden's tropical section features a flowering Ormosia monosperma among its rare species.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus name Ormosia from Greek 'ormos' (chain), species monosperma from Greek 'monos' (single) + 'sperma' (seed).
