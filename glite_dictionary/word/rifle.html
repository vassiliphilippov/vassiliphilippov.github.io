rifle

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈraɪfəl / (BrE)
a gun with a long barrel and a spiral-grooved bore, designed to be fired from the shoulder
long-barreled shoulder firearm

Examples

He cleaned his rifle carefully after returning from the hunt.
The soldier slung his rifle over his shoulder as he marched.
She learned to shoot a rifle at the firing range.

Synset Examples

he lifted the rifle to his shoulder and fired

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gun - a weapon that shoots bullets or other projectiles
  • Antonym: pistol - a small firearm designed to be held in one hand

Etymology

From French *rifler* (to scratch, groove) referring to the grooved bore.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04097375-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈraɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈraɪfəl / (BrE)
to steal goods from a place, especially by searching through it and taking things
steal goods by searching through

Examples

Thieves managed to rifle the safe before the police arrived.
Burglars rifled through her drawers in search of valuables.
During the chaos, some people began to rifle empty offices for electronics.

Synset Examples

During the earthquake people looted the stores that were deserted by their owners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loot - to steal goods, typically during a war or riot
  • Synonym: plunder - to steal goods, often using force, especially in time of war
  • Synonym: to rob - to take property unlawfully from a person or place by force or threat of force

Etymology

From Old French *rifler* (to plunder), probably of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02349564-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈraɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈraɪfəl / (BrE)
to search quickly and carelessly through something, especially someone's belongings, often without permission
search through quickly and carelessly

Examples

Someone must have rifled through my bag while I was out of the room.
Police discovered that the office had been rifled overnight, but nothing valuable was missing.
She rifled through old boxes in the attic looking for family photos.

Synset Examples

Who rifled through my desk drawers?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ransack - to search through a place or container in a way that causes disorder or damage
  • Synonym: search - to look for something carefully and thoroughly

Etymology

Perhaps from Old French *rifler* (to plunder, scratch up).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01321509-v
Synset POS: v
