edacity

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈdæsɪti / (AmE), / ɪˈdasɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being extremely greedy or having excessive appetite for food; extreme gluttony
extreme gluttony or greed for food

Examples

His edacity was legendary among his friends, and he always finished everyone’s leftovers.
The edacity displayed at the banquet surprised even the seasoned hosts.
History often records the edacity of rulers who indulged themselves while their subjects starved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gluttony - excessive eating or drinking
  • Antonym: abstinence - restraint from eating or drinking

Etymology

From Latin edacitas (greediness, voracity), from edax (greedy, voracious), from edere (to eat).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈdæs.ə.ti / (AmE), / ɪˈdæs.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
an excessive or insatiable desire to eat; extreme hunger or greediness for food
excessive desire to eat

Examples

His edacity at the banquet astonished the other guests.
She blamed her edacity on skipping breakfast that morning.
The novel described the king's edacity in great detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gluttony - excess in eating or drinking

Etymology

From Latin 'edacitas' (greediness, gluttony), from 'edax' (greedy, voracious).
