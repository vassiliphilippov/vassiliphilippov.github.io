Fouquieria columnaris

1. NOUN

/ fuːˈkɪəriə ˌkɒləˈnɛərɪs / (AmE), / fuːˈkɪərɪə ˌkɒlʊˈnɛərɪs / (BrE)
A tall, columnar desert plant native to Mexico and southwestern California, known for its honey-scented creamy yellow flowers; also called boojum tree or candlewood.
desert plant with tall columnar stems

Examples

The Fouquieria columnaris is often called the boojum tree and is unique to the Baja California desert.
Botanists are fascinated by Fouquieria columnaris for its unusual column-like appearance and scented flowers.
I photographed a grove of Fouquieria columnaris during my trip to Mexico's Sonoran Desert.

Etymology

Genus name honors French botanist Pierre Fouquier; 'columnaris' from Latin columna (column) for its shape.
