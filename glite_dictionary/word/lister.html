lister

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪstər / (AmE), / ˈlɪstə / (BrE)
a plow with a double moldboard designed to move soil to either side of a central furrow, commonly used in agriculture
double moldboard plow for making furrows

Examples

The farmer used a lister to prepare the field for planting potatoes.
Early spring is the best time to run the lister through the soil.
This old lister has been in our family for generations.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪstər / (AmE), / ˈlɪstə / (BrE)
an official or assessor responsible for preparing tax lists or property assessments in a district or municipality
official who prepares tax lists

Examples

After the census, the lister compiled a detailed tax list for the town council.
Each property owner was contacted by a lister to verify their information.
The annual report included tables created by the chief lister of the district.

Etymology

From the verb 'list' (to make a list) + -er (agent suffix).
