rebellion

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈbɛliən / (AmE), / rɪˈbɛliən / (BrE)
organized, armed resistance against authority or government, often with the aim to overthrow or challenge it.
organized resistance to authority or government

Examples

The government swiftly put down the rebellion before it could spread to other regions.
Her novel is set during a famous rebellion in 18th-century France.
There are still monuments commemorating the rebellion and those who led it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uprising - a revolt or act of resistance against authority or government
  • Synonym: insurrection - a violent uprising against an authority or government
  • Antonym: loyalty - the quality of being faithful to authority or government

Etymology

From Latin rebellio (renewal of war), from rebellare (to rebel).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈbɛljən / (AmE), / rɪˈbɛljən / (BrE)
an act of refusing to obey authority, control, or tradition; open resistance against established rules, customs, or power
refusal to accept authority or rules

Examples

Her decision to dye her hair bright green was seen as an act of rebellion by her conservative parents.
The spirit of rebellion was evident among the students protesting for change.
His constant questioning of authority was interpreted as a quiet rebellion at work.

Synset Examples

each generation must have its own rebellion
his body was in rebellion against fatigue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revolt - open resistance and refusal to obey authority
  • Antonym: submission - the act of accepting authority or yielding to control

Etymology

From Old French *rebellion*, from Latin *rebellio* ('renewal of war'), from *rebellis* ('waging war again').
