evaporite

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈvæpəˌraɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈvapəraɪt / (BrE)
a type of sedimentary deposit formed by the precipitation of minerals from evaporating water, especially seawater
sediment formed after water evaporation

Examples

The basin is filled with thick layers of evaporite minerals, including halite and gypsum.
Scientists studied ancient evaporite deposits to better understand the region's climate history.
Large quantities of evaporite can form in arid environments where lakes dry up.

Etymology

From Latin 'evaporare' (to evaporate) combined with '-ite', used for minerals and rocks.
