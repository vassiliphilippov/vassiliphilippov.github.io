Artemia salina

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈtiː.mi.ə səˈlaɪ.nə / (AmE), / ɑːˈtiː.mi.ə səˈlʌɪ.nə / (BrE)
A species of small crustacean commonly known as brine shrimp, found in saline lakes and used as food in aquaculture and aquarium industries.
a species of brine shrimp

Examples

Artemia salina is often cultivated as a food source for fish and other aquatic animals.
Laboratories commonly use Artemia salina in toxicity testing due to its sensitivity to pollutants.
Brine shrimp, such as Artemia salina, can survive in hypersaline environments where few other animals can.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brine shrimp - common name for saltwater-dwelling crustaceans including Artemia salina

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Artemia' (the genus name) and 'salina', from Latin 'salinus' meaning 'of salt', referring to its habitat.
