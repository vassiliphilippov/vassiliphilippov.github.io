gouger

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡaʊdʒər / (AmE), / ˈɡaʊdʒə / (BrE)
a person who attacks by gouging, especially one who gouges out another's eye
a person who gouges (esp. the eye)

Examples

The infamous boxer was labeled a gouger after he was caught attacking his opponents' eyes.
Stories of the brutal gouger spread quickly among the townspeople.
In old wrestling matches, a gouger was considered the most dangerous competitor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gouge - to cut or force out, especially the eye

Etymology

From the verb 'gouge' (to scoop or force out), plus agent suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡaʊdʒər / (AmE), / ˈɡaʊdʒə / (BrE)
a person who cheats or swindles others, especially through deception or fraudulent means
person who swindles or cheats

Examples

The landlord turned out to be a gouger who charged double the legal rent.
Tourists often fall victim to some gouger selling fake tickets near the stadium.
We've reported the gouger at the auto shop to the authorities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swindler - person who cheats others out of money or assets
  • Synonym: fraudster - person who commits fraud
  • Antonym: honest person - someone who acts with honesty and integrity

Etymology

From 'gouge' (to cheat or overcharge), likely from French 'gouge' (chisel).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
