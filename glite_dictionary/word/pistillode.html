pistillode

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈstɪloʊd / (AmE), / pɪˈstɪləʊd / (BrE)
A sterile or vestigial pistil found in a flower that produces only stamens.
sterile pistil in a male flower
medical

Examples

The botanist noted that the flower contained a distinct pistillode alongside its stamens.
In some species, the pistillode can be used to distinguish male from female flowers.
Although it looks like a pistil, the pistillode does not function in reproduction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pistil - the female reproductive part of a flower
  • Antonym: stamen - the male reproductive organ of a flower

Etymology

From Greek 'pistil' (referring to the female organ of a flower) and '–ode' (form or likeness).
