Phthiriidae

1. NOUN

/ θaɪˈθɪri.aɪˌdi / (AmE), / θɪˈθɪri.ɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of parasitic insects commonly known as crab lice, infesting humans and some mammals.
family of crab lice (parasitic insects)
medical

Examples

The Phthiriidae are known for infesting the body hair of mammals, including humans.
Medical researchers conducted a study on the life cycle of Phthiriidae.
Infestations by Phthiriidae require specific treatments recommended by healthcare professionals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: louse - a small parasitic insect

Etymology

From modern Latin Phthiriidae, based on Greek phtheir (louse).
