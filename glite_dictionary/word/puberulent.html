puberulent

1. ADJ

/ pjuːˈbɛrʊlənt / (AmE), / pjuːˈbɛrʊlənt / (BrE)
(of a surface, especially of a plant) covered with fine, short, soft hairs or down.
covered with fine, soft hairs

Examples

The young leaves of the plant are distinctly puberulent along their undersides.
Some species of sage have stems that feel puberulent to the touch.
Magnified under a lens, the seed pods appear puberulent with a delicate layer of tiny hairs.

Synset Examples

downy milkweed seeds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pubescent - covered with soft, short hairs; similar botanical term
  • Antonym: glabrous - smooth; lacking hair or pubescence

Etymology

From Latin puber (downy) + -ulent (having in quantity).
