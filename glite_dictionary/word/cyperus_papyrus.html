Cyperus papyrus

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈpɪrəs pəˈpaɪrəs / (AmE), / saɪˈpɪərəs pəˈpaɪərəs / (BrE)
a tall sedge native to the Nile valley, known for its stem used in ancient times to produce papyrus paper and other materials
plant used to make ancient papyrus

Examples

The ancient Egyptians famously used Cyperus papyrus to make scrolls for writing and recording information.
Gardening enthusiasts sometimes grow Cyperus papyrus in water features for its striking appearance.
Historians study the remains of Cyperus papyrus manuscripts to learn about early civilizations.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Cyperus' (the genus name from Greek 'kypeiros', a type of sedge) and Latin 'papyrus' (paper plant).
