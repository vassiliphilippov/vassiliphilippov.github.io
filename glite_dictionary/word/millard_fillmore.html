Millard Fillmore

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɪlərd ˈfɪlˌmɔr / (AmE), / ˈmɪləd ˈfɪlmɔː / (BrE)
the 13th President of the United States (1850–1853), who succeeded Zachary Taylor after his death and previously served as Vice President.
13th President of the United States (1850–1853)

Examples

Millard Fillmore became president upon the death of Zachary Taylor in 1850.
Many historians debate the legacy of Millard Fillmore's presidency.
The Compromise of 1850 was signed into law by Millard Fillmore.
