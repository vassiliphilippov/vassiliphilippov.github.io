ananas

1. NOUN

/ əˈnænəs / (AmE), / əˈnænəs / (BrE)
A large, sweet, fleshy tropical fruit with a terminal tuft of stiff leaves, commonly known as pineapple.
tropical fruit (pineapple)

Examples

The dessert was garnished with fresh slices of ananas and mint leaves.
In some countries, the word ananas is more common than 'pineapple'.
Botanists often classify ananas under the family Bromeliaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pineapple - tropical fruit with tough skin and leafy top

Etymology

From French 'ananas', from Spanish 'ananás', from Portuguese 'ananás', from Old Tupi 'nanas' (pineapple).
