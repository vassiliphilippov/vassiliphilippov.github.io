Panax

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæn.æks / (AmE), / ˈpæn.æks / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbs native to eastern North America and Asia, including the ginseng plants, known for their aromatic tuberous roots.
genus of plants including ginseng

Examples

Panax is widely cultivated for its roots, which are used in traditional medicine.
The most well-known species in the genus Panax is Panax ginseng.
Researchers are studying various compounds found in Panax for potential health benefits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ginseng - an aromatic root from Panax species used in herbal medicine

Etymology

From Greek 'panax' (panacea, all-healing), referring to reputed medicinal properties.
