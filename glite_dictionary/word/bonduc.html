bonduc

1. NOUN

/ bɒnˈduːk / (AmE), / bɒnˈdjuːk / (BrE)
A tropical tree that produces large prickly pods containing seeds resembling beans, used for making jewelry and rosaries.
tropical tree with prickly seed pods

Examples

The bonduc produces hard, bean-like seeds prized by artisans for crafting beads.
Farmers harvest the pods of the bonduc to supply the jewelry industry.
The seeds from the bonduc tree are often used in traditional rosaries.

Etymology

From French 'bonduc', from Arabic 'bunduq' (hazelnut), referring to the seed shape.

2. NOUN

/ bɒnˈdʌk / (AmE), / bɒnˈdʌk / (BrE)
A handsome tree native to central and eastern North America, notable for its large bipinnate leaves, green-white flowers, and large woody brown pods containing seeds sometimes used as a coffee substitute.
tree with pods used as coffee substitute

Examples

The seeds of the bonduc are sometimes roasted and used as a substitute for coffee.
Botanists admire the unusual pods and foliage of the bonduc tree.
Several bonducs grew along the riverbank, their woody pods dangling from the branches.

Etymology

From French 'bonduc', from Arabic 'bunduq', meaning 'hazelnut', referring to the shape of its seeds.
