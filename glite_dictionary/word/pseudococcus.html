Pseudococcus

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuːdəˈkɑːkəs / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəˈkɒkəs / (BrE)
A genus of mealybugs in the family Pseudococcidae, which are soft-bodied, sap-sucking insects affecting various plants.
genus of mealybug insects

Examples

The vineyard was infested with Pseudococcus species, causing significant crop damage.
Scientists studied the life cycle of Pseudococcus under laboratory conditions.
Effective pest control is necessary to manage Pseudococcus populations on citrus plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mealybug - a common name for insects in the family Pseudococcidae

Etymology

From Greek pseudes (false) + kokkos (berry), referring to their appearance.
