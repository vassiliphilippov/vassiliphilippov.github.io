Montezuma II

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɒntəˈzuːmə ðə ˈsɛkənd / (AmE), / ˌmɒntɪˈzuːmə ðɪ ˈsɛkənd / (BrE)
the last Aztec emperor of Mexico (1466–1520), who was overthrown and killed by the Spanish conquistador Hernán Cortés
last Aztec emperor of Mexico, 1466–1520

Examples

Montezuma II ruled the Aztec Empire during the arrival of the Spanish conquistadors.
Historians debate whether Montezuma II was captured or killed during the fall of Tenochtitlan.
The tragic fate of Montezuma II remains a central topic in Aztec history.

Etymology

From Nahuatl Motēuczōma, meaning 'he who frowns like a lord.'
