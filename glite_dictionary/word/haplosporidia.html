Haplosporidia

1. NOUN

/ hæp.loʊ.spɔːˈrɪd.i.ə / (AmE), / hæp.ləʊ.spɒˈrɪd.i.ə / (BrE)
an order of single-celled parasitic organisms in the subclass Acnidosporidia, known to infect aquatic animals such as mollusks and worms
an order of single-celled parasites in Acnidosporidia
medical

Examples

Scientists discovered that Haplosporidia were responsible for the decline in local oyster populations.
Research into Haplosporidia has helped improve understanding of marine parasitic diseases.
The life cycle of organisms in the Haplosporidia order is complex and involves multiple hosts.

Etymology

From Greek haploos (single, simple) + spora (seed, spore), referring to the type of spores produced.
