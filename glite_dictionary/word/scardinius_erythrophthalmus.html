Scardinius erythrophthalmus

1. NOUN

/ skɑrˈdɪniəs ɛˌrɪθroʊfˈθælməs / (AmE), / skaːˈdɪnɪəs ɛˌrɪθrəfˈθælməs / (BrE)
A European freshwater fish, commonly known as the rudd, that closely resembles the roach.
species of European freshwater fish (rudd)

Examples

The Scardinius erythrophthalmus can often be found in slow-moving rivers and lakes across Europe.
Anglers sometimes mistake the Scardinius erythrophthalmus for a common roach due to their similar shapes.
Bright red fins help to distinguish the Scardinius erythrophthalmus from other freshwater species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rudd - a European freshwater fish (Scardinius erythrophthalmus)
  • Degree Lower: roach - a common European freshwater fish similar in appearance to the rudd

Etymology

From scientific Latin; genus 'Scardinius' and species 'erythrophthalmus', from Greek 'erythros' (red) + 'ophthalmos' (eye), referring to the reddish eyes.
