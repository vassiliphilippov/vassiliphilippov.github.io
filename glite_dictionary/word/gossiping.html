gossiping

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑsɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡɒsɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of having conversations in which people spread personal or private information about others, often in a casual or idle manner
conversation spreading personal information

Examples

The office gossiping created a tense atmosphere among coworkers.
She tries to avoid gossiping, but it often happens at lunch breaks.
Continuous gossiping can damage friendships and reputations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gossip - person who habitually spreads rumors or private information

Etymology

From 'gossip' + -ing; 'gossip' from Old English 'godsibb' (godparent).
