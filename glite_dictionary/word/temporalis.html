temporalis

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛmpəˈreɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˌtɛmpəˈreɪlɪs / (BrE)
a broad, fan-shaped muscle on each side of the head, above the ear, involved in closing the jaws by moving the mandible upward
muscle on side of head that closes jaw
medical

Examples

The temporalis muscle plays a key role in chewing and closing the mouth.
After the surgery, she experienced soreness in her temporalis.
Clenching your jaw can cause tension in the temporalis muscles.

Etymology

From Latin 'temporalis' (of the temples), referring to its location near the temple region of the skull.
