malapropos

1. ADJ

/ ˌmæləprəˈpoʊ / (AmE), / ˌmaləprəˈpəʊ / (BrE)
inappropriate or out of place; not suitable for the circumstances
inappropriate; out of place

Examples

His comments at the funeral were rather malapropos and made everyone uncomfortable.
It was malapropos to joke about the weather during such a serious meeting.
The interruption was so malapropos that it derailed the entire discussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inappropriate - not suitable or proper in the circumstances
  • Antonym: apt - appropriate or suitable in the circumstances

Etymology

From French mal à propos, meaning 'badly to the purpose', from mal (badly) + à propos (to the purpose)

At an inappropriate or inconvenient time; inopportunely.
at an inappropriate or inconvenient time
archaic

Examples

He brought up the subject rather malapropos during the serious discussion.
Your call was malapropos; I was in the middle of an important meeting.
She laughed malapropos while everyone else remained silent.

Synset Examples

he arrived inopportunely just as we sat down for dinner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inopportunely - at an inconvenient or unsuitable time
  • Antonym: opportunely - at a convenient or suitable time

Etymology

From French 'mal à propos' (ill to the purpose).
