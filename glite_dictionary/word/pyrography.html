pyrography

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈrɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / paɪˈrɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the art or technique of decorating wood or leather by burning a design on the surface with a heated tool
decorating by burning designs into wood or leather

Examples

She learned the basics of pyrography in an art class at her local community center.
The artisan displayed a collection of pyrography pieces featuring intricate floral patterns on oak.
Many people find pyrography both relaxing and creatively satisfying.

Etymology

From Greek 'pyr' (fire) + 'graphia' (writing).
