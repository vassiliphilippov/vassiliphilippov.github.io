piscivorous

1. ADJ

/ pɪˈsɪvərəs / (AmE), / pɪˈsɪvərəs / (BrE)
(of an animal) feeding mainly or exclusively on fish.
eating fish; fish-eating

Examples

The piscivorous diet of the kingfisher consists almost entirely of small fish caught in rivers.
Certain species of bats are piscivorous, snatching fish from the surface of the water with their claws.
The aquarium has a separate tank for its piscivorous inhabitants to prevent them from eating other fish.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: carnivorous - feeding on other animals; meat-eating
  • Antonym: herbivorous - feeding on plants

Etymology

From Latin piscis (fish) + -vorous (from vorare, to devour).
