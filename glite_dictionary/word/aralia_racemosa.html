Aralia racemosa

1. NOUN

/ əˈreɪliə rəˈsiːməsə / (AmE), / əˈreɪliə rəˈsiːməsə / (BrE)
A North American perennial plant with aromatic roots and panicled umbels, also known as American spikenard.
North American aromatic perennial plant

Examples

Native Americans traditionally used the roots of Aralia racemosa for medicinal purposes.
The lush foliage and aromatic roots make Aralia racemosa a popular choice for shade gardens.
Researchers found several beneficial compounds in the extract of Aralia racemosa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American spikenard - Common name for Aralia racemosa, a North American perennial plant

Etymology

From New Latin, combining genus name Aralia and species epithet racemosa (from Latin racemus, 'cluster, bunch').
