Anglesey

1. PROPN

/ ˈæŋɡəlˌsi / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡləsi / (BrE)
an island off the northwest coast of Wales, forming a county and known for its rural landscapes and historical sites.
island off the coast of Wales

Examples

We spent a week exploring the beautiful beaches of Anglesey last summer.
Bridges connect the Welsh mainland to Anglesey, making travel easy for visitors.
Many birdwatchers visit Anglesey to see its rare species.

Etymology

From Welsh Ynys Môn, Latinized as Mona; Anglesey possibly from Old Norse 'Ongull's ey' (Ongull's island).
