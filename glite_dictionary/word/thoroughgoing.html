thoroughgoing

1. ADJ

/ ˌθɜroʊˈgoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌθʌrəˈgəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a process or attitude) carried out fully, completely, and with great attention to detail; not superficial or partial.
complete and thorough

Examples

The reforms represented a thoroughgoing change in the company’s culture.
She is a thoroughgoing perfectionist who checks every detail twice.
There was a thoroughgoing examination of the evidence during the trial.

Synset Examples

an exhaustive study
made a thorough search
thoroughgoing research

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thorough - complete, with great attention to detail
  • Antonym: partial - not complete; only a part

Etymology

Formed from 'thorough' + 'going', meaning going through thoroughly.
