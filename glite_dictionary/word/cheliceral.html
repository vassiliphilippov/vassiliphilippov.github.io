cheliceral

1. ADJ

/ kəˈlɪsəˌrəl / (AmE), / kɪˈlɪsərəl / (BrE)
relating to or resembling chelicerae, the mouthparts of arachnids such as spiders and scorpions
relating to the mouthparts of arachnids
medical

Examples

The spider uses its cheliceral fangs to inject venom into its prey.
Scientists studied the cheliceral movement in scorpions under a microscope.
The fossil displayed well-preserved cheliceral structures, helping to identify the species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chelicera - the pair of appendages in front of the mouth in arachnids

Etymology

From 'chelicera' (modern Latin, from Greek khēlē 'claw' + keras 'horn'), plus the adjective-forming suffix '-al'.
