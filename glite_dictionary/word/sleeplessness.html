sleeplessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsliːpləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈsliːplɪsnəs / (BrE)
The condition of being unable to sleep, especially for a period of time.
inability to sleep

Examples

She struggled with sleeplessness during stressful periods at work.
Chronic sleeplessness can have negative effects on your health.
His sleeplessness was caused by anxiety about the upcoming exam.

Synset Examples

accept your wakefulness and sleep in its own contrary way is more likely to come

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insomnia - chronic inability to sleep
  • Degree Lower: wakefulness - the state of being awake as opposed to sleeping

Etymology

From 'sleepless' + '-ness'. 'Sleepless' from Old English 'slǣpless', from 'slǣpan' (to sleep) + '-less' (without).
