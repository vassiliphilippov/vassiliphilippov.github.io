alliance

1. NOUN

/ əˈlaɪəns / (AmE), / əˈlaɪ.əns / (BrE)
the state of being joined together for a common purpose, especially by formal agreement between countries, organizations, or groups
state of being allied; formal partnership

Examples

The two countries formed an alliance to strengthen their economic ties.
Several companies entered into an alliance to develop the new technology.
Their political alliance lasted for more than a decade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partnership - the state of being associated or jointly engaged in an activity
  • Synonym: allied - joined by or relating to members in an alliance
  • Antonym: rivalry - the state of being competitors or opponents

Etymology

From Old French 'aliance', from 'allier' (to ally), from Latin 'alligare' (to bind to).
