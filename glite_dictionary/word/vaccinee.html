vaccinee

1. NOUN

/ ˌvæk.səˈni / (AmE), / ˌvaksɪˈniː / (BrE)
a person who has been given a vaccine, especially as part of a medical study or immunization program
person who has received a vaccine
medical

Examples

Each vaccinee was monitored for potential side effects after receiving the immunization.
The study tracked the immune response in hundreds of vaccinees over several months.
A vaccinee may experience mild symptoms such as a sore arm following the injection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patient - a person receiving medical treatment
  • Antonym: vaccinator - a person who administers a vaccine

Etymology

From vaccine + -ee, meaning one who receives the action.
