benevolence

1. NOUN

/ bəˈnɛvələns / (AmE), / bɪˈnɛvələns / (BrE)
An act of kindness or the desire to do good and show goodwill towards others.
an act of kindness and goodwill

Examples

Her benevolence towards the new employees made the office a welcoming place.
The community thanked him for his many acts of benevolence throughout the year.
Showing benevolence to strangers can inspire others to do the same.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kindness - the quality of being friendly, generous, and considerate
  • Antonym: malevolence - the quality of having or showing a wish to do evil to others

Etymology

From Latin 'benevolentia' (good will), from 'bene' (well) + 'velle' (to wish).
