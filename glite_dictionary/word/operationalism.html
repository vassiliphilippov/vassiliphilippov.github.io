operationalism

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːpəˈreɪʃənəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɒpəˈreɪʃənəlɪzəm / (BrE)
(Philosophy) The theory that the meaning of a concept or proposition is defined by the set of operations or procedures used to measure or prove it.
theory defining meaning by measurement operations

Examples

The philosophy of operationalism influenced how scientists defined physical concepts in the 20th century.
Critics argue that operationalism can oversimplify complex philosophical ideas.
His research was guided by the principles of operationalism, focusing only on measurable outcomes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: operationalist - a person or approach that adheres to operationalism

Etymology

From 'operation' + '-alism'; influenced by the procedures of scientific measurement introduced by Percy W. Bridgman (1920s).
