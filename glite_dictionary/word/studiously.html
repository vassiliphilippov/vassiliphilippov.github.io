studiously

1. ADV

/ ˈstuːdiəsli / (AmE), / ˈstjuːdiəsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows great care, attention, or effort, especially in studying or learning.
with careful attention or effort

Examples

She studiously avoided making eye contact with the interviewer.
He studiously prepared for the exam every night for a month.
The artist studiously recreated every detail of the historic building.

Synset Examples

she examined the data studiously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diligently - in a way that shows care and conscientiousness in one's work or duties

Etymology

From 'studious' + '-ly'. 'Studious' from Latin 'studiosus' (devoted, eager).
