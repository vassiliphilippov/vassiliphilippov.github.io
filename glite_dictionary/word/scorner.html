scorner

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɔrnɚ / (AmE), / ˈskɔːnə / (BrE)
a person who expresses contempt or derision, especially openly through remarks or facial expressions
person who expresses contempt or derision

Examples

The scorner dismissed their ideas with a sneer.
He was known as a scorner who looked down on everyone around him.
She met the scorner's gaze with calm defiance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mockery - teasing and contemptuous language or behavior directed at a particular person or thing
  • Synonym: disparager - someone who speaks disrespectfully or belittlingly of others
  • Antonym: admirer - a person who regards someone with respect or warm approval

Etymology

From Middle English 'scorner', from Old French 'escorneor', from 'escorner' (to scorn).
