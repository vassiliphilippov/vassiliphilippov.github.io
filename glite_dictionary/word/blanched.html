blanched

1. ADJ

/ blæntʃt / (AmE), / blɑːntʃt / (BrE)
(of a person or their skin) made pale, especially by shock, fear, illness, or emotion.
made pale from emotion or illness

Examples

She looked blanched when she heard the terrible news.
The sudden shock left his face completely blanched.
Her cheeks were blanched with fear as she answered the door.

2. ADJ

/ blæntʃt / (AmE), / blɑːntʃt / (BrE)
(of a plant or part of a plant) grown without exposure to light, resulting in a pale or white color due to the absence of chlorophyll.
grown pale by lack of light (plants)

Examples

The chef prefers to use blanched asparagus for its tender texture and delicate flavor.
When grown under a cover, endive becomes blanched and loses its bitterness.
Celery is often blanched in the garden by mounding soil around it to keep the stalks pale and tender.

Synset Examples

etiolated celery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: etiolated - (of a plant) pale and weak due to lack of light

Etymology

From Old French 'blanchir', from 'blanc' meaning 'white'.
