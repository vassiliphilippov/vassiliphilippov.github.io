plagiarised

1. ADJ

/ ˈpleɪdʒəˌraɪzd / (AmE), / ˈpleɪdʒərʌɪzd / (BrE)
(of ideas, words, or work) copied from someone else and presented as your own.
copied and presented as original

Examples

The professor rejected the paper because it contained several plagiarised paragraphs from online sources.
She was accused of submitting a plagiarised essay in her literature class.
Finding plagiarised content in academic writing can lead to severe consequences for students.

Synset Examples

used plagiarized data in his thesis
a work dotted with plagiarized phrases

Related Terms

  • Antonym: original - not copied or derived from something else

Etymology

From Latin 'plagiarius' (kidnapper, literary thief), via French 'plagier', meaning 'to kidnap' or 'to plagiarize'.
