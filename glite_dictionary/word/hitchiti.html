Hitchiti

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈtʃiːti / (AmE), / hɪˈtʃiːti / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people who formerly lived in Georgia and were part of the Creek Confederacy.
member of a Native American people of Georgia

Examples

The Hitchiti were once an important part of the Creek Confederacy.
Many Hitchiti descendants still honor their cultural heritage.
Archaeologists have found Hitchiti artifacts in several areas of Georgia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Creek - a member of a Native American people of the southeastern US

Etymology

From the name of the Hitchiti people, derived from their own language.

2. NOUN

/ hɪˈtʃɪti / (AmE), / hɪˈtʃɪti / (BrE)
the Muskogean language formerly spoken by the Hitchiti people of Georgia, USA.
a Muskogean Native American language

Examples

Few people today can still speak Hitchiti fluently due to a decline in native speakers.
Linguists have documented stories and legends in Hitchiti to preserve the language.
The Hitchiti language is closely related to other members of the Muskogean family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Muskogean - a family of Native American languages spoken in the southeastern United States

Etymology

From the self-designation of the Hitchiti people, a Native American group.
