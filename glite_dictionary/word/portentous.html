portentous

1. ADJ

/ pɔrˈtɛntəs / (AmE), / pɔːˈtɛntəs / (BrE)
Giving a sign or warning that something, especially something momentous or calamitous, is likely to happen; ominously significant.
ominously significant; foreboding

Examples

Dark, portentous clouds gathered above the city, warning of the approaching storm.
His voice took on a portentous tone as he predicted the company's downfall.
The old castle had a portentous atmosphere that made visitors feel uneasy.

Synset Examples

such a portentous … monster raised all my curiosity
a prodigious vision

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ominous - suggesting that something bad will happen
  • Antonym: auspicious - suggesting a positive or favorable outcome

Etymology

From Latin portentōsus, from portentum (omen, portent).

2. ADJ

/ pɔrˈtɛntəs / (AmE), / pɔːˈtɛntəs / (BrE)
Giving a sign or warning that something, especially something momentous or calamitous, is likely to happen; ominous or prophetic.
giving an omen or warning; ominous

Examples

The dark, portentous clouds gathered above the city, making everyone uneasy.
His portentous tone suggested that something important and troubling was about to be revealed.
A portentous silence filled the room as the announcement approached.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ominous - giving the impression that something bad is about to happen
  • Antonym: auspicious - giving or being a sign of future success; favorable

Etymology

From Latin portentōsus (marvelous, ominous), from portentum (portent, omen).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01889205-s
Synset POS: s
