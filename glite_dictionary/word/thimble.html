thimble

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɪmbəl / (AmE), / ˈθɪmbəl / (BrE)
A small metal or plastic cap worn over the fingertip to protect it while pushing a needle through fabric when sewing; sometimes also used as a small container.
metal cap for finger in sewing

Examples

She wore a thimble on her finger to avoid being pricked by the needle.
He collected antique thimbles from different countries.
Pour a thimble of oil into the pan before heating it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fingerstall - a protective covering for a finger, often used in sewing or first aid

Etymology

From Old English 'þȳmel', diminutive of 'þūma' (thumb).

2. NOUN

/ ˈθɪmbəl / (AmE), / ˈθɪmbəl / (BrE)
A very small quantity, especially as much as a thimble can hold; often used figuratively to mean a tiny amount.
a very small quantity

Examples

She added only a thimble of whiskey to the cake batter.
There was barely a thimble of milk left in the jug.
I only need a thimble of oil for this recipe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drop - a very small amount of liquid
  • Synonym: dash - a small quantity, especially of liquid or spice

Etymology

From Old English 'þȳmel', from 'þūma' (thumb), referring to an object worn on the thumb for sewing.
