refined

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈfaɪnd / (AmE), / rɪˈfaɪnd / (BrE)
(of a substance) freed from impurities or unwanted elements by processing.
freed from impurities by processing

Examples

The factory specializes in producing refined sugar from raw cane.
This soap is made using refined coconut oil for a smoother texture.
He refused to eat foods that were excessively refined or processed.

Synset Examples

refined sugar
refined oil
to gild refined gold

Related Terms

  • Antonym: crude - not processed or purified; in a natural state
  • Antonym: unrefined - not made pure or processed; containing impurities

Etymology

From French *raffiné*, from Medieval Latin *refinare*, from Latin *re-* (again) + *finire* (to finish, to purify).

/ rɪˈfaɪnd / (AmE), / rɪˈfaɪnd / (BrE)
(of a substance) made pure by having unwanted elements removed, usually through an industrial or chemical process.

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: raw - in its natural, unprocessed state
