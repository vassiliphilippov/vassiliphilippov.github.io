Calypso

1. PROPN

/ kəˈlɪp.soʊ / (AmE), / kəˈlɪp.səʊ / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a sea nymph who detained Odysseus on her island for seven years.
Greek mythological sea nymph

Examples

According to Greek legend, Calypso kept Odysseus on her island for seven years.
Homer's epic recounts how Calypso fell in love with Odysseus.
Some scholars interpret the story of Calypso as a symbol of temptation and captivity.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Καλυψώ (Kalupsṓ), a nymph in Greek mythology.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪpsoʊ / (AmE), / kəˈlɪpsəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of orchids with a single species found in northern North America and Eurasia, commonly known as the fairy slipper.
genus of orchids (fairy slipper)

Examples

The rare Calypso can be found in cool, mossy forests across northern regions.
Botanists were excited to discover a population of Calypso orchids in the new reserve.
The delicate flowers of the Calypso are easily overlooked due to their small size and hidden location.

Etymology

From Greek Kalypsō, a mythological nymph, alluding to the concealed nature of the flower.
