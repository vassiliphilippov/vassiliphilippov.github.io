Blastodiaceae

1. NOUN

/ blæs.toʊˌdaɪ.iˈeɪ.siˌi / (AmE), / blæstəʊˌdaɪ.iːˈeɪ.siːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of saprobic fungi belonging to the order Blastocladiales
family of saprobic fungi

Examples

The Blastodiaceae family includes several genera of fungi found in aquatic habitats.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Blastodiaceae during their survey of freshwater ecosystems.
Members of the Blastodiaceae play a role in decomposing plant material in moist environments.

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from 'Blastocladia' (type genus) and '-aceae' (family suffix).
