Midway

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɪdˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.weɪ / (BrE)
a decisive naval battle in World War II fought in June 1942, in which American forces defeated a Japanese fleet near the Midway Islands
naval battle in World War II (1942)

Examples

The Battle of Midway marked a turning point in the Pacific theater during World War II.
Many historians consider Midway to be the most important naval battle of the war.
At Midway, American forces managed to sink four Japanese aircraft carriers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of the Coral Sea - World War II naval battle between Japanese and Allied forces in the Pacific (May 1942)
  • Degree Lower: Pearl Harbor - Japanese attack on the US naval base at Pearl Harbor in December 1941

Etymology

Named after Midway Atoll, location of the battle.
