midway

1. ADV

/ ˈmɪdˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.weɪ / (BrE)
at a point halfway between two places or moments; in the middle.
in the middle; halfway between two points

Examples

She stopped midway through her speech to take a sip of water.
The bridge collapsed midway across the river.
We met midway between our houses to exchange the books.

Synset Examples

he was halfway down the ladder when he fell

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halfway - at an equal distance between two points

Etymology

From Middle English 'midwei', from Old English 'midweg' (mid + way).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00256895-r
Synset POS: r

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.weɪ / (BrE)
the area at a fair or carnival where sideshows, games, and amusements are located
area at a fair with amusements and sideshows

Examples

We spent hours exploring the midway at the county fair, playing games and winning prizes.
The children rushed to the midway, eager to try the rides and sample cotton candy.
Vendors lined the midway, offering everything from popcorn to fortune-telling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fairground - an area where a fair is held, with rides and amusements
  • Synonym: carnival - a traveling amusement show or fair

Etymology

Coined from the 'Midway Plaisance', an avenue at the 1893 World's Columbian Exposition in Chicago, which hosted amusements.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08632321-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈmɪdˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɪdweɪ / (BrE)
equally distant from two opposite points, places, or extremes; in the middle position
equally distant between two extremes

Examples

The school is midway between the library and the park, so it's easy to walk to both.
Her house sits midway along the winding country road.
They stopped at a café that was midway through their journey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: middle - the point or position at an equal distance from the sides, edges, or ends
  • Synonym: central - located in the center; in the middle

Etymology

From Middle English midway, from mid (middle) + way.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
