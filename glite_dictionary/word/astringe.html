to astringe

1. VERB

/ əˈstrɪndʒ / (AmE), / əˈstrɪndʒ / (BrE)
to constrict or draw together, especially by causing contraction of body tissues
to cause contraction; make tissues tighter

Examples

Lemon juice astringes the tissue in the mouth, leaving a dry sensation.
Some herbal teas are believed to astringe the throat and help reduce irritation.
The powder is used in medicine to astringe wounds and promote healing.

Synset Examples

Lemon juice astringes the tissue in the mouth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to constrict - to make narrower or tighter by pressure
  • Antonym: to relax - to make or become less tight or tense

Etymology

From Latin 'astringere' (to bind fast, tighten).

2. VERB

/ əˈstrɪndʒ / (AmE), / əˈstrɪndʒ / (BrE)
to cause to contract or become constricted; to draw together, especially in tissue or blood vessels
cause to contract or become constricted

Examples

The tonic will astringe the tissues and reduce swelling.
Cold air can astringe the skin, making it feel tighter.
Some herbal teas are believed to astringe mucous membranes in the throat.

Synset Examples

The cold substance astringes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contract - to make or become smaller or tighter
  • Antonym: to expand - to increase in size, range, or amount; to become larger

Etymology

From Latin 'astringere' meaning 'to bind fast', from 'ad-' (to) + 'stringere' (to draw tight).
