panoche

1. NOUN

/ pəˈnoʊtʃi / (AmE), / pəˈnɒtʃeɪ / (BrE)
A type of fudge made with brown sugar, butter, milk, and typically nuts.
fudge made with brown sugar and nuts

Examples

My grandmother always makes delicious panoche for the holidays, filled with pecans and rich brown sugar.
The recipe called for brown sugar to give the panoche its distinctive caramel flavor.
We served slices of panoche with coffee after dinner last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fudge - a soft, sweet candy made from sugar, butter, and milk

Etymology

From Spanish 'panocha' (raw sugar cone), referring to its brown sugar content.
