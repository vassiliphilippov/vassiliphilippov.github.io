Bassariscus

1. NOUN

/ bæsəˈrɪskəs / (AmE), / bæsəˈrɪskəs / (BrE)
A genus of small, nocturnal mammals in the raccoon family, including the cacomistles and ringtails, native to North and Central America.
genus of mammals (cacomistles, ringtails)

Examples

The Bassariscus is well-adapted for climbing trees in its native habitat.
Biologists observed a Bassariscus hunting insects at night in the canyon.
The Bassariscus population has declined in some areas due to habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cacomistle - A small, nocturnal mammal of the genus Bassariscus

Etymology

From neo-Latin, based on Greek 'bassaris' (fox) and Latin diminutive suffix '-iscus'.

2. NOUN

/ bæsəˈrɪskəs / (AmE), / basəˈrɪskəs / (BrE)
a genus of small carnivorous mammals in the family Procyonidae, including the cacomistle and ringtail, native to North and Central America
genus of small carnivorous mammals (Procyonidae)

Examples

The genus Bassariscus includes both the ringtail and the cacomistle, small mammals found in the Americas.
Researchers observed that Bassariscus species are highly adept climbers.
Both living members of Bassariscus share similar nocturnal habits and diets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cacomistle - a nocturnal mammal (Bassariscus sumichrasti) found in Central America and Mexico
  • Synonym: ringtail - a small carnivorous mammal (Bassariscus astutus) related to the raccoon

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'bassaris' (fox) plus Latin diminutive suffix '-iscus'.
