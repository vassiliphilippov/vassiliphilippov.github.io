alkaliser

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.kəˌlaɪ.zər / (AmE), / ˈæl.kəˌlaɪ.zə / (BrE)
A substance that neutralizes or counteracts acidity, especially in the stomach.
substance that neutralizes acidity
medical british

Examples

The doctor prescribed an alkaliser to help reduce stomach acidity.
People with acid reflux often benefit from taking an alkaliser after meals.
An alkaliser can be found in many over-the-counter medications for indigestion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antacid - a substance that counteracts stomach acidity

Etymology

From 'alkali' + '-ser,' equivalent to 'alkalizer' (American spelling).
