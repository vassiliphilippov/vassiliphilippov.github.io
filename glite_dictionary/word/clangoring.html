clangoring

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæŋəɹɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklæŋərɪŋ / (BrE)
a loud, resonant, and repeating metallic noise
loud, repeated metallic noise

Examples

The clangoring of the church bells echoed across the valley at noon.
She found it hard to concentrate with the constant clangoring of construction equipment outside.
As the factory doors opened, the clangoring of heavy machinery filled the air.

Synset Examples

he could hear the clang of distant bells

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clangor - a loud, resonant sound, usually metallic
  • Synonym: racket - a loud and unpleasant noise

Etymology

From 'clangor' (Latin: 'clangor', meaning a loud sound or noise) with the suffix '-ing' forming a noun.
