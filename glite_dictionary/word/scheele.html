Scheele

1. PROPN

/ ˈʃiːlə / (AmE), / ˈʃiːlə / (BrE)
Carl Wilhelm Scheele (1742–1786), a Swedish chemist who discovered oxygen before Joseph Priestley.
Swedish chemist; discovered oxygen

Examples

Scheele is credited with the discovery of several chemical elements, including oxygen and chlorine.
Few people know that Scheele identified oxygen before Joseph Priestley published his findings.
In his laboratory, Scheele made significant contributions to the understanding of chemical reactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Priestley - Joseph Priestley, English theologian and chemist who also discovered oxygen
