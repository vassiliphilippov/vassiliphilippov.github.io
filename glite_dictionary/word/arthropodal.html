arthropodal

1. ADJ

/ ɑrˈθrɑp.ə.dəl / (AmE), / ɑːˈθrɒp.ə.dəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of animals belonging to the phylum Arthropoda, such as insects, arachnids, and crustaceans.
relating to arthropods

Examples

The researcher studied the arthropodal characteristics shared by insects and spiders.
Crustaceans exhibit a wide variety of arthropodal adaptations to aquatic environments.
Some textbooks feature detailed diagrams of arthropodal anatomy for educational purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arthropod - an invertebrate of the phylum Arthropoda

Etymology

From New Latin Arthropoda (the phylum name), from Greek árthron (joint) + pous (foot).
