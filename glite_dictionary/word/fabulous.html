fabulous

1. ADJ

/ ˈfæbjələs / (AmE), / ˈfæbjʊləs / (BrE)
So extraordinary, remarkable, or impressive as to be almost unbelievable.
extraordinary, almost unbelievable

Examples

The team's fabulous comeback left everyone in disbelief.
He told a fabulous tale about his adventure that no one believed.
Their fabulous escape from the sinking ship made headlines worldwide.

Synset Examples

the fabulous endurance of a marathon runner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incredible - too extraordinary and improbable to be believed
  • Synonym: unbelievable - not able to be believed; extraordinary
  • Antonym: plausible - seeming reasonable or probable

Etymology

From Latin 'fabula' (story, fable), originally referring to things of fable-like quality.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfæbjələs / (AmE), / ˈfæbjʊləs / (BrE)
Relating to or told in fable or mythology; described in traditional stories and not based on historical fact.
relating to myth or fable

Examples

The ancient Greeks believed in fabulous creatures like the phoenix and the minotaur.
The tale describes a fabulous land of golden palaces and eternal youth.
Some claimed to have seen a fabulous beast deep in the forest, but there was no real evidence.

Synset Examples

mythical centaurs
the fabulous unicorn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mythical - existing only in traditional stories; of myth
  • Synonym: legendary - described in legend; famous in traditional stories
  • Antonym: historical - based on or concerned with actual past events

Etymology

From Latin 'fabulosus' (celebrated in fable), from 'fabula' (story, fable).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01942776-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈfæbjələs / (AmE), / ˈfæbjʊləs / (BrE)
extremely good or impressive; wonderful.
extremely good or impressive

Examples

We had a fabulous time at the concert last night.
The view from the top of the mountain was absolutely fabulous.
Her performance in the play received fabulous reviews from the critics.

Synset Examples

a fabulous vacation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fantastic - extraordinarily good or attractive
  • Synonym: wonderful - inspiring delight or admiration; extremely good
  • Antonym: terrible - extremely bad

Etymology

From Latin 'fabulosus' (celebrated in fable, legendary), from 'fabula' (story, fable).
