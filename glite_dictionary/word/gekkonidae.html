Gekkonidae

1. NOUN

/ gɛkˈɑnɪˌdi / (AmE), / gɛkˈɒnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small to medium-sized lizards commonly known as geckos, found in warm climates worldwide.
family of lizards known as geckos

Examples

The Gekkonidae family includes more than a thousand species of geckos.
Members of the Gekkonidae are known for their adhesive toe pads.
Herpetologists study the Gekkonidae to understand their unique climbing abilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gecko - a small lizard of the family Gekkonidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'gekkos' (gecko) with the zoological family suffix '-idae'.
