Hakea laurina

1. NOUN

/ həˈkiːə lɔːˈriːnə / (AmE), / həˈkiːə lɔːˈriːnə / (BrE)
A tall, straggling shrub native to Western Australia, known for its large, globular crimson-yellow flowers.
Australian shrub with globular crimson-yellow flowers

Examples

The garden features a beautiful Hakea laurina in full bloom each spring.
Native birds are attracted to the striking flowers of Hakea laurina.
She planted a Hakea laurina to add color and structure to her backyard.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Hakea', honoring botanist Baron Christian Ludwig von Hake, and the species epithet 'laurina' (Latin, meaning 'laurel-like').
