ulteriority

1. NOUN

/ ʌlˌtɪriˈæɾiɾi / (AmE), / ʌlˌtɪə.riˈɒr.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being ulterior; an underlying or hidden reality or motive beyond what is openly stated or apparent
the state of being ulterior or hidden

Examples

The novel explores the ulteriority of its characters' motives, revealing what lies beneath their words.
His explanation was so smooth that it masked any sense of ulteriority he might have had.
Many politicians are often accused of acting with ulteriority, hiding their true intentions.

Synset Examples

their conversation was limited to ulteriorities
a terrible feeling of ulteriority
his stories were too susceptible to ulteriority

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ulterior - existing beyond what is obvious or admitted; hidden

Etymology

From Latin 'ulterior' (further, beyond) with the English suffix '-ity'.
