platyrhinian

1. ADJ

/ ˌplæt̬.ɪˈraɪ.ni.ən / (AmE), / ˌplæt.ɪˈrɪn.i.ən / (BrE)
relating to New World monkeys with nostrils set far apart, or to people with broad, flat noses.
having broad or widely spaced nostrils

Examples

The platyrhinian monkeys of South America are distinguished by their widely spaced nostrils.
In anthropology, certain populations are classified as platyrhinian due to their broad nasal structure.
Researchers studied the platyrhinian features of several primate species for evolutionary analysis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: catarrhine - relating to Old World monkeys with close-set nostrils

Etymology

From Greek platys (broad, flat) + rhis, rhinos (nose), via scientific Latin.
