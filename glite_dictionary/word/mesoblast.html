mesoblast

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛz.oʊˌblæst / (AmE), / ˈmiː.zəʊˌblɑːst / (BrE)
the middle layer of cells or tissue in a developing embryo, which gives rise to muscles, bones, cartilage, blood, and connective tissue
middle embryonic tissue layer
medical

Examples

During embryonic development, the mesoblast forms many of the body's vital tissues.
Abnormalities in the mesoblast can lead to congenital defects affecting muscles and bones.
Researchers studied how cells in the mesoblast differentiate into specific organ systems.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: endoblast - the innermost germ layer of an embryo
  • Degree Higher: ectoblast - the outermost germ layer of an embryo

Etymology

From Greek 'mesos' (middle) and 'blastos' (germ, sprout).
