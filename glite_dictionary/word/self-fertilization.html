self-fertilization

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlfˌfɜrtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˌfəːtɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process in which an organism's own male and female gametes unite to produce offspring
union of gametes from one individual

Examples

Many plants are capable of self-fertilization if cross-pollination does not occur.
Self-fertilization can limit genetic diversity within a population over time.
Scientists studied how self-fertilization affects the survival of certain flowering plants.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cross-fertilization - fertilization involving gametes from two different individuals

Etymology

Formed from self- + fertilization, modeled on scientific Latin usages from the 19th century.
