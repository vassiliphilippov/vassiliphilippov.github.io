Aquilegia scopulorum calcarea

1. NOUN

/ ˌækwəˈliːdʒə skɒˈpjuːlɔːrəm kælˈkɛəriə / (AmE), / ˌakwɪˈliːdʒɪə skɒpjuːˈlɔːrəm kælkˈɪəriə / (BrE)
A type of columbine native to the Rocky Mountains, known for its long-spurred blue flowers.
a Rocky Mountain columbine with blue flowers

Examples

The delicate blue flowers of Aquilegia scopulorum calcarea brighten the high-altitude meadows in summer.
Botanists noted the unique long spurs of Aquilegia scopulorum calcarea during their field survey.
Gardeners admire Aquilegia scopulorum calcarea for its resilience and striking appearance in rocky soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: columbine - a plant of the genus Aquilegia, known for distinctive spurred flowers

Etymology

From Latin: Aquilegia (columbine genus) + scopulorum (of the cliffs) + calcarea (calcareous, referring to limestone habitats).
