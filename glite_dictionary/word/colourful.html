colourful

1. ADJ

/ ˈkʌlɚfəl / (AmE), / ˈkʌləf(ə)l / (BrE)
having striking variety, interest, or vividness, especially in appearance, expression, or character
strikingly vivid or interesting
british

Examples

The artist's paintings are remarkably colourful and expressive.
She led a colourful life, full of adventure and memorable stories.
The politician was known for his colourful language and lively speeches.

Synset Examples

a colorful period of history
a colorful character
colorful language

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colorful - striking in variety and interest (American spelling)
  • Antonym: dull - lacking vividness or interest

Etymology

From 'colour' plus the suffix '-ful', denoting 'full of color'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkʌlɚfəl / (AmE), / ˈkʌləfəl / (BrE)
having many different or bright colours; full of variety and interest in appearance
having many or bright colours
british

Examples

The garden was filled with colourful flowers in every shade imaginable.
Her wardrobe is known for its colourful patterns and bold designs.
We admired the colourful display of balloons at the festival.

Synset Examples

colorful autumn leaves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colorful - American spelling of 'colourful', meaning having many or varied colors
  • Antonym: monochrome - having only one color or shades of one color

Etymology

From colour + -ful; colour from Old French 'color', Latin 'color'.

british
