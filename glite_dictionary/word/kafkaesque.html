Kafkaesque

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɑː.fkəˈɛsk / (AmE), / ˌkæf.kəˈesk / (BrE)
characterized by a nightmarishly complex, bizarre, or illogical quality, often involving oppressive or surreal bureaucratic processes, in the manner of Franz Kafka's writings.
resembling Franz Kafka's surreal or oppressive style

Examples

The novel describes a Kafkaesque world in which citizens face endless red tape and uncertainty.
Many employees found the new regulations to be so Kafkaesque that it was nearly impossible to do their jobs.
His Kafkaesque experience with the government left him feeling powerless and confused.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surreal - having the qualities of surrealism; bizarre
  • Synonym: bureaucratic - relating to the complicated procedures of administration

Etymology

From the name of Franz Kafka (1883–1924), Czech writer, plus the suffix -esque (meaning 'in the style of').
