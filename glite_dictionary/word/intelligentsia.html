intelligentsia

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌtɛlɪˈdʒɛntsiə / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɛlɪˈdʒentsɪə / (BrE)
The intellectual and educated elite of a society, especially those involved in the arts, literature, or academia.
the educated intellectual elite

Examples

The intelligentsia of the city gathered to discuss political reforms.
During the revolution, members of the intelligentsia played a crucial role in spreading new ideas.
He was well respected among the intelligentsia due to his progressive writings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elite - the most powerful, rich, or talented people within a group or society

Etymology

From Russian *intelligentsiya*, itself from Latin *intelligentia* (intelligence).
