to identify

1. VERB

/ aɪˈdɛntɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / aɪˈdɛntɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to give the name or specific characteristics of someone or something; to recognize and name
recognize and name; specify characteristics

Examples

The witness was able to identify the suspect in a lineup.
Can you identify this plant based on its leaves and flowers?
The archaeologist identified several artifacts from the ancient site.

Synset Examples

Many senators were named in connection with the scandal
The almanac identifies the auspicious months

Related Terms

  • Synonym: name - to give a name to someone or something
  • Synonym: to describe - to give an account of characteristics

Etymology

From Medieval Latin identificare, from Latin identitas (identity) + facere (to make).
