muskmelon

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌskˌmɛlən / (AmE), / ˈmʌskˌmɛlən / (BrE)
A type of sweet melon with fragrant flesh, cultivated for its edible fruit and related to the cucumber family.
sweet, fragrant melon fruit

Examples

She served fresh muskmelon slices as a refreshing summer dessert.
The farmer planted several rows of muskmelon alongside cucumbers and squash.
Ripe muskmelon has a sweet, aromatic aroma that fills the kitchen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cantaloupe - a variety of muskmelon with orange flesh
  • Antonym: watermelon - large, green-skinned melon with red, juicy flesh

Etymology

From musk (referring to its fragrance) + melon; 'melon' from Old French 'melon', from Latin 'melopepo'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌskˌmɛl.ən / (AmE), / ˈmʌskˌmɛl.ən / (BrE)
a type of melon with a rough, netted rind, edible sweet flesh, and a musky fragrance, including varieties such as cantaloupe.
a sweet melon with netted rind and fragrant flesh

Examples

The farmer harvested several ripe muskmelons early in the morning.
Sliced muskmelon is a refreshing option for a summer picnic.
Many people enjoy the juicy, musky flavor of a chilled muskmelon on hot days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cantaloupe - a type of muskmelon with orange flesh
  • Degree Higher: melon - the general group of fleshy, edible fruits including muskmelon, watermelon, etc.

Etymology

From musk (for the fragrance) + melon; 'musk' from Persian 'mushk'.
