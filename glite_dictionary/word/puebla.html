Puebla

1. PROPN

/ ˈpwɛblə / (AmE), / ˈpwɛblə / (BrE)
a city in south-central Mexico, southeast of Mexico City, known for its colonial architecture and historical significance
city in south-central Mexico

Examples

Many tourists visit Puebla to admire its beautiful colonial buildings and vibrant culture.
The Battle of Puebla in 1862 is commemorated every year as Cinco de Mayo.
Industries in Puebla contribute significantly to the Mexican economy.

Etymology

From Spanish, named after its official title 'Puebla de los Ángeles'.
