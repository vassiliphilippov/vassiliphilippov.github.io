Seminole

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛmɪˌnoʊl / (AmE), / ˈsɛmɪnəʊl / (BrE)
the Muskhogean language spoken by the Seminole people of Florida and Oklahoma
language of the Seminole people

Examples

Few people today are fluent speakers of Seminole.
A collection of traditional stories was recorded in the Seminole language.
Linguists study Seminole to document its unique grammatical structures.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Seminola', from Creek 'simanó-li' meaning 'runaway' or 'wild.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛmɪˌnoʊl / (AmE), / ˈsɛmɪnəʊl / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people originally from the southeastern United States, especially Florida, descended from Muskhogean-speaking tribes.
member of a Native American group in Florida

Examples

The Seminole resisted forced relocation during the Second Seminole War.
Several Seminole traditions are still practiced in parts of Florida today.
He learned about the history of the Seminole people in his American studies class.

Etymology

From Creek (Muskogean language) 'simanó-li', meaning 'runaway' or 'wild'.
