amoebous

1. ADJ

/ əˈmiːbəs / (AmE), / əˈmiːbəs / (BrE)
pertaining to or resembling amoebae
resembling or relating to amoebae
medical

Examples

The scientist observed amoebous movements in the cell culture.
Some diseases exhibit amoebous characteristics under the microscope.
The bacteria developed an amoebous form to adapt to its environment.

Synset Examples

amoebic dysentery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amoeboid - having a shape or movement like an amoeba

Etymology

From Greek amoibē (change) via New Latin amoeba, with -ous forming the adjective.
