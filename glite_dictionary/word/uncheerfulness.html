uncheerfulness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈtʃɪr.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈtʃɪə.f(ə)l.nəs / (BrE)
a state or feeling of gloomy, pessimistic sadness; lack of cheerfulness
gloomy or pessimistic sadness

Examples

Her uncheerfulness cast a shadow over the entire group during the trip.
He spoke with such uncheerfulness that it was hard to remain optimistic.
Despite the festive decorations, a sense of uncheerfulness lingered in the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gloom - a state of partial or total darkness; also, a feeling of sadness or depression
  • Antonym: cheerfulness - the quality or state of being noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

Formed from 'uncheerful' + '-ness', with 'un-' expressing negation and '-ness' forming a noun of quality.

2. NOUN

/ ˌʌnˈtʃɪɚ.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈtʃɪə.fəl.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of lacking cheerfulness; gloominess or a lack of good spirits
lack of cheerfulness; gloominess

Examples

There was a certain uncheerfulness in the air after the news was announced.
Her persistent uncheerfulness affected the entire team's mood during the project.
Despite the rain and general uncheerfulness, they tried to stay positive.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cheerfulness - the quality of being cheerful or full of good spirits
  • Synonym: gloom - a state of partial or total darkness and also emotional or mental darkness; low spirits

Etymology

Formed from the prefix un- (not) + cheerful + -ness (noun suffix).
