hint

1. NOUN

/ hɪnt / (AmE), / hɪnt / (BrE)
a very small amount of something that is hardly noticeable
a very small detectable amount

Examples

The soup had just a hint of garlic, making the flavor subtle and appealing.
There was a hint of sadness in his smile as he said goodbye.
A hint of blue could be seen in the evening sky.

Synset Examples

he speaks French with a trace of an accent
a hint mockery in her manner
a tint of glamour

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trace - a very small amount of something
  • Synonym: tinge - a slight amount or flavor of something
  • Synonym: touch - a very small amount of something

3. NOUN

/ hɪnt / (AmE), / hɪnt / (BrE)
a piece of information or suggestion that indicates the possibility of an opportunity or advantage
indication of potential opportunity

Examples

She received a hint from a friend about a job opening at the company.
The detective followed every hint in hopes of finding the missing artifact.
He was grateful for the hint that helped him get an interview for his dream job.

Synset Examples

he got a tip on the stock market
a good lead for a job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tip - a useful piece of advice, especially about how to do something or about the likely outcome of something
  • Synonym: lead - information pointing toward a possible opportunity, especially in work or investigation

Etymology

From Middle English hinten (to urge, incite), of uncertain origin.

4. NOUN

/ hɪnt / (AmE), / hɪnt / (BrE)
An indirect or subtle suggestion or indication.
indirect suggestion or indication

Examples

She dropped a hint about what she wanted for her birthday.
If you need help, just give me a hint and I'll assist you.
He didn't take the hint that it was time to leave.

Synset Examples

not a breath of scandal ever touched her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suggestion - an idea or proposal put forward for consideration
  • Synonym: clue - a piece of evidence or information used in the detection of a crime or solving of a mystery
  • Synonym: insinuation - an unpleasant hint or suggestion of something bad
  • Antonym: direct statement - a clear and straightforward remark or declaration

Etymology

From Middle English 'hinten', probably from early Scandinavian; compare Swedish 'hinta' (to hint).

5. VERB

/ hɪnt / (AmE), / hɪnt / (BrE)
to suggest or indicate something indirectly; to make a statement or gesture that implies something without saying it directly
suggest or imply indirectly

Examples

He tried to hint at his feelings without being too obvious.
During the meeting, she hinted that a major change was coming.
If you want a raise, you need to hint it more clearly to your boss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suggest - to state or express indirectly
  • Synonym: to imply - to indicate indirectly without explicit statement
  • Antonym: state - to say clearly or directly

Etymology

From Middle English 'hinten' (to hint, suggest), probably of imitative origin.

6. NOUN

/ hɪnt / (AmE), / hɪnt / (BrE)
a slight or indirect indication or suggestion
slight indication or suggestion

Examples

She gave a hint that she wanted to leave the party early.
There was not even a hint of excitement in his voice.
The sky had a hint of orange as the sun began to set.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clue - a piece of evidence or information used in the detection of a crime or solving a problem
  • Synonym: suggestion - an idea or plan put forward for consideration
  • Antonym: explicit statement - a direct and clear statement, opposite of hint

Etymology

Perhaps from Old Norse *hynt* or related to obsolete English 'hent' (to seize, catch), meaning to catch a suggestion.
