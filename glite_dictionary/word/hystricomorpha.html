Hystricomorpha

1. NOUN

/ hɪˌstrɪkoʊˈmɔrfə / (AmE), / hɪˌstrɪkəˈmɔːfə / (BrE)
an order of rodents that includes porcupines, guinea pigs, chinchillas, and related animals
order of rodents including porcupines, guinea pigs

Examples

The order Hystricomorpha includes animals such as porcupines, guinea pigs, and capybaras.
Researchers studied the evolutionary history of Hystricomorpha to understand rodent diversity.
The dental structure of Hystricomorpha distinguishes them from other rodent orders.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'hystrix' (porcupine) and 'morphē' (form, shape).

a suborder of rodents that includes porcupines, guinea pigs, chinchillas, and related animals
suborder of rodents (porcupines, guinea pigs, etc.)

Examples

The Hystricomorpha include animals like guinea pigs and chinchillas.
Scientists are studying the evolutionary history of the Hystricomorpha.
Porcupines are one of the more well-known members of the Hystricomorpha.

Related Terms

From New Latin, combining Greek hystricos (porcupine) + morphē (form/shape).
