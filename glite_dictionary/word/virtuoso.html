virtuoso

1. ADJ

/ ˌvɝː.tʃuˈoʊ.soʊ / (AmE), / ˌvɜː.tʃuˈəʊ.səʊ / (BrE)
showing or revealing exceptional skill or mastery, especially in a particular field or activity
showing exceptional skill or mastery

Examples

Her virtuoso performance on the piano stunned the entire audience.
The chef's virtuoso approach to cooking turned every meal into a work of art.
He delivered a virtuoso speech that captivated everyone in the hall.

Synset Examples

a consummate artist
consummate skill
a masterful speaker
masterful technique
a masterly performance of the sonata
a virtuoso performance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: masterful - showing great skill or expertise
  • Synonym: consummate - showing a high degree of skill or flair; complete or perfect

Etymology

From Italian 'virtuoso', meaning skilled or learned, originally from Latin 'virtus' (excellence, worth).

2. NOUN

/ ˌvɝː.tʃuˈoʊ.soʊ / (AmE), / ˌvɜː.tjuˈəʊ.səʊ / (BrE)
A musician with exceptional skill, mastery, and artistry in their craft.
exceptionally skilled musician

Examples

The virtuoso captivated the audience with a breathtaking violin solo.
Critics called her the greatest piano virtuoso of her generation.
Many aspiring musicians dream of becoming a virtuoso in their instrument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maestro - a distinguished conductor or musician

Etymology

From Italian 'virtuoso', meaning 'skilled, learned, or of great value', from 'virtù' (virtue, excellence).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10775180-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌvɝː.tʃuˈoʊ.soʊ / (AmE), / ˌvɜː.tjuˈəʊ.səʊ / (BrE)
A person highly skilled and accomplished in a particular art, especially music or another creative field.
person highly skilled in an art or field

Examples

The young pianist was quickly recognized as a virtuoso by critics around the world.
As a culinary virtuoso, she could transform simple ingredients into gourmet dishes.
His reputation as a virtuoso extended well beyond the world of classical guitar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: master - a person who has complete control or skill in a subject
  • Antonym: novice - a person new to and inexperienced in a field

Etymology

From Italian 'virtuoso' (skilled person), from 'virtù' (virtue, excellence).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09781932-n
Synset POS: n
