headful

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈhɛd.fʊl / (BrE)
as much as a person's mind or memory can contain; a large amount of knowledge or ideas held in the mind
as much as the mind can hold

Examples

After reading all day, I had a headful of new ideas.
She left the conference with a headful of inspiration to implement at work.
The professor always seems to have a headful of fascinating facts.

Synset Examples

he has a headful of baseball statistics

WordNet ID: oewn-13789685-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈhɛd.fʊl / (BrE)
an amount that covers or fills the head, especially with hair or other substances
amount covering or filling the head

Examples

After the haircut, she still had a headful of shiny, thick hair.
The child came home from school with a headful of sand and leaves.
He spent years growing a headful of dreadlocks.

Synset Examples

a headful of tight curls
a headful of lice
