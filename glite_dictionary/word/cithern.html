cithern

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪðɚn / (AmE), / ˈsɪθən / (BrE)
a 16th-century stringed musical instrument resembling a guitar, with a pear-shaped soundbox and wire strings
old pear-shaped stringed instrument

Examples

The museum displayed a beautifully crafted cithern from the late Renaissance era.
She learned to play the cithern for a historical music ensemble.
Unlike the modern guitar, the cithern had wire strings and a pear-shaped body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cittern - a similar stringed instrument from the Renaissance period

Etymology

From Latin *cithara*, from Greek *kithara* (lyre, stringed instrument).
