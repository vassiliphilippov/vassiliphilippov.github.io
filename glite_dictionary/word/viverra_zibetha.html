Viverra zibetha

1. NOUN

/ vɪˈvɛrə zɪˈbiːθə / (AmE), / vɪˈvɛərə zɪˈbiːθə / (BrE)
a large civet native to India and Southeast Asia, known for its long body, short legs, and musky scent glands
large civet of India and Southeast Asia

Examples

The Viverra zibetha is often found in forested regions of India and Southeast Asia.
Zoologists study the scent-marking behaviors of the Viverra zibetha.
The Viverra zibetha has distinctive black and white markings on its body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: civet - any small, lean, mostly nocturnal mammal of the family Viverridae

Etymology

From Latin, the scientific binomial assigned by zoologists.
