to latinise

1. VERB

/ ˈlætəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈlætɪnaɪz / (BrE)
to cause (a person, group, or institution) to adopt Catholicism, especially in its Latin Rite or Western Church traditions
cause to adopt Catholicism

Examples

The mission sought to latinise the local Christian communities by introducing Roman liturgy.
Efforts to latinise the clergy met with resistance from those attached to Eastern traditions.
In the 19th century, many churches were latinised during the Catholic revival.

Etymology

From Latin 'latinus' (Latin) + '-ise', indicating transformation or adoption.
