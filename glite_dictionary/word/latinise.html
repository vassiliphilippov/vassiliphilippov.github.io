to Latinise

1. VERB

/ ˈlæt.ɪ.naɪz / (AmE), / ˈlæt.ɪ.naɪz / (BrE)
to write a language or text using the Latin alphabet rather than its original writing system
write in the Latin alphabet
british

Examples

Many historical texts have been Latinised for easier reading by Western scholars.
The Chinese name was Latinised to assist with pronunciation in international documents.
During the process of standardization, local signs were Latinised to accommodate tourists.

Synset Examples

many shops in Japan now carry neon signs with Romanized names

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to Romanize - to write in the Roman (Latin) alphabet

Etymology

From Latin 'latinus' (Latin), with the suffix '-ise' (chiefly British spelling).
