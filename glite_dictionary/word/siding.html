siding

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
material applied to the exterior walls of a building to protect it from the weather and improve its appearance
exterior building covering material

Examples

The builders installed new siding on the house to improve insulation.
Vinyl siding comes in a variety of colors and textures for modern homes.
Over time, wooden siding can become warped or damaged by moisture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cladding - external layer for covering buildings

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
a short section of railway track connected to a main line, used for storing trains or allowing others to pass
railway track for storage or passing

Examples

The freight train was moved onto a siding to let the express pass.
Several old carriages were left on the disused siding for years.
A signalman directed the locomotive into the siding during maintenance work.
