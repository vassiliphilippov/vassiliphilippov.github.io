provirus

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / prəʊˈvaɪərəs / (BrE)
the genetic material of a virus, especially a retrovirus, that is integrated into the DNA of a host cell and can replicate along with it
viral DNA integrated into host genome
medical

Examples

After infection, the retrovirus inserts its provirus into the host's DNA.
Latent diseases can result from a provirus remaining dormant in the genome for years.
Researchers study how the provirus activates to develop antiviral therapies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virus - a microscopic infectious agent

Etymology

From Latin 'pro-' (before) + 'virus'; refers to the precursor stage of the virus within the host genome.
