indiscernibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndɪˌsɝːnəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˌsɜːnəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The state or quality of being impossible to perceive or distinguish.
inability to be perceived or distinguished

Examples

The indiscernibility of the object in the dim light made it difficult to identify.
Philosophers debate the indiscernibility of identicals in metaphysics.
With perfect camouflage, the animal achieves near total indiscernibility in its environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indistinguishability - the quality of being impossible to distinguish
  • Antonym: discernibility - the ability to be perceived or distinguished

Etymology

Formed from 'indiscernible' + '-ity', based on Latin 'discernere' (to separate, distinguish).
