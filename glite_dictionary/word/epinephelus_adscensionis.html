Epinephelus adscensionis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪnəˈfiːləs ædˌsɛnʃiˈoʊnɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪnɪˈfiːləs adˌsɛnʃɪˈəʊnɪs / (BrE)
A species of grouper fish, commonly known as the rock hind, found around rocky coasts or reefs in the western Atlantic Ocean.
species of grouper fish found near reefs

Examples

The Epinephelus adscensionis is often spotted near rocky reefs in the Caribbean Sea.
Scientists study the population decline of Epinephelus adscensionis due to overfishing.
The colorful markings help Epinephelus adscensionis blend into its reef environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rock hind - common name for Epinephelus adscensionis, a grouper fish

Etymology

From Greek 'epi-' (upon) + 'nephelē' (cloud) and Latin 'adscensionis' (of ascent), referring to the species' scientific classification.
