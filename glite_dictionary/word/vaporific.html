vaporific

1. ADJ

/ ˌveɪpəˈrɪfɪk / (AmE), / ˌveɪpəˈrɪfɪk / (BrE)
having the qualities or appearance of vapor; resembling or characteristic of vapor.
resembling or characteristic of vapor

Examples

The morning air was filled with a vaporific mist that blurred the city skyline.
Her painting captured the vaporific quality of clouds as they drifted across the mountains.
The vaporific effect made the landscape look almost dreamlike in the early sunlight.

Synset Examples

vaporous clouds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vaporous - resembling or consisting of vapor

Etymology

From Latin 'vapor' (steam, exhalation) + '-ificus' (making or producing).

2. ADJ

/ ˌveɪpəˈrɪfɪk / (AmE), / ˌveɪpəˈrɪfɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance) capable of being converted into vapor; able to be volatilized.
capable of becoming vapor

Examples

The vaporific nature of the liquid made it suitable for use in scientific experiments.
Certain vaporific compounds must be stored in airtight containers to prevent loss through evaporation.
This chemical is highly vaporific, so it should only be handled in a well-ventilated area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: volatile - (of a substance) easily evaporated at normal temperatures

Etymology

From Latin *vapor* (steam) + -ific (making, producing), from Latin *-ficus*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02529869-s
Synset POS: s
