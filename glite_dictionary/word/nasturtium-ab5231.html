nasturtium

1. NOUN

/ nəˈstɝrʃəm / (AmE), / nəˈstɜːʃəm / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Tropaeolum, native to tropical America, with pungent juice and showy, long-spurred yellow to red flowers, often grown as an ornamental.
a plant with brightly colored flowers

Examples

The vibrant nasturtium flowers added a splash of color to the garden.
She garnished the salad with fresh nasturtium petals for a peppery flavor.
Nasturtiums thrive in sunny spots with well-drained soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tropaeolum - a genus of plants including nasturtiums

Etymology

From Latin 'nasturtium', originally a kind of cress.

2. NOUN

/ nəˈstɝːʃəm / (AmE), / nəˈstɜːʃəm / (BrE)
an edible plant with round leaves and brightly colored flowers, whose leaves, flowers, and seeds are used as flavorings
edible plant with flavorful leaves, flowers, and seeds

Examples

She added fresh nasturtium leaves to the salad for a spicy kick.
The chef garnished the dish with vibrant nasturtium flowers.
You can pickle nasturtium seeds and use them as a substitute for capers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watercress - an aquatic plant with peppery-tasting leaves, used in salads

Etymology

From Latin 'nasturtium' (a kind of cress), from 'nasus' (nose) + 'torquere' (to twist), referring to its pungent odor.
