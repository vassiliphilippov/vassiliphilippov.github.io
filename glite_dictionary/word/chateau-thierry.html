Chateau-Thierry

1. PROPN

/ ʃæˌtoʊ θiˈɛri / (AmE), / ʃæˌtəʊ θiˈɛəri / (BrE)
a battle fought in northwestern France in 1918 during World War I, where Allied forces defeated the Germans
World War I battle in France, 1918

Examples

The Battle of Chateau-Thierry marked a turning point in World War I for the Allies.
Many soldiers lost their lives during the fierce fighting at Chateau-Thierry in 1918.
The victory at Chateau-Thierry boosted morale among Allied troops on the Western Front.

Etymology

From the name of the town Château-Thierry in France, site of the battle.
