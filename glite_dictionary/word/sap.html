sap

1. NOUN

/ sæp / (AmE), / sæp / (BrE)
a person who is easily deceived or lacks good judgment; a simpleton
foolish or gullible person
informal slang

Examples

Don't be such a sap, that's obviously a scam.
He felt like a real sap after falling for her prank.
Only a sap would believe that unbelievable story.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool - a person lacking sense or judgment
  • Synonym: dupe - a person easily tricked or deceived
  • Antonym: wise - having or showing experience, knowledge, and good judgment

2. NOUN

/ sæp / (AmE), / sæp / (BrE)
the watery fluid containing dissolved sugars, salts, and minerals that circulates through the vascular system of a plant
fluid in plants transporting nutrients

Examples

In spring, maple trees are tapped so their sap can be collected and turned into syrup.
When a leaf is damaged, the sap sometimes leaks out and dries along the edges.
Scientists studied the sap flow to understand how nutrients move within the plant.

Etymology

From Latin 'sapa' (new wine boiled down), related to 'sapere' (to taste), via Old English and Old French.

4. VERB

/ sæp / (AmE), / sæp / (BrE)
to excavate the earth beneath something in order to weaken or undermine it, especially in military contexts
to undermine by digging beneath

Examples

The soldiers began to sap the enemy walls under the cover of darkness.
Miners used to sap tunnels beneath the fortress to cause it to collapse.
In medieval warfare, armies would sap enemy fortifications to breach the defenses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to undermine - to weaken or damage by secret means, especially by digging underneath

Etymology

From Middle French *saper*, from Old Italian *sappare*, from Latin *sappa* (mattock, hoe).

5. VERB

/ sæp / (AmE), / sap / (BrE)
to gradually weaken or deplete the strength, energy, or effectiveness of someone or something
to weaken or deplete gradually

Examples

The long hours at work began to sap his enthusiasm for the project.
Constant criticism can sap your confidence over time.
Her illness has slowly sapped her strength, leaving her exhausted.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to weaken - make or become less strong
