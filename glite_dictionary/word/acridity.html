acridity

1. NOUN

/ əˈkrɪdəti / (AmE), / əˈkrɪdəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being acrid, especially having a bitter or pungent smell or taste
bitter or pungent quality

Examples

The acridity of the burning rubber filled the room with an unpleasant odor.
He recoiled at the acridity of the chemical fumes.
The acridity in the air made it difficult to breathe during the fire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acridness - the quality of being acrid; bitterness or pungency

Etymology

From Latin 'acriditas', from 'acridus' (sharp, pungent).

2. NOUN

/ əˈkrɪdəti / (AmE), / əˈkrɪdəti / (BrE)
the quality of being sharply bitter or harsh, especially in language or tone
sharp or bitter quality, esp. in language

Examples

The acridity of his remarks left the audience stunned into silence.
She was known for the acridity of her wit, which some found amusing and others, offensive.
Political debates can sometimes be filled with acridity rather than thoughtful discussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bitterness - the quality of having a sharp, pungent taste or character, also harshness of tone
  • Antonym: mildness - the quality of being moderate or gentle

Etymology

From Latin 'acriditas', from 'acer' (sharp).

3. NOUN

/ əˈkrɪdəti / (AmE), / əˈkrɪdəti / (BrE)
the quality of being extremely bitter or pungent, especially in taste or smell
extreme bitterness or pungency

Examples

The acridity of the smoke made it almost impossible to breathe in the room.
You could taste the acridity of the lemon peel in the dessert.
She wrinkled her nose at the acridity of the cleaning chemicals.

Synset Examples

the acridity of alkali

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bitterness - quality of having a sharp, unpleasant taste or smell
  • Antonym: sweetness - the quality of being sweet in taste or smell

Etymology

From Latin acidus (sour) via French and Latin roots.
