lax

1. ADJ

/ læks / (AmE), / læks / (BrE)
(of a vowel) pronounced with muscles of the tongue and jaw relatively relaxed, as in the vowel sound in 'bet'.
pronounced with relaxed tongue/jaw (phonetics)

Examples

In English, the vowel in "sit" is a lax vowel sound.
Phonologists often compare lax vowels to tense vowels in their analyses.
The distinction between tense and lax vowels is common in Germanic languages.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tense - (of a vowel) pronounced with greater muscular effort; the opposite of lax in phonetics

2. ADJ

/ læks / (AmE), / læks / (BrE)
(of the bowels or similar organs) tending to empty easily or excessively.
easily emptied (of bowels)
medical

Examples

The medication made her lax, leading to frequent trips to the bathroom.
Some patients become lax after changes in their diet or medication.
Doctors may describe the bowels as lax when discussing gastrointestinal issues.

Synset Examples

loose bowels

Related Terms

  • Antonym: constipated - unable to empty the bowels easily
Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ læks / (AmE), / læks / (BrE)
not firm, tight, or strict; lacking tension or rigor
not firm or tight

Examples

The lax knot slipped easily, causing the tent to collapse.
After months of use, the jump rope became lax and hard to control.
If the wire is lax, electricity may not flow efficiently.

Synset Examples

a lax rope

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slack - not tight, loose or not held firmly
  • Antonym: taut - pulled tight, not slack

Etymology

From Latin laxus (loose, slack).
