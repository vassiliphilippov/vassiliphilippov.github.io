demarcation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːmɑːrˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːmɑːˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of creating a clear distinction or boundary between two things, especially concepts or areas.
conceptual separation or boundary

Examples

There is often a fine demarcation between professional and personal boundaries.
The demarcation of responsibilities helped to avoid confusion within the team.
Philosophers debate the demarcation problem in distinguishing science from pseudoscience.

Synset Examples

there is a narrow line between sanity and insanity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boundary - a line marking the limits of an area or concept
  • Synonym: distinction - a difference or contrast between similar things or people
  • Antonym: blurring - lack of a clear distinction

Etymology

From French 'démarcation', from Spanish 'demarcación', from 'demarcar' (to demarcate), based on Latin 'marcare' (to mark).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdimɑːrˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːmɑːˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a line or boundary that marks the limits of an area; the act of establishing such boundaries
boundary or marking of limits

Examples

After the surveyors finished, a clear demarcation separated the two properties.
The river forms a natural demarcation between the two countries.
There was a distinct demarcation between public and private land.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boundary - a real or imagined line marking the limits of something
  • Synonym: border - the edge or boundary of something, especially a country or area
  • Synonym: limit - the point or edge beyond which something does not or may not extend or pass

Etymology

From French 'démarcation', from Spanish 'demarcación', from 'demarcar' ('to mark off'), from Latin 'de-' + 'marcare' ('to mark').
