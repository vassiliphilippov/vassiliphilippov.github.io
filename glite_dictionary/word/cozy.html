cozy

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊzi / (AmE), / ˈkəʊzi / (BrE)
A padded cloth covering placed over a teapot to keep it warm.
teapot covering to keep warm

Examples

She placed a floral cozy over the teapot to keep the tea hot.
My grandmother knitted a colorful cozy for her favorite teapot.
The kitchen shelf was lined with homemade jars and a bright cozy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tea cozy - a cover for keeping a teapot warm

2. ADJ

/ ˈkoʊzi / (AmE), / ˈkəʊzi / (BrE)
suggesting a situation in which people are colluding or acting together in a secret or unethical way for mutual benefit
suggesting secret collusion or insider dealings

Examples

There was talk of a cozy relationship between the company and regulators.
She denied any cozy arrangement with her rival.
Many felt that the deal was a little too cozy to be legitimate.

Synset Examples

a cozy arrangement with the police

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collusive - acting together secretly to achieve a fraudulent or deceitful purpose
  • Antonym: aboveboard - open and honest, not secretive or underhand

3. ADJ

/ ˈkoʊzi / (AmE), / ˈkəʊzi / (BrE)
warm, comfortable, and sheltered, especially in a small or intimate space
pleasantly warm and comfortable

Examples

We spent the evening in a cozy cabin by the lake.
Her reading chair in the corner looks especially cozy on cold days.
The restaurant has a cozy atmosphere that makes everyone feel at home.

Synset Examples

a cozy nook near the fire
snug in bed
a snug little apartment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snug - comfortable, warm, and protected, often in a small space
  • Synonym: comfortable - providing physical ease and relaxation

Etymology

Probably from Scandinavian, related to Norwegian 'koselig' (cosy, pleasant).

warm, comfortable, and friendly

Examples

Synset Examples
