officiation

1. NOUN

/ əˌfɪʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌfɪʃɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of acting as an official, especially as a referee or umpire, in a sporting event
the act of serving as an official

Examples

The officiation of the final match was widely praised for its fairness.
Many fans were critical of the officiation throughout the tournament.
The coach said the team's loss was not due to poor officiation.

Synset Examples

the officiating was excellent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to officiate - to act as an official in a ceremony or event

Etymology

From Latin officio (to perform a duty) via English officiate.

2. NOUN

/ əˌfɪʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌfɪʃiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of performing a religious, ceremonial, or formal public duty.
the act of performing a formal duty

Examples

The priest's officiation at the wedding was dignified and moving.
Her officiation of the graduation ceremony was praised by students and staff alike.
The committee thanked him for his many years of officiation at public events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to officiate - to act in an official capacity, especially at a ceremony or event

Etymology

From Latin 'officium' (duty, service) via 'officiate' + '-ion' suffix.
