poser

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊzər / (AmE), / ˈpəʊzə / (BrE)
a difficult or puzzling question or problem
difficult question or problem

Examples

That interview question was a real poser and left me stumped.
She enjoys solving any poser that her friends throw at her.
The final round of the quiz contained a poser that nobody could answer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conundrum - a confusing and difficult problem or question

Etymology

From French *poser* (to pose), from Old French *poser*, from Latin *pausare* (to pause).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊzər / (AmE), / ˈpəʊzə / (BrE)
a person who poses for a photographer, painter, or sculptor as a model
person who poses as a model

Examples

The poser held a thoughtful expression as the photographer adjusted the lighting.
She worked as a poser for art students learning to draw the human figure.
Many respected artists rely on a professional poser to achieve accurate lifelike portraits.

Synset Examples

the president didn't have time to be a model so the artist worked from photos

Related Terms

  • Synonym: model - a person employed to pose for an artist, photographer, or sculptor

Etymology

From pose + -er, indicating one who poses.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊzər / (AmE), / ˈpəʊzə / (BrE)
a person who pretends to be someone they are not, often to impress others or fit in with a group
person who pretends to be something they are not
informal

Examples

Many people thought he was a true artist, but in reality, he was just a poser.
Teenagers sometimes act like posers to fit in with certain social groups.
She accused him of being a poser who cared more about image than substance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impostor - someone who pretends to be someone else to deceive others
  • Antonym: authentic person - someone who is genuine or real

Etymology

From pose (to assume a particular attitude) + -er.

Source: WordNet
