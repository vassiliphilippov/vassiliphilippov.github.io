alpestrine

1. ADJ

/ ælˈpɛstrɪn / (AmE), / ælˈpɛstrɪn / (BrE)
(of plants or organisms) growing at high altitudes, especially in mountainous regions.
growing at high altitudes

Examples

Many rare alpestrine plants bloom only during the short summer season.
The researchers focused on the alpestrine region to study climate adaptation in mosses.
The park is home to a variety of alpestrine flowers found nowhere else in the country.

Etymology

From Latin 'alpestris', meaning 'of the Alps' or 'of high mountains'.
