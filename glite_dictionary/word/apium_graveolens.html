Apium graveolens

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.pi.əm ɡræv.iˈoʊ.lɛnz / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.pi.əm ˌɡræv.iˈəʊ.lɛnz / (BrE)
A herbaceous plant native to Europe and temperate Asia, commonly known as celery, cultivated for its edible stalks and leaves.
herbaceous plant; scientific name for celery

Examples

Apium graveolens is commonly grown in gardens worldwide for its crunchy stalks and aromatic leaves.
Scientific studies often reference Apium graveolens when discussing the medicinal properties of celery.
In botanical texts, Apium graveolens is frequently cited for its historical use in European cuisine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celery - a cultivated plant with crunchy edible stalks, Apium graveolens

Etymology

From Latin: 'apium' meaning parsley or celery, and 'graveolens' meaning strong-smelling.
