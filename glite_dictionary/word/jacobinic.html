Jacobinic

1. ADJ

/ ˌdʒækəˈbɪnɪk / (AmE), / ˌdʒækəˈbɪnɪk / (BrE)
relating to the Jacobins, a radical political group during the French Revolution, or to their policies or principles
relating to the Jacobins (French Revolution)

Examples

The committee was accused of pursuing Jacobinic policies reminiscent of the French Revolution.
His speech was filled with Jacobinic fervor and calls for radical change.
Historians debated the legacy of Jacobinic influence on modern revolutionary movements.

Synset Examples

Jacobinic terrorism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jacobin - member of a radical political group during the French Revolution

Etymology

From Jacobin (member of a political club during the French Revolution) + -ic (adjective-forming suffix).
