Gelechia gossypiella

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈlɛkiə ɡɒsɪˈpiːələ / (AmE), / ɡəˈlɛkiə ɡɒsɪˈpiːələ / (BrE)
a small brown moth whose larvae bore into flowers and bolls of cotton, commonly known as the pink bollworm
species of moth, pink bollworm

Examples

The larvae of the Gelechia gossypiella can cause significant damage to cotton crops.
Farmers implement pest management techniques to control Gelechia gossypiella infestations.
Gelechia gossypiella is also known as the pink bollworm in agricultural contexts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pink bollworm - common name for Gelechia gossypiella, a pest of cotton

Etymology

From the genus name 'Gelechia' and species name 'gossypiella,' referring to its association with cotton (Latin: gossypium).

2. NOUN

/ ɡəˈlɛkiə ɡɒsɪˈpiːələ / (AmE), / ɡəˈlɛkiə ɡɒsɪˈpiːələ / (BrE)
a species of moth whose larvae, known as pink bollworms, feed on the seeds of cotton bolls and are notable cotton pests
a moth species; pink bollworm

Examples

The infestation of Gelechia gossypiella caused severe damage to this year’s cotton crop.
Farmers implemented new strategies to control Gelechia gossypiella in their fields.
Research on Gelechia gossypiella has led to improved pest management techniques for cotton growers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pink bollworm - larval stage of Gelechia gossypiella, a cotton pest

Etymology

From the genus name 'Gelechia' and species epithet 'gossypiella', referring to cotton (Latin 'gossypium').
