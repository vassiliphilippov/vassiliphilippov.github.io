unnaturalised

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈnætʃrəlaɪzd / (AmE), / ʌnˈnatʃ(ə)rəlʌɪzd / (BrE)
(of a person) not having acquired citizenship of a country in which they reside.
not having acquired citizenship

Examples

Many unnaturalised immigrants work and pay taxes in their new country.
The policy focused on the rights of unnaturalised residents.
He remained unnaturalised despite living in the city for decades.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: naturalised - having acquired citizenship of a country

Etymology

Formed by prefixing 'un-' to 'naturalised', from 'naturalise' meaning to grant citizenship.
