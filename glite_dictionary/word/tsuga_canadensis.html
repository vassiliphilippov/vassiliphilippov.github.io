Tsuga canadensis

1. NOUN

/ ˈtsuːɡə kəˈnædɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˈtsuːɡə kəˈnædɛnsɪs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the eastern hemlock, a large forest tree native to the eastern United States and Canada, valued for its wood and use as pulpwood.
eastern hemlock tree (scientific name)

Examples

The dense shade under the Tsuga canadensis made the forest floor cool and moist.
Many wildlife species depend on Tsuga canadensis for shelter and food.
Paper mills in the region use Tsuga canadensis as a source of high-quality pulpwood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eastern hemlock - common name for Tsuga canadensis, a coniferous tree species

Etymology

From Japanese 'tsuga' (hemlock); 'canadensis' meaning 'of Canada' in Neo-Latin.
