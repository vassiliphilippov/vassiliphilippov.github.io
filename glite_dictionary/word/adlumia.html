Adlumia

1. NOUN

/ ædˈluːmiə / (AmE), / ædˈluːmɪə / (BrE)
A genus of climbing plants in the poppy family, known for their delicate, feathery foliage and pale flowers, especially Adlumia fungosa, the climbing fumitory of North America.
a genus of climbing fumitory plants

Examples

The shady woodland was dotted with Adlumia gracefully climbing over rocks and trees.
Gardeners admire Adlumia for its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of pinkish flowers.
The botanist collected a specimen of Adlumia to study its unique climbing habit.

Etymology

Named after American botanist John Adlum (1759–1836).

2. NOUN

/ ædˈluːmiə / (AmE), / ædˈluːmiə / (BrE)
A genus of climbing plants in the poppy family (Fumariaceae), native to North America, with one species known as climbing fumitory.
genus of climbing plant (climbing fumitory)

Examples

The delicate flowers of Adlumia are often found cascading over rocky slopes.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Adlumia for its graceful, climbing habit and attractive blooms.
Adlumia thrives in moist, shaded woodlands of eastern North America.

Etymology

Named after John Adlum, an American agriculturist.
