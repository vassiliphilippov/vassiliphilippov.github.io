atonality

1. NOUN

/ ˌætoʊˈnæləti / (AmE), / ˌatəˈnalɪti / (BrE)
the absence of a tonal center or key in music, as an alternative to the diatonic system
absence of key in music

Examples

The composer's later works are known for their use of atonality and experimental harmonies.
Some listeners find atonality challenging because it lacks a familiar sense of musical center.
The shift from traditional tonality to atonality marked a significant change in twentieth-century classical music.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tonality - the organization of music around a central key or tonic

Etymology

From 'a-' (not, without) + 'tonality', patterned after similar terms in music theory.
