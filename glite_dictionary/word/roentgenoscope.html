roentgenoscope

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛntɡənoʊˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈrɛntɡənəˌskəʊp / (BrE)
an X-ray device that allows direct observation by combining an X-ray source with a fluorescent screen
X-ray device for direct observation
medical

Examples

The surgeon used a roentgenoscope to observe the patient's internal organs during the procedure.
Early radiologists relied on the roentgenoscope to visualize bone fractures in real time.
Before digital imaging, the roentgenoscope was essential for continuous X-ray observation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fluoroscope - instrument allowing direct viewing of X-ray images on a screen

Etymology

From Wilhelm Röntgen, discoverer of X-rays, plus 'scope' (device for viewing).
