vermiculate

1. VERB

/ vərˈmɪkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / vəˈmɪkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to decorate with wavy or winding lines, especially so as to resemble the tracks of a worm
decorate with wavy lines

Examples

The artist chose to vermiculate the vase, giving it an intricate, wavy pattern.
Ancient craftsmen would often vermiculate stone surfaces as a decorative technique.
She watched the baker vermiculate the top of the pie with delicate swirls of icing.

Etymology

From Latin 'vermiculatus', past participle of 'vermiculare' (to adorn with worm-shaped lines), from 'vermis' (worm).

2. ADJ

/ vərˈmɪkjələt / (AmE), / vəˈmɪkjʊlət / (BrE)
infested with or damaged as if by worms; showing marks or holes as though eaten by worms
infested or damaged as if by worms

Examples

The old wooden beams were so vermiculate that they crumbled at the touch.
She examined the painting's frame, noting its vermiculate appearance and tiny, twisting holes.
The archaeologist found vermiculate bones in the ancient burial site, riddled with worm tracks.

Etymology

From Latin 'vermiculate', past participle of 'vermiculare' (to make worm-like marks), from 'vermis' (worm).

Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ vərˈmɪkjələt / (AmE), / vəˈmɪkjʊlət / (BrE)
decorated or marked with wormlike, winding, or sinuous lines or tracery, especially in stone or other material
decorated with wormlike markings

Examples

The sculptor created a vermiculate pattern on the marble surface, adding a sense of movement.
The old building's facade featured vermiculate stonework that resembled winding trails.
We admired the artist's vermiculate designs, which meandered across the entire canvas.

Synset Examples

vermicular (or vermiculated) stonework

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vermicular - having a shape or appearance like that of a worm

Etymology

From Latin vermiculatus, from vermiculus (little worm), diminutive of vermis (worm).
