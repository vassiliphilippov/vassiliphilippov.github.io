partially

1. ADV

/ ˈpɑːrʃəli / (AmE), / ˈpɑːʃəli / (BrE)
To some extent; not completely or wholly.
not completely; to some extent

Examples

The bridge was partially closed after the storm for safety reasons.
Her vision is partially impaired in her left eye.
The company only partially accepted responsibility for the mistake.

Synset Examples

I felt partly to blame
He was partially paralyzed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: completely - in every way; totally
  • Synonym: partly - to a limited degree or in part, not fully

Etymology

From Latin 'pars' (part) via Medieval Latin 'partialis' plus English adverbial suffix '-ly'.
