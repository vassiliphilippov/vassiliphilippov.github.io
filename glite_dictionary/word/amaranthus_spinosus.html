Amaranthus spinosus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæməˈrænθəs spaɪˈnoʊsəs / (AmE), / ˌaməˈranθəs spaɪˈnəʊsəs / (BrE)
an erect annual plant native to tropical central Asia and Africa, characterized by a pair of divergent spines at most leaf nodes
spiny annual plant of Asia and Africa

Examples

Amaranthus spinosus is often found growing as a weed in tropical crop fields.
The spiny stems of Amaranthus spinosus distinguish it from other amaranths.
Traditional medicines in parts of Africa use Amaranthus spinosus for various remedies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amaranth - any plant of the genus Amaranthus

Etymology

From Latin: Amaranthus (a plant genus, from Greek 'amarantos' meaning unfading) + spinosus (Latin for spiny).
