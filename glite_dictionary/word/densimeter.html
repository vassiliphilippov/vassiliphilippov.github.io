densimeter

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛnˈsɪmətər / (AmE), / ˌdenˈsɪmɪtə / (BrE)
An instrument used for measuring the density or specific gravity of a substance.
device for measuring density

Examples

The scientist used a densimeter to determine the density of the chemical solution.
A densimeter is essential for accurately measuring the specific gravity of liquids in the laboratory.
After calibration, the densimeter provided precise readings for each sample.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrometer - an instrument for measuring the density of liquids
  • Synonym: areometer - an instrument used to measure the specific gravity of liquids

Etymology

From Latin 'densus' (thick, dense) + Greek 'metron' (measure).
