Anwar el-Sadat

1. PROPN

/ ˈænwɑr ɛl səˈdɑt / (AmE), / ˈænwɑː ɛl səˈdæt / (BrE)
(1918–1981) Egyptian statesman who served as president of Egypt and was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for negotiating a peace treaty with Israel.
Egyptian president and Nobel Peace Prize laureate

Examples

Anwar el-Sadat played a key role in brokering peace between Egypt and Israel in the late 1970s.
Many Egyptians remember Anwar el-Sadat as a leader who took bold steps towards diplomatic change.
The assassination of Anwar el-Sadat in 1981 shocked the world and altered Egypt's history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: president - head of state in a republic
