2. NOUN

/ paɪp / (AmE), / paɪp / (BrE)
a long, hollow tube made of metal, plastic, or other materials, used to carry water, oil, gas, or other fluids from one place to another
tube for carrying fluids

Examples

The plumber replaced the old pipe under the kitchen sink.
Gas flows through this pipe to reach the stove.
They had to dig up the street to repair a broken water pipe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tube - a long, hollow cylinder used for conveying fluids

From Latin 'pipa', from Vulgar Latin 'pipa' (pipe, tube), probably of imitative origin.
