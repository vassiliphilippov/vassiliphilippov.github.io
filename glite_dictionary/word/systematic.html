systematic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɪstəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɪstəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
(Biology) Relating to taxonomy or the classification of organisms.
relating to taxonomy (biology)

Examples

Recent studies focus on the systematic relationships among bird species.
She contributed to the systematic classification of flowering plants.
The systematic position of this animal has long been debated.

Synset Examples

taxonomic relations
a taxonomic designation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taxonomic - relating to classification of organisms

Etymology

From Greek systēmatikós (combined in a system).

2. ADJ

/ ˌsɪstəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɪstəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
done or acting according to a fixed plan or system; methodical.
methodical and orderly

Examples

The scientist conducted a systematic review of all available research on the topic.
Her systematic approach to organizing files made the entire office more efficient.
To solve the problem, he used a systematic method that left no detail unchecked.

Synset Examples

the investigation was very systematic
a systematic administrator

Related Terms

  • Synonym: methodical - done according to a systematic or established form of procedure
  • Antonym: chaotic - disordered and lacking organization

Etymology

From Greek systēmatikos, from systēma (organized whole).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02391502-a
Synset POS: a
