gasoline

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæsəliːn / (AmE), / ˈɡæsəliːn / (BrE)
A volatile, flammable liquid mixture derived from petroleum, used mainly as a fuel in internal-combustion engines.
liquid fuel for engines, from petroleum
american

Examples

The car ran out of gasoline just before reaching the nearest station.
Always turn off the engine before refilling with gasoline to avoid sparks.
Some lawnmowers use a mixture of gasoline and oil for fuel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petrol - British term for gasoline, 'fuel for engines from petroleum'

Etymology

From obsolete French 'gas-oiline', modeled after oil and gas.
