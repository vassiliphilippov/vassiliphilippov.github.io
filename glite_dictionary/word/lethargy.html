lethargy

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛθɚdʒi / (AmE), / ˈlɛθəˌdʒi / (BrE)
A state of physical or mental weariness; a lack of energy and enthusiasm.
lack of energy or enthusiasm

Examples

The heat and humidity caused a feeling of lethargy throughout the office.
After recovering from the flu, she still experienced some lethargy and tiredness.
His persistent lethargy worried his doctor, who ordered further tests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: listlessness - a lack of energy or enthusiasm
  • Antonym: vitality - the state of being strong and active; energy

Etymology

From Greek lēthargia, from lēthargos (forgetful), from lēthē (forgetfulness) + argos (idle).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛθərdʒi / (AmE), / ˈlɛθədʒi / (BrE)
A state of abnormal drowsiness, inactivity, or lack of energy, especially as a medical condition.
abnormal drowsiness or inactivity

Examples

After her illness, a deep lethargy made it difficult to get out of bed each morning.
The medication’s side effects included lethargy, causing him to feel unusually tired all day.
During the heatwave, a sense of lethargy spread across the city as people struggled to cope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stupor - a state of near-unconsciousness or insensibility
  • Antonym: vigor - physical strength and good health, high energy

Etymology

From Greek lethargia, from lethargos (forgetful), from lethe (forgetfulness) + argos (idle).

3. NOUN

/ ˈlɛθərdʒi / (AmE), / ˈlɛθəˌdʒi / (BrE)
A state of tiredness, inactivity, or lack of energy, often unusual or excessive.
state of unusual tiredness or inactivity

Examples

After his illness, he experienced persistent lethargy that made it hard to return to work.
The summer heat filled the whole town with a sense of lethargy.
Doctors became concerned about the child's sudden lethargy and loss of appetite.

Synset Examples

the general appearance of sluggishness alarmed his friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sluggishness - lack of energy or alertness
  • Antonym: vigor - physical strength and energy

Etymology

From Greek lethargia (forgetfulness), from lethe (forgetfulness) + -argos (idle).
