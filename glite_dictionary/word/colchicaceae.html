Colchicaceae

1. NOUN

/ koʊlˌkɪˈkeɪsiː / (AmE), / kɒlˌkɪˈkeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants that includes genera such as Colchicum and Gloriosa, sometimes considered a subfamily of Liliaceae in certain botanical classifications.
family of flowering plants (e.g., Colchicum, Gloriosa)

Examples

Many autumn-blooming bulb plants belong to the family Colchicaceae.
The classification of Colchicaceae has been a topic of debate among botanists.
Plants in the Colchicaceae family are known for producing toxic alkaloids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Liliaceae - a family of flowering plants, commonly known as the lily family

Etymology

From the genus name Colchicum, itself from Latin, relating to Colchis, an ancient region on the Black Sea.

2. NOUN

/ koʊlˌkɪkəˈsiːi / (AmE), / kɒlˌkɪkəˈsiːi / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants that includes the genera Colchicum and Gloriosa, sometimes separated from Liliaceae in certain classification systems
family of flowering plants including Colchicum and Gloriosa

Examples

The Colchicaceae family is known for plants that produce colchicine, a toxic alkaloid.
Botanists recently reclassified several genera from Liliaceae to Colchicaceae.
Members of the Colchicaceae can often be found in temperate and subtropical regions.

Etymology

From the genus name Colchicum, derived from Colchis, an ancient region on the Black Sea; the suffix -aceae denotes a plant family in botanical Latin.
