unable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈeɪbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈeɪbəl / (BrE)
not having the power, skill, or means to do something; incapable.
not capable of doing something

Examples

She was unable to attend the meeting due to illness.
He felt unable to cope with the sudden changes.
Without the proper tools, they were unable to complete the repairs.

Synset Examples

unable to get to town without a car
unable to obtain funds

Related Terms

  • Antonym: able - having the power, skill, or means to do something

Etymology

From un- (not) + able (having the power or skill), dating to Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00002098-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈeɪbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈeɪbl / (BrE)
not having the necessary ability, power, or means to do something (usually followed by 'to')
lacking ability or power to do something

Examples

She was unable to attend the meeting due to illness.
The machine is unable to complete the task without further instructions.
After the accident, he found himself unable to move his left arm.

Synset Examples

dyslexics are unable to learn to read adequately
the sun was unable to melt enough snow

Related Terms

  • Antonym: able - having the power or ability to do something
  • Synonym: incapable - unable to do something; lacking ability or capacity

Etymology

From un- (not) + able, from Latin *habilis* via Old French *able*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00308592-s
Synset POS: s
