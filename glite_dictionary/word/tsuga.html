Tsuga

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːɡə / (AmE), / ˈtsuːɡə / (BrE)
a genus of coniferous trees in the pine family, commonly known as hemlocks, native to areas of North America and Asia
genus of hemlock coniferous trees

Examples

Several species of Tsuga are found in the forests of eastern North America.
The needles of Tsuga trees are short and flat, distinguishing them from other conifers.
Some gardeners plant Tsuga because of its graceful, drooping branches and evergreen foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemlock - a common name for trees of the genus Tsuga

Etymology

From Japanese ツガ (tsuga), the local name for hemlock species.

Examples
