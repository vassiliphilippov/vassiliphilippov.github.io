rationalness

1. NOUN

/ ˈræʃənəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈræʃənəlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being consistent with or based on logic or reason
quality of being logical or reasonable

Examples

The rationalness of her argument convinced everyone in the room.
Scientists prize the rationalness of their methods when searching for answers.
He questioned the rationalness of the company's decision to cut research funding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rationality - the quality of being rational, logical, and reasonable
  • Antonym: irrationality - the quality of being illogical or unreasonable

Etymology

From 'rational' + '-ness', after Middle English formation patterns.
