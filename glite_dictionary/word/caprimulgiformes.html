Caprimulgiformes

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæprəmʌlˈdʒɪfɔːrmiːz / (AmE), / ˌkæprɪˌmʌlˈdʒɪfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of nocturnal birds that includes goatsuckers, frogmouths, and oilbirds, characterized by their large mouths and soft plumage
an order of nocturnal birds

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the Caprimulgiformes order in the Amazon rainforest.
Many Caprimulgiformes are known for their camouflaged plumage and quiet flight at night.
The Caprimulgiformes include unique birds like the oilbird and various nightjars.

Etymology

From Latin caprimulgus (goatsucker) + -formes (having the form of).
