portraiture

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔɹ.tʃəɹ.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɔː.trɪ.tjʊə / (BrE)
the art or practice of making portraits, especially painted or drawn likenesses of people
the art of making portraits

Examples

The museum holds an extensive collection of portraiture from the 18th and 19th centuries.
She specializes in photographic portraiture, capturing the character of her subjects.
During the Renaissance, portraiture became an important genre in European art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: portrait - a painting, drawing, photograph, or engraving of a person

Etymology

From Old French *portraiture* (representation), from *portraire* (to portray).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrtrətʃər / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtrətʃə / (BrE)
A description in words of a person's appearance and character; a verbal or written portrait.
verbal description of a person

Examples

His biography excels in its portraiture of the artist as a young man.
The novelist's skillful portraiture revealed the complexities of her main character.
Literary portraiture often captures both appearance and inner life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: portrait - a painting, drawing, or photograph of a person

Etymology

From Old French *portraiture* (representation or likeness), from *portrait* (to portray).
