andromeda

1. NOUN

/ ænˈdrɑːmədə / (AmE), / ænˈdrɒmɪdə / (BrE)
A broad-leaved evergreen shrub native to Asia, with glossy leaves and drooping clusters of white flowers, belonging to the genus Pieris.
broad-leaved evergreen Asian shrub

Examples

The garden was adorned with andromeda shrubs in full bloom.
We planted an andromeda for its elegant clusters of white flowers.
The glossy leaves of the andromeda add year-round beauty to the landscape.

Etymology

Named after Andromeda from Greek mythology; genus name assigned by Carl Linnaeus.

2. NOUN

/ ænˈdrɑːmədə / (AmE), / ænˈdrɒmɪdə / (BrE)
Any of several evergreen shrubs of the genus Andromeda, having leathery leaves and clusters of small flowers, found mainly in northern and arctic regions.
a genus of evergreen shrubs with leathery leaves

Examples

The garden featured several andromeda bushes with bright white flowers in early spring.
Wild andromeda plants thrive in the bogs of northern Europe and North America.
She identified the shrub as an andromeda based on its distinctive leathery leaves.

Etymology

Named after the mythological princess Andromeda; from Latin 'Andromeda', originally the name for the plant by Linnaeus.
