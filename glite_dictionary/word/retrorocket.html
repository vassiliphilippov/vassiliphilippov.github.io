retrorocket

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛtroʊˌrɑkɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛtrəʊˌrɒkɪt / (BrE)
a small rocket engine on a spacecraft or missile, fired in the opposite direction to slow down, stop, or change direction
rocket engine used for slowing spacecraft

Examples

The spacecraft fired its retrorocket to slow down for re-entry into the Earth's atmosphere.
Engineers checked the retrorocket system before the module began its descent.
If the retrorocket fails, the capsule may not reduce its speed enough for a safe landing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thruster - a small rocket engine used to adjust a spacecraft's position or orientation

Etymology

From Latin 'retro' (backward) and 'rocket.'
