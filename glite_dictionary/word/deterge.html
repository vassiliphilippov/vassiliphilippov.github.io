to deterge

1. VERB

/ dɪˈtɜrdʒ / (AmE), / dɪˈtɜːdʒ / (BrE)
to cleanse or wash away impurities, especially by removing foul matter from the body; chiefly used in medical contexts
cleanse by washing off impurities
medical

Examples

The nurse used a sterile solution to deterge the patient's wound.
Some ointments are designed not only to protect but also to deterge minor abrasions.
Proper care involves gently deterging the affected skin to promote healing.

Synset Examples

This drug is powerful enough to deterge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cleanse - make thoroughly clean

Etymology

From Latin detergēre (to wipe away, cleanse).
