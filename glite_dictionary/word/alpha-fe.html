alpha-Fe

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.fə ɛf i / (AmE), / ˈæl.fə ɛf iː / (BrE)
a magnetic form of iron (ferrite) stable below 906°C, commonly found at room temperature.
magnetic allotrope of iron
medical

Examples

alpha-Fe is commonly present in the Earth's crust at ambient conditions.
When steel is cooled slowly, it transforms into alpha-Fe, also known as ferrite.
Researchers study the magnetic properties of alpha-Fe for various technological applications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ferrite - a magnetic allotrope of iron present at room temperature
  • Degree Higher: iron - common metallic element, Fe, used to make steel

Etymology

From the Greek letter alpha (α), indicating the first form, and chemical symbol Fe (iron, from Latin 'ferrum').
