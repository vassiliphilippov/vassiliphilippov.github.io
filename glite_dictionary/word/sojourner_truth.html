Sojourner Truth

1. PROPN

/ soʊˈdʒɝːnɚ ˈtruːθ / (AmE), / səʊˈdʒɜːnə ˈtruːθ / (BrE)
(1797–1883) United States abolitionist and feminist who was freed from slavery and became a prominent advocate for the abolition of slavery and women's rights.
U.S. abolitionist and feminist (1797–1883)

Examples

Sojourner Truth delivered powerful speeches on abolition and women's rights throughout the United States.
Many historians view Sojourner Truth as a trailblazer in both the antislavery and women's suffrage movements.
A statue honoring Sojourner Truth stands in the U.S. Capitol to recognize her contributions to American history.

Etymology

Adopted name: 'Sojourner' meaning 'traveler' and 'Truth', reflecting her mission to speak the truth about slavery and injustice.
