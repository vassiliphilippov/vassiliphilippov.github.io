Sir Ronald Ross

1. PROPN

/ sɜɹ ˈɹɑnəld rɑs / (AmE), / sɜː ˈrɒnəld rɒs / (BrE)
(1857–1932) British physician who discovered that mosquitoes transmit malaria, winning the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine in 1902.
British physician who discovered mosquito transmission of malaria

Examples

Sir Ronald Ross was awarded the Nobel Prize in 1902 for his groundbreaking discovery about malaria.
Research into malaria changed dramatically after Sir Ronald Ross identified mosquitoes as the disease’s vector.
The legacy of Sir Ronald Ross continues in modern tropical medicine.
