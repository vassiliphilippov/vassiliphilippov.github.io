Anselm

1. PROPN

/ ˈæn.sɛlm / (AmE), / ˈan.selm / (BrE)
(1033–1109) An Italian Benedictine monk, philosopher, and theologian, who served as Archbishop of Canterbury and is regarded as one of the founders of scholasticism.
Italian Benedictine monk, Archbishop of Canterbury, philosopher

Examples

Anselm is best known for his ontological argument for the existence of God.
The writings of Anselm had a profound influence on medieval philosophy.
As Archbishop of Canterbury, Anselm played a key role in church reform.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'Anselmus', from Old High German 'Ansehelm' (protected by God).
