landline

1. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈlan(d)laɪn / (BrE)
A telephone line that transmits signals over wires or fiber optics, rather than using a mobile or cellular network.
telephone line using wires or fiber optics

Examples

We still have a landline at home in case of emergencies.
He called the office using the landline because his mobile phone battery was dead.
Many people are now disconnecting their landlines and relying solely on mobile phones.

Synset Examples

a land line can be wire or fiber optics or microwave

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mobile phone - A wireless telephone that uses cellular networks.

Etymology

Formed from 'land' and 'line', indicating a telephone line over land-based transmission lines.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈlan(d).laɪn / (BrE)
a telephone line that transmits signals over wires or fiber optics, as opposed to through cellular or satellite systems
wired telephone line

Examples

She prefers using her landline instead of a cell phone for important calls.
During the power outage, our landline was still working while the mobile network was down.
Please provide your landline number on the application form.

Synset Examples

a land line can be wire or fiber optics or microwave

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cell phone - a portable telephone that uses cellular network

Etymology

From 'land' (referring to the ground or earth) + 'line' (wire or cable), distinguishing it from wireless lines.
