waterproof

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɔtərˌpruf / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌpruːf / (BrE)
not allowing water to pass through; impervious to water.
impervious to water

Examples

Make sure your tent is waterproof before camping in the rain.
She bought a waterproof jacket for hiking in wet weather.
The watch is waterproof up to a depth of fifty meters.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: water-resistant - able to resist the penetration of water to some degree

Etymology

From water + proof, modeled after late Middle English sense of 'resistant to.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtərˌpruf / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌpruːf / (BrE)
A coat or garment made of material that does not let water pass through, worn to protect against rain.
water-resistant coat or garment
british

Examples

He put on his waterproof before heading out into the storm.
Don't forget your waterproof if it's going to rain today.
The hikers carried lightweight waterproofs for the unexpected downpour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raincoat - a coat worn to protect the wearer from rain

Etymology

Formed from 'water' + 'proof', originally in the 19th century.

3. VERB

/ ˈwɔtərˌpruf / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌpruːf / (BrE)
To make something resistant to the penetration of water by applying a special treatment or coating.
make resistant to water

Examples

Remember to waterproof your boots before hiking in wet conditions.
They hired a contractor to waterproof the basement after the last flood.
You can waterproof your tent using a special spray available at outdoor stores.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seal - to make something tightly closed so that nothing can enter or leave, especially air or liquid

4. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtərˌpruf / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌpruːf / (BrE)
A fabric or material that does not allow water to pass through it; impervious to water.
fabric or material resistant to water

Examples

Make sure the tent is made of waterproof to withstand heavy rain.
The jacket has a lining of waterproof to keep you dry.
They chose a waterproof for the outer layer of the coat.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: water-resistant - resistant but not fully impervious to water
  • Antonym: absorbent - material that soaks up liquids; opposite of waterproof

Etymology

Formed from 'water' + 'proof', indicating resistance to water; modeled after 19th-century waterproof garments.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04569020-n
Synset POS: n
