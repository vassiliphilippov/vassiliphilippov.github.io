George Stephenson

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒɔrdʒ ˈstɛf.ən.sən / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːdʒ ˈstɛf.ən.sən / (BrE)
(1781–1848) An English engineer and railway pioneer who built the first public railway to use steam locomotives for passenger service in 1825.
English railway pioneer (1781–1848)

Examples

George Stephenson is often called the 'Father of Railways' for his pioneering work.
The Stockton and Darlington Railway, engineered by George Stephenson, opened in 1825.
Many history books credit George Stephenson with revolutionizing transport during the Industrial Revolution.
