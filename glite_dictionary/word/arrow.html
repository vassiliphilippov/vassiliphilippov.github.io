arrow

1. NOUN

/ ˈæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈærəʊ / (BrE)
A long, thin projectile with a pointed tip and feathered tail, designed to be shot from a bow.
projectile shot from a bow

Examples

The hunter carefully notched an arrow onto his bowstring before aiming.
Medieval soldiers often carried a quiver filled with arrows for defense.
She shot the arrow straight into the center of the target.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bolt - a short, heavy projectile shot from a crossbow

Etymology

Old English *earh*, related to Old Norse *ör* and Proto-Germanic origins.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈærəʊ / (BrE)
a symbol, usually a straight line with a pointed end, used to show direction or indicate a relationship, especially in diagrams, signs, or computer interfaces
a direction or relationship symbol

Examples

On the map, the arrow points north to help with orientation.
Each arrow in the diagram shows the direction of data flow.
Follow the arrow on the sign to find the exit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pointer - a symbol or mark used to indicate a position or direction
