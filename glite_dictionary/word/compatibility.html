compatibility

1. NOUN

/ kəmˌpætəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / kəmˌpatəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the ability of two or more things to exist or work together without conflict or problems
ability to coexist harmoniously

Examples

There was a surprising level of compatibility between their working styles.
Before installing the new software, check its compatibility with your computer's operating system.
Their friendship was built on a foundation of mutual respect and compatibility.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incompatibility - the state of being unable to exist or work together without conflict

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'compatibilitas', from Late Latin 'compatibilis' (compatible).

2. NOUN

/ kəmˌpætəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / kəmˌpatɪˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
a state in which two or more people feel mutual understanding and can exist together harmoniously
mutual understanding; ability to get along

Examples

Their compatibility was evident from the way they finished each other's sentences.
The team's compatibility made working together enjoyable and productive.
Relationship counselors often discuss the importance of compatibility between partners.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incompatibility - the state of being unable to exist together without conflict
  • Synonym: harmony - a pleasing arrangement or agreement; agreement in feeling or opinion

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'compatibilitas', from Latin 'compatibilis' (able to live together).

Synset POS: n
