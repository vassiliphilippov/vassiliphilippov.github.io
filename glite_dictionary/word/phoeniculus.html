Phoeniculus

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈniːkjələs / (AmE), / fəʊˈniːkjʊləs / (BrE)
a genus of birds in the family Phoeniculidae, commonly known as woodhoopoes
genus of birds (woodhoopoes)

Examples

The genus Phoeniculus includes several species of African woodhoopoes.
Members of Phoeniculus are recognized by their metallic plumage and distinctive calls.
Ornithologists studied the nesting behavior of Phoeniculus in tropical woodlands.

Etymology

From Greek phoinikos (palm tree), relating to the habitat of the birds.

2. PROPN

/ foʊˈnɪkjʊləs / (AmE), / fəʊˈnɪkjʊləs / (BrE)
A genus of birds that is the sole genus in the family Phoeniculidae, known as woodhoopoes, found in sub-Saharan Africa.
genus of woodhoopoe birds

Examples

The Phoeniculus genus comprises several species of brightly colored woodhoopoes native to Africa.
Researchers studied the social behavior of Phoeniculus in the wild.
The striking metallic plumage of Phoeniculus species makes them easily recognizable among African birds.

Etymology

From Greek phoinikos (purple-red, crimson), a reference to the birds' iridescent plumage.
