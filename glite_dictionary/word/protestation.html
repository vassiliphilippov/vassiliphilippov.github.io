protestation

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊ.tɛˈsteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌprəʊ.tɛˈsteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a formal or emphatic declaration of objection or disagreement, often made publicly or officially
formal declaration of objection

Examples

Despite his loud protestation of innocence, the police continued their investigation.
Her protestations were ignored by the committee during the final decision.
He made a protestation against the unfair treatment he received at work.

Synset Examples

they finished the game under protest to the league president
the senator rose to register his protest
the many protestations did not stay the execution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protest - an expression or declaration of objection or dissent
  • Antonym: approval - agreement or acceptance; a favorable opinion

Etymology

From Old French 'protestacion', from Latin 'protestatio' (a public declaration).

2. NOUN

/ ˌproʊ.t̬əˈsteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌprəʊ.tɛˈsteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a strong or formal expression of disagreement, objection, or denial
strong declaration of protest

Examples

Despite his protestation of innocence, the committee found him guilty.
She submitted a formal protestation against the unfair decision.
His protestations fell on deaf ears, as no one believed him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protest - a statement or action expressing objection or disapproval
  • Synonym: denial - a statement that something is not true or does not exist

Etymology

From Old French protestacion, from Latin protestatio (a public declaration).
