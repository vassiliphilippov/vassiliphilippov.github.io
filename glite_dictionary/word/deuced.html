deuced

1. ADJ

/ ˈduːsɪd / (AmE), / ˈdjuːsɪd / (BrE)
(informal, old-fashioned) Used as an intensifier, especially to express annoyance or emphasis; damned or confounded.
intensifier, mild expletive
informal archaic

Examples

He found the instructions deuced confusing and almost gave up.
It's a deuced shame that we missed the last train.
That was a deuced clever idea, I must say.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damned - Used as an intensifier expressing annoyance or emphasis
  • Synonym: blasted - Used informally as an intensifier, mild expletive

Etymology

From 'deuce', an old euphemism for the devil, used in the sense of 'damned'.
