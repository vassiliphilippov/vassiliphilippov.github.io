to soft-solder

1. VERB

/ sɒft ˈsɑːdər / (AmE), / sɒft ˈsɒldə / (BrE)
to join or repair (metal parts) by melting and applying a soft, low-melting-point alloy (soft solder) to the joint.
join metals with soft solder

Examples

You can soft-solder the wires together for a secure connection.
The plumber soft-soldered the copper pipe fittings to prevent leaks.
He spent the afternoon soft-soldering broken metal parts in his workshop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solder - to join or repair with any type of solder (including hard solder)
  • Antonym: weld - to join pieces of metal by heating and fusing them together, usually using higher heat and no filler metal

Etymology

From 'soft' + 'solder', referring to the use of soft, low-melting-point alloy in soldering.
