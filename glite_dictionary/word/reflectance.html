reflectance

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈflɛktəns / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛktəns / (BrE)
the proportion of incident light or other radiation that is reflected by a surface, typically expressed as a percentage or ratio
proportion of reflected radiant energy

Examples

The reflectance of the snow made the landscape appear extremely bright.
Researchers measured the reflectance of different materials to determine which was most suitable for solar panels.
High reflectance surfaces are used to improve lighting efficiency in buildings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: albedo - the proportion of light reflected by a surface, especially a planet or moon

Etymology

From 'reflect' + '-ance', formed in analogy to 'resistance' and other scientific terms.
