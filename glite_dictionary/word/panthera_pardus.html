Panthera pardus

1. NOUN

/ pænˈθɪrə ˈpɑrdəs / (AmE), / pænˈθɪərə ˈpɑːdəs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the leopard, a large wild cat of Africa and Asia with a tawny coat and black spots.
leopard (large wild cat, scientific name)

Examples

The Panthera pardus population is declining due to habitat loss.
Researchers observed the hunting behavior of Panthera pardus in the wild.
Conservation efforts are vital for the survival of Panthera pardus in Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leopard - large wild cat with a spotted coat, native to Africa and Asia

Etymology

From Latin; 'Panthera' meaning 'panther' and 'pardus' meaning 'leopard'.
