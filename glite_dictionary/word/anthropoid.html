anthropoid

1. NOUN

/ ˈænθrəˌpɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈanθrəˌpɔɪd / (BrE)
a primate belonging to the suborder Anthropoidea, including monkeys, apes, and humans
primate of suborder Anthropoidea

Examples

The museum exhibit featured a skeleton of an anthropoid that lived millions of years ago.
Some scientists believe that early anthropoids first appeared in Africa.
Gibbons are classified as small anthropoids within the family of apes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: primate - an order of mammals including lemurs, monkeys, apes, and humans
  • Synonym: ape - a tailless anthropoid primate, such as a gorilla or chimpanzee

Etymology

From Greek anthrōpoeidēs, meaning 'man-like' (anthrōpos 'human' + -eidēs 'form').

2. ADJ

/ ˈænθrəˌpɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈænθrəˌpɔɪd / (BrE)
Resembling or characteristic of an ape.
resembling an ape

Examples

The fossil showed several anthropoid features, suggesting a close relation to modern apes.
His anthropoid movements and facial expressions made the performance strikingly realistic.
Scientists discovered an anthropoid skull in the ancient cave.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apelike - resembling or characteristic of an ape
  • Antonym: humanlike - resembling or characteristic of a human being

Etymology

From Greek anthrōpoeidēs, from anthrōpos (human) + -eidēs (form, shape).
