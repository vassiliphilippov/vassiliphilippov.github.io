incrementation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪŋkrɪmɛnˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪŋkrɪmɛnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the operation of increasing a numerical value or variable by one
operation of increasing by one

Examples

Each time the loop repeats, incrementation of the counter variable occurs automatically.
Careful incrementation is necessary to avoid off-by-one errors in algorithms.
The program uses incrementation to update the score after every correct answer.

Synset Examples

The second parameter provides an encoding of either one of the incrementation or decrementation operations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decrementation - the operation of decreasing a numerical value or variable by one
  • Synonym: increment - an increase or the amount added

Etymology

Formed from 'increment' (from Latin incrementum, 'growth','increase') + '-ation'.
