to liquidate

1. VERB

/ ˈlɪkwəˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪdeɪt / (BrE)
to get rid of someone, especially a threat, by killing them
kill to eliminate a threat

Examples

The mafia decided to liquidate the witness before the trial could begin.
The government secretly liquidated several opposition leaders.
He was assigned to liquidate a threatening enemy operative.

Synset Examples

The mafia liquidated the informer
the double agent was neutralized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to eliminate - remove or get rid of, especially by killing
  • Antonym: spare - to refrain from killing or injuring
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01329983-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈlɪkwɪˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪdeɪt / (BrE)
to convert assets into cash, especially to pay off debts
convert assets into cash

Examples

The company decided to liquidate several assets to pay off its debts.
She had to liquidate her business after facing financial difficulties.
Investors often liquidate their holdings during periods of economic uncertainty.

Synset Examples

I had to liquidate my holdings to pay off my ex-husband

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sell off - to sell assets, especially in large amounts or at a loss, often to raise cash quickly

Etymology

From Latin 'liquidare' (to make liquid), from 'liquidus' (liquid).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02261397-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈlɪkwɪˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪˌdeɪt / (BrE)
to settle the affairs of a business or individual by identifying debts and using available assets to pay them off, especially during bankruptcy or dissolution
settle debts by selling assets

Examples

The company had to liquidate its assets to pay off creditors.
After years of losses, the board decided to liquidate the business.
The court appointed a trustee to liquidate all remaining resources.

Synset Examples

liquidate a company

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dissolve - officially end or close down a business entity
  • Synonym: bankrupt - declare unable to pay debts

Etymology

From Latin liquidatus, past participle of liquidare (to melt, make liquid or clear), from liquidus (liquid).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02259303-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈlɪkwəˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪdeɪt / (BrE)
to settle (a debt or financial obligation) by paying it off completely
pay off a debt or obligation

Examples

The company had to liquidate its debts before it could be sold to new owners.
She managed to liquidate all her outstanding loans in less than a year.
After the court order, the firm was forced to liquidate its liabilities quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to repay - to pay back money that is owed
  • Antonym: to owe - to be under obligation to pay money back

Etymology

From Latin 'liquidare' (to make liquid, clarify), from 'liquidus' (liquid).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02357015-v
Synset POS: v
