sagely

1. ADV

/ ˈseɪdʒli / (AmE), / ˈseɪdʒli / (BrE)
In a wise and thoughtful manner.
in a wise manner

Examples

He nodded sagely as if he understood every word.
She smiled sagely, aware of the challenges ahead.
The professor spoke sagely about the importance of patience.

Synset Examples

she acted wisely when she invited her parents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wisely - in a way that shows experience, knowledge, and good judgement

Etymology

From sage (wise person), from Old French *sage* (wise), from Latin *sapere* (to be wise), with the adverbial suffix -ly.
