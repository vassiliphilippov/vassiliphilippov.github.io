childishly

1. ADV

/ ˈtʃaɪldɪʃli / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪldɪʃli / (BrE)
In a manner characteristic of or resembling a child, especially in an immature or naive way.
in an immature, childlike manner

Examples

He responded childishly to the criticism, refusing to listen to any advice.
She giggled childishly at the silly joke.
Arguing childishly over toys is common among young siblings.

Synset Examples

he acted very childishly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: childlike - having qualities like those of a child, especially innocence and trust
  • Antonym: maturely - in a mature, adult manner

Etymology

From 'childish' + '-ly', where 'childish' is from 'child' plus the suffix '-ish'.
