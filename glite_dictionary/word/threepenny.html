threepenny

1. ADJ

/ ˈθriːˌpɛni / (AmE), / ˈθriːpəni / (BrE)
Of little value; cheap or trivial.
of little value or trivial importance
british

Examples

He dismissed their threepenny complaints without a second thought.
The play felt like a threepenny imitation of classic works.
She wore a threepenny trinket she had found at the market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trivial - of little value or importance
  • Degree Higher: worthless - having no value or use
  • Antonym: valuable - worth a lot of money or importance

Etymology

Named after the 'threepenny bit', a British coin of small value.

2. ADJ

/ ˈθrɛpəni, ˈθriːˌpɛni / (AmE), / ˈθrɛpəni, ˈθriːpni / (BrE)
(of a nail) having a size designated as threepenny, typically 1 1/4 inches long.
having a size of threepenny (nail)

Examples

The carpenter asked for a box of threepenny nails to finish the trim.
She used a threepenny nail to hang the small frame on the wall.
For this step, only a single threepenny nail is needed per bracket.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sixpenny - a nail size larger than threepenny nails (about 2 inches)
  • Degree Lower: twopenny - a nail size smaller than threepenny nails (about 1 inch)

Etymology

From 'three penny', referring to the price of 100 nails in old British currency.
