revocation

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛvəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛvəˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act or state of officially cancelling or annulling something, such as a law, agreement, or privilege.
official cancellation or annulment
legal

Examples

The revocation of his driver's license was due to repeated traffic violations.
Following the revocation of the contract, the parties could no longer do business together.
The government announced the revocation of the controversial policy after public outcry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cancellation - the act of making something no longer valid
  • Synonym: annulment - the act of making something legally invalid
  • Antonym: confirmation - the act of making something definite or valid

Etymology

From Latin revocatio, revocationis (calling back), from revocare (to call back).

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛvəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛvəˈkeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the official act of cancelling or annulling something that was previously granted or decided, especially by a person or body with the authority to do so
the act of officially canceling something

Examples

The company received notice of the revocation of its business license.
His driver's license was subject to immediate revocation after the court ruling.
The sudden revocation of the contract surprised all parties involved.

Synset Examples

the revocation of a law

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cancellation - the act of deciding that an event or agreement will not take place
  • Synonym: annulment - the act of declaring something invalid, especially a law or agreement
  • Antonym: enactment - the act of making something into law

Etymology

From Latin revocatio(n-), from revocare 'to call back, revoke'.
