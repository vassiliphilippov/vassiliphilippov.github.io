mizzen

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪz.ən / (AmE), / ˈmɪz.ən / (BrE)
A fore-and-aft sail set on the mizzenmast of a ship.
sail on a ship's mizzenmast

Examples

The crew quickly raised the mizzen to catch the evening breeze.
A tear in the mizzen forced the captain to alter course.
He expertly trimmed the mizzen to improve the yacht's balance.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: mizzenmast - the third mast from the bow in a ship with three or more masts

Etymology

From Middle English mesan, from Old French misaine (foremast, sail), from Italian mezzana (middle).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪzən / (AmE), / ˈmɪzən / (BrE)
the aft (rearmost) or third mast from the bow in a ship with three or more masts, or the smaller after mast on a yawl or ketch.
rear or third mast of a ship

Examples

The old sailing ship had a mizzen that was shorter than the mainmast.
He adjusted the sails on the mizzen to catch more wind from astern.
The ketch's mizzen mast stood just ahead of the rudder post.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: mainmast - the principal mast of a sailing vessel
  • Degree Lower: foremast - the mast nearest the bow of a ship

Etymology

From Middle English mesan, from Old French misaine, from Italian mezzana (mainly sail), ultimately from Latin medianus (of the middle).
