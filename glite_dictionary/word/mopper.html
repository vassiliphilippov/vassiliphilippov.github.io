mopper

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑpər / (AmE), / ˈmɒpə / (BrE)
a worker whose job is to clean floors or surfaces using a mop
person who cleans with a mop

Examples

The mopper starts cleaning the hallway floors every morning at 6 a.m.
After the spill in aisle six, a mopper quickly arrived to clean it up.
She worked as a mopper at the school during her summer break.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: janitor - person responsible for cleaning and maintenance in a building
  • Synonym: cleaner - person who cleans buildings or surfaces as an occupation

Etymology

From mop + -er (agent noun suffix).
