bridal

1. ADJ

/ ˈbraɪdəl / (AmE), / ˈbraɪdəl / (BrE)
relating to a bride or a wedding; pertaining to marriage ceremonies or festivities
relating to a wedding or bride

Examples

The bridal party arrived at the church early to take photographs before the ceremony.
She chose a vintage lace gown for her bridal outfit.
The florist created a beautiful bouquet for the bridal table at the reception.

Synset Examples

bridal procession
nuptial day
spousal rites

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuptial - relating to marriage or weddings

Etymology

From Old English 'bryd-ealu', meaning 'wedding feast', from 'bryd' (bride) and 'ealu' (ale).

2. ADJ

/ ˈbraɪ.dl̩ / (AmE), / ˈbraɪ.dl̩ / (BrE)
relating to a bride or a wedding ceremony
of or relating to a bride or wedding

Examples

She wore a stunning bridal gown that captivated everyone at the ceremony.
The hotel offers special bridal suites for newlyweds on their wedding night.
They attended a traditional bridal shower to celebrate the upcoming marriage.

Synset Examples

bridal gown

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuptial - relating to marriage or weddings
  • Antonym: groom - relating to the groom (man getting married)

Etymology

From Old English brydealo (wedding feast) from bryd (bride) + ealo (ale).

3. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪ.dəl / (AmE), / ˈbraɪ.dəl / (BrE)
(Archaic) A wedding or a wedding feast.
(archaic) wedding or wedding feast
archaic

Examples

In those days, the entire village would gather for the bridal and celebrate for days.
A grand bridal was held at the manor, complete with music and feasting.
The tale describes the joy and splendor of the royal bridal attended by lords and ladies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wedding - a marriage ceremony, especially considered as a social event
  • Synonym: nuptials - the ceremony or occasion of a marriage

Etymology

From Old English 'bryd-ealo' (‘bride ale’), referring to a wedding feast.
