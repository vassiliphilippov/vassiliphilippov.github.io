brachiation

1. NOUN

/ ˌbreɪkiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌbrækiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
a form of locomotion in which an animal swings from branch to branch using its arms
arm-swinging movement between branches

Examples

Gibbons are known for their remarkable brachiation through the forest canopy.
The study observed how different limb proportions affect brachiation in primates.
Brachiation allows animals to travel quickly and efficiently among the trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swinging - moving back and forth or from side to side while suspended or supported

Etymology

From Latin 'bracchium' (arm).
