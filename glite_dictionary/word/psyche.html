Psyche

1. PROPN

/ ˈsaɪki / (AmE), / ˈsaɪki / (BrE)
A beautiful princess in Greek mythology, loved by Cupid, who became the personification of the soul.
mythological princess and personification of the soul

Examples

In the myth, Psyche must never look at Cupid, her mysterious lover.
The story of Psyche and Cupid explores themes of love and trust.
Artists through the ages have depicted Psyche as the embodiment of the soul.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soul - spiritual or immaterial part of a person
  • Synonym: Cupid - Roman god of love, Psyche's lover

Etymology

From Greek Psukhē, meaning 'soul' or 'spirit'.
