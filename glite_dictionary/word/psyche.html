psyche

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪki / (AmE), / ˈsaɪki / (BrE)
the human soul, mind, or spirit, regarded as the center of thought, feeling, and consciousness
the human mind or soul

Examples

The trauma left a deep scar on her psyche that took years to heal.
Exploring the human psyche has long fascinated writers and philosophers alike.
Stress can have profound effects on one's psyche as well as the body.

Synset Examples

his mind wandered
I couldn't get his words out of my head

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mind - the element of a person that enables them to think and feel

Etymology

From Greek psukhē (soul, mind, spirit).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪki / (AmE), / ˈsʌɪki / (BrE)
the human mind, soul, or spirit, especially as the center of thought, feeling, and motivation
the human mind or soul

Examples

Art can deeply affect the psyche of its audience.
Years of war left scars on the national psyche.
Dreams reveal the hidden landscapes of the psyche.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soul - the spiritual or immaterial part of a human being, regarded as immortal
  • Synonym: mind - the element of a person that enables them to think, feel, or reason

Etymology

From Greek psukhē (soul, mind, spirit).
