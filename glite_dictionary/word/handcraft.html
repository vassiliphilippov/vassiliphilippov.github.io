handcraft

1. VERB

/ ˈhænd.kræft / (AmE), / ˈhænd.krɑːft / (BrE)
to make something by hand, especially with skill and care
make by hand with skill

Examples

She handcrafts beautiful pottery in her home studio.
These chairs were handcrafted using traditional woodworking techniques.
He decided to handcraft a wooden toy for his niece's birthday.

Synset Examples

We handcraft all our paper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handmake - to make by hand
  • Antonym: to mass-produce - to produce in large quantities by machinery

Etymology

From 'hand' + 'craft', modeled after the noun 'handicraft'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01662901-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈhændˌkræft / (AmE), / ˈhændˌkrɑːft / (BrE)
an object made by hand, typically with skill, artistry, or traditional techniques rather than by machine
an object made by hand

Examples

She collects handcrafts from different cultures around the world.
The exhibition features handcrafts made by local artisans.
Buying a handcraft supports traditional skills passed down through generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handicraft - an activity involving the making of decorative or practical objects by hand
  • Antonym: mass production - the manufacture of large quantities of standardized products, often using machinery

Etymology

From 'hand' + 'craft', reflecting the practice of making things by hand.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03490738-n
Synset POS: n
