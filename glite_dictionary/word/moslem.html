Moslem

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɑzləm / (AmE), / ˈmɒzləm / (BrE)
of or relating to Muslims or Islam; supporting or characteristic of Islamism. Now regarded as old-fashioned; see Muslim.
relating to Muslims or Islam (old-fashioned)
archaic

Examples

The museum features an impressive collection of Moslem art and artifacts from the Middle East.
He wrote extensively on Moslem history and philosophy in his academic career.
Traditional Moslem architecture can be seen in many parts of North Africa.

Synset Examples

Islamic art

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Muslim - of or relating to Muslims or Islam

Etymology

From Arabic muslim (one who submits [to God]), via Turkish and older English spellings.

WordNet ID: oewn-02934823-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːzləm / (AmE), / ˈmɒzləm / (BrE)
a believer in or follower of Islam; an adherent of the religion of Islam
a follower of Islam
archaic

Examples

Many Moslem communities celebrate Ramadan with great devotion and joy.
The book provides insight into the lives of early Moslem scholars.
Although the term is now considered dated, you may still encounter Moslem in older literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Muslim - a follower of Islam; the standard spelling in modern English

Etymology

Variant of 'Muslim', from Arabic 'muslim' (one who submits to God).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09701687-n
Synset POS: n
