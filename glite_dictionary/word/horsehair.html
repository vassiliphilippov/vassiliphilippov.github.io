horsehair

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrsˌhɛr / (AmE), / ˈhɔːsˌheə / (BrE)
the hair taken from the mane or tail of a horse, often used for upholstery, brushes, or stringed instrument bows
hair from a horse's mane or tail

Examples

The violin bow was restrung with horsehair for a better sound quality.
Upholsterers once commonly used horsehair as a stuffing material for furniture.
He bought a vintage brush made from genuine horsehair at the market.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrsˌhɛr / (AmE), / ˈhɔːshɛə / (BrE)
A stiff fabric made from fibers from the mane or tail of horses, used especially in upholstery and clothing.
fabric from horse mane or tail

Examples

The antique chair was upholstered with horsehair, which gave it a distinctive texture.
During the 19th century, horsehair was commonly used in the making of mattresses and sofas.
She appreciated the durability of horsehair fabric in traditional furniture.
