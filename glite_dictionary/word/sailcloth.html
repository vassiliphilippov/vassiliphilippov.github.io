sailcloth

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlˌklɔθ / (AmE), / ˈseɪlˌklɒθ / (BrE)
a strong, durable fabric, such as cotton canvas, used for making sails and tents
strong fabric used for sails or tents

Examples

The boat's new sailcloth was both sturdy and waterproof.
Tents made from sailcloth are popular among campers for their durability.
Years of exposure to the sun can weaken even the toughest sailcloth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canvas - a heavy-duty, plain-woven fabric used for sails and other purposes

Etymology

From 'sail' + 'cloth', referring to cloth used to make sails.
