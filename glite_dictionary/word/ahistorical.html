ahistorical

1. ADJ

/ ˌeɪ.hɪˈstɔːr.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌeɪ.hɪˈstɒr.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
not concerned with or related to history, historical development, or tradition.
not concerned with history or tradition

Examples

The novel takes an ahistorical approach, ignoring the actual events of the period.
Many critics argue that judging art by today’s standards is ahistorical.
Her argument was criticized for being ahistorical and lacking context.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: historical - relating to history or past events

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'a-' meaning 'not' + 'historical'.
