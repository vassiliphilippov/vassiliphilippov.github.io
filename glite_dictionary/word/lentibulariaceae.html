Lentibulariaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛntɪˌbjuːləriˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌlɛntɪˌbjuːləriˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of carnivorous aquatic or bog plants that includes the genera Utricularia, Pinguicula, and Genlisea.
family of carnivorous aquatic or bog plants

Examples

The Lentibulariaceae family contains several species with fascinating adaptations for trapping and digesting small aquatic organisms.
Botanists conducted a study on the distribution of Lentibulariaceae in the wetlands of South America.
The delicate flowers of Lentibulariaceae are often mistaken for ordinary wildflowers despite their carnivorous nature.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on the genus Lentibularia.
