carper

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːrpər / (AmE), / ˈkɑːpə / (BrE)
A person who habitually finds fault or criticizes in a petty or trivial way.
someone who constantly criticizes in a petty way

Examples

She grew tired of listening to the office carper nitpick every minor detail.
Instead of offering solutions, he became known as a carper at company meetings.
No one appreciated the constant interruptions from the carper during the presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: critic - a person who expresses an unfavorable opinion; general term for someone who criticizes
  • Synonym: complainer - a person who frequently complains
  • Antonym: praiser - a person who expresses approval or admiration; opposite of carper

Etymology

From the verb 'carp', meaning to complain or find fault, derived from Old Norse 'karpa' (to boast, brag, wrangle).
