botryoidal

1. ADJ

/ bɒˈtrɔɪ.ə.dəl / (AmE), / bɒˈtrɔɪ.ədəl / (BrE)
(of a mineral or surface) having a rounded form that resembles a cluster of grapes.
grape-like in form (esp. of minerals)

Examples

The mineral hematite often forms botryoidal masses with a smooth, rounded surface.
Malachite samples displayed a striking botryoidal texture that fascinated the geologists.
Collectors prized the unusual botryoidal shapes found in the cave's deposits.

Etymology

From Greek 'botrys' (bunch of grapes) + '-oidal' (like, resembling).
