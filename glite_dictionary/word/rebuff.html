rebuff

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈbʌf / (AmE), / rɪˈbʌf / (BrE)
an abrupt or ungracious refusal or rejection of an offer, request, or approach
an abrupt rejection or refusal

Examples

Despite his polite offer to help, she gave him a sharp rebuff.
The company's proposal received a swift rebuff from the board of directors.
After facing a rebuff at the door, he decided not to ask again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rejection - the dismissing or refusing of a proposal, idea, etc.
  • Antonym: acceptance - the action of consenting to receive or undertake something offered

Etymology

From Italian *ribuffo* (snub, reproach), from *ribuffare* (to scold, reproach).

2. VERB

/ rɪˈbʌf / (AmE), / rɪˈbʌf / (BrE)
to reject someone or something in an abrupt or ungracious manner
reject abruptly or ungraciously

Examples

She tried to help, but he rebuffed her offers politely.
The new proposal was rebuffed by the board without discussion.
He rebuffs any criticism, insisting his decision was correct.

Synset Examples

She snubbed his proposal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reject - to refuse to accept, believe in, or agree with something
  • Antonym: to accept - to consent to receive something offered

Etymology

From Italian *ribuffare* (to scold, snub), from ri- (again) + buffare (to puff).

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈbʌf / (AmE), / rɪˈbʌf / (BrE)
an abrupt or ungracious rejection, refusal, or setback, especially as a show of disapproval or anger
an abrupt rejection or refusal

Examples

He regarded her absence as a personal rebuff and left the party early.
The proposal received a sharp rebuff from the committee.
Despite the rebuff, she remained determined to pursue her project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snub - a sharp or abrupt rejection or dismissal
  • Antonym: welcome - a friendly or positive reception

Etymology

From Italian 'ribuffare' (to scold or snub), from 'ri-' (again) + 'buffare' (to puff).
