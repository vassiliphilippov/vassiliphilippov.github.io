to festinate

1. VERB

/ ˈfɛstəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɛstɪneɪt / (BrE)
to act or move quickly; to hurry
to hurry; act quickly

Examples

She had to festinate to catch the last train home.
The doctor advised the patient not to festinate despite his anxiety.
When the weather turned bad, the hikers began to festinate toward the shelter.

Synset Examples

We have to rush!
hurry — it's late!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hurry - to move or act with speed
  • Antonym: to dawdle - to move slowly or waste time

Etymology

From Latin festinatus, past participle of festinare (to hasten).
