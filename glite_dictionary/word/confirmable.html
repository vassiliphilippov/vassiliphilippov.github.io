confirmable

1. ADJ

/ kənˈfɝːməbl̩ / (AmE), / kənˈfɜːməbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be proven true or false through experiment or observation; capable of being verified.
able to be verified or tested

Examples

The theory is confirmable through further scientific testing and observation.
We need confirmable data before drawing any conclusions about the results.
Her hypothesis became more confirmable as new evidence emerged from recent experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verifiable - able to be checked or confirmed as true or accurate
  • Synonym: falsifiable - able to be proven false by observation or experiment

Etymology

From Latin 'confirmare' (to make firm, strengthen) + '-able' (capable of).
