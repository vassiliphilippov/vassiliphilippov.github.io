brandy

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrændi / (AmE), / ˈbrændi / (BrE)
a strong alcoholic drink distilled from wine or fermented fruit juice, usually aged in wood
a spirit distilled from wine or fruit juice

Examples

She poured a small glass of brandy to warm herself by the fire.
The dessert was flambéed with brandy for extra flavor.
A bottle of fine brandy makes a perfect gift for special occasions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cognac - a type of brandy from the Cognac region of France
  • Antonym: whiskey - an alcoholic spirit distilled from grain mash

Etymology

From Dutch 'brandewijn' meaning 'burnt wine'; related to the distillation process.
