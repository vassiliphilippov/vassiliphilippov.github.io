tractableness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtræk.tə.bəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtræk.tə.bl̩.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being easily managed or persuaded; readiness to accept guidance or control
quality of being easily managed or persuaded

Examples

Her tractableness made her a favorite among her supervisors, who appreciated her willingness to cooperate.
The tractableness of the students allowed the teacher to implement new classroom routines smoothly.
While his tractableness helped him make friends, it sometimes led others to take advantage of his accommodating nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tractability - the quality of being easy to control or influence
  • Antonym: intractability - the quality of being difficult to manage or persuade

Etymology

From 'tractable' (easily managed) + '-ness', forming a noun.
