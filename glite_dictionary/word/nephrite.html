nephrite

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛf.raɪt / (AmE), / ˈnɛf.raɪt / (BrE)
A hard, green or whitish mineral consisting of a calcium magnesium silicate, one of the two forms of jade, less valuable than jadeite.
a type of jade mineral

Examples

The museum displayed a stunning necklace made of nephrite.
Ancient Chinese artisans skillfully carved nephrite into ceremonial objects.
Although similar in appearance, nephrite is generally considered less valuable than jadeite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jadeite - the other principal mineral from which jade is derived; typically more valuable

Etymology

From Greek nephros (kidney), relating to its historical use in treating kidney problems.
