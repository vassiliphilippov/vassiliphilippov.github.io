apparel

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɛɹəl / (AmE), / əˈparəl / (BrE)
clothing, especially of a particular kind or for a particular occasion, often used in a formal or commercial context.
clothing; garments

Examples

The store offers winter apparel such as coats, scarves, and gloves.
She chose elegant evening apparel for the gala event.
Many designers showcased their new lines of apparel at the fashion show.

Synset Examples

she was refined in her choice of apparel
he always bought his clothes at the same store
fastidious about his dress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clothing - items worn to cover the body
  • Synonym: attire - formal or elaborate clothes

Etymology

From Old French 'appareil' (preparation, equipage), from Latin 'apparare' (to prepare, make ready).

2. VERB

/ əˈpɛr.əl / (AmE), / əˈpar.əl / (BrE)
to dress or clothe someone; to provide with clothes
to dress or clothe

Examples

The royal family was appareled in exquisite gowns for the ceremony.
She appareled her children warmly before sending them outside to play.
The actors were appareled according to the styles of the 18th century.

Synset Examples

Parents must feed and dress their child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dress - to put clothes on (someone)

Etymology

From Old French *appareiller* (to fit, prepare), from Vulgar Latin *appariculare*; related to 'apparatus'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00047662-v
Synset POS: v
