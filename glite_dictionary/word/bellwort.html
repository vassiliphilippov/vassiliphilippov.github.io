bellwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛlˌwɜrt / (AmE), / ˈbɛlˌwɜːt / (BrE)
a North American plant of the genus Uvularia, having slender stems and yellowish, drooping, bell-shaped flowers
a North American plant with yellow, bell-shaped flowers

Examples

The woodland trail was lined with delicate bellwort blooming in the shade.
She planted bellwort in her garden to attract native pollinators.
Hikers admired the clusters of bellwort dangling gently from their slender stems.

Etymology

From 'bell' (referring to flower shape) + 'wort' (from Old English 'wyrt', meaning plant or herb).
