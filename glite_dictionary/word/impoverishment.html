impoverishment

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɑːvərɪʃmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɒvərɪʃmənt / (BrE)
The state of being extremely poor or lacking basic material possessions.
the state of being very poor

Examples

Decades of war led to the impoverishment of entire regions.
Rapid industrialization sometimes causes environmental impoverishment as resources are depleted.
The impoverishment of the family made it difficult to pay for basic necessities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poverty - the state of being extremely poor
  • Antonym: wealth - an abundance of valuable possessions or money

Etymology

From French 'appauvrissement' and earlier 'impoverish', from Old French 'empovrir', from Late Latin 'impoverire'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɑvərɪʃmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɒvərɪʃmənt / (BrE)
the process of making a person or group poor, or the state of being made poor
the act of making poor

Examples

Decades of war have led to the impoverishment of many communities.
The new tax policy may result in the impoverishment of vulnerable families.
Rapid deforestation can cause the impoverishment of local populations by destroying their livelihoods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poverty - the state of being extremely poor
  • Antonym: enrichment - the act of making someone richer

Etymology

From impoverish + -ment; impoverish from Old French *empoverir*, from Latin *pauper* (poor).
