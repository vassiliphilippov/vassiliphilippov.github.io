kip

1. NOUN

/ kɪp / (AmE), / kɪp / (BrE)
a gymnastics movement performed by swinging the legs out and down from an inverted position to an upright position, typically by arching the back and forcefully raising the chest
a gymnastics move to swing to upright position

Examples

After months of practice, she finally mastered the kip on the uneven bars.
The gymnast used a kip to transition smoothly into her routine.
Learning the kip is a crucial milestone for many young athletes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mount - a movement to get on an apparatus in gymnastics

2. NOUN

/ kɪp / (AmE), / kɪp / (BrE)
A period of sleep, especially a short or informal nap.
a sleep or nap (informal)
informal british

Examples

He only managed to get a quick kip before his shift started.
I felt much better after a short kip on the couch.
She likes to have a kip in the afternoon to recharge.

Synset Examples

roused him from his kip

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleep - a condition of rest in which the body functions are suspended
  • Synonym: nap - a short, light sleep, especially during the day

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from Scandinavian or Dutch roots for 'hut' or 'shelter,' later extended to mean 'sleep.'

3. NOUN

/ kɪp / (AmE), / kɪp / (BrE)
the basic unit of currency in Laos, equal to 100 att.
currency unit of Laos

Examples

She exchanged her dollars for Lao kip at the border.
Prices in the market are listed in both kip and US dollars.
The hotel charged 200,000 kip per night for a double room.

Etymology

From Lao ກີບ (kîip).

4. VERB

/ kɪp / (AmE), / kɪp / (BrE)
to be asleep; to sleep, especially as a nap or in a casual place
to sleep or be asleep
informal british

Examples

He likes to kip on the sofa after lunch.
We kipped in a tent during the festival weekend.
She was kipping so soundly that she didn't hear the alarm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleep - to be in the state of rest during which the body is not active and the mind is unconscious

Etymology

Perhaps from Danish 'kippe' (cheap inn) or Dutch 'kip' (hut), originally referring to sleeping quarters.
