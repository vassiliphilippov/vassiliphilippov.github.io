annulment

1. NOUN

/ əˈnʌlmənt / (AmE), / əˈnʌlmənt / (BrE)
the official act of cancelling something so that it is treated as though it never existed; the state of being annulled
the act or state of being annulled

Examples

The annulment of the contract left both parties free from obligation.
After the annulment of their marriage, they lived separate lives.
The company sought the annulment of the agreement due to fraud.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cancellation - the action of cancelling something

Etymology

From Late Latin annullamentum, from annullare (to annul).

2. NOUN

/ əˈnʌlmənt / (AmE), / əˈnʌlmənt / (BrE)
(law) the official declaration that cancels a legal proceeding, marriage, or contract, as if it never existed.
legal cancellation or invalidation
legal

Examples

After their brief marriage, the couple filed for an annulment on the grounds of fraud.
The court's annulment of the contract rendered all previous obligations void.
An annulment differs from a divorce because it treats the marriage as if it never existed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cancellation - the act of making something no longer valid
  • Antonym: divorce - legal dissolution of a marriage by a court

Etymology

From the verb 'annul', from Late Latin 'annullare' (to make to nothing).

3. NOUN

/ əˈnʌlmənt / (AmE), / əˈnʌlmənt / (BrE)
the formal act of declaring something invalid, especially a marriage or legal agreement, so that it has no legal effect
official cancellation or invalidation
legal

Examples

The court granted an annulment of their marriage after only six months.
After the discovery of fraud, the contract was declared void by an annulment.
The annulment of the election results sparked widespread debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cancellation - the action of cancelling something that was planned or agreed
  • Synonym: invalidation - making something legally or officially invalid
  • Antonym: ratification - the act of confirming or making valid

Etymology

From Late Latin 'annullare' (to annul), from Latin 'ad' (to) + 'nullum' (nothing).
