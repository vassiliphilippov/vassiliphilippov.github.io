antiperspirant

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tiˈpɝː.spɚ.ənt / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˈpɜː.spər.ənt / (BrE)
a substance applied to the skin, especially under the arms, to reduce or prevent perspiration
substance that reduces perspiration

Examples

She applied her antiperspirant before leaving for work in the morning.
This brand of antiperspirant promises 48 hours of protection against sweat.
Doctors often recommend a clinical-strength antiperspirant for people with excessive sweating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deodorant - substance that masks or eliminates body odor

Etymology

From anti- (against) + Latin perspirare (to breathe through)
