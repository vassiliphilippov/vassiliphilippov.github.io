reversal

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈvɝːsəl / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːsəl / (BrE)
the act or process of changing to an opposite direction, position, or course
change to an opposite direction or position

Examples

The camera produces a reversal of the scene, with left and right swapped.
A sudden reversal in the wind's direction surprised the sailors.
The reversal of the magnet's polarity altered the device's function.

Synset Examples

the reversal of the image in the lens

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inversion - the act of turning something upside down or inside out

Etymology

From reverse + -al; based on French 'reversal'.
