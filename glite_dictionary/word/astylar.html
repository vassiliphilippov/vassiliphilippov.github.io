astylar

1. ADJ

/ æˈstaɪlɚ / (AmE), / æˈstaɪlə / (BrE)
(of a building or structure) lacking columns or pillars.
lacking columns or pillars (architecture)

Examples

The new museum building features an astylar façade that emphasizes clean lines and simplicity.
Unlike the grand civic structures, this chapel is distinctly astylar in its design.
Certain modernists preferred astylar buildings, rejecting classical columns in favor of minimalism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: columned - having columns or pillars

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) + 'stylos' (column).
