Judeo-Christian

1. ADJ

/ dʒuˌdeɪ.oʊˈkrɪstʃən / (AmE), / dʒuːˌdeɪ.əʊˈkrɪstʃən / (BrE)
Relating to or having historical connections with both Judaism and Christianity, especially regarding shared beliefs, values, or traditions.
relating to both Judaism and Christianity

Examples

Many Western laws are influenced by Judeo-Christian values and traditions.
The concept of charity holds a significant place in Judeo-Christian ethics.
Scholars often refer to the Judeo-Christian heritage when discussing the development of moral philosophy in the West.

Synset Examples

the Judeo-Christian tradition

Etymology

Formed from 'Judeo-' (from Latin Judaeus, 'Jewish') and 'Christian'.
