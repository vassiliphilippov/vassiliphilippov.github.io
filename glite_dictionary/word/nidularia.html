Nidularia

1. NOUN

/ nɪdjuˈlɛəriə / (AmE), / nɪdjʊˈlɑːrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of small fungi in the family Nidulariaceae, known for producing bird's nest-like fruiting bodies.
genus of bird's nest fungi

Examples

Nidularia species are commonly called bird's nest fungi due to their unique fruiting bodies.
Researchers discovered several new Nidularia specimens in the temperate forest.
The structure of Nidularia helps disperse its spores by raindrop impact.

Etymology

From Latin 'nidulus' meaning 'small nest', referring to the nest-like structure of the fungi.

2. NOUN

/ nɪdjuˈlɛəriə / (AmE), / nɪdjuˈlɛəriə / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Nidulariaceae, characterized by small, nest-like fruiting bodies.
genus of fungi (Nidulariaceae)

Examples

Species belonging to the genus Nidularia are often called bird’s nest fungi due to their unique shape.
Nidularia produces fruiting bodies that resemble tiny nests filled with eggs.
Researchers discovered a new member of the Nidularia genus in the forest understory.

Etymology

From Latin *nidulus* (little nest), referring to the nest-like appearance of the fruiting bodies.
