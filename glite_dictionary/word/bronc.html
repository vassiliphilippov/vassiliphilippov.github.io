bronc

1. NOUN

/ brɑŋk / (AmE), / brɒŋk / (BrE)
a wild or partially tamed horse, especially one used in rodeos; an unbroken or imperfectly broken mustang
wild or partially tamed rodeo horse

Examples

The cowboy struggled to stay on the bronc during the rodeo.
She watched as the bronc bucked wildly, refusing to be tamed.
Every rider had to try their luck with a different bronc in the competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bronco - a wild or part-tamed horse (esp. in North America)

Etymology

Shortened from 'bronco', from Spanish 'bronco' (rough, rude).
