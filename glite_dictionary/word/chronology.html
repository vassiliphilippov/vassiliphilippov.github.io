chronology

1. NOUN

/ krəˈnɑlədʒi / (AmE), / krəˈnɒlədʒi / (BrE)
a record of events arranged in the order in which they happened
record of events by order of occurrence

Examples

The chronology of the king's reign was carefully documented by historians.
The police created a detailed chronology of events leading up to the accident.
She presented a chronology that traced the development of the project from its inception.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timeline - a graphical representation of a period of time, typically showing events in chronological order

Etymology

From Greek *khronología* (computation of time), from *khronos* (time) + *-logia* (study of).

order of events in time

Examples

He struggled to establish an accurate chronology for the ancient ruins.
Without a clear chronology, the story becomes confusing and hard to follow.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Greek 'khronologia', from 'khronos' (time) + 'logos' (study, discourse).

3. NOUN

/ krəˈnɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / krəˈnɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the arrangement or determination of the order in which past events occurred
order of past events

Examples

Archaeologists established the chronology of the ancient city's development through careful excavation.
The book provides a detailed chronology of events leading up to the civil war.
Disputes about the chronology of the paintings have led to new research in the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timeline - a graphical representation of events in chronological order
  • Synonym: history - the study of past events, particularly in human affairs

Etymology

From Greek 'khronologia' (calculation of time) from 'khronos' (time) + '-logia' (study of).
