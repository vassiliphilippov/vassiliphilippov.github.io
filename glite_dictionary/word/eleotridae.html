Eleotridae

1. NOUN

/ ɛliˈɑtrɪdiː / (AmE), / ɛlɪˈɒtrɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small, usually freshwater fishes known as sleepers, found in tropical and subtropical regions.
family of fishes called sleepers

Examples

The Eleotridae family includes many species adapted to a wide range of freshwater environments.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Eleotridae in Papua New Guinea.
Unlike gobies, fishes from Eleotridae lack fused pelvic fins.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on the genus Eleotris + -idae (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).
