disembowelment

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.ɪmˈbaʊ.əl.mənt / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.ɪmˈbaʊ.əl.mənt / (BrE)
the act or process of removing the internal organs, especially the intestines, from a body
removal of internal organs

Examples

The film was criticized for its graphic scenes of disembowelment.
In medieval times, disembowelment was sometimes used as a form of execution.
Veterinarians must sometimes perform disembowelment to save an animal after severe injury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evisceration - the removal of internal organs, especially the intestines

Etymology

From disembowel + -ment; 'disembowel' from dis- (opposite) + embowel (to put inside the bowels).
