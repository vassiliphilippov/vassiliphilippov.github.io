Calycanthus occidentalis

1. NOUN

/ kælɪˈkænθəs ˌɒksɪˈdɛntəlɪs / (AmE), / kælɪˈkænθəs ˌɒksɪˈdɛntəlɪs / (BrE)
a straggling aromatic shrub native to the southwestern United States, with fragrant brown flowers; also known as western sweet shrub or spice bush
aromatic shrub with fragrant brown flowers

Examples

The Calycanthus occidentalis in her garden filled the air with a spicy fragrance each spring.
Botanists studied the distribution of Calycanthus occidentalis across California’s warmer regions.
We planted a Calycanthus occidentalis near the patio for its appealing scent and attractive brown blossoms.

Etymology

From Greek ‘kalyx’ (calyx) and ‘anthos’ (flower); ‘occidentalis’ is Latin for ‘western’.
