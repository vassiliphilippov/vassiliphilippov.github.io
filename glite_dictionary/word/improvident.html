improvident

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈprɑvədənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɒvɪdənt / (BrE)
Not having or showing foresight; failing to provide for future needs or risks.
lacking foresight; not providing for the future

Examples

His improvident spending habits left him with little savings for emergencies.
The improvident farmer failed to store enough grain for the winter.
It would be improvident to ignore the possibility of future setbacks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: provident - making provision for the future; prudent

Etymology

From Latin 'improvident-', from 'im-' (not) + 'provident-' (foreseeing, prudent).

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈprɑːvɪdənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɒvɪdənt / (BrE)
Not having or showing foresight; spending or acting without careful consideration for the future.
lacking foresight or planning

Examples

His improvident habits left him with no savings by the end of the year.
It was improvident to spend so much money without considering future expenses.
The company's improvident investments led to significant financial losses.

Synset Examples

ill-considered actions often result in disaster
an ill-judged attempt

Related Terms

  • Antonym: provident - having or showing foresight; prudent

Etymology

From Latin improvident-, from 'im-' (not) + 'provident-' (foreseeing, prudent).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01905809-s
Synset POS: s
