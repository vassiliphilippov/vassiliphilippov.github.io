ack-ack

1. NOUN

/ ˈækˌæk / (AmE), / ˈækˌæk / (BrE)
Anti-aircraft gunfire; artillery fire set up to attack aircraft at high altitudes.
anti-aircraft artillery

Examples

During the war, ack-ack batteries were stationed around the city to defend against enemy bombers.
The soldiers could hear the constant boom of ack-ack in the distance as planes flew overhead.
An old photograph showed rows of ack-ack guns lining the coastline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiaircraft - (adj.) designed for defense against aircraft

Etymology

From World War I British signalese, representing A.A., abbreviation of anti-aircraft.
