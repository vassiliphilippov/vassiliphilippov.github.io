apoplectic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæp.əˈplɛk.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌap.əˈplɛk.tɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of apoplexy (stroke), especially marked by sudden loss of consciousness or movement due to the rupture or blockage of a blood vessel in the brain.
relating to apoplexy or stroke
medical

Examples

The patient suffered an apoplectic episode and was rushed to the hospital.
He was diagnosed with an apoplectic stroke caused by a burst blood vessel.
The doctor noted the apoplectic symptoms immediately after the collapse.

Synset Examples

apoplectic seizure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stroke - sudden loss of brain function caused by blockage or rupture of a blood vessel

Etymology

From French *apoplectique*, from Latin *apoplecticus*, from Greek *apoplēktikos* (relating to a stroke).
