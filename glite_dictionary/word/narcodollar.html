narcodollar

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑrkoʊˌdɑlɚ / (AmE), / ˈnɑːkəʊˌdɒlə / (BrE)
Money, especially US dollars, obtained from illegal drug trafficking.
money from drug trafficking

Examples

Officials seized a large quantity of narcodollars during the raid on the cartel's compound.
International banks are under pressure to monitor transactions for evidence of narcodollar laundering.
The conference focused on the impact of narcodollars on the global financial system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dirty money - money acquired through illegal means

Etymology

Formed from "narco" (relating to drugs) and "dollar"; popularized in contexts discussing the finances of the illegal drug trade.
