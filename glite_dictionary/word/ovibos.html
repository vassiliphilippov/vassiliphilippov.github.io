Ovibos

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.vɪˌbɑs / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.vɪˌbɒs / (BrE)
A genus of large Arctic mammals, including the musk-ox, characterized by long shaggy hair and curved horns.
genus of Arctic mammals (musk-ox)

Examples

The Ovibos is well adapted to withstand extremely cold environments in the Arctic tundra.
Zoologists studied the migration patterns of Ovibos across the northern territories.
Only one living species, the musk-ox, belongs to the genus Ovibos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: musk-ox - a large shaggy Arctic mammal of the genus Ovibos

Etymology

From Latinized Greek, combining 'ovis' (sheep) and 'bos' (ox).

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.vɪ.bɑs / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.vɪ.bɒs / (BrE)
A genus of large, shaggy mammals of the family Bovidae, consisting of the musk-ox.
genus containing the musk-ox

Examples

The only living species in the genus Ovibos is the musk-ox.
Research on Ovibos has provided important insights into Arctic adaptation.
Museum displays often include fossils from the extinct relatives of Ovibos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: musk-ox - a large, shaggy arctic mammal in the genus Ovibos

Etymology

From Latin 'ovis' (sheep) and 'bos' (ox).
