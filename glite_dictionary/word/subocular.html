subocular

1. ADJ

/ sʌbˈɑkjələr / (AmE), / sʌbˈɒkjʊlə / (BrE)
situated beneath or on the lower part of the eye socket
located below the eye socket
medical

Examples

The surgeon noted a small subocular swelling near the patient's left eye.
Certain fish species have subocular bones that help protect their eyes from injury.
The paleontologist identified a subocular ridge in the fossil skull.

Synset Examples

a suborbital bone

Etymology

From Latin 'sub-' (under, beneath) + 'ocularis' (of the eye).
