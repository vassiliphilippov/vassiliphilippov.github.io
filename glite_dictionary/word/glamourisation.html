glamourisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡlæmɚəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡlæməraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of making something or someone appear more glamorous or attractive, often in a superficial way.
the act of making something more glamorous
british

Examples

The glamourisation of the fashion industry often hides harsh realities behind the scenes.
Some critics argue that films contribute to the glamourisation of criminal lifestyles.
The glamourisation of war in advertisements has sparked significant controversy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glamorization - the act of making something more glamorous (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'glamour' + '-isation', British spelling of 'glamorization'.
