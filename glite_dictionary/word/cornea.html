cornea

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔr.ni.ə / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.ni.ə / (BrE)
the transparent, dome-shaped front part of the eye that covers the iris and pupil and helps focus light
transparent front part of the eye
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully examined the cornea for any signs of damage.
Infection of the cornea can seriously affect vision if left untreated.
A healthy cornea is essential for clear eyesight.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sclera - the white outer layer of the eyeball
  • Synonym: iris - the colored part of the eye surrounding the pupil

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *cornea* (cornea tela, 'horny tissue'), feminine of Latin *corneus* ('horny').
