Chicago

1. NOUN

/ ʃɪˈkɑɡoʊ / (AmE), / ʃɪˈkɑːɡəʊ / (BrE)
A gambling card game in which chips are placed on the ace, king, queen, and jack of separate suits (from a separate deck), and players try to win chips by playing cards that match those in the layout.
a gambling card game

Examples

We decided to play a round of Chicago at the casino last night.
In Chicago, you try to play cards that match those on the table to win chips.
She taught her friends the rules of Chicago during their weekly game night.

2. PROPN

/ ʃɪˈkɑɡoʊ / (AmE), / ʃɪˈkɑːɡəʊ / (BrE)
the largest city in Illinois, located on the southwestern shore of Lake Michigan, known as a major port and cultural center in the United States
major city in Illinois, USA

Examples

I grew up in Chicago and still love to visit the city's famous museums.
The wind was so strong in Chicago that my umbrella turned inside out.
Many people travel to Chicago to try its famous deep-dish pizza.

Etymology

From the French rendering of Native American (Algonquian) word 'shikaakwa' (meaning 'wild onion' or 'wild garlic').
