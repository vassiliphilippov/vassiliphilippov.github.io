soliloquy

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɪləkwi / (AmE), / səˈlɪləkwi / (BrE)
an act of speaking one's thoughts aloud when by oneself or regardless of any hearers, especially by a character in a play
speech made to oneself, especially in drama

Examples

During the play, Hamlet delivers a famous soliloquy that explores the nature of existence.
In her soliloquy, the character reveals secrets she would never tell anyone else.
The actor's moving soliloquy captivated the entire audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monologue - a long speech by one person, often in a play or performance

Etymology

From Late Latin 'soliloquium', from Latin 'solus' (alone) + 'loqui' (to speak).

2. NOUN

/ səˈlɪləkwi / (AmE), / səˈlɪləkwi / (BrE)
a speech in a play in which a character speaks their thoughts aloud, usually while alone, revealing their inner reflections
a speech revealing a character's inner thoughts

Examples

In Shakespeare's plays, Hamlet's famous soliloquy begins with 'To be or not to be.'
The actor delivered a moving soliloquy that revealed his character's deepest fears.
Writing a soliloquy can help students better understand a character's internal struggle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monologue - a long speech by one actor in a play or movie

Etymology

From Late Latin soliloquium, from Latin solus (alone) + loqui (to speak).
