ratiocinator

1. NOUN

/ ˌrætiˈɑːsəˌneɪtər / (AmE), / ˌrætɪˈɒsɪneɪtə / (BrE)
a person who reasons logically or engages in rational thinking
someone who reasons logically

Examples

The philosopher was praised as an excellent ratiocinator who always considered every argument deeply.
In the heated debate, she remained the calm ratiocinator among a crowd of emotional speakers.
Detective stories often portray the protagonist as a brilliant ratiocinator solving intricate mysteries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: logician - a person skilled in logic
  • Synonym: reasoner - someone who reasons or bases conclusions on evidence

Etymology

From Latin ratiocinari (to reason), related to ratio (reason).
