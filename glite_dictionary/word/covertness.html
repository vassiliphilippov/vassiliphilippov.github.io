covertness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌvərtnəs / (AmE), / ˈkʌvət.nəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being secret, hidden, or not openly acknowledged
the state of being hidden or secret

Examples

The covertness of the operation made it difficult for outsiders to detect any activity.
He was admired for the covertness with which he gathered sensitive information.
Maintaining covertness is essential for undercover agents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secrecy - the state of keeping something secret
  • Antonym: openness - the quality of being open and transparent

Etymology

From 'covert' (Middle English, from Old French, from Latin 'cooperire' meaning 'to cover') + '-ness' suffix.
