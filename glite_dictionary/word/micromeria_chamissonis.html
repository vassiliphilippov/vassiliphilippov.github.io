Micromeria chamissonis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.kroʊˈmɪr.i.ə kæˌmɪsˈsoʊ.nɪs / (AmE), / ˌmɪ.krəˈmɪə.ri.ə kəˌmɪˈsəʊ.nɪs / (BrE)
a trailing perennial evergreen herb native to the northwestern United States, with small white flowers and medicinal uses
herbaceous plant from northwestern U.S.

Examples

The leaves of Micromeria chamissonis were traditionally used by local communities to make herbal teas.
Botanists found a large population of Micromeria chamissonis thriving along the rocky coastal hills.
Researchers are studying Micromeria chamissonis for its potential medicinal properties.

Etymology

Micromeria from Greek 'mikros' (small) + 'meros' (part), after Ludolf Karl Adelbert von Chamisso, botanist.
