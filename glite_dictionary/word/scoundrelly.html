scoundrelly

1. ADJ

/ ˈskaʊndrəli / (AmE), / ˈskaʊndrəli / (BrE)
Having the qualities or behavior of a scoundrel; lacking principles or scruples.
like a scoundrel; unscrupulous

Examples

The politician was criticized for his scoundrelly tactics during the campaign.
Despite his charming smile, there was a scoundrelly glint in his eye.
The novel's scoundrelly villain delighted in causing trouble for everyone around him.

Synset Examples

the rascally rabble
the tyranny of a scoundrelly aristocracy
the captain was set adrift by his roguish crew

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unscrupulous - having or showing no moral principles
  • Synonym: scoundrel - a person who behaves dishonorably or unscrupulously
  • Antonym: honorable - having or showing high moral standards

Etymology

From 'scoundrel' (rascal, villain) + '-ly' (adjective forming suffix).
