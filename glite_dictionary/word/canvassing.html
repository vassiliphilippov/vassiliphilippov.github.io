canvassing

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.vəs.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkæn.vəs.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity of trying to persuade people to vote for a particular candidate or party, typically by going from house to house or contacting people directly
solicitation of votes in a campaign

Examples

Door-to-door canvassing is a crucial part of local election campaigns.
The party stepped up its canvassing efforts in key districts before polling day.
Volunteers participated in canvassing to encourage voter turnout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electioneering - actively working for a political candidate or party during an election campaign

Etymology

From 'canvass' (to solicit votes), which comes from the obsolete sense 'to toss in a canvas sheet' (from French 'canevas', canvas).
