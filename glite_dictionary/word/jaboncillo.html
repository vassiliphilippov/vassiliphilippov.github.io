jaboncillo

1. NOUN

/ haˌbɔnˈsiːjoʊ / (AmE), / haˌbɒnˈsiːjəʊ / (BrE)
An evergreen tree of tropical America bearing pulpy fruit that contains saponin, traditionally used as soap by Native Americans.
tropical American evergreen with soap-like fruit

Examples

The pulp of the jaboncillo fruit was once used as a natural soap.
Botanists found that jaboncillo thrives in humid, tropical environments.
Local people use jaboncillo for its cleansing properties and medicinal benefits.

Etymology

From Spanish 'jaboncillo', diminutive of 'jabón' (soap).
