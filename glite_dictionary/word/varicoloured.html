varicoloured

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɛr.iˌkʌl.ɚd / (AmE), / ˈvær.ɪˌkʌl.əd / (BrE)
Having areas or patches of different colors, usually bright or striking.
having different colored patches
british

Examples

The artist's varicoloured canvas captured the vibrant essence of the festival.
We admired the varicoloured feathers of the tropical bird as it perched in the sunlight.
He wore a varicoloured scarf that brightened up his dark winter coat.

Synset Examples

a jester dressed in motley
the painted desert
a particolored dress
a piebald horse
pied daisies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multicoloured - having many colors
  • Antonym: monochrome - having one color

Etymology

From Latin varius (varied) + coloured.

2. ADJ

/ ˈvɛrɪˌkʌlɚd / (AmE), / ˈværɪˌkʌləd / (BrE)
Having a variety of different colors; multicolored.
having many colors
british

Examples

The artist created a varicoloured mural that brightened the entire street.
She wore a varicoloured scarf that matched every outfit.
Autumn leaves are often varicoloured, displaying reds, yellows, and oranges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multicolored - having many colors
  • Antonym: monochrome - having only one color; in shades of a single color

Etymology

Formed from Latin 'varius' (varied) + English 'coloured'.
