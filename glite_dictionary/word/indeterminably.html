indeterminably

1. ADV

/ ɪn.dɪˈtɝː.mɪ.nə.bli / (AmE), / ɪn.dɪˈtɜː.mɪ.nə.bli / (BrE)
In a way that cannot be determined or precisely defined.
in a manner that cannot be determined

Examples

The outcome of the trial remained indeterminably uncertain for weeks.
Her mood was indeterminably melancholic, neither cheerful nor truly sad.
The meaning behind his words was indeterminably vague, leaving us puzzled.

Synset Examples

their relationship was of an indeterminably vague nature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indeterminable - unable to be determined
  • Antonym: definitely - in a definite manner

Etymology

From 'indeterminable' + '-ly'. 'Indeterminable' from Latin indeterminabilis, meaning 'not able to be determined'.
