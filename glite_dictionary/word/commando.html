commando

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmændoʊ / (AmE), / kəˈmændəʊ / (BrE)
a member of a military unit specially trained to carry out rapid, surprise attacks, often operating behind enemy lines
soldier trained for quick, surprise attacks

Examples

The commando slipped silently into the enemy camp under cover of darkness.
During the raid, a commando team destroyed the strategic bridge.
He trained for years before being accepted as a commando.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: special forces - military units trained for specialized missions

Etymology

From Afrikaans ‘kommando’, from Portuguese ‘comando’, meaning ‘command’ or task force.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈmændoʊ / (AmE), / kəˈmɑːndəʊ / (BrE)
A soldier or military unit specially trained to carry out raids into enemy territory, often using amphibious or irregular methods.
special forces soldier or unit

Examples

After hours of preparation, the commando team launched a surprise assault on the coastal stronghold.
The army sent a group of elite commandos behind enemy lines to sabotage key installations.
He trained for months to qualify as a commando in the special operations force.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: special forces - military units trained for unconventional missions

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'kommando', originally from Portuguese 'comando' (command).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
