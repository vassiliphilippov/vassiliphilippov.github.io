incineration

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌsɪnəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌsɪnəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of burning something completely, especially until only ashes remain
the act of burning something to ashes

Examples

Modern waste management often relies on incineration to reduce the volume of garbage.
The report highlighted the environmental impacts of medical waste incineration.
After the incineration of the documents, nothing but ash remained.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combustion - the process of burning
  • Synonym: cremation - the burning of a body after death
  • Antonym: to extinguish - to put out a fire or end the burning process

Etymology

From Medieval Latin incineratio (burning to ashes), from Latin cinis, cineris (ashes).
