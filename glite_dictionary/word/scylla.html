Scylla

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɪl.ə / (AmE), / ˈsɪl.ə / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a sea nymph transformed into a sea monster who dwelled on one side of a narrow strait and devoured sailors attempting to escape the whirlpool Charybdis on the other side.
mythical sea monster in Greek mythology

Examples

According to legend, Odysseus had to steer his ship between Scylla and Charybdis.
Many ancient sailors dreaded the waters haunted by Scylla for fear of being devoured.
The expression 'between Scylla and Charybdis' refers to choosing between two dangers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Charybdis - whirlpool monster facing Scylla in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Σκύλλα (Skúlla), name of a mythological monster.
