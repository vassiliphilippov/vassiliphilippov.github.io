Aster tripolium

1. NOUN

/ ˈæstɚ traɪˈpoʊliəm / (AmE), / ˈæstə traɪˈpɒlɪəm / (BrE)
a species of aster native to Europe, commonly found in salt marshes and coastal areas
a European salt-marsh aster species

Examples

The Aster tripolium thrives in salty marshes along the European coasts.
Ecologists noted that Aster tripolium is vital for supporting local insect populations.
She collected samples of Aster tripolium to study salt tolerance in flowering plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea aster - another common name for Aster tripolium

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Aster' (star) and species 'tripolium' (from Greek, referring to three cities, but origin unclear).
