polyglot

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɑːliˌɡlɑt / (AmE), / ˈpɒliˌɡlɒt / (BrE)
knowing or using several languages; composed of or written in several languages
knowing or using several languages

Examples

As a polyglot scholar, she can read research articles in six different languages.
The city is known for its polyglot population, representing dozens of cultural backgrounds.
The polyglot Bible includes texts in Hebrew, Greek, and Latin side by side.

Synset Examples

a polyglot traveler
a polyglot Bible contains versions in different languages

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multilingual - speaking or using several languages
  • Antonym: monolingual - knowing or using only one language
  • Degree Lower: bilingual - knowing or using two languages
  • Degree Lower: trilingual - knowing or using three languages

Etymology

From Greek poluglōttos, from polu- (many) + glōtta (tongue, language).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːliˌɡlɑt / (AmE), / ˈpɒliˌɡlɒt / (BrE)
a person who knows and is able to use several languages
person who speaks several languages

Examples

Maria is a true polyglot, fluent in Spanish, English, French, and Japanese.
Being a polyglot helps him connect with people all over the world.
The conference featured a polyglot who could interpret for nearly everyone present.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multilingual - person speaking several languages
  • Antonym: monolingual - person speaking only one language
  • Degree Lower: bilingual - person speaking two languages
  • Degree Lower: trilingual - person speaking three languages

Etymology

From Greek 'polyglōttos', from 'poly-' (many) + 'glōtta' (tongue, language).

Synset POS: n
