helmswoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛlmzˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈhɛlmzˌwʊmən / (BrE)
A woman who steers or is in charge of steering a ship or boat.
a woman who steers a ship

Examples

The experienced helmswoman guided the yacht safely through the rough waters.
During the regatta, the helmswoman made quick decisions that kept the boat ahead of the competition.
As the ship approached the harbor, the helmswoman maintained a steady hand at the wheel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: helmsman - a man who steers a ship

Etymology

Formed from 'helm' (to steer) and 'woman'. Modeled after 'helmsman'.
