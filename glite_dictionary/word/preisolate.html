to preisolate

1. VERB

/ ˌpriˈaɪsəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈaɪsəleɪt / (BrE)
to isolate something in advance, especially as a preparatory step in a process or experiment
isolate beforehand; in advance

Examples

To ensure accurate results, scientists often preisolate the bacteria before testing.
It's important to preisolate the protein samples ahead of the main experiment.
The researchers preisolated the compound to reduce contamination in subsequent steps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to isolate - to separate something from others

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'pre-' (before) and 'isolate'.
