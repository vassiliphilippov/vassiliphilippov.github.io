Rosa chinensis

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊzə tʃɪˈnɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzə tʃɪˈnɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A species of shrubby rose native to China, widely cultivated as an ornamental plant and an ancestor of many modern garden roses.
species of Chinese shrubby rose

Examples

Many modern hybrid roses can trace their lineage back to Rosa chinensis.
Gardeners prize Rosa chinensis for its recurrent blooming and vibrant flowers.
The cultivation of Rosa chinensis dates back hundreds of years in China.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rose - a prickly bush or shrub that typically bears red, pink, yellow, or white fragrant flowers

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'Chinese rose'.
