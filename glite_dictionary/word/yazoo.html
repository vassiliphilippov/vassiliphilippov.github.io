Yazoo

1. PROPN

/ jæˈzu / (AmE), / jæˈzuː / (BrE)
a river in western Mississippi, United States, flowing southwest to join the Mississippi River above Vicksburg.
river in western Mississippi, US

Examples

The Yazoo River has played an important role in the development of the Mississippi Delta region.
Many species of fish can be found in the waters of the Yazoo.
Flooding along the Yazoo has historically threatened nearby towns and farmlands.

Etymology

From a Native American (probably Choctaw) word, possibly meaning 'river of death.'
