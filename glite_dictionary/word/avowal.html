avowal

1. NOUN

/ əˈvaʊəl / (AmE), / əˈvaʊəl / (BrE)
a formal statement in which you publicly admit or declare something, often the truth of a belief or feeling
formal declaration or admission

Examples

Her avowal of loyalty reassured the entire team.
Despite his public avowal of innocence, many doubted his story.
The letter was an unexpected avowal of love.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confession - an admission of having done something wrong or of a belief
  • Antonym: denial - a statement that something is not true or does not exist

Etymology

From French 'avouer' (to acknowledge, admit), from Latin 'advocare' (to call to, invoke).
