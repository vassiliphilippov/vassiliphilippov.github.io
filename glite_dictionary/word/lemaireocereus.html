Lemaireocereus

1. NOUN

/ lɪˌmɛrioʊˈsɪərəs / (AmE), / lɪˌmɛəriəʊˈsɪərɪəs / (BrE)
A genus of tall, branching tropical American cacti with stout spines, funnel-shaped flowers, and often edible globular or ovoid fruit.
genus of tropical American cacti

Examples

The Lemaireocereus produces striking funnel-shaped flowers that bloom at night.
In arid botanical gardens, you can find several specimens of Lemaireocereus.
Some Lemaireocereus species bear edible fruit enjoyed by local wildlife.

Etymology

Named for French botanist Charles Lemaire and combined with the Latin 'cereus' (wax candle, torch, a reference to cactus shape).

2. NOUN

/ ləˌmɛri.oʊˈsɪriəs / (AmE), / lɪˌmɛəri.əʊˈsɪəriəs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American cacti, typically tall and branching, with stout spines, funnel-shaped flowers, and globular or ovoid, often edible fruit.
genus of tall, branching American cacti

Examples

The Lemaireocereus is prized for its striking tall, columnar stems and large edible fruits.
Botanists identified the cactus as belonging to the genus Lemaireocereus during their field study.
Some species of Lemaireocereus provide food and habitat for local wildlife in tropical America.

Etymology

Named after Charles Lemaire, a French botanist, combined with Greek 'cereus' (wax candle), describing the plant's shape.
