Guam

1. PROPN

/ ɡwɑm / (AmE), / ɡwɑːm / (BrE)
an island in the western Pacific Ocean, the largest and southernmost of the Mariana Islands, and an unincorporated territory of the United States.
island and U.S. territory in the Pacific Ocean

Examples

My cousin is stationed in Guam with the U.S. Navy.
Tourists flock to Guam for its beaches and tropical climate.
The people of Guam celebrated Liberation Day with a parade.

Etymology

From Chamorro 'Guåhan,' meaning 'we have.'
