gasohol

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæsəˌhɑl / (AmE), / ˈɡæsəhɒl / (BrE)
a type of fuel made by blending 90% gasoline with 10% ethanol, typically derived from corn or other grains
fuel blend of gasoline and ethanol

Examples

Many rural communities have switched to gasohol to support local agriculture and reduce emissions.
The government offers tax credits for vehicles that run on gasohol instead of traditional fuels.
Because it contains ethanol, gasohol can help decrease dependence on imported oil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethanol - alcohol used as a fuel additive or alternative

Etymology

Blend of gasoline and alcohol.
