Douglass

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʌɡləs / (AmE), / ˈdʌɡləs / (BrE)
Frederick Douglass (1817–1895), United States abolitionist who escaped from slavery and became an influential writer and lecturer promoting freedom and equality.
U.S. abolitionist and influential writer (1817–1895)

Examples

Douglass delivered a powerful speech on the importance of equality and freedom.
Many students study the life of Douglass in American history classes.
Douglass's autobiography remains a significant work in the literature of human rights.

Etymology

Douglass is an altered spelling of the Scottish surname Douglas, adopted by Frederick Douglass from a narrative poem after his escape from slavery.
