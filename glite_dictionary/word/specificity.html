specificity

1. NOUN

/ spəˌsɪfɪˈsɪɾi / (AmE), / spəˌsɪfɪˈsɪɾi / (BrE)
the quality of being specific to a particular organism or group, especially in biology or medicine
biological property of being specific to an organism
medical

Examples

The specificity of this antibody allows it to target only the infected cells.
Scientists studied the specificity of the virus for different animal hosts.
The specificity of the enzyme means it will only react with one type of substrate.

Synset Examples

host specificity of a parasite

Related Terms

  • Synonym: selectivity - the capacity of being selective or choosing specifically among alternatives

Etymology

From specific + -ity, from Latin specificus (specificus) and the suffix -ity (indicating quality or state).

2. NOUN

/ spəˌsɪfɪˈsɪɾi / (AmE), / spəˌsɪfɪˈsɪəti / (BrE)
the quality of being precise, detailed, or clearly defined rather than general or vague
the quality of being precise or detailed

Examples

The scientist emphasized the specificity of the experiment's results in her report.
Good job descriptions require a high level of specificity to avoid confusion among employees.
The specificity of this medication means it targets only certain bacteria.

Synset Examples

add a desirable note of specificity to the discussion
the specificity of the symptoms of the disease

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precision - the quality of being exact and accurate
  • Antonym: generality - the quality of being general rather than specific

Etymology

From French spécificité, from Latin specificus (specific) + -ity (noun suffix).
