flyweight

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈflaɪˌweɪt / (BrE)
In boxing and other combat sports, a weight division for competitors who are among the lightest, typically around 112 pounds (51 kg) in professional boxing.
weight class for lightest fighters in sports

Examples

She won the flyweight title in her first year as a professional boxer.
The flyweight division is known for its fast-paced and technical matches.
He qualified for the Olympics after dominating the flyweight category at nationals.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bantamweight - a weight class just above flyweight in boxing and some other sports
  • Degree Higher: featherweight - a weight class above bantamweight in some combat sports

Etymology

From 'fly' (suggesting lightness or small size) + 'weight', referring to the weight category.

2. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈflaɪˌweɪt / (BrE)
a boxer in a weight class with an upper limit of 112 pounds (51 kilograms), especially in amateur or professional boxing
boxer under 112 pounds

Examples

The reigning flyweight champion successfully defended his title last night.
She competed as a flyweight in the national boxing tournament.
Many great boxers began their careers in the flyweight division.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bantamweight - a boxer in the next heavier weight class above flyweight (up to 118 pounds)
  • Degree Lower: light flyweight - a boxer in the lighter weight class below flyweight (up to 108 pounds)

Etymology

From 'fly' (suggesting small size) + 'weight', first used in boxing in the early 20th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈflaɪˌweɪt / (BrE)
(in boxing and some other sports) a competitor who weighs no more than 115 pounds (52 kg), or the weight division itself.
lightest weight class in boxing/wrestling

Examples

She won the championship as a flyweight in the national boxing tournament.
The flyweight division always showcases thrilling speed and agility.
As a flyweight, he must carefully manage his diet to stay under the weight limit.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bantamweight - the next heavier weight class above flyweight in boxing
  • Degree Lower: light flyweight - the class immediately below flyweight in boxing

Etymology

From 'fly' meaning 'light' + 'weight'; first used in boxing in late 19th century.
