gametophore

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈmit̬əˌfɔr / (AmE), / ɡəˈmiːtəfɔː / (BrE)
(in botany) a modified branch or structure that bears gametangia, as found in certain lower plants such as thalloid liverworts.
plant structure bearing gametangia
medical

Examples

The gametophore of a moss plant often looks like a small leafy shoot.
During reproduction, the liverwort produces a gametophore that supports its sexual organs.
Botanists observed the gametophore developing new gametangia under the microscope.

Etymology

From Greek 'gametēs' (husband) and 'phoros' (bearing).
