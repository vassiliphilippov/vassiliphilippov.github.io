Manichee

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænɪˌki / (AmE), / ˈmanɪkiː / (BrE)
A follower of Manichaeism, a dualistic religious system from the 3rd century founded by the prophet Mani.
adherent of Manichaeism

Examples

The Manichee sect spread rapidly across the Roman Empire in the third century.
He was accused of being a Manichee because of his dualistic views.
After his conversion, Augustine wrote extensively against the Manichees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Manichaean - an adherent or relating to Manichaeism

Etymology

From Late Latin *Manichaeus*, from Ancient Greek *Μανιχαῖος* (Manikhaîos), from Mani, the founder of Manichaeism.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmænɪˌki / (AmE), / ˈmanɪkiː / (BrE)
of or relating to Manichaeism, an ancient religious movement combining elements of Zoroastrianism, Christianity, and Gnosticism.
relating to Manichaeism

Examples

Some scholars have discussed the influence of Manichee thought on early Christian theology.
The text offers a Manichee perspective on the conflict between good and evil.
That painting's symbolism can be traced back to Manichee beliefs about light and darkness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Manichaean - of or relating to Manichaeism

Etymology

From Late Latin Manichaeus, from Greek Manichaíos, from Mani (the Persian founder of Manichaeism).
