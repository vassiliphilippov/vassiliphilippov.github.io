First Earl Kitchener of Khartoum

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɜrst ɜrl ˈkɪtʃənər əv kɑrˈtuːm / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ɜːl ˈkɪtʃɪnə əv kɑːˈtuːm / (BrE)
(1850–1916) British field marshal and colonial administrator, noted for his roles in the Sudan, the Boer War, and as Secretary of State for War during World War I.
British field marshal and colonial administrator

Examples

The government appointed First Earl Kitchener of Khartoum as Secretary of State for War in 1914.
During the Sudan campaign, First Earl Kitchener of Khartoum led British forces to victory at Omdurman.
Many posters during World War I featured First Earl Kitchener of Khartoum urging men to enlist.
