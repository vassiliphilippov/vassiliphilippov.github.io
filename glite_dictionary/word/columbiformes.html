Columbiformes

1. NOUN

/ kəˌlʌmbəˈfɔːrmiːz / (AmE), / kəˌlʌmbɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of birds that includes pigeons, doves, sandgrouse, and the extinct dodos and solitaires
order of birds including pigeons and doves

Examples

The Columbiformes are found on almost every continent.
Extinct species like the dodo once belonged to the Columbiformes.
Many urban environments are home to numerous Columbiformes such as pigeons and doves.

Etymology

From Latin 'columba' (dove) + '-formes' (having the form of).
