rockweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːkˌwiːd / (AmE), / ˈrɒkˌwiːd / (BrE)
a coarse brown seaweed that grows on rocks exposed at low tide, commonly found along seashores
type of brown seaweed on coastal rocks

Examples

The tide revealed a thick layer of rockweed clinging to the exposed boulders.
Harvesters collect rockweed for use in fertilizers and animal feed.
Shorebirds often search for food among the tangled masses of rockweed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bladderwrack - a type of brown seaweed with air bladders, often called rockweed
  • Degree Higher: kelp - large brown algae growing in underwater forests
