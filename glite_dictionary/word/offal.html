offal

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːfəl / (AmE), / ˈɒfəl / (BrE)
the internal organs and entrails of a butchered animal, often considered inedible or discarded in some cuisines
animal internal organs and trimmings

Examples

Many traditional dishes use offal as a main ingredient, especially in some European countries.
The butcher separated the meat from the offal before packaging everything for sale.
Some people find the taste of offal too strong, but it is considered a delicacy in some cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entrails - internal organs, especially intestines
  • Synonym: giblets - edible internal organs of a bird, such as liver or heart

Etymology

From Middle English 'offal' (refuse, waste), from 'off' + 'fall', originally meaning what falls off during butchering.
