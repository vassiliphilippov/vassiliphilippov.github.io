HSV-II

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪtʃ ˌɛs ˈvi ˈtuː / (AmE), / ˌeɪtʃ ˌɛs ˈviː ˈtuː / (BrE)
a herpes virus that typically causes genital herpes in humans
herpes virus causing genital herpes
medical

Examples

A blood test can determine if you have been exposed to HSV-II.
HSV-II infections are one of the most common causes of genital sores worldwide.
Many people with HSV-II do not experience any noticeable symptoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herpes simplex virus - the group of viruses causing herpes infections
  • Degree Lower: HSV-I - herpes simplex virus type 1, usually causes oral herpes

Etymology

Abbreviation of Herpes Simplex Virus type 2.
