Habenaria leucophaea

1. NOUN

/ hæbəˈnɛriə ˌluːkəˈfiːə / (AmE), / hæbəˈnɛːrɪə ˌljuːkəˈfiːə / (BrE)
an orchid found in boggy or wet areas of the north-central United States, bearing racemes of very fragrant creamy or greenish-white flowers
a fragrant orchid of wetland areas

Examples

The Habenaria leucophaea thrives in marshy areas and produces a sweet fragrance in early summer.
Botanists are working to protect the Habenaria leucophaea as its wetland habitat decreases.
We spotted a blooming Habenaria leucophaea during our hike through the national wildlife refuge.

Etymology

From the genus name Habenaria (from Latin 'habena', rein or strap, referring to the shape of the petals) and the species epithet leucophaea (from Greek 'leukos' meaning white and 'phaios' meaning dusky).
