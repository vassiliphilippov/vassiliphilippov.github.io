scarce

1. ADJ

/ skɛrs / (AmE), / skɑːs / (BrE)
not enough or difficult to find because the supply is less than the demand.
in short supply; not enough

Examples

During the winter months, fresh fruit became scarce in the small village.
After the long drought, water was extremely scarce in the region.
In times of war, basic supplies can quickly become scarce.

Synset Examples

fresh vegetables were scarce during the drought

Related Terms

  • Antonym: plentiful - existing in large quantities; more than sufficient
  • Synonym: rare - not occurring very often; uncommon

Etymology

From Old French 'escars' (scanty), from Latin 'excarpsus', past participle of 'excerpere' (to pick out).

2. ADV

/ skɛrs / (AmE), / skɛːs / (BrE)
(Archaic) Only just; barely; by only a very small margin or a very short time before.
barely; only just (archaic, adv.)
archaic

Examples

I had scarce entered the room before the meeting began.
The winter had scarce ended when the rain returned in full force.
She had scarce finished speaking when everyone applauded.

Synset Examples

we hardly knew them
had scarcely rung the bell when the door flew open
would have scarce arrived before she would have found some excuse to leave

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hardly - barely; only just
  • Synonym: barely - by only a small amount; only just

Etymology

From Old French 'escarse' (scanty), from Latin 'scarsus' (sparse).
