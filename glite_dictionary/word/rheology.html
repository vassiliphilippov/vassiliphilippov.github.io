rheology

1. NOUN

/ riˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / riˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of physics that deals with the deformation and flow of matter, especially liquids and soft solids
study of deformation and flow of matter
medical

Examples

She specialized in rheology, focusing on how complex fluids behave under stress.
Recent advances in rheology have improved our understanding of materials like polymers and gels.
The company hired a rheology expert to optimize the consistency of their new product.

Etymology

From Greek *rheos* (stream, flow) + *-logy* (study of).
