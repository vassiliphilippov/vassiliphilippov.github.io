geophagia

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiː.əˈfeɪ.dʒi.ə / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː.əˈfeɪ.dʒɪ.ə / (BrE)
the habitual practice of eating earth, clay, or similar substances, sometimes due to nutritional deficiency or psychological reasons
eating earth or clay as a habit
medical

Examples

Geologists have studied cases of geophagia in various cultures around the world.
Some animals practice geophagia to supplement their mineral intake.
Doctors may diagnose geophagia when a patient persistently ingests soil or clay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pica - compulsive eating of non-food substances

Etymology

From Greek geō- (earth) + -phagia (eating).
