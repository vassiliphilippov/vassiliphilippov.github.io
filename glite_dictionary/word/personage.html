personage

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːsənɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsənɪdʒ / (BrE)
an important or influential person, especially in a particular sphere or context
an influential or important person

Examples

The foreign dignitaries greeted each personage with the utmost respect.
As a leading personage in the community, her advice was often sought after.
The novel features several historical personages whose real actions shaped the story.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: figure - a person of important or prominent standing
  • Antonym: nonentity - a person with no special or interesting qualities; an unimportant person

Etymology

From Old French 'personnage' (person of importance), from 'personne' (person).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrsənɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsənɪdʒ / (BrE)
a person, often one who is notable or whose identity is not specified or deemed important
a person, especially of note or unknown identity

Examples

A mysterious personage arrived at the party, drawing everyone's attention.
The novel opens with an eccentric personage wandering through the quiet village.
Every so often, a strange personage can be seen near the old manor house.

Synset Examples

a strange personage appeared at the door

Related Terms

  • Synonym: person - a human being

Etymology

From Old French 'personnage', from 'personne' (person).
