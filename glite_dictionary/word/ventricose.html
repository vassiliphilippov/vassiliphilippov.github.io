ventricose

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɛn.trɪˌkoʊs / (AmE), / ˈvɛn.trɪ.kəʊs / (BrE)
(of an organ or part) having a swelling or bulge on one side; swollen or distended in the middle.
swollen or bulging on one side

Examples

The pitcher plant has a ventricose base that traps insects.
Under the microscope, the fungus displayed a distinctly ventricose hypha.
The insect's ventricose abdomen stores nutrients.

Synset Examples

the ventricose gullet of an insect

Etymology

From Latin ventricosus (full-bellied, swollen), from venter (belly).
