Ottawa

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑtəwə / (AmE), / ˈɒtəwə / (BrE)
the capital city of Canada, located in southeastern Ontario across the Ottawa River from Quebec
capital city of Canada

Examples

The Canadian government is based in Ottawa, where Parliament Hill is located.
Many tourists visit Ottawa each year for its museums and festivals.
After moving to Ottawa, she quickly adapted to life in the nation's capital.

Etymology

Named after the Ottawa River, itself named after the Algonquin Odawa people.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːtəwɑː / (AmE), / ˈɒtəwɑː / (BrE)
a member of an Algonquian Indigenous people originally inhabiting southern Ontario and neighboring regions of Canada and the United States
a member of an Algonquian people of North America

Examples

Members of the Ottawa played an important role in the fur trade.
Several Ottawa elders attended the cultural festival last weekend.
The traditions of the Ottawa are celebrated in both Canada and the United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ojibwe - a member of a related Algonquian Indigenous people in North America

Etymology

From the Ottawa people's own name, derived from the Ojibwe word 'odaawe', meaning 'to trade'.
