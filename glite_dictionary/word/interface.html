interface

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪntərˌfeɪs / (AmE), / ˈɪntəˌfeɪs / (BrE)
a point of interaction or overlap where two subjects, systems, or areas influence or affect each other
point where two areas interact or overlap

Examples

Research at the interface of physics and engineering often leads to innovative technologies.
Environmental law operates at the interface between politics and science.
Many breakthroughs come from studying the interface of different disciplines.

Synset Examples

the interface between chemistry and biology

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boundary - a line or limit marking the edge of an area; a dividing line
  • Synonym: intersection - the place or point where two things meet or cross

Etymology

Formed from inter- (between) + face, originally in computer science, later generalized.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.t̬ɚˌfeɪs / (AmE), / ˈɪn.təˌfeɪs / (BrE)
(computing) a program or system that enables a user to interact with a computer, device, or software, often through a graphical or command-line display.
computing: means of user-system interaction

Examples

The software update introduced a new interface that made navigation more intuitive.
Developers aim to design an interface that is both attractive and easy to use.
You can customize the interface to better suit your workflow preferences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: user interface - the space where user and machine interact; another term for this concept
  • Degree Lower: command line interface - text-based user interface for entering commands
  • Degree Higher: graphical user interface - a user interface that uses graphics and icons

Etymology

From Latin 'inter-' (between) + 'face' (surface), originally meaning a surface forming a common boundary.

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ˈɪntərˌfeɪs / (AmE), / ˈɪntəˌfeɪs / (BrE)
(Chemistry) A surface forming the common boundary between two distinct objects, substances, or phases.
boundary surface between two substances
medical

Examples

The oil and water formed a distinct interface visible in the test tube.
At the interface between the two solutions, a reaction took place.
Scientists studied the molecular structure at the liquid-solid interface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boundary - a dividing line or limit between two areas or things

Etymology

From Latin inter- (between) + face (form, appearance), influenced by French interface.

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.tərˌfeɪs / (AmE), / ˈɪn.təˌfeɪs / (BrE)
a piece of hardware and related circuitry that allows two devices, especially a computer and its peripherals, to connect and communicate with each other
hardware linking devices (computing)

Examples

The printer connects to the computer using a USB interface.
Upgrading your hard drive may require a different interface for compatibility.
The company developed a new interface to link multiple types of external devices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connection - a point where two things are joined; in computing, the linking of devices
  • Antonym: disconnect - the process or result of detaching two devices or circuits

Etymology

From inter- (between) + face, originally in electronics and computing jargon (1960s).

Source: WordNet
