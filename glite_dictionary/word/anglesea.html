Anglesea

1. PROPN

/ ˈæŋɡəlsi / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡəlsi / (BrE)
an island located to the northwest of Wales, known for its scenic landscapes and historical sites.
island northwest of Wales

Examples

We visited Anglesea during our tour of Wales and enjoyed the beautiful coastal views.
Historians have traced ancient settlements on Anglesea dating back thousands of years.
The ferry from the mainland departs daily for Anglesea in the summer months.

Etymology

From Welsh 'Ynys Môn,' anglicized to 'Anglesea' historically, though the modern spelling is 'Anglesey.'
