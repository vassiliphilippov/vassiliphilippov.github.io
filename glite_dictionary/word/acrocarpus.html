Acrocarpus

1. NOUN

/ ˌækroʊˈkɑrpəs / (AmE), / ˌakrəʊˈkɑːpəs / (BrE)
A small genus of tropical trees native to Indonesia and Malaysia, known for their timber.
genus of tropical trees (Indonesia, Malaysia)

Examples

The wood of Acrocarpus species is highly valued in the construction industry.
Botanists discovered a rare Acrocarpus tree deep in the Malaysian rainforest.
The leaves of Acrocarpus provide shade for many understory plants.

Etymology

From Greek 'akron' (top, tip) + 'karpos' (fruit).

2. NOUN

/ ˌækrəˈkɑrpəs / (AmE), / ˌækrəˈkɑːpəs / (BrE)
A small genus of tall trees native to Indonesia and Malaysia, belonging to the family Fabaceae.
genus of tropical trees (Fabaceae)

Examples

The Acrocarpus genus includes trees prized for their rapid growth and timber.
Botanists discovered a rare species of Acrocarpus in the forests of Sumatra.
The timber from Acrocarpus trees is often used in construction due to its durability.

Etymology

From Greek akros (highest) + karpos (fruit), referring to the tree's fruiting characteristics.
