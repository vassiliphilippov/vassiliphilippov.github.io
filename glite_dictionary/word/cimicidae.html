Cimicidae

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈmɪsɪdiː / (AmE), / sɪˈmɪsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of wingless, flat-bodied, bloodsucking insects, including the common bed bug.
family of wingless bloodsucking insects
medical

Examples

Researchers discovered several Cimicidae species infesting the old building.
Members of the family Cimicidae are notorious for feeding on the blood of mammals and birds.
Pest control often focuses on eliminating Cimicidae in infested homes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bed bug - a small bloodsucking insect of the Cimicidae family

Etymology

From New Latin *Cimicidae*, based on Latin *cimex* (bug).
