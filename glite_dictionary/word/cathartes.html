Cathartes

1. NOUN

/ kəˈθɑːrtiz / (AmE), / kəˈθɑːtiːz / (BrE)
the genus of large New World vultures, including the turkey vulture and related species.
genus of New World vultures

Examples

The turkey vulture, a member of the Cathartes genus, is commonly found throughout the Americas.
Researchers studied several species in the Cathartes group to understand their scavenging habits.
Unlike other vultures, birds of the Cathartes genus use their sense of smell to locate food.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Cathartidae - the family of New World vultures

Etymology

From Greek *kathartēs* (purifier), referring to the scavenging behavior of the species.
