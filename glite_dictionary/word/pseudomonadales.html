Pseudomonadales

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuːdəˌmoʊnəˈdeɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəˌməʊnəˈdeɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of Gram-negative bacteria that are typically spiral, spherical, or rod-shaped, often motile by polar flagella; some species contain photosynthetic pigments
order of Gram-negative bacteria
medical

Examples

Pseudomonadales are known for their adaptability and environmental diversity.
Some members of the Pseudomonadales order play crucial roles in soil ecosystems.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Pseudomonadales that can degrade pollutants.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on the genus Pseudomonas plus the order suffix -ales.
