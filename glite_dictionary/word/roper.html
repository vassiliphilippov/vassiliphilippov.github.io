roper

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊpər / (AmE), / ˈrəʊpə / (BrE)
a cowboy or ranch worker who uses a lasso to catch cattle or horses
cowboy using a lasso

Examples

The skilled roper caught the stray calf with a single toss of his lasso.
She admired the artistry of the roper during the rodeo competition.
Every roper in the region hoped to win the prestigious championship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowboy - a person who herds and tends cattle, especially in the western US

Etymology

From 'rope' + '-er', meaning one who ropes.

2. NOUN

/ ˈroʊpər / (AmE), / ˈrəʊpə / (BrE)
a person whose occupation is making ropes
person who makes ropes

Examples

The village once supported a skilled roper who made cords for fishing nets.
As a roper, he supplied strong lines to sailors and farmers alike.
Historical records mention a roper working near the old docks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rope-maker - someone who makes ropes

Etymology

From Middle English roper (rope-maker), from 'rope' + '-er'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈroʊpər / (AmE), / ˈrəʊpə / (BrE)
a person who acts as a decoy to lure customers, especially into a gambling establishment with dishonest practices
decoy who lures customers, esp. in gambling

Examples

The casino owner hired a roper to entice tourists to the tables with promises of big wins.
Police arrested the roper after discovering that the poker game was rigged for unsuspecting newcomers.
He acted as a roper, mingling with crowds to find easy targets for the fraudulent betting scheme.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shill - a person who pretends to be a genuine customer to encourage others, especially in gambling or sales
