Elaeis guineensis

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈleɪ.ɪs ɡwɪˈniː.ɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ɪˈleɪ.ɪs ɡwɪˈniː.ɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a species of oil palm native to West and Southwest Africa, cultivated for its oil-rich fruit
African oil palm species

Examples

The oil produced from Elaeis guineensis is widely used in cooking and food processing.
Many tropical plantations cultivate Elaeis guineensis due to its high oil yield.
Researchers are studying Elaeis guineensis for its potential in sustainable agriculture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oil palm - a palm tree whose fruit yields palm oil

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Elaeis' from Greek 'elaïos' (of olive oil), and 'guineensis' (from Guinea, Africa).
