sake

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːkeɪ / (AmE), / ˈsɑːki / (BrE)
A Japanese alcoholic drink made from fermented rice, usually served warm or at room temperature.
Japanese rice wine

Examples

We ordered a bottle of sake to enjoy with our sushi at the restaurant.
Traditional sake is heated before being served in small cups.
He learned how to brew sake during his trip to Japan.

Etymology

From Japanese 酒 (sake), meaning 'alcoholic drink'.
