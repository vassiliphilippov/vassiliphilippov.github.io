resentfully

1. ADV

/ rɪˈzɛntfəli / (AmE), / rɪˈzɛntfəli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows bitterness or indignation at having been treated unfairly.
in a resentful manner

Examples

She resentfully agreed to help her colleague despite feeling overlooked for the promotion.
He resentfully handed over the last piece of cake to his younger brother.
The critic resentfully admitted that the play was better than anticipated.

Synset Examples

the best doctors would stay resentfully out of the national service, refusing to become the minions of a Minister

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angrily - in an angry manner
  • Antonym: cheerfully - in a cheerful manner

Etymology

From 'resentful' + '-ly'. 'Resentful' from French 'ressentir' (to feel deeply) and Latin 'resentire'.
