Mendelian

1. ADJ

/ mɛnˈdiliən / (AmE), / mɛnˈdiːlɪən / (BrE)
relating to Gregor Mendel or to the basic principles of heredity established by his experiments with pea plants
relating to Mendel's genetic principles

Examples

The disease follows a Mendelian pattern of inheritance.
Many genetic traits in humans are considered Mendelian in origin.
Her research focuses on Mendelian disorders and their genetic mechanisms.

Synset Examples

Mendelian inheritance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genetic - relating to genes or heredity

Etymology

From the name of Gregor Mendel, 19th-century Austrian scientist whose work founded the science of genetics.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03097143-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ mɛnˈdiliən / (AmE), / mɛnˈdiːliən / (BrE)
A person who supports or follows the principles of Mendelism, especially in genetics.
follower of Mendelism

Examples

As a prominent Mendelian, she championed the use of genetics in plant breeding.
The debate between Lamarckians and Mendelians shaped early theories of evolution.
Many early Mendelians contributed significantly to modern genetics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mendelism - The principles of heredity discovered by Gregor Mendel

Etymology

From the name of Gregor Mendel, 19th-century Austrian scientist and founder of genetics + -ian (indicating follower).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10328320-n
Synset POS: n
