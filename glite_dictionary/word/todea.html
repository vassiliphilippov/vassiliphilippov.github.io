Todea

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊ.di.ə / (AmE), / ˈtəʊ.di.ə / (BrE)
A genus of delicate ferns in the family Osmundaceae, native to the Southern Hemisphere.
a genus of delicate ferns

Examples

The Todea genus includes several species found in Australia and New Zealand.
Botanists studied the Todea ferns for their unique reproductive structures.
In damp forests, Todea often grows alongside other members of the Osmundaceae family.

Etymology

Named after Heinrich Julius Tode, German botanist.
