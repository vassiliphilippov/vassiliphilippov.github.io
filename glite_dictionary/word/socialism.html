socialism

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊʃəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəlɪzəm / (BrE)
A political and economic system in which the means of production, distribution, and exchange are owned or controlled by the state or the community as a whole.
political and economic system based on collective ownership

Examples

He has always been an advocate of socialism and believes in public ownership of industries.
Many countries experimented with forms of socialism during the 20th century.
The debate between socialism and capitalism continues to shape global politics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: capitalism - an economic system based on private ownership and free markets

Etymology

From French socialisme, from social (social) + -isme (-ism).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊʃəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəlɪzəm / (BrE)
A political and economic theory or system in which the means of production, distribution, and exchange are owned or regulated by the community as a whole, especially the state.
system of state or collective ownership

Examples

The country shifted towards socialism after the revolution, nationalizing major industries.
Many debate whether socialism leads to greater equality or to reduced economic freedom.
He wrote a book explaining the principles of socialism and its implementation in Europe.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: capitalism - an economic system based on private ownership and free markets
  • Synonym: communism - a political theory advocating classless society and communal ownership

Etymology

From French socialisme, from social (social) + -isme (-ism).

Synset POS: n
