Falco subbuteo

1. NOUN

/ ˈfælkoʊ səˈbjuːtioʊ / (AmE), / ˈfæl.kəʊ səˈbjuː.tɪ.əʊ / (BrE)
A small slender Old World falcon, also known as the Eurasian hobby, formerly trained for hawking small birds.
a small Old World falcon; Eurasian hobby

Examples

The Falco subbuteo is admired by birdwatchers for its agile hunting style.
Medieval falconers often trained Falco subbuteo to hunt songbirds.
During migration, Falco subbuteo can be seen across much of Europe and Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hobby - a small, slender European falcon (Falco subbuteo)

Etymology

From Latin: 'Falco' (falcon) and 'subbuteo' (a kind of bird of prey).
