basuco

1. NOUN

/ bɑˈsuːkoʊ / (AmE), / bɑˈsuːkəʊ / (BrE)
A low-quality street drug made from a mixture of cocaine paste and cannabis, commonly smoked in some South American countries.
low-quality drug made from cocaine paste and cannabis

Examples

The use of basuco has become a major problem in some urban areas.
Authorities are concerned about the health risks associated with smoking basuco.
Addiction to basuco often leads to severe social and health issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cocaine - a powerful stimulant drug derived from coca leaves
  • Degree Higher: crack - a potent, smokable form of cocaine

Etymology

From Spanish 'basuco', a diminutive of 'basura' (trash), referring to the drug's impurities.
