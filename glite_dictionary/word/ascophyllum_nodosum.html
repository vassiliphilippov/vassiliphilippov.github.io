Ascophyllum nodosum

1. NOUN

/ ˌæs.kəˈfɪl.əm noʊˈdoʊ.səm / (AmE), / ˌæs.kəˈfɪl.əm nəʊˈdəʊ.səm / (BrE)
a large species of brown seaweed commonly found in the North Atlantic, also known as knotted wrack, often used as fertilizer and animal feed
species of brown seaweed (knotted wrack)

Examples

Farmers in coastal regions often collect Ascophyllum nodosum to use as a natural fertilizer for their crops.
The shores were covered with Ascophyllum nodosum, commonly called knotted wrack by locals.
Scientists study Ascophyllum nodosum because of its high nutrient content and environmental importance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rockweed - common name for brown seaweeds of the Fucaceae family, including Ascophyllum nodosum

Etymology

From Latin: 'ascophyllum' from Greek 'askos' (bladder) + 'phyllon' (leaf), 'nodosum' meaning 'knotty' or 'knotted'.
