swoop

1. VERB

/ swup / (AmE), / swuːp / (BrE)
To move rapidly downward through the air in order to seize or catch something; to grab or take suddenly in a swift motion.
to seize swiftly by swooping

Examples

The hawk swooped down and caught the rabbit in its talons.
She swooped in and grabbed the last pastry before anyone else could react.
The police swooped on the suspects at dawn, catching them by surprise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grab - to seize suddenly or quickly
  • Synonym: snatch - to quickly seize something in a rude or eager way

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'swopen' (to sweep), of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ swuːp / (AmE), / swuːp / (BrE)
a swift, sweeping descent through the air, especially by a bird or aircraft
a swift descent through the air

Examples

The eagle made a sudden swoop towards its prey on the ground.
In one swoop, the fighter jet descended and disappeared from sight.
The children watched in awe as the bat made a quick swoop over the lake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dive - a steep, rapid descent through the air

Etymology

Probably imitative, or from Middle English 'swopen' (to sweep).

4. VERB

/ swuːp / (AmE), / swuːp / (BrE)
to move quickly and smoothly in a sweeping or curving motion, often from a higher to a lower position
move in a sweeping, curving motion

Examples

The eagle swooped down and caught its prey in the river.
Suddenly, a cyclist swooped around the corner at high speed.
The children swooped across the lawn, pretending to be airplanes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dive - to descend quickly in a steep path
  • Degree Lower: glide - to move smoothly and continuously along, as if without effort

Etymology

Perhaps of Scandinavian origin, related to Danish 'svaabe' (to sweep).

6. VERB

/ swup / (AmE), / swuːp / (BrE)
(of a bird or other creature) to move rapidly downward through the air, especially to attack or seize something.
move rapidly downward, often to attack

Examples

The eagle swooped from its perch and caught a fish in its talons.
The police swooped on the house early in the morning, arresting several suspects.
With a sudden movement, the cat swooped down on the unsuspecting mouse.

Synset Examples

The raptor swooped down on its prey
The teacher swooped down upon the new students

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dive - to plunge headfirst quickly and energetically; to move down rapidly, often in attack
  • Antonym: to soar - to fly or rise high in the air

Etymology

Probably from Middle English swopen (to sweep, rush), akin to 'sweep'.
