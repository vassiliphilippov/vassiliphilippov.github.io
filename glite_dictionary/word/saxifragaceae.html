Saxifragaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæksɪfrəˈgeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌsaksɪfrəˈɡeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of mainly herbaceous flowering plants, including saxifrages, found chiefly in northern temperate and cold regions
family of flowering plants (saxifrages)

Examples

Many alpine plants belong to the Saxifragaceae family and thrive in rocky environments.
Botanists debated whether some genera should be classified under Saxifragaceae or Hydrangeaceae.
The Saxifragaceae include both evergreen and deciduous species common in temperate gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hydrangeaceae - a related family of flowering plants sometimes merged with Saxifragaceae

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Latin 'saxifraga' (saxifrage) + '-aceae' (plant family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsæksəfrəˈɡeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌsæksɪˌfræɡəˈsiːiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants consisting of evergreen or deciduous herbs, widely distributed in northern temperate and cold regions, sometimes including genera also considered part of Hydrangeaceae
family of northern temperate flowering plants

Examples

Many alpine plants belong to the Saxifragaceae family, thriving in rocky and cold environments.
Botanists have debated whether to classify Hydrangea within Saxifragaceae or as its own separate family.
Gardeners value members of Saxifragaceae for their attractive flowers and ability to grow in shade.

Etymology

From Saxifraga, the type genus, from Latin 'saxum' (rock) + 'frangere' (to break), + botanical family suffix '-aceae'.
