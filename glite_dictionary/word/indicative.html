indicative

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdɪkətɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɪkətɪv / (BrE)
(usually followed by 'of') showing or suggesting something; serving as a sign or indication of something.
showing or suggesting; serving as a sign

Examples

The sudden drop in sales was indicative of a larger economic problem.
His nervous behavior is indicative of his anxiety about the exam.
Low levels of rainfall are indicative of a developing drought.

Synset Examples

actions indicative of fear

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suggestive - implying something without stating it directly
  • Synonym: symptomatic - serving as a symptom or sign of something

Etymology

From Late Latin indicativus, from Latin indicare (to indicate).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdɪkətɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɪkətɪv / (BrE)
(of a verb or verb form) denoting a mood used for making statements or asking questions of fact.
relating to the verb mood for statements

Examples

In English, the simple present tense is usually in the indicative mood.
The sentence 'She walks to school' is an example of the indicative.
Most everyday statements are written in the indicative form.

Synset Examples

indicative mood

Related Terms

  • Antonym: imperative - denoting the verb mood used for commands
  • Antonym: subjunctive - denoting the verb mood used for hypothetical or non-real situations

Etymology

From Late Latin indicativus (showing, signifying), from Latin indicare (to indicate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03105977-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɪkətɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɪkətɪv / (BrE)
The grammatical mood used to make factual statements, ask questions, or express objective events.
grammatical mood for stating facts

Examples

In English, the indicative is used to state facts, as in 'The cat sits on the mat.'
The sentence 'She goes to school' is in the indicative mood.
Most declarative sentences in English are written in the indicative.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: subjunctive - grammatical mood for wishes or hypotheticals
  • Antonym: imperative - grammatical mood expressing commands

Etymology

From Latin indicativus (serving to indicate), from indicare (to indicate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13823912-n
Synset POS: n
