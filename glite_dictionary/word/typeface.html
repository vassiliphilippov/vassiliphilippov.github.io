typeface

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪpˌfeɪs / (AmE), / ˈtaɪpfeɪs / (BrE)
a particular design of type, including all its sizes and styles, used in printing or on screens
design of printed or digital type

Examples

The magazine chose a modern typeface for its new logo.
Every chapter heading is printed in a distinctive typeface to make it stand out.
Graphic designers often spend hours selecting the perfect typeface for their projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: font - a set of type of one particular size and style within a typeface

Etymology

From 'type' (printing character) + 'face' (appearance).
