incalculable

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkælkjələbl / (AmE), / ɪnˈkælkjʊləbl / (BrE)
too great to be measured or counted; impossible to calculate
too great to be measured

Examples

The earthquake caused incalculable damage to the city.
Her generosity has had an incalculable impact on the community.
There are incalculable risks involved in such a daring venture.

Synset Examples

untold suffering
incalculable riches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immeasurable - too large or extreme to be measured
  • Synonym: countless - too many to be counted
  • Antonym: negligible - so small or unimportant as to be not worth considering

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (not) + 'calculabilis' (calculable).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkælkjələbl̩ / (AmE), / ɪnˈkælkjʊləbəl / (BrE)
too great or numerous to be measured or calculated; impossible to estimate or count.
impossible to calculate or measure

Examples

The value of her contribution to the project is incalculable.
Climate change could have incalculable consequences for future generations.
The damage caused by the hurricane was incalculable at first.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immeasurable - too large, extensive, or extreme to measure
  • Synonym: countless - too numerous to be counted
  • Antonym: calculable - able to be calculated or estimated

Etymology

From Latin 'incalculabilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'calculabilis' (that can be calculated).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00302637-a
Synset POS: a
