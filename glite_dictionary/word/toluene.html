toluene

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊluˌin / (AmE), / ˈtɒljuːiːn / (BrE)
a colorless, flammable liquid derived from petroleum or coal tar, used as a solvent and in the production of fuels and chemicals
flammable chemical solvent

Examples

The laboratory uses toluene as a solvent for various chemical reactions.
Exposure to high levels of toluene can be harmful to your health.
She learned about the properties of toluene during her chemistry class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benzene - an aromatic hydrocarbon similar in structure to toluene

Etymology

From French *toluène*, from *baume de Tolu* (balsam from Tolú, a town in Colombia).
