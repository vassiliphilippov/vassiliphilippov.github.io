Lamna nasus

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæmnə ˈneɪsəs / (AmE), / ˈlæmnə ˈneɪsəs / (BrE)
A species of large, fast-swimming shark with a pointed snout, found in the northern Atlantic and Pacific oceans; also known as the porbeagle.
species of shark (porbeagle)

Examples

The Lamna nasus is highly valued in commercial fisheries for its flesh and fins.
Researchers tagged a Lamna nasus to study its migratory patterns across the Atlantic.
Conservationists have expressed concern about declining Lamna nasus populations due to overfishing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porbeagle - common name for Lamna nasus

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining 'Lamna' (genus name, from Greek 'lamna', kind of shark) and Latin 'nasus' (nose).
