unreliableness

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌn.rɪˈlaɪ.ə.bəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˌʌn.rɪˈlaɪ.ə.bəl.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of not being reliable or dependable.
lack of reliability

Examples

We cannot proceed with the project due to the unreliableness of the data provided.
His constant tardiness contributed to his reputation for unreliableness at work.
The unreliableness of the old car made it a poor choice for long trips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unreliability - the state of being unreliable
  • Antonym: reliability - the quality of being reliable

Etymology

Formed from 'unreliable' + '-ness', following standard English derivational patterns.
