Chinook

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪˈnʊk / (AmE), / tʃɪˈnʊk / (BrE)
the language of the Chinook people, belonging to the Penutian family, traditionally spoken in the Pacific Northwest of North America
Indigenous language of the Chinook people

Examples

Only a few elders are now fluent in Chinook.
He studied the grammar and vocabulary of Chinook for his dissertation.
There are ongoing efforts to revive the use of Chinook in local schools.

Etymology

From Chinook Jargon, originally from a Salishan word for the people.

2. NOUN

/ tʃɪˈnʊk, ʃɪ- / (AmE), / tʃɪˈnʊk, ʃɪ- / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people of the Pacific Northwest, historically inhabiting the region around the mouth of the Columbia River.
member of a Native American people

Examples

The Chinook were skilled traders and established settlements along the Columbia River.
She is a descendant of the Chinook and proud of her heritage.
Archaeologists have uncovered ancient tools used by the Chinook in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chinookan - the language historically spoken by the Chinook people

Etymology

From Chinookan (indigenous language), possibly meaning 'people' or from a village name.
