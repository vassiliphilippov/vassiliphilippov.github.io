fimbriate

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɪm.bri.eɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɪm.bri.eɪt / (BrE)
having a fringe or border of slender, threadlike projections or processes
having a fringed border

Examples

Under the microscope, the plant's petals appeared fimbriate, with delicate, threadlike edges.
Some aquatic leaves have fimbriate margins, giving them a feathery appearance.
The biologist identified the fungus by its fimbriate spores, which were fringed with fine processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fringed - having a decorative border or edge of threads or strips

Etymology

From Latin 'fimbriatus' (fringed), from 'fimbria' (fringe).
