libber

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪbə / (BrE)
an informal term for a supporter of the women's liberation movement; a feminist
supporter of women's liberation; feminist
informal

Examples

During the 1970s, many young women proudly identified themselves as libbers.
My aunt was a prominent libber who attended protests and advocated for equal rights.
Some critics dismissed the activists as mere libbers, but their influence reshaped society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feminist - a person who supports feminism

Etymology

Formed from 'liberation' and the suffix '-er', modeled after 'women's lib'.
