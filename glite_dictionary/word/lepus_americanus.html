Lepus americanus

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːpəs əˌmɛrɪˈkænəs / (AmE), / ˈliːpəs əˌmɛrɪˈkanəs / (BrE)
a large North American hare, known for its large feet and white winter coat; commonly called the snowshoe hare.
North American snowshoe hare

Examples

The Lepus americanus changes its coat from brown to white to blend in with the snow.
Snowy forests are the primary habitat for the Lepus americanus during winter months.
Predators find it harder to spot a Lepus americanus in the snow thanks to its seasonal camouflage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snowshoe hare - common name for Lepus americanus
  • Degree Higher: hare - fast-running, long-eared mammal similar to a rabbit
  • Degree Lower: rabbit - small, short-tailed mammal related to hares

Etymology

From Latin: Genus 'Lepus' meaning 'hare' and species 'americanus' meaning 'American'.
