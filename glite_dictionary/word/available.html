available

1. ADJ

/ əˈveɪləbəl / (AmE), / əˈveɪləbəl / (BrE)
able to be used, obtained, or accessed; ready for use or service
able to be used or obtained

Examples

All conference rooms are available for booking after 5 p.m.
This model is available in several different colors and sizes.
I'm sorry, but Dr. Lee is not available to take your call right now.

Synset Examples

kept a fire extinguisher available
much information is available through computers
available in many colors
the list of available candidates is unusually long

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unavailable - not able to be used or obtained
  • Synonym: accessible - able to be reached or entered
  • Synonym: obtainable - able to be obtained or gotten

Etymology

From Latin 'valere' (to be strong, be of value), via Old French 'avaler'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00184353-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ əˈveɪləbəl / (AmE), / əˈveɪləbəl / (BrE)
(of a person) not busy, free to do something or to see someone.
not busy; free to do something

Examples

Is Dr. Smith available to meet with me on Friday afternoon?
I'm sorry, but Jessica isn't available right now; can I take a message?
Let me check if the manager is available for a quick call.

Synset Examples

he was not available for comment
he was available and willing to accompany her

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unavailable - not free or not able to do something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin availabilis (that is of value, worth), from Latin valere (to be strong, be well).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01066320-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ əˈveɪləbəl / (AmE), / əˈveɪləbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be used, obtained, or accessed; not currently occupied or engaged
able to be used or obtained

Examples

Is this seat available or is someone sitting here?
We have several rooms available for rent starting next month.
The latest update will be available to download tonight.

Synset Examples

the house is available after July 1
2000 square feet of usable office space

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unavailable - not able to be used or obtained
  • Synonym: accessible - able to be reached or entered

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'availabilis' (that is of use or advantage), from 'avail'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00781209-s
