Verbenaceae

1. NOUN

/ vərˌbiːnəˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / vɜːˌbiːnəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of tropical and subtropical herbs, shrubs, and trees, known as the verbena family, found mainly in the New World
family of tropical plants (verbena family)
medical

Examples

The Verbenaceae family includes many ornamental garden plants such as verbena and lantana.
Botanists have classified several shrubs in South America as part of the Verbenaceae.
Some species in the Verbenaceae are valued for their medicinal properties.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Verbena + the suffix -aceae (indicating a plant family).

2. NOUN

/ vɚˌbiːnəˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / vɜːˌbiːnəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of tropical and subtropical herbs, shrubs, and trees, chiefly native to the New World, including the verbena and related plants
family of tropical and subtropical plants; verbena family

Examples

The Verbenaceae family contains a wide variety of flowering plants found in the Americas.
Several species in the Verbenaceae are used in traditional herbal medicine.
The botanist published a paper on the distribution of Verbenaceae in South America.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Verbena + -aceae (a suffix denoting plant families).
