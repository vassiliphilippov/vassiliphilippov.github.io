Cosimo de Medici

1. PROPN

/ ˈkoʊzɪˌmoʊ də ˈmɛdɪtʃi / (AmE), / ˈkɒzɪməʊ dɛ ˈmɛdɪtʃi / (BrE)
(1389–1464) Italian banker, statesman, and patron of the arts, who established the power of the Medici family in Florence.
Italian financier and statesman, Medici family founder

Examples

Cosimo de Medici played a central role in making Florence a center of culture during the Renaissance.
With the support of Cosimo de Medici, many artists and thinkers flourished in fifteenth-century Florence.
Historians often credit Cosimo de Medici with laying the foundations for the Medici family's enduring influence.
