to encrypt

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈkrɪpt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkrɪpt / (BrE)
to convert information or data into a code, especially to prevent unauthorized access
convert information into code

Examples

It is important to encrypt sensitive data before sending it over the internet.
The company uses advanced algorithms to encrypt their clients' information.
You should always encrypt your files to protect them from hackers.

Synset Examples

We should encode the message for security reasons

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to decrypt - to convert coded data back into its original form
  • Synonym: to encode - to convert information into another form or code

Etymology

From Greek 'en-' (in) + 'kryptos' (hidden).
