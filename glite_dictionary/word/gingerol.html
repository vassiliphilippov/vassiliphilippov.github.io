gingerol

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪndʒɚˌoʊl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪndʒərɒl / (BrE)
an active chemical compound found in ginger, responsible for its pungent taste and aroma
pungent compound in ginger

Examples

Research has shown that gingerol may have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
The spicy flavor of ginger is mainly attributed to gingerol.
Pure gingerol is often studied for its potential medical uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ginger - the plant whose rhizome is used as a spice

Etymology

From 'ginger' (the plant) + '-ol' (used in chemistry for alcohols).
