Serranidae

1. NOUN

/ səˈrænɪˌdi / (AmE), / sɛˈrænɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A large family of marine fishes that includes sea basses, groupers, sea perches, and jewfish.
family of marine fishes (groupers, sea basses)

Examples

The Serranidae family includes some of the ocean's largest predators, such as groupers.
Many species in the Serranidae are important for commercial and recreational fishing.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Serranidae off the coast of Indonesia.

Etymology

From modern Latin Serranidae, based on the genus Serranus.

2. NOUN

/ sɛˈrænɪˌdiː / (AmE), / sɛˈranɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of marine fishes including sea basses, groupers, sea perches, and jewfish, found mostly in warm and temperate seas
family of marine fishes (sea basses, groupers, etc.)

Examples

The coral reefs were teeming with species from the Serranidae family.
Groupers, a well-known member of the Serranidae, are popular among fishermen.
Marine biologists are studying the evolutionary history of Serranidae.

Etymology

From Latin 'serranus', name of a kind of fish, and taxonomic suffix '-idae'.
