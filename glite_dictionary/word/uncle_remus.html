Uncle Remus

1. PROPN

/ ˈʌŋkəl ˈriːməs / (AmE), / ˈʌŋkl ˈriːməs / (BrE)
a fictional elderly Black storyteller in the American South, featured as the narrator of tales collected by Joel Chandler Harris and first published in 1880
fictional narrator of Southern folk tales

Examples

Many of the classic Br'er Rabbit stories are told by Uncle Remus in Harris's books.
Disney adapted the character of Uncle Remus in the film "Song of the South."
Literature scholars often discuss the influence of Uncle Remus on American folklore.

Etymology

Created by American writer Joel Chandler Harris (1848–1908) for a series of stories based on African-American folktales.
