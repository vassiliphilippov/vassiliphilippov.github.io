excitability

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˌsaɪtəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪkˌsaɪtəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being easily excited, especially emotionally or physiologically
quality of being easily excited

Examples

The excitability of children can make classroom management challenging at times.
Muscle excitability is crucial for proper bodily movement and response.
Her high excitability often led her to react strongly to minor events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritability - tendency to respond quickly to stimuli; sensitivity

Etymology

From Latin excitabilis (easily roused), from excitare (to excite), plus -ity.

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˌsaɪtəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪkˌsʌɪtəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being excessively responsive or sensitive to stimulation, especially in an organ or body part
excessive responsiveness to stimulation
medical

Examples

The increased excitability of nerve cells can lead to muscle spasms.
Researchers studied the excitability of cardiac tissue after exposure to certain chemicals.
Certain neurological disorders are characterized by abnormal excitability in brain regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritability - the quality of being easily irritated or stimulated
  • Antonym: insensitivity - lack of sensitivity or response to stimuli

Etymology

From Latin excitare (to arouse, to excite) + -bility (from Latin -bilitas, forming nouns denoting ability or capacity).
