outro

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌɹoʊ / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.ɹəʊ / (BrE)
the closing section of a piece of music, a radio or television program, or another performance
concluding section of music or show

Examples

The band's newest album features an extended outro that fades into silence.
During the outro of the song, the instruments slowly drop out one by one.
She composed a unique outro for each episode of her podcast.

Synset Examples

So, you said that you had an outro that you've been practicing?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intro - the opening section of a piece of music or show

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'intro', from 'introduction', using 'out-' to suggest an ending.
