underexposure

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌndɚɪkˈspoʊʒɚ / (AmE), / ˌʌndərɪkˈspəʊʒə / (BrE)
insufficient attention or publicity given to something or someone, causing them to be little known
insufficient publicity or attention

Examples

The artist struggled with underexposure early in her career, despite her considerable talent.
Media underexposure meant that the event failed to attract a large audience.
Some musicians complain that underexposure is a bigger problem than bad reviews.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overexposure - excessive publicity or attention

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'under-' (meaning 'insufficient') and 'exposure'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌʌndərɪkˈspoʊʒər / (AmE), / ˌʌndərɪkˈspəʊʒər / (BrE)
the act or result of exposing film, a sensor, or an image to too little light, resulting in a dark or poorly detailed picture
insufficient light exposure (photography)

Examples

The photograph suffered from underexposure, making it difficult to see any details in the shadows.
Accidental underexposure can ruin an otherwise perfect shot by making the image too dark.
Learning to avoid underexposure is essential for every aspiring photographer.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overexposure - exposing film or a sensor to too much light

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'under-' (not enough) + 'exposure' (from Latin 'exponere', to put out)
