Solandra

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈlændrə / (AmE), / səˈlandrə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American climbing shrubs with large flowers, commonly known as chalice vines.
genus of tropical American climbing plants

Examples

The Solandra vine produces large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers throughout the year.
Botanists often study Solandra species because of their unique growth habits and striking appearance.
Gardeners in warm climates sometimes cultivate Solandra for its ornamental value.

Etymology

Named after Swedish botanist Daniel C. Solander (1733–1782).
