leather-lunged

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɛðər lʌŋd / (AmE), / ˈlɛðə lʌŋd / (BrE)
(of a person) having a very loud and powerful voice, especially used to describe singers or performers.
having a loud, powerful voice

Examples

The leather-lunged announcer made sure everyone in the arena could hear the final score.
Her leather-lunged singing style filled the theatre from the first note to the last.
Fans cheered as the leather-lunged performer delivered an electrifying show.

Synset Examples

His leather-lunged voice echoed through the stadium.
The leather-lunged singer belted out high notes effortlessly.
She was known for her leather-lunged performances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stentorian - (of a person's voice) loud and powerful
  • Antonym: soft-spoken - (of a person) speaking with a gentle, quiet voice

Etymology

Formed from 'leather' (tough, durable material) and 'lunged' (having lungs), alluding to strong, resilient lungs capable of producing loud voices.
