methyltestosterone

1. NOUN

/ ˌmiːθəlˌtɛstəˈstroʊn / (AmE), / ˌmiːθəlˌtɛstəˈstrəʊn / (BrE)
a synthetic androgenic hormone used to treat testosterone deficiency in men, certain types of breast cancer in women, and to promote growth and weight gain
synthetic androgenic hormone
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed methyltestosterone to address his hormonal imbalance.
Some athletes have misused methyltestosterone for its muscle-building effects, despite health risks.
Treatment for advanced breast cancer may include methyltestosterone as part of hormone therapy.

Etymology

From methyl + testosterone, indicating addition of a methyl group to the testosterone molecule.
