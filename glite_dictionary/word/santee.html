Santee

1. NOUN

/ sænˈti / (AmE), / sænˈti / (BrE)
a member of the eastern branch of the Sioux people, also known as the Santee Sioux
member of the eastern Sioux people

Examples

The Santee were historically located in what is now Minnesota.
She is a descendant of a prominent Santee leader.
The Santee have preserved many aspects of their traditional culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sioux - a group of Native American peoples related to the Santee

Etymology

From Dakota Santee, the name of this Sioux group.

2. NOUN

/ sænˈti / (AmE), / sænˈti / (BrE)
the Siouan language spoken by the Santee people, a group of Dakota (Sioux) Native Americans
Siouan language of the Santee people

Examples

The Santee language is still taught in some Dakota communities today.
She studied Santee as part of her research into Siouan languages.
Efforts are underway to revitalize Santee among younger generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dakota - the language of the Dakota people, closely related to Santee

Etymology

From the name of the Santee people, a subgroup of the Eastern Dakota Sioux.

Synset POS: n
