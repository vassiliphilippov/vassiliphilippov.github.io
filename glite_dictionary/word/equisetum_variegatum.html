Equisetum variegatum

1. NOUN

/ ˌikwɪˈsiːtəm ˌværɪˈɡeɪtəm / (AmE), / ˌiːkwɪˈsiːtəm ˌvarɪˈɡeɪtəm / (BrE)
a species of horsetail plant native to northern North America, Greenland, and parts of Europe, characterized by slender, jointed stems and whorled branches
a species of horsetail plant

Examples

Equisetum variegatum is commonly found growing in wet, sandy soils along riverbanks in northern regions.
Researchers studied the distribution of Equisetum variegatum across Greenland and Scandinavia.
The delicate stems of Equisetum variegatum make it easily distinguishable from other horsetail species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horsetail - a type of non-flowering plant in the genus Equisetum

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Equisetum (from equus 'horse' + seta 'bristle') and species epithet variegatum ('variegated, mottled').
