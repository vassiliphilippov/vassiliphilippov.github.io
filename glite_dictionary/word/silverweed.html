silverweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvərˌwid / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvəwiːd / (BrE)
A low-growing perennial plant with leaves that are silvery on the underside, found in northern parts of the United States, Europe, and Asia.
perennial plant with silvery leaves

Examples

The leaves of the silverweed shimmered in the morning sunlight along the riverbank.
Gardeners admire silverweed for its attractive foliage and its ability to thrive in poor soils.
During the hike, we came across a patch of silverweed growing near the marsh.

Etymology

Named for its silvery leaves and resemblance to weeds.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvərˌwiːd / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvəˌwiːd / (BrE)
any of several plants with silvery leaves, especially certain twining shrubs of the genus Argyreia with showy purple flowers.
a plant with silvery leaves; species of Argyreia

Examples

The silverweed in her garden bloomed with striking purple flowers all summer.
He identified the climbing vine as a type of silverweed native to Southeast Asia.
Botanists are studying the adaptations of silverweed to dry and sandy soils.

Etymology

Named for the silvery appearance of its leaves.
