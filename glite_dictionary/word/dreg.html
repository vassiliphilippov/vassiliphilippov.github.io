dreg

1. NOUN

/ drɛɡ / (AmE), / drɛɡ / (BrE)
A small amount of residue or sediment, especially at the bottom of a liquid.
small amount of residue or sediment

Examples

There wasn't a dreg of coffee left at the bottom of the cup.
She drank the dreg of wine from her glass before leaving the table.
Even the last dreg of hope seemed to have vanished from his eyes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sediment - solid material that settles at the bottom of a liquid
  • Synonym: residue - a small amount of something that remains after the main part has been removed

Etymology

From Old Norse dregg (sediment), related to Old English dræge (dregs).
