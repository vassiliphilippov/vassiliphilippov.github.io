glare

1. NOUN

/ ɡlɛr / (AmE), / ɡlɛə / (BrE)
An angry or fierce stare.
angry or fierce stare

Examples

She gave him a cold glare when he interrupted her.
The teacher's glare was enough to silence the noisy students.
After the argument, he met her with a steely glare across the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stare - a long fixed look
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ɡlɛr / (AmE), / ɡleə / (BrE)
(of light) to shine with a strong, harsh, or dazzling intensity, often making it difficult to see.
shine with strong or dazzling light

Examples

The afternoon sun began to glare through the windows, making it hard to read.
Headlights from the oncoming car glared in his eyes, forcing him to slow down.
She squinted, shielding her face as the lights glared above the stage.

Synset Examples

The sun glared down on us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dazzle - to blind temporarily with a bright light
  • Degree Lower: gleam - to shine with a soft or intermittent light

Etymology

From Middle English *glaren* ('to shine brightly'), probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse *glara*.

4. NOUN

/ ɡlɛr / (AmE), / ɡlɛə / (BrE)
A strong, harsh, and dazzling light that is difficult or unpleasant to look at.
harsh, dazzling light

Examples

The glare from the setting sun made it hard to see the road.
She put on sunglasses to protect her eyes from the glare of the headlights.
The snow's white surface reflected a strong glare that was almost blinding.

Synset Examples

a glare of sunlight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dazzle - A bright light that blinds temporarily
  • Degree Lower: glow - A steady light, less intense than glare

Etymology

From Middle English glaren (to shine with a fierce light), probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian glara (to glisten).

Source: WordNet

/ ɡlɛr / (AmE), / ɡlɛə / (BrE)
(of light or a bright object) to be reflected with a strong, harsh brightness or brilliance.
reflect with harsh brightness

Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shine - to emit or reflect light; to gleam
