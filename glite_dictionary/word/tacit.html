tacit

1. ADJ

/ ˈtæsɪt / (AmE), / ˈtæsɪt / (BrE)
understood or implied without being stated openly
implied or understood without being spoken

Examples

There was a tacit understanding between the two companies not to compete directly.
She gave her tacit approval by remaining silent during the discussion.
The politician's silence was seen as tacit consent to the proposal.

Synset Examples

gave silent consent
a tacit agreement
the understood provisos of a custody agreement

Related Terms

  • Antonym: explicit - stated clearly and in detail, leaving no room for confusion or doubt

Etymology

From Latin 'tacitus' (silent, unspoken), from 'tacēre' (to be silent).
