Leporidae

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈpɔːrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / lɪˈpɒrɪˌdiː / (BrE)
the family of mammals that includes hares and rabbits
family of hares and rabbits

Examples

The Leporidae family is distinguished by its members' long ears and strong hind legs.
Rabbits and hares, which belong to the Leporidae, are found on almost every continent.
Researchers studied several species in the Leporidae to understand their evolutionary history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hare - a fast-running, long-eared mammal of the Leporidae family
  • Synonym: rabbit - a small, burrowing mammal of the Leporidae family

Etymology

From Latin lepus, leporis (hare).

2. NOUN

/ lɪˈpɔːrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / lɛˈpɒrɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of mammals that includes hares and rabbits, characterized by long ears and powerful hind legs
the mammal family of hares and rabbits

Examples

All species in the Leporidae family have teeth that grow continuously throughout their lives.
Rabbits and hares, both members of Leporidae, are found on every continent except Antarctica.
The classification of Leporidae helps biologists distinguish these animals from other rodents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hare - a fast-running, long-eared mammal of the Leporidae family
  • Synonym: rabbit - a small, burrowing, long-eared mammal of the Leporidae family

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Latin 'lepus' (hare).
