chafe

1. NOUN

/ tʃeɪf / (AmE), / tʃeɪf / (BrE)
soreness or irritation of the skin resulting from rubbing or friction, often accompanied by warmth or redness.
skin soreness from friction

Examples

After the long hike, he noticed a painful chafe on his inner thigh.
Athletes often use powders to prevent chafe during vigorous exercise.
She treated the chafe with a soothing ointment to reduce the soreness.

Synset Examples

he had a nasty chafe on his knee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abrasion - an area damaged by scraping or rubbing

Etymology

From Old French chaufer (to warm), from Latin calefacere (to make warm).

2. VERB

/ tʃeɪf / (AmE), / tʃeɪf / (BrE)
to make or become warm by rubbing, especially with the hands
to warm by rubbing

Examples

After coming inside from the cold, he began to chafe his hands together by the fire.
She chafed her fingers briskly to restore feeling.
The little boy chafed his arms to warm up after playing in the snow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rub - to move something over the surface of another with pressure

Etymology
