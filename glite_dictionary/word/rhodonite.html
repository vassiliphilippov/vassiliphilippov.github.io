rhodonite

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊdəˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈrəʊdəˌnaɪt / (BrE)
a pink or red mineral composed of crystalline manganese silicate, often used as an ornamental stone
pink or red manganese silicate mineral

Examples

The necklace was made from polished rhodonite beads, giving it a distinct rosy hue.
Collectors value rhodonite for both its striking color and its rarity in large, clear crystals.
He discovered a vein of rhodonite while hiking through the old mining district.

Etymology

From Greek 'rhodon' (rose) + '-ite' (mineral suffix), due to its rose-pink color.
