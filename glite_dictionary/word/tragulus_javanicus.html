Tragulus javanicus

1. NOUN

/ træˈɡuːləs dʒəˈvænɪkəs / (AmE), / trəˈɡjuːləs dʒəˈvɑːnɪkəs / (BrE)
A species of small hoofed mammal (Javan chevrotain), slightly larger than the kanchil, found in India and Malaysia.
small hoofed mammal (Javan chevrotain)

Examples

The Tragulus javanicus is rarely seen in the wild due to its elusive nature.
Researchers observed the Tragulus javanicus feeding at dusk near the forest edge.
Unlike the smaller kanchil, Tragulus javanicus prefers denser forest habitats.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: kanchil - a smaller species of chevrotain (Tragulus kanchil) found in Southeast Asia

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Tragulus' and species epithet 'javanicus', meaning 'of Java'.
