unbelieving

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnbɪˈlivɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌnbɪˈliːvɪŋ / (BrE)
Not believing in the existence of God or gods; rejecting any religious faith.
not believing in gods

Examples

Her unbelieving attitude toward religion caused tension in the family.
He spoke in an unbelieving tone when discussing spiritual matters.
Some people remain unbelieving despite being raised in a devout environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atheistic - not believing in the existence of a god or gods
  • Antonym: believing - accepting the existence of God or gods

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnbɪˈlivɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌnbɪˈliːvɪŋ / (BrE)
holding the view that only material things can be known, and that knowledge of spiritual matters or ultimate causes is impossible; skeptical of spiritual truths
skeptical of spiritual matters

Examples

Many scientists in the nineteenth century adopted an unbelieving attitude toward anything supernatural.
He gazed at the miracles with an unbelieving mind, convinced only the material world could be trusted.
Her upbringing was strictly unbelieving, focused on rationality and skepticism of all spiritual explanations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skeptical - having doubts or reservations, especially about religious or spiritual claims
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01309042-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌʌnbɪˈlivɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌnbɪˈliːvɪŋ / (BrE)
not accepting or doubting the truth of particular beliefs, especially in religion; skeptical or disbelieving.
doubting or disbelieving, esp. in religion

Examples

The unbelieving crowd listened in silence as the preacher spoke.
His unbelieving expression showed he doubted the story was true.
She took an unbelieving attitude toward the existence of miracles.

Synset Examples

a skeptical approach to the nature of miracles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skeptical - having an attitude of doubt or questioning
  • Antonym: faithful - having strong belief, especially religious belief

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'believing'.
