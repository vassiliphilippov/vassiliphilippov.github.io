Millikan

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɪlɪkən / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪkən / (BrE)
(1868–1953) United States physicist known for isolating the electron and measuring its charge.
U.S. physicist who measured the electron's charge

Examples

Robert Millikan's oil-drop experiment was crucial to our understanding of electric charge.
The discoveries of Millikan greatly advanced the field of physics in the early 20th century.
Many science textbooks mention Millikan when discussing the properties of electrons.
