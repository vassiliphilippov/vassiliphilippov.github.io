ism

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɪzəm / (BrE)
a distinctive doctrine, theory, system, or practice often forming the basis of a particular group or school of thought
doctrine or system of beliefs

Examples

Many critics argue that modern art reflects a variety of isms developed in the twentieth century.
She criticized all forms of ism, preferring an open-minded approach to ideas.
The workshop explored how different isms influence social and political thought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ideology - a system of ideas and ideals, especially those that form the basis of economic or political theory and policy

Etymology

From Greek -ismos (action or practice), via Latin -ismus and French -isme, used to form nouns indicating practices, systems or ideologies.
