sciolistic

1. ADJ

/ saɪ.əˈlɪs.tɪk / (AmE), / saɪ.əˈlɪs.tɪk / (BrE)
showing superficial or shallow knowledge; having only a slight or pretended understanding of a subject
showing superficial knowledge

Examples

His sciolistic approach to the debate made his arguments unconvincing to the experts.
The article provided only a sciolistic overview of a very complex subject.
Rather than admitting his lack of expertise, he made several sciolistic remarks during the meeting.

Synset Examples

his dilettantish efforts at painting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superficial - not thorough or deep; only concerned with surface aspects
  • Degree Lower: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness

Etymology

From Latin *sciolus* (having little knowledge), diminutive of *scīre* (to know).
