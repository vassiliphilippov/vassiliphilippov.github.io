orphaned

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔr.fənd / (AmE), / ˈɔː.fənd / (BrE)
having lost one or both parents through death or abandonment
having lost parents by death or desertion

Examples

After the tragic accident, the children were suddenly orphaned and left in the care of their aunt.
Wildlife experts rescued several orphaned bear cubs after the forest fire.
The orphaned kitten was found alone and hungry behind the house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orphan - a child whose parents are dead

Etymology

From 'orphan', ultimately from Greek 'orphanos' (bereaved).
