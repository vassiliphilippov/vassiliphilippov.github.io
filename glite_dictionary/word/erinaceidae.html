Erinaceidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛrəˈneɪsiiːˌdi / (AmE), / ˌɛrɪˈneɪsiːˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small insectivorous mammals commonly known as true hedgehogs, characterized by spiny coats and short tails.
family of true hedgehogs (zoology)

Examples

Scientists have classified the hedgehog in the family Erinaceidae due to its distinctive spiny coat.
The Erinaceidae family includes several species native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.
Recent studies investigate the evolutionary relationships within Erinaceidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hedgehog - A small, spiny mammal of the family Erinaceidae

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek 'erīnax' (hedgehog).
