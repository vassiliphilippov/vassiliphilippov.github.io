Ondatra

1. NOUN

/ ɑnˈdeɪtrə / (AmE), / ɒnˈdeɪtrə / (BrE)
A genus of semiaquatic rodents, comprising the muskrat, native to North America and introduced elsewhere.
genus of semiaquatic rodents (muskrats)

Examples

The genus Ondatra includes the well-known muskrat commonly found in wetlands.
Biologists studied the mating patterns of Ondatra in Canadian marshes.
Ondatra has adapted to thrive in a wide range of aquatic environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muskrat - a large aquatic rodent native to North America

Etymology

From scientific Latin Ondatra, derived from Canadian French 'ondatra', meaning muskrat.

2. NOUN

/ ɑnˈdɑtrə / (AmE), / ɒnˈdeɪtrə / (BrE)
A genus of semiaquatic rodents, commonly known as muskrats, native to North America and northern Eurasia.
genus of muskrats (rodents)

Examples

The muskrat belongs to the genus Ondatra, which is the only member of its genus.
Researchers discovered a new subspecies within Ondatra in a remote northern region.
The wetlands are home to several Ondatra populations, making them ecologically important.

Etymology

Modern Latin, from a Native American Algonquian word (about muskrat).
