dear

1. ADJ

/ dɪr / (AmE), / dɪə / (BrE)
held or wished for with deep sincerity; heartfelt.
deeply sincere or heartfelt

Examples

Her dearest wish was to see her family together again.
We offer our dearest condolences on your loss.
He expressed his dearest hopes for a peaceful resolution.

Synset Examples

one's dearest wish
devout wishes for their success
heartfelt condolences

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devout - earnestly sincere in feeling or expression
  • Synonym: heartfelt - genuinely felt or deeply sincere
  • Antonym: casual - not showing deep sincerity or earnestness

Etymology

From Old English dēore (precious, valuable, beloved).

2. NOUN

/ dɪr / (AmE), / dɪə / (BrE)
a sweet, innocent, and gentle person, especially a child; someone easily liked for their mild nature.
sweet or gentle person (esp. a child)

Examples

Everyone says Molly is such a dear because she always helps her classmates.
The little dear brought me flowers from the garden.
He's a real dear, always polite and gentle with others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweetheart - a person who is very likeable or endearing
  • Synonym: darling - a beloved or lovable person
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10263569-n
Synset POS: n

7. ADV

/ dɪr / (AmE), / dɪə / (BrE)
(adverb, chiefly literary) At a great cost or sacrifice; with severe loss, suffering, or penalty.
at great cost or sacrifice (adv)

Examples

He learned the importance of honesty and paid dear for his mistake.
The rebels fought for their freedom, but their victory came dear.
His pride cost him dear in the end, leaving him alone and regretful.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dearly - at a great cost; with much suffering or loss

Etymology

From Old English dēore (precious, costly). Adverbial use from the same root as the adjective.
