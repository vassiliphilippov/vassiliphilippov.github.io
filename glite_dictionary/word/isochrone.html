isochrone

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.səˌkroʊn / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.sə.krəʊn / (BrE)
A line on a map connecting points where a particular event occurs at the same time.
line connecting simultaneous events

Examples

The meteorologists studied the isochrone on the storm track map to determine wind speed progression.
An isochrone shows all locations reached by a rescue team within a certain time frame.
Geologists used isochrones to map the simultaneous deposition of volcanic ash across the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isopleth - a line connecting points of equal value of some quantity

Etymology

From Greek iso- (equal) + chronos (time).
