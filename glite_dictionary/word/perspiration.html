perspiration

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɝː.spəˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɜː.spɪˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The process of the sweat glands in the skin producing and secreting a salty fluid, especially as a way to regulate body temperature.
the process of producing sweat

Examples

After running a mile, a thin layer of perspiration covered her forehead.
Intense perspiration is common during strenuous physical activity.
His shirt was soaked with perspiration after working out in the gym.

Synset Examples

perspiration is a homeostatic process

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweat - the salty liquid produced by sweat glands

Etymology

From Latin 'perspiratio' (breathing through), from 'per-' (through) + 'spirare' (to breathe).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɜrspəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɜːspɪˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the salty fluid produced by sweat glands in the skin, especially as a result of heat, physical activity, or stress
salty fluid secreted by sweat glands

Examples

Her shirt was damp with perspiration after the long run.
Nervous perspiration formed on his forehead during the exam.
The doctor noticed beads of perspiration on the patient's face.

Synset Examples

sweat poured off his brow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweat - the salty liquid from the body's sweat glands

Etymology

From Latin 'perspiratio' (breathing through), from 'per-' (through) + 'spirare' (to breathe).
