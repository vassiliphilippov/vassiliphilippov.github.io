Anchusa riparia

1. NOUN

/ ænˈkjuːsə rɪˈpɛriə / (AmE), / ænˈkjuːsə rɪˈpɑːrɪə / (BrE)
a species of Anchusa native to southern Africa, with blue to red-purple flowers
southern African species of Anchusa plant

Examples

Anchusa riparia thrives in moist soils along riverbanks in southern Africa.
Gardeners prize Anchusa riparia for its striking blue to red-purple flowers.
The conservation status of Anchusa riparia is of concern due to habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anchusa - a genus of flowering plants in the borage family

Etymology

From the genus name Anchusa (from Greek ankhouza, a plant used for dye) and Latin riparia (of the riverbank).
