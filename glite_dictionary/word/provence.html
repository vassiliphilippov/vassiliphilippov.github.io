Provence

1. PROPN

/ pɹoʊˈvɑːns / (AmE), / pɹəˈvɒ̃s / (BrE)
a region of southeastern France, formerly a province, known for its landscapes, cuisine, and historical sites; now part of the administrative region Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur.
region of southeastern France

Examples

Many artists, including Van Gogh, were inspired by the light and scenery of Provence.
We tasted delicious lavender honey during our stay in Provence last summer.
The cuisine of Provence features olive oil, herbs, and fresh vegetables.

Etymology

From Latin 'Provincia', the name given by the Romans to the region.
