Cebuella

1. PROPN

/ sɛˈbjuːɛlə / (AmE), / siːˈbjuːɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of small South American monkeys known as pygmy marmosets, among the smallest primates in the world.
genus of pygmy marmosets

Examples

The Cebuella genus is renowned for its tiny, tree-dwelling primates found in the Amazon rainforest.
Scientists observed a group of Cebuella feeding on tree sap and insects.
Due to habitat loss, conservationists are closely monitoring the populations of Cebuella.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; formed as a diminutive of the genus name Cebus (capuchin monkeys).

2. NOUN

/ sɛˈbjuːɛlə / (AmE), / sɛˈbjuːɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of tiny South American monkeys, commonly known as pygmy marmosets.
genus of small South American monkeys

Examples

The Cebuella is the smallest genus of monkeys in the world.
Researchers recently identified a new species within the genus Cebuella.
Unlike other primates, members of Cebuella possess specially adapted claws instead of nails.

Related Terms
