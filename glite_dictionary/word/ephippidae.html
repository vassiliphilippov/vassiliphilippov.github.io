Ephippidae

1. NOUN

/ ɛˈfɪpɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ɛˈfɪpɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A small family of marine fish known as spadefishes, characterized by compressed bodies and a silvery appearance.
family of spadefishes (marine fish)

Examples

Scientists have recently discovered a new species within the Ephippidae family off the coast of Australia.
The unique body shape of Ephippidae makes them easily recognizable to marine biologists.
Many aquariums feature several members of the Ephippidae family due to their striking appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spadefish - a common name for fish in the family Ephippidae

Etymology

From Greek ephippos (saddle-shaped) + -idae (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).
