to befall

1. VERB

/ bɪˈfɔl / (AmE), / bɪˈfɔːl / (BrE)
(of something bad or unpleasant) to happen to someone or something.
happen to; occur, especially of something bad

Examples

No one could have predicted the tragedy that would befall the small town.
She worried constantly about what dangers might befall her children on their trip.
If any misfortune were to befall him, his family would be devastated.

Synset Examples

He promised that no harm would befall her
What has become of my children?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to happen - to take place; occur
  • Antonym: to befriend - to act as a friend to someone

Etymology

From Old English 'befolian', meaning 'happen to'.

2. VERB

/ bɪˈfɔl / (AmE), / bɪˈfɔːl / (BrE)
(of something, typically bad) to happen to someone or something, especially by chance or fate.
(of something bad) happen to someone

Examples

No one could have foreseen the disaster that would befall the city.
Great misfortune has befallen their family over the years.
We pray that no harm will befall you on your journey.

Synset Examples

It happens that today is my birthday
These things befell

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to happen - to take place; occur
  • Synonym: befallen - past participle of 'befall', sometimes used synonymously
  • Antonym: to avoid - to keep away from; prevent from happening

Etymology

From Old English 'befallan', from 'be-' (about) + 'feallan' (to fall).
