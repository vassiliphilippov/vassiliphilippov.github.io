speedway

1. NOUN

/ ˈspiːdˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈspiːdˌweɪ / (BrE)
a racetrack designed for racing automobiles or motorcycles, typically featuring a smooth, oval course
racetrack for car or motorcycle racing

Examples

The grandstands at the speedway were filled with cheering fans before the big race.
He dreams of one day competing on a professional speedway circuit.
After renovations, the speedway will host major international events next year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: racetrack - a track used for racing

Etymology

From 'speed' + 'way', first used in the early 20th century to describe racetracks emphasizing high speed.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspiːdˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈspiːd.weɪ / (BrE)
a wide road or track designed for high-speed driving or racing, especially used for motor vehicle competitions
road or track for high-speed racing

Examples

The new speedway attracted thousands of racing fans on opening day.
High-performance cars were tested on the speedway outside the city.
He has driven professionally at nearly every major speedway in the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: racetrack - an oval or circular track for horse or auto races

Etymology

From 'speed' + 'way', indicating a route for driving at high speeds.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04281333-n
Synset POS: n
