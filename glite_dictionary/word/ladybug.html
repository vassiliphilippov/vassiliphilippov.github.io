ladybug

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪdiˌbʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈleɪdɪbʌɡ / (BrE)
A small, round beetle, usually red or yellow with black spots, that feeds on aphids and other insect pests.
small, spotted beetle
american

Examples

A ladybug landed on my finger while I was gardening yesterday.
Children love to watch ladybugs crawling on leaves in the spring.
The gardener released dozens of ladybugs to help control the aphid problem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ladybird - small, round, spotted beetle (British term for ladybug)

Etymology

From 'lady' (referring to the Virgin Mary) and 'bug'; so named for its bright colors, which were associated with the Virgin Mary's cloak.
