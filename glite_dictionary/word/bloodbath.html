bloodbath

1. NOUN

/ ˈblʌdˌbæθ / (AmE), / ˈblʌdˌbɑːθ / (BrE)
a situation in which many people are killed in a violent and indiscriminate way
violent event with many deaths

Examples

The stock market crash caused a financial bloodbath for investors worldwide.
The report described the riot as a bloodbath with dozens of casualties.
Refusing to surrender might have led to an even worse bloodbath.

Synset Examples

a bloodbath took place when the leaders of the plot surrendered
ten days after the bloodletting Hitler gave the action its name
the valley is no stranger to bloodshed and murder
a huge prison battue was ordered

Related Terms

  • Synonym: massacre - the killing of a large number of people, especially those who cannot defend themselves
  • Synonym: slaughter - the killing of large numbers of people or animals in a cruel way
  • Synonym: genocide - the deliberate killing of a large group of people, especially those of a particular nation or ethnic group

Etymology

Formed from 'blood' + 'bath', first attested in English in the late 19th century.
