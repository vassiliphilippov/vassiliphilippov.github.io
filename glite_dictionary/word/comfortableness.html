comfortableness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌm.fɚ.tə.bəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkʌm.fə.tə.bəl.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of feeling at ease, especially in social interactions or relationships
feeling at ease in relationships

Examples

There was a sense of comfortableness between the old friends that made conversation flow easily.
Their comfortableness with each other was evident from the way they joked and shared personal stories.
She valued the comfortableness of their partnership more than any romantic gestures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comfort - a state of physical ease and freedom from pain or constraint
  • Antonym: uneasiness - a feeling of discomfort or anxiety

Etymology

From 'comfortable' + '-ness', forming a noun to denote the state or quality of being comfortable.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌmfɚtəblnəs / (AmE), / ˈkʌmfətəblnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being comfortable; a feeling of ease and relaxation without pain or distress.
state of being comfortable or at ease

Examples

The comfortableness of the sofa made it everyone's favorite spot in the house.
She admired the comfortableness of her new shoes after hours of walking.
During the long journey, passengers commented on the comfortableness of the seats.

Synset Examples

he is a man who enjoys his comfort
she longed for the comfortableness of her armchair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comfort - a feeling of physical or emotional ease
  • Antonym: discomfort - a feeling of slight pain, unease, or distress

Etymology

From 'comfortable' + '-ness', forming a noun meaning the state of being comfortable.
