Corvidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔːr.vɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.vɪ.diː / (BrE)
a family of birds including crows, ravens, magpies, jays, and related species, known for their intelligence and adaptability
family of birds: crows, ravens, magpies, jays

Examples

The Corvidae family encompasses birds renowned for their remarkable problem-solving abilities.
Both magpies and crows belong to the Corvidae, a diverse group of songbirds.
Researchers found that several species within the Corvidae exhibit complex social behaviors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corvid - a bird of the crow family

Etymology

From modern Latin *Corvidae*, based on Latin *corvus* (raven or crow).
