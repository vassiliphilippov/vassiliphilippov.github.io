stable

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈsteɪbəl / (BrE)
a building on a farm used for housing horses or other livestock
farm building for keeping horses or livestock

Examples

The horses sleep in the stable every night, safe from the weather.
They built a new stable behind the barn to house the livestock.
A groom is responsible for cleaning out the stable and feeding the animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barn - a large farm building for storing grain or housing animals
  • Degree Lower: sty - a pen or enclosure for pigs
  • Degree Lower: stall - a small compartment for an animal in a stable or barn

Etymology

From Old French estable, from Latin stabulum (standing-place, stall).
