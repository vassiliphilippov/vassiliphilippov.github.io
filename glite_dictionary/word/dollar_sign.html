dollar sign

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːlər saɪn / (AmE), / ˈdɒlə saɪn / (BrE)
A symbol representing commercialism or greed, often used figuratively to suggest a focus on money above other values.
symbol of commercialism or greed (figurative)

Examples

Some politicians are motivated more by the dollar sign than by public service.
He saw nothing but dollar signs in the plan, ignoring its ethical implications.
Whenever she mentioned the project, a dollar sign seemed to appear in his eyes.

Synset Examples

he worships the almighty dollar
the dollar sign means little to him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greed - an intense and selfish desire for wealth or possessions
  • Synonym: materialism - a tendency to consider material possessions and physical comfort as more important than spiritual values
