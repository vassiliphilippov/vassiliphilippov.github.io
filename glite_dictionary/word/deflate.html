to deflate

1. VERB

/ dɪˈfleɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈfleɪt / (BrE)
to cause something that is filled with air or gas to collapse by letting the air or gas out
let air or gas out and make collapse

Examples

When you deflate a balloon, it shrinks and loses its shape.
He had to deflate the tire before repairing the puncture.
After the party, they began to deflate all the decorations.

Synset Examples

deflate a balloon

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to inflate - fill with air or gas so as to expand

Etymology

From Latin 'deflare' meaning 'to blow away.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01439502-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ dɪˈfleɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈfleɪt / (BrE)
to reduce the amount or availability of money, causing a fall in value or prices, especially in an economy
reduce value or prices by lessening supply

Examples

The government decided to deflate the currency to control runaway inflation.
A sudden increase in interest rates can deflate asset prices in the housing market.
Central banks sometimes deflate the economy by tightening the money supply.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to inflate - increase the amount or value, especially of money or prices

Etymology

From Latin 'deflare' (to blow away).

3. VERB

/ dɪˈfleɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈfleɪt / (BrE)
to make someone or something feel or seem less important, confident, or impressive; to reduce in size, value, or importance
make less important or confident; reduce

Examples

The teacher's criticism seemed to deflate her enthusiasm for the project.
A series of poor sales figures quickly deflated the company's reputation for success.
He tried to deflate their excitement by revealing the project's many challenges.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to diminish - to make or become less
  • Antonym: to inflate - to increase in size or importance

Etymology

From Latin 'de-' (down, away) + 'flare' (to blow).

4. VERB

/ dɪˈfleɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈfleɪt / (BrE)
to cause a reduction in the level of prices or economic activity; to bring about deflation in an economy
cause economic deflation

Examples

The government hoped the new policies would deflate the overheated economy.
Rising interest rates are often used to deflate inflation and stabilize prices.
A sudden drop in consumer demand can deflate an entire market sector.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to inflate - to cause prices or economic activity to rise rapidly; increase the amount of money in circulation

Etymology

From Latin 'deflare' (to blow away, to lessen), via Late Latin 'deflatus'.
