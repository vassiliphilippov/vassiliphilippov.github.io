Antilocapra americana

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ti.loʊˈkæp.rə əˌmɛr.ɪˈkeɪ.nə / (AmE), / ˌæntɪləʊˈkæprə əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnə / (BrE)
A species of antelope-like hoofed mammal native to western North American plains, noted for its speed and distinctive branched horns; commonly known as the pronghorn.
North American pronghorn species

Examples

The Antilocapra americana is uniquely adapted to outrun predators on the open plains.
Scientists tracked the migration routes of Antilocapra americana across the western United States.
Unlike true antelopes, Antilocapra americana has horns that are shed and regrown annually.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pronghorn - common name for Antilocapra americana, a North American hoofed mammal

Etymology

From Latin: 'Antilocapra' meaning 'antelope goat' and 'americana' referring to America.
