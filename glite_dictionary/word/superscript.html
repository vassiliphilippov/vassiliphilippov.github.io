superscript

1. ADJ

/ ˈsuː.pɚˌskrɪpt / (AmE), / ˈsuː.pəˌskrɪpt / (BrE)
(of a letter, symbol, or figure) written or printed above and to one side of another character.
written above and to the side of another character

Examples

The exponent in a mathematical equation is usually written as a superscript.
Footnote numbers are often placed in superscript form next to the main text.
To denote powers, type the number as a superscript after the variable.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: subscript - (of a letter, symbol, or figure) written or printed below and to one side of another character.

Etymology

From Latin 'superscribere' (to write above), from 'super-' (above) + 'scribere' (to write).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsuː.pɚˌskrɪpt / (AmE), / ˈsuː.pəˌskrɪpt / (BrE)
a letter, number, or symbol that is written or printed above and slightly to the side of another character, often to indicate powers or references
character set above another

Examples

In mathematical equations, the superscript indicates an exponent.
The isotope of carbon is often written with a mass number as a superscript.
Please write the footnote number as a superscript after the relevant word.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: subscript - a character or symbol set below and to one side of another character

Etymology

From Latin super- (above) + scriptus (written, from scribere 'to write').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06832889-n
Synset POS: n
