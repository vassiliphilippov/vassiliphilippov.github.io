Sama-Veda

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɑːməˈveɪdə / (AmE), / ˌsɑːməˈveɪdə / (BrE)
one of the four Vedas, consisting of a collection of mantras and melodies intended for ritual chanting, used alongside the Rig-Veda in Hindu tradition
collection of Vedic chants and melodies

Examples

The Sama-Veda contains many hymns that are sung during important Hindu ceremonies.
Scholars often compare the Sama-Veda with the Rig-Veda to study early Indian music.
He spent years studying the Sama-Veda and learning its traditional melodies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rig-Veda - the oldest of the four Vedas, a collection of ancient Hindu hymns

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Sāma Veda' (Veda of chants).
