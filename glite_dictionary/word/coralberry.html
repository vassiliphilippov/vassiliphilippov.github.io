coralberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔr.əlˌbɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈkɒr.əlˌbɛr.i / (BrE)
a shrub native to Asia, especially Japan to northern India, with clusters of coral-red berries
shrub with coral-red berries

Examples

The coralberry is popular among gardeners for its attractive clusters of red fruit.
After flowering, the coralberry produces vibrant berries that last into winter.
In the wild, birds are drawn to the coralberry for its colorful, edible berries.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔr.əlˌbɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈkɒr.əlˌbɛr.i / (BrE)
A North American deciduous shrub cultivated for its abundant clusters of coral-red, berrylike fruits.
North American shrub with coral-red fruits

Examples

The garden featured a row of coralberry bushes, their red fruit bright against the green leaves.
Birds often feed on the coralberry during the winter months when other food is scarce.
We planted a coralberry near the porch for its colorful berries and attractive foliage.
