Pomatomus

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈmætəməs / (AmE), / pəˈmatəməs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Pomatomidae, which includes the bluefish.
genus of marine fish (Pomatomidae)

Examples

The Pomatomus genus is best known for its only species, the bluefish.
Marine biologists studied the feeding habits of Pomatomus in coastal waters.
The distribution of Pomatomus extends to temperate and subtropical oceans worldwide.

Etymology

From Greek pōma (cover, lid) and tomos (cutting), referring to the fish's mouth structure.
