biocatalyst

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊˈkæt.əl.ɪst / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.əʊˈkæt.ə.lɪst / (BrE)
A substance, especially an enzyme, that acts as a catalyst in a biological process.
substance catalyzing a biological reaction

Examples

The industrial process uses a biocatalyst to speed up the chemical reaction.
Researchers are engineering new biocatalysts for more efficient drug synthesis.
An enzyme is a natural biocatalyst found in living organisms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enzyme - a protein that acts as a catalyst in living organisms

Etymology

From bio- (life) + catalyst.
