squirt

1. NOUN

/ skwɝt / (AmE), / skwɜːt / (BrE)
an informal term for a person who is small or considered unimportant, especially a child or young person
small or insignificant person (informal)
informal

Examples

Don't be such a squirt—let your older brother handle it.
Even though he's just a squirt, he can be surprisingly stubborn.
The coach told the little squirt to keep trying and not give up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: runt - a small, weak, or unimportant person or animal
  • Antonym: giant - a person who is very big or important
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10455018-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ skwɝt / (AmE), / skwɜːt / (BrE)
A sudden, forceful discharge of liquid in a small stream.
sudden spurt of liquid

Examples

There was a sudden squirt of water from the hose when I turned it on.
A squirt of ketchup landed on his shirt as he squeezed the bottle too hard.
With a quick squirt, the juice shot out of the orange and onto the table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spurt - a sudden gushing stream or jet, especially of liquid
  • Degree Lower: dribble - a small or slow flow of liquid
  • Degree Higher: jet - a strong, concentrated stream of fluid

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound or action; recorded since the late 15th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07450700-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ skwɝt / (AmE), / skwɜːt / (BrE)
to cause a liquid to be ejected from a narrow opening in a quick, forceful stream, usually to wet or hit a surface
spurt liquid in a narrow stream

Examples

She used a water bottle to squirt the cat when it jumped on the table.
If you squirt ketchup on your fries, try not to make a mess.
He accidentally squirted the cleaning solution all over the floor.

Synset Examples

spurt the wall with water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spurt - to gush out in a sudden and forceful stream

Etymology

Perhaps imitative of the sound or action; first recorded in English in the late 15th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01316071-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ skwɝt / (AmE), / skwɜːt / (BrE)
to cause a liquid to come out forcefully in a thin, fast stream; to spray liquid out in a squirt
spray or force out liquid quickly

Examples

She squirted ketchup onto her fries before eating them.
The clown squirted water from a fake flower during the show.
I accidentally squirted juice all over my shirt while opening the orange.

Synset Examples

the boy squirted water at his little sister

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spray - to scatter liquid in small drops over an area

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound of liquid spurting, recorded since the late 15th century.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
