Arthropteris

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈθrɑːptərɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˈθrɒptərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical and subtropical ferns, typically epiphytic or lithophytic, found in the Old World.
genus of tropical Old World ferns

Examples

The Arthropteris genus includes several epiphytic ferns native to tropical regions.
Botanists have discovered a new species within the Arthropteris genus in New Guinea.
Arthropteris ferns are often found growing on rocks or tree trunks in rainforests.

Etymology

From Greek 'arthron' meaning 'joint' and 'pteris' meaning 'fern'.

2. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈθɑːp.tə.rɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˈθɒp.tər.ɪs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical and subtropical Old World ferns, many of which are epiphytic or lithophytic.
a genus of Old World ferns

Examples

Several species in the genus Arthropteris are found growing on tree trunks in rainforests.
The fronds of Arthropteris ferns are noted for their distinctive jointed appearance.
Botanists collected specimens of Arthropteris during the expedition to Southeast Asia.

Etymology

From Greek 'arthron' (joint) and 'pteris' (fern).
