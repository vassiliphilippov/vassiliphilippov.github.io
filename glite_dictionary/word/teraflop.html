teraflop

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛrəˌflɑp / (AmE), / ˈtɛərəˌflɒp / (BrE)
A unit for measuring the computing speed of a computer, equal to one trillion floating point operations per second.
unit of computing speed (trillion operations/second)

Examples

The new supercomputer is capable of performing over 100 teraflops, making it one of the fastest in the world.
Scientists used a 20 teraflop system to simulate complex weather patterns.
As processing demands increase, more systems are reaching multi-teraflop performance levels.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: gigaflop - unit of computing speed, one billion floating point operations per second
  • Degree Higher: petaflop - unit of computing speed, one quadrillion floating point operations per second

Etymology

Formed from 'tera-' (prefix meaning trillion) and 'flop' (floating point operations per second).
