strenuously

1. ADV

/ ˈstrɛnjuəsli / (AmE), / ˈstrɛnjuːəsli / (BrE)
in a way that requires great effort and determination; vigorously or forcefully
with great effort or determination

Examples

The lawyer argued strenuously in defense of her client.
She opposed the proposal strenuously, refusing to compromise her beliefs.
Environmental groups have campaigned strenuously against the construction of the new factory.

Synset Examples

he objected strenuously to the stand his party was taking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vigorously - in a way that involves physical strength, effort, or energy
  • Antonym: lazily - in a way that shows little effort or energy

Etymology

From English 'strenuous' (late Middle English), from Latin 'strenuus' (active, brisk).
