diwan

1. NOUN

/ diˈwɑn / (AmE), / dɪˈwɑːn / (BrE)
A Muslim council chamber or law court, especially in historical or administrative contexts.
Muslim council chamber or law court

Examples

The sultan held an audience with his ministers in the diwan.
Historical records were meticulously kept in the diwan of the Abbasid caliphate.
Debates over tax policy took place in the spacious diwan.

Etymology

From Arabic 'dīwān' (register, council chamber), adopted into Persian and Turkish with similar meanings.

2. NOUN

/ diˈwɑn / (AmE), / diˈwɑːn / (BrE)
A collection of poems, especially in Persian or Arabic literature, usually composed by a single author.
collection of Persian or Arabic poems

Examples

The library houses a rare diwan by the celebrated Persian poet Hafez.
Her doctoral research focused on the structure and themes within the diwan of Rumi.
He displayed a beautifully illustrated diwan containing centuries-old Arabic poetry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthology - a published collection of poems or other pieces of writing

Etymology

From Persian *dīvān*, originally meaning 'register, collection, council'.

3. NOUN

/ diˈwɑːn / (AmE), / diˈwɑːn / (BrE)
A council of state or government ministry in some Muslim countries, especially in the context of historical Islamic administration.
Muslim council of state or ministry

Examples

The Ottoman Empire administered its provinces through a diwan that made key policy decisions.
A new diwan was established to oversee financial reforms in the sultanate.
Historians often study the archives of the diwan to understand governance in medieval Islamic states.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divan - an alternative or obsolete spelling of diwan, also referring to council or government body

Etymology

From Arabic dīwān (government office, registry), via Persian.
