incorrigible

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkɔr.ɪ.dʒə.bəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɒr.ɪ.dʒə.bəl / (BrE)
(of a person or their tendencies) not able to be corrected, improved, or reformed.
not able to be corrected or reformed

Examples

Despite numerous detentions, the boy remained incorrigible in his disruptive behavior.
Her friends tried to change her mind, but she was incorrigible in her beliefs.
Many felt that the criminal was incorrigible and unlikely to ever be rehabilitated.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reformable - able to be improved or corrected
  • Synonym: incurable - impossible to cure or reform

Etymology

From Late Latin incorrigibilis, from Latin in- (not) + corrigere (to correct).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkɔrɪdʒəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɒrɪdʒəbl / (BrE)
impossible to correct, control, or manage; not able to be improved or reformed.
impossible to correct or manage

Examples

He is considered an incorrigible optimist no matter what happens.
The classroom was left in an incorrigible state after the party.
Despite many attempts at reform, the old system proved incorrigible.

Synset Examples

an incorrigible mess

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unmanageable - difficult or impossible to manage
  • Antonym: reformable - capable of being improved or reformed

Etymology

From Latin incorrigibilis (not to be corrected), from in- (not) + corrigere (to correct).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01478913-s
Synset POS: s
