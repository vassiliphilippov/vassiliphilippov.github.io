tomtate

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑmˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɒmˌteɪt / (BrE)
A small grunt fish (Haemulon aurolineatum) native to the western Atlantic Ocean, particularly off the West Indies and Florida.
a small fish found off the West Indies and Florida

Examples

Fishermen often catch tomtate near coral reefs off the coast of Florida.
The bright stripes on a tomtate make it easy to identify among other fish species.
Diving in the West Indies, I spotted a school of tomtates swimming in the clear blue water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grunt - a fish of the family Haemulidae, named for the grunting noise they make
