lam

1. VERB

/ læm / (AmE), / læm / (BrE)
to run away quickly, especially to escape from the police or avoid capture
run away to escape capture
informal slang

Examples

The thieves decided to lam when they heard the police sirens approaching.
If you get caught stealing, you should lam before anyone notices.
The suspect lammed out the back door as soon as the officers arrived.

Synset Examples

If you see this man, run!
The burglars escaped before the police showed up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flee - to run away from a place or situation of danger
  • Synonym: escape - to break free from confinement or control
  • Antonym: surrender - to stop resisting and submit to authority

Etymology

Probably from Scots *lam* (to beat or thrash), later meaning 'to hit' and then 'to run away.'

3. NOUN

/ lӕm / (AmE), / lam / (BrE)
a rapid escape from a place, especially to avoid arrest or capture
sudden escape, especially from police
slang

Examples

After the robbery, the suspects were on the lam for days before being caught.
The notorious gangster went on the lam to avoid prosecution.
Following the scandal, she disappeared and was rumored to be on the lam in another country.

Synset Examples

the thieves made a clean getaway
after the expose he had to take it on the lam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: getaway - an escape, especially after committing a crime
  • Antonym: capture - the act of being caught or arrested

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from Scottish English 'lam' meaning 'to beat or strike.'
