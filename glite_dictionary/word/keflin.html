Keflin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛf.lɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɛf.lɪn / (BrE)
A trademarked name for a cephalosporin antibiotic, used to treat mild to moderately severe infections of the skin, ears, throat, lungs, or urinary tract.
trade name for a cephalosporin antibiotic
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Keflin to treat her skin infection.
After several days of taking Keflin, he noticed significant improvement in his sore throat.
Keflin is often effective in treating urinary tract infections caused by susceptible bacteria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cephalosporin - a class of broad-spectrum antibiotics derived from fungi
  • Synonym: Keflex - another trade name for a cephalosporin antibiotic
