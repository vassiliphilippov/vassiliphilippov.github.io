Makaira albida

1. NOUN

/ məˈkaɪrə ælˈbaɪdə / (AmE), / məˈkaɪərə ælˈbaɪdə / (BrE)
A species of small marlin, reaching up to about 180 pounds, found in the western Atlantic Ocean.
species of small Atlantic marlin

Examples

The Makaira albida is prized by sport fishers for its strength and agility.
Researchers recently tagged a Makaira albida off the coast of Florida to track its migration patterns.
The distinctive elongated bill of the Makaira albida sets it apart from other fish species in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marlin - large game fish with an elongated body and spear-like snout

Etymology

From Latinized Greek *Makaira* (a type of sword or knife), *albida* from Latin meaning 'whitish'.
