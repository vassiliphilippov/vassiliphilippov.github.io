person

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːsən / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsən / (BrE)
an individual human being, as distinct from an animal or a thing.
individual human being

Examples

Only one person knew the answer to the question.
Every person needs food, water, and shelter to survive.
As a person, she is always thoughtful and considerate.

Synset Examples

there was too much for one person to do

Related Terms

  • Synonym: individual - a single human being as distinct from a group
  • Degree Higher: people - human beings in general or considered collectively
  • Antonym: animal - a living organism that is not a human being or plant

Etymology

From Latin 'persona' (character, mask, person).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrsən / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsən / (BrE)
a grammatical category used to classify pronouns, verb forms, and possessive determiners according to whether they refer to the speaker, the person addressed, or another party
grammatical category (first, second, third)

Examples

The novel is written in the first person, giving insight into the narrator's thoughts.
In English, verbs change form depending on which person is the subject.
Please use the third person when writing your report.

Synset Examples

stop talking about yourself in the third person

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: first person - a grammatical form referring to the speaker or speakers
  • Degree Lower: second person - a grammatical form referring to the person being addressed
  • Degree Higher: third person - a grammatical form referring to someone other than speaker or addressee

Etymology

From Latin persona (mask, character, person).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːsən / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsən / (BrE)
the body of a human being, especially as distinguished from their possessions or surroundings
the human body (esp. as distinct from possessions)

Examples

The guard checked every person for hidden items before letting them in.
She always keeps her passport on her person when travelling abroad.
No sharp objects are allowed on your person during the examination.

Synset Examples

a weapon was hidden on his person

Etymology

From Latin persona (mask, character in a play), later generalized to mean an individual human.
