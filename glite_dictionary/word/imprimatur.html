imprimatur

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪm.prɪˈmɑː.tər / (AmE), / ˌɪm.prɪˈmɑː.tər / (BrE)
formal and explicit approval, especially an official license to publish or a mark of authority
official approval or authorization

Examples

The new textbook could not be published without the university's imprimatur.
His work finally received the official imprimatur of the board after several revisions.
Major funding was delayed while waiting for the committee's formal imprimatur.

Synset Examples

a Democrat usually gets the union's endorsement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endorsement - public or official approval or support
  • Synonym: approval - the belief that something or someone is good or acceptable
  • Antonym: disapproval - an opinion that something is bad or wrong

Etymology

From Medieval Latin imprimatur (let it be printed), third person singular present passive subjunctive of imprimere (to print).
