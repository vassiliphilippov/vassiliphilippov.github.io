tetartanopia

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛˌtɑrtəˈnoʊpiə / (AmE), / ˌtɛˌtɑːtəˈnəʊpiə / (BrE)
A very rare form of color blindness in which there is a reduced sensitivity to yellow light.
rare color vision deficiency
medical

Examples

Cases of tetartanopia are so rare that their existence is often debated among scientists.
People with tetartanopia have difficulty distinguishing colors in the yellow spectrum.
The study aimed to identify new cases of tetartanopia using advanced visual tests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color blindness - inability to see certain colors or differences between colors

Etymology

From Greek 'tetartos' (fourth) + 'anopsia' (lack of sight).
