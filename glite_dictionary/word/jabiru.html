jabiru

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒæbɪˈruː / (AmE), / ˌdʒabɪˈruː / (BrE)
A large tropical stork with mainly white plumage and a black head and neck, found in warm regions of America and parts of Australia.
large tropical stork

Examples

The jabiru is easily recognized by its striking black neck and massive bill.
During our trip to Brazil, we spotted a jabiru near a marshy wetland.
The jabirus were feeding in the shallow waters at sunset.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stork - large, long-legged wading bird in the family Ciconiidae

Etymology

From Tupi (an indigenous language of Brazil) *jabi'ru* (swollen neck).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒæbəˈru / (AmE), / ˌdʒabɪˈruː / (BrE)
a large, mostly white stork native to northern Australia, with black wings and a distinctive black neck and head
large white Australian stork

Examples

We spotted a jabiru by the river during our trip to the Northern Territory.
The jabiru is renowned for its striking black neck and white plumage.
Conservationists are working to protect the wetlands inhabited by the jabiru.

Etymology

From Tupi (an indigenous language of Brazil) 'jabirú', referring to a stork.

3. NOUN

/ ˌdʒæbəˈru / (AmE), / ˌdʒabɪˈruː / (BrE)
a large black-and-white stork found in tropical Africa, distinguished by its red bill with a black band around the middle
large African stork with a red and black bill

Examples

The wetlands are home to the rare jabiru, easily recognized by its striking bill.
We spotted a jabiru standing motionless at the water's edge, waiting for prey.
Birdwatchers travel from afar to catch a glimpse of the elusive jabiru in its natural habitat.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'jabiru', from Tupi 'ỹabiru' (stork).
