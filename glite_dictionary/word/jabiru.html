Jabiru

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒæbɪˈru / (AmE), / ˌdʒabɪˈruː / (BrE)
A genus of large storks found in the Americas and Australia, notable for their long legs and massive bills.
a genus of large storks

Examples

The Jabiru is known for its striking black and white plumage and impressive stature.
Scientists observed several Jabirus nesting near the river during the migration season.
Unlike most storks, the Jabiru prefers to inhabit open wetlands and floodplains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stork - large, long-legged wading bird

Etymology

From Portuguese 'jabiru', derived from Tupi-Guarani 'yabi'ru' (swollen neck or bird with a swollen neck).
