ginglymus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪŋ.glɪ.məs / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪŋ.glɪ.məs / (BrE)
an anatomical hinge joint that allows movement mainly in one plane, such as the elbow or knee
hinge joint in anatomy
medical

Examples

The elbow is a classic example of a ginglymus in the human body.
Damage to the ginglymus may limit movement in the affected joint.
A ginglymus permits easy flexion and extension but restricts other movements.

Etymology

From Greek *ginglymos* (hinge).
