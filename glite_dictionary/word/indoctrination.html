indoctrination

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌdɑktrəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌdɒktrɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of teaching a person or group to accept a set of beliefs uncritically
teaching beliefs uncritically

Examples

The school was accused of indoctrination after promoting only one political viewpoint to students.
Military training sometimes walks a fine line between education and indoctrination.
Many parents worry about the potential for indoctrination in religious institutions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brainwashing - forcing someone to adopt radically different beliefs by using systematic and often forcible pressure

Etymology

From Latin 'indoctrinare' (to teach), combining 'in-' (in) and 'doctrina' (teaching).
