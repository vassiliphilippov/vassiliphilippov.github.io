to miscalculate

1. VERB

/ ˌmɪsˈkæl.kjə.leɪt / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈkæl.kjʊ.leɪt / (BrE)
to calculate or judge incorrectly; to make an error in calculation or estimation
calculate or judge incorrectly

Examples

She miscalculated how much time the journey would take, so she arrived late.
The company miscalculated its budget, resulting in a financial shortfall.
I realized too late that I had miscalculated the amount of paint needed for the room.

Synset Examples

I miscalculated the number of guests at the wedding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to misjudge - to judge incorrectly
  • Antonym: to calculate - to determine by mathematical methods

Etymology

From Latin 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'calculate', from Latin 'calculus' (small stone used for counting)

2. VERB

/ ˌmɪsˈkælkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈkælkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to make a mistake in calculating or estimating the amount, cost, time, or result of something
make an error in calculation or estimation

Examples

The engineer miscalculated the load capacity, leading to a structural failure.
We miscalculated how long the project would take and had to work overtime.
If you miscalculate the amount of paint needed, you might run out before finishing the wall.

Synset Examples

I had misestimated his determination

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to misjudge - to form an incorrect opinion or conclusion
  • Antonym: to calculate - to determine mathematically, especially by numerical methods

Etymology

From Latin 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'calculate', from Latin 'calculare' (to reckon).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00714218-v
Synset POS: v
