outlet

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌlɛt / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.lɛt / (BrE)
A socket or receptacle in a wiring system that provides a place where electrical devices can be connected to receive current.
electrical socket or receptacle

Examples

The lamp won't turn on unless you plug it into an outlet.
We installed a new outlet in the kitchen for our coffee maker.
Every hotel room should have at least one electrical outlet near the bed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: socket - an electrical fitting where an appliance or light bulb can be connected to the electricity supply
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04556057-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌlɛt / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.lɛt / (BrE)
a means or activity for expressing emotions, energy, or creativity
means of emotional or creative expression

Examples

Writing serves as an outlet for her imagination and emotions.
Many people find art to be an outlet for stress and frustration.
He needed an outlet for his anger after the argument.

Synset Examples

she had no other outlet for her feelings
he gave vent to his anger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vent - an outlet for expression, especially of strong feelings
  • Synonym: expression - the process of making known one's thoughts or feelings

Etymology

3. NOUN

/ ˈaʊt.lɛt / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.lɛt / (BrE)
an opening through which something can escape, flow out, or be released
opening for escape or release

Examples

The cave has an outlet at the far end, allowing water to drain out.
Smoke escaped through an outlet near the ceiling.
The river found a new outlet after heavy rainfall.

Synset Examples

he blocked the way out
the canyon had only one issue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vent - an opening that allows air, gas, or liquid to pass out of or into a confined space

Etymology

From out + let, meaning 'something that lets out.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03308910-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈaʊt.lɛt / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.lɛt / (BrE)
A shop or place of business where goods are sold to the public, especially one focusing on a particular brand or discounted items.
shop selling goods to the public

Examples

The new shopping center includes an outlet for designer clothing brands.
They opened an outlet in the city to sell excess inventory at lower prices.
Many tourists visit the outlets to find bargains on well-known labels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: store - a retail establishment selling items to customers
  • Synonym: shop - a place where goods are sold to customers

Etymology

From out + let, originally meaning a passage for exit, later extended to places for selling goods.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03753653-n
