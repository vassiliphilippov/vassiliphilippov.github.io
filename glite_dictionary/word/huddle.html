huddle

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌd.əl / (AmE), / ˈhʌd.əl / (BrE)
an informal or quick private meeting, especially to confer or discuss plans briefly
quick private meeting or discussion
informal

Examples

The team came together for a short huddle before the final play.
After the announcement, the managers had a quick huddle to decide on the next steps.
She called everyone into a huddle so they could share important updates privately.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: conference - a formal meeting for discussion
  • Synonym: gathering - an assembly or meeting of people
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07159124-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈhʌd.əl / (AmE), / ˈhʌd.əl / (BrE)
to crowd closely together or to crouch or curl up, often for warmth or protection
crowd together or curl up

Examples

The children huddled together for warmth during the cold night.
We huddled under the small shelter until the storm passed.
She huddled in a corner, trying to stay hidden from view.

Synset Examples

They huddled outside in the rain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cluster - to come together in a group
  • Antonym: scatter - to move or spread apart

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'hoderen' (to conceal, cover), of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02067916-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈhʌd.əl / (AmE), / ˈhʌd.əl / (BrE)
to crowd or come together closely, usually for warmth, comfort, or in response to a situation
come together closely in a group

Examples

The children huddled together under the tree to stay dry during the rainstorm.
We decided to huddle around the campfire for warmth as the night grew colder.
During the emergency, everyone huddled in the corner of the room, waiting for instructions.

Synset Examples

let's huddle together — it's cold!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gather - to come together; assemble
  • Antonym: scatter - to move apart in different directions

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'hoderen', of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02031072-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ ˈhʌd.əl / (AmE), / ˈhʌd.əl / (BrE)
a group of people or things that are crowded closely together in a disorganized way
crowded, disorganized group

Examples

A huddle of tourists blocked the entrance to the museum.
The children waited in a huddle near the school gate for their parents.
She spotted a huddle of reporters outside the courthouse.

Synset Examples

a huddle of frightened women

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crowd - a large number of people gathered together

Etymology

Origin unknown; attested from the late 16th century.
