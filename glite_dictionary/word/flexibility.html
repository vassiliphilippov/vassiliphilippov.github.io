flexibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌflɛksəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌflɛksɪˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being able to bend or be changed easily without breaking; the ability to adapt to different situations
the quality of bending or adapting easily

Examples

Good stretching routines can improve your flexibility and reduce the risk of injury.
Her flexibility in handling new tasks made her a valuable team member.
The flexibility of the material allows it to be molded into different shapes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rigidity - the quality of being stiff or inflexible
  • Synonym: pliability - the quality of being easily bent without breaking

Etymology

From Latin 'flexibilitas', from 'flexibilis' meaning able to be bent.

2. NOUN

/ ˌflɛksəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌflɛksɪˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being able to change or adapt easily to different conditions or situations

Examples

Her flexibility allowed her to thrive in the rapidly changing environment.
Remote work provides greater flexibility for managing family responsibilities.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rigidity - the quality of being inflexible or unyielding
  • Synonym: adaptability - the ability to adjust to new conditions

3. NOUN

/ ˌflɛksəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌflɛksɪˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being willing to change or be easily persuaded; adaptability to new ideas or circumstances
willingness to change; adaptability

Examples

Her flexibility allowed her to adapt quickly to the new team's working style.
Employers often value flexibility when hiring for fast-paced environments.
Negotiations succeeded because both sides showed a willingness to compromise and some flexibility.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rigidity - the quality of being unwilling to change or adapt
  • Synonym: adaptability - the ability to adjust to new conditions
  • Antonym: inflexibility - the quality of being unwilling to change or compromise

Etymology

From Latin flexibilitas, from flexibilis (that can be bent).
