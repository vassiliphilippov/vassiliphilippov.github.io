Commelinales

1. NOUN

/ kəˌmɛləˈneɪliːz / (AmE), / kɒˌmɛlɪˈneɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of monocotyledonous flowering plants, including various herbaceous species such as spiderworts and dayflowers
order of monocotyledonous flowering plants

Examples

The family Commelinaceae belongs to the Commelinales order of flowering plants.
Research on Commelinales has advanced our understanding of monocot evolution.
Many economically important plants are classified within the Commelinales.

Etymology

From the genus name Commelina, with the taxonomic suffix -ales indicating order.

2. NOUN

/ kəˌmɛlɪˈneɪliːz / (AmE), / kɒˌmɛlɪˈneɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of monocotyledonous flowering plants that includes various herbs, such as spiderworts and dayflowers
an order of monocot herbs (plants)

Examples

The Commelinales include plants such as the spiderwort and the dayflower.
Research into the Commelinales has revealed interesting evolutionary relationships among monocots.
Several ornamental species belong to the order Commelinales.

Etymology

From genus name Commelina + Latin -ales (forming order names in taxonomy).
