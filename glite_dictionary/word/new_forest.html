New Forest

1. PROPN

/ nuː ˈfɔrɪst / (AmE), / njuː ˈfɒrɪst / (BrE)
an area of woodland and heath in southern England, originally designated as royal hunting grounds in the 11th century, now a national park known for its ponies
national park and woodland in southern England
british american

Examples

We spent a weekend hiking in the New Forest and saw wild ponies grazing.
The New Forest attracts thousands of visitors each year for its scenic landscapes.
King William I established the New Forest as a royal hunting ground in the 11th century.
