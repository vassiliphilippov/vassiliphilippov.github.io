canoe

1. VERB

/ kəˈnu / (AmE), / kəˈnuː / (BrE)
to travel in or move a canoe through water
travel in a canoe

Examples

They decided to canoe down the river for the afternoon.
We canoed across the lake just before sunset.
She loves canoeing along the quiet canals during her holidays.

Synset Examples

canoe along the canal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paddle - to move a small boat through water using a paddle

2. NOUN

/ kəˈnu / (AmE), / kəˈnjuː / (BrE)
A narrow, lightweight boat with pointed ends, typically propelled by paddling.
narrow boat propelled by paddle

Examples

They paddled the canoe across the calm lake at sunrise.
During our vacation, we rented a canoe to explore the river.
He learned how to steer a canoe when he was a child.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kayak - a small, narrow watercraft typically with a covered deck, paddled with a double-bladed paddle
  • Degree Higher: boat - a vessel for traveling on water

Etymology

From Spanish 'canoa', borrowed from the Taíno word 'kana:wa'.
