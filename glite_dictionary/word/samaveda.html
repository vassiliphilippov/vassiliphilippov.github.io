Samaveda

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɑːməˈveɪdə / (AmE), / ˌsæməˈveɪdə / (BrE)
one of the four Vedas, consisting of a collection of mantras and melodies used in ancient Hindu rituals, often recited or sung in conjunction with the Rig-Veda
Hindu scripture; Vedic chants collection

Examples

The priests began the ritual by chanting verses from the Samaveda.
Scholars study the Samaveda to understand ancient Indian music and religious practices.
Many hymns in the Samaveda are performed during important Hindu ceremonies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rig-Veda - the oldest of the four Vedas, composed of hymns in Sanskrit

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Sāma' (song, chant) + 'Veda' (knowledge), meaning 'Veda of chants'.
