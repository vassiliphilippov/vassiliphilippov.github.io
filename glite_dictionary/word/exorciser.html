exorciser

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛksɔrˌsaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈɛksɔːsaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who expels evil spirits from a place or person, especially through ritual or prayer
person who performs exorcisms

Examples

The exorciser was called to rid the house of its alleged demon.
Throughout history, a skilled exorciser was seen as a powerful spiritual leader.
The villagers gathered anxiously as the exorciser prepared for the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exorcist - person who drives out evil spirits

Etymology

From Latin 'exorcizare' (to adjure, to exorcise), via Old French and Late Latin.
