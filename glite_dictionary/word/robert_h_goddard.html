Robert H. Goddard

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɑːbɚt eɪtʃ ˈɡɑːdərd / (AmE), / ˈrɒbət eɪtʃ ˈɡɒdəd / (BrE)
(1882–1945) United States physicist who developed the first successful liquid-fueled rocket, considered a pioneer of modern rocketry.
pioneer U.S. physicist and rocket engineer

Examples

The pioneering research of Robert H. Goddard paved the way for modern space travel.
Many regard Robert H. Goddard as the father of modern rocketry.
In 1926, Robert H. Goddard launched the world's first liquid-fueled rocket.
