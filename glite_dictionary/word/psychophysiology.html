psychophysiology

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪkoʊˌfɪziˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊˌfɪziˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of psychology that studies the relationship between psychological processes and physiological responses in the body
study of links between mind and body
medical

Examples

She decided to pursue a career in psychophysiology, focusing on how stress affects heart rate.
The conference featured a panel discussion on recent advances in psychophysiology.
Research in psychophysiology often involves monitoring brain activity during cognitive tasks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychology - the scientific study of the mind and behavior
  • Synonym: physiology - the branch of biology that deals with the normal functions of living organisms and their parts

Etymology

From Greek 'psyche' (mind) and 'physiologia' (study of nature).
