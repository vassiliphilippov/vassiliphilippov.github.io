iconography

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪkəˈnɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌaɪkəˈnɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the images and symbols traditionally associated with a particular subject, person, or movement, and the study or interpretation of these visual representations
traditional images and symbols of a subject

Examples

The exhibition explores the iconography of ancient Egypt, focusing on gods and pharaohs.
Political movements often use specific iconography to communicate their ideals and values.
He studied the religious iconography found in medieval manuscripts.

Synset Examples

religious iconography
the propagandistic iconography of a despot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symbolism - the use of symbols to represent ideas or qualities
  • Synonym: imagery - visually descriptive or symbolic representation

Etymology

From Greek eikōn (image) + -graphia (description or writing).
