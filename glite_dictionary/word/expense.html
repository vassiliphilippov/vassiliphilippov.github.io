expense

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspɛns / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛns / (BrE)
a loss, detriment, or sacrifice, especially of something valued or desirable, to achieve or obtain something else.
detriment or sacrifice to achieve something

Examples

She pursued her career ambitions at the expense of her personal relationships.
The country developed rapidly, but often at the expense of the environment.
He finished the project quickly at the expense of quality.

Synset Examples

at the expense of

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cost - the loss or sacrifice necessary to achieve something
  • Antonym: benefit - an advantage or profit gained from something

Etymology

From Latin expensa (things paid out), from expendere (to pay out).

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspɛns / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛns / (BrE)
an amount of money spent for a particular purpose, especially for work-related costs usually reimbursed by an employer
money spent, often for business purposes

Examples

Please submit your expenses from the business trip by Friday.
She carefully tracked every expense for her departmental budget.
Travel expenses are reimbursed if you provide the original receipts.

Synset Examples

he kept a careful record of his expenses at the meeting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cost - the amount of money required to buy, do, or make something
  • Antonym: income - money received, especially on a regular basis, for work or through investments (contrast)

Etymology

From Latin 'expensa', meaning 'things paid out'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13298909-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspɛns / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛns / (BrE)
an amount of money spent on something, especially for goods and services, often referring to costs that may be tax deductible
money spent, especially deductible cost

Examples

The company keeps a record of every expense for tax purposes.
Travel expenses can often be reimbursed by your employer if you keep receipts.
Their monthly expenses included rent, utilities, and groceries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expenditure - the action of spending funds; an amount spent
  • Antonym: income - money received, especially on a regular basis, for work or through investments

Etymology

From Latin expensa (payment, expenditure), from expendere (to pay out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13296518-n
Synset POS: n
