conservatory

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsɝː.vəˌtɔːr.i / (AmE), / kənˈsɜː.və.tər.i / (BrE)
A glass building or room, often attached to a house, used for growing and displaying plants.
glass building for growing plants

Examples

The family spent their afternoons relaxing in the conservatory among the tropical plants and flowers.
She designed a beautiful conservatory that overlooked the garden and provided year-round greenery.
Several rare orchids are grown in the Victorian conservatory at the botanical gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greenhouse - a building with glass walls and roof for growing plants

Etymology

From Late Latin conservatorius (preservative), from 'conservare' (to conserve).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈsɝː.vəˌtɔːr.i / (AmE), / kənˈsɜː.və.tər.i / (BrE)
a college or school where students are trained in music or other fine arts
college for music or fine arts

Examples

She was accepted into the prestigious conservatory to study piano.
Many famous composers have taught at this conservatory.
After graduating from high school, he enrolled in a conservatory in Paris.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: music school - a school specializing in the teaching of music

Etymology

From Italian conservatorio (music school), from Latin conservare (to preserve).
