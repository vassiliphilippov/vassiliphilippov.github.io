Santiago Ramón y Cajal

1. PROPN

/ sanˈtjɑːɡoʊ rəˈmoʊn i kɑːˈxɑːl / (AmE), / sanˈtjɑːɡəʊ rəˈməʊn i kəˈxɑːl / (BrE)
(1852–1934) Spanish histologist and neuroscientist, best known for pioneering work on the structure of the nervous system.
Spanish neuroscientist, pioneer in nervous system research

Examples

The discoveries of Santiago Ramón y Cajal revolutionized our understanding of neurons and brain function.
Many medical schools teach students about the contributions of Santiago Ramón y Cajal to neuroscience.
In 1906, Santiago Ramón y Cajal was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine.

Etymology

Spanish proper name; surname Cajal from his maternal family line.
