Vulpes fulva

1. NOUN

/ ˈvʌlpiːz ˈfʊlvə / (AmE), / ˈvʌlpiːz ˈfʊlvə / (BrE)
The scientific name for the American red fox, a fox species native to North America, often considered the same species as the Eurasian red fox.
scientific name for the American red fox

Examples

The Vulpes fulva is commonly found throughout North America.
Researchers have observed the behavior of Vulpes fulva in different forest habitats.
Unlike the Old World red fox, Vulpes fulva sometimes has a paler coat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red fox - a common name for Vulpes vulpes or Vulpes fulva, the species of fox with reddish fur

Etymology

From Latin: 'vulpes' meaning 'fox', and 'fulva' meaning 'tawny' or 'reddish-yellow.'
