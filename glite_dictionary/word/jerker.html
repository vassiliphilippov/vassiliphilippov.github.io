jerker

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝːkər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːkə / (BrE)
a person who gives a strong, sudden pull
someone who gives a sudden pull

Examples

The conductor acted as the jerker on the bell rope to signal the next stop.
He was the designated jerker for the contest, yanking the rope with great force.
As the jerker pulled, the heavy load finally started to move.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jerk - to pull or twist something suddenly and sharply
