devoir

1. NOUN

/ dəˈvwɑr / (AmE), / dəˈvwɑː / (BrE)
A formal act, expression, or demonstration of respect or duty, especially in social or ceremonial contexts.
formal expression of respect or duty

Examples

The ambassador paid his devoir to the queen upon arrival at the palace.
At the banquet, each guest offered their devoirs to the host in turn.
It was considered proper etiquette to present one's devoir at the beginning of the ceremony.

Etymology

From French 'devoir' (duty), from Latin 'debere' (to owe).
