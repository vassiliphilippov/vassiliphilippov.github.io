pierid

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪərɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɪərɪd / (BrE)
any of numerous pale-colored butterflies belonging to the family Pieridae, typically having three pairs of well-developed legs
a butterfly of the family Pieridae

Examples

A pierid landed delicately on the daisy, its pale wings gleaming in the sunlight.
The study of pierids helps entomologists understand butterfly migration patterns.
Among the collection, the pierid species were recognized by their distinctive white and yellow coloring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butterfly - an insect with two pairs of large, often colorful wings

Etymology

From New Latin Pieridae, the family name, itself from Latin Pīeris, a name related to the Muses and Pieria in Greek mythology.
