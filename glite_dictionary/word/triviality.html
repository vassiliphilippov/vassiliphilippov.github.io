triviality

1. NOUN

/ ˌtrɪviˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌtrɪviˈælɪti / (BrE)
an unimportant detail, fact, or idea; something that is insignificant
insignificant detail or matter

Examples

He dismissed her concerns as mere triviality and focused on the bigger issue.
We wasted time arguing over the trivialities instead of solving the main problem.
Don't let a small triviality ruin your entire day.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: significance - importance or meaning of something
  • Synonym: detail - individual fact or item

Etymology

From Latin 'trivialis' (commonplace), from 'trivium' (crossroads, place where three roads meet).

2. NOUN

/ ˌtrɪviˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌtrɪviˈælɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being unimportant, insignificant, or lacking in seriousness
unimportance; insignificance

Examples

She was frustrated by the triviality of their daily arguments.
He dismissed the complaint as a mere triviality, not worth his time.
Let's focus on important issues instead of getting lost in triviality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insignificance - the quality of being unimportant; lack of significance
  • Antonym: importance - the quality of being important or significant

Etymology

From Medieval Latin trivialitas, from trivialis (common, ordinary).

3. NOUN

/ ˌtrɪviˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌtrɪviˈalɪti / (BrE)
something that is not important or has little value or significance
thing of little importance

Examples

She dismissed his complaints as mere triviality and focused on the main issue.
The conversation drifted into triviality instead of tackling the serious matter at hand.
Let us not waste time on triviality when urgent problems remain unsolved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insignificance - the quality of being unimportant
  • Antonym: importance - the quality of being important or valuable

Etymology

From Latin *trivialis* (commonplace, ordinary), from *trivium* (a place where three roads meet).
