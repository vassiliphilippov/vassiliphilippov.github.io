spectrographic

1. ADJ

/ ˌspɛktrəˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌspɛktrəˈɡrafɪk / (BrE)
relating to or employing a spectrograph, especially for the analysis of spectra in scientific studies.
relating to a spectrograph

Examples

The spectrographic data revealed unexpected features in the star's light.
Scientists used spectrographic analysis to identify the chemical composition of the sample.
Advances in spectrographic technology have improved our understanding of molecular structures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spectrograph - an instrument for recording and measuring spectra

Etymology

From spectrograph + -ic, forming an adjective.
