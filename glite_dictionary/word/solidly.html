solidly

1. ADV

/ ˈsɑlɪdli / (AmE), / ˈsɒlɪdli / (BrE)
in a way that shows complete agreement or unity; as a single undivided group or whole.
completely or unanimously, as a group

Examples

The board voted solidly in favor of the new policy.
The community stood solidly behind the mayor after the crisis.
During the election, the workers supported the candidate solidly.

Synset Examples

the unions voted solidly for Roosevelt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unanimously - with everyone agreeing completely

Etymology

From solid + -ly, meaning 'firmly, as one piece'.

2. ADV

/ ˈsɑː.lɪd.li / (AmE), / ˈsɒl.ɪd.li / (BrE)
In a strong, well-built, or sound manner.
in a strong or sound manner

Examples

The castle was solidly constructed to withstand sieges and harsh weather.
Her argument was solidly supported by evidence and logical reasoning.
The chair felt solidly made and didn't wobble at all.

Synset Examples

a solidly built house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sturdily - in a strong and robust manner
  • Synonym: firmly - in a secure or steadfast manner
