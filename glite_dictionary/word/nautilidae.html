Nautilidae

1. NOUN

/ nɔːˈtɪlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / nɔːˈtɪlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of cephalopod mollusks characterized by their spiral, chambered shells, including the living nautiluses.
family of spiral-shelled cephalopods

Examples

The Nautilidae are known for their beautiful, spiral shells found in tropical oceans.
Scientists study Nautilidae fossils to understand the evolution of cephalopods.
Unlike most modern cephalopods, living Nautilidae still have external shells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nautilus - a cephalopod mollusk with a spiral shell

Etymology

From modern Latin *Nautilidae*, based on Greek *nautilos* (sailor, nautilus) + '-idae' (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ nɔːˈtɪlɪdiː / (AmE), / nɔːˈtɪlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of spiral-shelled cephalopod mollusks, including the modern nautilus and its close relatives.
family of spiral-shelled cephalopods

Examples

The Nautilidae have survived relatively unchanged for millions of years.
Researchers discovered fossils belonging to the extinct members of the Nautilidae family.
Modern Nautilidae are considered living fossils due to their ancient lineage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nautilus - the best-known living member of the Nautilidae family

Etymology

From New Latin Nautilidae, based on the genus name Nautilus.
