nebuchadnezzar

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɛbjəkədˈnɛzər / (AmE), / ˌnɛbjʊkədˈnɛzə / (BrE)
a very large wine bottle holding the equivalent of 20 standard bottles (15 liters), often used for display purposes
large wine bottle (holds 20 bottles)

Examples

At the celebration, they opened a nebuchadnezzar of champagne to impress their guests.
It took several people to carry the massive nebuchadnezzar from the cellar to the table.
The restaurant keeps a nebuchadnezzar on display as a conversation piece for wine enthusiasts.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: methuselah - large wine bottle holding 8 standard bottles
  • Degree Lower: salmanazar - large wine bottle holding 12 standard bottles
  • Degree Higher: sovereign - giant wine bottle, even larger than a nebuchadnezzar (26 liters)

Etymology

Named after Nebuchadnezzar II, King of Babylon; adopted in the late 19th century by the wine industry for large bottles.
