Chytridiaceae

1. NOUN

/ kaɪˌtrɪdiˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / kaɪˌtrɪdɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of aquatic fungi belonging to the order Chytridiales, many of which produce motile spores.
family of aquatic fungi

Examples

Species in the Chytridiaceae family are known for their flagellated spores.
The decline of amphibian populations has been linked to members of the Chytridiaceae.
Researchers are studying Chytridiaceae to understand the spread of fungal diseases in water habitats.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Chytridiales - an order of simple aquatic fungi

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Chytridium + family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ kaɪˌtrɪdiˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / kaɪˌtrɪdɪˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of aquatic fungi belonging to the order Chytridiales, many of which are known for their role in aquatic ecosystems and some species associated with amphibian diseases.
family of aquatic fungi

Examples

The Chytridiaceae family includes fungi that play a crucial role in breaking down organic matter in aquatic environments.
Scientists have studied members of the Chytridiaceae to understand their effects on amphibian populations.
Some species within the Chytridiaceae are implicated in diseases that affect frogs and other amphibians.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Chytridium and the standard taxonomic suffix -aceae.
