squawroot

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwɔˌrut / (AmE), / ˈskwɔːruːt / (BrE)
A tall herb native to eastern North America and Asia, producing blue, berrylike fruit and a thick, knotty rootstock formerly used medicinally.
herb with knotty rootstock, blue berries

Examples

Early settlers sometimes relied on squawroot to treat various ailments.
The forest floor was dotted with blooming squawroot during our hike in spring.
Herbalists once praised the medicinal properties of squawroot.
