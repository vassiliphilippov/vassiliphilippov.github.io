herbaceous

1. ADJ

/ ɜrˈbeɪʃəs / (AmE), / hɜːˈbeɪʃəs / (BrE)
(of a plant or its parts) having the characteristics of an herb, especially being soft, green, and nonwoody.
soft, nonwoody (of plants)

Examples

This garden is filled with herbaceous perennials that bloom in the summer.
Most annual flowers are herbaceous and die back completely after flowering.
The herbaceous stems make the plant easy to cut and shape.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: woody - having wood or characteristics of wood; the opposite of herbaceous in plants

Etymology

From Latin 'herbaceus' (grassy, of an herb), from 'herba' (herb).
