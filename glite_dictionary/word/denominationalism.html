denominationalism

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌnɑːməˈneɪʃənəlɪzəm / (AmE), / dɪˌnɒmɪˈneɪʃənəlɪzəm / (BrE)
strong or narrow-minded loyalty to a particular religious denomination or sect, often leading to division or exclusivity
adherence to a religious denomination

Examples

The rise of denominationalism led to sharp divisions among the religious communities.
His views were shaped by years of exposure to denominationalism within his church.
Critics argue that denominationalism undermines the unity of the broader faith.

Synset Examples

he condemned religious sectarianism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sectarianism - excessive attachment to a particular sect or party

Etymology

From 'denomination' + '-alism', originally used of Christian groups.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˌnɑːməˈneɪʃənəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / dɪˌnɒmɪˈneɪʃənəlɪzəm / (BrE)
The tendency among Protestants to separate into different religious denominations, or the advocacy of such separations.
tendency to split into religious denominations

Examples

The rise of denominationalism in the 19th century led to a proliferation of distinct Protestant churches.
Some theologians have criticized denominationalism for fostering division rather than unity among Christians.
Debates about denominationalism are common in discussions of church history and ecumenism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sectarianism - excessive attachment to a particular sect or party, especially in religion

Etymology

From 'denomination' + '-alism', reflecting the formation or advocacy of religious denominations.
