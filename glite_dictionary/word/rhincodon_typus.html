Rhincodon typus

1. NOUN

/ raɪnˈkoʊdɒn ˈtaɪpəs / (AmE), / rɪŋˈkəʊdɒn ˈtɪpʊs / (BrE)
a very large, slow-moving, spotted shark that resembles a whale and feeds mainly on plankton; commonly known as the whale shark, it is found in warm surface waters around the world
species of large, plankton-feeding shark (whale shark)

Examples

Scientists have tracked Rhincodon typus as it migrates across oceans in search of plankton.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect Rhincodon typus from overfishing and habitat loss.
The unique spotted pattern of Rhincodon typus helps researchers identify individual whale sharks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whale shark - the common name for Rhincodon typus, the largest species of fish

Etymology

From Greek 'rhine' (rasp) + 'odous' (tooth), and Latin 'typus' (type); scientific name for the whale shark.
