January

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒænjuˌɛri / (AmE), / ˈdʒænjuəri / (BrE)
The first month of the year, following December and preceding February.
first month of the year

Examples

We usually return to school after the holiday break in January.
Her birthday is in January, so she often celebrates with a winter party.
The city looks beautiful under a blanket of snow every January.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: February - the second month of the year
  • Degree Lower: December - the twelfth month of the year

Etymology

From Latin 'Ianuarius', named after Janus, the Roman god of beginnings.
