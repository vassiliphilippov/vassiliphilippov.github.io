muishond

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔɪshɑnd / (AmE), / ˈmɔɪshɒnd / (BrE)
A small southern African mammal of the weasel family, known for its strong-smelling scent glands.
southern African weasel-like mammal

Examples

While hiking, we spotted a muishond darting between the rocks.
The muishond relies on its scent glands to deter predators.
Farmers sometimes complain about the strong odor left by the muishond near their chicken coops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weasel - small slender carnivorous mammal

Etymology

From Afrikaans, meaning 'mouse dog'.
