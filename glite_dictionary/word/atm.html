ATM

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ tiː ˈɛm / (AmE), / ˌeɪ tiː ˈɛm / (BrE)
a high-speed digital networking technology used for the transmission of data, voice, and video in fixed-size packets
digital networking technology for high-speed data transfer

Examples

Our company upgraded to an ATM network to support real-time video conferencing.
The university campus uses ATM switches to ensure reliable data transmission between buildings.
Many telecommunications carriers still rely on ATM for backbone infrastructure despite newer alternatives.

Synset Examples

ATM is used for both LAN and WAN

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'Asynchronous Transfer Mode', a term from telecommunications engineering.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06289724-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ tiː ˈɛm / (AmE), / ˌeɪ tiː ˈɛm / (BrE)
a machine, often outside banks, that dispenses cash and allows basic banking transactions when a bank card and personal code are used
automated bank cash-dispensing machine

Examples

I withdrew some cash from the ATM before going shopping.
The ATM outside the bank was out of service last night.
Always cover the keypad when entering your PIN at the ATM.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cashpoint - a machine for dispensing cash, chiefly British English
  • Synonym: bank machine - a machine that dispenses money from a bank account

Etymology

Acronym from 'automated teller machine'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02980517-n
Synset POS: n
