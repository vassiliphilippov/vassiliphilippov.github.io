baldpate

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔldˌpeɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɔːldpeɪt / (BrE)
A North American wigeon (duck), especially the male, which has a distinctive white crown on its head.
a type of duck with a white crown

Examples

The male baldpate is easily recognized by its distinctive white crown.
We spotted several baldpate ducks swimming near the marshy shore.
Birdwatchers flock to the lake each spring to catch a glimpse of the beautiful baldpate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wigeon - a type of dabbling duck, especially the American or Eurasian species

Etymology

From 'bald' + 'pate', referring to the white, bald-like crown of the male.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɔldˌpeɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɔːldˌpeɪt / (BrE)
a person whose head is bald or has been shaved
a bald or shaven-headed person

Examples

The old baldpate sat quietly in the corner, reading his newspaper.
He joked that becoming a baldpate was a sign of wisdom rather than age.
A group of baldpates gathered at the annual reunion, proud of their shiny heads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baldhead - a person with a bald head

Etymology

From 'bald' + 'pate' (the top of the head), first recorded in the late 16th century.
