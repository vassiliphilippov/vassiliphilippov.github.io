viscountess

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪˌkaʊntəs / (AmE), / vɪsˈkaʊntɪs / (BrE)
A noblewoman who holds the rank of viscount in her own right or is the wife of a viscount.
female noble of viscount rank

Examples

The viscountess attended the royal ceremony in a stunning gown.
Traditionally, a viscountess would manage many of the estate's affairs.
Lady Anne became a viscountess after inheriting the title from her mother.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viscount - a nobleman ranking above a baron and below an earl
  • Degree Higher: duchess - a woman holding the rank of duke or the wife of a duke
  • Degree Lower: baroness - a woman holding the rank of baron or the wife of a baron

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *viscountesse*, from Old French *viscomtesse*, female form of *viscomte* (viscount).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪˌkaʊntəs / (AmE), / vɪsˈkaʊntɪs / (BrE)
the wife or widow of a viscount, or a woman who holds the rank of viscount in her own right
wife or widow of a viscount

Examples

The viscountess attended the royal banquet wearing an elegant gown.
After her husband's death, she became known as the Dowager viscountess.
The estate was inherited by the viscountess upon her marriage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viscount - a British nobleman ranking below an earl and above a baron

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman and Old French 'viscontesse', from 'visconte' (viscount).
