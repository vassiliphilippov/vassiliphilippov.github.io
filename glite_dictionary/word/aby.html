to aby

1. VERB

/ əˈbaɪ / (AmE), / əˈbʌɪ / (BrE)
(archaic) To make amends for; to atone for or pay the penalty for.
archaic: make amends for
archaic

Examples

In the old legends, the hero must aby his misdeeds with a perilous quest.
She vowed she would aby the wrong she had done, no matter the cost.
Those who defy the gods must surely aby their folly in the end.

Synset Examples

expiate one's sins

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expiate - to make amends or atone for (guilt or sin)
  • Synonym: to atone - to make amends or reparation for a wrongdoing

Etymology

From Old English *abȳgan* (to pay for, atone for), related to 'buy'.
