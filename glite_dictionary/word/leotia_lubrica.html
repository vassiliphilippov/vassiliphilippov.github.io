Leotia lubrica

1. NOUN

/ liˈoʊʃə luˈbrɪkə / (AmE), / lɪˈəʊtɪə ljuːˈbrɪkə / (BrE)
a small fungus with gelatinous, rubbery, yellow to tan fruiting bodies that grow in clusters, commonly known as jelly baby
species of jelly-like fungus

Examples

The slimy caps of Leotia lubrica were easy to spot along the damp forest floor.
Mycologists often refer to Leotia lubrica as the jelly baby fungus due to its texture.
Clusters of Leotia lubrica appeared after the heavy rain, glistening in the undergrowth.

Etymology

From Latin, where 'leotia' is a genus name possibly from Greek 'leōtos' (smooth), and 'lubrica' means 'slippery'.
