Senecio aureus

1. NOUN

/ sɪˌniʃioʊ ɔˈreɪəs / (AmE), / sɛˌnɛsɪəʊ ɔːˈriːəs / (BrE)
A species of herbaceous plant of the eastern United States to Texas, with golden-yellow flowers, sometimes invasive, also known as golden ragwort.
herbaceous plant with yellow flowers (golden ragwort)

Examples

The wet meadow was filled with Senecio aureus, its golden flowers brightening the landscape.
Gardeners sometimes struggle to control Senecio aureus, as it can become invasive in certain areas.
Botanists recorded the spread of Senecio aureus in several new counties this spring.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Senecio', a genus of plants; 'aureus', meaning golden, referring to the flower color.
