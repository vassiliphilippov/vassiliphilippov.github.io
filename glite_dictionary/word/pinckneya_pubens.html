Pinckneya pubens

1. NOUN

/ pɪŋkˈniːə pjuːˈbɛnz / (AmE), / pɪŋkˈniːə pjuːˈbɛnz / (BrE)
An ornamental shrub or small tree native to swampy areas of the southeastern United States, with large pink or white sepals and bark traditionally used for treating fever.
ornamental shrub or tree of the southeastern US

Examples

Pinckneya pubens is often found in the wetlands of Georgia and Alabama.
Gardeners prize Pinckneya pubens for its showy sepals and unique appearance.
The bark of Pinckneya pubens was once used as a remedy for fever.
