spadefish

1. NOUN

/ ˈspeɪdˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈspeɪdfɪʃ / (BrE)
a deep-bodied, disk-shaped fish found in warmer western Atlantic coastal waters, often used as food
a type of Atlantic coastal fish

Examples

We saw a large school of spadefish swimming near the coral reef.
The aquarium recently introduced several spadefish to its marine exhibit.
Fishermen along the coast prize spadefish for their mild, tasty flesh.
