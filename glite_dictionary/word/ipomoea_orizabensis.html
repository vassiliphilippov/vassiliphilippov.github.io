Ipomoea orizabensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ.pəˈmiː.ə ˌɒr.ɪ.zəˈbɛn.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.pəˈmiː.ə ˌɒr.ɪ.zəˈbɛn.sɪs / (BrE)
A species of tropical American morning glory, known for its showy funnel-shaped flowers.
species of tropical American morning glory

Examples

The Ipomoea orizabensis blooms with vibrant pink flowers every summer in the greenhouse.
Researchers are studying the growth habits of Ipomoea orizabensis in different climates.
Gardeners admire Ipomoea orizabensis for its rapid climbing ability and ornamental appeal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morning glory - common name for plants in the genus Ipomoea, known for trumpet-shaped flowers

Etymology

From genus name 'Ipomoea' (from Greek ipos 'bindweed' + homoios 'like') and species name referencing Orizaba, Mexico.
