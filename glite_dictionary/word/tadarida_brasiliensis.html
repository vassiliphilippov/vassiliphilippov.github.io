Tadarida brasiliensis

1. NOUN

/ təˌdærɪdə brɑːˌzɪliˈɛnsɪs / (AmE), / təˌdarɪdə bræˌzɪliˈensɪs / (BrE)
A species of bat, commonly known as the Brazilian free-tailed bat, found in the southern United States and Central and South America, recognized by its short velvety fur and migratory behavior.
species of bat; Brazilian free-tailed bat

Examples

During migration season, large colonies of Tadarida brasiliensis can be seen flying at dusk.
Researchers are studying the echolocation abilities of Tadarida brasiliensis in urban environments.
The Tadarida brasiliensis is known for its exceptionally fast and agile flight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Brazilian free-tailed bat - common name for Tadarida brasiliensis

Etymology

From New Latin: genus Tadarida + species epithet brasiliensis, meaning 'from Brazil'.
