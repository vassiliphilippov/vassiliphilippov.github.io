Cheilanthes lanosa

1. NOUN

/ kiːˈælnθiːz ləˈnoʊsə / (AmE), / kiːˈælænθiːz ləˈnəʊsə / (BrE)
A small evergreen fern native to North America, with densely woolly leaf stalks and lower frond surfaces.
species of woolly North American fern

Examples

The rocky hillside was covered with patches of Cheilanthes lanosa, thriving in the dry soil.
Botanists identified the Cheilanthes lanosa by its distinctly woolly fronds.
Gardeners appreciate Cheilanthes lanosa for its hardiness and attractive evergreen foliage.

Etymology

From Greek 'cheilos' (lip), referring to the indusium shape, and Latin 'lanosa' (woolly), describing the plant's texture.
