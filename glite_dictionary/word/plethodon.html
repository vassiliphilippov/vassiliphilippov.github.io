Plethodon

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɛθəˌdɑn / (AmE), / ˈplɛθədɒn / (BrE)
A genus of lungless salamanders native to North America, serving as the type genus of the family Plethodontidae.
genus of North American lungless salamanders

Examples

The Plethodon genus includes many species found in forests across eastern North America.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Plethodon genus during the survey.
Unlike many salamanders, members of Plethodon do not have lungs and rely on cutaneous respiration.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Plethodontidae - family of lungless salamanders

Etymology

From Greek 'plethos' (multitude) + 'odous' (tooth), referring to their many teeth.

2. NOUN

/ ˈplɛθ.əˌdɑn / (AmE), / ˈplɛθ.əˌdɒn / (BrE)
A genus of slender, lungless salamanders, native to North America and the type genus of the family Plethodontidae.
a genus of lungless salamanders

Examples

The Plethodon species are often found hiding under logs in moist forests.
Scientists discovered a new Plethodon salamander in the Appalachian Mountains.
Members of the Plethodon genus breathe through their skin, as they lack lungs.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Plethodontidae - the family of lungless salamanders

Etymology

From Greek 'plethos' (multitude) and 'odons' (tooth), referencing the numerous teeth of these salamanders.
