viomycin

1. NOUN

/ vaɪ.oʊˈmaɪ.sɪn / (AmE), / vaɪ.əʊˈmaɪ.sɪn / (BrE)
a basic polypeptide antibiotic, used (often with other drugs) to treat tuberculosis
antibiotic drug for tuberculosis
medical

Examples

The patient received an injection of viomycin as part of his tuberculosis treatment regimen.
Resistance to viomycin requires the use of alternative medications for effective therapy.
Doctors may prescribe viomycin along with other antibiotics to combat multi-drug resistant tuberculosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viocin - trade name for viomycin, antibiotic used to treat tuberculosis
