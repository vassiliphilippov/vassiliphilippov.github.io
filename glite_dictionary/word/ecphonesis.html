ecphonesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.foʊˈniː.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛk.fəˈniː.sɪs / (BrE)
An exclamatory rhetorical device, especially a sudden or emotional outcry used in speech or writing.
exclamatory rhetorical device

Examples

The ancient play features an ecphonesis that conveys the protagonist's despair.
He ended his speech with an ecphonesis to rally the crowd.
'O tempora! O mores!' is a classic example of ecphonesis in literature.

Synset Examples

exclamation such as ‘O tempore! O mores’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exclamation - a sudden cry or remark expressing surprise, strong emotion, or pain

Etymology

From Greek ekphōnēsis (an exclamation), from ek- (out) + phōnēsis (utterance).
