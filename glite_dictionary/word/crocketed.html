crocketed

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrɑːkɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈkrɒkɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a gable or spire) decorated with crockets, which are ornaments shaped like stylized curled leaves or foliage, typically found in Gothic architecture.
decorated with crockets (architectural)

Examples

The cathedral's crocketed spires towered gracefully above the city skyline.
Each crocketed pinnacle was adorned with intricate Gothic details.
Restoration work revealed the original crocketed gables beneath centuries of grime.

Synset Examples

a crocketed spire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crocket - an ornament in the form of stylized curled leaves, used in Gothic architecture

Etymology

From 'crocket', itself from Old Northern French croquet (a curl, hook).
