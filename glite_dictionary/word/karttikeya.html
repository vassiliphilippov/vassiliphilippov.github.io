Karttikeya

1. PROPN

/ kɑrˈtɪk.i.eɪ / (AmE), / kɑːˈtɪk.i.eɪ / (BrE)
In Hindu mythology, the god of war and bravery, commander-in-chief of the gods' army, and the son of Shiva and Parvati.
Hindu god of war and bravery

Examples

Many devotees worship Karttikeya during important Hindu festivals in southern India.
Stories about Karttikeya emphasize his valor and leadership in battle.
Karttikeya is often depicted riding a peacock and holding a spear.

Etymology

From Sanskrit Karttikeya, meaning 'son of the Pleiades' (Karttika), reflecting the month and star group linked to his birth.
