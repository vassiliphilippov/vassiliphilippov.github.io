trueness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtruːnəs / (AmE), / ˈtruːnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being loyal or faithful.
loyalty; faithfulness

Examples

Her trueness to her friends was admired by everyone in the community.
We can always count on his trueness in times of trouble.
They praised the trueness of his dedication to the cause.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loyalty - the quality of being loyal
  • Synonym: faithfulness - the quality of being faithful
  • Antonym: falseness - the quality of being untrue or disloyal

Etymology

From Old English 'trēowe' (faithful, trustworthy) + '-ness'.
