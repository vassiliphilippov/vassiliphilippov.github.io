abbot

1. NOUN

/ ˈæbət / (AmE), / ˈæbət / (BrE)
the head or superior of an abbey of monks, having authority over the religious community
head of a monastery of monks

Examples

The abbot led the monks in their daily prayers and rituals.
The newly elected abbot implemented reforms within the monastery.
During the medieval period, an abbot wielded significant influence in the region.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abbess - the female superior of a community of nuns

Etymology

From Latin abbas (father, abbot), from Greek abbas, from Aramaic abba (father).
