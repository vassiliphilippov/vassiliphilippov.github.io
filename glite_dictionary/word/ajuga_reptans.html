Ajuga reptans

1. NOUN

/ əˈdʒuːɡə ˈrɛptænz / (AmE), / əˈdʒuːɡə ˈrɛptanz / (BrE)
A low-growing European plant with spikes of blue flowers, commonly used as ground cover in gardens and naturalized in parts of the United States.
a blue-flowered ground cover plant

Examples

Ajuga reptans quickly spreads to form a dense carpet of blue in shady garden areas.
Gardeners often choose Ajuga reptans for its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
The vibrant blue flowers of Ajuga reptans provide early spring color in many landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bugleweed - common name for Ajuga reptans

Etymology

From Latin: 'ajuga' (a plant genus) and 'reptans' (creeping), referring to its growth habit.
