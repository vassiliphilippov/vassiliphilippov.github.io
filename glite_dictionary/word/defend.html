to defend

1. VERB

/ dɪˈfɛnd / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛnd / (BrE)
to protect oneself or someone else from attack; to act to prevent harm or danger
protect against attack or harm

Examples

The soldiers tried to defend the city from enemy forces.
She learned karate to better defend herself if necessary.
They were forced to defend their decision during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: attack - to try to hurt or damage someone or something

Etymology

From Old French *defendre*, from Latin *defendere* (to ward off, protect).
