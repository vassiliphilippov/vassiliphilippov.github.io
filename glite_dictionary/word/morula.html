morula

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔr.jʊ.lə / (AmE), / ˈmɒr.jʊ.lə / (BrE)
a solid ball of cells (blastomeres) resulting from the division of a fertilized egg, preceding the blastula stage in embryonic development
early embryonic stage; solid cell mass
medical

Examples

The morula is formed within days after the egg is fertilized.
Scientists observed the transition from morula to blastula under the microscope.
If the morula does not develop properly, embryonic growth can be compromised.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: blastula - the next stage of embryonic development after the morula; a hollow sphere of cells

Etymology

From Latin 'morum' (mulberry), referring to its appearance.
