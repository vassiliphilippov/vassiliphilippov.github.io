bigarade

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡəˈrɑd / (AmE), / bɪɡəˈrɑːd / (BrE)
a variety of orange tree producing sour or bitter fruit, commonly used as grafting stock
a type of bitter orange tree

Examples

The bigarade is often used as a rootstock for sweet orange trees.
Many marmalades are made from bigarade fruit due to its tart flavor.
Gardeners prefer the bigarade for its disease resistance when grafting citrus varieties.

Etymology

From French 'bigarade', referring to the bitter orange.
