authentic

1. ADJ

/ əˈθɛntɪk / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɛntɪk / (BrE)
worthy of belief because it is based on fact; reliable and accurate
factually reliable or accurate

Examples

The journalist provided an authentic account of the events, which matched other eyewitness reports.
Before using the data, we checked its source to ensure it was authentic.
Her biography relied on authentic letters and documents for accuracy.

Synset Examples

an authentic account by an eyewitness
reliable information

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be; authentic
  • Synonym: reliable - consistently good in quality or trustworthy
  • Antonym: fake - not genuine; counterfeit

Etymology

From Greek authentikos (genuine, original), via Latin authenticus.

2. ADJ

/ əˈθɛntɪk / (AmE), / ɔːˈθentɪk / (BrE)
genuine; not a copy or fake, and believed to be real or true.
genuine; not a copy or fake

Examples

The museum displayed an authentic ancient Greek vase that had never left the country before.
He wanted to buy an authentic signature from a famous writer, not a forged one.
Only authentic products will be accepted by the quality inspectors.

Synset Examples

an authentic signature
a bona fide manuscript
an unquestionable antique
photographs taken in a veritable bull ring

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be; authentic
  • Antonym: counterfeit - made in imitation so as to be passed off fraudulently; not genuine
  • Antonym: fake - not genuine; a copy meant to deceive

Etymology

From Greek authentikos (original, genuine) via Latin authenticus.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01119377-s
Synset POS: s
