repellant

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈpɛl(ə)nt / (AmE), / rɪˈpel.ənt / (BrE)
A chemical substance used to deter or drive away animals, especially pests or insects.
chemical that repels animals or insects

Examples

We sprayed a repellant around the campsite to keep mosquitoes away.
Many gardeners use a natural repellant to protect their plants from rabbits.
The product acts as a powerful repellant against ticks and fleas on pets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repellent - a substance that repels insects, animals, etc.; alternate spelling

Etymology

From Latin repellere (to drive back) via French 'repellant', related to 'repel'.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈpɛlənt / (AmE), / rɪˈpɛlənt / (BrE)
A substance or compound applied to fabrics to make them resistant to water penetration.
compound making fabric water-resistant

Examples

The outdoor jacket is coated with a repellant to keep you dry during rain.
Scientists developed a new repellant to improve the water resistance of sportswear.
Many umbrellas use a chemical repellant on the fabric for better protection against moisture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waterproofing - the process of making something resistant to water

Etymology

From repel + -ant, meaning 'serving to repel'.
