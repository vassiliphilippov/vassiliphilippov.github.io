Amanita verna

1. NOUN

/ əˈmænɪtə ˈvɜrnə / (AmE), / əˈmænɪtə ˈvɜːnə / (BrE)
a highly poisonous white mushroom of the genus Amanita, closely related to the death cap (Amanita phalloides) and often called the 'destroying angel'
a deadly poisonous mushroom species

Examples

The forager was alarmed to find an Amanita verna growing near the walking trail.
People are strongly warned not to eat Amanita verna because of its deadly toxins.
The Amanita verna is sometimes mistaken for edible mushrooms due to its innocent white appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Amanita phalloides - a deadly poisonous mushroom known as the death cap

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Amanita and species name verna meaning 'of spring.'
