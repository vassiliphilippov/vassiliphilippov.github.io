bodkin

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑd.kɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɒd.kɪn / (BrE)
a blunt, thick needle used for threading ribbon, tape, or cord through loops or hems
blunt needle for threading ribbon

Examples

She used a bodkin to thread the new ribbon through the waistband of the skirt.
The sewing kit contained a bodkin along with other useful tools.
A bodkin makes it much easier to pull elastic through a narrow casing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: needle - a small, slender tool with a sharp point, used for sewing

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'bodkyn', of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːdkɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɒdkɪn / (BrE)
a small, sharp-pointed tool used for making holes in leather or fabric, or for drawing tape or cord through a hem
small pointed tool for making holes in fabric or leather

Examples

She used a bodkin to thread the elastic through the waistband.
The leatherworker made a clean hole with his bodkin.
A bodkin is essential for certain sewing projects.

Etymology

From Middle English 'bodkin', of uncertain origin.

Related Terms

4. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːd.kɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɒd.kɪn / (BrE)
a long, slender hairpin, often with a decorative head, used to fasten hair; especially in historical or literary contexts
long, ornamental hairpin
archaic

Examples

In the portrait, the woman wears her hair up, secured with a jeweled bodkin.
The heroine drew a silver bodkin from her hair and used it to unlock the locket.
Antique dealers often sell ornate bodkins as collectible jewelry items.

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; recorded since the 14th century in Middle English as 'bodkyn', possibly from Welsh or Irish.
