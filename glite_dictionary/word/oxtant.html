oxtant

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑks.tənt / (AmE), / ˈɒks.tənt / (BrE)
A unit of angular distance equal to half a quadrant, or 45 degrees.
unit of angular distance; 45 degrees

Examples

In ancient astronomy, an oxtant was sometimes used to measure star positions.
The angle measured was precisely one oxtant from the starting point.
Dividing the circle into eight parts gives you segments of an oxtant each.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: quadrant - a quarter of a circle; 90-degree angle
