hortative

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɔr.tə.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈhɔː.tə.tɪv / (BrE)
expressing strong encouragement or urging someone to take action
strongly encouraging or urging

Examples

The principal made a hortative speech urging students to work harder for their exams.
Her tone became hortative as she encouraged the team to keep pushing toward their goal.
This document adopts a hortative style, emphasizing action and participation by all employees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhortative - strongly encouraging or urging
  • Antonym: discouraging - causing someone not to do something

Etymology

From Latin 'hortativus', from 'hortari' (to exhort, encourage).
