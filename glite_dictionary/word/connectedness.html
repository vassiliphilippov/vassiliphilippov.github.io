connectedness

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnɛktɪdnəs / (AmE), / kəˈnɛktɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the state of being connected or joined; the quality or condition of having links, relationships, or coherence with something or someone
state of being connected

Examples

The connectedness of the internet has made it easier to communicate worldwide.
Her sense of connectedness to the community grew stronger after she volunteered at the local center.
Scientists studied the connectedness of different species within the ecosystem.

Synset Examples

the connection between church and state is inescapable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connection - a link or relationship between people, things, or ideas
  • Antonym: disconnectedness - the state of being disconnected or separate

2. NOUN

/ kəˈnɛktɪdnəs / (AmE), / kəˈnɛktɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being connected, or of having a relationship between things or events
state of being connected

Examples

The connectedness of the two incidents became clearer during the investigation.
Many people today feel a lack of connectedness in their communities.
Scientists study the connectedness between environmental factors and disease outbreaks.

Synset Examples

there was a connection between eating that pickle and having that nightmare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connection - the act of connecting or the state of being connected
  • Antonym: disconnection - the state of being disconnected or separated

Etymology

From 'connect' + '-edness', formed in English.
