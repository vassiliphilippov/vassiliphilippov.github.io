bushwhacker

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊʃˌwækər / (AmE), / ˈbʊʃˌwækə / (BrE)
(disparaging) an unsophisticated or unrefined person, especially one from a rural or remote area.
unsophisticated rural person (disparaging)

Examples

He moved to the city and was unfairly called a bushwhacker by some of his colleagues.
The film portrays the main character as a naive bushwhacker struggling with urban life.
Though some considered him a bushwhacker, he was proud of his rural roots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hillbilly - an unsophisticated person from a rural area, especially in the U.S. Appalachians/Ozarks
  • Antonym: urbanite - a person who lives in a city or town, often implying sophistication compared to rural people

Etymology

From 'bushwhack' (to live or travel in wild country) and the agent suffix '-er'; originally referred to guerrilla fighters in the American Civil War before acquiring extended senses.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʊʃˌwækər / (AmE), / ˈbʊʃˌwækə / (BrE)
a Confederate guerrilla fighter who engaged in irregular warfare during the American Civil War
Confederate guerrilla in the Civil War

Examples

During the Civil War, the region was overrun by bushwhackers who struck without warning.
His great-grandfather was rumored to have been a notorious bushwhacker in Missouri.
The film portrayed the life of a bushwhacker operating behind enemy lines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guerrilla - a member of a small independent group taking part in irregular fighting
  • Antonym: jayhawker - a Unionist guerrilla fighter in Kansas or Missouri during the American Civil War

Etymology

From 'bushwhack' (to ambush from the bush) + '-er'; originally American English, popularized during the Civil War era.
