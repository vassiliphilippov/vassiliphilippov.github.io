occupied

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑːkjupaɪd / (AmE), / ˈɒkjʊpaɪd / (BrE)
(of a space, seat, or position) being held, filled, or in use by someone.
being held, filled, or in use

Examples

The restroom is currently occupied, so you'll have to wait a few minutes.
Every seat was occupied when the performance started.
She found her favorite table occupied by another group.

Synset Examples

she keeps her time well occupied
the wc is occupied

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vacant - not filled, occupied, or in use
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01627368-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈɑːkjəˌpaɪd / (AmE), / ˈɒkjʊˌpaɪd / (BrE)
busy or actively involved in an activity, so as not to be free to do something else
busy or engaged

Examples

She was occupied with paperwork and couldn't answer the phone right away.
He managed to keep the children occupied during the long trip with games and puzzles.
My mind has been occupied with thoughts of the upcoming exam all week.

Synset Examples

she keeps herself fully occupied with volunteer activities
deeply engaged in conversation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: busy - actively engaged in work or activity
  • Antonym: free - not occupied; available or not busy

Etymology

From Latin 'occupatus', past participle of 'occupare' (to seize, engage, or take possession of).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00294477-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈɑːkjəˌpaɪd / (AmE), / ˈɒkjʊˌpaɪd / (BrE)
(of a room, building, or land) being lived in, used, or leased by someone; having tenants or inhabitants.
having tenants or inhabitants

Examples

Most of the occupied apartments in the building were well maintained.
The house has remained occupied since it was built a century ago.
During the festival, every hotel room in town was occupied.

Synset Examples

not all the occupied (or tenanted) apartments were well kept up

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vacant - (of a room, seat, or property) not occupied; empty

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'occupy', from Latin occupare (to seize).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01314769-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈɑːkjəˌpaɪd / (AmE), / ˈɒkjʊˌpaɪd / (BrE)
(of a country, region, or area) seized and controlled by a foreign military force as a result of invasion.
under foreign military control after invasion

Examples

During the war, many civilians fled the occupied territories to seek safety elsewhere.
International law sets out rules for how an occupied area should be governed.
All communication was censored in the occupied city after the invasion.

Synset Examples

the occupied countries of Europe

Related Terms

  • Antonym: liberated - freed from enemy occupation or control

Etymology

From occupy + -ed, meaning 'taken control of'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01628448-a
Synset POS: a
