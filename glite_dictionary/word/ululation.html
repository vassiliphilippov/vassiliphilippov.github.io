ululation

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌljuˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌjuːljuːˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a long, loud, high-pitched cry or wail expressing strong emotion, commonly used in some cultures at celebrations or mourning
long, emotional wailing cry

Examples

The mourners' ululation echoed through the village during the funeral procession.
With a sudden ululation of joy, the fans cheered their team's victory.
Her piercing ululation marked the beginning of the traditional ceremony.

Synset Examples

he gave a howl of pain
howls of laughter
their howling had no effect

Related Terms

  • Synonym: howl - a long, loud cry expressing pain, anger, or amusement
  • Synonym: wail - a prolonged high-pitched cry of pain, grief, or anger

Etymology

From Latin ululatio (a howling or wailing).
