denouncement

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈnaʊnsmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈnaʊnsmənt / (BrE)
the act of publicly criticizing or accusing someone or something strongly and openly
public act of criticizing or accusing

Examples

The activist's denouncement of corruption was broadcast on national television.
There was a public denouncement of the new law by several community leaders.
His speech included a strong denouncement of violence in all its forms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: denunciation - public condemnation or accusation

Etymology

From French "dénoncement", from "dénoncer" (to denounce), from Latin "denuntiare" (to announce, warn).
