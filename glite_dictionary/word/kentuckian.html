Kentuckian

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtʌkiən / (AmE), / kənˈtʌkiən / (BrE)
A native or resident of the state of Kentucky in the United States.
person from Kentucky, USA

Examples

Many famous athletes are proud to call themselves Kentuckian.
As a lifelong Kentuckian, she grew up surrounded by bluegrass and bourbon.
The festival is a beloved tradition for every Kentuckian in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kentucky - a state in the eastern United States

Etymology

From Kentucky, the U.S. state, plus the suffix -ian denoting origin or association.
