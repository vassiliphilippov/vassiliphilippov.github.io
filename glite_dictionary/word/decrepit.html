decrepit

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈkrɛpɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈkrɛpɪt / (BrE)
lacking physical strength or vitality, especially because of old age or infirmity; worn out or weakened
lacking strength due to age or weakness

Examples

The decrepit old house looked as if it would collapse at any moment.
After the long illness, he appeared decrepit and frail, barely able to walk across the room.
The shelter cared for many decrepit dogs abandoned by their owners.

Synset Examples

a feeble old woman
her body looked sapless

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feeble - lacking physical strength, especially due to age or illness
  • Antonym: vigorous - strong, healthy, and full of energy

Etymology

From Latin 'decrepitus' (very old, infirm), from 'de-' (down) + 'crepitare' (to crack, rattle).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈkrɛpɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈkrɛpɪt / (BrE)
Worn out or ruined because of age or neglect; in poor condition due to long use or lack of maintenance.
old and in poor condition

Examples

The abandoned house at the end of the street looked decrepit and haunted.
He drove a decrepit car that barely made it up the hill.
Over the years, the factory became increasingly decrepit until it was eventually demolished.

Synset Examples

a creaky shack
a decrepit bus … its seats held together with friction tape
a flea-bitten sofa
a run-down neighborhood
a woebegone old shack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dilapidated - in a state of ruin or disrepair, especially as a result of age or neglect
  • Synonym: run-down - in bad condition due to lack of care or attention
  • Antonym: new - recently made or in good condition; not old or worn

Etymology

From Latin 'decrepitus' (very old, infirm, feeble).
