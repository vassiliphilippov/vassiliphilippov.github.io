lustre

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌstər / (AmE), / ˈlʌstə / (BrE)
a thin, often metallic, surface coating applied to ceramics or porcelain to produce a shiny or iridescent effect
shiny coating for ceramics

Examples

The artist applied a gold lustre to the vase to give it a brilliant sheen.
Many antique plates feature a delicate lustre that shimmers in the light.
This beautiful cup was finished with a pearly lustre to enhance its elegance.

Etymology

From Old French *lustre* (brightness, radiance), from Latin *lustrare* (to make bright).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlʌstər / (AmE), / ˈlʌstə / (BrE)
a gentle sheen or soft glow, especially that of a partly reflective surface
a soft, shining light or glow

Examples

The moonlight gave the lake a mesmerizing lustre that drew everyone's attention.
He polished the silverware until it regained its original lustre.
Her hair had a healthy lustre that sparkled in the sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheen - a soft, smooth shine on a surface
  • Antonym: dullness - lack of brightness or shine

Etymology

From Latin 'lustrare' (to illuminate) via Old French 'lustre'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈlʌstər / (AmE), / ˈlʌstə / (BrE)
A quality or appearance of brightness, brilliance, or radiance that makes something attractive or impressive.
a quality of brilliance or radiance

Examples

The polished marble surface had a deep lustre that caught everyone's eye.
Winning the award added lustre to her already impressive reputation.
After years of neglect, the silver lost its lustre and looked faded.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shine - brightness caused by reflected light
  • Antonym: dullness - lack of brightness or radiance

Etymology

From Latin 'lustrum' (illumination), via French 'lustre'.
