inauspicious

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnɔːˈspɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˌɪnɔːˈspɪʃəs / (BrE)
not giving or indicating a good chance of success; suggesting a bad outcome or ill fortune.
not suggesting success or good fortune

Examples

The ceremony got off to an inauspicious start when it began to rain heavily.
They considered the dark clouds an inauspicious sign for the marathon.
Her first day at work was rather inauspicious, as she arrived late due to traffic.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: auspicious - suggesting a positive or successful outcome
  • Synonym: unlucky - having or marked by bad luck

Etymology

From Latin inauspicatus (unlucky, unfavorable), from in- (not) + auspicatus (auspicious).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnɔˈspɪʃəs / (AmE), / ɪnɔːˈspɪʃəs / (BrE)
indicating that something is likely to lead to failure or bad luck; not promising success.
not likely to bring success or good luck

Examples

The meeting began at an inauspicious time, just as a thunderstorm started outside.
Their first encounter was rather inauspicious, but they eventually became good friends.
Launching a new product in a recession is considered inauspicious by many business experts.
