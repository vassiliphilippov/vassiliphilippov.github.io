Sphenodon punctatum

1. NOUN

/ sfəˈnoʊdɑn pʌŋkˈteɪtəm / (AmE), / sfɪˈnəʊdɒn pʌŋkˈteɪtəm / (BrE)
a species of reptile native to New Zealand, the only living member of its order, closely resembling lizards but belonging to a distinct lineage
a reptile species native to New Zealand

Examples

The Sphenodon punctatum has remained virtually unchanged for millions of years.
Conservation efforts have helped protect the rare Sphenodon punctatum from extinction.
Scientists study the Sphenodon punctatum to learn about ancient reptilian lineages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuatara - common name for Sphenodon punctatum, a reptile endemic to New Zealand

Etymology

From Greek 'sphen' (wedge) and 'odous' (tooth), with Latin 'punctatum' (spotted).
