contaminative

1. ADJ

/ kənˈtæməˌneɪtɪv / (AmE), / kənˈtæmɪnətɪv / (BrE)
causing contamination; making something impure by contact or mixing with something harmful or undesirable
causing contamination or impurity

Examples

The contaminative effects of industrial waste have devastated local rivers.
Improper disposal of chemicals is a contaminative practice that threatens public health.
Regular cleaning helps prevent the spread of contaminative substances in laboratories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contaminating - causing something to become impure
  • Antonym: purifying - removing impurities or contaminants

Etymology

From Latin 'contaminare' (to pollute, defile) + '-ive'.
