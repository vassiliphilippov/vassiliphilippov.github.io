Schizopetalon

1. NOUN

/ skɪˌzoʊpɪˈtælən / (AmE), / skɪˌzɒpɪˈtælən / (BrE)
A small genus of South American herbs cultivated for their showy flowers, belonging to the family Brassicaceae.
South American flowering plant genus

Examples

Several species of Schizopetalon are grown as ornamental plants in gardens.
The delicate flowers of Schizopetalon are often featured in botanical exhibits.
Gardeners in temperate zones sometimes cultivate Schizopetalon for its unique appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Brassicaceae - The plant family to which Schizopetalon belongs

Etymology

From Greek 'schizo' (split) and 'petalon' (petal), referring to the divided petals of its flowers.
