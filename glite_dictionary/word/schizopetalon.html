schizopetalon

1. NOUN

/ ˌskɪzoʊˈpɛtəlɒn / (AmE), / ˌskɪtsəʊˈpɛtəlɒn / (BrE)
A South American annual plant with deeply divided leaves and spikes of fringed, almond-scented purple-white flowers.
South American annual flowering plant

Examples

The delicate schizopetalon flourished in the greenhouse despite the cold outdoors.
Gardeners are drawn to the unique fringed blooms of the schizopetalon.
A lovely scent filled the air as the schizopetalon came into full bloom.

Etymology

From Greek 'schizo-' (to split) and 'petalon' (petal), referring to its divided petals.
