benzylpenicillin

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɛn.zɪl.pəˈnɪl.ɪn / (AmE), / ˌbɛn.zɪl.pəˈnɪl.ɪn / (BrE)
a type of penicillin used as an antibiotic to treat bacterial infections; also known as penicillin G
a penicillin antibiotic; penicillin G
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed benzylpenicillin to treat the patient's severe bacterial infection.
Allergic reactions to benzylpenicillin can occur in some individuals.
Benzylpenicillin is commonly administered by injection in hospital settings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penicillin - a group of antibiotics produced from Penicillium molds

Etymology

From 'benzyl' (the chemical group) + 'penicillin'.
