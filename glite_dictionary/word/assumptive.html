assumptive

1. ADJ

/ əˈsʌmptɪv / (AmE), / əˈsʌmptɪv / (BrE)
Accepted as true or real without proof; based on assumption rather than evidence.
accepted as true without proof

Examples

His assumptive explanation for being late was questioned by his boss.
The team's assumptive attitude led them to overlook crucial evidence.
She made several assumptive statements without any supporting data.

Synset Examples

the assumed reason for his absence
assumptive beliefs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: presumptive - Based on probability or presumption rather than certain evidence
  • Synonym: speculative - Based on conjecture rather than knowledge
  • Antonym: evident - Clearly seen or understood; obvious

Etymology

From Latin assumptivus (of taking up), from assumere (to take up).

2. ADJ

/ əˈsʌmp.tɪv / (AmE), / əˈsʌmp.tɪv / (BrE)
behaving in a way that is too confident or forward, especially by taking things for granted without justification
excessively forward or presumptuous

Examples

The new intern was criticized for being too assumptive during team meetings.
Her assumptive behavior made others uncomfortable at the dinner party.
It is important not to come across as assumptive when dealing with clients.

Synset Examples

an assumptive person
on a subject like this it would be too assuming for me to decide
the duchess would not put up with presumptuous servants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: presumptuous - showing too much confidence or taking liberties beyond one's place
  • Synonym: assuming - having or showing an arrogant attitude
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance

Etymology

From Latin assumptivus (of or pertaining to taking up or to assumption).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00207288-s
Synset POS: s
