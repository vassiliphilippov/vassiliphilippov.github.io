Dryopteris noveboracensis

1. NOUN

/ draɪˈɑptərɪs ˌnoʊvəbɔːrəˈsɛnsɪs / (AmE), / draɪˈɒptərɪs ˌnəʊvəbɒrəˈsɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A species of slender shield fern found in moist woods of eastern North America, sometimes classified within the genus Dryopteris.
slender shield fern of eastern North America

Examples

The Dryopteris noveboracensis is commonly found in shady, moist woodlands across the eastern United States.
Gardeners often choose Dryopteris noveboracensis for its delicate fronds and ability to thrive in damp soil.
During our hike, we identified several clumps of Dryopteris noveboracensis beneath the dense forest canopy.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Dryopteris' from Greek 'dryas' (oak) + 'pteris' (fern); 'noveboracensis' refers to New York (Nova Eboracum in Latin).
