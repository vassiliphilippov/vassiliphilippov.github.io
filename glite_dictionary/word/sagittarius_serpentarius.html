Sagittarius serpentarius

1. NOUN

/ sæˌɡɪtɛɹiəs sɚˌpɛnˈtɛɹiəs / (AmE), / sæˌɡɪtʃərɪəs sɜːˌpɛnˈteəriəs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the secretary bird, a large long-legged African bird of prey that hunts and feeds mainly on reptiles.
scientific name: secretary bird

Examples

The Sagittarius serpentarius is renowned for its striking appearance and unique method of hunting snakes.
Scientists observed the hunting behavior of Sagittarius serpentarius in the grasslands of southern Africa.
Unlike other birds of prey, Sagittarius serpentarius prefers walking on the ground to soar in the sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secretary bird - large African bird of prey known for hunting snakes

Etymology

From Latin: Sagittarius (archer) and serpentarius (pertaining to snakes).
