gargoylism

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrˌɡɔɪlˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːɡɔɪlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A rare hereditary disorder caused by a defect in mucopolysaccharide metabolism, resulting in severe skeletal abnormalities and intellectual disability.
hereditary disorder with skeletal and mental abnormalities
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the child's symptoms were consistent with gargoylism.
Historically, gargoylism referred to what is now known as Hurler syndrome.
Research into treatments for gargoylism has led to new advances in genetic therapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hurler syndrome - a type of mucopolysaccharidosis with similar symptoms

Etymology

From 'gargoyle', referencing the facial features associated with the disorder, plus '-ism'.
