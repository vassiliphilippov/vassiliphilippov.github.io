carven

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑːr.vən / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.vən / (BrE)
(archaic or literary) made by carving; shaped or formed by cutting into a surface.
shaped by carving (archaic/literary)
archaic

Examples

The statue appeared so lifelike, as if it were carven from marble by a master sculptor.
Above the ancient doorway stood a carven lion, weathered by centuries of rain.
She found her name carven into the old wooden bench, surrounded by other memories.

Synset Examples

the carved fretwork
an intricately carved door
stood as if carven from stone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carved - made by carving, formed by cutting

Etymology

Archaic past participle of 'carve', from Old English *ceorfan* (to cut).
