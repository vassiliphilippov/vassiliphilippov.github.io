Angus

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋɡəs / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡəs / (BrE)
a breed of black, hornless cattle that originated in Scotland and is valued for its beef
black hornless cattle breed from Scotland

Examples

The restaurant proudly serves premium Angus beef burgers.
Many farmers choose Angus cattle for their superior meat quality and adaptability.
The Angus breed is well known for its marbled beef and gentle temperament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hereford - a breed of red and white cattle popular for beef production

Etymology

Named after the county of Angus in Scotland, where the breed was developed.

2. PROPN

/ ˈæŋɡəs / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡəs / (BrE)
a god of love and beauty in Celtic mythology, regarded as a patron of young men and women
Celtic god of love and beauty

Examples

The ancient Celts believed that Angus would help young lovers find happiness.
Many legends tell of Angus transforming into a swan to pursue his beloved.
Angus was celebrated in festivals dedicated to love and youth.

Etymology

From Old Irish 'Oengus', meaning 'one strength' or 'unique strength'.
