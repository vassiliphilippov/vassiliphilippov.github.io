Hakea

1. NOUN

/ heɪˈkiːə / (AmE), / həˈkiːə / (BrE)
a genus of Australian shrubs and small trees, typically with evergreen, often spiny leaves and dense clusters of showy flowers
genus of Australian shrubs and trees

Examples

There are several species of Hakea in the coastal regions of Australia.
The distinctive leaves of Hakea make it popular in native gardens.
Botanists discovered a new variety of Hakea during their expedition.

Etymology

Named after Baron Christian Ludwig von Hake, a German patron of botany.
