Malecite

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæləˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmalɪˌsaɪt / (BrE)
a member of the Algonquian people native to northeastern Maine and New Brunswick
member of Algonquian people, Maine/New Brunswick

Examples

The Malecite have maintained many of their traditions despite centuries of colonial pressure.
She is researching the history of the Malecite people in New Brunswick.
A group of Malecite elders gathered to share stories from their community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Maliseet - alternative term for a member of this Algonquian people

Etymology

From French 'Malécite', likely via Mi'kmaq or Maliseet language.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæləˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmalɪˌsʌɪt / (BrE)
the Algonquian language spoken by the Malecite and Passamaquoddy peoples of northeastern North America
Algonquian language of Malecite and Passamaquoddy

Examples

Few people today are fluent speakers of Malecite.
The traditional stories were passed down in the Malecite language.
She is studying Malecite to preserve her cultural heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Passamaquoddy - the closely related Algonquian language of the Passamaquoddy people

Etymology

From French 'Malécite', likely from a Maliseet autonym or earlier indigenous terms.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06923293-n
Synset POS: n
