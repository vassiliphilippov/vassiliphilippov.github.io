Gerea canescens

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈriːə kəˈnɛsɛnz / (AmE), / dʒəˈrɪə kəˈnɛsɛnz / (BrE)
A slender, hairy plant with few leaves and golden-yellow flower heads, native to sandy desert areas of southeastern California to southwestern Utah, western Arizona, and northwestern Mexico.
desert plant with yellow flowers

Examples

Gerea canescens is often found blooming in the spring across the deserts of California and Arizona.
Botanists studied the drought resistance of Gerea canescens in its natural habitat.
The hairy stems and golden flower heads help identify Gerea canescens among other desert species.

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Gerea' and species name 'canescens' meaning 'becoming gray' or 'hoary'.
