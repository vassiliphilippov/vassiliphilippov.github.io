viable

1. ADJ

/ ˈvaɪəbəl / (AmE), / ˈvaɪ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
capable of living, developing, or germinating under normal conditions
capable of life or growth

Examples

The scientist examined whether the viable embryos could be successfully implanted.
Only viable seeds will germinate and produce healthy plants.
The doctor confirmed that the fetus was viable after twenty-four weeks of gestation.

Synset Examples

viable seeds
a viable fetus

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonviable - not capable of living, growing, or developing

Etymology

From French *viable* (able to live), from vie (life), from Latin *vita* (life).

2. ADJ

/ ˈvaɪ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈvaɪ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
capable of being done, achieved, or implemented successfully given existing conditions or resources
able to be done or achieved

Examples

This strategy is not a viable solution to the company's financial problems.
Scientists are working to develop viable alternatives to fossil fuels.
Given our current resources, building a new stadium is simply not viable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feasible - possible to do easily or conveniently
  • Antonym: impracticable - not able to be put into practice or carried out

Etymology

From French 'viable' (capable of life), from 'vie' (life), from Latin 'vita'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01828067-s
Synset POS: s
