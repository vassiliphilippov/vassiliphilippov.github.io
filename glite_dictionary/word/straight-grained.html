straight-grained

1. ADJ

/ streɪt ˈɡreɪnd / (AmE), / streɪt ˈɡreɪnd / (BrE)
(of wood or other material) having fibers or grain that run parallel to each other, not twisted or wavy.
having parallel fibers or grain (esp. wood)

Examples

This table is made from straight-grained oak, giving it a smooth and uniform appearance.
Carpenters prefer straight-grained timber because it is easier to cut and less likely to split.
The flooring's straight-grained pattern adds a clean, modern look to the room.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cross-grained - (of wood) with fibers running at an angle to the main axis
