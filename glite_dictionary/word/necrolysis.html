necrolysis

1. NOUN

/ nɪˈkrɑlɪsɪs / (AmE), / nɛˈkrɒlɪsɪs / (BrE)
the disintegration and dissolution of dead tissue in the body, especially as a result of a disease process or burn
breakdown of dead tissue
medical

Examples

Widespread necrolysis was observed in the patient's burned tissue.
The doctor explained that necrolysis can occur as a complication of severe skin reactions.
Treatment focused on removing areas affected by necrolysis to prevent infection.

Etymology

From Greek nekros (dead body) + -lysis (loosening, dissolution).
