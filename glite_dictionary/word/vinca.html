Vinca

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪŋkə / (AmE), / ˈvɪŋkə / (BrE)
A genus of low, creeping evergreen perennial plants, commonly known as periwinkle, often grown as groundcover.
genus of creeping periwinkle plants

Examples

Vinca is often used as a ground cover in shaded garden areas.
The glossy leaves of Vinca stay green throughout the winter.
Gardeners appreciate Vinca for its resilience and attractive blue flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: periwinkle - Common name for plants of the genus Vinca

Etymology

From Latin 'vinca', the name of the plant.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɪŋkə / (AmE), / ˈvɪŋkə / (BrE)
a genus of low, creeping evergreen perennial plants, commonly known as periwinkles, often grown as groundcover for their attractive flowers
genus of evergreen plants; periwinkles

Examples

The Vinca in her garden produces delicate blue flowers every spring.
Landscapers often recommend Vinca as a hardy groundcover for shady areas.
We planted Vinca along the walkway to add color and prevent weeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: periwinkle - a common name for several flowering plants of the genus Vinca

Etymology

From Latin *vincire* (to bind), referring to the plant’s trailing stems.
