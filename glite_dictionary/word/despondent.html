despondent

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈspɑndənt / (AmE), / dɪˈspɒndənt / (BrE)
in low spirits from loss of hope or courage; feeling or showing deep discouragement or dejection
feeling hopeless or dejected

Examples

After losing his job, Mark felt completely despondent and struggled to find motivation each day.
The team's repeated failures left the coach looking despondent on the sidelines.
She grew despondent when she realized her efforts had gone unnoticed.

Synset Examples

despondent about his failure
too heartsick to fight back

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dejected - sad and depressed; dispirited
  • Antonym: hopeful - feeling or inspiring optimism about a future event
  • Synonym: downcast - feeling despondent or discouraged

Etymology

From Latin despondent-, present participle of despondere (to give up, lose heart).
