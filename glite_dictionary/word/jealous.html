jealous

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɛl.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛl.əs / (BrE)
feeling or showing suspicion or fear that a rival may take away a person’s affection or attention, especially in a romantic context
suspicious or fearful of rival in relationships

Examples

His jealous behavior caused problems in their relationship.
She grew jealous when she saw her friend talking to someone else.
A jealous lover might constantly check their partner's phone.

Synset Examples

a jealous lover

Related Terms

  • Synonym: envious - feeling resentful longing for what someone else has
  • Antonym: trusting - showing confidence in others; not suspicious

Etymology

From Old French jalous, from Late Latin zelosus ‘full of zeal’, from Greek zēlos ‘zeal’

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɛl.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛl.əs / (BrE)
feeling or showing resentment towards someone because of their achievements, possessions, or advantages
resentful of another's success or advantages

Examples

She felt jealous of her colleague's promotion at work.
Many people can become jealous when someone else gets the attention they want.
He grew jealous of his neighbor's beautiful new car.

Synset Examples

he was never covetous before he met her
jealous of his success and covetous of his possessions
envious of their art collection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: envious - feeling or showing envy for what someone else has
  • Synonym: covetous - having a great desire to possess something belonging to someone else
  • Antonym: generous - willing to give and share with others

Etymology

From Old French jalous, from Late Latin zelōsus (full of zeal).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00892058-s
Synset POS: s
