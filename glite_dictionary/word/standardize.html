to standardize

1. VERB

/ ˈstændərˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈstændədaɪz / (BrE)
to make something conform to a set standard or norm, often for consistency or uniformity
make conform to a standard

Examples

The company decided to standardize its procedures across all branches.
Many countries have moved to standardize the size of electrical outlets.
New regulations require manufacturers to standardize labels for nutritional information.

Synset Examples

The weights and measures were standardized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to normalize - make normal or bring to standard condition

Etymology

From 'standard' + '-ize'; modelled after the French 'standardiser'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00468630-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈstændərˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈstændədaɪz / (BrE)
to evaluate or cause to conform to a standard by comparison with a set norm or criterion
evaluate by or adapt to a standard

Examples

Many countries have tried to standardize their testing procedures to ensure fairness.
To accurately compare the results, laboratories must standardize their measurement methods.
The company decided to standardize all its products to meet international guidelines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to normalize - bring or return to a standard condition
  • Antonym: to customize - modify according to individual requirements, opposite of making standard

Etymology

From 'standard' + '-ize', meaning to make conform to a standard.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00684355-v
Synset POS: v
