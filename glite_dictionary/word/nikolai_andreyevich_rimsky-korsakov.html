Nikolai Andreyevich Rimsky-Korsakov

1. PROPN

/ nɪˈkoʊlaɪ ˌændreɪˈɛvɪtʃ ˈrɪmski kɔrˈsɑːkəf / (AmE), / nɪˈkɒlaɪ ˌændreɪˈɛvɪtʃ ˈrɪmski kɔːˈsɑːkɒf / (BrE)
(1844–1908) a Russian composer known for his operas and orchestral works, often incorporating themes from Russian folk music.
Russian composer (1844–1908)

Examples

Many music students study Nikolai Andreyevich Rimsky-Korsakov's orchestration techniques in their courses.
The opera 'The Tale of Tsar Saltan' was composed by Nikolai Andreyevich Rimsky-Korsakov in the late 19th century.
Nikolai Andreyevich Rimsky-Korsakov was known for weaving Russian folk melodies into his symphonic works.
