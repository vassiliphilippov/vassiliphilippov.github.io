Aedes

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːdiz / (AmE), / ˈiːdiːz / (BrE)
A genus of mosquitoes that includes species responsible for transmitting diseases such as yellow fever, dengue, and Zika virus.
a genus of disease-carrying mosquitoes

Examples

Aedes mosquitoes are well known for their role in spreading deadly diseases such as dengue fever.
The city launched a campaign to eliminate Aedes breeding sites to prevent an outbreak.
Scientists study Aedes populations to track the risk of yellow fever and Zika virus transmission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mosquito - an insect that feeds on blood and can transmit diseases

Etymology

From Greek aēdēs (unpleasant).

2. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.iːdiːz / (AmE), / ˈiːdiːz / (BrE)
A genus of mosquitoes that includes several species responsible for transmitting diseases such as yellow fever and dengue.
a genus of disease-carrying mosquitoes

Examples

The Aedes mosquito is known for spreading yellow fever and dengue fever in tropical regions.
Scientists are studying ways to control Aedes populations to prevent disease outbreaks.
Unlike other mosquitoes, Aedes species often bite during the daytime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Culex - another genus of mosquitoes, some species carry diseases such as West Nile virus

Etymology

From Greek aēdēs (unpleasant) due to the pestilent nature of the mosquito.
