decayable

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈkeɪ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / dɪˈkeɪ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
liable to decay, spoil, or become putrid; capable of decomposing or rotting
liable to decay or spoil

Examples

Fresh organic waste is highly decayable and must be processed quickly.
Proper storage can extend the lifespan of decayable food items.
Packaging materials made from decayable substances are considered more environmentally friendly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perishable - liable to spoil or decay quickly

Etymology

From 'decay' + '-able', meaning capable of decaying.
