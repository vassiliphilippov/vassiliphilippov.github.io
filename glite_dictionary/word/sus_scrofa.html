Sus scrofa

1. NOUN

/ sʌs ˈskroʊfə / (AmE), / sʌs ˈskrɒfə / (BrE)
the scientific name for the wild boar, a wild species of pig originally from Europe, Asia, and North Africa; ancestor of most domestic pigs
scientific name for wild boar

Examples

The European Sus scrofa is native to forests across Eurasia.
Genetic studies show that most domestic pigs are descended from Sus scrofa.
In many regions, Sus scrofa populations have expanded due to reduced hunting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild boar - the common name for Sus scrofa, a wild pig species
  • Degree Lower: domestic pig - descendant of Sus scrofa, domesticated pig species

Etymology

From Latin 'sus' (pig) and 'scrofa' (sow, female pig).
