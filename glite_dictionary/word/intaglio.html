intaglio

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtælioʊ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtalioʊ / (BrE)
A printmaking technique where an image is incised into a surface, and the incised area holds the ink to create a print.
printmaking process using engraved plates

Examples

The artist demonstrated the intaglio process in her printmaking workshop.
Original currency designs often use the intaglio method for security and detail.
He admired how intaglio printing produced such rich, textured images.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: relief printing - printing process where the image is raised above the surface

Etymology

From Italian 'intaglio' (engraving, cutting), from 'intagliare' (to engrave).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtælioʊ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtalioʊ / (BrE)
an engraving or carving in which the design is formed by cutting into a surface so that it is sunken below the level of the surrounding material, especially on a stone or gem
sunken engraving or carving on a surface

Examples

The museum displayed a Roman intaglio set in an ancient ring.
He admired the delicate intaglio work carved into the sapphire.
Many collectors seek rare gemstones with fine intaglio engravings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cameo - a piece of jewelry with a raised (positive) relief image, especially one carved on a gemstone

Etymology

From Italian 'intaglio' (engraving), from 'intagliare' (to engrave), from Latin 'in-' (in) + 'taliare' (to cut).
