Chlamydia trachomatis

1. NOUN

/ kləˌmɪdiə trəˌkoʊˈmeɪtɪs / (AmE), / kləˌmɪdiə trəˌkəʊˈmætɪs / (BrE)
a species of bacteria that causes the sexually transmitted disease chlamydia and the infection lymphogranuloma venereum in humans
bacteria causing chlamydia and related infections
medical

Examples

Chlamydia trachomatis is the leading cause of preventable blindness and sexually transmitted infections worldwide.
Testing for Chlamydia trachomatis is recommended for sexually active individuals under 25.
A course of antibiotics can effectively treat infections caused by Chlamydia trachomatis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chlamydia - sexually transmitted infection caused by Chlamydia trachomatis

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Chlamydia' (genus name, from Greek 'chlamys', cloak) and 'trachomatis' ('roughness of the eye', from Greek).
