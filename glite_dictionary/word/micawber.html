Micawber

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈkɔbər / (AmE), / mɪˈkɔːbə / (BrE)
A person who is habitually optimistic and expects that good fortune will come, alluding to Wilkins Micawber, a character in Charles Dickens's novel 'David Copperfield.'
eternal optimist (from Dickens character)

Examples

Despite his mounting debts, Tom was a true Micawber, always convinced things would improve.
My grandfather is a bit of a Micawber, certain that ‘something will turn up’ no matter the odds.
Called the office Micawber, Jane always looks on the bright side during tough projects.

Etymology

From the character Wilkins Micawber in Charles Dickens's 'David Copperfield' (1850).
