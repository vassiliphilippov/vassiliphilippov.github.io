Hualpai

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːlpaɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɑːlpaɪ / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people originally inhabiting the Colorado River valley in northwestern Arizona
member of a Native American people in Arizona

Examples

The Hualpai traditionally lived in the arid Colorado River valley of Arizona.
A group of Hualpai elders shared their history with local students.
Tourists can visit the Hualpai reservation to learn about their culture and customs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hualapai - the Yuman language spoken by the Hualpai people

Etymology

From Hualapai, the people's own name for themselves, meaning 'people of the tall pines.'

2. NOUN

/ wɑːlˈpaɪ, hwɑːlˈpaɪ / (AmE), / wɔːlˈpaɪ, hwɔːlˈpaɪ / (BrE)
the Yuman language spoken by the Walapai people of northwestern Arizona
Yuman language of the Walapai people

Examples

There are ongoing efforts to preserve and revitalize the Hualpai language.
Few younger members of the tribe are fluent in Hualpai today.
She recorded traditional stories told in Hualpai to help future generations learn the language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yuman - a group of related Native American languages of the southwestern US and northwestern Mexico

Etymology

From the name of the Hualapai (Walapai) people, whose name means 'people of the tall pines' in their language.
