Fothergilla

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɑːðərˈɡɪlə / (AmE), / ˌfɒðəˈɡɪlə / (BrE)
a small genus of deciduous shrubs native to the southeastern United States, valued for their ornamental flowers and foliage
genus of southeastern U.S. deciduous shrubs

Examples

The Fothergilla in my garden blooms with fluffy white flowers every spring.
Many landscapers choose Fothergilla for its vibrant fall foliage colors.
Fothergilla species thrive in well-drained, acidic soils and partial shade.

Etymology

Named after Dr. John Fothergill, 18th-century English physician and botanist.
