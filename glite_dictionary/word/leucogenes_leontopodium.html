Leucogenes leontopodium

1. NOUN

/ luːˈkoʊdʒəˌniːz liːˌɒntəˈpoʊdiəm / (AmE), / luːˈkəʊdʒiːniːz liːˌɒntəˈpəʊdiəm / (BrE)
a perennial herb native to New Zealand, closely resembling the European edelweiss
New Zealand herb resembling edelweiss

Examples

Leucogenes leontopodium is often called the New Zealand edelweiss because of its similar appearance.
Botanists are studying how Leucogenes leontopodium adapts to harsh alpine conditions.
Gardeners value Leucogenes leontopodium for its silvery foliage and star-shaped flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edelweiss - a perennial alpine plant with white woolly bracts, native to Europe

Etymology

From Greek 'leukos' (white) and 'genes' (born); species name refers to resemblance to edelweiss (*Leontopodium alpinum*).
