ultramodern

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌltrəˈmɑdɚn / (AmE), / ˌʌltrəˈmɒdən / (BrE)
extremely modern; incorporating the latest developments or ideas.
extremely modern

Examples

The ultramodern building featured sleek lines and floor-to-ceiling windows.
Many young architects prefer ultramodern designs to traditional styles.
Her apartment is decorated with ultramodern furniture in bold colors.

Synset Examples

Dadism and ultramodern art

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: modern - relating to the present or recent times
  • Synonym: avant-garde - favoring or introducing experimental or innovative ideas

Etymology

Formed from ultra- (Latin for 'beyond', 'extreme') + modern.
