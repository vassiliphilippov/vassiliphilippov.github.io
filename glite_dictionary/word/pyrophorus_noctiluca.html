Pyrophorus noctiluca

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈrɑːfərəs ˌnɑːktɪˈluːkə / (AmE), / paɪˈrɒfərəs ˌnɒktɪˈluːkə / (BrE)
A tropical American click beetle species known for its bright, bioluminescent spots on its body.
bioluminescent tropical click beetle

Examples

The Pyrophorus noctiluca glows brilliantly as it flies through the night in Central America.
Scientists are studying the light-producing abilities of Pyrophorus noctiluca for potential medical applications.
Children in rural villages sometimes collect Pyrophorus noctiluca beetles for their natural illumination.

Etymology

From Greek 'pyr' (fire) and 'phoros' (bearing) plus Latin 'noctiluca' (night-shining).
