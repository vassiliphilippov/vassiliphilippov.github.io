to predetermine

1. VERB

/ ˌpriː.dɪˈtɝː.mən / (AmE), / ˌpriː.dɪˈtɜː.mɪn / (BrE)
to influence or shape an outcome or opinion in advance, often creating bias
to influence or bias in advance

Examples

The evidence should not predetermine the jury's verdict.
Cultural stereotypes can predetermine how people are perceived in society.
His early experiences seemed to predetermine his attitude toward authority.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bias - to influence, often unfairly, to produce a particular result

Etymology

From Latin prae- (before) + determinare (to limit, determine).

2. VERB

/ ˌpriː.dɪˈtɝː.mɪn / (AmE), / ˌpriː.dɪˈtɜː.mɪn / (BrE)
to decide, settle, or establish something in advance, often before all necessary information is available
to decide or settle in advance

Examples

The outcome of the experiment was predetermined by the initial conditions.
Some people believe that their fate is predetermined and cannot be changed.
The schedule was predetermined months before the event took place.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to preordain - to decide or establish something in advance, especially by fate or divine will
  • Synonym: to determine - to decide or establish something
  • Antonym: to improvise - to decide or act without prior planning

Etymology

From Latin prae- (before) + determinare (to limit, fix).
