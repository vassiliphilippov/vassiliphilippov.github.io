ruby

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːbi / (AmE), / ˈruːbi / (BrE)
a transparent deep red precious stone, a variety of corundum used as a gemstone and in lasers
deep red precious stone; gemstone

Examples

She wore a beautiful necklace with a large ruby pendant.
The ruby glowed vividly under the museum lights.
They used a synthetic ruby to create the first working laser.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sapphire - a transparent blue variety of corundum used as a gemstone
  • Degree Lower: emerald - a green precious stone consisting of a chromium-rich variety of beryl

Etymology

From Latin 'rubeus' (red), via Old French 'rubi'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈruːbi / (AmE), / ˈruːbi / (BrE)
A transparent red gemstone, a variety of corundum, that has been cut and polished for use in jewelry.
red precious gemstone

Examples

The queen wore a beautiful necklace set with a large ruby.
He gave her a ring featuring a sparkling ruby surrounded by diamonds.
The auction displayed a rare Burmese ruby valued at millions of dollars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sapphire - a blue or other colored variety of corundum used as a gemstone
  • Synonym: emerald - a bright green precious stone consisting of a variety of beryl
  • Synonym: diamond - a clear, colorless, precious stone of pure carbon

Etymology

From Latin 'rubeus' (red).

4. ADJ

/ ˈruːbi / (AmE), / ˈruːbi / (BrE)
Having a deep red color, resembling that of a ruby, blood, cherries, or tomatoes.
deep red color

Examples

The bride wore a stunning dress in ruby silk that caught everyone's attention.
His lips were stained a ruby shade after eating the cherries.
Sunset cast a ruby glow over the quiet city skyline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crimson - a deep red color inclining to purple
  • Synonym: scarlet - bright red color with a slightly orange tinge
  • Synonym: vermilion - a brilliant red or scarlet

Etymology

From Old French 'rubis', from Latin 'ruber' (red).
