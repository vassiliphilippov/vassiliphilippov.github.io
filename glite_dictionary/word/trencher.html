trencher

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛntʃər / (AmE), / ˈtrɛntʃə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to dig trenches, especially for construction or military purposes
person who digs trenches

Examples

The trencher worked tirelessly through the night to complete the fortification.
Experienced trenchers are often hired for large pipeline projects.
Each trencher was given sturdy tools to ensure the trench was dug quickly and safely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digger - a person who digs, especially as a job

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛntʃər / (AmE), / ˈtrɛntʃə / (BrE)
a wooden board or platter used for serving or carving food, especially in medieval times
wooden serving or carving board

Examples

In medieval banquets, meat was often served on a trencher instead of a plate.
The museum displayed a well-preserved trencher used during the Middle Ages.
They carved the roasted pig directly on the wooden trencher at the feast.

Etymology

From Old French *tranchier* (to cut), related to Latin *truncare* (to cut off).
