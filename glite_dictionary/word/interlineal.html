interlineal

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪntərˈlɪniəl / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈlɪniəl / (BrE)
Written or inserted between the lines of a text.
written between lines of text

Examples

The scholar made interlineal notes to clarify difficult passages in the manuscript.
An interlineal translation was provided for students learning the ancient language.
Her handwriting filled the interlineal spaces of the old textbook.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interlinear - written or printed between the lines of text, often as a translation or explanation

Etymology

From Medieval Latin interlinealis, from Latin inter- (between) + linea (line).
