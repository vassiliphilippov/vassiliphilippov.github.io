aunt

1. NOUN

/ ænt / (AmE), / ɑːnt / (BrE)
the sister of one's father or mother, or the wife of one's uncle
female relative; parent's sister or uncle's wife

Examples

My aunt always tells the best stories during family gatherings.
After moving to the city, I stayed with my aunt for a few months.
Each year, my aunt sends me a handmade birthday card.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uncle - the brother of one's father or mother, or the husband of one's aunt

Etymology

From Old French *ante*, from Latin *amita* (father's sister).
