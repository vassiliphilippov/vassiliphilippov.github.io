capiz

1. NOUN

/ kəˈpiz / (AmE), / kəˈpiːz / (BrE)
a tropical marine bivalve mollusk of the Philippines, valued for its large, thin, translucent shell, often used for decorative purposes
marine bivalve with translucent shell from the Philippines

Examples

The windows were decorated with thin capiz shells that glowed softly in the sunlight.
Artisans craft beautiful lanterns using capiz because of its delicate, translucent quality.
Many traditional Filipino homes feature panels made from capiz instead of glass.

Etymology

From Capiz, a province in the Philippines where the mollusk is abundant.
