family Callithricidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli kəˌlɪθrɪˈsɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfamɪli kælɪθrɪˈsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small New World monkeys known as marmosets, native to Central and South America.
family of marmoset monkeys

Examples

The family Callithricidae includes a variety of marmosets with unique adaptations to their arboreal lifestyle.
Researchers study the social behavior of monkeys within the family Callithricidae.
Many species in the family Callithricidae are threatened by habitat loss in South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marmoset - a small monkey native to Central and South America

Etymology

From New Latin Callithricidae, from genus Callithrix (type genus), from Greek kallithrix ‘beautiful hair’.
