ophiolatry

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊfiˈɑːlətri / (AmE), / ˌəʊfiˈælətɹi / (BrE)
the worship of snakes as deities or religious icons
worship of snakes

Examples

Ophiolatry was common in many ancient cultures where snakes symbolized fertility or healing.
The archaeologist presented evidence of ophiolatry in the excavated temple.
Some Hindu traditions include elements of ophiolatry in their festivals and rituals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serpent worship - the worship of serpents or snakes

Etymology

From Greek 'ophis' (snake) + '-latry' (worship).
