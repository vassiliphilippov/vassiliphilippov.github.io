deliverer

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈlɪvərər / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪvərə / (BrE)
a person who rescues someone from harm or danger
person who rescues from danger

Examples

The firefighter was hailed as a deliverer after saving the family from the burning house.
Many viewed the new leader as a deliverer who would bring peace to the troubled region.
She prayed for a deliverer to rescue her from her difficult situation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rescuer - a person who saves others from dangerous or difficult situations
  • Antonym: captor - a person who captures or holds someone against their will

Etymology

From Middle English *delivrer*, from Old French *delivrer* (to set free).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈlɪvərər / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪvərə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to take goods or packages to people's homes or businesses
person who delivers goods or packages

Examples

The deliverer arrived at noon with our groceries.
Please tip the deliverer for bringing your package on time.
A different deliverer works this delivery route each week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courier - a person or company that delivers messages, packages, or mail

Etymology

From Middle English *deliveren* (to set free, deliver) + -er (agent noun suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10020810-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈlɪvɚɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪvərə / (BrE)
a person who gives up or transfers money, goods, or property to another
person who transfers money or goods

Examples

The deliverer handed the package to the receptionist at the front desk.
As the deliverer of the funds, she made sure every payment was documented.
Each deliverer must receive a receipt after handing over the goods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: donor - a person who donates or gives something to another
  • Antonym: recipient - a person who receives money, goods, or property from another

Etymology

From Old French 'delivrer', from Latin 'deliberare' (to set free, deliver).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
