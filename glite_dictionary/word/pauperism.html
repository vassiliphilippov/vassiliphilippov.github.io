pauperism

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔpərɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpɔːpərɪzəm / (BrE)
the state of being extremely poor or destitute
extreme poverty or destitution

Examples

The rising rate of pauperism in the city alarmed the local government.
Social reformers worked to address the causes of pauperism in Victorian England.
Many families struggled with pauperism after the economic downturn.

Synset Examples

their indigence appalled him
a general state of need exists among the homeless

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poverty - the state of being poor
  • Antonym: affluence - the state of having a lot of money or wealth

Etymology

From French 'pauperisme', from Latin 'pauper' (poor).
