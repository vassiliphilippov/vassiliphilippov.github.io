fedelline

1. NOUN

/ feɪdəˈliːni / (AmE), / feːdɛˈliːneɪ / (BrE)
a type of extremely fine, thin pasta that is even thinner than vermicelli, often used in soups
very fine, thin pasta

Examples

The chef used fedelline to prepare a delicate chicken broth soup.
I prefer fedelline over other pasta shapes in clear broths because it cooks so quickly.
At the Italian market, I found a package of fedelline next to the angel hair pasta.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: vermicelli - a type of pasta slightly thicker than fedelline
  • Degree Lower: capellini - thin pasta similar to but usually even finer than fedelline

Etymology

Italian, from 'fedelline', diminutive plural of 'fedel',
