placeseeker

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪsˌsiːkər / (AmE), / ˈpleɪsˌsiːkə / (BrE)
A person who actively seeks or curries favor in order to obtain an official position or appointment, often with the implication of self-interest or lack of principle.
one who seeks office or appointment for personal gain

Examples

The politician was accused of being a mere placeseeker without any real vision for the country.
Many placeseekers attended the event, hoping to secure valuable connections with party leaders.
He was critical of placeseeker attitudes that prioritized personal advancement over public service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: office-seeker - a person seeking public office, especially for personal profit

Etymology

From 'place' (meaning official position) + 'seeker'.
