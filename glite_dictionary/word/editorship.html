editorship

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛdɪtɚˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈɛdɪtəˌʃɪp / (BrE)
the position or period of being an editor, especially of a publication
position of being an editor

Examples

After years of reporting, she was finally offered the editorship of the magazine.
His editorship was marked by several controversial changes to the newspaper.
She held the editorship for over a decade before passing the role to her successor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: editor - a person who is in charge of and determines the final content of a text, particularly a publication

Etymology

From 'editor' + '-ship', denoting condition or office.
