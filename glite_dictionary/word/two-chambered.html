two-chambered

1. ADJ

/ tuː ˈtʃeɪmbɚd / (AmE), / tuː ˈtʃeɪmbəd / (BrE)
Having two separate chambers or compartments, especially in reference to anatomical structures or mechanical devices.
having two chambers

Examples

The two-chambered heart of a fish efficiently separates oxygenated and deoxygenated blood.
Scientists studied the two-chambered design of the experimental reactor for improved performance.
A two-chambered stomach helps some animals digest their food in separate stages.

Synset Examples

the bicameral heart of a fish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bicameral - having two chambers, often used in reference to legislative bodies or biology
