notturno

1. NOUN

/ noʊˈtuːrnoʊ / (AmE), / nɒˈtʊə.nəʊ / (BrE)
A pensive and lyrical piece of music, especially for piano, often evocative of the night.
lyrical musical piece, often nocturnal

Examples

The pianist performed a notturno that captivated the entire audience with its gentle melody.
Chopin composed several works titled notturno, each offering a unique nocturnal mood.
In last night's concert, the notturno was the highlight, blending lyricism and introspection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nocturne - a musical composition inspired by or evocative of the night

Etymology

From Italian 'notturno' (nocturnal), from Latin 'nocturnus' (of the night).
