nightrider

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtˌraɪdər / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtˌraɪdə / (BrE)
A member of a secret mounted group, especially in the southern United States after the Civil War, who carried out acts of intimidation and revenge, often at night.
member of secret group intimidating people (US history)

Examples

The villagers lived in fear of the nightriders who terrorized the countryside after dark.
Historians have documented several incidents involving nightriders in rural Tennessee.
Some former soldiers became nightriders, targeting their political adversaries through intimidation.

Etymology

From 'night' + 'rider', referring to someone who rides at night.
