wizardliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪzɚdliˌnəs / (AmE), / ˈwɪzədliˌnəs / (BrE)
exceptional skill or ability, especially in a specialized area such as programming
exceptional skill or talent

Examples

Her wizardliness in coding helped the team solve complex problems quickly.
Many admire his wizardliness when it comes to solving tricky programming bugs.
The competition judges commented on the remarkable wizardliness displayed during the final round.

Synset Examples

His wizardliness in coding impressed everyone at the hackathon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wizardry - exceptional skill or cleverness, especially in a particular area

Etymology

From 'wizard' (a person with magical powers) + '-liness' (forming nouns denoting a quality or state).
