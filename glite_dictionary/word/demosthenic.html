Demosthenic

1. ADJ

/ dɪˌmɑːsˈθɛnɪk / (AmE), / dɪˌmɒsˈθɛnɪk / (BrE)
Relating to Demosthenes, the ancient Greek orator, or to his style of oratory.
of or relating to Demosthenes or his oratory

Examples

His Demosthenic eloquence captivated the entire audience during the debate.
The lawyer's Demosthenic delivery evoked images of ancient Greek assemblies.
Critics admired the Demosthenic force of her arguments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ciceronian - Relating to Cicero or his style of oratory

Etymology

From Demosthenes, the renowned Athenian orator (384–322 BC), with the English suffix -ic.
