prescription

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈskrɪpʃən / (AmE), / prɪˈskrɪpʃən / (BrE)
a written instruction by an optician or eye doctor specifying the lenses required for an individual
written order for specific lenses

Examples

The optometrist wrote a new prescription for my reading glasses.
She handed the prescription to the optical shop to order her lenses.
Without the correct prescription, the lenses won't properly correct your vision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eyeglass prescription - written specifications for corrective lenses

Etymology

From Latin 'praescriptio' (written order), from 'praescribere' (to write before).

2. NOUN

/ prɪˈskrɪpʃən / (AmE), / prɪˈskrɪpʃən / (BrE)
a drug or medicine that can only be obtained with written authorization from a doctor or dentist
a doctor-ordered drug or medicine

Examples

The pharmacy will not release the prescription without your doctor's signed note.
She forgot to refill her prescription before her trip abroad.
Some painkillers are only available by prescription from a licensed physician.

Synset Examples

he told the doctor that he had been taking his prescription regularly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: over-the-counter - a drug available without a prescription

Etymology

From Latin praescriptio (an order, direction).

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ prɪˈskrɪpʃən / (AmE), / prɪˈskrɪpʃən / (BrE)
an instruction or rule that is authoritatively prescribed, especially as a recommended course of action
authoritative instruction or rule

Examples

The coach gave the team a detailed prescription for improving their performance.
Success in this field often depends on following a strict prescription for study and practice.
His prescription for a happier life included regular exercise and healthy eating.

Synset Examples

I tried to follow her prescription for success

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recommendation - suggestion or proposal as to the best course of action
  • Antonym: prohibition - a rule forbidding something

Etymology

From Latin 'praescriptio' (written direction), from 'praescribere' (to write before).

4. ADJ

/ prɪˈskrɪpʃən / (AmE), / prɪˈskrɪpʃən / (BrE)
(of drugs or medicine) available only with a written order from a doctor; not sold over the counter.
requiring a doctor's written order (of medicine)
medical

Examples

The pharmacy will only sell prescription medication to patients with a valid doctor's order.
He forgot to renew his prescription drugs, so he couldn't get them from the store.
Certain painkillers are classified as prescription because of their potential for abuse.

Synset Examples

a prescription drug

Related Terms

  • Antonym: over-the-counter - available without a prescription

Etymology

From Latin 'praescriptio' (a written order), via Old French and late Middle English.

5. NOUN

/ prəˈskrɪpʃən / (AmE), / prɪˈskrɪpʃən / (BrE)
an instruction written by a doctor or dentist that authorizes a patient to be provided with a medicine or treatment
doctor's written order for medicine

Examples

The doctor quickly wrote a prescription for antibiotics after examining the patient.
You cannot buy this medication without a valid prescription from your physician.
She handed the pharmacist her prescription and waited for the medicine to be prepared.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: order - an authoritative direction or instruction to do something
  • Antonym: proscription - the action of forbidding something; the opposite in the sense of banning

Etymology

From Latin praescriptio, meaning 'written order' or 'direction'.
