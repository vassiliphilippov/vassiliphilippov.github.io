breakable

1. ADJ

/ ˈbreɪkəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkəbl̩ / (BrE)
easily able to be broken or damaged; fragile.
easily able to be broken

Examples

Be careful with the breakable dishes while washing them.
The sign warned that all items inside the box were breakable.
Glass is much more breakable than plastic.

Synset Examples

earthenware pottery is breakable
breakable articles should be packed carefully

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragile - easily broken or damaged
  • Antonym: unbreakable - impossible to break or damage

Etymology

From 'break' + '-able', meaning able to be broken.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00711461-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪkəbəl / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkəbəl / (BrE)
an object, especially one made of glass or porcelain, that is fragile and easily broken
fragile item; something easily broken

Examples

Please wrap the breakables in bubble wrap before putting them in the box.
The movers took extra care with the breakables in the kitchen.
She collects antique breakables such as porcelain figurines and glassware.

Synset Examples

pack the breakables separately

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragile - easily broken or damaged

Etymology

From 'break' + suffix '-able', indicating capability or susceptibility.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02897286-n
Synset POS: n
