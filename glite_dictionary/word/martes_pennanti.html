Martes pennanti

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːrt.eɪz pəˈnæn.taɪ / (AmE), / ˌmɑː.tiːz pəˈnan.tiː / (BrE)
A large, dark brown, tree-dwelling carnivorous mammal native to North America, also known as the fisher.
North American carnivorous mammal (fisher)

Examples

The Martes pennanti is noted for its agility in climbing trees in dense forests.
Wildlife biologists observed a Martes pennanti hunting in the snowy woods.
Many trappers once sought the fur of the Martes pennanti for its value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fisher - North American carnivorous mammal, common name for Martes pennanti

Etymology

From Latin: 'Martes' (marten) and 'pennanti', after Thomas Pennant, the naturalist.
