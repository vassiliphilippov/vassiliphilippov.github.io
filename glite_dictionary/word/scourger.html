scourger

1. NOUN

/ ˈskaʊrdʒər / (AmE), / ˈskaʊədʒə / (BrE)
a person who administers flogging or whipping, especially as an official form of punishment
person who punishes by whipping

Examples

The cruel scourger carried out his duties in front of the assembled crowd.
During the reign of the king, the scourger was a feared presence in the prison.
He was known as the chief scourger at the old naval yard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torturer - a person who inflicts severe pain as punishment or intimidation

Etymology

From Middle English scourge (to whip) + -er (agent suffix), related to Old French escorgier.
