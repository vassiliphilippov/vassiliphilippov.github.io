epigraph

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛpɪˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪɡrɑːf / (BrE)
an inscription engraved on a building, statue, or coin, especially as a dedication or memorial
an engraved inscription

Examples

The ancient temple's entrance bore an epigraph in Latin honoring the gods.
A marble epigraph commemorates those who contributed to the building's construction.
Each old coin was carefully examined for its unique epigraph by the historian.

Etymology

From Greek epigraphe (inscription), from epi- (upon) + graphein (to write).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛpɪˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a quotation or saying at the beginning of a book or chapter, intended to suggest its theme
quotation at start of a text

Examples

The novel opens with an epigraph from Shakespeare, hinting at the story’s central conflict.
Each chapter begins with an epigraph chosen by the author to inspire reflection.
Before the introduction, an epigraph sets the philosophical tone of the essay.

Etymology

From Greek epigraphē (inscription), from epi- (upon) + graphein (to write).
