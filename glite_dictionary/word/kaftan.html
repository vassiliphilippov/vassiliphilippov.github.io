kaftan

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæf.tæn / (AmE), / ˈkæf.tæn / (BrE)
A long, loose garment with full sleeves and a sash, traditionally made of cotton or silk and worn by men in the Middle East and North Africa.
long, loose robe with sash

Examples

The sultan wore an ornate kaftan adorned with gold embroidery to the ceremony.
She purchased a colorful kaftan during her travels in Morocco.
In some cultures, a kaftan is worn on special occasions and festivals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robe - a loose outer garment

Etymology

From Turkish 'kaftan', ultimately from Persian 'khaftān', meaning 'robe'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæf.tæn / (AmE), / ˈkɑːf.tæn / (BrE)
A long, loose-fitting woman's dress with wide sleeves, styled after traditional men's caftan cloaks from the Near East.
long, loose woman's dress

Examples

She wore a brightly patterned kaftan to the beach party.
The boutique sold silk kaftans inspired by Middle Eastern designs.
During summer, a kaftan makes for a comfortable and elegant outfit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caftan - long, loose garment with wide sleeves worn in various cultures

Etymology

From Turkish 'kaftan', from Persian 'khaftān', ultimately from Arabic.
