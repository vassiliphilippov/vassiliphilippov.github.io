to anthologise

1. VERB

/ ænˈθɑːlədʒaɪz / (AmE), / ænˈθɒlədʒaɪz / (BrE)
to compile literary works, such as poems, stories, or essays, into an anthology.
compile works into an anthology
british

Examples

She plans to anthologise the best poems published this year.
The editor hopes to anthologise several contemporary short stories for young readers.
His goal was to anthologise works by overlooked novelists from the 20th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to anthologize - to compile literary works into an anthology (American spelling)

Etymology

From the noun 'anthology' + the verb-forming suffix '-ise'.
