lilac

1. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪ.læk / (AmE), / ˈlaɪ.lək / (BrE)
of a pale purple color, resembling that of lilac flowers
pale purple in color

Examples

She chose a lilac dress for the spring wedding.
The sky turned a soft lilac as the sun began to set.
He painted the walls a gentle lilac to create a calming atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lavender - of a pale purple color, similar to the flower lavender
  • Synonym: violet - of a bluish-purple color, also a shade and a flower
  • Degree Higher: purple - a color intermediate between red and blue

Etymology

From the noun 'lilac', from Arabic 'līlak' via Persian 'līlak', referring to the flower.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪ.lək / (AmE), / ˈlaɪ.lək / (BrE)
a shrub of the genus Syringa, having fragrant purple, pink, or white flowers in large clusters
a fragrant flowering shrub (Syringa)

Examples

A beautiful lilac bush bloomed in the corner of their garden every spring.
The air was perfumed with the scent of lilac after the rain.
She cut a bunch of lilac flowers to brighten the kitchen table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syringa - the botanical genus to which lilac belongs

Etymology

From French lilac, from Spanish lilac, from Arabic līlak, from Persian nīlaj (bluish), from nīl (indigo).
