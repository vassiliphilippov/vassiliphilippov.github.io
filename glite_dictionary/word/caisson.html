caisson

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪsən / (AmE), / ˈkeɪs(ə)n / (BrE)
a two-wheeled vehicle used in the military to transport artillery ammunition, especially in the past
two-wheeled military ammunition vehicle

Examples

The old photograph showed a caisson drawn by horses across the battlefield.
During the parade, a caisson carried the flag-draped coffin with solemn respect.
Soldiers loaded the caisson with shells before moving toward the front lines.

Etymology

From French 'caisson', meaning 'large chest' or 'ammunition wagon'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.sɑn / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.sɒn / (BrE)
a large watertight chamber used in underwater construction to keep water out while allowing work to be done; commonly used in building bridges, piers, and other structures
watertight chamber for underwater construction

Examples

Workers entered the caisson to lay the foundation for the new bridge.
The use of a caisson ensures that construction crews stay dry while working below water level.
To prevent accidents, air pressure inside the caisson must be carefully monitored.

Etymology

From French 'caisson' meaning 'large box' or 'chest', from Italian 'cassone', from Latin 'capsa' (box).

4. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ sɑn / (AmE), / ˈkeɪsɒn / (BrE)
A large, strong chest or container used to hold and transport ammunition, especially for artillery.
chest for holding ammunition

Examples

The soldiers carefully loaded the caisson before setting out for the front lines.
During the parade, a horse-drawn caisson carried both the ammunition and the fallen soldier.
Old photographs show artillery and caissons lined up behind the battlefield.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ammunition box - a box for storing ammunition

Etymology

From French 'caisson' (large box), from Italian 'cassone', from 'cassa' (chest).
