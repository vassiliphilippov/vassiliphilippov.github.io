furlong

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɜr·lɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɜː.lɒŋ / (BrE)
a unit of length equal to 220 yards, chiefly used in measuring distances in horse racing and formerly in agriculture
unit of length (220 yards)

Examples

The horses raced at full speed for the final furlong toward the finish line.
The old map indicated a distance of two furlongs between the villages.
He walked a furlong down the country lane before turning back.

Etymology

Old English *furhlang* (furrow long), from *furh* (furrow) + *lang* (long).
