pasigraphy

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæsəˈɡræfi / (AmE), / ˌpasɪˈɡrafɪ / (BrE)
a system of writing using characters or symbols, instead of words, to represent ideas, intended as an international language
universal written language using symbols

Examples

The committee studied whether pasigraphy could help international scientific communication.
She was fascinated by pasigraphy as a means to overcome language barriers.
Early visions of pasigraphy inspired many attempts at universal writing systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ideogram - a written character symbolizing an idea without indicating the sounds used to say it

Etymology

From French 'pasigraphie', from Greek 'pasi-' meaning 'to all' and '-graphy' meaning 'writing'.
