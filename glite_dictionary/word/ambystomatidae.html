Ambystomatidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌæm.bɪˌstoʊ.məˈtɪd.i / (AmE), / ˌæm.bɪˌstɒ.məˈtɪd.iː / (BrE)
A family of salamanders native to the Americas, including species such as the tiger salamander.
family of New World salamanders

Examples

Members of the Ambystomatidae family are known for their distinctive larval stages.
The tiger salamander, part of the Ambystomatidae, is found throughout North America.
Researchers are studying the development of limbs in Ambystomatidae to understand regeneration.

Etymology

From New Latin Ambystoma (a genus of salamanders) + -idae (a zoological family suffix).
