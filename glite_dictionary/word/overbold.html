overbold

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvərˈboʊld / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbəʊld / (BrE)
excessively or improperly bold; showing more confidence or forwardness than is appropriate
improperly or excessively bold

Examples

He was overbold in questioning the manager's decisions.
The student's overbold remarks surprised everyone at the meeting.
Sometimes, being overbold can damage professional relationships.

Synset Examples

don't be fresh with me
impertinent of a child to lecture a grownup
an impudent boy given to insulting strangers
Don't get wise with me!

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bold - showing willingness to take risks; confident and courageous
  • Synonym: impertinent - not showing proper respect; rude

Etymology

From over- (excessive) + bold.
