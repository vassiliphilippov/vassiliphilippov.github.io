2. NOUN

/ siː oʊ / (AmE), / siː əʊ / (BrE)
a person who refuses to serve in the armed forces or bear arms on grounds of conscience or moral principle; conscientious objector
conscientious objector (military service)

Examples

During the war, he registered as a CO because of his religious beliefs.
Many COs were assigned to alternative non-combat roles during the conflict.
The government established a process to review the claims of COs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conscientious objector - a person who refuses military service on grounds of conscience

Etymology

3. NOUN

/ siː oʊ / (AmE), / siː əʊ / (BrE)
An odorless, very poisonous gas formed by incomplete combustion of carbon; chemical formula CO.
carbon monoxide gas

Examples

Firefighters checked the house for dangerous levels of CO after the alarm went off.
Incomplete combustion in car engines can release CO into the atmosphere.
It's important to install CO detectors in your home to prevent poisoning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbon monoxide - an odorless, poisonous gas formed by incomplete combustion of carbon

Etymology

Abbreviation of carbon monoxide (chemical formula CO).
