Parietaria diffusa

1. NOUN

/ pəˌraɪ.əˈtɛr.i.ə dɪˈfjuː.sə / (AmE), / pəˌraɪ.ɪˈtɛə.ri.ə dɪˈfjuː.sə / (BrE)
A species of herb that grows in crevices, characterized by long narrow leaves and small pink flowers that lack petals.
a herb with narrow leaves and pink apetalous flowers

Examples

The old stone wall was covered with Parietaria diffusa thriving in every crevice.
Botanists often study Parietaria diffusa for its unique habitat preferences.
A carpet of Parietaria diffusa added a dash of pink to the rocky slope in spring.

Etymology

From Latin paries, parietis (wall), referring to the plant's tendency to grow on walls; diffusa, meaning "spread" or "diffuse".
