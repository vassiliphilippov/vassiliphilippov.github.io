cadaver

1. NOUN

/ kəˈdæv.ɚ / (AmE), / kəˈdæv.ə / (BrE)
A dead human body, especially one intended for dissection or medical study.
dead human body (medical)
medical

Examples

Medical students performed their first dissection on a cadaver in anatomy class.
After the flood, the police recovered the cadaver from the riverbank.
A forensic pathologist was called to examine the cadaver for signs of foul play.

Etymology

From Latin 'cadaver' (corpse, dead body).
