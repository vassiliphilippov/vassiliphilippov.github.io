to minimise

1. VERB

/ ˈmɪnɪˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪmaɪz / (BrE)
to represent something as less important, significant, or serious than it actually is
downplay the importance of something
british

Examples

The company tried to minimise the impact of the bad news in their press release.
She tends to minimise her achievements and focus on her mistakes.
It is dangerous to minimise the risks involved in the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to downplay - to make something seem less important or serious
  • Antonym: to exaggerate - to represent as greater or more important than it really is

Etymology

From Latin 'minimus' (smallest), via French 'minimiser'.

2. VERB

/ ˈmɪnɪˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪmaɪz / (BrE)
To make something as small or unimportant as possible.
make as small or unimportant as possible
british

Examples

The company is trying to minimise its environmental impact this year.
You should take steps to minimise any potential health risks.
He did his best to minimise the importance of his mistake.

Synset Examples

Let's minimize the risk

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to maximize - make as large or important as possible
  • Synonym: to diminish - reduce or decrease in size, importance, or intensity
  • Synonym: to reduce - make smaller in size, amount, or degree

Etymology

From Latin 'minimus' (smallest) via French 'minimiser'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00428755-v
Synset POS: v
