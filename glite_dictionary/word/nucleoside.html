nucleoside

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːkliəˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈnjuːklɪəˌsaɪd / (BrE)
a compound consisting of a purine or pyrimidine base joined to a sugar, formed by the partial hydrolysis of a nucleic acid and lacking the phosphate group found in nucleotides.
compound formed from base and sugar in nucleic acids
medical

Examples

A nucleoside consists of a nitrogenous base linked to a sugar molecule.
The synthesis of a new nucleoside analog has potential in antiviral treatments.
Unlike nucleotides, a nucleoside does not contain a phosphate group.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: nucleotide - compound consisting of a nucleoside with one or more phosphate groups

Etymology

From 'nucleus' + '-oside', influenced by 'nucleotide'.
