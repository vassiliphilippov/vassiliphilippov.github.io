Chimborazo

1. PROPN

/ tʃɪmbəˈrɑzoʊ / (AmE), / tʃɪmbəˈrɑːzəʊ / (BrE)
a mountain peak in the Andes of central Ecuador, reaching 6,268 meters (20,560 feet) in elevation; notable as the highest mountain in Ecuador.
mountain in Ecuador, Andes

Examples

Mount Chimborazo is famous for being the point on Earth farthest from the center, due to the equatorial bulge.
He dreamed of climbing Chimborazo during his trip to Ecuador.
The snow-capped summit of Chimborazo can be seen for miles on a clear day.

Etymology

From Quechua or possibly from the Spanish adaptation of indigenous names.
