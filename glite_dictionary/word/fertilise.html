to fertilise

1. VERB

/ ˈfɜrtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɜːtɪlaɪz / (BrE)
to cause a female animal or human to become pregnant by introducing semen into her body
cause conception by introducing semen

Examples

The doctor used a special technique to fertilise the eggs during treatment.
Many animals fertilise the female internally, increasing the chances of successful reproduction.
Artificial methods are sometimes used to fertilise cows in modern agriculture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inseminate - to introduce semen into a female; especially artificially

Etymology

From Latin fertilis (fruitful), via French fertiliser.

2. VERB

/ ˈfɜːrtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɜːtɪlaɪz / (BrE)
to make fertile or productive; to cause to develop or grow more effectively
make fertile or productive
british

Examples

The new policies helped fertilise economic growth in the region.
Regular reading can fertilise a child's imagination.
They used natural compost to fertilise the garden soil.

Synset Examples

The course fertilized her imagination

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fertilize - to make (soil or land) more fertile
  • Antonym: to deplete - to make less fertile or productive

Etymology

From Latin 'fertilis' (fruitful, fertile), via French 'fertiliser'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00505351-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈfɜrtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɜːtɪlaɪz / (BrE)
to add fertilizer or nutrients to soil to help plants grow
add fertilizer or nutrients to soil

Examples

Every spring, gardeners fertilise their lawns to ensure lush green grass.
The farmer decided to fertilise the field before planting new crops.
You should always fertilise houseplants carefully to avoid overfeeding them.

Synset Examples

We should fertilize soil if we want to grow healthy plants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enrich - to improve the quality of something, especially soil
  • Antonym: to deplete - to reduce the amount of something, especially nutrients in soil

Etymology

From Latin 'fertilis' (fruitful, fertile), through French 'fertiliser'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
