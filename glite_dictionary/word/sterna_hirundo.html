Sterna hirundo

1. NOUN

/ ˌstɜrnə hɪˈrʌndoʊ / (AmE), / ˌstɜːnə hɪˈrʌndəʊ / (BrE)
A species of tern, commonly called the common tern, found in Eurasia and America, recognized by its white, black, and grey plumage.
species of tern (common tern)

Examples

Every summer, flocks of Sterna hirundo migrate along the coastline.
The Sterna hirundo can be identified by its sharp black cap and forked tail.
Researchers observed the nesting habits of Sterna hirundo on several European islands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common tern - the common name for Sterna hirundo, a widespread tern species

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Sterna' means 'tern' and 'hirundo' means 'swallow', referring to the bird's swallow-like flight.
