gip

1. NOUN

/ ɡɪp / (AmE), / ɡɪp / (BrE)
an act of cheating or swindling someone, sometimes considered offensive
an act of cheating or swindling
offensive

Examples

The ticket price turned out to be a real gip after all the extra fees.
Many customers felt the warranty was just a gip to avoid paying for repairs.
He warned me that the so-called special offer was actually a gip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rip-off - an act of stealing or cheating, especially for money
  • Synonym: swindle - an act of cheating or defrauding someone
  • Antonym: fair deal - an exchange or arrangement that is honest and just

2. VERB

/ dʒɪp / (AmE), / dʒɪp / (BrE)
(sometimes offensive) To cheat or swindle someone out of money or possessions.
cheat or swindle (offensive)
offensive

Examples

The mechanic tried to gip me by charging for work he never did.
I realized too late that the ticket seller had gipped us.
It’s wrong to gip customers, no matter how much you dislike your job.

Synset Examples

the cashier gypped me when he gave me too little change

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheat - to act dishonestly to gain an advantage
  • Synonym: swindle - to use deception to deprive someone of money or possessions

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'gyp,' possibly derived from 'Gypsy,' considered offensive due to ethnic stereotyping.
