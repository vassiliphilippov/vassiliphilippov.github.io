effectivity

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌfɛkˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ɪˌfɛkˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
the power or quality of producing a desired effect or result; effectiveness
the quality of being effective

Examples

The effectivity of the new policy was immediately apparent in the reduced error rates.
Researchers questioned the effectivity of the treatment in real-world settings.
To ensure maximum effectivity, the procedure must be followed precisely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effectiveness - the degree to which something is effective

Etymology

Formed from 'effective' + '-ity', after the pattern of words like 'activity'.
