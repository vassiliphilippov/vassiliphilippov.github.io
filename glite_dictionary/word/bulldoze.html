to bulldoze

1. VERB

/ ˈbʊlˌdoʊz / (AmE), / ˈbʊlˌdəʊz / (BrE)
to flatten or clear (an area) with a bulldozer or to forcefully remove or destroy something as if using a bulldozer
flatten or clear with a bulldozer

Examples

They had to bulldoze the old building to make way for the new supermarket.
The construction crew bulldozed a path through the forest for the new road.
Developers threatened to bulldoze the historic site despite protests from local residents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flatten - to make (something) flat or flatter
  • Synonym: clear - to remove unwanted objects from an area

Etymology

From 'bulldozer', the machine used for pushing earth or rubble; '-e' verb formation.
