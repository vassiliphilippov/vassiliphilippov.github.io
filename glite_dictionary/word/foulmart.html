foulmart

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaʊlˌmɑrt / (AmE), / ˈfaʊlˌmɑːt / (BrE)
A European polecat, a dark brown carnivorous mammal found in woodlands of Eurasia, which emits a bad smell when threatened.
a mustelid mammal emitting a foul odor

Examples

The foulmart is rarely seen during the day, as it prefers to hunt at night.
When threatened, the foulmart releases a pungent odor to deter predators.
Farmers sometimes blame the foulmart for disturbing their chicken coops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polecat - a carnivorous mustelid mammal, similar species in Europe

Etymology

From Middle English 'foul' (dirty) + 'mart' (marten).
