hemp

1. NOUN

/ hɛmp / (AmE), / hɛmp / (BrE)
A strong, coarse fiber obtained from the stems of the hemp plant, used to make rope, fabric, and other products.
fiber from the hemp plant

Examples

The ropes were made from strong hemp fibers.
Clothing made of hemp is durable and eco-friendly.
Farmers grow hemp for use in textiles and construction materials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flax - a plant fiber used to make linen

Etymology

From Old English hænep, from Proto-Germanic *hanapiz, ultimately of early European origin.

2. NOUN

/ hɛmp / (AmE), / hɛmp / (BrE)
A tall, coarse, annual plant of the genus Cannabis, grown for its tough fiber, seeds, and sometimes for the production of narcotic drugs.
plant of the genus Cannabis; fiber source

Examples

The farmer grew hemp for use in making ropes and textiles.
Industrial hemp is valued for its strong, natural fibers.
In some countries, hemp is also regulated due to its relationship with cannabis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cannabis - a genus of flowering plants including hemp and marijuana
  • Synonym: marijuana - a psychoactive drug from the Cannabis plant

Etymology

Old English *hænep*, from Proto-Germanic *hanapiz*, related to Greek *kánnabis*.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
