splitter

1. NOUN

/ ˈsplɪtər / (AmE), / ˈsplɪtə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to split logs, especially for building split-rail fences
person who splits logs for fences

Examples

The old farmhouse was built by a skilled splitter using local timber.
Every spring, the splitter would prepare the rails for the new fencing around the fields.
Before machines, a splitter was needed to craft each rail by hand.

Etymology

From English 'split' + agent suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsplɪtər / (AmE), / ˈsplɪtə / (BrE)
a taxonomist who classifies organisms into many groups based on relatively minor differences
taxonomist favoring fine distinctions

Examples

In evolutionary biology, a splitter may divide a genus into several different species.
The museum's new director is known as a splitter, creating more categories for the fossil collection.
Debates between lumpers and splitters are common in taxonomic research.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lumper - a taxonomist who classifies organisms into fewer groups, focusing on broader similarities

3. NOUN

/ ˈsplɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈsplɪtə / (BrE)
a worker who splits fish and removes the backbone, especially in the fishing industry
worker who prepares fish by splitting

Examples

As a splitter at the fish processing plant, she became highly skilled at preparing cod for drying.
Many experienced splitters can process hundreds of fish in a single day.
The job of a splitter is essential in producing high-quality salted fish.
