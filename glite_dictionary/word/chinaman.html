Chinaman

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪnəmən / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪnəmən / (BrE)
(offensive, archaic) An offensive and outdated term for a man of Chinese descent.
offensive term for a man of Chinese descent
offensive archaic

Examples

The term Chinaman is now considered highly offensive and should not be used.
Old books may refer to a Chinese immigrant as a Chinaman, but this language is outdated.
He was wrongly called a Chinaman in the early 20th-century newspaper article.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪnəˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪnəmən / (BrE)
(cricket) a ball bowled by a left-handed bowler to a right-handed batsman that spins from off to leg, typically a left-arm unorthodox spin delivery.
left-arm unorthodox spin delivery in cricket

Examples

The bowler surprised the batsman with a clever Chinaman that spun sharply.
His specialty is the Chinaman, which has puzzled many top batsmen.
Left-arm bowlers often practice the Chinaman to add variety to their attack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: googly - a deceptive spinning delivery bowled by a right-arm leg spinner in cricket

Etymology

Cricket term, probably from Ellis Achong, a player of Chinese descent whose bowling inspired the term.
