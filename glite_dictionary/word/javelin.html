javelin

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæv.lɪn / (AmE), / ˈdʒæv.lɪn / (BrE)
A light spear designed to be thrown, used as a weapon in the past or in modern athletics competitions.
a light spear for throwing

Examples

The athlete threw the javelin more than seventy meters in the competition.
In ancient times, soldiers often fought with a javelin and a shield.
Coaches teach proper javelin technique to maximize both distance and safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spear - a weapon with a pointed tip, used for thrusting or throwing

Etymology

From Middle English 'chavelin', from Old French 'javelot' (dart, spear).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒævəlɪn / (AmE), / ˈdʒævəlɪn / (BrE)
an athletic event in which competitors throw a long, slender spear as far as possible
athletic event: spear-throwing

Examples

She set a new record in the javelin at the national championships.
The javelin requires both strength and precise technique from the athletes.
During the javelin, the stadium fell silent as the competitor prepared to throw.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shot put - athletic event where a heavy spherical object is thrown
  • Synonym: discus - athletic event where a heavy disc is thrown

Etymology

From Old French javeline, diminutive of javelot (spear, dart), possibly of Celtic origin.
