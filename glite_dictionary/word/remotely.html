remotely

1. ADV

/ rɪˈmoʊtli / (AmE), / rɪˈməʊtli / (BrE)
In a distant or indirect manner; to a very small degree or by only a slight connection.
in a distant or indirect way

Examples

It is not remotely possible that she will agree to those terms.
Her suggestion was not even remotely related to the topic being discussed.
He did not seem remotely interested in what they were saying.

Synset Examples

when the measured speech of the chorus passes over into song the tones are, remotely but unmistakably, those taught by the orthodox liturgy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: directly - in a direct or immediate manner

Etymology

From 'remote' + '-ly', from Latin 'remotus' (removed, distant).

2. ADV

/ rɪˈmoʊtli / (AmE), / rɪˈməʊtli / (BrE)
to a very slight degree; hardly at all; only slightly or faintly.
to a very slight degree; only faintly

Examples

It is remotely possible that the plan will succeed, but I doubt it.
He wasn't even remotely interested in the topic they were discussing.
None of the offers were remotely close to what she was hoping for.

Synset Examples

it is remotely possible

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slightly - to a small degree or extent
  • Antonym: considerably - to a much greater degree; substantially

Etymology

From 'remote' + adverbial suffix '-ly'; 'remote' from Latin 'remotus' (removed, distant).
