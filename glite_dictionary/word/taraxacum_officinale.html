Taraxacum officinale

1. NOUN

/ təˌræksəkəm əˌfɪsɪˈnɑli / (AmE), / təˌræksəkəm əˌfɪsɪˈnɑːli / (BrE)
A Eurasian plant commonly known as the dandelion, widely naturalized as a weed in North America, with yellow flowers, toothed leaves, and fluffy seed heads; its leaves are used in salads and its roots and flowers can be used to make wine.
Common dandelion plant

Examples

The leaves of Taraxacum officinale are often added to salads for their slightly bitter flavor.
Researchers studied Taraxacum officinale for its potential medicinal properties.
In spring, fields are covered with the bright yellow flowers of Taraxacum officinale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dandelion - common name for Taraxacum officinale

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name 'Taraxacum' (possibly from Arabic 'tarakhshaqūn', meaning dandelion) and Latin 'officinale' ('of the storeroom', used for plants with medicinal properties).
