to impale

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈpeɪl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpeɪl / (BrE)
to pierce or kill with a sharp object, especially a spear or pole, by thrusting it through the body
pierce or kill with a sharp object

Examples

In ancient times, enemies were sometimes impaled as a gruesome form of execution.
The hunter accidentally impaled his boot on a sharp branch while walking through the forest.
She watched in horror as the villain threatened to impale the hero with a steel spear.

Synset Examples

the enemies were impaled and left to die

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pierce - to make a hole in something with a sharp object
  • Synonym: stab - to wound or kill by thrusting a pointed weapon

Etymology

From Latin 'impalare', from 'in-' (in) + 'palus' (stake).

2. VERB

/ ɪmˈpeɪl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpeɪl / (BrE)
to pierce or transfix something or someone with a sharp stake or point
pierce with a sharp stake or point

Examples

The knight was impaled by the enemy's lance during the tournament.
She impaled a piece of chicken on her fork and took a bite.
The artist impaled fruit slices on thin wooden skewers for the display.

Synset Examples

impale a shrimp on a skewer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pierce - to puncture or pass through with a sharp object
  • Synonym: skewer - to fasten or pierce with a pin or skewer

Etymology

From Latin 'impalare', from 'in-' (in, on) + 'palus' (stake).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01446945-v
Synset POS: v
