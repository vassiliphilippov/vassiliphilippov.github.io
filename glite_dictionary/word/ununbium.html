ununbium

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌn.ʌnˈbɪː.i.əm / (AmE), / ˌʌn.ʌnˈbɪː.i.əm / (BrE)
a former name for the chemical element with atomic number 112, now known as copernicium
former name for element 112 (copernicium)

Examples

The element ununbium was discovered in 1996 by a team of German scientists.
Before being officially named, ununbium was used in scientific literature to refer to element 112.
Research on ununbium helped scientists understand the properties of superheavy elements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copernicium - the chemical element with atomic number 112

Etymology

From Latin roots: 'un' (one), repeated to represent the digits one-one-two (112), plus 'bi' (two) and the standard '-ium' ending for elements.
