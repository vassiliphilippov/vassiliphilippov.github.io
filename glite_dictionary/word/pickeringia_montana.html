Pickeringia montana

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪkəˈrɪndʒiə mɒnˈtænə / (AmE), / ˌpɪkərɪŋˈiːə mɒnˈtɑːnə / (BrE)
A spiny evergreen shrub with showy rose and purple flowers, native to dry, rocky mountain slopes of California.
spiny Californian shrub with rose-purple flowers

Examples

The steep hillside was covered with dense stands of Pickeringia montana.
Botanists studied the adaptation of Pickeringia montana to the region's dry climate.
Hikers admired the striking rose and purple blooms of Pickeringia montana among the rocks.

Etymology

Named after Charles Pickering, an American naturalist; from Latin 'montana' meaning 'of the mountains'.
