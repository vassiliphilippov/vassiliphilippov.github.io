moleskin

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊlˌskɪn / (AmE), / ˈməʊl.skɪn / (BrE)
a durable cotton fabric with a soft, velvety surface, used especially for clothing and protective garments
soft, durable cotton fabric

Examples

His jacket was made of moleskin, which kept him warm throughout the winter.
The tailor recommended moleskin for its durability and comfort.
Many work trousers are sewn from moleskin to withstand heavy use.

Etymology

From 'mole' (the animal) + 'skin', originally for its resemblance to mole fur.
