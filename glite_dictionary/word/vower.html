vower

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaʊ.ər / (AmE), / ˈvaʊ.ə / (BrE)
a person who makes a solemn promise or vow to do something or behave in a certain way
person making a solemn vow

Examples

Many religious vowers dedicate their lives to service and prayer.
The ceremony was attended by several vowers who pledged their commitment to the cause.
Throughout history, vowers of chastity have played important roles in spiritual communities.

Synset Examples

young vowers of eternal love
there are many vowers of chastity but few who observe it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vow - a solemn promise or assertion

Etymology

From 'vow' + '-er', following English agent noun formation.
