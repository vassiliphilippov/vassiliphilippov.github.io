pikestaff

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪkˌstæf / (AmE), / ˈpaɪk.stɑːf / (BrE)
The long wooden shaft or staff of a pike, a type of spear formerly used in warfare.
shaft of a pike weapon

Examples

The soldier leaned heavily on his pikestaff after a long day's march.
During the battle, his pikestaff snapped under the force of the attack.
In the museum, a centuries-old pikestaff was displayed alongside other medieval weapons.

Etymology

From 'pike' (a type of spear) + 'staff' (a long stick), both of Old and Middle English origin.
