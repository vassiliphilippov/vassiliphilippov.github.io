eidetic

1. ADJ

/ aɪˈdɛt̬ɪk / (AmE), / aɪˈdɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to exceptionally vivid and accurate visual images, as if seen in a photograph; often used in psychology.
having vivid, photographic visual images

Examples

Some children with eidetic memory can recall pages from a book with remarkable clarity.
The artist's eidetic power allowed her to paint landscapes in flawless detail from memory.
Researchers studied subjects with eidetic imagery to understand the nature of photographic memory.

Etymology

From Greek eidetikos (pertaining to images), from eidos (form, shape).
