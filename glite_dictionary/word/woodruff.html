woodruff

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdˌrʌf / (AmE), / ˈwʊd.rʌf / (BrE)
A fragrant perennial plant of the genus Galium, typically with small white flowers and narrow leaves, used in flavoring and as a ground cover.
fragrant perennial plant used in flavoring

Examples

Sweet woodruff is often used to flavor traditional German May wine.
The shady patch in the garden was quickly covered by woodruff.
The dried leaves of woodruff give off a delicate, sweet scent.

Etymology

From Middle English woodrove, from Old English wudurofe, meaning 'wood plant of pleasant smell'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdˌrʌf / (AmE), / ˈwʊd.rʌf / (BrE)
Any plant of the genus Asperula, especially sweet-scented species often found in woodland areas.
plant of the genus Asperula

Examples

The shady patch in the garden is covered with fragrant woodruff.
Some traditional German desserts are flavored with woodruff syrup.
Botanists observed several varieties of woodruff in the forest understory.

Etymology

From Middle English 'woderove', related to Old High German 'wudero', meaning a type of plant.
