topology

1. NOUN

/ təˈpɑlədʒi / (AmE), / təˈpɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study of anatomical regions of the body and the relationships among structures within those regions, such as muscles, nerves, and arteries
study of body regions in anatomy
medical

Examples

Medical students often study topology to understand how different organs relate to each other in a given region.
A detailed knowledge of topology helps surgeons plan complex operations more safely.
His research focuses on the topology of the brain, mapping out the connections between various anatomical structures.

Etymology

From Greek 'topos' (place) + '-logy' (study of).

2. NOUN

/ təˈpɑlədʒi / (AmE), / təˈpɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of mathematics that studies the properties of shapes and spaces that are preserved under continuous transformations, such as stretching or bending, without tearing or gluing
branch of mathematics on spatial properties

Examples

She is studying topology to better understand continuous transformations of geometric shapes.
One fascinating aspect of topology is how a coffee cup and a donut are considered equivalent shapes.
The concept of connectedness is fundamental in topology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geometry - branch of mathematics that deals with points, lines, shapes, and space

Etymology

From Greek 'topos' (place) + '-logy' (study of).

3. NOUN

/ təˈpɑlədʒi / (AmE), / təˈpɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study of the physical features and history of a particular place, especially as revealed by its topography
study of place's physical features and history

Examples

The topology of the valley reveals clues about its ancient glacial past.
Researchers analyzed the topology of the region to understand erosion patterns.
Modern maps often include details on the topology of important historical sites.

Synset Examples

Greenland's topology has been shaped by the glaciers of the ice age

Related Terms

  • Synonym: topography - the arrangement of the natural and artificial physical features of an area

Etymology

From Greek topos (place) + -logy (study of).

4. NOUN

/ təˈpɑlədʒi / (AmE), / təˈpɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the arrangement or configuration of elements (such as nodes, links, and devices) in a communication network
network configuration or layout

Examples

The star topology is commonly used in local area networks.
Changing the network's topology can improve performance and reliability.
Understanding topology is essential for configuring complex communication systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: network - a system of connected computers or devices

Etymology

From Greek 'topos' (place) + 'logos' (study), originally referring to the study of geometric properties, later applied to networks.
