amphibolite

1. NOUN

/ æmˈfɪbəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / æmˈfɪb.ə.laɪt / (BrE)
A metamorphic rock chiefly composed of amphibole minerals and plagioclase feldspar.
metamorphic rock of amphibole and plagioclase

Examples

Geologists discovered a layer of amphibolite beneath the granite bedrock.
The mountain range contains significant deposits of amphibolite, giving the landscape its dark appearance.
A thin section of amphibolite reveals its characteristic interlocking crystals under the microscope.

Etymology

From amphibole (the main mineral component) + -ite (rock or mineral suffix).
