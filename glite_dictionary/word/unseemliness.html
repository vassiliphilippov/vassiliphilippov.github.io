unseemliness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈsimlinəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈsiːmlɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being inappropriate or not proper, especially in social behavior or appearance
the state of being improper or inappropriate

Examples

His constant interruptions displayed a remarkable unseemliness that shocked everyone at the meeting.
She disapproved of the unseemliness of their loud laughter during the formal ceremony.
The unseemliness of his remarks made the discussion uncomfortable for all present.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impropriety - behavior or action that is not proper or appropriate
  • Antonym: propriety - behavior that is considered correct and proper

Etymology

From unseemly + -ness, meaning the state or quality of being unseemly.
