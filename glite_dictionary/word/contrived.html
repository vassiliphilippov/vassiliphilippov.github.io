contrived

1. ADJ

/ kənˈtraɪvd / (AmE), / kənˈtraɪvd / (BrE)
deliberately created rather than arising naturally or spontaneously; seeming artificial or forced.
deliberately created, artificial

Examples

The movie's happy ending felt a bit contrived to most viewers.
His apology sounded contrived and insincere.
The plot relies on several contrived coincidences to bring the characters together.

Synset Examples

a novel with a contrived ending

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artificial - made or produced by human beings rather than occurring naturally
  • Antonym: natural - existing in or caused by nature; not artificial

Etymology

From past participle of 'contrive', from Old French 'controver', from Latin 'contropare' (to compare, invent).

2. ADJ

/ kənˈtraɪvd / (AmE), / kənˈtraɪvd / (BrE)
deliberately created rather than arising naturally; artificially formal or lacking spontaneity.
artificially formal; not natural

Examples

His apology seemed so contrived that no one believed he was truly sorry.
The dialogue in the play was criticized for being contrived and unnatural.
She adopted a contrived air of innocence whenever the topic came up.

Synset Examples

that artificial humility that her husband hated
contrived coyness
a stilted letter of acknowledgment
when people try to correct their speech they develop a stilted pronunciation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artificial - made or produced by human beings rather than occurring naturally
  • Synonym: stilted - stiff, self-conscious, or unnatural (especially in speech or writing)
  • Antonym: natural - existing in or caused by nature; not made or caused by humankind

Etymology

From the verb 'contrive', from Middle English 'contreven', from Old French 'controver', from Latin 'contropare' (to compare).

Synset POS: s
