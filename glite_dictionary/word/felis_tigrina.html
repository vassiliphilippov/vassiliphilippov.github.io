Felis tigrina

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːlɪs taɪˈɡriːnə / (AmE), / ˈfiːlɪs tɪˈɡriːnə / (BrE)
A medium-sized wildcat native to Central and South America, distinguished by its dark-striped coat.
Central/South American wildcat species

Examples

The Felis tigrina is often spotted hunting at night in the dense forests.
Conservationists are concerned about the declining population of Felis tigrina due to habitat loss.
The characteristic markings of Felis tigrina make it relatively easy to identify in the wild.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Felis' (cat) and 'tigrina' (tiger-like).
