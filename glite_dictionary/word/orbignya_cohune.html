Orbignya cohune

1. NOUN

/ ɔrˈbɪɡ.njə koʊˈhuːni / (AmE), / ɔːˈbɪɡ.njə kəʊˈhuːni / (BrE)
A species of tropical American feather palm whose large nuts produce valuable oil and a form of vegetable ivory.
tropical American feather palm

Examples

The oil extracted from Orbignya cohune nuts is valued for its versatility in cooking and cosmetics.
Artisans sometimes use the vegetable ivory from Orbignya cohune seeds to carve buttons and jewelry.
Many tropical forests feature Orbignya cohune palms growing in dense clusters near rivers.

Etymology

Genus name 'Orbignya' honors French naturalist Alcide d'Orbigny; 'cohune' relates to local Central American naming.
