Salamandra maculosa

1. NOUN

/ sæləˈmændrə mækjuˈloʊsə / (AmE), / sæləˈmændrə mækjʊˈləʊsə / (BrE)
A European salamander species with dark skin and distinctive yellow spots, commonly known as the fire salamander.
a European spotted salamander species

Examples

The Salamandra maculosa is often found in damp forest habitats throughout central and southern Europe.
Researchers have studied the toxins secreted by Salamandra maculosa as a defense mechanism.
In the wild, the bright patterns of Salamandra maculosa serve as a warning to potential predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire salamander - Common name for Salamandra maculosa

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Latin 'salamandra' (salamander) with 'maculosa' (spotted).
