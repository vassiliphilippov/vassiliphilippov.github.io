first-string

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɝst ˌstrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ˌstrɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a player or team) being among the regular or main members, especially as a starter or first choice.
regular or starting member of a team

Examples

As a first-string quarterback, she led her team to the finals.
Only the first-string players practiced with the head coach today.
He was promoted to the first-string lineup after several impressive games.

Synset Examples

first-string players

Related Terms

  • Antonym: second-string - not a regular or starting member of a team; a reserve player
  • Synonym: starter - a player who regularly starts games for a team

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɝst ˌstrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ˌstrɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a team member) belonging to the group of regular players who usually start the game, rather than substitutes.
starting player; not a substitute
american

Examples

He was promoted to the first-string lineup after showing impressive skills in practice.
The coach decided to rest his first-string players before the championship match.
Unlike last season, she's now a first-string guard for the basketball team.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: second-string - a player or team member who is a substitute rather than a starter

Etymology

From the phrase 'first string,' originally referring to the primary set of strings on an instrument, later applied to sports for first-choice players.
