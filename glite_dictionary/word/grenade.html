grenade

1. NOUN

/ ɡrəˈneɪd / (AmE), / ɡrɪˈneɪd / (BrE)
A small bomb designed to be thrown by hand or launched to explode and cause damage in a specified area.
small explosive bomb for hand or launcher use

Examples

The soldier threw a grenade over the wall to clear the building.
During training, recruits learn how to handle a grenade safely.
The blast from the grenade damaged several vehicles nearby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bomb - an explosive device used as a weapon

Etymology

From French 'grenade', originally meaning 'pomegranate' due to the shape resemblance.
