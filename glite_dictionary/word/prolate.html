prolate

1. ADJ

/ ˈproʊ.leɪt / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.leɪt / (BrE)
(of a spheroid or body) having a shape that is elongated or extended along the axis from end to end, like an egg.
elongated, egg-shaped

Examples

The planet takes on a prolate shape due to rapid rotation.
Some viruses have a distinctly prolate capsid, unlike the more common spherical forms.
An American football is roughly prolate in shape, differing from a true sphere.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: oblate - (of a spheroid) flattened at the poles; the opposite of prolate
  • Synonym: ellipsoid - a surface whose plane sections are all ellipses; related geometric solid

Etymology

From Latin 'prolatus' (extended, stretched out).

2. ADJ

/ ˈproʊ.leɪt / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.leɪt / (BrE)
(of a spheroid or geometric body) having a longer polar diameter than equatorial diameter; elongated along the axis of rotation.
elongated along polar axis (geometry)

Examples

The planet's rapid rotation causes it to become slightly prolate in shape.
Certain seeds are prolate, appearing elongated rather than spherical.
A rugby ball is a familiar object with a prolate form.

Synset Examples

a prolate spheroid is generated by revolving an ellipse about its major axis

Related Terms

  • Antonym: oblate - having the equatorial diameter greater than the polar diameter

Etymology

From Latin prolat-, past participle of proferre, meaning 'extended'.
