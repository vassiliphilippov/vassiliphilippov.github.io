mizenmast

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪz.ənˌmæst / (AmE), / ˈmɪz.ənˌmɑːst / (BrE)
the third mast from the bow in vessels with three or more masts, or the aft and shorter mast of a yawl, ketch, or dandy
third or aft mast on certain ships

Examples

The captain ordered the crew to lower the mizenmast during the storm.
On a ketch, the mizenmast is noticeably shorter than the mainmast.
They repaired a tear in the sail attached to the mizenmast before continuing their journey.

Etymology

From Middle English 'mesen-mast', from Old French 'misene', from Old Italian 'mezzana' (middle), referring to its position.
