quislingism

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪzlɪŋˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈkwɪzlɪŋɪzəm / (BrE)
the act of cooperating traitorously with an enemy that is occupying one's country
traitorous cooperation with an occupying enemy

Examples

The new regime was widely condemned for its blatant quislingism during the occupation.
Accusations of quislingism followed those who had worked closely with the enemy authorities.
History remembers his acts of quislingism as a betrayal of national trust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treason - the crime of betraying one's country
  • Antonym: patriotism - devotion to and vigorous support for one's country

Etymology

Formed from 'Quisling', after Vidkun Quisling (1887–1945), Norwegian politician who collaborated with Nazi Germany + -ism.
