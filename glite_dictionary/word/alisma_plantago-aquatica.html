Alisma plantago-aquatica

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɪzmə plænˌteɪɡoʊ əˈkwætɪkə / (AmE), / əˈlɪzmə plænˌteɪɡəʊ əˈkwætɪkə / (BrE)
a perennial aquatic plant, also known as water plantain, with broad leaves and clusters of small white or pinkish flowers, commonly found in marshes and shallow waters
aquatic plant with broad leaves and small flowers

Examples

Alisma plantago-aquatica can often be found growing along the edges of slow-moving streams and ponds.
Botanists recorded the first flowering of Alisma plantago-aquatica at the nature reserve last summer.
The broad, pointed leaves of Alisma plantago-aquatica make it easy to identify among other marsh plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water plantain - common English name for Alisma plantago-aquatica

Etymology

From Latin: Alisma (the genus name), plantago-aquatica ('plantain of the water'), describing its resemblance to plantain leaves and aquatic habitat.
