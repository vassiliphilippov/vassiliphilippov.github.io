animatronics

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ə.məˈtrɑː.nɪks / (AmE), / ˌæn.ɪ.məˈtrɒn.ɪks / (BrE)
the technique of making and operating lifelike robots, especially of animals or people, often used in entertainment settings such as films and theme parks
lifelike robotic figures for entertainment

Examples

The lifelike dinosaurs in the exhibit were created using advanced animatronics.
Many theme parks use animatronics to bring their attractions to life.
The movie's special effects team included experts in animatronics and puppetry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robotics - the scientific study and application of robots

Etymology

Blend of 'animate' and 'electronics', popularized in the 1960s by Disney.
