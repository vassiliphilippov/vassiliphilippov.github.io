Allies

1. PROPN

/ ˈælaɪz / (AmE), / ˈælaɪz / (BrE)
the nations allied with Great Britain, France, Russia, and their partners in opposing the Central Powers during World War I
powers allied against Central Powers in World War I

Examples

During World War I, the Allies worked together to defeat the Central Powers.
France and the United Kingdom were leading countries among the Allies in World War I.
After joining the war, the United States provided significant support to the Allies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Central Powers - nations opposed to the Allies in World War I

2. PROPN

/ ˈælaɪz / (AmE), / ˈælaɪz / (BrE)
the group of nations, including the United States, the United Kingdom, the Soviet Union, and others, that fought against the Axis powers during World War II and later formed the United Nations
coalition of nations opposing the Axis in WWII

Examples

The Allies celebrated victory in Europe on May 8, 1945.
During World War II, the Allies coordinated military strategy against the Axis.
After the war, the Allies played a key role in establishing the United Nations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Axis - the nations opposed to the Allies in World War II

Etymology

From ally, ultimately from Latin 'alligare' (to bind to); used as a collective name from the mid-20th century.
