to understate

1. VERB

/ ˌʌndɚˈsteɪt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈsteɪt / (BrE)
to describe or represent something as being smaller, less important, or less serious than it really is
describe as less important than is true

Examples

It would be wrong to understate the importance of this discovery.
He tends to understate his achievements when talking to others.
The report understated the financial risks involved in the project.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to exaggerate - to represent something as greater or more important than it is
  • Synonym: to downplay - to make something appear less important than it is

Etymology

Formed from 'under-' (meaning less than) and 'state' (to say or describe), first attested in early 20th century.
