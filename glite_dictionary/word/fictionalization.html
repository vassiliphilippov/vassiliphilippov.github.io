fictionalization

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɪkʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɪkʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
A literary work that is based partly or wholly on real events but is presented as if it were fiction.
work based on real events but written as fiction

Examples

The movie is a fictionalization of true events from the 1960s civil rights movement.
Her latest novel is a fictionalization of her grandfather's experiences during the war.
There is ongoing debate about how much creative liberty a fictionalization should take with historical facts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adaptation - the process of adapting real events into art or literature

Etymology

From 'fictionalize' + '-ation' suffix.

2. NOUN

/ ˌfɪkʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɪkʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of creating a fictional version of something, especially by presenting real events or people in a fictional form
the creation of a fictional version

Examples

The author’s fictionalization of historical events brought new interest to the story.
Many movies are based on the fictionalization of real-life crimes.
His documentary blurs the line between fact and fictionalization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adaptation - the process of adapting something, especially from one form to another

Etymology

From 'fictionalize' + '-ation', formed from 'fiction' (from Latin 'fictio') and '-alize'.
