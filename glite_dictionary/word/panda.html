panda

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæn.də / (AmE), / ˈpæn.də / (BrE)
A large, black-and-white bear-like mammal native to bamboo forests in China and Tibet, known for its distinctive coloring and diet of bamboo.
large black-and-white mammal of China

Examples

The panda spends most of its day eating bamboo in the forests of China.
Many zoos around the world have pandas as one of their main attractions.
Scientists are working to protect the habitat of the endangered panda.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant panda - another name for the panda (Ailuropoda melanoleuca)

Etymology

From French *panda*, possibly from Nepali *ponya* (bamboo eater).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpæn.də / (AmE), / ˈpæn.də / (BrE)
a small, reddish-brown mammal native to the Himalayas and China, resembling a raccoon and distinct from the giant panda; also known as the red panda.
small, raccoon-like mammal of Asia (red panda)

Examples

The panda spends most of its time in trees searching for bamboo and berries.
Unlike the giant panda, the panda has a long, bushy tail and reddish fur.
The zookeeper explained that the panda is more closely related to raccoons than to bears.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red panda - another name for the small, reddish-brown mammal native to Asia
  • Antonym: giant panda - large black-and-white bear native to China, distinct from red panda

Etymology

From Nepali 'ponya', via French 'panda'.
