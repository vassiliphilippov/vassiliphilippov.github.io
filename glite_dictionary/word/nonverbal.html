nonverbal

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˈvɜrbəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈvɜːbəl / (BrE)
lacking verbal skill, especially in using spoken or written language; not able to communicate effectively with words.
lacking verbal skill

Examples

Many children with autism are considered nonverbal because they have difficulty speaking.
The school offers special support for students who are nonverbal and need alternative communication methods.
She developed innovative ways to teach literacy to nonverbal learners.

Synset Examples

rural students often come from nonverbal backgrounds

Related Terms

  • Antonym: verbal - having skill with words, relating to spoken or written language

Etymology

From prefix 'non-' (not) + 'verbal', from Latin 'verbalis' (of words).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00501228-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ nɑnˈvɜrbəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈvɜːbəl / (BrE)
Communicated or expressed without the use of spoken or written language.
not using words or language

Examples

Her nonverbal cues made it clear she was upset.
The psychologist administered a nonverbal reasoning test to the child.
Much communication between people is actually nonverbal in nature.

Synset Examples

a nonverbal intelligence test

Related Terms

  • Antonym: verbal - using words or language

Etymology

From prefix 'non-' meaning 'not' + 'verbal' (from Latin 'verbalis', from 'verbum' meaning 'word').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00501107-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ nɑnˈvɜrbəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈvɜːbəl / (BrE)
communicated or expressed without the use of spoken or written words; involving gestures, facial expressions, or other visual cues rather than language
not involving spoken or written words

Examples

She relied on nonverbal cues like eye contact and gestures during the interview.
Children often express their feelings through nonverbal communication, such as smiling or crying.
The teacher noticed the nonverbal signals of confusion among her students.

Synset Examples

the study of gestural communication
art like gesture is a form of nonverbal expression

Related Terms

  • Antonym: verbal - using words, especially spoken ones, to communicate

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'non-' (not) + 'verbal' (pertaining to words).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00500009-s
Synset POS: s
