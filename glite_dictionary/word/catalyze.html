to catalyze

1. VERB

/ ˈkætəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkætəlaɪz / (BrE)
to cause or accelerate (a reaction) by acting as a catalyst; to make a process or event happen more quickly or easily
cause or speed up a reaction or process

Examples

Enzymes catalyze chemical reactions vital for life.
Her arrival helped catalyze changes within the company culture.
The discovery catalyzed a wave of innovation across the industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to facilitate - to make a process easier
  • Antonym: to inhibit - to slow down or prevent a process

Etymology

Formed from 'catalyst' (from Greek 'kata-', down, + 'lysis', loosening) + '-ize' suffix.
