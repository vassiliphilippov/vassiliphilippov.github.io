sorbate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔːrˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɔːˌbeɪt / (BrE)
A material that has been or is capable of being taken up by another substance by either absorption or adsorption.
material absorbed or adsorbed by another
medical

Examples

When carbon dioxide acts as a sorbate, it can be trapped by activated carbon filters.
The sorbate was detected in the porous host after the absorption experiment.
Researchers examined how the temperature affected the rate of sorbate uptake in the material.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sorbent - a substance that takes up another substance by absorption or adsorption

Etymology

From Latin 'sorbere' (to suck in, absorb), influenced by scientific usage of 'sorb-' meaning 'to take up'.
