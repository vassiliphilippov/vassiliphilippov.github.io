snort

1. NOUN

/ snɔrt / (AmE), / snɔːt / (BrE)
a sudden, rough noise made by forcing air through the nose, often to express displeasure, contempt, or amusement
rough noise made through the nose

Examples

She gave a loud snort of disapproval when she heard the news.
His comment was met with a snort from the audience.
With an indignant snort, he turned and left the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grunt - a low, short guttural sound, often to express effort or displeasure
  • Synonym: sniff - to draw air audibly through the nose to detect a smell or express emotion

Etymology

Imitative origin, representing the sound produced.

2. VERB

/ snɔrt / (AmE), / snɔːt / (BrE)
to inhale a powdered drug through the nose, especially as a means of taking a recreational drug
inhale a drug through the nose

Examples

He was caught trying to snort cocaine in the bathroom at the party.
Some people snort crushed prescription pills to get high.
She regretted the time she decided to snort a line of ketamine with her friends.

Synset Examples

The addict was snorting cocaine almost every day
the kids were huffing glue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inhale - to breathe in; to draw air, smoke, or gas into the lungs

3. VERB

/ snɔrt / (AmE), / snɔːt / (BrE)
to make a sudden, harsh sound through the nose by forcefully exhaling air, often to express anger, amusement, or disbelief
exhale sharply through the nose

Examples

She couldn't help but snort with laughter at the comedian's joke.
The horse snorted and stamped its hoof impatiently.
He snorted at the suggestion, clearly unimpressed.

Synset Examples

The critic snorted contemptuously

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sniff - to draw air audibly through the nose; to inhale sharply through the nose

Etymology

Imitative of the sound; related to Middle English 'snorten', akin to 'snore'.

Source: WordNet

/ snɔrt / (AmE), / snɔːt / (BrE)

Examples

6. NOUN

/ snɔrt / (AmE), / snɔːt / (BrE)
A sudden, harsh, disrespectful laugh, often made by forcing air noisily out through the nose.
a disrespectful, noisy laugh

Examples

She let out a loud snort at the ridiculous suggestion.
His joke was met with a snort from the back of the room.
Unable to hide her contempt, she made a rude snort of laughter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giggle - a light, silly laugh
  • Synonym: snicker - a suppressed laugh, often disrespectful or mocking
  • Antonym: sob - to cry noisily, often the opposite emotional outburst of laughing disrespectfully
