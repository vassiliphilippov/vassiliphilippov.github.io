sibyl

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪbɪl / (BrE)
(in ancient times) a woman regarded as oracular and able to foretell the future; a female prophet or fortune-teller.
female prophet or fortune-teller

Examples

The ancient Greeks would consult a sibyl before making important decisions.
Legends describe the sibyl as delivering her prophecies in mysterious riddles.
In art, the sibyl is often depicted holding a scroll or book of visions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oracle - a person through whom a deity speaks; a prophecy
  • Synonym: prophetess - a female prophet; a woman who foretells events
  • Synonym: soothsayer - a person who predicts the future; fortune-teller

Etymology

From Latin Sibylla, from Greek Sibylla (prophetess).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪb.ɪl / (BrE)
(in ancient Rome and Greece) a woman regarded as an oracle or prophet, believed to possess divine inspiration and foretell the future.
female prophet or oracle in antiquity

Examples

The ancient Greeks believed each sibyl could see into the future and interpret the will of the gods.
During the ceremony, the sibyl pronounced prophecies in a trance-like state.
Many legends describe how a sibyl would deliver her messages from a cave or sacred location.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prophetess - a female prophet
  • Synonym: oracle - a person who delivers authoritative or wise statements or predictions

Etymology

From Latin 'Sibylla', from Greek 'Sibylla', meaning prophetess.
