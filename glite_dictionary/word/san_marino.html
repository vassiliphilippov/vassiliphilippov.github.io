San Marino

1. PROPN

/ sæn məˈriːnoʊ / (AmE), / san məˈriːnəʊ / (BrE)
the capital and only city of the Republic of San Marino, situated on the slopes of Mount Titano
capital of San Marino (country)

Examples

The historic center of San Marino is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
Thousands of tourists visit San Marino each year to enjoy its medieval architecture.
From San Marino's city walls, you can see stunning views of the surrounding Italian countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: San Marino - a European microstate surrounded by Italy

Etymology

Named after Saint Marinus, the founder of the city-state.

2. PROPN

/ sæn məˈriːnoʊ / (AmE), / san məˈriːnəʊ / (BrE)
an independent republic in southern Europe, entirely surrounded by Italy; the smallest republic in the world and the oldest surviving sovereign state, founded in 301 CE.
an independent republic surrounded by Italy

Examples

Many tourists visit San Marino each year to see its medieval architecture and stunning views.
Despite being surrounded by Italy, San Marino maintains its own government and postal system.
The football team representing San Marino is famous for its enthusiastic spirit despite its small size.

Etymology

Named after Saint Marinus, a stonecutter from present-day Croatia who is credited with founding the state in 301 CE.
