mysophobia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪsəˈfoʊbiə / (AmE), / ˌmʌɪsəˈfəʊbɪə / (BrE)
An extreme or irrational fear of dirt, germs, or contamination.
fear of dirt or contamination
medical

Examples

His mysophobia made it difficult for him to shake hands with others.
People with mysophobia may obsessively clean their homes to avoid germs.
After reading about bacteria, she developed a mild case of mysophobia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: germophobia - fear of germs or contamination
  • Antonym: agoraphobia - fear of open or crowded spaces

Etymology

From Greek 'mysos' (uncleanness, dirt) + '-phobia' (fear).
