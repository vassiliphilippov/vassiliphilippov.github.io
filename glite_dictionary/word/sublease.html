sublease

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbˌlis / (AmE), / ˈsʌbliːs / (BrE)
a lease agreement made by a tenant (lessee) who rents out all or part of the leased property to another person
a lease from a tenant to another

Examples

The tenant signed a sublease with a friend to cover the last six months of her rental agreement.
It's important to read the sublease carefully before moving into a sublet apartment.
He offered a sublease on his office space while he worked remotely abroad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sublet - to rent all or part of a leased property to another person

Etymology

From sub- (under) + lease; patterned after 'sublet'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13269827-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈsʌbˌlis / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌliːs / (BrE)
to lease or rent all or part of a leased or rented property to another person
lease property to another tenant

Examples

After moving abroad, they decided to sublease their apartment to a friend for a year.
Tenants must get written permission from their landlord before they sublease any part of the property.
She is currently subleasing her studio while traveling for work.

Synset Examples

We sublet our apartment over the summer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sublet - to lease or rent property to another where the original party is still a tenant

Etymology

From 'sub-' (Latin: under, below) + 'lease'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02214043-v
Synset POS: v
