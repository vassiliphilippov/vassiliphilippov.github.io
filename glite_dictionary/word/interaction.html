interaction

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntəˈrækʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntərˈækʃən / (BrE)
the process by which two or more people or things affect each other through mutual or reciprocal action
mutual action or influence

Examples

The interaction between the teacher and students made the class more engaging.
Social interaction is important for children's development.
We observed a fascinating interaction between the chemicals in the lab.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: communication - the exchange of information
  • Antonym: isolation - the state of being alone or separated from others

Etymology

From Latin 'inter-' (between) + 'action', from 'actio' (a doing).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.tɚˈræk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈræk.ʃən / (BrE)
(physics) The process by which energy is transferred between elementary particles, or between a particle and a field, or between fields, typically mediated by gauge bosons.
energy exchange between particles or fields (physics)

Examples

The electromagnetic interaction plays a crucial role in the behavior of atoms and molecules.
Physicists study the weak interaction to understand processes like beta decay.
The discovery of the Higgs boson improved our understanding of field interaction in particle physics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: force - a fundamental interaction in physics (gravitational, electromagnetic, strong, weak)

Etymology

From inter- (between) + action, modeled on the scientific vocabulary of the 19th century.
