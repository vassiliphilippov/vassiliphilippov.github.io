Aristolochia serpentaria

1. NOUN

/ ˌærɪstəˈloʊkiə ˌsɜːrpɛnˈtɛriə / (AmE), / ˌærɪstəˈlɒkiə ˌsɜːpɛnˈtɛəriə / (BrE)
A North American woodland plant of the birthwort family, with slender stems and heart-shaped leaves, also known as Virginia snakeroot.
North American birthwort plant

Examples

Aristolochia serpentaria thrives in rich woodlands across the eastern United States.
Early herbalists valued Aristolochia serpentaria for its supposed medicinal properties, especially against snakebites.
Botanists often identify Aristolochia serpentaria by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and slender stem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Virginia snakeroot - another common name for Aristolochia serpentaria
  • Synonym: birthwort - plant of the genus Aristolochia, valued in traditional medicine

Etymology

From the genus name 'Aristolochia' (Greek aristos 'best' + locheia 'childbirth') and species name 'serpentaria' (Latin, 'of snakes'), referencing its use against snakebite.
