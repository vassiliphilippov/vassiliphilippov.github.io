champaign

1. NOUN

/ ʃæmˈpeɪn / (AmE), / ʃæmˈpeɪn / (BrE)
An extensive tract of level, open land; a plain.
level open plain or field

Examples

They rode across the vast champaign, stretching as far as the eye could see.
Wildflowers dotted the champaign, adding color to the open landscape.
The army camped on the champaign before continuing its march toward the mountains.

Synset Examples

they emerged from the woods onto a vast open plain
he longed for the fields of his youth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plain - a large area of flat land with few trees
  • Degree Higher: plateau - an area of relatively level high ground

Etymology

From Old French champaigne, from Latin campania (level country).
