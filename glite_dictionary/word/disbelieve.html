to disbelieve

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪsbɪˈliv / (AmE), / ˌdɪsbɪˈliːv / (BrE)
to not accept something as true; to refuse to believe.
refuse to believe

Examples

Many people disbelieve stories that seem too extraordinary to be true.
She found it hard to disbelieve his sincere apology.
Despite the evidence, he continued to disbelieve in climate change.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to believe - to accept that something is true or real
  • Synonym: doubt - to feel uncertain about something

Etymology

From Latin 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'believe'.
