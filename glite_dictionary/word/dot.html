dot

1. VERB

/ dɑt / (AmE), / dɒt / (BrE)
to mark or cover with a small round spot; to place a dot on or over something, especially as in writing
to mark with a dot

Examples

Please dot each 'i' carefully when writing your name.
The teacher asked the students to dot the correct answers on the test sheet.
Make sure you dot your i's and cross your t's in the application.

Synset Examples

dot your i's

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spot - to mark with small spots or patches

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'dot' (small mark or speck).
