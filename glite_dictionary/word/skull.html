skull

1. NOUN

/ skʌl / (AmE), / skʌl / (BrE)
the bony structure that forms the head and encloses and protects the brain in vertebrates
the bony skeleton of the head

Examples

The archaeologists discovered an ancient skull buried beneath the ruins.
Wearing a helmet protects your skull from injury during accidents.
The medical student carefully examined the anatomy of the human skull.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cranium - the part of the skull that encloses the brain

Etymology

From Old Norse 'skalli' (bald head).
