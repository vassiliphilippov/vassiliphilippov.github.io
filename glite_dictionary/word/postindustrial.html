postindustrial

1. ADJ

/ ˌpoʊstɪnˈdʌstriəl / (AmE), / ˌpəʊstɪnˈdʌstriəl / (BrE)
relating to a society or economy in which manufacturing has declined in importance and services, information, and research have increased
relating to a shift from manufacturing to services and information

Examples

The transition to a postindustrial economy has changed the city's workforce and landscape.
Many postindustrial societies invest heavily in education and technology sectors.
Some researchers argue that social structures also shift in the postindustrial phase.

Synset Examples

postindustrial countries

Related Terms

  • Antonym: industrial - relating to industry or manufacturing

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'post-' (after) and 'industrial', indicating 'after the industrial period'.
