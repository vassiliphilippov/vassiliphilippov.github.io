southern

1. ADJ

/ ˈsʌðərn / (AmE), / ˈsʌðən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the southern region of the United States, especially in terms of culture, traditions, or geography.
of the southern U.S. or its culture
american

Examples

She grew up in a southern town known for its rich history and warmth.
The restaurant specializes in southern cuisine, offering dishes like fried chicken and cornbread.
He spoke with a southern accent that immediately revealed his roots.

Synset Examples

southern plantations

Related Terms

  • Antonym: northern - relating to or characteristic of the northern region of the United States

2. ADJ

/ ˈsʌðərn / (AmE), / ˈsʌðən / (BrE)
Coming from or situated in the south, especially referring to wind.
from the south, esp. wind

Examples

A southern breeze brought warm air to the region.
The boat struggled against the strong southern wind.
Farmers worry when persistent southern winds threaten their crops.

Synset Examples

a hot southerly wind
southern breezes
the winds are southerly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: northern - from the north; especially of wind
  • Synonym: southerly - from the south; especially of wind or direction

Etymology

From Old English sūthern, from sūð ('south') + -ern.
