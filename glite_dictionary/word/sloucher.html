sloucher

1. NOUN

/ ˈslaʊtʃər / (AmE), / ˈslaʊtʃə / (BrE)
a person who has a habit of slouching, often standing or sitting with a drooping posture
person with a slouching posture

Examples

My mother always told me not to be a sloucher and to sit up straight at the table.
The teacher noticed several slouchers at the back of the classroom and asked them to adjust their posture.
Although he was bright, his sloucher stance made him look less confident during interviews.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slouch - to stand, sit, or move in a lazy or drooping way
  • Antonym: non-sloucher - a person who stands or sits upright

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'slouch' + the agent suffix '-er'.
