Mullus auratus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌləs ɔːˈreɪtəs / (AmE), / ˈmʌləs ɔːˈreɪtəs / (BrE)
A species of goatfish known for its bright scarlet body with two yellow to reddish stripes along its sides; commonly called the red goatfish.
a species of red goatfish

Examples

The fisherman was excited to catch a rare Mullus auratus during his trip.
Marine biologists study the diet of Mullus auratus to understand coral reef ecosystems.
A display at the aquarium featured a brightly colored Mullus auratus swimming among the coral.

Etymology

From Latin: 'mullus' (a kind of fish, mullet) and 'auratus' (gilded, golden).
