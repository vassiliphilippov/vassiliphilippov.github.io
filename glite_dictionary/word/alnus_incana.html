Alnus incana

1. NOUN

/ ˈælnəs ɪnˈkeɪnə / (AmE), / ˈal.nəs ɪŋˈkɑː.nə / (BrE)
A species of alder tree, native to Europe and parts of Asia, known as the grey or speckled alder, with grey bark and serrated leaves.
species of alder tree (grey alder)

Examples

Alnus incana is commonly found along riverbanks in central and eastern Europe.
The leaves of Alnus incana are distinctly serrated and pale beneath.
Unlike its relative the black alder, Alnus incana prefers drier soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grey alder - Common name for Alnus incana, an alder species with grey bark

Etymology

From Latin: 'Alnus' meaning alder, and 'incana' meaning greyish.
