agaric

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡærɪk / (AmE), / əˈɡarɪk / (BrE)
a fungus, typically with a cap and gills, some species of which were used to make punk for fuses or as tinder
type of fungus used for tinder or punk

Examples

In the past, agaric was commonly used to prepare the punk for slow-burning fuses.
The spongy texture of agaric makes it suitable for use as tinder.
Collectors sometimes dried agaric for use in traditional fire-starting methods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mushroom - a fleshy, spore-bearing fruiting body of a fungus, typically with a cap and stem

Etymology

From Latin 'agaricum', from Greek 'agarikon', meaning a kind of fungus.

2. NOUN

/ əˈɡærɪk / (AmE), / əˈɡarɪk / (BrE)
A type of mushroom with an umbrellalike cap and gills on the underside, belonging to the order Agaricales.
mushroom with cap and gills

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with bright red agaric mushrooms.
Some species of agaric are edible, while others can be highly poisonous.
She photographed a rare agaric she found growing beneath an oak tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mushroom - fungus with a stem, cap, and gills, especially one used as food

Etymology

From Latin agaricum (tree fungus), from Greek agarikon.
