Helianthemum scoparium

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːliˈænθəməm skoʊˈpɛriəm / (AmE), / ˌhiːliˈænθɛməm skəʊˈpɛriəm / (BrE)
A woody, yellow-flowered perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.
woody yellow-flowered perennial plant

Examples

The slopes were covered with Helianthemum scoparium in full bloom.
Botanists studied the habitat preferences of Helianthemum scoparium.
Gardeners value Helianthemum scoparium for its bright yellow flowers and hardiness.

Etymology

From Greek 'helios' (sun) + 'anthemon' (flower), with Latin species epithet 'scoparium' meaning 'broom-like'.
