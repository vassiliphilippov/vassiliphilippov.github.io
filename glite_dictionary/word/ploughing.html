ploughing

1. NOUN

/ ˈplaʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈplaʊɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of turning over the soil with a plough in preparation for planting crops.
action of turning soil with a plough

Examples

The ploughing of the fields was completed before the spring rains arrived.
Traditional ploughing methods require a strong team of oxen or horses.
He enjoyed watching the ploughing competitions at the rural fair.

Synset Examples

he hired someone to do the plowing for him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tilling - preparing land for crops by mechanical or manual means

Etymology

From Middle English 'plouen' (to plough), from Old English 'plōgian' (to plough), related to 'plough'.
