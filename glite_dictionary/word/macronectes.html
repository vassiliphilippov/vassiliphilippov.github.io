Macronectes

1. NOUN

/ ˌmækrəˈnɛktiz / (AmE), / ˌmakrəˈnɛktiːz / (BrE)
A genus of large seabirds known as giant petrels, found mainly in the Southern Hemisphere.
a genus of giant petrels (seabirds)

Examples

Macronectes species are the only petrels capable of scavenging on large carcasses.
The largest members of the Procellariidae family belong to the genus Macronectes.
Both the northern and southern giant petrels are classified under the Macronectes genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant petrel - a large seabird in the genus Macronectes

Etymology

From Greek 'makros' (large) + 'nectes' (swimmer).
