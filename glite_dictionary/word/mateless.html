mateless

1. ADJ

/ ˈmeɪtləs / (AmE), / ˈmeɪtləs / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) having no mate or marriage partner.
having no mate or partner

Examples

Despite the joy around her, she felt acutely mateless at the wedding.
The mateless bird called out through the night, searching for a companion.
Many people thrive while mateless, finding happiness in independence and friendship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: single - not married or involved in a romantic relationship
  • Synonym: unmarried - not having a spouse
  • Antonym: wedded - having a spouse; married

Etymology

Formed from 'mate' + '-less' (without).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmeɪtləs / (AmE), / ˈmeɪtləs / (BrE)
having no sexual mate; not paired with a mate.
without a sexual mate

Examples

The lonely swan remained mateless throughout the breeding season.
Some animals live a mateless existence if they cannot find a partner.
He wrote poetry about his mateless life and unfulfilled love.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unpaired - not having a mate or partner
  • Antonym: mated - having a sexual mate or partner

Etymology

Formed from mate (partner) + -less (without).
