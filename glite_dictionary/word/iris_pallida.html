Iris pallida

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪrɪs ˈpælɪdə / (AmE), / ˈaɪrɪs ˈpalɪdə / (BrE)
A European iris species known for its soft lilac-blue flowers.
species of European iris with lilac-blue flowers

Examples

The gardens were filled with the delicate blooms of Iris pallida in late spring.
Botanists admire Iris pallida for its pale blue petals and pleasant scent.
She planted Iris pallida along the border to add a touch of subtle color to the landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iris - a type of flowering plant with sword-shaped leaves and showy flowers

Etymology

From Latin: iris (the flower) and pallida (pale).
