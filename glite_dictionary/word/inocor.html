Inocor

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnəˌkɔr / (AmE), / ˈɪnəkɔː / (BrE)
the trade name of a drug used intravenously to treat heart failure by increasing the strength of heart muscle contractions
drug for heart failure (trade name)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Inocor for the patient's worsening heart failure.
Administration of Inocor requires careful monitoring of blood pressure and heart rhythm.
After receiving Inocor, the patient showed improved cardiac function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amrinone - generic name of the drug marketed as Inocor
