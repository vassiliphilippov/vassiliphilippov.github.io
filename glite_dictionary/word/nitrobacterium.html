nitrobacterium

1. NOUN

/ ˌnaɪtroʊbækˈtɪriəm / (AmE), / ˌnɪtrəʊbækˈtɪəriəm / (BrE)
a type of soil bacteria that plays a role in the nitrogen cycle by oxidizing ammonium or nitrite compounds
soil bacteria in the nitrogen cycle
medical

Examples

The presence of nitrobacterium is essential for maintaining soil fertility.
Scientists study how nitrobacterium affects crop yields in agricultural research.
Improper use of pesticides can harm nitrobacterium populations in the soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nitrobacter - a genus of bacteria that oxidize nitrites to nitrates

Etymology

From Latin 'nitrum' (natron, potash) and Greek 'baktērion' (small rod, diminutive of 'baktron', rod).
