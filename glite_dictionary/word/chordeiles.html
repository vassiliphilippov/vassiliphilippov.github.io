Chordeiles

1. NOUN

/ kɔrˈdaɪliːz / (AmE), / kɔːˈdaɪliːz / (BrE)
A genus of nocturnal birds in the nightjar family (Caprimulgidae), commonly known as nighthawks.
genus of birds (nighthawks)

Examples

The common nighthawk belongs to the genus Chordeiles.
Researchers have observed several species of Chordeiles migrating at dusk.
Members of Chordeiles are known for their distinctive booming calls during courtship displays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nighthawk - a bird belonging to the genus Chordeiles

Etymology

From Greek khordeílēs, an unidentified bird mentioned by Aristotle.
