Catostomidae

1. NOUN

/ kəˌtɑːstəˈmɪdiː / (AmE), / kəˌtɒstəˈmɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of freshwater fish known as suckers, closely related to the carp family (Cyprinidae).
family of freshwater sucker fish

Examples

Species in the Catostomidae family are often identified by their fleshy, downturned mouths.
The river ecosystem supports several types of Catostomidae, prized by some anglers.
Researchers study Catostomidae to better understand freshwater biodiversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cyprinidae - family of freshwater fishes including carps and minnows

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'katastom-', meaning 'with a down-turned mouth,' and the zoological family suffix '-idae'.

2. NOUN

/ kəˌtɑstəˈmɪdiː / (AmE), / kəˌtɒstəˈmɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of freshwater fish, known as suckers, closely related to carp (Cyprinidae), found mainly in North America.
family of freshwater fish (suckers)

Examples

The Catostomidae are often found in rivers and streams throughout North America.
Biologists studied several species within the Catostomidae to learn about their adaptation to freshwater habitats.
Members of the Catostomidae are commonly referred to as suckers because of their distinctive mouths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cyprinidae - family of freshwater fish including carps and minnows
  • Synonym: sucker - common name for a member of the Catostomidae family

Etymology

From New Latin Catostomidae, based on genus Catostomus (from Greek 'katastoma', meaning 'mouth') and taxonomic suffix -idae.
