agrimonia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæɡrɪˈmoʊniə / (AmE), / ˌæɡrɪˈməʊniə / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Agrimonia, characterized by spikelike clusters of small yellow flowers, commonly found in temperate regions
genus of plants with yellow spikelike flowers

Examples

The field was dotted with clusters of agrimonia and other wildflowers.
Botanists studied the medicinal properties of agrimonia in traditional remedies.
We identified several species of agrimonia along the riverside trail.

Etymology

From Latin *agrimonia*, of Greek origin, possibly from *argemōnē* (poppy, an eye-healing plant).
