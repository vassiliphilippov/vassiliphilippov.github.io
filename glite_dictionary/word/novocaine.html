Novocaine

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊ.vəˌkeɪn / (AmE), / ˈnəʊ.vəˌkeɪn / (BrE)
A brand name for procaine hydrochloride, a local anesthetic used especially in dentistry.
local anesthetic (trade name for procaine)

Examples

The dentist used Novocaine to numb my tooth before filling the cavity.
After a shot of Novocaine, I couldn’t feel the left side of my mouth for hours.
Many patients prefer Novocaine because it allows them to undergo dental procedures with minimal pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procaine - a synthetic local anesthetic drug

Etymology

Trade name formed from 'novo-' (Latin, 'new') and '-caine' (as in 'cocaine').
