denominational

1. ADJ

/ dəˌnɑməˈneɪʃənəl / (AmE), / dɪˌnɒmɪˈneɪʃənəl / (BrE)
connected with or restricted to a particular religious denomination or sect.
relating to a religious denomination

Examples

The school has a denominational affiliation and promotes values of its faith tradition.
She faced denominational prejudice when joining a different church community.
The organization provides denominational instruction to its members.

Synset Examples

denominational prejudice

Related Terms

  • Antonym: non-denominational - not restricted to any particular religious denomination

Etymology

From 'denomination' + '-al'; 'denomination' from Latin denominatio (naming).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02099131-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ dəˌnɑːməˈneɪʃənəl / (AmE), / dɪˌnɒmɪˈneɪʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a particular religious denomination.
relating to a religious denomination

Examples

The school has a strong denominational identity and follows Methodist traditions.
There are many denominational differences among Christian churches.
The organization is not affiliated with any denominational group.

Synset Examples

denominational politics

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nondenominational - not restricted to a particular religious denomination

Etymology

From denomination + -al, meaning 'relating to a denomination'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02721561-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ dɪˌnɑːməˈneɪʃənl̩ / (AmE), / dɪˌnɒmɪˈneɪʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to the face value stated on a banknote, coin, or stamp
relating to the stated face value

Examples

The bank released new denominational series of coins this year.
Collectors were interested in the stamp's denominational value and rarity.
All denominational amounts are shown clearly on the front of each note.

Etymology

From 'denomination' (face value), derived from Latin 'denominatio' (naming).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02721717-a
Synset POS: a
