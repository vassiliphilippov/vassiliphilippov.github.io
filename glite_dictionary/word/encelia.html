Encelia

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈsiːliə / (AmE), / ɛnˈsiːliə / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, commonly known as brittlebush.
genus of shrubs (brittlebush)

Examples

The dry hills were covered in blooming Encelia after the spring rains.
Researchers studied the resilience of Encelia shrubs in arid climates.
Many desert trails are lined with the bright yellow flowers of Encelia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brittlebush - common name for several species of Encelia shrubs

Etymology

Named after German botanist Christoph Entzelt (Latinized as Encelius).
