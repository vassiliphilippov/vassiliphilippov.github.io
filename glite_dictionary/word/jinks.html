jinks

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪŋks / (AmE), / dʒɪŋks / (BrE)
boisterous and playful festivities, especially those involving noisy and mischievous behavior
noisy, playful festivities

Examples

The children's birthday party soon descended into cheerful jinks and laughter.
After the awards ceremony, a night of jinks awaited the triumphant team.
As midnight approached, the jinks in the village square grew louder and more spirited.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revelry - lively and noisy festivities
  • Synonym: merrymaking - the process of enjoying oneself with others, especially at a party
  • Antonym: sobriety - the state of being sober, especially as the opposite of noisy festivity

Etymology

Perhaps from the card game 'high jinks', originally meaning a drinking game in 18th-century Scotland.
