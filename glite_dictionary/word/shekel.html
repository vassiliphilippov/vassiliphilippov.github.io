shekel

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛkəl / (AmE), / ˈʃɛkəl / (BrE)
the basic monetary unit of Israel, equal to 100 agorot
basic unit of currency in Israel

Examples

The price of the souvenir was fifty shekels.
During our trip to Tel Aviv, we exchanged our dollars for shekels.
She paid the taxi driver ten shekels for the short ride.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: agora - a subdivision of the shekel, worth one-hundredth of a shekel in Israel

Etymology

From Hebrew *šéqel*, meaning 'weight' or 'shekel'.
