deviser

1. NOUN

/ dəˈvaɪzər / (AmE), / dɪˈvaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who invents, creates, or plans something, especially in a careful or clever way
person who creates or plans

Examples

As the chief deviser of the strategy, she was credited with the team's success.
The deviser of the puzzle designed it to be both challenging and enjoyable.
Historians praised the deviser of the new voting system for his innovative thinking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: planner - a person who makes plans
  • Synonym: inventor - a person who invents or creates something new

Etymology

From Middle English 'devisour', from Anglo-Norman, from Old French 'deviser' (to divide, devise).
