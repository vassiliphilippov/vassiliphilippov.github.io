vena portae

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪnə ˈpɔːrtaɪ / (AmE), / ˈviːnə ˈpɔːteɪ / (BrE)
a short vein that carries blood from the digestive organs to the liver
vein carrying blood to the liver
medical

Examples

The vena portae delivers nutrient-rich blood to the liver for processing.
A blockage of the vena portae can lead to serious medical complications like portal hypertension.
Surgeons carefully examined the patient's vena portae before proceeding with the liver transplant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: portal vein - the main vein carrying blood to the liver from the intestines

Etymology

From Latin: 'vena' (vein) + 'portae' (of the gate), referring to the portal of the liver.
