established

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈstæblɪʃt / (AmE), / ɪˈstæblɪʃt / (BrE)
having been in existence or accepted for a long time; recognized and generally accepted as valid or important.
recognized and long-existing

Examples

She quickly became known as an established artist in the local art scene.
The established rules of the organization must be followed by all members.
Researchers referenced several established theories when designing the new study.

Synset Examples

the established social order
distrust the constituted authority
a team established as a member of a major league
enjoyed his prestige as an established writer
an established precedent
the established Church

Related Terms

  • Antonym: new - recently created or introduced; not established

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈstæblɪʃt / (AmE), / ɪˈstæblɪʃt / (BrE)
accepted, recognized, or securely settled as true or existing
securely settled or recognized

Examples

The theory is now an established fact within the scientific community.
She joined a well established company with a long history of success.
It is established practice to check all calculations before submitting the report.

Synset Examples

that smoking causes health problems is an accomplished fact

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unsettled - not resolved or firmly decided

Etymology

Past participle of 'establish', from Old French 'estableir', from Latin 'stabilire'.

WordNet ID: oewn-02136915-s

3. ADJ

/ ɪˈstæblɪʃt / (AmE), / ɪˈstæblɪʃt / (BrE)
accepted or proven to be true, especially after careful examination or evidence is provided
proven or accepted as true

Examples

The prosecutor pointed to the established facts that supported their case.
It is now established that the disease is caused by a virus.
She used established methods to ensure the accuracy of her results.

Synset Examples

the established facts in the case

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unproven - not yet established as true or certain
  • Synonym: confirmed - proven to be definitely true

Etymology

Past participle of 'establish', from Old French *establir*, from Latin *stabilire* (to make stable).

Source: WordNet

4. ADJ

/ ɪˈstæblɪʃt / (AmE), / ɪˈstæblɪʃt / (BrE)
(of a plant or animal) introduced from another region and continuing to exist in the wild without cultivation or further human assistance.
introduced species persisting in the wild

Examples

The established population of rabbits has adapted well to the new environment.
Many established species can outcompete native flora in certain regions.
Once a plant is established, it no longer relies on human cultivation to survive.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: invasive - (of a species) tending to spread aggressively and cause harm
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01039104-s
Synset POS: s

5. ADJ

/ ɪˈstæblɪʃt / (AmE), / ɪˈstæblɪʃt / (BrE)
(of a way of thinking, custom, or practice) recognized and generally accepted, especially because it conforms to accepted standards or traditions.
recognized and widely accepted

Examples

The report was based on established scientific principles.
He prefers to follow established procedures at work.
Her views differ from the established norms of the community.

Synset Examples

a conventional view of the world

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unorthodox - contrary to what is usual, traditional, or accepted
