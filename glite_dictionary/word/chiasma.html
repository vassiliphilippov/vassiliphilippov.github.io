chiasma

1. NOUN

/ kaɪˈæzmə / (AmE), / kaɪˈazmə / (BrE)
An intersection or crossing of two tracts, especially nerve fibers, in the form of the letter X.
intersection of two tracts in X shape

Examples

The optic chiasma is where the optic nerves partially cross.
During meiosis, chromosomes form a chiasma as they exchange genetic material.
Damage to the chiasma can lead to visual field defects.

Etymology

From Greek 'chiasma', from 'chiazein' (to mark with an X), from Greek letter 'chi' (Χ).
