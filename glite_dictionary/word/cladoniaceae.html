Cladoniaceae

1. NOUN

/ klæˌdoʊniˈeɪsiːˌiː / (AmE), / klæˌdəʊniˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of lichens that includes reindeer moss and cup lichens
family of lichens (reindeer moss etc.)
medical

Examples

The Cladoniaceae family is best known for containing reindeer moss, vital to Arctic ecosystems.
Several species within the Cladoniaceae are important indicators of air quality due to their sensitivity to pollution.
Scientists are studying the diversity of Cladoniaceae in boreal forests to understand environmental change.

Etymology

From the genus name Cladonia, itself from Greek klados (branch).

2. NOUN

/ klæˌdoʊniˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / klæˌdəʊniˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of lichens characterized by branching, shrubby or cup-shaped forms, including reindeer moss and cup lichens
a family of lichens (including reindeer moss)

Examples

The Cladoniaceae family includes many species commonly known as reindeer moss.
Scientists have been studying the role of Cladoniaceae in Arctic tundra ecosystems.
You can often identify members of the Cladoniaceae by their distinctive cup-like structures.

Etymology

From the genus name Cladonia, after Greek 'klados' (branch), with the Latin suffix '-aceae' denoting a family.
