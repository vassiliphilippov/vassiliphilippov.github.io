demagoguery

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛməˈɡɑɡəri / (AmE), / ˌdɛməˈɡɒɡəri / (BrE)
The practice of seeking support by appealing to the emotions, prejudices, or fears of the public, often through impassioned or manipulative rhetoric.
emotional, manipulative public rhetoric

Examples

The senator's speech was dismissed as mere demagoguery by his critics.
History has often shown the dangers of demagoguery during periods of social unrest.
Rather than offering real solutions, the candidate resorted to demagoguery to win over voters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demagogue - a political leader who seeks support by appealing to popular desires and prejudices

Etymology

From Greek 'demagogos' (leader of the people) + '-ery' (practice or behavior).
