Proto-Indo-European

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊ.toʊˌɪn.doʊ ɪˈrɔ.pi.ən / (AmE), / ˌprəʊ.təʊˌɪn.dəʊ ɪˈrəʊ.pi.ən / (BrE)
the reconstructed ancestor of the Indo-European language family, believed to have been spoken before any recorded history
reconstructed ancestor of Indo-European languages

Examples

Many modern European and Asian languages can trace their roots back to Proto-Indo-European.
Linguists use comparative methods to reconstruct the vocabulary of Proto-Indo-European.
The concept of Proto-Indo-European is fundamental to the study of historical linguistics.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Indo-European - the family of languages descended from Proto-Indo-European

Etymology

From Greek 'protos' (first), Indo-European composite indicating the earliest form of this language family.
