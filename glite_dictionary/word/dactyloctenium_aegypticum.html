Dactyloctenium aegypticum

1. NOUN

/ ˌdæk.tɪ.loʊkˈtiː.ni.əm ˌiːˌdʒɪpˈtɪk.əm / (AmE), / ˌdæk.tɪ.lɒkˈtiː.ni.əm ˌiːˌdʒɪpˈtɪk.əm / (BrE)
a species of creeping grass with finger-like spikes, commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions.
a species of creeping grass
medical

Examples

Dactyloctenium aegypticum is often used as a ground cover in sandy soils.
The spikes of Dactyloctenium aegypticum resemble fingers spread out from a central point.
Farmers sometimes cultivate Dactyloctenium aegypticum as forage for livestock.

Etymology

From Greek dáktylos (finger) and Latin aegyptium (Egyptian), referring to the finger-like appearance of the spikes and its presence in Egypt.
