kerb

1. NOUN

/ kɜrb / (AmE), / kɜːb / (BrE)
The stone or concrete edge forming the boundary between a sidewalk and a road, usually raised.
edge between pavement and road
british

Examples

Mind the kerb when stepping onto the street.
The car accidentally mounted the kerb and damaged its tyre.
Children waited on the kerb for the traffic light to change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curb - the raised edge between sidewalk and street (American spelling)

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'curb', influenced by British usage.
