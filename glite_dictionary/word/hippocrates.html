Hippocrates

1. PROPN

/ hɪˈpɑkrətiːz / (AmE), / hɪˈpɒkrətiːz / (BrE)
(circa 460–377 BC) A Greek physician regarded as the father of medicine and traditionally considered the author of the Hippocratic oath.
Greek physician, father of medicine

Examples

Many medical students still take an oath attributed to Hippocrates.
The writings of Hippocrates influenced Western medicine for centuries.
Hippocrates is often referred to as the father of modern medicine.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἱπποκράτης (*Hippokrátēs*), personal name.
