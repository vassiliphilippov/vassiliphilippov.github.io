Lycopodium lucidulum

1. NOUN

/ laɪ.kəˈpoʊ.di.əm luːˈsɪd.jʊ.ləm / (AmE), / laɪ.kəˈpəʊ.di.əm luːˈsɪd.jʊ.ləm / (BrE)
a species of club moss, also known as shining firmoss, found in North America with shiny, evergreen leaves
a species of club moss (shining firmoss)

Examples

The forest floor was carpeted with Lycopodium lucidulum, its shiny leaves glistening in the morning light.
Botanists often study Lycopodium lucidulum to understand the diversity of club mosses in northern forests.
A patch of Lycopodium lucidulum can be found growing near streams in shady woodlands.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; 'Lycopodium' from Greek 'lykos' (wolf) + 'podion' (foot), and 'lucidulum' from Latin meaning 'shining' or 'bright'.
