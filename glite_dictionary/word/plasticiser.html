plasticiser

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæstɪsaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈplæstɪsaɪzə / (BrE)
a substance added to plastics or other materials to make them more flexible or pliable
substance that makes plastics pliable

Examples

The manufacturer uses a plasticiser to ensure the vinyl remains flexible over time.
Adding a plasticiser improves the workability of concrete mixes.
Some plasticisers can leach out of products, raising safety concerns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plasticizer - alternate spelling, especially in American English

Etymology

From 'plastic' + '-iser', indicating an agent that makes something plastic or flexible.
