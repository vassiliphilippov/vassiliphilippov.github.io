Ovid

1. PROPN

/ ˈoʊ.vɪd / (AmE), / ˈɒ.vɪd / (BrE)
A Roman poet (43 BC – AD 17) celebrated for his elegiac verses on love and his influential works such as 'Metamorphoses'.
famous Roman poet (43 BC – AD 17)

Examples

Ovid's poetry profoundly influenced later European literature and art.
Many students read Ovid in Latin classes to understand classical mythology.
The story of Pygmalion was first popularized in Ovid's 'Metamorphoses'.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Virgil - Another major Roman poet, author of the 'Aeneid'
  • Synonym: Horace - Contemporary Roman lyric poet

Etymology

From Latin Ovidius, the poet's family name.
