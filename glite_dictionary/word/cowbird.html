cowbird

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈkaʊˌbɜːd / (BrE)
A North American blackbird known for following cattle and laying its eggs in the nests of other birds.
North American blackbird laying eggs in other birds' nests

Examples

A cowbird laid its eggs in the nest of a smaller sparrow.
Farmers often spot cowbirds following their herds in the field.
The unique breeding strategy of the cowbird fascinates many birdwatchers.

Etymology

From 'cow' + 'bird', referring to its habit of following cattle.
