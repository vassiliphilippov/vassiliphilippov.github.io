humorously

1. ADV

/ ˈhjuːmərəsli / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmərəsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows humor or is intended to make people laugh.
in a humorous manner

Examples

She humorously recounted her misadventures during the trip.
The headline was humorously phrased to catch readers' attention.
Despite the bad news, he responded humorously, lightening the mood in the room.

Synset Examples

Dickens had humorously suggested a special service of intercession at St. Paul's Cathedral

Related Terms

  • Antonym: seriously - in a serious manner

Etymology

From humorous + -ly, where 'humorous' is from Old French *umor*, from Latin *humor* (moisture, fluid), originally referring to bodily fluids believed to affect temperament.
