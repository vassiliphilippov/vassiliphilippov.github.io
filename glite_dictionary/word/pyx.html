pyx

1. NOUN

/ pɪks / (AmE), / pɪks / (BrE)
A box or chest in which coins from the mint are stored while awaiting assay.
box for coins awaiting assay

Examples

The newly minted coins were sealed in a pyx before undergoing official testing.
Traditionally, the pyx was opened during a formal ceremony to check the quality of the coins.
A rare pyx from the seventeenth century was displayed at the numismatic exhibition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assay - an analysis to determine composition or quality, especially of precious metals

Etymology

From Latin pyxis (box), from Greek puxis (boxwood box).

2. NOUN

/ pɪks / (AmE), / pɪks / (BrE)
a container, usually made of precious metal, used in churches to hold the consecrated wafers of the Eucharist
container for Eucharist wafers

Examples

During the service, the priest placed the consecrated wafers in a pyx for later distribution to the sick.
The small gold pyx was carefully carried to the hospital by the minister.
She opened the ornate pyx to show the hosts inside before the procession.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ciborium - a vessel for holding the Eucharistic bread

Etymology

From Latin pyxis (box), from Greek pyxís.
