Fredericksburg

1. PROPN

/ ˈfrɛdərɪksˌbɜrɡ / (AmE), / ˈfrɛdərɪksˌbɜːɡ / (BrE)
A town in northeastern Virginia, United States, located on the Rappahannock River; notable for its historical significance, especially during the American Civil War.
town in northeastern Virginia, USA

Examples

Tourists often visit Fredericksburg to explore its many Civil War battlefields.
The train from Washington, D.C., makes regular stops at Fredericksburg.
He moved to Fredericksburg for its charming downtown and historic atmosphere.

Etymology

Named after Prince Frederick, son of King George II of Great Britain.
