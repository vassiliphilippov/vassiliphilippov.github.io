gratifying

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡræt̬.əˌfaɪ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡræt.ɪ.faɪ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
Giving pleasure or satisfaction; pleasing to the mind or feelings.
pleasing; giving satisfaction

Examples

Winning the award was a truly gratifying experience for the whole team.
It was gratifying to see her hard work finally recognized by the company.
The support from the community was both unexpected and gratifying.

Synset Examples

sweet revenge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pleasing - giving pleasure or satisfaction
  • Antonym: disappointing - failing to fulfill expectations or hopes

Etymology

From the verb 'gratify', which comes from French 'gratifier', from Latin 'gratificari' (to do a favor to).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡræt̬.əˌfaɪ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡræt.ɪ.faɪ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
Giving pleasure or satisfaction; making you feel pleased or rewarded.
giving satisfaction or pleasure

Examples

Her kind words were especially gratifying after the effort I put into the project.
The team's success was personally gratifying to everyone involved.
