testosterone

1. NOUN

/ tɛˈstɑːstəˌroʊn / (AmE), / tɛˈstɒstərəʊn / (BrE)
a naturally occurring hormone produced mainly in the testes, responsible for the development of male secondary sexual characteristics
male sex hormone
medical

Examples

In some cases, low testosterone can cause fatigue and decreased libido.
Athletes are sometimes tested for abnormal levels of testosterone in their blood.
During puberty, testosterone levels rise, leading to physical changes in boys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: androgen - a group of hormones that regulate male traits and reproductive activity

Etymology

From testis (Latin for 'testicle') and the chemical suffix -sterone (from sterol and hormone).
