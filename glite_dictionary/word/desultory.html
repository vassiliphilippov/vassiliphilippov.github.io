desultory

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛsəlˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈdɛz(ə)ltəri / (BrE)
lacking a clear plan, purpose, or enthusiasm; moving from one subject to another in a disconnected or unfocused way.
lacking plan or focus; aimless

Examples

He made a few desultory attempts at cleaning before giving up.
Their desultory conversation drifted from sports to politics without much focus.
Her desultory reading habits meant she never finished a book.

Synset Examples

desultory thoughts
the desultory conversation characteristic of cocktail parties

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aimless - lacking purpose or direction
  • Synonym: random - done without a definite plan or pattern
  • Antonym: purposeful - having a clear aim or goal

Etymology

From Latin desultorius (‘leaping, skipping’), from desilire (‘to leap down’).
