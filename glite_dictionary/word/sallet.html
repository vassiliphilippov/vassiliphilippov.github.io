sallet

1. NOUN

/ ˈsælɪt / (AmE), / ˈsalɪt / (BrE)
a light helmet with a visor slit, worn in Europe during the late Middle Ages
light medieval helmet with a visor slit

Examples

The knight donned his sallet before heading into battle.
In the museum, a beautifully preserved sallet is displayed among other medieval armor.
Artists often depict late medieval soldiers wearing a sallet in battle scenes.

Etymology

From Old French *salade*, from Italian *celata* meaning 'helmet'.
