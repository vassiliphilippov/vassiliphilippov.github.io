Byelorussian

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪəloʊˈrʊʃən / (AmE), / ˌbaɪələˈrʊʃən / (BrE)
A native or inhabitant of Byelorussia (now known as Belarus).
native or inhabitant of Byelorussia

Examples

Many Byelorussians emigrated during the early 20th century due to political upheaval.
The festival celebrates traditional music and dance of the Byelorussian people.
After the dissolution of the Soviet Union, several Byelorussians returned to their homeland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Belarusian - a native or inhabitant of Belarus

Etymology

From Russian 'Byelorussiya' (White Russia), an older name for Belarus.

2. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪjəloʊˈrʌsiən / (AmE), / ˌbaɪələˈrʌsiən / (BrE)
the East Slavic language spoken in Belarus; also called Belarusian.
language of Belarus

Examples

She learned to speak Byelorussian while living in Minsk for two years.
The official documents were translated into Russian, Polish, and Byelorussian.
There are several dialects within the Byelorussian language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Belarusian - the modern name of the language spoken in Belarus
  • Degree Lower: Russian - the East Slavic language spoken in Russia and parts of Eastern Europe
  • Degree Lower: Ukrainian - the East Slavic language spoken in Ukraine

Etymology

From Byelorussia, former name for Belarus, combined with '-an'; Byelorussia from Russian 'Byelaya Rus’' (White Russia).
