deforestation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːˌfɔr.ɪˈsteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːˌfɒr.ɪˈsteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of removing or clearing forests or trees from an area, usually on a large scale.
removal of trees or forests

Examples

Large-scale deforestation in the Amazon threatens countless species of plants and animals.
Many countries are taking action to slow the rate of deforestation and protect natural habitats.
Excessive deforestation can lead to soil erosion and increased greenhouse gases.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reforestation - the process of planting trees to restore a forested area
  • Synonym: logging - the activity of cutting down trees for commercial use

Etymology

From French 'déforestation', from 'déforester' (to clear of trees), ultimately from Latin 'foris' (outdoors).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdiːfəˌrɛstəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːˌfɒrɪˈsteɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of clearing or removing forests or stands of trees, often resulting in damage to the environment.
clearing or removal of forests

Examples

Rampant deforestation has led to a significant loss of biodiversity in the region.
Many organizations are working to combat deforestation and promote sustainable land management.
The Amazon rainforest is under threat due to ongoing deforestation for agriculture and logging.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: afforestation - the process of planting trees to create forests
  • Antonym: reforestation - the process of replanting trees in an area

Etymology

Formed from de- (removal) + forest + -ation (action/process).
