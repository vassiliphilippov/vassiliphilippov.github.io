to rediscover

1. VERB

/ ˌriːdɪˈskʌvər / (AmE), / ˌriːdɪsˈkʌvə / (BrE)
to discover something again after having forgotten or lost awareness of it
to discover again

Examples

During her travels, she managed to rediscover her passion for painting.
After years apart, the friends decided to rediscover the city they once loved.
Many adults rediscover childhood interests when they have children of their own.

Synset Examples

I rediscovered the books that I enjoyed as a child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to discover - to find something for the first time or become aware of something previously unknown

Etymology

From re- (again) + discover (from Old French descovrir, from Latin discooperire: to uncover).
