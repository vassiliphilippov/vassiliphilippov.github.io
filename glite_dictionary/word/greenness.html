greenness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrin.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɡriːn.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being green in color, especially resembling the color of growing grass
the quality of being green in color

Examples

The greenness of the newly sprouted leaves signaled the arrival of spring.
She admired the deep greenness of the emerald in her ring.
After weeks of drought, the steady rain brought back the greenness to the fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: green - a color resembling fresh grass

Etymology

From Middle English grenesse, from Old English grēnnes (greenness, youth, inexperience), from grēne (green).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡrin.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɡriːn.nəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of not being ripe, especially in fruit or vegetables
unripe state (of fruit/vegetables)

Examples

The greenness of the bananas meant they were not yet ready to eat.
She judged the greenness of the tomatoes by their color and texture.
Due to its greenness, the fruit tasted sour and was difficult to digest.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ripeness - the state of being fully mature or developed, especially in fruit or vegetables

Etymology

From Middle English greenness, from green + -ness.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡrin.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɡriːn.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or appearance of being lush and rich with healthy, flourishing vegetation
lush, healthy appearance of vegetation

Examples

After the long rains, the valley was filled with a vibrant greenness that stretched for miles.
The greenness of the lawn made the garden look especially inviting in spring.
Photographers often seek the deep greenness of forests for their landscape shots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verdancy - the quality of being green and lush with vegetation

Etymology

From Old English 'grēnnes', from 'grēne' (green) + '-ness'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
