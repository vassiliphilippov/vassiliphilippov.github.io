Picrasma

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈkræzmə / (AmE), / pɪˈkrazmə / (BrE)
A small genus of deciduous trees found in tropical regions of America and Asia.
genus of tropical deciduous trees

Examples

The bark of Picrasma is used in traditional medicine for its bitter properties.
Several species of Picrasma are cultivated for their ornamental value in tropical gardens.
Researchers studied the ecological role of Picrasma trees in Asian rainforests.

Etymology

From Greek *pikros* (bitter) referring to the bitter taste of some species, plus the standard botanical suffix -asma.

2. NOUN

/ pɪˈkræzmə / (AmE), / pɪˈkræzmə / (BrE)
A small genus of deciduous trees native to tropical America and Asia, known for their bitter bark and use in medicine.
genus of tropical deciduous trees

Examples

The bark of Picrasma is often used in traditional medicine for its bitter properties.
Several species of Picrasma can be found in the tropical forests of Southeast Asia.
Researchers are studying Picrasma for potential new pharmaceutical compounds.

Etymology

From Greek pikrasmos (bitterness), referring to the taste of the bark.
