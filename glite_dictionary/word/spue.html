to spue

1. VERB

/ spjuː / (AmE), / spjuː / (BrE)
to eject saliva, phlegm, or similar substances from the mouth
expel saliva or phlegm from the mouth
archaic

Examples

In disgust, he spued the bitter medicine into the sink.
The dragon spued fire and smoke from its nostrils during the play.
She spued out the salty water after an unexpected wave hit her face.

Synset Examples

The father of the victim spat at the alleged murderer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spit - to eject saliva or another substance from the mouth

Etymology

From Middle English 'spuen', from Old English 'spīwan' (to spit); related to German 'speien' and Latin 'spuere'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00101710-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ spjuː / (AmE), / spjuː / (BrE)
to eject the contents of the stomach through the mouth; to vomit forcefully
to vomit forcefully
archaic

Examples

He was so seasick that he began to spue over the side of the boat.
The child felt ill and spued his dinner onto the floor.
After a night of heavy drinking, she found herself spuing in the bathroom.

Synset Examples

After drinking too much, the students vomited
He purged continuously
The patient regurgitated the food we gave him last night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vomit - to eject the contents of the stomach through the mouth
  • Synonym: to regurgitate - to bring swallowed food up again to the mouth
  • Synonym: to spew - to expel something forcefully; to vomit

Etymology

Middle English, variant of 'spew', from Old English 'spīwan' (to spit, vomit).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00076153-v
Synset POS: v
