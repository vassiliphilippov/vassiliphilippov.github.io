Balzacian

1. ADJ

/ bælˈzækiən / (AmE), / balˈzækɪən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of Honore de Balzac or his literary works, especially in depicting complex, realistic social environments.
relating to Honore de Balzac or his works

Examples

Her novels create a Balzacian panorama of society, capturing the ambitions and struggles of a wide range of characters.
The film offers a Balzacian portrait of 19th-century Paris, with attention to social detail and personal ambition.
Critics praised the Balzacian depth of his storytelling, noting its realism and complex character development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dickensian - Relating to Charles Dickens or his works
  • Synonym: Proustian - Relating to Marcel Proust or his works

Etymology

From the name of Honore de Balzac (1799–1850), French novelist, plus the English adjectival suffix -ian.
