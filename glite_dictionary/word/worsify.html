to worsify

1. VERB

/ ˈwɜːrsɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɜːsɪfaɪ / (BrE)
(informal) To make something worse; to worsen.
to make worse, worsen (informal)
informal

Examples

Sometimes, trying to fix a small issue can accidentally worsify the situation.
His careless comments only served to worsify the tension in the team.
You shouldn't worsify your problems by ignoring them; take action instead.

Synset Examples

She tends to worsify her problems instead of solving them.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to worsen - To make or become worse

Etymology

Formed by analogy with verbs ending in -ify (such as 'clarify'), from 'worse' + '-ify'.
