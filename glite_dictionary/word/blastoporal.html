blastoporal

1. ADJ

/ blæˈstɑːpərəl / (AmE), / blɑːˈstɒpərəl / (BrE)
relating to or associated with a blastopore, the opening that forms during early embryonic development in some animals.
relating to a blastopore (embryology)
medical

Examples

The blastoporal region is critical for gastrulation in amphibian embryos.
Researchers examined the changes in blastoporal lip cells during development.
Displacement of the blastoporal opening can affect the body axis formation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blastopore - the opening that develops in the embryo of some animals

Etymology

From 'blastopore', with the suffix '-al' meaning 'pertaining to'. 'Blastopore' from Greek blasto- (germ, sprout) + pore (passage).
