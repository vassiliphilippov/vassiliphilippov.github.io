milline

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɪˌlaɪn / (BrE)
An advertising unit equal to one agate line appearing in one million copies of a publication.
advertising unit: one agate line per million copies

Examples

The price of the newspaper advertisement was calculated in millines to determine its total cost.
Because the ad ran in a million copies, its rate was set at one milline per agate line.
Historic rate cards often quoted charges in terms of millines for large-scale print campaigns.

Etymology

Blend of 'milli-' (from Latin 'mille', thousand) and 'line'.
