Chlamydera nuchalis

1. NOUN

/ klæˌmɪdəˌɹə nuːˈkælɪs / (AmE), / klæˌmɪdəˌɹə njuːˈkælɪs / (BrE)
A large species of bowerbird native to northern Australia, known for its elaborate courtship displays.
large bowerbird of northern Australia

Examples

The Chlamydera nuchalis constructs elaborate bowers to attract potential mates.
Researchers observed the unique courtship behavior of Chlamydera nuchalis in the wild.
Northern Australia is home to the remarkable Chlamydera nuchalis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: great bowerbird - common name for Chlamydera nuchalis

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Chlamydera' meaning 'cloak bearer' and 'nuchalis' meaning 'of the nape' in Latin, referring to the bird’s plumage.
