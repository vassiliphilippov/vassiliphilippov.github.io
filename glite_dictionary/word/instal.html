to instal

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈstɔl / (AmE), / ɪnˈstɑːl / (BrE)
to formally place someone in an office, position, or role, often with a ceremony
formally place in office or position

Examples

After winning the election, she was instaled as the new mayor during a public ceremony.
The university will instal its new chancellor next week in a formal event.
Traditionally, the judge must be officially instaled before presiding over cases.

Synset Examples

the new president was installed immediately after the election

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to install - to formally place someone in an office, position, or role

Etymology

From Medieval Latin installare, from in- (in) + stallum (stall, place).

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈstɔl / (AmE), / ɪnˈstɑːl / (BrE)
to set up equipment, machinery, or software so that it is ready to use
set up equipment or software for use
british

Examples

The technician will instal the new air conditioning unit tomorrow.
Before you use the software, make sure you instal it correctly on your computer.
They decided to instal a security camera outside the entrance.

Synset Examples

install the washer and dryer
We put in a new sink

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to install - set up equipment, machinery, or software so it is ready to use (American spelling)
  • Antonym: remove - take something away or uninstall it

Etymology

From French 'installer', from Italian 'installare', from Medieval Latin 'installare' (to put in place).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01572394-v
Synset POS: v
