stunned

1. ADJ

/ stʌnd / (AmE), / stʌnd / (BrE)
knocked unconscious or unable to act, usually as a result of a heavy blow or shock
knocked unconscious; dazed

Examples

He was stunned by a blow to the head and collapsed on the ground.
After the accident, she lay stunned and unresponsive for several minutes.
The boxer remained stunned after taking a powerful punch in the final round.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unconscious - not awake or aware, especially after being hit
  • Antonym: alert - fully awake and attentive

2. ADJ

/ stʌnd / (AmE), / stʌnd / (BrE)
unable to think clearly or react, often because of shock, surprise, or a heavy blow
unable to react due to shock

Examples

She was stunned by the unexpected news of her friend's arrival.
After hitting his head, he lay stunned on the ground, unable to move.
The audience sat stunned, struggling to process what had just happened.

Synset Examples

he had a dazed expression on his face
lay semiconscious, stunned (or stupefied) by the blow
was stupid from fatigue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dazed - unable to think clearly, often because of a shock or blow
  • Antonym: alert - quick to notice and react to things

Etymology

From Middle English 'stunnen', from Old French 'estoner' (to stun, daze), from Latin 'extonare'.

3. ADJ

/ stʌnd / (AmE), / stʌnd / (BrE)
so shocked or surprised that you are temporarily unable to react
so shocked or surprised you can't react

Examples

She looked stunned when she heard the unexpected news.
The team was stunned by their loss in the final seconds of the game.
He stood in stunned silence, unable to process what had happened.

Synset Examples

an amazed audience gave the magician a standing ovation
I stood enthralled, astonished by the vastness and majesty of the cathedral
astounded viewers wept at the pictures from the Oklahoma City bombing
stood in stunned silence
stunned scientists found not one but at least three viruses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astonished - feeling great surprise or amazement
  • Synonym: shocked - feeling a sudden, intense surprise or disturbance
  • Antonym: unfazed - not perturbed or affected by surprise or shock

Etymology

Past participle of 'stun', from Old French 'estoner' (to stun or astonish), from Latin 'extonare' (to thunder).
