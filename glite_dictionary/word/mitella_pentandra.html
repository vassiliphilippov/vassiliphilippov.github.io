Mitella pentandra

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈtɛlə pɛnˈtændrə / (AmE), / mɪˈtɛlə pɛnˈtændrə / (BrE)
A small perennial plant with leaves forming a basal cluster and slender racemes of tiny greenish flowers, native to northwestern North America, California, and Colorado.
a small North American plant with greenish flowers

Examples

Mitella pentandra can be found in the moist, shaded forests of the Pacific Northwest.
Botanists often distinguish Mitella pentandra by its tiny greenish flowers arranged in slender racemes.
Hikers in Colorado sometimes encounter clusters of Mitella pentandra along alpine streams.

Etymology

From New Latin, genus name 'Mitella' and specific epithet 'pentandra' meaning 'five stamens'.
