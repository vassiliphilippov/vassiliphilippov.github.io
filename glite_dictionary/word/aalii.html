aalii

1. NOUN

/ ɑːˈɑːliː / (AmE), / ɑːˈɑːliː / (BrE)
a small Hawaiian tree (Dodonaea viscosa) with hard, dark wood, often used in carving and landscaping.
Hawaiian tree with hard, dark wood

Examples

The craftsman chose aalii wood for the sculpture because of its strength and dark color.
Many native Hawaiian landscapes include the resilient aalii tree.
Researchers studied the role of aalii in preserving local ecosystems.

Etymology

From Hawaiian 'ā’ali‘i', the local name for the species Dodonaea viscosa.
