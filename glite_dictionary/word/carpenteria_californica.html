Carpenteria californica

1. NOUN

/ kɑːrˌpɛntɛəriə ˌkælɪˈfɔrnɪkə / (AmE), / kɑːˌpɛntɪərɪə ˌkælɪˈfɔːnɪkə / (BrE)
An evergreen shrub native to California, known for its glossy opposite leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers.
California evergreen shrub with white flowers

Examples

The garden's centerpiece was a beautiful Carpenteria californica in full bloom.
Many native plant enthusiasts admire the resilience of Carpenteria californica in dry climates.
We planted a Carpenteria californica to attract pollinators with its fragrant white flowers.

Etymology

Genus name honors Dr. William Marbury Carpenter, an American botanist; species named for California, its native region.
