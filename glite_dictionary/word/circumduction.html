circumduction

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɝː.kəmˈdʌk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɜː.kəmˈdʌk.ʃən / (BrE)
the circular movement of a limb or the eye, allowing the distal end to move in a circle while the proximal end remains relatively stationary
circular movement of a limb or eye
medical

Examples

The physical therapist assessed the patient's shoulder circumduction after surgery.
During the exercise, try to perform full hip circumduction to improve joint flexibility.
Eye circumduction can help relieve strain from excessive screen time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rotation - turning a body part around its axis

Etymology

From Latin circumductio (a leading around), from circum- (around) + ducere (to lead).
