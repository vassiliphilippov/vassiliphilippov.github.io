densitometry

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛn.səˈtɑː.mə.tɹi / (AmE), / ˌdɛn.sɪˈtɒm.ə.tri / (BrE)
the measurement of the optical density of a material, typically by passing light through it and measuring transmission
measurement of optical density

Examples

The technician used densitometry to analyze the photographic negatives.
Bone densitometry is often used to assess osteoporosis risk.
Advances in digital densitometry have improved the accuracy of laboratory measurements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: densitometer - an instrument for measuring optical density

Etymology

From Latin densitas (density) and -metry (measurement).
