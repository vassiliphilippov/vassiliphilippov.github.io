shrink-wrap

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃrɪŋk ˌræp / (AmE), / ˈʃrɪŋk ˌræp / (BrE)
a clinging, transparent plastic film that is wrapped around an object and shrinks tightly to it when heated, typically used for packaging.
tight plastic packaging film

Examples

The DVDs were sealed in shrink-wrap to protect them from dust.
She removed the shrink-wrap before opening the new appliance.
The gift basket arrived fully covered in clear shrink-wrap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plastic wrap - thin, flexible, clear plastic film used for sealing food items

Etymology

Formed from 'shrink' + 'wrap', referring to the wrapping's property of shrinking when heated.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃrɪŋk ræp / (AmE), / ˈʃrɪŋk ræp / (BrE)
a thin plastic film that is wrapped tightly around objects and shrinks when heated, used especially for packaging and protection
thin plastic film for packaging, shrinks when heated

Examples

The DVDs came sealed in shrink-wrap to prevent tampering.
Fresh produce is often covered in shrink-wrap at the supermarket.
After manufacturing, each toy was packaged using shrink-wrap for added protection.

Synset Examples

They decided to shrink-wrap the pallets for shipping.
I saw them shrink-wrap the items before the sale.
The new product was shrink-wrapped to ensure freshness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plastic wrap - thin, clear plastic film used for sealing food items

Etymology

From the words 'shrink' and 'wrap', referring to the film shrinking tightly when heated.
