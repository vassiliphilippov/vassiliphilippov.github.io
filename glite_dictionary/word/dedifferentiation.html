dedifferentiation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːˌdɪfəˌrɛn(t)ʃiˈeɪʃ(ə)n / (AmE), / ˌdiːˌdɪfəˌrɛn(t)ʃiˈeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the process by which specialized cells or tissues lose their specialized features and revert to a more primitive or unspecialized state
loss of specialized cell function
medical

Examples

Tumor formation is often associated with dedifferentiation of normal tissue cells.
The loss of muscle identity during repair is a result of cellular dedifferentiation.
Research focuses on reversing the dedifferentiation that occurs in degenerating neurons.

Etymology

From de- (expressing reversal) + differentiation.
