solo

1. VERB

/ ˈsoʊloʊ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊləʊ / (BrE)
(of a pilot) to fly an aircraft alone, without a co-pilot or passengers.
fly an aircraft alone (pilot term)

Examples

After months of instruction, she finally got permission to solo for the first time.
Student pilots typically solo after demonstrating basic flying skills.
He soloed yesterday and couldn't stop talking about the experience.

Etymology

From Italian 'solo' (alone), from Latin 'solus' (alone).

Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊloʊ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊləʊ / (BrE)
a flight in which a pilot flies an aircraft alone, without any other person on board
a flight with only the pilot on board

Examples

The instructor watched from the ground during his student's solo.
Every pilot remembers their very first solo as a milestone in their training.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: duet - a flight or performance by two people together

Etymology

From Italian 'solo', meaning 'alone', from Latin 'solus'.
