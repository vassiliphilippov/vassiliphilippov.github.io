whiner

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪnər / (AmE), / ˈwaɪnə / (BrE)
a person who complains or voices dissatisfaction frequently and in a petty or bothersome manner
person who complains a lot

Examples

No one likes working with a whiner who never sees the positive side of things.
Instead of being a whiner, try focusing on problem-solving.
My brother used to be a whiner when he was a child, always complaining about everything.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complainer - someone who complains frequently
  • Synonym: grumbler - someone who grumbles or complains habitually
  • Antonym: stoic - someone who endures hardship without complaint

Etymology

From 'whine' (Old English 'hwīnan', to whine, make a humming sound) + '-er' (denoting agent noun).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪnər / (AmE), / ˈwaɪnə / (BrE)
a person who complains excessively or in an annoying manner
a person who complains a lot

Examples

My little brother can be a real whiner when he doesn't get his way.
The coach told the team there was no room for a whiner on the field.
Instead of being a whiner, she took action to improve her situation.

Synset Examples

Stop being such a whiner and face the challenge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complainer - a person who expresses dissatisfaction or annoyance frequently
  • Synonym: grumbler - someone who complains persistently or mutters in discontent
  • Antonym: cheerleader - a person who encourages or supports others enthusiastically

Etymology

From 'whine' + '-er', where 'whine' comes from Old English 'hwīnan' (to whiz, whistle).
