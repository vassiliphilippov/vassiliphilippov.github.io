tailstock

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪl.stɑk / (AmE), / ˈteɪl.stɒk / (BrE)
the adjustable part of a lathe that slides along the bed and supports the free end of a workpiece, aligning with the headstock
movable support on a lathe

Examples

The machinist adjusted the tailstock to securely support the long metal rod during turning.
Before drilling, the technician locked the tailstock in place to prevent any movement of the workpiece.
Proper alignment of the tailstock ensures precision and stability when machining cylindrical parts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: headstock - the fixed end of a lathe that holds the workpiece and often houses the driving mechanism

Etymology

From 'tail' (end) + 'stock' (block or support); refers to its position at the tail or end of the lathe.
