cardoon

1. NOUN

/ kɑrˈduːn / (AmE), / kɑːˈduːn / (BrE)
a tall thistle-like Mediterranean plant, related to the artichoke, cultivated for its edible root and blanched leaf stalks
Mediterranean plant with edible stalks, related to artichoke

Examples

The cardoon is prized in Mediterranean cuisine for its tender blanched stalks.
She planted a cardoon in her kitchen garden next to the artichokes.
Only the stalks of the cardoon are typically eaten, often cooked in stews or baked dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artichoke - a Mediterranean plant cultivated for its edible flower heads

Etymology

From French 'cardon', from Latin 'carduus' (thistle).

2. NOUN

/ kɑrˈduːn / (AmE), / kɑːˈduːn / (BrE)
a southern European plant (Cynara cardunculus) with spiny leaves and purple flowers, grown for its edible leafstalks and roots
a plant with edible leafstalks and roots

Examples

The cardoon thrives in Mediterranean climates and is prized for its tender, artichoke-like stems.
At the market, she bought fresh cardoon to use in a traditional Italian stew.
Gardeners recommend blanching the cardoon stalks before harvesting to improve their flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artichoke - a similar plant with edible flower heads

Etymology

From French 'cardon', from Spanish 'cardón', from Latin 'carduus' (thistle).
